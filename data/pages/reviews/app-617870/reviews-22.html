\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust gonna try to balance out the recent underseved hate on this dlc... I bought it for full price which I almost never do, but I've been loving the TWW games so much that I had to. I had a good time with the campaign and the balance issues that others are complaining about are quite exaggurated. Yes the tomb kings get armies for free with no upkeep, but their economy is absolute garbage so paying for infrastructure is extremely burdensome of you're trying for rapid expansion. Other factions can just disband one or two of their stacks, play safe with their armies at critical territories and build up their infrastructure fast as hell. Not so with the tomb kings. You're crap economy is your crap economy and you need to be constantly raiding / sacking outlying territories to keep it going or the confederations forming around you will overwhelm you. I dunno about lower difficulties though, but playing on "very hard" I didn't find the tomb kings to be easier than any other race.

My main criticism is the books of nagash end game. Especially on higher difficulties, the vortex campaign can really run away from you, but I didn't feel that desperation with gathering the books. They were just kinda always there just waiting for me to come get them. Maybe if you had to collect all of them instead of just 5? That would force more conflict with the dark and high elves that just went insane with confederations by the end of my Khalida campaign. They both were at war with me but were in such distant lands I didn't need to deal with them much.\t\t\t\t\t
