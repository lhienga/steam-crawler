\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey really shake up the traditional style. You don't have ANY upkeep costs, but you make very little money (at first). You rely on unit caps for anything past spearmen, with military buildings increasing those caps. Army caps are limited to their technology tree and special mechanic (Canopic jars). Their vortex campaign (if that's important to you) is about collecting some special books around the map instead of ritual resources, so potentially more traveling if you aren't just trying to expand out and paint the map.

Army wise they are similar to undead from game 1, but they have ranged. You have your chaff units that gets thrown away to either tarpit or soak up damage, then you have your monsters (and archers and other ranged) as the backbone. Now tomb king monsters are BEAUTIFUL. They're called constructs and they are pretty well done, and lots of fun to play with. Especially the tomb scorpion which is arguably one of the best units for groups, not becasue of the stat block, but because of the animations. To quote MilkandCookies "These suckers will dodge, duck, dip, dive, and dodge anything enemy infantry will throw at them."

The sad part of the dlc is Arkhan the Black. He's alright, but they didn't give him anything to support the vamp units he gets meaning you really don't use them that long. Still, would recommend, but at $18 I'd say wait for a sale for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is by far the best DLC for Warhammer 2 (unless you still need WH1 than buy that first), because unlike the Tomb Kings in 8th edition the Tomb Kings in Total War are IMO the best developed race in the game and here's why:

- They need to build buildings to unlock a certain AMOUNT of a unit. This makes building doomstacks nigh impossible and thus makes it so that even in the late game, you will still have to create varied armies. It also makes it so that you don't have to deal with those annoying supply lines as you don't pay upkeep.
- The Vortex Campaign doesn't have a timer and there are no competing factions. You can take as long or short as you want to complete the objectives of collecting the books of Nagash.
- The books of Nagash all give your faction a buff so unlike the other vortex objectives they are actually rewarding.
- The fact that you don't have to pay for troops (after you have bought the building) is balanced by the poor economy of this faction. This means that every time you do earn gold, it feels like a big permanent upgrade to your kingdom.
- The Tomb Kings are quite neutral meaning that you can play the diplomacy game how you want, rather than being forced into the order/undead/greenskin/evil corner. Especially because gold is so important, diplomacy/trading also has some real value for this faction.
- The jar mechanic is not too intrusive (like the old WE amber mechanic) but does remain relevant unlike for an example the dwarven mechanic which you can just ignore.
- The unit roster is very varied so unlike many other factions you won't be doing the exact same thing every battle. (Vampires: Wind of Death spam, Skaven: Corner Camping w ranged/artillery, etc.) + Ushabtis are badass
- Lords all feel unique enough and have a unique enough starting location to play multiple campaigns of this faction, without feeling too repetitive.
- Settra doesn't serve\t\t\t\t\t
