\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly the only reason to play as the tomb kings are for their gorgeous and powerful end-game units. I love being able to field large and free armies but Without spamming or cheesing various exploits (autoresolve missles, sack cities):

-their economy is absolute crap in the beginning and is almost entirely reliant on trade (doable but annoying)
-their traditional units are absolutely useless in every regard other than waiting for the contructs to get there. Cav is slightly redeemable but mainly because I use those types of units in an ok manner so I'm a bit biased.
-constructs are locked behind settlement levels (duh), but those settlements experience some EXTREMELY slow growth. I thought Norsca was bad, TKs are an absolute grind to Tier 3

*bonus round : their book of magic is designed to offset their unit's overall not-goodness except the catch is that they have one or two AoE spells, so good luck with the micro of deciding who gets buffed and who doesn't while the Bretonnians are playing red rover with the one construct you start with.

Tomb kings can indeed be a powerhouse faction...after spending about 100 turns just clicking end turn over and over waiting to get your settlements to build up. on top of that, their lords are slightly overwhelming. Settra is great for cav and stabalizing rapid expansion. Arkhan is a powerhouse of a mage, and (I forget her name)'s skills and campaign effects make fighting as the TKs slightly more fun. All in all, the biggest thing that will ruin the TKs for you is their slow growth; you legitimately need to raise armies for the purpose of subverting their innately slow infrastructure development. You, as a player need to think about what kind of time investment that is. That's a lot of turns to create a 'campaign army' for the purpose of getting provinces developed fast enough to compete with a fixed Malus Darkblade. How much do you love the egyptian motif? How often do you want to hear settra talk about the desert heat? The answer for me is "wait for a sale and take a week off from work"

Edit: I completely neglected to mention their item crafting mechanic. it's awesome to be able to deck out all my lords and heroes with what they need to be great. but without expansion, you really will forget it exists until you go through and start spending money that's already not so readily available.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe tomb kings don't confederate and their playstyle is slow. Yes, the point is to be slow, but while a lot of armies have gotten huge amounts of attention, the tomb kings remain slow, clunky, and a force very similar to early Bretonnia in extreme slow motion in that you play the early game more like a puzzle than a grand strategy. The issue for tomb kings is that the early game can last until other nations are playing late game and chaos is destroyed. Provided the AI has ignored you, you will be out of money from bribes. Provided it has not, you are gone, because nothing in your roster does anything but cry against midgame units before turn 60.

Economically, the tomb kings are the definition of poverty. Yes, sure, you don't have to pay for units, but starting units are easily and effortlessly swept aside by even the most trivial of shielded opponents, serving as distractions. You get the anvil, and then you get the hammer by turn 100+, until which your army is completely worthless chaff that can neither attack nor after the cavalry changes stall very well. Early buildings will see you supplied with up to 50 gold/turn and prices in the thousands in exchange for next to nothing, with you being forced to be a horde and a non-horde faction at once with none of the benefits of either, and forcing you into wars to advance at all, where your MO is praying to sigmar that they have someone else to care about, no friends, no capacity to ambush, no ability to fight your starting stack, and no real way to win, but also a lot of money. You want to fight armies that are really strong but that can't hurt your worthless chaff, and you want to trade away your money for jars but your jars for money. Early events are normally negative, being things like "lose X or Y", and you have no real allies.

Some units are effectively worthless, too- flocks of djaf deal so little damage they can lose to unassisted artillery. Skeleton horses are pathetic beyond measure and lose to goblins, despite requiring a T2 building slot and thousands of gold to produce; sure, they're free, but they're not better than skeletons, you just buy them like they are. Late game, unless you fell for noob traps you'll be met with dullness in lack of challenge, but 'early' game you will struggle to so much as dent a single enemy army with your 2 stacks of skeletons that take 8+ turns to replace- if the enemy has two stacks, you're basically ensured a loss before turn 60. This means that you will not be expanding practically at all before turn 60, because doing so is delayed suicide, and you will need to rely on a trading partner for funds and special units alongside your FLAT RATE OF TEN UNITS OF TRADE GOODS that cost 750 to make.

This gives you access to a worse version of the dwarven crafting system, which allows you to make magic items that generally aren't as good as the dwarven ones, but frequently cost much, much more! You also have to use this to get your regiments of renown, and your first one is the flyer unit that can't kill artillery. The others need gold, which depending on king, may not be feasible, such that some of them are just locked out of RoR for most of the game. But hey, perhaps you can get one of the AI to be your friend at turn 200 when you can finally afford to start bribing them! It's not likely they've done anything but kill you at this point, but perhaps if you give them enough money to kill you, you'll be making it all back in just a thousand turns!

In-battle, they're hit or miss. Your spells are largely RNG based and by losing, you get a pointless heal and a unit of extremely short term ushabti that aren't enough to turn the tide and won't help you do more than harass the ranged units that are by the time you get the ushabti almost out of ammo anyways. With smaller forces you don't qualify for heals or ushabti, though, so they're frequently a moot point. Your units are slow and tire basically as soon as they appear on the map, your archers have a bunch of ammo that only hurts T0 units, and you have a very limited number of them, and your starting construct will eventually need serious save scumming to keep. These constructs require T5 buildings, so you won't be replacing them if you lose one, you'll just lose your territory, and TK with no land is just a collection of the worst units in the game. Even if you regain momentum, you'll be so laughably far behind any other faction that you might as well quit.

Their big objective is to find books. This is handled poorly, because allies don't give you their books. Allies with books need to be de-allied, then you need to kill them to get their book. Remember, trading is vital, so you won't want to get books before turn one thousand or else you'll be untrustworthy! By the time they're realistic, books are worthless trinkets that provide things like "+10% research rate" after all your research is one turn and "line of sight over areas with gold" after you've gotten gold, "wound recovery -1". Wow, wound recovery -1, and all I had to do was betray all my alliances? What a steal!

Oh, and chariots are their specialty. Do you find it tiring to fight twenty battles of clicking slightly in front of an enemy and then clicking the enemy ten thousand times per fight per unit, only to repeat the process every turn forever? Try Settra out you'll sleep like a baby every night.

Most of this could change in one patch. You could drop the cost of T1/2 buildings, make trade actually viable for your lack of natural trade partners, make the early game units work midgame with a reformed research tree, and/or make the mortuary cult worth visiting, perhaps treating it more like the workshop and allowing the player to invest in units to improve their performance. Make allies give you books. Confederation. The later game doesn't need to change at all, outside of perhaps flattening their power curve a mite, but the "early" game is such a horrible slog it makes me regret buying this, and the patches since have largely left them in the dust from whence they came.

TL;DR: This faction is just a worse version of pre-patch & pre-fix Couronne but slower and without flying units or easy access to artillery, and you can buy Hades instead of this for the same price, then Bretonnia instead for a better experience here.\t\t\t\t\t
