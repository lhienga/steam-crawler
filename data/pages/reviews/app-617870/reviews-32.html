\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Warhammer Franchise has always been a money grab. Doesn't matter which part of it you got invested into.

But.

Creative certainly developed their DLC policy over the last few years. Happily earning some extra cash, they also keep developing the core-game incrementally. This DLC is pricey, for sure, but it delivers a lot of content, too.

The Tomb Kings come with no less than 4 legendary lords all different to each other regarding play style, starting positions and the conditions and beginning challenges thereof.
The faction itself has an agenda deliciously detached from all the other factions of the eye of the vortex campaign and doesn't bother at all with who wins that (partly) annoying race for said vortex.

The army roster is challenging, but versatile and unique and the tomb king's campaign mechanics are capturing the look and feel of the faction just perfectly. A rather difficult early game challenges you to make do with the simplest, cheapest and weakest of units, while researching your way back to technologies and knowledge of your former, ancient glory. Nice treats as not having to pay neither upkeep nor recruitment costs allows for quickly fielding a second army of skeleton trash, simply drowning your enemies in spectacular, huge scale battles. Fighting other tomb kings with just as large armies is especially fun (and quite challenging).

Whereas the vanilla warhammer 2 faction roster remained pretty traditional compared to any other total war title (juggling between economy, upkeep and costly recruitment, but without any constraint), the tomb kings come in fresh (no upkeep, no cost, but limited recruitment numbers of specialized troops depending on number of supporting buildings built faction-wide) and with a lot of hidden humor and pretty deep strategical possibilities.

In my opinion, the most exciting faction implemented in the most exciting way, so far. If you found the vortex campaign a little stale, then tomb kings are your relief. The only downside I've found so far, is the lack of eye-candy. While their art-style is certainly great, the visual representation on the battle field feels somewhat lacking. Too boney and too uniform in appearance. Fair enough, they're all dead and we all look the same once stripped of skin and flesh, but hey, still, could've been somewhat more mummified, right? :D

Cheers!\t\t\t\t\t
