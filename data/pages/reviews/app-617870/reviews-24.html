\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCoolest faction to be added yet.

On Legendary, the no-upkeep but limited general count was a big game change where it's usually prohibitively expensive to run multiple armies early on. Economy is a tough slog to figure out if playing as Arkhan, but it scales really well with territory, and Settra is a pretty easy start.

Tomb Kings is all about provincial control. Fielding more elite units means owning more territories with the same types of military buildings. The more provinces you control, the more you can field, but generating money can be difficult as the tomb kings don't outright have cash producing buildings. A lot of what you'll have to feed your empire with is raiding and looting, which strikes a good balance between watching your territory and being careful not to over-extend.

This made the mid-late game slog more palatable in ME, thrown in with the Books of Nagash collectible rewards and the mortuary cult made some serious incentive to travel out and not turtle. The winning objectives of rebuilding Nehekhara and rebuilding the pyramids was also a really cool touch.

Tech was interesting too. It's broken down into dynasties with their own flavor, researching the right ones for your army composition at the time is important, and each one unlocked a Lord from the lore that makes it even cooler. All the faction leader choices are incredibly powerful, and features one traitor (Arkhan the Black) if you'd like to focus on fighting other tomb kings with some vampire count units mixed in.

The Quest Battles are pretty cool, and the fact you can play TK in Vortex and not have to race for the Ritual is really nice too. Filling these in with mortuary cult items means you can have some very serious gear variety and really beef up your lords. All the starting positions mean you'll have pretty dramatically different experiences in MK and Vortex, which is great.\t\t\t\t\t
