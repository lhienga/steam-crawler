\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Bullet Point DLC Review:

-A new Race Pack, similar to Wood Elves or Beastmen from WH1, but this time no campaign. You make up the cost this time by getting much more in terms of faction mechanics, and 4 Legendary Lords instead of two, all of whom play very differently.
-The Tomb Kings are an Undead army, like the Vampire Counts or Vampire Coast, largely focused in the Southlands. They consist of free-cost cheap chaff units, and expensive constructs created with a Canopic Urn resource.
-Settra, King of Kings, focuses on Tomb Guard and Infantry. Khatep the Heirophant focuses on magic and priests. Khalida the High Queen focuses on poison and ranged units. Finally, Arkhan the Black is a Tomb King fighting the other Tomb Kings, and gets special access to Vampire Count units, and is friendly with other Vampire factions.
-Faction Campaign mechanics revolve around awakening new Dynasties of Tomb Kings for bonuses, and obtaining Canopic Jars for new technologies, and for high end special Construct units, like Ushabti, Necrosphinxes, and Heirotitans. In battle, the Tomb Kings can summon Ushabti after enough casualties have happened. You can also use Jars and Resources on the map to craft unique items and objects for your Lords and Heroes.
-Khatep uniquely starts far away in the northwest of Naggarond, instead of the Southlands like the other three, so provides a very different gameplay experience. In the Vortex Map, Khalida also starts in the southwest jungles of Lustria.
-Tomb Kings do not participate in the Vortex Ritual Race, instead seeking out the Books of Nagash for their own, non-Vortex related needs. This means these campaigns are a nice change from worrying about ritual resources constantly, and intervention armies.

While one of the pricier DLCs, if you're interested in playing the race, it's absolutely worth it. Far more fleshed out than the WH1 race packs, with more content and more campaign mechanics, it's worth your time, if you want to play them. If not, then pass - as always, the content is still added to the game for you to engage with - it's just not playable unless purchased.\t\t\t\t\t
