\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat DLC. I already spent a few hours with the Tomb Kings (High Queen Khalida) on Legendary and it has been great so far. Also i havent experienced any crashes or major bugs yet. The campaign feels balanced so far (turn 75) but i cant say anything about lategame right now.

Positives:

+ new mechanics, which are unique to the tomb kings: no upkeep at all! standard spearmen and swordsmen have no recruitmentlimit but for better units you need special buildings to increase your unit-cap.

+ crafting system for items and even units which can be very powerful (basically better Regiments of Renown)

+ strategic ressources on the map are more important now and you will have to make decisions which settlements you want to conquer to get the ressources for crafting items and units.

+ the full roster (except the Bone Giant aka Necrolith Colossus) from the official armybook is included. There are even "Nehekaran" units which arent in the armybook but they fill the gap between the meatshield trash-infantry and the elite tombguard perfectly imo. i am kinda fine with CA cutting the Bone Giant because you have a few other constructs which fulfill a similar role.

+ interesting techtree. you can unlock separate dynasties which give you buffs for your different unit-types. you can even get special warlords for each dynasty.

+ lorefriendly rites and armystances: i mean whats cooler than summoning a sandstorm when enemies invade your lands (attrition for enemies, increased ambushchance and an ability to make one unit of your choosing invisible in battles)

+ unit diversity: i cant decide which unit is my favourite one tbh. ushabti, necropolis knights, sepulchral stalkers, hierotitan, warsphinx, chariots, tomb guard, casket of souls. you name it. every single one of them is fun to use in their way and have great animations.

+ different winning condition for the tomb kings in the vortex campaign

+ good voiceacting really brings this faction "alive" if you know what i mean :)

Negatives:

couldnt think of alot of negative stuff yet...but i guess towards the end of the campaign there always were problems in the TW series but i hope they fixed some stuff with the patch which was released at the same time as the DLC. i will update this review in case i experience any major flaws later on. but for now:

- tomb kings dont really have strong units at the beginning. the basic swords- and spearmen are really trash and it can be hard in the beginning of the campaign against a few factions. especially against lizardmen and dwarves (TKs dont have alot of armourpiercing earlygame) you cannot afford to lose or misuse your archers or your better units (f.e. Necropolis Knights and Sepulchral Stalkers with Khalida) because you have to efficiently deal damage with them while your trashy units just hold the line because they wont kill any units of the those LM or dwarves alone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros
+Unique Building System
+New Postbattle options
+Dynasty System is exciting take on techs
+Army Upkeep System
+many legendary lords, all feel unique
+(practically) complete roster
+cool final battle
+books of nagash force you to travel the world and fight every army
+very well done animations

Cons
-special units are highly limited in number
-special units dominate to the point that normal skeletons feel like wet paper
-

This DLC is one of the best CA has ever made. It adds a brand new faction, the Tomb Kings, who ahve a wide variety of new and exciting campaign mechanics. There is a crafting system, new regiments of renown, and a unique tech system. However, the most exciting thing about Tomb Kings is their unique recruitment method, where you get all of your units for free. To balance this, all units but your basic footmen have a cap, which is only increased through building military recruitment buildings. The army roster is complete, only missing the necrolith collosuss (bone giant), but the addition of the hierophant (magic bone giant) more than makes up for it. All the LL are cool (I've tried 2, Arkhan and Settra) and their new skills and item system is great.

Overall, 10/10 CA. Good job, and I hope Ogre Kingdoms get this amount of attention!\t\t\t\t\t
