\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was the first campaign pack for Total War Warhammer 2. It offers 4 unique Legendary Lords, a campaign distinct from the Vortex race, new race mechanics, units, lords, heroes, lores of magic etc. Even today the Tomb Kings are a fun race to play and are worth the purchase.

They have a rough early game because they have unit caps on their more elite units but this is compensated by no supply lines penalties and no upkeep on units. They also need to research technologies to unlock new armies (in the late game they can simply buy another army slot for money and canoptic jars).You can also strengthen your lords and heroes with unique items and units from the Mortuary Cult to make them more effective in battle. All of this combined makes the Tomb Kings ideal for painting the map because at a certain point they will reach a state in which they will drown their enemies in bodies.

The LL use the same Tomb King mechanics and have little unique faction effects (Arkhan gets access to some vampire units, Settra gets bonuses to Tomb Guard, public order and is a good melee fighter etc.) but their starting locations offer a different level of difficulty.

Unfortunately the Tomb Kings only offer 1 generic lord option: the Tomb King. A well rounded fighter that after a couple of levels gets access to the Khemrian Warsphinx mount. Hes also immortal from the start. Except for that there is nothing special about him. Just a standard lord doing his job of leading armies. Fortunately you can buff him with the items from the Mortuary Cult.

The heroes are the Tomb Prince, Liche Priest and Necrotect. Tomb Prince is a melee focused hero that can easily kill enemy infantry on his chariot. The Liche Priest is always useful because he gets access to magic. The new Lore of Nehekara is a niche lore of magic because it focuses on buffing your units. Overall it's not a bad lore and it definitely has its uses. Lastly the Necrotect is a hero that focuses on constructs. He increases your unit cap for the elite constructs and heals them in battle. A must have in construct heavy armies.

In battle the Tomb Kings use a large variety of troops. At first you will only use basic skeleton warriors and archers. After a couple of turns you can reinforce them with some cavalry, chariots, then slowly phase the skeletons out for Nehekaran Warriors and Tomb Guard. From tier 3 onwards your settlements will give you access to the best Tomb King units: constructs. There is a large variety in here. From monstrous infantry to cavalry and single entity monsters. Some of them are more specialized than others (Sepulchlar Stalkers and Necrosphinxes murder larger entities) but in the end due to unit caps you will be forced to use a varied army. Your armies will also be supported by the Realm of Souls mechanic. Basically you will get 3 free heals and 1 summon of monstrous infantry when your army receives enough damage.

Overall this DLC is still good despite the passing time and offers good replay value for the money invested. It's definitely worth the full price\t\t\t\t\t
