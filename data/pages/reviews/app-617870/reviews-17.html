\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAujourd'hui, 24 janvier 2019, je fais la critique d'un DLC sorti le 23 janvier 2018, c'est-à-dire il y'a un an. Pourtant, à mon avis, Rise of the Tomb Kings est le meilleur DLC sorti, du moins pour la série Warhammer Total War.

Premièrement, contrairement à Curse of rhe Vampire coast , que j'ai aimé malgré tout, les unités sont sans équivalences, car aucun copier-coller, comme c'est le cas dans le dernier DLC. De plus, je trouve que les mécaniques sont extrêmement plaisantes. Ainsi, le recrutement des unités ne coûte rien, étant donné que nos forces sont composées de squelettes, morts-vivants et d'immenses statues. Inversement, le recrutement est limité en fonction des bâtiments disponibles. J'aime beaucoup, car cela nous oblige à effectuer des choix, puisque nos généraux ne pourront pas tous obtenir les meilleures unités dans leur armée. De plus, il y a une cohérence au niveau du recrutement, car je ne pense pas que les morts-vivants demandent une solde de combattant.
Contrairement, à Curse of rhe Vampire coast et au jeu de base, j'ai joué avec l'ensemble des 4 seigneurs, et ce, avec grand plaisir. La voix, l'emplacement et la personnalité de chaque héros est très chouette.

Au final, je conseille vivement ce DLC.

Cordialement,

Chu Chu Nézumi\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*The lastest patch (Aye Aye patch) introduced a bug were, after collecting one of Nagash's books, your army will always take attrition after conquering/razing a city (It is avoidable though by not going after that campaign goal). I always had a Tomb Kings campaign going, but now have to wait months until CA might fix the bug to complete it.

This WAS* among the best DLC in either both games, adding a very unique faction to the game (yes, there are 4 Legendary Lords but they all have essentially the same buildings/units/playstyle). The Tomb Kings don't have to pay unit or army upkeep but instead hire units from a capped pool that is increased buying building more barracks etc.

It also offers a way a build certain magical items in-game through collecting an extra in-game resource, which is a nice touch. I really wish they had expanded on this to allow them to be able to give youe temporary faction buffs to overcome the slow pace of the campaign.

Beyond that, they still need some love. The early campaign can vary from boring to near impossible (somtimes both). It takes about 70 turns to really get going. There's a lot of mundanity before you actually start having any fun with them.

The new lore of magic is interesting, but you'll rarely use anything beyond because the damage dealing spells are not worth it. Their healing abilities need to buff model resurrection substantially - especially since the standard lord doesn't get it until Level 10. And the healing received from casting is a mere pittance campared to the Vampire Counts/Pirates, making their unique Lore that much more worthless.

The mid tier units in campaign are barely used - cavalry, chariots and dual sword infantry especially. They are good units both in terms of use and aesthetically. The problem is they are restricted too much in the early game were they would be useful and by the late game were they are abundant enough they are obselete.

Overall its a great DLC - I've played my favourite Total War campaign ever playing as Khatep but I'm can't recommend buying it until they fix the bug I mentioned above.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has been further enhanced, the new race "Tomb Kings" are absolutely fantastic and beyond stunning! With Tomb Kings this Total War Warhammer title does receive probably the very best DLC of Total War History ever.
The flavor of unique and amazing models, animations, texture and detail to the characters and unites alike is simply awesome.
I highly recommend adding this to your collection any day of the week.

The only major lacking element is that of both Technology and Skill choices for either of the Factions within the Tomb King DLC, it does feel a wee bit dry that they only can get Tomb Kings as Lords and suffer from the Skills those very same lords have in regards of not being very interesting.

By a long shot Arkhan the Black should have gotten a more interesting focus around being both Vampire Counts and Tomb King related, spreading either vampire corruption or at the very least be more of a hybrid faction to pick and choose from.
After all this is Nagash's right hand man we're talking about.

High Queen Khalida should be way more focused around destroying her cousin Neferata and her Lahmian bloodline for good, so in general she should be the more anti-vampire and that of the venom/poison she should add into the Tomb Kings faction.

Settra the Imperishable should be been given the most attention of all, since he is the King of Kings, the politics and diplomacy of his once golden empire should still play a stronger role within his campaign as well as the ressurection of his Empire and the defeat of Nagash.

Grand Hierophant Khatep is in one weee bit of a weird spot, he should not be stucked at such a boring yet punishing start position to begin with. His main quest should beyond an shadow of a doubt be more important in his campaign, since he seeks forgiveness in the eyes of Settra and the ressurection of the Mortuary Cult as a whole within the Tomb King faction.

7.5/10 is my score of this DLC, the setting and the overall faction conflict is simply awesome and the drive of the faction itself spells out why your unites are free and your settlements are so costly.
The strength and weaknesses are shown through and through with this DLC, the unites and 4 Legendary Lords are amazing.
However mind you, I hope it gets improved in the sense of the Tomb Kings hatred towards the Vampire Counts / Vampire Coast - Aka - Undead minions of Nagash.\t\t\t\t\t
