\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the WH 40k fans on the fence, think Thousand Sons meets Necrons = Tomb Kings. Nuff Said.

In all seriousness though, this is an outstanding DLC for an already great franchise.

I've never played a tabletop game of Warhammer Fantasy im a 40k fan at heart (go Necrons!). So I don’t know how close to "codex?" the Tomb Kings are, but the gameplay is truly worthy of the relentless undead fantasy.

The first few battles I played, I was shocked at my line troop losses! But wait... Did someone say Ubshanti in your back lines? Oh and free unit recruitment! Oh My! Skele's for everyone!

Just this feature alone makes the DLC worthwhile. Balance that with a limit on 'Lords' and poor early game income + Black Books of Nagash makes this a standout DLC for the franchise.

Hats off to CA

Four new starting Lords, each with noticeably unique differences in battle stats, diplomacy, starting bonuses, locations, and starting units. The faction plays like an undead faction: really crappy low tier units, and highly specialized and very good high tier units. Pound for pound, the Tomb King roster is not the strongest, but their unique traits like Real of Souls healing, calling an Ushabti unit after enough skeletons have died, and strong magic buffs/debuffs from lords, heroes, and even units like the Hierotitant all make the Tomb Kings extremely fun. I can't stress enough how pleased I've been with the Tomb Kings. 10/10, well done CA\t\t\t\t\t
