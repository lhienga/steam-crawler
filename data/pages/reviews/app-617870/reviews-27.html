Pros:
No Army Upkeep cost (inlcuding Hero's)
Very Good Beast Units
Unique Crafting System using trade resources and a mummy currency to build Lord and Hero items
Can Research Unique Items using said mummy currency that lords can use for decent army boosts
Mid and late game you can build multiple massive armys since there is no army upkeep
Only faction that gets Beast Archer and Calvary Units (I think?)
Decent Archer Infantry
The Legendary Lords are Really damn good and cool

Cons:
Infantry Units are probably some of the worst POS in the game (Meat Shield Strat's)
The one artillery unit they get is pretty sad
A couple of the beast Units are fairly sub-par considering their infantry is terrible
Early game is fairly difficult since access to better units is capped by how many military building you have built
Will likely lose 1v1 battles between Lords against most factions (traveling in pairs is recommended)
Hero Characters are also fairly sub-par compared to other factions

Okay now opinion:
My thoughts on them is once you have access to endless amounts of their best infantry units this faction becomes a well rounded faction. They really are not good at any one thing they are just average at everything making horde style tactics very effective.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe quality of dlc for both Warhammer 1 and Warhammer 2 is reminiscent of a game franchise that gets better with every game. You would usually see in franchises like those, better gameplay, animations, story, and content. A good example of this is the Witcher seires. Creative Assembly has taken that same approach to their releases of dlcs. In the beginning of this trilogy the dlcs were a bit lackluster (Beastmen especially). But with each new race that came out they seemed to take in account what they could have done with the previous dlc and improved on the next one, which comes to the Tomb Kings. These guys are a breath of fresh air in both gameplay mechanics and visuals. I'm also glad that the development team listened to community feedback and gave us more ways to play the faction instead of giving us a lifeless mini-campaign that inflated the price when most people would play it once and never touch it again, if they played it at all that is.

The life of this faction is in its charismatic legendary lords, and it's wild constructs that it can field. CA did such an amazing job this time around with animations and models. Kudos to them as well for making constructs stand completely still when idle, a really nice touch. When I look at all that was offered with this dlc, it just makes me a little sad that the earlier races in the trilogy didn't recieve similar attention. A ghorgon or jabberslythe seems like a real possibility with the team that worked on this dlc, alas we will probably never get them. With all that said, I highly reccomend picking this dlc up because it embodies great game design and you can feel the passion that went into them. Thumbs up from me!\t\t\t\t\t

Tomb Kings brings four new lords as well as a new area of the map to the southeast to wage war. The new lords are great and the new units are fun to play with, but where the tomb kings really impresses me is the unique faction mechanics.
