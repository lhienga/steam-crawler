\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably my favourite dlc thus far. Most interesting units, favourite legendary lords, etc.
Comes with four different legendary lords. All of them interesting in their own right. Although their start points vary in vortex and mortal empires, they are mostly located in the africa-esque continent, however one of the lords starts in North America with the dark elves. (I know the continents have different names, I just cant be asked to remember them)
The tomb kings have the interesting gameplay change of not having to spend money on upkeep or on purchasing units. Unlike every other faction in the game, summoning extra soldiers doesn't cost anything. Ever. The units are always free, however you are limited in how many armies you can have and how many good units you can have in them.
This shifts part of the difficulty of the game from managing how many people you can have in an army without bankrupting yourself, and puts that focus instead on deciding when and where your armies will be located.
At the start of the game, you can only have one army, and to get more, you need to research them, so until you get your second twenty-stack out in the field, you're stuck with one army fielding about. The advantage being that you get a 20 stack faster than any other faction in the game.

This whole gameplay changeup really feels livelier than the rest of the dlcs I've played, atleast. (I still don't have chaos or wood elves) and the play with the sandy skeletons feels not overpowered but not underpowered either. The troop choices are not bad, they're relatively similar to most of the other factions in the game, and you have some really interesting choices further down the line. I like that some of the non-lord troops can cast spells, (Mostly just the titan), and the spell casting overall feels nice for the group.

Yeah, tl;dr, highly reccomend. 8-9/10. I approve of everything about this dlc.\t\t\t\t\t
