\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEver wanted to play the villains from the mummy movies. Dreamed of an army of mummies, and wanted statues coming to life? Liked the Indiana Jones movies? Then you'll love this faction. The Tomb Kings are a fantasy undead ancient Egyptian faction that is the Warhammer world's only true neutral race. All they want is to hate what Nagash the great Necromancer did to them and keep holding on to their gold and desert lands. The Tomb Kings have a very unique campaign set up and an army of skeletons and statues. It is one of the two favourite factions of the reviewer (Lizardmen are the other).

TLDR;
The Tomb Kings DLC sets the gold standard for Warhammer race dlc's. A fully implemented faction with almost nothing missing having excellent and interesting campaign mechanics. The Tomb Kings are an ancient undead Egyptian army with one of the most unique campaigns in the game. Their units cost no money so you can have as many armies as you want. To get better units you build buildings with the gold that you do earn to increase unit caps. Their army is split into weak infantry meant to hold the line for a time while your living statues, the constructs deal the damage. With a strong focus on big single entity units anti large is a big threat to you. Cavalry and air capability is very poor however. A decent to excellent ranged capability combined with interesting artillery. The Tomb King exclusive magic lore is lackluster. The realm of souls ability is useless for the most part except the final stage which is very usefull indeed. Finally the Tomb Kings are also able to craft their own equipment for their lords and heroes via the mortuary cult.

The Army and its Aesthetics
The people of Nehekhara, the Tomb Kings were once the high point of human civilization in the Warhammer world. However they were betrayed by Nagash the Great Necromancer. He wanted to kill them and raise the living and the dead up as his undead slaves. Whilst Nagash was stopped the ritual succeeded and the people of Nehekhara rose from the dead. Instead of being in a golden paradise however they awoke as skeletons. Their anger at this fact drove them to isolate themselves from the world caring only to maintain their holdings, gold and tombs. They are the Warhammer world's only true neutral faction.

Their army consists of two parts. The skeletons and the constructs. The skeletons are basically standard spearmen, archers and cavalrymen like most factions have. They do have the standard undead bonusses making them a bit better. Their role is to hold the enemy units while the constructs do the damage. As all constructs are unliving statues of various sorts they are not exactly nimble so they can be damaged somewhat easily by anti large units.

The main variety of the army comes from the constructs themselves. They range from extreme long range single unit killers such as Ushabti Great Bows and Bone Giants to the infantry crushers of the Tomb Scorpions. Due to the animations of the Tomb Scorpion its very difficult for enemy infantry to actually damage them so they can tear through enemy units. The real line breakers however are the sphinxes. The Necrosphinx is a single large entity killer while the warsphinx bounces through enemy front lines like a happy kitten. Its probably the cutest and one of the most effective units in the game. In short the skeletons are there to be the meat shields while the very specialized construct kill the enemy off. The Tomb Kings Army requires more micro then most armies but not ridiculously so. Their artillery also provides the needed long range cover and is decent.

The Good
The most unique part about the Tomb Kings is the fact that none of their units cost any money. Their armies are free. On the flip side you are extremely limited in which units you can recruit. If you want more than basic skeleton units you are going to have to build buildings to upgrade your capacity for those units. Since your income is much lower than most factions, these buildings cost an arm and a leg. This creates a very unique playstyle on the campaign map very different from most factions

As the Tomb Kings you don't just gather gold as a resource but also canopic jars which allow you to use the mortuary cult system. This systems will allow you to craft items for your lords and heroes aswell as unlock special units and gives you greater control over your faction.

The realm of souls mechanic can be very usefull but also very bad. The Ushabti you can summon at the end can be a real game changer in some battles where you can charge your enemies rear in a very sensitive spot. The heals you get at the 25% percentages is quite useless though.

The Bad
I don't really have bad points for this dlc. Only two somewhat minor negative points. The unique lore of magic for the Tomb Kings isn't very good. Its servicable but there are better. Also the tomb kings early campaign is quite hard. You are very limited in the amount of armies and higher tier units you can recruit due to the unit and army cap system. Meaning early game you have only very poor units to work with, with no easy way to improve your situation.

There is one bad point though, this army has no MUMMY'S at all! :)

Price
The Tomb Kings set the gold standard for Warhammer DLC and is the best stand alone DLC for game II. You get a full well rounded faction that is not missing anything beside one hero type and some unique characters. That said the price of 20 Euro's is quite steep especially given that the base game is 4 races for 60 Euro's. 15-17,5 Euro's would be more appropriate in my book. But 20 Euro is not unreasonable either. Don't expect discounts above 50% though (they do come often though)

Overall a strong recommend. The best Warhammer II DLC with a gorgeous army, unique playstyle and the most unique campaign gameplay in the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe sequel to the best strategy game ever made. Which is also the best strategy game ever made. Also the best use of the Warhammer licence in gaming.

Each race plays differently and each has a massive amount of character and charisma. The graphics and sound are lovingly crafted, nothing in this game has been skimped. Each of the factions have fully fleshed out graphics, sound and story. It all gels into an immersive atmosphere which can suck you in for hours.

The Battles are exciting to play and watch. With Dragons and giants charging into phalanxes, vampirates fighting lizardmen the game is both a tactical and spectacle masterpiece. This is a polished game. It was clearly crafted by a team that loves Warhammer and the result shows. A truly great game, worthy of soaking up endless hours.

The Total War: Warhammer games are similar to the Lord of the Rings franchise in structure. While each can be played separately, they truly transcend the individual product when played together. All the factions you own in the first game can be played in the second. Both in multiplayer battles and in a grand campaign mode called Mortal Empires. This game is brilliant. This series is a masterpiece.

I whole wholeheartedly recommend 100/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great faction to add to the game that will most likely get an expansion in Warhammer 3 with the ultimately evil Nagash. Tomb Kings are unique in the sense that their units DON'T require upkeep! Now the downside to that is you have unit caps on your best units and an army limit, as well as buildings still cost money but you don't make an awful lot. For those of you that think "Man that sounds hard" I have some tips. First, you can recruit faster than anyone else and have two full armies at the first few turns, get a second lord and in both your armies use local and global recruitment to super recruit two full armies early while you rush down all the heathens in your lands!. My second tip is explore! Take a well rounded army to sail the seas, if you can win skull island battles you can earn around 6,000 to 20,000 gold for your buildings meaning you can get a lot done and have access to elite units faster.

Tomb King units are a mixed bag in the current game state, their infantry are usually "ok" in Singleplayer where the AI won't abuse you (much), however in multiplayer they are very vulnerable to fire spells and can die too quickly. Their construct units are amazing however and not to be underestimated, khermri warsphinxes almost look like giant happy puppies just trying to get some love from the enemy before smashing them to dust underfoot, ushabti have great value and so do nehekharan horsemen and necrosphinxes.

While definitely due some attention in terms of buffs and possibly a few extra units they are a unique faction with some very fun units, along with stacking regeneration buffs in combat based on amount of units lost. The shortfalls I mentioned earlier I personally don't feel in campaign but definitely something to consider in multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t
