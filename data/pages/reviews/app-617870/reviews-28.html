\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun DLC with a decent unit roster and a unique story. The rest of the world is being torn apart by the collapse of the vortex, factions warring with each other to be the first to restore it, but you are the undead and the worries of the mortals don't concern you.

One of the best parts of this DLC for me is that it doesn't end when you complete the story quest chain. There are two different victories, and completing the story quest line gives you a major bonus to help you stomp the mortals into submission for the conquest victory.

I've been playing the Total War series since the original Shogun Total War, and this DLC gave me the biggest starting challenge of any of the previous games and DLC's/Expansions. It is definately not for beginners, as you start off incredibly weak, and almost every unit type has a very low cap on it. But once you can get past the Savage Orcs in the first dozen or so turns, you build up momentum, and can become a steam roller (if properly managed).

All in all, this is a unique spin on a pretty decent game. While it is a bit overpriced, as all CA's DLC are, and I despise the piece meal, nickle and diming of faction DLC's, it is one I would recommend getting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC is why I bought into Totally Warhammer 2. Some of my favorite units in the tabletop (may you rest in peace) and interesting mechanics that make it fun for a newcomer to learn in a slightly more forgiving environment.

Pros:
-Interesting mechanics that differ from the other factions in the game, feeling like a true expansion.
-Really cool units and great animations (totally subjective, but it was a huge selling point for me).
-Great diversity in the leaders available.
-Good faction to learn the other game mechanics with (I was overwhelmed with other factions trying to learn what was goign on without getting stomped on).

Cons:
-Arguably the mechanics can be a little too forgiving. The ease you can recover from a defeat seems almost a little too good (but again, great for beginners).
-A bit pricey when you buy into the game and the DLC at the same time (if not waiting for a sale). Worth the price otherwise, IMO, due to the care that was put into it.

I love this faction and the DLC. Definitely worth it for a new way to play the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - I'm on 50/50, but i'm tipping to the positive


So the Tomb Kings DLC adds some new features to their particular faction, the three large ones worth mentioning is
1. ''Making trade resources valuable'' - Although i disagree
2. ''Army limit capped at 1, increased by research''
3. ''Armies have no upkeep, but all units above basic can only be fielded a certain number of times across all armies, increased by research''

Now i should mention for clarity - that i have completed most Campaings in Warhammer 1 and Warhammer 2 on legendary difficulty, i do not consider that a boastful statement as in almost all situations have i auto-resolved 90% of my battles (You may disagree with this approach but i still state that having to gimp yourself to make anything challenging points to a flaw in core design)

So where does Tomb Kings land on this with their 1 army and limited troops? Well... Pretty much exactly the same, within 60 turns of my first Very Hard campaign i have blazed through the desert, defeating all natural enemies, raised my army cap to 6 and.... Yeah, that's that - now begins the almost laborious task of multi-stack auto-resolving the rest of the campaign map (Except the Item Battles, of course)

There were many skeptics to the idea of No Upkeep armies and i must say - they have all been proven right, i often found myself utterly ignoring Phyrric Victories because those troops could be re-raised in 2 turns. Does this fit the fantasy of the race? Absolutely - but it makes for very poor balancing

The Trade Resources having been made ''useful'' beyond their regular trade price is also a point of contension for me - with the new system, The Tomb Kings can use it in order to craft various items for their characters, but to remind you incase you are skimming this review - 90% of battles can be autoresolved. So are Trade Resources really useful now? I mean sort of, more then they were - but calling them useful i feel is still a strech, especially since the vast majority of those resources are conveniently available to you within the starting domain of the desert


So in conclusion; Do i feel you should get this DLC? Yes. as much as my review might speak for the opposite, that is only my opinion, and given the success of the franchise, put against the fact that most legendary difficulty achivements have 1% completion rate - If you are buying this DLC, my above issues will in most likelyhood seem like min-maxing, and you will not care. In which case - this DLC adds plenty of flavor and new things to do to keep the game fresh\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst thing first, before I talk about the pros and cons, this DLC is absolutely worth it, and you should seriously consider picking it up. Tomb Kings feel distinctive from all other factions (even Vampire Counts), have well fleshed-out mechanics, and some of the most badass-looking and powerful monsters in the game. However, this faction is not newbie-friendly due to their unique pacing and unit management. Thus I highly recommend you play with some of the more standard factions like High Elves or Dark Elves before trying out Tomb Kings if you're not that familiar with Total War games. Anyway, without further ado, let's dive right in the nitty-gritty shall we?
THE PROS
+ Four unique Lords with unique starting positions and powers (SPOILER ALERT - Settra on a Warsphinx is the ultimate wrecking ball).
+ Interesting rosters of monsters and monstrous units.
+ Very complimentary synergy between units within an army - more apparent and effective than other factions.
+ A completely new campaign objective (as opposed to meddling with the Vortex) of collecting the Nine Books of Nagash reinforces the theme and identity of Tomb Kings, making them stand out from other factions.
+ Unlike Vampire Counts, Tomb Kings have decent missile units and artillery pieces.
+ Quite well-balanced roster in the ability to counter: armor-piercing, anti-large, anti-infantry, poison attacks, etc.
+ And in my opinion, Tomb Kings have the best ultimate monsters in the game: Necrosphinx, Warsphinx, and Hierotian absolutely dominate the battlefields.
THE CONS
All of the problems I have with Tomb Kings are not because of design flaws, but rather questionable implementations (which are much more easy to fix).
+ Currently, Tomb Kings are extremely weak and slow to build up momentum in the early to mid-game. This can be very frustrating if you find yourself surrounded by better established factions and settlements.
+ Your basic skeleton units are utter garbage, and your battles would end with multiple unit wipes since they'll be making up the bulk of your forces for a very long time.
+ Tomb Kings' economy is a pathetic patchwork that barely holds itself together (even without the upkeep cost). This is way too punishing since Tomb Kings arguably depend much more on their treasury for progress than the other factions.
+ The need for all-out expansion is especially stressed while playing Tomb Kings; you need more settlements to support your economy, enlarge recruitment capacity, and havest resources for the Mortuary Cult a.k.a. crafting. You could easily get knee-deep in diplomatic and military conundrums for it.
+ Their asymmetrical capability vs. other factions is quite doubtful. I didn't have the chance to fight all factions in the campaign, but the ones I managed to battle seemed a bit more capable than my armies even with a powerful composition of units.
FINAL THOUGHTS
I had a lot of fun playing Tomb Kings, they are definitely one of my favorite factions regardless of the problems I've just mentioned above. I think they're a bit underpowered right now, and like all the added factions, they'll get better by a few patches and tweaks to fully manifest their awesome strength. And the prospect I'm looking forward to, is Creative Assembly dusting off a few blemishes here and there, and let the acient glory and majesty of the Tomb Kings be whole!\t\t\t\t\t
