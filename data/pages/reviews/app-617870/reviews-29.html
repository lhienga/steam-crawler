\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Tomb Kings are very good (and long waited) addition to Warhammer II, unlike other expansions, they don´t come with a mini campaign, but instead (somewhat) ready to either join the Vortex or the Mortal Empires one.

The deal is, that in the Vortex campaign, unlike the other factions, the Tomb Kings, don´t interact with the Vortex in anyway, rather, they are in their own objectives, which can be a bit strange but also good or not... Because, if you might felt too under preassure of the Vortex mechanics, the Tomb Kings can play in their own pace without a single care in the world, which is a blessing, but it also feel a bit strange that you don´t interact in anyway. That said, their objective is rather look around for the 5 Books of Nagash, which are spread in the world, between some locations or rouge armies.

On the Mortal Empires however, since it is sandbox, the Tomb Kings fit in perfectly.

As a faction, the Tomb Kings are very fun to play - their units look really cool and have amazing animations, lots of unique units, their factions mechanics are very cool, such as Realm of Dead (where the fallen units in your army during batle might trigger special effects), the Mortuary Cult where you can craft magica items and unlock units of renown, the simple but fact you don´t need to pay upkeep for your units mean that you can really feel leading hordes of undead and other stuff (such as you research the wisdomw of old dinasties and unlocking unique lords) is a blast to play.\t\t\t\t\t
