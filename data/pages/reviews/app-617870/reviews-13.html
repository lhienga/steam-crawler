\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely worth the money. Good news: None of the factions start in lustria in ME. Settra is awesome and Tomb Kings have free armies.

Khatep starts with a Hierotitan and he can get a casket of souls mount. His campaign is a migration campaign done right.

Arkhan the Black is a pretty tough campaign at the start because you start with two settlements. All the tomb kings will hate you but you have access to vampire units, are unaffected by vampiric corruption, and can collect all books of nagash.

Khalida's campaign is almost as easy as Settra's. You start in the southlands fighting vampires. Much like Settra, she's great in combat and she gives archers +20 ammo.

Settra the Imperishable (and many other titles) has a pretty easy start. He's an absolute beast in combat and gets a warsphyx as a mount. His faction bonuses decrease construction time and buffs growth and public order. I highly suggest Settra as your first tomb kings playthrough.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally TK are by far my favorite race and I have to disagree with Rapha89 on quite a few points he made.

"Chariots are weak" I disagree but they're definitely not something I would use without playing Settra(He gives them very good charge/speed buffs). They do however take up alot of your time to make good use of. They are not a "attack and forget" unit like he was apparently using them for. I rarely play Settra(I'll explain below) but when I do I'll take him and 4 Chariots with a Lore of Light Priest in tow. It's simple really, you just smash into their back lines over and over or the flanks of any infantry in battle. The initial barrage will knock half of their units flat onto their asses and killing a good amount of them in the process.

You IMMEDIATELY move the chariots away and swing around for another smashing. Never ever leave chariots to just...melee. Not to bring reality into this but it fits the scenario quite well. Try to imagine 12 skeletons on a chariot not moving getting surrounded by 60+ orcs. A shiny vehicle with some big wheels planted into the dirt doesn't exactly even the playing field.

Settra after getting all of his talents has really high charge damage(and gives the chariots a very large bonus too) but he also can blow up onto packs of units after he's charged into the middle of them. The really important unit in this setup is the Light Priest. Overcasting timewarp(+75% speed) on Chariots makes them go super speed plus you can use the Net(immobilizes entire groups) on anyone that tries to disrupt your chariot parade. Then you proceed to run them over and there is nothing they can do about it, or you cast banishment(aoe damage) right on top of them. Shoot off some magic missiles(Shem's burning gaze) at any lord/hero in range whenever you get the chance to as well.

Now admittedly I don't do that often because I personally find it draining doing that every fight but it *is* fun for a bit. Plus there is one major drawback to this strategy, it's way better on Settra and honestly I really don't care for him. I'm not into Warhammer lore but from the little I picked up(like Rapha said in his review) he is suppose to be super strong. Well...he's not. Literally I think the only reason to pick him over Khalida would be to do the chariot tactic above but even then she could do it at about 75% efficiency. So one part of his negative review is correct, Settra is very weak and his faction/lord buffs aren't even worth mentioning. The ONLY positive to him is his starting town which is quite good but you can easily rectify that by modding in "start anywhere" and moving there with the other lords.

"Scorpions are squishy" Well yeah, I won't disagree with that. I mean they have what...30 defense? Even a pack of skellies won't have trouble connecting blows on it. But Scorpions should not be taking blows. Scorpions have very fast attacks and insane armor piercing damage. Flanking lords/heroes is a good way to use them but personally I use them to chop down calvary(I hate human calvary). I don't let that "Anti-infantry" tag fool me, these guys are very good at eating armored horses. But again DON'T let them take the hits. Let the calvary charge into whatever and immediately set the oversized scorpion upon them. He reduces their numbers quite fast unless it's like the grail blessed brettonia ones. If you are having trouble keeping your scorpion alive or just generally want to buff them, get a necrotect hero.

I don't even know what to say to him calling the Hierotitan useless. Admittedly it's way too damn slow. To the point a overcasted timewarp on him won't change anything. Aside from that it's a walking god. If you do decide to buy this dlc and end up playing Khatep(personally my favorite lord), he starts with a Hierotitan. If you're waiting for the enemy to come to you just make sure the titan is far out in front of you so he can actually attack things lol. Don't worry about lords/heroes killing him. 95% of them won't be able to do much to him till mid-game or even later. The cherry on top is he has the light priests magic missiles spell(Shem's Burning Gaze). After a lord/hero tries running from him he can just blast them and finish them off.

It looks like I'm trying to write a guide instead of a review, not quite what I intended but those points on the negative review above are mostly misleading and I don't want people to pass up Tomb Kings because of it. But this game has a steep learning curve so if he just played a few TK games and charged 3 scorpions/chariots ahead to fight alone I could see why he'd think they were weak. Basically Tomb Kings are insanely fun and I highly suggest you get this DLC. If the price tag is too high wait for a sale, they should be having a 50% sale here soon in the summer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are wondering what Rise of the Tomb Kings DLC is like...well did you watch the trailer? You did? Did you enjoy what you saw? Also yes you say??? That is exactly how battles play out. Large monster units devastating infantry of other factions.

Bought it primarily because in my play-through of the High Elves (Order of Loremasters) on Vortex I never really encounter them in the campaign. All their factions were wiped out early, so figured it would be interesting to play a faction that had units I had not yet seen. 156 turns in on Mortal Empires with High Queen Khalida and I'm having more fun with them than I had with the High Elves with Teclis or with Thorgrim Grudgebearer and the Dwarves that I played the Old World campaign with in TWW1.

Biggest plus is they fit any Total War play style you are use you playing because they have such a variety of units. Have solid infantry, artillery, cavalry, chariots, monsters, flying units, lords and heroes, etc. Only weak link I'd say is there standard ranged infantry, most just due to lack of variety as their skeleton archers can still make quick work of poorly armored/leadership units like Clan Rats. Guess Im just spoiled coming from High Elves that had Lothern Sea Guard and Sisters of Avelorn (own Queen and Crone DLC too).

Where they truly shine is their monster units. Coming from Dwarves (who had none) and High Elves (who had flying one like dragons and phoenixes) it was just nice to see so many to choose from and they all have such different roles. Like the Bone Giant has great ranged attack but can hold his own in a melee fight. Scorpions who you can deploy in vanguard and hide to take out unsuspecting ranged/artillery units. Khemrian Warsphinx, which is my favorite because it literally hops into infantry sending them everywhere, while the archers on its back pick people off. The Hierotitan (the monster who you see in the end of the trailer) is super slow but make up for it in awesomeness with his laser death eyes, stomping on enemy units and has casting abilities. And there are six more besides the ones I just mentioned! (I count Casket of Souls as a monster unit)

Is Tomb Kings DLC worth almost $19? Debatable. Is it worth $9.49, the price I paid for it on the Lunar New Year sale? Absolutely!
