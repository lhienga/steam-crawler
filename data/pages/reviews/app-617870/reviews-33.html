\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA tomb kings story:

I am the exiled Grand Hierophant Khatep.

From my pyramid on an island east of Morathi's home I plan my rise to fame. Forgiveness beckons.

I expand quickly and without mercy, rolling through powerful dark elf units with skeletal warriors and powerful magic from my fellow liche priests. I occupy any settlement with a good climate and raze the rest.

My new lizard neighbours to the south, the Hexoatl, offer a non-aggression pact in the wake of my swelling territory. I accept, they will be the supporting pyramid stone as I sink those of the dark elves. My priests discover an ancient king beneath my pyramid and summon him: another general for my armies.

Declaring war on Morathi I draw her armies south, then my new army besieges her capital from a mountain pass. Before she can turn back I fight, smiting her forces with my mighty living statue, which throws bolts of magic and leeches Morathi while it hammers against the front line. I join my ancestor in his siege and take Morathi's throne. Within, I discover the book of Nagash I have been pursuing. It summons deadly sandstorms in provinces I attack, and brings me a step closer to my ultimate redemption.

I find a new ally in a skaven clan on my eastern border. They are unreliable, but powerful. I attack and raze the dark elf settlements that surround them. They are of little use to me, but my vermin friends occupy them readily. I fight off waves of minor elven defenders seeking to reclaim their lands, then strike the north west coast while the skaven distracts Malekith.

Ah, perfect desert territories. But they are not enough. I use one of my increasing number of armies to reclaim previously razed settlements. Their economic detriments are of no concern. I claim a second book of Nagash which makes trade more lucrative - why an ancient tome of Nagash contains secrets like "how to get the best profit margins selling dyes" is a question for another time.

I set my sights toward Naggarond, signing non-aggression with the northern tribes. I myself stay within sight of the capital, occupying the territory south of it. My armies march in from the newly acquired east coast, occupying and razing as they go. Many were lost to the snow.

I now prepare my assault, yet as I do the capital beams with a purple light. It stretches between elven settlements and reaches into the vortex over the sea. Chaos forces appear as though summoned, and raze the territories around Naggarond.

I will destroy Malekith and his fortified city. I will cleanse the corruption with my liche priests. I will then march south, for more books of Nagash. Between me and the books stands a friend... the Hexoatl do not tolerate me in their lands, and have by far the strongest armies seen in the new world.

I must have my redemption.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen people discuss problems with DLC, I'm completely on board. I've played the games like Mass Effect 3 where content that should have been in at release was intentionally held back so that it could be sold as "extra" content later.

Fortunately, Tomb Kings are not another example of this. They are a race completely unlike playing the Undead, with different strategy elements both on the battlefield tactical level and on the empire constructing. CA has developed the completely new system of crafting for them, and their unique system of no upkeep but limited recruitment means that they play differently than any other faction I've ever seen in a Total War game.

My only gripe is that the crafting system should also be adopted for most other races in the game, but ESPECIALLY the poor Dwarfs. How come anyone can craft magical items before them?

While I agree 20$ is steep, I think this is overall worth the price. But certainly snap it up if you see it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
