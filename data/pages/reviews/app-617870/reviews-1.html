\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like sitting around waiting to be destroyed, this is the faction for you. Everytime I try playing the Tomb King faction, I'm disappointed. The construct units are super cool and reasonably good. That is about all the good I can say about this faction. This faction is profoundly flawed in terms of logistics. Far worse than Bretonnia. The fact that the number of armies you can field is mostly determined by interminable research (20 turns or more to add an army) means that you expand much slower than other factions. Which means that by the time you are able to expand, the factions around you have already expanded like crazy and have leveraged that expansion to field more armies and get better units. When one of those factions decides to eliminate you, you're done. They will submerge you with armies and there won't be anything you can do about it because there is no way for you to field more armies regardless of how much money you have. This faction leaves you with very few choices:

1: sit on your few cities and defend them with your few armies so that you can use the building slots for buildings that allow you to increase the number of quality units in your armies while you wait to gain another army (only viable option as far as I can see). It will allow you to survive the early game, but your late game is still screwed.
2: build defences in your cities and lessen the number of good units you can have in your paltry few armies, which makes expansion almost impossible because your armies suck.
3: try to expand as quickly as you would do with another faction by building only unit buildings and no defenses, which means that you have more cities than you can defend and they are super easy to destroy. Considering how each unit building costs very hard earned money and directly affects the quality of all your armies, a lost city is utterly crippling for this faction. You are stuck in an endless loop of rebuilding your cities and you cannot move on.

There is basically no way to win with this faction unless you are very lucky. You simply cannot survive if you have several enemies or an enemy that is stronger than you. The few armies you have leave you with very few strategic options. I have never seen this faction take over the map in any game I have played. How can you take over the map with so few armies? Think about it. This faction is a total rip off. Don't bother with it at any price point.\t\t\t\t\t
