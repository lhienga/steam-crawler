\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot disappointed by the content itself, the faction is very unique and has plenty of mechanics, lords and unit types right out the box. It seems the Tomb Kings have a unique twist to add to every mechanic in the game. This is easily the best faction DLC they've made so far.

Undead skeletons and ancient egypt speak to anyone's imagination, and at a glance it seems they must fit very well within a fantasy wargame. Armies of skeletons from empires long forgotten coming out of a uninhabited and unknown desert far away just sounds absolutely awesome. I think they've done this idea justice, the skeleton armies need nothing to sustain themselves and therefore cost absolutely nothing. That's right, free armies. You just pull some bones out of the sand and voila, there's an army. To somewhat balance this ability the skeletons must do a lot of research into past dynasties before they can resurrect them, and the recruitment of more powerful units is limited by the size of the empire.

Another mechanic is that they don't care about the vortex, they're given another goal: conquer several major locations on the map. This means you need ruthless and quick expansion, the Tomb Kings can't win by turtling their own continent. The skeleton armies are tasked to overrun every corner of the world. They're not an "evil" faction though, and since you're not warring for the vortex you won't necessarily be hunted down by other factions.

My problem is that the price at 20 dollars is very steep. There's full games that cost as much, and this is just one more faction added to a game you can already play. I feel that despite the world of content it offers, this pack should cost half of what it does now. Going to carefully recommend this because it's actually really good, but you have to pay a premium fee for it as well.
