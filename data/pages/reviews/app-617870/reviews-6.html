\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is by far the most unique race in the game, but even so, its the worst one.
Here are just some points on why this is my most hated race to play as in TWW2:

-You pay no upkeep for your armies, which is one of the biggest challenges to control in the game, but you are limited to have only garbage tier units until mid-game (70-100 turns in), so have fun playing with basic melee infantry that die like nothing and archers with horrible accuracy.

-Since you pay no upkeep for your armies, you need to build high tier `construct` buildings to increase your capacity for a high tier unit you wish to use. But here is the catch, construct building are EXTREMELY expensive, from 7500-12000+ gold to make, And guess what? They only give 1-2 slots for said unit, so you need to make A LOT of tier 4-5 buildings like these to cover only a single army.

-Unlike Vampire Counts, who have some good leadership buffs and give immunity to psychological attacks, you have no units (except heroes) who keep your units from disintegrating. So not only you can only have low tier garbage units, but they also start losing their HP at about 50% if in melee, which you can`t really control in a large battles.

-You will have the worst cities in the game. Period. (I do not count the Wood Elfs cause they can only make outposts).
Not only every single building costs thousands upon thousands of gold, it gives extremely little in exchange. So most of the time you will only upgrade your every main settlement per province, and leave the little ones at tier 1 until you get that said province capital to tier 4 or 5.

-The only way to make money outside the obvious sacking and raiding, is the single building that gives you 50 gold per turn at tier 1, up to 150 gold per turn at max tier 3. 150 GOLD IS NOT EVEN GONNA COVER ITS COST FOR THE NEXT 20 TURNS.

-They have the worst autoresolve system out of all. I just tested this out, and a full army of tier 2 skeleton warriors with a lord vs a single Malekith (yes, he was alone, lvl 16) had a 10:90 chance to win, or better yet, no chance to win. You will be constantly playing manually even tho you can see how ♥♥♥♥♥♥ the enemy settlements/armies are.

There are a lot more bad stuff, but really I can make a whole seminar on: `Why you should never buy/play the Tomb Kings`

ABSOLUTELY DO NOT RECOMMEND BUYING THIS DLC UNLESS YOU HAVE NOTHING ELSE TO PLAY.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTomb Kings is the best major DLC made for a Total War game and will forever act as my benchmark for future large DLC. For a relatively "low" price of 19 euros, you get a whole new faction containing four unique legendary lords with separate playstyles and starting positions. The campaign mechanics for Tomb Kings are the first truly uniqe faction mechanics made for Warhammer II. All your units are completely free and lack upkeep, but any mildly decent unit is restricted by a building enforced cap. This means that you are unable to spam overpowered units in the mid game, but have to rely on endless hordes of skeleton spearmen for much of the game. This is offset by the sheer versatility and usefulness of their constructs that get more abundant the further you progress. By the end of the game, Tomb kings are extremely powerfull and can field countless cheap doomstacks.

Their soundtrack is also a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ banger. Makes you feel like you're in the opening sequences of the Mummy movies.\t\t\t\t\t
