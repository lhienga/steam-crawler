\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRise of the Tomb Kings... Where do I even start with this? Well first this is one of the best, if not the best, Total War Warhammer DLCs ever released, all of the added lords are absolutely incredible, each unique and with their own niche.

Do you want to play as arguably the greatest monarch the Warhammer world has ever seen? The same man who is a virtual demigod, with an ego bigger than Kholek Suneater, public order buffs out the rear, and a voice that fills even the gods themselves with an uncontrollable urge to prostrate before his eternal majesty? You have Settra the Imperishable, the King of Kings, the Great King, Khemrikhara, The Great King of Nehekhara, King of Kings, Opener of the Way, Wielder of the Divine Flame, The Great Unifier, Commander of the Golden Legion, Bringer of Light, Builder of Cities, Protector of the Two Worlds, and many many more

Maybe you want to play as an evil necromancer warlord, who served the first and greatest necromancer to ever live, and as a reward can now drown his enemies in waves upon waves of freshly resurrected skeletons? If that's the case then you have Arkhan the Black.

Perhaps you want to play as a disgraced queen who lost her life and crown to her vampire ♥♥♥♥♥ of a sister and now as an undead is a living embodiment of poison who rides atop a stone serpent capable of ripping apart entire armies? You've got High Queen Khalida.

Or maybe you play as a grand mage who was exiled for failing to fulfill an ancient promise to Settra the Imperishable, and now must reestablished a base of power on the other side of the planet all the while using actual souls as artillery shells and commanding the living statue of a god? You have Grand Hierophant Khatep.

Basically my point is there a lord for everybody, as for the units, oh don't even get me started, sure you have the traditional skeletons, skeleton cavalry, skeleton archers, etc. But who the f*ck cares about those when you can control living statues of gods, soul catapults, and god sphinxes that can butcher even Star Dragons like they're overgrown lizards? You will never know true joy until you hurl an army of 20 dragon slaying god sphinxes into a sea of disgusting vermin and watched as they each blend your opponents into a fine red mist of blood and bodies.

As for the campaign itself, the Tomb Kings don't play by the putrid rules of the lesser living, the Vortex? Never heard of it, let the living kill themselves over it. Instead the Tomb Kings has an entirely different campaign objective to work towards, they must hunt down a multitude of armies in order to gather the Nine Books of Nagash, allowing them to control the Black Pyramid, a magical engine with such power it can free the entirety of the Tomb Kings race from their curse of undeath, and transform them into a race of golden demigods with the powers to match.

Now for the competitive players, if you're wondering how good the Tomb King faction is compared to the others based purely on strength, then don't worry the Tomb Kings have you covered, as in the current moment, they are one of the top three best factions in the game in terms of combat capabilities in PvP settings, as for PvE, Tomb Kings are one of the best factions late game as they do not pay any upkeep on any of their troops meaning by the end of your campaign you may have more than 20 armies of god statues and super sphinxes sitting around, all waiting to pounce on the next unfortunate soul who happened to look at you funny that one time at that one tea party.

All in all Rise of the Tomb Kings is an absolutely amazing DLC that is 100% worth your money.\t\t\t\t\t
