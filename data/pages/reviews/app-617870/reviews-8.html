\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo right out of the gate, I'm just going to say, if you like the game at all, get this.

This race pack is easily one of, if not the best DLC for TWW2. Within you get a new faction, a race of very heavily Egyptian themed skeletons who inhabit the area equivalent to North Africa in the warhammer world. I'm not a massive lore nerd unless it comes to Skaven, but as far as I'm aware, the difference between them and the other two undead factions is pretty simple. The skeletons and other Ex Hammer Horror employees and Potc castoffs are puppets, simple sacks of bone and meat marching along at the behest of their Dracula overlords. The tomb kings are still sentient, they are once living people resurrected by a curse, albeit still ruled by tyrannical kings. They don't have access to the lore of vampires, so will stay down once killed, (I think they get resurrected post battle) and don't heal constantly, but have a much more flexible roster, with actual archers and artillery, as well as a lot of horrifyingly good animated statues to compliment.

I don't jest when I say they are really really good. Infantry are weak but unbreakable and usually quite numerous, and you have a monster roster matched only by the lizards, all of which are also unbreakable and tough as hell. You have a strange mechanic that will send out a blast of healing three times during the battle, and after this will allow you to summon a unit of ushabti (Think anubis warriors from the mummy returns) at a very inconvenient spot for the enemy, which is very handy for holding the line and gives you a free flanking unit. The roster is also gorgeous to look at. The skeletons look a lot better than the gw minis, (In my opinion, calm down) as they are far more spindly and less exaggerated, and the constructs look incredible. My personal favourite was the Necrosphinx, a massive golden, winged centaur that will butcher most cavalry and large targets (It will lose to a dread Saurian) and can even be used to terror route infantry in a pinch. You also get 4 legendary lords, Setra who is a caster that rides a construct or an op chariot, the latter of which doesn't seem to be able to turn, but that's ok as SETRA DOES NOT SWERVE, grand hierophant khaptep who is caster who can mount an artillery piece, Khalida, who is a cav focused lord with lots of poison attacks, and Arkhan the Black, who is like a tankier setra who casts lore of death and has some vampire counts units. All one can really ask from an army, great to play and great to look at. Unlike the beastmen, we actually got almost the entire roster as well!

On the campaign side, you also have a pretty unique setup. The tomb kings don't pay for their units, either in purchase or upkeep. You can have literally unlimited amounts of the chaff units, and others you gain increased capacity for as you construct certain buildings. As you don't really need money income for anything other than building and diplomacy, your economy barely exists (Although you can still make plenty from sacking enemy settlements). You gain increased capacity for armies and heroes through your research. It's a very unique system for the game, and one I like very much, you are encouraged to be quite expansionist, but at the same time you aren't hamstrung by the need for it like the wood elves once were, and can happily sit back and slowly let your settlements grow, maybe raze and sack if you don't want to immediately expand. The fact you have to put quite a bit of effort into getting your bigger units makes them feel all the more special when you do get them, and you'll love your giant bow wielding giant all the more when you do get to use him in battle. To top it all off you also have a workshop where you can make items for your characters.

So, as much as I love this, I do have a few criticisms. The tomb kings only have one lord type, and desperately need a caster lord. For some reason you can't confederate the other tomb king lords. You have a sadly limited number of heroes, as you unlock them through the research tree. It doesn't bite badly until late game, but I find I always have less than I need, and endgame is characterised by a terrible shortage with my only recourse is a dilemma that pops up every 100 turns or so and only gives one. There's a near useless rite to summon a special necrotect who will build up a ruined settlement to tier three that is honestly nearly useless, as he spawns at your capital and the AI is so obsessed with settling every ruined settlement that he will very rarely get a chance use his ability. This rite could probably be swapped to bring about the hero dilemma instead, it would be far more useful. The last criticism is related to the chariots. The Tomb Kings chariots are marvellous early game, but get forgotten very quickly (Except as a mount, they are great there), as they are just too fragile to stay useful for mid and late game. For such a thematically appropriate and iconic unit, it's sad, and they could probably use a chariot of the gods unit as well as a mount.

As you can read for yourself, the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ are minor, if you already like the game, do yourself a favour and try this out. You won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
