\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBEWARE Co-Op Players.

I'm on the fence with this game as I haven't spent near enough time playing it but certain Co-Op downsides have spurred me to let people know.

Unlike the first Total Warhammer, it seems the Eye of Vortex campaign "locks" the choices both players have when playing. For example, if one were to play as Morathi with the Dark Elves the other player has no choice but to play as Malekith. And then there's Tomb Kings. The sole reason I bought TW2.

Tomb Kings are also "locked" and furthermore one of the Tomb Kings, Arkhan the Black, cannot Co-Op mode with anyone. Lore is one thing. But when the first game freely allowed Vampire Counts to ally with the likes of Empire, Dwarves or even Wood Elves, it is a disappointment when discovering the second does not allow it.

Finally, it seems there's a paywall lock as well. My friend and I could not play Eye of Vortex campaign, even Head-to-Head, with myself as Tomb Kings, and him without the DLC. YES, BOTH PLAYERS MUST OWN THE DLC.

So beware if like me, you were lulled into thinking you could Co-Op freely with a friend in the Eye of Vortex campaign. It is not so. Hopefully these issues are just oversights by CA but in today's age of Loot Boxes, half-finished games and early access.. that hope is dwindling fast.

These issues are not present in the Mortal Empires Campaign, which leads me to believe these issues are intentional. So for a fun Co-Op experience, Mortal Empires is where it's at.\t\t\t\t\t

First off four unique lords that are great, there is variety and the campaign is nice.Then theres the race itself which is a breath of fresh air both in campaign and battles.
Playing like the vampire lords with crumble they again bring a important focus to lords and heroes with raise dead style abilities.
And dear god do all these units look smexy! Like I could spent hours to watch the units and see all the little bits and bobs which I find important.

All in all these things alone already make it worth buying, as my negative points are going to focus on the lore and a few things in the campaign.

At the time of writing this review the campaign as something that is a bit of a issue for me, when taking none tomb king settlements they remain a settlemenet of the race it was meant for. That is to say an orc settlement remains an orc settlement. While tomb kings have a great ritual that lets you get an architect to turn a place into a level 3 settlement that states "He will make it a place suited for a tomb king"
It's a petty thing I know I know but in general tomb kings would find any other type of strructure/layout highly confusing.

Then theres the lore issue, that arkan the black wants to get the books of nagash is fine because he would wanna controll the black pyramide. But to pretty much every other tomb king but him this place is like a biohazard, nagash is the reason the tomb kings woke up like skeletons and not in paradise like they were promissed.
Seeking to controll it to expand their power is a wrong way, especially settra whom thinks Nagash and all his stuff is the absolute worst. While nagash did the whole necromancy thing the tomb kings soldiers are not animated dead. Just the dead brought back to life as a skeleton.
Honestly the only way this would've worked is if the pyramide got active and the tomb kings are worried that letting it run just like that would bring even more dispair.
Thus collecting the books to try and stop this thing from going off again would be a priority. (and dont get me started on some of the book locations.)

I could go on but thats the major gripe I have with the story, but all in all pretty good game worth buying this DLC.\t\t\t\t\t
