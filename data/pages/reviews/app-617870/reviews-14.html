DLC adding a new race of undead. We got four new legendary lords here, three of them are pretty standard except for one who is able to recruit vampire units. I'm not saying that these lords are boring or weak, they have cool designs and lots of interesting abilities. The race itself has very interesting mechanics, and one of the main ones is the fact that our units are free, but we can only recruit them in a limited amount. Our undead empire is also supported by the Books of Nagash, scattered all over the world, giving us powerful campaign and battle map bonuses. I would also like to take into account the well-made infantry units, and maybe the best monster units in the game. It is also probably the only campaign that refers to Warhammer 40k so much.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought it on sale. Not sure if worth full price. However - this is by far my favourite faction in the game. Perfect undead nation that plays like Medieval Total War - hold enemy down with trash, flank with strong units, and wipe them out.

Some tiny mechanics add to the flavor - there's no upkeep, but pretty much no income. So the cash you grab by winning battles or sacking settlements counts. On the other hand when you occupy or slaughter enemy prisoners you get faction currency that unlocks regiments of renown or extra gear. You get chariots that slaughter isolated units of infantry by repeated charge, but cannot withstand a prolonged battle. Chariots suck against cavalry from what I've seen so there's that too - enemy fields cavalry so you need to deploy your chariots ASAP but not too soon.

Fun!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's a controversial opinion to what obviously is the general opinion about this dlc:
Unless you really wan't to play with Tomb King's don't bother buying it. Ill tell you why In topic's:

- Settra the Imperishable: I decided to buy this dlc after learning the lore behind Settra and how cool and powerful this guy and his army is. But yeah... leave that powerful guy and his army for the lore because in the game he is completely lacking in power. Settra get's beaten easily in melee by pretty much any other legendary lord... ohhh wait but he is a spell caster... yeah and his spells are also lacking and weak compared to other spell caster LL's. Heh! So much for being imperishable...

- Chariots: The chariots are useless In the end game, yes they last a bit longer than cavalry in melee but their charge just don't seem to work, they can barely run over the third line of a low tier infantry unit with no charge defense.

- Mid game units: Infantry is good and so are the Ushabti but the Scorpion Is very squishy even against infantry.

- End game units: Oh man this was the biggest disappointment, you take a looong time to unlock them which is ok, can't really expect a barren desert with a few cities lost to the sand to have high growth speed. But then when you finally get them... nothing... NOTHING! but the Khemrian Warsphinx (that If playing with Settra you will get one from the start) and the Bone Giant Is worth the army slot.
The Necrosphinx Is only useful against cavalry and smaller monster units, they will deal very little damage to infantry unlike other big anti-large units, speaking of which, It will need support if It gets In a fight with a Carnosaur or both will end up routing.
The Khemric Titan Is just a complete tease. It looks really good but It will disappoint you when you finally get it. Seriously It Is completely useless other than making you produce more winds of magic juice.

- Buildings: Well since you need to build army buildings again and again so you unlock more unit capacity you'll end up not building most of the other stuff which you won't mind because they aren't necessary since they provide very little income and Tomb King units cost nothing to recruit and don't require upkeep.

- Mechanics: You will get Organ Jars after battles which you will spend on researches and some Kingdom events. Kind of like when you play with Teeny-weeny from Cult of Sotek but without that very cool spell you get in the end. You can also acquire Books of Nagash that will be spread around the world in possession of rogue armies or factions. Each book will give you some permanent buffs, Similar to The Vampire Coast coins you get from defeating captains...but don't expect anything as awesome as the sea shanties here either.

Whelp I probably left something out but this puppy Is bored and want's to go play something else. Doubt anybody Is going to read this anyways. But If you took the time to read It what I took the time to write... I'm grateful If you think it helped. Cheers!\t\t\t\t\t
