\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey really hit it out of the park for me with this DLC. Right off the bat, the production value is really good, be it sound design, drawn cutscenes and voiced intros, unit and building detail or even special large vs large animations of for example Hierotitans when fighting giants. But to be fair, it had damn well better be polished after the Foundation Update delays.

They get RoR and other special units from their faction resource combined with trade resources and acquire special items the same way (via the Mortuary Cult mechanic). That means that trade goods have a real effect on your campaign, allowing you to actively choose the items you want for your lords and heroes and resurrecting the Legions your army needs. Their faction resource is amassed rather slowly by tech, conquest, quests and character skills. They have 4 sub-factions with 2 and 3 quest battles each. What I'm trying to say is that the ammount of content in this release is refreshing. You can also play them in ME and the Vortex, while they cannot intervene in the Vortex, other factions will still use that mechanic to compete.

I will not go into army composition and units because I'm sure you've watched a YT video by now and that information can be readily found anywhere. Economically, they are slow boomers with initially low income but no expenditure on military. Building diversity is similar to other factions, there are factions with less special buildings, but certainly also with more.

Their starting positions are well distributed and every lord feels much more unique than usual. The tech tree is big and slow to progress in, but besides the usual unit upgrades, it features unlockable special lords and campaign boost techs, as well as allowing you to unlock heroes, which for other races require buildings instead.

Overall, I'm really happy with this release.\t\t\t\t\t
