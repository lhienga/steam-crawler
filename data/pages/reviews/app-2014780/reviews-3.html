Понятно, что наши любимые Ламинары не будут ничего делать с 12 версией. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- When you play the game, you actually feel the weight of the aircraft. Every single movement depends on how you control it.
- I really like vanilla airports. The way they look is stunning (especially US airports).
- The graphics are beautiful; Reflections on the aircraft, water, amount of world vegetation, and soft shadows look amazing.
- Love the tiny details of world generation. e.g., moving cars on roads, light poles, buildings, etc.
- The number of aircraft in this game is pretty big. Also, they all look just as beautiful as in real life (except for B737. It's just bad, so you can consider this as a con).
Cons:
- Awful optimisation. It took them almost a year to somehow optimize the game so it actually became playable (however, still, it's not the best).
- Despite the above-mentioned pros regarding graphics, the clouds are just ridiculously horrible. They look pixelated EVEN if your Anti-Aliasing is set to 8x MSAA.

Overall it's a decent flight simulator that represents all the beauty of the sky and aviation in general. I'd give it a solid 7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite my amount of hours in-game, I played all of the Flight School as well as my flight path from my main house's airport to my holiday house (about 1 hour flight with a DA62--my plane I own) and not only was it extremely realistic and enjoyable to fly, it was captivating. X-Plane's main competitor MSFS cannot compare. The large aspect with MSFS is to have "experiences" of the world, with a simple, easy to pick up form factor. X-Plane is brutal to the new player, but extremely rewarding. They DO NOT have completely accurate airports, unless your pockets run deep enough, but it is easily worth every penny. The two airports I like to fly most are detailed pretty nicely, so I do not need a 100% accurate model, but if you do it will cost you on average $35-$40 for a handcrafted airport. Extremely well-made game, and edges out MSFS by a landslide in accuracy and enjoyment for the seasoned Aviation Enthusiast.\t\t\t\t\t
