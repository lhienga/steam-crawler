\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing Age of Empires II since 1999. It remains one of my favorite gaming experiences. All the core RTS elements are present, but the rock/paper/scissor gameplay is unmatched. Without a doubt the core DE game and Return of Rome are the best value if you can't afford every DLC. That provides you with the majority of the game's civilizations as well as access to the updated and most playable version of the original Age of Empires. The depth to this game is endless, with each civilization feeling a little bit different and allowing the player to tackle scenarios in distinct ways. Perhaps the only downside is the sheer breadth of the game for those starting out. The original release of Age of Empires II had 13 civilizations, but now, if you purchase every DLC, there are 45! This is great for people who have kept up with the game, but may seem overwhelming trying to discover every civ bonus and identity if you are new to the series. Too much content is rarely an issue, but for anyone considering the competitive scene it may be tough to get your feet under you. All in all, however, this is one of those rare near perfect games. For me it is a 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
