\r\n\t\t\t\t\tif this looks like you'd enjoy it I can almost certainly recommend with only one reservation.

Speaking only for the singleplayer campaign side of things (which a vast majority of the 125.6 hours clocked at time of writing have gone into), I can definitely say that even just the base game there is no shortage of material to play through and no worries the quality of most missions. Thanks to the inclusion of DLCs from previous editions of AoE2, there are some 25 campaigns of 4-6 missions each and a smattering of loose scenarios in the base game. I still haven't completed many of them. You will get your money's worth. The game is designed well if a bit of a challenge to get your head around, in particular because the tutorial campaign is eye-wateringly inadequate.

The real question you must ask yourself is how much time you have available and are willing to dedicate to this game. The game almost demands a certain confidence and aggression on your part, which I don't think many new players will naturally gravitate to. Unfortunately between a complicated set of unit interactions and a stubborn AI, a 45-60 minute mission can easily turn into a three and a half hour stalemate for the inexperienced player (and judging by comments I've seen, I'm hardly alone in that experience). Frankly I think that it is inexusable, but for a 25 year old game we're unlikely to see anything be done about it. If you can spend say, entire evenings on this game I think it's an easy recommendation. If you're someone who juggles many responsibilities and whose gaming sessions are the occasional 90 minutes, I don't think you'll easily manage to get deep enough into the game for it to stop being frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t
