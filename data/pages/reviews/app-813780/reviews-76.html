\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has too many bugs for me to recommend it. The game itself is awesome, and sometimes you can have a lot of fun with it, but I can't in good conscience encourage people to spend money on a game with so many bugs. And every time the devs "fix" one of the bugs, they introduce new ones to take its place.

The good:
- it's a very deep RTS with lots of interesting viable strategies and randomly generated maps keeping every game fresh
- many QoL improvements over the original AoE2: you can shift-click to queue multiple commands for a unit, you can attack-move, there's control groups (aka unit bookmarks), there's a global production queue, etc
- there's matchmaking! with a big community so you can usually get matched in about 2 minutes. with someone of the same skill.
- you can spectate live matches in-game, which is a nice way to enjoy the game without the stress or commitment of actually playing.
- balance improvements over the original, such as much-needed buffs to melee infantry
- very strong AI opponents. in most RTS games (including original AoE2) the hardest AI setting is just the second-hardest with a massive artificial advantage, but in this game it plays fair. it plays like a human in so many ways you wouldn't expect. it's very tough, and rewarding to beat.

the YMMV:
- music. i'll always miss the original music from my childhood. thankfully, most of the definitive edition's music is remixes and new tracks rather than removed tracks, but I wold still rather the originals, especially the main menu music.

the bad (mostly bugs):
- horrible unit pathfinding. it is so common and so frustrating to lose games because your units just go the opposite direction as you order them, or they just get stuck and can't attack anything. villagers take the long way around obstacles for no reason, and get stuck on each other, or just stuck on thin air, and forget their task and go idle.
- lots of other in-game bugs. town bell (tells your villagers to hide from attackers) not working properly. villagers ordered to build a wall sometimes skip a tile for some reason, letting the enemy in. etc.
- lots of out-of-game bugs too. lobbies bug out. menus bug out. and when the menus aren't bugging out, they're the laggiest thing in the known universe for some reason (the game itself does not lag).
- they removed LAN multiplayer :( (actually, there's still a button for it. but it's inside the multiplayer menu which is inaccessible if you're not connected to their servers. brilliant)
- questionable monetization. they're selling DLC civs, so you can face people in ranked games who are playing civs you can't. it's not necessarily pay to win because the DLC civs aren't blatantly stronger than the base ones, but still kinda . they're also hella overpriced for a game that already costs money.\t\t\t\t\t
