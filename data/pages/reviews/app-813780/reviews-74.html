\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing on and off this game for about 20 years with previous versions included (HD/AoC disk version).

Let's start with the positives:
  • The game balance is about at its best it has ever been civilization wise.
  • There are updates being released that address balance
  • Many Quality of Life features that are exclusive to DE - like the queue of farms (from AoC) expanded to "auto farm reseed"
  • There is a queue system for ranked play
  • New textures & 4k textures available
  • New campaigns. Although most of them are behind separate DLCs, the content is there

Now to the negatives
  • Although Public Update Preview (PUP) is usually available before a new patch, it's not uncommon for game breaking bugs to make it past the phase. This includes games crashing as well as "infinite relic multiplication"
  • Pathing. One of the core systems to an RTS game, updates claiming "pathing improvements" or "fixes" usually made it worse.

Conclusion
You, the person reading this, is probably asking right now: Why is the review negative with the positives list being so much longer?
In one word: pathing.
In longer explanation:
As I have mentioned I've been playing the game on and off for about 20 years. And I want to cry now that I uninstalled the game since it is one of the games I love.
The game breaking bugs usually got patched quickly after being found. I could see past some of the non-trivial ones. Though, it still happened more often than not, it is not the end of the world as it got fixed and I could continue playing the game.
The point when it started to go downhill is when pathing, a core system, was started being altered. It was slowly getting worse, occasionally getting better. However the current state is very bad.
If you're interested how bad, take a look at the following video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=apKc3Q1xQyE
In game it feels like units are not listening to the command. Units will shuffle around instead of walking, villagers randomly stop and walking 8 tiles around the building rather than going around the building from the other side which is about 3 tiles.

Do I recommend this game?
If you want to play only single player and you don't play multiplayer, possibly. You might not notice the pathing. Still, be warned
For anyone wanting to play multiplayer/ranked games: Big No. Units seemingly not listening to commands should be an immediate turn off.\t\t\t\t\t
