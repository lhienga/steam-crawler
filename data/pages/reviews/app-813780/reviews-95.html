\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended when on sale if you played the original. Still recommended at full price if you love RTS.
Paid: 14.99GBP

PROs
+ great long term value and replayability
+ nice to see that this oldie got its proper remaster
+ fun campaigns
+ great music
+ lots of civs
+ micro transactions are great for investors, and you have the choice not to buy
+ some nice automations like farm auto re-seed and 1st scout auto scouting
+ lots of mods
+ you aren't forced to play multiplayer, and if you do, please play with 3 friends, or you'll lose interest as you lost the first 50+ games which can get very frustrating
+ not a hectic as StarCraft
+ lovely rock, paper, scissor mechanics
+ lots of people are still playing this game
+ a lot less toxic community than League of Legends, CoD

CONs
- performance poor on i5-8350U with Intel HD 620 iGPU (22FPS)
- graphics cannot be dialled down low enough
- if 1 player lags, everyone's game experience suffers as everyone's game slows to the weakest player's experience
- no improvement to unit path finding which is still atrocious 24 years after OG AoE2's release
- zoom out contents directly linked to monitor resolution: 2.5k on 27" or 4k on 32" recommended
- English voice acting is worse than OG AoE2, and everyone sounds generically American without any effort of putting on an accent
- Ranged units don't have a melee attack like in Battle Realms or Metal Fatigue (really underrated)
- the game snowballs very quickly as many inefficiencies add up later on

Issue with multiplayer:
- 20% of matches are smurfs ruining everyone's experience
- waiting to find 4v4 matches takes 10-15min
- you are forced to play maps you don't like
- you can only ban 4 cards on 4v4, but we only want to play Black Forest, Amazon Tunnel and Arena...
- you need to commit to at least 45min time investment per game incl. waiting time
- can be really stressful when you haven't played in a many months, and you need to constantly multi task\t\t\t\t\t
