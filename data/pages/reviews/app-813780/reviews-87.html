\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don’t get why this game is hyped so much. I loved AoK as a kid and after 30 minutes of playing the so-called “Definitive Edition”, I’m already disappointed.

When you first start the game you immediately find out that everything your cursor touches is read out. That’s a great accessibility feature. What’s not great is that the voice is unbearably robotic and fails to even pronounce the game’s title correctly. It also makes awkward pauses while reading. I wonder if this is really useful to visually impaired players.

What’s bad about this is: How do you turn it off? The toggles are not clearly labeled, so I had to google to find them. Side note: There are three toggles, one controls the voiceover in the menu, one ingame and the third I forgot. Only the one for the menu is enabled by default. What’s the point of that? Once you’re in your first game, accessibility is toggled of by default.

I launched the tutorial to see if anything meaningful changed. The visuals did, but not much more. Oh, and the beautiful Scottish voice from the William Wallace campaign got replaced by someone who didn’t even try to sound Scottish. What a shame!

Playing the first “how to get good” campaign (which I find a nice addition), I tried to change the hotkeys, since I like to scroll using WASD, which isn’t possible by default. Guess what? There as a whole menu for changing the hotkeys, which is broken at least since January 2021! This is so infuriating. You made the effort to make the game accessible to visually impaired gamers, but fail to make hotkeys work for 3 years⁉

Further into the campaign I noticed that clicking buttons doesn’t work about half the time. No, I’m not too dumb to read my resource stocks. I have more than 50 food, enough housing, click the button to create a pawn, and nothing happens. Only after I move the cursor, I can click it. This occurs so often that I wonder if the devs even played their game once during ‘testing’.

While aiming for gold in the resource gathering challenge, I repeatedly found that pawns would stand idly for a few seconds before remembering that they’re shepherds and killing the next sheep. That’s infuriating when you’re always a few seconds short of obtaining the gold medal. I thought with 2023 technology fetching the next task for a pawn should be instant, but well…

I still have my old copy of AoK, but it doesn’t work on new Windows versions because Microsoft decided not to bundle a codec the game needs. With a game this ♥♥♥♥♥♥ (that used to be great), I really wonder what Microsoft’s cut in the sales is.

I got the game for €5, and I’m still pondering whether I should return it.\t\t\t\t\t

PROS

CONS

RECOMMENDATION
If you're a fan of real-time strategy games, I highly recommend Age of Empires II: Definitive Edition. It's a great way to experience a classic game with nice graphics and gameplay.
