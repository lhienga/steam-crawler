I was one of those was introduced to AoE when buying AoE2 Gold Edition (Base + Expansion). The original campaigns were alright, but I honestly found the real meat of Age of Empires lie in the skirmish and multiplayer action. Age of Empires II is set in the medieval period and players progress across four R&D stages (as with any AoE game). Players build up their bases, managing their population pool between villagers, military and support units to eventually eliminating opponents. In addition to the traditional conquest skirmish match, you have additional modes with their own strategies.
Between the original and current resurgent releases, Age of Empires II has maintained a strong player and modding community. AoE2 has remained the preferred entry for Age of Empires' competitive scene - Competitive AoE sounds a lot like the "Rugby" of RTS with Empire Wars (short form action-heavy format) and traditional full-length games both having their respective E-Sports presence.
AoE2HD got peoples' attention on Steam, Definitive Edition debuted the franchise on XBOX made became a household name for Game Pass users. If you're wondering how this game fairs on a gamepad... Surprisingly well, actually - Gamepad vs Keyboard/Mouse is like comparing Automatic vs Manual: Each have their strengths and weakness, but ultimately come down to personal preferences given how intuitively AoE plays on an XBOX controller. If you prefer automation, you're going to warm very quickly to controller. Purists however will naturally consider that blasphemy and play as they always have. Any which way you play, AoE2 Definitive has Cross-Platform multiplayer for players new and old to enjoy!

Would I recommend Age of Empires II Definitive Edition?

If you consider yourself a self-respecting real-time strategy connoisseur... Absolutely!
With the William Wallace campaign newcomers will have concise set of tutorials to give a solid introduction to the basics of AoE and a skill foundation to build upon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had thousands of games and thousands of hours of gameplay on this laptop. Their October/November 2023 update made it completely unplayable. A game that I'd previously been able to play without issue now crashes my computer every single time I attempt to play it. Now it doesn't last more than 10 seconds on the main menu before it crashes not just the game, but my entire computer.

I've tried:
uninstalling and reinstalling the game
Disabling overlays
Disabling all mods
reducing fps and graphic quality
Running game as administrator
Disconnecting internet and starting game offline
Increase virtual memory size to 32GB
Updating windows drivers
Updating Nvidia drivers
Disabling any antivirus programs before running the game
Opening the game in windowed mode

Nothing works. This is by far the most amount of effort I have put into getting a game to work again. No other games have ever been this troublesome to run. And the sad thing is it USED to work just fine on this same machine. So it's not a hardware issue.

Here's the machine specs:
Machine is a Razer Blade laptop
Intel(R) Core(TM) i7-7700HQ CPU @ 2.80GHz 2.80 GHz
16GB RAM
NVIDIA GeForce GTX 1060 (14GB total memory, 6GB VRAM)
Windows 10 Home (64-bit operating system, x64-based processor)
DirectX 12 (DirectDraw enabled, Direct3D enabled, AGP texture accel enabled)
