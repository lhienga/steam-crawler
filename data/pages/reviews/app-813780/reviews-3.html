My only criticism - and it's nowhere near enough to be a deal-breaker - is that they are adding too many civilisations with new unique units and buildings, and no *true* way to disable them even if you untick the DLC in Steam. Along with patches that rebalance some old OP civ and team bonuses for obvious reasons.

AoE2 is supposed to be a game where most civs are the same with a few different bonuses and limitations to their tech. This is what separates AoE2 from other RTSs such as Age of Mythology, StarCraft, etc. where each civ has wildly different flavour.

This goes slightly against the original design and intention of the game but really not enough to be a dealbreaker. If that's enough to be a deal-breaker for you, and you want the true OG experience but with modern compatibility, then get the 2013 "HD Edition" instead in which such content can be disabled from in-game which also entails disabling some balance reworks.

Really you should just get both this one + the 2013 HD edition, you won't regret it if you like AoE2 in any capacity.\t\t\t\t\t
