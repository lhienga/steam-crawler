\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's just a very well made game, I highly recommend it. Tons of quality-of-life features that you rarely see in other games, it's satisfying, it runs well (unless your opponent in multiplayer is producing lag). It allows for a lot of creativity in your approach. There are also tons of campaigns that take a long time to play through, in the good sense.

There are issues with the game (currently, unit pathing is in a pretty bad state), but it still receives active updates and the issues are tolerable, unlike in many other games.

Learning to play well requires a lot of practice, but you can just play single player, with friends, or simply let the ranked matchmaker place you in the lower brackets. Low-skill ranked is probably more fun anyway. Playing skirmish games against the AI is fine, but the AI gets rather predictable.

You don't need any of the DLCs to have a full experience. The DLCs add new campaigns and new civilizations (with the exception of "Return of Rome", which adds some other things as well, but that's not that relevant). There are plenty of civilizations in the base game to play as, so I would recommend getting a feel for the game and then deciding which of the DLC civilizations you're interested in.\t\t\t\t\t
