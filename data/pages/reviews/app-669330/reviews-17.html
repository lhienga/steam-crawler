\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechabellum...

Well, been playin this game since it first surfaced and been playin it ever since. It´s a Short Fun game at times. Games usually dont last more then 20 Minuten and mostly in Teamgames. 1v1´s are often shorter.

This game has a pretty nice base Idea. Giant Stompy robots, mechas and all kinds of Sci-fi stuff in an visually pleasing autobattler. Since RTS games are basicly dead, I came to this to feed my competetive strategy itch.

The Playerbase is small but Solid as far as I can tell by the time of writing this review. But why do I write a negative review even if I´ve played 180 hours of it. It can´t be that bad, cant it?

Well. I played this much, mostly because of the fact stated earlier, its a quick nice round without a major time invest. You know, one of the kind where you come home from Work/School/whatever and can just play a round or two without spending the whole evening on it. Since I am a mostly Functioning adult, I often find myself just with a couple of hours every second evening to play.
Anyway, since there are no real alternatives for this, I stuck with it for a while. After all, I paid money for it.
Let me get the good things out of the Way and then I will showcase, why I do not recommend it.

-Good Looking/Nice Visuals + Sounddesign
-rarely gets overwhelming on you, if you watch the game play out you basicly allways see whats happening
-short rounds 5-10 Minutes solo, 5-20 Mins in 2v2
-Unique as of now, there is no game comparible to it (even if its an autobattler, it plays differently)
-Honestly, if you´re willing to learn and improve yourself, quite addicting in the beginning

Now lets get to the negatives:
-Imbalance, wich is a big thing in basicly every RTS or strategy game. There some units are stupidly overpowered/carry way more weight then they should while others are usefull in 1 of 100 games
-Leveling up, while its logical for some kind of ingame progression, for your units to level up and get stronger. It often scales badly. A level up just doubles all the stats of the Unit(except speed and range) wich can quickly lead to snowballing where suddenly 1 units completly dominates the battlefield way beyond its designated role.
-Game is based on a Card system. Each round you get 4 cards/abilities to choose from wich sometimes allready decide the outcome. Like, your enemy just randomly picks the exact counter to your Units, wich can totaly happen.
-the "meta" of the game is basicly dont have fun. There is an MMR/Elo in place and like all competetive online games, some people are so starved for positive reinforcment that they do everything to win. Dont try new tactics, there are streamers out there who do that for you 10 hours a day. Copy everything, dont think for yourself and use every bit of cheese the game offers. Honestly, in my 180 hours the most fun games where when the game initial released, since no body had a clue what they´re doing and everyone tried stuff out. Nowadays you basicly see the same 3-5 Units every game and the outcome is often dictated just by a simple missbuy/wrong decision.
-Toxicity. Ya, well. I am not new to online Gaming and this game is just no exception. Ranging from Insults to Cheating/smurfing
-This game runs on Peer to Peer. If you ever have played Peer to Peer games before, you know how this can be abused. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tAddictive Autobattler that is just a RNG dice roll. You could also just sit in front of a slot machine.

The Graphics are decent, but for a middle-class PC the game gets slow and jerky in the later Rounds. For an Autobattler that seems just bad.

The Community is mostly just chatting but in the game toxic. Who ever thought it a good idea to chat with your opponent.

The Target priority of the Game is blatant bad. Seems to me, the Game checks once for every Unit which enemy Is closest and then follows until it gets attacked by something closer or the target dies. While this could be utilised for strategies, it is most often not intentional and just annoying to see Units running around and getting blasted by long range weapons. And let’s just not talk about the Hit-zones of not direct weapons.
Since there are 2 hights (Air/Land) it is very hard to see in the game where a Unit actually is. That makes the targeting even worse and with that, the understanding what went wrong.

The game does nothing in terms of balance and development. The shuffling start Units/ Round Cards and special Types of commanders should let the player not play all the time the same Meta. But sadly, over all the time I`ve played the Game the spamming of some Units just went overboard. The stone, scissors, paper principle just does not work in this game. The most valued Stat is always range and with that comes the Level up. So basically, every game is either a downward- or an upward spiral with extra steps. The last update with free extra Units does not solve this issue in any way. In most Rounds it just makes it worse.

It’s like the Developer just wants to Randomise everything in the game to the maximum to push the slot machine moments.

The ELO and Points system I cannot fathom to this day. Some games I win and lose points, some games I lose and get points. But that’s imaginary Points on early access, so whatever.

The in-game shop for emojis is okay-ish. But to buy a Skin of a Unit you would need to play about 600 games … for ONE skin … (-_-“)
But you can always use real money for that… in an early access game. At this point it looks as if the “early access” is just an excuse to not release it as a finished game.

So if you read to this point, just wait for the final game ... or just get something else from the overflowing early access market.\t\t\t\t\t
