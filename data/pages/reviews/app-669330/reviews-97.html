\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game may be fun in the future, but as of July-23 it's worth avoiding. A new MMR system has been implemented that replaces the previous combat power (CP) system. CP was a measure of approximately how many games you'd played, so would match people of similar experience, not skill. MMR is a not-overly transparent attempt to introduce an ELO-esque system into the game. This is resulting in massively mis-matched games.

The game currently has 3 cliques around this:
- The first say that it's fine, good, even perfect and you just need to play 20 or so games to get your MMR to where it should be, then you should be at around a 50% win/lose ratio
- The second say that they've played 2x-3x that number of games and still get a massive discrepancy in their matches, with the MMR not at all being a reflection of skill and the resulting matches being far worse than the previous approach
- The third group say that they're casual, want to play for fun and losing 20 games to be matched with people their own skill level is not acceptable, so they uninstall

The PvP mode is the only mode worth considering. PvE is a lazy attempt where you can plan for and counter nothing because the opposing forces are random each round. When the devs have finalised what all these scoring systems are and people have played enough that the MMR is settled, it MAY be worth revisiting.\t\t\t\t\t
