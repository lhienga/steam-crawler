\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR:

- Even in it's alpha state, I would give this 11/10 and would definitely recommend. The gameplay is insanely addictive and also a very unique experience.

- It's a AAA $60 game for a fraction of the cost, and you can't tell it's an alpha. Devs are active, but the game is basically complete.

- Almost endless replayability.

- Takes minutes to learn but is still extremely addictive/tense/exciting/enjoyable.

- Great community.

* * * * * * * * * *

Where to start? I don't know. I almost never write reviews, so obviously there is something significant about this game.

Let's just dive in:

- 11/10. And not just for people that like a certain genre. I mean, in general, this game is a 11/10.

- This game is unique and primed to be highly engaging and addictive.

- Very simple to learn and enjoyable to master.

- Not too complex or complicated, but still has enough variety to avoid being boring.

- Gameplay mechanics are highly addictive and very tense.

- Exciting replayability.

The sky is the limit how you want to approach the game, but again, without all of the grueling aspects of needing to read a highly detailed 3000 page manual on how to play the game.

The game does a very good job of staying both simple and exciting.

* * * * * * * * * *

Someone commented that: 'This game is everything that we love about the RTS genre...if they got rid of everything that's annoying about RTS games and replaced them with highly addictive and exciting elements, instead.'

I agree with this comment.

* * * * * * * * * *

What's brilliant about it is it's unique simplicity.

At it's core, this game's genius borrows from and elevates elements of Rock-Paper-Scissors, Tug-of-War, Card Games, Board Games, and RTS, (probably in that order)...but it's basically it's own genre and somehow manages to take all of the most exciting and addictive elements of these genres and somehow puts them all into one game...and somehow keeps everything very simple:

- There is no micro-managing or need to focus on 101 different details.
- Battles are short enough to avoid any kind of boredom, but long enough to provide enough excitement.
- Battles usually last about 10 rounds, and it's not uncommon to lose the first half of the rounds, but to then come storming back to win. Incredible tension without needing to sit there for an hour micro-managing.

* * * * * * * * * *

- Unique and rewarding experience. Highly energized and engaging gameplay.

- The community is awesome and glad to answer questions. In my first several days, I haven't noticed anyone being rude or obnoxious.

- Lots of supporting You Tube content online if you want to dive into different aspects of the game or learn from the experts.

- A surprising new experience!! I can't really underscore this enough. It's not your typical game and you'll be very glad you tried it.

* * * * * * * * * *

A while back, there was a study done on 'what makes twitter/facebook/you tube addictive'.

It was amazing to see the way that human biology and psychology were manipulated in order to keep people engaged with Twitter.

From the placement of the buttons, to the constant updating, to the reward/like/dopamine centres of the brain, to the communal instincts of mammals, to the need to learn news, to the need to have something to talk about, etc. It's all fine-tuned to keep people engaged and addicted.

Well, Mechabellum is basically a bit like the 'video game equivalent' of Twitter/Facebook/You Tube.

* * * * * * * * * *

What to expect:

Your first game you might start with some artillery and some ground units.
In the first round your artillery might decimate your opponent.

In the second round, your opponent might make some air units or some speedy swarms that your artillery can't easily target and wipe out your artillery.

In the third round, you might make some anti-air to counter him easily taking out your artillery, or flame thrower unit that can lambaste their speedy swarms.

In the fourth round, your opponent might make some big units, like an air ship, that targets both ground and air units.

Etc. Etc. Etc.

So it definitely has that rock-paper-scissor element...but each unit has enough different upgrades to keep things from getting boring, but also you don't need to memorize a 300 page manual and all kinds of different micro-managed or game specifics, either.

Also, there are wildcards that can give added perks and strengths (and weaknesses) to different units.

* * * * * * * * * *

At the end of the day, the gamers are left with a riveting and addictive experience that draws them back for
more and more. This game has at least the replayability of 'Counterstrike' or 'COD' or any FPS game in terms of finding different ways to get the objective.

Regardless of your "favourite genre", I can't imagine a gamer not liking this game.

Even in it's alpha state, I would give this 11/10 and would definitely recommend.

* * * * * * * * * *

Apologies if the review is a bit long or scattered.
I'll come back and update in the future.
But I wanted to at least get this out there so that people don't miss out on this awesome game.\t\t\t\t\t
