\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLovely game, and there's quite a bit of strategic depth to be experienced here. I'll talk a bit more about my experience beyond simple gameplay though.

Chat is actually pretty interesting here. I very much enjoyed the auto-translate function that allows me to communicate with people I normally wouldn't be able to (this is something I hope more games will emulate in the future). The global chat (at least channel 501, which is mostly English speakers) is, for better or for worse, completely unmoderated, and this brings out some very interesting trolls and people starved for attention. They can be interesting in small doses, for sure.

While I did enjoy the game, I ended up walking away from it mostly because of the chat, too. I guess I'm too thin skinned to have people gloating over their wins, or just the creative insults I have had hurled my way playing the game. I know that's not really Dev's fault how people chat online (and there are the rare occasions where you actually have a conversation about your strategy that was really interesting), but ultimately, there are a lot of other competitive games I can be playing without being insulted and otherwise made to feel bad.

Still, if you like auto battlers and strategic games, you'll have a great time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually never put reviews for games but I feel like this one is an hidden gem and the community around it deserves more love.

Why should you buy this game?
- It's actually really cheap for the time you will sink in it :)
- You love(d) auto battler custom maps on warcraft 3 or Starcraft 2 like desert strike
- You want a competitive experience with very little RNG that follows the mantra: easy to learn, hard to master
- a game plays in 15-20 minutes, you can play it cooperatively in 2v2 or in 1v1, vs Bots or compete in community challenges.
- It's not a real time strategy game, you play your turn simultaneously with your opponent and then see the result of your strategy (principle of auto battler for people not knowing)

Why you shouldn't buy this game:
- You really don't like the theme. I'm myself not a big fan of the "mech vs mech" theme, but I love the gameplay enough so that I don't care too much.
- You are not into competitive gaming: it is true that, at the moment, this is a 100% competitive game. Even if you can play it vs bot, the salt of the game is its competitive match (1v1 or 2v2).

But if these 2 points are not an issue for you, I really suggest to try this one out. I see it as a nice replacement for starcraft 2 when I don't want to havean intense micro match: you have the same strategic/tactical focus, but since it's turn based, you have the time to think about your strategy and plan execution.

Things to improve (in my humble opinion):
1- I personally hope they will bring more visual diversity for the future. The game is currently highly symetric, which is great for balance but I miss the assymetry of other games: having aliens ot other race would be a big balance challenge for sure, but it would increase the immersion for me.
2- Balance: at the moment we can feel that the balance is not always great, but the devs are reacting and updating the game at their pace.
3- quality of life. There are a few things that would make the game so much better for competitive play: having an unranked option to play. Once you arrive at a decent MMR, you can feel a bit paralysed to try new things because you can lose your MMR. It shouldn't be the case. I would also love to have an option to "jump back" into a lost game to see what you could have changed (move a unit, put another one) and tr to "solve" the puzzle of your loss.

For the rest, 8/10 game, 10/10 value for 15 EUR, can become a 10/10 overal in the future if the development continues.

Edit 21-03-2024: much more RNG added into the game. It became interesting but also quite frustrating, waiting to see new balance changes allowing to finally have that perfect auto battler it could be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put a lot of hours into Teamfight Tactics, Hearthstone Battlegrounds, as well as several other auto chess variants, and this one is definitely my favorite. It's easily the most skill-based game out of the ones I mentioned, but still has just enough RNG through cards between rounds to keep games from playing out exactly the same or being too deterministic.

Being able to customize the available techs on your units is great, allowing you to create your own play style. And because you can build whatever units you want (and can afford) on any given turn, you can actually play out your desired strategy instead of hoping that 5 other people in the lobby aren't doing the same thing and preventing you from getting offered the units you need.

The balance is pretty decent as well. It's not perfect, but everything does have a counter. Balance updates happen fairly regularly and are handled very well - they use minor tweaks one way or the other, as opposed to the Blizzard Entertainment strategy of buffing to S+++ tier or nerfing to the point of being unplayable in a single update.
