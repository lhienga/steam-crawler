\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechabellum is basically a recreation of me pretending to make Axis and Allies figurines fight each other as a kid, but with badass future mech technology. And it's round-based, unlike its Starcraft-contemporary Direct Strike, giving you time to analyze what went wrong in a specific matchup or formation, rather than feeling like you're always on the back foot. Some units do have clear cut answers, resulting in Rock, Paper, Scissors shenanigans between you and opponent. But various tech upgrades for units allow them to serve a specific purpose for specific matchups as well, opening the door for some creative synergy between unit combos.

The cards in-between rounds are good for mixing things up and bolstering your forces with more manpower or beefing up bigger units. The cards range from fire bombing your opponent's side of the field to increasing a unit's HP by 200%. There are also special unit cards that grant a specified squad of units at no additional cost. These typically also help patch up weak spots in your army or make openings you may have not realized otherwise.

Unfortunately, these unit cards tend to highlight the game's balance issues. While many units have tech upgrades that greatly amplify their damage or tankiness, things like the Wraith and Mustang either have access to insanely powerful tools or just plain versatile ones. EMP is effective at answering many of these issues, but it still stands that units could still use some tinkering balance-wise. The game seems to update often enough to where anything could change at any moment, so this isn't a terrible drawback.

All in all, this game is 10 bucks. It's fun to learn, hard to master and you're bound to get a lot of mileage out of it for what your paying. My biggest complaint is the UI needs some work *bad* and there needs to be more options for custom games, like changing the bot difficulty or changing deployment timer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you look for a game where you feel in control of the battle this is (currently) not for you. This iteration has made the game unapproachable for new players and will feel like a slot machine.

I played this game with great enjoyment almost daily since the open-beta. Normally I would not make a review based on a patch update. However, the way the developer did portrait the situation has destroyed my faith in them and I am worried about the direction they go in. Of course, early access is meant for changes and breathing life into the game. You should however respect the vision and spirit of the game that the players participating in the test phase have rated so positively.

The current patch offers random units between the rounds and the value of this drop increases over the course of the game. This means you can be served the perfect counter to your opponent's strategy for free while they may not get the same advantage out of the options depending on the specific board state. You need a deep understanding of how these units work, what their level means, what is most likely picked by your opponent and where they will be placed by your opponent.

They did label it as "Chaotic Fun" and then in hindsight explain to the community it is intended for the game's diversity of units on the fields and to allow more strategies. Yes of course there are now more strategies possible, but it is not because the game or the underperformed units are fun now. You are merely forced to pick these units now since they are offered for free. Instead of balancing the units for a diverse meta, they try to shoehorn the lesser picked units into the game by free unit drops.

On top of this, none of the core mechanics like economy or unit capacities and special abilities were adjusted. This leaves a mess of way too many units appearing at all once, making the experience exhausting and tiresome.

Stating to be satisfied with the "positive impact" this patch has brought seems dishonest and disregards the numerous negative feedback on the official discord server. This shows that the developer is out of touch with a dedicated part of their player-base.

It is really a shame since the game has been greatly entertaining and did not feel stale at all before. Nobody forced you to pick meta units, you could still win by using uncommon methods. It is now more stale than ever before since players simply build defensive compositions of chaff and wait for the huge unit drops to fix weaknesses. Most of my games have gone from carefully placing and leveling units to making a single line of robots and wait for a big drop to decide on a strategy.

I will update my review if there is a significant improvement of the situation. As it is now, I strongly advise to look for another game.\t\t\t\t\t
