\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start with the bad first. The game feels very rough. The UI and menus are very very dated, making the game feel like you're playing something from 2006, and not in a good way. Battles in general take far too long and the game doesn't give you the option to speed up the fight until certain conditions are met. The music tracks get repetitive very quickly.

That said, there is a lot of depth and variety in this game. While their is a big emphasis on countering the enemies units, there is a lot of validity in pushing your own strategy as well.
The thing that genuinely impresses me is the large variety of choices the game allows you to make during deployment. You only have so many credits, but you have a ton of options on how to use them. I've yet to feel like I only had 1 or 2 things I could do.
You have the units themselves, tech upgrades, your base structure upgrades, flank deployments, temporary utility devices, unit rank ups, ect.

Despite the battle phase taking far too long, matches are actually very quick. This is good as it never makes you feel like playing the game is a huge commitment.
The 1v1 and 2v2 format really helps with matchmaking queus. Regardless of the time of day, I can consistently find matches within 30sec-1min of queuing.

The game is still very new and done by a small team. There is a lot of room for this game to grow into it's own.

At a 15$ price point I can honestly recommend it even with it's flaws. It can only get better.\t\t\t\t\t
