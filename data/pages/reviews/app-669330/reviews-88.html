\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCaveat: Your enjoyment depends entirely on your competitiveness. If you don't being brutalized for hours while learning the basics, it's a good game that you'll enjoy.

Mechabellum is, currently, a great concept ruined by a lack of low and mid level play, and a lack of mechanics to balance out skill discrepancies, such as handicaps.

Matches take about 10 minutes, and while the mechanic of countering your enemy over multiple rounds gives you a chance to respond and recover, experienced players have often sealed your loss in the first 2-3 rounds. Mistakes in initial unit selection and placement can cripple you completely.

But, between experienced players, and lessons learned, a lot of the problems are mitigated, and it's an extremely fun competitive experience.

The gameplay itself is very interesting.
- You unlock, purchase, and place units over a series of rounds, repeatedly countering your enemy as they counter you.
- The game has many mechanics that are easy to understand, but difficult to master how they intertwine and interact.
- Units can...level up to become stronger...be given new skills...be modified out-of-game customization..be modified in-match with specializations...
- Placement and timing are critical elements, and repositioning units, or building screening elements, or synergistic groups, are all important to learn

Unfortunately, the player base is small and currently shrinking, and having a space for low/mid level players is going to rely on the game growing, which probably won't happen.\t\t\t\t\t
