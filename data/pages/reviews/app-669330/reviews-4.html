The game is, and has been, rife with issues since launch. there's a patter of one unit(or a combination of two units) consistently being overpowered while everything else remains useless. One month it's Marksmen, the next it's mustangs, stormcallers, steel balls, crawlers, or rhinos - now it's scorpions. And now, for some reason, the devs thought it would be a great idea to reward players every few rounds with a bunch of FREE UNITS - like RNG is going to make it any more fun. Spoiler: it actually ruins any semblance of strategy other than rushing towers (so we're back to launch with that stupid mechanic.)

After coming back to the game I find it in a far worse state than when i left it a few months after launch. They seem to have somehow broken all of the counters that *did* work in their efforts to balance the game. Mass crawlers lose to steel balls and sledgehammers without even having arc light support (makes no sense but whatever, who knows what the devs are smoking anymore). And don't get me started on EMP; it's been OP as hell and invalidated most possible strategies since launch - it's only gotten worse.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game could be a lot of fun but somehow the developers don't know what kind of autobattler they want to do. The amount of randomness and luck based things they put in the game is baffeling. This game is based on countering and predicting your enemies strategy, but everything can completly fall apart in one round. In between rounds there are 4 cards to choose from which can boost units give you a skill or you can get additional units. But sometimes the cards are pretty unbalanced. For example you can be winning every round and build up your army but then get killed in one round because in the late game the nuke card shows up and kills almost all of your army, there are shields and other things to counter skills but in the late game shields pop to fast to help you with that. There are a lot more examples like that. Units being to strong or it takes to long to counter them. If you have bad luck with your starting units and your opponent gets lucky he will have a strong board with leveled up units and it is really hard to get back into the game. I think the most obvious tell, that the developers don't seem to understand how to balance their game can be seen in the survival mode. In this mode you can win more rounds by cheesing with overlords then actually trying to play it correctly and that has been like that for more than a year. For a casual playing this game is way to unbalanced you have to put a lot of time and research in to get any good. There is potential in the game but in my opinion they would have to make it more skill based and less luck based.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been enjoying the game a lot. I am leaving a negative review to bring visibility to this issue.

1. Make the flanks a techology that can be unlocked so there is one round visibility on when you can expect flanks to be utilized or need to divert resources. Even the threat of the flank needing to be built up would be interesting.

2. Rework the mechanics of 'battlefield effects' -- they're a great idea with terrible implementation. Maybe make it round oriented, where the player unlock them and it is announced during the build phase. (E.g. fixed and regular cadence).

For both of these items, you can be in a good, long back and forth match, then it just gets blown up by RNG. It's ultimately extremely unrewarding and makes you feel like your match was a waste of time.

Some positives:

1. I love the implementation of the random squad cards, adds a lot of variety and opens up the strategies you can use.

2. The core mechanics of the game are very fun and I will recommend to friends.\t\t\t\t\t
