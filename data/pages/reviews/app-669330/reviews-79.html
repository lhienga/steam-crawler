\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPost "Chaotic Fun" patch edit:

TL;DR You should try the game out and decide for yourself, there is a lot to like here. This is probably still the best autobattler on the market if what you care about is skill expression. I've changed my positive review to negative because there was a large change to the game which was communicated poorly and I feel that the players were lied to by the devs about the permanence of the change.

This was one of my favorite games prior to the patch, and while it had problems I was hopeful there would be solutions that kept the spirit of the game intact. I've held off on this review as I was hopeful the changes would be temporary as advertised but that seems to not be the case.

While the Chaotic Fun patch has (at least in the short term) seemed to increase the variety of board states, I feel that it has done so at a rather large cost: building an incremental advantage has ceased to be nearly as meaningful. The sudden dumps of high value turns completely shift the game from one about building your game plan from turn 1, it's now more about adjusting your game plan around the power spike turns.

I liked building an insurmountable advantage through my understanding. I liked when I could see I had made too many mistakes to come back in a game anymore unless my opponent started making mistakes. Those games are mostly gone now, because a massive injection of units is just around the corner. Did you wait too long to start trying to scale your single target DPS units? No problem, just pick the high level Marksmen or Melters from the next unit dump. Have you fallen too far behind on chaff? Here's 6+ packs of Steels Balls ready to click Mech Div on.

These problems aren't that big of a deal at a very high level, no one rated highly is going to have these massive holes in their composition normally. The problem for me is, if the free unit turns are so much more impactful on the game state than the other turns, why are we even playing those other turns? That's a bit hyperbolic but my point is that this game WAS about scaling your army in a rather linear way with some major restrictions: Credits (money) and Deployments (number of units). The way to have sudden power spikes was to buy techs on units or to buy more expensive units (either higher tier or higher level) all of which was more or less predictable. Now I don't know if I'm going to have to deal with 6+ Wasps with shields next turn or maybe multiple leveled Fortresses so I play as low-commitment as possible until the free unit rounds, at which point I tech decisively and hope to finish the game before I'm punished for committing.

I'm not saying there is less skill in this new reactive way of playing (in fact there is likely more in some ways) but I am saying the game "feels" too different to me. It's a different game, with a different way of playing and it's not the same game I've been enjoying for the last many months. The changes were communicated to us as a 'temporary fun and chaotic experiment' when in reality they were already set to be implemented permanently with some minor tweaks.

To get out in front of people claiming "skill issue" or "adapt or die" I'm currently rated 1932 MMR and my win rate has slightly improved since the patch. My problem isn't that I'm not winning games or that I don't understand the implications of the change, it's that it's not the same game anymore and much of what I found enjoyable about the game is largely diminished.

If I could leave a neutral review I would because Mechabellum is still a good game, my major complaints are the lack of honest communication by the devs and how large an impact on how the game feels by making the change (free unit rounds are more important than normal rounds, and there are more credits and deployments in the game meaning more value on the board and the value of credits and deployments have changed without there being any balance changes to account for this). Hopefully, in time, some of my gripes about the current patch will be addressed and I will come back and change my review to positive again and play more. If not, then this won't be the game I liked anymore and I'll play something else, which is fine. Either way I hope this game succeeds whether I play it or not. I had a lot of fun and it was well worth the 15 USD.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been an auto-battler fan for awhile (mostly TFT), and mechabellum really hits the sweet spot for me.

The level of agency with each team build brings an element of reading one's opponent as the primary strategy of the game, if you enjoy strategy and countering maneuvers--give this a go. There is also enough RNG to keep the game spicy, with different loadouts and specialists to change the style of each game played.

The only improvements that I would implement would be better multiplayer, I will lay out each point in detail:

Better lobby control - There is no option to make a friends only lobby, a small detail for sure, but that QOL update would be huge. Additionally, after making a custom game, there is no option to remain with the same group-- I often play with 4 friends in 2v2 custom lobbies and it would be nice to have the option to stick together.

Better bots for custom multiplayer games -- Many times, I will have 2 other friends who want to play, which eliminates 1v1 as an option and one person is saddled with a bot. Having the ability to adjust the difficulty of that bot for fairer matches would be wonderful.


Honestly, those gripes that I had are very small in comparison to everything that they are doing right with the game, and I will be playing this game for a long time for sure--hope to see you on the battlefield!\t\t\t\t\t
