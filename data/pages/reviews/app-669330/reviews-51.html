\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA strategy game of developing an army composition, countering your opponents, and anticipating their reactions. Every round starts with a development phase and a battle phase. You research, upgrade, and place units during the development phase. When both players are ready, the battle phase begins, revealing the units they placed on their side of the battlefield and the fight begins. Winning a round reduces your opponents HP by the number of units you still have remaining on the battlefield.

Fans of the Starcraft 2 Arcade game Desert Strike and Total War will also enjoy the similarities in competitive army composition building, with each unit having a credit cost paired with purchasable upgrades every game to further creativity and strategy.

The game is extremely well balanced with the fact that every unit has a counter, and every counter has a counter. Even if one unit is "OP" it only serves to strengthen its counter, and the constant updates the developers release to hotfix balancing reins in those units anyway.

This isn't some pay to win crap, either. You cannot upgrade units out of game to get a 1% bonus to your unit over someone else's by purchasing booster packs and unlocking units.

ALL UNITS ARE ALREADY UNLOCKED FROM THE START. However, playing and leveling up gives you "tech points" which allow you to purchase different upgrades for units, giving more devastating upgrade options to your units.

Overall Mechabellum is an impressive strategy game with an ocean of depth. I could see it could see it becoming an e-sport in the future, and the in-game tournaments feature recently added is just the start of that.\t\t\t\t\t
