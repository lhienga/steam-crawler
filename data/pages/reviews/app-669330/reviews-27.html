\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an excellent little strategy game. It feels as though it could seamlessly integrate into the Starcraft Universe if Blizzard had acquired it and named it 'Terran Wars.'

You'll definitely sense that some units are overpowered and seemingly unbeatable until you start using them yourself. After a while, you'll get defeated, too. The game is not just about countering units but also involves counters for upgrades, positions, and compositions. People often complain, but with the right combination of these four elements, everything is conquerable.

As of January 2023, big things still missing:

An UNDO BUTTON.
A faster fast-forward option in-game.
Replay rewind.
Join After Disconnect feature (it doesn't happen often, but it's needed).


OVERALL:
The game boasts excellent balance and flow, with usable abilities and upgrades. It's destined to be free-to-play eventually and will make for a great free game. The 'shop' mechanics are currently a bit underdeveloped, which is acceptable. This game offers better netcode and observer options than many major games. It's fantastic if you're into this sort of thing.

Overall, I'd give it a 9/10!

Radical Change Is Needed

Having put a significant time into the game, I want to recommend this game but simply can't. It has potential but has critical flaws.

If things don't change it will be yet another one of those games with great potential, could have been excellent, but never was. Lately, a several in the community have communicated to that they are quitting.

Here are my shot thoughts on why this is:

Pros:

Graphics: great

Strategy: simple but deep

Unit Designs: excellent


No So Great:

Audio: not bad, but not strong either

Micro-transactions: skins for different levels of the same unit when units upgrade levels potentially every round - yeah...

Interface hard to get around with an unnecessary learning curve


Bad:

Dev Direction: It seems excellent at times but long-term, unfortunately somewhat myopic

RNG: cards are too powerful, and often unlucky draws can spell doom and snowball from round 1 - games generally are frustrating and can seem random for players

AI: units always attack the closest unit, even if they are no threat and cannot do damage (i.e. air units attacking crawlers that cannot hurt them while getting torn apart by units they would normally crush). Unfortunately, this decision seems intentional, as some units have settings that allow for "bigger unit priorities."

Micro Placement: placement by a single square can make a huge difference in combat, turning the tide in battle. Position of a unit will often beat out general strategy - learning micro placement is not engaging gameplay.

No Undo: Brought up many times by the community, this is a very basic function and needed - devs have said this was not easy to do. I don't get it - should be easy to do.

Toxic Community: League of Legends Levels. Much of this comes from the AI issues above and new players needing to understand why their units are not doing things that make common sense.

Performance: large battles are a slide show to the point where you cannot see what is going on in the battle.


It has great potential, but if they do not radically overhaul things, it will never reach it potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're like me and trying to get into a new game as a solo player I would not recommend this game. The game mechanics are simple enough basically rock, paper, scissors. What kills this game is the low player count. There's not a ton of "new players" to match up and the ones you do match up with is a 50/50 chance it's someone who's faking their mmr because they want to beat up new players. Also vet players don't want to play with you because they're afraid of losing their mmr because the higher they are the more they have to lose playing with you. Making it hard to find people to play with without random matching with someone creating the issue I mentioned above.

The game is fun in theory and I can go into details about some of the other issues I ran into such as broken metas etc..., but i'm not going to. I'd just take the money and find a different game. unless they fix the low player count also fix the matchmaking issues such as pairing you with someone 3X your combat power or mmr and broken metas then maybe this game can be fun for new players, but for now I wouldn't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
