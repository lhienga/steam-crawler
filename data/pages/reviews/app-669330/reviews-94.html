\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun and surprisingly well-balanced online PvP autobattler. My favorite things about it include:

- There's always possibility for comeback - counters are so well thought out that taking your opponent off-guard with the right card, tech or unit and coming back from behind with good strategy isn't uncommon
- Games are short - usually taking no more than 20-30 minutes
- Allows for the realization of an RTS fantasy, but without the APM barrier required for playing competitive RTSes
- Includes a 2v2 mode, duo queueing with a friend allows for quite a few fun strategies

Despite being in early access, the game feels complete and quite polished. The devs have shown they care about user feedback and update quite frequently. If the genre seems like your thing, give Mechabellum a try!

TL;DR: If you enjoy mecha and competitive RTSes, but are too old to keep your APM up with the kids, this is a good fit for you.

PS my feedback about game being well-balanced maybe should be taken with a grain of salt, as of the time of writing this review I had only about 1400 MMR. But from what I saw so far in my 20-ish games, there didn't seem to be any obvious OP, unfun or non-counterable tactics - or at the very least they're not prevalent.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, this game has a ton going for it.

First: Awesome mechs. If you are a fan of mech games at all, I have no doubt you'll quickly fall in love with watching these mechs stomps and zoom across the battlefield to blow each other to shreds.

Second: The strategic gameplay. It's incredibly fun always predicting what your opponent is going to do next; what mechs might they deploy? Which tactical card did they choose? Which tech are they going to upgrade their mechs with? Are they going to flank me with air units? The list goes on endlessly and you have several ways to deal with the many problems laid out before you.

Third: It's just plain fun. Even if you're NOT into auto battlers, there's an itch to scratch here of deploying an armada of heavily armored units (or not so armored) so tactically that you may completely baffle your opponent.

I love this game. I hope it blows up and becomes extremely popular. The devs deserve it and I want to support this game as it likely becomes a staple in my library for years to come.

BUY IT. IT'S 15 BUCKS.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll play some PvP, but that generally isn't my thing so I bought this because I've liked similar games and I liked the look and premise of this one. I think it is in need of more single player amenities like a campaign of progressive puzzle-like maps with units unlocking as you go and better, more consistent, AI, but I really like the gameplay and overall feel of the game. For those interested in solo play, the practice mode is a selectable-AI skirmish and where I've spent most of my time. The other main single player mode, survival, is high on RnG and can feel pretty unfair and non-fun. The last solo option is the player made challenges and those vary widely as you'd expect, but can be fun. I'm recommending it because it has good units, nice graphics, solid gameplay, and does what it sets out to do. I'd recommend it for PvP players. For solo players, it is probably a no for many, but I feel like I got my money's worth and find my self coming back to try different build strategies so it is a thumbs up from me.

*Update*
So, I got into PvP a bit after all and it is pretty non-toxic and fun compared to other PvP games I've played. I wouldn't really recommend it for single player or offline play, but it is a solid PvP game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember buying this game for my sister a few years back. Didn't think much of it at the time, but I thought it would be a nice gift for her. I remember seeing her playing it from time to time, the message indicating that she'd started playing it popping up on the corner of my screen. There was always a certain small pride in seeing that, because to me it meant that she was enjoying my gift.
Last September she committed suicide.

After everything had more or less settled down from that, I sat down and booted up her computer. All that was on there was Chrome, Steam, Word, and a few games. One of them, of course, was Core keeper.
She had played this game to pieces, clocking over 170 hours and owning practically everything the game had to offer. She had used every square inch to its maximum potential, and was making ridiculous amounts of loot. It was only then did I see just how much she loved my present. No other game she played quite captured her affection as much as this one. None of her friends really knew about it, however; it seems like she preferred to keep it to herself. Like it was her own little bit of heaven she could turn to whenever she wanted.

And because of that, I can't help but smile whenever I play this game.

I cannot recommend it enough; every bit of hard work the developer put into this masterpiece shows in every single aspect. The music is catchy and memorable (I'll often find myself humming certain tunes from it), the world feels lived-in and natural, and the gameplay is incredibly laid back and enjoyable. It's hard to stop once I get invested in the game; minutes can easily turn into several hours. And now, with the multiplayer beta, I play for even longer with my friends, each of us dedicated to our desired jobs. Most of all, however, we're all just happy to be together.\t\t\t\t\t
