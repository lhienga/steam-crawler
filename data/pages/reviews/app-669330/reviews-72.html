\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechabellum has a lot going for it, like the fact that it's a strategy game where you finally don't have to worry about your APM. Or that it's a neat autobattler where you get to see cool mechs fight. Or that there could be a lot of potential for fun and creative builds. However, for now, I still really don't recommend Mechabellum. It has a lot of potential, and it's still in Early Access, but it needs a lot of work in order for the fun parts to shine through.

First, is that the overall balance is extremely bad. This results in some units/upgrades/combos absolutely dominating the game. The Marksman, the Steel Balls, the Arclight, the Overlord, these are just some examples, but honestly it goes far beyond just units as well. At a certain point, every match just boils down to the same handful of cheese combos and who can get them up first. Most of it can't even be fairly countered, and it's all just so boring and infuriating. On top of this, Mechabellum is also vulnerable to competitive players constantly 'theorycrafting' the fun out of the game in search of overpowered strategies for cheap and easy wins... Making balance all the more important. Then there's the PvE survival mode, which could've been very fun, if it wasn't for the AI getting a STUPIDLY unfair advantage over you in hard difficulty and above.

Second, is that I'm personally not a fan of this counter>counter>counter gameplay. I get why counters are a thing, but I don't get why it has to be so intense, where one unit can practically insta-delete another. I would really like to see some more units that don't immediately get blown up by counters.

Lastly, this game suffers from a snowballing/win-more effect. Once a player has a winning strategy going early on, then their opponent is just playing catch-up after that. The winning player will have all the initiative, and their opponent will be stuck constantly trying to counter whatever they build next, with comebacks being rare. Unit leveling, where units can level up and get much stronger after getting some kills, is something that worsens this effect a LOT. All this results in way too many games being a total boring stomp. I really feel like losing players need a boost to be able to counter all this, like nerfed leveling and more supplies for every subsequent lost round or something.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very well done strategy game that has many faceted nuances to it. It takes a bit of getting used to all of the various ways in which you can use, and upgrade units (I'm still learning). Also, upgrades can mitigate counters or otherwise change units to fit other roles than their un-upgraded form does.

Unit positioning is also very key as placing a unit in the correct position can make the difference between winning and losing. Units can't be moved and they will attack the closest unit to them. Thus, it can get frustrating when you want your crowd control unit to target the numerous small, weak units but they end up targeting the big unit since it was the closest.

Having said all of that, unit balancing is very well done. I'm impressed they are able to make each unit have a purpose and can fit a role. They also do balance patches frequently to try to further even things out.

Games are quite quick so it works well for those of us with busy schedules who just want to hop on for a game or two.

Overall, I can't say too many negatives about this game. It can get frustrating when you lose battles and you aren't exactly sure why (this can happen frequently in the beginning). The unit placement issue can also get really frustrating at times; nothing worse than not placing your units exactly correctly and having them attack the units you don't want them to attack (this is a skill that comes with practice). However, if you can overlook those issues and learn to play the game well, it is a well done strategy game.\t\t\t\t\t
