\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recently had an itch for a Supreme Commander style RTS game and had no idea what was out there. I searched Steam for "RTS" and Mechabellum showed up. It looked cool and was released during this year, so I picked it up not knowing a thing about it.

The first hour or so was rough, I was not digging the old man style auto battler system at all but as I started to understand the game I grew to love it.

Games are fast, about 8+/- rounds or 15 mins. At the start of every game you choose from one of four random perks, your opponent does the same but with a different set of random perks. Each perk comes with a base set of 2 units unlocked, during the game you unlock more units with currency that you acquire during the match, both sides get the same amount of currency to work with and currency gets higher and higher with every round.

There are maybe 16 units total in the game to pick from all with 4 special ability unlock slots. You can spec out your units with different abilities if you choose. Abilities are unlocks but you unlock them very fast.

At the end of each round you assess what worked and what didn't and predict how your enemy will counter. Counter with new units, new abilities to their units, unit rank upgrades, or save cash to afford a larger unit next round, etc.

I like this game because games are short and I don't get my panties in a twist if I lose. In Supreme Commander I would always play turtle matches where you have 30 mins to build and once you get into a fight it's probably 60 mins into the game and sometimes you die instantly or games never end. With the variety of unlocks and random perks, every game of Mechabellum is different and I suppose that's what holds my interest with this game.

This is a great RTS game with quick games that are enjoyable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest auto battler I've ever played across any platform. Not too simple, but not too hard. A small warning for anyone who really gets bugged by seeing their rating go down since there is not an unranked queue. There is a small amount of meta progression in the form of unlocking alternate upgrades for your units, but if you like the game you will eventually unlock them all in no time. Zero Pay-to-win aspects. There is enough unit and tech variety to not constantly get creamed by the same copy-paste army from different players.
At the current $15 dollar price point its probably best you look up some recent youtube videos on the game if you're unsure you'd like playing an auto battler. If you do like auto battlers and/or like watching tons of units clash on a battlefield then I suggest you buy this game right now. There are other gamemodes than just the classic 1v1 pvp match. There are also rudimentary bots of different difficulties to play as well albeit they are a bit dumb and basic. Also comes with a sandbox for either testing units on your own terms, or if you just wanna mess around and make huge/goofy battles.
I've had zero technical issues on the hardware side of things, and the relatively rare bug with how units work in-game.
If your pinching pennies then slap this game in your wishlist as for they have plans to release this game for free. If it's free at the time of you reading this then stop reading the dang reviews and try it out.
10/10 relaxing strategic fun
Edit: Currently put at not recommended. The original main gamemode of mechabellum has recently gone through a semi-drastic change. Atm the original mode will now be confined to custom lobbies ONLY. While I do still feel the base game is fun, I no longer find it fun enough to recommend at current game price and unstable nature of game mechanics. Will update recommendation status once things become more concrete.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoesn't quite scratch the itch what Underlords left before Valve quit support but quite enjoyable auto battler and 1vs1 queue is fast, at least at saturday and for new player. I would recommend for pals who enjoy auto battlers and it got occasionally same feel than supreme commanders although this is not rts.

For some reason otherwise good tutorial didn't explain that you can buy buffs from base buildings.

I like a feel of game a lot, big bots goes boom! Nuke goes BOOOOM!. Crawlers going by crawling, loving it. But meanwhile I find games quite repetitive and combat feels, at least after few games, very paper scissors rock. I think I would love to see way more unit synergy, way more randomess and more diverse and more gimmicky units.

I am by no means game developer, this is early access (what isn't nowadays?) and many things are great in this game, but I would love to see more. Gold saving bonuses? I think it would be cool to see if random upgrades, not by getting kills, would be funnier. Maybe shared roulette to get upgraded units like tft? Combining units to upgrade and get room? Seasons where some units are locked of? Unit which only charge forward? Unit like nydus from SC2? Stealth system? Units skills should be probably something else but more attack/range, those feel unrewarding.

Definitely should be steam achievements.

Buy it, it is fun.\t\t\t\t\t
