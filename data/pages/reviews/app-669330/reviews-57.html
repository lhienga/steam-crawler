\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly one of my favorite games. Enough big overall macro play combined with the small micro play that changes everything. Sometimes knowing that because you put a unit one tile too far back which caused you to lose an interaction you would've won putting it one more forward can be frustrating, but a learning experience as you learn the different interactions between the units, and even between the units on your own side of the field to attempt to protect your win conditions.

THE MECHS

The design, feel, and gameplay of the mechs feel amazing. Every mech was created for a specific purpose with a specific Rock Paper Scissors formula to every other mech in the game, but when you combine all of these at once it turns much more intense, all of a sudden your Artillery that was countering the tanks and other things aren't doing their job as they fire useless volleys into the ground missing their targets. Those snipers your had for the big units are suddenly massive overkilling a small robot allowing those big robots to pick off the rest of your army. There is so much going on with these mechs that feels different from any other game I've played. And the ability to buff or change certain normal interactions through the 4 techs you choose to put on before going into battle makes every game feel different. This has been the only autobattler I've ever enjoyed because it feels more like a Halo Wars game than an autobattler.

THE MENTAL

I won't lie in saying that this game has taken a big toll on my mental well being. No game has ever made me feel more stupid and even lost when I don't understand WHY I've lost, or more, what I could've done to prevent it. But when you play this game with friends? It's a whole different game, I've got a nice Guild we've created outside of the game where we do round robin 2v2's so everyone is matches against the other person and playing with the other people. This has been genuinely some of the most fun I've had in a game trying out stupid meme builds, Such as full spawning with vulcan, marks, fangs, and then maxing out your fangs and marks tech. Is it super viable? Not really, takes way too long for not enough pay off, is it funny? Extremely, seeing an almost empty looking field turn full in the first second of battle is always funny to look at, especially if you waited to buy those techs much later and spawning an army out of nowhere.
I would highly recommend getting a group of friends to play this game with to help with the mental doom that playing 1v1's can cause whenever you are on a losing streak.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit Feb 2024: There have been a lot of negative reviews of the game recently. This is because of the recent "chaotic fun" update which gives players powerful free units in the middle of the game. What draws many people to Mechabellum is the way it rewards skill and minimizes the effects of luck -- it is very difficult to beat an opponent who is even just a little better than you. Contrary to what people think, the update does not change this. The free units add unpredictability to the game as you can no longer plan out your entire game from turn 1. However, since both players are offered the same free unit choices, the free units are fair and the winner is still determined by skill and not luck. If anything, tryhard players should be happy about this because it adds another dimension to the decision making which makes the game harder to master. Players must now adapt and think on their feet, which requires a lot of experience and skill. The people who are upset about the update are the people who prefer predictability and enjoy planning out their composition ahead of time. I don't think there is anything wrong with this play style, but I feel like the update is healthy for the game as it introduces more variability and people don't just play the same stuff over and over.

Original review: I'm a big fan of autobattlers and this is hands down the best autobattler on the market right now. When I first heard about this game I wasn't keen on the mech theme, but somehow it managed to hook me right from the start. Even before you know what you're doing it's stupid fun watching everything explode. Then you start to learn the strategy and it's so deep you can never get to the bottom. 187 hours in I am still playing with basically the same set of content (only 1 unit has been added) and it still feels like I am learning something new every match.\t\t\t\t\t

Great game for strategy lovers like me. You need to learn how the units react to each other, learn the "meta" and adapt on the fly to the RNG of the bonuses the game offfers each turn and to what your opponent is doing. The battles really feel epic with missiles and lasers flying, giant robots clashing with hordes of smaller units. The design of the units is really nice and the sound is great too. There is nothing to criticize here. I hope the game is a commercial succes and that they will transition free to play successfully without making the game less good.\t\t\t\t\t
