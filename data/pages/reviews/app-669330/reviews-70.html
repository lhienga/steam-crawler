\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the learning curve is a bit steep at first, this little gem has grown to be one of my favorite games.

Rarely have I ever come across a game that can simultaneously make me feel like an unparalleled strategic genius and as dumb as a rock in the same play session.

This autobattler is all about positioning and countering. Every unit has an effective counter in the form of another unit. Like a 16 way game of rock paper scissors. However, even if you counter your opponents moves perfectly, if you position the units poorly, you will be doomed to failure.

The game is still in development and the developer intends to release it as a free to play title when it leaves early access; however, for $15 I definitely feel like I've gotten my money's worth.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn auto battler focusing on squads of units in ~15 minute battles in 1vs1 or 2vs2. Mechabellum shakes up the genre by unlocking and buying from a pool of units versus the usual of picking a unit from a random assortment you don't have control over. Units can be upgraded if they get enough damage off to increase their health and damage themselves and upgraded themselves from the same money your buying new units from.

Limited resources and a rock-paper-scissors nature of the units means positioning and what unit runs into who largely defines battles and having combinations of unit backing up others creates a large amount of depth.

Game already feels feature complete to me even if the devs are talking about adding things like more units to the game. Tutorial goes through the basics very well and the bots do a great job of getting you on your feet.

The game is actively being developed and changed from the devs and if you already like the genre and the gameplay looks attractive to you it will be well worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game because I like mechs in general and autobattlers to a degree. After playing for some time, this game is just frustrating. Major balance changes invalidate strategies between patches and the "meta" switches to 2-3 builds that dominate every match due to poor unit balance. There are however, 2 major problems with the game beyond that - RNG and snowballing.

RNG - how the game works is you pick between 4 cards at the beginning and between every round. The 4 cards at the beginning are the hero and 2 units you start with - you do not get the same choices your opponent does. This means they can get options that are strictly better than yours and basically just win the game right at the beginning. The other problem is with the 4 cards - you get a choice between 4 every round and you and your opponent get the same card choices. The cards have different powers, equipable items, or upgrades. Based on what you and your opponent are building, some cards can decide the game as soon as they appear. If you draw giant hunter (50 credits every time you kill a giant, 100 cost) and your opponent has 4 giants while you have 0, enjoy being able to spend 100-200 more credits than they can every round after that. You can only sell a maximum of 1 unit per round (using a technology that costs 100 credits to unlock) so it isn't like they can rapidly adapt to you getting that. I get the reason behind limiting how much you can change your army setup, but it leads to a ton of relatively even games being decided by an rng mechanic.

Snowballing - this is when you or your opponent wins the first few rounds and becomes strong enough to where you can't counter them. Honestly most games I play are decided like this. They get a better hero / unit pick at the beginning and then they upgrade their units and you can never come back. There is no good way to reliably come back if you are evenly matched because upgrading a unit (which costs half of the initial unit cost) doubles their health and damage. This leads to whoever is winning early to pull further ahead (since their units get more kills -> more xp -> more upgrades) and any "counter" you think of can get countered the next round by them. In other autobattlers you face a random opponent each round so there is a chance for you to catch a break, in this one you face the exact same person the whole match.

All together, this leads to a very frustrating playing experience. I would say probably 75% of my games are decided by whoever gets the better unit/hero picks right in the beginning, 20% are decided by some lucky card draw, and only the remaining 5% are due to superior strategy.

Final edit:
I've finally uninstalled the game. I kept playing hoping it could be a good "competitive" outlet but was just constantly disappointed. The matchmaking is bad so you get matched with people +-200 MMR (or worse), patches are slow to come out, terrible balance so you face the same 3 strategies every game, and you get horribly punished for trying anything outside the small meta since skill in this game is 90% knowing what is meta and what counters what.

The first couple dozen hours are decently fun since you (hopefully) get matched with newer people and can try out new stuff but then it all converges and just gets extremely repetitive. The "cool factor" of mech battles wears off pretty quickly with all the flaws. For a non-free competitive game, the "staying power" is too poor - just look at the player count over time. I definitely do not recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI shall be listing the Pros and Cons separately.

PROS:
1) This game is not energy intensive. Since moving out of my parents' house I have been coming home from work exceptionally drained from the new job both mentally and physically, So I haven't really been playing a lot of games. I've had the energy to write my book(s)(link in bio check it out haha), but actually playing competitive games hasn't been a big thing for me. But The past twoish weeks have had some of my worst shifts at work and almost every day I've come home and played the 2v2 with one of my good friends.

2) This is the most strategic auto battler yet)that I am aware of). You have a good unit variety, each of these units has tech upgrades that can change how they work or just be an overall boost, you have two layers in the air and the ground, they could theoretically add moats/rivers and stuff in the future on more advanced maps with more units then, you have a card system between each round that can impact your strategy either a little bit or make you do a full 180 on your current strat, you have battlefield skills which IMHO shouldn't be in the game but they are another level of strategy nonetheless, and probably most importantly you can flank your opponents which can result in three separate fronts.

3) Because of my previous points in #2 this game also has insane levels of replayability. You run into unique strategies every now and then, while not simultaneously seeing the same "meta" strategy every game.

4) There is a 1v1, a 2v2, and they are planning to make a 4-person FFA. The 1v1 is standard and fine. The 2v2 has three distinct types of maps with my personal favorite being the compact map where your opponent can't flank you and your buddy in between each other. The difference between the 1v1 and 2v2 modes is immense and personally, I love 2v2 significantly more because of all the more strategies you can do.

5) there is really good balancing in this game. An example is the Stormcaller unit I believe they were overpowered and are still really strong. Why did they get a decrease from Op to just stronk? because they are a GROUP of artillery that even out-range snipers to start off with and deal considerable damage with a good AOE. To combat this rather than simply nerfing them the devs added a deployable that shoots down their missiles similarly to how another tech allows the Mustang unit to shoot them down. I love this type of balancing because that same unit can shoot down other types of missiles and isn't just good in this one situation.

CONS:
1) When I first started playing this game there was no MMR, just combat power which honestly always went up because you didn't lose enough per loss. But since they have added MMR for a better matchmaking system. I. Do. Not. Want. To. See. My. MMR. This is an auto battler that I would like to chill out on and enjoy myself without genuinely caring if I win or lose. However, there is no option to simply "hide" your MMR. I actually quite like the fact that they have a good matchmaking system. But I do not like the sense of loss I get when my MMR drops, even though I have been making an active effort to simply not care. I am not alone on this either, I have seen many people go into the discord asking for it and it honestly sounds like an easy addition. But the devs still have not added this.

2) Secondly, even though I make the points in #2 and #3 under PROS, this game has some amazing overall balancing but there is more than one tech which is more than a little busted. There is this unit in the game called the arclight. This little guy is the worst unit in the game, BUT if you decide to invest a few thousand supplies into tech upgrades and level upgrades(as they get more xp) this guy turns into an absolute unit that is super fun to have. Its hard to get this 'carry arclight' strategy going but it is super fun to pull off. Meanwhile, there are other units such as Marsmen that are good to start with and additionally can get upgrades that make them honest to god Overpowered. The marksmen have a really good range and deal a lot of damage from the backline. But they can also get EMP on their shots which slows AND cancels out the tech on enemy units hit, as well as the Elite Marksmen which is what makes a unit a "carry"(just like with the arclights) by giving more range and damage per level, on other units its just a good upgrade but on Marksmen it is just freaking busted man. Then you have other units such as the steel ball, this guy does its job really well almost OP even. But then it is EXCEPTIONALLY bad against stuff it is bad at. However, their OP upgrade is Energy absorption which lets them steal health from their beams. That part isn't OP what is Op is the fact that it also boosts their health by SIXTY percent. Granted this can be shut down by any of the five units that have the emp shot upgrade. Then there are a few more but I'll stop myself because I've already been rambling.

3)Notice in CON#2 how I said it takes a few grand in supplies to get going and is hard even then to get it started? The community whines like crazy about how carry arclight is busted but then will sit there and dismiss you that carry marksmen is busted. The clear difference is the fact that you need all four upgrades to get carry arclight going while only two to get carry marksmen going. The other issue is the fact that carry marksmen work with almost any type of front line to sit behind while arclight themselves are usually the frontline because of how much you have to invest in them to really get them going. But then I've also seen a marksman one-shot giant units while Arclights still struggle. But I'll digress on that one. BASICALLY what I'm trying to say is that the community is full of just biased idiots(nicest word to think of) that will defend their beloved OP strategy to no end while other just good strategies get dismissed like crazy.

4) in PRO#2 I said it was theoretically possible to add a naval layer of strategy to the battlefield. Yeah, that will NEVER happen at this rate because the ai is DESIGNED to be the dumbest piece of trash imaginable. Its designed so that all units will go to attack the nearest enemy and lock on until that enemy is dead. This means that so many brain-dead situations arise in the game. I've had carry arclights lock onto a shield while the enemy units come outside of said shield and kill my arclight while said arclight is just laser-focused on the shield. Remember this is how the AI is DESIGNED. I have had a marksman focus on a swarm of little guys while the air unit was neck and neck with the little swarm on the ground. Behind the marksmen, I had plenty of guys that could take care of the swarm but I needed that marksman to kill the air unit. I've also had numerous occasions where my units are locked on a unit that started at the same distance as another enemy unit. But the one that was locked on wasn't attacking my units while the ones that weren't locked on had free reign to kill my units. Remember this is how the AI is designed and you will be dismissed when you inevitably get tilted and bring it up in the discord.

Honestly, I have about three more points I could list but I'm hungry so I'm gonna go grab a bite to eat as well as not knowing if there is a character cap for reviews

Overall I LOVE the game but the cons are what make me extremely hesitant to recommend the game to newer players\t\t\t\t\t

No micro means you don't need high APM, making it a game of quick decision making rather than quick reflexes. Games proceed at a solid pace, you can generally finish one in 10-20 minutes unless they start running crazy long. If someone is stomping the other person the game mechanics are set up to make that match shorter, while if it's a close match it's more likely to run longer, which tends to mean you spend more time playing against opponents of your skill level, and games tend not to feel "won" for long: close games generally feel like you or your opponent is one turn away from adding an ingredient to their combined arms soup that swings the tide in their favor, while, as mentioned above, if you're winning hard you just win and move onto the next one (or vice versa.)

Good stuff.\t\t\t\t\t

- Nice and clean visuals
- In-game tournament system
- Has both 1v1 and 2v2 modes

Negatives
