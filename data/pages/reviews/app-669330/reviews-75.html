\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey took Supreme Commander and made it into an autobattler!

Mechabellum is leaning much more into strategy and tactics than more traditional autobattlers. At the start of each turn new emblems and items are offered to the player for purchase, although the selection is random they are offered equally to both. The only uneven offer is the initial choice of commander/units, which is good because it encourages players to go into different playstyles. There is also only 1 opponent, so generic good unit placement is gone, you always have to take your opponent into account, which is rare in autobattlers. All unit types are available for purchase at all times (if you have the cash) and the units level up via battle rather than gamba. So you can't randomly get destroyed by a random three star unit, in this game I have seen nine star units and they grow because they are fed and that's someone's fault. It's a game of constantly starting and putting out fires, turnbased.

If you dislike the rng/gamba element in autobattlers this is the game, if you think autobattler is a dirty word this game might change your mind. I do hope Mechabellum takes off, it's going to be free to play at release but the developers has earned my money.\t\t\t\t\t
