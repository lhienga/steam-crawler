I'm a long time player of RTS games but never really stick with any single game for too long because multiplayer often seems inaccessible without investing huge amounts of time in getting good. Committing hotkeys to muscle memory and doing hundreds of actions per minute? I don't have time for that.

Mechabellum removes that aspect so that what remains is a battle not of who is quickest but more of a pure battle of strategy and out thinking your opponent. This game can be very chess-like (seriously). You will still be rushing to get everything done between turns but Mechabellum slows down the pace to allow you to think strategically and plot your next move.

There are some great creators including RAT and Cobrak creating videos to help you learn the meta and the multiplayer is an absolute blast. Games swing wildly between victory and defeat and there are many intense moments as you drop down to your last bit of health then come back and steamroller your opponent.

I usually don't care much for games that completely leave out any storytelling or characters. Mechabellum has none of this. But it's a rare case of a game that's managed to keep me coming back week after week because of how fun the combat is.

Great game and hope we can see more things to come from it as tweaks continue to be made.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is, in essence, making decisions about which units to place where and what upgrades to get and when to deploy abilities, with the goal being to counter the units of your opponent(s) who are doing the same. Combat is all simulated automatically so you can use the time to analyse how the fight goes and what you can do to turn the tide in the next round. All the units have strengths and weaknesses. There's a lot of possible variety due to the way random upgrades are presented and the combinations of enhancements you can equip to your different unit types.

It might seem a little simplistic, but it's quite addictive!\t\t\t\t\t
