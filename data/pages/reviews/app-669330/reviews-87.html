\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort review: Great game that is cheap, is going to be made F2P, and is a very fun casual auto-battler that fixed some of my biggest annoyances with other auto-battlers, being RNG and an astronomically high skill floor, however, probably would not recommend for hardcore auto-chess players since the rate of content will probably not be as fast as something like TFT or Dota's auto-chess (if that's still a thing, never played it)

It's been quite interesting comparing this to another auto-chess game I used to play a lot, being TeamFight Tactics from Riot, as this game has fixed some major issues I had with that game, being RNG, having to constantly swap up my win condition, and it's massive skill floor or barrier to entry. Since aside from the initial selection of units, you and your opponent have the exact same options each and every round, so it feels a lot more enjoyable personally knowing the options I have are the exact same my opponent does, it removes a lot of the RNG out of things like getting enough copies of a character or the items you need, making the whole process a lot simpler and more streamlined. While your gameplan can and will shift depending on what your opponent does, it feels a lot more in your control in general, knowing what units your opponent had unlocked, their specialists and the cards they can pick in that round, and I enjoy that personally quite a bit.

The barrier to entry is also a lot lower, since the items and specialists are quite simple, mostly being powers, unit items you just put on, or static upgrades to your army or a specific unit, meaning I don't have to spend nearly as much time learning and dealing with the information overload, which I constantly get whenever my friends get me to try and play TFT, with constantly rotating sets, items, item build paths, it's just too much for someone who doesn't care to play the game enough to learn it all, only for the set to change and I have to do it all again, this game is far simpler in that aspect and I love it. Pick it up and play the same day, learn for yourself. In addition, only having to focus on one opponent I find helps me personally as a casual player, not having to worry about what 7 other people are doing every game

They also hold in-game tournaments, which is cool as hell, and I love their wave defence PVE mode, been spamming that a ton too, even if the fang and melting point spam make me want to die :)

Not going to really touch on balance, because they do make constant changes both based on high and low mmr games, read their patch notes to see further details and I like that they balance for both high and low skill matches, helping the casual and competitive players

However, with all that being said, the rate of content is not super fast, and I can imagine it would get old if you played it day after day, so might not be the game for hardcore players, but for casuals? 100% would recommend, good recommendation Bricky\t\t\t\t\t
