\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiant Robots and player v player battles that are more about having a plan, and reacting to the other guys plan, than about how fast you can click.


You place down giant robots in a series of rounds, while the opponent also places his robots. There are also some special things ("spells" type things) that can happen, but both sides are offered to choose from the same list of specials so you can have some idea of what your opponent might hit you with.

THEN, the computer controls the robots and they battle, until 1 side is eliminated. The loser takes damage to his "hit points" based on how many of the winner's robots are left.

THEN, the new round will start, BUT all of the old robots from previous rounds are back alive, in their original places. You cannot move the old robots, (*unless something special) so those are stuck in your original position, meaning that WHERE you place them is a part of the strategy.

There is a timer for the rounds, so you know the game will move at a timely place, but its not really about being fast. This is about trying to have a plan to beat your opponent, maybe by suprising him with something special, maybe by hitting him on a flank where he has few or no robots. or maybe by anticipating what he might try to do to you, and planning a trap.

It's suprisingly fun. I like mechwarrior type games, and sometimes I just spectate on someone else's battles, for fun, or for ideas. and playing a round is only about a 10-20 minute commitment.

Totally worth trying. or look at a "lets play" video or something.

But, you should know that the devs eventually will make the game free-to-play, and have cosmetic monetization. so, I dunno, if the game's cost is a lot for your personal finances, maybe just try and watch people play and wait for it to be free.

Anyways, I think its totally worth a try! Have fun!!!!

They made 3 different types of maps, and a survival mode, and all the devs had to do was make it a shared battlefield, where you and your teammate can fight in the same lane, not just two maps slapped next to each other. It just doesn't make any sense to me why they would do that.

Their FAQ states they aren't planning on fixing this right now and it sucks.

Other than that, great unit selection. the battles are fun to watch, the counters are interesting and there is plenty of satisfying strategy to consider, but this being strictly a 1v1 game makes it lose a lot of long term interest for me. especially when it could be a solid 2v2 game with so little effort.
