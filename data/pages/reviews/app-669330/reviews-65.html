\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good game until you spend more than a dozen hours on it. You quickly realize the RNG component of the game(The card selection between rounds) is so swingy with so much weight behind it that most strategies fall completely flat. As of august 2023 I can't recommend the game because while the fun mechanics and gameplay is there, the balance most definitely isn't.

A lot of people praise the complexity/nuance of Mechabellum but as of this writing every strategy can be boiled down to 4: Elite marksman carry(marksman/arclight), Steel Ball+Mustang, Rhino+Mobile Beacon, and Fang+Fortress. Every composition is either an offshoot of those 4 or gets absolutely crushed by them.

For the devs: Arclights shouldn't get anti-air tech, Mobile Beacon needs to be reworked(either higher unit priority so its actually possible to intercept or reduce the range), stormcallers in their current iteration are terrible design and rather than reworked they should just be removed, and cards should be a bit less impactful(currently they can be game deciding and I'd argue any RNG that can outright win in a player vs player game is inherently unhealthy for that game).\t\t\t\t\t
