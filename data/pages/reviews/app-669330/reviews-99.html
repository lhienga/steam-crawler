\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEver wanted to command an army of mechs, but have the micro skills of an elder with arthritis?
Try mechabellum, as it does the micro of playing the game for you, while you can sit and watch the glorious spectacle from afar. Your role is not to shoot the enemy, but to arm your forces adequately and intelligently to counter your enemy.

It's a game of chess... except you have massive ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ robots armed with more guns and explosives than your average american household, and you have the destructive power of the gods themselves in the form of ballistic air strikes.

The satisfaction of outsmarting your opponent and seeing their forces crumble like a dry leaf is immense. However, it does have its problems, mainly in the form of progression and map variation; the map is very small and health pools are super low, which means that most matches end before you can truly start seeing battles of epic scale, and the progression lacks purpose at the moment as it feels barebones and uninteresting.

Overall this game is very good, it has a very solid gameplay loop and foundation. At the asking price its a damn steal.
A solid 8/10!
