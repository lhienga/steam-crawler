\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCaution VERY ADDICTING!
Absolutely incredible game! Think Starcraft without all the micro bs and no worrying about building or gathering resources. Strictly unit placement and countering your opponent's units between several rounds until there's a winner. I play with my buddy and unlike other 1v1 games that can become tedious this one has EXTREMELY different games each and every time because of the random start up units, the player power, and the different powers to choose from each round. It's still in early access but the ONLY thing I think that would make this game better is to add different races. Again, comparing it to Starcraft, it feels like you're playing with only Terran or "mech" units at this time. It'd be INCREDIBLE to have alien races too like a biological zerg, or futuristic protoss race or something.
After 445 hours the game NEEDS additional units or races to be added a lot more quickly than one new unit every month or two. I'm BEGGING the developers for multiple races. That would make this game perfect but overall still a fun game.

Don't listen to the babies crying about getting random units once every few rounds. Makes the game a lot more intense and if you're good it doesn't affect anything for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechabellum is a turn-based auto-battler which have become rather popular as mods for existing game. This, however, is a wholly unique entry without any preexisting characters or franchise.

The Good:
+Very strong emphasis on countering units in a rock, paper, or scissors style combat.
+Lots of different tricks, tactics, and even a bit of customization of units.
+Matches are fairly short and can get very close.
+Presentation is generally good with some very nice explosions and particles.

The Bad:
-The game is still being hotfixed and balanced often, which it needs as some units get overtuned.
-You can cheese the game with meta-defining strats. Especially at higher tiers of play.
-A few to many zap-guns and pews-pews in the sound department. I think the guns need to be bassier.
-Very few gamemodes.
-The music is average, but there's not nearly enough tracks.

Overall:
Mechabellum is a lot of fun. I find it's best to relax with a couple of matches and then leaving it for the day, even if it is very tempting to go for just "one more match". If you like the autto-battle genre, you'll probably like this. However, if you get bored games with a limited roster quickly, save your money.\t\t\t\t\t
