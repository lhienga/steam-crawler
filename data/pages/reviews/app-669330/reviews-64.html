\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI stopped playing this for a bit then went back and realised better the issues with it. The game itself looks and plays great, its simple and effective which means you can jump on and grab a game and that part of it is fantastic.

The issue is that the randomisation and sheer complexity of counters and counter counters means that you will lose games and it will have nothing to do with your skill. Players will get a step advantage from the first round cards or from a counter play and then its a question of whether you saved your random perk or made the wrong choice and run out of rounds to counter.

This gets worse in the later rounds when the snowball effect takes hold and skill goes out of the game vs sheer upgrades which dominates any strategy at that point. Early spam can carry through and win a game.

You'll still win 60% of games and like a fruit machine when it wins its good and when it loses its bad - you just keep pushing the button and you feel useless at times with no way to rapidly counter a position due to the limitations on what you can change each round.

The balance is probably the cause here allowing unit spam plays to dominate other counter units once they are upgraded which is a real shame as the frustration out weights the enjoyment overall as even when you win you feel its not always down to skill\t\t\t\t\t
