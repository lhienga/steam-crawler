\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike much of the community, I generally saw this as a game with a great base and nice mechanics making small steps in the right direction with balance and updates. I'm a veteran of strategy games and have played a lot of them over the years, and this one had the foundations to be another great one.

I didn't like the chaotic "fun" patch in theory, and when I actually played it, I disliked it even more. It's not a game I want to play any longer. I was a fan of almost every update before this since I started playing, including new items, balance changes, and new units, but the chaotic "fun" patch took away the strategic elements I liked most about the game, and turned it from a game I thought about even when not playing, to a generic mediocre strategy game I don't even consider opening.

Before, it was a game of managing your position in the game. Are you trying to do as much damage this turn and push for an early victory or minimize damage and play the long game? Can you get your setup online before you lose? Can you shut down your opponent's tower rush while building a solid army of your own? These storylines played out over multiple rounds.

Now, the early rounds barely matter, and games are largely decided by the unit drop round which can change up the whole look of a board. Instead of balancing putting together your strategy against countering your opponent, a single round can change more than half the battlefield, and basically nullifies anything that happened before. It feels like Quiddich from Harry Potter. Sure, getting 10 points at a time is nice, but whoever gets the Snitch wins the game. In chaotic "fun" patch Mechabellum, whoever plays better on the 2nd unit drop round usually wins. Both wins and losses feel much worse.

I'm not against change, but if this is the direction of the change then it's not a game I'm going to play any more, and not one I can recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
