\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR:
I can't condone micro transactions (MT) while "still" in EA, fair warning, they are only Cosmetic, so, to each persons tolerance on that.
I personally enjoyed the system prior to the micro transaction change where you could earn currency through level ups (which seem unlimited) and through an auto-miner that would auto fill slowly but encourage you to sign in every 2-3 days as that's as much as it would hold before it would stop "mining" credits. (This system still exists! They are just making mech cosmetics that are only purchasable with real money as of the writing of this review and now I'm unsure what is next)
But the major issue, in my mind, is how the early addition of MT speaks to what to expect in the future of the games development.


TLDR (not including Micro Transactions):
I had a good time, it was well worth the money, there are issues encountered later on in play but early play is pretty manageable. If the game gets mass appeal I could easily see the meta ruining the game though since balance is a continual struggle, they are amazingly quick to work on, but have not mastered yet (it's EA).


GAME PLAY:
A pretty solid auto-battler, there are some confusing balance decisions and their most recent units/unit upgrades have been pretty swingy, but that I feel is to be expected and they are pretty quick on balancing it out.
There are some interesting unit compositions that can be created, but there is a meta that people stick to once it's found which can be really disappointing and you can only break out of it with luck of the cards that both players get to chose each round. (Example: a player can be trouncing your board by getting a good melting point and Vulcan at lvl 3 or something and then the cards give you auto lvl 3 steels balls or something and it overturns their strategy if they don't also take it or try to counter with an effective strategy.)

There was once a time where people got the same starting card choices which ended up creating more balanced matches, but now both players get different starting randomised hands but each card choice per round is the same for both, so this can create some more "interesting" fights, but one sided depending if RNG is not on someones side. There is a reason why the previous system created similar match-ups, there was and still is a "right" choice to each card unless you've invested into a specific strategy.
I personally still have a good time experimenting with new combinations, but there are some mechs that just collect dust (even when I experiment) now since some are not up to the power scale, this applies to both new and old mechs. And some upgrades are actually so situational they are detrimental in any other composition, so you have to think about unit upgrades to take into each match (each unit (except for one) can only take 4 of their upgrades per match)

Auto-pathing can be confusing/frustrating, but I think that's an Auto-Battler quirk.
