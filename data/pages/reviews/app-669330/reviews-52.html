\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFellow single-player gamers, this title probably isn't for us despite being very cool.

Calling it an auto-battler is borderline dismissive of the depth of strategy involved here. It's more like a game of Magic the Gathering or Warhammer that plays out over multiple rounds. No, you don't control the troops directly, but you never ever feel like you should be. It just feels right, which is a lot coming from me - I normally dislike turn-based strategy games and I actually like this game.

Still, I can tell I'm going to get about 10 hours into it and stop playing, because the focus is on the multiplayer aspect and I do not, under any circumstances, play multiplayer games. I really would like to see more long-term options for us single player people. Something like a roguelike campaign mode would be good - there's the glimmer of concepts LIKE that right now, but they're not coherent and it's random if your run will be playable beyond the first two rounds. I'd be willing to pay 5 or 10 bucks for a DLC to have a robust single player option.

The game also has some visual readability issues. It's extremely difficult to figure out what is happening in the actual battle, because there's no visual separation between you and the enemy. I would like much stronger visual identification of which units belong to who, so it's easier to tell what did and did not go well as it's happening. Especially as a new player, it's an absolute firehose of information to try and take in, and the visual confusion makes it that much harder.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn General it is a great game, very nice auto battler and does have some depth, however it is running into balance issues with units that is sucking the life out of the game. Patches are to far apart for how much they are willing to do in them so for an entire patch cycle or more you will end up playing against the same control paste armies. Since there is also no choice to play a casual or ranked modes be ready for a swarm of try hard using that exact same build on repeat. So much so it is not uncommon for 3 of my 5 games i usually play to be against the exact same builds, just with slight differences.

I like this game but this has been a problem since it has opened to the public, whether it be mass rhino, ball stang, fang fortress or the current SC spam. there needs to be some way to either choose to not play against the people who are just trying to win at the cost of the enjoyment of the game such as a "Classic" or unranked mode, or more attempts to balance the spam ability and make strategies more based on the cards so that someone cannot force strategies regardless of choices, therefore opening up more variety in the game.

For now i am still hoping for changes but one or two more patch cycles and these issues are not addressed this game will sit on the dusty shelf of all the other steam games that could not handle their core issues.\t\t\t\t\t
