You will get to learn different strategies from other players and try to adapt them, only to find that the counter you were missing on the person who just trounced you, is now in the hand of the person you are fighting.

Good stuff!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechabellum is a combination of an auto battle game and a turn based strategy game. You can choose what units and where they are, but not where they go or what they do. There is a wide variety of units and upgrades, you also have cards that can change a unit or grant an ability. It's an fragile web of what combats what, for example, a Vulcan is meant to fight swarms, but a large enough swarm can still kill it, another example, scorpions are an anti swarm unit that can two shot some of the most durable units in the game once they get a few upgrades. Mechabellum is a strategy game that has a lot of room for you to mess around and just have fun. Progression may be slow and cosmetics may be hard to get but at the time of writing this Mechabellum is on version 0.7.33 so that may not reflect on the complete product. Mechabellum is both unique and entertaining, it's worth playing if your a fan of any sort of strategy game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTerrible UI. Between the menus, the in-game interface in multiplayer/observer, and the unit modifications, you're going to be missing information and get burned by it. The UI itself is also uninspired, making the bland interface of starfield look good by comparison.

Poor monetization, to the point that I'd trust a mobile game's conversion rate over what this offers. Instead of making interesting reskins for a single unit, you must purchase one third of one unit's skins, and it's only made available at random since the shop only allows for a couple slots at a time. For whatever reason, they decided to monetize the ability to predict who wins. Furthermore, the skins shown so far have been "this guy red instead of orange" or "this guy have yellow tertiary color instead of grey." And they're asking for 5-10 bucks for each.

Aggravating gameplay. For an autobattler, some of the strongest units are the RNG . Oh boy, I sure love gameplay that has extreme variance based on what the number generator decided was going to get hit or receive target focus.

Nonexistent balance. Because each round provides random cards from a huge list, sometimes you just get because the game hands your opponent the perfect answer to what you've dedicated to, or reinforces their playstyle while you're left high and dry. The beginning operators have their health randomly rolled, the operator options randomly rolled, the starting units randomly rolled, and your opponent receives different choices.

MMR system is completely whacked. You get 20 for an equal-level player, 0-5 for someone 200 MMR below, and hundreds for someone 200 MMR or above. Because the playerbase is tiny, it's not unusual to get put against people way above you. I'm talking 500-1000 MMR above you. Some jackass on that 1000+ MMR above me decided to surrender when he was clearly winning, which shoved me way above where my MMR should've been, so now I get even more people above what I should be facing and I mostly lose while not getting back down to my level. I recommend new players IMMEDIATELY CONCEDE if they're placed against someone higher than them, not because the match is lopsided in their favor, but to preserve what little fun this game has to offer by keeping your MMR growth natural.\t\t\t\t\t
