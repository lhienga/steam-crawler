There are certain good combinations of units. But there is usually a way and a path forward to win.

I at the end of a day, play games to de-stress and have fun. With the current update with MMR of opponents, its is basically like playing ranked in any other game (stressful). Every match you see your MMR as well as your opponents, however the disparity between mmr of myself and opponents is often 400+ given that there are no more than 1000 active players at once in this game. and 95% of the time i get matched with an opponent with severely less MMR then me. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: If you love the part of RTS games about where you're trying to constantly counter the opponents units you'll love this too. It's highly addictive and you get into games very quickly.

I don't care much for autobattlers. They are usually very RNG heavy, and rotating between different opponents means you have to focus on building a well rounded comp rather than playing specific counters. This is a completely different experience.

Ironically, even though Autochess was a originally a Warcraft arcade mode, this spin on autobattlers has much more appeal to a RTS lover like me. That's because this game focuses on units rather than heroes. It sort of remind me of Nexus wars from SC arcade if anyone remembers that as fondly as I do.

Being a game about units rather then heroes with crazy abilities, it's very visually clear and easy to tell where your comps are failing. There is a constant back and forth of trying to pick the hard counters to the opponents unit comp, but also trying to preemptively guess how they'll build to counter your eventual counter and so on and so on. It's really fun in the same way that trying to have build an army composition that counters your opponents' in an RTS is, but without the hell of having to maintain your APM. The dopamine levels when you pre-guess what your opponent is going to place and you already have the counter down is insane.

It's quite polished for an EA game. I have yet to run into bugs and I get into matches within seconds. Probably my only complaint is that there's only one repetitive song for each gameplay phase, so I muted music almost immediately.

I think anyone can have a great time with this game if they love strategising and having that feeling of hard reading your opponent. It's fun and addicting in a extremely basic level and I can't recommend it enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA potentially good game sadly hampered by a small (and shrinking) player base, poor matchmaking and serious smurfing issues that both causes and exacerbates the former two problems in an ouroboros of decline.

The game itself is pretty good, and plays like you would expect from an Auto-Battler style game. Available funds increase per round, which opens up new strategic avenues, there are advantages to winning prior rounds - but not to the stage that you can't pull things back. It has a level of complexity that makes it easy enough to pick up, but also gives some depth. Units and their tech upgrades are relatively well balanced, and the devs do seem to work hard to continually tweak things as issues become apparent.

There are unfortunately some pretty solid power combos and strategies, and as a new player you are going to lose if you don't learn these. Sadly the game can quickly feel like homework, as memorization of such tactics with a small dose of 'If X, do Y', will usually result in victory over tactical thinking in the moment or attempting to be responsive.

For me though, the main issue is the Smurfing. Once you find your 'level' of MMR you will be very likely to encounter new profiles that are somehow able to beat you every round. It makes the game pretty inaccessible to newer or more casual players, and you will regularly have to endure games that don't even feel close or fair. This is not a climate to which new players come, nor one that retains most.

Unless you're willing to put serious time in, watch a lot of tutorials and carefully study the meta to be a top player - I would give this one a swerve.

I've given this game a really good go and spent a lot of time on it, and some of the matches have been highly enjoyable. Even losing can be fun, as many close games are very back-and-forth. But unfortunately too many of the games are not enjoyable at all, and it doesn't seem worth enduring those to get to the few appropriately levelled opponents for good games.

I'll be giving this a miss until they sort this issue out. With the player base dropping rapidly, I sadly don't see that happening.
