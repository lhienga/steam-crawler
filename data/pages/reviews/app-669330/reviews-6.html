\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechabellum is an auto-battler game that has a lot of depth.
In this game, knowing what unit to use to counter each unit is as important as to know how to position your units, as some battles that should be lost on paper can be won simply by placing your units in a way that counter your opponent positioning.
For each unit of your army, you can chose 4 upgrades that you will be able to purchase on the field, some synergizing with each-others, others balancing out the demerits of another, and many being able to completely change the way the unit act and react to other unit on the battlefield. For instance, the Fortress is a powerful brawler, but can only hit ground unit, making it vulnerable to air, but it has an upgrade that allow it to shoot out anti-air missiles regularly, annihilating most air units you can throw at it in a few waves of missiles.
.
If you want guides, don’t use those from Steam as most of them are obsolete (some even have the old image for the hacker), and go check out those from YouTube and the Discord instead.
Speaking of Discord, you can join the Mechabellum server to ask question to other players, give your feedback, report bugs and so on.
.
A few notes that I have are:
→ it currently require more than the 10GB asked on the store, consider a margin of around 4 GB of free space. The game currently require a bit over 7.3GB to be installed, update included, and can sometime require more than 3.5GB of free space to run during long games with many unit on the field… and when you don’t have enough space anymore, at best the game crash, at worst you have to forcefully shut down your computer.
→ sometime, unit path and animation can bug, notably with Marksmen, that can lock in their walking animation, and the War Factory, although this is rare. However, especially if you consider how this is an early access, this can be ignored 99% of the time and the game is stable enough to let it go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechabellum: A Masterpiece of Strategy and Mayhem

In the vast expanse of the gaming universe, every so often, a game comes along that not only captures the imagination but also redefines its genre. "Mechabellum" is one such game, a shining beacon in the realm of auto-battlers that takes the player on an adrenaline-fueled journey to the newly colonized planet of Far-Away. As a Commander of an army of awe-inspiring mechs, the game challenges your strategic prowess and decision-making in massive PvP battles that are as thrilling as they are visually spectacular.

Customization at Its Finest

One of the standout features of "Mechabellum" is the deep customization it offers. From the agile Wasp light aircraft to the towering might of the Vulcan, the game boasts a diverse roster of mechs, each with its unique strengths and weaknesses. The ability to customize these mechanical behemoths and their abilities before marching into battle adds a layer of depth that is both engaging and rewarding. The satisfaction of tweaking your units to perfection and watching them demolish your foes on the battlefield cannot be overstated.

Strategic Depth Like No Other

At its core, "Mechabellum" is a chess match of epic proportions. The strategic placement of your units on the battlefield and the anticipation of your enemy’s moves are critical to victory. The game brilliantly forces players to think several steps ahead, combining the perfect mix of units and abilities to counter whatever the opponent throws at you. The inclusion of game-changers like airstrikes and crawler bot swarms adds an unpredictable element that keeps every battle fresh and exciting.

Visually Stunning Warfare

The graphical prowess of "Mechabellum" deserves a special mention. Watching massive armies of mechs clash on the battlefield is a spectacle to behold. The attention to detail in the animations of shrapnel flying, incendiary bombs exploding, and huge lasers cutting through the air adds a cinematic quality to each engagement. It’s not just a game; it’s an experience that immerses you in the chaos and beauty of mechanized warfare.

A Learning Curve That Rewards

For those wary of jumping straight into PvP combat, "Mechabellum" offers a robust AI to test your strategies against. This feature is a godsend for newcomers and veterans alike, providing a stress-free environment to experiment with different unit combinations and abilities. It’s a thoughtful addition that ensures when you do step into the PvP arena, you feel prepared and confident in your ability to lead your army to victory.

Conclusion: A Must-Play Title

In conclusion, "Mechabellum" is more than just another entry in the auto-battler genre. It’s a meticulously crafted masterpiece that demands strategic thinking, rewards creativity, and provides an awe-inspiring visual experience. Whether you’re commanding a swarm of Wasp light aircraft or unleashing the destructive power of the Vulcan, every decision feels impactful. For anyone looking for a game that combines strategy, customization, and sheer spectacle, "Mechabellum" is an epic journey you won’t want to miss. Prepare to command your mechs to glory and etch your name in the annals of Far-Away.\t\t\t\t\t
