\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game for those who are into co-op games, with a very broad range of difficulty. The game has quite a few things going for it. First off, a very simple control system that is easy to learn but not overly minimalist. Next, a cute artstyle that is light on performance. Also, it features a roguelite system of being able to upgrade your train (though more on this later). Lastly, the game oftentimes creates a crazed panic between you and your companions as you desperately try to keep the train going.

Overall, especially with friends, you will almost certainly have fun. However, I have a few problems with the game. My biggest problem is with how difficulty works. There are three ways the game incorporates difficulty: the setting you choose at the beginning of the game, which I'll refer to as the "difficulty setting" and the speed of the train, which increases as you progress, and biomes, which get progressively harder. The difficulty setting determines how hard the layout of the map is, not at all how the speed of the train increases/functions nor the difficulty of the biomes. Therefore, for me and my friends, who usually played on the Medium difficulty setting, the early few stages were quite a breeze. But, after a certain point, the difficulty just spikes and we find ourselves dying quickly. To me it feels odd that the terrain generation (i.e. the amount of resources and impassable rock) does NOT scale (unless you count specific biome hazards), while the other two aspects do. Something just feels off about the early game.

Other than that, this game is not really one you can go back to again and again and grind out in my opinion, as it lacks much content. Yes there are new wagons to buy and new characters to unlock, but imo there are not enough of either to make the game feel constantly fresh.\t\t\t\t\t
