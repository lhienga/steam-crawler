\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnrailed is a fun game to play with friends, local/couch co-op makes it accessible to others, I very much appreciate this aspect of Unrailed. The visual aspect is attractive and the characters are fun to look at.

However, the visual design makes it difficult to distinguish player from the environment. I don't consider myself a player that needs accessibility features, but a game scale factor, especially on smaller 15 inch screens, is absolutely necessary. This ultimately results in frustration and disorientation.

Another reviewer found the game was too punishing, had no real checkpoints, lacking tutorials and having a trial and error mechanic to push the game forward. The golden rule is to reward players for things they do correctly/achieve. This isn't fully designed into the progression of the game. That is the reason why you probably play this game a few times after purchasing.

Summary:

Positives
+ Visual design
+ Local co-op
+ Controller friendly
+ Music

Negatives

- Visual design
- Accessibility
- Repetitive
- Player Rewards

As much as I dislike some aspects, I do still recommend it, but only during a sale +- 10 euros/dollars.\t\t\t\t\t
