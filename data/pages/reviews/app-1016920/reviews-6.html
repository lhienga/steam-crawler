\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA party game about coordinating to construct railroad tracks and keep a train running for as long as possible.


Unrailed! tasks you with laying tracks in front of a running train, taking care to have the track avoid obstacles. You’ll have to remove trees and rocks to clear a path, using the resources gathered from them to construct new track pieces as well as bridges used for crossing bodies of water. Your train also requires water to run, with a limited supply that regularly needs to be refilled in order to stop it from catching fire.

To manage all of this, you’ll have to distribute the different tasks between players, making sure that everything is taken care of in a timely manner so the train never runs out of track. Coordinating all of this quickly leads to things becoming rather hectic, often leading to rather entertaining moments as you and up to 3 of your friends struggle along. You can also play Unrailed! on your own, getting support in the form of a bot you can give commands to, which in my opinion noticeably raises the game’s difficulty while not being as enjoyable.

As a round of Unrailed! progresses, you’ll eventually be able to connect your track to a station, granting you a break and providing some currency to spend on upgrades. These allow you to add various wagons to your train, with different abilities such as collecting gathered resources, creating dynamite for faster resource removal or temporarily stopping the train amongst many others. You can also upgrade your weapons to improve their efficiency.

With each of these stops, your train’s movement speed will increase, making maintaining it more and more difficult. You’ll eventually have to upgrade your train engine, which slows it back down and also brings you to a new biome, each of which features unique environmental challenges to mix up the basic gameplay. This continues until you’ll either manage to reach the final biome or, as is much more likely, your train runs out of rails and crashes, sending you back to a previous checkpoint.

Getting through the entire game is quite a challenge, with the game additionally challenging you to make your track as long as possible, by waiting longer before switching biomes. As you play, the game rewards you with various cosmetic skins for your character, giving some decent incentive to continue playing, get further and complete certain bonus objectives. As for the presentation, the game’s voxel-like art style matches its content quite well, with good-looking environments and neat animations making it a visual treat.


With all that said, I think that Unrailed! is a good party game with lots of fun to be had, though I also found it becoming a bit too challenging at times, tipping over from fun chaos to genuine stress.\t\t\t\t\t

My main complaint is that placing the rails when playing online sometimes feels a little awkward, but that doesn't take away from the great co-op roguelite experience of this game.\t\t\t\t\t
