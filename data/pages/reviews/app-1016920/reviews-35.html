\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're reading this review because you're wondering if Unrailed is a fun multiplayer experience:

Yes.

If you're reading this review because you're wondering if Unrailed is a fun single-player experience:

Yes.

If you're reading this review because you're wondering Unrailed offers a good cost-to-hours ratio:

Yes.

This was easily one of my best random/last-minute purchases I've made on Steam. I wasn't entirely sure how it all worked, whether it'd be fun, if it'd be fun for very long, etc. etc. etc. when I was looking for something new on a store, but decided to grab it to play with some friends and am so glad I did, because this is an awesome multiplayer experience and offers some of the most frenetic and crazy cooperative action you could hope for.

For the uninitiated, the goal of the game is to take your train as far as humanly possible; this job breaks down into three tasks:

1. Obtaining raw materials

2. Bringing those raw-materials back to the train to process into track

3. Placing the track created to extend your train's path

Any one of these three can provide a challenge on their own, but not content with a mere "challenge" Unrailed demands all three of these tasks be performed as the train at the center of it all chugs along, ever-forward, to a demise that may only be delayed by the continuing placement of track. It's game over if the train reaches the end of the track, as just like in real-life, it will instantaneously explode upon contact with anything but rail.

Every run is divided into individual sprints from station to station, with a pause between each in which players are able to purchase new wagons to add to their train or upgrade those they already have. The currency used to purchase wagons can be earned by reaching a new station, completing the random challenge specified for that level (an example being one that requires you to keep your train from burning), or finding and acquiring the hidden token that is placed somewhere in each level. Players have the option to continue on to the next biome by upgrading their front car to a more powerful engine. They may also choose to delay upgrading to save more currency before moving on, but this requires more and more risk, as each level, the train's speed increases, with the only way to reset it being to upgrade the front car, progressing to the next biome, and starting the whole process again.

With the overview out of the way, I want to reiterate, this game is amazing, terrifying, adorable, infuriating, rewarding, panic-inducing, and addicting. Losing on a long, difficult run is absolutely miserable, yet so often, I find myself starting right back up at the beginning and thinking of what new combination of wagons and strategies I should try next. I'd be hard-pressed to find a game with anywhere near the entertainment-level, depth, and replayability that Unrailed provides, at this price, or any other! The devs also seem awesome, so how can you go wrong? You can't! Do it! Do it now!

P.S.

There are also other game-types including versus mode, but as far as I can remember, I've been too hopelessly addicted to endless mode to try them all out, they're all on my list to try, though!\t\t\t\t\t
