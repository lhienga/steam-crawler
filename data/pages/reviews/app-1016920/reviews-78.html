\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo to be clear... DO NOT BUY THIS GAME AT FULL PRICE!

It's not worth it. It's fun, but honestly pretty repetitive. It did surprise me with it's level of depth for what is basically a track laying game. But this game goes on sale pretty regularly. I think I picked it up at 70% off (something like that). Then it's worth a punt for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is quite fun, but overall it was too frustrating to give a positive review.

First issue is the imprecision of actions. Using the axe/pick on resources or creatures requires precise positioning and it's easy to miss your target, and often the action gets interrupted.

Next issue is dealing with wagons. Very often I accidentally place resources next to the storage wagon, or take stuff from it while the intention was something else. Dashing into brake wagon sometimes doesn't register, especially if it's transparent from ghost wagon and the rail is curving, i only manage to dash into it properly 25% of times.

My biggest issue is placing the tracks in narrow areas or when the train is close to the end. It is a nightmare, requiring you to face the direction you want the track to go, but you also have to be in specific position that's not always clear, and I often end up placing the tracks wrongly.

There's no way of tracking special bolts needed for final level until you get to the last biome. And then when you collect them all, what do you get? Non-playable map that acts as credits. Disappointing ending.

Save system is another painful spot, it took us a while to realize checkpoints only get set at the start of biomes, and lost progress quite often. If you change the seed of the game, it changes difficulty and other settings as well, beware of that. "Allow checkpoints" box gets disabled if you load an old save, make sure to enable it again, we also lost progress due to this.

In singleplayer, the bot that you can command is very buggy and often gets stuck while it can clearly execute the given command.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to start this review noting i got this game for $8aud (around 5usd) and i think that's about what this game is worth imo. Thats because while this game is good, its also limited. While the game has a really strong foundation of gameplay, it doesn't feel like they've done enough with it. I think its because after a certain point, it feels like the base mechanics aren't getting enough development. New biomes end up feeling like the old biomes, except you have to use a different material to build bridges or some other minor change to the way you play to keep the train going. I think whats missing in this game that other procedural/randomly generated games have is the ability to express yourself in the gameplay. Most games like this seem to have a commonality of giving players enough leniency to play their own way. In minecraft, you get to choose what house you build, in Deep rock galactic, you get to specify what weapons/perks you take etc. This game doesn't feel like you have that choice. Cart upgrades feel like too much of a group choice to be your own choice and the gameplay kind of naturally encourages you to be efficient. This game is good, but it needs an avenue where players can create their own fun. Probably the easiest being just mods.\t\t\t\t\t
