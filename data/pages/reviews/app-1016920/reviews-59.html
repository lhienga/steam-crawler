\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as co-op party games go, I would classify Unrailed as Excellent.

The premise is fairly straightforward: a train that cannot stop needs a railroad built ahead of it before it derails and crashes. The task for you and your co-op friends, is to collect wood and stone to craft rails so you can lay track ahead of the train to prevent it from derailing. If the train reaches the end of the track before you lay your next piece, you lose. If you successfully navigate the procedurally generated level to the end and your train makes it safely to the next train station, you progress to the next level.

The big challenge is working cooperatively with your team. Every piece of rail requires 1 wood and 1 stone. But there is only 1 axe for chopping wood and one pickaxe for breaking stone per level. So you have to be very intentional and efficient with who is doing what task. We often assigned woodcutter, stonecutter, track maker, and track layer roles. This helped keep jobs defined and assigned, and helped us stay out of each other's way (there is player collision so you have to be careful in tight chasms!)

The levels also get more difficult as you progress. In the early game, you are mostly concerned with cutting paths through forests and mountains. But as the game goes on, certain areas cannot be destroyed, so you have to be careful which direction you choose to take your train. Even further into the game, the environments start to change, adding new upgrades for your train, but also new challenging terrain to navigate.

I like the train modules. I like how you have to input wood and stone so it produces a rail. I like how you can upgrade your existing modules, and add new modules, between levels. The pacing of the game was very good. The end game levels are very challenging, but by the time you get to them, the difficulty has increased at a reasonable, incremental level, so you feel adequately prepared for the challenge. The different difficulty settings make it easy to turn up the heat when you want a more difficult, chaotic experience. I also liked that the game challenges you to find rare currency on each level to use to upgrade your train, which gives you incentive to explore and pay attention, and it gives a secondary objective besides simply surviving to the end of the level.

In my experience, the multiplayer connectivity was excellent. We never had any issues with the netcode.

I would highly recommend this game for anyone looking for an engaging co-op puzzle/party game. To compare it to other similar games, I would say it reminds me of the co-op chaos of the Overcooked series, Out of Space, and Lovers in a Strange Spacetime.\t\t\t\t\t
