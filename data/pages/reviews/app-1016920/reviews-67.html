\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(I wrote a full review for this game and it disappeared immediately after posting it, so I'm not going in depth this time.)

The game is missing so much information it's not only fair to call it "unfinished", but also accurate. Yet, somehow it's V2.+something, that staggers me. At least 90% of the games information is missing, some of it gives cryptic clues that don't actually tell you anything at all and some of it is flat out false (even the wiki is missing information).

There is no in game chat, voice or text. All you get is a terrible emoji system, which is as bad as it sounds. Good luck trying to tell your team "We're going for the achievement, so we have to travel in the wrong direction for between 8 - 12 levels" using only smiley faces and thumbs, 90% of all communication is impossible.

Other players are the worst part of the game, and it's not their fault (mostly...). Due to the game not being finished (I don't care what you say, when the descriptions don't describe, that's not a description, that's a place-holder for a description) and the absolute clown decision to not have communication, nobody actually knows how to play because the game doesn't tell them how and those that have learnt how can't explain to anyone because all we have are emojis.

If you try playing alone you'll be stuck with 1 AI bot, who is beyond useless. it can't handle any difficulty above easy and will bug out for both simply and bizarre reasons. It doesn't know how to walk threw a wagon designed *solely* to be walked through and will freeze on the spot if it touches it, and sometimes pirouettes on the spot because the train... is at a... right angle?... I don't know, it just doesn't work sometimes for seemingly no reason.

It's a shame because it's a good idea for a game and when it all comes together and all the nonsense stays out of the way, it's pretty good. However, the problem is OTHER PLAYERS, the people you *need* or you can't play. If you're a total noob who doesn't mind dying on level 2 out of 7, this is the game for you. If you want to be good and actually take on some of the higher difficulties, for the love of god turn around and run away from this game as fast as you can, I promise it will end very badly.

15? HAH! No. Freakin'. Way. If you can get it on offer for 5 then at least you'll get your moneys worth of enjoyment while you learn how to play, but it will still end badly. Once you are no longer a noob the game is over. Remember, it doesn't matter how good you are, you are reliant on a team of random, uneducated people who you cannot communicate with, most of which are pretty bad and can't hold their own even on medium, don't even bother trying hard or extreme.

It's hard to hate the players though, they are just doing the same as you, trying to play an unfinished game with no direction or instructions and a seemingly intentional decision to prevent them from learning. The devs made so many stupid decisions it genuinely feels like they hate me and this game is my punishment for existing.

A game summed up; Played for 3+ hours in a game with no pausing, saving and has permadeath. Died because all 3 team mates did nothing but build track but not place them.

Fantastic concept, pathetic execution, infuriating experience, 3 / 10.\t\t\t\t\t
