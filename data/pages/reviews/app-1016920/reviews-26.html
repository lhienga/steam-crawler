Notes

Unrailed! is supposed to be a cooperative game in which you and your friends lay down the foundational tracks for your train. You'll progress through different types of biomes and encounter a lot of challenges and resource and role management as well as role throughout your runs. I love the pixelated art style and the music is absolutely incredible and matches with the game as a whole.

Gameplay

When you start out in the lobby, you are able to customize your character skin (and later on unlock new ones) to distinguish between you and your group, although it is possible to get the same skin in a lobby. Once world generation has occurred and you pop in, you'll start out with some basic train carts:
- Engine (what moves your entire train, duh)
- Water cart (keeps the engine cool - you'll need to refill a bucket with water to maintain water levels)
- Resource cart (houses resources you mine/chop to be sent to the rail car)
- Rail cart (crafts rails for you to place down in front of the train to stop it from derailing + lead the train)

The game gives you 3 starting tools:
- Pickaxe (mines iron)
- Axe (mines wood)
- Bucket (holds water/removes snow)
Each of these resources are necessary. Iron + wood go into the resource cart and fed into the rail cart to produce rails (they will also be used for other things). Bucket maintains the water cart water level.

There are other carts like dynamite, compass, efficiency, ghost, etc. which I won't fully go into since there are guides and wikis you can search up and figure out each do and figure out the most endgame strategies.

After a certain amount of distance, you'll see a structure with a rail next to it. It'll be a train station where you can park your train. Here, you'll be sent to a "blueprint" area, where you add carts or upgrade carts as well as save/exit to lobby. You'll learn later when you play the game more that you can skip these (and gives progress towards an achievement).

The goal of Unrailed! is to get as far as possible before you derail your train. After each train station, your engine will pick up speed. Upgrading the engine will allow you to pass through new biomes. To "end" a run normally, you'll have to upgrade your train engine to the max.

You control your character from a top-down angle and the screen will slowly move left to right, following your train speed and direction. This gives a couple of warnings:
- Don't leave anything behind, since they can get hard to get if you need them. They will, however, teleport back to you at a train station, so it isn't gone permanently.
- Just as you can lose your tools, you are also able to lose yourself. Your player icon will show that you're off the map, but since you can't see, you'll have to memorize the previous layout in order to navigate back into the game frame yourself.

There are in-game emojis (also AI commands if you play with AI solo) to which you can communicate in-game.

Summary

While this game is soloable (solo runs), it is more fun to play in multiplayer. They do have a Discord server for LFGs if you don't have any friends.

Overall, the game is a massive blast to play, especially with friends! Highly recommend getting this game 15/10!\t\t\t\t\t
