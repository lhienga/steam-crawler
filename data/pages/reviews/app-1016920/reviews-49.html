\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnrailed is, to put it simply, a game which shines as a coop game due to how complex it is in its simplicity. While the objective itself is pretty straightforward, making a train which is constantly moving forward arrive to its destination by clearing a path for it and extracting resources to make rails, the game add enough considerations and quick thinking problem solving involving up to 4 players to be worth playing. Wagon upgrade choices have to be weighted, and mechanics of some levels throw wrenches in the plan requiring adaptation. This is not, though, a game without faults. A lot of things are not explained, or poorly explained, and you will have to search online for some basic informations, as well as some more in depth ones. So you can't really expect to really become efficient unless you do some of your homework to learn and put some effort into it.

And this, in turns, is part of why the second problem exists: Unless you play on hard or extreme, you're going to meet absolutely atrocious players which due to incompetence, lack of awareness or sheer imbecility will prevent you from having fun. I estimate a general amount of 90% bad teams for 10% decent to good ones. We're talking people afk for at least a good minute if not more, people who start a job and quit halfway to do something else, THREE players doing the same job and one trying desperately to do the job of three persons all by himself because it's the only way to not lose the game, mind bogglingly terrible decisions such as "Hey let's make the train pass directly over a lake at the bottom of the screen where we have no room to allow people to cross instead of going around where it's all flat terrain!", people constantly dashing and making others drop what they are carrying, so on and so forth. This game deserves a better community than it has, and sadly, that's not something that can be changed. This is bad enough that you can see 3 persons sitting doing nothing next to a stack of rails watching the train crash, as you notice the disaster going to happen and rush to said stack to try to save the situation, only to arrive too late. It's disheartening.

Still if you can gather a group of intelligent friends, I'm sure it can be quite enjoyable, but as it stands, it's not the reality of the online as it is now. You know how much I hate to say of a game that it's "fun if you play it with friends" because usually it means the game is bad in its design. The game as it is, can stand for itself. It won't win any awards, as it's simplistic on almost all of its aspects bar maybe the decision making one. Yet there is some simple charm to it that makes you come back to it.

The biggest problem is finding a team worth working with, and a lot of time, you will open the game, check a few games, find some bad players/decisions in all of them, and then close the game because you realise there is no one you can realistically play with when you do feel like playing, and have to go play something else.\t\t\t\t\t
