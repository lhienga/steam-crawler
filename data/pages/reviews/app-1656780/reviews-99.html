\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game truly amazing. I am not one for lengthy reviews so I will keep it concise for my fellow ADHD friends out there.
You can customize so many things, you play style, your armies, you strategy, your equipment, your talents. So for those who like to just try different ways to play, there is so much to offer.
In terms of combat, the combat is well made with units having their own individual powers and impact to fights. Allowing yourself to swap between your reserves and active units in your army to switch your lineup before a fight to try and increase your odds of winning.
The game's tutorial does an okay job of teaching you the game, but it gives you a good baseline to figure the rest out yourself. Some in good ways, some in bad in my opinion. The translation is fine, literally I don't get the problem. An incorrect word can be found here and there, but in terms of understanding whats being said, at maximum I may not have understood one time. However I know what message is being said nearly 100% of the time.
The 10 factions also have so much variety between them its genuinely overwhelming, in a good way, that you don't know where to start. The only way to start is to just play.
My only gripe is the UI. It is not very intuitive and not very smooth. Everything functions fine, it can just be slow to control everything going on at once. However I get better slowly but surely at managing it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the demo during the recent Next Fest for about 6 hours, in addition to any time you see listed here.

Twenty-some years ago, I was in the right place at the right time to play the absolute hell out of the Heroes of Might and Magic III demo. Limited funds, lots of time, and a bright-eyed and endless hunger for what might lie around the next corner. "What if I really leaned into the ballista? Wow that's a huge stack of troglodytes... could I get one of those?"

And now I am older and this is grounds for the very same. From town building to heroes, even down to the resources chosen, this is all quite familiar. But despite falling into some of the less-exciting patterns of its ancestor, it manages to inject just enough new spin to reignite my curiosity once again.

Core to this is the choice to make the battle system real-time, with each individual unit acting for themselves. At first blush it seems chaotic— mash the guys into each other until the bigger stack wins— but the more time I spent with it the more I realized every unit was a specific piece I could set just so as to harness their unique properties, and lead my army into outperforming what simple numbers would suggest. It's a little like Total War in that way, though the unit abilities far outrange that series in terms of creativity.

It's that creativity that takes this game above its shortcomings. Every time I feel the late game get a little bit rote, or tire of a particular style of army, I need only choose a new faction or just another hero to crack open the horizon yet again.

I want more things like this. Familiar ideas taken and run off into the deep distance with wild abandon. The personal passion evident in handcrafting these hundreds of little digital figurines, now offered to me in a big ol' bag of possibilities? It feels good.\t\t\t\t\t
