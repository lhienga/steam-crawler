\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero’s Hour offers grand strategy in a bite sized package, providing enough mechanics and considerations to be deep and interesting, but not overwhelming. The games are relatively short and the many factions, wide variety of procedurally generated maps, and loads of options makes for high replayability.

In Hero’s Hour, you select a faction and balance income, building, and warfare on a randomly generated map. In the early game, you build your town, conquer neutral resource gathering settlements in your starting area, level up your heroes, and build an army. In the late game, you use your economy to pump out units, ferry them from your base to your main army, and conquer the other factions in the game.

HH could be considered an “autobattler,” meaning the battles play out mostly on their own. But the player can cast spells and direct troop movement in a broad sense, the interaction keeps them from feeling stale and hands off.

The game’s length is relatively short. It goes without saying that the more Ais you put in a game and the bigger the map, the longer the game, but games on smaller maps with a few AI’s typically last 2-3 hours. In my 7.5 hours so far I have won twice on normal, once on easy, and have lost a game on normal. The game can be challenging!

I would recommend Hero’s Hour for someone who wants a satisfying grand strategy experience without putting 15-20+ hours into a single game.\t\t\t\t\t
