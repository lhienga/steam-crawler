\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good game but with flaws. Hopefully they will be fixed within future updates.

Pros:
- 11 fractions and every seems unique, with unique units, gameplay and specialization. They're fun to play and provide really nice variety of tactics
- Procedurally generated maps, even the same map will be different on the next playthrough, also very good map editor included
- Battles are fun to watch and sometimes can get really intense. Beating up 2x stronger army with smart planning and deployment of units gives a lot, really a lot of satisfaction
- HoMM nostalgia feeling
- Decent soundtrack overall, i like the most Wild city tune, as well as Lament

Cons:
- AI is a blatant cheater. Literally AI feels like having unlimited gold and they gain units from nowhere. Even on Normal and Easy difficulty. It has nothing to do with strategy. It really kills the pleasure when you know you play against silly, but heavily cheating bots. Twice I took over the AI city in the fourth week and guess what? They had EVERYTHING built. How can you hire a powerful army and get enough resources to develop 100% of the city in 4 weeks? Only AI can. This should be fixed, because really playing vs AI is little to no fun
- Mediocre pixelart. It's not bad, but i think it could be much better, because the game actually looks worse than HoMM 1 from 1995
- No campaign, no story
- Poor balance of fractions, spells and heroes
- Inaccurate descriptions of unit skills, spells etc.
- No online mode, only Hot Seat

After all i think it's a good game, worth the money. I give it thumb up also to encourage developer to keep working on the game as it has potential. Just fix AI, add Campaign with story, repair balance, get better descriptions and add online mode - it will be a great indie game then, probably the best HoMM successor.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed the OG HoMM and MM games. I will be using these to compare to Hero's Hour.

Hero's Hour brings back the nostalgia of the HoMM series with some changes;

-The story.
HoMM had grand stories, painting scenes and describing components in detail you would have missed if you skipped past them. You will not find any story in Hero's Hour. Hopefully this is something they will add. This would be the the most significant improvement the title could make at this point.

-The combat. No longer a chess board of decision making. More of an eye candy skirmish, which takes out the big brain moments of a fully turn-based system.

-The AI. They seem to have no self-worth as they plunge themselves at you repeatedly to wear you down and gain levels. This kind of behavior should be punished harder globally on harder settings.

-Difficulty settings. Add a summary of what each difficulty offers. I assume it's only for resource balancing.

I didn't explore;
-Difficulty other than Normal
-PvP
-Hotseat
-any maps beyond the first 2 listed on the smallest maps.

The game is fun. It's chill if you're winning, it's punishing if you're losing.
At this point;
>>> Adding a sense of continuity would drastically improve the experience <<<\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the concept of this game. The RNG can be extremely harsh, CPU opponents are aggressive but easy to defeat if you play more aggressive and don't make minor mistakes... they are difficult until they are not, and my biggest issue is the "flee" mechanic which punishes you heavily for making a tactical retreat against a stronger opponent. It is almost less damaging to stand your ground.

In other words, this game is hard even on easy mode, and it is hard to understand all the mechanics of the game which are represented but not explained. To understand the difficulty of the game: I lost and had to replay the tutorial 3 times. I have found out how to win, but literally one false move and everything goes downhill like an avalanche. Certain buildings are REQUIRED to win, and if you don't have them specific to each race, you will lose... fast. This is a hard to recommend game, but for some people I can see it being a match made in heaven.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so this game. How best to put this?

I like this game, but not for the intended reasons. I enjoy the feeling of power I get when I overwhelmingly demolish my enemies watching them slowly bleed away to nothingness. This is an amazing feeling and is exceedingly hard to replicate properly.

Magic is incredibly broken in this game and with a proper set up, which can be quite easy if you pick the right stats and get lucky. Well this game can be immensely broken in such a way, battles that should only take a few minutes at most suddenly take longer to finish than my failing marriage. Why, you ask? Because summoning is a beyond broken ability. I'm still trying to exploit the other stats. Like attack, and defense to create a literal God in this virtual world.

Just last night it took me three hours to kill a little over 4000 clowns. The reason was because I summoned 2000 magma elementals, 1600 mana elementals, and 3000 water elementals at the very beginning of that battle. I keep half if this because if some items I have. I think my frames dropped to less than a frame a second.

It was brutal, and I loved every second of it.

As a regular game with regular building, the game is okay, you get new resources to work with every week, and the beginning can be a slog since you might start in an area you can't really fight anything. But it's not a bad strategy game, it's relatively simple.

Do I recommend this game?
If you like resource management rpgs that auto battle for you, yes. If you want to feel like a power mad summoner who destroys everything around you with hordes of easily replaceable units. Absolutely. Otherwise I think it's an okay game that fills a niche genre.\t\t\t\t\t
