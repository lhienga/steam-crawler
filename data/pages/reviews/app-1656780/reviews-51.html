\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very good strategy game. It asks you to not think too hard about the finer details, which is something I can appreciate. The autobattle system is a very welcome addition to the genre, battles are quick and fun to watch. Not to mention there's actually a great deal of strategy that goes into unit placement and movement. The fast paced nature of the game as a whole is a breath of fresh air for the genre and I believe it's the best one I've seen so far. People who enjoy a very calculated combat system where you control everything won't like this one though.

The campaign map is very random, and runs almost feel like roguelikes at times with how much a lucky or unlucky streak can completely affect the game's outcome. Although it can be frustrating, it improves replayability. This is totally fine in a PvE game but it suffers in the PvP.

The art is very cool and varied, I love this style of pixel art. It does exactly the job it needs to do, and it compliments well the craziness of creatures flying all over the place and dying in droves.

The main draw for me is the variety in strategies and setups you can go for. Although some heroes, factions and strategies are WAY stronger than others, it's still fun to try out all kinds of different ways to play that actually have a different feel.

As for improvements that need to be made, I hope they refine the quality of life side of things, it needs some work. For instance, placing units on the field at battle start is a real pain if you want a specific formation. It basically asks you to not care about it but some strategies rely a lot more on positioning than others, like Order's Rally ability which gives your hero an aura buff. The clunkiness of placement and unit control in combat is quite a detriment to the enjoyment of a strategy like that. So there's a sort of disconnect between the game's mechanics saying "Hey just sit back and enjoy the chaos" and "You need to have good positioning to be effective". Horde is a big offender of this and needs a lot of work for the faction to feel good as a whole.

As stated before, the game is very unbalanced. They recently did an update that nerfed certain things like mysticism, but there is overall a big gap in power. I hope they keep introducing more updates in the future. However, the most recent update introduced crashes so I hope that gets fixed soon. This game is completely unsuited for serious PvP because of the unbalance, and the intense randomness. I think that's an issue because multiplayer is a big way I think this game could go further and get more playtime. If they wanted PvP to become good, they would need a lot of balancing as well as mirror maps, and probably some other adjustments to randomness and such.

In addition, the hotseat function is pretty terrible. It would be nice to have some proper networking. Until then I can't see it taking off at all. Still, the singleplayer mode is good enough.

In conclusion, it's a rather fun game. It still needs a lot of work, but I believe in it from the small time I spent playing it and I certainly got my 20$ worth. The Hardcore+ difficulty is a fun challenge, I like small duel maps where the games don't last long, because a longer game could see you waste time on an unlucky run. But as long as you know the way you enjoy the game, it can be a great time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game released on steam only 3 months ago it's well put together and definitely worth the 20$ asking price. It is an auto battler, so don't expect real time strategy and tactics aside from positioning units on the battlefield and using a dizzying list of spells. The hero's you pick can also vary your playstyle, with unique artefacts, buildings and equipment that drastically change your starting experience keeping the game fresh on the same maps which are also randomly generated.

It also has a steam workshop page that's developing, with a comprehensive guide on how to add your own mods and sprites.

A few downsides though, I wouldn't recommend higher difficulties for newbie players. Start at normal or easy even, get the hang of the game and the system, cause the harder difficulties follow Total War logic, giving the AI a boost in their economy, resulting in them fielding mass armies at hardcore while you only have a single hero.

Another issue is the start of game. You wanna focus levelling a single hero as neutral armies can be pretty powerful to face. Resulting in a single overpowered army that can curb stomp anything in its path. With other hero's being delegated to collecting resources or basic chaff defence. This isn't too bad but it enforces a dominant strategy that goes against its replayablity.

All in all I can recommend this game for what it is, a beautifully designed homage to Hero's of Might and Magic. Def deserves a spot in your library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame has some fun features and is very clearly inspired by the Heroes of Might & Magic games.

However after playing a single scenario for 4 hours there are definitely some issues that need to be addressed to make this game more fun for the players.

AI
1. It's clear that the AI blatantly cheats to artificially make the game harder, no matter what difficulty you set it on. I had numerous times where I would engage the enemy AI, and the game would tell me the battle against them would be "Easy", stating that my army power was much, much higher than theirs. And when in battle, the victory bar on the top would show my army at 80-90% win chance.

Once the battle started my forces would start out strong against theirs. Then after killing most of them, the AI would pull out a handful of units from nowhere that proceed to blow through all my units. Even my strongest units that clearly should be able to handle them (things like my elite troops that were Power 25 or 30+ being decimated by their 10-15+ power units). I would then proceed to lose the battle, and the AI would run around claiming all my buildings before running back up to their base and INSTANTANEOUSLY refilling their army back to the same units they had before.

2. How are the AI able to instantly refill all their lost units and add new units before the week is up? Again, there were multiple instances in my game where I would stomp through some of the smaller AI heroes (only had 20-50 small units), and within 1 turn they would already be back running around with the same, or even more units than what they had before. I get they probably had the Field Hospital upgrades for some units, but they usually always refill 100% of the missing units, or more. If the player is limited by the units they can acquire each week, why does it feel like the AI gets to "cheat" again and just buy units whenever it wants?

Combat
The real-time auto combat is fun, however it's very barebones and needs some big QOL improvements to really make it shine.

1. The ability to choose what units you add to the field needs to be tweaked. Having a limit to how many units you can have out before you throw some in the Reserves is nice, but it needs to be indicated clearer how many "points" each unit takes up on the field count. I found myself doing lots of guessing and trial and error when attempting to switch out units from what the game "auto picks".

2. The UI needs to be updated to make it easier to select and choose what units you want to add to the field. Trying to add X amount of units from a group of 100+ is really time consuming when you have to split them individually on the troop UI and add them to the field (which again runs into the issue with not knowing how many "points" each unit takes up on the field, and having to again do a trial and error until you stop going over the limit)

3. Auto combat is nice, but there needs to be a way to target specific units. Currently you can tell units on the field an area you want them to go towards, but that's all they'll do. Say the enemy has a strong unit that you want your units to swarm and focus on to bring down. Currently you could select your units and move them over that way, however they'll just choose to fight whatever they feel like and ignore the unit you really want to bring down. Having a way to tell the units "hey, attack this please" would make it a bit more bearable during fights instead of watching your units ignore the strong one that's just ripping through them.

Summary
Overall there's a lot here for a great game, but in its current state I'd suggest holding off until there's some Quality of Life changes to how the AI and combat functions. It currently gets too frustrating seeing how broken the AI gets, and how the game lets them cheat to win fights and come back from a loss so quickly when you're limited to restocking troops once a week.\t\t\t\t\t
