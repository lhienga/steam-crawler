\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game's overall very good and brings back that nostalgic feeling of playing 'just one more turn', however is not flawless in it's mechanics / gameplay / optimisation.

While having many different races that use different mechanics is a good thing having heroes that more or less use the same talent trees makes game feel tedious at times as usually you will follow the same path to gain and use the most overpowered abilities. In other words, it makes playing each game feel the same.

Game sometimes seems to be slowing down for no apparent reason. And for no apparent reason it manages to speed up back to normal speed without any intervention. Still makes me wonder if this is a bug or just optimisation issue.

Some of the race specific mechanics might need some balancing as well as some of them sound utterly useless or not fun in practice compared to the others.

Of course, those are issues that show up after hours of gameplay. And despite all of them you still gonna enjoy every hour of your game sessions. Totally worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, I played over 100 hours of this game and have more than half of all the achievements. I feel I'm qualified to review this.

If I could choose just 3 words to describe this game:
- Whimsical
- Chaotic
- Addictive

I give this game in its current state an objective 6/10.

It was hard at first. I had never played an auto battler before, things were confusing, there's ♥♥♥♥ all over the screen in every battle, and I didn't know what anything really did... It was kinda fun though so I kept trying. After 10-20 hours I started to figure out how to play; that's when I couldn't put it down. There are some optimization issues with lag, maybe a bug here or there, but the more you learn the game the more enjoyable it gets.

I've won Hardcore difficulty games with every faction. If you struggle to win at this game, you haven't figured out how to break it. And trust me, breaking it is where most of the fun really is. (Hint: try a hero with the devour ability)

As far as content goes, the game is definitely a lightweight and setting customizations are somewhat limited. The DLC is a nice addition, I think it's worth it if you can stomach playing more than 24+ hours of the base game. The game is still very young, we'll see what TOIO does with it next.

$18 not a bad price at all. If you are on the fence, get it on sale.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaw a description somewhere of this game as a "glorious mess." I think that fits pretty well and it gets a soft thumbs up from me.

This is very much HOMM with a real time combat element once a fight is engaged. There's no campaign, just a bunch of different maps and factions you can choose from. And while there's a structure to skills, spells, units, towns, and items - it all goes out the window pretty quickly. Once you get a feel for the game you can take advantage of so many things - which is part of the fun.

The game is turn based and you can save at any time outside of combat. Makes it easy to just keep it on in the background. Combat can suck up incredible amounts of resources and end-game battles with thousands of units will bog down even very high end systems. Definitely work a purchase on sale if you enjoy HOMM type games.\t\t\t\t\t
