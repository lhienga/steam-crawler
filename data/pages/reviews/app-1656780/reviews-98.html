\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed the demo, so ignore the low hours. I'm too young to know what HoMM was like, but this little gem definitely has me interested in it.
Pros:
-Battles are fun and satisfying
-Progression is palpable--big units send hordes of basic infantry flying (literally)
-Exploration is exciting and rewards are meaningful
-Each army feels a little different and specialized (unless you're a necromancer)

Cons:
-Battles are chaotic and tactics are more or less impossible (makes them extra fun to watch tho)
-Factions were kinda imbalanced last I saw. Necromancers kinda kick everyone's ass, even after getting nerfed
-A new game's start can be quite tedious. You'll be surrounded by "Impossible" enemies and might spend up to a full week doing nothing but exploring and buying troops at your base.
-End game can also be a little tedious as you run around and try to squash every last Hero

I feel a little bad saying this, as I know from the Discord how much work has gone into this game, but this is pretty much just a lite auto-battler to be enjoyed when you can't find something meatier to get into. That said, it's probably the best of its kind I've encountered and it's turned me on to turn-based strategy in a big way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCrashes quite often, but other than that issue (that will probably be fixed eventually) it's a fairly good recreation of the old heroes of might and magic strategy games. If you dislike those game you will not like this one.

So i will go through a few notable things about the game.

They don't have upkeep and that is a double egged sword as the AI can snowball really badly if you aren't paying attention. The game design is a bit old in a few places, but in that they accomplished recreating the feel of this type of game quite well. If your the type to get quite annoyed by those little things you probably won't like the game all that much.

The factions are unique in that they all have different units and about 3-4 unique mechanics. Which is actually quite appreciated from my standpoint of most modern RTS games have 2-4 copy pasted "unique" factions. Although a few of the factions don't really seem that balanced in a few places which i personally attribute to massive amount of factions in the game. With so many factions there is bound to be one or two that can't really compete. But with map customization you can balance the game if a faction is under/over powered although a bandage on that issue.

The in-game journal is not necessarily that well done. There is a lot of units left out of it and one even had its text going out of its box.

But yeah that's my limited unimportant thoughts from my first experiences, so there ya go.\t\t\t\t\t
