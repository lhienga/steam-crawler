Even has a "skirmish mode" where you can test out custom armies against each other - although nothing gets saved and you have to set up the battle each time which can slow down gameplay.

Plus, this game has highly rated mods already on Steam Workshop - e.g., a custom Lovecraftian faction (like Delirium), new neutral units, etc.

After playing enough of this game, the lore is interesting and I hope the developers continue adding faction/unit lore over time.

While it's an amazing game, improving certain aspects (like skirmish & lore) will make it even better. New non-mod factions are always exciting, but probably not as necessary anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero's Hour is an incredibly well-written love letter to Heroes of Might and Magic. I've been looking for a game as good as HoMM 5 for so long I've lost count, and the much-beloved HoMM 3 just didn't do it for me. Hero's Hour, however, is a great time I recommend to all fans of strategic RPGs or RPGs in general. It's an easy 8/10 game for me.
Positives:
+Really nice pixel art
+For the price, you get a huge amount of content, a map maker in case those in the game weren't enough, a mode for only battles, online multiplayer... Plus, the supporter pack costs practically nothing.
+Said content also has great variety, featuring a whopping twelve factions, a gigantic variety of skills, creatures and artifacts and a spellbook the size of a bible.
+Information about said things is also to-the-point and easily accessible.
+Great, catchy music capturing the style of old-school games, but with modern quality and even the occasional vocals in a song.
+Accessible, but challenging, featuring customizable difficulties and great quality-of-life changes you'll have to find a good use for. For example: Buildings that spawn troops weekly don't actually charge you for the troops, and they're automatically turned into caravans that home into the nearest hero/town nearby. You can also decide where they go manually by turning them into Regiments, and you can turn your towns'/heroes' troops into caravans/regiments too. Regiments can also fight independently of their heroes.
-Game tells you the name of the songs currently playing, THANK YOU!

Negatives:
-Unlike HoMM, where only the strongest units cost a certain resource along with gold, weaker units in HH also frequently cost resources AND gold. This can create some frustrating situations where you're constantly out of said resources if the map doesn't provide you with a source for a while.
-Outside a few actions, battles play out automatically. This can be a major turnoff if you prefer moving your troops around carefully like chess. Not to mention big battles can drag on without being able to provide imput, and they can have performance issues too.
-You can't load a save in a battle (yet)
-Lore, but no actual stories/cutscenes. Very minor nitpick here, though.
-Occasional crashes and inconsistensies with the autosave. When you save in the middle of a turn, loading it goes back to the beginning of the turn.
-Supporter pack DLC content only available by browsing the game's local files, with the exception of the special sprites.\t\t\t\t\t
