\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an HOMM3 connoisseur, I really like the basic gameplay loop. However, there are a lot of oddities present at this time that make it a bit difficult to really enjoy to the fullest potential, and they almost all revolve around communication:
1) Tooltips are often poor or missing critical detail.
2) Selectable icons are barely distinguishable from already chosen options (e.g. hero skill tree)
3) Battles are chaotic and difficult to manage or really understand where the pack is going (shows the strength of the core HOMM3 single stack abstraction for simple gameplay communication)
4) The city screen is lacking tooltips and any identification on the graphical side to communicate available buildings (thankfully the building 'tech tree' menu does show this clearly).

Aside from those types of issues, it's a very fun game and has THE most important element which the remaster of HOMM3 lacked - random maps.

I do hope they address difficulty and the 'points' system with deployable units. I think it is in place to keep combats 'more balanced' but in some circumstances and with later monster modifiers, it is a bear to rock up with 1200 power hero stack but only be able to deploy 400 because of arbitrary restrictions. I think it needs modification. Definitely recommend for fans of the genre though.
