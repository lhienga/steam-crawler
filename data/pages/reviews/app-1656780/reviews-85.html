\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game, the 8-bit nostalgia, and the overall idea is really really good. The artwork and the sound I think are both on point, and I really enjoyed them a lot. Execution however leaves an awful lot to be desired. I think the most concise way I can put this is, no matter what difficulty or how to change the settings, at some point you experience the AI rush something akin to the Zerg meta of old. If your sole focus is not building your town EVERY turn, maxing your army in every way, you eventually get horde rushed and absolutely destroyed. It’s a real pity the developers don’t understand/see the potential this game has as an RPG adventure if they would just chill out with the zerg rushing and min/maxing it could be an incredible experience.

Another issue, they have a fundamental issue with regard to hero development, if you don’t rush and immediately get the tavern and begin recruiting new heroes and then make sure to level those heroes against lower level enemies on the map, you literally have no upgrade/experience path for the newer heroes. This is such a fundamental gameplay issue I am stunned this has not been addressed, I cannot believe this has not been noticed by the devs before now.

I hope with future patches they can change the trajectory of the game towards something a little less akin to StarCraft and more like Heroes of Might and Magic, I’d be more than happy to change this negative review if they did. But until then I cannot recommend people spend money to buy this game\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero's Hour has already given me more fun than I had with HoMM back in the day, primarily because of the autobattler-esque manner in which Hero's Hour tactical battles work. You build your engine and try to break the game before another player breaks it harder. Or the AI, which isn't brilliant - but unlike something like a 4X game, the AI doesn't need to be brilliant here. It just needs to generate a benchmark of resistance to tell you whether or not your engine was sufficiently busted.

Side note: the tactical battle puzzles (Skirmish mode) are both fun and extremely educational. Don't miss them! Solving them made the otherwise-overwhelming unit variety make sense to me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: Huge fan of HoMM 1-5 since the 90s, hated 6 and 7. I was reluctant to buy this game because of many reviews citing cheating a.i., unfair difficulty even on easy, technical issues and forced rush gameplay lest would you auto-lose.

My first impression after 4 hours: I didn't experience any of the criticism.
Played the tutorial and 1 skirmish "Hero of the hour" on easy against 3 opponents. I picked the Order faction. Difficulty and game progression felt exactly like HoMM. Conquering the home region was very easy as the difficulty setting would suggest. After 2 weeks the first enemy entered my region with a sizeable force and better hero than mine. This was the "oh crap, let's check if I'm below 2 hours to get a refund" moment.

Turned out to be not that tough of a fight though and from then on I slowly progressed and captured the other 3 factions by week 8.

I get that it can be frustrating when you start on "normal" difficulty and get rekt. But if you lose on easy, then just watch some YouTube videos for god's sake. Games used to be way more difficult than AAA titles theses days.

Now to the technical issues: Never had a single CTD, not even a lag in battles with 1000+ creatures.

Well worth the price tag and a great alternative to "HoMM 3 Complete" (not the awful HD version on Steam mind you! Buy HoMM 3 Complete on GoG and get a HD mod!).
Hero's Hour is not nearly as all-time great as HoMM 1-4 and if you don't own these classics, buy them now! It's still a very good game in my opinion.

rating: 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
