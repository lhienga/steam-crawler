\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn incredibly clever version of the classic 'Heroes of might and magic' formula that does what we all have dreamed of at some point. If instead of having one unit with a number of a hundred, we could have a hundred units of them on the field. Yes, every unit you recruit will fight on the battlefield as a separate entity. The magic in this game rivals H3 in how diverse and how unbalanced it is. It really does feel like every run is unique with how much this game focuses on randomizing it's content with random town layouts, random skills for heroes and all the different set items you can get.

There are a lot of different factions with well made unique mechanics and hero abilities. This game isn't afraid to be as unbalanced as Heroes 3 and can make your games insanely chaotic and fun outside the randomness of the game. However, i have heard a lot of complaints that this game is difficult even on lower difficulties. Personally I haven't experienced that but i am a Heroes veteran so even the highest difficulty is a fairly easy walk in the park. So if you've never played a Heroes game in your life, be prepared that this game can be quite hard to understand and master. So much so that all the bad reviews that aren't about crashes are about how difficult this game is to understand and how impossible it is to beat.

All in all, it's an incredibly well made vision that gives you a 'what if' kind of game based on HoMM series. Will it keep your attention for as long as the HoMM games did? No but it will certainly keep you entertained for a while.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA simplified Total War game that's generally fun, but repetitive. There are a lot of races to choose from, each race have their own tech tree, heroes, units, and features. It is quite feature-rich and fairly enjoyable game, where you can use various strategies such as a mage build, or powerful heroes, or economic dominance, etc.

The control is intuitive, and how you position your troops does affect the battle outcome.
It was fun to fight a tough battle and win, especially when defending castle.
However, once your get past early game, even on Hardcore+ difficulty, everything kind of fall apart.

First, the difficulty.
The AI is not smart. It will attack if your castle or Heroes is weak, but also sending all their heroes all the way from one corner to the opposite corner to capture a single building and go home, even if I have captured all their building around the AI castle.

There are some super exploitative builds that make your Hero overpower. Pretty easy to do if your map happens to have those building, or a hero with certain skills. It's not inherently bad as AI can have the same skills. You need some luck for AI to exploit it, but oh god, the game broke if AI do exploit it.

I played a round that deliberately avoiding those exploit to have some challenge, and somehow at about week 10, one of enemy weakest unit have 20k hp, 3k damage, single-shot all of my unit. My Hero, with 60k army power, lose to the enemy 8k army power, to one single type of unit.

So I did a long run, dragging the game all the way to week 80 (turn 500+), and I realize there's no way to play without exploitative build. At some point enemy will trigger an exploit making unit that has crazy HP, or crazy damage, or both. There's no option, you have to exploit.

Second, the horrible performance
Unless you are running the most powerful CPU, it will easily drop to just single-digit fps at mid game. Once you open up most of the map, there are so many particles on the map that kind of lag the game, going to 30fps for me.

In battles with thousands of units, you can get to 1fps territory easily. The battle speed is tied to fps, you will start having 10min, 30mins, to hours long battle at mid to late games. At week 80, most battle take up to 30min at least, you just stare there, or play other game during the battle.

Lastly, the battle system don't work in late game battle
The first issue is massive army, like thousands of unit on screen tanking your fps. Then, it is almost certain at some point enemy will trigger exploit and you have to do the same.
If you exploit HP and Damage, you don't need to do anything, you can win with the weakest troops.
If you go with spell boosting magic, you can have skill 1 shot enemy, or summon so many unit that guarantee victories, like summoning hundreds of phoenix costing 50 mana out of 4k mana.
If you try to win by army size, you will lose for sure. I have lose 90k army power, 20+k units, to enemy 8k army with those overpower unit.

It's just not a fun experience at mid and late game.

I used to recommend the game, but with expansion now making it a $25 game, I can't recommend a game that basically force you to finish fast, or exploit the game, and have horrible performance optimization, at full price.

Consider purchasing it only if it is on sales.\t\t\t\t\t
