\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've dropped a decent bit of time in now and think I've experienced a fair bit of the game. So far, after playing the Beta a bit and seeing the direction it's going, I like it. It's a fun game, with a lot of depth and the factions on the surface may seem similar, but with time to flesh out this will be a very fun game.

Right now the issues I see are the lack of proper diversity with the cities, and only having a single city per region basically makes any bonuses to help defend the city are virtually useless. Complaints about the game being too hard are simply being made by people that are bad at video games. I've beaten a few games on easy and normal now and it's certainly not too hard. The complaint I will say is that there's virtually only one 'valid' play style, otherwise the game *is* impossible. In order to actually beat the game now, you basically have to throw all your units into a single doom-stack and run that around the map clearing out your starting area. If you don't do this, you'll capture everything in your area way too late and when you AI comes for you, you'll never stand a chance as they will simply doom-stack as well. There's virtually no viable way to make smaller armies useful right now because of the doom-stacks. If a lower unit cap was put on the generals, I think you'd greatly increase the play styles that were viable and allow people to actually think about their army comp beyond making their doom stack.

Also, the inclusion of no proper multiplayer is baffling to me, why wasn't that out on release? You can steam play together with a friend and it actually works very well, but no proper multiplayer really should have been out on launch.

So overall, a very good first impression, I do think it needs work and don't think it's 'complete' yet at all. I'm going to call it a 3.5/5 and with a few updates and a few small tweaks this will easily move up to 4.5 or 5 even. I don't regret the purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember watching my dad play Heroes of Might and Magic 3, this brings back so much nostalgia. Not only does it feel like those old games, but it puts it's own twist on things. It's so nice I can start a large map, play all day, and end the day with a victory.

Pros:
- Lots of variety in the gameplay, I got 30 hours in and can easily go another 30+.
- Lovely sprite art
- The randomly generated maps are well put together
- Each game feels fresh and new, even if you pick the same options.

Cons:
- Game is in beta, so things are not optimized. Also game isn't well balanced, there are some definite needs for adjustments.
- Randomness can actually make the game easier or harder, needs some adjustments.
- It can be hard to learn/The trait system needs a revamp.
- Difficulty is a bit off.

Suggestions:
- Adjust some randomness. I got an artifact that gives dragon mastery +2, and with my character traits was setup I was getting 1-2 Red Dragons per fight. By the end of the game I had like 30 Red Dragons and was just unbeatable. Other games I get some pretty mediocre artifacts and was a struggle until the very end.
- There are Heroes with fantastic with combat and heroes that are fantastic at development (cheaper units/more units, etc.), the heroes great at development are a must have. You buy them, level them to level 9 or 10 then just leave them. Forget about them. Have them go sit in a castle or outpost to guard. Don't have that, if you want Heroes to have development bonuses, make that a separate system or just remove development traits all together. If you don't have a hero that gives bonuses to development, you just lose. Design it better like making it a different trait tree or have all 2nd tier traits be a developmental trait.
- Easy difficulty feels like very easy, the AI will derp around a lot and you are allowed too as well. While normal feels like hard, on normal I am allowed a few mistakes or a couple times of poor optimization in my strategy, maybe trying one or two new things. Hardcore feels impossible, only way to get a victory is with 0 mistakes, only using strategies and builds that are effective, like Hardcore+ should be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic fun, and a must-try for all HOMM3 fans.

In fact I think it has the potential to be better than the game which inspired it, but it's certainly nowhere near there yet. It's faster to play, and the fights are unashamedly chaotic auto-battle segments (with the ability to chuck spells onto the battlefield to spice things up). BUT, it still needs a lot of balancing, and for me it's just far too hard at the moment. I would like to see an easy mode which is a lot more 'peaceful', for those who just want to roam the map building up unstoppable armies. The current Easy mode should be renamed Normal, the current Normal mode should be... well, you get the idea.

The game looks to still be in active development, so I remain hopeful that it will one day reach its full potential.\t\t\t\t\t
