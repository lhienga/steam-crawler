Don't fix what isn't broken...
Pros: #1. It's literally 'Heroes of Might and Magic' (HoMaM) but with auto-battle-ish combat

Cons: #1. Each battle has a limit to the amount of 'army power' that you can put on the battlefield. This means that you'll never be able to crush the enemy and also means that you'll constantly have your army chipped away at after each battle. This limitation makes the game more punishing and also dramatically extends the TTC (time to completion) of every map. I remember having played all the HoMaM games growing up, that often I'd spend 60hours+ playing on a single map and that was WITHOUT this 'army power limitation' that this game has. I wish that the limit was either completely removed or a simpler 'total units limitation' was implemented instead, because the current iteration of the 'army power limit' literally only serves to punish the player and to dramatically slow the TTC. If something isn't broken, don't fix it... and there was nothing wrong with the way that HoMaM did 'army power' or overall unit growth.
#2. When I started a new game, I had 14 'Impossible' power battles around my starting position... this should NOT be like this. It should be like in HoMaM, where all the battles close to the player's starting position are either 'easy, average or challenging'. Having that many 'Impossible' power battles in the starting position means the game is designed wrong, or there's something broken in the map RNG.
#3. The game has a huge amount of missing tool-tips. There were easily 5 times when I said to myself "why isn't there a tool-tip being displayed here?"
#4. You have no choice with regard to choosing your starting heroes OR what skills / bonuses they have
#5. No fps options
#6. No borderless fullscreen mode
#7. Can't change resolution size when selecting fullscreen mode
#8. No colorblind accessibility options

In summation: It's literally HoMaM, but the army limitation in this game makes the game constantly punishing and slows down the TTC. If the 'army limitation' was removed, then I'd recommend this game with no problem... but with it in the game, plus all the other issues highlighted above, it's simply NOT a game that I'd recommend you buy over a game like HoMaM4,5,6 or King's Bounty: Armored Princess / Crossworlds... in fact, I'm pretty sure that you could get ALL those games for around $18 if you get them on sale. So I just can't recommend this game, since it's not better than HoMaM5 which is 15years old and much cheaper.
