\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not good at traditional 4X strategy games, so a strategy game along these lines is easier for me to understand. I would say this is more like a 4X-Lite or 4X-Mini instead, because of a few things such as the basic concepts of battles; battles are for the most part automatically played on their own with some occasional clicking by the player in order to do things such as using spells or retreat, for example. Afterwards the claimed bases must be defended from potential future attacks from the enemy by maintaining a large and diverse army at that base. There is some resource management, but it is basically just for the home base and that does not need to be continually monitored and modified as the game progresses; building up the home base unlocks stronger forces and abilities, which in turn allows stronger fights to be won.

It is a relatively simpler concept and because it involves less micro-management than a traditional 4X game, it is more along the lines of a game that can be played casually for short breaks during the day. This is not to say that there is little content to the game--There already seems to be a diverse variety of classes, troops, abilities, and items in this game, and the randomization allows the game to be a little different each time. I will admit that I am not entirely good at this game yet so i still have some difficulty from time to time; now if this was a traditional 4X game I probably would give up out of frustration and not give it another try, but I keep coming back to this game. Apparently the developers will be adding more content, so we will see how the game changes and evolves as time goes on.\t\t\t\t\t
