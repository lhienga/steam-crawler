\r\n\t\t\t\t\t TL:DR: Bought on sale . A fun turn-based/action HoMM3-like. Best for local multiplayer and quick games, as it's not without it's problems and a fair share of bugs. Loving 16 bit art style and super nice details. This is great product that needs some polish and redrafting still, With this low price it's a bargain and I will be grabbing the DLC too, but I also kinda feel like somethings could be better now or in a future sequel. Optional turn based combat style would be cool for example.

Long story: Had the 1.6.0 Demo for two years on a HDD I loved to toy around from time to time but forgot to invest in. Surprisingly latest version that I bought remembered all the achievements I got. There are surprisingly many improvements and additions since that but even with added content the optimization issues.

- Hunting skill mechanic are utterly broken and has been from 1.6, if anything it becomes even more spammy with the rewards in 2.6. This is the biggest disappointment between versions as it's more about Hydra feeding than anything else but to do even that "safely" or efficiently hasn't been implemented either. There's not really a good reason to spec beyond 3 and yet it's in the top layers of skill pyramids. This in a game the puts out paid DLC is pretty sucky

- The game has some severe optimization issues with high unit and resource counts that drops the frame rate down and stuttering in both in over world and in combat which is the worst general thing about the game. Noticeably improved between old version and new. Usually this doesn't come into play on normal games but I like to test features and the games AI to see how it reacts. Still, I recommend Oligarchy skill and using anything that can drop unit and unit type counts in favor of Swarming and tactics, as well as fielding less than 10 heroes. I wish there was more options for the graphics and turning off idle animations.

- The AI is kinda clever and dumb at the same time, it can for sure spot out a weak stronghold of yours half way across the map and aim at it but it can also get into non-accomplishing loops like sailing back and forth, sending a hero with less and less units every week on the same spot if it's naval access is locked. It can be unfair seeming even in Normal difficulty if it gets a optimal roll, usually with early diplomacy rush but most often it doesn't understand the value of protecting it's own base so they are generally easy to win on a war of attrition and sneaking bases from under them.

Here are some of the fun broken combos i discovered in my first day playing, I will add to this list over time

1. Beat an impossible defense of my hometown with just a high level spellcaster and a stack of gobbos, echocasting duplicate on the gobbos set to defend until the frames drop to 1 per second and then attacking with a whole screen of gobbos.
2. Getting a Delirium Hero to level 5 Glare ( a spell that confuses the enemy and will cause him to attack himself, with a small chance to add the unit to your stack permanently) running around the whole map adding all sorts of different units to my army until i gathered units from all factions and then steamrolling the remaining enemies with my deathball.
3. With the Order I did a Ballista run with Paladins who heal themselves, setting them to defend to watch my Overpowered Ballista and Mortar bombard my enemy to pieces.
4. With a hero that has the spell frog rain and the ability that when units die they cast random spells, bombard a town with exploding magic frogs
