\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn interesting evolution/different take on HoMM-like turn-based strategy series :)
Turn-based battles from HoMM that often turned into repetitive slogs switched to fast-paced autobattler, where positioning is often more important than raw numbers.

+ decent tiles (map moreso than units, I wish units were a tad bit more detailed and distinguishable on the battlefield)
+ charming animations (little things like floating pickups on water or airborne gargoyles dropping enemies to their deaths)
+ variety of castles, units and spells
+ fast pace - it would beat any HoMM title for quick multiplayer sessions if not...

- lack of online multiplayer (very bad for a title that seems made for multiplayer skirmishes in mind)
- no single player campaign
- no story and lore of any sort, even in the form of item descriptions
- AI could get a little more polish, in the 2 hours I played it never retreated once and wiped full armies on walls several times. It should be able to retreat as even in 26 year-old HoMM2 the AI can do it. It also seems to make sub-par decisions like rushing the towns while ignoring nearby threats (but I have not tested higher difficulty settings, maybe it plays a bit smarter on those)

7/10, would recommend for any fans of the genre, tho for 5-7 euros less than it currently costs or maybe as part of a bundle.\t\t\t\t\t
