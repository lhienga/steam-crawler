\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love this game as a time killer and cool down from other comp games that I play... that being said I feel that there are a couple of things I would love to see.

1. I saw that there will be a solo campaign, which is the number one thing I want to see, but I would like it to be where you can start a new campaign for each faction and you constantly go against the other factions. Of course increasing in difficulty. If you can implement almost a star based completion where based on the number of units lost, battles lost, or how long you took to win you will get a certain star rating. This will make it in my opinion so that even if you beat the campaign there is always something more to do. (specifically for those completionists out there.

2. Multiplayer/ranked online. I enjoy playing against "somewhat competant" AI as it makes the game that much more enjoyable as it is not always a walk in the park... that being said eventually it is not something I will endlessly play this game for. If there is an online competitive option where you get a ranking based on wins and where you can play against multiple other people like a 4 man 1v1 I would be in love. I am a simple man that loves being competitive and adding that small component would keep me loving this game for a long time and playing for longer than just a game a day. It feels good to win, but it would feel better to be at the top.

3. this is my miscellaneous section.. Is there anyway to implement more of a strategic foundation to the game? For example, editing the way troops behave like making it so that say your imps only go for the back line while your tanks stay in front of your ranged attackers and battle the opposing tanks? This would be cool to implement as I try to perform this strategy as it is, but they always get distracted.

Can you thorw on a timer or notification for when you can buy more troops? Maybe I am just brain dead, but I have yet to understand what the parameters for buying new troops is,

Can you throw in an indicator for when (kind of like chess) an enemy troop is able to travel to you and attack you on their turn? They still might not be trying to attack you, but being aware that this can occur might change up how people approach that situation.

LAST, BUT NOT LEAST
As it is can you add a completion to the current maps to show you completed for example duel with this faction at this difficulty so that I can either feel as though it is a trophy and do it again as another troop or just remember I beat that map as I have terrible memory lol.

So far I love this game and cannot wait to see what it brings to the completed game!!!!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great strategy game if you like games like Risk.

The base game (without DLC) has 12 factions. Each of these factions are strong in their own right and can win games. Each has their own focus. For example, Delirium has a cool perk where when you hire new 'heroes' they are avatars of your hero. Thus, their level, upgrades, and artifacts coincide with your heroes. This makes for very strong leaders, as opposed to the other factions who hire much weaker heroes. Pyre's specialty is a hell-portal that can be upgraded and continuously gives you units throughout each fight.

You start off with one city that's not upgraded at all. You will get a small sum of gold each day to start, and you can begin by upgrading your city/hiring units. There are many mines/other outposts on the map that can be captured to give ore/gold/wood/crystal/mercury/sulphur each day. In addition, your town will give you more gold/day as you upgrade, and you can upgrade your units/make them more powerful. Each town has 6 base units you can hire, all of relative strength to other factions.

Heroes will get a choice of buffs as they level up. Some of the potential upgrades are: increase all unit health by 5 and attack by 1 (great for weak units), red dragon (get a % of dragon blood after every battle, 100% gives you a red dragon unit), elemental (create elementals after battle). There are many, many more upgrades.

Artifacts come in many forms, but you can wear a whole set (cape, hat, legs, gloves, weapon, etc.). They will give a variety of upgrades: sight (see farther), attack, defense, etc.

There is also magic. How it works is each hero will have certain spells (towns will give you spells, as well as certain upgrades). Your magic will replenish some each day, so consecutive battles will result in no magic (depending on how much you used). I've never used Arcane (a faction) but the spells seem to be powerful, and there is a large variety, from comets to lightning to turning enemies into frogs to buffing your own units.

Most of the map is shaded black, but it becomes clear as you move closer to the black parts. The maps come in many shapes and sizes and offer a variety of factors. For example, some maps have many towns and are huge, some have few towns and are small. Each will come with a different number of factions (the highest is a 7 person battle right now, yourself and 6 other factions).

Basically how the games work is you spend quite a few turns in your own small area. So far that I've seen after 7 or so games, the areas are all blocked off by fortresses or docks (where you can buy a boat and sail around the ocean). You will spend this time capturing mines, fighting enemies, upgrading your towns, and hiring more units until you can beat one of the battles at the exit. The game has a rough guide for how hard a battle will be: easy, moderate, challenging, hard, near impossible, impossible.

Once you leave your area, you will be able to explore and capture more towns/mines/etc. There are also a ton of random events, such as the fountain of youth, places that give you weekly units, cloud cities, and much more. After that, you try to outpace your enemies and win. Once you have captured all the cities of a faction and killed their remaining heroes they are eliminated.

There is a lot of replayability in this game. Seeing as there are 12 factions, you can do at least 12 games without getting bored. Though I will say the loading time is frustrating for games with a lot of factions (at least until some die off). Worst it gets is probably about a minute between ending your turn and going again.

There is also a skirmish setting on the main screen, which might be a good place to start. Its a faux battle where you hire units to try and kill the enemies. They take ~2 minutes from beginning to end, though you might lose. This will give you an idea of how to use your units in battle (even though the battles, besides magic, are relatively hands off in a real game).

9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAdorable unspeakable horrors await! Hero's Hour brings tons of charm and personality to the HOMM formula. Pixel-art graphics aren't usually my thing, but so much of the art and animations in this game are just MUAH! *chef's kiss* It's a fairly chill take on fantasy TBS - a lot of games will be over in an hour or so, and victory has felt kind of inevitable in my runs so far (normal difficulty). Just build every turn and feel out your heroes' strengths, don't get in tough fights too soon, and you'll stomp the AI. Still, watching your units evolve and getting the feel of different heroes and factions is super satisfying, and the battles are just the right mix of awesome and hilarious. Overall it's a horror-comedy "cutethulhu" vibe with a really nice balance of "Awww..." and "Awesome!" One place I'd fault the HOMM games, especially the later ones, is they feel kind of sterile. Hero's Hour isn't high strategy, but it's great fantasy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game could have been amazing, but it falls short in a few critical areas.

First, the graphics looks like dog shart. Don’t get me wrong, I love pixel art, but this game has very low quality art.

Second, the battles are a mess which makes it difficult to follow what is going on. Anytime a group deploys on the battle field, all the units in the squad sort of explode from one point scattering in all directions as if they were amped up on LSD or something. This results in a huge unorganized twitchy tangle of units.

Finally, the controls/interface are terrible. Trying to deploy units in a strategic way, making squads and giving them limited commands is clunky, imprecise and tedious.

It is a shame, because I love this type of game. Unfortunately, I cannot look past its many flaws.\t\t\t\t\t
