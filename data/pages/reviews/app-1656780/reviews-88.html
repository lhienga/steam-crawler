\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA direct homage to the Heroes of Might and Magic series, just unique enough to not feel like a clone. The main difference is the battle system, a mostly-idle RTS style (as opposed to the turn-based minigame from HMM). This battle system de-emphasizes the combat in favor of the building / exploration / moving armies aspects of the game.

Hero's Hour is worth playing if, like me, you really liked the HMM series but were disappointed by the latter's complete lack of innovation over who knows how many sequels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn incredibly fun HoMMage that draws inspiration from Heroes 2-7 while being distinct in both cadence and tone. The real-time battles are chaotic and delightful and inexact - troops will die and you will lose, but this seems to be accounted for and takes the pressure off of any one battle while also encouraging experimentation and allowing for comebacks. I've only played one map so will wait to provide a more in-depth review; however, I can provide some context for many of the complaints I read:

1) "I kill their heroes but they keep coming back."
All heroes (including your own) auto-run from a lost battle and respawn at a friendly town. The only way to definitively kill a hero is to defeat them while they are defending a town.

2) "I kill their heroes but they keep coming back... with huge armies."
Hospital buildings available in most (all?) towns allow a % of troops lost in battles to respawn at a friendly town. Also, many skills in the game allow for creature recruitment either on the adventure map or in battle, and many towns have buildings that allow for extra recruitment of creatures at an increased price. That being said, I wouldn't be surprised if the computer cheats.

3) "There's limited spell/ability information."
This is true, and it lends itself to playful experimentation, but I can also see how this would be frustrating for those who are comforted by precision.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBasically HOMM with three differences:

1. Graphics are much more basic
2. Combat is real time based with less control (but some)
3. Games are usually won or lost in the first 2-3 weeks and longer games are rare (about 15-20 minute games)

If you are cool with these points, the game is fun and has potential.
Personally my thumbs down comes from point 3: I dislike the rush gameplay and got sick of being forced into it. You have to do it, because the AI is doing it as well. You will die to impossibly strong armies, if you don't rush.
Rush in this case does not only mean you need to rush buildings and army, but also bonus perks that somehow add to your army (like bodyguard, summons etc.). "Just" having the max possible army from towns is NOT enough to make it past week 3 on most maps.

So not exxagerating here: You really need to rush hard to even beat normal AI.
If you do however, even the hardest AI is too easy. I was unable to find a happy balance of challenge and fun gameplay. You either die horribly before even getting to level 7 heroes, or you dominate the game by week 3 and get bored.

As a reference: There is even a guide for an achivement that shows how to win on max difficulty in 4 turns. Four. Turns.\t\t\t\t\t

Now, in the early game, the game runs really smoothly but I noticed that I had a bit of an issue later on in the game due to having some crazy units in my game. The issue: battle lag from the effects. I plan to mess with the settings to see if there is a way to turn those off/down as, though they're fun, it gets rather wild. I mean... I was the the Order castle that also had Pheonixes and my hero had a curious spec in demonology so there was a lot of fire, demons, arrows, and flying monster in my crazy level 25 hero's group.... casting resurrection and turning all the enemies monsters against them... may have also added to that lag... IDK, possibly.

Still, I really enjoyed my first game, it was crazy and fun. I'm looking forward to playing more games. The fact that the map will be random as well will be a lot better than how it was in HOMM... there was only so many times you could play the same maps in that game before you lost your mind and creating your own took a very long time to make a decent one.\t\t\t\t\t
