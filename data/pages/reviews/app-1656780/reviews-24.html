\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't often write reviews for games, it's just not something I seek out. I did feel it was worth speaking up in this case though. Hero's Hour is a good game. It's fun and has a ton of potential, but that's the problem. While the game has incredible potential, it's still a long way off from being what it promised to be. And that's fine, these things take time. Games are hard to make, and at times it can be a thankless job, so normally I give the benefit of the doubt to developers that are working hard to polish and improve on what they've made.

Releasing a paid DLC for what boils down to a new faction, a few new units and skills, and some maps when the game still doesn't even have a single player campaign or story mode of any kind is more than a little rough. Other people have already mentioned the issues with the Bodyguard skill as well, but the issues are more widespread than just reworking that one skill. The meta of Hero's Hour is almost entirely limited to snowballing. Build your big bad hero with all the power ups and units you can cram into it and overwhelm whatever else is out there. There's very little encouragement to use other heroes for much else beyond scouting and collecting resources.

These are all issues that could easily be overlooked for a game that's still more or less in it's infancy, but releasing DLC for a game before the core gameplay is finished is somewhat disingenuous. I have almost 100 hours in this game, I really do enjoy it. It's fun, colorful, and has a good depth of mechanics with each faction having somewhat different play styles. It's a good foundation to build something amazing, and I still hope that's what will happen. But if the developers are establishing that the core game is finished, and any major future changes will be in the form of DLC, it's hard to recommend it to someone else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent HoMM-style indie game. Fun, good variety, good challenge, enough changes to feel fresh (after hundreds of hours of HoMM that I've played over the years), doesn't get bogged-down in details and instead just focuses on the fun stuff (power scaling & huge battles). There are lots of ways to claw your way back from setbacks (thanks to the infirmary mechanic).

Tip: If you want a really fun style, Kahz Loki is my favorite hero. Rush Logistics, then Scouting, then pile everything into Devour + Dragon. Continually Devour your own Dragons for insane stat stacking (100+ Atttack & Defense). Find a Tower of Mages to continually stack Knowledge. Then find a Brilliant Orb to do one big Spellcasting spec. In that instant you also become the most powerful mage possible.\t\t\t\t\t
