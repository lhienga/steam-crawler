\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I used to play Heroes 3 (and 4) a lot, I was always intrigued by the imagination of these massive battles playing out in real time with all individual units present and individually acting and fighting in front of the hero on the battlefield. Hero's Hour promises this exact idea. So, in theory I should absolutely be in love with this game, right? Unfortunately not: As is the case with many fantasies we have in life, they do not turn out to be as great in reality.

First off there is some clear things not as good when compared to H3: childish pixel graphics style, the extremely basic animations, the mediocre soundtrack and basic level art. I am not a fan of the audio and visuals and especially H3 did really well on those, imo they were a huge part of the atmosphere and feeling of that game. Yet, if everything else is great in this game I would still give it a 10/10, and I have played and loved pixel-graphics games before.

There are some things that are equally good: the town build-up, hero progression, spells, points-of-interests in the level, generated random maps, unit roster and town roster are all well done, even in the cases when their design was changed from the almost perfect original H3 design. it is still great. The changes do offer some interesting new possibilities.

Now to the bad part: The battles themselves just did not do it for me and they are a huge part of the game. They feel chaotic, hard to control and hard to understand and due to them now being real-time they lost a lot of tactical aspects too. The UX and UI could be much better in the entire game but at this part of the game the lack of good UX really irritated me. The units are clustered by type but also they are all individually moving. Ranged units block each other with the ones in the back not being able to shoot but there is nothing you can do about it because it is one big pile. Units attack each other with a lot of floating damage numbers but you get no real sense of what units are doing what, how effective each unit is or what you could do better next time. H3 is the exact opposite: you see cause and effect very transparently since each unit attacking each other and each spell cast is a discrete step of the battle that can be observed individually. To me that was part of the charme of H3. Amidst the chaos of Hero's Hour's battles I lost interest in the game very quickly, although it is clear a lot of love and work has been put into the game. It is just not a game for me unfortunately, although I would have loved to love it\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA HOMM3 rip off with auto combat and a freackload of crazy factions, units and abilities.
At first I was disappointed of this game, I felt it was lacking in some departments
Main issues, at first glance, were the chaotic autocombat and the balance.
After a few hours of play, I realised that combat is not that chaotic, and that the game is oddly balanced, just it's not what you would expect.
In respect to HOMM3 the main focus is your hero, main castle economy is just a kicker to let them shine with their crazy abilities. Every faction has some exploits (at least the few of them i tried to work with) which allow them to snowball through the map.
In combat, selection of units and positions may be crucial, even if you have not direct control of units, first times it looks like a chaotic mess, developers likely knew about this issue and put in the game a bunch of combat skirmish scenarios, in which you select a restricted number of units to face an enemy force, I highly recommend to try this mode if you want to increase your knowledge of units and their weaknesses and synergies.

So, I had a dozen hour of play, I expect I will have at least a few dozens more, I had fun, I think it's enough for the price, but not enough for the potential this project could have had.

Pro
- There is a lot of content, in number of factions, maps, exploration
- Maps are randomly generated thus increasing the longevity by a wide margin
- Factions and heroes are extremely diverse from each others and require different play-styles
- Matches last a few hours at most

Con
- there's not single player campaign
- the game screams for an online multiplayer mode, I hope they'll implement it in the near future.
- please, change the autocombat musics, I dislike them! OST in the rest of the game are nice

7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
