\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game scratches an incredibly specific itch for me. There's a turn-based strategy series out there called Dominions, where you play a minor god and fight all the other wannabes to become the one true god. Battles play out without direct player control: you can only queue a set of orders, and most units have memorable quirks (like the ability to convert enemy units, or invisibility). The unit sprites are 2D sprites set against a roughly 3D background.

To me, Hero's Hour is that battle-system layered on top of a Heroes of Might and Magic overworld. It's an excellent offering because I can play out those exciting hundred-unit battles with chaotic and unpredictable magic without being overwhelmed by Dominion's finicky resourcing & supply system. I love watching my little units get buffed and do their cute little murders! If (as a little kid) you loved watching ants swarm each other, or loved watching arrows advance on virtual battlefield game-shows, this will scratch your itch as well (I know I'm not the only one!)

Each faction also brings to the table different mechanics, enough to differentiate and give an edge (except Pyre--they get 2 different mechanics). Again, this reminds me of Dominions: Decay is Late-Age Ermor, Enclave is Middle-Age Ctis, Order represents the various bless-heavy nations, Delirium is R'lyeh, Pillar is T'ien C'hi and so on.

Honestly I'd only suggest 3 more 'improvements':
    \t
  1. The ability to kit out strong units with artifacts. Maybe there could be one 'pseudo-hero' unit for each faction that you can kit out with swords, buffs, and multiple attacks to make a super-unit. Would be fun to see Fluffy the supermassive canine crash into the enemy's stronghold.
    \t
  2. Eliminate the coin-flip spell.
    \t
  3. More movement options. So far only Pyre has extra movement options, would love if (say) the Dwarven faction were able to tunnel, etc.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've ever played Heroes of Might and Magic or even Age of Wonders to some extent, then you'll likely feel familiar with this game. The core mechanics of Hero's Hour are almost identical to HOMM series games with the exception of battles. In HOMM, battles are turn based, whereas in HH, battles are essentially real-time with minimal control over your actual units, other than to issue movement commands, order to defend (stand ground) vs attack, etc. The real control over battles comes in the form of magic, which is also quite similar to HOMM. Every single unit in each group of similar species will appear in the battle, which I find quite nice. It definitely simulates the feeling of a large scale battle, in a tiny space.

A lot of people say that this game is quite difficult, and it is. This game is brutal at first until you learn a few key strategies early on to quickly develop an army and level up your hero faster than the AI can. After almost 60 hours on record, I still struggle on even normal difficulty unless I'm intentionally cheesing with a broken combination of spells or hero perks, such as making my hero an unstoppable force or having an army of nearly 100 red dragons.

There are also a lot of complaints of performance issues - this is something I too can relate to, which surprises me for what this game is. Its all pixel graphics, yet my computer struggles on lengthy games and larger maps, with the largest pain point being the map "Patience", after several hours of playtime. I occasionally drop to less than 20 fps randomly until my next battle, which leads me to believe its something flawed in the game itself as opposed to actually being intensive.

PC Specs:

CPU: I9 13900KF
GPU: RTX 4070TI
RAM: 64GB @ 6400 Hz

Overall, I really enjoy this game and definitely feel that I've received my money's worth plus some. Here's to hoping for the possibility of more content and especially multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun, lots of depth, wonderful style. Can be challenging or relaxing at your choice and has many cool things you can do and a ton of variety to it. Its creative, love the pixel art, and generally love the mechanics. Turn based tactical play where you focus on building an army, managing resources, exploring, and somewhat tactical combat just really hit the spot.

While there are a few things that are a bit strong / weak and there is some minimal variance to how you approach each run I do not think that's a bad thing, for example things like the Devour skill or free summons being kind of a tier above everything else, and the whole focusing on a single hero is generally the best way to play. But there is always going to be an optimal way to play, and it just so happens I quite like focusing on one dude and summons so it certainly suits me.

Particularly for single player that kind of balance is not to important, its fun to find broken and optimal strategies, and you can always chose to try a different approach if you want a challenge to. So overall the game gets a thumbs up, and I find my self coming back for another game despite thinking I am done with it on the regular.

That being said, I am rather disappointed at the lack of campaign or pvp online or otherwise. Lacking both these features is a pretty huge turn off, and it seems to be a rather intentional choice. With one or both of those the game could quickly turn into something amazing. But as of now it just kind of falls a bit flat. A bunch of different maps, but none of them are really that different, offer any story, or new challenge.

It just mostly boils down to can you find a broken build, pray you don't get assaulted either to early, or by more than one thing at a time. And even if you are only attacked by one thing at a time after you have had time to prepare, you have to just hope you get attacked from an angle you are close enough to defend. You cant realistically spread your forces out as all but the most hyper focused hero will get easily overwhelmed, and you pretty much just cant beat the AI in speed / numbers.

I have beat it on the hardest difficulties and the largest maps as well, so its not a matter of "get gud" either, these are just some core issues.

Very close to being an amazing game, and I could not justifiably give it a thumbs down, but it desperately needs at least one of, if not all of: Campaign, PvP, or a little more balance in how things get to go on the offensive (whether that's you getting attacked early, or trying to rush your opponent down.)
