You will love the hell out of this game.

Hero's hour combines all the best features of the Heroes of Might and Magic franchise and puts an unique spin on them, with a cute and beautiful minimalist pixel art style the game is a joy to look at.

The unique real-time half-auto combat within the turn-based strategy overworld makes the game feel extremely unique and fun to play, the fight pausing whenever you attempt to cast spells allows you to think for as long as you need and aim your spellcasts as perfectly as you want.

I very much recommend this game to any strategy game enthusiasts out there, while the price tag is somewhat hefty, it's very worthwhile during a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt ins't very hard to see that this is a somewhat shameless remake or reboot of Heroes of Might and magic. The game is almost identical in every way with a a few exceptions. The obvious difference is the pixel graphics style, that works quite well even if it is getting pretty overdone at this point. The second main difference is the combat. While the main games runs in the standard turn based pace, combat is more RTS with real time spell use and cool downs. It works pretty good, though it can be hard to employ much strategy other than just mashing the right units against theirs. Larger armies limit what you can put in the field, so as you take casualties, you can send in reinforcements from your reserves. Setting up your waves with the right units is somewhat important.

AI is reasonable, does not seem to overtly cheat, but I haven't played at harder difficulties. There is a bit of a time crunch as the AI will grow in strength faster than you can, so you need to be pushing them as soon as you can,.. turtling doesn't seem to work well in this game. Lots of customisation in game set up allows you to tailor your experience.

Overall a great game, an easy buy if you are a fan of HoMM, and still a good turn based/RTS fantasy strategy game if you like that genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of Heroes of Might & Magic, or just general turn-based strategy-RPG with resource management, heroes, town development, units, etc.. this game is absolutely for you.

I played a good amount of hours on the demo during Steam's "Next Fest", and was immediately hooked.

It's fast-paced enough (especially on small/tiny maps) that you can play a game in an hour or possibly less, but has options to increase the map size and amount of enemies so that you can get a multi-day campaign going. Different types of equipment, spells, unique buildings, hero classes, and the vast amount of units allow for a good variety of playstyles as well.

To my surprise after I purchased the game, it seems there's also workshop support - so that's a huge plus as well in my book, and should help keep the game fresh long after it stops receiving updates.

All in all; I'd say if the trailer interests you, and if you like the game's description and reviews - just get it. I've already gotten my money's worth out of the demo - and I have no doubt the full game will be even better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a HOMM3 expert. I played a few hundred hours on easier difficulties and I prefer not to have five heroes roaming about because that gets boring. I was really hoping to find a fun, modern take on the game and it got about 90% of the way there before tripping at the finish line. My primary issue is that the AI is insanely overpowered in ways that do not make sense. On medium difficulty you will come across AI opponents that progress far faster than anything you seemingly can accomplish so its always a losing fight. I've had a couple games on easy where I squeaked out a win but I still found myself going, "How could the AI possibly afford those all those units so quickly" or "How did they get past that gatekeeper in week 2 that is still "impossible" for me on week 4" all the time. I'm going to keep playing and see if my thoughts change but at this point playing against the AI is just a chore. I want to take time building up a city, hero, and army. Not need to be completely perfect on every turn, every build, and unit choice so that I can even attempt to keep up with the AI.

Take a look at some of the other reviews and you will see similar issues. Why do I defeat an enemy hero with a ridiculously strong army only for him to come back in like ONE DAY with an equally strong army when mine is far smaller, I only have one of them, and only surviving due to bunkering at home? Where are all these units coming from, where are they getting the resources, and how are they getting them 5x faster than me?

Until there is a MAJOR difficulty balance, I'm going to have to say pass on the game. Devs have said they are looking into it but it needs a real overhaul.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game on itch.io and buy it on steam as soon as it's published. I must say there are quite few differences from the itch.io game. Some better some worst. Overall it's a great game. But here are some adjustments required for developer to consider.

1. Not enough info on anything.

Upgrades of the utility buildings are not defined clearly. You just pay and see what it does.
Some skill upgrades on heroes are not defined. For example necromancy definition states " After combat...... Each rank also lowers the mana cost per unit" by how much? Is it some percent times rank? or is it 1 mana times rank?
Most of the skills descriptions feel like dodged explanations. "Amount depends size of the army, mana used in battle, number of enemies killed, number of owned units died...."

Player can have information on their own units but you can't find anything on enemy units before battle. This makes some easy battles lost and hard battles not so hard. After some point difficulty of battles does trick player and it just becomes blindly attacking the opposing sites without any strategy.

EDIT: This was my bad there is a lexicon in the pause menu. Also there is a way to access lexicon without pausing. However I'm not going to delete this part since finding lexicon is hard. There can be an easy way to find it.

Solution: I see that developer didn't want to overwhelm the UI with details. But a simple solution can be adding a in game Codex, Wiki, Library or such. (Like civilopedia in Civilization games)

2. Reinforcement deployment.
It's complicated as hell. You can't just add 2 or 3 units from lets say 100 unit stack. It adds a different flavor into the battle but it is impossible to control what to add to your initial combat army. There is a button for filling up the remaining power. But most of the times it just complicate things even more.
Also on the same topic you can call your reinforcement army to battle but in which order or which units are not controlled by the player. It feels like some random dudes on the bench are joining to the battle when you are about to die. So they come to fight to die as well. There is a bar for reinforcements to arrive but if I can't make the decision on who to call it feels pretty much meaningless.

Solution: Before the battle player should divide the army however they like. Taking 30 units from 200 stack shouldn't be hard. Just select the stack and give an option to player for division. Next, player should be informed on how much reinforcement armies they have. And lastly player make the decision on their reinforcement armies. First reinforcements should include these units seconds should include these units ...

Don't get me wrong. I'm saying this again. This game is a great game. It just needs some quality of life updates. Overall it's somewhere in between Heroes and M&M 3 and Heroes and M&M 4. Heroes doesn't feel overwhelmingly powerful. Real time combat is definitely better than turn based. Spawning all the units on the battlefield makes you feel the chaos element in the battles.\t\t\t\t\t
