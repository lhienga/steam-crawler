\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this game. The music is nice, the factions give you a wide variety of ways to test out how you might wanna battle. The battles are fun and overall, I think this game is pretty fun. That said, there are a few quirks with the game that leave me dissatisfied with my time and money spent.

I'll admit, I might not be the best at strategy games like this, but it feels like the enemy AI on normal is relentless. You make a single mistake and you can hardly recover. The enemy AI's armies outmatch you if you have a single slip-up in the game. And even beyond that, what made me write this review is having all too many runs ruined because of this ridiculous mechanic.

When the enemy AI goes to attack your city, if they can, they will ALWAYS pick a fight with your weakest hero even if you have 3 or 4 other heroes that can defend the city better. Heroes in this game are like commanders, but more importantly, apply passive buffs to the armies they command and can bring in spells to change how the battle goes. Having a hero you send off to take weak camps or primarily trail off to collect other resources without taking fights can be the sole reason between victory and defeat.

This is a massive issue because usually you'll have your first hero hog a large majority of the experience and become powerful. So your other heroes will be fairly behind even with the bodyguard mechanic. The bodyguard mechanic makes it so that new heroes enlisted to help your faction will have better armies based on your strongest army/hero. So it encourages you to try and handle the mass of the land with extra heroes. However, if those weaker heroes die, they will be sent to your city and forced to defend it, even if your strongest hero is sitting in the city. You win the game by going to the enemy city, fighting their defenses, and capturing it. You have to go there. You can't just camp in your city and hope for the best.

What really made me write this review is also the fact that when you go to the enemy city to fight them, you have to fight their strongest hero even if there are weaker heroes nearby, giving the illusion that you have an easy fight.

Honestly, it feels like the enemy AI is just cheating at that rate. They get to pick better fights than you no matter what. I've lost so many games that I might've been able to win based on this decision alone. It's beyond frustrating.

As I mentioned before, this is the normal difficulty of the game. However, playing on easy is an entirely different story. I literally took the main faction's city and didn't realize this until an hour later in my playthrough. Technically you win by taking the city AND clearing out all of the enemy armies. So you either have a boring time taking over the land or play perfectly.

I adore this game. I didn't spend 23 hours to uninstall it and remorse over the money I spent on it, but it's just unfair. Until they change the scaling of the enemy AI to not be so overbearing on normal and not force unfair fights, I personally cannot recommend this game to my peers or anyone else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an alright game, kind of like an auto battler version of the old HOMM games. But it does have some issues that'd I'd like to see fixed.


PROS:

- Many different factions, thus allowing different ways to play.

- Lots of different units, that have different abilities.

- Retro graphics mostly looks nice, but some sprites seem much higher quality then others, which can look a bit odd and inconsistent.

- Music is ok, sounds like HOMM music. However, it can get a bit boring after a while, so maybe add some more sound tracks.


CONS


- Bad/Cheating AI. This is the biggest issue the game has. I will say that the cheating/braindead AI needs a rework, they're far too stupid and cheat way too much, like we're talking Creative Assembly levels of cheating AI here. AI Summoning armies out of thin air and getting free resources. Just make a competent AI and you won't have to resort to giving it cheats. Like if it really must cheat only give it those cheats on the max difficulty.

- Bugs. There's some bugs in the game. I have had a couple of crashes. So Hopefully that continues to get ironed out.

- Frame Rate has a meltdown sometimes. Hopefully some more performance optimisation can be done to improve this. I did see it got better in a recent update, but I think there's probably still room for improvement.

- Some balance issues. Nothing too crazy, but some factions seem straight up better then others. I have played them all a few games each to gauge how they play.

- Releasing a DLC when the base game is missing content is not smart. I read that there was meant to be a campaign in the base game, but it's not there? So I'm not sure what happened with that. The dev should probably finish the base game before releasing a DLC.


VERDICT:

Now, should you buy the game? Well that depends.

I'd say if you played the old Heroes of Might & Magic games, yes. Buy it. It's worth it for the nostalgia alone, even if the game is a bit rough around the edges and suffers from dumb AI.

As for those who have not played the old heroes games, I'll leave it up to you to make your own decision. I mostly bought it because of it reminding me of the old HOMM games and while it's not perfect, it's not bad either.

If steam had a neutral rating, that's what I'd give it.
