When the game is made to favor AI and gives them every advantage possible with fast troop spawn and infinite resources, it's not a challenge. It's just broken.

I liked the concept. I liked the gameplay. I liked the different factions and powers.

I HATED the unbalanced AI. Completely bogus. I shouldn't be getting out swarmed when I'm maximizing troop production from the start of the game. Yet somehow, the computer shows up with a twice my size army when it's possible to only recruit a certain number of troops each week.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a decent game as it is right now, with many things you would expect from heroes of might and magic, and some twists. I'll compare it to HMM2 and say what it does right and wrong, since it's definitely inspired if not a clone.

1.) Is it better than HMM2? No.
But keep in mind, HMM2 is an amazing game. This is also a good game, but it's not quite on par.

2) Why not?
Well, the graphics are a part of it, the factions and units are another, and the battle also is just not QUITE as good as HMM2. I understand this is a creative derivative, but choosing 8 bit type graphics is, no matter how you slice it, a downgrade. The units sort of crash together and are not, to me, as clearly defined overall as HMM2. And lastly, the battle doesn't really work as well as you'd think.
The real time vs turn based is subjective, but to me it would seem that it would be better to stack units and make it turn based because of how the game is, OR make realtime battles play a bit more like a proper strategy game. This game doesn't do either particularly well, and your units stick together and just sort of mosh instead of fight in formation and such, and getting a cohesive line takes a lot more work.

3.) Is there any redeeming value?
Of course there is.
HMM2 is very old, whereas this is definitely a fan product, and definitely can have more DLC's expanded content. Unfortunately there doesn't seem to be workshop support, which would have been a nice feature and made the content potentially infinite, and thus, infinitely replayable, but the author chose not to do that .
However, there's hope, and as more people play there's a possibility for more refinements to things like battle map size and formations, sieges, graphics, factions, and skill trees.

4.) is it worth buying for $17.99?
Kinda. I'm not saying it's everyone cup of tea, but if you could get $1 an hour out of a game generally thats a great deal. Will i? probably not, I see myself playing this for 10+ hours and already i played a signficant amount to feel satisfied for awhile with merely 3.6 hours, but it's definitely not something I will forget about on my hard drive.

5.) Is there anything really wrong with it?
Well no, but the game gets a little laggy on big battles, its a known issue. I think if the author refines a few features most of my reservations would be gone.

I do recommend this, id say it's a 3.5/5.\t\t\t\t\t
