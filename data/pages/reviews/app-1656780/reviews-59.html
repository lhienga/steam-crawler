\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHeroes of Might and Magic ordered from Wish !

The core game has been taken from the HOMM series and that is great since I really like these game. However the real time auto battler is not what I have been looking for. It feels like that I have too little control over my army and what they are doing, therefore everything just clashes in the middle of the screen. Besides putting archers in the back line and everything tanky and melee upfront, while the fast units try to flank, there is no more tactics involved. Now add spamming magic from your hero into the mix , which is way to impactful even in the earlier stages in the game. Since the spells are aquired randomly, you may be getting a headstart as a mage (First Levels) while a warrior hero needs more time to scale (12+ levels). Warrior skills buff your army with abilities and some stats, but you can add other simelar powerful effects with spells, so why bother playing "Might" heroes at all.
Often the battlefield is just too small to do serious flanking manouvers what makes the focus on magic yet again very important, as the units tend to stick around in the frontlines making them the perfect target to nuke them with your AOE Magic, that can be spamed!
The game is faster than HOMM and I like that, but the lack of control over your army in battles, the clear imbalance of spells and the design choices regarding the battlefield manouvering makes it feel somehow like a poorer version of itself.

Maybe its your game, but for me, sadly its not!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHad game on wishlist for a while but it's just bad and not worth the games it imitates. The auto-battler combat immediately degenerates - sprites are about 8 pixels each and there's 100 of them on-screen at any time, so good luck having any idea what is going on once you click "start battle". Any pretense of strategy goes out the window 1 second into every fight. On a more subjective note, the aesthetic identity of each faction is severely diminished by the overly basic graphics and hands-off combat. All fights and units feel the same - you're just bloating your melee blob and your ranged blob and have to make sure your blob is always bigger than the enemy's blob. I'm not much of a graphics snob but the only way this game could be more visually confusing and uninspiring is if it was ASCII-based.

On a much more objective and crippling note, this game's back-end AI is literally non-existant. Everything is scripted to prop the enemy faction(s) up throughout the game. The enemy doesn't have to do any of the things you do; Its units cost nothing. There is no limit to how many units it can recruit. Defeated heroes instantly respawn with bigger armies. This kind of ultra-transparent theatre really has no place in a strategy game in 2022 - even an auto-battler one. You could play through an entire campaign of M&B:Warband using only auto-resolve and get a more balanced and accurate experience. This is beyond basic and shouldn't be used for anything beyond early marketing footage, to actually have this as the engine for enemy players in-game is kind of a kick in the nuts.

Considering the basic graphic profile of the game I'm not sure why it even needs to exist (sorry to be blunt). You can get better graphical fidelity - and a MUCH better game - from just buying Heroes of Might and Magic III on GOG. It'll prolly cost you less than a coffee and much, much less than this game.
