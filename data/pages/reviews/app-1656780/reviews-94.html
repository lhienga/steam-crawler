\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can see how this game has a very positive review from the people that purchased it thus far. Pros:

- It's got a huge number of units, lords and factions
- It's colorful and cute and the graphics are pretty decent
- It's ticking all the Heroes of Might and Magic / Disciples / Age of Wonders boxes, which is a painful genre to be a fan of at the moment. Age of Wonders, arguably the best on this list, came out years ago. The new Disciples is not well reviewed and Heroes hasn't released anything in years

But there are cons to the new things and issues with the bloat, call these lesser cons:

- Autobattler is fine, and I think if I knew the (8 billion) units well enough it would be easier to control the battlefield, but that is exactly the problem. Rather than being a strategic battle where you direct troop and apply spells, really the only control you have is setting up where your troops start and spamming as many spells as you can at the enemy. All the choices you needed to make were on the strategic map with army composition and picking your fights. This feature is actually working against the other pros of the game at the moment and exacerbating the...
- Balance issues. Honestly I don't care about this, I prefer the huge variety over the illusion (and often fleeting state) of parity. You don't get asymmetry without a little imbalance. I think this will get ironed out over time should the team continue working on it post launch, but this isn't early access and its sold very few copies, so buyer beware. I think this will turn off plenty of people who don't want to beta test factions.

Then there is the absolutely WILDLY poor decisions (and things I will not change my review to positive until fixed):

- 3 saves per game... Honestly I would say this smacks of "mobile" games, but with a 2 gig install size (for what exactly?) I can't imagine it's going on a phone
- WAY TO MUCH reliance on RNG, and I would have never expected that. Yes Heroes had a map gen, but it really didn't create the best experience. I've had starts I just walk away from and that shouldn't happen. Additionally, and most confusing of all, it seems the building requirements have some RNG component? I've had a building required for another in one game for it to not be the next. There is absolutely no reason for this. It adds nothing. This game needs some premade level designed maps as well to offset the RNG of the maps

I can't see a lot of hours going into this game and I don't see a lot of hours in the reviews. At 20 dollars with balance issues and graphics like this I expect more from the game play. The fact of the matter is I can get like 3 different Heroes titles, Age of Magic, Disciples and probably other close ones likes King's Bounty for a similar price and I have put hundred plus hours in some of those.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother fantastic game I thought would only live in my hopes and dreams.

Everything (except for the combat) is based off of Heroes of Might and Magic III, and this is a good thing.
While the combat in HoMM was tactical and turn based, Hero's Hour decides to replace this with giant armies fighting each other in a mostly autonomous way. You can choose what units start the fight, cast spells, and sort of tell groups of units where to move though they handle it more like a suggestion.

As a person who has been a fan of the older Heroes of Might and Magic games for 20 years, and as a person why enjoys auto battlers a great deal, this game is perfect. A coming together of genres that if someone had described to me I would have called "tedious and boring" but to see and play it is real treat.

Thanks for planning out my next ~1000 hours of gaming devs. Great job!\t\t\t\t\t
