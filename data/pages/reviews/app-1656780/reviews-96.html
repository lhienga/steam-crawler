\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero's Hour is a love letter to fans of Heroes of Might & Magic 3.

The map exploration and town development is almost exactly the same as in HOMM (with some very welcome quality of life improvements).

The battles are very different. Rather than turn-based strategy, the game's combat offers real-time strategy, placing you in charge of positioning your units and casting offensive and defensive spells. But your minions have minds of their own. Sounds messy, but it works surprisingly well!

The sound design is great. The art style might be polarising, but I've been charmed by it. The variety is excellent across the board: many factions, units, artifacts, spells, heroes, talents...

As wonderful as Elden Ring is, Hero's Hour might be my pick for GOTY 2022! ^_^\t\t\t\t\t

It strongly reminds me of Age of Wonders and Heroes of Might and Magic, and it's a strong successor to both types of titles. While some skills need a bit of rebalancing (demonology/dragon king is honestly too strong), the game on delivery is full of content and has a ton of different playstyles you can enjoy. It does suffer a little bit from optimization, but this only really becomes a problem super late game when you're fielding thousands of troops. Even after having played through a full game, I'm honestly just excited to try out the other civs and see different playstyles. Definitely worth a play through.

Dev, PLEASE add multiplayer that's not just hotseat. Remote play together is myeh and suffers from bad lag spikes, I can't even begin to say just how many fun hours I would get out of this if I could just enjoy it with my homies without having to deal with the mess that is hotseat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a faster pace version of heroes of might and magic (HOMM). I think the strategy was dumb down a bit as the battles are more automated. I have the breakdown of different elements below:

Heroes / Artifacts - very similar to HOMM. Lots of variety for artifacts, sets, and hero skills you can level up. Unlike HOMM, the skills are in a skill tree and a previous skill must be equal or higher to level up the one you want. This skill tree causes 2 issues, your skills are set (cant choose the skills you want in your tree, but they arent random for the good and bad) and some skills are at the bottom of the tree and or behind skills that are horrible. If the bottom tree skills were the better ones, that would make more sense, but they are almost randomly placed between the different heroes.

Skills - they need some major balancing especially the faction related ones. One faction has their 3 magic heroes spawn elementals every spell cast. The might heroes steal units once every 2 days. Considering you can do battle a couple times a day, I would rather have 5 elements spawn every spell cast in every fight than to steal 5 units every 2 days. Another skill gives 25% chance to learn 1 spell on level up, but you automatically get 1 spell and 50% increase to effectiveness if you select pyromancy (fire skill) and it's less random. Bodyguard is a bit out of control too. You get half the difference in power between main army and current hero up to 100 power per skill. Some units can start maxed with bodyguard 5, so just put all units in main army, 1 unit in new hero, and you have a 500 unit army for free that auto revives after every battle. 500 power is more than you can place on the battle field initially. There is also a skill to make the lower level creates change to the highest, so a work into a dragon. At lvl 5 you can be getting 3 dragons per battle! lol Far better than the skill that gives 2.5 dragons per week at lvl 5. Another faction skill gives you ore for winning battles but then lets you trade ore for units and stat increases!!! By level 20 I have 20 points in each stat!

Battle - my biggest issue. At half speed it is slow enough to have some strat. If you try to increase during battle the game crashes right now. Fight is real time and you can stage your units on the field before the fight which is great. Spells and hero stats greatly impact the battle. The issue is stupid group selecting of units and poor Ai. I cant tell just 1 unit to run away like a dragon almost dead and even when they do go to where I say they keep trying to turn around and fight. I cant select the exact units I want to move to the exact location I want like air units moving fast and flanking archers... When the battle starts it is semi auto minus you spell casting.

Units - each faction and tier has very different units with different stats, abilities, etc. There are counters to each unit. You can select ones that have more synergy with your hero too. They did a great job here minus archers. Archers with "long range" are great and work well even when armies are larger. Each unit needs a certain amount of space between it, so if you have a large melee wall in front of a large group of archers, they can still shoot. If you have regular range or especially short range, your archers could be doing nothing. Now the archer skill can add range, but not all heroes have and some are lower on the skill tree. I can see needing a skill to help short range archers, but normal range, that's lame. Early game this isnt a problem though.

Map - basically copy paste from HOMM with some new structures for upgrading your hero. I like some of the new options. Maybe my biggest issue is the heroes can move so far, especially with hurricane, town portal, and celerity. You can be almost anywhere on a medium map in 1 turn. It's nice for speed, but annoying for base defense especially if you were fighting a human. AI doesnt exploit as much,

AI - I dont know how the AI does it, but they will attack me early with multiple heroes with large armies. They have to be "cheating" for resources. I hate how the AI is early game. Late game they arent smart enough to properly upgrade their hero and use skills/spells.

Random complaints - outside of battle I cant see my hero's army power or his power cap in battles. When selecting a hero from the tavern I cant see their skill tree. Many heroes get some random lvl of bodyguard when you purchase and I havent figured out why or how. I had a hero start with lvl 5 body guard which was at the bottom of their skill tree and skills before it were both lvl 0. When I looked at his stats before buying it didnt show bodyguard.

I have been playing it because too late for a refund and it does bring back my HOMM memories. It gets old / repetative fast especially since there is less strat in battles. It also doesnt have a campaign like HOMM, which I loved, but this is also not a $60 game. Worth $10 if you have time to waste.\t\t\t\t\t
