\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMeh is how I would describe the current state of the game. It has potential, but is limited by the bugs and other problems. Looking around the forums like on Steam, Youtube, and Reddit, there are several points I want the devs to focus on.

- Bug and other problems like fps slowdown on large battles, which makes end game and bigger battles in the game almost unplayable, or actually unplayable with crashing problems
- AI adjustments so it is not just one main strategy it goes for every single run
- Control over battles I would want a bit more in depth, even if still kept in an auto battle way. Such as extra commands for units, more control on where you can direct your units, etc.
- Campaign I'm not so big on vs multiplayer, at least for this game. Multiplayer, if implemented right, would allow more diverse strategies, although overbalance would be bad for the game
- Some way to add variety and strategy, something like a simple political/team system. Members you can team up with to go against other teams. Even if the devs do not want more than 1 commander to battle at a time, if other team members could provide reinforcements for you, and give rewards to battles they wanted you to help them in.

- I get that DLC is meant to help fund more development for the game, but sorting out bugs and making the "foundation" of the game strong should have been a priority over releasing it in its current state. Not the only person to say this, but still important

Review rating will change if/when community issues of this game are fixed\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHOMM3 meets Circle Empires.

I've played HOMM3, Kings Bounty and many other similar titles for thousands of hours and this game is hard to put down. It's really addictive and really fun.

Hero's Hour removes many of the limitations and balance issues that existed in HOMM3.

More akin to real armies, your heroes can have as many different stacks of as many units as you like and battles are controlled by how many units you can have on the field at any one time, and when these units are depleted, you deploy backup reserves. This may seem limiting, but it isn't because everyone else has the same limitation.

Heroes Hour encourages you to develop more than one hero by not only respawning mobs, but also respawning artefacts and resources so that it makes it easier to build other heroes.
This is further encouraged by the limitation of OP spells like Town Portal only working once per week and the non-existence of Dimension Door. HOMM3 basically encouraged you to ignore non-magic factions, build one mega hero with Expert Air Magic and Expert Earth Magic where you would simply keep building towns and upgrading your mage guild until you got TP and DD, and then you would win the game.
Kings Bounty also suffered from encouraging OP magic builds, and from what I've played so far, it seems like this problem doesn't exist in Heroes Hour where magic vs. strength seems to be much better balanced.

You'll have enough resources to build your town and recruit all of your creatures every week, unlike in HOMM3 where you couldn't build your town and support the recruiting of all of your units with the town + 1 mine for each resource once your town was built.

I really like the live RTS style battle idea, but it is very poorly implemented in this game. If I had more control like I do in a standard RTS of how I deploy my units and how I control them on the battlefield, this would be much better. This was something I found really good in Circle Empires, and if they did something similar in Heroes Hour, it would feel much better.

The way to access the spell book is really badly designed and flipping pages often doesn't work! I should be able to open the spell book with a key press and have a spell book appear the same way it does in HOMM3 or Kings Bounty. It would pause the combat while I select my spell, and then I would cast my spell in the area I wanted to cast it in.

The game gets incredibly laggy during big battles. It's a Pixelated game and this shouldn't happen at all!!

All in all though, even with the issues that game has that can be improved upon, the fun far outweighs the frustrations and I'm losing sleep because I can't stop playing!! I'm always doing just one more week or doing one more battle and I can't stop!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBad first, good 2nd...

Quite a lot of bugs where the built up amount of units you can hire, which roll over from week to week, resets to 0. Now though, I can't continue the file I've put 12 hours into because it keeps crashing at the end of my turn after the most recent patch.

Design wise, placing and balancing the amount of units on the field before play is not intuitive as you can cut a stack on the field in half, but you can't choose a specific amount to place or remove. Just, place them all, cut them in half, or place exactly one, it goes a little deeper than that but I'm not sure how to word the other more minor yet irritating thing about that specific system.

Here is the good stuff.

If you loved HoMM3, this game is a clone, but with very different yet equally exciting combat. I know I will be sinking many hours into this because I love this style of game, and overall this game is well done and heading in a very good direction...

With all that said, I have to give it a no for now, as it's too rough around the edges. If you love HoMM3, you will like this for sure, but to the average person who is seeing this style of game for the first time, wait to see if the edges are smoothed out in the near future, or you catch it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is a fun take on the HoMM formula with numerous improvements:
+ combat is real-time with pause, NOT an auto-battler (all the people complaining about being obliterated at higher difficulties obviously treat it as an auto-battler and get wrecked by the AI);
+ magic and spells are hugely varied and very powerful with interesting effects (not boring Fireball; Bigger Fireball; Biggest Fireball...);
+ huge faction/units/heroes/skills variety, so huge that I have played nearly a 100 hours and still cannot decide which one is my favourite, everything can be made into a winner;
+ tons of quality-of-life improvements over the HoMM formula (portal spell for every hero; no need for hero-trains to move units to the frontline...)
+ game is still actively supported, new content is added for free regularly and bugs get fixed in a timely manner.

Negatives:
- no campaign, no story, no lore;
- no pre-made maps;
- random map generator sometimes bugs out and places a few buildings that cannot be accessed on the map.\t\t\t\t\t
