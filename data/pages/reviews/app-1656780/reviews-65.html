\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found this game to be so mind numbingly pointless after a few hours. At first it's very addictive, and aestheticly pleasing. The pixel graphics are nice and the animations on such simple sprites are neat, but at the end of the day, they are all just little pixel blobs that reveal nothing more intricate upon zooming in. It's a bit too simple looking for my tastes.

But you may love it, seems many do. I'm not taking away from the possibility of this being enjoyable and addictive for those who like the game. But it's not for me. I am not grasping much in the way of strategy here. While this game is an RTS/Turn-Based hybrid, I find much more depth and strategy in turn-based games like Fallen Enchantress and other 4X titles. And for the RTS side, there's much more genuine strategy to hold my attention in the earlier Stronghold games for example.

In this game pretty much no decision matters. Collect resources. Claim resource mills. Spam create units. More units = more battles won. Doesn't matter what the units are, they are all just pixel blobs that are either ranged or melee, that shoot or hit enemy pixel blobs. Spam click fireball spells on enemy units. Spend resources on other things. Doesn't matter what the resources or the things are. If you have them (and you will), you can get the things. Rinse repeat. Every battle looks the same.

And finally, if you have an ultra-wide monitor, be prepared to click way outside of most buttons for them to work (any buttons that are on the left or right sides of the screen). This issue has been reported ad nauseum in the discussion group and still hasn't been fixed.

My play time was too high for a refund. I can't say I got my money's worth with this one. Uninstalled. 4/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has a lot of promise to it, it's a simplified version of Heroes of Might and Magic and uses some procedure generation to create maps so you have a decent amount of variety between games but the developers fell for the age old trap of AI cheats instead of actually making the AI play the game. Like in HOMM games your towns have a finite amount of growth for each unit type per week, and it's a very very small amount of units that the player gets to play with. The AI on the other hand just spawns heroes with full armies, and it seems to default to spawning its armies on par or slightly above what the player's largest army currently is. If you had control over the combat this kind be interesting but they also decided to get lazy and removed control of the combat in favor of general direction orders and RNG. The Devs seem to take pride in the fact that you have to try to cheese the game to win, and luckily their poor design and coding has left plenty of bugs in the game for players to exploit, but it doesn't make it a good game.

If you like the idea of an AI that can show up on week 2 with four full armies of high teir units against your single army of low tier uncontrollable units then this is the game for you. If not Heroes of Might and Magic still exists and is generally speaking a much more playable game.\t\t\t\t\t
