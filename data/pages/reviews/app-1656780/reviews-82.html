\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero's Hour is an incredibly charming game all the way through. The graphics seem very simple, but are incredibly clean and polished. The battlefield can be a bit of a mess on the endgame, but it's still a delight to see immense armies slug it out. Artifacts with different effects and set bonuses also add another layer of customization to your hero.

A big diversity of interactives, maps, units, monsters and spells keep the game fresh for a good long time. About to hit 10 hours of play time and yet i still see different things here and there. The fact it has a workshop and incentive guides for modding tells me a lot, you can really feel the love poured into this game by author and it's community.

Playing with different factions actually gives you a distinct experience, to the point that i've felt interest to try them all instead of sticking up with my favorite all the time like i usually do for these games. No copy and paste stuff with a different skin, they all do different things and excel at other areas.

I see many complaints about the difficulty being obscene even at lower levels, and i personally disagree. Contrary to some reviews i've taken a look at, which have prior experience in similar games like Heroes of Might and Magic or even previous versions of Hero's Hour, i never played any of these. Jumped in on the Steam release, failed many times at first and eventually got around to understand it's mechanics. Normal is very casual for me at the moment and i have an enjoyable time.

That being said, try playing against one opponent only at first until you understand the game better, then you can try climbing the steps. I feel like the amount of opponents has a way bigger impact than changing Easy, Normal or other difficulties. While you're learning how to play, it's also important to try different aproaches, like having more hero's or focusing on leveling up certain skills.

I recommend it very much. Having lots of fun jumping into a few matches after work, and there's still many factions for me to try with.\t\t\t\t\t

The core gameplay loop is fun but feels shallow after about 2 hours. I enjoy watching when big armies clash but to be honest there doesn't seem to be much strategy or depth to the combat besides hoping for the best outcome. Overall it feels unbalanced.

The combat is incredibly shallow (I don't feel that it needed to be shallow either.) all the units collide and you spam your best spells. The city tech tree is kinda lame, in that you generally have enough resources to build a building every turn, and every building takes only one turn, so it does not really matter the order you build in. The balance is bad, though I think that is inevitable in a game like this, so its not really a complaint, but it is hard to replay a game 50 times when you already know which hero skills are OP. All that being said, I had fun playing.\t\t\t\t\t
