\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm torn on this one. Got it on sale, so I'm leaning towards reccommend.

It's fun, and after I got into it I enjoyed it more than expected. But I'm not sure if I'll go back. The replay value feels a bit thin if you're not playing with friends.

The balance is not great. Some spells are insanely OP. The tags for mobs mean nothing. Sometimes I can win against "Impossible" other times I'll get absolutely run over in what I was expecting to be an "Easy" fight.

The UI is meh. I can't see everything on the screen but making it even 10% smaller made it blurry. Controlling troops in combat is horrendous.

After you get some troops it is much too easy to take over towns and checkpoints. Even if you fully upgrade one, it stands no chance when you get attacked by a hero and you can't skip the fight. This also means you can just take one hero and run through enemy territory capturing everything; so if you wanted to chill a bit and slowly expand your borders, you won't get that here. The troop type also isn't that big a deal. Just spam big numbers.

It definitely needs more than a 2x speed button. The later, huge battles take forever.

AI is limited. They also don't seem to attack each other unless I'm missing something?

All that said, it IS fun. The variety of units, heroes, spells artifacts, etc is varied. The factions all feel different and unique though you can hire any faction as well. I guess the issue is that it feels like every good thing comes with an asterisk next to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game suffers from a serious lack of balance and wrong design in certain aspects. Here are the ones that imo make the game totally unplayable and should be fixed asap before any more content updates.
1. the spells are too op and lack balance
2. the armies consist of single units instead of squads which, in turn, will turn your army into a big blob of 985436827349 different units. to solve this problem the devs should force smaller units, like various foot soldiers, into squads, like a squad of "100 goblins" etc... this way you won't have to deal with your army of 100 dragons being spammed with things like 4 soldiers 2 goblins and 3 flies. it's just stupid.
3. the pacing is all over the place. u will have a maxed-out castle before even encountering the other player. at this rate, there's just no point in the whole building and upgrading system. either remove it completely and give everyone maxed-out castles from the very start; or make it much MUCH harder to build new buildings. like, make the price 20x more idk.
4. on the same note, you should also make it much harder for the player to build higher-tier units. most of a human nation's army should be consisted of normal foot soldiers, with various other elite units here and there. in this game, it's the exact opposite. Imo the higher-tier units need to be more expensive and "less diverse". you need to make it so that the player actually struggles and puts most of his resources into upgrade/unlocking a unit in a certain orientation and even maybe gets punished by getting soft-locked out of outer archetypes.
I usually do not give a damn about a game enough to post this kind of design recommendations in the reviews, but watching a game full of content and with decently active development like this, and seeing it being ruined by such obvious and stupid mistakes just makes me wanna facepalm. hope they fix it. I can see this one actually being a really good game after some proper design changes.\t\t\t\t\t
