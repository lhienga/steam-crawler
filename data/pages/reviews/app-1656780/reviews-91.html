\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has cute pixel graphics and an amazing soundtrack. It oozes personality!

All the factions I've played had something unique to them, both in charm and in strategy. As soon as I got bored with a faction, there were another 10 waiting for me, bringing fresh mechanics which changed the gameplay reasonably.

I'm not usually into RTS, but I might say that I like the combat better than HoMM3 - it's so satisfying to see units really fill the field in enormous numbers. I always play as a magician, as combat would seem lackluster if all I got was unit bonuses; choosing my spells wisely give me something to do. I wish for more fine tuning in giving unit orders; at least, devs should allow me expand and decrease the selection area, so I'm not forced to make ranged units move into enemies. Also, it would be nice if I could order ranged units to attack somewhere instead of moving (maybe that's possible and I'm missing it?).

With standart config I didn't feel the exploration very rewarding - there was always a lot of stuff to do near my base, and resources were rarely scarce. But the game offers some degree of customization and now it's playing much more like HoMM. In my most recent game, I had to set sail searching for a Mercury mine or else be cut off from developing my stronger troops. This is how I like it!

There are some bugs around, particularly with the UI, which I use resized (my monitor is small) but this brings some trouble here and here. Some of it was already fixed, so I hope these rough edges will be polished soon. The AI sometimes stomps you even on easy, but after I learned the mechanics of my faction I was able to counter them, and even win a Normal game without much hassle. It might be however quite unforgiving to newcomers to HoMM-like games.

I hope this game gets fine tuned and I fervently hope for online PVP. I'd surely push it on my friends so that we can play together :3 the game overall is very charming and fun, in many senses even better than HoMM3.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR
Pros:
+Impactful and interesting skills that modify your strategy
+Nostalgia, feels like HOMM3 (I hope this isn't too subtle of a joke)
+Great music
+Enjoyable new QoL features and mechanics not present in HOMM3

Cons:
-Sprite art is only okay, will not wow you at all, HOMM3 had better art tbh
-Some hero skills are much weaker than others and not really worth taking until you have to, this could be fixed with patches though
-Magic focused factions seem underpowered in early game and overpowered in mid/late game, hopefully spells are rebalanced to be more useful all throughout the game because I feel this is where a large bulk of combat strategy is built from (along side a hero's skill tree) and it would be interesting to implement it into combat earlier on when your forces are weak in order to try and win harder fights earlier on with savvy use of magic.

Pretty neat. I will be playing this more for sure. Hoping it evolves into a bigger and better version someday. If you played HOMM3 this will feel familiar.

OST is limited, but most are bangers (the Asia inspired faction town theme is GOAT). Sprite art is ok but nothing to write home about, which makes sense for combat; but gives the game a childish, almost "Adobe Flash" like feel when traversing the overworld. A far cry from the art direction of HOMM3 in this humble gamer's opinion.

Not much to say on overworld traversal. You get exp, you gather resources, you encounter events; it's pretty much HOMM3 with some unique features added. Always getting to see enemy forces and a difficulty rating is great, so is being able to send out units without a hero and caravan external dwelling creatures back to nearest town. Great upgrade to the HOMM3 formula.

I haven't spent enough time yet to really get familiar with the units, but from the ones I've seen so far, they do seem to be reasonably balanced. That being said, like HOMM3 before it, it seems that raw numbers are the primary deciding factor in whether you will win or lose a battle. I have yet to have a hero win a fight when they were outnumbered, although one of my mages almost did once but was forced to flee.

Skills can feel really impactful with some, and barely existent with others, might need some tweaks there to make each skill a bit more interesting and worth taking so that one skill isn't just ignored for the entirety of the skill tree until you can't get anything else (extra hero health and a bit of healing to nearby units about to die did not feel that noticeable, whereas gaining an entire red dragon unit after every few combats was INCREDIBLY significant). Spells did not have much significance early on, however they became utter game changers in the mid-late game. This is okay, but not great since magic focused builds will inevitably need to turtle a bit early on, while the other early game powerhouses sweep across the land mopping up mobs, resources and artifacts, making them less viable to *get* to their hyper magic late game. I think devs may need to bump up spells' early game presence just a bit (lower mana costs or higher dmg output would be nice on the poo poo spells) to at least give them a shot of facing down the early game rushdown factions.

All in all though, this is a good game and while it doesn't completely scratch that HOMM 3 itch, it does enough to mostly satiate.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimple , fun with enough strats and tactics to keep things interesting.
it might seem like the only strat is to swarm your dudes at the enemy BUT its a bit more complex then that. Many battles have been turned with the efficient use of spells and directing particular troops to flank or engage with other weaker troops.
Selecting the right upgrades for your heros is key too.
You want the right upgrades for your given situation. Need more manpower ? then you need necromancy or demonology or recruitment upgrades etc.

The factions are varied and interesting each one lends itself to a slightly different play style keeping things fresh each run. The different nodes on the map provide upgrades , bonuses and access to elite troops which makes every run unique.

only thing I dont like is the difficulty, you need to be hyper aggressive at higher difficulties because otherwise you get steam rolled. I like to build my strength and fortify my cities and outposts but if you arent hyper aggressive the AIs will just take everything and get super powerful.

TLDR: a fun little strategy game with surprising depth and variance, AI could use a little tweak but other than that its worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

Fair warning...this is a rewrite of a review I had before. The reasons that I had for not recommending this game have been removed, and so I am changing my answer. Of course, I reserve the right to change my mind again in the future.

If you’ve played any of the Heroes of Might and Magic games, you’ve played this. What this does better, however, is the very wide range of groups you can play with, and the autobattler. You can start as 1 of 12 factions, and have up to 7 opponents, not including neutrals. You beat other parties, capture locations, acquire resources, and eventually capture the other factions headquarters.

You start out in your own corner of the world, with a small force. You are locked behind a gate to a central location all the other factions are also locked away from. Building up your forces to get through that gate announces to the world that you are ready for the big times, and now that your opponents don't outnumber you by 4 overpowered armies to one, you have a chance of winning.

The autobattler is a nice touch. Aside from troop placement, you have nothing to do except cast spells, authorizing reinforcements, and plan what to do next. The combat looks pretty good, and the troop explosion looks just like fireworks.

You can also build up your hero with skills, bonuses, and gear, although the gear and some spells are random. I like to make sure that I have the treasure found before I move to the other locations.

I also have to give a shout out to AI programming. Assuming that the AI doesn’t cheat, it is pretty well written.

There was one flaw in the game that made this not worth buying, though, and that was the difficulty. With patches, this no longer seems to be in effect for the factions I have played as. Previously, there would be my doom stack (all my units in one stack) facing off against the opponent, who had 4 or more armies, all listed as Impossible in the rating system. Now, the roles are reversed!

I am delighted that the programmers listend to the frequent complaints, and took action. While not as "pretty" as the HOMM games, I'll be playing this a lot more than I ever did them.\t\t\t\t\t
