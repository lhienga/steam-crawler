\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the HoMM3 successor that ' New World Computing,/Contraband Entertainment' never quite managed.

Hero's Hour evolves the game-play by adding more choices with more depth, and having a real-time auto battle system.

It took me a couple of battles before i started enjoying the auto battle system, due to me:
1. expecting it to be a turn based battle (HoMM3 style)
2. Understanding what agency i had i had as a player during the battles.
However, 10 hours in and I believe this Auto-Battle system is a perfect choice to change up the (HoMM3) formula, reinventing it and keeping battles fun and engaging.

Building up your settlements/armies and exploring the map is never dull. Hero's Hour has a very fun world with tons of variety for replay-ability.

This is a great game for those who enjoy HoMM3 style games, but want something new & interesting from the genre.

A couple of things I wish were a little different are:
1. It took me a minute to adjust to the super low res pixel art, it is not bad by any standard, but the sprites could use more definition/clarity,
2. Turn times for cpu players takes a while, it feels like they are taking their turn in real-time.
These are both minor issues when compared to whats on offer in Hero's Hour and I am sure I will get many more hours of fun out of this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero's Hour is a spiritual successor to the old school strategy game hero's of might and magic. Hero's Hour nearly captures the greatness of its legendary forefather. But comes short in a few areas, and finishes strongly in others. To start out, the game and its ideas and systems are fantastic. The talent tree enabling multiple styles of play and character identity is really good. The factions are in abundance and have different play styles. The maps are large. there are lots of different maps to choose from. You can even randomize the layout of maps to keep things fresh. The game is clearly made with love and defined intent on the behalf of the developer. This is all good. However, the game has shortcomings that will not only turn away new players, but perpetually frustrate current and dedicated players. Lets begin with the AI: The AI is terrible. Plain and simple. And I don't just mean in terms of stupidity, it also cheats. Yes, the AI cheats. The AI will spawn in units when there are none to purchase. Making the game unfairly hard and frustrating. This aspect of the AI inhibits thoughtful strategy. There are times where I find myself debating whether or not to siege a city, only to remember that the AI can amass additional units out of thin air, making me worried and hesitant when I otherwise shouldn't be. On top of that, the AI in combat can be buggy as well. There have been a number of times where I have watched a unit or a squad of units stand still and do nothing, or wander aimlessly. And no, they were not under any kind of spell. If there is one thing that needs immediate and drastic improvements, its the AI. Another issue I have is the purpose of neutral cities and hero's. They seem to only exist to further the players experience bar. They're often to strong to fight early and will wander the map aimlessly. They pretty much do nothing. They need to have more impact than simply being a means to an end. Returning to the AI problems: The AI will make incredibly stupid decisions. There will be times where an AI with a massive army doesn't take a siege it would win; or a hero battle it would win. I have noticed on the portal heavy maps that the AI has a tendency to run in and out of the portal for seemingly no reason. Once again, the AI needs a rework. To finish this long winded review, I want to say that this game has been incredibly enjoyable, in spite of its flaws. It brings back a very fond and nostalgic feeling of being a kid and playing the original hero's of might and magic games. The game is made with love, and very much cares about doing a HOMM successor justice. All in all, the game at its core just needs refinements. The systems in place, the factions, the AI, the maps, all are good in their intention, (and some in execution) but all require refinement. Once this game becomes refined, I firmly believe it will be an all time great game, and the best successor to the HOMM franchise. I hope the devs continue to work on this massively potential filled project.
