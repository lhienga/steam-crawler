\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame looks charming and cute on the surface, but is rotting underneath. There are no controls for units in battle besides your spells and choices you make to build units are impossible to tell what's working and what isn't. Is unit A better than B? I have no idea since all battles are mosh pits that give you no information on what's going on. Are those spell you're using effective? No clue, just click the ones that seem useful and hope for the best.

The AI is also hilariously unfair and needs serious rebalancing before I'm ever playing this again. The way the AI is set up literally forces you to play as cowardly and defensively as possible or you will get steamrolled. This is true even on the lowest difficulty. Want to ease into the game and set it to "easy" so you can learn what works and how to play? Want to try a new faction or make fun builds to actually have fun while playing? Too bad. Have fun getting obliterated for daring to not play the most meta garbage available. When the guides for the higher difficulties of the game are literally telling you to exploit the game, something is wrong.

Stay far away from this unless you only want to play with friends or like repeatedly losing until you figure out the only way the game lets you play it. I figure I'd give this some time to grow on me, but now that I'm 5 hours in I regret not refunding this.\t\t\t\t\t

Good arcade style total war game with random scenarios lasting from 30 minutes to several hours. Has some skirmish modes where you can experiment to find just the right counters in a one off battle. Could use a story mode to introduce you to the different factions and their play styles.

The gameplay loop can get a bit repetitive and usually just becomes who can mass the bigger force when playing vs. AI. The only exception to this I found was flame spirits. Holy Moley can those demolish an enemy.

The game also had a few crashes, which weren't the end of the world since there is a save at the start of every turn. But the interruption can break your concentration/immersion.

The music also abruptly changes quite a lot which sucks because it has some groovy music but too jarring of a change from one tune to another (city -> world map & end of turn music transitions in specific).\t\t\t\t\t
