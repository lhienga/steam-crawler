\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood:

> Runs flawlessly on linux

> Aesthetically pleasing

> OST has some impressive tracks


Bad:

> UX:

>> Event dialog boxes and other interactables are closed if you open other parts of the interface. This leads to frustrating encounters, e.g. you can't check your items/spells/other heroes/most things after a choice is presented without some penalty (since interacting uses a movement point). In some scenarios, you can't even interact a second time, losing the reward.

>> Massive lack of information (affects every aspect of the game).

>>> Example 1: Playing Pyre, picked the Samurai hero (Caprice). An apparently unique lv1 skill (Devour) reads: "Devours a group of friendly units. Doing so gives a temporary boost to all units in battle and gives permanent attack and defense bonuses to the hero. Devouring permanent units give the largest boost". NO MORE INFORMATION. No numbers, scaling info, level up bonus, units consumed count, nothing!

>>> Example 2: No battle information, except how many units each side lost. Unless you have several brains and pairs of eyes, there's no telling which unit did the most damage, tanked the most, cast spells, etc.

>> Minor UI bugs

>>> Example 1: in the lexicon, select a faction. Double click a hero to "stick" their skill tree, click another faction, go back to the first faction, the skilltree will be open until you restart the game. It doesn't go away if you reopen the lexicon. This blocks the information behind the skilltree, which is quite large.

> Balance

>> I can't even... I know balancing is very hard. And I know players always want *more* variety/units/factions/... But the latter won't matter if things are completely imbalanced, *especially* in a strategy game. How many times are people going to play a match to the end if it feels like throwing dices around?

> Performance

>> I have a 3900x (24 core ryzen 9). After playing the tutorial for a while, I started a single-player match in one of the biggest maps. Pressing 'end turn' on the THIRD TURN of a match that is supposed to last more than 100s-1000s took 20s. I just measured. It might sound like I'm being picky, but you press 'end turn' ALL THE TIME, and having to watch several progress bars for the other factions is so annoying. I would accept this if I had a weak machine, but that isn't the case. Yes, the actions are sequential, but I DOUBT this thing is properly optimized.

> Combat / Simulation / Physics

>> During combat, the units walk around and collide with each other like they're all covered in soap. Do you remember those slippery levels of Super Mario World, or ice levels in other games? The entire battlefield feels like that, 100% of the time. If units have mass, it's neglected so much I didn't notice it was there in the first place.

> AI and final rant

>> Take this point with a grain of salt, I haven't played enough to assess the AI as well as the topics above. Still, I had an unpleasant tutorial match in which at some point, the enemy hero just kept resurrecting and rushing to my town. He was able to reach me with a very high frequency (every few turns, never more than 5), and every time his army was VERY STRONG (est. difficulty: near impossible or impossible). Even though I won a couple times, I was losing units way faster than I could replace them, but this didn't seem to happen for the enemy faction. I still don't know what's to blame here, but I'd like to have something to look at. Maybe they had insane resource income at this point, with their power only limited by # of units available to recruit? It's impossible to know, there's no log. Maybe there will be some charts when the match ends, but that's just an optimistic guess.

Current impression (with few hours post-launch and no playtime during early access): It *seems* the one(s) with relevant decision making power just went on adding features, but rarely stopped to polish them or even play the game. Many issues mentioned here or in other reviews don't require extensive playtime to notice, but impact the experience considerably.

On the bright side, if the company decides to polish, balance, rework the interface, etc. - taking their time - they'll have an excellent title on their back. I hope they don't go greedy and jump to DLCs, change projects or disappear. Hero's Hour has lots of potential!\t\t\t\t\t
