\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply put, if you enjoyed Heroes of Might and Magic 3, you are almost 100% going to love this. It's a very close hewing homage that basically answers the question "What if instead of the direction HoMM went, they kept at what worked well?". The strategic layer of gameplay with building up towns, exploring the map, collecting resources and artifacts while trying to keep your armies strong enough to take on challenges remains functionally identical. What is changed is the tactics of combat. Unlike HoMM3 where you have a turn-based hex grid tactics game that is all about the alpha strike you have a mass combat autobattler where you have little to no control over individual units and instead just focus on your spells that have cooldown timers instead of a turn based timer. This may be a divisive change, but I would probably sum it up as "Worse highs, better lows". Which is to say, the combat isn't as engaging when facing the most important combats but there is also a LOT less tedium with the average "boring but not quite autocompletable" combat.

It may sound like I am damning this game with faint praise and implying that it's just a clone but that's really not the case. More factions, more units, and quite a large amount of new and fun abilities on units and skills on heroes show the devs weren't just trying to copy the game, they truly love the core gameplay and wanted an updated version. I think they hit it out of the park.There are definitely some drawbacks though. Although HoMM3's graphics didn't age particularly well, the pixel graphics for this seem primarily to be a choice based on "We aren't a big studio and art is not our focus". It isn't BAD art per se, but it won't be winning any rewards and it can actually be harmful how low resolution all the units are because you can miss a lot of details in combat. The game offers you a rich encyclopedia but even with it you are going to semi-regularly just be completely confused what something does more than a few times until you learn the game itself. The music is completely unremarkable and treads the line between being so inoffensive you don't notice it and annoying given limited tracks and track time.

Overall, I would probably rate this game an 8/10. For the price it's absolutely 100% worth trying out, even if you have never played a HoMM game, though if you have played the genre and nothing clicked...well, it's still cheap enough that you can probably see if this improves on the formula for you, but if you passed that's also understandable.\t\t\t\t\t
