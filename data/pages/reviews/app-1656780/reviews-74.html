\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine Heroes of Might and Magic, but fun*

What you lose is micro-management of fights. It's automated and though you can cast spells and direct things a bit, let's be very honest and acknowledge your computer is better than you at that. Therefore, animated battles of pixellated armies.
What you gain is a very fast-paced game that is viable if you want to do a hotseat of six players without anyone committing suicide by the end of the game twenty years later.

This is game is so fast at everything (launching, setting the game, playing your turns and the battles) that you will be able to enjoy it wherever and whenever you want, alone or with friends. It's an absolute win. You might not play it compulsively everyday for the next 20 years, but I'm sure that if you're into that kind of games you could play it for the next twelve years.

(*disclaimer, I love HOMM, X-Com and everything turn-based, watch my gaming list, but for whatever reason these games can't ever be played "for the next ten mins before class / work / sex" because they load forever and turns last longer than Russian invasions.. this one can be played for a very short period of time and it's great)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t30+ hours in, review wrote 14/03/2022.

The most important question! Is this a Heroes of might and Magic clone in the good sense of the word.

Yes and No.

While the gameplay is quite similar, it differs in some areas.
The map gameplay is a lot quicker, there is far less things to discover because if you compare to screenshots of HoMM3, you can tell there is a lot less detail, less nokes and crannies to dive into, this means the everything is quicker, and the maps are cleared much faster. I think it probably has to do with the random map generation.
What it does do is introduce some random mob spawning in the maps, it seems fixed but i am not sure. (fixed when they spawn).

The towns or factions all differ from eachother and the units however and here comes the major issue i get, units dont matter, yes in the early game say first 2 weeks it does but then your hero takes over and is so overpowered your units are just there to be there(cannonfodder). The units are evolved into elites, think of tier 1 evolving into tier 5. Its insanly overpowered.

Mages can use summoning spells to swarm the battle with units and you just look at the carnage, fun at first but gets boring quick.

The talent trees can be seen by for every hero in the map selection. This means leveling up isnt just a random choice of what you get but you can already decide your strategy before hand and as said before, its so easy to pick overpowered heroes.

This game makes me wanna play HoMM3 again instead of the other way around. It has potential but it needs some balance tweaks and map generation tweaks and for the love of god.. more music and less high pitched music, i turned it off after hearing it after one hour. Graphics is just a taste you like it or not, iam a bit on the fence, the lack of detail makes it hard to identify with something.

I give it a 6/10.\t\t\t\t\t
