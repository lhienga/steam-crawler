\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the moment of this review my playtime isn't much (8.4 hours). So, the game. How good is it? Well, it is quite alright. Its main catch is variety. Variety in factions(I think there is like 10 of them, I didn't really count), variety in skills of heroes, variety of situations you can get yourself into. The crushing defeat to what should've been an "Easy" encounter and surprising victory against "Impossible" odds. Yeah, those are quiet possible.


Although, I say that its main catch is variety, many of you will notice its resemblance to Heroes of Might and Magic. Because, while the combat isn't anywhere near similar, the map exploration and base building is almost the same. Hell, even some buildings on the world map look the same as in Heroes IV. But, in functionality they are nowhere near them. The fountain that gave mana to your hero in Heroes? Now it exchanges that mana for permanent elemental units. Of course, it is excluding standard resource buildings like ore mine and lumber mill.


City-building is the same as Heroes V. Your city has a level and it rises as you build more structures in that city. Buildings vary from faction to faction, having some generic structures like Market among them, creature dwellings and specialised constructions, like, the faction of Arcane having Spire of Magic that gives your heroes randomized one-use spells that cost 0 mana that switch in-between battles. Not going into specifics, again, variety.


Now, before we get to aforementioned battles, we need to speak heroes. As in characters in this game, not game series. Each hero is classified into Warrior or Spellcaster. While it's possible to make your warlord into spellslinging menace it is quite easier to make him into generic headbasher. Each hero at level 1 has 2 skills. One faction-specific and one common. Each hero's skill tree is randomized. Let's say some Jodi has a skill Mysticism on a second row of skills, and Kntrulath has the same skill on fourth row. There is much to say about heroes, but I would like to talk combat now.


THE MAIN DIFFERENCE FROM THE HEROES OF MIGHT AND MAGIC. Combat system. It is real time. Thats it... Okay, not really, but if you don't want to read further(and if you don't how the hell did you get to this point in the first place?), then all you have to know is that when you encounter an enemy on the map, you just place your units closer of farther from your enemy and press battle and then it will begin in real time. They will fight and THE FITTEST TO WIN will win. If you want a more detailed explanation then there isn't really one. You place them on your half of the combat screen and then the battle will just start. You don't need to control them much. You have commands like "Defend" which will make them just... stop(DO NOT PRESS "DEFEND" BUTTON), and "Attack" which is a default command at the beginning of combat. You can select some units and "Order" them to move to specific places and as far as unit control goes, that's it. If your hero have spells, he can lob them anywhere on the map. Do read the spells, by the way, if the tooltip says that it will hit "anyone and anything" it will hit ANYONE AND ANYTHING. The only complicated thing in combat is that you often can not place all of your units on the battlefield at once, at least when you have a large and strong army. The game has this ceiling of Unit Power which tells you how much and strong units you can place on the battlefield at one point. If most of them die you can send Reinforcements from the reserve you left at the start.


Now, we come(cue Cyberpunk OST) to the real CONs. I spoke about the not-so-intrinsic combat system where you essentially just watch dummies beat other dummies, but it's not really a bad thing. It just means you don't have to be a strategic genius to pull of some impressive things. It is quite satisfying to watch YOUR red dragon go to town and it is terrifying to see the same red dragon go to town, but just against you. I digressed, but I just had to say it. The real bad thing is balance. It is not broken per say, but when I had to against Horde faction I just knew I was in for a ride. THEY CAN UPGRADE THEIR UNITS IN BATTLE. Only in battle of course, but it just means they don't have to waste their time and resources on upgrading buildings. And oh my god is it INFURIATING to see the almost dead Horde army only to see their shamans upgrade and gain another healing ability at which point it just goes to defeat.


But, talking about balance in combat and factions isn't really my forte. I main Inferno in Heroes III... But, if I can't speak balance in combat I can speak balance in heroes. Because some of the skills they have range from OP broken to useless. I played Conclave and had this hero that had a FACTION skill that tracked some "beasties" which upon defeating should've dropped some trophies. I encountered those beasties exactly 0 time. But, you guess what? He had a skill that allowed him to recruit red dragons after several battles. Which is... OP. He didn't even had this skill that far in the skill tree. At level 2 I already had it. And by level 8 I had 3 red dragons which is a lot. But going back to useless DO NOT PICK "Oligarchy". It raises health and damage of your army, but decreases amount of units you can place in the battle by a whooping 30%. It is too much. And that decrease scales up with level ups of that skill. I watched as my dragons were picked off one by one, because they didn't have the support of other units. Though, to be perfectly honest, most of the skills are just good not too game breaking or detrimental LIKE OLIGARCHY IS.


What about the bugs? I didn't encounter them much, but I wasn't actually looking for them. The game crashes though. Not too often, but it can be quite annoying.


What about optimization? And this is the main turnaway of this game. Whether you are on a big world map, or a massive battlefield you WILL see that frame rate drop. Oh my god, I CAN NOT PLAY ON BIG MAPS?! I WANT TO 1v3 ON A BIG MAP BUT I CAN'T BECAUSE MY PC COUGHS METAL DUST WHEN IT TRIES TO PROCESS THOSE MAPS. COME THE ♥♥♥♥ ON. I played CIV 5 on a gigantic map against 7 other civs and I didn't waste 10 minutes of my life watching the turn bar of AI opponents go from left to right. WHICH I DID IN THIS GAME... Okay, I watched youtube while AI did their things, but THAT IS NOT THE POINT.

TO THE CONCLUSION WE COME(Cyberpunk). This game is alright. While the balance thing can detriment the positive experience and optimization of this game sucks sus it is still a good game. Maybe my rants above have made a contradiction to a positive review I'm supposedly writing, but the game does not lose its main catch in all of those things. I genuinely excited to see how my army will fair against the HORDE, and genuinely thoughtful of my hero's skill build. I play on small to medium maps, but compensate it with more opponents, who sometimes fight against each other. And it is just so satisfying to see two dumb AIs go at each others throats over a lumber mill. Especially if they were "Impossible" to defeat just a turn ago.\t\t\t\t\t
