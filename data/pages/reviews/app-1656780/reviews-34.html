  • Try to control a map covered in fog of war.
  • Capture townships and recruit conscripts from different factions.
  • Get resources to upgrade towns with building, Gear up with artifacts and battle neutral opponents for buildings.


    One major difference (besides the graphics obviously) is the combat. Most describe it as an 'auto-battler', and I do find the reserve system annoying, but the game has you place your troops and give them all basic instructions in battle. Not to mention your hero has access to spells. On the scale of the combat that all makes sense. You are not pitting teams of 5 or 6 at each other, but armies of possible hundreds and a 'turn-based' approach for that (as the rest of the game is) would simply be to cumbersome.

    All that to say that the store page and the reviews kept me from buying this for awhile; It looked cool so I wishlisted it and held off for awhile - not knowing if I would really find it "worth it". I can not stress enough how much actually playing it made all that go away. Seriously I wish I had a demo or something.
