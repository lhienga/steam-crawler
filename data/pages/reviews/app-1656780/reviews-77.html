\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game to death, it definitely is a HOMM inspired game while still having some of its own ideas.

Plus its got features that I wish older HOMM games had, like mixing units from different factions without a debilitating morale boost, converting captured settlements into your faction and letting your hero actually fight in combat.

If I have to say anything negative about it, the game has balancing issues, you are either completely unstoppable or lagging behind with no way to catch up, at least that is how I experienced it so far.
The game also needs more campaign types instead of just defeat all players, they should add a teasure hunt scenario, or slay a particular beast or collect all special artifacts.
And a personal nitpick, have brief descriptions of the creatures, due to the simplistic graphics its kind of hard to figure out what I'm looking at sometimes.

Overall I recommend this game, especially to fans of HOMM like myself, it's still very fun and addictive and has potential to be even better. I give it a 8/10 in its current state.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've very much enjoyed the time I've spent in Hero's Hour so far. It's a fun take on the Might & Magic genre from a few decades ago. There are a few bugs here and there to be aware of, notably when it comes to the music and sound effects (these can switch off randomly during play). It's also important to note that there is very limited multiplayer support for this title. Hotseat only. The game would be much, much more compelling to me if there was a way to play online (realtime or async turns, either way!). In any case, if you enjoyed the old M&M games, give it a whirl. Certainly worth your time at this price point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo for starters, I've never played HOMM, so I will not be comparing the two.

Hero's Hour is a fun game where you spawn on a randomly generated map and your goal is to conquer the other players. You do this by building up your town allowing you to summon units, then defeating forts that block your access to neighboring regions or by using boats to avoid the forts. Heros in this game start with two skills: one of two different skills associated to their faction, and one basic skill that any character could have. These skills then go off into trees, where your levels in a skill as well as hero level dictates the max level of skills on the trees. Skill trees can have some very fun and unique playstyles, so its well worth trying a range of characters.

I've seen quite a few comments about long games being impossible as well as the fact that the AI are too strong. To sum these comments up, I believe both of these to be completely false (with some notable exceptions). I've almost managed to complete a normal game on every faction, and most games would be completed by week 12-15, being around 3-4 hours per game. Usually, my heroes by the end of the game are between levels 15-20. The game can definitely be extended beyond this if you want to, but you would need to change some of the options on the initial setup to elongate the game. Do note, that the above numbers are from me just casually playing and winning games.

Don't get me wrong though. Some AIs are VERY hard to play against. Specifically, I've found that the AI with the starting trait for Mercury Blobs in the Pillar faction often times is impossible to defeat. Mercury Blobs combined with enough time and a Tent can make them able to easily beat every army thrown at them. On top of that, the pillar trait prevents them from dying, so they're a bit strong. If you want to avoid them though, just simply choose all the factions in the game. Its really as simple as that. Tide also has a similar issue for a very different reason: Tide is the only faction that can use boats regardless of if there is docks on a map. This means that they can very easily auto-win "landlocked" maps because they can steal players expansion lands or zerg cities early on in the game, whereas the other players can't due to the lack of docks.

I think what it comes down to is that by this point, people expect AI in games to be a pushover, but this games AI can be very disruptive and hard to fight if you don't make sure to pay attention. You need to make sure to have 4-5 armies, with one primary army and the rest non-primary armies. The primary army focuses on conquest of players while the non-primaries focus on expansion and defending your home territory. Utilization of your Town Portal spells will be ABSOLUTELY necessary to prevent you from getting things nabbed behind your back, which the AI is incredibly adept at doing. I think another issue is that some people don't consider how strong unit econ in this game is. If your hero has skills that give you free units for battles, they are almost 100% necessary for two reasons. One, they alleviate some losses you would normally get during battles. Two, often times, especially later in the game, you can take fights and win flawlessly. This means that the units generated after are basically just free units to extend your army; You want to keep taking easy battles in an attempt to build up a progressively more dominant army.

As for the tutorial, I will say that it is lacking in some areas. One thing that I thing needs better explained is how different buildings on the map work in terms of how they work for heroes and how often they can be interact with. To quickly help new players, here are the three different types of interactables:

One-Time Interactables: Interactables that can only ever be used once.
Weekly Interactables: As the name implies, they can be interacted with by any hero once per week.
Per-Hero Interactables: Similar to One-Time, but unlike the above can be used per hero rather than just once. A majority of interactables that change hero stats or give XP are this type of interactable.

I hope that these tips help all you guys who decide to try this game. This game is actually really enjoyable if you put the time and effort to learn the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor those of you seeing comments mentioning unfair/cheating AI, rushing being the only strat, the game being too hard, etc. I have never once run into those issues besides when I didnt know how to play the game. This game on its surface level is an autobattler army simulator, but underneath the surface is game that I can only describe as a wet dream for me. I play board and card games that rely on looking for combos or how game mechanics function with one another. If you are a gamer who enjoys that, who enjoys finding combos and builds each unique and fun in their own way then this game is meant for you. My praise for this game cannot be said enough.

Here is an example of a game I was playing. Playing as the Horde I used a hero who would launch a wall of minions at the enemy, and every death of my own my empowered the rest. Mix that with an ability to level up your army through combat and at the end of combat you turn units into stronger ones you have one hell of a build. Your wall of flesh and blades breaks down the enemy for your elites to be empowered by the slaughter of their comrades.

TL;DR: If the game is too hard play horde.\t\t\t\t\t
