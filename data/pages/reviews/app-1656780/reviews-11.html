Overview & Verdict
Hero's Hour is a decent strategy/tactics indie game with a HEAVY influence from Hero's of Might and Magic games. I waffled on giving this game a positive review due to the inconsistent difficult and poorly balanced skills. However, the positives outweigh the negatives, and Hero's Hour provided plenty of enjoyment.

Pros
+ Pure HOMM nostalgia
+ Auto battle is fast and fun with surprising depth by selecting unit compositions and placements
+ Bunch of factions with unique mechanics and distinct experiences
+ Many unique and interesting hero skills
+ Tons of spells with interesting effect (e.g. shrinking, evolution)
+ Actively developed and improved with frequent updates and re-balancing

Cons
- Pure HOMM nostalgia... arguably not enough to be distinct
- Skill tree is too deep for the level more campaigns are finished
- Guilds provide too few spells given how many spells are in the game
- Reinforcement UI is difficult to control even when you have the hang of it
- Performance can be poor in large battles or zoomed out
- Campaign mode is effectively "Custom" map, not an actual campaign or story to speak of
