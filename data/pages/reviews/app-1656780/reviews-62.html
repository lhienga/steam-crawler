\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Very good and solid fan game based on Heroes of Might and Magic III, combat is chaotic and mostly out of your control aside from gloriously devastating spells. 11 Factions all with unique mechanics and play styles, adds good replay ability. Game functions perfectly aside from some larger maps stuttering, and some large battles were incredibly laggy, so beware if you have a not so good computer.

If you loved the Heroes of Might and Magic games, for more than just the combat, and this game's art style is appealing to you, you will almost definitely enjoy Hero's Hour. The game functions very similarly to HOMM3, in all but combat, which is a chaotic, live, mesh of pixel knights and gobos, but still with the incredibly fun and devastating spells you'd expect from HOMM3.

This is clearly a fan's game, but don't think this is just a pixel Heroes 3 clone with wacky combat, I respect the devs for changing and adding their own unique mechanics, with combat, skill trees, map encounters, etc, while still keeping the Heroes vibe. This game is designed to be more fast paced than its predecessors, I finish most 1v1 matches is under a hour. Because of this slightly faster pace, you end up playing a lot differently on the world map than in other Heroes games, which was fun to experiment and learn with.

I have 22 hours so far with Hero's Hour and the game still has my interest,I have only tried 7/11 of the factions, all of which have unique mechanics and playstyles. So I would say this game is definitely worth the price. This game has truly impressed me with the amount of effort and care the devs put into it, who are pretty active btw, looks like they're currently working on a 12th faction.

That being said, I will quick list the few issues I did have with the game.

1. The AI is not great (Not that any Heroes AI has been), they will leave their castle unattended right before you go to attack it, AI Heroes will often get stuck and just not move for 5 turns, only to randomly start functioning again, and i'm almost positive the AI have infinite mana in battle, and are just coded to wait in-between casting, making drawn out battles with AI heroes rough.
2. The AI cheats, when one of your heroes lose a battle, they get sent back to your castle, if you have one, and be unable to move or function for 2 turns. If an AI hero loses a battle, the same thing happens, except on their next turn, they will instantly fill up on units and move the next turn. Ex: I finally killed some demon hero who had an ability that let him generate a couple mercury goos every day, I see him respawn at his castle with no units, I hit end turn, and he has 60 mercury goos, along with every unit type from his castle.
3. The game runs perfectly, however larger maps will occasionally begin stuttering, not ending even on exit and loading back in, and I've had a couple character's whos armies got so big that every fight with them was in half speed from lag. I have a pretty good computer, that has been able to run games like Elden Ring pretty well, if you have a not so great computer this could be a serious issue for you, as the game has no graphics options to help.
4. In battle the spell book is accessed by moving your mouse to the bottom of the screen, however if you move your mouse just a centimeter past the bottom spell Icons, you will end up moving the screen down. The game is paused during this, so it doesn't actually screw you in a fight or anything, its just annoying and happens a lot lmao.

Thank you Devs for making another great Heroes game, I really thought I'd never see another.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome!

Wakes a lot of nostalgia for HoMM 3!

I love the vibrant and fun art style as well as the modern take/additions to this style of game (roaming neutrals, WORKSHOP SUPPORT, randomized: map, "town upgrade tree", POI's, etc).
Another, seemingly simple yet awesome feature, is the "advanced options" on top of all that the game brings! You can basically tip the scales towards making the game easier or harder in many different ways (amount of: starting resources, roaming neutrals, POI's, etc. Also Difficulty of: roaming neutrals, stationary enemy armies, upgrading requirements, etc).

The combat was a nice surprise, seeing it at first i didn't think i'd work well, but then playing with it i'v fallen in love!

The game is also filled with lots of wonderful music and themes!

Since this game is very new and still being worked on, it has it's fare share of bugs (but i don't mind them) but even then i am still super satisfied with my purchase of the game and will absolutely recommend that anyone that is interested in this game should buy it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero's Hour is a lot of fun. It plays like games such as Heroes Of Might And Magic with some differences.
Firstly, the graphics are vastly toned down from the aforementioned game...not that that detracts from the experience, of course. The graphics make the game charming, in their own way.

Secondly, the fights you come across take on an auto battler format. You can cast spells or choose whether to be attacking or defensive, but you are otherwise in the hands of the fates. You will get an easy, moderate, hard or impossible information tag at each battle site to help you. Chances are you'll win all the easy battles but they still do vary in strength. I have gone in to easy battles and lost way more troops than I expected to. I suspect it has much to do with the type of troops you face.

And this is something I love about the game. There are so many different types of troop and this is mainly because there are so many race types to play. The depth of choice in this game really is fantastic.
I love the amount of battles you can face. You'll start in an almost enclosed little area near your city where you'll battle to collect resource mines and to boost your armies. Then you'll slowly spread out to meet stronger enemies and eventually the AI opponents. The game is a sort of skirmish format with a set number of AI enemies. I've not actually met an AI yet (on smallest map size) but I've enjoyed the game so far still.
I do love the amount of enemy types I've faced and I love the fact that new armies spawn across the map. There will always be something to fight.

Each hero you own levels up and can pick from a good number of skills to improve their ability in battle. There are also spells and I'm dead impressed with the variety of skills and spells. I've had 5 or 6 heroes in my times so far playing this game and each has been so different. My first hero was able to recruit many new troop types after battles and my latest does a lot more damage whilst summoning elementals all over the fight maps. I'm really keen to see all the skills.

There is one thing I've not liked so much up till now. In my first run I got so many troop types that it made it tough to organise things. When you have a lot of troops it squeezes the bar up. Add to this that the UI interface for your troops is not fullscreen and it can be a bit of a nuisance organising a lot of troops.

What I do like in this regard is that when you do have a big army you don't go in to the battle with all your troops. Some will stay back as reserves that you can send in to battle at set intervals. I haven't done the tutorials (I'm gung ho, man) but I am sure there is a strategy to organising your armies and setting reserves so that you send just the right type of troop in to battle to change the course of war. I've had moments where my new troops arrive and I suddenly start destroying the enemy. I may be wrong. It is something I'll have to look in to.

I just like the reserves mechanic though.

Overall, I am just very impressed with the game. This is no early access title either. It's a full release and it's really rather cheap for the incredible amount of gameplay you get.

Don't be put off by the graphics. It's really quite a pleasant experience to watch your troops battle it out in pixellated form.

Thoroughly recommended game if you like your turn based strategy games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a soulless copycat of HOMM3 with minimalistic graphics and auto battles which is not per se a bad thing. There is some depth in strategy but you will soon realise there are combos of skills on certain heroes that are basically required to smooth sail through enemies. Still, you can get some fun from playing this game for few hours or more if you plan to play with other people. Biggest problem with the game though is a really bad optimisation. It's literally unplayable on big maps and with more than 1 AI enemy. There are no graphic settings to tinker with so don't think you can go around this. I can't even play windowed mode because it's stretches weirdly and I can't set a resolution. Overall I would recommend this game but not for this price, It's waaay overpriced for how janky it is.\t\t\t\t\t
