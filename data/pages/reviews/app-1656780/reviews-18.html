\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like it. GOD I want to like it! But I'm having trouble with it. No matter the difficulty, no matter how much you stack the odds in your favor, the enemy will always manage to get GOBS upon GOBS more armies than you. I don't know man, maybe I just suck at it. I'm willing to believe that's possible. But the game is just so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ unnecessarily hard. The original Heroes III was like that as well, so maybe that's just by design.

Anyway, don't buy it if you are expecting anything other than a very STEEP learning curve. It's a good re-imagining of Heroes of Might and Magic III, it really is. But make sure you know that you know you're getting into a game that will be a lot of trail and error. Restarting to get a good map placement, luck, and trying different methods (Getting gold first? Massing an army? Exploration??).

I'm past the 3 hour mark so no refund and I don't necessarily wanna take money from the developer. I'm happy to support them. Just make sure you know the game is hard. They DID put in a lot of options in for starting with higher resources and the like, so I'll try some of those.

I like the damn game, it's just HARD. :D\t\t\t\t\t

There isn't much room at all to try new things, or play a faction you might enjoy aesthetically. Most of the starting heroes aren't up to snuff either. Yes I'm bad, but that's why I was playing on easy, to learn what I could do with the game's mechanics! But I just can't get that far and I really can't be bothered to put more effort in.

Very aesthetically pleasing art style though, hope things change! I want to love this game real bad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t4/10

Bought the game for 15 dollars on sale with DLC and had to request a refund. I love indie games, and indie devs, but the current state of the game requires more work to feel even worth the 15 i spent on it,

1. Tutorial is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. Poorly explains anything, uses terminology that is not explained, doesnt explain its turn system or combat system, doesnt explain building very well and exploring isnt explained. Due to poor tutorial it is hard to grasp a feel for the game as a first time player. I am sure if i was well versed in these genre i would understand it more, but i am not and it does not make up for that fact.

2. Combat feels bad, unrewarding and skittish. Positioning before a battle is meaningless in most fights because the enemy mobs are so poorly optimized they are able to just "jitter" through your armies to your weaker units. Even without abilities that allow them to do so (Like flying or such)

3, Sprites are poorly designed. They feel like they should be a placeholder for the actual assets and not the assets themselves. Trees look nice, buildings look nice, mobs and units look like bloated colored chunks wiggling around my map. Is it a mushroom or a elemental? Based on looks you might never know. I do understand sprite art is supposed to leave some details to the imagination, i am aware and thoroughly enjoy sprite art, this does not convey the spirit of the creature or mob that the name represents it should look like.

4. Clunky gameplay is the final nail in the coffin. Besides poor explanations, boring combat and undesirable creature assets the game runs like a bad port of a phone game. Menus open and close due to the game registering multiple clicks, pause in combat feels like its implemented by accident and some of the maps are just so poorly generated that you may have to start a match over just to get out of the first few weeks.



The fact that this game is fully released, has DLC and none of these core features will ever be (except performance maybe) rectified i dont think i can recommend this game. I dont believe i am the designated audience for this game, but i rolled the dice on this and wish i didnt. :(\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game, similar to Heroes of Might and Magic. It has many pros, but also a few cons which are fairly major and make the game hard to play past ~10 hours. Overall verdict = 6/10.

Pros:
- Easy to get started and learn game mechanics
- Fun/cute graphics
- Lots of different factions, heroes, units and spells to discover
- Many different skirmish maps to choose from

Cons - the really bad ones:
- The game lags and chokes hardcore
- On medium+ map, if you have discovered > 50% of the map, everything starts to slow down
- In late game, if you have large armies, fights will slow down and can take an excruciating long time to end
==> The devs really need to upgrade the engine AND allow to skip / auto-resolve battles

Cons - the not so bad ones:
- No campaign mode
- Game lacks an easy to consult info, such as character stats, unit stats, army strength (there are ways to get that info, but it's not intuitive and kind of painful to navigate; if you love tracking stats in games, then you'll be very disappointed)
- Music is super repetitive and just not very good

Overall would recommend for a bit of fun, but all the add-ons are useless for now imo, given there is very little incentive to discover new maps and grow new armies considering everything will slow down and your game will basically become unplayable.

If the devs fixed this massive engine issue, and added features to better track stats, I think it would make this game stellar and highly replayable. As-is, it just isn't.\t\t\t\t\t
