\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're like me and you love games like, Total War, Age of Mythology, Age of Wonders you'll probably like this game!

I've played on a couple of different difficulties so far. Only made it through one of the more massive maps once on Challenging. Not sure if its because of the 'low' difficulty but the AI on water levels leaves much to be desired. Nonsensical movements all around. They ignore many different types of terrain like the saw mills and whatnot if there is an enemy presence. Favoring instead to run straight towards you. Leaving you free to pillage on the way to them.

The city building is an important aspect in games like this, so I'm glad to say it is handled rather well. The game is turn based using a system of days and weeks. As you might expect you have seven turns till the next week starts and you can recruit more troops from your cities and monuments. You can build a city building once a day, which in my opinion is a little too fast and makes you notice each improvement a little less around city level 3/6. Each faction has an ability or a gimmick and the more fun ones are the undead.

The ability to use mercury for your soul siphon on undead faction basically is the most downright broken element in the game. This allows you to make thousands of troops and all it costs to make more is mercury (which you can buy with a market) and attacking anything in sight. Pair this with a hero that has legion and swarming and you've got an unbeatable army on your hands.

The spells are awesome in this game, as are the modifiers. The devs know this, so they make it so mana is scarce and regen is slow. There's plenty of options to choose from. Skills are much the same as spells, they scale to ridiculous heights. Occasionally you can get a hero after buying the tavern that has something stupid like bodyguard rank 9. That particular clown hero I'd send around without a single unit and he'd wipe the floor with anyone he came across as long as my main army was still going strong.

This game is above all else an auto battler. If you play total war games with the skip feature on for most of the battles, this game has that. You do miss out on a lot of the fun though.

all said and done
7.5/10 for the gameplay (once you get to a large enough army the novelty of new units wears off)
7/10 for the music (its original but there needs to be a few more tracks)
9/10 hero rpg system
8/10 city building
5/10 AI
10/10 pricing

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo after forty hours of being obliterated by clowns, dragons, and floating starfish all while jamming to a sometimes rocking soundtrack, I've come to realize that I like this game more than I thought I would. Sure the game can be frustrating when the AI suddenly has his sixteenth industrial revolution while you're still banging sticks and skulls together, and the UI can be a smidge clunky at times, but I've found myself easily ignoring these issues in favor of admiring the sheer amount of charm this game oozes. Charm is an essential element of this game. If you don't like the style of gameplay or the art style itself, you will NOT like this game. But if you do, goodness gracious are you in for a fun time.

For context, I have played this game with a partner for the vast majority of my 40 hours. I am not one to speak on the singleplayer experience alone. But if you get a friend or two, some time to kill, and some good snacks to munch on, this game is an absolute joy. The music (looking at you Sawtooth Peak) has been living in my head rent-free ever since I picked the game up, to the point that I bought the Supporter Edition just for the soundtrack alone. The chaotic little minions you have at your disposal are diverse and expendable so losing a few (hundred) skeletons is never an issue, and I am still encountering enemy types that thoroughly bamboozle me.

For someone who was just looking for a cute strategy game to play during the off-days with a friend, I was pleasantly surprised by this. You will have to overlook a few flaws, that much is true, but do I believe those issues outweigh the positives? Hell no. The game's current review rating, mostly positive at the time of writing this review, surprises me greatly. I've gotten more joy out of this game than I have out of many of the recent releases in the past handful of years. So play it! Try the demo if you're on the fence, but give this game and the devs plenty of love <3
