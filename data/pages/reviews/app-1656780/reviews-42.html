\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only recommend this game with much hesitation, because it's priced low, and because I think HoMM also had a lot of issues. I think it's great the devs attempted to reinvent HoMM in a more concentrated way, and the work on factions is great, they really feel and play differently. However I have some deep issues with some core components of this game.
1) Difficulty: the AI seems to develop better than the player, no matter what (I think it cheats pretty significantly, even on normal difficulty), and you're essentially playing catch-up until you find one of the completely OP mechanics on the map, and then you can roll over the AI entirely. It does not feel like you get to apply your wits much, the gameplay basically goes from surviving through build and resource optimization (and taking what fights you can walk away from relatively unscathed), to beating the AI and the environment (fights rated impossible become a breeze) entirely and without any more challenge. Huge space for improvement here.
2) Fighting system: you can rarely deploy your whole army, which makes a battle actually a string of small battles than one big fight. This makes for long and fairly passive/repetitive stretches of play, apart from spells and some direction you can give your army, as you're just watching variations of the same battle otherwise. This results in frustrating attrition levels even during "easy" battles (some of which can and will wipe out your army). Some unit and hero abilities can break this somewhat, by raising the number of troops you can field at once, or getting enemy units to fight for you, The tactical aspect, beyond unit mix and initial placement, is not very present.
3) Artifacts: in 25h of play, I think I maybe could get 3 pieces of an artifact set once. This feature just does not work. Even with the plunder hero ability that spawns artifacts after battles, you might get 10 copies of an "ultimate" level artifact during a playthrough, but you'll be stuck with 3 incomplete sets that you will never find the 3rd item for. Artifacts also become fairly anecdotal in power once you find an OP building to abuse.

The city and resources management are ok, the special buildings on the map are also interesting, with a few that can be abused to deliver overpowered results. Abusing such buildings is the single most powerful mechanic to get a win in this game, and by far. I am pretty sure this isn't what the devs set out to achieve. It would be great if the core gameplay could provide opportunities to make meaningful decisions and get ahead rather than relying on what feels like exploiting a design loophole.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy review would be neutral, but more on the negative side of it, and here's why.
I really just wanted to test this game out, because I'm more of a strategy game player myself. And looking into this game, showcasing some Heroes of Might and Magic tactical with some RTS features, I thought I needed to give it a try.
I don't think 2h was enough to test it out, but here I am at 4h and :
- I think it's a refreshing game by its design, new items, new factions, etc... it's a good change from old HOMM, even though the similarities are numerous..

BUT

- AI: It's clearly mentioned that the AI is cheating. I simply played a large map with 3 players (2AI). One was done by week 4, the other one, even though I'm currently stealing all of his resources generators, just popped up an "Impossible" army outside of his castle, although I have 2 heroes level 15+, and last time I checked, I could have taken his castle for easy
- The RTS component is clearly gross. Besides macro actions such as Attack, Defend, Flee, there isn't much to actually do on the battlefield. You can move units, but only in chunks depending how close they are. You can't really move the units you want to move.
- Moving those units will just make them move. Don't expect them to move and hit.
- So HOMM still has more strategy than Hero's Hour.

So it's a very pale copy of HOMM as it is right now. I think this game has a lot of potential, and hope the developer(s) will take those reviews into account for next updates!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to give this game a bad review so much its insane, but for some reason I can't.

The AI is bad. Soooo bad. Not even because they are bad in the auto battler sequences, because they aren't the best there. It's that once you've defeated the AI's best hero just one time the game is over because they keep blindly send their hero's towards your territory to their slaughter over and over again until they are laughably weak. But somehow the rest of the game is able to overcome its unfathomably stupid AI.

The factions are packed with flavor, each one is worth at least a couple of games each because each faction has two different classes of hero's within it. The skills your hero learns are very impactful and unique, and the auto battler sequences have more strategy involved than it would seem at first glance.

The foundation of this game is great, it just needs polish and some BIG fixes to AI, but even as it stands I would say it is worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t
