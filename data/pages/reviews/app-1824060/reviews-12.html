\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing Warhammer since the first. I have played every single faction and most campaigns. I have to say that my current campaign as Valkia the Bloody is the most fun campaign I've EVER had. I always play on Normal difficulty, so this is the first time that I've actually really strategized in a Total War game. The AI is really aggressive, but that makes it a lot more fun. Most campaigns get boring around turn 60 after you get really powerful and no one can challenge you anymore. But I'm now at turn 100 and the AI is still finding ways to screw me. I love it.

Champions of Chaos get a bunch of quality of life improvements. I feel a lot more attached to my lords now because you can send them through different phases until they eventually reach demonhood. While this already existed in Warhammer 3, it's a lot better here because it takes longer to unlock and the rewards feel more powerful. You can also upgrade your units with experience as well instead of just ditching them once you unlock a higher tier, which is a feature I'd like to see for other factions as well.

A lot of people have been complaining about the price of the DLC. It's close to the same price as a race dlc, even though it doesn't have close to the same amount of content. It's kind of like a double lord pack, but I would say that it has less content for each individual lord than a typical lord pack does. Usually in lord packs, each lord has their own mechanics, but each lord more or less has the same variation of new and existing mechanics in this dlc. So you could even argue that it has less content than a normal lord pack.

Regardless, I have been having a ton of fun with it and I love the new update. If you're new to the game, you might want to try out some other factions first though and wait for this one to go on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest player reviewing best DLC of RESKINS
Champions of Chaos is a DLC that reskins a number of already existing units in the Warriors of Chaos race DLC. All of the following units are reskinned 2-4 times: Marauders, chaos warriors, chosen, chaos lords, chaos sorcerer lords, exalted heroes and sorcerer heroes. The DLC adds Daemon prince lords (new lord) and Chaos war shrines (new unit). It also comes with 4 new legendary lords, which is a large focus of the DLC. I have played all the new legendary lords and existing ones.
I would recommend this DLC only if you're a fan of the warriors of chaos and if you're a collector who already bought the majority of DLCs of Warhammer, then might as well get this one too. It is better than a number of DLCs released before.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA bit of a mixed bag. You get 4 new legendary lords, all of which are fun and cool. It introduces a few new mechanics involving warbands and such - also fun. It will also improve the monogod roster a bit, making lords like N'Kari better late game. A new goal in the main campaign (I have not completed this since I have been playing immortal empires).

The reskinned units do have slightly different stats and voicelines, but animations and everything else are the same. Asking $16 for this is far too much - I would only recommend it at a discounted price.\t\t\t\t\t

Total War: WARHAMMER III - Champions of Chaos
A very good DLC, that adds as many as four new LLs for each of the Chaos Gods, along with improving the mechanics of playing Chaos Undivided. Moving on to the DLC, each of the new Legendary Lords has some really interesting abilities, gear, and ways to play. Also worth mentioning are the new units added for each Chaos God (while the units are similar, there is enough variety here), and the change that allows Chaos Undivided to conquer cities, and while most of them are useless and mechanically similar to Wood Elf cities, Chaos can create truly massive, dark fortresses in specific places on the world map (accordingly to Warhammer Fantasy lore). The LLs from Warhammer I, have also been improved, and they play really well, not to mention the fact that Be'lakor came in the form of a playable faction that uses all the new features, and more. There's also a change in recruiting where we can develop (or "evolve" if you prefer) units like Pokemon using the experience gained by those units. All in all, it's a really solid DLC with a lot of content, and the price is not that bad.
