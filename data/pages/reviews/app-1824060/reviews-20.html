\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the DLC being mostly remodels (only the Chaos Warshrine is a new model) it offers plenty for the asking price.

First of all it fills the holes of the daemonic rosters. Nurgle can recruit armored Chaos Warriors,Knights and gets regeneration from his chaos shrine. Slaanesh gets a decent anvil with warriors and chosen units to his incredible hammer. Tzeentch gets a frontline to protect his ranged units and Khorne gets more killy stuff. This improves the gameplay and makes the armies of the Chaos Gods more flavorful.

This DLC also funds the Warriors of Chaos rework and it's quite massive. The daemonic gifts, path to glory, daemonhood, the entire warband mechanics (basically your marauder evolves into Chaos Warriors and higher), new daemonic fortresses, marking your units and lords to create god flavored armies brings lots of freshness to the old WoC faction

The 4 lords share the new WoC mechanics with an added twist coming from their respective deity. Azazel gets hefty bonuses with order factions to make seducing them easier. Festus gets his own uniqe plagues. Vilitch gets his own Changing of the Ways and Valkia her own version of bloodletting. The new campaign is also a welcome change from the Realm of Chaos race.

Overall I feel that this DLC gives you a good investment return. Lots of new units, new heroes and lords bring much needed faction adjustments and the 4 new Legendary Lords offer different starting positions and challenges.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAdds a decent number of much needed units and mechanics to anorexic daemonic factions. Chaos undivided and the various servants of singular gods are now more distinct, along with a broader long awaited overhaul. Your everyday lord can transcend to daemonhood and are now more than a bunch of wandering hordes, able to project power from fortified cities.

Daemon factions themselves range are still a bit ♥♥♥♥. While getting a bunch copypasta warriors, with a bit of a Dark God's flavor which they should've had from the start, they remain far weaker. Since the Warriors of Chaos rely on a limited number of settlements that can be used as Chaos Fortresses, they get a decent income and small buffs from those forts. But the Daemon factions which can fully utilize any settlement, they mostly get screwed.

Having the same settlement mechanics as typical factions, while being expected to make their income from fighting, makes many incredibly difficult to simply survive as. As daemon factions are expected to protect and expand their territory—hated by most factions, thus constantly being attacked. Tzeentch for instance can hardly maintain a single starting army while controlling two provinces.

All while as a Warriors of Chaos faction, you can safely leave a chaos fortress and go on a rampage. Recruitment is instantaneous, only improved by the passive increase of chaos corruption, and veteran units can be upgraded to their stronger counterparts at a click. They don't require an ounce of infrastructure to field an endgame army, yet daemons practically need to construct a functioning society with plumbing!

Long overdue, with work yet to be done, but a welcome upgrade for the original game's first DLC faction. Now if only they'd fix the ones that came with the base game.\t\t\t\t\t
