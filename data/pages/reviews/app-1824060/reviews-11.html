About free update for WoC
It's a good update...for those who don't want to think about the economy, about anything but fights. Personally, I get bored quickly. And for the endgame it's pain in the ass because you just can't recruit strong units in your army, you need to upgrade them again and again. And if the gameplay for the Archaon, Kholek and Belakor may be fun, because you can make 5 different armies, 4 for each God and 1 undivided, gameplay for the dlc character is boring af because you can go only for undivided or 1 Chaos God
About new dlc
Lords
So you have a really famous characters who represent his God. If we take these characters as units on the battlefield - they're awesome. Their models are good (rip Azazel's hair) and they're really strong. But, playing as one of them on global map? They have nothing to entertain you. Even Vilitch doesn't have a good changing of ways mechanic, it's just a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ version of it. They don't have new mechanics, just ♥♥♥♥♥♥ versions of Monogod mechanics. If you could play them for their Monogod faction, it would be really cool, but you can play them in boring WoC faction only. It doesn't make sense why you can't confederate Vilitch playing as Kairos etc.
Units
And there you have new...reskins. With this dlc you'll get the same mortal units marked for each Chaos God. Do they look cool? Yes. Do they have differences? Yes and no. They're the same units, but some have more or less melee defence, attack, or some kind of magic damage. I'll tell you that, if you like playing monogod factions - this dlc mustbuy for you. Because you'll get these units for monogod factions too, and these factions need them a lot. I can't imagine now how I can play Kairos or N'Kari without my front line of mortals. It was painful to play them before without mods. Some units just broken (Units with hellscourges, they have 0 armor piercing damage). And you'll get access to mortal lord which can evolve into the demon prince too, but a WAY easier than WoC do this. If playing as WoC you must reach 30 lvl and get 10k souls for this ascendance, for the monogod factions it's just like...hit the lvl 15 and you can get it...yeah, that easy. And with this dlc you'll get 1 new unit, chaos warshrine which is cool, but seriously, one new unit for the dlc?
Conclusion
So we've got reskins, 1 new unit, 4 badass LLs but with no mechanics for the price of a race pack. It's not worth it at all. If the price was lower - yes, for those who love chaos faction it's must have, at least for the monogod factions. But this just don't worth the price. I still want this type of dlc in the future for the Vampire counts, Empire, Dwarfs, Norsca but for this time with new mechanics\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst I will say that this isn't a bad DLC and if you are a fan of the Warriors of Chaos then you should definitely grab it. I have decided to leave a negative review because it's a DLC format I do not wish to see again, at least in its current form.

But first let's start with the positive:
- The new LLs are amazing, the animations, the voice acting, the models, everything about them is so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cool. Hearing Vilitch taunting Thomin during the campaign was a pleasure to my ears.
- There are a lot of new units allowing you to build very thematic armies.
- CA outdone themselves with the Warriors of Chaos rework, the Dark Fortresses, the Gifts of Chaos, the Warband, it's all thematic and super fun to use.
- Their unique campaign in the search for Zanbaijin is alright, the final battle was fun but the campaign is a bit short.

Now let's talk about the bad things and I do have a lot to talk about:
- The new LLs should have been added in their respective Monogods in my opinion. I don't really see the point of creating four distinct races that are a mix of Daemons and Mortals all aligned to each Dark Gods if you're not going to give them all their aligned characters. The worst one here is probably Azazel as he is a Daemon which explicitly lead armies of Daemons in the lore, he is the Commander of Slaanesh's Daemonic Legions. At least being able to chose if you start a campaign as WoC or as Monogods with them would have been nice, we get this choice in custom battles so why not in campaign ?
- This time there are no unique mechanics for each LLs (think of stuff like Ikit's Workshop, Eltharion's prison, etc), to me it's quite a step back in terms of quality. Almost every single lord packs has added unique mechanic for the new LLs, they add a huge replayability and it's a real shame we don't have any this time. Only thing unique about the LLs is that they are borrowing a weaker version of the Monogods mechanics, I'm not a fan of that.
- There are a lot of units.... but almost all of them are reskins. I understand that CA couldn't make entirely new models for every units of the DLC since there are so many but I do wish at least the generic lords and heroes would have unique assets, they only get new helmets, capes and weapons. The same goes for the Warriors and Chosen, I would have liked new pauldrons for them at least. Some units like the Forsaken are only recolours, generic Daemon Princes are just using Daniel's assets. I wouldn't mind the recolours if they were free but for paid content it's not really good imo but again given the amount of units I get it. If there is one faction where there is a massive potential for very unique and creative visuals it's Chaos so only doing reskins with a few unique assets is such a missed opportunity imo.

So as a conclusion I will say that I liked this DLC, it's a solid one but it's not something I wish to see again. This new format of lord pack is a step back imo, we have a lot more quantity yes but at the cost of quality and personally I want to go back to the classic two LLs lord packs, my biggest issue here is the lack of any unique mechanics. I would rate it 6/10, it's decent but could be a lot better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I could give this a middling review instead of an up and down thumb I would; but I have to lean negative on this one.

The Champions of Chaos DLC adds some much needed love to previous Warriors of Chaos lords and the new Chaos/Chosen units for the current Monogod factions are a welcome addition to their roster.

However, the content that's intended to be highlighted here is the 4 new legendary lords - each dedicated to their own god. And these lords, while unique and having some fun gimmicks, don't have the fun and the staying power of the reworked and older lords.

Older Chaos lords can pick and choose between all four chaos god units and gifts whereas the new lords must be dedicated to one. The problem is a limited and singular focus on one god gives you a roster that isn't exactly developed.

Once more time has passed and new units added to these four new lords perhaps they will be more fun to play. Right now, however, they're just a slog. If you really want to play the reworked older Warriors of Chaos lords and love the monogods this would be worth a purchase. As I said before, however, the content being highlighted here is the four new lords; and they just aren't fun to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr I honestly kinda like it, but much like the patch it debuted alongside it currently feels like it's in beta and you should avoid it unless you're planning on doing an Archaon or Be'lakor run in the near future. Or you REALLY like one or more of the included characters.

Before I begin: I'm reviewing JUST this DLC, I'm only going to acknowledge the accompanying Warriors of Chaos rework in passing.

When Creative Assembly announced Immortal Empires, they were very upfront about it being a beta. I appreciated the honesty, really. Much better to expect bugs, unfinished stuff and eccentricities beforehand rather than have them ambush us. And to their credit, IE is very well polished!

However, they also announced that this DLC would drop alongside IE. I was excited because I love the Warriors of Chaos and it has two of my favourite lords from the fluff (Valkia and Azazel respectively).

Thing is, this DLC feels like it's in beta too, but unlike IE they gave no indication that this'd be the case. I honestly can't tell if it IS in beta or if it's just shoddily made, either.

As far as visuals, presentation and combat effectiveness go all of the titular Champions are GREAT. They add much needed personality to Chaos as a collective and they're a joy to watch and listen to. It's really hard to fault them, you can tell their designers were big fans of them and it really shows.

But that's where the unambiguous positives end.

When this game came out, one of my prevailing thoughts was "Hmm, it feels weird that Chaos is split up into five separate factions". With IE now out, it's six, and that feeling has only grown. For future readers' sake, you should know that this DLC dropped alongside a pretty significant rework to the Warriors of Chaos. Naturally, the Champions are bundled in with this faction which means they share mechanics with Be'lakor, Sigvald, Kholek and Archaon the Everchosen.

Thing is, I don't think they should've been.

The mechanics given to the WoC are pretty clearly intended for Chaos Undivided, but they've attempted to shoehorn monogod Champions into this framework. It's not quite square peg-round hole, but having tried all four Champions there are some pretty noticeable issues that pop up.

First of all, there's the units. Most people will say that they're reskins because... they are. No debate. I actually LIKE the reskins, conceptually; having thematic armies is great as Be'lakor or Archaon given that they prevent any one Lord falling off due to the new Authority system. Over time, you eventually have numerous lords leading various godly warbands around the world. It's a cool system and it gels well with the Undivided lords.

Despite this, I have three major issues with the new units.

The first is that they don't really align very well with the archetypes of each specific god and feel very samey. Slaanesh units don't get much of a speed buff, Nurgle units slow down but don't get enough health to offset how much damage they'll take approaching ranged units, Khorne units are a bit stronger in melee but ultimately gain no traits to make them worth using over Bloodletters, and Tzeentch units are ultimately just normal Chaos units with barriers. Of these four, the first two suffer the most - Slaaneshi units are actually slower on average than the numerous daemons you'll be getting from Gifts and Nurgle units really suffer that speed reduction because of how minimal the other stat increases are.

I'm also not sure where to slot this in, but the new Hellscourge units have zero AP and are mostly useless unless you end up fighting Helman Ghorst or any other lord who fields 0 armor units regularly. There are mods on the workshop that either give them AP or binding abilities and it helps massively.

The second issue is that there's no real feeling of DEVOTING to a lord. The stat differences between god units are minimal at best and oftentimes the only ones that matter end up mattering in the worst way possible. If you're playing Undivided there's no real reason not to commit to full Tzeentch armies given that their lords get two good Lores and all of them have barriers which are STILL obscenely useful - especially in sieges!

Speaking of lords and heroes, this is where the third issue comes in: Chaos Lords and Sorcerers can only only devote to two gods, and they're different between Heroes and Lords. The Chaos Lord can ascend to be a Chaos Lord of Khorne or Slaanesh. If you want a non-caster lord of Nurgle, too bad. Only the Chaos Hero can ascend to Nurgle - and Khorne, so no Slaaneshi melee hero. Tzeentch doesn't even GET a melee lord or hero. I know it might sound weird to demand more superfluous reskins when I just complained about superfluous reskins.

"But that's just the units, how about the actual Champions?"

In a vacuum they seem good, and as individual units they're incredibly strong. The biggest problems come from the mechanics being Undivided centered. See, WoC lords recruit units from warbands in the local province. Each unit has a % chance to be put into the pool up to a cap. The Champions in this DLC get numerous bonuses to recruitment cost reduction, which only get better as you go along. Problem is, a lot of WoC buildings, heroes/lords and traits output Chaos Undivided corruption. If this reaches a certain point, it applies a -5 growth to other corruption - including Chaos God corruption. This, in turn, stops you recruiting your specific units. Sure, you can recruit Undivided units and convert them immediately, but this ultimately does cost favour and if you're in an economic slump with no good plunder targets, you'll start to feel the inability to benefit for your recruitment cost buffs.

Oh and Valkia - the Khorne Legendary Lord in this pack - can recruit Sorcerers. Yeah. I think that says it all.

Ultimately, this DLC feels like a beta for the WoC rework that got put out a bit too early. The Champions are a bit jank and honestly would've been better under the Monogod factions given how WoC is clearly meant to be Undivided. Most of the "new" units are entirely lacking in identity and feel like set dressing and ultimately there's nothing truly NEW in this pack because even the LLs use recycled mechanics from their Daemon equivalent.

...You might be wondering why this review is positive, despite all the negativity.

It's simple: Everything in this pack is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ great if you play Archaon or Be'lakor. As I said above, having a unified force FEELS great, and Archaon especially feels like an actual unifying force - especially once you start confederating every other WoC faction. Taking a bunch of random Norscan marauders and turning them into an elite fighting force augmented by Daemons and powerful ascended Lords is just sheer excellence, WoC really shine after two whole games of being an absolute joke. This is first and foremost an augment DLC for the two aforementioned LLs, but boy does it shine as that.

Do wish there were Marauder archers or something though, this faction really hurts for ranged units.
