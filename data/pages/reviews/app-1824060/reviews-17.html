\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis will be a review based off the fact that i gave each new character a go for around 3-6 hours each so will define it as each faction in order played and an overall perspective, based in the immortal empires map (beautifully done btw) on very hard campaign

Vilitch (Tzeentch) : Starting north at the very far right side of the map above Cathay, the curseling gets a pretty decently easy start as one of the first territories you conquer also allows you to end up vassaling the nearby norscan tribe and by expanding westward, could even vassalize archaeon and kholek to join your side as allies if defeated. As for his playstyle, you get a minor ability to manipulate fate and magic on the campaign map but unlike Kairos, most uses are lesser base such as scouting territory, stealing winds of magic, or occassionally breaking an alliance of another. In battle, magic plays a strong role and the use of having alot of magical base troops alongside some pink horrors does give quite the advantage at melee or ranged, as a lord itself, can be quite tanky, not known for fighting as much as being an armored tzeentchian mage but holds its own pretty well

Valkia (Khorne): Starts north of the dark elves territories, as a faction it doesn't get much uniqueness that hasn't already existed from Skarbrands faction which is the Bloodletting bonus, however as a character, probably the toughest of the 4 to take down, on top of using a spear/shield combo, her item can grant invulnerability to damage for a short time and does pretty well against multiple types of foes. army wise its khorne, war and honour, masters of melee, hate magic. what makes this one more interesting is that there is a nearby sea lane you can take to take a trip to the lizardmen to begin a mini province there as well as allying with dark elves. so it broadens the range at which you can battle and conquer as thats what khorne is about

Festus (Nurgle): Begins in brass keep surrounded by Empire factions, this faction gets probably the best mechanic of the 4 being to create plagues and concoctions of different potions similar to Ku-gath but altered to his own strange ways. As a lord, has access to spells and going with that is a few abilities he can have active at all times, couple examples are constant aoe damage to units around or constant healing to allies around. while he's not a fighter, as his name leechlord implies, is a leecher in battle. Nurgle is known for playing slow and being slow, so as a warband is where it felt lackluster and often times it didn't seem like there was a reason to expand much beyond the empire with the playstyle

Azazel: Close to where Festus starts but north of kislev and around norsca, becomes pretty easy to get those tribes allied with you campaign wise. has the ability similar to N-kari where you can seduce units of the enemy to take some of them on your side, the fun thing with azazel is being able to deal with kislev earlier on, as a lord, extremely weak against ranged, but his fashion in battle is as graceful as it is deadly which helps out alot in melee the way he fights, but can be knock downed for some reason. army wise its quick and deadly.

Overall: think the addition of 4 characters for the dlc is pretty well done, and the system for having the ability to upgrade marauders into chaos warriors and beyond gives that lore feel of gaining the favour of the dark gods. Research trees are all pretty much the same but aligned with each chaos faction. Having a choice of both divided and undivided for the faction system to gain units from the higher realms or other benefits that apply across your entire faction is useful. Unit additions were okay

The downsides though is not the upgrade system itself but how it works initially. you cannot recruit even basic marauders anytime, all of the gaining units to recruit is apart of a percentage chance system so at times it felt that you'd spend turns waiting to build up an army and let alone if you're lucky enough to have an army capable enough of battling or defending half the time so you'd have them sitting in a dark fortress until its ready. The main difference with these factions is their form of play, which is meant to be more of a warband approach than a build your empire approach. think its somewhat of an expanded horde playstyle but think the only thing that wasn't so great about it is not even having basic marauders recruitable at any time so it took time to move past where you start and can get overwhelmed should other factions grow big soon enough. its good as a dlc and adds flavour but the recruiting system makes it a bit hard to do alot else in it

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is kind of in the 'Greek States' territory for DLC claims. For R2, the Greek States DLC (Athens, Epirus, Sparta) had a similar claim that there were like 50 new units or something.

That is basically not at all the case.

What is here is a decent-ish rework of WoC with a bunch of pallet-swaps with one or two stat tweaks. A lot of Chaos units now can be 'Chaos Warrior OF NURGLE' or 'Marauders OF SLAANESH'. I hardly consider these to be new units... they are like taking Mario and making him into Luigi, or Wario, and giving them one or two small stat tweaks like higher HP for Nurgle versions, better charge for Slaanesh versions, etc.

I also am not a huge fan of the four LLs. I'm totally cool with them being Warriors of Chaos LL's... that part I am fine with, though I know others weren't. But they just seem so... meh, I guess, in implementation. Festus doesn't feel much different from the existing Nurgle LL. In fact, the factions for these four WoC LL's basically are just the mono-god traits. And with LL's that seem not that dissimilar from the mono-god LL's.

It's not all bad... I do like the campaign mechanic with souls, and the upgrade system that is now in play is decent the more I get used to it, where marauders now upgrade into chaos warriors, etc.

But this is nothing like 50 new units... it's mostly reskins or pallet-swaps, along with cross-over units now that come from the WH3 mono-god base game factions.

Overall, it's somewhat misleading to claim that there are 50 new units, which is repeating a comment in my review of the Greek States Pack DLC for R2 something like a decade ago or more here. And like other reviews are probably saying, some of these 'new' units really could have just been in the base game version of WH3 to begin with... why WoC was held-out of WH3 originally, I have little clue. Not that the 'new' units really add a ton of abilities or changes... again, it's basically reskins to align with the four mono-god factions.


TL;DR - This is not a huge game-changer of a DLC, and the 50 new units are more like a few new LL's with mostly reskinned versions of units that, in some cases, date back to TW:WH1.\t\t\t\t\t
