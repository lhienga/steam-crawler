\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC adds four new Legendary Lords to the Warriors of Chaos roster as well as a large number of new units. Some of these units are similar to those familiar with the old Warriors of Chaos pack from Warhammer I, however they look nice and allow for greater visual variation in WOC armies. Other units are however totally new like the Chaos Warshrine. There are also new heroes who are aligned with a specific chaos god. These new units and heroes are available to the Daemonic factions as well which fleshes out their roster which a lot of players felt was a bit lacking.
The real appeal of this DLC are the new lords. Each lord is aligned with one of the four chaos gods and borrow mechanics from the Daemonic factions of Warhammer III, meaning that they each play differently to each other on the campaign map. The new lords have a unique campaign on the Realms of Chaos map for those tired of the campaign from the base game.
Overall if you like playing as Chaos factions in Warhammer III this DLC gives you a lot more toys to play with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's compare Champions of Chaos to some previous race pack DLCs in terms of what they actually add-

Beastmen:
3 Legendary Lords
1 Generic lord
2 Heroes
22 Units, 15 of which are unique (that is to say, there are 22 units added by this DLC if you count everything, but only 15 if you count each variant once like counting Ungor Herd, Ungor Herd with Spears, Shielded Ungor Herd and Shielded Ungor Herd with Spears as a single unit- Ungor Herds)
7 RoRs
Completely unique campaign mechanics

Norsca:
2 Legendary Lords
1 Generic lord
3 Heroes
28 Units, 14 Unique
9 RoRs
Completely unique campaign mechanics

Wood Elves:
2 Legendary Lords (plus a FLC LL later)
2 Generic lords
3 Heroes
22 Units, 15 Unique
7 RoRs
Completely unique campaign mechanics

Tomb Kings:
4 Legendary Lords
1 Generic Lord
3 Heroes
24 Units, 18 Unique
10 RoRs
Completely unique race and campaign mechanics

Vampire Coast:
4 Legendary Lords
1 Generic lord with 3 variants for lores of magic
3 Heroes
25 Units, 21 Unique
9 RoRs
Completely unique campaign mechanics

So the average value of previous content is:
3 Legendary Lords
1.2 Generic lords
2.8 Heroes
24.2 Units, 16.6 of which have unique models
8.4 RoRs
Entirely new campaign mechanics top to bottom

Champions of Chaos:
4 Legendary Lords
0 New generic lords, 4 variants of already existing lords that players already paid for
0 New heroes, 4 variants of already existing heroes that players already paid for
37 Units, only 7 unique and 1 that's actually brand new and not just a reskin of already existing units that players already paid for
4 RoRs
Reused campaign mechanics from the Daemons of Chaos factions, plus a fun little bar at the top of your screen to fill by doing things you would have done anyways

So that value on LLs is fine, and nobody is complaining about that. The actual titular Champions of Chaos are pretty cool. The issue is the incredibly lazy content that fills out the rest of the DLC. No new lords or heroes, just reskins of existing content with a few stat tweaks but no new abilities. And 37 units sounds pretty good compared to the average of around 24, but when you realize that LITERALLY every single unit but one is just a recolor of 6 year old models that players already paid for you realize how bad the value actually is. And the RoRs added by this DLC are all regiments of old units that, you guessed it, we already paid for.

This content really should have been part of the base game (which is still basically early access 7 months post release). It's not like it's terribly expensive (in fact its a few bucks cheaper than other race packs, although the slightly more expensive DLCs still provide much more value per dollar), and the WoC rework is definitely fun (although the promoting units mechanic and chaos fortresses were "heavily inspired" (read stolen) by mods for TW WII), but it's a matter of principle. I'm of the opinion that this DLC is a considerable step down from the previous standards of quality and were it not for the fact that the majority of the content for the WoC rework is gated behind this DLC (which is totally unprecedented, until now you have been able to use all the rework mechanics regardless of ownership of DLC as seen in the WE rework released alongside T&T or the Beastmen rework that was free for people who didn't buy S&F) I would have skipped it. Unfortunately CA has decided to twist the arm of their players into buying a subpar product seeing as the new WoC are nearly unplayable in campaign and WoC and all 5 Daemon races are literally unplayable in competitive multiplayer without this DLC\t\t\t\t\t
