\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRifts are gone-gone and you can finally enjoy the campaign - and can even make your own personalized rifts for jumping around.
That is probably my biggest pro.

I actually don't care about the re-skinned units, there is already a lot of love involved in doing them; ie. Festus' warriors/Forsaken look astonishing sick, while for Valkia they are just brutal². And I don't zoom in for every tiny thingie while on the battlefield.
Am using a lot of the new units instead of the vanilla-faction specific stuff and the upgrade-system is pretty decent. Kinda reminded me of the Amazon-DLC for Troy. Good stuff

Can only speak for those 2 factions and I enjoy them a lot. This is what I would have imagined Warhammer III be like....

No rift-race *sigh* (!)

"The Icequeen is no match for the Gorequeen" :Þ

There are of course tiny bugs, like missing unit-sets, but those can be fixed easily either by yourself and checking the workshop. A few units don't receive their upgrades from the Lords-tree or other circumstantial buffs. (ie Forsaken of Nurgle don't get the Gift-boost for missile resistance, Skullcrusher get absolutely no boost for Valkia)
But those are rather minor.

Decent performance otherwise on my rig, no crash so far (11400F + 1070Ti @1080p) - as it included the Immortal Empire patch.

Highly enjoyable, but I need to get back in brewing more concoction.....\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmidst the sea of positive reviews, I'll play the devil's advocate and give the cons of the DLC. If you are a fan of any the legendary lords, read their books etc, then pick the DLC up as much of the budget went toward their spectacular animations and models.

However, if you do not care for the legendary lords themselves then don't pick up the DLC at the full or even 10% off price because there is pretty much only one new unit, the rest are reskins of existing units, especially the marauders and light cav. While subjective, I think the reskins for the chosen and warriors are well done. However, only the heroes have received new animations. The chosen, one of the most elite units in the setting still use recycled animations from their regular warriors counterpart.

In some ways, this feels like the day one DLC, obscured behind a later release date simply because the monogod rosters are extremely incomplete without the pack. There was a picture of a tzeentch warrior before the release of the game but that was cut out so it could be included here.

If there is only 1 new unit, what about new mechanics? Sadly, the 4 lords simply borrow mechanics from their demonic factions without adding anything unique in the likes of grom or taurox. You get 1 new final battle but only if you play on the ROC map not immortal empires.

And yet somehow this DLC costs more than a standard lord pack simply because there are 4 lords instead of 2 which I don't really care for. I rather have quality over quantity. I could make a mod that gives you 64 new legendary lords with borrowed mechanics from other factions. Had there been a monstrous unit for each of the 4 gods then I would have recommended this without a second thought. To those who say reskins are inevitable are making a strawmen argument. Why not give us unique looking chaos spawn instead of the reskins we currently have? It is a shame, given that the last Warhammer II DLC had much fairer pricing and quality. I am not averse to paying more for more content but this is just disappointing. Perhaps inflation has hit the chaos wastes hard.\t\t\t\t\t
