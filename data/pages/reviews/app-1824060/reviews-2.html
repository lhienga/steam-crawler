\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most fun faction in the game next to Beastmen and Nakai.
Vassalizing is the bread and butter of this faction. In normal factions its about conquering or allying, but with WoC it's all about the vassals. You can vasselize everyone through sheer force except lizards and dwarfs (bribe only), and it's awesome.

Of the 4 you get;
- Azazel: Sigmar's former friend and has best lore of the 4. His faction is weak without the SFO overhaul mod, but vassals are your main goal. He's a glass canon in battle and his bonus to vassals is the best of the 4 (immune to psychology).
It's fun to vassalize Kislev but you're almost encourage to just take over all of Norsca. Azazel is a lover first, let the vassals deal with your troubles.

- Vilitch: a beast on the battlefield, a one-man doom stack with sweet mechanics and a decent buff to vassals (shielding). You'll have fun with him if you want to vassalize the East, but Dwarfs by this point usually become a nightmare to deal with.

- Valkia: She's fun and Khorne in this game is awesome to use, just the beat-em up faction, like orcs but human. There's nothing better then turning dark elves into rage monkeys via vassalizing. She gives them Frenzy which is temporary immune to psychology with bonuses, but you lose it when your health drops below half, which is why Azazel's mechanic is supreme of the 4

- Festus: the best lord of the 4 and he's my absolute favorite in all of WH3. Not only is he a walking healer via his clickable aura ability, but his army is unstoppable with single entity units huddled around him.
There's nothing more fun then vassalizing the entire empire ordertide and making the other ordertide confused as to what to do with you. Your biggest threats are Archaon and the Daemon Prince. You can go north instead and vassalize Norsca but

BONUS (seperate DLC): Sigvald; He's near Valkia but with Azazel's faction bonus plus undivided and can recruit from all 4 factions. He's incredibly fun to play and makes his entire army Expendable (army doesn't lose Leadership if you lose them). He's an excellent fighter, can heal and doesn't need an SFO overhaul mod to make his faction good


Festus is Bestest but Sigvald is Supreme\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat you get

The reworked Warriors of Chaos faction

4 legendary lords, one for each Dark God

A new Realms of Chaos campaign set apart from the race for Ursun

The faction: The warriors of chaos are the mortal followers of the Dark Gods. Rather than building empires the warriors of chaos destroy their enemies and vassalize others. You can claim a number of specific settlements on the map as dark fortresses form which your primary forces can garrison and build. Everything else is best left to whatever factions you want to vassalize. Norscans are the most common vassal and each dark fortress has a norscan tribe attached. Though you can vassalize anyone by beating them down to their last settlement and then capturing it. Anything you control outside of dark fortresses are weak little outposts that usually are not worth much and not defendable so its often better to gift these to a vassal.

Souls: Warriors of chaos capture and sacrifice enemy souls to the Dark Gods. This functions as a secondary currency you can spend to activate boons from the Dark Gods. Access to a God's bonus is restricted depending on the champion, for instance Slaaneshi themed champions can get 4 offerings to slaanesh going but none to Khorne. Much like their tech tree it varies a lot based on what legendary lord you pick. As you spend souls you unlock the option to use new more powerful bonuses. This is how you access the daemons of chaos roster for Warriors of chaos who otherwise can't recruit these units. So if you want a bloodthirster of khorne you better give souls to Khorne for awhile. My personal favorite is the undivided bonus that lets you shatter the walls of major settlements. Makes taking them much less of a hassle.

The Warriors of Chaos also have a unique unit recruitment system. You take a lowly maurader of chaos and fight and keep them alive and as they power up you can give them marks and turn them into other units.

They come in five flavors.

Undivided are a generalist faction worshiping chaos as a whole. Most monsters such as chaos Giants, manticores, and trolls fall into this category as do the aspiring champions.

Azazel leads the Ecstatic Legions, a Slaaneshi themed army. He is capable of seducing enemy units before a battle is started and giving enemies the mark of Slaanesh making them more vulnerable to vassalization. Slaaneshi units specialize in speed and damage prefering fast hard hitting tactics, cavalry, and chariots.

The Twisted Twins are the champion(s?) of Tzeentch and has access to magic faction abilities to confuse and befuddle others. Their warriors have barrier and specialize in spell casting and they have access to the flying doom knights.

Valkia the Bloody is the champion of Khorne and specializes in hard hitting melee combat. While all chaos warriors are melee centric she is perhaps the most basic attack move style of gameplay. Set your furious berserkers loose and watch them chop the enemy up.

Festus champion of Nurgle. My personal favorite. If you wanted to play Ku'gath but found his campaign and mechanics frustrating or annoying Festus is just Ku'gath but better. Spread plagues, blob your units up to heal them, laugh as the enemy focus fires on a monster and fail to kill it because Festus' healing aura is too strong, and then blow them up with a giant zit. Mark of nurgle makes units slower but extremely durable.

Daemon Princes: Your generic lords can accomplish paths of glory that give them bonuses for doing certain things. What's more they can take on marks that make them champions of specific Gods and over time if they prove themselves worthy they can turn into a daemon prince for that God. This delevels them some so be warned. However its a nice addition that makes leveling up even generic lords fun.

I have a lot of fun with this faction and its a nice change if you want to take a break from painting the map as vassals can be surprisingly self sufficient. If you want to build tall, let minions do the empire business, and focusing on burning the world down this faction might be for you.\t\t\t\t\t
