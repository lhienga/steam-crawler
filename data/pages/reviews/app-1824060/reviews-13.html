\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC causes more problems than it solves. Firstly, it simply not what was wanted or what the game needed. No one wanted 4 lords for the Warriors of Chaos (5 with Bel'akor's inclusion in Immortal Empires). There are still currently 4 factions in Warhammer III that have a single lord and all the factions are screaming for development. Instead we continue with them having a single lord for atleast the rest of the year. This is worst when considering lords like Azazel and Valkia would make much more sense with their respective chaos gods.

Additionally, the majority of the units are simple reskins that should have been included with the Warhammer III to round out he bare-bones rosters of the chaos factions. The only actual new units are the Chaos warshrines which... aren't great. Their neat, but not really that useful unless you're Nurgle. When talking about the reskins as well, I admit most of the Chaos warriors recoloring with new helmets and weapons look good, but specifically Nurgle's Marauder units look aweful, like they've just been dipped in Neon green.

The lords are overall pretty ok. Vilitch is easily the coolest with some amazing voicelines. Valkia is disappointing in combat, imo, and the design they went for her is okay, but honestly not what was expected due to her amazing novel art. Festus... really doesn't impress in visuals, but I'm not a fan of the character anyway, his voice acting is great. Azazel is alright, having fought him, he has a really cool model and voice lines.

The DLC isn't inherently bad, but it has an increased price with significantly less REAL new units and for a faction that didn't impress in 2016 instead of any of the 7 new ones that could have benefited from the first DLC after Warhammer III's disastrous launch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is valid criticism that warriors of chaos needed a re-work badly and locking the meat of it behind a DLC is a little messed up but I think the quality of the work done here justifies it compared to CA's DLC work in the past. Is this worth 15 dollars for everyone? Absolutely not. Is this DLC quality content? It absolutely is.

Chaos is so important to the setting and this really puts them in a good spot thematically and gameplay wise. The system where you rank up your units to upgrade them from a lowly marauder all the way to a chaos undivided aspiring champion or a doom knight of Tzeentch feels great mechanically and is thematic to the warriors of the faction proving their worth and devotion to the chaos gods to receive further boons and blessings.

I think the voicework deserves a shoutout and takes it beyond what many claim is a shallow re-skin. I've mainly been playing with Vilitch the curseling so far and man, they have amazing voice lines at the level of the best skaven and the iridescent horrors of monogod tzeentch in the base game.

The campaign is as intense or chill as you want it to be. It uses the same map more or less from the base game realm of chaos but it takes out the soul race and annoying rift spawns. It is more like a very relaxed vortex race like from the base campaign in the second game where you are essentially collecting a resource that fills a bar but without city wiping armies attacking you every time you reach a new tier.

I buy Total War Warhammer DLC day one and I would put this at a level with the prophet and the warlock for TWW2. This is the cherry on top for a rework of the most painfully in need of an update faction from the previous titles and rounds out the roster of the 4 monogod faction launched with this game.

Here's hoping that this is indication of the DLC to come for TWW3 as we see things like Chaos Dwarfs and another dwarf rework.

One warning, as of 2.0.0 and the launch of the immortal empires beta there is no way to confederate in the campaigns with other WoC factions, only vassalize. While the vassalization mechanic has been spruced up a bit this means don't come into this right now with hopes of doing an Archaeon map painting everchosen murderfest with all the WoC legendary lords. I very much enjoy the campaign mechanics that this lord pack brings. Not a fan of the price of the DLC - 10% off for me means +3% instead of +13% in tax that I have to pay on Steam - try and find places that haven't caught up with the Canadian tax code yet to buy games from, grumble.

Enjoy the speed of the campaigns, the brevity. It's nice to be able to play and complete a campaign in a day, and of the 2 out of 4 lords I've played so far, the campaign finishes at around 100 turns on Very Hard difficulty. Final battle is fun, like a gladiator pit. Making Norscans my vassals means that I don't have to worry about settlement building - build up the castle settlements, keep pounding around the map to gather souls. Barely ever need to make a second army, and when you do, it'll be full in one or two turns due to warband recruitment, so it's not going to be a huge drain on the coffers. Build army, use army, disband army. Keep your main lord running around and jumping from battle to battle, razing and subjugating as necessary.

Haven't tried the new lords out in Immortal Empires yet.

Recommend, so long as you get it somewhere cheaper than here.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played all the champions on release in the immortal empires campaign on Legendary + Very hard battles, 200% endgame crisis for at least 70 turns and this is my ranking:

1. Azazel: I had an absolute blast! I even finished long, ultimate and endgame victory. Seduction can give you an edge in close battles and even in hopeless battles it can help to decimate the enemy for the next battle. The Seductive Influence works awesome and allows very engaging diplomacy shenanigans. Disclaimer: my vassals did not bend me over a single time, and I had a lot!

2. Festus: at first, I was a bit skeptical regarding the "striped down" plagues and the power creep for Festus himself. But I must say even if Festus is a very powerful lvl1 lord, there are still meaningful upgrades in his skilltree. Nevertheless, he is not OP since he brings obvious weaknesses to the table. His plagues surprised me the most. I felt his plagues provided way more utility to the campaign compared to the plagues of Ku'gath. He has a tough start, but also the tools to deal with it.

3. Valkia: I am not a Khorne fan, but got what I expected: Powerful melee faction with powerful melee lord. Her Blooletting ability felt more or less meaningless, especially compared to Skarbrands Bloodletting. 15% extra movement after winning a battle is nice, but not more. If that extended to extra movement after razing a city, it would be better. If you want, you could include mages in your Khorne army. Beside that, her faction is pretty bland.

4. Vilitch: I am very disappointed by him. The "benefit for high magic" is pretty much negligible. His changing of the ways is borderline useless and never worth the souls. I tried the "Spawnify" on a full allied stack, it did nothing, so I did not bother any further. Only breaking an alliance seems somewhat useful, but it is way too expensive. (btw. as Azazel, I was able to do far more sophisticated diplomacy shenanigans). Vilitch himself as a melee mage hybrid has a lot of wasted potential: he is decent at both, but not amazing at anything. His unique skills are all underwhelming to the point that I prefer a generic lord.

For all champions, it would be nice if you could upgrade your gifted daemon units (e.g. Daemonetts to Exalted Daemonetts or Seekers). All in all, not the best DLC they ever made, but still good. I had a lot of fun playing the first two and not a bad time playing the other two.\t\t\t\t\t
