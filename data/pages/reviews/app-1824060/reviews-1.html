\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough I didn't want to give this DLC a negative review, I will have to for a few reasons.
 

Roster diversity: when you play them for the first time, for instance, in Valkia or Festus, it doesn't matter; you will just have the same units but in a different reskin, and lorefully it makes sense, but what triggers me is that you can't have armies in which you have 8 bloodthirsters and 8 skullcanos and the rest are normal chaos warriors because you have restrictions on how many demons you can have, and it is just so annoying.

 

Dark fortresses and settlements: The concept of Dark forts looks and sounds good, but in reality it is just so annoying, and that's mostly because 95% of forts are in the northern part of the map, which makes replayability extremely low. And normal settlements just give you nothing, so you have to give them to your vassals, which will give you 2 coins per turn.

 

CoC are just too strong, which is also a big contributor to the low replayability compared, for instance, to faction like Tomb Kings or Vampire Coast which not only make your gameplay interesting but also fun.

 
Overall, a good concept on paper that is just poorly realized. If certain things would change, like nerfing them or giving them higher demon caps, or straight up removing the caps and giving them more forts across the world, it would be really good DLC, but it's just boring.\t\t\t\t\t
