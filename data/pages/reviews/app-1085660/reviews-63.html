\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a Destiny 2 launch player, and my overall feeling towards the game is high. Let me give you my thoughts on Pro’s and Con’s.
Pros:
The game has always been ready for any new players to the game. Game controls, fluidity of the game, story content, and beginners’ content availability has always been a low hurdle.
The story is massive, the scenery is beautiful, and the back story content is massive, so if you like large scale story lines and beautiful games join in.
Raids, PvP, group activities such as Gambit (Hybrid PvP), and Seasonal activities all encourage you to make friends and just hang out.
Also, if you just like the casual jump on and play and get off, you can do that as well.
If you like a grind for loot, Destiny 2 does that well, without over burdening you with a time sink. If you like to craft weapons to your liking, you can do that also.
There are many third-party apps to help manage inventory and give you a better user experience to the game, so you are not stuck with just the Destiny 2 UI. Destiny Item Manger, D2 armor picker and light.gg to name a few.
All in all, Destiny 2 has a great play style to suit many types of players, with clans, friends, and a fireteam finder for those looking for help or new friends, it is a great game for that.

Cons:
Destiny 2 and not your typical tank and spank style game. Raids can get a bit complicated in their mechanics and can cause some frustration among the players.
PvP match making can be tuff. There are many times you are paired up against a team you should not be paired with. This is due to the ping style match making.
Sometimes we get new weapons that are just recycled, and not new weapons. There really is too many Exotics in the game to make them really “Exotic”.
There is simply never enough Vault (bank) space to keep your inventory in check. Third party apps help with that.
You cannot craft armor, only weapons, and the exotic catalysts (perks that make exotic weapons better) sometimes require friends or random people wanting the same thing to help obtain.
Destiny 2
Trials of Osiris (High End PvP) has weapons that can only be obtained by the best PvP players. This is a barrier to causal players, as most people cannot obtain them, and they give significant advantages to PvP players.
Think of Trials as a paywall.
There is a huge micro transaction system called Eververse in the game. Although it is not required to play the game nor is it a barrier of any type, it is just annoying.
