destiny 2 is a good shooter, sure, as you get to play 3 unique classes with their own gimmicks; ranging from the stalwart titans intent on bashing the enemies or shielding their allies from harm, to the tricky hunters that can clear out the largest bosses and hordes of enemies with their varying supers, to the rough warlocks that are space wizards with guns

only problem i have is that most of these cool features are often locked behind dlc; mainly ranging from the expansions that have really high costs, to the season passes that just give people a VERY bad case of FOMO; since it means you have to grind for the stuff now or end up losing any (and possibly all) chance of gaining the items that were a part of the current season pass
sadly, i just cant recommend it as it is; mainly since the development's kind of in a weird position (from what i know, and the fact that the final shape's been delayed for a good while), and that the DLC is just a bit way too overpriced for a game that is supposed "free to play."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"I hate Destiny 2, it's my favorite game."

Steam shows almost 2300 hours, but I migrated to PC from PS4 back in Beyond Light. My Historical Heatmap says 5241 hours across the game's lifespan. This game sits second highest in hours spent playing for me, right behind a decade+ of crippling WoW addiction while growing up. All that being said, I cannot recommend this game.

For starters, please, PLEASE do not hit "Add all DLC to cart" on the Steam page. There's a lot of redundancies in the content there. Do some research, ask a D2 Vet that's been playing for a while, SOMETHING. Have someone help you figure out what to buy if you decide to get into the game. Just know that, unless you get it on sale down the road, this game is going to set you back a couple hundred dollars in content alone. God help you if you get in and see cosmetics you like and have the means to afford them, lol.

The game may be marked as "Free to Play", but it's more of a "Free to Try". You NEED the extra DLCs (all of them, imo.) to fully enjoy this game. Missing even the Forsaken DLC, which has been gutted via sunsetting, prevents you from getting into a raid and a dungeon, which can hold you back from joining friends/clanmates. We struggle with this in my clan all the time when we get new people, and it sucks every time.

The Seasonal model, while supposedly changing in The Final Shape (we'll see about that; "Episodes" just kinda sound like fewer, longer "Seasons" so far), is bland and played out at this point. The slow drip of (lackluster) story content week after week hasn't been enough to really keep my attention for the past 3-4 seasons now. I've gotten to the point now where I play a little bit on Season release week, then let the game sit and rack up content over the next 5-7 weeks, only to burn through the entire Seasonal quest line in a single sitting once it's all available.

Other than a feeling of obligation to my friend group/clan, which has me logging in to get weekly Dungeon runs done or an occasional raid (less of the latter recently, unfortunately, with the majority of our group burning out), I just don't feel that draw or desire to play the game as heavily anymore, and I don't foresee The Final Shape changing that.

When the game is good, it can be REALLY GOOD. When the game is bad? It's the absolute WORST. We're in that downswing period right now, with a massive content drought until June 2024 (Current Season of the Wish started Nov 28, 2023). It's so unbelievably frustrating that Bungie has such an amazing cast of characters, universe, and lore within this series, but constantly squanders all of it with horrible business practices and confusing/lazy game design decisions.

TL;DR: If you've avoided it this long, continue doing so. If you have a group of friends that you can get in with to teach you the ropes and reliably experience all the excellent end-game content, go for it, but be ready to shell out the $$$.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell what can I say about Destiny 2? I used to love it. It used to be one of my main games. I have deinstalled it after Lightfall and never reinstalled it. I have been playing this game since the release of Destiny 1, mostly on Xbox which is why I only have 1.200 hours logged here on steam. All the desire I have to play Destiny comes from a place of Nostalgia of what this game once was, not what it currently is. And honestly that's about all that holds this game still together, I can't imagine why people still play this game aside from dreaming about the past. I have no idea why people still let Bungie abuse them like that, but I chose to not let Bungie abuse my time and my wallet anymore.

The game is too expensive and every bit of time you invest gets absolutely ♥♥♥♥ on when Bungie decides to make the weapons you grinded for useless by not letting them reach the new required power for the new content. Just search Destiny 2 Sunsetting on YouTube. So if you do want to play this game be prepared to essentially loose your non exotic gear at every new season. This is not a game about collecting guns, it's a game about regrinding guns that you already had with slight stat or skin variations, because your already farmed gear got trashed.
They are leaning more and more into straight up just recycling content, and I don't mean seasonal stuff (which is also more of a chore/pay gate than an event). They use entire missions from deleted content as "new" content.

Which speaking off, prepare to never ever actually own this game. Bungie has already removed 3/4 of the games overall content in a mix of fomo and their own inability to code. So at any time (usually when a new DLC comes in) Bungie proceeds to delete the oldest content in the game. If I had to take a guess it's because they coded the item lists incredibly lazy. You might as why something as old as WoW can keep all the dlcs in the game while Destiny can't? Well I did a bit of research and apparently there is essentially a List where all the items in the game are listed. If the game needs an item it can look on said list. Now WoW actually has tiers of lists. For example they have a Main list and all the Main list contains are the names of the individual DLC lists. So WoW needs to do something with an Item, it goes to the main list, ask what dlc this item is from, DLC 2, then it goes to the DLC 2 list and grabs the item from there. Easy. Destiny has everything on 1 GIANT list. Imagine reading through thousands of unsorted items on a list to find the stuff you need. That is why they have to delete old content, the one list they use only has so much space. So if this list issue is the reason for the deletion of content then it's just lazy by Bungie. Again WoW has no issues with keeping Content around. It's too late for Destiny 2, they need a better approach for Destiny 3 (which is unlikely to be made since I think Destiny 2 will just be held on life support until it slowly fizzles out in 4 years).

Another point is that the game requires you to probably spend 300-400 euros/dollars to actually get the remaining not deleted Content. Because it's not like FF14 where you buy a DLC and get all the old DLCs with the purchase of the new DLC. Instead you will have to still purchase all still available DLCs. Then unless you pay for the Year pass you will have to pay for every single Season and the seasons aren't really as good as they used to be.
If you want to bring friends into this game, tell them they should expect to pay at least 300-400 euros/dollars as well.

The game heavily relies on fomo (fear of missing out) in most aspects, wanting you to check vendors constantly. They finally actually changed the way you aquire mods which are important if you want to do build crafting for higher content. For most of this games life these mods were on a rotation on 2 vendors in the tower, forcing you to log on every day to see if the mod you needed is now there.

The free to play option is more of a free trial as you almost get nothing. Final Fantasy 14 lets you play until level 70 with their free trial without time restriction, which is a sizable chunk of the game. Destiny 2s free to play version is more of a free trial to see if you like the controls and the settings, you'll very little to actually play.

It does not have a Subscription, but be prepared for lousy story (lately, it was amazing back in the day, but oops that's deleted now) and be prepared to feel like you just stepped into the story at it's very end. You will miss a lot of background knowledge (although they are removing traces of that content so that new players don't even know anymore that there was more game). Be prepared to spend the price of a full game like Baldurs Gate 3, for 2-3 hours of meaningful contnet when you buy the newer DLCs and then hours of sensless boring grind. To put it into perspective, the grand, big DLC Lightfall had less content on launch than some small seasonal content in the past. The Final Shape might not even get a 3rd Darkness Subclass to round out the abilities, they might just get a reskin of existing supers.

Skins are not worth the 20-40 euro price point as they most often look very bland. Most of the games efforts seem to be going into making stuff for their Cash shop Eververse.

I hope that just based on the sheer length of this review, you can see that I really liked Destiny 2. And that I really want to like it again. But it's become entirely overpriced ♥♥♥♥ with a very shrunken community of mostly new players, whales and YouTubers, that act as if they don't see what's wrong with the game or they haven't played long enough to run into these issues . If Bungie does something bad, the Community is mad for 3-4 days then goes back to shoving money at Bungie.

If you want to buy Destiny 2, spend those 300-400 dollars for all the content, skins, etc. Then go for it. You might even like the first 50-100 hours of the game because as a new player you're not missing these old experiences that have now been deleted. You haven't played the recycled content before like I have.

So buy this if you like, don't let me persuade your choice. If you are okay with the prices of everything, the paid events, the removal of content you paid for, the overbearingly greedy store that sucks all the cool loot out of the gameplay to sell as skins. I am merely writing this to mourn a lost game that I used to love.

If you do happen to play it. You can't even get into that feeling of Nostalgia anymore of doing cool ♥♥♥♥ with your friends, because all of the awesome content has been removed. If you think it's worth the hundreds of dollars to scrape up the aftermath of a great game, go for it. But don't complain that Bungie is robbing you of time and money. This is your very lengthy warning. Sadly there are so many better games out there to invest yourself into nowadays.\t\t\t\t\t
