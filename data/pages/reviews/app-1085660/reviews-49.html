\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith over 6000 hours of game time, I find spending much of my time helping kinder guardians (new players) from the Destiny 2 LFG discord with various activities and find myself asking “would I recommend the game?” I’ll start off with the determination and then go into the reasons as a complex question like this does have its nuances that deserve expansion and discussion.

No, I would not recommend this game to people considering the game UNLESS you have an existing player ready to sherpa you into the game. While it is clearly a game that I enjoy having sunk over 6k hours (Steam and Xbox) into this comes down to 2 things Nuance and I’m trapped, someone for the love of God save me.

Let’s start off with the 2 biggest elephants in the room, cost and content.

COST
To access all content in the game now (including the next DLC for sale at this moment) you are up for bare minimum of A$434.8 if you shell out in one hit. Or A$284.8 for the base DLCs and an additional A$268.05 for additional content as you progress (includes season and dungeon costs) for a whopping A$552.85.

CONTENT
If you join now, several campaigns that are relevant to the story are no longer playable and have been removed from the game in a move called “sunsetting” which means to understand what is going on you better be ready to watch multiple hours of YouTube. But it gets better, there used to be a series of creators that would compile story content on YouTube but Bungie didn’t like that, so they put a stop to it. Now we have the “Cutscene Archive” which fails to capture the little moments outside of cutscenes that provide context and flavour so now you can’t even get that.

So how much is missing you ask, let’s do a summary. 5 of the 8 Of the campaigns (9 once Final shape launches) are completely inaccessible, 1 entire social zone – completely inaccessible, 20 of the 23 season stories - completely inaccessible.

NEW PLAYER EXPERIENCE
The community has long urged Bungie to do something to make the onboarding of new players better. As the removal of the first 55% (approx) of the game, new players are dumped into a zone with no idea what is going on and very little direction on where to go and what to do. If you do get into this game your best bet is to get into one of the D2 LFG discords, meet some people and hopefully find someone like myself that is willing to go round with you and help you out.

GAMEPLAY
This has always been the one standout of Destiny in that the moment-to-moment gameplay is enjoyable, the weapons feel good and the abilities feel powerful. Grouping with friends and clan mates is easy and is really the best way to play the game with crossplay functioning flawlessly for the most part.

STORY
This is a bumpy one, for every “win” there has been 2-3 “losses”. Be this poorly executed retcons, direct contradictions or just poor design all-round exemplified in the most recent expansion “Lightfall”. A lot of the issues would seem to stem from poor project management skills within the development team. A basic understanding of your existing story and its intricacies would have allowed most of the issues to be avoided. There are multiple examples of this however that would delve into the realm of spoilers. As someone who is known to have a deep understanding of the story and lore of Destiny within my circle of guardians,
it is extremely common to be asked “what does this mean” for basic information that should be readily demonstrated by the game.

In conclusion
Yes I love the game, No I wouldn’t recommend spending $500 on it but at his point I’ve invested too much to leave now so I’m trapped till the end

Luckily for Bungie, this is what perfectly synchronises with those returning players, tugging on nostalgia to bring them back. Probably to play through the first glitzy cinematic hours before realising at the other side is just some huge grind of a generic repeatable event that requires minimal participation to complete and therefore is not engaging.

Just leaves a sour taste in my mouth, the whole thing.

Especially after I learnt of the way they recently treated their staff. Shameful.

I won't be coming back Bungie, sorry. No dice.

This company and this game do not deserve the amount of support and players they get for regurgitating content and preying on nostalgia and FOMO. Play something else.\t\t\t\t\t
