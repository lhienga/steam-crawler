\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the potential to be one of the best MMOs out there but is locked behind countless paywalls, menus (i'll explain), and the constant need for external help. First off, if you are going to be playing Solo and not willing to spend equal times reading about the game as actually playing it. Don't even bother buying it. It's going to be a painful experience alone. Some encounters and dungeons require knowledge of "secrets" which are honestly really cool if they were locked behind asinine jump puzzles. Movement is a big part of this game. Developers instead of standardising movement in order to make play easier have developed 2 counter intuitive jump. One of which does not allow you to gain altitude if you are going down. Meaning if your trying one of these jump puzzles and got an upgrade that reduced your movement speed - you won't make the jump you just made. In fact you'll probably bounce off the ledge instead of grabbing hold. Then instead of having a saving grace ability or jump. You'll slowly fall to your death. Sometimes resetting the instance, or putting you on a 45 second timer where your friends have to come get you. All of these is not during a combat phase. There is no reason to have a 45 second spawn timer for a movement puzzle. Again if your doing this solo just don't. One of the classes has a basic triple jump and it's the only one I've had any long term enjoyment playing. Even then when you lose mobility - the stat tied to movement you won't be able to access parts of the map unless you change gear. I wish I was exaggerating.

The paywalls are incredible and exhaustive. Did you buy a seasons pass? Great it was only good for 1 season and you paid $10-20 for one dungeon and access to it's weapons. Doesn't matter if this is extremely old content that would just be fun to explore if they actually gave it to you in anyway that doesn't require in game currency. I've spent easily over $400 on this game for 450 hours played. The last expansion + partial catchup was given to me as a present and I wouldn't be back if it wasn't for that. They spent $60 to get me current, only for us to later find out that it wasn't current at all. Then I spent $40 on a set of season passes that I thought were actual content but it only equated to 1 more dungeon. After that non-sense I wasn't willing to put more money in and my friends sponsored me to keep playing with them. Which I really enjoy, I just wish I didn't have to keep my credit card on hand. Now once you've unlocked those paywalls you then have to earn it. On every character. Want to swap to play a warlock instead of the super simple hunter? Great - re-enter a learning curve. Then once you get the hang of it be prepared for over 40 hours of catchup quests you have to do just to be able to do the same content your main has earned. Almost nothing carrys over to your alts. Only weapons that are equitable.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do and I don't recommend.

Originally i got recommended to play Destiny 2 by a friend and I finally started in August 2020 during the Solstice event. At the time it was great fun to play - even solo i enjoyed it. But it soon occurred to me that the "Free 2 Play" experience was very very limited. Yes you got access to all locations (Mercury, IO, Titan, Mars, The Tangled Shore, Nessus and the Dreaming City) , the base game story of the Red War and the two Year 1 expansions of Warmind and Curse of Osiris, could play Vanguard Strikes, as it was back then, Crucible (PVP), the Gambit game modes, and of course the Eva Lavante Events. However once you had done the Year 1 Campaigns - there wasn't really much to do except grind out levels for the season pass. This lead me to buying the Forsaken and Shadowkeep expansions to continue playing. Since then i have purchased all expansions and have got about 2.3K hours in 3.5 years.

Now today, however, the F2P experience is... limited - for better and for worse. You can no longer play Year 1 Campaigns, or the Year 2 Campaigns (Forsaken Expansion) as these have now been removed. Five of the above mentioned locations have been pretty much removed (you can visit parts of them in this current years Seasons) and been replaced with Europa (Beyond Light Expansion), The Throne World of Savathun (The Witch Queen), and Neomuna (Lightfall). For a new player that might be interested in the "What happened up until this point?" or is interested in lore - you can't play the game to find out what happens. You'll have to go to YouTube or the DestinyWiki to find out the Year 1 stuff and the majority of Years 2 - 5. On the plus side you can do some Legacy Raids and Dungeons, Fireteam Finder now exists for the solo players looking for groups in game to do the Co-op activities.

So for a new player right now that is reading this - do i recommend Destiny 2? If you are going to free 2 play it i wouldn't as you still don't really have a lot to do - especially if you are going to be a solo player. If you purchase everything and become "P2W" sure - but try and get into some community areas cause solo Destiny 2 is a bit of a pain even with the fireteam finder.\t\t\t\t\t
