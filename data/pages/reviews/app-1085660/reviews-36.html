\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLets talk about destiny as a whole:

over the past 10 years destiny has been a franchise built on potential, potential it has only seldom come close to meeting. With the presentation of its narratives being strenuous and confusing, the content itself being lacking in quality or reward, the game play being a mess in what it wants to be, its honestly no surprise that the game has had to crutch on manipulative tactics to keep its player base around.

I am a veteran player of this game. I played destiny 1, I was there for TTK and RoI, the destiny 2 beta, forsakens launch, and lightfalls, I saw it all, but I won't be seeing the end. Lightfall has been a massive wake up call to me. The game charges the price of a near AAA title but cannot provide anything close to the offerings of a full game. The game removes content at the end of its yearly cycles which is not only terrible for the story telling, and content offering, but is ultimately anti-preservationist. With reveals of internal ideas like "over delivery," as well as their internal views of their own employee's, the cracks of the facade grow ever more visible.

Destiny at its core was a very interesting and potential filled world. There was so much possibility, the prospects of a game that keeps its story going continuously is an interesting premise. But it has all been squandered by poor management, player manipulation, and greed. I honestly cannot say this game is worth getting into from any perspective, as the bad outweighs the good.\t\t\t\t\t
