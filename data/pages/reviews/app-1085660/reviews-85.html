The top-tier cosmetic content, even after investing in pricey DLCs, remains hidden behind micro-transactions. This, combined with the game's subscription model that requires regular purchases of season passes, can become burdensome. For those looking to dive into PVP, be prepared for a steep learning curve; the lack of high-tier armor obtainable, resources, and essential weaponry can make it daunting. With modes like Gambit and strikes not feeling rewarding and offering repetitive experiences, the overall enjoyment can be hindered from the core three activities with extremely long queue times. While the game might offer rich experiences after extensive gameplay hours, the initial hurdles, especially for new players, are considerable. Would I recommend Destiny 2? Only to those who are ready for a significant time and financial investment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDO NOT RECOMMEND.

That hurts to write about the most competent, smoothest FPS looter experience currently available on any platform. And at least from the players' perspective, it is a technical marvel. Usually.

The good? In addition to the above, it has one of the best playable maps in all of FPS history in the EDZ. Weapons are interesting and the differences make them meaningfully unique. Buildcrafting is alive and well here. There's EXCEPTIONAL Hollywood talent on the credits here making the NPCs quite memorable. And yeah, the player counts for a game that's nearing a decade in age, means that regardless of my opinion it's a pretty damned successful game. The raids are usually a good time. There's just a lot that Bungie designed well here.

The bad? Whew. That's gonna take a minute. Let me just say first that a lot of what's wrong with this game comes down to Bungie cultivating Fear Of Missing Out (FOMO) as their most valuable developing resource. Indeed, they're showing the gaming world that you can bleed a population of players for years and years by applying generous quantities of FOMO to them and watching them pawn their kidneys to have the new shiny, whatever that may be.

Explain? Sure. First there are seasonal armor sets that can't be farmed. Once the next season starts, you either have the armor set (5 pieces per class, three classes, means 15 pieces) from last season or you probably aren't ever getting it. Same for raid uniques/legos/exotics. The raids are rotating now, I guess? There's no guarantee that a raid that disappears this year or this season will ever come back again, so if you didn't get a particular piece of armor or a certain weapon that's unique to it, then you're ♥♥♥♥♥♥ when that raid goes away.

That leads me into the problem of sunsetting. There are a couple areas that aren't in the game any more that were in the original release of Destiny 2, Titan and Io. The first and second year expansion areas, Mercury, Mars, and the Tangled Shore, are both sunset now. That also means that the entire vanilla Red War campaign, the first expansion campaigns Curse of Osiris and the forgettable Mars one, and potentially the best expansion in the entire light-and-dark sage, Forsaken, are all off-limits to players now. I hope you got everything that you wanted from them. I guess you can search for years-old YouTube videos to see how it went, but you can't live the story of Destiny 2's first two years of content.

***YOU CAN STILL PLAY EVERY DAMNED MOMENT OF DESTINY 1'S CONTENT ON PS4 AND XBOX ONE, BUT YOU CANNOT PLAY THE NEXT TWO YEARS OF CONTENT BETWEEN THEM AND THE MINDLESS DRIVEL THAT NOW SUFFICES FOR THE DESTINY 2 STORY.***

Sadly you can't play that on PC, or I'd be doing that because D1 was the better game.

Anyway, mindless drivel. That's the story. You have a nine foot tall surfer guy as the destination NPC for the latest year. They've all but totally ignored the drama between Ikora and Zavala over how to respond to Cayde-6's killing (which, again, you can't witness because that content is sunset). Your jump from D1 to D2 will fail completely to give you any sense at all of why the Speaker is absent (can't see what happened there without YouTube because D2Y1 is sunset). "Hey, who's this lady with the poncho and the bird in the tower?" Great question! The answer has been sunset. Why's Uldren Sov a guardian? HA! Sunset content! In its place, you have now the Fallen and Cabal are friendly (well some are) to the last city, the factions are gone, and Queen Mara Sov is easily the most boring NPC in the game (when the Uldren/Crow thing could have kept her a stupendously interesting NPC if even a mediocre writer had a crack at the story). Oh dear mother of pearl, don't get me started on all the now-dumb NPCs and which ones are gone and how it's all sunset and you get to start over with ... [checks notes] ... Shaw Han? Yeah. Him. Some ghost watched a few reruns of Saved By The Bell and decided to resurrect Slater with Screech's personality. THAT is your introduction to the game now, and for all the lack of imagination at Bungie, THE EFFING COSMODROME IS YOUR STARTING MAP. AGAIN.

Big picture, they removed a lot of the first few years' content, and the most recent few years have been braindead and absent much originality or soul. By now I'm cheering for the Darkness to just put a fork in the whole damned game because it's clearly been done for about 3 years.

The Exo Stranger was much more interesting when we had no idea who she was and she'd just pop in sometimes for a quick cutscene and we wondered at each other on reddit if she could maybe be a Bray. Cayde-6 was possibly at his best wandering a barn at the Farm with Colonel Sanders.

It's gone. It was a special sort of controversy since, especially with Forsaken, that content removed was content that people paid to play. The excuse was that the game's footprint on your HDD/SSD would be too big if they left it in, but again I say that's a problem for them to solve on their end if it's a problem at all. Many would be fine dedicating a good 200-300GB of drive space for the game if it meant keeping the content they thought they bought, but that option was never presented to the players.

There have been many many changes to things like UI, armor, perk rolls (used to be fixed instead of random), and so on. Not all have been good. Overall it's an improvement from Destiny 1, but not a NEEDED improvement necessarily. In some ways it's been a step back from D1.

Now on to the money. You pay a full AAA game price for the game. You pay as much or more again each year (FOMO) for the new/current content year, which includes one destination and a several-mission campaign and maybe a couple strikes and probably a raid. Oh, and a dungeon. Definitely not a new Gambit or Crucible map, though. Then you have the seasons. You should get like 4 seasons to a content year, and that's either bought piecemeal or packaged for a higher price with the content year. So a $60 game (admittedly it has a free track) and another $100/year to keep up with the story. And yes, a lot of armor and weapons are tied to the content year, so if you don't own it then you can't get the loot. Just like the seasons.

And there's still a cash shop. Yes you earn Bright Dust slowly through the season, and a lot more of it with the paid season pass, but you also can just buy Silver and spend it to buy all the same cosmetics: sparrows, shaders, weapon and armor skins, emotes, ghost shells, and finishers.

AND ALL OF THIS IS PROBABLY OKAY IF YOU HAVE SOME FRIENDS TO PLAY ALL THE CONTENT WITH. But for us solo casual types, it's garbage. At the beginning of the current season as I write this in September, 2023, there was an unskippable mission I had to complete. I couldn't do anything else when I logged in. And as soon as that mission ended, I got thrown into a 3-player fireteam for what I guess was the seasonal activity (something between a mission and a strike). And guess who's underpowered for all that? Yup. THIS KID. So all I did was run away and keep getting downed by enemies I could hardly damage. FUN! It's unskippable. You can't jjust exit that stuff, go to the Tower, grab some gunsmith bounties, and go do PAs on the EDZ for a while. Nope. And then you load another guardian, and it's the same two unskippable missions again. This is every 13 weeks. Every season. You can't just go skip the seasonal stuff. YOU HAVE TO DO IT BEFORE YOU CAN DO THE CONTENT YOU ACTUALLY EFFING WANT TO DO.

That's when I uninstalled the game. The Final Shape is gonna have to go on without this filthy casual.

Anyway, between 500+ hours on Steam, some time on PS4, and some more PC time on Battle.net before the Steam migration, I'm convinced now that the best years of Destiny ended years ago.\t\t\t\t\t
