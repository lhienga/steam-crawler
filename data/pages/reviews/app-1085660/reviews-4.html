\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had Destiny 2 downloaded for years in my Steam library but only ever booted it up to dabble in the open world here & there. In In other words I never ran missions or did any serious PVE/PVP activities, I only fiddled around to just have some good ol' fashioned FPS fun. I did this because the game felt too far gone to bother "getting into." People had been playing it for many years; the amount of content in the game could fill a cargo ship; and the convoluted menus, mechanics, and in-game systems felt entirely too overwhelming to fool with. Fast track to early 2024 and I finally decided, "You know what - let's just try and actually get into Destiny 2." And I did. I started learning the game, its intricacies and its myriad systems. I played through the Shadowkeep campaign and am currently working my way through The Witch Queen. I participated quite a bit in the recent Guardian Games. I even pre-ordered the Final Shape + Annual Pass in anticipation of seeing how the story continues to unfold (and hopefully by that time I'll have completed Lightfall and other content). All of that to say, I've had a blast so far. Sure, the game feels confusing and overwhelming...but it's a widely-popular live service game that has been around a long time so that's to be expected. And while Destiny 2 isn't the kind of game I can just sit back and sink untold hours into (yet) I still nonetheless enjoy playing the game with purpose (as opposed to just messing around) and am happy I chose to finally start taking it as seriously as I do any other game I want to actually experience and enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t
