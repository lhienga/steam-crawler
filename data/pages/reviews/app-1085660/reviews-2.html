\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr - i don't think destiny 2 i a good game, but i think you can have fun playing it.

i've been putting off this review for a long time because I didn't really know how i felt about this game. if you're a person looking to play this game - my short answer is "don't." I don't think its worth your money and I certainly don't think it's worth your time. if you're a person like me that has been playing since launch, really likes the lore, enjoys how the game feels, etc etc etc... then sure, play the game. I have understood a very clear separation of "am i enjoying the game?" and "is the game good?"

am i enjoying the game?
hell yeah! i have a lot of fun doing buildcrafting and stuff like that in games, and the different subclass+exotic+mods combos are so neat to me! i love being able to really push the boundaries of the game and see what makes things tick, from speedrunning to super stacking, its a lot of fun for me... ive been doing raids and endgame stuff a lot more with discord servers and the new lfg finder, and its been a crazy fun time applying builds to real situations. the story is engaging and there are like, 3 or 4 different storylines that I think are really neat and cool aesthetically... the guns and the tools and the "stuff" are just so cool and well designed in most cases. having my own little character backstory and lore to go alongside this wild ride is very cool to me and i like that :)

is the game good?
no. destiny 2 is not a good game. its ♥♥♥♥♥♥ over the community, the playerbase, and even its own development team over and over and over again. i have working theory that ever since the engine and game structure tweaks in Beyond Light, Destiny 2 has been transitioning into a ship-of-Theseus "Destiny 2.5".
The original story from Destiny 1 has evolved into something much larger in scope (perhaps too large?)
The original playable content has been altered or outright removed.
The original loot in this *looter shooter* has been eclipsed by newer content, or completely powercrept/effectively disabled with sunsetting
The original buildcrafting/mods setup has been changed or overhauled so many times that it can be difficult to have a "main buiild" for more than a month, and something entire subclasses (which can cost money) are entirely worthless compared to others.
The original content engagement was mostly freely handled, but more and more loot/gear are locked behind paywalls or timegating, and because nearly everything in the game is limited-time exclusive, it indoctrinates a heavy amount of FOMO into the playerbase.

The original scope of Destiny 2 was cool and it made the game fun to play, but the scope of the game has changed so drastically since launch, and I think that the Destiny 2 we knew and loved at launch just isnt this game anymore. If you're expecting that game, you're going to be disappointed. If you're expecting a company slowly losing employees to save their game and make it perfect in their finale DLC, you're going to be disappointed. but if you can find little things in the game like lil OC lore or raiding with friends, and you can look at the game for what it is (NOT looking past the flaws, but recognising the flaws for what they are alongside the fun parts) then you too can have fun playing destiny 2\t\t\t\t\t
