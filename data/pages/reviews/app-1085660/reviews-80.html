\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of writing this review do I recommend Destiny 2 for newer players? It's a maybe. I think the game is great, lots of content and grind to sink into, you get content drops every few months, granted, that content is paid, but you do get new weapons, exotics and armor along with balance changes and weapon and armor updates every now and then. I purely recommend this on the PVE side of things, I believe that Destiny 2 PVP can never be truly great given the sheer amount of moving parts and potential outlying problems in the sandbox. It's fun, don't get me wrong but if you're looking at Destiny 2 as a PVP only game, you may as well choose something that leans more into personal skill like Valorant or Counter Strike.

The PVE is phenomenal if you have friends to play with. The raids and dungeons are a lot of fun IF you're comfortable with soloing, finding groups via LFG or if you have a group of friends that you play with. It's not the most approachable content, but that may be changing in the coming months with the in-game LFG getting added sometime in the next little while.

If you like collecting weapons, armor and styling your character, this definitely is a great game to do that. Some of the collecting methods for older exotic weapons is a little tedious given the requirements for purchasing them at the exotic kiosk, but you do have an avenue for acquiring exotic weapons where their original means of getting them are no longer in the game.

The sub class system is very good for really honing in on what exactly you want to do with a sub-class, but it is a little overwhelming if you don't know what you're looking at or if you don't know what the abilities do. It also is VERY expensive to unlock all of your sub classes and will require a lot of dedication to fully unlock every sub class on a new account.

The F2P content is solid at this point, as far as raids and general content go, but there is no story available to you as a free to play player as far as I know. You have the timelines introduced this season which summarize important details about the past few expansions and where we are in the story right now, but at the same time one mission is not enough to really give a new player a good idea of what exactly they are jumping into, given some of the characters that have made a return in recent years.

It's mixed for me. I love the game, but I do have a hard to recommending the game to newer players. If you have someone helping you understand what exactly is going on, I think that Destiny 2 is one of the most fun games I have ever played as far as pure gameplay goes, but for a lot of the game it feels like unless you know exactly what you're doing you're going to have a hard time really enjoying the world and the details and secrets within. The lore is incredible and the backstory of many of the factions are interesting and help paint a picture of a storied and rich universe with complicated relations between the different races and factions within the lore. The main problem with this is most of this content and lore is not really touched on in the majority of the story leading up to this point. This is becoming less of a problem now, as more and more important story is being shown on screen, but there is still a LOT of room for improvement in this area.

Overall, it's a time and energy investment like any other live service game, but there is 10 years of story you need to catch up on in order to really appreciate everything that's going on in the story right now. Gameplay is amazing and always makes me feel so cool, but to get to that point it's a lot of practice and reading your abilities. If you want to put the work in, it's totally worth it. I would at the very least give it a try since it is free to start.\t\t\t\t\t
