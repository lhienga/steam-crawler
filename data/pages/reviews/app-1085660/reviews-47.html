\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a tough review to write. After 8 years, do I feel I can provide an honest review at the end of 2023?

Do I want to recommend this game..? Yes, absolutely. Never have I been so excited to play a game before, Destiny was my jam, my sweet escape. The gun play, the lore (and I'm not even a lore guy), the fit, finish and polish of the game play. It was perfect, it arguably is still perfect.

I almost wrote this review mid 2023, before the Bungie fallout occurred over their mass layoff's and -45% earnings miss. But I waited, maybe another Forsaken moment will occur, maybe Savathun will resurrect and breathe life back into this studio that use to be so good at making captivating content. And then the stories hit, corporate cuts after corporate cuts, the leaks about leadership ignoring the state of the game from their community managers, who have been begging them all along to walk back some of their practices. All for naught. Destiny is a rotten husk of its former self, a leathered carapace of big studio corporate greed.

There are bountiful examples (from excellent creators) of their shady practices; micro transaction purgatory, sunsetting paid content, recycling said content as "new content" for a cost, creating "Starter Packs" for exotic weapons that were available through now sunset quests, event card microtransactions, dungeon keys, and the now infamous $60 (or $99) Lightfall expansion. It goes, on, and on, and on, and on. Please believe the reviews you see. Bungie has set the bar for truly deceptive micro transaction policies. It's not as blatant and outright as some Activision examples, but it is far more abusive.

The trailer for "The Final Shape" (which was supposed to be included in lightfall until the studio decided to split it), is so desperate for a nostalgia grab I have zero confidence in this studios ability to close out this 10 year saga with a grand finale with an original, emotional, cohesive conclusion. Lightfall undid all of the good will, excitement and trust that Forsaken and Witch Queen resurrected in the community. The original story tellers have moved on, or been let go, and replaced with message pushing megalomaniacs. Lightfalls delivery dumbfounded everyone, it was hard to comprehend at the time how it could have gone......so...........wrong. Hindsight, now we know.

It's clearly evident in their continued tone deaf policy decisions like the "Season of the Wish: Starter Pack." that the once great developer that WAS Bungie, is dead. And they're oblivious to the fact that their core group of players that would purchase "Marathon," are now likely having serious doubts about the studios ability to deliver a positive experience before their profit experience. I hardly write game reviews, I think everyone should experience a game for what its worth, good or bad. I cannot however, condone the constant, subtle drip of abuse that Bungie has pushed since sunsetting - described as a necessary evil in order to deliver new content. We turned them into the monster they are today by accepting that fate. I for one am walking away, 8 years of commitment, before the final chapter. That last sentence should be all the review you need.

-Abital, Bungie Battered Spouse Survivor

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tContext: I played pretty much all of D1 and release of D2 through Shadowkeep where I got off the train. This will be a combined review of both experiences.

Coming off of Halo, I thought Bungie couldn’t really do wrong. I wasn’t the biggest fan of Reach (self sacrifice loses its punch after the third character does it, loadouts in multiplayer were a turn off) but I figured it was because they were focusing their resources on making this story and campaign experience like nothing else.

And then they didn’t. Story was bland, I sorta felt the power of being a space lighting god with guns (Titan main, closest thing to Master Chief at the time) but things weren’t clicking for me. Raid puzzles just seemed like an exercise in running straight into a brick wall to figure out the answer. PVP and Strikes were fun but quickly became repetitive.

I told myself I would give them one more shot with D2 and it got better. Gameplay got cleaner, story was more focused on reviving society and everything seemed to be getting back on track. Forsaken, which I still think is a lot of peoples favorite, provided a lot of meat on the bones for player activity and interactivity as well as some of the best designed encounters in strikes.

Then Shadowkeep came out. And it was more of the same. And the hive aren’t really, gameplay wise, interesting enemies. And the loop got staler and more depressive. And after that the wool was lifted from my eyes and I saw all the skinner boxes behind it.

I’ve promised myself I wouldn’t go back and have been clean and sober since a Beyond Light free weekend years ago. It’s hard to see Bungie, who used to make some of the best FPS roller coaster levels turn into a dopamine drip feed machine. I’m lucky they make it so easy to avoid through expensive expansions that you both can’t play but also need to access all content. I just long for the the days of Bungies “show don’t tell” one man army player motivated feats of legend. I hope to see it again some day.\t\t\t\t\t
