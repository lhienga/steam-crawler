\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say. I've tried to love this game so often. Launch year was rough business, ToS and Warmind were very poor early expansions. It almost started looking up when Forsaken released. Then sunset occurred, and I didn't return for a long time. Dabbling a little with Beyond Light, and the monetization at the time just felt oppressive. So it didn't take me long to bounce off again, until I returned during the leadup to Lightfall. Gods, it's a disaster now. Keys to do dungeons, seasons, another for Xur, and holiday content even gated by another pass when it used to be accessible to everyone. There's...nothing left here anymore. Nothing new. It's rehashed over, and over, and over. I feel the most for the PVP players, who haven't had a true shakeup with maps and content in a very long time. This isn't even touching the eververse, where you can spend over a thousand dollars on 15-20+usd outfits a piece. Bikes, ships, shaders and emotes. It's...just cancerous. It's a frozen day in hell, for they managed to be more greedy after leaving activision than before.

It's so disappointing. Under the excessive, oppressive monetization and greed. There's a pretty good game in there, if it all wasn't copy-paste month over month and charged 20usd for the luxury of doing it all again. Especially when the new content always makes your gear irrelevent, even if you'd gotten it a week before.

Then finally, the cruel firing wave through some of the last longtime members was the final straw for me to leave this review. Stay away from this title, what good there was is only a memory left on repeat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2: A Game of Potential Marred by Corporate Greed and Community Strife

Destiny 2, developed by a passionate team at Bungie, stands as a testament to what happens when the pursuit of profit takes precedence over player experience. Despite the developers' evident dedication to the game's success, a deep divide between their aspirations and the profit-driven decisions of Bungie's executives has led Destiny 2 down a perilous path.

The heart of Destiny 2 lies in its potential—a potential marred by the overshadowing presence of microtransactions. The game's descent into what can only be described as "microtransaction hell" is disheartening. Players find themselves caught in a cycle of paying exorbitant amounts for half-hearted, reused content released under the guise of expansions, leaving a bitter taste in the mouths of the loyal fanbase. The S3 Iron Banner ornaments, a prime example of this, feel like a skibidy toilet of disappointment, with little value for the substantial investment required.

One cannot discuss the state of Destiny 2 without addressing the enigmatic figure of Trevor Allen Lozano Riopelli. It's evident that the developers' passion is often at odds with the profit-driven decisions made by executives like Riopelli. The result is a game that struggles to balance the pursuit of financial success with the desire to create meaningful, engaging content.

Trials of Osiris, once a pinnacle of Destiny 2's competitive scene, has seen its own downfall. The introduction of ximmers, along with the infiltration of racists, fascists, and GJake lovers, has turned what was once an enjoyable experience into a plague-ridden arena. The once-thriving Trials community now contends with issues that tarnish the spirit of competition and camaraderie.

As Destiny 2 grapples with its internal conflicts, the community yearns for a resolution. The potential is undeniable, but the shadow of fanum tax looms large over the game's future. The destiny of Destiny 2 is inextricably linked to the willingness of Bungie's executives, epitomized by Brandon Oliver Riopelli, to prioritize the player experience over profit margins.

In conclusion, Destiny 2 remains a rizzler of untapped potential, a game held back by a disconnect between its passionate developers and profit-driven executives. Only time will tell if the guardians of Destiny can rise above the trials they face, overcome the community strife, and steer the game back on course towards the greatness it has the potential to achieve.

I love destiny 2, don't play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a game you want to play with friends. It's also a game that is hard to tackle unless you're willing to pay out of pocket for all of the DLCs and expansions, which can get pricey and INCREDIBLY off putting for new players.
The game is fun overall. You'll spend countless hours shooting and looting. If player vs. player is your thing, there are plenty of competitive modes. If you want to do player vs. environment, the game shines here. There is plenty of loot to earn. Plenty of abilities to utilize. With Super 3.0 and Mod 2.0, the game offers plenty of buildcrafting. The gunplay is also great. You can synergize everything or just create some weird Frankenstein build.
From Vanguard Operations (Strikes) to Dungeons, and Raids. From Ritual activities to Story missions, there is plenty to do. Or you can jump into the Crucible and play against other players.
