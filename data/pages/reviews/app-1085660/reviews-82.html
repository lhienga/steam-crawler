\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing Destiny since the beta and I gotta say... the reward vs time spent in this game has become just about non-existent.

Game play is solid. You can have a lot of fun with the right people, and it's a great feeling completing your first end game content piece with your squad.

Story-wise the game just doesn't make sense sometimes and the seasons don't really feel like much effort was spent n them... you'll have a week in seasons where they ask you to go do a couple patrols or go from Earth, to the helm, then to some cave in the throne world just to hear someone talk about something. Then you get to go back to earth to report it instead of just sending them a message or something.

Choice is basically nonexistent in the game. there's no real choices to affect the story. Your guardian is basically a VERY silent protagonist that speaks once or twice every year or so it feels. It'd be nice to be able to affect the story in a different manner depending on choices you make and compare your play-through with friends.

Light matters very little now... You can be max level and still ride the struggle bus through some end game content because it's all light capped now. Not that a lot of the gear you get from those activity matters. You can complete a raid or dungeon dozens of times and still never get the gun or armor piece you're looking for. Unique guns are great when they first come out, but are quickly nerfed because it becomes the new meta. Bungie has a huge balancing issue every season. There's always a build or a weapon that absolutely breaks Crucible.

Crucible has it's ups and downs, but mostly is ignored for adding new content like big maps with vehicles or a map builder. You'd think for a company known for making Halo, that'd be a standard feature. If you're not a crucible player, then you're gonna dislike ALLLL the quests that force you to kill guardians or something that unlock legitimately good weapons.

Weapons are interesting. You can build guns with pretty crazy features, but can't install different sights, change a stock, or really customize it the same way most shooters do. Honestly a big shortcoming in my opinion as it's kind of an industry standard now. Why I can't mount a shotgun or a grenade launcher on my rifle baffles me when fighting space monsters wearing armor and using space magic weapons.

Supers are fun, but there are DEFINITELY supers that are better than the others. Some are just made for support roles, area control, or shielding parties from something that's about to hate crime you and your friends. Most players never use them... They just use the highest damaging and least effort super they can, so not much variety. you won't often see a Titan throw up a shield using their super to protect you and your buddies while you ctrl+alt+del the boss. You'll probably see a lot of Well Warlocks, Barrage Hunters, and missile Titans...until the metas change again.

All in all, I can't say that I'd recommend this game as they've had a lot of shortcomings... Lots of broken promises with launches and it doesn't appear that Bungie really respects your time within the game as a lot of your hard work can feel pointless. It's not a game where you can log in for an hour and do a quick mission and get what you need. Often times you can spend hours and come out with nothing to show for it. They also recycle a LOT of assets. Same models, but different colors, armor models cycle back in, but not a lot of the good one's from D1. You'll do missions going backwards through a story mission, instead of them just designing a level for it.

Old raids and strikes come back, but are generally the same.
