\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to lead off with the truth - I love this game. I started playing it back in 2018 and both the content over the last 5+ years, along with the friends made, have definitely helped me through some good and bad times. With almost 1,000 hours on Steam and an equal amount, if not more, on Xbox (before crossplay), I can confidently say this is a great game. However, I DO NOT RECOMMEND IT to anyone who is just getting into the game (not a returning player, but someone brand new). There are many reasons that can easily be found by a quick search on Youtube or google, so I'll keep it short:

-Frequent bouts of content drought due to Bungie redirecting their people to other projects (e.g. Marathon) that are funded by the excessive amount of micro transactions that are shoved down your throat at ever moment possible, and the rising costs of Destiny's content.
-Key components of core playlists have been neglected for a long time with, widely viewed, lackluster updates on a relatively sparse basis.
-Developers have openly come out and stated that they are giving up on key elements of the game because they made changes the community openly opposed, and were surprised it didn't turn out positively (I'm looking at you Gambit - RIP).
-MICRO. FRICKING. TRANSACTIONS. Bungie has proven that it is not hard to make armor sets/weapon ornaments, but do so and hide it behind a (*n on average) $20 dollar price wall. For those that aren't aware, that's essentially paying for 6 months of content on something that's shiny and blinky.

I could go on but it's a review and not a rant. For those of you that want to play - by all means! The community has some diamonds in the rough and it has a good amount of content to keep you entertained. Just be prepared for unbalanced classes, lack of technical support when something breaks (look up "the craftening"), and an absurd price tag. The game may come across as "free," and there are some elements that are - but if you're wanting to actually get into the game, and/or play with some of your friends who are already into it, you're going to have to buy the expansions. And the dungeon keys. And whatever Bungie has decided to put a price tag on.

With the final season ongoing, and the last expansion postponed from February to June of 2024 (a 188 day season, mind you), I wouldn't waste the money. The story is a monster to catch up on (DCV made it even worse), and by the time you've done so, the game will more than likely be on life support, leaving you wondering if the $400+ was worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
