\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a lot of Destiny 2, and I'll continue to play it. For most people, though, I can't confidently recommend it.

Pros

* Amazing experience. The gunplay, cosmetics, missions, and visuals are stunning.

* Nearly endless supply of content if you're an achievement hunter.

Cons

* The story is almost intentionally convoluted. It's near impossible to follow as a newcomer. You effectively have to watch fan-compiled plot breakdown/summaries on YouTube to follow what's happening.

* Content disappears. Love a mission and want to share it with others? Hopefully they're leveled up enough in time... It might just vanish completely forever (RIP to The Whisper).

* Loot grind is entirely unsatisfying. Swathes of garbage for what feels like no really desirable endgame gear. There's a select few pieces of gear that I worked for and actually am glad I earned, but it's only special weapons called exotics. There's no satisfying ways to grind for good armor, and the vast majority of non-exotic guns feel forgettable. If you came from the Borderlands series, prepare to be super disappointed.

* Waaaaay too many mechanics. There's a huge number of resources to manage, and few of them seem at all rewarding. Many different one-off mechanics like crafting that are hard to keep track of.

* Season pass rewards feel pointless. I only get the season pass for access to what is effectively the next plot steps. The cosmetics you can earn through the season pass have never appealed to me.

* Leveling up is SUPER confusing. To keep activities challenging, power scaling happens in various activities. It's not obvious when this happens. A given bog-standard enemy might take 3x as long to take out with the same gear and level depending on what activity you load into. Sometimes a gun feels like a pea shooter, sometimes it feels like a real weapon.

* Content is truly dumped onto you. Sometimes you'll open the game and be hit with 2 intro videos, loaded into a starter activity instead of the activity selection screen, only to hit another cutscene and more banners after you come back from another activity. It doesn't feel like suddenly there's new content to explore, it's like the game is saying "HEY TIME TO PLAY THE NEW STUFF I DON'T CARE IF YOU HAD OTHER PLANS!" It's jarring at best, and infuriating at worst.

So who is this game for? It's for someone that wants to unwind a deep, twisted, convoluted mess with a really cool story that takes a lot of work to unravel. If you WANT to wade through layers upon layers of complexity and achievement hunt to no end, the experience of this game is truly something to behold. I kind of like this, which is why I'm continuing to play it. If you just want to casually play an online MMOFPS every now and then with some friends, look elsewhere. This is simply not a casual game in any sense.\t\t\t\t\t

The game is repetitive and unless you've been practically playing from the first few seasons of the game, you miss out on a majority of the story not to mention many other things that have since been just totally removed from the game.

Don't get me wrong, it's an interesting premise to have an ever evolving world with an interesting storyline. However, there's next to no support for new players to catch up. There's too much to do and no guidance on how to do it. The game play can actually be pretty fun, but this game is just a void for your time and money at this point. In a world where companies are constantly competing for your attention, Bungie just expects that you'll keep up the constant grind with very little reward. Hate to say it, but if you want to see a grindy game done right, go try out Warframe.\t\t\t\t\t
