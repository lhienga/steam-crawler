\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is always more things going wrong with Destiny 2, and always fewer things going right. With shockingly few good new additions, the game feels like it's been running on fumes for years, sputtering away as the frantic pace of its release schedule drains any and all creativity from the sputtering development team towards a series of confusing, uninteresting and horribly expensive filler arcs.

Like all live service games, Destiny 2 suffers from a genuinely depressing amount of artistic erasure; the veneer of Bungie's creativity is constantly drained and tarnished by the frantic release schedule imposed by its seasonal system - hard to find the time, inspiration and energy to come up with a genuinely impactful and interesting story expansion when you have to slave away at making a filler season on a perpetual cycle. And as a player, it's hard to be genuinely invested in this rotation of dull, uninspired content doomed to evaporate once the next season hits.

If you have not invested any kind of time or money into Destiny 2, I urge you to keep it that way. The story as it stands cannot even be experienced in full ingame anymore, as many parts of it have since been removed from the game altogether - what is left is difficult to decipher, as the live service structure of the title takes precedent over the coherent structure of the main story, leaving you to run around collecting crumbs of missions to form something vaguely resembling a narrative.

I don't think there is much good in the future of the game, or in the future of the studio. The game, as it stands, struggles to remain legible as it limps its way to the advertised finale of a drawn out and exhausting story. I no longer care enough to learn what will happen then, and I am convinced nothing of value will happen next.\t\t\t\t\t

Because of the unforgivable way in which Bungie has handled this mass layoff, I have uninstalled the game and will not spend another minute or cent on any Bungie product again. There are other, better games available from developers who do not treat their employees like garbage. Don't play this game, it is not worth it. Details below from Paul Tassi ( https://twitter.com/PaulTassi/status/1719396652996317488 )

• The layoff decisions came directly from Bungie management, not Sony. This is not about Sony replacing Bungie employees with their own people.

• Many employee benefits (though not health insurance), only last until the end of the month if you're let go. Laying people off on the 30th means a single additional day of coverage.

• Many employees had unvested shares as a result of the Sony purchase. These shares would be received based on staying the with company for a certain number of years following the sale. But those shares revert to Bungie if you leave, even if you're fired, which is what's happening now to many of those affected.

• Many Bungie employees that were fired found out in the morning when they were locked out of services, logins, email access revoked.

• Others who instead found out in meetings were told not to tell team members themselves, as teams would be told "by other means." Many employees were unable to say goodbye or exchange contacts.

• Many team managers were not told at all about who on their team was being laid off, and only found out as it happened.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan it sucks to say this about a game that i used to really love and care about, but here we go. This game just keeps getting worse, I've been playing since Destiny 1s launch, and right now is just an all time low. recent news of bungie/sony laying off 38% of their team including composer legend Michael Salvitori who has been with bungie since before even halo, making some of their best osts. This layoff also includes most of their community and social media teams, which previously brought news out about the game every week, keeping us up to date with changes and also communicating our voice and opinions about the game to the developers.

Bungie also continues to increase their prices on expansions, season passes, and content as a whole. This game is advertised as "free to play" but that couldnt be further from the truth. The f2p gameplay is just doing really bad boring missions and then having access to do certain content and raids that you actually cant do because you're missing the content and abilities from dlc. So its just an illusion. Either way, the best parts of the game are hidden in dlc, which for all the dlc (which you need essentially as this is an mmo in a sense) it would cost you about 60 usd for the legacy collection, 50 usd for lightfall (recent expansion), and 30 usd for the armory collection. This is excluding annual passes which come with season passes (can also be bought individually), but also come with dungeon keys (which cannot be bought individually) which are needed to access dungeons which are a really good way to acquire gear and weapons that can only be obtained from said content.

This is also excluding buying the expansion releasing in 2024 (which has recently been delayed after laying off employees), which is 50 usd. Though this is just the normal edition and will not give you the season passes or dungeon keys, only The Final Shape + Annual Pass will give you these bits of content, which is 100 usd. Pre ordering The Annual Pass also gives you an exotic weapon (highest possible weapon tier) right now that will be exclusive to buying this annual pass and will be unobtainable by normal edition players for the remainder of now to the entire first season, which is usually about the first 10 weeks of the expansion launch, though the duration of this weapon being locked might be longer this time around as they're revamping the 4 season structure in favor of 3 "episodes" which essentially seem like seasons, but with even tighter time gated activities.

The Final Shape + Annual Pass package also comes along with cosmetics that will only be available to this edition. To which, there is an entire cash shop attached to this game, who could've guessed. About 99% emotes cant be obtained in game, only through the cash shop. There's also cosmetic armor that looks really cool, locked behind paywalls in the cash shop. Essentially every finisher animation cant be unlocked for free, and you'd need to either get one from the battle pass or buy some from the cash shop. No weapon skins can be unlocked for free and can only be bought in the cash shop. many more cosmetics locked behind here like ghost shells, sparrows, etc.

This game as i mentioned also has a 4 season structure. Essentially there are 4 seasons that launch and in order in between expansions. Each season brings new content to consume in between the wait for expansions. This structure may sound good on paper, but each season is just a copy and paste with a different code of paint and a new story. The new game modes they introduce in seasons are usually just the exact same thing everytime with a minor twist. These seasons just dont provide enough change to be any fun anymore, and they also cost the quality of the expansion that will release after. Comparing any destiny 2 expansion besides Frosaken (opted out since its an expansion made by three development teams) to the destiny 1 expansions are night and day. The Taken King just had more quality and a longer campaign compared to the campaigns we get in destiny 2 expansions.

TLDR: this game is not free since you need the dlc for the actual good parts of the game, and the dlc is extremely expensive.\t\t\t\t\t
