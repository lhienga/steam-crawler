\r\n\t\t\t\t\tToday is a sad day. I have finally come to accept that the last of the good developers at Bungie have left. Long gone are the days of tested and balanced game play and engaging mechanics. Now all we have are bullet sponge enemies and gimmicky junk in a cosmetics store that cost a disgusting amount of money. Will I still play the game? Yes, I will finish the fight and see through the game until the end of Final Shape as I have already sunk too much in to not. But I will under no circumstances waste my time on any future Bungie projects. I should have known the problems of the company were deeply rooted in the leadership from back when they slapped the loyal fan bases in the face time and time again with PlayStation exclusives after Xbox built their name, careers and fame. It's over folks.

For those of you that have already put thousands of hours in, finish it off and move on. For those just starting, get out now. For those who have not yet played, keep looking as this is not the place to spend money or invest time.


Just as a note, the fact that Destiny 2 hit the platinum tier in seam sales for the year and still they supposedly missed their revenue target by nearly 50% should be all you need to know for how pathetically out of touch the leadership are.\t\t\t\t\t

I miss this game. Part of what made me love the game so much was just a byproduct of being a kid, anything could grasp my attention as a kid. I never really understood that until I was already so committed to Destiny that no other game would be able to replace it. I've put enough hours, enough effort, and enough faith in this game that there won't be another game that will take it's place for me, not truly. I could see myself reliving the feelings I got for a moment but not the emotions, the magic wouldn't be there. There is an immeasurable amount of memories I've made in this game with close friends. Memories that are etched in my mind, more deep than many IRL experiences. Somewhere along the way, the magic revealed itself as a curse.

I first played immediately after the April update in The Taken King from D1. As a teenager, the open world and infinite possibilities caught me. Little did I know, this game would change my life. I soon found myself immersed fully in the Destiny universe. As highschool was less fulfilling than I'd hoped, I would begin to unconsciously dissociate from the life I was unfulfilled with, and would live it up in Destiny. I was able to refuel my ego that I left behind in sports through Destiny. I fell victim to the cycle between getting better at PvP and getting stronger in PvE.

I stayed in this realm for years. I let highschool pass me by while I convinced myself that my time spent in this game would be enough to make a career out of at some point. I wish I could tell my past self to just enjoy the time instead of trying to make it mean something financially, because it did mean something. I was just too damn distracted with some imaginary goal to see the real value it had. These days, I see my friends playing the game sometimes and wish I felt like playing with them. I know I've just used up the time I had to enjoy the experience of Destiny 2.

For what it's trying to be, Destiny 2 succeeds imo. Everyone is disappointed in the game nowadays but I think most people neglect the fact that experiences are meant to be temporary, and Destiny can only provide so much content before there just isn't another way to do things without changing the primary gameplay loop. That same gameplay loop, gets old. It got old for me, and I spent a few years doing my best to prolong it. In the end, I realized I couldn't keep it up and gave up on the game.

That temporary experience, is made even more temporary when the providers of such an experience are struggling and dangling from a cliff. One of the things that kept me playing the game all these years was the promise of more, but even if the financial situation is resolved at Bungie, the assurance is gone. Now, we just get the same recycled stuff, melted down and rebuilt, disguised as something "new".

If you haven't experienced an MMORPG, Destiny is OK. Just don't expect it to ever meet your expectations for what it could be. You'll always be disappointed with something in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
