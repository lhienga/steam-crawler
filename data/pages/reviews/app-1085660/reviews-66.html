\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSunk cost fallacy and a lovely clan keep me around, but in its current state I cannot recommend Destiny 2 to any person in good conscience. It's a huge game with a legacy spanning nearly a decade, but has repeatedly shot itself in the foot to keep players questioning each new decision. Content and stories are limited by seasons, mostly unplayable after the year is spent. New weapons cause fluctuations in a delicate and competitive meta that punishes using anything less than the best. I have never seen a game so riddled with bugs, glitches and connection issues--some that persist for years--that it needs frequent patches and downtime to fix. Each fix brings new problems, and so on. I think every expansion, as of typing this message, costs hundreds of dollars to acquire without a sale, even if each expansion's seasonal content is completely gone.

The game has its enjoyable parts, but the best of Destiny 2 just reminds me of other games that I've played elsewhere, for less time and much less cash. Great gunplay makes me think of Remnant 2 and Titanfall 2, but I didn't have to grind out each gun's godroll to get my money's worth. A challenge in the endgame with beefy enemies that hit punishingly hard reminds me of Nioh 2 or Elden Ring, but Destiny 2's endgame feels more like a slog than an achievement at times with nothing but cosmetics or marginally better (randomly rolled) weapons awaiting those who use rather selective meta builds. I'll always treasure memories of goofing around in raids with my friends, but I'll find good company in other games like Warframe or Deep Rock Galactic.

In light of current events and delays in the 'grand' finale of the game, I am not optimistic about upcoming developments. Whether the team at Bungie makes The Final Shape a success or not, Destiny 2 has already made its mark in the gaming community as an unforgettable experience. Whether it goes down in history as a legend, or an example, remains to be seen.

I will dip my toes into the game now and then, especially to raid with old friends and clanmates, but I'm hesitant to spend any more money on this game. I cannot imagine a new player struggling to keep up with it all, and finding out how much content has been locked off from them forever.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tListen, I haven't played in years for a good reason. Every time in the last 2 years that I've thought about coming back to this franchise Bungie has jacked the price to play, added pay to win weapons, renegged on promises, added some new predatory system or simply dropped the ball on content timelines. I played D1, I played D2 at launch and came back a few times. Add in my play time from before they migrated to steam and I probably have another 300-400 hours. Don't install this game, there is nothing here for you.

PVP is dead. Deader than dead. Enjoy content that's 4 years old with all the same glitches and more on the pile. Enjoy the same 3 metas and "brand new weapons" that are the exact same models from launch with a new shader and power crept perks. Hacking isn't as bad as it used to be but the few people I know who even bother to still play this dumpster fire still mention it every other match.

Highschool Freshmen creative writing assignments made 5 minutes before class have better composition and plot. The characters are carried by their VA's and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, they can't even save some of the plot lines that have been published.

PVE hasn't changed much either. Same boat as PVP, it's a rotating meta of the same old weapons with one or two new perks to roll and whatever overpowered pre-order or seasonal pass gun Bungie is pushing to increase short term sales. Raids are a joke, dungeons are over priced and half as engaging as they need to be, strikes have barely changed besides adding what amounts to the Tilt Skull from Halo back in the day.

Now with Leadership axing every staff position besides Management, you know the department most responsible for the 45% drop in sales due to poor direction, there is little hope that the ship will be corrected going forward.

Let it burn. Bungie is dead. This is just a walking corpse, put it out of it's misery.

Don't pre-order.

Don't give them a cent more.

Goodbye Bungie, best wishes for the devs that cared and I hope the little guys land on their feet.\t\t\t\t\t
