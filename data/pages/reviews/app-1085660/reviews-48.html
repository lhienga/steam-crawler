\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*sigh*
The Destiny franchise, an amazing game that I have been playing since the launch in 2014. I was 10 when I started playing this game now a young adult. I can say this game was the pinnacle of all the games I've played, but over the recent years Destiny 2 has not been doing so hot (like most games after a long time).
With around 1,000 hours on D1 + D2 on Xbox and 570 on PC I can say that my review has some legitimacy to it, while take it for a grain of salt for this was my experience, just another player to this amazing story of the Light vs. The Darkness.


This game is not what it use to be.


Would I recommend Destiny 2 to new players? Sure, but only because they have not been exposed to the game in earlier stages when it was 100x better. Would I recommend it to returning players? Hell No.

Past the Forsaken Expansion (my personal favorite and a lot of my friends too) and split of Activision from this project the content has gone downhill. In the current state of Bungie I can understand that it is hard to keep producing expansions that can live up to the hype of Forsaken, but they haven't, not even close.

Some of my complaints/negatives:
-The grind for weapons in the past 2 years have felt non-rewarding
-The story-line has taken a turn for the worse
-Seasonal content is scattered, hard to follow, isn't rewarding
-Funky exotics that are insanely good then Bungie cant handle it and nerf it into the ground
-Crucible META's that get fixed either in one day or one year
-Usability & Synergy between weapons that you will have to grind for to be able to do higher-end content (and I mean it, if you aren't running certain weapons that you have to grind HOURS and DAYS for each season (sometimes every few weeks) you wont be able to complete higher end content unless your an amazing gamer) While being a student playing in my free time just doing repeat missions to get one weapon for it to be outdated the next week really took a toll on why I don't play that much anymore
-Build-crafting, while I do agree that this has gotten better, the addition to more mods, intrinsic traits, more customization, it also takes a toll. Having armor builds that pair with weapons (go back to my last point) don't last very long. Each season every mod becomes disabled and you have to restart: New Armor, New Weapons, New Builds.
-You will have to play WEEKLY just to keep up with the season (and you don't get any good rewards). This is just some of the things me, friends and the community have experienced. The game now is just meh...
Basically the game is just too much grinding for it to feel not rewarding due to it being outdated shortly after. I can firmly say half of my hours in this game was just getting weapons to create builds, then having to redo it next season or during the season.

This next section is from what I've experienced and heard but no concrete evidence:
Recently Bungie has laid off around 30-50% of their employees for not making budget cuts with pre-purchasing of expansions & content, yet blame was put onto the player-base. Before the expansions were $10 cheaper and content was 100x better, now each expansion costs the same as a brand new game and you can't get into it if your not playing weekly. This may be why no one is pre-purchasing expansions and waiting for it to come out to hear reviews first... (which will most likely not be good following the curve recently). With such news the Final Shape expansion was pushed back HALF a year, causing even more refunds.
While knowing this I still purchased the Final Shape expansion purely for nostalgic and story-wise content reasons. Being a player since the very start it is hard for me to just stop playing right before we defeat the darkness (at least my hypothesis). Will I be grinding the game? No. Will I be doing weekly story missions and content? No. Will I do the final raid? Yea, probably (just because I've done the last few raid races). Back to my point, I am only continuing supporting Bungie because of a new game they are working on after Destiny called "Marathon", but mostly what they have done in my life: friends, experiences, life lessons and goals. Whoever says "it's just a video-game" doesn't understand what this game has done for me and yes, it is hard to explain.

As a final conclusion: This game is not worth playing OR getting back into.
While story and lore-rich (which I love and Bungie is great at (except the story for the last 2 years which has felt "jumpy")), playing the game is no longer fun. While I'm extremely thankful for the team working hard over the past 10 years, I don't believe I can whole-heartedly recommend this game which brings me to my low review. 2-3+ years back this game was a 11/10. Now if I had to sum every aspect of the game into one score (which is hard for me) I'd give it about a 4-6/10.

Hopefully this review helps you make a decision if you are thinking about playing for the first time or returning. If your new, give it a shot, meet some people that can guide you through the game and what it once was. When I think about this game that's what it was about. If your returning and need to purchase a few expansions to catch back up, don't, save your money for something else.\t\t\t\t\t
