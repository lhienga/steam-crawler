\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a time sink that draws you in with its amazing visuals, music and gunplay. If you're not careful however, it quickly becomes an addiction, which seems to be the norm with most live service games today.

I would still recommend this game, but if you're a new player, try the free version first. You won't get the story, but you can't get the entire story now anyway even if you're willing to pay, because it doesn't exist in-game anymore.

At the time of writing (Sep 2023) this is my recommended path for a new player to tackle this game: try out all the free content first - core playlists (Vanguard Strikes, Crucible & Gambit), the free dungeon (Prophecy) and the free raids (Vault of Glass, King's Fall & Crota's End). These alone will give you dozens of hours of varying gameplay, if you're trying to get all the loot.

Catch up with the story using the few in-game (free) missions, and the rest on YouTube.

At this point, if you're still interested in buying into the game, get the expansions on sale. They are discounted frequently, usually at the start of a new season, and during Steam's big sales too.

The community around the game is a mixed bag, but if you ignore the bad apples, you'll find lots of useful resources and tools to make your time with the game easier. Plenty of developers have created amazing websites and apps to ensure that you don't waste too much time dealing with the game's many systems.

Most importantly, do not fall for the FOMO traps that the game sets everywhere. You can ignore the game entirely for 9 months in a year, and then come back for the final season to catch up on an entire year's worth of content, without having to deal with the weekly drip feed schedule of the story.\t\t\t\t\t
