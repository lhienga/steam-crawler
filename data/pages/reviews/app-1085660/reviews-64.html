\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is going to be a mixed review. I'm going with a positive for now, but it may change depending on the next expansion in 2024.

I started playing it because of the lore. There is an incredible amount of lorebooks, short stories, and citations to find that develop the world of Destiny beyond what you are playing. It paints a rich, complex world populated with diverse alien races and with a history going back billions of years. Taking the time to connect the dots really enriches your gaming experience.

I stayed because the gameplay is very well done and really fun. It's a dynamic FPS, you have to move a lot and shoot a lot, combine your weapons with your powers ; it's very satisfying and you feel powerful when you manage to do what you want.

When I started, the base game and its two DLC were available and free : they were a great introduction to the story, the characters, the mechanics. Then I bought the Forsaken expansion, which blew my mind. Then I bought the Shadowkeep expansion, which was less mindblowing but still awesome. Then things started to change.

From the Beyond Light expansion onwards, Bungie started gradually removing old content. As of now everything before Shadowkeep is gone for good. Not only does this create story problems for new players, it means there's no sense of stability or nostalgia : you always have to play (and pay for) new content.

Witch Queen was a great expansion, but after that the game gradually became worse. The story is going everywhere and nowhere, seasonal activities all feel the same, and Bungie is relying more and more on selling things in the in-game store, the physical store, overpricing expansions and locking everything beyong a paywall. For example, if you buy a season that introduces a new dungeon, you don't automatically have access to that dungeon, you have to pay for it separately.

Currently, new players can find things to do as they have everything to explore (though story-wise it might be difficult to follow), but for older players the game has gotten disappointing.

I will keep playing until the Final Shape, but at the moment I plan on stopping after this expansion as Destiny 2 does not bring the joy it once did.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't want to leave this review. Destiny is the first game I vividly remember falling in love with back in the xbox days. Granted, my xbox broke before the taken king even came out so I missed what many would consider the golden age but loved the hell out of jumping off the map on Atheon to self res as sunslinger, glitching the bridge in Crota's End, and playing with my friends.

Preordered Destiny 2 as soon as it was announced and for the first few years I kept a warlock, titan, and hunter all at max light and grinded like hell. I've solo flawlessed all dungeons except for the new ones, have a bunch of seals including Rivensbane, I'm experienced in and have sherpa'd every raid except for RoN, new Crota's (which I've yet to run), and Scourge of The Past, which was sunset a couple of days after I started getting back into the game after a break. This brings me to the first issue.

Sunsetting. Man, does it suck. I loved the Leviathan raids - well, mainly Levi and Spire, but Eater was fun too. What I really miss, though, are the guns. My Midnight Coup and Ghost Primus will forever hold a place in my heart and my vault. My perfect rolled Duke Mk. 44 from the era where I loved hand cannons. Whisper of the worm meta was honestly a blast too, though that's still fine and just another gripe I have.

The original community is also a shell of what it once was. I've had multiple friend groups that I primarily made from joining 5 stacks doing raids and ended up joining one of their guilds for a while. Was a blast, we ran weekly raids, a bunch of nightfalls, and just overall grinded together. At some point (sometime after Forsaken, not sure when exactly), we all just take a break from the game. I think I'm the only one that ever came back, one is sometimes on but even then. And I can't say I blame them.

It just feel like Bungie doesn't value player time. And then, obviously, there's Lightfall. Personally, I liked lightfall. It was fine. I've always loved Calus and felt it was a good enough end for him, and day 1 RoN was a blast, but it just felt... unnecessary. The fact that the cutscene is very clearly spliced into 2 and everything we do somehow happens in between is honestly insulting. Like. We literally did nothing. Granted I had really high hopes for Lightfall. I love the Witness, Rhulk's easily one of my favorite characters in all of Destiny lore, and Vow's a GOATed raid. I just didn't feel the passion/heart was there. I've been trying but struggling to find my own love for this game again. I love the armor sets that have been coming out, some of the new exotics are great (Hierarchy of Needs my beloved), I try mixing up my builds but I just can't find the spark again. It's just the same old season after season, and now we're somehow gonna have another 7 month season leading into TFS, which... I sadly don't have very high hopes for anymore.

And, obviously, the lay offs. Like. Bungie, what are you doing. Obviously it's all terrible and an absolute nightmare for everyone involved but I still can't wrap my head around Michael Salvatori being included. I mean, are you serious? The music is, far and away, the best and most consistent part of this game. The nostalgia I have for the older tracks, the absolute awe that the DSC music inspired when you first exit the ship, the menu themes from Destiny 1 and 2. Come on, man. Now we won't even have that to fall back to in the future.

Anyway. Just my 2 cents, or 20 at this point. I could go on and go more into detail with all of this but it's all been said before and I'm sure it'll keep being said in the future. I'm disappointed, Bungie.\t\t\t\t\t
