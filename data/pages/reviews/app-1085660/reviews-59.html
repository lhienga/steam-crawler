\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 has a lot of great elements. Game play is fantastic (Ability, gun play, movement mechanics in particular). The lore is engaging and great. The downside is nothing you do really matters. Destiny is one of the stingiest looter games I have played. It feels like giving out loot, especially good loot, is not what the game wants. A grind doesn't bother me but when a company fails to even explain their loot mechanics there's a problem.

The seasonal model has made for a bland experience. I can wait until the end of the season and complete the entire season (seal/title included) in a few hours. That's what a 3 month season boils down to...time gated activities that you can complete weekly in minutes for potentially one cutscene that is impactful to the story. Everything feels phoned in at this time.

I wouldn't recommend this game to new players. The onboarding experience is terrible. Expansion/DLC pricing models are hard to figure out and should have been packaged years ago. Certain expansions have had content vaulted but the DLC still costs money. Bungie seems to invest in ways to better implement predatory tactics to get more money out of players then develop a system to bring new players up to speed.

I intend to continue playing to finish the the Final Shape but it feels like an obligation to see the conclusion to the story I've been playing for years more than wanting to actually play the game. Thank God for a good clan and friends I've made that make playing this game still fun occasionally through the conversations we have.\t\t\t\t\t
