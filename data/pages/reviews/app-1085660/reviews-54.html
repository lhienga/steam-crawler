\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's Destiny, what can you say, you either love it or hate it. Sure, there's a list of problems larger than the the EncyclopÃ¦dia Britannica, but it's a huge, gigantic game.

Pros:
  • The gunplay is sublime. One would expect nothing less from the original developers of Halo, but there really isn't anything that feels as good to play.. the gunplay is so polished, so smooth.
  • You can do as little or as much as you want. It's a looter so you get more by doing more, but once you've got your footing you can get baseline stuff done pretty quick/efficient. I don't have time to play it as much as I want and I come back to it and it's fine.
  • While there are microtransactions, like above, while you can spend as little or as much as you want, you don't really have to spend anything (outside of the yearly DLCs/season pass). Thankfully, a very large percentage of the microtransactions (and I'm talking like 90%+) are basically limited to cosmetic items, something that I personally don't care much about. Some of the items can be gotten w/o paying via RNG, while others are pay-only, but again, if you don't care about visually acessorizing your characher(s) in a certain way, you don't really need to pay.

Cons:
  • The Free-to-Play version, while it exists and is fully playable, severely limits the things you can do and experience. Every year you'll in essence be buying a new game in the form of the Yearly DLC. And it would really be a waste to buy the Yearly DLC w/o the Yearly Season Pass, so it's the Deluxe Yearly Bundle which retails for $99 (although it does get discounted, even pre-launch). The F2P aspect, IMO, is more to get your feet wet and see if you like the game.
  • As of late, it seems like some seasonal activities are low-effort recycled content. Some of it happens to be boring content that I don't do anyway, it just sucks when it's one of the activities you have to do for a given season.
  • Seasons can be hit or miss, some are stellar while some are lame.
  • If you're a highly competitive PvP player, there are various balancing problems there. I do not fall into that category and don't even play PvP on the regular, although for my skill level it's mostly fine when I do play special events like Iron Banner.

I love this game and even with the problems Bungie is having (as a company) I will continue to play it until the story ends (like I did Destiny 1), but that's definitely not the case for everyone.\t\t\t\t\t
