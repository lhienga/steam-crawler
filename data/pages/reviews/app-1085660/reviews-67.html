\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe truth of the matter is Destiny has always had some of the greatest potential in gaming. It is a master-class in terms of shooting mechanics, world-building, and community. I've played since the earliest tests of the original Destiny back before release. I've skipped maybe two minor seasons over my 10 years of playing. Community sentiment has always been back and forth as we've found ourselves receiving truly incredible expansions in the past, but then inevitably Bungie would miss with content, with aspects of the game feeling barren and forgotten.

I've never left a negative review, even when I thought this game was at its worst, because I always had faith they would pull it back and achieve the dream for what Destiny could be. This has now changed. Learning not only of the incredibly shady business practices of dropping so many talented employees in the most tasteless way possible, members of their so-called "family" crying at their desks as their teams watched on in horror having no idea this was coming and not knowing if they were next. What really hurts to know, is that many talented developers in the studio, who love the game and see its potential, have been forced into not realizing it for purely monetary reasons. Destiny was always a carrot and a stick, always waiting to finally get a hold of it, but now we realize the carrot was going to be behind an Eververse pay-wall.

Do not start Destiny, as it stands its not worth it. I really hope there is a major shake up with management and they figure it out, but for the first time ever, I really doubt it.\t\t\t\t\t
