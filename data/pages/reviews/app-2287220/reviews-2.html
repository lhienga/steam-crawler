\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really love watching the cars drive and watch the (almost) whole race - it's like watching a real F1 race most of the times. And the noise they make when downshifting etc. ahh... pure bliss.

I did need some time to get into the game. To understand how stuff works and to appreciate things. But it also needs time from you. If you are okay with always skipping practice/quali for example and go straight to the race it might not take as much. And also if you play the race with 16x mostly.

But if you really want to get the most out of your practice and quali sessions and then enjoy the race, you'll be sitting on it for a few hours. But it sometimes gives me the same feeling compared to watching an actual race. With the tension and the ups and downs. And then you start designing new parts and you can see your cars getting faster and faster, better and better. Or even a grid penalty will be depressing at first, but then you see your car climb up the ladder and overtake after overtake and it's making the race even more interesting than before (obv.)

But I do get that some people or bigger fans of F1 or bigger fans of Manager games come to expect a little bit more (i.e. build your own team - which they are bringing in F1 24) - but for me, "a novice", it's actually kinda fun. It's, as I said, time consuming but once I start I can hardly get myself to stop playing. I always say to myself "one more prac session, one more quali, one more race, alright one more designing a part, okay lets try this part in the next prac again" etc. - I, imho, can recommend the game. But best watch some review etc. too to get a better feel for it or make use of the 2 hours as much as you can to test it out\t\t\t\t\t
