\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had SC2 on the Gamecube and it was my favourite game for years. I've been waiting all along for a game the rivals this masterpiece.
SC3 was OK but it didn't equal the second. By SC4 I'd given up hoping for the improvements I wanted from SC2. I didn't even attempt SC5.
I think SC6 is great fun and has a lot going for it but I would have never paid 75. Thankfully I got a good deal for the game plus season pass during the winter sale. I'd recommend waiting for a sale and getting all season passes available at the time because this will add a lot of value to the CAS (Create a Soul) feature. There is also a load of CDLC which is good fun, and a really passionate and talented modding community.

Things I REALLY like:

- CAS: I've already made a nazi samurai, a Hindu goddess, Monkey (from Monkey Magic), Pious Augustus (Eternal Darkness) to name a few. It's great fun and I spend most my time creating characters to play with.

Things that impress me:

- The graphics are very good.

- Once you get the hang of how certain battle tactics work you can start to have fun (some significant changes since SC2 i.e. critical edge and reversal edge, not able to guard parry low attacks, only one button combination for grabs though you still use these with different directions for the different grabs)

Anything else:

- Namco have released these season passes which include new characters and kit for CAS. Not sure how long they will keep doing this but as mentioned the modding community will likely keep things fresh for some time to come if Namco give up. Sadly you pay dearly for these which is quite cheeky considering there isn't a great deal extra you get for the price.

Things that irritate me to the core but I pretend they're OK so that I don't have a nervous breakdown:

- Finding matches online "easily" is only possible through "Ranked" (you can still be waiting >5 mins, and you have to set network speed to "any" to have the best chance of finding a variety of opponents)

- As there are seemingly so few people online, you will get matched to the same few people over and over, who can be 50,000 XP while you are still on 150.

- The above scenario will result in 3 rounds of not being able to get up off the floor and then you have to wait another 5+ mins to have the same thing happen again. Weekends are a bit busier.

- Both the Libra and main story modes have the most annoying between-battle comic style story telling I may have ever encountered, and the narration makes me want to pull my hair out. You only unlock museum items as your compensation, plus the final boss Inferno who you can't use online anyway. I refuse to play these modes as they annoy me that much. Why, Namco, are you always getting distracted with these pointless extra features that distract you from making one single, excellently produced story mode? Even SC2 didn't have an excellent main story mode but it had a purpose and it had variety and it focused on just.... having fun.

Solutions:

- Give players more control over how they want to play. Let players send invites to their friends for matches.

- There has got to be more than 3 people available for me to play each night. Please improve this so I can more easily find new opponents, who are perhaps within the same 1000 XP. It's not my connection I have fibre BB and usually have the best connection in other online games.

- Let people invite their friends to both casual and ranked matches, and reinclude the 8v8 versus team battle mode. If people are worried about their ranking being made redundant -think about online chess e.g. Lichess. Just do the a similar thing.

- Hold regular tournaments for different experience levels and allow anyone else to spectate (again like online chess). This would help legitimise people's ranking if they want to prove to the world they are good.

- Scrap Libra and any other similar venture in future games. It's just not necessary. Focus on the main "story" and just make it fun, like in SC2 where you had to navigate dungeons and novelty objectives, and you actually won things like weapons. Nothing wrong with a comic style storytelling but just make it interesting, purposeful and stylish. It shouldn't be physically painful to endure.

In conclusion:

It's Soul Calibur, without a doubt. The game we have loved for years. Obviously a lot of effort goes into producing these games, so thank you Namco for keeping this going. However, it seems the same mistakes are being made after all this time. Please focus your efforts on less features of the game: 1) online = freedom 2) CAS 3) Main story = fun

It would be good to know what other people think about my comments, whether you agree/disagree with anything and why.

When did you start playing this franchise and do you like the way it has progressed? What would you have done differently?\t\t\t\t\t
