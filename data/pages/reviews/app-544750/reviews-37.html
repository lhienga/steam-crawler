\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe new SoulCalibur, the first one also released on PC, is in some way a return to form for the franchise. The fighting, while always very polished and precise, is enriched with soul charges, lethal hits, guard impacts and reversal edges. The latter are a sort of cinematic stand-off that can be resolved in a rock-paper-scissors kind of way, by pressing an attack button, guarding or dodging the attack entirely. While the new mechanics alone are not much news, putting them on top of an already solid groundwork does the trick and elevates SoulCalibur VI above most games in the series, from that point of view. The story seems to be a retelling of SoulCalibur’s plot, venturing a bit into the beginning of SoulCalibur II, but more attentive fans will be able to notice and remember (from SoulCalibur V’s ending) that there is more going on than just that. Because of this, many old faces return in the fighting roster, and several from SoulCalibur V are no longer present, mostly for the better. The guest characters, Gerald of Rivia from The Witcher series, Haohmaru from Samurai Shodown and 2B from Nier: Automata work well, but only the former really fit in the universe. The character creator seems to be taken from the last game in the series, slightly improved, but it is still only possible to use the fighting styles from the main characters (DLCs included). Both the audio and visual design are great for this genre, and the multiplayer aspect is just as fun. The biggest problem of SoulCalibur VI is the single player mode; while offering two long and winded story modes (Libra of Souls and Soul Chronicle), the delivery in both is incredibly poor and often times boring to experience. In some “stages” there isn’t even a fight, just text to read… Such a pity that Namco Bandai didn’t pour more resources into this, it would have elevated an otherwise amazing fighting game even higher. This way, SoulCalibur VI is a chore in single player, but, thankfully, a lot of fun otherwise!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game, it is a guilty pleasure of mine and I enjoyed playing it, but when I take a good hard look at the game, I have to admit it’s not something I would recommend. The story is a contrived good-vs-evil soap opera that exists just to introduce new characters. The characters themselves are bombastic and over the top. You can literally knock the clothes off your opponent. And all of that is *fun*, it’s definitely a certain teenager-in-their-parent’s-basement-drinking-soda-to-stay-up-all-night sort of vibe to it, and if that’s what you’re looking for, than this is the game for you. Thing is there really isn’t anything to it that isn’t done better in other fighting games. Game mechanics, storyline, fan-service, it’s all good enough in this game, but it’s not really *good*. I want to recommend this game, I enjoyed this game, but the only reason I bought it is because the Soul Caliber franchise was part of my childhood, and unless it was part of yours too I don’t see a reason why you should buy this perfectly adequate, but ultimately mediocre game.\t\t\t\t\t
