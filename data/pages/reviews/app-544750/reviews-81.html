\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not very familiar with fighting games. The last fighter I played before checking SC6, was some Mortal Kombat game that I played in early 90th on my friend's Sega. She lived nearby, so when we were not climbing on trees, roofs and fences - we played it rather often. But, as you can see, this is a part of distant past, so I will review this game as a newcomer without hardcore experience in fighting genre in general and SC francise in particular.

Let's start with the story mode. Definitely the weakest part of the game. Most of the stories here are weaker than in adult movies. They are so boring that I almost dropped the game after 1,5 hours of gameplay. One of the story doesn't even have any gameplay at all - during the whole episode you just keep listening pompous mumbling of one ancient hermit and that's it. In the end, I finished all episodes, and I can say that there are few actually good stories, but most of them come from the DLCs. Story mode is also extremely easy, so you can finish it by randomly mashing buttons with you legs. As you can see, it also kills any motivation to improve your skills.

Next one is Libra os Soul mode. And this is where the game managed to hook me. The story is still meh, but now this is your own adventure. You replay the main events at another angle with your own character. I can't say that there are some deep mechanics or cutting edge decisions, but entire gaming loop is very fun. Create you character - finish some quests - lvl up - get some new weapons - more quests - get some upgrades - more lvl up - make some good/evil choices, etc. I really spent a lot of time here. Even more, here you can also battle characters, created by other users.

Character editor. Another great feature. You like specific main character but want to change his fighting style? Just want to fix someone's face? Or create totally unique character? - All this is possible. You have really a lot of freedom here. There is a lot of great unique characters, created by the community. There are many troll-creations as well, but you have an option to switch them off. I personally do not think that it is a problem, as far as creator invested time and efforts to create something orignal. I mean, this game already has 2B and Geralt, so why would you be shocked by Jesus or skeleton in pink kitty costume with chipmunk voice? The only creations I dislike are those low-effort garbage, created within 2 minutes.

While the characters editor is a great thing itself - it has some issues. Many pieces of armor or clothing underlap each other while the game allows you to combine them. At the same time - there are pieces which (afaik you can notice) definitely should harmonically fit each other, but the game doesn't allow you to combine them. Weird. The game could also have more stickers/scars/facepaints options. And more additional items to wear (you can have only 3).

About community creations in particular. You can upload your own or download someone's creation. The problem is - there is no adequate way to see the list of creators/creations. You can just scroll them back or forward. Slowly. Painfully. Imagine having a thousand channels on your TV, without a way to choose specific one instantly, and you need, let's say 529th one? If there will be Season Pass 3, I hope they will x3 their efforts on Editor. This will bring back more players than any other character DLC.

About tutorial. You may say: "What the hell man? Are you for real going to waste time on tutorial in your review?" - yes, I am. Because this is crucial part of the game. It is extremely informative and very noob-friendly. Probably one of the best (if not the best) I've seen. You have a lot of general tips and tips specific for each characters. You have ingame tutorial in Libra of Soul where you prectice general moves.

You also have separate tutorial room where you can see full list of all moves and combos for every character, you can actually see how all these combos look in action so you won't have to guess if you repeated combo right or wrong. Even more, you can see full list of all buttons you pressed in tutorial. Thus, if you randomly made some cool combo, but have no idea how - you won't have problems to repeat it, because you can just check that buttons list.

You can also set specific behavior for your dummy opponent. Any moves, any attacks, any AI. Which is extremely usefull. Struggle to survive against specific enemy move? Just make your dummy opponent to repeat this specific move again and again and practice how to counter this as much as you need. Of course at first tutorial section looks very complicated and overweighted with tons of information, but step by step, step by step and you will handle this. The game has tons of moves, attacks, combos. Animations are very beautiful as well. And you will need to squeeze everything from the tutorial to improve your skills.

Arcade and Versus modes. Here you fight against CPU opponents. In arcade you fight in 8 consecutive matches with random opponents for bronze/silver/gold medals. In versus you fight either with CPU opponent or with another player via second controller. You can use here your own characters, created in character editor. Unfortunately you can't fight against them in arcade. But you can in versus. Strange. Would be great if they'll add an option to create your own "ladder" in arcade.

Multiplayer. Casual matches and ranked matches. It's rather self-explanatory. Let's talk about it's issues instead. First - it's player base. And it's rather low. From time to time you will struggle to find any opponent at all. So you either have to play MP in specific time, or to find some buddies for private lobbies. Another issue - match making. It's a common thing to see a match between a plyer with 0 RP and 70k+ RP. A-a-and it's rather far from fun. Probably this is partially a result of the previous issue, but still.

Technical issues. I didn't have a single bug in any mode, but sometimes game tries to give me some hardcore console experience by dropping FPS to 30. And it doesn't matter where - sometimes during battle, sometimes in character editor, sometimes in main menu. Not that often to call it a problem, but nevertheless. When you choose your character for battle there may be even more severe FPS drops.

Conclusions. I couldn't give you retrospective analysis of the whole francise, so I can't say if the serie evolves or degrades. But I covered all what the game has at this moment. And it has a lot to offer. It has a lot of issues as well, but I suppose that it is safe to say that overall, the game is capable to give some very good experience.

Probably one last word about pricing. Definitely it is not good to butcher game for tons of additional dlcs. It is especially painful issue for fighting games. But if we compare SC with another fighting games (yeah, yeah DoA, I see you there) - probably this monetary policy is comparably acceptable. If you are planning to take it - get Deluxe one, so you won't make my mistake. It already comes with SP1 (you will need those parts). And you may get SP2 later on sale to cut some expenses.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDISCLAIMER: I am posting this on behave of my close personal friend [EM] Prodigy101 (STEAM_0:0:51200497) for he does not own this game but has played it on a regular basis with me through steam remote play together.

Soulcalibur 6 is not like most soulcailburs and for sure nothing like soulcalibur 2, which has been my favorite childhood game, which may have a play in the bias I have against this game, but this not why I am giving it a negative review, so here me out on what I have to say before you make your decision on buying this game.

THE GAMEPLAY: The fighting mechanics have had little changes over the years for different soulcailburs and this one is for sure one of them. First thing to note is the parry system, Now soulcalibur always had some sort of counter attacks in play in their games but never a fully made parry that can be done on demand (At least not in any I played). I have such a love hate relationship with this mechanic in fights for quite a few reasons, one of them being that it disrupts the flow of the fast pace combat too much and takes away the fun of the flashy moves which is instead replaced by the ultimates/supers and power attacks/unblockables. Though the parry has a nice change of pace to the constant button mashing and attack spamming from newer players, this helps with less cheese and stops obvious predictable attacks, though it just does not fit well here in this game, and even more so with how poorly made it feels along with this games pretty poor hit detection at times. Two, the super attacks. I hate the supers that characters can do, they are near instant, uncounterable, and hard to block, you have to already be blocking in advance to counter it or be at a distance enough away from it to not get hit by it, and even worse if you both use your supers it's who ever hits it faster wins and the other just loses theirs altogether, how fun. A new thing in this game which I hate the most is the stupid reverse edge mechanic that plays like rock, paper, scissors, it's very cheap and plays like a guessing game half the time which ruins the flow of the fight and outright feels unfair at times.

THE CHARACTERS: First of all, the roster is small and gets boring quite fast, though you can create your own versions of fighters, they still have to use the souls of the characters in the existing roaster, which will only be fun for so long. The balance is wack as balls too, nightmare, aswel, and a few others that are just straight up over powered and unfair to fight against, nightmare has a hard counter ability that he can just pop at will to regain the initiative over his opponent like they mean nothing and has hyper armor in his super, making him always win super v super battles and counters. Characters also don't feel the same like in older games either, so you may have to relearn a bit to get used to them. The only thing I am happy to see is geralt guest stars in this game with his own map, so that's a plus, he is also pretty cool and fun to play.

THE CONTENT: The game is quite pricey for how little it offers, the small roaster and bare bones character creator make this game boring quite fast, along with very little stages to fight in and non sense high dollar DLC for characters you have to pay for, make this game feel like an unloved cash grab and insult to die hard soulcailbur fans. The soundtrack is repetitive and boring, the story was the same as it is in other games and remains stale from what I remember.

THE SUMMARY: This game still brings nostalgia from the older games, but the characters look different from other games and even feel a bit different too, it's slight revamped combat system and ultra flashy moves over blow the games charm and fighting aggravating to play rather than fun. It's extreme lack of content and small roaster with paid for fighters that make it more annoying and difficult to download/make custom characters, along with a very small and boring selection of stages will have you bored quicker than most free games you will find already on steam. I really hate to make this review so harsh, I wanted to like this game, but the lack of love for the series clearly shows, which is why I am showing my lack of love for it in return, 5/10.\t\t\t\t\t
