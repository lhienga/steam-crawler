Summary
This fighting games is quite fun to play with as it brings the use of weapons and 360 degrees movement similar to "Tekken" and "DOA" franchises. The combat segments and storyline is fairly decent compared to previous installments.

As a fan of the "Soul Calibur" franchise, since the very first game that was in PlayStation 1, Soul Calibur VI seems to be a remake where the story is between the 2nd and 3rd game.

Each characters have their own story and perspectives, apart from that, the character customization has been enhanced compared to the previous games which is quite a boost.

Gameplay
Players follow the story of multiple characters regarding the Cursed Sword of Destruction called "Soul Edge" and the Sword of Salvation called "Soul Calibur". Each character has their own unique abilities and movesets like most fighting games.

The character creation "campaign" game is a recurring theme of the Soul Calibur franchise, players can create their own character and play the story whilst making decisions that tilt the character allegiances towards good, or evil.

Graphics
The graphics are quite beautiful and smooth with little to no delays using RTX2080 on maximum settings. The overall graphics retains the colourful settings as it was in the previous games, instead of opting the hyper-realistic graphics.

Audio
Audio supports THX Enviromental Mode that pin-points 3D sound making the sword fights feel "alive". The music along with the voice acting is also very good as it was in the previous installments.

Custom character voices can also be changed, not only in tone, but frequency and speed which. This option is very rare in any games including games with custom characters.

Controls
Controls are rather basic like any other fighting games, the combos are extremely simple (depending on characters) and moves are more time-oriented (Example: Holding Back+A for 1 second or instantaneously does different attacks)

Playing using a Keyboard so far has no issues and is quite comfortable. Obviously, using controllers is the best option.

Bugs/Issues
Bugs: No bugs found yet
Issues:
1. Some characters are quite overpowered
2. Some moves are very cheesy (quite common in fighting games)
3. The campaign (Libra of Souls) missions are unfair as the AI has insane handicaps (Example: Enemies does not flinch or is invulnerable and requires to be knocked out of the ring)
4. In campaign mode, weapons or items cannot be sold or disassembled (this forces players to do additional unnecessary farming)
5. The campaign (Libra of Souls) mode is quite lackluster compared to Soul Calibur III in the PlayStation 2
6. Arcade mode also lacks the amazing interactive cutscenes available in Soul Calibur III
7. Higher levels of Libra of Souls are more of a chore and not fun at all since enemies are more overpowered and includes idiotic rules to handicap the players even more. Not only higher level difficulties will cause the AI to PERFECTLY read all player inputs, they can counter you BEFORE the animation even begins (many cases their moves are IMPOSSIBLE for any human players to perform with perfect frame counters, etc)

Conclusion
Overall a very story rich fighting game, where players get to connect with the characters played with their own unique stories. Libra of Souls Campaign mode is quite disappointing (when compared to previous games) due to lack of story, depth and animations.

The paywall DLCs to access existing characters from previous games is really bad with multiple negative ratings. Fighting PvP is also quite straightforward and fun, can be played using Steam Remote Play. For those whom enjoy character customization, this game is highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
