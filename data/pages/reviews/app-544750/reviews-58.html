Criminally underrated and overlooked fighting game, both in general and within its series.

This is easily in the top three best Soul Calibur games, if not the best one for solo and more casual players.

The game has an enormous amount of content and does something I wish the series did ever since I first touched Soul Calibur II: An actual in-depth story mode for each of the character from the roster with full voice acting. Sure, conversations are in a Visual Novel format, but I will take those interactions over the "very little" to "nothing" that the other games gave us. This delivers, and Soul Calibur VI manages to also give us another complete story mode in the vein of Mission Quest (SC I) and Weapon Master (SC II).

In case you're a newcomer and wish to know about the story of the fighters, and you feel like you need to play the first game in order to understand anything, don't worry; the "VI" in Soul Calibur VI is sort of a lie. Soul Calibur VI's story is a reboot in a different timeline from previous games, and retells the story Soul Calibur I with some narrative differences, and goes as far forward in time as 1590 AD (equivalent of Soul Calibur II to IV) with a completely different story. If you wish to learn more about the story and the fighters (cause I know some people like to play fighting games for the story), this is arguably the best starting point all things considered, as this is in the realm of "familiar enough yet entirely self-contained".

But how is the core fighting? I'm glad you asked. The speed is pretty much on par with Soul Calibur II (which is what apparently all the pros wanted) and everything else is here and accounted for, including the some of the better mechanics from Soul Calibur V. The only odd new mechanic is the Reversal Edge, which is more flawed online than offline since it is an effective defensive/counter-attack move and avoiding it through side-stepping is harder with lag. At the very least it's not OP at middle/higher levels of play, since it's extremely punishable.

There are only two spots where the game slightly disappoints: The base roster without DLC is a bit slim, and the graphics aren't exactly stellar. They're still very good, but nowhere near the tech marvels we're used to for the series. At least the soundtrack still slaps, and in case you'd still not be into it (for some baffling reason), there's the option to pick tracks from the older games! (although they're unlockables)

Also, mind installing this on an SSD, or an HDD with a system that works on a separate SSD. The loading screen on SSD for starting a fight is about 6 seconds long (counting the Versus animation, maybe the game is already loaded idk), which is about equal to Soul Calibur IV on Xbox 360 installed on the hard drive. But on HDD? It can take ages, especially on old gen consoles, some of them clock between 30 seconds to a full minute.

Other than those slight issues, this is a must own.\t\t\t\t\t
