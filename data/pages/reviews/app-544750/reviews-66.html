\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI previously enjoyed SC4 on PS3 and I'm enjoying SC6 on PC. I'm no fighting pro, but the controls are relatively straightforward (once you get the hang of the universal special moves) and the create-a-character story mode that has you fighting around the world.

That said, match load times are a bit sluggish given an SSD and a ton of RAM (on the order of few seconds -- what are you even doing, game?), it could sorely use some antialiasing, and the tutorials could certainly be more comprehensive, but it all does work neatly together. If you liked previous SoulCalibur games I don't see why you wouldn't like this one.

Unless you are a fairly hardcore fighting game enthusiast, I find that fighting games get pretty hit-or-miss; sometimes you just don't like the way one plays or the mechanics it encourages. With its relatively "grounded" realism (as far as fighting games go) and use of different weapons for different ranges (as opposed to fireballs etc.), SC is what floats my boat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe core fighting mechanics of the game are solid, but I feel like some of the "challenge" modifiers in single player weren't playtested at all, and the AI can go eat a giant bag of ♥♥♥♥♥. On the higher difficulties, not using any inputs will just cause the AI to become confused, not really doing anything besides using an occasional random attack which may or may not fit the situation, but by god the second you press a button, it will instantly auto counter whatever input you use. It's like playing against someone with some sort of 99% auto-counter/auto-block hack. Even more, the AI can just teleport. I have recorded it being midair one frame, then crouch blocking the next. In short, the AI flat cheats. It forces the player to adopt a playstyle of "What one attack will cheese this AI best?" So you pretty much have to juggle the entire SP, which is frankly just not fun and boring. Plus, the AI likes to abuse using Critical Edge even when they have absolutely no soul charge (Looking at you final boss in a certain pyramid).

What really kills the game for me is it has some SERIOUS issues registering and executing inputs compared to other, FAR more polished games. Most games seem to have a slight 'buffer,' for lack of a better word, where if you input a punch slightly before you can, you'll still execute the punch. Basically, your timing doesn't have to be frame perfect. Soul Calibur seems to have a massive buffer, so you'll input a command, but say you get hit, well when you land, it'll still execute said command within the context of when you inputted. So lets just say you input a sweep kick, but the enemy hits you and sidesteps. After you recover from the hit, even if you change your inputs to reflect the new direction the enemy is in, you'll still sweepkick the ground where they were literal seconds before. If you button mashed, the buffer seems to save two-three of those commands, so you'll uselessly sweep kick the empty ground facing the wrong direction three times in a row, while you helplessly input a horizontal attack in the other direction. Oh, and the Aerial Control is a straight up lie. It will only work for me in the tutorial. In actual fights, though? Doesn't matter what directions I input, I am only going in the direction I was tossed. If the AI decides to juggle, there's absolutely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ I can do about it. A fact which the AI is more than happy to abuse on higher difficulties.

The reversal edge thing is kinda cool on the face of it, but I don't like having a luck mechanic in fighting games, which are supposed to be entirely skill based.

The character creator is pretty awesome, not gonna lie.

3/10, I'm going back to Guilty Gear and Skull Girls

Addendum: To make the game more enjoyable, seriously, fixing the AI and the controls would take the game from 3/10 to 9/10 easy. From a technical standpoint, I don't know how that's achieved, I've only ever modded FPS games, and it seems to be an engine problem, but I can't imagine it would take much. Maybe just give the AI realistic reaction times, and give them the same limitations as the player, IE, can't teleport into a crouch block to cancel a juggle, or moves slightly telegraphed instead of Nightmare being idle on frame, and his sword is hitting me in the face literally the next frame? That would be cool and not make me hate the game.\t\t\t\t\t
