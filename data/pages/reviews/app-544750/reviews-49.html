\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFull disclosure: the last time I played a fighting game was in second grade, so I have zero experience in the genre.

That said, this game is a great introduction, especially coming from soulslike games, I was very impressed with the combat system.

I remember in old MK it was all about special moves for each character, nothing much deeper than just spamming a couple of best moves over and over.

In this game, however, there are both general rules for attack and defense, and a rich set of combos for every character. I only scratched the surface so far, but the possibilities are infinite.

I don't feel overwhelmed either, because there's a very good tutorial, which teaches you the basics in a few easy steps (took me less than 30 minutes to go through it). You can practice different combos in training mode on passive or active enemies, and then try your hand against NPC in either story mode, versus or arcade matches.

Character creation is top notch, I didn't even know fighting games could have such a good redactor. Your own character adopts moveset of one of the main characters of course, but there's still a lot of "build" variety in my opinion. And they look great too.

Online seems to work fine too, and though there's not many people on Steam, I did get matched today 3 times in about an hour with no issue. You can enter a "standby" mode in training, so you can practice on NPC while waiting for a network match.

Of course, Versus mode supports two players at one PC, so you can play with friends. For example, my little brother kicked my a$$ so hard yesterday, even though he just picked the game, and I've been playing for hours.

Can't say how the game compares to any other fighting game, but I liked it a lot, and definitely going to sink a lot more hours into Soulcalibur VI.\t\t\t\t\t
