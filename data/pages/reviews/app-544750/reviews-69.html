\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game itself is fine, but it lacks allot of polish.

Its a total downgrade from previous games like IV. It has less game modes, less replay-ability, less customization options baseline, less effort put into the main story, less hidden characters, just less of everything in general.

Game is poorly optimized and has the crisis effect of running like trash on very high end hardware. Online matches are basicly unplayable to the point that they have "Lag training" to play with super lag the game makes for you.

However, being able to apply textures and "stickers" onto custom characters and stretching and forming standalone objects to make truely insane outfits is a very big appeal!

If they would add a tower of souls like they had in 4 where you could fight endless waves that got harder and harder one way, or gear based progression path the other, it would add allot of replayability to the game.

TLDR; Needs more game modes, customization content and game optimization.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst impressions review, just a disclaimer.
I'm somewhat of a fighting game casual, I have a few titles here and there and this is my first Soul Calibur game. Going into this, I was worried I wouldn't be able to follow the story or really care about the characters, but the two story modes do a good job of introducing you to the world if you're new (particularly the story mode that focuses on the character roster, since it features voiced mini-drama recaps). The character customization is diverse and has plenty of options to make your own fighter. Gameplay itself is reminiscent of others in the fighting game genre and the range of characters provides varied playstyles even if some weapons repeat (such as a few characters having rapiers). The game runs very well on my 2019 gaming laptop with medium-high graphics.

My only negative point is that the keyboard controls are not optimal. I never had a controller and bought one just to play this and once I started using the controller, I felt much better. If you're a diehard fighting game lover, chances are you already have a controller, but if you're like me, I recommend getting one.

Overall, a very good first impression and I think if you are curious about the game, it is certainly worth a shot!\t\t\t\t\t
