\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSoul Calibur VI is a great 3D Fighting game and one of the few that feature ring outs. If you play Tekken you'll find the movement, along with some other fight elements kinda similar, but it takes some time to get used to. Blocking is different and the moves and strings are performed in a characteristic SC way. The gameplay is fluid and consistent and the character animations are amazing. The netcode is good and the fight mechanics are satisfying and one of the best in the genre. The music in this game is just amazing (10/10), in my opinion, and the stages are gorgeous. In terms of content it features all the game modes you're used to in fighting games. Story mode with a main timeline and also individual timelines by character. Battle, which has your typical modes like Arcade, Versus and Training. Creation mode, which in my opinion is the best and most complete in a 3D fighting game and "Libra of Soul" which is the story mode where you create your own character and move through a more personal experience of the Soul Calibur Story. It features RPG elements like leveling up, main and side quests, buying and upgrading weapons, meeting the other game characters... All this in an open world where you can move freely. In addition you have a "Museum", which is a gallery for all the unlockable content related to game art, media and lore from SCVI and past entries. The shortcomings of this game are the lack of more cutscenes to solidify the story experience. "Libra of Soul" despite having the battles and some cutscenes displays the dialogs with a visual novel style and re-uses a lot of assets for non important characters. Also Online matches are sometimes hard to find in this PC version of the game.
In conclusion, SCVI is a great 3D fighting game and playing it is enjoyable and fun and its combat system and characters are intricate enough you can lose countless hours to master them. If you like character customization you'll have a lot of fun with Creation mode.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fun enough, I guess? Wish there were a "~" rating because the game is mechanically mostly okay (aside from one big mechanic, but more on that in a minute), but Bandai Namco can't help themselves and keep mucking up a good thing.

Anyway, the character creator is only sort of good in single player, and potentially busted in online (character sizes change stats, and also I hate playing against intentionally "wacky" create-a-characters). There's no option presented in-game to play online without custom characters, no "pure" mode where you don't have to play against a Soul of Astaroth abusing the size gimmick for easy wins, or a Soul of Voldo with giant apples covering their weapons to decrease their readability. This will never change, however, because Bamco wants people to spend money on their costume pack DLCs so they can show off their sword Barbies online. Local gameplay feels... fine. SCVI is fun to play with friends, feels like old times in a lot of ways.

Here's the big reasons why I don't play it very much anymore:

1) The Reversal Edge mechanic (allegedly just rock-paper-scissors, but not really because some moves are better than others) stopped being fun the second time it happened to me. I didn't initiate it, my opponent did. Bamco, don't make me play RPS in the middle of my fights, just let me fight my opponent. Briefly stopping the match to go all SUPER COOL SLOW-MO so we can both push a button and then see who pushed the right one (spoiler: Astaroth did) is lame and not at all exciting. Why isn't it exciting? Because there's already plenty of exciting guessing games to be had in each character's unique move list and play style. Adding another layer of guessing games (especially one that forcibly takes me out of the action in order to play something as basic as rock-paper-scissors whenever my opponent pushes R2) isn't fun, but in fact very, very tedious. Let's be honest: if I wanted to play RPS, I wouldn't be playing Soul Calibur.

2) Bamco's DLC practices are predatory, nasty, and unfortunately completely expected now. They regularly lock finished content from the base game behind a launch-day "season pass," evident in SCVI with (at the very least) Tira. Then, after you've bought Season 1 (a 50% markup on the base game) just to be able to potentially play it on the same level as others, they hit you with a Season 2 pass that adds another ~55% markup. Season 2 always starts with another fan-favorite, "mysteriously missing at launch" character to manipulate players into buying the pass without reading, followed by a bunch of old content you should have received as free updates (because ported assets from SCV should cost the player nothing). Pass 2 then fills out with an extra-lukewarm new character, and at least one unbalanced/overpowered character that ruins the game for months (or potentially forever, like Leroy in Tekken 7) before the "78% off!!" Steam sales start, and Bamco decides to finally think about patching their clunky new DLC to keep those dwindling review scores up.

tl;dr: It's mostly enjoyable to play, but mechanics like RE are dubiously entertaining at best, and the DLC is overpriced (and/or just lazily reused assets from older games), but that's not a surprise at all from an anti-consumer company like Bandai Namco. If your soul still burns, only pick up this one when it's on a big sale, because SCVI still isn't as fun as SCII.\t\t\t\t\t
