\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the moment, online has very few players. It takes a long time to find an opponent in ranked, and almost nobody in ranked is new to SC6, making it very difficult for new players to gain ranks. To make matters worse, there appears to be no penalty for rage quitters. For unranked, there are no quick matches, so the only option is to play in casual lobbies. While there are some players new to SC6 in lobbies, it can be a poor experience. Some hosts will just kick players that beat them. Some veterans will join rooms created for beginners and just destroy new players. Some players trash talk constantly. However, when able to find games against other similarly skilled players, the game is very fun (though I can't say that I like Reversal Edges). Against players with good connection, lag is acceptable, though there are some random disconnects. In any case, since it's not easy for new players to get games against similarly skilled players in online matches, my review will mostly cover the single player experience.

The main single player mode in SC6 is Libra of Souls. The player creates a custom character and go on a text based adventure with very light RPG elements. I was hoping that I can unlock and equip abilities that really alter gameplay as I progress (like Street Fighter Alpha 3's World Tour mode). Unfortunately, it seems like the only character progressions are increased HP and being able to equip higher level weapons. It's not even possible to form a party with other SC6 or custom characters.

Rather than learning and equipping abilities, the player can add enhancements to a weapon. Unfortunately, enhancements can only be added to weapons that have empty enhancement slots. If there are already enhancements in the slots, that weapon cannot be edited at all. This really limits weapon customization.

As for the enhancements themselves, they're mostly just generic and uninteresting stat increases, such as higher critical damage, higher attack damage, etc. I was hoping for mods that can functionally alter gameplay (for example, a mod that has a chance to make my attacks unblockable, a mod that lets me cancel any attack into a Critical Edge, or a mod that gives me a powerful new move, etc.). Fighting games are about mind games and AI cannot play mind games like human players. Because of this, single player modes in fighting games need something to drastically change the way the game is played to make it fun playing against the AI. Unfortunately, due the the lackluster weapon enhancements, I felt like I was doing nothing more than playing a regular fighting game against the AI, with little to truly enhance the gameplay like actual RPG's.

In LoS, battles have certain conditions. Sadly, majority of these conditions are no different than stat differences. For example, one encounter may gives increased horizontal damage, but worse vertical damage. The few instances where there are actual mechanical differences, they're rarely for the better. Slippery floor is just awful. I could be on my last opponent, who is 1 hit from death... then my character just slides out of the stage and I have to restart from the first opponent... Some of the opponents have powerful advantages. Combined with AI reading inputs and having instant reflexes, these encounters were more aggravating than fun.

Character creation is really good, and the reason I bought the game on sale. There are tons of things one can do with the character creator. And it's not just custom characters that can be edited. Existing SC6 characters can be edited, too. Players who enjoy character creation can spend hundreds of hours in the creator mode alone. My only gripes with the creator are that you can't copy and paste creations between Libra of Souls and the character creator mode, and too many customization pieces float above/around the character's skin instead of properly fitting.

Fighting games are not designed to be played against the AI. However, due to the deep combat systems, a fighting game can be an amazing single player experience if one features a full fledged single player campaign with deep RPG-like elements. SC6's Libra of Souls misses the mark. In the end, I suggest playing a single player game over SC6 if you just want to play alone. However, if you do enjoy playing fighting games alone, then SC6 is one of the better choices. Despite its downfalls, Libra of Souls is decent and still better single player content than what you get in most other fighting games, and the character creator is really good. Not only that, but if you can get games against other similarly skilled players, the game is really fun. I do recommend waiting for a sale, though.\t\t\t\t\t
