A Tale of Souls and Swords, Eternally Retold

SCVI is a weaponized fighting game, that lets you swing horizontal (A), vertical (B) or kick (K) your opponent's ass, with a colorful roster of silk smooth anime crushes, there's a rapier guy, an Italian man that's really into BDSM, a nunchaku pirate, a very sexy ninja/demon killer and a promiscuos mistress with a whip sword...not enough?? OK , there's an edgy humanoid monster with a gigantic sword that comes with a manacing venous eye stuck into the plate of it, symbol of the corruption that took the best of him, umh...he is evil and also very evil .

Still not enough, I suppose.
We have Yoshimi-

In Deep Analysis
(sort of)

SCVI is a really solid...I can't stress that enough, REALLY SOLID entry in the franchise, maybe second only to your [] insert your childood Soul Calibur game here [/i] ]. Every character feels vaiable and in fact it is, each one of them with their own given sub-mechanics and mix-ups to catch off guard your opponent, and perform very cool combos (because, let's face it, most of us strive just to flex our best combos against our opponents). Althought I've personally run into random problems with the pc version, like sudden disconnection from the match or occasional frame rate drops for whatever reason, Soul Calibur VI lives to the standard of the series, and stretches them forward.

Someone could say that the new cinematic standoff moves (aka "Rock/Paper/Scissors instance") can break the action or can result cheap to some: I can see you saying that as a beginner, and yes, it can be frustrating, but believe me, they're punishable too, you just have, like in all fighting games, to extend your knowledge one fight at a time. It's a time/effort process, that...requires a bit of luck sometimes.

I heard many praies about the story (Libra of Souls), but also that it was rushed out of production, and...I cannot recommend it at all, there's no excuse to it...it's an empty shell, dull and generic. Libra of Souls is just a cover up tale to say "look, we were not really going to make an engaging story, because we really had no time left at all, so, we're gonna let you scroll on a never ending wall of words just to let you take a meaningless pivotal decision between good or bad at the end of every written dialogue, that culminates into a fight against a stunned AI that just can't keep up with you". I cannot see anyone replaying it, aside for unlocking every achievement, by furiosly pressing "skip dialogue" at every occasion. Arcade mode is what you expect from it, nothing less, nothing more.

There's also a thing called "Soul Chronicle", which is basically a separate story mode for every character, that introduces a slightly more detailed background and the different reasons why every one of them is searching for the Soul Edge (still not very enjoyable). To be clear, the lack of a substantial and engaging single player mode doesn't bother me at all, it should never be the focus of a fighting game, so, I'm not complaining, I'm just saying that, if you're in for that, searching for something similar to SCII or SCIII story mode, prepare to be disappointed...and I mean "SFV Story Mode" level of disappointment.

Music is on the avarage. Sound design is also ok. Voice acting selectable in English or Japanese.

The only reason to justify the purchase of SCVI for me is the online multiplayer, and for some aspect, the character customization too (assuming you're good with it). Even tho I find Soul Calibur VI to be very enjoyable to my eyes and my incipient tendinitis, it's not worth the full price at all. I digitally bought it on PS4 at Day 1, and I'm still mad at myself that I wasn't able to wait for a fair discount, waiting two years before deciding to buy it again on Steam at a reasonable discount.

Now, for a summary of the goods, the bads, and also the "uglys" of the game:

The GOODs
- Solid roster
- Balanced gameplay
- Network multiplayer working as intended (unless you're a wi-fi warrior)
- Easy to learn...
- Cool looking combos
- Intuitive training mode
- Different stage designs
- Character creator, if fully exploited

The BADs
- Abscence of an engaging story mode
- ...hard to master (as every fighting game should be)
- Somehow not for every PC, despite the outdated graphic
- Character creator, if taken lightly

The "UGLYs"
- Clean but outdated graphics (same engine since 2009 I believe)
- Recycled and poorly optimized costumization pieces from SCIV and SCV inside the character creator
- Players can use their own creations in ranked matches, creating a sense of confusion with pros and
cons that should define and distinguish every character from another (strenght, agility, versatility, etc.)
- Plenty of hidden mechanics to take in consideration if you're not familiar with fighting games

Conclusions

I find Soul Calibur VI to be a very satisfying fighting game to play and master and I recommend it to everyone that wants to dig in the series and his characters for first time, and also to those who are more accostumed to the franchise and are expecting a level of familiarity coming with it. Not at full price tho.\t\t\t\t\t
