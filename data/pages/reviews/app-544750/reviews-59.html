\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor offline play, yes.
Online, not so much. It's hit or miss whether you'll find any matches this long after release. I haven't hand very much luck, but maybe you will. Defiantly pick it up on sale though, it's not worth full price imo.

It may be called "Soulcalibur VI" but, a more accurate name would be "Soulcalibur V-2: We're sorry about replacing your favorite characters with kid versions edition"
If you played Five, it's all basically the same mechanically wise. With a few move-set changes to the characters, a new stand-off attack/mechanic, new "ultimate moves" so to speak, and a sort of burst limit form that amps up your attacks and move-set.

If you're only coming for the character creation, you'd better be ready to look into modding.
There's both more, and less options for creation this time around.
More player/character models to chose from in the form of "races"
But over-all, less clothing options (even with all the DLC) not to mention some item/armor sets from IV and V are missing pieces in this game. Understandable considering the game's devs were given and insultingly small budget sadly :(

In short, mechanically speaking this game is great, and has a rock-solid core for a fighting game.
But, is lacking everywhere else.

However, there's WAY more single player content here than in V.
Story modes for each character (albeit with motion-comic cut-scenes)
and a big "adventure" time mode with rpg-esk mechanics built in if that's your kind of thing.

It's not perfect by any means, and the devs defiantly had to make the best out of a bad-hand.
But overall, i've enjoyed my time with the game, and will probably keep playing until I burn myself out.
Hopefully if VII ever gets made, they'll get a fair chance next time around.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt could be one of the best fighting games.
But it is not. And that is why:

There is the Creation Mode, which allows you to create your own fighter, and this creation editor is pretty decent.

What is the problem you ask then?
There is no option to disable other players 'creative' outfits.
So, in-network battles your eyes are going to bleed and you are going to meet:
Tasteless emo-weeb-deadinside-anime boys
Tasteless weeb-anime girl-ninjas made by people without a glimpse of fashion taste and understanding of the color combination
Ugly lizards with big sausages
Clown from Ronald McDonald is also here
And so on

The sad part is, there is a mode for solo play. And in this mode, there are primary neverending missions, called astral fissures, where you fight other players' characters. So if you don't go online, you still going to meet one of the infamous characters listed above.

People asked for the possibility to disable custom character's look in their world for years, just google forums posts, go even into the Steam discussion, where people want to look at, quote: " to look at characters designed by PROFESSIONAL artists, not some 'android unity resource flippers' levels of quality" and that there just "isn't any point seeing a bunch of badly created weebs, smelly lizard freaks, and some clearly satanic oriented individuals".

It is completely unaesthetic and ruins game impression, perception, and immersiveness.
I came here to enjoy medieval battles between cool solid characters.
If I wanted childish tasteless meme s**t I would go play Garry's mode, Fortnite, or something.

Unplayable, because it is hard to play with eyes bleeding.\t\t\t\t\t
