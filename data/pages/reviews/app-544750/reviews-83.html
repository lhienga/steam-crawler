\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSoulCalibur VI is a great game... If you're looking for a horrible online experience.
I recommend every bit of this game. From the little story, to the character customization, this game is worth the play.

However, if you're like me and play fighting games online for pvp, then this game just isn't really set up for newcomers.
Unlike other fighting games on this platform, the ranked is dead. After waiting around 5 - 10 minutes to find a suitable fighter, they're usually LEAGUES above you. Included with this game is being able to be juggled in combos, so if you're hit, you might as well say goodbye to your health. I guess you could say just play casual, but only sweats play this game now. Getting good is really only worth it for tournaments or for playing with friends. Otherwise, you're playing a fighting game against bots.

But i will say the character variety, moves, and combos are lots of fun to pull off. They're flashy, strong, and satisfying.
Soul Calibur is nothing like other games, and i do recommend this.
But if you're new, looking to get into the game and online, you better get ready to grind.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
-Has guest characters Geralt, YorHa Unit 2 Model B and Haohmaru playable.
-The Create-A-Soul mode has more gender fluid armor pieces and even customizable armors the main characters wear.
-Easier combat mechanics and combos
-Lip movements are fully synchronised between Japanese and country languages (At least, with English)
-Yoshimitsu is no longer a posessed wooden doll
-You can make a custom character your own main character in a preset campaign for Libra of Soul mode and make choices that (probably) impact the end of your campaign.
-The customisation is updating almost every 6 months or so, so there's more voices and clothes to choose from.

Cons:
-Some characters previously playable in Soul Calibur 4 are locked behind a paywall ($5-$6 but still)
-Very little weapon selection, maybe up to 6 or 7 weapons per character, where Soul Calibur 4 had 15-26 choices.
-No Star Wars characters (without the help of mods)
-Armor and weapon stats are only visible in Libra of Souls and besides that, the only stat modifiers outside of LoS is height in the Create-A-Soul mode.
-There are 10 stages (18 with DLC), but often cycles between 7 in Arcade.
-AI difficulty becomes more annoying than challenging (Normal+ makes AI use attacks that use the least amount of frames to reach you as your guard is down. Sometimes, this is all they do. Very Hard adds spam guard impacts that don't even appear until blocked, thus countering Inferno's spammable attacks).
-Loading times for subtle selection changes in Create-A-Soul takes 5-6 seconds. This applies to everything but colors and stickers, which doesn't make sense when certain hairstyles break in battle and don't require 5-6 seconds to switch from perfect to torn/cut.
-Only 1 creation stage is available and has high quality textures and particles which probably add to the load times.
-Armor/Clothing options will cancel out or underlap some other items or clothing (Socks can't be worn with Setsuka's sandals, Yoshimitsu's arms can't be worn with almost all undergarments but his own, shoulder pieces don't single out with single arm clothing/armor).
-Only 8 stickers are allowed on a maximum of 48 slots of 12 creation items
-The Lizardman and Malefic races doesn't have gender options, as well as a small amount of armors for the Lizardman race and no hair styles for the Shapeshifter or Colossus races.

Summary: Play this if you like Soul Calibur 4 and 5, making custom characters with limited game mechanics, or anime games in general. Don't play this if you expected more (free) content like Soul Calibur 4 had or if you want to have fun playing against AI. I personally will ride this game out until they stop putting in more content, but for you viewers who want to buy the game, just catch a sale like I did in late April/ early May. Game's not worth the whopping $90 to be giving players frustration and broken devices, so get it on sale for $14.\t\t\t\t\t
