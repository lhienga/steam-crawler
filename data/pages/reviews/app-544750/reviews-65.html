The thing that they done in SC2 that I really love is how they handle 3 guest characters in each consoles (Playstation, Nintendo and XBOX). I love how they do guest characters in SC2, in SC4 they have 4 guest characters and the way how they handle it, it's the same with SC2, but only 2 consoles (Playstation and XBOX), each of them have 2 available guest characters and each consoles have 1 DLC guest character, in SC5 and Broken Destiny they only have 1 guest character and SC6 they have 3 guest characters which is not bad, it's decent, I like the 3 guest that they put in the game.

I don't have any problems with the characters, some of the old cast is back, at least they bring back Hwang as the final DLC, there's some characters that are unbalance compared to others but I don't mind that at all and the character creation is fun to do. I enjoyed it, there's a lot of variety to choose from, there's 16 races and there's a decent enough equipment for your character to equip, it doesn't have that many, only have outfit from other characters and only a few original equipment to choose from, but you can still do something creative with characters outfit in soul creation.

The combat of Soulcalibur VI is great, there's actually changes of combat from their previous games, but not that noticeable, the movements speed is around the middle, it's fast but not that fast and it's slow but not that slow, basically it's a decent speed, but at least the speed is better than Soulcalibur IV,

The graphics is good, the game have a decent enough single player content, there's Battle at least, you can watch bots fight, Arcade mode it's fun, I enjoy playing the arcade mode and that's where you can easily get soul points, which is can unlock a bunch of pictures in the gallery and cosmetics in character creation, if you're a completionist, you'll probably enjoy unlocking it, there's the Chronicle of Souls, it's the story mode of Cast characters and Mission: Libra of Souls, it's a Story mode again, but this time you're the main character, there's a bunch of contents in Libra Souls, so you might enjoy playing that game mode, also you can get soul points by exchanging it with the currency, which is Gold, 100 Soul points is equivalent to 500 Gold and a bunch of missions.

Overall, the game is great, it's fun to play and enjoyable, the only problem is the lack of players online, there's not that many people playing the game now, I just wish the community wasn't dead, but for what the game offer?? yeah I recommended it to people, from a fan of Soulcalibur and people who is new in the game, the Controls is easily to understand and the combat is satisfying, you can easily do combos with it,

All said and done, I gave it a 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t

Nope, games actually a broken mess, with unstable and unreliable online play, as well as a tier list so broken it makes the majority of the cast unviable. Just a mess. And the base character creation is pretty .


This game has some of the worst online I've ever seen, it is basically unplayable.

The matchmaking alone is criminally bad, making you wait in ranked while twiddling your thumbs for people who will never come, and if they ever miraculously do, the ping rate is so high and the input delay so grevious that it makes any actual hope for competitive gameplay an impossibility.

The casual play, for whatever reason, is relegated to rooms, where people wait to play against the host, who seems to have a gross host advantage that just makes it frustrating to wait all that time anyways.

I know this game is now getting pretty old, but these problems were also present at it's launch. I felt nostalgic trying to play this again but it's aged liked a fine turd, and reminds me of why I had stopped playing when it launched.

An other game in the modern era of Bandai Namco that feels like a half assed effort, where the devs had a lot of heart, but the company behind them just forced them to churn out the cheapest online possible so that they could capitalize on DLC crossover hype.

I can't really recommend this game, mostly for it's online failure. Tekken definitely learned from these mistakes, matchmaking is much improved and you have training with a dummy while you wait for matchmaking.. but Soul Cal 6 is a poorly optimized mess, that hits you with a mini load screen with every click of the menu and incredibly unbalanced characters.

Hypothetically, I should have hundreds, maybe thousands of hours playing this game, like I did it's previous iterations, but it just fails too hard in the most important ways. Incredible potential, wasted.

I can pray for a Soul Cal 7, maybe some day we will see it, but it needs to be better than this unplayable online experience. Sad state of fighting games this is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR;
It's fun while it lasts, but doesn't last long. Buy it when it's on sale.

MORE:
The gameplay is as solid as ever.
The fighting is smooth, easy to learn and hard(ish) to master. Many characters have inherited their moveset from the previous chapters, so if you're a fan of the franchise your muscle memory will do the hard work for you.
There's a decent amount of content for single player too, if you aren't looking for anything fancy and you don't get bored too quickly.

Sadly, the difficulty is all over the place. SP story mode is dull, while Libra of Soul has some weird spikes and can become frustrating when matched against over-leveled opponents with unfair advantages or absurd modifications.
On the other hand, Arcade difficulty can be manually tweaked, so you will find a good challenge if you turn it up to eleven.

The story acts as a soft reboot of the franchise and is... Well... A generic and bland fighting game story about power, destiny yadda yadda yadda, narrated through stylish (but static) pictures with endless exposition and rare (very rare) cutscenes. It's there just to get you straight from one fight to another, but lacks any real charm, IMHO.

The Create Mode lets you (surprise) create your own fighters, and there's a decent choice of attires you can mix to create something unique. You can also customize existing fighters - a feature I'm not very fond of, but it's there and makes up for the lack of alternate pre-made costumes.

ONE FINAL NOTE: I do miss the old days when new characters had to be unlocked by playing, instead of purchased... But I guess I'm just a soul from a different era.\t\t\t\t\t
