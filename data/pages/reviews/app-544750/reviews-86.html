\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all - I am not a fan of modern beat em ups. The genre had its golden age in the 90s (Super Street Fighter, Body Blows, old Mortal Combat etc.) where the fight was enjoyable and challenging but it was later simplified and ruined.

However, I spent much time playing Soul Blade 2 on PS2 - which was well made and original at that time - so I thought I should finaly give a chance to Soulcalibur. I played PvP only with a friend.

Unluckly, the game is terrible:
- As I mentioned, like all modern beat em ups the game is simplified to the point it resembles mobile "clicker". You could think you have plenty ways of attack but in reality it is all about hunting for 1-2 combos that deal most damage. The rest is mostly pointless. So there is not much actual fight in such games - just click proper combination better/faster than your opponent, watch flashy animation, repeat.
- The combo system spoils the fun. Instead of the dynamic fight, the game slows down or stops all the time (but it does not look as spectacular as it should) showing you some chaotic, jumpy animation, during which you have (sometimes) time to react. At some point we agreed with a friend to not use certain (most powerful) combos but more traditional ways to fight - and trust me, game became MUCH better, challenging and interesting!
- Last but not least - I don't mind unnaturaly sexy female warriors in fantasy games and I am not some puritan freak but come on - almost all female characters look like from some catalogue of perv sex toys. And dont even start with "but it is Japanese/Anime/Whatever style...". You know its stupid excuse. :) There are plenty of good Japanese-style games that don't look like that. This is kids game (it mostly lacks blood and the fight is too absurd to be considered brutal) - do you really have to put enormous boobs and XXX outfits in it to sell it to kids (or adult creepy dudes)? If producers would spend as much time and energy on making actual combat enjoyable as on coding the motion of those enormous breasts - this game would be a lot better and I think a lot of people would enjoy it more.

Game returned.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay is fine, it plays very similarly to the past few releases of the Soul Calibur franchise, but this game really shines with its character customisation. I play a lot of D&D, but I often have trouble figuring out in my head what a character or NPC looks like, and my artistic skills are below that of a 10-year old so there's no way I can draw or paint something to get a rough visualisation on paper. This game has so many options for making a bipedal character that I can whip up something pretty accurate to the image in my head in under 10 minutes. The character customisation has a very comprehensive list of clothing, armour, weaponry and accessories that can have their colour changed to match. In addition, various stickers and scars can be placed on the character to add more depth to its overall design. As a game I would rate SCVI 6.5/10, it's a mildly interesting fighting game from BANDAI NAMCO that is outshined by the companies' own Tekken series (for good reason, if you're looking for a decent fighting game Tekken 7 is a much better option) and isn't quite as good as the games predecessor, being Soul Calibur V. As a character customisation tool for non-artsy idiots like me, I would rate SCVI 9/10, dropping a mark for its somewhat lackluster customisation DLCs. It's fantastic for what I need, and well worth what I paid.\t\t\t\t\t
