\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFairly good fightergame for those of us who dislike memorizing millions of combo's or is lacking in the dexterity-department. Buttonmashing actually gets you a long way, but still, a small amount of brainactivity is needed. So, if you're looking for a casual fighter with amazing visuals: this is it. But the basegame is pretty barebones, a lot of great fighters from the series are hidden behind two season-passes. I'd advise you to buy the game only when on sale and then add the season-passes, so you'll get the full experience for a reasonable price. Character customization is a blast by the way (spent more time there than in the arena actually), but the game completely lacks immersion. If you expect to marvel at your painstakingly created character in action-packed cutscenes, you'll be deeply disappointed. No cutscenes, no CGI, just the creator and the fighting stages. What makes things worse, the writing of the backgroundstories (both official and custom-fighters) is awful, never surpasses elementary school level and is brought to you with a Powerpoint presentation from the nineties (text says: two bandits emerge, screen shakes) and a huge load of text to click through (or actually enter and u-button through). So there's a lot of room for improvement. But the basics (fighting and character creator) are good enough to give a thumbs up. On sale. With both seasonpasses.\t\t\t\t\t
