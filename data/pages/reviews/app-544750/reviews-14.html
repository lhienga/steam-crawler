\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a long time fan of the series since 2001, but also super casual. So this will be a ramble of my likes and dislikes.

Soulcalibur VI is a good continuation of the Soul series! Not without its faults, but certainly a more complete package than Soulcalibur V. Despite its late entry, its an ideal starting point for series newcomers as the story appears to take place around the beginning of the timeline. Though it's not a simple do-over ; Something has changed from the previous timeline with the arrival of Azwel and whatever's going on with Kilik now.

I've enjoyed 2B as my main for this entry. At some point, the series decided to add some flashy anime-esque and scif-fi craziness to the roster, and I'm all for it. While I don't care much for Geralt, I appreciate the new freedom to use guest characters' movesets in creation mode this time around.

Azwel is a loveable villain full of theatrical puns, and I approve.

I really enjoyed Raphael & Amy's story. Certain story bits that were only painfully hinted at in SCV, were all but confirmed by some very deliberate dialogue from the writers.

I do miss ZWEI's unique stand style and Dampierre's psuedo-randomness. SCV was the first entry I finally found characters I was drawn to aside from Raphael, so it was disappointing to see they were not popular enough to remain with the series. Oh well - the newcomers have been a fun enough replacement. Raphael's movelist has changed so much throughout the series, my aging mind kept getting his moves mixed up from previous games, so I've decided to drop him completely.

The rock-paper-scissors mechanic was not something I cared for, but it's manageable to get around after some practice.

It's more than worth it on sale for the fun character creator alone ; these days it frequently goes down to 90% off.
