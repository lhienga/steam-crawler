Most things were the same as I remembered, except the Soul Charge system is vastly improved over its introduction in SC3. Back then, it was more of a Taunt that gave you a temporary power boost for pulling it off. Now it's basically your Special Meter, and that works for me.

It's a remake of Soulcalibur II. If you're crafty, you can even use Creation Mode to remake all three of the missing guest characters; Link, Heihachi, and Spawn.

I found that the spread of fighting styles across the selection of characters was wildly varying, with something for everyone. I played as Link or Nightmare on my GCN copy of SC2, but switched to Siegfried in SC3 because they made Nightmare lame. And yet, I landed on Amy as my preferred choice here. (Nightmare is once again awesome too though...)

Arcade Mode, Main Characters Story Mode, Custom Character Story Mode, and Versus. Creation Mode is quite nuanced, and I spent more time making guest characters (or playing dress-up with the existing ones) than I want to admit.

Some people might be Mortal Kombat or Street Fighter fans. Maybe Tekken or Dead or Alive. But I will always be there for the tales of souls and swords, eternally retold, transcending history and the world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want to get into fighting games, this ain't it. If you want a thrilling story, this ain't it. If you like single player campaigns of thrilling highs and lows, this is tepid and uninspired. And also, the character customisation options are limited as hell, so good luck making an actually cool character.

Combat's fine, but there's no in-game tutorial. There's a guide book that lists all your characters moves, but it's shrouded by fighting game lingo that - while it makes complete sense a fighting game veteran - is overwhelming for new players. What the ♥♥♥♥ is a 22A? Sure, people who are familiar with Tekken or Mortal Combat might be able to pick it up and go, but without experience or dedicated research, none of it makes sense.

As an aside; the Reversal Edge system is hot garbage. Interrupt the flow of the battle to play a rock-paper-scissors minigame that simplifies and makes a mockery of the choices we normally get in fighting games. It's guard impacts without the commitment nor skill.

Soul Chronicle, the story of each character following the appearance of Soul Edge and its subsequent vanquishing, has an entire character's story just be 8th grader's prose without a single fight. That's right, an entire main solo campaign dedicated to an arbiter of balance of death, delegated to reading piss-poor prose.

Even once you do get into the story, characters never face personal challenges that force them to grow, nothing of value is lost or gained, and the epic premise of the game that "everyone is after Soul Edge" never results in anything for the majority of characters. Where are the temporary alliances and treachery of all these power-hungry people chasing an ultimate weapon? Where is the rat-race to beat everyone to the top and reign supreme? Why is every character story just setup for a mediocre self-insert story with absolutely no payoff, flaunting a black-and-white morality system just the same as bloody _Fable_ from 2004.

... As another aside, how come Sophitia, who is a mediterranean woman blessed by a god of forging and crafts, blonde-haired and blue-eyed, pale as marble, and going to battle the devil in a miniskirt? Why doesn't she have an olive complexion, at the very least hoplite armour (despite her using a sword not a shield), and dark hair? Capcom back in the day really just went "oh she's a foreigner, make her look Aryan and call it a day."

A lot of newbies seemed drawn to the game by it's character creator. Problem is, it's fairly lacking in a lot of customisation options with effectively no means of unlocking new ones. Unless you want to pay the cost of two full price new releases for all the DLC's, which only expand the roster by a quarter and add a bunch of out-of-place customisation options. How about if you want to just make do with what you've got? Well, half the options are bland sheets of cloth that hover an inch or two off your character's body, so tough ♥♥♥♥ I guess. Try as you might, it's just not great.

... All in all, the single player experience is ♥♥♥♥. Onboarding newbies isn't cared for. Character creation is sorely lacking. DLC triples the cost of the game to add a quarter as much content. Don't get it, even on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
