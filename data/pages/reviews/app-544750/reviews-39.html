\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good game with some flaws that at times I don't believe are the developer's fault, but rather Bamco's fault for not having much faith in the series after V.

The game definitely looks cheap and made on a budget, unlike Tekken 7 et al; characters look fake and plastic, the scenario looks like modified UE assets that look so obvious they distract you, and the portraits in the Soul Chronicle mode look very uncanny.

The story modes are pretty cool, and I'd say, in terms of lore, VI has the best story of all games, especially for those who are new to the series, which makes sense since the story is a retelling of earlier games. The presentation, however, can turn some people off, as yet another budget cutting strategy, they made almost all story portions of both Soul Chronicle and Libra of Soul visual-novel like. I am not a huge fan of VNs myself anymore, however, I can tolerate them, especially because the illustrations are pretty... too bad they're not in 1080 x 1920 in the Gallery. Why did they make Zasalamel's whole LoS story just him doing VO with a single frame in almost all of its entirely though?!

The big gameplay gimmick, Reversal Edge, may be controversial and is likely a big reason why this game's online mode, as of October 2022, is dead. I can personally take it or leave this rock-paper-scissors mechanic, though I really miss the times when Soul Charging was just a button combo with a quick charge like in the first few games. Besides that, the combat feels great as usual, in a pace more similar to the older games also.

I like the new character Azwel, he's a cool new character with the mad, evil scientist trope, though I'm not a fan of his Necrid-like moveset. Groh is... alright. He's cool to play as, but even Z.W.E.I. was more interesting than him. Geralt is alright too, I suppose, he certainly doesn't feel too out of place in the universe compared to Heihachi, Spawn and the Star Wars characters. I absolutely LOVE the redesigned Hwang from the second Season Pass however, he may have a gimmick that may be bittersweet but I got automatically sold on his design and his new story.

With all said, then, I'd say this game is better than both IV and V, but definitely not as great as I, II and III. I recommend it if you want a rather easy fighting game to play, and if you don't care about online play, as long as you get it on sale. It seems to go on sale quite often and with a large cut however, so get it when it happens and have fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was able to recreate Musashi from FGO in the Character creator. Pretty legit, not going to lie. A lot of the cosmetics are recycled from SCIV and SCV, but you work with what you have.

Main downsides of the game is exclusive collaboration characters from previous games won't return, like usual. That and some unnecessary adjustments of movesets means if you mained a certain character in previous titles, you're going to have to relearn them. Again.

Overall, the game feels like a mix of SCII and SCIV. They still haven't brought back Weapon Master Mode for whatever reason. I don't know why.

I still recommend the game, even if it's not super active anymore. But that's mostly because I'm biased from having grown up on the Soul Calibur franchise as my main fighting game. For more hardcore fighting game fans that aren't as biased as I am, Mortal Kombat 11, Injustice 2, or Street Fighter might be better options for 2D fighters. For people that like 3D fighting games, I might also recommend Dragonball Xenoverse 2 instead.\t\t\t\t\t
