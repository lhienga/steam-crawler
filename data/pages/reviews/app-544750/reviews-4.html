\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my favorite among many fighting games (with weapons) since the first one called Soul Edge (1995, Japan version) / Soul Blade (1997, Outside Japan version).
Yeah, not Soulcalibur at that time. Why the name changed? The reason Soul Blade received its name, as opposed to the original arcade title of Soul Edge which was used in Japan in 1995. Because when Namco attempted to apply for a trademark for the game outside of Japan, they were sued by Tim Langdell, the owner of Edge Games. That's why.

And after all the dust has settled, the world was in peace and live in harmony again, then Soul Blade becoming Soulcalibur in 1998. The universe is in sync, all's right with the planet. Yay!

And now in this 6th franchise, finally; Geralt Of Rivia and also YoRHa No. 2 Type B, commonly known as 2B from Nier: Automata and also Haohmaru from Samurai Spirit (JPN) / Samurai Shodown (USA) are joining the fighters.

You can customize your own avatar or just choose any of your liking and play right away.

Too bad the style of background stories from each characters felt bland and boring. I was hoping for 3D movie-like custscenes by each tales. But instead, just got voice overs and sounds effects only with still pictures of figureheads and background arts. Well, at least it feels more like those audio drama / radio theatre back then in 1920s to 40s.

But the matches are fun, simple enough and fast, especially with much more easy Critical Edge Attack to unleash just with one glorious button.

Well, any games are not truly perfect. Each has its own good and bad aspects.

The most important thing, this Soulcalibur is enjoyable to play for all. Fun for the whole family.
