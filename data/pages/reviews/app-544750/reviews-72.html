\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPro:
- Combat is overall pretty fun. Players exchange "turns" more frequently than in most other fighting games. Makes it stand out.
- The execution difficulty barrier is rather low, so it doesn't take much input practice to get started.
- The custom character creation tool is great. You can make almost anything happen with effort and creativity.
- There are 29 characters, all of which have unique movelists and mechanics, which makes for a good amount of variety.
- "Soul Chronicle" is a decent Arcade Mode. Lets you play the main cast's rebooted canon stories, which is around 20 hours worth of content. "Libra of Souls" adds another bunch of hours, but is less interesting.
- Visuals and soundtrack are both solid. The game looks really nice.
- Geralt is an excellent guest character, he even has his own short Soul Chronicle story.
- The community is active and online tournaments are common. Additionally, there are Bandai Namco's official tournaments.
- Training mode is convenient and even has preview clips for character moves.

Neutral:
- It is relatively demanding in terms of hardware, make sure you are well above the minimum requirements.
- You can use custom characters in the ranked queue. This is great for people who love their character creations, but awful for those who want matchup consistency.
- The game is very light on combos. Aside from very specific situations, characters can't land more than 3 or 4 attacks in a row, even from a good combo starter. A positive for people who don't like sitting through combos, negative for those who like executing or watching them.
- On the same note, the game often requires you to buffer inputs for multi-hit attacks way in advance. I assume this is to make it extra punishing if you whiff a combo attempt, but it feels weird at first.
- 2B is just "kind of there" for the vs mode and character creator. Has her own stage, but doesn't interact with the rest of the roster, didn't get her own story, etc. A nice guest character either way, but very underutalized.

Con:
- Very expensive.
- Quick and precise diagonal inputs are important, so you get lots of misinputs on a gamepad. Meanwhile you also need quick access to 10 different buttons (excluding the 4 for movement), or use the 2-button inputs on a keyboard, which feels awkward. I imagine it isn't much better with a fight stick, so I wonder what type of controller this game was designed for.
- The game uses peer-to-peer delay netcode for online play, so online PvP can be miserable when the netcode doesn't hold up.
- A large portion of character creation pieces are DLC.
- Voice acting and writing can be "cheesy", or outright immersion-breaking.
- The Libra of Souls custom character rpg mode isn't good. Weak story and rpg elements barely matter.
- The game has no real tutorial. Libra of Souls only shows you the basic universal mechanics (like blocking and movement). More is only hidden in several pages worth of text in Training Mode. For the rest you'll have to look up community made guides online, which vary greatly in quality and time efficiency. Overall, learning SC6 is tedious, but a requirement to play it with intentionality.
- One the same note, there is a lot of artificial complexity due to hidden mechanics. For example, there is no visual indicator for how many of Geralt's signs the player has unlocked, or how many Analysis Points 2B has collected. If you didn't do your homework by reading their training mode pages or an online guide, it will take you forever to figure out that those mechanics exist at all, not to mention what they do.
- The Soul Chronicle stories are a mixed bag. Half of it is good, the other forgettable filler content.
- Too much blatant fanservice for my taste.
- Most cinematic combat animations are very long, which interrupts the gameplay.
- The "Reversal Edge" parry mechanic has a rock-paper-scissors gamble system on who gets the advantage after. Strikes me as out of place.

SC6 shares many of the same faults as a lot of other fighting games.
The singleplayer content is okay at best and tedious at worst. It doesn't prepare you for pvp and the delay netcode also isn't inviting. The 3D combat on its own is the game's greatest strength, but not flawless either.

Ultimately, it is not worth the full price at all. I bought the "Deluxe Edition on sale at around -70% and would say that it was only barely worth the $25/. Meanwhile the game wants me to drop another 34 bucks on the second season pass.
It is both overpriced and has way too many issues for me to be forgiving with it.

Buy only on sale and for the PvP, and even then only if you are confident that you'll put in the time to study how the characters work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game takes everything I love about Soul Calibur and takes it up a notch! I'm a fan of 2 and an advocate for the somewhat unpopular 5, and I'd say this game has something for any SC fan. The fights and controls work perfectly, the balance feels right (which was an issue for me with 4 and 5) and the character selection is lovely. The story modes work for what they are, and are an improvement from 4 and 5.

I have a couple complaints but they aren't big. The biggest one is the lack of some very iconic characters. I could name a bunch, but I'll simply say Aeon and move on from there. The second is little to no outfit options from 5. I understand the overall opinion of 5, and that this game is somewhat of a reboot, but 5 gave us some of the best outfits for our returning cast. Most notably, my main Raphael who sported the vampire garb. A DLC that brought many veteran outfits back would be amazing, but I believe this game has completed its DLC path.

Overall, I think this game has much to offer, even if you're not a huge fighting game fan. I am, but even when I was a kid with no interest I still enjoyed 2 very much, and I think my young self would feel the same towards 6. It'd make me so happy to see more people try it and potentially fall in love with the series, so definitely check it out! :)\t\t\t\t\t
