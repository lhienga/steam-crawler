\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn awesome game for fans of the genre and newcomers alike! If you're contemplating on which fighting game to get on a sale, this is your go-to choice. In this review, I'll try to highlight its strong points while also underlining some negative features from my perspective.

Unlike other games of the genre, eg Tekken, it goes into great depth story-wise. One storyline follows the adventures of a custom made warrior and the role he played in the main story. This one can take forever and the narrative can feel like reading a novel. (great if you want to practice Japanese, like I did) The other storyline follows the background stories of all the main characters (but not 2B, to my disappointment) and offering a deeper insight in the world of soulcalibur. (what I felt was missing in Tekken). However, the visuals are rather low-budget as they only use sketches of the characters and no cut-scenes.

Otherwise, you can just go straight to the fighting part, where many choices await you with different and unique playstyles that will keep you coming back to try them all. Even after choosing a main, I could spice up my gameplay with new combos after learning the basics and keep the fun coming.
One of its strong points is the customization for the fighters with an abundance of options to choose from, like clothes, hairstyles, colors etc. and countless combinations to come up with. (unfortunately, you cannot preview the objects before selecting them). I think I've spent more time customizing than actually playing the game. You can create some very eye pleasing outfits to make the game as sexy as you can handle, and the clothing can get ripped off in combat. I will say no more, no need to look any further for waifu material! ;P

And now for some features I found annoying.
First of all, the reversal edge feature. I find it completely useless as it is based mostly on luck and the AI will often abuse it, just to irritate you more.
Then the ability to execute a critical edge twice in the same round! It's utterly irritating when you have to sit through the whole sequence (I wish I could simply skip it) two times in a row.
I also want to point out the wide difference in difficulty when fighting in Arcade mode. When playing even on the "very hard" setting, I found some heroes' level mediocre while others would not let you breathe, always one step ahead of you (esp Voldo, Cervantes, Astaroth). On the same note, playing offline pretty much limits you to the "arcade mode" and the story mode. And the arcade can feel at times too long with 8 consecutive battles.

Overall, I can safely say it's my favorite fighting game to date and I strongly recommend it to anyone looking to have a good time hacking and slashing clothes to shreds!

My character was basically at the end of it, and I had this weapon that didn't even need half the statistics it did, but I guess you need that stuff on it to survive the end scenario random battles. Oh, and word of caution: Watch how you travel. Each type of terrain you pass increases the cost. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a little surprised by the reviews, but I think that's because I have a different take on the game than some others do. As such, I am evaluating the game for two different groups of people: 1) the hardcore/ somewhat interested in the fighting genre player, and 2) the casual player who just wants to relive some of their old memories about Soul Calibur.

1) The Hardcore Player

Soul Calibur is constantly being updated, with the Meta changing here and there. Just like all fighting games, there is a lot of depth: normals, special moves, grapples, frame data, unique interactions, and something akin to Injustice there is a sort of "character power" unique to all the characters (for example, Taki does chip damage with certain attacks or Amy gets a lot of extra damage after using "rose" attacks).

These really spice up the formula to the old soul calibur that you know and love. The online community depends wildly on what type of matches you seek, but there is several outside sources that provide great insights into the games, AND there is a huge group of players for each specific character as they have their own discords (you can find them on reddit easily).

The competitive scene took a hit with COVID, there is still an active and loyal fanbase, but it is hard to say it is a must buy for those who really want to get in the competitive scene. If, however, you are just looking to spice up your fighting game genre and you haven't picked up Soul Calibur VI, I would recommend it if the following applies to you:

-You enjoy elements of casual and competitive play (IE character creating, learning multiple characters, story of the game)
-You don't mind a close-knit community that you want to be a part of
-You generally appreciate the Fighting Genre and the games included

2) The Casual Player

I can simply say that the Casual Players should get this game. If you have interest in Soul Calibur, this is the BEST ONE to start playing because the fighting mechanics are so simplified (but there is still enough depth to not get bored), and because there is a wide range of characters to choose from. The online community has drastically lessened, but that doesn't mean there still isn't a ton of casual players and 'beginners' that seek to only fight people with their knowledge of the game.

I think that if you like the guest characters, as well, you should consider buying this game as they are some of the most unique fighters to ever be introduced into the series.\t\t\t\t\t
