Fights are great.
Customization is unbelievable detailed.
But in my opinion this game is a step back... second storyline Libra of soul is pretty bad and boring, compared to SoulCalibur 3 Chonicles of the sword game mode. It is just poorly written, boring visual novel crossbreed with fighting, but in SC3 you had a tactical game + fighting genre mixed together that given me so much fun back then... And as i was prepared to see upgraded version of game from my childhood, I was crushed.. it just boring visual novel with lazy voice acting and all you do is skipping every line of the dialogue... it is so bad... No opportunity to play some challanges as it was back then to defeat collosus for medals and much much more. There you have some sort of challanges but.. I dunno, I had no fun doing this ♥♥♥♥, because maybe devs needed to put this as a game mode with levels for accomplitioning variety of challanges.
In summary, you have 2 ways, read a novel of each character with a lidl fighting or read a whole War and Piece in SoulCalibur univerce with some sort of weapon, food and mercenary management, lidl fighting again mixed with some challanges and thats it.
I did not played online because Soulcalibur in my opinion is PVE fighting, its more interesting to get into the story, singleplayer story of you, your character as it was back then. Epic journey of each character their storyes bound together in this dangerous world with clunky qte(that was actually interesting feature in SoulCalibur3,). Pretty arenas with wonderful view...
Sadly.. but, it is what it is, Im respect game developers for what they done back then and hope they will continue doing amazing continuation for this title, not this cosmetic DLC they're doing.
Only hope I have, hope that they will put their ♥♥♥♥ together and start moving towards greater good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played a Soul Calibur game since part 2 and I can see I haven't missed anything. Same characters fighting on boring, flat surfaces.

You'd think that since each level is confined to such a small area, that the graphic detail on each would be astounding, but it's all just stale and forgettable; even Inferno's seizure inducing trash pile of a stage.

Yes, the controls are tight, but that's to be expected when you're using the same movesets and animations from 1999 with only a couple of new additions.

Overall, it's just a weak game with subpar visuals and missed opportunities. There's no evolution here; just lazy design with tedious story modes and poorly implemented rpg elements.

If you've played Soul Calibur on the Dreamcast, there's no reason to play this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like Soulcalibur a lot.

It was my first fighting game and even though I was crap at it with time I think I've come to appreciate a lot of what this game does right and does different from other fighting games.

However, I don't think I can really recommend VI to first time players.
The lack of any comprehensive in game resource for training/learning kills it dead for new players.
There's also the AI which is botched. The AI has a habit to just, stand still. Not blocking or anything, just standing stock still waiting to get hit. All adjusting difficulty seems to do is decrease the regularity with which this happens.
On easy its a literal punching bag that will never even hit back, but even on the highest setting it still happens.

Actual gameplay against other humans is great fun.
Each character is unique and reasonably well balanced, I love the move sets its very easy to learn the bare basics on your own which is more than enough to defeat the AI.

But then there is the DLC
About a quarter of the roster is DLC and buying them is basically re-buying the game all over again.
Not to mention the stupidly overpriced customisation DLCS.

My advice, If you like 3D fighting games get this game on sale.
If you don't like them already then this won't change your mind.\t\t\t\t\t
