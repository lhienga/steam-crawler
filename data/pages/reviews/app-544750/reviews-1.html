\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hadn't played a Soul title since SoulCalibur 2 and this game felt like not a day has passed since then. I do not mean this in a nostalgic, "good ol' SoulCalibur" way, I mean it in a "oh my god, these characters are programmed the same way as the original Metal Gear Soild", where the enemy just reads the input on your controller and reacts accordingly, so your winning strategy is figuring our which moves your opponent is NOT programmed to counter and just spam them. I literally faced an enemy who would go berserk on unblockable attacks each time I pressed "forward" on the analog stick while staying perfectly still when I put the controller down. This is pathetic.

Moving on. The game consists of four modes; story, arcade, Libra of Souls, and multiplayer. The multiplayer servers are predictably dead so long after original release (and yet, multiplayer achievements are still there to ensure you won't 100% the game). Note that I tried to enter multiplayer with another person in my household playing their own copy of the game on their own system and the game could not match us up. The arcade mode is standard, with nothing worth noting. Story mode basically allows you to get accustomed to the endless drone of text and dialogue by playing a few fights with each character to unlock their library entries.

But the grand disappointment comes with the Libra of Souls, which apparently is the flagship mode of this game (perhaps also its predecessors, I wouldn't know). Here, you can made your own character and play a variation of story mode, where you lead your character through the story of the game, fighting random enemies and the franchise's signature characters along the way. The game fails so hard to hold your attention with actual gameplay, that LoS is essentially 90% text, dialogue, and a few cutscenes, wasting endless hours by pressing the skip button to get to the few, and far between, moments when you have to select a response to something happening in the story. These responses are pretty much inconsequential, except for the choice between "good" and "evil" deeds that lead to the two separate achievements.

Finally, I wish to address "Geralt". SoulCalibur VI is not the first game to include vanity characters that have absolutely nothing to do with the game itself. However, SoulCalibur VI is one of the few where said vanity character is poorly woven into the story to justify putting his face on the cover art. Seriously, if after 7 iterations of your franchise you need to include characters from different games to make yours appealing to consumers, you are doing something wrong.

I'll give it this, the multiplayer experience is enjoyable and the game looks nice.

Needless to say, you will not find fun here. You will find hours of exasperation and frustration at the fact that enemies weaker or identical to yours beat you. You will find numerous unobtainable achievements to mar your record. You will find hours of pointless dialogue and text. The only chance you will enjoy this is if you are one of those weirdos who play videogames not to relax, but to challenge themselves. While I have no issue with you guys, I wish publishers would promote games made for you in a way that allows the rest of us to know that this product is not addressed at us in any way. I will be staying away from this franchise and this developer for the foreseeable future.\t\t\t\t\t
