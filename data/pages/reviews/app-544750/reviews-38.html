\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR, I like the game. But ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ does it have problems.

There's a lot to like about Soulcalibur 6, far more than some folks give it credit for. Lotta ways to make your own character, lotta fighting styles to choose from. There's even two story modes, one involving the game's roster, the other, a journey for your own custom character to undertake. Good stuff. People complain about the RPS mechanic, but I kinda like it. Adds something different from the kinds of fighting games I've played before. And even with the one-button special, it's unlike Tekken 7 in that it doesn't immediately pause the game the moment you activate it: you can get easily interrupted, which isn't something I'm used to after playing so much Tekken 7 where Rage Drives, like certain other moves, slow the pace of a match down a bit even when it's blocked, or its Rage Arts that straight up pause the fight during the starting animation. So Reversal and Critical Edge to me aren't at all things that take away from this game. They're just more cool, anime af fighting game ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ to do. They haven't been as easy an "I WIN" button just because they're much easier to block, interrupt, and dodge than specials in other games I've played.

But that's about it for the positive things I have to say at the moment.

Lotta things around the core game itself, I heavily dislike. DLC pricing, the fact that a lot of it was Day 1, that the "Deluxe" Edition doesn't include the second Season Pass, and what's most important for a lot of people who might wanna pick this up: online ranked is a desert. People playing are few and far between, and you're likely to be matched up with longtime veterans who will taunt you every round they beat you, or pluggers. You'd think with how few there are still playing this online (because let's be honest, a lot of folks are just using this as a way to make their favorite vtubers as characters and watch the CPU fight itself), the ones remaining on the ladder would be better about that crap, but not really. And of course, the netcode. Now, while I'm not new to fighting games, I'm relatively new to playing fighting games online. But even to a novice like me, I've come to notice that a 3-bar connection against a wifi player in Tekken 7 with its rollback is far superior to a 4-bar wired connection to someone you're playing against in Soulcalibur 6. Is it utterly unbearable? Not to me, but again, I'm new to online play, and I've dealt with lag and other crap from other games that this doesn't bother me so much. But now I do know the difference and why it's a deal-breaker to other people.

That said, I want MORE people to pick up this game. Even now. Unlike a lot of other fighting games with horrible DLC practices, at least the base game's got enough content in there that I think it's still worthwhile without 2B or Geralt or any of the other customization fluff. There's enough sexy options for the coomers to enjoy breaking into with their specials during a match. There's enough characters with varying different fighting styles to enjoy this game for a good while, and the characters themselves are ((((relatively)))) cheap as single DLC, even cheaper during a sale. And again, the two story modes.

It sucks to see a game that looks this good and plays this well despite its age stagnate like this, especially considering it's from the same company who've shown they can do so much better. Absolutely get this game on a sale. Play it with your friends. Repopulate Ranked, because I honestly doubt there'll ever be a Soulcalibur 7 given the state of this game, and even if there will be, it'll be a long, LONG way off considering Bandai Namco's got Tekken 8 to focus on and hopefully do even better on that they've done with Tekken 7. Even with all this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, Soulcalibur 6 doesn't deserve to die. Unfortunately, I missed out on this series, and this was my introduction. But I haven't played a 3D fighting game that's got all the weird, unique nonsense this game has, and it'd suck if there comes a time that there isn't anyone to play this game with at all ever again.

UPDATE

I tried.

I tried REALLY hard to enjoy Soulcalibur 6's online. When I'm matched with the right people, it's fun even when I lose. Most times, it's diehards and pluggers. Even worse considering the netcode. It takes minutes to find a match, and that's you HOPING it'll be someone your level. Now, the most fun I'm having with this game is the same kind of fun a lotta of folks I see posting screenshots or videos do: making sexy characters and making them fight. Though I don't do CPU vs CPU because I still wanna play the damn game. It's still fun to me mechanically. And a lot of the things I said that make me recommend this game still hold true even after I suffered through the online.

But I'll tell you this much: if you really did come to look at Soulcalibur 6 for its online, stay the ♥♥♥♥ away. Don't do what I said about trying to repopulate. You'll be alone. It'll be thankless. Four out of the five people you'll be matched with in an evening will either wreck you, or plug. But if that one good match out of five is worth it to you, then you still better be damn sure you REALLY like everything else about this game. Its mechanics, its characters, its singleplayer modes.

Outside of that, get Tekken 7 instead. Same company. Also a 3D game. Far better quality online. Far better systems. Aged well visually, too, even when you compare how old it looks now that visuals for Tekken 8 are out. Going back to that game's online after trying this was like water in a desert. A massage after a workout that got me injured. It's being kissed on the cheek by my main as she effortless follows my button prompts to set up her combos, her safe pokes, her power crushes, her rage art and rage drive, even against a 3-bar wifi player. I love Lucky Chloe. And she better be in Tekken 8, Harada.

If you're ever picking up Soulcalibur 6 NOW, even on a sale, then for the love of God, know what you're getting into. Curb your expectations for what you're gonna get out of its online, whether ranked or casual. There are far better fighting games for that. But if you just want this game's focus on weapon-based combat, its anime af specials, its character roster, and its character creation that lets you create your own waifu who gets more and more naked as she (or he, whichever you like) has her armor broken during a fight, then ABSOLUTELY, it's still good for all of that.\t\t\t\t\t
