\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAside from the lack of base-price discounts, it's pretty good. Wait for a sale, especially for the DLC. The cosmetic DLCs are only new parts for the custom character creator, and from what I've read not worth the money at all.

Online Multiplayer is fairly dead at this point (if you're in a major region, you will usually be able to find opponents in matchmaking during prime time), though there's usually a few casual lobbies open.

As you should be able to tell from the marketing material or general knowledge of the franchise, Soul Calibur VI is a weapon-focused fighting game with full-3D, 8-directional movement (similar to Tekken).

While I'm not an expert, either in general skill level or mastery of this specific game, what I've learned is that SC6 is a pretty offense-focused fighter. Your main defensive options are standing/crouching block, Reversal Edge, Guard Impacts (character specific), or simply using the 8-d movement to get out of the way. Lastly, meter-burning to enter a powered up state can be used to push your opponent back and give you a bit of space to breathe; assuming you don't interrupt their string of attacks with a well timed raw super instead.

Blocking can be effective enough, but with the expansive move lists on each character, you will need in-depth character knowledge and/or good reads on your opponent to have any success with blocking, assuming they don't get a grab read on you. Reversal Edge is a special stance/charging state attack, during which you deflect most normals, and ending with an attack that initiates the... Rock Paper Scissors part. The fighters square off, and you have a moment to input Horizontal, Vertical, Kick, Block, or Move. Moving is risky as it depends greatly on the hitbox and frame data of each fighter, whereas H, V, K follow a straightforward RPS triangle. Don't even ask me about Block. Some characters have unique inputs available for this face-off as well. Reversal Edge is an interesting but somewhat controversial mechanic, a counter, comeback, and mixup mechanic all in one.

Lastly there are Guard Impacts, which are specific moves unique to each character, which can interrupt/counter enemy attacks with proper timing, putting you in an advantage state.

Generally, if you're on the defensive, you're losing. You need to break your opponent's offense and either reset to neutral or reverse the momentum on them. Rounds are quick (default match is a best of 7 rounds), and it only takes a few good combos to close one out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBut barely.

I bought the "Definitive Edition" when it was on sale. I assumed this would come with all of the dlc. Or why not just have a more expensive bundle with all of it? So ya, easy to see where this is going.

Graphics are pretty nice. Bigger dogs in the yard out does this puppy by a bit but it ain't bad.

Music is badass but forgettable if that makes any sense. It's so hard to explain. I don't find myself needing to mute it or it getting on my nerves at all. But there is really nothing remarkable about it.

Sounds... probably the best part of the game. Weapons clanging, hits and voice lines mid fight. All good. There doesn't seem to be any pre or mid fight voice lines that are character specific. Mid is forgivable but no pre is a bit of a shame.

Story. It. Exists. I've played through the main story and then it seems each character has their own mini story to play through. And I only tackled one of those. But... it's all subpar. The main story is told through lots of exposition and stills. With average at best voice acting. And without spoiling anything the story itself is lack luster.

For other single player content there is a character creator and a mode just for playing that character in. I'm not interested personally so I did not mess with it. There is also an arcade mode, local vs and training. And a gallery so you can re-live unlocking various unlockables... I assume.

Online has ranked and casual. I didn't try casual. But ranked took a decent chunk of time to find an opponent. I wouldn't say it is dead, I have found folks to play with, but how long will vary. I've waited 1-2 minutes and I have waited longer. But never so long I gave up.

DLC. Everyone's favorite topic. As I mentioned I bought the "Definitive Edition" which is their only bundle. But it does not come with all the DLC. You will be missing 4 characters and at least 1 stage still yet on top of like 3 packs of character creation crap. So for a complete package you will end up having to buy more stuff later. It's not a super big deal. It just makes the game deceptively more expensive and just feels like poor marketing all together.

The game play is solid. The last SC game I played was 3 with both 2 and 3 being played heavily. And characters I played way back when feel almost exactly the same. Some moves either no longer exists or was moved around to different buttons. But it's a small amount of moves. And the main cast remains the main cast. In fact I'd say they have not added much in terms of new characters over the years.
There are some meter stuff going on, supers and some weird ass quick time event. I only figured out how to do a couple of supers and didn't learn anything but there is a lot of added depth

The single player is disappointing. The online exists but who knows for how long. Not all the DLC is included in the biggest bundle you can buy as of typing this. What it has going for it is... it looks and feels like SC. Very bare bones with next to no bells and whistles.\t\t\t\t\t
