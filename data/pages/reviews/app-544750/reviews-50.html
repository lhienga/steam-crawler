Ah, I can still hear the violins right now when I read this sentence.
Originally 'Soul Blade' on Playstation 1, a fighting game I've played countless afternoons on, if not for the entertaining battles then it was for the story modes in which you could unlock new weapons per character by defeating enemies that just a bit were more difficult than in the usual arcade matches.
Over the years the name of this franchise changed to 'SoulCalibur'.

See all of the game's characters (without DLCs) in action here: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOIpxtTwcS8

Speaking of characters, in addition to the names associated with the franchise, you will also notice that you will be able to choose characters from other game franchises to fight with such as 'Yoshimitsu' from Tekken and even 'Geralt' from The Witcher. (In Soulcalibur II you can also fight Link from The legend Of Zelda)

Just like in previous parts, part VI also has a story mode in it.
This time there are two different story modes.
The first is the Soul Chronicle, and this mode is very similar to what was in previous installments.
You follow a story for each character that all take place in a certain time period.
The other mode, the Libra or Soul mode, in this mode you make your own fighter, which I personally think is a nice addition.
In this story mode you will travel across a world map to perform missions in different locations, a bit like the story mode in the original story mode from Soul Blade.
Also every fight will be disadvantageous to you,
one opponent will have extra lives or the other can regenerate health, which makes it a lot more difficult for you, but it doesn't always have to be easy, right?

The fighting mechanics still feel good and smooth in the year 22, you won't have to force yourself to perform the combo you want to do, but be prepared for a prepared AI, a must-have for Soul Blade fans anyway. a great recommendation for fans of fighting games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStarting with the gameplay:

The gameplay feels like an entire different ip than the Soul Calibur games of the past. Even the last one, which replaced most of the cast with bootleg versions of iconic Soul Calibur characters and had a meter system as well, feels closer to SC, SC2, SC3, and SC4/SC4BrokenDestiny. All of the new mechanics (reversal edge, soul charge, lethal hits) make for a dishonest game with an awkward flow to matches due to far too damaging/long combos, a super mode with dishonest moves that do extreme damage on hit/chip damage on block/huge advantage on block, and a mechanic that stops the game for a terrible mini-game. The 8 way movement was improved compared to the last two games, but its nullified by most of the cast having a multitude of moves that unnecessarily jail on block when they should be steppable/duckable and really good vertical moves that track sidestep. What is the purpose of the rock paper scissors mini-game that's supposedly in the game to teach people how to respond to certain classes of moves such as verticals and horizontals if verticals aren't steppable for a good amount of moves across most of the cast? Most of the cast have launchers that will lead to good damage on hit, great damage at the wall, and ring outs, but the most you can get for a punish is an AA or BB for barely any damage depending on the push back of the move ON BLOCK (some have awkward push back that cause even those to whiff on occasion). Couple that with the tracking of these launchers, they equal moves that are no longer purposed for whiff punishment/block punishment or a well timed read and instead encourages tossing it out whenever because at worst, you'll get hit with an AA. These moves used to be steppable and punishable by strong moves such as the stabs/shield bashes the Greek characters have for an example to encourage people not to throw them out whenever, but instead to use their brain to take a risk with a read or to punish unsafe moves. There are other moves across the cast aside from launchers that lead to chunky combos and have similar properties that are safe enough on block and have weird push back that allow a player, who in previous games would have lost their turn (without using an impact to take it back of course), to continue to press. The simplicity that Soul Calibur gamepley used to have is completely gone and replaced with obnoxious moves and hollow complexity.

Single Player content:

The character creation is slightly better than 5, but has many of the issues 5 had such as awkward fitting clothes, clothes for body parts don't mesh with others properly and clip, and many items that should be able to go together on different body parts are not allowed to be placed. Still the best character creator by far in any fighting game. Decent overall I guess but I wouldn't call it good. The game was apparently made with a low budget because it was meant to see if the franchise could still move units, which it seems to have done considering it got two seasons. But, because of that, I don't want to be too harsh on the creator mode.

The story mode is ... well the story itself isn't bad, but its just text bubbles and fights against horrible looking create-a-soul opponents. The same goes for Soul of Libra or whatever its called, only you can make your own character to play through it. I don't want to be too harsh on these two as well because of the budget they had to deal with, but they really aren't very good.

Overall, this game is a soulless cash grab entry that's only purpose was to see if the franchise could sell a good enough amount of units not to be permanently shelved. They added multiple gimmicky/ dishonest mechanics that looked cool in theory to see if that would help it, and although the game sold well, you can see those things ruined its longevity by looking at the active player count (or just try finding matches in ranked and casual). And I wasted 680 hours of my life trying to force myself to like it anyway because I LOVE Soul Calibur so much. This game isn't Soul Calibur though. Wouldn't recommend it unless its on sale/ you really love making characters and maybe playing with those characters against your friends. Here's to hoping they made enough money to make another and go back to the identity that made this franchise iconic and fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played Soul Calibur 2 (GC), 3, and 4(PS3), this game's not bad at all. The gameplay is very fun. It feels a lot simpler than the other games, but that's not always a bad thing. Plus I quite like how a good few characters actually have their own unique gimmick that in some cases even really fits their character/backstory much like Kilik having been affected by the Evil Seed to turn evil when he Soul Charges or Siegfried having his Dark Legacy mode. Might Siegfried's Dark Legacy mode be a reference to Soul Calibur 3's opening where he feels like his arm is turning back into Nightmare's claw, but it ends up just being in his head?
The only thing I can really say leaves a good bit to be desired is the creation. I was at least able to absolutely nail Kotarou Fuuma's look from Sengoku Basara; though I wish there was a silent "voice" option due to him; but especially due to me liking to create other characters from the Sengoku Basara and Samurai Warriors franchises and some others due to the time period in which this takes place; in Japan, it was the late Sengoku period; I do wish that either there were a lot more face/facial hair options or that the facial hair wasn't tied to the face option. Having seen the Shimazu Clan during Libra of Souls, I especially wanted to create Yoshihiro Shimazu; especially Samurai Warriors' version due to Astaroth having a hammer as one of his weapons. I do wish that the faces for the humans and the many other human-like races (Outcast, Hidden Village, etc.) weren't all the same, however, and that there were many more options for the Outcasts' ears and tails than just the default rabbit-like ears and either one of two tails or no tail, and that they didn't have normal human ears along with their beast ears.
On the subject of Libra of Souls, it doesn't appear that anything changes based on what race you create your character to be, however, does it? I went as an Outcast, and it would be fitting if at least most of the "generic" non-ally characters actually made at least a passing reference to my species. Granted, you can change anything about your character at any time, including race and gender, but still, it would just be cool to have that little bit of lore actually be referenced where it should matter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fun to play casually, but a couple of the mechanics are...not conducive to fair competitive play. The reversal edge mechanic locks you into a roll of the dice hit. you can just guard out of it but its a big part of play. Its literally rock paper scissors. you and your opponent each pick one type of attack and then its resolved. there is no information about what they picked so you either guess lucky or you dont.

There's a large single player game, story is fine for a fighting game. It's actually pretty fun until you hit high levels and then you have to start cheesing because The ai is not a true opponent, it just reads your input. At high levels it reacts to everything instantaneously. it cuts thru your block and somehow cancels out your critical edge(cutscene super move). But there's usually one or two moves that 'breaks' it. As in it just doesnt respond, so you end up spamming that move a lot.


You wont miss anything if you dont play it. It's a bottom tier fighting game for a reason.\t\t\t\t\t
