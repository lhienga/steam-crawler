This series needs to go back to its roots, I'd wager to say that it needs to go all the way back to the days of SCII which felt like a much more complete game especially the original version of Soul Chronicles ( Libra of Soul as it's now known ).

Libra of Soul still feels like an adventure, but not being able to find and wield Soul Caliber and Soul Edge as well as a much smaller variety of weapons for each character just feels so lazy. SCII even had Soul Caliber and Soul Edge designs for every single character which made them all the more unique as well. All weapons outside the default weapon came with stats such as stronger damage as the cost of handling i.e weapons were slower but hit harder.

All that is missing from just the Libra of Soul mode. I hope another SC comes around, because SC6 feels somewhat empty. Still fun to play, but SCII, III and IV had much more to offer to keep you playing long term.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSOUL CALIBUR! I never played the first, nor the second, neither the third or fourth but I did play the fifth.

If I could explain this franchise it is a unfortunate downfall. I mean it seems to be getting worse and worse with each entry and now we are here. To I am guessing the last game in the series.

Combat - This is Soul Calibur and it hasn't really changed too much and all they did was add a few mechanics here and there. The game is a lot more easier to play now, so anyone could jump into the game and play it and after sinking in an additional 8 Hours it is fun. I mean 24 Main Character's 6 DLC's each add something unique to the combat system and stand apart from one another. I played this game awhile back with a few friends and I am glad that the main game is fun too play.

Story. Hahahha, Okay so take the most generic story in the world and that is the campaign. I wanted to try the campaign and that was a BIG MISTAKE. Their are two versions of the campaign one you make with a player and one that is with the main fighters in the game. Which one is better? Well The main fighters story is over faster...so it depends. Look the story is generic, your on a hunt to stop an evil dude from ruling over the world and it is up to you and only you to do it. The problem is it takes it story so seriously that it becomes boring to sit through. Personally I wouldn't recommend playing through it.

Creation - So Bandai thought like they did in IV & V is still release cosmetic clothing in DLC Packs. It hurts to pay a few bucks to unlock a few extra clothing items and it really isn't worth it. I mean the creation system is there but most of the interesting gear is in the DLC Packs. But you can still do a lot with this system as people have demonstrated.

AI - Almost to near a complete joke, their was very few times I felt challenged by an AI even on the hardest of settings, only because most of the time they are easy. The ones who are annoying are the enemies who have faster attack animations then you, who always break through my guard. But it is still enjoyable.

The problem is...the game doesn't offer many modes. I mean you got online but you deal with Bandai's servers so laggy. Besides a time attack mode, a forgettable campaign and a creation mode this is all that stands out. the problem with this game is their isn't a lot of content. Even with DLC characters their is nothing being added to keep players coming back and overall it feels like a bland game. Pick it up if it goes on sale or you wish to try Soul Calibur.

It's just a 6/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game in many ways but I have to leave a negative review for the greedy, shady business practices Namco and Bandai are engaged in. Day-one DLC means that they surgically removed content from the full game in order to nickel and dime us while they sell the dissected pieces back to us one by one. Season passes that don't include off all of the DLC only make matters worse, the entire point of a season pass is that it includes all the DLC at a discounted price. Not only is this behavior greedily anti-consumer, but the side effects are even more dire. They're intentionally creating an unfair playing field by locking a quarter of the characters behind DLC pay walls. This is unforgivable. The most important thing for a fighting game is to create a fair and balanced playing field. Immediately putting a quarter of the characters behind a pay wall means many people will never get to use them or train with them, putting them at an instant disadvantage compared to people who had the extra money to spend. They pimped out Tekken and they pimped out Soul Calibur, cheapening the games and ultimately cheapening the entire franchises just so they could extort twice the cost of the game from each customer. It's sad and it's disgusting to see these proud and respected fighting franchises reduced to this.\t\t\t\t\t
