\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe main story mode (Libra of Souls) is the best I've seen in any fighting game. It is presented in an RPG where you create your own customizable character to interact with other characters in the cast as well as NPCs to achieve objectives within the story. The art, story, and general gameplay are done well and worth both the time and monetary cost in my opinion. There are a few challenging fights at higher levels.

The character-specific and general story mode (Soul Chronicle) has really nice art and is presented alongside the character portraits, backgrounds, and contains voice acted lines. The fights aren't very difficult and the spotlight is very much on the character stories.

Character customization is far above average and I would personally put it a half step below Tekken but in return you can actually create an original character to fight with.

Online play can be fairly laggy at times and people can and do ragequit. There is no forfeit option in battles, either.

The mechanics are excellent for the most part with fast, fluid gameplay. One of the few fighters that doesn't seem to be dominated only by 60-70 % damage combos and parries.

I fully recommend this game - but have a few cautions.

Caution 1. If you are picking up this game now or in the future (hello future) you should be aware that the online play is more or less dead. You are unlikely to find much in the way of matches and the people remaining might be more interested in trolling or screwing with you than fighting.

Caution 2. Despite having solid mechanics otherwise, the reversal edge and RPS follow-ups draw the fight out and can get quite annoying when you fight people who solely use that. It's not so much anything to do with difficulty and more to do with the annoyance and bastardization of the gameplay that it presents.

Here's to hoping that a new Soul Calibur comes out in the near future (hopefully sans the reversal edge and RPS follow-up system).\t\t\t\t\t
