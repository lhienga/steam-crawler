\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to recommend this game, I really do. But as of now, I would stay away from it.

There are multiple issues with the game that make it hard to play. The game itself runs fine, no crashes or anything. The only stutter is at the autosave.

For the graphics, it has its moments. Some things look cute, some are a bit clunky. Low graphics have no foliage, that happens from high and up. The light for some reason doesn't work well from medium and up. It tries to make shadows, and it fails. The shadows look a bit better on ultra, when it's softer. It still is a dark line going over almost the full map and making everything under it so dark that it's hard to see.

The game has a tutorial that gives you some short missions. I would've preferred it to be one big tutorial, because now you spend more time in loading screens than in the actual tutorial. As for what it explains, it does the bare minimum. It explains how building works, how hunting works, how taming works etc. But it doesn't tell you anything about the details. How to get berries. What buttons make building easier. How to start your own tribe and what it needs. Or that certain buildings need a roof, but it only tells you once it complains about lacking a roof. It doesn't even explain creations well. Ok, it let's you click the button to assign it to someone. They will now talk about it every night until it's done. But how do you get the points, who to assign to it? And it tells you something about how knowledge is lost by people dying and then tells you to build a stone to write stories on. All it says is that knowledge is lost. What does that mean? How does that work? How does the writing on the stone work? (It has a number, and I think it's overlapping with how many points you have in creations. Up until that point is saved, I guess?)

But the worst thing is the pacing. It's absolutely awful and not something they seem to have put a lot of time in. I have fully unlocked multiple recipes in creations. But the one thing to actually be able to use it, I can't unlock. You need cooking in order to cook, makes sense. Cooking is unlocked by getting points in food preparation. Makes sense. So why is it that the only thing that gives points at first barely gives any? You get it by eating, drying food and collecting that. The characters eat every night, and sometimes during the day. Yet, as of now, I'm at year three with 97 points in it. I think cooking is 100. Everything else except hunting (the other slow one) is at 300.

And the game is incredibly slow. I've played at 8 times speed for the entire game, and it's still slow. It takes around 10 seconds for one hour to complete. At 8 times speed. With 24 hours a day, 10 days a season and 4 seasons a year, it takes 160 minutes to complete a year. That is 2,5 hours, on 8 times speed.

If you want to compare it to how long it takes to do things: People age 2 years every one year. You start with 13 year olds, at 14 they get married and have kids. The bar for some reason says they become elderly at 14 years old, but they need to be 21 years old. Pregnancy takes up 30 days, ~70% of the year. This is 120 minutes, or 2 hours. At 8 times speed.

Babies stay useless for 40 days, or a whole in game year. After that they're a toddler for another year. Until the age of four, I can't confirm this fully because that's what the bar says, and the bar lies. This means for a baby to grow up and become an adult, it takes five hours.

Jobs usually take a few in game hours. Let's say four to six hours. Some things take longer, some are way faster. Six hours is one minute per job. Let's say you want to make clothes for all your people, because winter is coming. It would be roughly eight minutes for a job then. But people don't work 24/7, they eat, they sleep, they take breaks. I barely finished the job at the end of winter, with one guy on it every working hour.

Food is another weird thing. They eat 10 at a time for the basic foods. 15 for less basic foods like milk, and 1 for prepped meals like cheese. Who eats 10 fishes the size of their body? It takes 100 milk to make 7 cheese. 100 milk is good for around six to seven meals, and so is 1 cheese. It works, I guess? But why waste time on making cheese when it's the same as milk.

They prefer eating fresh food over dried food with the basic settings, something I quickly turned off or they wouldn't even bother making dried food. You can rearrange the order yourself. It misses a function to eat the things that'll spoil sooner.

Then there's quests. Things other tribes want to know. I'm not sure how they decide on that, but giving me a quest to teach someone about how good a certain ingredient is before I've even unlocked that ingredient (Aka we don't know it exists) is odd. It leads to a constant stream of fails until you've unlocked scribing. When you do, it doesn't matter if you yourself don't know anything about dogs, you wrote something down about hunting and that's what matters.

The priority system is good, it works like expected. It starts you off with a few people having one thing at 1 and the rest at 3, but it's easier having most people not do that task at all and having one dedicated person for it. With how you can switch what to focus on per task, it makes it pretty easy to switch over when something is needed. Although it would be preferred if they'd get all things evenly, so one thing doesn't run out because they only want to collect the top thing.

A minor thing is the text. When having a store room for example, the numbers are at the bottom right corner if facing top down. Any other angle and it becomes messy as to which number counts for what. Also, the names don't have spaces between first and last name, but dire wolf has a space between dire and wolf :(

Gameplay is fun, until it's two hours of staring waiting for things to happen or to be done/unlocked. You can send people out on trips to gather herbs, which is like a rabbit hole. The hunting outside does take you to the place, as of now it's completely empty besides the few animals you are hunting. Which usually is a group of three, two adults one juvenile.

All in all, this game frustrates me so much, 8 times speed feels like 1,5 times speed. Anything lower and it literally seems like they're walking in slo-mo. The game doesn't progress until you unlock the creations, and with how hard it is to get enough points, it's a waiting game.

It can become fun, when there's more added to it. Once they fix the game pace, so research doesn't take a full real day to progress. For now, I'd say only take it if you can handle this.\t\t\t\t\t
