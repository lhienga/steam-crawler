\r\n\t\t\t\t\tActually one of the more enjoyable RPGs I've played lately, but not without its faults.

Quick summary:
> Solid graphics with only the occasional stuttering with all settings maxed on a modern system with an RTX 3080 Ti.

> The overall plot is something easy to mess up, with the typical "colonists invade the natives, will our character throw away his allegiance to save the natives" setup being pretty easy to turn into a giant cliched mess. The storywriters actually managed to pull this one off by taking a more balanced view of why the island was being invaded and that the natives weren't entirely blameless for the events that occurred.

> Sidequests were storyline driven, not "go pick up 10 items and come back." I was interested enough in the sidequests to actually complete most of them.

> Companions were actually interesting to interact with and usually had comments during the various dialogues with NPCs.

> Not really what I'd call open world. Most places on the map correspond to an actual quest location, and if you go there before you get the quest, there isn't anything to do. I'd highly recommend just doing the quests, you'll explore 80-90% of the map that way anyway.

> Combat was okay, but not great. I used magic the entire time, which was fun, but most battles were pretty much cast stasis and then mash the light magic attack. If you're looking for deep combat, you'll probably be bored here, but I did enjoy the combat more than Tales of Arise.

> There are skill trees to fill out, but they're not particularly deep. For magic, you could pick up 4-5 extra abilities, but by the end of the game I was still just using 2 spells plus the default light attack. There was never really a need to go for complex combos, and boss fights on hard difficulty felt more like just mashing buttons and popping mana potions until it died.

> I enjoyed this game more with mods, particularly the mods that allowed for grabbing all the talent abilities for charisma/lockpicking and that allowed for purchasing memory crystals from vendors (allows for resetting the skill tree). Being able to pass all the various checks during the storyline/exploration actually made the game a lot more enjoyable.

> This was an interesting game when playing as a good guy, but it's probably not a particularly interesting game when playing as a bad guy. If you're a good guy, the storylines intersect and finish in a satisfying way. If you choose the evil path, I doubt it's nearly as satisfying as playing renegade Shepherd (yeah, yeah, I know renegade technically isn't "evil," but it's close enough). That said, I only chose the relatively good options during morality checkpoints, so I might be wrong. It just seemed like choices were basically "good results in success" or "evil results in failure," based on how the plotline ended up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really good game. It is *very* story-centered and you can only explore the world so far before you won't have a choice but to progress the story. As a completionist, it definitely keeps you locked on trying to do a bunch of stuff upfront and the only way to over-level (if you prefer to play that way) is to continuously run around the maps at each point in the story and fight the enemy spawns to reach higher levels (best xp gains are from the quests). While I have not beaten the game and am catching up to where I was on Xbox, I can tell that there is not a way to reach max level simply following the questing (so if you're going to go for the "unlock all [class] skills" achievements in a single playthrough, you'll need to respec to get them). I do agree that in some aspects that the game feels like it plays slow, and you do end up running around a bit (no mounts as far as i can tell), but this game does not have a combat focus and is more about actually telling the story (which is certainly on the more unique end of games that I have played). The combat is rather simple, certainly not like WoW or FFXIV, but it gets the job done. I give the game props for being made by a smaller company, and not a well-known company (at least from what I can tell). I feel like this game is great for people that want a change of pace from combat-focused games and/or those looking to try out an RPG if they do not play one. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a huge fan of RPGs like this, and I've played some mediocre ones, but this is probably as mediocre as it gets. I'm not saying it's horrible, and for a small studio it does have some more impressive bits, but the overall package is lacking. The story and characters are just ok, nothing special. The companions specifically irk me since they do that thing that's so common where they'll automatically trust your character and ask you for your help. Not much build-up. The combat and exploration are just boring. I don't even mind reused assets within the environment (which there is a lot of), but when the actual gameplay/combat is repetitive on top of that, then I have a problem. There are some janky RPGs like Dragon Age for example that have a lot of problems, but have very strong aspects that boost the entire package and makes the game memorable/enjoyable. Greedfall doesn't have any very strong parts. I hope the next game is better!\t\t\t\t\t
