\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game because I was bored and it looked mildly entertaining.......Well was I misjudging. Open blender lid and add dragon age 2 and 3 a touch of witcher 3 and a pretty decent amount of fable 2 the lost chapters. This game has a fantastic story line and THANK GOD there isnt fetch quest. There are some null moments in the game but overall the 13 hours I've invested were well worth it. The class upgrade options really give you a great ability to build your character however you see fit. However you start make sure you start with lock picking 1 and after you move to the new area you don't have to continue it if you don't want but its extremely helpful in the starter area. I'm a huge fan of bioware games, well maybe no so much inquisition and Andromeda but before that a huge fan and this reminds me a lot of how those games that were masterfully designed. 8/10 so far. One huge pet peeve has been avoided thus far and that is I have not had to try for an extended amount of time or google/youtube what I need to do next. The game is very smooth and very user friendly. The amount of exploration available is just a tad overwhelming at first but when you come to understand the scale of the map it becomes much less intimating. I circle back around to this game was very well crafted and designed. The singular complaint I have so far is your companions get in the way and npcs wont move out of the way which is not helped by your character moving like they just learned how to walk yesterday. Ive noticed often that when I move into a room your companions will position left and right of doorway which is extremely smart programming. This is not a deal breaker for me and I am loving this gaming experience I just want to put the little things out there for possible patches in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

Combat: Is it clunky? It's not polished as far as real time combat might go. But it's certainly no clunkier than the Risen games. And if you make use of the pause function, it plays a bit more like SWOTOR or Mass Effect and can result in pretty satisfying combat.
Walking simulator: No more than any Elder Scrolls, arguably(undeniably) not even close to that bad.
Boring enemies: I've only just entered the 'wilds' of the game, having left New Serene. It does seem they tried to go for a more 'naturalistic' or 'realistic' vibe in that they didn't seem to spend a huge amount of time coming up with an library sized bestiary, instead sticking with a few basic hostile creatures and interspersing them with hostile humans.
But there are some nice surprises and the BB's definitely can give you a fun and challenging fight.

Basically, from a gameplay angle this is kind of like grown up Risen, mixed with some SWOTOR/ME, and polished off with some inspirations from Witcher. It's a solid all around RPG from what I can tell.

Story wise, I can't say much about it. It feels pretty Dances with Wolves/Avatar with some heavy over-dramatic colonial/native vibes. It creates a caricature out of the complex realities of warring industrial nations, the discovery of new lands, and the attempt to walk the line of peace and diplomacy. But it isn't overdone in a way that makes you feel like there's a "MESSAGE" involved.
It seems like you have some room to roleplay a certain sort of attitude for your character, but, honestly, I just haven't gotten that deep into the story or relational systems.\t\t\t\t\t
