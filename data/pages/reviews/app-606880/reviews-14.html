\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKeep in mind that this review was made before reaching the 100% completion of the game, for this reason it will probably be subject of changes and updates in the near future.
I think that this game was treated in a poor way by reviewers and even tho I agree on some criticism about some mechanics, I still find this game to be highly enjoyable and solid.

PROS:
-Decent graphics including armors, textures, details etc.
-Lovely setting (I love the late 1600's/early 1700's vibe and the baroque period architecture! Absolutely fantastic
-Solid main plot, again I stil didn't end the main quests nor the side quests but up until now it was very interesting indeed, the factions, lore and the protagonist choices are superb! I love the diplomat role that de Sardet portrays, the skill trees are also cool too.

SO-SO:
-I've read many reviews saying that the combat is clunky. I don't find it to be clunky, I started playing in 2023, perhaps it was changed and improved but clunky combat and animations are game such as War of Rights and AC Mirage.
This was generally alright, not too frustrating but yeah it gets pretty repetitive after a while, nonetheless it didn't bother me that much to simply wanting to quit the game, like in others case

-I didn't find any comment speaking about this, or at least very few, but this game is rather uhh, "politically correct".
What I mean by this is that it fills the diversity quotas, I usually don't like woke games but this game doesn't spread a political propaganda or narrative, it fills quotas and that's basically it. You'll find africans, (white people?) with Sikh headwear who look like indians, females holding high command positions etc. Still this isn't annoying because the game isn't bias, it doesn't represent a faction with all of a skin color and make them good and others bad, all factions have positive and negative traits, no distictions or bias on that. Another reason for it being understandable is that it is a fantasy setting, still I found way too much of this "diversity and inclusivity" for my tastes let's say 5 (sometimes even up to 7) on 10 NPC's are either indians, africans or women, a bit too much not gonna lie, especially in roles that are highly unlikely no matter what.

CONS:
-With maximum graphics on my gaming PC when you load in certain areas it have some annoying lag-spikes every now and then, i've decreased the graphic settings a bit but yeah it's not mastefully optimized tbh.
-As far as I know there are no horses in the game so it's basically a walk simulator, very long distance on foot, fast travels exist and are very useful but still it's an upsetter
-Some quests get very repetitive and boring but I found most to be decent

Final rating: 8.5
The devs had low funds and it's not a TRIPLE A game but they created an intriguing and appealing scenario and universe. I'm enjoying it alot and I can't wait for Greedfall 2!

EDIT after 100% completion (All main and side quests)
I really enjoyed the game, I loved the suprising turn of events and the general plot.
The graphic is very decent and I crashed only once in the entire game.

I highly recommend this game for players who like GDR's, Open World 3rd person fantasy/ somewhat historical videogames.
It has some issues and it's not perfect (keep in mind that it was made by a small studio and they did a good work anyway) but basically your role as diplomat makes you wander a lot and you sometimes feel like a courier going back and forth, that may be annoying to someone, but I was pretty much absorbed into the role so it wasn't that annoying to me.

You can't continue playing after the end of the main questline so make sure to do all the side quests previous to that! (You get a warning anyway)
Make sure to do your companion quests too!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely loved GreedFall.

I was looking for a game to fill the void left by Dragon Age, so I got GreedFall as soon as it came out. And my goodness did it deliver.

To me, the game's greatest strength is its characters. Not only the player character and companions, but all NPCs as well. They all represent their faction to an extent, but through them you see different facets of each faction, and you may change your opinion of one faction or another because of the characters you meet. I was particularly impressed by the contrast between Thlme and the Bridge Alliance : the first quests you do for them give you a general idea of who they are and who you want to root for, but once you are on the island their roles kind of reverse and you may find yourself cheering for the other faction.

Characters also have both positive and negative traits, that feel tailored to players who may like or dislike that specific kind of character. You can support someone who appears kind and empathetic and then learn they also have a cunning ruthless side. So do you keep on supporting them ? Does the good outweighs the bad ?

I also found that several choices were more nuanced than what could be expected from this kind of game. Sometimes it's not just about being diplomatic/kind/ruthless, it is "do I reveal the truth despite the chaos it might cause or should I lie to keep the peace ?" or "do I agree with sacrificing some people to save others ? (and is it even my only choice ?)" or even "what do I do when it gets personal ?" ...

You should play the game just to experience that, honestly.

Other good points include the combat and crafting systems, which are fairly intuitive and permissive, so if it's not what you want to focus on you don't have to spend too long on it. I played on normal difficulty and had no problems, but there is a lower difficulty that really makes combat easy if you don't want to think about it at all. It's also a beautiful game that makes you want to explore every new place you discover.

Finally, depending on your choices you get different endings, and you don't have to do what you were originally sent to the island for. It is entirely your choice.

The conclusion is GreedFall blew my mind and I can't recommend it enough to anyone who likes RPG. Go play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall is a solid game: solid setting, combat mechanics, story, etc. It's not the best but it's serviceable: jack of all trades, master of none. However, I did not finish the game despite putting about 30-40 hours into it because it honestly overstays its welcome.

The two biggest critiques I have go hand in hand in that this game is enjoyable in the beginning but gets more and more tedious the more you continue to play. Mission design goes like: talk to this NPC to progress the main story, NPC needs a favor first before he gives you what you need so it splinters off to another quest you have to do in order to get the info you need in order to progress the main quest. This goes on for the majority of the game and the cherry on top is that you have to manually walk to almost every objective with the exception of a few times the game gives you the option to skip the trip.

There is fast travel in this game but you can only do so from the few camp points scattered at any given hub world. This game is not open world, it's more hub-spaces akin to something like borderlands or dragon age inquisition. There's no fast traveling from the map menu, you have to literally walk everywhere.

This game could've been a solid 10-20 hour game but it's unnecessarily stretched out to 40+ hours. Even on sale, I don't think I could recommend this game. Not because it's a bad game in terms of story, gameplay, etc. It's a bad game in that it's overly bloated and it just takes way more of your time than it deserves. Just go to youtube for a summary of the story and look up the ending and save yourself 40 hours of your time you could dedicate elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall is a compilation of mediocrity. The story, the combat, the relationship with the companions, the exploration and the ending are all mediocre. When you finish the game, you're not glad that you've made it to the end, but that you are not going to play it anymore.

Now, it looks like this game is ugly. It is not so bad to be avoided, but it not so good to make me feel satisfied while I was playing it. The fact that it lasted almost 60 hours is not something positive (30 hours could have been enough).

The story: it starts quite well, for the first 5-8 hours it even seems fun, then the exploratory part begins, here you start to feel that feeling of mediocrity of the game. The problem with the story is that it's too fragmented, you follow important missions and then a lot of side quests that break up the story by constantly adding things to do. At first, they are fine, but then they start to get boring.

The fights don't help, as the combat mechanics are too simplistic and don't change much in how they play out. Even with bosses the combat is not very satisfying, it only changes that you must hit them with many more blows. The underlying problem is that the moves and actions to do are few, soon the fights become repetitive, and you only do them to increase the level of the character.

Exploration is a part that will take you a lot, you often must travel miles in the various areas of the game to reach the objective of the mission (fortunately there are points that, if unlocked, allow you to get to certain parts of the maps quickly). In my opinion, they could have reduced the areas of exploration both in number and size. Expect to return to some areas often.

Relationships with comrades is another part done with mediocrity. There are the usual missions that allow you to increase relationships with them (even sentimental), but everything is treated in a simple way, there are never real insights between the members of the group.

Technically it's (guessed it) mediocre, the graphics are certainly not next gen, but I never pay much attention to it. Let's say that technically it doesn't shine, it stays in line with everything else, but it never had any problems or bugs.

If Steam also had the neutral vote, this game would be the ideal candidate to get it; instead, I must give a vote that doesn't represent my vision of the game. I don't feel like giving a negative vote, after all I've finished it (and I don't finish a bad game), so I'll give it a positive vote even though I know that it doesn't fully deserve it.

If you want to buy it, wait for the sales, it doesn't deserve the full price.
Final score: 65/100\t\t\t\t\t
