\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo maybe it gets better. Maybe. But there's something in the literary field we call "the hook." The hook is the principal that a book has between 5 and 10 pages maximum to grab your attention, before it has failed to properly interest you. It's why so many books start "in media res." I'm a big fan of "in media res." Greedfall, however...
Greedfall starts you about 19 years before anything interesting happens. The writing is...bad. Just bad. About what I would have expected from a 1910's pulp novel. The characters are flat, including the main character, and the world is...empty, and meaningless. And before anyone thinks, well, of course, it's a AA game...no excuses. I've played games made by 2 people with more life, heart, and thought put into them.
The combat might as well not exist. Ever play Vampyre? Yeah, it's way, way worse than Vampyre. And Vampyre's combat is so basic and braindead as to appeal primarily to a slightly advanced species of garden slug. Greedfall's is worse. Every single combat encounter, regardless of your focus, magic, melee, or guns, comes down to dodge/block, attack, dodge/block, attack. You can't take advantage of any openings in melee, because the game rockets enemies too far away. You can't combo any attacks, because enemies ignore hits to smash you half to pieces. The only difficulty comes from your own rapidly fraying patience, because the entire system seems deliberately bad to disguise the intrinsic lack of creativity and skill.
But no, you want to know about the story. Well, prepare to be bored. The first 3 hours of the game is just a series of boring "you thought was A, but is actually B" gotcha quests that simulate both creativity and originality, when in reality, the entire issue with this game is that the writing is some of the worst I've encountered outside of a children's book. If the writing was good, I'd excuse the bad combat. But the bad combat is all this game has, because you will spend 9/10ths of the game walking from one conversation that is poorly implemented to another, with zero actual depth to any of it.
2/10 for actually running. The UI works. That's about all i can give this waste of time, money, and storage.\t\t\t\t\t

It comes with some intrigue, investigation, and competing interests that you can try to align to one cause (rabid religious fanatics, cold-hearted scientists, extreme naturalists). You got guns (rather OP but resource-intensive), magic, and melee. You got collectible armor and weapons, some light romantic flairs (follow guides if you want to get them - some choices are easy to miss, especially if you're skipping dialogue).

A few plot twists I saw coming, and others I did not. It's not the deepest of stories, but it's not shallow either. It favors people trying to make nice with everyone, but that's a balancing act. You have the occasional hard choice you must make in favor of one that makes the other unhappy.

Not quite open world, but it has several open regions (going into some at early levels can indeed kill you - no level locking, only story locking).

Replay for different endings/sides/romantic interests/playstyles

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like to start with the negative before positive - but overall I love this game and have become mildly obsessed with it the past week.

Negatives -
1. The combat controls are uncomfortable and clunky to me. I've never modified keyboard controls for a game and I wished I was more familiar with that after dying many times to the first boss.
2. At the moment I'm upset because I spent 20 hrs on the story line and despite doing the proper dialogues, have somehow managed to cause a character's romance/dialogue option to disappear and had already saved. There isn't a reason that I've been able to find - all I did was pick one of the basic dialogue options from the beginning to listen to it again for fun. Then when I wanted to pick the romance options they were gone. Now I may have to restart.
3. The companion romance conversations and scenes are limited. There are nuances in the story to help build up, but it can feel lacking. One thing that bothered me most is that romanced characters and friends don't change their transition or goodbye to the MC in a way that fits the mood/relationship. For example, you'll have the romantic line as a lover, scene, and then the character will go, "Anything else?" in the exact some tone you've had since the beginning of the game. It'd be nice if the romanced companion also had an interesting idle spot in the legate's home.
4. I wish some areas have more variety - which may be due to studio finances - but many of the governor's areas look similar and your home, so on. As a side note, it would be nice to personalize your home.

Positives
1. In my opinion the story is interesting and one of the highlights are the characters. Each character, including the background rulers you interact with, are fleshed-out individuals. None of them fit the "bad" or "good" category easily. You'll have a seemingly "nice" character who manipulates you and the result is peace, but the betrayal leaves a bad taste. It truly feels like you can trust almost no one and you get paranoid (at least I did)
2. As short as they are, the romances are wonderful and the body language is well done for the character models.
3. Your side quests may get repetitive, but often you can do quests without much fighting if you have proper skills and your character actually can sprint fast - so unless you're weighed down it's not a chore like other games might feel.
4. The game is gorgeous - the music is gorgeous - and it's nice to just wander around a bit. It simply looks beautiful. You even have nice hairstyles for the characters.
5. Your dialogue options actually match what your character says (I've had issues in others like DA) where the character's words don't match at all and then I've had to reload.
6. I also can't say enough about character development. All of your companions will occasionally make comments even on quests not related to them. It helps create an attachment to the team.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gave this game a pretty fair chance and I just can't get into it. I enjoy the setting, I enjoy the magic, I even enjoy the native population of the island though it can be a little stereotyped at times. I however could not even begin to care about the PC(even and maybe especially after the obvious "surprise" of his bloodline), you're the savior and hero because that's what the story said needed to happen. The game punishes you for focusing on quest chains over others, including side quests. For example, at some point in one of the cities there is a coup; this is the *main* story quest, eventually there is a point where depending on your friend level one of your companions will betray you. Then you find out, ALL of your companions have the chance to betray you; so you better do those friendship quests ASAP or you'll be forced to kill characters you've been building up for the last however many hours.

Long winded review aside, if this game had a QoL overhaul and less walls of text for a tutorial I think this game could be great. Also way too much "chatting" dialogue, easily 4 or 5 hours of my playtime is just watching the characters stare at each other and vomit exposition at each other, rarely is there emotion.\t\t\t\t\t
