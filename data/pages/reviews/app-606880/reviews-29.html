\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is (almost) at the same level as those AAA games by big names such as Obsidian or Bioware. Almost. The story is engaging, with consequences here there and everywhere, Ask the wrong question and people would refuse to talk to you, or outright get hostile. Kill somebody and leadership of factions may change hands. The game-world is beautifully rendered with menacing beasts justifying their roles as guardians of the island. Clashing opinions and interests between factions where you will risk enraging one while attempting to help another. This game is truly one of the best role-playing experiences I've had since Mass Effect trilogy.

Still, the lack of hefty budget enjoyed by bigger studios shows. Remember the asset reuse fiasco of Dragon Age 2? Tough not as blatant, the game reuses assets to the point that the interior of every palace is the same, and so is every barrack, tavern, and even house. Facial expression is passable but often rendered not synced to the spoken dialogue. Those technical deficiencies are abound. Still, the game is pretty stable with just minor non-game breaking bugs.

Combat is difficult, for the most part because the camera angle is unfriendly. But tactical pause can be your friend. Magic system is very limited but is open to all classes as optional skill expansion. Companions are opinionated but you can sway them to your way of thinking if you choose the right dialogue/action options. All in all, the good outweighs the bad in this game.

And one idea that probably should be copied by bigger studios: this game has the coolest "loading screed" ever. Instead of getting the "tips and tricks loading screen", we get a small "camp in the woods" where we can adjust team members and loadout, do some shopping, or craft potions/equipment. This feature is very useful because you will probably want certain members with you to help convince a particularly hard-headed faction leader, and yet you prefer another member for combat leading to meet the said leader. This feature gives a new dimension to role-playing experience.

TLDR: this is a good game worth its price. Especially if you enjoy exploration and political role-playing. Lack of budget shows in lack of technical polish but if you are not put off by unsynced lips, you will find 50 to 60 hours of very enjoyable experience.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am 20.1 hours in and I am thoroughly enjoying it. I even recommended it to my friends to buy it now while it is -70% off.
If you loved classic RPG's like Dragon Age, The Witcher, with a New World kinda vibe and style this might be the game for you. Fast pace combat that kind of bridges fantasy and steam punk into one enjoyable ride. The characters are likable and interesting enough to keep you involved in the story.

My only issues is the companions don't really seem to help you. Even in the first boss they are no where to be found and you have to solo a giant monster that can kill you in three hits. It kinda makes having companions unnecessary if every big fight depends on you. This is the difference between Dragon Age and where it is more like The Witcher. You will have to figure out your build early to defeat these creatures because your companions are not the heroes you thought they were, they only help you with weaker scraps, thus on the big fights you will need Magic and stasis to stop enemies from tearing you to shreds. You wont be able to focus on melee and gun play early to progress; to me that is unfortunate because I happen to dislike magic in all games, and prefer the tanky melee fighter.

I give it a 7/10 and root for the underdogs. I am hoping in GF 2 they allow the companions to have a more vital part in the big fights. And make it feel like a cohesive team.\t\t\t\t\t

Overview:
GreedFall lands you in the middle of an adventure, War, Sickness, Strange Creatures, a New World. Take command of a Legate faced with keeping diplomatic arrangements alive and those that would cause an imbalance at bay.

Pick and design your characters traits, features, and abilities as well as their path. Several choices you make along the way will determine the style you take. Each play style will give a very different feel to the game and make sure you learn your character and master their abilities.

As you progress more and more of the world becomes available to you and you will soon find your way through a massive world with many areas being visited over and over again. Yet, with each visit, things seem to change in tone, scenery, or even from peace to war or vise versa.

Combat:
At a normal difficulty the combat is easy at first with a pretty decent curve as you progress through the middle. Yet, even at 10-15 hours of game play I found myself fighting enemies that I had to learn their weaknesses and my strengths in order to over power them. At higher difficulties I would imagine those fights would be punishing.

Dialogue:
Other than a few typos I have noticed. The dialogue is pretty strong and it hardly ever repeats itself unless you talk to someone you have already talked to. You can tell a great amount of time went into the voice overs and the dialogue.

Story:
Not at the end yet but so far I have experienced many twists, turns, betrayals, and surprises along the way. The thing I love about this game is the unpredictable nature of what the next mission may be hiding. Even a lot of the side quests have left me guessing only to be completely different from what I thought would happen.

Price:
I picked it up for $10 and I bought the DLC just because an extra $3 for some cool stuff didn't seem to bad for me. I needed another RPG to play anyway. I'd say it was well worth $10 as I already have 33 hours at the time of writing this review and I have plenty more to explore and will more than likely be well over 50 hours before I am done. $0.20 an hour for game play is pretty great compared to a lot of the other games I have played.


TLDR:
Get it on sale for a great deal, DLC is neat but I've found plenty of things that compete with it. Game has a loads to explore and do and will easily entertain you for hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though game was released in 2019, the graphics feel like I'm playing 2012 game at most (with all settings at ultra and resolution 2560x1600). Low quality player and npc characters (animations are so unnatural they almost exert physical pain) with unnaturally built characters, whose movement looks like from the Monty Python's "Ministry of Silly Walks, poor customization of keybindings (forget variable perspective).

Meele fight is basic at most, feels more like constant quick-time event with the same actions repeating over and over. Forget the gunfight, cause you won't even get to aim the sidearm on your own (auto-fire as only weapon key-bind available), thus feels like hunting the ducks.
Didn't touch magic though, so maybe there is one thing they did right (not sure whether I want to find out after what I've seen).

Pause menu instead of expanded movement seems like yet another joke. It actually makes the game even more difficult than it needs to be, since its counter-intuitive and located under both tab and space keys. Inventory screen looks like some poor imitation of the one from the Witcher 3.

Dialogues are yet another drawback: they sound so artificial that when paired with the low-quality npc animations I could barely hold myself from shutting the game down immediately.

As a conclusion, this game is definitely not worth it's full price. On a discount, MAYBE for the story (should it get any more interesting later in the game).
