I will say that melee combat was less than optimal, but I enjoy mage combat. Guns are overpowered and the controls lack some finesse. It's not perfect, but it was fun, sappy, and I haven't shed this many tears over a game since my first time playing Mass Effect.

I wish there was more mob diversity, it always seemed to be the same bear or lizard like creature. The DLC was funny, because you hate her within 10 seconds of her opening her mouth, and it made the ending of the DLC satisfying.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent role-playing game, whose very rich and engaging story will bring you into an alternate 17th century, where the continental civilizations are fading away, decimated by a terrible blood poisoning disease. A miraculous island discovered two decades ago seems promising for a cure, its totally unknown flora and fauna, as well as the magic used by its inhabitants, offering many new paths to explore. The landscapes are magnificent, from the darkest ominous forest cover, to desert rock plateaus, passing by sunny green glades, and the cities offer the characteristics of the country they represent, the bubbling Hikmet dedicated to even the most controversial sciences, the stifling Theleme and its implacable faith in the "Luminous" whose opening messages have been misguided by bloodthirsty inquisitors, and finally your city, the New Serene, dedicated to trade above all and trying to preserve its position of neutrality.
It is possible to start a "romance" with 5 of your companions, but unlike the BioWare games, speaking nicely to them is not enough. After each of the 3 personal quests of the targeted character, a very specific line of dialogue will be the only one to make you progress in his heart, so that he then asks you for a private meeting. An achievement is obtainable for each seduced companion.
The only annoying point in my opinion is due to the system of skill points, as the limited number of which requires, in order to acquire the most interesting elements during the quests, to perform a gymnastic of saving the game before each important dialog or action, reloading the game and changing outfits when you saw an ability is needed you don't have actually, and keep in inventory specially "crafted" suits for each essential skill ( stamina, science, lock picking...), and even, for the rarest pieces of equipment and certain achievements, to totally reset the points of all the characteristics of the character, just to go from 3 lock picking points to 3 vigor, or science... (on the condition of course that you have acquired the crystals needed for this). This prevents you from immersing yourself in the rich story and breaks the roleplay part of the game, which is otherwise excellent, and that's a shame.\t\t\t\t\t
