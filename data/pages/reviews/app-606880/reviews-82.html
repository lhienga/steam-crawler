\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is pretty good.
It's an action RPG similar to Mass Effect, Outer Worlds, and Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic.


-Story
You play as a Legate of the Congregation of Merchants searching for a cure to a mysterious magical disease, building relationships with party members and the other factions as you explore a new magical island. Every faction is corrupt in some way and you can help to fix them and negotiate peace between them. Thankfully, it has a satisfactory ending similar to the fallout games.

-Gameplay
It's a 3rd person action rpg where you and 2 party members fight random beasts and thieves as you explore open areas of the world. You can customize your playstyle with skill points. You can use tailor your character for heavy weapons, lighter swords, magic bolts, guns, traps, throwable potions, and to imbue weapons with special effects. You can dodge and parry.

-Music
It has a mysterious and beautiful theme song. The rest of the music is technically good and fits the game but is just not that memorable. Most music is just kind of ambient.

-Flaws
Sadly you end up fighting the same enemies over and over and a lot of the enemies feel the same. And at some point the combat feels kind of pointless because you don't get much reward from it. Enemies will feel like an obstacle to be ignored. Also, there is a lot of walking.


Overall, the game was fun and entertaining. I loved the story and faction interaction and the combat (when you actually fight things that matter). Definitely get this game if you like Mass Effect/Kotor/Outer Worlds.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid effort for a mid tear game. At times it feels like a AAA release, maybe from a a generation or 2 ago. It plays well and it feels like choices matter, because they do. Most of the time, the good choice is obvious, but then others not so much, and even the "right" choice has consequences. The companion set-up is great, you get a feel for the different characters of each and even though you end up using one or two far more often than the others, you still feel like engaging with them all. The story isn't groundbreaking but it and the setting make for an enjoyable time.
Combat is alright, not too complicated, not overly simplistic, but not very dynamic either. You settle into a groove based on your combat type and even when switching weapons, there is not a lot of skill needed.
Cut scenes, though, are where you find the most wrinkles. Whether its NPCs standing in front of the camera, or blocky movement, or - what bugged me most- characters looking at the wrong person when speaking. Sometimes, in a three way scene, persons A and B will be talking, and even though they are addressing each other, they will look in-turn to C and speak. its weird, and its common.
Overall, though, if this game had come out in the early 2010's, and was up against Skyrim or GTA5, it would have been huge, Today though, its still a solid effort from a relatively small developer. Its got a unique style and take on the western RPG and some really cool features (travel camp is a great idea), and I hope this studio get to do some more, with the right budget and time, these guys could do something great.\t\t\t\t\t
