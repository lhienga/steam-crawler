\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story is great and characters are well written. But that couldn't make me ignore certain flaws of the game. The character animations, especially running, looks outdated for a game that came out in 2019. Also, even though the game looks like an open world adventure, it feels more like a semi-open world. What I mean is that the areas usually feel like a big, one directional maze instead of a vast place where you can wander in any direction and explore. The wilderness locations feel a bit repetitive and not too alive. They look similar to each other and you keep encountering same types of aggressive beasts, which makes it a bit boring after a while. Another minus point for me was how some of the quests made me run back and forth at the same area multiple times.

Overall, even though I found the story quite engaging and well written; I decided to uninstall this game after playing it for 13 hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinished Greedfall. Too much dialogue. Over-extended. Characters feel uninteresting and useless. Combat is solid, but enemies are boring and unrewarding. Pacing is really an issue when Devs want players to detach themselves from main quest to go do boring sidequests.

  • Companions and all the other characters do nothing to the story. It's just you going around earning people's trust.

  • Too much "go there, talk to that guy". You, a "diplomat" going left and right talking to everybody for chunks of dialogue just to "get their trust". Forced fetch quests. You really learn how to use that Skip Dialogue button.

  • Feels like a lack of big events. The story would have been better shorter. STORY SPOILER Now I feel like Kurt saving me when Coin Guards tried to take over made it boring. I'd have preferred the backstab+bossfight+meaningful death; but he's the only good character.

  • The most you'll see is that one little fight with earth bending in a finished battle against colonials near the time you arrive at the island. This little scene is also in the trailer.

  • DLC The De Vespe Conspiracy was cheesy and funny, but ends too empty. Small quest, everyone is fine and it was just pointless to the main story. I didn't even realise it was a paid DLC.

  • The ending is underwhelming, then comes picture screens just to tell you: "Sorry, you didnt do 20 sidequests for that faction".

Greedfall is incredibly weaker than Dragon Age Inquisition because of lack of meaningful characters and big events. The Witcher 3 also has a lot of dialogue, but the story, big events, cutscenes and characters make up for it. Vampyr is bad, flooded with dialogue, but at least has cool combat and meaningful tight bossfights.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStrange mix, the map, the encounter and crafting feel like a JRPG. The story structure is more of a modern ARPG like a mass effect style RPG. The combat is very dynamic and a mix between a JRPG and an action RPG.
The story suffers from the mobility (linear map, invisble walls, fetch quests, etc) that are common to JRPG. You go form A to B to activate something, collect something, speak to someone.

Visually it's pretty, there is a lot to do but feels empty.

Overall, the game is not bad nor very good. It's a good middle tier game that will make you enjoy your time ; while you discover the world.
A good RPG it you don't want complicated scenarios nor combat and like the setting.\t\t\t\t\t
