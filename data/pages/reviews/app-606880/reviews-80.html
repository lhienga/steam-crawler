\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt over Lvl 10 after 30 hours and a couple restarts now, I think I have a pretty good handle on this game. Greedfall really grabbed me. It's set on an alternate world where tech like flintlocks and magic simultaneously exist. It's a novel take on the punk genre substituting a late Renaissance setting for Steam or Cyber. As such characters look like extras from "The Three Musketeers" and themes of colonization, evangelization, and exploitation of a "new world" and its native population all play out. I am really enjoying the setting and lore of this game, its renaissancepunk aesthetic, along with the mechanics and character build options. The quests and factions are pretty interesting as is the take on sneaking/stealth and disguise. My only negatives were the repetitive nature of some interior sets and that there is no jumping (though I am informed that this is not completely unprecedented a la Dark Souls by my nephew). If you can catch it on sale like I did then all the better but it's still a very solid offering even at the full price of $35 when you compare the hours you get out of such a game to the cost and time a movie theater runs. While the game really excels in plot and story but again it does reuse location assets a bit too much for my liking. Some locations like private residences and the governors' mansions all look too similar that it's obvious and very noticeable. Still I'm really enjoying the story so I can overlook these relatively minor faults. I'd give it a 4/5* and think it was a steal when I picked it up for $16 at 60% off on a weekend sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWould I recommend this game yes, but with heavy warnings.

It's a good RPG and an interesting spin/setting that we haven't gotten to see a lot of; most RPGs tend to stick with the Space or High Fantasy theme. The Colonial theme was a fresh change and fun to experiment with. If you enjoyed RPGs like Mass Effect, Dragon Age, etc, you're likely to enjoy this one. If you've never played an RPG before or haven't played one were your choices influence the game as a whole; I would suggest finding another better RPG to play first....my suggestions being Mass Effect or Star Wars Knights of the Old Republic.

The Good:
*The music is solid and could/would listen to it on its own. It's very dark brooding atmospheric stuff.
*Player customization felt like we actually got to make the person we wanted and it made sense within the world. This applies to not only physical characteristics but also how one chose to portray them....ie evil, neutral, good.
*The Skill/ability trees were very easy to understand and use upon level ups.

The Bad:
*The second half feels underwhelming compared to the first half. I don't know if it got rushed, if it got away from developers, or what, but it becomes less fun and/interesting as the game went along.
*Issues with skipping quest lines. I made the decision to skip certain side quests, but then would get caught up in them due to talking to the quest giver for a different reason. Basically I would finish the quest I wanted to do and then it would go into dialogue for the other quest without me initiating it.
*The inconsistency of when it would let you jump automatically from one spot to another mid quest. Sometimes it would give you the ability to go ahead and wait in an area or go to the next area in the middle of a quest. But other times it would make you travel through the game when all you're doing is going back and forth between two points.
*The team campfire was overall annoying more than helpful. Anytime you switch zones on the map it takes you to a campfire area where you can adjust your team, buy/sell, craft, etc. This would have been useful if we had only been able to do those things in a limited number of spots but each zone has at least 2 to 3 and so it quickly becomes pick destination, load camp area, quickly leave camp area, and wait for actual zone to load.
*Several locations have basically the same map/layout. This wouldn't have been to bad, if the exteriors of these places were similar but the developers made each of these areas very unique only to drop the ball on the interior.

It's not a bad RPG by any means its just not a great one either. I'd rank it 6.5 out of 10 and would suggest looking for it on sale. This review comes without trying the DLC; if I get it as some point I might edit if it makes a difference in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
