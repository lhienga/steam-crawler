That isn't to say there are no great ideas in the game. I did play for 6 hours (at the time of writing) since there seemed to be fairly competent general narrative, but at the same time there lies the biggest issues of the game: "how the story is told".

So the elephant in the room, the game is tedious and totally in love with FedEx quests. Most quests have you ping-pong between 2-4 npc just delivering what they were saying to the other npc or finding out that the princess is in a another castle and that again you must go and speak to different npc. Maybe the game develops to be better at later point, but really 6 hours of FedEx was enough for me.

The game just feels like poor mans Dragon Age: Inquisition, and most people would be better off playing that game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes. I would strongly recommend this game, even though there are things that could have been done better.
As I currently progressed into "mild" obsession with this game, if you like good story and RPG where you can actually see that each of your choices and action matters and you are not deterred by my below listed issues I encountered, you should go for this one.
It will be quite long rant. But in overall this game left strong impression in me, and in overall, even though there are faults and space for improvement, it is positive impression. Therefore, I had to write proper review. Pity there is not CZ localisation for PS because I would already forced my brother to play this :D


What I loved:
+ The word and animation is beautiful.

+ You can customize character (maybe not very heavily but you can).

+ Story is beautiful, rich and engaging. You really get the feeling your actions and choices affects it even as you go. Not some far away ending.

+ Side quests actually have strong relation to the main story. You will not have the feeling of repentance or that they are pointless. They are not about fetch this and bring it there, as I encountered in some games. But they usually serve to make you better understand what is happening on background of the story and to develop relation with your companions or factions. As I progressed because of the side-quests I could see how the pieces are clicking together much better. And they also impact the main story in some ways.

+ Characters you interact with are great even if you will hate some of them deeply :D

+ Cut-scenes that takes in account your character look, your equipment and companions that you picked to go with you. I usually appreciate if I have the freedom for example to look around during cut-scenes, which is not possible here, but I found out I dont mind the slightest. Plus there are some dialogs or observations triggered outside cut-scenes, where you can move.

+ I really liked that the companions offer sometimes their insights or comments on the ongoing situation. My favourite stays Vasco: You are now under stern eye of God of Light. I hope that your underwear is clean. And I even found it rather nice, how they serve as indicators of situation during battle. When you are low on health or armor is down etc. As I am now re-playing on extreme difficulty and also hid all exploration and battle interface, its rather useful :D

Issues
Let's now go with the negative aspects of this game. I was rather annoyed at the very beginning be following 2 things but they stopped bothering me:
- you have no way to rebind keyboard/mouse because right-mouse is default blinded to not only parry but also to "cancel". So if you want to rebind anything else to right-mouse, you have no chance as you will always cancel. But once I got used to the binding, its fine.
- you will probably find NPCs that are not story relevant rather annoying. At the beginning I was not able to decide if I was more annoyed when they were standing there and doing nothing or when they were aimless wandering back and forth. Especially if they tell you, when you try to interact, that they really really need to hurry somewhere and dont have time for you. It also resulted into most boring brothel in history of (at least my) gaming :D But to be absolutely honest after first 2 hours, where I attempted talking to everybody, you just start ignoring them and they fall into background. And I realized I don't care. You might still notice later in the absolutely uneventful brothel, or when you visit the inn where they usually span on table for me and stand there :D But whatever... if the cook wants to stand on table, its not my problem. She will probably not cook much :D or the guys in palace that will mop the floor even if there is dead guy laying 10 centimetres away from him without any care in the word. But you know how servants are :D

But probably the biggest problem for me in the end were completely copied interiors and their layouts. Come on. There were not that many buildings in the end for not to at least try to create them little bit differently from each other. But from this I got the feeling that there is single construction company for whole continent and Teer Fradee that builds only this muster house with following policy. "You want different wallpaper? You are out of luck. We can maybe do version with plain stone wall. You want your bed one meter to left? We said NO! You don't need the doors on left? Neverminded we can seal it off but the doors stay." That really bothered me. I am not sure if developers were running of time or budget or they simply got no patience left or got lazy, but it would be big step up if somebody though about this. At least the 3 main palaces could be different....come on..at least slightly....

My last point is totally minor and probably just my irritation that I missed it on my first run by exactly one sentence. It's about how romances work. Not to spoil anything vital, let's say that some characters for me showed the dialogs with little hearts on side which clearly indicated that could lead to romance. BUT not for all characters. I am not sure if that is a bug or by design but even step away from romance with them (as I found out after my first play-through) nothing showed at that time, which made me believe that either they were not available to my character gender or that the romance is to come later in game. Well, it was apparently not the case and I missed the one I wanted by one freaking sentence. And it was pretty early in the game so I still expected that it might come. To me, it didnt make sense, because the sentence I picked in my opinion should not stop any romance progress. It sounded to me at the time like tentative opening. But no other chance ever came. Which is also my second point to this romance thing. It's quite pity that some romances lock-up early in game without chance to do anything about it. And you will not even notice it happened sometimes, if you do not read manual before. I avoid manuals as I do not wish story spoiled and in RPG I usually stick to my choices. I pretty much understand that if you offend the character during dialogue they are not interested. That's fine. But come on. Some chance to initiate something later would be great if you didnt kill it completely with them. (Also there could be little bit more reflection of the relationship after its established but that is not that big of issue. Its just me being picky.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall is a surprisingly well-crafted game that will definitely satisfy the Action/RPG crowd out there. It has an engaging and winding story that definitely is worth exploring. While only a AA-quality game made by a minor dev team/publisher, you can really tell they had a love and passion for Greedfall they made! I'll quickly list some of the things I enjoyed or did not enjoy about the game:

PROS:
  • Story telling.
  • World building.
  • Environments and set pieces.
  • RPG elements, while simple compared to others, were satisfying enough to make me want to experiment between the 3 main archetypes and the 3 additional sub-types.
  • Pseudo-open world exploration. Not completely linear, but not completely open either. You are, however, rewarded for exploring the various sections of the island for treasure and gear.
  • Companions were all engaging, had depth, and I ended up becoming invested in all their stories.
  • The islander's language is completely unique and was made for this game.
  • Reputation system with factions and companions.
  • With the exception of boss/mini-bosses, fights are quick violent affairs that can be ended in a matter of seconds with the right combo of moves/skills. This lends itself to a feeling more grounded and "realistic-ish".

NEUTRAL:
  • Came for the combat, stayed for the story and worldbuilding.

CONS:
  • Combat is a little too easy. If you want any sort of challenge at all, I highly recommend you start at Hard and work your way up from there. Once you hit around level 20 you start to feel like an unstoppable killing machine. I ended my completionist playthrough at level 40 and ended up with tons of excess skill points I had no use for.
  • Questing involves a LOT of backtracking. Thankfully there are lots of fast travel points, but these only make it tolerable instead of downright annoying.
  • Final act in the game feels rushed. The build up through the whole game is slow and methodical, in a good way, then BOOM it's all over just like that?
  • There needed to be more consequences to the choices you make during main and side quests. Yes there is a rep system, but they could have leaned into it more.
  • The cities and villages felt barren. Yes there were NPCs living there and there's a day/night cycle that changes things slightly but I felt they really could have done more to make these locations feel alive.

MY PERSONAL RATING: 7.5/10
Anything above a 6/10 in my personal rating is worth buying, however I did wait for Greedall (and its DLC) to go on sale before making the plunge. I was surprised at how much I ended up enjoying this game. I do recommend picking up its 2 DLC as they add some great early-game QoL items, a whole new area of the island to explore, and 6 new unique weapons.\t\t\t\t\t
