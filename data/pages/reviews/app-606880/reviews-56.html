\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall. Where do I begin with Greedfall. This is one of those games that people either love or hate. It does some things incredibly well and some very poorly. This game is in a fantasy New World setting (the term used to describe the Americas during the early 16th century when it was first discovered by Europeans).

This is a deep character driven RPG where dialogue plays a big part and being involved in the story is important as every side quest is very much connected to the whole story.

Pros

- Very detailed fantasy story with extremely developed characters all tied nicely to the bigger picture.
- Some of the best side quests I have played since the Witcher.
- Excellent voice acting. Maybe the best part of this game.
- Several factions and lot of flexibility in which ones to side with and how each choice affects the overall relationship with them.

Cons

- Animations are bad. Lip syncing is off, just looks janky.
- Lot of issues which can be put down to being developed by a small team like repeated interiors, empty town centres, npcs repeating the same 3 lines of dialogue every time they see you etc.
- Enemy AI is pretty lacklustre as well. You have to sometimes walk right up to them to "activate" them and then they start attacking you.

So did i like it ?
Yes, very much but I am well aware that this would not be most people's cup of tea. The best way I can describe is imagine watching a movie made in South Africa or something. Its in English, the accents are a little different and it may feel limited from a technical stand point but they do things differently. And you like it. That's how i felt with this game.

The setting plays a big part, the great voice acting, the in-depth quest design and the unusual fantasy setting all contribute to this. Just don't play this if you are going in for the combat or if you don't like listening to lengthy dialogue with a lot of political intrigue.

You are a diplomat and dealing with multiple different factions and managing your relationships with all of them is the main part of this game.

I had pre-ordered this game and no lifed it when it came out. All the 40 hours or so you see here were at launch inside a week after release. I haven't done this since for any other game.

Its hard for me to describe this game properly. Watching a bit of gameplay will help greatly.
