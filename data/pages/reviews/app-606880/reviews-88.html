\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3.5/5. Plot summary: More trees, less a*hats.

This is a conversational RPG heavy game, and it gets a 3.5 from me due to the imbalance with the combat portion of the game, the requirement to take certain abilities if you want more conversation options, and the fact that those ability points are spread too many levels apart. The romance also feels tacked on, rather than a natural part of the game. Otherwise, the plot is good, the companions are good, the world is good. It's likely an alternate reality of the Celts with the continental Europeans - French, Spanish, Middle Easterners - trying to take over the island. I liked the integration of the side quests to the main quests, so you didn't feel like you were missing anything, and your actions did have an impact on things.

Overall, this game is better than middle of the road, but less than stellar. If you like RPGs, you'll likely like this one. If you're more into combat games with a RP aspect (e.g. AC, FC), this one lacks.\t\t\t\t\t

With that being said, it probably my first 'Con' . . Once you finish the game, you won't be able to walk around the world anymore..

Now, onto the rest;
- I am a big fan of RPG's, and I must admit it took me some time to get used to the combat system and the pause option, but once I got the hang of it, it's really great.
- The characters you play with and interact with are absolutely amazing, you really feel like you live in that game world and get actually attached to some of the NPC's.
- You get to play with a team of friends and they all have unique skills and also give you unique dialogue choices and outcomes..
- Small dialogue choices can have big impacts on the game, which I absolutely loved..
- There are some flaws in the game; but knowing it's such a small studio that developed it, it did not bother me at all.. Honestly I've had more fun playing this game than any other RPG I've played the past couple years.

I reaaaaallly recommend this game and I hope there will be a sequel, this was just an amazing experience.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's pretty, which is what sold it to me, (actually I watched a playthrough on Youtube), but it feels half finished. NPCs, if they're not walking or sweeping often just stand there. The Coinguard brothels are especially amusing. The voice actors are all talking about how naughty the joint is, but everyone is just standing. Not standing close together, not sitting on some of the throw pillows, DEFINITELY not bow-chika-wow-wow behind some curtains. Nope. The tawdry delights are just... standing and staring. They don't even walk around.

The environment is nice, but cramped, built a lot like a maze... Want to go to that thing right there? Nope, you can't jump three feet down, or you can walk all the way around it before you get to what you can see right now. Sometimes the walkmesh itself is bad. Want to sneak between those two trees and avoid a monster, or how about walk up that little corner of slope? No way. Sorry, you need to be funneled into that fight right there.

The characters are interesting, but it's really obvious who the bad guys are; they're the arrogant tw@t-w@ffles WHO WON'T SHUT UP about how great they are. Thank God for the escape, or randomly, the backspace key. There really needs to be a 'shoot this jackass in the face just so I won't have to listen to his arrogant twaddle' button.

Cool thing, you can romance any number of characters, but Siora has to wait for half the game to be over before you get more than 'hi, how ya doin', never mind all the natives you save, or how many quests you do for the local chiefs.

Combat... urgh. The weakest part of the chain. I do like that the 'shift' key lets you do any number of things, but inventory doesn't. Kind of realistic, I mean fights don't stop when you're rummaging in your pockets. I DON'T like that I can spam the potions, or hotkeys and then I have to wait... and wait... and wait for my toon to decide 'oh, you want me to drink a potion now'. The potions and powers should all be labelled the 'I'm going to dink around and get hit once or twice more before X happens'.

But it's really pretty. And obviously made for a controller, not a keyboard.

Oh and here's a funny thing, since I changed my review to a 'thumbs down' it must suddenly be reviewed...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, quick story...
When I initially purchased this game I only played 30 min of it and I did not even leave the starting area. I just could not get hooked, but that was my fault because I was playing too much fallout and skipping dialogue (Z spam is very fun). Essentially I was shooting myself in the foot by skipping dialogue in a story rich RPG and getting frustrated that I was not getting hooked. Why? Because I am a potato.
Fast Forward a year later and I download the game again, and my god. I am an idiot. The fact that I ever put this down is evident of that. This is one of the truest ROLE-playing games I have ever played. You actually pick and play a ROLE, you refine that ROLE (or you die like me because I am indecisive and want to be everything), and your actions/inaction has consequences. As well and not thinking or listening(^^) will affect how this game plays out. I did not want this game to end. This game has some flaws, and can seem slow at times, but what game does not (plz dont fight me)? This is an amazing game and brings back some nostalgia for me as a longtime fan of the Fable series (*Checks notes* Fable 3 release date: October 2010, 12 years ago at the time of this review)
All in all, if you are looking for a RPG in an interesting setting, with compelling characters/factions; look no further.

I AM DE SARDET, LEGATE OF THE MERCHANT CONGREGATION, AND YOU WILL PLAY THIS GAME!!!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, and foremost, I want to give a huge thank you to the entire team that worked on this project. I can't even imagine how it all began and what happened along the way. I haven't delved into any news reports, I can only speak of my experience in game. It was such an ambitious project and while it had some rough edges, overall I was constantly impressed. It's a little sad that in these times people forget to add how beautiful the environments are, but this is one game quielty pushing the limits and expectations so much more than it deserves to. I would compare it favourablly alongside and even above games like Mass effect and Fallout in terms of graphics in the landscape.

As for the main gameplay system? Sorry to the devs, but it is a mixed bag. Its has some really great features, shackled by some really bad ones. You can make all manner of potions traps and 1 off effects in game, but it alays stops and break your out of combat to use them. On it's own that's not a bad thing. Fallout made VATS and did this thing so very well. This is and isn't VATS. You do pause, and use your things, but in this case it completely breaks the flow. VATS never wasted time, never gave you time. You failed vats, you might as well never use it. You fail this, you can still lay 5 traps and relocate out of the boss range.

As much as I doubt this will ever reach the devs, if it does just know, I'm a gamer, and I really REALLY want you to win this battle. You doing well makes my experience as a gamer so much better. I know there are many toxic troll online, and I just want to say I'm not that. I really do appreciate what is there now, but I hope I can give you some ideas on how to streamline this for the next episode, which I really hope is in development.

Also yes, I might have good ideas, in one area, but if that breaks the game in other areas well not even I would push that change. I respect how difficult the role is and I'm only here to offer free feedback, and I'm sure many others would too, so it's not just a pool of one offering suggestions.


So in the end, Great game, solid team, buy it if this is your theme/ 10\t\t\t\t\t
