* Lovely scenery.

These shortcuts that have been taken really bring the game down and it's actually quite sad.

If you're going to buy it, buy it on sale and if you find yourself frustrated by the obvious skips where it was rushed, stop. It doesn't get better.\t\t\t\t\t

===========================================

Embarking on this journey, I was immediately drawn in by a unique and captivating scenario, one brimming with intriguing characters and a well-crafted narrative. The awesome synchronization of the English language further enhanced the experience.

However, despite the promising start, I couldn't shake the feeling of disappointment as I delved deeper into the gameplay mechanics. The repetitive nature of the tasks and activities quickly became apparent, casting a shadow over the otherwise enchanting world and engaging plotline. It pains me to admit that even with my best efforts, I found myself growing bored and disengaged.

Ultimately, the strength of the initial world-building and the compelling narrative couldn't quite compensate for the mediocrity of the gameplay. While the story's potential was evident, the gameplay failed to match its allure, resulting in an overall average experience.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNothing revolutionary here, but I really enjoyed the colonial theme and setting. I could feel a lot of Dragon Age inspiration here in the way the world and the lore is set up. Narrative wise, the story takes a while to really kick in, as they spend a good chunk of time flushing out the factions and introducing characters. The side-quests are mostly optional, but they do actually impact the outcome of some of the main plot points due to the karma system in place for both companions and factions as a whole.

Voice acting is pretty good, albeit sometimes the lip-sync can be a bit off. The combat takes some getting used too but after a couple hours you get the hang of it. The main gripe here is the lack of enemy variations. You'll mostly be killing the same types of creatures (some new textures, but using the same harness) and of course other humans. They threw in some bosses that have some lite mechanics but are mostly tied to the main story. The skill trees are nothing special but give you a decent variety of options in combat.

All in all, a decent narrative driven rpg that takes place in a lost colonial world that's full of political strife and conflict. If you have some downtime and are looking for something to enjoy and relax with, this could be something to check out. I'd recommend catching it on sale if you can.

7/10\t\t\t\t\t

Here's how the quest is designed:
Talk to quest giver > walk to fast travel point halfway across the map > Talk to Person A > Walk halfway across the map to talk to Person B and C > Go back to Person A and talk > Walk to fast travel point halfway across the map > combat/ persuasion check FINALLY > walk to fast travel point halfway across the map and talk to quest giver BUT sometimes the game forces you to wait for a day first before completing the quest then talk again to the quest giver.

My suggestion the developers: Keep the quest design simple.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am having troubles to decide whether Greedfall is “the best door-opening simulator of 2019” or rather “the best backtracking simulator of 2019”.
Jokes aside, Greedfall is a neat game. That is if you are interested in an RPG of course. The combat system is decently elaborate, the world is well crafted, and the main story promises to be intriguing. However, if there is one thing Greedfall has for it more than anything else than it is “potential”. Despite doing a lot of things right, there are some other things Greedfall simply cannot exceed at. To name a few, dialogues need a bit more attention and some severe cases of backtracking and “copy past” are in dire need of a fix. Simply put, some polish here or there is just missing. Overall though Greedfall is a good game which I can recommend to RPG fans. And since Greedfall 2 is announced already, I am very much looking forward to its release and if they managed to make use of the aforementioned "potential". ;)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tit was a fun 40 hours journey for me. i really love the setting, instead of medieval fantasy it is more of age of discovery\\renaissance fantasy, which is very rare. if you are a fan of rapiers, flintlock pistols and muskets as well as fancy clothing and feathered hats, this is a game for you.

main character being a legate, diplomat is also a very interesting decision, considering the setting. you get your intriguies, deception and conspiracy on every corner.

combat is ok, i can't say its very good nor that its very bad. i played only with one class and focused on one handed combat, pistols and traps, so can't say if experience differs for other builds. traps are clunky and most of the time i was not using them, but, the trap build lets you unlock explosive potions, alchemical coatings and bombs later on, which are quite impactful if you are careful with stocking up consumables required for them to work

gear is lacking and gear system kinda puts me off. unofrtunately fully upgraded purple sword you purchase from merchant is much better choice than unique gear you find by completing quests, exploration, building relationship with factions. i was especially disappointed after completing De Vespe DLC, as gear i recieved was crap compared to one i had on my character and that it can't be upgraded at all

there's a bit of backtracking here and there and sometimes it felt out of place. one particular was more annoying than other. imagine going to archive to get a document, then show it to npc, then talking to another npc, the going back to previous npc so they send you back to archive and cycle repeats. but most of the time its not that bad. there wasn't a time when i felt like "no, i'm done" despite this

all in all, i enjoyed my time in this game, hence recommending it. its not a witcher 3, but its not bad either. you could say mediocre, as long as you don't consider it a bad word =)\t\t\t\t\t
