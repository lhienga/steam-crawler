\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe phrase "Champagne taste, lemonade money" springs to mind when describing the developer Spiders.
There ideas far outweigh there budget and Greedfall is no exception.

POSITIVES
* Voice acting (although the Irish sounding accents for the natives was bizarre)

* An intriguing story which touches on subjects not often covered in video games

* A great levelling system with great crafting and RPG principles

NEGATIVES
* A very blend main character with even blender companions

* Whist good in design at times the visual's are re-used numerous times (Many interiors are completely identical)

* There is alot of walking from point to point with not much happening in-between

* A lack of NPC's in most area's

* Very lacking in enemy variety

* No mini map, this game needs it badly.

* A lot areas are inaccessible but appear to be the opposite (paths you can't walk down, also can't walk through bushes or at times jump down a level)

This isn't a bad game by any means and whilst I enjoyed the story there is too much mediocrity in between to want to continue playing.

Spiders are a very small group of developers and I admire again what they have created here but it's bogged down by a budget far lower then the ideas that Spiders have.

I wish a big developer would buy or support Spiders as an independent developer so they can finally bring the public a game that there imagination deserves.\t\t\t\t\t

Kiodin Score: D = Its OK

It does some nice stuff, but it fails on key points for me. So here it goes my Best 3 Worst 3 review, with a short conclusion in the end:

Best 3 (Pros)\n\n1 - The visuals: I like how detailed and beautiful the environment looks. Also, how the cities feels like real cities.\n\n2 - The setting: I like the idea of being a diplomat and dealing with politic issues between nations.\n\n3 - The combat: although a bit janky, its interesting and its well put with skills and perks. Even in a less is more approach, it gives the player some choices about gameplay styles. \n
Worst 3 (Cons)\n\n1 - Walking Simulator: Every open world game kind of suffers with this issue, but this is a special one. Unnecessary walking is just too much and it killed the experience to me.\n\n2 - Blandness: Everybody looks the same no matter the tribe or faction. The main character is too much a good boy sometimes as well to the point of being a naive empty shell.\n\n3 - Cringe moments and Incoherences: There are a lot of moments in movie sequences, dialogs and story resolution that just makes no sense or couldve been better written. \n
Conclusion

I think Spiders is on the right path and Im happy there will be a sequel to this game. My exp with Greedfall 1 was so in the middle that I dont know if Im in to the next game. However, they are clearly on the right way. Maybe review the scope and add some life quality upgrades (maybe an overhaul) to the exploration and the next game will reach its potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExtremely "safe" game in a bad way.

Gameplay-wise:
It's quite the generic for the RPG genre. Guns are OP though and half the game is power-tripping on better and better guns, then you see that awesome six-shooter revolver that takes the same to reload as a single shot pistol, you purchase it from the merchant, you power trip hard, and then combat starts to grow stale.

Story-wise:
Being a pre-metal civilization of hunter-gatherers with an average lifespan 30, child mortality rate of 70% and literacy rate of 5%: Good.
Being an enlightenment civilization that lives in brick and mortar houses, likes to trade, starts to build water sanitation, schools, universities, ocean going ships, can work with a multitude of metals and alloys... etc: Bad.
Basically noble savage trope, with a big case of white and black solutions. There's usually a grey solution but it's just a not so good white or not so bad black.

The worst possible native you encounter is the only native open to trade with the colonies and colonial influence. He is arguably the worst person in the game since every other single villain you encounter is just misguided or became a villain out desperation and shortsightedness.

The Jesus stand-in character didn't rise to heaven, it took to the sea, arrived on the island and became propaganda for the noble savage trope.

Also you always have a good, and bad choice, maybe a middle of the road one. But there's no problem (given you pick the good choice) you leave feeling like maybe you did not do the right thing, there's always one better choice.

Basically a strong case of White savior, mixed with black and white problem resolution.
Which kinda makes a ludo-narrative dissonance, with the theme of noble savage and ecofriendly=good, because you are are fixing half the problems by giving bad people, bad animals or bad monsters, strong cases of high-speed lead poisoning. You do that with your gun made of metals from a mine, forged by burning fossil fuels, spreading lead around, propelled by more minerals from mines.

Extreme plot summary: The world has magic-spaceAIDS-scurvy, you go to the new world to find a cure, the only "lemon juice" to cure it is living an eco-friendly life of hunter gatherers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pleasantly surprised by this game. It is obvious the devs were on a strict budget, so there are many areas of the game that would deserve more work, but it offers a bit of everything I want from an RPG, although some features are mediocre at best.

The fantasy setting of early colonial era is interesting. I was sceptical about it at first, but I'm glad I was proven wrong.
Playing primarily as a diplomat was unusual and welcome. I mostly liked the dialogues, the choices seemed meaningful and I liked the story overall.
The autumn environment of the island is beautiful and overall quality of the graphics is good enough. The game also ran very stable (no crashes).
I liked the armour and weapon aesthetics and their customisation thru crafting was simple but nice.
Combat system was not great, but not terrible either. You can use any combination of melee, magic and guns/alchemy. Not very in-depth and can be somewhat repetitive, but I enjoyed it nevertheless.
Character progression has many skills, attributes and talents to choose from and picking the most useful ones at the beginning was a bit tricky, but once I figured it out, it was OK.

Building interiors were copypasted a lot and I'd like more interaction with NPCs, this is where the game felt most "cheap".
Romances were also meh - you have to select a correct dialogue option at each occasion which is too punishing and the outcome is not very rewarding. Use a guide or a mod.

Last but not least, the game suffers from an unnecessary immersion breaker in the form of forced diversity of characters. For example, the island natives having the same ethnic "mix" as the colonists simply doesn't make sense.

Anyway... in short, if you like games like Dragon Age, play this. It won't blow your mind, but it is a solid RPG.\t\t\t\t\t
