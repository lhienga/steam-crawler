This was more than worth it.
But exactly a few dollars more.

Here is a pretty decent timeline of what happened while i played GreedFall... So I start out frustrated with the combat and how much random talking there is. The voices are good and accents are great but way too much talkie, talkie. Basically your cousin is sick and want to help him out with the rona cure. So you search far and wide for it but you can't get it unless you help people along the way. So you will barely get your Medieval RPG fix out of this one if that is what you are looking for. Which is really tough because as you play the game you realize that there could be some pretty drastic changes that would turn this game into one of the most popular games in the grossly overpopulated arena. I mean how often is there a medieval RPG with magic? Never? No. Every month, every week, day... I digress, but what makes them special IMO is how gritty the game is and why you are fighting somebody. For example they go into the whole brothel, chambermaid thing and do this like G rated version of it. But I am like slicing people open with a sword just to get to the front door. So... Why are there just pillows on the ground and an earring to show there that was once a woman there? There should be nude women everywhere, there should be spilled wine, there should be magic substances, I mean you could just like really make this little scene worth it and its just like a fumble. Go hard in the paint. F it.

Because the mission I really cared about had a short cut scene, and very direct speech, and I will tell you it really grabbed me and I was liking that a lot.

The crafting, the upgrades, the weapons, the talent tree, the bombs , the guns visually and the guns magazine(details are smart.), the big swords, the big hammers visually, and the teammates magic was top notch. I especially enjoyed when my teammates would die and I would feel like I need to save us all. Oh. I really enjoyed when I first killed those steroid-wolves. Then the steroid-bats. But then I figured the strategy and wanted more. I like that I drop too many points in something I didn't really want like a ring talent spec and I have to reset with the crystal because I want the most out of the build I am going for. But the apparel is like... What are you going for here? Pirate- Indian? Spanish Inquisitor-cowboy? I hate to be mean, I really do... But also you had all those people read all those lines and you have to know, NOBODY is going to listen to that. Its gotta be condensed. How did you even organize that?

Anyway get the game, write a review so there will be a dlc or GreedFall 2 where the devs fix everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeurd tír to mad!

It's not perfect and some of the dialogue options go in very, very unexpected ways. But the quest design, the environment design, the worldbuilding... they're absolutely astounding. The way something introduced as a tutorial fetch quest in old Serene winds up coming back again and again in both the Egon and Treason questlines is nothing short of a master class in making even the small things feel worth a player's time. And the choice to base the Islander cultures on proto-Celtic influences instead of the more common tropes of Native American or African cultures was a great way to address colonialism without making a pastiche of those still living with it's systemic ramifications in such a real way as to make the whole thing unpalatable.

That said, there are harsh consequences you're not flagged about if you delay a character's quests too long, or exacting needles to thread in certain cases - especially Siora's romance - that are hard to land without consulting a guide. I think Spiders went a little far into the 'don't handhold' and made some things feel practically obtuse.

On balance, though, Greedfall is a surprising hit for me. A game that navigates colonialism without being a train wreck is worth merit on it's own, but add in some really exceptional writing, some engrossing story, and beautiful, just beautiful, landscapes to get lost in and you get something that punches well above a relatively small studio's weight.

And we can't forget that it's got some legendarily memeable lines: "Things are about to get dicey!" and "Are your boots worn through?!" are favorites among my community in particular. And once I started to stream the game, I got fascinated enough with Yecht Fridi that I've tried to learn the conlang as best as I can.

Kwa awelam seg.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI beat this game a few weeks ago. I'm gonna say that it was okay. Your decisions do have an impact and there is a ton of world building with some pretty cliche character designs, but some good interactions here and there.

Pros:
-World Building
-A good plot twist
-Interesting equipment crafting bonuses
-Multiple Endings/Choices have impact

Cons:
-Way...Way too much back and forth travelling. You will spend the majority of your time running from place to place with small unimportant reoccurring encounters.
-Relations with companions did not seem to have an impact on the end of the story like it did in say Mass Effect 2.
-If you have the wrong skills you miss out on loot, and you cannot build a party to fill gaps.
-Companions are all forced on you at the start, but at least they have good side quests.
-Poor equipment diversity

Notes:
-I used the same equipment the whole game I found a hat at the start that gave +1 intuition and that was the greatest piece of equipment I found. I wore a standard blue breastplate and the same necklace the whole game.
-There is stealth and multiple paths to completing side quests but these seem to peter off towards the end. The first few side quests i did had multiple different ideas including some I came up with by myself that actually worked.

TLDR: Its a pretty stock RPG with good world design, but kinda just poor execution. Its okay.\t\t\t\t\t
