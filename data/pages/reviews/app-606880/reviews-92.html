TL;DR & Other info:
-\tI got this game on a sale (AU$18)
-\tPlayed on a mid-high rig PC
- Have not played the DLC...yet
-\tIf you enjoyed the Dragon Age trilogy you’ll definitely love this!

Pros
-\tBeautiful visuals and atmosphere
-\tGood animations in both cutscenes and in-game
    \t
  • Contributes to higher engagement level and aesthetics of the game
-\tA wide variety of unique fauna and bosses
-\tSubtle and small amount of loading times
-\tEvery area has a unique design
-\tAmazing and beautiful character creation
  • However, would be nice to have a wider range of skin tones
-\tCharacters
    \t
  • Short but informative dialogues
  • Amazing performance from all voice actors
  • Companions and (interactable) NPCs feels like they have lives outside the player’s control
– Absolutely love this!
    \t
  • Unique personalities
  • Enjoyable companion quests
-\tChoices matter
    \t
  • The game rewards players where it’s due
  • Consequences are equally given
  • There’s not a moment where a quest felt unfair, linear, or simple
  • Completed quests are mentioned and impacts both story and other side quests
  • Can completely avoid a fight to continue a quest
-\tMultiple endings/outcomes
    \t
  • Albeit slides like Mass Effect
  • Satisfying endings no matter which ending you choose
-\tBittersweet ending
-\tCombat is not perfect but if you play it without opting for the dominant strategy of guns and magic then you would find it a great challenge where you use items and well-timed dodges and parries to win an encounter
-\tModerate-High difficulty

Cons
-\tDominant strategy: Guns and/or magic
-\t2 choices for ending (but can vary depending on your stance with different factions and relationship with your companions)
-\tFiller NPCs lacking animations (simply standing on one spot, not a big problem)
-\tRooms/interiors are reused in the main 3 cities and native huts
-\tNo companion-companion banter/interaction
    \t
  • Only quest interaction (i.e. Kurt commenting on/about Siora’s quest, etc.)
-\tUn-equippable hats
    \t
  • Great at times, but sometimes it’d be nice to see De Sardet’s beautiful face.
-\tOnly 1 Expansion DLC, this game is fantastic, I’d like to see more content!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I really enjoyed my experience playing this game. There are a number of positive aspects to it as well as some negative ones;

Postives
  • The setting and lore is great, something different from generic medieval-inspired fantasy
  • Many of the characters are well designed and have interesting backstories/personalities
  • Many of the player choices throughout the game are well done and do come back to bite you later
  • The armour designs and customisation is rather good
  • I like the difficulty levels; some people like the challenge, some just want to enjoy the story

Negatives
  • The back-and-forth during some quests can get tiring and annoying rather quickly
  • The world can seem very empty despite its size - e.g. no reason really to explore
  • Although the lore is great, I feel as though i've only scratched the surface of it
  • The companions, although well written, don't get much time to shine outside of their own quests
  • I would of liked the endings to have more of an impact depending on your choices Like being able to save Constantin at the end under certain conditions
  • The fighting mechanic isn't really my cup of tea

I will definitely play Greedfall again, although some of its shortcomings put me off playing it again so soon after finishing it. I would say that it needed more varied side quests, more reasons for players to explore the world and perhaps more varied endings depending on your choices through the game. For what it's worth though, it is a brilliant game for people who enjoy this sort of choice-heavy, customisable Protag RPG games. I hope the continue to create similar games in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
