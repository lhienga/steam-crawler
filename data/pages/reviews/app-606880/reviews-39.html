\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so there's a stipulation in getting this game. MODS!!!
I played this game for some time now and what I realized is it's a jogging simulator with lots of long slow dialogue with some relatively lazy voice acting. I found myself running around the world that seems to be the same look with no changes to biomes or looks. Furthermore, I found myself skipping most of what people were saying and just reading the subtitles quickly. Also, combat is not necessarily challenging either. What I really recommend when getting this game is modding it from nexus mods with a few things that will make your life easier.

Arena Loot (Allows you to loot the bodies in the Arena before leaving)
Better Compass (Adds more compass markers so you can traverse the environment easier)
Vibrant Reshade (Makes the world WAY better to look at and removes the nasty brown filter on everything)
Increase Party Size (Allows for all your characters to travel with you which may seem like a cheat but makes conversations so much more interesting when everyone inputs their opinions, also makes combat look super cool with everyone working together)
Map Anywhere (Makes fast travel way more bearable and removes a lot of the aimless running around)
New Camera (Gives you an over the shoulder look and makes exploring feel better)
Sheathe (Makes your weapons appear on your belt which makes your character look cooler)

Also, place the game on extreme mode as this makes your leveling and progression feel more satisfying and lore friendly as you go from some snot nosed emissary to an experienced explorer. Plus, having 6 characters battling all at once makes all other modes feel way too easy.

Hope this helps!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUndoubtedly one of the worst games I ever played. Usually a bad game is just not worth even bothering to write a review, but this one very much deserves it.
Starting from all the instantly obvious things – game looks, plays and feels like it was made a decade ago. It has it all: poop filter, “open” world full of invisible walls, hilarious mechanics and face animations.
Diving a little deeper you’ll discover, that this game up to ~70% consists of just running around. After talking to person A, you’ll run to person B to inform them about person A’s opinion, and then back to person A – because why not? In one of the quests it had to be done 6 (!!) times, with absolutely nothing else done in between, not to mention persons A and B were on different maps – traveling between which, I forgot to say, has 2 separate loading screens. Why? Because why not!
On your jogging simulator journey you’ll occasionally meet copy-paste filler enemies with hilarious design and animations, placed randomly with no purpose, that look pretty much like a bad wolf from a children’s book.
And if you thought that only random enemies are copy-pasted – oh no, my friend, so are the “bosses”, and some of them seem to be pasted directly from a certain other (actually good) game.
At this point I was playing with no other intention but to see what else this game can screw up – and, lo and behold, a DLC I purchased could not be accessed in-game, a bug well known for a very long time that developer has done absolutely nothing about.
Right now you must be asking yourself – but is there anything good to say about this game? And yes, there is! You see, I bought Greedfall and Steelrising (another game from this developer) at the same time, and played Steelrising (game released after Greedfall) first. During the Steelrising playthrough I was 100% sure that game is an embodiment of clunk, poor optimization and bad game design. But Greedfall is much, MUCH worse, so in a certain sense, the developers are moving in a right direction. At this pace, in 10 years they could make a decent game.
To sum it all up – do not regret buying and playing through it. It will definitely make me appreciate actually good games more. So if you are extremely bored and have absolutely nothing else to do…. No, I still can’t recommend this one ;)

P.S. OK, everything I said above still stands, but I'm changing the review to recommended, cause for whatever reason I miss playing it... No way I'll do another playthrough, but still...\t\t\t\t\t

All the positive reviews are spot on. Great storytelling, fun combat although it does become predictable. Frame rates are great, little to no stuttering, just purchased the DLC's, can't wait to finish the game.

This game gives the player a feel of what happens when greed, religion and politics combine
to destroy third world (natives) people for all their resources. So many world powers throughout history have used religion or some other BS reason to kill native peoples to get their resources. And that is where this game excels. The story or narrative should be taught to children worldwide, about what not to do to people in third-world countries or countries rich in resources.

I hope the second game stays with the same theme or a similar idea. To say I am looking forward to Greedfall 2 is an understatement.\t\t\t\t\t
