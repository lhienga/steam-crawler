\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very interesting, and I like the setting. The premise is quite unlike any I've ever played featuring elements of the Witcher, Assasin's Creed and maybe even a little bit of Bloodborne. I am surprised it was released by such a small studio, which is compensated for by such a deep story.

My one complaint is that immersion is difficult to engage in regarding different cultures/factions (not so much the natives) due to inconsistent character design. I appreciate inclusivity, but it is difficult to understand the political aspects of all the factions which are only distinguished by costume design (and tattoos) as opposed to demographical distinction. The issue falls short of expectation because, faction motives are not consistent throughout the story and conflicts are confusing. Often times it feels like the wrong choice is always made. Also, random npc interactions are extensive, almost to the point that they are gruelsome. At the same time, the main character's interactions with relevant supporting characters feels a tad lacking.

Overall, this game filled a niche for me after the Witcher 3 and I'd recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
