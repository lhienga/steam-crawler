\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo steam recommended this game to me and I said no because I literally thought it was a pirate ship game. Rediscovered it through an article recommending games that were similar to Witcher 3...and to my surprise, I had given it a thumbs down! I said what the heck, it's on sale, if I hate it, no biggie.

That being said...I LOVE this game. Main story is great. Side quests feel meatier than just go retrieve this item for this person. Combat is intuitive and easy to master - of course I played a ranged mage, so I might be a little biased. Great twists. Definitely worth a second or third play through depending on who you want to romance/which quests you want to change your choices with/your combat style. Kinda bummed you can't get all of the achievements in just one or two playthroughs since some of them are dependent on your choices that you can't change later. I wish there were a few more side quests to do, but I guess fewer substantial quests are better than numerous wimpy ones.

Only issues I've come across are minor things: most of the town buildings (taverns, governor halls, major landmarks) are just the same structure recolored. Doesn't hurt the story, but kind of a bummer not to see a little more creativity with world building. I get that in a certain time period, some buildings DID actually look the same, but not every aristocrat would want the same floor plan if they have boatloads of money.

Another bigger issue I had that was a little annoying but not game breaking: your companions will not prompt you to talk to them like in many other games (Fallout comes to mind), so you have to keep checking with them every so often to see if there's any other dialogue options for romancing or side quests. And if there's nothing new, you can keep asking them the same thing over and over with no new responses.

Once you romance a companion, there's not much that changes other than they give you a pet name and you can sleep with them ONE TIME in a 5 second cutscene that is way more implied than anything steamy. I guess I was expecting closer to Witcher 3 with the several options of women you can pick and choose from and have various fun romps with. No biggie. I just wish there was a little more immersion in how your new love interacts with you?

I do really like that depending on what companions you have with you, they will have dialogue options and the availability to allow them to interject on your behalf and possible save you charisma/intuition points. And there's a few different combinations of companions that would make each quest or playthrough slightly more interesting depending on who you have with you! My personal favorite is Vasco (who I obviously romanced) + Petrus. I wish you could have a party of 4 when certain companions are required to be in your party for quests as I am very much a multitasker and sometimes want the best of both worlds + an extra person that's hanging around but not participating in combat. This happens a handful of times in game for certain quests, but it's not an option normally.

Some characters are misgendered in dialogue and in captions - there were several times I was talking to a Naut and they were referred to as he/him/a man when it was a woman. Also since you can choose your gender for your main character De Sardet, some quest dialogue refers to you by default as a man/he/him even if you chose female.

Also if you change your game settings or options and forget to save, as soon as your character dies in combat and you load the last available save, you will revert back to your old settings which is kind of frustrating. I think there might be a bug on caption sizes because I changed the text larger on accident and then reverted back to the normal small option, and I got half small and half giant.

If you want to look up guides on certain quests, there isn't a lot to be found on the internet, unlike some popular games (Skyrim, Fallout, etc). Maybe because it's only a few years old? Still, it was nice to see that most guides that you can find would NOT spoil the story at all, but would say things like "do 1, 2, and 3 and then you will have a conversation with X character to further the story". I'm all for spoilers when I'm looking for them but it was nice to find no accidental ones.

Overall a 8.5/10. Would be a 9-9.5 if they were a little less...lazy? on some game development things to keep the immersive experience going. But maybe that's not what they were going for? Anyway. Solid work from Spiders!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game overall.

What I really like is how some of the quests can be completed in a variety of ways. And the order you do quests can also have an impact. Combat is good too with a souls-super-lite touch. Technique skill tree is God Mode. You can always animation cancel with dodge which makes combat relatively easier than other games that have souls-like style. What I don't like is that 1/4th of the quests are a bunch of "go-here-talk-to-this-NPC" quests. It gets pretty obnoxious.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started to play this game a couple of years ago and then stopped, as I had played as a mage and this felt quite boring. After finishing another RPG, I decided to give it another try and started from the beginning. Overall, I liked the game, despite its flaws. The characters, albeit representing fighter stereotypes, were interesting and the romance was compelling.
I general, I liked the story... at least up until the moment where a major character of the game became the main antagonist. But despite the game trying to explain this change also by letting the antagonist speak, I felt that this plot twist was weird and at times foreseeable and still out of place.
Combat did not always run smooth as the main character was at time a bit clumsy, The companions, however, were quite good at supporting the main character in combat.
The graphics were nice and the maps, despite being different, separate, smaller maps, were interesting to explore.
To me, the most interesting built was the technical one, since as a mage you can't rely on too many spells. I did not try the warrior yet, but in general I play either as a mage or as a rogue/technical character.
Altogether I liked the game and found it entertaining.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWe really should on insist on having 10 minute videos of a game actually being played on Steam. I could have seen how awkward and clunky movement is, how even ladders are so needlessly basic, that you can dance around with a sword but cant walk across a 6' long beam without first spending a skill point that you only get once per 4-5 levels (someone apparently thought balance was a really cool thing to emphasize), that combat is limited and rather basic, that there seem to be endless fetch quests, that you're bizarrely limited by one 6' tall wall, but not others, that closely guarded warehouses can be entered by simply using a side door...
This is a game I might have been slightly impressed with around 2005, but certainly not 2019. I would say it comes across as imbalanced in that a lot of effort was put forth on plot and backstories etc, but not nearly enough on mechanics, so immersion and enjoyment suffer. In short, I'd say nice try, but a swing and a miss.\t\t\t\t\t

-- broken dashmove (often moving in wrong directions)
-- broken autotarget (your charakter can only autotarget/autoaim however magic projectiles will often be shot into nowhere in hectic fights)
