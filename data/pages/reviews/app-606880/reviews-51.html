\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd say GreedFall is a 6.5/10 - It's clear a lot of effort was put into the development of the game, but some easily avoidable design flaws caused it to miss it's mark. I enjoyed the unique story and setting, the combat system was enjoyable, and the graphics, art design, and voice acting were especially good. I didn't play this game when it first came out because I'm not personally interested in 17th century settings, but this turned out to be one of my favorite aspects of the game (flintlock pistols were very cool!)

My main gripe with the game was that questing requires a ~lot~ of mindless back and forth travel over complex, tunnel-like maps with very limited fast travel. I probably spent a third or more of my play time travelling between already visited objectives. I get the impression this was an intentional (bad) design choice to artificially extend play time. The looting system became another headache - no group loot option, and a confirm dialog opens for almost every looted body. Not fun after looting thousands of corpses! (This would have been an extremely simple thing to fix, and on PC there is a mod that does so.)

Itemization, crafting, and economy all felt a bit like afterthoughts and didn't contribute much to the experience. I'd also say the game doesn't offer much replay value unless you just want to try different combat styles. Overall, worth playing, but has some rather annoying design flaws.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly the game is solidly okay.

It's a bit rough around the edges, there's a lack of polish in certain areas (like, for instance, characters/text calling you "he" and "sir" when you're playing a female character), the way fast travel works is frustrating from time to time, and there's just this feeling of wanting more.

I was really wanting for some more lore, maybe more things to find when running around (the world in general feels pretty empty... the environments themselves look really great). I especially wanted more companion dialogue/banter/interactions overall.

BUT I think the game has a lot of heart and the developers clearly cared about what they were making and that shined through most of all to me. For how small the studio seems to be from watching the credits, I think it's impressive what they've managed with the game.

I personally enjoyed it overall. I'd definitely recommend it, but probably only on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
