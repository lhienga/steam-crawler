\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
1. Visuals - the game boasts some nice scenery and the Victorian Era setting is beautifully done. There is room for improvement, of course, but then again - where isn't?

2. Story - it's quite not bad. Unexpected events and plot twists are always a nice surprise, and this game has some. A disclaimer - I didn't finish the story, but the part of it I saw was very much to my liking.

3. Equipment improvements - I liked the gradations and the effects of the imporvements overall, but see cons.

4. Quests - many people complain that the quests are mainly go there and talk to someone. I want to remind such people that you play as a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ diplomat - it is therefore your ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ job to go and speak! But enough of that - overall, the quests fit well within what the character does and the storyline. Some quests have timers that are ticking and compel you to do what is required or else.. see cons.

Cons:
1. Gameplay - the evasion and block/parry + three types of attacks seem novel at first, until you realize that it's ALL there is the game has to offer in terms of melee combat. In no time does this game become a simple sequence of evade + Q or LMB + evade + Q. Magic is practically non-existent - there are essentially 3 spells + the magic ring and that's it! Think about - no fireballs, no cool spells, no weapon enchantment, no combat with staffs, no robes, no nothing. 3 spells + a ring. Period.

2. Characters - they are bleak and uninteresting, the main hero most of all. The protege displays very few characteristics of a leader in conversations (no idea why companions choose to follow him/her, but they do. Maybe that's because they are as colorless as the main char), their positions as regards different matters are very often badly explained. Worst of all, the player has very little control over most of the decisions the character does, which leads to even lower understanding of what drives them. Having dialog options that display different attitude at least (akin to Paragon/Renegade options in Mass Effect) could have improved things considerably, but alas..

3. Quests:
a) Some quests have no clearly-defined time frame. You need to go there and save someone oooor you can choose to wait a year "hyperbolizing, I know", and only then go and save someone. And it matters not if that someone has a band of thugs in their house - they'll wait, too!
b) you have very little (if any) choice over how well to perform quests.. What if I don't want to be a diplomat but a thug who twists necks rather than his tongue to get things done?
c) the game is linear. I'll explain - if you have 3 main quests, you ofttimes need to complete all three in order to progress further. Choosing a path is not an option, not from what I saw at least.

4. Crafting - some customization is present, true, but the materials required to craft something are always the same! And, to make matters worse, you can't improve the actual edge of the blade/axe / head of a hammer. I mean, seriously?

5. My personal favorite - no swearing! Idk about you, but I refuse to take an RPG seriously if people don't swear in it. The main char's "shipload of trouble" finished me

Overall, it's a game with a decent story that has poorly written characters and bleak gameplay. Not recommended\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game, though I don't love it. The story is good, and the characters were likable, but there is something missing to really draw you in. Character customisation is great and being able to easily switch your 'build' is a nice touch.

Most of the game works with semi-open areas, which I generally like better than open world games. That being said, in a lot of cases, I didn't really feel rewarded for exploring, since a lot of area's are rather empty, aside from enemies. What I liked least is that the game provides you with fast travel points, which pretty quickly leads to you just running from fast travel point to fast travel point.

That said, I did feel like the game really encourages you to think diplomatically and in my opinion choices did feel like they actually mattered. Overall I had a great time playing this game, so I would definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't get me wrong, even though I'm giving this a 'NO' it was still generally a good game, and If I could give it a neutral thumb I 100% would as it had an equal balance of good and bad that makes me just want to say "Try it yourself"

For me, it started to wear out its welcome about 30 to 40 hours into the game when the excessive and unnecessary amount of running around began to get annoying, I payed less attention to exposition and story/narrative, the native accent made me cringe the more i listened to it and the whole thing just turned into a bit of a repetitive chore that kept giving more just as you hoped it would end. The combat was probably its strongest point mechanically and gave you plenty of options and many chances to reset your skills to try something new if you so wished to.

Aesthetically the game was stunning, not a masterpiece but there was definitely some love put into it, from the locations to the design of armour and weapons as well as combat animations. The setting was fantastic, we don't often get 17th century-styled fantasy games (or I just don't pay enough attention to see them). I wasn't a fan of the map layouts though and the towns were real ass. I came into the game with the expectation it would be one big open world map but it was actually quite limited, though there were many locations to visit, they mostly looked the same or recycled for the most part.

There were definitely elements of the game that weren't given ENOUGH to make them matter, like the faction specific armours which at first was a nice touch when you could wear a factions armour to walk around their restricted areas without risking reputation, but it very quickly becomes apparent that this mechanic wasn't properly utilised to its full potential, and even then for what it was used for, seemed kind of pointless and just extra fluff to make it appear like there's more to it than there actually is. I probably only had to use it twice in the entire play through, but it was forced and you don't get anything special.

The DLC was short and somewhat disappointing unfortunately, its like 30 mins to an hour of content that i guess was suppose to add some intrigue and make it feel like it was going to throw a spanner in the works by undoing all your efforts to forge alliances but it was extremely underwhelming in the end. There was an effort...but if you're going to throw in a political villain its gotta be strong and destructive instead of a limp and lifeless filler.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember hearing about GreedFall when it was releasing in 2019. There was interest, but I kept passing on the game as I didn't know what kind of game it was. Fast forward to 2022 where I found it on Gamepass. Played it a bit, and ended up buying it during the recent summer sale as the game had been taken off of GP. Really glad I finally picked it up.

GreedFall takes place largely on the fantastical island of Teer Fradee where you take control of the main character De Sardet. Through your abilities of diplomacy in a system similar to the Dragon Age and Mass Effect series, you'll explore the land to solve or cause problems for many different factions.

The game sports a very solid combat system that is truly built to be played as you see fight. There's enough points to freely develop your character however you see fit, and there's a lot of depth to go along with it. The reposte system is surprisingly responsive and can really help you turn the tide of battle. And when you unlock the roll, you're pretty much invincible until the final animation plays out. I went as a mainly focused short sword build and was able to deal a lot of damage very quickly.

Alongside combat, you have traits and attributes. The attributes allow you to equip heavier armor, higher tier weapons, or make your magic abilities better. The traits allow for making conversations go your way more easily with the charisma and intuition abilities, as well as being able to explore more areas via Vigor or being able to craft with Science and Craftsmanship. The only downside I found is that you get one point every four level. Though you are able to add a trait to your armor as well as getting a companion bonus from your allies, so it's not entirely necessary to max out every stat right away.

All in all, the game definitely has Bioware inspirations, but does enough of its own thing to carry an identity of its own. The world is very interesting, the companions are all well written and work well in the story, and the locales are extremely beautiful. The game is also a little janky with some areas that obviously needed better optimization and could've used a bit more proofreading, though none of these ever made the game unplayable. The only major complaint I had is the fact that you get a trait point every four levels and have to work around it, especially early in the game if you go Charisma like I did. This can also be a problem with the skills and attributes that basically have you focus your character in a specific combat path. But considering you sometimes will go long stretches without fighting and get plenty of materials as well as gear pieces from exploring or helping people out, there's never a problem of encounters being unfair. And if the combat is too much, the game has the Discovery mode, which makes all fights basically null and void.

Tl;dr I can definitely recommend this game to someone looking to sink a lot of time into a big pseudo-open world experience with a lot of choices that do ultimately matter on how your playthrough goes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall's setting is truly unique and the major selling point of the game. The decisions you make do feel meaningful and influence some big story moments, and you aren't provided too many opportunities to farm reputation points (except for one faction) so you could find yourself in a situation of wanting to give it a second playthrough to get a better relationship with some characters or factions. The initial mystery, setting, and some decent, but no outstanding, characters will make your time with the game enjoyable.

The game's biggest struggle is that it's very rough around all its edges. Combat is there and it's passable; nothing outstanding but definitely workable. You'll often get frustrated by certain enemies the first time you encounter them until you realize the only true strategy in this game is damage. Maximum damage downrange is the way to win every fight, don't try to be too cute or too strategic, it isn't that kind of game even if it wants to be. Dialogue and writing have a number of grammatical errors that don't stop the game from being comprehensible nor detract from some solid voice acting but there are going to be moments where people go from being him to her to their and things of that nature. The mystery and character are initially interesting but you'll be left thinking, "damn just a little more polish would have made so much of the game thrilling and compelling".

The developer has consistently improved between all of their other games at each step, and with Greedfall being their most successful to date and now scheduled for a sequel, it'll be very exciting to see all of those improvements brought to such a fantastic setting in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
