\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt broke me.

I initially bought this Game, because I really enjoyed playing Dragon Age and I read some Reviews that if I liked those Games, I will enjoy this one too.
Fair to say, I've been lied to. So much, that I'm writing a Review.

I hate to leave Games unfinished, so maybe, some time I will push through this but right now I simply can't. After it already taking me 20 hours of playing to actually start enjoying the Game and getting engaged in the Story, while almost slamming my face into my table everytime I heard the words "legate of the congregation".

Now 40 hours in, I was hoping to bring this to an end, but I shall keep being proven wrong.
While I do have to give Credit where Credit is due, there so far have been no side quests that didn't give more insight into the lore and story, but then again there also have been no side quests that didn't make me run from A to B over and over. 30 of those 40 hours might have just been me running form place to place fighting the same 3 types of monsters over and over and it's sad, really. Especially when everytime I think I'm making Progress 5 new Quests pop up, that make me feel like having to do everything all over again.

The Game has so much potential, the Worldbuilding is beautiful, your Companions actually feel important to you (or not as much, looking at you Petrus), even if the romance mechanic is terribely done,
the whole "magic Island, magic beasts and unknown Gods" idea couldve really played out well, but it just isn't.

I'm no longer suprised about the small amount of players having actually received the "late" game achievments.

I might go ahead and see what I can get out of this Game by modding it. We'll see\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally liked this game. I did not play all the side quests yet, although most of them and I recommend playing them before the main quest, because there is a point of no return and the game ends after you finish the main quest. Beautiful environment. captive story.

Missed being able to jump. you can climb though but it's not the same. I did not like navigating, because it's different from other games I like to play like Fallout 4, Skyrim, or Witcher 3 and there were many places I could not reach or pass, while in my opinion that should have been possible.

But I really liked what they did with the camps and instead of loading screen you can be at a camp space and talk to companions, upgrade armor, weapons, sell, buy or create potions etc. This makes it more immersive and useful.

Some quests got a little twisted, because I was roaming the lands and got in places that for example activated the second part quest in the journal, while the first had not even started yet. This was no game-breaker though, because by taking on another first and than retrying the first one, did start the quest as it should.
Many role-playing/ character development options, which make me want to play the game again. And there are a few mods for the game. I say a few, because it's not nearly as much as there are for Witcher 3, or fallout 4 for example.
The grammar police would have a field day however, because there are a lot of he/she mistakes and other errors in the subtitles and fewer in the vocals. I saw there's a mod for that ;-)
But with all that said... Yes I would definitely recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

I see lot's of people not recommending this game because of the gameplay, and how it was mostly walking and slow dialogue. While I do agree that there were many points in which the dialogue was buggy, and or slow, it was still a good overall game. I say that if you like RPGs and story-rich games get this. That being said I don't think it would be a good idea to get this game while it is not on sale. For people who like story (like myself), I found it to be very lacking, especially toward the end. There was no real motivation for the character as a whole aside from being a diplomat. I do like the many companions and their stories in their specific side missions, but after their missions are over the characters never talk again unless it's for another main mission. I just never sympathized with any of them. As for the story, and more importantly the ending, instead of a cohesive narrative, it ended with you making a final choice and a narrator telling you how that choice affected the world over a slideshow of the different places you went with no sense of ambiguity. That is what I call Lazy Story Telling. \t\t\t\t\t
