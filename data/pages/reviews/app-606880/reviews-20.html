\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game, but there's always something that's just a little off at every stage I played.

For starters, the controls are bad and can't be remapped on a controller. You can use Steam Input if you want all your menu controls to be weird, but it's a pain in the ass for such a basic feature.

The worldbuilding is neat, basically feels like Mass Effect 1, except in the 18th century. Keep Mass Effect 1 in mind; this is a AA title and you'll see roughly equivalent production values throughout. Character customization is C Tier; not terrible, but leaves a lot to be desired. Body settings in particular are underwhelming. Also, for plot reasons, your character has a big, ugly birthmark on their face that can't be removed.

The RPG systems are kind of cumbersome. There's a lot of bean counting that feels like the game would be better without (stuff like managing how many musket balls and healing potions you're carrying). I couldn't find a way to respec, which is a strange choice in a game whose systems aren't particularly well described in-game. The item system is bad, using a Skyrim-style build-your-own-pause-menu that pulls against the Soulslite combat. Basically, the game can't decide if it wants to be a fast, action-heavy adventure or a slow, cerebral dungeon crawl.

The pacing is a wreck. The prologue is *painfully* slow, and the main game never gets over it's love traipsing back and forth without fast travel.

The writing is the game's high point. The world building explains everything quickly, and most of the characters felt well realized. But then the mechanics but in and knock you out of flow state.

All of this adds up. Every little problem is compounded by the others. And the result is a game that very rarely feels *good* without interrupting itself via some other unoptimized mechanic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good effort by the makers but it feels like they are very inexperienced in storytelling.
Harsh for the studio but I would not recommend buying this full price.
6/10

The game looks great and the music gives me the feeling i'm at Universal Studios Movie Park. But that's just not enough to call this a good game.
For instance, I do not understand the lay-out of the maps they made (these are visited by using an overview map). Why do I want to explore every corner of a map but do I end up being let down by the so called 'loot' in some chests you find? It's never worth it..
Besides that, every time I visit a new area I encounter so many places where I can not do anything. Seems like I have to return there when I get a quest. The order just doesn't make sense. I addition, while progressing through a quest there's a lot of backtracking just because you have to talk to a particular person.

Anyway, too much complaining about the choices that this studio made and I hope they learn from it for the sequel. I'd still recommend playing this beautiful game but my advice is to just focus on the main story.
Final note: I have not yet completed the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall is a RPG like Drakensang, it is child friendly, you cannot do really "evil" things, like slaughtering all the natives, like the conquistador Cortez did in the night of sorrows. It's a pity, but only a minor one. There are some more sad or violent scenes for example your mother is ill and will die and you enter a fresh battlefield where the mother of someone died, so this person breaks down in tears, but that's nothing a 12 years old couldn't handle, some of this themes are handled even in TV shows for six year olds like "Redwall". I do not understand the pegi 18 and FSK 16 rating. Maybe because the story is a little complex and involves many factions but there is no "grey moral", "bad" things are depicted as brutal and evil. There are no sex scenes it fades to black before actions start but you see kissing and hugging.
The story is very well written and even the minor quests are good, sometimes.
The world building is ok.

The gameplay is repetitive and not really hard. I chose the gunpowder path and my method (just shoot everything) proved successful. Ressources are respawning so you can grind yourself "unlimited" ammunition. The RPG aspect besides the story was not really in depth for me.

At the time this game was released there were better and more complex RPGs, I think that's the reason why many people complain about the "shallow" world in the negative reviews. I think you should give it a try.
I even liked the colonial setting, although it has a French touch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great title, following in the steps of BioWare brilliantly, if not without its flaws. Being the work of a small team, the game's minor bugs and gameplay frustrations can be excused, for a well-written story that does not overstay its welcome. The side quests were also very interesting in how they served to build the world and its lore.

Some of the negatives:
- Inconsistent quest design and automatic fast travelling before / after quests, with some quests exaggerating a little bit on the "fetching" and back-and-forth;
- Somewhat repetitive music in some areas, with only one track for that area playing every time you go through it;
- Too many loading screens in between fast travel locations;
- Frustrating fast travel system, which doesn't add that much to immersion, due to its "fast travel break" in the middle; would've been better to have a simple "open-map-and-click-location" fast travel system, and explain / incorporate it into the lore in some way instead;
- Somewhat empty world in some locations, with not enough merchants or blacksmiths;
- Not enough backstory and voice acting in companion characters, who don't react to most events in the game (the only exception being the DLC), and instead offer generic phrases every time you speak to them;
- Inconsistent main story escalation in the last third of the game, with not nearly enough time to actually let things develop and escalate to that point;
- Somewhat inconsistent motivations to some of the characters, especially in the last third of the game.

Some of the positives:
- Very interesting story overall, with mostly interesting and lore-enriching side quests;
- Very good DLC;
- Very interesting gameplay loop and RPG implementation;
- Very well-written soundtrack overall;
- Beautiful game, with mostly beautiful and well-designed locations, especially in the cities;
- Beautiful and somewhat varied armour pieces and weapons;
- Interesting creature designs;
- Game brings back a BioWare-inspired story-based RPG, which I think a lot of people miss, and it does it well, overall.\t\t\t\t\t
