\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was looking for a richer RPG experience during which your choices impact the course of the story, where you can befriend or antagonize your companions or even entire nations. I was recommended this game and I can attest that Greedfall offers this experience and it does it well. The story, worldbuilding and RPG elements made this game really enjoyable.

It is not without fault however, and the main one will become glaringly obvious a few hours in: you'll be walking. A lot. While there are options for fast travelling you'll still find yourself running from one place to the next a bit too often. The combat is OK. Not terrible, but not excellent either. It can stand on its own but may start to feel repetitive towards the end of the game.

Overall, I greatly enjoyed Greedfall and I will definitely be checking out the sequel which is currently in the works, though I do hope they will take the feedback here to heart.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying Greedfall is a frustrating experience. The landscape and towns appear wonderful and well crafted, with different architectural styles to suit each faction. Sadly this is only skin deep and once you look beyond the surface sheen, the shortcomings of the world become all too clear. The most immersion-breaking aspect of this is that the same models and layouts are used for buildings in different towns. It's as if Soviet planners have invaded the game and just re-used the same building templates hoping that no-one will notice. The animations used throughout the game are jerky and feel unnatural. Generally there is a lack of polish everywhere. With older games, where graphics were hardware limited, these sorts of issues were not as jarring. The animations, textures, poly counts, level design etc. were all at a similar quality level and fit together. With Greedfall there is a discrepancy in quality with respect to different elements, and as a result it does not fit well together. A missed opportunity in my opinion.

Is this redeemed by the gameplay? Hardly. There is nothing groundbreaking here. That doesn't mean it's bad, but merely acceptable. So many of the missions require you to run from one end of the map to the other, and even with fast travel options, there is a lot of running around from point A to point B. A complete waste of time.

Battles are a mixed bag. Some are a pushover, others feel almost impossible. One in particular near the end led to near rage-quitting as it was just too hard as the enemy was able to kill me in two moves. In contrast, the lack of balance was very evident as the final boss was far easier than this. I was expecting the hardest battle of the game where I would need to use every little trick I'd discovered, and it was practically over before it had started. Such an anti-climax.

The redeeming side of this mess: the story is interesting. It's certainly different to the conventional fantasy lore found in many RPGs, yet it weaves seamlessly into the world so that magic, firearms and melee can all co-exist in a logical manner. It feels natural. There is intrigue galore, and whilst I haven't tried different branching options that are available, it seems there are several ways that each quest, and indeed the main storyline, can be completed. There is no 'right' or 'best' choice and this means that when playing you can focus on the story and what you feel is the option you want to take, without worrying whether it is the most optimal choice that the game wants you to take. I found the main storyline was engaging and worth playing to the end. Thanks to the story I definitely got value out of the game.

If I had to choose between a well polished and good looking game with lacklustre story or this imperfectly presented gem of a world and its story, Greedfall would be my choice every time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a big RPG fan and will admit to being slightly conflicted over whether to recommend Greedfall or not.
I'll start with a pros and cons list (numbers in brackets link a pro to a con):

PROS:
- In general looks really nice (1) with some very atmospheric areas and lighting.
- In general the story is well written and NPCs/companions are well set up.
- Fully voice acted (2)
- A large world to explore (3)
- A big choice of weapons and armour (4)
- Lots of loot drops from human opponents/chests
- Pause to queue up skills in combat
- Lots of main story and side quests (5)
- Companion side quests to keep you busy for ages

CONS:
- (1) Can be quite a few pop ins even when being run on a very powerful rig off of an M.2 drive
- The native accent is horrible. It IS fully voice acted (2) but sounds like a person trying imitate someone with a strong Irish accent trying to do an Afrikaans accent. Horrendous.
- The 'large' world (3) is very linear most of the time.
- Waaay too many invisible walls/no go areas/bad scenery collision boxes. This can get very frustrating when you get stuck 2 feet to the left of a tree stump or can't drop down a foot tall ledge or jump up an even shorter ledge.
- Progress in stats/skills feels VERY slow, almost frustratingly slow. This means you may get a really nice weapon or bit of armour (4) from the massive assortment and have no realistic chance of ever equipping it because you only have 1 endurance and it needs 5 but you only get one stat point every 3rd level and you need to level up agility for some weapons, strength for others and accuracy for guns. This leaves you with lots of good but totally unuesable loot.
- The leaves and debris blowing around for no reason. I can understand dust etc in a space sim to give you a sense of movement but not here!
- The controls can feel a tad unresponsive at times, especially dodging
- Flakey companion AI can leave them standing there doing nothing in a fight or getting hung up on a rock half a mile back down the path.
- Quick travel only from camps/houses. THIS GETS OLD very FAST.
- Passing time only at camps/houses. So if you get a 'come back at night/tomorrow' in a quest then 9/10 times you will have to trudge back to another location to pass the time then trudge back again.
- Some very tedious quests (5) run to talk to A who is miles away from a fast travel point, they send you to B who is also quite a way from a fast travel point. They run you back to A who gets you to go and explore at C then you have to report back to A. Lots and lots AND LOTS for running the same route over and over.
- No mini map and an actual map that is barely passably as a helpful device.
- Door animations to hide are loading that are just plain annoying.
- Assassins creed running inertia....urgh.....

It may seem that i am ragging on Greedfall but I have 35 hours in so far so it must be doing something right. I will give it a thumbs up but with a caveat that you'd best get it on a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
