\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game and think it deserves more than 'Mostly Positive' reviews. Expecting this game to be like the likes of Witcher and Skyrim does the game a disservice as these two games are made by huge AAA studios, whereas GreedFall is developed by an A Tier French development studio, Spiders. GreedFall is more akin to the old Bioware games than anything else.

Story in this game is fantastic and hits all the classic RPG story notes that make them so great. Your companions are really different to one another making them unique, who you grow attached to over time. The factions on Teer Fradee are all really well crafted, with each one having their own power struggles over the other. Plus the struggle between the Natives and the people from other lands is written really well. The choices you make in the story do matter in the end and building your relationship with your teammates and factions really makes a difference.

The gameplay is also really well polished and Spiders has come a really long way since Bound By Flame or Technomancer. Combat is really well done with three different base play styles to mix together with other styles (I personally went the Technical route). Enemy variety is enough in my opinion to keep you interested over the 40 hour playtime.

Graphically the game is very pretty, the landscapes are really well made and the cities feel big and sprawling. The music in addition, although nothing too amazing, does a good job to match the atmosphere of their respective areas or factions. Combat music is pretty good too.

The game does have its flaws, side quests can be a bit long winded, conversation animations are limited and there is a lot of back tracking. But I think these are easily outshone by the games strengths like the RPG elements, combat and presentation.

So, if you're a fan of the old Bioware games or you're wanting to role-play as a Legate of the Merchant Congregation, then give it a go!

Overall I thought this game was great and I'm very glad I finished it. Very excited for the sequel!

TO MY HEALTH, AND DEATH TO THE OTHERS!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is so much I want to say about this game, I feel like I could write an entire novel about how this game made me feel. But I will try to keep it as brief as I can.

Gameplay is similar to Mass Effect, but the story... My god, the story absolutely drew me in, in a way that I did not expect.

I have tried on two other occasions to pick up this game, only ever just getting off the boat onto Teer Fradee. But the moment I met one of the natives, I realized this game had a lot more to offer than the tutorial segment had let on.

The side quests are very engaging and dramatic, and I developed a genuine connection with all of the squad members. I honestly did not expect to love this game as much as I did. The setting is unique, mysterious, and beautiful. I found myself more immersed in this game than I have with any other in years.

While this game was obviously created on a budget, the story absolutely shines through all of the jank. I never encountered any game-breaking bugs or glitches, and the only true complaint I had about the game was enemy variety, which is honestly quite good compared to a lot of games today.

The developers' passion and creativity really shined through on GreedFall, and that creativity and passion is something sorely needed in these times. More studios should strive to tell stories like Spiders was able to tell here. Thank you so much for this game. Truly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Fantastic Game and great RPG with flaws.

The best part, by far, is the writing. Top notch quality all around. You absolutely can fulfill the fantasy of being a Renaissance era Diplomat in a colonial situation.

The Flaws are as follows:
-Some quests are tedious. A little to much going to point A to point B.
-The Combat is inconsequential. Like many games of this type (wishing to be a conversation simulator) they would be far better served emulating Disco Elysium rather than Dragon Age. Set the game to Easy and just breeze through the combat to get to the good stuff.
-There is some AA jank in the cameras and controls
-The Design of the game means there is really only one optimal way to play. Max Charisma and Intuition, maybe a bit of science later.
-This is a game where the rule is absolutely positively do all the sidequests first, as soon as you get them.
-You can ONLY fulfill the fantasy of being a Diplomat in the Renaissance era. Don't try to be anything else. This is Star Trek, not Star Wars.

If you go into this game with the above in mind, you'll have an amazing time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor accessibility reasons, this game was unplayable.

The text is absolutely tiny and only the dialogue captions can be made larger. But, when increasing the size of the captions, the letters themselves get bigger, but the spacing doesn't adjust accordingly so the letters and words just get squished together (sometimes to the point of overlap).
Not only that, but with many of the letters and documents the character can find, there is very little contrast between the small text and the busy background of the 'paper' which makes it even more difficult.

It's really hard to play and enjoy a game, especially a story-driven game, when you can't get the important information.

(if you have no issues with small text, feel free to ignore this review)\t\t\t\t\t
