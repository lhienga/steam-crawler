\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is alright. It has a good story, characters, good voice acting and dialogue.

However it can feel like a boring tedious slog at times, the quests are annoying as there is so much back tracking and facial animations are terrible, their faces look so stiff.

Even though the world building is good the game follows a narrow linear path, it doesn't feel truly open world which is quite barren and empty, a lot of the areas look the same boring, nothing feels exciting or worth trying to explore as it's just empty and you aren't faced with making any hard drastic choices. You get minor choices such as to spare someone or kill them.
The game doesn't have much depth.

It has quite a lot of errors in dialogue of mixing up him and her, she and he even in the same sentence.
e.g. I was talking to a male NPC as part of a quest and the dialogue to select says: ask her about what he has done since crossing.

Combat is nothing special, rather boring just spam left mouse button a couple of times, dodge and repeat till dead.
In combat companions repeatedly say the same phrases continuously way too much, which is annoying and make the characters seem more like machines and less like real people. Just saying a phrase once in battle would be more realistic or to have much more variety in what they say.

This game is no where near a fantastic game, it doesn't do anything amazing or epic, only does most things sufficiently but not excellently.

I'd rate it 6/10 ok. Needs a lot of improvement to be a good game. I don't hate the game nor do I love it.
I recommend only on sale and if you value story over polish and game play as despite the games short comings I enjoyed following the story and doing most quests.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomeone had the genius idea to make a world that is mostly shaded brown and penciled in a boring plot about exploration and colonialism. There is some mystique in the beginning, but the boring color/design makes it quite clear that the experience will be dull. Crunch past that, and you'll find a lot of writing and dialog but most of it is just serviceable. It doesn't help that the combat is also bland. Even when you don't mind the appearance or designs, you'll start seeing similarity in mechanics. Customization and RPG elements are also, somehow, boring. This might be because gaining items and customizing them doesn't feel special or has meaningful variety and appearance. Combat makes this feel worse when the classes barely feel much different from one another. Sure, one uses magic. One uses guns. One uses two-handed weapons. It all looks fine and pretty on paper until you experience about an hour of the overall combat and find out it is pretty undercooked and doesn't really feel impactful.

With pure will, you could tough through the flaws and also experience an empty RPG system in a brown/gray muddied world of nature's horror creatures, customize yourself in design in historical European inspiration that hardly changes as you play which stays too similar and basic to make new designs enjoyable, and experience the retelling of Pocahontas with Monsters to unravel a story which is slow, unexciting, and predictable. You just need to spend more than half your time walking through woods stuck in the time of an Autumn's dusk.

If you are looking for a game of this genre, I can't see how one could find enjoyment where almost every aspect and feature of the game could be summarized with "Ok" unless your list of ARPGs is starting to dwindle and this happens to be on sale. Most good games (Especially RPGs) are enjoyed because one or a couple of aspects are considered strong and make the other "Not great" aspects fine enough to ignore. Greedfall's biggest downside is that it does not have a strong point anywhere in sight.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an extremely good rpg which wins a lot on worldbuilding, lore and immersion. A late '700 pike and shot world is extremely rare to find in videogames and here is extremely well done from the details in building, dresses, speak and everything around you.
I would put it in the immersive sim genre, even if you can't interact as much as I wanted with the world. The immersion is granted from the beginning, the adventure setup "let's go explore and conquer a newly colonized island" is fantastic as the passage and immersion into a new world, which is the most important part of the game and its promise.
If you really like long walks in the woods, boy you are in for a treat. The game is not short, it's about 40 to 50 hours, the story is long and convoluted. Maybe too much in some parts, the ending is really a struggle, not painful but they could have done better.
This is not a AAA game from a big studio, it's a AA eurojank and it delivers extremely well. Combat is simple but it has a lot of customization as well as the rpg part, even if you play a pre-determined character.
There is of course the "jank" part which are animations, voice acting and a general "man I wish they did this part better" but it's the price to pay if you know the genre. Also equip and items have a ton of stats and the game requires a bit of farming around for crafting and everyday maintenance. Its not a problem here but it would have been way better with a better customization system. Stealth is awful, they should have never put it in the first place, it's so useless, not customizable (you dont'have stealth abilities to upgrade or items) and easily avoidable.
There is also a memory leak problem with videocards with less than 8gb. I have a gtx960 and I could run everything at ultra but if I put ultra on textures you have the typical memory leak stuttering. Just reporting the issue.
But here the merits overwhelm the flaws.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI once had a long and detailed review here. No point.

Spiders is an indie dev company so please adjust your expectations accordingly, and the game is relatively cheap. With that being said... I still think the game sucks. Combat is boring not to mention a lack of interesting perks or talents, lack of an open world means very limited battle spaces and that brings a whole slew of problems of it's own like mobs randomly going from 5% to 100% while your resources stay low from the "last" fight you were in. The story was not impressive and Spiders shouldn't have gone with a "best ending" because it's been severely detrimental to the dialogue and character development.

I keep hearing Spiders compared to Bioware or Bethesda. They are not even close... but again, Indie devs need to be afforded some breathing room.

I do not recommend this game. Greedfall 2 is in development and they've used the "open world" tag. Let's see if it actually is open world. Would be nice if I never had to visit that camp (just a hidden loading screen) ever again. Make the world interesting. Give me reasons to explore because in this game there's none. This game was a let down for me... but I can see why many might like it. I still would not recommend. I'd rather load up Mass Effect LE and try a Renegade run.

In my opinion, the thing that would make Greedfall 2 an actual GOOD game? Passionate devs. Maybe Spiders is passionate but they need to show it. They have great ideas but they never flesh out all the details! DETAILS! Like how a book in Skyrim could lead you to an unmarked location where you find a "scene" that has environmental story-telling which tells an extremely sad story requiring you to pickpocket a key off an innkeep to open a cell that's connected to the main story or maybe even a companion arc.

I hope Greedfall 2 doesn't make playing through 1 a necessity. I don't think I'll be buying any games made by Spiders anymore but I'll be following this sequel because I'm interested in where the devs go from this pile here.\t\t\t\t\t
