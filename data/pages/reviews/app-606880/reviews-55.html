\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly... This game is pretty mid.
60% walking, 20% cutscenes, 20% combat.

It's a semi open world game. There will be times that you can't just move on and explore further because the game won't let you descend/climb certain ledges, trying to nudge you to go where it wants you to (in a not so discreet manner)

The fast travel is weird. Most of the time you have to walk to the end of the map (marked with a fast travel symbol) Which just points back to the fact that 60% of this game is walking.
Around the map there are some fireplaces scattered around that enable fast travel too, you have to find them and uncover them first. Which ofc leads to walking around.
The world feels pretty empty, which I guess leads me to being way too focused on the act of walking.

I first played this game in 2020 and pretty much dropped it after just 30 min of playtime. Something about the way the NPCs talk and convey the information turns me off, and makes me feel instantly bored. If you asked me what this game is about my anwser would be "I don't know... Something, something... Colony.. Monsters...magic...something...plague".

I'm the type of person to exhaust all the dialogue options in the games I play. Yet in this one It's like I don't care, let me just go and fight monsters.

The combat alone doesn't make this game worth picking up though, most people would call it bad. Some would call it souls-like (check the tags) For me it was fine (lower end of fine) , but if I wanted to play a game soley for combat I would play something else.

I'm not enjoying it so I can't reccomend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played it through, but only to see the ending. After about 20 hours, it was more a chore than fun.

The graphics are nice, the story is mostly very good, with different faction you work for, a lot of people you actually get to know. This could have made this a great game almost in the league of Witcher 3.

BUT: The missions themselves are often boring. Some missions only consist of Go to X. Talk to X, X cannot help you but sends you to Y, Go to Y. Y says, that he has seen something that might help you, so try to find Z. You run around, find Z, talk to them, and they say "Yeah, I think you need to go to W". And so on. (No, I'm not exaggerating...)
Some missions are confusing. For example, you are supposed to free some rpisoners from a mad doctor, but you don't have the key. You try to find it in a house, but you cannot enter it. Why? Because you first need to actually talk to the prisoners so that they can tell you to find the key, then suddenly the door is open. There are several annoying bugs like that.

And the talking to people is often really boring. Dialogue is slow, you often only have one dialogue option but still need to click it to fake action. And several of the options you actually have do not affect the game in the slightest.

And yeah, choices matter. What this mainly means is that you have to do all side missions, the more you do, the better the ending (especially the side missions for your companions). Apart from that, not much to choose, and most of it quite obvious.

The number of opponents is very limited, there are 6 or 7 types, not more, throughout the large map. So fights start to get boring at some point. Add to this that after some time you are not really in need of loot anymore - so I often evaded the fights or even when I finished them, I did not bother to pick up the loot.

The companion system (reminiscent of dragon age games) is supposed to make a difference (the game even says "chose your companions depending on their fighter archetype), but I never noticed a big difference in how difficult the fights were. Just choose the companion that works for the faction you are dealing with and that's about all you need to think about.

All in all, this could have been a great game if they had put as much care into the mechanics as they put into the story (which is godd IMO). But as it is, it is just a bit too repetitive and boring.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI guess this is what passes for a "good" RPG these days. Sigh. As a bit of an RPG fanatic myself, I'm convinced that developers have forgotten how to make good RPGs. Gone are the days of KOTOR, Dragon Age Origins, and Mass Effect. I read a review comparing this game to Bioware's masterpieces, even going as far as to say that it even exceeds them. BARF.

This game has all the tapestries of a "modern RPG"; a semi-open world; crafting; skill trees; ugly women; shameless social justice politics that hinder creativity; grey/green/blue/purple/gold tiered items; people with exclamation points on the top of their heads...here we go again. Here. We. Go. Again.
But on paper this should tickle my fancy. There's characters and companions, levels, loot to be found, and a good combat system. Even though its busy, it's a nice combat system and reminds me of Kingdoms of Amalur. (Unfortunately there are other things in this game that reminds me of Kingdoms of Amalur, and that's not a good thing). Then why am I finding myself quitting the game halfway through? I should be engrossed in this game and not posting a review....

Well, right off the bat something is wrong with this game. I could feel it, but I couldn't put my finger on it. And then the death knell for the RPG occurred; I began to skip through dialogue. Because I'm not invested in the world, the characters, the story. Of course I don't care, I have no idea what's happening, there's no stakes at play, and there are no characters for me to care about. Greedfall did not take the time to world build and build up a clear story. I don't really have much of an idea of what's going on other than I need to find a cure for this Black Plague disease.

What are the stakes?
Where is the cast of characters?
Who is the antagonist?

Come on people. This is writing 101. There's barely a cast in this game. We need supporting characters and they are nowhere to be found. Apparently all of the politics in your realm lies with your cousin, and that's it. It becomes even clearer that there are problems with the plot of this game with how quests are distributed. I find myself quickly running back and forth from one objective to the next, revisiting old areas over and over. The developers' excuse was that they wanted people to complete quests "at their own pace" and do it "when its convenient" but that's just an excuse. Part of the fun of a "story driven" RPG Is you know, the story. Players want to complete quests one at a time because they are engrossed in the plot of the quest. Asking players to juggle quests just points to lazy level and quest design and players will forget the plot of many quests - especially since the developers didnt even bother to include basic summaries of these numerous quests in the journal.

This is compounded on the fact that nearly all of the areas of the game look the same. They all begin to blend together as you run around like a madman talking to one native tribe to another. You're a diplomat after all. Sounds fun right? Not really. As the player I already have very little investment in the plot considering it's just a skeleton, but compound on the fact that I'm a diplomat and many of these quests deal with political situations. BORING. Where is the excitement? What stakes do I PERSONALLY have as De Sardet? Apparently nothing. Where are the antagonists? Are there any to be found?

Just remember, the natives NEVER DO ANYTHING WRONG. Understand? They are all nature loving hippies and the European-inspired powers are always the aggressors. They are very bad people because they come from a fantasy 16th century Europe and they're all very bad colonizers. Oh I get it. Let me guess, the real bad guys are GASP, YOU!? Is that it? The European powers are the bad guys? How predictable.

The relationships between characters and companions in this game; meaningless. I have no desire to get to know these people. They are boring because the world is boring. They have nothing to say and are just there to help you in combat. Why are my female companions ugly as sin? Is this more social justice nonsense? My god. This is awful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper fun, involving story, lots of sidequests, fully voiced. About 40hrs to clear all quests & dlc on extreme.

The talents are all useful and gate various exploration aspects, so while there's multiple ways around a problem, the talent skills never feel like they're wasted.
Combat's fun and rewards parrying.
I like that melee, magic, and ranged all feel viable, though ranged has ammunition cost and magic's kind of op. Hybrids seem to work well, since it gets expensive to spam magic and potions are costly (but can also be crafted).

Camera / targeting kind of annoying in tight corridors / when you're up against a wall, but at least you can fully pause in the tab/space tactical menu to manually select targets. Becomes less of an issue later when most of the combat takes place in open areas.

Maybe a bit too much running around for the sake of running around. Not sure what the point of timed quests is when some force you to campfire and wait, while others automatically cycle time forwards. Feels like it was added for minor flavour early on but then the devs realized how dumb it was in practice and got rid of it down the road... but also didn't redo the early quests.

Itemization's kind of weird. Items have quality rating and attribute requirements, and either come with sockets or without. Sockets can be filled with green (cheap materials), blue (uncommon), or purple (rare/pure) upgrades you forge at a blacksmith or craft if you've slotted the attribute (wasted point since it's a very minor gold cost to do it at a npc). Items both increase in strength with ascending quality (0 to 100) and with requirement (1-5 attr) - usually. It should reward specialization. There's a large power spike between 0 and 1, and then gradual improvements. The result of this is that 1-attribute weapons for some archetypes such as pistols and 1h axe/mace filled with purple upgs are basically competitive with endgame 100-qual legendary weapons because they don't have sockets, and while you do get 10% dmg per main attribute, the weapons are basically within 10-20 attack difference, so it just feels kind of like wet noodle upgrades in that the weapon you can upg at lvl 18 is basically BiS for the rest of the game. Of the legendary weapons, a disproportionate amount are 2h swords with similar stats - feels like wasted opportunity.

Tends to randomly crash every 5 or 6 hours, has some weird texture bugs too.

But all in all a really fun game and the story's good.\t\t\t\t\t
