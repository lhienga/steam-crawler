\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the quintessence of almost. Not quite AAA material, and not quite A...

You will find floating boxes over terrain, actors refer to you as the wrong sex, copy & paste locations and characters, horrifying texture optimization and memory leaks. (How i addressed that is at the bottom of this review)
The game has straight video game logic, this path is blocked by a branch and you cannot proceed. You need level 3 athletics to turn your body sideways to pass a crack in the wall that you could fit an elephant through. You need this ability to throw a vial.

But then you have some of the best voice acting i have ever heard, the missions are really meaningful, incredible vistas and sights to see, group party dynamics that are unique, and combat is pretty solid, the sound is fantastic. The sound when you step over pebbles is spot on.
The developers were right there at the cusp of making this AAA material, then i guess gave up?
A witcher competitor, but the witcher it is not.

This game starts off with more pleasantries then a courting ritual, very European aristocracy. Yes, yes, we have been complimenting for 5 minutes now, can we please stop stroking each other off.
Once you get through all of the gratitude and compliments storming at you like great winds of the dust bowl you finally get into what the game is about. Your a diplomat for a wealthy family and this game reminds you on a regular basis through dialogue and mission styling's. All very cleverly done. While some of the leaps in logic are astounding, they are overall sensible and rational and how you decide to handle paradigms feels like it truly matters. You see results right away, then again reminded of your actions later on.

I use some mods that make my playthrough more memorable. Its astonishing i can have all my teammates follow me around and they still are a part of the dialogue, provide feedback, and are part of the mission, yet this doesnt break the game somehow.

23 hours in, and i feel i have a long way to go still.
I am surprised at how invested i became in this story, its terribly good.
Overall i give this a 7/10, with some polish this could absolutely become a AAA title and a 9/10. But i think it may be abandoned from what i have read.

The optimizations in this game are very bad, there seems to be memory leaks and i scoured the internet looking for fixes.
I'd seen stutters and little to no effect from various resolutions, windowed mode, full screen, lowest graphics settings, disabled Exploit Protection, turned off Vsync and anything else I could think of or find.

After 23 hours of testing, the only thing that made a difference for me was setting the Dynamic Resolution slider all the way down (to clean up the results I use Re-shade).
Also, setting the Textures to Medium, let it load, then setting Textures back to High/Ultra helps with the stuttering. I can only venture a guess that it clears the cache. (Must be done each session, or when stutters occur. Doesn't really help with inventory stutters.)
These were the ONLY things that made my audio and game stop stuttering. (I still have max settings applied. The game still has rare stutters but its playable.)

My computer can run ARK near max settings yet struggles with this game even with these "fixes", I do hope Spiders or whomever comes back eventually to fix these memory leaks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7/10

I recommend buying this game when it's on sale. The combat system is sometimes a bit clunky but overall serviceable. The dialogue is pretty good and so is the story. The graphics are also decent, only the faces are sometimes a bit weird but that's something that's tolerable since it only affects minor characters. Another con is the constant walking from point A to B to A in some quests.

I suggest installing 3 mods from Nexus Mods:
- Better Compass, which enables you to see all accepted quests in the compass and switch between them easily
- Quick Loot, which lets you area loot with a button press instead of looting every enemy one after another. It also removes the weight from materials which makes inventory management much easier and less annoying
- Map Anywhere, which lets you fast travel from anywhere on the map instead of from only designated spots. This makes the constant walking a bit less annoying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile playing this game it's hard not to see the many choices these designers made as part of the reaction to Skyrim. I do not mean that as any kind of criticism, rather I rejoice at it's slightly experimental nature. It takes many of the things we expect from an RPG and does them reasonably well, if not very well. It's crafting is surprisingly deep while simultaneously being much smoother and less cumbersome than Skyrim's crafting. It's combat is interesting and very action oriented. While I would have some notes for the designers (how are magic rings that shoot magic blasts a melee weapon?!) I consider this game to be an interesting playthrough with multiple branching quest paths, making it likely to be a game I come back to quite a few times. The rough edges that might be seen are likely the result of this game simply being the first action RPG that this studio has produced and I very much look forward to others, in this series or others!\t\t\t\t\t
