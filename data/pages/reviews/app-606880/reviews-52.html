GOOD TYPE OF WOOD

I will start with this little advice - don't bother with Steam reviews about GreedFall.
I'm not sure what some people expected from AA game made by studio with other "wooden" games in their portfolio. If you bought this game for anything else than story and cool world setting, then that's fully on you. It's not that hard to do basic 5 minutes research about the product that you want to buy.
So, should you give this game a chance or not? It depends on what do you expect from RPG. Story over everything else, even if gameplay is not-so-great? Then GreedFall is just for you. In any other case, carefully read my full review below and decide yourself.
TLDR for the lazy - average game that excels in telling a good story.

WORLD
This may be hugely biased, but I loved the semi-open world of GreedFall. It was a great mix of new world colonialism and victorian era.
Fairly quickly we're introduced to the main factions that are ruling the world. All of them are heavily based on our real world (nobody expects the Spanish inquisition!).
You can favour some of them, but ultimately is rather easy to please everyone. You just need to be a good diplomat :)
Because of really well made location designs you could truly feel the difference between old (developed) world and "newly" colonized island. Honestly, it's impressive how good everything looks like despite lack of variety in models (give yourself a few hours and you'll see almost all models that the game has to offer). It wasn't a big problem for me, but I understand that it may bring some boredom after several hours of walking from place to place.
There's almost zero interactions with the world, outside of main/side quests. You can talk with some NPCs (few lines of dialogue), find hidden treasures or do the mini quests from notice boards. Besides that, there's nothing to do. I understand that budget was limited, but it shouldn't be that hard to create few in-game books with extra lore. These small things are needed for good worldbuilding.

STORY
First and foremost, if you don't want to be bored to death by gameplay (which is the biggest problem of many people) drop your stupid pride and play the game on easy difficulty. Steamroll all battles and enjoy the story.
And, oh boy, there's a lot to enjoy. It took me almost 30 hours to finish ALL quests (including DLC) and I enjoyed most of the content.
Main story may appear rather simple at start, but it keeps getting better and better with time. It's understandable why everything was designed that way. As a diplomat we need to slowly build connections with other factions, dig some dirt on governors, get favors. Overall, classic RPG experience. If you loved older Dragon Ages or Gothic series, you'll love the world of GreedFall.
After everything unfolds (or sometimes before) you can clearly see the chain reactions that caused certain events to happen. Companions loyality quests are connected with the main story, so remember to do these quests as soon as you can!
Speaking of companions... Such a wasted oppurtunity. Spider... guys... you had some excellent characters like Siora, with hundreds of commentary lines, but you can't create anything meaningful for them? Only one quest line for each person? Really? QUESTS
I decided to separate my opinion about story from my opinion about quests itself. Why?
In my opinion, problems with GreedFall being boring for so many people stems from severe lack of side content. Good story is a good thing, but you need something extra to achieve greatness. And like I said above - in GreedFall there's almost ZERO extra content! There's nothing else to do besides going from quest to quest.
So, no. I don't fully agree with other reviewers about boring/repetitive quests. There's some repetitiveness, but it's not caused solely by quests (unless we're talking about some screwed objectives, where you need to do something illogical to move the quest further; you can find a few instances of this).

GAMEPLAY
The biggest offender regarding the "boringness" of GreedFall. You can have the best story in the entire genre, but lack of interesting gameplay and lack of diversity may kill your game for many people.
I will start with the combat. By the design, it's not a bad system. It tries to mimic combat from The Witcher 3, but with higher variety of weapons. The problem is, that The Witcher 3 combat was already boring with that stupid dodge + spam lmb type of gameplay. Spiders studio tried to salvage some issues by adding armor mechanic. Basically, you need to destroy enemy's armor before you can touch his actual HP. Does it help with anything? Nope. It's too easy to deal with it with fury attacks (stronger attacks) or simply run-n-gun from afar.
Maybe it wouldn't that bad with higher variety in opponents. Hard to tell, because game is super limited in that area. There's only a few types of creatures, humans in different armors + with different weapons, and few variants of bosses.
There's also tactical pause (spacebar by default)! No idea why, but it exist.
Stealth is pointless. There's literally nothing to steal (because stealing mechanic doesn't exist) and AI is too dumb to make sneaking viable.
It's even worse when you need to escort or follow someone. NPCs are slower than you which is annoying. They will stop going if you're not near them. Not even talking about constant blocking on every barrel, rock or stump. Why someone thought that these quests are good idea? Everything needed to make them work is so damn wooden!
On the positive note - I really liked the character growth linked with location accessibility. If you have certain traits, you can find hidden places with treasures or different opportunities for you to finish your task.

VISUALS AND AUDIO
There's not much to tell about it. Game looks nice. Just nice. Nothing will blow your mind, but also nothing will actively hurt your eyes. Okay. I lied a little bit. Animations may hurt your eyes a little bit, especially when there are close-ups while something emotional happens on the screen.
I have no complaints about audio. Soundtrack is great and it fits the game world perfectly. Voice acting is also pretty good, especially language of tribes.

BUGS AND PERFORMANCE
Massive amount of small bugs and glitches. Clipping with objects, pathing problems, few instances of invisible enemies (mainly while crouching behind objects), "shaking" npcs while talking, sometimes enemies couldn't start their patrol routine (reload was enough to fix it), once I couldn't leave the dialogue option and alt+f4 was the only option to salvage this.
Performance is also lacking. It wasn't terrible, especially with lower settings, but you could feel huge FPS drops in several locations. Resting camp (location between teleports) was somehow the biggest offender. Drops from 100+ fps to 20/30? That's rough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMeh, you got better to do with your time.

It's a seemingly promising game in the tradition of a Piranha Bytes jank-RPG with lots of free-roaming and plant parts to farm.
True to janky games the pick up button and the dodge button are the same so you need to be exactly aligned, it's not exactly "snapping" to the loot either. So far I can follow. I even finished Elex (1).
The first few hours introduce a rich setting between new world colonialism and victorian plague which is delivered over reading dialogue rather than by playing. The main story will surely pick up some day but not in the first nine hours it seems.
Combat is kinda ok unless you get stunlocked/blocklocked and it's pretty basic all in all.
Lots of running around which is ok at first because the vista is pretty but it gets old regardless.
Quests are largely boring.
Aside from the first two areas the rest seem unfurnished like the grand city in the west. Lots of empty space to map out with some berries and samey monsters in between. Like some creation kit abandoned mod.
UI is a mess of bad design choices and makes Skyrim's look good.
It's like Gothic meets the Witcher but bad, wasted opportunity.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere are some other titles / subtitles that are a bit more illuminating than the current one: "Divinity Lite," "Skyrim Mobile Version," "Uncomfortable Fantasy Trope Simulator.'

This is at best an amusing rpg with a tried-and-true plot that recycles some of the most popular elements and mechanics of other famous rpgs into its own construction.

It is at worst a derivative, uninspired, mediocre plot slog, with insensitively portrayed 'natives' and a lot of invisible walls.

I was in my first hour of play, enjoying the game despite lowering my expectations, when I got soft locked for the first time. Luckily the autosaves are frequent enough and I lost very little progress. This game has potential. The framework is there. The system is in place. However, it seriously lacks originality and authenticity and the natives look like actors from with face paint.

They say that cliches are only bad if they're done badly, and unfortunately in this game they have been done badly.\t\t\t\t\t
