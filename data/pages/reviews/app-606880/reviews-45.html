\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those rare RPGs that is great in every single aspect, graphics, music, gameplay, combat, characters, voice acting, world building, story telling, good gods the story telling in this game!

GreedFall is right up there with the golden age Bioware RPGs like KotOR, DA: Origins, and Mass Effect. It is visually gorgeous, combat is really fun and very varied depending on how you choose to tailor your character and what you're faced with, the world itself is very captivating and keeps you coming back for more even after completing the game.
It also plays heavily into the fact that as De Sardet, you are a diplomat first and foremost, and actively punishes you for not behaving appropriately. That being said, running a playthrough as Scumbag De Sardet, Biggest Arsehole of the Century, is something everyone should try, because even if it hurts your soul, it is so much fun and lets you see parts of the game you probably wouldn't otherwise get to experience.

One thing I found that is rather unique in this type of game is that there are no side quests that are just 'busy work', each one of them adds something to the story, and many, if not most, of them will have an impact on the choices available to you further down the road.
You get good sized group of companions to accompany you on your travels across the island, swapping them around for various missions feels very natural, as they each belong to a different faction and will have more to add to specific scenarios than the others might. The characters are for the most part well fleshed out, you come to care about them and their fates, making some of the choices really difficult, some even break your heart a little.
The game lets you heavily impact the story, and there are so many different variables to discover that may change simply depending on which order you complete quests in, or who you bring with you, and thus replayability is very high.

The game is not without flaws, but they are very minor and easily overlooked.
Overall, this game is a gem, and I for one can't wait for GreedFall II and a chance to experience more of this world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game really surprised me, I wasn't sure what to expect but I really enjoyed it.

STORY: It is actually a really good story, i thought the concept was very unique and I think it really stands out compared to other games. The pacing is a bit slow tho. Characters are interesting and it feels like a Dragon Age game with their dialogue. I think the voice acting killed me tho. Main characters not bad, side characters on the other hand were not great.

GAMEPLAY: If you loved Dragon Age Inquisition, you will feel right at home with this game. If you hated the game play of DA:I like i did, you might struggle a bit. Clunky and not very smooth combat. I wish this were a more open world game, it is a bit linear and I think it suffers due to that. Runs smoothly on the PC, so thats a bonus.

GRAPHICS: Not bad, not great. I would say average.

VERDICT: Don't expect this to be like a Witcher 3: Wild Hunt. It is not. BUT I think the story had a lot of heart and the ending honestly hurt me. Me cry. Get while it is on sale. I think you might enjoy this game, just give it a few hours to tell its story :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has been relatively fun. The world is need. It was poorly developed with regards to multiple facets. In about 10 hours played so far, I have discovered things that make me question trying to even play any further, but the story and world are good enough to make me keep going. The items below leave me somewhere in the middle on liking this game.

For one The Arena was super lame. It was offered way too early in your character development as a quest you cannot complete till you get a bit stronger. It mentions a option to bet but its not in the game, which would have been good for the early fights to be able to upgrade gear.

There is no AutoRun. Third party apps required to not strain my hand holding down shift and w non stop to get round the world. This function should be in any and all over the shoulder RPG games.

A Huge thing is combat is horrible in any place that has trees as they do not make the trees transparent as you spin your camera around where the trees are in between you and your character, leaving you at the mercy of enemies attacking you without being able to see them to time you blocks.

The dodge function is somewhat janky and should have been made a roll when trying to dodge to the side as well so when trying to sidestep an attack you often get hit anyway. It works best shifting backwards but if there is crap behind you or an invisible wall before you hit an actual wall you get pinned between it and your enemy with crap ability to move to the side to circle back to the other side unless specifically locked onto that enemy in front of you which helps a bit with rotating around them. However that gets pretty crappy if you have multiple enemies or allies around you. Ultimately it is super easy to get backed into a corner.

Things that were good:
Some of the darker decision making items such being able to allow your group members to exact revenge improving your relations with them or their tribe or things like that make the world a bit darker with a realistic nature to the time and experience of what it is like to live in a world of monsters and disease.

The crafting system was relatively enjoyable. Being able to keep the gear pieces you like and upgrade them to some extent is nice.

Having a team you choose with customization options is also something i enjoy in games such as this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith about 25 to 50 hours of content and multiple endings where choices are important
The best part of this game to me is the story, there is a good amount of lore and you get to decide your relationship with each friend and nation based on your decisions. If you decide to explore everywhere and gather stuff around as well as do contracts you can easily have 60 hours of content. There are elements like nations/factions, friend companions and side quests. Your decisions also influence the game ending.

Character creation allows you to be either a female or a male
There isn't much to customize around, you can pick stuff like skin, eye and hair color, limited hair types, and some face presets but you can't modify your physical aspect.

It feels strange at first but once you get used the game becomes really entertaining
There can be some struggle in the beginning to understand the game mechanics and combat physics, but once you get used to it the game becomes really good to play.

Clean UI
I found the UI to be quite clean and there is also options to hide it outside or in combat.

Immersion could be better
You can sneak on your enemies but the sneak mechanics aren't very immersive, enemies won't notice you in front of them sometimes and your followers are completely ignored but you can play a pretend game and it's fine. The NPCS though could use some adrenaline and serotonin help, most of them are not doing much and they seem rather unhappy with their existence. Some places feel somehow empty due the lack of objects and npcs as well as small wildlife variety, but it's not that noticeable unless you like to watch npcs while you eat popcorn, things are still there.

Most skills are "add more damage"passive ones but there are some interesting ones too
There are some interesting things in this game like traps and weapon enchantment, but most skills are passive ones that add more damage to the weapons. The melee combat with heavy weapons isn't that great but you can add some magic to it and make it fun. I had most fun with technician's traps, firearms and one handed swords and magic skill trees.

There are swords, longswords, 1 and 2 handed heavy weapons, firearms, rifles, and magic rings.
I wish there could be a bigger variety of items, not to mention you feel like you gotta wear a nation's jersey every time with the lack of more personal looking sets, to make it clearer, each armor you can buy or find in the game belongs to a faction so you can easily look like a guard and not so easy to unlook like a guard or someone else.
Some of them look good though and you can actually make your character look like a character from a musketeer movie with blue cape and noble hat combined with a flintlock and a rapier.

You can craft potions, traps and enchantments from a list that doesn't need to find recipes, except quest recipes. Weapons and armors have upgrade slots but can't be created
Crafting system is only about upgrading items or creating consumables, you can't really craft a weapon or armor just upgrades, but each upgrade changes or adds something visual to the item which is nice.

Big semi-open world.
It's not an open world game, instead there are multiple regions you can access and the world building has really good looking landscapes and cities but at the same time due to physics limitation a certain path must be used instead of creating your own (Think about Witcher 2). Many cloned interiors make things predictable and less immersive.

Not a really challenging game, it's more focused towards the story.
If you like to focus on stories without being bothered with the difficult, you can find a good balance playing on hard or extreme. Some skills and items feel overpowered making it easy to deal with enemies once you learn the tricks.

Not a single crash or game breaking bug.
The graphics are really good and the performance feels good too, except I couldn't use clothes physics on the ultra due to some weird stuttering (playing on ultra 4k) but the rest was fine

Bottom line: it's a good game and I can easily recommend for people who like RPGs and a good story.\t\t\t\t\t
