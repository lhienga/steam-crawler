\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game. I really did, but I just could not bring myself to do it...

(Bad stuff starts at 4th paragraph)

Let's start with the good:

The world is beautiful! Absolutely everywhere you look, the island that you find yourself on has the most beautiful, colorful scenery that really makes the place feel exotic and mysterious. I enjoyed finding places that overlooked the areas I was in to look out over the island. It was breathtaking at times.

The combat is not that bad. It's pretty decent, though mostly involves attacking, dodging, blocking, and using hot keys to heal, shoot, or use magic. Once you get the hang of it, fights get pretty easy, even among some higher level enemies.

The leveling system is good. I think I saw some complaints about the leveling system, but it's not awful. There are 3 different areas where you can level up. Basic skill points (which accrue with each level up), Attribute points (you acquire every other level (I think)), and Talent points (which might come every 3rd level). I think it's good, and you can level up instantly if you wish. There are some skills that are locked behind requiring multiple skill points in your basic skill point tree. Not a big issue, because you can get more skill points by exploring the world and finding "skill shrines." I dumped all of my talent points into Charisma because I got tired of doing fetch quests for people, and I learned that if my Charisma is high, I can just convince them to give me what I need without doing the quest. Overall, not a bad system. More complex than, say, Skyrim's, but still not bad.

That's really where the good ends. Here's the bad:

The world is too linear. I went into this expecting an open world. What I got was a bunch of separate areas that make you feel like you're in a larger open space or arena, but in reality, it's just a maze of linear paths that you have to follow that might take you to your objective, or it might lead you to a dead end.

It's really just a bunch of fast traveling. I really don't care for fast traveling in games, so going from one area to the next is basically just fast travelling, but with extra steps. It doesn't matter if your destination is 3 hours away or 1 day away, every time you go to a new area, you pause at this 'little camp' where you have the opportunity to craft some stuff, buy things from a merchant, and talk to your comrades or switch out members of your party. That could be done some other way. It's so frustrating trying to travel somewhere, and I keep having to stop at this little campsite before moving on!

The quests are annoying. This game doesn't really hold your hand. I have never needed to use a guide so much in all my life just for the main questline. I didn't even finish it. I gave up!!! There are certain side and main quests that tie into the overall main quest, but it's hard to know which ones you're supposed to complete or trigger to progress the story. A couple were sort of out of the way, and with the help of a guide or obscure comment from when this game was new, I managed to find where I was supposed to go.

There are so many fetch quests. Doesn't matter what sort of relationship you have with a person or faction. They'll still want you to 'run here, get this, come back.' Which will trigger an avalanche of other quests from running there to get that from someone who needs you to run over there to get this to take to that to get to there. It was just so frustrating.

Loot and gold drops are abysmal. I defeated about 5 or 6 different bosses, and they all drop about ~5 gold or less. These are some hard fights, and I can't even afford to replenish my health potions and ammo with that! Of course, you do get trophies from the bosses that are meant to be sold to merchants, but they might bring you in 150 gold or so. It's just frustrating to run out of potions in a fight, finally kill the boss, and get 5 gold for all that hard work. It felt like I was playing an MMO.

The story is fairly boring. Despite the world, the lore, and the interesting mix of Spanish Conquistador meets magic wielding natives, I could not bring myself to get invested with the story. Maybe because the story was a never ending cycle of repetitive quests and 'going here and there to do that' stuff.

I know some people really enjoyed this game and it was widely praised, but I just don't see the appeal in it. I felt like I really wasted the $3 I spent during the sale to get this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first saw Greedfall, it looked like a promising RPG. I've put it on my wishlist and eventually bought it on sale.
Most of the story in Greedfall takes place on Teer Fradee, a mysterious newly-discovered island inhabited by natives who possess strange powers and communicate with spirits. While the rest of the world is devoured by plague, this island seems unaffected and subsequently draws the most powerful nations in search for a cure. You take the role of De Sardet, a legate (chief diplomat) of the Merchant Congregation, who was sent alongside his cousin Constantin to represent the nations interest on the island. The religious nation of Theleme and the science-oriented Bridge Alliance have also found their way into the island, along with the Coin guard, the seafaring Nauts, and some other factions of less importance. You'll play a crucial role in the events that follow, uncovering ancient knowledge as well as the truth behind your own existence.
The story is definitely the strongest suit of this game. That doesn't mean that the story is incredible though, only that other aspects of the game are worse off. The story could have been incredible if the choice/consequence system was implemented correctly. You are rarely presented with a choice, and the effects of these choices are very minor. There are tons of things in the game that I would have done differently, only that isn't an option (well, I guess slaughtering half the town wouldn't be, you being a diplomat and all..) The graphics are good enough (especially the exteriors) and so are the music and voice acting. Unfortunately, everything else is pretty bad. More than 80% of gameplay consists of running from point A to point B and speaking to NPCs. Combat is terrible and gets repetitive after the first couple of fights. Forget all about tactics, the strike-dodge-strike approach is the only viable strategy in this game. The AI is incredibly dumb, making your companions totally worthless in a fight. On the other hand, the game is ridiculously easy even on the hardest difficulty so the companions effort in combat isn't all that important. Character development is as linear as it gets, so you can also forget all about builds. There is also the fact that not a single weapon or armor has any special ability. Considering there are only several spells available in the whole game, it's no wonder that combat is boring and repetitive. There are only several types of enemies as well, very disappointing. Still, despite all these deficiencies, the game is still moderately amusing.

Greedfall turned out to be more of an adventure game than a RPG, since all RPG elements were done terribly. I would recommend it to gamers who love long walks and a good story.

My score: 6/10\t\t\t\t\t
