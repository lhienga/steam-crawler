\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd really like to recommend Greedfall because its refreshing to see an RPG of this type from a smaller developer, but it just has too many flaws to be an enjoyable experience. I spent about 40 hours thinking 'surely it's just about to get interesting' and then realised it wasn't but I was too committed at that point not to finish it. It's not so much a case of anything being especially bad but rather everything just being totally mediocre.

Among the positives are the graphics, and the lighting effects in particular are very good. (although character's facial animations are not very well done) There are plenty of items and a reasonable scope to customise how your character fights. The overall aesthetic of the game is quite nice too, with a sort of renaissance style which sets it apart from other similar titles. Whether the story is interesting or not is mostly going to be a matter of personal taste. For me it had occasional moments of interest, but don't expect Dragon Age levels of storytelling, and most of the twists and turns are fairly predictable.

The game world itself is fairly big, it's 'semi open world', but it very quickly becomes apparent that there is only a thin illusion of depth. For example the cities and towns you visit are full of NPCs, but they all just sort of stand around doing nothing - likewise enemies in the world map just stand stationary until you get close enough for them to attack you. The whole impression is of a world which is just sort of lifeless and cardboard cutout.

Likewise many of the game's mechanics appear detailed at first, but quickly reveal their simplicity. For example there are a ton of items to craft, but only about 10 basic crafting ingredients, and almost everything you can craft can just as easily be bought or found in the field. You have a house in each of the three main cities, but it's literally exactly the same house with no detail differences at all, and you can't customise it in any way whatsoever. There are travel pauses where you visit an encampment when travelling between locations, but nothing to do in these that you can't do everywhere else in the game. Overall it feels like the developers just threw in everything they could think an RPG should have but didn't put any effort into fleshing it out.

Combat plays somewhat similar to The Witcher 3, however I found the controls frustratingly unresponsive at times, especially during some of the harder enemy encounters the game throws at you. Frequently I was resorting to hammering a button and swearing at the screen in the hope my character would actually do what I wanted them to. You do have several companions who help you out in fights, and these characters are actually probably the game's most interesting element. They're all very distinct and unique and the story missions you can complete for them which reveal more about their back stories are actually the most interesting quests the game has to offer in my opinion. There is also the ability to romance certain companions, although it's pretty simplistic.

And that brings me to the quests, which is by far the game's biggest problem. Almost every single quest in this game follows the following sequence: Talk to person A, who sends you to talk to person B on the other side of the map. Person B sends you to person C in another corner of the map. Person C sends you back to talk to both person A and person B, and then you talk to person C again and the quest is resolved. Possibly you might fight some enemies during that process. Sound fun? I understand the player character is a diplomat so being sent on repeated journeys to talk to people kinda makes sense, but it just becomes so damn tedious and boring that I really feel the only way to get genuine enjoyment from it would be maybe to play a drinking game where you have a drink everytime you have to traverse the map to speak two lines of dialogue to someone for the third time in a row. You'll most probably die before you complete the game.

Overall, unless you're really desperate to play every Witcher clone out there, this is a game which you can do without.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI became interested in this game for two reasons, one that it was a Bioware-esque action RPG, and the other was the setting, which looked interesting. It didn't disappoint in either aspect.

The game's world is perhaps what stands out the most. The colonial-era inspired fantasy setting is a very unique idea, and it’s very well executed. There are multiple different factions, each with their goals and beliefs, and the dynamics between them form the backbone of the game’s world. The game starts off a bit slow in this respect, at the beginning the factions felt a bit two-dimensional, but as you progress through the story and get to know them, it gets more and more interesting. I particularly liked the fact that they created a unique language for the natives – it really feels like you are meeting another civilization.

The visual design is also very well done, I loved the 16th-17th century inspired setting, both in terms of architecture and clothing. The clothes and armor are especially cool looking, though I particularly love the fashion of that era. This is in stark contrast to the indigenous villages, their clothes and face paintings, but these were also great looking, and gave them their own identity. The rocky, forested wilderness, which we often visit, was beautiful too. It’s really a joy just to walk around the locations. So much so that I feel it is a cardinal sin that there is no photo mode in the game. Well, at least you can turn off the hud. There’s one weak aspect though, the interiors. They are nice aesthetically, but there are only a few variations and these are often reused. At least the main faction town halls could have been unique. I should add that I did install a mod that turned off film grain and depth of field. I can’t stand these effects.

The other main aspect of the game is the talking to NPCs, running around for quests, and combat RPG gameplay reminiscent of Bioware’s games, with our companions and the decisions we make throughout the story being core parts of the game. The latter is important, as our choices shape our relationship with the factions and with our companions, and affect the main story, which can have several different endings. I think the story has been interesting and engaging, with some nice surprises along the way. It touches some heavy themes, and it fits the world very well. Our companions are a very colorful bunch, I think they are interesting and well written characters, who are integral to the events of the story. I liked them. This is also partly down to the voice actors, who did a great job. Of course there is also the possibility of romance, which I liked, although I think this part could have been better developed. However, considering the limited budget of the developers, I feel it's not bad.

The RPG system is not very complicated. Our equipment are weapons, armor and various accessories, which can be upgraded, and our character have different abilities that can be leveled up. Most of these are combat related, but some of these affect other parts of the game, which I thought was nice. Aside from the obvious things, like Charisma's effect on dialogue, Science can help with detective work, or Intuition can help with finding hidden paths, for example. These can also allow us to solve certain quests in alternative ways, or even without fighting.

I didn't have high expectations of the gameplay itself, but I have no complaints. There are a lot of dialogue and a lot of walking around for quests. Maybe a bit too much walking around, but I didn't mind it, because the environments are so beautiful that I just enjoyed looking around. One or two extra fast travel points would’ve been nice, but that's it. The quests were mostly interesting, with multiple solutions, I didn't feel like I was running around for nothing. The only problem I had is that there are times when you have to wait for certain events, but the game doesn't allow you to wait for a specific time interval, only until a specific time of day, like sunset or sunrise. In such cases, I had to wait for the quest event in real time, which often took minutes.

The combat was really fun though. I already like the sword and musket era, but the game throws magic into the mix, which makes it even more interesting. There’s a certain charm in running out of mana, and then just being able to grab your single shot pistol to fend off a charging enemy. Towards the endgame, I could even put an entire enemy group into stasis and just shoot them one by one, which was brutal. I enjoyed it, though there's not much enemy variety, the combat was fun, and I really liked the boss fights.

I'm really glad I found this game. Its interesting, unique setting and story, and beautiful locations really captivated me. Highly recomended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn ARPG with great world building and world design that has an ok story until it starts to fall apart by the end. The factions system is something I didn't care for, as the general story amounts to most factions viewing the natives as savages and you, the character, having to constantly try to appease the other factions so that you get the best ending. As far as companions go, I really only like 3 of them and didn't bother with the companion quests for the others. The thing this game has going for it is the combat, especially for the mage and the world design. However the skill system can be very frustrating. Certain points in the story will require you to have certain skills, and luckily if you are friendly with a companion you achieve a boost to that skill, but only if they are in your party. I have had to run several times to the nearest camp to switch out a companion just to run back to pass the check. It just felt like a time waster, which is what can also be said for the combat. After about 20 hours, i switched to easy from hard because the combat was starting to become a chore, especially with enemies respawn after a set amount of time. Overall this game does a lot right, but I do hope Greedfall 2 is a true open world with a more complex and nuanced story.\t\t\t\t\t
