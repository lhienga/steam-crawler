\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a good RPG game with a solid story, atmosphere and decent gameplay.

But there are some gripes with it. You will be doing a lot of backtracking, since you can't just fast travel from anywhere in the map to somewhere else; you'd had to find a campfire or a travel point to use fast travel and they aren't exactly plentiful. In the midst of backtracking, you will be immersed in the beautiful atmosphere of Greedfall. If you like scenery, this is your game, since there's really nothing else except for scenery. The world feels barren, with no mini-encounters or combat along the way to spice up the mindless bore of walking across vast open spaces. Exploration feels unfruitful; a lot of Greedfall's chest give minor loot like a couple of coins and 1 ammo. Even chests that seem ornate from the outside or those that require the highest lockpicking skill requirement give mediocre items. They are almost never exciting, which is one of the core experiences of a open world RPG game.

I do think the story at least makes up for it. The game overall is still a serviceable piece. Combat is straightforward and easy. A bit finnicky at times, but you can just ignore the different weapons and beeline the 'Guns' tree and easily beat the game. The story what was engaged me the most and kept me playing till the end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Lots of story to be unlocked, and I like story so win
- Alliance building makes for some diplomacy based choices
- The party options are pretty great*
- Flexible character builds
-- All melee, all magic, mix of both
- Some character customization
- big map, and if you are like me, you can explore all of it as soon as the area loads, get all the campfires and then just fast travel every time you have a quest.

Cons
- If you like dialogue no problem, if you don't there is a lot of it
- Alliance building is not particularly difficult, so while interesting not challenging
- I have nothing bad to say about the party mechanics
- The character builds could be more flexible. The magic route is weak. Really the skill tree (spiral) is a little simplified, so I stopped really needing anything from it after a while. I would just place skill points to use them. The firearms, blade, and blunt weapon options were much better in variety. Magic is basically six spells, three of them only for when you are actively wearing a ring (as oppose to having a melee weapon in your hand) the other ones are 1) freeze one guy, 2) freeze an area, and 3) explode an area.
- Character customization is only for facial features and hair. After you set this at the beginning, you can't go back and change it. I am not too upset by that, just pointing it out because I was excited by the customization.
- The big map would be better if it was not for the fact that I was perpetually getting turned around. I am not sure what it is about the map (scale, ambiguous borders, landmarks not intuitive or something) but I have to flip it open every few seconds at times to orient myself. For comparison, I play a game with the same set-up, linear compass on hud, way points, and map with cursor for character orientation, I have never been so turned around as this game. Once you memorize the map and stop using the map all the time its awesome.

*Comrades (my favorite part apparently)
-- In fact you can deck them out better than yourself, because they also level up (in set patterns) when you level up (strength characters get stronger or wider range of melee weapons etc.)
-- In fact since they all come from different factions you can swap them at will to assist in different missions
-- You can also friend and romance them
-- They move out the way when you cast stasis so that they don't mess up your spell. Which is so useful that I can't even describe (though if they've started their animation it is too late)

Overall: I really like this game. There are things that are annoying of course, but I thoroughly enjoy it for what it is. I never get stuck while running around because there's something in there that just kicks in every time I fall into some odd corner or tripped over people in my party. The party members always get out my way. Stasis is my absolute favorite part of this game (especially with the armor erroding effect). The travel campifire feature is also another of my favorite facets of this game. If the game has to load then you might as well let me do some party maintenance while we are at it. If you just want a good RPG with some dialogue that's not high fantasy flavored I recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
