But before you dive into this game and try to unfold the mysteries of the new world, I have to point out some of the negatives. In addition the really good feeling of mystery that the game builds up the hole time, might lead to a small disappointment in the later stage of the game in my opinion. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've just started, but I've already decided that I love this game. I play a lot of modded rpg and the storyline and character development and the uniqueness of the relationships rival (or exceed) any of the other games I've played. I love depth, and like a good book, that often takes a bit of time to develop. The voiced characters are superb, and there are so many variations that I stay immersed--not like some where the NPC's are on repeat. Yeah, I know, there's a bit of duplication with the settings, and with the population in general, but it's also pretty reasonable to expect of any game. If you've a mind to do it, then you can explain it.

I just imagine that there's some organizational architect that each of the factions and kingdoms use as a template for constructing their own buildings. And it's done. Not an issue for me. The fast (and sometimes slow) travel is a bit...how would I say it...clunky, but it's certainly not something that bothers me. The landscape and cityscapes are so wonderfully detailed, I don't mind walking, running or skipping (lol, not literally, but in my mind) along the pastoral settings, or along the city streets, listening to either the birds or the background chatter and activities. I swear I even saw a fox doing a dump along the path. That kind of NPC character interaction certainly happens in the streets of the cities with the guards. Might be one of the mods I loaded, but it's that level of detail in the play that keeps me captivated.

The equipment upgrades, skill building and weapons/magic/alchemical deployment and getting used to game controls on pc required a bit of attention. But I don't mind the challenge. It just makes Greedfall all the more interesting and I realize I'll have to develop those skills during the next playthrough--which I'm already anticipating. There are so many options for skills and character development that it will take several playthroughs to advance those skills, kind of like real life.

There are so many surprises it literally makes me laugh. After dozens of plays of Skyrim, Cyberpunk, Fallout, Horizon and Mass Effect, (and way too many years of waiting for Starfield) I'm so happy that I discovered Greedfall. This storyline is now at the very top of my list of enjoyable games--and like movies and series, I just rate them for entertainment value. I laugh, smile and yes, even cry at points while playing. It's that poignant. Fabulously written and wonderfully voice-acted. If you're an rpg enthusiast who enjoys character interaction, I cannot recommend Greedfall highly enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hate that I love this game.

TL;DR: The core gameplay is good and you can tell the small team of devs really tried their hearts out for this game. However it falls flat in conveying the story strongly which is highlighted by okay-ish voice acting.

What I love: The basis for the story is super intriguing, colonial America style with some fantastical flair. The core experience is good, combat is easy to learn. Lots of different places to explore around the island. The range of personalities your companions have and their backstories are pretty well fleshed out. Even some major characters (even some more minor characters) have pretty well thought out backstories. The game (at the end) rewards you for doing all those pain staking side quests (more on that below).

What I hate: Lets address the biggest burden this game has... voice acting. While it isn't *bad* by any stretch, it isn't good, it's just okay. There's is innumerable situations in the story where the voice acting does not match the feel or vibe of the conversation or context of the situation; which really hinders the story telling to be mostly monotone. The story itself isn't inherently grabbing. It further suffers from many fetch and retrieve quests or "Hey I know you're here but I need you to go to virtually the other side of the entire island to talk to this person, then go to the other side of the island and talk to this person then come back here and tell me what they said" tasks that just take away from the game. I understand quests like that are necessary some times, but not 10 times in the main quest line and another 10 or so times in the side quests.

What to expect going in: Do not expect a The Witcher or a Skyrim, or a Diablo, or even a Dragon Age caliber game. Go with the expectation of a AA or even A title. There was a small team that worked on this and you can tell they put their heart and soul into it.

All that said I have hopes that most of that will be better in the second game and I really look forward to playing that when it arrives.\t\t\t\t\t

That largest similarity being an overwhelming number of fetch/filler quests in an open world that distract from the story and clunky combat and exploration. Given that I cannot recommend this game as much as I hoped it would be great.\t\t\t\t\t
