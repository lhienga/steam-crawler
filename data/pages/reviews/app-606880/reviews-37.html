\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't really recommend this one. While the under-utilised historical influences are pleasant and the voice acting is strong, the negatives outweigh the positives.
One of the first things to draw my attention was the basic errors in subtitles' spelling and grammar. Two examples I noted were the misspelling 'sollicit', and the use of 'rebel rousers' instead of 'rabble rousers' - amateurish, easily correctable mistakes.
Some of the side-quests are fairly interesting, but a large amount of your questing time will be spent running between NPCs. This has driven me from other games in the past, and it's done so once again with GreedFall.
The lore has some potential, but I don't think it is introduced very effectively to the player. The term 'lore dump' would be an apposite description of what the player is subjected to in the first minutes of gameplay. A more informative opening cinematic would help here.
The combat is serviceable but becomes repetitive fairly quickly, and dialogue sequences are consistently verbose.
All things considered, the interaction of 17th-century New World and European fantasy aesthetics is very refreshing, but as an RPG GreedFall quickly falls flat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhoever says this game has a great story is lying. It is a poorly executed story riddled with plot devices.
Some examples:
- One of the first quests is debunking some potion vendor as a charlatan by making him drink his own potion. Instead of getting him to drink from one of the 50 sealed bottles in front of him, you have to run around and find a bottle from his storage.
- People will randomly attack you without any explanation as to why or who they are, including in the charlatan quest. They have no relation to the vendor.
- When you follow the captain to the ship, he goes a big detour just to conventiently guide you to a battle area where out of no where a monster escapes from an unrelated ship, just so you have something to do.
- You can't get past specific guards because they recognize you as an outsider. But when you wear one of their red hats they suddenly have no recollection of ever having seen you and just let you in.

Open world mechanic are poor too.
- No horses, walking everywhere
- Frequent loading screens. You run to the end of the road and it asks you if you want to "exit" and then it loads a new map. It does not feel like an open environment
- There are carts that take you from A to B for a fee (since there is so little fast travel) but half the places you might want to go to are not one their list.

RBG elements are excessive
- You have three separate character trees
- Then you have all the engineering vs magic vs combat abilities you can focus on
- But since combat it so simple and because the basics aren't working, there is no incentive to engage in any of it

Controls suck
- Combat is a joke. You can get away just smashing the same button
- Moving controls are sluggish
- You have to pause combat to drink a potion. And then drinking the potion is so slow that the enemy knocks u over in the meantime

Graphics look like potato
- Does not look like a game from 2019 but rather 2015
- Environments look the same. How come the city in the old world looks the same as in the new world?
- It would severely lag all the time, making stealth impossible.

I still have hopes for Greedfall II but they better fix this stuff. This game has so much potential\t\t\t\t\t

Greedfall is a Solid B-Grade RPG.


Let's start off with the story. Greedfall follows Legate De-Sardet in their journey from their home continent to the Isle of Teer Fradee. Along the way they will come into contact with a number of old-world and new world factions. Your goal as De-Sardet is to ensure peace between your people and the others (or not, depending on your choices) as well as finding a cure for the plague affecting the old world. It progresses slowly, but not without keeping your interest. If you play your cards right, you can get a happy, if not overly optimistic ending.

Moving on to gameplay. Greedfall is reminiscent of titles like Dragon Age: Origins, and Knights of the Old Republic. Unfortunately, the game suffers from a number of issues that are present in those titles as well, at times experiencing them worse. Combat is relatively simple, but not without many different skill-lines to take to change it up. Unfortunately, it can be made very easy with a gun and the duplication glitch (which can eliminate monetary issues as well).
While the landscape is beautiful, you'll find yourself running back and forth between quest objectives more times than you can count. The game has a decent number of fast travel points, but could either do with more points, or allow you to fast travel from anywhere. Seriously, De-Sardet must have some wild callouses.
It's worth it to do as many side-quests as possible, both for the different factions and your companions. When companion affinity is maxed, you'll be given a bonus (such as craftsmanship for Kurt, or intuition from Vasco) which can help you manage your attribute points better.

Lastly, I'd like to talk about the game's art-direction and themes. Greedfall takes place in a colonial fantasy setting, which you almost never see. The cultural styles present in the various factions are interesting, from the Bridge Alliance which draws heavily from the Ottoman Turks, to the Natives of Teer Fradee who reflect Celtic and Indigenous American cultures. Unfortunately, each Colonial culture seems to have hired the same architect, as every interior (notably the Governor's mansion from each) looks exactly the same but with a different coat of paint.
The themes in the game are obvious, criticizing colonialism and the exploitation of both indigenous populations and their land. If you strive for the best ending, then you'll be working towards the unification of Teer Fradee's people with the new colonial residents.


TL;DR - Nice RPG with a lot of running back and forth which is easily exploited. Long-winded, but keeps your interest. Feels like Dragon Age/KOTOR in a colonial setting, with heavy criticism of colonialism. 7/10 is pretty accurate, It's a bit rough at times and Spiders needs to improve upon the game's shortcomings and issues for GreedFall 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do apologize in advance, because things I found too annoying about this game may have been powered through if I just played a little more, but I promised myself I will not force myself to play through games I do not find enjoyable.

First things first, the graphics are okay, but faces and voice acting are horrible. Either does not convey the emotion very well which is a major downside, and the first dialogue MC has with the guy who comes to find his brother... You know you usually say something, wait a second for the other person to react, then continue. That word vomitt was just horrible.

Second thing, character creation. It is not class based RPG, it is a skill based RPG, why do you force me to choose an archetype without actually trying the archetypes first? I mean you have a combat tutorial right after this (if you mention how older games like Arcanum or Baldur's Gate did not let you try that either, well, they did not really offer you a comprehensive combat tutorial either did they), why do you not let me try that before choosing?

On that note let me say a few things about combat. Firstly, the camera. You are not a 3rd person shooter, you are a RPG game with lock-on combat system, why do developers persist with this asinine over the shoulder camera that adds nothing to the combat experince and is jarring and annoying, especially when it swaps shoulders on its own without a prompt from the player. Secondly who approved the control scheme? LSHIFT for dodge and space for pause? I am pretty sure it these types of game SPACE is usually used for rolling/dodge if the game does not use it for jump, you already had a TAB for combat pause, why did you feel the need to additionally bind it to SPACE as well. Thirdly armor mechanic, what does it add to the gameplay? Character wanting to use Firearms as his main form of damage will be heavily penalized just for choosing this form of combat and I can see that from the get go. I mean, magic ignores the armor, melee uses blunt weapons to break through and firearms character uses... yeah, you guessed it, traps. I can see this is such a horrible design where you give disadvantage to one archetype from the get go and again... why? It is not an additional challenge, it is an inconvenience. You may have as well added additional HP to enemies and it would have added more freedom to the player, like this you are basically forcing them to play a certain way.

Fourth thing, the story... I mean if you are trying to frame it as an epic story set in a fantasy world. Please do so. And the writing may honestly be ok, a little bit bland for my tastes (like your mother telling you you are going to find a cure for your people, basically shoehorning your reason for this little venture, when you just may want to find a cure for profit, etc.), but I am not reading a book. This a video game and there are many things to enhance the overall storytelling experience. Imagine you get to the room with you mother (and do not get me started on a placement of that room, a lady of the household has a room right next to the training grounds?), there is a handmaiden actually tending to your very sick mother and then you have a very short dialogue with her about your mother's condition and then you tell her to leave you alone for a bit or something. This would have felt much more organic and the world would have felt as if actual people lived in it and not just props.

Last but not least Hogwarts Legacy steers your character like a ship full of drunken sailors, but you went the opposite way and when you want to go left or right your character twitches like a crack addict on his fifth day binge.

In the end I guess those are minor annoyances, which most people even do not realise or do not agree with, but I cannot in good conscience ignore these and call this game good and recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
