I dislike this game intensely, and I do not know that I can continue playing it. I will try to go beyond some of the criticisms I already read about this game.

I despise the character, who is most assuredly not who I would have chosen to roleplay were I given an actual choice. You cannot even pick his/her name. Piloting around this moralizing wussy is fingernails-on-a-chalkboard grating. The most basic decency triggers drawn out lecturing delivered in a voice that sounds like Prince Valiant: Social Justice Warrior.

Without the character's interminable yammering with every NPC he meets, one realizes there is very little game playing going on. What took hours to get through the first time, took mere minutes the second time thanks to the skip buttons. And the minutes spent "playing" were mainly running around to the next person to hit Esc-Esc-Backspace at, then run back to the first person to skip through their conversation too. Why does everyone spend so much time talking with so little to talk about? Was this project simply an employment program for voice over actors?

Combat is sparse and unsatisfying. Before quitting to try a different character build, I was in 4 battles. In two of them, my companions were removed. That's fine, because basically I can just button mash and pin enemies to the scenery. But why have you given me these bots only to take them away before I even know how to fight alongside them?

And why are these companions following me? The first and second were literally forced upon me by other people and tell me so. The third approaches on the street and then is suddenly ready to start killing people alongside me because we apparently both have the same problem with green facial mold.

Why is the merchant in the 2nd town a clone of the merchant in the first town?

Why is the cabin boy 30 years old?

Why does the pilgrim look like old Geddy Lee?

Why didn't I heed the reviews?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf Dragon Age and Skyrim had a baby it would be Greedfall. It has taken certain traits from both parents and implemented them into a version that stands alone. It is also the only game I have felt compelled to complete all achievements (well, except for the Resident Evil Demo; I mean who doesn't like a freebie).

Greedfall is a game for most ages by offering a wider range of difficulty settings, specifically the discovery level. Most people will assume I am talking about younger players but I represent the other end of the spectrum: my first video game was Pong, released in 1972. This may come as a shock but over time, as you age, your reaction time just isn't what it used to be and certain mechanics just frustrate the heck out of me. Discovery allowed me to experience the story and hone my skill. It was kind of like a casual, nursing-home play through which built up my confidence to try the next time in a harder difficulty until I tackled extreme mode. I mean gaming should be fun; it is kinda hard to WASD when your hand is clutching your chest trying to stop a real heart attack because the boss nearly one shot you.

Being an avid RPG fan for decades, I have learned to love the lore of a game. But back in my day, I had to traverse two continents in Azeroth, on foot, in all kinds of extreme conditions, through enemy territory, to achieve the lore master title; we didn't have that easy peasy flying mount the kids have today. The story and choices offered by Greedfall made all those hours worth it in this game. To keep the playthroughs fresh, I would take different companians for the same quest so the dialogue would change and I was intriqued to see what would change in the story. The key to a good RPG is immersion; a story should really elicit strong emotions from the players. I can say there was one faction I didn't mind telling to get off my lawn.

While I really love this game, my playtroughs were back to back: as soon as I finished one, I would restart a new game with different achievements in mind. I must admit that a period of OCD hit and this became an old-fashioned grind fest to finish everything. When the last achievement dinged, I was exhausted and needed a protein drink and a nap. While putting ice on my wrists to decrease the swelling of carpal tunnel, I told my kids I would NEVER 100% another game...then I saw it: Greedfall 2 coming soon. I just put in an order for an oxygen tank: I got this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as RPGs go, this is probably the worst one I have ever played. The story seems interesting enough and it is probably the only thing that kept me going as far as I got. Characters are bleak, uninteresting and you really have no idea what drives them. You get no choice of the kind of character you play; you are a diplomat with a silver tongue, who, if you don't spend in the Charisma talent, could not talk their way out of a wet paper bag... yet you are still a diplomat, take it or leave it.

Skills are just sad. No thought process has gone into combat whatsoever. Dodging and blocking may seem novel at first, but it quickly gets repetitive and tedious. Every fight feels the same... cast a spell, shoot your gun... rinse and repeat until the enemy is dead, move onto the next area. This is the extent of the game. And speaking of spells, i **** you not, there are 3... yes, just 3 spells, plus a few magic rings. No pretty fire, no manipulating your enemies to fight for you, no invisibility, nothing. There is nothing interesting about combat, and no other way to approach it other than run in and do the same repetitive crap you did in the last fight.

How anyone thought that this was even a remotely decent game seriously needs to go and play some good RPGs... Skyrim, Fallout, Cyberpunk, Fable, Kingdoms of Amalur: Reckoning... hell I would take Runescape over this trash.\t\t\t\t\t
