\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn enjoyable rpg to an extent. Combat isn't too bad and the visuals are nice. Characters are okay and voice acting isn't bad. However the game is a bit janky, facial animation is bad, has some memory leak issues and some bugs here n there.

It has a lot of big open maps but a lot of them are also very linear like a whole lot of trees, bushes and big rocks blocking everything causing you to go down one path most of the time.

There's a lot of different skills to unlock and progress in which can allow you to do things like open certain dialogue choices and enter areas that require some agility however a lot of these are just dumb and annoying like why can't the character walk through a gap in the wall just because they have low intuition? bit dumb.

I do wish there was more enemy variety, the monsters you encounter repeat over and over and it gets a bit boring killing the same ones all the time. There's often repeats of bosses as well which is also a shame.

Its a decent try at an rpg but it fails in some areas, it has some good points and is enjoyable but feels dated like it could have come out 10 years earlier. Hopefully the sequel address and focuses on improving and fixing the problems that this game has. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off I enjoyed the game, Fun gameplay loop, and a world with lots of immersion and RP potential. if you like RPGs you'll have a good time here.

Spoilers for the rest.

Progression-
Perhaps I over leveled early game, but it seems, or it felt like I had too many attribute points, I had gone down both the entire mace, magic and the inbetween trees and still had lots of points to spend that I just didnt need, since I was doing the cleric build.

Factions-
This is sort of the meat of the review and my only real problem with the game. So let me preface this with an acknowledgement that colonialism is a difficult topic to cover with tact while in a game trying to have lots of factions, 5 to be exact merchants (player faction), natives, mercenaries, puritans, and muslims (we'll get into my naming convention later)
Natives-
The natives I think were handled poorly due mainly to a difference between the way my character feels and the way I feel about many of their actions. Through the entire story the natives are (according to the story) unambiguously the good guys, and I understand this sentiment they are defending their homeland after all, however its difficult for me to sympathize when they kill civilian colonists constantly, try to attack the merchant (player) faction all the time, especially since the player is there nominally on behalf of the governor, and most of all are attacking the player and causing them problems on a regular basis, even though I had the native companion in my party the entire game. One quest in particular I think is a great example, Player is told that there's a problem at (player faction)'s lumber camp, player finds that some workers have been murdered, and the foreman is standing in the way of some natives trying to get into the lumber camp. Eventually you find out that the local chief specifically told a hunting party to murder the workers. Okay if this was anybody but the natives how would the governor or local guard etc react. That's an act of war! But the player character solves the problem by talking the a different village chief, who punishes the first one by sentencing them to hard labor. Let me make this clear, the game expects me to side with somebody who murdered my fellow citizens and was punished with (lets be real) a slap on the wrist. If I had murdered two natives they'd have my head, and it would be reasonable, but I the "civilized" man is supposed to just be happy about the natives getting their way (into the lumber camp), and away with the murder? The entire game the natives treat every faction poorly, including the player faction, and the game's tone suggests they have the moral high ground, and are the good guys.
All in all I think there should have been more wiggle room for the player treatment of the natives, rather than always relenting to their wants.

Mercs-
I actually like the way the city guard is portrayed, malicious incompetence in the leadership, and corruption in the lower ranks. I dislike how other factions treat the city guard, as if they are usually competent and honorable, (I understand there's a blackmail and influence questline) every time you meet the city guard (even though the player is nominally second in command of the merchant faction, the mercs main employer) you are treated like just wandered in and is asking some ridiculous question.
I think the best case would be in the future to have characters have consequences for their poor leadership, maybe the player can have input to the governor about the next in line etc.

Puritans-
I also think this faction is poorly done, their zealotry gets in the way of having realistic characters, damn near all of them are blood thirsty maniacs, for no reason, the natives seem to be very disconnected from their religion, rarely mentioning it, or doing anything with it, so why are the puritans so hell bent on torturing natives to get them to convert, seems the best way to convert them would be to just send a preacher to each village, and frankly if the natives weren't so bloodthirsty I would think this faction was out of place.
I think the move would be to tone down a little bit the "burn heretics" rhetoric, but keep the debt peonage and church abuse, this would both reflect real life, and be a much more subtle way to make this faction feel evil.

Muslims-
I know they ain't muslims, but they wear turbans, and obsessed with science, its hardly a stretch, instead of a 2001 stereotype, its a history book stereotype, which is better, but hardly compelling. Feels like with this faction they(developers) weren't willing to make much of a statement, standard evil scientists experimenting on people but nothing really unique.
I'd like to say add more flavor here, but given the flavor y'all added to the natives and puritans, I'd prefer no flavor.

Merchants-
Sort of the second best so far as good guys, the faction is not technically in player control, however they also don't do much without player prodding, which is good gives the player a lot of control over the actions of the faction outside of new serene. Its clear at least to me that the player is supposed to be split between their loyalty to their cousin and country on one side and moral good and the natives on the other, but as I've already gone over how the game's moral grandstanding falls on deaf ears of dead (mostly defenceless) colonists, so its fairly easy to pick a side, although the natives have a very useful companion so there's that.


all in all a fun game, and I would recommend to anybody who likes RPGs\t\t\t\t\t
