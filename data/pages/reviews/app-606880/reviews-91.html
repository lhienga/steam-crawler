\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoy narrative RPGs, such as Bioware's Kotor, Mass Effect, and Dragon Age series. Greedfall attempts that style, but it's writing falls short. That's not a game-breaking thing, but I play for narrative and immersive stories. I ran with a female protagonist and frequently got the "he" pronouns (even the dialogue options mis-gendered an NPC). I play the game with subtitles but got distracted with the various misspellings in them. It might have also been the quest order I selected, but there is a significant repeat of voice actors for NPCs in this game; it makes it harder to immerse yourself in the world hearing the same voices over and over and over... (If you played Dragon Age, you'll recognize Blackwall and Dorian's voices a great many times). It only really got on my nerves when I spoke to an NPC who was the same voice as a current companion in my party. By that point, the world does not feel immersive, but small, artificial, and cheap.

The cinematics are also very flat, and it can only be salvaged by great dialogue and story -- which Greedfall, unfortunately, doesn't have. I didn't feel any attachment to the companions after 15 hours of gameplay. Greedfall is a type game I really wanted to like, but it frequently felt cheap and unpolished. If you are a story-lover like me, you're going to be underwhelmed. If you're less picky, I can only really recommend this game if it's on sale.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI managed just under 40 hours into this game before losing the will to complete it. It feels like it wants to be the next Dragon Age, but the game lacks any compelling aspects to keep me playing, I felt like I was forcing myself to play to get my moneys worth which is not how a story driven game should feel. The combat was extremely repetitive, I was not invested in any of the characters, despite some of the voice acting being above average and the incredibly linear story and 'go hear/do this' type quests which could be pulled right out of an early 2000s era RPG made the game a chore to play.

Credit where it's due, the world building wasn't bad, building a new IP is hard and this a fresh take on a fantasy world but it is overshadowed by an awkward 3d game engine with 2d movement, the lack of the Z axis to jump felt awful in many parts of the game where getting caught on scenery was straight out of games from a decade ago and the invisible walls while navigating the open world areas was extremely frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally got around to playing and completing this game after having in my library for a while. I wasnt a huge fan of the setting, it personally didnt hold much interest for me, but it was executed well. The characters in the game were the strong suit. The voice acting was excellent, and the development of the characters was realistic and interesting. I consider it a bit of a shame that Spiders didnt push the character development for the companions more and have greater dialogue because they were all rather interesting, and their personal quests fleshed them out well. I enjoyed the combat for the most part I thought it was well balanced with the correct amount of challenge and good fun with a decent number of build options. Would be good if they balanced the ultimate abilities a bit better as bomb was the standout there, but thats a minor gripe. My only real bugbear was the running around fetch quests. Although you werent actually fetching stuff there was a LOT of running backwards and forwards and I wasnt a huge fan of that. Quest design with the exception that I just made was actually excellent. The side quests all had story to them and had their own twists and turns. Towards the last fifth of the game I did find myself running head first towards the ending as I had had my fill. My character wasnt really going to upgrade much more, build was complete, and I wanted to try and get to the resolution of the story, but I felt as though the last 5th of the game was a little dragged out, but that might just be me. Overall its a great underrated RPG from a smaller studio who absolutely deserve a try, each one of their games seems to get better and better so I will definitely keep supporting them in the future.
In summary here are the pros and cons.
Pros:
~Excellent characters
~Interesting setting
~Enjoyable, fun but challenging combat (basically normal is like Deathmarch in witcher 3)

Cons.
~Quests can involve quite a bit of running back and forth between areas
~Because its so character driven it can be hard to keep fully engaged for the entire length of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
