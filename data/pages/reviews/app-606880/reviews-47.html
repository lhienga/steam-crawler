\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I really want to give this game a thumbs up, I just can't get myself to do it.

Greedfall is in my opnion a small step up from other Spider games. But just a small one. At first I really enjoyed most aspects of it. The graphics were beautiful, the story fairly interesting, the voice acting good and the overall style was terrific.

But at some point in the game (rather quickly), I just ran out of wind. Mainly because of the lack of actual gameplay. The world would have benefited immensely from being true open world, being not so much of a copy paste job (everything feels like a re-visit), and even more so from feeling actually lived in. It is so empty. It is the same kinda bad as Dragon Age II.

The combat gets old really quick and seems more like a nuscience than actual fun. And if you remove that, it is basically a walk-and-talk simulator, with a fairly boring story and uninteresting characters, that you never really get involved in.

I hope they keep making evolving their games and next time focus more on a intriguing and dramatic story and interesting gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't really understand all the negative reviews I've been seeing here.

I bought this game (in sale) and in my opinion, this one is totally worth it. It's a cool RPG which I would describe as a mix between the style of The Witcher and Fable. You can choose how you want to play this game yourself and face a variety of around ten types of foes and bosses while exploring a beautifull world in a very immersive setting.

Pros:
- Story
- Voice acting
- Combat system (not too hard but challenging enough)
- Beautifull environment
- Sound and effects
- User friendly interface and skill system

Cons:
- This game needs a horse option
- Dialogues are sometimes too long but skippable

Conclusion:
If you are a very demanding player that want excellence on all levels this might not be the game for you but if you are a more forgiving player who likes to get sucked into a unique world and enjoys gameplay above perfection, this is definitely the game for you!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is good. A bit rough around the edges, but good.

The good:
-The combat is fun, guns feel like lightning you use to smite your enemies. Honestly, other devs who wish to add guns to 3rd person RPG's could really take note from this game.
-The major story line is solid.
-Companions! You will like at least 2 or 3 of them.
-You really get to feel like a respectable envoy, building relations with other nations.
-The ideas for the world are solid and there is a really great foundation to a world here. World building gets a solid B+.

The bad:
-The combat... It can be a bit janky, it's not smooth. The camera doesn't always do what you want and neither does your character. sometimes you will dodge in the wrong direction to no fault of your own. Movement is also awkward.
-The Natives accent. My god it is so bad it is painful to listen to at first. I understand why they gave them an accent, but they could have done anything other than this.
-The dialogue. It isn't very deep and usually pretty bland. Interactions with you companions and the people of the world just aren't that intriguing.
-The quests. These don't really feel that impactful and often times you are just running around for the sake of running around it feels like.

I still had a good time as I have already played all the 3rd-person-build a group of companions-RPG games that are available.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
- Fun RPG
- Excellent story, lots of intrigue and depth
- Utterly gorgeous to look at, really beautiful varied environments
- Interesting characters to meet along the way
- Great commentary on colonialism, politics and explores other social topics as well

The average
- Some interiors are copy and paste with a different texture which feels a little lazy
- A lot of quests are run here, go back here, run here and complete but for the most part the plot carries these annoyances

The bad
- Lighting at the beginning was all off and looked washed out (other complain of this too)
- Far too much running around to get some minor things done, not streamlined at all
- The combat, the controls are so hard to master that I basically turned the difficulty down so that I got plot and ignored the combat which is a real shame as the combat with sword and gun and magic sounds like a great system
- Cannot re-map right click to make the above combat easier, this seems like a rookie mistake

All in all thought I would recommend this game as a time sink if you get it at a discount. Definitely get the De Vespe add on as well as this short story is great insight into the politics of this world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've completed a fairly thorough playthrough, and I thoroughly enjoyed it. But I still wouldn't say I recommend it, broadly.

Greedfall is a gorgeous game that tells a compelling story in a fairly unique universe. The voice acting is impeccable. The scenery and sounds and cutscenes are excellent. If you want a dialogue-based, linear story to follow, this is a fantastic experience.

However, in most of the RPG mechanics, this game falls short significantly. The leveling system is obtuse and narrow, with points being fewer and further between than most games; some skills don't work together (as in, one will literally stop working if you take the other), and generally the 'archetypes' are inflexible. Luckily, it doesn't seem to matter much, since very rarely does having or not having a skill have any effect on the outcome of a mission or conversation. The equipment and customization options are also limited -- i only changed my gear maybe two or three times across 35 levels. Combat is actually decent, but repetitive. It does require a little more attention than most other sword & magic RPGs, and it is easier to die, generally.

But the most limiting factor has to be the linearity. The game takes place in a set of several areas, which are gorgeous and quite large, but ultimately just meandering hallways interspersed with the occasional battle. There are invisible walls everywhere that manifest in different ways that aren't always clear to the player, such as a broken door which you can't interact with (even if you have the right item to fix it!) until you reach a story mission that sends you there. Other times, climbable walls simply refuse to allow you to climb them, or they throw up a random barrier in a pathway that will later disappear, with no indication as to when or how. Even in the large, linear maps they give you, exploration is discouraged if not actively punished. And you will spend a LOT of time just running from fast travel point to fast travel point. Thankfully, they allow the character to sprint infinitely.

Really good as a movie, not great as a game. Would love to see an open-world version with more depth to the character and gear customization.\t\t\t\t\t

If you want a game, with actucal mechanics that intertwine where your actions matter in the world, then this game it's not that. I woudl at least expected to be a nice chracter builder, so you know, play with various builds that make the combat different yet as goos as any. This game is limited there too, the combat is weak. You basically have the same apporaches and effects reagrdless of "build", you have same feeling and same skills and magic just called different or have a different way of making it happen, but feels the same. Liek if it's magic skill or potion is still the same basic magic thing effect. Feels too simple, no depth and same regardless of choice of "build". Oh, and all are based on something consumable. There is no continuity to combat movement, like combos, that you can create or arrange differently. Swing and block or dodge, throw grenade or use magic. I mean, the ingredients are not the problem, as many other games start similarly simple, but the recipe is flawed. Something is missing and i don't like it.
I don't feel a sense of progression either, even if there are levels and skillpoints and all that, the combat seems the same.
So most of your activity is scripted or rinse and repeat, not really much to do in the world, to play with it in a way that is not forced, wich would had been ok if the combat would had been fun at least. I feel that every aspects are lacking.
Tried it long ago and unistalled it. Now tried it again after about one year, to at least finish it once, but i can't really. It's too simple, too bland, too boring. Tried to find a better way, a different build, tried harder difficulties, still bad. Its core desing is where the problem lies i think. And it's not like it doesn't have other issues regarding controls, or movement and animations, the approach on no jumping and the pause system and all, but all that clunkiness can be ignored if the core gameplay, the combat and build variety would had been fine. I play games for gameplay not just for story.\t\t\t\t\t
