\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot going for it, but it has a lot more going against it. The biggest problem of the game is how the quests are implemented, they make you run all over the map to complete a simple side quest like gathering information on someone and after 15 steps of talking with different people the quest simply ends with no resolution. max out reputation with some groups and they still don't trust you, ok.
The combat is a problem, playing with traps and poison is the worst, I have to constantly hunt for ingredients for my traps otherwise I cant use any of my abilities. Monsters have this instant attacks that are impossible to predict or defend if you don't constantly parry. Human move into position to attack, monsters just bite you or roll into you out of nowhere.

The story is so lame, characters motivations are so weird and half the time character just do a 180 to move the story forward. and its long ohh so long, the story is quickly explained in 2 minutes, but having to travel the world twice just to talk with one person takes so long.

Items are interesting but the skill tree isn't ,attributes and talents are okay, but the idea that having different attributes and talents gives you possibility to complete quests in different ways is quite wrong, its more like if you do not have the correct attributes/talents you have to do more steps to complete a quest.

I never advise for games to add ways to fast travel, but this game needs it bad, walking around is not fun, going to cities is not fun, whoever thought it was a good idea to make them into labyrinths is an idiot. Even if I can fast travel from fireplaces and other points, its not enough, let me open the map and teleport from there, by the end of the game walking was a major issue and I could take no more of it. If you want me to go back and forth to do quests at least let me do that ♥♥♥♥ quickly.

All in all the game is not bad, but adds so much ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and time sinks that make it a boring game with not much meat/content to it. Combat is bare, writing is not great, the story is okay but not presented in the best way. And at last the major problem of the game is the voice acting, most native characters sound like they are doing a bad imitation of an accent, some sound Indian, some Russian, some I don't even know, their accent falls off at some points and then it changes, this makes no sense and I fail to see how could it have been so bad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper fun game that i def recommend you get if you are looking for a politically driven open world. i bought this game when it first released and it took me a while to actually sit down and play it after i gave up. The first few hours can give it a bad light just because of the excessive amounts of dialogue and not much combat so ill split my review into pros and cons.

Pros:
The voice acting is the best gaming has to offer. All the voice actors bring so much life to the characters (my favorite being an old man that seems like he is on the za lol). Combat is not amazing but it wasn't boring and could be quite challenging at times. The game had a diverse range of weapons (for melee anyways (will touch on later)) that each brought their own use and cool design. Now ill touch on the skill tree which i found actually really nice and different for an open world game. The nice things about your skills is that they are weighty meaning if you choose one skill you could be missing out on the benefits of another (ex: i needed charisma to convince someone to help me but i had invested my skills into lock picking thus i failed that dialogue but later in the mission instead of doing extra bs to find a key i skipped it and just pick locked the door). The skills are my favorite part of this game that adds uniqeness.

Cons:
As i was mentioning earlier the melee weapons are cool and fun but the magic.......left to be desired. you have a very simple magic tree that doesn't have a very wide range of variety. Its fun and cool at the start but you will find yourself wanting to be a melee fighter most of the time but on the bright side you can always change class mid game. i also will say the choices you make in discussion with the npcs are very fun but it seems your decision always end up with siding with the natives. i wish there was more of a moral grey area between all the factions so that the choices you made would be more conflicting and weigh more.

extra comments:
people are complaining about reused assets but honestly....who gives a flying f$%#. The main reused assets are the buildings but to me that didn't matter cause they weren't the places i was exploring anyways so why care. Also people hated how the skill tree was set up but to me it was great because it really made you choice how you wanted to play. you are forced to pick a fighting style rather than being a jack of all trades.

in conclusion i recommend you get this game and try it if its on sale or you are looking for a new open world game to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, this is a tricky one.

On the good side, the game has both a compelling main and many good side story lines. The game world is really pretty and the graphics hold up well for an ageing game.

However, almost all quests contain a ridiculous amount of running here and there and back and forth and hither and thither and when you think you have run your legs off and come close to the end of the quest it turns out that it was only the first part of three more involving just as much running. The quest design is just really, really poor. This is not helped by the stupid map paths that are long and winding with closed sides and virtually no shortcuts. You can't pass through tree branches, climb tiny slopes or climb over small fallen trees. So not only have you got to run back and forth a lot, you also have to run long ways round every obstacle.

There are also some pretty big flaws in some quests where one stage can't be completed but the game ignores it and lets you finish the questline anyway. only then you are left with the quest stub that can't be completed in your log. Another quest sees you unable to pick a member of your team to accompany you on that member's personal quests (or any other quest) but you can still carry out that quest. Only, when it comes to dialogues the game just pretends that the member is present and speaking.

Less disrupting but still annoying are the several quest dialogues where NPCs you are talking to gets mixed up and refers to the male NPC as she/her and the female NPC as he/him.

Combat is pretty straightforward and unimaginative and higher level mobs and bosses can be a long grind. This game's strength lies in its story and combat is mostly a hindrance to the flow of the plot. Thus I only play the game on the easiest setting. I'm not interested in the combat grind. After all, I'm supposed to play a diplomat.

So, do I recommend it or not? Well, Steam does not allow for ambiguity here. You either recommend it or not. I'm going to go ahead and give it a thumbs up with the proviso that the price is heavily discounted and you have the patience to seemingly waste a lot of time on unnecessary running around and pretty boring fights. If you stick with it you'll have a pretty decent story game.\t\t\t\t\t
