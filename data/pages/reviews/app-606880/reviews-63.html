\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn spite of the game's blundering narrative issues, it's still easy to get hooked into the rhythm of exploring, crafting, brawling, investigating, and interacting with the host of characters and beasts, while getting lost among the beautiful lush greenery of Teer Fradee. Running into more challenging enemies or engaging in boss fights are a particular treat, since it's an opportunity to pit your hard-earned combat abilities against formidable foes. And tucked within the story, as hackneyed as it is, are occasional glimpses of genuine humanity, such as De Sardet's close relationship with their cousin Constantin, who's also the new governor of Teer Fradee.

But ultimately, because Greedfall is so cavalier about its colonialist themes, and because it plays it safe by sticking so closely to the template of open-world RPGs, it doesn't really feel revelatory in any way. Instead, it's content to be just another digital playground--just another world filled with magic, riches, secrets, and monsters for players to shoot and loot at will. I did have fun when I got lost in its familiar RPG loop, but its lack of nuance or innovation prevents it from being truly remarkable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: So, I'm going to do something I rarely do here. I'm going to give this game a 10 despite the fact it's really an 8, because I truly want to support this project and see Greedfall 2. Im only 23 hours in, so I barely have scratched the surface, so I may write more later, or change my opinion. The interface issues need polish, but all my criticisms are minor compared to HOW MUCH FUN I AM HAVING. Thats the bottom line. Calgon take me away. I feel transported to the New World in a 17th Century swashbuckling adventure with magic, like Sinbad the Sailor. What else can I say?

Setting: I LOVE LOVE LOVE this premise. I havent seen a colonial RPG since I cant remember when. The use of the guns are great, and really immersive - BOOM! And the swashbuckling outfits rule. I cant wait to get more into the politics.

Exploration: Similarly, there ALWAYS seems to be another path. There were even maps I completely explored only to realize later I missed something. You need to look carefully and be aware, and the scenery is gorgeously rendered and (mostly) immersive, although yes, as one reviewer said, sometimes creatures are just standing around (as if they are all smoking a cigarette) waiting for an adventurer to show up to attack them, which could have been done JUST slightly better. Other times though, I saw sleeping monsters heaving their chests up and down and found them cute, so its not awful, just not perfect.

Combat: I find it PERFECT personally. I read some reviews where people said it was weak, but I have absolutely no idea what they are talking about. Its better than Witcher 3, IMHO, although I enjoyed that as well. I find it amazing. This is EXACTLY the speed I want to play at. On Normal Difficulty, at 14th level (light armored swashbuckler, dueling pistol and cutlass, using dodge constantly and poison), although getting much easier with level bonuses and better weapons, I can still die or have to use resources (poison or ammo) to get through tough combats. I certainly need to pay attention even on the easier fights. I even tried Hard Difficulty and that DEFINITELY ramps it up. If you want a challenge, try that. Even if you rock this sort of thing, I found I was using more resources to ease some combats on Hard. I cant even imagine Extreme Difficulty, I assume thats Elden Ring masochism. Also, I feel the game would be totally different with different characters (i.e. a mace, not dodging, magic, etc.), so cant wait to replay ;) maybe on Hard.

Dialogue: Some interesting choices, but not quite mass effect level yet. The voice acting is SUPERB. I LOVE LOVE LOVE the English *AND* IRISH and other Gaelic accents, which I rarely hear in games. The natives though throw me off. Not sure what kind of accent they are going for with that ;) Love the factions so far.

Artwork & Graphics : Really nice. Not quite crisp like COD or Battlefront, but the textures are very good on ultra, actually. And the art work is truly amazing. Beautiful countryside and sordid diseased towns. I dont feel ripped off at all, in fact, I feel transported to a Colonial wonderland on a New World.

Character Building: Very interesting so far. Flexible. You can undo mistakes and experiment. So far proceeding at a pace where I feel I need to make meaningful choices with limited resources (choose this when I level up or that?) but I found with clever use of purchases you can ease some of these burdens (getting +1 to certain talents).

Performance: Excellent. My computer was a beast - 7 years ago - and on ultra handles running around in combat with multiple enemies no problem. That said, there is that weird frame rate drop when enter and leave areas in town, to turn a corner too quickly in the city or something, and you do feel like its 1985 or 1995 hardware wise. If that kills it for you, then yeah. Still, did I mention Im running this on ultra on a computer that is 7 years old and its amazing performance??

Interfaces: Hit or miss. I give it a 7. Many things are easy to use (lots of camp sites let you store gear, rest and regain health without potions, get companions, etc.) but when you fast travel although you have your choice of campsites you cant see where they are, so thats not helpful at all. Also, menus are not always intuitive. I cant figure out how they default sort is arranged, its useless. You constantly need to CLICK CLICK CLICK to get it to sort to something useful or more commonly just scrolls on the bad sort since its just faster. Also, if you sort it by name and then switch from Weapon to Armor it resets to the weird useless default. Needs work. A lot. But its not game breaking or annoying enough.

So far, I dont have any memorable gripes other than these - they arent really cons, they are too minor, but they are:

Keyboard Interface: Some keys are partially hardcoded for no reason. For instance, I hotkeyed E to End, i.e. I set End to Interraction but it only partially works - it always does SOME things that the E key is supposed to do, but always doesn't do the other things. For instance you can get people to talk to you with the End key, but you cant use it to quickly sell items like the E key. Even when you delete the E key from any command in the command menu, E key is STILL the hotkey for sell or buy. I got it around it by using my programmable Razor keyboard (making End = E) but its annoying. Really? This is sorta inexcusable and stupid.

Minor Immersion Breaks: Silly things, like the same merchant and arena guy being in every city at the same time. Ok, I know this isnt triple A but really? Also, I can loot crates in villages and coin barracks and no one cares? Maybe because they think Im nobility so I can what I want?

Graphics: The game stutters in town but only when I turn a corner at top speed or open a door into the town square. In all fairness, my computer is 7 years old.

***

Conclusion Overall, must buy. Haven't had this much fun since Elex.
