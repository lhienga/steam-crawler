A simple "recommend/do not recommend" is tricky for this title but: Yes, I recommend this *if* you're a fan of RPGs in general.

Map structure is very similar to Dragon Age: Inquisition, with zone maps that you travel to and explore, not true open world.

Combat is active but not difficult on normal difficulty if you keep up with your equipment.

The quests and story are where this game both shine and fall flat. The lore is interesting, the colonial aesthetic is refreshing. There is plenty of fighting to be had travelling from place to place to complete quests, but the quests themselves are mostly talking and maybe a bit too much backtracking. In addition, some of the NPC sidequests don't fit into the overall narrative very well, with one character in particular referencing things you may not have discovered yet if you're the sort of person who does all the sidequests first.

Overall, it's cheap and fun but it won't change your life and you likely won't hold other games up against it as a gold standard. But if you're in the market to spend a few dozen hours in a new world (see what I did there?) it's definitely worth checking out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not good. I'm an rpg fan and i am starving for more decent ones to play, but even i do not want to bother going through with this. I'm glad i waited for the best sale possible, but i wish i hadn't bought it in the first place.
I try to finish games before I write reviews, this will be one of the exceptions. I'm about ready to drop it and after 21 hours I'm confident that I've seen what the game consists of.

Starting with the 'good'. The setting and timeline is interesting, but they're far from utilizing these to their full extent. The writing and characters ranges from 'ok' to meh, with some twists. The look of the game overall isn't anything amazing, but it's passable. Out of everything, i care about that the least.

Then for the bads; The gameplay.
What little enjoyment could be had from this game is ruined by the gameplay.
The combat is awkward and bare bones and the amount of skills to unlock is minimal to say the least. I made a mage and you can only get a couple of spells for the whole game.
You have your basic attack and special attack, both of which are homing magic missiles.
Dodge ability.
Buff ability.
Stasis, freeze an opponent until it takes damage. A skill mages start with.
AoE stasis, which is the ultimate unlock of mage tree eventually.
And 2 hybrid spells, an aoe attack around yourself in melee range and a heal.
Pretty early on I understood that I will only really be using magic missiles with some dodges when needed. I thought I would try building a melee hybrid mage, so I can use my mana for range attacks and then go melee when i'm regenerating mana. Turns out that is not possible, because you cannot use your mage dodge ability unless you're in your range/mage form. If you swap to a melee weapon the game disables the mage dodge that you've unlocked as well as the mage parry ability which you have by default and feels good to use. Without the dodge you get stun locked or simply die in a few hits.
So, my mage gameplay literally consists of me clicking on my mouse for basic homing magic missiles and dodging when needed. I could use what few mage abilities i can get, but they're largely redundant and cost inefficient.
The dodge ability is is forcibly bound to double press configuration which is really annoying to use.
The enemies can follow you with their attacks even if you DO dodge them.
The boss fights are an absolute chore to get through. They have very limited move sets, but almost one shot you if you're hit and they have a â¥â¥â¥â¥ ton of hp. If the devs didn't have the budget to make a more functional combat system and better enemy move sets and animations, then at least do us the courtesy of not wasting our time with sleep inducing super tanky bosses. You will want to hang yourself if you actually die to one, because you have to start it from the beginning.
Despite going through all of the side quests and exploring the maps thoroughly, i didn't find any mage gear until after 15 hours. I could've bought some, but that's seems a bit unfair considering i'd found tons and tons of swords, maces and guns for free.
The sneak mechanics are infuriatingly bad and buggy. Thankfully there isn't that many stealth sections and they aren't usually very difficult. However, there are other times when they make you tear your hair out.
The quest structure is too often made to waste your time. You're tasked to run back and forth and back and forth and back and forth. Over and over and over and over again. Sometimes i would try take initiative or do things out of order, investigating a room before talking to a dude for example. But whatever i'm supposed to find from said room only appear after i've talked to the dude, even if there is no time at all between them and even if i AND my character already know i will need to investigate the room. So by trying to think for yourself, to explore on your own or streamline quests and save time ends up with you only wasting MORE time.
The conversation system is terrible. You don't always know what your character is going to do even if you're just initiating a conversation. You don't have a lot of options within the conversations and there doesn't seem to be many differing consequences, though as of right now it's hard to say conclusively since i haven't finished the game.

tldr: Overall, if you play it, the game will become a terrible chore sooner rather than later. It's not as if it was doomed completely, but the core gameplay loops severely ruin what the game had going for it.\t\t\t\t\t

This is a very interesting game about being a diplomat for a Trading nation as your nation settles in a new colony. There are many continental (Euro) powers and then various factions of native tribes all with their own goals, rivalries and relationships with one another. Your goal is to settle disputes and secure your nations success whether that be through peaceful dialog or just killing everybody and you can't please every faction.

It can be a little rough around the edges at times, but nothing that noticable. I'll take a flawed game with some actual creativity and soul over so many of these generic AAA games that have perfected the art of bland inoffensive drivel.\t\t\t\t\t
