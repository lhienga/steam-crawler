The levels are small and branching linear - this is absolutely not an open world where you can explore and discover. The character writing and dialog is way over the top and on the nose. I can tell the developers tried, but it just suffers from a huge lack of good design and vision, and clearly a lack of time to implement great mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t71 hours of good gaming. This is much like the game Dragon Age. You can have up to 2 AI companions that fight with you. You meet the characters as you play and they join you. You equip them, but everything else is automatic. You can pause the fight and plan some tactics, I like that.
You develop your character as you level up, choosing what skills to invest in based on your fighting style. You can be melee or caster. I chose a bit of both. I leveled up to level 40. I dont think there is a max.
There is a main quest line as well as side quests and I did them all, including the De Vespe DLC. I think the DLC is worth buying.
Graphics are quite good and the sound is too. Plays great on ultra wide screen. I used a 3440x1440 monitor. The picture is not stretched out! Controls in the game are good and typical.
You have a map to use that you uncover as you adventure, so it is blank in the beginning. The world is large and there is lots to do.
If you like games like Gothic and Dragon Age then this is a must buy IMO. There is a narrative at the end of the game that changes based on your choices during the game. There are choices you make through parts of the game that influence that, and its not all just one big decision at the end. There is a big one at the end, but its deeper than that. The story is quite good I think and the voice acting is great IMO.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought it on sale, only cared about the core game, so no DLC experience.

The game has decent voice acting, sound design, graphics and RPG mechanics. The story is predictible, but interesting enough. I played with other Spider games it "clones" the standard BioWare formula well. This game feels AAA, even though it comes from a relatively small though experienced studio (their other games are very good as well, with unique worlds good story and decent RPG mechanics). It has some clunkyness and some minor bugs to it, which seems to be staple with the engine they use. The formula of magic vs science is a bit mixed up here, but the general tone is overused already obviously. The main character is customizable (and not gender locked) which are big plusses in replayability and accessibility. The mechanic revolves around around a mixture of warrior/tech/mage tree that is freely customizable, but in the end worth sticking to a build. It can be changed midgame too which is a very nice touch. I think all three have a very good distinct feel to it. The guns are powerful and have a very nice feel to them, but need alchemy. Combat is decent enough to make the combat tree interesting. Mages are useful and good though it is more of a support role here (crowd control, heal). I would say their main focus is tank/damage/support -> warrior/tech/mage.

I can safely recommend it to anyone who likes the genre and wants a new story.

Completionist run on the highest difficulty with all side quests, without DLC took 64 hours, final level without grinding was 39. I don't think it is very replayable as there are choices, but it is mostly good or bad decisions so if you get it right at first (it is easy to do so) you have seen everything.\t\t\t\t\t
