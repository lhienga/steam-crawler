\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOWNS:
- Instance based. I believed it was a seemless exploration game of an island. Instead it has sizeable regions you have to quicktravel to. So the isle is much smaller than I hoped.
- 100 Characters that all sound the same as they hired less than a handfull of voice actors.
- God aweful recycled interiors (of buildings). They all look the same cause they all are the same. Despite the 3 cities comming from 3 distinctively different cultures which you can see on the outside of the buildings.
- Many small alternative paths to completing a quest (charming NPC, blow a wall, hidden path, stealth, pick a lock etc) that are all very unapealing as the violent solution will always reward you with vastly more XP and gold!
- Conversational crossing and broken story. By which i mean, if you complete the quests in different order than what is considered 'ideal' by the developer (especially concerning the vespe DLC) some of the NPC will have weird conversations and reactions from violent to instant "kiss your boot" attitudes.
- You can specialise (with a full playthrough) in 2 of the 3 specialty branches as a character (warrior,rogue,wizard). Wizard is however grosely OP as it is a ranged fighter and unlike rogue requires no resources to use skills (just mana). So all you do is kite every enemy you encounter.

UPS:
- Breathtaking sceneries, characters, clothing and overal graphics. Real eye candy imo!
- Nice story with a good twist, though predictable to a certain degree and sometimes requires large amount of suspence of disbelief concerning NPC behaviour. (eg: A few empty words will make an NPC renounce his faith. I mean come on really? It's like James T. Kirk hacking the Kobayashi Maru at times).
- Lovely and straightforward crafting system with graphical effects for the armor and weapons.
- Very interesting, though sometimes counter intuitive, skill trees
- Very distinctly different team members with their own personalities and skills you really notice a different in and out of combat! (as opposed to some games where comrades are all the same useless or completely OP)
- Great voice acting and fully voiced! (despite there being only a handful of actors)
- Good boss fights
- Good combat system in theory, though parry needs to be revamped as it doesnt work half the time.
- Good amount of content worth 60-100 hours on higher difficulties.
- A nice variety of weapons and stats the game feels like a single player MMO at times. As a matter of fact I believe this team would ace the making of a grand MMORPG if they could raise the funds.
- Has a decent amount of replayability to explore alternate choices and options. Though only a die hard will see it through a second time I suspect.

Verdict:
- Though the developer clearly cut corners or had a limited budget, and the fact the game was developed for the toyboxes instead of an actual computer, the money that has been spent went into all the right places making the game enjoyable and worth buying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNearly 60 hours in, and I'm still not sure exactly how I feel about GreedFall.

On the one hand, the game is riddled with flaws. It's mechanics are dated, reminiscent of Dragon Age: Origins or Mass Effect, both of which predate GreedFall by more than a decade; it's filled with bugs, some of which softlock players out of specific achievements or quest outcomes; the dialogue is poorly-written, often losing track of who it refers to, veering entirely away from the subtitles, or displaying the captions in French - the list goes on.

Despite that, the game does have an odd element of charm. The core storyline is a cosy, enjoyable narrative with a clumsy, but ultimately decent conclusion. Likewise, while the sidequests and romance sub-plots can lack depth in places, they're not overly demanding, and the player isn't left feeling cheated at their conclusion. There are several routes to most quests (some of which are, admittedly, utterly ridiculous) and the central character builds (warrior, war mage and gun-toting rogue) are satisfying to play, if somewhat easy to cheese.

All in all, GreedFall gets a recommendation from me, even if it's ultimately not a perfect game. It's worth coming back to, and that counts for something.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGenuinely excellent game, happy about my 40hrs I put into it. The graphics are gorgeous, and I find the characters to be generally interesting, and storyline engaging (though I wouldn't say that it was exciting all of the time. The beauty of everything made me not mind the slow bits, however). I think the worldbuilding is quite strong, and I could really get a sense for what each nation was like, the general temperment of their culture, etc. By the end, I was left feeling that I would very much enjoy a sequel, to see what happens afterward.

A quick note about the combat, however: it's pretty much the same throughout the game. It gets a bit more interesting later, sure (more abilities unlocked, etc.), but mostly it's the same. That might be because I used magic, but since there isn't really a lot of variety in the enemies, I'm inclined to my playstyle wasn't the issue. The bosses, especially, are more or less the same set of 4-ish the entire time. I was left feeling like the combat was there, and it was fine, and didn't detract from the rest of the experience. I'm not convinced it added much, though. I suppose I could start again from the beginning at a higher difficulty, using a different play style, sure... but I'm not sure I want to play more of the game *that* much.

Overall, good game, lovely story and visuals, happy I played it. If you're looking for amazing combat and many and varied boss fights, you should probably look elsewhere. If that is less important to you, you'll probably have a great time here. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedFall shines when it comes to story and roleplaying. There are so many branching paths for quests, both main and for your allies' personal quests that even if you miss them on the first playthrough, it gives you an excuse to go back and play again to see how you can change the world of the game. The mechanics also blend spectacularly with the roleplaying and exploration, allowing for any desired outcome if you're strategic about how you allot your points to build your De Sardet player character.

The characters are fantastic and well-designed, making you wary about how you might negotiate decisions with them and their respective factions. Vasco and Síora were my main companions, though each of them has something to offer and are great "middle-men" for each of the factions in the game. Each of the factions you either love or love to hate, which is perfect for any playstyle you're going for. Thinking about doing a Solomon Kane witch-hunter build? You can do that. Want to have a curious scientist and expert at flintlock tech? Yup. Or maybe you want to go native and immerse yourself among the tribes of Tír Fradí. Go for it! (That's what I went for).

It's also a breath of fresh air to have a fantasy game that's not based on the middle ages, but is also not a complete one-for-one of irl colonial Europe. The world of Tír Fradí is mysterious and alien, with a fascinating culture and language put together by dedicated designers and linguists. I spoke with Antoine Henri recently, the creator of the Yecht Fradí language, and his knowledge on Proto-Celtic (which the language is based on) is spectacular.

I sincerely hope Spiders does more with this IP, it's by far one of the best roleplaying games I've played in my life. Hopefully we could get a sequel or an even bigger DLC than "The De Vespe Conspiracy". Were Spiders ever to do a crowdfund or pre-release campaign for another game in the GreedFall universe, they have my full support. Spread the word on this game and don't sleep on this sleeper hit!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite originally being put off by mixed reviews upon release, I am glad that I have given Greedfall a chance. It has pleasantly surprised me. The characters are interesting enough for you to want to know more, but the main star is the island and its mysteries. As a side-quester I do not believe I have gotten too far into the main storyline and I have been playing for 24 hours in total (possibly 4 of those have been on pause while life interrupted), so there's plenty to do, see and explore. The only downside for me is the camera controls, which feel just a bit too slow to catch up, and are a bit of a pain in combat (although the combat live pause, reminscent of Star Wars KOTR, has helped with that). I'll probably do a full review once I've finished the game.\t\t\t\t\t
