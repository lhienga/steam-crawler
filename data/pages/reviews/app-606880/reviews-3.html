\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a traditional adventure romp with some genuinely interesting missions and locations. The graphics are very good and the voice acting is well above average. There's lots of loot to find, albeit all of a similar type, the over-arching story is good and, IMO, it's worth the asking price.

There are all the usual problems associated with an open-world game. Although you can choose the order in which you do the missions, you have to proceed to solve each mission step by step. You can't go straight to the solution, even though you might have guessed it. For example, you could find an (empty) locked prison cell and an empty desk, but after having accepted a mission to free a prisoner, the cell suddenly contains a prisoner, and the keys to the cell can be found in the desk that was empty a few seconds before. You might see an unusual plant and want to harvest it for later - but you can't - not until you perform the mission that requires it.

Also there are the usual movement restrictions that keep you within the confines of the game world. So, although you just scaled a ten foot fence you find yourself unable to step over a small rock (you can't jump - I spent the first few minutes of exploration frantically pressing the space bar to jump and it just pauses the game). And although you can proceed to an area by blasting your way through a weak stone wall, at other times a fallen tree blocks your path. But the answer to all of these 'problems' is "its a game". The barriers are there and you just have to accept them. If you can do that, this game will keep you entertained for many hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't finished this just yet, but I am having a grand time still nearly 20 hours in. If you enjoy the setting of Colonialpunk, and you enjoy the second wave of Bioware RPGs (Jade Empire, Mass Effect Trilogy, Dragon Age Trilogy), you will probably like this too! This is a AA studio punching above their weight and still being able to stand on their feet. This game has a soul, which so many games fail to have. The clear love that the studio has for the RPG craft for a decade and a half now shows. If you can handle some jank, there is clear passion in this game. From the lore to the characters to the world to the choices to the whole feel of it. This is one of the best third person RPGs to come out since the mid 2010s. And easily the best presentation of the Colonialpunk genre in a video game. If you are a fan of the diplomat role in fantasy/historical stories, this is also a dream come true, it pulls it off even better than Dragon Age: Inquisition's Inquisitor role.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory is ok-ish. At first you're introduced to a vast new world with interesting lore, you get to meet various new characters and learn their backstory, you get to diffuse various conflicts between 3 clans, fight different creatures and (somewhat) learn about the island's secret. But all of these falls off in the later half of the story. Ending will make you ask "wait, that's it?". It's almost like they lost all passion during the development.

Game play is...ugh. It runs fine on low-spec devices. But there are numerous bugs (from simple grammatical mistakes to NPCs getting bugged out). Some are even game-breaking bugs. And the fighting is....weird. Sometimes enemies will aggro on you while ignoring it's closest enemies. Sometimes traps wont activate and your enemies will just walk over it. Sometimes your character will fire off magic/shot to an entirely different direction. It definitely will take some time to get adjusted to.

All in all, it's a semi-decent rpg that's recommended only at sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe developers of Greedfall have undeniably showcased their prowess in crafting an immersive and complex story set within a richly detailed world. However, it's important to note that while these aspects shine, other elements of the game do not reach the same heights of excellence. For those unfamiliar, Greedfall epitomizes what is known as an "AA" game. This classification signifies a title that aspires to the ambition and scope of AAA games but is developed with more modest resources.

Greedfall is a game that offers a lot to the player, but it also falls short of its ambitious goals in several areas. The crafting system is present, yet it often feels inconsequential to the overall gameplay experience. Exploration is encouraged, but players will frequently encounter invisible walls that abruptly limit their freedom to roam. The game promises meaningful choices, yet the narrative feels somewhat predetermined. Moreover, while the gameplay mechanics are there, they can feel somewhat underdeveloped.

However, it's not all criticism for Greedfall. The game excels in its storytelling, character development, and the atmosphere it creates. These strengths form the core of the game's appeal. For players who value a compelling narrative and engaging characters over deep gameplay mechanics, Greedfall offers a rewarding experience. It draws favorable comparisons to games like Technomancer, Gothic, and the first two Witcher titles. Fans of these games will likely find much to appreciate in Greedfall's similar blend of storytelling and role-playing.

In summary, Greedfall is a mixed bag. Its story, characters, and world-building are of high quality, capturing the player's imagination and drawing them into its world. However, the gameplay can feel grindy and somewhat monotonous, potentially detracting from the overall experience. The game won't appeal to everyone, especially those who prioritize gameplay depth and variety. But for those who can overlook these gameplay shortcomings in favor of story and atmosphere, Greedfall offers a rich and engaging adventure reminiscent of earlier classics in the genre. In essence, while it might not fulfill every expectation, Greedfall stands as a testament to what can be achieved with passion and creativity, even if it doesn't have the budget or technology of a AAA title.\t\t\t\t\t
