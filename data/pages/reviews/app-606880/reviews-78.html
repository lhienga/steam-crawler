\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay is quite solid, and the combat is quick and well varied with a nice range of abilities and methods of play to try out a variety of options. The writing is pretty good and the story is quite strong, with some solid voice acting as well as some very nice cinematics sprinkled in. I really loved how intertwined some of the stories and missions are, and how they might change rather dramatically depending on the order you do them in or your choices in some of them. The visuals are quite nice too, and the game does a really nice job of setting up its aesthetics and own style that makes the world feel fairly fresh, when it comes to fantasy rpgs. Plus it runs quite smoothly, even on my older pc with pretty much no lag or stuttering.

As for issues, the systems are very varied, but they are not terribly deep-each ability pool only has a few abilities for each, but as combat won't last too long and can be managed rather easily as you figure it out, this isn't so much of a problem as you don't really need it to be super complex. There are also a fair number of bugs, usually due to the varied options you have when it comes to completing missions, but they're nothing gamebreaking and are typically very minor. The only other issue I really had was that the subtitles are often misspelled or use the wrong grammar, but considering it's a French studio having to translate it all into English, and knowing the pain in the keister English can be, it's a minor bother that I could have just as easily fixed by turning them off.

Overall, I'd definitely recommend it; it's a bit rough around the edges, but it's a fun and intriguing game with some great gameplay elements and excellent writing. I cannot comment on the DLC as I haven't played it (yet), but the base game alone is a great time and I got a lot out of it!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game presents 3 different paths for you to spec in and I opted to go the Mage route and played on Normal difficulty. The first act is an extended tutorial to understand the basics of the game, combat and a couple companions are introduced with an awesome boss fight before sailing to the island of Teer Fradee. Unfortunately, this is where the game started taking a downward turn for me.
The second act is designed to introduce the character to the key factions throughout the island, but the quick travel forces a 'travel pause' every single time you move, and it quickly devolves into a walking simulator in the same jungle biome fighting a handful of monsters every couple hundred meters. The kicker here though is by the time I was level 15, all of my encounters were over in a matter of 2 or 3 spells with exception to boss fights, and to my dismay (level 31 at the writing of this review) I would have the best equipment for a mage build for the rest of the game. With the addition of skill alters in the environment to find, I had maxed out the entire mage tree by about level 24. I find it extremely funny that the game actually has at least 2 different cut scenes telling you that you're journey is far from over and to be patient. But when the challenge is gone by the half way point, and the music is bland and repetitive, and your companions won't shut up about you being low and needing to take a potion every 3 seconds, this game has now become a chore to finish. At the time of writing this, I think I'm at the preparation stage for the climax and it's such a dreadfully boring process that I actually needed to pause and warn off others that would consider this game.

Key Whining: item progression and variation are lack luster; music is slow and grinds my nerves every time I'm in the governors offices; combat needed more spells (mages had 2 defensive spells and 3 offensive spells, one fury, one auto and one parry); quick travel only done at camps instead of menu, leading to A LOT of walking; barely any variety between environments and clans. For those complaining about increasing difficulty, making an enemy a bullet sponge doesn't make the combat any less stale when it's only a bat, wolf, crocodile or human solider variation.\t\t\t\t\t
