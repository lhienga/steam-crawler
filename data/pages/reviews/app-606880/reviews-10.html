To whoever reads my review/comment, please do consider just spamming your gun and keeping distance. It's pretty much what I call meta in my opinion. Moving on from my gameplay, it's a little unfortunate that there weren't any options for auto walking/running, or I may have missed it in the game controls. Furthermore, the distances per location in the map isn't too bad, averaging around 400-700m per travel spawn to the other side of the location.

I do recommend exploring the place and looting whatever you find marked or indicated in your field of view. Doing that allowed me to end up with countless materials and craft a lot of items that I didn't end up really using. But who knows? You may need certain potions for certain circumstances. From what I remember, I believe I ended up hoarding 400 rounds for my rifle and over a hundred health potions. The resources to craft these items aren't difficult to find, quite literally scattered all over the place.

Another thing to mention is always make sure you are also upgrading your companions' / party members' gear. It will help you in the long run and won't require you to focus on their HP mid battle. Though I do suggest that you craft / acquire some resurrection powder or something of the sorts, it will help you when your allies have fallen. And another thing is...ABUSE the battle pause menu. Give yourself some time to cool down every battle so you always know what you are doing and aren't just spamming your weapon in hopes it kills the enemies. Trust me, I've learnt that the hard way.

With that in mind, make sure you are also using potions to your advantage. And also activate any spells before getting into battle. I, for one, always use my shield spell before every boss fight as it does increase the amount of shields you have for a certain duration. Another thing to note is that be aware of what spells you have activated and how long they will last for, same thing goes for potions and other effects. I sometimes overestimate that duration of potions and spells, which lead me to death a few times.



A fair warning to those who cares about appearances...The Naut tattoo you get from doing Vasco's requests doesn't look too pretty. It looks like a beard and there are no other patterns.

[END OF SPOILER]

I could go on and on about this game and its' story but I believe I have said enough as my fingers are getting a little tired from typing this entire commentary. But now we reach my conclusion..With everything I've said and mentioned, there are quite a handful of things you can do and complete in this game, but I was too lazy to do any of the other side quests and ventured on with the main story. I did manage to do a bit of Petrus's quests, but completed both Siora's and Vasco's stories. As for Aphra, I was too lazy to start anew and simply left it at that.

Greedfall is something I would recommend to those who seek a new open world RPG game and is interested in politics, public relations and decision making. As this game covers all of that.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this game isn't triple A and I'm totally pleased it isn't. When Triple A releases something as unrewarding as Starfield and it makes some of these overlooked and underappreciated gems from the past 3-5 years actually look pretty damn good, you know something is wrong with the industry. Yes, this isn't GOTY material, in fact I wouldn't even give it a 9 out out 10. Its a solid 8 out of 10 RPG that highlights how Spiders keeps improving with each and every title they release. Its inevitable that they come out with a banger here eventually, if you've played their games from the last decade you know that each game is a flawed but always progressing experience that started out with titles that were a chore, gradually became slightly better than "meh", to wow this is better than expected until now finally I'm actually excited for GreedFall 2 to release.

This studio learns from its mistakes and has been gradually growing and adding to its skillset with each title that it releases. I can't wait for GreedFall 2 and if you find this game on sale, definitely give it a shot. These guys will never be Larian but I could definitely see them shaming scrubs from Bethesda in the near future. For $20 or less, this game is a steal but I'd definitely pay no more than $30. Lets see if GreedFall 2 finally hits that $50 quality mark that Spiders is so close to achieving. Have fun and enjoy the entertaining adventure with a unique twist on party based RPGs.\t\t\t\t\t
