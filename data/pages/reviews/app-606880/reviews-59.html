\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR
7/10 Buy on sale. Fun enough. Last third drags.

Fun combat, beautiful world, and an entertaining enough story. The natives are annoying but I got numb to it after a while. The different factions are all unique and create an interesting world where things are drawn on very black and white lines. The Bridge Alliance is all about science and the pursuit of knowledge at all costs. Theleme on the other hand is a theocratic nation known for their heretic burning Inquisitors. Big old world Catholic Church vibes. The Nauts are sailors and run ALL the sailing... no one can sail without hiring a Naut to do it... and armies? Why, the Coin Guard are the monolithic mercenary guild that will work for whoever hires them. Your character, De Sardet, is with the Congregation of Merchants, the folks in the middle between all the other factions and in charge of trading.

The game has a faction system, Natives, Theleme, Alliance, Nauts, and the Congregation. Every story action you make will affect your reputation with said faction. Certain quest elements are unavailable if you have a poor faction standing, however this can be overcome sometimes with Perception or Charisma dialog options.

The game starts off strong with being on "the Continent" and preparing to set out for the "newly" discovered island of Teer Fradi. After some tutorial missions and a boss fight you end up on the island. From there you set out to explore, scour the land of all resources while murdering every animal you meet. All while traveling between factions and solving their problems.

The game is pretty strong for the first half of the game. The last 3rd of the game felt rushed and the ending is ultimately not that satisfying. The world they created though wants me to revisit it. Teer Fradi is beautifully crafted, so much in fact that other elements feel.. neglected.

There are some immersion breaking bugs; NPC's getting stuck on geometry and walking into nothing forever... people popping in out of nowhere, people getting stuck in walls.... that kind of stuff.

The game IS fun to play and DOES have its moments where it shines. Melee combat, guns, and magic all have their balances and different uses. (GUNS FTW!!) The overall feel I get of the game is that the developer had to finish it before it was... finished. I wouldn't mind seeing more in this world from this developer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t46 hours and i have completed all in game quests (aside from finding all the professors notes) and the DLC. With that frame of reference I will provide a short readable review:

Pros
*Mass Effect-esque gameplay with dialog choices which often have actual different reputation effects (verified on save games)
*Adequate quantity of side quests without feeling overwhelming
*Ability to realistically complete all side quests during main quest line
*Very nice graphics. Played on highest settings and enjoyed the visuals
*Absolutely no difficulty obtaining materials for anything you would want to do
*Actual variety in character archetypes which are fun to experiment with

Cons:
*Loot system does not feel very rewarding. You always have an overabundance of everything and often a legendary weapon drops or is found which you either cant use due to extreeme requirements, or is a duplicate of what you are wielding with a different skin.
*Legendary weapons cannot have modifications like every other weapon, and their only advantage is secondary stats. Primary stats are often weaker than modified epic or even rare weapons.
*Lots of quests making you walk back and forth long distances in areas with no fast travel. It feels very un-necessary, especially when at other times a quest will just teleport you to the end after completion.
*combat with bosses feels pretty repetitious, but the combat is decent and if you get into using traps, coatings, bombs, etc you can really liven it up. So maybe a neutral.

That's about it. The storyline is really great, especially if you have avoided internet spoilers. It is hard to google any help sites on this game that dont somehow ruin the ending (especially if you google choice consequences). The setting feels fresh in a world of Gun combat or medieval games, and the companion characters are well done. The voice acting is superb, especially on main characters. Honestly one of the best examples of believable voice acting I have played in memory. Period. The setting is well rendered down to small details and really made the world feel rich. The abilities you can choose between for your character actually made the choices tough in some areas (other than talents, you dont get much choice there... straight lines) but you can carry a variety of chest armor which you can craft bonuses into.

If you like an RPG, get this game. Especially on a sale, but It is worth full price.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis falls into category of: You'll like/love it or you wont and will drop it fairly soon in game.

Personally I found Greedfall to be a solid RPG. Story is good over all weaved in various missions/stories of
issues that you would expected in colonial age, lowish-magic world to have. Which is why missions seem
interesting and most of stories engaging (for people that are into this kind of story/setting). All that is
accompanied with good and not over the top characters with good voice acting. Game takes something
around 55-60h+- complete in standard difficultly with most of things done.

Combat is more action oriented with which fits the game well. Combat complexity is not high.
And feels really close to Witcher 2/3. Mentioning this similarity in controls. Same as that game (IMO)
it's worth switching over to controller. Just cause of that went back to originally owned PS copy.
Which also now includes free PS5 upgrade for a bit better graphics/performance.

I really hope that recently announced sequel Greedfall 2 will be equally good or better.

Some notes:
* Camera sway can be disabled now in options. (wasn't so on launch sadly...)
* Best enjoyed with controller.
