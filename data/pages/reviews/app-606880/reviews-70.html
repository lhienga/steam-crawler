\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was wondering why nobody on my friends list had not finished this; now I know why! The game gets extremely repetitive and dumb very fast after an extremely promising start. At first the game seems to be the most generic RPG, but this is not necessarily a bad thing as the game is very enjoyable at the beginning. However, it soon drizzles away with poor combat and mechanics.

Most games of this genre either have a set character that allows for a more in depth story telling experience or a character creation which allows for more customization and role-playing. This game has a set character who acts like a blank slate and is a complete moron without a personality. The companions are paper bags when compared to those of Dragon Age or Dungeons of Naheulbeuk.

The beautiful aesthetic of the game is wasted by the rest of what it provides which is a huge shame. I do not recommend getting this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIgnore what "reviewers" say about the game on the internet and do nothing but beat the game into obscurity.

Despite the wonky moments that pepper the game (resulting in a few bugs or glitches), Greedfall is a great experience for people that want to experience an RPG that wasn't polished to a respectable shine.
The story is engaging and the world you can explore is sizable, allowing you to ocasionaly feast your eyes on beautiful scenery.
Characters are diverse and each have their own unique tone to them. They also bring various advantages to fights and sometimes can even outperform the main character in combat.
Combat is difficult and semi-realistic, but as long as you master your dodges and properly upgrade your armor then combat can be fun and sought after. Enemies scale with the main character meaning you will probably never be at a lack of a challenge during encounters with packs of enemies or beasts.
Talent systems enable you to built up your character however you want, making you feel powerful in every encounter. You want to shoot guns and wave magic at the same time? Yes you can. You want to use heavy armor and use big weapons while throwing poison flasks around? Yes you can.

If you are an RPG fan, you like to custom build your class and like stories that revolve around intrigue, then you should give this game a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot my favorite game. It started off interesting enough, and I enjoyed that there are multiple ways to do things. But after about 8 hours, the game started to get repetitive. Lots of quests have you just aimlessly running back and forth between locations with no way to fast travel. When you do get to fast travel, you go through this weird campfire in-between zone with a merchant which is just bizarre. It's not open-world, so there are very defined paths that they want you to take, but it makes the landscape completely unrealistic because you are just walking along paths with high canyon walls all around you. And lastly, there are tons of dead ends in the games. No reward for exploration - you might spend 5 minutes exploring a corner of a map, and there will be literally nothing there.

It's the first time I've played an "open-world" RPG and not enjoyed it in a long time! Maybe I'm spoiled. But for being a relatively new game, I think it has a LOT to improve on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I started with this game, invisible walls and lack of any jump put me off for a bit...then I started delving deeper into it and I haven't been able to stop playing since.
Story is excellent, quest design has yet to show any simple quest that would not have some decision/twist in it and voice acting is amazing. Those are the things that keep me hooked to this game and going over every single side-quest before I go on to finish main story.
However, if you expect some amazing combat features (I agree it looks flashy in trailers), you will be disappointed - playing on hard difficulty and while at the beginning I was dying from time to time, now it is just press aoe spell button twice and group of enemies is dead, dodge has no cooldown so you can spam it all day long and yeah, it is servicable at best.
Despite this, I would recommend this to any story-driven rpg fan out there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedFall is the story of the scion of a waning family, our protagonist, whose family uses the last of their influence to garner her a low-level diplomatic position. This position requires her to travel to an island whereupon a few governments connive for resources and rule. In the midst of this political turmoil, our protagonist goes on a quest to kill all of the feral dogs on the island.

The feral dogs are well animated, and there are a lot. It seems like every few minutes the player is called upon to use the character's abilities to murder feral dogs. Killing feral dogs earns the character combat experience, which unlocks new abilities and tactics to utilize in solving the island's feral dog problem.\t\t\t\t\t
