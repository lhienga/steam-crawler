\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedFall is a wonderful game to play and experience at any pace. The story is good and engaging and is in my opinion the best part of the game. The dialogue and voice acting is great and the characters are interesting and likeable. Your decisions and choices you make as you build your character with your skills, attributes, and talents create a well-balanced combat system that is fun and creates for multiple different builds and playstyles that suite whatever way you want to play the game. The element of decision and choice having significance is what makes an RPG game feel rewarding and worth while to put your time into. The choices you make also affect the outcome of the story and your reputation with the factions wtihin the world of the game. Creating a good foundation for replayability for the game. The only negative things I can say about this game is the lack of mob variety, lack of interior building design variety, lack of armor/clothing set variety. If you want to give this game a shot I would recommend picking it up on a sale if you can, but the game is well worth the money for the game play and experience that it offers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tyeah... well... i wanted to (and did) play the game until the end, so i guess i would recommend it. The main story line was interesting. But there are some but’s.

-\tNice world. But exploring it is pretty much a waste of time. If there is a place, some mission will send you there anyway. No need to visit it beforehand. Most special equipment is connected with some quest too.
-\tNice/interesting side missions. But honestly, most of them feel like a walk simulator, even more if you explored the places you have to visit already. Sometimes there is a “bring me to” option, sometimes you walk the same way back you came from, which adds nothing to the game.
-\tGood comrade missions. But honestly, don’t expect your comrades to be something “deep”. And even worse: they hardly talk. I often switched them to get the “logical” set up for a mission, but pretty often they said/added nothing.
-\tCombat. Well, if you are on keyboard+mouse and want to use m4+m5: it doesn’t work (used a keybinder sw to bypass that, still…). Dodging is also a bit annoying, resulting in close combat to be … to much of a pain (for me!). so I went magic. Which honestly was rather a boring gameplay.
-\tWhat REALLY annoyed me was: I am normally playing a “good guy” (well, good girl this time, but duh), but this game is to much. This game offers “playing the game as 100% good guy” and “playing the game wrong”. But it also often doesn’t make sense. Most of the times you can’t even choose how to deal with the bad people. At the beginning it made sense that you cannot just “kill” them (your role is ambassador after all), but the longer this keeps on the more annoying it gets. You kill like 50 people, just to spare the guy at the top? After all they did? And if the game letz you choose for once to kill one of the mass murderers: Hugh reputation lose, sometimes so much, that the perfect-good endling isn’t even possible anymore.
-\tIn general there are often situations in missions where you would prefer to choose yourself how do deal with it, but the game doesn’t allow it. It did annoy me at the beginning, but I kinda accepted it at some point.

Still… overall, enjoyed it, cause the story was interesting and the ending closed the game. so i recommend it.
even more if you are the good guy type of player, don't explore randomly, use a controler and don't mind the lack of choice. :P\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game that displays no understanding of its components. Something far less than the sum of its parts. And those parts are hamfistedly stolen from other, more competent, executions of the genre (mostly the Witcher 3). It smells of the passionless exploitation of a trend that had already become stale when it was released. Why bother?

Every way of engaging in this game is extremely shallow. Combat is floaty at best, itemization is boring, and it would be better if character customization just didn't exist. Exploration suffers from the need to motivate your garbage character choices. You can fight the same three basic enemies over and over again on your way to the same skill point shrine copied and pasted all over the place. You can engage in fetch quests to gain experience to get more skill points unlock more uniteresting elements of your character. And the story is the worst. Of all topics that deserve a light touch, colonialism is probably one at the forefront. None of that here. Kill everyone or don't. The difference what cutscene you get at the end.

I come back to the question: Why was this made? It says little, it innovates none, and it can't even manage to be interesting in its failure. I eagerly await the sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
