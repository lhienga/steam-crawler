\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYet another game where I really need a neutral option instead of just yes or no. If you kinda like RPGs, look elsewhere. This game will just frustrate you. If you love RPGs enough that you're willing to overlook quite a few shortcomings, the game offers a unique setting and some real fun.

What sort of shortcomings am I talking about? Well, first off, nothing game breaking. The game works just fine and the save system is pretty good. The problems are just stuff that get annoying like:
  • Tons of running between quest objectives.
  • Pretty weak combat. My mage started with two abilities. By the end of the game, he had 4 abilities and a way to make one of the originals affect an area. Enemies are original, but there aren't many variants.
  • Easily cheesed combat. If you're starting to lose, just get out of range.
  • Cheap interiors. The island has three palaces. All three have the same layout, though the foreign palaces sometimes block doors. The outdoors (where you spend most your time) is nice and varied, so I've got no idea why they copy/pasted building interiors all over the game.
  • Your wanderings will lead to finding various places where quests will take place later. Those places will be strangely empty until the quest starts. Then, all of the sudden, there's people there that have supposedly been there for months!
  • Seriously, LOTS AND LOTS of running! For instance, in one quest you find people working an unsafe mine. You need to check the deed to the land, so you run back to the campfire, fast travel to your home, run into the palace, and run up to the very top floor to talk with the minister. He has you run down to the basement to get the deed, then run back up to the top floor to read the deed with him. Oh, turns out he doesn't understand it all that clearly, so run back outside to the noble district to chat with the person who wrote the deed for clarification, then back to the top of the palace to get an official notice that the deed is no longer valid, then back to your house's fast travel point, then back to the mine! Points for realism, but sheeesh!!
I did give this an up vote, though, and for good reason. If you can get past the cheapness of it all, the game is fun! The quests may make you run around a lot, but they are interesting quests, not "go hunt 10 bats" junk. The overall story is good, too. All the factions have good and bad people in them (this is not a game where the natives are saints and the colonials are devils). The outdoor landscapes are quite beautiful, too, so at least you're running around in nice places. I was looking forward to playing the game right up to the end.

I didn't get the DLC. It looks kinda pointless - the game already has more than enough content.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an enjoyable game worth playing for an RPG lover. It features three versatile classes which each share a skill tree at different starting points. You can use one or two-handed heavy or bladed weapons, handguns, rifles, or a magic ring. It is an action battle system with a tactical pause most similar to Dragon Age Inquisition. There is a crafting system for improving your arms and armor, making potions, or quest-related items from materials you acquire through exploring or from merchants. The game features six companions and six factions and tracks your relationship with each. I'm not sure what max level is but I was level 35 at the end of the game, having completed the majority of all quests. You can create your own character, but your background is fixed.

Likes:
-Interesting companions w/ optional romance
-Interesting story full of political drama
-Easy, intuitive controls and battle system
-Historical inspirations w/ realistic armor and weapons
-Great dialogue
-Mod Support

Dislikes:
-Many aspects of the story are predictable
-Restrictive fast travel system (but there's a mod for that)
-Most locations all look the same
-Despite the nautical themes, nothing at all takes place on a boat
-Confusing maps and level designs will give you a headache\t\t\t\t\t
