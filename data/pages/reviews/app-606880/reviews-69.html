\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did not finish the game, I was just too bored.

Awful camera, clunky movement, clunky combat and most importantly bland and boring story and characters.

The world should feel large, open and exciting, instead it feels tedious and underwhelming. The visuals are great, but the world does not feel alive. Enemies stand around in groups waiting for you to come within aggro range.

The dialogue and story is ok, but drags on for so long and is often so irrelevant it is hard to care. The voice acting ranges from excellent to awful, but the worst part is that some of the voices do not always match the look of the character.

The companions were a major selling point for me, but their role is mostly limited to support in combat.

I feel there is a good game in here wanting to get out, but it falls short in pretty much every aspect.\t\t\t\t\t

The story is well thought through and interesting, I found myself glued to the seat just as much during my 2nd playthrough as I did during my 1st.

All in all, Greedfall is a gem, in my opinion.
If you like Dragon Age, you're sure to like Greedfall as well.
Spiders has done an amazing job with this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKind of a mixed bag but overall worth the time.

The Good
It's a beautiful island to explore, the scenery is very lush and lovely
Combat while easy is very fluid, the controls (controller) are very natural
The story won't win awards but for RPGs it's pretty good.
Decent amount of content, I skipped the hunting chests bit, got no time for hidden object games.
All armour items have their own graphic, you can really play dress up.
No fuss, from starting the game you can be back to last save in seconds, load times are very quick
Pretty much bug free. At least I can't remember any, which kinda says it all

The Meh
I played full caster and it's totally OP. But I can live with being all powerful. Once you get mage dodge, it's pretty hard to die.
Too brown man, too brown. But I fixed that with reshade, no biggie
Music. Music? There's music? Total non event
Voice acting. Some good talent but why does a guy called Fernando talk like Sean Bean? There are many Sean Beans in this game (I can't stand Sean Bean).
You will meet the same old guy in many places in many disguises! You will know who I mean

The Bad
The scripts are howlingly bad and omg the native accents are hysterical. The cringing attempt by the writers to use long flowery phrases without actually understanding them properly is sometimes just too painful. I skip native dialogue for the most part due to the awful accent. Who decided this...

I did find the whole colonial background a bit uncomfortable and it skirted a bit close to the whole 'white saviour' rescuing noble savages (who are of course 'close to nature'). But maybe I'm overthinking. I think the heart of the game is in a kind place.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly don't get all these negative ratings.

This is a great RPG, with great story, great missions, great graphics, and different ways to solve a task, and choices to make - a game which is really close to Dragon Age games.

However, I must admit there are quite some issues:
- BUGS. I usually don't really care about these, but when there are mission breaking bugs in an RPG, it's really irritating. And I don't think these will be ever patched now
- The fight system. There is nothing particularly wrong with it, it's just very basic
- There are a LOT of running around. And I mean, a LOT
- There are a LOT of dialogues, which is basically a good thing in RPGs. But here, I've often felt like I'm playing a visual novel. It was just a bit too much for my liking
- I practically maxed my character by around half of the game (I got all the skills I need and almost the best equipments, etc.), and an RPG without levelling up and upgrading is just... boring, and continue to play felt a bit like a chore. Would have been nice some more (usable) skills and more equipments.

But nevertheless, this is a very solid RPG, and I definetaly recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t

You'll often come across a bush that has a five-foot radius around it, even though if you were to go through it it would just take you to the road steps away. Sometimes because of these major invisible walls, you can get stuck during combat, so try not to corner yourself, otherwise you'll perish!
A lot of the NPCs, even some 'major' ones, share the same face, which can get annoying and confusing. If you're not paying attention, you might get confused as to why the guy who sells potions in one town is also the guy committing war crimes in another. Wish there was more variation.
I wish the companions spoke more. And the romances are okay, although if you're not careful you can lock yourself out of them pretty easily and early on, so I suggest looking up a guide or modding.
Another thing that annoyed me, at least when I was in my earlier levels, is the fact that it takes forever to take a potion. Like, to take it 'out of your pocket' and drink it. I died quiet a few times waiting for my character to take the potion out. And I wish fast travel was done differently, but that's just personal preference. Also sometimes characters will talk about things that you don't know about at the time and you'll just have to go along with it until slightly further in the game where you actually learn what's going on, which can be a little confusing. It didn't happen often though.
But the game is amazingly beautiful and I thought the conflicts, characters, and companions were well written (although again, wish there was more to the companions). The world is interesting and the monsters/guardians were very cool! The combat can be fun once you get into it and you can get pretty quick at controlling the battlefield. Leveling up was okay and some of the skills you gained weren't really helpful but also were...but not really. So it made them feel kind of useless.
Overall, it's a pretty good game with, in retrospect, minor flaws overall. It took a while to get into the story, and it was great seeing all the quests I did actually connect to the bigger picture. The ending was a bit of a let down (meaning it ended suddenly, THE END, roll credits), but everything from the first major mission to the last was enjoyable.
