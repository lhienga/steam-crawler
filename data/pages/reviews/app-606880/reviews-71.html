\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs this a AAA game that wows and amazes you at every turn? No. Is it something that you simply cannot live without, makes you want to call off work just to play? Probably not. What it is, however, is a very entertaining and worthwhile game. The story is decently compelling, the characters aren't bad, and it has its charms that grow on you until the end of the story and you are left asking "wait can I get some more?"

Pros:
The story is fairly straightforward and not especially branched, but is compelling and has some twists that keep you interested
There are multiple endings
The characters are fairly dynamic, which is refreshing
Factions, and your interactions with them, are important to the story
The world building is solid, and the lore goes surprisingly deep
For a smaller studio, this game is honestly really impressive

Cons:
The combat gets repetitive, especially once you get good gear/perks that fit your play style
The graphics and animations, even for a 2019 game, are not really impressive. More like adequate.
The environments, while slightly varied, are all rather similar. I get that the geographical area of the game according to the lore is small, but get used to looking at temperate forest/plains
The game was obviously built with clear engine limitations, so individual exploration areas are not large and many require one or several loading screens to navigate the map.

Overall, I'm a sucker for a good story with interesting characters. This game definitely delivers in that regard. A single playthrough is not really long if you only focus on the main story, but the amount of world-building and character development through the side missions and dialogues is really deep and immersive. It offers you the potential to role play different personalities and you get to see their effect on the world. Want to be selfish? Dialogue and story reflect that. Want to be the great hero? Dialogue and story again. Want to simply do the job, no questions asked? See above. Overall I would definitely recommend this game, especially if you like RPGs that let you RP.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t There are few modern games that I never regret launching. GreedFall doesn't have me gripped and it's not usually my go-to when I have an hour of free time, but I always enjoy inching through the story-driven adventure with my trusty companions. I'm interested in the characters and factions and the environments give me a grimmer Assassin's Creed III feeling (my fav AC game).
To me, GreedFall is an epic adventure that lays low in the world of flashy ads and overpromised ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. The combat is simply enough and not frustrating. Today's young Warzone gamer might hate this experience but my fellow Bioware fans should enjoy it.
The setting is my favorite bit, combining new world discovery with mystical monsters and magic. It makes for a crazy cool alternate reality past that shows the damage and collision of peoples on a planet ridden with manipulative imperialists, even if the foot soldiers do not recognize the harm they do on a large scale.\t\t\t\t\t
