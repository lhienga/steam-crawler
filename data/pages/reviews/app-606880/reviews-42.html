I enjoy the world, art style and characters to a certain degree and I believe the concept of exploring a new world along with the difficulties of navigating the politics of the various visitors and natives alike to be refreshing. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was on my wishlist for quite some time, until I was finally able to get it when it was on sale.

However, due to my high expectations for it I was really disappointed...

Pluses:
relatively bug/glitch free, if you forgive occasional NPC standing on tables and simillar things.
Quite easy to understand the game
Quite easy to understand basic relationship between each nation
some quest. Not often there are 'brign 10 planks' quests.
quite well depicted natives
UI that pleases the eyes. All in one or simillar colours.
relatively good AI for companions. no blocking entrances, and so on.
the designs of the big beasts.

Negativities:
The biggest NO for me was that even though you are mainly playing as diplomat, your ability to negotiate is heavily limited. You can only use charisma to convince others to accept your suggestion. (or use other things to do it, yeah but to the very same result. you cant come up with new posibilities, alternative suggestions, help one party or second party, no...).
I can't very well write specifics of my decisions because of massive spoilers but strictly speaking, when you get betrayed you can do absolutely nothing about it, only accept it as a fact. you cant punish the individual in any way, you cant declare him your personal enemy, or anything simillar. Same goes for various quest - you investigate something, find out whats the problem is, the individual basically offers bribe but you dont have the option to either report him or either take the bribe, you instantly have to go and report him. Or when you deal with one problem you've been tasked to solve, but learn about another in the meantime, you can't go back and report only that the problem you've been tasked is dealt with, you automatically mention the other thing aswell, when you could keep silent instead.

other then that:
- all building available to enter have the same interior as others. (by type of course. all building for gouvernors look the same, all residencies and other quest related houses are the same...
- lack of loot. or more specificly lack of its 'variety'. No matter which box you open, you see about fifteen various items all over again.
- The map navigation and understanding is hard to understand. you can return to the town from every travel point in the area but you have to use specific travel points to get into areas behind them.
- some quests, that are basically just walking simulators.
-Native language. I love me some custom langauges but this phonetics sound worse then german language sometimes. So hard, badly sounding, rough. Also it appears that every native on the continent can speak english in quite detail, yet no 'english'-men can speak native language.
- there are exceptions but the voice acting in general sounds really unproffesional. add: the follow-ups could have been way better. if you speak to a person and he goes angry and starts shouting "What is the merchant thinking?! He must have thought that we would never find out! Rest assure that there will be punishment for him!" and in the next second he goes into soft, smiling "How can this old proffesor help you today?"
- animals and mobs spawn in pacs in one points. They do not roam through the forest. Getting old MMORPG wibes here.
- some 'hitboxes'. you cant go under certain trees with wide tops, as the hitbox involves the air under the branches aswell.
-Once you are locked in quest for a companion, you can't switch to other one, no matter how desperate the situation might seem.
-quite hard system requierements. I can run much more visually pleasing games on relatively high setting but here I was happy to run it lagg-less only on the lowest settings.
- only about 10 preset faces everywhere on the world, just wearing different clothing and occasionaly different voice...

In summary, if you catch this game on a sale, give it a go but I would never go for the full price, it's just not worth that much, mostly because the hard limits in playstyle. As a diplomat you should have countless posibilities on how to deal with certain situation, choices, choices, choices... here you either success at the percentage chance check in one posibility, or you dont. ..\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's like getting Dragonage from Wish, but overall it's an OK game. I wouldn't say a good game though. Definitely worth playing if you don't pick it up at full price.

What bothered me the most was the lack of fast travel and no mini map. The invisible walls on the maps where you can't head through bushes or trees are annoying as all hell, especially when you have to run around the map from camp to camp to be able to fast travel. A mini map would really help so you don't have to constantly check the main map to make sure you're going the right way.

There isn't much by the way of character customisation in the game once you're out of the character creator. Clothes/armour etc are all the same style but just from different factions. You can spice them up with mods but this only changes the cosmetics slightly.

The companions add very little to the game besides some fetch and carry style quests. Go here. Fight. Come back etc. It also irks me to no end that you're stuck with each companion while their mission is in progress, rather than the mission just not being available while they're not with you.

The game lacks finesse in little things which set it apart from other games of this type. Annoying things which would improve the game if fixed. One example being buying clothes/armour. They equip when you buy them rather than just going in to inventory, so you have to spend another 5 minutes finding what you were wearing and re-equipping it.

Choices don't really matter. There is just a single choice towards the end game that makes a difference to the outcome of the endings, and then a second choice at the end. These seem to be the only choices that make any difference, so all the game play and reputation build with factions is for very little, if nothing.

I really wanted to like this game more than I did. It feels like a grind just navigating the game world and that drags it right down. The missions all feel like time sinks. Go here. Talk to somebody on the other side of the map. Go back to be able to talk to somebody else. Go here, click this. Blergh.

Having said that, it's a decent game overall and worth a play through. I can't see myself coming back to it as there isn't much replayability. You might as well just leave a save before the only choice that matters and play from there to experience all the endings. Which I've done.\t\t\t\t\t
