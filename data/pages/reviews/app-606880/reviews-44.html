\r\n\t\t\t\t\t5/5 stars. This game is a great RPG if you like engaging and enjoyable stories. The UI is easy to use, the graphics are great, the combat is fun. Characters and companions are memorable. Choices in the game are fairly meaningful. Widescreen monitors are supported. Keyboard/mouse or controller both work well. Modding the game works fine. The only thing I would change is allow the users to respec more often and also to allow us to use all of our companions at the same time (both of which can be fixed with mods). The world could also be a bit more open. It's about as open world as Dragon Age Origins (big open map, but invisible walls everywhere so jumping from one place to another is impossible, but this is done to control where the user goes because of quest progression). It definitely feels like the purpose of the open world is solely for questng, which isn't bad, just a room for improvement.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t4/10

+ Plot overall is okay-ish. It has a rather deus-ex-machina kind of an abrupt end, but otherwise mostly makes sense. Most things you will encounter is part of some broader narrative and side quests actually help to better understand situation.
+ Crafting is both useful and not-overwhelming
+ Music does its job well. Probably the pro of this game
+ In many situations there are different ways of finishing the task

- With that being said - towards the end narrative is lacking. Immensely. There is plethora of things mentioned in passing, sometimes even in main storyline, which haven't found any explanation whatsoever.
- Dialogues are there mostly to waste your time or remind you n-th time of the same thing. They are plain annoying and not much beyond that
- Cut scenes are literally cut. There is no moment-between. Once one character is done with talking, there is jump to another face (same goes for moments where there is supposedly something happening in the meantime)
- Mechanics require nothing of you. Zero tactics, crafty builds, none of that. You go deep into three "weapon" aspects, two abilities, and utilities as you see fit (the latter hardly matter in the end, most of the things are within you reach anyway)
- Fights are repetitive. People complain about zones to which enemies are assigned. Personally - I don't see how you could leave those during fights with demanding enemies. And those others are hardly an issue anyway (well, perhaps for melee character they are, but then again, you should know better than to go for melee)
- Ending is completely unrewarding and cheesy
- There are numerous situations where you would expect to have some say as to how your character would react, but that simply doesn't happen. MC is incorrigibly gullible and otherwise self-less.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say that I am a bit "on the fence" about Greedfall, but overall, I liked it. It does have its shortcomings: yes, the characters' mouth animations don't quite match the dialogue, it is very "Fed-Ex"-ish, and it takes you by the hand quite a bit - quest objectives are glaringly obvious (no pixel-hunt here). But the overarching story is pretty good, as is the voice acting, the various landscapes at times are striking, and the combat is not over-the-top (although many hard-core controller-jockeys will say it is too easy). Crafting and modifying your weapons and armor is straightforward and useful, there are a variety of weapon styles and talent paths to follow, and some interesting companions to pick up (and romance?) along the way. I played it with "The DeVespe Conspiracy" DLC, and it integrated into the main story line pretty well - although it did make for awkwardly shoe-horned interactions with various faction leaders during that sequence. I also liked the ability to save any time; always a big plus. In sum, Greedfall felt like an interactive story, and despite the fact that there are apparently five different endings, I never felt like I was driving the action so much as being led through it. Still, it was an entertaining ride.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo much jank!
-it's pretty common to see a NPC get themselves stuck in a corner and just give up on life staring blankly at a wall. You walk into the tavern and find some dude walking on the table, that kind of stuff.
-rigid and uncanny facial animations
-art assets are unnaturally copy-pasted throughout the world
-odd stuff like the blacksmith just hammering away at the anvil while you are engaged in combat just a few feet away

Stuff I didn't like
-early game ammo was so scarce that I had to re-spec. You go to buy ammo from the market and they only sell maybe 8 bullets at most... WTF? You dump all your points into gun play but can't use your guns!?
-Move out of the way, things are about to get dicey! The in-battle dialog repetition frequency is terrible! Your companions say the same lines of dialog over and over and over again so frequently I found myself seeking out help online, ultimately downloading a mod to have the combat dialog removed.

I love this game so far
The main purpose of this game to be a narrative driven experience delivers 10/10. The narrative holding a near similar level of quality to great titles like Dragonage, Witcher Wild Hunt, KOTR, Last of Us. I'm genuinely interested to see where the narrative goes next. The voice acting is top-notch, the combat is workable, the visuals while being overly re-used have a way of drawing you in. I would definitely recommend this game to other players.\t\t\t\t\t

It certainly has some shortcomings compared to games with a bigger budget like The Witcher 3 or The Elder Scrolls but was still very much worth my time.

The Pros:
- I liked the explorer age setting and the storyline even better than Skyrim with its dragons, dragons, dragons. Diplomat-style dialogs are also nice.
- The companion system is a big bonus of the game in my opinion, with interesting and diverse characters that blend in well with the overall storyline. It reminds me of the Gothic series or the Fellowship of the Ring.
- The combat system is fun even though too easy at some point. I'd recommend to play on the highest difficulty level right from the beginning, if at least a bit of a challenge is wanted. It even yields a special Steam achievement. Difficulty can be turned down at any point in case it is perceived too hard (but then the achievement is gone). I also recommend to start with a technician build, to me it appeared most versatile and fun, even if I usually prefer magic builds in other RPGs. Lastly, I recommend to buy the Adventurer's Gear DLC which is NOT included in the Gold Edition! (You can try the game without the DLC though, it can be added at any time.)
- The campfire / residence system is a HUGE relief, having quick travel, crafting, and loot box basically available everywhere on the map without walking too long.

Neutral:
- Graphics: By far not as nice as The Witcher 3 but not bad either.
- Length: I spent 120h on the game for almost 100% completion. For some people that might be too short, I actually liked that it is not dragged out for too long.

The Cons:
- Limited creature types: Not so late in the game you have seen them all.
- Invisible walls: I don't like them in RPGs, I wish GreedFall could do without them.
- Dull cities: Little NPC interactions; too many obvious "script loops"; not much of a reason to stay long inside the city walls\t\t\t\t\t
