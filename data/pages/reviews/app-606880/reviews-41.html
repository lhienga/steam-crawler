\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, I'm really trying to love this game, but...
The concept IS there (I mean, the setting? I'd die for that setting!), the locations look nice, believable and it's clear lots of love and thought went into making them...
Then the quests grab you by the neck like a poor defenseless kitten and start pulling you EVERYWHERE. It feels like an exam, you're not allowed to think, just go A, go B, do C and while you're at it you might as well do F to yourself.
The protagonist...
The game gives us an illusion of making our own De Sardet-only an illusion since in Greedfall the protag is clearly a set character (which is fine by itself, many games do that and are just fine) but there are literally no dialogue options in most quests. You have to just click through every option and might aswell skip everything because your knowledge as a player changes nothing when the game hand feeds you your every choice. Why think when you can follow breadcrumbs scattered all across the island, right? Might aswell just remove the dialogue alltogether and turn every conversation into a cutscene.
Back to the player character, our dear De Sardet, that feels like going OOC all the time. One second they seem capable and actually a fun character, the next? Lemme run back to my old teacher (two days journey back and forth) and ask for advice! I know that technically a diplomat's hands are tied in some instances, and there's more to it than making decisions on the spot-but then why does our protag constantly go out of their way to help the locals if their involvement in some matters could potentially risk war (or at least a little bit of political scandal) between their nations? That lack of consistency is really baffling sometimes-and that includes the npcs and companions (cousin's exempt of this, he's a good boie).
Also, someone thought it would be a good idea to make enemies de-agro and heal instantly when you leave their aggro zone, even when they themselves know you out of it-very fun, I assure yall. X'D
The trailers got me carefully excited back when I first saw them, decided to finally try the game despite the bad reviews I saw and... Well I'm not really regretting that choice that much, I had my share of enjoyment from this, but I definitely expected more.
So, yeah. As things stand, couldn't recommend this game to anyone who enjoys a good rpg. It surely has potential, but that potential is not realized.\t\t\t\t\t
