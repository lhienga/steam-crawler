\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely love this game, can't wait for the next one. Until then, I will be replaying this game over and over. There's still so much to explore and enjoy.

However, like with anything, there are some points of feedback to address (most of which I am certain Spiders have already seen before, yet here I am...):
  • Tethering - Enemies in combat will run back to their original spawn and heal to full if the combat goes too far out of the ideal range. It breaks immersion and can be frustrating.
  • Enemy Spawns - Entering combat feels very predictable and scripted. It would be nice to have enemies roam when idle, now it just feels like all enemies (or NPCs in general) have no purpose in life other than to fight me. It would be nice to have this flow feel more organic.
  • Lock-On vs Combat Camera - I'm not sure if this is just a personal thing, but I find the lock-on camera more difficult to play with than the default combat camera. However, the default camera doesn't allow for precision and I end up spamming ranged weapons, hoping to hit the correct target (this is especially true for magic builds), which makes some spells wasted by accident. Maybe I need to get used to this one, but lock-on felt like it was giving me less of an overview of the enemies in combat, limiting my ability to switch targets.

Despite these points, it was still an incredibly immersive experience. I enjoyed the combat, being able to fully play as 'De Sardet' and feeling connected to the characters. Later on in the game I also really enjoyed optimizing my build and really using the skills from the skill tree. I'd definitely recommend!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTook a chance on this title and got it on sale... so glad I did! Honestly, I'm surprised how much I'm enjoying it. Yes, its got a bit of janky/glitchy animation while climbing and running about and the character modeling isn't top tier (don't watch their mouths while they're talking, its a horror show) but the story really hooked me in from the start. Despite not being the most polished game, I'm liking spending time in the world of Greedfall and even the very basic early customization makes it feel like you can create a unique character and enjoy a deeper roleplay.

I can see why other reviewers are reminded of Fable and earlier Witcher games. Choices matter and paying attention to politics and your action/inaction has consequences. I'd say that despite having a few irritaing flaws (somewhat boring recycled environments, repetitive combat with limited enemies, companions screaming the same catchphrase EVERY TIME you engage in combat, and a somewhat lazy crafting system) Greedfall is still worth picking up and even worth a replay or two. I'm already thinking of my second play through and I haven't even finished the first one.\t\t\t\t\t
