Now "I get to be the one to say, WOW I have missed out"
THIS Excellent RPG in every way ! ( Except camera and a few graphics oddities. )
No bugs, No game breaking faults, No crashes.
Excellent original story as a diplomat. Soooo a lot of talking and thinking, action takes second.
Story actions and Voice response has consequences. UI, inventory, crafting, upgrading, even AI , ALL superior.

Travel encounters run into creatures you must fight.

Before Leaving camp, ' Choose wisely Ohh young diplomat ' From Alllllll your stuff and AI
Many camps for you to discover, and later fast travel to.
Save game like your life depends upon it. ( Saves not available in game difficulty above 'Normal' )

TL;DR
Fun, but fell flat in a few places, but nothing that was a major letdown. Good if you spot it in a steam sale, like I did!

Greedfall was quite fun, and the strategy aspect of the combat had a good feel to it, using stasis, magic, etc. Being able to pause mid-combat to analyse the situation and proceed according to a plan was fun.

The story was intriguing, if a little more aligned towards political intrigue at times. I like that kinda stuff, so it was up my alley!
The voice acting was pretty good!

And the environments, if a little samey are definitely quite detailed. Definitely felt remote island enough to sell me, anyway!

However, It does feel like it fell flat in certain regards.

Equipment and Gear was my main gripe - I feel like I'd gotten the best gear I could about midway through the game. I was ignoring vendors about halfway through, except to only dump my sellable items, and all the gear I'd picked up for the last few hours.

The crafting system mostly revolves around upgrading your current gear, and it's slotting your gear with enhancements which are locked behind crafting levels, which you can only level up every few levels, where it might be more beneficial to level up something like Vigor to get around easier, or Charisma & Intuition to make sure your dialogue/pursuasions don't go wrong. The upgrades, while useful, only really felt impactful on the chestpiece "tool" slot, otherwise, it's a plain armour/balance boost, which might be beneficial if you're on extreme, but on normal, ehh. I was running around in the last few quests without a hat on because the hats are very silly.

Skill tree:
A nice, expansive tree where you can pick your style and improve it further! But, it does feel like you're starting to buy skills just because you have enough points and it's just "Well, there's nowhere else for them to go.." - I was playing a Long Blade/Stasis user and by level 25 I was kind of wondering where to put points that weren't going to be wasted in weapon types I don't use, or skills I wouldn't end up using.

On the other hand, I felt like I was having to constantly re-spec my talents as I was trying to play diplomatic, but to sneak around things, I sometimes needed vigor.. and I hadn't spec'd into it at all. Becuase I didn't have enough points.

Dialogue/Subtitle disconnect - Only a minor one but more of an accessibility gripe. Despite me being perfectly well hearing, I use subtitles because it just helps me parse things a bit easier. There was sometimes a few changes in the spoken dialogue and what was written in the subtitles. Nothing completely major changing tone or anything like that, but still jarring.

And finally: The story/characters.
Honestly, apart from the Natives, and the Nauts, the rest of the factions seem to be exaggerated versions of what might be what I've termed "Bastard Colonials" - While it was fun at first, watching your own character act a bit dumb when you've just made a choice and still get a little bit colonial on the natives, even when you just helped them (I won't be spoiling) was a bit strange.

I won't be ragging on the archetypes because they're perfectly valid criticisms of actual colonial times, and I very much wanted to get each nation faction including the merchant congregation and give them all "you ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ idiot" wedgies. But that's a good thing, really.

The story was quite fun, and felt myself engaged with it, what, being a wiccan/nature-lover and all-round Bastard Hater but the ending (at least the one my choices led to) was just a slideshow of the characters resolutions and what happened after the final battle. Not sure if I got a 'bad' ending, but it's certainly felt a little anti-climatic.\t\t\t\t\t
