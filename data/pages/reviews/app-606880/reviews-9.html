\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an ok RPG with one truly annoying feature that has made me stop playing it. The quests are fine, if a bit linear, but the overall premise of the game is interesting. Combat mechanics are ok, if a bit laggy, except for one thing - enemies in group fights keep resetting. The game design is such that if you stray out of a defined area then the fight will stop, enemies will return to their original position and they will return to full health. This is fine, in principle, but the way it is implemented means that most of the group fights I've been in has reset at least once. I reached one fight where it reset 5 times before I gave up on the game. The enemies came out to meet my group, which meant we were right on the edge of the defined fight area and any dodging or retreating reset the fight and enemies returned to full health.\t\t\t\t\t

3. Great, amazing graphics due to a simple but very smart choice. It's the 17th century and they made people and environment (trees, houses) look like Rembrandt and Goya paintings. It adds to a very authentic and cinematic atmosphere.

Overall a great RPG game. I can't wait for Greedfall 2 now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, what a fun surprise. I've had this is my library for forever, I remember years ago I started it up, played 5 mins and then just didn't stick with it, I got caught up with something else and I was really put off in the tutorial with movement and combat. So now I finally decided to give it a fair chance and I found myself actually really enjoying it.

The combat is ok, its not horrible, but it is very clunky, which is a good way to describe most of the game, clunky a little janky, a lot of the places are copy paste, the head of each faction practically has an identical house to each other etc, Graphics are nice though and more importantly the story and characters are really good. Not great, but a nice change of pace, a unique story with unique characters.

This very much reminiscent of old Bioware, like Jade Empire, or even reminds me of the fallout games, outerworld. Where its more action based, with rpg elements, with companions that have their own questlines and decisions to make in the overall story. Its fun, in lots of rpgs you start out as a nobody and work your way toward greatness, in this one, you are already important, you're the diplomat for your cousin the mayor and its your job to solve various issues throughout the city and neighboring cities, to try and keep the peace. It's enjoyable to throw your weight around and sift through the corruption and politics and what I like about this title specifically, is that your companion quests aren't just one and done quests, they have a few quests for you to do as you progress. They all have their own individual stories that almost feel important to do, because they supplement the main story as well. I really liked this, I thought it was a great way to immerse you and help you feel like these characters were individuals.

Near the end I felt the game got a bit tedious, and I liked the creativity and uniqueness, was it the best story ever told? Best rpg of all time? No, it does have quite a few faults, the romance for example has almost no buildup, I wish there was a little bit more complex political intrique, you can unite all factions, but by being able to do that almost helps to make the game feel a little shallow, a lot of the issues you dealt with as a diplomat felt very straight forward, it didn't seem too complex, every faction could be reasoned with and all seemed to toe the line. I would have loved for there to been more factions, more players in the game within the factions. I also would have liked more variety in gameplay, but for what is here, its very enjoyable and I had a great time with it.

It's not a short game, Took me 50 hrs, including side quests, but again, to not do the side quests for this game feels wrong, the sidequests all really felt worth doing. It wasn't like collect 50 of this item etc, they were all story forward.

Really fun good game, recommend for anyone who likes this style of game. Wasn't the best, of the wrpg genre, but a solid entry and worth a go.
