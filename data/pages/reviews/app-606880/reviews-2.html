\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoy this game. At this time I have not completed the game, although I will be doing so because it's fun.

Some of my opinions:
1. Graphics and world are beautiful, the world/town style feels like a mix between the Witcher 3 and Skyrim, two of my favourite games of all time.
2. The outfits for your character are cool.
3. I like the option you have to disguise yourself to get into certain areas, although I think the mechanism could use some refining as just wearing the coat of another faction isn't really enough of a disguise in my opinion. I like the thought though.
4. If you are looking for an open world experience like that of the Witcher, Red Dead or Skyrim then this game will disappoint you. It is open world in that you can go where you want to go whenever you want to go there and you are not forced to follow a single path, there is room to explore the areas. However, there are loading screens between different areas of the island, it's not a smooth exploration model necessarily and most areas that you can discover are empty if explored outside of a quest. They only become populated or interesting during the quest that involves that area. This to me is not the worst thing. I find the missions and quests to be fun and I am happy for the most part to follow along with them and discover the world and its secrets that way. It's something to bear in mind though if you are looking for a game with completely free exploration.
5. I really enjoyed the choice-making element of the game and the ability to improve or damage your relations with different factions/groups. I find that to be very satisfying.
6. Contrary to some other comments, I quite like the combat style. That said though, I don't typically play games for the combat so if you do, this games combat style may disappoint you. As someone who engages in combat in these games to continue with and fuel the narrative, I'm happy with it. I find it to be quite easy to use and even a tad challenging when played on easy (I know, how lame of me to play on easy :') ).
7. I think the voice acting in the game is done pretty well.
8. Occasionally dialogue doesn't make complete sense to me, feels a bit like they are talking about something that hasn't been introduced in a good way, like I'm playing catch up or thinking "What are they going on about?". Not very often though.
9. I enjoy being able to develop your relationship with your companions and that you can always have 2 of them with you. They also mostly have something to say during quests which I really like, makes them feel more alive. Sometimes their individual missions felt a little bit random though.
10. The creatures in the game are cool and unique. The guardians are especially cool imo.
11. While I really like the cities in the game and their different styles, there isn't much motivation to explore them as you can't access almost any buildings, most people have nothing interesting to say unless they're part of a quest and the most you can find in the cities on a whim is a crate with some goods or something. Find that to be a bit sad. Really like how original the houses of the natives are though, as well as their culture.
12. I like the incorporation of using time for certain quests, like where it tells you to be in a certain place in a certain number of hours, that kind of thing.

Overall, I am having lots of fun with this game. Going in to it I didn't have many expectations though and I would keep in mind what exactly it is you're looking for in a game before buying this to make sure you don't end up disappointed. I'm really looking forward to continuing my playthrough and can't wait for Greedfall 2!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoes this game have flaws? What game doesn't? As of now, March 18th, 2024; Spiders has about 70 employees. When this game was in development, they had 40. If you want my honest opinion, this is a great game if you can find it on sale. It's not a AAA title and you WILL see odd facial animations, copy/pasted building interiors, NPCs that all have similar faces with different hairstyles, the same wolf bear enemies, and voices that don't seem to match the age or characteristics of an NPC; but guess what? I still think this is an awesome game. Those complaining about it being too hard are just not used to the combat mechanics or are trying to do things that are above their level, or don't have the gear. Spiders is a french company, so these typos people keep mentioning are not just lazy typos, it's because they speak french as a first language. You will see a lot of words that are supposed to have a "Z" in them, like "realization", and they will have an "S" where the "Z" is supposed to be. Give them a break, we get what they're trying to say. Obviously in a game like this the biggest selling point is the lore and backstory that all hardcore RPG fans want. There's disagreements between your science based faction that is willing to do unethical things in the name of science, and then there is your hyper religious faction that wants to convert everyone. There you are, right in the middle, refusing to choose sides, but like most games, you will have to eventually. Then there is the poor natives that are caught in the middle of it all.

Now, there are some things that the game does that are weird. I liked the idea in the trailer that I can use guns like a pirate, but guns in this game work like a potion or a consumable would. You can't just run around with a gun and snipe folks. You press the bound key, and shoot someone. Does it look cool? Hell yeah. But I would have liked to spec guns as my main attack without spamming the "shoot dudes" key. Also, and it's not a bad thing, there are a lot of ways to complete quest and based on how you have your skills specced, you will be unable to do some of them. For instance, there is not a jump button in this game. How it works, is that you level up your Vigor with Talent points you get every few levels. When you come across a wall you can scale, simply press the button to climb up. Or jump a gap. Sometimes you will come across gaps that require 2 or more Vigor, and you cannot cross. Either make a mental note, and come back later or find another way to pass. Also, in the Prologue, you won't level up enough to get two talent points so I suggest using your one point, and get Lockpick. There are a lot of chest in the prologue. Nothing meaningful, but I hate coming across chest that I can't open. And once you leave, you can't come back.

All in all, if you loved Dragon Age: Origins or other good RPG games, I say Greedfall is a game that you have to try at least once. But don't pay full price for it. Find it on sale if you can. If not for the dated graphics, and lack of itemization, I would recommend more. But if you're a true RPG fan, graphics shouldn't mean much. I mean I just beat The Witcher 1 haha!\t\t\t\t\t
