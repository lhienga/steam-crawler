\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBad story, repetitive gameplay.

There are some really neat concepts here, but none of them are executed on particularly well. The story hinges on tired, overdone "natives are savages, we will bring them enlightenment" garbage, none of the factions you're supposed to rely on are good *except* for the natives, and there are story beats where you're *forced* to do things that offend and hurt the native tribes, so the game doesn't give you a faction you can be happy to spend time with.

The fighting mechanics aren't terrible, but it lacks depth. Skill points come so slowly that once you've put a couple points into a playstyle you're pretty much locked in for the rest of the game - but a given playstyle doesn't have any novelty once you lock in, you just do the same attack over and over against every enemy.

There are elements of this game that aren't bad, enough that I managed to play long enough to decide to finish it despite it's best efforts. But if you're looking for a game with a good story or fun fighting mechanics, look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first the game seems kind of clunky and difficult to navigate, but as you continue to explore the story and environments you start to get a feel for it all. The story is interesting and has some cool twists in it so far. The world of Greedfall is well put together in my opinion. Of course, certain architectural details are copy and pasted into the world, but I feel like it fits with the era this game is set in. The companion system is actually responsive in the sense that they don't box you into corners with no escape and they're actually useful in combat, unlike some other games I'm sure most of you will know about. The "crafting" system is a bit lack luster, but it's not terrible. You can't actually craft weapons/armor, but you can craft modifications for nearly anything you find in the world. You can craft potions, ammo, and another set of items called alchemical concoctions. All in all I would recommend players giving this game a real chance before passing judgement on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish to share my experience with the game. I just finished the game. Went to the steam page and noticed its mostly positive reviews which is really unfortunate.
This is one of those game where the low/average positive review percentage gives a wrong impression. And I am glad that I gave this game a try. I started this game on the 14th and today is October 19. In just these few days, I have 47 hours in this game, including dlc.

Preferences aside, I want to share some of the good and bad things of the game.

The Good: Amazing architecture and world. I feel like you could teach architecture with the governor's palace in this game. It's magnificent. The armor designs are beautiful. You can upgrade your armor and you can actually see the armor upgrade on the armor.

The gameplay was made so that the player can do a quest or tasks in their own way and not be restricted. For example, you have craftsmanship, right? But let's say, you don't want to spend points in craftsmanship.
simply wear a armor with +1 craftsmanship. And your companions will also give +1 to a certain talent.
So I only had 1 point in craftsmanship. But equipped a armor with +1 and companion with +1 and with it, I have max level craftsmanship.
Same thing with science, lock-picking. Even in quests, where you need high level lock-picking, the key is always very close. So you can easily find it.

The story of the game is that there is a plague called Malichor which has no cure. But a new island is discovered and people are hoping to find a new cure in this new miraculous island.
There are multiple factions. The Congregation, the Theleme, the Bridge alliance. There are also Coin guard which are hired mercenaries used as guard in the island, The Nauts and also the natives which consists of various clans.
Every quests you do, affects the reputation with various factions and even companions. You can have 2 active companions in your party and they will also affect the quest with new dialogue option or the reputation score. You can infiltrate an area by wearing the faction cloth instead of sneaking.
The dialogue, voice acting is really well done. The companions are unique with very good quests. Even the side quests are related to the main game.
The story tone and pacing was always good and serious. It didn't try to be funny. Everyone was never out of character.
As the legate of the congregation, you meet up with the other alliance and factions and learn more of the inner workings of these factions and obviously even solve some of the corruption that exists within. The dlc was also good. I highly recommend doing the main quest and all the sidequests as they are connected.

Also the game ending are shown like fallout games like new vegas where the game says how my actions for quests have affected the world and I am just gonna say it, this is something I really like. It makes me feel like my player character has made a big affect on the world.

The combat is actually good and it works. The skill system is good enough that you will continuously get strong and your play style will actually change. This is something I was worried about since often time, the gameplay loop of many games can get boring.
The skill system fixed most of my worries. Here is how my game play improved overtime in the game:
When I first started I was a warrior class with sword, mace and gun. Simple fights.
Then I unlocked coating which allowed me to apply magic, elemental, stasis or poison affect on my weapon. So different coating and better dmg against enemies. And also made potions with science.
Then I unlocked stasis which basically stops an enemy for a period of time. So basically I use stasis spell and then attack. And also unlocked healing spells. So didn't use potions that much.
Then I unlocked bombs and traps. So when I fight against many enemies, I can use these. Also unlocked armor spell which regenerates armor.

So finally my player is one handed sword user with coatings to add status effects and stasis spell to stop enemy, with spells to heal hp and armor while also having bombs and traps to fight multiple enemies and also high level potion in case of danger.
So the skill system has a lot of freedom.

Performance wise, I am also happy. Got a potato pc. Only had 1 crash in my entire playthrough with one instance where my player fall off the map and even then nothing happened. My player simply teleported back to the field with no problem.

The Bad: It still suffers from the common open world rpg game issue of huge open map or hub areas. The quests require you to travel huge distance by running. Even with fast travel from camp fire, the travel from campfire to destination alone can be very boring. But I feel like this is something that can overlooked cause of the quality of the story and quests.
And honestly how many games have successfully overcome this problem? So I feel like simply calling it as running from point A to B is a harsh description.
Even though I have praised the combat and its skill system, many people might still dislike its simplicity compared to action based game.
Some of the sidequests can be easily missed since you have to talk to the quest giver who may be inside a building. But personally, the quest givers are usually in a building that is frequently traveled so there is low chance of missing it. But the sidequests are so important to the story elements I feel like missing these might be a huge miss in your experience of the game.

The game also doesn't have post game content. So you have to finish contents and dlc before doing the final quest.
But all in all, I highly recommend this game. The beginning is slightly difficult since you start at level 1 but this will quickly change once you gain some levels. Even with some of its generic flaws, I consider this game to be a great experience which can enjoyed by other players who like open world rpg games and others too if they are willing to try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do recommend this game but its not without many things I don't like.

Ill try to avoid spoilers but there will definitely be some. ill try to put spoiler check marks before and after each one.

Lets start with the things I did not like first.

The houses make no sense, most buildings even important ones are copy and paste identical and its annoying because its not like you can go in many buildings anyways. Some houses have no kitchen or bathroom.

To piggy back on the first issue in the governors palaces also copy pasted but the little servant kitchen areas accessible by the ladders also is a poor design.

So all and all the architecture is nice and all but the design of some of them and the redundant layout of some of them kinda makes it suck.

Takes a year just to drink a potion i feel. this is a love hate. its more realistic and i do like that but it can still be a pain in the ass during combat.

Some quest you have to option the hard way and you can almost completely miss them because the map does not tell you someone inside a building wants to talk to you until you enter it.

Romance options are completely miss-able and easy to screw up.

There sometimes a needed for a better understanding of a quest order in the beginning i constantly was worried id miss something.

There are this annoying invisible fight zones enemies are in and if the battle goes outside of it it will instantly heal and walk back to the zone. Early game when you are not that tough and dodging to save your life this is extremely annoying because you'll be close to killing something or winning then boom start over.

There is a huge lack of emotion in this game, there is some situations the emotion of the scene or event could be played on way more but its just short and brief. Like a good writer could have probably brought tears to your eyes a few times in this. But alas nothing its just like oh that sucks and move on. Now one the endings i wont spoil, it is kind of sad despite its brief moment.

Using a blacksmith or alchemist should help you not need to know science and crafting honestly or there be some way to help them get to the top tier for you, or simply cost a lot seeing as money is not much an issue in this and honestly almost useless outside of buying ingredients you run out of to upgrade equipment.

Wish the npcs did more things, some are but most just do the same thing over and over or nothing at all and just randomly stand around, many of them look awkward. and even out of place.

I wish this game got a bigger budget. Theirs so many parts of the game that could have called for some really awesome cut scenes but they ether had very brief weak ones and most areas had none at all.

Especially the battles, the ending battles, the outpost battles all of them could have been crazy and it was all kinda like meh. The game speaks of armies but besides one battle ground that has many dead bodies (which gave me hope i would see much more of that and more) there was sadly none.

I also don't like that after the end of the game theirs just a cheap explanation of the ending similar to other games which i did not like that style of ending, but what really bothered me is i could not visit my friends and play a little. You get a relationship with people you cant even see in the peace times in the end, which before i forget those relationships could have been shown more. Simple things like some happy moments throughout the game just drinking with them, eating with them, sitting with them stuff like that.

and final disappointment is well part of the very end. The end mission was kind of anticlimactic. The boss fight was meh, but the fact that (POSSIBLE SPOILER) ..............................the god could not even defend himself or even his guardian help him was kinda like...why? It was sort of a rushed ending like lets get this game over with. (possible spoiler over)

Now lets get to the things i liked.

the game play is kind of really hard at first and even at a high level towards the end of the game theirs times you can get beaten unless maybe you are playing on easy or something.

The thought you have to put into when applying skills/attributes/talent points is great and its awesome that you have chances to re spec your character if you screw up.

I love how blunt your character can be and sometimes he is funny though i wish they added more to his humorous responses sometimes. I like having different choices in conversations and how to settle matters.

Being an emissary, a diplomat was fun and now i wish it was an option in more games. Instead of always some all mighty hero or something.

Also i love the lighting of this game. its not the best i have seen but for 2019 its great especially at night time and the little lantern you carry i love that. I like walking a lot and just enjoying the scenery of everything. and listening to the sounds. there is a few areas where the sounds will change like you'll get near a pond and all the sudden hear frogs and stuff. very noticeable and appreciated the devs get much praise from me on this. So don't ruin the game play by adding any other sounds or playing max brightness. i even turned the in game music off.

I do like there is more than one ending and choices for the ending and that's all i will say on that to not spoil anything.

I like that when you beat a guardian there bodies stay there like come back way later and its still laying there. Things like that i notice, although i wish there were times to have them on your side especially if your going down a path that would makes sense.

(another possible minor spoiler. and my last point.) .............................getting all your homies to roll with you for a min although i wished it was not so brief and you really got to battle along side them more and more emotional moments with them missed. it was nice for what it was.\t\t\t\t\t
