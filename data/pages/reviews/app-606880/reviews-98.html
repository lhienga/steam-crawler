\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an awesome third person, open world rpg, set in an island with natives, and several other factions.
It´s really a great game, you can have companions, you can decide the way you do the quests, you create your own character and you can decide between three styles of combat but you can combine them making the combat very dynamic and fun,


you can decide between warrior (two melee weapons swords, maces/axes, and firearms) techinician (traps, that evolve to throwing alchemy mixes, firearms, and swords) and magic (spells, magic attacks and maces/axes) i combined the and i could throw alchemy mixes, fire rifles/guns, do magick attack and spells and when my mana was depleted i changed to a melle combat with my mace.

Its an open world that is divided in several areas that you can individually explore each one in a setting with magic, natives and invaders. The beginning of the game is cool because is in a "small" zone to show you the mechanics and several missions very interesting to do, that zone is very similar to white orchard in witcher 3, and then you move to the big map.

The story is amazing with very good twists and charachter development for you and your companions. Yhe decisions you make direcctly and indirecttly have impact on the world, the missions and companions and your relation with the factions. There is a little bit of romance that is fresh and good to the game.
You have several abilites like persuasion, intuiton (unlock new dialogue options) , crafting (make and improve armor), alchemy (make healing, attacking potions, alchemy mixes, etc)

I really loved that according to the companions you currently have the side quests, missions and quests can unlock new ways or dialogs. The companions are really one of the strong point of this game, their dialogs, interactions and quests are excellent.

It has his limitants such as the world structure the not so variatey of enemies (humans,-+10 different types ofmonsters, 4 mini bosses), no mini-games, like cards dices, etc or extra activities (fishing, and that such) but in general is an excellent game for me

It has amazing replayability value. It´s a fairly enough long game, i finished it in 42 hours with all the side quests done and the dlc included.
The dlc is a good expansion that adds a new zone to the map, a really good quest, new enemies, sepecial gear and weapons but it isnt that long, although i recommend getting the gold edition.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I'm a little conflicted with this game, as it tries to follow the formula of success that has come from similar games like Fable 3, but stumbles in other areas.

Pros:
-Between traveling from one place to the next, you can set up a camp in between, and craft, buy/sell to a merchant, and swap out characters in your party. Honestly the coolest feature I haven't seen in many games.
-Combat is fun and can be challenging at times, even on normal.
-Leveling feels good, and you need to be somewhat mindful of what to put points into first...very DnD style, which I always like.
-For the price tag the game has, the graphics are surprisingly beautiful.

Cons:
-While some of the story is decent, there are parts where I wish there were dialogue options. For instance, one group was capturing another faction, and that other faction retaliated and I peacefully dealt with it. Then I went back to the group that was capturing the other faction and told them of an impending attack from that faction. Instead of trying to get them to release the people they captured, all I got was a "Oh thanks for the heads up" *Give XP and rewards*. Just feel like the story could have used some more polish with some more options.
-Controls are Rockstar 3rd person level, meaning they're kinda clunky. I get they don't want you turning on a dime, but this is a video game, and clunky feeling controls are one of my biggest pet peeves. Also, the camera and controls are wonky when going through doors, so I've learned I had to stop moving the camera for a few seconds while going through the door and coming to a stop. Thankfully, the rest of the game is fun and good enough that I can forgive it, unlike GTA. If they polished it just a little bit more, I would have no complaints.

I will update as I play more, but 6 hours in and only spending less than $20, I'd say it's already been worth the price, and my grievances are small compared to what they could have been as I play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like Dragon Age Inquisition, then this game is a close cousin in terms of scale and story and general gameplay mechanics. While both the scale and story do not hit every mark of that once golden standard by Bioware, it comes very very close. If you are reading a lot of the negative reviews most of them stem from them wishing this was in essence a Dragon Age game. While this game is a general vibe of Dragon Age and Bioware remember, this is not Dragon Age and this is not Bioware making this game, giving this game a chance will not disappoint you if you enjoy RPG game play with live, non turn based combat. Additionally, the story may sometimes not always give enough depth to the story or characters, when probably
most appropriate to. You will still find all the traditional hallmarks you are looking for in a story driven game in which your decision affects the ending. My one serious complaint is the Mass Effect 3 ending in which you receive a slide show instead of a full length cut scene. I put 81 hours in crafting my perfect ending i think i show get a cut scene showing my efforts. Do not let negative reviews dissuade you away from this game especially if it is on sale.

For those of you that are complaining about the game, especially at its current state, just stop. You are one of the many reasons that games like these are becoming more rare. (Specifically RPG games formatted like this) Grade this on the merit of what the game is in its totality, not put 2 hours in and call it bland, most RPGs are boring until you are pass hour 10, you have to give the story time to unfold alittle.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this game should've had a multiplayer option, it's still a fun solo adventure with plenty to offer. There are plenty of story quests, side quests, and missions to keep the player entertained. The world is pretty extensive and has fast travel mechanics that help it not feel so much like a running simulator (I'm looking at you, New World). I've really enjoyed the companion quests so far and look forward to seeing what else the game has to offer.

I have two critiques, one regarding the leveling system and one regarding the fight mechanics. While the leveling system works fairly well, the process to gain attribute and talent points is extremely slow, especially considering you run into needing those skills almost constantly. The skill points are easy to obtain (you receive them every level up plus there are skill points that can be collected throughout the world), I think it would've been better to give attribute and talent points those opportunities as well because receiving one point every five levels just isn't it.

When it comes to fighting mechanics, they are smooth for the most part. I like the use of firearms in combination with melee weapons and the in-game pause can be useful during larger fights. However, at times it feels like your character gets stuck, especially when fighting Guardians. While I can appreciate stagger and knock back in a battle, the character response time to those affects is extremely slow, leading to you being stuck in a cycle of knock back and stagger attacks. The character simply responds really slowly at times and makes it difficult to maneuver into a better position.

Other than those two things though, I have enjoyed the game so far and would recommend it to anyone looking for a decent solo experience.\t\t\t\t\t
