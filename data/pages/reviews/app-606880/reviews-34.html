\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedFall is an absolutely stunning RPG that takes players on an incredible journey through a mystical world full of ancient magic, intrigue, and adventure. Developed by Spiders and published by Focus Home Interactive, this game has been highly praised for its captivating storyline, beautifully rendered environments, and engaging gameplay mechanics.

The game is set in a fantasy world filled with unique creatures, stunning landscapes, and complex political intrigue. Players take on the role of De Sardet, a member of the Congregation of Merchants who is sent to the island of Teer Fradee to find a cure for a deadly disease that is ravaging the continent. Along the way, De Sardet will encounter various factions and characters who will shape the course of their journey and determine the fate of Teer Fradee.

One of the most impressive features of GreedFall is its ability to seamlessly blend different gameplay mechanics to create an immersive experience. Combat is intuitive and nuanced, with different weapons and skills allowing players to take on foes in a variety of ways. The game also offers a deep crafting system that lets players create new items and upgrade their gear. Additionally, the game features a branching dialogue system, allowing players to make meaningful choices that will affect the game's narrative.

The game's stunning visuals are certainly another highlight. The team at Spiders has created an incredibly detailed world filled with unique creatures, structures, and natural landscapes. The game's soundtrack is equally impressive, with expertly composed music that creates the perfect atmosphere for each scene.

Overall, GreedFall is an exceptional RPG that offers a unique and captivating experience that any fan of the genre should consider. With its engaging story, memorable characters, immersive world-building, and expertly designed mechanics, it is easy to see why this game has been so well-received by players and critics alike.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is good enough to keep you playing it. Its combat system is good, builds are varied enough for you to wonder what different builds feel like when playing them and the setting of the world is interesting. However, the game has many flaws.

The game doesn't have a fast travelling system per se, though you can teleport from camp to camp located in different areas of the world. This is, at times, maddening when you have to move from one map to another because of tedious walking. The game also has fixed spawn points so you will find the exact same enemies in the exact same spots. This becomes a major issue during the end game, where there are no challenges to be had and you have to revisit some location multiple times and having to deal with same enemies all over again.

The protagonist is bland. The dude speaks with the same tone of voice throughout the game and has almost no flaws. If this dude were a drink, he would be water.

Game starts strong and becomes tedious the more you play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall is a great semi open world, party-based action-RPG that will definitely scratch that Mass Effect itch with an original story, an engaging combat and a multitude of choices that affect the world and events that play out. However, it is rough around the edges, re-uses much of the assets and things like character design, player character builds and item variety leave a lot to be desired. Pairing this with odd quest design but with surprisingly thoughtful quest narratives that even surpasses of The Witcher, Greedfall is a mixed bag that regardless of its shortcomings will keep you engaged throughout.

The story takes place on the island of Teer Fradee (aka “The New World”) where you play as De Sardet, a legate of the Congregation of Merchants (a state resembling late medieval Venice) and have to navigate diplomatic ties with 3 major and several minor factions all at odds with one another to control the island. The theme of the story touches on Colonialism and the effects stemming from it where you and your actions will shape the island future.

During conversations, you can steer the direction and the subsequent action to your will. However, this is very limited as you have only a few skill check options and there is no morality system but instead a loyalty system. This means that conversation are linear and have no room for role-playing outside of the boundaries. While you can resolve some situations with high enough Intuition or Wits usually, the standard Charisma option is dependent on pure luck factor. All of this means that this is more of a linear RPG experience where you tag along for the ride instead of driving compared to a game like Divinity or Pillars of Eternity.

Quest narrative is well written and delivered with an okay level of voice acting, in some cases a secondary quest is on paar with the narrative writing of The Witcher. In fact most of the political intrigue, the origins or the mystery of the island and the Natives you will only learn from secondary or loyalty quests. However, the quest design is problematic and you will see this coming up in countless reviews titled “running simulator” because most quests designed to run from one place to another and then back again in an arbitrary way not considering the player’s time or a narrative consistency.

This exacerbates the notion on how I can best describe the game is “inconsistent”. Some examples: In some quests, you do have the option at the end to conveniently travel back to an NPC without running back, and then in most quest, you are not. In most conversations your companions are bystanders and do not offer anything in the narrative, but then at some quests they do weight in with their own view. The lack of conversation options and the inconsistency of quest design leads to frustrating moments where you have to walk from one end of the map to another just to tell someone that the job is done or completely miss your attempt to romance a character because you answered one prompt wrong and there is no way to recuperate.

Combat is a mixed bag, but when it works its quite engaging and it reflects the developer’s previous work. Your class will determine your playstyle (3 classes, Tank, Rouge, Mage) but don’t expect too much customization as only a few active skills available after significant levelling. A fast spaced, almost Souls-like combat where you can use abilities and utilities to quickly dispatch enemies. However, multiple enemies will show the weakness of this system where sometimes even with the right timing you will get constantly knocked down and in a frustrating manner enemies can spam you to death. There are also major boss battles with a distinct boss attack patter that suffers from the same frustrating issue where the enemy pulls incredibly combos out of nowhere and knock you down in rapid succession.

When it comes to graphics, the environmental design generally is fine at times even beautiful, but sometimes it is hard to identify the right crafting ingredients from the scenery. Factions lack a wholly distinctive flavor other than the surface touches and moreover, character design and animations are generally lacking and for a game released in 2019 overall, it is outdated.

There are also numerous small problems, like the reused assets for interiors and city design, the huge amount of lookalike NPCs or the lackluster itemization. However, with all this Greedfall still manages to captivate with its original story, interesting quests and even though of the shortcomings of its action-RPG’s systems, they still work just enough to keep you engaged.

This game is a solid package, that is definitely rough around the edges but the foundation is there and will keep you engaged until the end where all your decisions will culminate in a final battle. There is no doubt that this game could have used a continuous support and quality of life enhancement over the years from the developer to make it better, but alas they have moved on to Steel Rising and eventually will GreedFall 2. We can only hope that the issues are learned from and rectified to make the sequel (actually, a story prequel) better in every way.

While we are waiting on that next installment, get onto Greedfall with an open mind, sit back to absorb the story and I am sure you will have a great time on Teer Fradee.

6/10 Above Average\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll be brief. It is GOOD.

The companions remind me of the best parts of Mass Effect 2, and the moment-to-moment writing qualities remind me of Witcher 3. These games brought me some of the best memories ever and I do not compare modern titles to them lightly.

Now, this is an AA title. The game's not as long and do not offer as much as these two classics, but it does not out-stay its welcome and it is genuinely so rare to find anything even close to its overall quality. If I am disappointed with Outer Worlds, I am surprised with this.

So when will the other boot drop? Where's that 'but' coming?

Well, the quest structure is BAD.

See, there's quest, without spoiling anything please experience it with me. Now, everyone knows that there are indigenous people and new colonial settlers in the game right? They came into conflict over a miner near a forest in this quest. Interesting premise. So what do you do?

You go to the location, investigate;

go to your mentor for advice;

go to the archive to fetch a document;

go to the previous governor to discuss the matter;

go BACK to your mentor to discuss it a bit more;

go to the Indigenous chieftain to negotiate;

go back to the site and investigate some more;

go to your master, for more advice;

go the archive again;

go to the previous governor again;

your master again;

site again;

chieftain again;

and then you return to your capital and end the whole thing.

Please understand that between each lines there are MULTIPLE LOADING SCREENS and MINUTES OF WALKING, EACH. I am not lying when I say that I am FURIOUS about the game wasting my time like this.

Dear quest designers, how have you come to hate me so? How have I wronged you?

That's the one issue I do have, otherwise great game.

Production value: 7.5
plot/lore: 8
Core game play: 6

Overall a 7 and a HARD recommendation. Just remember, while the quest designers HATE you, the artists, coders, writers, level designers and all other devs LOVE you. Try to remain calm during fetch quests, tis good for your health.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think I've heard this game described as a flawed masterpiece that harkens back to the good 'oul days of KOTR and I think that's incredibly accurate. It's got incredible world building and lore and all the elements of a satisfying RPG. Voice acting is excellent and truly enjoyed the story. For a casual gamer like me, this is a solid B+.

First of all, kudos to the team for tackling such a huge project of this scale, every time I found myself with a minor quip I had to remind myself of how much work must have been put into making all the other elements of this come together (i.e. differing dialogue for partners, consequences of choices etc.). Boss fights were great!

My minor grievances were oddly enough navigating: it was sometimes frustrating to be exploring in an area and then not be able to actually go back up it (sometimes because of character characteristic checks, but sometimes due to actually being physically blocked). It'd be nice to have an option when it's not a character check to go back up / down without having to essentially go through the entire map again.

Sometimes I found myself a bit frustrated with the inability to fast-travel from anywhere like the Skyrim system, it would make some of the quests less tedious going from point A to B; when in the travel menu, it would also be useful to see the map and where you'd be travelling, instead of just the name of the location and an exclamation mark. It's been said in comments that it can feel a bit like a fetch party, and I certainly found myself feeling that way a few times for the side quests though I suppose that's inevitable.

Some of the animations were a bit janky at times; the biggest improvement I wish for the next round would include better facial animations.

All this to say, after just having finished it, I'm excited to see that Greedfall 2 is on the horizon and hope to perhaps see some of the improvements that gamers noticed could help the experience along the way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall is unfortunately a game with very little soul. Characters are voiced fairly well, but between the awful facial animations and some of the lackluster writing, they just feel fake. The world feels empty - all wilderness sections pretty much look the same; several corridor like clearings within a light woodland that you run through without ever feeling you are exploring much of anything. Cities all look the same, even down to the governor estates in each city being exact model copy and paste building.

The quests are also off. They are so time consuming and un-engaging. Talk to this person about nothing much. Fast travel and run across to the other person and discuss something bland. Go back to the original questgiver and get told to sneak somewhere and steal some documents. Run about an illogically maze like building that just wouldn't ever be built as a functional structure in this semi realistic world. This kind of quest I must have done at least 10 times throughout the campaign. It's just not exciting. The grand narrative has some good moments, but they are bogged down amidst the ennui.

Sometimes good combat can save a game, even one so heavily focused on story and character development, but Greedall fails to do this also. Some enjoyable tricks in combat are fun to use, but this is entirely ruined by there only being 4 different enemy types (excluding bosses) through the whole world. The only thing you'll find out in the wilderness is small packs of wolves, bears or armored lizard type things. In the cities and villages you fight humans. You parry and attack these enemies, or you dodge and attack them, or you sling some spells or let off a pistol blast. There isn't much going on to think about or strategise over.

I dunno man, grab this game for $5-$10 like I did if you are really bored. Personally, even though i pushed myself, I couldn't finish this game. Like me you'll probably end up wishing you just started another Witcher 3 playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I bought this game, luckily at a greatly reduced price, I was hoping for a Dragon Age Origins type story and play style. Instead I find an extremely boring and empty world. The buildings that you can explore are empty; without lore or incentives and the "Open World" feels dull and scripted as you fight the same sets of spawned enemies over and over again. The characters are very bland, to the point of not even caring if they have side quests since you know it will be devastatingly boring to follow. Its not totally devoid of enjoyment but its close. If you can get it 80% off go for it but anything more would be a waste in my opinion. Maybe I have been made bias by Witcher III and other AAA titles but if I had one word for what this game is to me...it would be "Pointless." Hey, maybe you might like the grind and enjoy it. I keep trying to get into it, but the lackluster story and characters keep making it a chore and it just seems....Pointless.\t\t\t\t\t
