\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - In lot of ways this game reminds me of Witcher 2, and Mass Effect 1, both in style of game, but also in sense that it feels like it's just around the corner of joining the all time greats such as Witcher 3, and Mass Effect 2

Things I disliked most:
❌Lack of environment variety - cities and open-world kind of feel the same. Unique quests do set them apart, but it is definitely something noticable after 10-15 hours of gameplay
❌ Lack of enemy variety - what I mean by this is mostly aimed at those brown bear-like creatures, there are soo many of them, and it becomes very annoying to see them for 100th time. What makes it noticable is that all of them have this dark/brown color, so even if there is different enemy, because it has same color it feels like it's same enemy.
❌ (small nitpick) Not being able to fast travel from anywhere - this is very small thing, but after ~15 hours it was bit annoying that I couldn't just teleport to other areas, but had to walk to the campfire and then teleport, it's not big issue but something that annoyed me

Things I liked most:
✔️Ability to solve quests in multiple ways - Here is some example - you can convince guards to let you in, you can disquise yourself as sailor and walk around like in Hitman games, you can craft bomb and blow up wall, you can make sleeping pills and poison drink so guards fall a sleep, or you can just fight through. In my opinion this is essence of great RPG and this game has it! :)
✔️RPG mechanics - charisma, science, crafting, vigor, lockpicking etc. they aren't all feel like they have place and are useful, while at same time not feeling like they are too much
✔️Story, characters and quality of quests - absolute highlight of game, quests are on par with some of the best RPGs out there, very fun.
✔️Being able to pause time in fight and plan out next move - YES, more games need to add this, it reminds me of Dragon Age Origins
✔️Companions - All are unique, interesting, have well written lines, great quests associated with them, and you can romance them. They have been great addition to the journey, and they don't make game too easy, so no reason to not bring them with you.
✔️Boss fights are cool + combat is fun - it's not Sekiro/Elden Ring, but it's pretty good, it doesn't feel too hard, and it doesn't feel too easy, I have played as technician and combat felt balanced on Hard difficulty
✔️Arena - I love when RPG has arena, and this one is fun + has nice quests associated with it.


All in all, I have enjoyed this game very much, and am excited to see what devs do for the future! I will recommend this game to anybody who enjoyed more "talk" oriented RPGs (like Witcher and Mass Effect)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Has some great ideas, but is not enough butter spread across too much toast. Don't buy it for more than $20.


Greedfall is not a bad game. In-fact, I would say that there is a lot of good to see across it's large fantasy island. However, Greedfalls uncomfortably short in a LOT of areas, that leaves the experience feeling half-baked.

The most obvious of it's issues is reuse of assets. With the exclusion of the rare cave. most of which feel grey and dull, every structure you can enter is reused multiple time. With some of these, maybe you'd be forgiven. Maybe the Nauts construct every warehouse on the island, and they do it identically, every single time. However, 7 other buildings that are always identical are:

Every guard barracks, fighting arena, prison, and inn on the island, all contained in one building
The Congregation Embassy in every city, including in the town OWNED BY THE CONGREGATION
Every native villager building with a roof
Every building owned by a secondary faction inside of a town
Every building owned by any non-native faction's business building
Every building owned by any non-native faction's barracks
THE CAPITOL PALACES FOR THE INDIVIDUAL NATION'S GOVERNERS!

Different areas may try and change what assets are sitting in a room, one faction will have a lot of pillows, another will have a lot of pews, but they're just the same buildings. It can become disorienting when you leave one of these identical buildings, especially ones with fast travel points, and realize you're not in the city you though you were.

This same reusing of assets leaks elsewhere, from enemy re-use, which is less noticeably, to it's individual stories, which are often posed are ethical dilemmas, except for the fact that the person "in the wrong" in every situation is cartoonishly evil, making the game's "preferred" outcomes to be quite obvious.

Like underdone bread, Greedfall shows some promise. Especially early on, the game's combat was varied and enjoyable, and I found the proximity between story and gameplay was especially tight. I found a couple of the characters had engaging stories, and I was compelled to finish 99% of the game's content, (an early mission about finding pages proved beyond the grasp of my attention) but, by the end I had to drag myself across the last 6 or so hours of death-by-papercutting Dark Souls bosses for an absolute let down of an ending.

So, should you buy it? Not for more than $20, even if you like the kind of combat and story this game presents itself as. I suppose it's not too bad, for a game literally made by Spiders.\t\t\t\t\t
