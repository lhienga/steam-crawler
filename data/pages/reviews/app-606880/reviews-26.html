\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not sure if I can recommend that game, it is nice, and I am kinda enjoying it right now, but the combats have one major flaw, which really ruins it for me :
If an enemy attacks you, you need to dodge those attacks, and the enemy will follow you, which is nice, but dodging moves you further away from the enemie's spawn point, and if you're getting too far away from the spawn point, they will run back towards the spawn and instantly regenerate all their life, even if they were just next to you before... This is for me a major flaw and I have absolutely no idea why the developers implemented it that way, what were they thinking?... The enemies don't even need to run far from the spawn point, only 20 meters or so... This is especially bad if you're fighting bosses (imagine fighting a difficult boss for 20 minutes straight, where you consumed all your health potions, then the boss goes back to the spawn with full health...), and certain attacks can also push them too far away from the spawn point, which would heal them entirely...

Dear developers : Please, if you're making a GreedFall II, don't make that same mistake anymore...

EDIT : Now that I finished GreedFall, I give it a 7/10, it is really good but also sometimes really annoying at the same time, annoying because it has many small issues I#ll list bellow:

- The issue I mentioned above is pretty bad, but I kinda got used to it by trying to stay as close as possible to the ennemie's spawn point

- The map is divided in regions, and each region is kinda like a "maze" that has walls everywhere, those walls are sometimes visible, sometimes they aren't, so if you want to go from a point A to a point B, you need to constantly open the map otherwise you might take the wrong path or you get stuck at a dead end. => The issue here is that there's no minimap that is constantly visible on the screen, that would have been really helpful.

- Fast travel works from checkpoint to checkpoint, but there are not enough checkpoints, sometimes you need to walk quite a bit to reach a checkpoint => It would have been less annoying if we could fast travel from anywhere.

- Certain side quests take hours to finish because you constantly need to move from a point A, to a Point B, to a point C, then back to point A, then back to point C etc. The fast travel issue makes it worse.

Then for the rest, this game is amazing, I enjoyed the story, the characters are well written, many choices that have a true impact, multiple endings, and to get the best possible ending, I would suggest to do all side quests. Without the issues I mentioned above, it would have been a 9/10 easily.

But can I still recommend this game? The issues I mentioned are annoying and will probably discourage a lot of players to continue playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a good game. You should try it.

I love RPGs. They're all also kind of bad. The term brings so much with it. In addition to quantifiable varied character progression RPG is supposed to have a complex story, choices in that story, a world with deep lore, exploration, character customization, some degree of living world simulation, even crafting and romances are expected for some reason. Most good and beloved RPGs do all of that and botch some, if not most parts of the formula. You don't have to be a fundamentalist grognard to acknowledge that Mass Effect games have boring character progression and customization as well as exploration, rewards, and so on.

Greedfall is interesting in that it does everything you expect RPG to do, and it does so at least decently. It looks very cheap while doing so, everything is recycled many times, the enemy and equipment variety is limited, and there are like 5 interior layouts repeated throughout the whole game and 10 generic NPC faces. Almost every location is indistinguishable from the other, they're all a forest with some cliffs. This didn't bother me though, and sometimes the game manages to look pretty. Modern technology can render some very nice-looking parquet and it's criminally underused in modern gaming! And there aren't many bugs, it's all fine from a technical standpoint, and works well on Steam Deck.

But, as I've said, all the parts of the game are solid. Character progression choices are interesting, varied, clear, and instantly noticeable. The combat is quick and tactical as it can be for an action RPG. The story is OK, and the choices you make feel well articulated and meaty - it never feels artificial or non-sensical like it often is in BioWare games. Even small choices in dialogue, while rare, all feel interesting in important. There's some exploration in the game and appropriate rewards. Crafting is there and it's well done, engaging with it is an interesting choice. Even the challenge is fine and consistent, a real rarity in RPGs! I can't say anything bad about this game.

Except I'm bored to death. When people talk about AAA development they often call big games too mainstream, devoid of artistic expression. I never saw it like that, I think even big flops like Mass Effect Andromeda had some grand artistic vision behind them. And Greedfall is so bland and unremarkable. You might think the setting looks interesting, it's 17th-century Fantasy Europe colonization. And while there are some nice tricorns it's bleached and sanitized too: there are benevolent colonizers with some bad people among them interacting with the noble savage aboriginal hippies. It is remarkable how this game doesn't make me feel anything at all, I had to launch something else to make sure I haven't just become numb to earthly delights. If I play some flawed bloated misguided game I feel both irritation and interest and sometimes awe. None of that here.

So I don't recommend this game. But it's an interesting exhibit. I think it might be interesting to try if you're into RPGs and have nothing better to do with your time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe graphics are great. The magic and fighting style is very good. However, a full weekend of play, all I've seen so far are those 'back and forth quests'.

You start in a city, and run to the docks, at the far side of same city. Then the quest sends you back to where you started, then to the dock, then to the starter location, then the dock, the starter location, the dock, the starter location, the dock....are you getting the point. After spending 5 hours doing this, you are finally setting sail to the new land. Which...is a city....and...you run to the government building, then the dock, then the government building, then the dock, then the government building, then the dock....once again, are you getting the point?

Not sure when this game becomes fun, but I wasted a whole weekend searching for it.

The fact, that the game literally starts off with back and forth quests and no 'adventuring', is either a very bad start for a game; or this is all there is to your game. Not smart for a game designer to bore your patrons, as the start to a game. And the 3 street fights, consisting of 3 weak 'hoodlums' each, are all the fighting I have seen for a full weekend of play.

I'm hoping it will get better soon, the game developers did go through a lot of effort on graphics and the fighting style....would be a waste of $50., if back and forth quests are all this game does.

If you have the time to waste, and enjoy quests that require endless repeats, then this is definitely the game for you. If you realize you can play 70 other games with same level of graphics and good fighting mechanics, then you may want to keep your $50., and look elsewhere.

Fighting Mechanics: 3.5 star
Storyline (Lore): 3.5 star
Character Customization: 2 star
Gameplay Control: 3 star

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat were they thinking?!

I had high hopes for this game and when I started it, I felt like I was going to spend a good amount of time enjoying this world. The opening area seemed fairly fleshed out and had a few side quests to introduce the game mechanics but it quickly took a very .... odd turn in terms of story and mechanics.

The first sign of trouble was when a giant tree monster bursts out of a ship into a Victorian era harbor, which you kill, and then no one has anything to say about it. No one! Not a single "wow what the (*&* was that?" The game just carried on and the characters were like "Yaaaaa lets go sailing!" The combat was a little wonky but you get used to it and then it was sort of fun? Like I didn't hate it but I wouldn't write home about it either.

OK, so I ignored this first issue and began exploring the next major area which is this beautiful city on the new islands that everyone was talking about in the first area. That is when I discovered jumping is a skill that requires skill tree development. You can approach the 3 foot gap but your character will say "I need more practice." Very odd but maybe its a one off? Nope. They even made turning sideways to fit into a small area a skill to be learned? Your character needs training to turn sideways?!?! Who thought this was a good idea. This is basic level exploration in other games walled off with skill checks. It is incredibly stupid and makes navigation in the larger world far less fun.

So eventually, after some generic run around quests I set out into the country side where the game opens up a bit into a series of "openish" areas. Problem is you cant jump, cant turn sideways, and the map is filled with mild slopes which are insurmountable to your character.

The good news, or so I thought to myself was that I have a lot of exploring to do now with all the question marks on the map. Wrong again, There is nothing in this world. I explored out to "Tir dob" to complete a mission quest of hunting something. Along the way I discovered a missionary camp, 2 villages of the locals, and a small city called camp Eden. Well not a single npc in these areas had anything for me to do or even say. I entered a chieftains house for the first time and his greeting was like "your back, and now you know our secrets. please keep them to yourself" and I'm like "MOTHERF****** WHAT SECRETS?!?!?!"

Finally near the end of my experience I followed the main story to St. Matteu or something to that effect. A large, beautiful Victorian city. I beat up some old religious man, talked to an old women briefly, and explored the entire city. That was it. There was nothing else for me to do in this area?!

Now this game is very unfriendly to explorers going off the rails, in that, it literally walls off sections of the maps just because? Just logs or sparse branches your brave wanderer cannot possibly pass regardless of the fact your shoot magic out your ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hands. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a tough review to write. Greedfall's not a bad game, but it's not a game I can recommend given its shortcomings.

I'm only 8-odd hours in but it feels like 30, and it feels like I've seen everything the game has to offer.

Pros:

• Unique setting
• Combat feels ok
• Nice visuals
• Decent branching story options on occasion

Cons:

• World feels very cheap: This is not so much an open world game as it is a game with linear paths that occasionally have multiple linear paths. They are also very small in scope and painfully lacking in environmental story-telling and interest.
• Combat is very simplistic and one-note. One attack button, very basic offensive and defense options.
• Loads of less than stellar design choices, some of which affects the way missions play out.
• Poor time-passing system: You may be tasked with coming back in 2 days only to have the option to wait either a few hours, 12 hours before, or 12 hours after. Why tho? It's really dumb and inconvenient for no reason.

There's the start of a great foundation here, but it needs much more work before it's worth spending your time on. There are just far too many far better games out there, even much older ones, than to spend it on Greedfall waiting for a moment that never comes.

I have hope that Greedfall 2 will deliver on what Greedfall attempted.\t\t\t\t\t
