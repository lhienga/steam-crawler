I love and hate the ending. I worked really hard to get everyone to like me as much as possible in the game to get the good ending. But because of events just prior, I chose the bad ending. I felt like I made the right decision :P I was angry at the time but I think it just shows how invested I was in the story and the characters and how betrayed I felt. Which is good! All in all I'd give it a solid 7/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall does not do anything spectacular gameplay wise, but the quests and especially the side-quests are interesting; one particular quest has a topic which has not been covered before. The aforementioned quest I really enjoyed. As I stated before it does not reinvent the RPG genre but it keeps drawing me back to play it, you can see this game was made with thought and passion by the developers which I believe to be the reason to be enjoying this game so much. Passion is lacking with some of these big budget AAA developers and it seeps through infecting the game, with micro-transactions littering nearly every release, you have none of that here, it feels like an old school game.

One thing I would say is the game does not feel truly open world as many areas are broken up into loading screens and it can feel quite linear, but there are still many areas within the game which allow enough room to explore. I wish Spiders would make a Greedfall 2 to improve on this aspect, but alas, they generally only create new and unique titles; so this idea may never suffice, but it would make a welcome one. While talking about the world and the look and feel of the game, I do really love the aesthetic and design of the cities and surrounding areas, but one negative of this is that many areas in the world look the same because the world has the same colour palette throughout. If you don't mind this aspect, Greedfall is an enjoyable game. I left this game idling in my library for quite a while, so I'm glad I finally gave it a chance.

I keep coming back to Spyder hoping for the day when the team matures into the sophisticated, AAA studio I hope it can be. This game is not that step however. The title has good graphics, although facial animations and mocap are amateurish. The game performs reasonably well, with good optimization. Gameplay is similar to Assassins Creed, but without the much needed verticality. However, where the game fails spectacularly is the story. The story is basically a fantasy take on Avatar, with plenty of european hate and native love. You could predict the story's outcome without playing a full minute. If the gameplay and presentation are above average but the story is wholly unsatisfying - what's the point?
