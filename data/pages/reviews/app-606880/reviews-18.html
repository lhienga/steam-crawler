\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreedfall is a good game with mediocre mechanics, a not-very-good story, weird bugs with pacing, and hilarious balancing issues. What makes it good is the worldbuilding, the creation of a very interesting location with relatively interesting factions, values, and a conlang for the natives that is well constructed and immersive. Part of the ending, however, made me just want to play the game that is alluded to there.

There's some comedy flaws in this game - NPCs will spawn as you open the doors to their room, and you can see them animating away from a T-pose into doing push-ups or sitting down. Once you learn how to craft ammo, you can just shoot everything and basically beat the entire game gunning everything down and dodging (just like real life). Vigor and lockpicking (taking shortcuts and picking open chests) seem both really useless compared to the other skills (which give you the ability to equip good gear). The story is thin and predictable, and the idea that you can at the end of the game just be everyone's best friend without having to make any real compromises is just kind of a bad story.

But this is a game that was made with a lot of investment by the story writers and world builders, and I think the overall experience is solid. Just don't expect to wander around the world and find a bunch of things to do - stick to the quests and sidequests you encounter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust bad. Exploration sucks, you get locked out of so much content because you're missing the respective skills, because you're pretty much forced to put all points in one, which locks you out of anything else (Wanna pick locks? Well, forget about jumping, sneaking, crafting and talking!). This wouldn't be so bad if your companions were actually useful besides yelling for you to drink a potion every 5 seconds. In any other RPG with a party, you would have different party members that could do different things. Cant pick locks, because you put all your points in crafting? Dont worry, your Rogue can open it for you! Not this game though, no sir.
Getting back to "exploration sucks", the world is just EMPTY. I admit, I'm still in the early stages of the game (I guess), but seeing big areas that give you 0 reason to explore just hurts my soul. At some point I found a giant boss and thought "hey, this guy has to be tough and there has to be something amazing hidden behind him!". Well, said boss did exactly 3 moves and took me around 5-10 minutes to kill, because he's a health sponge. Anyways, after the fight I find a sword nobody in my "party" could use, BUT I did find a tomb entrance behind him. "Well, there you go, there MUST be something amazing in here!" 2 or 3 scuffed fights caused by a bad camera later I find A SINGLE JOURNAL PAGE. WOW, I REALLY FEEL AWARDED RIGHT NOW!
And talking of awards, in my first hours of gameplay I found around a thousand containers (+ lootable corpses), which all contain pretty much the same junk. I legit found the same armour pieces DOZENS of times.
Idk, the world around this mess seemed kinda interesting, but the gameplay just stinks. If you happen to be REALLY bored, and really need to throw out some money for some reason, I guess you could give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
