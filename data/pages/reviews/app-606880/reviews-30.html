\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoped this would give me a Risen/Gothic fix but unfortunately it fell flat.

Pros:
*GFX are nice/decent
*Companion quests flesh out the side characters
*Characters are likeable
*Story is decent
*Quests are interesting(story wise)

Cons:
The game didnt have a high budget and it shows.
*Itemization is crap. You find many items be it weapons or armors but for me at least they were all worse than what i already own. I maybe changed my armor/Weapons like 3-4 times max the whole game.
*Combat is decent but gets bland since nothing much changes. You can spec into other branches which gives you the additional skill or two but that doesnt change combat that much.
*You cant walk into most of the buildings and the buildings that are there are copy paste buildings.
*The wildlands have different layout and look like such but they are too similar in style so none stand out.
*Imo game overstays it's welcome and should have been shorter since well it's quite monotonous if there werent the quests.
*Dont buy the DLC, its not worth it.
All in all i was kinda dissapointed. Wanted to like the game. The framework is nice, they could have made a much better game out of this if they had worked on it longer and overhauled the combat system and put more effort into the variety of the game. Hopefully the 2nd game that is already announced will fix the shortcomings.

6/10 from me, It's paınfully mediocre but I'd still recommend it so the devs get supported for better projects in the future.(if you buy it, buy it on sale)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a scam. It's advertised as an open world RPG but by the time you realise you've been decieved, you're no longer eligible for refund thanks to 3 hour intro. Huge open gameworld is in reality only 10 or so small corridor maps (basically dungeons, but outside) littered with invisible walls and objects with weird hitboxes. Instead of role-play you'll get a running simulator with brief fights and long dialogues you have to listen if you want to have a clue what's going on. Those conversations are major part of the game and are fully voiced and as a novel approach in RPG-s - you can only listen, so you wont interfere with carefully crafted narrative you could not care about less...
Good things: looks pretty, soundtrack isn't annoying and worked properly, encountered only 1 minor glitch.
In conclusion: Annoyed the ♥♥♥♥ out of me at 4 hour mark, played it only to verify that rest of the game wasn't any better. If you want an open world RPG, this is not the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: A solid AA rpg with very open progression and combat that feels clumsy at times, but ultimately is more a way to move you through the story. a story that is at least somewhat interesting from what i have seen, and tackles a series of moral questions along the way. Its no writing masterpiece like Fallout New Vegas, but its not half bad.

With that out of the way, im now going to ramble about some ♥♥♥♥ that doesnt really matter, but explains why i enjoy the game outside of the gameplay and story.

The dev team for this game (Spiders) is a company i keep my eye on. I mean this in the best possible way, but they always appear to be learning their job on the fly.

let me explain: Greedfall is a decent (quite good for a AA studio) game with some interesting ideas that feel mostly ironed out and a plot that, while not perfect, Tells a story about a tricky topic. Greedfall came out after technomancer. Technomancer was a Mediocre game with some interesting ideas with a story featuring a moral dilemma about weather or not its ok to slurp water out of someones body, and a side of racism. Technomancer also came out after Bound by Flame, A very very rough game that had a couple interesting ideas that felt like a beta test of those ideas with no concept of balance, and a moral dillema that is very shallow, where the "choices" feel mandatory.

Their new game: steel rising, looks even better than greedfall. My point is that I appreciate a dev team with heart. Are their games always good? god no, i wish i could scrape my hours spent in bound by flame and technomancer back. However, instead of just being a meme and fading into obscurity, Spiders ran with it, improved, and cultivated a cult following by expanding on the systems they had in those games. Greedfall feels like the first one of their games i can confidently recommend. It is of course still very flawed, but a lot of effort went into getting this whole game dev thing figured out. We often take it for granted, but even something as sloppy and gross as bound by flame is quite far beyond what us and our friends could do.

So snag it on sale if your interested. Its worth taking a risk and slinging some money at a company that, for all the flaws in their product, are trying their best.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: 7.5/10

I took my time to get around to play this game and I'm kind of regret to wait this much. Why? Let's break it down:

Story/Character:

Not a very original idea but still, better than "a nobody saving the world" cliche. Being a diplomat and walking around politically untouchable is a refreshing feeling. Going to a faction leader and saying "♥♥♥♥ your ♥♥♥♥♥" to their face and walking out without getting arrested by guards and hanged afterwards is really nice. De Sardet had a distinct character just because of this role.

Setting of the game is also refreshing from the regular "medieval era swords and magic and stuff". Using firearms and (al)chemical bombs feels so natural in the game, rather than a gimmick. On the contrary, i felt the existence of "light" magic was more out of place than anything else (and... I played a magic build De Sardet).

NPCs and Factions:
Sadly NPCs were mostly bland. Siora is a must in a 3 people party and your third character can be interchangable. Kurt was my choice for most situations but he wasn't "necessary", unless there is a faction related quest. Sadly, Vasco and Aphra were both an afterthough for me. Petrus was alright, as he had some background connection to De Sardet.

As for factions... Natives were nice, but seeing so many different skin colored native in a very small island was, shall we say, irrational. I think they should've stick with a single "racial" type and just go with it. Still, it wasn't a major issue, just felt a little silly.

Merchant Congregation should've had more details, quests and lore. They were literally the least shaped out faction in the game.

Coin Guard and Nauts as independent factions were a different approach and existence of an mercenary faction responsible for the security of all other factions is politically funny. Existence of Nauts is also a little silly, as they are the literally only faction that can sail the seas. While there is a "science" faction as Bridge Alliance, it's highly unlikely these guys were the only ones that have discovered the art of navigation. But I digress.

Theleme, if you set aside the whole inquisition issue, funnily were the more humane of the two major factions in the game. Won't say more about these guys as it can be considered spoilers.

And last but not least, Bridge Alliance's sciency, progressy but "let's murder them savages FOR SCIENCE" approach was a surprise. Also seeing a lot of Turkish names in a major faction was an even bigger surprise.

Graphics:

Well... nothing special but I enjoyed the lighting effects, especially sunsets around the isle. Overall graphics were "decent" but probably most AAA lovers in 2023 wouldn't find it appealing. As for me, it was completely acceptable. However, character models could've been more diverse; even 15+ year old games had better diversity for npc faces. Monster models were also a bit repetitive and non-descriptive. I didn't realised what I was fighting until they started rolling towards me or started kicking me.

Character armor & fashion styles are carrying the entire game though. Walking around with a Tricone and noble attire, rather than full plate armor (which is still an option) or the classic "robes and a wizard hat" is what I've needed in a game.

Sound/Music:

Again nothing special. I usually take notice of the soundtrack in games but I can say there were nothing catchy in the entire game. Ambient sounds were also bland. Still, none of these were bad or distrupting so I count that as "ok".

Gameplay:

Game started challenging but after a little while, combat became repetitive and easy. I finished the game on normal difficulty and aside from a certain combat at the end of the tutorial, I didn't die once. Magic build path for the main character literally solo'ed the entire game, without a need for a tanky companion. Getting stasis as the starter ability is a broken idea. I completed the entire magic ability tree by the mid game (maybe even earlier) and literally rolled over every single enemy, including bosses.

Using firearms for a magic character is not essential but a great extra damage source. Building your entire character on firearms is sadly not possible, as the firearms in game are single shot weapons so you must also use either melee or magic to balance a fight.

I should also note that game is not completely hack'n slash but it's diplomacy elements are mostly based around a couple skill checks. If the devs had worked on that side more, Greedfall would've shined among it's counterparts.

Lastly, I need to add that stealth has almost no meaning in the game, not that it's necessary or anything. I had to resort to stealth in only one or two situations just because of my roleplaying choice, where I could've just dived into the mob and start banging.

In short:

Greedfall's main premise, at least for me, is it's art and setting. Gameplay, graphics and sound are all passable too but it might disappoint some people. Game itself is not extremely long (can be done in 50-55 hours, easily) and doesn't require a lot of commitment. For CRPG gamers, it should be a solid "buy while on sale".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood combat, RPG elements, exploration make GreedFall an enjoyable game.

The combat is something that will happen a lot in the game. It is challenging without being frustrating, though over enough time it can become repetitive. The boss fights present a special challenge here.

Greedfall is an RPG which means that you can expect to build your character with skills, attributes, and talents. The system is a little confusing at first, but it plays well. It also offers the ability to fix mistakes made in leveling up with "memory crystals" which allow a player to reset their stats. These are fairly rare, but there are at least 7 in the game, so there is some flexibility and options if you would like to play a different way in a play through. Dialogue is expected with your team mates and they affect quests and dialogue with other NPCs which is good. The reputation system is a little clunky, but it works out and it doesn't take too much effort to figure out what you should do to improve relationships with factions.

Finally, I very much enjoyed the exploration elements of Greedfall. The maps were very well done, and you notice lots of little hidden elements placed around, including crafting materials (which are everywhere). The game isn't open world, and you do have to play on preset maps, but they were immersive with options to find hidden treasure and secret paths.

Some misses: the stealth system is pointless. You might be able to kill one enemy before you trigger a fight, so it isn't very effective. Generally, it's better to go with a disguise if you need to go somewhere you're not supposed to be, and that is exclusively in Naut territory since everybody else just attacks if they're enemies. The crafting systems are also a bit underwhelming. You can customize items which is good, but neither crafting needs more than 1 point to let you be effective at it (there is a common item that boosts weapon/armor crafting and a companion perk that maxes it out early and the potions aren't that special). I'd also say that there are combat options, like coating your weapons with oils that are rather pointless. They can be useful for boss fights, but otherwise aren't worth the effort.

To conclude: this is a good game and I'd recommend it to friends. It has room for improvement, but nothing is game breaking in it to warrant not recommending it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: 5/10
- Cannot even bring myself to finish the game its so boring (just fyi)
- Gameplay is linear
- Assets are reused way too much
- Story is slow, and generally boring
- Weird fusion of eastern and western gameplay/design

Gameplay: 5/10
- Its slow & glitchy
- You must either attack once, then block/parry/dodge once (rinse and repeat), or just shoot everyone from 1000 kilometres away
- Your attack animations can always be blocked (and take forever) while enemies can attack without the animations playing properly
- Crafting does not seem to matter
- The game really wants you to role play as a class, and assigning attribute points feels very restricting
- Monster fights are just very slow versions of dark souls fights (and much easier)
- Some monsters attack instantly with no windup animation (hard to predict block/dodge)
- Very poor optimisation with low framerate and lots of stuttering even on lowest graphics settings
- The game blocks off early areas with high skill requirements, to encourage you to go back later. I have read however, that the loot you get is never worth it
- You can easily get stun-locked by enemies chaining their attacks. This is arguably just a skill issue however

Story: 5/10
- Clearly time and effort went into building the universe. But that universe is confusing and boring
- Guards/people disrespect you constantly even though you are from a noble family and hold a very influential position
- Initially Serene's (starting city) atmosphere is flawless (dead bodies in the streets, bandits, plague, etc (all well presented and very historically accurate things) but it is all copy and pasted and the allure wears off very quickly after arriving at New Serene
- Alliances and merchant guilds are the official factions, instead of kingdoms/republics (kind of confusing)
- Characters are believable and realistic
- The era that this game is set in (late 17th century) is a great choice and the style is fantastic
- Companions are very limited, but acceptable in their development

Final thoughts & final rating: 5/10
- I believe that if the studio applied itself, Greedfall 2 could very easily make GOTY
- It is very rare for me to not be able to continue a game. Because of that, I cannot in good conscious recommend this game; but there is something about it that sort of works\t\t\t\t\t

A beige game
The prologue area and a lot of the other areas are bathed in a weird, muddy beige colour, and this is a metaphor for the game as a whole.

Character Development
It's ok - you do have to make some core decisions as you go on. The biggest complaint is that beyond the mid game, the decisions you make seem less important. In terms of your combat abilities, you'll find by this point you have access to the core tools you need to complete the game, and anything you add beyond this is just another string to your bow rather than a game-changer.

Gameplay
Basically, a lot of running around. A lot of fetch quests, despite the developers ludicrously claiming before release it would contain none. The twist is, as a diplomat, most of the time you're "fetching" information rather than items. All the running around feels like pointless busywork and padding.

There are one of two moments in the game when you're forced to make important decisions, but most of the time you have little agency - being offered only one option.

The sense that you as the player are not really an active participant in the world is heightened by poor quest design. It often feels that you are following a set of non-intuitive steps to reach the resolution the developers want, rather than thinking through a puzzle yourself.

For example, one quest involves you unmasking a snake oil salesman by getting him to drink his concoction, which he does not want to do. This doesn't make sense during the quest because you'd assume that a snake oil salesman is trying to pass off something everyday as a miracle cure and therefore would have no problem drinking it. It only makes sense at the end of the quest when you find out his true motives.

Combat
It's pretty average, and gets boring fast. There are very few enemy types and those there are can be split into 2 types. There are bosses, where you've just got to spam dodge a lot, and groups of animals/humans, where you spam area effect weapons a lot. That's about all there is to it.

World Design
The core problem is that it is an indie studio trying to create a world on the scale of a AAA game. This means corners are cut. Assets are endlessly reused. All cities look the same. All wilderness areas look the same.

Lore and setting
This is a letdown. They made the interesting decision to go with an early modern colonial setting - very different from the average Medieval fantasy setting. But then the developers do very little with it. There are so many themes they could have explored - the Age of Enlightenment and the rise of science, social class, the rise of competition and nationalism, the growth of capitalism, the decline of religion etc.

The one relevant area they do explore is colonialism, but the treatment rarely goes beyond cliche. The natives are all archetypes of the "noble savage" - courageous, virtuous and ecological. There are all the postmodern notions that their knowledge is of equal value, or even superior to, Western knowledge.

The politics of the game are overtly anti-colonial, but you could argue they try to have their cake and eat it too. Compared to actual history, colonialism does not appear as much of a threat. There are people who wish the natives harm, but these are shown to be "bad apples". The people in charge are invariably fair and want to live in harmony with the natives, which isn't what happened in history.

Overall, the game world appears shallow and lacks imagination. It largely nameswaps things from the real world - e.g. Christianity is turned into a religion based on light - but these things are represented with none of the depth that their real world counterparts have.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an older game that was sitting in my queue and recommended by Steam as "Gamers like you love" or something to the effect.

Anyway, I won't bore you with a long winded review and while I did finish the game (Main quest and all Side quests sans the location camp one) I found this game overwhelmingly frustrating. It is 90% a walking simulator, walk for five minutes, 2 minutes of action, walk for 5 minutes, see a cut-scene, walk for 5 minutes rinse and repeat again and again... and again.

The combat itself is ok, though variations of the enemies is sorely lacking and gets to be.. "oh, them again" pretty quickly. Crafting isn't needed but I will say the mechanic behind upgrading weapons and armor is well done.

Finally the story is just, and I mean just interesting enough to continue through the end. But that could be just sunk cost fallacy because by the time the above starts to frustrate the heck out of you, you've already invested a dozen or so hours.

Anyway,.. oh yeah, the maps are also incredibly frustrating and you are forced to only walk the paths the developer laid out.. 6 inch rock in the way, sorry can't step over. A tree branch in your path? better go around and to get where you need to go is a forced loop around the map.

Anyway, I definitely won't ever play this again and forget about any DLC. Steam's algorithm "Games you'll love" was most certainly wrong on this one.\t\t\t\t\t
