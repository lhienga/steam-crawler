\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the coolest RPG games I've ever played and one of the coolest Action RPGs where we have a team. I must admit, this production has a few drawbacks.

Benefits:
- Game length 30-50h (If 75% of side quests are ignored, 30h will be)
- We are dealing here with the Colonization Age, i.e. the years 1401-1700 (XV-XVI century) - the only notes that suggest what period of colonialism this is from come from the DLC
- A quite nice inventory system, although some elements are taken from other game genres (In my opinion, information about the quality of the item from 0-100 is unnecessary for the colors of the inventory)
- Upgrading armor and weapons is adding extra elements or replacing them with something better
- Depending on our choices, we will unlock or block certain side quests (you can notice it when you go through the game again)
- There are romances in the game which is a nice addition to the story
- The game has a semi-open world despite the corridor construction and dividing the whole world into large areas
- The combat system is quite pleasant and simple - you can feel the weight of the weapon
- I recommend playing on a higher difficulty level than NORMAL/MEDIUM because only then do you feel the constant threat from enemies
- The soundtrack is extremely atmospheric and reflects the spirit of the island's indigenous culture (the story DLC introduces several new tracks that are very telling when it comes to intrigue)
- I really like the culture of the natives because it doesn't resemble anything known (These are the assumptions of the spot culture, which develops at its own pace, unlike many spot cultures that have not developed for hundreds of years).

Disadvantages:
- some idiot in the resolution scaling option forgot to add digits how much the resolution is lowered (there are 10 jumps, where you can assume that the scaling jump is every 5%)
- Romances are very simply organized without major fireworks and every character to romance is Bisexual (absurd)
- NPC routines are often broken or absent, the latter is more noticeable and this problem also applies to animals (In this respect, the game is Wood)
- It slipped my mind, but there were children in one of the trailers - there are no NPCs of this type in the entire game
- The most annoying is the vegetation that lacks physics and the stupid tree branch is an impassable wall
- Stupidly placed invisible walls because often the game does not allow us to reach the edge to admire the views
- Sometimes you can see badly fitted objects because there are holes in the textures: bookshelves making for the wall, Faith at the entrance to the palace in the city of Hikmet, tiles on the bridge when leaving the palace in Hikmet (in fact, it would be imperceptible if the texture of this object to be double-sided), simplified water / sea texture over a longer distance where it can be seen quite often and it is very noticeable
- The door opens and closes both ways
- When entering buildings, NPCs outside stop being rendered, and vice versa, it is mainly annoying in stealth quests
- I wonder why Kurt can't carry any gold two-handed sword with something like 100 I understand the limitation to symmetrical swords but for Kurt not to be able to carry a sword that you get from Guardian Arenys is a mockery
- When you have the first task from Patrius active and the coup begins, you will not be able to rejoin him to the team, which results in silent dialogues in Patrius's task - I haven't seen such a BAGOLA for a long time
- The game practically does not punish us for kleptomania
- How to put it? - each positive ending of the plot ends with the death of the main character, and nothing indicates that this has happened. Yes, it can be explained, but the bitterness remains
- I have to mention the broken difficulty level (really game developers should go back to three difficulty levels):
.1. Explorer = Mighty Idiot
.2. Easy = Idiot
.3. Normal = Easy
.4. Hard = Normal
.5. Extreme = Hard.

Final grade 7/10.



To jeden z fajniejszych gier RPG jakie grałem i jeden z fajniejszych RPG Akcji gdzie mamy drużynę. Nie ukrywam ta produkcja ma parę wad które kują.

Zalety:
- Długość gry 30-50h (Jeśli zignoruje się 75% zadań pobocznych to ze 30h będzie)
- Mamy tu doczynienia z Epoką Kolonizacyjną czyli lata 1401-1700r (Wiek XV-XVI) - jedyne notatki które sugerują za jakim okresem kolonializmu ma się doczynienia pochodzą z DLC
- Dosyć przyjemny system ekwipunku mimo, że pewne elementy są wyciągnięte z innych gatunków gier (Moim zdaniem informacje o jakości przedmiotu od 0-100 jest zbędna przy kolorystyce ekwipunku)
- Ulepszanie pancerzy i broni to dokładanie dodatkowych elementów lub zastępowanie nich czymś lepszym
- W zależności od naszych wyborów odblokujemy lub zablokujemy sobie pewne zadania poboczne (można to zauważyć jak się ponownie przechodzi grę)
- W grze występują romanse co jest fajnym dodatkiem do opowieści
- Gra posiada półotwarty świat mimo konstrukcji korytarzowej i podzielenia całego świata na spore tereny
- System walki dosyć przyjemny i prosty - czuć ciężar broni
- Polecam grać na wyższym poziomie trudności niż NORMALNY/ŚREDNI bo tylko wtedy czuć nieustanne zagrożenie od wrogów
- Ścieżka dźwiękowa niezwykle klimatyczna i oddająca ducha rdzennej kultury wyspy (fabularne DLC wprowadza kilka nowych utworów które są bardzo wymowne jeśli chodzi o intrygi)
- Bardzo podoba mi się kultura tubylców ponieważ nie przypomina niczego znanego (To założenia kultury plamiennej która się jedna rozwija swoim tempem w przeciwieństwie do wielu kultur plamiennych które przez setki lat się nie rozwijały).

Wady:
- jakiś idiota przy opcji skalowania rozdzielczości zapomniał dodać cyfr o ile jest obnirzana rozdzielczość (jest 10 skoków, gdzie można założyć że skok skalowania jest co 5%)
- Romanse są bardzo prosto zorganizowane bez większych fajerwerków i każda postać do romansowanie jest Biseksualna (absurd)
- Rutyny NPC są często popsute lub nich niema, to drugie bardziej rzuca się w oczy i ten problem tyczy się też zwierząt (Pod tym względem gra jest Drewnem)
- Wyleciało mi to z głowy ale na jednym z zwiastunów były dzieci - w całej grze niema tego typu NPC
- Najbardziej irytująca jest roślinność, która ma braki w fizyce i głupia gałąź od drzewa jest ścianą nie do przejścia
- Głupio porozstawiane niewidzialne ściany ponieważ często gra nie pozwala nam dojść do krawędzi by popodziwiać widoki

- Gra praktycznie nie karze nas za kleptomanie

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like the game. Game runs smooth, the combat is interesting and it feels good, the story, up to a specific point is good. There are different factions in game with which you build reputation with.
Most of the side quests actually provides additional information on the world, main story line or your companions which is a big switch from kill 5 bears kind of side quests we get in a lot of games today. Even better some side quests derive from main story for additional explanation of the events.

Skill tree is divided into mele combat, technique and magic. There are 3 main weapon type: blades (one-handed, two-handed), heavy weapons (one-handed, two-handed) and divine rings (ranged). They consist of 3 different attacks (left click, left ctrl and Q). And other types of attacks that uses either regenerative resource (mana - spells, fury - Q attacks on weapons and ultimate), or craftable resource (bullets-guns, alchemical mixtures-traps, bombs and weapon coatings). Whole skill tree is available to all classes so it is possible to make a wacky build, but, as always, jack of all trades will not be as powerful as a focused build. There are attributes that supplement skills and talents which only indirectly affects combat, but mostly not at all (Charisma, Science, Vigor, Craftsmanship, Lockpicking, Intuition). While playing you also get memory crystals (I got 7 of them) that lets you respec your character completely, that means: class, skill, attribute and talent is reset.

A lot of quests in game are linear, which is not a terrible thing in my books, too much pointless choices just makes the important ones uneventful. But the problem is that choices that you make in game are either good or evil. No gray area.

The problems I could see were:
There could be about 4 additional enemy groups, 3 of them should be human enemies because most of the time you are killing beasts and it gets dull.
The world is a bit big for the amount of fast travel points it has. Some quests require you to run between 2 npcs multiple times and that travel is very tedious. This could really be fixed by more fast travel points especially in cities.
No autorun, is really annoying.
Factions, even though some of them are at war with each other, do not care if you are helping the enemy. You do not lose reputation with faction X if you are helping faction Y and faction X and Y are at war with each other.

Now the big one. SPOILERS

The world suffers from a plague called Malichor, it is a humanity ending event. You sail with your cousin Constantin to this new island to find a cure to Malichor. You are very fond of each other and there is never any foul play between you and Constantin. Constantin is a prince and is now a new governor of your faction, and you are a legate, a diplomat. After some time, it turns out Constantin is also infected with Malichor. Which makes finding out a cure even more pressing. During conversations Constantin starts turning from a lovable fool character to more mature person and a competent leader, you also see him actively suffer because of the disease which makes the player start caring for him a bit.
And then Spiders (developer) decided to fire a competent writer and hire a guy that went to same writing school as Ryan Johnson but is somehow even less talented. Since Malichor was not world ending enough they decided to make a Saturday morning cartoon and make Constantin lose his mind and make him a villain that wants to become a god, and the only way to stop him is to unite all the factions and fight him... And the cure for Malichor: stop polluting the environment. Expectations subverted.\t\t\t\t\t
