\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery project has its potential, some higher and some lower. Greedfall has a very high potential, though I don't believe that it reached its potential. The setting for Greedfall provides the highest hope for an amazing experience. None of that is to say that Greedfall was terrible, in fact the combat was probably the most enjoyable aspect of the game. However the game at times can feel unpolished, and I will explain what I mean by that to the best of my abilities.

Setting
Right off the bat, I have to mention the setting of Greedfall. It's probably one of the more interesting ones, and it provides a lot of hope of having a phenomenal gaming experience. You come from a continent that is being ravaged by a plague called the Malichor. You are the legate of the Congregation of Merchants, which seems to be some sort of diplomat. An island called Tir Fradee was just discovered not too long ago. 15 years ago, the Bridge Alliance settled a city there, they are one of the allies of the Congregation of Merchants. But their rival, Thélème has also settled there, they are also an ally of yours. There is also the natives of the island, who collectively form their own faction.

So as the legate, your job is to form cordial relationships with each of these factions, trying not to anger any of the others. Though with each of these 3 factions holding some sort of animosity between them, you're bound to sour relations from time to time.

So does the story also live up to the intricately crafted scenario that the setting provides us? I think so. There are major plot points that I will try not to spoil. Some of them are more obvious than the others. Though even the most obvious one has the greatest reveal, you really feel like you ARE the main character in that moment, witnessing the reveal. It is so perfectly done. On the other side of things, even the least obvious of these plot points have clues in the story that one can follow. You end up looking back and thinking "How did I not realize this?"

Combat
The combat system is very satisfying, and it provides ample enough challenges, particularly when you're facing the strongest opponents. Though at the same time, it is very simple. You have a basic attack, you have a kick, you have a special attack, and then you have a flintlock, depending on your character build.

The most important of these is the fury attack. Your fury builds up by attacking and blocking attacks, and when the meter is full, depending on your character build, you may have 2 or 3 different special attacks to choose from. But often times, the best strategy is to play defensive. Block the incoming attacks and then unleash your special, you feel rewarded when you discover when is the right time to commit to an attack, and when you should wait and defend.

Unpolished
There weren't very many bugs to speak of, and none of them game-breaking. However at various times in the game, it feels like more work could've been done to take it up a notch. Cutscenes not making any sense, dialogue not making any sense, the worst instance I found is when one character was having a particularly strong emotion, and then the next second they're talking as though they never had any revelation, there was no transition.

A good piece of advice I can give someone, is to talk to the governors of each city after a major quest occurs that involves their faction. I did not, and so at the end of the game, I had a very long exposition dump from one of the governors, and it didn't make sense until I realized that he was talking about previous events in the game. It feels like there should be a quest marker to speak to the governor when such a thing happens, but alas there's not.

Conclusion
Greedfall gives me hope. I think the developers of this game have incredible potential. In particular, I was shocked when the managed to make a plot point that was so obvious to me, have a reveal that was filled with so much emotion. It really took my breath away. However, Greedfall is not the game where they reach that potential. In fact the game was hard to get through for the first few hours. But when you hit those major plot developments, you understand why people love the game.

Myself? I think the game had potential to be great, but there's so much that takes away from it that could've been easily fixed. I'm dancing around a 6 and a 7 for this game. It wasn't bad, I would definitely recommend it, but I think it could've easily been a solid 8. I'm going to stick with a 7/10 on this one, not a bad score.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Imagine The Witcher 3 but with 90% more jank and 2000% less budget."
--The Spiffing Brit, Sept. 2019

I wanted to like the game, I really did. It has an interesting setting... and that's it.

The game wanted to be the Witcher 3 with it's action RPG focus with a splash of Dragon Age for real-time-with-pause tactics but doesn't really come close to the quality of either.

First and foremost, a game should be fun and this is for the first few hours. But once you've settled on your desired combat method (and abandoned it for firearms), settled on a primary weapon (which you'll use exclusively) and a secondary weapon (which you'll almost never touch), and gotten used to the enemy types you'll encounter; combat will become so stale you'll cut the roof of your mouth on it.

Enemy design is limited to human tissue paper, large mammal HP sponge and large lizard HP sponge. Bosses at least had interesting design, but a limited move set and ages of telegraphing make boss encounters little more than extended mob encounters.

The single worst aspect of the game; however, is the absolute MISERABLE quest design coupled with some of the worst level design I've ever played. I really hope you like fetch quests that take you across multiple load screens. Or, hell, even when a quest takes place entirely in one load zone, I hope you like trekking half of a kilometer to pick up a seed or kill a critter so that you can foot it a half of a kilometer back just so the game can tell you to take your seed/critter bit to the opposite side of the world map... so that you can be sent right back to where you had just left. I know that fetch quests are a bit of a running joke for RPGs, but this game takes them as a weird point of pride.

Every map is a nightmare of bland wilderness with inexplicable dead ends and pointless obstacles. This is incredibly disappointing because the game does have some truly beautiful set and backdrop design. It's entirely ruined when you find yourself having to backtrack 3 minutes because you accidentally turned down the wrong path because your compass said your quest marker in only 15 meters to the east, but OOPS this is a dead end. I know you can physically see the marker floating above whatever idiot you're supposed to talk to, I guess you should start hoofing it back to that main road and talk 10 steps further up. I'm generally not a fan of minimaps, but CHRIST this game needed it.

Those are the worst offenders. The game also suffers from uninteresting characters (I genuinely couldn't care less about ALL of the companions), uninteresting factions, predictable story. Hell, even the epilogue is boring.

Save yourself the time and money and just download the Witcher 3 with some mods that add Victorian outfits and flintlocks.

TL;DR Bad combat, boring characters and story, HORRENDOUS level design.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClunky dog **** game. IGN should be embarrassed with themselves for giving this game an 8/10.

--The graphics are very okay, nothing to write home about in 2022.
--The world is fairly empty and uninspiring, taverns for example sound like they're full of people but usually only have 3 NPCs and a bartender standing in random positions. They tried for a european-meets-new world renaissance look but everything just looks extremely generic. It's like someone just played around with the Unity 5 free 3D models and made a game from them.
--The combat is bad. Seriously bad. And anyone who says its anything but should seriously re-evaluate their standards. Games that are 20 years old easily have smoother and more satisfying combat than this game.
--The player animations and movement are extremely awkward. At first I thought it was meant for a controller but I was wrong, they just suck.
--Small decisions really make this game a pain in the *** to play. For example, there's no way AFAIK to zoom the camera out. This means the player character takes up around 30% of the screen when you're just walking around. Another nausea-inducing decision is a little camera zoom motion whenever you open the door. This seems to add nothing to the game. When opening doors you also lose control of your character for a second which means you have to fight the controls until you regain control. Just having your character knock open a door would have been a far better decision.
--Continuing with the above point, player character control is so bad I feel like I am constantly fighting to get my guy to do something which again leads to an extremely poor experience in combat.
--I got about 6 hours in and the story was boring. Boring, boring, boring. "European colonists" attack "natives" is the thinly-veiled story they're trying to go for. Of course I didn't get to it but obviously (because the PC has a birthmark type thing on his face) it will come out that he was one of the natives all along and yadda yadda. Boring, boring, boring.

In conclusion: This game does absolutely nothing even remotely new in its genre. The animations are bad, story is boring, combat is clunky. Save your money for the love of all that is good and holy. If you're buying this because we haven't had a game in this genre come out in a while, don't. Just be bored for a bit until Elden Ring comes out.

“The word "shovelware" was originally used of CD-ROM compilations of mediocre-at-best commercial games that had previously been released years before.”
~TVTropes

Spiders. Spiders never change. And I'm not talking 'bout those cool MCU spiders. I'm talking about that French company. Together with Focus Home Interactive, they've made a pretty much perfect duo. A perfect duo of crappy shovelware that is, because since the guys released their cheap and ugly nonsense called Mars: War Logs back in 2013, it was pretty much the same exact game over and over again. As long as we won't count their first project – a cute, yet somewhat unfinished game called Faery: Legends of Avalon, all of their titles were the same. I mean it. It was literally the same exact thing over and over again. You buy their new game, you start it and you're like “FFFFUUUU.... not again...”.

Long story short, the idea was to provide us with some basic action RPG experience. We've got some basic stats, including charisma that can be used in dialogues (like everything else, stats are exactly the same in Spiders' games), we've got a series of tiny maps that you'll need to cross again and again, we've got corridor-like level design, we've got about 20 hours of gameplay, we've got factions we can ally with, we've got a couple of basic choices and we've got Fallout-like ending that depends on them. Doesn't sound that bad, but... it is. See, there are two things about Spiders. First – they're terrible developers. And second – they don't give a dang 'bout it. And as the result, they just keep re-using the same crappy code that was far from being good to begin with.

I totally remember how ugly, clunky and cheap Mars: War Logs felt back in 2013. When I played it on my PS3, I felt like it was something from PS2 at the very best. Can you imagine they just kept doing it right until 2019? Heck, I won't be surprised if they're still doing it under the new publisher who bought them in 2019, but we'll see, we'll see. What's important now is that, like I already said, Spiders and Focus are a perfect duo. See, Focus are scammers. There's a lot of shady activity there and what they did together with Spiders is a perfect example of that. Long story short, the usual formula was as following: they asked Spiders to make another one of their copy / pasted games from hell. Then they made some fake screenshots and began selling the game for a AAA price. Right until people started seeing the new product for what it really was – an utter garbage. Then Focus usually gave the game its real price tag – something around $10, and continued selling it in “Come on, it's only ten bucks!” way, while also putting it into bundles and stuff like that. In other words... yeah, it's a scam. Sure, we've seen worse in Steam and even the most major publishers are scumming us in the very cheap ways (because it just works), but still, it is what it is.

When I played their previous game, Technomancer, I was barely able to believe my own eyes. I mean, good God, y'all! The game that felt cheap and terrible in 2013 was remade in 2016?! Seriously?! So, when they've announced GreedFall (yeah, you tell us about greed, Focus!), I was kinda... I dunno, curious? I mean, nobody's shameless enough to keep selling us the same exact game for more than six years, right? Right? * SIGH * Good God, y'all... Good God...

To be honest, though, GreedFall does look better than its predecessors. For example, you can clearly see bigger budget here. Can't say that it looks good enough for 2019, but at least this one doesn't look like a bloody PS1 game sprinkled with some modern tech on top. Some landscapes here are actually kinda cute and for Spiders it's a huge achievement. Even the monster design is not so bad. The story became a bit better too. Sure, it's still painfully boring, all the characters are shallow as f and so on, and yet, it's way better than what we had before and choice at the end makes at least some impact. Also, they've finally tried to add at least some of the proper open-world-like feeling to it, so... yeah. I guess, I can say that it's at least a little better than what we had before.

Unfortunately, only a little. Because under somewhat tolerable surface, what we've got here is exactly the same piece of... code. No, seriously, it's the... same... exact... crap. And even some of the initial impressions begin to disappear very quickly. Take open world thing for example. Believe it or not, it's a lie. What we've got here is yet another bunch of narrow corridors. Except this time Spiders tried to hide that fact under some cute backgrounds that look like an open world environment. Without actually being one. One look at the local map is enough to see the truth, though. You guessed it. Same exact corridors. Like I said, open world in this game is a lie. No cake here. Move along.

Everything else works the same way. It's just the same old Spiders thing all over again. We've got the same exact clunky battle system, which combines melee with magic spells (there are also muskets this time, except they don't do any real difference), UI is simply copy / pasted from previous games, the quest structure is the same and is exactly as confusing as before (there's a couple of changes, but they only make things more confusing), there's the same exact arena, there's the same exact companion system, etc, etc, etc. In other words, you've played one of Spiders' post-Faery games – you've played them all. And that's exactly what we've got here. Again. Sure, here and there you will notice some tiny little differences. For example, GreedFall comes with its own “unique” camp system. But guess what? It's barely worth mentioning. You just find the right place, press the button and voila! Here's your new camp for resting and crafting. Here's your “big” and “important” difference from Technomancer. Locations became a bit larger too, but since they're painfully empty (no proper open world activities in this game, just walking), it only makes more time for you to get from A to B. In other words, it only makes things worse.

So... yeah. If you really want to know about what Spiders are all about, you may check this one out. Just because it's as close to the real deal as they can get. But honestly, just take my advice and stay away from them. You see “Spiders” on Steam Store page? You close that f*cking thing right away. Because nobody should waste their time and money on shovelware. But guess what? Their new game is almost finished! It's called Steelrising and (surprise-surprise) it's an action RPG. I still hope that all this nightmare will end with Spiders / Focus partnership (GreedFall was the final game to be published under Focus) and the guys will do better under Nacon, but honestly, there isn't much hope. I mean... the guys can't even come up with proper titles! Think about it. Spiders are formed by people who worked on Silverfall series. And what are they going to give us after the game called GreedFall? A game called Steelrising. Imagination level – demigods. Not.\t\t\t\t\t

The visuals of this game are stunning, the combat is fun, and the dialogue and story really paint a wonderful picture of this world. One of my most notable parts of the story was securing a patent! I was not expecting it and it made me laugh! As a person who loves to specialize in dialogue focused characters the choices and how the traits of the character interact with how the story is told makes it feel almost real.

I will mention that this game can be quite hard to run on a not as good computer even on the lowest setting. Also there are some bugs (My most common one is falling through the world after descending from anywhere and then teleporting back to where I was trying to descend to. I recommend this game to anyone who loves any of the following; visuals, combat, dialogue, sneaking, planning, magic worlds, fun characters, and interesting combat.\t\t\t\t\t
