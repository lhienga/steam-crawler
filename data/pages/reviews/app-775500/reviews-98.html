\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScarlet Nexus is a good game. It does some things well, like the combat system and the wild story. Other things are not quite as exciting like the side quests (generic but entirely skippable so that's good) and the bonding between characters (gift giving and leveling up relationship stuff). But nothing is particularly frustrating.

For an RPG with an anime-style story, the game has really good momentum. It starts with your classic anime story: superpowered child soldiers fighting bizarre nightmare monsters. It quickly goes right off the rails and kept me wondering what was going to happen next. Personally, I prefer games that take the big swings that Scarlet Nexus does than ones that play it safe with the same old plot twists.

The combat system is also quite fun. There are a enough layers that get added on that you always feel like there is more you can do or try but you never feel like you're forgetting a system or not using some option because it's completely unnecessary. It's easy to keep up with everything the game offers you without feeling overwhelmed.

If you like RPGs with combat systems that lean more towards the real-time action side, you'll probably like this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSCARLET NEXUS is an action JRPG by Tose and Bandai Namco.

The game features two playable characters who are Yuito Sumeragi & Kasane Randall. Both characters have separate stories and unique playstyles to take other monsters called Others. The player attacks enemies with close combat and hurl objects at them with gravity powers. Some thrown objects require a simple button prompt or control stick movement to execute a move.

Alongside them are party members that help them out with SAS (Struggle Arms System). The SAS mechanic allows the player to use abilities from the other party members. For instance, Yuito can use Pyrokinesis from Hinabi to burn enemies, and Kasane can use Electrokinesis from Shiden to electrocute enemies and turn on switches.

The sidequests are a big problem since they don't add much to the game and feel shallow. I also wish there was more enemy and level variety.

While there's a lot of room for improvement, SCARLET NEXUS is still a solid, competent game that's well worth checking out!\t\t\t\t\t
