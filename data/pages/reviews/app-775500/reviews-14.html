\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an anime, action RPG this game is quite solid.
The combat has a unique take on the tried and true "break" system many action RPGs are a fan of using. This one involves using abilities of your MC and party members to whittle away at an enemies weaknesses and possibly trigger elemental reactions to really push their break. Once the enemy is broken you essentially get a prompt to get to execute the enemy (and rather stylishly too I might add). I personally found that the combat become a bit of a drag for me later in the game. Some fights just felt like the enemies had way too much health or just wouldn't break and that's probably more on my misunderstanding of how to get the most out of the combat system but it still caused me to lower the difficulty as fights were no longer becoming a fun part of the experience.
The game is quite visually appealing in combat and just walking around. I wish they took advantage of this more and took the time to make more talking sequences into full cutscenes because there really can be a lot of talking in this game. I've come to expect this from anime RPG games at this point so I went in expecting this but it's really boring watching stock expressioned faces with moving mouths talk rather than characters with full motion moving around and conversing in a space. We tend to get the anime art itch scratched in these games with the dialogue also in there but then we're just staring at faces vomitting text. Maybe they could get away with this 10 or so years ago but anime production has been increasing in quality and these games need to step up and commit to keeping up with the times.
The story is anime quality and nothing to swoon over but it is solid and I found it interesting enough to keep me going most of the time. This is a game that requires at least two full playthroughs to get the full story as the game has you choose between 2 MCs to start with and you are locked into that one for that playthrough. I played them alongside on another and would swap whenever hitting a new act/chapter or when it felt like I hit a good milestone in the story.
The music is also rather fun and interesting. I have no idea what genre to classify it as but it reminds of a mix of what Persona 3 and 4 had to offer with upbeat tracks that sometimes have vocals but you're not really sure why as they tend to be nonsensical lyrics (not bashing it just trying my best to put into words what I was hearing). Overall though the tracks are all fun, unique, and set the mood effectively.

I'll recommend overall because I do see the potential for others to enjoy the combat especially. I really do think it was just a personal issue I had with grasping it. As I found out though if you can't enjoy the combat, the rest of the game doesn't have as much to stand on. So try it on a free weekend or the demo if that's still up and make sure you enjoy the combat before committing to a full purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most slept on games ever ngl. A little short and I got the full ultimate edition on sale but I would definitely have paid full price for the base game. Combat is very smooth and action-packed, also pretty good difficulty balance on normal mode.

I really don't get why everyone bashes on the story, the first 2 phases are a little slow but phase 3 is just a sudden jump in how epic the story is. The game then cools a little until phase 8-9, story's still pretty good in that part but doesn't hit the peak that phase 3 hits.
Then phases 8-12 (the final phase) are just constant peak and super fun. The dlc also adds in a few extra optional cutscenes that add a lot to the main antagonist's background and actually relate him to all the other events in the game rather than just the last few phases. Without the dlc, I will admit that the main antagonist feels a little too separate from most of the events of the story.
Also need to mention that this was all just on the Yuito playthrough. I haven't even started Kasane's side of the story yet. 30 hours for one playthrough is alright since Yuito and Kasane's playthroughs are supposed to be pretty different, wish it was a little longer though.

Also amazing music and visuals, really love the brain-cyberpunk theme they did here. Don't know any other game like it. For what it's worth, the game also runs pretty well on weak devices. Getting around 30fps at 1080p on my i5-1135G7 laptop with only the iGPU, which is still far better than what I'm used to (Nintendo switch 💀).

basically, the game is epic and one of my favorites in a long time, can't believe that it was completely overlooked after release. Probably even my favorite game I got this year, even more fun than the new zelda game. Hope this game gets a sequel sometime\t\t\t\t\t

Instead of being motivated to play the game, get to know everybody etc. Now I'm not motivated PLUS I don't understand why I should have friendly conversations with your backstabbing teammembers that are out there to kill you. The gameplay in the story demo was easy, except that non mandatory fight against Kasane. The gameplay after the story demo gets cranked up to a higher level. I'm baffled that this game seemed like a big project for the developers and ultimately manages to fail on so many fronts. No wonder no one is talking about this game, it's simply not worth your time. The gameplay, the biggest strenght of the game, doesn't offer enough depth and progression to justify a purchase and go at it. I played this game after Astral Chain on Switch, Astral Chain is superior on every front.\t\t\t\t\t
