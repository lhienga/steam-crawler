Tip: If you intend to do 2 full playthroughs you might want to do Hard on the 1st one and Easy/Normal on the 2nd one, to reduce the tedium of repeating the story, then increase again to Hard/Very Hard for post-game challenges. I suggest starting with Yuito because the most annoying/illogical party members are on Kasane's side.

I completed 100% Steam achievements in ~65hrs. Gave up on in-game achievements / post-game challenges (too grindy)

‚úÖ‚úÖ Dumb but fun combat. The game gives you a lot of buttons to press for combos and buffs, but it's easy and you can get by with mashing any skill in any order, or you can do proper "rotations" if you want to feel cool. I really enjoy it, not every game needs to be deep. It takes a while to level up your party members and unlock everything though, so it's hard to judge from the early game. Chaining skills that refill your bars to chain more skills in late game makes you feel like you have the power of God and anime on your side.

‚úÖ Stylish graphics, I love the "brainpunk" concept. If you have a decent GPU from <5 yrs, use this mod to max out the settings. But it still looks good on potato.

‚úÖ Music is nice, chill blend of electronic and orchestral soundtracks. The weakest track is probably the "nu-metal" with police sirens that plays during Brain Field, it's not interesting or extreme enough to match the graphics change or convey the supposed power/risk, but I guess it fits the teenage characters. There's no music-changing feature, which should be an obvious feature for the hideout; other Bandai games like Code Vein have a jukebox, but in this game with brain computers I'd expect to change music anywhere, or even collect tracks as part of gameplay (could replace the awful side quests or tie into the gifts / social gameplay).

‚úÖ I generally don't like the "social RPG mechanics" (giving gifts to party members), but the result is kinda cute. Party members will use their gifts with different idle animations, and the hideout becomes slowly more decorated. It's not just some friendship number going up.

üòê Missed opportunities with the setting. I like the near-future design combining modern buildings with holographic displays and brain-computer interfaces. But we never really interact with any of the technology in-game. We have network-connected brains yet all side quests are picked up by talking to random NPCs face to face. The brain messages use a standard text message UI, no cool HUD.

üòê Generally too easy even on Very Hard, once you have enough skills/upgrades to do crushes quickly ("poise" damage leading to instant kills). However one or two bosses can be nearly impossible on Very Hard if you're even slightly underlevelled, due to mechanics with narrow timings or soft DPS checks. Thankfully, you can change difficulty at any time (there are no achievements based on difficulty). I powered through on Very Hard just to see if I could but it was not worth the time, definitely recommend dropping the difficulty for any annoying sections.

‚ùå Total playtime is not that long for a JRPG (~25-30 hrs), but it can feel draggy with all the cutscenes, reused areas, low effort side quests, and "rest phases" that break up each game chapter with the social mechanics. Plus, you're supposed to play it twice to see both main character's perspective. Even if you like the game you might find it too much. It would've been better if they focused on one main character's POV for the game, and then filled in the gaps with an epilogue (or potentially an expansion/sequel).

‚ùå‚ùå Bad writing. There are some interesting plot elements like the convoluted time travel, the origin of the Others, but the overall writing is pretty bad. The story is essentially teenagers saving the world while learning about the power of friendship and how to talk about their feelings, which is a tired anime trope but can surely be done better than this. Characters will literally interrupt world-changing events to talk about their feelings, often directly to the person whom they're having conflicting feelings about. The "bond episodes" (relationship side quests) are particularly bad, fan-fiction quality writing.

This is what the typical scene feels like:

I used to not trust you, but now I trust you, but maybe not, but at least you're nice, oh btw we're being attacked right now by monsters but I'm still talking because all these feelings are spilling out of my pockets like mom's spaghetti, ha ha remember that time we had spaghetti with <character who died 7 chapters ago>? I'm so sad they died, but I got over it and will live my life to the fullest now. Do you like spaghetti? I like spaghetti but I thought you didn't like spaghetti, but I guess you do (liking spaghetti is my only unique character trait so it's very important that I can talk to you about spaghetti). In conclusion let's be friends, but only if you want to! I'm very shy and have 0 self-esteem.
