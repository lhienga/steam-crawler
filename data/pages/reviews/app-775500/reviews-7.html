\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is... Strange I don't hate it, but I don't recommend it if it isn't on sale. And this is for three reasons

The story has no clue what it wants to be and starts like 50 different plot threads. And wraps them all up in the most unsatisfying way by the time the game ends.
Scarlet Nexus starts as something like a cop drama, you play as one of two protagonists Yuito or Kasane. Both characters get there own specific campaigns until about halfway through the game. Until the story suddenly switches on what its actually about. More on that later
But during the sort of cop drama phase you wander around the city as a futuristic cyber cop hunting aliens called Others.
Throughout this opening, you'll see so many interesting plot threads. Government conspiracy, exploitation, classism, Dictatorships. The whole nine yards of classic Cyberpunk tropes. And when the game is covering that. I would argue its amazing. Watching things go up in flames and lines being drawn as the two protagonists pick different sides and are forced to fight each other in a war is interesting to watch. And honestly fun to experience as you will fight either side in a boss battle several times.

Whats frustrating about all of this... Is its Scarlet Nexus's B-plot. And honestly? that's an insult to B plots since they drop everything the minute the A plot starts.

And the A plot is straight up awful, even when it was set up all throughout the game its so underwhelming and frankly boring that it hurts the game as a whole.

The A plot is a time travel story. A time travel story that fits about as well as rotten tuna on a fresh hamburger.

And the worse part is all the interesting things from the B plot. Just get resolved off screen. Every single thing resolved not because you the player resolved it. Or you the player were there to see it resolved. You the player watch a cut scene of someone travelling back in time and hearing the resolution second hand in a line of dialogue.

The time travel rules are awful too. No real follow up, needed I just wanted to say that.

Finally to wrap up the story, the time travel lets in so many plot holes Just by existing you wonder if the script was written by chatgpt or a block of swiss cheese. But to be honest I think chat gpt could have written a plot with less holes. I cheddah believe that until the day I die.

Next Characters: Some are good, some are alright some are the most generic monstrosities that Bandai Namco have ever created
Yuito is you're classic dough eyed anime protagonist #69
He wants to help everyone do the right thing blah blah. The game tries to give him depth in multiple points, but by the hog tying his development to the time travel you create a messy charcter that has no beginning middle or end. His arc is equivalent to a soggy spaghetti noodle.


Spoilers for various plot points regarding Yuito: Quarter way through Kasane's story her group will time travel to the future. The future is a post apocalypse with only one survivor. Yuito and apparently the apocalypse is his fault because his psychic power is causing it??? Not gonna lie I watched this via YouTube as after I did Yuitos story I didn't want to play Kasane's story and Yutio's story glosses over this plot point. Honestly the specifics don't matter all you need to know is that his power is causing it and unless he dies or somehow looses the power the future is doomed. So what if i told you a major plot point of Yuito's story is the fact hes loosing his psychic power? The power needed to start the apocalypse? The game has no clue what to do with this, and hopes you doesn't notice the discrepancy. especially since Kasane really doesn't care or have much urgency to save THE WORLD after a few hours. Now for something even stupider, Kasane will be unintentionally framed as the murderer of Yuito's dad! Now naturally Yuito breaks character for a bit and wants to go on a revenge kick. For three minutes. Then suddenly he kinda drops it? like hes still bitter. But he treats it more like she forgot his birthday. Its really strange. And then near the end of his story, just before the campaigns get fused into one story. Hes actively making nice with her, even before its revealed who his real killer is. Yuito somehow has less growth or plot than you're average harem protagonist. I cant name you three of his personality traits if I tried. Yet the game treats it like hes the next Odysseus.
He needed somewhere for his arc to start, and it needed to be resolved.
Is his arc dealing with the corruption in the government and the tense relationship he has with his family. Is it his best friend trying to kill him? Is it dealing with the murder of his father? Him causing the apocalypse?
The game has no clue

The other characters tend to fare better there nothing new, and the more interesting characters get shafted on screen time. But there still interesting enough that I would say there a decent selling point if you want that classic power of friendship game.


Finally we arrive at combat...
Its good. but messy.

Its good as a 3d action fighter. Following in the footsteps of games like Dmc and bayonetta, you're not gonna see some kind of sick combo video but the game is serviceable enough. The main draw is the psychic powers, using telekinesis to throw cars and swing around huge wrecking balls and excavators never gets old. Later you can link with you're party members and use there powers. And these new powers can be super fun, at first. Until you realise most of them just = do more damage for some time. And all you're upgrades are just going to go into getting uptime on these skills.
Its not bad, but its flawed and poorly balanced


The game isn't bad? Its fairly unique, but just cause its unique that doesn't make it good. When playing the game I couldn't get the thought out my head if there wasn't a better game left on the cutting room floor. And that's not good for the game, if I'm playing a twentyish hour game. And am wondering if there wasn't a better game lost in some poor developers disk drive.

If the games on sale pick it up I don't regret my time for the most part. But as a side if you want to avoid being disappointed if you even get just a little bit invested the moment you get to the level where you climb a mountain. Alt f4 out of the game open notepad and write a fanfiction about what happens next. It doesent matter how good you are at creative writing. you will come out with a better story\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually start the review with the game's mechanics but there are points to be made here and stories to be told so bear with me. Or don't. This thing will be wordy, anyways. Nexus is a hack and talk and slash action jRPG and as such, there are two obvious segments of game's loop to be discussed here.


.. There's blood on my hands, but I'm sure in the end I will prove I was right..*

The crawling is a very solid base for the gameplay. There is everything here to provide a reliable 30+ (double that if you decide to finish the stories of both of the protagonists) hours of entertainment. There are numerous mechanics at hand and the game encourages - more accurately: enforces - mixing up the use of at least the few basic ones. I have encountered the reviews saying that, especially in the later parts of the game, the combat is tedious and repetitive and while I have enjoyed it till the very end, I cannot completely dismiss such opinions. Admittedly, some of the final dungeons are a bit lengthy. Furthermore, certain skills are locked behind leveling up both the protagonist and his (or her) relationships. That said, having maxed out my team, I've always had numerous options of interacting (and by interacting I obviously mean the merciless slaughter, since maxing out the relationships and picking certain skills makes the combat effortless on the default difficulty) with the antagonists.

The dungeons are always linear with the objective clearly displayed and only the most basic puzzles scattered along the way. The enemies are not particularly varied either.


..is light and clear, petals in the wind.. *

The second axis of gameplay is the standard visual noveling. During this phase, the protagonist talks with the team members, participates in bonding events that develop their relationships - and, as mentioned before, grant and enhance skills - crafts items and gives gifts. The crafting system is very basic and barely deserves a mention, still it is obligatory for creating gifts for the team, which makes it an important part of the game.


..is an extremely hard crystalized alumina, used as an abrasive. Hand in hand with sapphire.. *


The graphics are positively wonderful. And yes, I realize the fact that somewhere in the dark recesses of my nearly abandoned memories, lurk the times of graphic cards with the staggering 2 megs of vRam onboard and games running in 320x200px and (hopefully) about 20fps, so yes, I am perfectly aware of the fact that I might be impressed easily. And yet, behind how Scarlet Nexus uses the UE4, there is an idea. And ideas are bulletproof.

Take the general design. There is a heavily pronounced, almost comic-like distinction between the foregrounds and backgrounds. Most of the cutscenes are edited to resemble the static screens of a visual novel but rendered in a real time, so all the alterations to the looks of protagonists made by the player remain visible. And then those static screens smoothly transition into action when needed.

Additionally, the locations look spectacular. The use of holographs for example. Most of the trees are holographic. So are the flags and banners, and the fireplace inside our base. There is a nightclub inside of which there is a holo-lake all over the floor, populated with virtual fish swarming around the protagonists' feet as they walk around.

Attention to detail is prevalent throughout every aspect of the game. The gifts that you offer your party members not only don't instantly disappear like in other games employing this particular mechanic, but the characters put them on the display in the team's hideout and are seen interacting with said items later.

Music is more than OK. Does what it is supposed to. If the graphics in Scarlet are SS (on a scale from SSS to F) the music is a solid B+.


.. of a brilliant red colour..*

I've recently reviewed an uncannily similar story: telekinetic superhero kids, ancient conspiracies intertwined with teen drama slice of life. I've absolutely disdained the experience. It's all there, 2 reviews back. And yet I can't stop praising Scarlet. At first I thought it was because that last game was a visual novel. That the hours of hacking and slashing gave me the needed relief from the walls of text. But no.

It's the protagonist all along. He is extremely optimistic - and not in an artificial or exaggerated way that jRPG characters are often guilty of. His optimism seems learned and trained, often extremely challenging to maintain in the face of events he faces. He is definitely not the one of the 'saint' type who apologize to every enemy they defeat and is not the one to heroically (and pathetically) sacrifice his life at the first mention of that being required possible (I'm looking at you 'Tales of...' here), he is willing to explore the possibilities, to look past harms done to him even if he states it openly that he cannot forgive the guilty party. In short, he feels alive. And wildly likable.

I realize that perhaps if I had chosen the other protagonist (both their stories and personalities differ significantly) my opinion of this game might have been less favorable. Still, while not as great as the lead, the other members of the party are a tremendously enjoyable bunch. Sure, most of them are stereotypical to the fault but the authors somehow take all those clichés and gracefully build on top of them. For example, the 'clumsy girl destroys a valuable piece of equipment' scenario. While other games play this to establish the character, here it is used to make the entire party cooperate in order to overcome the situation, which in turns tells us a little bit about everyone and even does that in a particularly elegant way.

The story itself is the Fast and Furious of jRPGaming. It's an absolutely delightful concoction of mildly scientific fiction, traditional (but still engaging) teen drama and absolute, over the top, perfectly self-aware silliness that hits you when you least expect. Just take look at this (or don't since it's a mild spoiler from somewhere around one third of the story): The protagonists discover that their powers enable them to travel through time because it is not actually the telekinesis, but the manipulation of gravity that made them throw the objects around up to that point. It instantly made me think how - in a very limited, teen sci-fiction scope of narration - it made sense since all that gravity and mass folding the spacetime wibbly-wobbly stuff. And then, in that same sentence, without even blinking the eye, they mention how they're going to use it to launch the entire MOON two millennia into the past. I absolutely loved every second of it.

And, quite surprisingly, there are times when Scarlet displays some ambitious depths and attempts at symbolism if you're patient enough to look for it. Eight plus out of ten, 7th place on my 2023 game of the year** list. When you look at it this way, you might have actually read this last paragraph to know my opinion instead of struggling through this entire, not particularly sober in retrospect, rambling. Tough luck.


* I had no idea for the paragraph tiles, therefore I used the definitions -random thoughts associated with actually- for synonyms of the word scarlet: therefore blood on my hands is carmine and also a direct quote from Demons and Wizards, the crystalized alumina thing is ruby, the one with the petals is cerise, brilliant red being the scarlet itself.

** MY list, therefore, the year when I've played this game, not the year of it's release.


Do with this knowledge whatever you want and as always, try to have a wonderful life.

An underwhelming and bland experience...

I'm glad I bought the game on sale and only the standard edition.

Storywise, it is a heavy narrative game. Tons of talking and reading. The game's story is presented with still image panels with transitions. Just like reading a manga or a comic. Almost similar to Visual Novel style of presenting the narrative. Game's story is a little bit edgy on some parts. It starts slow and will get interesting just a tiny bit and gets crazier and weird as it goes but still feels lackluster all in all. The whole plot of the game is good but how they executed it is purely a mess. The story felt tasteless that following the complicated story a boring chore.

Combat is good. Stylish animated action sequences and satisfying executions. Lots of mechanics to learn and apply in combat. There are tons of enemy variations. The designs look creative but it is not for me.

World and exploration is linear with a bit of segways but still linear. It's not an open world game and there are tons of invisible walls even on the city section. The game's exploration feels restrictive as there are ton's of loading screens which breaks immersion. Levels are really repetitive because they re-use the same maps even on the main story. Some levels are really long so it feels like a chore and a slogfest to navigate through the main story using those maps. Also enemies on those levels are repetitive making the experience so bland.

Graphics is good. Everything is cool and stylish looking but after many hours it feels repetitive and boring.

I completed Yuito's storyline and watched Kasane's storyline on a short recap video as I cannot waste hours and subject myself again to a bland gameplay. Might play this game again someday, only time will tell.

All in all, I don't recommend Scarlet Nexus for it's repetitive level design, repetitive enemies, bland and poorly executed story. The combat is good though, but it's such a shame that you have to deal with bland levels and enemies where combat feels button mashing. I'd rate it 7/10 as I know the plot is good but is just poorly executed.

You're mileage may vary with this game, if you want to check the game, I recommend for the historically low sale and only the standard edition. You can then check the DLC content if you like it, good luck!\t\t\t\t\t
