\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’ve got some mixed feelings about this game to be honest, but I’d recommend it in the end.

Here’s why:
Just to get the bad stuff out of the way first, here are the cons (which aren’t terribly bad, but very annoying sometimes)
- A few hours in, you’ll start to feel like this game gets very grindy. Go somewhere to find someone or something, enemies spawn while you’re doing that, rinse and repeat. That’s the entire game. It’s really not open world, as maps are just one huge point A and point B. Combined with the fact that side quests are just fetch quests you need to go out of your way to do is also annoying (as the game already feels like one huge fetch quest).
- Story is pretty slow moving (which doesn’t help given the above point either). It’s one of those stories where something bad happens, then that leads to some other bad thing happening, and then all of a sudden the world isn’t what it seems anymore (not a spoiler, you’ve seen that trope before).
- With what I’ve discussed, the main thing is that you just don’t feel a sense of progress a lot of the time. There are two story modes essentially, and it took me months of playing this game on and off just to finish one of the stories. Your mileage may vary though.

Here’s the very good stuff though:
- This has got to be some of the most satisfying combat I’ve ever felt in a game. It is very unique and feels good once you’re in the flow of the combat. It gets more complex later in the game, but it’s all pretty manageable, and using the various abilities is fun and doesn’t feel confusing at all.
- Art direction is awesome. It’s anime combined with a high fidelity sort of realism, plus cel shaded environments that don’t feel cartoony. It’s a combination I haven’t seen in any other game yet.
- Music is a jam. It ranges from jpop to industrial to breakcore and basically everything in between. I wish more game soundtracks were as creative as this one.
- Characters are pretty cool with the exception of the cringe and annoying ones (Shiden and Tsugumi), and every line of text in the game is voice acted, so you don’t have to read books worth of dialogue. The bonding episodes make for good breaks from the action as well.

I was surprised that some things really fell flat for me, but I guess I’m just one of those people where if the game’s style is awesome, then I’d sit through it and probably like it in the end.
