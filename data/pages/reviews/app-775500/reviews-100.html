\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply put Scarlet Nexus has a nice blend of high tempo button mashing action and story rich cut scenes with likeable characters. With that said there's a lot of improvements needed if BANDAI NAMCO want to turn this new IP into a series. The game feels more like a AA title than a AAA one. Yet the game is priced as if it were a AAA game. It's also a very short game. A single playthrough will take you about 25-40 hours to do depending on your play style (doing all the side quests and viewing all the bond episodes). A 100% run will take you roughly 70+ hours and will require a second playthrough as there are two playable characters.

I really enjoyed the high tempo combat system. The game does a good job at easing you in with all the different mechanics and although there are efficient ways in defeating certain enemies, I never felt like I was "forced" to do so to succeed.
The story in my opinion was also one of the game's strongest points. As someone who is jaded when it comes to video game plots, this one had me interested throughout. It was a blend of sci-fi and steam punk with time travel and plot twists. But what really felt good was the pacing. The cut scenes never felt like they dragged on too much. The ratio of combat to story felt good for the most part. Also the entire game (or almost) is voiced with really good voice acting. I was pleasantly surprised by that.

Unfortunately though the game is far from perfect. The biggest issue I had with it were the maps. They were small and very linear with basically nothing to explore. The character's visuals were very nice but the maps were so bland and lacked any kind of details. Especially the last few maps. It borderline killed my experience. There's also very few maps in the game and you'll constantly revisit them for either the main plot or side quests. There also isn't much in the way of enemy variety and everything level-scales through out the game so you never really get a sense of power as you level up.
The other thing that really bothered me, and might seem like a nitpick, is that the game constantly throws combat tips at you even after you've reached the point where you're a pro. Usually this wouldn't bother me but it pops up in combat and obscures your vision... And there's no way to disable it in the options. I seriously couldn't believe that. And of course there's no 21:9 aspect ratio support, so your ultrawide screen won't help you with this issue.

I recommend Scarlet Nexus as overall I enjoyed my time with the game but it's a one off experience. With a bigger budget I'm sure this could be quite an epic adventure that could rival the "tales of" series. But for this price and the content you get, it feels off. If you like story rich action JRPGs then get it but wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
