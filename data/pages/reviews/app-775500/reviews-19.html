\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSleeper anime hit - should have gotten more traction than it did. It's a new IP, and so suffers from some unevenness - two protagonists with playable stories (that you can't play side by side) were a mistake IMO.

However, the combat absolutely makes this game - there's a clear sense of increasing power as you go through, with team mates initially just being along for the ride , and towards the end you're kicking monster ass 1-2-3. The variety of enemies is lacking, with later ones just variants of earlier ones, and the game basically tries to remix them in different arenas, which while fun, ultimately becomes shooting gallery ish. The early maps are beautiful and evoke the sense of a proto dystopian world well, but endgame maps are just abstract JRPG ♥♥♥♥ and so detract from the experience. The game lacks a sense of place as a result - there's two "city" sections but they never really show how people are living.

The story is a mixed bag to me - Kasane route is absolutely the better route, simply because Kasane has a great arc, and she's a more proactive protagonist, and is believably badass. Yuito in contrast is the typical naive anime hero who gets handed command of a platoon later out of nowhere, "power of friendship" plays in all of his interactions, and is a spectator/reactor overall. Unless you're a sucker for story, I'd recommend playing Kasane first just because Yuito will have you going "Huh? What? Where's that guy? Who's this?", with the game hiding all the cards from you until quite late when the arcs merge.

Makes me feel as if Kasane was the true protag all along with Yuito a second fiddle, but they went with a dual protagonist just cause.

Why a positive rating, then? Because the combat is great, the characters are decent, the story is messy but interesting enough till the very end.

The team deserve to have a sequel where they can expand on some of the cool hooks they've set up. Do it, Bamco!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScarlet Nexus has style at a level on par with Persona 5. The brainpunk aesthetic is just as good, if not better than the red and black 'comic' style Persona 5 has. On top of that, the music is also just as phenomenal despite being two completely different genres. Combat is fun and fluid especially in the late game when all options are available to you. Comboing SAS abilities is satisfying as hell.
The story starts of very strong with a strong element of mystery, but unfortunately the quality of the writing stumbles a bit once time travel is involved. Would have been a much stronger plot if it entirely revolved around the government and their secrets. In terms of characters, it's also a mixed bag. Some characters are quite well written and have clear motives for how they behave (Karen, Kagero) whilst others are simply enjoyable to see on screen (Hanabi, Tsugumi, Kagero again). On the other hand you have Shiden, whose a little prick.

Overall, this game has pros and cons, but I ultimately loved it and the majority of its characters. Hoping for a sequel in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start this off with a quick overview of everything: Scarlet Nexus is a game with a fun fighting mechanic, an amazingly detailed and designed world, that trips, falls, and skids on it's face. Equipment is practically useless, just buying the next cooler, more expensive weapon and moving on. Slap whatever equipment you have on and you should be good to go. Within the first two/three hours of the game, I was completely lost as to what the story was. If you play games for visuals and game play this game is for you, I highly recommend just straight up skipping the story as to not infuriate yourself. However! Towards the end of the game, even the game play is repetitive and feels a bit grind-y.

Now going breaking it down-

*GAME PLAY: As stated before, its fun and moves well. The few problems I did have with the combat I could fix easily with the skills on the skill tree. Tales of style weapon upgrades, pretty much useless equipment that just eases whatever stat you have issues with. (For me its usually HP) Flat but fluid combat. For those who take combat easy and don't want a complex fighting system. I don't have a lot to say here, it's the only reason I still have this game, that plus I ran over the hour for return.

*STORY: As a person who plays games BECAUSE of story, it was so frustrating to sit and watch as the story passes by while having no clue what was happening. I had no grasp on any relationships, and felt no attachment to those ?relationships? so nothing that happened felt impactful or honestly made sense. The main character (Yuito) is so wishy-washy, the main character is too stuck in her ways (Kasane), none of the characters ever have their reasoning or relationships fully expanded on, the villains are supposed to be morally grey when in reality it's just the mush-brained characters can't decipher between common sense and feelings. The story is basically incomprehensible. And having to play TWO >16 hour RPGs just to get a little bit of a clue as to what's happening? That's ridiculous.
*DLC STORY ADDITION: Worthless. Makes the mess of a story even more of a mess. Getting texts from the people supporting the woman who killed my dad saying they just want to talk/hang out? Why in the world would he ever agree to that, or be friendly with those people?

*OTHER: Graphics are really nice and every map is nice to look at. I love the art style of this game and the aesthetic choice. Not quite cyberpunk, but not quite dystopian. Every cutscene looks great, the character movements are fluid, the idle animations are cute, and the intro characters have when sharing powers is super cute and give the bare minimum of characterization. The UI looks nice but has odd button choices and the mouse feels useless in the UI. Definitely built for consoles.

Had a lot of the foundations of a good game. Just isn't.\t\t\t\t\t
