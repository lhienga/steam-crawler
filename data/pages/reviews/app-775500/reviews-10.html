\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScarlet Nexus is a story driven 3D anime action game.

pro:
- Combat plays like a typical hack-and-slash game, but with simple combos and a ton of mechanics. I found it to be the most fun once I got to the point that I could steamroll basic enemies.
- The story pacing is good. It spans ~20 hours, develops quicky, doesn't get side-tracked.
- The soundtrack is good (though not my genre).
- The English voice acting is good.
- Bosses are challenging (on normal difficulty).
- Graphics hold up fine, looks good and meets expectations.
- Most cutscenes and dialogue segments can be skipped.
- Levels and gear can be carried over to NG+, even if you change characters.
- There are post final-boss endgame challenge missions with new or powered up bosses, etc.
- You don't miss out on anything important by skipping sidequests up until the endgame.
- Both Kasane's and Yuito's story route have a few interesting story scenes and bosses that you don't see as the other, while the main plot remains the same. I'm torn between whether or not those alone are worth doing a full second playthrough, but the NG+ system lets you progress much faster and you can skip all the cutscenes that you have already seen, so ... eh.

neutral:
- The characters are a mixed bag. Some are interesting, but others just bland stereotypes.
- Aggressively Shōnen anime. Has an unskippable anime opening cutscene after the prologue, the story is "power of friendship"-themed, characters gasp at everything, etc.
- Fast run speed, but some dungeons are oversized to match, so it evens out.
- Lots of dialogue cutscenes. The story is told more like a movie than through gameplay.
- The game crashed once during a cutscene after a boss, but it auto-saves frequently, so I didn't lose progress and I didn't face any other performance issues.
- It is often categorized as an rpg, but barely offers any customization. You don't get to make any story decisions and there is only one ending. There is appearance customization in form of outfits (many are DLC) and minor decorations, but leveling up or upgrading gear just means numbers go up. There are only 2 weapon movesets, that of Kasane and that of Yuito, and both share a lot of mechanics.

con:
- Low enemy variety.
- Basic enemies are way too tanky on normal difficulty.
- Low environmental variety. 80% of the game takes place on city streets, city ruins, digitalized city ruins and construction sites. The game is very linear too, there isn't much to explore either.
- Enemy designs are gardening tools, plants and household objects on legs for no reason. Simply doesn't match the story.
- Too much information gets frontloaded to the first hour of playing, can be overwhelming.
- The "Poor Communication Kills" trope is at work here, lots of drama happens purely because characters withhold information for no reason. To the game's credit, the characters usually get around to share info eventually, just very late.
- I felt like the story writers were at odds, or changed direction during development. Some important plot points get left in the dirt (especially looking at Naomi's story).
- Combat voice lines are repeated annoyingly often, like the "Attacks to the outer shell are working!"
- Lots of little time wasters that can't be skipped individually, like the Brain Field activation animation, having to wait for npcs to finish writing their text message replies (why on Earth would you simulate that in a videogame?), the unskippable 10 second dialogue scenes that happen if you give a party member a gift to raise their SAS level, the unskippable anime opening cutscene that follows the prologue, the tutorial popups, etc.

Conclusion:
It's an okay game. Has lots of flaws, but is worth a shot if you can bear anime stereotypes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate 02/01/2024: Kasane Story review!

An Incredible Action realtime JRPG with lots of anime cutscenes

Scarlet Nexus wants you to pick one of the two characters; Yuito Sumeragi (assault swordsman) / Kasane Randall (blade juggler kind of fighter) and immediately throws you into a massive playable Anime.

The main gameplay loop is a nice mix with Action moments and Anime cutscenes, although many of the latter can become mildly boring.

Brainpunk dystopian future, brain-eating monsters and psychic powers
Now matter what you pick, but your main character will always use one kind of power: Psychokinesis.

In the world of Scarlet Nexus, each human being may born with an unique psychic power of his own, ranging from elemental manipulation (fire, electric) to body-augmenting skills (near-invulnerability, hyper-speed, shadow clones, invisibility, teleporting skills...)

What's incredible in this, is the SAS... it makes a OSF soldier able to "borrow" someone's powers for a little while.

The main story thing is that creatures known as "Others", mutated by "Extinction Belt particles, from outer space", seek nothing but eating human brains in order to survive. There are lots of them, and each has a very unique (grim/fearsome) look and skills.

Anime bonding moments with the OSF members
In order to progress the story and unlock more upgrades for your SAS (borrowed) powers, you must create stronger bonds with the other fellow OSF soldiers that will accompany Yuito/Kasane. This means you have to buy them gifts and engage in (semi-passive mostly video-only) story events. Many of them are optional, but also very required if you want to become unbeatable.

New Game + / Kasane's Story
Unless you started with Kasane on your NG0 / blind / first playthrough, she somewhat fits the NG+ theme better than yuito. Her story covers the other half of the obscure plot, and unmasks the biggest secrets you barely get to see as Yuito.

Her early/mid game plays out differently, as she's more focused on doing ranged damage, and her party covers offensive powers first. You won't have access to many defensive powers until late game, and you might have to practice her a bit before fighting well.

This also means you get double "anime bonding moments", but they explore different aspects of the whole squad.

Performance and gameplay
Don't expect super-tight gameplay for many battles, as the underlying game system kind of encourages spammy moves.

Booting the game on "hard" difficulty since the very start will likely force the player to actually learn some enemies, and act accordingly. Nothing too difficult overall.

Game's quite light on resource usage, and runs great/smooth for the 95% of the time.\t\t\t\t\t
