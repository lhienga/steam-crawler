\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScarlet Nexus is a game that had potential to be something great but falls flat due to it's poor writing and uninteresting characters.

The setting and lore of the world was initially intriguing, and is what made me decide to buy the game after playing the demo. I was playing the Yuito route but have decided to uninstall the game before the final phase due to my ever growing frustrations.

For a large portion of the opening act of the game, you are caught in between the conflict of two major cities and slowly uncover the mysteries of the strange circumstances you find yourself in. This kind of premise has potential to be interesting if the viewer has some sort of a connection with the characters who are experiencing said events. However, the entire cast of characters is bland, one note, bloody annoying at times and lack any sort of depth to them. This becomes slightly less egregious further into the story but Scarlet Nexus has failed to make me care about any of the cast within my 36 hour playtime.

Scarlet Nexus does eventually turn into quite a wild story which was a welcome change as I was getting bored with the current plot line. However, as my interest was peaked because of the mystery, once my intrigue wore off, I was left with the same cast of boring characters going through the motions again.

***Spoiler Alert***
The writers of Scarlet Nexus are very inconsistent in how they write emotions. Near the start of the game where Yuito's father (JOE MAMA) is murdered, Yuito doesn't seem to care at all. The only sort of emotional reaction we got was the cutscene of him hugging his father's dead corpse at the crime scene but outside of this, Yuito seems completely unbothered about this all the way until the end of Phase 10 (keep in mind Yuito's father dies in like Phase 2 or 3). Until the big thing in Phase 10 happens, it is Yuito's companions who seem to care more about his father dying than Yuito himself. Yes, he does pursue Kasane's platoon for answers but don't see any sort of emotional reaction other than "yeah... my father is dead and I'm a little sad". This was such a missed opportunity for developing Yuito's character in the early chapters, and when they do eventually address it, it's far far too late. In fact, they could have made Yuito an interesting character using this opportunity but instead we are left with a boring protagonist who never really has anything interesting to say.

This is in stark contrast to how they write Nagi's death. When Nagi dies due to his accumulative injuries, we actually get to see the emotional impact it has on Yuito and his companions. It's far from being a broadway performance but it's handled significantly better than his father's death (his name is literally Joe, literally asking to be killed off whenever the plot feels like it).
***End of Spoiler***


One thing I really liked about Scarlet Nexus was the combat, however, this does become extremely repetitive towards the end, and is completely overshadowed the poorly written plot. For me, the plot and characters is the glue that holds all the components of these type of games together, and when I am so disappointed with it’s execution, the combat becomes a chore rather than fun and engaging.

*** Another spoiler warning ***
One last major issue I have with the way this story was written is Ludonarrative Dissonance (thank you NakeyJakey). There are many many times throughout the game where your efforts don’t mean anything. The game even initially tricks you into thinking that they do at the start of the game.

In the first chapter, playing as Yuito, you are made to fight against Kasane as part of training. When I lost the fight because I wasn’t prepared, Kasane was awarded. Not wanting to give up, I reloaded a few times until I was able to beat her. This felt amazing because I felt that all the effort I put into beating Kasane was being rewarded when the story acknowledged it.

Unfortunately, this kind of payoff LITERALLY NEVER HAPPENS EVER AGAIN because the plot demands that you follow whatever linear story line they setup. The story will often chooses to completely invalidate your efforts in beating a tough boss by making you just lose anyway or purposely obstructs plot progression with characters who have no importance what so ever and only serve to drag out the story.

I can think of two particularly ♥♥♥♥♥♥ instances:
1.\tThe fight with Kasane’s platoon in Kikuchiba
2.\tFighting Nagi inside of the Old OSF hospital
In the Kasane fight, even though you have to KO all of the Kasane platoon members in gameplay, once the cutscene begins you are shown that all of your efforts were meaningless because the story demands that they are actually in a draw before a random girl shows up and lets Kasane’s platoon escape because of how bloody distracting she is. THIS GIRL LITERALLY NEVER SHOWS UP EVER AGAIN IN YUITO’S STORY LINE. I understand that she is an antagonist in Kasane’s perspective, but she had never been introduced until now in Yuito’s plotline. Rather than feeling like a probable coincidence, it feels like the writers pulled this out of their ass because they just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ forgot to make sure she has a prescence in both perspectives.
Another equally egregious instance is when you fight Nagi in the Old OSF hospital. If you die in the fight then you are forced to retry which was understandable. But even when you beat the crap out of Nagi, he is apparently completely fine and Yuito just collapses because of stupid plot reasons. What the actual hell was the point of making us beat Nagi and punishing us for losing if you intended for Yuito to collapse anyway? This could have been handled so much better.
*** Spoiler End ***

There are many more issues I could detail like how the revive system doesn’t seem to work half the time and how some characters are purely there to be a annoying without any redeemable or interesting traits or conflict.
TLDR: Do not buy Scarlet Nexus if you want to care about the story that you will be spending 25-30 hours playing through because it will leave you feeling soulless and disappointed. Would not recommend to anyone.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game had a good amount of potential, and for me, fell on its face.

The setting and the world itself is really interesting, making me want to explore it more despite the somewhat limited info given.
The character designs, powers, effects and set-pieces are all great, making me want more. But this is where the positives end.

Characters are emotionally uninspired and really shallow, as if the entire script was rushed or things were added in retrospect. Development is either non-existent, or really stilted and inaccurate for the character that is trying to be portrayed.
In the story, there were several times where it was clearly meant to be a sad moment, but as I wasn't given the time to make any real connection to the characters, I would either laugh at the fact the game was trying to get me to care or I would be outright frustrated that the game clearly wants you to care about something.

Game-play is cool, but frustrating. I expected there to be a block or parry function, but its all dodging.
Several times I died to the camera moving about of its own accord (or not at all).
There were times where I had meters of clearance in my dodge of an attack and yet got hit anyway.
BASIC enemies can kill you in a few hits, and others can be spongy as (usually bosses, but some grunts can be a walking tank), making killing them not a skill feat, just a time investment, even when you are well over-level. Not to mention enemies can outright stun-lock you in combat, which is fun...
The 'link' system is a cool idea, but they all feel weak, even with max affinity.
'Brain Drive' should be a thing you can active with a button press after filling the gauge, not just activate 'kuz you filled the bar.

This game feels like the Devs started creating a combat game and moved it to story game with combat, which is ironic, considering the story feels tacked on and the combat is the best part.

Buy it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
