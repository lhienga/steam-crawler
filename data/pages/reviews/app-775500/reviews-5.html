\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game gave me a very mixed experience. Some parts seem very well designed and thought out while others seem like just filler. The later areas in particular just seemed like a slog where you have to go through very samey areas fighting wave after wave of the same 4-5 types of enemies. The battle system can be fun, but there are certain times where the game throws tons of enemies at you on screen and it's hard to keep track of anything, it just turns into a mess. Fighting one big giant monster was usually fun though. The default dodge is garbage though and really hurts a game with this kind of combat. Story and characters were ok, although the plot seemed rushed at some points.

Overall: There's parts about this game that are great, but I can't really recommend it due to how repetitive and tedious some areas became to fight through. I had to just lower the difficulty all the way to very easy from normal towards the end of the game to see the ending, not because it was too hard, but because there were just too many waves of the same enemies the game kept throwing at you and it became very tedious and boring. I played all the way through the male character's story and have zero interest going back and playing through the female's story just due to the tedious slog of samey enemy waves. I think they probably could've cut about 1/3 or so out of many of the dungeons and the game would be better for it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStepping into the era of modern hack and slash genres, Scarlet Nexus welcomes you to its brain-punk world of technology and supernatural powers. It’s a new entry for fans of iconic hack-and-slash franchises like Devil May Cry and Bayonetta. On a side note, I’ve reached phase 2 of the game in less than ten hours of playtime (6.2 hrs to be exact), and I believe it's a good start to share my initial thoughts on Scarlet Nexus. So, prepare yourselves as we move things and ratings around in this spoiler-free review!

Immersive Sounds and Graphics
As we immerse ourselves in the world of Scarlet Nexus, the game unfolds a captivating audio and visual experience for our inner weeb.

The game welcomes you with striking and immersive background music that sets the tone right from the start (I’ll leave that to you!). The choice of sound effects for every button press and menu selection enhances the overall user interface experience. Whether opting for the Japanese or English voices, the emotions conveyed are spot on! The game also has a dynamic soundtrack; seamlessly transitioning from chill background music as you start to explore and navigate to intense background music as the tension also heightens during combat scenarios. The sound effects, including those for attacks, finishers, powers, and enemies, contribute to the immersive experience of the gameplay. Notably, the satisfaction derived from the sounds of throwing and smashing objects at enemies also takes the game to the next level.
The game's environment feels similar to watching a 90s anime, thanks to its art style that immerses players in the game. However, there is room for improvement in the texture used; a slight enhancement could elevate the overall visual impact.

Mysterious and Compelling Story
Aside from the captivating audio and visual experience, the story of the game also has an engaging narrative. Imagine this: you are in a brain-punk world with advanced technology granting people psychic powers, however, humanity faces a threat from deranged mutants known as Others. As humanity’s last line of defense, it's up to you and your team to take extreme measures.

Moreover, I like how there are separate storylines, which you must complete to uncover all the mysteries in this brain-punk world. As I’m currently in my first playthrough with Kasane (the one on the right), I’m still unsure how different the storylines are or how much they would impact them. Nevertheless, I hope it’s something worth the wait.

Memorable Characters
But what’s a mysterious and compelling story without memorable characters? In Scarlet Nexus, you get to choose your protagonist between Yuito Sumeragi or Kasane Randall.

Let’s start with Yuito Sumeragi! He is a typical anime bright, pure-hearted, and positive-minded male (or young recruit in the game). His power is psychokinesis, which allows him to move objects using his mind. Also, he wields a katana for he specializes in close-range combat. So, for those players who love to go crazy with their katana and go straight into the frontlines, Yuito Sumeragi might be the one for you!

The next one is Kasane Randall (my favorite actually)! She is a typical anime kuudere who only shows her soft side to the one she trusts. She is also a new recruit who has a strong bond with her sister, Naomi Randall. Like Yuito Sumeragi, she also possesses the power of psychokinesis, More than that, she wields flying throwing knives, which splits into six! Pretty good for mid-range combat. So, for those players who love dancing and slicing with their throwing knives, Kasane Randall might be your perfect match!

The other characters in the game are just as fun and interesting as the ones I’ve previously mentioned. Most especially if you're a fan of characters typically portrayed in anime, I assure you that this game will truly unleash your inner weeb. Since Scarlet Nexus offers a wide diversity of characters and personalities , I'll let you discover the remaining ones!

Personal Opinion and Rating
In addition to my previous review of the game, here are some pros, cons, and good features based on my observations and opinions.

Pros:
JRPG Party System:
The game features a JRPG party system, where having a party is a must, especially in combat. You can also change their combat tactics/behavior based on your strategy in the party settings.

Various Characters/Party Members:
The game offers a variety of characters who can join as one of your party members, each having a unique power. You can share and combine powers with your party members, boosting your attack power and gaining various status effects. Thus, you can create different party/power combinations depending on your strategy, or you could simply stick with your waifus or husbandos.

Character Enhancement:
Characters in the game can level up through "bond episodes" where you spend time with your chosen party member. This enhances their power and status effects, resulting in a stronger attack power. You can also upgrade their status using plug-ins (buffs) acquired from shops, missions, or defeating enemies.

Enemy Designs:
The enemy designs in this game are so weirdly good! They are impressive because everyday things turn into weird, creepy, and satisfying monstrosities. For instance, a flower pot head with ostrich-human legs in stilettos is considered somewhat normal in this game, which again is really good!

Everyday Things as Weapons:
Do you want to throw and use everyday things as weapons? Well, I present to you the Scarlet Nexus! They have every single thing you can imagine. You name it! Whether it's cars, posts, boxes, pipes, tables, or chairs, everywhere and everyday things are weapons in Scarlet Nexus!

Cons:
Camera in Combat is Difficult:
Maintaining a stable and following camera during combat is sometimes challenging. For instance, when focusing on attacking one of the enemies, you can be attacked from all sides without even noticing. The camera may deviate from your desired angle, making it tricky to focus on your targets.

Difficulty Achieving Perfect Dodge:
Every time I attempt to perform a perfect dodge, I still end up getting hit. It's frustrating because a perfect dodge can be achieved when a projectile, an object thrown at you, or an enemy attack is extremely close in a matter of seconds. Sometimes, there is also a slight delay. Even so, how I wish it was just my reflex issue.

Limited Power Choice:
Throughout the story, you only have psychokinesis as your power. Since all characters have distinct powers, I wish players could also try other powers. Although it's good that you can somehow grasp other powers and enhance your abilities by sharing with your party members, having the option to choose your power would be a valuable feature.

Good Features:
Loading Screen:
One outstanding detail during loading screens is that when you are solo, it's just you displayed. However, when you are in a party, both you and your party members are shown on the loading screen.

Transition of Cutscenes:
Another impressive detail is the transition of cutscenes, which is combined with three types: storyboard panel, cinematic, and combat mode. All of these elements are incorporated very well, significantly impacting the overall game experience. I find this feature superb, especially since it was my first time seeing it.

Cosmetics/Character Visuals
You can equip your character AND YOUR PARTY MEMBERS WITH A VARIETY OF COSMETICS. AND! THESE COSMETICS CAN BE SEEN DURING CUTSCENES, WHICH IS SOOO SATISFYING.

With its pros, cons, and impressive features, Scarlet Nexus, despite minor issues, stands out as a remarkable brain-punk game. It successfully demonstrated its potential in the modern hack-and-slash era. Unlike other games in the same genre mainly focusing on melee-based weapons, Scarlet Nexus innovatively incorporates powers into traditional hack-and-slash gameplay. With everything that has been said, I give it a final of 8.8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, is the game decent? I'd say so. But it's not enough to keep myself hooked. The combat is okay--not something that could keep me in for longer, but it does feel serviceable to some degree. Having played other ARPGs, I definitely felt a pattern that kinda kicks me out from liking it, trying to find something like Devil May Cry (which was definitely the wrong place to). Graphics and performance are actually pretty great; I didn't encounter any stutter when playing and only encountered one bug where an enemy decided to just not attack me and become immune to all damage. Still, it ran pretty well for the short time I was playing it, and has a likeable art style.

My problem was some of the gameplay design choices that doesn't line up to my likings, as mentioned above. It wasn't bad per se, it's just that I expected something more, not realizing that this was an ARPG, and then when introduced to the combat, felt like it couldn't hook me. The deal breaker was definitely the story and the way it was written. It just feels like it has not enough engagement to be interesting personally. I'm pretty bad at criticizing story, so I will only say that the pace at which it was written (and the general plot itself I guess) was just not to my liking.

So, is it wrong if you like the game? Of course not, I'm just some other guy on the internet. If you like this game and its combat, then by all means, play it and enjoy it. I, turns out, am not one of the intended audience of this game. But maybe you are, so give it a try, then refund or continue to play it. It's all your choice, and I'm just leaving my review here.\t\t\t\t\t

Anime RPG. In those games, psionic-based technology is a conversation point, but in this game it's mostly a means to an end.

Don't let philosophy prevent you from enjoying this game however, while it may not explore the word-building and implications as much, that does not mean the material isn't there. Everyone in Scarlet Nexus, except for a small percentage of people, have psionic powers, and all of the technology in the world interfaces with these. Some characters are powerful enough to be basically superheroes, those are who you play as, the OSF, or Other Suppression Force. The brain-based technology is so prevalent in Scarlet nexus, some of the tools fade into the fog of ubiquity. The SAS, for instance, is a combat tool first and foremost, allowing you to gain a new supporting power that can often mean the difference between destroying an enemy, and failing to keep up your offensive, but it also is a piece of psionic technology that basically copy-pastes someone's psionic powers onto your own, sort of like grafting a map of their brain over yours. It also hurts like hell apparently.

Worldbuilding aside, the SAS is also a good microcosm for the combat system as a whole. Unlike other RPGs, Scarlet Nexus operates similar to Stylish Action games like Devil May Cry, or Bayonetta. It gives you a wide array of tools you can use, attacks such as the Backstep attack/Forward Spin, Psychokinesis Throw, Rush attack, Rising launcher, etc. and then lets you use all of them to defeat your enemies. In fact, if you don't use the right tool at the time, especially the right SAS, you will get hurt at the harder difficulties. (I'd recommend playing on Hard, then scaling down if necessary). Unlike Stylish Action games, there isn't a Style meter, but the SAS abilities are like DMC Styles. You got one which shocks enemies, one which reveals invisible enemies (more important than you think) one which duplicates your attacks, etc. and depending on the foe or your current circumstances, one of them will be more important to use than the others. The aim of the game is knowing when you should use the right ability, and then executing it.

There is also a dodge step. If you do the dodge step the instant the attack would have connected, you can unleash a fully invincible counterattack. The perfect dodge, if you can nail it, basically reverses the flow of combat in your favor instantly, unless they are out of range and you misinputted the melee attack instead of the ranged intercept. I cannot get the perfect dodge completely reliably, but once you can, you don't have to worry much.

The SAS is also connected to one of Scarlet nexus's most important components, the supporting cast. At the beginning of the game you get to pick one of two characters to play as, who have the same power, Psychokinesis, but slightly different fighting styles and roles in the story. Yuito has a close-combat fast approach and is generally heroic but kept in the dark, while Kasane does area-of-effect and ranged attacks, is blunt and disconnected from her peers, and goes through more important story beats. Whoever you pick will be the main character, while the other is a light antagonist at first and later on more like a supporting character. Of those supporting characters, there are over nine, and they initially split into five-dude groups (until they all join together and you get every SAS). These characters include, a grizzled veteran who has psionic control over a physical deformity, a scrawny professional kid who lifts weights and teleports, a cute young lady with a crush and fire powers, and many more. Most of them can be leveled up by going on outings with them at scripted times, and giving them gifts. The outings are exceptionally good, providing great characterbuilding for the side characters when you can get them. At first they usually end with a boring moral, thought by the character you control, but all of the "Bond Episodes" fill in more about the teammate's personality and continue their arc, and the outings later in the game can get crazy, and really good.

The biggest thing this story has is time travel. Because of story reveals, characters can time travel, but it does a lot of collateral damage, and they don't know how so it isn't used a lot until mid-late game. If you think there are mechanics related to this you would be wrong, time travel is merely a story device, but it is one that near exclusively makes the story more complicated. Several reviews that don't recommend this game bring up the insane story as part of the problem, and I understand them, it detracts slightly from the potential worldbuilding they could have had. But I also applaud how crazy and hard to follow it is, and I wear the fact that I understand the story with pride. I'm not going to explain it here, that would be wrong, you should experience it's brain-melting glory for yourself.

It's got good anime graphics, what more can I say on that?

The art design is distinctive. Everywhere the world is dominated by what appear to be holographic images called "Visions," these Visions are actually mental illusions and interfaces that can be interacted with people's psionics, and anywhere you look you will see many Visions at once. The visions that represent your character's powers and Psi-bertech are the coolest however. The red-orange cables of SAS is akin to the titular Red Strings (Read: synonyms to Scarlet Nexus) and the psionic world that is entered in states like Brain Drive or time travel is so stylized, filled with the Red Strings swirling about in the dark void. Any objects there also show up as visions. Look, the point is the world looks cool, and the Visions dominating the world set it apart from other future tech scapes with ubiquitous holograms.

The enemies you fight are called Others, and their design is stranger than many cosmic horror entities (though not necessarily scarier) and their backstory is similarly wierd as can be. But it doesn't matter how the 2.3 meter tall flower-filled vase with mannequin legs got there, only how I can style on it. Then you fight human enemies and find out they fall into one of two classes: critters with guns while you are immune to bullets, and absolute bosses who can take you and your team on, sometimes even single-handed. The human boss fights can be some of the most challenging fights, because unlike the Other boss fights, humans are not giant, their attacks are much less telegraphed, and their powers are unpredictable and very dangerous.

Side quests are stupid and boring. Only do them if you need to for a gift or a Bond Episode. You probably will need to for a gift or a Bond Episode, and they are also your main incentive to go out exploring the world aside from buying new stuff. The side quests are a bad incentive, and have no real stories to them or meat. It's always "Go here, kill that thing, pick up this item, phone it in." Very boring, pointless, repetitive, and stupid. Luckily, they are overshadowed by pretty much everything else, unless you're going for 100%, in which case, blessings upon you, good luck, enjoy your RGB Gamer gear.

Ultimately this kludge of text walls sums up my feelings on Scarlet Nexus, though I think I need to reiterate that I like this game a lot. I like this game a lot. Give it a try if you like: wierd time travel, wierd enemies, brain-punk, anime, good character-building, or stylish action-lite gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
