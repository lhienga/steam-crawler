\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this was a unique experience. It was pleasant but still challenged me to continue at times with a water puddle of a story, but has such engaging combat and characters it kept me going through both playthroughs.

The gameplay is pretty simple but evolves with the skill tree. The combat gets interesting with the SAS system where you borrow powers from your friends to make unique combinations for devastating attacks depending on the enemy you face, who are all different and require unique tactics against. To be honest, some enemies were â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥ with stun attacks and AOEs, especially when they get together and gank you, but that's where the simple git gud comes in to overcome such challenges. Tho it could get very repetitive sometimes. The enemies' designs were also horrifying and cool.

The game is set up with 2 campaigns/playthroughs with our 2 protagonists. Both have their unique playstyles whose stories intertwine, but their stories are made differently as Yuito is left in the dark for much of the story as something akin to a question arc while Kasane gets her revelations a lot faster and works as more of an answer arc. So I'd recommend playing as Yuito and if you like the game enough, Kasane. The story was very whatever, both the plot and pacing were very serviceable, but it's saved by an amazing cast of unique characters. The cast is elevated even more with the bond system which works by doing bond episodes where you spend time with the supporting characters individually and uncover their quirks and challenges they face which I found very therapeutic. You also increase your bond by giving them gifts which begin to shape the hideout which adds to the personality of the cast.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a summary, the story is interesting but potentially confusing. Despite that, the main hack'n'slash gameplay is really fun! The character writing isn't a strong point either, but DLCs improve on this and give some enjoyable bonuses.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The first playthrough is a blast, and endgame content is interesting, but playing through the second story could get boring due to significant narrative overlap in the first and last thirds.

(Tip: Keep your first save so you can transfer both characters into the same save when you beat both)

Gameplay is fun for hack'n'slash gamers, with a very interesting style-switch system. There are a lot of nice applications for the special abilities. Have fun comboing! If you like DMC's gameplay and are craving a more unique take on the cyberpunk aesthetic, you'll really enjoy this.

Your allies are JRPG companions, which comes with nice side quests but has the downside of only being able to get higher tiers of their abilities by giving them gifts. the gift grind is not too crazy, but a bit boring. XP grind is not a huge issue, either. Just use the "New Combat XP" item that comes with the game.

The story is really interesting. It gets a little confusing at times, but it's still neat.
Fair word of warning, there is significant overlap between the two characters' stories in the first and last thirds of the game. There are also heart-wrenching, disturbing, and somber moments ahead. A huge theme is coping with life's difficulties and not isolating yourself.

There are not too many fully-animated cutscenes, but the few that are there are super high-budget eyecandy. Besides that, there are a lot of visual novel-like still images and dialogue, but it's still done in a stylish way.

Like older games, there are also quite a few secret cosmetics and items. Stick around after beating the game and you'll be in for a delight.

100% must play when it goes on sale for 75% again. ($15 USD)\t\t\t\t\t
