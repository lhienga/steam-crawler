\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: Since this game has anime elements in terms of story, or characters, if any of those elements are something you don't want, then this might not be a good game for you.

Considering it was from Bandai Namco, It feels a little like code vein and god eater in some parts. not all, but if you've played either of those games, you might see where/what i meant. (never played tales so I can't say anything about that)

OP is catchy and although not the same songwriter as God eater, it somewhat reminds me of it because of the rock theme.

BGM sounds similar to God eater, but mostly Code vein. some combat zone bgm tho sounds like persona for some reason (i.e Mizuhagawa's theme)

combat-wise, although some enemies might seem repetitive and at some point locks you in for a few waves, having to mix and match the powers of allies can easily negate/counter/deal with them making combat really easy, and not too repetitive (unless you are farming that same spot over and over then yea). I also like having to execute more than 2 enemies in one execute move.

as for basic moves, Although many people might say having double jump or air dash should be a "normal" thing and not an upgrade, I think this game is trying to say that you start from nothing and are learning everything slowly. At the end of the day, you'll have enough skill points to upgrade the entire skill tree. If you are having difficulties fighting and leveling up on harder difficulties, then start low. no one's forcing you to start at a high difficulty when it's your first time playing. (there is an option to change difficulty anyway. otherwise, you can just play new game + to have your level carried over)

Characters in this game are at least grounded to the fact that even if they may be fighting "aliens", no one is crazy enough to be smiling in combat like a crazy war freak being. I believe it's because they want to set a dramatic feeling or at least say that this is serious matter. (idk, i just don't like chars smiling crazy when faced in a world that is like an apocalypse. maybe if the setting was like fantasy and not apocalypse, then sure why not)

Some may also say that the combat moves here is a bit janky or whatever since you can't cancel attacks or something. Like how you can easily be attacked by another enemy cos you're animation-locked fighting another. But the game already has other ways to counter that. Like using hypervelocity or Sclerokinesis. These skills can be cast even when animation locked and in most cases, saves me.

Combat in this game requires you to mix and match the skills of your friends, with your char's own. Sure you can kill with just your own powers (normal, special, psychokinesis) But doing so, restricts you in more cases than one. if you want to go the challenge of not relying on your friends powers all the time, then sure you do you.


The only thing i was hoping was for more large monster bosses where you'd do something special (either as QTE or something else). Which is why I would sometimes just run the simulation battles for those bosses . As well as human enemies where there is something special happening. Like when fighting Kasane as Yuito or the other way around, and you activated the special object for the pole and your opponent does the same

Overall, it's a decent ARPG. I can't really say i'd recommend this 100% since there are a lot of diff players out there, but on a personal level, if you like anime, action, satisfying sfx in combat, then you can check this game out. there's a demo anyway.
