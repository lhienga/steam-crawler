\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only finished ONE of TWO play-throughs. My thoughts so far...

It's an anime game. All the tropes you're expecting are here.
Friendship > Swords.
Relating to every single member of your team in some way and being the overly nice guy.
No actual bad-guy. Everyone is just acting on what they believe. The evil is relegated to monsters.

I think there were 2 things that really got to me. The game CONSTANTLY has "Win in the game, lose in the cutscene" scenarios. I disliked that my first actual bossfight was skewed to me losing and thereafter, made me wonder ever fight if I was meant to just be an underdog.
The other thing was the HUGE immersion break. Some characters just... Wanna hang out while in the middle of what is described as a full on war. Don't get me wrong, I laughed really hard thinking about getting dinner with someone who wanted to cut my throat a day ago, but it was a bit much.

The game suffers from, "We made a really awesome, spectacle fighter, with weird enemies (awesome designs) but they're so few in number that they have to do the pretty upsetting combat of just slowly upping the number of enemies they throw at you.
The combat is fun, but I really would have loved a dodge/parry that interrupts my attacks. Not a huge issue, I got over that quick.

The game is stunning to look at, plays well and combat is pretty fun for the most part. I wish they did a better job of explaining things,
Combat Visions? Oh, you mean something like Partner Attacks. Brain Field? Oh, you mean something like Temporary power up. Brain Crush? Oh, you mean something like a Finisher?
The terms they use are awful, don't help in clear detail for someone new and make it a info-dump for players that get far enough.

Yeah, I'm more into Western game systems, but for decent reason I think.
Either way, if you can put up with an anime game for a bit, it's good.\t\t\t\t\t
