\r\n\t\t\t\t\trating: 5/10
It could be way better if they invest more money on this (I know Bandai Namco spent most of the money on Tales of Arise, and that was pretty good one). The background is pretty grand: futuristic world, psychic power, monsters called Others......the battle system has much potential, yet the plot was not as good as I except.
1) I'd like to have some real animated CGs like what you have in the Scarlet Nexus anime, not those Powerpoint-like still scenes (which small indie games usually take for budget issue).
2) The execution of story, the last stage, is really terrible. The heroes are facing both the pressure from the final boss and the persecution from the government, but you "saved the world" simply by defeating that man and that's the end? where did the government go?? The whole middle half of the game is about uncovering the bad deeds of government while hiding from their brainwashing, but I actually never see its presence.
3) The first play-through as Yuito is very confusing. From Yuito's perspective, Kasane, heroine, suddenly wants to kill him without explaining a word. This happens at first 1/3 of the story. The heroine team then keeps trying to murder him throughout the middle stage, and the reason is finally revealed at last 1/4 of the story, which makes me confused during whole the middle game. I'm in doubt with the capability of the one who wrote the plot.
4) Character growth is almost 0 and the story about Nagi really lacks development, compared to Tales of Arise from the same company. For example, Seto's death is a classical heroic one, but Nagi, the one saved by Seto in price of his life, just instantly got caught again......yikes, the fate of commoner maybe? Fubuki was able to protect everyone else because they are either from famous political families or industrial powerhouses.
5) This is world where heros fight against monsters, yet almost half of the bosses are your teammates/friends.

Well whatever the complaints, the game is not bad. It's just not as good as we except.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Very interesting and unique story
- Combat feels great
- In-depth skill tree
- Runs very well
- Unique art style, character and enemy design
- Good length (about 30hrs long)
- Visually striking environments

Cons:
- Dialogue is poorly written.
Characters over-explain everything, repeat information as if the player instantly forgot what they just said, and constantly refer to other characters by full name including any official titles (Woah! Is that Former Major General Fubuki? We should talk to Former Major General Fubuki. Guys, I need to do this for Naomi. Naomi would want this. Naomi is the reason for my actions, because Naomi would want me to. Naomi, is that you Naomi? Naomi I'm glad I found you Naomi.)
- 90% of the game's cutscenes are presented in a visual novel style. Only during major story beats do they switch to fully animated cutscenes. The visual novel style isn't bad, but it makes it feel lacklustre in-between fully animated scenes that look great.
- There is way, way, way too much dialogue.
- The final chapter of the game suddenly ramps up the difficulty to 11 out of nowhere, and it is the longest chapter in the game. I like increased difficulty but there was no warning that there would be a 5 hour long final chapter filled with only the hardest bosses in the game one after the other.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDamn, I had this wishlisted for so long but now I actually bought it on sale it was very disappointing.. I was hoping it would be similar to Code Vein but I guess they didn't like it when their game sold 2 million copies and had to go back to this ancient style.

Animation and voice acting are done very well, as expected from BANDAI NAMCO, I liked the way the cut-scenes are made in game engine so the transition between scenes and gameplay felt seamless. But, unfortunately this game suffers from the biggest issue in most anime style games for me, the gameplay... Basically you fight few mobs, invisible wall opens up, next mobs, rinse and repeat.. Sort of reminded me of Honkai Impact and even MiHiYo realized how ancient this system is when they changed their system in Genshin.

My second complaint would be the difficulty of the game is way too easy, and I picked the hardest difficulty, maybe it'll get harder later on but honestly I can't justify forcing myself to play the game until the refund window closes, especially with the other problem mentioned above.

If you like these style of games, by all means, have fun. This is just my personal opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
