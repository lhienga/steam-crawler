\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
1) The combat system reminds me of DMC, with simpler combos. It also allows for assists from your teammates, in terms of combining powers such as telekinesis, teleportation, etc. Overall, combat is quite fun and intuitive. However, the later stages can be quite a slog to get thru.
2) I love the aesthetics of the games, particularly the city design and costume design,

Cons:
1) The story is all over the place, and not particularly engaging.
2) Tonally, it does not make any sense. In one scene, your former teammate had killed your father. In the next, it's like "I know I killed your father, but do you still wanna hang out in the cafe and talk about stuff"? Yes, it's that bad.
3) Dungeons are somewhat repetitive. Cities do not have much in terms of activities or side quests.

Overall, I would still recommend the game if you think of it more as a DMC style brawler rather than a JRPG.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a story about two seemingly unrelated people finding each other and changing the lives of everyone around them. This is a story about acceptance, companionship, and love. If you do intend to play the game, please put in the time to play both sides of the story, i.e. both main characters. After that, you NEED to either play the additional DLC "Episode Karen: Unknown History" (part of the Brain Eater Pack) or watch the coverage of it on YouTube in order to gain a greater depth on the main story. I promise that your appreciation and respect for that character (the aforementioned Karen) will increase 10x, and the feelings he holds with the words he says will hit 100x harder.

It took me 15 hours to really get into this game (more than half-way into my first playthrough), but I'm glad I stuck with it. It's crazy how with just that DLC my fondness for the game and the characters within it increased immensely, because without him, the game/characters as we know it would have never existed in that form. His impact on the story is so much larger than you would have thought it was if you just played the base game. Also, probably the single best thing about this game was the background music. My mind is always going to wander to some tracks from this game: that's just how great the music is.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
-World setting is cool and has lots of potential
-Combat is a bit too simple but overall fun
-OST is pretty nice
-Performance is great, most settings max with AA off even on a 1050ti
-Not too long of a game which I think can be a plus
-Voice acting is great (played the game in JP text and voice, cant comment on EN)

The bad:
-Lots of wasted potential with the story telling. First half was great but the latter half.. not so much. A bit too contrived
-The best way to experience the story for the first time is to actually create two saves - one for Yuito and one for Kasane and alternate after each chapter. My personal suggestion is to start with Kasane's side. However, this is not ideal if you are looking to unlock all achievements and 100% the content (everything should be done on a single save in that case)
-Because of the repetitive nature of having to play 2 mc's, I can't help but feel like they should have done something like nier automata where it is purposefully designed to alternate. Lots of the story can be trimmed and characters removed and really nothing of value would be lost. It really could and should be a more streamlined, tighter story.
-The bond scenes with party members are pretty disappointing, some ideas are great but they don't really fit in timing wise with the main story. You kinda have to do it though to unlock better abilities
-Cut-scenes are mostly stills, think like a visual novel. Honestly not great and all should ideally be replaced with animation (which they do have in a small number of places)

Overall i think I can give it a 7/10. Worth it for the ~$15 I got on sale. Would still be interested if there's ever an expansion of the universe/sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think Scarlet Nexus makes a decent first impression, then runs out of gas, then becomes fairly infuriating. The story is absolute nonsense. It is incoherent, and I say this as someone who finished and understood 13 Sentinels. The fact they expect you to play the story twice to "get it" is not really acceptable. The characters are generic, unlikable, boring.

The combat is fun and brisk, but the lack of enemy variety, bland linear levels that are basically just a conveyor belt from one encounter arena to another, and "noise" of the fights makes it difficult to enjoy. There is just so much going on it is difficult to dodge and do all the things they want. There are some neat gimmicks: Some of the environmental special objects you can use with telekinesis are really fun. There are also some neat enemies that specifically require your team's abilities, and when mixed with others, are nice variety. But, overall, it just isn't interesting enough to sustain a 20 hour playthrough.

The RPG element is incredibly boring. There is no gear progression (just buy the next best weapon) and the skill tree is fairly boring. There is no way to stand out or make choices that matter.

Finally, the game's final section is an absolute grind. It is hours of fighting the same handful of enemies you've seen the entire game, in increasingly more fiddly, grindy combinations. It is tedious, lacks variety, and without a story to drive you forward...it just doesn't work.

Platform: I played this on the Steam Deck, and it met my expectations per the Verified label. Performed very well. I had zero issues with this game on the Deck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and foremost, this is a game about blowing up monsters using cool psychic powers. It's a game that makes "throw cars at people" one of your basic buttons. Your combat progression is almost entirely based on upgrading new superpowers and combining them together in ever-larger ways. By the endgame my staple combo was summoning clones then stopping time to freely team-blast a tough enemy with lightning.

If that sounds cool to you, then I think you'll like this game. If not, then you'll probably be better off elsewhere.

The plot itself is an earnest helter-skelter B movie with all sorts of things going on (don't be fooled by the slow start of the demo). I enjoyed it, but it's no artful realist piece if that's what you're after. I recommend against worrying about the fine details.

There's an interesting gimmick in that you're asked to pick between two protagonists from whose viewpoint you'll see the story, but neither character sees the complete picture. I played through both characters simultaneously chapter-by-chapter, and I recommend it; I imagine some plot points will seem confusing or abrupt otherwise.

(For completionists, note that there are some achievements you can only get by playing through the other story in NG+, though I'd still recommend playing through in parallel.)

The first few chapters of the game have an annoying number of pop-up tutorials, made worse by applying to both stories, though I wouldn't think it a dealbreaker.

The game also has some very pretty & outlandish scenery and enemy design going on, and it really helps to sell some of the dramatic moments. I hope the artists had as much fun as it looks they did.\t\t\t\t\t
