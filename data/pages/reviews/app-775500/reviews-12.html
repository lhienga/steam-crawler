\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Scarlet Nexus" it looks great, has a captivating story, cool voice acting but somewhat par gameplay.

In a way, it's more like a Virtual Novel game with gameplay elements, with the gameplay being OK.

I think those who love action-drive anime/manga storylines would enjoy "Scarlet Nexus" and in someways, I feel like there is more a lean towards reading vs. gameplay. And some gameplay elements, it's more of a rehash of re-doing things you've done before which becomes monotonous.

In some ways, it's a bit sad because for such a cool game with cool characters and design and cool storyline, developers must have had a hard time trying to figure out how to create a compelling well-balanced game.

But regardless, I did enjoy the game, as I enjoy reading a lot of manga and play a lot of virtual novels. Those who want more action and less reading, may not like this game that much.

So, a question some may ask is if the DLC is needed. If you are a completist, then go for it. If you can get it cheap, go for it. There are bond episodes which lengthens the game (especially if you beat it with one character and play the EX New Game with the other character). But aside from cosmetics and Bond stories, one can pass on it. I got it for the bond stories (yes, more reading! I don't mind, but if you do...then avoid).

As for the cosmetics, I thought I can have everyone decked out in all red like what you see in the illustration but it's not the case. Although, Yuito's red costume is awesome.

Other than that, again, I'm cool with "Scarlet Nexus" and I give it a recommendation, but that's only if you like a lot of reading and you are digging the overall character design. If you are looking for exciting gameplay, this game is not it. In fact, many times I felt sleepy because I felt I was rehashing things over and over again. It does get old quick and wish developers just created new environments and not having gamers revisit areas. Especially in an area where you had to look for keys and with a bond episode, you're going to have to do it all over again.

But "Scarlet Nexus" is a reader's delight! You are captivated in its fascinating storyline and cool visuals, like a comic book. The voice acting is also pretty cool too! But the gaming part of it...it's a bit lackluster.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScarlet Nexus is a 4/10 Game, the Gameplay is a 9/10 the Story is a 2/10

Good Things --

It's a Hack and Slash, Spectacle fighter with heavy JRPG elements, where all of the abilities feel rewarding and necessary.

The combat is the best I've experienced in a JRPG, I've seen it described as dumb and simple but to me it's fast paced and in depth enough without ever being clunky or feeling dumb, skills and abilities are unlocked at a steady pace, 3 hours into the game you'll have access to enough of the features to get a feel for how combat will be later on when more enemy types are introduced and your attack options multiply.

There are new mechanics introduced past the midpoint of the game, and if you choose to raise your "bond level" with the characters during "Standby phases" you'll still be unlocking abilities up until the end of the game.

The animations for special moves and combo's with team mates are fast and don't bog down the flow of the fights, it's really smooth, the only negative thing about combat is that someone who hasn't played the game wont have a clue what's going on when they watch it.

The sound design and UI work really well to inform you of exactly what's going on, there weren't any annoying beeps or popups and the advise the party members shout out doesn't get repetitive.

Bad Things --

The only badly implemented gameplay feature that I can think of is the sidequest system.
Let's take the sidequest "Surprise Test" as an example, note that all sidequests use this same system though.
The sidequest "Surprise Test" has the objective "Defeat 2 vase Paws in mid-air", if you choose to track that quest, it'll only show the name of the quest on your HUD under your minimap it wont tell you anything about the objective on your HUD, you can only see the objective in a sub menu that takes 5 button presses to reach, this is such a basic problem with a simple fix, I can't believe it was never patched in.

The story is a 2/10 at best, the main plot of the game and world building are some of the worst I've ever experienced.
There are gaping plot holes and far too many abrupt shifts in tone or character.

The story is incredibly slow, out of the first 3 hours in the game 2 hours and 5 minutes are cut scenes, and 99% of those cut scenes are still frame CGI comic panels, only a handful of very short cut scenes are animated, I'd say out of the 10 hours of cut scenes and dialogue, only 5-6 minutes have animation, the only saving grace is that they're fully voiced by good voice actors.

During those first 3 hours, 19 named characters are introduced, with first and second names and even ranks/nick names, it's overwhelming and I found myself 12 hours in still forgetting peoples names.
Typically JRPG's introduce characters at a much slower pace, there is one cut scene in particular 90 minutes into the game where 12 characters are standing in a room talking and it's nearly impossible to follow because it isn't animated.

Basic world building such as "Why do I have super powers?", "What am I fighting and where does it come from?" being story elements that wont be answered until much later in the game, and even then they wont be explained in detail.
I can kind of forgive the game hiding things like this to later reveal it as the plot unfolds, that makes sense to do if you're going to have a plot twist or any kind of big reveal, the problem is that instead of being lied to then finding out the truth, you go from nothing to the truth.

The main plot of the story also feels heavily influenced by the anime Attack on Titan and Neon Genesis Evangelion
Major spoilers for this one but if you've watched the first 2 series of Attack on Titan you'll see the comparison,
The People on earth are the Eldians, the People on the moon are the Marlyans, there's injections that transform people into monsters, Yuito uses his brain to send a message to every citizen when he shows them the video of Kaito speaking about Yakumo, the extinction belt is essentially the walls and the titans, it's so blatant at times

There are really strange choices with the structure of the game and the tone shifting wildly,at one point an ally Kaguro Kills Joe Sumeragi, Yuito the protagonists father and less than 10 minutes later you can call them on the brain phone and go to a restaurant with them to increase your "bond level".
There's no reason that the bond story couldn't have taken place in a later section of the game as Kaguro doesn't become an ally until much later, so increasing his bond level to gain combat benefits has no effect until much later, it's totally pointless, this is just one example of this kind of tone shift that makes the game hard to take seriously.

Despite having about 10 hours+ of cut scenes, and a 50+ minute ending sequence, the ending felt rushed, a lot of the questions I had about major elements in the story went unanswered.
This isn't surprising as so many story threads are started and random events take place that only add more unanswered questions.
List of unanswered questions
If 99% of the population have psionic ability why isn't this shown more, why does it seem like only a few people have abilities, for example none of the OSF soldiers you fight wave of use powers to attack
Are there still people on the moon? Tsugumi mentions at the very last minute of the game that there are no poeple on the moon, despite Kaguro saying there are people stuck there earlier in the game
Did the people on the moon have powers, Kaguro is from the moon 2000 years ago and has powers despite not being born on earth after the powers developed, the game stats that the others "Seemed to be unstoppable for a number of years until something naturally triggered a part of the brain to develop special abilities to a few people", if the others only showed up AFTER the colonists landed on earth and fought them for a few years, Kaguro shouldn't have any powers at all
The game states that the others fall to earth from the extinction belt, but we never see how this happens, it also says that others are people who were transformed, and I'm not talking about Naomi and Alice, there are multiple times where it mentions that large numbers of people are turned into others, but this is never brought up, it would've been a cool plot point if the new himuka goverment was using the mind control to make the citizens forget that people turn into others when the extinction belt particles contact earth, but this isn't what the game says happens.
We never really get a conclusion for what happens to Togetsu at all, is it all completely destroyed along with the design children?
When Naomi is turned into an other, when playing as Yuito she's barely mentioned after that part despite it feeling like a major plot point at the time, this could be because Naomi is to Kasane what Nagi is to Yuito, so she's not as important to Yuito but it feels like a big issue
Yuito never brings up using the red strings to save Nagi and Kasane never mentions using it to save Naomi, this could've been a great plot point where they decide not to be like Karen, but they don't use it
When Naomi is shot with the metamorphosis bullet the rogue NDF soldier is actually aiming at Kasane, this is never explained, transforming Kasane into an Other would mean that only Yuito's Gravikenesis could be copied by Karen, it makes no sense for them to want to do this, It's never explicitly stated that Karen is working with the NDF, but he betrays the OSF and moves to his base in Seiran, which is where the NDF are, if he was behind the NDF attack then it would make no sense to transform Kasane without copying her power first she could even be used as an Anchor point like how Kasane uses Yuito as an anchor point\t\t\t\t\t
