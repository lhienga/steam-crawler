\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great Action-RPG with extremely fun combat, crazy enemy design and a wild story, but small weaknesses in presentation.

This action JRPG really got me hooked with its fun and fast-paced combat. While it gets a bit easy towards the end (if you are experienced with similar games I recommend playing on the hard difficulty), the flow of combat, different encounter setups and team abilities that you get keep it fresh for the majority of the playthrough. The Psychokinesis ability of the main characters allows you to throw objects from the environment (from rocks to cars to chandeliers) towards your enemies and are really well done and smoothly integrated into combos.
The game is pretty linear, both in terms of plot and dungeon design, but you can revisit old areas to levelup, get items and fulfill some simple sidequests. This is not meant as a negative, as some of the best games of all times (for example most of the Final Fantasy games) are designed this way and it does allow for a more integrated pacing of story, combat and the "down time" sections in between chapters.
The story itself is pretty good, but there is a lot going on. For about 2/3 of the game, whenever I thought that I knew what the game was about, a completely new plot thread was picked up and another twist thrown my way. If you are bored of predictable games, this is definitely a plus for you ;)
While most of these different strories, plot points and setpieces get resolved by the end of the game, there are a few, potentially big things, that get only superficial closure, with some of the main characters saying that they want to tackle these problems "in the future". Not sure if this is a hint at a possible sequel or just an anime "what will they do next" trope approach, but these do not present big cliffhangers or an unsatisfying conclusion overall.

One issue with the game however is the presentation: While the voice acting is fine, most of the "cutscenes" are presented as a series of still images or minimal movements over dialogue. This gives some of these cutscenes a bit of a visual novel like feel. So it's not necessarily bad, it is not high-fidelity AAA level of conversion cutscenes and might have been to keep the budget in check a bit. And while this is fine, if not super impressive for the dialogue heavy cutscenes, a few of the more action packed scenes also get this treatment, which really lessens their impact (most bigger of the action setpieces get "proper cutscenes" however). The game also sometimes struggles to hit the sweetspot between too much and too little exposition.
Also, some of the enemy models are reused quite a bit, only with slight changes to looks and sometimes a different elemental weakness or their weakpoint is moved to a different location. As the Enemy designs are super wild and psychedelic, I can see why the devs reused some of them, and there is a ok variety of enemies overall.\t\t\t\t\t
