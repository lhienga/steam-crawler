\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally obviously made on a tight budget but they managed to get a good game out of it. While most of the story is told in slideshows as a trade-off, pretty much every non-npc line of dialogue in the game is voice-acted and very well, and there's a LOT of it too. The story itself is alright, veers off into crazy 90s JRPG territory but that's kind of inevitable when you're trying to sell a high-concept story on a budget of 10 bucks and some gum. There's also two protagonists with entirely different stories & parties and they frequently cross over, adding heavily to replay value.

Also you get a neat base that slowly fills up with crap you gift your teammates(and they will start adding their own junk as you get closer with them) which is fun. Giving your gardening friend some plants, for example, ends up with flowers and vines all over the place. Give someone a booze set and bottles will show up in the kitchen. They'll also goof off with this stuff in their spare time, too. It's a nice bit of characterization on a budget.

Gameplay itself is decent enough though somewhat hard to get used to, it's mostly about managing a suite of buff-like powers that alter your moveset in subtle or overt ways. Every action is very committal(for a character action game) and enemy damage is pretty high so you need to know your enemies' weaknesses and prioritize threats to effectively fight. Of course there's also a lot of items to help brute-force your way through so it's not TOO punishing on lower difficulties.

Having different party members(and thus powers) most of the time means that both playable characters approach combat situations very differently too. Both characters do share a power in psychokinesis, which typically means weaving hurling junk at enemies into your combos but occasionally manifests in rad ways like surfing a bus through a crowd of enemies. Overall it's not super in-depth but it's good enough and doesn't overstay it's welcome.

Anyway it's not a life-changer or anything but it's a high-concept story told decently well on a budget and is an easy recommendation to buy if you like good ol' anime action games.\t\t\t\t\t

Have an apple pie recipe:

Ingredients
1 (14.1 ounce) package pastry for a 9-inch double-crust pie
¾ cup white sugar, or more to taste
1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
6 cups thinly sliced apples
1 tablespoon butter

Directions
Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C).
Line a 9-inch pie dish with one pastry crust; set second one aside.
Combine 3/4 cup sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl. Add more sugar if your apples are tart.
Layer apple slices in the prepared pie dish, sprinkling each layer with cinnamon-sugar mixture.
Dot top layer with small pieces of butter. Cover with top crust.

Bake pie on the lowest rack of the preheated oven for 10 minutes. Reduce oven temperature to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) and continue baking until golden brown and filling bubbles, 30 to 35 minutes more.

Serve warm or cold.

Enjoy!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a curious beast. I didn't know when purchasing that there are two woven story lines to play through. Having come from playing Nier Automata, that excited me. Until I got about 15 hours into the game.

The tempo of the game is story akin to Persona, then combat. The problem is that the combat becomes samey very quickly, and the story doesn't hold up enough to make it feel like it's worth pushing through. I didn't even finish one story line, let alone start the other. Ended up looking for the remainder of the story on YouTube.

I am not sure I can recommend this game, when something like Nier Automata does just about everything better. The one place this wins is graphics - it does look great. I just didn't care for any of the characters.\t\t\t\t\t
