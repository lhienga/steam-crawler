\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed as Kasane.

I know some people quite liked the combat, but I found that it felt bad. It's very easy to get locked into an attack animation and get punished, which would suggest being more tactical in when trying to attack. However, there are often many enemies flying around from all sides shooting projectiles you can't even see because they're behind you, which suggests a much more active approach. The combination just feels like a frustrating experience of getting hit when trying to do things.

Enemies are damage sponges. Could be fine if you like the combat. Apparently this also changes when you get your allies to friendship level 6. I hadn't bothered crafting enough gifts to get there by the end of the first (and only) play-through.

The pacing of the cutscenes was too slow. Even manually advancing the dialogue, things move slowly. It almost felt like padding. Which also applies to the final section of the game: a really, really long sequence of fights. The fights started to feel very same-y too, like there wasn't much enemy variety: changing the element of the attack didn't really help.

I didn't really like the characters. They start off a caricactures, and end as slightly more detailed caricatures. "You tried to kill us, lets be friends" just didn't hit for me.

Story was fine. Near the end, I just played on very easy to try rush through and finish things as fast as possible. Was still too slow to feel like it was worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

1. The combat in Scarlet Nexus is a true masterpiece, combining telekinetic powers with fast-paced action. The ability to wield objects in the environment as weapons adds a dynamic element to the battles, making every encounter a visually stunning and strategic affair. The synergy between character abilities and the engaging enemy design keeps the gameplay fresh and exciting. It's not just a button-mashing fest; it's a symphony of telekinetic chaos that you orchestrate with precision.
2. The SAS (Struggle Arms System) introduces a unique twist, allowing players to connect with their party members and utilize their powers strategically. This cooperative element enhances the combat depth, making team synergy a key factor in overcoming the challenging foes that the game throws your way.
Scarlet Nexus weaves an intricate narrative that keeps you hooked from the opening scenes to the grand finale. The world-building is impeccable, with a dystopian future that feels both captivating and haunting. As a member of the Other Suppression Force (OSF), you unravel the mysteries surrounding the Others – bizarre creatures threatening humanity. The intertwining plotlines, character backstories, and unexpected twists create a narrative tapestry that is as emotionally resonant as it is gripping.
3.Character development is another strong suit. The diverse cast of OSF members brings a range of personalities to the table, and as you delve deeper into their stories, you can't help but form a strong connection with each one. The game masterfully tackles themes of identity, memory, and the consequences of wielding extraordinary powers, adding depth to the overall experience.
4.Scarlet Nexus doesn't just tell a compelling story; it presents it in a visually stunning package. The art style, character designs, and futuristic cityscapes are a feast for the eyes. The attention to detail in the environmental design and the fluidity of combat animations showcase the dedication of the development team to delivering a top-tier gaming experience.
5.In conclusion, Scarlet Nexus isn't just a game; it's an immersive journey that sets a new standard for action RPGs. With a combat mechanism that feels groundbreaking and a narrative that tugs at the heartstrings, this game stands tall among its peers. Whether you're a fan of action-packed gameplay, compelling storytelling, or simply appreciate the artistry of a well-crafted game, Scarlet Nexus is a must-play. It's not just the best – it's a masterpiece in its own right.\t\t\t\t\t
