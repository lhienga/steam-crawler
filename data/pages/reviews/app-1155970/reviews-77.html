\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe narrative is compelling yet the choices you can make are strictly determined by roster plus some RNG thus lending to a 'map by scum saving' Not a show stopper but coupled with the three 'modes of play' it seems to be pointless to try it on anything other than casual.

PROS
What I liked was the overall ambience in the story-telling. It encouraged me to be nosey yet polite as I was compelled to garner trust. I admire these principles and enjoyed pursuing the individuals stories which hinted at a wider world.

CONS:
Some basic explanation of the temporal rules afore playing as time is everything yet I have no idea what time it actually is, occasionally told how long an action will take, yet double clicking a previously explored region does not and so reinforces scum saving techniques as a faux kind of tutorial to learn these essential rules.

For casual play, this is a fascinating game and well worth the AUD$11 or so, I feel.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVideo games have always been a huge part of my life. As I've gotten older I find it harder and harder to get totally absorbed by them like I did when I was a kid. I'll pick up games for a few hours and then get bored and never touch them again.

Roadwarden is one of those rare titles I play once a year, maybe once every few years, that reignites my passion for video games and makes me feel like a kid again. Undertale, hyper light drifter, hollow knight, rimworld, disco elysium, celeste; roadwarden deserves to be mentioned in the same breath.

The compelling but simple mechanics are a testament to the timeless genius of traditional D&D tabletop elements. The writing is inspired (good video game writing? what the ♥♥♥♥?), the sepia pixelated art-style is immediately impressive, and the OST is start-to-finish bangers.

The main draw is that (much like DE) information about the world is doled out slowly and methodically with a few major twists and turns. At the beginning of the game the peninsula feels massive and unexplored, harboring a near infinite variety of strange beasts and magical oddities. As you progress the map quickly begins to feel smaller until every nook has been searched, every creature slain and every NPC dialogue option exhausted. The illusion of infinite depth cracks and you see the game for what it is.

8-10 hours of near perfection for $10. For me the game is a 10 because anything that feels a bit unpolished only contributes to the intimate indie feeling and my only legitimate complaint is that there isn't more of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend this game, I had a wonderful time playing it. I came into it having never heard of it, I just stumbled on it during the steam sale, the art style caught my eye, and after trying the demo I very quickly realized I would enjoy it and bought the full game.
The world and story really immersed me, I found myself thinking about the game when I wasn't playing it, what I needed to do next, what decisions I should make. The writing is good, the cast of characters is varied and interesting, I enjoyed getting to learn more about each character I met, there was often a lot more depth to them than I expected.
I played through it twice, the first time my Roadwarden did achieve their goal, but I failed to accomplish a lot of the other things I hoped to, so I almost immediately started a second playthrough! Which was much more successful, although I'm still tempted to play it again sometime soon and see how differently things can turn out.
The choices around your Roadwarden are nice, I really like the dialogue choices and the moments where you decide who your character is, as well as building up skills and collecting items to be able to get through different encounters. I struggled a lot more with money in my first playthrough, the second time I got to the point where it largely wasn't an issue for me,
The lore around the world felt like it was introduced at a good pace, and I enjoyed the general concepts around it too, like the different views on the wilderness and the creatures, it made me want to know more. The length is good, I'm a pretty quick reader so I probably got through it faster than others might, I was definitely disappointed in a way when it ended but that just speaks to the quality of it, it didn't overstay it's welcome by any means, it didn't feel padded out unnecessarily, it's a nicely contained story.

There's more eloquent reviews, (I think this is actually the first ever review I've left) but I wanted to sing the praises of this game because it hit in exactly the right spot for me, and if you enjoy text-based RPGs I would say absolutely pick this one up, heck, even if you don't, just try the demo! See if it draws you in like it did to me. Excited to see if the dev makes more games like this, because I'll certainly play them :) (and coincidentally noticed I own their older game, Tales From Windy Meadow, through a bundle, so I'll be trying that!)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend this game to anyone who has an interest in fantasy or role playing games. It is heavily text based so prepare for a lot of reading, but I was so absorbed that I finished it in 3 days after downloading it. Also, the developer is very nice! I found some grammatical errors while I was going through the game and made a note of them, and messaged the dev on twitter about it and he responded to say thanks and that he would fix them in the next update!
For someone who has almost no experience in games like DnD but has always wanted to get into them, this was an amazing introduction! It was thoughtful and engaging and I was very immersed in the story, I felt real urgency when things were happening.
I think I got lucky, because my first play through was almost a complete success (I never ran out of money, I never got close to dying, I think I got a good ending for most characters except for 2 of them), but I'm excited to replay and try a new path.
Overall, be prepared for a lot of reading, utilize the quick save function, and have a fun time! Also, I played the first time on the setting with no time limit, I recommend this for anyone who has never played this type of game before because it reduced a lot of my anxiety about finding things in time.
However if you regularly play these types of games, I think the time limit would be fun and challenging!\t\t\t\t\t
