\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very nice slow text based game. Fair warning though, it's very slow paced with little combat, with stats mostly being gained or lost from more menial things like getting a bad night's rest. Most time spent is you walking the outer forest paths to meet the villages and pass news between them. It's a very specific story for a specific audience, if the idea of just being a person walking around in the woods helping out any random people you see doesn't appeal to you then maybe skip this game. I've also often heard the setting being compared to the Witcher, particularly the books.

For a review on the basic game mechanics, I'd say it's pretty solid as far as text based adventures go. The game very rarely asks you to input specific words, and most of the time the answers to those situations are pretty obvious if you're paying attention, with one exception being a certain spider. Otherwise the game generally presents you with a wide array of options that never felt too restrictive. The game also has 4(5 if playing as the mage) stats that change how events play out. As a general rule, being full on food and health helps you be strong, appearance will generally save you time because people will be more likely to give you/progress quests when you first meet them, and armor stops the other stats from going down as often. Just vaguely try to keep all the numbers up and you'll be fine. The game also has 3 classes, Warrior Mage and Scholar. The game recommends warrior at first, but they all have somewhat similar capabilities in general life. The warrior starts with better gear and has strength based abilities while at high health, the mage has a magic pool to use on stuff but has a few input issues which are annoying, and the scholar can gather potions and herbs and can read which comes in vaguely helpful at random times.

The game has 3 difficulties, but I'd only recommend the hardest for challenge runs after you've beaten the game once. The other 2 mostly decide whether or not the day limit will be active, which is a bit of a point of contention. Personally, I like the idea of the 40 day limit because i'm a weird person who thinks that endings that don't answer all the questions are cool, but if you're not good at rpgs and tend to do things slowly, or want to see all the quests and not get any bad endings then that's what the easy mode is for.

Oh, and this game has a lot of transcribed accents. I dinnae think it much a problem, but ye may ne care for it\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an avid fan of fantasy, Roadwarden truly delivers, maybe not in the mainstream idea of your high fantasy story where you progress through the game until you fight or become a god.
Instead roadwarden’s world presents you a subtle yet realistic take on a wild and untamed Archipielago with characters struggling to survive and settle, each with interconnecting stories, motivations and a past for you to discover all while never stripping you from your own deductions until you discover the truth, or not.
While simplistic and bare-bones as it is, Roadwarden sticks to its strengths and does them real good.
To some it might seem just like a glorified choose your own story book.
But damn it’s a good one.

Truly incredible, really does make you feel like batman 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
