\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a big fan of this genre is what I would say before I played this game. This game is phenomenal. I couldn't stop playing. The game has different endings (Sorta, the ending is mostly the same but it tells you what happened to everyone when you leave.) Due to this I don't find much replay value in the game, however the story and the choices you have are great. One thing I was dissapointed about is I couldn't convince this druid to betray her towns mayor even though I showed all the terrible deeds they did but other than that it was a great story. I feel like the ending when you go to the island was a bit underwhelming. I was expecting tons of monsters and fights, barely being able to survive and finding the person I was looking for that was key to the story. However when I got there they were just dead. Turned into a shell. I was hoping for more in depth story about the island. Maybe it would be an entirely different area to explore but no, its just a few fights and some weird animals that force you to feed them.

Other than that the game is great, well worth the $7 I spent and the $11 it currently is when not on sale. I hope in the future we can receive some DLC for this game as I was sad when it ended as that's when I stopped playing.

The developer boasts 30 hours yet I beat all the quests, however I did no-life this game for 2-3 days. I see this game being beaten regularly in around 15-20 hours.

Other than that I suggest playing the game in the casual mode, in the other modes you're restricted to 40 and 30 days. My play through took around 130 days.\t\t\t\t\t

What to expect:
* An intelligently written and wide-branching story with plenty of surprises and a few frustrating moments.
* I enjoyed that you can lie, backstab, betray while being loyal to someone else, or no one.
* Choices matter and change your experience significantly.
* A wide range of settings that change how you go through the game. The way I played it was to turn off the time limit and enjoy every day.
* Minimal but tasteful graphics.
* A good soundtrack that will accompany your journey that you might find a little repetitive.

A few issues that aren't show-stoppers but worth mentioning:
* I got stuck in a loop after I made a powerful enemy. The enemies thugs would beat me up every night I slept in their territory.
* Using your background skill (scholar, fighter, mage) wasn't exactly intuitive to me in the beginning. I was quite deep in the game when I figured it out. You need to click on the specialist icon in order to get new choices in your interactions.
* It should not be so hard to stay ahead of your expenses. You're either living in complete poverty, or you're gaming the economy to stay ahead. It's honestly distracting.
* Managing how the story ends feels abrupt where there might have been a few open ended options.\t\t\t\t\t
