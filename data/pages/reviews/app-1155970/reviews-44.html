\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are games with a rote, scripted story that plays out the same regardless of your choices. This can work great, The Last of Us is a classic, cinematic storytelling can be enhanced by the tone of gameplay. When you add the ability for the player to affect the outcome, it suddenly becomes a lot harder to make it feel really feel like a story. New Vegas is probably the best example I can think of, but even then it largely feels like you're choosing between three stories, like the whole thing is built for you to have an impact, and the game makes you feel like a powerful agent, but there's this lingering sense of construction and artifice, that this was a world built for you to save.

This game is, quite simply, one of the best game stories I have ever played. You have tremendous control over your interactions, really genuine agency, and yet the world remains the world, you have to think, you have to care, these are people whose needs you need to consider to achieve your goals. And I've never cared about goals so intimately as in here? There's no capacity for ludonarrative dissonance here because the choices you make in the gameplay are literally the story. Every open world game alleges to this sort of "forge your own path, make your own story" intention, but this is one of the only games I've played where the journey just completely felt like my story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat and innovative text adventure game! Endlessly replayable, fun world and lots of interesting characters. Also noteworthy is the lack of combat mechanics, so no more rolling two dices to beat some skill stat ala Fight Fantasy, and I find that a welcome change. Every enemy encounter is unique in its own way and rewards filling out your bestiary ahead of time.

My main complaint is that the world is so different than typical fantasy, but there isn't nearly enough art to properly convey it. From example, unicorns are mentioned quite a few times in the game, but I had to remember to go read the bestiary to realize that unicorns in this game are more akin to giant rampaging rhinos. And a lot of creatures are like that, with names thrown around but easy to miss what those creatures are actually like. If the game had more art, it would be much easier for me to be immersed into the scenes rather than trying to figure out if the monkeys in the game looked more like real world monkeys or something completely alien. This is an aspect that old Fighting Fantasy / Fabled Lands books always excelled at: amazing art that help you immerse into the game world.

Still, definitely worth playing!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short but awesome and well-written adventure that will keep you glued to the screen.

Roadwarden creates a world that constantly makes you feel like you're just a member of it and not the classic overpowered hero of many other RPGs, and yet never punishes you for your mistakes so harshly that recovery is impossible. You can feel comfortable taking risks knowing that, even without reloading a previous save, you can always bounce back.

You are also always rewarded for just being yourself. There is no "meta" way to win the game; do what you think is right for you or the character you're roleplaying and you will get somewhere interesting. Perhaps not where you planned, but no path you can take is dull or uninteresting. In addition, the game is chock full of choices that really only matter for the player: small gestures that reveal what kind of person you would be if you were thrust in that situation. How you greet others, or interact with your horse. These small choices don't carry consequences for the game's progression (usually), but make you feel more connected to the world.

And the world is very peculiar and interesting. By focusing on such a small territory, the developers filled it to the brim with secrets and interesting facts to discover. At the end of the game, I discovered that I really cared about it and the people that lived there.

I would heartily recommed Roadwarden to anyone looking for a relaxed and complex adventure that's a bit different from the usual. Such a lovely game.\t\t\t\t\t
