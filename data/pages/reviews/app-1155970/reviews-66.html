\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Roadwarden has been one of my favorite games this year. Almost like a single-player DnD adventure game with a wonderful mix of character building, combat, and exploration. At some locations you need to type in actions that you want to do in that location. I've heard that some people struggle with these more open-ended prompts, but I've found that an open mind and a couple tries are enough to solve them. I love the exploration style in this game and how the map style doesn't clutter the locations too much. One problem I've had is that I never seem to have enough money or food, even with fish traps (yes, plural), foraging, and selling every bit of antler, horn, and pelt I can get my hands on.
A fair warning, however, this game is very text-based and if you skim through the lore you'll likely miss a clue for the quests. I've had some problems with the quests in Gale Rocks as the mini-tasks for the villagers are not put under quests and if you need to look back at them you have to scroll up into the archive, which gets deleted after a certain point. The quest information is organized in massive paragraphs in chronological order, which is honestly kind of painful to read and makes it difficult to look back at any info you forgot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is the sort of game I have been looking for my entire life. It just scratches that 'itch' that other games can't seem to fully reach. The game itself is something similar to other Choose your own adventure games in a way, but the added art and music make it a truly wonderful experience.

I've played plenty of games that are text based and have RPG esq vibes to them but this one was the only one that had me immersed for the entire journey.

I haven't been able to finish this beauty of a game because I'm just too afraid for it to come to an end. The world seems to come alive as your journal is filled out with the common folks words of advice or thoughts. Your goals are tied with the people you talk to and the information they give you, though the information often feels somewhat vague this just makes the experience better as you find yourself sleuthing through what you think is real and important compared to what isn't.

I wish there were more games like Roadwarden out there in the world and I truly believe that this Dev has created something truly amazing. I hope there is some sort of sequel or similar styled game that Moral Anxiety studios works on in the future but if Roadwarden was all they had in store, I would be more than content with this quality piece of work.

Thank you guys for making such a lovely game!\t\t\t\t\t
