\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden was one of the most immersive and atmospheric games I've played all year. It's a game that drops you into an unknown land that you have to attempt to understand and respect as its new warden. Virtually all of your decisions are relevant to the progression of the game and there are tons of things that you will miss or be unable to do on your first playthrough. The illustrations and soundtrack are also fantastic. That being said, this game is quite literally a novella. Don't expect incredibly flashy visualizations and be prepared to read the equivalent of a 150-ish page book.

This game has a level of love and attention that's often missing from a lot of contemporary games. If you enjoy esoteric and mysterious narrative experiences as much as I do, you will absolutely love this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is brilliantly executed text-based adventure game with a solid gameplay containing thought-out RPG and survival elements.

The writing is excellent and drew me quickly into its grim world, filled with vile creatures, both monster and men, but also hope, humanity and beauty. The world, characters and monsters are always described in just enough detail to let your fascination run wild while avoiding drawn-out walls of text. In a similar vein I must give a big shout-out to the soundtrack and sound-design which really helped immersing me into the game.

The first playthrough took me 17h and as most of your choices matter a lot, there is tons of replay value.
I give this game a hearty recommendation, even if you are not a fan of the genre. Definitely one of my favourite games of the year.\t\t\t\t\t
