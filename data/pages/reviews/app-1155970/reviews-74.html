\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you love fantasy and good writing, buy this game. Play it all the way through. Let yourself make mistakes. You'll not find a more authentic interactive storytelling experience anywhere else. Unlike most Choice Game style choose-your-own-adventure, the choices I made felt real, because the greater consequence of each decision made was not a reflection of where I was leading the plot, but rather who my character became in relation to the world around them. Does my Pagan Fighter stay loyal to his faith, or buckle under the pressure of the tide of civilization? Am I willing to give up a greater goal just to preserve the trust and confidence of a friend? Even choices that seem inconsequential can come back around. Your story will -not- end in a satisfying manner if you're hoping to conquer and win everything. It will however, end in catharsis if you view the ending as the culmination of who your character was. What sort of Roadwarden were you? Mine was so focused on helping others, that even if some will remember him as a hero, he died without fanfare in intervening in a random robbery, years after his greatest achievements.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe author has created a persuasive world. I spent multiple nights plagued by nightmares from this game, the dawn birds outside my bedroom window mistaken at first for the howler monkeys stalking outside the game's shelters.

It does have some timing problems, making it mostly impossible to beat the first run through without loading constantly to hedge against mistakes. It's just impossible to predict what items will be usable, even if you know of a clear use for one. And occasionally writing mistakes by the authors make things work the opposite of how they are supposed to.

That being said, there are a number of cool moments where you know exactly what to do because of some tip you earned by asking around the continent earlier in the game.

It's a text adventure, so you better be ready for eye strain. Note to the devs, a large print mode would be nice for us older folks, you know! After all, we're the only ones who remember the days when text adventures ruled.

Overall, this game greatly exceeds the potential suggested by the early access demo. I recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really nice text adventure about exploration of a backwater region of a fantasy world as a freelance adventurer (who happens to have a corporate boss with their own agenda).

What I like about it is that it's a story driven game, but in a completely open world. There are quests to do and mysteries to solve, but it's completely up to you to discover them and to prioritize them. There are area's more dangerous than others, so you will have to use your own judgement if you want to risk it when you find them, or wait until you have more experience. There is also a fairly strict time-limit, so you won't be able to do everything and help everyone you may want to, which leads to some interesting choices as you get closer to the end.

I quite like the setting as well. It's dark fantasy, somewhat bleak, but the people you meet still manage to be funny and determined to do better. Well most of them.\t\t\t\t\t
