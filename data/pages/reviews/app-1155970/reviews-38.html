\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game's understated art style and music allow the audience's focus to settle onto the writing, which evocatively describes a harsh but intriguing world. Even more impressive is that unlike so many visual novels, ROADWARDEN is only very rarely overwritten and barely ever does lore dumps for the sake of lore dumps. Everything you learn about people, places or creatures does end up having a practical purpose in-game. Learning one village's backstory helps you make appropriate offerings to them when you first meet. Learning about the ecology of the setting's monsters helps you make better tactical decisions in combat. The game's RPG elements are also brilliant, and a welcome addition to the VN formula. There is a surprising diversity of builds or strategies to pursue, especially for such a mechanically light game.

Roadwarden is a beautiful narrative game and many of its details and questions will linger in your mind for a long time after playing it.\t\t\t\t\t
