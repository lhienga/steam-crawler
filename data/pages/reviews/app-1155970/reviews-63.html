\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is missing a lot of lore necessary to make the world believable. For example, the characters presented are not plausible within a primitive society stuck in a constant battle for survival. Historically (in the real world) males fight and dominate hierarchical power structures. For some reason the author flips this truth upside down but does not give any story explaining why so many females are in combat and at the top of the power ladder. To be believable, either the rules of this world must be greatly different or important historical facts are missing. Your main character doesn't even notice this oddity even though your character comes from a more civilized part of the world. Unfortunately this breaks immersion throughout the story. There are other flaws, but that example is repeated the most often.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery well written and immersive text-RPG. If you're like me you will find yourself caring for the land and its people and do everything in your your power develop it, clear and protect the roads, improving communications and trade. The atmosphere and writing is very good and discovering the different towns, their story, culture and inhabitants gave me the same feeling as reading a good fantasy novel as a teen. The game is visibly a labour of love and the universe is very well thought out. I definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
