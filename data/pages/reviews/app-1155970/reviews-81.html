\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic RPG in a genre that I wish had way more traction and games.
(Similar games I highly recommend: Disco Elysium, Sorcery! Games, The life and suffering of Sir Brante, Age of Decadence, Vampire the Masquerade: Night Road)

What I appreciate the most over similar games is the freedom of approaches and exploration, the secondary objectives that help you alter outcomes or mend broken relations, and the amount of branching paths and decisions you can take. They really make this game feel like much more than a visual novel where you pick one of a few possible options and continue to the next page.

As a (spoiler-free) example: Found a rare item or material somewhere? You might have the option to sell it to several different people, some of which you might have to discover to begin with or spend a long time traveling to, and they might value things differently. Or you could keep it and it might turn out to be way more useful later on in a Quest, or even be enhanced or transformed at some point.
Or you could even consume or use it in one way or another, from the inventory screen.
You never "miss your shot" in this game because you went past that page or relevant option, what you have will always be there for you to consider.

Or a less practical but just as important example: Need to convince someone of something? You'll really need to earn their trust actively, and you never know what you might discover from other quests or characters or items later that might help sway them one way or the other. And unlike other games in the genre, failing (or failing to find) one or even several of the options does not have to mean that character, quest or location is now locked. You might even upset characters or think you've failed quests, only to find something later that might mend relations or let you take a very different approach.

And to top that all off, there's also several stats and proficiencies you can choose to build up during the course of the game, which open up even more options or make your travels easier, with several stages for each. And several achievements and bragging rights to collect.
All of this helps make this feel more like a true RPG rather than a streamlined or restricted visual novel where you HAVE to pick options in the right order to get the outcomes you want.


The discovery aspect is also done incredibly well, especially considering the entire game is built on Renpy: Almost every scene is black at first and has stages to it, different (potentially dangerous) parts you can explore and choose your approach for, and those pieces only reveal on your screen as you choose to step that way. And the world is filled with tension, danger and shady (or overly friendly) characters and secrets, all of which you have to actively find.
Getting into a village doesn't just reveal everyone to you, you have to FIND people through connections, rumors, recommendations or even literally searching for their names in a text-box. That goes for locations or events aswell: Several places you explore and people you talk to will simply just have a textbox where you type out exactly what you want to do or look for in a simple manner, much like the very first truly text-based RPG games (Kick tree, drink, dig bones, stab, Bob, smith, etc).
And of course you'll only know what to look for through looking at the scene, using your imagination, or paying attention and taking/reading through notes. You might even MISS things, mention the wrong thing, or open up entire new paths simply by paying attention to the world your character is in.


The ONE negative thing I have to say about the game is related to the Ending, and some unclear mechanics when it comes to *when* your choices actually matter and go into effect, and when that agency is taken from you:
!No ingame/story Spoilers, but technical/mechanical spoilers ahead!

Throughout the entire game I was under the impression that you would have at least some sort of input and (big) choice(s) at the end that would let you actually decide how to wrap things up or set the final cogs into motion with all your influence, but it all just comes to a full stop on the last day, and plays out the ending slides, depending on the choices you made *throughout the game*
The problem here, and this is true in 2 more parts in the game, is that it's never made clear to the player that there will NOT be a later option to change his approach, change his mind or actually make a decision in any way, despite having the full capabilities and reasoning to do so, and despite exactly that happening so many times throughout the journey. And so many conversations hinting towards at least 2 big different solutions or approaches to the overall issue existing.
But there's no actual choices to make once those slides roll, and that could have been made a lot clearer. It felt like you as the Player being ripped out of your character and watching him just sit there and accept everything happening around him as those slides roll.
And that did leave me with a few minutes of confusion and a bitter aftertaste.

If there's one thing I would wish for that would make this game a 10/10, it would be an update or DLC that wraps up the story a bit better by giving you some final choices, or making it much clearer exactly WHEN your agency fully ends. It makes perfect logical reasoning why your character would be able to do this at that point, is supported by everything the people tell you during your journey, and would allow for a much better feeling of "closing the book" on your own terms, in true RPG fashion.\t\t\t\t\t
