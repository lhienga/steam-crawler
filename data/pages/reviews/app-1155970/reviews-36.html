\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeep narrative game with a *lot* of meaningful decisions to make along the way, and only very few of them are clearly good/bad but instead generally have a lot of nuance. A surprising amount of content and things to find/explore as well, and despite my time with the game (~12 hours) apparently I still didn't find everything.
I'd recommend it to anyone that enjoys games that let you roleplay characters the way you want them to, enjoy worlds where humans act with their own motivations but no one is really evil, and people who enjoy fantasy worlds where 'monsters' are just animals.

Also, I recommend playing this on the easiest difficulty if you enjoy immersing yourself in fantasy worlds. There's still plenty of things you can screw up, but you have a lot more time to explore and just enjoy the world - which since world building is such a big part of why this game is cool actually makes me feel like I 'won out' by making that choice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was an incredibly immersive experience from beginning to end. The world-building was fantastic and made me want to experience more stories from this world. The decisions were often difficult and required real consideration, speaking to how invested I felt in these characters. Sometimes it felt that no matter what you did, people would be unhappy. Choosing to do the "right" thing (or whatever is most appropriate for your warden's path) was difficult at times and I appreciated the resulting self-reflection. I highly highly suggest this game to anyone who appreciates a great story, unique characters, and a well-done fantasy world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe wall is still standing. There are no wolves around, no stench of blood. Good signs.
\tThis should be the place I've been looking for. I was supposed to meet with a group of soldiers, but I hear no voices, no sounds of labor.
\tThe gate is ajar, the camp isn't safe. It may keep out the goblins and pebblesrs, though not the beast folk, nor trolls. Night is drawing near.
\tFara breathes heavily, she's had a long day.
\tI dismount her, my heavy boots hit the ground. Pain of the long ride catches up with me. I stretch out, bringing my back and legs some comfort.
\tAll I want now is a table , a decent chair, a nice mug of mead and some warm stew.
\tWith any luck, my axe won't be needed here.

\tIf this is a military encampment, it doesn't look the part. Plenty of wasted space, and the fire pit is cold.
\tTwo soldiers are sitting at a table, they appear tired and disheartened. They look in different directions, paying no attention to one another. One of them is holding a cup.
\tAs I approach, it takes them a few breaths to glance in my direction. The first soldier greets me with a wave of his hand. There are bags under his eyes, his beard is unkempt. Despite his simple shirt, he's wearing durable, decent boots.
\tA mace, with a head covered in iron hangs at his side, though he doesn't reach for it.
\tI look at the second solder, and just like me, she's wearing a gambeson. It fits her a bit loose, as if she took it off a corpse. Her head is shaven, perhaps protection from lice. Her eyes weary, yet kind. She smiles at me.
\tConsidering the detachment was sent here half a year ago, these two certainly look the part. Though there should be more of them. Eight, I believe I was told.
\t
\t"It's nice to see an unarmed traveler in this forsaken ♥♥♥♥ hole, makes me just a tiny bit hopeful." The bearded man says in a strong, yet timid voice. "You'll be staying the night with us, I guess?"
\t"We're soldiers, he and I." adds the armored woman. Her posture switches from half asleep to relaxed. "We'll do our best to keep the camp safe, if you were to take the first watch, it would be a huge help. Travelers ought to help each other, wouldn't you say?"
\tI think for a moment, to fully rest, I'd need a good nights sleep. Reluctantly I agree.
\t"Fantastic!" she rubs her hands together. "I don't remember the last time I had more than half a nights sleep. The hours before midnight should be the calmest. Just wake us up if anything happens".
\tThe man flashes me a wide smile, "It's easy to wake us up, just yell." then takes a drink from his mug.

\t"I was hoping to meet with your sergeant, could we talk for a bit?"
\tThe woman lets out a loud sigh, dusts off her gambeson, and steps towards me. She pays little attention to the sword at her side.
\t"We most definitely can, you and I, though holding this rank is still somewhat new to me. Are you a messenger? Did you lose your mount?" Observing me with curiosity. "Or are you looking for help?"
\t"I'm your new roadwarden, my horse is tied up outside." I reply.
\t"Really?!" The solder in the shirt leans forward. "That explains how you arrived here in one piece, and all by yourself."
\t"Better bring your horse inside the palisade." adds the sergeant. "We have no hay, but I bet it dreams of dropping its saddle." A long pause. "I'm Tulia by the way." \tShe reaches out her hand. "Haleth." I reply.
\tHer grasp is confident, the shake is slight. "Just keep your horse away from the tent." She steps away. "We don't need to smell it's dung."
\t"Ah, there's one issue." The soldier in the shirt rises to his feet. "We've no tent to spare, you'll have to use a blanket or something."
\t"I should get accustomed to sleeping on the ground anyway." I reply.

\tI walk through the gate. Fara looks around and snorts anxiously.
\tNot many humans could ride a horse. She's not only taller than I am, she's also bulky, and as heavy as she's strong. I can get into the saddle within a single breath. Most people wouldn't know where to even begin. From every side, she's a wall of flesh.
\tHorses were brought to The Dragonwood from the conquest in the South. They can trot for a long time, though won't outrun some of the local monsters. My palfrey needs me to survive, though, without her, I too would be lost.
\tShe's my only companion here. I want her to feel at ease.
\tFara takes a few steps towards me, scolding me with another snort. I scratch the bottom of her neck, with strength and confidence, just the way she likes it.
\tHumans see useful animals, and even pets, as monsters in disguise. Getting emotionally attached to them is believed to lead humans to their doom.
\tI know horses need companionship.
\tI speak gently and lead her to the fire pit. Removing the saddle makes her nicker with relief. I take a couple of minuets to examine her back, just in case. While the riding equipment is not that heavy for such a strong animal, with enough time it starts to chafe.
\tI wish she had something better to eat than this shabby grass. I should look for an inn.
\tI need to unpack.

\tI haven't brought that many things, and I lost one of the sacks while fleeing the Crimson Corps Eaters. Worst of all, I have no rope left, maybe the soldiers could spare one. Shouldn't cost more than a dragon bone.
\tAside from the travel set, I own a few valuable possessions, essentials for my trade.
\tI spent my life's savings on decent combat equipment. A fine gambeson, an axe made of steel and a reliable crossbow with bolts.
\tI unpack and inspect my belongings. My water skin isn't pierced and the spare clothes are still here. Just in case, I take a look at my wooden bowl and mug, my cape, tinder box, bandages, food rations, knife... nothing special or too cumbersome.
\tFrom time to time my routine helps me avoid mistakes, this doesn't always make it any more exciting.

\tI return to the soldiers, who are sitting at the table again. They are observing my horse and chatting between themselves. My stomach growls at the sight of them eating out of wooden bowls.
\tOne more bowl was put at a previously unused end of the table. I can sit down on a tree log.
\tI join them and take a look at the meal.
\tIt is cold gruel, the meal eaten in times of hardship. This specific bowl is filled with water, hog millet, some strange looking cereals and blueberries.
\tWelcoming me with a meal, even a humble one, is beyond their duty. Soldiers live with and for their companions, constantly on the move from one part of the realm to another, making sacrifices to protect their group as they face dozens of hideous creatures. Their lives are filled with discipline, hardship and camaraderie.
\tRoadwardens, on the other hand, learn how to work by themselves. We seldom engage in open combat. Patrolling the same roads for years on end. We help the settlements stay in touch, maintain commerce, settle down, and forge friendships. When there are no laws to follow, we use our own judgment.
\tDifferent responsibilities, different lifestyles.

\tI eat quickly, not focusing on the taste, then speak with Tulia.
\tShe looks focused and is choosing her words carefully. Looking away when gathering her thoughts.
\t"I'm afraid I can tell you less than I would like and less than I should." Nodding toward the other soldier. "As you can see, there's not a lot of us left. At the beginning of summer there were eight of us, including our previous sergeant. Five are dead, one has run away in tears."
\t"We're also strangers in this land." adds her companion.
\tI smile. "Any piece of information may help me do my job."
\tThe man leans forward, his legs shake nervously. He sounds like a kid asking a bard to sing on more tale, tell a joke, or do a magic trick. Whatever it takes to escape from boredom. His untrimmed beard hides a much younger face than I originally thought.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat RPG, worth its price.
Plenty of decisions to make: tons of subtle choices that affect your stats and social standing, and dozens of life-changing decisions that really make a difference on how story goes.

3 classes to choose from encourage replaying, but I feel like I explored almost all it has to offer in one playthrough. I will try playing it over some time later.

There is a lot of LGBT mentions - among few hundred villagers in this medieval setting, there are at least a dozen gay pairs, and a 3-person family. I'm no homophobe but it felt very forced and unfitting. Struggling medieval villagers needed to make many children to survive, not wonder about their own sexuality.
If you're a LGBT hater, this might hinder your fun.\t\t\t\t\t
