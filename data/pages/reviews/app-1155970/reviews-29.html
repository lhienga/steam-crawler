\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to give this a heavily qualified thumbs up.

Pros:
* rich, evocative writing
* excellent soundtrack
* interesting game world with various familiar factions (undead, druids, etc) reimagined in unique ways
* as someone in another comment said, "it's more Witcher than The Witcher."

Cons:
* I chose an alchemist character to have high speech, and I still failed many quests and ended up "failing" the game. Why? Many conversation options are "do or die" and if you "fail" a speech check, certain paths may be blocked to you with no alternatives
* You may unwittingly do certain events out of order, leading to blocked paths. For example, everyone warns you that "going east through the heart of the forest" is deadly, so I saved that for later which may have been the wrong choice.
* Even if you do everything at the right time, certain options are never offered again. For example, one quest involves placing a number of objects around the map. At one location, I was told I could place the object secretly, against someone's wishes. I didn't want to do that. Later I changed my mind, but when I revisited the spot, the option wasn't offered again.
* Constantly at a loss for funds. It was a good idea to limit player funds, but they are really too tight at normal difficulty, making things unpleasant.
* Completely artificial binary choice towards the end. You are given the option of proceeding alone, or recruiting 4 companions. I could only find 3 companions, and didn't have enough money to hire more anyway. This feels very arbitrary: why four? Presumably because the author didn't want to code options with fewer than four.
* one faction is presented as the "bad" faction. I tried to choose a more nuanced approach to that faction and it just failed miserably, and in the epilogue it became clear they were supposed to be the "bad" faction. For a game with subtlety, this wasn't very subtle.
* Although the game is generally grim and realistic, it is very woke when it comes to so-called gender dynamics. Every leader in the game is female, and there are several gay and polyamorous characters. This seems rather odd, given the quasi-medieval setting. In places with hardscrabble existence, gender roles tend to be traditional.

Conclusion: worth playing for the atmosphere, and for supporting small devs. But it has some game-breaking questline issues that can lead to an overall unpleasant playthrough.

Edit: just played through it a second time and achieved all my goals. Based on the second playthrough, I defintely suggest going with the warrior class, not the alchemist class (not sure about the mage as I haven't played that one yet). Another note: even on this second playthrough, I finished the game with no days left of the 40-day countdown, which means you're almost required to fail the game once in order to know how efficient you need to be with all your tasks.

Still encountered some weird bugs, but overall: a strangely moody and intense fantasy adventure, recommended.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh this is so, so good.

In terms of genre, it actually reminds me a lot of Quest for Glory, an old Sierra game that took their existing adventure game template and added RPG-style progression elements. Like Quest for Glory, you get to choose one of three classes at the outset which affect your possible puzzle solutions. The tone is completely different from a Sierra game; Roadwarden is much grittier and much more focused on ordinary life rather than fairytale heroism. But the feel and experience of progressing through the game, in a story-rich environment where you slowly win the friendship and trust of strangers in a distant place, is very very similar.

As other reviewers have mentioned, though, there's a lot of reading. This is basically a novel. Come prepared.

The writing is superb. There's a lot of richness in the descriptions of environments and people, and a lot of aesthetic consistency between the pixel art, the text, and the music. The soundtrack varies from minimal folk to post-rock and it's consistently strong. The pixel art is spare, but that feels congruous with the mood and it emphasizes the text rather than distracting from it.

Overall, highly recommended. If you are looking at this and thinking "I like the vibe of this", you'll love the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I found this to be an enjoyable game. It's certainly not for everyone, the vast majority of gameplay is just reading and making choices. I find it hard to get into fantasy most of the time, but this had a decent enough mix of fantasy and reality that I found it mostly immersive. The overall plot was interesting and you get to interact with a lot of different characters with varying backgrounds and religions. It definitely scratched the "adventurer" itch. My personal ending was pretty much a downer across the board, even though I felt like I made pretty good decisions overall. Not sure how many different endings there are. In any case, I wouldn't change my choices and I wouldn't play through it again, but it was a lot of fun in that first play through.

If you enjoy text-rich narrative games, you will want to check it out.\t\t\t\t\t
