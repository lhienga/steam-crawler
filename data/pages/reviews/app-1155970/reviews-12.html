\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is only really 1 thing I have a serious issue with and its that vitality is not sufficiently explained in the paltry tutorial sequence. Because of that, I spent 10 hours blissfully ignorant as to exactly how my stats actually worked, and that caused a lot of delays in terms of getting things done.

I understand a lack of roadsigns and guarded villagers, I understand inns costing money which is a scarce resource early on, but you NEED to explain to people, that in order to pass most skill checks, you need vitality, and to have vitality early, you need to sleep, in an inn (or similarly cozy place) while not being hungry.

I rage quit after giving up 3 rations to the swampy guy because i kept getting to the thorns too late in the day to walk in the gate (wth?) but honestly, if you just explained that, I could have saved 1 of those because id have had some vitality (or at least could have been reasonably expected to have some) for the sole thing I wanted to do in that place in the first place.

This seems to be a somewhat common wrinkle on the game per this reddit post:

https://www.reddit.com/r/Roadwarden/comments/xfhau3/how_do_i_restore_vitality/




Like everything else, will probably take another stab at it in a bit when i forget some of the spoilers and locations of things. But since its an adventure game, a text ehavy one, starting a new save isnt that appealing, so im going to say that I do not recomend this game unless they really really drill into the player that "vitality is important, and here is how you actually get it".

A 1 time explanation is not sufficient, it needs to be explained in the UI.


Another thing, for how much text the game has, there is a shocking lack of info in quest logs as well as menus. Got a hint that some place i needed to get to was "west" of where I was. Turns out it was north west. Was also told that "it was really far away", was actually just down the street so i ended up making a beeline through the most "lore-wise" dangerous road in the game to discover that it put me on the wrong side of the map. Feel like there are bad hints sometimes, from sources that should be reliable, like the mayor of a town, or someone thats lived in the area for years.\t\t\t\t\t

Early one morning, he was walking along the shore after a big storm had passed and found the vast beach littered with starfish as far as the eye could see, stretching in both directions.

Off in the distance, the old man noticed a small boy approaching. As the boy walked, he paused every so often and as he grew closer, the man could see that he was occasionally bending down to pick up an object and throw it into the sea. The boy came closer still and the man called out, “Good morning! May I ask what it is that you are doing?” The young boy paused, looked up, and replied “Throwing starfish into the ocean. The tide has washed them up onto the beach and they can’t return to the sea by themselves,” the youth replied. “When the sun gets high, they will die, unless I throw them back into the water.” The old man replied, “But there must be tens of thousands of starfish on this beach. The boy bent down, picked up yet another starfish and threw it as far as he could into the ocean. Then he turned, smiled and said, “It made a difference to that one!”

You are a Roadwarden. Not a god.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExceptional. The writing & atmosphere are incredible; there's just the right balance struck between visual details and descriptions... it really makes vivid images pop right into your mind's eye. The soundtrack is also second to none. Very evocative, atmospheric, and full of mood.

The game mechanics and moment-to-moment play are not super deep, but they are handled very well. I do think it could explain itself just a little bit better (for example, the bathing equipment you can buy is infinite-use, they're effectively permanent upgrades to any bathing spot). There's a lot of quests and thread to unravel, a lot of which are interlocked.

The story is also very engaging, and the cardinal sin of fantasy-adjacent games (hitting you over the head with a noun-filled lore dump) does NOT happen. There are actually some Kentucky Route Zero style bits where you get to decide what the background of some things are. The way this world, this bit of country, its wilderness are portrayed... it's all really really good.

I have only three minor complaints with this game.

- I don't have a great memory with names. The game has a "journal" section that tracks a lot of information and names, which is great! However, it doesn't track all of them. The game already highlights a lot of important words; I would have loved to have small tooltips to remind me who's who sometimes.
- The soundtrack IS great, however it loops the same couple tracks for a LONG portion of the game's introduction segment. Don't let that deter you.
- As of the time of writing, cloud saves do not fully work. They seem to only work within the same OS. Windows saves get synced between Windows computers, but don't show up on Steam Deck, and vice versa.\t\t\t\t\t
