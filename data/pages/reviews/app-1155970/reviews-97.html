\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is a phenomenal fantasy rpg experience.

The writing does such a superb job painting a portrait of the world and the people therein. People and land that are often hostile to the player. There's a well-worn, tired feeling to the peninsula that seeps into every crevice like the ache of a poorly-healed wound. But as the people warm up to you, the villages begin to feel more and more like the embrace of an old friend.

The sound design also aids in fleshing out the world, especially when away from the sparsely populated villages. There's such a rich layering of the sounds out in nature that the wilds feel full of life, unbothered by the humans that sometimes struggle to survive there.

The music is great, and uses instruments (like electric guitar) you may not expect to hear given the setting of a rough hewn fantasy world. There's a quiet, haunting quality to some of the tracks that just evoke an intense longing -- maybe for change, maybe for safety, maybe for connection.

If you decide to pick it up and try it out, I suggest doing your first playthrough on the casual mode since it has no time limit and is a bit more forgiving. There's A Lot of stuff to explore here and uncover -- lots of layers of mystery and intrigue. It's a bummer to invest a lot of time into a playthrough and realize that you're not going to be able to conclude it satisfyingly without more information that's a bit tough to wrest from the peninsula on one's first foray.

Regardless of what difficulty you pick to start, though, this game is definitely worth a deep dive.

Honestly, the only complaints that I have about the game are:

- The song that plays at the Pelt is way too short for how much time is spent there. The repetitiveness makes is kind of grating over time, at least for me. And it's seemingly the only song that loops that fast, so it's a shame. Especially given how great the rest of the music is in the game. And like, that song isn't bad, it's just way too short.

- It's seemingly possible to softlock yourself on some side quests. Almost certainly this is a bug but it's frustrating to not be able to finish certain side quests correctly, if you've already met certain other "main story" quest completions.

- Some of the dice roll dialog/action options seem less to chance than they are seemingly checking progress on other things (like progression of trust or other values). Like, if it is actually a chance thing, then the dice roll works for an icon but if it's not that much up to chance, then it should be a different icon, like maybe one that evokes allegiance to a faction or something, to give a clue to the player that they need more trust or something.

And that's about it for negative things. If you like rich fantasy settings for rpgs and like reading well-written prose, give this game a shot. It's really great.\t\t\t\t\t
