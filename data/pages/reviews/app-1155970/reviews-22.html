\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an addicting fun role-playing game! It's mostly text based, but choose your own adventure style has never been better. A lot of reading here, so if that's not your thing you might want to skip this one. The story is richly in-depth and gripping at times. The graphics, music and ambiance adds a lot of fluff and flavor to the game. The text is very descriptive and the choices you make actually matter and make a difference. This gem of a game kind of reminds me of the old solo role-playing adventure paperback books I used to play when I was younger. I used to really like the ones that you had to keep track of stuff on paper, like items and skills. This game also has that and your journal keeps track of everything, including quests, people and places etc.

I can tell there was a lot of work that was put into this game, and the journey is excellently crafted. I haven't got to the end of the game yet, but I have died a few times. A great adventure is to be had here! Have fun and enjoy the experience! Just writing this review makes me want to play it some more. I might start over and try a different type of class, not sure yet.

10/10 - Tell em' Large Marge sent ya! Hahahahaha!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI very much enjoyed my time with this game. On the surface, the setting appears to be rote gritty fantasy, but there are enough twists and turns in the worldbuilding to keep you interested throughout your entire playthrough. I was discovering things up until the very end of the game, and even then, I was hesitant to leave just in case I missed something. And, ostensibly I did, as the game allows you to make many meaningful choices that irrevocably change the course of the story. This game features the hardest boss battles of them all: conversations with important people.

The art is beautiful, though that's apparent from the store page. And of course the writing is amazing; It's how you'll be interacting with the world. The game keeps track of most things for you in your Journal, but there were definitely a few points where I was kicking myself for not utilizing the Notes system. The game definitely doesn't hand-hold you, you're free to miss things and screw up things that you can't un-screw. That is, unless you quick-load. I like what they did for Hovlavan, but, I don't know, I feel as though there could have been a better way of implementing it. Though that's a very minor gripe for a great game.

If you like reading, gritty fantasy, and getting immersed in a strange world, you'll like playing Roadwarden.\t\t\t\t\t
