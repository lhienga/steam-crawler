\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review comes from someone that is not familiar with text based roleplaying RPGs, my only experience with this type of game is Disco Elysium, which is also the reason i picked this up. Disco Elysium left a mark, an itch for more like it, more to sink into, and this delivered.
This game reminded me a lot of the book "Spook's apprentice" i read some years ago, it's a more grounded approach to fantasy which i really like, i guess the term would be Dark fantasy? I loved the setting, it feels fleshed out and real, and the ways you affect little bits of the world with you decision, as in, choosing whether [city] did great after the war, or if they are struggling and starving, all in a passing conversation with and NPC about your time there, that's really charming, and helps setting up the world for the character you want to play.
The gameplay really gripped me, too. The resource management, including time management, leads to interesting decisions which feel impactful and satisfying, and have repercursions in the rest of the game. And there were parts which i was a tad bit surprised by some gameplay mechanics, but i'll not spoil that. Overall an ejoyable experience, that i'll definitely give time for a second playthrough. This was good! real good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it had an amazing and captivating start, Roadwarden's story did not have enough to keep me wanting more. Basic tasks are easy to understand, and going back and forth between places is the 90% of the things you will be doing.

Most of the places you visit don't require another visit ever again, which is a shame since you are kinda forced to spend most of your time just hoping from one village to another doing mostly menial tasks.

The idea of using words to investigate places and people is a very interesting mechanic, but a lot of times you are left with no ideas what to do with it until much later on, which halts your progress by quite a lot in your first time around.

On top of that, the game kind of forces you to do multiple playthroughs to experience everything and to get everything, which is not something I would do just out of fun.

In the end, the game is not bad, but it lack proper character involvement and storytelling to hold onto its players.

A very low-mid 5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat does it mean to be a "Roadwarden"? This should only be answered by your own ideas of what the word means. Are you just, or just surviving?

This is an excellent adventure supported by the writing. It *does feel*, like a real place. Like a strange fictional, but magical place that somehow offers a glimpse into its past.

I wanted to search it as the days grew lesser. I felt the urge and need to progress despite the stillness of the world; there is always *something* to be done. Time will simply slip away as the days grow shorter. Not much different from our own life.

A truly enjoyable, crafted, experience. I am tired, and I must rest.\t\t\t\t\t
