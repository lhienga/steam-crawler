It pairs lovely writing, minimalist art and excellent music together with a really rich RPG system that is packed with branches and responsive characters, rather than dice rolls and statistics. I loved every moment with the game, and it's really worth dipping into.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is an excellent text-based adventure and well worth a look. I thoroughly enjoyed the roughly 12-hour playthrough, where I met fascinating characters, unravelled intriguing mysteries and burned an alarmingly high number of corpses.

If you’re on the fence, do keep in mind that, like Disco Elysium and Citizen Sleeper, there’s a ton of reading in this game (obviously). Thankfully, there’s a demo you can check out before purchasing to see if it’s your kind of thing.

It’s well worth the entry price, and I’d say there’s some replay value. When I finished my playthrough, I immediately started thinking about what I’ll do differently the next time I ventured into the peninsular.
