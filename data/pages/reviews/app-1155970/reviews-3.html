\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent text based adventure.

Visuals: Despite being a text adventure game, the pixel based graphical style fits the game world quite well. The art is generally used as a backdrop for the scenes you will be leading, and they are never distracting. The colour palette was excellent for the atmosphere the game decided to take.

Audio: Excellent choice of music really enhanced the atmosphere throughout the game. Not much else can be said about the audio, though I might be missing something.

Gameplay: As a text based game, the things that can be done here are limited. The addition of time limits, day and night cycles, hunger, and armour mechanics are wonderful additions in enhancing the gameplay and the atmosphere however.

Story: The story itself is one you make up for yourself. You decide your character's motivations and goals, a thing that is sorely missed in the so called "role-playing games" spat out by big studios these days.

As a "roadwarden" you are tasked with clearing and understanding a dangerous part of the world. At the beginning, you are a stranger in a strange land. It feels like there might be danger behind every corner. Slowly, over the course of the game, you get to know and understand your ward, build up places of safety for yourself, understand the people of this land better, and even make friends. The change is gradual, and you are given plenty of opportunities to express your character and if you are well prepared, you should be able to survive your adventures.

Overall, I really enjoyed my time with this game. Would recommend.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend Roadwarden.

This game has an incredibly well written and immersive story. The soundtrack is elegant in its simplicity, and is varied enough that you don't get tired listening to it as you read walls of text. The art style gives just enough for you to build a concrete picture of your location while leaving a lot of details up to the satisfaction of your own imagination. The choose your own adventure game play works in tandem with the numerous important decisions you make that affect you the rest of the game. You really feel like you have to work hard to make a name for yourself while playing a delicate balancing act of politics not to favour any one faction too much at the detriment of the others. The time management and resource gathering doesn't take away from the game but adds to it. Altogether, I had an amazing experience with this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is a text-based RPG in a world where nature is dominant over humans, who live in the shadow of horrifying monsters and herds that punish aggressive deforestation with slaughter. Sent to explore a remote peninsula that has attracted the attention of merchants from one of the land's few cities, you have 40 days to clear the trails, make allies, uncover lost secrets, and find your missing predecessor. All the while, you have to worry about the needs of survival in a frontier where everything wants to kill and no one is really interested in helping out. At first.

The game seamlessly transitions from managing basic resources needed for survival to delving into a complex web of interlocking rivalries, friendships, and interests in a tribal frontier. It's easy to die or miss something important, but thankfully you can save and reload at will. There's a lot of reading, so bear that in mind, but it's well worth it, easily the best $5 I've spent on a game in years.\t\t\t\t\t
