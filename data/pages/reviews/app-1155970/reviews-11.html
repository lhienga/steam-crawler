\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game for 7 hours straight.

You're a Roadwarden, someone who keeps the roads free from monsters, obstacles, and highwaymen, while building relations with the locals and relaying messages between towns. Your primary task after being sent to the Dragonwoods by the capital is to track down the previous Roadwarden named Asterion. You get to build relationships with the characters (which is sometimes helped by your chosen faith - mine was pagan. Go druids!) and slowly uncover the land's secrets. You get to meet a reclused sorceress who may or may not have an addiction problem, a bear of a woman who owns an inn, a vain and cunning (but sincere once you gain her trust) mayor who is deeply connected to a tragic incident that befell a local town years and years ago, several monks, a lot of druids, a male version of Poison Ivy, a lotta undead (burn 'em or not, up to you), and if you're lucky - a unicorn.

There's tons of little aspects to the game like dragonbones (turned into shillings. I'm not kidding), timed quests, whether you're a fighter or pacifist, class perks, unique inventory items (that bait you into selling them only to regret doing so later on because oh ♥♥♥♥, they're important), how each town thinks of one another, bed-and-breakfast which slowly becomes free (if you're dedicated to establishing good reputation), influence on your character's worldview, and more.

Creeks was my favourite town by a long shot. I'm not sure if the Roadwarden can choose where to settle down yet but I'm settling down in Creeks. It was also interesting how no one had seen a horse before save for mine. The capital, Hovlovan, vaguely reminded me of King's Landing though some dialogue options seem to help you flesh out/choose how you want it to be like. Trust is a great factor to learn more information in the game as well as get better endings (and achievements).

This is the first time I've played a text RPG like this. I honestly didn't expect it to be so well done. At first I thought it might be like D&D, but no, the story is pretty centered though there's a lot to unpack. it's like a little CYOA because of how varied the endings each character and town get - it all depends on what you choose to do and not do with the information you have. The story, gameplay, and characters successfully kept me at it even until my eyes were begging me to go rest.

I'm going to play another round of this to unlock the other achievements, mainly find the Tribe of the Green Mountain . Cheers. 8.9/10
