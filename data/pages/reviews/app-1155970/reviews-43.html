\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would give this game an easy 9/10

Positives:

Great price for the size of the game. A single playthrough in normal difficulty will take around 15-25 hours, and there is some level of replayability if you want to find other endings and with a different class.
Amazing OST, it really fits the atmosphere and was very enjoyable
It can be very rewarding for the more observant players, as there will be some events or interactions where you can really mess up and lock yourself out of interesting storylines if you weren't paying attention.
Active dev in the community forum
A VERY wide diversity of endings, not just for the main character but for side characters, locations and storylines.
Easily one of the best games I've ever played, I'll be sure to be on the lookout for games in this style or by this developer in the future.

Negatives:

You'll probably have to save-scum a bit at one point or another. Either you'll run into a big cat and lose all your life, hunger and armor or you'll make a trip somewhere only to realize that the trip was useless and you've lost half a day, or you'll fail a quest because now you can't get to were you neeeded to be in time. Without a bit of save-scumming your first playthrough will have some very annoying parts, and you'll probably have to play the game again to get a resolution to all the main storylines. Obviously save-scumming isn't the end of the world and it's a nice way to change the difficulty, but if you end up abusing it too much it really takes away a lot from the story.

The diversity in the game can be a little excessive at times considering the setting. There are gay, lesbian and bisexuals individuals, a polyamorous couple and even a nonbinary person. If this were Cyberpunk 2077 or a game in a setting where that makes more sense then it won't break your suspension of disbelief, but in a game that has a very medieval setting it absolutely takes you out of the setting. Maybe if it were addressed directly or involved in the story, e.g. the main religion in the game opposes these beliefs but the pagans are accepting of them (so it becomes another plot point in the game) or vice versa, it would be less noticeable and wouldn't break the player's immersion, but as it stands it did end up doing that a lot. However, the way that diversity was added was the correct way. Instead of it being something that you immediately discover about a person (which can end up feeling like forced inclusivity with no purpose other than pandering), their diverse sexual preferences were usually something you'll only discover after talking to them for a bit, and the way it was mentioned was very natural and logical and did not feel forced. So maybe the setting wasn't the best for this kind of inclusivity and it could have been integrated into the story to make it more reasonable, but at least it never felt very forced, just a bit jarring at times.

The pace falls a little bit halfway through the game and it occasionally feels like a little bit of a struggle to continue, but it picks back up. Or maybe I only felt that because I completed it in two sessions so I just got tired halfway through, who knows.

Occasionally you will make decisions that seem very small but will actually completely affect the ending that you get, which can be a little unexpected. This one is less of a con and more of an in-between, as it leads to interesting and unexpected endings but can sometimes lock you out of desired endings simply because of a small decision that you made much earlier on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game, with deep emphasis on forming and understanding relationships between various groups of thought. I liked how the interactions weren't solely "he says this, she says that" but also describing discrete movements such as "showed palms facing up, stormy eyes, scowl, walked heavily, voice cracks, kept hands hidden behind back, etc.". Roadwarden does an amazing job of describing the world around you, from how the people are, to what the environment looks and feels like.

My play through lasted ~41hrs and i was able to complete and experience all interactions in the game while playing under the difficulty setting of normal (40 day limit). In the end I had about 15 days to spare before closing my journey. Your choices in the game matter greatly; from your choice to how you respond to people, to what your personal thoughts are, and especially to how you administrate your time and make the most of the day.

The game is all about reading and interpreting situations based on what you read, as such, if you enjoy reading this game is highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

The characters you interact with all feel like they have their story, and their place in the world. Every item can have great merit. Each coin can make the difference between keeping well on your feet or struggling the next day. Even with completing one run of the story, further runs create a very different adventure through additional available paths, and to uncover what might have been missed.\t\t\t\t\t

Flavor review

Life on the Peninsula can be summed up in one word: challenging.

From the very beginning, I knew this journey would be difficult. At the same time, I was both right and wrong. Right, because indeed, life here is difficult. Wrong, because the extent of this difficulty was so great that I was forced to redefine the meaning of this word for myself. What I used to call "difficult" before is very different from what I now call "difficult".

I have faced many trials and made many difficult decisions here. The decisions, dangers, secrets, people, and leaders are so numerous that the 40 days I had to fulfill my mission felt like a single week.

Life here is plural. Numerous villages and people need help, work, items, messages, protection, and even people seeking revenge... wherever I went, my mind never ceased to find objectives and challenges to fulfill.

A major challenge was prioritizing whom to help and where to go. These were the shortest 40 days of my life. Time seemed extremely limited here: the Peninsula is vast, and the wilderness is untamed and brutal - many days were lost on these treacherous roads.

Every soul in this place has their own goals, needs, frustrations, and individual difficulties, and they struggle fiercely to secure what they believe is best for them.

It was my job to explore as much of this Peninsula as possible, while simultaneously ensuring the road was safer for myself and everyone else. All of this while I faced my own difficulties: being attacked by wild beasts, struggling to find safe shelter, and striving hard to keep both my pocket and stomach full.

Autumn has finally arrived. And with its arrival, I begin my departure. Today, I embark on my journey back to the Cities, proud of my accomplishments here, yet frustrated by my inability to do more.

My impact on the Peninsula was real - I know that. But I have but the strenght of just one man. Clearly, I failed to do everything I promised, which weighs heavily on my shoulders as I leave. This weight is alleviated because I also directly and indirectly improved many lives with my work in this place. While I saved entire villages, at the same time, I abandoned others completely to their own bad luck.

I bring back to the Cities everything I have learned about this place, its people, their difficulties, and - perhaps the most important for the Cities - the wealth that can be found here.


Regular review

Roadwarden has a LOT of content. It's definitely worth playing and even replaying the game. It's highly unlikely that you'll complete 100% of the game's content on your first playthrough. There are many details that, depending on the priority and order you complete each individual mission, take more time to discover. Having known such details earlier could be a huge time-saver for you, leaving you with more time for adventuring on the Peninsula.

The game is immersive, and the atmospheric music/sound effects are good and help a lot on the immersion. In the first few days, I felt an uncomfortable repetitiveness in the music, but that quickly passed once I uncovered and opened up new routes for exploration.

Personally, I give this game an 8/10 rating. It's a narrative-driven game, which may not appeal to everyone. If you're not willing to read (and read A LOT), narrative games may not be your thing (and that's okay, if you don't enjoy it, refund it).

My biggest issue with this game was the feeling of being poor, extremely poor.

Narratively, it makes a lot of sense, considering your character's mission is to explore a place facing many hardships. The help and services you provide are hard and demanding, and the payment for your work - in most cases - does not meet the effort you input.

And as you face all the hardships of this place, all while paying for shelter, food, and services - this all combined quickly drains your pockets. With an empty pocket, you need to work. By working, you can't explore. Without exploring, you can't fulfill your mission and uncover the various secrets and stories of this game.

That said, I highly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
