\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: The game miserably fails the immersion check multiple times during less than 10 hours of play. That's a pretty big issue for RPG if you ask me.

The world in the game is meticulously described as unforgiving and dangerous so the setting of harsh environment where every character/beast is fighting for survival sounds perfect for your very own adventure, right? Wrong, you will play the way you are supposed to and that's it. E.g:
- I just killed 2 griffons, the rest run away. I should be able to load the carcasses on the horse and have food/materials/money for days, right? Nope, that would have made the adventure way too fun, we can't have that.
- I encountered a distracted beast blocking a path. I should be able to make use of my armor/scale piercing crossbow and shoot its uncovered back to have food/materials/money for days, right? Guess again, we can't have you going that way yet so...
- I found the lair of foxes in an old building. I decided to come back later as I should be able to...and...for days right? Nope, can't visit the location again, even though it's right ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ there, next to the road to another village.

If you don't consider immersion the core of RPGs then the issues described above won't bother you. Good luck and have fun in that case. I just wanted to shed some light on some OBVIOUS issues, that NONE of the positive reviews mentions.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI definitely have enjoyed playing this game. Its perfect for those who like a more story driven option. I'm not much of an achievement hunter, but trying to uncover all the possible outcomes of the game is what keeps me playing. If there was any criticism, I do wish that there was greater variation in the character interactions or choices. It feels like 90% of the game is the same each playthough no matter the character or class meaning you need to perform the same interactions with the same NPC and conversations are repetitive. Good if you want to complete the challenge modes and speed run the game, I guess. After 2 rounds, I'm already 75%+/- complete so after another 2 there won't be much replay ability.

Despite that, I would like to keep my eye out for future games from this developer. I would love to play something similar again, maybe with some different class abilities and something along the lines of "randomly generated" towns within the map. Say there are 15 'faux towns' with NPC's and each time you start a save it takes a random sampling of 4-5 of those created towns. It would certainly add to interest and longevity if you could look forward to meeting different people, having different tasks, different interactions between villages, and different conversations every time you played. A LOT more programming, I know; it would be a monster of a game, but it seems there's a lot of positive feedback around games like these and I think with added variation you could really bump up that price quite a bit and people would willingly pay it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything about this game just hits right on the mark, from the amazing world building to the intense and strategic combat with rich and vivid descriptions and the compelling politics and relations between the characters and factions deeply woven.
It feels so satisfying to spend hours going through the peninsula, unravelling the secrets that the northerners are hiding, as your relationship with them constantly evolves, and seeing the tangible payoff of all the work you've done in the relationships or as the icons in the sidebars pile up stretching across the screen. This is especially impactful with the amount and variety of choices the game presents, working towards the chosen starting goal, favouring a faction and choosing weather to put morals/ reputation over greatly needed resources. Which leads me into the great balancing of the game: there is a near constant struggle of balancing hard to replenish vitality, costly food and amour, deceptively important appearance and being rewarded lots of money only to splurge it all on equipment for increasingly difficult battles; moreover, the amount of days given to explore was the perfect amount for me, enough to complete all the major quest lines and deeds while not enough time to fully complete all side quests and extra pieces, stopping the endgame from feeling empty and without much to do (what happened on my second play-through when I knew most of the secrets and obviously completed it sooner).
As someone who regularly plays D&D and consumes D&D content, this game is one of my favourites of all time, I love the freedom you can get in some sections as you have to input your own prompt of what to do or who/ what to ask about and the very unique interactions outside of the dialogue box that contribute to the story, leading to many great puzzle solving moments that drove me to interact with the world much further than choosing new dialogue option 6 after npc 17 told me to complete x, y and z. I would best describe road warden as fantasy disco elysium (another one of my favourite titles) with a broader and more open world, and would really recommend to anyone else who enjoyed that game or literally anything else I mentioned above.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLOVE this game. It's so cool. It's all text-based, but it manages to be open and not at all linear, yet guided enough for you to know what you're working towards at any given moment. It's like your personal DnD session. You choose your path, religion, and convictions. Then you go explore the unfamiliar place, making decisions. Sometimes you choose from options, sometimes you have to investigate by yourself by typing. There's always something happening, always new surprises.

It's so cozy, yet what you do matters and you have a responsibility to learn and pursue whatever goal you set for yourself. Great worldbuilding. After a while, you get a sense of place and achievement when your actions change something (for the better, hopefully). Love the artstyle. It truly feels like a journey. The game doesn't hold your hand, but it's never unfair. You feel in control, yet you can't always make everything right. It's so fun and exciting to explore everything, complete quests, gain people's trust, one step at a time. Make decisions based on your own beliefs. You can always save and reload, so you can choose if you want to just play it out or rewind a mistake you made.

10/10 game. Probably won't play a second time since exploring for the first time is what I loved most about it. Still, worth every penny. Can't recommend enough.\t\t\t\t\t
