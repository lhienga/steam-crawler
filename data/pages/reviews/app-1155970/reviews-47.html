\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very good game. Thoughtful--I like how it depicts no one as strictly good or evil, while making it clear that some character have clearly done very bad things. Shows a deep understanding of people, and how we justify our own actions.

The item gathering and status-juggling was satisfying and well balanced. I became very invested in a lot of the characters. I adore Dalit particularly, the badass giggler.

I liked the text-entry puzzles, though I feel they need a little more work. Responding to more words, or giving more guiding hints. (spoiler:) For example I knew the spider in the heart of the forest was dead long before I figured out what specific words were needed to convince the game to move forward. I wish the parser had responded to 'dead' or 'safe'\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an exceptional game that I liked very much. The descriptions combined with the art style are evocative and thematic and fit very will with the atmosphere of the game. This is one of the best "make your own adventure" games I have ever played, and I have played a ton of them.
Decisions matter in this game and you will find yourself stressing about them. For a more casual experience, I would suggest playing the "Easy" mode which would allow you enough time to do most of what you want to accomplish and make a lot of money by gambling etc. However, the absence of a time limit of the easy mode does detract a bit from the atmosphere of the game, so for those willing to play and replay this game (and it is good enough to justify at least two playthroughs) I would suggest the normal difficulty.

Eudocia for the win.\t\t\t\t\t
