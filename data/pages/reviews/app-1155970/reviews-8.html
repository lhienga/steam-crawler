\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome people would definetly look over this game because of its text-based nature. However, to my surprise, The Roadwarden turned out to be an absolute masterpiece. It's storybuilding and rich lore really immerse you into a world full of magical creatures, local politics, unexpected turning points and grim battles. Without going into much detail as to avoid spoilers,

The Roadwarden's game mechanics are based on making educated decisions based on the knowledge you may have at said point, where gathering information on certain creatures or events may give you the upper hand. However, your attributes and equipment also play an essential role in this, as having an extensive arsenal may provide new possibilities, as well as your well being and full stomach give you better chances at physical challenges.

In this world, speaking with the locals and helping them give you insight on different matters, such as quests, hints at unexplored opportunities, and even better deals for the future. They're not only helpful, but have such interesting backgrounds and personalities that make you empathise, pity, or even hate them. Each decision you make may come later to burden your conscience, but may as well brighten it, and there was no single one of them that I hadn't taken a few minutes to consider their outcomes.

All in all, the game felt rich, intriguing, and fun through its full lenghth, and would recommend it to any Witcher, The Elder Scrolls, or RPG fan in general.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a traveling "freelancer" you arrive on a mysterious peninsula with a seemingly simple task, from a merchant guild.
Soon you are drawn into an complex ambiguous world full with stories, mysteries and adventures.
The pacing is slow at first, and it allows you to explore, gather information and get a general feeling of the setting.

The world is rich and seems alive. The interactions with various characters and factions is genuine. Their beliefs and motives understandable. Sometimes there is no clear "right" path, just the one that you choose. And you have the freedom to choose from many different paths and ways to approach the tasks and problems you have to solve.

There will be lots of reading. That's how the game will tell you it's story. But the artwork which accompanies the text, really helps with the immersion and allows the player to better visualize the events.

For me this was the first time playing a game which is presented this way, and I must say that it really hooked me. The rather large amount of text didn't feel overwhelming or "too much" like I have experienced in other games where the gameplay is tied to something else and on top of that the story and lore is just plastered with boring texts or books.
Here the text IS the main gameplay mechanic and I think that's way it works so well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have NEVER been fond of text based adventure games. I have Aphantasia, so I do not create vivid images in my mind. But for some reason - mostly the overwhelmingly positive critiques - I gave this game a shot. And I do not regret it even a single bit. I have been taken back to my childhood where I was reading quite a bit without my minds eye. I simply soaked up the atmosphere created by words on pages. And this game? Does it for me so much I want to go back to reading!

I long for this game when I am doing something else. I wish to continue the story. I want to undo some of my choices. I want to do another run, make different choices. I am really, really invested in the villages and some of the people. I have a hard time making choices when I see that two characters I like are at odds with one another. The story behind the southern village? Phew. I knew something was wrong about this particular other village.

The writing is excellent and full of life. The storytelling is amazing. The dialogue is not cryptic at all, something I sometimes hate about some adventure games .

Did I mention that the soundtrack and the little illustrations add perfectly to the vibe?

Anyhow. Buy the game, wether on sale or not, and enjoy this experience. If there are any games just as good out there, I need to play them now. :)\t\t\t\t\t
