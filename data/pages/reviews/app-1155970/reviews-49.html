I played a lot of text adventure games growing up in the 80s and I wish they were all as beautiful / easy as this is to play. The world is intriguing and I feel just as inexperienced as a new Road Warden might be. I'm playing it on steamdeck and first pass at the game was very buggy (would freeze frequently, mouse would disappear, popup steamdeck keyboard would cause in game hotkeys to trigger) but after the most recent patch it is playing smooth as butter. I didn't like the default gamepad setup for the game and heavily modified mine but its been a seamless and enjoyable experience ever since then.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: 4/5 - probably the best RenPy game you will find on Steam

The game claims to be an RPG but that's a bit deceptive - you do roleplay as a roadwarden, a sort of mix between ranger and messenger boy and have to solve various quests for people but the rest of the normal rpg trappings are not there, no xp, almost no gear and no character stats to improve over time. Oh, there is a character sheet, that I opened twice in my run, showing some vague characteristics like piety, trust and ... how many animals you spared. This is all very obtuse until you actually get into the meat of the game. The game can better be defined as a choose-your-own-adventure with a time limit and a few survival elements.
This sort of game hinges on the quality of the storytelling and I can say without a doubt that it is great in this case. The world is very well set up, the characters believable and complex and the atmosphere is moody and intriguing. It if was a novel I would buy this in a heartbeat. What's it about? You are tasked with exploring and preparing a scarcely inhabited peninsula to be integrated into a trading network. You have to figure out what the locals are like, what's the status of each location, what the resources are, figure out what can be trade, figure out what the threats are (wild or otherwise), find out what happened to the last guy they sent and oh yeah, don't get killed
The rest of the gaming elements are there to make the traversal of the story more interesting:
- 3 possible character backgrounds that vary the interactions with the world a bit
- a selection of personal goals that act as a secondary main quest
- a food, vitality and (depending on your choice) magic system that make the solutions to the problems you encounter more... risky

So far so good, you interact with people, find out what they need, go places, face dangers and try to solve them in whatever way you can. So what's the problem? Well, the time restrictions
If you play on normal you have 40 in-game days to finish your task. Sounds like a lot right? Well, there is also the fact that you need to find shelter at night so you do nothing but sleep in that time restricting you even further. I finished my first playthrough with a dozen quests unfinished including the main ones and looking back I can't image how you would get everything done in that time limit without a walkthrough or having a couple of failed runs under your belt. Replayability is fine and all but for this type of game it just means skipping most of the text and save-scumming for the few rng encounters to play out better.
The only other nitpick I have is that you kind of need an external notepad because the in-game journal, while good, misses some potentially important info. All in all, a very good experience and I hope the team improves on future projects

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn Illustrated text-based RPG with a ton of soul and wonderful world building. Where other games have recently had me lose interest due to boring or generic narrative and story; Roadwarden had me staying up way too late multiple times. Sometimes I was getting close to solving a quest, or I discovered how to explore a new part of the map, or I found a new piece of gear that would help me solve a problem that I couldn't figure out, etc. After finishing my first playthrough, I was aware of many things that I wasn't able to solve in my time on the peninsula (normal mode gives you 40 days to do your roadwardening duties before you have to return to those who sent you). I don't remember a single day in game day where I thought "okay, what do I do now?". There is always something, or someone pulling your attention.

Roadwarden also rewards you for being prepared, which honestly feels great in an RPG. Some situations can mean death without the right equipment, information, or planning. I had a notepad open on my second monitor for taking notes of things I thought were important. I probably wrote down too much, but there were numerous times where the info I wrote was a big benefit to me.

All that to say, I loved playing through Roadwarden and it is proof that any genre can be fun and exciting with enough care and passion involved in it's creation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGive this a chance! Really!

This is a great indie, text-based RPG overall. I'm a fan of many different varieties of RPGs and this being my real introduction into what feels like a truly polished text-based RPG with a great pixel aesthetic packaging.

It has is own brand of lore and storytelling that gives it a fresh feeling from the stereotypical AAA RPG games out there and I love the survival style mechanics to really choose your own adventure. There's a lot I haven't gotten into yet but I had high hopes coming in and the feeling is still there after trying a few hours at it.

I recommend this for the casual players who enjoy something with rich storytelling, some interesting world building and those who enjoy having to apply strategy to your adventure RPGs.\t\t\t\t\t
