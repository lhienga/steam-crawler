\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent adventure RPG that will run you 10-15 hours on a first go.

Anyone who enjoys survival games and text heavy RPGs will find a lot to love in this game.

You are a Roadwarden and your job is to not just survive, but thrive in the dangerous frontier filled with monsters and sparsely populated, highly agitated, utterly distrusting local settlements with all sorts of historical, cultural, and personal baggage. And you will be operating under a time limit, harkening back to Fallout 1's well liked search for the Vault water chip replacement, and does so with just enough wiggle room that you should be able to pull it all off with 5-10 days to spare if you figure out how to do things well. And even still, you won't be able to do everything so the directions of focus you choose will determine how far you make it down certain paths. And there's likely outcomes that can be very different if you progress faster.

As for the narrative, the story is presented excellently. The writing is significant but not overwrought that it feels like it's overstaying and overstating things. Quest design is fairly open ended and often most solutions you can think of are on the table which means you should think before you do something. Just because you CAN doesn't mean you SHOULD. And credit to the game, it does not care if you decide to abuse quick saves or not. It's not worried about holding your feet to the fire. Experiment to your heart's content and enjoy the twisting and weaving component of the game's structure.

All in all, it's worth any RPG fan's attention.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game. I love the visual style they went for and the warm beige/brown/orange tones they used throughout.

Unfortunately, it just fell flat for me. I don't really know how else to explain it other than it was boring. The music is very repetitive and progress is very slow paced. I haven't played that much but from what I've experienced so far, I can already tell there will be a lot of backtracking involved. In my short time playing, I ran into many dead ends or doors with my only options being something along the lines of: "Come back here when you have X". The game lets you "choose" where to go, but if you don't follow the predetermined story path, you will quickly run into nothing useful and you will only have wasted time.

I'm also not a huge fan of entering a room or area and having a list of options like this:

- Check the bed
- Check the chair
- Check the chest
- Check the rug
- Check the lantern

I mean... of COURSE I will click all of them... if there was a clue or item under the rug, just cut to the chase and give me a "check entire room" option from the start and give me the clue... I don't know... Those kinds of option choices seem a bit outdated if you ask me.

Combat is a bit strange, you roll "dice" but you never see the results of the die roll so you're not exactly sure how your roll influenced the outcome.

Also, the game has a time limit. You have 40 in-game days to complete your mission. If you want to play the game without the time limit, you have no choice but to play on the easiest "story mode" (weaker enemies, easier combat, etc.). A normal or hard mode without the time limit would have been appreciated.

All in all, I just feel that for all the reading you do in this game, not much happens. I almost fell asleep while playing at one point. Maybe it's just not for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a *meh* game, but I don't think I can play it anymore since it bores me to no end. The main problem is that it feels *tedious* to play.
There are quests that you'd need to travel back and forth to complete, and the trigger to advance those quests also waste your time. Like you got an item that can fend off a mob (pebbler). You see that mob in the road. What do you do? You should be able to use it right there, yes? But nope, you need to trigger the event first, get out, and then click back in to use the item. The problem is not just it wastes time, but it makes you doubt if you misunderstood the item or read the text incorrectly. Since you couldn't use the item you have the first time, it made you think you got the wrong item or you need to find another way through. But really you just needed to click on the same location twice...
The characters feels somewhat realistic and I like that there are people you can trust, not everyone is out there to make your life miserable like in other "choice matters" games. But at the same time, I don't think I care about most of them, since they don't make a difference and the reward for helping them is not that great... at least from what I've experienced so far.
And then there's the time limit - I'm sure it's somewhat generous on normal mode, but when you have to spend about 6 days just walking back and forth between the first few locations, it just feels like you are not making progress... besides making some money.
I'm sure there will be people who found this game to be magical and fun. But for me, after two seesions I no longer any desire to play more.\t\t\t\t\t

The peninsula's secrets reveal themselves at a satisfying pace, though if you choose to play on normal mode the 40 day time limit feels fairly looming.
