\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really very unique. I have never played other games like this yet.

Pro:
- Some parts of the adventure have text parsing function, i.e. you type in the names/places/things that you want to know more, or to type in actions during some battle. Information becomes of utmost importance in the gameplay due to this, and ideally you could open a blank word doc or take pen & paper to write down whatever names/info you gather from all the conversations with NPC.
- NPC gives realistic feeling, because the way you interact with them affect their relationship with you, and affect how much ♥♥♥♥ they are willing to tell u/let you do. You can't even take quest if you have awful relationship with the NPC.
- You cannot buy things from or sell things to NPC as and when. They decide what things are useful for them to buy, and the price is quite dynamic throughout the game, depending on your relationship with NPC.
- The extensive Bestiary and Glossary gives a lot of description and context to the game world. Solid world-building from the game dev.
- You start the game with only one main quest "Exploring the Penisula", but the side quest list is so long that you just feel so good when you clear a bunch of them near the ending. A lot of side quests are like a chain reaction, you will need to find out how to solve one after another and make your main quest smooth.
- Good plot twists throughout the story, I have to say I really never foresee some of them. And you get to really decide who do you want to stand with.
- Lying/White lies are almost necessary if you want to achieve more than average. Don't be an Immanuel Kant.
- Save slots, save slots everywhere. I made the mistake of playing this like a rogue-like game at my first play-through. No, this is meant to be a save-load game, use the save-load function to achieve more.
- Very LGBT friendly I suppose. The way of lives there could be very different from our way of lives, which makes the fantasy world more interesting.

Cons:
- Really a lot of text, you must be at least comfortable with reading texts in order to complete this game. Big chunk of text appears even when there is really nothing happening at all. Most of it is to describe the environment for immersive purposes.
- Not a lot of pictures and colors, visuals are only available for map and environment. You have to imagine how each NPC looks like based on text description. Man I have a shock of my life when I realised Tulia has "shaved head" during one of my adventures with her close to the ending. I must have missed reading something during my first encounter with her.
- Sometimes the text input part can get really annoying when you do not have good imagination/brainstorming abilities. You will know what I mean when you are stuck at the dolmen.
- Fighting/battle is a bit underwhelming. Do not bother getting better weapons, it may seem like you have a lot of battle option, but the best way is to just "Run" for most of the creatures. Knowledge is your best friend, so you should refer to the bestiary. Small little tricks written there is much more useful most of the time.
- Music are really depressing at some places. Sometimes listening to the repetitive tune for too long makes me feel sick, I guess it's part of the immersive experience.
- No ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ or romantic relationship possible. This is not Witcher series if you are into that. Is it just me or Eudocia really feels nice to live with

Overall: The game is worth its price. Story is great with surprising plot twists. A lot of different choices available during conversations. Will likely support again when they release the Windy Meadow spin-off.\t\t\t\t\t
