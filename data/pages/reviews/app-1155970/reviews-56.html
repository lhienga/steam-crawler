\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short charming choose-your-own-adventure story where you roleplay as a roadwarden, which generally consists of patrolling around a circular loop of a rural peninsula to keep the roads safe, and running errands for various people from nearby settlements, along with the more specific task of finding your missing predecessor, and securing cooperative deals with the local settlements for your big city employers.

The game involves a lot of reading, which is perfectly fine as the writing is enjoyable, and functional – gathering information is important for directions to points of interest, interactions with people/creatures you’ve yet have to meet. It also has fallout/fallout 2 style epilogues to show your impact upon the world at the end, which is nice. Not all choices are impactful, quite a lot of them are flavorful, but still enjoyable as it allows you to express who you are and sculpt the world according to your tastes

Gameplay

Gameplay consists of picking one of the provided text options, and occasionally typing out something specific to do – it’s a choose-your-own-adventure story.

You pick one out of three classes early in the game, each with unique equipment, skillset, and situational options/dialogue choices
  • Fighters have an advantage in physical interactions, along with better quality melee weapon and armor, and an expensive crossbow that other classes need to save up to purchase.
  • Mages have access to magic-based options in and out of combat.
  • Scholars have access to alchemical crafting, which has various applications in and out of combat.

There’s a bit of resource management involving managing food supplies to keep your stomach full, managing your health/vitality, keeping your light armor intact, and keeping your appearances presentable. There’s also some light inventory management involving your tools and equipment.

There is an adjustable time limit when you start the game, but the middle settings provide you more than enough time to finish everything to do on the first blind playthrough, unless you are wasting a lot of time or really bad at time management.

The save/load system is very handy and conveniently implemented, with enough compulsive saves you have a lot of freedom to experiment in explorations and encounters, without worrying about ruining a run and needing to restart.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tScratched a retro itch I didn't know I had.

It's a very pleasant adventure game-♥♥♥-visual novel with a lot of replayability if you go into it blind, due to a number of different decisions (which do, in fact, matter) to make and people to please (or displease).

This isn't a crapsack world, but it's not a happy high fantasy one, either. The woods are dangerous and growing more so by the day. Tightly knit villages are doing everything they can to survive, and the Roadwarden can aid them, or try and steer them from a questionable path.

The casual attitude toward queerness and gender was really refreshing for a world that comes off as pre-medieval, with a pseudo-Christian church slowly absorbing Pagan communities.

I love the pixel art but it almost makes me wish the soundtrack were equally retro. The relaxing acoustic guitar occasionally got repetitive but was atmospheric and pleasant enough that I wouldn't call it an issue.

Highly recommend for visual novel readers and fans of very retro PC gaming.\t\t\t\t\t

This is one of the most well crafted, diverse and beautiful games i have played in years.
