\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an absolute gem. The story is compelling, the pixel art is well done, and the music will leave you humming it days later.

I'm a huge fan of narrative RPG's with multiple endings. I've played most of them that have come out in the last 30 years. From my first dive into text based games with Hitchhikers Guide to the Galaxy, to the latest triumph from Larian studios Baldurs Gate 3. This game holds it's own.

It's not full of flashy combat, it doesn't haven the most amazing graphics, and the system is basic. Yet, it is compelling. I'd never even heard the term Roadwarden before this game, and now I've incorporated a whole cadre of them into my TTRPG D&D 5e world. Such a brilliant concept, and so logical. The characters are deep, the story is complex, and the bad guys aren't at all who they seem to be at first. I'd have been proud to put something like this in front of my players.
