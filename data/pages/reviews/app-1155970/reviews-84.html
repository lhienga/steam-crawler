\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is magical and deeply involving, yet is only a small corner of a complex and engrossing world. I feel deeply in love with this world the more of it I uncovered and the deeper I was able to dive in. The introducing segment was brilliant in how it taught me how to play, teaching me to intuitively interact with it's world to survive and interact with the people within it. There being not much in the way of guides or walkthroughs, I was mostly left to my own devices and was surprised how much I could decipher without it anyway. And I'm glad I did!

It's like reading Earthsea again for the first time; catapulting me into a fantastical world, but grounded and sublimely human. I'm going to be hollering to every friend I have about this game for a while, thank you so much devs!!

And I loved my horse Furrow very very much, the real MVP of the game. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent text-based RPG, with only a few negatives and a bunch of positives.

The Good:
* Excellent storytelling, characters generally felt believable and motivations make sense for the setting.
* Deep lore without being too difficult to navigate
** Rewards careful reading of notes/journal information without being too painful if you overlook something
* Forgiving save system that gives you enough save slots to reduce tedium and trial-and-error, and allows you to save (almost?) everywhere and at any time.
* Items and skills are interesting and varied. Most items have at least one obvious use and a handful of secret/less obvious uses.
* Descriptions and tooltips are interesting and often entertaining (particularly those relating to minimal/maximal traits and statuses)

The Neutral:
* Some interactions with characters and the environment are non-obvious, primarily those relating to acquiring trust.
** On the one hand this makes sense since several characters are hostile towards you from the start, and don't give you a step-by-step for earning their trust.
** On the other, many areas offer rather straightforward descriptions of what is missing/required to proceed, which usually includes a description of exactly 'how' the missing thing may be obtained.
* Some items are prohibitively expensive (even on casual) when exploring the areas they would be most useful in.
** This makes sense since it encourages prioritizing goals instead of wandering around aimlessly, however the actions/quests needed to obtain discounts are often those that would have benefited directly from having the item in the first place.

The Annoying:
* Prioritizing certain missions can save a significant amount of resources by obtaining free services (often shelter), but it isn't made obvious that this should be a priority.
** Goodwill is linked to 'discounts', which is directly told to the player, but it isn't made clear that some characters will offer critical services for free, leading to a bunch of wasted time/money.
* Some missions require a sizable amount of backtracking and running around, which can feel annoying when the destination is the place you were just at.

Overall 9.5/10: An excellent game that makes me wish this were just part 1 of a series.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou've heard of walking simulators, now get ready for a reading simulator!

If this game was a book, I would have finished it in one sitting, or as close to one sitting as possible. As it was, I finished it in like 15 hours spread across three sessions, one of which stretched well into the wee hours of the morning. At some point I would love to replay it and focus more on a different part of the North than I was inclined to on my first playthrough.

The world is beautiful, imaginative, the characters are extremely real, and the writing is very good. It has a realistic/gritty fantasy feeling without being too depressing (well... I'm sure it's possible to make decisions that lead the world to an extremely depressing state... but it's not consistently depressing). The portrayals are thoughtful and sometimes endearing, sometimes exciting, sometimes philosophically provocative.

Really awesome tabletop RPG + novel + choose-your-own-adventure book type feeling. I don't read novels super well these days, but I used to love reading fantasy novels as a kid, and it was easy for me to focus on the narration.

Would love love love to see more content from these creators.\t\t\t\t\t
