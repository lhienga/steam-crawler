\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI might update my review after watching my partner play this game on my recommendation, so If I catch something really special that I missed my first time though I will be sure to add that.

There is so much to this game, town after town, encounter after encounter, character after character, all of it was pushing me to see what was the next stop on the road. It is difficult to start to unravel what makes me enjoy this game so much, but I want to emphasize the effect the game had on me by saying;

I never expected to enjoy a game where I was prompted to manually type in questions.

This game actually uses this feature tactfully to both paint it's setting and force my engagement with some of its cooler moments. There is an entire town where to get access to any of its services you have to directly ask for them, but its wisely put in towards the mid-point where you actually know what you would want to ask for.

I loved being laughed at for asking dumb questions to these people.

You have such a rich cast of characters, all with history and futures that they are worried about and it makes it all the harder to make choices that either prioritize your mission, their lives, or your wallet.

The game makes it engaging to worry about rent, I love it. Every morning it rains I audibly sigh because I know I had plans that I now have to reevaluate, it's wonderful.

I highly suggest you give this game a weekend of your time to act as this peninsula's roadwarden.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery old school text-based adventure, challenging but with intriguing world-building and a sense of reward from unpicking the many tangled mysteries in the story. The pixel graphics really suit the format, and have the feel of tattered, yellowed old treasure maps. I liked the soundtrack a lot too. It felt really fitting for the atmosphere the game is building, and each bit of the map had its own motifs that really conveyed the personalities of the locations.

I felt frustrated at times when I couldn't think of the right input or wasn't sure what to do next, but there's not actually much in the game that you can fail so badly that you can't progress at all. I think I got lucky and managed to achieve most things in my quest list and got an ending that didn't feel bad, though I was playing with no time limit, and for how long it all took me, I am certain I would have struggled on a higher difficulty. If you need a lot of guidance in a game, this might not be suitable. But if you like that old school style of having to really work at figuring things out for yourself, this could be a really satisfying game for you.

I felt like the vibe is kind of like a cross between Disco Elysium and the Witcher books, both of which I like a lot, so I'm saying that in a complimentary way.\t\t\t\t\t
