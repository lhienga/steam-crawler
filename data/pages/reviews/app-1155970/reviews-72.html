\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI normally don't leave reviews, but after completing the game (and maybe doing it again for another ending), I feel that the game deserves praise.

While this RPG is certainly for not everyone (it's mostly text-based, old school RPG with lots of reading), for those who would love to experience a lore heavy, low magic dark fantasy setting, there is nothing better. You arrive in a harsh, dangerous land with monsters and people who can also be monsters. You can build alliances in the different settlements and the options to do within the 40 days limit on normal is simply staggering. Your choices truly matter in the game, everything has consequences in the end.

A fantastic game and a true hidden gem of that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is a classic text-based adventure game with a very flat-lined story. You play a roadwarden, or just some awkwardly defined ranger with no particular skills. The adventure starts, as most RPGs would, by stranding you in a scenario with barely any resources and effectively no real choices to make. The only stressful note is the difficulty selection shortening the experience in amount of in-game days spent.

After roughly 15 hours of game play, I don't know why anyone would think it has RPG elements. It does not use anything role playing-wise, it's a choice adventure game. First and foremost there are several screens in this game, which I've never looked at because they don't actually make any sense. The character sheet for instance never changes beyond its boring initial state. There are no combats in this game which don't have any plausible outcome, because there are no real choices to make after drawing your axe.

A lot of the puzzles are nonsensical word games just designed to make you waste your game time. The writing feels plainly poor with a lot of quests not finishing or just becoming hunts for needles in hay stacks. A lot of the quests I've finished, I couldn't even say whether they were finished without confirming it through the journal. You get to a point, where you spend more time re-reading all the automated notes trying to figure out what the hell to do next.

This kind of game is the reason why King Quest, Space Quest and the likes died a long time ago. The best solution to solve them is just to wait for someone to randomly figure it out and just copy his solution. I was excited with this game, but it's boring and massively overrated. I do not recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
