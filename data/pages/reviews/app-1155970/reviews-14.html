\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust couldn't get into this game and fairly shocked others could. The writing is overly verbose and the writing is really all there is to this "game" it's a choose your own adventure book with very few actual choices that matter.

The setup is a fairly boring world with nothing interesting going on. It feels like a recent Disney movie in terms of an all-inclusive society that doesn't actually make any sense compared to how real societies formed in history. I didn't find them, but the game was begging me to find the Necromancer village that everyone else feared and I'm sure they would have ended up being nice but misunderstood people.

The mechanics that do exist aren't explained very well, and you'll have to do things like, figure out how to properly take a bath; I'm not kidding that is a mechanic you'll need to think about. Woah woah woah, don't bathe in that river, it has mean fish!

The text entry portions are pointless as almost no text actually works, which I would be embarrassed if I pushed that out to market since Zork was better at this forty years ago.

You'll often be given different text options to describe something that happened in the past or away from the area and I have no idea if I'm re-making the world with my choices here or just lying as two options and i wasn't paying enough attention to know that only one of my options is me telling the truth. Either way nothing I said appeared to ever really affect things.

In three hours I think I accumulated a dozen quests, none of which were actually interesting and made me think "yeah I can't wait to solve that problem." Especially when the payout for quests I did do is like 3 copper bits I might be able to buy a piece of bread with. Lady I almost got eaten by a bird lizard and a giant spider delivering your messages, you can shove those bits where the sun don't shine.

I'm honestly shocked this is current sitting at Overwhelmingly Positive reviews, when the third achievement in the game, which takes about 2-3 hours to get, is sitting at 25% completion. That means 3/4ths of players just sit down to play this once and go, "Yeah I think that was enough." But don't take the time to leave a bad review because it's more of a meh experience than a bad experience.\t\t\t\t\t
