\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game that is not for everyone. You play as a roadwarden entrusted with exploring the region, forming relationships, building alliances, solving problems, and occasionally fighting foes. It's a text-based RPG that shone brightest for me when certain relationships with people or villages blossomed after putting in the work with various quests and mundane jobs. However, it is a slow game that often lacks clear direction, even in the end game.

I completed the game once and was fairly satisfied with my ending, but I probably won't play it again anytime soon. The combination of your character's gradual progression, the amount of back-and-forth traveling it takes to complete most quests, and the hard limit (unless you're on easy mode) on the number of in-game days means that the game generally follows this path: an early game that is a slog of rarely ever having enough health, armor, money, or time; a mid game that is the most satisfying part of the game where starvation and poverty are no longer concerns and you can finally make progress with all the different quests you've accumulated; and an end game where you eventually run out of having enough in-game days left to complete most of the quests remaining, so you kinda just move around doing mundane work until the end approaches.

This game has its great moments, but it does feel like a chore at times and the pace can unexpectedly grind to a crawl. Few things are more demoralizing than talking to a minor character and being faced with a wall of text to read that is more filler than meaningful to your journey. I also had to regularly stop and check my quests, check the map, check my inventory, and read through the glossary of characters I had met to figure out what to do next. I think that could have been addressed by having more prompts about what quests certain items or characters were related to - too often I had collected a bunch of items and had forgotten who they were supposed to be for.

As for the good stuff, the characters and relationships you run across throughout the game are great and it's very satisfying to see your character go from a relatively blank slate to someone with alliances, a reputation, and maybe even a few enemies. Unfortunately, due to the time constraints this game imposes, I don't think it's possible to end up on good terms with everyone or even complete every quest unless you're on easy mode or really figure out how to min-max the game, so choose wisely when deciding who to please and piss off.

I like the game and would still recommend it, but I wanted to like it more.\t\t\t\t\t
