\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is a complex game that uses aspects of RPG in an unconventional manner: instead of dice rolls on a board, you are required to move your piece (the main character) efficiently on the board, in order to fulfill your mission in the required time.
- Lore: the Northern peninsula is inhabited by tribes that are mostly isolated from one another. Hovlavan is a powerful realm seeking to absorb this peninsula into the fold. However, it is a harsh land, with mysteries and perils that need to be dealt with. Roadwarden is filled with magical creatures and very classical fantasy themes which interact with each other through the player.
- Art: it is a simple game. The map is its main aspect and, aside from detailed "close-ups" from each location, there isn't much to see that is not coming from your imagination.
- Music: blends in with the setting through very situational compositions.
- Gameplay: for this RPG, you don't have many chances to use dice rolls; the focus is on the map and on your journey. You must secure the pathway to travel efficiently from place to place, and thus fulfill your mission as a roadwarden sent by Hovlavan.
- Story: the previous roadwarden met an unknown fate. You arrive in the peninsula to learn from the people you encounter and the clues left by your missing predecessor, in order to solve mysteries, secure the roads, and initiate trade agreements that will help Hovlavan and the guild. Along the way, difficulties, dilemmas and the harshness of the land will hinder you, giving you a chance to make your own decisions, albeit in a way that is limited by the framework of the story or its mechanics.
Roadwarden uses different aspects from many genres. It is an interactive book, with RPG elements, and its lore is convoluted, but rich. If you manage to persevere through the many difficulties you will encounter, the result of your actions, although already expected or even previously alluded to, may be ultimately satisfying in the end.
77/100\t\t\t\t\t
