\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm *really* enjoying this game. It's the first text-based game I've played, and I'm more of an RPG player. It feels significantly more grounded in reality than most other RPGs I have played, which (speaking as a reenactor of the medieval and renaissance periods) is a huge breath of fresh air.

Pros:

+Rich lore: The game takes place in an area with well-connected NPCs, quests have branching storylines, and the Roadwarden's deeds have consequences which impact others. I would classify it as "low fantasy." Humans are the only "race," but there are fantastical beasts and magic present.

+RPG elements: The Roadwarden may choose from one of 3 classes to start with, which drastically alter the way the character interacts with the world. In addition, your character's skills improve as they progress the story, and they acquire equipment which aids them in their journey.

+Gender-neutral terminology for both the main character and their horse: The Roadwarden and their trusty mount can be whatever gender and color the player wishes.

+Great music: sets the atmosphere of where your character is and the situation in which they are involved.

+Replayability: With 3 different classes to choose from, 3 different difficulties, several different dialogue trees to follow, and multiple endings, players can find themselves returning to a different experience with each playthrough.

+NPC diversity: I personally appreciate the deliberate inclusion of NPCs of many different skin colors, body types, and hair types, as well as people of different life partner preferences.

+Generous save system: Learning to use the F5/F8 quick save/quick load combo is essential to ensure your playthrough goes (mostly) the way you want it to.

Cons:

-A lot of reading: It is a text-based game, after all. It is an interactive story. This requires the player to pay special attention to the details of the dialogue and text describing the scenarios.

-Low visual effects: Again, it is a text-based game. It does include visuals with each scene, and those scenes change slightly as the character accomplishes goals, however the color scheme is less-than-vivid ("OK, apply the medieval filter") which may not appeal to some players.

-Can be vague: Some puzzles may drive the player to frustration, simply because they don't know the exact word to type to solve a particular puzzle. I had to rely on the internet to solve more puzzles than I would like.

-NPC dialogue learning curve: I appreciate the level of detail that the creators went through to give NPCs from different areas different accents and dialects, it added flavor to the lore. However, some of them require quite a bit of re-reading to ensure you know exactly "hwat" they're saying."Ne ye worry t'much though, 'tis doable."

-Some minor grammar issues: Normally not a big deal, but in a text-based game, correct spelling and grammar are critical. It's not rife with errors, but some further editing wouldn't go amiss.

-Limited replayability: Once a player knows exactly what each scenario presents, it becomes somewhat trivial to read it through each time. I found that much of my time spent replaying NPC dialogue trees and areas that I knew well was simply tedious. The fun comes from doing things you haven't done before, or finding something new because of a different style of play.

I highly recommend this game if you're looking for a low-stress, dark, historical fantasy story where choices matter. If stunning graphics and slaying hordes of demons with your greatsword "Doombringer" are more your thing, perhaps save this game for later.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter the first couple of hours, I almost refunded this game. I thought it was too slow and had TOO much text.

I'm glad I didn't.

Although initially difficult, when you start to have an impact on the world, you suddenly feel the power that the writing can have. And thankfully, in a text-based adventure, the writing is the star of the show. It is immersive, distinct, and interesting. I cared about the characters, towns, monsters, history, ideas. And now, having finished it, I want more. There is still so much untapped potential here for content that I want another game already.

The music is noteworthy and beautiful too. It's unique, amplifies emotions and is not intrusive - it fits very well.

I just wish the ending would have more impact. The main questline (Asterion) is rather lackluster (where is the climax???) and the finale is kind of pointless. Certain things are set up to be hopeless but I believed that if my character tried and did everything he could, the world would be influenced. It was not. In the end, things become what you think, despite your best efforts. Still, learning about the world, (a core-mechanic of the game), was very fun and satisifying in itself.

Also, I save-scummed! but only because I really cared what would happen and because I was running out of time before Spring (40 days is not enough).

If you have the patience for a text-based RPG, you won't regret purchasing this game. It made me think and it made me feel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen it comes to text based rpg's, Roadwarden nails it. I've read many interactive novels such as Citizen Sleeper, I was a teenage exocolonist, choice of games, and Our Life. Although they're all good and have their own unique storytelling, Roadwarden stands out among the others due to its strong story writing and interactions with the npc's. The music and the art-style are done nicely but made to let your imagination fill in the gaps of how the world/creatures look like.

If you buy this game, expect heavy reading, thoughtful planning and lots of quicksaves (if you're like me and want to make npc's happy).\t\t\t\t\t
