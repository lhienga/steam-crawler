\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"The truth is - nobody has what it takes to be the Roadwarden."

A stirring sales pitch. I'm sold body and soul. Guess nobody has what it takes to ride around empty roads and talk to random loonies. And why the? You're a member of a profession, not the Batman.

I'm a friend of well-written fantasy meaning not this game. So you go to explore this nameless peninsula. Nameless? People name anything they touch, not to mention land they live on. At first I thought the place is crawling but wouldn't you know, at every turn there's a friendly prosperous settlement of several hundred souls. Beats me why such a bustling place would need exploring. Also beats me why folks who have lived there for ages would need a complete stranger for anything.

Folks are just happy to help you. Also they're just begging to deal with the outside world (two hours ride away) but can't without the mighty Roadwarden clicking through pages of text. Call 999-ROADWARDEN.

A lady laments her lost, presumably dead daughter. The daughter is alive and well in a fishing village two hours away.

Why are you so special? Apparently you got the only horse in the world. That's your superpower. A palfrey. There - a fancy word so you know this game was made by history buffs. I'm bowled over.

Mostly you click through pages of not too original fantasy tripe. Goblins? Wouldn't be fantasy otherwise. Healing potions? Ditto. Dozen religions with sillier and sillier names? Getting there.

It's like a pixel disco elysium who would like to ask the witcher out for a date but doesn't have the guts.

Writing varies between okayish and teenage edginess. You arrive to a big bustling community. Everybody is happy to meet you. The guard is happy to show you around. Everything is cake. And the writing goes "you grasp your dagger. If this is a trap, they die first." Go get them, tiger! Don't forget your angsty Batman voice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot gonna lie it took me a bit of time to properly get into this game as I wasn't really quite used to text-heavy RPGs where there isnt really any voice acting or stuff like that. But once I properly sunk an hour or so into it, I really started enjoying it

Certain things I liked:
- Music fits really well, it feels somber and melancholic but certain locations like Creeks and Old Pagos once you fix their problems are kinda upbeat, which is a nice touch
- Actually learning about how to do certain things is really cool. For example, paying attention to certain details can help you figure out things really quickly and unravel the mysteries about the peninsula
- Story overall is good, and you can see the impact your choices make on the game affects all the locations and NPC's

Some things I think could have been better
- Some quests feel pretty annoying to do, I think they might have just bugged out for me and I wasn't able to fully complete some in the game? Specifically Elah's 'clear the eastern path' quest I had finished completely; I cleared the road, all the locations, the fallen tree, got rid of the wolves and the runner, and even all the stuff in the hidden path to the Tribe of the Green Mountain, but I still wasn't able to finish the Creeks questline. I also seem to not be able to do more for Gale Rocks either, with the dialogue option for the coopers staying greyed out despite doing everything I possibly could with them. Might have just been a bug or maybe completing certain quests or failing some prevent you entirely from finishing them.
- Some of the 'input' choices feel very weird. Basically in a select few locations to search stuff or asking NPC's about locations or people, you have to put the specific thing you're looking for. But I think some of this could have been removed entirely, atleast for places like Gale Rocks .

If I were to put a number for what id rate the game out of, I would give a solid 8/10. Good story, good music, and each of the three classes have unique variations on the same situations so you will have a nice amount of different things to do. The game also doesn't take very long to finish, maybe 10 hours or so if you're going for a more completionist run.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh... Roadwarden. This game is best enjoyed like a good book by a warm fireplace. This is an awesome visual novel with many different "butterfly effects" and endings.

The 36 hours i spent were just one play through on regular mode (40 days). At first, I was concerned about the time limit, but this game is not about being a completionist.
