the visuals are nothing basic, but that's ok. They're not the focus here.

the soundtrack has some great tracks, especially the track for Creeks, which reminds me of Hollow Knight's soundtrack for Greenpath.

the writing is obviously the highlight here, especially the world building, and the pure capacity this game has for Roleplaying choices, most of which the player has no way of knowing if or how they might affect the game later on.

the only problem I have with this game is it doesn't promote replaying much. it is easy to over-rely on saves, and because my first run was thorough and I read everything I could, I now don't feel like replaying the game since I dont want to read the same stuff, and will instead be focusing only on items, stats and decisions I had not previously chosen.

Basically, as impossible as it might be, I wish this game had more Randomness and was more repayable.

Solid Game: 8.5/10

I did encounter a small error when I asked Iason about the monks, it writes 'they were carrying wood and stone, but I don't what for' (I think the word 'know' is missing)\t\t\t\t\t
