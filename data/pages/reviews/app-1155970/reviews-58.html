\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Roadwarden" is a narrative-driven RPG set in a post-apocalyptic world where the player takes on the role of a Roadwarden, a member of an organization tasked with protecting travelers and maintaining order on the roads. Its a game that blends narrative-driven gameplay with a rich, immersive post-apocalyptic world. The game is reminiscent of classic text-driven games like "ZORK" and MUDs which are near and dear to many old gamers heart. Roadwarden successfully captures the spirit of those old-school games while also offering modern gaming features and mechanics.

The game features a branching narrative with multiple endings, where the player's choices and actions have consequences on the story and the world around them. The game also has a unique combat system that blends turn-based and real-time elements, where the player must manage their resources and make strategic decisions in order to survive.

One of the standout elements of "Roadwarden" is its immersive world-building. The game's post-apocalyptic setting is rich with lore and backstory, and the player is given the opportunity to explore and uncover the mysteries of this world through their interactions with characters and the environment. The game's art style and soundtrack also contribute to the immersive experience, creating a moody and atmospheric world that draws the player in.

Another strength of "Roadwarden" is its emphasis on choice and consequence. The game presents the player with moral dilemmas and complex situations, where there is no clear right or wrong answer. The choices the player makes not only affect the outcome of the story but also impact the world and the characters around them. This creates a sense of agency and ownership over the player's experience, making each playthrough feel unique and personalized.

The game's combat system is also noteworthy. It combines turn-based and real-time elements, creating a strategic and dynamic experience that requires the player to make quick decisions and adapt to changing circumstances. The game's resource management mechanics, such as limited ammunition and fuel, add an extra layer of challenge and tension to the combat encounters.

However, there are also some potential drawbacks to "Roadwarden". The game's narrative-driven focus may not appeal to players who prefer more action-oriented games or those who prefer a more open-ended experience. Additionally, the game's reliance on player choice and consequence may also result in some players feeling overwhelmed or frustrated by the complexity of the decision-making process.

In conclusion, "Roadwarden" is a fantastic computer game that successfully captures the spirit of the old classic text-driven games us old gamers enjoyed over 30 years ago while offering some modern gaming features and mechanics. The game's emphasis on player choice and consequence, its immersive post-apocalyptic world, and its innovative combat system make it a standout in the RPG genre. Fans of classic text-driven games and curious modern RPGs alike should definitely give "Roadwarden" a look.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRather then toss out vague praise about this game, I'd rather share one of my encounters in Roadwarden.

I returned to the riverside where I spotted a howler with an abandoned hide. I chased it off earlier and took the hide to Creeks, where I asked if any hunters recognized it. They did; it was common equipment for any hunter going on long hunts. And with three hunters still missing from the village, I concluded this riverside was a prime spot to track one of them down. As I retraced my steps to the spot I found the hide, I noticed tracks and drag marks along with the worrying signs of dried blood. A large predator might have carried off one of the missing hunters. As I followed the tracks, the painful screams of howlers filled the air. When I looked around, I spotted nearly a dozen of them. All of them sat on trees, alternating between shrieking at me and throwing anything on hand at me. I wasn't prepared to face this many howlers. So I turned back, determined to find a way through later.

A few days later, I returned with a rather smelly jar. A drifter, I escorted to safety, promised its contents would scare off any howler. I opened it and tossed out the troll urine from inside; it stank so much it could make the dead turn their noses away in disgust. Yet it worked. Half of the howlers turned tail and ran deeper into the forest, leaving the remaining braver howlers. Next, I pulled out my willow wand and focused all of my pneuma to unleash a powerful shock-wave. It tossed some of the remaining howlers off the trees, and they scattered like the other brethren. With no other tricks in my pack I put my trust in my axe and gambeson, and charged forward against the remaining howlers. The fight against them was messy and my gambeson would need serious repairs, but I eventually reached the end of the tracks and drag marks. At the end of it lay human remains. I consider ending my investigation here and returning to Creeks, only to discard that thought. The lead hunter might not accept my words alone, so I gathered any unique belongings left behind. The enclosing screams of other howlers soon surrounded me. They won't risk another fight, however their collective screams already made my head split and my stomach churn. Even though I didn't need to, I gathered the bones too. The village deserves a chance to give their respects to the dead hunter. With that finished, I quickly left the howler's territory. Even after I reached the riverside, my head and stomach still ached.

When I returned to Creeks, the lead hunter accepted the hunter's dead belongings and bones with shaking hands. He quietly handed me some money in return and hoped the other hunters were still alive. I had a feeling they weren't.\t\t\t\t\t
