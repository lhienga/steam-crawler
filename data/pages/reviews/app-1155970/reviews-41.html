\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSince this is a narrative game, essentially a 'choose your own adventure' thing, your enjoyment will rest very much on the writing. For me, it wasn't quite a love at first read moment, but definitely the concept of the Roadwarden was intriguing. That was enough to keep reading which in turn won me over entirely.

It is incredibly impressive how alive and distinct the characters, locations, and situations feel from one to another. That hinges very much on the quality of the writing and effective use of accents. The art of the game is excellent for giving you a mental framework to imagine things against, without getting too specific to keep your brain from engaging the written word.

The mechanics of the gameplay are simple, and they're enough to propel the story without encumbering it. Days and nights are meaningful, and as I wrapped up my journey I felt the same bittersweet feeling I do anytime I finish a good book. The journey was so fine I did not want it to end and as the final week came I wanted more time to properly say goodbye. But as with so many things in life, we must make the most of a situation and never truly have control over it.

It is alluring to go back and play again, or to use a guide to see what I missed. But at the same time, the first play through was so rich it is hard to imagine any version except the one I made will feel right. So many games advertise 'choices matter' and I think Roadwarden succeeds where so many fail. The joy for me was in the discovery, the limitations, my mistakes, and missed opportunities. The imperfections in my character feel real in the same way my own imperfections do, and this was a really enjoyable way to examine those thoughts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really excited to play this game, as it looks absolutely beautiful with a great color palette, but it ended up being a let down. Firstly, the pacing was very slow. Perhaps it was because very little of the game is dedicated to making choices. You mostly spend time asking NPCs questions related to world-lore, and while the worldbuilding has some neat plotlines, most of the time, I'm listening to a tavern keeper speak fondly of the animal they took along as emergency rations, or making up some irrelevant detail about my character's backstory that doesn't affect anything. I have mixed feelings about the writing. A lot of sections have some very crisp descriptions, and conversations have a natural language to them, but there's just not enough beauty in the writing and the characters to hold my interest. If I'm going to read so much, I'd prefer to read other content.

Overall, it's more of a book and less of a game, and the railroadedness of it combined with the painstaking detail to the world made it tedious for me to sit down and play. Wish it had more game in its blood.\t\t\t\t\t
