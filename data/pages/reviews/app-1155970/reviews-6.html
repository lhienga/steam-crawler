\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is a Choose Your Own Adventure novel for grown-ups, and it’s very good if you’re into reading a lot.

You get a tightly constructed narrative that digs into tribalism, colonialism, and rural/urban tensions. Now, the narrative has a time constraint which seems to have been somewhat controversial among some players. Personally, I liked the time constraint - it kept the story from dragging, and I enjoyed my travels in the peninsula all the more for knowing that I couldn’t possibly turn over every stone in the time allotted. It also gives Roadwarden some replay value.

The isometric pixel art is serviceable, though the palette wears on you after a while. The music is equally serviceable, paired with a quality natural soundscape that greatly enhances immersion. The game’s design allows for quite a few different approaches - mercantile, diplomatic, evasive, combative, pious, and even despairing - which is fun, though your attitude has only the most minor of impacts.

In the end, though, this is a text-based game, and the writing is what matters. Here is where Roadwarden shines. It keeps things focused on people, their problems, and the consequences of their collisions. It’s full of grounded, human stories - expertly woven into the fabric of a unique and well-rounded fantasy setting. Dialogue is punchy and the narration walks the line between dense and descriptive with ease.

Roadwarden’s press release states that the stories, designs, art direction and worldbuilding are all the work of one individual called Aureus. If that’s true, that’s singularly impressive creativity. Roadwarden is clearly a labour of passion, and is one of the finer games of this stripe that I’ve played.

I heartily recommend Roadwarden to anyone who enjoys a good book.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is an amazing CYOA-type of game. You're tasked with exploring a largely unknown peninsula, full of beasts and locals who are distrustful of you. However, you can freely choose which personal goal to pursue and how to approach all the challenges awaiting you. Do you prefer to be a heroic fighter, putting others' needs before yours and working towards making the peninsula a better place for everyone? Or rather a scumbag mage who cares only about coin and works toward undermining the independence of people on this piece of land? All of this is possible and the game gives you a rich set of choices and actions for each of the situations that you find yourselves in. The choices have heavy consequences. There many unique items scattered across the land differ in their utility and can be a lifesaver in some cases.

Just be aware that the game is basically 100% text-based and the only graphics are for things like the maps and items. Still, if you like RPGs or books Roadwarden is a great game to play, at least once.\t\t\t\t\t
