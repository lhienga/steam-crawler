Good attempt to mimic an RPG, spoiled by netflix editors

Game is a text based attempt to make an RPG.
While the interface and a world backstory is quite good, the other parts are not so much.
I'd like to recommend it but every part of the game is spoiled in some way. E.G.:
- The world setting is quite interesting, but it is filled out with characters that can not exist there at all. They're probably made just to meet the current US agenda. Which it terrible for RPG in general and especially for a text-based one.
- Player is provided with two ways of progressing by collecting information, but those conflict with each other and both has a poor implementation. And that is core mechanics of the game.
- There are a lot of choices, but only few of them has consequences.

Other than that the core and an idea of the game was really good.
It has a good visuals, different ambient sounds for locations, a nice simplified character representation and other small things. That is a shame something with such potential was spoiled that hard mostly by netflix editors.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great text-based RPG that rewards you for exploring thoroughly, thinking critically, and writing down notes. Playing it feels like reading a Forgotten Realms fantasy novel, except you are the one deciding how the story will unfold, because you control the protagonist.

In terms of gameplay it's similar to text-based RPGs and point-and-click adventure games from the 1980s and 1990s, but with better visuals. In terms of content it kinda reminds me of Planescape Torment crossed with Fallout 1 -- there's more conversation than combat, the game has a time limit, and when the game is over you get to see what happens to each of the settlements you discovered based on your actions throughout the game.

I left the game running a few times so my hours on record are inflated but there's easily 30+ hours of content in this game if you take the time to resolve every quest and explore every location. I loved the game so much I binged it over 4 days, and would definitely recommend it to others.

However one consideration I will call out is that less than 13% of players have the achievement for finishing the game -- presumably because many don't have the patience to play/read through so many hours of text -- however if you enjoy reading and you've made it this far in my review I'm confident you'll love Roadwarden. For only $11, why not?\t\t\t\t\t

Single-Player Tabletop at its Finest


Full video review on 2 Headed Hero!

Roadwarden feels like a cozy tabletop RPG session with one player (you!) and one very ambitious dungeon master. Placing players in the dusty boots of a Roadwarden, which is basically a hybrid park ranger/highway patrolman, the game lavishes you with rich descriptions of a living, dangerous world where survival is paramount and a single misstep can mean doom. Every choice feels weighty and important, and your decisions also go far in shaping the world in many unexpected ways.

This game also provides one final stroke of brilliance by allowing your character to choose their motivations early on. With the railroading nature of most CRPG plots, this simple addition seems so obvious in hindsight but is usually entirely overlooked.

If you're missing a tabletop group in your life, Roadwarden goes far in filling that gap. Highly recommended!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs I got older due to lack of time I got harsher on which games I pick and play especialy story rich ones. If an RPG doesn't try hard enough for their writing I drop them immediately these days. To my surprise Roadwarden had the best writing in an RPG I've encountered since Disco Elysium (which is the best RPG game ever made in my opinion). And the music was just as good as the story. They reflected the atmoshpere so well I ended up hanging on some areas more than I intended to just to listen the music more. Even now I just want to hang around with the old druid dipping my feet into that stream and listen to the music playing on that area.

Even though they are very different on scale, setting, gameplay wise Roadwarden did everything It did so well I would put It on the same pedestal with Disco Elysium.

In summary this was a text-based RPG that was like the "choose your own story" books back in my childhood which I loved to explore. I've been able to finish the story once but I think I might revisit the game a few more times to see how It would change with different approaches. I'm glad I encountered this gem through a curator I follow.\t\t\t\t\t
