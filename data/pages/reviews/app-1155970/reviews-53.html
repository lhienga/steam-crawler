\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat immersive experience! Roadwarden successfully brings all the great things from choose-your-own-adventure books/games of the past with modern design sensibilities.

Highlights:
- The art, music, and writing are all equally beautiful.
- Role playing choices felt meaningful.
- The fantasy world is believable and never feels like a power fantasy.
- I never felt like the game was excessively hard/harsh, but also never easy. Again, not a power fantasy.
- Despite the previous two points, the ending rewards (and punishes) you for your choices and actions throughout your playthrough.
- Plenty of replayability.

All in all, a must play for lovers of RPGs and narrative games with choices. It's refreshing to have a game where not being able to successfully complete all quests does not feel like you are missing out and is instead part of the expected experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden is a fantastic text-based RPG. In an untamed land, you, the Roadwarden, are tasked to explore the wilderness, find friends and foes, solve mysteries and common problems, follow your own personal goal (whichever you may choose) and find out as much as you can about the land in 40 days.

The soundtrack is mellow and fits the tone of the game perfectly, as does the pixel art. There is a *lot* of reading - obviously - but the writing style is very approachable and concise, yet provides a lot of depth. I also found the game's difficulty to be perfectly balanced. You do have to take care of several attributes, like nourishment or appearance, but none of them - aside from vitality - directly lead to death when depleted, but change the outcome of situations. Traveling through the world takes time, so you find yourself planning your route depending on your tasks. In no time you will have a mental map of places to sleep, or to get food, or to bathe, which is incredibly immersive.

The game autosaves quite often and has a quick save, so you can take different approaches to see different outcomes. I was surprised by the depth of some of the choices, many of which don't take effect until much later. You can form alliances, friendships or rivalries through your actions and they all lead down different paths. The replay value here is insane if you choose to do so.

This was a very fun journey and I am happy I went on it. If you like visual novels or choose-your-own-adventure type games, Roadwarden is one to check out for sure and highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
