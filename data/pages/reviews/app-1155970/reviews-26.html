\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRoadwarden paints a compelling, vivid world, and its characters hint at depths that, as a mere traveler, you know you'll never fully understand. It's definitely the best cRPG I've experienced so far (maybe a few Choice of Games games come close to being this memorable), and it's mostly text-based.

I could criticize it for a few ways it can be a little obscure (for example, the mystery of the dolmen was mildly annoying, for me), but mostly the adventure treats you reasonably fairly and there are usually multiple solutions to problems. It also mostly seems not to judge your ethical decisions other than in how characters respond to them.

It does a great job balancing your relative powerlessness and aloneness with the substantial impact you can make. The sheer number of weird beasts and their descriptions and habits is gobsmacking, and often the advice from the experienced huntress is "run away" or "if you encounter them, you're at their mercy." Also, I'm not sure if I've played another character before who can't read - that was interesting.

Overall, it's really satisfying. It gives you some goals, but also asks for you to make your own goals. Highly recommend to anyone who doesn't mind reading carefully, especially to people who are interested in being game masters. The writing isn't too flowery and isn't too sparse - it services the story very effectively alongside the simple (yet pleasing to look at) art and the excellent soundtrack.\t\t\t\t\t
