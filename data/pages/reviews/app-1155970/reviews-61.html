\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked this up on sale, and didn't really know what to expect from it - the sort of choose your own adventure vibe looked like a fun change of pace from shooters and action rpgs.
I was pleasantly surprised!
The premise is you're basically a scout sent to see what the hecks going on in this mysterious peninsula - a place overrun with monsters and magic and danger. I don't want to give any of the fun mystery elements away, but I found myself engrossed in the world, and my choices on who my character was really seemed to matter in my ability to make decisions. I tried playing as a mage and a druid/nature guy, I'm definitely gonna go back and try another backstory and class, maybe just play on the unlimited-time mode so that I could try and unravel more of the history of the place!
I did all I could within my 40 days on the peninsula, yet when I had to leave I still had so many mysteries and problems to solve - one man would be truly hard pressed to fix the entire place by himself! The endings were more or less small blurbs about the future of each of the tribes, but this really feels more like one of those 'the journey is the reward' games.
The writing is great, the world setting is filled with danger and intrigue, and I thoroughly enjoyed it.

Pick it up!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on a whim and was pleasantly surprised, couldn't help but binge through it in 10 hours straight - very much like a good book you just can't put down and end up reading way past your bedtime.
Great narrative, great soundtrack, good gameplay flow.
Very forgiving, maybe a little too much.

Despite the beautiful pixel art illustrations, it indeed plays more like an interactive book, so be prepared for lots of reading and learning about the world.
There's a journal with a quest log and a text log to aid with remembering info, however if you allow yourself to be immersed into the world you'll hardly ever need them.

Lastly, my biggest gripe with it is that the writing deteriorated towards the ending. Found myself skimming a lot because the details rapidly became less and less consequential.\t\t\t\t\t
