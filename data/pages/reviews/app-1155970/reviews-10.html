\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm tempted to categorise Roadwarden as one of my favourite... books? Texts? Literary experiences? It did that thing that all art aspires to do, it challenged me. It challenged my sensibilities and emotions, made me feel at turns desperate, confident, outmatched, prepared, ashamed, proud, and loved. It dropped me into a world in which I was hopelessly out of my depth and said "go on, figure it out". In this regard it is a uniquely human experience among RPGs - there is no power fantasy here, just your wits and useful yet limited skills. This is a game that rewards paying attention to its world and characters, solid preparation, and care. The writing is excellent - the world and characters of Roadwarden are among the most grounded and immersive I've encountered. If you like text-based adventures (Citizen Sleeper or the Sorcery! series) or just regular RPGs, please give this a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend this game! It has entered my personal "hall of fame" for videogames. I've only played one game similar in style, Citizen Sleeper, and apart from the obvious genre difference (CS is "Sci-Fi" Roadwarden is "Fantasy") I believe Roadwarden has a great deal more RPG elements.

Things that I love:
The writing
The world
The characters!
The choices
The action!
OH THE MUSIC IS WONDERFUL TOO!

This feels like an adventure when you play it. I'd also like to mention the agency your character has is far greater then what may be implied from the start. The impact you make on the Peninsula you warden, is grand.

10/10, have a go at this great game which will have you weighing up how you'll spend your limited time doing what you got to do. (There is a mode to play it without a time limit, I recommend the "standard" difficulty first though, it's much more fun that way).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe smallest choices have the strangest impacts.

Roadwarden is an incredible visual novel. I just can't say more than that, because that's the soul of it. Anything else would be inadequate. It takes an interesting look at community, tribalism, colonialism, the effects of economic domination and systems of reliance.... but mostly it's about being a roadwarden. And that's mostly about not getting killed and eaten! Among other things! Your personal agency is literally unmatched.

Your choices are more far reaching than you think, and little action is wasted for an inquisitive mind. If you like puzzles, politics, earning peoples' trust, uncovering secrets, and weighing your safety against exploring just ONE MORE dark tile on the map... you'll like this game. I LOVE this game.

A couple of minor annoyances/ quirks:

The 'save' menu is separate from the quicksave/quickloads visible from your main UI screen. That's a function of it being made in ren'py, so it's not exactly unexpected. It's just easy to miss during the course of regular play. If you want to take advantage of multiple save slots for exploring branching narratives (and you can save at literally ANY point of dialogue), hit escape and go to save. I would recommend NOT save scumming as much as possible for your first playthrough. The writing is hilarious, poignant, and unexpected. Just trust it and see where your actions take you. But yeah. Save files. They exist.

Second, the muted colors can be a bit hard on the eyes. I wish this game had some alternative color schemes for reading, since there is a LOT of it, but it's beautiful and matches the artwork, giving everything a very "legends and myths" type feeling. Something about those sepia tones just make you think of a story, told so many times over with the details changing..

There are two font options, one a pixel font and the other a serif, though I found both a little hard to read at some points. You can get a good look at your options from the game trailer though, so you won't be surprised.

Finally, you can make notes!!! IN GAME!!! And you will use it!! Or else!!! Type- er- write! Your thoughts into your journal for easy reference later, when you're trying to remember that person who so-and-so told you about... er.. at that place.. uhh...
Listen. Just trust me. You're gonna need it.

10/10 Penicillin shmenicillin. We don't need that, we've got healing potions!\t\t\t\t\t
