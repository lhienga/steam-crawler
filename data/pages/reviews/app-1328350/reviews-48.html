-For an indie game, it's quite the package for the price. Levels are decently long.

-Banger soundtrack

Overall, must-play boomer shooter that stands with the likes of DUSK and Ion Fury. Amazing what a small team could put together.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10/10 game. It's extremely fast paced, lots of guns, lots of ammo, lots of interesting enemies (which is a massive point in and of itself), and fun maps.

There's a dodge, a fast paced slide, a grapple, a double jump, some wall slides, teleports, options for wall grabs, the list goes on. The combat is fast paced, guns have alternate fires, and I found myself using almost every gun in most situations. The enemy designs are all well done and it's nice to see some really new kinds of enemies that aren't just the stock standard. Story is fine, too. Music is phenomenal and map design is fantastic.

I played on No Pain No Gain.

I think there's only three points I'd dock it for:
1. There's just too much money. I'd bought every upgrade and gun type way before the end of the game.
2. There's too much ammo. I was frequently maxed out on just about everything.
3. The difficulty for the first 2/3 of the game is very easy. Like, way too easy. Then it ramps up really fast and gets really hard. The final stretch of the game was super hard in places (in generally a good way!). I just wish the whole game had been more difficult. Not sure I'm a fan of the final two bosses either as I was mostly just getting killed from one-shots.

I still whole heavily recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTurbo Overkill is a really fun game. A long game, but a fun one none the less.

As far as I know, this game is made up of three episodes and each one has eight sectors to play through. Sectors are huge and can take a while to finish, especially if you're a completionist. Fortunately for us, we play as Johnny Turbo, a heavily augmented "street sweeper" who can get from point a to point b faster than you can in other games can with noclip turned on.

Johnny's mobility is nuts. He can triple jump, double dash, rocket/shotgun jump, grappling hook, and chainsaw-slide his way through a sector, killing anything with a pulse while discovering secrets. Just remember there are at least 24 sectors to get through. And if you've gone through all that and finally beaten the game but find yourself still wanting more things to eviscerate with your chainsaw augmented limbs, you're in for a treat. In addition to its lengthy campaign, Turbo Overkill comes with secret levels to unlock and community made custom levels to try out. There's even a free demo available, which is itself longer than other games are in their entirety. So yeah, you'll definitely get your money's worth.

While I might think the game can run a bit overlong, resulting in repetitive gameplay (which is easily remedied by playing in small doses), I'm still giving Turbo Overkill a solid recommendation. Why? Because far be it from me to ♥♥♥♥ on a dev for putting too much content in their game at a single price, no DLC needed.\t\t\t\t\t

Movement is fast and based on constantly bounding around or sliding on your chainsaw-leg, the sole difference being no ground pound. The weapons are at least unique, but mowing down enemies as the most badass cyborg known to mankind with no other gameplay reason than "theyr'e shooting at me, I wanna live."

Level design kicks ass, but it also kicks my ass with sometimes unclear approaches. Enemies feel one-note, except for the big bruisers, who I can promise I remember and would like to forget. The game feels, looks, sounds, and plays fantastic...but since my friend made this comparison, I haven't been able to reject the notion.

But it's just a funny joke based on the development/release cycles, concepts, over the top theming, gameplay, title similarity...okay, maybe he has a point.

The story is at least a fun, 80s-like fanatical romp. Remember Far Cry 3: Blood Dragon? Imagine that but more ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ insane.

Ultimately, this game, despite being different enough, does feel like Ultrakill at the end of the day. And if yoou ask me, that's okay. Good, even. I like Ultrakill, and I like this. And honestly, I'm just happy to have these coexist.

Besides, last time I checked, you couldn't lock on to targets, fire micro-missiles, slow down time, teleport inside of an opponent, use a chainsaw as a leg, and use an honest-to-god orbital laser as a weapon in Ultrakill.

Good hunting, Sir!\t\t\t\t\t
