\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts Doom eternal on roller skates. Its a fun blast that just throws everything at you and keeps escalating straight off a cliff. The game copies a lot from other media but if you stop to think for a while the styles it borrows can feel a little stitched together. Because of the copying of game mechanics too, some of them transfer the same flaws as the original games or they just feel redundant here.

The primary and secondary fire modes don't always complement each other and some weapons and secondary fire modes fill up the same purposes, just like in doom eternal. The dash is there but the chainsaw slide makes it feel redundant and it could have been a fine thing to base the movement on its own. Wall running is there just like in titan fall but it doesn't come in to play during the combat unlike in titan fall. The grapple hook doesn't feel that intuitive with the speed of this game.

During the heat of the combat I started forgetting some of the abilities like slow-mo and chainsaw arms because there is just so much to use but not enough reason for it always. Because the game has less of a focus on resource management I could find my self in a longer battle than I expected and run out of ammo with little to no ways of making it back.

The game is most fun at the medium difficulties as a focus requiring breeze because on the higher difficulties the enemies, even the smaller mooks instakill you. This creates frustrating repetitions and the loose game mechanics don't facilitate "dancing on the razors blade" like playing.

The story is chaotic and starts to veer VEEERY heavily in to Doom eternal DLC territory with techno babble, Ai gods -and sidekicks. The best parts of the story are the more tragic moments of seeing Johnny becoming something that he doesn't recognize and how his past is fading away from him. The question if that is even a bad thing is very interesting to me. Sam carries all of the exposition and I see no reason why johnny couldn't have a voice or toughs of his own akin to Blazkowicz from Wolfenstein. Johnny isn't an empty icon like Mickey mouse nor is he an established hero like Doom guy. The music goes above and beyond. (I liked the game, I just have critiques of it.)\t\t\t\t\t
