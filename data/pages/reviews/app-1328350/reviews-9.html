\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first few hours had me on the fence, but then I started having a lot of fun afterall.

There was something graphics-related that looked a bit scratchy or off to me. I lack the technical knowledge to specify what it is exactly, but there's no issues with motion blur or anything like that.

The game has many 'arena shooter' parts which is not my favoured style for FPS. On top of that, the movement is very fast with a bit of parkour and it is just a very untypical FPS experience. The game reminds me of Doom Eternal quite a bit actually.

Turbo Overkill is one of the most impressive FPS's, especially given the size of the dev team.

The game mixes in more features from other FPS's than you would normally get. From wall jumping, grappling, vehicles, bullet time, sliding, and more. Most of these features are implemented well. One thing however, after getting the grapple, you get an additional crosshair on enemies that you can grapple, and I found that this made it more difficult to spot my actual shooting crosshair.

There is a lot of power fantasy to the experience, your guns feel OP, but at the same time, enemies are very threatening and the game is no walk in the park.

While its not a personal favourite, I really enjoyed Turbo Overkill in the end. I imagine very few people that play don't at least come away impressed on some level.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMajor Pros:
1. Nails the feeling of becoming more powerful over time better than most other games I've played, especially other boomer shooters. Actual RPGs need to take cues from this game on how to make the player start small and feel like a god by the end.
2. Lots of freedom to define your playstyle since the game has strong customization that lets you favor specific parts of your kit and every weapon has some way to deal extreme damage.
3. This game is a must-play simply to see just how over-the-top and insane the setpieces get. Turbo Overkill is the definition of a game that's as cool in reality as it is in your head.
4. Most of all, it's just a super fun game to play. Clearing out varied enemy encounters in varied environments is simply some of the most fun I've ever had in a video game, and it would be enough to make me recommend the game even if it didn't have great aesthetic and storytelling qualities.

Pros:
1. Great music, visuals, weapons, voice acting
2. Extending off that last bit, it's really cool to see great cutscenes in a game like this. They follow a similar formula to Doom 2016/Eternal and have a similar sort of appeal with giving the player's avatar a lot of personality without saying a word. The very definition of doing more with less.
3. Turning the slide into a melee attack is very clever, I like it even though I kinda miss not having a regular melee weapon.

Cons:
1. Enemy readability isn't great. You look at a mess of metal and body parts and need to figure out in half a second whether you're looking at an enemy you're supposed to close the distance on or stay away from. The biggest issue is that the pump shotgun's alt-fire is the strongest attack you have against half the enemies and is useless against the other half. Figuring out which is which in the heat of the moment can be hard and obnoxious.
2. Especially near the end of the game, the levels take FOREVER. Like over 30 minutes per level. They could have easily been 15-20 with just a bit of fat-trimming. I'll admit that this makes the experience seem grandiose and epic with how much endurance each level requires, but it still can test your patience.
3. Bosses are pretty lame. Not horribly lame, but not great either. Mainly it's just that there's not a ton of middle-ground between the bosses being ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and way too easy. And no matter which one you're up against, they're going to be way too bullet-spongy.

Major Cons:
1. If retro shooters want to bring back having 10 weapons while also introducing a bunch of character abilities that need their own keys, they need to step up their game with making the controls accessible and sensible. Pressing 7 to switch to a weapon in the middle of a fight is ridiculous, and the player should not be responsible for finding a sensible bind for it. Combine this with the weapon wheel being weirdly laggy and delayed, taking like half a second to appear after you start holding Q, and the game's combat ends up a lot less accessible than it should be. In particular I have a bone to pick with binding micro-missiles, an ability you need to hold down to use, to Mouse3, a button that's very difficult to hold down.
2. So if the controls are the biggest issue with the game, why don't I just rebind them? Well that brings us to the second biggest issue with the game: Spoilers in the menu. The primary appeal of Turbo Overkill is this unparalleled feeling of anticipating what insane things will happen next in the story and gameplay. This part of the experience would be massively damaged if you knew exactly which enemies and weapons would be around the next corner. Unfortunately, the resources the player has available spoil exactly that.
Rebinding your weapon and ability keys in the menu requires you to tactically not look at most of the options unless you want to be spoiled on the insane superweapon you get in Act 3 while you're rebinding your micro-missiles halfway through Act 1. The worst by far is the beastiary. I hope you don't think to follow the tooltips telling you to learn more about the enemies in the beastiary, because for some reason they have all the enemies from Acts 2 and 3 while you're still in the middle of Act 1.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best single player shooter since Doom Eternal (Even though there's barely been any since). I've played a lot of boomer shooters in the last few years and this is the best one since Dusk in my opinion. This game nails the movement and shooting. The movement is very quick with the double jump and double dash. The arsenal of weapons feel great to shoot and i looked forward to upgrading them as the game went on. I like the art style and cyberpunk aesthetic the game has and it has a cool soundtrack accompanying the gameplay. The levels themselves are pretty good overall, there were some that were outstanding like the end of ACT 1 and i like how big the scale is in a lot of them. As for negatives i thought the story was pretty throwaway and the game overstayed it's welcome around ACT 3 because there wasn't many new ideas being thrown in to keep the engagement high. Overall i highly recommend to any fan of shooters.
7/10 (Good)\t\t\t\t\t
