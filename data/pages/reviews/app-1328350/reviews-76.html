\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just directly started on the hardest difficulty, didn't even try the easier ones - so these comments are specifically about the hardest difficulty.

You can die 0.2 seconds after making a minor mistake, but after your death, you pretty much always know what you did wrong. I practically always knew what I should change to avoid doing the same mistake on the next round. I like this.

  • In most cases, you die because you chose a movement route that went directly towards a hard-hitting enemy instead of strafing around it.
  • Sometimes you die because you strafed into a wall that you forgot about, stopping your movement for only a fraction of a second - but just enough for the bullets to hit and kill you.
  • Sometimes you die because you fall into the void.
  • Sometimes you die because an enemy (or 10 of them) just spawned on top of you and you had no idea they will spawn there. This is the only part that can feel like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ when it happens, but it's something that you will instantly learn for the next try.

The checkpoints are common enough and respawning is fast enough, that sometimes getting through a fight can feel similar to Super Meat Boy - reset after reset, until you figure out a working route. It can arguably feel a bit frustrating and "trial & error" at times, but on each death I always feel like I learned something new about the particular fight.

The amount of "trial & error" also goes down as you keep progressing through the game, as you become more familiar with the level design / enemy placement philosophy as a whole.

---

A comment about the weapon design: many weapons have primary fire which feels like the same machine gun with different bullets. All weapons have secondary fire modes, which are fun and quite unique - but I feel like the primary shots could've also been a bit more different from each other.

Basically if I make an active choice to pick a certain weapon for the current fight, it's because I think their secondary mode fits well for the particular enemies - not because of the primary fire. I guess it's fine this way too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI barely understand why people keep calling this a "retro shooter". Does this look like Quake or Duke Nukem to anyone? If you travelled back to 1996 and showed Turbo Overkill to someone who had just got done slaying Shubb-Niggurath, they'd either keel over dead from shock or think it was a greater technical marvel than the time machine you just pulled up in. Understand I'm not criticising Quake or Duke Nukem, I just want to make the point that this game's no throwback; it's bleeding edge stuff.

The game's got loads of, for want of a better word, gimmicky mechanics. But the incredible thing about them is that they're all useful. Grappling on rockets, grabbing enemy missiles with the plasma rifle alt-fire, telefragging with the telefragger, the chainsaw leg, of course, are all usually one possible answer to any problem. Moreover it's all incredible fun that I only stop playing because it's so intense.

I could wax lyrical about this game for much longer than I have already, but I'm quite tired and I can't recall ever reading a Steam review that was over three paragraphs long myself, so I'd be hypocrite to ask others to do so. Wholehearted recommendation for Turbo Overkill from me, it's a wonderful game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is THE most fun shooter I have ever played!
The fast action is god-tier and litteraly all guns feel powerful and have their place and when the enemies line up nicely for you, just slide through them and slice them up with your chainsaw-leg. Even the pistols you start with are good and towards the end become one of the most powerful weapons with temporary massive damage that instagibs the fodder and 2-3 shot kills most other enemies. The second to second action is litteraly the most fun i've had in a shooter.

I thought before i bought and played it that this game left alot to be desired in terms of graphics, but let me tell you its as pretty as it needs to be. The character modles are are low detailed but the only time you will notice that are in some short cutscenes. Combat moves so fast you wont be able to tell enemies are low poly, and the guns you hold i think look quite nice.
The soundtrack is also so damn good and fits perfectly with the grity cyberpunk theme of the game.

I do feel like i have to give one negative criticism of the game. Several times and definitley more than would have liked I had no idea of how to get to the next area. It felt like they intentionally hid the the intended path forward in counterintuitive ways. Maybe im retarded but i dont think i should have to look up a walkthrough just so i can get from point-A to Point-B.\t\t\t\t\t
