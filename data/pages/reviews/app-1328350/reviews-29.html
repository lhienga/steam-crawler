\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best shooters of 2023, in a year already filled with amazing games.
An over-the-top comic-book-like gorefest made by FPS-fans for FPS-fangs.

Pros:
+ Extremely fast-paced combat in the vein of DOOM 2016
+ Very fluid and fun movement mechanics
+ Awesome cyberpunk visual style
+ Good variety in gameplay with some vehicle sections
+ Head-banging soundtrack
+ Great over-the-top voice-acting with voice-actors like Jon St. John (Duke Nukem), Gianni Matagranno (Gabriel from Ultrakill), Tay Zonday (Chocolate Rain) and Patricia Summersett (Zelda from Breath of the Wild and Tears of the Kingdom).
+ A lot of bonus levels and features
+ You get to kill Gianni Matragrano multiple times while he trashtalks you

Cons:
- The small flying bug-robots are annoying
- The final levels are close to dragging, but fortunately end just before they become too long\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Turbo Overkill has retro-inspired aesthetics, I'd compare it most directly to Doom Eternal in terms of gameplay. The ballet of both combat and movement abilities, switching between different weapons, using melee takedowns to recover health, armor, and ammo, and using environmental interactivity to get around the combat arenas works beautifully. Unlike Doom Eternal, fights aren't as prescriptive and there's plenty of room to approach them in your own way. The graphics are beautiful and sound design and music are top notch, the game runs well and I encountered very few bugs and glitches in my platythrough.

If there's one criticism I would have it's that like it's id inspiration, some levels overstay their welcome a bit. Overall though, this is a strong recommendation and top contender for my game of the year.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is almost incredible with some major flaws I cannot ignore.

the first critical flaw is motion sickness issues, I'm not one to feel like throwing up when playing games, and I've never been diagnosed by a doctor for motion sickness, the incredible speed of the game started to get to me a tiny bit when I first picked it up, but its negligible, but, and spoilers ahead, however, if you want to know my general review, at least one level has terrible flashing lights, the hardest difficulty is unfair (skill issue on my part prolly) and some other small things. if you ever get this game, and wanna start on a hard difficulty, go to the second hardest.

the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ car level, is impossible to play, not the first level where you're driving the car, but the one after that, the flashing lights, and the level designed around jumping on cars made the already difficult game, into something I can't beat, which was a bummer since I was enjoying the game up to that point. I don't know if its the first few rooms, or if its the entire level, but its awful.

now, no good criticism comes without a solution, and the way I would solve it is by getting rid of the constant flashing lights on the textures, I checked for a bit in the settings for a way to turn off the texture flashing, but I failed to find anything, I'd also reduce some other motion in the levels (make cars flying by a little rarer) if that, and thats it.

I have other issues as well, but its mostly for the hardest difficulty, which I played on for my experience of the game, you will get insta killed at 100 heath by almost anything, even some of the junk enemies, but health is also stupid rare, and it makes a gameplay style of just don't get hit, this promotes playing like a ♥♥♥♥♥, until the chainsaw power ups later, which barely help execpt for staying up with your health, rather than surviving most harder fights.

solutions I would make to make the harder difficulties more fun will be

A: make micro missiles refuel your armor with every enemy hit/killed (doesn't stack so you can't hit and kill and get shields twice), this makes using your missiles more wisely exciting, as its directly tied to not only blasting enemies, but your survival

B: make the chainsaw leg heal on hit rather than kill, and buff its healing. (wanna note, if this is what it already does, then mb, I only really noticed the healing when killing enemies, I couldn't get back in to check, and theres no wiki to check ether) I think heal on damage, or at least heal on interaction (with a cool down on each enemy) would make it more fun to get active in fights, making it heal on hit then cool down would stop any chance of it being spammed.

C: nerf junk enemy pls :pleading:

the reason why I even say this is because of the shot gunners early game and the grenaders later on (episode 1) and this is because, if your at 400 health, the max as far as I know, you will still instantly die, now, throughout the game, I didn't really need to run and hide, but the grenaders surely made me do it, and it was more boring than anything to have to fight them.



If none of this is a issue for you, buy the game INSTANTLY, this deserves all the praise it gets, but its worth noting that there is lots of motion, and flashing lights, and that its not a game where the hardest difficulty feels like the 'intended' experience, like I would argue with Ultrakill.

TLDR: Motion sickness issues from someone who usually doesn't experience motion sickness. Hardest difficulty not fun (play it second if you do play the game) buy it if you can stand both tons of motion, and flashing lights, at the same time.\t\t\t\t\t
