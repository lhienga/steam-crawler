Shadow the Hedgehog lays awake at night wishing he was Johnny Turbo

I've never played a game that has weaponized movement as well as this game and it is absolutely phenomenal to play! Once all of the movement upgrades have been unlocked gravity is more of a suggestion as you weave around the map like an angry hornet bisecting enemies with your chainsaw leg.

The weapon selection is great for an old school shooter, and there are some fun and unique options thrown in, like a telefragging sniper rifle or an orbital laser(that works everywhere). All of them served various niches and felt well designed and satisfying to use, although I'm not exaggerating when I say I killed at least 80% of enemies in the game(hardest difficulty) with the cheggs. Not only is sliding around cutting people in half a blast, but with the right upgrades it's not only viable, but one of the best weapons in the game! Also, you can grapple onto your own rockets!

Level design was nicely done, and I appreciated all the platforming sections, it would've been a tragedy to have such beautiful movement mechanics and not get to flex them. Speaking of which I would 100% play a version of this that was just platforming too, love me some first person platforming! There were a good variety of areas and the developers did a good job of exploring some fun locations while still fitting the cyberpunk theme. The freeway/train fight, fighting through a debris field in orbit trying to escape the planet, and Vector-4 where all some immediate standouts as far as cool locations go.

Few games can make going this fast this fun! I would strongly recommend this one if you have a need for speed, or just want a quality retro shooter.\t\t\t\t\t

Turbo Overkill: A Cybernetic Symphony of Chaos and Fun

Introduction
Ladies and gents, gather 'round for a tale of cybernetic limbs, apocalyptic AI, and a chainsaw leg – yes, you read that right. Turbo Overkill, developed by the legendary Apogee, is a throwback FPS set in a world where a rogue AI named Syn has wreaked havoc, and it's up to Johnny Turbo, our heavily modified mercenary protagonist, to save the day in the most over-the-top way possible.

Level Design: A Beautifully Grotesque Playground
Epic doesn't even begin to cover it. Turbo Overkill's levels are sprawling masterpieces, crafted with verticality and laden with secrets that beg to be discovered. The arenas demand adaptability, offering a delightful blend of beauty and grotesqueness that keeps you on your toes and your eyes wide open.

Gameplay: A High-Octane Power Fantasy
Fast, frenetic, and fabulously fun, Turbo Overkill feels like Doom Eternal had a baby with a cyberpunk thriller. It's a power fantasy in the truest sense, with satisfyingly gory kills and a challenge that's tough yet fair. Ten hours in, and I've only scratched the surface of the first episode. This game is an adrenaline junkie's dream with continuous upgrades that keep things fresh and exciting.

Enemies: Gritty, Creative, and Surprisingly Smart
The enemies might not be Mensa members, but they're as gritty and creative as they come. They force you to think on your feet, offering just the right amount of variety to keep each encounter thrilling and fresh.

Weapons: An Arsenal That Packs a Punch
Every weapon feels purpose-built for destruction, and let me tell you, using your chainsaw leg never gets old. The guns are responsive, impactful, and downright enjoyable to use. Power-ups scattered throughout the game add even more flavour to your arsenal.

Graphics: Pixel-Perfect Mayhem
Turbo Overkill embraces its pixelated roots with chunky, yet magnificent, art. It's gory, it's biological, it's cyberpunk, and it's absolutely glorious. The animations are top-notch, and the visual feedback is spot on, ensuring you're always in the thick of the action.

Soundtrack: Beats to Butcher To
The techno, cyberpunk soundtrack pulsates with the game's frenzied pace. It might get a tad repetitive, but you'll be too busy enjoying the gameplay to notice.

Story: Surprisingly Engaging
For a game called 'Turbo Overkill' featuring a protagonist with a chainsaw for a leg, the story is unexpectedly engaging. And let's not forget the iconic John St. John, whose voice acting adds another layer of awesome to the mix.

Conclusion: Apogee's Magnum Opus
Apogee is back and better than ever. Turbo Overkill might just be their best release yet, setting a high bar for the future of FPS games. For those who grew up in the 90s, it's a nostalgic ride; for newcomers, it's a glimpse into what made that era so great. This game isn't just recommended; it's a must-play. Nearly perfect in every sense, Turbo Overkill is a beacon of hope for the FPS genre. Strap in, rev up your chainsaw leg, and get ready for a ride you won't soon forget!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne can compare this to many games (dusk, necromunda: hired gun, EYE divine cybermancy, doom eternal, ultrakill) but honestly it seems to beat out all the competition by incorporating their elements into a more cohesive and fine tuned experience than any of them. I think this might be the best first person shooter made in modern times be they retro inspired or not.

Movement is fast, fluid and responsive and the speed is insane. The best thing about this is that the levels are clearly made with this mobility in mind being very large scale and accommodating to the mobility of the player. Often times you'll find yourself backtracking through levels finding other paths thanks to how non linear they are, running into newly spawned enemies guarding a door you previously weren't able to open.

The way the game handles these enemy encounters is great too, rarely ever do you run into anything you could call a bullet sponge, instead most of the difficulty comes from evasion and quantity of enemies and it allows you to fully utilize the movement freedom you've been given, including a chainsaw-leg-slide boosted by rockets that can one-shot hordes of cannon-fodder. In-short this is a fast game about flow where a couple hits of enemy fire can mean the end in mere seconds.

The weapons at your disposal are entertaining, each having an alternate firing mode to switch to that greatly alters the behavior of the gun. Some even feature three firing modes.

The game also features an upgrade system that allows you to add behaviors and traits onto your body parts through cybernetic augmentation. These are meaningful and tangible effects that really influence how you play the game and change things up. An example is the ability to wall jump, another is adding extra chain explosives to the rockets from your rocket glove.

Vehicle sections are placed evenly throughout the campaign and do a great job of breaking up the pacing by switching up the gameplay a bit, they're very entertaining.

Graphically the game is serviceable, looking just about good enough for them to be able to carry the visuals through often solid and other times downright inspired art direction and the game runs very well.

The story is simple and the tone is campy and over the top, being mostly there to set up the gameplay but it does escalate further and further as a relatively local conflict balloons into something much grander in scale.

The music ranges from solid to great being made up of mostly synth/rock blends and during the final third of the game it incorporates orchestral elements that work surprisingly well and are somehow reminiscent of lotr and the matrix trilogy.

Definitely get it, even not on sale, if you're at all a fan of fast paced shooter action you'll probably love this.\t\t\t\t\t
