\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTurbo Overkill is good, occasionally great, and sometimes even excellent. The combat and movement fundamentals are exceptional: zippy movement, great air mobility, the potential for killer near-infinite airtime hijinks, a well-rounded arsenal, and solid enemy variety mean it's a joy to play.

But it is a generally one-note game, which is an issue considering its relatively long ~12-hour campaign. There's never a sense of ebb and flow to the level design (or overarching level progression), with its broadly forgettable and occasionally over-long levels sticking to the same "incidental combat leading into wave-based arena battle" loop for most (if not all) of its playtime.

Don't get me wrong; it executes that loop very well, perhaps even amazingly, and it's undoubtedly worth playing if you're an FPS fan. But just be warned that its post-Doom Eternal top-speed-all-the-way design, where everything is in service of that combat loop, can get quite tiring by the end.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm mainly writing this review to give a MAJOR DISCLAIMER FOR STEAM DECK USERS: For me, at least, when I tried this on the deck, it ran great, but the deck defaulted to using keyboard and mouse controls, which didn't always work well, and it seemed weird because the game makes mention of gamepad controls in its settings. I tried a community controls template and that fixed some issues but introduced either. Then, it struck me to try just changing the Steam Deck's controls from the official default to Gamepad (with mouse trackpad) and it works perfectly.

In short, the game has full gamepad support that does work on the steam deck, but the deck doesn't choose gamepad controls by default for some reason. Make sure you switch them when you start this game on your deck.\t\t\t\t\t
