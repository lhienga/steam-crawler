\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrustrating.
Don't get me wrong, sliding into enemies with a chainsaw leg is awesome, but since it's the most effective way to fight enemies, there's little point in using guns for anything other than blowing up explosive barrels. Sliding into explosive barrels resulted in most of my deaths, which was also not fun.

Secret hunting doesn't doesn't require much more than telling which bit of neon isn't supposed to be part of the backdrop.

It was extremely difficult to tell what the enemies are about to do. I'm not sure whether to blame that on The quick movement, blood splatter, other sfx, enemy silhouettes, or enemy animations. In Quake or Dusk, I can look at enemy animations to see what they're going to do and act accordingly. I can't do that here, so the only thing I care about is enemy position, so I can slide into them.

I restarted the campaign twice and bumped up the difficulty because it was too easy (Edit: The hardest difficulty is still too easy). The increased difficulties don't make the game feel harder, just more punishing for getting up close. Getting up close is the point of the game though right? Because they gave you a chainsaw leg?

Not-Shodan was spooky.\t\t\t\t\t
