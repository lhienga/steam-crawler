TLDR: Great gameplay, over the top story, chainsaws and more chainsaws.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a fun game made by an amateur developer, and it shows.

The game itself is fun, with understandable mechanics and fun enemies. if you like other movement shooters such as ultrakill or doom you will like this. just buy it its worth it. but here are some cons that ive found.

Difficulty settings
the difficulty settings in this game rely on the same awful method of every other game in the genre of making you a glass cannon. its very obvious they didnt test the different difficulties, because on the higher difficulties the game becomes impossible without cheesing the game. higher difficulties increase enemy dmage output, projectile speed, and walk speed. later difficulties prevents you from buying ammo (not a big deal), and prevents health packs from spawning outside of killing enemies. the final difficulty level, murder machine, is something i can oly describe as â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥. 6x damage, 2x projectile speed, increased enemy run speed, and no health packs outside of enemies. they did not test this difficulty level, while playing on thsi difficulty it is simply luck whether you survive or not, and its impossible without using two specific augments. it is very common to enter a boss with no hp, and be unable to beat it because you cant heal, forcing you to restart.

speaking about the bosses, they suck. theyre just boring, simple, and one dimensional. revolving around a specific gimmick that youve seen a in every game known to man, or just being boring. it doesnt help that the cutscene plays everytime you start again. because they didnt think putting a checkpoint after the cutscene was a good idea.

oh yeah, the checkpoints, they suck. checkpoints happen before big fights (not after so if you die after a big fight you just spent an hour on you gotta do it again), when you interact with a shop, or when you pick up a collectable. there is a non zero chance of soft locking yourself by interacting with a shop just as your about to die. the reason its this way is because the game is designed around quicksaving for some reason, which you cant do on the later difficulty levels.

overall the game is fine, dont play it on the final difficulty level and you should be fine. its a good game made an amateur developer, and it shows.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTurbo Overkill 3/4

Turbo Overkill is probably the most ambitious oldschool/boomer shooter I've played, certainly my favorite since Dusk. Turbo nails that frenetic pace of combat I love from the most recent of these FPS titles like DOOM: Eternal or the aforementioned Dusk. Turbo is more conventional in that it's generous with its ammo and it doesn't subtract from your arsenal as the campaign goes on. This had the effect of the game starting to feel repetitious the longer it went as I had solidifed my preferred tactics and the game didn't really encourage me/ask me to deviate from them. That said, the campaign stays fresh with new locales, set pieces, and a few new mechanics introduced with each of the game's episodes. The movement controls like butter and the shooting feels great. The only thing I think that's lacking comes with the audio. I wish the enemies had more barks to give them personality. Aside from that, this feels like a AAA game: the campaign is surprisingly long, each level feels like it has its own song, there are secret collectibles to seek out to gain access to secret levels, and that's not even to mention the mod support.\t\t\t\t\t
