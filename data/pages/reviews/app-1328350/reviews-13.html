\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemember when games used to be fun? This one does. At first I wasn't enamored because the enemies were a little mid, but it turns out they aren't supposed to be Halo elites- they're just fodder for your many, many toys. They just keep giving you cool explody toys and all the room in the world to play with them.

Great map variety, and design. One problem I usually have with these retro shooters is that I'm often getting lost due to samey level textures and no map. Not here, the directions are always plain as day because either bright neon doors aren't open yet or enemies are spawning in X direction. This keeps the momentum going and dodges a common pitfall.

Speaking of, the movement in this game is ridiculously cool. You have a chainsaw dash that is omnidirectional, plus two jumps and two dashes you can use in the air, plus other goodies I won't spoil. And the level design lets you use ALL of it. You are soaring through the air blowing up baddies and the devs don't care if you ever touch the ground again.

Story is a fun backdrop for all the killing you're doing, but what they nailed is the constant escalation. You are always going to more and more ridiculous places and killing bigger and badder dudes. Vehicular sections are fun and don't overstay their welcome.

The one issue I had is that some bosses are small and so fast that I have a hard time hitting them under normal conditions. Other than that it's an incredibly good time. Game just lets you have fun- a must-buy if you like retro shooters with an emphasis on mobility\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was a fairly fun and well made game at the start. I really enjoyed the pacing and the story-telling. However, after half-way point story completely derailed and branched out which was quite confusing and made me forget about the entire plot.

Then a lot of one-off mechanics were added, like flying car, motorcycle and mecha sequences that serves pretty much no purpose other than "why not".

But the worst offender was the quests becoming mindlessly repetitive. You can easily tell the developer looked for a way to prolong the lifetime of the game to increase play hours, which is understandable. However, the way to achieve this was simply gimmicky and diluted the whole experience. Simply put, one mission is divided into 5 different parts. So instead of "shoot corruption to clear the path", it becomes "shoot corruption in 5 different spots to clear the path". This essentially makes you do the exact same thing 5 times

This method gets exponentially worse in further levels where each of those objectives get their own branch of several other objective. For example, you need to destroy a terminal in 5 different locations. However, when you get to the terminal, you need to "kill" each terminal 5 times before the mission is completed. So what could be one mission artificially becomes 25 different mission.

Apart from that, boss fights also become repetitive, where one boss has 3 phases and each phase has 5 separate healthbars. So you have to kill the same boss 15 times. You get the idea.

The game was cool at the start but it overstayed its welcome. The meaningful part of the game is basically half the length of the entire playtime. If not for that problem, it would be quite a pleasant experience.\t\t\t\t\t
