\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor 2023, if CRPGs have Baldur's Gate 3 and action-adventure games have Tears of the Kingdom, first person shooters have Turbo Overkill. It's such a fantastic game for like 95% of its run time. Movement feels great and you're provided with a ton of options to spice things up like a grappling hook, two different dashes, wall hopping, and wall sliding. The level design is great, with some more linear levels and others that feel far more open. While you start off with only a measly single chainsaw leg, by the end of the game, all of your limbs are chainsaws; this is the closest thing to a Chainsaw Man game that we're gonna get for a very long time, if forever. Plus the neon dripping cyberpunk and 1980s synthwave aesthetic is excellent. The setting wears its inspirations on its sleeve, ranging from Blade Runner and Terminator to the pink and purple palm trees often found in vaporwave album covers. Also that soundtrack though, I keep going back to All Aboard the Pain Train and Sunset Synthetica because they're so catchy.

I do have some complaints, despite this being one of the best games I've played in 2023. While Turbo Overkill has 22 levels that range from good to amazing, the last two levels feel very weak, partially due to the final two bosses. One has three exhausting phases and really should have been the final boss. The final level is just kind of mid until you get to the true final boss, which was shockingly weak for this game. One section of that boss is highly reminiscent of the Bed of Chaos's final section from the original Dark Souls, and that's not a good thing to be compared. There were also some glitches, including one door that wouldn't unlock at the end of a level (thankfully easily fixed with noclipping) and some of the collectibles in the last two levels not spawning for some reason (even more reason not to like those two levels). There's also some unlockable bonus levels too if you want to mess around some more, but I didn't like them as much as the main game.

But that doesn't mean that the other 22 levels aren't great and worth experiencing for anyone who enjoys boomer shooters. This is easily one of my favorite shooters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100% the most fun you can have with a chainsaw for a leg.

This isn't just another nostalgia-bait retro shooter. Great mechanics, great level design, great story, great music... Super fun all around. The progression takes you through new abilities and weapons really nicely, with the levels providing plenty of different ways to do things utilizing all your various abilities.

My first playthrough has been on "regular joe" difficulty, and I do plan to replay it at a higher difficulty just for the fun of it!

If I *had* to complain about one thing it would be this: Certain 1st-person games tend to give me motion sickness. Never figured out why. This is one of them. It's ot the motion, Portal has never done it and the movement there is probably even weirder. It's an easy game to load up for a short play session though.
Oh, and the sound effects design could use some tuning. I had to turn the volume down to keep the "collecting coins" sound effect from blowing my skull up.

Now if you'll excuse me, this chainsaw gets itchy if I don't lubricate it with some blood periodically, gotta go.\t\t\t\t\t
