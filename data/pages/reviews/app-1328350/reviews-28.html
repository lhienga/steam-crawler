\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile too bloated for its own good, especially during the final episode - sitting around 12 hours for an average playthrough - this ♥♥♥♥ is nothing short of a triumphant accomplishment. How was this made by ONE GUY??

That's it for the bad. Now onto the good:

- Combat almost never gets dull. Enemy variety and design is excellent, you're always able to pick out who is who and act accordingly - something that is crucial for this game with the amount of stuff that can be happening on the screen at any given time.
Your arsenal is also expertly balanced. You can use every single weapon you're given to the very end of the game, and they will still remain viable. All of them also serve their own unique purpose and are better at taking down certain enemies than others, so you'll always be switching back and forth. Nothing ever goes unused.
- The soundtrack is an absolute BANGER. Boomshoots cannot stop dropping the best soundtracks, and this one isn't made by Andrew Hulshult! (throwing no shade, he does superb work as well. he's just everywhere lol).
- The set pieces are absurdly high quality, rivaling the scale I've seen in AAA PROJECTS.
Again: ONE GUY DID THIS.
- Art style is stunning. The neon-soaked streets of Paradise are constantly gorgeous to look at. Some might complain that the environment variety gets a little same-y, and while I don't disagree with that, it's so well executed that it barely bothered me during my playthrough.
- Voice acting is superb across the board (though the dialogue can be overly corny, possibly intentional). Can't go wrong with Jon St. John!
- CHAINSAW LEG
yadayadayadayadayada

You get the point. Go buy this, I shouldn't need to sell you on it anymore.\t\t\t\t\t
