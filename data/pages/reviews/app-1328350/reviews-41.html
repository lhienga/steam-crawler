\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a fantastic game, a blend of familiar faces that manages to feel fresh and fun the whole way through. Part Ultrakill, part Doom 2016, I even felt traces of Serious Sam in there at times. Although there are multiple inspirations, the biggest influence is definitely Ultrakill, and that's a great thing. There were even times that I thought to myself, "I should be swapping weapons more if I want to get a good rank on this level." And even though it won't get you an S-Rank, this game is well designed enough that you do end up getting a good reason to swap weapons often, in the form of a swapping = damage augment.

I think that's the theme of this game, really; well designed. There were only one or two times that I had to look around for where to go, meaning the level design was really great and it flowed naturally. All the weapons were satisfying and fun to use, toting interesting secondary functions that give each weapon depth. It should go without saying that the movement is fantastic and fun while also being easy to pick up and go fast right away. Love some big boss fights and, while the story wasn't anything profound really, it's a good backdrop to get you to said boss fights. My only complaint really would be that a couple of the levels in Episode 3 dragged on a bit, but that's no big deal really.

Overall, this game was absolutely delightful and the pictures on the steam page don't even do it justice. It's actually way more fun than it looks. If you're even slightly interested, I'd definitely recommend it. Without hesitation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend sticking to the first three difficulty options for first-time players to minimise frustration and to acquire all upgrades before even thinking of attempting to play on maximum difficulty (unless you are a die-hard shooter fan). On maximum difficulty enemies do almost seven times their regular damage, so many more enemy attacks gain one-shot potential. Completing the game on maximum difficulty is required for an achievement, but think hard if you even want to try for it. The way these shooting games usually increase difficulty is by gradually locking the player in tighter spaces with "super heavy" enemies, who have area-of-effect attacks which damage the player only.

Enemies annoyingly spawn out of thin air, have basic physics from 1998, cannot climb, and cannot be pushed off ledges. The player, on the other hand, is highly mobile - he is able to double-jump (even triple-jump with an optional upgrade), double-dash, and freely change direction mid-air. Unfortunately, the basic shotgun and rocket launcher primary fire sounds and feels a little weak. In actual fact, the chainsaw leg at times feels more effective than guns, especially after getting upgrades for damage and health/armour regeneration on kills. On a more positive note for gun enthusiasts, the crappy starting pistols do redeem themselves eventually when their overcharge upgrade is unlocked, which makes them the best weapon in the game as long as you don't miss your shots.

The game features a crippled quick-save option, but it does not seem to be available in locked arenas until the fight is over. These saves are "soft" - they get erased upon completing the level even if you click "RESTART" at the end. Maximum difficulty does not allow quick-saves, though checkpoints are fairly frequent - at least until you fight the multi-phase bosses. On higher difficulties dying at any stage during such a boss fight means restarting from the very beginning, whereas the first three levels save boss-phase progress.

The game goes on for a bit too long for what is on offer here in terms of story and combat variety, but for those who want more there is an endless wave mode as well as 24 secret levels to unlock via finding collectible items in the regular levels. These items are well-hidden in nooks and crannies, but at least there don't seem to be any invisible walls or indistinguishable switches to the same effect, so there is no need to spam the use key while walking along the walls, finally.\t\t\t\t\t
