\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can wholly recommend this game to anyone who likes Doom or Ultrakill.
Its incredibly fast paced and offers plenty of opportunities to go crazy with your weapons. It remains balanced throughout and the only time i found myself struggling was when i wasn't using my arsenal to its full capacity. There is a wide variety of enemies that all function differently, while never feeling like too much of a bullet sponge. I started off by playing the demo and i bought the full game almost immediately after finishing it. You get excellent air control and plenty of opportunities for dodging. Theres no unique gimmick or advanced tech that this game runs on, and mostly keeps things interesting through its fantastic set-pieces, super fun weapons and high-speed movement.
The campaign is extremely long, providing me with roughly 20 hours of content (i was taking my sweet time experimenting, exploring and getting lost though, so expect something closer to 15 hours). The gameplay changes up frequently so i never felt like it was getting boring. Fair warning, the story takes some time to get started and only really speeds up around the halfway point. Despite this, the story was actually really fun to follow along with, the characters were interesting, and the cutscenes were funny. The ending scene has me hyped for a sequel.
The visuals are fantastic and really bring that "boomer-shooter" vibe into the 2020s. The enemies, weapons and levels have great readability most of the time even if sometimes its a bit vague on where you need to go.
One major help in improving my proficiency was discovering how much the game depends on the chainsaw-leg. You should be using this thing every 5 seconds with the armour and heal gene addons to keep your health up.

My only minor criticism is that in terms of performance, Turbo Overkill is fill-rate limited.
This wont be a problem for most as this game can easily run at 60fps 1080p with no issues even on mid-range or even low-end hardware.
Considering it has a native TAA implementation, (and a very good one at that) it would be a massive help if there was some sort of upscaling tech, such as FSR 2.2 or FSR 3 to maintain high framerates at high resolutions. The game frequently dropped below my target 165fps at 1440p and i ended up having to cap it at 120fps. I know, these may be minor problems considering most people don't play like this, but the performance was similar to Doom Eternal at max settings, and i expected it to be much better considering the art style of the game.

All in all, just buy the game, its some great fun.
Good Hunting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis might sound like an obvious overstatement, especially since I'm writing this after 4 hours of gameplay, but..BUT, this might be one of my favorite games ever.
Since I love both doom and cyberpunk this feels like a suspiciously perfect fit for a goofer like me. The inspiration is very obv to a point one might say this straight up just IS doom, but no,... its so much better.
It's funny doom with both the weapons and movement on steroids. It's set in a world controled by a ROGUE AI, which is also like doom slimy tendrils virus thing. And most importantly, you have a chainsaw leg. Yes, a chainsaw leg with which you can slide super far while slicing a dicing enemies. I havent gotten there yet, but you can literally shoot a rocket and grapple onto into with your hook. You can wall run, double jump, have 2 charges of dash + you can chainsaw slide all you want. So yeah, take doom, add cyberpunk story and character cyberware customization, add funny and add retro FPS look and thats what this is.
Also it's apparently been made mostly by one dev.

This game is ridiculously awesome.\t\t\t\t\t
