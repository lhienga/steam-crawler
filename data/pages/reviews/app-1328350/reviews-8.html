\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gotta be the negative Nancy here. Mainly because the highest difficulty "Murder Machine" is so ridiculously unfair, it's crazy:
No quicksaving, even though the game has quick saves otherwise, no health or armor pick ups except from some enemy drops (Like WTF), most enemies (even the weakest ones) can one-shot kill you, or at least take away most of your health in a blink of an eye, enemy projectiles are either hit scan or so incredibly fast and numerous that it's either impossible to dodge them or completly luck based.

Because of the no health or armor pick ups basically your only way to get back health and armor is by having certain implants and then doing chainsaw kills. This is fine when you have low tier enemies to kill but not when there are only high tier enemies left.

I played a lot of old school fps and I usually play my first run on the highest difficulty and sure it was hard at times, but never as hard and unfair than in this game.
What pisses me even more off, is that this game has an achievement for beating the game on the highest difficulty (Unlike for example Doom (2016) and Doom Eternal or Doom 1 and 2 in Doom 3 BFG Edition which all have ridiculously hard highest difficulty but thank god no achievement for beating it). And despite all the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ I managed to beat most of the game on it, even though a lot of the fights took me like 50-100 tries, maybe even more. Yeah, was very "fun"
But then you get to the boss fights which are even worse. And for some reason the devs thought it was a good idea to include 3 bosses just in Episode 2. Episode 1 had 1 boss fight which is more than enough. I managed to beat the first boss in Episode 2 but the other 2 are so extremly unfair I couldn't beat them. Honestly they would be doable if they would at least include check points after every boss phase, but that would be too fair I guess...
So, what did I do: Well, during the game you collect tech chips in secret areas. And each of them varies the game which you can activate through the main menu. Some of them make the game even harder but some are basically cheats, like insta gib (which for some reason works on some bosses but not all), infinite ammo (which isn't even needed as the game gives you way too much ammo) or infinite turbo time. Thank god those cheats do not void the achievements, so after having tried those bosses way too often, I decided to use them at those points.
I "love" it when a game is that hard you basically have to use cheats to beat it...

Yeah I get it what some of you might say: just lower the difficulty. Yeah, fair point but in my opinion a game on a highest difficulty should still always be fair and doable if you properly learn the game (which I did).

But what about the rest of the game:
Well, honestly the rest of the game is mostly fine but still far from perfect:
-some of the weapons are so weak, like the super shotgun (which is a crime in itself), the chaingun or the plasma gun, basically after some upgrades you just need a few weapons to effectively kill enemies, namely the regular shotgun (only for alt fire), the assault rifle, the rocket launcher and sometimes the sniper.
-soundtracks: some of them are really good, others some annoying techno bs
-the devs tried to include a story but that was so boring and generic, they might have just cut it entirely, same with the characters, all but Sam were super annoying, sometimes dialogues are even pretty cringe

So is it all bad? No, of course not. At least the non-boss fights are mostly quite fun if you ignore the underpowered weapons. If you don't care for achievements or beating a game on it's highest difficulty, this is a decent old school fps, otherwise it's just pain and because of all the pain this game gave me, I have to rate it negative.

Cry about it.

Honestly the best way of playing the game is to play your first run on the second highest difficutly unlock all upgrades and collect all tech chips.
Then if you care for the highest difficulty achievement, just use the cheats to beat it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of my favourite genres, so I really wanted to like Turbo Overkill but I just couldn't get into it at all.

Experience:
I breezed through the first handful of levels in 30 minutes without any challenge from the enemies, the biggest obstacle being the environment which all blended together which killed the pace when back tracking was required.

Core Mechanic:
The sliding mechanic is a nice idea, but in my experience it was WAY too OP, and given your seemingly unlimited arsenal there just wasn't any friction; I was plowing through the enemies with no issue but also without much satisfaction.

Aesthetics:
I also bounced off the aesthetics which felt really unfocused to me; a cute robot on the walkie talkie, a military guy smoking a joint on the main page, the borderlands inspired vending machines and the enemy design all felt like they were pulled from different IPs in a way which, for me, didn't come into an interesting whole.

imo new age movement shooters like Ultrakill, post void and Chop Goblins do this genre right; simpler and more focused. Turbo Overkill just didn't scratch that itch for me; simply ending up with a load of cool mechanics and ideas crushed together into one over produced and scatterbrain package.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's indie cyberpunk DOOM Eternal without the chainsaw handicap and more mobility. There's a good variety of enemies, weapons all have specific purposes and at least one alt-fire mode, the movement systems are excellent and have good customization options, the level design is fantastic, and the bosses are all well-designed with unique mechanics. It took me a while to power through the first Act because it just felt like a long tutorial in a tediously by-the-numbers cyberpunk aesthetic cityscape, but Acts 2 & 3 have a crazy amount of environmental and aesthetic variety. In fact, despite looking like a low-brow boomer shooter on paper, this game addresses cyberpunk themes better than most other recent games in the genre and slowly adds more biopunk and body horror elements.

There's a lot of bang for your buck here and it's definitely better than most nu-boomer shooters I've played. The only objective problems I can think of were some high tier enemies having too much health, enemy explosive weapons doing too much damage, and some bosses being a slog because of lack of checkpoints. This all might've been just because of bad difficulty scaling though because I was playing on Hard. Definitely worth picking up if you enjoyed Doom Eternal's design but thought it was missing parkouring chainsaw legs. Overall, Turbo Overkill was a blast and was much better than I expected.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis *has* to be the most adrenaline-fuelled, manic, massively over-the-top thrill ride you can play today. Whatever mechanic you like in your FPSes, Turbo Overkill's got it but polished to absolute perfection.

The wonderful thing about T.O. is that if you can dynamically adjust the difficulty by pre-selecting the difficulty level and then adjusting it with tech chip mods. For example, I played at "standard" (aka Street Sweeper), and it was a nice challenge for a Doom/Quake/Duke veteran, with lots of high-speed combat in tight hallways and huge spaces, but I was picking up hidden "tech chips" along the way. Collecting all 3 on a level gives you a game-altering tweak that can help or hinder.

If you fancy seeing what it's like playing as a porcelain teapot, sure, there's a mod that makes you die on the first hit. But, if you've already done a level and want to replay it to get an achievement or missed secret, or you're finding a level too hard, there are mods that can make you pretty much invincible. There's one that lets you play in slow motion all the time. There's another that gives infinite ammo, and one that makes all enemies insta-gib. Even the most uncoordinated granny who's never seen a mouse before would be like death itself with those three tech chips.

After I got about halfway through the game, I got a little fed up with hunting for ammo, so I turned on "infinite ammo". By the end of the game I found my reflexes slightly too slow for the last few levels, so I turned on "infinite Turbo Time", which made those levels passable.

But, be warned: This game is friggen un-put-downable. The levels are one long hyper-intense smash-fest at warp speed set up to give you maximum changes to rock like a god. Anyone who played doom and tried the -turbo 250 command-line switch will know what I'm talking about. The game just feels like it's running at a million miles an hour...

But, like in Max Payne, as soon as you kick in Turbo Time (bullet time), everything slows down to a wonderful anime-like action sequence. You can take your time lining up shots. You can speed over chasms and drop a few grenades down, or pop a couple of rockets into the mouth of a big beastie, and then land safely on the other side before spinning around and nailing a headshot at the sniper on the ledge above you...

The really big warning is... if you play it long enough, when you go into the outside world, everything feels like it's in slow motion. I found myself driving almost too fast and needed to force myself to slow down. This game is so FAST, and just so INTENSE, it makes your brain run at 250% doing jumps, leaps, shots, rebounds, dodges. It all feels so natural as well. The controls are beautifully set that you find yourself executing a ballet of perfect death, a whirlwind of chainsaws and miniguns, leaving just a fine red mist behind you. So, when you turn off the PC, step out into that big blue outdoors, and you're "just" walking along, it's like in futurama when Fry drinks a hundred cups of coffee and he can see the flapping of a hummingbird's wings. There's even a loading screen hint that says "If you want to know what Turbo Time is, drink 100 cups of coffee".. It's *EXACTLY* like that.

The weapons are AWESOME. there's no "BFG" so to speak, but the Ion cannon comes pretty close, making the full standard set of FPS weps from Melee to OP exotic complete. The sniper rifle that lets you telefrag enemies has so many brilliant uses, it's an interesting twist on a mechanic I've never seen before. The grappling hook can be used to wonderful effect at dodging and leaping, just like it did in Quake 2 Capture The Flag...

Simply put, the devs have sat down and written a list of all the epic things they loved from Duke, Doom, Quake, Quake 2, Halo, Doom (2016), and a myriad other games and said "What if we put just the best stuff in, and leave out the filler".

The music is absolutely gorgeous. Some of the levels have their own themes, and the game itself has a theme that ripples through the pieces. Taking on an army of mutants in Turbo Time while a thumping techno track blasts from the speakers... Listening to the bullets whizz by my ear in Surround Sound, listening to the distant explosions of monsters that just caught my rocket volley to the face... It's all so wonderful.

There are also challenges and arenas you can test out your skills on. Some of them are immense fun.

I can't recommend this highly enough, but there IS a lot of bad language. a profanity filter would be appreciated with possible alternate dialogue for anyone who doesn't appreciate the F-bomb being dropped all the time (not the wrist-cannon mod). I often had to pause the game when my kids were anywhere about, lest they hear someone's dropping S- and F-bombs like confetti.

I'm a completionist, so I've hunted all over for the tech chips and cassettes, hence the 60 hours playtime. I reckon a speedrun would be about 10 hours, with a solid playthrough being about 30.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fine, unfortunately not amazing.
It does feel like style-over-substance, and it's noticable how there is a big coverage by Retro-FPS community in reviews, but almost 0 activity afterward, like in guides, let's plays and the like.

I do, however, want to congratulate the small dev team for delivering a finished and good game to the finish line

I'll be honest, it took me quite a few attempts to "get" the game. I did find the game boring, frustrating, or tedious at first, even thought to leave a negative review.
But then I decided to just get through to the end, and in the end, it clicked.

I could not shake of the feeling of kitchen-sink design, that it's unbalanced and doesn't fit together well.
I learned to stop caring and just go "chegg goes brrrr" and found the experience quite enjoyable.

If you come expecting the tight and smart design of Ultrakill, lower expectations. Don't feel weird if you forget that you have certain guns/abilities/upgrades and use the same 2-3 guns on anything that your Chegg (titular chainsaw leg) does not outright instagib.

Weapons do feel really kitchen-sinky, like anything goes, doesn't matter if it fits really.
Like after 50h I still don't really know what's the tactical difference between two Uzis and Twincendiary Machinegun. And the flamethrower mod of above-mentioned gun? Game doesn't really give you great feedback on how much damage fire does, it sometimes obstructs what you are trying to burn, and also has confusing death animations, where grunts stay upright burning, and it's not clear who's dead, who's burning, and who managed to escape the flame.
And same-ish problems occur all around your arsenal, they aren't designed to interact with each other really, there are only few clear interactions with questionable utility, like grabbing your rockets with plasma rifle. Oh yeah btw, how does Plasma rifle really fit in? Why, again, would I use it above my 2 machineguns, and it also has this meme-beam, at least it's not as bad as in Doom Eternal, maybe it's projectile grab is somewhat useful.
Arsenal feels like it tries to copy Doom Eternal, but shares it's pitfalls.
I only found useful altfire of Magnums, altfire of Waster shotgun, Boomer shotgun and Uzis, ah also maybe Rocket Launcher
See how I mentioned altfires? Because the primary fires feel like trying to kill an ant colony by shooting individual ants with a slingshot.
I just overused the OP chainsaw leg and didn't feel good about it, as if I was playing wrong

Enemy design is also at times questionable. For me it felt that either I would mainly receive inconsequential chip damage, HP rarely being lower than 80, but then I would catch some stupid and unfair attack that would 1-hit-kill me outright, with almost full HP/armor
(main offenders being Sloth's rockets and armored flamethrower dudes).
Higher difficulty settings only make this problem more noticable, and in general feel untested, unbalanced and lazy
(yeah, hehe, x6.66 damage received, funny number, ah also way faster projectile speed, have fun catching instakill rockets with your head, no hp/armor pickups BTW)

In order for me to start enjoying the game, again, i really had to start ignoring these issues, turn the brain off, and just enjoy blasting whatever\t\t\t\t\t
