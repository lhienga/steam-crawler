\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThoughts after completing the campaign:

Overall, buy the game if you like anything in the retro-FPS or movement shooter genres. The quality and experience is amazing, with some notable exceptions.

Best things:
- imaginative weapons
- movement mechanics which enhance the shooter core rather than distracting
- amazing visuals and sound effects
- ridiculous amount of work has gone into creating some set-piece levels, like a whole mech suit area which could have been a game in itself!
- mostly very good interesting enemies
- a lot of ingenious level design

Bad things
- boss fights are terrible - many many phases which largely involve very repetitive tasks and killing legions of enemies you already killed loads of. Sometimes less is more. pick the 1-3 most interesting ideas and build your boss fight around that.
- the writing is awful - thank goodness cut-scenes can be skipped because this is the most cliche, grating dialogue. Maw is the most boring cookie cutter villain ever.
- still plenty of bugs - mostly enemies walking through closed doors, sometimes soft locking you into arenas where you need to kill everything to proceed


Basically, my summary would be that most of the time the "more is more" attitude of the game works in its favour, creating some really over the top action. BUT in things like the boss fights and cut scenes it goes the other way, and it just becomes a chore. Overall easy recommend though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe absolute HEIGHT of gaming excitement.

Turbo Overkill is a Retro FPS developed by Happy Trigger Studios and published by the revived Apogee Entertainment. Following the story of Johnny Turbo fighting as a Street Cleaner in the cyberpunk city of Paradise against the force of the morally corrupt super AI known as Syn. The game features an absolute masterclass of both gameplay, aesthetics, and, general awesomeness as you blast through THREE action packed episodes plus a plethora of extras! Starting off as a Early Access release, it's absolutely incredible how Turbo Overkill was not only a really awesome game that early on, but managed to carry a constant rising action all the way to the credits.

Gameplay of Turbo Overkill is of very similar to style that Doom Eternal or ULTRAKILL, where most combat encounters are focused on Arenas and using the enemies as resource pinatas. Turbo Overkill however feels like a middle ground between that style of FPS as well as those described as Boomer Shooter. Since, some enemy encounters feels more naturally placed, most pickups are actual pickups in the environment, and level design tends to loop on itself instead of being a linear path to the finish. The shooting in this game is just incredible. Almost every single weapon manages to both, not be irrelevant as the game goes on but also have unique aspects that makes them so much fun to use. Like the pistols having a homing charging shot that can track multiple enemies, or the chaingun have both a bullet and flamerthrower mode you can swap between, or even just the fact you can slice enemies up by just sliding across the ground just boils to just absolutely peak movement shoot design. Where you're given the tools to defeat your foes and there's plenty of different options to take them out to your liking. Furthermore as a half-mechanical killing machine, you also have a wide plethora of augments to mix and match on your body parts. These give you buffs to specific aspects to your character abilities, like being able to gain HP or Armor on Chainsaw kills or giving you faster recharge times and similar buffs. Though Turbo Overkill tends to ramp up these upgrades as the game goes on, in the context that you keep getting better and better augments to mix in with your loadout. Even more so, Johnny gets a grappling hook for even more mobility, and gets his hands on so much more that I feel like that it would be too spoiler-y to just explain everything you get in this game. Boss Fights are also interesting as there is quite a lot of variety here, something you wouldn't normally see in these kinds of games. You do have your standard, shoot it until it dies fight, but you also have fights that feel more like 1-on-1s with the 3 bounty hunters that hunt you down during the game. A great mix of boss fight styles that actually succeeds on not feeling entirely underwhelming in the grand scheme of things.

Now Style and Music wise, Turbo Overkill is just a near-perfect blend! As with other Retro FPS throwbacks on the market, Turbo Overkill uses a lowpoly artstyle mixed with pixelation similar to how PS1 textures appear. However, I feel like the game manages to use this as a advantage to it's artstyle rather than existing to cut costs on modeling. This is because of the overall Cyberpunk theming plastered nearly all over the game. The vibrate colors mixed with the concrete and trashy buildings flows really well with this modeling and texture style. Plus, it still looks great in isolation as well as during actually gameplay where you're often speeding around and fighting. The music on the other hand is absolutely stellar, fitting the tone of each level amazingly with techno synths, orchestral, and solid metal to capture a unique cyberpunk vibe. Furthermore, as the episode progresses the tone of the music keeps ramping up with everything keeping a constant moving force.

Overall, Turbo Overkill is simply a fantastic game and a outstanding shooter. So many of the ideas and concept crammed into this game just work perfectly with each other to create on of the most fun and gratifying shooters I've played since... I think maybe Doom Eternal or ULTRAKILL? (And the later isn't even out at the time of this review) It just succeeds at giving you that constant pushing force in the gameplay and story without making you feel overpowered, more so that you have the tools and skills, and proceed to win fight after fight without being trivial in the grand scheme of things. Even after the main campaign you still have a ton of content with the secret levels, arena mode, and even a full blown level editor. Apogee are back, and Turbo Overkill being their first published game in their returns proves that they're still the height of gaming excitement!\t\t\t\t\t

Top-tier shooter, unique ideas and great-feeling movement
Turbo Overkill's chainsaw leg sliding and movement mechanics caught my eye in pre-release footage. I gave the demo a go and from the skatepark-like area in the first level, I knew I was onto something special.

Similar elements to Doom Eternal, but less constrained
Put simply, Turbo Overkill takes the Doom Eternal approach to the weapon roster. The weapons have their own strengths/weaknesses within different scenarios, with alt-fires to counter specific combat situations. However, unlike Doom Eternal, the game doesn't feel like it punishes you for not "playing the right way". You get a little more freedom to play your way and are never explicitly told what works best when faced with particular situations.

Additionally, the gameplay takes more inspiration from classic shooters. Occassionally you'll be locked into arenas, but the level design is closer to the layer-on-layer, interconnecting and wraparound map layouts of classic games.

Map design & movement, perfectly synergised
Chainsaw slide damage increases when you move at higher speeds and the maps allow you to take advantage of this quite often. You also have a dash ability, a double jump, wall sliding and even wall-jumping with the right augment. Your weapons allow you to get some air also.

While navigating the levels, it's easy to recognise how the designers have competently crafted levels around the movement mechanics. In approaching classic tropes like "train levels", the movement permits the designers to include some original and unique flourishes, preventing these areas from feeling too derivative.

The best movement-shooter I've experienced so far
I've just beat the second level of the second episode and I know there's plenty more to experience in the coming levels. But unless the game takes a sharp dive, I don't see the point in updating this review once I've completed it.

So far, the game has been consistent in pleasuring my lizard brain and if it stays the same until the end, I'll be a happy bunny.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWtf is this production value ?
I was thought it will be your regular indie boomer shooter, but just wtf.

The gameplay is out there with Doom eternal and Ultrakill, a really solid roster of weapon with quickswitching and a lot of movement options (dash, double jump, shotgun jump, rocket jump, wall ride, telefrag, grappling hook...), and the chainsaw slide is really cool and well integrated.
The pacing is perfect, new weapons, new mechanics almost every levels, vehicule sections and some very good platforming ??!
The set pieces are insane especialy in the final episode.
The bosses are fun and varied wich is rare for a FPS.
The story is badass and epic and there is some really cool cinematics (like a lot, but short, like in doom eternal) and the voice acting is nice and funny.

TLDR, this game really feel like an AAA game (the only give away is the modernised retro art style wich i dig), this is surely one of the best FPS of all time.

Can't wait for a sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
