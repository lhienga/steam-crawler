OK! Fine!
But I won't say that much: Turbo-Overkill truly rocks, and it rocks so much that enters with its own right in the pantheon of the modern boomer-shooter. I got the copy in EA and unlike most, Trigger Happy Interactive did deliver not only a well rounded experience... but one that has a fre****g Identity. Not only style, but an Identity of its own (in a genre that, although full of truly fun games, some of them lack a... personality... Turbo surely doesn't).
I mean the game is a cohesive experience: weapons, movements, power up, enemies and all the rest are perfectly thought to be together and not just mix-mashed like 'Hey, this is cool, just throw in it. Doesn't matter if it doesn't work that well.
And that, dear friends, means an astonishing gameplay which separates the games from the rest of its peers: multiple secrets that actually have a purpose (they unlock new and new LEVELS!), and not just to be a collect-athon (which is not bad, but for me are a time waster and nothing else), more than 20 hour of gameplay (now almost a rarity for modern FPS) in single player mode with all the levels being FUN, parkour that doesn't break (mostly) the game but actually is required in order to survive higher difficulties, and a f***ton more. (It even manages to deliver a good story, with some great voice acting).
Now, it does have some downside: sometimes the checkpoint system can mess up your game and some levels being too... long (which is weird, usually is the contrary), and the main character doesn't utter a word (even when damaged).
But again, nothing of the technical problems can even touch this masterpiece of game! Really recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
