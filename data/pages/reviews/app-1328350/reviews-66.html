The Story is well told and has a lot of entertaining moments that never make it dull. The Characters are really well voice acted and are always fun to watch.

The Settings are so well developed. The cyberpunk setting is pretty cool and the level designs for this game are just a joy to play through.

Visually, it's amazing. Performance was solid throughout my playtime and i didn't come across any bugs or glitches. The Art Direction is perfect with it having a modern retro style about it that fits nicely with the boomer shooter genre.

Sound and Audio were brilliant with the soundtrack just being filled with banger after banger and the audio is so satisfying especially with the guns.

In terms of gameplay, I think that this might be the best boomer shooter you can get on steam. Movement feels smooth and fast pace. Weapons are all well varied and it never felt like any weapon was useless. The secondary fire for these guns also adds a lot for players to experiment with. The augment upgrades are pretty well developed and gives variety as to how a player can approach the game. The enemies are a great challenge and differ from one another in the best ways possible. Finally, the boss fights in this game are just awesome with the 2nd last boss being a treat to destroy.

Overall, Turbo Overkill is definitely one of the best boomer shooters that I've ever played and feels the most polished out of all of them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally good game with one of the most fun ive had gameplaywise in recent years, IF you choose the right perks that fix some of the games "issues".
Must haves are invincibility while dashing, because otherwise there is no way to midigate damage at all.
Perks that give you health and armor on chainsawleg kills are pretty "mandatory" as well.
The game is vastly unbalanced in pretty much every regard. There are weapons that are significantly stronger than others while some of them are completely useless resulting in me never using them.
Some of these levels were the best ones ive played in an fps game ever.
You could generally say that the highs and lows with this game are equal. Sometimes the game is phenomenal, sometimes it really drops the ball.
Unfortunately you feel the episodic release with this one because the game is all over the place in terms of pacing, weapons and ability unlocks.
In the second episode you pretty much get all the abilities at once that change traversel, thus resulting in the leveldesign adapting to the new movement tech.
This renders the first episode of the game useless since these abilities were introduced later on.
Generally speaking the second episode is by far the best one.
The third episode has its moments but isnt as good as the second or the first one in my opinion.
The story is weird and changes directions constantly as if the creators didnt know how to conclude things or drive the plot forward naturally.
Music is an absolute banger for the most part but gets worse and uninspired closing in on the end of the game just like the leveldesign.

Overall and considering the playtime I would still recommend the game, but maybe not for its full pricetag. If its on sale you should grab it if you liked doom eternal because thats the closest game to compare this to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly thought this game was just going to be a like every other boomer shooter, and while it was that, it was more, WAY more.

The gameplay brought so many refreshing changes to what we come to expect from these types of games. The chainsaw legs and eventually the arms became so fun to slaughter anything and everything that was in my way. The guns were pretty great to use as well, a lot of them have very useful and creative alternate firing modes. that being said, I would say there are a couple weak links to them as well. The Boomer and the Plasma Rifle were not as fun as i hoped and didn't bring that much to the table. The enemies in this game bring a nice challenge while also making them easy enough to kill. The bosses were fun and had a lot to offer, although I think the Episode 3 Maw bossfight was really repetitive and even a little annoying. The upgrade system brought a lot of potential to ways you could customize yourself into becoming an absolute murder machine.

As soon as I started the game, the visuals already fascinated me before I even killed a single enemy, and that's probably what convinced me to buy the game. The bright and beautiful skyscrapers of Paradise just blew me away, I just found myself getting lost at the sight of the games visuals. As I kept playing, I still found myself to be impressed by the game's background, especially in episode 3 where the Vermillion Front and their huge spaceship basically screams for your attention. I don't usually care about cut scenes that much, but they are very well done in this game, they always either show a badass display of pure badassery or make some pretty funny jokes within them . I guess what I'm trying to say here is that the art style and direction is pretty dang good.

While I wouldn't say it's particularly amazing, but the plot of the game deserves at least some credit because most devs of this genre of games generally don't care about that. It's a very simple "beat the Big Evil Bad Guy" story, but most if not all the characters have wonderful voice actors and are very expressive. Even Johnny Turbo (The guy you play as), who is a silent protagonist, expresses a lot of character through the movements he makes in cutscenes.

I did not know what to expect out of the soundtrack but OH MY GOD is it awesome, There is a lot of diversity in the tracks, ranging from a very fast paced techno style to aggressive metal to a whole damn orchestra. I don't even know what to say about beyond this point. It's not just plain cool, it's OVERCOOL

So yea I think that's pretty much it.
Stop reading this and play the freaking game!\t\t\t\t\t
