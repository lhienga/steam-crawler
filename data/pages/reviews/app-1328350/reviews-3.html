\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely underrated, great game. I went in expecting just another average boomer shooter but it really develops past expectations as the game progresses in pretty much every element.
Starting off with the most core mechanic of boomer shooters: the shooting. It stays true to the tried and true method of no aiming down sites, no reloads, satisfying gibbing animations, and extremely fast weapon swapping.
The arsenal in this game starts off basic (pistol, shotgun, assault rifle) and then evolves to include incredibly novel weapons (orbital laser, a rifle that telefrags enemies) that standout even in a genre saturated with great gun ideas. Additionally,
upgrades are well thought out and all the aforementioned basic weapon archetypes become something WAY more. They never become stale to use.
Secondly, movement in this game is very fluid and familiar. Accelerate or stop on a dime as you skate past enemies delivering your preferred brand of death. It doesn't try to reinvent the wheel in movement tech but I much prefer it that way. Sliding, dashing, double jump are industry standards and it does a good job of adding a gimmick to also make them lethal.
Then we have story. Most stories are pretty minimalistic or basic in this category. But while basic, T.O.'s story is so over the top that it stands above most others. The cutscenes were way more enjoyable than they had any right or need to be, and the characters actually intriguing with a personality that places them beyond being just another voice or target.
Coupled with a great story, we have incredible, lengthy level designs. There are some absolutely gorgeous sets that hit the requisite cyberpunk theme, but also some truly awesome set designs. One in particular for me was fighting in flowing traffic on top of flying vehicles backlit by the glow of a neon city, and then boarding a moving train to fight on, in and under it, to finally disembarking and continuing at a station.
Lastly we have the gimmicks. It's not enough for games nowadays to just have guns and sprawling levels. T.O. decided to take the route of making movement in this game deadly and at higher levels, a necessary weapon. But it only adds to the fluidity of the game if properly used. There are also numerous body upgrades you can swap out that, while not drastically changing your playstyle, will add to it in satisfying and noticeable ways. The final important gimmick to mention is a favorite of mine: bullet time. I'll just add that I'm happy they included it so you can see your destruction in glorious slow-mo while also providing a brief respite amidst combat chaos.

As a final note, I have to say that the demo truly does not do this game justice. I tried the demo and was on the fence of purchasing this until it went on sale as it felt like another average boomer shooter. The demo only superficially demonstrates the core game mechanics loop and fails to elaborate on all the wondrous points above since it's a demo. If you're looking for a game that's not so supremely complex like Ultrakill but hits that quake-like / doom feel of fluid combat, this won't disappoint. Hope this game somehow hits it big and the devs add more onto it, but right now it seems very underrated as a sleeper hit.
