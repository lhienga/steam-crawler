\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best retro-style shooter released this year, but it has its caveats. Its gameplay flow has more in common with Doom Eternal than something like DUSK, which you may have seen from other reviews, but it does have enough retro elements with its level design to somewhat warrant the label of "boomer shooter", particularly in the earlier levels. Weapons and upgrades are doled out at a brisk pace, so there's always a sense of progression and becoming more powerful. Your chainsaw leg is available from the get-go, however, and it is by far one of the most enjoyable aspects of the game. The story is...there. It's not particularly strong, but you're probably not coming to a game called Turbo Overkill for writing on par with God of War. It does become surprisingly prevalent in the second episode, and Gianni shows up, as is mandated by his eldritch contract to appear in all retro shooters, but it more or less serves as a backdrop to take you on your various fits of extreme violence. My main grievances with the game lie with the pacing of the last three levels, particularly the third to last and second to last. Throughout most of the game, the pacing of the level and encounter design maintains a delicate balance between providing unique scenarios that start out bonkers and escalate to really f***ing bonkers while knowing not to overstay their welcome. A mech section towards the middle of episode 3 is a standout moment. But the last three levels are long. Much longer than the rest of the game. By the time I was halfway through the third to last episode I was ready for the game to be over, but it gets dragged on longer than it needs to. The second to last level is a boss fight, and I found it to be quite enjoyable and paced just right. Until it had another phase. And then another. I won't go into specifics due to spoilers, but a specific weapon is needed to permanently damage it, and I found the method required to be finicky at best, and didn't always work when it was supposed to. Those two levels are really where most of my problems lie with the game, and only because they aren't as markedly excellent as the rest. Overall, Turbo Overkill has enough style and substance for any modern retro shooter, and it's impressive that such a small team could make something so meaty. Highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best FPS games of the last decade, hands-down.

It builds off of the formula that Doom Eternal started, of fast-moving combat set in large arenas with an emphasis on rapid switching of weapons and using your full arsenal to its max potential. But then it refines that formula to absolute perfection, and then crams so much more on-top of it. I almost don't want to say more because experiencing some of these moments for the first time is insane, but some of the levels and set pieces in this game will blow you away. The arsenal is nuts, with every gun having an alt-fire mode and usually multiple functions. Some of the highlights include a minigun that transforms into a flamethrower, a telefrag sniper rifle (yes you read that right), and an orbital ion beam cannon.

The weapon arsenal, enemy variety, and overall feel of the combat is perfection. It skips straight over that rough patch that Doom Eternal had early on when you don't have all the tools you need and are getting your ass handed to you, and instead goes straight into that amazing combat loop of flying around an arena like a madman blasting everything in your way.

And it's not a short game either. You get 3 full chapters with each level being utterly massive. I'd say its about a ~20 hour game if you're playing on a challenging skill for yourself and dying often. For $25 you're getting an excellent experience with plenty of replayability.

The only downside I can even think to come up with is that the story isn't too present, but honestly that's par for the course with these type of nu-throwback shooters. What story is there is entertaining, but is mostly just background dressing for your murder-spree. The VA however is excellent for every single character in the game.

Anyway, stop reading this review and go out and buy this. If you were a fan of Doom Eternal, you're going to absolutely love this since IMO it far surpasses DE. If you're just a general fan of FPS games, especially the boomer-shooter throwback variety, pick this up and give it a shot. I can guarantee you won't be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I rather like Turbo Overkill, It's quite enjoyable. However, it has been grossly overhyped in my humble opinion.
If you are easily impressed by surface-level gloss, and flashy, over-the-top mechanics, then I imagine you'll probably
be foaming at the mouth about as much as everybody else is with this game.

But for me, whilst I do indeed enjoy that flash and spectacle, Turbo Overkill is incredibly unfocused and rather
poorly balanced at times. It's hard to elaborate on those points without going into excruciating detail but to give you a vague idea of what I mean...

The game features 10 weapons, all of which have at least 1 unlockable alt-fire, and boy howdy does it not need all of that crap. The game has 3, (yes 3) fully automatic hit-scanning weapons. It has 4 if you want to count weapon alt fires, and whilst the game makes a loose effort to justify all of its arsenal's existence, ESPECIALLY by the late-game it's just not enough.

The game's augments (upgrades) system can become so hilariously broken that even the game's tankiest foes will get wiped off the face of the earth if you so much as sneeze on them. For example... you can stack Double Chegg, (an upgrade that gives you 2 chainsaw legs, doubling its damage) an upgrade that boosts your chainsaw leg damage against all enemies, an upgrade that boosts your chainsaw leg's damage against high-tier enemies, and that alone just melts anything in the game.
And most of the stuff you can upgrade in the game augment system can be broken like this.

Now the sum total of how the excess and questionable balancing impacts the overall experience is that it makes the game incredibly mindless at points. I don't end up using like half the abilities the game gives me because it's like... well I'm already brokenly overpowered... why do I need to be *more* brokenly overpowered? The game tries to counterbalance this by making the enemies glass cannons, but how quickly I die is not the problem...

The problem is that the combat in this game is akin to stepping on ants.
Ammo is treated like candy, weapons can become busted as hell and are just damage hoses
with little difference in terms of the roles they serve, again it's just... mindless,
and what little meat the game has gets stripped away by the time you reach episode 3.
I feel like I've not really done justice to my complaints, like again... without going into the nitty gritty
it is hard to properly convey the scope of these issues... but I imagine most people don't want to read
the equivalent of a wiki article.

Now I have plenty more I could say in terms of what I both love and don't care for in this game,
but there are plenty of other people kissing this game's posterior, saying the things I
would say, and most of my other grievances are significantly less important.

I would recommend it, it is good...
But it is far from being the best FPS game ever made or something.\t\t\t\t\t
