\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't normally write reviews, but this game is too damn good and too damn underrated for me to not write one. Turbo Overkill is an absolutely phenomenal game, and while it does have its issues, they're very minor or hardly affect the main gameplay at all. The gameplay itself is amazing, and if you want a good ass movement shooter like ULTRAKILL, this game is amazing. To summarize everything, the gameplay is amazing, the story is good(albeit a bit flawed, I still enjoyed it nonetheless), the mechanics are amazing, the graphics are straight up beautiful, enemies are fun to fight, and there's endless content in here! Literally, there's an endless mode, but there's also a level editor, secret levels and "tech chips"(basically cheats that you can activate) that you can unlock in every level, an infodex, custom difficulty which just got added, achievements, and a great campaign that's a blast to play and replay through. As of writing this review, I've unlocked 100% of the achievements, and I've got to say, Turbo Overkill nails what makes a good FPS fun! Highly recommend this underrated GEM of a game! My only issues are that the level editor can be a bit jank at sometimes(e.g. not allowing me to use the "save and play" button properly to playtest or freezing on the loading screen when trying to leave) and that parts of the story are a little lacking, but I can't really think of anything else wrong with it! BUY THIS GAME\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJesus Christ this is some impressive stuff.

In its best moments it can give Doom Eternal a run for its money,

The combat loop is excellent, you fly through the arenas at breakneck speed and use your whole arsenal all the time. None of the weapons and powers ever feel redundant and unnecessary, the balancing act is *chief's kiss*

The pacing and set pieces are *almost* always top-notch too: there is something sick and distinctive in every single level. Even the vehicle sections are totally amazing, the bike and the mech ones especially.

And it looks and sounds absolutely gorgeous, and it's mostly one guy project, are you kidding me?

9/10
===============================
I hated the last two levels tho. They are the definition of an overkill (*ba dum tss*).

The penultimate boss arena just drags on and on and on and on, way past the point of not caring. 12 stages. 12 gоddamn stages. With annoying platforming in between and Nihilanth-style teleportation to the separate arenas mid-fight. And *now* you decided to start punishing players for jumping-into-the-abyss, in the penultimate level, seriously?

And there is the final level, when the game out of nowhere presents the most annoying gimmick in the entire thing - time trial fights. I was screaming at the screen at that point. And there is the sensory overload endless final boss after that. Your cute companion S.A.M even complains about it in game. Guys, if you felt the need to lampshade it - maybe just tone it down a little bit, huh?

All of it left me exhausted in the end, and not the good kind of exhausted. And that a shame, the game this excellent deserved better send off than "thank god I'm done with it".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe pace of the combat in this one is absolutely off the rails. It feels like every mechanic straight-up breaks the game, and once you find your rhythm, it is incredibly addictive. After 11 hours it still feels like i haven't even scratched the surface of whats possible when it comes to play-styles and level routing.

8/10

Here's where the disclaimer comes in. The physics are not for everyone. Even some that liked DOOM 2016/Eternal might find them difficult to get a handle on. The sheer speed you have right from the beginning can be overwhelming. I could see people not making it through the first 5 hours due to the difficulty the speed alone creates. The last disclaimer is more of a nit pick, but every level seems to last too long (except the first couple). If they were 2/3 the length that would be perfect for me.

Over all its a great game if you are itching for a new fast-paced shooter, but be prepared to acclimate to the speed and play-styles you'll need to get good.

Just a side not, i wasn't sure if I would get used to this style of retro graphics, but they grew on me pretty quickly.\t\t\t\t\t
