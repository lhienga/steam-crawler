\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot at all a Boomer Shooter as many people have labelled it, but rather a different take on the nu-doom formula. The game starts off a little bit rough until it introduces more weapon mods and higher tier enemies, but once they do come in you have a game that is polished, challenging and extremely rewarding. Movement is fluid and responsive with large, circular arenas. Enemies are all immediately recognisable and have distinct weaknesses (and also utility to the player, like how the trash mobs much like in recent doom games are your resource pinatas). The weapons are all varied and every single one of them is useful throughout the entire game. The only major problem the game has is it's questionable boss fights that drag on for way longer than they should, but other than that it's an excellent alternative take on the formula of Doom 2016 and Eternal, and it comes pretty damn close to rivalling Eternal in terms of quality.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE LAST SET OF BOSSES AND THEIR COLLECTIVE 100 FREAKING FORMS CAN SUCK MY CYBERNETICALLY ENHANCED COCHLEA!!!

In short, first few levels are fun but after that, continuing this game started to feel like a chore, sucking out all the variety and action out of it.

Then the game starts feeling like a Doom Eternal clone that forgot to implement the glory-kill health mechanic as a constant feature. What is more, unlike Eternal, quite a few weapons seem tacked on or their mechanics just a gimmick. You will even feel compelled to bypass most of the spongy enemies (and in fact most of the game) by abusing the chainsaw slide with the according augments.

Enemies can pulverize you from off-screen in mere seconds.

Art Style seemed competent at first, but soon things just became a shiny neon mess, especially when equipping any powerup.

A Story is present...Sadly it gets dragged down by the horrendous overacting in the found audio logs, that try to be endearing like many cheesy 80s / 90s action flick but are just grating.
Oh and the final cutscene sets itself up for a big final choice, but abruptly ends on a cliffhanger. What makes it worse, it shows you that no matter how much effort you put into defeating any previous boss in the game or how heavily any character gets mutilated, nothing is of lasting consequence, the stakes are non present and they can simply bring back any anyone, especially the most annoying characters which should definitely have stayed dead!

But the Music is good!\t\t\t\t\t
