\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA stellar game. One of the first boomer shooters in a very long time to bring a fresh feeling into the genre, and the only FPS I've ever played where sliding is an actually interesting and useful movement mechanic. The gene splicing system is well implemented, though I imagine there's a clear meta to it (jet boosted chainsaw legs with regen is surely the only option), and I had the feeling that I had control over core parts of the experience.

You can also really see the dev(s) learning as they go. Act 1 is gives you solid fundamentals with a bit of experimentation, Act 2 is much more ambitious and therefore has higher highs and (vastly) lower lows, and Act 3 takes all the bad parts of Act 2 and refines them into something which really works. The game definitely hits its stride in Act 3 - a worthy comrade for some of my favourites from the genre, like Doom Eternal and Dusk.

All that makes the game's flaws more annoying, though I am nitpicking here. For example, the writing is pretty bad - normally that doesn't matter in a game like this, but the Act 2 bosses are the most cringe inducing characters since the cast of Borderlands 3. Speaking of the bosses, boomer shooters usually have issues implementing fun bosses and this is no different, the bosses in Act 2 being the lowest points in the game by far - cringe and a huge departure from what makes the rest of the game fun. Not ideal.

Similarly, I understand that "corruption" is there to provide unique fights in which the player has no choice but to use weapons they don't use much. That said, like with any shooter, not all weapons are created equal - some deserve to be used less because they're not very fun as far as preference is concerned. Limiting the player's options in such an arbitrary way reduces the fights to a gimmick, and that becomes incredibly frustrating on the highest difficulty (Murder Machine). Given that Act 2 is bloated with corruption fights and the aforementioned bossfights, it feels like the game very nearly loses its way at certain points.

Finally, there's some issues with what could've been my favourite gun: the Telefragger. It's such an awesome concept and is executed well. That said, it's tragic that the visual effects of charging the weapon up basically blind you, so hip-firing a charged blast just isn't viable. Feels like a massively wasted opportunity. Also, if its base fire was hitscan (or at least a little faster) then it would be a good long range option, akin to Quake Live's railgun. Sadly the projectile is just too slow, and combined with the visual effects of the charged shot it means this very cool weapon just gathered dust for most of my time playing.

Anyway, I had to get those silly little points off my chest. Like I said, the game is top-notch. The lows are far outweighed by the highs, and it succeeds where so many others in the genre have failed. I feel sorry for Prodeus being sold in a bundle with this game, because it's entirely eclipsed by the sheer fun Turbo Overkill has to offer. Give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
