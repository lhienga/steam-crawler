\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, I cannot recommend this. It is fun at times, but usually extremely frustrating and relentlessly difficult. Ion Fury is a far, far better example of this kind of game.

The game has great premise and starts well. Unfortunately, it just descends into an overwhelmingly difficult and frustrating shooter. This game is hard. Very, very, very hard. As you move into the second episode, the game removes all of the interesting exploration type gameplay that we experienced earlier and just descends into relentless, seemingly never ending fights against very difficult enemies. When you have finally finished one, another starts just a few minutes later. You need time to take a break!

When you have just about got through one wave of enemies, another, unexpected wave comes with far more difficult enemies and you have already used up your ammunition and are out of health and armour. It is a constant slog to try to get through each part with a little more health to get to the next stage. Once, I finished a very hard end to a level with multiple stages but the game bugged so I could not proceed and had to reload and do it all over again.

The game is extremely long - too long for the play style. The interesting aspects of cyberpunk and upgrades are just completely lost. I have now played it for about 16 gruelling hours and have only just made it to the end of the second episode (out of three in total).

The game makes an extensive use of platforming and it is often not clear where you need to go. You also need to be very accurate, so progressing in these levels requires a frustrating amount of trial and error.

The final straw was the second large boss battle. You cannot save during boss battles, which I personally do not like. There is a bug where I have just experienced instant death, multiple times, even with 150 health points. The game, mid gameplay, just ends and you die. You then have to start the whole process again. After about the tenth attempt, and getting almost to the end before this happened, I just stopped. I have had enough. I could not face it any more and all enjoyment had long since gone.

Ultimately, the considerable difficulty, unrelentingly unfair quantity of enemies, and overlong nature just combine to outweigh the positives. I really, really wanted to like this which is why I played it for so long, but, ultimately, it is just far too hard and far too unbalanced. You do not want to continually feel overwhelmed!

There is one notable exception which must be made. There is a level where you are riding a bike and this is wonderful. Truly one of the best experiences I have had while playing a game. Unfortunately this, on its own. is not enough to save the whole game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best "modern retro" FPS games I've played so far. The game is not holding back and gives you all the tools to go completely berserk. Movement is extremely fast and smooth, you're jumping, dashing and zooming through the level, if not slashing through lines of enemies with your chainsaw leg.

Levels are nice and varied, ranging from tight corridors to open futuristic cityscapes. Enemies are not that much of a challenge on their own, although there are some tankier ones. It's the arena style battles that can get pretty challenging, where hordes of enemies come at you in different formations.

The guns are nice although the double barreled shotgun feels a bit useless compared to the other guns and the rocket launcher is pretty clunky. It generally does not really matter which gun you use, which means you can take every fight the way you want. A little more game design could have been put into that, as in the new Doom games. It's still a lot of fun to wreak havoc with this arsenal.

It's clear the developers don't take the game too seriously and want to deliver the most wacky, fun experience they can. I think they succeeded at that.\t\t\t\t\t
