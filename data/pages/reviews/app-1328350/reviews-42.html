Best Retro Shooter I ever played!
Turbo Overkill is secretly a triple A game with its own original story set in a cyberpunk world which contains:
-A lot of level variety from small to large levels. Each level took about a half hour to complete with the exception of boss fights. It took 16 hours to complete the whole game which is split into 3 episodes.
-Awesome soundtrack for each level with their own theme. (Cyberpunk themed music with rock, electronic, tech/synth beats)
-Vehicle based levels where you get to drive a flying car, hover bike and a mech!
-Fluid parkour movement with augments that lets you customize how you play. (slide, wall running, double/triple jumping, grappling hook, air strafing)
-A lot of enemy variety and hard, challenging boss fights. (You will feel real adrenaline during the fights)
-It has reply value because of steam achievements and hidden collectibles to collect through out each level.

The only bad things about this game are:
-found some minor bugs where texture fighting was going on
-there are way too many enemies which can be quite overwhelming, especially the weak mobs placed on every single platform and area that you gonna step on. You also gonna have to fight the exact same type of enemies which are hard to kill and be like "oh no! not these guys again >_>"
-the shotguns do not feel powerful compared to other games.
-on some levels and areas you can get out of bounds.
edit* -endless mode is bugged at wave 17 with an unkillable kite enemy.

If you played and liked games liked Dusk, Doom Eternal, Bioshock Infinite, Halo 4 then you will like this one too. It has many similarities to these games and it feels like playing all of them as one! It is absolutely amazing and I highly recommend it if you want to experience being THE BADASS CYBERNETIC CHAINSAW MAN\t\t\t\t\t
