\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTook a while to get going, but it was worth it.

During Episode 1, the gameplay is nothing special. When Episode 2 rolls around and you've unlocked most of the weapons/mechanics and the levels occasionally force you to fight in arenas, that's where the game picks up. That's when it starts to feel like Doom Eternal gameplay. Otherwise, it's too easy to just kite/funnel enemies and kill them without much difficulty.

I will say that the Chainsaw dash is also a little OP, especially when you've unlocked the perks to get armor and health per kill. With no real limit/cooldown to using the Chainsaw dash, it was a little too easy to fly around the map killing dozens of trash mobs and getting health and armor. In many cases, it was more beneficial than using my weapons. I feel it should be another tool in the arsenal, but not a primary means of offense.

The music also became noise after a while. It's always going at max intensity even when there's no action going on. Eventually I had to mute it and play my own music.

What did me in was a bug I encountered in the final series of boss fights where the Cyber Maw boss stopped spawning after reloading a checkpoint -resulting in a progress blocker. I didn't care enough to restart the whole level to fight the previous bosses again just to get my progress back, felt like I saw enough of the game at that point. I might replay the game down the line when the game has less bugs.

Despite my complaints, there is a lot of fun to be had here. When the game is at its highs, its insanely addicting. It is clear the amount of effort that was put into making the game fun. The game also has a lot of charm, humor, and homages to 80s/90s content.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCyberpunk perfection. A lot of this game is so well done that its hard to believe all of it comes together. The music, gameplay, and the striking visuals in each map is an excellent thing to witness. I have some minor issues with the game, but nothing crazy. However, the big issue I have with this game is the difficulty. Playing through the first two episodes when the game was 1.0 was a pretty smooth ride, maybe a bit too easy on UV difficulty. Waited a bit for any issues to be ironed out before playing through episode 3. I fired up episode 3, and was met with an entirely different game completely. If you seen videos of this game before these patches, I assure you they were not playing the same game I was.

Look, Turbo Overkill is a pretty generous game with checkpoints and allowing you to quick save most of the time, but the game turned into one shot simulator really quick. The last couple of levels were really awesome but tipping the scale into ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ kaizo status. Almost every high tier enemy has the ability to one shot you, hell a random projectile from a mid tier enemy could instantly kill you, full to dead. Some of the combat scenarios had some of these enemies in hard to reach spots that dying was pretty much a common occurrence. Now this game has a lot of tools to kind of mitigate and get around the difficulty, but man, the game doesn't have great audio direction for some of these abilities. You will die and asking your self many times "what the hell killed me." I also got hit with a spear from a enemy you can't even see with the fog on. If this is the intended experience, I respect the hell out of it, but I don't see a whole lot of people pushing through what I did on UV or above. I love challenging games, but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ I was praying that I wasn't walking around a corner into a one shot ability after a long multi phase boss so I don't have to go through that again. It felt like I had to play perfect or I was not beating some sections of the game.

Damn, what a game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this is the best first-person shooter (FPS) game I've played in a while, and I've played a lot. I'm a sucker for any kind of cyberpunk-themed game, but I've been playing others of late and "boomer shooters" more generally (Deadlink, W40k: Boltgun, Trepang2, Sprawl, etc.) and not having nearly as much fun as with Turbo Overkill (TO). I've seen some posts about how it's the love-child of one developer, and I have no idea whether that's true because this is better than most of the AAA FPS I've played of late.

Seriously, the mechanics never cease to be fun, the weapons are well thought out and fun to use, the alternate fire modes are often just as glorious and inspired as the original Unreal Tournament, the movement mechanics are fun without turning into a boring wall-running simulator (I'm looking at you, Sprawl), ... I just never fail to have a great time with this. Now I'm only about 5 hours into it, so I've yet to see everything, but just when I thought I had done everything the game has to throw at me, I get in Johnny's car and start mowing down EVERYTHING in sight!!! Now I'm flying around this crazy beautiful neon nightmare cyberpunk metropolis in some crazy level and don't even want it to end.

Unless you downright HATE this type of game, or you're one of those players who can't stand anything that doesn't have the most bleeding-edge or extremely realistic graphics imaginable, then I do not see how you will fail to get your money's worth out of TO. This thing is an absolute gem, and I will be following this developer closely going forward, likely buying everything he turns out.\t\t\t\t\t
