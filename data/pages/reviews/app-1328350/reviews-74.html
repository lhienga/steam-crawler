\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's alright; if you're looking for a high octane zoomer shooter that stands out from the rest with a new aesthetic, then this is for you. Enjoy chainsaw sliding folks to death. Personally, the lustre kind of diminishes with time and I was more just happy it was over by the end.

I take issue with the writing and boss fights. Cyberpunk stories need to ask more interesting questions than "are you still human if you have prosthetics", which isn't so much as treading old ground than it is pushing yourself down a highway by licking it. I do appreciate that Turbo Overkill does have a strong(ish) story focus with cutscenes having you arrive at a location and more as well as voiced characters that do things and have independent goals and relationships. I am a bit miffed that a lot of intriguing details are left in audio logs; this is another case of too much lore and not enough story. There are several characters who exist only in these logs who I find very intriguing and who I wish I could've seen in the campaign proper, even if their existence boils down to "stop sacrificing your humanity for prosthetics". I have the sneaking suspicion that elements of the Doc's audio logs constitute medical malpractice when the story is trying to make him come off as the reasonable one in a world gone mad.

I love Ripper, though. She's such a fun antagonist. She's almost entirely unpredictable and I have no idea what her deal is. Either she's carrying a torch for Johnny and murder is her love language, or she actually does hate his guts and feels like one death wasn't enough for him. I have no idea what her priorities are and I love her.

Having a resource loop dependant on killing enemies is cool except it takes away from the ability to make fun one-on-one boss fights, and almost all of Turbo Overkill's pit you against what I will admit are awesome bosses with cool mechanics and unique moves surrounded by the usual batch of enemies that I've already mowed down thousands of on the way there. C'mon; give me a good 1v1. I've earned it. Stop distracting me with these nongs.

There is one enemy that really burns my toast: during the third episode, clones of the second episode's final boss will attack you. They are about as strong but with less health; they have one fast attack that strips off most of your health in a single hit, leaving you to die at the second. That's not the worst part, however; is worst part is that they spawn ages after the last checkpoint. If they kill you (and they likely will because they're very good at that), you have to go through that garbage all over again. Synthetica Square would've been my favourite level of the third episode but it's the worst because of those blasted Aberrations. The tragic thing is that this could've been averted if the hardest difficulty allowed you to Quick Save but you can't, as if Murder Machine wasn't hard enough. For a game that otherwise is very generous with checkpoints, this Aberration nonsense spoils so much.\t\t\t\t\t
