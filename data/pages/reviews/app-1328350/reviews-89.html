\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTurbo Overkill is the best shooter we've gotten since Doom: Eternal without any exaggeration.

For a year so stacked to the brim full of amazing games this might be the one that will likely fly under several people's radars in sharing a release month with Baldur's Gate and Armored Core 6, but it's a wildly solid release that I recommend to anyone who's a fan of shooters.

Great set pieces, fun weapons, great enemy variety, and all the trappings that one might expect of a solid single-player FPS experience. The story is charming in it's own right, and the voice acting is also surprisingly solid for an indie title.

This game is marketed somewhat like a traditional 'boomer shooter', and it is in a sense of aesthetic. It closely follows the schematic of what made Doom: Eternal such a solid entry in the genre, though. Extremely good air control and movement mechanics (air dashes and momentum based sliding that can slingshot you around the arenas), multi-function weapons, light customization, fun cooldowns, and a somewhat looser 'Speed Chess' gameplay loop that id initially came up with for Eternal.

This game is by far not 'nearly' as restrictive as Eternal was, but that doesn't make the game any less fun. It allows for a greater sense of traditional player expression, and by extension may have a more broad appeal because of how divisive Eternal's initial release was.

Turbo Overkill is well worth the asking price, and gets my strong recommendation for anyone who likes FPS games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnly 3 hours in, and wow. Spotted a review of the game the day after the 1.0 release on a website, and the description of "Doom Eternal meets Cyberpunk" had me hooked from the get go.

I am absolutely loving the music, it's right up my alley. The graphics are good, real good. Pretty, but not taxing but man do I love the aesthetic. The guns are are good, the upgrades to your body are good, when enemies dies they turn into a nice bloody pulp. So many different ways to kill things, but by far my favorite is the chainsaw leg slide,

SO. MUCH. FUN! just sliding everywhere chainsawing enemies left and right, even the big tough ones aren't safe from your leg saw.

And I'm only the 3rd level in ... I know there's MORE guns and fun waiting for me!

My only gripe, and it's a minor one I feel, is the lack of a map, some of the heights I've gotten too and accidentally fallen have made retracing my steps to get back up ... interesting. Not impossible, and not a deal breaker by any stretch of the imagination.

Can't recommend this game enough though, it's SO good!

I picked it up on sale, but it's WELL worth the full price they're asking for!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a Cyberpunk First Person Shooter where you can chainsaw slide through hordes of enemies, shoot rockets and grapple on them, Telefrag into enemies to taverse the map, become Chainsaw Man and and throw enemy (and your own) prjectiles back at them.
I could leave it at that and would just recommend you either buying the game or checking out the Demo!

But I won't, cause there is so much more to love about this game.
First off, 24 Levels (AND THE SAME AMOUNT OF SECRET LEVELS), each of them visually diverse in different environments. It's all Cyberpunk Themed, you travel through severs, over rooftop, a toxic wasteland, through a powerplant and later on even in space.
The levels are overall really well paced, you got mostly swift and fast combat encounters, some bigger and longer arenas that are broken up with some platforming section which never last too long.
The combat is really tight. You got over 9 weapons, each of them with purpose, there is nothign that doesn't feel useles, except maybe the Minigun at times, but even that one has a super good Alternate Firemode.
Most enemies are running around shooting at you and while their behaviour doesn't overall change, the variety comes from the projectiles they throw at you. Some projectiles are threatening for different reasons, which is somethign you gonna particularily feel on Murder Machine, the highest difficulty where you end up dead in less than 2 seconds for some enemies.
I spent most of the time playing on Murder MAchien Difficulty with Triple Enemies just for the fun of it.
That Soundtrack is also top notch, though it really start bopping starting from Episode 2.

Anything else?
Oh yeah, there is also and Endless Mode, which is great fun and a Level Editor!
This game is through and through a full experience, and one of the most fun shooters around.

My personal GOTY in a year where ACVI comes out and actually has to match this.
