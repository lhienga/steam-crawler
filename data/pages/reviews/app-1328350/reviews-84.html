\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMost of the bad things other negative reviews pointed out (bloated levels, redundant weapons, etc) are a result of this game having absolutely no ideas of its own.

Outside of the chainsaw leg, you've seen everything Turbo Overkill has to offer before (and done better by other games). I don't have a problem with derivative work generally but this game is so bloated with it that it becomes more distracting than enjoyable. Whether it's the set pieces or the weapons, it's all stuff you've seen in Bioshock, Borderlands, Half-Life, Doom Eternal, Titanfall, ULTRAKILL, Halo, etc etc.

Speaking of bloat, the latter half of the game is full of it. Tedious "destroy x number of objectives to progress" mission structure is used to lazily pad out the levels all while you get to suffer through uninspired Borderlands 3-tier writing as you do it. Even the dialogue in the final few levels is something you've probably heard before if you've ever watched a sci-fi/cyberpunk film before. By the end I just didn't really care about what was going on anymore.

Turbo Overkill is in the vein of overall mediocre shooters like Prodeus and POSTAL: Brain Damaged where it's an ok enough game but ultimately pretty forgettable. These games are being massively carried by the current hype/wave surrounding indie 'retro' shooters and once that fades people will most likely see in retrospect that these games were very average and didn't actually contribute much to the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
