yeah it's pretty good

if you're looking for a good boomer shooter this is it. turbo overkill is a ridiculously unhinged blood-and-guts romp that keeps you playing. it’s not perfect by any stretch of the word, but it shines where it counts and at least seems aware of its flaws to some extent. absolutely pick it up if you’re interested at all.

with that being said, i have opinions about this game.

i think my biggest gripe with this game by far is its balancing. you're given ten whole-ass weapons (on top of your chainsaw leg), each with their own alt-fires and upgrades and techs. this, on the surface, sounds dope as ♥♥♥♥; you're never strapped for options in any fight and you get a wide variety of tools at your disposal. the problem with this is that of the ten the game gave me, i only really used three.

the chainsaw leg is too good. it's a no-cooldown, momentum-building behemoth of a weapon that just melts crowds of squishies. this is exacerbated by the upgrade system, which allows you to double its damage as well as gain health + armor for each kill you get with it. this, in effect, almost completely invalidates half of the weapons in the game. assuming your double damage leg isn't enough to take on stronger enemies on its own, the sawed-off shotgun cleans them up instantly. you're able to become this disgusting vampiric semitruck that just bulldozes through levels, and it doesn't take long for you to reach this point. that's not even counting the late-game chainsaw arms, which (while fulfilling my chainsaw man power fantasy), only further reward this playstyle, giving you another semitruck option fuelled by... kills with the chainsaw leg.

on the one hand, this is cool. it feels awesome tearing through clouds of enemies, the chainsaw leg making that satisfying "bzzzzt" sound every time. on the other hand, though, this rends the game a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cakewalk, and so many awesome weapons are just completely invalidated by this strategy. the telefragger is a sniper rifle which, when charged, teleports you to your enemy and telefrags them. that's so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cool. however, excluding some options where the map design forces you to use it, i never got much use out of it. it's got an enormous windup, you have to stay still to charge it, and it only kills one enemy. i could get the same result by just grappling to the enemy and pressing ctrl. there are tons of guns that fall into this trap: plasma cannons that allow you to catch mid-air projectiles, flamethrower chainguns, orbital strike cannons. all overshadowed by a shotgun and a leg.

the pistols are a really pleasant exception to this, because they actually scale later on in the game. you get a powerup in the second act which reworks them entirely: should you hit ten or so consecutive shots in a row, your pistols become supercharged and deal thousands of damage per shot, the effect only wearing off once you miss or switch weapons. this, coupled with the bullet time upgrade you get later on, is actually comparable to shotgun-chainsawing, and encourages you to switch up your strategy. it was a new and exciting way to play, instead massively rewarding consecutive single-shot accuracy as opposed to raw speed and manoeuvrability.

i wish the other weapons had reworks like this. unique mechanics that gave you huge rewards for switching up your playstyle, encouraging you to switch weapons even if you're not out of shotgun ammo. as it stands, however, i have yet to find a practical application of the plasma cannon. i think this game would really benefit from a style meter. you don't have to go full ULTRAKILL, but an incentive to switch weapons/playstyles beyond a self-imposed handicap would be nice.

and before you tell me to just play on murder machine, i did! this game's approach to difficulty, however, doesn't change my criticisms one bit. i found myself still being rewarded for chainsaw-shotgunning, the only difference being i would occasionally get one-shot by one specific type of enemy (who would have one-shotted me regardless). if anything, higher difficulties actually reward this playstyle more; you need to move more quickly, preserve health and armor and deal with squishies before they deal massive damage. the telefragger doesn't exactly sound like a useful weapon there.

it's really good and you should get it, but damn i hope there's a balance patch at some point.\t\t\t\t\t

Gameplay mix of Titanfall and Doom Eternal, cyberpunk setting, Shodan as a main villain, over the top weapons, chainsaw leg(s....and arms), satisfying gory killing spree mixed with fun platforming sections, varied missions and environments, top notch soundtrack, and simply perfectly entertaining gameplay loop, where after one or two missions I had to make brakes because of how intense and exhausting it always became... in a good way. But then the final two missions came. And it suddenly became exhausting... in a bad way. Why did they have to end it with these incredibly tedious boss fights, where you have to kill not only one, but two bosses multiple times. It simply dragged way too long, and instead of ending with a bang, it ended with a disappointment. Nevertheless, it is till one incredibly good game. I am just disappointed that I can't say that it is.... a pure perfection.\t\t\t\t\t
