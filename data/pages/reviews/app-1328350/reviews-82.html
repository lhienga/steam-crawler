  • Awesome movement with double jump and dash, slide, air control and grappling hook.
  • You need to manage health and armor by killing enemies and picking up items around. There are some weapon and character upgrades in non overwhelming amount. Combat is challenging on higher difficulties, boss fight can be tough but fair, it greatly improved since early access.
  • 9 weapons with alternate fire that can be selected at any time plus 2 special abbilities. Some weapons or mods are pretty unique, some are standart for such shooters. Also powerup pickups. Weapons switch fast, sound good and there is also quick swap in place.

  • Music is a mix of different electronic genres, rock and orchestra. Some pieces reminds me of that Mick Gordon's "alive" electronic sounds and it's great. There is official OST release. Sound design and voiceover is on point.
  • Graphics could be better in terms of texture details but art style and animations did the job. Enemies and pickups don't blend with environment, UI is easy and readable.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, so, so good. The gameplay is smooth, the guns feel great, the movement is fluid. I have not dumped this much time into a game over such a short time for a while. Every weapon has it's place and doesn't feel underutilised. The music, graphics and gameplay add up to be my new favourite shooter.
However, I do have a couple of issues:
It is incredibly easy to skip cutscenes accidentally. You'll be holding a movement key and accidentally skip a cutscene before you realise you even are in one. To remedy this I'd suggest changing which keys can skip a cutscene, or increase the time it takes to skip.
Finding the keys for the modification bins doesn't seem worth the hassle for now, with a lot of good ones being able to be purchased from the shop as opposed to looking for the keys.

This is still a incredible game even with those issues, and I would definitely recommend buying it.

P.S. If you put Carpenter Brut on in the background, the game gets even better.\t\t\t\t\t
