\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an amazing Boomer shooter with awesome Cyberpunk setting. The music is superb. The movement provides many options. The progress of unlocking new abilities and upgrades is really motivating.

The visual set pieces made my jaw drop time and time again. The shooting is fun as hell with great amounts of cyber gore.

The achievements are fun to unlock while there is a somewhat lengthy campaign. If you select all tech chips in a level, you can unlock togglable cheats. If you select all cassettes in a level, you get an extra creative secret level per level. That's some nice amount of content right there.

On top of all that there is also an endless mode which provides even more hours to be played.

The story being told in the campaign is much more gripping and provides more personality to the main character and his antagonists than any of the Quake games.

All in all I can whole-heartedly recommend this gem of game to any FPS lover.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTwitchy, fast paced movement shooter with a chunky retro cyberpunk aesthetic. It's up there with Ghostrunner and Titan Fall 2 for me as far as movement shooters go.

Fun gun and character abilities and upgrades, especially some of the late game unlocks that greatly change how you can move through a level or just have more chainsaws. Gory and violent with some of the more memorable, straight to the action level opening cut-scenes I've seen in awhile. Quality voice acting that really brings the characters and bosses to life. Collectibles are rewarding to explore for and are generally easy to find. Huge replay-ability, one unlockable secret level per story level, co-op, wave survival game modes, and custom community maps through the workshop. Cannot recommend enough.

Oh and Johnny Turbo? Yeah, that guy ♥♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlay as Johnny Turbo and saw, grind, blast your enemies to hell as you battle the evil AI bent on dominating this cyberpunk dystopia. Turbo Overkill has creative level design, creative gunplay, and creative visuals that all mesh together to make a great game. You have CHAINSAWS on your LEGS!

Design
The level design is awesome, the game keeps introducing new features, combinations, or layouts to keep things fresh. On some levels you get level-unique vehicles such as hover cars or motorbikes. The boss fights are great, they are engaging but never unfair. I love the use of leitmotif in the game. The music is great, fits the theme and augments the brutal gameplay.

Weapons
The progression is also great, with new weapons and tools being introduced to you throughout the game, as well as upgrades for existing weapons. The weapons are creative too, your starting pistols lock on to and splatter groups of enemies, your first shotgun can launch an electric stun blast, a gun can call in a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ orbital strike laser, and you get a sniper rifle that telefrags enemies.

Upgrades
There is a plethora of creative upgrades that you can customise to fit your playstyle. Some can even change your playstyle alltogether. I was very happy late in the game when I got an upgrade that allowed near-instant weapon quickswapping for some nasty combos (however it may be a liiiiittle overpowered in that it allowed me to ♥♥♥♥ out rockets at a faster rate than a chaingun, but who cares it's a blast)

Story
The story is pretty simple. I didn't expect to like it, but I actually enjoyed it quite a lot. Each level starts with a cutscene that characterises the crazy badass that is Johnny Turbo. I grew attached to these scenes, your AI companion, and the big story changes at the end of each chapter.

Conclusion
Overall a fantastic zoomer shooter... I heard people are calling these zoomer shooters nowadays? Overall Turbo Overkill sits slightly above Doom Eternal and slightly below Ultrakill in fun factor for me at the moment.\t\t\t\t\t
