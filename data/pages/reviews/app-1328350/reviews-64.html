\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty fun and polished for a fast paced shooter.
On the technical side, the game ran well and I encountered very few bugs: in a few cases I got stuck in the level geometry, but then a failsafe teleported me back to a safe spot after a few seconds, and in another case I got softlocked by a broken level trigger but reloading the last checkpoint fixed it.
All the weapons are fairly useful and interesting, and they can be combined in creative ways instead of the extremely boring "use A on B" seen in Doom Eternal.
Enemy variety is pretty good, even the lowest tiers are different enough to require a bit of attention.
The levels also aren't bad, but you have so much mobility that it's easy to sequence break by accident and in some cases skip past large chunks of them.
Secrets aren't terrible, no wall humping required in this game, but given the sheer size of most levels they can be pretty tedious to find even when no attempt is made to hide them: in some cases, the opposite is true and you'll just walk into a "secret" by looking to your side as you follow the intended path.
Difficulty is odd: I was playing on Street Cleaner (the middle of the 5 difficulty levels), and around the halfway point the game went from "very easy" to "very hard", with plenty of attacks dealing 300+ damage when the most you can stack is 200 health and 200 armor.
At least, the augments you collect in-game and the mutators you unlock by finding secrets offer plenty of ways to fine-tune the difficulty level: for example, you can enable infinite ammo and instagib to breeze through even the hardest difficulty, or disable all weapons but your chainsaw leg and triple enemy spawns to turn even the easiest difficulty into a bit of a challenge.
Finally, there's a level editor and that's always nice.

Worth playing, even at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom start to finish this game never slows down, i don't think i have ever played an arena shooter with such a break neck pace. Every chapter the game gets bigger, better, more elaborate, its truly impressive just how much this game offers especially for the price.

Combat is about as much as you would expect for a modern arena shooter. Fast paced movement, dashing around, avoiding several hundred projectiles. The movement is silky smooth, dashing, sliding around on chainsaw legs, grappling enemies for a boost, teleporting to nodes, its all incredibly fluid and never turns into a frantic mess.

The arsenal is here, and it SLAPS. I'm still amazed people can come up with such new ideas in a genre thats as old as i am. You have your classics, the pump shotgun, super shotgun, plasma gun, BFG-like, but they all feel new as you progress. Chapter 1 is probably the weakest chapter, not because its bad but because it doesn't offer the same arsenal you get by the end of chapter 2. Throughout the game you'll get coins to buy and upgrade, turning weapons that may become obsolete in others games, into powerhouses that can still offer alot of damage into the late game.

The only negatives i have really are the incredible difficulty spikes on anything other than normal. Chapter 1 on the 2nd highest difficulty was a challenge, but a fair one. Chapter 2 adds new enemies that will insta-gib you out of nowhere with little to no feedback as to where it came from, from whom, it gets very frustrating to go from actual 100 (max armor too) to 0 in a blink of the eye. Although i enjoyed my time with the game, its a VERY long arena shooter. My limit for games like these is around 10 hours, which is usually about the time these kind of games starts to wrap up anyways. Turbo is a bit longer, but you feel that extra 2-3 hours.

Apogee deserve alot of credit for defining an era of games. Turbo Overkill 100% WILL blow up from the likes of Civvie11 and other boomer shooter tubers. Its only a matter of time. Good Hunting apooge, glad to see you back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTurbo. Chainsaw. Leg.

Turbo Overkill is an excellent modern first person shooter. A member of the twitchy shooter club, it is a fast paced game that encourages constant movement and weapon switching, while providing excellent environments where to do so, with an outstanding weapon rooster, and good enemy design (with some caveats here and there).

The music is overall excellent! The composition and progression throughout the game is just great, perfectly in tune with the game themes, and pumps you up just right. The music sounds production (that is, the mixing and post production) is also outstanding, making the whole thing very cohesive. The same goes for the sound design; weapons sound are very nicely done, and so are the enemy, environmental, and voices for the most part. I will note that there is some recurring "sound stacking" bugs with coins and some specific sounds (remember BUILD Engine sound stacking bugs? Exactly like that) that do need to be addressed, because they can occasionally hurt :(.

The game graphics are also beautiful; the levels themselves are colourful and very detailed, and the backdrops, set pieces and in game cinematics also show a great level of care into making sure that everything is detailed and pretty, but still allowing the player to (mostly) understand what to do and where to go at all times. Fittingly, the level design is amazingly well done; they are giant environments, and even with the usual trope of keys and backtracking, still make for immersive set pieces that flow really well.

Your movement options are very versatile and overall quick; sliding (with a side of chainsaw), dashing, multiple jumping, and grapple hook, all of which encourage constant and rapid movement, both of which are 100% required to survive basically all the time. Everything you could want out of a modern FPS is there, and done very well (except for the starting delay of the grapple hook which I just hate ¬¬). It reminded me of ULTRAKILL at some points, but here, the flying around shooting things and looking awesome is done both out of necessity, and just pleasure :P.

The weapon rooster is just great; every single type of weapon that was ever done in an FPS is included in it (except for a BFG I guess). You get 10 slots overall, plus extra modes for each which almost gets you 20, and despite the quantity, all are implement solidly. Mostly every type of play is accommodated within it, and while enemies have "weaknesses" that encourage using specific weapons, you can still do your own thing if you want. On that note, the implant upgrade system is nice; it allows to bolster specific skills and movement options, but with limited slots, forcing you to choose specific ones depending on what you favour the most.

The enemy design is pretty nice too; graphic wise, all look amazing, and fit a function within battles and overall in the game. Johnny is very much a glass cannon; the game makes it clear that movement is required at all times, but it will occasionally thread over the line of cheapness, rather than challenge, and this is probably my main and only complaint about the game: The cheap instakills.

A single enemy can just wreck you with some of their attacks, and the game will repeatedly throw hand fulls of them at you at some points, along with buckets of common enemies. On Streetcleaner difficulty, I counted 3XX damage from a SINGLE attack that left me alive with 4 health from full health and armor (and some seem to do even more than that!), and even lower enemies can just take 1XX health/armor in a single swipe. In all cases, there seems to be some "random" variation of damage; after some out of nowhere deaths that pissed me off, I stopped to probe damage from single enemies, and found that sometimes they would do 20 damage, and sometimes 150, with no clear indication of why. The solution was basically to get good and not get hit at all, but as noted, it felt pretty cheap at some points. I do not want to think how bad this would be on the higher difficulties :(.

That said, I do not think this overshadows all the good things the game is and does at all; Turbo Overkill is an outstanding example of a modern day FPS that implements basically everything we have learned about these kinds of games throughout the years, and does so well.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best modern shooter you can find. Certainly has the best movement in an FPS I've ever experienced. Double jump, triple jump, sliding with a chainsaw that deals damage to enemies, sliding midair, wall-jumping, wall-running, grapple hook onto environments and onto enemies, rocket and shotgun jumping, and using a sniper rifle that teleport you to enemies. This game has is it ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ all.

Levels beside being well designed, have all these gorgeous backdrops and vistas to look at, and progressively get more aesthetically absurd and just insane to play. They're also populated by an incredibly diverse set of enemies and mixed with both challenging arenas and just fun enemy encounters. Even the bosses are great and memorable, and all the weaponry you find feel sooo good to use.

Great game and a guaranteed good time.\t\t\t\t\t
