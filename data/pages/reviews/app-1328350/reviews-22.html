\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginally I posted a negative review, but honestly the game is so close to being perfect that I couldn't let it stand. I'll definitely touch on the flaws but first let's talk about the positives.

Pros
  • Maps are highly detailed, feature gorgeous lighting, and generally strike a good balance between traversal and combat sections. Not to mention the awesome weather/ambient effects which help to set the tone for each map. TLDR, maps look great and play great.

  • The core movement is insanely good. It's highly responsive and allows for actions to be chained together seamlessly, and there's loads of neat movement tech to learn as you continue playing.

  • Enemies for the most part are fun to fight and have unique roles during combat to ensure the player is always on the move. Really my only gripe with them is that some are pretty quiet and/or lack sound effects you've come to expect from other FPS games (wakeup sound, roaming sound, etc.).

  • Weapons range from practical to over the top, and each features a unique alt fire. If that wasn't enough, every weapon in the game can be upgraded twice at various terminals throughout the campaign. Upgrades usually provide passive buffs or unique alternate fire modes which can turn even the weakest of weapons into an absolute powerhouse if used correctly.

  • THE MUSIC... that is all. Seriously though, the soundtrack for this game absolutely slaps, and is easily one of the best OSTs to have ever been created.

  • Player upgrades, and the upgrade system for that matter, are generally well thought out. Instead of a skill tree, you gain access to "Augments" which can only be equipped on certain parts of the player's body, and they usually only provide passive buffs to abilities you already have access to. Progression wise, they're dulled out either by finding a secret key and unlocking a crate within a level, or by purchasing them at vending machines scattered around. Aside from the in game currency and limited amount of augment slots, some augments won't appear until certain sections of the game, and some are only unlocked by beating the game. All in all I think the system works pretty well to ensure the player isn't OP but is still able to make progress towards their preferred playstyle.

  • The story manages to be both serious and lighthearted at the same time, and is one of the few times I've actively found myself not skipping cut-scenes just to see what insane crap happens next. There's lots of fun references to classic FPS games & famous action films which will definitely give you a good laugh and fill you with nostalgia, but there are also quite a few times where I feel genuine emotion for the protagonist and some of the side characters as the story progresses. I'm not saying it's the greatest story every written, but it's suitably cheesy for the kind of game this is while still managing to be engaging and captivating.

Cons
  • Difficulty balancing was and still is a huge issue. In Early Access, the hardest difficulty "Murder Machine" was incredibly easy, with only a handful of attacks doing any meaningful damage while the rest did next to nothing. Now the difficulty does indeed live up to it's name, but you're the one getting murdered, machine! Granted it is a joke difficulty, but good luck trying to find any enjoyment when 70% of the enemies in the game are able to kill you in one hit, with explosive & melee focused enemies being the worst offenders. The thing is that the next highest difficulty, Serve Me Pain, is far too easy... and even then there are some enemies that can come out of nowhere and instakill you. Thankfully there is a custom difficulty option, and it is by far the most enjoyable way to experience the game imo, but in all honestly it feels like a band aid solution for what is arguably one of the most horribly balanced games I've played in recent memory.

Honestly if the difficulty balancing wasn't so dang bad, this would be in my Top 5 FPS games of all time, but even still every other aspect of the game is so incredibly well honed that I can somewhat overlook it (definitely helps that there's a custom difficulty option now). There are some nitpicks I have still like not all enemies having unique sound effects for waking up etc., or how only a handful of augments are genuinely useful while the rest are gimmicky, but other than that the difficulty balancing is really the only thing I dislike about this game. I would hope that over time more adjustments are made to how damage is calculated, but regardless I definitely think this is a game every FPS fan should experience for themselves.\t\t\t\t\t
