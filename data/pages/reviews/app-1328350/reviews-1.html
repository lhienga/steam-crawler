\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game after finishing DUSK and Quake 1. It holds up, one of the best retro shooters I've played and one of my new favorite games. If you like cyberpunk and retro shooters, this is is champ.

Verdict -

The atmosphere is peak cyberpunk, a colorful but gritty dystopia accompanied by a bombastic sythwave soundtrack that keeps you banging through the levels. The graphics are a slick modern-retro. The combat keeps you on your toes like it should, the story is pretty cool, the main character is cool, the chainsaw leg is super cool. The game is just cool, man. Did I mention how cool the soundtrack is?

CONCLUSION - If you like retro style shooters and/or enjoy cybperunk settings and 80's movies, you need to get this game.
If you only buy games on sale, this game is wholly worth the full $25.


Gameplay -

The gampelay is what i wanted from a retro fps. Zipping around the map and mag dumping fools. You get a sweet chainsaw leg ability to mess around with, you get alt-fire upgrades for your weapons and body augments to upgrade and customize your play style. Enemies are abundant and can swarm you and bigger enemies get pretty tanky all of which encourages on the fly thinking and constantly switching up your strategy mid combat or die trying. I played on Street Cleaner difficulty and had a good time tho it's not uncommon for encounters to get a little sweat at times.

Level Design -

I like how the level design encourages flinging yourself around the arena, it pairs exceedingly well with the spray and pray attitude of your weapon loadout. The only downside being the movement mechanics can feel inconveniently floaty at the wrong friggin time. None of the levels took me more than an hour but I spent a lot of time looking for secrets which aren't easy to find.

I think the game peaks as a whole in Episode 2 and the second half of Episode 1. The setting and stakes are tactfully introduced and the payoff feels good. The level and encounter designs are on really on point here. Vehicle's were the low point for me in my opinion but some of the levels are cool and there aren't a lot of vehicle sections and they don't overstay their welcome. Episode 3 is where it hits the fan. Levels are a fustercluck of enemy swarms and a generous helping of tanky enemies. You cannot relax for a second especially in the final boss rush. This jams up the flow of combat which does a little disservice to the mechanics but the game very much ends on a bang and i can respect that

Story -

I didn't buy Turbo Overkill for the story but I was pleasantly surprised. On top of playing like a cool 80's action flick, I got pretty invested in Johnny's struggle of having everything taken from him while being thrust into the role of galactic savior. It fits perfect for the setting and puts and extra gear into the gameplay. The bounty hunters are a bit whacky without going over the top and have real motivations, even if it it's just getting paid. I love how the main villain is played as an unapologetic edgelord D-Bag
My only two gripes are 1. conquering the universe is an overblown threat, galaxy sounds a lot more believable and more threatening. 2. no Ripper gf :c\t\t\t\t\t
