\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Spoiler warning for most of the game*










In all honestly, this game doesn't really live up to the hype for me. I bought it when only the first episode was out, and the game in general felt like a slog to get through. The combat encounters weren't really that fun to deal with, and the levels felt way too long. A thirty minute level of the same thing over and over gets boring after five minutes. The enemies don't synergize with each other or have unique qualities. Its just more health and more dangerous attacks that can destroy your health bar in a second. The weapons also have the same issue, as even some of the cooler weapons such as the telefrag sniper rifle are rarely used in combat encounters. Also the amount of weapons in the game is often annoying to navigate around. I often found that when I would scroll to the next weapon, to far forward or backwards making it so I lost the weapon I was originally trying to choose. Also a lot of the weapons do the same thing differently. For example, the shotgun that you first find and pickup feels great as it can stun enemies and shoots somewhat fast. Meanwhile, the second shotgun you obtain right after is the same thing with a slower fire rate, and more damage. Its secondary ability can be considered worse due to how it doesn't stun and does relatively the same damage. The two shotgun example can also be shown with the machine gun and assault rifle, as they practically do the same thing. Then later, in the second episode, they added the weird cell gun, that is basically the assault rifle with a beam ability that does almost no major damage. It however showcases something rare in this game, as I believe it synergies with the second, more powerful shotgun bomb, making a big explosion. However I rarely used this as other weapons did equal damage more easily. Also, when episode 2 came out, these problems got even worse. The new enemies just felt like tanks that I just had to dump all of my ammo into, not being very fun. The addition of more bosses had me excited, but the execution was lackluster. The bosses instead of being similar to the former, artifact-zero boss which was a somewhat enjoyable fight if you didn't get insta killed, were just pressing the T button and barraging them with as many bullets as possible. However the final fight with maw was exciting and fun, but it was doomed to the same fate of the other episode 2 bosses. Press T and shoot your chain gun until it runs out of ammo. The release of episode 3 continued with the enemy and weapon problems as the new additions weren't really that interesting and fun to play with. The second part of episode 3 really upset me however. After waiting, the first level I'm greeted with after a cliffhanger is an utter slog to get through. It's simply just not fun or enjoyable to run around to three checkpoints which take ten minutes to clear each. After this, the final boss fight against maw was incredibly disappointing. It didn't feel challenging at all and the only hard parts were when he spawned a horde of enemies that were annoying to deal with. After this, the next boss was also not challenging at all as it simply was just shooting at a humongous target. Giving where credit is due however, the game has improved majorly from episode one, as during that version of the game almost every combat encounter felt incredibly unfair. This was later fixed however, which made the game far better. In the end, the new updates just added to the slog of the gameplay and I never really found myself truly enjoying the game to its fullest extent.\t\t\t\t\t
