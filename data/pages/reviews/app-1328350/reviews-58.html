\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTurbo Overkill takes the Boomer shooter to the next level. This is the best iteration of classic first-person-shooter fun Ive played yet. Surpassing both the classics and the modern entries. Everything about this game is just top-notch. The level design is both great and varied with lots of different locations and some crazy ideas. Searching for key-cards never gets in the way of the fun, because it is always pretty clear where to go, sometimes even guided by markers. There are quite a bit of Ghostrunner like parkour elements in the game but the movement controls are way better executed than in most other FPS games, so that you always feel in full control making these sections fun instead of trial and error frustration.

Enemy variety is great as well and boss fights are fun. There are even vehicle sections that are also a lot of fun. Even more fun are all the weapons and upgrades that the player obtains during the gameplay. Each weapon can be enhanced for alternate fire modes and more punch. All of them feel great to use and it is difficult to find a favorite. There are also character upgrades that enhance movement, damage types or give you more money, health or armor from dead enemies. Tons of stuff to play around with and find your own playstyle. All of this is presented in super stylish low poly graphics with fantastic special effects and tons of environmental variety. Dark back alleys, sprawling cyberpunk cities, chromed skyliners, moving trains, space stations, pest-infested sewers, you name it.

The 15 hours ride never gets boring and there are also five different difficulty levels to fine tune the player experience. Last but not least the absolutely brilliant synthwave soundtrack that pumps the adrenaline into the veins during intense firefights deserves a lot of praise as well. Overall a tremendous game that is a steal for the asking price. Get this ASAP if you are even slightly interested in frantic FPS, cyberpunk or overly stylish video games.\t\t\t\t\t
