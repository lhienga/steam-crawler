\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA masterpiece, up there with the likes of Half Life, Doom 2 and Duke Nukem 3D. And I'm not even being hyperbolic. It genuinely is that good. It's a modern classic and I don't doubt people will be talking about this game for years to come.

I could sit here and nitpick all day - the second act drags a little bit towards the end and for some reason the game froze for me after the end credits rolled - but these issues don't detract in any way from just how polished the game is. It's a masterclass in boomer/movement shooter action. The developers understood the assignment. They knew what the audience for this genre wanted and they delivered.

Map design is unbelievably tight and well thought out. All the weapons are fun to use and remain relevant until the end of the game. Enemy design is fun and they compliment each other perfectly. Visuals are fantastic, music is S tier, voice acting is flawless from start to finish. I just can't fault any of it.

Just when you think you've seen it all the game throws another pleasant surprise at you. It just doesn't seem to end.
If you're into boomer/movement shooters you *have* to get this. This isn't game of the year, this is game of the decade.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply put, the best FPS since Doom Eternal. Think of something you want in an FPS game, this game has it - Awesome gun feel, silky smooth movement, great enemy design, great level design, tons of variety, fun story that stays out of the way of the action without feeling like an afterthought, a catchy soundtrack, I could go on.

Of particular note is the level design, and how friction-less it all is. There isn't a map in Turbo Overkill, but as a serial map checker I am happy to say I only got lost maybe 2 or 3 times in the entire 11 hours it took me to beat the campaign. That timeline is just for the main story by the way, if you want to play arcade, endless mode, or find more collectibles for secret levels, there's even more value here.

Long story short, if you like FPS games at all, buy Turbo Overkill. If you don't like FPS games, buy Turbo Overkill and let it change your mind. In a year stacked with amazing games, Turbo Overkill stands proudly alongside them.\t\t\t\t\t
