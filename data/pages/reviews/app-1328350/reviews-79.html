\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere have been so many quality doom-likes these last two years it's overwhelming. For the most part, they are all creations of sincere love and hard labor. Some rise above their peers, distinguishing themselves with exceptional qualities be it in game-play, re-playability, multiple modes, secrets, online leaderboards, custom map editors, incredible map layout and visual design, 3D modelling, 2D sprites, the list goes on and on.

I'll say these game fall into two categories. Those of special distinction that rise to levels of rare exceptionalism - the quantity and quality of which we've seen recently is staggering. This is Doom-likes only, specifically.

In no particular order for the category of Exceptionalism Beyond Expectation:
Turbo Overkill
Deadlink (personal favorite, I just love well made rogue-lites)
Prodeus
Dusk
Nightmare Reaper (deserves even more distinction...indescribably so)
Gloomwood (though arguably more of a stealth game - but sufficiently unique and of quality to be top tier).

Again, in no particular order, Doom-likes of great quality, in some cases bordering on extreme exceptionalism:
40k Boltgun
Ultrakill
Project Warlock II
HROT

Honorable Mention: While not a doom-like but rather a Hotline Miami style fast paced top down shooter - OTXO gets top marks.

There are no doubt others I've either forgotten or have yet to play. But we are living in a golden age renaissance of the doom-like shooter and holy F am I hear for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is something really special about this game, the quality and attention to details/weapons/animations/level design/gameplay and music is something I haven't seen since the original Perfect Dark came out, well that's what my initial thoughts were.

Runs on the Unity engine, and while the developers have managed to get the best from a difficult engine than most there are some fps issues on the level infestation, it might just be limited to my setup or this level but then MSI AfterBurner showed no bottlenecks, with only 4 cores really doing most of the work below 50% and the others around 20%, GPU 59% usage, maybe it's a thread optimization issue.

But I have to mention some downsides of the game, it is hugely dragged out at the end (unfortunately they didn't know when to stop), add to that the difficultly ramps right up despite changing the it's level, also they take away the quick save system leaving you to repeat sections over and over till you want to pull your hair out, they do add it back later for a bit but then you are on a timer to kill waves of enemies (they also take the quick save away again) while trying to navigate which way to go, the end boss fight feels like it never ends along with what comes after it.

Anyhow at the start when it's doing things good it's Just that magical (also earlier levels are shorter which is good, they feel better than the massive longer ones) feeling you get from very few games, but with endless bugs after release and the way they chose to do the last two awful tedious levels/boss fights really is sour to the point I got to the last terminal and just gave up (as If the MAW battles weren't enough) after what seemed like endless retries of trying to do it on time and falling with the grapple hook or missing with the sniper shots while the timer runs out, then having to do it all over again, this is not fun and IMO not what fun gaming is about but more about frustrating the player to the point of hating/forgetting what was good about the game to begin with.

Despite the bugs I recommend the first two episodes as there is still some fun to be had but I do not recommend episode 3, as a full game I do not recommend so maybe get in a sale and skip the last episode to save yourself endless frustration and annoyance.\t\t\t\t\t
