\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game that has a lot to offer. Very dynamic gameplay with fast paced movement, a LOT of combat options and enemy variety, cool level design (maybe sometimes too big for its own good) aswell as a good story with cool characters.

Despite that fact , it also has some issues. The checkpoint system is completely f'd up at some times, respawning you in the middle of fights, and sometimes not checking after passing key points in certain levels. The arsenal at your disposal is so big that there's a good chance you end up forgetting about some of the options during your playthrough, and i feel like some weapons/abilities are kind of gimmicky and unnecessary, which can end up in confusion. Movement feels really nice when moving around levels but can feel punishing during heavy combat, especially at higher difficulty. Overall i'd say that, despite being a little over ambitious on some aspects (which is hard to complain about in this day and age) , this game really hits the nail on the head with what it's trying to do and 100% deserves the money it's asking for. If you're looking for a fast paced , challenging experience, i can guarantee you'll have a great time !

8/10

Turbo Overblown

Released in 2023, Turbo Overkill is a fast-paced arena-shooter FPS developed by Trigger Happy Interactive and published by Apogee Entertainment.

As Syn, the worlds most advanced AI, is threatening to take over cyber-metropolis "Paradise", Johnny Turbo, half man and half machine, cleaves a bloody path through hordes of grotesque amalgamations of corrupted flesh and twisted metal.

DISCLAIMER: Turbo Overkill originally released in early access and took approximately 16 months to fully release.

Personal Thoughts:

Turbo Overkill has everything going right for it. The setting, the music, the gameplay - everything just clicks. Controls are tight, action is high-octane and the enemies just die left and right. A power fantasy for all shooter fans with the right amount of gumption, guts and gore.

And then episode one ends. Issues start showing. Levels become larger. Too large. Many more enemies appear. Too many. "The pacing is done for", I start to think, as episode two finishes with double the clock of episode one. Episode three turns into a drag. Turbo Overkill suddenly turned into Serious Sam on speed. Bossfights now start to force me to constantly restart. The chainsaw leg has power creep issues to the point where other weapons just become useless. Visuals start to become overwhelming - ending up annoying me and tiring me out. The soundtrack starts to fade into the background as it becomes increasingly cacophonous. After finishing the game in a grueling die-through because of the final boss (which you have to fight three times with at least three phases... with close to no checkpoints) I start to realize that Trigger Happy Interactive probably took too many ambitions onto themselves. It feels like the game lost focus at some point around episode two and wanted to be something increasingly bombastic, impressive and powerful with every level they added. Unfortunately, increased effort does not reap increased rewards... at least not this time. The charm of the demo I immediately wishlisted and early access game I supported seem gone. Yet many players seem to enjoy Johnny's adventure and I don't want to sour their experience in any way. Turbo Overkill is definitely not for me, even though I am a big boomer shooter fan and heavily supported the early stages of it. Guess I judged wrongly on what this game was going to turn out to be. Shame, but maybe you'll enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t♥♥♥♥. Yes.

Jazzed up Doom Eternal gameplay in a cyberpunk future with beautiful neon pixel art.

Could not tell you anything about the story, didn't pay attention. Seems deliberately over the top and cheesy.

Gameplay is smooth, fast, vertical, and rewards skilled quickswapping. Staying airborne for huge portions of combat is possible and satisfying. The grapple leaves a little to be desired when compared to Doom's meathook.

Occasionally forgot about some of my weapons and abilities but generally I used all of them. Loved augmentations and upgrade system.

Wish the wallrunning was better incorporated into arenas but that's because I'm still waiting for Titanfall 3.

There was a bizarre fog in a couple levels that I assume was a glitch but aside from that there were no technical issues on my 8 year old machine.

There are a handful of level designs and combat encounters that took clear inspiration from Doom Eternal; this might be offensive if they were done poorly or if the game didn't stand on its own two chainsaws but it does so it feels more homage than theft.

Any complaints are nitpicks. Delivered exactly what I expected and nearly everything I want from a movement shooter. Absolutely incredible.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTurbo Overkill is an excellent movement shooter with a surprising amount of writing and story for a game that's really all about going fast, shooting everything that moves and sliding around with your chainsaw leg. The music is good, the sound design packs a punch and the chunky old-school graphics are charming. And most importantly the game itself just feels good to play.

The game's challenge level felt pretty good throughout except during the boss fights which tended to be huge difficulty spikes.

However, I will say that at about a dozen hours for a playthrough the game does start to become a bit stale towards the end, even as the plot ramps up to 11 and beyond. Unfortunately, as I neared the ending I was starting to feel that I was mostly playing to see things through to the conclusion rather than playing for its own sake. With that said, though, you will get a lot of game for your money if that's of importance to you.

All in all Turbo Overkill is a great game for what it is -a challenging and incredibly fast paced shooter with a fun chainsaw leg gimmick- that unfortunately manages to overstay its welcome by just a hair.\t\t\t\t\t
