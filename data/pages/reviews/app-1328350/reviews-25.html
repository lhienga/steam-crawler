\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo the Point
=============================
Start to finish, absolutely one of the best boomer shooters available, don't skip this one

Beaten on Serve Me Pain ( Very Hard Difficulty)

Good Stuff
============================
- The game does an amazing job of putting you in every possible situation where you can learn to play it. You'll do fights in small spaces with limited weapons, wide open areas with tons of kiting opportunities, each enemy arena is like a puzzle that reveals more and more of what the game is about, by the end you will feel like a vet, able to navigate the games many systems and nuance with ease.

- Each encounter has a dozen viable ways to approach it. If you want to simply kite enemies that's possible, there's usually a spot to sit back and fight conservatively and other times there will be powerups that you can engage when just the right enemy appears. It makes it that you never need to tackle encounters in the same way and it's incredibly refreshing.

- Fantastic minute to minute gunplay, the selection is widely varied and each gun feels great. Also need to talk about the melee attack, the chainsaw slide, which could honestly have it's own paragraph but it's so amazingly fun to use in combat, adding maneuverability elements to already great gunplay.

- Splicing, which is this games skill tree, works wonderfully augmenting you basic moves in interesting and varied ways. If you want to make things a bit easier just go all in on chainsaw slide mods but there's tons of mods for experimentation.

- The level designs are so varied an expressive, it really helps to define Paradise as it's own living breathing world. The levels have a great variety of openness, verticality and explorability to keep you looking for every hidden item and pick-up. You'll see everything from cyberpunk slums to idyllic almost alien societies to something out of a John Carpenter movie.

- The story, which I know is not the focus of a lot of FPS, is actually really quite good. Some twists and turns and some quality voice acting. I'm looking forward to see how things progress in future releases

- The game is tough, but it is incredibly fair. Many times if you stop and think about what you're doing wrong you'll find a way to address and overcome it. This is especially true on the games many bossy fights, which seem overwhelming at first until you look closer.

Bad Stuff
=====================
- No map or minimap means you can sometimes get lost
- When things get really heavy in game it can be hard to determine just what exactly killed you, it'll become obvious on replaying but it's annoying in the moment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTurbo Overkill is pretty much everything you can want from a boomer shooter.

-Fast movement speed, with a chainsaw slide, double jump, wallrunning, and dashes
-A wide variety of guns with that feel good to shoot on top of having alt-fire modes for extra usability
-A decent array of enemies, that require different methods of defeating
-Good music that gets you are heart racing
-Vehicle set-pieces that are an unexpected and enjoyable surprise
-And satisfying boss fights

Only real complaints are the "redness" in of some levels in Episode 2 and 3. It just made it hard to see what I was shooting at times. Some battle arenas near the end where more annoying the challenging to fight in. Humor could also be hit or miss at times, but not enough to hamper on the experience.

In short: You should buy this game and OVERkill every last enemy you see.\t\t\t\t\t
