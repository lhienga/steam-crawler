\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like Doom: Eternal? Do you like Cyberpunk? This is what happens if both of those games had really sloppy drunken sex. It's fast, it's brutal, but it just feels so good. The combat is turbo charged, with a dash and double jump system similar to Doom Eternal. You be launching off jump pads and switching through a massive arsenal of weapons regularly. Every weapon has an unlockable Alt-Fire mode, and all of them are just straight up badass. I won't say they're all necessary to make it through the fights, but they're all so cool and fun that you'll want to use them anyway. That is...when you aren't sliding around on your chainsaw foot like a cyborg motorcycle from Hell, grinding everything in your way to meat chunks. I would say it is on par with Doom: Eternal in fun factor, but it is a tiny bit more dumbed down. There isn't quite as much resource management or target prioritizing here. But I think that's okay, it's just a mindless shooter. Get in, kill hordes in 2 seconds flat, get out.

The level design is fantastic, with big open areas that utilize verticality in the most fun ways, as well as tight corridors that make it all too satisfying to line up hordes of enemies and chainsaw them up. There are 3 video tapes and 3 blue chips in every level, and they're well hidden without needing you to scour every single pixel of every level to find. I haven't found any secret doors, and while that may be a bummer to some, I found it refreshing. I hate having to mash the E button while I face grind into every wall in games, so not having to do that here really lets you focus on the ACTION.

Last but not least, the aesthetic and general vibe of the game is perfect. It's gritty and dark, while having the perfect amount of 90s game cheesiness and badassery. Your characters name is Johnny Mother ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Turbo, and you have a chainsaw for a leg, and you are sent to a city called Paradise to destroy a rogue AI named Syn. You're not getting anything too out of the ordinary here, but what you're getting is masterfully done for what it is. The noises, the voice acting, the billboards and posters on the walls...it all looks like it was ripped straight out of a 90s game and I couldn't be any happier with it.

I love this game, I logged 7 hours into it after launching it for the very first time, and plan to log many more hours. I'm especially excited for this full release because there is a level editor and I'm hoping this game picks up and the community makes a slew of amazing custom levels!! I would say at sale price right now ($16) it is an absolute STEAL. Even at the planned $25 though, I would say it is worth it. You're missing out on an absolute GEM if you don't pick this bad boi up.\t\t\t\t\t
