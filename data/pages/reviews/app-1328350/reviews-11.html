Turbo Overindulgent more like...

I had very high expectations for Turbo Overkill as I've seen it appear on many "Best of 2023"-lists. And while it starts off very strong, taking a page out of the new wave of Boomer Shooters that are reminiscent of DOOM 2016/Eternal in terms of movement abilities, it does unfortunately fumble the ball and severely outstays its welcome.

Episode three just becomes a constant slog of the same encounters spammed over and over again complete with a boss rush sequence at the end as well. By the time I was done, clocking in fifteen(!?) hours on the highest difficulty, I was completely drained. After the credits rolled and seeing the other modes offered in the main menu I just couldn't fathom why anyone would want to bother with the Endless or Arcade options.

It's far from bad mind you and does plenty of good such as the frenetic combat itself, the epic synth-wave soundtrack or being able to upgrade Johnny Turbo's abilities to your own liking and playstyle. Sadly though the positives are buried beneath far too much fat that desperately needed trimming. And while I understand its odd to complain about there being 'too much content' I feel like a snappy retro FPS like this could've been much more enjoyable at half its length. I would even go so far as to say it would increase its replayability if it was shorter. However even some of the levels themselves just drag on and on and I shudder the thought of having to go through all of it again just to uncover the remaining collectibles.

Its core mechanics are certainly fine and when you're chainsaw sliding into multiple enemies right into a jump pad and unleashing an auto-missile barrage at the height of your jump in slow motion, seeing enemies below explode in a mess of gibs and health drops before grapple-hooking into another foe on the periphery of your vision it definitely hits that endorphin rush.

That said when all the chaos unfolds even the visuals themselves can become an assault on the eyes what with the exaggerated cyberpunk aesthetic and areas where the colourspace devolves into a saturated fluorescent mess. It all adds to the feeling of exhaustion which makes this game difficult to recommend.

Ultimately Turbo Overkill is a prime example of "too much of a good thing" and dilutes its own experience by artificially padding its length. So get it cheap, play the first episode and then decide if you want to stick with it. According to the achievement completion rate many of its most vocal fans certainly haven't...\t\t\t\t\t
