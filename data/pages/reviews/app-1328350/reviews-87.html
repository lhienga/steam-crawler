\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is quite hard to put into words how good I think this thing is, but if you'll allow me a few, I want to add some context to maybe help make a point: In all honesty, at the moment I've become a bit fatigued with all the "Boomer Shooters", that's not to say i don't enjoy them or anything, but we have been fed well over the last few years and I think I'm pretty full, so yesterday with the release of 1.0 of this game I decided to boot it up as I bought it in early access initially and enjoyed it but didn't think too much of it at the time, I remembered it as a cool shooter with fast movement and a funny chainsaw-leg gimmick that still kinda blended a bit into everything else but I figured now that it was out I'd give it another go.
...
I binged this thing over the last day because I couldn't get enough of it; it was fun and fast but what started getting to me was the sense of scale and ambition, an early level had me going through a tribute/parody of the Megacity "Peachtrees" from the Dredd movie, I thought that was kind of fun and novel until I looked down about 20 minutes in and realised how many floors of this building I'd fought through... this level wasn't using the aesthetics of the megacity as a fun reference, it was using it to create an immense sense of a journey because I didn't fight through a bit of a megacity, I fought through all of it, floor after floor going up and up, sometimes outside and looping around to higher floors, hitting the roof and still going...The scale of it was overwhelming and yet the game made it feel effortless, the frame-rate stayed high, the level's pacing felt well tuned, and the combat and platforming all flowed into each other with variety and great pacing and I was just floored by it...
And then the game refused to stop being constantly interesting and ambitious and fun; the sights and sounds were varied with little vehicle segments that didn't feel just gimmicky but actually felt like they gave certain levels their own individual sense of pace and flow. I don't want to spoil it but some of the levels just feel like such an adventure and yet beyond that the combat remained fantastic to match; this is a game that up at no point did I find any gun redundant, even the starting pistol became a gun that I was using consistently in the last levels of the game; every gun has its moments to shine and yet it was never in a way that I felt arbitrarily forced to need to use a certain gun, I used every gun because every gun was useful right up to the end.

Beyond that though is just the absolute speed you can get up to in this. A lot of air control, chainsaw legs, double jumps, and boosts can all be chained together for absolutely insane jumps and platforming that the game does expect you to do and yet it's never overwhelming; it's always smooth and responsive and I never lacked confidence in making some of the absolutely absurd jumps the game demanded. Obviously different people have different tolerances for first person platforming but for me I adored it and never had any frustrations with it.

Look I can go on all day, the game ran really well for me, the game looked great, sounded great, the soundtrack was varied and fun and there were some absolutely phenomenal tracks, especially in the last episode, Add to that the fact that custom levels are a thing and then I look at the price of this game and honestly....I think that's a steal, i would easily recommend this at twice its asking price, I genuinely would.

I really do think this was something special and my only worry in saying that is that I'm overselling it, I don't want to ruin your experience in doing so but at the same time I want to be honest about my experience, and it was a phenomenal one.
If you find yourself enjoying this even half as much as I ended up doing, then you will have had a really good time :)\t\t\t\t\t
