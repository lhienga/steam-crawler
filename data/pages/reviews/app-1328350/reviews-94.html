\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a millenial, I highly recommend gen z to play this boomer shooter!

In all seriousness, this has to be the best retro shooter that I have played for a very long time -- maybe even the best one in this millenium. It has everything that was considered fun in the 90s: a great soundtrack, interesting "vertical" level design, a neat selection of weapons, tons of blood and gore, rocket jumping, air dash, wall runs, a totally bonkers story and -- last but not least -- a chainsaw leg slide! I also really like that the game allows you to play at your own pace -- you are not constantly forced into close-quarters arena combat, but you can choose whether you want to engage head-on with you chainsaw leg or whether you want to solve problems in a remote manner using the plasma rifle or gatling gun.

If there is one thing that I have to critize then it's the fact that the chainsaw leg somewhat destroys the game's balance: Engaging pretty much all less-than-boss enemies with your chainsaw leg is by far the most effective way of dealing with them -- especially when you upgrade the chainsaw leg to restore your health and armor after each kill, you become pretty much unstoppable...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed for 50ish hours during Early Access, 1.0 is out now and I highly recommend this game for anyone who enjoys fast paced shooters.

Turbo Overkill's gameplay is a lot of fun - a consistently evolving arsenal combined with a respectable enemy roster and huge yet challenging arenas, where you get to flex your jet propelled chainsaw leg (that you can use to slide around or activate midair to headshot enemies, I love that ♥♥♥♥). You can tell various amazing games have influenced Turbo Overkill, and the main dev Wadaholic has picked picked up some key inspiration while maintaining the game's unique identity.

Easily a strong 9 to a light 10/10 that belongs among relatively recent hits such as DUSK, ULTRAKILL and DOOM: Eternal. Not perfect, but still an amazing game among boomer shooters.\t\t\t\t\t
