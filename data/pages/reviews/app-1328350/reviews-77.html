\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game, easily Game of the Year contender for me.

I bought this game randomly, expecting a 4-5 hour bit of throw-away entertainment, but instead, I thoroughly enjoyed a 15 hour adventure, where every single part was firing on all cylinders.

The game-play was incredibly smooth and addictive. If you've played the Doom franchise or shooters like it, then it'll feel pretty familiar, however, I think this game actually surpasses the combat loop in Doom/Doom Eternal, there's something about it that's a lot more free-flowing and responsive. There's a wealth of weapon mods to keep the experience fresh as the game goes on, and the game really rewards exploration.

The world and graphics are beautiful. If you needed anymore proof that art style is king, this game looks fantastic.

The characters are appropriately fun/serious. The game doesn't take itself too seriously, but also doesn't bombard you with jokes. It's a good balance.

The soundtrack... I dunno even know man, you just gotta hear it for yourself. All I can say is thanks to Chipper Hammond, Nikola Nikita Jeremic and Tim Stoney for going all out with it. (Also thanks for putting it on Spotify, some devs don't always do that).

VERY minimal performance issues, like a slight drop here and there near the end of the game when the fights have a lot of enemies. I'm pretty sensitive to this stuff, so trust me, it's perfectly within reason.

My only real gripe is that it could've had tighter pacing, there were a few stages near the end that I think could've been shortened, but it's nowhere near as egregious as it could've been, because the environments are distinct enough, and the combat is always evolving. Still though, tighter pacing would've been a bonus.

This game is clearly a labour of love, and I'm glad it's being received so well. Great job Trigger Happy interactive.\t\t\t\t\t
