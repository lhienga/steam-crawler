\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you'e in the mood for something to scratch the Doom: Eternal itch, and possibly surpass it, Turbo Overkill is well worth the money. A full play through took me close to 10 hours which, for the price, is a steal considering how much fun it is.

Most importantly for any game: I loved the gameplay. For the entirety of the game, it's never not fun. There are some vehicle segments that I didn't love, but they were certainly acceptable and even fun in comparison to other vehicle segments in other FPS's.

The shining star, however, of Turbo Overkill is how it blends the movement and combat. From the very first encounter until the final boss, the game is constantly upping the ante with what it throws at you and what the tools it gives you to deal with it. The game, much like Doom: Eternal, makes you feel more powerful than the things you're fighting.

I highly recommend this to anyone who enjoys over the top actions games and/or first person shooters.\t\t\t\t\t
