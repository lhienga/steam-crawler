\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery unique rogue like strategy game. Its very challenging, very rewarding, and absolutely a "one more stage" for hours on end type of game.

90 hours of play for me to beat a full run, and there are multiple ships, and even multiple layouts for each ship, which offers different play styles and a lot of options. My 90 hours so far have been with only the starting ship, as I was determined to try and beat a full run.

A real gem of a game, and one I see myself coming back to for a long time.

A few tips for beginners:
Its tempting to stop in shops early and often, but try and upgrade your ships defensive capabilities as early as possible; for every damage you take early, that's less money for upgrades and gear later, as you'll be spending more early on repairs.

Drones seem underwhelming, but using defense drones to protect early-mid game from missiles can be very worthwhile; if you see a drone recovery arm, its a pretty solid combo.

Try and get a Scrap Recovery Arm early (multiple if you're lucky); its one of the few upgrades I would prioritize early over defenses if its available, since the earlier you get it the more you'll accrue over your run.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust got my 3rd win on normal with advance content and I must say, people that complain the game is too RNG-based just needs to learn the game. On the surface the game may seems like an RNG mess, and it kinda is, but it's RNG is what makes this such a satisfying experience, because nearly all encounters (at least in normal mode) is a breeze if you just have good planning. There are players who have higher than 90% win rate on hard mode, so I'd say the game is fair if you know what you're doing

If you die in Sector 1 and 4/5 yeah it might be because of bad RNG, because I feel these are where RNG can really mess you up because you aren't prepared yet, but if you die in other sectors, your planning was messed up somewhere. Going past Sector 1 without shield upgrades, going past Sector 4 without a way to get pass a 3-shield ship, going into deep sectors without any of the important systems. Fighting the flagship without any way of disabling the cannons, etc. These are some mistakes that I have learnt to overcome now. And many of the most dangerous threats the game throws at you is easily negated by good system upgrades. Scared of beams? Upgrade shields. Hate missiles? Get a defense drone, or cloaking, or hacking. There's that 10% chance that RNG completely screw you over and none of these errors are your fault, but 90% of the time efficient pathing and macro decision-making will get you enough utilities that most encounter can be dealt with without taking too much damage.

And then there's the micro. Simple things like system targeting, micro-pausing, using cloaking to dodge volleys, switching weapons around mid fight to better suit the situation, to more complex things like power management, crew killing, diving, using 2 power oxygen to tank a hull breach, venting out oxygen to manipulate targeting, venting and kiting intruders into the medbay. Hell, your ship doesn't even need oxygen for half of the fights, so that's a free power for 20-30s. With ok micro and setup you can turn the Flagship into a joke. Once you get the hang of it you will realize how much you can do with just a little resource. And with good micro you inherently get more scrap to upgrade your systems, as you need less scrap for reactor upgrades and you use less scraps for repair.

Overall I'd say the game is way deeper than it seems on the surface. It's not a Crusader King-esque RNG fiesta, but an intricate problem-solving and planning puzzle, where you will have to always be prepared for the worse. It's a timeless masterpiece of a rogue-like that, despite the simple look, is complex and rewarding to play and master. Definitely will try to full achievement this gem.\t\t\t\t\t
