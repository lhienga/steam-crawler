\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying this game is like learning to ride a bicycle. At first we fell off after riding a few meters. After many tries we learn its idiosyncrasies and we get further before falling off. Each time we fall off we get a score, there is no save and we have to start over from the beginning. This game is different from most games that I have played. It has no strong storyline, nor good visuals or music to distract you. This can be seen as a plus or a minus. The plus argument is that the simplicity allows for a pure experience. Overall I think it is a minus, even though I find this game interestingly challenging, it would have been more entertaining had this game also had those elements.

I recommend buying this game when it's on discount. It is an entertaining experience that more than matches its price. This game is a solid foundation upon which to build something on that could offer a much more entertaining experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great unique roguelike which I return back to, at times.

Pluses:
+ Uncommon interesting gameplay
+ Diverse: has quite a number of starting ships, ship parts, systems, alien races. Your quest and location dialogue choises often depend on the gear you get along the way, so you often can make interesting use even of a simingly bad parts
+ Quite chill and meditative, as for a roguelike
+ Amazing calm ambient soundtrack (best tracks imo: Last Stand, Milky Way (battle), Hacking Malfunction (battle)). Most of the tracks are great and are nice to listen in a background, respect to Ben Prunty! This soundtracks also has some really cool remixes on youtube

Minuses:
- Time-consuming. One session may take several hours of gameplay (lengh often depends on how fast your ship can destroy the enemy one, which may be pretty slow with different parts). and it's usually sad to leave a plathrough unfinished

One of the best roguelikes and definitely stands out with its unique strategy-like gameplay\t\t\t\t\t
