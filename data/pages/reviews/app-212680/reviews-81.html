Will I get that ultimate weapon that will complete my ship and make it the killing machine?

Oh. I'm dead. Ok,

Just one more run.

I have had this game in my Steam Library for close to a decade now.

I have not unlocked all of the ships, and I have only beat the final boss once.

Get according to Steam I have over 130 hours in this game, which makes it one of my most played games.

It is a game I continue to come back to. I want to beat it again. I want to unlock more ships and more configurations.

Each time I start a run, though, I realize I probably won't. I'll likely be eaten by space spiders, or my ship will be destroyed by another stronger, more agile ship.

But I'll have fun doing it.

It's the gameplay loop. The difficulty curve. The RNG. The game feeds you these easy ships to defeat early on. And you will. Your beginning lasers will cut through the hull of the Pirate's ship in no time. You'll get loot, maybe even a better gun. Your crew XP meters go up a little bit.

And that's the hook. Those first few jumps there is very little risk. You make some decisions, you win, and you get rewarded. Endorphins flood your brain and you jump again.

Your ship feels strong in the beginning. And you have a clear path to make it stronger. Maybe.

But each jump means harder enemies, more difficult decisions, but the rewards are greater. And then you start not getting what you want . . . The game gives you guns you don't want, of floods you with missiles you aren't using . . . So you are forced to adapt, to get creative.

Your brains loves this, too. So that even when you die, you want to try again. You want to feel that strength in the beginning, that power. And then you want to feel the creativity flow through you as you scrap to survive till the bitter end.\t\t\t\t\t
