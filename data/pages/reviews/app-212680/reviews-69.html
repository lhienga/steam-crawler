\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFTL: a very popular, unique roguelike but personally it really, really frustrates me in unnecessary ways. Each run can get really long, and you can fail in significant ways at any point. One "wrong" move, one chance taken, one gamble that doesn't pay off and it can ruin your game. When the game goes to great lengths to explain certain things, but not others, it feels more like a recurring oversight than deliberate design. The devs clearly knew this when developing their next game - Into the Breach solves this completely.

Trial-and-error difficulty is fine if your game is forgiving enough, but FTL is not forgiving. If the game thoroughly explained and made clear the risks you are taking before asking you to make them it could be unforgiving, but it doesn't. Time and again I ran into new nuances to the game systems that simply aren't explained. Whilst in sector 6 with a full crew and a very well-equipped Mantis ship, for example, I teleported 2 boarders onto a drone ship. The drone ship proceeded to cloak, and I suddenly found out you cannot teleport crew members OFF a boarded vessel while it's cloaked. me for not reading the wiki! Rinse repeat for almost every other combination of mechanics, possible encounters, etc.

That pretty much sums up the game experience - a very charming, interesting, but incredibly frustrating title that just isn't fun for me. It's challenging in all the wrong ways. Play it if you like wasting time and raising your blood pressure. Otherwise, go play Into the Breach.\t\t\t\t\t
