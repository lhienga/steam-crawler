\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: If you like micromanagement, constant pressure, and/or RTS style games - you will probably like this game for all the room to maximise the efficiency of every single thing you do. I don't, so I don't.

I just can't get into it. Roguelites and roguelikes are meant to be unforgiving, and this game is certainly that. Unlike a lot of my favourite rogue____ games though, this game is very happy to have you believe you are doing okay. You can get through pretty much all of the game and then hit an absolute brick wall in the final boss. We're talking 'you should feel stupid for thinking you ever had a chance' sort of brick wall. Most rogue____ games have mini bosses to sort of check you, and give you interesting and fun rewards. Not so here. The things you can do in this game feel very restrictive, and very standard. No room for game breaking combos, no room for wackiness, no room for "oh ♥♥♥♥ this item is CRAZY!". It feels bland quite quickly.

It's very hard to understand the risks you are or aren't taking. A lot of these sorts of games indicate a percentage chance for an outcome. Here, it feels totally random, for the worse. Oh... this option that gave me a mediocre, or good reward last time now ends my run. Okay.

Ultimately, it also feels like there is relatively little room for fun builds. The game is hard enough, and totally unforgiving (iT DoeSnT HoLD YoUR Hand) that wanting to do something a little interesting feels more like giving up than having fun (see criticism of last boss). It is INCREDIBLY easy to get stuck behind an uninformed choice and lose because of it, often times you make a choice that maybe you shouldn't have (no way to know you were taking a risk or not) and lose 30 minutes later because what you did back then was not optimal.\t\t\t\t\t
