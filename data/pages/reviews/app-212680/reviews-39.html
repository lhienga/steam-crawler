\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've bought this game on 16th November and it's now 17th December as of time I'm writing this. I've started playing since I got it and I've been binging it nonstop ever since. I spent countless days playing it for 10-14hrs straight doing nothing else. Currently I have 351hrs in game after a month-ish. In the process since I started playing, I got addicted, I was in madly in love with the game, I got way too familiar with the game's mechanics and events and then naturally when that happens, I started losing some interest.I'm coming here to write a review after a 13hr binge day doing some self reflection on my binging month with FTL.

I think the game has a really strong replayability and retention but it's not based on the fact that it's very difficult which then is supposed to entice people to keep restarting runs in the hopes that they'll finally win, no. I feel it's got a strong addictive gameplay loop that's half based on the almost complete unpredictability any run can get and the near certain death scenarios that the game can and will eventually throw at you which keeps you on your toes at all times.

FTL is among a few roguelikes that takes itself seriously and actually abides by the rules of the rogue genre properly. Some deaths feel total bs, sure, but it's done fair and clean. The game was designed with the core principles of the roguelike handbook in mind. I say this because many games in the genre today have watered the concept down too much and aren't as engaging when they're either:

1. Too easy because you'll get bored
2. Too long (a game should last for a long while but it shouldn't be like a playthrough of Fallout 4)
3. Unfair because there's mechanics that don't take the permadeath mechanic seriously (i.e. imbalance of items and enemies)

After getting really into the game, I realize that FTL has a decently high skill ceiling which is why there's a dedicated community that analyses strats and discusses items, paths, ship layouts etc. in depth on the forums. The game subverts your expectations with the trailer's simplistic art style and graphics, I swear. It's anything but as simple in terms of gameplay. And the beauty is, you can jump into it as a first time player on any game mode and you'll have decent fun regardless. Getting consistent win-rate through requires you to master knowledge of events, pathing, enemy AI behavior, timing of weapon fire rates that has you making spreadsheets in your head and drafting a rough plan for each fight. This makes the game fun for long time players, keeping things diverse and challenging so you don't just drop it after you pick the game apart for all the info you can get then steamroll through it without effort.

I had quite a few good runs once I got better at the game, when I knew what outcomes to certain events were, where to find certain things and when I started getting habits of things to do and not to do. The runs where I was annhilating everyone weren't that fun tbh, The runs where you're barely scraping by and having to work with the god awful items you have are the ones that are memorable. Ones that keep you engaged and paying attention to every detail, every resource and carefully hatching plans, those are the good runs, and they don't win most often. That's why I got hooked on FTL.

I think the game is suitable for everyone of all ages tbh, like I can even see easy mode accessible for kids. Plus, it's not that time consuming. Kids that have free time don't have to commit their entire lives to it like most god awful grindathon games of today, high school teens could fit in a run or 2 in between social life and school work or whatever, college/uni undergrads, busy parent having an hour of freedom a day to themselves when away from responsibilities. The game has achievements and ship unlocks. No credits, premium 2nd currency for exclusive unlocks, extra skins, DLCs and expansions, live service. That's it. If you get the game, you're buying a game, not a second job. I'm biased obviously but... grab a copy if you've got some free time and nothing else going on\t\t\t\t\t
