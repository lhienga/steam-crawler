\r\n\t\t\t\t\tManage your crew and upgrade your ship as you flee a wave of rebel ships through outer space. Good ship-to-ship battle simulation with pre-designed ships that can be outfitted with different modules and weapons. Board enemy ships to fight hand to hand, cut enemy ships with beams, set their medical bay on fire with a bomb, deploy defensive or offensive drones, get stealth, upgrade ship stats and watch the crew get better at the jobs of manning the various systems. You will have to deal with fires, oxygen levels, and ships attacking you while you are partially disabled in a nebula. Gather strange aliens for your crew to have a variety of strengths and weaknesses to see if you can survive and beat the relatively short game.

Very hard on normal difficulty and you can just get bad luck and there's nothing you can do but die and start over. You can't save and load to try a battle multiple times, so be sure you are okay with a permadeath game. You will unlock new ships for certain achievements which gives the somewhat-short game a lot of replayability and consolation prize if you die.

Not the best game ever and doesn't live up to the hype but I had some fun and it's worth the $10 price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe art, music, and barebones story are all nice. The mechanics are fun although often times frustrating. Overall I can't recommend this game due to how over half of the runs feel like the game is out of your control. Almost every event is a random chance you will just take damage, be crippled (such as losing a crew member), or you get a small reward. Sometimes even with 60% dodge, the enemy lands their entire first volley on you and you're screwed. Sometimes you reach the final boss feeling very prepared, with a plan, and just as you're getting ready to fire... They stealth, charge their weapons, destroy your weapons and shields, and you lose the fight in the next 30s without being able to counterplay it whatsoever.

FTL is a very frustrating game full of RNG, I've won once or twice and both times felt like the game gave me a free win with lucky outcomes. Every time I've lost it felt like some random ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• got thrown my way and my strategy meant nothing. I can't recommend this game for anyone who wants more than about 20hrs of gameplay, because beyond that, the frustration sets in and you realise how shallow and repetitive it truly is.
