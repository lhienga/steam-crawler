\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing this game for over a decade. It's amazing.

As a roguelike with a seven sectors of content each game, you know the time commitment you're in for when you start a game and you can always pick up where you left off, so you can play for an hour between work and appointments without ionizing your personal ship's battery. (If you're a very methodical player you *might* get three hours out of a session, max, but more typical wins are in the one hour range.)

The mechanics and concepts are very easy to learn, which makes it easy to get started. However, learning how to use these mechanics effectively takes significant time and gives a very satisfying challenge as you experiment and discover effective tactics. Veteran players can win half of their games or more, but novice players can go ten or twenty (or more!) games before their first win as they learn how to face unexpected challenges.

The lore (such as it is) is just enough to create a goal and an interesting universe without trying to be a lore-heavy game. (For example, it is clear that the Engis and Zoltan have some sort of special relationship, but what it is and why isn't explicitly stated.) The lore gives flavour and hints at mechanics that will be useful (such as that Mantis prey on Engi and, so, you might encounter Mantis randomly in an Engi sector), but otherwise isn't essential.

The music is stellar.

Modding is possible but annoying, as there is no convenient WYSIWYG modding system like modern Steam games. This game was made before Steam Workshop integration and I doubt the developers thought it would take off like it did, so they didn't make modding a priority. That being said, once you get past the initial extract-this, put-files-here, run-this-executable nonsense, you can get the hang of running mods on it without too much trouble. (As for creating mods, this takes a bit more dedication but if you love the game and want to it is definitely possible.)

Honestly, the developers did an exceptional job at this game. It set the bar for crew-management roguelikes and its classic art style, amazing music, and simple mechanics makes it very replayable and genuinely timeless; I could easily imagine young adults enjoying this game in 2043 as much as a new player enjoys it now, without it seeming tremendously dated.

Very highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
