\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is a lot to like about this one. Mechanically, aesthetically, creatively I really liked it. I just wish it wasn't so ridiculously overtuned difficulty wise. Call it a skill issue, but I think a game that demands borderline perfection just to reach the credits on easy mode is bad design. This game would've really benefited from something akin to Hades' heat system, where there's a challenging, but doable baseline that can be built on for the players that do like getting continuously beat down by their video games. The roguelike formula is draining, especially when there's no real tangible progress between runs, and so a roguelike designed to take 40+ hours of work just isn't a great idea for the average player (that number is based on what I've read at least, the only reason I didn't quit then and there was because I assumed those were overestimates). Most people can only have a great run ruined by one 'mistake', which half the time is just getting unlucky with an encounter or an event, so many times. After fourteen hours I got a great run that destroyed the first stage of the rebel flagship. If the game ended there I'd be much more forgiving, but as it is getting a run good enough to get all three stages dealt with is a bit much. The reaction of beating a game like this after 15 hours is joy and pride. After 40 hours that turns into nothing but annoyance at best. Again, if you like your games to beat you down, go for it, if you don't, steer clear no matter how enticing the rest of the package is.\t\t\t\t\t
