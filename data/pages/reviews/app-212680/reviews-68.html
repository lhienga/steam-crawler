\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn simple terms:
The good:
-Great music
-Nice interface that shows what you need to know.
-Many interesting concepts
-Many different ships with different play styles
-Achievements (if you are into that)
-Interesting story/details
-Lets you save often

The bad:
-The RNG is just too much.
-Starting zones define if you lose or not in a lot of cases.
-Feels slow, specially when you feel frustrated.
-You need to learn a LOT if you want to do good in the game (knowing events, zones and managing crew)
-You will encounter impossible fights that will make you reset.
-It will save even when you don't want to.

In more detail:
So many things in the game are good, but the gameplay loop is just hard to deal with.

Some runs are just lost from the very start, for many reasons, from map layout and events to enemy encounters, this is FAR more noticeable when you play a ship that is not the base/starter ship.
In other occasions, you will encounter fights you WILL lose and you have to run away. The problem: The enemy gets lucky and gets you engines or your pilot room in a very unfortunate time, not only that the delay that entails lets it hit your engines and your medic station, and suddenly fire is on the engines and a hole in your medic bay. By this point, if you don't have a full crew (which happens most of the time) you simply die.
This is made worse by the slow nature of the game, having to wait for systems and the engines to either fight or escape. Making each encounter lasting at least about 2 to 10 minutes. If you lost on the first sector(map) this does not feel like much, but it does when it happens 4 times in a row or when you reached sector 5, where an encounter like that feels like being cheated.

And people will say "it's because you are not good enough" and I will say: "Tell me that when you only got one single gun, the one you started with, against a ship with many shields or rockets. Or when the sun decides to be funny and turn your ship into a fireball."
It is terribly frustrating. It is interesting for the challenge, but chances are you will feel cheated and betrayed by RNG when you suddenly miss 5 shots in a row.

Now, I haven't tried the advanced mode of the game, I cannot say if it is better like that or not, but the game insists to play the original mode before getting into the advanced. But just the original is too frustrating to keep on with it.

If you have time and like suffering, you may like this.
If you don't have too much time, this will not work for you, even with the save system, because if you leave for more than a day or two, you'll be clueless and most likely lose. This will only make it more frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t
