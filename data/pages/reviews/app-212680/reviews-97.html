Quick text review:

Rebels are disturbing the peace of the federation (A different federation) and it's up to you to outrun the rebel fleet in your ship while surviving the dangers of space in order to bring back information that can turn the tide:
-Very functional visual representations of your ship as well as that of your enemy but it looks good enough and it's a great way to see laser weaponry hit multiple on board rooms until the entire thing blows up
-Nice enough sci-fi music and moments of quiet to relax you in between dangerous encounters
-Nothing else to the overall main story but there are a bunch of encounters with good descriptions (No voice overs) and these encounters don't necessarily always have the same choice end in the same result... sometimes
-Procedural space with different star maps to travel to instead of rooms where you fight an enemy, make a choice, purchase something etc.
-Your rewards are new weapons for your ship, new crew members to operate and defend your ship and scrap to upgrade your ship
-It's quite fun when your ship stays ahead of the progressively stronger opponents and quite a jolt when you have the exact needed upgrade or crew member to get an overwhelmingly positive result
-But if your ship lags behind and say doesn't have the capacity to punch through their shields it's kind of a slow death
-No roguelike elements really... you just can unlock new starting ship types and layouts for the run which is nice but getting the layouts especially is tough and this adds flexibility and not necessarily power
-Combat is fun if automated to an extent (Target a ship part and activated weapons will fire when ready until the target was changed)

Practical Concerns:
-Good value for money (R1.01/ hour for me - $ and Â£ values in video)
-Small download size (274 Mb on the hard drive)
-Easy to run (Some systems may have to use "stretch fullscreen" instead of "native fullscreen")
-Pretty kid friendly
