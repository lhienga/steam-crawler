\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I had fun but unfortunately being one of the first (I think FTL encouraged the creation of a lot rogue-like games in the 2010s, despite the genre being around for so so long) it falls into few patterns/holes that future games would learn from/avoid. Despite each scenario you encounter having written descriptions (wonderful!) it boiled down to either fight, nothing, or receive punishment/reward. I'll be taking a look at the wiki since I'm very curious what things I have missed, and I really like the "blue option" mechanic a lot, where if you have the right equipment or crew type the game will tell you which option is the best one to take, and I bet there are a lot of scenarios in the game that I hadn't discovered the blue option for.

Though I personally won't play it again, it consumed the first half of my week, and I will be honest and say I could not beat the game, but I am sure random chance or few tweaks in how I played would eventually grant me victory. This is not the how I feel about a few other games out there, but with I never felt cheated or that the game had "made me lose" on purpose. In that way it had a slot machine like feel, where often I was saying "one more run" multiple times in a row. In that way I liked the game, and I think it was trying to encourage players to take an 'all-rounder' approach to ship/crew building without openly telling them. I found that if you went all in with a particular strategy (like only drones or only dodging) you often found lots of early game victories, which took a lot of time to do, but it only took 1 or 2 late game ships to humble you (like a hacking ship targeting your drones, or a boarding craft). If you did an all round approach, you often avoided doing as many encounters and being more careful with your money, but I tended to get farther into the game with few near deaths.

Each run is completely separate with no carry over between. For example, on one run you may find a stranded person and he joins your crew. On another, you may find the same scenario, but he turns aggressive and damages your ship. In one run, he killed one of my other crew members and disappeared. At that point it felt like I was rolling the dice, and wish I had a way of seeing either modifiers or percentage chance of success, which again might have been available if I had the right gear or crew.
