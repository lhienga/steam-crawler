\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFaster Than Light is a strategic roguelite where you manage a spaceship that has to overcome an intergalactic menace. Along the way, they visit a plethora of different star systems. You will fight other spaceships, take on missions, enhance your spaceship, recruit all kinds of aliens for your crew, buy weapons and droids to defend yourself from threats, and more! The game excels at offering a wide array of possibilities that translate into multiple strategies to use on each run. It is a perfect example of using the concept of managing variables in roguelites to maximize replayability.

But what I love the most about FTL is its atmosphere. It has subtle, otherworldly music that is very calming. I think one of my favorite moments in the game is when, after a fierce battle, I decide not to move to the next area but just stay floating in space; watching my crew first fix everything and then just work and move around the spaceship. Those moments are filled with calmness, and, with the great FTL’s music in the background, it lets me fulfill my fantasy of feeling as if I was exploring the vast universe in my spaceship. I almost can hear Bowie saying “And the stars look very different today.”\t\t\t\t\t
