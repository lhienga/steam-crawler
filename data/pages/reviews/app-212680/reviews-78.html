\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhenomenal game. Took me 43 hours to beat the game on easy but I adored every second of it. The combat is easy to learn and hard to master, and has many ways of approaching it. Do you want to destroy their ship or try boarding it? You'll also be making some important decisions. Is this slug really trustworthy? Should you help this prisoner escape or hand them over for a bounty? Some decisions could get you a new ship while others could put holes in your current ship. Your most important decisions will be the ones where you have to spend scrap. Is it worth it getting a teleporter system and a cloning bay and diverting to a boarding playstyle, with it costing so much? Should you get more crew to man your systems or get a new weapon? Be warned, however, this game is very difficult. Many will not make it past easy, and it will take a good amount of time before you get your first win. You will make a poor decision that will leave your now empty ship shattered in the vacuum of space. But every time you lose you'll learn from it, and maybe unlock some new ships or ship types as well. I suggest taking a look at the achievements tied to whatever ship you're playing, they'll be in the escape menu when you're playing. If you complete at least two of them it will unlock the type B of that ship. If you reach the final sector with the type B, you'll unlock the type C of the ship (if it has one, two ships don't have a type C). I ran out of things to say, just get the game you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came to this game very late. But it's a classic for a reason.

It's a typical roguelike. Short runs of around 2 hours. You navigate through a series of sectors, fight opposing ships, crewing and powering your systems in order to win. Weapons to attack, Shields to block, Engines to evade, plus other stuff like Doors, Sensors and Medbays. There's a lot to think about, you can spend your money to improve your ship whenever you want and there's deep strategy in working out what to improve and when, or whether you should be saving for a shop, where you can recruit more crew, buy weapons, heal up and buy new systems and bonuses.

Around 50 different weapons, 20+ ships to pilot and a huge variety of random enemies with different weapons and systems to fight - there's a lot of variety.

I prefer turn-based strategy games, but please don't be put off if you're the same. You can pause at any time to manage what's going on and the game effectively stops after each encounter as you consider your next move.

A few little complaints - as I've got better I've found most of your winning ships end up looking very similar. The 20+ ships feel very different at first but you soon have to patch up their weaknesses and get rid of the quirky weapons in favour of the 5-10 most efficient ones.

It's a difficult game, not crushingly so if you're used to this type of game though. Sometimes the difficulty manifests itself in frustrating ways: opposing ship loadouts are random, so in the first sector specialised ships can run into enemies that hard counter your strategy and there's nothing you can do but take a bunch of damage and run away - it's RNG of the most irritating kind. As the game progresses you can mitigate tough opponents and develop countermeasures - but that first sector can be a doozy.
There are also a bunch of events in the game that give you choices, but the consequences are often random - you may lose a crew member, you might get a reward. The game doesn't tell you what's at stake, so either you learn the hard way which events are bad or you look each one up on the wiki - neither feels very satisfactory.

But overall a great, addictive strategy game.

Picked it up on sale for under $2 which is ridiculous for a game that I've played for almost 200 hours.\t\t\t\t\t
