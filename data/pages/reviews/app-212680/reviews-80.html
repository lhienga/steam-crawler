\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has two major flaws that turn it from a definite recommend into a definite do-not-buy.
1. The smaller flaw is the UX. Everything is painfully slow, and there's no way to speed up healing/repairs/repositioning outside of combat. This extends each run by a combined ten-ish minutes of simply sitting around waiting. Not a big deal at first glance, but consider that people online generally say that it took them 50-200 runs to win once (on normal difficulty), which adds up to A LOT of sitting around waiting.
2. The game is NOT hard, despite what everyone's saying. In the sense that it's not difficult. It simply has the same problem as Darkest Dungeon: unmediated RNG. You can get severely hobbled by RNG early on, which will make continuing the run pretty much pointless, because enemies will outpace you in terms of power growth, and you'll need an incredible lucky streak to catch up.

All in all, the game was fun in a somewhat frustrating way for a bit. But once I won a run and read up on strategies online, I realized that there's no real complexity to the game and all it takes to win is to become overpowered, and the anticipation that a lot of the fun hinged on, went poof. RNG can be fun when it's done right. FTL's devs did not do it right.\t\t\t\t\t
