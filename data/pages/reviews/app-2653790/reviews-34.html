\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI went into this game not knowing much of what to expect, and I'm happy to say that I was pleasantly surprised with what I got!

Ultimately the concept of the game is simple but effective! A walking simulator with a puzzle twist to it. Most walking simulators I have played so far have not been anywhere near as mentally stimulating as this, which truly makes The Exit 8 stand out to me in a positive light.

There isn't much of a story to the game, but that is understandable for what it is, and in my opinion a deep or detailed story isn't necessary for a game to be good.
The gameplay is simple but effective and truly what makes the game. As a puzzle and puzzle-type game enjoyer, I found this to be delightfully challenging. If you aren't great with memory-type puzzles be prepared! This game has you looking at all the details for anomalies and occasionally questioning your sanity and whether or not you're seeing a change.

The visuals are great in my opinion! Perhaps not the fanciest or the best, but it's detailed in the areas that matter and overall it's an enjoyable (and unsettling) environment to look at and be in.

There isn't much to say about the soundtrack as there isn't really one, however I do quite enjoy the sound effects and find them to be well-made. From the buzzing of the lights to the sound of your footsteps on the tile, I feel like I'm truly in those halls wandering each level and I love it.

Overall I really enjoyed this game and the memory skills challenge it brought. It may be hard for some and easy for others, but regardless of just how challenging it is to you I highly recommend giving it a try if you enjoy puzzle or memory games! I know that this is one game I'll be coming back to frequently whenever I want to challenge my memorization and observation skills.
