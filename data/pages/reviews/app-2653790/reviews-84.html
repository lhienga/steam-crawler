\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the concept and it's got soul but some weird quirks keep it from being fun and make it just frustrating instead. You might get lucky and have a better experience. I wish I could recommend it even though it's short since it's only 3 dollars but it seems like your experience can just randomly be bad because of how the game is designed.

When you start out you'll see some anomalies of differing degrees of intensity, which can be creepy or maybe scary, but as you continue playing those same anomalies are seemingly removed from the pool and won't occur again. This is good in theory because repetition would be boring, but it ends up meaning that the only anomalies that can occur are not as obvious (the ones you miss stay in the pool) and you'll end up spending time searching the corridor only to find nothing different and move on, when in actuality there was a stain on the wall somewhere or something and you get reset back to the beginning.

So past your first 10 or 15 minutes it ends up just being a glorified spot the difference game which isn't really fun in my opinion. I managed to make it to the 8th corridor a couple of times early on (and apparently just missed something immediately after) but the next hour or so consisted of me painstakingly examining every little object for the remaining, much harder to spot anomalies, and it ended up killing the entire atmosphere of the game and making it a chore.\t\t\t\t\t
