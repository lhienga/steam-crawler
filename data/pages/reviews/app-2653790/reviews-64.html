\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE IF YOU HAVE LOWER GRAPHICS ON YOUR PC!!! -- This game is 99% playable on a lower-res/non-gaming laptop. However there is ONE ANOMALY that you will never see without knowing about it first. Even in high-res it is hard to see; but in lower-res, it is barely visible even when you know to look for it.

If you want to know what that anomaly is so you can be prepared:

There is a 'face' in the ceiling between 2 lights just after the ceiling-mounted Exit 8 sign. This 'face' is just 3 splotches are just slightly darker splotches, almost like a mild water stain.

Look up pics of it online, and even then you will likely miss it several times before finally 'seeing' it. For a visual of what it looks like in-game with a non-gaming laptop, you can look at the video on my page or on YouTube and go to timestamp 25:35 to see it in low-res AND a photo of a high-res version, which is still pretty hard to see.

https://youtu.be/znTyZM1npIY

Now, for my general review:

This was an unexpectedly REALLY FUN game. Lots of scares and spooks to surprise rather than startle. (i.e. no cheap jump scares). Essentially, you walk down the same hallway over and over, and if you see an anomaly, you turn back and restart the loop in the opposite direction. If there is no anomaly, you keep going forward to reach the next hallway.

It was amazing how they were able to make the most mundane things feel sooooo creepy! I loved every second of it. Also, I found out after playing that you can also INTERACT with many (all?) of the anomalies for even more scares. I had no idea, so now I have to go back and check out all of the spooks I missed, even after finding all anomalies.

For such a simple game, I'm impressed with how much FUN they were able to pack into this thing!

10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first playthrough, I would say the game is perfect. Very minimalist design that jumps straight into gameplay, simple mechanics a child could grasp, and a goal so straightforward that it requires no actual instructions (but gives them anyway). Very chilling effective scariness without using any cheap jumpscares, but not too scary for the regular puzzle solver.

But on repeated playthroughts, I notice a fatal flaw: it never repeats the same anomaly, instead it cycles through all the anomalies until you've seen them all, then cycles through them again. This makes the game progressively harder the more times you play it, regardless if you win or lose.\t\t\t\t\t
