\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStrengths:

1. Atmosphere: The game masterfully captures the feeling of concealed spaces – those unstintingly familiar yet eerily empty places like backrooms and abandoned stations. The low-fi lighting, echoing sounds, and repeating textures weave a chilling ambience that lingers long after you exit the game.
2. Gameplay: The "spot the difference" mechanic is simple yet strangely addictive. Every loop feels like a new puzzle, and the satisfaction of finding a previously missed anomaly is immense.
Price: At a budget-friendly price.


Weaknesses:

1. Shortness: The game can be completed in roughly one to two hours, depending on your detective skills. While the replay-ability adds some longevity, some players may wish for a more expansive experience.
2. Frustration: The subtle nature of some anomalies can lead to moments of frustration, especially on later loops when the pressure to identify them quickly ramps up.


I highly recommend The Exit 8 to anyone who enjoys atmospheric horror, puzzle games, and exploring the uncanny.
