\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as short horror walking simulators go, this one is very unique and actually unsettling. You're basically just walking through a tunnel constantly as though you're in purgatory. As you walk through the hallway you will notice things out of place. Some of these are very obvious, like an ocean of blood pouring through the hallway, but others are very subtle, like a strange imprint of a face in the ceiling or a sign's eyes following you. What makes the game unsettling is that you're looking for these inconsistencies, but since some of them are subtle it's actually creepy when you notice they're there. You might walk through the hallway several times and not even notice some of them, like a doorknob in the middle of the door frame instead of where it would usually be. If you see one of these inconsistencies, you're supposed to turn around. If you don't then your progress will reset until you pass through the normal tunnel eight times and reach exit 8.

For such a simple concept it's actually pretty fun. I will say the only thing I don't like is that when the jump scares "get you" it simply goes to a black screen. I would've preferred some horrifying end for our unnamed protagonist. Once you understand how the game is played it can be completed in under an hour, but for a budget walking simulator horror title that's pretty common. It's unique, playable and does get under your skin a bit.
