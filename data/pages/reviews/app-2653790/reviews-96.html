\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short but sweet experience that can be completed in around an hour. Keep that in mind when choosing to buy this, though I got it on sale for cheap and am happy with the price I paid.

The best way to describe this game is a 3D interactive spot-the-difference puzzle. You are dropped into a seemingly-infinite sequence of corridors based on a Japanese underground tunnel. There is one 'standard' sequence of corridors and your job is to decide if you are in the standard sequence or an anomalous one. Sometimes the anomalies are extremely obvious and sometimes they are extremely subtle, so you have to pay close attention to everything happening around you.

The aim of the game is to reach exit 8 and each time you fail to identify if you're in a standard or anomalous sequence, you get put back to exit 0. This means you need to get 8 sequences correct in a row in order to win the game. The gameplay is simple and there's not much more to it - once you do, you win and that's it. The anomalies are randomised each time so you can't rely on a simple walkthrough, though you will eventually identify all the anomalies through trial and error.

What this game does well is capture a very liminal experience in video game form. Simple as the environments are, having walked similar looking underground tunnels myself in Japan, it feels authentic. At the same time, the artificially lit, mostly empty, and seemingly infinite tunnels capture a sense of liminal eeriness that thankfully never dipped too far into spooky/horror territory. This combined with the bizarre and varied anomalies left an impression on me beyond a simple spot-the-difference game. It was a short experience, but I very much enjoyed the time spent with it.\t\t\t\t\t
