\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCheck out my play through here:

https://youtu.be/WFyJcZwa7DE

'The Exit 8' takes elements of 'PT' and 'I'm on Observation Duty' and makes you feel paranoid, uneasy and will leave you second guessing upon every loop. Minimalist design aesthetic and the specific non-music approach allows you to focus on the Foley work at play - the smattering of feet in the distance, the clink-clink of the air vents - can be probably completed in 15 minutes or 40 minutes dependant on if you're a completion hunter and want to try and find all the anomalies.

PROS:

- Interesting premise with minimal design.
- Completion Hunters will want to get all the anomalies
- Unnerving horror - not gory

CONS:

- Game drops you right in without title screen, UI could be improved so that player feels they have access from the beginning to tweak any adjustments before playing.
- No 'story' to speak of - (subjective whether this is just an alpha preview for something in the pipeline)
- Some players may feel scrutinised for their lack of spacial awareness in capturing the anomalies and may feel frustrated because of this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tend to not recommend games which you can complete before the steam time limit of 2 hours to refund, but I think this game is thoughtful in it's design and I want to support the developer in the hopes that they continue to make interesting games, or expand on this one.

In short, this is a cross between a "Spot the Difference" game and a "Walking Simulator."

Things this game does well:
- Sets a tense and at times eerie, but never overly oppressive atmosphere.
- Not all anomalies are equally difficult to detect which keeps you guessing.
- Sound Design (or lack of) sets the tone of the game very well.

Things I wish were different:
- Different modes or variants for a little more content.
- More anomalies of various kinds.
- More interactions with said anomalies.

A good example of what I mean in the last one is that some anomalies are quite obvious to the point of being almost unavoidable, such as when the red water flows down the hallway or the camouflaged human but present alternate fail states if you linger in the hallway for too long. I think this is a great touch and these interactions are by far the most interesting part of the game.

Overall, interesting concept. Quite short and simplistic for $5 but interesting enough to not feel egregious either. If you're into these type of weird, slightly suspenseful walking simulators, then you'll probably enjoy this game. If you're not then you shouldn't start with this one as it is quite short and doesn't have much content.\t\t\t\t\t
