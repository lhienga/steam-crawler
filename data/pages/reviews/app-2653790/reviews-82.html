\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginally, I was going to give a very hesitant recommendation. I was about an hour in and got close to beating it (as the developer is kind enough to warn you in the store page) and I felt like it wasn't really worth the price for an hour experience. I almost beat the game multiple times after that and I didn't manage until 4 hours of gameplay because I'm blind to a lot of minor details.

So, TL;DR of that: If you're blind like me, it's 100% worth the money. If not, you might have mixed feelings or regret buying it (but hey, under 2 hours, you can refund if it's really that bad). All in all, I think it's a recommendation.

My impressions:

The gameplay itself is a unique twist on I'm On Observation Duty style games where you spot anomalies. The twist being it's one infinite hallway and you must turn back if you see an anomaly. This means it's essentially one "house" of these style of games so it's inevitably less worth the purchase versus games in the same genre for nearly the same price.

At first, I thought it was far too subtle, but after beating the game and seeing all anomalies, I do think they're about the right amount of subtlety. They designed some fun and interesting anomalies and a couple are 'dangerous' and reset your game.

It's a short experience, but I think it's worth the price for how unique it is.

Things I worked out while playing:

Something I wish I had known is that you seem to be able to go back when you see it reset to Exit 0 and check what anomaly you missed. I'm not 100% sure, because I feel like I went back and saw anomalies I definitely checked for. If it is the case, this is crucial to learning the game and I wish it would be on the "tutorial" sign.

Another thing that's important that I'm pretty sure is the case: You can take as long as you want before you see an anomaly to check if the hallway is normal. I started to wonder as I was going whether simply walking backwards or turning backwards would count as "going back", but I eventually figured out they don't (up to actually leaving the hallway of course). I started to take my time and actually check everything and it made the difference.

It got to a point where I was questioning myself so much that I made a list of the anomalies. I'm still not sure if I jotted them all down, but here's what I found if you're curious/frustrated:
Man - !!HUGE MAN!!
Man - Runs fast (dangerous)
Man - Glitched face
Man - Looks at you
Man - Smiles creepily
No Smoking sign - Duplicated all over the walls/floor/ceiling
Floor - Yellow tiles are smudged instead of lines
Floor - Yellow star pattern in the line
Walls - Tiles change size/move (Unsure of this one)
Ceiling - Smudge that looks like a face near the middle
Ceiling sign - Whole sign is upside down
Ceiling sign - Concourse instead says "Turn back" repeatedly
Ceiling lights - Angled weirdly
Ceiling lights - Lights go out at the end of the hall
Ceiling lights - Lights start to flicker halfway down the hall
Security camera - Red light on and following you
All posters - Grow larger
BLC poster - Person melted like water splashed on it
Makeup poster - All posters change to it
MOVEFES poster - Creepy image
Security cam notice - Entire page follows you along the wall
Security cam notice - Shifty eyes (returns to normal for a second or two)
Security cam notice - Eyes follow you (likely just bad timing on my part and it's not a separate anomaly from the shifty eyes one)
Door 1 - Disappears
Door 1 - Banging on door
Door 2 - Open with someone creeping on you in the darkness
Door 3 - Unlocks and swings open as you pass it (dangerous, but just don't go in)
Door 3 - Handle in the middle of the door
Vents - Black ooze coming from them
Special - Twins (dangerous if you get too close)
Special - Blood waves (dangerous)
Special - Wall man (dangerous, but can escape)
Special - Stairs up (dangerous)

Update: I tested and watched other people play the game and I think I can conclusively say there isn't an anomaly for each poster, which I thought would happen which knocks the total anomalies down to 31 or 32 total. Pretty decent amount of them, honestly. Weird number though. Makes me think I'm missing something.
