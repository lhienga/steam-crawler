\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been following the development of this game on Twitter for a while now, so I was super excited to see the game had finally released into Early Access!

The first thing I want to say to get it out of the way, is that I did try playing on Steam Deck and it did not work well. Alex posted a video on Twitter of the game running on Steam deck so I was excited to try it out, but it did not work well, from my experience. The UI was buggy but the worst part was that the game would crash whenever I tried to exit a level. After it crashing on me 3 times in 15 minutes, I decided to hold off on playing it on the Steam Deck. I just want to warn anyone going in thinking it will be a good game to play handheld, that as of now, the game does not run well on Steam Deck.

Now on to the PC version of this game, where I've played about 7 hours: I have already completed all of the quests in story mode. I know it is still in Early Access and there will be more quests added in the future, so I understand, although I do wish there was more, because I had a ton of fun with the quests! I am looking forward to future updates when more quests are made available. The puzzle quests were an especially unique feature and I'm excited to see how that is utilized in future updates! As for the other types of quests, they were all well done but I would love to see more quests centered around fixing up a space. A few of the quests would put you in a bare room and give you a list of furniture and you would place the furniture however you wanted to, and those were good, but I always find it much more rewarding to be given a space that is full of junk that I can throw away or organize, with furniture that needs to be rearranged or repurposed into the space. I would also like to see quests have some requirements such as where some objects should be placed or what color the furniture should be.

There are some concerns, mostly about some bugs or general UI issues. The most irritating bug happens when changing colors for furniture. Sometimes the furniture doesn't change color when you select a color and sometimes when you use the color picker it turns the object into a random color before you've even clicked on anything. The other thing that has stuck out to me the most is the camera not scrolling far enough to the edge when working on large rooms. There's an invisible wall that cuts you off from moving any further so you can't get up close to things past that wall. It's still functional, but its irritating not being able to get your screen centered over outer edges of large rooms because of this restriction. One more bug I ran into today was after buying the most expensive apartment. I opened up the balcony just to see what an "outdoor" space would look like and when I left, walls had suddenly popped up around the balcony so that it looked like a normal room. That balcony also doesn't have a door, so I'm not sure how anyone living there would get out there, but that's just a nitpick.

I haven't messed around in creative mode yet, so I'm not sure if that presents new features, but career mode gives you so much money for owning properties that I could build anything in Career mode without being worried about money, and there aren't any items that are locked behind progression. The properties were easy to buy too because they weren't very expensive. I finished all quests (and skipped the one where you had to push the cart through the crowded store because that challenge was awful) and purchased all available properties, all in about 5 hours. I would like to see some sort of management page where I can look into the properties I've bought and see exactly how much revenue they're bringing in so I know where the random increases of money are coming from.

Overall, its a very solid game so far. It has tons of furniture options and I like that I can change the materials and colors of different parts of all of the items of furniture. I will say it is currently severely lacking in textures like tile, fabric, and wallpaper materials, but as this game is still EA, I'm sure there will be more options in the future. Also, this game would benefit greatly from workshop integration. I'm not sure if Alex plans on adding this game to the community workshop, but if he does, I am excited to see it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot of potential. The features that are already here (and there's a lot of them) are very intuitive and allow for the type of creative freedom that is very reminiscent of The Sims. It's also nice to see that this isn't just a sandbox experience, but an actual game as well where the player can earn money cleaning up, renovating, and decorating various spaces, to then buy their own properties to do the same, but with less restrictions. For those like me that do enjoy a good sandbox, it's also available and only requires the player to go through a small tutorial to unlock it.

Unfortunately, the game suffers from game/feature breaking bugs in its current early access phase. For the most part, the game runs smoothly and without too much of a hitch, but when bugs do pop up, it does tend to get in the way of the experience and in a few cases, it might put you in a position where furniture just snaps somewhere into the room, the undo button doesn't work or the game simply crashes.

Another thing that currently seems to be limited is the amount of content you can play around with. Although enough to get a good sense of things, it'll be important that the library of assets increases drastically to keep people interested in the game and allowing them to express themselves a lot better. My only hope is that the developer doesn't go the same route as The Sims and makes furniture packs into paid DLC along the way. That would be a surefire way to lose all traction, considering that the audience open to these games is really looking for alternatives to EA's money grabbing tactics. But I have a feeling that the pricing of this game indicates that the developer doesn't want to lure people in to then put things behind a paywall, so I'm optimistic about free future content expansion.

All of the above issues, however, seem to be rather straightforward and based on the overall quality, presentation, and performance of this game in its current state, I'm not worried at all that it will just be a matter of time before these are fixed and the game will be ready for a full launch. It is for that reason that, despite the drawbacks, I give this game a positive rating and I wish the developer all the best in getting things up to snuff.

TL;DR: A great little cozy game with a strong technical foundation, in need of bug fixing and more in-game content, but on the right track to becoming a great indie title.\t\t\t\t\t
