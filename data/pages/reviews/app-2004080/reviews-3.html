\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA staggering amount of AI is used to make this game, which was very disappointing. It makes it blatantly obvious that this is just a cash-grab, with little to no love or care put into it. The graphics are hypnotically unoriginal.

The gameplay footage does not match the previews at all. While the mini games are fun and original, many of them, like the plumbing and domino, are broken and complete before you even have a chance to solve them. The menus are annoying to navigate, they are not intuitive nor do they make much sense, there was not much thought behind how a menu should work.

There is no real sense of progression or difficulty since all of the furniture is dirt cheap, and after making the first store investment, the rest of the money flows in with absolutely zero effort necessary.

I really wanted to love this game, it genuinely seemed like everything I wanted. Bugs in early access on release day are to be expected, obviously, but the overwhelming use of AI generated assets and the lack of love put into the game leaves much to be desired. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game for a little over two hours and here are my thoughts so far:

The game is simple, relaxing, and a lot of fun. If you like decorating rooms in the sims or House Flipper games, then you'll more than likely enjoy this game as well. There are two modes to play: story mode and sandbox mode. I've spent most of the time in story mode and I love the challenges and rooms available to play. The puzzle rooms are well done and provide a bit of a challenge, but not so much that it's frustrating or nerve wracking. (And I say this as someone who failed the domino room several times...) This game truly is centered around going your own pace and doing things your own way.

Sandbox mode was better than I expected it to be in EA. There are tons of furniture options and a room editor, so the player really can create to the limits of their imagination. The controls for decorating really allow you to go wild with different colors, styles, materials, etc. I personally love being able to make everything match with the same wood tones and paint colors; the game makes is really easy too! There's a duplicate item function and an eye dropper tool so it's simple to design the perfectly matching room! (If that's your cup of tea.)

Now on to the few things I hope to see improved:
- It is a bit frustrating not being able to zoom in/out while an item is picked up since it rotates the item instead. Different buttons for these would be nice.
- There are some furniture items I'd like to see added in the future, like corner options for kitchen counters.
- Speaking of counters, it is a bummer not being able to edit the colors or styles. Maybe this is a bug, but if not, I hope to see it changed in a future update. It's been one of the only major furniture items I've found so far that isn't customizable.

I'm really enjoying the game so far and I'm looking forward to see where it goes from here until the 1.0 release!\t\t\t\t\t
