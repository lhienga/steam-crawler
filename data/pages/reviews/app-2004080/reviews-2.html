\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know that this game is only in early access, so I’ll do my best not to be too critical.

For starters, I love the game. I love the stylish polygonal design of all the furniture and animals. And also it’s like a top-down version of House Flipper, which I’ve sank an unholy amount of time into.

But, that might be where my pros end. Again, I know it’s in early access, but I hope that these can be added and resolved soon enough. First off, I’m not the biggest fan of having to go all the way back to the main menu just to change graphics settings, all it does is just waste time. You should be able to do that just by pressing escape, but you can’t. Second, I don’t know if this is just my experience or not, but when I put my graphics settings to either medium or even low, all of my shadows will start flickering, but they stop when I set the shadows to high or ultra. My last gripe is just something that I hope they add later to the game and that is CONTROLLER SUPPORT. This type of game is perfect for controller usage.

Unfortunately, I can’t recommend the game at the current state. It’s only $14 USD, but I’m still getting my money back. Hopefully I can come back to this once it’s in a better state.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has enjoyed the Sims thoroughly, I do find this game fun to play. I put around 2 hours into it so far.

There are two 'gamemodes' if you like: story, which you follow along with different clients, buy real estate to work on, complete puzzles, etc. There's also sandbox, which is a free build mode. I found both of these quite nice to play around with.

Now this game is still in EA, so I did come across some issues. Sometimes after using the colour picker, my cursor would remain stuck as the dropper tool. I also really struggled with placing curtains and moving doors, an L shape room provided rather tricky as it kept aligning with the adjoining wall instead. I also found not every item can be customised, my dream of a white monitor crushed. Also some kitchen items.

Despite these, the game has a nice variety of items, textures and colours. The story mode is very varied and isn't too challenging with the puzzles, however the option of a hint (domino room because finding them repeatedly was half the problem) would be nice. Furthermore, since the rooms aren't being interacted with, being able to place bigger items closer together (thing bb.moveobjects for a simmer) would be nice but isn't a necessity.

Overall, I'd recoomend this to anyone who likes a relaxing gameplay and enjoys decorating and building up rooms! I'm excited for what the Dev will work on once out of EA, particularly mod support. Hope this was helpful :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is within the first 72 hours of the game's release in Early Access and my review will be edited as time goes on.

02/21/2024 - Pros and cons vary inbetween.

Pros:
Sizing the items. - I greatly enjoy that this is given from the very beginning.
Color Wheel. - Pretty self explanatory.
Pets - A small selection of animals gives it a more life like feeling.
Earning Cash - It's nice to know that putting time and effort into your projects gives you a long term cash flow.

Now... for the cons: (This will include spoilers on the story mode)
Dominos - Now I love this idea, but I am not a fan of the fact that if you should fail on just one domino, you must restart the entire level.
Grid - The grid is hard to understand, you can adjust it, but it doesn't give an indepth idea of what you're doing fully. The grid also being white is not favorable.
Cash Flow - Now while I did label this as a pro, there is also a con that comes with it. You have no idea how the projects you put time into bring in your cash. You don't know how to increase or decrease the income, there's nothing that let's you specifically make a store into something; library, cafe, store etc etc. You don't know how you're earning what you're earning. I like to have an itemized list of what my properties are doing for me, what I could do to improve them, and possibly a way to make them focused on a type of shop. Even if it's a simple list, a list and an explanation of what I'm doing would be extremely helpful.
Tutorial - There is a lot of mechanics that the tutorial does not explain. There are features that I have no idea what they do because it was not explained to me. Having a longer tutorial that explains all the features is better than having a short tutorial that doesn't explain everything.

Obviously this game is still in early access, so please take my opinions into a small note and try the game for yourself. This game does have great potential and I am very interested in seeing which path it will take. For a one man team, this game is extremely entertaining.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThí is a relaxing and fun game where I can have my dream setups and rooms. The graphics are simple but pleasant, and the gameplay is quite smooth and intuitive
However, the game does have a few flaws. First, it can be quite buggy at times. I experienced several crashes during my time playing, and I also found that some of the furniture and decorations would not snap into place correctly. Second, the game's selection of furniture and textures is a bit limited. I would have liked to see more variety in terms of both style and color. And it is sometimes a bit laggy, especially when changing the color and texture of the furniture.
Overall, Furnish Master is a fun and enjoyable game that is perfect for anyone who loves interior design. It is a bit buggy and limited in terms of content, but it is still a great way to relax and de-stress. I am looking forward to seeing how the game develops in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't get me wrong, I do belief that the core concept of the game is there and I also don't mean to be a big douche on the developer. It kinda reminds me of Unpacking with the isometric view, but with a larger emphasis on creativity and an actual Sandbox mode, the latter of which I'd like to see so much in Unpacking. The interactive furniture is also neat as well, being able to manually open cupboards and wardrobes and place items within them.

Unfortunately though, even thought its in Early Access, the game just feels a bit too buggy to fully enjoy properly. One of the bugs that I encountered was during the kitchen decoration mission in the main campaign mode. In said mission, you need to place down all of the furniture given to you. I placed down three kitchen cabinets, only for me to not like where I placed them and used undo to get rid of them. However, the game still thought that the cabinets were there according to the objective in the top right of the screen, and that the fact that the cabinets weren't showing within the selection of furniture I still had to place down. This forced me to reset my entire progress on that mission just to fix this issue. I should also probably mention that during a mission, you can't just leave it and then come back to it later; you have to restart the whole thing again if you wanna do that, which is rather dumb.

I also like how you change the colours and textures of practically any object. However, the textures themselves could do with some more choice. I mean, as of writing, there's only one fabric texture.

Look, I don't mean to be harsh considering that this is, again, an Early Access game and that the developer plans to work hard on the game and improve its issues before its full release, meaning that I think Early Access is a good idea for it. It's just think that you should wait around 4-6 months before purchasing this game. That way, hopefully most of the underlying issues the game has should be fixed, or at least improved upon. But for me, the current state of the game makes it hard for me to recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually do reviews but i do think despite some of the minor bugs i've experienced, this is a solid relaxing game. If you like games like unpacking or house flipper you kinda get the best of both worlds. I like that its not just strictly redecorating and renovating, there's a level for 'plumbing' where you resize and rotate pipes to fit the puzzle. There's also hidden object mini games with domino's, puzzle pieces and car parts. You also have the freedom to decorate multiple spaces of your own with lots of furniture and color options. I like that there isn't many rules at all - besides some in story mode - and you can pretty much do anything your interior designer heart desires!
I will say, however, I do wish there were more levels in story mode because I finished the game in just a few hours.
8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
