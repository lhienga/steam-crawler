\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game's main worth comes from it's Zombies Mode. Without any DLC purchased, the game itself will be scarce in content. Thanks to a lackluster campaign and a nearly unplayable multiplayer riddled with malicious cheaters, we're left with just the one base Zombies map: Shadows of Evil. Given this fact, I would recommend having at least the Zombies Chronicles edition of the game, but obviously the Zombies Deluxe edition will be better as it contains all 5 original post-launch Black Ops III Maps, as well as Zombies Chronicles which brings 8 classic Zombies maps back from World at War (again), Black Ops & Black Ops II.

A quick review for base-game content:

Shadows Of Evil. The map itself is phenomenal, and the new mechanics Black Ops III added to zombies as a game mode give a needed sense of progression to game-play and new tools for the casual player to achieve higher rounds than they might have on previous Zombies titles. Topped with well-aged graphics and an amazingly curated art style based on a Lovecraftian Horror-esque setting, Shadows of Evil is still worth playing above any Zombies games that have come after Black Ops III.

Gobblegums. Love them or hate them, Gobblegums are a new part of the game that still remains completely avoidable. With a good system to buffer their use preventing imbalance in game-play, Gobblegums are able to give a wide range of effects from a fighting edge to a "Get out of Jail Free Card" and more, once per round for free, and at an increasing cost throughout the round thereafter. Core Gobblegums, which aren't limited in quantity are strong but not over-powered and can help speed up game-play and help you out in tight situations. Mega Gobblegums, which have limited uses and must be obtained through RNG to use in-game, seem like a good idea on paper but with so many variables in play, it's effectively impossible to get enough of these limited Gums to comfortably use them on anything but a special occasion. Both, however, can give a tasteful balance to game-play, and are exceptionally useful if you're interested in the complex parts of this installment of Call Of Duty: Zombies. Either plan a fun Gobblegum deck for a run on a Chronicles Map, giving a new spin to an old map - or gear them up for a try at an Easter Egg and unravel the somewhat drawn-out and contrived story of Black Ops III: Zombies.

Weapon Kits. Gone are the days of iron sights and random reticles from Pack-a-Punch. With the Weapon Kits system, level up to unlock each playable Black Ops III gun for customization, then use them to unlock new attachments and reticles for your sights. A simple but welcome change that can give a feeling of achievement even to the most uneventful maps. Weapon Grinding! Gotta Love It!

Play Black Ops III: Zombies. It's the best Zombies.\t\t\t\t\t
