\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore i start my review i have to mention 2 things:
1: As of now, the security risk older Call Of Duty games such as this one possesses seem to still exist though i haven't experienced it myself. There are no mentions or evidences that clear this issue from the developers anywhere on the internet so be extremely careful if you want to play the game.
2:My review is solely based on the campaign and nothing else.

Black Ops 3's story is unlike any other COD games before it. It takes an incredibly sharp and weird turn at how it tells its story. Its beyond confusing and left me with more questions than answers after completing it.
Now, this doesn't mean that it was bad, quite the opposite, it was engaging and thought provoking.
Some downsides come from it being so confusing though which i can compare to reading a heavy book. It gets exhausting.
Now for the gameplay, i found the addition of powers fun to use and the freedom of creating your own loadout in campaign great. What i didn't like was the lack of enemy types, encounters and in general things that made older Cod games' action scenes fun and not as repetitive as this one. Bullet sponge enemies that won't stop spawning, boss fights that lack creativity and almost everything else related to the core gameplay could've been so much better which in turn would've elevated the already great story much higher.
Because of such disparities in gameplay and the story (also the security risks) i can only recommend it to people in search of a good story or a fresh take on the COD franchise. Then again, if you don't mind the security risks, it has tons of other multiplayer modes where the gameplay is actually fun.\t\t\t\t\t
