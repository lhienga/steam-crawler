Call of Duty: Black Ops III - 2024 review

Ah yes, Call of Duty: Black Ops III, a delight! I've played COD titles religiously until they introduced the harsher SBMM in Modern Warfare 2019. I'm a big Treyarch nerd, and I've got probably between 3000 to 4000 hours throughout the Black Ops franchise. Nostalgia aside, this game is one if not the best. Campaign is pure dogsh** (probably the worst COD campaign ever, not even being dramatic), and multiplayer was incredible in its prime (now it's obviously dead and filled with cheaters), but honestly, we're all here for the Zombies gamemode (especially thanks to the custom maps!). Without further ado, I'll break down into specific categories why the Zombies gamemode makes this game so great.

Pros
  • First and foremost, shout out and a gigantic thank you to the modding community that is very active and keeps on cooking incredible maps that are Treyarch-worthy. Seriously, I can think of between 20-30 custom maps that I enjoy more than 90% of vanilla zombies maps. The content out there is magnificent, and easy to find. Without the mapping and modding community, this game would have died ages ago. The replayability is wonderful: Zombies is already a replayable gamemode per se, and thanks to the incredible people working on gigantic workshop projects even as of 2024, you'll always find something new to enjoy.
  • The game's nearly nine years old (time flies!), and yet, it still looks incredible. The vanilla maps always looked great, and there's a ton of custom maps with even greater graphics than them. The art style and the HUD can also be incredibly unique depending on the custom maps you're playing, or whether you've implemented workshop mods to tweak those aspects. You won't be let down by the visuals.
  • The vanilla zombies storyline is great, the cutscenes are enjoyable, and once again, the community keeps on adding short unofficial lore to this already notable timeline, so it just keeps on getting more interesting!
  • The gameplay's the key element here. I feel like Black Ops III is the pinnacle of the Zombies gamemode from a gameplay perspective: you've got great movement, new additions like operator gadgets, and yet a great balance with the traditional system (such as perks, pack-a-punch...), amazing Easter eggs, a perfect mix between easy and challenging maps, and much more. It's perfect!

Neutrals
  • Although the AI is mostly doing what it's supposed to do, AKA zombies chasing you, but there can be the occasional bug here and there, especially on custom maps. Zombies may get stuck, rounds may not end, etc. Since those AI-related problems are pretty rare though, I'll be keeping this point as a fact rather than a downside.
  • Again, depending on mods and custom maps installed, stability and performances may not always be great. I've got an RTX 3070 GPU and a i7-11700F Intel processor (which aren't nearly the best specs as of today, but still on the higher ends of things), and some maps may drop under 50 fps in 1080p resolution, or stutter a bit. Performances mostly depends from the creators and their time put into fixing said issues, and vanilla maps always run perfectly on my end. Same as before, I'm keeping this point as a fact rather than a downside, because 90% of the time, stability and performances won't be an issue on a high-end rig like mine.

Cons
  • I know this is a bit of a reach, since most people planning to buy this game are already familiar with how the Zombies gamemode work, but for those who aren't, be prepared for long, long games. Whether you're doing a challenge map, an Easter egg or going for a high-round, this gamemode is known to take hours before it ends (unless you mess up early, which means most of the time you'll be replaying your map instantly, further proving my point), hence it isn't friendly towards people with limited playtime per session. Yes, the game can be paused in solo, but I feel like pausing a zombies match for too long can sometimes take you out of it.
  • A lot of streamers and youtubers have experienced hacking problems. There is a decent amount of hackers in this game, just like any older COD. However, if you're not a big content creator, you should be fine. It's extremely rare that the hackers will target randoms, and it never happened to me with over 200 hours dedicated to zombies and always online. There's also a community patch to protect you covered by easy to follow YouTube tutorials if you're really scared of this issue. Nonetheless, since this problem is real and Activision doesn't care about it, I feel like this is a cheap shot towards a very active title that's still in the Top Selling category of Steam after all those years.
  • Even though the game has incredible replayability, at some point Zombies will get repetitive, since the core foundation of this game focused around PvE doesn't have any real progression once your game's over. I'll always come back to this game, but I cannot say that I will play it on a constant basis. I feel like most people will also feel this way.

TL;DR
From a perspective focused purely on the Zombies gamemode, Call Of Duty: Black Ops III is amazing. The problem is Activision: fully knowing this game sells like hotcakes, they won't try to fix an ongoing issue that causes content creators to quit. They also know that nobody plays the campaign nor the multiplayer, and yet the price tag hasn't changed after 9 YEARS! As always, corporate greed is the minor downfall of this game. I definitely recommend it, it's a lot of fun especially with friends, and you'll get your hours worth, but Activision itself is the sole reason why I think you should leave it in your wishlist until there's a considerable sale.

Rating: 9/10 \t\t\t\t\t
