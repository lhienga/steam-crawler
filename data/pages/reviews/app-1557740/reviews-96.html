\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRounds is a party game which brings so much variety to it’s core design that it creates ​a funny and never tiring experience with friends. This platformer shooter puts you ​against your friends in a duel on simplistic maps. The only catch is for every 2 rounds ​you lose (out of a maximum of 20) you can select an augment to modify how you ​attack and defend. The art style is crude and simplistic, but so unimportant for games ​of this genre that it isn’t even worth mentioning.



The gameplay is very simple, you have a pistol with an amount of ammo, and you have​ a block which is on a cooldown and can deflect bullets. These 2 features in itself are​ bland, but the augments combine with each other to make every game different.​ Augments vary from changes to your weapon like the ability to have a spray of 40​ bullets, to changes to your block allowing you to freeze your opponent in time, to​ passives like life-steal or a HP increase. The combinations at your disposal allow you​ to cook up so many wacky builds, but always feel grounded in some way. My personal​ favourite is to turn my pistol into a barrage of bullets that home in on the enemy and​ poison them afterwards. It truly is commendable the sheer number of augments they​ have made, and the freedom that they provide in what your playstyle will be due to​ how different they are from each other. Considering it is a party game designed for​ couch/LAN PVP, I have spent a surprising amount of time playing this game and 30​ odd hours in I am still not bored of the mechanics.​



If you are in search of a funny but also complex game to play with friends either online​ or on the same computer, I highly recommend this game. Fans of the stickman fight​ game will be delighted to know that it is the same developer, and that it is essentially​ an evolution of their design philosophy. Overall this game is an absolute gem and a​ testament to just how successful a simple game can be, If the variety and replayability​ is there.​
