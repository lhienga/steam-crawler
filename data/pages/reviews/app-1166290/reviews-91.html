\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game! I heard it was very similar to Papers Please (I'm still yet to play it), but I have a feeling this game has won my heart.
You simply play as a death spawn, and choice do in fact matter. You can either stick to the rules, make your boss happy and go about being a loyal grim reaper. Or... you can turn to a more rebellious side, just be careful not to get fired. Either way, just do what feels right for you!

The characters are charming (Mortimer best lad), and there are multiple endings. My first play through I... am not sure if I expected the outcome or not, I'm not surprised to say the least.
There is philosophical dialogue splashed about here and there that genuinely made me sit back in my chair and think about things for a while. Not to seem all edgy but death really is something interesting when you truly think about it. Death is just simply what you make of it in the end. Honestly though, I'm not scared of death really, it just seems like something really boring :/

P.S. Don't ask about the plant department.\t\t\t\t\t

Excellent Art-Style, a Bit Existential, Funny!

You know how there's two certainties in life? How'd you like to control at least one of them. In this game you play as a Grim Reaper in a sort of Astral Bureaucracy that determines when people are to die. Your job is to mark papers that describe people to determine if they live or die. Will you save the world or destroy it? The Power is Yours!

The gameplay is pretty simple, you're given instructions each day of how many people need to die. You can choose to obey or ignore these orders, and your choice will have a tangible effect on the world which you can find out via your in-game phone or just a snowglobe that you can purchase in game with your meager salary as a middle-manager.

Your choices have tangible weight and you can use the profile text to determine if your bringing about a Utopia or Armageddon. The story is pretty interesting and peppered with a bit of sardonic humor and even a little bit of existentialism. The world is build beautifully making you understand the motivations of the characters and why you're there.

I'd rate Death and Taxes a 10/10. It's a good time and multiple endings add a lot of replayability\t\t\t\t\t
