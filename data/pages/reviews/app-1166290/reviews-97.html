\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHard 6/10 - it is alright.

The art is great and I enjoy talking to most of the characters I come across, but the main bit of the game (Choosing who to kill; Giving me a moral dilemma to decide upon) isn’t that great, the constant “drama” of “oh, don’t you feel BAD about killing all these people” is meh, it’s not like the people you decide to sentence for “death” can argue with you, you only get a small snippet of their life. Maybe that's the point because after you get some items for your desk you realize just how will some of the decisions affect the world humans live in and then it becomes a numbers game, especially after the first playthrough. So in that sense, it succeeds in both death and taxes.

The choices do matter and will lead you into a lot of different branching stories to explore, but they kind of just die out and you realize you don't care. There is no time limit to your choice, so you can decide for as long as you please, but it lacks a sense of excitement and urgency a game like “Papers, Please” offers and turns into a boring grind to get to the end - for some a plus, for me a minus. Maybe it’s done to mimic the dull days of an office job, but that’s something that isn’t fun in actual life, so why add it in-game.

I don't mind having politics in game, but don't box me in one of the sides, if the game is about choice, give me the choice to roleplay as an absolute human garbage.

The customization is generous but ultimately pointless.
The soundtrack is good.
Overall, it's lacking. I'm disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
