Bound by Fate - live or die. Choose wisely.

In Death and Taxes you play as a grim reaper whos been created by Fate. Fate also happens to be your employer and he is the person who you must be reporting to at every end of the day. The gameplay is fairly simple. Each day we sit down at our desk and are given various profiles of people who Fate wants us towell, decide their fate. Each day comes with some specific requirements such as having to kill a certain amount of people or having to spare people between a certain age. The question throughout the game is who do you kill and who do you spare?

Whoever we choose to kill or let live also has an impact on the story as these affect the status quo within the human population. We can check out how our work is affecting the living by keeping an eye on our mobile phone which displays a news feed on how life on Earth is. This is one aspect in determining what ending we get when the job is done.

You can either aim for certain endings or just abide by your own morals. Do you kill the bad and save the good? Or do you play God and try to make the world a better place? The choice is ultimately yours but it may not end up being the right or proper choice. This is what makes Death and Taxes a wonderful game. There are so many directions the game can go and its really straight down to your actions.

Overall Death and Taxes is an interesting and entertaining game. The first few runs offer some great choice based gameplay and it is honestly tough being a Grim Reaper. However you might gradually find it to be quite a chore for trying to get all the endings as there isnt much variation in dialogue choices or human profiles. Nevertheless with a few comedic lines and human profiles its a great little game of life and death.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven the afterlife won't save you from the office duty, it seems.

That's what you're gonna do in Death and Taxes. Assuming a role of employed reaper, you decide each day whom to kill and whom to let live. Every choice you make will have impacts upon the human world. You get some clues from the news received daily on the smartphone. You end the day and earn some money to spend on drinks, clothes, or gadgets (that is, if you follow your employer's instruction). Overall it's a lovely game with nice soundtracks. Marvelous dialogues, I must add. And the narrators have done a superb job.

My concern is about the game's replay value. You are going to get bored at some point, reading all those brief biographies and repeating your work routine day after day. Which is kinda cool in a sense if you think about it. I mean, the game kinda captured the essence of office life there. What an irony.
