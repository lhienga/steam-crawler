\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that's an unique gem. I mean, yes, the basic premise is the same as in Papers, Please, but the general tone is completely different and this game feels larger somehow. The idea is simple: you choose who lives and who dies. There are some rules set out by your boss, Fate... but the, shall we say, interpretation of those rules is up to you, if you even choose to follow them.

Of course, every choice has some kind of consequences. It's a little hard to see at first, but eventually you'll start noticing the bigger picture. What do you want the human world to be like? The game's quite political - in a way, this game asks you for your opinions on matters like taxes, the environment, industry... and even though ultimately your choices boil down to marking a profile to LIVE or DIE, a clear web of interdependent little things forms itself over time; a crystallisation of your political choices.

The aesthetic itself is great. Elevator jazz with a dash of doom, suits, skulls, cigarettes, a strong drink after work... and some existential questions left to ponder at 3 AM when you can't sleep. Unlocking new customisation options for your deathly visage is one of my favourite parts. As expected, the writing is also pretty nice, with quality voice acting to make it go down smoother. Many conversations feel like you're making a difference in the big ol' death institute, and who knows, maybe you are?

Yes, you are. The game has multiple endings. The ending I got was very satisfying for me, so I'm going to put a dot on this one. But if you want, the game straight up asks you at the end: hey, would you like to try again, maybe get different ending this time? So if that's what you like, the game has plenty of paths to take. And no worries; there are so many mortal profiles to judge over, you probably won't run into repetitions for a while.

All in all, a good time. Perhaps a little dull at times, but that's what this is about, isn't it? A grim reaper at a desk. In fact, the mundanity of it all is one of the themes of the game. Looking for a sharp, unique experience about balance, conformity, and the nature of life? Brew yourself a nice cup of coffee (or tea; coffee seems more thematically appropriate, however) and enjoy the show, even for the full price.\t\t\t\t\t
