\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn Death and Taxes, you play as a grim reaper who has to go through life in an office, filling out paperwork every day. You can do all sorts of fun things between your shifts -- chat up the bartender, discuss plant death, decorate your desk -- but the majority of your time will be spent navigating your boss' requirements of you as you go about your job. That "job", however, just so happens to be that of deciding who lives and who dies. It's as simple as a stamp on a bit of paperwork. Just that easy.

Death and Taxes isn't exactly an... exciting game? In some ways, it is tedious, a fact that comes up in quite a few of the negative reviews. ( It's also funny, and poignant, and lots of other things, but "tedious" is what might make you hesitate to buy this, and therefore what I want to talk about specifically. ) I wouldn't be dissuaded by that fact, though. I think, in many ways, an "exciting" game would have defeated the point of the central dilemma and message here. Your character's day-to-day life is repetitive. It's dull. It's disconnected from life and all the impacts of your decisions. But that's what makes this game hard-hitting for me: it is so easy not to care, or to care only in the abstract, when the consequences of your decisions don't actually reach you. The parallels to things in the real world probably don't need to be sketched out for anyone. Death and Taxes is exactly as wonderful as it is because, while you're surrounded by personality and pathos in the form of your coworkers and office, it reminds you how easily life-changing decisions can be made from distant offices or desks -- and it asks us to remember to still care about the bigger picture, even if you can't see it firsthand.

Or not. You can also start an apocalypse! You have to love the balance of a game's narrative and that urge to get all the achievements you can...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game when it was on sale on itchio a long awhile ago, so a lot of my time went there rather than here. This game is one where the premise of gets in the way of the game itself.

This game is like Papers Please (sorry for comparing these two but its best to do so to understand what I am going to say), with the idea of you having a direct effect on the world around you while at work. The biggest issue with this game compared to one like Papers Please is the time of your effects on the world and events. You are not told about what happens in the world due to your actions until the day after you have done, but nothing visually changes, its all just word of mouth despite how powerful your actions are.

The second issue with this game is how little you feel connected to the world in which you "control". Someone else has already mentioned it in their review in better detail but it doesn't really feel as if you are connected with the world because of the fact that you are already dead and will never actually interact with everyone who isn't dead.

I would sadly not recommend this game, I would just recommend anything else instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, quite clearly this is a game that is meant to be played through multiple times. This is obvious in various ways but I do not think it is at all a spoiler to say that.

And with that out of the way: I can't bring myself to play this game again and that is 100% a compliment.

In my first playthrough I basically got exactly what I wanted in terms of consequences. But the writing, the characters, the real impact your decisions seem to have made me hesitant to "risk" that world. I know that in other playthroughs you are supposed to mess with the systems: let everyone live, let everyone die, let the best people live every time, let the worse people die every time no matter what.

But, I got what I wanted out of my first playthrough and the writing, the world, the characters, were so believable that I didn't want to deviate from that path.

If I ever do play this game again, it'll be because I played one final time getting that optimum ending again so that way I know I've left it with a good ending, no matter what.\t\t\t\t\t
