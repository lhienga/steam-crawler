\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeath and Taxes is a fun and quirky little game. The idea is unique and compelling and the execution is clean and simple. My first playthrough was very fun and took somewhere around 1.5-2 hours. I was initially drawn to the game because I enjoyed Papers, Please and I like anything that is themed around death and/or the afterlife. This is NOT like Papers, Please, but I do recommend it and it IS worth full price, considering that the full price is pretty cheap. I think that paying thirteen dollars even for just an hour of entertainment is a solid deal.

Although I recommend this game, I do want to temper certain expectations so that nobody is disappointed. Gameplay wise... There's not much, in all honesty. The majority of the game is choosing whether or not paper profile lives or dies. The fun comes from reading the little blurbs on the profiles and playing god, deciding who deserves to live or die the most. Other areas of "fun" are the short dialogues with the few NPCs in the game.

The writing is average. There's nothing particularly amazing about it, but it's not bad either. There are some funny bits to keep it interesting. There's also full voice acting for the characters, which is admirable and does add to the game. In my opinion, some of the voices are kind of obnoxious but that's a very subjective opinion and overall, I think that the game would feel much emptier without.

The biggest negative of Death and Taxes is its lack of replayability. I have 7.2 hours logged, but around 5 of those were achievement hunting since I originally was trying to 100% the game. I currently have 45/60 achievements but for some reason, a couple of achievements are bugged for me so I've stopped trying and I've uninstalled the game.

There just isn't enough to do or read to warrant playing multiple times. There are different endings (I've gotten all of them), but those are also not worth playing multiple times. I practically had all the profile blurbs memorized after my second playthrough. Eventually, you're just clicking as fast as you can, desperately trying to hurry the animations so you can fulfill (or not) the 1-3 simple instructions on your missive. I wish there were more profiles that could randomly show up and more rules I had to consider when marking my choices. There's also a disconnect between the player and the humans/world, which makes the decisions easy and take away from any emotional weight.

Ultimately, I have to reiterate that I do recommend this game. It's cheap and the first playthrough is fun, even if there's not much meat afterwards. I think that there's a very sincere effort from the devs and it's a cute game. For a short, indie game, it's incredibly charming and definitely a hit.\t\t\t\t\t
