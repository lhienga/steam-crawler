\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLazy game.

This game opens with a lot of expectation with the good art and voice acting and world building. But the initial good first impression doesn't lasts long.
I got bored halfway and rushed through the days, barely reading the bios, without talking to anyone in the bar, just finishing my job and talking to Fate and going to sleep.

The game suddenly ends with no buildup to the story. Someday its your final day and you barely notice.

There are just many better "bureaucratic decision simulator" games out there. Avoid this.
I can recomend Headliner Novinews as an example of far superior writing for a game of this kind.

*Spoilers ahead*

And I got the "Goodest Ending", on my first try, with no effort. (and I did slaughter everyone in two different days)

You are never conflicted for your decisions, there is no buildup, there is no climax, no plot twist, no foreshadowing. In the beginning of the game, your boss is all the time talking about "balance", how you cant just kill everyone, etc. suddenly he decides to go in a vacation, he comes back and have an epiphany where he decides he wants all of mankind to die. One day you just walk into his office, one day like any other ordinary day, you tell him "if you are unhappy maybe you should just step down and let me do your job", and he agrees, and thats it. You save mankind, everyone is in an utopia now, best ending. The end.


Really? FFS.
I bought this game knowing this is a Papers Please clone and that it would have no real gameplay, but this genre of game are expected to have great writing to compensate for it, and this game lacks it.

Also worth noting, Fate's plan was to exterminate mankind and I followed his orders multiple times to kill people involved with medicine and technological development which in theory should prevent the best ending, so there is a chance the game is completely bugged.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI actually really enjoyed this game. The story was cute, funny and morbid all bundled up with cute graphics. It certainly has its similarities to Papers, Please in terms of gameplay as well. The voice acting was also pretty good. Reading all the news definitely makes your choices much more interesting by showing the consequences of them. I loved that there were various endings, too. It definitely is one of the better choices matter game.

The only issue I had was when one of my save files randomly got reset. The dev was quick to help me out (I was able to find a backup file in the save folder). It had seemed that my save file had been corrupted somehow. Other than that, I had no issues with the game.

Overall, I definitely recommend giving this game a try. Especially if you enjoy CYOA or choices matter type of games.\t\t\t\t\t
