\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's rather shallow, short, and plays too much to stereotypes. The game focuses more on utilizing "this guy abuses kids/drunk drives/etc." cues than instigating meaningful decisions. Striving for any sort of good ending, it's almost always better to let some charitable old guy live than some mediocre girl live because the game assigns more "good points" to his living than "bad points" to her dying. Being able to immediately find the "right choices" based on these stereotypes or whatever agenda was being played to (the right to tax and bear arms came up quite a bit) made for bland, monotonous gameplay. As a side gripe, it focused too much on implementing pop culture references in the game than it did to address these perceived shortcomings. What it boils down to is that while the game has a very interesting concept, art design, and soundtrack, it fails to stimulate enough thought in a setting where decision-making is the core of your gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
