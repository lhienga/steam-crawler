Problem one, the devs decided to use the term "they" to refer to single individuals, but the files you get on each person in many occasions also make references to someone related to the person of the file, so it can create nonsensical sentences as "Mary is trying to reconciliate with their grandson Cody, they love sewing and they moved to a house in the rural area". Doesn't matter if you clicked the wrong option of dialog or marked live on the wrong person, it's saved and it doesn't allow you to go back to the start of the day. Very much amateur stuff, dev probably wanted something as "you have to live with your choices", but i'm sorry, only the most incompetent office would force an employee to file something that was obviously a mistake and that's not the impression the game tries to communicate with its characters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeath and Taxes is a 'Papers, Please' style game where you sit at your desk and decide who lives or dies. Along the way you get little bobbles to spruce up your work space and to make your job so much easier.

In the end the content of the game is simple, well executed, and enjoyable. There are multiple endings so the replayability is pretty high. The main con is that each run is only about 2-3 hours. The pro to these runs is you have the option to bring everything you bought in previous runs with you, so money wont be an issue.

If you are looking for a short game that you can only play once i'd wait till it goes on one of many sales to pick it up. If you were looking to explore every achievement and ending I would pick this up at it's normal price.

It's clear that love was put into this game and I would highly recommend this game. 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
