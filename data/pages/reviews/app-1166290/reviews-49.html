\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou are Death. You kill people - or, rather, decide, who gets to die and who gets to live. And your goal is to make sure the world keeps turning.

Each day you are given some instructions by Fate, and need to follow them closely, to keep the world in balance. You get to meet a lot of ... creatures in the grim office: your superior Fate I've mentioned before, their cat lady Pawdington, lovely barkeeper Cerri, and your collegues from other departments, including this rumoured plant department you are not supposed to talk about. With this, you have 28 days to avert the crisis unfolding on earth and save the humanity.
But do not worry if you fail - after all, no-one can escape death and taxes.

The game is very reminiscent to 'Papers, Please' in form that you need to make a single decision based on provided evidence, to achieve your goal. You are given a set of seemingly correct instructions, which, however, does not necessary lead you to the victory - and you have to balance your decisions against those instructions and your interests.

'Death and Taxes' is very adorable game made with lot of love, and a lot of humour, even if it gets a bit on the dark side sometimes. Characters are cute, graphics is lovely, and the story behind is nice, even if a bit short. Here and there, there are some little neat moments you can experience - imaging suggesting to Fate that afterlife is a dating game with creatures of underworld!
Character customization adds one more layer of fun to the game. How about dooming people to death while looking as a nice catboy?.. cat-grim-reaper?..
The replayability is great as well, along with few normal endings arising from world falling into a disarray, there are few unique endings as well.
As usual, I wouldn't evaluate music since I am a very picky eater in this regards.

All in all, the game is great little toy you can have a lot fun with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow! That is the word that frequently came to my mind after (and during) my first playthrough of “Death and Taxes”. Hard to believe that a game with a title consisting of the words “death” and “taxes” could be so much fun. Though calling a game fun in which you have to decide who has to die as a main game feature is rather strange. But maybe this is one of the reasons I like this awkwardly funny game so much: it constantly forces the player to make difficult decisions, life and death deciding decisions really and delivers them with a melancholic kind of humour.
How often do you get to play a game that is funny and sad, deep and shallow, boring and exciting at the same time? “Death and taxes” offers some deeply philosophical thoughts in a very entertaining fashion. Best of all: you have to think for yourself. Get it at full price, please, as this gem of a game deserves every penny you have to spare.\t\t\t\t\t

While I didn't mind playing through the game in my initial attempts to gain the achievements, I don't find another 5 or more play throughs to be an enjoyable thought. Be warned that if you get in the same predicament, you will need to start over from scratch and do everything a second time to make the achievements pop. Very sad about this, but I have been told there is no other fix by the developers.
