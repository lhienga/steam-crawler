\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to enjoy this game, but I will not lie, I found it absolutely insufferable.

It definitely has its strengths, the voice acting is good and the art style is nice, but these in no way compensate for the other aspects.

I found all of the characters deeply irritating, so much that I just wasn't motivated to engage with any of the side-activities outside of work and eventually just gave up on them entirely. Everyone speaks for too long, with nothing to say, and that made the dialogue grating.

Besides that, the consequences of the actions seemed so far removed that none of the choices feel especially emotionally affecting. While this may have become more challenging later on, a few hours in with no emotional weight just made the experience boring, and hardly encouraged me to play any longer.

On a separate note, I'd say the price point is a little too high for what it ultimately is, but all of this is just my experience.

I'm sure lots of people will and do enjoy this game, but I couldn't recommend it to anyone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: It has all the look of a finished game, but the main gameplay loop and some of the mechanics are just underdeveloped. It is technically a playable game, but after having played it, I don't really feel like I would have missed out on something by skipping it.

----

Truth be told, I feel a bit poorly about giving this a "don't play" recommendation, but at the end of my playthrough I just have no desire to pick this up again or do another round. There are wonderful little touches throughout, and clearly some real thought and effort was put into this, but there were also just other decisions that seemed bizarre or negatively affected the experience. It seemed like the folks who built this really loved their product (which isn't a bad thing), but they loved it too much to trim the fat when they should have.

Case in point, the Emporium. Each time you make a purchase from the emporium, the shopkeeper immediately goes into a diatribe about the object and how he got it. It'd be fine if it were a one-liner, but having to skip through multi-click dialogues (especially if I bought multiple items) got tedious. Doing something as simple as making the character of the shopkeeper terse while he was on duty at the shop, but having him spill his (metaphorical) guts in the tavern could have been a fun way to mitigate that.

I tried going to the tavern a few times and interacting with the patrons, but I didn't really find it entertaining or worthwhile to stick around. That leaves the other characters: Fate and the voice in the mirror/conscience/whatever. The former was fine as a character, and as fleshed out as he needed to be. The mirror on the other hand....

This game bored me I think because the choices you had to make were so..... simplistic. Do you kill the schoolteacher who spends her time volunteering to help underprivileged youth? Or the big bad corporate overlord who makes billions of dollars on speculative investing? Deviate at all from the right choice in the game and the mirror would nag at you.

Maybe choices that force you into the gray area was not what the game was aiming for, but it seems like it's a big missed opportunity to me. There were one or two days of work in the game that really made me think, but overall the people you were evaluating were pretty obviously "right" or "wrong" according to the game, and it just was kindof a letdown to not play with that ambiguity.

I'd be interested to see what else Placeholder Gameworks could produce, and they absolutely have potential, but I didn't really enjoy this particular game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love this little game, and I remember commenting on it on Imgur some time ago when it was being posted there. I was excited then, and I'm excited now!

So the premise of the game is super simple: You're a Grim Reaper - Not THE Grim Reaper - whose job is... To sit at a desk, mark profiles of humans to live or die, talk to your boss, go to bed, and... Repeat. For a whole month. That's it.

However, the game emphasises more than just simple Papers Please-esque gameplay. It emphasises a deeper story, a deeper finality, and more dialogue and choices. And like it's... "Predecessor", it has a bunch of different endings depending on the choices you make. The story is intriguing, the voice acting is well done, the characters are sincerely interesting, and the music is surprisingly pleasant, if a bit simple.

That said, the game also sports a Streamer Mode, where players in your chat can vote on who lives or dies, allowing for much more randomized playthroughs.

All in all, the game gets 2 big thumbs up, a big thank you, and a pawsitive review. You'll understand that last one later.\t\t\t\t\t
