\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am currently in the midst of attempting to 100% the game, and was going to wait until I had finished, but I feel like writing something now.

Death and Taxes is a really fun little game. My first play through was by FAR my favourite one, and I thoroughly enjoyed my second replay as well. My first play through, I really enjoyed trying to balance making the world a better place whilst also appeasing the boss. I will not spoil endings here, but the first ending I got is my "true" ending to the game (I am unsure if there even is a canonical one).

The characters are enjoyable. Fate particularly is really well done in my opinion. It's tough to really go into depth why without unfortunately spoiling the story so the best I can say is just play for yourself. I felt Fate's voice actor did an absolutely astounding job (as did the rest of the voice acting team!!)

Additionally, I really like the world building in this game. It's not mandatory, but the flavour dialogue has really brought out a lot of creativity within me.

Not everybody is an achievement fiend, and typically I'm not, but for some reason this game I've latched onto and feel the need to 100% it. The MAJORITY of the achievements in this game I would say are really fun and good. Some of which you have to go out of your way to attain, but usually feel worth it. However, the last couple of straggler ones can be particularly frustrating.

Which is unfortunate, as personally I think even the achievements that are less enjoyable could be good with small changes! For example, For the drinks one, having somewhere you can see in some way or form what you have left. Something similar perhaps for the achievement in which you have to listen to a lot of the stories (which wouldn't be so bad if you didn't have to quickly speed through 28 days just for one story). For the imfamous one, just some way you can tell the numbered values of people (of course I imagine it would be a late game item), etc.

However, with all this talk about achievements, I've heard people be quite dramatic about it. It's a really enjoyable game. I don't think most people need to 100% it, I think playing through a couple of times is plenty and can leave you with a great experience.

I'm still determined to complete all of it and complete the steam trading card deck, purely because it's been such a long time that I've been genuinely captured by the setting and concept of a game. 100% Would recommend a playthrough or two for the story, though perhaps getting 100% of achievements isn't necessary. After I got past the 85%-ish achievement point, they started to become a bit draining.

Well done to the team behind this game, I look forward to see what they make in future :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCute fun game to play, good in short bursts especially. There are 60 fun achievements, and different things to do in the game if one pays attention, with added tiny clever secrets to find.

I look forward to playing this on Steam Deck when I get my Deck finally this century. I do think this is one of those perfect Deck games, one of many, of course. I may even stop playing this game early just to save it for Steam Deck. I am doing that with a few of my games now. I am mostly strict mouse/keyboard, but sometimes I like to relax with fun controller games...Children of Morta...Stardew Valley...Tails of Iron.

Get this game if you like games like "Papers, Please". The play style is sort of like that, just replacing Stalin-esque austerity with corporate business American austerity. Instead of drab concrete housing boxes it is drab concrete business cubicles. Somehow this drab office ends up being fun.\t\t\t\t\t
