\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is an interesting concept, but for me personally, lacks originality in the writing. After not too long, it feels a bit stale, and the characters all seem to almost "preach" the game's overall point. I'd have preferred a more nuanced, finessed approach, where "show, not tell" is applied, as the game tries to spell everything out for you, in terms of their philosophical take and the overall "hidden" message of it. This takes away from the depth and quality of immersion into the experience, as it feels like I'm being told what to think about death, humans, the universe, etc., rather than allowing the player a bit more "philosophical freedom".

It also feels a bit repetitive as far as the gameplay loop is concerned. You'll be doing pretty much the exact same tasks every single day, in a way that is not all that fun, unfortunately. This might of course be by design, as the game is about bureaucratic affairs and the boredom that comes with them, but I would have liked something a bit more stimulating, with less "gameplay friction". The fact that the writing is attempting to broach "larger than life" subjects with a shallow depth does not help, because it also becomes something to "click through" rather than an interesting conversation that you'd be looking forward to having after clicking on profiles and doing the boring bureaucratic bits.

Ultimately, it is a nice game, the art is very well drawn, and it's overall a really interesting concept, but it's let down by shallow writing, occasional "on-the-nose" voice acting, and a repetitive gameplay loop.\t\t\t\t\t
