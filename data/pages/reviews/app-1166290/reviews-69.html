\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would say that this game is fairly short in comparison to a lot of other games out there (in my experience, it's about 4-5 hours or so of gameplay, but I'll talk more about this in a moment), but it has tons of possible routes / combinations for the ending.
The 'combination ending', as I'm dubbing it, is based on the statistics of certain elements within the game, most of which (if not, all) are based on the choices you make in-game. Thus, creating lots of different endings, and subsequently lots of achievements too! So, if you're the type of player who likes to get all of the achievements and different endings, this will probably keep you occupied for a while, depending how fast you blaze through games.
As for the length of the game, at the very end you are presented with an option to either end the game entirely (a standard restart, I suppose) or restart the run, allowing your character to keep their knowledge regarding the ending, and also all of the items they have acquired, making it slightly easier to achieve the ending you want next time around.
I can see myself playing this game when I fancy playing something for a while but don't necessarily have the energy for a game like Portal, for example, which requires a fair amount of brain-power. Death and Taxes is engaging enough to keep me playing and tailored enough to make me feel like I'm not playing the exact same thing over and over again (in my opinion based on my experience). Not to mention the dialogue between yourself and the NPCs! I love how much thought and development has gone into each NPC, and the fact that they all have voice actors really brings them to life. The VAs did a really good job in my opinion!
If you're looking for a short, somewhat relaxing yet engaging and entertaining game, then I definitely recommend Death and Taxes.\t\t\t\t\t
