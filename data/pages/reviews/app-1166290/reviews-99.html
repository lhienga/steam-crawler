\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough I did end up enjoying a lot of this game - there are 2 horribly annoying things about it.

As pointed out by other reviewers, your choices don't really add up to much. All the dialogue options end up really leading to the same conclusions. Honestly - the demo is pretty much how the game is. With not much of a story otherwise. I want dialogue options that mean more and more story. when a game is repetitive, a story is the life saver.

The second annoying thing is the writers refuse to use any forms of pronouns. They say it is so when you're choosing who to live or to die - it is not based on gender. Okkaayy... but then they also don't use pronouns with each other? It's awkward. REALLY AWKWARD. There is so much that could have been done with gender. Like achievements and story lines where you kill all the women or vice versa. I don't like the constant "they/them"... it pulls me right out of the game.
I've never seen this in a game and now I'm glad that I haven't. If i could change one thing here it would be to add gender pronouns. Its one thing to be inclusive and politically correct. It's another to force it on all the players. Why not make it a settings option?

You should still buy it on sale though as there are interesting things about it. Like the art. It's beautiful and the game is a decent time waster. It's an interesting concept. And despite the annoying issues above, i was on 2nd play through before losing interest. 6 hours total. I got it on sale, so 6 hours is not so bad. Please keep in mind, that's about 1.5 play throughs.

Overall I think its fun but i wouldn't pay full price. Grab it on sale and have a few laughs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked this game up on a whim having previously enjoyed other 'admin sim' type games such as Papers Please and Orwell - I'm really glad I did as its a very worthy addition to the genre. The concept sounds a bit off beat, but the humour and art style keeps it charming rather than morbid.

You play as a bureaucratic Grim Reaper who dispenses death with a sharpie mark on a profile, rather than his trademark scythe. At the end of each day you report in to your boss, Fate (and his cat!) who will evaluate your performance and how your decisions have shaped the world. It's a fun little game that can be completed in an hour or so - I feel like my first playthrough took me twice as long as subsequent ones since I was really agonizing over each decision, whereas by my third run it was 'just another day at the office'. Probably some social commentary to be made there, but eh...

There are multiple endings to work towards so the game has great replayability factor - though my one criticism is I wish there was a little more variance in the profiles each time.\t\t\t\t\t
