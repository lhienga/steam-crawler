\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe concept of being the Grim Reaper who decides who lives or dies, is a responsibility. But nobody told you it'd be like Papers, Please until you actually play it. Which is exactly what it's like. Death and Taxes takes the idea of Papers, Please and makes it about choosing life or death instead. Makes for a nice game, but it's miles are limited.

It's advertised that your choices matter in this game, making it sound like you'll have a grand impact in the direction. I can't quite say that's entirely true, least to the extent it's suggested. You affect the outcome of what's reported on your phone on your desk and how the boss reacts to your decisions and reports. I came into this game wanting to be a neutrally balanced Grim Reaper but after awhile, the profiles do get harder in choosing who goes or not.

Then the rules themselves force my hand to work against my role, forcing me to allow corrupt CEOs live while I had to kill a 20 year old aspiring student. Ah, such is the way of these things, it sucks.

But what also sucks is that my enjoyment of the game got drained after awhile of playing. I do credit the VA, the visuals, some level of charm and it's atmosphere.

Though there's only really room for one monotonous job in my library and I'd rather be at the station instead.\t\t\t\t\t
