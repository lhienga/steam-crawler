\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSad to say that if I have to choose between an aye or nay I incline to say nay. This game has great ideas and some concepts. But in the end the result is a short and unfulfilling game that is quickly forgotten. It has some rememberable aspects which are soon quick forgotten.

Things this game does good:

Introducing Character
Dialogue
World building
Art
Story

Things the game doesn't do good.
Gameplay
Interaction between characters
Connecting parts of the world together
Character development
Lack of challenge
Game ending

Death and Taxes is a game about becoming an office worker after you die and now you're the one that has to pick and choose which human dies. You need to fulfill certain requirements to earn a day's wage by for example killing half the "appliants" that land on your desk. All the while the story progresses as you learn about where you are, who works at the office and what your place is in this place. You influence the world in its entirity by the people you kill or let live depending on what their job is, what they want to achieve and what the current state of the world affaires are. It's a simple game with simple mechanics and it's a straight forward desk job. Mark people so they live or die with a marker. But you can read about their job and their character and their dreams so sooner or later you start to decide who you let live and who you kill based on what you want to happen. You want a world that progressess well? Kill the thief, execute the corrupt banker and let the school teacher who dreams of becoming a bee keper live. You kill that one important environmentalist and suddenly environmental disasters start happen more frequently and the world becomes less inhabitable... oops.

Sounds fun on paper right? Except your choices seem to matter not so much, the rewards aren't that rewarding and there's no pressure of you. A lot of things get introduces (like characters and interactable objects) but they don't really develop nor do they interact with the world you are in. You can have some deep conversations with them and get to know the world and themselves, but you don't get more out of it than that. There's no interaction beyond the first get-to-together and the game is too short to make the characters fully fleshed out and interesting. It all feels a bit too empty and uninteresting.

There is a great potential in this game or something like it that has more character development and world interaction to really find out how everyone got here and how big the place is where you work.\t\t\t\t\t
