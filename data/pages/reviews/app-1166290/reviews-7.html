\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like existencialism in my characters stories, and this delivers while also meeting with what I would expect in a game about demise, reapers, and life. Death and Taxes is really a simulator of just "a totally normal office job" that tries to touch our hearts by presenting us with a lot of human life stories facing death and not the best orders... latest, with which we will decide their fates.

Others' reviews, considering that its gameplay converts into a repetitive loop, aren't wrong. The experience can get pretty unvaried, but in this case, I consider that this characteristic is part of the "charm" of a simulator being a Grim Reaper doing its work in an office through paperwork. Precisely, the characters also express how dull their job is.

It may not be worth replaying this game again and again just to obtain all the achievements. But, even considering this, I come back from time to time to Fate's office to just chill and play with having the threads of destiny in my hands.\t\t\t\t\t

Quite a hefty game with hidden tasks and options based on choices. Ever want to 'Judge thy neighbor' without having to go to confession? This is the game for you!
------------------------------------
The 'Wholey' Cannoli:

Death and Taxes is a play of the phrase originated from Big Ben Franklin. It shows that no matter who you are, or what you believe in - we all go to the same place, and pay the same price. It's pretty simple - pick who lives and dies based on the orders from the boss. Sometimes the orders are simple, and sometimes he asks you to do 7 things at once.

Inflict terror upon the humans -- or spare them. Want to doom them all? Let the worst of us live, and the best of us die.

Freewill to follow or break the rules as you please, just be willing to deal with the consequences of your actions. *All* of your actions.

Take a personality test and get a fancy drink and a nickname, chat with other employees who are just as miserable or gleeful as you are!

Customize your look with /A LOT/ of options - and buy fun little deedly-doos for your desk!

Win a useless reward, and attempt to pawn it off. Complain about your job right to your boss's face. Pet a kitty!!!!

...There is honestly A LOT to do in this game that is surprisingly hidden. The options for each play-through are massive. You can get different endings, unlock new things, find out secrets, get some hilarious achievements that make no sense, and in general, piss-off Fate - or you know, be his best friend.

Whatever you want. You were literally born..er...summoned... to do this.\t\t\t\t\t
