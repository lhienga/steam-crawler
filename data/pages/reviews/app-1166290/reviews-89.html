\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a case where I wish there's a neutral button because I truly found this game charming. However, the "good" ending leaves a very sour taste in my mouth. In the way that I think it encourages a rather dangerous fascist/eugenicist way of thinking.

Basically, the premise of the game is that you're a grim reaper and you decide who lives and who dies. Every day you have a quota to fill. Your choices has a consequence on the state of the world.
So very quickly, I learned that to balance the state of the world, I have to think of these NPCs in terms of their contribution/harm to society. Kill an ecologist, the environment suffers. Kill a terrorist, crime decrease.

For those of you who has read Death Note... yes, you CAN be Kira, if you so choose.

While the game doesn't explicitly tell you to think of people that way, giving you a quota of people to kill and rewarding you for killing the "right" NPCs encourages that kind of thinking. A lot of these NPCs aren't scumbags, they're misguided people with dangerous ideas, or simply people who could not contribute to society at that very moment due to their troubled circumstances, and you're asked to weigh these people's lives against one another. Who can contribute more to society, the climate scientist or this depressed retiree with nothing going on in her life?

Since you're neither a lawmaker nor a life coach, you're a grim reaper, you can only choose to kill these people. You cannot point them in the right directions.

What disturbs me is that you CAN indeed create a utopian on earth by killing the "right" people. And regardless of the irony/sarcastic tone your mirror tells you for that... the game doesn't really examine that very critically. You're made to feel successful, powerful, and characters are commenting on what a good job you're doing.

I think this is the key difference between this game and "Paper, please", the latter of which, you're trying to do the least harm in a difficult circumstance. You have the means to assist in a revolution but you do NOT create a utopia by choosing the right people to kill.

That's fascist thinking. And that's... a bit dangerous if unexamined.\t\t\t\t\t
