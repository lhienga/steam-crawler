\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really looking forward to playing a Paper’s Please type game again. I wanted to feel like a cog in some massive bureaucratic mechanism expected to emotionlessly do my job. I didn’t get that. Instead, Death and Taxes was more of a “meme” game. It cracked jokes, tried to be funny – which is totally fine – but it felt like it was aiming for more of the redditor audience. Though, there were points in which the game tried to be serious and make you feel guilty for damning humans to death, but it comes off as shallow, and that’s with me reading every human bio and trying to make correct moral decisions. Also, later in the game, you can literally do whatever you want and forget the rules. I won without really knowing why.

The characters you meet, barring one – your manager – have absolutely no impact on the story what-so-ever. The gameplay loop is thin and never changes. Things you do that affect the world come up as extremely short “tweets” on an app loaded onto an in-game phone. They’re so short and boring they really don’t make you feel much guilt or pride pertaining to your actions.

4/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried to like this game. It sounded cute and its comparisons to Papers, Please had caught my attention.

Sadly, it is pretty dull and boring. The idea is interesting, the conversations are alright, but it really doesn't have alot of variety. You'll be doing the same thing every in game day. Rinse and repeat with there being a little variety in what is required for you to finish the day correctly and move on.

What it doesn't give you is any leniency or ability to change your style without being punished. Fate, the death manager, tells you to kill at least 2 humans who are both engineers. Not bad, you get 4 humans to judge, kill the two engineers AND a real jerk who deserves to die. Then you get reamed out because Fate said to kill only two humans! No. .no...the paperwork clearly said at LEAST 2 humans...not ONLY 2, there is a difference...

Overall, cute premise, some of the interactions are meh, but it was just so monotonous and boring. I didn't feel any interest in proceeding through the storyline as I did in Papers, Please.

Good try, just off the mark with this game. Had the game refunded.\t\t\t\t\t
