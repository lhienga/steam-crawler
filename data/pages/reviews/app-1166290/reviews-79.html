\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeath and Taxes is about a game where you play a Grim spawn and try to figure out the inner workings of your job as you make judgments on who get to live and who doesn’t. The visuals are very appealing, the music and sound effects are of great quality, and the gameplay is simple enough to where you don't have to think as much (if you don’t care about following certain rules, etc). An added delight is that there are voice overs for Fate (honestly, a nice little touch). I will admit there is a bit of reading involved in this game, but it does not take away from the game's charm (playing it when the right mood hits is key lol). I recommend this game for those that get into those philosophical moods about life, death, morals and ethics.
    PROS
  • Very aesthetically pleasing visuals; cool customized character accessories
  • Great soundtrack, sound effects, and voice overs
  • Interesting themes and concept
    CONS
  • Interesting story, but gets dull and boring very quickly (especially after second playthrough)
  • There is so much reading (between the profiles and dialogue)
  • Not too much replay value

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI kind of feel like going easy on Death and Taxes because the game is harmless, but I can't. Let's be clear, the writing _is_ what has to carry it. Yet, for all it's chin-stroking, it's both drowning in silliness and not funny. The profiles you are given are shallow, largely because of excessive irreverence, and this is supposed to be what frames your decision to let them live or die. It's just so surface level, and that's before they get buried in lame quips. I can not overstate how painfully unfunny I found this game. I may have too dark of a sense of humor already, but the attempt here came off as childish.

Boy, isn't it grim we're literally making life and death decisions based on such insufficient information? Clicking "Death" next to a goofy backstory doesn't really get that across, though it was amusing the game thought it did. Maybe there's some revelation later, but I found no compelling reason to care. It wore me down. I started skipping huge chunks of conversations, barely skimming profiles and eventually just haphazardly making decisions. Turns out the game doesn't really care. Speaking of which, when you break the rules you may not get paid. Beyond a funny hat or a lamp that sheds some light, no pun intended, on what your choices are doing, money seemed fairly pointless as well.

So, the story has no teeth and the gameplay pretty much doesn't exist. Papers, Please, the game this most closely resembles, asked if you could stay true to your morals, and what being a good person even meant against the backdrop of a world in which you are just another expendable cog. With no more than a book of rules and an the ability to grant admittance, your actions had an impact on yourself, your family and the world. Death and Taxes seems to aspire to such, giving you power over life and death, but fails on both ends. I assume Fate calling you a rebel or admonishing you for being too reliable were meant to compel you in some way, but without anything to reward me with or take away I couldn't pretend to care.\t\t\t\t\t
