\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game because I really enjoyed Papers Please, Beholder, and Don't Feed the Monkeys (highly recommend DFtM), and this was the cheapest of the games I haven't played in this genre. Death and Taxes is HORRENDOUSLY boring. It's 5% actually playing and 95% clicking through unnecessary conversations. At the end of each day you talk to your boss which becomes tedious early on. These conversations sometimes last longer than your actual shift. Also, it's unclear what the results are your actions are. You look at Tweets at the beginning of the day to see what happened, but super vague and not interesting to read. While I appreciate the stylistic choices of how the game looks, it just makes the world look like blah and makes you feel like blah. The Tweets especially are just black text on white background. Maybe if they put in pictures I'd actually be interested in what's actually going on. Because you're not technically 'alive', earning money is useless if you don't need to spend money on food or medicine for your uncle. I found myself racing through the game to the end to maybe get some sort of conclusion to everything that's happened, but I was left disappointed. Don't spend your money on this. Instead spend a couple dollars more and buy an actual game.\t\t\t\t\t
