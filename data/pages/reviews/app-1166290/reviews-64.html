\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game with two main mechanics: (1) you sit at a desk and select who will live and who will die, and (2) conversations where you have specific options to pick from. There is more to this game than you might initially think, as there are many potential causative branches to follow, but multiple playthroughs are required to get to the most interesting parts. It was good for about 10 hours of fun for me, or 2-3 attempts. Your Mileage May Vary

THE GOOD:
- It's amusing to try different story branches, both good and bad.
- The writing is decently good
- The profiles, and the news items, can be quite funny and entertaining
- It's fun to figure out how the game works and become expert at it. There's some experimentation required, and it's fun to try out different ideas and see what the hidden rules are.

THE BAD:
- Trying to figure out what the impact of your live/die choice will be is a game of "guess what the designer is thinking", which can be frustrating at times. Fortunately, there are tools you will acquire over time that will help you know and remember the consequences.
- The tools for understanding the consequences are very hard to figure out how to use! I had to look up how they work.

THE UGLY:
- When sitting at your desk, manipulating items can be a bit weird. The last item touched should really be kept on top, whereas the game just sorts everything into an arbitrary order, so you can move things around and have them disappear under other things while you're still moving them.
- The desk can get very busy, making it easy to mis-click
- At the desk, it's easy to place objects on top of each other in a nonsensical way, or set things down in empty space.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game because someone(and steam) likened it to Papers please.

It's not even close.

The game is....trying in spirit it feels like to be something like Papers please, thrusting you into a role of seemingly mundane work that may or may not play bigger role and balancing out your own interest against your humanity or more empathetic narrative of the world at large etc.

It has all the core formula for it plus some really nice visuals and voice acting, yes...but it lacks one most important aspect to really get to you.

Character.

Not to say it lacks all character, no, there are quite a handful of quirky interesting characters to meet, yes, but ultimately problem is you just can't really connect to the subject of those you are supposed to empathize with, in this case the humans that you either need to kill or let live. The game does way too good of a job of making it mundane and as days go on I couldn't figure out what separates one file from another apart from shallow sentences on the paper that frankly just kinda tells me how I should feel in a sense. The 'consequences' play out, yes, on something of a twitter feed but even that feels league removed.

Now to be fair, maybe the game does get better after 2 hour mark but in all honesty I couldn't bring myself to play more than what I've played because I was literally falling asleep and found myself just ticking away at the file willy nilly without even reading anymore.

If you're someone who got here thinking this game would be like Papers Please, I say pass and keep searching.\t\t\t\t\t
