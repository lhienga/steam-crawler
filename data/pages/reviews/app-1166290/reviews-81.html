Great concept but lack of content.

No spoiler review
I do enjoy the first hour of the game but after it feels really repetitive and too slow. There are very few things you can do in each day (Go to your office > Do your job > Meet Fate > Go to your room > repeat). Of course you can meet that pirate guy to buy things and Interact with some items but that's it. And you are forced to do it everyday.

The most fun part is to meet new characters in the bar and talk with them and the game only gave you twice a week. It makes weekday incredibly boring. Interaction with Fate is nice but it gets pretty stale pretty fast after a while.
When the cat took Fate's place was surprising and I do enjoy it a little bit.

All the character I meet in the lounge (or Bar) are really interesting and I really like it but sadly they are just one time interaction and they are gone. I did hope I could go to other places or meet more people.

The Story is okay but I don't find it engaging even in the most tense situation I don't feel any tension at all. The game always told you that your choice have consequences but I don't feel any impact and it makes me really bored because I have no idea what's going on after (There are short messages that told you what happened if you killed or let someone lived but that's it).

You can only knows the consequences after you bought an item from that pirate guy but It still feel kinda underwhelming.

Spoiler heavy review here

After you beat the game once you will unlock a New game plus where you can keep all your items and costumes from the previous session. I do know that the game unlocked new dialogues with Fate and other characters.

Because you know what is the Fate's real plan is, objective now is to get all the endings. That means you have to do it all over again but with slight changes (or go full meme mode to see his reaction) and it's too tedious to go through.

Sadly you can't explore other department (like plant department) or the archive even though they sounds very interesting. It has a build up and lore that sounds fun to drive into but most of it are just one shot thing they bought up and never mention it ever again.

I want to like this game, I really do, my sister even bought this game for me and told me how awesome it is but sadly I can't enjoy it.

If you like a simple slow-paced story Point & Click with some twist you might enjoy it more than I do. The game has it charm and nice characters to interact with but don't expect an enjoyable gameplay loop. (It plays like Paper Please but with no tension).

Hope this review help.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn interesting, if short, game about bureaucracy.
I'm approaching this through the lens of Papers Please.
Some mechanical differences:
- you get explicit statistics on how your actions affect the world (e.g. more prosperity, worse for the environment)
- no time limit on choices
- related, choices are permanent (unless you restart the game)
- no way to replay individual days

I didn't like these differences at first, but I think they serve to set the game apart in a good way. You don't feel rushed into making a mistake, and your choices feel higher stakes since you cannot undo them.

The story is somewhat interesting, but I think that its exploration of how bureaucracy can be dehumanizing doesn't work as well as Papers Please. Choices feel more meaningful, to me, in PP: there are many more rules to follow, you are under a strict time limit, and errors cost money that could be used for food, medicine and heating. There is a constant trade-off between making your quotas, and helping people, all while memorizing increasingly arcane procedures. In addition, there isn't a single central villain. The characters in PP have their own motivations, governed by the limitations of the system they are part of; they aren't just the puppets of The Evil Guy, who is screwing up everything with his dastardly plans.

Overall: worth it for the price, but don't expect anything too profound.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rate this game a 4.5/5 stars. It is a very cute game where you use strategy depending on what outcome/ending for the world you are wanting to achieve as there are multiple endings with a story. You are the grim reaper created by fate and every day fate will give you a list of rules to follow, it is you're job to figure out who you want to mark live or die and every choice you make will effect the outcome (just like in real life) then you report to fate for a daily report of your work. Fate will then pay you coins or not depending on if you followed the rules. There is a shop run by a pirate skeleton that you can buy things from, some items will help you with your job along the way. There is also a bar you can go to every weekend to buy drinks and talk to some of the other characters.
Although I loved this game there are a couple things that I found to be negatives imo and they are as follows: for ppl who want to 100% the game achievements, you can't replay a week or a day, instead you need to replay the story from the very beginning with all it's daily/levels and this gets to be somewhat tedious. I'm trying to get them all but my brain is bleeding now and I feel as if I need a break lol! The 2nd thing that I found rather annoying was when reading each person profile it was gender neutral. For example: instead of saying Susan is retired and she enjoys gardening, it would read as Susan is retired and they enjoy gardening. Not too much of a big deal but a little annoying in a distraction sort of way. Still recommend playing this game for anyone who hasn't yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing for 3 hours & all i am hoping is that the game will improve to at least justify the high price i paid for this game. I based the purchase on downloading the demo which i totally enjoyed however after buying it i found that so much of it was omitted on the demo. For instance, not once did the demo show that you had to be on an elevator approx. 4 times of every day the grim reaper went to work. Why wasn't this tedious, boring, repetitive part not shown on the demo? I feel that all demos should show exactly how the game proceeds as it seems rather deceiving & obviously done on purpose just to promote sales. Had i known that a quarter of the game so far would consist of just going up & down an elevator i would never have purchased it. Since i tend to play any of my games slowly due to arthritis i will continue to give it more playtime on the hope that this "tedious" game improves & if it does i will revive this review to reflect that. However, at this point i don't recommend it. Just to note, i have bought several games that haven't turned out to be as good as i hoped (haven't we all!) but i never felt misled after purchasing them based on trying out the demo. I would normally never ask for a refund due solely on it being not as good as i thought since most things we get don't usually come with a guarantee but in this case my choice was based on the demo which turned out to be misleading & inaccurately presented.\t\t\t\t\t
