\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's such a fun game, with plenty of replayability. In just 5 hours, I managed to get the worst and the best endings in 2 runs.

You play a Grim Reaper, created by Fate in order to work the bureaucracy of reaping the souls. You can follow the orders or do whatever you want, bring chaos or peace, be the victim of feline theft, buy yourself some new clothes and faces, meet people at the bar and read the news of the consequences of your actions.
I really enjoyed the New Game+ mode which allows you to replay with all the items you have. Also, the NPC react differently to your specific face and clothes, the game also knows when you replay it, allowing you new dialogues options.
The graphics and designs are enjoyable, the game is fully voiced (but your own character), each widget has some fun or useful interactions, the souls you have to spare or kill are certainly interesting and well-written, this game is full of humor.

Overall, I am glad I picked that little gem!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs the newly appointed Grim Reaper, I must say that "Death and Taxes" is a game that perfectly captures the essence of my job. The attention to detail in creating the afterlife bureaucracy is impressive, and I feel like I'm really a part of this world.

Every day, I sit at my desk and sift through the endless paperwork, making crucial decisions on the fate of souls. It's a tedious job, but the game mechanics are so intuitive and easy to use that I never feel overwhelmed. And the fact that I get to wear a cool hooded cloak and carry a scythe is just the cherry on top.

One of my favorite aspects of the game is the variety of tasks I'm given. Whether it's deciding whether to let a soul move on or to send them to purgatory, or deciding whether to follow the rules or bend them, every decision feels like it has real consequences. And I can't help but feel a sense of satisfaction when I make a tough decision that ultimately benefits the greater good.

But it's not all sunshine and roses. There are some tough decisions to make, and sometimes I feel like I'm in over my head. Mortimer assures me that I'll get used to them, but I'm not so sure.

Overall, "Death and Taxes" is a fantastic game that any reaper worth their salt should check out. Mortimer and I both highly recommend it. Just don't forget to take breaks and stretch your bony fingers every now and then.\t\t\t\t\t
