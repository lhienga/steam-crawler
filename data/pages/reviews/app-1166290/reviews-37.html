\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeath and Taxes is the pinnacle of the business simulation genre. You play as a newly created Grim, an up and coming accounting superstar (or massive failure) depending on how you play. Your goal is simple: pick up your magic pen, check whether the human lives or dies, rinse and repeat until the world ends or prospers.

I had a lot of fun with Death and Taxes, the artstyle is beautiful, the music is calming, the writing is very engaging, the voice acting is excellent, and the gameplay, while simple at first, slowly evolves into an ever more complicated battle for the fate of humanity, all at the comfort of your office desk! I also adored the surprisingly versatile character customization with there being plenty of ways to make my Grim look fabulous while in the office. If you were to ask me what my favorite part of the game is i'd have to say it's a tie between the various conversations the player has with Fate and reading the vast amount of people's profiles.

My only con for the game is how monotonous the 100% completion is as you'll likely have to replay the game 20+ times (with each run taking around 25+ minutes and getting very repetitive at times) due to how luck is a pretty substantial factor in the achievement hunt.

Overall, Death and Taxes is an excellent 9/10 game that, in my opinion, you'll only find cons in if you are an achievement hunter.\t\t\t\t\t
