A decent introspective narrative adventure.

Presentation: 3/5

Pretty hand-drawn graphics and decent music, though a messy soundscape and an uninteresting art-style leave some to be desired. Voice acting lacks impact.

Narrative: 4/5

Death & Taxes asks the player what a human life means and by what we can judge its worth, but a flat gallery of characters and an unengaging plot did not captivate me. The writing is altogether decent however, and I can understand why this game appealled to others.

Gameplay: 1/5

A boring mimicry of Paper's Please which did not appeal to me at all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a pretty fun story based game.
First off, if you dont know anything about the game, try out the demo it'd give you a good idea on what gameplay is like so you can make an informed decision.

Then if you like the Demo, it is still worth getting the full game as you get 3 more weeks of game play with fully voice acted diolog, time off content, a proper conclusion to the game, and newgame+
The Demo will just give you a general idea on what the gameplay feel is like so you can figure out if you enjoy it, and enjoy the writing style of the game.

Once you get the game itself, my recommendation for how to play is.
Just play normally, look through each profile, and meticulously weed out who you think should die and who should live.
All while getting items from the shop to improve your work.

Then when you finish your first run, if you still want to play, go achievement hunting and try to get alternative endings.
Your newfound understanding of the lore, as well as your objects which you bought on your previous run will help you.
It has a lot of replay value.
The only spoilers in this review is the fact a newgame+ exists\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is cute, but a bit derivative and I found it not very engaging. the mechanics are a simple version of stuff that we have all seen in papers please, and the worldbuilding around it is nice and fun in the start, but a bit limited. In 8 hours I did two playthroughs, but honestly I did not get a lot out of them.

I did not feel in any way troubled by any of the moral decisions, mostly because you cannot really develop any interest or attachment to the characters you are judging, so I did not feel the moral weight of any decision - reading the news is definitely not enough, and often the message you get is confusing. I found myself just picking different sets of people just to see what would happen, but sometimes it felt like the scores (once I could see them thanks to an object) were kind of random and did not really make sense.

The game is not bad, but it is also not that good. I would not recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
