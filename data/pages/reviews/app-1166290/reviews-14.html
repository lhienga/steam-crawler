\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was such a fun game! Despite the somewhat serious subject matter and stressful requirements, this game was strangely relaxing for me. Is it due to the music, the supernatural characters, the art style, or all of the above? I am not sure.

I actually finished this game back in August 2021. I got all the achievements except one, then left it alone for over a year. I was trying to get the EGG achievement without using the cheat engine. I used worksheets to calculate the values for each person, but it was very, very difficult. Thankfully, the achievement was updated and no longer requires either extreme luck or using other software! I acquired that achievement back in November 2022, so this is quite a delayed review. Life comes first!

Going back to this, I enjoyed going through this game blind, but I enjoyed it even more when I replayed for achievements. There are some fun ones that show some cute and cool details in the game. There are also some challenging ones. It was somewhat tedious having to go through the motions that I would like to skip (e.g., traveling, required dialogue), but since the removal of needing to cheat or have extreme luck for EGG, I can look back at these with a smile now.

I've seen people compare this with Papers, Please. I have not played that one yet. Since I enjoyed this though, I look forward to playing that one too!

I purchased this with a 50% discount (6.49 USD) and I feel like I got decent playtime out of this. If you tend to only play games for the main story without trying to go for the achievements, you won't see the other aspects of this game and may find it repetitive. I recommend trying out the demo to see if you might like this. If you do not enjoy it, stay away. If you somewhat enjoy it but are still uncertain, I recommend waiting for a sale. If you are an achievement hunter, the achievements are all reasonably acquireable now, so you are safe lol.\t\t\t\t\t

Play as a Grim, choosing who lives and dies for daily pay. You can toe the line, doom humanity, do your best to save humanity, and throw some back with the homies at the bar.

I love that you can change your Grim's appearance, and buy different appearance options and trinkets from a salty old pirate. Some of the items you can buy become super helpful to succeeding in the story, which makes it even cooler (but it's not necessary).

I genuinely adore this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother review said this doesn't have the depth of Papers Please, and they're correct - but really, despite the surface similarities, this is a totally different type of game once you get into it.

Although it seems like it'll all be about the difficult moral decision of choosing who lives and dies, it's actually not all that difficult to decide - the humans whose lives rest in your hands rarely have much personality, and with the absurd nature of the game's world (i.e. you are a skeleton working under Fate, who occasionally leaves his cat in charge for you to report to), it's easy to feel distant from the moral choices you're making.

Instead, this game is really about enjoying the goofy jokes, strange world (I love the various gags involving the plant department), and quite rich story with multiple endings (I haven't unlocked them all, but the one that I did unlock was funny and morbid and very enjoyable). Your choices don't matter a great deal, but in a way that's very topical for the story - a recurring dialogue with Fate, after all, is about whether your choices matter at all in the grand scheme of things.

There's quite a few unique characters to meet and talk to, fun costumes to wear, and plenty of mysteries that I still haven't discovered just yet. You might not have to face tough moral choices in the course of working as a Reaper, but it's still pretty fun to get a drink at the end of the week and talk to a cactus about life.\t\t\t\t\t
