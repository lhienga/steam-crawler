In this world nothing can be said to be certain, except ...

Bugs:
There's actually nothing worth mentioning, as far as bugs go. It's a fairly simple point-and-click type of game, so it runs smoothly on practically any system and there aren't any noticeable problems with performance or crashing. And even if your game does crash (which I have yet to see happen even once), the auto-saves are so frequent that you won't lose any real progress. I mean, worst case, you'll have to redo one conversation. But, in this game, those are pretty brief, so it shouldn't take you more than a minute or two to catch back up to where you were.

Narrative:
This one of those instances where a game's entire premise is hinged on two things: RNG and Choices. If you know what you're doing, you can get the best (goodest) ending within your first two runs. I'd recommend spending your first run reading every line of text carefully and visiting every area every day. You'll want to get to know all of the characters and save up as many Darksouls (the in-game currency) as possible so you can afford all of the widgets that'll help you get all of the stat-specific and character-specific endings.

For such a short game (my first run probably took me 2 or 3 hours), there really is a lot of narrative elements packed in. There are a handful of colorful characters to speak to (which you'll want to do in multiple runs, to see everything they have to say) and a lot of the profiles of the people you can spare or kill have funny little tid-bits about their life, the world around them, and sometimes about one of your coworkers at the Office. Reading the news only to find that your actions have formed a new religion or that you created a cursed object is really entertaining, and there are SO many strange events you can cause throughout the world. This is a game whose story never truly gets boring because even if the main elements stay relatively the same throughout, all of the little things change and snowball into bigger world events.

Really, there needs to be an expansion for this game where you can interact with the characters around the office more, or where there are more characters to interact with. There's so much potential for side stories and little easter eggs that I almost feel like there should be a sequel. Which, that's a rare opinion for me to have when it comes to point-and-click games. Usually, I've had enough after one game, and the sequels are usually boring by comparison. But here, I'd actually love to see the devs keep adding to the lore.

Game-play:
Like I've said, it's a point-and-click game that relies heavily on player choice. Over the course of 28 work days, you are effectively deciding the fate of humanity. You choose who lives and who dies on any given day. Through that, you can cause severe catastrophes or wonderful miracles, or both. It's simple, it's concise, and it can also be unforgiving at times.

Now, I took 1/10 away from this game's score because I think the systems that utilize RNG are absolutely annoying as hell. No matter how careful you are, how deliberate you are with every choice, the game will punish you over the most petty little things. After almost 20 hours and over 20 runs, I still couldn't get the balanced ending because one death would completely ruin my stat average, and no matter what I did, I couldn't fix it. I wanted so badly to get all of the achievements, and I got pretty close (95%), but I finally had to give up on ever getting the last remaining ending. And it's for the same reason I couldn't get the last hidden achievement. It's 100% entirely RNG-based, and without a cheat engine, it's literally impossible. The Devs have specifically said as much in interviews. I respect the hell out of them for that, but I always find it completely unreasonable and it's made it so that I'll never get those last achievements no matter how hard I try.

Other:
I may be in the minority here, but I don't like a lot of the voice acting in this game. There are a few characters that definitely give solid performances, and even made me laugh with the way they deliver lines. Overall, it's the writers who should get the credit for making me smile during the game. The way Fate delivers certain lines always makes me cringe as he just adds weird emphasis to certain words in a way that doesn't seem very natural. Cerri sort of dodges in and out of different accents, also providing unnatural emphasis to words that should just sort of be ... thrown away? And Mortimer, who's arguably the most important character in the whole game (aside from Fate, I guess) sounds like his dialog was recorded on a 30 year-old microphone without a pop filter. His acting is decent enough, but the weird ambient noises and mouth sounds are sort of distracting. And he has a lot of dialog, so you hear it pretty often. Honestly, Florian and FRANK WHITTLE have the two best performances in the game, and neither of them get as much screen time as they deserve. Both of them have the potential for some humorous side stories, but you only really get to talk to each of them one time in the entire game, which is really disappointing.

I took away 1/10 for the voice acting and the voice mixing because it does detract from the game some times.

Summary:
I do really love this game, despite my criticisms of it. It's witty, addictive, and deceptively complex. I just wish there was more to it ... and less RNG.\t\t\t\t\t
