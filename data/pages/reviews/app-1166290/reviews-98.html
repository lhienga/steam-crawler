\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthe game is solid and could be a good amount of fun for some people. however the melodrama of everybody panicking and having an existential crisis of choosing who lives and who dies is quite boring to me. The moral high ground most of the characters stand on telling me that death is bad and how bad i must feel sending people on my desk to die is brought down with the fact that if I stamp "live" to somebody im just garenting them to show up on my desk again later to receive the “die” stamp. that and the fact you see devils and angels whenever you are in the elevator means that there is an afterlife, which removes any weight of death completely.

Honestly if the concept of death or your consciousness ending never bothered you the game’s themes doesn't offer much. I just wanted a cool game about death doing paperwork but it is content with waxing poetic about how terrible death is like it was that whinny CGP grey video.

So although it is a good game for some. I can not recommend it as i didn't enjoy myself.
Although I do love seeing indie (or should i say inDIE) games getting out the gate and becoming successful. And i do wish them the best of luck in future endeavors to the developers.

If you like numbers: art and style get a 10/10 but the rest gets a 4\t\t\t\t\t
