\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been no-lifeing this game for the past week, and can confidently say this game is right up there with Mario Kart and Mario Party for its friendship ending qualities.

You can play this game in parties of up to 4 in quick match, or up to 8 in custom games, with splitscreen multiplayer of up to four players per machine (though if you have a dinky computer you might run into trouble if you do more than two per machine).

The controls are clunky but consistent (this feature is easily the biggest selling point for me). Take the time to learn how they work, and your friends will watch in awe as you send them flying into the stratosphere! I would highly recommend playing with an Xbox controller or equivalent.

There are microtransactions in the game at the time of writing, but they deal only with cosmetic skins that grant no competitive advantage. Also, the game is very generous with both its cookie currency and its Nemo Bucks currency, so the player should have no trouble amassing enough to purchase their favorite skin in the item shop without spending a penny after the initial purchase of the game. The player can easily earn both currencies by completing daily challenges, as well as by leveling up.

In my experience, the game is relatively bug free. I've seen some clips of people's heads getting stuck in the ground, but according to lots of patch notes, issues are quickly addressed, and any jank that is in the game is completely intentional.

All in all, great buy. Play with friends, or make your own by playing a few quick match games and friending other people with mics.\t\t\t\t\t
