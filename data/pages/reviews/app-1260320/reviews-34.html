REVISED (2/27/2024), after about +10-12 hrs of play. Original is below.
In general, do not buy this game if you are are going to be playing to win - which includes if you'll be wanting to obtain new skins, because you need to level up or obtain achievements to get them or the currency needed to get them.

If you are confident you can play exclusively casually (which may be easier in custom games that have less players, where you have more control), this game can be very chaotic and goofy fun.

However, this is a casual game that thinks it's a competitive game like its other live-service cousins. It relies on you having a more laid-back mindset, but the various progression, unlock, and achievement aspects often unwittingly revert you to a competitive mindset as you strive to achieve goals paved out for you. And when that happens, the gameplay instantly devolves into watching a wheel spin for 15 minutes at a time, waiting for it to stop in the hopes that you might be rewarded a singular cookie crumb.

Then again, don't just read this and think "oh golly, this is the worst game ever, because this guy said so! I'm going to tell all my friends that this is the most horrible game in existence and tell them to pass it on!" That's not want I want you to take away from this. I just think you should examine this game very closely to see if it's right for you.

Original
If you are a completionist or have a completionist mindset, think twice about buying this game. This game relies on your willingness to laugh at failure to be fun - but it has an achievement for EVERY map requiring you to win 5 times on each of them, as well as other achievements for every map with absurd conditions. This creates competitiveness, which drains all of the fun out of the game; failure here is very rarely your fault, and instead usually just bad luck, which is incredibly infuriating when you're driven to win. Even besides the infuriation, if you play to win, the game just feels incredibly stale because you're not laughing.

You also can't leave a match you aren't enjoying, because there's a 2 minute quickmatch penalty every time you force-leave.

If you're playing with friends rather than in Quick Match, this game is fun (no achievements in custom mode means no completionist drive to win), but do note that this game (allegedly) cannot be played offline, as was originally advertised - although you can have two controllers on one computer, you need a connection no matter what. The devs claimed that this was a misunderstanding; however, I don't quite believe them, to be honest.

Also: this is a live service game. Microtransactions, lootboxes, seasons (but no passes, thankfully), the works. Perhaps that's why I don't enjoy this game, but greatly enjoyed other goofy games like it (Stick Fight, Spiderheck, etc.) in the past. Take that how you will.\t\t\t\t\t
