\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe problem with Party Animals is that you can easily get paired with a teammate who actively gets in the way of a win. Maybe that will change later, but for now you can't reliably know whether your team will help or hurt you.

I just finished a match in which my teammate literally went AFK after contributing nothing to our team. If you leave the game, you get a 2 minute ban from playing quickplay again. So the game punishes YOU when your teammate has given up and you leave the game because there's no chance of winning at that point. Pretty cool design flaw.

Also, it seems like some people might be cheating. As weird as that may sound, there have been instances in which players recover too rapidly, make their hits too often, and generally seem to have a "stronger character" than the other players. It could be skill, but it doesn't seem like it to me.

It's still a fun game. However, I don't think I would recommend it unless you go in with friends, and, for me, that means a negative, don't recommend review.\t\t\t\t\t
