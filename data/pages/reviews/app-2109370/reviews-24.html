\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been waiting for a WW1 RTS. This is not it.

Combat:
At it's best, it's a massive war of huge armies clashing together. Taking just inches of land feels rewarding, but not rewarded. You may do a push on the enemy trench, but unless you get a capture point, your line won't move. Your units are clumsy, and barely ever go where they are told to. This makes storming even harder, because they'll be shot while fumbling out of the trench, walking back and forwards, getting stuck on everything.

Defensive Options:
The defenses are very varied, which is a plus. You can utilize trenches, machine guns, mortars and barbed wire. Building defenses can only be done in the preparation fase, and then they are anchored. Trenches stay throughout the game, making them progressively more fortified.

So what's the problem? Well defenses costs supply. Alot of it. And so does your infantry, as well as your artillery. If you dump too much into your defense, you'll barely have any supply left for artillery barages or units.

Campaign map/Tech tree:
The campaign map is... Fine. It's not a 4X game, but it works. This is where you spread out your regiments, making some fronts stronger, and others weaker. The tech tree is varied, but unbalanced. Aircrafts are often far better than trench upgrades and tank upgrades are better than infantry etc.

Conclusion (TLDR)
The game focuses alot on historical accuracy. If you're into that, you might just've found your game. is this a good RTS? No. It lacks quality of life, and focuses alot more on looking dramatic, than being efficient.\t\t\t\t\t

We are also shown how the new weapons of the era began to affect the outcome of battles in small but significant ways such as with planes and tanks. Overall, this game does a wonderful job of providing a fun but accurate depiction of WW1 western front trench combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for strategic game that feels like the western front in WW1 then this is it. It feels like you are fighting your way through the muddy fields of Flanders, with thousands dying for little gain. When attacking tactical battles are interesting, with you having to quite carefully choreograph your artillery, infantry and other forces to gradually break down the enemy defences.

On the strategic level the auto-resolve is heavily weighted based on difficulty. On lower difficulties you can use it and avoid the tactical battles, particularly on defence. However on harder difficulties the auto-resolve is heavily stacked against the player and I end up fighting every battle on the tactical map, and unfortunately this undermines the game. There variety largely comes as the technology advances and plane, chemical weapons and tanks appear and change the battlefield. I'd like to see more maps and more diversity in maps too. The AI is quite poor at attacking. Even simple defensive lines (e.g. dual trenches with infantry and minimal support) can defeat the AI with little player involvement past the initial setup. So winning on defence outnumbered even 4:1 is quite straight forward.

On defence the AI does better, although it doesn't seem to use more advanced trenches or barbed wire. Strategically the AI is generally quite passive and easy to manipulate into the posture you want by encouraging it to spread its defences thinly around your "doomstack".

So generally a good buy, but with caveats, and I'm keen to see how the developer expands on these very solid foundations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fine game, with a lot of potential. I love the premise of a tactical/strategic system like this. However.

-The strategic AI needs a lot of work. In the 1914 start, which is what I played through, the AI would concentrate a huge amount of force to take an objective, leaving sections of the front open for counterattack and encirclement.

-The tactical AI is okay at defense, and terrible at attack. With a little experimentation and the right layout I frequently found that I could hold a position against as many men as the AI could throw (27 companies, supported by 3 batteries) with 10 companies of men and no artillery support. Defensively, the AI doesn't reinforce their positions that are being taken or attempt to counterattack already taken positions, but does a decent job hardening their positions.

In the 1914 start, with the strategic blunders the AI does, I was able to end the war by mid 1916. I'll bet that multiplayer would lead to a different outcome, but that's hard to do when the game can take days to get through.

One of these days, I'll try the 1916 start, but as it is I can't find myself replaying this anytime soon without some major reworks of the AI.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was the experience of my very first match:

- I'm not great at RTS campaigns so I boot up skirmish mode
- Meticulously create a colossal British unit with pretty much every type of tank and infantry being in the roster
- The AI must be screwed now
- Start the match and send out two tanks on their own to attack the enemy's left flank
- They do a good bit of damage to the German trenches but are eventually destroyed by artillery
- Send out about 200 soldiers to attack the same flank. One minute later they have all been killed
- Oh sh**
- Send out a flamethrower team and infantry company to the middle and get them to take cover behind a tank
- What's that red circle on my soldiers? OH.
- They all die to artillery
- There's a spot on the enemy's line which has no trenches whatsoever. Send out a third of the army to conduct another sneaky flank and attack them from behind
- Everything is going well until the troops are spotted by a German plane. Artillery proceeds to kill off around a quarter of them
- Charge through the forest with what is left of my battered soldiers... straight into a German MG nest
- The force melts like butter before my very eyes
- F*** it
- Gather every remaining unit on the map, about 900-1000 infantry and four tanks, to rush the middle trench since the enemy is surely exhausted from the fighting

- The enemy was not exhausted from the fighting. Only thirty people actually get into the German trenches

- German tank pulls up and a company of Germans start rushing those thirty soldiers
- Try to send them back, but they immediately disappear into the fog of war
- All gunfire stops, the music cuts out, and I stare at my screen in horror for 4 minutes solid


9/10 Very good depiction of WW1 combat but I wish that dead bodies stayed on the map a few minutes longer\t\t\t\t\t
