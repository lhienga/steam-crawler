\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTakes a while to get the hang of, but there is a fun payoff after the learning curve. Combat is challenging and for the most part is engaging.

Suggestions:
>Light artillery does essentially no damage, even to soft targets like soldiers out in the open (game wants you to use exclusively for suppression). Would be more engaging if light artillery had some effect on light targets, with heavy artillery used for armored and entrenched targets.
>Some cool features like gas shells cost ridiculous amounts of supply. I.e one round of gas canisters which can displace (not entirely kill) 4 companies from a small area cost as much as fielding 10 companies of your own infantry, or an entire artillery battalion.
>There are some bugs in trench movement where companies of infantry will just run by each other, or your infantry will inexplicably decide to get out of cover to move to the next trench over.
>Instead of the massive 100 per turn penalty starting at 1918 it would be more engaging to have an increasing turn penalty on national will (i.e 1916 would be 10 pts per turn, 1917 would be 20pts/turn, 1918 30 pts/turn, etc.)
>There's no unique animation for units with flame throwers in trench fights which is a little disappointing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good for about 50 hours. After that the opponent becomes predictable, almost all battles are no longer a game of wit and strategy but rather resources and tanks. It was a lot of fun figuring things out, almost role-playing as a WWI commander, where everyone that dies does because i said so. Fills a great niche of a WWI RTS and grand-strategy.

For $35, 50 hours of game play is pretty good. Safe buy if you like RTS, total war games, grand-strategy, WWI historical fiction, or siege-craft. Safe to buy on sale if you are curious about any of the previous topics.

I do not recommend this game to people that may be expecting historical accuracy, a fast paced action packed experience, to not be weighed down by the guilt of just ordering 15,000 young men to their death, the ability to listen to music while you play (nothing seems to fit for such a depressing game), or to find glory on the battlefield.

There are no heroes here, just death. you send them to die for only 100 feet of land. Good luck to any that enter the trenches and God bless those that leave them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to see through an entire campaign before writing a review, thinking that perhaps the depth would eventually come but unfortunately it did not. I enjoyed the time I had with the game but It didn't make me want to come back for another play through.

In the early game it is overwhelming, even after playing the tutorial. The enemy fields vastly more troops than you and they simply overrun the points for your first few battles. Oddly enough, getting absolutely wrecked, repositioning and managing to barely snatch a stalemate from the jaws of a sweep was the most fun I had in the game. After about three or four battles I understood how to build secondary trench lines, overlap fields of fire and rotate / withdraw companies. Once this was done I had no issue and even supposed major defeats were easily turned into major victories. Killing 5 to 10 times the number of enemy soldiers was the norm it felt like.

I spent the entire campaign on the defensive, the enemy bled their national will out and never took a single hex while I managed to take only three or four. Attacking however proved very difficult and unsatisfying as the enemy will have an insane gnarled mass of trenches, bunkers and gun nests to deal with. Observation balloons are required to know where to place your artillery, but the combo of balloons and artillery will mean you can only field about half as many men as the enemy. Perhaps balloons should stay on the field between battles? Right now the only thing that stays in trenches and barbed wire, all other investments are reset.

In terms of upgrades it mostly felt unimportant. Through the course of the campaign I purchased no additional infantry companies (unsure if this is even a mechanic), never got far enough to research tanks or artillery, put zero points into espionage. Based on how supply worked I felt forced into logistics and infantry with a smattering of air and defenses (Machine guns and barb wire were the only useful things I found). The only units I had available to purchase at any point were the air wings. I never even used advanced trenches...

Id like to say I played it in an unsatisfying way and that this is my fault but when ever I tried something new it just felt too expensive and inefficient.

My recommendation, allow balloons and artillery to stay on the field. Otherwise the supply to attack in the same way the enemy does just isn't available. Perhaps give a moral boost to troops when they capture a command point to encourage scrappy fights to continue after your supply is spent. Or, make aggressive tech more accessible. Move tanks and artillery a bit earlier, make them less expensive to research, or balance it out by making logistics and infantry less crucial.

I played on Soldier difficulty as the allies. For the price I had hoped the game would hold me a bit longer, victory was untimely unsatisfying and of those 14 hours probably 7 felt almost like a chore where I would tab out to watch a video or something before going into the next battle.

One final note, I actually don't mind that the battle AI seemed kind of dumb. Missing infantry shots and such, I know I whiffed a few. However I wish the strategic ai would move around and attack more places. I got very good at defending the same three locations. Finally, issuing commands to my units felt a bit messy. Whenever I would issue commands they would waste time walking in a circle, run out of a trench they were in instead of moving next to it or just refuse to switch targets which made dealing with tank waves a bit frustrating.

I'll revisit this game after a few revisions and update my review if my opinion changes.\t\t\t\t\t
