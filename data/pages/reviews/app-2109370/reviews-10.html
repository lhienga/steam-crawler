\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; The battle AI is bad, battles are hyper repetitive, and you can win WWI in 1915 without trying too hard.

If I could give a 'mixed' review, I would. Battles take place on what can charitably be called 10 maps, which is a problem because there *is* a dominant strategy. If you know anything about WWI, you know that sitting in your trench and *never attacking* is the correct move. To its credit, this game gets correct in a neat historical fashion. The problem is that you learn to just sit back and let the enemy fight into you, even when you are attacking. Yes. This is how WWI was fought, but it doesn't make for interesting gameplay. I got to thinking about this and began wondering if there even *is* a way to make a WWI RTS game *fun*. WWII games can be fun often due to the volatility of the situation. WWI is so static that even major advantages on either side were often nullified by virtue of trenches with modern rifles being historically defensible.

Also, the game macroeconomy is kind of terrible? Like, I played as the Entente and won the game in 1915 (!) by bankrupting Germany (!!) because the AI kept attacking me over and over. I didn't need tanks, gas shells, aircraft, or even infantry helmets. The AI is so terrible at battle tactics that it burns through all of its macroeconomic power in... 4 battles? 5?

The concept of a cool game is here, but this one just fails to live up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so close to being amazing and it kills me. There are a few things that prevent it from reaching the heights of greatness:

- Your units can only shoot in A SINGLE DIRECTION from trenches, meaning if you capture an enemy trench, your soldiers will face YOUR TRENCHES and will be unable to shoot at enemy troops. This is utterly unacceptable in a game based on trench warfare. Until this issue is fixed my review will remain negative.
- Suppressive barrage prevents ALL firing. Instead they should reduce firing efficiency, not prevent your men from shooting entirely.
- Speaking of firing, it should be much easier to tell if your units are actually shooting. Sometimes I will see the target icon appear but I can't tell who or where they are shooting. Sometimes it doesn't even look like they are at all.
- Battalions get wiped out WAY too quickly. This is partially justified because WWI, but even at the beginning of the game when it's entirely bolt action rifles your battalions will be destroyed in a matter of seconds.
- There is no way to undo an order on the campaign map, leading to one misclick quite literally having the potential to ruin your entire game.

The first issue is paramount and I am kind of miffed at why the game has been out for nine months and it still hasn't been fixed. It really sucks because there really are no good WWI RTS games on the market. I want this game to succeed.\t\t\t\t\t
