\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game a lot. It has been added to my historical war games collection to reflect the tactics and combat of the time (Company of Heroes for World War 2, Ultimate Admiral for 18th century naval warfare, Total War Series for other periods and so on). The combat is highly attritional, with emphasis on infantry and artillery which is very reflective of the combat of the time. This does make the battle sometimes follow the same theme but that was warfare in this period. The fact I need to keep my supply stock in consideration prevents me from just spamming artillery strikes or infantry in order to win battles. While some of the mechanics are sometimes annoying (infantry getting out the trench when I wanted them to travel through it) overall I think it’s pretty good. The AI is responsive and intelligent, to the point where I have lost battles by not paying attention. Losing one of their forward trenches will make my opponent send forward troops with artillery support to try and take it back rather than just sitting there for me to reinforce. Look forward to seeing if there will be expansions (Eastern Front, Italian Front, Middle Eastern Front).
Edit: Almost forgot, would really enjoy even more historical battles in the future, Battle of Hamel in particular.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is quite unique as there are not many WW1 strategy games out there but it does get repetitive rather quickly once you find out that the best way to win is suppressing with arty then charging in for melee from the side. Once you have taken one point you just rinse and repeat for every offensive battle, after the first few turns the enemy stopped attacking me so every battle was an offensive one, not sure if this was because i had taken land and reduced their national morale but it got rather boring. Once you figure out the supply system it becomes easy and turns into a grind of repeating the same tactic over and over. Plus the AI is rather simplistic and predictable, after a couple of battles i had figured out the AI movement patterns and was confident that they would not counter attack so could strip all the unit from my defence after their initial attack in an offensive battle. Also i'm fairly sure the AI cheats and can see your units all the time and will use their arty to great effect if you don't keep and eye on your infantry, even if you have placed them out of sight (I'm aware of the observation balloons but they still know where i am when they are not using one) which is a pet hate for me in games.

I have encountered some path-finding issues like units getting stuck to buildings or doing unnecessary movements when attempting to enter or exit a trench, especially annoying when you take extra casualties because of them running back and forwards. Though not very common its very frustrating when it happens. Also could do with a line of sight indicator as I've noticed some obstacles or terrain block fire but its hard to tell from the perspective given which can make it hard to plan out your defensive line.

Having said all that it stays true to the feel of WW1 but IMO the research needs to be quicker as it gets repetitive when all you have is infantry and artillery. Maybe an option to increase research points earned would help the pace of the game. I understand its a WW1 game but it still needs to be fun, looking forward to what Modders can do though.

In conclusion it might be worth waiting awhile to purchase for these issues to be sorted, unless it's on sale as you will still get hours of fun out of it even if there as some frustrating things.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am pretty terrible at strategy games, but have been enjoying my time with this game so far and looking at putting more time in when I can. The WWI aesthetic is great and the gameplay seems to match the spirit of the conflict as closely as I think you can while keeping the game fun. There is a bit of a learning curve due to how vulnerable your infantry are, but once you learn to master the interplay of artillery and charges it's a really cool and unique experience. Thankfully they were kind enough to include a pause button as well, wish that was standard in the genre for us with grandpa-level reflexes.

The devs have released a number of patches post release which seem to have fixed a number of complaints about the game, but from my perspective there's still a few things I'm really hoping they will look at soon:

- Pathfinding can be very frustrating at times, and it can feel unfair to lose infantry because they decided to meander up to a trench, or walk backwards, or worst of all get stuck on a trench on the way to another (EDIT: I think this is actually a mechanic that is not explained). Units should also not leave the safety of trenches without some kind of 'force move' command. Due to how quickly your companies get shot to mincemeat, these kind of issues really impact on enjoyment when they happen.

- Lack of blood and gore just makes the battles feel a bit sterile, like watching ants go at it. Also as everyone else does would love to see more work go into the map permanence being way more extended.

- Some research options seem really expensive for the limited benefits they give such as mortars or barbed wire. I specced early into gas for my first playthrough and instantly had to reset after I saw the cost per shot... (Read as: there are a lot of noob traps in the research tree. I don't feel as bad about it now)

- Outside of arty + rush there doesn't seem a lot of ways to tackle attacking in the early game at least (despite the game time ive restarted 3 times and still havent gotten very far into the tech tree). It's a shame that flanking trenches seems to be ineffective (the AI can fire side and rearwards from their trenches) EDIT: The trench mechanics aren't well explained in the game, but there is actually quite a bit of depth here. Trenches have both a front and back firing arc. There are blind spots at either side which means you CAN flank enemy trenches if you attack right from the side without taking damage in most cases. Learning this made the game 10x more enjoyable for me as it becomes about how to pick apart a base rather than just bumrushing it from the front. I don't know why they didn't spend more time explaining this in the tutorial since it's literally the most important aspect of the early game.

Outside of these complaints, I'm having a ton of fun with this game in my spare time. Glad I picked it up and would recommend if you are interested in the era.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPetroglyph delievered what they promised - a great game with interesting premise and interesting mechanics.
Digging trenches, setting up defences, storming the enemies fortifications is certainly more fun than real life WWI soldiers had while doing so. As far as I can tell, the game is -historically- fairly accurate. Having units of different nationalities speak their own language/regional accent (as is the case with the Bavarians, which I adore) is delightfully refreshing, even though I have to say that a few more voicelines probably wouldn't hurt.

However, there are a few things I'm going to have to criticise - first and foremost, it doesn't make much sense to me why you don't get to keep any captured command posts or even your defenses in case of a cease-fire. I understand that this was likely done to avoid unnecessary complications when reinforcing regions and attackforces while also giving the player the opportunity to improve on eventual weakpoints, but it still is slightly confusing. That being said, I'm also rather sceptical of the requirements to capture a region, having to achieve multiple great victories does take some time - then again, that is what makes it historically accurate as battles were not won over night but rather - as the game likes to remind you - inch by inch. Lastly, there is one thing that, although minor, manages to really annoy me; the water texture. Is there any way to improve it? Guess I'll check in the graphics menu.

All in all, this game is great and definitely a must-play if you are into WWI and strategy games reminiscent of Empire at War.\t\t\t\t\t
