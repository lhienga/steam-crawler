\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, I'm not going to say its a perfect game. Or a great game.

But its a solidly good middle market experience.

Pros:
Excellent art direction. From the shell holes to the cinematics, to the unit cards. There is quality here.

Gameplay loop. Nothing is more satisfying than coordinating a multi layered artillery barrage with an infantry assault. Prebattle decisions matter in how easy the follow on battle will be and its honestly a good synergy I want to see evolved upon.

Historical authenticity. From the artwork between battles, to the very way you fight battles, it really reinforces just how terrible and hard this war was on people. Imagine not having a birds eye view of the battle and no radio, coordinating an assault on a few hundred meters of trenchline. Yeah YOU the player can easily trump the stupid AI, imagine doing this in real life.

Cons:

AI is dumber than Conrad Von Hotzendorf with the persistent stupidity of Luigi Cadorna. Two men who deserved actual execution by dogs for their incompetence.

Economy is out of whack. I went into the campaign on trainee mode expecting to be a hard as nails experience where i'd have to husband my resources, coordinate my assets, and research ways to bypass enemy defensive advantages. Yeah I didn't need to do that, it turns out just coordinating your artillery on enemy trenches and flooding them with your own guys is all you need to do. A single length of barbed wire is 10 credits, an infantry squad is 5. A length of barbed wire should be I don't know, 5 credits before research, and 2 with research. The starter trench at 2 credits is fine, but the next step up should not be 30 credits, the same as a machine gun nest. Thats just the battle economy. The Grand strategic economy is more balanced, but again I played on easy and the AI was way less aggressive than I expected. I finished my first campaign in 1916, by that time the enemy had only just researched rolling barrage and tanks and I only encountered it for a grand whopping total of 1 battle.

Sticky squads. The infantry squads were a bit sticky when telling them to move trench to trench. I blame the game's over adherence to the tile system for melee, there's probably no fix to this without reworking the entire engine I'm afraid.

Over all I give it a solid 7/10, needs work but I was satisfied with my purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA different sort of strategy game concept with a lot of interesting ideas, but also a few small flaws and some performance issues.

The overall concept is what you'd expect if you know WWI's basic history: the western front has become a stalemate in the trenches. Right away, the game makes it clear that this is all about that strategic stalemate, with no real room to move around or through enemy units without fighting into them every time. The strategic layer is about finding weak points in the enemy's front, not about out-maneuvering the CPU. Which fits with the historical setting.

And the game does have a strategic and tactical layer. Strategically, you manage National Will (essentially, strategic hit points before you lose the campaign) as well as gold (money) and supplies (stuff spent in battle). Tactically, you fight out the battles or auto-resolve them, and how you fight the battle can turn into more gold/supplies spent or less when you finish and go back to the strategic layer.

Tactically, the game is mostly good. Units start in trenches, but can leave them and fight in the open and go on the attack. But again, this is a game about the trenches... the trenches are far safer, and if you go on the offensive, artillery, tanks, or other supports are needed to actually get units alive to the enemy lines. Tanks are wonky though... lighter tanks move like race-cars, and heavier tanks don't seem to do enough damage. What's worse, there's not really a way to fight back against tanks other than use artillery or bombers. Aircraft work mostly well, but their utility is limited by the fact that they, and tanks, can be permanently destroyed, unlike your corps.

So the game is largely fighting battles to lower National Will, cash, and supplies of the opponent as economically as you can, since each Corps will never 'die' but instead costs more and more of those strategic resources the more damaged it gets in tactical battles.

The science/teching on the strategic layer is really well done. It crescendos well and ensures that not every tech can be take. So, pick the ones you will actually use, from grenades to better trenches. That said, some teching paths are almost mandatory... getting the logistics techs seems far more important than the intelligence techs, for instance.


There are a few significant problems, though.

Performance-wise, the longer a session in the game goes on, the more it seems to slow-down. Not sure if it's the coding, but I once played through an entire campaign without leaving the session and the cutscenes at the end were lagging behind the audio by a wide margin. So, some coding issues and optimization problems, though I did not crash in these first 9-10 hrs, so, stable but just some fine-tuning needed.

The CPU is omniscient I think, even on lower difficulty levels, in tactical battles. I could understand barraging a static MG nest it no longer can see, but the instant a unit leaves a trench at the very back of my line, artillery comes in, even if there's no possible way it should see that unit (as in, I took out the observation balloons and there are no other CPU ground units in sight). And the artillery comes in instantly from the CPU... which, that's awesome AI programming if it's on just the higher levels, but even as low as Normal (soldier) difficulty and down, it still looks to happen. A few adjustments could help, here.

Lastly, a lot of balancing is still needed. Siege Arty is an almost instant equalizer no matter the situation, because it will rip a bunch of enemy trenches out passively and as far as I can tell cannot be actively countered. Tanks, especially lighter tanks, are ridiculously fast and/or have other balance issues. Hitting a MG nest with arty a few times will kill it, even though it's dug into the trench line, meanwhile blasting enemy light arty in the open with my heavy artillery takes forever to kill it. And some of the structures that can be built in a tile are imbalanced... morale boosting structures are completely ignorable, but anything that improves the tactical supply cap in battle is almost mandatory to stand a chance.



OVERALL, it's a good game, and reminds me of a bit more complex Ultimate General: Civil War, except set in WWI, of course. It's an indie-type game with excellent tactics and a slightly too simple strategic layer. And a few performance issues.

But it really nails the concept of 'trench vs trench' combat, where the victor simple is the one who wears down the other opponent first via better efficiency and logistics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti want to write a posotive review for this game genuinely want to, was excited for this the moment i heard it was in the making and whilst it does so much incredibly well there are just so many issues ive noticed that for me ruin the game and have made me quit the game until all or at least some are fixed.

pros:
- the game finally fills that ww1 strategy that i feel many of us who looked forward to this game wanted filled
- it portrays the war exceptionally well especially in regards to the attention of detail and the small tid bits of history given before missions (historical battles)
- the campaign is great in of itself large much to do and you can get decently invested into the whole logistic/support section of the game
- Research tree is large and in depth allowing for a somewhat varied plastyle and priorities
and a handfull of other things i cant currently think of.

Cons:
- persistent maps
ie the trenches and whatnot really cool and thought on these tiles youd eventually see massive networks of arty and MGs so on yet was disapointed when fighting on the same tyle they often time werent the same at first this felt bugged until i read into it and realized that its not a basic tile gets attacked sam map but rather from where the tile get attacked from thus if your first assualt comes from left but the second one from a right hand tile you need to rebuild two new entire trench networks. I can understand the reasoning behind this to a degree but tbh it limits the chance to have these massive trench systems let alone seeing them at all really as the AI is quite passive ive noticed (at least on soldier which is the recomended difficulty for the game) also devs why the bodies, buildings and craters disapear like i thought wed have a chance to click something that would allow us to determin how much battlefield damamge would stay it wouldve been awesome to see a nice lush green map with trees turn into literall mud after a couple turns of fighting yet that seems to not be the case especially infantry disapearing about 10sec after dying with these graphics is absolutly not needed and just kinda ruins the immersion and understanding of how many you just sent to their deaths as the battlefield always looks pristine as if the fighting just started over and over again.

-Light arty suppression
Arty was a massive aspect of ww1 i understand but the fact that i can shut down an entire trench network or have my entire trench shut down with a handfull of light arty barrages just feels kinda cheap there are moments where this entire game evolves into sending dude into a trench for an endless mellee (this goes for defending and attacking) as all that will happen is spawn 3 or 4 light howitzers and boom the enemy or you cant do ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ aside from waiting to get stabbed, exceptionally frustrating with your mg positions as getting them to fire correctly is difficult due to them being limited to the flanks of said trenches only meaning infantry gets remotly close frags gone (i know armoured nests exist but they are so far down the tech tree the first year or so in the game youll get pounded with that) maybe lower the effect or duration dunno but just please dont make it as super strong as it is currently

- campaign/Battle time
i understand its ww1 and that only lasted from 1914 - 1918 but why limit us in the campaign till like 1919 yes it might not be historical but i mean just let us play past said years if you so wish its always stupid when devs put a masssive brick wall to your fun when you just wanna research everything and see what can happen. why are the battles capped at 20min you feel like you gotta rush non stop know this might be to kinda get the ww1 feeling going of just sending blobs of flesh over the top as fast and efficient as possible but it would be nice if liek total war we would be allowed to decide if you wanna keep the base game 20 min or maybe unlimited or some other number.

Artillery as a whole:
seems weird as mentioned above it can absolutly win you matches by simply bombing the ♥♥♥♥ out of them but then with the heavy art its sooo expensive gas costing like 70 supply i think or 60 not to sure being the most underwhelming whilst it gets them out of the trenches it does not kill at least for me it didnt it simply removed maybe 20% of their moral and they were slow AF getting away from it, i dont want gas to be this OP killing machine but unless gas mask have been researched i dont wanna just do moral and then have em walk back in the trench as if nothing ever happened airburst seems to be quite good against INF in the open didnt test it against entrenched INF, but once again really really expensive which pretty much had me avoid heavy art throughout the whole game as i felt it didnt add much and was to expensive compartivly to the lighter variant.

theres a couple more minor stuff going wrong and some i cant remember as of now but yeah thats about it.
35€ for this game imo is far to high where it currently stand in content and whatnot but for round about 25 or 20 id say its absolutly worth it its a fun game that is half decent and set in ww1 something ive looked for a long time to find.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is close to being a very good WW1 game but there are several issues with it that prevent this.

For starters the mouse courser in game is "off" is the best way i can put it, there are time's when you can have the courser over a unit card or the button to either upgrade or delete a trench at the start of the battle and it just won't register. In a battle this can result in entire wave's of your own infantry being wiped out.

Second, unit movement is janky. If you move two units who are far apart instead of converging on a trench when told to enter it they will instead stay spread apart right up until they are at the sides of the trench and then enter it, getting destroyed in the process. But my biggest issue with the unit movement is when two friendly units move forward in the trenches and they run into two enemy units in a signal tench or in between trenches. Instead of either fighting each other or just passing by, they will instead either sit there like its a traffic jam until ordered to move ahead a second time, If you do this there is a decent chance one side or both will get OUT OF THE TRENCH and jump straight into machine gun fire. This is infuriating especially when they won't move to cover after getting out and get stuck on the tench even though they are outside and getting mowed down. Most of my losses have been caused by this happening 4-5 times in a single battle.

My third and final major issue is the turn and battle timers. Ya know for a war about battles dragging on endlessly and ground being taking in inches and not miles this game seems to want the player to attack, attack, attack. especially in the battle section of the game. Even with elite infantry and with 3-4 heavy arty and 2-3 light arty supporting, in my experience attacks can average with 4-5 mins remaining and that's with the AI not spamming infantry nonstop from the rear. I get having a timer so you can't just cheese the AI with arty is necessary but 20mins is a bit much, and the turn timer for the 4x side of the game is really unnecessary and limiting to what the player can do in one playthrough. At the least i'd like the option to turn the time limits off.

Also no gore or option to keep the bodies on the ground permanently? bruh momento. I don't care if it fries my pc, plz make it happen.

If these issues are fixed then I would definitely recommend the game, and I hope they add more front's in the future after these issues are fixed.\t\t\t\t\t
