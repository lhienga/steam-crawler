\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother game that sits perfectly between Steam's binary review system. I was really excited for this game, but it honestly feels like messing around on Zoloft. Try as you might, you're unlikely to experience that big moment where it all comes together.

The framework and premise of the game is solid in that it intentionally desensitizes you to losing an entire battalion in a second, where such losses would be unsustainable in other RTS setups. You're provided with the tools necessary to build your own trench line, and attack the opponents, but just the bare minimum of tools. There's no landscaping or modification of terrain, pick your trench, bunker, wire and just plop them down. Don't like that bunch of trees or the house the enemy can use fifty yards in front of your line? Too damn bad. The resource limitations prevent you from building a wall of death, and while balanced, it isn't really fun.

Once the field of battle is set the action begins, and frankly, its just rock, paper, scissors with better graphics. If your tanks are on the right flank, and your enemies are on the opposite flank, its just a race to capture points on the respective flanks as sending even one take to defender the open flak would result in that tank slowly crawling across the map accomplishing damn near nothing. Raider and attack-themed units aren't substantially better than their defensive counterparts which leads to the most efficient strategy of just insane numbers of the cheapest troopers with a few tanks. Airplanes are a good thought, but pretty slow to respond and relatively useless outside of bombing a fixed position. Tanks and infantry move too quickly to land decent strafing runs or do some anti-tank work. None of it feels particularly organic or interesting. You pick the right or left side of the map, throw everything at a flank, and roll up the enemy when you inevitably overrun them.

There's no night raiding. No capturing of prisoners. No meaningful depth in defense (lack of resources and mechanics). No booby trapping or repairing of trenches. Ultimately all the building blocks are here and with some work and the addition of the workshop there is definitely hope, but right now you're going to buy it, do a few skirmishes, a bit of the campaign, and realize that it just isn't a particularly fun game.\t\t\t\t\t
