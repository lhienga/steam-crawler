\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is a**. I wanted some strategy/grand strategy game different than total war and HOI. Initially this looked like it could be it... I liked the simple UI as it was easy to understand, very dynamic research tree that impacted play style, and a nice campaign map.

Gameplay. From the first battle onwards I dreaded this game, even more so when I researched planes, and incredibly so when I finally researched tanks. Planes are a waste of supply, just garbage, but I guess during WW1 thats historically accurate so I didn't care too much.

Tanks are definitely the worst feature on this game. They are incredibly fast, destroy all infantry units, outrun artillery with ease, and are buggy to all hell. If you click enough times, a light tank can cross an un crossable trench. Weird feature but ok. If you get close enough to enemy tanks they can't shoot you with their cannon but instead you stare at each other without moving for 5 minutes until one of your machine guns kills the other. Planes and raiders can somewhat counter tanks but the planes cost way more supply than its worth to kill a tank, just spam tanks and you win ez. Raiders are countered by even a 10% health company of basic intrantry. So... practically useless... All infantry research upgrades, trench upgrades, machine gun upgrades, basically EVERY upgrade that ISN'T a tank or mortar is useless against tanks. Despite how you would like to play, the AI will rush tanks 10 times out of 10, and in order to counter tanks you need to rush tanks too. Completely takes away from the dynamic play style this game could potentially offer. Tragic.

I am positive I could've found more "advanced features" if I played long enough but ever round and battle was a monotonous slug and I hated it. The only moment of enjoyment I got from this game was seeing the campaign map, research tree, and easy UI for the first time.... everything quickly went downhill from there. I had enough wasting my time, and ended my campaign in 1917 at 800 to 200 war support. Practically GG at athat point but I didn't even care to finish anymore. Oh and apparently the AI cheats, doesn't surprise me and I don't care anymore as I am probably not going to play this game ever again unless its on a $5 steam sale bundle with some other playable games.

TL;DR the balancing is garbage, wait another year I guess if you really wanna try this out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing a fan of strategy games as well as history, I must say this title is addictive. The strategy turn based game is combined together with a very well thought through real time battle system. The RTS battles in terms of scale, mechanics and achieving certain military objectives closely resembles another great position, Ultimate General: American Civil War, but with greater variety of weapons and tactics. The combination of the turn based strategy and RTS battles brings very good memories of the Imperial Glory game.
The game combines known strategy games elements and puts them together to create a unique experience - a rare achievement these days. The history layer is very nice with some in game animations, photos, real war events that have some influence over the course of your campaign.
This is not a grand strategy as you know it from Paradox and that's great cause then we wouldn't get the rich RTS battle system with a tactical trench planning tool.
I finished the Tutorial and the initial Campaign on the middle difficulty layer ('Soldier') within 44 hours. Now let me get to the Central Powers campaign on a higher level of difficulty ('Veteran') :)\t\t\t\t\t
