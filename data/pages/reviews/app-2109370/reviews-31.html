\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally beat an allies campaign after almost 10 hours of gameplay and i think after completing a single campaign i can be honest about a few things.

First the game is pretty fun. If you like strategy games and have an interest in world war one this is a good buy but only on sale. I regret i paid 30 or 35 bucks on this game as in my opinion its more like worth a 20 dollar game at best.

The reason for that leads me to point two which is a downside of the game, which is its seeming lack of content. And by that im mostly refering to the campaigns. For one apparently they dont last long any way you wind up winning. I won by getting the germans national moral to zero and my campaign took me three days to fully complete which in hours took a grand total of about 6 hours. But apparently the war ends if you cant complete the game by national moral or taking the enemies capitol via the wars historical end date. which makes the campaign short any way you do it seeing how one turn is a month. I kind of wish there was just some way my campaigns could last longer than it did.

At that if youre like me youll likely only get two campaigns worth of play out of this game. one for allies and one for central powers and then youll be done with the game. Unless you want to try going for different technologies during other campaigns i dont see why anyone would play more than two campaigns.

Another point is in a single player campaign the AI needs to be fairly good. But in this game the AI is both annoying and predictable with artillery in that it seemingly has no fog of war since even with out them using baloons they can still target my approaching infantry with artilery well beyond what im able to see with out balloons. And its predictable because going into battle 90% of the time on a battle youre attacking on theyll attack you before you have troops ready to attack and they seemingly always know exactly where you have less troops on the field and push there.

The AI at least as of the time of writing this as far as the tactical map is concerned is wonky as heck because at least when i played my allied campaign not once did the germans ever attack one of my tiles. Im almost positive its some bug due to the most recent big update that the germans never once attacked me in my campaign because when i did my first failed campaign pre update the germans were more than happy to attack me with doom stacks by turn two. The fact in my completed campaign i was never attacked a single time made the campaign a bit boring. So hopefully that issue will be resolved in a future update.

I guess the last downside, which is extremely subjective to the individual, is the graphics. On the tactical map units have no animations when moving. they just simply slide across the map to the tile you put them on and the battles honestly dont look that great. Even Ultimate general civil war had better graphics and art style than this game does.

I personally dont mind the sub par graphics but i know there are a lot of graphics heavy people where graphics of a game make or break a game for people for whatever reason so if youre one of those people that may be a thing to consider.

But as a world war one rts game its not that bad. I enjoy it for what it is myself. Just wish the campaigns were a bit longer and not as repetitive. I also feel the devs missed out on making a multiplayer head to head campaign mode.

Id recommend this game to anyone that is interested in the idea of a world war one rts game. Just do yourself a favor and buy it on a big sale. while its a good game its 100% not worth the 30 or 35 bucks or whatever they ask for it full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwful game. I highly recommend it.

The historical notion that WWI generals were ignorant or deluded of the realities of trench warfare is a historical lie to excuse themselves from the responsibility or accountability of their decisions and stubbornness. They knew exactly what they were doing, and every battle that I play in campaign I keep asking at its conclusion, why would anyone want to repeatedly push young men to their deaths for little or no gain. All I want to do is tell the political leaders, "you need to find another less lethal way to resolve your feelings about the death of some privileged Arch Duke that nobody cares about."

I'm still playing early campaign, so I'm yet to taste the full horror of tank, gas and barbed wire warfare. I'm certain things are only going to get worse. I'm looking forward to the carnage and disgust. Ugh... this game is such indictment on human history.\t\t\t\t\t
