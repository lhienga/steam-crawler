\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTitle: The Great War on PC: A Disappointing and Monotonous Experience

Rating: ★☆☆☆☆

The Great War on PC had the potential to be an exciting and immersive gaming experience, promising a realistic portrayal of one of the most significant conflicts in history. However, it ultimately delivered a tedious and unimaginative journey that fell far beyond its ambitious goals.

Right from the start, it became apparent that the game lacked the depth and creativity that were initially promised. The gameplay mechanics quickly grew monotonous, offering little variety or innovation. The repetitive nature of the missions and objectives made it feel like a never-ending slog through the trenches, devoid of strategic decision-making or engaging gameplay elements.

One of the most glaring issues with The Great War was its campaign's progression, or rather, the lack thereof. While the initial stages seemed somewhat promising, the game rapidly deteriorated as it approached the later years of the conflict. Instead of presenting players with new challenges and evolving scenarios, the game became a monotonous cycle of watching your regiments get maimed and mauled, with little control or influence over the outcomes.

The game's lack of attention to historical accuracy and immersion further contributed to its downfall. Despite the promise of a realistic portrayal of World War I, The Great War failed to capture the era's essence. The environments felt stale and uninspired, leaving players disconnected from the events unfolding on the screen.

Moreover, the game's graphical and technical aspects could have done more to compensate for its glaring flaws. The visuals could have been more impressive, lacking the detail and polish one would expect from a modern PC game. Bugs and glitches were also prevalent, further detracting from the already disappointing experience.

In summary, The Great War on PC is a game that fails to deliver on its initial promises. Its lack of variety, monotonous gameplay, and unimpressive later campaign years make it an underwhelming and uninteresting experience. The game's inability to capture the essence of World War I and its lackluster technical execution only add to its downfall. If you're looking for a truly immersive and engaging historical war game, it's best to steer clear of The Great War.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was going to leave a positive review with the stipulation: Buy it on sale, there's still a lot of issues but the bones of the game are good.

I decided to play another campaign to see if it was any different at a much higher difficulty...oh boy was it.

The AI cheats...BAD. You can use this to your advantage by baiting them into attacking a "weak" spot, but it's still pretty rough. If you decide to not have a weak spot, the AI will continually send troops to attack your "weakest" spot, but if you shuffle troops at all (and bear in mind the AI isn't supposed to see all troop movements) they will switch it up and attack the place you just moved troops away. Or even worse, they'll attempt to run past ALL of your defenses to try to get to your HQ because you didn't spawn any units in there. A human wouldn't try that, but the AI will. Personally, I think the fix for the last issue is just: Devs, if the AI has NOT captured ANY CP's, don't let them try to grab HQ. Problem solved.

The pathfinding is somehow even worse. It was bad but not horrible at lower difficulties, but at higher difficulties the game becomes very punishing. Fine, no big deal. Except it kind of is when you have entire regiments getting deleted instantly because they decided to path out of the trench for some reason, or path close to an enemy trench before moving towards their location, or just standing still because they're stuck on terrain and getting obliterated by artillery. Turns out, regardless if you have your trenches connected, if you want them to move along the trench line, you have to shuffle them along one trench at a time which takes ages. But if you try to move them directly to a trench at the other end, they will step out of the trench and get immediately deleted. And then you have to deal with the frustrated feeling of "I didn't tell them to do that." Devs, if there's a continuous trench line from one place to another, force the units to move inside the trench. There was 0% of the time I decided to move troops from one end of the trench line to another to reinforce and I wanted them to run outside of the trench instead of staying inside.

Definitely not recommendable at full price. If you are itching for a WW1 game get on sale. I'll happily wait this one out for a year or two of not playing until the devs fix the many, many issues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is probably the closest we'll ever get to a legitimate Total War: WWI game, and it delivers. However, the gameplay is more to that of the Ultimate General series.

---------------------------------------

I went in pretty skeptical due to the negative reviews, but after watching a few gameplay videos I was convinced enough to give it a try. Worst case, refund before 2 hours and forget it.

Well, I just logged off of the game for the first time after playing for 2.4 hours straight, and I'm totally sold.

--------------------------------

The devs seem very active and listen to the community. The biggest complaints that the top negative reviews have right now regarding no mod support, no campaign options, customization, etc, the devs have addressed and said they're working to add them, and that they'll provide more information in the coming weeks (writing this as of May 19th 2023).

The ability to place your trenches and other defenses as you unlock them, and have them carry over to the next fight in that area is incredible.

The tech tree in the campaign really makes you have some tough choices that drastically impact battles. Do you take the advanced trenches? Improved supply lines? Conscript infantry? What about barbed wire? Mortar pits? More elite Riflemen? Flamethrowers? Planes? Artillery? Chemical weapons?

You can rush whatever you want at the loss of somewhere else in the tech tree. You can right off the bat take tier 2 trenches and barbed wire, making your defense extremely potent, but that's at the loss of you then having to wait to research better supply lines, so you get more points in each battle to spend on defenses/units. Or you could really make your riflemen OP early game. Give them better training and armor (more health/damage), but now you only have basic trenches that artillery can murder you in.

--------------------------------------------

The one gripe so far is that the AI is a bit dumb when it comes to getting out of a trench. If you're in a trench and you order your men to charge out, they can sometimes get stuck waiting for all their soldiers, leaving you open to enemy fire. Entire units can get wiped out this way in a matter of seconds. I think it's something to do with the placement of the trenches themselves and the generated exits/entrances, as I've had other times where a unit will charge out no problem from a trench before the enemy even has a chance to open up.



If you are a fan of WWI, older Total Wars like Napoleon and Empire, or an Ultimate General fan, you'll love this game. The devs seem committed and open to feedback, and are actively taking community complaints and working to fix them or add in desired content.\t\t\t\t\t
