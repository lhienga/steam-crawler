\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really suck at formatting reviews. Which is weird because I write essays galore. So forgive my formatting and hopefully this is understandable. TLDR this a great game. At least an 8/10 but potentially higher depending on how much you like the setting anywho

-initial thought and concept
If you always wanted a challenging strategy game(whether turn based or RTS) But were always a little shy about playing those games on hard mode( i mean, an RTS isn't like an FPS. An FPS you die and respawn and maybe you doing good or bad, an RTS one bad move can cost you a company worth of tanks, with no points to replace them and you're stuck putting up a feeble resistance as you slowly die)

This game is a great entry into challenging strategy games. If you already prefer challenging then you'll be right at home here.

-the scheme of manuever(the combat concept)
The game, like its FPS counter part Verdun truly captures the essence of ww1 trench warfare. Stagnate fronts, gaining inches at a time, debating if even attempting to gain inches is worth it since it won't have an effect on the strategic map, but maybe it'll low the national will of the opposing team.

Frantically researching something, anything to get an edge

And launching attacks you're confident in because of numerical superiority. Only to suffer appalling casualties thereby forcing you to abort or settle for small gains when you projected great gains initially. And that's after you spent the first 9 minutes of a match spending hundreds of supplies on bombing them with artillery. I'm an artillery guy. I felt confident with the amount of guns I could field early game. I felt confident after I softened up the enemy. But that all means little without a large number of infantry to assault. Normal rule of thumb is a 3:1 ratio an attacking force. In this game. You want to bump that up to a 6:1 because whole companies will be destroyed before you even reach the first trench line.


-the non-combat big picture
Money and supplies come in modest amounts each turn until you research better tech. Forcing you to think logistically. Tanks and aircraft can be purchased whenever you want(when researched) but infantry you can only get through world events(same kind as in Hearts of Iron 4) or research benefits(to put in simply, you can't just buy them like a regular RTS). Thus you have to think conservatively and efficiently, losing a corps can hurt, by then enemy may have a weak point in there lines so you seek to buff up your forces their for an attack....at the cost of maybe creating a weakness in your own front.

-innovative thinking
The pre battle trench structure building is intuitive and awesome. There more engineer savvy will greatly enjoy the defensive lines they can build.

-final conclusion
What this strategy game gives is something not all strategy games give and its the most basic function of being a leader

Decision making
Officers and leaders have many responsibilities. But the BARE minimum they must do is make a decision. Maybe they come up with their own conclusion, maybe they're NCOs and junior officers feed them information that they then compare and contrast. But at the bare minimum. They must make a decision

This game will force you to make a decision and sometimes not the one you'd like.

Problems have solutions, dilemmas force someone to choose the least worst choice. The goal is to force dilemmas on your enemy.

But here you will at first be thankful to just give them a single problem while balancing your dilemmas.

Definitely recommend. It's not typical RTS easy and not XCOM level BS brutality.\t\t\t\t\t
