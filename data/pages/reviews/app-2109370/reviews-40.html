\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the game I have been looking for! A perfect example of a strategy game made right! If you history lovers want a full experience of war through a fun and tasking game then this is the answer! The tutorial is extremely well done to teach you all the fundamentals of the game, from planning to research. When I first saw this game I was very wary, until I decided to buy it and give it a try. And now I am sure it was worth every penny!

The game is based on true events of WW1 and offers in depth game play of both the Allies and Central Powers. You take command of entire armies on the grand stage of Europe using a map similar to that of strategy games such as Crusader Kings or Civilisation so it is grid based. You can chose which armies to use in attacks or defences, including management of provinces and supply. There is an extremely large research/tech tree to improve you're game until the heart is content! These include upgrades for things like Armies, Trenches, Supply, Effectiveness and much more. So far I have only played the tutorial and I am already excited to hop into the main game and play WW1 my way!

The battles work as told: You will load in to see a battlefield, very similar to no mans land. You will be able to place defences such as trenches, machine guns, artillery, bunkers, barbed wire and many more. You will also be able to deploy troops to trenches and bunkers. Once the battle has began you can deploy troops and tanks to use to attack or defend objectives/points on the map which you need for supply. There are many ways to win in this game, these include defending points until the enemy exhaust their supply or moral drops, attacking and capturing objectives to raise supply and moral whilst depleting the enemies and you can also win by outlasting the enemy until they surrender.

There is soo much more I could say about this game, but a last It would take forever!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all - this is very fun and very unique strategy game set in the first world war. War where people were dying by the hundreds, thousands even with each trench charge. And it will happen to you as well in this game, especially if you don't learn how to play it.

Forget about assembling your units and just sending them all to enemy frontlines in hope that they will overcome them like in most rts. They will all die. Enemy is waiting for you in the trenches and as soon as you come in range they open fire and slaughter all your units. You need to time your advance with artillery barrage to cover your advancing troops and give them time to arrive at the enemy trench. Once you capture enemy trenches you should ALWAYS stay and move inside them if possible. Remember that WW1 was mainly trench warfare. If you step outside into the no man's land you can get killed very easily. Later on you (and your enemy) can even research snipers which will automatically shoot on squads that are not covered inside the trench. You also build the defences at the beginning of each battle which cost resources so you need need to be carefull. For example you can invest more into the defences but then you might not have enough to call reinforcements or to call artillery barrage.

Second part of the game is the tactical map where you transport your armies to desired positions and choose where you want to strike. You can also build new units there but only machines. In case of soldier regiments you will get more of them as the war progress (each turn is one month) or you can unlock some via research. This is a war of attrition, you can't just train new and new soldiers until you overcome the enemy. It's quite original and also makes sense if you ask me.

So far after 24h of playing I had zero technical issues. Music is great and very fitting to the time period. After all its been made by legendary Frank Klepacki.

In case of AI - Im playing on the soldier difficulty (third one out of five) and sometimes its quite tough. But its perfectly doable and most of all - it's fun. One example. I had very little of resources and had to carefully plan the assault on enemy positions. Sadly the assault failed and since i couldn't call any more troopers I thought that I will have to restart the map. But then the AI gathered all of his remaining forces and tried to do assault on his own which failed as well. That left the enemy trenches without protection (except for few machine gun nests but without enemy soldiers protecting it I was able to move around and destroy them safely) and I managed to win in the end.

Like I said at the beginning, this is quite unique strategy game set in the WW1 and if you are in the mood for something like that you should have a great time just like me.

I just secretly hope that in time we gonna get some more. Like dlc where we could conquer the whole map, not just western front and creating alternate universe where war kept on going even after year 1918. Possibly allowing us to research some fictitious technology, unlocking new units and so on.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWonderful concept. Atrocious game.
Probably one if not the worst RTS/Tactical RTS games i've ever played in my entire life.

Everything you've read in the bad reviews, unfortunately, it's true.
I am winning my campaing, that's the worst part. When it comes to the global map, it's pretty much about taking down the morale of the enemy nation. Stick to that plan, and you'll be fine... but if you want to fight... this is simply not a fun game.

The whole "cheating IA" is, sadly, extremely true. The enemy has trillions of supplies and gold, this means you'll be fighting 3 times the manpower you have, regardless of the difficulty. Ofc, they'll unlock everything they need as fast as possible, no matter how ilogic it sounds. If the enemy needs an upgrade, it'll have it next turn. It essentially unlocks several technologies at the same time, giving the player a massive disadvantage.
Combat on the other hand, it's not about tactics, but mainly supplies. Supplies are essential, but your opponent will run out of these only on the early game, after a few turns, you'll notice the enemy can deploy MASSIVE trench networks, planes, ballons, tanks, artillery and my god... Infantry, TONS AND TONS of infantry... I'm not talking about ten units, i'm talking about more than 30 units minimum in battle and at the same time (the limit is actually 30... but aparently only for the player.)

Now, you might think this is easily counter reacted. Well, let me tell you why this game is so criticized.

Essentially, in a good scenario, early and mid game, you'll have between 1400 and over or more than 2000 supplies in battlefield. This can buy 30 units of infantry, some tanks, at least 4 o 6 artillery units, 1 o 2 ballons and set a trench-network. And lets say, maybe you can add a few more things like tanks and siege artillery. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent World War One RTS. Really interesting scale and cool unique mechanics, which make the game a lot of fun. However, there are many balance issues in the game which can make it quite annoying: tanks are fairly OP, artillery is ridiculously expensive but very weak and generally the Axis faction is FAR harder to play than the Allied faction. The actual flow of the campaign is also 'off' as the game rarely reaches 1918 (as by early 1917 the Axis has usually run out of morale). There is also no way to achieve 'breakthroughs', as most provinces take 2/3 Major Victories to fall, meaning that it takes 3+ turns (three+ months) for a province to be taken, even if it is being barely defended.
Lots of potential, but unless you specifically are a RTS fan it is probably worth waiting for the balance issues to be fixed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first game as Entente, so far, was very successful. The AI was never able to get a single control point. They got into the first trenches, but as soon as they poked the second line, my MGs and defense troops went wild. Only focused on a very deep defensive and slow strategy. National moral of the Germans plummeted down so quick, they lost in 1917.
In the last month, they introduced tanks, but at this point their moral was so low, I opened a wide offensive for the last 100 points and won.
I bet I missed a lot of content because of my strategy. I'll try a second campaign on a harder difficultly and see how it goes. Didn't use gas, tanks, bombers, barbed wire. My strategy was so successful, didn't need much.

Seeing a lot of reviews talking about cheating AI and I wonder if it is just a misunderstanding of the game mechanics. We are used to having dynamic offensive games with a lot of action. This is not the game for that. I went slow, defensive and methodically and found it very easy.

For me, this is the game I wanted for over 20 years. I saw Total War type games and was like: "WW1 please!" But yes, the topic does not support an action packed, interesting game. Consider me a niche customer?

I enjoy the game so far. Maybe there is a lack of replayability, If you know how to beat the game. Maybe some DLCs or new game mechanics will change that?
The UI in pause mode is very wonky and that is the biggest drawback in my opinion. A lot of miss-clicks and no update on the map when you click makes it harder than it is. Clicking on trenches and miss is so frustrating, because there is no visual update at one point. I would consider that a bug. The number of troops I lost due to miss-clicking in pause mode and later just standing on top of the trench getting obliterated must be considered a digital war crime. You really need to develop a habit of clicking, pause, re-clicking and repeat.

I can recommend this game for people who really wanted a World War One strategy game, like me. I hope some mechanics getting an update and a polish. It is still rough on the edges, but I got my dream became true with this game.\t\t\t\t\t
