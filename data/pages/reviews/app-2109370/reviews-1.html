\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat concept, huge potential, but I think: a flop

Very helpfully, the tutorial takes just over 2 hours, so you won't be able to refund if you do try to play it. Helpfully enough, the tutorial was also about the most fun I had playing this game, the game proper is a massive pain in the ass in comparison.

You get access to a fraction of the assets a commander in the first world war would have had access to, no lewis guns / chauchat / bar / mg08/15, no mobile machine guns of any sort until you get tanks, no mobile mortars ever, infantry grenades are generally reserved exclusively for vehicles and structures and the few units that can target infantry with their grenades have utterly abysmal range.

You have no mobile recon assets, your infantry is nearly blind when it matters most and once you get tanks they're obviously even blinder, you are completely reliant on stationary observation balloons. This is fine on some maps but is near useless on some of the larger maps (Lille is going to become a recurring theme here). With certain weather conditions the game can become as good as unplayable because of this.

The only thing that reliably suppresses enemy infantry in their trenches (so that you can bring up your infantry without it being a guaranteed massacre) is light artillery, which has enough range for about half of the maps, and is as good as useless on the other half (especially Lille), heavy arty has a little more range but suppresses less reliably. The only mobile suppression asset you can get is the Saint-Chamond tank, which can only suppress using its main gun which just so happens to be the single jankiest thing you can ever try to make work.

On the topic of artillery, you do get access to creeping barrages, smoke, gas shells, and airbursts. These are all prohibitively expensive and do not feel worth their cost per barrage or the number of turns necessary to research them. You can call in off-map siege artillery as well, this is incredibly effective but is also over 20x more expensive per barrage than either of the regular barrages for on-map light or heavy arty, ensuring that you will be able to use it about once per battle at most.

You have no way to attack ground with anything but your artillery, so throughout the entire winter you will be unable to realistically win battles because the enemy will be capable of engaging your troops while remaining invisible and unsupressed.

The worst part of it all is the infantry pathfinding bugs, upon receiving orders to move to a nearby (and often connected) trench segment, your infantry will regularly get out of the trench and get gunned down rather than just walking down the trench they're in safely. A game nearly entirely about trench warfare has non-functional trenches. Upon being ordered to go over the top and advance into no man's land, some of your infantry will often exit from the wrong side of the trench and then get stuck on their own trench. This can be rapidly fixed by entering another trench, which will teleport the any stuck models into the trench, but this still often allows only half of a unit to actually advance, making them even more vulnerable than they already are. In some cases, your infantry will fail to even pathfind across supposedly open ground (among other maps, Lille).

Of course, none of this is a problem for the campaign AI. They combine stupidity and passivity in a way unseen in any £30 RTS game before this one. 9 times out of 10 they will simply sit still and wait to find out if you will win or lose due to any of the aforementioned issues. They often send out a tiny probing attack at the start of a battle. This will never get anywhere and will never be repeated.

You may well also not be able to play multiplayer without recruiting a friend. I may have just checked at a bad time for the servers, but if you're still intent on getting this make sure to check its SteamCharts page before buying as average player numbers are very low, and most are playing singleplayer. Still on the topic of multiplayer, there is no multiplayer campaign in which you could challenge a friend and remove most of the issue of the passive, stupid AI, you can only play the singleplayer campaign or skirmishes.

I got this on offer for 65% off but I still wouldn't recommend it, even at this price, if you want to experience trench warfare you'd have a better time on a road trip to Eastern Europe. If the infantry could do as they were told, or if you had enough control to micro them into doing the right thing, then I could consider recommending it. But as it is, a trench warfare game has bugged out trenches, ensuring that the core element of the game doesn't work properly and that your experience will be primarily unenjoyable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWasted Potential

I originally bought this game back in Fall of 2023 and I really enjoyed what the game had to offer at the time. The gameplay loop was difficult to get down but rewarding when I could finally orchestrate massive offensives to break the deadlock and start pushing out of the trenches. I believe this game had an incredible amount of potential when it comes to innovative and brutal strategy gameplay, but I feel that this potential has been squandered by the blatant lack of upkeep by the producers and the developers.
Back in September, I was too wrapped up in trying to learn how to play the game to notice the copious amount of bugs and minor flaws that wouldn't kill all interest in the game for me outright but definitely hamper the experience. Things like the timer getting stuck and launching a few seconds ahead when the game is being put on X2 speed and the tanks being able to go over trenches that were too wide for them if you spam the controls just right were difficult to notice at first but eventually became apparent as I played the game more, and they definitely contributed to the game's general lack of polish. I am fine with buggy games as long as they are recognized as such and eventually given a proper detailing to fix some of the more glaring issues, but due to the game's seemingly absent production team I don't forsee the buggiest aspects of the game being ironed out anytime soon.
The sudden switch from regular but fairly small updates to a complete lack of studio involvement was difficult to process when I first started noticing people talking about it a few months back. Now, as Petroglyph seems to have moved onto 9-bit armies, we might not be seeing many major updates anytime soon. Despite some compatibility being given to a small but dedicated group of modders, I just don't see any point to coming back to this game to replay 2 campaigns and a handful of heavily scripted battles.

TL;DR
I really loved the game when I first got it, but after the severe lack of polish becoming blatantly apparent and the devs seeming to have pulled the plug on any future development, I can't find any reason to keep playing. I really enjoy the concept and the gameplay, but it gets repetitive far too quickly and there's not much afterwards to keep me involved. If the game is at a steep discount then I suggest you pick it up but otherwise I'd hold on until if/when we ever get a definitive update on the production team's stance for this game's future.\t\t\t\t\t
