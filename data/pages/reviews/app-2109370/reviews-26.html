\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite all the negative reviews, I purchased the game and am quite please, especially for the price (it's not like this is a $70 AAA game). Sure, the AI is kinda dumb (your soldiers will insist on leaving their trench to assault a connecting trench and sometimes your units won't listen to orders) but overall it's fun and the mechanics are generally effective. This is very similar to the Ultimate General series games. If you like those, than you'll probably like this one. 100% worth the purchase price. It does get repetitive but the historical battles offer some additional fun after the campaign. Hopefully the dev's will keep improving things, but overall the game is fun to play, challenging (except on the easiest difficulty which is too easy), and strategically engaging. Definitely recommend if you like strategy RTS and WWI.\t\t\t\t\t

Most of the speciality troops (I.E. Flame, Grenadier, Gurka) feel incredibly under powered and wasteful to use compared to elite infantry. Maybe useful in a human wave attack, but not if you're using artillery well and snaking through the enemy trenches especially after the mid game. AI seen to have an uncanny understanding of the weak point of your lines which is easy to exploit with troop movements, gas masks seem to have little effect if trying to move through your own gas, and cloud makes it prohibitively expensive. Game play gets a bit repetitive and AI only seems to attack on the Campaign map with doom Stacks. Units seem to get "stuck" around some map objects like the control points or occasionally tents. And if you misclick when in the trenches fighting, prepare to lose the whole squad because they will fully exit and then try to re enter while getting mowed down by any near by machine guns or rifle men. All that said, Devs seem to be planning some updates and are listening to player feed back and likely the game will improve with time. As it sits right now, if you can pick it up for $30 or so its definitely worth a playthough as Allies and Central Powers\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt pains me to leave a negative review for this game. I admire its ambition and vision, but I can’t bring myself to enjoy it. If you love WW1 and strategy games, maybe pick this up on sale.

Its campaign map is not fun. True to World War 1, the line is very static, and you can spend all campaign just trading blows across the line and whittle down a national will victory. Sometimes you get the opportunity to reinforce, move units around, research, and build new buildings, but not often enough that you feel engaged by those aspects, and they don’t make much of a difference. Most of the turn is spent clicking around and seeing where along the line you can get a minor victory, and taking that battle or auto resolving it. In my opinion, It does not amount to a satisfying experience.

The game is at its best when in the tactical battles, which are brutally hard unless you spam artillery. I enjoyed making decisions on how to set up my trenches and spend my resource points, but man when the action started it was VERY hard to make anything happen. If you’re on a defending battle, you’re most likely going to get creamed because the AI picks battles where it has major advantages. If you’re on the attack, even taking a capture point and then calling for a cease fire still results in a stalemate and wont get you much on the campaign map. If feel like taking a capture point should result in a minor victory at least, because now you have a foothold on their side of the map.

I really tried to like this game and I admire petroglyph studios, but I cant bring myself to recommend this game unless you’re a hardcore strategy game fan and huge into WW1.
