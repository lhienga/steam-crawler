\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHuge potential but needs a lot of tidying up
Namely:
- Infantry AI pathing. It is too annoying and irritating to march infantry to their deaths because you have to individually micro each one into a trench to fight. Some kind of A-move where infantry automatically fight in the nearest trench would go a long way towards improving gameplay
- I get that infantry regenerate with gold, but destroying artillery should have some level of semi-permanency, at least lasting within the same turn. It makes no sense to attack a region, obtain a great victory, and then launch a second attack into the same region from another side and have to fight everything all over again

So far this game is fun on the defense - its satisfying to set up an effective trench network and watch the AI die trying, but attacking is very frustrating\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust completed a full campaign of The Great War: Western Front. All in all, I believe it was one of the better games to come out this year and I certainly enjoyed it as a whole. Here I will list out what the game got right and got wrong:

Pros:
Very immersive and clever usage of the concept of Trench warfare and the war of inches. I especially liked the concept of National Will and the preserving or breaking of National Will as opposed to simply just conquering the enemy.

Thought that the aviation and tanks were well implemented and were brutally effective when used rights, adding a proper tactical flavor to the game.

The idea of the Allies being multiple factions in one was interesting, each nation having their own bonus but also weakness while the Germans have cohesion but less specific bonuses.

Cons:
The main problem I had with the game was the implementation of supply, you only had a limited amount of supply which got drained very easily over the course of the battle. When you had a small army and a short amount of supply, this make battles impossible to win. Especially when firing artillery costs supply in of itself. It would be interesting if the concept of supply lines was introduced into the battles, as long as you could protect them, they could provide a trickle of supply over time.

The battles were mostly repetitive, especially in that they took place in the same territory. I feel this could be solved with adding more variety to the territories (Such as adding urban warfare and street battles to locales such as Brussels or Paris as well as thick woodland to other territories.) I also believe it would be interesting to add naval warfare to a game like this. Naval battles and amphibious landings did happen during the First World War, such battles could have their own rules and would add more variety to the battles.

I thought research wasn't well implemented. You only got two tech points per turn unless you completed objectives which made research and development very slow. Potentially the development of laboratories which speed up tech point gathering could help on that regard.

Despite these criticisms, I highly recommend this game to anyone who is interested in World War One because I think it is one of the best games on the conflict.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can count the amount of notable WWI strategy games on one hand. Even less if you exclude wack tower defense games or other derivatives.

Great War: Western front delivers on the premise with genuinely unique and period accurate gameplay. I'm still amazed that they managed to make a great battle and strategic layer around exclusively the western front, and really capture that grinding attrition feel without draining it of fun in the process.

I will say, it does get a bit grindy playing the same battles over and over again after a while - but I strongly recommend beefing up the AI difficulty for a dynamic and challenging experience that will leave you exhaling a big sigh of relief when you finally get that great victory.

There are some minor bugs around getting in and out of trenches, on rare occasions enemies will become immortal / untargetable, and sometimes the campaign AI makes some truly questionable decisions. But aside from that this game is a huge breath of fresh air and really well designed. Big recommend to strategy fans.\t\t\t\t\t

All the "fun" tools for artillery such as gas, shrapnel, and smoke are way too costly for what little advantage they give. They either need to be fired longer, last longer, or be a little cheaper. I can't tell you how many times my bombers just fly from one side of the map to the other to attack a single machine gun, when there's three of them at it's original destination. Which wouldnt be that much of a problem if it was just optical, but their "hitbox" is messed up aswell, leading to alot of weird behavior.
-Aircraft AI refusing to fight: about 60% of the time i call in a plane, it will not do anything. Fighters tasked with air superiority? They will do sightseeing right beside the enemies while getting shot. Bombers tasked with bombing something? Will about 80% of the time fly around the map, only hitting the intended target area by accident.

Those are just the most simple but most common mistakes that make the game hard to enjoy for me. One other, major, thing is, that it somewhat doesnt matter what you do, the game has some events it will always fire, disregarding the things you have done, the stats you have achieved. My first campaign after "release" constantly gave me bad events as the germans. Even though i won 95% of my battles with victories and with very low casualties. My National Will, which shows how "good" the war is perceived by your people, was always rising, at the point where the game decided that the war was over because my people didnt wanna fight anymore i had 883 of 1000 National Will, the Entente had about 220. I had made major advances towards callais, took verdun without a fight, and took 4 provinces south of it. All that with minimal losses mind you. When i say minimal i mean 1 German for ~20-25 Allied soldiers in losses.
But the kicker is, even though i got events explaineing how bad the war was goin for me, and how good for the Allies, and how i signed an armistice because i couldnt fight on etc. The game simply continued after that. THe events were apparently not connected to what really happened? They seem hardcoded following the historical facts, which is problematic. Considering i lost alot of resources and National will simply because in history i did some things that were bad. But i didnt in the game. Whenever there were choices regarding things that would lead to an involvement of the US in the war, i always choose the route of not messing with the US. But oh wait, on the historical date the US joined the war, they simply joined the war. Even though all the things i choose in the events were the exact opposite of the historical things.
As a side note, i won the war about 3 Months later by reducing the french National will to 0. Which was significantly sped up because after the historical end date, there is a constant -30 National will per Turn for everybody.

Another thing that bothers me is the fact you cant kill enemy "units" in the strategical sense. Sure, you can wipe tens of thousands of men continously, but their Corps and whatnot dont care. They only thing you can kill are special Corps, like Tanks, Planes and Siege Artillery.w
Meaning that you cant drain the enemies elite or trained soldiers until they only have conscripts left. In the whole campaign i was fighting with regulars against some sort of elite infantry, even though i regularly deleted them from the face of the earth, be it with Gas or ungodly amounts of artillery.


TLDR:
Doesnt matter what you do, the game will run its historical course. If you are okay with that and the plethora of bugs, weird and/or unexplained behaviour of your units and other mechanics, go for it. It is satisfying to make the enemy disappear in clouds of poison Gas and a constant rain of heavy artillery.
