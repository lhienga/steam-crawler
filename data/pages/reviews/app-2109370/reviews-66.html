\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove most things about the game, there is a big learning curve and don't suspect the game to be easy. Here is my review from nearly 30 hours of game time:

Pro's:
- That did a great job of nailing the WW1 vibe. Expect to lose men, this is inevitable, and i mean it. You will need to throw bodies at the enemy in order to overcome the opposition, it is a grind, and every inch will truly be paid for in blood.

- The AI is smart and human like, they will find the weak positions in your trench network and attack relentlessly until they either achieve their objective or have sustained to many loses and will call for a cease fire which you in turn can accept or counter attack and take the day for yourself.

- I absolutely love the scale, I've had battle where 50,000 men have died on both side in 20 minutes.

- The flow of tech is great, you will start with small dig out for trenches, and really only having artillery and infantry at your disposal, later in the campaign you will have a wide range of tools at your disposal all lethal in purpose. Gas attacks, air burst shells, tanks, flamethrowers, siege battery, aircraft, Storm troopers. In order to defend yourself against these new assets you will have concrete bunkers, concrete MG nests, mortars, and a whole lot more. With each year that passes the battlefield changes tremendously.

Cons
- The AI is relentless with artillery, expect your static defences (MG'S, Mortars) to be hounded by artillery for the whole match. It become's crucial for the concrete defences as the AI will seriously Shell everything.
- It can be unfair how the AI targets troops, as soon as you move troops out of trenches or move them to a different trench the AI will shell them as soon as you give the order, it is almost like it also tracks their way point and knows where to shell to get them exactly, half of my loses are usually from what feels like the AI having laser designated artillery.

- Another point from above is the AI artillery, when calling reinforcements in from your rear lines (back of the map) they will be shelled immediately and be at half health before they even get to the front line.

- seems like a bug that the AI has unlimited gold (not supply talking about campaign map currency)
- The main selling point i thought they where pushing was the persistent battle fields, that seems to be not implemented (other then trench networks and destroyed houses remaining)
1. bodies despawn after 5 seconds
2. craters don't stay
3. tank wrecks don't stay
This is WW1!!! thats what makes the game feel alive come on guys that was your main selling point. I want to see how atrocious and disgusting the war was.

Extra things
- With people believing the AI gets more resources, i don't believe this to be true (Devs have also said it's not true) you notice on defence that the AI will run out of supply quite quickly, unless they have a large number of cores stationed in the province. On offence though they seem to have a lot of supplies, but that's usually because they attack with a lot of cores and it's more then likely they have depots. The thing is though that i would actually say if they had extra supply it's good right now, it's a challenge. Once you learn how to deal with the AI they actually become very beatable so i think the supply situation right now is good. I know for sure when i attack i usually have 1300 supply and i can throw like 30,000 troops at them.

SUGGESTIONS (PLEASE READ DEVS)

- there 100% needs to be a 30 second delay on artillery, this would stop units getting sniped and make the artillery not so crazy because right now with light artillery you can bomb trenches for a whole game (right now i think light artillery is meta as the cool down is like a second long and the radius is massive (which i thinks a bug)) This would also make it feel a tad more realistic as it feels like there is a UAV flying over my trenches designating targets and the arty is way to full on if you know what i mean. If the player or AI wants to use artillery so much they should have to buy more batteries.

- Give us the ability to buy more cores in the campaign
