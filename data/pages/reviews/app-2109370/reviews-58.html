\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere aren't a ton of WW1 games out there, most notably because its incredibly difficult to create 'fun' out a slog. But this is the closet/best thing we have to a WW1 RTS/Total War-like game, and Petroglyph has done a great job. I love the persistent trenches, and seems to capture the sense of WW1 very well.

A few complaints, the AI very clearly cheats FOW, Supply and Tech. They will match you in everything even if you speed rush certain tech trees, they'll hit your units with artillery outside balloon LOS as they come onto the map or move between trenches. I understand balancing against a player is difficult, but the cheating is a bit punishing/over-used.

A few wishes, I'd like to see persistent capture points. I should gain some kind of advantage after sweeping a hex for the next battle and gain the ability to build trenches to fit a new front line. It would reward and push players into taking more risks during the battles. Also more camera controls and an option to zoom out more.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a conditional no until the AI is made less awful to play against. For what is genuinely a really good game set in a lesser-covered area of gaming (until relatively recently), the AI makes it feel like trying to shovel away an endlessly refilling pile of sand. Honestly, its like playing against the Chinese wave attacks in 1950. Can't wait to watch my supplies run out while the AI just pulls 400 units out of its ass and covers the map in them over and over again until i have nothing left only for it to spawn yet another wave of 2000 conscripted Frenchmen just to stomp all over the dead corpse of my army. And then of course the AI doesn't need to worry about campaign supplies while mine just dwindle away because a) you dont get enough and b) the AI keeps obliterating your units by sheer unstoppable, rule-breaking manpower. And then there's your own AI which conveniently keeps deciding that move orders from one trench connected to another should go over ground even though they could easily move through the trenches, then they just stand around in the open for a bit being shot to death, while the enemy AI simultaneously laserbeams artillery strikes onto them in 0.001 of a nanosecond.

Not that its all negative of course, I actually like the strategic overlay in a strat-tac RTS for once. It looks nice and offers plenty of useful strategy options all without being a cluttered, overcomplicated mess. The UI is pretty clear though i must admit that sometimes there is some delay selecting units during battles. Building trenches is honestly very relaxing and easy to do and its nice to see a well-implemented building mechanic in a game like this, usually buildings are either too vulnerable or too useless but the buildings here feel effective and worth placing. I was worried about the game being a buggy, slow mess after playing the demo a few months back, but honestly they have improved performance a lot and it runs fine (after updating my graphics card driver, it was crashing before but i haven't had one since i did that). And honestly, its a really fun game thats probably still worth it if youre big into the history of the period, I just dont think I can keep playing against the Great War AI version of the ant colonies from the Fourth Indiana Jones movie. If Petroglyph fix the AI, I'll change the review to a positive one.\t\t\t\t\t
