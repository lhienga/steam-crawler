\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not terrible, but certainly isn't worth the price. First and foremost, the gameplay. It's fun for a couple hours and then it loses all novelty. It is practically an arcade game, you push this button so this happens. then you watch a bar go down or up. Hopefully it goes up so I win. While that's a little harsh I expected way better for a tried and true company and for a game released in 2023 for this price. The units die extremely fast, I was okay with the infantry health, but the tanks? Frankly, it's stupid as ♥♥♥♥. Some of the emplacements have too much health, including trenches that can't be destroyed without siege artillery. Even the base trenches. I'm pretty sure after shelling and shelling something would be ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up, especially since it's sand and wood. The AI of the infantry and other units are ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ trash, tanks can and did go over trenches during the war, that's why theyre tracked, but they don't they go around. Infantry doesn't know when it wants to be in a trench. Send them forward and sometimes they go in and out for a good couple seconds, with the health constraints they either die or are practically dead. There's a lot wrong with this game, and standing up to other games, it doesn't really come close. I could go on and on but I have other things to do.

Second, the graphics. I know graphics don't and shouldn't make or break a game. However, with it being a larger company. stacked with the engines and graphics resources available in this day and age it seems like a shill game. Huge amounts of indie games with small teams and small budgets could blow this game out of the water. Modders making mods for free can do better than this. The maps are trash, they look like star wars empire at war, petroglyph's previous game. Honestly, it feels and looks like a mobile game, reiterating the arcadeiness I referred to earlier. For a war game, the explosions need to be excellent, the weapons need to be hard hitting. It just isn't, it's underwhelming and disappointing. The sounds of the weapons are not only terrible, but when there's thousands of men fighting and shooting, it sounds like a little kids birthday party with the confetti pops I can buy at walmart. It's just sad to be honest. The vibe is supposed to be dark and gritty, highlighting the destruction and death, but it doesn't feel like that. Remember the Halo tv show? Me neither, but these media pieces share that in common. That they ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up the vibe. Halo is supposed to be a dark military game, death and destruction. The struggle of humanity. The show didn't care, no big battles, no genocides, they cared more about chief's feelings. Same with this game partially, It doesn't feel dreadful, it doesn't feel like a human struggle. Feels like an arcade game.

Third, technology tree. While none of its is bad per say, a lot of emphasis was built upon this mechanic. How it can change your game completely. It doesn't. it can help you in battle but, referring to the arcade topic, it is using the same button you always used. now it does more damage, now it has smoke. My troops have more health but there would be no way I could tell. It doesn't change how I play. If it does, it barely does. Gas attacks? Research masks. get them out of there. It's not innovative.

There are some good things, I like the persistent battlefields. The defense building is fun. I always enjoy a good WW1 game, there aren't a lot of them, however it's really hard to get right. This game didn't get it right, they made another star wars empire at war. It even has the same unit stacks, and extremely linear gameplay.

Overall, if it was 15 bucks sure, have at it. The graphics aren't industry standard, not even for indie games. The AI is bad, the gameplay isn't innovative and it's boring. Not a bad game to waste time, but it's not good in its current state and honestly I'm getting tired of the games as a service. If you guys have any opinions or disagree with what I said, please say so. I'm always open to that. Have a good one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall pretty good. A lot of nuance and options to both attack and defend. A pretty deep research tree that extends that to the strategic layer. If you are into strategy games I can recommend it for the current price.

That said there are some issues. Costs are way off for instance. Troops are absurdly cheap so is suppressing the enemy. Everything else is way too expensive in comparison. Defenses are far too expensive. You can spam basic trenches but even the first upgrade costs 30 supply which is just not worth the bit of added protection against artillery (which the AI doesn't use that much anyways).

Artillery is also way too expensive for what it does. I have to disagree with some of the other reviews that call it useless. It hardly is. Softening up the enemy makes it a lot easier to take trenches once your troops get in there. Otherwise the AI can just keep running reinforcements into them while yours have to cross no mans land. However anything but the basic abilities is not even remotely worth it. Airburst costs 70 for some reason despite only being marginally better than basic shelling. Gas costs 80 which is nuts. It gets the enemy out of the trenches which can be great if done right but it still seems overly expensive. Thats 16 basic infantry for a single gas barrage that might clear 4 trenches at most.

Though this is not as bad as some make it sound. Infantry is cheap to buy but expensive to loose unlike artillery. Losses cost gold reserve and every loss on the tactical level looses you points. Though the latter doesn't really matter as long as you take the enemy control points.

Pathing in trenches is also a big issue. Staying in trenches is absolutely vital obviously as your units get torn apart if they get out in the middle of the enemy network. But sometimes they just get out for no real reason. For instance when you click a trench slightly too far despite it being connected. Or most annoyingly when they pass enemy units in a communication trench they often get stuck and then thrown out.

Finally the AI also needs work. It really loves to mass charge your trenches while barely using artillery. All you need is a second and maybe third line of trenches and they all get themselves shot trying to take the first one with barely any losses on your side. They are fine on the defense and have even caught me off guard with counter attacks a couple of times.

It needs work and balance patches most of all but overall it is a fun game anyways.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is very good. However, it is not very polished.
UPDATE 20.4.2023 for Patch 1.1.0: This singlehandledly fixed like 80% of all issues the game had. Big ups to the devs for listening to the community!
Once I play around with the patch I'll fully update the review.

This is the best execution of WW1 in game form that I know of. It's built specifically for WW1 and its historical aspects, and all of them give off the right feeling.
The art style in cinematics and music score are supreme, and there are a ton of real pictures, drawings, videos and quotes.
So if you're looking for a WW1 sim, you'll enjoy this game, even at full price.

If you're looking for a well made Real Time-/Grand Strategy game, you should probably wait until issues are fixed or buy it on sale.
There are various (minor) bugs that cause slight annoyances, and a lot of seemingly simple improvements that would improve the gameplay experience by a lot.

Currently the biggest hold backs are:
  • Limited Campaign Duration. There is a hard limit set in place and no customization options.
  • Questionable Balance. Partially related to the above, some campaign map buildings are simply better than others. Research points are very limited. Focussing everything in one branch to create a one-trick-pony army and stomp the AI is way stronger than branching out and having a wide arsenal available. Also tanks are probably way too strong.
  • Unreliable Aircraft. Aircraft can't be controlled by the player, which would be fine, but their AI has issues actually finding and firing at targets.
  • Battle Maps. There is a very limited amount of possible Battle Maps, and since trenches only persist on the exact tile-to-tile connection, this gets repetitive at best and confusing at worst.
There are more or less issues, depending on how much you care about the specific aspects.


So all in all, if you're interested in this game, you'll likely have an enjoyable playthrough. As of right now though, there is a limited amount of things to do, so you have to decide for yourself if that's worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeeing a trend here with reviews and complaints about game difficulty:
Y'all can't attack and defend because you neglected logistics. You rushed tanks and planes and gas, and now you can't use any of that because you're short-sighted. The logistics is perfectly fair, you're just really bad.

With that pet peeve out of the way, here's my actual review:

Yes, the battlefields do save destruction, but it's complicated. The destruction of buildings and trees is saved. Trenches are saved. Destroyed Trench encampments are saved. But the craters are more different.

When you replay the same edge, the map is noticably more damaged, with less grass, more mud, more craters, etc. This is what the game description is talking about with persistent artillery damage. Sorry, but this game isn't Minecraft: the landscape isn't yours to sculpt. Honestly, it's probably better this way, as otherwise you'd get people like Spiffing Brit creating a hole to China using artillery barrages.

Here's the actual concession for the persistent battle system: when you sweep a battle (i.e., achieve a complete victory), the territory you were attacking loses a star. I assumed the loss of a star would mean my army has "advanced" and so the battlefield along that edge would be refreshed, as RP wise, I pushed the front line. But when I reentered the same battle to push the tile again, I was replaying the same map. Though this is a concession, from a gameplay perspective, this was probably a good decision, as it allowed me to experience the persistent battlefields even with my numerous victories. So you just have to accept that these persistent battlefields are only abstractions for the miles and miles of built up trench lines along each hex edge.

So beyond the main selling point, how's gameplay? How's the battle system? That's what we're all here for right?

It's brutal. You will be annihilated if you walk outside a trench until you suppress the enemy with artillery or melee combat. Beyond this, the combat is accurate and the devs didn't fall into the numerous retrospective stereotypes that usually plague WWI depictions.

See, most offensives in WWI were incredibly successful for the first couple of days, with favorable causality ratios for the attacking party. But once you've taken a trench, your reinforcements have to travel over no-man's-land to reach the new frontline, leaving your "victorious" men stranded and getting slaughtered by counter attacks. You have to just keep throwing men into your foothold until you're either pushed out or you managed to make something stick. This is how it works in the game, and it makes me feel like a butcher 😁.

For those who have trouble defending, here's a tip: Don't try to defend your first trench line. Retreat, regroup, and then push back. Make them bleed for every inch of territory they try to take. It's called defense-in-depth, and it's why most real trench systems had three interconnected layers of trenches, not just one.

Tanks and planes are (rightfully) moral support. The devs acknowledge the tech for WWII blitzkrieg was simply too far away to break the stalemate, and the only victory possible was jamming the metaphorical meat grinder with thousands of bodies.

Siege artillery is often more hurtful than helpful, which is also accurate. Destroying the trench system you're trying to take makes your job harder.

How's the campaign?

Campaign-wise, the game is too easy, actually, at least on regular difficulty. It was simple to widdle the enemy down to zero national will by consistently winning battles. As long as you don't get greedy, are willing to concede when necessary, and most importantly, PAY ATTENTION to the buildup of enemy forces, it's a slam dunk. I played my first game as Germany, and my national will was always higher than it was at the start of the campaign.

I'll report back after I try the harder difficulties.

In conclusion, is Great War: Western Front an innovative masterpiece? Hell no. The graphics are ugly, the gameplay is repetitive (historical accuracy isn't always fun, unless you're a masochist who likes attritional warfare), and the campaign isn't particularly long or deep.

But it's fun, and worth $40, especially for the music. 7/10, would kill millions of men again.\t\t\t\t\t
