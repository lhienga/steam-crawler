\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game shortly after release, and didn't experience any issues. I am an avid consumer of WWI content of all mediums, and this was a welcome addition to my collection of WWI video games. My experience to date is solely from "Soldier" difficulty, which equates to medium difficulty.

My initial gripe was that from the start I was wanting for more variety in the forces that represented France. One of my biggest pet peeves with WWI representation in the Anglo-sphere is the simplification of French involvement in the Great War. I initially felt this was the case with The Great War: Western Front. I was excited to see Commonwealth Troops represented in the form of Canadian, Indian, and Australian troops (and all with their own voice lines!) But I was left wanting troops from France's own extensive empire.

Much to my delight, this was recently added! France has gained French Foreign Legionnaires, Troupes de Marine (French Marines [but in the service of the Armee de Terre, long story short]), North African and Sub-Sahara African troops (again, with unique voice lines!), and not to mention a heavy tank, the Saint-Chamond! I have to say, these have been excellent additions that I wish to see continued (for France I would love to see Indochinese colonial troops, and adding more regional armies for Germany would be great avenues for future updates [Germany received unique Bavarian Army troops that have been quite troublesome to my Allied Campaign!])

I think the best game mechanics are those that in some way match a core aspect of WWI, such as the hexagonal campaign map. Having more sides against an enemy controlled hex allows for consistent attacks each turn, and if "Sweeps" can be achieved, regions can actually be captured fairly quickly. In an abstract way this mimics commanders capitalizing on bends in the trench lines, either to create overlapping fields of fire, or multiple avenues of attack. My personal favorite strategic experience so far was encircling 10 German Divisions, and gradually taking the territory by sheer attrition (surrounded tiles cannot attack out, and lose a control star each turn they do not have a supply line to a supply hub).

This is true also for the tactical battles, where a balance must be struck between adequate allocation of supplies between artillery, infantry, and support equipment (trenches, barbed wire, machine gun and mortar emplacements, spotting balloon locations, etc.). This can create situations were delays have to be made so troops can be staged, artillery guns reloaded, and aircraft refueled. These tactical pauses feel accurate, and with some imagination, a player can imagine the soldiers waiting to hear the artillery landing before charging towards the enemy trenches. Difficult decisions must be made, like when an attack has to be called off. Perhaps you expended too much supply with preparatory artillery barrages, and this now means the troops you have already deployed would be cut down by enemy machine guns before reaching their trenches. Battles feel rewarding, and roughly capped at 21 minutes, they are balanced nicely into multiple sessions. However, I have found myself caught in the "One more Sweep, and I capture the territory" death loop at least a few times.

One of the best parts of the reoccurring battle sites is how they adapt overtime to changes in technology and troop availability. However, as another user stated, the more destructive elements of the war (craters from artillery barrages, dead war horses, more visibly destroyed forests, and maybe even markers that suggest substantial losses [friend or foe]) would be welcome additions. As it stands, trenches and barbed wire are the most permanent installations, and some individualized changes to reoccurring battle sites as mentioned above would diversify the limited supply of tactical battle maps.

All in all, I was happy with the game, but now I thoroughly enjoy and recommend it. The devs seem to have a true passion for this project, and I look forward to both their changes, and the changes that will be available through the mod support released recently.

TL;DR: Good game, great support, and a promising future. On les aura!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: I got this game yesterday and have 14 hours played. Send food and water please. But for real, if you are a history buff and/or WW1 interests you and you've enjoyed turn based strategy games with an RTS element like the Total War series, then get this game; you will not be disappointed.

Overall, this is a really good game and I applaud Frontier for doing something different and bringing us a WW1 game which few have tried. Great aesthetic on par with the time period (music, weather effects, dreary atmosphere, etc...). Simple controls and pretty quick to learn but difficult to master. Even though it's slow trench warfare, battles are exciting. Playing defense has a bit of "tower defense" element to it as you build your layout of trenches, MGs and mortars before the battle starts and it's fun to learn what layouts work and what can be improved from battle to battle. There's a large tech tree which can't be fully completed in one campaign so that adds a slight element of replay ability to the game. Also, I see some people complaining about corpses disappearing but if you turn the slider all the way up, many persist for most of the battle and are slowly replaced by new ones so I'm not sure if this was recently patched or if people just want all the bodies everywhere at all times. For me, what's there now is pretty good immersion.

My only complaints would be I do agree with others that the graphics are a tad lackluster for 2023. This honestly doesn't bother me at all as someone who still plays Medieval 2 Total War consistently but if you're looking for top notch visuals, you might be disappointed. Also, misclicks on the battlefield are absolutely devastating. One wrong click can start a units animation to leave a trench unintended and they will get wiped in seconds if in range of an enemy trench or MG. You learn to be careful with this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn summary, to me this game is a 4/5 and I love this genre of games. To the average gamer who enjoys COD or RPGS, this would probably be more of an objective 3/5 to most gamers and I don't see most of those type of gamers committing over 20 hours on this and would probably have declining interest after about 10 hours. If you get bored easily or don't like the grind of a ww1 setting you won't enjoy this game. If you love it, this game is for you and easily worth 20 hours given the low price point in my opinion.

I don't normally make reviews, but I have been keeping an eye on others reviews and the updates pretty closely.
I have put a lot of time into this game and have a pretty good feel for it. I have always been drawn to ww1 in both games/casual interests (books/movies etc) so naturally this was an easy buy before it even released. I would say BF1 is one of my all time favorites.

Now to my actual review.

I quite frankly love playing this game its a mixture of some of my favorite games. You are commanding the theatre and also micro managing the battles. Think of it like Total war to an extent having babies with maybe company of heroes, iron hearts, steel division, and other RTS games. I don't have to spend an hour doing a battle like Steel division 2. I can get in and play a couple of battles almost daily.

I am on my third playthrough on elite difficulty and have been very impressed with the updates the creators have continually added. Troop movement, computer difficulty, random bugs are getting addressed and I am excited to see what kind of mods come out.

Realism - Pretty solid..choose your tech and strategy and grind it out. This game won't be capturing tiles every turn and will be a slow grind. I do however enjoy this and felt they replicated this feel pretty well. Plan your attacks and manage supply and you can make big gains.

Graphics - Not that great compared to say COH3. I wish they would have maybe made them a bit better but all in all it doesn't affect the gameplay and I can live with it.

AI - I have mixed feelings regarding the AI. At times it pisses me off, but its them finding a weakness and exploiting it. At other times I feel as if the AI is too dumb or simply not difficult enough and this is on elite difficutly. For example, when the AI is the Allied faction and acting with 11 corps to my 2 from Verdun....This should be a very easy win, but for whatever reasons they cannot take it. Perhaps they just dont have the tech yet but its rather appalling...go overkill on arty and bomb me out or something. With enough machine gun emplacements I set it to fast forward and its always an easy win. If a player plays quite defensively early on (defense always has the advantage typically), they will rack up national will as the AI tries to aggressively choose many battles which result in catastrophic losses. I hope that they can change this a bit and make it more evenly matched or atleast make the AI a bit smarter when attacking.

General gameplay - pretty happy with the mechanics of it. Pretty simple to pick up and play after a brief learning curve. They seemed to fix most of the bugs regarding soldiers moving through trenches but every once in awhile there is a bug as with any games.

Being critical - I wish that the companies had historical ties. They did just update with bavarian troops so that was cool, but would have loved to see "51st highland division" or something and gain experience over time to a veteran unit but the way they made the game you don't "lose" troops so i don't see that happening. Other aspects I would have liked to see was if you capture a point you get to keep it for the next battle or have some trench systems stay in place or the option to remove them. Once a player gets to tanks, it seems almost to OP, but I haven't used them after the patch. Defensively, it seems to easy as I can just make three line trench system and move troops around to counter the attacks. Some maps seem to be wider and I can't hold the line...hence wishing I could demolish trenches. It seems they did fix the bugs regarding putting troops in trees but I still see issues happening when I have one unit in them and 10 companies all die in the trees from that one company....also how the hell are you supposed to take verdun in a 20 minutes time span...I have been unable to do this.

All in all, a great game, hoping this third playthrough doesn't come to easily. Im in 1916 and the AI national will is just tanking due to me being strategic on attacks and quite defensive...which is repetitive but I am not quite bored yet. The tech makes it challenging and offers different ways to win. For 35 bucks I can't complain given my time put into the game...the question is how long will I be able to play this game...New campaigns could be a great idea in other theaters (eastern front, italian front, or the middle east)

Tips - Go heavy on arty. 5 heavy/3 light is a great combo for softening up the front lines prior to an attack. 3 minutes of barrages and its easy to wipe out the remaining defenders. It seems the siege cannons are also improved and they are devastating...especially in battle.

Best of luck!
