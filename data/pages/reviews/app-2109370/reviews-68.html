\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOthers have been complaining about the frame rate, but I haven't seen any issues except when you move the screen to where more than one plane is flying. Then you get a bit of a stutter.

This game is a very fine example of WWI combat, which is to say it isn't for everybody. You want armies slashing through enemy ranks and gaining ground? This isn't that game. It is a war of yards. New tech that comes online can spice up the battlefield by changing the tactics and making life a lot deadlier for your poor bloody infantry.

Good: Great graphics and I love how the trenches you build are still there for the next battle. The AI is pretty decent and will do counter attacks if you break through. You are going to see a lot of units chewed up quickly out of the trenches. The soundtrack is wonderful and has period songs playing as well. Sound is also very good and when the pounding of artillery and crack-crack-crack of machine guns stop for a while, the silence is a little disquieting.

Bad: With this type of war battles can start to feel grindy very fast. You have to get the highest level of victory (which is damn near impossible at the early stages) to knock down the hex's star rating. Knock them all down and you claim the hex. I would prefer a point system where you start pushing the enemy back the more victory points you acquire in that hex, which you only get with a major victory or above.

If you really want a WWI game that is a slow, attrition slugfest, this is a real gem of a game. I do like it, but think it needs a few more tweaks to let it really shine. For those that want quick action, advances and get bored when it doesn't happen, this is definitely not for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, Total War WW1, something ive wanted since playing Total War fall of the samurai. The foundation here is fantastic, but there are tweaks and adjustments needed very badly. Extremely simple stuff like just tweaking the values of some numbers here and there in the code. As is its still great but can be frustrating until you learn to work around some stuff. Luckily the Devs have already addressed many of my issues. *needs mod support

Pros
-Solid large scale battles
-Awesome defense mechanics, building your trenches, mg/mortar emplacements, and barbed wire are awesome and fun to experiment with
-Resource/research management is solid
-Ai really knows how to turn the heat up even tho it still cheats with resources and knowing things it shouldnt like troop positions deep in your lines(con). They will find your weak spot and hit it hard.

Cons
-As above the Ai knows things it shouldnt, idk if their Spy balloons work better then the players or what but they drop bombs on your troops well into your lines and will drop them where you order your troops to move immediately after clicking there. (I think this may have been fixed in todays update, it feels better today forsure)

-Ai has a huge resource handicap even on normal difficulty, you could have an even matched 4 battalions vs 4 battalions battle but the enemy will have enough supply to field all 4 battalions while youll be stuck running out of supply after 2. People like to argue this fact but ive watched the enemy field over 30k soldiers(140 companies at 10 supply each 1400 supply), still build tons of emplacements(5-6 Mgs per cap), and drop arty the entire match. (This is also feeling better)

-Resource handicap Cont. When your battalions get wiped out they dont die, they are reinforced at the expense of gold (you have no choice it will put you negative). I struggle to manage my gold even when i dont buy anything just paying to reinforce my units casualties. Yet when i play defensively to stock pile resources for an attack i still go broke reinforcing units lost to enemy attacks, Even tho im repeatedly putting huge casualties on them everytime they attack 1.5k-2k gold worth every attack, while im loosing roughly 500 gold worth each time. Yet im too broke to afford anything and they can relentlessly keep up with those losses 2-3 battles per turn.

-The Ai will laser beam your weapon Emplacements with arty and typically kill them with 1 barrage (you need 2 for them) and wipe out your defensive line quick if you dont use infantry to screen in the front of your lines to draw enemy arty fire.

-Suppression is OP as hell, people and weapons well outside of the impact of arty will be suppress and unable to fire. (todays update increased the physical size of the light arty barrage, making it feel a little better. Because before the suppression was happening well outside the actual barrage)

-Emplacements such as Trenches and barbedwire are persistent through multiple battles at the same location once built (awesome) but they keep charging you the full price it cost to build them each subsequent battle which is insane. Maybe charge a small upkeep.

-Mg and mortar emplacements dont carry over and should.

-Battle field persistence is unheard of tho advertised. Impact craters and destroyed building despawn, aswell as bodies. All of these things should stay atleast through the current battle. Add a setting adjuster for performance if thats the issue.

-blood and gore is a must but completely void of. Add it as paid DLC like Total war always does IDC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWho the hell named this? "Great War"? This isn't great, It's terrible! Thousands of young men dying to gain mere inches of ground, just for it to be lost days later, and for years on end.

Joking aside this game is exactly as advertised with a few bugs, though I wouldn't call it extremely buggy. In this day and age, this is one of the best compliments I can give on game release.

The AI is good at finding weakness in your lines... when it has vision, which you can prevent. The research tree is comprehensive and will make multiple play throughs unique, though you are almost forced to go down the logistics tree due to constrained resources if you want to go on the attack at all. You really feel the troops digging in over the years, which is aided by technology, supply, and troop count over the years. Weather and persistant scars on the battlefield are a reminder of the passing years, and you feel it.

The best WW1 strategy game I've ever played and I really think they nailed it. I'm honored to give the first review of this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game with a huge amount of depth. First couple of tries I got frustrated until I realized I actually needed to implement a plan and not just throw armies at battles. It may look like WW1 Total war, but it is not! Where is the enemy in strength? Where is my strength? Where are there salients that could draw enemy strength away? Where are there places I can break through? If the enemy is massing in one place, where are their reserves I can pull there? Can I shorten my line to reinforce more efficiently? Do I actually need to attack at all this turn, or would I be better served banking up resources for an offensive in the next two turns?

On the tactical maps I really struggled at defending objectives at first until i realized that a stalemate was essentially a victory on the defense, And I didn’t need to defend every victory point, just enough to stop the enemy from getting a great victory. This allowed me to build stronger defensive positions with the supply available. On the attack, it’s all about keeping enough supply (usually 250+) in reserve during set up to ensure you can overrun the enemy flank and then take apart the rest of his line or defeat the counterattack. In the end, is really a lot about efficiency.

Now that I have the mechanics down, I just won as the Allies by July of 1916. There is a lot here to play with, but I worry that the formula will frustrate others. Can recommend you give it a try, but realize it is deceptive in its need to develop a strategic plan.

Also have not yet gotten any access to the later technologies like tanks as both of my successful campaigns have ended in 1916. Focused more on the logistics tree to keep the gears of war turning. The techs are definitely a tough thing to balance and when enemy bombers show up and you have no counter to them all of a sudden, it can force you to adapt real fast; but it definitely gives you that uncertainty of a WW1 commander. How will the war change next?
