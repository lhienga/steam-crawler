\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRepetitive gameplay that gets old. Which is a shame bc as other reviews say it has a lot of potential despite the pretty rigid limitations on tactics & etc. from the WW1 setting.

One big thing missing from the single player campaign I think is a veterancy/unit xp system. There are tiers of troops but they are locked in when they spawn, so the only way to improve your troops is through the tech tree, which means nothing you actually do on the battlefield matters. In games like Ultimate General one of the big hooks for me is upgrading units as you progress through the campaign and developing a core of elite units you get invested in. In a WW1 game I expect a bit of a meat grinder, but after playing a while in the campaign and fighting the same battle over and over I just got bored.

I was really patient with the game, expecting some sort of hook to emerge to keep me engaged and went past the 2 hour mark for a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fun and fairly unique. It is of the rock-paper-scissor exaggerated combined arms approach of games like panzer general rather than an accurate wargame. Although not accurate it does get the atmosphere of the great war across very well.

Improvements it needs:
- Multiplayer campaign. I have no interest in single battles, but endlessly adapting to check another human opponent over the course of the war would be amazing.
- More maps. It's really inexcusably repetitive in the campaign to fight over the same geography a dozen times.
- A little bit more realism, e.g. pinpoint arty strikes should be a late war thing, tanks should break down. Units should disobey orders on occasion. Suppression small arms fire on trenches and the use of cover in no mans land should be possible etc. etc.
- There should be a separate campaign mode that causes the loss of regions in a single battle rather than using the star system, just to cut down on the appallingly realistic drudgery.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomething of a tepid recommendation, but a recommendation nonetheless. The core of the game is solid, but it could really do with some ironing out of the jank - infantry pathfinding is a little all over the place, and bombers border on useless because of how unreliable their targeting is.

The game is also too easy (at least on the default difficulty), largely because of how passive the AI is; I was only ever attacked once in my entire 30-hour campaign.

That said, the game does a good job of capturing the feeling of the infantry and artillery tactics of WWI, and the battles themselves were generally satisfying if not tactically challenging. On any map where you can place your artillery to hit all of the enemy points (most of them from what I can tell), there's not much the AI will do to prevent you from softening them up with heavy artillery, suppressing with light artillery, and walking your infantry in, rinse and repeat. The one defensive battle I did get to play was against superior numbers, and that was really fun - I actually had to weigh what defenses to use my supply on and such. This is part of why I think the AI should be more aggressive, because it was really disappointing to not get to do that more.\t\t\t\t\t
