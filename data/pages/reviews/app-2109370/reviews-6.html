There are some path finding issues, the ai seems to know exactly where you are at all time even when hiding in the woods, and some other quirks. Overall though I really enjoyed my time in the game.

It took some trial and error to learn how the campaign plays and what tech to prioritize. Everything looks so cool but you won't be able top get all the tech, Maybe if you get to Decemeber of 1919 but I think you actively have to try to delay the game to get that date.

It took me 22 turns to achieve my first national will victory. I'm excited to try different strategies and see if I can win before then now.

The real challenge of this game is attacking. Trying to take all of the control points especially on the larger maps can be hard especially if you don't have your artillery placed well.

If you are a fan of the Great War youtube channel I think you will enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

It doesn't matter how many enemies you kill, they keep coming back with more and more! My resources are limited by these people have an infinite amount of people and shells to throw at me. I thought this is called the western front? Why does it feel like I'm fighting Russia? If You want to fight back to back over nothing, gaining nothing, losing soldiers that are harder to replenish than the enemies that have no drawbacks, this is the perfect game for you. Otherwise, stay away! This game is tedious. I'd rather watch an hour of porn than continue to watch waves of enemies run into my machine guns over and over and over and over....\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI probably will play a few more hours of this, but the question is would I recommend it.
Short Answer is No because it falls short of its development potential.

i wish i could officially give it "try if you have spare cash!"

AFAIK the Devs stopped working on it towards end 2023.
Shame for what could be a very good game with options for DLC and expansions.

I did play at medium level. Won by 1916 with the Entente (French Belgium and UK/Commonwealth) by lowering Central power National Will.
The national Will concept is interesting as a win condition but does not work very well. It is possible to alter the setting at Campaign start, including (effectively) turning it off.

The basic game concepts and mechanics are viable and could be very entertaining.
The AI is not.
It is sluggish to attack,
About you only option during an attack is to corner camp. One or two Corps can hold off an attack by 7 or 8, as the AI is so uncoordinated. Build those MG nests and get some AT guns later game.

I like the supply mechanic which controls all aspects of battles. You want to fire artillery costs supplies want to send in the bombers costs supply, need reinforcements costs supply. You have a limited amount each battle .Eg heavy artillery can eat through your supplies very fast which means the AI has very few trenches left but you cannot afford the reinforcements to exploit your breakthrough!
you have to balance the different military elements to win, and only infantry can take victory emplacements.
it is possible to improve the amount of supply you have in any given battle by various means.

Whilst I agree with Devs it should not be possible to research everything, the amount you can research by the time you win is fairly pitiful.
overall the basic elements of the research tree are good but certainly need tweaking.
It is not possible, AFAIK, to buy extra research with your monthly gold income. But there is a mechanism to buy extra military supply.

You do have the option to add more research points during Campaign set up which i recommend. but try and be balanced. at most you will get 2 research points per month, so 12 will give you a six month stock at game start. it is also possible to give the AI the same.

i am about to venture into an ultra diff campaign with a few custom settings.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is good and I enjoyed my time in it but it also leaves sour taste of classic Petroglyph blunder. Let me explain.
Petroglyph has this thing when they have great and interesting ideas for RTS but then something happens during development and the ideas come out half baked. The potentian is there and the core is good but there is always this missed great opportunity. FFS guys can't you at least once finish your product properly before beying forced to leave it to rot?!

Do I recommend this game? Yes, its good but it could be way better and has some issues that you should be aware of.
Positives:
+Setting of the Great war
+Immersion
+Basic concept and game loop
+Fitting soundtrack
+Localised voicelines
Negatives:
-Strategic map AI
-Limited options of almost anything
*Trenchbuilding is too simple and shallow (three levels of basic firing trenche, shelter, MG and mortar nests, communication trench and two types of wire and nothing else)
*Lack of some types of units (no mobile machinegunners, no special american units with shotguns...)
*Almost no infranstructure (in battles you can build wires, ammo depos, observation baloons and shelters for infartry... thats it! No gas cylinders, no blockhouses, almost no bunkers except the shelter, and concrete nests)
-Overpriced artillery (ammo depo is improvement but it is not a solution)
-Once your wire gets destroyed you cannot place new one on that spot in another battle wich makes no sense and it might be an ignored bug
-Almost no customisation for skirmish and online MP and I mean setting up the battle and not some stupid visuals.
-The ammount of missed potential for this game
-Basic animations

Now lets get closer to the game. There are few gamemodes: Campain, historic battles, skirmish and 1v1 online multiplayer.

In the campian you will become the leader either the Entente (Allies) or the Central powers. You will oversee the enitre western front and deploy your units, you can also buy special units and research new technologies.
When either you or the enemy AI decide to attack a sector, the game changes from turn based front overview into realtime battle. After the battle there will be some medal and awards and based on your performance there can be either increase or decrease of "national will" wich is sort of score and it represent will of your population to fight, if it reach zero you loose (same applies for the enemy). You cannot buy more infantry units, you have either wait for event or research specific tech (there are like 3 or 4 leves of it) wich will give you more units. The costs of units in strategic phase are ok, variaty of units could be better but its enough. On the strategic maps there some special sectors with unique terrain and bonuses (for example, Verdun. tactical rts map has several forts for owner and it gives moral boost for units.). The events while improved since launch still aren't that important and usually mean opportunity to gain either national will or resources. The AI on strategic level is... bad. It's not overpowered or cheating no, no, no. It's rather passive. It will never attack you unless it has like 10:1 superiority so when you spot enemy massing for attack you just sent there like one or two units to slightly increase your strenght to be like 4vs10 instead of 1vs10 and enemy just leaves for another sector you can do that forever.

Before the tactical battle you can build trenches, MG nests, artillery bateries (limited by number of your units in the sector) and barbed wire with limited supplies (based on number of your units in the sector and upgrades of that sector), you can also deploy units for 50% discount with the same supplies. Then the battle begins and you either try to hold down your ground as deffender or capture points and trenches of the enemy. After the battle starts you can still call in more units but for full price. My only problems with these battles are limited scope of battle and absolutly unballanced economy of battle. They give you all the toys like tanks, poison gas shell for heavy artillery, creeping barrages for light artillery, bombers etc. and the lock it behind unholy cost in supply. You cannot properly use gas in campain untill late game when everyone has a gas mask because gas is super expensive (standard barrage is like 8 supply and gas barrage is like 80 supply!) yes they added ammo depos wich decrease the cost of any barrage by 50% but it still sucks and if you go for gas in research you then lack in other departments wich are often more important.Also you can call in air support but you have no control over it and its AI is terrible.

Historic battles: A sort of challenge mode. You play one of the historic battles with limited resources and specific objectives. Most of the historic maps are played on skirmish maps (one exception is battle for Passchendale with unique map). There are like 6-7 historic battles.

Skirmish and MP: Tactical battle with custom army against enemy AI/player on one of the maps from campain. This mode suck because we cannot set up almost anything! You can choose your army units and map you are playing... Thats it! You cannot set the AI to be either deffender or attacker, you cannot choose it even in MP with other player wich means the games often end up as starring match with artillery duels because both of you decided to be the deffender. You cannot change starting supply (there is one modifier wich can sort of change it but its clunky). Thankfully they added one patch that let us at least customise enemy AI at least a little bit.
