\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's rare i write reviews for games played but feel like i must for this

One must wonder: "how the hell do you make an RTS in WW1, a war which was essentially "yeah bomb and gas them until your fourth wave of Infantry takes one trenchline?" but somehow miraculously, Petroglyph were able to pull it off. giving you the same old goal of "take point XYZ and their HQ" but *how* you take it is up to you. the tools, research, resources are in your hands and thus must act accordingly and with sense. i am genuinely impressed that there it's a game that you'll have to experiment with to know what tactics to use, what units to deploy and where to send them/when to properly use them. timing is key

Yet at the same time, no two battles are the same (Unless you've been attacking Bruxelles 5 times from the same tile which uh...don't please) always offering a different layout each time (albeit once you begin to memorise the maps shown, it's easy to act accordingly but don't be fooled)

Now of course there are downsides: sometimes units move awkwardly, moving forward then regrouping only to be mowed by rifle fire, units outside of trenches orienting towards trenches already in the midst of melee combat and thus, getting mowed down by rifle/MG fire and the comedic launching of grenades across the map because one German Rifleman is near a machine gun and all of a sudden the Kaiser's Army are armed with M203s is more funny than annoying to see.

Then comes the absolute bane of my existance: Not tanks, not constant arty fire, not interceptors trying to nail my balloons...♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥...mortars

Mortars somehow have more of a destructive potential on tanks than Heavy Artillery, Raiders and Elite Infantry with A-T rifles combined, easily popping a tank in at most 3 salvos. in addition to completely suppressing any infantry unit anywhere near it's impact radius making it a nightmare to not only attack at, but defend from. allowing enemy forces to waltz right in, smack your boys and take back lost land often leading you to either send in bombers (which at 90 supply is expensive and their bomb targets are unpredictable) or launch round after round of heavy artillery at them that could be better spent shelling trenches

One other gripe i have is the rather disappointing lack of the A.I, even on vet mode, refrain massively from attacking even if they have the advantage (which at times i have been at a sheer disadvantage, i was still able to plan and defend accordingly and successfully, racking up a rather horrifying body count). in each campaign playthrough i've done, at most i've only been attacked probably two times with the player often resorting to doing the attacking which if you know what you're doing, becomes very repetitive and mechanical

Overall despite the setbacks and what people may say about the game, i would heavily recommend trying it for yourself before buying as it's definitely not to everyone's fancy but from my time playing it, is very enjoyable and gets the old thinker a good exercise. if the devs could iron out the issues many have pointed out, this game could very well pan into a broader series like the FPS Verdun\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was somewhere between a positive and negative review for this game but ultimately decided on a negative review for the following reasons. For what this game is, it is about what you would expect. It is essentially sandbox gameplay. The problem is the campaign and units aren't overly interesting, and once you figure out the economy and tech tree it doesn't really feel that it adds a lot of variable to the gameplay. There is also no active multiplayer whatsoever. I looked for a multiplayer game and there was no games being hosted so I tried hosting for a bit and no one joined so essentially you're going to wind up playing campaign or skirmish. I also would like to see mod support openly integrated into the game how games like company of heroes openly invite modding. Imagine this game with player made maps, units, game options, etc. As far as I could tell it is not openly integrated into the game. If this game wants more replayability without an active multiplayer it needs more skirmish options like the ability to increase max supply or maybe add more ai teammates/enemies and make the battles larger.

Ultimately I think this game has a decent production quality and has some good elements to it, I just think there is more to be done to make this a replayable and active game. With some changes and updates I think this could be a more solid game but as of now it just kind of feels like it is missing a few key elements.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love RTS games, I love historical games, but this is the most boring glorified mobile game I've played for awhile. I could have played longer to see if it got better but than I risk running over the 2 hour mark and I already know this is not worth $35+.

1. AI has hacks to make up for a bad AI.
2. Game mechanics are boring and shallow, especially early game.
3. Menus are crowded, over sized, and poorly laid out.
4. Mod support is lacking to say the least.
5. Unnecessary loading screens for 2023
6. The graphics are just ugly and bland, you will not find yourself admiring units.
7. I hate the manpower/supply system.
8. Even clicking to move troops feels disgusting. Want to raid a trench? I hope you don't click ON the trench, you have to click ABOVE the trench. If you click on it like some wacko, your troops just stand outside of it and eat bullets.

9. It's WAAAY too expensive for a glorified mobile game. I would not pay more than $15 for this game. Look at other reviews, even positive ones rarely surpass 50 hours of game play.
9.5 On top of the already over-zealous price tag, they have a 'Victory Edition' that adds nothing to the game for $5-10 more.

2/10 the game runs but you'll close it out soon enough because watching paint dry is more entertaining and visually appealing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game. There aren't very many games, especially strategy games that have a WW1 setting. The game has a few good ideas. Spending resources to build lines of trenches, barbed wire and machine guns is fun. The trenches persist (somewhat) between battles, giving the battle maps some continuity. There is grand map that reminds me a bit of when I used to play Axis and Allies with my friends a long time ago. Defending your trenches from waves of infantry is satisfying.

It just doesn't quite add up to enough to make it worth buying. The graphics are quite bad. It looks like a game that made over 15 years ago. There is a "command limit" resources that limits the number of infantry units you can command at once to thirty. I get the impression this was just because the game engine couldn't handle more at one time, not for a gameplay reason. The battles are very repetitive. This is made worse by the fact you have to fight it out multiple times in one region to take it. Once you secure part of the enemy trenches, it's a given you will with the round unless you run out of resources, but this is tedious and takes a long time. I would hate to think a bad review could discourage other smaller developers from making a game in this setting, but I really just can't recommend it.

edit: after continuing to play, I found a much worse problem. Once you beat the AI in a few battles it runs out of gold. When this happens it is unable to attack for what seems to be the rest of the game. Totally broken, unfortunately. It also sounds like the game has been abandoned by the developers so this and other issues won't be fixed. Save your money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game appears to be a beautiful recreation of the first World War at first glance, yet quickly you see the thin visage of the game fall to highlight a surprisingly thin RTS with frustration being an unfortunate addition owing to the numerous bugs which arise.

Minus:
-Having only a couple hours in the game, there is much more that I wished to see, but am aware that means playing the game for longer than I can bring myself to.
-The game runs significantly poorer than many other RTS games (Total war/Men of War/Toy Soldiers) despite coming out in 2023.
-Your soldiers die so much faster than the enemy, which normally is a great representation of trench warfare; however, when they assault the wrong trench, or refuse to climb into a trench due to poor path finding, the quick death quickly becomes an "Alt F4" prompt.
-The cursor appears to be off, meaning clicking on units is an annoying chore. This was something which I've tried to work around to no avail, even pausing everytime I wanted to select units.

Pluses
+the sound is incredible, I am tempted to listen to the soundtrack for the immersion, whilst playing a WW1 mod in other games.

Do not buy until the game stabilises.\t\t\t\t\t
