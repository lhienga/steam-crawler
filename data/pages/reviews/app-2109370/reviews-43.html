\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe only reason that i can wholeheartedly say this game is ever worth a buy is because its the only one of its kind to fill this WW1 RTS niche

Every action you take culminates to you playing across most of France, Belgium, and the Rhineland but only on 12 maps with a painful persistent trench system. Whilst defending you set out your trench works as marketed. The issue with the persistent battlefield is that it only applies to what ever enemy tile is attacking you: for instance you get attacked from one tile-you build and defend; and next battle you are attacked from a different enemy tile on the same map, YOU DONT GET YOUR PRIOR TRENCHWORK. That leads to UNBELIEVABLE REPETITION across these maps.

You do have the capability to encircle enemy forces but UNITS OF BOTH SIDES DO NOT GET DELETED UNDER ANY CIRCUMSTANCES. Once you have a successful envelopment of enemies, the tiles bleed control stars till you own the Provence. After about 2 or so turns all the enemy units return to their own front lines.

During battles friendly tanks after giving their first order WILL NOT auto engage enemy targets. this leads to a bizzare isue where enemy forces can run around your tanks with out them shooting at each other. Tanks are unbelievably micro manage nightmares that can turn the tide for you, but are too painstaking to use.

Lastly, troops can only melee in designated portions of the trenches. You and the enemy can move forces through a single trench network at the same time creating a comical, yet annoying issue of 2 sides trying to kill each other walk by one another and give out GG highfives at the end of a junior high sports game

What I would've liked to have seen from the devs:
-more maps or procedural generated maps
-friendly tanks have some degree of auto targeting
-enveloped units actually being deleted instead of warped
-multiplayer campaign

I am a complete fan of Petrolyph's Star War's Empire at war. its just a bit disheartening to see this game have these shortcomings\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo after having completed a central powers campaign i give it a 7/10, a good game overall with some issues.

Pros:
-Does a good job at making you feel the absolute grind that was WW 1, the battles for the most part on the RTS portion feel nice for the most part, that is until you get tanks, more on that later.
-Game is fun to play if you don't mind the slog that is first world war, although this isn't going to be for everyone as taking ground really isn't the name of the game here, draining the enemy's will to fight is a legitimate, although slow, way to win.
-Tech tree is done well, you will not get enough points to research everything, so figuring out your play style and focusing on that is a must.

Things that are not bad per say but could go with improvements:
-Tanks are a too overpowered, once i got tanks I used them to essentially just sweep enemy trenches, then moved the infantry up to claim the point, I was taking less then 1,000 losses, usually around 300 to 500 with this tactic in a game where deaths could easily get to 15,000+ in a battle depending on the situation, toning down tank damage or adding in a dedicated, but expensive, anti tank rifle unit or turn down the damage tanks do would help solve the issue.
-Machine guns and mortars are borderline useless until you get to the fortified one in the tech tree, once spotted they get blown to bits by artillery, lower the cost of both would make them more viable because I just use the 30 supply on extra troops rather then the AI spending 9 to 18 supply to destroy them.
-Espionage leaves a little bit to be desired, you click a button, and it either a success or a failure, that whole portion of the tech tree I ignored for the most part, just went down the path to get to sabotage of buildings on the strategic map, and that was it. i feel like some of the abilities should be a little cheaper or be tweaked a little to make them worth it.

Cons:
-The AI on the strategic map is way too passive, like incredibly so, they won't attack even with superior numbers and supply, meaning you can attack weaker areas of the front and they won't apply pressure elsewhere all the time when they easily could.
-I wish that there were other fronts besides the western front in the game, I know the game is about the Western front, its in the name, but an eastern front with the Russians against Germany and Austria-Hungary, or a southern Front between Italians, and Serbians against the Austro-Hungarians would add a lot to the game.
-It's a similar issue with historical battles, in my opinion there could be a few more, 1st battle of the Marne and the Battle of Liege in 1914 wouldn't be bad picks for example.

All in all a decent game and hopefully a spring board into a broader experience.\t\t\t\t\t
