\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game all around! Yes, the graphics are a bit underwhelming, but there is a lot going on in a simple to pick up package with this one. You control your respective side in WW1's western front on a grid based strategic layer, then zoom down into persistent trench warfare for tactical battles - whenever you face off between those two hexes, you will get the same map with all the carnage you had from the last battle(s) still present which is awesome. Tons of different units and nationalities are represented, each with their own unique modifiers.

The game has a unique and simple way of simulating WW1, in that your "currency" is split into two parts - gold reserves and supply reserves. The lives of your men boil down to how much of the two they require to do their job, and when they fall they are easy to replace but the cost in gold and supply is not. Manage them well so you can plan your offensives, or have enough to stave off the enemy's.

I **HIGHLY** recommend starting your first campaign in the 1914 scenario without playing any tutorials, just jump right in and figure things out like the actual commanders had to. You will have the most authentic experience trying to figure out how on earth to break the deadlock, and you will slowly figure it out as you choose technologies to help you evolve your army into a modern and efficient fighting force. After surviving numerous harrowing battles, checking your astronomical death toll, you might just break the opposing side's willpower and end the War to End All Wars.

Please support these devs so we get some sequels!\t\t\t\t\t
