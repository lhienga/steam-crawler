\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this game does have a lot of good elements to it, its flaws are too great and so I cannot recommend unless you are a hardcore WW1 guy.

The main issue with the game comes from the AI. It is really bad and easy to cheese. On the campaign map the AI doesn't know how to effectively manage the front so you can make a deathstack and face little response from it for a few turns. Similarly, in battles the AI prioritises filling up all the trenches, which means if you route a unit you already know another unit is on its way to replace it. This is exploitable because the AI will choose the spawn on the side of the map that can be pre-fired with Artillery. Heavy Artillery is therefore super Overpowered as you sit back and bombard the enemy with 15 guns and then when they have run out of units stroll in and take the points. This is compounded by how relatively cheap heavy arty is to fire, and the fact that even if you have barely any troops the AI will not push this clear advantage.

Because of the way supply works you are incentivised to throw loads of units onto one tile to maximise the supply and number of units that army can bring into battle-there are no drawbacks to doing this (such as combat width). In addition the AI makes no distinction between units so as long as you have enough on a tile they will not attack, even when it has an advantage.

Jumping off the back of AI the pathfinding and responsiveness is also lacking in this game. Especially when a single click can decide instantly whether a unit will take a trench or be mown down. If it was implemented in a more delayed and turn-based fashion it would be historical; it is difficult to control 1000s of men in real time without mistakes or delay, etc. However, I don't think this is what the game is going for, instead it comes off as clunky, as I painfully watch my units try to do a U-turn or barely scrape by a trench's range, as they stroll into artillery fire...

The campaign map also fails to provide enough to do. Even if you are bad at the game it is very hard to lose, because the AI will frequently give you minor victories or stalemates. Technically you only need to win one or two major battles across the whole war in order to win-the problem is that the game becomes so dull that you get bored and make a deathstack to push through and end it quicker. While it could be argued that this is realistic, the game fails to provide other ways of engaging the player.

The game will give you for instance missions to accomplish although these can all be ignored without it really impacting the game. In addition, they rarely have any effect on what is actually happening across the front, and also are at odds with how the game wants you to play. You may attack in the south because it is the most lightly defended or in the center, but the game wants you to fight in Verdun? The game gives no reason to roleplay and drag things out when you can avoid them and destroy the enemy.

There are little events which rarely are any inconvenience either. The game features research which is simply a spiderweb or different buffs and unit types. As previously mentioned though you only need to go for the techs that give you more money to spend on units and better artillery. All other unit types or buildings are too expensive to justify, and rarely provide more benefit that the starting artillery.

I think that if the game had strong AI that could really put up a fight then many of the other flaws/criticisms would be far less noticeable. The WW1 gameplay would be engaging if you were fighting tooth and nail to win the war, and this was the desired effect but falls flat. It is engaging for the first campaign where you are learning the ropes but unfortunately once you get the hang of it things get fairly easy and predictable.
