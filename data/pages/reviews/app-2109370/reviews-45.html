\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very nice grand strategy/RTS game.
Turn based campaign on a map of the western front. The end goal is either taking the enemy's HQ or demoralizing his/their nation(s).
There are reasearch options that make both your battle and home front more efficient plus resource management. Manpower is represented by ''gold bars'' while equipment and ammo as ''supplies''. There is also an espionage layer, which is pretty much useless. You decide where you attack the enemy and they decide where they attack you every turn. You need to watch enemy troop movement on the grand map in order to anticipate their next attack move and reinforce accordingly. The AI reacts to your moves the same way.
Battles are fought on a battlefield that isn't really that persistent as devs claimed. Sure, the destruction is there, the trenches stay, but the destruction isn't player made, it's gradual over time. I have some reservations about the AI. It is very challenging at the begining, but once you figure it out, you can steamroll it during the offensive missions. The AI is very good on the offensive though. Some defensive battles gave me a real sweat!
The FPS sometimes drops, there are some technical issues with pathfinding and units sometimes glitch when moving in the trenches. Those bugs should be easy to stomp for the devs in the coming months.
Overall a very nice game and I really look forward to it's future development. Maybe Italian front?

8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a great and unique game, which stems from the fact that it is a WW1 RTS. The game concept evovles around the stalemate on the western front. Thus it is a game of attrition, who bleeds out faster, you or your enemy? On easier difficulties it is nethertheless possbile to fight a more mobile war, where you can take some regions per round (around 2-3). Be warnend this game is different than any other rts you played before, and that is one reason why I love this game. The main difference is the more than realistic time to kill. Your soldiers are dying very fast once they get in the line of fire. Gunfire, MGs, Artillery, planes and tanks will kill your infantry faster than I any other game I played before. Every attack has thus to be planned carefully. A mindless onslaught won't win you anything than sure defeat.
The campaign focuses on your battles you fight. A complex economical system doesn't exist and isn't necessary at all. What you have to manage are your gold reserves and your supply, if you are running out of those thing, you will have a tough time fighting.
Thus said, the game is for RTS enthusiasts, who are interested in WW1. Newcomers may feel the game concept of "fighting for every inch" a little bit frustrating, but this isn't any other rts, it is "The Great War".

I only can recommend this game. It is very unique in the way it functions, which stems from the nature of trench warfare and it is brought to life in a brilliant way.

And btw. did I mention the awesome music?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing to preface this review with a simple statement: I like the game. I think it is fun and it's different enough that I get enjoyment from it. The market for strategy games set in World War I is non-existent, and the wannabe historian in me is happy with what I have. However, I wanted to post this review as a warning: This game only does the bare minimum that is expected of it and is incredibly lacking in terms of passion and presentation; in other words, it knows that it has been released to a desperate consumer base with no real market competition.


When I say it does the bare minimum, I mean in every aspect: The mechanics are bare minimum, the audio and visuals are bare minimum, the AI is bare minimum, the design is bare minimum, and even the "meta" is bare minimum. Put together they function as a game, but it is a game that will instantly be displaced and forgotten the second another developer makes something with even the most minuscule of improvements.

The game utilizes a strategic map that plays like a turn-based board-game, with battles being fought on a local real-time map between participating portions of a whole army. At first, the game is very clear about its intentions: taking ground from the enemy is a slog. It is possible to play though an entire campaign of this game, and win on purpose, without ever taking a region from the enemy, and vice-verse. That being said, the game still requires you to be extremely mobile with your troop placements, as the enemy AI will constantly shift the bulk of its army all over the front in order to attack the areas where your brigades are not present in large numbers. This is initially a cool concept, as it requires the player to be more actively engaged with the campaign map due to the AI's tendency to ball up a corps of 20 brigades and attack a single region with them. But in practice, it results in a never-ending game of late-stage Chess where every turn is just you and the AI putting each other into indefinite "check." This is because you cannot move a brigade and attack with it on the same turn and brigades have an unlimited movement range, meaning that you can move 20 brigades to attack position, end your turn, and then immediately find that the enemy has pulled 20 brigades from all spans of its territory to defend the front-line you're about to attack.

This ultimately resulted in me not even caring about the offensive aspect of warfare in this game, especially since the AI will still follow through with an attack, even if I moved a superior countering force into position. They inevitably lose this assault, and I end up bleeding 20+ war support off of them, while gaining 10+. You can win the war just by defending your territory from suicidal attacks. Attacking is made further pointless by the fact that regions have a defense value denoted by a "star level," the idea being that, if you get a "Great Victory" (and only a great victory) then you will take one star off of the region's defense value. The issue with this is that you can only attack a region one time with a corps or division from a neighboring region, and your success in defeating the enemy is based solely upon the numerical advantage of your attacking force. If you do not defeat the enemy and capture the region by the end of the first turn, then come next turn, the AI will have pulled upwards of 20 brigades into the region, rendering any further attacks completely pointless. Also, any stars that you bled off the region with successful attacks will simply regenerate each turn that you do not attack the region, so even winning multiple "great victories" that completely swept the enemy off the field results in absolutely no ground gained. I understand the artistic design and intended idea behind this choice, but it would've made more sense if "Grand Victories" were simply rarer to get, and resulted in a complete takeover of the region, while lesser victories could bleed off the defense stars. At least then, there'd be a reason to engage in offensive game-play.

What's more, the map seems to be biting off more than it can chew in terms of scope. I'm sure that the intention was to make sure that all of the documented engagement points of the Western Front were represented, but given the pace of the game, it's just too much. It's mostly infuriating because of how easy it is to fix: Air-wings should only be able to station at airfields, and any air-wing should be able to participate in a battle from a different region if they're within a certain distance from it. Siege artillery should be able to participate in battles up to a certain distance away, and supply hubs and other support buildings should be able to have their effects carried over a distance, perhaps at diminishing returns. But for all that is holy, I should not be building 20 supply stations, canteens, hospitals and airfields in every single region all across the front. It's madness I tell you.

My biggest issue with a lot of World War I media is that they seem to always think the best way to represent the horrors of industrial warfare is by showing a single machine gun mowing down hundreds of soldiers in seconds. Yes, I am aware that World War I was largely fought by Napoleon era-minded generals that had issues coping with the modern incarnations of small arms and artillery, but the fact of the matter is that an entire infantry company isn't going to run forward in the open, bayonets fixed, while being slaughtered by a fully automatic sniper rifle. An infantry company has two formations it can use while moving: Column and Skirmish. The first one increases their movement speed (I hate this trope) while making them easier to kill, while the latter lowers their movement speed (why?) while making them harder to kill. The main issue with this mechanic is that, once they're on the field, you are never going to have them in column formation unless you're dumb and just want them to die, and when you pull them onto the field, it's usually to send them into a trench anyway, which automatically switches them to skirmish formation. It's just dumb and there's no mechanical reason why you should be able to switch between the two manually. But the second and most glaring issue with this mechanic is how "skirmish" formation actually works in the game, which is that it doesn't.

A machine gun that is shooting at a company which is skirmishing will only kill everyone in it slightly less quickly than if that company was in a column, this is because the machine guns fire with such consistent accuracy that they might as well be a sniper using a semi-automatic rifle and a 100 round magazine. Advancing companies (in skirmish formation mind you) also will not attempt to utilize cover or concealment, they will not lay down and crawl to make themselves harder to hit, and even heavily wooded areas offer no cover bonus what-so-ever for any units moving through them. Industrial warfare is already brutal without the comically over-the-top "tally-ho" "The future is now, old man" caricature of Napoleonic line warfare that is constantly represented in World War I media. And I haven't even said the best part: Artillery is almost entirely worthless against a company that is in skirmish formation. Yes, if the company is in a column, then it will get absolutely plastered by artillery. But when in skirmish, the effectiveness of artillery on them is cut in more than half. So instead of a situation where machine gun fire is forcing hundreds of men to go to ground, while artillery beats the ♥♥♥♥ out of them for hours until they finally retreat, you instead get god-tier machine gun crews with kill counts in the thousands, and artillery that only acts as a minor nuisance. Again, it's madness.\t\t\t\t\t

I had an entire company of infantry wiped out behind my lines by a single airplane, something that just is completely unrealistic. Planes played no serious anti-infantry role. Tanks are available in 1914 despite playing no serious role through all of WW1. The majority broke down or got stuck before even reaching enemy lines late in the war. Enemy infantry units can stand next to each other in the same trench and not fight. If you order them to occupy the same space they will engage in a combat to the death where one side is wiped out and the other nearly so. I was strongly biased to love this game due to my strong desire for a WW1 game of this type, but this just isn't ready.\t\t\t\t\t
