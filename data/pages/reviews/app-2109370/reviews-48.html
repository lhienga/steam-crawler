\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGenerally recommend with a few caveats:

1) Pathfinding and combat logic are broken for both sides during tactical engagement, I've watched the flag for multiple units float off randomly while the unit moves elsewhere. Units will sometimes move into trenches, sometimes not, an error which can cause major issues. Adversary units will repeatedly walk past each other in trenches without fighting because the combat is anchored to nodes in each trench section.
2) The game portrays the attritional nature of the war reasonably well. This means repetition and tradeoffs in depth of the tactical battles because you'll fight repeatedly for the same ground even after a major or great victory.
3) Graphics and audio are not to current standards.
4) Unlike many similar games, you won't be able to research the full tech tree, which introduces some interesting tradeoffs and prioritization.

Overall, if you're ok with repetition and limited graphics for a reasonably accurate WW1 game, would recommend. If you're looking for a true Total Experience, this is not that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: I'd recommend this as a novel strategy wargaming experience but only just. A great idea is lost in poor technical execution which fails to capture the scale and cost of the war.

Pros:
-Unique setting
-Fundamentally good gameplay
-Beautiful landscapes

Cons:
-Repetitive
-Basic technology can be used throughout the game.
-Bodies & shells fading loses epic-ness of the setting.

Longer:
When I first heard there would be a WW1 game I was beyond excited. With so few entries in the series, this game and subseuqent sequels/expansions covering the other fronts of the war had the potential to dominate the setting.

Despite the tutorial, my initial attempt on my own was a dismal failure with about 2500 troops gunned down 'gloriously' without even seeing a picklehelmet or smelling sauerkraut. From there, however, I developed a formula of 2 heavy guns, 3 light guns and four control groups which didn't fail me for the rest of the game. Figuring this out for the first time felt like a real success. However these battles became monotonous, and auto-resolve was harsh even when I outnumbered them 3:1.

Far from a static line, I was sweeping the enemy constantly, encircling huge pockets and winning the German campaign (on mid difficulty) by turn 11 and the Allied campaign (on 4/5 difficulty) on turn 8 (mid 1915). The campaign is moderately interesting. I don't like star system for taking provindes as this literally forces you to fight multiple times on the same battlefield- reinforcing the same-ness. Instead, a dynamic battle with long-maps, where attacks will reach a natural stalemate as they exceed their observation & artillery range would be better. Defenders should have additional supply bonuses (depending on the star level), allowing you to slowly grind down the enemy while meaning that taking 1/2 checkpoints actually has an impact.

The best moments are when the battle is flowing, desperately defending against waves of enemies or repelling overwhelming enemy counter-attacks.

However the offence can get boring. Enemies will build trenchlines that connect the whole map. Because infantry can only be targeted by artillery in trenches (at least early in the war), the game becomes a conga-line of feeding units into hand-to-hand combat (probably the least engaging combat mechanic).

I think the moment that took the shine off the game for me was about 3 hours in. I had a brutal conga-line-fuelled battle over one section of the enemy trench. The foe and I fed company after company into this trench - probably 4000 men died in about 50m of trench. And I felt - nothing. I had no connection to these bodies nor to the feeling of loss constantly pushed in the cutscenes.

The landscapes look stunning but the battle scars and bodies fade very quickly. The Infantry models are very simple and they felt more like ants than people.

I also find that most technology upgrades aren't worth using. This is due to supply (resource) imbalance. Infantry companies cost 10 supply. Standard heavy bombardments cost 9 - airburst costs 70 and gas 80. That means they will need to kill 7-8 squads to pay their cost. Basic light artillery bombardments are also far too effective - making rolling barrages riskier and more costly.

The historical battles are where the game really shines when it can set-up a dynamic & interesting contest with unique conditions.

Overall I like this game but I don't love it. I really wish I did. I didn't comment on minor bugs here as I'm sure they'll be fixed soon enough. I'd recommend it as a unique experience and I'm sure some/all of these issues will work itself out through patches/mods. 7/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI meant to write a review of this game but didn't get to it until now. Super fun game but I have a lot of problems with it. #1 problem is the 'Living World' aspect of it. All that means is the more times you fight over a single piece of land, there will be pot marks on the ground from shells landing. However these won't be from previous battles, they'll be there just because this is the 5th time you fought on the same map. For example I finally noticed the shell marks from the 3rd time fighting on the same map but I had already conquered all the enemy control points on this map, blown up one of their armored encampments with an underground mine but every time I replayed this map the enemy had the exact same control points & the armored gunner spot I had blown up was reinstalled despite how when you blow these things up, there's a huge dip in the ground. 1 positive thing but extremely minor & still a negative is that at least my previously built trenches were still there but even still my trenches didn't have the machine gun posts so I had to reinstall those every time fighting the same map for the 5th time. Super tedious.

Another tedious element in the game is when you're moving units you have to select them all at once, so lets say you put 30 artillery pieces & 5 tanks on one section because you wanted to destroy the enemy trenches, okay now you have to move those 30 pieces & 5 individually by clicking one at a time instead of just being able to move the entire group with a button click or some sort of obvious key press (ctrl/shift/etc).

Still really love this game, it just needs a lot of Quality of Life improvements.

Another negative I'll point out is the trench warfare isn't that interesting. It's not soldiers aiming at one another from a hundred yards away, it's pretty much reduced to just throwing bodies at fortified positions and hoping you have enough men arrive to kill everybody. The combat isn't as slow as I would think WW1 would be. I would just feel like WW1 combat would be incredibly slow where every half hour there might be soldiers firing at one another in the trenches with a 90% miss rate and even that 10% that did hit weren't likely to result in instant death but instead get infected and then slowly die from trenchfoot or disease spread by rats. My greatest criticism of the game is how fast it is despite how the game reiterates at loading screens multiple times about how it's a war of inches.

Now that I've written 2 paragraphs about why this game sucks, why am I giving it a thumbs up? Because that's just how bad the gaming market is for slow burn strategy games. That even though I have all these problems with it, I really do enjoy the niche that these developers have carved out for themselves by making this game and I deeply appreciate it. If I wanted to play another game like this right now I would have 1 alternative and it's a top down WW1 trench game (On The Western Front - Great game btw). Games that are unique and not following a trend will always be rewarded and coveted by me.\t\t\t\t\t
