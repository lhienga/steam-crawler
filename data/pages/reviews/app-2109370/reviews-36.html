\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game with a few caveats, I will try to discuss things that haven't been overstated in the reviews.
To begin with what I think stood out:
- The balance of the frontline, especially once you take ground and thus stretch your lines.
- The Ai did a good job in the campaign at surprising me with the direction of its attacks
- The need for combined arms warfare to take and especially hold ground
- Unique setting for a grand strategy game

What I think could be worked on
- Length of campaign, I think we are looking at roughly 50 turns from memory, most grand strategies will go into the 200-300 range. If the campaign was a per week basis I think it would give the developers an opportunity to cover more of the history and to allow yourself and the enemy more time to plan/develop attacks and defence, also emphasising the balancing of the main resources, supply, national will and money.
- Certain areas of the research tree seemed pointless on Soldier difficulty, I rushed tanks (I felt taking the route opposite the French would help) and then started rolling enemy positions. A good tank rush with suppressive artillery will save you so many infantry.
- Wanted more variation for trench design, felt the ability to design effective arcs of fire limited working with the system used
- Wanted smarter AI trenches, connecting everything with comms trenches is not a brilliant idea
- Larger scale conflicts (with greater than 30 units, I could accept more of the graphical quality as it is to see the game at scale)
- Larger maps/longer fronts
- I thought the design decisions for taking a tile to be rather curious, to have to capture all enemy points time and again. I would prefer to see myself starting where my offensive last left off, if I capture A point and not B, C, D, E I would like to still hold A point, creating a tug of war each time you play the map. This coincides however with larger maps, with all maps having more points to contest.

Outside of these things, I would love to see more content come in the way of different fronts and theatres and potential expansions to the series with a refinement of the mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so close to being really good. There are several key elements that are missing that would put this game over the edge to being phenomenal. The first of these being the lack of campaign multiplayer. The AI is so brutal, even on higher difficulties, in that it smashes itself repeatedly into you, suffering major loss after major loss. This would be completely remedied if playing against another human being.

The AI, at a tactical and strategic level, is incapable of beating a human being due to how it was trained. It seeks the weakest point on the campaign map or the battle map and will repeatedly attack it, even if met with failure. This leads to a very gamey feeling where you can exploit the AI's inability to strategically position itself. The AI will often leave 1 corps on a hex when it seeks to overload one point on the line. This usually leads to it not breaking through where it overloaded (usually with 9 corps on two adjacent hexes) and a province being taken wherever it left that single corps.

Attacking is also horridly unbalanced. I understand that it is WW1 and the warfare is inherently stationary, but even when you start unlocking things like gas or tanks, it is nearly impossible to make a meaningful attack (great victory in this case) on a hex with more than 2 corps on it (this is more due to the supply system and the time limit). Compounding with the aforementioned suicidal AI, this leads to the most optimal strategy being one of passivity; allowing the AI to throw itself onto your proverbial sword until it runs out of national will.

This would be fun if tech was unlocked at a slightly faster rate. In both my campaigns, the war was over by 1916 and I had just started getting gas (first run) and tanks (second run). The decision to make progression like this makes sense, at least in terms of replayabilty, but it needs to be slightly sped up. There are vital things in the infantry tree, like: more soldier health, morale, damage and other specializations. All of that, while important, can be considered almost tangential compared to the two most important trees. These being the logistics and trench trees. Supply is king in the game, so it makes getting better supply buildings imperative if you want to win. Due to the way the AI behaves, it also makes getting things like concrete mg emplacements (ai will destroy non concrete with bomber and artillery spam even if detrimental to overall assault) the most valuable tech. Flight is useless, beyond getting the first tech to just have fighters, due to the exorbitant cost of using bombers. Intelligence is useless because the AI is beyond predictable. This wouldn't be such an issue if research came in slightly faster.

Other things that are missing, other people have already mentioned in their reviews, so I won't touch on them heavily. There is a lack of gore that seriously wounds the atmosphere of the game. This is, paired with the rather dull sound design, lets the overall game down. The mg nests should stay between battles, as well as the artillery and any wire. There should also be some form of procedural generation, or at least a better deal of variation between the maps, as they are all quite samey. Overall persistence, such shell cratering from artillery strikes should be enhanced. Corpses, in particular, need to stay. The pocking and scaring of the land over the course of the war, done by the player, would add a great deal to the experience. The unit variation is not a big deal for the allies, as the bonus system is actually quite nice (except for the Canadians). However, the Germans could use slightly more in terms of variation. The trench fighting needs to be reexamined because, as it stands now, whenever a trench is assaulted and you begin moving companies to engage in melee, you end up playing a game of ring-around-the-rosie with the AI. I've seen it stressed elsewhere, but the battle timer needs to be longer as well. Political pressure to attack and win should also be a factor. The missions are weak in this regard. National will is too easy to lose and is much harder to regain.

Despite all this moaning, the game is fun. Doggedly resisting enemy assaults and timing up ones of your own is a good time. Gassing people out of trenches and then mowing them down with your infantry on their fire step is very rewarding. The game really comes into its own when there is a decent amount of tech in the field (around mid game), but by then, the end of the campaign is at hand. With a few changes, this game could really be something special, but as it stands now, it is at best fun, but incomplete, and at worst, is a boring let down from what was promised.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrothers,

The following summarizes my experience of almost 38 hours at Hard (Not the hardest) difficuilty:

- The game takes place during the first world war. Trench warfare is the main theme.
- I had the feeling that i was playing some sort of a mobile game.
- Denuvo implementation is causing performance dips and stuttering.
- Many aspects of the trench warfare are terribly implemented.
- Do not get fooled by the propaganda of ex-xxx studio developers worked for the development of this game.
- Graphics are severely outdated.
- Very few unit choices. Unit models lack any sort of detail. Same model, french is blue. German is grayish, british is brown. Same model is copy-pasted 250 times to create a company.
- You can only purchase tanks, siege artillery and aircraft. Not infantry units.
- The only thing persists from one match to another are trenches and barbwires. You have to buy that mg every single time even it is not destroyed during the battle. Artillery craters disappear.
- Corpses vanish after 5 seconds. No gore, no blood, no limbs flying around. Graphics are prefectly 'Sterile'. Artillery craters are simply non-persistent sprites and do not provide any sort of cover.
- Unit and ability balances are all over the place. A single gas shell shot is 80 supplies (8 standard infantry company cost), airburst 70. What the hell? Siege artillery shot which 'Can' destroy one (Or more if you are lucky) enemy trench costs 200 supplies (20 standard infantry company cost). I mean, why? What were they thinking? That huge monster shell cannot take out enemy artillery positions either. Believe me i have tried. Just call in 20 infantry compaines instead of using that ability. You will wipe out the enemy completely.
- Regrettably the most stupid zergling you can find out there in the stars has at least twice the IQ score when compared with your units.
- Pathfinding issues. Unit models are jumping in and out of the trenches. Units sometimes slow down to snail speed in order to gather together and get wiped out by artillery. Moving a unit from point A to B without losing it is a big headache.
- I am unable to find a way to give multiple orders to a unit or a selection of units like we did on other strategy games by simply queuing them using shift.
- The hardest part of the game is choosing multiple units and giving them orders.
- Your units and enemy units 'Peacefully' pass through the same trench without engaging each other. It is simply dumb.
- Shift + w key combination retreats a company. Make sure to remap all the keys before you start your journey.
- I strongly believe that the AI maphacks. Without scouting, the enemy knows your weakest point and swarms it. Any unit outiside the trench gets artied urgently by the AI, regardless of the fog of war.
- You will be playing the same map over and over again. Each battle is the carbon copy of the previous one.
- If you want to capture a hex, you have to assault it and capture all points multiple times. When each round can take up to twenty minutes, the grind is real.
- AI can artillery your units and positions with pinpoint accuracy 7 days of a week 24 hours of a day without any supply penalties. You cannot. AI artillery hits you like Mohammad Ali, yours just tingle a little bit. AI abuses artillery to the maxium level. Immediate artilleryexpresss service if you have 2 or more units together regardless of FOW.
- You will smash your keyboard when your units jump out of the trench for absolutely no reason and gets shot to bits.
- Units glitch during movement inside the trenches. When an enemy unit glitches, you cannot kill it.
- There is a turn limit for the campaign.
- You cannot unlock the tech tree completely due to the campaign length. Some of the researches are pointless.
- Intelligence branch of the tech tree is utterly useless. You can finish the campaign without a single point of investment to that tree.
- Tanks are a joke. They can get blown easily with regular standard rifle fire. When you use them, you mop the floor with AI units effortlessly.
- Your planes can dogfight with other planes but cannot shoot a barn sized stationary flying baloon. You have to unlock the mission by wasting research points. Very questionable design choices here.
- Bomber planes you call in by using 90 supplies (9 standard infantry company cost) fly above your head a few times and leave the scene without bombing the mg nest, mortar nest or the artillery pit you have ordered to bomb even if you had vision.
- Even the most basic unit has grenades to throw at artillery pits and mg nests but not enemy trenches.
- Supply mechanic is not described properly. You have global supplies and unit supplies. Global supplies do not refill, unit supplies do. However when you start your attack / defend there is no way of telling if you have exhausted unit supplies and started going through the global supplies.

The downsides of the game i have tried to explain above is my experience so far, your mileage may vary. However, as it is, i do not recommend purchasing this game unless you get a discount of 80%. Do not be tricked into buying it by watching the sponsored videos. This game does not deserve that money, believe me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the beginning of a masterpiece of a game if it is taken in the right direction. The game play provides an accurate depiction of the first world war, is easy to learn, and is fun and engaging. The artillery is fun to use and building up your defenses adds a variety to the battles having to worry about supply which often times dictates how you approach your engagements. There are enough troop types to keep it interesting yet simple(new ones are always welcome) and the infantry and tank game play truly is something of another scale. The major flaws which currently restrict the full potential of the game are the length of the campaign and the limited amount of maps. The campaign mechanics are intriguing and make sense historically however the length of the campaign needs to be increased. I loved researching new technologies and watching my trench lines grow however I felt it all ended too soon and I was only able to brush the surface of my tech tree and just begin to break the stalemate of the western front. In my opinion the campaign should be increased in length to one week per turn. This would allow for the full research of the technologies in the tech tree as well as allow for more opportunity to advance on the capitol of your enemy giving a better idea of the scale and length of the great war as well as a more fulfilling experience .
