\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game that does a pretty decent job of capturing the atmosphere of brutal, grinding trench warfare of the first world war.

Unfortunately, that's where the positives stop.

Pathfinding is a disaster, which means your units will jump out of trenches unnecessarily, committing suicide. Also, they will mill about and run back and forth between two close trenches 2 or three times before jumping into the one you want them to assault, giving your enemy free shots the whole time.

The AI is about as capable as your average rock. It does not react appropriately to any of your moves. Its idea of an attack is running at a trench line with a bunch of unsupported companies, and firing a little bit of artillery. Winning defensive battles when outnumbered 6-1 with a K/D of 10:1 or higher is easy, boring, and repetitive.

The game is hilariously easy once you've seen the movement 'quirks'. Winning before trenches build up to begin the true grind, and technology advances to any of the interesting parts (tanks, gas, aircraft, etc), is trivial, even on the highest difficulty.

Do not waste your money, I wish I hadn't.

1 star out of 5.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to give this game a positive review, despite agreeing with many of the reviews which have pointed out the problems in the game. The reason being that most of these issues are quite minor and I feel with more feedback, and perhaps mod support these problems are not game-breaking in the long term.
I played through the Allied campaign in around 16 hours, and like others found issue with having to set up my front multiple times depending on the angle of the enemy's attack, but was helped somewhat by the AI repeatedly attacking the same tiles. Given that the game is set after the Race to the Sea in early 1915, both sides should have rudimentary trench networks in all sectors already.
As others have pointed out, some aspects of the tech tree are pretty useless and tanks are way too powerful. In reality even when tanks were used en masse like at Cambrai, they were slow moving, prone to break down and vulnerable to artillery. Here they are too fast to be hit with artillery, and tear through defences until they are stopped by massed rifle fire (?).
Skirmish mode too needs a bit of work. Selecting a period of the war (Early/Mid/Late) needs to be contextual - ie Early should not allow tanks, flamethrowers, bombers etc as this is anachronistic for that stage of the conflict (changing uniforms for each period would be nice also). This mode also needs a lot more maps, (again opening the game to mod support would be a way to achieve this) and the ability to remove or increase the time limit.

The actual battles are a lot of fun mostly, and the music and setting are great. Overall, I'd say the game is ok-good as is, but has the potential to be great if the devs keep working on it.\t\t\t\t\t
