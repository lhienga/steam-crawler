\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou want glory? You want honor?? Go somewhere else. There is none to be found in mud and trenches of the Western Front.

What sold the game to me was when I fought a battle and had to seriously debate calling artillery down on my own men.

When the Germans attacked what I thought was an out of the way location with 7 corps versus my 4 I knew I was in trouble. Campaign fog of war really bit me there. I had to huddle my men around the main HQ and give up a lot of the map even before the battle started. No MGs and 2 light artillery (next to nothing in supply at setup). When the battle started and I realized I couldn't see very far past my trenches. No big deal, we've got the advantage right?

WRONG.

I watched no less than SIXTEEN, SIXTEEN, infantry units pour out of the fog of war directly on top of my trenches with barely any time to get any shots or artillery off. The three units (two more in a concrete bunker) I had to initially cover that area didn't quite evaporate and they did lose, but the sheer panic and distress I felt was real enough. I very much considered calling artillery down on my own men in the trenches still fighting in melee.

It really hit me then. Was it worth it to order artillery on my own men just to hold the line? Was it worth it to kill my own men so that maybe some of them and definitely their comrades behind them had a better shot at making it? So the enemy wouldn't take and fortify the area and being force me to send even more men into this area to fight.

We just barely held the area around the command post. The next battle will be even more hell. I can't wait.

TL;DR:

This is THE World War 1 game you've been wanting.

The game will be a grind and you will not get everything or everywhere you want. Battles are amazing and brutal and you will absolutely lose more men than you ever thought possible. It's chaos and panic and sheer determination to gain or hold one more trench.

The atmosphere and music are superb.

There's a lot of misconceptions in these reviews. Game is literally called Western Front. Stop complaining they missed a theater of the war.

1.) You will not get through the whole tech tree. You're not supposed to. Focus on what you want.
2.) Artillery is not there to kill units. It's there to suppress enemy trenches and defenses so you can get your men in close.
3.) Battle time limits are there so one side doesn't just sit forever and/or just shell the enemy for 40m. It adds tension to the battle because you have to decide whether to push for a better victory/defense or try and hold your gains with what time you have left.
4.) Persistence is there. It's just the craters that aren't going to be as immediate every time. Everything else is there.
5.) Supplies, supplies, supplies. You will do nothing without supplies.
6.) The AI is good and WILL punish your mistakes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally, really wanted to like this. I've been waiting forever for a decent WW1/RTS/FPS, basically a decent WW1 game - if anyone says Battlefield I'll scream.

Sadly, it's just not all that good if I'm honest. I want to be fair, so I will say the campaign map, the systems within it, and the strategy side feel good, albeit a little 'shallow' but good and logical.

But then, the battles, oh the battles.. they legitimately feel like a mobile game, there's no easier way for me to describe it. Basic animations, units B lining from here to there, ignoring machine guns as they go, and diving head first into trenches only to be obliterated.

When artillery goes off you have the expectation of thunderous claps and devastation. Nah, it's literally a couple of explosions, nothing really happens, with some standard 2D textures to show artillery did go off in that location. It's just so immersion breaking it's crazy. I wanted the freedom to barrage areas before attacking, but even when you get that freedom, it feels so underwhelming it's just not worth it.

Lastly, the speed, fire rate, and general speed of the battles. Everything feels like it's going at 1.25% speed instead of 1%, this is one of the many things that made me feel like it was meant to be a mobile game if I'm honest.

I suppose I was just hoping for some battle realism, which sadly wasn't delivered, at all, in the slightest. But the campaign in itself is quite fun, just sad that you have to enter the battles and sit through all the rubbish within. Not for me, not now, but maybe one day I'll come back and see how it's getting on. In the mean time, I'll be waiting for the next WW1 title.\t\t\t\t\t
