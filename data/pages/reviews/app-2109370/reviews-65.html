\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll preface this review by saying that I do absolutely recommend this game. It's not perfect, it has its problems..but it also has its moments and is something very new and very fun when it hits.

It takes your basic RTS gameplay and spins it along the interesting yet novel paradigm of WW1 style gameplay which is the game's biggest draw. You will build trenches. You will throw waves of men at the enemy, you will watch them get mown down and torn apart..but this is also the game's biggest flaw.

When everyone else is trying to make the next e-sport, The Great War just tried to make the next great game with original mechanics on top of your basic gameplay. And in doing that it largely succeeds - if you're a fan of the RTS, or just trying different games in general, you should give TGW:WF a try.

But remember that flaw I was talking about earlier? Let's go back to that. You'll see cool rolling barrages, actual human floods trying to take trenches, and you'll really enjoy the gameplay that this offers. Then you'll see another cool rolling barrage, another human tsunami trying to take that trench. Then you'll see another rolling barrage, another high-tide of bloodthirsty animatrons moving towards that trench...then you'll see some more arty fire, some more men. Then you'll realize you've fought this battle before, a hundred times before, and that is the moment when you'll realize this game's biggest strength is also its biggest flaw - the somewhat asymmetric gameplay other RTSs offer you isn't exactly here. I say "not exactly" because they do try to break up the otherwise monotony of fighting over the same parcel of land with some interesting mechanics; different types of artillery fire, tanks that provide morale bonuses, units with special abilities, a tech tree that offers you interesting special abilities like exploding a trench from underneath before the battle starts or chemical warfare. But sometimes it just..isn't enough.

Don't get me wrong here - I like TGW:WF, but this game could be improved massively by just a few quality of life fixes that exist in almost every other game out today. Incessant loading times between monotonous battles just makes them that much more monotonous - and those load screens become even more monotonous when you have to constantly click through 'continue' to get past them. I don't need to read a non-worthwhile quote from a random flag officer of the time, I need to get into the battle more quickly because there's going to be a million of the same one in the next hour of gameplay. No hotkeys for unit abilities on the battle map screen, you can't even map them? Why? The dozenth time I have to manually click to set up an arty barrage makes the just this much more appreciable in other titles. This game at it's core was built for multiplayer. The single player campaign becomes a battle of exploits instead of a meaningful and powerful historical exploration. It feels more like a zombie simulator because of how the AI is able to send torrents of reinforcements. It should be more a total war style where you get what you get at the start of the match. Being able to spawn another literally 20,000 more troops mid battle is frustrating and makes your artillery feel pointless. You spend 10 minutes hammering trench lines and get ready to push your assault just to have them filled back in less than 8 seconds later. The artillery is another issue I have. The way it is displayed on screen and how the damage is actually modeled is not accurate. From what I have been able to approximate is the combat visuals are just there for show and are about as accurate as the visuals of a HoI4 units. The real combat is spreadsheets and dice rolls behind the scenes and nothing you physically see is really happening. For example, I shell with 2 heavy artillery and a light artillery on a balloon, I will watch tens of those shells hit that truck that is tethered to the balloon and take no damage but then see a shell hit yards away and then see the life go down. Same is true for shelling trenches. I will watch almost every shell land in the trench on top of the guys and they will take practically no damage. Then sometimes it makes them route instantly. The artillery should be covering a much wider area with less accuracy but each shell destroys what it hits. I have a few more grips but I am sure nobody wants to read this much,

With all the negativity aside, it is still a really fun game to experiment and puzzle around with. Play it like a unity of command with better graphics and real time. you will have a blast. If you are expecting a total war style game and combat, you will be very disappointed. I do enjoy this game for what it is but a lot of people were snake oiled with this games marketing.

Give it a shot, you can always return it if you hate it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think I have ever played a game that has more perfectly encapsulated the futility and frustration of trench warfare. You will send whole armies worth of troops head long to their death to take a single star off a region that has 5, meaning you have to do it all over again 4 more times to actually change the battle lines. Timing arty with inf assaults is absolute essential, and even once you get the timing down expect the first wave to be annihilated if you succeed at all. As the defender, you are pounded into the dirt by constant artillery barrages and seemingly endless waves of enemy forces, limited by your scarce stock of supplies. You must continually feed more men into the trenches to replace those killed in barrages or butchered in close range fighting. Your initially stalwart defenses will be haggled by the end of a battle, battered if not broken, and more often than not you will fight two or three more battles in the same region on the same turn, all the while holding your ground and bleeding the enemy dry.

This is not a matter of breakthrough and maneuver, this is bleeding the enemy white before they can do the same to you.

I Love it.
