\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight up front, The Great War: Western Front is a really, really good strategy game. It's honestly incredibly how well the developers managed to encapsulate the tactical and technological developments of WW1 into a game. It's even more impressive how fun they made it.

Starting a campaign in 1914, both you and the enemy will have infantry, trenches, and artillery, but that's about it. The battle maps will be pristine farmland that you dig your first trenches in. If you're smart and aggressive, you might be able to win some early offensive battles. Then the tech tree kicks in. Suddenly the enemy defences have barbed wire and machine-gun emplacements. Suddenly your own machine gun emplacements are getting destroyed by enemy aircraft, and you haven't researched any counter yet so you have to desperately cling on with only your poor bloody infantry.

1916 rolls around. You've teched into siege artillery and rolling barrages. The pristine battlefields are gone, replaced with crater-covered hellscapes. Your infantry charge into the enemy trenches covered by your massive artillery batteries, and then dig in to defend against counterattack.

The core gameplay loop of the tactical battles is incredibly satisfying once you get the hang of it. You need to get your infantry onto enemy control points without them getting shredded, and the enemy needs to do the same. In order to pull this off, you have to effectively coordinate with your artillery, timing your barrages just right to suppress enemy defences while you get close. On the defence, you need to lay out your trench networks smartly, since the enemy will be doing the same to you. Actual WW1 tactics work well here, like putting lightly defended trenches up front to absorb the enemy's first wave with reinforced trenches behind creating overlapping fields of fire.

The only negative I've run into is that the default difficulty setting is way too easy (I think in response to complaints that it was too hard around release.) I started a second campaign with a higher difficulty and it's been really fun.

Great game, well worth supporting.\t\t\t\t\t
