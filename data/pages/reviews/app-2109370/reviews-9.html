\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's close to being really good, but not quite there. Music is good. Sound is good. It is pretty easy to win by the normal National Will system, in which you get more/better victories than your enemy, so you would probably want to play with the "Endless Campaign" setting on. A fun game for the first few rounds but at the end of the day it's pretty limited and repetitive. There are maybe a dozen different maps and, while I understand the front lines in WW1 did not move much, fighting the same map over and over gets pretty dull. If you are wanting to get in close to witness the big charge, you will be disappointed to learn there is hardly a zoom at all. You can't even rotate the camera to try to see things from a different angle. Path finding is kind of janky and if you accidentally click outside a trench, you get to watch your unit go through the full "over the top '' animation while getting gunned down by the enemy before you can order them back into cover. As for the AI, AI will always pick one edge of the map and start throwing people down that side, so much so that I completely stopped putting any defensive structures in the middle to keep from wasting resources. Several technologies are of questionable value. Mortars don't seem to do much against infantry in trenches and they have slightly more range than a machine gun. Researching tanks or planes will, for some reason, automatically grant the enemies those units as well. A rolling barrage, which is a forward moving wall of artillery shells, somehow does less damage to exposed infantry than a generic suppression barrage. The player can get 8 additional infantry units through tech and a few more through events, but for the most part, what you start with is all you get for the game. Surrounding units on the campaign map does not give you the ability to eliminate them, instead capturing the surrounded region will allow the enemy to redeploy those units on the front-line next turn. Basically, you are better off surrounding areas and ignoring the enemies inside so the enemy cannot use them. The modding community is not super active, so you can't really go looking for game changers there. Finally, the multiplayer only allows skirmish games, so you can't even play a campaign against your friends.

Overall, I found it to be disappointing. It would be fun to come back to here and there for maybe an hour or two at a time, but definitely not a game that will have you hooked. It might improve with future development, but the last game patch was September 18, 2023 and there has been no word from the devs if they intend to continue adding content. If you really want to try this game, only get it on sale, not currently worth the $35 USD they are asking.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun game, has helped to renew my waning enjoyment of strategy games.

Positives
- The game looks very nice. If you zoom in on the models they are a bit funny, but overall the game has a great aesthetic. The artists also clearly showed interest in emphasizing the particular brutality mostly unique to WW1, and it was successful.
- Audio design. The quiet times are quiet and the fights are deafening feeling. I definitively recommend playing with sound and adjusting it to do the setting justice. I found the beginning settings very fitting and just adjusted the master volume to my desired level.
- Satisfying victories. This game will punish you for neglecting the basics. The right balance of infantry, artillery, and information is the difference between 2,000 dead men with nothing to show for it and 1,000 dead men and a mile of battle scarred dirt as reward. If you neglect one, you will be punished for it. Not enough artillery? Good luck even reaching the trench line. Not enough infantry? You may reach the trenches, but good luck in the melee. Not enough information? Wait, why is there a machine gun there? There goes that offensive. The right balance is the difference between 2,000 dead men with nothing to show for it and 1,000 dead men and a mile of battle scarred dirt as reward.

Negatives
- infantry movement can sometimes be awkward. I have found myself getting annoyed at how the infantry moves at times with it wanting to maintain a cohesive formation. It will cause infantry models that are running towards a trench to run back to where they were and then run forward again. This is aggravating in a game like this where timing is vital to success. There is thankfully a solution to this problem. More infantry.
- The campaign seems fairly easy to beat without having to really try. I won't go into detail how, but I was able to defeat the Germans as the Allies in mid 1915 on my first play-through and without playing the tutorial. I will see how harder difficulties are, but I believe this game will be best enjoyed if you actively seek to enjoy it and not just beat it and be done with it. Enjoy the tactical battles and don't use the strategic map to avoid playing the core parts of the game. Similar to the total war franchise in that regard, but the comparison ends there.

Some other reviews have mentioned trenches being single directional in firing. This is incorrect, but there is a design oversight that the area of attack cone displays in the direction the trench was originally oriented towards, not the side that is currently in use.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an excellent take on the 4x/RTS genre for a WWI setting. The only issue is that all of it feels incredibly half baked with zero balance done whatsoever.

It's nice to see a proof of concept that modern warfare can be done in the Total War style of gameplay, but this is not the game to buy for it.

Main gripes:
-Graphics are very...mobile? I feel like I'm watching a high quality mobile game play out.
-The unit balance is strikingly bad, as is the combat mechanics for infantry. Trying to just get units to fight other units feels like I'm trying to solve a dumb series of if/then statements.
-The way artillery is implemented is very interesting, but inconsistent
-Not being able to use infantry to suppress a trench when pushing is strange
-Infinite grenades is uninspired

There's just a lot of stuff that's half baked into a really great foundation\t\t\t\t\t
