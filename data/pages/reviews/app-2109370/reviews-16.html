\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoy Grand Strategy games and RTS, this game is great for spurts of entertainment before it quickly dulls out. That being said, it without a doubt has much future potential. The devs have leaned into this, making mod compatibility a main focus lately.

The campaign is the main focus, singleplayer only atm. I like it a lot, as it does make you have to plan out each turn by deciding where to spread out your units, spend your limited resources, plan offensives / encirclements and pick what research paths you will go down knowing you won't be able to research everything. This aspect of limited resources, research points, units and time scratches the itch for strategy very well. That being said, it leaves much to be desired in further complexity and challenge. I went straight into the game on the 2nd highest difficulty and found myself able to hamstring the AI by continously beating them in battles, which causes a loss in money and supply. These losses in turn make it harder for the AI to win future battles, causing further losses and more loss in money / supply. Positive feedback loops like this should be fixed. The other issue is time; each turn is a month, and the game ends in 1919, so you have like 50 turns total. Each turn I've fought minimum 2 battles, which are 20 minutes long on the short end. This means each turn is an hour if you're lucky. Too long. Battles need to be shortened.

Aside from being too long, battles are also horribly repetitive. Fun, but repetitive. There are really only 4 unit types (infantry, aircraft, artillery, tanks) and you only really use infantry and artillery. Of that, there are different types of infantry but they're barely distinguishable, with speciality varients still being used in the same manner.\t\t\t\t\t

The battles, if you want to win them, are deliberate affairs. Timing artillery barrages to cover advancing troops is crucial, and even a slight screw up will leave hundreds dead upon the field. Then it's a careful advance through enemy lines, keeping your infantry in trenches as much as possible and dropping artillery strikes to suppress enemy troops and MGs.

On the defensive, placing trench networks, MG nests, spotting balloons etc. is satisfying and it plays like a particularly bloody tower defence.

That said, the game has a few issues. Firstly that battles do become repetitive (inevitable when dealing with a war which was the definition of insanity), I recommend treating it as an occasional game. Something to dip into for an hour or two here and there. Any longer at a stretch and it becomes dull.

Second, there are a couple of slightly irritating issues with pathing and ordering units. Sometimes units in a trench, ordered to move to an adjacent trench will decide to climb out of the trench to get there. Frequently they then get mowed down, and it's highly annoying. There's also a tendency for units to stop for a second when given a new order, which similarly tends to get them killed if you're trying to order them back into the damn trench which they just left for no good reason, for example.

Third the AI could be better, especially when it's attacking. I've so far never come even close to losing a defensive battle. Part of the problem is that the AI tends to repeatedly attack the same part of your lines, making it very easy to wait to see which part of the line they're going for and then you just redeploy all your forces there and they will pointlessly attack your massed forces. The other part is that the AI doesn't commit enough troops to the attack. It may get harder as the war goes on and new units get deployed, an obvious counter to this mass defence tactic would be siege artillery but I've not seen the AI deploy it (yet?). Similarly on defence the AI tends to try and work out where you are going to attack, and redeploy a lot of troops there, making it quite easy to feint by massing your troops in one area at the start of the battle then simply redeploying them over the other side.

Overall it's an interesting and different RTS, well worth picking up, particularly on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
