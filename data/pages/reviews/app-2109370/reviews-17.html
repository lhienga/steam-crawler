\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Great War: Western Front is certainly onto something great. The mechanics play beautifully and the RTT/RTS aspect is something that has not been seen or done in a World War One setting. I love the game-play and the ability to be as creative as you want with your tactics on the battle maps.

My only complaint is that I'd like more map availability or diversity and perhaps different types of units to differentiate the Early, Mid, and Late war periods. I would like to see French Cuirassiers in 1914 and a fleet of French Tanks in 1918. I'd also like some more niche infantry model in the unit, I'd love to see 1914 infantry officers leading a charge with a saber or NCO's in the trenches directing men.

Overall it is an excellent game that deserves way more attention than it is getting. The historical fan base of Total War, that has been neglected for a decade, would eat this game up and hopefully turn the 'The Great War: Western Front' into a beloved franchise. It's ability to convey the complexities of combat during this great and horrible conflict was done beautifully.\t\t\t\t\t

Probably the best WW1 game I've played but it still missed a few marks that would have made it very epic.

The cons:

The stars system to conquer new terrain is quite annoying and makes the game very repetative. If this star requirement to capture new areas would be slashed in half, I would perhaps enjoy it.

Killed units disappears after some time, making the game lose the ww1 carnage feeling. Simple fix is to make the killed units within an active battle AND between battles stay put. EDIT: Now there is a mod in the workshop that fixes this!!! :D

I had a hard time creating a relation with certain units. Would be cool if one could follow the development of some battalions, like veteran battalions and such. But now I am being picky :)

The pros:

The tactical aspect of the live battles are amazing. Battling back and forth for trenches can be experienced like in no other game.

Hand to hand combat inside trenches are done fairly good, much better than anticipated.

Artillery works well.

Creating and setting defenses is fun and requires tacical and strategic thinking.

The leveling system to get new technolgies are outstanding.

Conclusion:

I can recommend to buy the game, and get hours of gruesome WW1 slaughter. While the game missed some marks, it is still very entertaining and gives an unique WW1 RTS/Strategy feeling\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood but could have been amazing.
The first few hours in the 1916 campaign of TGW:Western Front are some of the most fun I ever had in any strategy game and it really brought WW1 to live.
Then snipers came (which shoot any enemy on almost the whole the map as long as they are out of the trenches and not in a forest... your units just melting away from enemy you cant see and who should see you), then tanks came (which put any WW2 king tinger to shame...), then I found out bombers destroy tanks (but only when they feel like it) and then I became aware to take Verdun or other fortified places you need to sweep (best form of victory) 4 times.

So what started as a semi realistic RTS with some truly great ideas like the battlefield staying (to a degree) as they were in the last battle and an amazing trench system (although Id say trenches are slightly too good against infantry, human waves are not extremely costly like in WW1 but simply 100% futile) and a good global supply system, turned into a game that incentivizes cheesing the AI even on lower difficulties or just rely on just rushing tanks and use them to break easily through any enemy reliable each time.
Costs are also frankly all over the place and the 30 unit limit is imo at least 1/3 to small for the maps but in the end all these small issues come together to make the game feel unbalanced and too "arcadey".

It is still highly fun to blow up some trenches or build impenetratable defenses for the enemy to rush in futile attemps...\t\t\t\t\t
