\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo with someone who has almost 50 hours on the game, in my experience it is one of the best WW1 rts out there. It is because it is the only WW1 rts I know of. The devs took a risk making this game because it is a topic that is very interesting and bland at the same time for a game. What I mean is the battles where interesting, but the way battles were fought were very repetitive which you will see this a lot in game. So the gameplay is what you would expect in a WW1 rts, from mass usage of artillery, machine guns, trenches, and tanks, planes.


Their is one problem though the bugs. Most bugs have been fixed since release but there is still one major bug that absolutely pisses me off. That would be when you send your companies over the top and click on a opposing trench for them to take, half the company gets stuck in your trench and the other actually charges. This means the soldiers stuck in your tench still have the general icon over them, so the soldiers in that same company that charge get mowed down by the opposing trench that would have been taken if those troops did not get stuck on your trench. It is very hard to explain, but if you have played the game I am sure you have encountered this extremely common bug, and not only is it annoying, but it can change the game from you winning to you losing.


In conclusion the game is still very fun to play, you can even do historical battles from the real great war. Those battles would be the second battle of Ypres in March of 1915 and the Somme-Verdun 1916, and many more. If you wanted to know if America is in game they are, so is the ANZAC's, SEPOY's, and French foreign legions (French African colonies). So wide range of units to choose from, oh and also for Germany you get the Balvairens. I really hope the devs do not abandon this game it has some great potential there just needs to be some minor improvements and maybe some more units for Germany and the game will be great. So I say you should buy it, but for now buy it on sale like I did.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: All in all a decent game. Grab it on sale if you enjoy slow thoughtful combat. Probably not for you if you prefer the reflex/APM style tactical combat like Star Craft. Unit path finding isn't great and definitely the most frustrating aspect of the game. But late game most frustrations are dissipated and you have enough tools that combat becomes interesting and fun

Good but not Great. Definitely captures the slog of a war that WW1 was. Most of my issues stem from the poor path finding that most people are complaining about. Occasionally a unit will get obliterated and I won't understand what happened to them which is frustrating. But ultimately, it's a decent game. Learning to use artillery, researching tanks, and learning to exploit trench blind spots is what takes the game from a real dumb grind to tactically puzzle that you need to work through. The time limit is dumb and I wish you could turn it off, but you've eventually become much more effective in tactical battles that it won't be a factor. Espionage was a bit of let down and wasn't really worth investing research in. The later espionage tech can be useful but it's just too much wasted research points in order to get there. Tanks are the game changer and definitely make combat more interesting.
