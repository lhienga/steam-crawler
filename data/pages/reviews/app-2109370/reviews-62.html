\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing for refund here.

So I can not recommend the game.

You may ask now why? I dont even have 2 hours, so why can I not recommend?

Well, the fundamental mechanics of the game are not working well.

The AI is working on a cheat engine like a 90s RTS AI with some new bonuses.

1. The AI has a major surplus of supplies and it will use the advantage hard against you, attacking AI positions feel like storming against the walls of Verdun. I counted the amount of supplies it used and it was far above my weight class, even though I had the supply dominance (more troops, supply depot)
2. The AI sees everything, the AI will know where your troops are, even if you are out of Line of Sight (no hiding behind the forest for you). It seems like when it had a balloon and looses it, it will keep on seeing everything as before. So useless to eliminate enemy recon capabilities.
3. The AI seems to counter your battle deployments straight in its deployment. Putting a major part of troops on a flank since you plan to use it as concentrated attack? You can be sure that the AI will place 6 machine gun positions right in your way plus enough layered trenches to stop you.
4. Auto Resolve seems to get better results than manual battles. Like the AI could resolve battles in a way that was impossible for me. Again the AI using its quite unlimited perception to attack hidden spots in my lines which should be invisible to it.

Overall horrible UX regarding unit control. The units react very janky.
You can easily miss an icon which will directly lead to annihilation of your troops.
And pls dont expect any modern mechanic of an RTS beyond the year of 2000.
You want to give your troops a moving command with multiple directions?
Too bad for you, they only can run in straight lines into the machine gun. Except u want to micromanage 10 companies in an assault while also keeping the rest of your front alive.
Line of Sight seems to be buggy sometimes too, I had revealed area of the map which showed empty trenches only to be suddenly full of enemy troops as I got closer, which is bit weird when your guys are just 20 m away from it in the first place. Not sure what is going on there sometimes.
Oh and your boys are going to love it to move to another trench not through the trenches but by going over the edge, and they are dead, again...

The promised "persistence" is, well, kinda a lie?
The only persisting thing are trenches and defenses. You could bomb the living ♥♥♥♥ out of the enemy, the ground there will look fine the next time. So the "burned" impressions of the battlefield is a generic modification applied to the central no mans land even if you didnt burn those areas in the battle before.
Troops are quickly vanishing into thin air when they flee, or when they are dead, which is kinda sad that nothing is lasting long...

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is really good actually, all of the tools it needs to be great are there

However

At the moment it seems the AI is blatantly cheating in 2 primary ways
1. The game is based around a supply limit, essentially it's the currency you use to deploy units, call in artillery etc, it's become very clear the the AI has access to far more supply than the player (In battles where I know for a fact I have significantly more supply than them, when I'm running out of resources they are still calling in hordes to counter attack)

I don't mind when a game is hard, but as it stands now it's frustrating that however well I plan or lay out my trenches the enemy will pinpoint strike my machine guns and overwhelm my lines with more infantry than I could hope to deploy.

I really want to like this game, despite the issues above and I'm looking forward to promised patches and changes but right now fighting against seemingly endless hordes of enemies just isn't enjoyable and I'll be taking a break from it until the patches start rolling

I hope someday soon I'll be changing this to a recommend!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished my second campaign as Germany and thought I would give my thoughts!

Pros:
The AI: I am really impressed with the AI! Their tactical skill in combat is actually pretty dang good. They do a great job of supporting infantry attacks with artillery and can overwhelm you if are not careful when they laser in on a point. They usually seem effective on the field map but often make the bizarre choice to leave a territory with one regiment. This throws you some free wins. I actually kind of liked that though because it gave me a chance to do easy offensive battles.

Defense: I really enjoyed the defensive battles in this game! The enemy sends swarms of guys at their target and fully support them. You have to be quick in reinforcing after each wave to not get overwhelmed. It is a blast to watch when a wave of thousands just evaporate against your trenches!

Field map: I enjoyed setting my army up for war! I felt like there is opportunity to outsmart the AI with better management of gold reserves.

The Units: Some units were just fun to use. The elite infantry is great and can be spread around the front. Getting the sniper improvement makes the infantry feel better. MGs are fun when they don't get knocked out right away. Balloon busting with planes is really great too!

Cons:
1. The Research Tree: This is my biggest complaint by far even though it might not bother most people. Probably 70% of the tech has almost no value. Worst example is the intel tree which could be skipped if you want. In both games I finished in 1916 without ever getting to tanks or many of the techs I wanted to try out. Would love a mod that doubled research speed or something like that.

2. Repetitive: This will probably be the hardest part for most people. Fighting every battle will guarantee good results but it can wear you down doing the same fights over. Not sure what could be done to fix it though.

3. Offense: Offensive battles always take considerably more effort and supply than defensive. I struggled with any attack where they had more than 2 regiments. It seemed like I needed overwhelming supply to get a solid force of artillery for support. I think it would be more fun with tanks but I could never get that far in the tech tree. The AI will also counterattack undefended targets during the battle upping what you have to worry about each time.

4. The Costs: Costs vary wildly. Some are just outrageous for minimal benefit. The advanced trenches are egregiously expensive for little value added. The Siege artillery is fun but often too expensive to be worthwhile. Usually the most cost effective defense is pure infantry which is unfortunate. Many other instances where a fun thing isn't worth the cost.


Good game overall!! Room for improvement/balancing but as a whole I really enjoyed it and would recommend it! I hope it gets modding at some point to adjust some things.

Quick tip: If you take Ypres at the start of the campaign as Germany it will lengthen the enemy lines to 16 and reduce yours to 14. This will give you an advantage across the front!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would say this is a solid foundation that can be built on.

Positives
-Huge battles, lots of infantry and ww1 chaos
-Great tech tree, really feels like ww1
-high casualty count, there can be pretty bloody offensives done by either player or AI
-good intro and ending videos
-easy to learn and yet still deep enough to deploy different strategies
-Being able to counter attack when defending against AI can lead to awesome moments
-Morale system is neat
-Events in the wider world are cool to see

Complaints
-World events should have more to them, or interact instead of just a flat 10 morale hit each time or whatever affect they have.
-AI can be relentless with its assaults that don't go far but absolutely push you to your max with supply.
-Bugs where units will be invincible
-interface with trenches can be annoying when you accidentally tell a unit to leave its trench to get shot up by the enemy.
-Was able to auto resolve after getting a 100 morale difference from AI and won around mid 1916, balancing there would be ideal.
-Have more to do with the overall campaign map, assign commanders to different parts of the western front would be neat
-Versus campaign mode, they had it in empire at war, why not here?
-MG nests don't seem to have much value right now, two AI arty can take out an mg nest but takes four of your heavy arty to do the same

Overall, I think this is a good foundation to build on, all my complaints can go away with simple tweaks or added futures down the road. If you want a fun more easy to get into ww1 game, this would be it. If you're looking for deep paradox level of campaign gameplay, look else where.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR:
If you want a realistic(~ish) WW1 Grand strategy RTS you find it. It's not perfect but this is the closest you can have to a real WW1 ERA Grand strategy RTS.

Cons:

-As Persistence is the main selling point, and while it does exist, there is one persistence which isn't implemented. If you capture 1 or all of the capture points or "Sweep" the enemy (Sweep is when you fully cap all points, capture/destroy the enemies HQ, essentially having full map control) and win. the next battle you play in that battle field, for example Verdun-Tionville, you expect that what you captured in that precious battle wold retain to your side, but no it does not. The game essentially resets all progress done from the previous battle. For a War where gains in inches are praised, the game does not respect any gains in meters you did. BUT this can be explained due to balance since a sweep mean the enemy left the region giving it to you.

- MG is basically a GAU-8, while Machine guns are deadlier than any of us (non-military) can think of. MGs here are just unrealistic, they can kill 500+ soldiers in 10 seconds like damn *that's a lot of damage."

- Arty is a potato cannon, while the airburst (against trenches) and Gas shells do a lot of damage, HE against soldiers in open field does not. Considering that 60~% of casualties was made by artillery it's a bit unrealistic. Have HE do the exact same damage to open field soldier as Airburst to entrenched soldiers. Again can be explained by balance, you can't just bomb your way to victory.

- Capturing enemy trench. While battle lines/trenches are made so that the front trench can't fire behind them, so that when it is captured the enemy can't fire at your trenches, it's funny to think that your soldiers are just waiting for the enemy to charge them and not fire at them just because the trench is facing the opposite direction.

- Bad movement in trenches, moving in your trenches is fine. The problem is when ordering your soldiers to attack an attached neighboring trench. The soldier would climb up the steps above ground and start moving towards the other trench. Problem here is that they are exposing themselves to rifle fire, machine gun fire, and arty fire. essentially killing them (please fix this T_T)

- Cheating AI, I'm fine with them cheating in order to avoid a curb stomp. But what I hate is the "iseedeadpeople" cheat from WoW3 (RIP). So I just captured the eastern cap point first, then the western cap. I have this genius Strategy to attack from the Eastern cap point, while the western soldiers would probe the enemy HQ. But no nevermind, go ahead bombard the 10 companies I spawned to the east, and ohh what's that? there's a wave of soldiers defending their eastern flank now? ignore it my friend just a coincidence. And before you say it no balloons exist. And the eastern cap is far enough from the HQ that they should not see each other. And I can see there movement due to my probing attack from the western flank. And it was during a blizzard storm (idk if it does reduce vision but it should).

Pros:

- Death are staggering, a battle can have 30-40k losses on both side per battle, especially in Verdun-Thionville, and towns near Calais, and the battle results in a stalemate. Very costly but it happens.

-Very realistic, (outside or MGs and Artys) Morale and number of soldiers are well done. And using the undermine and watch the enemy fly is satisfying.

-Persistence of trenches and barbwire is good, like really good. I was testing the campaign in the southern end of the battlefield and made a halfhearted trench (basically a straight line) with a matching barbwire. I eventually lost since I only had 1 corp vs their 3. Two years later the Germans attack that town, and I was like "oh this trenches are so bad lol" you can see your mistakes and apply the things you have learned the past 2 years.

- Winning/losing mechanic, I just love it. Having 20k+ deaths and the result is a stalemate with a negative Will reward. Contrast it to 5k deaths and with 12k kills which results in a major victory and pushing the enemy out, it's beyond words. And this is the first time I saw victory implemented like this.

The So-so:
- Story, aside from tutorial there's nothing else. No trench conversations, soldier daily life etc. I mean it's fine look at Total war.

- Grand strategy implementation, there's just something missing, and IDK what it is. Maybe the map is small? Idk really. the Game is only the western front so no false advertising here.

- Scale of time, 1 turn is 1 month. skipping turns for gathering supplies can easily stack and surprise it's 1918.

- Kinda dumb AI, the two moments were I earn a sweep was with Verdun vs a 2 star town. Once I blitz one of their cap, the enemy basically YOLOed that cap for 12 mins straight. Luckily for me I had 6 cannon screaming, and when it was close to falling I just spawned more soldier from that point. After the last 5 mins the enemy is basically fully spent, and I just walked in the other cap (had to destroy a few MGs) and waltz in there HQ.

- Raider/grenadiers/shocktroopers/stormtroopers their number is unrealistic. how can 2 companies of about 50 soldiers total can decimate 2 regular or elite companies totaling to 450 soldiers in the enemy HQ, it's too unrealistic. increase their number to at least 75~100 but make them as squishy as the original 25.

Conclusion
The game is not as realistic as it want's it to be, and some persistence is lacking, and there are few problems with the game. But if you want a realistic WW1 era RTS you found it. While the bad can be explained with multiplayer balancing, the good alone can make this game the best WW1 era RTS with grand strategy. if you want a grand strategy game get this for 60~% sale. If you want an RTS game get this at 25% sale, if you want a WW1 Era RTS Grand Strategy you found it get the deluxe edition like me. JK! just get the base game, and if you wan't to support the devs buy the deluxe edition, the deluxe editions only has a few JPGs for wallpaper, a PDF manual, and the soundtrack. Completely optional.

TIPS:

Cannon positioning based on cards, the squad cards are placed according to their position. i.e. eastern most soldier/arty will be displayed at the 1 position of the card, and the next unit to the right would be the second card etc. So your arty at the left most card is your left most arty. Kinda given but I just noticed this 5 battles in, while scrambling which light arty can reach the enemy MG nest.

Light arty to suppress and destroy emplacement, Heavy arty for killing as well as suppressing the soldiers.

Don't worry about losses just keep spamming soldiers, ESPECIALLY if you know you can win. e.g. you have 9 corps vs their 4 corps and you have 6 arty vs their 2.

Keep you armies in a region to a minimum. more companies = more supply usage, so just add more soldier to location where you want a to do an offensive or is a major location like Verdun or areas near Calais, and keep at least 2-3 infantry corps to low priority regions for defending, they are enough at least based on my experience. 1 arty and air company is good to have but not necessary. Just upgrade their supply depot for more MGs and place a few MGs to key areas and DO NOT spam it at the front. Just defend the cap points and HQ, priorities which one is more exposed and especially the sides of the map. AI likes hugging the sides of the map, though do not rely on this info. the AI can attack through the middle sometimes.\t\t\t\t\t
