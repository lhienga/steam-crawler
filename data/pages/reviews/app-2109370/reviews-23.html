\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finished a campaign on each side of the war.

First of all I think the game has a solid foundation. The changes to the battlefield, the corps system with the (almost) unlimited manpower and the combined arms are all great aspects to have for a fun experience of the First World War.

Attacking and defending are both interesting and provide the player with some challenges. The more technologies you unlock, the more options you have to overwhelm the enemies defences or anihilate everything your opponent may throw at you. The fights inside the trench networks is one of the best aspect of the game in my opinion.

The campaign map can be a bit boring however, even by the standards of the western front during ww1. There is just not much to do apart from moving around your forces. The Ai will do the same and you can pass 3 years without anything happening. I think it is a mistake by the studio to not include some form of penality for the player or the Ai for not attacking. In real life, there was an enormous pressure on both armies for conducting offensives even if they were doomed to fail. Yet I feel 0 urgency to attack. I bet I could play the entire game without ever attacking or being attack just by moving my units around. The magic number is 4 army corps and the Ai will not attack. There is no veterency system or chain of command which I think are pretty much staples in theses kind of videogames. The campaign missions are not very interesting and most often than not, I complete them by accident. Making them harder or having better rewards would make them more interesting.

For the battles, the unit variety is limited and most of the elite soldiers do not have enough the deferentiate them in my opinion. Some are bad in melee and some are good. That's it. By the end of the war there were portable machine guns. Maybe having a type of unit with them could be great. One thing that is very frustrating is doing a misclick with an infantry unit when you try to make them advance in the trench network. It takes some time for them to go back inside even you you immediatly cancel the order. They immediatly die to ennemy while they stumble in and out the trench.

The planes, gas and tanks are nice options but honnestly you do not need them to win. The Ai can't resist a normal attack with artillery and infantry. I think supplies are a bit too strong in this regard. Having a different ressource such as amunition could make this kind of playstyle less powerfull.

For the bugs I did not find anything gamebreaking.

I think the game is not that good at the moment of this review but I think it is gonna be really fun in a few patches (or dlc maybe?). 7 out of 10. Maybe 8 out of 10 later.

Game play wise its difficult and harsh yet very very tactical, you can send company after company to attack a point and have them get destroyed by artillery, machine gun or enemy infantry in the trenches its brutal combat shapes how you play by making you think about using the weakest of some very strong defenses and weighing the options of using your artillery and air forces in cohesion with your ground forces the national will system also plays a large factor into making you pick and choose large offensives onto certain territories and whether or not the people back home will be able to handle the war any longer.

Overall i love this game it helps me kill time while im bored i greatly suggest.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith this game i recognize the need for a "Meh" button as a thumbs up is far too strong, and so is a thumbs down. There is a lesson here, something about indie games making war games. At its heart this is a decent game, and does an interesting job modeling WWI. It is however, simple. simple to the point of feeling like it could be a web browser game. Its an interesting contrast with Grand Tactician Civil War. The game is very similar in appearance on the tactical battle field, simple sprites and all that. A RTS game hoping to model the conditions of a complex and horrid war. Unlike Great war, GTCW is almost too complex, and unless you wanna put on your war hat and beat down the slavers over a week straight, causal play feels impossible. Great war is much more of a simple model and doesn't ask too much from the player, in fact, almost too little is asked.

So what i am saying is don't expect a great game, nor an epic war simulator for the Great War, but, like the waves upon waves of men you will send to their death, a solid time killer. Easy to jump into and jump out off. If that's what ya want, buy it, if not, do not.\t\t\t\t\t
