\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't make reviews often but this game is worthy of a fantastic review, this games existence has essentially been needed on the RTS spectrum for a LONG TIME, considering the fact that in the past users that wanted this experience would usually go about it through mods for instance the Total War Napoleon Great War Mod, WW1 Mods for military sandbox games, and so forth. This games tone as well as design feels a bit rustic as well as worn out demonstrating the literal bloody horrors that was WW1 perfectly on top of all of that you, the general sitting on his/her ass drinking tea/eating a cake or pastry while you just casually send thousand's of young men to their deaths. Essentially I'm just going to list some of the things possible with this game:

1. You can build trenches & utilize wartime equipment

Not a lot of games surrounding WW1 command often focus on the logistics or engineering that went behind WW1 especially with the technological feats major nations had to overcome during the period that WW1 took place from 1914 --> 1918 but this game essentially flips it around and says "Alright general construct trenches and pray to god the enemy can't do jack about it." and I don't mean that lightly either. Before multiplayer matches begin or other battles begin in-game you can construct elaborate trench systems as well as defenses before a battle begins allowing you to maliciously construct death zones for the enemy to walk into which is satisfying as hell when its properly executed, this includes actually referring to WW1 tactics and how trenches were actually set up with communication lines as well as main trenches.

If trenches are not your style then you can use....I don't know...use every known artillery piece in the game and shell shock the hell out of your enemy? I mean seriously this game is a love letter to artillery and I love it. Artillery is greatly utilized and it even sticks with historical accuracy for instance rolling barrages, smoke screens, and gas attacks are just some of the things you can use artillery for. Someone who knows how to use artillery can definitely cause hell if they know how to use it. On top of that you got accurate support structures such as WW1 military balloons used for scouting, underground mine explosions which shoots a massive explosion in the air, flak/anti-aircraft trucks, small suppression buildings, machine guns for example, that are both reinforced and not reinforced, barbed as well as razor wire slowing the enemies advanced and don't get me started with the amount of craters you can create to make it look like a true no mans land, which brings me to the second point.

2. Battalion Size Charges and statistics

I am glad that the game does its best to truly demonstrate the scale at which men that you were sending into battle were dying, this game will let you know how many men are dying under your command and you get to see the amount you've lost by the end of the battles you play. For instance in a multiplayer match I lost 9,873 Infantry which demonstrates the sheer brutality that happened during that battle. The same goes for statistics regarding tank as well as air unit losses.

3. Story and Art Design

This game has a great narrator that starts the game off with introducing how WW1 happened in the first place with visually satisfying art styles, at least through cut scenes/film sections, and you won't be out of luck when it comes to a bit of care in making sure designs were constant with the rustic feel. The design is a bit old which many players have noted but it does give the game an interesting vibe that forces players to think to the past in my opinion.

Overall great game, I'd send thousands of troops to take trenches with heavily fortified machine gun nests again, I'd shell the hell out of the enemy, and just 10/10 well done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately I cannot recommend this. The premise is good, the upgrades look interesting, but in the end the gameplay choices made by the developers turn this into a grind. Just the attacking is a grind, as units cannot shoot out of trench systems and can be easily surpressed by a bit of artillery, turning the game into a melee contest. The winner here is usually the attacker (or whoever has more supplies). Sure the upgrades you can get are nice, especially tanks, but... you can easily finish off the AI as both sides less than 2 years into the war (earliest i got was late 1915), so barely more than a year of war.

The persistiance is incorrect as well, you cannot keep any emplacements you build or artillery... it just vanishes between battles, even though this would make the game way more interesting as investing into these and keeping them alive could be more crucial for the same fight. I also am not a fan of the "star system" with conguering regions. It makes more sense to avoid regions like that and not defend them as much at the same time, just because it takes longer to take over.

As soon as you figure out how to beat the AI, you will do it with a 100% guarantee, as long as you have the supplies. The systems are so interesting, but in the end it is so unfortunate, how easy the game is to beat.

I really hope this game gets more updates or at least some modders, because otherwise, I do not see it being more than a cool scenario game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\twhile i do understand some of the gripes im seeing in the negative section, i can 100% say those people are over-conflating their gripes.

the game is great. it captures the monotony and horror of sending thousands of men to their deaths just to gain a small fraction of land. artillery is devastating, gas attacks are obviously a thing. theres a pretty expansive tech tree that branches out in 4 or 5 different directions and ALL the tech is useful in some way. wether you want to invest in better trenches, logistics, air superiority, or infantry, its up to you how you play the war out. you start off in early war with no tanks, planes, or gas, and you research those things over the course of the war which adds alot of variety to how battles play out.

the only issue i have is related to annoying trench mechanics that honestly seem pretty poorly thought out. what do i mean by this? well, in this game you can have up to two infantry units per trench. the issues arise when it comes to transferring men throughout your trenches. if your trenches are full, you cannot tell another unit outside of the trench system to just USE the trenches to navigate instead of waltzing out in the open and getting immediately gunned down by entrenched enemies. if trenches are 'full' you should still be able to send units through them for navigational purposes, i dont understand how thats not a thing considering how integral it is to not losing 5 companies of men in the blink of an eye. charging enemy trenches feels wack at times too, because ideally you'd have all your infantry pour into the front trenches during an assault, but no sorry. two units per trench, everyone else has to sit outside the trenches like idiots while getting mowed down. this feels really poorly thought out and needs tweaking ASAP, as moving through trenches is a pretty integral part of any WW1 game.

overall i dont think the game deserves all the negative press, it just has some annoying bugs and poorly implemented mechanics that the developers have already stated they're aware of and will work on in the next patch. if you like WW1 games, you'll like this for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a YES, BUT set your expectations because it's easy to misinterpret what type of game this is. It's putting you in the role of a commander or general who issues orders to sections of his army. It feels like you're directing troop movements on a map. You cannot micromanage your troops. You cannot rotate an artillery emplacement and you cannot change your infantry's stance. You can only order them to Move or Attack and the events unfold using background dice rolls. You're observing the result of your orders as if they were being fought in a real battle. Realism of unknowable events that may occur during an actual battle be damned because it manages to make the game feel shallow and limiting. I don't enjoy issuing an order and crossing my fingers the enemy's health bar goes down faster than my health bar based on invisible background rolls. It would have been great if it allowed us to get into the nitty gritty, control individual units or squads, order them to take cover behind buildings and fences, issue different move levels, change their stances, and how they should attack a trench. Yet, I enjoyed it.

The terrain is persistent, BUT any progress you make such as capturing the enemy trenches and control points does not carry on to the next battle. Similarly, even though your trenches and barbed wire will remain there, you have to re-purchase and set all of your emplacements again (artillery, MGs, and spotting balloons). Every single time. You will have to re-fight the same battle and recapture what you already captured last time for some reason even though you sweeped the entire map, you will still start on your side the next turn on the same front. This is what makes campaign mode repetitive and boring.

Then most glaring problem that I agree with other reviewers on is that the enemy AI is omniscient both in a battle and on the campaign map. You build up an entire nest of fortifcations, trenches, and machinegun cover, but if you leave just one gap, the AI will run a full horde through it without any reconnaissance, probing attacks, or warning. They just go for it right off the bat without bothering to send a single attack on the rest of your line. Similarly, on the campaign map, you have a 30 unit frontline with a few gaps in the line that you don't have enough units to fill because this game does not allow you to directly recruit new units. You can only get new units by unlocking a few technologies granting you new units or through events that happen by historical year or milestone. The frustrating part is that the enemy AI manages to find and move through those gaps unconstested behind my lines without mistake and without reconnaissance that I have as a player. It's not attacking any of my occupied tiles. It's cheating. You can't attack the enemy and take their tile in one turn to cut them off, it will require multiple turns and battles.

For potential buyers: If you like trench/defence-based games, enjoy setting up fortifications and trenches, find enjoyment in a battle even if you lose by treating it as a story, and are thinking of buying this game because it looks appealing based on what you see in the Store Page screenshots, then yes, do buy it and try out Skirmish mode for a quick preview. The gameplay is intuitive and basic enough that you can get right into it without obscure orders/UI buttons. If you just like WW1, think of it as a WW1 themed game, it's not going to get as grand or deep as a 4X or as simulated battles as in Graiteam's games. It has a straightforward "world campaign map" as a background to the painfully simplified combat system that still hits all the WW1 spots noticeably above average. It's less gritty than "World War 1" (2005).

For the devs: It's awesome that you added a Skirmish mode, it's exactly what I've been waiting for, but you need to add a Save feature. I love the focus on fortifications and defence during deployment. But, though I enjoy spending time setting up a perfect defence, I want to be able to Save my progress. Currently there is no Save feature in a Skirmish battle. It's not fun having to keep doing it from scratch every time. It take a lot of time setting one up.

Also, I couldn't find the Hide HUD button. The game looks great without all the high saturation unit icons ruining the vibe of sickly grim death unfolding on the peppered earth or the dogfights in the sky.

I was secretly hoping that the unit damage mechanic was similar to that of the Call to Arms and Men of War series, but it's closer to Company of Heroes where infantry cannot be killed by physics-enabled shrapnel and explosions, they instead lose health until they are destroyed.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed about 50 hours offline and would recommend except it reliably crashes my PC about every 2-4 hours. No other software I have does this. no warning, no dumps, just a messy reboot of the PC. Does this on both my PC and laptop.

Otherwise a great game. 7/10 stars.
Moderate replay value as there are too little variety in battle maps and no variety in the initial campaign setup. If the hardware problems were sorted I'd see myself having sessions of playing this game once or twice a year. More than that then the limited variety in the game play would become boring.
Balance needs refining and Tanks are way OP as they never breakdown or are unavailable due to maintenance issues as was historically the case.
Terrain is just for show, there is no advantage in placing units on high ground and no dead ground to exploit. Buildings don't affect LoS let alone fire. Mud and river beds don't even slow or tire troops. You may as well be playing on a giant football field.
Forests are the only terrain with any affect, you can see through them but have limited sight into them and they provide light cover for troops and are impassable by tanks.

However the game does give some of the feel of trench warfare and you do need to plan your assaults and marshal your resources. The challenges are mostly do-able and rewarding. Game play is fair to good.

Remember that this is a GAME and not a simulation! Historical accuracy is low as you would expect from a game with reasonable game play.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe core idea of the game is really solid and manages to convey the feeling of WW1 using play as much as narrative.
Ground is paid for in blood and even small mistakes can see hundreds of men scythed down in seconds. For for the first little while it's a unique experience learning lessons the hard way and watching the carnage wrought by your errors and successes

Unfortunately the cracks begin to show all too quickly
-UI design is pretty bare bones, it's hard to even know which unit you have selected or how to utilise specialist units
-Combat is overly simplistic, basic infantry is just mashing health bars together
-Enemy AI fails to account for even the most basic tactics and will sit there eating shells to the face until you're good and ready to attack
-The level of detail is underwhelming, after half a dozen battles Verdun still looked almost pristine aside from the slowly growing collection of trenches

In conclusion, it's a really interesting concept and some excellent ideas unfortunately let down by overly simplistic and opaque execution\t\t\t\t\t
