\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game... and I did. Here's how:

First, I opened an encyclopedia to WW1 and after seeing picture of trench warfare combat, realized that the game mechanics generally match the tedium of trench warfare, which is not maneuver warfare.

Second, I realized that the AI bombing MG positions and attacking with numerical superiority is probably just common sense. Using some heavy duty deductive reasoning, I determined that having a 3:1 ratio of enemy to friendly might mean that my units were in jeopardy. Thankfully, that very same deduction led me to the conclusion that I should not allow the enemy to overwhelm me before it was too late. As a result, I experienced virtually no AI cheating which has been described and found the game to be fairly easy.

Lastly, I looked deep down inside and asked myself if I wanted a game which might present a challenge, and furthermore, might cause me to think a little bit, maybe slightly. The answer was, "yes". I decided that if I wanted to play Angry Birds and shoot birds at pigs slightly differently for hours, then I'd probably just do that and embrace the casual gamer lifestyle

Friends, I argue for the game because I find the negative reviews to be revealing of a lack of intelligence, commitment, or misunderstanding of the game's representation of history. Frankly, it's embarrassing to read complaints that the AI is too difficult because it can see the player or suppresses the player. You can literally counter-attack to destroy the enemy's ability to see you 99% of the time. Expecting to hold a region against 8 corps with 1 corps is probably unreasonable as well. Reviews which cite an inability to defend against massive waves should come with an admission of inability to think strategically.

My only complaints are that the enemy does not strategically react to my moves to encircle them. I ended a campaign in 8 turns by encircling about 25 German corps in France, and at that point, they could no longer attack due to a game mechanic which prevents "islands" from attacking. Other than this, the game is great, and I wish they would revert back to the more difficult AI settings which existed previously or at least make it an option. 20% of the player base is not only dumb, but outspoken and is ruining the game because they can't seem to figure out that the AI will take action to prevent them from winning, and they should analyze, adapt, and overcome.\t\t\t\t\t
