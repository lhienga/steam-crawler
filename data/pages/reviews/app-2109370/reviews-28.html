\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter close to 50 hours of game play I'd say The Great War: Western Front is... fine.
Its not as exciting as I had originally thought it would be. I played before the updates and afterwards.
It was welcoming to see the updates added and I enjoyed the game for what I got out of it.
At almost $0.75 per hour of entertainment I think I got most of my worth, but the replay-ability dips after a couple games once you get a handle on everything.

I really like the style of the game with its classic RTS twist along with the tabletop view campaign. But it doesn't quite satisfy my need for replication or simulation of the sheer chaos that truly occurred in WW1. I'm talking about deaths en' mass. But it seems that you're limited in the RTS battles as you're limited with the amount of units that can be deployed.
Most of my campaign involved pincher moves where you attack on the flanks avoiding MG nest or any other defenses from the enemy trenches. You then push on the flanks while artillery hits the trenches and defensive positions in the rear. Once you've created your gap you then flush with more troops. Typically this results in low casualty. While that's sound logic for the game it doesn't quite fit in with the historical battles. Aside from the historical inaccuracy, I just don't see any logic in allowing for flanking so easily when no mans land should be a zone of horror and vulnerability. Instead its where you can feel safe without worry of much carnage. Unless, of course, your enemy snipes with their artillery. I dont have any issues with the enemy having endless supplies of artillery rounds. They typically last during the first couple minutes of battle but afterwards I don't see anymore continued use.
Yet, when looking at the artillery, the positions typically in the rear can be reached quickly when the infantry rushes and flanks enemy trenches. Once in the rear you can destroy their artillery without the worries of death from above for the rest of the battle.
Bombers seem useless as they will circle around where you selected (within view) and MIGHT drop bombs on the enemy's defensive positions or they may just circle around until they run out of fuel and return without any effect. Its cool to see the dogfights but again not really exciting. Just two biplanes circling each other until one is overwhelmed by numbers or is simply out-maneuvered.

So again I think the game is... fine.

Overall I believe this game does WW1 very well. It is grueling, static warfare, and it's status is out of your control other than sending your men to die in a futile attempt to end the war as soon as possible. You are going to use boundless resources and boundless soldiers in this attempt.

I hope "The Great War" becomes a series. There is much to unpack with WW1, the western front is just the start.

Things I would look for in future games/small changes to make to this game:
-remove technology, release it via event or through some arbitrary system outside of the players control on a historical timeline
-more army related systems, such as a more in-depth morale system. Maybe a commander with speech abilities or force rotation mechanics that punish you if you don't rotate your men from the front line.
-More divisions, bigger divisions, no in map unit caps - In the current game I would make artillery and observations be their own caps, with the primary 30 caps only going to infantry and tanks. Base artillery and observations on resources or just don't cap them at all. Maybe with the morale system force the player to decide and make it punishing.
-Resource price adjustments. I can't see myself bombarding the lines of my enemy for a week straight like the somme in 1916. Less starving more death and destruction. In the current game I would make trenches much cheaper at a minimum.
-Static trench network attachments - MGs/Mortars\t\t\t\t\t
