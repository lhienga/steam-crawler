\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the game but it isn't without it's flaws:

The pros:

1. The setting. First off, we rarely get WW1 games, much less strategy games like this.
2. I think it gets a lot about the setting right. Especially early on in the war, attacks against trench-lines are brutal affairs of attrition. Even with technology that comes later, they can still cost a lot of lives on both sides. For example, when I first got tear gas shells, I dropped them onto some trenches further back on the enemy's line from the front after I had managed to secure a foothold. It forced the enemy infantry right out into the open where they were just mowed down.
3. I like the sound. The music is pretty good, the weapons sound suitably crisp, and so on.
4. It looks pretty good. Not amazing. But the trench-lines, especially, I think look good. Massed infantry attacks across no man's land also looks suitably scary, both for the defender and attacker.
5. The strategic layer has some neat ideas, some of which I think work pretty well and appropriately for the period. For instance, your attacks are constrained both by your money (to replace dead soldiers) and supply (to actually get living soldiers into the field, artillery shells, and so on). You have to spend both of these on both attack and defense. So hasty attacks will soon see you out of both which gets really bad when the computer can turn around and attack you next turn.

6. I'm playing on Linux so I get that weird pixel cache error whenever a loading screen pops up. It's not a big deal and the game runs fine beyond that so far.

The cons:

1. The unit pathfinding. It is VERY important for infantry units to avoid getting fired on in this game as they will all die very quickly. So, it is quite annoying when my infantry decide to hop out of the trench and charge across open-ground when there is a perfectly good pathway through communication trenches to where I want them to go since that inevitably results in most of them dying. On that note, communication trenches function strangely when units from opposing sides pass through them. They seem to get... both stuck there, until one will just magically pop out into the open. It seems like something should happen when two enemies meet in a communication trench.

2. Unit LOS is affected by terrain such as hills. This isn't really laid out at all in the tutorial and from the camera perspective, it is hard to tell. Even moreso, there doesn't seem to be an easy way to determine this when you are laying out your trenches, so it is pretty easy to have this massive blind-spot in them.

3. The map persistence advertised is there, but it is... strange. It makes sense, I guess, when attacking the same square on the strategic map that there could be multiple tactical maps. Is it picked just one after the other for consecutive attacks? I am not sure. Beyond that, I think that the actual maps themselves should get more torn up as time goes on. There's grass in an area I've been fighting over for months that just regrows. INfantry corpses fade away pretty quickly which I get for a performance reason, but a large part of the horror of WW1 was how awful no man's land was.

4. I don't know if I recall in the tutorial that grenadiers could take out tanks super quickly. That was a fun surprise. On that note, I don't find them all too useful when I can just get two infantry companies for the same price. At best, they seem good on defense for the aforementioned AT units. At least some of them should, I think, be relatively hidden from enemy LOS. I'm not a WW1 expert but from what I recall, German stormtroopers, especially, were good at getting up close to the enemy trench-lines to launch surprise attacks. Here, they are seen from a mile away.

4. I don't think the battle fatigue mechanic is penalizing enough. A few corps that have thrown themselves against my defensive lines 3-4 times in a turn with horrific losses should be combat ineffective afterwards, I think. Things die fast enough that I do not know, really, if morale penalties are that big a deal.

5. There are some impervious machine gun bunkers on the Verdun map (At least I didn't have anything at the time that could bother them despite taking all the trenches around them.) It would be good if there was a warning about that.

So, I think it was worth the money, especially if you like WW1 stuff at all. It's got some neat ideas and I think after some patches it will be a very good game but right now there are some problems with it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA unique twist on the implementation of trench warfare during WWI. Most of us are familiar with the strategic vs. battle map system that Total War brought us. Same thing here, but you need to remove the concept of taking territory here. This is a game of attrition with some fun stuff along the way. The tech tree is pretty rewarding, it brings a smile to my face to coat the battlefield in chlorine gas :) The long lasting effects of war on some of these territories that see multiple battles is pretty cool. All your defenses from prior battles are still there. That huge crater you left, still there. Miles of barbed wire, yep, still there. I like that. Any fan of TW series or CoH will enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
