\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished my first campaign as the Entente/Allies and I really enjoyed it. Battles are bloody, gaining a foothold in an enemy trench and pushing into their cap zones while you shell their reinforcements was very satisfying, as was mowing down hordes of enemies as they tried to take your own. Being able to create and upgrade your trench networks across several battles was a cool mechanic, you start out not having much, only able to place a few simple trenches, but by the end of the war, my most attacked areas had incredibly complex webs of defenses that let my troops move from the back to the front without ever exposing themselves, seeing the battlefield get progressively torn to shreds was great as well.

There are some issues:
- Some people seem to think the AI cheats in regards to the Fog of War, I can't verify this, usually, when they attack one of my weak points or drop artillery on my advances they had an observation balloon up somewhere, so to me it seemed legit.

-The campaign map is just Ok, nothing special, you move units around, they can move as far as you want per turn (but can't participate in battle until the next), buy some upgrades to increase local supply, morale, etc., and conduct espionage on the enemy. It's very simple but functional.

-Likewise, the economy tied into the campaign map is super basic. Just money and supplies. You use the money to buy things like tanks, planes, siege artillery, province upgrades, or more supply which is used to go on the offensive. Other than researching tech there aren't really any ways to interact with gaining more money, and the events that let you choose between more funds, research points, or supply are few and far between (but there are at least one or two missions you can complete going on at any given time to increase this).

-As mentioned above, events where you have to make decisions on what resources to gain and lose, were uncommon. I think across the whole first campaign I got less than a dozen. Also, my national will (basically how close you are to losing the game) got very high very fast as a result of numerous victories I always picked events that got me money at the expense of like -10 national will. I think more variety in these decisions, and more frequency would be interesting, letting you choose between money, will, supply, tech, and maybe even free divisions or province upgrades or something of that nature.

-Auto resolve can be very effective, and that helps towards the end when I just clicked my last few battles to deplete the German's national will into victory. Battles can be tedious towards the end, but throughout the early-mid game there is a pretty steady supply of new tech and units to keep them fresh, from fighters and bombers to tanks, and eventually flamethrowers and such. I think adding more late-game unit variety (if possible) could help, like units that are somewhat ahistorical, but very powerful (maybe rifleman that has some soldiers equipped with squad automatic weapons and such)

-I also think on that note the game could be more engaging if the infantry was a bit more capable than they were irl. Being able to fire potshots into trenches as they advanced, or take cover across No Man's Land while they crawled forward, possibly being stuck in between trenches unable to go forwards or retreat as they get pinned. On that note I also found special units like grenadiers and flamethrowers to be a bit finicky regarding when they chose to use their special weapons, vs. just jumping into a trench to fight it out in melee.

-I noticed places like Verdun were rarely (not once in my game) attacked. I think it would be cool if every once in a while both you and the opponent got special objectives to at least attack historic areas for extra supply/money/moral/etc., so that way you can experience some of those great battles outside of the "Historic Battles" game mode and in your own campaign.


--Very, very, very minor pet peeve, but as someone who has to do a lot of historical research and write papers about them, the excessive use of the passive voice and switching between past and present tense during the cutscenes kinda got under my skin lmfao. Just be warned you might get PTSD from a paper you turned in to your professor in college at some point.

I know that's a lot of "issues" but they don't really take away from the game as it is. It's an interesting strategy game, not overly complex to learn and easy to get into, but when you learn how to combine your units and weigh your wants against your needs and expenses during battle it can be very interesting. I had several battles where I reached zero supply and won the day with just a handful of units remaining, which was very rewarding.

Overall, for the price, I think it's worth it, the Allied campaign took me a little under 14 hours to complete on medium difficulty, and I imagine the German campaign will take about the same, plus the inclusion of historic battles so on a dollar to hour ratio I think this game has a lot to offer. Even still, I imagine another allied campaign would play out very differently for me the second time around. Replayability in strategy games is very important to me, and I can say I am definitely eager to replay this one. On that note, I'd like to see mod support or even expansions for more longevity, but what it offers on release feels finished and fairly well-polished.

For experienced strategy gamers, I definitely recommend ramping the difficulty up as I found I just swept the Germans under the rug on medium.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: I do not recommend this game for everyone.

The Great War is not your standard strategy game where you make lots of progress of the campaign map (unless you plan and perform really well), this is a game of attrition. The lack of conquering new areas on the map and essentially being stuck in the same areas for the entire game can definitely feel frustrating. in addition, the AI is definitely overpowered/omnipotent which can make for a very frustrating experience initially. However, once you play through for awhile you will discover ways to overcome the AI fairly easily given the right circumstances.

With all this said, this game excels at capturing the feel of how World War 1 is described. It truly is just sending troops through the same areas over and over, almost always to their deaths, until eventually you break through and capture some land. The element of the trenches/defenses that you build carrying over from battle to battle on the same map is also a neat addition, and allows for some fairly intense battles towards the end of a campaign. I do wish, however, that this worked for everything you built during each battle because at the moment you have to rebuild artillery, mortars, machine guns, air balloons, etc which I understand are moveable but it is slightly tedious to place these things in essentially the same areas every battle even if they weren't destroyed.

My biggest complaint is that the enemy AI seems to develop at an absurd rate given the resources provided to you during the campaign. For example, you start off only receiving one research point each turn (eventually it becomes 2) and while you are able to earn more through events you still always end up behind wherever the enemy AI is. The one full campaign I have finished so far, the enemy AI had unlocked planes, tanks, and siege artillery between 5-10 turns before me which even if I had completed every event I would not have been able to match. This leads to almost every battle mid-game to have a predicted loss for your side, which you can definitely overcome when you engage in each battle yourself but still becomes tedious especially when you consider you need to earn a major/great victory in every area 2-5 times before you capture it. This leaves me questioning how someone can actually conquer the main victory city (which is an achievement) rather than win based on natural will, but I assume on the lowest difficulty this is less of an issue.

Having said all this, this game is still extremely fun especially for those who are fans of defending against waves of enemies rather than constantly being on the attack. I hope that the developers eventually release more variety to the German side, either through allies or adding more units in general, and I would also enjoy eventually experiencing the other theaters of war in World War 1 besides the western front.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR: This is probably the first dedicated WWI RTS to actually get anything about the period right and manage to be interesting and fun too. As a WWI nerd/RTS fan, this feels like the game I've been waiting my whole life for. Overall balance and gameplay isn't absolutely perfect, but it's very solid both historically and interest-wise. Graphics are... fine, but even if it looked as good as total war I'd probably spend most of the time zoomed out anyways like total war, so, whatever-- the lack of voice line variety honestly bothers me a lot more. But overall, I'd recommend it to anyone who's interested in WWI and/or wants a very different feeling, period realistic-ish RTS game.

There are some issues-- as others have mentioned, fire cones are tough to see in winter time. I personally haven't noticed the AI doing any crazy artillery fire as others have mentioned; it does seem to fire just ahead of where your troops are moving at times, but any decent player could do that, so it seems to be more like actually good AI for once rather than cheating AI. My biggest real issue with the game is that although trenches (but not other intact defense structures, for some reason) will remain through multiple battles, you seem to have to pay for them each battle, which kind of defeats the point IMO, especially given how expensive upgraded trenches are compared to basic ones. Being able to keep upgrading your defensive line would make a lot more thematic sense IMO and also dissuade Falkenhayn-esque strategies of attacking the same region over and over again (which would also make sense).

Hopefully, we'll see fixes for these issues sooner rather than later, and then maybe some extra DLC, like a start of conflict campaign, eastern front campaign, Italian front campaign, and then of course, dare we hope... a united grand campaign? A few more different unit types could be nice too, like light infantry, mountaineers, or cavalry. Take my money devs!

Again, this is a very *different* kind of RTS game, but it's very good at its particular thing.


(And now for a long rant about smallish balance gripes based on gameplay vs my own study of the conflict:)

The AI does seem to have a lot more supply/units than I do, but that may be a function of the Entente having more resources than Germany did as IRL and/or Veteran difficulty, idk. Still though, the fact that the AI could consistently lose soldiers at a 3:1 ratio when it attacked and I played the battle out, and keep me on the ropes in resource terms, (not to mention apparently being ahead in every tech field) seemed like a little much. The other thing I figure I'll mention on Germany vs Entente balance is that it's kind of a bummer as Germany that you only have conscripts, regulars, and elites, while Entente gets different bonuses for every country's soldiers, plus they have elites. Idk that that's so much stronger or whatever, but it's at least more interesting. If Belgium or Canada can have their own unit traits, why not Prussia/Saxony/Bavaria/Baden-Wurttemberg? Or, at least let a German player turn elites + conscripts into regulars or vice versa, just... give me some kind of options before buying the latewar weapons.

On that note, unit balance-- elite soldiers do shoot a bit better than regulars or conscripts, but the real advantage is they're better in melee-- which makes sense, and it's absolutely situationally valuable for assaults or counterattacks, but is it really worth 10 times as much as conscripts? I dunno, I found myself using elite corps for supply and mostly using regulars with a few elites sprinkled in in actual attacks. Likewise, raiders or flamethrower soldiers give you very valuable devastating damage that you can't get elsewhere, but it's *extremely* situational, they cost 6 times as much as regulars, and you only get 1/9 the number of guys.

OTOH, tanks are *incredibly* powerful, especially in groups, and not terribly expensive to deploy, either strategically or tactically. They will be killed by sufficient volume of rifle/MG fire eventually, but it's more theoretically than practical. Artillery does very little damage to them, plus they're quite fast/responsive, so you can dodge most of any given barrage anyway. The game tells me that grenade infantry will kill them, but actually doing that is tough (grenade infantry are fragile and expensive with short range, and tanks can fire at entrenched enemies, such as your approaching grenade infantry, while moving away). I feel like artillery or... something ought to be a bit more effective against tanks (maybe a shorter ranged "direct fire" option, idk); atm the best move seems to be to rush the tech tree to tanks, which, incidentally, is the main reason I was able to beat the campaign. Making tanks a bit slower would also help quite a bit, IMO.

Light field guns only deal suppression, which makes sense for entrenched enemies, but they still do no damage to infantry advancing in the open, which is kinda dumb IMO, considering that breaking up infantry attacks was the designed purpose of such weapons. I could see balance being problematic there (you could break up any attack before it got anywhere near you)... but couldn't they at least slow the enemy down a bit? Like maybe part of being suppression could also be that you can't move so fast because you gotta take cover, that'd make sense IMHO.

Lastly, defense structures feel a little weak, or maybe just too expensive, I'm not sure. Like, even after you get the cheaper barbed wire upgrade, it's still kinda expensive for something that only slows the enemy down somewhat, and will be destroyed by some artillery barrages, crushed by tanks, or cut (I guess) by the AI's stream of attacking infantry over time. MG nests are pretty deadly, sure, but they still get suppressed like everything else, and they're kind of expensive. Likewise, the highest level firing trench is vastly more expensive than basic trenches, and it does keep your guys safer from artillery... but they get suppressed pretty much the same, which is a much bigger issue IMO. Taken together with the fact that you (seem to) get charged for all your defenses every battle, it kind of prevents you from doing any really fancy fieldworks, which I feel like is kind of the whole thing for WWI, so that's a bummer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll review this game simply to dispel a lot of the misinformation I'm seeing in some of the other reviews.

First off, trenches can shoot to front and rear, yes they have a default direction but I think this is for the animation of the trench itself, facing of the troops etc. Troops in the trench cannot shoot laterally, left and right, which makes sense both historically and practically. Trenches were traversed to both protect from artillery concentrations and to provide mutual support, same deal here.

Second, AI cheating? well I do get the flanks of my defense attacked a lot, probably because if you were going to assault a trench you probably wouldn't do it directly into the face of the defense where their obstacle belt and supporting arms are registered. So what do you do? You be smart and arrange your defense in depth, or layers. Line of troops with another line behind still covering the same sector, only two companies can enter a trench at the same time, so if they rush one section, only two companies will be allowed to enter and fight while the others get massacred by the lines behind and the first will probably not be strong enough to evict the first line troops. Do you think the enemy can always see you? He probably can because hes using a balloon, You should too.

Disperse machine guns and mortars to cover multiple avenues with redundant guns as backups, and don't have them situated in the very first line unless reinforced by riflemen. I try to group machine guns in threes (by sector) covered by rifleman.

I have seen the AI make some ridiculous mistakes too, like counterattacking a sector I've recently taken by rushing with no artillery support, so while they may know what is going on and when, they mess a lot of stuff up too. When the enemy is reinforcing a trench by moving up connecting trenches, try dropping heavy artillery at the intersections, this will cause casualties to all the companies attempting to move up, weakening them so your assaulting infantry can easily sweep them aside, especially if they are elite.

Elite Infantry, if your not using them to assault, expect to take massive casualties and have a massive reinforcement wave if you make a breakthrough.

Artillery, Light is a suppression asset (and I think a little damage too), Heavy is a casualty producing, damaging asset, they literally explain that in the tips in loading screens so I'm not sure why people aren't getting that. Is it realistic? not really, but its a game mechanic so whatever. If artillery were to be tweaked, I think that rolling barrage should be the default method for Light artillery with 6 supply, why? It was less reliable then a pile-up barrage, and it often left behind troops to be slaughtered when they got stuck in the mud and couldn't keep up (Which happens all the time in this game!), not the most preferred employment method. Like the default light artillery barrage covers a huge radius, suppressing everything the radius touches, has a quick cool down, and only uses 6 supply against the rolling barrages 15.

Artillery should be harder to coordinate too. I get that its a game, but if you planned all your artillery by time sequence at the beginning of the map, that would be more realistic and a lot more difficult. I don't hate the artillery mechanics now, bottom line, its the King of Battle, so saturate the enemy with it before you jump the bags.

I like the detail of using all of the colonial troops for the British, but why not the French? they had them too, or am I missing something?

I think the French should have been a separate faction and all factions should have a separate tech tree IMO. Maybe separate the campaigns between French, British, and German sectors? Might get you some more mileage out of a pretty narrowly focused campaign. I also didn't see a whole lot of difference between the factions aside from color. Like when you only have one tech tree it kinda limits the flavor of playing as other nations. Some of the unit special traits were kind of bogus too.

I didn't like that there were no real benefits for a great victory besides national support and the sector loses a star. I get that to draw out the game you have to limit the effectiveness of offensives, but if I sweep the enemy off the board I should get that sector. It didn't happen enough for it to be game breaking either. I had more cease fires and minor/major victories than I did great ones.

Its not a perfect game, but man, the maps look great. I could spend hours just building the trenches in the opening phase alone and then watching them get bigger and bigger as battles progress, very cool, though barbed wire should be less expensive and more common.

I also think raiders should get a buff or maybe go away considering there are no night operations to use them on. Had a hard time finding things for them to do.

Very very satisfying when you finally pull off a well timed, sequenced, sweep of all objectives.

Definitely a great way to simulate WWI combat and make it fun and quasi realistic. I have really been enjoying my time with it so far and bravo to the developers for not charging an arm and a leg for it either.\t\t\t\t\t
