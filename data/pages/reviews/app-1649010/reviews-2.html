\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEden Mutant Year Zero was an inferior X-COM like game with poorly balanced and frustrating combat, though it had an interesting setting and engaging characters.

This successor is an inferior Mutant Year Zero. Enemies dispense with tactics and simply run adjacent to your characters, relying on their large health and armor pools to tank through your attacks while they shred your team. The cover mechanic is awful, rather than damage protection it's a flat chance to miss, except I've never once seen it affect enemy fire. Perhaps it is a poorly explained or implemented mechanic.

Available weapons are poorly balanced, some vastly better than others, and there are few of them. Sniper rifles are mostly useless due to the bizarre range accuracy penalties. They barely have more range than an assault rifle in the game, but take massive accuracy penalties if you are even a few spaces inside their max. Range is also frustratingly short for most weapons, the shotgun has 4 less spaces than some of the sniper rifles, but hits an arc, making it the obvious choice.

Line of sight mechanics don't seem very consistent. I've been unable to shoot an enemy 2 spaces away because there was a low wall or bit of trash in the way, but I've also been able to shoot and affect enemies through walls and vice versa.

The upgrade system and skill systems are shallow and uninspired and progression is tediously slow. Thanks to the bad decision of trying to shoe in some kind of poor stealth mechanic, which can either overpowered or useless depending on the map and enemy groupings, is only eclipsed by the awful decision to retain damage and skill cool downs between combats.

The characters are more annoying than interesting with some some questionable decisions and racist overtones related to the "brother" bot, and the plot is dull, dull dull. I might be able to ignore these things if the core of the game, the tactical portion, actually held up.

As a fan of this genre I tried very hard to like this one but I found it more frustrating and annoying than enjoyable. So much potential here, so poorly implemented. If you can tolerate or work around the serious flaws you might have fun with this one, but I doubt it. I couldn't get past the 10th hour and I try to give every game a really fair chance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very two-faced for me.

The characters start out as charming, but throughout the game remain the flat and superficial archetypes they are. The setting has an intriguing and a mysterious start, with plenty of environmental story telling and bits of lore to put together. Then the second half everything gets over-explained with no character development and very obvious plot developments. The story comes across as very formulaic and generic. I don’t mind the linear story, but I do mind the poor writing.

The graphics have a unique and gorgeous touch to them, animations look solid. The sound design is juicy and nice. But the dialogues are filled with sloppy design and forced exposition… very immersion breaking to me. At some point the one-liners started annoying me: all potential danger was "Disneyfied" and "Marvelised".

The combat starts out amazing with a cool stealth mechanic and challenging tactical decision making. It was challenging for about 10 hours, the enemies felt threatening and overwhelming. But then halfway, resources become abundant and I could just waltz through hordes of enemies, without careful positioning. To some the power fantasy might work, but for me it takes away any wanted tension.

The cool turn-based combat is just enough to save this game from a negative review. The predictable story, poor scaling into late game and cringy one-liners let me quit and not complete the game (which is rare for me). But I can see this will entertain others.\t\t\t\t\t
