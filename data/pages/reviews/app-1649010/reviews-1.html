\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall 8.5/10 - Recommended

Story / Plot - 5.5/10, the story was a bit slow and didn't really hold my interest after the early part of the game. The story unfolds very slowly and by the end you are focused more on finishing the game than uncovering what's left of the story.

Combat - 9/10 - this is what you'll spend most of your time in the game doing. The combat plays very similar to XCOM 2 with the standard move, shoot, and overlook options for most characters. There's some limited melee options with 2 of the characters that can be very powerful when used properly. There is also a cover systems (half and full), high ground, and explosive barrels you can shoot to damaage foes.

Graphics - 9/10, this game will make good use of your high end CPU and graphics card

Sound/Voice - 10/10, most of the characters have professional quality voices and the sound and sound effects are very good overall. I didn't turn on the music, so I can't speak to that.

Bonus Points for Developer Name: The Bearded Ladies\t\t\t\t\t

Design "decision" after "decision" screams this game does not want you to play it.\t\t\t\t\t

Way too many bugs, years after it's come out and after googling to see if anyone else had experienced the same issues, I saw they had like 9 months ago and the devs apparently are too busy spending the money they suckered people into shelling out for this instead of fixing things.

Sad.

Arena fight about 2/3 of the way through the game is designed to be difficult, so they don't let you save between waves, WHAT COULD GO WRONG WITH THIS DECISION!

I don't remember seeing this mentioned and so imagine my surprise when I had to shut down the computer to go to work (later battles can really really drag on in time, as i started the arena almost 2 hours before I had to leave and barely finished wave 4 before i had to log off) and came home to find out none of my progress had been saved.

Oh well.

Tried again, this time since I knew what was waiting for me at one point about halfway through the foes change and I decided to use their system of changing your gear mid battle, figuring as this was a problem I had last time dragging the fight on (using fire procs vs robots wasn't helpful) I clicked C to change my gear.

Game froze.

Had to ctrl-delete out of the game and OF COURSE, no progress was saved.

By this point I'd completed 6 waves of a 5 wave Arena encounter and had credit for nothing.

Did I mention this is a main quest line so I can't actually finish the game unless I can get through this clusterfu of a game design decision?

Dudes, if you're gonna make things 'competitively rough' for your audience the least you can do is put the time in to make sure your shi7 works.

At least your fiction writing has matured up to an 11th grade level in the 5 years between game drops.

Do me a favor, I got the game on sale so use the $20 I spent on this to buy a book or two and read them, paying particular attention to character development issues.
