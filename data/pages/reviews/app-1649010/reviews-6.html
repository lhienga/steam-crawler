\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMiasma Chronicles is made by the same developers as Mutant Year Zero: Road to Eden. MYZ:RE was a surprise hit for me, fully utilizing the engrossing world of the Mutant Year Zero IP to craft a unique experience equal parts stealth,turn based strategy, and RPG (though a little light on the latter). I had high hopes for Miasma Chronicles since this was the developer's third game in the stealth/Turn based strategy genre, the second being Corruption 2029 which I haven't had a chance to play. Unfortunately, despite this being the third attempt Miasma Chronicles fails to improve or innovate upon any of the core design choices of the previous entries.

The core gameplay loop consists of a few different actions:
1. Enter a zone
2. Explore the zone collecting plastic (this games version of scrap)
3. Find enemies
4. Pick enemies off in stealth ambushes to wittle down their numbers before fully engaging in turn based combat
5. Engage in turn based combat
6. Finish exploring the area for any loot like extra scopes, or relics

While the above is a bit reductionist, it does well to highlight how incredibly similar this is to MYZ:RTE. So similar in fact that the entire game I couldn't shake the been there, done that feeling. This feeling of familiarity runs deep in everything, from exploration, to stealth, to the turn based combat, to the perk system, to worse yet, the story. Steam has a pretty low character limit on reviews so I will limit discussion of some areas.

In MYZ and Miasma Chronicles you MUST pick off enemies before engaging in full on combat. The game effectively becomes puzzle game for a few minutes between each encounter, where you must figure out exactly how to pick off isolated targets without alerting the entire camp. To do so you must use silenced weapons (there are only 2 in the entirety of Miasma chronicles), in conjunction with abilities to pick off targets. As the game goes on this doesn't necessarily get harder, but requires you use more resources to pick off higher health/armor targets. While this system was novel in MYZ:RTE it quickly wore out it's welcome in Miasma chronicles, partly because this game is longer, but also because the developers didn't add any new tools or mechanics to improve the stealth combat. In fact they went backwards in some areas like the removal of invisibility and many crowd control abilities from MYZ. Don't get me wrong, I enjoyed this gameplay loop the first time around, but after a few attempts it would be great for improvements and additions added rather than a regression.

There aren't really any additions to the formula that bring any new excitement to exploration, stealth, or combat, so the driving force behind exploration becomes the story.
The story is told via conversations with NPC's when starting or completing a main quest, or through audio logs or journals littered throughout the world. Unfortunately, Miasma Chronicles fails to make any improvements in this department either.
If you have played MYZ:RTE the story here retreads alot of the same themes, ideas, and plot points. As just a single example that won't spoil anything- In MYZ:RTE you leave your home in a post-apocalyptic wasteland when your father figure goes missing. Throughout the game you learn about the ancients (humans as they existed prior to the apocalypse), and content with themes about figuring out one's place in the world. Miasma chronicles takes place in a post-apocalyptic wasteland, where Elvis (the protagonist) is in search of his mother figure who has gone missing, in the process learning about the Originals (human's prior to the apocalypse), all the while learning about who he is and his role in this world. While all stories can have similarities the retreading of MYZ was just too much to be coincidence. I frequently caught myself thinking, "they are doing this again, seriously"? Even without the retreading of old ground the story does't have much going for it besides being a passable heroes journey- boy leaves comfort of his home to cross a threshold into danger, ultimately becoming the hero.

This game is good, and I recommend it, which is counter my entire review. I still recommend it if you enjoyed MYZ:RTE as it follows the exact same formula, for better or worse. If you liked the first game then more of the same should scratch that itch, just don't expect any improvements or changes whatsoever. I also recommend it if you have not played MYZ:RTE at all as everything presented here will be a fresh experience. But if that is the case and you haven't played MYZ:RTE go buy that one instead- the world and characters are more interesting, the combat and stealth has more variety, and it is cheaper.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are multiple games with simplified XCOM combat mechanic like: Gears tactics, Mutant Year Zero, etc.
Miasma is the best of them all.
Here are some pro and cons.

Pro:
- Combat (addictive, mostly in the second half of the game)
- Atmoshpere
- World (settlements, areas)

Cons:
- Main characters are irritating (Elvis and Jade), they are too cringe for my taste. Luckily the combat mechanic is the most important for me.
- Bugs
- Not enough weapons and mods
- Broken economy (everything is too expensive)

I hope it get a sequel and I hope they adding these into the game (for this in DLC or in the sequel):
- More unit in the squad (6-8)
- More diverse weapons and mods

If you like the XCOM combat mechanic (of course the simplified version), this is a game for you. I enjoyed and I had this "just one more turn" feeling when I played. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStarted out fine but encountered more and more bugs the more I progress, almost wanted to drop the game but was already invested so finished it in the end.

I guess I expected this game to be 100% turn-based strategy but no, it involves stealthing and taking down lower hp mobs which are not in range of others or distracting them before u finally engage the rest which takes alot of time tbh so do take note on this if you are looking to play this game

List contains spoilers, read at ur own accord.

Pros:
-Nice graphics, was amazed
-Music was nice and fits the environment
-Has quest tracker and pathing
-Plenty of EXP to help you hit max level, also lots of side quests with their own stories
-Level recommendation is stated for the area so I know if I can handle the mobs
-Game pauses when you alt tab even for cut scenes
-Lots of weapon choices & free skill reset to customise build freely
-Can teleport to any map from anywhere
-Dialogues get played as you travel nearer to them which are related to the current story progress instead of random stuffs
-Lots of throwables to utilise during fights
-Able to buy back whatever you sell to NPC, though at higher cost
-Puzzles to get those weapon crates were challenging

Cons:
-Camera view is fixed, cant zoom in or out nor turn/rotate left and right, only could use WASD to move screen to look around, which kinda feels restrictive
-No mini map or marker to show where I am, spent quite some time navigating towns due to this
-Backtracking with no way to mark the map/area and I had to teleport to every area to find as the area requires much higher levels to attempt, by that time I don't remember clearly wheres the location anymore
-Only 2 stealth weapons given the whole game, and they are only tier 2 so damage is limited and you need to land critical hits on both weapons to take down mob stealthily in some cases
-No loadouts, so forced to equip the above mentioned 2 stealth weapons & equip them with attachments before every fight which at one point I just gave up and used them as part of my main setup as I am not gonna keep unequipping attachments every fight
-Failing stealth kills is very punishing that you feel like saving before every attempt to kill to not miss the for example 90% shot to hit + 50% critical chance, which feels necessary or most of time you will just fail the fight due poor positioning from just trying to stealth kill and not in a position to engage in a all round fight
-Miss-able side quests if you don't travel back to old maps, some which are nice rewards though not necessary
-Story felt ok at the start then quickly went downhill, then had some nice parts but the ending was bad imo so overall was below average for me
-Just when I thought I am gonna have a nice final boss fight with actual lots of hp & mechanics, but nope just spawn lots of mobs which I just one shot everything with the Alpha Editor's Miasma skill
-Lack of indicators like: Miasma Barrel skill doesnt show the AOE before planting it, no weapon range indicator before ambush, number of enemies on screen and in range if engaged
-Plastics aka the currency feels lacking as you only can get a limited amount throughout the game, farming Arena is possible but I don't feel its worth the effort and time
-Limited to 3 characters in your team & fights although you have more, weird but I guess thats how they do balancing but I ended up not using the others, only during the final fight when forced to

Bugs I Encountered:
-Alt tabbing breaks the sound effect of the game, had to alt tab a few times for it to be fixed, sometimes it comes back after awhile
-Characters were dropping into the ground and unable to move, encountered this a few times and had to re-load saved files.
-Characters getting stuck on debris or rocks on the ground and unable to move, encountered this a few times but managed to solve but running far away and it auto teleports over. Was annoying as once I was hiding from enemy and this happened so I had to re-load saved file as being spotted.
-Enemy moving over to attack but got stuck in animation and did not attack and nothing could be done and had to re--load saved file and restart the whole fight
-Cover do not work most of the time for yourself but works for enemies, only had a couple of missing through the whole game whereas the enemy always gets the high miss chance
-Cut scenes animations were scuffed for my playthrough not sure if bugged or its just this way, one good example is during breaking wall part i think there was suppose to be a beam effect but there was nothing on my screen so i just watched Elvis raise his hand to the wall with nothing coming out
-When enemies are knock downed, I have seen my character shooting the air instead of at the enemy when the screen zooms in for the animation
-Not sure if this is a bug or intended, I split my team to prepare for ambush by positioning them nicely but realised that I was not in range after I enter amubsh so I proceed to turn off ambush but my team decides to run back to my main character and I have to reposition them again, annoying af as I had to keep doing this just to check if I am in range to ambush properly
-During the Arena fight for my main story progress, I got kicked out of the Arena because a cut scene got played and was teleported out before I could loot any of the Plastics, there were plenty and you can't even go back to loot as the path was blocked intentionally, not sure if intended or not. Nonetheless the way it should have been was only trigger the cutscene after you exit the Arena by yourself so that players can loot first
-Weapon stats sometimes not showing up after you switch to another weapon

Would not recommend unless on massive discount or major bug fixes\t\t\t\t\t
