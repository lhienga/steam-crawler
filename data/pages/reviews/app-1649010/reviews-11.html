\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMiasma Chronicles is a Tactical RPG set in a post apocalyptic America. We follow Elvis - a young man looking for his mother, Diggs - his mechanical "brother" and Jade a mysterious one-eyed girl as they try to save their home from the Miasma and the the First Family.

Game play is tactical turn-based like most CRPGS. Though tactical mostly in the sense of preparation over combat. It is always best to take out stray enemies with stealth ambushes in order to whittle down the enemy forces before engaging with the rest. I thoroughly enjoyed the combat although there are some enemies near the end that can be a tad bit frustrating. Overall though, it's pretty good.

The story is one of the best parts of this game as all off the main cast are compelling in particular, the main character Elvis. He spends a portion of the game looking for his mom and trying to comprehend who he truly is. The darkest hour is amazingly well done though I was hoping that it would drag on a little longer and take more time to resolve. Overall, I really like the story.

7.5 out of 10 - too much Miasma\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found this game to be enjoyable. I like the characters, the story, and the combat. The game does have one somewhat glaring problem which I seriously hope they will address.

The only way to heal effectively is to use the heal pots, which are prohibitively expensive. Unless you are playing on the easiest mode, which heals 100% after a combat, you will likely spend the majority of cash you collect on heal pots. This leaves little cash for anything else, especially in the early going.

I play on easy because I found the cost of healing on high difficulties prohibitive. But that said, at this difficulty level the combat is easy as well. They need to do one of two things. Ideally you would be able to set difficulty parameters. Here you could allow full heal after combat, or, when you return to a city. At the same time you could have a combat difficulty slider to determine how difficult combat would be.

The other option would be to tune the economy so the heal pots are not so dang expensive. Dev's please address this. Clearly I am not the only one reporting this issue.\t\t\t\t\t
