\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game definitely deserves a neutral rating. But i decided to keep it a thumbs up.

Another potentially great game, "consolified" and became mediocre. People always say why are you hating the consoles? Just play it on your favourite platform, it is available on PC too right? Well guess what, in order to make it playable on consoles, devs usually reinvent the wheel, try to find a control scheme that is applicable to gamepads. And ruin basic controls in the meantime. This is one of those games.

If you are planning to play this game via keyboard and mouse beware:

You move with WASD out of combat, i'm ok with that, nice change even. You can use your mouse to move in combat that's good. BUT you need to press 3 to 4 keys in order to take an action. Which hinders the games flow. So you want to shoot an enemy? You first need to go into shooting mode by pressing 2. Because by default you are on move mode. Then cycle through the visible enemies with Q and E just to find the right target. Press spacebar to execute the shooting. Yeah, not ideal. I highly recommend using a multibutton gaming mouse and assign some keys to those extra keys. It becomes playable.

Graphics (apart from the overused sharpening effect), effects, world building, voice acting, all very good. FSR is unusable.

Story is ok, it grows on you. RPG elements are very shallow, game practically wants you to play stealth. Which extremely fun.

You cannot toggle UI elements with the same key which i find FRUSTRATING in any game. You press M for the map f.e, but need to hit Esc to get back in the game. This lack of feature needs to die, come on it's 2024...

Cover system is a complete mess and needs rework. If you are expecting an xcom level cover system here, you are going to be dissapointed.

There is no healing out of combat without using any medkits (except for leveling up) which is kinda rare. This is not a good design and can make you stuck somewhere, if you are not careful and plan ahead.

I recommend getting it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've actually liked the game a lot, nice sci-fi plot and settings, nice character progression and nice characters too.

I liked the way story is passed through interaction with old world artefacts in a cosy way (you don't have to read long papers and books).

Tactical component is kind of limited, cover systems is really not leaving much to the tactics, but battles are still quite entertaining.

My only problem with 505 Games is they controls system, why for the love of god you'd do WASD navigation system in turn-based tactical game ?

also something for consideration for those who want to buy, I found it's quite expensive since the game is quite short too. I'd expect 40 hours of main story gameplay for these money, but had 27 including ALL side quests. Maybe wait for the discount.\t\t\t\t\t
