\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA new game from the developers who made Mutant Year Zero, which has a few improvements to the combat. The game is heavily based around sneaking and eliminating enemies silently to thin down the actual fight. Sometimes you are even capable of clearing a map without the enemy reacting, if you plan it well. For some reason that feels rewarding, although "guns blazing" has quite a bit of impact upon it, yet more dangerous.

The story makes an interesting perspective upon the climate with a solution made that goes wrong. Your little pack of characters traverse the world in order to fight back the Miasma, but also those who seek to control it for the worse.
Despite the world is torn from Miasma, then it's made with good detail. The town of Sedentary seems lively with chatter in all corners. The Miasma itself is "alive" with a weird twisting and swirling presence in all the zones you explore.

One small downside to the game is the use of cover. This may only be present in Light Tactical Mode, but it's weird when you have a "Full Cover" on a character resulting in a 20% chance of being hit and surprisingly that seems to happen rather often when enemies shoot at you. It could be different in Full Tactical Mode, but due to this it's definitely best to exploit as much as possible by eliminating enemies silently, or use skills to control a fight to avoid taking damage.

Overall a fun and engaging game and I look forward to any possible sequel, if it happens.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, this is a good game, but it has flaws and does not hold your hand. If you like serious TBS/T games, then you will probably appreciate this game. It is hard. You have to really strategize each encounter and snipe as many baddies before an encounter as you can, or you will be doing a lot of battles over and over again. Compounding this is the fact that there is not tutorial or mini map to guide you along the way. It's trial and error, baby. You also better be ready to scavenge because you won't be able to upgrade your weapons or buy medipods for low health during battle if you don't. All that said, the graphics are good, the story is interesting, and the gameplay is satisfying. However, it is buggy. It crashed on my Steam Deck about every hour, and I would have to reboot it. It probably works better on a gaming computer, but it's unstable on the Steam Deck. Also, the comments about cover during combat being broken in favor of AI are true. If you have to choose between a good shot or good cover, choose the good shot with no cover, and you will find out that this helps you win battles more often.\t\t\t\t\t
