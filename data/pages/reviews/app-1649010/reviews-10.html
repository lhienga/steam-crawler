\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I would have preferred a direct sequel to Mutant Year Zero, this wasn't a bad offering all in all. I cleared the game in about 22 hours clearing every side quest but one (I looked it up, it was a fetch quest and the reward was meh). The characters were well written and likable, voice acting was good if a little hammy in certain parts but it kind of helped sell the sci-fi trope heavy setting, gameplay was the weakest point getting a little repetitive in some spots and the endgame starts to feel like a bit of slog with high level, high HP enemies becoming spongy and a chore to constantly flank-shoot-flank-shoot over and over.

With the tiny flaws, it's definitely worth a play, the graphics and story alone make it a standout. This game is pretty, and the story takes some nice twists and turns without ever becoming TOO predictable. If you enjoyed year zero, give it a shot. If you never picked up one of these titles, start with Mutant then hit this one, you wont regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI completed one playthrough and fundamentally I just don't recommend the game.

The Good:
The Freeroam/Stealth/Combat loop is decently fun allowing some strategy before proper combat begins and allowing some tactical freedom during combat.
The art style is interesting enough, and while enemy type is fairly limited their design is pretty good too.

The Bad:
The plot is extremely linear offering no choices in a game ostensibly about identity. It is also plain bad, boring, and lacking personality.
Every single "hero" character is annoying. The main "hero" character takes annoying to a point of barely coming across as a complete character, but rather just a series of random knee-jerk reactions wrapped in a weird childish yeehaw skin...

If you have nothing else to play and this game has been STRONGLY discounted, then maybe. Otherwise I'd recommend saving your time and money for more interesting pursuits.\t\t\t\t\t
