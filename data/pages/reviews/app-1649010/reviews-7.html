\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think I can recommend this game. There last patch was over six months ago. They updated this game for a month and then ditched it. There are a lot of weird design decisions that I mention that should have been patched. Mutant Year Zero seems to be the better game and so I hope to pick that up soon.

*Mild World Building Spoilers*
The premise and setting are really good. It's a post-post apocalypse setting(?).
The world starts to succumb to climate change and is saved by this one mega-corp.
Edezen goes on to basically run the US, Canada, and some of South America.
They use the "Miasma", or the precursor to it, to eat plastic and save the world basically. Miasma turns evil and becomes the catalyst for the current apocalypse. Some OK humor for the setting. A penis joke here, a Homer Simpson joke there. The usual post-apocalypse, "they worshiped something called the Inter-Net" type of humor. I chuckled at some stuff.

Some of the characters I enjoyed.
Elvis is the main character and he slowly starts to unravel the plot and how he fits into the world. The "twist" at the end of the game is out of left field. He all of a sudden absolutely hates the group? I get Bha and Jade lied about stuff, but to ditch them and especially Diggs felt out of character. I get the idea, but it just felt so sudden. Not to mention, in the final fight he has several lines of dialogue about how he hates everyone. Again, Bha and Jade and Mason I can understand but not Diggs. And the most egregious thing about the final fight is he is invincible until you hit the arbitrary ads kills and then he 180's again back to a good guy. Tonal whiplash, holy hell.
Diggs is my favorite out of the cast. Diggs stays pretty consistent in tone and character progression.
Elvis and Diggs relationship develops steadily.
Jade kind of throws a wrench in that as she is the typical badass who doesn't care about anything. She barely opens up throughout the game and then in the final bit of the game, gets all chummy with Elvis and calls him her brother. ABSOLUTELY not earned whatsoever.
Bha is only OK. She is kind of one note throughout the story. She has one goal and does everything to reach that goal. Aside from a couple lines of dialogue at the end of the game, she never really questions what she did to Elvis.
Mason is pretty much a non-factor in terms of character. To say anything about him would be spoilers and so I dislike him. You get him so late in the game that I already had established roles and so again he became a non-factor.
None of the other characters in the game really matter. The final boss is limp ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• in terms of impact to the story aside from a mention in the early game, some cutscenes after chapter completions and end game. In the final battle, I threw him down to the ground, teleported him back up, jumped on him and then bum rushed him with Diggs. Fight won on standard difficulty, light tactical.

Gameplay was only alright.
I like XCOM-likes. I really liked the hub area gameplay in this.
I would love to see an XCOM game use this in a kind of offshoot with rebels during and before XCOM 2.
The puzzles were mostly easy, but I liked that as the combat was more what I was wanting to play.
There is some jank during gameplay. The stealth system is weird.
Only your currently selected party member can be detected so if you have the group following behind you they can never be detected as long as you don't switch to them. Completing an ambush puts everyone back in the standing position and I have accidentally swapped over to another character and been spotted. Very frustrating. Also only two stealth weapons. A lot of oddities during gameplay that I feel should have been found by QA and ironed out before release and especially after launch.
I played the difficulty that reloaded your weapons and refreshed your cooldowns after every encounter. Playing the harder difficulties which don't do that seems like it would involve a lot of skipping turns so you can reload and wait out your cooldowns.
The upgraded versions of consumables that can heal or regenerate watts on multiple party members can only do so in combat. You can't heal or regen for everyone in the backpack. Weird design decision.
You can reset your skills at any time, but don't get enough skill points to max out every tree. This just felt tedious. It felt like I had to micromanage my companions before every encounter. Really throws you out of the immersion having to menu constantly.


There's more I disliked but I just finished my campaign and am tired of this game. I don't really recommend it, especially because they only gave it one month of updates before ditching it. Some good ideas that I hope other games take away, but all-in-all an AVOID.\t\t\t\t\t
