\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've played either of the Bearded Ladies previous release. Corruption 2099 (not on steam, why?) or the more successful mutant year zero, you should be instantly comfortable with the game mechanics as they are but for a few changes the same.
Of those three games Miasma Chronicles seems to full in the middle. it doesn't have the charm of Zero and feels for all the world more closely aligned to 2029. It's a solid enough game with decent mechanics and solid story. My main issue with the game is the wildly uneven difficulty through out the game. Even on normal it throws a ridiculous amount of enemies at you in basic encounters forcing each encounter to feel like an exercise in frustration. Weapons don't really get that much better as the game progresses and i never felt even when at max level i was much stronger than at the start, I merely had more tricks to use.

I would love to give the game a neutral rating here as there is fun to be had but as it's either good or bad I need to recommend the game on sale knowing it might frustrate.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA worthy successor to Mutant Year Zero. I do wish they had tied MYZ into the lore/world-building of this game since there are mutants, but know knows, maybe DLC?

The good:
1. Turn-based combat has been improved and is generally much smoother.
2. There are a bunch of new tactical options, but the general map clearing strategy is the same: Sneak, Snipe, Ambush whatever remains.
3. The characters are interesting and generally well developed. VA is well done, even for random npc chatter, which I enjoyed running around discovering after each major event happened.
4. The maps were very well done, with unique zone specific flavor, and are believably post apocalyptic.

The meh:
1. Most later enemies are immune to half your tactical options. Not very fun to customize skills and weapons just to have them be useless a couple maps later.
2. Unique weapons are great, but most of them are found too late, and/or they are outclassed by late game 'generic' weapons because most unqiues are tier 2 or lower and their damage is sub-par.

The bad:
1. The story does a good job feeding you tidbits and slowly laying out more of the puzzle that is the MC's story. UNTIL it nosedives and the MC pulls a 180 and becomes the last boss basically. If this event was going to happen, it should have been before the final stretch, when you basically lose the MC as a playable character. That way he gets some sort of redemption instead of the truncated/forced ending we got.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery similar to their prior game; for a value proposition you have to ask if it is worth 50% more. For some of you the answer is probably no.

The combat is very fun, several paths to success, many more to failure. For most of us picking the enemy apart before combat is important. Like their prior title, each character has things it specialize in; there is enough flexibility where your sniper doesn't have to be a sniper but on the same note you can not field three brawlers.

There is enough variety in enemies to make it interesting.
AI is decently done, some flank, some charge, they'll use specials.

I have had several bugs, none have been game breaking but it can get irritating. Your mileage may very.
UI/inventory bug where you have to get in and out, in and out, in and out, to modify weapons.
Once I had the overlay stay on after the start of combat where I couldn't hit E to close the pop up or control the action, had to kill the game.
I've just about rage quit due to follow/group. Game is designed for you to scout the field and pick your point of attack. So many times I test line of sight, decide to keep looking and everybody magically groups even though I had them ungrouped and hidden elsewhere. Not really a big deal from game play but annoying time waster.
Elevation is 1/2 baked. One weapon can bounce shots but it can not do elevation change; it'l bounce of the empty air if you are on top of something like the second level of a building. There are talents for boost if you have elevation but no penalties for shooting up. Being up high isn't that great most of the time benefits are limited, coverage is often missing and there are no penalties for them attack you up.
Speaking of coverage. It might as well not exist. Deductive reasoning, if full coverage gives 65% reduction and I get hit 4 out 5 times in full coverage then their base change to attack needs to be 145%. I know it is fun to complain about RNG; this is literal, my experience is they very rarely miss. I assume it modifies the AI so they don't try shots quite as often as if you weren't in coverage so coverage isn't quite useless but yea, expect to be hit and position accordingly. Kind of the opposite problem from Xcom where 99% chance to hit would miss.
To be fair, a 55% change to crit seems to be a functional 80% so it goes your way sometimes. Frankly being able to pick off enemies quicker probably works in your favor as your always outnumbered. If there are modifiers being applied to make this make sense it isn't displayed.
The mechanic of crit + kill restores 1 AP is inconsistent. I get it without killing sometimes and don't always get it when I do make a kill.

Despite all these minor bugs, still fun.
They'll probably patch fix most of these if you wait but none of these are a deal breaker for me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game, but a flawed one.
The mechanical part of it is very well made. Combat are fun, smart and interesting. The graphics are very good. UI can be frustrating at first, but you get used to hiet very fast.

The game's major flaw is the story. For the whole time (i'm writing this right after i finished it) it hesitates between :
1) A tongue-in-cheek, over-the-top dark humor Fallout parody with random comedic lines and "so bad it's good" story
2) A very grim, hopeless, survival tale with close friends boiled and eaten, slavery and murderous robot-gods

The whole thing is very weird, characters are inconsistant (especially the main character) and IMHO the story would have been better if a choice had been made between 1) and 2)

as someone else said : It's a good game, but could have been a great game.\t\t\t\t\t
