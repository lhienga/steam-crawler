\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMiasma Chronicles is an underrated gem of Tactical RPG genre. It deserves much better reception than it initially got. Its very similiar to Mutant Year Zero, which was a previous game from these devs. It merges elements of stealth, tactics and RPG in interesting ways.
I actually think Miasma Chronicles is a better game than Mutant Year Zero, which makes the fact that its so underrated even more disappointing. Devs expanded all mechanics present in their previous game and managed to craft an interesting post-apocalyptic world. Characters and story are rather simple but likeable with enjoyably cheesy voice acting and fun twists along the way.
Graphically Miasma Chronicles is quite a looker, for a game using isometric perspective. Environments are richly detailed and well designed, with many tactical options available. Particle effects are especially stunning! Performance wise its quite demanding and requires a decent rig or lowering of graphical settings.
I was playing on Extreme difficulty and found gameplay to be pretty challenging. Every encounter required I "thin the herd" using stealth, before engaging in open combat. Fortunately, stealth has extra depth due to introduction of sound lures, used to separate enemies from groups. Stealth is well balanced as the number of silent weapons is very limited and you'll be only able to kill low tier enemies in stealth.
When detected by enemies, combat switches to regular XCOM-style affair. Fortunately, its has more depth as each character is highly customizable via itemization and skill trees. I quite liked the skills available and the fact you could freely respec each character at any time. It made me micro-manage my squad depending on the type of enemies I was fighting.
I had no expectations for Miasma Chronicles, but I'm happy to say that its a great tactical RPG and manages to stand out in the sea of XCOM clones. Its strange it was so under-marketed, considering the pedigree of the developers and high quality of the final product. I really hope it manages to find its audience and we get to see the sequel, as the story goes in interesting directions, but feels like just the beginning of a bigger saga.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter completing this game, I am certain that I cannot recommend it. The gameplay is extremely difficult and I wouldn't consider it remotely fun on any difficulty other than the easiest. The tactics are thoroughly broken as the hit calculations for your team and the enemies don't match, nor do they match the values presented in gameplay. On the light tactics mode, partial cover should cause you and your enemies to have a 50% chance to hit, but in practice you have about a 10% chance and your enemies have a 90% chance to hit. Critical hit chances are equally ridiculous. On full tactics, the hit chance drops further to the point where you almost can't hit anything unless you are flanking them on an adjacent square with a shotgun. At higher difficulties, your squad doesn't heal (as much or at all) between battles, and the enemies have more hitpoints and do much more damage. This might appeal to some hardcore gamers, but unless you like a ton of save scumming, avoid it if you are a casual gamer.

Now for the worst part, the writing and plot for the main story. I have family from Kentucky and I was initially very impressed with the voice acting and writing. The satire and commentary of modern society is hilarious and very on the nose with many points. When it comes to the main storyline, however, it is so filled with overused tropes, poorly explained or justified situations, and it just doesn't make sense or feel reasonable to anyone with a brain. I don't know what the writers were thinking when they came up with the plot, but I almost quit playing the game 3 times because of how bad it got.

In summary, between the bad writing for the main plot and the poor gameplay experience, I would struggle to give this game a rating over 3/10. It might be a good game for some people, and I'm glad I actually finished it, but I was extremely let down and frustrated by the way the game ultimately resolved.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverwhelmingly Positive:
Only about half way through, but the story is fleshed out, the world is interesting, the loot is well distributed, the characters are diverse and voiced well. I was afraid I would not get into this game, as the previous release from this developer, Mutant Year Zero was well made, but just didn't pull me in.

With Miasma Chronicles, the developers have created a compelling world one wants to explore. The experience so far reminds me of old Fallout games. It starts a bit rough, as the enemies never miss (not sure if this is a bug or not) , but after a few power ups, it balances out, and the game starts to blossom. Definitely worth your time.\t\t\t\t\t
