House Party is a party & love simulation game. It's fun and at the same time, you can communicate with various people at a comfortable party location. It comes to an end by solving numerous missions related to different characters.

While it's fun to play, There were disadvantages that disappointed me.

1. It's all about fixed (pre-set) communication between player and one specific npc. That is, it only consists of conversations between the protagonist and certain NPCs, and there are no elements of relationships (communication, love, jealousy, quarrels, etc.) between the other characters. The progress of the game story, which consists of one-way communication with these player-specific characters, disappointed me a little bit. It would have been more perfect if over time it had been such a complex simulation that randomly triggered a love or conflict event between npcs so that the player's actions could influence these events. Overall, The game is too simple. Just don’t expect to much if you consider buying this game.

2. I want the map to be a little bigger. I know it’s a well-made map, and It's a map where you can feel both comfort and pleasure while playing this game. But honestly, I felt the map was a little small. Especially, it would be nice to make the space of the house a little bigger and wider.

3. Considering the value of its contents along with the limitations and disappointments mentioned above, the current price is a bit expensive. I think 10~15 USD is appropriate for both main+all DLCs. (If you look at the potential for future developments, the value and its price may change.)

In conclusion, it's a love simulation game that's worth playing. But the current price seems a little expensive considering the quantity and quality of the game. I recommend you buy it when it's over 70 to 80 percent off. And don't expect too much! Just think of it as a mediocre 3D visual-novel genre game, and you can have fun playing it.\t\t\t\t\t
