\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short, didn't disappoint. Everything that is advertised that the game offers is really present. It's amazing how many different story lines the developers have been able to fit in this game. I can't even imagine how long it would take to discover all the possible solutions without peeking into the walkthrough.

Although this game is written in a very imaginative, humorous and detailed way, in other respects it still suffers from the typical issues of video games:

* After a few hours of playing, the same repeating remarks made by NPCs become very annoying. The same can be said for the soundtrack that accompanies the game (although the main melody is pretty catchy);

* graphics could be better (or more detailed);

* It's all too obvious that NPCs are located and move only between very defined destinations, which also gets annoying pretty quickly (especially in this type of game). There could be more chance and unpredictability.


All in all, however, it's a very good and entertaining game, and probably the first of its kind (ordinary AVNs don't count), which gives hope that similar games will appear in a more refined and grandiose form in the future. In any case, House Party has made a successful opening.\t\t\t\t\t
