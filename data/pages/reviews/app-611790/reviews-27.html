\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really sorry I have to write a negative review for the game because I think it has a lot of very great ideas, and I would like to encourage more productions of that genre.

House Party is sold as a "party simulator" when actually it's the first-person equivalent of a point&click game: you explore freely, talk to people, find items, and combine and use them to discover more things to do. Basically, I love the idea.
Graphically, it may not be the most optimized 3D game, but for the budget, I think it's absolutely correct. I was even a bit impressed.

So what's the problem, you'll ask. Well, the problem is the same as many very old point&click: the solutions are completely obtuse. Do you remember the monkey wrench puzzle in Monkey Island 2? Well prepare yourself for a nostalgia trip into the worse parts of memory lane.
Did you enjoy having to start all over again from scratch when not doing exactly the requested chain of actions locked you out of half of the remaining game? Happy times indeed.
Or having to play a game from start to finish with the walkthough open on your lap? Well, nowadays, the walkthrough will just be opened in another window, but same idea.

Basically, I think it's too bad there was so many great ideas, and that all those ideas are spoiled because of very few completely outdated mechanics. It's so much more frustratng because I see it could have been a very great game.

Maybe I just didn't go into it with the correct state of mind...\t\t\t\t\t
