The Bad:
1. Dear lord, why does this game take so long to load? I'm pretty sure N64 games needed fewer load times.

2. The animations are clunky, and the character models clip into each other too much during the lewd scene. The poor quality really unmotivated me to go after more lewd scenes.

3. Sometimes dialog to start a mission (called opportunities) just doesn't trigger. Or characters get stuck in a loop and you can't progress in an opportunity.

4. Some opportunities are tied into other ones, sometimes multiple others, and it's super easy to mess something up and get locked out of completing something.

5. Also sometimes opportunities just won't complete for no reason that is clear to me. It seems like I completed everything I needed to, but I'm not getting credit for it.

6. Because of all of the other issues with opportunities it makes it hard to figure out what I can and can't do to move forward with the game. I spend time running around trying to get something to trigger a new opportunity, and no one has anything new for me, and I can't do anything.

The Good:
1. Fully Voice acted, and for the most part, they all do a good job.

2. The character dialog is well written. Funny, and sounds actually sounds like something a real person would say.

Final Thoughts:
There are much better-looking games out there, and ones that work better as well. I would say if you're okay with reloading every 5 minutes because something didn't work right and can wait the 100 years for it to load each time, then give House Party a shot. It has some well-written dialog, and the open-ended and free-roaming gameplay is an interesting concept for this kind of game. I just don't think this team can execute it well\t\t\t\t\t
