\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere might be some immature fun to be had. But devs have been at this for years and game is still poorly optimized. Janky unintuitive controls (fighting mechanic), still loaded with game breaking bugs (can't move/look around). Actually more buggy now. Most open-world games take less time to load that this 'house' map. House textures are mostly fine, but character models are a decade out of date. Not saying the dev team is lazy, but their lack of experience is evident.

Most of the VA is cringe. Unless you know the characters already, can't tell whose even speaking with poor lip-sync. Would love to turn on subtitles only but they are so small and run-on so long its essentially illegible.

Much of this could have been forgiven in the games early stages, but the devs have had years. Now add on the fact they are selling DLC while the base game is still in EA, nor in a remotely polished state. Gone from just sad to scummy.

As for the gameplay loop, that's far more subjective but I just found it tedious and boring. And college humor got old quickly. Game's not bad enough to remove from my acct, but has earned a "Hidden from Library" achievement. BRAVO!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, bottom line up front: There are better ways to see CG tiddies.

Pros:
- The Devs are actually pretty awesome and enthusiastic about their game and community. Major props for that.
- Constant updates and content additions.
- The character models look good, if not fantastic; they appear to be models converted from Daz Studio assets. They serve their purpose.
- The voice acting is better than you'd expect.
- The sex scenes
- The scenario editor allows users to create their own "adventures".

Cons:
- The "humor" isn't that humorous. It's juvenile at best but what can we expect from a porn game?
- The updates often require starting the game from the beginning. It gets old having to find pain meds for Patrick and obtain booze from Frank over and over.
- Character animations can be a bit awkward and stiff.
- The addition of internet personalities most people have never heard of; at least the people I know have never heard of them.
- The scenario editor allows users to create their own stories BUT there are very few user made stories and most I've seen are just designed to get the characters engaged in sexual activities.

So I decided to try House Party. Yeah. It's not that funny, at least in my humble opinion. Sure I got a chuckle or two here and there but the humor here is on a juvenile high school level. Let's be honest, though the game is supposed to be for people ages 18 and over, the real target consumers are socially awkward, under-aged boys. The humor is appropriate to that age group I suppose.

The game looks pretty good in the slightly dated way that Daz Studio's Genesis 3 characters do. Don't get me wrong, the models serve their purpose and have physics applied to the most appropriate anatomical elements. As the game has developed over time, the overall look has improved greatly. The animations are a bit stiff and somewhat awkward at times and the occasional graphical glitch will rear it's ugly head. Oddly, the glitches can tend to be funnier than the intentional humor. I got a good laugh out of watching one of the female models run into difficulty navigating the stairs. She suddenly started rag-dolling while impaled on the hand rail for the rest of that game session while continuing to carry on conversations as if everything was normal.

The subject I want to discuss the most deals with the DLC content. The devs began adding so-called celebrity guest characters to the game. They're all internet celebrities so don't expect anyone from what would be considered "mainstream". The first was Lety from Lety Does Stuff. Who's Lety, you ask? Exactly. I'd never heard of her, but then people from my generation don't typically follow what the millennials do online.
Then there are The Game Grumps. Who are they? Exactly again. I'd never heard of them. Supposedly they are a couple of really funny video game player/critics. They're not funny. At least, they're not funny in the game. I don't know if they are in real life because, frankly, I haven't cared enough to look them up. It's as if they were told, "Hey we wanna put you in the game, so send us some stuff you recorded and we'll build a scenario around it." The Grumps tried to be funny, I suppose, but I personally didn't think so.
Finally we come to the third super special, secret, soon-to-be-revealed celebrity guest! For a couple of months House Party players were teased about the identity of said guest and invited to guess who this sensational celebrity could possibly be based upon nothing more than a silhouetted image. The weeks passed, the teasers kept coming and then the day arrived for the BIG REVEAL! It's DOJA CAT!

F**K YEAH! DOJA CAT! AWESOME!

Wait, who's Doja Cat? Exactly once more. Yet another, I assume, celebrity from parts of the internet I have never visited. Here I was hoping for, I don't know, Angelina Jolie or maybe some other personality. That's what I get for letting my anticipation get the best of me. I'm not knocking Doja Cat personally, I'm sure she's nice.

I really don't know what else to say about the game. The controls are responsive and I was happy about the inclusion of controller support. I do like the fact you have to actually work for the sex scenes rather than having them served up in some contrived, unrealistic scenario like you'd get from Big Sausage Pizza porn.

The developers are highly enthusiastic about the game. I'd sure like to see that level of enthusiasm in other studios. The devs very much appreciate their audience and often integrate the recommendations of their players into the game. They really put a lot of time and effort into the game and constantly push updates, bug fixes, and new content. Which leads me to another point I want to make.

Updates tend to alter the game enough that players are forced to restart the game from the beginning which gets stale really fast. The majority of my almost 42 hours of play time consist of sporadic play sessions that end up with me starting over from the beginning. The humor, as I've stated, does not weather very well through multiple viewings.

Uh. That's it I suppose.

Overall, I have trouble recommending House Party. It's not terrible. It's not great either. It's not overly expensive. Still, there's not enough here to keep me engaged and there are other ways to see CG nudity out there if that's all you are playing House Party for.\t\t\t\t\t
