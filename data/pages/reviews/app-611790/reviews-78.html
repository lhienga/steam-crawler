\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game in July 2017 to support this small team in Early Access. Played it for a few hours back then to test it out, had less content out at the time too but, figured I would get back to it when it was fully released. I liked that this game was pushing that line of normalising nudity in games. Personally have always found it an oxymoron how the industry is fine with murder, drugs & other adult rated themes yet, some nudity/sexual themes to be more taboo.

Now though they have removed content, the nudity/sexual parts of this game out of Early Access/The Game & are charging you separately for it. Content in Early Access is subject to change, it's part of the development cycle, I get that. What isn't normal though is for a developer to remove content out of a game, a big selling point of a game too, & later charge you separately for it. This game was sold as a game with sex & nudity. The devs even talk about it in the Early Access notes, a quote directly from the page;

"This game is all about choice, and hearing from our player base has better helped us understand which kinds of choices are most important to the experience. The answer usually had to do with more sex positions. You can’t say we didn’t listen.”
Reading between the lines, seems they wanna charge now for the part, as they see it, & maybe the player base too "most important to the experience".

I had a patch they had on their website installed at the time, I believe later in 2018 they made it a free patch on Steam to install but having removed the game from my ssd to make space, never installed that separate DLC & now have to pay for it. I have emailed them about it too for a free code but, I got no reply back. *update they had old email listed by mistake on website but, still got back with a free code after checking there & getting back after a while*

If you are ever going to grab this, do it at a very high discount imo, bargain bin sale & there's no guarantee they don't repeatedly sell uncensored patch's either for any future dlc. or things like that but, how they move from here has yet to been seen.

I get the team is small & doesn't have Valve money but, there are ways to go about things with a more consumer friendly approach. I'm sure if they needed more money to fund the growing ambitions of the game, they could get it if transparent & consumer friendly about it.

- my 2 cents

Your project is in Early Access, no game is going to stay the same there, & will have things change. That's the whole point of a Early Access, it's not a Final Product. That's fine in most cases & potentially necessary for a small team like yours, & like I said the growing ambitions of the game. You also have a Patreon Page & have a good amount of support there monthly..Separating content though & charging people separate for it is exactly what it is, you can rationalise it w.e way you want with w.e cognitive biases but, ima call a Spade, a Spade.

Even if you wanted to appeal to "ALL" lol for the sake of debate, you could still separate the 2 at no cost & still give people, even that small minority of your player base the option to do so, at no extra cost. Like a game incorporating both 1st & 3rd perspective in a game. Instead of having 1 over another, or charging for "how it's presented" you let people jump between & pick for example. To free cosmetic changes from colours, to outfits, & earned progression, or charging for things like that & "how it's presented" instead, etc. What you choose to monetize, is what you choose to monetize.
