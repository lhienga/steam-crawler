\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know it's something about the dialogue and the way the faces are animated, but this game gives me a frequent and strange sensation similar to when I played Vampire: The Masquerade - Bloodline. I couldn't give you any one specific example but this games comes off as something by Stephen king or Lovecraft or Something Else Entirely. But... my brain is weird and works weird... I wouldn't think too hard about it.

Mooooving on

House Party comes off as a simple idea, you're a person, and here is a party. Horror game vibes, am I right, fellow anxiety people? Very quickly the party mingling gave me a plethora of fairly straightforward ideas to work on to improve your standing amidst the strangers. The people you meet are fairly distinct and provide well written dialogue. A few hours in, I have lived this groundhog day many... many times. Always reaching for the torch of sweet burning victory, I am met by Frank who through one reason or another has caught my shenanigans and sentenced me with an induced coma. Sometimes I attempt to be stealthy in my maneuvers only to be met by his skilled protegee Leah who proceeds to stun lock me harder than a hammer does to repeated headshots on a Rathalos.

Planning is needed, timing and cunning my tools. What are my goals? Do I even have goals? Does time and space collapse around this house forever condemning me for my sins?

Oh hey, a skeleton key to the bathrooms AND a beer? :)

Now I sacrifice precious beers to buy trust, strike up friendships, alliances, conspiracies, and a thermos containing destiny itself.

Am I playing as an infant god playing around in a micro universe of it's own creation? Learning to dodge and weave in the tactical withdrawal and placement of items, whispering twisted truths to set others off to pursue my goals under the guise of free will, turning the party goers against themselves so I can grab a victory I cannot even visualize. Who was I coming into this party? The stories Derek told echo of a familiar but distant memory to what I have become in these repeating lives in an infinite night. What will I be at the end of this? What is the grand line of events to arrange and set in motion? What domino effect will unfold as the manifestation of my will? Or... am i just a puppet to it's will? Will I finally stop misclicking in the dialogue with Derek and wind up with him trying to beat me up? I don't know.... but I intend to find out.

10/10 Fantastic party simulator that is probably(?) not a secret horror game. Also with adult elements if you want them, nothing negative to say about the craftspersonship regarding that at least.

Buy it, enjoy it, question what lies beyond the night sky because of it. House Party.
