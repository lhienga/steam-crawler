\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst I'd like everyone who is possible reading this review to note that this game is, first and foremost, a puzzle game. Specifically a point-and-click type of one! BUT DO NOT LET THAT TURN YOU OFF FROM BUYING IT.

House Party is one of the rare early access games that got continuous updates, actually listened to player feedback, and full released it's promised product. The best part is, the game still gets free events and the occasional update and the devs are very active on the Steam discussions page and such! That alone is worth a round of applause and plenty of praise, I think.

Anyways, about the actual game: House Party is unique in that, despite all the lewdness and fun humor, it has deep point-and-click puzzle action going on. And not like, some baby easy stuff either! The house holds a ton of items that you can use and interact with in a lot of different ways to progress different stories. It's honestly surprising how many times you can restart the game and find something you've never noticed before in a simple two-story house.

Besides the puzzle aspect, it's also a fun sandbox-y type game where you're free to basically do whatever you want. Grope people, piss on them, flash them, all of that good stuff! The several ways you can combine items and "prank" people (among other things!)

The dialogue/story is also surprisingly deep. Shmoozing a character and getting them to open up to you and their insecurities provides some actual good plot to an otherwise silly game.

Here's a short list of pros and cons:

Pros:

- Inclusivity! For all my fellow gays, bi's, lesbians and everything inbetween, there is definitely stuff for you in here. Despite the male to female character ratio, the fact that you CAN romance the guys is a huge bonus. There is also a female character option and everyone is more than happy to get it on with you. Heck, even asexuals can enjoy the game for the story. No explicit DLC needed to enjoy this game at all.

- Story

- Graphics

- Replayability

- Freedom to do what you want ingame

- Humor is on point and the dialogue consistently makes giggle

Cons:
- Probably the biggest one that quite a few people were/are upset about. The explicit content was originally part of the base game, but was separated and monetized. I personally didn't mind as I bought the game after the change happened, and I understand the devs separated it so people could play the game without getting banned on platforms and so the devs could release it on other storefronts. Even still, it still kinda sucks!

- needs more bois at the party pls devs <33

--------------

All-in-all, It's absolutely worth the money. The devs showed they care about the game and its user base. For a small game, it's surprisingly complex and there's more getting added to it every so often. It does tend to get reptitive, but it is the perfect game that you can play around in for a few hours, forget about it for a couple months, then come back and play again. Can't wait to see how Office Party turns out!\t\t\t\t\t
