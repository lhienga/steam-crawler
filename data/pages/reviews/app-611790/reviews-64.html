\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's worth a play-through or two for the dialogue, but a lot is still pretty bad, even post-official-launch now.

Combat is a meme... it's the worst combat in any game I've ever played if I take it seriously as something meant to be a useful mechanic. Now, I *think* combat is supposed to be like the guns in the Battlefield series that do like 5 dmg, so, yeah, it's a meme. It would be okay-ish if just Frank obliterated you every time, but attack literally anyone and it's like you've ascended to lewd Dark Souls level difficulty.

The gamespace itself... it's literally a House Party, so, I can't get overly upset by that. But it's a cramped house... from Alpha until now, it's still, still, when I fired up briefly just a day or so ago, a pain to get around NPCs on the stairs or in the sliding door to the backyard.

I mean, it's mostly a fetch quest with a few very minimal elements of puzzle solving or crafting.

I applaud, however, the addition of Game Grumps (for free, no less), and the QoL type changes to some of the lewd stuff as well as some added other stuff (it has an intro, now, like a cinematic-style intro). And it's worth noting that this is one of the few lewd games with *both* full voice acting as well as a 3-D environment... most similar games on Steam are visual novels and/or do not feature voice acting. So, if you enjoy BaD or Acting Lessons, House-Party does has some advantages over those despite how limited it can feel.

But while I give it a thumbs up, be aware that the only real draw is the irreverent tone and few good set-pieces of dialogue. Otherwise, it's a pretty small game-space to exist in with NPCs that don't really act that zany while idling in the background most of the time.
