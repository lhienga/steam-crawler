\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I enjoyed my time with this game, I feel that with a $50 price tag, 5-chapter story, and being mainly locked to Sotenbori there is not much to justify the price. For newcomers to the Yakuza series or those who haven't played Yakuza: Like A Dragon, this game may is not the best.

I specifically have issue with the story and pacing. I was hoping the story would be a little longer especially where there were time jumps or just small slideshows explaining how the story went. While I understand the game's relatively short development cycle (6 months according to a dev), it's easy to see in the lack of fleshed-out gameplay elements. Going into the game I was really hoping to go back to Yokohama and relive the battle between Kazuma Kiryu and Ichiban from Like A Dragon. Unfortunately this only talked about after a massive time jump. Even an appearance by Ichiban as a Castle opponent would have been satisfying.

I would recommend the game only if you've played Yakuza: Like a Dragon AND have played most of the other Kiryu games. It effectively explains Kazuma Kiryu's whereabouts and his appearance in Yakuza: Like a Dragon. The ending is particularly well done, then transitions into the upcoming Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth game and bundled demo for it.

I do like what's been done with the Castle and think the system could integrate a lot more battles and opponents to make it interesting. To get some of the achievements and skills in the game, you effectively need to grind battles over and over again which loses it's charm pretty quick.

Overall, I think this game exists to ease you into the Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth game coming out next year. It does an OK job at explaining the side of Kazuma Kiryu during the events of Yakuza: Like a Dragon but most of the payoff comes from the ending. However, I would hesitate to recommend this game to casual fans of the series or newcomers. I do not think $50 is a good price for the game ESPECIALLY since you can get Yakuza 0, Yakuza Kiwami, and Yakuza Kiwami 2 for $18 in a bundle right now.
