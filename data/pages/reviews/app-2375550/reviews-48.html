\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely loved this game. Its events occur at the same time as Like a Dragon's, so it's quite a self-contained game story-wise, but it still took me 20+hr to beat the game and side content (notably, substories and arena, didn't quite never got into Pocket Circuit racing).

As Kiryu's last dance approaches (presumably), we get to see him in action again from the ending of Y6 until the ending of Like a Dragon. This time around, Kiryu's some kind of secret agent with gadgets and all, which give some variety to the combat gameplay. First few hours of combat fell a bit stale and lack options, but variety quickly grows and combat gets funnier very soon as you start unlocking new moves.

We get the same old Yakuza Style, and the brand new Agent Style, which is a more swift, agile combat approach in which you can use Kiryu's gadgets. Some of them felt pretty useless (cigar and drones), but thread and rocket-powered shoes felt like they added variety and added some strategical options, so a nice change of pace. It's not like fights are very difficult anyway, specially since this game allows us to unlock Tiger Drop as early as chapter 1...

Music and voice acting are top tier as usual and the graphics are the same formula as ever: gorgeous for main character models, more modest in environments, and some PS3 sprinkle for NPCs...

If anything, I would complain that unlike in Y6 or LaD, Gaiden's new characters didn't really leave a mark on me. Kiryu and the old dogs steal the show, really. Though I would like to see Akame back, she seems qualified to take over as a new kind of Florist of Sai.

All in all, if Like a Dragon franchise is the kind of game you enjoy, this is close to the greater ones, so don't hesitate to give it a try. Solid 8-8.5 for me.\t\t\t\t\t
