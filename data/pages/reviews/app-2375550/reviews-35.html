While it is kind of a spin-off and by far the shortest game of the series, I would consider this to be up there with the best LaD games.

I highly recommend this if you liked the rest of the series. Definitely don't start with this one though, it's pretty much LaD 7.5, so it spoils every game so far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA painful sort of parting.

Let me state up front that, despite what Sega's marketing claims, this is the absolute worst place to start the Yakuza franchise. Not because of its quality, but because this feels like the closing epilogue to a saga that spans close to two decades in releases.

I've played Yakuza for going on twelve years at this point, ever since importing the Japanese release of Yakuza 5, then emulating the originals, and slowly working forward from there. It's given me great joy to see the new waves of popularity RGG have rightfully gotten in the wake of Yakuza 0's debut in the West, and it gives me even greater joy to see that rather than sit on their laurels, they've consistently pushed themselves to make each new release as great as it can be.

Gaiden is a side game, and that nature is plenty evident in everything from its skeleton to its skin. It simply lacks the wide variety of content that the mainline franchise is well known for. It tries, and my hour count- I hope- serves as sufficient evidence to show you'll get plenty of bang for your buck, but I would strongly encourage you to temper your expectations somewhat before diving in, and I think you'll be pleasantly surprised as a result.

I don't want to speak overlong on the story, because I consider it Gaiden's greatest strength. If you've been following Kiryu through the course of his life, from 0 to 6, then this absolutely *is not* something you want to sit out. Speaking personally, it's the sweetest kind of pain seeing what he goes through in Gaiden. It's the sappy, Valjean-esque sort of drama you've come to expect from Yakuza, but it hits home all the more simply because it's Kiryu. To see an immovable force of nature show the scars and strain behind his stoicism wasn't only deeply moving, it was heart-wrenching.

Because of Gaiden, because of the momentum RGG have been picking up with Yakuza 7 and Lost Judgment, I am wholeheartedly certain that Yakuza 8 will be their crowning masterpiece, and a game that'll leave me utterly broken with tears.

I cannot wait.\t\t\t\t\t
