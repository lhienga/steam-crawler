\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSet between 7 and 8, a conclusion to a certain bodyguard's story. The main story is very short relative to past games, so it is very obvious it is being padded out with Akame side content to slow down the progress. During the early parts it does not affect the pacing that much, but during late game the point requirements forces the player to do all the substories and minigames for points, on top of grinding for stupid amount of money in the Castle, to upgrade abilities.

The battle system is like Judgement(two styles), one for solo and another for group. Classic battle style is good from the get go but Agent requires a lot of investment in abilities to become usable and eventually very fun. Suzuki Taichi's basic moves are greatly simplified in this entry so being able to call 20 drones to do the fighting instead is great to have.

On the topic of fights, from a pure game play pov there are no memorable fights nor bosses in the mainline quest, maybe in a longer game there will be more time for character developments to hype things up, but boss fights are shockingly plain. Past entries you have some random guy that bust out the door with machine guns, and chainsaws, and some other . In this entry the most interesting boss is a dude wielding a tanto that gets knocked out before he can even show anything.....

Perhaps the most praiseworthy aspect of Y7.5 is the graphics. On a mid range set up I can max out every graphic setting and still run the game stable at 60fps. The lighting, assets, special effects and detailed character models looks amazing in most environment. Compared to Y7, it felt like there is a greater emphasis on uping the saturation and contrasts to make cut-scenes more dramatic, sometimes it gets too dark to see any details on default gamma settings.

Overall: Amazing teaser for Y8, play it.\t\t\t\t\t
