Kiryu has never felt this sluggish in combat. This game took 6 months to make and it shows - I can feel the filler (and that's saying something for the Yakuza series). Filler in pace, stat building, side quests... but most egregiously in the story itself - the place where RGG has always shined.

I understand that publishers and studio pressures often force artists to accelerate development and churn out product. I'm just sad it affected this protagonist at the tale end of his story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game does not at all let you know it was made in the span of 6 months in parallel with 2 other games from the studio.
As a Like A Dragon entry, it's well paced and punches way above its weight with a very cohesive narrative that still has its classic mood swing charms, yet it avoids the messy plot webs of the PS3 era games that might make it too nauseating for less committed fans. Kiryu's characterization is even stronger than it was in the previous games, but it still finds time to explore and discuss the formerly-titular organisations that this franchise is actually about.
As a standalone game, it is one of the shiny gems of 2023 with a characteristically strong desire to make an enjoyable product and tell a compelling story. The downside i can see in playing this game as a person unfamiliar with the series, however, is that structurally, Gaiden is heavily reliant on callbacks and references to at least 4 previous titles, from characters that are mentioned to events that are retold, which can leave a new player with gaps in the understanding of the overall story.
The $50 price tag, though, might just be a bit too steep for a game with about half the playtime of a mainline title, especially considering that most of the secondary content is reused from previous titles.
Overall, this is one of the best entries of the franchise, and its a fairly high bar.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9.5/10

An absolutely beautiful love letter to Kiryu and the "Yakuza" saga of Like a Dragon. While it seems Infinite Wealth will be our farewell to Kiryu, Gaiden feels like a heartfelt farewell to the games that introduced us to him.

The gameplay was a lot of fun. I'll admit, I definitely favored one style over the other, but even primarily using one style throughout most of the game, I still had a blast playing. As per usual, this game is chock full of minigames and side stories to invest your time in to, and they are absolutely worth it! Also in usual RGG fashion, the soundtrack was phenomenal. While being a game focused on Kiryu, it featured a cast of primarily new characters, and I loved them. Akame was an instant favorite for me. Shishido and Hanawa were strong favorites as well. But the story is where this game absolutely shines. It maintained continuity from the stories that came before, while also building up immense anticipation for the next game to come. The ending in particular was phenomenal.

This game was absolutely meant for the Yakuza fans who have been along for Kiryu's entire journey. If you're new to the series, this absolutely should not be your starting point. But it is a fantastic destination to look forward to.\t\t\t\t\t
