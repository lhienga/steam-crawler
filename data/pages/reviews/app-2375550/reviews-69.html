\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter Yakuza 6, I was very skeptical of the idea of Kiryu returning in any role as a protagonist or major character in future games. However, the game does a wonderful job connecting Yakuza 6 and Yakuza: Like a Dragon together, while also setting up Infinite Wealth in a way that makes me extremely excited to see what happens next.

For a shorter title compared to RGG's other works, the game is still full of content that can very well take over 30 hours to complete. Side content like the Coliseum are obvious stand-outs, and there's a decent selection of substories to go through as well. Combat still feels fun to mess around with, and the soundtrack features a very different feel that other entries haven't had (in a good way). For me though, the story, while relatively short, was the obvious star of the game, featuring a great cast of characters and a deeply emotional ending that broke me more than any other Yakuza game.

In all, Gaiden is another excellent entry in the franchise, and one that makes me very excited to see what's in store next for Yakuza fans. While I wouldn't recommend this title for newcomers, the payoff after playing all previous mainline titles is fantastic, and very much worth waiting for.\t\t\t\t\t
