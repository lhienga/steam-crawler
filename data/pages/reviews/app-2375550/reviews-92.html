\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, for being a "shorter" side game in RGG's eyes, this game is a complete and worthwhile package. Absolutely incredible game and a perfect installment to the Yakuza/LAD series for longtime fans. Gaiden is definitely the kind of follow up I desired after Y6 and Y7. ESPECIALLY Y6.

It follows up perfectly on Y6's ending, and really does feel like an important companion piece and closure on a game whose ending is kinda controversial. This game makes a ton of callbacks and connections to previous games across the series with good emotional payoffs, if you've played Kiryu's saga, this game ABSOLUTELY hits like a pink truck.

Normally, most RGG games don't require you playing any prequels, but you'd definitely miss out on a lot of emotional hits if you haven't played Kiryu's games. Not to say it's required, but I feel like this game does a lot that draws on previous games that would be lost on someone starting only with this game.

Also, this game has big spoilers for both Y6's and Y7's endings. Definitely would recommend playing at least Y7 prior.

This game's combat is hella fun, the new Agent style took a bit of getting used to, but omg when it's upgraded more it's definitely fun as ♥♥♥♥. Yakuza style is the best version of the DoD style in the Dragon Engine we've gotten.

The side content is pretty great too. Kinda liked the fact that this was a smaller game. I actually finished every substory and coliseum fight. Even beat the hard super boss fight, which is awesome. Never done that before in a LaD/Yakuza game.

The main story is definitely short, they definitely weren't joking that it was a shorter game. BUT, it has some of the coolest action set pieces and moments I've seen in a Yakuza game. I took, SO, SOOO many screenshots during the course of this game.

The final boss's sequence was actually perfect. Like, the perfect boss, ideology, set pieces, to send off Kiryu's final chapter (as the stand alone sole protagonist). It somehow equals or tops Y5's for me, which is no small feat.

Might be the best final boss of the main series.

Also, the ending absolutely emotionally destroys you. It hurts so bad. (But in a good way)

Gaiden feels like a capstone piece to everything that Kiryu is as a person by current day. Huge fanservice game, great connections, tie ins, callbacks, and paving the way for the future.

Peak.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthe perfect appetizer until yakuza 8 drops.

for those curious but not fully on board yet, yes, this is canon.
taking place alongside yakuza 7. (think chain of memories and 358 days for comparison)
I recommend playing yakuza 7 first as this game will more or less spoil that game, even if it glosses over certain details.
outside of main story, you're mostly given fetch quests, with traditional sub stories and mini games sprinkled throughout.

this game is notable shorter than the rest of the series. if you B-line it, you're looking at roughly 15-20 hours. that said, the main story is designed to be incredibly difficult in hopes to prevent you from doing that and instead spend time elsewhere in the game.
I did majority of what was there, outside some fetch quests, two sub stories, and a handle of completionist challenges in yakuza fashion (play every minigame, grind out the hostess clubs, etc) finishing the game just a little over the 29 hour mark.
sadly, there doesn't seem to be a legendary difficulty. I like to play as much as I can on normal mode for my first run, then run through it again on legend, but its not here. sure, there is hard mode that will have to satisfy me, but when your series is known for having that extra step up in challenge, it doesn't entice me further when its gone. that said, I do intend to go back and play more eventually, but releasing in the window it did, I can wait till yakuza 8 drops happily.

final verdict: 9/10
TLDR: theres nothing bad here if you're simply hungry for more yakuza\t\t\t\t\t
