\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike a Dragon: Gaiden was a very fun game with some few negative points i'd like to address here aswell.

Pros:
- The visuals are amazing. Character Models, Textures etc are high res

- The combat feels fun and every hit feels like it really has big impact on an enemy.

- Skill Upgrades offer meaningful improvements and not just small boosts. After my first two DMG upgrades i could really feel the difference in how fast enemies got knocked out!

- the Story was incredibly good and the ending a good way to lead into the next main game!

Cons:
- The new Agent style felt like it was TOO gimmicky. At the end i've never really used it whatsoever because the Yakuza style just offered everything i wanted.

- having real life scenes for the cabaret was awkward and a bad decision.

- the Request System that works kind off like the one for the Judgment Series ultimately offered too little variation. There were like 4 quests that offered an interesting storyline while the rest was just a pretext to have some random small gangs show up that you needed to beat up enough times while running through all of Sotenbori..

- For all the hype around this feature i just didn't like the Coliseum at all... For example why would i want to cripple myself playing other characters that barely have any good combo? And those rumble fights were just pure chaos. I didn't decide to play through this completely at the end.


Overall i still recommend it for any Like a Dragon fan out there, though, since it explains well what was going on between Y6 and Y7!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long time Yakuza fan, this game is NOT a good entry point to the series! Repeat: this is NOT a good entry point to the series! There are plenty of moments where this game nods to older entries and I can't imagine how you'll experience it if you're in the dark by starting with this game.
I know RGG is pushing the "LaD" title on us, but I'mma stick to Yakuza because I'm stubborn.

With that said, overall, I'd recommend this game.
While this is a short Yakuza game - by Yakuza's standards at least - it's still filled with the usual stuff you'd expect from Yakuza. It's got the plot, characters, events and side stuff + explanations for all that and it all comes together pretty darn well.
As far as gameplay goes, the latest entry for Kiryu and his combat in the Dragon Engine is Kiwami 2 which I didn't really enjoy. Gaiden has improved on this quite a bit, but it feels worse than Lost Judgement; it's still good though.

If I have a complaint, it would be that this game is HUGE in terms of download size - almost 90 gigs - but for the wrong reason; namely the cabaret girls are all high quality videos which are 20 sodding gigs all by themselves. I didn't ask for this, so kudos to those who are desperate for it I guess.
I know there's the demo, but that's irrelevant.
Also, we don't get to fight Ichiban. Boooooooo.\t\t\t\t\t
