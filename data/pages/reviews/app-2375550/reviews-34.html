\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very good its worth your time if you have played the other games connected to Kiryu (Yakuza 1 through 6). This game feels like they tried to make a better conclusion to Kiryus story then yakuza 6 as well as kinda leading into his story in 8 (Ill mention the "kinda" in the spoiler part). Gameplay is solid as usual and the minigames are just as strong as before.

The Agent style as a fighting style is the fast style of this game {most comparable to Crane in the Judgements), but with a more defensive profile (kinda like Snake from LJ but in role only) and has gadgets to mix it up and make groups easier to fight. Agent styles gadgets are sorta disappointing overall, since Id usually use spider gadget for most groups and Seprent boots for heat build on the street, for the other gadgets, I barely used drones other then early games and never used Firefly other then for the extreme heat move with it. Overall great style but the gadgets feel sorta lack luster.

The Yakuza Style is Kiryus style from Y6/ YK2, but tweaked a bit and has a better game feel then those games since it has less early Dragon Engine jank. The damage output for this style is insane and makes Agent feel lacking damage wise. Alot of moves came over but some got removed or tweaked so it doesnt intrude on Agents group handling niche, most of it is fine but some changes I dont necessary get, like the grab finishers being stuck to extreme heat when theres better options in extreme heat for Yakuza style for groups (or you just switch to Agent for groups). Yakuza style is the boss killer style of this game and due to its damage output makes fights go faster then Id wish (played through this game on Professional), probably just a personal thing and it doesnt make coliseum any easier though. Yakuza style was my go to for this game and felt great the entire time.

Coliseum is the main minigame with a quote on quote storyline connected to it (same sort of the story of there being 4 kings as main minigames after 0), and within the story you have to play in the coliseum. This allows some fleshing out of the minigame, primarily with alternative characters to play as that were not playable before and those characters being put into group battles. Unfortunately even a half fully upgraded Kiryu will be able to handle more then the other characters (except the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ chicken man, hes pretty good only because of his special move), maybe I just didnt level them up enough though. For the group battles, they are fairly alright, with so many guys helping out its really hard to see what your buddies are doing except the healers because they have a huge aoe heal visible.

Into the spoiler section because I dont want story spoilers up at the top of the review: As per usual the story is solid for an RGG game, it sorta gains the same bonus that the Kaito Files has for being only 5 chapters long, since some of the games like 7 or LJ felt like they dragged in some sections. Mad when announced, Loved on release!

Like a Dragon Gaiden: The Man Who Erased His Name is a spin off game which details the story of Kiryu Kazuma more or less during the events of Yakuza 7: Like a Dragon.

First and foremost, I remember I was pretty annoyed when this game was announced as I remember (perhaps incorrectly) reading somewhere that the writers were done with Kiryu's story and that Kasuga Ichiban would be the new protagonist going forward and that turn based combat would be how the games would proceed. As someone who fell completely in love with Y7 I remember getting frustrated at the idea of going back to beat 'em up. But quite frankly I was just wrong in my entirety.

LAD:G plays like all the Yakuza games before Y7, it is a beat em up but the story itself is significantly shorter than what we're used to from a Yakuza game, if you're like me and like to do a lot of side content (not to 100% completion, just messing about a bit) and doing the main story, you're probably talking about 25-30 hours for a standard playthrough, still a lot, but less than what we're used to. Much like every Yakuza game, the story is compelling and it's very easy to get invested in.

Gameplay is similar to Yakuza Kiwami 2, feels great, punches and kicks are heavy, overall very satisfying. Although I'd say one of the best improvements is the seamless blend of running around then transitioning into combat. No more little "Crack knuckles" mini cinematic, no more money counting up at the end, it's literally just run around > Guy wants to fight > You fight him > Money added. There is no downtown between any of the random encounters in the game, which is a huge improvement as I'm sure a lot of us have been frustrated at times having to do random encounters in previous games one after another; this feature really saves a lot of time.

Has everything that hallmarks at Yakuza game, daft little mini games, a bunch of substories, bigger sidewquests that hold their own weight alongside the main story, it's all here.

Overall I can definitely say that my initial opinion on this game being released was wrong and I should have had more faith in the Development Studio and the Writers, I guess I just wanted more Ichiban that I forgot about just how good it was to be Kiryu again. If you're looking for a short quick beat 'em up, or something just to tide over that Yakuza desire until Infinite Wealth comes out, this is the game to play. 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
