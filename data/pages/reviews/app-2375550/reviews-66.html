\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I disagree with SEGA that this is a good point for those who haven't played any Like a Dragon games before, for those who have kept up with the series, it is an excellent albeit short entry in the overall story that helps wrap up a lot of hanging plots in the series. This game leans pretty heavily into plot points from Yakuza 3-6 and a little of Like a Dragon 7 so it would behoove anybody thinking of playing this game to play those at the very least.

As far as the gameplay goes, the combat is back to the older beat-em-up style offering many of the previous moves from Yakuza 6 with an all new Agent style that starts off feeling a bit underwhelming but evolves into a fantastic means of crowd control by the end. Many of the series' staple minigames are back which serve as less of a healthy distraction in this game and more of the bulk of the overall gameplay.

Ultimately, I believe that this game may be a bit rough for those who are not familiar with the series already and may be a bit short for those who aren't super into the franchise or aren't one of those who force themselves to complete the game 100% but outside of that, it still holds up modestly well to the other entries that precede it.\t\t\t\t\t
