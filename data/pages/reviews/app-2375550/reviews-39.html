\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike a Dragon Gaiden: Them Man Who Erased His Name is an amazing side story that takes place during the events of Yakuza 7/LAD. It rewards players who were invested with Kiryu's story with callbacks from previous entries and paves a way for Infinite Wealth. If you have only played Yakuza 7/LAD and want to know who this Kiryu fella is then this is an okay starting point but it is highly recommended that you start off with Yakuza 0 or Yakuza Kiwami (Yakuza 1 remake)

Combat feels better than Yakuza Kiwami 2 and is akin to that of the Judgement sub-series offering two Combat styles, those being Agent Style and Yakuza Style. Agent Style is a new style utilising gadgets to take out multiple enemies and helps with crowd control while the Yakuza Style is akin to that of previous entries and is better for 1v1 battles.
Upgrades are a mixture of Yakuza 0 and Yakuza 6 where you need Money and Akame Points to unlock new skills which is fine, the Coliseum is a great way to grind money while exploring the world of Sotenbori and The Castle nets you Akame points.

There are only 2 locales being Sotenbori and The Castle which is again fine, as both have great set-pieces and help the world feel more alive

The DLC gives you the Legendary fighter pack in which you are able to play as 3 legendary Yakuza in the Colosseum Only and also fight against them in Special Matches. Those being Goro Majima – The Mad Dog of Shimano, Taiga Saejima – The Slayer of Eighteen and Daigo Dojima – 6th Chairman of the Tojo Clan

My only gripes with this game is that there is no NG+, No Dragon of Dojima Outfit for Kiryu to wear + can't take off glasses and Staff Roll/Credits not being in the Replay Cut-scenes

I'd get the game if you want to see Kiryu's side of Yakuza 7/LAD and want to play the Demo for Like a Dragon 8: Infinite Wealth or wait for a sale to get the digital deluxe.\t\t\t\t\t
