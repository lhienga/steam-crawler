\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYakuza is one of my favorite game series of all time. It has its highs and lows, and even my least favorite game, Yakuza 5, is still something i would consider a very good game respectfully. Some people still deemed me a bit harsh torwards it, and honestly after playing this i'm realizing how right they were because ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

Like a dragon gaiden is the first game that was made post the departure of its usual game director, Toshihiro Nagoshi, and i didn't think it would make that much of a difference. My bad relationship with this game starts with what i have seen so far of Yakuza Infinite Wealth.

Hawaii as a setting is fun on paper but in execution? Yakuza is a very political series, regardless of how you want to see it. And the game has made its stance clear by putting how much emphasis on how it is part of the USA and you get to dress up as a cowboy to shoot down the natives while the party has no darker skinned characters to begin with. Oh and you're friend with Richardson now. When in Ishin released this year there was so much emphasis on how colonization was bad and you even slashed the ever loving ♥♥♥♥ out of his old time equivalent and his men. Its only made worse by having a animal crossing happy home paradise type game set in a real life area and its primarily done for tourists... around a time where a ♥♥♥♥ ton of natives of hawaii lost their homes. Kinda leaves a bad taste in your mouth, especially when you consider Ichiban was literally fighting his own brother for the rights of the less fortunate and minorities only a mainline game ago.

So that kinda soured my experience but i figured "infinite wealth isn't out yet and this new spin-off is coming soon, perhaps it will help contextualize some things and build my curiosity torwards where the story goes and make me want to check out infinite wealth after... Yakuza is a game i play for the story first and foremost after all... and uh... yeah even on that aspect the game is just not great.

I'll say the game's greatest strenght is the improvement on its combat, besides some weird changes, its good for the most part especially compared to past dragon engine RGG games. Sonic the fighters is fun too!

As for the negatives uh...
-If like me, Kiryu's part in yakuza 5 was your least favorite cause it just felt like kiryu torture porn making him miserable for the sake of it, that's this game's story from the get go. Forget the potential of starting with a clean slate with a new alias as a super agent. That goes out the window really ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ fast for the saaaaame tired ♥♥♥♥ of 'we need you back in the game'.
-I figured after 7 we went past the whole "chinese/korean people are evil" trope of early game but they really insisted on getting back to this ♥♥♥♥.
-Yakuza always had softcore porno stuff that was always exclusively optionnal. And most of it was usually in-universe and in-engine stuff that still keeps you somewhat immersed in the characters and the world you live in. Gaiden really really REALLY wanted you to go through their creepy live action recorded first person cabaret club where you have the least normal conversation possible with a porn actress and then put so much emphasis on how much of a degenerate and pervert you are when you literally cannot progress the story without going through it. That ♥♥♥♥ felt straight out of trash like 'the seducers' type game it wasn't fun nor immersive like it insisted so much on being.
-There are moments where Kiryu really feels out of character and has the most passive or neutral stance to the most horrible ♥♥♥♥ happening before him without any hint of disgust or remorse, like straight up slavery. And Kiryu is a christian that goes pray to a church now? Did i miss something?
-Most of the 'positive' of the story is just cheap underwhelming fanservice mean't to go "REMEMBER THIS GOOD THING THAT YOU LIKED?! REMEMBER IT?!" but in this case it feels more like a desperate way to keep your attention rather than actually making you meet some old friends

Just... tonally Gaiden (and to an extent, infinite wealth) feels a lot more like how Sega always pushed the marketing of the games rather than how the games actually were in practice/play. No matter how improved the combat or gameplay feels Yakuza is a series primarily carried by its characters, narrative and tone and right now it just feels so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ wrong and doesn't build me any confidence in the future of the series. I might revisit this game later and i could end up being wrong about 8 but its not particularily looking good for me so far.

And honestly trying to have any crititism or conversation about that leads to people going "ITS JUST A VIDEO GAME" or "STFU YAKUZA IS PEAK" which to me translates to "stop asking questions. Just consume the product and get excited for the next one to come out". SF6 had some yikes bits in its handling of its story and touchy subject but i did not expect street fighter to be a pinnacle in story telling. Yakuza i know can do better and its not looking this way. So anyway im going back to sf6 and wait for Ed to come out for more world tour adventures.\t\t\t\t\t

Minigames didn't change and that's good. They even brought back pocket-circuit racing. I won't cry missing baseball ;) Another colliseum like minigame with it's own story brings a lot of gameplay and if you buy the dlc, you will be able to play as Daigo for the first time with his OG theme. Story was shorter but it's hard to come up with something new since you know where kiryu story ended and where he was during 7 and yet they still managed that somehow. Karaoke is back with new songs. A lot of new well done and fresh characters. Akame is def. one of the best side-character they made. You get 2 fighting styles, could use one more for crowd control because agents style is kinda tough without heat. New cabaret club girls with real life videos unlike Yakuza 0. Side quest bring a lot of nostalgia from other, older games as well as Judgment series. Overall ? 9.5/10 could be longer. But hey Y:8 definetly will be. GOTY of 2023 for me.\t\t\t\t\t
