\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI mostly got this to check out the demo for the next Yakuza game. This one feels like an extended DLC that should've been part of another game rather than sold as it's own thing. I'm a few minutes into the demo for Infinite Wealth and already enjoying it more than I did the last half of Gaiden. I was just getting so frustrated at watching Kiryu/Joryu whiffing all his attacks instead of hitting anyone laying defenseless on the ground and then getting sucker punched by those enemies the second they stand back up. The more I played the game, the more the flaws in the combat system started to reveal themselves. Outside of using some of your Heat Gauge when a button prompt appears next to an enemy on the ground, most of your attacks and moves are going to whiff right over top of them. I really don't understand how it's so hard for Kiryu and all his allies to hit enemies lying on the ground and by the final chapter, I just wanted to finish the game already so I could check out the demo.

The story in this feels short and rushed. It's over in 5 chapters and most of the 5th chapter is an hour or so of consecutive fights and cutscenes. Plus it all takes place in one tiny map with no good means to avoid enemies when simply walking around given they spawn at almost every intersection and there's not enough space on the streets to run around and avoid them. I don't really play these games for their side mini games as much as I checked them out for the story and combat. This is only the third one I've played beside the turn based one and the samurai one. I liked both of those more than this one and the best thing I can say for this one is it made me wanna finish both of them too and check out the demo for the next one.

Don't get me wrong, the combat can be a lot of fun when things are flowing right, but then you or an ally will knock an enemy to the ground and then you just stand there throwing attacks that go right over their heads. The agent gadgets as well. Maybe I should've been leaning on Yakuza mode throughout the game more, but the agent gadgets were honestly a lot of fun throughout the whole game. The only thing that sucked about them is what sucks about the combat as a whole-defenseless enemies on the ground are so hard to hit and knock out.

If you can get this on a sale for like $20-40, I could see myself recommending people check it out cause it does have 20-40 hours of content here, but I imagine you might get frustrated with the combat halfway thru like I did and I had/have the Infinite Wealth demo to look forward to now that I've pushed thru to the end-without that motivation, I probably would've abandoned the game 10 or so hours ago and gone on to play something else.

I know some people say/think you have to know all the lore from previous games to appreciate the story in this one, but I don't think you do. I liked the story as much in this as I have in the other 2 even if I haven't finished either of them yet. Extra context can always be filled in later if I wanna play more of the older games. Dropping the game or at least considering it had nothing to do with the story in this game and everything to do with getting tired of the combat by that point. I think if there were mechanics in the game for your character and his allies to determine an enemy was lying on the ground and adjust their attacks and gadgets accordingly, it would've ended up being a lot more fun throughout and I might have been willing to keep working my way thru the Colosseum fights after beating the game. As it stands, though-even the combat in this feels rushed and not fully fleshed out-like it's meant to be part of a big expansion DLC that instead became it's own "full" game. I went into it pretty excited and am leaving slightly disappointed but with the hope that they could keep refining it and make it better at some point-although I don't know how they would do that.\t\t\t\t\t

The story in this game is really good, even if it is a bit short, the new characters are cool and the ending was incredibly sad.

The combat however... what were they thinking? This game is trying its hardest to be like LJ, and it fails in every single way. The launchers are very limited and inconsistent, and the air time of the juggle lasts for only 6 seconds.................. Why????? RGG also decided to remove enemy queuing, meaning that multiple enemies spam attacks while you're half a second into a combo, which is very infuriating and makes comboing in fights with multiple enemies basically impossible unless you spam a million Agent gadgets beforehand. Also the dodge in Agent is absolutely useless, it's pretty much the original Ishin's wildancer dodge.

RGG really fumbled this game. It is not worth $50 even if you exclude everything I said about the combat, get it on sale if you really must.\t\t\t\t\t

I could not recommend this game more.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory (no spoilers):
Definitely conscious of the character. This game does justice to Kazuma Kiryu's past, ideals, burdens and history.
It does an excellent job at feeling as a perfect focus for him, with its occasional callbacks to past games, with it not being too little, but not too much either as not to feel like complete fan service rather than a well founded game. So yeah, if you want a game, even if short (5 chapters in lenght only) that gives a gives a more proper send off to Kiryu, this is a entry you shouldn't skip by any means. (Why would you want to skip it, anyways?)
Kiryu has much more attitude than in many previous games, too.
The ending made me drop tears of both sadness and joy.

Gameplay:
The gameplay is, to put it simple, fun.
Agent, the new fighting style, is presented as a more tactical one, being able to overwhelm hordes of enemies. However, as you progress through the game, it becomes less than that to become more of a quick, easy way around finishing fights. And no, it's not because the character being bound to get stronger as the game progresses, but rather than it not being as tactical as presented, but more of a silly mix up of gadgets that justify the name of the fighting style, some of which are of quick and of more slow use, but all of them being able to finish the battle in a minute, or 8 seconds in most cases. It doesn't mean it's not fun, because it's the complete opposite, but it's definitely not a before and an after when it comes to battles styles in the series either.
Kiryu's signature Yakuza fighting style (now being the fighting style that you unlock as you progress a little further into the story) has some minimal changes that might not be for the convenience of the player too, like it being powerful at the price of being slow, and the beloved ¨finishing hold¨ move being stuck behind Heat Mode. Even so, this is the most fun it has been playing with Kiryu in the Dragon Engine so far, to say the least.

Duration and side stuff:
The main story is around 12 hours, but that was no relevant for me because of how the side content of the game does a wonderful job at stretching the game to around 30 hours.
Side missions. Customizable clothes. Minigames. Immersive hostesses. Pocket Circuit Races. Colisseum fights and the entirety of the Castle. Even being able to play as any of the characters you recruit to join your side at the colisseum.
All of that felt like a fitting part of the main story as I went through it.
What I'm trying to say, if you want the game to be longer and more enjoyable than what only 5 chapters sound like for a Like a Dragon/Yakuza game, all you need to go is to go out of your way and have fun. I cleared all of the colisseum fights with a S Grade and reached rank 30 in the Akame missions, and it was like having a buffet served in front of me that left me with a smile and conversations topics for the future.

DLC: Owning the Legendary Fighter Pack DLC is fun, but definitely not a must to overcome the colisseum. In case you think it will be defining to your enjoyement of the game if you don't own it because of how the characters there are some of the glorified cameos of the series. (Looking at Majima still spinning as the fight ends is fun, though).

Conclusion: Akame is very cute. Having the game's duration in mind might help you savor and enjoy the game and its events more. Buy the game on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
