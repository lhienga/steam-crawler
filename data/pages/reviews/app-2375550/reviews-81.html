\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing this game I can say that this was more than just an after thought for Kiryu and his story after 6, it feels like this was genuinely made to be from the start of 3. The amount of references to many of the games in the series along with meeting certain people from a certain similar series, shows that they really wanted to solidify this game into the series.

I highly recommend playing this only if you've experienced the game Yakuza: Like a Dragon and the games before, since many of the events that take place in this are related to key events of that game.

The combat feels more polished than what Kiryu was left with in 6, making him feel the best he's been since Yakuza Kiwami. Agent Style adds a new feel to the game, akin to hack and slashes with the way Kiryu mows through crowds of enemies using his new Spider gadget. Yakuza Style felt like a lite version of Kiryu's iconic Dragon style, which isn't bad since it is taking the backseat for Agent Style.

The game definitely feels like one you should complete in one playthrough, since the length isn't as long as the other titles in the series. The game starts off with many cutscenes, but you'll start to see more and more things unlock as it goes on. You'll be doing yourself a disservice if you rush through the game, as you won't get the most bang for your buck. The game has so much to offer with it's coliseum mode. It gives us a chance to experience the game outside of the main Yakuza cast. Not to mention if you beat the game, you can play the trial of the next game in the series.

Overall, the story alone was enough to keep me involved, but the substories and the new content kept me wanting to play more. Real Yakuza play Like a Dragon Gaiden: The Man Who Erased His Name\t\t\t\t\t
