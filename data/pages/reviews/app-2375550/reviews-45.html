\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game was more than I expected, pretty fun honestly. Originally felt that I regretted buying it (First time buying a game at full price) because of how small the chapters were, but with all those chapters and the story unfolding even more after the events of Yakuza 6, its still really worth buying. Even the sub-stories were enjoyable (🗣️🔥🗣️THE ♥♥♥♥ IS A NORMAL SUB-STORY??? 🗣️🔥🗣️) and is just overall fun to follow whatever the hell is happening. Long story short, the game made me go back to enjoying the series because of how much I missed it, and made me contemplate if I should play the entire Y0-Y6 Series again because I missed it quite a lot, and because of this game I might as well.


Although I still do have some nitpicks with the game, with how much they could have added even if its simple ones (Some are fixed with the mods you could download) One of them are some parts of the combat, while fun and interesting that you could literally just freaking juggle them in the air Tekken Combos style (Please Kiryu for T8) it felt really lackluster and feels like there could have been some other moves added (Installed Like A Dragon Gaiden Deluxe, which gives newer move and combo possibilities for his base move-set) and if even, add back the style switch that he had from Y0/YK1, another random combat related cope I got is how some of the heat actions are hella specific, like full hp and run into some enemy or something lol. Last other nitpicks are mostly end-game related, like why can't I fight the previous bosses again? (Again a mod that could be downloaded to fight the previous bosses was created) But overall? definitely worth the purchase, hella fun to play especially if you have your brains off just to have a good time beating up people you randomly see on the streets holding the strong attack to fly them across the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sky.




Oh and uh pocket racing is back 🚗 🔥🔥

I was hesitant about another game with Kiryu (Joryu) as the main character, but Gaiden proved that RGG Studio knows what they're doing with Kiryu's story even after Nagoshi's exit from the series. Gaiden made me really excited for Infinite Wealth, hoping it really delivers.

Good:
- Tight, shorter story compared to other Yakuza games. Gave me a punch in the gut in the ending.
- All new characters are memorable. Tsuruno my favourite pathetic man.
- Decent number of minigames.

Not so good:
- Agent seemed a little weak, two styles aren't as fun Lost Judgment's 3 style combat.
- Having to grind coliseum.
