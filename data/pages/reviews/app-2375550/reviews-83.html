\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have conflicting feelings on this game. It's a really good send off to Kiryu's entire journey, 0 through 7, so if you've played all of the games it is worth it for that alone.

Most of my issues come from its combat being a downgrade from Lost Judgment. You can't really seamlessly switch styles as easily as LJ allowed you to do, it's better played similarly to the old games where you switch styles depending on what the situation demands.

The enemies also have an obnoxious amount of super armor (cannot be stunned) so Agent style feels really bad if you try to use its combo strings, Yakuza style practically carries the entire combat until you get upgrades later on.

Other than the weirdly paced start of the game, the story hits it out of the park in hindsight for me. It might allow Kiryu to be phased out and allow Ichiban to finally flourish after Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth. The final boss is basically Kiryu beating up the representation of most of the struggles he had to face in the series.

I think the game is a bit overpriced for how short it is compared to the rest of the games, but at the same time if you've played every other game it's also a must-play. Get it on sale or just buy it idk\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game really feels like the twilight of Kiryu's life and it does it in a way that doesn't overstay it's welcome. The game doesn't overstay it's welcome with an artificially extended play time. This game feeds you the story that brings Kiryu between LAD 6 and 7 and sets him up for his story in 8 (which I suspect will truly be his last as a fairly literal passing of the torch). You go back to all of the best minigames. You get callbacks and references to some of the best highlights of the games prior. It really feels like the entire game is Kiryu looking back at his life and experiences and taking it all in one last time. I'd recommend it even without prior LAD experience, but like. If you played the others, this one is a must. It really does feel like a love letter from RGG to the franchise that made them what they are today.\t\t\t\t\t
