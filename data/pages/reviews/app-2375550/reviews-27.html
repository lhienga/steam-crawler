The Man Who Wasn't Able to Erase His Name
Not for 50$, no. Game felt just as bad as Kiwami 1 mixed with Yakuza 3.
Yakuza 8 trial was tempting but after i finished Gaiden that trial felt as RGG+Sega were like "hey, at least you get Yakuza 8 trial" *crickets chirping*.
If you bought both Gaiden and Yakuza 8 you paid twice for Yakuza 8. 'Like a dragon: Gaiden' should be free or paid DLC for Yakuza 8 like 'Majima saga' was put into Kiwami 2.

Game feels really rushed. Imagine weird child of Kiwami 1, Yakuza 3 (mostly bad storytelling) with some things from Kiwami 2, Yakuza 6 and Judgement. These were my overall impressions.
If i wasn't familiar with Yakuza series and Gaiden was my first game i'd drop it after playing first chapter. Or even maybe first hour, because:
  1. things that could be optional and put as substories were obligatory to progress main story. Just like stupid missions with kids in Yakuza 3. This dumb intentional dragging.
  2. top notch dialogues like:
    - you're Kazuma Kiryu
    - no, i'm Joryu
    - you're Kiryu
    - no, i'm telling you
    - dude...
    - bro, i swear
    - tell me one more time you're not Kiryu i gonna beat your ass
    - okay
  3. nothing much to do besides Colliseum. Trailer looked promising with karaoke and cabaret, but reality is disappointing. Almost.
In the second chapter things get better.

Fights using agent style felt less soy as fights in Judgement games. You have gadgets that actually are fun to use against crowds. Drones suck and useless, explosive cigs are fun and chaotic. Yakuza style is okay, but i didn't like that you can evade only when you're locked on an enemy. And i couldn't figure out how to switch targets. Also switching styles was completely inconsistent and unresponsive. Animations prevent you from switching styles and this was really annoying when you're in the middle of a big fight.
There're Colliseum fights. They're more likely fun than not. Fighting crowds of weak or medium enemies was okay. But when tanky or just strong enemies come out, fights turn into cheap simulator of:
  • trying to punch through enemy's blocks
  • trying to hit evading enemy. Specially with guns (remember Kiwami 1 boss fights?)
  • waiting until enemy's phase wears off, because if it's on you can't deal damage or deal very little damage
  • incosistent damage. That Justice guy either one-shot-kills-you with his laser, either he just deals some damage
  • 934856 health bars

As for other minigames, they're just lazy copypaste from previous games:
  1. Karaoke. Okay, but 1 new song
  2. Cabaret. You're guest again like in Yakuza 6 but now hostess are recorded live on videos. Probably because it was easy to produce than fiddle with in-engine cutscenes. Overall impression is okay, i guess. But quantity over quality crops out
  3. Pocket circuit. Nothing changed at all. You spend more time navigating through obsolete menus than actually playing the game. And it is still random number generator
Substories are nominal. Most stories are non-brainers like fetching stuff to people as it was in Yakuza 3 or doing some 3-minute substories like "go and beat somebody" or "wander to some random point several times and talk to people or fight and substory ends"

Main story makes almost no sense. Everybody still knows that you're Kazuma Kiryu in Gaiden and even in Yakuza 8. I guess writers just had to pull story in Gaiden towards Yakuza 8. Ending should've bring some tears but i legit laughed out loud when Taichi saw camera and started telling story of his life thinking it's camera for Kiryu (camera shows many graves, not just Kiryu's).
