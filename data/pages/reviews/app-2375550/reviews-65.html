As someone who's played all the Yakuza titles, starting with Yakuza 7 Like a dragon, then 0-6, and with the Judgment games, I absolutely felt so emmersed and invested into the game at the very start to the very last moments. When I've seen the trailers for LAD Gaiden and seeing it's gameplay, features, new additions along with us figuring out where Kiryu went after the ending of 6, it was one of those games that I simply HAD to pre order and play on day one! And I'm so happy I did.

(WARNING: SLIGHT SPOILERS TO PREVIOUS GAMES BUT KEPT TO A MINIMUM, NO REAL SPOILERS TO THE STORY)
For those wondering, LAD Gaiden takes place after the story of Yakuza 6, and during the events of Yakuza 7 Like a Dragon, where we figure out and follow the story as to where Kiryu went and disappeared to after the events of 6, and how he ended up in 7 Like a Dragon. 3 Years after the Y6 you understand quick and clear why Kiryu did what he had to do to keep his loved ones safe and erase his name. His challenges and trials during the game to keeping his situation a secret as he's supposed to be a dead man. Only for him to realize Yakuza guys can't take a hint or keep a secret.

The story as always is heaven sent, with clear and very concise understand everything without having to go back. It sucks you in just like every single game they've made for Yakuza. The boss battles and long battles are always a joy to get into, and you feel like a kid in a candy shop just from doing the wacky side quests. And upgrading Kiryu with his Agent and his Legendary Yakuza style was a pure joy grinding for and then taking it into battle with some hooligans on the streets.

There is only 5 chapters but of course there is no sense of this game feeling short at all. There is plenty to do, as long as you are not in a point of a story where you can't go out and explore. They will make that clear, but you can really take your time with the game without feeling like you have to prioritize anything. Here's just a taste of what you can do.

*Pocket Circuit Racing (Yes it's back for our 50 year old Kiryu XD)
*Fight in The Castle Coliseum (Yes it returns and it's so easy to make money for upgrades)
*Karaoke (Real fan service here with references to the past Yakuza titles)
*Akame Requests (which is reskinned wacky side quests but the wackiness and humor is still here. With a TON of references and special guest appearances from past games and even from Judgment.)
*Buy Clothes for Kiryu (Coliseum, Free Roam and Story)
*Go to the Casinos
*And of course give knuckle sandwiches to street punks

I also wanted to speak a bit on the final chapter without spoiling anything. It was the perfect tie in and ultimate way to finish off the game with preparing for the Omi Alliance and Tojo Clan disbandment. Main reason why I love this so much is because I got to see Ichiban and his friends there, and (in a way) seeing them fighting side by side protecting Watase and Daigo. I was totally fanboying from start to finish, from the moment the camera pans into the room before his speech just seeking and looking for Ichiban like it's Where's Waldo, to seeing Kiryu come in with that punch and remove his glasses to Ichiban, Maejima and Saejima. Seeing this through Kiryu's perspective and seeing the dragon himself meet his successor and equal, along with that heart filling smirk Kiryu gives to Ichiban when he saw him reveal his dragonfish back tattoo really helped make this game for me. And to end it all off with a epic long battle with the Yakuza legends, and then to finish off, a boss battle to end the game that will have you remember the events and villain of Yakuza 5. Just a big chef kiss.

And of course, Kiryu's breakdown and him crying at the closing moments of the game was enough to bring me to tears just like everyone else. We all want Kiryu to be happy and he deserves it and the world 10x over for his heart of gold and influence on others. I'm hoping there will be some closure and happy ending to his story, but I'm already looking forward to Infinite Wealth so much.

In conclusion, RGG has made yet another 10/10 classic for the Yakuza franchise. THIS, is what gaming is about, and what AAA gaming should be. No bugs, god sent story, ultimate fan service, never ending action, fun and remarkable gameplay, and no micro transactions or nickle and diming fan-bases. From the bottom of my heart, thank you RGG. You guys deserve the whole world and more for making incredible games. I pre ordered this game from you guys, took a day off work just to play it, ordered pizza and I didn't regret it not one bit.

I'm already putting down my pre-order for Like a Dragon Infinite Wealth and it's going to be probably the best birthday gift (or in my case belated Birthday gift since my bday is on the 22nd of January) I will ever receive. I'm looking forward to seeing Ichiban and Kiryu side by side together against the evils they will face.

Thank you for all the memories and heart gripping stories RGG! Never change!\t\t\t\t\t
