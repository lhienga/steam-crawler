\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly if you've played other games in the series you probably know what you're getting into. I'd recommend this if you're familiar with most of the 0-6 games. If you've only played 7 then you'll definitely get a bit out of it but you'll be missing a significant bit of broader context that makes the whole thing meaningful and sometimes emotionally significant for both the main character and the player. Having played most of the series, I was actually tearing up at the ending. I wouldn't really recommend this as a starting point. The story has a few warts but nothing that made me want to stop playing the game.

Aside from that, this might be the best version of the "Virtual Tour" component that the series is famous for. The collectibles and side missions really encourage the player to observe the environment much more actively than in previous games. I felt like I noticed more details and there were several instances where the game rewarded me for paying attention to the scenery.

Combat is good but I personally feel like every game has its good and so-so design choices. This one does more right than wrong but I'm not sure that its my favorite iteration of the combat system. At the end of the day it maintains the fun core of what makes these games great: combat that can be brutal or hilariously absurd.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10/10 Story, 8/10 Gameplay - Overall 9/10.

Outstanding game - As someone who's played the entire series, this is a love letter to fans, and those who've followed Kiryu's story from his life as a young punk to his life as a surrogate father to his kids. I am sobbing at the ending, and dear god I am not ready for Infinite Wealth.

You really should wait and play the other games to understand Kiryu's story before this - it makes a lot of the callbacks and some of the fights themselves have just that much more oomph to them. The combat sadly is not as smooth as Lost Judgment, despite Kiryu's style switching and Yakuza style being as brutal and weighty as ever. Thankfully, juggling is back and Kiryu can do it, which is honestly pretty awesome.

The story though, wow. Remarkable, and while it may have some filler, it was engaging to me from start to finish. The last act was a highlight, and it def shows the writers and whole team are much better at crafting a proper ending to their stories. I don't think I've cried that hard over a game in years.

Will always be a fan of RGG Studios, and hope to see more and more from them as the years go on. Just make the Yakuza style hit slightly faster FFS.\t\t\t\t\t
