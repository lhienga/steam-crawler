\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an amazing game. This game is the pure Like a Dragon/Yakuza experience without all the failing of the other games. The combat is very good, importing many of the improvements made in the Judgment series and altering it to fit Kiryu's gameplay. Juggling and mortal reversals make a return, while Extreme Heat has become very akin to EX Boost. The only thing I would complain about in combat is there is a fairly low amount of heat actions, but the ones they brought into this game are excellent. The progression is pretty good, returning to using money and the new Akame Points to buy upgrades. Bringing money back into skills make it really fun and interesting to actually earn money, due to its direct impact on gameplay now.

The story is amazing. It ties into Yakuza: Like a Dragon but from the perspective of Kiryu, giving us a fresh look at the events of that game, while also tying up Kiryu's story, since Yakuza 6's bittersweet ending left something to be desired. All of the new characters in this game are to become instant favorites, they did an amazing job with the cast of this game.

Visually this game is beautiful. The game is visually darker than other Dragon Engine titles, but they also use more highlights and brighter colors to offset the darker shading present, giving us one of the most striking titles yet. All the UI and menus look sleek as well. Everything is expertly shaded, animated, modeled, and rendered to the point where they would splice in real-life footage cause they were so confident. The quality of the visuals is so good that there are no more pre-rendered cutscenes, the in-game just looks that good now.

The game length is about 1/3 of a full Yakuza title. Not the worst thing though, since it bypasses the long, confusing stories of the previous game with a short one that does everything that it needs to. Plus, if you need more game to play, there is still tons of side content to dig your teeth into. All of the side content in this game got an expansion (except for golf, sorry DJ Khaled). The Colosseum is tons of fun, with a huge amount of fights to do. Pocket circuit is back, with more races than ever to do. The Akame Network side content is filled with either well-written personal stories, or silly one-offs to fight homeless guys or convenience store clerks to recruit to your army. The cabaret minigame is all live-action 4K footage, which takes 23.4 gigabytes of space on your computer sadly.

OST is full of bangers, its on Spotify if you want to listen after you play this game. Game reuses some songs from Yakuza 0 and Judgment/Lost Judgment for substory music, which is kinda funny. All the karaoke tracks in this game are good, some being brand new and some being remastered ones from previous games.

Overall, an amazing game. Taking the best from the previous games while improving on the shortcomings of previous Kiryu games makes this a must-play for Yakuza/Like a Dragon fans. Can not recommend this game enough. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
