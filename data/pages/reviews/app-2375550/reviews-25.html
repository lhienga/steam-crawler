\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know what black magic RGG Studio worked on this game to get it to run so perfectly but this is so fantastically optimized even including DLSS all while boasting very nice visuals around Sotenbori. This leaves me very hopeful for 8 especially after 7 didn't run especially well on my PC.

Beyond the performance, the gameplay was another area that blindsided me. Besides 7, the only other Yakuza game I have played was 0 and I really didn't jive with the combat; it didn't click and I really didn't find it fun. But Gaiden is fantastic and so smooth, it really just feels super slick to play. I think I can attribute that to the increase in tools you have as Kiryu in his Agent style; that and the fact that boss fights are super responsive.

By and far the best part was the story as is typically king in my Yakuza experiences so far. The only lull was the forced Akame Network level and Coliseum Rank; I don't like forced side content especially when it messes with pacing. Everything outside of that was fantastic and tells a really great story of what Kiryu has been up to. It really is a testament to how much love RGG puts into this series with how much time and effort they are willing to give to this side story. A worthy note; the end of the game has a real series standout moment that had me crying like a baby; just another example of how well written this series is.

Again didn't really engage too heavily in the side content beyond what was required to progress the main story but there was a good amount here for a spinoff and everything I touched was quality.\t\t\t\t\t
