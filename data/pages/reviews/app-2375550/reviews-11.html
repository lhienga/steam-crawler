\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoved the game, bought it on sales. Would 100% wait for the sale. In terms of a reboot, it's clever, fun and feels more like a teaser rather than a full fledged Yakuza game.

I would say that the MSQ is probably 7-8h. The rest is substories, grinding the "Castle" which has a Coliseum, which in itself is not new aspect but like each Yakuza game, there's some level of mini game that makes you stupid rich. It's the prettied Coliseum of most Yakuza game in my opinion. You can use the taxi to get on it, which I felt was breaking my immersion that it's a boat in international waters. The critique I've read so far is that you can play with other character in the Coliseum but their moves/fluidity is far from being fleshed out and is by far a let down.

As for the story, I feel it's pretty good, this feels like a kind of "Multverse" where Kiryu looks not older than 35 yo but this appears to be set in the future when the kids from the orphanage are in their early 20's. The vilains are mid, the overall dept of the Yakuza line of story is not good. Well to be fair, it's still pretty fresh, I like that Kiryu works as Joryu a covert agent which works for some covert government agency also collaborating with the CIA... Kind of sexy spy who get in to leave the life of crime, protect is children while pretending to be dead. (an absolute easy card pulled each time in Yakuza).

So overall the structure of the game is good. The fluidity of the fighting system - the way it's been kind of dumb down is still very fun. The gadgets are super fun and OP AF. The levelling can be pretty much fully done by chapter 4. Which is 2/3 into the game. Doing all the substories unlock some nice stuff including the 2nd point system needed to level up. So they are kind of extremely important.

Like every Yakuza game, we are in extremely familiar territory. The mini-game are stale and basically feels like a copy-paste of all the universe and feels lazy. It's a plus and a con. The pocket circuit comes back and it's a blast! The datacom/SEGA console old games are ok but when I play Yakuza I'll admit, I'm not interested in that. The Karaoke is also not scalable (no difficulty level adjustment); I suck at it! Still enjoyable. The Cabaret is ok. It's a film cutscene of JAV actress, which is OK I guess.

Like in every Yakuza game, you have a side partner which here it's Akame who was a ok new addition but didn't felt like a mark in the Franchise. Meaning, I wouldn't care if she's not in the next game. She share a drink with you, she talks about why she's doing what she does. There's an attempt to build a rapport, but only feels like a tick in a box to achieve a level of narration, rather than an ally that's actually OP (like all past close allies usually are in Yakuza game) that you find either funny, interesting or add tons of fun to the game.

By the time you're ready to do the final chapter and you've completed S rank all of the coliseum, gotten all the sexy expensive gear, you've filled up in potions and food. You've had some fun challenges in the Coliseum, you're thinking, Let's freaking go!! Last Boss, Last chapter, action pact +100 low level yakuza, with 30 mini boss and 5-6 big boss fight... Right? Nope! It's OK but it's just not good enough, In fact after you're done with it and you're like... Oh... Ok.

There's No New Game + , much MUCH shorter than other games. The added features are mid and overall the polishing of the scenes and the custscene as still very good. It's a solid 6, low 7/10 - Still pretty ok
