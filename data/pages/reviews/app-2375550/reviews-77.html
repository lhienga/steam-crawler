Hits you hard in the feels...you will cry at certain points in the story. Combat is also very dynamic and fun, which really makes you wish, that upcoming games of the series will pick up this kind of combat system again, because the next title will be a turn based one.

Well, what can i say: It's a Yakuza/Like a Dragon Game: Amazing story, great setting and combat system and a lot of stuff to do that keps you from progressing the main story, because you have such a good time doing stuff and ignoring the fact, that you have important things to do, that could cost or save lifes...but well, playing Pocket Circuit is mandatory. Oh yeah, an finally you can change (and create your own) outfits during the main story and not only in Premium Adventure...finally! But why this late in the series?!

Looking forward to Infinite Wealth and the continuation of the Story. Also: If you didn't play all the other Yakuza/Like a Dragon games yet - play them in chronological order, before you play this game. Otherwise you would ruin the experience AND miss out on hours and hours of amazing games, with some of the best storylines in the entire video game industry.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverview:

Depending on how you look at it, this is either a low-effort dlc-into-full-game spinoff or part of a proper send-off of the Kiryu Era or objectively both.

Part of the reason why some would buy this game is that it is possible RGG will move on from the older Yakuza action games as they continue onwards in the Ichiban era, but moving on seems hard for both the developer and the players.

Supporting this game might send a message they should do more action spinoffs like this and Judgment now that turn-based is the MAIN game.

Regardless - the point of this game is that it is intended as a final sendoff for those who were invested into the older games and an excuse to make people pay for Kiryu. You get backstory of Kiryu addressing events from Yakuza 6 and Y:LAD and a linkage + demo to Infinite Wealth.

Spoiler Advisory for old and new players:

To be clear, this is game for the fans from the Kiryu era. It is heavy on references that need most players to play those game to appreciate them.

If you are new, ensure you played at least Yakuza: Like a Dragon before you play this game to avoid spoiling yourself. Knowledge of Yakuza 6 is recommended but not optional.

While you should play this game before Infinite Wealth, but it isn't *strictly necessary* based on the story I seen for myself. Not everything about Kiryu in IW is addressed in this game but if you love Kiryu anyway, just play this game before IW if you can.

Why you should play this game (slight spoilers):

- You are a big RGG/Kiryu fan like me
- A lot of references to older games. Probably most of all the Kiryu Y0-Y6 games.
- You enjoy the fan service, familiar minigames and familiar 4-king-something subplots
- You enjoy dressing up Kiryu
- You think live-action girls cabaret is awesome (meh)
- And you think it's cool to play as an Agent (it's a little absurd and tech superhero-like)
- You want to see the latest Sotenbori (it's the ONLY main map in this game. the rest don't really count)
- You want to try the Infinite Wealth demo (unlocks after completing game)
- You are okay with the pricing

Why you should wait for a sale or skip for Infinite Wealth (again, slight spoilers):

- You expect new and fresh things. There's not much 'new things' aside from agent style and cabaret. The main driving point of this game is just narrative and sentimentalism. No new minigames, just a reboot of Pocket Circuit. And a Coliseum.
- It's quite obvious this game was indeed finished in 6 months. Most of the stuff here are carried from older games. Some parts feel abit unpolished. Just adjust your expectations accordingly.
- There's only one completely new karaoke song. Horrifying!
- There's half of the side stories, and only 1/4 of them is interesting (imho). I never really liked the four-king subplot but here it is, occupying 2/5 of the substories.
- It's too expensive for half the content of any Yakuza game
- You aren't interested in the minigames, sidestories, coliseum, challenges, etc. Then you probably will get even less value of this and probably can finish the game in 2-3 days.

Also the Judgment crossover is one of the bigger sidestories. But it has no dedicated voiced lines for story, just recycled grunting. Google yourself to find out more.

I love crossovers but this one just feels abit meh. Consider it as bait.

Side note on Difficulty:

Dev tries to make the game more accessible through 'action assist' and a 'combat tip' that show things that the game think is helpful. The video is ok (abit annoying) and can be toggled off, but the action assist doesn't work very well, trigger things without your consent and makes you unable to learn the combat, manage your heat bar or fight unpredictably.

Recommend most people to just play normal mode at start. The economics of this game is easy enough for you to eat regularly and prep enough heals.

TLDR: Only Real Yakuza/Kiryu Fans should buy this game. Everyone else should wait for a sale or play any or the older games, or just invest in Infinite Wealth. That game is likely to be rad based on the demo I played.\t\t\t\t\t
