\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike a Dragon Gaiden: The Man Who Erased His Name might have the longest name in the franchise but is the shortest game by a long shot. Taking me 23 hours to complete with side content this game is a blast! Fun combat, intriguing story that has a great ride all the way to the end. One of the best boss fights the game has ever seen and of course the coliseum. Fight as Majima or even Chicken Head. Also shout out to the sonic fighting game in the arcade!

Gameplay:

+New fighting style is great fun big shout out to the spider whip.
+Mini games galore, some of my faves are here, Pocket Circuit and all the sega arcade games are not to be missed.
+Small Map makes it easy to traverse for smaller side content.
+Progression is hella fast.
/Coliseum is so much fun but a little short.
- Some Side content is boring and just fetch questy.
-Some battles are too easy!

Story:
+Kiryu is back
+Funny dialogue
+Great heart wrenching ending
-Middle falls flat due to its shortness, the stakes arent as high as other yakuza games.

Graphics:
+Some scenes look great and the game runs very well on PC and especially steam deck! (able to plug external 1080p monitor and still hit 40fps)
+Colours a vibrant and pop out the screen.
-Feels the same to all yakuza games since 6.
-Some NPC's look off.

Overall:
8/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic entry to the series taking the form of a love letter to RGG games past and present. Taking place 4 years after Yakuza 6 and alongside Like a Dragon 7, this offers a story explaining Kiryu's disappearance and eventual role he plays in Like a Dragon 7. The story is enjoyable and does a great job of making me anticipate his crossroads with Kasuga Ichiban.

Everything hits all time high notes of the series. The models and graphics are fantastic. The detail is extraordinary without feeling uncanny valley. Facial animations, skin and cloth detail, everything. The music fits and enhances scenes and fights as great music should. Some bangers are Deadly Struggle, Psycho's Anthem and Soul of the Ogre.

The fighting is as good as ever. Utilising Kiryu's usual Dragon of Dojima style being the powerhouse and a new Agent style, there's plenty of ways to switching things up and delivering a creative beatdown. Agent style had some fun gimmicks like gadgets and essentially being similar to the Rush style of the past, whenever you use a gadget (save for the Spider) it slows the combat to a halt.

It's weak point is content. Don't get me wrong, it has PLENTY of content. The usual fare like pub games and arcade games, gambling, etc return. Cabaret dates also come back. The absolute GEM of a game that is Pocket Racing makes it's return as well. Sidestories take the form of the Akame Network. But the game is half the usual length. It's on a comparably smaller map taking place in Sotenbori. I could easily clock in 40 hours in the beginning messing around with quests and games but the whole game took that long to finish.

Overall a great package, I think a more fitting price would be 40$ CAD when comparing it to the content we get of other games, but it still offers a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
