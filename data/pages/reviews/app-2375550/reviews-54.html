The story is your typical Yakuza charade. It starts strong, falls flat in the middle and gets really hype towards the end. It also expects you to have finished Yakuza 3, 6 and at least LaD, although some Side content is better with the knowledge of 0, 1, Kiwami 2 and Judgment / Lost Judgment.

The feeling of the Combat is on par with Lost Judgment, although the complexety is not. Joryu has access to Agent and Yakuza Style. Agent is a new one, faster than Yakuza, but deals less damage. You also get access to Gadgets in combat, each bound to one of the Face-buttons of your controller. Yakuza style is slower but allows to charge attacks. The game also features a coliseum that allows to fight as the Peapole you recruit, each with their own movesets, but without any heat actions. On that note: Majima, Saejima and Daigo had their moveset severely reduced from previous games.
The game isn't very long. I've finished it in around 30h with all Side content besides Amon.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy expectations were pretty low considering how short the Kaito Files from Lost Judgment was and the devs statements on the length of the game being compared to that made me think $50 was pretty steep for it. But funnily enough this is probably my game of the year despite its length. With my only real gripe being the lack of NG+ for it, as I think the lack of it hurts the replayability especially with its shorter length. A first time playthrough (watching cutscenes and all) would probably take about 7 hours give or take. Hitting the Platinum only took about 25 hours (one of the easiest plats I've achieved honestly.)

The story (without getting into spoilers) takes place during Yakuza 7. And is pretty much the events of that game through Kiryu's eyes. And was far more emotional than I had ever expected it to be.

The combat reminds me a lot of the first Judgment game. With the 2 fighting styles focusing on crowd control and 1 on 1s respectively. Agent is like Crane where it focuses on crowd control (albeit more with gimmicky gadgets and not Yagami kicking everything.) And "Yakuza" being the slow, but hard hitting 1 on 1 style. Unlike Judgment though, the CC style is actually really useful in battles with bigger groups what with its speed, bombs, rocket shoes and wire grab. Kiryu can air combo now, and it's actually really cool setting up into them and getting creative with what you can do.

I was surprised with how much they actually managed to do with a game that supposedly only took half a year to make. But with how the studio does really well with their "less is more" approach to their open worlds, I guess I shouldn't be too shocked.

The story is very emotional with good fan service moments throughout, and we see Kiryu in a couple different ways we hadn't seen before. The combat is fluid and allows for a lot of creativity and shenanigans (though yakuza could be a lil faster) and the side activities are like a "Best of" collection from the series. This is probably one of my favorites in the series despite being like a third of the size.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t150 words or less review:

Gameplay:
An action-RPG at its heart, Like a Dragon Gaiden combines its seasoned-past with their newer IP, Judgment, to craft an engaging and combo-orientented brawler. While there could be room for a little more refining in a specific fight style, gameplay never felt boring and there is always something new to do. Even if the player doesn't love the fighting mechanics, rhythm-based karaoke, pocket circuit racer, shogi, darts, pool, and much more offers a variety that makes this game addicting to play from start to finish.

Graphics:
With AAA games making it harder to stand out from the crowd, LaD still manages to capture the world and its characters with very little infidelity. Facial expressions, pores on a face, and detailed landscapes makes this game still beyond worthy to look at. The only aspect stopping it right now from being more "modernized" is raytracing

Music:
Not "gotta-download-this-album" worthy, but still music that purposefully matches the tone of the moment,

Story:
Long-time fans will love the story of Kiryu's tale into obscurity. The questions of legacy and if a legacy is worth it if the person cannot witness it all unfolds and proves to be the Dragon of Dojima's strongest chracter study yet

Score: 8/10 (One of RGG studio's best action-rpgs yet, with a story that will satisfy longtime fans and motivate them to see what comes next)\t\t\t\t\t
