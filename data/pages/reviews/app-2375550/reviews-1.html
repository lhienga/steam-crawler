\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my least favorite Yakuza game, but still worthwhile for hardcore fans like myself. I give this a thumbs down in part because I do not believe this game deserves to have an "Overwhelmingly Positive" reception, especially compared to other entries in this series. I still enjoyed aspects of this game and found it decent overall, but there are simply too many problems to ignore.

The story has its funny moments and the ending is a highlight for Kiryu's character, but everything prior to the final segment of the game is kinda lackluster. The Daidoji faction is unbelievably incompetent and put "Joryu" in the public eye an awful lot considering they want the world to believe he's dead. Another problem is that the antagonists are undercooked and, despite being powerful and cool, aren't particularly well-written. The "Nishitani III" character is blatant fanservice and doesn't really have much going for him aside from being a rehash of the Nishitani character from 0, the connection between the two never even being elaborated on. Just in general, this game does a lot of info dumping about its characters' backstories and it feels really lazy. I'll stop my story and character critique here to avoid any major spoilers, but suffice to say, this is among the weakest in the Yakuza series aside from the last, like, 45 minutes.

The side content is quite mixed as well. The "substories" in this game are not organically stumbled upon in the world, but rather must be initiated by visiting Akame and sitting through an introductory dialogue to what's going on. However, the substories themselves are still quite funny, entertaining, and occasionally heartfelt as always, so it isn't that big of a deal for me.

What's more of an issue is the smaller fetch quests where people on the street will ask for random items, food, photos, or even just grabbing an object out of reach. I've always loved the Yakuza series for doing side content like substories that don't feel like complete and utter chores, and exist mainly just for entertainment value. Gaiden's MMORPG-esque fetch quests stand in direct opposition to everything I loved about the side content in this series. Other games have had bad or mediocre side content before, but in Gaiden's case they are especially egregious because they're tied to both combat upgrades and even story progression in some cases. ("We gotta level up the Akame Network before we do the next thing, Joryu!")

Speaking of combat upgrades, yikes. Not a big fan of the upgrade system here. Granted, it is better than the Yakuza 6 and Kiwami 2 system, but not by much. For one, I just think it's dumb that there are two different currencies to keep track of when upgrading. Why do I need to use Akame Network points AND actual money? The money doesn't even make sense like it did in 0, which took place during the height of Japan's economy in the 80s. I've already gotta spend loads of money just upgrading the Akame network to begin with, anyway. Not to mention side activities like outfit customization, cabaret clubs, and pocket circuit racing. It doesn't help that this game lacks a New Game+ feature, either. Although considering RGG's recent practices they'd probably charge $15 for it anyway.

Oh, and speaking of RGG charging you for things that they shouldn't, what's up with the 3Jima DLC? I was genuinely excited to preorder knowing I could play as them again in the arena, but they're so limited. The basic rush combos are mostly there, albeit feel way worse than their OG gameplay, aside from Daigo who has only ever been a boss character. Perhaps the worst part though is that they can't perform heat moves or do any special attacks. You charge $8 for three characters that can only do rush combos? The animations for which are just copied over from other games? Yikes. At least we have modders to fix that.

The last thing I'll touch on is combat. Kiryu's gameplay feels smoother than it did in any other Dragon Engine game, that's for damn sure. If I'm being honest, I barely used Agent style because that gadgets he uses kinda suck and don't control well. The rocket boots are clunky. Drones are practically useless unless you upgrade their durability (there are a million other upgrades I'd rather spend my points and yen on anyway) and even then it isn't worth the trade-off of leaving yourself vulnerable to attacks while calling in your drones in the first place. The only gadget I ever bothered using was the wire for crowd control and initiating a juggle combo in case I wanted to mess around with that. However, I mostly just stuck to Dragon style as it did better damage and it was the style that had more appealing upgrades. While this is definitely an improvement from previous Dragon Engine games, I still think RGG has failed to surpass the combat in the older engines. 4, 5, 0, and Kiwami are still superior both in terms of variety and overall smoothness. (I also liked three's combat more compared to 6 and K2 but I know that's a bit of a hot take.)

So TL;DR, the story is all over the place but the ending is fantastic, the new antagonists are cool but poorly written, the side content was a mixed bag, the DLC is not worthwhile, the upgrade system is improved from 6/K2 but far from perfect, and the combat was smooth albeit still not as good as Kiryu's pre-Dragon Engine combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9,5/10 Wonderful game!

I had so many questions when I played Yakuza: Like a Dragon. Like "What was Kiryu doing before chapter 12?", "Where was he?", "Is he okay?", etc.
Most of my questions are solved with this game.

It's a wonderful spin-off game that takes place after Yakuza 6 and during Yakuza 7. It also offers a preview of Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth (e.g. why the story takes place in Hawaii).

The story is just as emotional as the last Yakuza games. The graphics look almost realistic. And I love the gameplay. Finally we get the good ol' beat 'em up game that almost feels like a Dynasty Warriors game. XD

The only thing I don't like about this game is that the game is too short compared to older Yakuza games. Only 5 chapters. You can play through the game in 10-12 hours.
Therefore, I would advise you not to buy the game for the full price. It's only worth it if the game costs less than 20 euros. Unless you really want to experience the story of Kiryu and not watch it on YouTube. Then you can get it.

Overall:
To sum up, I can tell you that the game is very good. However, it feels like a DLC for Yakuza: Like a Dragon.

You can also play the demo version of Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth, which is also interesting. If you don't want to have the demo version on your PC because it takes up too much disk space (25 GB, iirc), you should delete the Runtime2 folder in the game's installation folder.

I look forward to playing more of your older games. ^^
