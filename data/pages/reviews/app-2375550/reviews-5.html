The story, the gameplay, the seriousness, the normal SANE characters — this is what I've been missing a long time.
I completed it with pleasure.

The next game in the series turned out to be "The Man Who Erases His Name".
And, oh my...
This was brilliant.
Mature, serious story about real human beings. Well-shot, well-directed, and well-written. With LOTS of interesting activities.
The game and story, that modern sh*tmakers from american or european companies will NEVER make.
I wanted to stay in this world as long as it's possible, to play through the main story, complete all the interesting sidequests, perhaps even get all the achievements.

Yesterday, I completed the main story and felt a little emptiness inside. I want more.
And fortunately for me - there IS more.
Feels like I've discovered a whole new world.


9/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike a Dragon Gaiden is an amazing, short but well-polished title, considering it took only six months of game development.

Similar to other fans of the series, it was a joy to return back to an action brawler genre game with Kiryu ("Joryu") at the helm as the main protagonist. The new agent style, probably inspired from James Bond, was quite unique and interesting to play as, especially when clearing large mobs. Though for most of the game's key bosses, the tried and tested yakuza style with heavy hitting moves still held strong, especially with pretty cool juggling physics thrown into the mix too.

Another key feature was the upgraded coliseum which many agree must be the best iteration in the series thus far. It didn't felt like a chore as each mode has its own unique challenges, which kept things interesting. I thoroughly enjoyed the chance to recruit other fighters and form my own team to fight in the large scale battles. Definitely a thumbs up to also be able to include and even control the legendary yakuza fighters - daigo, majima and saejima, in these arena battles.

Substories were interestingly woven with the unique Akame network storyline which felt fresh in the very familiar backdrop of Sotenbori. Completionist achievements were very manageable and it was a really nice touch to also be able to re-experience Sega Racing Classic / Daytona USA 2 - many fond childhood memories of this at arcades!

The main storyline was well-paced, given it's meant to be a short title. It helped to fill the many supposed plot gaps between Yakuza 6 and Yakuza 7, whilst also featuring a new niche location being the Castle. I was blown away by all the fancy neon bright lights of the area, which truly brought out that premier nightlife feeling in the map.

Overall, RGG studio never ceases to amaze fans with their work, be it large scale numbered titles or even the smaller titles such as Like a Dragon Gaiden. I'm personally looking forward to sink a sizable amount of time into their next title - Infinite Wealth.\t\t\t\t\t

a short game (compared to the rest of the games) but very fun with the agent ablities (i loved spamming hornets in the coliseum . :3) the combat was super fun in both agent and yakuza style even though it took abit to get the hang of it especially after LAD being turn based

cried alot at the last chapter because of kiryu joryu's reunion with watase and the three tojo clan superiors, and the scene of the surveillance camera feed of his fake grave in okinawa,,,,, and is also the first game in this series i 100% in my first playthrough! (maybe it was me being biased considering how this is lowkey a fanservice "final" game for the kiryu fans)

definitely play this AFTER Y7:LAD!! if you were like me, thinking if they could play gaiden before LAD, trust me when i say not to because theres MAJOR spoilers in the last chapter for y7 + the story of gaiden hits harder (?) when you know what happens on ichiban's side

don't think i've written such a long review before but this game has definitely given me post game depression!! wish i could erase my memories of this game just so i can replay it knowing nothing about it and the story again :)\t\t\t\t\t
