\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Man Who Erased His Name is another entry in the long running Like a Dragon / Yakuza series of third person action games. This is a sequel to Yakuza 6, and features a new playable character called Joryu.

When a powerful Yakuza clan believes that Joryus true identity is actually the legendary Dragon of Dojima, Kazuma Kiryu (despite the fact that Joryu wears glasses, while Kiryu doesnt wear glasses, and hes definitely not the same guy wearing a terrible disguise), Joryu is drawn into a plan which could define the future of the Yakuza.

Story

\tWriting is good, and contains the usual dramatic scenes, twists, and betrayals that the series is known for. This is also a story about how much someone will sacrifice to protect their family.

\tTMWEHN also connects to the later chapters of Ichiban Kasugas story in Like a Dragon, which means we already know how this part of the story ends, however we do learn more about Joryus role in those events.

\tI really like Joryu, so I hope we see him again as the main character in another game. There are also a few other interesting characters, including government agents and Yakuza members who want to use Joryu for their own agendas, and a young woman called Akame, who runs a network which takes requests to help homeless people.

\tCutscenes are cinematic and have good graphical quality.

\tI played with the Japanese voice acting, which has good quality, but only the cutscenes are voiced, with in engine conversations during both the main quest and substories, being text only. Yakuza 6 was fully voiced (I cant remember if this was also the case with Like a Dragon), so this is a backwards step.

Gameplay

\tMost of the action takes place in two main locations, Sotenbori (previously seen in Yakuza 0, 2 and 5), and an amusement park for the rich, known as the Castle.

\tFight against gangs of Yakuza and other thugs in real time combat, using a combination of punches, kicks, and various melee weapons, while building up your heat meter to perform powerful finishing moves.

\tJoryu has a new agent fighting style which allows him to use various gadgets during combat. The spider can trap enemies in webs, but I didnt find the others that useful, so I preferred to use the classic Yakuza style.

\tBoss fights are fun, mainly thanks to the responsiveness of Joryus dodge move allowing him to quickly evade their attacks, while also setting up opportunities for a powerful counter attack. I was actually able to win most fights through skill, instead of relying on drinking the drug stores entire stock of toughness zs to stay alive like in some of the older games.

\tBesides the main quest, there are various activities to keep you busy.

\tAkame Network involves stopping groups of thugs from beating up civilians, and simple fetch quests (mainly to buy food or medicine from shops). This is basic copy and pasted content similar to what Ubisoft fill their open world games with, but they do reward you with cash which can be used to buy ability upgrades.

\tSubstories the best one features a cameo appearance from a familiar character, but unfortunately most of them lack the wacky tone of the previous games, and can also feel a bit repetitive, for example three of the substories ask you to defeat gangs of thugs, but each gang involves the exact same sequence of 6 fights.

\tFight in the Castles coliseum against single opponents or larger groups for huge cash rewards. Many of the substories allow you to recruit allies into Joryus clan, which can take part in team fights. However, I dont like the time limits, especially at platinum level, where you have to fight 15-20 enemies who all have massive health bars. Winning is less about skill, and more about how much money youve spent upgrading your damage to KO enemies quickly.

\tWhen you want to take a break from the action, theres also the usual wide selection of minigames, including darts, pool, golf, karaoke, sega arcade, pocket circuit racing, poker, blackjack, mahjong and shogi.

Technical

\tIt took me just under 30 hours to complete the story and most substories and coliseum fights, which is a decent amount of content, but still shorter than most games in the series.

\tControls worked great on my xbox 360 controller.

\tI didnt have any problems with bugs, crashes or framerate.

\tI recommend using DLSS to clear up some distracting edge flickering, which can be particularly noticeable on Joryus glasses.

Recommendation

Like a Dragon Gaiden: The Man Who Erased His Name is a good game which existing fans of the series should enjoy. While the gameplay feels like just another Yakuza game, its definitely worth playing to continue the story. However I would advise waiting for a sale due to this being a shorter game.

As the story directly follows the events of Yakuza 6, this wouldnt be a good place for new players to join the series. I would advise starting with Yakuza 0, or the Judgment spinoffs which have similar gameplay but a separate story.\t\t\t\t\t
