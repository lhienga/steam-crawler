\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely one of the best Yakuza games in my eyes, but probably one of the worst to start with. With no context I think a lot of this might not really hit and at this point, this story is built upon half a dozen games worth of narrative and it's so satisfying to experience it if you've played all the other games. It's chock full of fan service for the earlier games and really hits all the spots you wanted Yakuza 6 to hit, it's a far more personal game for Kiryu and really focuses on him as a character and has some of the best cinematography in the series, so many of the shots are gorgeous and the choreography and spectacle of the fight scenes are great.

This iteration of the arena is so much fun, my only complaint is that I wish there was a bit more content to it because it's so addicting but can be cleared pretty quickly excepting a few of the very high end matches which require pretty set strategies. The team hell rumble matches especially, are brilliant. I loved collecting random characters from the other games and engaging in a massive chaotic brawl with them, gangster pokemon is always fun to engage in with yakuza games. You've got Mr Masochist, Gary Buster Holmes and Sodachi here, just to name a few.

The new agent style is fun to use, but sadly I found it to be entirely overshadowed by how obscenely overpowered yakuza style is, but it remains very useful for thinning out large crowds. The store pages says you should adapt your style to the situation but you don't really need to... Kiryu has always been a bit of a monster, but he does SO. MUCH. DAMAGE with yakuza mode, especially in extreme heat mode and as a result I found this to be by far the easiest game. It is very satisfying to channel spider-man and definitely non-lethally throw some street thugs into the river though. Jetpack loafers are also good, albeit a bit clunky to use like the bomb cigarette, but it always remained a quirky random style to use for a change rather than a must-use.

I also think this had some of the best acting in the series. Kiryu has always been a very stoic character but his actor really got to show off what an incredible range he has here. The entire ending sequence was so well done.LET THIS MAN SEE HIS CHILDREN AND GRANDCHILD\t\t\t\t\t
