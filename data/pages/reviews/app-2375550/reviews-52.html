\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEveryone is so unbelievably stupid in this game it's unreal.
Most of the story up until the last chapter feels like an utter waste of time at best, and offensively idiotic at worst. This is a story that honestly didn't need to be told and could've been avoided if 7 was written a little bit better.
Shishido is aight. Nishitani exists to be yakuza 0 jacking off and isn't a real character who doesn't contribute to the game in any meaningful way. But he's just so goofy :DDDDDDD he has to be in this game!!!!
The pacing of this game is so bad that no character gets time to gets naturally developed. All the gaps are filled in with random story/ lore dumps out of nowhere that are so jarring as to be laughable.
The daidoji exist to just be annoying. They scare kiryu, but then do nothing if he reveals his identity or beats up their officers. They also send him into the public and to face other people and just hope no one recognizes him? ok. If you're gonna make a story where kiryu breaks rules regardless of the consequences, it has to feel like he's actually standing up to an organization that'll DO SOMETHING if he breaks those rules.
This game also has an INSANE amount of filler. Some chapters can feel like nothing happens, killings hours of your life.
The last chapter has a lot of great parts, including the shishido boss fight and the ending cutscene. But yeah, it takes 20-30 hours of agony to get to like 1-2 hours of greatness.

The arena side content is sometimes okay, but grinding new characters (even the dlc characters) is sooooooo boringggggg
The akame network is the most grindy, boring, mind-numbing garbage ever and is basically mandatory to upgrade your skills, so it's unavoidable AND painful! awesome!
I think akame could've been a neat character, but it feels like she exists to dish out busywork and thats it,
Most of the side stories aren't anything special. I like the one with one of the sunshine orphans, as it lets you (and kiryu) take a more critical look at kazama, his hypocrisy, and his legacy.

The combat in this game is eh? i think the dragon style is okay, the spy style is so jank and only exists so there's some way for kiryu to interact with huge crowds of people. Sometimes the combat feels peak, sometimes it feels like the same ♥♥♥♥ people hate about yakuza 3 with the jankiest hitboxes and dumb, relentless blocking.

It's not that this is a "bad game" per se, but it's a bad yakuza/ like a dragon game. I can't recommend it at full price or even half price. Maybe at like $10 or something.

We need to let Kiryu go. We've tried to move on from him in 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, in 7, and now in LAD Gaiden and 8.
Judgment showed back in 2018 that rgg can introduce a series in the yakuza world that's awesome and explores new angles.

Oh, and the "I'm not Kiryu, I'm Joryu" bit is just stupid.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's difficult to review this game without spoiling plot points in Yakuza 6 and Yakuza Like a Dragon so I'll just point out pros and cons so you can decide if you want to get this chapter of the saga or would rather watch it on a video.

[PROS]
- STORY: The story picks up where Yakuza 6 ended. I feel like many people might have had issues with the way the story ended, so this one tries to expand on what happened after the events of 6. And the story happens at the same time Yakuza Like a Dragon's plot happened, so if you played that one before you'll be able to piece together pieces and understand better the reason behind some of the plot points in that one too. And the quality is pretty good, without spoiling anything the ending had me fighting the tears off.
- COMBAT: I've heard multiple opinions on the combat of this game, but as someone that has yet to play Judgement the combat in this game feels polished. Again, I jumped from the combat of 6 into this one and it feels so much more polished. Game has two styles: Agent - A style that is characterized for being ultra fast but low damage and Yakuza - Basically the style of 6 where you hit like a truck but attacks are way slower, leaving you open to getting hurt. So, if you liked the combat in 6, it's here. And if you wanted new combat you also get that and can easily switch mid-combat with the push of a button.
- SIDE CONTENT: Lots of minigames to play. It doesn't really introduce a whole lot of new stuff, rather it brings those minigames that were more popular and polishes them, giving us sort of a "Best of" selection of things to do. Things like Pocket Racing, karaoke, coliseum battles, they all make a very strong comeback.

[CONS]
- STORY: This game is the shortest in the entire saga. And it's not an exageration, it's VERY short. I don't know how regional pricing might work for other countries, but right now this is the most expensive Yakuza game in all of Steam, so having a game that you can complete in around 35 hours (I took my sweet time to play it, tried all minigames, completed most of the side content, walked around the map instead of running and still finished it in 32 hours) for that price is one thing you might wanna keep in mind. The quality is there, RGG put out a fantastic product, but it's short.
- COMBAT: While the combat is polished, the fact that the game is so short gives us a smaller list of heat actions and all of the abilities are gained with money rather than experience or training. Depending on how much you play the side content you might be super weak the entire game or super strong, there's no really in-between. And the game ends before you can get properly comfortable with your fighting style.
- SIDE CONTENT: Again, the fact that the game is so short makes this part a gray area, while there are plenty of side activities and these are well done you don't really experience a whole lot before the game ends. The number of side stories in this game is surprisingly small, and now instead of getting them by walking around and helping the residents of the city, you are given side stories from a side-quest hub in your hideout which I don't know ... for me it sort of robs the charm these games have. You're not a good samaritan helping people anymore, you're an agent hired to do odd jobs. And I know it's only for this game but I can't not mention the tonal shift the game takes. Yakuza games are always a serious story with a side of silly. This game is very serious from beginning to end, couple of side stories are funny but overall the game mantains a very serious tone which might charm some and polarize others.

[ACHIEVEMENTS]
This must be the easiest game in the series to 100%. I've seen people do it in around 45 hours. The Completion List means nothing, you don't have to finish it on a high difficulty, and I'm pretty sure you can complete all the achievements in your first playthrough if you have the patience for it. Personally, it disappoints me a little, but I also understand how it can be due to the length of the game.

[SUMMARY]
Overall a very strong addition to the series. One that in a way was needed to tie up all of Kiryu's journey on a nice bow and send him for what might be his final adventure in Infinite Wealth. I'm very satisfied with the product I got, but if you think the price might be too much and want to wait for a discount that is a very valid option.\t\t\t\t\t
