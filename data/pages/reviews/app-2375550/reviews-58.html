"I play through every Yakuza" Part Gaiden

General:
I absolutely LOVE the new agent style, you get so many fun toys to play with it makes combat an absolute blast, while dragon style is arguably better, agent style is much more fun and entertaining to throw enemies around, blow them up and move at blazing speeds.
The main story is a lot shorter than your usual Yakuza game, though that also means it's much more condensed, it picks up fast and all chapters have a good bit of meat to them, unlike other game where one chapter can end after just going to a phone booth.
I also liked the Ending-Sequence a lot.

Substories:
Most of the substories are rather short, alltough none of them are the basic "oh, no. Con-Man" ones. There aren't really any that are as good as the best ones from other games though but they were still fun to complete.
The requests which are sort of mini-substories were a bit tedious and not all that rewarding even though it felt good to complete all of them. Though they mostly consisted of simple fetch-quests or photograph the thing quest. Though they are pretty optional so it's not a problem.

Minigames:
I really love the minigames in Yakuza and alltough they were only the one's you'd be used to, I had a blast playing Mahjong and finally understanding what's going on since I bothered to learn it for the first time.
Big Minigames: (2)
- Pocket Circuit
- Coliseum / Hell Team Rumble

Unique Minigames (Not counting those which are in most other games like Shogi, etc.) : 0
-

Conclusion:
Alltough technically a shorter game, I did pretty much everything including Amon in this and got pretty much the time I would expect from a numbered entry. I would very much recommend this game and also recommend learning Mahjong, it's actually pretty good once you understand what's going on.\t\t\t\t\t
