The graphics in this game are top notch and this game is really well optimized in terms of PC performance requirements. I love that RGG Studio incorporated juggling into this title's gameplay like they did with Lost Judgment. It's really fun especially when switching between Agent Style and Yakuza style constantly. Continuing RGG Studio's long track record of great and memorable video game music, the soundtracks in this game are all very catchy and enhance the experience greatly. I also enjoyed the variety of the minigames in this title. While not my favorite set of minigames out of all RGG Studio games, I still had a blast playing through all of them, with the Coliseum being my personal favorite as it allows you to play as any character available in the Coliseum which is a first for the series!

The new cast of characters introduced in this title were all a delight to watch from start to finish. Despite the shorter runtime in comparison to other RGG Studio games, this title's story is just as focused and even more engaging than some of RGG Studio's other titles. In fact, this title made me really empathized with Kiryu even further and I really love this game's conclusion. I would recommend to play this game after Yakuza 6 and then Yakuza: Like a Dragon to truly enjoy this game's story to the fullest.\t\t\t\t\t
