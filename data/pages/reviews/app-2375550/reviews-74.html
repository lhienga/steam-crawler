\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA quite short entry with probably the most managable 100% achievements in history of Yakuza saga (mostly because it doesn't require you to play mahjong for completion, don't worry though, mahjong is still included in the game).
To indirectly quote one of the comical substories - they really went balls out with this one. We get the marvelous, over-the-top combat with wacky special agent/007 style gadgets, which in my humble opinion culminated in the most memorable Amon fight in the series.
The main story consists of five chapters, which together with requests (substories), colliseum and minigame completion required for 100% achievements take about 35 hours, possibly much shorter if you decide not to take your sweet time with it. After completing the final we get to play premium adventure mode which allows to experience all side content. There is no NG+ implemented (yet).
Once we finish the story we get the possibility to play the demo of Infinite Wealth. The transition from finale to story demo is quite smooth, but once the first combat starts one might realize how bittersweet this demo inclusion is. After hours of having a great time and silly fun with Gaiden's actual combat system we get... the JRPG turn-based combat. Of course it had some major revisions since release of the 7th installment, including combo attacks with party members, possibility to move around during combat (crazy, I know), and area of effect display for AoE skills and attacks. Those changes allow a much more tactical approach, but still walking away from the great active combat style to turn-based one is the saddest part of the series current direction.
So grab Like a Dragon Gaiden and enjoy the action combat, we Yakuza fans all know and love, in its possibly greatest display, as it may very well be the last time we get to see it.

I intentionally avoided mentioning the story not to spoil anything, so let me just say that it is very good. Especially the finale. The finale is perfect.\t\t\t\t\t
