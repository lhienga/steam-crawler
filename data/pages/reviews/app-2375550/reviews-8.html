\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a shorter game, I was blown away at how much I enjoyed this one. I was expecting a quick adventure just to explain Kiryu's whereabouts betwen 6 and 7 but man I was not prepared for how much they developed and fleshed out Kiryu's character in the mere 5 chapters it has. While the combat isn't as good as something like Lost Judgment, the story, characters, voice acting, and presentation were just amazing. It was great to see how far RGG has come as it constantly reminisces about its older titles, letting you join Kiryu on a trip down memory lane. This one is easily in my top 3 Yakuza games. Of course, it has its flaws, but the way they handled a modern Kiryu post-6 was great.

And outside of the main story, the shortness of this game actually benefits it imo with a smaller and shorter set of substories and side content quest chains. The coliseum was fun but just short enough to not let its simple gameplay get too repetitive. And pocket circuit was a nice return even if I didn't play it that much.

This game is a love letter to fans of the Kiryu games so this is a must-play if you're interested in his story. It gives me high hopes for how 8 handles him too. Thank you, RGG.
