Like a Dragon Gaiden: The Man Who Erased His Name


Like a Dragon Gaiden is unlike previous LAD games more of a side story like experience, but even then it's still a LAD game. With beautiful visuals, it still has the classic vibe and side stories/minigames but with a much more compact story. And man, what a story it was! Also my personal goty.


(My thoughts on the story)
I'm going to keep this very short considering that the story isn't all that long either
so I'd rather want you people to experience it yourself.
But man to me this was the game everyone (me included) needed
that weren't agreeing with the way Y6 ended and used some of its characters.
Overall the story is great man, lots of intense and crazy moments happening, with some fun stuff as well.
But I am gonna tell you this, this game broke my soul man :')
This game also overtook 6 as my favorite Kiryu game. Yeah, it's that good!


(Gameplay)
Gameplay is crazy, the fact that Kiryu has more than 1 style again and for the fact that we can JUGGLE as well in this game. You start with the new style Agent, it's the style that you acquire from the Daidoji, it is basically a mix of Yagami's Crane style with a more Agent like vibe I guess??
Either way it's more fast-paced compared to the second style as that is more for a brawler and street fight type way, the move style for agent is more of a martial arts tactical fighting style.
Both styles also have a color, for Agent being the color blue and for the other red. Agent style is also a great way to start juggles when going in heat mode. Overall, you're gonna get a lot of Judgment/Lost Judgment vibes when playing the game. But Agent style is not only fighting no, no, you have like 4 gadgets you can use, a spider-man like web/string that comes out of your watch to pull enemies to you or push them away, a cigaret that's basically a bomb. Freaking rocket boots, and drones that can chase enemies.
Agent style is crazy hahaha there is so much from it. And of course for the second style we have
The Yakuza Style as the game names it (normally being dragon style but oh well). It is vastly improved from the versions of Y6 and Y:K2, being overall much smoother and fleshed out with some changes, new move sets and finishers, he can basically do his iconic kick pose as a finisher. But dude do you kick the ♥♥♥♥ out of enemies when upgrading it oh man enemies went flying hahahahah.
I do gotta say this in all honesty, this is I think is the only game where I haven't relied on heat moves as much compared to the other games, I was really mostly just fighting with juggles and charged attacks
so that did kinda surprise me a little. Overall the combat in this game great, although a combat speed could have been nicely added, It's good but not LJ level good as that one still takes the cake for best RGG combat let's say it's better than K2 and 6 and on par with JE combat considering that one also has 2 styles.
However, one huge new thing has got to be the feature that you can actually personalize Kiryu. That's right full on character customization, I freaking love this feature in this game
and I really hope it doesn't just stay in Gaiden, but actually carries on to other games as well.


(Substories and Minigames)
LAD Gaiden still has the side stuff like Substories and Minigames, as they will always be a core piece of these games. this time you have something called the Akame points, in order to get those Akame points you'll have to do Akame missions/substories, as in this game substories are actually a checklist that you do in order to gain more bonding with Akame, it's kind of unfortunate that it's not randomly like the previous games
but as for Kiryu being an Agent like person, it kinda makes sense that he is doing it this way, and either way the substories are still fun to do, especially for some! But you'll have to see it for your self.
Mini-games, oh man POCKET CIRCUIT IS ACTUALLY BACK, I love this minigame man, one of my favorite things to do in Y0 and K1, and I'm really glad they brought it back one more final time, aside from that another mini-game that returns to the Dragon Engine is Pool, yes that was missed as well for some odd reason and that is also still fun to play, one other new thing is that some point you'll unlock a hideout that has an actual Sega master system which is pretty nuts, has around 12 games in it, tho some you got to buy at like ebisu pawn something close like that.
Also, later in the game you'll unlock a new play environment called The Castle, there it has the casino/Japanese gamble games, a cabaret with LIVE ACTION performances yeah it's super weird honestly, and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ does it take so much space around 28 GB????? (0_0?).
Outside of that you also got the place where you personalize Kiryu, The Boutique which has all kinds of clothing options.


(Enemies, Colosseum and Bosses)
Enemies are your typical street guys or yakuza like the previous games and not the unique types like in LAD7. However, they are very advanced like the enemies in the Judgment games, so when you have a low upgraded Kiryu, be tactical as to how you fight them because lots of them fight back hard.
A returning feature to the game is The Colosseum, which also is featured in The Castle, but man is this the best version of the Colosseum we all know.... EVERY FIGHTER IS PLAYABLE, and no I'm serious every fighter in it is playable, if you preordered the game you get playable Majima, Saejima and Daigo (or if you buy it separately as DLC). But ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ actual playable Daigo is nuts, lots of the fighter have their own move sets alto some also have move sets from previous bosses and enemies, pay close attention, and you'll see it. The rest you'll have to see for yourself, as it's too much to say about it.
Now, then, the other thing this game does great are the bosses. Every, and I mean every, boss in this game is just great. You do have some mini bosses, but they are also good. But the main bosses? ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, very fun to fight to and to some degree quite challenging, also there is a returning feature that the enemies will use Mortal Reversals, as they were first introduced in LJ, Gaiden also has them for some of its enemies and all the bosses. Now I will stand on this with all my heart, this game has the best final boss fight in all LAD, yep there I said it! Overall just the final boss fight was peak LAD moment it has 3 action phases with the last one having a different OST.... ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, I loved this boss fight.


(Soundtrack)
The soundtrack is quite a take cause holy damn this ♥♥♥♥ good! What's surprising is that most of the composing was done by a lot of new people and by some returning composers
that did the OG Y1 and 2. The boss fights, fantastic osts, the battle themes for both styles are really great, long battle themes also great, personally the main theme Fading Away hits in every spot for me that's some tearjerker stuff right there if you just even read the lyrics. The third phase of the final boss really nails it for me man, I've been constantly listening to the soundtrack a lot!
It really is still something that RGG studios does for every LAD game of theirs.
Having an absolute banger of a soundtrack!


That concludes my review for Like a Dragon Gaiden: The Man Who Erased His Name.
Even though it's shorter, this game was very much quality over quantity to me from the gameplay, the great story and characters, and even the side stuff.
I really hope we get more Gaiden games, maybe for other characters like Majima or Akiyama
there are tons of options! I also hope this is really not the last Kiryu Action game...
Cause if so, I'm really gonna miss it :'(


(9.8)\t\t\t\t\t
