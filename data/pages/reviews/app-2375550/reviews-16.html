\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great story with a lot of fan service and a brilliant combat system.

I played Judgment and Yakuza 0 before this game and can definitely say that the combat feels much nicer and crispier. The only annoying thing is the ragdoll stun attacks that just force you to wait a few seconds to recover. The problem is so much worse with pistol enemies who just stun spam you. Also surprisingly there is no quick select of items like there was in Judgments. That just makes it more annoying to heal as you need to go through the menus every time.

In terms of story, it is engaging right from the start and I am glad that you do not need to play all the yakuza games to understand what is going. The only game I would say you need to play before this one is Yakuza Like a Dragon as the story would make a lot more sense if you do. One minor complaint I have about the game is that there are a few times where the story is locked behind progression in the open world. The grind is not too bad and it honestly never affected me as I was already doing the side objectives anyway but I still find this game design annoying.

The fact that this game is shorter than others in the series actually plays in its favour as it picks up much faster and the story is just overall lean. That is especially praiseworthy as these yakuza games tend to be a bit bloaty and consistently run about 8 hours too long. Here the story is to the point.

Overall a great game and a nice change of pace to have a brawler as a bridge between two huge 60+ hour turn based rpgs.\t\t\t\t\t
