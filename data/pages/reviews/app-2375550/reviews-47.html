\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started off "Like a Dragon Gaiden" with a strong feeling of déjà-vu, concerning the sceneries, map and gameplay - a feeling that progressively vanished as I went on playing the game.

So, true; the bases of the gameplay are relatively the same as before, even if each Yakuza game has the talent to show some notable differences for this matter. The map consists in only the city of Sotenbori, and later on the castle, and at start I wished Kiryu could have evolved through a different area, city, as he was supposed to be "incognito".

Well . . . fact is, now that I've finished the game, I feel just blown away by those few dozens of hours I've played, and its' captivating story; it's a Yakuza title that is very worthy to appear in the series, even essential - with an ending that is certainly long, but "Grand Finale" style. And I think that what strongly participated to my feeling of satisfaction in the end was, as for other Yakuza games I've played (including the Judgment ones), I just took my time, with the mini-games, with the Coliseum, and the Akame requests - and all just fit together perfectly, the game was a real blast and don't regret one bit playing this to the end. I also felt that the combat difficulty was a bit higher, which I appreciated.

It isn't perfect though, some lengthy grinding may be necessary for the Coliseum for instance; or once again, the map is actually the most limited in the series - but whatever, Like a Dragon Gaiden is a total banger, mostly if you care to not rush it and explore well all of its' map and everything it has to offer.

So if you already played and liked some games of the franchise, you can buy this with no hesitation ; and if you're a newcomer, my personal advice would be to play the games in the most chronological order possible, starting with Yakuza 0, the first game in the series, and a most well reputed one, for all its' great qualities and fun. But overall I can only recommend such a great game, that made me feel that RGG took all possible care in respecting their audience and followers, and executed that close to perfectly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly floored by how much I enjoyed this game. It's a short one, well for a game in this series, only having a small handful of chapters and a fair bit of side content. The combat in this game is fun. The Agent Style adds quicker moves together with gadgets that are fun to use in certain fights when you've figured out how to control your opponents with them. The Yakuza style is probably the most fun Kiryu has been to play in a non RPG game in a good while. Overall the usage of these two styles together can make fights with enemies and bosses alike interesting if you use them in fun ways.

A fair bit of the side content is related to The Coliseum which gives you your standard 1 V 1 fights of past games, vs groups of enemies and the new mode where you have a team you assembled face off against an enemy team. This mode is fun when figuring out how to best support your team to make sure they don't get eliminated whilst fighting everyone although leveling up said team mates does feel a bit tedious and time consuming unless you have a lot of money on hand already. That being said, when your team is the appropriate level it's a fun side mode to figure out how to handle battles. You can also play as the fighters you recruit although they have a limited moveset so Kiryu is usually who I stuck with.

The story and music is some of the best the series has had. Almost all of these tracks are amazing and the composers of them went above and beyond! The story is genuinely something I did not expect to hook me as it is a side story that seems like lead up to Infinite Wealth but oh how I was wrong. This story goes from a small steady thing to one of the most engaging bits of story this series has ever had. I am honestly amazed with how they hooked me so good towards the end. This is a must play experience for fans of Kiryu's story.

Beyond that this game has side activities you would expect from the series. Golf, mahjong, gambling, cabaret clubs, side stories, pocket circuit, locker keys, Master System games, arcade games, claw games, pool, shogi and darts.

This is a game that is worth it for long time series fans and if you are new to the series start with Yakuza 0 or the first game on PS2, otherwise this game will probably not do as much for you.\t\t\t\t\t

Gaiden is the new peak of Yakuza
As a reminder:
Do not play this before completing Yakuza 0-7
This game is a conclusion of all the Yakuza games released to this day. Playing this first would be the same as watching the final Season of Attack on Titan or any other good series before Season 1-4. You would ruin your experience.

Now for the review:
Kazuma Kiryu is back, for better or worse, depending on how you see it, but on a technical level, this game takes everything that made Yakuza 6 and Kiwami 2 amazing and improves it quite a bit:

Combat
Kiryu keeps his Dragon of Dojima style, which is now called the Yakuza style, but it’s slightly nerved in comparison to Y6 and K2. They made it slightly slower and took away two of the side-steps, you can only do one now (and you get a roll upgrade later) otherwise the moveset is mostly unchanged.

The star of the show is the new Agent style. It’s mixture of Yagamis Crane and Kiryus old rush style with some new gadgets, which are really fun to use.

Gameplay wise Agent style is superior, especially on harder difficulties, due the nerving of Yakuza style and it generally being faster.

One of the biggest changes in terms of combat is the number of enemies you can fight at once. Some battles (mostly in the Colliseum) makes you fight up to 20 enemies at once.
After this game, One Man Army Kiryu definitely deserves his title.

Minigames
The main story might be shorter but in terms of minigames and side content, Gaiden has almost as much content as a normal Yakuza game:
  • Improved Pocket Circuit Racing
  • Improved Colliseum with it’s own Subplot including Team Battles and the opportunity to control and fight with different characters other than Kiryu.
  • A mixture of Judgment side cases and and the classic Substories. It’s presented in a similar way as in Judgment but play out like the classic funny substories we know from Yakuza.
  • New and old Karaoke songs, including Bakamitai
  • Billiard for the first time in the Dragon Engine
  • Hostess Club (They made it worse and more creepy by using real actresses, but it’s there for the people who like it).

Additionally, something, that doesn’t really fit into any of the Subcategories:
You change the appearance of Kiryu now.
Some outfits from older games return or you can just dress him up as a clown, and it will affect almost every cutscene!


Now for the most important part of the game:
The Plot
In terms of story writing, it's kind of amazing to think that the entire situation of Kiryu, where everything started to go downhill for him, begun in Yakuza 5 or more specifically it's ending.
Before that, all Yakuza games had it's of own concluded stories without any big impact for it's successors, but with Yakuza 5 and successor Yakuza 6, everything changed:
  1. Kiryu ends up in prison due the aftermaths of Yakuza 5 (2012)
  2. Haruka leaves Morning Glory Orphanage due unwanted attention by media (sometime between 2012-2014)
  3. Haruka meets Yuta (2014)
  4. Haruka gets hospitalized due her involvement with Yuta (2016)
  5. Kiryu fakes his death to protects his family (2016)
  6. Which leads to Gaiden (2019-2020)
  7. Which then leads to Infinite Wealth, due the affairs in Gaiden and Yakuza 7. (2023)
The entirety of Yakuza 6 and Gaiden could have been prevented, if Kiryu would have been there to protect Haruka. The decision Kiryu made at the end of Yakuza 5 to go into prison were the trigger for everything that took place afterwards. You could even go further back and say that Kiryus decision to let Haruka pursue her dream of becoming an Idol let to all of this, which is impressive to think about.

You could say, that this is all an one big arc and it's still ongoing for 11/12 years now.

If the writers of Yakuza, or Like a Dragon, how it’s called now, planned this from the start, it’s actually really good story writing. Now we will have to see how it continues in Like a Dragon Infinite Wealth

The sum it up, without spoiling the game, this game has definitely the most emotional scene in the entire franchise with one of the best voice acting pieces I’ve ever heard, which would even make the toughest Yakuza cry.
