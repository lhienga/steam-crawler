\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the game Yakuza veterans dream of at night.

Like a Dragon Gaiden is a spin-off of the Like a Dragon series which takes place after Yakuza: Like a Dragon and lets the player take control again of Kiryu Kazuma, after having previously been retired as the main character in LAD.

Gaiden is a short story game with some good side content that will last you roughly 50 hours in total to 100%. The story is very nice, albeit nothing mandatory to understand the greater LAD story.

The gameplay is brawler gameplay at its peak. Every hit you dish out feels like you truly play as Kiryu at his peak. The Dragon Engine has been refined to make for an extremely satisfying combat system and night settings that make you forget reality.

Furthermore, a demo of Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth is part of the game if you are interested in that.

Unfortunately, Gaiden should only be played by people who have played every other main game, apart from Infinite Wealth is this game is more of a fan service to veterans than it is a game for newcomers to the series. If you are one of the latter group, you will absolutely love this game.

However, due to the short playtime yet high price, wait for it to be discounted. Gaiden is not worth the price they charge in full, unless you are a die-hard fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a new player to the Yakuza series, do yourself a favour and look at Yakuza 0 instead. You're in for a fantastic time and (hopefully) the first step in a long and enjoyable journey.

If you're a returning player to the series, then you know what you're in for.

The Man Who Erased His Name is (and feels like) the concluding chapter to the 'traditional' Yakuza plot line. The torch is being passed on and this truly feels like the last horah that fans can indulge in. Everything is familiar, from the plot, to the setting, to the side content and to the combat.

The game was conceived while the RGG team was working on Infinite Wealth and consequently had a short development time. The story itself is short and the side content is largely recycled. Regardless, I still enjoyed my time. I do recommend playing it before tackling IW because it does give a lot of context and is a solid bit of fun.

My only gripe with it is Kiryu's combat moveset and stances this time around. While they're solid in their own right, they did feel restrictive compared to previous games. But hey, the man's getting old. And if that's my only gripe, then that says enough about whether you should give this title a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
