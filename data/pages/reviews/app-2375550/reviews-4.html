with only 5 chapters this is kinda on the shorter side for a yakuza game.

I didnt like what they did with the previous game, the role based ♥♥♥♥ really didnt fit in the game in my opinion, and yakuza the man who erased his name really washed that bad taste out of my mouth nicely.

i didnt regret playing like a dragon, i cheated quite a lot and even installed the "like a brawler" mod [HIGHLY RECOMMEND], but ichiban kasuga was an interesting character to get to know, and it ties in nicely with this game.

the ending made me cry, the combat was satisfying, the daidoji fighting style was too easy for me, thank god the yakuza fightstyle was a thing. Ive been playing this series for almost half a year one, chronoligcally, and to know there arent any games ahead of me after the next one is kinda weird.

TL:DR i recommend this game for those who liked all yakuza games and found the last one meh.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother great Yakuza game. This game has everything you expect from a Yakuza game. Great story, interesting characters, fun side missions that are more than simple fetch quests.

Combat is great. I love the inclusion of all the gadgets for your second style of combat. You really feel like an agent using wires to tie up your opponents and exploding cigarettes to defeat your foes.

Story is a great tie in between Yakuza 6 and shows some of the events in Yakuza Like A Dragon from a different perspective. It does a great job getting you ready for infinite wealth. The game is fairly short for a Yakuza game, which I actually liked. It was not too short but does not wear out its welcome. The story does have one or two confusing aspects, like why Kiryu insists on keeping up the charade he is dead, when literally no one in the Yakuza universe believes it.

The only issue I would say is I see some people saying this is the perfect game to get started with the Yakuza series. I would disagree and say this is one of the worst games to start with. True, most of the characters are unique to this story, but the game will spoil aspects of Yakuza 6 and pretty much summarizes Like A Dragon, spoiling the game but lacking the emotional depth the game has. If this is your first time playing Yakuza and the Like A Dragon cast is going to be used more and more going forward, it would be a horrible introduction to them. Works great as a refresher, but not a starting point.
