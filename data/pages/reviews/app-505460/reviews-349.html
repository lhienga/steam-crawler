\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor anyone having read some of the bad reviews: It's common for griefers and bad apples to try to give a last "f you" to the game after being permabanned by leaving a negative steam review.

There is no way for the community to permanently ban a player. While a massive amount of reports can ban a player for a few minutes to up to an hour, perma bans are distributed by the devs only, and only reluctantly.

While people do have legitimate bad experiences with groups of players abusing the report system, abuse will lead (and has led) to consequences. So if you read a review where anyone claims they got permabanned from the game for "doing nothing" or "not playing the game properly" they're straight up lying to you. Permabans are used to get rid of alternate accounts and the worst of the repeat griefers and hackers.

That being out of the way:
I enjoy Foxhole. There's no other game that manages to blend resource gathering, manufacture, combat, strategy and tactics at all, let alone into a functional and overall fun experience. As everything is done by players, there's always things to be done and almost anyone will accept a helping hand, or show a new player the ropes.

The community is overall great; I have seen no other game with this much fan art, stories, and other media compared to the relatively small community. In-game you will find mostly friendly people, though with a few thousand players in the same world you're bound to find the odd... outlier.

That being said, at the time of this review the game is still lacking in certain aspects, especially concerning some of the short-term gameplay loops during resource gathering, building and manufacture. The combat is by far the most complex part of the game as of now, though some insist some of the simpler mechanics have a charm of their own.

Overall the game is already more than worth playing as you'll find one or more roles you enjoy doing, and may just pull you in for a few thousand hours as well.\t\t\t\t\t
