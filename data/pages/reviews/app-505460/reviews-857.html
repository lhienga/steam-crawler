\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really excellent and unique game. It really does capture the feeling of being in a real war top to bottom. From the intense front line experience of pushing into an an enemy town behind your armor with artillery raining down to the awesome teamwork and logistical skill that goes into creating and maintaining supply lines, I've never quite played anything like it. The combat is really exciting and feels tactical and realistic for the most part (besides maybe dodge/weave shotgun and nade style that excels for close range), and it manifests into some really incredible dynamic battles where eventually taking/holding the town/bridge after hours and hours of intense fighting and shelling feels like a real victory.

The coolest part though IMO is while all thats going on on multiple fronts all over the war, its only even possible due to hundreds of logistics guys on each side that work tirelessly to farm resources, refine them, turn them into supplies the troops need. Every bullet you fire or bandage you use has to be made in a factory by a real player. Those guys really are the ones who win the war, as any soldier who has run out of grenades and howitzer shells in an intense battle can tell you. And its really so impressive seeing their coordination and effectiveness, in my experience so far they have done an incredible job keeping us supplied (I have recently been trying to learn this role myself to help out that way from time to time too).

So if you want to feel like you're fighting in and contributing to a trule large scale war, and you're down for a unique (but fun) combat system this is the game for you. You can play any role you like, a battle medic, an artillery gunner, a fortifications engineer, a tank commander, or even a supply truck driver or scrap miner. Your side will need all of these to win the war.

Of course there are occasional trolls and annoying people but they are far and few between, so I think for anyone who has played much multiplayer games in a past its not much of an issue. For every one there is at least a dozen guys working hard to help your team win. So my guess is most of these negative reviews complaining about the community/mods are mostly just players without the thick skin necessary for a large scale teamwork game, but thats just speaking from my experience.\t\t\t\t\t
