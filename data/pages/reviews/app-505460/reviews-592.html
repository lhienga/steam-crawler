---Personal opinion---
Im hooked as of writing this review so i hope i dont seem too biased in favour of this game but one of the best things about the game in my opinion is the fact you can do what ever the hell you want! wanna be a medic? DONE! wanna be a truck driver? DONE! wanna work together with friends to recreate D day? DONE! the replayability of this game is HUGE & whats even better is that the community is one of the best online communities i have ever encountered, on my first day random members of the community took hour out of their day to teach me and a bunch of other players the basics of the game almost taking it on themselves as boot camp instructors.

before i keep my rant going my final review for this game would have to be a 9.5/10, this game is incredible, the graphics may be pretty decent & the audio may be middle road but the thing that makes this game so good is the gameplay and community. and the only thing holding it back from a 10/10 for me is if they were to find a way to bring back voice chat with the opposing sides soldiers & maybe adding in a better tutorial instead of the current open map with signs every once and a while telling you the basics of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Summary in Bullet Points
I'll try to stay abstract here because the actual content of Foxhole is in flux, more than most Early Access titles.
  • New Genre: Massively Multiplayer War Gaming.
  • Single Instance: Meaning, all players inhabit the same battlefields in the same war, which last for weeks until one side fulfills the conditions to achieve victory.
  • Real Wartime Economy: Every weapon, item, fortification, and vehicle is generated through player action using supplies and materials gathered through player actions, including but not limited to ammunition, fuel, and respawn tickets. All of which are then delivered to the front through the effort of logistics players.
  • Tech Progression: Over the course of each war, the game's technology progresses from WWI to WWII through, you guessed it, Frank Stallone player actions.
  • War variability: From one war to the next, the devs will alter the game slightly. These changes come in many forms. The goals in mind seem to be variability of the meta and playtesting of new features/balance.
  • No Microtransactions Commitment: For 20 dollars, you buy the entire game. There are no deceptive, piecemeal monetization practices.

Main Review
Ahh Foxhole, such a tremendously clever, creative, and frustrating independent project. Any accurate review of this game requires an honest disclaimer, that being:
"Foxhole is an Early Access prototype of a conceptually unique game in an all-new genre, developed by an independent studio that is not afraid of risky innovation - even at the cost of temporarily breaking and/or ruining their game".

The state of Foxhole is perpetually volatile, meaning no review is accurate for more than a few months at a time nor can you trust your first impressions, be they good or bad. Remember, this game actually is early access. Unlike other early access titles, it is not a buggy, unplayable mess helmed by an absent, negligent, or incompetent developer. Clapfoot Siege Camp works diligently, regularly adding(and/or removing) new features, vehicles, weapons, maps, etc. and everything is subject to change. It's an odd sort of problem caused by having both tremendously ambitious developers and only one shard, or instance, of Foxhole. I qualify that as a "good problem", despite the frustration it causes.

One major flaw to this set of designs is that Foxhole's enjoyability depends on having a large, active, and healthy playerbase in order to fill out each army and have competitive, exciting wars. Luckily for anyone considering a purchase or returning players, that playerbase has grown substantially in the past year, from a rough average of 300 concurrent players to the current 2000 concurrent players. This is wonderful and for the sake of the players and the devs, I hope it sustains.

For a fair price of 20 dollars, you buy the entire game. Unlike most studios operating today, Clapfoot Siege Camp aren't trying to double-dip into your wallet. Early in their development cycle, Clapfoot Siege Camp made a commitment to its players, rejecting microtransactions as part of their business model and have held true to that promise. That matters to me... a lot.

For the reasons above, I wholeheartedly recommend Foxhole.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrankly, compared to other MMORPGS, there is nothing like Foxhole.

Despite that, for some folks this isn't their cup of tea. If you are hoping to be an "OP" character and lead charges, and go Master Chief on folks, this isn't the game for you (or where you can buy OP weapons/vehicles etc). The game was created to be a bit of a "grind" in a different way. You have to time rushes. You have to communicate to individuals who accidently shot you minutes ago because they were scared for their life. The grind, also is where technology relies on people in the backlines AND your willingness to assist in helping them create said tech. The grind is being patient at times, but if you care about what you do, you will always find things to do. I find myself leading new players to disrupt supply lines and ambush those who are charging.

The realism of the game is unforgiving at times. You will find yourself literally watching a bridge and sand having fun conversations with folks who've been watching the bridge for hours, repelling pushes. OVERALL, the community is AMAZING.

I have to applaud the creators. No microtransactions, no random skin related DLCs, just focus on warfare. Please don't add microtransactions.

Weird thing about the game despite its continual premise, you can play for 45 minutes and make an impact. In theory, you can hop on the game for 30 minutes, and still contribute to the larger goal...however the biggest impact is coordinating with friends (or the ones you meet along the way) Just don't assume you'll get thanked for doing work for logistics or for killing a wave of individuals with gas (Foxhole doesn't even tell you if you did).

Oddly enough, this reminds me a lot of the military. Sometimes, you are the guy prepping the materials. Sometimes you are the one in the "hurry up and wait" chiling in a bunker for hours. Sometimes you are the individual who flubbed up their load out and forgot key equipment.

That is the fun of the game.

I can see, in the future, that Foxhole could add two more factions to keep things spicy...and add the political element often found in war.

I applaud the creators. Also don't rush the bridge.\t\t\t\t\t
