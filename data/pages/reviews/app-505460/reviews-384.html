\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is an interesting game. It's not hard to recommend, because I think that this is the kind of game that has a lot of things to appreciate about it.

The length of the battles, the impermanence and permanence of what you do on the battlefield, the way that players interact with one another, and the way the interact with the other team. The feeling of being simultaneously able to make a difference and that you're just another body. The fact that others share these feelings and that brings out a camaraderie between players that few other games can match, and the ability to easily communicate with other players in a way that keeps things fairly immersive manages to really enhance that feeling of camaraderie.

The ability to choose how you help your team, be it gathering raw materials, refining them into usable materials, turning them into usable supplies, delivering things where they need to go, building bunkers and setting up encampments, resupplying ammunition, healing your fellow soldiers, man the big/mounted guns, drive tanks, sniping from afar, and more. You'll probably find yourself constantly swapping between many of these, all towards the goal of capturing enemy hexes.

All of this really sells this feeling of identifying yourself with the "war effort" that no other game has managed to capture in my opinion. When you're playing it feels like you're a soldier in something far greater than you, and when you're out you want to know how "our boys overseas" are faring.

This game manages to get players to effectively role play without being about role playing. It's not about suspending disbelief, nor do you need to try to find a RP server; the game is structured in such a way that players end up naturally role playing, without the cringe usually associated with that.

If you decide to play this game, I recommend using the voice chat system. People who don't use their mics and solely rely on the text-based chat system kinda break the immersion in my opinion, and they really make good communication hard.

For most players, I'd say to bring a mic, bring a beer, and slowly immerse yourself in it over a few days. It takes a little while for things to really click in your head, but once they do it's an experience I don't think you can get anywhere else.\t\t\t\t\t
