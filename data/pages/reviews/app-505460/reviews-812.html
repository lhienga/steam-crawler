\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBased off of the experiences from the 0.26 update.

Wait and see. In its current state, don’t recommend.

Currently not really fun and an exceptional amount of grind.

It used to be previously that one could spend an hour or two, gather the supplies needed to run a light tank (or other vehicle), build one and then off you go with your friend until you either ran low on supplies or were destroyed by the enemy. Used to spend a lot of time with a couple mates doing about a hours prep and then running a tank or halftrack into the wee hours of the morning, trying to help where we could stemming gaps in the line or pushing the frontline. Being part of a vehicle crew in the game can be an amazing amount of fun when you’re coordinated and feeling like your part of a larger thing.

When not doing vehicle crew stuff, running logistics as a side thing was pretty neat as well. Get a truck run supplies where they are needed, simple.

After update 26 a lot of things logistics wise got changed. The cranes and crate bit are neat and is useful. Certain backfield towns turned into logistics hubs with bustling activity.

The grind to get stuff however is too bloody extreme. The update also caused things such as materials to make tank shells and mortar ammo to take hours to set up. In one instance I was able to drum up about 6 friends to play for a night. Our goal was simple, scrounge and make the supplies needed to run a gunboat to help along one of the coast bridge fights. Four hours later, we had just managed to get the supplies needed for the boat. As it turns out, you cannot do things like make vehicles one at a time, you need supplies to make boats and tanks and the like in batches of 5. The amount of R-mats needed to do that sort of thing is, quite a bit. By the time we got the gunboat in the water and supplied we were down to three folk. We managed about a hours’ worth of play time before we had to call it a night. Parked the boat at a safe harbor, and not surprisingly by the next day it was gone. Which meant if we wanted to do that sort of thing again, we’d have to start the grind all over again.

Working with light tanks is about the same but cranked up to the extreme. Tank shells are sort of doable to find and get supplied with albeit you may need to travel across the entire map to do so. Something that occurred even before the update. However, tanks need petrol to roam. Petrol which is nigh on impossible to locate and horded by whoever finds it.

Running basic logistics is, okayish. It can be somewhat frustrating to have to travel across three maps in order to get something as basic as soldier supplies or to get the HE Mats you need, and then run back. But the principles there are mostly alright. The cranes and crates work pretty effectively with the logistics network. The refine ratios are absolute garbage though, and tie into the whole 5 hours of work for 1 hour of fun. You need a stupid amount of raw materials to make a tiny amount of anything that is not basic guns and ammo. In hindsight, no it is not okayish. It kinda sucks now.

But the devs really missed the mark in the later game vehicle stuff. The video they show with 4 light tanks moving forth? Don’t really see, and will never see with random players anymore. Light tanks are a rarity outside of organized clans that can support the absurd logistical requirements. Five hours of work for roughly one hour of fun really is not that fun anymore, of the seven or so folks that I played with most have quit simply because again, it is frankly too grindy.

It used to be a game you could hop on and maybe play for a few hours with your friends. Anymore it feels like you need a few hours before you can even do anything. We did have some success with trying to run a mortar carrier (APC with a mortar tube in the back), but even then, finding or requesting mortar rounds could be hit or miss and sometimes require a long travel time as you crisscrossed the maps. I really want to like this game again, (I loved it when they had their big update last December and January), but in its current state it simply is too much effort for too little of payoff.

Cannot really talk about the population imbalance between the Colonials and the Wardens as I quit before that occurred. From the sounds of things however, its heavily slanted towards the Wardens max popping and the Colonials having barely enough to stem the gaps. Every weekend the Colonials basically get steamrolled by the Wardens and their high pop.

In short however, those that just want to pick up a rifle and run at the enemy? Sure, could still maybe suggest it. Those that want to run a vehicle or the like? Don’t bother at this time, wait and see if more positive reviews come at a later date citing that they fixed the issues with the grind.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible fun if: you are willing to take risks, ready to die (a lot), farm occasionally, spend time honing your skills and cooperate with your fellow players. In my opinion this game is best enjoyed with friends to back you up but still engages single players. I recommend you give this a go! This game has incredible pacing if you set out a schedule right. You can go from an intense firefight with your comrades dying left and right but then later have a more calming time carrying out supply runs and manufacturing resources. Personally I prefer to get online, carry out 2 or 3 supply runs before going to the frontlines and battling for an hour or 2 (if its a long battle), maybe setup some defences as well, and then calm down for a bit with some relaxing supply runs to where I've been fighting or some other place.

Be warned though. This game is not really for the casual audience, you will need to invest time into the game to learn the mechanics and help your fellow players. Sometimes your plans will go completely awry and you might get smashed in an engagement... but that's ok because you learn from every death and always get the chance to improve and think of new tactics. Basically if you want to go into this as something to play for an hour or two on the weekend I would suggest you pass as you probably will not have enough time to do anything substancial as battles can rage for hours and supply runs can take upwards of 30 minutes to deliver.

Don't let me stop you though as you can still have an odd weekend firefight if you like!
Good luck to anyone new to this game and I hope you stick around so you can experience everything this game has to offer.\t\t\t\t\t

You'll understand what I mean when you try to go for that 10 minute drive to deliver supplies to a front line but it ends up being 30 minutes because you keep rubber-banding the whole way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a very unique game. It's a community-driven, teamwork oriented war simulator where every gun, every bullet, every vehicle, and every fortification is built and transported by a player using materials gathered by a player.

There isn’t much of a learning curve since most everyone is willing to help you understand if you are willing to help out.

The combat itself is very unlike other war games since it is top down. The best all-purpose strategy is to lay down when you’re being shot at since prone can make you nearly invincible. You can get into foxholes and shoot out of them as well which is better than going prone in most cases.

There is an issue as of the time of writing this where you can see and shoot farther by using the corner of your screen. I believe it’s up for debate whether or not this is an intended mechanic, but it results in very awkward camera movement during fights.

It’s not all about the combat, though. You can be a full time farmer if that’s what you enjoy. People will appreciate it and you will be able to hang out amicably with lots of people from across the globe.

If you don’t fancy the idea of hitting scrap piles but transporting materiel looks appealing, you can do that too! You can be a wartime trucker whenever you’d like.

There are logistic hubs that contain the facilities needed to produce materiel from scrap. Once you have enough experience and respect from your fellow players you can run the operation! You can manage people who ask for jobs, handle the logistics of loading and unloading crates, and run the factories. This is not a role for beginners, however, since it requires you to know what your team needs.

Foxhole has a lot of issues but is a unique experience. If it looks good to you I would recommend picking it up after a big update so you can learn the new content alongside everyone else. It also tends to go on sale during these times.\t\t\t\t\t

From Hole to Hole

TL;DR The game is fun with lots of interesting mechanics and things to do, but the player base has the potential to turn you off very quickly if you don't run into friendly faces right off the bat.

So I started playing Foxhole way back during the beta, and when the game finally came out I decided not to purchase it because I felt a little bit robbed that there was no "Thank you for testing" discount regardless how small. Despite this, I enjoyed the game but I couldn't justify £14.99 for what was offered when the game released. Jump forward to a few months ago when the game went on sale and I decided that I would see where the game had come, and I can confirm that for £9.99 the game is amazing, with £14.99 still pushing it a little.

The game itself is fun, there are logistics, vehicles, weapons, combat and base building, all of which are fun to play with the only part feeling like a chore being the refinement of scrap (which is resource you as the player collects) and BMATs/RMATS (Which is a building material used to make buildings and defences and a resource that is used to upgrade structures and make more complicated gear) both of which take an awkward amount of time to make.

The problem I had with the game was that it has a very dedicated fan base, which isn't bad on its own, however, they are determined to win at any cost, even if it means not enjoying the game. From the moment we stepped foot on the ground and saw the many vehicles and trucks driving around we knew we wanted to be a part of it. We decided that we were going to build a vehicle to go help our fellows on the front with some wheeled support (seeing as tanks hadn't been unlocked at this stage in the game) because that seemed like a good idea at the time. We wanted to explore what we could build and how we could build it, but at every turn we had a player telling us that we should be using our time differently, constructing something else or doing something "useful". I can understand this a bit better now, but as a new player to the game and wanting to explore all of the systems the game has to offer, getting bossed around was very frustrating, especially when to us (at the time) we were helping.

So to conclude, the game is fun, colourful and amazing with a group of friends. £9.99 was a steal for what I got, and if it weren't for the kind of sucky experience I got at the start of the game £14.99 no longer seems that bad. Overall I would say pick it up and give it a try with some friends when it goes on sale, but unless you are 100% positive that you want to go through the motions and work your way up rather than just jumping into the deep end like me and my friends did then I would steer clear and get something like Factorio to just have a blast with for around the same price.\t\t\t\t\t
