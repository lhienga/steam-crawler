\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the kind of game that some people may love, while others may also despise. Foxhole is a top-down military shooter that relies on players do to everything in order to succeed - From gathering resources and supplying them to the front lines, healing and reviving team mates, manning a machine gun to prevent enemies from pushing and commanding a tank. You are not a super soldier, you're a cog in a huge machine that is almost completely built of actual players.

Because of this when you first get into the game it'll be extremely confusing - Ask around and normally there's an experienced player who is willing to explain the basics. There's a lot of mechanics underneath the simplistic style but these all follow logical steps and are easy to pick up.

What I like most about this game is that no matter what you are doing, you are still contributing to the total war effort. Doing something as menial as gathering scrap resources and driving them to a refinery means that builders can create building materials for defences, which in turn lets the front line soldiers defend territory from the enemy. Every little bit helps! If you want to fight on the front line, you can join a combat operation on the map and fill a lot of different roles such as medic, machine gunner or demolitions / AT gunner. All are useful and are much appreciated.

To conclude, Foxhole creates an experience that is unlike anything else I have played. If you like team-oriented 'realistic' shooters, this may well be a fantastic game for you to sink your teeth in to.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm not 100% sold on the top down shooter nature of the game (Note that it's not an RTS - I've seen that confusion before) but it works well enough to back up the great concept and technical aspects. Go Collies!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun Game
====================
Foxhole is a very fun game. I like that PvP is team based with two warring factions. Game play left me with no feelings of frustration even after multiple deaths, perhaps this is due to the fact that you can still see your team progressing so it still feels as if you are making a contribution. That being said, team play is the only way to win so unlike many other games that offer equally viable solo or team play, team play is certainly the focus here. This doesn't mean that you can't solo, but to solo means primarily support through the logistical portion of the game, which is often behind the front lines, and a bit of a grind in my opinion.


Difficulty for anyone that is Red/Green color blind
====================
I should mention one more thing. If you are Red/Green color blind, like nearly 10% of the world, then you will find it difficult to know when you or others need to be healed. You can tell when you've been injured, at the moment it happens, but you won't be able to tell how badly and if you miss the injury indication then you'll have no other clue, unless someone else runs up to you to heal you. This is because there is no health indicator. Sadly this also means you won't make a very good medic unless you can simply remember who was injured during a fight. Despite there being so many people (mostly males) that are Red/Green color blind, I'm still often surprised at how long it takes for game developers to accommodate these users, if ever.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a slow starter, without a good tutorial.... The tutorial is there you just have to run around the main spawn. However I can see how new players could be put off from the game during the tutorial.
For how simple the game looks it is actually quite complex, from crafting, logistics and tactics to just pure combat.
The highlight for me so far was advancing on a town, with enemies hiding in the bushes whilst looking for cover with my machine gun to provide firepower for the rest of my squad listening to very co-ordinted team talk (using the ingame voip) of a mortor strike, someone was providing corrections and spotting for a crew of mortor teams.
To be honest I havent played a game where there is so much teamwork, to people crafting items, mining resources or doing logistic runs to and from the front lines, to medics and mortor/tank teams, it has been a great experience.
The only downside is, if you are looking to just jump into combat it can be a little slow. Getting to the front line can be a nightmare some times, having to either run, craft your own vehicle or risk waiting for a helpful logi truck driver to take you to the fight. Once on the front line I would not recommend just running in like an under equipped rambo, as even though you can set your spawn point to a closer position to the fight, you will often find yourself spending most of your time running back to the fight after you have been shot to hell, which if you keep doing will be wasting the tickets at that spawn location(which are topped up by the very nice logi runners - but can take time).

Overall i'd give it a solid 8 out of 10, definatley worth the low price tag, even more so in the sales.

Have already recommended to friends.\t\t\t\t\t
