\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has potential but unfortunately there are design decisions and bugs that make it a disappointing experience. There are also players that exploit broken mechanics right before your eyes.

Design Decisions

1. Asymmetric Technology

This means that each faction has different equipment. This could be fine if the equipment was functionally the same however it isn't. In terms of the tech tree per faction, the "equivalent" weapon you use is not equivalent by any means.

A simple example would be: "Dusk" ce.III (Colonial) vs. Aalto Storm Rifle 24 (Warden).

At first glance you'd think "Yeah, these look the same. They're both assault rifles." However if you go try to use them, you'd notice that the "Dusk" is superior in terms of accuracy and ammo capacity. This could be fine, but the weapon is exclusive to the faction. You can't get it unless you steal it from the enemy which can produce them at will.

This imbalance extends to further segments in the game, the most prevalent being Artillery.

2. Field Artillery vs. ?????

Yes, that's correct there are question marks there. What is the equivalent to Field Artillery for Warden? That's right, there is none. There is quite literally no equivalent to Field Artillery.

What is Field Artillery?

Field Artillery is a mobile device that fires 120mm shells with an effective range of 100-250m and can be moved by infantry. It does not require a vehicle to move.

The closest equivalent Warden has is the Light (120mm / 100-300m) and Heavy (150mm / 100-300m) Artillery, but there is a key difference. These are IMMOBILE devices that require a crane to move.

One faction has mobile artillery that can be moved with only infantry where ever they want anytime. While one faction has to quite literally spend 20 minutes getting a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ crane to setup their artillery. Seriously, what the ♥♥♥♥ is this? You can't honestly expect to compete when a faction can setup artillery infinitely faster than the other and relocate it whenever they want.

3. Bayonets

For some reason bayonets one shot infantry. A legitimate strategy is to run around like Sonic the Hedgehog and skewer people. It's rare to see, but some people exploit this and will spawn camp a bunker base skewering people who are spawning in.

4. Garrison AI Aggro + Vehicles

A feature in this game is that you can steal opposing faction vehicles, however there are key issues with this that lead to players exploiting. Specifically if an enemy faction enters a friendly vehicle, that vehicle to Garrison AI becomes an enemy. The AI will then proceed to shoot at the vehicle until destroyed effectively wasting resources. This leads to strategies that involve suicide dashing into enemy bases to compromise a vehicle, then going AFK in the vehicle until it's destroyed. You die but the vehicle is destroyed which is a win due to the resources involved in creating it. Once an enemy is inside the vehicle, regardless of where they are, you can't get them out of the vehicle without destroying it. This portion of the design is being exploited by some and you will encounter it.

5. Water

The water in this game is a joke. Touching the edge of a shore causes you to put your weapon away. Crouching in some instances near the water does the same thing. Good luck trying to shoot someone when you're stuttering between animations.

You'll probably have a good time until you start encountering things that shouldn't be the way they are.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun but slow. Very slow.

That this game has survived with this many players after 4 years is amazing. about 1500 players around any time!

But, the game itself is very very ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ slow, despite the adrenaline fueled moments of "we need this done quick".
This game is Not about the shooting, it is all about logistics and construction. Soldiers pushing a frontline are important, but not as important as the logistics(logi) trucks that supply the frontlines with all the guns, ammo, grenades and other recources you need to keep a front going and a war winning. Pushing a front means nothing if you then get pshed back without anyone having build up defences in the ground you have covered.
Routinely, you will find yourself playing for 5 hours not really having accomplished anything: pushing the front a few streets just to have it be pushed back tot he same spot you started at 5 hours ago. Even worse is when you log out of the game fot the day, and you come back tomorrow finding your frontlines pushed back a whole town or 2!
The respawn timers are nothing short of abismal: 30 seconds to get back into a fight is not fun in the slightest, especially if you up that to up to a minute(!) because "realism" (legit, the respawn timer is calculated on how many times you die and some war statistics on how fast reinforcements could make it to a front).

People who arent in gulds/outfits/groups (regiments) will find nobody wished to push up because of the BS long respawn timers, and the fact that your gear drops when you die, making it so you need to regear every time you come back to life. Once all the supplies run out, that is it. fall back and try agian.
I fought at a front where the enemies had 3 tanks at their disposal, but they are too scared to push tem forward, so we just spend 2 hours trading nades and bullets untill I logged off for the day. Came back next day finding the enemy had pushed 2 tows into our teretory, making my contributions useless.

Second, logistics, or logi asit is known here. This ♥♥♥♥ takes forever. You need to find a truck, then get it to a refinery, then get basic materials (bmats) to a factory to make crates of weapons, ammo, nades, etc etc, to then ferry all that to a frontline. This entire process takes up to an hour, and most of that time is spend driving from place to place or waiting on the factory to complete your orders.

If you are expecting a fast paced game, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ turn away now, this is anything from fast-paced. The combat takes forever, the construction is a giant (but nessesary) timesink, the vehicles and ammo are too expensive and time consuming to build and use and the public players around you are as chicken ♥♥♥♥ to push up as the enemy is too chicken ♥♥♥♥ to push you out.
If you are looking for a slow paced construction game, where you have some intense battles at the frontlines, then this game is amazing. But do keep in mind that everything takes literal hours.

At the end of the day, unless you have literal hours to spend, dont bother with this one. The concept is amazing and the open world fight is a great gimmick to have, but because of the shear size of the map it takes minutes to get anywhere, and doing 1 logi run could take over an hour(!) to complete, provided you dont get blown up by enemies behind the frontlines\t\t\t\t\t
