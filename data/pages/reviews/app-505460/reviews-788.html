\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReviewing foxhole is weird. It's a game I've put about 160 hours into at the time of writing this review,at the same time it's a game I can't recommend to everyone. This isn't a game that if you want non-stop action all the time then look away. If you're looking for a team focused semi-realistic war game (they take some liberties for the sake of fun) then you might like this. When I say war game,I am including the idea of supply lines,fortifying areas with defenses,and even the downtime of guarding/patrolling. While I do love this game and plan on playing it for a while,there is a issue that I want to let new people know. Even though they did update the training courses for the new players,when you talk see if someone can walk you through it. The tutorial boards give the basic info on things and don't always translate or make sense until you get into the actual game. If you can get a person to coach/train you,Most of those people will be more then happy to explain more indepth about the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had previously left a very scathing review on this game nearly two years ago, and recently I had chosen to revisit it, so let's have a little preface first.

When I left this game it was a grind. The frontlines were a slog until Battle Tanks( at the time) were teched and logistics and support were a second job in the game. Not to mention a ever-increasingly frustrated and small player base. At the time there was a forced scarcity that just... Made support and backline players wanted to brain themselves since they had to grind for hours and hours to get meager supplies.

I left this game frustrated and unhappy, and soon forgot about it.

Well, by chance I had saw an update featured on my Steam Community and took a look.
Seeing new weapons, new tanks, and a surprising community growth.

I think I've sunk about 100 hours into the game in the past 3-4 weeks.

So, what changed for the better?

-No more forced scarcity. Resource to material ratios are now more forgiving and all for the mass production of supplies.
-Faction specific weapons, vehicles, and soon to be uniforms and likely much more. Everything is unique and not reskins of the same weapon or vehicle! They all have unique attributes, perks, and drawbacks.
-Ever expanding world map. With the recent dev stream I was blown away. 14 whole new regions on an already large map.
-Battle Tanks were removed and replaced with more balanced options. (Very based)

OVERALL PROS AND CONS?

PROS:
-Dedicated dev team that has actually turned things around for a game that I thought was going to die. Who says I don't like being proved wrong?
-A wide variety of jobs and roles. Want to be a frontline bada**? Go for it, Rambo. Want to be a medic? Patch me up fam. Want to learn how to build defenses? There's some helpful videos out there, Bob. Want to be a backline manufacturing machine? The Archon loves those who toil. Want to deliver supplies to the frontline from the factory? You're a hero and everyone loves you.
-Two very unique factions with two different playstyles. Colonials are more of the offensive faction, and the Wardens being defensive. (I'd love to see faction specific buildings soon, Siege Camp.) Not to mention both factions have very interesting lores behind them, with more being added (Thanks Matt.)
-Variety of combat. City fighting where you're slogging it out from street to street and house to house, open fields where tank lines brawl with one another hoping the other breaks. Intense bridge fights where there's always the possibility of the stalemate being broken, brutal sieges and desperate defenses.
-Growing Community. This game has a very passionate and dedicated community. From wonderful and talented artists to amazing newscasters and song makers at PressCorps and Ministry of Propaganda, this game has a community like no other. Sometimes for the best, sometimes for the worst.

CONS:
-Camera(Will be addressed in next major updateSOON) The camera kind of sucks sometimes, you have to corner your reticule in order to maximize your range and view. This will be fixed soon, however.
-Toxicity. Some people in this game can be.... Toxic, to say the least. But that's the case with most online games nowadays.
-Alts, Griefers, and Cheaters. Ah yes... The unholy trinity. Foxhole is a game where when you choose a side to fight on, you're locked in for that war after 15 minutes. Well, some unsavory folk REALLY want to win. So, they buy the game on a separate account and join the opposing faction in order to grief, spy, or just be a general ♥♥♥♥. It's becoming a problem, but not to the point where the entire game crumbles because of it.
-PARANOIA. Due to the aforementioned problems above, both sides are gripped with paranoia and zealous witch-hunting for fear of griefers and alt accounts. I've seen a couple of clueless noobs get jumped on in world chat because they didn't know any better, and it took a few minutes for them to actually clear it up that they were genuine noobs.
-Lack of direction for new players.

Alright, this is a big one, and due to paranoia on both teams I have to stress this so you don't get ganged up on by toxic vets scared of alts. This game has a lot to take in. There ARE people in this game who will be more than glad to help you learn the ropes. There is a tutorial, but it's very bare bones. If you want to learn this game your best bet is to ask someone with a higher rank for help.
If you choose Wardens, feel free to approach me, the lads at FMAT, or just give a shout in world chat that you're new and wanna learn the game from someone. I might think about trying to compile a noob's guide on Steam and publish it as a quick TL;DR for noobs.

OVERALL
This game is great and has made a phenomenal comeback in my book but there are some community caused problems that need to be addressed in the near future. The paranoia brought about by certain nefarious folk in the game has caused for some toxic people to fear monger and potentially harm the growth of the game. But believe me when I say these issues are a lot more minor than what I say they are. You can still have heaps of fun in this game.
Clans can sometimes be helpful, but you really have to find the right one tbh.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I enjoy the concept of a game where no one is a one man army, people need to work together and actually plan and this is only the third game that I can think of that keeps in mind players having to deal with logistics. After Arma and Planetside 1.

That said, the community gets annoying and the developers make more and more focus on logistics that it adds many unneeded steps.

I honestly think too much is lacking into making the game fun and the constant extra steps added to make a meta not viable anymore gets frustrating.

For example, a common strategy would be to have many players equip as many grenades as possible and hop onto the back of an apc and toss them over it into an enemy base, their weight, carrying capacity and time to equip was reduced.

Along with that the time it takes to build things, it's quite annoying.
I can understand that this is so players work together, build things and don't equip everything and rush.\t\t\t\t\t
