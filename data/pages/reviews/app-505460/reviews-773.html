That gun you're holding? Forged in a factory and brought to the front lines. That trench you're standing in? Dug by a player with a shovel.

I used to think it was just a cool gimmick. But having real logistic routes that can be cut off by real attackers, causing the front lines to starve of supplies and turn the tide of battle is just oozing with organic and genuine warfare and a massive scale.

If they're still hostile and confrontational, just hit esc, spawn somewhere else and avoid that brick-wall of a person.

I doubt that'll happen though, as there are far more players who are just looking for everyone to have a good time and are more than happy to answer any questions you have. If they're not too busy or stressed with a logistic shipment or literally fighting someone, they should be willing to help you out.

If you actually read this far, and should you decide to play, I hope you freeze to death like the cool kids do on the eastern front.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE GOOD: The best MMO war game I have played. Period.

Imagine two armies at war where absolutely everything from ammo fabrication to base construction, artillery and front-line fighting is made by players and you will have this game. Fighting a war in this game requires an unprecedented amount of teamwork yet somehow this game has managed to convince absolute randoms to team up and cooperate for the sake of their fictional nations. All of this accompanied by absolutely brilliant combat and massive battles.

The amount of cooperation between internet strangers I have seen in this game is beyond comprehension. Sure, there are trolls and salty dudes as in every game but most people you will come by in this game is quite friendly. Don't listen to people that say that you need to be on a clan for this, most players aren't.

THE BAD: This game is early access. As such, some features still need to be polished or are at sub-optimal state.

The most relevant, as many people mention in reviews, is the logistic side of each army (the "logi"). Doing logistic work (mining raw material and fabricating weapons) can sometimes be a pain, as developers have somehow managed to create a tedious system around it. Sometimes raw resources are hard to come by and complex equipment takes lot time to produce, which ends up annoying or boring the lesser amount of players running the supply side of each army. That sucks and I hope developers fix it eventually. Yet this is a sand box, if you don't like logi, then don't do it.

DISCLAIMER: This game can be hard to learn, specially the logistics side of things. So if you are new, ask for help. People are quite friendly here. Also, remember that everything you see was made by someone. If you don't know how to use a piece of equipment, DON'T USE IT AND ASK FOR HELP. Don't waste away someone else work. If you do, don't blame them when they get angry.\t\t\t\t\t
