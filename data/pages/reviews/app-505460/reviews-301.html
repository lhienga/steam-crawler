\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR- Came in already knowing what it was about, still surprised me with how unique it is. Get this game if you are on the fence.

So, I originally got this game cause one of my clans from another game decided to migrate over from one game to this one. I watched a video explaining the basics of the game and what it's about. Knowing how everything is dependent on other players and how different everyone's experience could be, I said to myself "Alright, it's on sale, it looks and sounds good, SCREW IT. Let's see how it is."

Let's start with my first actions in the game world. I logged in, Picked the Colonials (as my clan is part of the Colonials), did the basic training tutorial to get a feel for the mechanics and stuff, and figured out how to spawn into the main map. I started by doing some driving in a truck to get some supplies to the front line base and started joining in. Instantly I got killed while getting no kills myself other than spraying and praying with a machine gun and getting four kills (Which is taboo, I'll explain later). I took a step back after getting killed again, watched a basic video on how to effectively shoot, and instantly got better. I got back into the trenches and we advanced on the enemy players next to a friendly armored machine gun clearing the fields and some trenches. We were on the defense, and many in-game days and nights were spent defending the base from the enemy players seeking to overtake us. However, we ended up retreating due to their sheer numbers and letting them take over our position. However, we are winning this war still.

Now for what makes this game unique from any other top-down multiplayer war game. EVERYTHING. It challenges you to not take loot for yourself like a normal player, but instead take loot for your team. There are people doing all sorts of jobs completely by themselves, without the game assigning any classes. ITS ALL UP TO YOU HOW YOU WANNA HELP. There's people who drive around the map delivering supplies back and forth, those who provide intel and then come back and tell us, medics, and people like me who do mostly frontline fighting.

Speaking of the combat, this game is not in a rush to finish a round like PUBG or Fortnite. Wars could take WEEKS of real world time, and there are active players 24/7 fighting, supplying, and sabotaging for their team. We are talking THOUSANDS of real people doing this at any one time, for literal weeks. Firefights can last a long time. I once spent an HOUR of my 3 hours with this game in a trench full of my team exchanging fire with enemy players, each of us trying to push forward. And unlike most games, EACH SHOT COUNTS. Each bandage, gun and bullet was either crafted or found by another player (or players) which means that every shot missed is a waste of supplies. Also, there is no hit markers or exp gain. You get ranked up from commendations by other players, and you do not get sole credit for a kill, which is different, but good as well since it really exemplifies the team effort to get that kill together as a team.

There is a lot more to explain, but I would recommend taking the chance and getting this game. It's fun, has an active community, and you will not regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
