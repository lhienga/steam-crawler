+ Wide range of equipment including guns, grenades, artillery shells, binoculars, snipers, just loads.
+ Tonnes of vehicles from motorcycles, bikes, trucks, tanks, boats.
+ Every piece of equipment and vehicle must be made by a player and transported to the frontline like some sort of WW2 truck simulator.
+ Must mine resources in order to make the equipment/vehicles.
+ Huge reliance on a strong supply chain.
+ Can loot the entire inventory of the enemy you just killed.
+ Almost all buildings, trench, sandbag and emplacement is built by a player.
~ Can run out of ammo or med supplies in a fight extremely fast.
~ The whole mining, crafting in a factory and truck simulation game play is important, but not for everyone.
- Inventory management is clunky, takes time to gear up when you die.
- Game seems extremely hard for new players, 5-10 hour learning curve just for the basics.
- As with all 100% multiplayer games, will become a useless product when the playerbase falls to zero.

This is a huge 1000+ player war where supplies and cooperation are vital.
You need to respect the time someone put in to mine, craft and transport the gear you just pulled out of a base.
Without them, there is no war.

Could see why this may not be for everyone, but loving it so far.\t\t\t\t\t
