I have played this game for about every moment of free time I have had for 3 weeks now and I feel like there has been no long term feeling of progress. every game gives you a feeling of progression which I would say is vital to the enjoyment of the game in the long term, you get gear and levels in rpgs. you get farther in the story, you get more money, you get good enough at a fighting game to fight the best people, whatever.

Foxhole doesn't have that. in the 3 weeks I have played it, it feels like nothing has changed since the first day I started. You will be fighting in the same spot, in the same regions and towns and against the same bases. For weeks at a time.

I don't want to play anymore not because its a bad game but because I feel like i'm spinning my tires in the same spot. if you know you need that feeling of progression you should go elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think anyone with even a hint of interest should play this, mess with the Logistical tutorials for an hour, and then spawn on the frontlines for your 2nd hour before a refund. The one battle I got into was such a thrill because of the context of this game's whole shtick and accomplishment of said shtick. I could easily spend 1000 hours on this kind of game, but decided not to for bad game design decisions on the necessary Logistical side of the game. That being holding down LMB to gather scrap, build Every structure, and manually driving a truck as a necessary gameplay loop someone has to do and is so heavily weighted on success. So I admire the game the more I learn of it, but I won't personally play it for 1/2 of those 1000 hours would be spent holding LMB, or having fun on the backs of people who'd I consider scammed into needing to do so for me. I'd much prefer if Logistics was all about queuing decisions on what to produce for 20 minutes, and then going out there to actually play the game whether it be defending your bot truck supply lines or keeping watching over bases, or doing what's already showcased with going into frontline warfare. That way, every player is involved with Logistics and the Shooty part of the game. As it's currently, these experiences are wholly separate and cater to very different players. who both need to be playing the game for it to function.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tConclusion: Requires an unhealthy amount of time to commit. Toxic people ruin the experience.

Overall, it's a good game with interesting concepts. Great game for those who have an infinite amount of time for gaming. Very addicting and you put more time than you intended to. However, I have some issues and have to give a negative review.

Logistics is literally a slave job, not a game. Everything that has a value comes from someone wasting time on holding a left mouse button or W key. The game is not so much about tactics or team play but it's who has the best weapons/tanks at the right time of 24/7 constant war. And I haven't even experienced trains or facilities, which most people complain about now.

One thing I liked a lot about this game was that I could help my teammates by bringing material, building structures, rescuing wounded soldiers, etc. And I had some good moments too.

But there are some people who apparently have serious communication issues. Today, I was helping someone's building and he suddenly shot me for no reason. He later said he thought I was building something that he didn't want there (which I was not, I was building one of his blueprints), but how could that be a legitimate reason to kill a teammate?

I actually had a similar moment before, too. Someone made tank traps in the middle of the road and since it was a busy road, many tanks/trucks were stuck. I saw it and I went to grab a wrench to dismantle them. And when I came back and started dismantling it, someone suddenly shot me for no reason. After respawning, I went back to the place and tried to talk to the guy who shot me but then he came with a truck and ran over me and killed me again. No explanation from him.

Moments like this, getting killed by the teammates I wanted to help, ruined the game for me. Even though the deaths didn't cost me much because it was close to the base, the absurdity of team killing made me feel very uncomfortable. It seems some people take this game too seriously or can't understand why this game is multiplayer and cooperation/communication is encouraged.

Another major serious issue in the game is that, even after the 1.0 release, voice chat is broken for some people (Error message: Voice Chat Error [2] 2-5031). I don't even know the devs are aware of it and working on fixing it. The guy who killed me from the first above story told me he was warning me to stop building in voice chat, but how could I know what he said when it's broken and I can't hear anything? There wasn't even an indication on screen that someone was talking. So, be warned, the lack of voice chat can get you killed by a teammate.

-----

Other complaints:

# General
- Starting location.
When the game starts, you spawn at a random location of Home island and you have to search for another location to deploy to the actual world and you have to walk to it every time. Why can't we spawn at a specific location of Home island so that it's easier to find the deploy spot or spawn directly at a location we want to go? Very strange design.
- Lack of practical tutorials.
There is a tutorial in the first region but it doesn't explain much about how the game works. You have to watch someone's youtube videos to understand the very basic mechanics. IIRC, they don't teach you the basics of logistics at all so it takes a long time to figure out how to do a simple logi run. For example, I spent way too much time to figure out how to pick up containers from a seaport until someone kindly explained I had to package the container before picking it up with a crane. If logistics is a huge part of the game, everyone should learn how to do it in the tutorial. I have seen frontline people asking for bmats when the seaport in the same region has a lot of them. The current tutorial is not friendly to new players at all.

# Driving/vehicle
- Driving is not fun.
The minimap is too small and you have to keep checking if you are taking the correct road. Also there's no indication of other vehicles around you. And you can't see far on the screen. So you can hit people by accident and kill your teammates. And then, railways and gates can be cumbersome for drivers because it slows you down and you have to spend more time and focus while driving. The most boring part of the game becomes more boring as the time progresses.
- Squad lock.
There is a mechanism called "squad lock", which can be used for vehicles so that it can only be used among a specific group of people. Strangely, squad-locked vehicles can be driven by a non-squad player and they can put stuff in it. It doesn't make sense and it can actually cause trouble. I once used a squad-locked truck to do a logi run. And after spending some time, when I reached the destination and tried to unload the stuff, it said I couldn't unload from a squad-locked vehicle. Why do you let me drive the truck and put stuff in it then? I wasted time and probably also someone else's time because of it.

# Map
- No preset logi list for bases.
Every time we have a new front line, someone has to make a message post for logi list so that logi people can understand what is needed at frontline. But the logi list is not permanent so people have to upvote it to keep it alive (is this really a necessary process?) and it still can disappear after some time. And that poster can't make another comment on the map without deleting the logi list because one person can only make one post. Why can't we have a separate style of message dedicated for logi lists?
- Not user-friendly colors on the map.
The green/blue distinction on the map has far too less contrast and is too hard to tell apart, even for non-color blind people. Very inaccessible and unkind. Probably my biggest issue UI-wise.\t\t\t\t\t
