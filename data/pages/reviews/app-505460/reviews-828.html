\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen playing foxhole since 2018, since then the devs have released insane content that they once said would never be added.

The devs have been a little quiet since the inferno release which has been becoming normal in the past years, though I don't mind it as the updates have become increasingly more filled with content, balances, and bug fixes. One thing this community sadly forgets though is that every war is a playtest and we are always bound to encounter some retarded bugs that do eventually get fixed.

We complained back in 2019 about logistics so they added flatbeds, containers, freighters, and cranes. We continued to complain about logistics and every year they add more ♥♥♥♥ like changing the GSUPPs and shirts to their own queue in the factory and creating a MPF (Mass Production Factory). Recently they made one of the biggest updates yet by releasing player built facilties which took a load off of logi as it allowed coordinated groups to mass produce ammo, petrol, tank variants and other ♥♥♥♥♥.

In the update they also added trains which allows us to move insane amounts of resources, tanks, weapons and logistics over long distances with ease. At first there was no way to manage the tracks but once we complained they fixed the entire problem by adding cabooses.

(I'm not saying logistics is perfect but they've done a lot to make it way easier then it was before.)

Fire mechanics got added which was ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ awesome but also incredibly overpowered and annoying to deal with, they rewrote the entire fire mechanics system in a month to balance it out for us.

Foxhole is usually quite fun, you can take up tons of different roles from ferrying logistics to keep the front alive to sneakily partisanning the enemy logistics to choke their own fronts, the only true big problem is alting which right now the entire community is focused on alerting the devs about so hopefully they will fix it, the devs DO listen to us you just need to be ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ patient.

I'd recommend foxhole, it's fun but do remember that teamwork gets the job done your not gonna be able to supply an entire front or build an entire mega facility by yourself consider joining a regiment or getting some mates to join you and work together :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPRO:
-Constantly in development (new content, tweaks)
-A lot of players during nights
-Strong community presence

CON:
-Takes time implementing new features
-Cheaters and multi-account users (They can get banned you just need to report them)
-Game can feel overwhelming on Shard-2 servers with a lack of player (hopefully phased out with Entranched)

Story:
When I first started this game back in single match days, where maps where queued into by zone. There wasn't a seamless transition or multi-map wide logistics. It was barebones, not a lot to it. It was stale, had a lot of problems and after about 5 days playing I put it down. But I came back when 3x3 grid was released, much more interesting. Maps had really improved and the community grew in many ways, more players was great. However, there were more childish players, cheating or having two accounts to play both sides (Still is a problem). But I took it for what it was and moved forward. Since I've comeback yet again, hex areas have been introduced, faction specific weapons, tanks and play-styles and a lot of flavor (bunkers and trenches system for building was a great addition in my opinion).

I've played with a lot of great people joined various Warden Clans and have led and assisted in a lot of joint operations -mostly during late nights NA. I had a blast. After a while, it also got stale, with wars ending very very quickly. The population was lopsided and winning for the sake of winning was boring. I wanted a journey - a long drawn out war, where both sides were fighting tooth and nail over small land gains. But I knew it would take time. I was complaining a lot about night time fighting and wanted flares introduced (They eventually came - but it took time). So I vowed not to return to this game until it was fixed.

Now the game is back on track. It's MILES ahead of where it was when they first started. Yes, this game is an evolution. A myriad of trinkering and testing. It's not perfect, we know that, the devs know that and that is why they are "in it for the long haul".

Disclaimer: If you're one of those people that spends 3000hrs playing a game then tells me you wasted your money buying this game, that doesn't compute to me. 125 days. Say it with me 125 days. You spent 3000 hours playing a game to complain about spending time playing a game. You literally played this game nearly 2 hours a day for 5 years. You got the money out of the game you invested tenfold.\t\t\t\t\t
