This game is a blast, but the Devs are tone deaf to their community - I do not recommend buying the game until they decide to address issues the community has brought forward (with solutions!) and decide to do next to nothing or a half-measure of moderation/attempt at fixing the game's problems.

-ALTing - buying a second copy for greifing the other team or destroy vital infrastructure or supplies to undermine the war effort.

-Multiboxing - Kinda the same as ALTing but for one team - used to crew an entire tank, push gun, artillery, etc - basically you're playing World of tanks at that point and the fun of teamwork is lost

-Hacking - Several instances have arisen where hackers kill players who are WELL outside visible range (night mostly)

-Logistic problems - Many Quality of Life fixes have been made but facilities and other means of creating supplies is just a pain at some times as well as drawing supplies out of storage - an operation can take several hours to set up for only the tactical advantage to be lost within that timeframe of staging.

-Home Island and training - IS SEVERELY OUTDATED AND NEEDS A REVAMP - needs to force new players to actually learn mechanics and not just jump in and mess about and cause their team more harm than help - let the clans submit training material its not hard to get this right...

-Dev 'solutions' - Some of the solutions devs have pushed are undermined or outright ignored and push a new tank variant in players faces as a bait and switch, only for it to be terrible or overpowered - the return of BTs and their variants.

-Localization - as of today (Feb 10 2023) Russian players JUST got a chance to buy the game and have flooded front lines with inexperience and a massive language barrier between other players. This has resulted in many instances where people shoot each other out of frustration or unwillingness to cooperate, meanwhile devs have raved about language packs and chat filters to allow only certain languages to be read - this hurts the community and you cant cooperate if you cant communicate, I have to learn basic russian now to even get a chance to help these guys

​

TL:DR - Game GOOD, Devs are the step-father you never wanted, and just simply watch as bad things happen to the community and does very little to reprimand those who/what causes trouble.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA whopping 700 hours in this game. When I first started playing, I considered this something new, fresh, and exhilarating. Though it remains an enjoyable and thrilling experience, the development studio has been slow to address issues, though this has recently improved. My The Good, The Bad, The Ugly review follows.

Tldr version:

The Good- Exhilarating war with a role for everyone. The players really make this thing unique and fun. Real life band of brothers vibes on every front. MOST players very welcoming and friendly to newbies. Whether you are a grunt in the meat grinder, a medic treating the wounded, a truck driver, or a salvager, you've got a role and respect since everyone contributed to the war effort.

The Bad: Grind for Logistics mixes EVE Online with Factorio. If you have the stomach for that, it's still unsettling. Balance issues remain that strike deep and harm the grand wars. Still buggy with an unhealthy dose of lag in many regions. Region queues, while necessary, can wreck your game when your squad or friends are in zone, and you can't deploy to join them. You can go elsewheren to die for your side, but it's best played with friends.

The Ugly: Cheesy, albeit mechanically possible, work arounds sneak by developers in betas. Players find them quickly and exploit them until patched.

Longer Version:

The Good: Fill a variety of roles, fight in amazing battles, join squads and regiments and coordinate either small skirmishes or massive tank battles. Or die in the trenches under heavy shelling and machine gun fire. Those aspects were incredible, and still are. The players, though, they make it a truly incredible experience. From shouting for medica to save them and thanking their madnrushbtheough bullets, to Logistics players dodging ambushes and mortars to deliver critical supplies, to the sounds of the Battle Bus zipping troops with grenades to the front, to shouts of Valhalla! Mind blowing and incredible.

The Bad: Have you ever been to Deadlands and rubber banded in-between everything? What about seen a tank commander flying cross your screen as if transported from a game of Quidditch? Ghost obstacles that block you but never render. All of this remains problematic. There is very little attention to these bugs.

Balance remains a key concern, but are slowly moving in the right direction. Avoiding the dev bias remarks each side tosses around, there are remaining balance issues players have voiced for months and years. I recall, during the 1.0 announcement (you can find it on YT) , a dev laughing about how his changes would make the balance team upset. It wasn't tongue in cheek. It was real. Not something that builds trust in the community and, frankly, should have been reprimanded.

The Ugly: Cheating players that join one side to spy or sabotage it (alts) remain an issue. Region queue issues are killing the experience, especially as one side losses players as the war progresses towards defeat. The disruption of the modding community was a hard pill to swallow and shows the overall lack of thoughtful solutions to some of the issues regarding cheaters and balance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am updating my review after multiple changes were announced, and while I won't go into them here, suffice to say these changes <i>should</i> solve or at least abate most, if not all of the major complaints I'd made in the original review.

It will take some time for the community to lose its paranoid toxicity, but I am now generally willing to recommend.

I will be leaving my previous review below, as to better prepare new players as to the community they will be joining for the foreseeable future, and as a reminder of the mountain of negativity the same community had to level at the devs to get ANY response, let alone changes

Foxhole, where to truly begin? Foxhole is a 24-hour semi-arcadey war-sim, where every grenade thrown, round fired, litre of fuel burned and weapon held was made by the effort of other players. Having played for some time over it's lifespan thus far, Foxhole CAN be a very fun, but a rising epidemic in-game has massively impacted every part of the game, and makes the game hell even when you are trying your best. The problem is unscrupulous players using alternate accounts, "Alts" to sabotage the opposing team without repercussions. The epidemic of Alts has created an environment of paranoia, fear and hostility throughout the community; with much of the hostility towards new players, whom are often mistaken for Alts due to simply lacking the game knowledge to avoid playing poorly and losing materiel. however, the fear of Alts and more general sabotage has resulted in anyone, of any experience level or playtime being targeted for harassment for even the most minor of perceived infractions, failures or mistakes.

To make all of this even worse is the lack of ANY communication from the devs, with not even a cookie-cutter "we hear your concerns!" to show they are working on a solution. Some circles in the community believe that the devs inaction is a result of greed, as even known Alt Accounts are rarely, if ever, given a permanent ban; This light punishment is fuel for a conspiratorial fire that argues that the devs are avoiding perma-bans to encourage alt-ing by demonstrating that alt accounts are safe from permanent repercussions.

If and When these concerns are addressed, I will update this review; until such a time, however, I have one concise statement to sum up my review:

STEAR CLEAR

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t03.07.23 edit: Some of my concerns may be ironed out with the info from the most recent update 52 dev livestream. Will update review after 3-4 weeks of hands-on once game updated.
04.14.23 edit #2: Still leaving this as a not recommended. The new UI improvements were badly needed, and are appreciated (despite them needing to have been there at 1.0 start). Devs seem content to all-in on facilities being the stop-gap for 70%~ of vehicle content, making what was once in the past a modest trip with mats to the garage or MPF for tanks a multi-stage, dozens hours, uptime-bloated job to get to the same result, without an equivalent exchange in fun. I'm sad I feel this way as you can imagine, given my hours invested, but i'll likely be on hiatus unless they make facilities supplemental (as originally stated) and not a gate.

---

I've been playing this game for a very long time, first picking it up sometime years ago when devs were still debating whether to include a text chat, boats were in development, and quartermaster was a socially appointed role in charge of delegating where guns, ammo, and resources went. It was a neat game, and I put it down while waiting for more updates.
Sometime in war 83 (each war lasts about a month) I picked the game back up again and all-in'ed on it.
I marveled at the fact that 99% of everything used in wars were mined up, refined, and produced by players, and that true defensive positions, bunker complexes, were designed, made, and maintained by players.

I became proficient in and known for building and I took pride in it. The actual building was endless grind and maintenance requiring many players for any kind of efficiency or speed, and garrison supplies, which required raw material and extensive production and delivery time. Yet building was and still is considered one of the most critical aspects of the game, as its the only thing stopping the enemy from rolling over sparsely-automatically-defended AI towns.

Prior to the 1.0 update, despite constant incessant tribalistic bickering between the two factions, Wardens and Colonials, most could reasonably say the game was in a goodish place; everyone can still agree there is nothing like Foxhole.
With the 1.0 update came flame weapons and yet heavier weapons capable of easily reducing dozens of collective hours of building, maintenance, and supply to rubble in less than an hour. Newly constructed bases need at least 24 hours of constant supervision to enable them to automatically defend themselves, or risk two experienced players with flamethrowers burning down a minimum 6 hours of work (not including prep) in less than 10 minutes.
Many variants of tanks and cannon pushguns were locked behind new facility systems requiring their own heavy investment; however, without any additional communication tools informing others what to expect from a facility, what it needs, and what it can produce, there are (still) no reasonable in-game means to coordinate efforts beyond temporary map call-outs and shouting blindly in chat for what you need and what you can give.
Most tank types, including the biggest and most unique, Battletanks and Superheavy Tanks, cannot be stored privately anywhere, meaning unless you take prohibitively annoying steps to secure them, it is plausible for people who have directly invested nothing in their construction to use a simple wrench to take them.

As someone who has invested and continues to invest so much time in the game, it pains me to say that the game isn't in a healthy place. I do hope the devs at minimum allow facilities to perform a supportive role, such as giving optional enhancements or modifiers to tanks instead of outright restricting their production to them, add an optional "fire resistant" upgrade to bunkers, and add additional in-game communication tools such as a bill-board structure acting like 24 hour map signposts needing refreshed. This game is a truly unique, compelling, cooperative experience dragged down by predatory-mobile-game-levels of grind; it may only require an arguably cheap one-time cost, but it will completely consume your time in mindless tasks to "get to the good parts," and depending on your preferred play-style, unceremoniously take those good parts away anytime.\t\t\t\t\t
