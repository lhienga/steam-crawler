\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo many, Foxhole is a frustrating game for many, being only one person on one of two teams with Hundreds and sometimes Thousands of players on at any given time. I played for a while when the game was in its early development and now returned for the Winter army update while these are my thoughts on it so far. Starting from your first log in (After choosing a team which you are locked into for the rest of the war. I am a Warden.) you are dropped in a home region being given no directions as well as only being given locations on the maps that only give basic instructions on what to do when it comes to fighting, logistics, building, driving, and asset explanations. Me and a few other veteran players have found we have to personally show new players what to do, how to play, and show some of the more advanced mechanics to give them a easier transition into the game, even using the basic facilities in the home region to do so, however this is only use introducing them to the game they themselves have to learn from experience that on your own you can't change the course of the war, except for a few rare scenarios. The deployment maps are not really defined to the point where some new players have to ask veteran players where to go. Choosing where to spawn however may seem confusing is rather straight forward, I recommend newbies start with being near the front lines to understand how the core combat mechanics work in real time. The aiming mechanics are a little weird when trying to shoot at opponents on a completely different height whether it be down a shallow hill or up at someone standing on a mountain. And if you aren't careful can even shoot a teammate in front of you. On the back line or logistics side you have to manage you materials in factories as well as the supply in bases across the front line. Lastly teamwork, as I said before you are one in a thousand players so you have to work together. I often see new players asking or even staying near the highest level player even if they seem competent or not which is okay but there is no organization which led to the creations on numerous clans to try and organize these people. I've talked with a few players who I helped out and even came to me asking if they should join one or feel pressured to join. Especially when when they occupy massive frontier bases and just expecting these non-clan members to essentially be a grunt, though I don't blame them for this, its not entirely healthy either. There for I fell that I feel it is reasonable to give a negative review. But you should STILL TRY IT.

Green Man Bad
I also recommend you check YouTube I recommend a YouYuber "I Saw a Bear" as a start.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Note: I did not cover naval warfare in this review because I personally don't have much experience with it. I will probably add more to this at a later date.

TL;DR
1. New players need a better introduction to the game
2. Game play while not bad can be repetitive and grow boring to some
3. Clan culture while not inherently bad can be toxic to a degree
4. Difficult, grueling, but satisfying in the end.
5. Don't be afraid or nervous to ask other players questions, we love to see the community grow and new player enjoying the game as much as we do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole, what a name it has become. Unfortunately I couldn't recommend this game.

The idea of the game is brilliant. Having a structure of the bigger picture. Large map, from base to frontline. And all in between.This all, already being said better so am not going to and will get to the point.

Your spending hours and hours on building trenches, bunkers and tanks. Gathering resources and manufacturing them. You have to do it all in a row because you can't save your progress.When you finally have all the ingredients to make it, or someone shoots your blueprint. Hard worked resources are gone, or even worse they steal your tank...As same for the trenches and bunkers you made. The next day you come back and your allies disagree about the layout and destroy your hours and hours of work.

This all combined it basically leave you only with the combat. And to be fair. There are way better combat games then this one.Don't get me wrong the idea is unique and good. And what I understand of players that played this game from the beginning, it made progress.  However it's far from fun.\t\t\t\t\t
