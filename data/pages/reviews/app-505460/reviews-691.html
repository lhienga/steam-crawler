\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOther than people yelling at their mics, this game hardly provides any fun
unless you're completely commited with a few of your friends
and you're prepared to literally slave away for the sake of playing the game.

I do not exactly like anything about this game, although it has a bunch of stuff.

Logistics are literally slavery, for some reason they've decided to include mining to a game about WW2.
Driving trucks are fun in a Eurotruck simulator kinda way without the actual fun driving physics.

The combat is alright unless someone shoots you from out of your screen. I do not even mean artillery, you have to line up your screen diagonally so you can see more.
Then you get gassed, or run out of stamina, or get shot by an npc foxhole then get slowed and die, then you have to run all the way back, then run out of stamina running back. The bloodbath is fun for a moment, then gets repetitive quickly.

Artillery, and vehicles... or let me say any progression in general is so unnecessary in this game. Because it is so streched into such a long period of time and such a large map, and for what? So it will feel like a real war with hundreds of people? To make you feel minuscule? Then it works because i feel like everyone just plays the easiest way, like an average joe forced to fight. A few mortars and and gas grenades to clear chokes, then rush to death. You rarely see anything interesting. Because if you try to grind for a tank, or an apc, it will take hours to make, then another half an hour to carry it to the front line. Then you will still risk losing it all in just 5 minutes. It really all depends on one mistake and you will lose it.

The defenses are built very chaotically because usually enemy comes from every direction. And when the battle is done, the bunkers and trenches are left to rot. Nothing strategic about it. Just randomly build them when necessary.

What's persistent about this game is how grindy and how much of a bother this game is.

I will still give this game the benefit of the doubt even after 10 hours, and say that probably, if you join the discord, and follow the war, commit to the community and the game for an hundred hours, it might be fun. But i've yet to see droplet of that fun.\t\t\t\t\t
