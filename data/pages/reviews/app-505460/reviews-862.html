\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game after seeing some amazing looking YouTube videos and I did have some fun running around doing my own thing however once you start getting into the meat of the product it all seems to fall apart.

I joined up with a clan of experienced folks with the idea of getting into larger operations and despite the meticulous planning of those guys it was an absolute sh1tshow. It's too easy for people to use 'spies', basically dual-logged across both factions to watch the oppositions movements and worse actively steal blueprints, grief others etc. This is supposedly a bannable offence though doesn't seem to slow them down.

When those griefers weren't interferring it was the game that was causing problems. Three times we sent a large invasion force with dozens of barges full of tanks, trucks loaded with supplies, troops etc and the moment we tried to cross a zone border things just broke. Tanks and trucks would just vanish into thin air during the transition, sometimes whole barges. We pulled back, contacted the 'moderations' which are basically GM's/first-line customer support and waited. To paraphrase the response - 'bad luck, deal with it'. So we rebuilt over the next few hours and tried again only to have the barges and gunboats that made the crossing all end up stacked on top of each other. Yes, literally a vertical stack of ships and barges loaded with tanks etc all piled on top of each other and unable to move. 2 GM's and a developer turned up but vOv. My APC despawned and I ended up drowning in the middle of the ocean. This was about 5 or 6 hours AFTER we started out.

So all in all, the idea of a grand large scale war that you're a part of sounds great but the reality is the game will make that very difficult to accomplish and when you add in the griefing and exploits it becomes an unenjoyable experience. Yes there is some fun to be had, but it's hard to see all your progress undone because a griefer drops artillery on your defensive position and wipes your guys out so the enemy can rush in unapposed.

Get the game on sale if you really must but otherwise wait it out and see if the devs fix it or the community jumps ship.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on Dec 19, this review is from Dec 27. During this 1 week period I've logged 53 hours in this game.

I cannot on good conscious say anything about this game to try to convince you to play it. I've felt the lowest lows and the highest highs. The last game that has made me feel this bad about my self-worth is Rust or Everquest. I would not recommend this game to a friend or even an enemy.

I spend 8 hours driving resources from one location to another, building defenses, sign out thinking i've accomplished something. Then a mere 7 hours later I sign back in and we've been completely overrun and everything I labored over is dust in the wind.

Next day I change gears and focus just on infantry battles, maybe taking part in some artillery. It's a meat grinder. Don't get attached to your gear. Your gear does not belong to you, it belongs to the ground.

The first day playing this will be brutal for new comers. If you do buy this game, I'd recommend doing the Skirmish side map and just messing around before jumping into the global battle.



300+ hours edit:

this game is still a frustrating exercise in futility. as an individual your contributions are tears in the rain. with no long form progression or incentives for playing you are literally throwing away time that could be spent on bettering yourself as a person.

people in this game will yell at you over coms over minor infractions or perceived slights.
you will yell at people over coms over minor infractions and real slights.
there are large imbalances in roles in terms of finding the fun in the gameplay. I don't think anyones fantasy is to doing logi for a bunch of ingrates. I could spend hours farming bmats and delivering it to newly established areas or delivering 120s to an artie crew and only get a few commends for hours of work. or i could just drive up to an active front, wait for a bunch of people to respawn and dump a bunch of ammo and SS and get dozens of commends for basically no effort.

people will ask you to hand deliver things to them and they can't be bothered to do it themselves when there's literally a storage depot in town overflowing with bmats and shirts. logi will completely abandon regions and you slowly lose ground to attrition. people who build with a binary vision, no concept of temporary measures or defense, building with what you have for value. or people who spend all of the bmats building mg nests that a tank will just come through and destroy in a few minutes.

despite all of my complaints I can't stop coming back to this game. i'll spend all weekend doing a lot of stuff that basically amounts to nothing. I've quit this game several times in my head only to wake up the next day and say "I wonder how callahan's pass is doing today" (spoiler alert it's awful)

once again i cannot recommend this game to anyone and i would never ask a friend to play this with me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game really does it's intended job well, and better at it than any other game I can think of. It's playstyle is unique and it's features are completely player dependent, a game in which would definitely not survive if it didn't have the flourishing playerbase that it does. A 'shooter' game, a war game, but in a very different setting than typical games. Every construct, every objective, it's all player crafted and created. Destroyed, rebuilt, captured it's all done by large scale amounts of players. Logistics, teamwork, operations. So much goes into the game and every player has the ability to play a tremendous role, without ever firing a single shot if they so wish. With almost sixty hours of playtime in about half a week of owning this game, I can decisively say I'm hooked to it and it's community. Some of the best fun i've had playing games in a multiplayer environment. I have already made friends I will probably never stop talking to. The developers are great, transparent with the community and still place in active updates to keep the game fresh. I absolutely highly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
