\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is the most ingenious multiplayer title to come along in ages. It's regular bottled lightning, but the devs don't seem to know what to do with half of it.
The combat in this game is some of the best out there, because the ongoing war adds context, and the potential loss of material adds tension. Every gun and bullet, aside from a small token stockpile each 'match,' are made by players.
The problem is that making guns and bullets is grindy, unsatisfying, and almost purposefully unfun. The use of autoclickers is mandatory, not for any competitive benefit, but just to stave off repetitive motion injuries. The devs seem aware, and almost proud, of the fact that this aspect of the game is both unfun and actually, factually, physically damaging to its own playerbase. A claim few other games can manage.
In an old dev blog, they even claimed that 'holding left mouse, unmoving for hours,' was part of the games identity and couldn't be changed. Does that sound like your idea of fun?
In essence, Foxhole is one of the best 'war simulators' I have ever seen, but the fun of combat is paid for on the back of other players the developers seem almost maliciously insistent should have the worst experience possible. Be it their primary gameplay being holding a button with just enough interruptions to avoid automation, or waiting for timers the devs keep increasing because of some bizarre notion of balance solely through wasted time. The devs themselves admit to using workarounds and external keypress holding software to avoid these parts of their own design, and joke they wouldn't even play it because they 'have a job already.'

I'll consider coming back to this review after full release to see if they've figured out how to design the backend half of their game, but for now my vote is one of no confidence. Never before have I seen a dev team adamant that their game not even being enjoyable is part of 'their vision.'\t\t\t\t\t
