I bought Foxhole in early-access, about 2 months prior to the Entrenched Update and I absolutely love this game. I have by far the most hours on this game compared to any other I own and frankly it shows.

Foxhole is a game that is incredibly addictive, you can easily go from one position to another in a very fluid manner, with each position incredibly fleshed out and exciting. I'll include an example from earlier in this war:

I, a medic, near the Iris (east side of the map) needed medical supplies and looking on the map found some in a bunker about 500 meters away, running there I found the medical supplies I needed to heal people and found a truck with fuel. As no-one claimed the truck, I thought it would be better to run logi (logistics) to the frontline bunker, so people won't have to run to the backlines to get medical gear like I did. Upon leaving to get medical gear from a logi port, I passed a decayed base (keeping that in mind) I delivered the supplies and returned to the decayed base to repair it.

In the span of about 50 minutes, I went from medic, to logiman, to engineer and each felt like I would have a clear definable method of aiding the team and my allies. Now some of this is due to my short attention span, but on a good front you can have hours of fun just playing one role. (Infantry, tanker, medic, support).

Even the scrapping and material making can be fun in small doses, put on some music and drown your eyes with scrooping, or run materials from the far backlines to the midlines. It's incredibly fun.

Additionally, the community is one of the most engaging and welcoming to new players that I have ever seen(for the most part, more on that below). My first 300 hours was all done without the aid or membership of a regiment, and I remember those days fondly. Community engagement can lead to all sorts of fun just with people being themselves, or playing characters on a battlefield.


So why the "No" Vote?

The stereotypical cry for attention. Simply put, as I engage in higher levels of Foxhole discourse I have noticed the developers have ways to just not care about listening to their audience in ways that make us feel heard. I have two examples of this.

First is the LOGI Union. A group of international players who worked to form a real-life union for an online game. It sounds crazy, but it happened (and is still happening). It was big news in the community, lovers, haters, and the in-between all talked about the Logi Union; it was even covered by a couple Games News Channels. The following update, there were some marginal changes to logistics mechanics, but there was no official acknowledgement from the developers. It was as if they were some big company trying to strike-break a union by offering cookies and crumbs to get them to stop. Despite not fully committing to the Union myself, it was an obvious sign that things would continue in that manner.

Second, is the problem with alts and cheaters. To preface this, I will say for a long-time I have resisted this complaint, a lot of people use "alt" as an insult to to derogate the other people or regiments in the game and can be highly divisive within the community. "Alt" stands for a person who creates another account in Foxhole to join the other team than their main account to purposely sabotage logistics, vehicles, bases, and intelligence (planned operations that sort of thing). However, this war I have seen first-hand multiple people who gas out entire townhalls full of people, or drive cranes with emplacements into rivers, try to steal tanks from others, and generally be a nuisance.

This has always been a problem, however never at a level that has committed vast grievances across the whole community. In the end, oftentimes alts do the work of partisans, and those who are more obvious are caught (but never banned), but the real problem is the damage it causes to the community. Take a quick scan of other negative comments, many come from new players who got "caught" as an alt and were quickly mass reported. Alts know what to do, they are veterans who go in with a specific task and leave before they can be caught. A new player makes a mistake and they are lynched for it. Because these days more people are quicker to judge, mistakes have been made that led to waste of resources and we are more suspicious as a whole and I hate that.
The other day, people were talking in region-chat about a suspected alt, I thought they were just being babies about a new player. 5 minutes later in the height of an artillery barrage, he gassed out the townhall, we survived, but it makes me weary to trust new players. And I went through that experience as a new player.

Siege Camp and the development team of Foxhole hurt me. They insult the players' intelligences and they roll out updates without a word of acknowledgement. I will continue to play, but I do not recommend new players to until this review becomes positive.\t\t\t\t\t
