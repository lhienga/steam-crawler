\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't know what to expect coming into this game as i'd never really played anything like it. Like probably many on here I had seen the game being played by a few youtubers. Eventually I decided to shill the coin and give it a shot. If you like building towards research/being a delivery driver then this game has it. If you like fighting on the frontlines it also has that. As you've probably seen in trailers (or game play) the game is mostly played on a large multi hexagonal map with sever loading between hexagonal areas. There is a pop cap on the hexagonal areas which can make reinforcements and logitics a problem during over saturated fighting times. In the game there are clans that you could join or choose not to. Depending on the clan it can either be a boon to your playing experience or a sour one. In my case it was 50/50 experience. I understand running a clan isn't easy sometimes and can stress people out (when you would think the point would be to have fun). This game is played by both immature young children as well as immature older adults, and when these people run clans and are stressed they tend to take it out on anyone in the clan instead of understanding this a game to have fun playing. Sadly, they both tend to be in almost all clans while the chill ones tend to be in smaller or otherwise not in a clan at all. There are some chill people in larger clans but they are few and far between. If you decide to join a clan don't feel any sense of loyalty to them. If you feel you're not having fun in a clan then join a different one or not one at all. You don't owe them your fun time.

Besides that, the developers listen to their player base and are constantly improving the game. The mechanics are pretty polished at this point and are only getting better. The game can be really fun at times or even almost all the time. Don't let other clans bullying get to you.\t\t\t\t\t
