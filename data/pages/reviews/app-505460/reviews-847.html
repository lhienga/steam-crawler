\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite it being in Early Access, Foxhole at this point feels like a complete game that is a lot of fun to play.

You control a single soldier working to win a massive war by helping your team control several large maps. You're not given any instruction, so you can support the war how you see fit. There are several ways to contribute. If you want to fight, do you want to be an infantry solider? If so, do you want to be on the front lines, backing up a squad as a medic, or sitting in the bushes or a foxhole as a stealthy sniper? Perhaps drive or be a gunner on a tank or boat? The choice is yours. Not interested in fighting? Well, every bullet, gun, and item used in the war needs to be crafted, maybe gathering or running supplies and troops to the front is more your deal. Like to build? Focus on constructing automated defenses to secure areas and make it easier for your team to respawn/harder for the other team to take back. There's something for everyone here.

This game really shines in its multiplayer interaction. The community is mostly non-toxic, and people seem serious about winning the war (on both sides). You can award commendations to soldiers you feel go above and beyond, and can be commended (and promoted) yourself. Once you hit a certain (early) rank you can organize operations and squads (and you can join them at any time), so organization is not that difficult. Turn on voicechat for the full experience.

One challenge that I see is that Foxhole doesn't hold your hand too much at the start, and it's possible for some players to come out of basic training (if they can find it) confused. However, all you need to do is ask for help in chat and it's likely someone will help you.

In short, Foxhole strikes a great balance between coop and competitive and offers ways for any type of gamer to get involved.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is one of the most fun top-down shooters I played, or one that I would keep playing. The ability to do whatever job you want is very cool. This game gives you the freedom to do anything (military wise). This game doesn't hold your hand at all, I mean it shows you the basic controls, but if you want to learn the logistics, infantry, building, etc, you pretty much either learn on your own, or ask around. The community is one of the chillest communities I've seen in my gaming life. If you find a high rank (SSgt or above mainly) they'll usually tell you the ropes of what you want to learn. Yes, there are bugs, and a good amount of them, some can be game breaking (e.g - Truck going off a barge freaks out and your some how on the barge horizontally not vertically, but give it some slack, it's Early Access and I bet the team isn't that big to fix everything. Every reward comes from your comrades not the game (rewards usually come through commends if you want to rank up). This game is not forgiving and does not reward you. I Remember when I made some defenses on a part of a region for 3 hours and just to have it get destroyed by the enemy with mortars. You can lose or win something so easily no matter the defenses, just be tactical and honestly, if it's major defenses you'd probably just bomb it to kingdom come. There's actually stuff I didn't really talk about in this review, but I don't want this review to be lengthy.

So far, I think it deserves an 8/10. It's a YES for me.\t\t\t\t\t
