\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: Persistent world where the economy is 99% player-created aside from the starting locations of raw resources and logistics towns at the beginning of each war. Anything available for the public remains available for everyone else in the game to use and continue providing and fighting for your team.

Clans (regiments) of all sizes want to carve out their slice of the world to make a difference and help provide for the fight where it's most helpful. And the majority of clan groups are very helpful, willing to teach new players about what to do to accomplish things, and provide direction to understanding the game's fundamentals better.

Devs are semi-hands off with the community in a game where cooperation is near-mandatory, but the player-base came together to build a social construct that works to get people where they want to go to play the parts of the game that's most fun for them. There is 1 official Foxhole discord for the global community to commune, but the rest of the collaboration and decision-making is a 100% player-created and moderated society.

International community of more than a dozen languages yet still this large of a community can overcome language barriers to work together.


Cons: Not 100% of the world is player-created: the raw resources, victory points to capture, and which towns have which logistics buildings are set at the beginning of each war and remain until the end of the war (average about 13-20 days to complete a full war).

Because the world is a persistent state, everything you build and provide is available for everyone else to use, which comes with 2 mechanics: 1) other players are able to interact with, utilize, and take away things that may have made more sense to keep there or use differently. 2) everything that is player-created must be used/maintained or else it will decay and eventually disappear from the world if left inactive long enough.
The 1st point can be circumvented with locking your vehicles and creating private stockpiles, but those need to be continuously maintained to remain locked specifically to you as well (vehicle locks in the world last about 5mins, and each player can make 3 private stockpiles per Storage Depot or Seaport that lasts 50hrs).
The 2nd point can be circumvented by using the vehicles, weapons, etc. before it decays, or by providing Supplies (supps) to prevent structures from decaying.

Because of the ability for players to utilize others' created equipment, some players choose to be toxic and claim public things are theirs and grief/troll to prevent others from playing, or if a clan-sized group bands together to act toxic, they may think their efforts are worth more than non-clan or that others will misuse gear, and they may prevent people they've deemed unfit from playing differently.
Luckily both of these scenarios can be circumvented with the player-reporting system and by not retaliating in a toxic manner; the world is so huge that no single clan, regardless of size, will ever accomplish so much to affect the entire war effort, it still requires a large amount of cooperation to win the war. And if someone does something toxic to slight you, use the F1 screen to find the player and Report them.
Once a Report is filed, devs DO engage, investigate, and issue bans of varying degrees depending on how severe the infraction is.


TLDR; Foxhole is an absolutely massive undertaking of a game requiring a large amount of cooperation between people from all over the world. There's a lot to be done, every little bit helps, and expect the same pros and cons here as will come with interacting with dozens of players on the internet from around the world in any other multiplayer game.
(Also 'U' enables/disables voice chat if you're tired of hearing people talk)\t\t\t\t\t
