\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere really isn't another game quite like Foxhole, as far as I know. The community has rough members but by and large my intro has been stellar. This is definitely a game where it is more fun to talk and be social, which isn't always easy. I promise you though, the reward for toughing out the awkwardness is well worth it. Find people who talk, stick with people who are making plans, and don't be afraid to be up front if you don't know something. Ask questions!

In terms of gameplay, I love the top-down style. The line of sight features remind me of Project Zomboid, and I find myself scanning the distance and sweeping corners much like in that game. The slower, methodical controls reward patience and a cool hand under pressure, as it takes time to accurately sight in on weapons. While it takes a bit of practice to move with grace, once you learn the ropes it becomes a novel and engaging experience.

There's nothing quite like spawning in the capitol, learning the basics of logistics, shipping equipment YOU made from scrap you scavenged, loading shells into artillery, then charging in as infantry to help the armor mop up what's left. Foxhole rewards every player with unique and various ways of contributing to the front's success. Whether you're a partisan behind enemy lines, a foot soldier in the meat grinder, or logistics and scouting, every little bit helps.

This game's a steal at its full price, and even better on sale. If you're on the fence, try it out for an hour. Just remember that communication and teamwork are very important in making this good game great for everyone involved.\t\t\t\t\t
