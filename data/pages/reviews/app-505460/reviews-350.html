Also Foxhole is a team-game where EVERYTHING you do either is helpful or detrimental to your team. What I recommend if you are new to the game is to join one of the big open squads and ask for help. An example of this on the Colonial side is the WL LOGI and on the Warden side you have SPUD and they will point you in the right direction for what you want to do. This game can be played as a casual solo player but to get anything done you need to join a regiment. Also "mass reporting" is not as big of an issue that some reviews make it sound. Does it happen? Yes, but you usually have to do something exceptionally toxic/stupid for that to happen and it lasts for 1h. You can't get permanently banned by players, unlike what some reviews claim. It is possible to send videos to the devs for review which usually lead to a 3 day suspension if it is severe, again not a permanent ban. Can you get a perm-ban? Probably with enough video evidence but that is for the deves to decide, not players.

Also everything that is not in a PRIVATE stockpile in a seaport/depot is considered public. End of story. You putting "private parking" doesn't make your tanks private or safe it makes you look stupid, especially when you log off and partisans start to show up... But what is a partisan?

Partisans:
The people that go behind enemy lines, place listening kits to get intel from enemy watchtowers, etc. They are also really good at finding that one tank that a player thought they put in a great spot "for later" and are now using to harass the logi-lines. I suggest you play as a partisan with a few friends (or solo) because it is a lot of fun and you are 100% guaranteed to find undefended tanks, fieldguns, trucks etc on both the Warden and Colonial side! If you think your base/parking-lot is safe there are entire groups of partisan on the enemy team ready to prove you wrong.

Frontline:
If you are new to the game go to a bridge fight and learn how the guns work, jump into a tank, fire artillery or save people as a medic. Please note that both both frag-granades and gas granades can be cooked and friendly fire is a thing. There is also no such thing as a friendly mine however they only trigger on vehicles that are considered "armed" so in other words if your vehicle can shoot or if a gun can be mounted on it. Flatbeds and logi trucks doesn't trigger mines.

Logistics:
Logistics is the backbone of every single war. Without LOGI you can't push, you can't defend and you can't have fun no matter if you are a salty vet or new player. Everything, from ammo to tanks and massive bunker complexes in the game are all player made and LOGI are the boys and girls that farm resources, refine them and make all the toys everyone else use to have fun in the game. They drive for hours on end to get equipment to where it is most needed and are the true heroes of every single war. Always commend your logi!

Engi:
The reason all oceans are so salty is that once upon a time a Foxhole engineer decided to take a bath. And that still was not enough to wash away all the salt. How do I know this? Because I'm a Collie Engineer. But there is a very good reason why engineers are the most salty/toxic players in this game. If you are not completely screwed over by the terrain and building mechanics in this game there is a 100% chance, without fail, that someone from your own team is going to absolutely ruin your day, week or month. This is usually when new players want to "help" or FoxKarens don't want to be told to please not do something. Let me explain.

You first need to know that everything in this game; boxes, sandbags, barbwire fence, containers, bunker pieces, emplaced guns etc cost Garrison Supplies to keep alive. Every. Single. Thing. The cost of things is also determined by the tier of AI garrison that your base has teched. For example, the Small Garrison tech (that also allows for permanent AI) keeps everything in an 80m radious around your base alive for 2 G-fuel/h/thing. Large Garrison reduces this cost to 1 G-sup/h. G-sup/B-sup are the only thing LOGI will not supply because it is a pain and you are forced to make them yourself, usually in the backlines. If the base runs out of sups it decays and dies.

When you make a new base you need to tech it and choose what to prioritize from the 3 tech trees - Base. Garrison and Command. Base will always tech as long as your bunker has activity around it however both Garrison and Command requires modifications. Once a core has unlocked a tech that tech stays with the core until it is "tapped" (enemy reset) even if you remove all modifications. To complete the Garrison tree you need a total of 24 modifications and for the Command tree you need 40. Below are the rough times it takes for your bunker to tech up in REAL TIME and to fully tech a base you have to spend a bit over 2 weeks just waiting.

"Base"
-Provisional Garrison ~10-15 min
-Small Garrison ~8-10h
-Large Garrison ~3 days

"Garrison"
-Infantry Garrison ~10-15 min
-AT Garrison ~5-10h
-Howitzer Garrison ~3 days

"Command"
-Observation Bunker ~2-3h
-Concrete ~24h
-Deployment Point ~3 days
-Advanced Bunker ~3-4 days

Here is the thing with building. All it takes is one FoxKaren that wants to play the game "their way" to completely throw away an entire front and weeks of work for hundreds of people. No joke. If anything is parked in front of garrisons it blocks them from firing. Same goes for when you build in front of them, to a certain extent. Sandbags are the bane of every single builder. If you want your base to survive never, ever under any circumstances bring sandbag pallets to your base.
You are also not allowed to remove a building piece after 3 hours from when it was dug or change a garrison to a different one after it has been built. However you are allowed to reserve pieces that you have dug for two days. Now let's say you forgot to refresh that reservation and you had left some space for future AT-garrisons. A helpful player comes up and decides that these places would be amazing for rifle garrisons and builds them. Now your bunker has no space for Anti-Tank. It is also past the 3h mark from when the piece was dug so you can't delete and replace it. You also can't switch one garrison for another so you are now forced to spend an absolutely absurd amount of granades to blow up that entire piece with all connected bunkers and since blueprints work differently when you place them close to something that has been dug out you might have to delete several more pieces just to make everything fit back together. I almost forgot that since you're now throwing granades at your own bunker that counts as friendly fire you are more then likely going to get the 20min weapons ban over and over and over again for several hours trying to fix what took 1 "helpful" player ~75 secounds to build.

Lastly I want to quickly adress the "toxic community" and "Salty Vets" that keeps popping up in reviews. Salty Vets usually tolerate new players as long as they communicate, ask questions and actually want to learn how the game works. However if you choose to be a FoxKaren and think you can do/say whatever you want because you are a "paying costumer" the community have no qualms about putting you in a 1h time-out along with video and picture proof of whatever it is you did. As stated before, only devs can ban players and it is with video proof. If someone manage to get a perm-ban it might have been a problem with the man in the mirror and not the "toxic" community... But I am just a salty vet with +2k hours in the game so what do I know? ^^

For everyone that is not a FoxKaren welcome to Foxhole!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a good game plagued by absolutely disgusting community and several quite odd design decisions.

It is a game set in an interwar period in an alternate reality. As the technology progresses, so does the war: from trench warfare with bolt action rifles to artillery duels with tanks and automatic weapons. Foxhole has heavy focus on logistics: very few things manifest out of thin air; every gun, magazine, truck, tank and shell is manufactured by players and delivered to the front by players.

Secondary focus is on the teamwork. There isn't a single combat vehicle that can be operated solo; most require crew of two to four people. Same with logistics and construction: it can technically be done solo, but it's much better with a group. Organization is absolutely necessary to do anything beyond 'one guy with a rifle'.

It is a neat concept, similar to what Squad has but on a massive scale. However, there are ugly things under the hood.

Construction gameplay is divided in two parts: 'hold down LMB for hours to build it' and 'feed it supplies daily so it doesn't decay'. Both of these are just time sinks, exacerbated by the paramount importance of construction.

Logistic gameplay is similarly split into three parts: 'hold down LMB for hours to get resources', 'wait for timer on factory orders' and 'press W to deliver it to the front'. There's a bit more variety here than in construction, but in the end of the day you're either waiting on a timer or holding down one button.

Combat is actually really fun; infantry, artillery, naval, armored and guerrilla are all very well done, with asymmetric balance and some depth. You're not Rambo, you're regular guy with a regular gun: you can't mow down hordes of enemies for hours without reloading. Your tank isn't a magic metal box that repairs itself; it runs out of ammo, fuel, requires maintenance after several engagements, etc.

The worst part of the game is the community. While organization is absolutely necessary to run any kind of operation, the best way to enjoy the game is to not engage with community at all. Foxhole is filled with reddit troons, discord pedos and clan leaders on power trip that can and will mass report and tempban you for anything they don't like. If you're seriously considering getting into foxhole, enjoy it with a group of friends - you'll save a lot of brain cells by ignoring everyone in global chat and major discord servers.

Lastly, weird design decisions. 60 second spawn timers, timers to pull equipment, timers to build things, timers to rebuild things, timers on crafting, border bases, technology mechanics, quirks with shooting into trenches (or up and down in general), weather system - all of this left me wondering why is it done this way and not any other way.

The other example of weird design choices is weather system. Long-requested feature was implemented in the most unfun way possible.
When it's snowing, you freeze to death and vehicles freeze solid in 5-10 minutes outside. How to unfreeze a vehicle? Build a fire. Terrain not suitable for building? Sucks to be you, this is the only option.
When it's raining, vehicles are unaffected, but infantry rapidly accumulates mud to a ridiculous point: at maximum muddiness you cannot carry a rifle, a clip of ammo and a grenade without being overencumbered.
There are uniforms to help with cold and rain, but by large the only thing weather does is slow everything down.

All in all, Foxhole is a game with a neat concept, held back by bizarre dev ideas and irredeemably woke community that would benefit from arsenic injections.

Update for 1.0: devs tried to address logi grind, but somehow made it worse.
New Facilities seem fun and add some depth to logi gameplay, but in practice it's a case of building $1000 factory to make three $5 trinkets. This update is a massive failure; Siegecamp made one step forward for two steps back.

Update for 1.52: devs tried to address maintenance costs for facilities, but somehow made it worse(starting to see the pattern with the updates?). Now you're punished for someone digging out a massive bunker or building a giant facility 300m away from you. New maintenance system basically incentivizes griefing your neighbours. Beatings will continue until maintenance supply situation improves. On the upside, developers allowed securely storing combat vehicles now and added several other quality of life features. Normally you'd praise update for this, but in this case these features are so long overdue the only praise they deserve is "f*cking finally you slowpokes".\t\t\t\t\t
