\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game! Bought it at full $20 price several days ago, and it was completely worth it!

There are many different ways you can play in the game, but it boils down to two: fighting and logistics.

Fighting is obvious: you pick up equipment from your spawn (guns, grenades, medical stuff, etc.) or build a vehicle (Light tanks, half-tracks, artillery, etc.) and head to the combat to shoot the enemy.

However, doing the above is impossible without logistics. Litterally everything you use is the game is produced by players, (no passive supply!) so it is EXTREMELY important for many players to constantly supply the fighting forces with the immense amount of producible equipment, including guns, ammo, grenades, medical supplies, radios, gas masks, and lots more. (usually players who want to use a vehicle will produce it themselves)

Even though logistics is very important, many players find it boring and refuse to do it, sometimes leading to supply shortages of some equipment. Therefore, is you decide to play this game, try to allocate at least some time to logistics. If you find it boring, I've personally found that it's much more enjoyable while listening to music (more specifically Johnny Cash) or chatting in discord. Whatever you way is, try to do at least some logistics to help keep up the fight.

I would highly recommend this game, as long as you're ok with doing some logistics every now and then.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHello players of Foxhole,
I'm afraid I'll be leaving a negative review on this game for the reasons I'll be elaborating below, as a player who had 400 hours in it.
First of all, there is Friendly Fire in this game, so you might be able to damage your allies either in purpose or by accident.
While driving, you are able to run down your allies, or you could hit your allies by accident while opening fire on enemies. This is not exactly the point I have a complaint about.
There is a reporting system in the game and the reports/logs are not being checked/controlled by neither the game masters nor the developers. Your whole team can report you because of one accidental friendly fire tap, at minimum you won't be able to use guns for 20 minutes, if there are enough number of votes, this ban time could change between 1 to 3 days.
There are also trolls who abuse this system in purpose too.
When you go up to the admins/developers about this problem in forums, they either ignore you or reach an unfavorable conclusion.
All things considered in the end, the game becomes a toxic experience rather than being fun.
Other than that, it's actually a nice TPS game with a WW2 theme. But the bad stuff that I've mentioned above way outweights it's good sides, resulting in you uninstalling the game. I hope I was able to explain that well enough.
