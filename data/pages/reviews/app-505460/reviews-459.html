\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is probably best described as something like Planetside 2, but with the depth and mechanics of Eve Online, controlled as a single unit from Company of Heroes. It's very complex, but the community is great at teaching new players how to play.

It's pretty fascinating from all sides as everything in the game is made by players, transported by players, constructed by players, and controlled by players. So when you pick up a rifle, ammo, and a jeep to go fight the front lines, all of that had to be mined by players who built mining tools. They had to build refineries to get those resources, build a base to house that refinery, built trucks to supply those bases and transport those items to the front lines.

There's an insane amount of depth that allows basically every level of investment to find something of value.
You want a base builder game? Join the engineering teams
You want an action game? Join the front lines
You want Euro-Truck sim? Be a hero and transport resources with the supply dogs

There's a huge investment to this game which can definitely be a drawback. It's not exactly easy to learn and you basically need to find a group to teach you how to play. Luckily that wasn't too hard as the community is shockingly great at such a huge scale. Fortunately though, the investment required is also something you can somewhat chose as well.
