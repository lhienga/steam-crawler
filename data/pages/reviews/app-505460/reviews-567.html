\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI actually really like this game, I'm not one for the top down view and I would generally regard that part of the game as a negative but overall it's actually a lot of fun. Be prepared though if you go in solo, this is a game designed with cooperation in mind and between clans and just veteran players in general most of the high tier resources are almost impossible to come by unless you're willing to wait for them or are just lucky enough to happen upon them. I would also add another word of caution, if you want to enjoy some of the crafting aspects of the game I would avoid playing during the 5 pm EST and later window. This is when a lot of people are on and you'll find entire clans that clog up factories with 4-6 people making it impossible to craft anything unless you have an in with them.

Out side of these small negatives the game is overall pretty great, I've spent most of my time resupply FOB's and have had tons of fun working together with other small groups of people farming up materials, making bullets and then going to the frontlines and distributing and shooting the very bullets I just helped make. This is the only game I know of where you can get this kind of experience and it's well worth the 20 bucks.

Also screw shard 1, shard 2 is where it's at.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEngaging game with a plethora of "roles" to partake in whenever/however you like. Definitely rewards good teamwork but can still make solid individual contributions as well. The top-down only view takes some getting used to but is also an effective balance mechanic. Every group has its miscreants but the strong majority of the community is excellent (Warden and Colonial). People are typically very accepting of newbies and rarely unwilling to genuinely spend time and resources to help people get into the game.

General recommendations if you're getting into the game:
1 - certainly watch a couple YouTube videos to get a sense of the play but mostly just jump in and start running around. People will help you.
2 - unless you have friends already playing, don't sweat which side you choose (Warden or Colonial). Just know you can't change sides until the end of that war (can take 1-3 weeks irl)
3 - be willing to experiment. the game has a lot of facets to explore and "roles" to take on. When you get bored in an area/role, try another.. they're all interesting (to personal taste obviously)
4 - it is more enjoyable (obviously preferences apply) when playing in a group so check out the various regiments (aka clans). often a regiment will focus on an area of play but there are "generalist" regiments too. You can easily leave and rejoin so just explore and have fun.\t\t\t\t\t
