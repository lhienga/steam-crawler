\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have enjoyed some time playing Foxhole, however there do seem to be some glaring issues the devs need to focus on to help propagate a healthy life of the game. At less than 100 hours I'm probably not too qualified to go into specifics. However I think I can speak from an important viewpoint of a relatively new player who also is willing to look at the problem from both sides (player vs. devs).

Community communication and interaction is essential. Both players and devs need to be up front and honest, but also civil and kind. It's clear a lot of the community wants to like the unique game mechanics offered, so it's important to work WITH the devs instead of BLAME them. This is of course means the devs need to work with their community as well, providing many outlets for communication and acknowledging problems which have been vocalized.

I do have a personal complaint regarding the maintenance of buildings/fortifications. If you want players to be excited about playing with complicated bases and logistic systems that are both massive time + team sinks, there has to be a more reliable system to engage with. I love logistics in games, but I just can't get into it in Foxhole. When I think of Factorio, Satisfactory, Dyson Sphere Program, Mindustry or many others, those games have great positive feedback loops. I'm not asking for more complexity, but I would love to work toward a better balance of complexity versus practicality. As it is, hours and hours of work offers very little to feel great about, and it can be lost SO easily.\t\t\t\t\t
