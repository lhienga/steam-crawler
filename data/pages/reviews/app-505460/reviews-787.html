\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can say that this game, in it's current state in the December of 2019, is worth the purchase, atleast on its sale price of 11 dollars. This is an active war game, where just about everything requires player input. You want rifles? A player has to make them. Can't build rifles? you need materials. Don't have materials? A player has to deliver them. Don't have materials to deliver? They have to be excavated. Don't have good enough tech? Players have to research it. Not enough forward operating bases? Players need to build them. The whole war is completely player input, basically. So, I'd say this game has a very good concept, as for the execution, it's quite good actually.

For the gameplay, I'd say you have to have quite a specific taste. Logistics runs take a very long time to complete, not too long but still quite and there's alot of process going on inside it.
Maybe logi runs aren't your style, well there's the combat. I wouldn't overly praise this game in it's combat. It's not bad, but just not something you'd rush to play over another game if you were purely looking for that gameplay element. The aiming system is pretty wonky, and sometimes can ruin the game a little bit, and being a top-down view game it's pretty weird how your vision works. The communication and tactics is the real gameplay here, pretty much and that element is done very well. The community is actually pretty good and makes for quite an enjoyable experience despite what other reviews may say.

Overall, I'd say the community is what makes this game, if you have a bunch of good people you're going to have a good time. If you get a bunch of toxic people then it's just not going to be fun, but I assume that's how it is in most online multiplayer games.\t\t\t\t\t
