\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is 90% work, 10% play.
Most days both midweek and weekend you're sitting in a queue for 20-60+ mins to get into a frontline hex, even during night shift when the player count is super low there's still a queue.
Constant bugs: lately mass disconnects,
30 seconds-1 minute respawn timers if you're playing frontline infantry.

You build a base/facility, and are required to feed it Garrison Supplies, which is fine but the devs haven't changed the production/consumption math. They want to force large groups of players to have a shared burden, great theory, but explain why you're trying to force that upon players that do not find that fun, instead of making it easier for the players that choose to do it?

You play logi, you do everything that's not fun just to provide for your teammates and feel appreciated, you still have to sit in queue just to drop off your supplies and not fight, imagine producing equipment for 1-3 hours, taking a 15-25 min drive, refueling along the way, checking your radio to avoid ambushes, navigating the roadways, only to arrive at your destination and have to wait 10-80mins to enter the zone and drop off your truck load.

Alts/trolls/griefers: are everywhere and rarely punished, in game clans/vets have to pretty much police any poor behavior. The ingame reporting system's worst punishment is a 24-48 ban. And usually doesnt go into effect same day.

1600 hours in, a few years spent playing, amazing concept poor execution and poor management genuinely chase players away in droves. The concept and game play is great, it's fun enough for you to come back everytime there's something new or when you forget why you left. But it's not a game that respects your time, or your fun. The core devs are a bit weird to say the least, and just are not on the same page with the community or the market. Their business model still reflects the game as it was when they released into early access 5-6 years ago. The interest is there, the community is passionate, the game sells, gets picked up by content creators, and while it doesn't actually lack longevity; it punishes you if you and your friends do not make it a second job, which would be fine if it was a subscription based model. But for a game you pay for once, the amount of time you spend putting in work i.e. doing non fun things, like scrap mining, driving, swinging a hammer/shovel in the same location for over 60 seconds if you want to build something, the amount of time you spend having actual fun is minimal. Granted when you do have fun, it's amazing, nothing else really like it in other games.

In my opinion it's a very min/max game becuz of the time investment required to do anything, once you get a few hundred hours in and you realize how inefficient or wasteful doing the fun stuff is, kinda forces you down the PVE rabbit hole.
I could kill 300 players a day in one life, and have less impact than a guy logging on at 3am for 15 minutes and setting fire to a base that took 3 hours to build.

If you're thinking about buying, I wouldn't. You'll get your money's worth in terms of hours you put in, but you'll spend 20% of your time in a respawn screen, 20% pulling your gear, dying looting in combat, 15% of your time getting yelled at for not knowing wth is going on. And truthfully you log in again after that, either all the map gains you made are either gone or you're fighting in the exact same place 48 hours later.

Minor gripe, I've been around for 50+ wars, even winning a war doesn't feel as rewarding as it should to be honest, there's no character progression tied to it, no perks, or history log to say you participated in these battles. No medals or commendations, no leaderboard.

Most fun you'll have is after your first few days where you kinda figure things out, but its all downhill from there after you learn how to do everthing else in game. All of it is actually fun, but is it enough fun to justify farming? Probably not.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA truly unique game that is a real treat for anyone interested in WWI or WWII history, team-based games, or factory management. The game has vast, beautiful maps with large scale player-run operations. The game is what you make of it- you can make stuff in a factory, deliver stuff to the frontlines in ships, trucks, and trains, or spend all day simply banging away at the enemy with a rifle. There are tanks, naval landings, a well developed artillery system, massive player-made bases, and the option to play either as a solo (like me) or join a clan to team up for larger operations.

Two criticisms most common in negative reviews are 1) the player base is toxic, and 2) the best stuff isn't available for solo players. There are some toxic people out there (as in any online game) but in my experience with the "Warden" faction by far most folks prefer to help new players than to harass them. If you ask for help and say "I want to learn how to do X"- most of the time someone will volunteer to show you how it works (I do!) People will also trust you more after your first few hours in the game, once they see you aren't a fake account for the other side (an "alt"). As for the "best stuff" being limited to clans- it's true that some of the fancy stuff like trains and supertanks are too big an undertaking for an individual, but you can still drive a regular tank or take part in almost anything else as a solo. And many clans are casual "play as much as you want" operations.

You can play without a microphone, but for best experience having audio is strongly recommended (especially for combat, less so for logistics players). I'll also note that this game requires a time investment for best results- infantry can shoot each other for 10 minutes at a time, but tank operations usually require 1-2 hours to pick up a tank, drive to the front, fight, and return to base.

So I hope to see you in the game- and don't hesitate to say hi if you do!\t\t\t\t\t
