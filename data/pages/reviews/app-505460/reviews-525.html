\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game when it was very early access and only recently purchased it. I actually had a complete blast - but the learning curve was big and there wasn't much in the way at the time for teaching you the game.

Fast forward a couple years later and I finally bought the game on sale for a small 15 bucks after tax. That said for the enjoyment I've gotten out of it, knowing what I know now about the game, it would have well been worth a 60 dollar investment.

This game offers something unique that no other 'war game' offers. A campaign map completely populated in player characters - where every bullet, every weapon, and nearly every defense is created by players. The tactical possibilities are endless and while I've played totally solo so far - its easy to get on the front lines and aid, and squad up with randos for the night - no need for a clan - I can say that knowing the full extend of the tactical possibilities makes me oh-so-badly want to create a regiment for this game and dedicate my time to it as my 'everyday game'.

Whether you're crafting supplies on the backline, stockpiling weapons, running logistics, firing shots on the front lines, or running operations behind enemy lines --- everything you do counts. Every body, every weapon, every resource gathered, counts towards the overall war effort. I've never played a game, that even as a small insignificant number in a grander scheme of things, has made me feel like what I do has so much value. Whether its holding a certain bridge at all costs, making sure my guys on the front lines has resources they need, or building and strategizing defenses to prepare your guys for a defense on a large enemy push. This game makes me feel like what I do counts - and the best part is, that at 11 hours in, it didn't take long for me to feel that way.

And I feel like I've only begun to scratch the surface of what this game has to offer - a loyal dedicated and helpful community - and a sense of accomplishment and meaning to my role as a soldier in a large scale war.

This game has been fantastic as a solo player - but by god, I can't wait to see what it will become as I gather regulars to play with on a daily basis, to put our minds to common strategies and purpose.

I've never been so immersed into a game like this before - I didn't even think I'd like this game. But here I am... I am here. And I don't think I can leave now!\t\t\t\t\t
