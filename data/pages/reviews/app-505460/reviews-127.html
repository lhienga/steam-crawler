\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI start off by saying this game is a great concept with okay game play but the clan community ruins it for me.

The game play is okay, it's fun but gets kind of stale after a while doing the same thing. My largest issue isn’t really so much the game play but it's with the Clan communities. There are a lot of reviews that mentioned this already. It's been a while since I have played the game but I doubt anything changed since I have last played it. I participated in the War #100 on the Colonial side and ran logistics for a few days to learn the ropes of the game with another friend. We ran basic supplies and fuel to the front lines and it was fun but after a while I wanted to explore the infantry side to the game. I wanted to try to sneak into enemy lines and see what I could do. I was walking through a clan made compound when I came across this person who immediately became hostile towards me even though we were both Colonials. I explained to her that I was just passing through but I found the encounter pretty odd. I looked into the area further and found out the clan she belonged to was hoarding vehicles and supplies and several signs were posted that said something in the lines of the lines of “This belongs to so and so clan do not take”. Ironically a while later the Wardens made a push into the hex and destroyed the compound and the surrounding area. Those vehicles could have been used but instead this clan wanted to hold onto stuff that was not being crewed. It almost feels this game has a sort of Clan Oligarchy, and the common player just gets the crumbs.\t\t\t\t\t

Everything from the fortifications at the front to the weapons we use are player made and player delivered. Some people would say that this is too grindy and to be fair, there is a lot of grinding if you want to make high end stuff like battle tanks. However, almost everything can be made very quickly and without much grinding. If you work with other players, then almost nothing takes much time. And if you don't want to do logistics, there's nothing stopping you from just going to the front and involving yourself as a member of a large operation.
I recommend using proximity chat as it eases the flow of everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredibly Unique game with a mostly friendly player base that loves to complain. The best way to play this game is with a group, you will find hundreds of them on the official Foxhole discord, but I have found that the best way to find people to play with is to head to a front line and find a group you like there. Foxhole has the same issues any online game has with toxicity, horrible people, and people who need to touch grass, but If you can look past that, it's really an excellent title. I played around 700 hours in 5 months while in between jobs and I don't regret the time spent, but I would recommend making sure to take a break from the game time to time to avoid burnout. Don't feel like you have to play all the time everyday and certainly do not build any structure that requires maintenance by yourself. I have spent a significant amount of time on both of the teams in game, the wardens and the colonials. Whatever you hear online about the two, just know, they really aren't that different, so just choose whichever side has aesthetics that appeal to you more.

TLDR: Overall I would recommend this game to anyone looking for a unique experience, but I would also recommend waiting till a sale as this game really does deserve at least 20 hours of playtime before you decide if you like it or not.\t\t\t\t\t
