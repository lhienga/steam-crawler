\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short:
A good premise, mixing Factorio-like supply chains with a top-down shooter. Ammo and equipment is made from scratch and shipped to players who use it on the front line. Choose whether to build or fight. Unfortunately marred by a toxic player base and poor combat mechanics.

The good:
- The supply chains are fun and feel meaningful.
- There is great depth to the equipment and buildings.
- The world feels vibrant and alive.

The bad:
- The player base is toxic (and I say this as a regular Dota 2 player). Expect to be killed, obstructed and cussed out by your own team.
- The combat part is poorly done. You will spend a long time doing nothing, and whatever you do is likely to have 0 impact.
- A lot of veteran players "game" the combat mechanics, e.g. running in circles making them impossible to hit, before knifing your whole squad one by one. It feels like cheating and is frustrating to deal with again and again.
- Whether your team is losing or winning is out of your hands, and playing on the losing side just feels meaningless. You can not switch teams or restart in any way, so you just keep on losing for weeks.
- There is no player progression, adding to the general feeling of meaninglessness.

In short, this game will frustrate you. The Factorio approach works for the supply chain, not so much for the combat. A Satisfactory-type FPS would proabably have worked better as a base.\t\t\t\t\t
