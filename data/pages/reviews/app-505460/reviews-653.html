\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine something like Company of Heroes, but where each unit is player controlled, or sometimes controlled by multiple players. On a massive persistent map with wars that can wage for weeks.
I was wary of the reviews mentioning team-killing and toxicity, but in my experience so far the amount of friendly, funny and decent people you'll meet utterly dwarfs any toxic players to the point it's a non-issue. Just figure out how you can be helpful and you'll fit right in, whether you want to join a big regular group of players, or just want to drop in solo and help out a random group you come across, which is how I'm having the most fun at the moment.

This game is an amazing experiment, and it's mind-blowing to see how what you get to enjoy at the front-line is built on the hard work put in by people who play logistics and engineering, which make up the backbone of the effort. And yet, even a single player in the right place at the right time can turn the tides and be extremely disruptive to the enemy.

And my favourite thing about all of this is the sense of teamwork the game creates. I feel I've contributed well to a few skirmishes, defences and pushes I've been involved in, either in combat or as a medic. Or even by just being economical with what I take out of the bunker with me. But after all of this, there's nothing personal to show for it. No kill feed. No scoreboard. Everything is focused on the war effort. You can even be firing a mortar sighted in by a helpful spotter and not know directly how many people you're hitting, if any. There's no tracked kill/death ratio (and never will be). Everything is for your faction, and it's entirely about the outcome, and not a statistic or a number.

For anyone interested in trying it that is worried about any toxicity or team-killing; spend a few minutes reading the guides or watching some tutorials on YouTube. If you know how the game works you're more likely to work with your team rather than against it while you're finding your feet.
As for the team-killing, don't pay too much attention to the chat feed (which shows team-kills), as it's common and expected to punch a disconnected player to death so the things they were carrying (and logistics have put effort into getting to the front-line) aren't lost forever. That still shows up as a team-kill, even though it's literally helping the team and the right thing to do.

I've never played anything quite like this. It's crazy to think that every firearm and every magazine I load into it has been manufactured by someone, perhaps from resources someone else harvested, and then likely delivered to the front-line by yet someone else, for however long it lasts until someone gets taken out. A crazy experiment that seems to actually work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't have a single problem with this game, every single thing about it is fun. If you're a fan of playing RTS games, but you also like controlling an individual unit, you're going to love this game. If you like large scale strategy games, you're going to love this game. I can't begin to explain how indepth and complicated this game is, while at the same time being very simple once you figure it out.

The ONE drawback with the game is that it is SO indepth and complicated that it can be incredibly daunting. Foxhole doesn't have a tutorial system implemented, so for now the best way to learn will be Youtube videos and asking other people in game for help.

MAKE SURE YOU USE the in game Game Voice chat! It makes a HUGE difference! It's proximity based, so you'll only hear people who are on your screen or just off of it.

Once you've learned the ropes, and have decided to spawn into a zone, IMMEDIATELY let people know you're new and looking for something to do via the voice chat function. You will have a far more interesting gameplay experience if you start communicating with people the very first second you start playing the game. If you can't use voice chat, make sure you're speaking in Local (All) chat and tell people you're new and want to know what to do. If you do this, you can't go wrong.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGeneral Design:
Rarely have I have been in a match with so many people and yet felt so alone.

Foxhole fell for very basic trap of trying to give feeling or large scale by cramping alot of people in a small area rather than giving them tools to communicate and work together. In game systems to facilitate team work are small to none existent.
If you want to play with the group games does not offer anything more than bare bone squads which is very basic squad system, sidebar will be fillied with dozens of 1 or 2 man squads with rarely any following a particular objective or a goal. Leaving the game being at most a single player game with rare moments of player interaction.
While one might argue that if you wanted to play with a team you can join a clan but that does not justify short coming of in game system , One can join clan in any team game but Post Scriptum, Squad, Arma, CS all give more than enough ways for players to work together INGAME not needing to rely on 3rd party softwares and etc.

Gun Play:

Game uses same camera angle like Mobas like Lol and Dota,however this camera presents many issues for an FPS game something which is not an issue on those games, this being elevation while Lol has no Elevation and Dota having very distinct and predictable areas where elevation chances, in Foxhole not only elevation is more important its also much harder to realize as game does very little to help you understand it , best you can do is aim a weapon which lets you see on what area you have line of sight on, but this often does not equal where you can get shot from. This results in very annoying and irritating fiirefights with player dieing from angles they thought they were covered from.

Additionally since aiming is easy and developers wish this game to be semi realistic with its damage models, meaning that it wont take more than 2-4 shots to kill you, game had to have alot of RNG regarding weapon handling, from how accuracy, stability, cover work is random. Often times you will see guy infront of you maybe at point black but you wont hit him cause RNG was not in favor other times you might, due to decides what elevation solider aims for , you might just be firing over the opponents head because hey are running down an incline so aiming behind them makes shots go alot higher than you want them to.

Construction and Map Design:
Since player generated content has to also include alot of structures although this mainly means fortifications , spawn points and repairing predetermined ones. While initially it sounds good to give players proper area to build structures at alot of a map is a flat cover less terrain resulting in most battles devolving into trench warfare as attacking as infantry is little to impossible vs dug in enemy while attackers are stuck in open field with nothing to get behind. So it is not unsuprising that battles often times are stuck at exactly the same spots. Naturally one would say that use of tanks and armored vehicles should be used to beat defenders however as said due to disconnect between majority of players armored vehicles are rarely used in combined fashion rather being a toy which select few drive until it is destroyed


Logi:
While i am no Euro truck simulator fan , I do enjoy round of Logi in squad or post scriptum in Foxhole is utterly boring experience. Only real risk to Logistics are partisan fighters who camp back lines roads, however the game give players ability to instantly ping exact locations where partisans are active so even competent team of partisans will kill few vehicles before being hunted down or Logi drivers just use any other road.

Overall:
This game suffers with common issue of half assing everything instead of full assing one thing, its desire to be this huge all encompasing war game resulted in each individual aspect being unrefined and shallow.
Gun play is confusing, Logi is mundane and construction is very repetetive\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing foxhole for about two weeks on and off, and I can recommend the game. However before you buy it, be aware of the following things that might put you off:

1) Your stuff: There is almost no way to easily and reliably keep your stuff reserved for yourself (apart from raw materials), especially when you are on your own. Whenever you start playing again, you might find out, that your vehicle or supplies have been taken by somebody else. This is supposed to be a highly cooperative game and it tries to prevent hoarding. As a general rule do not try to hoard or reserve stuff. use it when you have it or expect it to be gone.

2) Your role: There are several ways to impact the war and you might find out that your initial role won't be as much fun as something you stumble upon. Don't be frustrated if you are disappointed when things don't work as you imagined. It will take some time until you have seen several aspects of the game and know which role is fun in which stage of the war or situatio.n Also you are but one cog in the wheel. You can really impact a single situation by playing intelligently, but that's about it. Even though you might give your best, your team might lose just as well.

3) Learning: Although this is not a particularly difficult game to learn, you do make frustrating mistakes. Whenever you try to do something you do not know how it works ask players in game or read the wiki, before you go ahead just to find out that things don't work as you thought they would.

4) Time: A war lasts a couple of days or weeks. You won't be able to be there all the time and while you are in real life you will start to wonder how things are going at the front. You will feel bad when logging off while things are in a tight spot. If you feel the game is too time consuming that's because you joined a game that is running 24/7. If you feel you need to take a break leave out on the next war. I already notice how this game affects my real life, so watch out for escessive gaming.

5) Other players: Most players are really nice and help you and you can have lots of fun with them. But there are also new players who do not yet know how things work and will for instance take stuff from places stuff should not by taken (by convention not by rule). Also it is said that there are cheaters and players who do not cooperate. Also there are many player who might not speak your language well. As this is an English comment I will specifically name the French players - who are just as cool as the rest of them - but some do not speak English very well. Anyway learn some French. It's okay to know some French.

6) Winning and Losing: Some players really want to win and they are very well organized. However there are also players who are not well organized, and you will lose simply because of that fact. You won't be able to organize those people and you WILL lose. Do not be frustrated about the lack of organization. Do your part and find to find a niche to have fun that does not require winning. Some of my best moments have been while losing. Joining the winning or losing side is a matter of gameplay choice. Joining the losing side is great fun if you enjoy defending or desperate measures.

If you are having doubts about the game, it probably isn't something for you. When I first saw the game, I immediatly saw why I wanted to like it but I was also instantly afraid of many of the negative aspects I mentioned.

BUT: I wanted to like the game and decided to put up with the more frustrating moments and it has paid off very well for me.\t\t\t\t\t
