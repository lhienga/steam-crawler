\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is that one big-scale warfare game that you tought didn exist,but it does,and it is amazing.
You join a faction at the start of a war(wars end apparently,but it can take weeks,or even months)
You can be many roles that help in the war in many different ways:
-Frontline combatant:You can be a normal soldier,a storm-trooper,a sniper...depends on your plan and loadout.
-Engineer/sapper:You can take materials and build structures or take high explosives and blow them up.
-Guard(Manning a defensive structure)
-Medic
-Vehicle crew
-Logistics: this dudes make long trips in trucks to make resources(guns,ammo,materials,etc) and transport them to the front lines.
-Partisans:This dudes band together and venture behind enemy lines to disrupt their supply lines(destroying supply trucks,blowing up bridges,that sort of thing..)
Those are the "roles" that i have seen so far,there is also people that look for resources to stockpile them in certain buildings or use them to research tech or upgrade buildings,not sure how to call them, maybe that counts as part of logistics.
There is also levels and ranks,you gain levels by playing and you earn rank points by recieving commends from other players,you can get a lot of those when you play logistics since the game will tell them what you just delivered to their nearby base and ask if they want to commend you.This is not the only way of getting commends of course but is one of the most effective ones.
Here is a story of one of my first times playing(if not the first,i dont remember if it was the first):
-So i was there just not knowing what to do and some dudes with french(?) accents arrive in an APC thing.
-They ask if i want to go behind enemy lines to cause some damage while they are expecting trouble from the other side.
-I go,they commend me so i go from nothing to Pvt.
-We cross a lake in the APC
-Thing go relatively well ,they were not expecting that it seems.
-we try to go back by the bridge,they corner us.
-Apc falls from a cliff to the river,everyone dies,except me,the driver and the gunner.
-Next day,i see a meme in the official subreddit about how the Colonials(our faction) were bullying the Wardens(the other faction) with our APC incursions.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, the game is a great, unique experience. It strikes a harmonious chord between overly realistic simulation and action-oriented combat. There is really something for everybody in Foxhole, as everything that is used to fight has to be made by the players, and the roles you take are really up to your imagination.

There are flaws with the game, but the developers do spend a lot of time seeking resolutions to these problems. However, there are two issues which just seem to be amounting and would have to take some drastic changes in course to resolve.

The first is the widening seesaw of balance the game sees as larger, more exciting content gets added to the game. This results in victory streaks that last up to a half of a year (yes you heard me right). One of the largest regiments even had to swap sides to throw it off with intentions to prevent nerfs. While victory streaks have existed in Foxhole for a long time, the duration of the wars has only increased consistently as the map expands with newer content.

The second is the direction that content is heading; the recent major Naval Warfare update mainly featured large ships that only the largest of regiments could make and field, leaving a very disappointing update for the vast bulk of the player base. Going from just gunboats for years to reworked gunboats alongside destroyers, submarines, and battleships is sort of like if only tankettes/scout tanks existed for years in the game and then suddenly battletanks and supertanks were added, without ever seeing any medium or heavy tanks added, except to an even greater scale. I find this to be a major misstep in the development process and think it has a lot more to do with marketing than anything else, as less than two months in the average player count is already down to a third of what it was earlier when the major update hit, showing that retention is lacking because the content is just not for the players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally, I love this game. I don't have much experience writing reviews, but I will try to put my feelings and opinions on this game as best I can in the following few sentences. First of all, the sense of immersion I got when playing this game. This felt like fighting in an actual war. The need for players to set aside their crave for the action that is normally so common in a war game, and just build defenses, repair existing ones, or manage logistics to keep the frontline fighting really immerses you in the feel of an actual war.

Speaking of action, it is intense and exciting. Whether short or long, clashes between each side are always important, as players die, respawn, and rush back to keep fighting. Unlike other games, losing these fights means a lot. Letting an enemy push through and destroy a base where players are respawning can have serious consequences, sometimes as much as losing part or the entirety of the region.

Teamwork is an extremely valued and important part of the game. Everything from basic defenses to entire buildings is made easier with a group. Vehicles sometimes require two people to operate to their full potential. For example, Jeeps with HMG's need both a gunner and a driver, and a person riding shotgun is always good.

As for roles, Foxhole doesn't do "classes" instead, there are roles. You can be whatever you want, as long as your local base has the supplies to accommodate you. You can look into the base stockpile, where every item has been made and transported by players, and pick and choose what you want to bring to the frontline. You spawn in with a hammer, a pistol, and 2 pistol clips. From there, you can choose whatever you want (but don't take too much) and go off to do your job, whether that be healing players, fighting off the enemy, bombarding the enemy with howitzers and mortars, or driving logistics trucks. This allows for a flexibility that I love.

All in all, I love this game.


P.S. I will always love the feeling of placing down an anti tank turret, knowing that it may help fight off an enemy incursion later on, even when I am not online.\t\t\t\t\t
