\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is simply amazing.

I've been waiting a long time for a game like this. It manages to do just about everything right. The fights are exhilarating, the concept is great and the gameplay is everything you should want from a war game. Every battle feels organic since it's all player controlled from gathering resources to base building.

I saw some negative reviews about a toxic player base but I have yet to run into it after 40 hours played. I found the players to be extremely helpful, informative and a lot of fun to play with. Some of the veteran players can appear bossy but as a newer player you should drop your ego and listen. I suspect people complaining about that are just sensitive with being told what to do. It's a game that requires a ton of teamwork so you should get used to that or maybe this isn't the game for you.

If I had one complaint it would be about AI defenses. I understand why they did it but it needs a lot of tweaking. It makes sense for behind enemy lines but when you're on the front line in the middle of a battle you shouldn't have to worry about AI firing at you, too. I am still new so I might be confused about how this works but as of right now I think it's a problem with not only how the game can be played but the idea of Foxhole altogether as a player run game.

All in all- Foxhole is a really great, unique game that is a lot of fun to play.\t\t\t\t\t

I've been looking for this type of game for a very long time. Love it.

- If you liked the core concepts of Eve Online (player driven supply lines, player driven world, massive scale, persistent world), then you will be glad to know Foxhole is the exact same in spirit. I played Eve for 10 years and Foxhole is the most similar game I've found to Eve.

- If you're new, it's very easy to get lost in the open world. And it's very big -- like it will take you many hours to run from one side of the world to the other. Don't run around for half an hour trying to find the front. In the 'home region' (the area you first spawn in), just ask in the local chat if someone can show you how to get to the front. (Can press 'T' for local voice too.) It shouldn't take you more than 30 seconds to get to an active front line. If you get lost, just press escape and 'Return Home'

- Two factions (Colonials and Wardens) fight each other in a persistent war over various zones. Wars typically now last at least a month. There are millions of player casualties in these wars.

-In this game, you are NOT a hero. You are a normal soldier. You will die a lot -- to bullets, artillery, tanks, being run over, gas, and even freezing to death in the cold. This game DOES NOT have a KDA board and that's totally against the spirit of the game anyway. It's way more about strategy and objectives than kills.

- The Colonials and Wardens have different items. They have different weapons and different vehicles. In general, Colonial weaponry is about mass production, speed, and being aggressive. Warden weaponry is more quality over quantity, a bit more defensive-oriented, and hits very hard.

- Both the Colonials and Wardens have very active regiments (the game's term for clan/guild). Both factions have many [in]famous 'loyalist' regiments (loyalist = they ONLY play a certain faction). Some of the craziest fighting you'll see is when regiments go toe to toe. Without a doubt, regiments are the actual driving force in this game -- but even as a random person you can tag along and help (usually by being cannon fodder for regiment tanks!)

- Culturally speaking, Wardens are way more regiment-focused than Colonials. It's best to be part of a regiment if you want to play wardens or else you might not know as well what all is going on. Meanwhile, with Colonials, it's much more common (sometimes encouraged) to have random people tag along with big operations run by regiments.

- Be warned: once you join a team, you can't switch teams until the current war is over. That could be weeks.

- 99% of what players use is player made. The rifles, ammo, guns, gas masks, grenades, barbed wire, tanks, shells, artillery pieces, bunkers, jeeps, bandages -- literally everything is made by players. This means resources are scarce and many battles are simply decided by which position is supplied the best.

- 'Logistics' players create items their team needs and haul these items to the front line. They are the backbone to everything.

- 'Partisans' are players who go behind enemy lines and disrupt the enemy. They intercept enemy logistics trucks, steal enemy vehicles, etc.

- 'Builders' are players who build bases. These players build defenses and forward operating bases, like bunker complexes, that have defensive bunkers and intelligence gathering capabilities. Land can easily be taken when proper defenses aren't built. When you see wild swings in the war in terms of ground taken, it's usually because one team didn't build proper defenses and were easily overrun, or undersupplied.

- The community is not nearly as toxic as people say. On the contrary, it's one of the best communities I've seen because most people are longer-term oriented and that means older players which means more mature playerbase. Many players will go out of their way to help you and answer questions. (This is my experience on the Colonial side.)

Everything you do in Foxhole will contribute some way or another to your war effort.

Sieging towns, destroying enemy encampments, Intel gathering, trench warfare, artillery bombardments, you name it, they have it. Also, there is no "levels or ranks" that you have to pay or level up to unlock. Its very different in foxhole. Depending on how good your faction's logistic's personnel are, you will have what you need. This is very important as most of the time, the spawns are not too far away, but getting your gear back takes some time, plus walking back to the frontlines is a pain so medics are just there so you dont have to do that again. ALSO, GAS GRENADES ARE OP. We were being held up in a stalemate between two trenches, me and some other people manage to find a blind spot to flank around and all of us just start chucking gas grenades into the trenches and it was like disturbing a hornet's nest. A lot of the enemy started pouring out but not to repel us, but to get away from the poisonous gas. At the same time, an Armored MG car pulled up and managed to pick them off by dozens. it was amazing to witness and participate in. One last thing, I recommend joining a faction/clan early on so they can better help you understand the feel of the game and whatnot.
