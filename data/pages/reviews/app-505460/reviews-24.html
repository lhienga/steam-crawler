\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great if you plan to play along with friends or get along with randos.

Definitely not the type of game you will be playing solo in terms of getting your own gear, vehicle or base. This game will require you to use and share resources with the people in your team. Sure you can build your own base, vehicle and gear, but its going to require you quite a bit of time to do that since the game is meant to be multiplayer and not solo.

If you prefer to just grab a gun and go, then this game is still a good option as you can just grab an equipment someone else made on the frontlines bases and just go to town. I do have to say that if you want to focus more on driving the fun armored vehicles or artillery pieces, then you might need to ask around, find a abandoned one, steal from the enemy, or try build one yourself as these assets are valuable to the war and can easily change tides if played right.

Overall I say its a good game, with a decent community. Definitely try it out if you want to actually play with people (Not just see people). If you prefer the same type of game but for solo play, then you can take a look at Running with Rifles as that game has a pretty similar gameplay to this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole takes the concept of a massive multiplayer shooter/war game and fleshes out the full scale of war by adding in the logistics of actually supporting a war effort of its size. Raw materials are harvested to create manufactured goods which include the weapons you fight with, the vehicles you drive, the support buildings and infrastructure across the whole campaign map. This gives the game so many different play styles whether you're looking to jump straight into the front lines as a grunt, supporting soldiers on the ground in tanks and other armored vehicles or being one of the logistics men who put in the massive amount of work it takes to keep weaponry, vehicles and supplies going to the front lines. These are just some of the various aspects of gameplay that Foxhole has as I'm just barely scraping the surface. Foxhole has everything I want to see within the genre but I can't recommend that people should buy the game due to the community.

The in game experience is unfortunately failed by how the community chooses to act and play the game. While there are plenty of great clans and solo players that help make the experience positive, all you have to do when you launch in to your first war to see the problem is look at the global chat feed. It's nothing but and endless stream of toxic s**t about why certain people think teams are losing, alt accusations, players stealing resources etc. When you pick your side in the war (Wardens or Colonials) you will be locked into that faction for the remainder of the war after a period of time in game. It prevents players from simply abandoning ship mid war to "win" but also traps players on teams where certain clans will steal public resources and guard them for their own operations, certain players will simply just sabotage the team's operations because they want to troll or potentially are "alting" where they'll play as one faction to sabotage it to help friend's or clanmate's. While I really love what this game is on paper I'm harshly reminded what the actual experience is whenever I get the itch to reinstall and play. In the end Foxhole is a great game thats sabotaged by it's own players in the pursuit of victory in game.
