\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot of major warts but it's pretty unique and it's improving as development progresses.

Pros:
* the LARP-factor is really top notch. The aesthetics and strategic / combat mechanics will hook you if you're at all interested in WW2 stuff.
* lots of opportunities for organic teamwork-based strategy that richly rewards strong organizers and keen tactical thinkers.
* you can make hero plays from the backline - advancing and positioning a gun emplacement in just the right spot can be worth a hundred kills

Cons:
* Your allies are almost as much of a hindrance as the enemy. The game is heavily reliant on experienced players volunteering to herd noobs and the clinically stupid. Unfortunately, preventing noobs from making common mistakes is FREQUENTLY a more valuable use of your time than doing something good yourself.
* A number of mechanics, ESPECIALLY the tech tree, provide ample opportunity for misguided or malicious players to sabotage your team in ways that hurt ENORMOUSLY.
* Harping on the tech tree some more - it's necessary for volunteer players (called Quartermasters or QMs) to AFK in an idling truck in order to block griefers from stealing critical tech resources. It's an absolutely terrible system and players have been begging for a rework since day 1.
* The game allows you to manufacture ammunition for guns that your team does not have and will NEVER have. Noobs on your team will manufacture this ammunition - A LOT of it.
* Clan players constantly horde supplies and armored vehicles in attempts to take over tech advancement or assemble assault convoys that rarely, if ever, succeed.
* Logistics tasks are plagued with obnoxious and totally unnecessary delays and timeouts. A crucial skill for good Logi play is knowing how to minimize time spent waiting for Assembly steps. In extreme cases getting this wrong may leave you staring at a menu shift-clicking every ten seconds for OVER TEN MINUTES.
