\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked that there is no K/D ratio. You don't know how many kills you did or how many times you died unless you count manually. This forces players to play be more team oriented rather than showing off their individual skills.

Game has many issues but it is still possible to escape into it.

The main negative about this game is that many times you might find yourself in a situation when after playing for like 6 hours straight in an active combat, you will get a feeling of not achieving absolutely anything useful towards your faction eventual win.

For example, you might die to static AI defences or spammed tanks bunch of times, you might even kill a few dozen of enemy infantry in the trenches. But after 6 hours frontline won't move a bunch. Enemy Static defences are still there on the high-ground. Nothing changed. So you feel that you, yourself accomplished absolutely nothing.

You might think, OK, in this case I will go build a tank destroyer or some big mortar to counter enemies. But guess what? Even if spend tons of hours crafting this things, bring them to front and shoot a bunch of times, enemies will just instantly repair the damage, and you still accomplished nothing.

In a typical FPS match you at least have your K/D ratio.

So, if you are a solo player like me, you myself, you must be aware of this side of the game.

Edit: However, even as a solo player it is possible for you to create entire industrial complex Factorio-style and manufacture trains and heavy tanks for others to use. The manufacturing system is kind of non-trivial, so if it's your cup of tea, then go ahead, fellow solo wolfs!\t\t\t\t\t
