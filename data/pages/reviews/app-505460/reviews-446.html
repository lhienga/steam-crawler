The wardens are almost a professional army and the colonials are a zerg faction (due to streamers/youtubers gathering people and also noobs). The balance I feel is good. The problem is all to do with the community; they are extremely unhelpful, judgemental, and elitist. Many other reviews echo this sentiment and honestly I certainly felt it was unfair. I thought "oh well you had this bad experience and judged the entire game on it" but I am here to confirm what they say is absolutely true. Clans walk all over new players, new players in groups, even old players if they're not affiliated with another clan that can actually tell them to shove it.

As a new player with limited knowledge and learning, I wanted to do artillery barrages. To do this you need to have permission to use someone's artillery piece (good luck) or to just make it yourself. I thought to myself, yeah I want to be self sufficient, the rest of the team is working together so lets not be a hindrance on them I'll prepare EVERYTHING myself and gather everything to make what I need, myself. I did this over the course of about 5 or 6 hours, gathered materials which are already camped by clans so you need to be on the trigger and get driving quick, then I put in some production lines on the factory to make my arty shells, then I manufactured the actual cannon itself, made my own transport trucks so nobody had any right to take them from me. I made a little encampment to solidfy this, made a sign marking the territory "please do not take" and stockpiling stuff into the encampment. But, with this so-called encampment, it contains a stockpile which is publically available. I thought to myself, who would drive all the way over here at this intersection to take something from me? I don't have a lot anyways, especially compared to large clans pulling in TONS of materials and working the factories 24/7, so I placed completely unfounded trust into the general public. The materials I gathered were quickly stolen overnight, thankfully the log showed me who took them and I reported them to the developers for stealing my materials despite me taking all the steps I needed to, to what in my mind was reasonably sequestering myself in such a way that people would not think my things were available for their use. The clan who took my materials did not apologize, were not remorseful, and were not at all helpful and were incredibly rude, condescending, and were blaming me for being a victim to their thievery. "You should have put it in a private stockpile" | "You should have hidden it better" so on and so forth. Only one person actually wanted to help me and stood up to the leadership in my absence, with my friend vouching and fighting to get those materials back for me. After about an hour and a half this specific person went above and beyond to truly make things right and was a proper gentleman about everything, meanwhile the clan acted like actual children threatening me and my friend if we ever tried to retaliate or mess with them.

This person is the sole reason I still have the game installed. The only reason I didn't refund this game is because when I decided to refund it I was at 2.4 hours of game time so I couldn't; it was denied. I would refund this game in a heatbeat and get my 22 dollars back or whatever it was. The combat is janky, you cannot suppress enemies with machine guns, you cannot decide the vertical angle you aim at so you end up shooting the ground or above an enemy's head, meanwhile everyone else is extremely experienced, makes fun of you for asking how to do better, and just kills you easily with zero issue. One tap.

In summary, logistics is a rat race and you will be backstabbed, stolen from, belittled, and disrespected for trying. Frontline combat is atrocious and you are not allowed to do what you'd like to do in an effort to contribute.
