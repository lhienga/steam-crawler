\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat concept but has a major flaw. Their choice of game engine limits the number of players to 200 on a server (up from a default 100). Their map has several servers (regions). There are several sub-regions within each region so if you have more than 200 players on a single server they cannot fully play in every area within the region. So fighting two wars in the same region is a pain in the ass despite the map intending on having wars on multiple fronts within a region. To make matters worse is these areas need resupplied, which requires players to enter and exit the region, but with a limit of only 200 players (most of which are fighting) it makes it difficult to supply the war efforts. Basically this logistical heavy game, which requires multi-regional access is in need of major solutions. Until then this game is nothing more than a queue simulation trying to be a game. So if you enjoy waiting hours (yes sometimes an hour) to actual do something in the game besides pew pew then I'd wait to play. With this game being so close to gold release I would not hold my breath that they fix something that has been an issue for years.


Edit: After years of development the game suffers not from it's own design, though there are a few technical issues, such as above. More so it suffers because the core concept behind the game requires players who are like-minded. This is as close to a military sim game as they come, which requires everyone on a side to come together to win the objective. The problem is developers want to ensure that solo players can do anything but individuals doing whatever they want hinders the core concept behind the game. Name a real life military where individuals can do whatever they want. This isn't just limited to individuals, companies are also guilty of doing their own thing and not working together with the rest of their faction.

tldr: Cooperation in a game that requires all to work together is lacking but that's more on the player-base than the developers, but the game developers also do encourage that type of behavior by designing a game that ensure solo players can do everything in the game, resulting in weak defenses, weak logistics, and weak morale, leading to player retention issues.\t\t\t\t\t
