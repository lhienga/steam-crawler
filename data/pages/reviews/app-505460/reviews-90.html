While not perfect at times, the game manages to capture large-scale combined arms warfare in fantastic fashion. Now while you can't turn the tide of battle on your own, Foxhole still manages to make you feel like almost every person plays a small role in a battle's outcome, all while taking place in large-scale wars that can last several weeks in real-time. A unique game where you're able to play as a cog in a war machine.

I think this game is an amazing gem. It's a world of constant war where basically all of the in-game situations are for players to create.

In terms of gameplay and fun, it really depends on how you play.

Playing solo - is mediocre.

As mentioned earlier - you're just a cog, a game is balanced in a way that only by a collective effort of a lot of people major changes to the frontline are achievable. If you're looking for MVP SOLO 360 NONSCOPE CLUTCH - there are games that fulfill this need way better. The most fun solo experience is when you're able to communicate with other random players and team up to achieve something. Or just admire the beauty of horror that is happening around you - the situations this game generates are fantastic, you will constantly hear a soldier in a side tranch calling for a medic, an officer yelling at you to push a fortified MG encampment, constant artillery shells taking lives of your fellow soldiers - and much much more, you can find examples of such situations in a lot of different reviews here.

There is also a solo logi experience that basically comes down to driving your truck from salvage fields to a refinery to the factory to the frontline while listening to a podcast or watching a video on a second screen and trying to understand what kind of supplies people actually need at the moment based on map intel. It may be fun if you enjoy the feeling that you actually help your fellow men and the guns you brought may be used to eliminate enemy soldiers, or medical supplies may save many lives.

Small group - fun.

With a small group of 3-5, you are open to much more content such as armored warfare, artillery, infantry squad, sabotage group, gunboat crew, simple base building, and more, but not everything. The game really opens up a lot and you can start to feel your impact but it's still not much. You won't be able to comfortably sustain industrial facilities or large bases with such a small group unless you're investing way too much time in the game, and without those, you won't have easy access to high-end armored vehicles. It's not too limiting but you will feel it anyway once you stumble upon an enemy battle tank or a super tank.

Being a part of a regiment - fun?

This is a way a big part of long-lasting community plays. It's basically a big group of people that are well-organized, with usually their own industrial facilities, consisting of smaller groups that specialize in different types of activities (like armored warfare, artillery, and infantry) and they plan out big operations. They have the most impact on the frontline and wars in general. Being part of such communities comes with the burden of social responsibility, since operations are executed in specific time and you have to participate in those to be noticed. I'm not up to those kinds of responsibilities since I have at least some kind of social life, so there's not much I can tell about that experience.

Community

For some reason, a lot of people say that the community in Foxhole is toxic. I assume those people are new to the internet in general. Of course, there is some level of ♥♥♥♥-talk present, but from solo and small group experience, the community is one of the best in video games. Tons of cases where random people came up to help, explained some complicated game mechanics, risked their lives to save me and get to the medic. There was even a case where our group tank was stuck in trenches and another fellow tank took all of the enemy shells while a squad of infantry tried to dig us out of it, we made it out thanks to their help. ZERO, I would like to repeat ZERO cases of intentional griefing or ruining behavior spotted. It might be luck but considering more than 100h of gameplay it is safe to assume that those cases are really rare. Maybe people are talking about regiment communities, but as already mentioned I wasn't able to be part of those.

Overall - I recommend this game since it's a truly unique experience and devs deserve the support.\t\t\t\t\t
