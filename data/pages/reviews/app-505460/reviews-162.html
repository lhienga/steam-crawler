And yet, this game nearly kept me forever, because it is exciting being the only remaining medic in the midst of a push. Being the one that forces a breakthrough by will and revolver alone. Being the partisan squad running in a Silverhand, shouting 'Death before Dismount' as you duel one of the enemy tanks hunting for you, running out of fuel on the return trip and having to sneak back to your own lines to grab fuel... This game breeds stories like this, ask anyone with more than a few hours, and they will tell you 'About that time that I/We/They----"

All anyone wants is for this game to get better, for the developers to show that they also love their own game as much as we do. And if they don't, well, I wish them luck regaining any of the players they've abandoned when their next game enters Early Access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, the point of my original review still stands: The dev communication, while better, still struggles in ways from lack of a dedicated CM. Decisions seem impulsive and whimsical.

The game struggles with server issues due to population in any major update, bringing to question whether the devs can actually pay for the servers or deliver a game that is capable of living up to the Vision™ or the supposed max player count. When there is an issue, the servers frequently fail to enter their recovery mode correctly, leading to unfair outcomes. And then if the devs intervene, because they have no clear or stated policy, that likewise ends up looking unfair.

Notable balance issues can go unaddressed for long periods of time as the devs insist on keeping balance tied to milestones in a roadmap rather than using incremental balancing common to modern PvP games (keeping in mind the average war length these days is well over a month). This, in combination with the questionable communication, leads to periods in the game where one faction or the other perceives the game/devs as biased.

The lack of good CM also means that the community is still one of the most hyperpartisan battlegrounds you'll encounter outside of American Twitter.

Oh, and a good chunk of the game is still a Sisyphean chore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, let me say that I have never played a game like foxhole, I love the game and while I do not have that many hours in it I have sunk many more than my playtime into learning about the world and experience of foxhole and I managed to get a couple of my friends to play it and we have shared experiences going tanking and battling together.

However, I cannot recommend this game to people, even on sale, because as much as I want to do things as run an artillery operation for a few hours I cannot ignore the problems present within the game. For starters the devs do not communicate. At all. They might occasionally pop up when excited for a new update but outside of that they are silent and do not listen to the concerns of the community in the slightest, one example of this was the L.O.G.I strike where a large portion of the player base either stopped playing or played different aspects of the game to try and get the devs to introduce quality of life features for logistics players. First off what other game can have a portion of its player base strike in order to get the devs attention! but also the fact that they had to do this is astonishing and shows the devs lack of communication. Especially considering it took multiple wars for the devs to even acknowledge the strikes existence (not aware of the exact time frame but it was around a month from memory). The devs still to this day don't listen or acknowledge the community at all and it shows. There are other concerns from the community such as ALT accounts which can ruin the joy of the game and while I do agree that alt accounts are a big problem I haven't seen any and so am not the person to talk about it.
What I can talk about is their recent update which launched foxhole out of early access. Inferno introduced many exciting features, many of which were absurdly unbalanced but thats a different discussion, the one I want to have is that it basically stopped me and my small friend group from being able to play the game properly. With the recent update the game has introduced facilities and many features are now locked behind them, the problem is that facilities require a huge amount of effort to maintain and effectively locks me and my friends from a massive amount of content. During our last tanking run we noticed a captured mortar tank, My friends immediately went "that looks fun" and I began laying down what i would need to make one, I should also say that logistics is so tiresome that in these operations I will do all of the work of making everything and driving it to the front letting them log on just to experience the actual joy of using the tank or whatever, then I realised that this mortar tank which had been in the game for years was now locked behind facilities meaning unless I spent a good amount of time looking around for a public facility that would let me make this tank we couldn't do it. This is applied to many things including things such as rocket artillery and many variants of tanks such as the mortar tank that would have been fun to mess around with.

With recent events in the community and the pushing out of an artist who reminded me of the joy of playing foxhole and helped me get the motivation to organise our operations and just how split and scummy the out of game community can be I have finally decided to post this and stop playing until the devs finally fix their game. I have to say I don't think they will. If you made it to this point congratulations, just know that I will miss this game and the memories it has brought but I cannot bring myself to keep playing this game.
Nga Mihi\t\t\t\t\t
