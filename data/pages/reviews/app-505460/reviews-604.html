\r\n\t\t\t\t\twell worth the $20 bucks. this isn't an FPS war... it's like an isometric, diablo-esque WW2, mmo, except without all the tedious grindy mechanics of an MMO. You spawn into the war, and you figure out what role you want to play in a massive war across a huge map. The logistics macro-economics kinda reminds me of starcraft for some reason, you gotta get into a supply truck, find resource nodes, farm them and then bring them back to a refinery for processing into materials needed to make weapons. Someone's gotta do it, and honestly, it's kinda fun carrying a whole lot of valuable resources / weapons to the front line with the uncertainty that you're gonna get ambushed by enemy players along the way. The map scale is awesomely huge. It can take near 20 minutes to get from the back part of your controlled map to the front line - and you've only covered half of the war zone map. The community is incredibly helpful and friendly as well. You really get a sense that teamwork is important and that your role, whether it be handling logistics upgrades, farming for resources or fighting for base captures. I really recommend watching some youtube videos about the gameplay basics. There's a bit of a steep learning curve, but the complexity of the game is worth it. Simple on the surface but lots of mechanics and dynamics to learn.\t\t\t\t\t

the TLDR, its 100% worth it.

I was told to select the "training server" by the game since it was my first time playing, apparently its a server dedicated to helping new players learn the game.

I was lucky enough to find Player ran tutorials, Experienced players were literally rounding up players as they spawned in, putting them in a squad, and then step by step, showing them how to play the game.

They went over the important information first, (vehicles, guns, building, medical) and then told us we were ready to go to war, or we could stay and learn about the rest of the game. ( i opted for the latter)

After about 1 hour of training, i felt like i was ready for war.

I spawned in the middle hexagon, (salt something) and was instantly told by a player to get down, as soon as i crouched the player that told me to get down had his head shot off and all i had was a pistol.

Several gunshots later and i heard somebody announce it was all clear, i "gracefully" looted my fallen savior, and followed the emerging allied troops as we moved up the road to the north,

It was hell, they had MG nests, sandbags, trenches, tanks.
we had some grenades and rifles,
we began to edge up the line, placing our own trenches and defenses, and began to push them back towards something called "the pit" which they had made a fort out of at the top of the road.

There they started unloading on us with artillery and waves of enemy troops, but we held fast, our medics were going nuts running from one injured person to the next, random craters were formed by the dropping bombs,which was our only cover. and gunfire was everywhere, i got my first couple kills in this encounter, and looted a sub-machine gun from a fallen enemy, i had plenty of ammo that i looted off of him and saw that the enemy's right flank (their left) was completely open, i pointed this out to the guys in my crater, about 5 of us, and we made a mad dash for the flank.

we weren't spotted and were able to push all the way to the enemy's supply line, we noticed several supply trucks coming in, 3 of us split off and threw grenades onto the trucks, while me and (we will call him Jimmy) stayed and covered, as soon as the trucks blew up, the enemy was upon us like flies on ****.

the 3 that were exposed tried to use the downed supply trucks as cover but were quickly over-ran.
Jimmy started throwing grenades on their position, blowing up the first wave of enemy's but it was far from over, 3 tried to rush into our little crater and i shot them down with my sub-machine gun, but jimmy only had a rifle and wasn't able to keep up with the demand for fire, the next wave almost over-whelmed us as they started using cover and gained fire superiority, pinned down and unable to do much, jimmy and I began to attempt an escape, but i was shot and was too injured to go much further,
Jimmy didn't want to abandon me, and attempted to fend them off, but i convinced him to leave me behind.

I began to shoot and cover jimmy's escape, must have taken several of those B******s with me before they got me.

Jimmy survived and was able to regroup with the main line, our little distraction and Jimmy's survival caused the enemy to over-extend on that flank, letting us wipe them out and force the line back at almost a sprint.

this was an amazing play session, and i had so much fun. i cannot wait to play tomorrow.\t\t\t\t\t
