\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe design is replete with minor to moderate flaws arising from their assumption of a NEET consumer base.
Some of it is ameliorated by clan play but only if you're into that, with all its pros and cons. Not NEET? Not Clanning? This game is less likely to please you beyond the honeymoon, should it please you at all.

Game and metagame gatekeeping is a real problem. The most obvious example is with the tech tree and research process. You will likely never be meaningfully involved. You will also be *told* to use 3rd party apps for the *right* to participate in things like metagame voting processes (wherein random people will want your phone number to authenticate which faction you're 'actually' playing for; will tell you that your IRL opsec concerns are silly conspiracy theory nonsense; and that surveillance capitalism isn't real but is yet more silly conspiracy theory nonsense; and that you can trust Facebook and Discord etc etc etc).

I don't regret giving it a go. There was some top-tier emergent teamplay stuff on the front line that arose around me that took me right back to the tournament scenes of the 90s and 00s. It's was genuinely great. However, this is the dragon you'll chase like the one-in-ten games of LoL. Meanwhile you'll be queuing (maybe for a whole hour!) for the interesting combat on servers that are oversold for their capacity, or doing virtual Charlie-work that also requires you to queue for resources and to queue to enter the front lines, but also requires you to log in every 50hrs lest any private efforts will be lost to public stockpiles!
