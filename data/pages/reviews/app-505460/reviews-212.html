\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most compelling part of Foxhole for me is how immersive it is. I enjoy military history and this game feels authentic to me in many ways.

The game has plenty of action, such as storming machine nests with grenades, supporting a tank assault by targeting enemy anti-tank assets, or calling down an artillery barrage on stubborn enemy defenses or a full-scale enemy assault. But what really speaks to me about this game are the frequent moments of personal adversity and heroism.

•\tA friendly soldier is hit and goes down in the middle of a firefight. They are writhing on the ground, with little time left before they bleed out. Bullets are still flying everywhere, but a medic charges into the maelstrom to bring the wounded soldier out and patch them up. Sometimes the rescuer is successful. Sometimes they suffer the same fate as the one they sought to rescue. But it never stops being impressive.

•\tThe enemy is pushing hard, and the defenders need more firepower to hold the line. There’s an abandoned machine gun in no man’s land that would help. It was spotted earlier in a ruined foxhole littered with the castoff equipment of soldiers killed earlier in the fighting. This machine gun requires at least two to transport, though- one to shoulder the gun itself and another to carry the tripod. One soldier turns to another. “Want to help me go get that gun?” They’ve never met before, but each one knows this could make a difference, and that they couldn’t do it alone. Under cover of darkness, they move to recover the gun, both on the lookout at the edge of their vision for enemies skulking in night.

•\tThe front line needs equipment badly. Medicine, rifles, ammunition, grenades. The back-line logistic folks work feverishly to fill the orders coming in from the front. Some of the supplies are ready, but others are going to take a while longer. The supply truck driver wonders if they should leave now with what they can, or if the soldiers in the thick of it can hold on for just a little bit longer…

•\tThere is an assault underway, and it’s time to gear up. A soldier finds to their chagrin that, while there are dozens of rifles in the armory, due to a logistical oversight there isn’t a single round of rifle ammo to be had. The soldier grabs a rifle anyway and, readying their pistol, heads out, confident that there will be plenty of ammo to be found among the corpses of the fallen.

These situations that I’ve experienced in the game, and many others like them, scratch my itch to live variously as a soldier in wartime. The game is explicitly not a WWI or WWII game, but no other game in my opinion is a more believable simulation of fighting in one of those wars.

P.S. Teamwork is HUGE in this game. If you don’t have friends who want to play with you (I don’t), make some in game (I did). In my experience, it’s a very welcoming community. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
