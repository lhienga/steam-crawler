\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere do I begin with this fine specimen.

Easily able to see myself investing far more hours into this game, wish I'd played it a hell of a lot sooner. The players are operating at levels of simultaneously deranged retardation and autismo-grade intelligence I could only put on par with something like Space Station 13, it's truly something beautiful.

Of course the flow of such varying ability would not be present without the game itself. Simply put, it simulates the economy of war exceptionally well and shows just how imperative a constant stream of supplies funneling from the backline of a war front to those exact fronts really is. Just as important, disrupting your enemies supply lines can be a crucial element to succeeding in those fronts all the same and the hardware employed to rock/paper/scissors your enemy becomes that much more detrimental when trying to keep up with supply and demand as the battlefields evolve.

I bought this game about 8 months ago and what inspired me to actually play it was the complexity. My impressions at a glance were "Isometric WWI shooter" so try not to make the same mistake I did.

You wanna relax? Help out logistics by farming scrap, refining it, producing supplies at the back line, transporting what's needed across land or sea to seaports or warehouses in bulk in shipping containers to get them further up and then move them to your frontlines in haulers. If you prefer to fortify, grab some basic materials and start building because ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ things get even more complex there with a highly modular building system that provides ridiculous flexibility so you can get creative but if you're aiming for something large scale don't expect it to be done and dusted in seconds. Do your part, you'll be loved by all.

You wanna go big? Grab a weapon and get the ♥♥♥♥ out there buddy! Woah woah nah not from the stockpile, there's loads of corpses out there on the field! Walking out of the base with nothing but your fists? Now you're cooking with gas. Should be simple enough to understand but you're going to die, alot but so will everyone else. There is no scoreboard, no kill feed, just you and your weapon as another cog in the machine.

If you're going to be out in the field, you'll definitely want to know how you can help. Technically there is no class system in this but rather uniforms which optimize the equipment you can carry. An example would be the standard Soldier Uniform you spawn in allowing for "Light Kinetic Ammunition" to stack so your basic rifles and submachine guns that you'll find in abundance, it will also reduce the encumbrance of those same firearms, they're your bread and butter fighting outfit. Compare it to a Medic Uniform and you lose your ability to stack magazines and those standardized weapons encumber you more than they would in the Soldier Uniform. However! You now possess the means in which to stack bandages and blood plasma and first aid and trauma kits are reduced in their encumbrance instead which makes you far more efficient in patching up the wounded and reviving those that are down but not out for the count.

Needless to say, there's a lot to be done on the frontlines.

- Be a combat engineer and help build and maintain the trenches, rebuild shelled towns into fortified garrisons, possibly all while under a hail of gunfire :D

- Go be a grunt, manpower is in high demand, shout out to Colonials on Shard 2. Quit being ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ by teamstacking the winning side and join the underdogs. Without a challenge you've got no fight, you've got a steamroll.

- Help out the wounded as a medic, your boys will thank you for it and you'll be saving on shirts that would be spent on respawning them if they'd have bled out which in turn helps out logistics! Furthermore, go nab critically wounded if you can. They're essentially a resource in the form of a corpse than can be ferried back to field hospitals in order to turn them into more respawns, saving logistics further!

- Riddle the battlefield with lead by setting up in a foxhole as a light machine gunner and stop them from blitzkrieging your position.

- Smoke out that pesky yet comfy light machine gunner from their foxhole as a grenadier with gas grenades, hopefully he doesn't have any brains and isn't wearing a gas mask or better yet a gas mask with no filter. Grenades will break their lines if you're able to get close enough with fragmentation otherwise structures will crumble when you land high explosives nades onto them. Same as letting them get too close to your lines, feel free to rush a tank with anti-tank stickies if you want to do some real damage to enemy armor.

- Operate tanks in crews of up to 3 and break their lines with the best of both worlds, high protection and high firepower but don't overestimate your advantage, these things will deteriorate under sustained penetrating fire and when your tracks or engine go kaput then you're up ♥♥♥♥ creek without a paddle. Pull back every so often and get your combat engis to top you up and combine pushes with infantry to maximize your effectiveness. Beyond that you've not just got tanks but assault ships, armored cars, field guns, motorcycles, mechs (only on Halloween) artillery both mobile and stationary from mortars to 300mm BIG ♥♥♥♥ storm cannons and even a pushbike for the more eclectic sort!

If you're looking for a mix of the two, operate behind enemy lines as a partisan. Tear down enemy watch towers to limit their intel on you. Level bridges with satchel charges to deny a path. Block roads with AT mines or other vehicles then ambush logistic routes as they drive through then torch their shipment or even try to steal it and transport it back to your boys if you can. Scope out high priority targets for your artillery batteries to target and promptly shell. Be a real thorn in their ass by finding their spawn and running in with a bayonet and shanking every unsuspecting bastard in sight, you'll likely kill 5 or more before they even realize what the hell is going on and if you're feeling particularly spicy feel free to run in and beat the enemy to death with your bare hands...your BEAR hands, you legend!

If you wanted additional layers of complexity that may see battles in the same territories being fought completely differently beyond that of the state of the map and doctrines employed, you've got unknown variables in the way of time and weather. Wind will end up working with or against your artillery to shorten or lengthen your maximum range, the night will set in and reduce everyone's vision making flanking a highly fruitful endeavor under the concealment of darkness, storms will make trench warfare even more hellish with the buildup of mud on you, increasing your encumbrance the longer the war wages, snow storms will hinder your ranks by decreasing vision even during the day, making sniping impossible and constantly wear down those in static positions without any shelter, potentially killing them from the freezing temperatures. Build a campfire and cuddle your brothers, it's only gay when those baby eating Wardens do it c:

Play Foxhole on hard mode (Man Mode) and join Shard 2 Colonials today. Their morale is unbreakable, their recklessness is unrelenting and their autism highly functioning. Shameless recruitment I know but it's necessary in this case.

What's more to say? Take your pick. Foxhole emphasizes teamwork and you sure as ♥♥♥♥ will not succeed without it. Don't be a ♥♥♥♥ and play nice, talk to people, it's a very social game and ♥♥♥♥ will get done the more cohesion there is among your cohort. You see that truck full of suicidal bastards driving right through all that gunfire about to throw a warehouse worth of mammons on a single pillbox? That's teamwork.\t\t\t\t\t
