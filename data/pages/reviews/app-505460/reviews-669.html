\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\tDirect from Steam\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll begin this review by saying that this game has a huge potential. As a milsim fan, I think this is a great step towards a hopefully long way of war games basing their gameplay on more than the polar opposites of shooter and strategy, mixing and matching both to create a unique experience, depicting war as a true team effort, from grunt to general.

The initial idea is genius, want a tank ? Ok, do you need to unlock it with experience? Get lucky with available tanks for your team ? Do you get it by being on top in the leaderboard ? Do you pay for it with collected points ?
None of that. If you want a tank, mine ressources, refine them at a player built refinery, then construct it at a player built garage. But that's not all, you'll need fuel and ammo for it, you'll also need binoculars for the commander to spot targets and gas masks for the crew not to die in chemical attacks. All of those are player mined, refined then built. And that's still not all, you'll need trucks to carry out this logistics achievement, transporting ressources from facility to facility, those also need to be built and refueld. This tank, once finished, is going to be precious, you're going to need to use it smartly, to carry out frequent repairs, refuel and resupply, all of which are going to require player obtained ressources.

TL;DR : If you want a tank, it's going to need all the logistics a real tank would require.

This applies to every aspect of the game, wether you want to be a medic, an artillery crewman, a ship captain, a machinegunner, a sniper, a base builder, a logi runner or just your average grunt fighting in the trenches. There are dozens of roles to try out and the journey to prepare yourself for it is as much a part of the game as it is actually doing it on the front.

BUT

And there is a but, otherwise this review would be a sincere and heartfelt recommendation.

The economy of the game has been broken. You see, before recent updates, you were given a choice : run into battle with publicly available resources to aid in the war effort as your average run of the mill soldier, or grind some logi to get to a better role and unleash untold destruction upon the ennemy. It was fair, and available to everyone with a bit of time on their hands. As a result, battles were spectacular, tanks and armored cars roaming the battlefield amongs machinegun fire and artillery shells as masses of soldiers held the line.

But recent updates have made resources extremely scarce, mainly the high tier ones. Turning the game from an epic fantasy 1920's world war simulator to a grimy depressing grindfest where armored vehicles are available only to large organised clans where members slave away around the clock with logi, only to stash the fruit of their work away from the public ; and the teams resort to cheap zerg rush tactics to conquer zones.

This choice is very much unexplainable as this game has no dlc, no premium content, no grindable unlocks which would justify this kind of change. (And frankly, I hope it never does) If you pay 20bucks upfront for the game, then why shouldn't you be allowed to enjoy all of it ? Perhaps it was made to increase realism and the rarity of powerful weapons ? In which case, all it did was remove the accessibility and the fairness of gameplay options. Was it to stop clans from stashing massive amounts of resources ? In this case, why would a team based game punish the effort of players getting together to get their team to victory ?

Whatever the reason, it was a bad call. But all hope is not lost, the game is early access after all. If the devs ever revert this change, I'll be more than happy to edit my review, recommend the game and remember it as a milsim masterpiece. Until then, I would avoid it, expecially if you don't like grinding.\t\t\t\t\t
