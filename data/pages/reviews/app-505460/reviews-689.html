\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStrengths:

-Variety: There is a lot of stuff you can do in Foxhole, the odds are high that you will find something that suits you. A few things of what you could spend your time with are, crewing a tank, sneak behind enemy lines and steal tanks or other valuable stuff, be medic, build your own bunker base, do logi(kind of eurotruck sim), be anti tank infantry, and more...

-Community: This game has for sure a helpful Community, which is needed since the game itself doesn't hold your hand. You should not be shy to ask in worldchat if you need help with something. Sometimes, especially during steam sales, players will offer "training courses" for newbies. I recommend to take part on one if you get the opportunity. The only downside i see in our community is that you might get team killed if you try to build trenches, so just dont build them and you will be fine :D

-Content&Updates: The game already has a lot of content and more will get added. Devs do a dev-stream every 2 weeks or so and reveal new vehicles, weapons, features, QoL-improvements and so on.

Weaknesses:

-Newbie-unfriendly: As i said above, this game doesn't hold your hand. There are tutorials in the region you spawn in when starting the game, but there is SO much more to learn. Most important is that you find out how to actually join the war and get out of the starting region. You can join the war at the icon with crossed swords on the map, once you deployed at the frontline you grab a weapon from the base and there you go.

-Bugs: Foxhole has a decent amount of bugs, most of them are ignorable, but some are very annoying..."Spawn Point not available"


Overall a unique game you can spend a lot of time with, as casual or as no-lifer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t~ DIFFICULTY ~
🔲 My 90 year old grandma could play it
🔲 Easy
🔲 Normal
☑️ Hard
🔲 Dark Souls

Foxhole is a very confusing game, for new players at least. The controls are strange and need time getting used to, the tutorial is a joke and barely teaches you, and the top-down yet 3D view is not very ideal.
There are plenty of kind players that are willing to help you out, which is great, but be prepared to put in a good number of hours just learning how to play the game. And, if you've finally learnt and decided that the game isn't for you and requested a refund, your request will be rejected because you've put in over two hours.

If the devs had made a third person view with an actual tutorial, then the game would be much better.

~ GRAPHICS ~

🔲 MS Paint
🔲 Bad
🔲 Meh
☑️ Good
🔲 Beautiful
🔲 Masterpiece

Graphics are quite good. It's this cartoony yet realistic style. The map is unique and very detailed.

🔲 Ear-rape

🔲 OK.
🔲 Good
🔲 Beautiful

I remember hearing only one piece of music, and that's the menu theme, which isn't even that good. To be fair, though, music doesn't fit this type of game. The SFX is decent.

~ STORY ~

🔲 Terrible story.
🔲 OK story.
🔲 Good story.
🔲 Epic story

Foxhole doesn't have a story. You just pick a faction, enter the map, and fight. There is some lore for those who want it.

🔲 Underpriced

🔲Could be cheaper
🔲 Overpriced

Foxhole's price is OK for the amount of content in it.

🔲 High end
🔲 NASA computer
You don't need a gaming PC to play this. Just your normal office PC with an OK graphics card.

~ LENGTH ~

🔲 Average (15-50 hours)
🔲 Long (50-90 hours)
🔲 Extremely long (90-110 hours)
☑️ No ending

Foxhole has no end. Ingame wars can last for days, weeks, months even.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a lot of RTS's and FPS's and I can honestly say there is nothing quite like this game out there. It feels like a community driven strategy game where each player can help their team in a variety of different ways in order to gain a tactical or strategic advantage. Some people complain about the logistics aspects of the game, but honestly I think it adds a level of immersion that many other games lack. Need to knock out some enemy bunkers but don't have a lot of grenades? Well then your job just got a lot harder. Running low on ammo or respawn tickets? Well then you better make sure to loot bodies and carry the wounded back to a field hospital. I'll admit the movement and combat feels kinda clunky and takes some getting used to but once you figure it out, it is a surprisingly fun shooter. When you're crouched in a trench with LMG rounds whizzing passed your head just praying that one artillery shell doesn't find the bullseye, it feels pretty terrifying. This game has a surprising amount of depth and I think there is something in it for just about everyone to enjoy.

Side Note: Spent 3 days in game fighting over a river crossing where both sides pushed back and forth across the bridge. Each time the Wardens advanced they would be mowed down by machine gun fire from our trenches, each time we advanced we would get smothered by gas grenades on the bridge. Artillery from both teams was so danger close you couldn't tell who was hitting what. I built a sign on the road that read "Crossing of the River Styx"

I still don't think we have taken the bridge. 10/10 would get lit up like a Christmas Tree again.\t\t\t\t\t
