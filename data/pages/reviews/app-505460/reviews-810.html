\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you purchase this game, there are two possibilities.

One, it will simply not click with you at all. If it does not, it will be entirely, thoroughly unenjoyable, though it may take a few hours just get to a combat zone to realize that you do not enjoy it. For instance, if you want to play solo, you will most likely find it very hard to enjoy foxhole; people who tell you "oh it is great!" are often the very first people who will lecture you if you complain about how extremely difficult it is to do things alone: "Imagine trying to do things solo in a TEAM GAME/MMO." If you have any desire to play solo, or to have any sort of progression, or to have a game that you can just sit down and play for two hours at most and have fun with, I urge you to put the idea of playing Foxhole out of your mind. You will not have fun with this game. Period. Don't even finish this review: Foxhole is not and never will be for you.

But two, there is the possibility you will like Foxhole. You will be drawn into its world. You will love its charm, its world, its thrill. I think this is possibly the worse of the two outcomes. Because you will find yourself drawn in, and slowly, very slowly, you will be beaten down. If you play now, the worst thing that could happen is you fall in love; because the game will change often, and very often you will hate those changes. At first, your concerns will be small things: you'll find this annoying, or that. A mechanic is very frustrating to deal with, you hope that it changes, you write up a suggestion after talking to people about it and several updates pass and nothing changes at all and you're still waiting six months later, a year later. You file a request for a moderator's help, they show up maybe in four hours if you are lucky or friends of the mod team; otherwise, 12 hours is not uncommon. Logistics grinds you down, frontline is unfun as soon as you want to actually make progress and not just mindlessly meatgrinder (and I want to say there are better games for doing mindless meatgrinders), but it is mindless enough that you don't really need to think, and that's just like having fun I suppose.

The infantry mechanics are inferior to other infantry games. The vehicle gameplay is inferior to other tank games. Logistics is unique, but it's also very unenjoyable on its own and players burn out hard. It's worth noting that the players who seem to enjoy the game best are partisans; namely, those who get fun out of making others suffer, because suffering is never in short supply. You'll find that you are frustrated when you put a lot of effort into something, only for it to be lost due to something entirely out of your control. And that's true of many things in Foxhole, and perhaps of Foxhole itself.

And that is entirely by design. You will find that as much as you care, and as much as you are the cog in the machine the game seems to want you to be, the game will NOT respect the time you spend playing it; it is content to waste the time of its players through needlessly long, even punitive timers on every single activity, and the ability to lose everything you worked on for reasons you couldn't know ahead of time. Often people compare it to a mobile game, with similar "waiting" requirements; but instead of paying to skip, the waiting is the point!

In short, you will find yourself rather like a drug addict; you keep playing because you are trying to relive that first hit, that moment when it clicked and you had so much fun, even long after that stopped being the case. You will become like a majority of the veteran players of the community; even as far up as the administrator of the foxhole discord, Sir: you don't play, you don't like where it's going, and yet you stick by because you are in love with a ghost, the game as it was when you first started playing; perhaps the game was never even that good, it was just nostalgia, but you remember it so vividly...

Foxhole is a game that wants to be *persistent*, it wants to have events that *matter*, and it wants to be player-determined, but it does not care if it leaves the players behind in doing so. The game marches on persistently, leaving players behind every patch. The developers will tell members of the community with 4000 hours who have been with the game since the beginning and just want to see it succeed, that they are not part of the vision, not anymore. Any individual player is just a tester and little more. No wonder the community is embittered.

Foxhole struggles to maintain a population that lets the game be playable, as it is simply *not* playable when it is too low. It's unfun, just fighting buildings and defenses instead of people. The people who seem to play foxhole most consistently are those people who are so devoted to their faction that they play not for fun, but to win, out of *obligation*. But they still hope to have fun; or perhaps, they don't understand, or don't want to admit to themselves that they are not having fun. These people will be the source of a lot of the positive reviews you see; they are in essence trying to recruit people; sucker people into the game, really just so the population is high enough that the game can be enjoyed at all.

Ask yourself: if the game is so good, why does Foxhole struggle with players so much? Why 9000 Very Positive reviews, and a playerbase of 200-400? Why does it drop from 1600 players during a sale, to 1/4 of that? Or worse... I look at the highest playercount in the last seven days, it topped out at 535. The lowest? 186. (By the way, if you want to play in the "wrong" timezone, you essentially will be missing out on most of this game, and there is only one server, so.) Why is it that, even though all of the moderators are volunteer, unpaid members of the community, only about six of them actively play? The answer is that the people in this community have an impressive ability to ignore their own feelings because if they *didn't*, they *wouldn't be playing*. They would have left already, like so many others leave foxhole. Perhaps for that reason, the people left are almost to a man crazy to some degree (sometimes quite literally; the widespread tendency for community members to have mental issues feels like no accident), which may contribute to why the developers seem to fear their veteran playerbase.

The developers are mostly nice people. But if you bring concerns to them, they'll be patient, they'll listen, but they'll kindly tell you that it is to be fixed at some later point, and that is that. If you are expecting anything more, don't bother. They make it a very real point of pride to tell their players: "If you are not having fun, you should take a break and come back." If you have any concerns, "Come back at 1.0". So much so that it has become a meme in the community, although an increasingly bittersweet one as the advice is followed: "Come back at 1.0".

So to you, the prospective buyer of this game, I am telling you to do, what the developers tell us. Wait for 1.0. Wait for them to fix all of these things. Your foxhole friends will stop playing, as mine have begun to. Perhaps you'll be the last of a clan you formed, unwilling to let go, acting out old grudges from two years ago while newbies start down the same path you tread long ago.

Conclusion/TL;DR: Whether you made it here or skipped to the end, what I bring to you is the developer's own warning: come back when it is finished. Come back to Foxhole at 1.0, not now. Come and see it then. See if they addressed the concerns of the community - if they fixed issues that had been problems forever. If they haven't fixed Foxhole for 1.0, then so much the better that you didn't buy it now. And if they do fix Foxhole by then, playing foxhole in its current state will have only soured your future fun, by leaving a bad taste in your mouth and a wistfulness you cannot explain, as it has for so many thousands of other players and former players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was always on the edge of being boring, but updates have made the game cross it. It pains me to do this because I played for 100's of hours and it was fun, but asymmetry feels unfair in the moment even if it is balanced in the grand scheme of things. Tasks that were already boring were made worse, and most importantly the game is currently a stalemate where nothing happens. The devs forgot that I don't want to feel like a solider in WWI, I want to play a game. Some people say in later war the game will be more interesting, and while I hope they are right I honestly don't care, they made the boring parts to long, I would rather go play something that is fun now. The game was on the edge of greatness, but the devs' vision helped ruin it in my eyes, they pushed too hard on things they should not have. Trenches are quite cool, but they just don't fit, and the aiming system makes it so hard. Also ques mean that the few good fights can't be fought it, and more and more fights are bad. My prophecy about what more players would do seems to have come true, we can't fit everyone. The increased grind is dumb, they don't even have a cash shop to make them want it. You can do very little solo now, even less than what you can do, and the game doesn't draw me in enough for me to want to commit enough to join a group. I might give the game another chance later this war and maybe once it hits 1.0, but I don't think I can recommend it right now, I hope that changes, because it was quite fun.

This is my old review from a different time, maybe my tastes have changed, maybe the game is worse, most likely some of both, but I don't feel this is true anymore, I leave it as a reminder of what the game could have been:

Foxhole is a great game and I would totally recommend getting it. Foxhole is trying to do a persistent war across many days and weeks where the players do almost everything with world war era tech, that few games have ever done well (about the only example I can think of is Eve online, but Foxhole has far less of a barrier to entry as Eve). The developers are not sellouts and have gone on the record that there will be no microtransactions. Your actions have large scale consequences, and since there is no artificial classes, you can do almost everything within a week or 2 of playing, although it will obviously take far longer to become experienced, and if you try to grab a tank on day one people may object. Almost everything is made by a player, which is why we don’t like things to get pointlessly wasted, but even if there are a few trolls most people will help you if you ask in world chat for someone to show you the ropes. Foxhole places a large emphasis on logistics, so if you are into the behind the scenes stuff that most games gloss over that makes an army run, you should definitely take a look

Recently some people have been shouting that the game is dying, but that is not true, and if Foxhole had the player numbers suddenly just by 10 times, we would not be able to accommodate everyone, so the game is not dead or dying by a long shot, and the map can be reduced so the game is still functioning fine with even small numbers of players. There have also been some balance issues of late, but the devs are responsive and none of the issues are game breaking, plus they are all getting fixed.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really difficult game to review.

The developers have made this game in a way that really needs to be commended. For starters most of early access or all of early access (I cant remember) the game was free. (Edit: Still early access but for a long time it was free). You did not have to pay someone to test their game.

What started as a few maps with a few weapons and bare bones stock assets has developed into something really ambitious and great looking (On Paper). Tons of content has been added to this game for free. New defenses, guns, vehicles, boats, construction equipment, stock assets replaced with custom ones that fit the two factions.

Graphically the game looks good for what its trying to be and the sound design is pretty solid.

Don't worry its not all sunshine and roses. Game play is where the problems start to appear. If you have ever played Running with Rifles its similar just much better looking. The game is pretty daunting for new players unless your just going to the front lines. Even then the game suffers from the same problem many top down games suffer from.

In the corners we put our trust. You will be moving your camera more than you do just about anything in this game. Making sure your corner is pointed to the enemy so you have maximum range. If not, you risk being shot by someone you cant even see. Your eyesight is limited by your weapon. So a rifle with iron sights will let you see further than a sub machine gun. Effective range and vision are the same thing and they shouldn't be. Oddly many of the above problems are reduced when night arrives as players cant see far at all and you often end up shooting at where you saw tracers.

Additional supply depots required! Your team are the fighters and the workers. Currently there is only 1 server or a group of servers that are connected as different territories. When the war starts each side has very basic tech and its up to you to mine the necessary resources, refine them, and make everything from tanks to bandages. Your Moriarty from Sherlock Holmes. People need to do this, they need to drive trucks, build defenses not just on the front lines but the supply lines and back territory to prevent infiltration. As the war goes on you use tech you find to get new stuff. Armored cars come out, and anti tank rifles to counter them. Its a very interesting early war to late war tech progression. Mines can be automated and your logistics should get better as you get better stuff.

All of this sounds great right? You get the Germans who love Euro Truck Simulator to drive rifles and stuff to the front lines and the people who want action can build up the front and launch breakthrough attacks.

The problem is its hard to coordinate with so many people. Many people like doing logistics. I just put on a podcast, get in my truck and try to interpret what people need. Which can be very hard. Combat can be great fun but it can also be a great lesson on why artillery is demoralizing and how armies just break apart and flee because of a decisive battle.

The wars last too long for the current player base. The dedicated players and clans are on often and help organize a territory (Or they at least try to) but eventually things just start breaking down. Logistics overstocks towns that are being encircled, critical defenses are neglected till they decay to almost nothing, organized clans lay waste to clueless disorganized players.

Eventually the losing side will see less and less players logging in which means logistics and further research is hopeless and the enemy gradually takes town by town after clearing the automated defenses. Defenders are outnumbered and under geared.

Currently one faction the Colonials just cant seem to get it together. They lack the necessary large clans to help keep logistics semi working and provide morale boost with effective offensives. Some say the teams are stacked and while it seems that way its hard to prove. I really think the developers need to reduce the number of territories and make the wars not last as long. Currently the Colonials couldn't pour water out of a boot if the instructions were on the heel. So maybe have less territories open, which requires more coordination, and intelligence.

Am I saying dumb the game down, well maybe a little. There are already not enough players for 3 or more territories in conflict, so maybe have players focus more on the territory map and not the big risk board. Regardless of what you think of my suggestions something has to happen.

A good Comp stomp can be a lot of fun with friends. The problem is the Colonials are not AI who will always log on to die endlessly. They are people who are asking themselves "With my limited time is this what I want to spend it on?" If people enjoy getting beaten into submission they will do what Paul Giamatti does on Billions and get a Dominatrix.

I want to recommend this game because of how impressive the developers have been and its only $19.99. But the game is dying and has serious problems that while I hope they get fixed, I'm not sure they will or if there is even still time. Hopefully I can change this to positive in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
