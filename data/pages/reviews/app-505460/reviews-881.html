\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have sunk many hours into this game and can safely say, it is definitely not for everybody.
However, that being said I want to make sure anyone reading this review knows why:

If you enjoy ww1/2 era games where you are single handedly taking out enemy outposts, this is not the game for you.
If you enjoy being able to make a real difference by soloing an objective, this is not the game for you.
If you enjoy being a one man logistical army, this is not the game for you.

All of the above mentioned things have the same thing in common. If you enjoy doing things by yourself, you are not going to have fun with this game.

You are a single inconsequential soldier in an army of other inconsequential non-special soldiers. However, when you put together a group of these plain soldiers, they can become a powerful fighting force, or a logistical machine capable of making a real difference in the war effort.

This game is about teamwork and doing what is best for the side you are fighting for. Nobody cares or can even see what your KDA is. Nobody cares how much total scrap or bmats you have moved in your truck. What they care about is if you are helping the war effort in a meaningful way.
If you just want to play by yourself and not have to deal with anyone else (griefers included), move along, Foxhole is not for you.
But if you want to be a part of something bigger and get that adrenaline rush when your squad storms the enemies FOB you just blew a hole into, or feel truly accomplished when your logistical teamwork starts pumping out tanks like a factory line.
Then this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game with a very large world that almost every inch of it is possible for combat. It has world conquest modes making hundreds of players work with eachother to defeat the enemy faction. With men on the front and men in the back running logistics this is one of the most unique open world warfare games I have found by far. There is no play time limit, no campaign or single player just pure online fighting. You can make friends and even have a good social time while breaking apart your enemy. Active resource makes it feel like a war of attrition, every item used or supply taken spawned can possibly turn to a great downfall as you run out of supply and become over run by enemy, or the other way around. If you enjoy working with massive amounts of players and partaking in massive player vs player warfare this game is just for you.

Types of players:

Soldier - Just your average run of the mil soldier constantly running a long the front attacking and defending against what ever the enemy brings.

Leader - This type of player is rare, but a high ranking person who knows what is needed and what is requried to be done. You now and then see them running around the front rallying soldiers and coordinating every one to pull off that needed victory in a batle. They also plan special attacks such as beach landings or flanking manuevres or can be seen operating an armored battalion.

Logistical Person - The true heroes of the war, they take time and effort to do a job almost no one wants to do, but they do it anyway as the war needs its supply. You should thank and commend these lads more, as they build the defences of the towns you defend and bring the ever so needed supply to the front to fuel your attack.

Special Operator - Whether they be manning a mortar or a tank, these people are uncommon but still found and when found are using some sort of utility to widdle away the enemy from mortar barages or being an operator of an armored vehicle.

They are plenty more types of players throughout the game that all hold their special (or useless) role in the war effort.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the past 2 weeks, I have accumulated 26.5 hours out of my 37.9. The 11.4 hours before these 2 weeks, were probably made in the two weeks after it first was released. After coming back from a year long hiatus, I can say for sure, that this game has improved immensley. From the tech tree, to the factories/facilities system, to just the refining of combat, these past 2 weeks have been so fun. Me being the grindy sort of person, I play mostly a logitics person. The first time I came back, the logistics in endless shore were unstoppable, our QM was just giving us orders left and right, and I felt like even though I had just came back, that I was a huge part in the war. We had stayed up all night, coffee after coffee, grinding and grinding, by the end of the night, our Logi Operation had diminished from about 8 people, to just me and the QM. Me and him stayed up an extra hour after everyone got off, just making bmats, endless bmats. The next day I came back, I had learned that the leader of the attack operation had been toxic, and had been kicked off after all the teamkilling he did, he was also very egotistical, on the bright side though, he had made huge land advances. It didnt matter though, all the hard work we had done in endless, was gone, the collies had taken it back, and now we were pushed up in weathered almost a third of the map. But that wasnt gonna stop me from putting in that work to take it back. After just a half hour of doing Logi, our Logi op had enough, so we began to start doing things ourself, we had built an FOB down south of wraiths, and we began to start moving into wightwalk. We did it! We actually took it, and with that, we had also built up our op. More people saw that we were taking towns, so more people joined us. With more manpower, we went for crows next. With the help of a guy who knew how to use mortars, we took it, but it was much bloodier. After that, we went for Dullahans, which once again, we steamrolled. At this time though, it was late and people began to get off. With alot of people gone, the last 4 of said, "Lets get one more". So we went, and with bruteforce, took Northwatch, built it up, and that was it, we had finnaly taken everthing back! I was exhausted, so I ended up getting off. The next day though, I saw that with everything we had done, our team began to get back into endless. That feeling of knowing that you did something to affect the tide of a war, a war that we were going to lose in 2 days if noone was doing anything, to a war of hope, a war that we can actually come back from, is amazing. Fast forward to now, we are back to where it was that 2nd day, not alot of land left, on the defense, barely holding. It sucks to know that all that hard work that you put in is just gone, like that, but thats why I play, to get to that feeling, taking back everthing, and affecting the outcome of the war, that feeling of "I changed the outcome of a game". Though the game might not be perfect, its good enough to keep me coming back for that feeling, and not alot games can do that. 10/10 in my book.

TL;DR
A game that gives you that feeling of "I did something to change the outcome of this game", a feeling not alot of other games can do.
