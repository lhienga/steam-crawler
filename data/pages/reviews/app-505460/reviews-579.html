\r\n\t\t\t\t\t The game is an excellent demonstration of modern massively multiplayer games' capabilities. The game offers opportunities for solo players and regiments alike, from entire, region-spanning operations, to small partisan operations, cutting off much-needed, player-made supplies to open fronts. Even though every way to play the game is so different, it all helps in the end to achieve a goal hundreds of other players are fighting for alongside you. That small partisan operation can deny the enemy spawn crucial spawn tokens, that region-spanning operation can decay defences, and the logi-bros in the backline fuel it all.

It goes without saying, however, that certain aspects of the game need to be refined. But, after all, it is what you get for the first game of its kind to delve this deep into the intricacies of war. Games like Planetside 2 offer large, hundred player servers, but dont include structure building or logistics at a level like Foxhole. Some aspects that do need working on, is developer-player communication, tediousy of logistics, and the decay of structures. Recent updates have been fixing some of those issues, however.

I am glad to see the developers working on a new game, complete with it's own engine and different systems, to improve or possibly innovate on this genre and game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo the 1.0 update is out and it is... very VERY rough.

Lets go through the new features: Facilities (factories), trains and fire.

First is fire, they added flamethrowers, flame tanks and fire artillery. In the launch balance a hand held flame thrower is comically bad. If you use your entire backpack of fuel on a wooden watch tower (second cheapest building) it will catch fire and burn for about 5 seconds taking 5% damage. This is a building that can be destroyed with a single clip of machine gun ammo. What I am saying is nobody uses these.

Flame tanks are much better but are only available multiple weeks into the war. Flame artillery is exceptionally powerful but the vehicles that fire it are both on the same team.

Second is facilities, lets start with the good. You can build oil refineries now so oil isn't the half finished mess it once was and the good fuel is somewhat accessible now. The vehicle facilities can turn vehicles from one variant into another which basically fixes the awful vehicle tech system the game has had up to this point. Prices are comically high but whatever.

Now the bad facilities don't actually make very many things, if you want small arms regular ammo, BANDAGES, respawn items or anything except for ammo for artillery you still have to go to the old towns. So this isn't at all a replacement for the existing system its just more difficult version of that system involving more driving and bickering with your own team.

Now for the real problem, Facilities decay at lunatic speed, unless you have people bringing fuel to the power plants at all hours its going to start decaying and fast. Basically without a very large group spanning many time zones you won't really get to play with this feature unless you play it like a second on call job.

Third. Trains.

Its week 3 they unlocked sometime in week 2 and I still haven't seen one in motion! They are so expensive, the rails (which rapid decay lol) are also expensive that once again you probably won't actually get to interact with this feature. At best you will passively observe someone else use it.

Which is a real shame, it looked like a lot of work went into them and the game is being played as if they don't exist.

Now for the original review which was about the toxicity of the community. Which has not changed... If anything they are even saltier now.

Strange update:

Sadly while the game itself is good [with the exception of a general disrespect for the players time which is sort of unavoidable in atritional war]. The community surrounding it is... toxic? in strange ways and probably best avoided.

Normally that wouldn't reflect on the game itself and wouldn't be appropriate in a steam review.

But due to the way that game play in foxhole works the player base itself is VERY much a part of the experience. And communication out of game is necessary in order for a lot of the meaningful in game activities (artillery, major pushes, long term supply and most other coordination. Which will bring you into contact with people you likely would wish you didn't have know about and CERTAINLY won't want to talk to.

Even more problematically the largest community hubs the subreddit and the discord server are both moderated directly by the dev team. Which means that the lack of proper moderation of these spaces actually does fall on the developers.

Quite a weird situation.

But yeah try your best not to engage with it outside of the game itself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t185 Hrs in and I wouldn't recommend, Toxic Clan dominance TK'ing and I played on shard 2 for New players, mechanically the game is very interesting but extreme spam of powerful AI turret structures and the race to get them to turn on dominates effective play. The logistics inventory system is a exercise in tedium with one having to continuously hang around to shift+click for the joy of a percentage windows update to complete and then repeat...
The visual range at which you can see opponents and kill them will vary on the resolution you have tough if you use a wide screen format
My biggest gripe though is how many of the features of the game are designed and implemented in a way that your fellow players actions will actively antagonize and creates bad feeling between your fellow team players...
Take for instance the storage yards big hubs of activity with one single platform to spawn in vehicles and fill crates creating grief when anyone takes to long, but worst the garrison supply system every structure that is built puts a demand on a central supply bunker base, encampment etc. once supply runs out everything starts to decay rapidly and as everyone can build structures and defenses willy nilly with no consideration of effectivity or cost to maintain there is a cycle of build up and decay and honestly it creates bad feeling between players, the pervasive atmosphere of suspicion of "ALT's" or alternate accounts players who will join the opposing side with a different account to sabotage and destroy high value vehicles resources etc and all of this is fundamentally a design error for a MMO IMO

So why am I still playing? Primarily this a critical EA review, there are enough gushy shill 3 hour play time reviews with dozens of likes to make this review irrelevant to the games overall rating. I for one wanted to see it through at least one campaign and as bad as the toxic clan interactions are there are lovely interactions with players and the game is intriguing, quite unique and in EA so things are bound to get better right?\t\t\t\t\t
