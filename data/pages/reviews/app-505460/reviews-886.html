\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have, as some of you may have noticed, over 100 hours in this game.

As it is I cannot recommend a purchase.

Some basics;

This is a massive multiplayer game that involves hundreds of individuals per region. The world map is divided into between 12 and a handful of regions depending on the circumstances of the world situation (i.e. war, skirmishes). The mechanics of the game are individual, each person plays as an RPG like 3rd-person top down trooper who walks or rides vehicles to get from point A to point B, often responsible for carrying raw materials, processed materials or fighting equipment.

The fights involve 2 factions of vaguely inter-war period design called Wardens and Colonials, there is no particular limit to which faction you join, except during a "war" which may last for weeks and usually involves the maximum number of regions.

These regions can support one another but are rarely populated if they do not lie directly adjacent to an enemy territory. Some are capable of supporting naval conflict but all can support conflict involving various infantry weapons and tacitcs as well as a handful of combat vehicles.

The Good:
This game has voice communication and a system of quickly organizing groups to push different objectives.

A multi-layered system of game-mechanics, involving fortifications, logistics, squad tactics and tools such as gas masks, explosives, vehicles, difficult to carry machine guns, water crossings and terrain cover.

Finally, a carefully balanced resource system to allow different equipment to be researched, resources processed, and put into the hands of friendly forces pushing objectives across the spectrum of the war.

The Bad:
This game has a few exploits which drag down the overall quality of the game, there are for example various ways to abuse the terrain height to ensure un assailable firing positions, on top of walls that ought to be inaccessable for example.

The game rewards rank through a voting system, so those who organize with their friends will find themselves able to farm votes and become high rank though they may or may not have actually contributed time and effort towards the actual game. The ability to vote is completly independent of play.

However the most serious problem is most certainly that the moderation is somewhat crowd sourced, multiple times while playing the report system has been abused by a handful of players who will ban their teammates in order to steal equipment or silence complaints. With the game being developed by such a small company there may not be much choice if there is to be a way to prevent team-killing, spamming obscenities, stealing equipment or otherwise throwing the game.

But when the groups that are engaging in those activites are the ones reporting others the system has no way to differentiate and will ban the players trying to enjoy the game as it was intended.

My experience has been one in which this exploitation of the ban system is abused while I am playing perhaps 10% of my total game time. Each time is incredibly distinct in my memory and the toxic behavior is what stands out more than anything else about this game.

Twice this has happened to me, more times it has happened to others. I have seen players get thrown out of the game for trying to take the equipment they made to a front, others were not happy with and were reporting a griefing player or were even shooting back at a player who was teamkilling but the teamkiller's squad, that would abuse the players who try to report their squadmate, would mass report over the course of an hour and get the whistle-blower auto-banned.

The In-Between:
The fortifications which are built will disappate as time goes on, what this means is that, usually within a day or two, everything built up during the course of a fight will have to be re-built at an ingame time of potentially tens of hours. This is ostensibly to allow the game to progress and not get bogged down interminably.

While, neither positive nor negative, a necessary evil, if there was a way to more permenantly make an investment in your sides victory then I think it would improve the game, but as things stand it may not be optimal. What you do in a game that may last over a week will only be significant for a day or two at most.

Suggestions:

The rank system is a social construct. But, without a way to see the actual game-play contributions, the game defaults to a system that is less about helping your side win and more about creating often toxic and petty social cliques. This is disasterous and has a huge impact on the entire playerbase.

The game needs a way to deal with game-breaking behaviour without turning itself into a gang-land style tyranny. Random dudes who hook up with a handful of friends do not need to be given de-facto mod privledges, it is insane that this happens.

TL;DR
Game is technically impressive, has a small problem with exploits, and has a serious problem with toxic and abusive players who can become de'facto mods.

I really liked the game when everything worked as intended a lot, but I absolutely would not recommend it at the moment.\t\t\t\t\t

- Extremely addictive

- Devs are literally one with the community
- Skill based gameplay where situational awareness is key

If you want to have action and feel like you accomplished something in the couple hours you have for games, this is not the game for you. The problem is too much realism but not enough. There is the trench stalemated lines like that kind of warfare had, but no organization or command get the teamwork together and make progress. If a person has the time to join up in a large clan and play together under a common goal, then sure but I don't have that kind of time. Until something is done to speed up the pace and make the time put in worth spending I can't recommend this game. One quick example, all the vehicles are locked by players, and you can wind up spending an hour or more trying to obtain one, by time you get one you're out of time to continue playing so what is the point?\t\t\t\t\t
