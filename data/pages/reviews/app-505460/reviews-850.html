\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery little thing you do can affect the entire war greatly. Whether it be from collecting scraps and refining them, or destroying a single pill box with a grenade, every one of your actions matter. You play with hundreds of other players on a large scale map and every single player has the potential to do anything. You can be a combat engineer, building fortifications and forts to help your team defend. You can be a medic, serving on the front lines and patching up your teammates as battles rage on. You can be a logistic driver, collecting materials and crafting weapons to feed the endless demand of the front line soldiers. Or you can be an everyday solider, with any weapons of your choice to counter the enemies you are facing.

That is why Foxhole is fantastic. You aren't constrained by certain classes or roles. You play as yourself. If you want to run logistics, you're free to do so. If you want to fight, you can do that too. If you want to do a bit of everything, that's totally available to you. You're just one cog in a giant war machine, the main and supporting character of the war.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAllow me to start with the positive things about Foxhole:

  • The original concept of the game as a war simulator is interesting and promising.

  • An active development team, that usually tries to stay connected to the game "Current and available" playerbase.

  • Impressive graphics and world design details.

  • For an early access Foxhole doesn't suffer from game breaking bugs, and whenever that happens usually the developers are quick to react to them.

  • The game runs pretty well on mid-range systems, only suffers from stuttering in windowed fullscreen mode due to having vsync grayed out in that mode for some reasons.

  • Reasonably priced for what it offers.

  • A fairly welcoming and mature community, where you will find many people who will offer to help and teach you about the game.

Now for the long and unfortunately negative things about Foxhole:

  • Winning conditions require a lot of grinding and hard work to achieve and maintain, which is not a bad thing, however they can easily be sniped by literally one guy, resetting multiple days of work, in a matter of minutes.

  • The Winning/losing faction in this current meta (low population) is determined by the available number of players for each faction, there is no balancing mechanic to this issue at all and the game suffers in most areas because of it.

  • The game feels unfair and punishing for those who do the backline hard work, in Foxhole you actually need to do everything from scrapping and gathering materials, refining them, then using the new materials to build and craft everything, like weapons, ammunition, vehicles, and defenses among other things, it's a long and hard grinding process and it’s 100% unrewarding, especially when it comes to building as it is actually the most time and material consuming part of the grind process, let me put it this way, the game for one reason or another suffers from low population most of the time, especially in the middle stages of the game, so in order to prevent one faction from steamrolling the other faction that suffers from the low population you can build Ai defenses, the problem is, these defenses in low population are still useless, take for example the Bunker wall, a building you unlock in the tech tree (which will be mentioned later) very late in the game, and you spend tons of material and a lot of time to build a defensive line out of them to protect a town or objective, this formidable structure can be easily picked one by one and out-ranged by a man with a RPG just like that. I get the point that the developers don't want the game to be PvE but rather PVP, but in the current state and the low population it's most of the time a PvE game where the E is just a target practice.

  • If that wasn't enough to demoralize you as a builder, the developers and for purely server performance concerns, have implemented a Decay system, where every building on the map except Town halls and Forward Bases suffers from decay, gets damaged overtime and eventually gets destroyed on its own. unless you add to that long and hard grinding process of building them, another long and hard process to maintain them.

  • A false sense of democracy and freedom of choice in a “War” simulator game, intensifies in the technology tree bad design, which pushes people into splitting resources on multiple projects in the tech tree at the same time, hindering any chance of getting a specific tech unlocked as soon as possible, and resulting in another unnecessary factor of prolonging the game. And it's not like there is any strategic depth or benefit about how the tech tree branches in Foxhole, eventually you can unlock everything, let me put it this way: It’s like you are being attacked by a wild animal, and you are given the choice to spend 200$ to get a pistol to defend yourself right now, or you can spend 150$ today and another 150$ tomorrow to get a rifle the day after, in Foxhole most people go for the 2nd choice for some reason, thanks to the design of the tech tree.

  • The gunfight mechanics are linear in the current patch, you only need to press X to prone to have the maximum accuracy, double click on the enemy and they are dead, or WASD in circles like crazy to dodge bullets while effectively being able to shoot and kill your target, nothing strategic about it, unless you try to flank around sometimes then maybe. I also found that the best way to kill an enemy is to get close and personal where accuracy doesn’t matter.

  • As I mentioned in the positive part of the review, the community is helpful to new players most of the times, offering guidance and information for people who ask for it, however in the process it seems that sometimes the developers rely too much on this in a way that it doesn’t always provide the best expected results, for example so far, the game lacks any proper tutorial on how to play the game, the argument is that the game keeps changing in every update therefore having a tutorial is pointless as it needs to be updated as well with every patch. However, in my opinion I don’t see any reason why not if the developers are serious and I know they are on making the game more appealing to new players, as I know many people probably hesitate to ask others for help out of pride sometimes or lack the ability to communicate with different languages.

  • Another aspect of the game where the developers rely too much on the community to help is the moderating system or to be honest the lack of it, if you notice or even witness any suspicious behavior, you can report it to Foxhole official Mod channel on discord, however you are always required to provide an evidence about your claim materialized in screenshots or even video clips sometimes. It's not a bad thing in principle the issue though most of the times you are too late to do anything about it. So, in order to properly achieve any results from reporting you need to be always on guard to quickly press F12, or if you are dedicated enough maybe activate Windows game bar and use the recording feature... and hopefully you can catch the hacker in time. Due to this issue, the game suffers from a noticeable amount of hacks and exploits, I believe in fair gameplay and sportsmanship, so I personally witnessed and reported both my side and the enemy’s using exploits such as farming bots, speed and map teleportation hacks, and someone using an invisibility exploit to run around and throw sticky bombs, that and the fact that people use multiple accounts to spy on the other faction to get intel about their defenses and where they stockpile their valuable resources.

In general Foxhole mechanics are designed for many players, however with the consistent low population nowadays, these mechanics feel unfair and punishing, and experienced players usually take advantage of this problem to heavily sabotage and delay an already long and stalemate gameplay experience, the issue though so far there are no obvious intentions to address this problem and balance it, in fact somehow it’s considered part of the game and you should deal and live with it.

For me personally I will still be giving Foxhole the chance to grow and improve and I will probably update my review based on those improvements, and don’t get me wrong my review here is just a tough love for a promising video game, I love to see it gets better and have more playerbase. and this is the only reason I'm reviewing it in the first place, trying to cast a light on something really negative about this game from my own experience, and when I intend to recommend a game, I try to think if the game worth recommending to friends and family, to have fun and invest time on... unfortunately in the current state I can’t recommend Foxhole.

If you reached the end of this long text, I sincerely would like to thank you for spending your time reading it, and remember this is only one man’s opinion, Thank you! \t\t\t\t\t

In foxhole whenever you first join a war you get to chose between two factions, The Colonials AKA Goblins, and The Wardens AKA Blueberries. Both of the factions have different play styles with the current Asymmetrical system in the game. foxhole also requires a lot of time and dedication when playing so you can be there for hours. The whole game is player driven

Asymmetry, To start foxhole for past 6 months has started to go for a more asymmetrical feel to the game. were one faction gets something and the other gets something completely different. the devs have said that most games when dealing with asymmetry tend to go for a rock paper scissor kind of approach to balance and they wanna stay away from. this in turn causes the balance of the game to be skewed favoring one faction more than the other even if they aren't bias. both factions have different vehicles that do different things and now both factions also have different weapons.

All your time will be gone, i have owned this game for 2.5 years and in that time i put in 1000 hours which isn't a lot compared to other players. there are some things tho which require babysitting like Bunker bases which wont even get into. and the fact that you have to prepare stuff if you wanna do really fun stuff. sometimes there might be a public tank and shells but most of the time there isn't so you need the materials to make the tank and shells so to get a head start you wanna do that before they are unlocked.

Player driven, by far this is my favourite part of the game. The whole game is player from base building to reconnaissance and even progressing through the tech tree. in the beginning of each war you get a limited amount of supplies to start you off but that is it no more help. so you want guns, you must first go to resource field collect it refine it and then use the materials to make guns. once your are done making supplies you can store it for later or deliver to a front line to help the soldiers. all of the defenses and bases at one point were made by other players. as the devs put it themselves the players are the content. sometimes a war might be cut short but that is about it for dev intervention
