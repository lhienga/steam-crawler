\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReminds me a lot of Eve Online. Massive real time warfare with multiple dimensions including mining materials, manufacturing, transport, artillery, front lines infantry combat and sneaky spec ops stuff for the gamers who want to be intel guys, spotters, or disrupt supply lines. All buildings and materials are player made, from mining the scrap metal that eventually become tanks, structures, etc. I haven't played around with upgrading technology or buildings yet, but that is a thing also and a collective effort that the entire war effort benefits from together.

You earn a persistent rank in the game that is awarded by other players commending your efforts, which promotes team work surprisingly well.

In my first experience in game, I was pushing up with some tanks, as infantry, across a bridge which turned into a major choke point. We battled on that bridge for hours, as the skirmish line went back and forth. "Logi's" were trucking in supplies to keep attrition under control. Eventually, the enemy snuck a squad behind enemy lines and mined the roads our logistics trucks were using and blew a truck full of ammo to pieces. The logistics dude wrangled up some other dudes, myself included, to come hand carry the supplies to the front line. Most of us were slaughtered by ambushes, but I made it through and supplied the camp as reinforcements retreated back behind the line to clean up the enemy squad. Many commendations and a promotion for the effort. The game play can be magic.

Looking forward to how this game shapes up toward release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA jank-filled mess of good ideas and weird ones. The isometric shooter side of things is somewhat unsatisfying, with combat feeling more like a matter of luck than skill and positioning. Infantry side combat is largely bland. The game encourages sitting in a foxhole [funny enough] and acting as if its a WW1 simulator, waiting for the other side to come over the top. However there's no real system to dictate large group orders. If you do not use outside products such as discord to coordinate with groups, you don't really see massive offenses on purpose. Vehicles are very very glitchy, with players getting ejected and thrown hundreds of meters, or vehicles getting permanently stuck against [and between] things that make no sense. A pair of bicycles can permanently trap a tank for instance if two players wished to troll someone.

The bread and butter of the game is the logistics system and base building, but it's largely unbalanced and unrewarding as resources you gather tend to be instantly stolen and taken off for pet projects, vehicles built for the front never seem to actually get there unless you port them there. And if you do manage to get them there, they either get glitched out or someone drives them into a corner of the map for "safe keeping".

Finally, the devs seem to have a ton of ideas but almost no one going back and balancing things out. Despite there being something like five types of artillery and mortars, they all have about the same splash damage and all have the same range relatively. Some vehicles are innately useless or serve no purpose other than to meme. The entire winter war concept was a cool idea but had absolutely no execution behind it. E.G. it can start snowing which causes troops and vehicles to freeze solid unless you're near a bonfire or inside a building. No cold weather clothing or other methods to prevent freezing in the open. So an RNG that gives no warning [meteorolgist role in the future?] can completely...freeze... a front to an annoyingly slow slog of people jumping in and out of bunkers to shoot a few times then run back inside to warm their hands.

Overall TLDR there's a ton of promise and the game can really be fun with friends. Solo-play is not rewarded and would recommend against picking it up unless you plan on joining a group. Game mechanics are halfbaked and don't expect anything interesting to change anytime soon. Took far to long to get to this point and nothing seems to be changing quickly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole used to be fun, and I considered it a good game. However, updates to balance in the last four months have significantly worsened the overall experience of the game. As such, I'm changing my review to a negative one.
Foxhole in its present state is plagued by a few major problems:
1. every activity in Foxhole is a huge time-sink. Respawn timers are 30-60 seconds long. Gathering resources is slow and unengaging. When operating a tank, most of your time is spent sitting around repairing, reloading, or waiting for daytime.
2. Balance is terrible. Equipment and vehicles are unique to each side, and their stats vary wildly. As a result, one side or the other usually has a major advantage. The developers are slow to fix balance issues and frequently introduce new equipment that further breaks the balance.
3. Communication between developers and the playerbase is extremely lacking. Feedback is ignored, issues are not fixed for weeks or months after players report them.

Save your money and your time. Play a different game.\t\t\t\t\t
