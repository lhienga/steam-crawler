\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing but not for everyone and I can't stress this enough.

THIS IS A HIGH STAKES MULTIPLAYER GAME!

I will try and use this review to drive a point trough that many people might not immediately get when approaching the game for the first time. People discover this game and become enamored by the concept of a massive player driven MMO war, that's what happened the moment I discovered it and I had a blast playing it ever since. BUT! I repeat myself.

THIS IS A HIGH STAKES MULTIPLAYER GAME!

At first glance this might seem absurd right? This is the game where you play as a single soldier in a massive war, a cog in the machine, your actions are mostly minimal and irrelevant in the grand scheme of things. How could this possibly be a high stakes game? All of those statements are true, however the counterpoint lies in the fact that we are all fighting in a SINGLE month long war, and people REALLY want to win. The stakes become even higher when you consider people are spending hours every day building, gathering resources, planning, training, recruitnig, studying just to get that W.

This game is not like a single 25 minute match on your average MP game, people really get passionate about their faction and winning the war. This passion is, in my opinion, one of the most amazing and unique aspects of the game, It is the main driver behind the comradery and dedication you will often find on the battlefield. This passion brings out the magic moments of cooperation where hundreds of players organically acomplish something amazing like an epic last stand, an overwhelming offensive or even building a megabase.

But on the flipside, everyone expects you to be a team player. And being a good team player might not seem exactly obvious when you are starting the game. The vast majority of the community is super receptive of new players and someone will almost allways help if you are struggling with any aspect of the game, just ask around in voice or text chat. Does this mean your actions as a single new player actually have a significant impact on the front or even the greater war itself?

NO!
(99% of the time at least, there are some things you can do that can really screw everyone up, but don't think too hard about that. Just don't build any bridges by yourself and you will be fine.)

Your negative actions or missplays won't cause everyone to lose, but people may call you out on it. Not because you are going to tank the war by yourself, but because you might not know what you are doing. Not knowing obviously does not make you a bad team player. The problem arises when you don't know what you are doing and you don't care. In this case you are not being a team player, and people care about that, players are passionate and they won't be receptive of new players wasting public resources selfishly.

Now, a few ways not to be a bad team player when starting out:
-Be humble and always assume you are ignorant! If you are not sure how something works ask first before wasting public resources !
-If you want to learn a new weapon ask how to use it, don't waste all the public special weapons by dying while brute forcing your learning!
-If you want to drive a tank just ask for a tank crew, don't just drive one by yourself!
-If you want to build a base ask if you are in a good spot!
-Never build bridges without massive infantry and vehicle support behind you.

Fundamentally you will have to understand this is not a game about YOU, In Foxhole we all win together, all the thousands of players on the same faction. This goes for new and old players even some elitist player clans. If you become humble and understand that we are all in this war together, you will experience the joy of being a good team player in an amazing community.

FOR CALLAHAN!
Eat babies!(or whattever the green faction stands for ;p)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game that is truly innovative, breaks the mould in a variety of ways; the freedom of player decision is truly amazing and makes for some highly engaging gameplay, from designing/building bases and then defending them from incoming attack to conspiring with other teammates to assault or invade key strategic areas, at the very least pushing your teams front line forward and at most making for truly captivating as your team wrestles with the enemy for tactical advantage in a region, player made clans help to coordinate efforts between clan members and ultimately help the entire team, having to supply forward bases and constant repairing of buildings under fire is a small price to pay for such a great experience.

Shooting and driving mechanics can be a bit jank at times, but that is a negligible issue for what this game brings to the table.

The problem lies with the total player control and clans being as big as they are, if you plan to be part of a clan then i assume you'd have little to no issues. However from my experience being a solo player, trying to stay committed to the front or even having fun can be strained when interacting with some clan members. The total power given to players means that some members of clans will act insanely pretentious, and will at best disregard other players thoughts/ideas outright, and at worst you will be tk'd if you are seen to be interfering with their work, even if that isn't the case. From my experience they're no point trying to reason with these individuals as they will simply argue against/disregard your point.

TL;DR if you're a solo, be prepared to be overworked and underappreciated, while higher ranking, less competent players experience the opposite, just like the real military!\t\t\t\t\t
