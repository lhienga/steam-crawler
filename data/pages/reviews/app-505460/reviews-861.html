\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible. I went into it expecting to be disappointed by under-delivering and buggy game mechanics, empty worlds, and a toxic community, but was very pleasantly surprised on all counts. That said, of course you occasionally run into a troll or griefer. It's an online game. Thankfully, the game mechanics allow for these people to be kicked and/or banned quickly. Thus, most of the people are truly helpful and committed to bringing their faction to victory. I'm particularly impressed by the developers' focus on teamwork. Everything is player-made: the walls you hide behind, the bullets you shoot, the vehicles you drive, the binoculars you scout with, the bandages you bloody, and the guns to which you cling. As a result, players must coordinate logistics to supply soldiers fighting on the front line. This keeps the game unique, fresh, and diverse. When you're tired of being on the receiving end of mortars and HMG fire, your comrades will gladly shower you in commendations for collecting scrap metal and organizing supply lines to refine it and deliver player-manufactured items to the front line. When you're bored of collecting supplies, grab a rifle, med kit, hammer, tank, or gunship and bring hell to the enemy or fortify a defensive position. Further, any particularly dangerous weapon--tanks, gunships, mortars, howitzers, and others--are team-operated. Remember playing Battlefield, where one player gets all the kills because he happened to snag a tank? No more. In Foxhole, the smallest tank requires a dedicated driver, gunner, and (ideally) spotter/commander. Heavy tanks require a crew of five. The developers' focus on squad weapons and logistics/manufacturing beautifully balances the need for teamwork and independence in a way that requires you to coordinate with your comrades in arms, but isn't frustrating. Some of the reviews here say that if you want to be THE soldier, this game isn't for you. If you want to be A soldier, it is. I completely agree. Your character is not a god-man capable of sponging up bullets and tank shells like a Shamwow. If you want to dash behind enemy lines and gun down 35 foes while insulting their mothers and drinking Mountain Dew, you should stick with Call of Duty. If you're more into coordinating with team members, thinking tactically, and engaging in brutal battles that sometimes last for several (real life) days over contested areas, this is for you. It's hard for me to believe this game is still in early access. Sure, it has some occasional glitches, but I'd honestly consider it worth its full price as-is, even without promise of further development.

Now, you've probably seen reviews noting some variation of the assertion that the devs favor one faction over the other. I have not personally found this to be the case. Granted, I don't peruse Reddit, Discord, or anything like that, nor do I pay particularly close attention to dev posts, but nothing in the gameplay suggests the claims of favoritism to be valid. Much of the controversy seems to stem from a bad tactical call on one faction's part. A recent update permitted (difficult to acquire) missile launches on positions. According to reports, one faction struck a heavily-contested area at a particularly bad time and ended up harming themselves and their team's morale more than their foe. This likely contributed to the turning of war's tide. It seems to me that people are upset the devs are not doing anything to rectify this tactical error (and even claim it was sabotage). Personally, I consider the laissez-faire approach here a very positive thing. The point of this game is teamwork. The devs are not our mothers and should not hold our hands and correct mistakes our teams have made. Bad things happen in war and it sucks, but I'm here to enjoy the game regardless of victory or defeat--not to be babied into a comfortable victory, immune to the consequences of poorly-executed actions.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start of by saying this
"When 4 years ago I bought this game, I recived what I expected. If I would be given same choice, but today I would deffinitly not buy it."

Over past 4 years game has constantly been becoming worse and worse. I could write an endless essey about all the problems so let me bring 4 major ones.

Problem number 1: Devs don't read feedback.
You probably heard that one a lot, not only in this game but in the others. Well, I will spare you standard "they don't prioritize community ideas" because that has a fair point to it. Instead let me tell you how game developers of this game has killed the active feedback. You see, there used to be server and closed groups of players who would be beta testers for new updates. But that was killed by devs, for no particular reason. And you can notice, because ever since updates started to have more bugs and became more and more unfriendly towards players (for example great freezing mechanic). Some people would say "but there is discord channel for feedback" yes there is, and game developers themselves said they read it rarely. Others will say that "well, there is devbranch before each major update" and fair enough, it is there, but once again, developers not only take feedback comming out of it in very limited amount, but they usually listen only if there is a massive outcry, and I mean it. It took whole game community to convince them that their freezing mechanis affecting moving vehicle is bad idea.

Problem 2: Imbalance
Not gonna go very in detail into this one, but let me tell you. Back in the days when both faction had symmetrical design, game was not balanced, but it was a very small, hard to fix difference. And then the assymetry came, the bane of this game. Ever since it was introduced wars has not been decided by which faction works harder or plays smarter, but instead by developers tweaking stats on equipment and it's placement in tech tree. As of now devs try to achieve balance trough powercreep, adding more and more powerful vehicles, which renders like 90% of avaible equipment unviable. Developers calim that in the end it will all make sens once everything they have planned is introduced, but with that logick, why play game when it's not ready?

Problem 3: Diminishing importance of player.
Now this might be complicated to understand, but let's try to break it down. Game developers said that in Foxhole you are not "The Soldier" but rather "a soldier". That on it's own is not a bad design philosophy, you know we cannot all be heroes. But ever since I started playing updates kept decreasing importance of what I am doing in the game. I am mainly logistics player and 4 years ago when I bought the game I could supply fragment of frontline with help of 4 more people. Nowadays, I'd need a squad of 20 people to achieve maby quarter of that. And if you played any multiplayer game, you probably know how hard it is to get 20 people to work together. All major updates have been nails in the coffins of different playstyles in this game. Industry of war added cool toys we asked for and killed logi with its absurd mechanics. Arms Race added more cool vehicles and killed logick and balance with assymetry and powercreep. Trench warfer added cool looking fortresses and made builders not matter, or require to play 8 hours a day to matter a liite, funny enough in the end your two weeks of hard work can be rolled in matter of 2 hours by "Balanced" equipment (that is same for bot Wardens and Colonials). And finally, Winter Army killed infantry gameplay with assymetricial small arms and absurd weather mechanis. Next on hook according to devs is naval aspect of game.

Problem 4: Nightcapping
Picture situation: You just finished 8 hour long operation, doesn't matter weather you pushed a region or build up a maginote line. You look proudly on your work knowing how much effort you put into it. You go to sleep and wake up next day, just to see that all of it is gone. You check the map logs and realise what took you 8 hours to do was undone in 30 min. Nightcap is essentially when one side makes huge gains with little to no resistance, because opposite side is in different timezone. This effectivly punishes you for playing in active hours and rewards playing in low population. This problem has been in the game ever since I bought it, but over years it's magnitude increased. I am not saying "Forbid people from playing when there is no resistance" but at least introduce some kind of mechanics that mitigates imbalance, so people playing in high population hours don't feel like their time was wasted.

Bonus problem: Developers dishonesty and war manipulation
To not make this reviev longer with unnecesarly drama, just imagine Game's Community Manager knowing that one of the game's administrators abused his mod powers to gain advantage in game, and when players discovered that he was defending him.
About whole war manipulation, in game developers controll three major aspects:
-Tech tree layout, which decides what side has to pick between and at what point of war (not symmetrical between factions)
-Tech drop, which decides how fast you can gain resources allowing teching, effectivly slowing down or speeding up the procces for one side.
-Queues and respwan timers, which means that devs can flip a swith and suddenly you are outnumbered 2:1 in map, but reinforcments cannot come because developers are introducing artificial queue. Same goes for respawn timers, they can give one side 10 second respawn and the other 80 second respawn to turn tide of battle in favour of one side (happens to both sides)

All of that begs the question: "Why would you even care about war if in the end it is game developers who decided its outcome"

tl;dr and summary:
Deffinityl not reccommending game at its current state, it is not worth your time nor money and you buying game just to leave it after 20 hours are allowing developers to continuesly make the game worse.\t\t\t\t\t
