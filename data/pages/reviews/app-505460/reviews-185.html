\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start by saying this game is unique and there truly isn't another like it. You definitely can get many hours of enjoyment out of it, but it is plagued many issues that aren't immediately apparent to a new player.

The idea of this game has the possibility to become incredibly popular, but I don't think the developers are competent enough to lead the way for this emerging genre of persistent warfare. The game is incredibly janky, and engine limitations mean you will have to wait for hours in queues to get into the most active fronts. The map is divided into hexes, and each hex has a maximum capacity of around 200 players. This means there is a hard cap on the amount of players that can be playing and the closer you get to that cap the more laggy and glitchy the game becomes. You will be plagued with frequent disconnects, rubber banding, and desyncs. The engine and servers simply cannot handle large amounts of players which really puts a damper on the idea of "massive persistent warfare." The devs current solution to this problem is to open multiple shards when there is a large influx of players, but much of the community considers the main shard to be the "true war" which leaves you wondering why you're investing so much time on an alternate shard when the outcome doesn't matter. It also goes against the whole point of the game which is to be one massive persistent war.

There are also many issues with certain mechanics in the game. I'll start with building as it is an integral part of the game, if no one is building and fortifying bases you're faction will get steamrolled. Basically building gameplay boils down to collecting materials and holding down LMB. Infact it is often recommend to turn on click lock while building because it takes hours to build a simple tier 2 bunker base, and once you want to scale up to a concrete base it starts feeling like a second job. The amount of time needed is insane. Obviously the time requirement can be lessened with multiple people working on a base, but currently there is a shortage of people willing to build because the gameplay is atrocious. There are also many advanced building techniques that are not well documented, so it's very easy for new builders to build a base that will most likely die immediately to the enemy. Then there is the issue of supplying your base once it's built. Because of engine limitations, the devs have implemented a decay system to limit the amount of structures in a hex. The only way to prevent a base from decaying is to supply it with a certain item that is produced at factories. Because everybody needs this item for their base, the queues at the factory are often maxed out and it could take hours or even days to get enough of this item to supply the base for a week. If you want your base to operate the entire war this will be your life, collect materials for garrison supplies, add your order to the factory, supply base, repeat. This process causes burnout very quick which is why you'll often see abandoned decaying bases in the mid and back lines.

Next up is Logistics. You'll often hear people say that Logi wins wars and that's cause it's true. If no one is supplying the front line you'll find yourself having to fight the enemy with pistols and no more respawns. While logi gameplay can be very rewarding, it is very repetitive. The logistics chain boils down to farm resources, refine materials, produce supplies, deliver to frontline. While you can do all of these it is generally considered inefficient and time consuming so you'll generally see people only farming and refining, or producing and delivering. The people delivering to the frontlines are always going to be the ones getting commends, but it's important to remember that none of it happens with out the other parts of the chain. Farming resources is much like building, just hold down LMB and watch some YouTube. Some might enjoy this, but for me it turns into a repetitive grind really fast. There's also other often overlooked logi tasks such as, refueling fuel containers, preventing decay of backline base, and refueling salvage mines. There is also the new facility gameplay, but I can't really give an opinion on them as I don't use them, but generally they seem to suffer from the same decay problem that bunker bases do.

Now for combat. Generally I think combat is fun, but there are some issues. The top down perspective of the game is charming, but it can cause issues with aiming at enemies that are behind cover. You can find yourself in a situation where you can't hit an enemy in a trench, but they can hit you in your trench. This is definitely subjective, but I believe grenade spam is an issue as well it's too easy for one player to effectively zone out a massive area with gas grenades. Moving across rocky terrain is incredibly janky because it's hard to get a sense of if you can jump over certain rocks or not. These are just some minor issues, there's also balance issues as well but I won't get into them here.

For the last part of my review I'd like to talk about alts. I truly believe this is one of the worst things in the game currently as it allows one person to completely destroy a factions stockpiles. Basically a player will buy a second copy of the game and joining the opposing faction to sabotage their stockpiles. All the alt has to do is grab a truck full of supplies, or a tank and drive it into water to destroy it. There is very little recourse players can do to fight against it, and the devs have been choosing to ignore it even though it's against the code of conduct. Its been a growing problem over the years and only seems to be getting worse.

Overall I can't recommend the game in it's current state. Even though it is unique there are just too many problems currently to make it rewarding enough for the time invested.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boy. Where to start...
This game has insane potential and the vast majority of it is being wasted.
Playing in a large environment with hundreds of other players where everything is player-made is incredibile. It can be very fulfilling and very frustrating at times, but definitely worth the time.
Combat is engaging even if the learning curve is steep, but once mastered a few concept one can easily feel like actively contributing to a front. Every faction has his own unique load out with some option more viable than others: someone likes the asymmetry, others complain that sometimes it's too much.
Artillery is amazing. Even just loading shells while under counter artillery fire is lit.
Tanks are very fun. Again some can dislike the asymmetry (with tanks is sometimes more evident for obvious reasons)but playing in a tank line while the infantry is pushing is really something.
Boats are basically non existent (just 2 models, but there are rumours of some future naval update) and not very viable.
Building...oh man. Not only it requires basically a degree in foxhole physics and engineering and a long amount of time, but upkeep cost are out of scale. During long wars is impossible to maintain big bases if you have not a consistent flow of material and people to help out. More than anything you have to come up with anti-griefing solution cause the game doesn't provide anything in that regards.
Why then a negative review? Speaking with more experienced players than me (literally thousands of hours) I realised that the game is affected by the same bugs/glitch/exploit since 50 wars ago. Yeah a lot of critical issue were solved, but a series of long standing bugs is still there.
The servers of this game are . No other way to put it. 9/10 times on high pop frontline hex get lags spikes and random dc on both faction. How the hell an MMO is supposed to work without proper servers?
Late game lack some serious firepower.Sure nukes are in game, but spotting rules are just ridicolous (and largely exploitable turn out) and Super Heavy Tank are a joke, more of a resource dump than an effective weapon.
Alting and griefing has seen an exponential rise in war 100, on both factions. There was no addressing the issue by the Devs. Imaging working hard to have enough resources to build whatever you want to build and then seeing a no rank driving your tank in the river. And then reading in local that he has been doing the same for the past days, being banned a couple of days each time.
Last but not least there's a severe lack of communication by the Devs. Don't know why, but i don't think you can maintain a healthy MMO if you don't communicate with your playerbase. Hell, it looks like there isn't even active modding on the server (atm only ABLE server is up). They only communicate when major update comes out, and even then they just address the new content and not others issue that the community brings up.
TL;DR game has potential but is not followed by its own devs it seems. Play it at your own risk because, very unfortunately, I don't think is going to last long. Hope to be very wrong.\t\t\t\t\t
