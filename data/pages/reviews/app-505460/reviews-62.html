Unfortunately, toxic players seem to be everywhere, and it's a daily struggle to protect your resources from would-be thieves.

Whenever my friends and I attempted to build our own facilities, gather resources, and contribute to the region by constructing infrastructures for everyone, we encountered this kind of behavior. And if they aren't stealing from you, they might resort to griefing your base.

You might wonder if there's a way to report these disruptive players. The truth is, there is a reporting system in place, but it only works if you can gather enough reports. This means that if a large group decides that you're an inconvenience, perhaps because your base is in a location they desire (a true story I've experienced), they could falsely report you, resulting in an automatic ban.

It's quite unfortunate because the game itself is enjoyable and has the potential for even more fun. However, the situation has worsened as the developers seem unwilling to address these issues. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tWant to set up a base with friends? Sounds fun right? Good luck, for a couple of reasons.

1. Everything you make can and probably will get stolen. Nothing is protected. You can lock your vehicles, but people will break into them and steal them. You can squad lock your facilities, but that doesn't even seem to work. If you're in a squad with friends, as I am, you're going to constantly have to deal with over-selective or under-selective rights. You can't lock a structure your squad-mate built, only they can, and it may not even work. You can't unlock a vehicle that your squad-mate locked personally, unless you break into it like a common thief, a process that takes shockingly little time. I spent hours grinding out base supplies just two nights ago, and when I get on the next day, we're missing most of our supplies, which took hours to grind, and I catch someone leaving with hours more and spend half an hour in a stand-off with the guy (go find a better way to live your life, [DOE] Beetle) as he tries to gaslight me into thinking the base actually belongs to his friend. People will even steal stuff that's easier to make than it is to steal, like our liquid container. And if you finally make something nice, after days of hard work, like a Taurine Rigger, it'll get yoinked the first night you sign off, with NO WAY to find out where it went or who stole it. Was it partisans? Friendlies? The same person who robbed us yesterday or someone new?

2. If you don't play for a few days, the whole thing will deteriorate into nothingness. We have to move 8 truckloads of scrap every single real life day if we want our mid-sized (6 factory buildings, 2 power plants, a resource transfer station, and a small assembly station) base to endure. That's a chore that will take probably 2 hours, every single day,depending on how available the local scrap yards and mines are, just to keep what we've already spent hours building.

This game is undoubtedly great for some things, but if you want a casual game to play with friends, to build a base and support the war effort, then find something else. I want to love this game so much, but when you can have no true security (I'd even be happy with just an unbreakable 5-day squad lock that can only be used within friendly territory on vehicles built by squad members. We need some sort of protection that can't be broken in less than a minute by partisans, trolls, or thieves) and when you need to grind hours just to maintain what little you've built. The decay is too aggressive for small groups to be able to do anything effectively. It needs to be halved, or quartered, or more, and the supply consumption modifier needs to not be so aggressive.\t\t\t\t\t
