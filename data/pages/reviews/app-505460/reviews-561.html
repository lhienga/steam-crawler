\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played for very long, but I do say the game is fun. In this review, I will leave all the pros and cons I have found, and I will accumulate them as I play on and on, and whether this is a good review or bad review will be determined by the pros outweighing the cons and vice versa.

Pros:

1. Long-Term Games. In other games, games don't last long, no more than 15 minutes or so. But in Foxhole, a simply revolutionary game, each round lasts as long as a month! So you don't think short-term in this game, you have to think long-term.

2. Players Do Everything. When a player fires a bullet, two people allowed it to happen. The first would be the person that fired the gun (obviously), and the second is the logistical person who made the bullet. Although stuff like this is in quite a few games, it goes really well with a massively multiplayer constant war game, where you have to establish and defend supply lines, or else partisans will destroy the supplies, which leads us nicely into the next pro.

3. Multiple Frontiers. In Foxhole, there are so many things you can do, from running supply lines, to building defenses, to fighting on the front, to being a partisan, to sniping, and so many other things. While there is a healthy variety of things to do in other games like C.O.D and Battlefield, that doesn't compare to Foxhole, which has a VERY, VERY healthy amount of things to do.

4. Co-operation and Community. Before I specify, advice: JOIN A SQUAD. THE GAME IS SO MUCH BETTER ONCE YOU DO THAT. In the Foxhole Discord, there is a clan/squad recruitment channel. There you can find clans for basically every language and timezone (if its a big squad, timezones don't matter much). While most servers are all around for objectives, and you can sign up to do whatever you want, not all squads do that. Avoid joining squads that only have one objective, like the M.P Squads that just root out partisans. However, back to the thing, once I joined my division, the 1st Royal Lancers, I had an absolute blast in Foxhole, and had so much fun co-ordinating supply runs and attacks with people in the voice channel. Now, sadly, we move to the cons.

Cons

1. Stalemates. While this is the only con I have found so far, stalemates can happen very easily, and usually suck most of the fun in the game, which turned my friend away from playing it, after trying twice, and both times being trapped in stalemate. From my experience, it seems that stalemates almost always occur at bridge crossings, with Wardens on one side, Colonials on the other side, and no-mans land in the middle. That's why I reccomend staying away from river battles, because most of them go on for days. Anyways, that concludes my cons, now let's move on to Military Tips and Fun Tips.

Fun Tips

1. Join a squad. While I already said this, you should immediately join a squad and co-ordinate on Discord with your squad, it makes the game so much better.

2. Avoid spawning at Stalemates. This especially applies for new players, as if their first experience is a stalemate, they'll probably be turned away from the game, while they might actually enjoy it. But people in general should avoid spawning at stalemates, if you want to have a good time in Foxhole.

Military Tips

1. Use Special Stuff to Break Stalemates. While I haven't seen much strategies be used, this is a strategy I've theorized. By special stuff, I mean gas grenades, artillery, and tanks. The vast majority of the time, people rarely use gas masks, if they're even in supply in the first place. This makes the soldiers extremely susceptible to gas grenades. Although gas grenades might be more useful at clearing out trenches, as from what I've seen, gas masks are used more often in stalemates. With artillery, you can use it to break enemy trenches, foxholes, and basically any defensive structure or vehicle. Grenades also work, but are much less efficient, and probably won't reach across the no-man's land. With tanks, they can easily just destroy the entire opposition, or at least weaken it significantly, which would allow a rush to break the stalemate.

2. What Medical Stuff Should you Have? You should always get Bandages, and sometimes you might want to get trauma kits and blood plasma. When you're bleeding, applying Bandages will stop you from bleeding out, and needing to be revived. Trauma Kits are used to revive critically wounded soldiers, and they consume blood plasma. Consider blood plasma ammo to trauma kits, and bandages ammo for med-kits. Med-kits aren't useful, unless you want to be a full medic.

3. When to advance in Trench Warfare? In trench warfare, which, most of the time, isn't a stalemate, you want to wait out the enemy. In Foxhole, when you're in a trench, you're basically immune, because the enemy can't get you unless they are extremely close. The same applies for bunkers, although sometimes a good marksman could shoot you through there. Anyways, when a soldier emerges from the trench, which usually only has 2-5 people in it, you kill that person, and once it's been weakened, charge with a few other people, if they're willing to come (if you're with your squad, this shouldn't be a problem).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt would be unfair of me to not recommend this game. Although it has many flawed design choices, it is still an experience like none other, for better or for worse. The game sets itself up to fulfill a task that very few games tried to do, and the developers are exhaustively dedicated to improving it, and receiving what equates to pocket change when you compare to the budget of AAA games. I've seen companies and small studios give up sooner to considerably smaller projects. This is a passionate piece of work, and one that has not yet been tainted by corporate greed.

I would be incorrect if I tried to say this is an MMO, as the population is not massive by any means. A consistent few thousand people, but these people are fighting in the same world map for the same war, spread in multiple regions. Every gun, bullet and vehicle is manufactured and mantained by the combined efforts of hundreds of players. And everything must be accounted for and taken to the frontlines with logistical line of supplies.

The wars are won by the most resilient, but also the most clever, as your team must develop a strategy to bring down the enemy defenses and conquer the map. You will not accomplish this alone, so you should be well aware that this is not a game meant for the solo player. You will be frustrated and disappointed many times if you try to dive into this by yourself. It is possible, but you will miss most of what the game has to offer, as many of the mid to late game tactics and tools are only available to groups that can sustain their HQ of operations and logistic flow.

The game can be very demanding and time consuming, as you'll have to likely log in every single day even if for a little bit. But if you do it as a group you should be fine. It doesn't try to be realistic in the visual and tactile sense, but this is perhaps the closest a game will ever get to simulate a war. If the concept of "persistent warfare" vaguely entices your imagination, you should definitely give it a try. You'll either hate it and spend 10 hours in it, or you'll love it and hit 2000 hours without noticing.\t\t\t\t\t
