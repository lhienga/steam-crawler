\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole feels like it has a pretty good mixture of 'realism' and fun. It is definitely arcadey, but the added bits of realism, things like mortars working best when in tandem with a spotter as opposed to doing both yourself. I had started paying attention to this game when it first entered EA and only recently picked it up.

Am currently really enjoying playing this, it helps that there are several friendly players, some of which even take their time out to start ops with the goal of helping new players. Where they will run them through small operations like raiding small enemy bases or teaching them how to be part of a naval assault. Those kind of people really help this game, being just nice people who want to help others get good and have fun. As long as the community has members like that and the developers keep adding in fresh content and balance, this is the kind of game I can see myself consistently returning to for years.\t\t\t\t\t

Original (and somewhat accurate) review:

Foxhole is an undeniably unique game. It's a mixture of Euro Truck Simulator, Day of Defeat, EVE Online, Grand Strategy and Base-Building, drenched in the feeling of overwhelming teamwork and scale, and then brought to life by the Unreal engine.

The premise is simple: Two sides will engage in a persistent simulation of a WW2-style conflict. The catch to just picking up a gun and charging into the fray is that *every single bullet fired, tank destroyed, grenade thrown, and structure built was built by a player*. Every person on the front lines across a massive, connected span has to be resupplied by other players, and they have to gather materials, refine products, and assemble deliveries so YOU can keep fighting this war. Should you choose to join them, you'l be thrust into the beating heart of your team's logistics centers, where passing minute another player in a truck unloads their cargo of resources, and every passing minute more guns and ammo are created, ready for yet more players to load them into crates and transport them to a hungry encampment on the front line. Everywhere you look, co-ordination and teamwork are top priority. Players co-ordinating an attack on enemy oil infrastructure. Players working together to harvest a massive scrap field. Players working in harmony to man tanks, artillery, and logistics.

I could go on, but you get the point.

I reccomend this game.
