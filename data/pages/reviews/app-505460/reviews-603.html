\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI logged on for the first time and there was someone there waiting as I spawn in. "Oh, hello, private, are you new to the game?" I was scrambling to figure out how to talk back "you can press 't' to talk". Oh, but I don't have a mic. No problem. He is super helpful and friendly. He runs me through the training course. "It's not really that great or intuitive of a training area unless you have someone to show you" he tells me. He shows me how to hop over obstacles, sprint, equip wrenches and guns and such. How to shoot off a few rounds, throw a few grenades and then he tells me "and this is logi", we harvest a few resources, build a few.... "Wait!" I interject to him "this is a lot to take in, maybe i might need more time to learn everything" He laughs and agrees.

He wants to take me to the front so he shows me how to deploy to the capital of the game, blemish, and we build a supply truck, go to the factory and build some supplies and drive them up to the abandoned ward and drop them off at a base. He leads me to the front. "Huh, it's unusually quiet" he says, as we are sprinting north. "it's usually-" suddenly the quiet is shattered and my mentor, this random person who has invested nearly an hour or more into training me at this point, is shot right before my eyes and falls to the ground dead. OH MY GOD. The terror. I run into a nearby house and hide, pointing my gun at the door. Nobody comes. But the gunfire gets closer and closer. Multiple guns. I peak outside and see 4 or 5 of the enemy to my right so I sprint to my left only to realize that I am completely turned around and I have no idea which way is north or south or where the base we had just come from was even located. I was majorly disoriented and alone. I see that I am surrounded now by enemies and run into another nearby house only to see an enemy crouched in the corner pointing his rifle right at me. I frantically fire off a couple rounds but it is all in vain. I am shot and killed. I am nothing but cannon fodder.

Over the following days I dig in with the boys in the trenches. four hour shifts only to gain meters that are lost by the next time I log back in. supplies are always low. There's never enough ammo, never enough guns or other important materials. I decide to give logi a try. I start with a hammer and a supply truck and end up running an iron ship with 5 resource crates and a harvester. I become a logi machine, churning out hundreds of soldier supplies a day, and running ships up the coast to important cities, waking up in the morning and seeing that my efforts seem to have made some difference. Still, the enemy fights back and gains a tech advantage. They get tanks and capture over one night entire regions we have spent the last week painstakingly capturing and defending. It takes another week or so until we finally are able to push back enough where victory seems imminent.

Then I wake up in the morning and the game is just.... over.... No cutscenes... no explanation of the war, no debriefing. Just a message letting me know it's all going to happen again. No closure. I am just cannon fodder.

There are no achievements. There are no big bonuses or rpg character creation elements. there are no hero storylines or rambo archetypes. My biggest takeaway from the game is that it embodies the feeling of trench warfare quite well. Gaining or holding ground (especially on a bridge) feels like trying to claw your way up a rock wall. investing a couple hours mining, refining and then building supplies only to lose the truck load of finished materials to "partisan" enemies who kill you and steal your truck right near the front lines, really feels like a punch in the gut. And the game ending after weeks of emotional investment without any closure whatsoever except the acknowledgement that it will all be happening again leaves me empty with the feeling that it was all in vain and yet it is all so much fun and I'm ready to try again. There is a lot to improve but it is still early access and I'm glad I picked this title up. Foxhole is a good cannon fodder simulator. Prepare to die. A lot. And to have fun, too.

It's all in vain and at the same time it's a whole lot of fun. Oh and I hope you like collectivism.\t\t\t\t\t
