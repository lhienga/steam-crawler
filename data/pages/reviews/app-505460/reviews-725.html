\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATED 3/8/23

I can't say I agree with every decision the devs made, but this game is so much fun. I've always wanted a war game with a little more "open-endedness" in the sense of how you can contribute to winning. This game is just that. A massively-multiplayer sandbox war game. Besides the towns and settlements, almost everything is player-made from player-gathered resources. It's one of those games. And it's great. While it is far from perfect, this has easily been my favorite multiplayer game I've played in the last 5 years. Worth every penny.

Look out for the streamers, and honestly the devs are kinda blowing it currently. That being said, still met some of the best people in this game since I started playing and would still recommend to everyone.

*********************

Update - 3/8/23: This game has changed so much since I first started playing, and I don't play it anymore really mainly just due to life. But despite the saltiness that comes with the blind faction loyalism, the blood-from-stone feedback loop the devs have largely operated on over the years, the Foxhole reddit exclusion zone of high schoolers too angry and horny 90% of the time to have civil discourse combined with a very noticiable lack of moderation skill by the dev team--AND--quite frankly, just some really stupid decisions they've made, I still will always recommend everyone try/buy Foxhole for the single most unique gaming experience I've encountered on a multiplayer scale

To Siege Camp, AKA CLAPFOOT: Look, I like your game, but I strongly disagree with many aspects of how you built the game, and I don't think you guys have always made good decisions regarding it's development. Just because you have made smart decisions for Siege Camp, doesn't mean that translates to smart decisions for Foxhole (and sometimes have a direct inverse relationship.)

All that being said- Thank you Siege Camp. For providing a vehicle in which I had a meaningful 108 days of time spent in your creation and all the great experiences and people I had the privilege of encountering throughout it all.

As much as I hate the way you guys operate, I'm sure if you ever release Anvil, I will be the guy who sighs before unzipping their pants upon booting it up.

P.S.
Fix the texture gaps on your 7-year-old maps, Matt, like c'mon man. If those were your kids, they'd be in 2nd grade by now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has big design flaws and it seems the devs are not going to fix them. Notice that the game has been in development since 2016 and still, the big pillars of the game, like Logistics and combat for example, are broken and not entertaining at all.

The devs are changing some systems competely (like the tech system) around every 2 big updates but without learning from past iterations. For me it looks like the devs don't really have a plan how this game is supposed to look like in the end and just make decisions on the fly without thinking about what consequences it might have and if it actually benefits the overall experience.

It doesn't help that the devs have no sense of proper balancing. Changes to existing things like weapons and vehicles happen sparsely but are often so extreme that it either becomes overpowered or nerfed to the point of being completely useless (the shotgun for example).

The reason for this seems to be that the devs are playtesting very little, are not playing their game at all and rarely listen to feedback from the community. The result is a broken and bug riddled game that is 10 times more frustrating than entertaining.

But while these points are already bad enough, the worst thing this game has to offer is the community. It's the most toxic, salty and generally unfriendliest community i've ever witnessed. If you're going to play this game get used to insults, being called racial slurs and voice chat spammers so loud that you have to fear for your eardrums. Don't even try to use the modmailing system because you will most likely not hear from them for a while, if at all. Furthermore this game is riddled with people using exploits, hacks and alt accounts to sabotage the enemy team and almost none of them are getting banned.

My conclusion is: If you don't mind holding down one button 90% of your time; don't mind having your progress you made in several hours destroyed by one of the million bugs in this game or by a griefer; if you don't mind being insulted constantly by enemies AND teammates alike; don't mind not hitting the enemy even though your accuracy is at 100% because he literally dodges your bullets like an anime character and then instantly hits you because he become good at exploiting the accuracy system;
If all that doesn't matter to you, then go on and get this game on a sale (it goes on sale every 6 months), but if you do mind then do your wallet and especially your psyche a favor and avoid this game.\t\t\t\t\t
