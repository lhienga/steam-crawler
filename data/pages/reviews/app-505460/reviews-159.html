Bigger vehicles get blocked by smaller ones. Like a bicycle can stop a tank or a tank cannot push a jeep/truck. Battle tanks can push things so it makes no sense this does not apply to other vehicles.


ALTs are pretty terrible. I have witnessed them multiple times in my first week scouting out defenses for future coli pushes. Torching down bases in the backlines. Taking tank gunner seats sitting doing nothing. Dumping items out of factories. Driving vehicles into water like partisans. And the list goes on... Its been terrible and I know when a major coli push is going to happen by the sheer amount of alts running around prior. Its a problem on both sides and it needs fixed! Its very easy to tell an alt apart. They typically have no rank or very low. Then they run around doing things like experienced players would do. When otherwise new players stand around confused asking for help. The sea of new negative reviews about ALTs ruining the game is very justified. I watched multiple clans just drop their shovels and walk away from game after being griefed by alts a dozen or so times.

AI also just turn off on the front line during low pop hours. Because not enough people (10) set a spawn zone in a town hall or bunker hosting the AI. Doesnt matter how well you build something when the AI just turns off because low population across the entire map. I spent hours bringing supplies and building up to an area to stop infantry and tanks. Only for it to all be destroyed by a couple dudes with mammoth grenades; since ALL the ai was off from low pop. Wow what a game mechanic... And Im not the only one this broken feature has ruined the game for. I personally know a dozen people who quit playing after all their work gets sidelined/destroyed during low population hours. They wake up the next day thinking their effort meant nothing spending a full day building defenses only for it to be lost from not enough people online in that area to defend. Which shouldn't that be the point of AI? To defend when no one is around even with all its other shortcomings! The warden side loses all its progress in the early mornings. That took days or hours against real players to capture. Im sure the coli players have a similar experience in areas. Seeing all your hours of work just zapped away not from players earning that area. But practically giving it away to whom ever walked in. Is about the biggest punch in the face this game gives people. And this is a prime reason people quit playing.


Remaining review

The voting system to allow building in towns is just trash. Its a pain to get people to vote just so a town can "unlock" things like standard defenses or the ability to repair seaports. meanwhile a small crew of partisans can come over and satchel anything in a very fortified area within a couple minutes easily. Crippling that town for multiple real days awaiting people to vote! Its trash and unlocking town assets should be a far faster timer overall. Dependent on how long that town was held prior and other towns around it. Because right now only voting seem to make any quick difference and good luck getting votes without clans doing it.


There is no way to prevent people from stealing your vehicles. There is only a 45 min squad lock that half way works. While some vehicles let people still steal them with a squad lock and others just lock the inventory. It is so painful spending hours either building vehicles or stealing them from the other faction. Just for some fresh spawn with a wrench to grab it walking by because you parked it in your base. This is by far one of the biggest issues in foxhole. Squad lock needs to last "at least" 24 hours and completely lock anyone from tampering with it. While also showing who locked the vehicle to report any grievers.


Coli advantage of putting out more vehicles for less cost as their manufacturing faction bonus. this is fine I dont dislike it. But the wardens are suppose to have harder hitting vehicles with more armor. Where as on the battle field the only thing that matters is numbers. The wardens with supposed better tanks for higher material costs is not something I could see myself fighting coli tanks. we typically have two to three tanks while the colis have five up to nine. I would be fine with this as balance. Except the warden tanks take about the same amount of hits and repairs as the coli counterparts. Wardens also have less or no additional slots in their tanks for spare bmats. In practice and what players typically bring for the front lines I dont see much difference between the factions in tanks. If anything the coli have far more useful options with either range or bigger guns for less cost. Wardens rarely have any tanks in public storage even in late game. Meanwhile reddit shows us posts of 100s of tanks in coli public use. Its pretty insane of a difference when the warden tanks hardly have an advantage against such numbers. In general I rather all tanks just be a little better against other tanks in specific roles as a counter. When you hit another tank there is no meat behind it. It takes multiple tanks hitting a single tank to make progress. Or the tank simply backtracks and repairs coming back in a couple mins. This lasts for hours... Both sides have an all or nothing balance in this area. In conclusion, warden tanks are line tanks. They need players to form tank lines to be effective. Coli tanks are generally well rounded to either be alone or in a line with the bonuses of cheaper mass production and spare slots for bmats. Which makes defeating their 5,10,20 tank groups very difficult. Its not untypical to see one or three coli tanks just drive up to a town hall or bunker destroying quickly. They can take enough hits from most anti tank to destroy these buildings before dying.

At first we all thought "oh cool weather it must be like this across the map" and as others spawn around realizing its not snowing anywhere on the coli side in the north regions. We think ahh that is strange. Then it happens again, again, and again... Then there is wind changes. Devs like to change the wind when certain coli targets in an important fight are damaged. I personally seen wind rotate 360 degrees on a flag within 1 hour in the fight for Iris from Reef. Long term players/spotters never seen wind change so rapidly.

Yeah makes sense"...... "The developers cause things in the game to turn the tide for a win on the enemy side?! What?" Example 1: In war 100: we captured Ferimor (A key subregion) and maybe an hour after we captured it, the game started to snow (which is a weather effect and can cause negative aspects like frozen vehicles and freezing to death). "Yay!" I thought, thinking I can finally have some time to build up a base until the snow subsides, but one issue. The snow was only in the city of Ferimor, and snowing on the side of The Wardens. The snow was NOT ontop of the Colonials which were right outside of Ferimor, causing us to become weakened, and after a few days of battke, we lost Ferimor. Then, a couple MINUTES AFTER Ferimor was recaptured by the Colonials... The snow all of a sudden just stopped.

Example 2:Two weeks after Ferimor was lost, a surge of brand new Russian players entered the game, and due to their lack of experience, inadvertently caused the mass loss of ground for the Warden cause. TO STATE: There is no problem what-so-ever with new players, but the fact that little of them spoke English or even had Voice Chat enabled, made it incredibly hard to teach the do's and dont's. So, we were pushed back to "The Linn of Mercy" region, which consistent battle for the important Logistics town "Ulster Falls" took place, with Colonials and Wardens alike switching ownership. After Wardens Ulster for quite some time, we were able to unlock the Seaport (unlimited supply of storage) and just a few hours after this building was unlocked, it started to snow just like in Ferimor. The Colonials pushed through and captured Ulster Falls, and again... JUST LIKE THAT... The snow stopped.

Example 3: During the defense of Ferimor, a ""Server reset" was established, just as we began to heavily fortify Ferimor. It worked as 1.You get disconnected with a message that your spot in the server will be saved | 2.DC happens and you must wait an unallocated amount of time for the "Reconnect to server" button to be highlighted | 3. You must wait five REAL minutes after reconnecting in order to play again. The Hexes are all independent servers, and just the Region which Ferimor is located at had this "issue" and servers have a max population, so when half of the Wardens (and I also assume Colonials) got disconnected, people from other Regions walked in, and the people whom got forcefully disconnected were unable to hop back in due to mass queue times.

Example 4: The developers update weaponry overtime, and give both sides different variations with different accuracy, damage, and others. The 94.5MM Anti-Tank gun was added to the game. The Wardens received a 94.5MM emplaced, non-movable anti-tank weapon, whereas the Colonials received the same exact caliber, but in a push gun variant which can also hide in plain daylight in the snow. Artillery is also a thing, and the most powerful movable artillery is the 150MM artillery guns which have a set max range. The wardens max range: 300M, the Colonials max range 350M, meaning there is no possible way of counter artillery.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat idea of a game, that suffers from lack of competition. Fantastical semi-oper world with persistent warfare, without any individuality for a player - everyone is just a cog in a war machine, but implementation varies from a system to a system - from good to absolutely braindeadly bad, community can be both great and bad, but I haven't got any problems, and it's really fun and engaging in organized group of nearly any size for most of the activities, it runs well on nearly any hardware.
But developers are probably the least communicative I've ever seen for a videogame, and that is on top of existing problems: most major are alts and griefers, that ruin enjoyment for everyone without any permanent repercussions, but we also have occasional stability issues, that led to extremely quick population loss after release of 1.0 version, holes in the maps, outright bad working mechanics, like getting out of rough terrain, balancing issues. Also, feedback is generally ignored, even when they introduce questionable and huge design decisions.
Overall, it's not a bad game, but right now (update 51) it's extremely hard to recommend due to long-existing problems, that developers do not solve nor even address.\t\t\t\t\t
