\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really excited about the latest update, but it quickly waned. It made a lot of cool additions, but made the game's worst problem even worse. That being solo play being extremely unfun. The tools for naturally occuring cooperation are simply inadequate, and the game requires it to do anything fun or impactful (with few exceptions).

Because of this, clans absolutely dominate, by using external tools that should be in the game anyway. If you actually try to do something as a solo player other than die on the front line, you're actively sabotaging your team, since a clan can always do it better. So expect to be yelled at, voted out, team killed for trying to play the game solo. Or, if you're lucky, spend copious amounts of time trying to make a difference, then watch your efforts get destroyed far faster than the time you spent to accomplish them.

Now, as to how it was made worse? One of the ways you could have fun before was be a generalist on the front line. Go to the front with nothing, find 3 guns, 20 ammo and a medkit to boot on dead bodies. Change your role depending on what you find, and help out your team accordingly. Well, now with the new uniforms in place, you can't. You need a specific uniform to be a medic for instance, so those 20 bandages will just rot on the battlefield. Who's making the uniforms? Well, the clans for the most part, since solo logi is also a pain in the ass. So again, you depend on them to have fun.

tl;dr: go in only if you want to join a clan, devs refuse to let you have fun otherwise\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has a few ways to play, basically boiling down to logistics or frontline. Depending on how you choose to play, it's either really boring or a gruelling charnel house of boredom. Seriously, this game is incredibly boring. Even when playing with a friendly regiment you are stuck listening to a garbled litany of acronyms and shortforms that you quickly get tired of asking about.

I've played steep learning curves before, but with Foxhole I can't see it being worth the effort. Once you figure the game out, you are still just a statistically meaningless cog in a massive machine. It's interesting, but for the amount of menial work and drudgery involved, you should at least be getting minimum wage. If you want to simulate hitting barrels with a hammer or running back and forth in front of a bridge for 2 hours, go for it. But there are better ways to waste your life in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWARNING : Addictive game this review is during early access.

This is a quite Fun game, first time I tried it I played for 5 hours straight, during the night. Paying for it next day :P

The mechanics nice, aiming takes a bit of getting used to it, and stalemates in the trenches are very frequent, it feels like WWI with WWII weapons though so it can be very deadly and long.

It comes down to attrition I think the side that can put enough pressure to the other side to deplete the Supplies will get to advance and take objectives.

Which means coordination is very important in all levels from the front line to the supply lines to industiral production behind the combat lines.

Overall a positive fun gameplay!

Areas for Improvement:

Starting Area

The Tutorial Basic Training and Logistics Training requires some revisions. It was chaotic in some areas people were mixing equipment spawn boxes, dispersing vehicles and thus affecting the effectiveness of the tutorial for everyone else.

I would prefer if there is some more guidance for the player with Tips and Messages popping up on the screen eventually maybe even a voice over, of the Training Sergeants, and Logistics Officers. Vehicles should despawn when not used for a set time (5-10 minutes) by any player and respawn in the approrpiate training areas.

The Bandages, Medikits, etc, are not explained well. It is not clear how to use them to heal one self or heal others in the field. So I never successfully used any of it once deployed.

War Area

Once Deployed, I mainly fought in the front lines. I also built front line structures that I could with the hammer and also repair some structures, I wanted to act as a medic too but never figured out how that works as specified above. There were people doing the manufacturing and distribution of supplies and I wanted to try that out too after 2-3 hours of intense fighting, needed some respit.

But it is not clear and easy to find where all this happens. I went in to what seemed to be a town behind the lines but was completelly lost as to what I can do there.

Industry

Industry related sites need to be better identified on the maps, and maybe the whole logistics process streamlined, I think it is too granular in some areas and maybe too complex in others.

Mind you maybe it is the way the tutorial is done at this time which makes it seem complex too, maybe with a better tutorial it would be clearer to players.

Combat Visibility

The aiming takes some time to get used to, But after a while one understands a bit how it works more or less. LOS (line of Sight) plays a very important role. Albeit I find myself trying to change aiming elevation quite often. It does not. The rules are LOS or nothing.

I would prefer if the target stickiness is a bit more persistent also as enemies move too fast at times (we all move and try to avoid enemies) but also I think going invisible happens too easy and fast too at times.

Still something to tweak and gauge about.

Verdict

A definite winner here, very fun game indeed!\t\t\t\t\t
