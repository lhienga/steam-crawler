People often talk about the toxic player base.
Honestly I just play politely and in a friendly manner. I don't tend to have any issues with other players even the ♥♥♥♥♥. Usually they calm down if you don't mirror their toxicity. Honestly toxicity doesn't accurately describe most of my encounters with these types. It is passion. People get wildly passionate about this game which itself should tell you something about the kind of game foxhole is.

As to devs not doing their jobs. I don't agree with this gripe at all. The devs are constantly creating content and changing the game. I find it refreshing. There is a certain amount of burnout with this game because you can get very invested in any particular war. I take a war or two off when this happens and love returning to often a different game from when I last played. The developers are very open with the directions they are taking the game and are constantly communicating with the community.

If you are a new player take a moment to go on youtube and watch some new player tips it is worth your time. Oh and kill the partisans who cut the logistics line to the front you are on it is always more important then fighting on the front.

To the high hour negative reviewers I say there must have been something fun about the game or you wouldn't have invested the time you did. Just remember it's not your war, you are a cog in the machine. Take a break come back and if you still feel that way rinse and repeat until you no longer feel the need to play.

Will you run into overly emotional twits? Sure, it's the internet ignore them act politely and carry on trying to do something positive for the team. There is no other game like Foxhole and that unique experience is what keeps me coming back.\t\t\t\t\t

Not to mention with the stupid placing of logistics centers logi has to drive around 20 minutes just to deliver these goods to the frontline.

Don't get me even started on the efficiency system, the fact that it is still in the game is a testiment to how the devs love to ignore the playerbase. It turns the wars into an unfun grind where you have to grind for a specific resource just to make your bases defensible. It prevents the losing team from making a comeback but stops the winning team from being able to finish the job quickly, prolonging wars long past when they should have ended.

Listen up newbies! This is for you. The last problem I care to outline here is that devs have done NOTHING to maintain the playerbase above the 300 player average that there is when there isn't a sale or update. The game might have 600+ players now but it will drop soon, and with such a large map (in the current war, but honestly all the previous wars had too big of a map for 300 people) you will have large, boring, empty spaces to "fight" in. And logi will sure enjoy driving through 3 empty maps to get to the front line.
