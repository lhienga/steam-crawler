\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's unlike any team game I have ever played. Most team games are just a bunch of individuals working together for a simple objective(capture the flag, domination, team death match, etc.). My first day playing I just happened to see some people gathering materials and decided to help them. Next thing I know we are building bases, coordinating what materials we need, running supplies to fronts that need it, planning/guessing how the enemy will move through our area, catching partisans that are trying to disrupt supply lines, stealing enemy supplies, and having a blast just sitting around and talking. No other game brings this kind of purpose to play. It keeps bringing me back.

Front line combat is very difficult. Push an inch, lose a mile, gain a mile, lose an inch. It is still early game so it is supposed to be a stalemate to allow everyone to farm and build tech. I got to hold a bridge and fight back an overwhelming force. I drove a tank on the front lines to try to push and succeeded only to lose the ground an hour later. That was day 2. Day 3 we are building a massive base in anticipation of an approaching enemy. Theorizing where the enemy would or could come from. We were so busy setting up trenches, bunkers, infrastructure, tech, supplying the base, expanding, bringing up gun emplacements, and ammo for said guns.

So yeah if you wanna run and gun there is that game play here. Wanna sneak behind enemy lines and setup listening posts to see troop movements or to disrupt supply lines; that is here. Wanna farm materials for hours on end; got you covered. Like building stuff? Well guess what, everything the players use down to the uniforms on their back is player farmed, player built, and player supplied. You can run out of stuff on the front line and have to request supplies be moved up and then have to wait for those supplies to be driven up. Unlike other games though it is easy to learn and easy to master. Which brings me to my last point; the community. I don't know how many times over the last couple of days I have seen people say "Running basic training for new players. If you have questions we will help." I saw one of these training sessions with thirty people listening, learning, and doing. All so that they can be effective in the war effort. So yeah try it out it's cheap, it's fun, it's frustrating, it's relaxing, and it's satisfying. It's honestly what ever you want it to be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game that provides a unique MMO-style experience; an isometric war game with two factions fighting in a persistent campaign where every item except the pistol and the hammer you spawn with are crafted by other players. From a gameplay perspective it works quite well and can be an extremely entertaining and engaging experience. Where Foxhole really misses the mark, so much so that I generally do not recommend it to anyone I socialize with, is in its community.

Foxhole has well earned its reputation for having a toxic community that attracts many of the less savory members of the greater gaming community, and it has this reputation for one simple reason: Lack of community management by the developers. Either because the developers don't see the issue with it, or don't care to deal with it, extremist and highly toxic individuals were allowed to flourish in the game for quite some time. So much so that highly inappropriate conversations could be witnessed in global chat channels with surprising regularity. Eventually the developer decided to take the Reddit approach to community management and offloaded the responsibility on to the player base - Now with an upvote/downvote system and comms rating for global chat channels (but not voice comms or local chats) every player in the game gets to decide who may and may not speak in these channels. Too many downvotes and your communication rating gets downgraded, and your ability to use global text channels removed. As these channels include Intel and Logistics channels, this means your ability to play the game (and assist other players as well) may be impacted. Furthermore, automated conduct report bans were also created allowing players to temporarily ban players from the game outright. As a result the visibility of the more extremist elements were reduced, but only ones which are on the truly extreme end of social acceptability. In truth, what this means is that only the most extreme forms of racism, discrimination, etc. are vilified and handled by the community, while lesser forms of discrimination, general toxicity, and occasionally even some degree of racism are left to flourish. In other words you can be an ass in Foxhole all you like as long as you don't cross the line, or piss off the wrong clans (if a clan decides they don't like you and their members are willing to report people if told, they have the power to ban you from the game with mass reports.)

What this means in practice is that while Foxhole may be a downright wonderful game experience minus some balance issues and some creative decisions that result in some less-than-fun experiences for some, your social experience will be another story entirely. In an MMO-style game social experience is important if you want to do things other than just shoot at the enemy, which is only one small piece of the game. If you choose to keep voice communications on, and you actually make use of the text chat, you WILL with 100% certainty encounter people who will tell you that the way you play the game is wrong; they will tell you aggressively, loudly, and occasionally even with a teamkill thrown in that they are better than you and that if you don't listen to them you are wrong (despite themselves often not being very good at the game.) You will encounter other players who suffer the same event with regularity. As a player of the game since its earliest days I still encounter individuals of this sort often multiple times per session often trying to harass other players over VOIP, and occasionally even myself. However, because there is no community management of VOIP conduct, and little to no visible moderation of the game done via the developers, this means that the only way to avoid it is to not use VOIP at all and hope that ignoring these individuals doesn't result in them attempting to abuse the aforementioned community regulation systems or outright gunning you down. With regards to the communication upvote/downvote system getting banned from global chat channels can happen even to well-known and respected members of the community, and has in fact happened many times since the introduction of these self-managing community systems.

So while from a gameplay standpoint I find the game to be a unique experience that players of other intricate, collaborative games like EVE Online would quite enjoy, I can't in good conscience actually recommend it for the lack of community management. Especially in today's online world, community management is an extremely important part of any game of this sort and is not a responsibility that should be offloaded onto the game's own community. When the social experience plays an important role in the goings-on of this game and one's ability to extract the most out of it, I also can not separate this issue from the rest of the game and simply say "it's a good game but just turn off the chat and VOIP."

There are a lot of people who will tell you that the community management systems aren't actually that big of a deal and the community isn't "that toxic," that the likelihood of you ever being mass reported and banned is quite low and "most of the people saying that actually deserved it and lying about how they got banned." In some cases they'd be right, however they conveniently leave out the part where it absolutely can and does happen, and they leave out the issues that aren't quite as severe. Is everyone in the game a toxic, racist jerk who will teamkill you and tell you want to do? Absolutely not. Are you going to get regularly communication banned or game banned for just trying to play the game normally? Extremely unlikely. Can it happen? Absolutely. The developers also do offer a way to report abuse of these community management systems, but should people really need to file such reports in the first place? Furthermore, the community in my experiences places little trust in abuse reports, as the issue remains ever present and as of War 83 several high-ranking players have had to adjust their approach to global communication to avoid being given poor communications scores for things as harmless as helping coordinate logistical movements, or providing updates for the faction's technology level.

For those reasons I do not recommend this game to others, and will continue to not recommend it until the developers commit to internal handling of community management, or a hybrid approach with far better handling of the existing mechanisms and better handling of abuses of the system.

As a final note, I want to make it very clear that in my entire time playing Foxhole I have never once been banned, received poor communications, or anything even remotely close to either. I am not an aggrieved party salty about being punished for some misdeed. I'm just a long-time player disappointed with the state of the game and its community at present.

- Addendum: The developers also have had, and continue to have, a very lax approach to handling same-team griefing and people using alternate accounts to grief the enemy team. Again, because the responsibility for these issues was offloaded onto the community some vectors for griefing cannot be investigated or handled appropriately, and methods the developers actually do introduce for mitigating these issues are subpar. While this is especially relevant at the moment of writing this addendum (Dec. 2021) it has been a prevalent issue for years just like the toxicity problems.

- March 8, 2023: Developers continue to utilize community-based moderation despite severe issues with griefing and players running alternate accounts to sabotage the other team. While they have, in response to negative reviews, finally added some automatic countermeasures to make griefing harder, they have continued to eschew adding real moderation systems. The moderation systems in place have continued, leading to rampant witchhunting and in-game toxicity, especially toward new players.\t\t\t\t\t
