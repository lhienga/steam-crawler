\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStay well and truly clear if you're mostly a solo or small group player. The game and its devs have long since abandoned making this game fun for those demographics.
The latest update for naval only doubles down on this.

In inferno, once easily accessible vehicle variants and more where all shoveled into the terrible Facility system. Facilities serve the purpose of making what was once a relatively simple logistics process into literal nightmares of tedium and constant upkeep. Only large clans have enough manpower to reasonably run facilities by splitting the maintenance tedium among members and building out large enough facilities to make the higher end vehicles. This has largely resulted in large clans hoarding resources and keeping everything locked to themselves, and really why wouldn't they. There do exist some that do what they can to share access, but that is essentially a bandaid to the hurdles the devs intentionally put between the average player and their ability to access those things.

For the naval update the devs have decided this was all a great idea and have doubled down. All the new toys are still facility only. All the new ships and spgs have exorbitant costs and construction times. Even the basic trailers are facility made only. And to add insult to injury, the new towing mechanic screws over field gun arty with nonsense undeploying and redeploying to change the aim, and the towing isn't even fast enough to be considered useful anyway. Yup, even a basic function like towing was made to be so hopelessly inept at doing something basic like pulling a gun to the front without needing a flatbed, it is instead an entirely useless mechanic.

The average small time player is no longer part of the dev's vision for this game. So save your money and go else where, it simply isn't worth your time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole can seem really daunting at times because you're kind of just dropped into the action with only a little bit of a tutorial which doesn't explain much other than the basic controls. This is the kind of game where you kind of have to figure things out as you go and listen to what other players tell you when it comes to doing certain roles. This game isn't going to be everyone's cup of tea. Logistics can be tedious and combat can be as well.

However, with all that being said, this is honestly one of the best games I've ever played, period. There really isn't any other game that quite captures the vibe that is Foxhole's mix of quiet, back line logistics and deafening, sprawling front line battles with dozens of players. Being there for a major battle at the end of a war where both sides are throwing everything they have against each other are some of the most cinematic experiences I've seen in a game before. Whether you want to dives into the complex depths of logistics or you just want to be a guy with a rifle in a trench, there is something here for you. If it's a game that you think you'd find interesting it's one that I highly recommend looking into.\t\t\t\t\t
