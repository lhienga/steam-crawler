\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a pretty shy person in my life...one reason why MMO style games have always appealed to me.
Social interaction but on my own terms without feelings of judgement.

Foxhole was one of those games that came up on my store list all the time but i felt put off but the camera. And knowing that as a mostly solo player i might feel alone in this war.
The very moment i logged in for the first time i was greeted by veteran players holding a "New player boot camp" in the home sector of the game. They walked a massive group of greenhorns through each station and taught us the very basics and even took us to the front after as a group to experience the war and the cycle of death-rearm-regroup.

Once you are on your own Foxhole is a game of choice...be what you want to be! Start on the front lines and fight...get frustrated and change it up! Become a logistics driver and learn the value of each bullet and bandage you use in the field.

But the creme here is the moment to moment social interaction. I've gone from arguing with people...to being friends with them and playing together all day. Random chats while in the trenches become special moments when the bombs start dropping on you.
Like all games with a social component comes the toxicity of those bad apples. Foxhole is no exception to this...BUT...this is one of the few games where i have seen the majority of the community reject those bad apples. Where someone says something very inappropriate, and everyone standing there isn't okay with it. And reports the behavior.

I play as a front line combat medic and i love every moment...i love the talks i have with the community...i love helping my team.
Join us and get some PTSD of your own!
Amazing unique game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is unlike any other game I've ever played. Admittedly, I was lucky to find a nice regiment full of helpful players, but the online camaraderie that Foxhole provides is unparalleled. That being said, there is a lot to take in and figure out in this game. From harvesting, to refining, to producing items at the factory, front line skirmishes, tank battles, spotting, gunning, etc. There is a lot to understand and do. This is also an MMO, so expect to sink a LOT of time into it--and I do mean A LOT. This is not the "on for an hour" style of gameplay. Prepare to commit hours of your life to the war front.

Finally, their communications audio is garbage. For an alpha game, the gameplay, visuals, etc are actually really good. However their communications audio is GARBAGE. Squad chat is about 3x louder than local, which would be okay if there was even the most basic of functions--- a volume slider for squad/local independent of one another. But most of the time I find myself yelling "what?" to fellow people I'm playing with because someone in squad wants to tell a funny story about 6 wars ago. It's very frustrating. If they fixed their communications audio, I could see me giving this game a solid 8 or 9 out of 10. As it stands though, I'd give it a 6. This game is all about communication, and their comms are garbage.\t\t\t\t\t
