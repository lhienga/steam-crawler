\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWasn't sure about the game, I liked some of the things shown and mentioned but coming from an Arma mindset I believed the isometric perspective might take away the immersion and intensity of fighting a war.
A buddy of mine was into the game and with the game being on sale I figured it would be fine to hop in when I have a couple of weeks off from work.

I jumped in and in just a couple of days I racked up roughly 25 hours of game time! :')
The community is easily amongst the best in any game I've experienced and the teamplay in Foxhole is very refreshing.
With logistics and supporting roles being easily as, if not more, important to the war effort you'll always be able to contribute even if you don't feel like picking up a rifle and start blasting after a hard day at work or something, I'm sure there's folk playing that never played as a rifleman at all and are having a blast.
Personally I'm quite fond of being a combat medic, patching folks up and reminding them to eat their five-a-day and that bullets have the right of way.

For new players, don't be afraid to admit you're new and ask. It's better to actively ask for help rather than bumbling about like a right . 99% of the veterans are kind and helpful if a bit tired of the large influx of new players from the sales but you'll almost always have someone able and willing to show you the ropes.
On my first day I was wandering around looking where to be useful when I got invited to hop in a tank and shown how tank crews work, got a crash course in being a Medic, and damn well held the line as a rifleman. On my second day I was hauling much needed supplies to the front, then switched to providing medical support and even joined a small patrol to root out infiltrators behind our lines, we need to keep the supplies coming after all. After that I got back to patching folks up at the front, and you bet we held the line.

Remember, Wardens eat kids.\t\t\t\t\t
