\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is pretty fun and addicting. However do note that not being a part of a regiment (clan) will severely limit your options, because you really can't move the frontline by yourself. You can do a lot of things solo (solo logistics, solo medic, solo partisan, solo infantry, etc), but for stuff like driving a tank or shooting artillery you need multiple people. A large part of total player time is being spent doing logistics - gathering and hauling resources, making weapons and vehicles, etc, basically it feels like Euro Truck Simulator. If you have multiple people, you can really improve the efficiency by making each person do one thing, you can make tens and hundreds of tanks for the frontlines, you can run massive frontline operations with those tanks. That isn't to say you can't enjoy the game solo, but it really shines the most if you are cooperating with others.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do love this game, as apparent by my hour count, but the 1.0 update was a breaking point for my enjoyment with the game, as the grind/effort balance required for fun within the game has continued to shift heavily towards the grind/effort side of the scale.

The past few updates have been an ever increasing strain on the logistics and builder portions of the player base, a slow and steady power creep mixed with growing disparity of balancing issues across both factions (some balance issues can take months or years to be addressed if they get addressed at all) add in mechanic abuse and half baked features, it's begun to slowly but surely catch up with the game.

Add to the fact that many QOL features are still sorely missing, poor moderation, a horribly neglected tutorial, and a bug list (some region/war altering) with some bugs dating back years at this point being left untouched with updates. Not to mention the dev team that doesn't have much interest in communicating with their player base until its to late or never at all is frustrating.

As a result you have a foundation of toxicity, burn out and outright frustration growing within the player base, from green to grizzled vet alike. A quick check at the properly level headed and thought out conversations of the official reddit, discord or steam forums will and can show you this easily. (pointless angry fluff and good memes to sift through thou)

I hope that I can one day change this review to a positive one, and begin recommending to people again. As I believe this game is unique and one of a kind, with a good sense of the grandiose scale of combined arms warfare while stressing good peer communication to win, and offering an unlimited canvas for memories of long past war stories to tell others, along with being apart of mostly fantastic and dedicated community.

But for now, as for the reasons mentioned above, I cannot tell friends or potential new players to invest their time or money within the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wonderful concept and extremely deep mechanics.

However, the game feels very unwelcoming toward a newer player, with multiple warnings of how your actions effect other players. This leads to a newbie feeling shame, as well as being overwhelmed by the complex mechanics. I was reported during my first hour of gametime after I tried my hand at the logistics side of the game. While I was only warned, I feel that a new player should be able to freely experiment with game mechanics without being harassed by more experienced tryhards that feel an intense urge to "play hardcore".

The game's biggest strength is it's biggest weakness, the large amount of player to player interaction, and the intense competition, creates a hostile environment for the newcomer or casual. I really wish I could enjoy this game, but i'll be moving away from it until the community is more receptive.\t\t\t\t\t
