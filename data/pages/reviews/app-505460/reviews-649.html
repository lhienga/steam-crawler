\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd describe this game as a complex isometric crossover between Company of Heroes and Brothers In Arms. 80% is planning and 20% is intense action. An extremely in depth war game where having your supply lines in order will be a determining factor in your faction's success just as much as plunging yourself into combat will be. After all, how will you defeat your enemies when there's no one to bring you guns and ammo? Fuel? Vehicles? Tools? Sometimes the best course of action will not be to fight, but to take cover and wait for your enemy to become impatient and make the first mistake. Where you might initially have the upper hand, but will lose an engagement simply because your squad's morale breaks first. Beyond all this, the community is very welcoming to new players, and if you're willing to be taught the ropes, there's always someone willing to teach you.

A word of caution, this is not a casual game by any means, so expect to put in several hours every session because there's always something to be done, always resources to be gathered or moved, there's always battlefields to be reinforced, and bases to be defended. This game is still being worked on and only improves with every update. Though I don't have the time to play as often as I'd like, I highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

Community is pretty good since the game doesn't cater to show-offs that only care about their K/D ratio. I think I only experianced one time running into a couple snobs who were reporting people for little reason, but the vast majority of the encounters were fun or funny. In my first 10 hours I experianced a pretty authentic take on WWII style warfare. Trying to prep a base for onslaught and getting shelled by artillery fire, holding bridges. Pushing with the tank lines. Pushing across rivers on foot to sneak around and flank. Going from trench to trench. Psuhing through cities. Grenading tanks and yeah, holding up in fox holes.

I have not taken the time yet to learn logistics and will be shortly. But supply and demand is a pretty heavy thing in the game and does deserve more note. I think the game might benefit from air support. But I could see that getting complicated. Maybe make it a rare thing. Hard to produce.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game, you can easily get quite invested in whatever role you get into.

Logistics seems to be the most rewarding. Collecting scrap for the refinery, making resources, using that to make supplies / weapons / ammo, delivering to the front lines by truck / boat. This will get you commendations fast!

However doing general combat or being a medic saving / healing people for hours on the front line you'll be lucky to get a single commendation, as people have to go out their way to click commend on you.

Would be nice if the developers make improvements;
- To general controls and make it feel less 'clunky'.
- Improve the shooting mechanics / line of sight camera controls.
- Improve server performance queuing into regions (takes 20+ minutes at peak times to get into regions, then suddenly 'Connection to server timed out').
- Please do more to encourage / stop the players who don't seem to want to play as a team in a team orientated war (locking vehicles / stealing vehicles, team killing etc).
- Make a more user friendly tutorial / introduction for new players rather than dump them on a island with signs to read.\t\t\t\t\t
