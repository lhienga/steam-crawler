\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first got this game because it seem to have a lot of gameplay aspects that I liked, and that were pretty unique. I liked the gritty feeling to it, the unique perspective and system of shooting, and the idea that is was a constant, live world war with players all around the world. Plus, the game had pretty good specs and ran mostly fine. The graphics are really good and I like the art style.

For those not interested in reading my entire thesis, here are the bullet points for why I wouldn't recommend this games:
  • Foxhole is highly social. You will not get the most out of it without a mic and discord server with friends or a 'regiment'. Most vehicles require multiple people cooperating and communicating in order to use them. This isn't why I'm not recommending the game, I'm just putting this here to warn others like myself who thought they can play this solo/casually.
  • Total lack of QoL features. Doing even the simplest things in the game requires an excessive micromanagement, mouse clicking and keyboard pressing.
  • Time is wasted liberally in this game. Logistics is a constant, tedious grind, all so OTHER players can have fun. Lack of QoL features also wastes time. If you have a job or an actual life, then forget about it, because playing this game is basically a second job.
  • The game makes you feel guilty for playing it. Since every weapon, magazine, tank etc is made and delivered to bases by players, wasting these materials is bad. Knowing how awful logistics is, you basically avoid using anything more advanced than a rifle in fear of losing that weapon and thus wasting another player's time.
  • Occasionally the playerbase is toxic and takes the game far more seriously than they should.
  • The game has a research system. When a war starts, 90% of the equipment in the game will be flat out unavailable anyway, regardless of whether you have the materials to produce them. You have to rely on logistics players to deliver aluminum to engineering facilities to advance the research for your faction. This takes IRL days, so you're stuck with your basic rifle anyway.
Below I'll expand on this points in more detail.

As I said, the game is highly social. If you don't have a mic and were thinking of playing Foxhole casually/solo, say after work at the end of the day, then this isn't a game for you at all. This is a hardcore game and will take hours of your times just to do very little in the grand scheme of things, and to get the most out of it, a mic is the minimum requirement. I'd also recommend joining a regiment. Most of the game will simply not be workable otherwise.

That was a bit of a bummer, since I don't have a mic and don't intend on getting one, but what really killed my joy for the game is the total lack of QoL features, and the fact that the developers seem focused on expanding the boring aspects of game as much as possible while minimising the fun parts.

In any other game, when you spawn in, you automatically have the weapons and kit you need. In this game, you have to spawn in a rear base, travel for five minutes (usually on foot) to a forward base (closer to actual combat), manually set your respawn so that in the VERY likely scenario that you die in combat, you won't respawn again in the rear base. Then you have to manually go into the base's inventory/stockpile and equip a rifle and some magazines. You almost always equip just basic stuff, because people die so often in this game that if you want to use anything more complex, you have to be 100% sure you can use it effectively, otherwise when you die that weapon is likely lost. That's IF you equip anything, btw, because people die so often that it's often encouraged to just navigate your way to the active warzone and loot the gun and the appropriate magazines from the bodies littering the ground (while dodging bullets). I'm not going to lie, that part is actually kind of fun, but sometimes you just want to get into the game and shoot stuff without all that time wasting time scavenging off bodies.

You COULD grab the SMGs/RPGs/Tanks/etc if you want, but unless you're very experienced, you're probably just going to waste it by dying immediately. Thus, the game makes you feel guilty for doing basically anything fun or unique in the game, because all the equipment/resources are extract/refined/manufactured/delivered by players. That's ignoring the fact that often groups of players will claim ownership over resources/equipment, so that you can't use them even if they're publicly available. If you try to, you'll be team killed.

Plus, the inventory and equipping system is janky. Say you have already set your spawn in a forward base and you've just been killed. When you respawn you have nothing but a pistol, so you have to manually access the base's inventory, grab a rifle and magazines (which isn't instant btw, it takes "assembly time"). Then you have to close the base's stockpile, open your inventory, transfer the rifle from your backpack to your hands, close your inventory, press 1 to equip the rifle, then press R to load it. You have to do this every single time you die (which is a lot). I just wish a rifle and 1 magazine/clip were part of the "soldier supplies" to begin with, and that when I transfer a weapon to my hands from my backpack, the game automatically equips it instead of forcing me to press 1 every time. If you swap a rifle for an SMG, the game automatically unequips you, instead of just keeping you holding whatever is in your main weapon slot. It's janky, unintuitive, tedious, time-wasting and unnecessary.

Another thing, because of the social and hardcore aspect of the game, it tends to attract sweats, tryhards and buzzkills. They take the game far more seriously than it deserves, and so they ruin it for everyone else trying to have fun.

Another thing that I briefly touched on before is that this game will waste hours of your time. Grinding for logistics, delivering supplies to the front, equipping basic weapons to go fight, just to die and wait some more to respawn. This is how logistics goes: once you have made or requisitioned a truck, fuelled it, driven to a scrap yard, bashed the scrap, filled your truck with scrap, drove to a refinery, refined the scrap into usable materials, delivered the materials to a factory, manufactured guns/ammo/whatever, then congratulations! You can now spend the next 30 minutes driving back to the forward base, loading several servers in the process along the way (since the map is so huge that each region is a separate server, so to travel between regions requires loading time), just to deliver a dozen rifles and a handful of bandages that they needed an hour ago, and which someone else has probably done already. That's if there aren't enemy "partisans" (i.e. enemy players in friendly territory) that don't kill you during your journey, thus undoing about an hour of two of progress.

This game is TEDIOUS. Torturously boring. Logistics is so convoluted and dull, that half the game is unplayable except if you're playing a podcast or youtube video in the background, while playing truck driving simulator to the front. It sounds cool and unique on paper, but actually playing it is frighteningly dull. All for what? So you can support OTHER people's fun? To deliver a tiny amount of supplies that probably won't make a difference, since every war lasts about a month?

The game feels more like a social experiment than a game. As a result, I can't recommend this game unless you're looking specifically for a game that is a chore from start to finish, in which you can waste hours ans hours of your life contributing minimally to anything in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNeat game, but the time investment is way too much for someone playing by themselves. You need a group of about 5 players regularly playing to really enjoy much of the game. Building and gathering is extremely time intensive and all of the work that can go into a base of 50 hours building and mantaining can go up in flames very quickly if you don't log in regularly.

So if you bypass base building and upgrading vehicles you got the combat, which is decent. The combat is a bit simple but due to the sheer amount of equipment from field guns, tanks, and small arms equipment smg, rifles, grenades, and flamethrowers etc you get alot of tactical options. With all those tactical options though, you again need a group to work with, that or rely on the many many random people out doing their own things.

So you may log into fight a bit and have a very boring stalemate of people running around willy nilly dying one by one with no aim, or you log in seizing ground inch by inch through well coordinated pushes, or better yet blitz the land with a group of people that synergize well and use the gear for awesome combined arms. There is alot going on in this game thanks to all the equipment being player made, but this also hampers the game because you just can't do much without a large group or extremely dedicated small group.

I can't recommend a game like this just due to it being too unreliable in delivering a fun experience, I don't want to my fun to be completely dependent on many random people and can't force my friends to play a game. Still cool game when all the cogs are running.

P.S sorry to all those I convinced trying this game out\t\t\t\t\t
