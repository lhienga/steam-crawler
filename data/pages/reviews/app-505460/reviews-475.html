Join in and enjoy your fancy new thousand-yard stare of your own due to (but not limited by):

1. Having to experience getting shelled non-stop for literal days (yet refusing to give up that front and miraculously succeeding);
2. Getting your buttcheeks saved by a medic then watching him getting obliterated moments later with an enemy surprise machinegunner;
3. Somehow managing to survive one of these occassional terrifying human wave HE rush in the middle of the night. Good, have a breather, because in a minute or two even more of them going to come over.

Suddenly someone yells GRENADE! as you see it landing right next to your buddy. Dammit, he's gone. Medic is lying wounded in the dirt, slowly bleeding-out and a second later you get stabbed in the back with a bayonet.
5. Spending too much time on the bridge battle always hoping that today is the day that we'll push through. Problem is, the enemy thinks the same.
6. Doing logistics work and you're about to deliver the so much needed supplies, ammo, building maters and weapons to the bunker base on the frontline. Just couple of minutes more. Suddenly, enemy partisan squad ambushes you.

And that's not even the gist of it.
Join up now, start playing, rage quit after couple hundred of hours, come back a week and a half later, start enjoying it again. Rinse and repeat.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWar 83:

What a war it has been... From the very beginning, the endless bridge battles. As a Warden I can think of the following organic stories that have come from this war.

- The 7k TMAT incident.
- The "day of doom" when colonials teched ISG and we began a general retreat.
- The "day of doom" when colonials teched artillery and rained down shells for 24 hours straight.
- That time an Asian clan tapped Macha's and gave us hope that we might actually take Jade Cove.
- The storm cannon race, and battle that kicked off the end of the bloody stalemate.
- The conquest and re-conquest of Feirmor.
- PORT TABASCO!
- The Defence of Isawa and the emergence of SPUD as a powerhouse in their own right.
- The hundreds of thousands who died trying to get into Blemish.
- Finally cracking Fort Compensation and taking Scurvyshire
- That dark weekend when the colonials counter attacked, and we lost a lot of what we had taken.
- The parties, music and dancing while waiting for Fort (I can't do the ASCII art) to tech up.
- Finally, after so many days of death, we breach the walls and are inside Blemish. The victory parties were incredible.
- Taking Origin and Asstown.
- Nuking Asstown, Sitaria and Mercies all in a the span of a few days
- All the cheeky backline taps, and of course, credit to the Colonial Rangers for pulling off a capture on The King. Not our finest moment.
- The Defense of Evil Eye, 34 seconds to doom
- VICTORY!

So much Drama, so many stories. This was such an epic war. I will not forget it.

----------------------------------

Now for the bad:

- Hackers/exploiters are present and not always dealt with in a satisfying way.
- Alt accounts are a big concern, although I believe the problem isn't as big as people claim.
- The very nature of persistent war fare essentially pits different timezone populations against each other... Flavors of Rust here...
- Building feels like an absolute chore

This game shows some amazing promise. It is one of the few war games I've seen with a ground up focus on logistics. The MMO RTS style of it is unique, and dares to try something that would be shot down as impossible or impractical by many. What exactly do you call this genre? Hivemind real-time strategy? The camradery that is possible is incredible. There are a lot of things that should be improved, and should be made more fun an interesting, but being a Early Access game it shows amazing promise and incredible ambition in it's scope.\t\t\t\t\t
