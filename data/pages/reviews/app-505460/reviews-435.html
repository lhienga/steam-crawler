\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan, I really wanted to enjoy this game. I really did. But at this point, I can almost say I'd rather be working at McDonalds or something. At least I'd get paid for that.

TL;DR: Game has terrible mechanics. Doing logistics is the most boring crap there is. Imagine you're going office work, but without getting paid. It's like you spend two hours mining stuff on Minecraft, but then you don't get to use what you mined, in most cases. Frontline combat is not on a good state either; infantry combat is hot garbage, sometimes frustrating when the handling of your aim or your character gives you the middle finger, and the recent introduction of cheaply mass produced tripod guns (such as heavy machineguns and anti-tank weapons) has made life hell to those who would dare pick up a rifle, or even an armored vehicle, and go out do something for king and country.

Oh, and the community does have some bratty, ill-mannered dudes who really sour the experiences you can have with genuinely good people. Also people alting to grief on the enemy thing is a thing. And you'd have more luck talking your concerns to a wall than to the developer team. Seriously, get something better to do with your time.

======

Alright... Well, where to begin? I firstly had a blast with this game. I read the reviews about it, and to be honest, the stories of experiences with toxic players and people playing on alt accounts on the enemy side were daunting, but I still got attracted to the idea of playing a game were team effort was a major theme. I first played on Shard 2 (when that was a thing), and had a generally good time there. I would play on my own, and eventually drift towards playing logi. It was BORING as hell, but hey, I did it for the team, so that's what counts. I guess.

I think a good reason why I had such a good time in the game was because Shard 2 players were generally much nicer and cooperative. Eventually, the devs stopped supporting wars on Shard 2, and every Shard 2 player (who didn't quit the game) moved to Shard 1. It wasn't as good. Had a few encounters with asshats who would steal our ♥♥♥♥ and then proceed to do a little trolling teamkilling some of us when confronted. Not to say people just stealing containers while I was loading ships at ports. Like, what the hell, man? Why?

Now, with 0.47 dropping (at the time of writing), this war, War 85, has got to be what got me to quit. By this point, I was in a clan of Shard 2 bois. They were, still are, fun people to play with. Shortly after the war started, we built a bunker base by a crossroads. Later on, we find signs outside our base placed by the dudes next door. They want our base gone because we had "taken their spot" - even though we were in the region days before they came. From that on, there has been some back and forth with them, threatening us to blow up our base and what not. I don't have all the details, as most of this is coming from our builder guys there. To not make the story too long, we ultimately agreed to surrender the base and let it rot. We told them they can demolish it if they want. And now they're ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ because the base is decaying, and it is out fault we are not supplying it... Like, dude what??? Hello????????

That's the most recent case of BS comming from other players that I have experienced. I have had good experiences with people, but I also had experiences with bitter people who would sooner put a bullet in their skull before muttering "please" and "thank you".

Now, onto the actual game content. It was........ Decent, up until last update. Update 0.46 (the one before the actual one) had its issues. Common bugs, glitches, and worst of all, exploits. Logistic gameplay is a boring, barely rewarding piece of crap that makes retail work look exciting as hell. You just hold left click if you're scrapping, the W key if you're driving, or sit on your arse if you're producing stuff at a factory. The only rewarding thing about all of this, is that whatever frontline you're supplying is going to last a little longer. Not like it matters, because it's common for frontline players to carry more than they need only to die soon after.

If you play Foxhole for the logi, I suggest you go play Minecraft instead. Or eurotruck if you somehow enjoy the driving on this game. More fulfilling, and you don't have to deal with people stealing your stuff.

Infantry combat isn't much better either. It is, at least, more exciting than doing the exact same thing for hours. Aiming in the open is "alright" - you can aim towards an horizontal angle, but you cannot adjust the vertical angle, resulting in situations where you cannot fire at someone, but they can fire at you with no issue. Not only that, but combat inside buildings can get quite frustrating; unless your mouse is directly on top of the model of whoever you want to shot, your aim will likely go to the roof. Literally. It will stick to the ceiling of the structure you're in (if you're trying to aim at something within the structure), meaning you will be firing at the roof and not the dude who's about to blow your face off.

The recent introduction of the new tripod guns has only made matters worse. You see, to produce most heavy materiel (such as hand-held rocket launchers, light machine guns, field guns and armored vehicles), you will need a resource called Refined Materials, or Rmats for short. Rmats are much, much more scarce than the Basic Materials (Bmats), which are used for almost anything. The scarcity of Rmats meant that the heavy-punching stuff was scarce as well, so the battlefield was not littered with them big guns.

The new tripod guns, however, take no Rmats to produce. You can make 5 with just 200 Bmats. Now the battlefield is littered with heavy machineguns that can one-hit you on top of having huge ammo boxes to fire for stupidly long periods of time. The HE/Anti Tank, plus the MGs themselves, are also quite good at eating through armor. So, not only is infantry screwed against this, but the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ tanks are as well.

Add to this that the game is still littered with bugs and exploits. One well known exploit is placing blueprints (a plain blue model of the structure you are going to build) around bases or things you want to protect from artillery. The blueprints will tank the artillery like it's nothing. Another one is slipping artillery field guns within bunkers to protect them - something that shouldn't be possible and is clearly not intended. Some (common) bugs include getting stuck in terrain geometry, cargo ships spinning like a beyblade when crossing regions, connection issues, among others.

And now, you try talk about your concerns to the developer team. They don't care. Or at least it looks like they don't care - they got your money already, so why bother? The issues with Logistics gameplay have been so profound and so old that the players that play Logi have formed a damn union, and have attemped in several occasions to voice their complaints to the developers. And what are doing the devs about this, you may ask? Well... They lowered the resource output of mines... Making logi work an even more tedious task. They half-fixed this, but still, what the F U C K ??

All in all, I think the devs are taking the dreaded path of feature creeping. It looks to me that they are trying to make the game look attractive, while ignoring, completely or partially, the calling issues that the more veteran player base has been pointing out. I know some of you know what feature creeping does to a game's development. You get a game with tons of content, but half of it is a hot mess that sooner or later will drive players off the game. I really hope this is not going to be the case for Foxhole.

As I said, I really wanted to like this game. I love the concept, and I enjoy team games. But this game as it is right now, is something I cannot enjoy. I suggest you try something else meanwhile.\t\t\t\t\t
