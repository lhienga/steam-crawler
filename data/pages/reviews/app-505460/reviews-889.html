\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game was really good, and then the 0.16 update happened.

Before this update, you could as the community says "run and gun" which gave infantry engagements some fluidity and added a skill ceiling that wasn't rock bottom, you had to learn the skills you needed to run and gun and mix it up with the best players in the game.

Now I don't know if it was their original intention or if they've been persuaded by sections of the community but now in 0.16 the infantry meta is: grab a gun, sit in a foxhole/pillbox or behind a sandbag and shoot. If you're brave enough to venture out from these positions you'll press your X (go prone) button as much as you'll press WASD. It's now turgid pitched engagements of attrition that last for hours. Plinking rifle bullets at defensive structures that are spammed all across the map. But elements of the community love this change (arguably those who were poor at run and gun).

Now everyone is the same, there's few movements or tricks to learn to gain a competitive edge. You're the same as the guy next to you, sitting behind defences plinking away at the enemy defences about 30m away. A soul destroying joyless waste of life until someone decides to throw 10+ frag grenades your way. A poor version of it's previous self, if you like shooting games which reward aim and movement, don't buy it.

Edit @ 19/09/2018: I've been trying for a long time to voice this concern, it seems according to the reviews that others share the same view and generally it's those players who could put up with all the other stuff that was bad about the game who believe that 0.16 has ruined it. Judging by the player count statistics ( https://steamcharts.com/app/505460 ) it would seem they've made a series of erroneous development decisions. I don't know why but the number of players playing the game is dropping consistently, more so since the 0.16 patch.

Now I can't say that the drop in player numbers is a direct result of the change to infantry combat in 0.16, but judging by the reviews since it must be a contributing factor.

Edit: 16th Nov 2018

So... 0.17 came out and it appeared as though Clapfoot Studios had listened to people in the playerbase who were complaining about the awful combat experience they decided to implement in 0.16. And it was OK, not as it was but it appeared to be a happy medium between those who can't aim and those who can. What wasn't to like? Well it turns out the combat changes that took place in 0.17 and subsequently carried on into 0.18 were after all - a mistake!!!

As soon as the 0.19 update dropped, the combat in 0.16 returned. People who had returned to the game as they'd heard the combat was fixed (from our point of view) were told that the combat changes in 0.17 and 0.18 were a mistake and that in 0.19 that mistake had been rectified. This combat meta appears to be Clapfoot Studios preferred method, their vision. It seems that they do not want the game to be skill based, no lone wolf action. So basically if you're not in a clan and you're up against a clan - you will lose.

Sadly it's come to the point where my Foxhole clan has decided to quit the game en masse. Pretty much all of us have over 1000 hours in this game, and if you've read this far on this review you'll know I personally have over 2000 hours. I'd say that's enough to give you an accurate review of the infantry combat system. Anyone who tells you they like it must dislike shooters that require the mastery of a skill in order to mix it up with the best players.

It's very sad, this game had bag loads of potential, but I do feel as though it's not just Clapfoot Studios who should shoulder the responsibility for this, unfortunately there are members of the community, some who operate on behalf of Clapfoot in a voluntary capacity who appear to support these changes despite seeing for themselves and being aware more broadly of what these changes are doing to the player base.

As of this update I cannot recommend you spend money on this game, the best I can say is to buy it, try it but for the love of god make sure you do whilst you can still get a refund so you've not lost your money as I have.

Good luck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, back in August of 2018 a wrote an absolutely glowing review of this game. I loved almost everything about it, the logistics was fairly simple and easy to do, the player base was helpful and fun, and it was honestly one of the best war-crime simulators I had ever played. But, it's been two years and another 200 hours worth of gameplay since I made that review, and I hate to say it, but the game is s##t. Let me explain...

Logistics: Logistics has been overhauled, making it much, much more time consuming. Before if you really, reeeaaaaaly wanted something, you got off your lazy A## and did it yourself. Now, you just put markers on the map and beg for someone to bring something other than rifles,smgs, or grenades. Logistics has become such a pain that now I think only clan members do it (And I'll talk more about them clans later, don't you worry). So, they've made the system waaaay more annoying, but whats your reward? commends, worthless commends.

Weapons: They've added a couple of new weapons, snipers, lmgs, Velkas. But it honestly doesn't matter, any weapon that is even semi-decent will instantly be nerfed into oblivion (Rest in peace Big Iron, you where to beautiful for this world). They also make no sense what-so-ever. Seriously, if the revolver is worse than the semi-auto handgun, than why does it cost MORE? Also, (this is %100 true in real life btw) apparently changing your rifle from semi-auto to full-auto decreases its damage.

Vehicles: Imma keep this one short and real with yah chief, just don't. Unless you enjoy spending two hours scrounging together resources to make somethin just to have it be destroyed in .03 seconds, then just steal them like everyone else.

Playerbase: You'll encounter a wide array of people in foxhole, I encountered a lot of my friends playing this game. Truth be told, this is probably the only aspect of the game that I still find appealing. I honestly had more fun trying to befriend colonials than I did killing them. You'll meet quite a few cool people too. Buuuuuuut, there are always THOSE people, the a$$-Hats that plague every military game with a communication feature. The guys who sit there and pretend like they actually enlisted to fight in the war. The guys who spend more time calling their team "trash" than they do playing the game. You'll encounter a lot of these guys in foxhole. It's really sad too, the interaction was the only thing keeping me coming back to this game.

Clans: I know what you're thinkin, "Isn't this technically part of the playerbase"? And you're right, it is. ut these guys are a whole new caliber of cancer. They'll use alt accounts to spy and ruin your four hours of preparing and planning, they'll abuse the vote-kick feature to basically get you insta-banned, and honestly, they take the game too seriously. The problem is that unless you play with a clan, you won't get anything done. You can't assemble a rag-tag team of wackjobs to save oarbreaker from the communists (btw RIP oarbreaker the new version is trash).

Gameplay: Over time, the gameplay has gotten more and more bland. It's either keep throwing resources at the enemy till one side breaks, or shoot AI structures for 5 hours. Certain aspects of the game feel heavily neglected, like the naval and artillery gameplay have had only minor changes other than the addition of the "Storm Cannon" AKA the Clan Cannon. Now, alot of games do this, so why doesn't it work for foxehole? Well, there's no reason to play the game. There is no kill counter, there's no award for "Most Kills" or "Most bmats produced", there's nothing. Your only incentive to play is to interact with the increasingly toxic community. So towards the end of my time playing, I would find myself quitting because there was no point.

Conclusion: Foxhole is a hollow shell of it's former self. It really does make me sad to think that most people won't get to play the same game I did. They won't get to play "Cowboys VS Colonials" or end up in a POW camp after attempting to defend a Collie in his tax-evasion trial. It's kind of ironic, the most fun I had playing this game wasn't crewing a battle tank, or using a storm cannon. It was playing UNO with the enemy from across a bridge, it was becoming king of the mole people. that's the foxhole I played. Honestly, I will happily put up another glowing review if the devs can manage to fix their game. So, after two years and over 250 hours worth of fighting, I think it's time to go home.








But my original statement still stands....

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe concept is great. The execution is not.

Few years ago the devs installed an asymmetric system, wherein both factions have different weapons and structures. The inability of the developers to take into account player feedback as to the balance of the game has turned the whole community into an insane and toxic mess.

When the game is good, it's great. But most of the time, the last patch buffed one faction, and nerfed the other into oblivion. The devs are to blame for this. They keep items stats hidden from players, who have to datamine them to find any useful information and leads to even more arguing and partisan bickering between players of both factions.

I look forward to the day where another company reuses the concept, but actually makes a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
