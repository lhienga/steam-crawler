\r\n\t\t\t\t\t A 24/7 mmo war between two factions competing for weeks to see who wins. There's two methods of gameplay that being logistics(Logi) and frontline combat. The experience of frontline combat can be very varied and intense with many possible tactics, subterfuge, loadouts, armored war machines, toxic gas, AT rifles or launchers, bayonets, grenade launchers, lmg/hmg, mounted weapons, trenches, and artillery. While logi is totally casual and relaxed as a solo where you may harvest materials to process into finished products that can then be delivered to frontlines to maybe gain commends that'll rank up your character.

If you're looking for something more serious and organized you could join a squad or regiment/clan that can coordinate to accomplish ambitious goals like organized tank pushes to take a Bunker Base(BB) and territory, fielding several artillery pieces from which they'll rain death on the enemies positions and possibly destroying the enemies BB. If you like being a seriously sweaty gamer that wants to achieve epic things like building tier 3 concrete super BBs with massive storm cannons, participate in tank pushes made up of dozens of tanks, etc. there's a lot of room to be a super sweat. To join a regiment/clan is akin to joining a locked squad; you need to ask for an invite. Some regiments may be massive and run Discord servers where you may need to apply as well as to stay in the loop of the goings on.

The only indisputably negative aspect of this game is the lacking tutorials that cover only the basic of basics and everything else needs to be learned by yourself, asking others for help(most people are helpful unless they're arrogant super sweats), or looking up and reading the wiki constantly when you come across new aspects or items of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Thank you so much for the experience, it has been a wild ride.

The experience that this game offer is unparallelled. The "persistent war" is core selling point of this game and it is what got me interested in this game. Everything your factions, your friends or the enemy do will persist till the end of a war which can last for at least a week or almost a month, and it is fascinating to see the change in the landscape and tools as the war goes on, new buildings , new technology and new armament being brought to used to win a war. You might form a bond with other players or even faction (clan) as you play and then you might be participate or involved in Foxhole community (Discord, Reddit, Steam's Community etc.) giving out suggesting, looking up new meta, further balancing changes which may even contribute to the future of this game!

However, as you becoming involved and *invest* in this game, you may start to realise something - something very vital. The time that you spend and money in this game is not worth the experience that you get. The mechanic in this game tax on your time quite significantly, i.e. taking enough item out from a storage(base) may around 15 seconds and walking to the front line will be around 15 seconds, producing item for other players to use also take quite some time.
