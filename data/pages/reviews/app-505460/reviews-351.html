\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat is this game?

It is a top-down shooter set in a technological era that seems to be between World Wars one and two. Victory is achieved for one of the two factions when they earn 32 victory points spread out across the many hexes of the map. The technology between the two factions is asymmetric and there are pros and cons for selecting each faction.

What’s good about this game?
Your gameplay experience will vary depending on where you want to devote your time. Will you be a frontline soldier, selling your life repeatedly to gain an inch of ground, or to throw back invaders? Will you spend hours alone in the enemy backline with a wrench, causing as much havoc as you can by destroying enemy vehicles and robbing them of their resources, cutting enemy supply routes and harassing other players in the resource fields as a partisan? Will you devote your time to learning the intricacies of fortification and bunker building to preserve the backline or reinforce newly-gained enemy territory as an engineer? These tasks can all be extremely rewarding and leave you with a sense of accomplishment depending on your individual tastes. There’s something for everyone in this game. Various levels of gameplay are available to help make sure there’s a place for just about every kind of play style you’d want to partake in.

What’s bad about this game?
Ultimately, the cons of this game derive from moderation being abandoned by the developer as they hide behind the excuse of this game being “Community Driven”. Being reported by a certain amount of people, regardless of the truth of the accusation, will result in a ban. Any time you make a comment in the World, Intel, or Logistics chats your comment can be down-voted. The result of continued down-votes from other players is a ban from these faction-wide chats. This problem is further exacerbated by the fact that with the age of this game, veteran players have essentially become a collected group of separate cliques that can muscle their clan-mates into exploiting this mechanic to silence and remove players who don’t like the way you play the game. That private stockpile you’ve built up to run to the front line? Gone since the timer on it will expire before your automatic ban is lifted. Want to coordinate with different fronts to see what vehicles or weapons would help them the most? Well that’s unfortunate, because yesterday you got into an argument with a prominent member of a large regiment and they’ve decided you don’t get to speak anymore.

There is no better example of this than the players who actively police the technology progression system. Using game mechanics to vote for or drum up support for technology in game is vilified if you’re not voting for the technology that’s been agreed upon in a Discord group. This merry band of self-important narcissists (Self-proclaimed “Tech Maids”) routinely abuse these moderation mechanics to silence people who express discontent with their technology hoarding.

If you get this game, avoid confrontations with members of large regiments. Use the materials you earn through your work for your benefit. Do not say anything about any of the materials you've collected, and use the technology materials you earn through mining resources to vote for the technology you want as an individual. Install OBS and record chat history so you can prove you've not violated terms of conduct if you receive a communications ban.

This is coming from someone who's spent their entire time in the Warden faction. If the Colonial experience differs, please share.

**EDIT**
I've tried the Colonial faction and I have found the QM to be a lot less insufferable than the TM.
