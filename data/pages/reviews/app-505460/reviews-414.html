\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This game is incredibly addicting and I actually recommend people try it out, but after having invested 543.8 hours in the past several months I'm burnt out and sick of the blatant faction imbalance. I guess my mistake is caring about whether my faction wins or not.

This is one of those rare games that only come along every few years that I've deeply fallen in love with. I actually recommend playing it, but the reason I'm giving it a thumbs down is that I've become too invested in the game to the point that playing the game is like delving into a toxic relationship. I love the game to death, but the game doesn't love me back lol.

It's the exact same sort of addiction and love I had for World of Warcraft for so many years. MMO's beg you to get invested and fall in love with the game, but the developers nerf your class or your faction into the ground so no matter how much you try or how often you play ALL day it just doesn't matter and you get steamrolled, so you gotta turn your back on the game for a year or so until a new xpac or patch hits that improves your gameplay.

Foxhole is a game that I love to be sweaty about, but I just can't stand the idea that no matter what my faction does we'll just get steamrolled because the opposite faction has way more people and the mechanics that are supposed to balance faction population don't really do anything. It seems like whichever faction is winning is based on trends in the player base, because new players join, old players quit, and some clans will switch sides from time to time. It's entirely possible that the Wardens will eventually win a war again, maybe even the very next war, but right now as I'm playing it's really obvious that I picked the wrong faction if I actually care about winning.

I'm actually having dreams about losing wars in Foxhole and it sucks so at this point I think I need to take a break and go outside or something lol. I got too emotionally invested, but it's because the game is great.
