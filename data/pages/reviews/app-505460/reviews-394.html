\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is conceptually beautiful but practically unplayable. Everything takes forever to do. You're punished by the game's mechanics if you try to work together which is ironic considering it's a game dedicated to the act of collaboration.

It's not often that hauling logistics actually matters in a game but Foxhole makes it entirely unsatisfying. The cranes are slow, trying to collect goods to move itself is a 20 minute process, if you want to haul it you need a specific flatbed truck and good luck finding one, then you drive 20 minutes to the front lines, and then even when you do haul everything up front it's gonna be 20 minutes to get your truck back to haul more things. The driving itself isn't even satisfying since it is very 'arcade' like with no physics or anything to keep you engaged. You literally hold W for 15 minutes.

This might have the single worst aiming system I've ever seen in a game. You don't aim so much as you point in a direction and hope. Half the time the person you're aiming at is in a forest or building and is difficult to even see let alone hit. You also can't aim anywhere near as far as you'd think which can be tedious. It's hard to judge terrain elevations, in fact it's almost impossible, and knowing who you can and can't hit due to this becomes a feat in itself.

Artillery is important to winning battles but the act of doing so is boring. You will either spend 20 minutes loading shells out of a truck into a gun or listening to someone tell you where to fire, if you even have a spotter. Maybe this is boring regardless of what game it happens in so I suppose I could forgive it.

Collecting resources only becomes enjoyable about halfway into a war. Before this it is a miserably slow process. Other than that it isn't too bad but at the end of the day you are playing a skinner box. There's nothing else you can really do to speed it up or make it better. At some point someone will have to sit there for a minute and hold down their left mouse button.

Building is hell and a miserable, thankless task. If you try to collaborate the developers punish you. Only 3 people can build something at a time for no discernible reason. Keeping your bunker supplied means you dedicate probably an hour of your life a day to supplying it or else it will fall into disrepair. The defences for bunkers is laughable and most of the time people will just walk around them or in the case of tanks will casually roll up to the anti-tank pillbox, kill it, and then stroll back to repair. The placement system is really finicky and there's no taking into account the size of a trench you make and how long it will take. A small tiny connector takes just as long as a full length one to build.

The community isn't as bad as some people in the reviews claim. There's definitely a cult like mentality around everything and there's a good mix of children and man children. There's plenty of people who will be more than happy to tell you what to do, which can be a good or bad thing. I think the only issue with the community is a lot of specific operations can be carried out by clans so if they go missing or decide to give up that force multiplier is gone however I think their impact can be somewhat overblown. I've seen random people capture towns while 'organised' groups completely fail. There are times however where you can see the difference and it's mostly in tactics. The few clans I did interact with were okay but I could never join them. There are definitely piles of hoarded goods all over the place that never see use due to private stockpiles however which just comes off like wasted effort.

Time is a very big factor to this game. It takes time to assemble things, it takes time to make things, it takes time to scrap, it takes time to dig. I suppose if you want a massive time sink you could try this game out. My suggestion would be to have a second monitor so you can watch youtube videos since that's what you'll be doing half the time. If you must play this game, do 'backline partisan'. Partisans can undo hours / days of work in about 30 minutes and it's one of the only satisfying parts of the game.

I think this game serves as a strong reminder that real wars are mostly mundane with sporadic action and that's the perfect description for Foxhole: mostly mundane with sporadic action.

Update 2022/10/06: IT GOT WORSE. If you're a masochist you might like it though.\t\t\t\t\t
