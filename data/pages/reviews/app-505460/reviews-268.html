\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game follows a concept similar to other MMO games like V rising, and tries to apply it to a persistent WW2-like grand war.

The community is very good, although a bit awkward. The teamwork you will see happening in this game will leave you with a nice feeling after playing.

The battles are amazing, but here comes the huge flaw which breaks the entire game. The outcome of the war is not decided by these amazing battles. The outcome of the war is decided by a few groups of people driving around and destroying tediously-built bases while the enemy is offline/absent. Then it depends on a few special individuals having the patience to rebuild these bases using "Construction Vehicles" which need to be slowly driven there from another base.

The developers seem to lack direction when it comes to overall game design. This game has so much potential, but it is squandered by not having the innovation needed to diverge from the existing format. A solution that I could think of off the top of my head, would be to only allow some regions to be contested at a time. The key thing to make sure of is that the enemy should be allowed to defend their bases. In WW2, whole fortresses were not destroyed because the enemy was offline. It really breaks immersion, and spoils the fun of the game.

Overall, this is a fun game to try out for a couple of days. The flaws will quickly become apparent and unbearable for a lot of people.\t\t\t\t\t
