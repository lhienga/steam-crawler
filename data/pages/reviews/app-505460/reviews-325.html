\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith the upcoming release of 1.0 I want to share my long experience with the game to encourage potential buyers of this game. A TLDR is I love this game and the people I play it with, it's like no other game I've ever played.

If you happen to purchase the game after my review and have an interest in the logisitcal system feel free to add me on steam or leave a comment on my post! I'm always looking to help teach new players who have the intrigue or interest in the system I spend the majority of my time interacting with!

Hello and welcome to Foxhole! If you're reading this I presume you're interested in the game. I originally found this game last year after the release of the Winter Army update. I've been involved in Foxhole since about War 72, we are currently embroiled in war 95.

My personal selling points for this game includes:
-The length of combats, wars can last days, weeks, or even a whole month giving players various stages of the war to invest themselves in in multiple ways.

-The world environment is mostly player generated. At the start of the war, each faction begins with a set number of territory it controls. With that territory are bases that spawn it with the world, relic bases, town halls, and logistical infrastructure. Those buildings begin the game with a set number of basic rifles and ammo, vehicles, medical supplies and the like. Anything produced or built after the fact, from the player made ports and bases, to the gun in your hand, to the tank you crew, the car you drive, even the shirt on your back is made and maintained by the player base.

-The robust community. Within foxhole the are what is called regiments. Regiments are like clans from other MMORPGs. You get a group of people together set out on a goal, or theater of war and your coordinate with your regiment to operate as advised by whoever leads it. There are also squads. Squads are also player generated but you may be in as many squads as you like. You may come and go without penalty, and organize activites that may be outside of your regiment's scope. Every squad or regiment is unique in function goals, even timezone and language spoken, if you look hard enough I PROMISE you'll find the right regiment for you! (Solo play is also completely viable, although some larger scale projects may take more time, I'd encourage you to make friends with similar interest in the game to interface with at the very least. A friend who loves logi, or a fellow tanker.)

-Wars are always dynamic and it forces you to be strategic as to how you respond to new and rapidly changing environments. The best tactician and those who think outside the box help us with the war!

-Factions are unique! Each faction has their own tools and thereby strategies to help it's side win the war. We have the Wardens, and the Colonials. Each new upgrade or piece of research may give one faction the upper hand over the other and so knowing when to play defensive over offensive is a crucial evaluation to make.

-Noticeable player impacts, big and small. You as a player can have an impact. Not matter if you're an infantryman on the front-lines, the medic to those infantryman, the builder of their fortifications, the player supplying the base, or the scrapper and manufacturer. Any player can have an impact no matter where they decide to place themselves. Impacts include making friends on the front and supporting one another, getting a clutch shipment of repair materials under artillery fire, supplying bases for maitenance, forming a tank line with your friends, and much much more!

My only current gripe with the game is the down times during certain active time zones. There are not currently as many SEA and Oceanic players as EU and NA/SA time zones, and so that leads to variations in the density of game play depending upon where you are.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you wanna play this game solo, it's pretty fun. You just have to understand that people play it with a regiment. Yes those people can seem toxic due to them giving you orders. If you encounter a toxic person or someone has a horrendous mic just mute them and move on and go to another front, could even come back the next day. Toxic people are in every game, they are few and far between. Take a break if one pushes you over the edge, it truly does help.

Is there balancing issues, yes, when the war only has back line refineries logistics becomes a literal nightmare. Are there balancing issues on the front, not really, maybe? It's a mixed warfare game so, it feels very powerful no matter how you play and even powerful if play as a team. FYI, you don't have to be in a regiment to work as a team.

Overall great game, would recommend, definitely before update 1.0; it's a big update!

^Above definitely still stands for 1.0 release. I do think the entire map needs to be redone with the new features in mind. It is like putting small legos and big legos together; sure it can be done but it is just horrendously awful to do right now. The PvP/PvE is still fun though and would recommend for that.\t\t\t\t\t
