\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hate this game, it's amazing.
+ Good community that is cooperative and friendly (for the most part)
+ Gameplay is slow and methodical, requiring planning and coordination on both small and large scale to get things done. This is no run'n'gunner.
+ Persistent world means events that occur on one side of the map can affect the other (one front collapses, meaning another front has a flood of soldiers and pushes through)
+ Logistics is key to play, fronts being pushed due to enemy supply loss instead of sheer force.
+ Light lore helps with immersion and morale - For Callahan!

- New players will be overwhelmed, the community being quite elitist when lower ranks under-perform or mess up.
- Lots of jargon and mechanics to learn if you want to be even competent (I have 120 hours and still barely understand many of the concepts in this game. I still get lost finding regions on the map.)
- A few glitches that annoy and impede you (falling through trenches into the watery void, some trench walls not climbable etc.)
- Updates can sometimes feel one-sided, vehicles not always being asymmetrical.

My advice:
* Join a clan. Lots of people will claim that clan man is bad, but if you find a casual community it will help you learn the game faster, let you partake in organised operations with big, complicated pushes, and make some friends in the meantime.
* Respect ranks. This is not absolute, of course - everyone has a brain, and this isn't a real military. But rank is a soft indicator of experience, and a Lieutenant is going to know more than a Lance Corporal about the game and what to do. If the cards are down, listen to your superiors - or at least the most authoritative voice in the chat.
* Use voice chat. Not only is this fun for roleplay and immersion, being able to talk to others instead of typing it out will make gameplay more dynamic - moving forward while yelling to push, climbing out of a trench yelling 'grenade' etc.

I do enjoy this game, faults and all. Logging on after a break or two always ends up being a blast. I hope it will stay this way for a long time.

Praise Callahan!
- [HoC] Disco\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginal review after a few weeks playing: Amazing concept I hope other games catch on to in the future, unfortunately the developers can't keep modders out of the game and it has lethal balancing issues. There are just two factions and the same one wins and the other loses every time. Fun to play for a while but, sorta like a pay to win game (this isn't one, but it is "like" a ptw game), the enjoyment wears out when the futility of it all catches up to you.

Update after a couple months playing: I think the devs are making good progress and seem to be pretty active with the game which, hopefully, is a good thing. They've added more tutorial/explanation type tools lately which should make it much easier for newbies. Foxhole isn't super complex, but if you don't want to be completely lost and worthless to your faction, you are basically at the mercy of asking questions of players around you for your first several hours. This should be less true now than months ago.

Concept is still excellent.
Game play is quite good (minus vehicles getting stuck too easily) but unique so it takes getting used to.
Tech seems balanced but balancing player numbers on each faction and keeping experienced player numbers balanced is probably a lost cause if you want to continue giving players a choice of which side they play on. Which leads to the problem of scumbag people who use alt accounts to sabotage. Devs need to monitor this as closely as possible.

GET RID OF SQUAD LOCKING VEHICLES.
If you're gonna play Foxhole, you need to accept that you're just one guy on a team with several thousand other players. Your actions alone very rarely change the course of a war like you're Rambo or Masterchief but you can make a big impact if you find a role you enjoy. There's something for everybody and you're free to do whatever whenever: logi roles gathering and distributing resources, building, defending, vehicle and infantry combat, becoming or stopping saboteurs, managing technology research, etc\t\t\t\t\t
