\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game. Everything (EVERYTHING) is player made and player driven. Guns need to be built in factories and supplied to bases to be used by soldiers. Tanks need a way to get fuel, artillery needs a path to get munitions, medical supplies need to make it to the front and soldiers need "shirts" to respawn. Failure at any point in the logistics lines can cripple war efforts. Being a partisan/guerilla actually makes sense in this game as attrition is not an abstraction - it's very very real.

In some ways, the combat isn't even the highlight of the game. It's okay, don't get me wrong - but the real moments the game shines is when multiple regiments work closely together to execute a plan properly and the enemy folds beneath it. Super satisfying. The big battles where multiple squads come together to take advantage of cover and terrain in the cities or trenches actually FEELS like a war movie.

What the game NEEDS to do better is a proper series of tutorials (MANDATORY I would say) to help explain the fundamentals of the game. The community does a pretty good job of helping new players through, but education shouldn't be on the community to do. The arcane technology research system needs some clarification and basic logistics should ABSOLUTELY be explained to new players. Sometimes they dont know just how much time and effort goes into making one piece of equipment that they squander and waste by bumrushing the front.

Anyway - get the game, turn on your mic, talk in local chat with people, give it a little time and you'll have a really rewarding experience. Some of the people are cringey mil-sim wannabes but they are generally really cool people.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBridges and spamming AI bunkers straight up ruin this game.

By that I mean that once a bridge gets destroyed, the rebuilding effort required is game breaking. You could have 20 people on one side of a bridge ready to make a push, and despite having all the resources required to rebuild it, as long as there is 1 person with ANY weapon on the other side, they can destroy the bridge the moment that you try and build it.

I have seen dozens of people being held up on one side of a bridge by 2 people and 1 AI bunker on the other for hours and hours. Players just give up in frustration, head off somewhere else and that region of the game loses all players.

This gets repeated in the new area that the players went to until you hit another blown up bridge and the cycle repeats as no one wants to spend the next hour of their free time repeatedly clicking on a bridge to have it blown up a fraction of a second later.

The bunkers also create this kind of impassable wall of defence. To fight human players you need specific strategies and weapons to fight effectively, however fighting the AI (as they sit in bunkers and cannot be harmed in any way by small arms fire despite small arms fire being able to destroy bridge building...) requires building specific explosives.

This constant need for 2 competing sets of equipment means that the whole front grinds to a halt every time your targets swap from humans to AI as everyone stops, camps, and then tries to figure out where to get explosives from.

In the mean time, half the people who were involved in the battle have left because fighting AI is no fun and the whole thing falls apart.

All in all, a great concept, but the balance of AI defences and bridges ruins all hopes of movement in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
