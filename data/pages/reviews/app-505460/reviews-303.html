\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile generally I don't play MMOs, this game hits different.

It's a really fascinating experience. The fact that every weapon, piece of ammo and vehicle is produced somewhere behind the frontlines and then transported using trucks, ships and trains is just mind-blowing for me. Theoretically you can see no enemy soldier throughout the whole war by just focusing on building facilities, infrastructure and bunkers. Also, there are clans, organizations and regiments dedicated to different aspects of the war effort. There are guys who mine stuff, transport stuff, produce stuff... you get the point.

But even when you get to the frontlines the game doesn't lose the feel of complexity. Using tanks, for example, requires a whole crew of people (commander, driver, gunner, engineer etc.), who have to communicate and cooperate with each other to achieve anything. Artillery requires you to use binoculars to spot enemies and set the right azimuth and angle. Even something seemingly simple like being a medic can introduce some interesting new mechanics like bringing critically wounded soldiers to the hospitals to retrieve soldier supplies. And there's a ton of other roles you can play as and every one of them has its quirks.

The community is generally very helpful and nice. Developers continue to add new stuff to the game. Sometimes they aren't communicative enough though (see logistics strike or how broken the report system is).

Nonetheless the game is a great experience and I highly recommend it, especially if you have a group of friends to play with (the more, the better).\t\t\t\t\t
