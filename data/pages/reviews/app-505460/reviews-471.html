\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game where individuals actively work against each other in order to defeat the enemy. In fact, one of the paradoxes of Foxhole is that while every individual thinks they're the best on the faction with the worst luck, the faction is far worse than how good any individual thinks they are. This inescapable skill cap can be explained by how every time you play anti-infantry, your anti tank sucks, and your faction loses, and every time you play logistics, your QRF sucks and your team loses. When your teammates try to point out that you're the common denominator, you could ignore them completely because they don't speak the same language.

Best top-down massive multiplayer online war sim currently available. I have barely any hours in this game, however, I find it to be good enough.
As the two factions are asymmetrical, it fundamentally can not be "balanced"
The community is dedicated, probably because most are addicted.

There is something for everyone to do: Base Planning, Base Construction, Firefights, Frontline Logistics, Armor Combat, Medic, Backline Disruption, Firefighting, Assorted Relevant Roleplay, Resource Collection, Artillery, Manufacturing of Supplies, Quick Response Forces, Base Defense and Repair, Transcontinental Cargo Shipping, and more! Every role can be played sweatily, or casually, it's a sandbox.

Foxhole tries to be too many things and ends up being bad at all of it. Too many aspects deserve to be refined. I don't design nor develop video games so my opinions are largely invalid.

The developers do NOT value your time, and many mechanics feel like they include waiting *just because*. The developers try to introduce some Random Number Generator based things in Foxhole to increase relevancy of teamwork and tactics (instead of pure "how-fast-I-can-snap-my-mouse-to-head-shot"), but just end up making the game feel unfair. Not only this, many underlying mechanics that the player base should be able to understand for a fair playing ground is not explained in any way, and community management and communication is nonexistent except for a stream every third of an year and vague patch notes without solid numerical data.

If this is a concept foreign, this may be Squad but each match is a battle of a war, perhaps Hearts of Iron but you are one single troop.
Every player is equally irrelevant, yet each individual can accomplish something to change a situation, and as a group turn the front, and as a faction win a war.

I would not discourage you to purchase and play Foxhole just because of the glaring shortcomings, but under no circumstances must one play this game seriously. Do not exceed your limit, and if you find yourself checking up on the war even when not playing the game, just stop. Commitment and detachment will ultimately yield no benefit.

Many newer players feel disrespected as saltier and players with more hours (or higher ranks; refer to penultimate paragraph) may find their actions suspicious or similar to that of griefers. As numerous other reviews probably explained and, the community is extremely dedicated and have found mostly unpunished (in terms of developer and moderator action) success in buying and using "alt"ernate accounts on the opposing faction either in methods of obstruction, destruction, or espionage. The fact that these accounts have had no actions of permanence taken against them in part leads to cynicism against all new accounts, and make newer players probing the bounds of the game and the customs of the community perceive discrimination. This is what many reviews of newer accounts reflect and are not exactly representative of the complete experience.

However, if they do not communicate with other people in a massively multiplayer online game, it is not completely the community's fault. Perhaps don't argue with people yet. Newer players should not have the expectation that their world view is superior and neither should older players think all newer players are ignorant. Though too many are.

The rank system is dependent on receiving commends from other players which accumulate and once at a certain threshold, ranks the player up. While player actions can entice other players into increasing the quantity received, it does not precisely reflect the effort players have put into the game nor the experience which they possess. In fact, levels, in-game statistics, and hours played are all not definitive to the actual quality of the player. Many members of regiments commend each other and often have ranks which far exceed the norm, and commend farming by exploiting channels such as stockpile submission pop-ups have also occured, so try not to judge a player by their rank.

Every bit of respect for the developers and their work but with the state of the game, it can not be recommended with good intent and sound thought.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the longest reviews I've typed up - there's a tl;dr at the bottom of the review.

I'm not quite sure how to explain what Foxhole is to someone who hasn't experienced it.
It's an MMORPGTDS;

Massive: The map since Entrenched update is flipping massive - each hexagon you see is a unique server, and each one has unique resources, cities, layouts, best places to fortify, etc. If you are looking for the combat you go towards the front. If you're looking for that chill vibing (I call this "Runescape level grinding") you go to the backlines and run logi. I should also mention here that a "war" can take as long as a month or two even! As of writing this review the main server ("shard 1" or "live 1") is on War 83 which appears that it will be a Warden Victory (nice), but War 84 will essentially "reset" the war, so on so forth. There are websites dedicated to showing the situation in each of the 2 main wars (shard 1 and shard 2). Even if you don't own the game check those out - the sites are free.

Multiplayer Online: Everything you see in-game is player made or player built. There are very few static items in Foxhole. If you're in game and you see a bunker system that was player made. If you see a few outposts or watchtowers those are player built. Every single weapon, ammo, tank, truck, boat, shell, artillery gun, **EVERYTHING** is player made. You can fight at the front using the player made materials to take more land or you can work in the backlines making said materials to be used at the front. Heck you can do nothing but run finished materials toward the front and farm a bunch of thank yous from the boys and girls at the front.

RPG: Okay this one is a bit of a stretch - Depending where you are, you are going to experience a form of roleplay. Whether you're harvesting materials to be sent to the factory to make things or are at the front shooting the enemy - you're going to feel immersed in this world and you're going to feel like the character you play as. Doing logi? What if enemy partisans attack you? You need to have that in your mind. You're going to fly down roads rushing as fast as you can to get those critical supplies to the boys and girls crying for them at the front. You're going to feel that roleplay whether you know it or not.

TDS: Top down shooter: Personally this is a Nostalgia trip back to when PS2 was the most advanced and popular console out there. There was an old WW2 shooter I played on it that was also top-down like Foxhole is. This used to be a problem however since the Entrenched update the camera feels soo much more natural to use than ever before. For those of you coming from a game like ARMA or Battlefield it's gonna feel weird at first, but after some time and practice you'll grow to love this part of the game.

Now for some recommendations;
1. If you intend to stay on 1 side across multiple wars please find a clan from that faction and join it. Many of the big plays and big moves are clan-based. You can find clans from the Official Foxhole discord server or the faction-based servers. You can play solo of course, but I've personally found it more fun when working with a clan
2. Know you're going to die, and know the enemy can loot your body. Don't take so much stuff into combat just to get dropped by a random arty shell and give the enemy all your resources. Remember you can always fall back and resupply or resupply from the enemies you drop.
3. It's okay to flip flop sides from war to war, but don't flip flop in the same war. "Alting" is not okay, and both sides will actively purge members of their factions that do this.
4. Check your microphone settings. Foxhole uses a weird VoIP function for the in-game chat, and even with a podcast-quality boom mic I've had weird issues with this.
5. **Don't be afraid to ask for help** - it's okay to be a noob in Foxhole, just ask those around you for help and 99.99% of the time others will be happy to help. A new player in a faction means that the faction will continue to grow, which is important for that faction as veteran players get burned out and fresh new recruits come in to learn how to play the game.

TL;DR: You should buy this game, hop into a server, figure out what you wanna do, learn how to do it well, and just have fun. When in doubt ask for help, and enjoy your time. You'll learn quickly that Foxhole can consume whole weekends if you're not careful, and it's def. worth the 30 dollar pricetag.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt all started with the "The Current Foxhole Timezone Meta" that I saw one day on my suggested yt videos, to which led me to other videos of Foxhole, to where I am today. Go watch some gameplays of Foxhole and see if it interests you, read this review that addresses some issues and my experience playing it.

Foxhole is an experience, it fulfills that certain niche what other shooter games can't. There's so many things you can do in Foxhole, rather that be fighting at the frontlines, being a tanker, arty crew, logistics, all sorts.

To get this off, about the reviews of people getting mass reported by clans, yes that's a thing. Now this would differ from which side you play, there's The Colonial Legion, then there's The Wardens, I'm a Collie player myself so most of this will be from the view of playing the Colonial side. Now given the time I played this game as a solo, I never had any problems with any of the Collie clans, don't bother them they wont bother you, simple. If there's a problem, just communicate with them and sort it out, no biggie. Foxhole is a team game, so muster up the courage and be social, communications is key. Now the Collie community itself, they are really friendly and willing to help new players, all you gotta to do is just ask in world chat or someone with a high rank(Yes there's a rank system in Foxhole), don't be afraid to ask for help, Collies are willing to help one learn. From what I've hear though, Warden clans seems to be really toxic, just not from biased Collie players, even those who turned over from the Wardens side. And to quote a Major who once was a Warden player, "All they ever talk about is femboys stuff" and like to spam the C word... when you do the sperm thing you know lol. But yeah you do you Warden players.

Now to the mass reporting topic... Only time I've seen this happen is when there's an alt(alternate accounts), because you are faction locked for every new beginning of the war. Alts are the worse, low degenerates that cheat just to help their faction win the war, like common, we're all playing to have fun, not be ♥♥♥♥♥. For the most of I've heard, my fellow Collies don't tolerate this kind of action on our side as well so if you are found to be alting helping Collies, you will be reported from your fellow Collies, hopefully Warden players have the same morals.

But overall, the gameplay of Foxhole is unique, its like Squad, but just a bit more detailed. Also Foxhole is a top view camera so no first person. But the gameplay makes up for that. You don't have to be in clans or anything to play Foxhole, you can do solo logi and still help contribute to the team.

Join today and fight for The Colonial Legion, the hardcore faction because Devs are biased towards the Warden Faction, with this Entrenched update, they literally have a new tank that renders AT pillboxes useless, AT pill boxes have a total range of 45m, their new tank... also have a range of 45m, put two and two together, USELESS AT PILLBOXES DEVS. Aside that reviewer, The Colonial Legion - Hardcore mode, The Wardens - Easy mode.

Enjoy your experience and hope to see you on the battlefield :).
