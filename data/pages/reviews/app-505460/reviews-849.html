\r\n\t\t\t\t\tswiftly approaching 500 hours in game, I think it's clear that I enjoy the game... but for those who are wondering if they would like it... here is what to expect.

Every piece of equipment has to have its base components farmed by one player, another player move said components to a refinery, another (or sometimes the same) then turns the refined materials into usable equipment, they hand it off to a logistics truck driver or put it in the stockpile, then someone requests said supplies and a driver brings it out to them. This is every single item in the game. This makes equipment extremely valuable, and much of the game focuses on efficient use of said equipment or stealing said equipment from the enemy. Ultimately, it's a game of logistics networks doing battle.

If you want to Rambo with your shotty and extra ammo to kill some baddies... maybe this isn't the game for you. If you love the idea of seeing your small part affect the war as a whole, watching your team collectively strive for victory, and ultimately win as a unit... this game is for you.\t\t\t\t\t
