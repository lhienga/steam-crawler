There is a lot more to this game however. You have logistics which can be different. You could either collect scrap to make items, you can run supplies to backline bases to keep them from decaying, or you could run supplies to the frontline to keep everyone stocked and able to keep pushing forward.

If you want to frontline you can either use basic gun and shoot the other people, you could be a medic running around healing your team and reviving fallen soldier, you could man a tank or artillery and start shelling the enemies making their defence harder, you could grab a hammer, shovel and some building materials and help make the frontline base stronger to repel attacks.

If none of that sounds interesting you could go into the enemies backline with some others and sabotage their supply lines, destroy their supply hubs to reduce their production, or try to steal some enemy tanks and cause some havoc before you get taken out.

There is probably more to do but that is all I have done and enjoyed.

And yes, some reviews say the community and devs aren't the best. But from my entire time playing I haven't experience anything like that yet. Most people in the community are just having a good time trying to win a virtual war over the course of days, weeks or even months.

Excellent persistent world concept, appealing aesthetic, shooting most weapons and driving vehicles is well done and satisfying, many different roles.

But more than one year after the full "release" (which doesn't mean anything), I can list several issues:

- The front line regions (hexes) are either deserted OR there is a queue of dozens of people (likely because the game is centered around big clans);
- When I finally get to a region with some action, I get stuck in the corner of a building due to a bug and have to leave again (many instances of getting stuck in the terrain or around buildings for no reason, this is not "1.0" worthy);
- The performance is not good. It seems the game is heavily CPU limited, as driving through factory areas DESTROYS my FPS.

I also dislike some game design decisions:

- People suicide too much because the life of a foot soldier is completely worthless. Although this somewhat matches what happened in the Great War, no, they did NOT do solo kamikaze grenade runs to blow up an empty car. And kamikaze Japanese pilots did so to try and sink freaking aircraft carriers, not destroy a light tank or a house;
- Outside of shooting and driving, the gameplay is pretty poor/non-existent. Building is mind-numbing, you swing a hammer, sometimes missing the square hitbox that was right under your cursor for no reason. Shooting mortars is frustrating, because even if you try to quicksope binoculars, there is a recovery time so that you can't even see if you landed the shot. Only one person, the spotter, will see that;
- Ressources are often hoarded by clans (Regiments). To build my own simple car, I had to steal rare ressources from ressources fields that were empty most of the time.

Here are a few QoL changes I wish to see in this game:
- Binoculars allow to animation cancel the mortar recovery time, allowing all players to see the result of their shooting;
- Keybinds that allow to move forward or build without holding the key for several minutes. Most MMOs do that. Even Skyrim does that;
- Allow the player to keep his loadout if logged out in a safe town center that wasn't captured by the enemy. Sometimes, finding the precise items I want can waste minutes by having me drive a bunch for no reason. As a consequence, I hide even my most basic items in a single crate on the map, which is a risk and another needless waste of time.

These are just examples. I hope to see lots of QoL before the game inevitably becomes deserted.\t\t\t\t\t
