\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the deepest games I've played. There's great front-line infantry gunplay (deeper than you'd expect from a top-down shooter), rich vehicle battles, artillery, stealth missions into enemy territory, plus a really interesting backline logistics system that supplies all of this stuff. It really does achieve the goal of so many open-world MMOs: to motivate players with the organic world of the game itself, rather than arbitrary quests. I just drove a truck full of materials from Foundry to Blemish not because a questgiver told me to, but because I know my fellow players in Blemish need those materials to craft guns and medical supplies for the front line. I hear the desperate calls for supplies from the front line, and I know what I'm doing matters.

The social element is incredibly important, there are so many things in the game you can't do solo, working together in small groups is crucial. Probably the thing I like most is that everybody's equal, no player has more power than anyone else, and the game doesn't reward you for being greedy or selfish. Everybody is working together toward a common goal.

There have been some serious issues, though. The release of player-built factories ("facilities") in 1.0 was interesting, but really went against the cooperative spirit of the game, giving players good reasons to hoard resources and lock them away from the public. The game's most vocal players on social media tend to be jerks, prone to paranoia, witch hunts and crocodile tears and the usual drama you see in any multiplayer game's discord and subreddit. But my biggest complaint is that the developers don't engage with the community much. When problems and issues are pointed out, we rarely hear from the developers that they're looking into the problem, or planning a solution, which leads to more infighting and paranoia.

As I write this, the game's Steam review is "Mixed": that mostly reflects a coordinated review-bombing by the player base intended to get the devs' attention about real, serious problems with griefing and hacking in the game. And eventually, the devs *did* respond, with a new update that improves game security and addresses most of their concerns. Their response was so thoughtful that it really changed my mind about the future of the game, and I hope other players will change their reviews in light of this.

In short: play this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost a year ago I got this game, it looked... different, something new. And holy ♥♥♥♥ it was. For the massive Pros...
DO YOU LIKE TO BLINDLY FIGHT FOR YOUR COUNTRY, GRAB A RIFLE, A BANDAGE, 2 CLIPS AND CHARGE...
The game is extremely patriotic for whichever faction you choose, you will find each one has its own slurs for the other, Warden's/Fembois/Sticky White Nutty liquid drinkers/Baby Eaters, Colonials/Goblins/Furries and other harmless name calling. Don't take it to heart, as you have a whole faction that backs you into bullying the other side. You will find propaganda is used almost constantly throughout playing which only adds to the immersion.

DIFFERENT PLAYSTYLES!
Are you a calm and reasonable person? Do you like to Farm and play games like Stardew Valley?
Or do you like to Drive a truck for hours, back and forth, supplying bases with equipment? Then LOGI is for you!

Are you a fellow scumbag? Do you enjoy destroying things with ease? Do you enjoy hurting the person I described above? Then PARTISAN'S are for YOU!

Are you a simple person, liking things go boom? Do you want to experience the true horror of WW1, as you are shelled for hours by Rockets and Shells? Do you enjoy watching your friends die in front of you, as the poor medic behind you is set on fire?
Then FRONTLINE SOLDIER IS FOR YOU!

Are you a leader? Do you enjoy planning things and bossing others around? Do you enjoy big rolling lumps of metal going against other large lumps of metal? THEN TANK COMMANDER YOU ARE!


For the cons...


When you get this game, first join, there is little to no tutorial, there are youtube videos like HelpingHans who really does help you understand different mechanics, but just like most things in life, you'll learn through mistakes, very costly mistakes.
THE NEW PLAYERS have to understand, that usually the higher ranks consist of 2 people, the toxic veteran, and the helpful veteran. If you meet the toxic one, don't be discouraged, keep playing and you'll have some experiences that you won't forget.
Cheating, is not as much of a problem as other things, but it does exist, through the use of mods players can use different mods or exploits in the game to gain an advantage through different means. The devs are currently working to fix this.
And the big con, you'll find that many vets and other players get annoyed at different things due to balance or the extreme rigour that doing logi is. The devs are always working to maintain balance but the War can always droop to one side as Logi towns and Resource fields are taken over, tech is progressed, and Towns being supplied. The other problem is player count, whenever there is a big update the servers are split into 3 different wars, which effectively splits the playerbase.


To sum it up, I do recommend getting this game if you have the time to learn it, otherwise wait for a sale. It is truly one of a kind.
