\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo where to begin? I've been playing Foxhole for over two years, for most of that time I've led one of the most successful clans in the community and I was also a Game Moderator for roughly a year. But most of my friends have quit or are quitting.

Why? Simply put, the Devs. Between subpar moderator tools and policies, rolling out updates nobody asked for and ignoring things that have been begged for, zero community management outside of the Discord, and a declining player base there really isn't much left going for this game. I got my $20 out of it a long time ago but I can't promise that new players will with the direction things have been going.

A while back I realized that the Devs don't care about making a long lasting game anymore; the life cycle of this project is already coming to a close and the Devs know it. In the meantime the Devs will continue popping out shiny new toys in updates that ignore actual issues and they will run a Steam Sale immediately afterwards. We have ALL seen this a dozen times on Steam but for some reason we're bending over backwards to defend the Devs. I did it for two years and it's about time that we took a step back and realized that we're the victims of the next Indie Steam flop.

I haven't turned on this game in over a month but I decided to finally make this review after I saw the most recent DevBlog. Logistics is arguably the part of this game that has suffered the most through development and the Devs have done literally nothing that the logistics players have been begging for with the exception of the stockpile logs which is an insanely simply feature and never should have had to be asked for in the first place. On top of that the population has been suffering for months, to the point that entire maps are empty for large parts of the day, and instead of looking to the community and asking why we're leaving the Devs roll out a new, larger map....which makes logistics harder and spreads out the thinning population even more. Get ready for the next Steam Sale boys, it's coming soon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is an alternate era (around the times of WW2) game where 2 armies battle for territory. Players run almost everything, from gathering resources to building weapons, supplying areas and fighting. The only AI involvement is through buildings known as Foxholes, which are still player built. And no, there are no foxes and has nothing to do with becoming intimate with the rear of said animals.

Aspect
Review
Gameplay
The game has many different roles for players to take on and they can be changed dynamically, rather than having one set role. You can collect resources, transport resources to the front or fight against others on the front line. You are not restricted by role, rank or anything like that and can do effectively everything in the game from the start. The game has a variety of things to do, so if you aren't a fan of shooting things there's still something here.
Graphics and Optimisation
The graphics are fairly simple, but honestly quite fitting and attractive. They may not be as fancy or realistic as "Call of Duty 573: Running out of Titles IV" but fits the game very well and isn't an eye sore, so does the job well. The game is well optimised, I didn't notice any frame drops at all and I could safely run this game at Ultra with a High Tier PC from 2017 (no idea where that puts me now).
Additional Content
You pay once, and that's it. And the developer wants to keep it that way. It's good to see a game still keeping to this old way of doing things, that quite honestly, was way better than DLCs, Expansion Packs or... Microtransactions. Eugh./
Value
For a measly 15 or $20USD, you can get this game. The full game, no DLC or Microtransactions and hours and hours of fun. With multiple roles and wars lasting actual weeks, you won't get tired easy.

There are some bugs, but generally I have ended up laughing at the bugs rather than being mad. And hopefully, they add more content too!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most addictive and best MMO I've ever experienced. If you are on the fence about buying this game, DO IT. It may ruin your life because there is so much to learn and do. You will be pleasantly surprised by how good and helpful the community is. You start off with no rank and no experience, a fresh conscript. You will rely on other players to teach you how to be an effective member of your faction. You are free to decide how you spend your time: Frontline soldier is the most common for starting players. After learning to use basic weapons and medical supplies, you may notice a shortage of supplies hindering your ability to fight and gain ground. This is where logistics comes in. Logi players have a wide range of tasks including: Harvesting materials, refining those materials, creating weapons and supplies at factories, stockpiling those crates of supplies and delivering them to various fronts. Without Logi, you have no weapons and no ability to respawn when you die. Now, trucks delivering these supplies are vulnerable to enemy players known as partisans. They would love to destroy your truck and use your valuable supplies to help their faction. Builders help secure transport routes and fronts/towns. All the roles work together: Communication is the key. You will have a blast playing this game, and if you choose the Colonial faction, I would be happy to teach you personally. See you in the war!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe progress between now and Pre-Alpha has been very impressive. Massive amounts of content has been added, from vehicles to weapons to maps, along with entire gameplay mechanics previously not in the game. The devs continue to be hard at work and for the most part seem to know what they are doing. The occasional amateurish decision is often, with feedback from the community, overturned or changed into something more positive.

There isn't a game like this out there. This is one of the most dynamic online games currently in existence, where indeed everything you do can have a direct or indirect impact on the war. It has a bit of a learning curve but at the same time you'll be able to jump into the action pretty quickly. And if you can't figure something out, just ask in chat and people are always willing to help. On that note, the behavior in-game is pretty decent. Toxic behavior pops up now n then but is usually quickly squished with use of the report system.

There's room for solo hero moves, there's room for flanking assaults, there's room for sneaky partisan action, there's room for massive invasions... Or simply be a frontline soldier.
Then there's multiple forms of logistics - If you enjoy collecting resources, you'll feel right at home.
If you enjoy processing resources and turning them into weapons, ammo and supplies - the war depends on these people.
Ferrying passengers from a quiet spawn base to a busy frontline.
Taking a motorcycle and patrolling the logistic lines, protecting the logi runners.
Flanking around with boats, cutting off howitzers bombarding your frontline base, blowing up bridges to interfere with enemy supply lines, stealing materials and vehicles from inside their territory and using it against them.
I could go on and on, as there really is a lot to do, a lot of options, a lot of roles you can fulfill.. Ever changing, always up to you. Be a medic one moment, a grenadier the next, and a tank commander a minute later.

With every weapon, clip and construction material being collected and assembled by players, everything you do has an impact. Even if you just spawn and immediately die - it'll cost a spawn supply from the base you just spawned. Which in turn may influence when the next logi drops off shirts. Which influences more and more... You know... The Butterfly Effect. If you look for it, you'll notice it here constantly. In a good way.

2 hours is way too short to try this game and form a reasonable opinion, that's the only reason I've got to not say something like "Just give it a try! Refund if you don't like it!". But you should already have a pretty decent idea if this is the kind of game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is unique, never have I encountered a game like this and I don't expect any other game to one-up Foxhole in the genre. Any time soon at least.

I admit, there's a difficult learning curve and at times the game can be a bit dull, but my goodness, is it worth it. Hours upon hours of tense, amazing and gritty combat that really makes you feel like you're fighting against those bloody Warden grunts!

Join a tank division, gather scrap for the ongoing 24/7 war effort or even become a spec-ops guerilla fighter! Ambush a tank filled with salty Russians using only a side-car motorbike and an RPG launcher! Play a part in huge, gory last stands.

This game will forever hold a special place in my heart. 104 in-game hours may not seem like much, but it's been some of the greatest hours of my life. Rest in peace private Cat-man.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are no heroes in Foxhole.

There are no kill streak rewards. There are no cosmetic upgrades to strive for. There is no stat tracking. There is no single player that will make or break a round, because there aren't really rounds. Not in the normal sense of it. There are no predefined roles.

This is a world at war. You may choose to interact with other players as much or as little as you wish, but this war requires contribution from the players on either side, and the more coordinated side will win. Players who choose to join the fight on the front line in order to push that line ahead are no more essential to the effort as the players who choose to gather resources to manufacture the weapons, ammo, and supplies necessary for the actual combat. And both of those groups of players are completely useless without the players that fill the job of grabbing a truck to run the supplies from the factories to the forward bases. And any progress made is easily lost again without the players who spend their time strengthening defenses to hinder the enemy. Every role needs to be filled adequately to be successful. No one person will win this game for their side by working alone. No one player will gain an advantage by spending more real money on DLC.

In order to find your glory, you must actively participate in the effort and earn it.

The gameplay itself is a bit of a learning curve at first. Invest the time to ask questions and search the wikis, and you'll get it. The combat is chaotic and requires thought. You'll often be killed without ever seeing the enemy who killed you, and the only time you'll be informed of your killers name is when you're accidentally (or perhaps intentionally) killed by someone on your side. Friendly fire is common in the confusion of combat, as you won't always be able to clearly see where your allies or enemies are. You will often need to simply fire into where you think the enemy might be, and there will be no telling of whether or not you actually hit anything. There is a clever system to adequately punish gratuitous team killers, and a clever system to reward valiant effort.

This game requires patience, coordination, and communication. If you're focused on stats and polishing your numbers, this isn't the game for you. If you can find contentment in becoming part of a greater whole, then jump on in.\t\t\t\t\t
