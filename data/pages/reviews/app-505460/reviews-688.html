\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, after only 30 hours in, here's my take on this game.

It's one helluva adventure where no matter what role you are (and trust me, there are lots) it's a bit to learn, but the players by vast majority are friendly, helpful, and paitent with those learning thr ropes much like myself. Here was my adventure:

Playing as Colonials, we were assaulting from The Rush in the Tempest Islands, a long slog where lots of infantry were butchered trying to gain ground over the bridges in a huge fight. After respawning at a forward base for the hundredth time because of the bloothbath assaults, a random player of a higher rank approached the group of infantry at the base saying he needed two volunteers. Myself and another volunteered out of pure curiousity. MAN was I in for a surprise. The vet had a gunboat ready at the coastline, and had us get started loading it with shells. Once loaded, we by sheer luck met another gunboat. After our vehicle commanders spoke, it was decided we were going to naval barrage Warden infrastructure and vehicles along the coastline. Running much like a tank crew, we ended up on a few hour long escapade, callong in targets, navigating hostile waters, raining hellfire down on the Wardens. After enough damage and casualties had been caused, it allowed our infantry to push up two whole grids (Lots of territory) and capture lots of enemy supplies and infrastructure. Our naval fleet was unfortunately sank after enough AT fire brought us to the sea depths.

This was only one batyle in the war of course. But was it epic.

Just by volunteering out of curiousity I ended up playing a key role in helping the huge push that lots of players were in, assisting the entire faction. You are one person in a sea of persons running a full scale war machine, but you can have such an impact on the outcome of the war.

So that's my newcomer tale. Go experience for yourself!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis could be a good game, alas has some serious deficiencies when it comes to basic game design elements. I wish I could rate as neutral.

I will still upvote it but just because I appreciate the effort that went into this game.

Fundamentally the game just does not reward combat. Then is has some seriously silly game mechanics. For example keeping the screen tilted lets you see and shoot further. You see, there is no zoom and your view is limited by the screen but guess what, the diagonals are longer ... really? Is that the skill this game rewards? The ability to move around, aim and shoot while keeping the screen tilted 45%? Dear God!

The game would greatly benefit if shooting ranges were shorter (or the accuracy fell off more rapidly) and if people could take more damage. Most fights are endless stalemates, because of the lengthy travel back to the fighting points.

I could go on, but fundamentally, in my opinion the infrastructure is there, it is the game design that is borked.

This could be made into a runaway success. All the designers need to do is tune some parameters, make the game more approachable, put in a better feedback loop (work --> reward) and just make the player characters a little more durable. No new features are needed, just rework what you have.\t\t\t\t\t

And here, on this ""small"" review, I'm not even listing all the problems the game have.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a wholly unique experience, but a TL;DR if I had to describe it would be ARMA meets RuneScape meets EVE's Economy with a LOT of fat trimming.

For a more detailed review;
WAR ON THE FRONT
I have probably the least experience on the front lines then anywhere else however, it is a war game so I have some. And the time I have spent in the trenches I loved, there surprising depth to the number of tactics a team can use too outwit the enemy such as building a trench line towards the enemy, using chemical warfare, using artillery, or just plain old blanket of bullets. Playing as a medic really feels fun to do with being able to carry allies and having to manage resources. And I really underestimated how fun it was going to be too just have a set of binoculars and a radio keeping my teammates informed of the situation.
I do have a problem with the camera and how it controls, I don't know how to fix it myself, however the camera just always feels just too short of what I need to see and makes the whole combat experience feel just a little cramped as it seems I have to be in spitting distance to see well enough to shoot someone.

LOGISTICAL SUPPORT
This is the first war game to make this fun for me. Arma barely had it and squad was mind numbing, but the longer supply chain, variety of supplies, and the whole process of working with your comrades to be more and more efficient with getting supplies to the front.

There are definitely issues, tech feel just a tad too grindy and the camera is still annoying but overall its the best logistical side I have ever played in a game like this.
