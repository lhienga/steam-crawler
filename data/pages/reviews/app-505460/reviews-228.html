\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a very eccentric game with a very eccentric gameplay and experience

Normally, I would, probably, never play this kind of game
But, I came across it as I felt depressed in my personal life, and Foxhole became a perfect medication, providing me with a distraction with some social activities.
But, as my depression went away, my attitude towards Foxhole started to change.


Great game on paper
But, as it often happens, where it comes to competitive environment, players are not interested in a gameplay as it is, but victory whatever the cost.
For this game it means exploiting bugs and game mechanics weaknesses.
I'v played this game for over 200 hours, about 2-3 month
I joined one of the biggest clans out there
Initially the game was really fun
logistics, teamwork, huge maps with a lot of players...
But, eventually, the other aspect of the game became more and more apparent:

1. Low pop time
One side seems to almost always have more people spending their time playing than another, that leads to "Steamroll" wars, where one side wipes another because there were no players on the enemy team. Its pretty much PvE
Proper PvP, with more or less equal forces having fun, is very rare, accidental.
2. Rush/Spam
At some point I realized, that everything is this game is a spam/rush
Not because its fun, but because that's what everybody is doing
Soldier life is priceless, and suicidal tactics are, pretty much, the meta of this game
Most of the time, players grab one weapon, like grenade, or a bayonet, and rush forward to kill a very certain
vehicle/structure, the other side answers with its own suicide tactic. That's pretty much Foxhole gameplay in a nutshell:
Mammonth rush to destroy structures
Sticky rush to destroy vehicles
Gas spam to gain ground
Grenade spam to gain ground
Satchel rush to destroy structure quickly
Bayonet rush to decrew a gun
Tank rush to destroy enemy outnumbered vehicles
Artillery spam to destroy any enemy...

May be this game should be named a Spamhole or a Rushhole
Any time people try to stay alive, do some kind of tactics, they get countered by yet another suicide spam/rush
And the only option you have is to respond in kind
Logistics and building, from my perspective, mean little, because men-hours are a decisive factor
One team may have countless tanks, weapons, structures and other resources, but have very little people to use it.
From my experience the most damage is done by very cheap equipment used in a spam/rush suicidal manner

Timezones are a huge factor
For some reason players seem to be spread very unequally between them
You might see very active Warden during EU and East coast NA timezone, and Colonials during West-coast/Asia and vice-versa
That leads to a very unbalanced PvP experience, where the game, eventually, forces you to spend ever more time playing, to the point, where even 18 hour day is not enough. At the moment you and your friend go to bed - you might loose all that you build/manufactured, all that ground you captured... Or, you need more players on your team to support you while you sleep. But there are no apparent team balance, so, essentially, most of the wars you either steamroll and win, or get steamrolled and loose...

May be the best name for this game is a TimeHole? Timesinkhole?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith over 1,000 hours in, and having been a part of this game since version 0.1 in June 2017, I can say that while I love this game to death, and the idea of it, I still can't recommend it.

1. Grind. Logistics is grind. not merely grind, but hold down left mouse button grind. Hold down W and wait grind. It's an endless cycle of torment and grind. Not only that, but alot of stuff is just plain too expensive. because of this, alot of people will panic if they see a low rank newbie use certain things. A tank sounds fun, but it takes alot of time and effort to make that tank, transport it to the front, supply and fuel it, and then hope that there's a solid crew around that can make the most of that investment.

2. The game, while not hostile to new comers, is utterly unforgiving. Because of the grind and lethality of weapons, this game can be very unforgiving of newcomers and it refuses to allow just anyone to drop in and play. Tanks are just one part. Artillery is tedious A.F.

3. Communication. Alot of this game requires solid communication, which is why squads exist and why clans (regiments) can accomplish so much. Without communication (keyboard is fine for some tasks) things quickly spiral into oblivion. I've found that herding cats is easier than getting a squad to form up as the cats are more agreeable than humans half the time.

4. Time. Because it takes alot of time to get anything done, you can find yourself stuck in the exact same spot doing the exact same thing for hours on end with no real progress on either side.


Despite all of this, I will say that the community in general is very open and welcoming of newcomers, you can almost always find help if you ask for it, and people will be willing to take you on and show you how to use vehicles and artillery and all the fun stuff if you pay attention and are willing to learn.

The grind is a ♥♥♥♥♥, but as long as you respect the effort put into things, and be reasonable in your item usage, you can typically go far enough with relatively little.
People are people, but if you're charismatic enough, and have a microphone, you can get your goon squad up and from there you can go on your own adventures -- from blowing up enemy supply lines and buildings, to stealth capping towns and being a part of major offensives.

I ♥♥♥♥♥ about this game because I love it. IF you are willing to put in the time and learn, this game has what no other game out there can provide. IT's like Men of War or Company of Heroes where the players are the units. Just as any RTS, your most important unit is your humble builder/worker/resource gatherer. There isn't a wrong faction to pick -- they both whine about the other and have their own silly quirks of culture, which varies from the silly to the crude and vulgar, and Charlie Shard is the most open and welcoming of the servers for newcomers.

If you are willing to put up with all the obnoxious BS this game will throw at you, this is one of my all-time favorite games even though the BS is real, and it will destroy you if you aren't prepared to accept it.

This game, being a social one, is MUCH better with friends. If you like it, and you get your goon squad up, there is no limit to the amount of absurdity you can pull off. We even have freaking nukes, and one of the greatest stories of all time was when one faction nuked themselves when nukes were first put in. R.I.P. Jade Cove.\t\t\t\t\t
