\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't normally leave reviews. I have way more hours(800+) invested in this game than I'd like to admit, it is that good. While it isn't perfect, this might be one of the best games I've ever played in the MMO genre. I would not classify this as a twitchy game at all so if you are older, like RTS games, like MMO games and used to play shooters you might like this. It is unique and has endless hours of gameplay to offer.

Pros:
  • MMO with an ending, each war ends and then restarts. Yes, there are game loops in this game the require a grind but only if you want there to be. I find myself grinding 1 war to get resources for something fun and powerful, like a battletank and then the next war, more casually playing. These options help prevent burnout.
  • Great co-op play with friends. It is a full pvp game.
  • Death, destruction, absolute chaos. Big battles are intense. Honestly, I've had more fun losing in a last stand than winning a lot of the time.
  • Attention to detail/realism. For example, to fire artillery, you need a trigger man, a guy who loads the shells manually and a spotter who provides coordinates to fire at. You also have to make the artillery piece and shells, then transport them to the front.
  • Sandbox/player made elements. The logistics/economy/factory side of this game is amazing. It is complicated and has a learning curve but it's actually kind of fun. I normally find that type of activity to be tedious(it still can be at times) but Foxhole does make this interesting.
  • Tanks are awesome.
  • Naval update was a game changer, excellent new content there.
  • Allows for different play styles. You can be casual and have fun at the front or deep dive into the extreme resource gather, building and facilities end of the game.
  • Community - there's some cool people who play and that will help you out if you ask.

Cons:
  • Learning curve. It isn't easy to figure out at first. There's a training area that was okay when I first started but not great. It is a true sandbox and you have to figure things out as you go. It can be overwhelming and it took me months to figure out the way things work. I feel like I'm just now getting to the point where I'm comprehending all the elements of the game(end to end) and their importance. Go to the front early and fight, that will get you hooked imo.
  • Time investment - you can play casually but if you are like me, you might find that hard to do. There's always stuff to be done which is bad and good.
  • Connectivity issues and bugs sometimes. Not terrible or game ruining, but not extremely polished.
  • Can be a little too much sandbox at times. It gets frustrating when people take your important stuff from your facilities without asking. There could be some improvements to the game mechanics around that in my opinion.
  • Community - for every 5-10 good people, there's a few that are just toxic or annoying for no reason. Can be brutal.
  • Larpers - Way more than you would expect but maybe I'm just not into that.
