But this runs against a couple of problems; the community is HORRIBLE and TOXIC, sure some people are friendly and helpful. But the loud majority likes to smell their farts because they think they are a higher rank than you or have a significant faction, reporting you if you don't listen to them or just playing the game in your way. This results in many players fighting and dying on the frontlines as simple soldiers because they are too scared to join a faction or do something important for the war effort. There is also the problem where a faction hogs all of the production of vehicles and artillery, causing a whole front to be lost because that faction is AFK or fighting on a different front.

My second point is building is repetitive and stupid; when I first got this game, I was intrigued by the building mechanics. I asked myself, " what will players build, and what intricate designs will players design." This turns out to be a total CRAP, sure, you can build some impressive stuff, but the meta is to spam howitzer bunkers or pillboxes, turning the base into a mess. This does not only make it look bad, but it's a massive drain on computer performance. This resulted in my computer dropping THIRTY fps when at bunker bases.

The third point is that logistics is the most BORING and ANGERING part of the game; the average logistics player has to travel for HOURS to get materials, some people might like this, but do you know what all people collectively hate, QUEUE times, imagine that you have been driving for an hour to get to the front, you look down at the mini-map, you see your close. You finally get there and travel, BUT NOPE, get ready to spend 20 minutes in the queue because the region is at max player capacity. And when you finally get into the region, you get sniped by some partisans with some RPGs, losing all of the materials you collected, setting you back to square one with no truck.

In summary, this game is excellent on paper. Still, it sucks in actual gameplay. I have seen more cooperation between players in Balloons tower defence, and the toxicity is worse than a Modern Warfare lobby; the building sucks and is horrible on the performance of your computer. Finally, one of the game's main selling points is a total waste of time. I wasted 72 hours of my life playing this unfinished, horribly made game, so save yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA top down persistent "MMO" game with a really big map where fighting, building and logistics are all done at the player level functioning entirely off of community coordination and communication. Some parts excellent, some parts finicky, some parts tedious and some parts rage-inducing. A lot of hidden mechanics you either need to research or intuit. It requires a decent amount of investment to enjoy, so unless you have several hours to spare don't even bother booting it up. The investment and emergent gameplay are probably my favorite aspects, from "well we need a medic guess I'll do it," to "this base is now my building project," or "NO, that is *my* machine gun, and I'm going to keep it safe no matter what."

"Random drunk dude built a tank from scratch and needs someone to man the gun and call targets," is probably my favorite kind of player. I spent about 3 hours on that the other day. It was a recon tank so we just rolled around the front to scout the fortifications of a massive enemy line, decided we'd be better off hitting logistics trucks due to enemy density, crossed into a region and were immediately confronted by an antiarmor tank and escaped, rolled back to a garage to fix the armor then decided to hit an unmanned back line to kill enemy fortifications and map intelligence, where we ended up linking with a couple random dudes doing sabotage. That translated into meeting up with an entire allied front and three heavier tanks and at least thirty or forty infantry, and due to our vehicle role we ended up doing reconnaissance for the front and taking out lighter defenses for the infantry to move up.
We eventually shifted to let the front advance on it's own while we harassed random infantry trying to flank, and then we got dogpiled by ten dudes with high explosive grenades and the turret couldn't keep up with them so we got exploded. Note: This is not always going to be your experience. Sometimes you can spend good time on the same life, then go to the front and immediately get rekt. The front is at your own risk.

The entire time I heard the driver periodically ripping a bong and singing like a silly man.

Honestly I think it's a great game and it's really progressed since the alpha release. It's just kinda difficult to just jump into and you end up relying heavily on other players to teach you how game mechanics work, because very very little is spelled out. But every time you learn something new, you get to pass it on to new players when the horde comes from a sale. Heck, I'm about a hundred hours in and still am asking people how things work. Foxhole is not for everyone and especially the easily discouraged or easily bored. You have to work with other players to make the real fun, never ever be afraid to ask questions, and don't worry, someone is always willing to help you out.\t\t\t\t\t
