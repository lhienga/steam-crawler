\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the time of review I do only have 106 minutes in this game but honestly, it's some of the best 106 minutes I've spent in a game in a while. Now, normally I'm not an online gamer, I prefer my singleplayer stuff, but the idea of a continuous online war going on was just too intriguing to keep me away.

Foxhole is a (I believe) WW1 massive online war simulator. Think something like Helldivers but bigger and more complex. Not only do you have to worry about killing and not being killed, but you also need to worry about supplies being made AND shipped/trucked to where you are. You need truck drivers to bring ammo, medical supplies guns and more to the front lines otherwise, you're out of luck.

And that's my favorite thing about this game, is the way it has emergent gameplay. I was sitting at townhall, we were getting hit hard, pushed back, I was on trauma (reviving fallen soldiers) while another guy was healing them. One guy was running to the front lines and hauling them back and the ones that didn't make it were taken by another guy to the field hospital. People are talking in chat about where a medic is needed. A truck of supplies came in and people were cheering when guns were being deposited into the stockpile because that's what we were missing.

It was one of the coolest moments in online gaming I've been apart of in years, probably since the days of MAG on PS3.

Yes I'm 106 minutes in right now but I still highly recommend this game. A note though: the player base does seem a little toxic on the Steam forums, and a little on reddit. Not so much from Discord from what I've seen and really not at all in game it's self. Of course maybe those I've been around have been good but just a warning. This game is community based and the Steam discussions really do show a toxic nature I haven't seen in game.\t\t\t\t\t
