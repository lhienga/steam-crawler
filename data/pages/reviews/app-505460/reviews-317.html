\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't recommend this unless you read this full review and it clicks with your specific set of interests.

The upcoming 1.0 release with trains and player factories intrigued me. Basically Factorio but militarized and an MMO. The idea of running logistics grabs my attention more than jank top-down gun battles where death comes quickly and from someone you didn't see due to (perhaps a little too realistic) sight-lines causing models to unload.

It's fun figuring out ways to get supplies to forward bases that need it. The issue comes with the MMO of it all meaning actually producing said supplies is a lot of clicking through menus designed to stop players from moving *too many* raw resources too quickly. Then there's the fact most communication happens in a big open channel with only the option to filter to one part of the army's messages. Want to try and coordinate a busy seaport thats jammed to a standstill? Too bad, the infantry have decided to spam chat with a certain three-letter word for a fluid (as they do every half-hour).

There is a very cool game here but - like other MMOs that rely so much on player interaction: hours of work can be undone in seconds.

There is fun to be had, but at least from the logistics side you're spending most of your time patching parts of a supply chain left in tatters by other players who're more interested in making toys to go play with themselves than stockpiling necessary resources or moving them to the front.

From what little experience I've had, there's cool passive lore to the game and it is ELECTRIC if you're in the server while something interesting is happening. Last night a nuke went off and a second was barely prevented. Chat went BANANAS. People are still bickering in the Wardens about having nuked one of their own cities for strategic reasons a few days ago.

When things are entertaining, they're VERY entertaining.

When they're janky, it's EXTREMELY janky.\t\t\t\t\t
