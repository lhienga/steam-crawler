\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first ever review, but this game deserves it and the story of the HMS "Baby Shark" is one of greatness.

I logged in at 3.30 AM wanting to play for an hour max before going to bed. At once I saw a crew looking for a fourth member for their gunboat. I joined in and as soon as I reached the docks, we got destroyed from a dirty collie boat camping the docks in Oarbreaker Isles, never even fired a shell. We rebuilt, restocked and set sail, took out the enemy gunboat in revenge and proceeded to shell the fort and defenses at Strong Beach. Once they were down we had to resupply. Afterwards, we helped another operation who were about to land at Conclave and try to take it. We shelled the northern beach to open up a landing ground for them and wished them the very best.

After another resupply we headed on the west side of the main island, we shelled Pram Road and helped our infantry getting pushed back at the Ides as best we could until we had run out of shells. Yet another resupply and we had to aid our brothers invading Conclave as two enemy gunboats were harassing them. But the numbers disadvantage couldn't deter us and we headed for battle. We managed to destroy both enemy gunboats with precision fire and we swung around to Kukri and Castor to further shell enemy defenses and FOBs.

The next resupply had us shelling the base at Infans and the fort at The Palisade as well as many static defenses around those points. The boys on the ground were starting to push hard as we were shelling enemy backlines into oblivion. The next step was to help our comrades push into Pram Road and then Requiem.

It was a major offensive, we had just researched tanks. A combined infantry, tank and naval operation was set in motion. We shelled the enemy defenses at Pram Road hard, the armored spearhead pushed in and we kept up with them, shelling Requiem and opening up the ground for the tanks. Enemy FoBs, AT Guns, Foxholes, CVs, engineers, they all had shells with their names on them. We swung around the south side of the island and entered the bay at Camp Posterus. Our boys on the ground were fighting hard, they had pushed the Collies back into their base. We kept shelling all we could find, enemy logi at the factory, howitzers giving our boys a hard time, anything that moved. The Collies couldn't mount a proper defense against us, neither on land or at sea, but their base had strong fortifications and our armored division couldn't keep up a sustained assault. Soon we had to go resupply as well, but another friendly crew had our backs and gave us ammo right then and there. The battle at Requiem turned into a slugfest, our engineers dug in and built defenses so we went back around near Doe's head to assist our comrades. We had taken a direct howitzer hit from the enemy base, but we had an important task at hand, so we had no time to repair. We shelled the enemy hard at Doe's head, but some sneaky Collies finally managed to get up to us and give us some RPG shots. We killed 2 of them, but 2 RPG shots were enough to finally sink us.

In the end, after a bit more than 7 hours, we had taken down 7 enemy gunboats, an APC which sneaked past our front and harassed our logi, 3 forts, 5 FoBs, countless enemy static defenses, watchtowers, CVs, engineers and logi players before finally the HMS "Baby Shark" met its end. I can't express the pride I feel in our crew, our logi who always kept us resupplied with Lt Arty shells, the people helping coordinate attacks and our boys on the ground fighting the good fight. This game is a shining example of an amazing community and it really gives weight to all your actions.

Thank you shazbot, voidsteppin, Kowalski and later on Whynot, Proto and Lokian for being the best the mighty Warden Navy had that day. Exemplary soldiers, who ruined a ton of Collie lives at Oarbreaker Isles while being the only gunboat on our side for a big chunk of the time. Thanks to Treebro and his crew for joining the big offensive, as well as resupplying us later on.

Buy the game, communicate with people and you are bound to have tons of fun. See you on the battlefield to shoot up some Colonists!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a really solid and fun shooter that has fun mechanics that force you to rely on your team to survive. Out of all the "realistic war sim" games I have played foxhole has honestly done the best at immersing me in its world that the devs have crafted. The game does this by doing the "you are just a soldier" concept that games like Battlefield, Star wars Battlefront and others do. This one is unique in the fact that it hammers this in. Tanks require entire 3 or 4 man crews to work at max performance. The mortar requires a second person to use and building and distributing resources to the frontlines requires the work of a lot soldiers which form the logi branch but, I won't go too in depth in that can of worms. While there is a lot more that makes the game great (shooting, interesting map, lore, etc) I really think it wouldn't be as great if it wasn't for the you are just a soldier thing I mentioned earlier. Since every small victory feels like
progress to winning the war instead of feeling like winning some ordinary cod match.

I really suggest this game to people who are looking for a fun team game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE #26: The Update that Broke Logistics Players

I have played 740 hours in this game, so I suppose that I have my money's worth. The game community has fun people, the concept is neat, and I have had some enjoyment playing Foxhole. This is certainly one of those quirky Indy games whose success I have supported.

Unfortunately, I cannot in good conscience recommend this game to new players even at a discounted "Steam Sale" price. The reasons for this are quite simple: the game is unbalanced, filled with glitches, bugs and errors, and has fundamental game play problems. The game has become so horribly broken in its efforts to be improved that many players, myself included, have decided to uninstall their copies. There are simply more enjoyable ways to spend your free time than playing Foxhole... Like being slapped in the face.

Most importantly: everything in this game is made by players, and to make it after Update #26 (the most recent update) is an ABSOLUTE GRIND. God awful and thankless. This involves standing around a pile of "raw goods" for hours holding your left mouse button while your character "mines" Sulfur, Components or Salvage. This also means hours for processing queues. This also means hours for production and transportation. In short, the Noob at the front who grabs ten mortar shells and wastes them on trees just wasted four hours of someone's life. All of this was an annoyance but never really a problem until now.

The game is not balanced. Both factions do not have access to equal resources. The Devs scoff at the idea and shrug their shoulders claiming "RNG," but in reality its a skewed and poorly constructed game design without effective oversight or play testing. In essence, if you buy this game, except to PAY to be a permanent Beta Tester for developers who really do not care to respond to actual fundamental game play problems in their product.

In its current state, Foxhole requires hours of thankless horrible mind-numbing and finger-killing grinding to produce extremely expensive items which have a marginal effect against the speed and ease with which the adversary can build cheap and powerful buildings, and rebuild them, thereby making effective attacks a serious chore (and bore). Both factions do not have equal resource availability which means the Colonials have an abundance of oil and explosive material, so if you play Warden this means you will be forever starving for goods. If the result of this is "RNG," that means subsequent Wars will likely present the same problem where one side has an extremely unbalanced advantage over the other.

The new map design is sloppy. Expect frequent crashes, being "stuck," and other mystery bugs while the region design promotes glitchy play. You can be murdered when crossing a region without ever loading your screen which causes your PC to freeze into a constant-loading screen. There are longstanding sight, aiming and vision exploits which have never (and will likely never) be fixed.

In short: this game is a permanent Alpha. If you want to pay to be a play tester for a broken game which seems to pride itself by "adding content" without addressing its fundamental bugs, imbalances, and game play issues, have fun. You might enjoy it for a few hundred hours before you realise that it is really just an awful, horrible mess and a nightmare grind.

R. I. P. Foxhole. You were a fun concept that turned into an awful experience.\t\t\t\t\t
