\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPropper review will be established once the devs acknowledge the LOGI union strikes statement about logistics in the game

This game has a really cool idea, and at times can be a really fun team based game on a scale you've never seen before! It has it's issues at times with pacing and establishing yourself though...

The premise of the game effectively, is play as one of the two sides in this alternate reality with tech reminiscent of the world wars, the endgame being capturing the majority of "victory towns" on the map. The twist, is the game is one massive war, the server is one massive one split into multiple maps all running at the same time, with hundreds of players active in each map or 'province', running a war that is entirely player driven. People will collect resources available on the map and take it to refineries, take the established materials to factories to produce weapons and gear for the rest of the faction, other players will use those weapons and ammo and plan their assaults, their fortifications, and when it all comes together it's really frickin cool. Wars can last several IRL days, a week or two even, frontlines making little progress akin to World War 1, where one side pushes and is halted by endless artillery, mines, and the like that turn the area into a propper "No man's Land".
The combat, because of stuff like this, can be fun.
Getting involved further however, such as building your own field guns, or boats, or tanks, or running logistics (making gear to ship to the front) can be outright stressful, as the spacing between resources to gather and produce all this stuff can make say, building a gunboat, and then the ammo for its 120mm gun, can take like, 30-45 minutes, and if you aren't smart, that boat can go down almost immediately due to a well aimed tank that noticed you rolled up while you were zeroing the gun.
Or say, youre transporting a field gun to the front that took you 30 minutes to get the resources yourself to make because no one else had, and you lose it because of "Partisans" who snuck into the supply line that no one found out about until you did, just now.
So yea, its a fun game, and I can enjoy logistics sometimes, but if supplying the war effort isn't your thing/doesn't sound cool, definitely wait for it to get better, and just stick to the normal war stuff.\t\t\t\t\t
