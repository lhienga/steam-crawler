\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a unique game with an ambitious premise and style all of its own. There is no other videogame in the world like foxhole. It is a fantastic concept given over to one of the most ineffectual and unthinking dev teams to ever see the light of day.

Do not play this terrible game. There is nothing this game can offer you besides an immense and complete waste of your time. Do not be suckered in by the flashy taglines and genuinely fun premise, it's flawed down to it's core at every level. Balance is more or less nonexistent, all gameplay decisions are made randomly with little to no player input.

On top of clunky, frustrating gameplay and bad balance, this game has one of the worst communities I've had the displeasure of experiencing. It is actively antagonistic towards itself, griefing and cheating are rampant and the developers have little interest in addressing this whatsoever.

The community is chock-full of bad actors who want nothing more than to cause drama and conflict, the developers don't take any action towards maintaining the health of the community at all or improving discourse.

This game is a great premise turned into a radioactive dumpster fire, avoid at all costs and spend your time on something worthwhile.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t90 hours in this war sim where there is no chain of command and a mix of solos, coordinated groups and uncoordinated groups all attempt to contribute to a war effort, has taught me that context is everything.
Judging in haste is the root of so many problems, but sometimes rapid and decisive action will snatch victory from the jaws of desperation.
This is probably so similar to urban warfare when strategic communications is fragmented and tactical groups have to utilise whatever is available, when information=SNAFU*signal/noise^(fog_of_war/0)
Surely heroes are wrongly shot for spying and partisans are wrongly granted access to vital stores all the time in real life chaotic war zones.
Probably most everything in the other reviews, both good and bad, reflects a genuine experience of this game. Elite solos trying to help the side despite bands of organised idiots. Organised vets trying to manage their resources despite chaotic solos... Sometimes wonderful emergent stories arise from this total muddy bloody churn. Reminds me a bit of the old Commando comics, or Battle Picture Weekly.
This is totally great, I do love it to bits.
I just hope they fix the alt problem and the vote-ban problem (which does need to be fixed) without spoiling the fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got a lot more than I was expecting out of this, and I have only scratched the surface. First I want to say though, this is an MMO that respects your time and makes you feel accomplished while doing it. Everytime I ask for help on something people are quick to answer and make sure you're helping out the way you want to!

Even if it's as insignificant as holding the line at a town or player-built base. On the smaller shards (servers) there has often been a very good push and pull in the skirmishes where you're pushing them back and then they change it up by starting to throw gas grenades, so you respond with mortars or armor, and then they respond with an answer. It is a great feeling and isn't just zerging from point to point until the zerg dies. The main shard (server) is a bit more chaotic and not my pace, but that doesn't mean it can't be for you. Maybe you have some friends or met some people that want to go behind enemy lines and stop logi runs so their supplies dry up. Combat has a lot to offer.

If the above doesn't really do it for you, there's more! Maybe you feel you want to support the war effort a different way. So you go and grab a pick-up truck and start doing logi runs. If you like gathering grab a hammer and start whacking salvage till you fill your truck up. If gathering isn't your thing though, feel free to leech off the stocked resources in the backlines. Then convert it into a base resource to make whatever you think the frontline needs! Maybe they even added a map marker with a list saying what they need (With timestamps!). I spent about 4 or 5 hours alone doing a logi run between two locations. Sadly Elksford was lost after 3 days from when I started, but damn did it feel like I made a difference during that.
Maybe you like neither of those and want to realize your inner architect. Grab a hammer and shovel and get started then. Towns are pre-determined locations, but don't have much to them, so start adding to it! From bunkers, trenches, garrison buildings and more. Hell with the latest update they added trains. So why not make a train system from a major supply town to the frontlines!

These are all systems for you to enjoy at your own pace, on your own time. Maybe you became a mortaring pro, or saved a frontline that was in desperate need, or built a base to withstand any invader. Just fall into the rabbit hole that is Foxhole.\t\t\t\t\t
