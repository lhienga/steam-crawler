\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tend to go into games like these (open ended PvP sandboxes) cringing a bit and expecting an overly toxic experience from sweaty enemies and allies alike, however, I was pleasantly surprised to find neither. The general attitude of the player population is very forgiving and eager to educate new players. It's not difficult to find yourself in a unique and memorable situation quickly. A great part of the appeal of games like this is the experiences you gain and the stories you get to tell.

The sense of community here is quite strong. You are part of a much larger machine and people tend not to have time for things like nitpicking or salt. That stuff gums up the gears of war, and neither side wants that. I did find the occasional know it all, but their voice got quickly drowned out by the dozens of other people in the area vying for command. It's much like a real war in this sense with the chaos of combat quickly quashing any personal grudges or drama.

While standard rifleman combat can be quite simplistic, there's no shortage of things to do for every type of person. More complex roles such as artillery or scouting are readily accessible. Roles can be filled by anyone at any time at any rank. It's nice not to be restricted in this regard. You're free to do whatever you want right from the get go. While this can be detrimental at times with new players 'wasting' resources, as long as you have the spirit and are making an attempt, you'll be contributing to the effort.

One of my critiques of this game would be the logistics element. It's an incredibly tedious, but necessary part of the experience. Since the devs are still working on improving logistics, I won't hold it too hard against them, but considering the importance of it, I'd say it should be sorted out first over adding new combat toys since none of that is possible if logistics isn't happening in the first place.

Overall, I'd say this game has great potential for the future as long as the community continues to grow and stay engaged with an active dev team. I look forward to seeing the final result of the development progress as the growing pains are slowly settled.\t\t\t\t\t
