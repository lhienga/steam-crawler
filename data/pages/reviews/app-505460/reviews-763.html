\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe backbone of the gameplay, Logistics, is mechanically hostile to the casual/solo player. From resource extraction to refining to manufacture to delivery, the execution is simple and brain dead (such that it is an open secret that experienced builders and logi uses autoclickers and macros of some sort) but time consuming and labor intensive. You only need to press W and M1 but you will easily be spending most of an hour doing something as simple as delivering basic materials - a resource used for the manufacture of most goods as well as repairing and construction of buildings and vehicles both on the front and on the backline - by your lonesome. Chances are you'll also drive into an ambush and lose all the time invested in that truck and its contents. The devs themselves have explicitely stated that it is a design goal for solo logistics to be completely unviable in favor of clan operations. In other words to effectively participate in logistics a player must either focus solely on mining or refining and manufacturing or transportation. When a side reaches low population logistics breaks down due to the mechanics deliberately making it inefficient for players to try to assume more than one function in the supply chain.

The impact this has on the rest of the game is immense even if a player does not touch any logistic gameplay at all. Combat at the beginning of every war cycle is fundamentally the same because the only goods that can be produced reliably are goods that can be sourced off of the salvage resource. This means the basic rifle and grenade punctuated only by the occasional appearance of something exotic. Any weapon or ammunition that requires sulfur or components in its supply chain is rare enough that if it looks like a teammate will incompetently hand it to the enemy than it is wise to teamkill them in order to prevent its capture. Forget about piloting a tank or warship because not only will the clans have already stripped the component and sulfur fields for their own use, it represents such an incredible investment in time and labor that they'll teamkill you because incompetence is indistinguishable from deliberate sabotage.

Go play something else like ARMA or Squad because at the very least you won't be wasting a lot of time for nothing. Hell, go play War Thunder too because at least you'll get something out of grinding in that game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is very fast becoming one of my favorite games since the last update. It's well optimized, it's easy to get into, the player base is helpful. You can play this game casually or binge it to your heart's content. What's insane is with every new update that introduces new weapons, defenses, vehicles and more, massively changes the dynamics of the perpetual war, much like in real war where advances impact the battlefield. With everything in the game being made and transported by players, this truly is a full-scale war. Territory is always long fought over, and even small gains ring in as minor victories. This game has fantastic meta, and even has some of the issues faced in actual war such as disrupted supply lines, working with what you have at the frontlines, and other things which create challenges for players. There's an organized chaos to it all that makes this one of thee best and most immersive war games on the market (despite the art style which may suggest otherwise). With so many roles to fill from crafting, logistics, artillery and frontline combat and more, you can easily carve Foxhole into your own experience based on your style of play.

I highly recommend this game to anyone who enjoys strategic war games, shooters, crafting, isometric games and more.\t\t\t\t\t
