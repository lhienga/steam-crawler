\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game, but as of now I cannot recommend Foxhole to someone else in its current state. In a game that demands so much of your time, it is far too easy for bad actors to ruin days if not weeks worth of time in potentially minutes. The developers have come so far in their ability to offer robust and different game mechanics to a niche community eager to explore these concepts, but fall completely flat in giving the players the tools to adequately protect their assets from others on their own team who would see their efforts burn.

Foxhole is a truly great concept, but at the time of this writing has poor execution due to it's shortcomings in both moderation of its player-base as well as not giving the players the proper means to protect their own assets in a way that isn't akin to a second job. This game does not respect your time already.. This makes it insult-to-injury when hours of work evaporate with no trace as to why. And when probable cause is truly found, seemingly nothing is ever done. It is a MMO with no external authentication, with a different identity being a simple steam name-change away, and the developers have time and time again stayed silent regarding these matters to the point where I could compare the state of things to the Wild West.

I want to get excited about playing Foxhole, but these barriers to the fun have created an aversion that destroys further desire to play. The developers have shown their ability to listen, given enough feedback- so hopefully this gets their attention. My wishes are to be able to return to this review and reverse my decision. I sincerely want things to improve- not for the game to die.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: If you're really interested in the game and need a review to help you decide, you're at the right place, but if you don't like reading consider the other reviews.

If you read much of the recent reviews, you'd probably get the gist of the situation right now. Always starts of as the game being a great concept with different working parts to such a player-driven system, from different levels of logistics all the way to the many frontline roles you can fill in, before it devolves into criticism concerning in-game and community moderation, toxic factionalism, QoL issues and more.

My intention for this review is to leave an unbiased review (i.e. leaving out equipment asymmetry)so that those who are looking to buy this game know what they're buying for. It also stands as a message for the developers that, despite all of the major updates they have done and all of the shiny new toys we have, much of the game mechanics remains unchanged and ill-suited to the current state of the game.

There are two main criticisms that people might want to consider:

1. In-game Moderation
Because persistence plays a major part in Foxhole's gameplay loop, it's to no one's surprise that having a good anti-cheat system, or even just an active moderation team would stop a lot of the cases you usually don't hear on these reviews.

Cheaters and alt accounts aren't as prevalent as people think, however because of the game's small playerbase and poor tutorial system, they can seem more prevalent than they are and new players can be accused of "alt behavior" for something they didn't know was an unspoken rule of the community. There is also the case of toxic behavior and factionalism, however much of it can be attributed to the lack of in-game response that riles people up and causes paranoia.


2. Poor QoL
Like I said, the game is very player-driven and people can choose what they want to do, however, unless you are taught that there are actual needs to the war you most likely wouldn't know that the non-combat (logistics, building, etc.) gameplay loop takes a lot more time and effort out of the lives of people than it should.

The map isn't properly adjusted for the new logistics systems in place, base building and facility management is more of a second job to many at this point than it is an "engaging part of the game" and causes burnout, there is little in the way of a proper tutorial for new players, and so many smaller issues that have caused minor uproars all the way to an actual, organized strike because of these issues.

Many will mention that the developers are out of touch and do not know what actually happens in-game, and this is mostly true. While I understand the limitations of their QoL updates because of how the game engine was built or because of time restrictions, I have NEVER seen "good enough" community engagement even in the form of a simple Q&A outside of devbranch (which has a completely different atmosphere than actual wars).


TL;DR, the game is good on paper, ignore much of the toxic factionalism and focus on playing the game itself and it's actually not that bad of an experience. Many core mechanics and the gameplay loop needs to change to accommodate its current state, more QoL needs to be addressed, and there needs to be more active moderation in both in-game and the community.

Don't get me wrong, this game has had some great wars and I and many of my friends have had great experiences over the years, however, that doesn't change the regular gameplay loop that we are forced to endure. There are certain roles that only some people are willing to fulfill, and only so much you can do, and that's okay and kind of the idea of a persistent war game, but that doesn't excuse the shortcomings in other areas that could elevate people's experience. I love this game, the 1k hours of gameplay would tell you enough, but I would not recommend it to anyone in its current state.

Edit: Okay, things have changed a bit. In-game moderation is still player-dependent but we have been equipped with tools and lines of communication that so far have been effective. Even better QOL as well as mechanics overhaul and additions that have made the game more playable.

While I wouldn't recommend it entirely I believe that it's still a relatively enjoyable experience as long as you don't delve too deep into the community.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a veteran of this title with nearly 3800h spent in the game I honestly cannot recommend it.

TLDR:
The Bad:
- no in-game moderation, hardly anyone (if anyone) is dedicated to handling reports, no in-game oversight in cases of cheating.
- developers don't care about the community, interact with it or listen to it.
- secondary accounts are used to enter the enemy faction and can wreak havoc easily with game mechanics allowing that to happen.
- poor new player experience, outdated tutorial.

The Mixed:
- game heavily focused on interacting with people/working with people.
- niche game, might not fit everyone, not really geared towards casual players. (long crafting timers, buildings producing the good stuff need upkeep)
- questionable balancing to say the least.

The Good:
- said niche is rather unique, you are just a single player in the sea of many and need to work with others to achieve anything, giving a realistic experience of being "just a cog in the war machine"
- complicated logistics system is rather interesting, as is the equipment with each faction having entire arsenals unique to them.

The Long Read Edition:
The developers of this game have been ignoring the voices of the community that attempts to highlight the problems with this product, such as:
- The absolute lack of Moderation.
The punishment system in this game consists of other players making reports that might get reviewed and handled... or not, the studio is small so it has a very small (if any) amount of people dedicated to this specific task.
Reports - when spammed in a sufficient amount in-game - can get you automatically banned for a period of time ranging from 30 minutes to about 2 months, making the system prone to abuse, it has been used to vote-ban people before. (allowing groups to target specific individuals or using their numbers to suppress other groups)
You cannot report enemy faction's players nor can you communicate with the enemy faction as the [ALL] chat that allowed it was removed, so if you see a cheater? Send a support ticket and pray.

There are no moderators in-game, there was a system of game moderators that were drawn from the community, however once upon a time one of them decided to become the equivalent of an AC-130 during an enemy operation and unleash hell from above using their ability to fly and spawn any item they wanted.
Needless to say, community moderators ceased to exist shortly after that and devs took on the game moderation upon themselves.
Given how many complaints there are in places like Reddit or even the reviews here, I'll leave you - dear reader - to formulate the opinion on how well they are handling that yourself.
Personally? I would say that I am still waiting for any serious effort to be put into that, but I've quit the game months ago.

- Alting - alting consists of a player buying this game on another Steam account, logging into the enemy faction and harming the other faction via their actions.
For example - alts can ruin days of someone's progress by stealing expensive or time-costly items and despawning (dropping them on the ground until the server cleans them up) them or getting in someone's tank and driving it into the enemy frontline, getting it killed (bear in mind the most powerful tanks can take a literal day to build and supply)
Why? Why not, you won't get punished for it, even when your identity was revealed due to the above report system and moderation issues.

- Cheating - (thankfully) seems to be a minor issue as alting is easier and less risky for a much larger effect.
Before EAC was added to this game, I met a dude that would reduce his respawn timer to 0 and would also blatantly use a wallhack and an aimbot.
I can't tell whether (or if) the introduction of EAC helped in that regard, I hope it did, but if you do meet one, pray that your reports get answered.

- Underwhelming new player experience - When you join the game, you spawn in the Home Island of your faction, where the tutorial area was largely untouched since around 2018, result being that you as a new player can enter the actual game lacking basic knowledge, thankfully there are good people that dedicate their free time to either sitting in world chat or in the Home Island and teaching the newbies actually useful stuff, but that's not always the case and cannot be relied upon.
Personally, I don't mind being thrown into deep water, but I believe everyone deserves to learn the game their way, so a good tutorial is very important.

The lack of communication with the Dev team leads to them adding features that are either completely against what the community asked for or something that the community did not consider more important than more critical issues, like the need for better equipment balance, implementing a system that tackles AFK players taking up server slots and more serious measures being taken against alting and more.

Now this is a mixed bag so it's entirely dependent on how you look at it and individual experience:
- A major part of the game's experience comes from the community - from the minor chats with people to people forming clans and having experiences that draw them closer to each other.
But of course not every social interaction will be like that as the community has severe issues with toxicity and it can range from being great to being one of the most petty and vile.
- The game is rather niche and requires quite a bit of time investment if the player wishes to achieve something "significant".
for example: taking a town can be a lightning fast strike or a WW1 level attrition war with tens of thousands of artillery shells being fired over the course of battle.
This makes the game not particularly geared towards casual players, as just making the resources and producing a decent tank can take hours whereas making a base and upgrading it takes days.
A casual player with say 1-2 hours available each day can get by by doing simpler tasks like playing infantry or joining a clan/receiving equipment for free as sometimes groups make a production run of those and give their surplus away or just hopping aboard a vehicle if the crew finds itself short handed and needs someone to fill a slot.
- Balancing can be hit or miss and the dev team can also be hit or miss regarding how they tackle that.

Now despite the criticism, I would like to point out that the game has good (or also mixed, depending on your perspective) features that can make it enjoyable.

- The game features an interesting and a rather niche form of gameplay with the player character being a minor, individually insignificant part of the world, a "cog in the war machine" that must cooperate with others to achieve anything, for example: to operate a tank, someone has to drive it, someone has to operate the gun, someone has to command (not always) and finally - as is the case with pretty much any combat vehicle - someone has to provide logistical assistance with fuel, repair materials etc, all of which are a result of someone's work, making cooperation and interaction with the community a quintessential part of the game.
- The equipment each faction has is rather unique, while it leads to balancing issues, it is overall interesting with each faction having a relatively unique loadout available to tackle enemy forces with.

Overall, the game has it's moments, but the negatives and how they've been allowed to persist through the years outweigh the positives and thus make me not recommend this title.\t\t\t\t\t
