\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn amazing game pushing the boundaries of MMOs. If I had to describe the feeling this game evokes in one sentence, it would be " the excitement of Factorio with completely random people". It's rare to find a game that calls out to you to keep coming back. But this game shows how one person can absolutely change the tide of one battle and the tide of a war. The way that everything has to be manufactured truly brings a new level of depth to the experience, while still allowing you to play at whatever level you feel comfortable at.

Be the brave soldier still standing while your comrades are respawning. Be the medic keeping a whole push alive, Be the scavenger bringing back supplies so your base doesn't run out. Be the builder digging trenches so your comrades don't have to hide behind trees, or build emplacements manned by the base AI to stop a catastrophic push.

Got a friend? Be the tank crew turning the tide of battle. Be the mortar/artillery crew making the other side want to quit the front and fight somewhere else. Get a small boat and do some partisan shenanigans.

This is the game where you can have moments like the last soldier surviving in the forest with an AT grenade and not being noticed by the tanks or infantry as you throw the grenade and disable the tank, saving your base at the cost
of your life. You can experience the true horror of sieges as you fight to the last respawn.

Now of course there is still much to be desired from the developers in terms of balance, some mechanical changes like logistics and building QOL. But I've seen a lot of innovative games, and Foxhole is an actual playable environment, unlike games like Elite: Dangerous, this game is both a mile wide and a mile deep. I can't wait to see what else Siege Camp has in store.\t\t\t\t\t
