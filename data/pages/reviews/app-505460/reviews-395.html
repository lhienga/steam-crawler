I'm closing in on almost a year of fighting endless wars across the unforgiving lands of Caovia, and while the game is not without it's problems I can look past a lot of them because of how fun this game is. And that's the biggest thing that people seem to leave out in other reviews: In its barest form, Foxhole oozes fun. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere can I even begin with this game...

This game as a concept is incredible. An MMO where hundreds of players work seamlessly together in a total war with a fleshed out logistics system, fun tank combat, etc. Unfortunately, the concept is the only thing holding it together.

Lets start with the bugs. The god awful bugs. Do you like not being able to speak to other players in your world? Do you like relogging or restarting your game up to 5 times to solve an error? Perhaps you also like getting stuck on nothing or dying because the game wouldn't accept a command in time. This game is a bug fest. Usually nothing game breaking, but I swear every single session I would encounter some sort of annoying bug. I have played this game for a year now and none of these have been fixed.

Next lets address the game design, which I think is the biggest flaw of this game. Logistics is a primary focus of this game, with raw materials needing to be mined, processed, then have their processed materials transported to factories for manufacturing then finally moved from manufacturing to the front line. This is a complex system that currently relies entirely on a few select people doing most of the work and the vast majority not contributing. After all, who would want to log into a game and spend 2 hours clicking on a pile or driving around trucks that handle like garbage? This means you end up with a few people doing most of the work for the entire war effort.

In fact, this system got so bad that the entire segment of players that do logi came together and went on strike because the devs were actively making the system worse than it already was. The devs finally relented as they observed the entire segment of their playerbase that keeps the game running take off and hurt the entire experience. My personal conspiracy is that the already struggling engine could not survive a logistics system that allowed bases to always be well stocked and supported, and that players truly enjoyed working in. There would simply be too much stuff for it to handle, so by making logistics inherently tedious and annoying they can artificially prevent this from ever becoming a problem.

Next up, game population! Unfortunately, the devs haven't been able to make more than around 100 players on both sides operate on the same server. This means the entire map is broken up into regions which each have their own population and performance. This leads to some huge huge issues, primarily in logistics. Since the majority of the playerbase isn't interested in participating in the boring logisitcs loop, this means most players entering a theatre in a high population part of the day don't actually bring supplies with them! This leads to constant calls from those fighting within the region in question for supplies from the backlines. But even if someone does go load up a truck with supplies, they can't actually deliver them to the region because it is at capacity! So you basically enter a queue and wait for 10-20 minutes until you can finally enter the region.

Waiting is a constant in this game. Waiting for supplies to process, waiting for trucks to be loaded, waiting for queues to be empty, etc. This game takes your time apologetically, and most people actually watch videos or play podcasts while they do this to prevent going insane.

Another gigantic issue is faction balancing. Each faction has its quirks, and neither is really more powerful than the other. However, no one believes this and the alternate faction is always more powerful in their eyes. The big issue the tech tree the devs have implemented. This tech tree changes every war and different items are received at different times. This creates some massive issues because one faction will get a tech early that the other faction has no counter for, changing the entire dynamic of the battlefield. The devs do this purposely to stir up drama and prevent things staying the same.

Anyways, there are a thousand other issues with this game concerning community, gameplay loops, stablity, politics, censorship, and more. Unfortunately I am tired of this game and am angry at myself for putting so many hours into it. I am afraid I will be uninstalling it and moving on. This game can really be fun sometimes and if the basic issues were solved it could be truly great.\t\t\t\t\t
