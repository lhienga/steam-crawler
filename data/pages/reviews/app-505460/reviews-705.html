\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't write many reviews but I have seriously been enjoying this game so I decided I would write one.

The game concept is pretty unique. You can join the Wardens or the Colonials and once you choose one you are in that faction for the remainder of the war.
Now the war: The two factions fight each other 24 hours a day. 7 Days a week, even on Christmas, until the war is won.
The actual gameplay is top down on your soldier. Your soldier is a regular guy. There are no heroes in this game. Every player is just a soldier fighting in the war.
One of the most intriguing things is that the war effort is very all encompassing. Soldiers have to do everything from mine the resources to make the tanks, guns, bombs and ammo to building defenses to actually producing and delivering supplies to the frontlines. Logistics is an important part of the game. Players need to organize themselves to not only produce enough resources but also to get them where they are needed. The enemy will try to stop this by doing raids on logistics routes and mines so it is important to think carefully before taking that truck full of ammo to the front. "Is the road safe, Does Eastmarch need this more?"
The map is massive and it will truly feel like a you are a single soldier fighting in a giant war. Most wars you won't even see half the map personally. While you are offline other players take up the mantle.
Combat is tactical and deep. Vehicles, various weapons and support equipment make the game fairly complex to keep things interesting.

Most of this has been praise but there are a few glaring issues with the game. First I'll preface it by saying that this game is early access but is 100% playable and has few bugs and none game breaking.
In game communication. There is local and group voice chat as well as various different text chats but in a game that is all about team work the game really lacks any way to meaningfully organize your men. There are squads but that is the extent of the organization.
Balance: I don't see this as a huge issue but lots of players do: The factions are asymetrical and during the beta which we are in the devs are constantly changing and implementing new tech and vehicles which makes the game feel unbalanced. For example: recently the wardens won 6 wars in a row! Now the devs have buffed the colonials faction to the max and it feels as though the wardens are fighting an unfair fight. I'm sure this will be fixed but for some it can be frustrating.
Grindy, The game feels grindy in some aspects. Your team needs to do logistics to win the war but a lot of the logistics tasks are very time consuming, like mining for resources and driving trucks around. I think this could be reduced to an extent and I see it as a negative. Railways are an upcoming thing on the devs mind so that may solve this.

TLDR: Good game. 100% playable early access. Not for a casual gamer. Very realistic persistent war simulator.\t\t\t\t\t
