\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWho am I to foxhole? A longtime community veteran. I have, in the past, run a "Military Academy," where I organized with other vets to create videos, guides and the like in multiple languages for new and returning players alike. I have a lot of long lasting and deep connections to players on/in both factions.

Foxhole is a game unlike any other. It's a game that one can easily sink hundreds, if not thousands of hours into. There is a thriving community online of players and "retired" veterans alike who still communicate with one another about the game.

Years ago when I started playing, if you'd have asked me if I enjoyed the game, I'd have given a resounding yes. This game is everything a war gamer could ever want, and more, for the first 20 hours. Then you start to understand some of the issues affecting the game, and for the first little while... It's fine. You learn to love the jank. You learn to work around the issues and play the game for what it is.

However, a couple years back, for various reasons (some of which I find justified, others which I do not,) the developers outright and explicitly stopped communicating with the community, they dropped their community manager, and now only communicate on exceedingly rare occasions and through the foxhole official discord, despite having their own website and their own subreddit where communication would be much more visible and effective.

This has led to a downward spiral of community toxicity. I don't think it's right, but I understand where it has come from. The lack of communication and in game moderation from the developers has lead to the players becoming increasingly factionalized and aggressive to those in their own faction they perceive as griefing and people of the other faction at large over perceived developer bias affecting game play. (IE, a buff to one faction is never properly explained, leading people to make snide comments about dev pet factions, etc, rather than the devs getting ahead of the buff/nerf with data and explaining the WHY)

Another issue this has lead to is a feeling that the developers are out of touch with the reality of their own game. They continue to make the systems around logistics and building increasingly difficult, complex and time consuming. A single person can no longer effectively do anything they want. While this does encourage team play, it also encourages factionalism, tribalism within each faction, and hardcore resource hoarding and toxicity. The devs are creating systems that no-one asked for, while neglecting quality of life updates that the community has been begging for over literal years

Foxhole as it currently stands today is still a fundamentally functional game. However, over the years certain systems have built up to the point that they have become miserable and unfun to interact with. Logistics and building have become increasingly complex, well beyond anything anyone in the community ever really asked for. The solo player is relegated to frontline combat or hoping that they can find a squad or clan or group participating in the activity they would like to do, otherwise they will find themselves ineffective.

You can absolutely still have fun in this game, and I still often do, but I have long since stopped recommending the game to friends as I genuinely believe the game is no longer worth the time and energy one needs to invest to find that fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn an ever changing "REAL" conflict game in-which no two wars are the same (abar for shard one and meta players ;) ). you can visit different ever expanding or declining fronts offering players a huge variety of play styles in a differing locations with FRIENDS and strangers forcing people to come together to adapt and over come different obstacles the game throws in your path. Foxhole is probably one of the closest things we have to a "REAL" war game, but unfortunately where this game falls down on its sword, are some of the things that truly set it apart from other games of the same genre.

You can see how the developers have tried to make things move at different speeds with different techniques for instance having to farm then craft then ship goods and supplies slows a front down. making it so unless players are willing to sacrifise their time they will be fighting a loosing battle to those who do but also creating artificial shortages, much like what we can imagine would of been experienced in war times. which then leads me to my point where this game falls down is that most wars start off with a lot of players and end with a lot of players but in the mean time players come and go and this leads to decaying of bases and the constant upkeep of bases with garrison supplies and that then eating into players actual lives as if your not on within a set period of time your supplies go public and your base decays. (again from the developers side i can see the issue where players will farm a lot and then stop playing and those resources are forever in limbo inaccessible by the current player base who may need them)

Also seeing alot of other issues where players who are either older players who have lost interest or people buying multiple accounts and games being able to "sabotage" the enemy team by joining with no intention of helping but to hinder and costing people hundreds of hours of work with no real punishment.

Added in facilities with the last major update which is all good and well when everyone works together how its insinuated but unfortunately players seem to have a main character syndrome and why would i want to spend literal hours of my real life time when i can let the one guy do all the work and piggy back off of the success helping myself to all the proceeds whilst you see the same few people doing the same boring monotonous work over and over for hours everyday just to "maintain" what they've already spent hours building and then eventually dying off never to be seen in the game again for some other poor player to come and take their place. Wrongly assuming this facility update would be able to give those people a break and they've now ended up having to spend even longer doing the same tasks with no real reward other than to upkeep a necessary base.

Other things that could be much simpler to address like terrain tool usage and leaving trees blocking roads and pathways hindering players who are the ones who do most of the core work in the game.

Over all its a great game honestly one of a kind! Everything you would want it to do for a casual it does perfectly (gun play, movement, HUD) but it does need some more heads coming together from the development team and the community as ways to address the real issues this game is currently experiencing. I personally don't want do down vote as i see hope and potential and do truely love the game! good luck game developers! please listen to your community! Not all just a few!\t\t\t\t\t
