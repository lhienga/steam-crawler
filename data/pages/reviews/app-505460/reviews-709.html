\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: as of the writing of this review i stopped playing, went back, and then stopped for a year. virtually none of these problems have been fixed, and they removed crucial BASIC weapons from the colonials, while letting the wardens keep them.

do not buy this

tl:dr: bad dev, dont buy.

I want to recommend this game to you. I want to tell you its a great idea executed well, that the gameplay is solid, that the game is well on its way to being finished, and that the core mechanics function beautifully. i want to, but momma didn't raise a liar.

you can see how much time i put into this game, i WANTED it to be good, i TRIED to enjoy myself. but i cant.

The game has promise, the concept is great. who wouldn't want a massive war with hundreds of players? who wouldn't want to be able to build the defences themselves, design the bunkers and trenches, bring the supplies to the frontline? but here's the problem, doing any of that is tedious at best, and pointless at worst.


the first major problem is that nothing is explained. the bare basics "heres a rifle, heres how you use it, heres a factory, craft a crate of guns, heres a car, drive" thats what you get in a tutorial. thats ALL you get in a tutorial. want to build a massive concrete bunker complex? want to make the maginot line look like a sandcastle by comparison? good luck with figuring out how it all works.

building a base requires supplies to maintain it to keep it from decaying, which wont even help until it reaches tier two. how does it reach tier two? nobody actually knows. you set spawn, click that you would like it to be tier two soon, and wait. do respawns help? depends on who you ask. does supplying it make the time to tier two shorter? depends on who you ask. is building near it helpful? maybe. its never explained by the devs, never outlined anywhere with concrete info, so the only option is asking in chat. ask in chat and you get 5 different answers from 10 different people, in-between the usual bickering you get in local chat already. so have fun deciding who is right, who is wrong, and who is just screwing with you.

in order to even build your base, you have to tirelessly hammer each bunker to upgrade it, shovel the ground to even built it to begin with, and to top that off, concrete it all. concrete is made with a mixer, it takes a rare resource, and slowly makes concrete over time, once it is teched. concrete does not stack. a full inventory of 9 concrete, over encumbers you, making you walk like a geriatric turtle. every structure upgraded takes anywhere from 20-75 concrete. so have fun humping concrete back and forth for literal hours, once you finally get the concrete upgrade on your base, which can take DAYS to unlock. by the time i was mostly finished my second major base build in my third war, i was expecting the game to ask me to buy gems to speed it up.

in order to keep your base stocked, you need supply,you need respawn materials (shirts) guns, ammo, base supplies (garrisons/bunker supplies) and all the other bits and pieces a soldier might need when they spawn in. to get them someone somewhere has to have made a crate of the stuff, so you can bring it to your base by truck. if you are lucky, your truck wont wig out and get stuck on its nose, or wig out on a bridge and blow up in the lake.

Front line combat should be this games saving grace. surely you can avoid those bits and just grab a rifle and have a blat shooting the enemy. but that gets into some of the even bigger problems. early game, its just rifleman slogging it out against each other, which can be fun once you get grenades, as spamming them is a blast (pun intended). the problem however is that there is no command structure. rank means nothing but "hey ive been here a long time and did stuff" so nobody cares. have a clear way to flank the enemy? enjoy telling everyone at the front that and watching them not give a sh*t. fighting at a bridge? enjoy everyone running across it over and over, dying in the same spot, and accomplishing diddly squat. until decent armour is unlocked the front lines are largely a stalemate, once it gets unlocked, the wardens win again.

that gets into the tech tree. the tech tree is asymmetrical, so each side has different vehicles, that serve different functions. this means, one is objectively better then the other, and one side doesnt get the good one. the devs know this. they know which vehicles are OP, and which are not, they just dont care. the colonials get open top armoured cars and a tank destroyer with the engine on the front, oh, and also an open top. enjoy getting disabled in 1-2 shots or 1 sticky, and getting a grenade dropped on your head. the colonials get a siege tank with barely any range, and they recently took away armour piercing mortar rounds and main battle tanks. the wardens? would you like a heavily armoured assault tank? with two main guns? that can outrun a light tank? thank can reverse on rough grass faster then a naked man with a sticky grenade? enjoy! would you like that fixed and made less over the top? enjoy getting bent, thats the only option you have.

on a totally utterly unrelated note, the wardens have won 6 out of the last 7 wars, since the new tech update and the silverhand. im sure thats not indicative of anything, as the wardens are keen to frantically assure me every time i mention it.

the teching system ensures that the tech tree will never work properly. whoever farms the most tech materials (iron and aluminium) gets to sue it to vote towards a tech, so if the choice was between a machinegun and a rubber mallet, and someone scrapped enough, guess what we get to bounce off the enemy helmets? enjoy not being able to use global chat (remember, thats where you get literally all your info on how things actually work) as everyone bickers about which tech is actually worth teching, and the good stuff loses half the time. incidentally, local chat is also where you hear about enemies behind your lines, attacking logi, destroying towers, or maybe just up the road from you about to turn you into a bullet receptical. have fun trying to keep track in between all the pointless bickering, since nobody moderates this game.


did i mention nobody moderates this game? thats another massive problem. have someone alting, taking stuff and tossing it in a river, stealing tanks and destroying them, etc? report them, and nothing happens. if enough people report the same person, they get a 2 hour ban, so enjoy being brigaded for pissing off a clan one time, while every troll and griefer takes a 2 hour nap, comes back, and does it all over again.

to top all this off, lots of bugs that existed in my first war in september, exist now in november, so enjoy having to relog to fix them, while in the midst of heated frontline conflict, or busy with an operation. what happens when you relog at the front line? well in backline, you relog and go back to where you were before. frontline? you go to the home island, and you have to wait in the long 5-8 person queue to get to the busy frontline region you were literally just in. hope you werent doing anything important, or didnt have a very good spot you were defending from, or a useful set of gear that the front was low on replacements for!


I would love for this game to reach its potential, to get where it needs to be, to be a finished product i could enjoy a lot. but the devs dont listen to user feedback, they dont respond to reports on players, and as far as i can tell, they actively picked a side int he wars, and arent keen on helping anyone else. i wasted almost 1000 hours of my life trying to have fun, trying to make the most of this game, and wanting it to be as great as it COULD BE. it will never be that great, it will never get fixed. maybe buy it on sale if its low as dirt, and you want to play casually now and then? but if you want a long term game to really get into, save yourself the heartache, and go elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

After a week I joined a clan and we were having a good time. I took a VAC ban for no reason. Neither steam nor the developers were able to do anything to correct this. Best simulator of war and one of best experiences you can get while playing with your friends. Also interaction with other players is quite fun and there is a lot of space for roleplay. You simply get here realistic experience and feel of war. Game is very detailed and you have to contribute to war either as resource gatherer, manufacturer of war equipment, logistic to the front, engineer building bases, defences or digging trenches. It is stunnishing how many options you have in this game to turn the outcome of the war.

Devs seems to add content very often and are very active. Since I bought the game more than year ago, game have changed a lot / in a possitive way.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first review, don't really know where to start.

Been playing this game since pre-alpha versions. It had its up's and down's as in every other early access games.
Concept and imagination of this game is wonderful, however the realization is terrible with every new patch.
When playing this game you had a feeling of belonging somewhere and doing something to the progression of the war.
Nowdays (past year) developers are inclined to making wars last longer at all cost. And that cost is in my opinion was too much.
The 'frontline' infantry combat is kinda destroyed totally. 80% of gameplay is stalemate due to shear amount of automatic defences you opponents can build in this game. Thus resulting that little active frontline you have turns out to be just PvE.
Logistic for (not-clan members) is also kinda destroyed. For example if you wish to play with a tank, you have to first farm out a large amount of resources (we talk about hours in game time, just to gather), process those said resources,
then take em to vehicle factory in order to build said tank. But that's not all, also you have to farm other types of resources to fill that tank, with necessary stuff. Shells, machine gun ammo, fuel etc. It also takes a long time to bring the tank to the front, now comes the best part - After all that effort (which can easily go even up to 10 hours or more, due to large clans hogging all the resources) you can get blow up in matter of seconds, due to infantry moving faster than majority of armor in this game, and with few stick grenades bye bye your effort. And that's actually good part, at least you lost it in combat. The bad part is since you cant 'store' or 'safely park' or ' lock' your vehicle anywere. If you don't watch it 24/7 it usually gets stolen in first 1 hour of you leaving it somewhere. And lest face it, no one can play 10+ hours just to build something and then say good bye to it.
While we are at subject of stealing, game moderators work is usually bad, the occasional abuse of power here and there i can understand, but not to even bother to kick players who grief/teamkill/insult you/steal your stuff/ etc i cant understand. Even when you go full on support in making tickets they require of you, 90% of the time is thrown in water. But don't worry because YOU can get permanently banned for some irrelevant or not worthy punishing reasons.
(never been personally banned, but for someone who doesn't care about those things, shear amount i've heard about, makes me wonder)
On population and community in this game i also feel its in tremendous decline,
frontline destroyed - no players who like frontline.
Logy destroyed - no players who like logy.
Suporting clases - witout frontline and logy who are they gonna support? - no those players too.

The only players still playing are those hard core fanatics who doesn't have anything better to play and do.
And players who come to see friends and kill few hours in the game, since game is based on huge amount of co-operation and combined effort of multiple players, you cant but make few friends here and there.

So in the end for conclusion:
- Is the game fun? - Yes, it still is, i'm one of those fanatics, its good time killer.
- Should you BUY it? - NO, Absolutely no. Maybe in a few years if developers decide to listen to gaming community, instead of trying to block and slow everything. Maybe.
p.s. Devs do say they listen and talk with community - but their view of community is 10-20 like-minded persons. So NO, they don't listen to community. Don't fall for that trick. They also do sorta- weekly streams where they have Question and Answers at the end, but if its question they don't wanna answer you can ask it 20 times, they will just miss to read it. And i'm not even gonna start with wasting 30+ min of every stream on anime/hentai pictures.\t\t\t\t\t
