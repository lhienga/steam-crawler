\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole remains a pretty unique game.

After quite a few more hours, a lot more experience gained, and a whole lot more stuff being added/changed,
I still believe it is worthy of your time and money, let me elaborate.

First off, don't be intimidated by bad reviews claiming atrocious behaviour, like purposeful team killing, mass reporting, etc. Such occurances are incredibly rare and as long as you're not a , you'll do just fine.

So, where to start? Foxhole is a expansive top-down view shooter, where the massive war is fueled by the players, for the players. Adding a whole new level of deph simply unheard of in any other game since, for good or bad logistics can alter the outcome of entire wars. So, everytime you find someone firing a gun (that is not a pistol) you can think, "Someone made that gun, and that round." This was the selling factor for me, but maybe you need a bit more convincing.

The game is divided between two factions. The Colonial Republic, and The Warden Empire. Such factions all have their cultures, doctrines and little niches into them. The Colonials are designed to be offensive and mobile, their culture is based off of the Roman Empire, and their equipment, specially their uniforms and trucks resemble U.S army equipment. The Wardens are designed to be more defensive and focused, they have gaelic/germanic cultural elements and their equipment is similar to european nations, and they dress up like French soldiers from ww1. Now, they are not based in reality, the world and setting are completely fictional, and there are no good guys, neither bad guys, only the war.

Foxhole offers a large veriety of playstyles and roles one can fill. Be it in combat or logistics, there's a spot for everyone.
In combat, you can be a:
Average grunts, that can have many, many weapons to choose from, machine guns to rifles, submachine guns, grenades, gas grenades, amongst others.
There are also many land vehicles, from Jeeps with mounted heavy machineguns, to RPG mounts, tankettes, modular half-tracks, light tanks, SPGs and assault tanks.
There are also naval vehicles, such as Gunboats, barges and vessels that are able to carry equipment for landing operations.
And finally, the artillery. Both sides have 150mm heavy artillery cannons and the massive, 300mm storm cannons. However, the Colonial possess a 120mm field artillery cannon that is quite effective.

In logistics, you can be a:

Scrooper, that with manual labour, extracts and refines salvage, sulfur, or components for the manufactory of basic materials, explosive materials, and refined materials, respectively.
Producer, that takes the refined resources and utilizes them in the production of guns and ammunition, explosive ammunition and gas grenades and finally, grenade launchers and combat vehicles, respectively.
Hauler, that upon taking the produced goods, that come in crates with varying ammounts of goods, can put them into large containers to be shipped to supply depots or seaports, that will diminish the travel time of frontline suppliers. Haulers can also haul resource containers filled with raw resources, that will ease the production of large quantities of such resources.
Frontline Supplier, that takes his trusty truck, loads it with useful gear and drives to the base closest to the frontline, where the men will have access to the equipment provided.

That's a overview of the roles this game has to offer, and there are many others. However, this review is long enough as it is. So, yeah, just buy Foxhole. You will most likely not regret it, at all.
