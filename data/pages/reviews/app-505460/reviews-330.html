Edit:
After hundreds of hours later, i can confidently say that Foxhole is one of my favourite and most beloved games of all time. VERY few games have such a special place in my heart.

Whether you want a chaotic war simulation with lots of action, or a relaxing way to spend your time while listening to a podcast, this game has you covered. It is pretty cool actually, because sometimes in the backlines, you can forget that a brutal war is even going on, it is so dynamic!

The community has some bad apples (like every community on Earth, afterall) but those are vastly outweighed by the solidarity the war encourages. Everyone has a common purpose, to win the war. If players help each other out, they help the war effort. I enjoy logistics, and occasionaly make tanks for people who just want to fight without sinking hours into making their expensive toys. Even if i dont get to see the fights those tanks get into, i know that i have made their crews happy and helped push the enemy back in the process. Cooperation is a win-win situation, and everybody knows it!

If you are willing to sink (a lot of) hours into this experience, i urge you to not be swayed by the reviews mocking the community and the fact that many features seem "locked" behind teamwork. I was almost a victim of such reviews myself. Listening to them would be such a huge and frustrating mistake..

Oh and, go Colonial, FOR THE LEGION!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, let me get to the point... this game is awesome, and you will hate it.
Which it is at the time, depends on the outcome of your fights at the time.
I have ragequit/"alt+F4"ed more than I want to admit to in this game, but I still refuse to uninstall every time, because I know better times will come.

There propably is a gameplayloop for everyone.
Wanna collect ressources alla Runescape? This game got it.
Wanna craft all sorts of ♥♥♥♥? Yupo, the game's got that too. Or do you want to build up an intricate(ish) bunker network? You guessed it. You can do this in the game.
You can drive tanks and other vehicles, both for logistics and for actice war.
You can be a medic, like I am doing currently, and try your damndest to keep your allies alive... sometimes more successfull, sometimes less successfull.
Or, you can be a bracve frontline soldier, that risks his live actively engageing with the enemies in honorable (most of the times) combat.
Or indeed you can be a partisan, and go behind the enemy lines, to disrupt and sabotage the enemies logistics.

Give the game a try of at least 10 hours, try every gameplay loop thoroughly. Just because one isnt for you, doesnt mean the game overall isn't.

Edit to mention: The tutorial is VERY basic... if you don'T know something, just use voicechat to ask and you most likely will find someone that'll be at least willing to help where they can\t\t\t\t\t
