\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell At first I thought I hated this game and I was going to refund it, It was a great concept but annoying gameplay mechanics.
But then I just kept coming back, I usually hate games that involve grind but it didn't feel right to use up supplies on the frontline without knowing the effort that the other plays went through to get them there. After figuring some logi out and getting frustrated watching people suicide our supplies, I started asking what senior ranked people were doing and joining in on their adventures and before I knew it I was talking *** while we took towns by tank lines, desperately calling out enemy positions , covering while our allies refuelled and did some field repairs. This was some solid hours of gameplay and I should have stopped playing there. But I was curious and logged back In some hours later to see what happened to the region we claimed.

There was a solid base starting to be built and I inquired how I could help. The Logistic hero’s listened to our requests and also using the claimed materials from the enemy’s desperate retreat; we setup a huge fort town, the head builder explained to me how it was optimised for quick upgrading. Of course I thought I'd be able to log off, but our cement hadn’t even hardened yet and the retaliation dribble turned into waves. Taking up a left behind tank I was now commanding randoms on how to use its quirks to your advantage (After watching some fellow noobs bravely suicide our only other tank into the enemy lines.)
I was making a difference as we worked together adapting to everything the enemy threw at us. I felt a genuine camaraderie with the main builder and his friends. We held out against earth shattering artillery, tank pushes and our supply routes getting cut off. I learned and listened and it felt genuinely epic as we somehow managed to defend despite the overwhelming numbers and weaponry upon us. Some enemies were notably cunning in their approach but they were few amongst the majority of waves impaling themselves on our bayonets and donating their ammo and supplies. But those wiser rivals meant there was a tension, they didn’t take big risks and we felt the slowly tightening a grip on us as dry cement became a distant dream. That base was destroyed by various means and rebuilt in desperation several times. In the end the I imagine the loss wasn’t fun for the common player who hadn’t invested all the time into this changed landscape, and I imagine they went to repeatedly suicide over a bridge somewhere else. It felt like we were left alone as a hail of explosions and gunfire seemed to hit everywhere but our lone tank and the head builder. In the end we tried retreating with what resources we had but we were surrounded. We spawned back in the home region just to breathe, then said our goodbyes and logged off because the sun had risen in the real world.

For the next day or so there were many people I encountered who talked about the epic, but failed defence. “You were there!? Man that was something!” So I highly recommend this game if you like teamwork, it’s almost impossible to navigate success alone especially with all the quirks of gameplay that I still curse to this day.

ART OF WAR
This game captures the essence of war, from logistics to building infrastructures and to the front line, (i haven't tried them all in actual war tho)

the game is immersive and very fun, learning curve is not that steep so you can learn everything even in the tutorial area; it all comes down to your game sense, utilizing information and tactics.

why you wanna buy the game?

  • doesn't have a skill tree so no skill snowballing purely based on tactics and teamwork
  • variety of activities, from being in logistics delivering and producing weapons ammos and vehicles even garrison and soldier supplies to the front line, becoming a medic that makes noobs alive, building infrastructures and fortifications to establish defensive positions and being a noob in front line dying for like 15 times in a row.
  • if you have a regiment/clan TACTICS and TEAMWORK comes into place, yes those things you've learned in HOI4, total war and u geek reading the art of war is highly applicable.


cons
  • ♥♥♥♥ tons of griefers
  • lots of noobs dying and consuming your base supplies leaving their equipments and ammos in the front
  • kinda makes you short sighted.
