\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a tricky game to discuss. For a lot of people it's this epic PVP warfest that can go for weeks with the front shifting constantly, others it's a grind-fest plagued with griefers and cheaters hampering all the fun. So for me I'll give my won 2 cents from how I've seen it go.

Early to mid-game is this fast dynamic battlefield, people throwing ideas out at the wall to see if it will work. Barges of riflemen running down the shore because there are no gunboats yet, massive zergs of grenadiers rushing bases because there is no big heavy tanks or artillery to fill that role, or massive columns of men trying to flanking bases before AI tech makes them into nothing but shoes and goo. This is the fun time.
But as we go later into the game this stops, lines either stagnant or breakdown to the point a whole hex can be overran because so much time and resources are stretched just to keep the front line intact. Anything other than the highest tech'd resource heavy armor gets used and anything not that tends to stick in the back lines, unwilling or unable to move forward to push a point. The game turns into attrition where the first gun fired might be the only one needed. People get burnt out, others turn to sabotage, fingers get pointed, morale shifts on a dime, clans abandon sites.
Could the devs do more? Absolutely. Both sides suffer from a litany of issues that can be addressed, people have reported what the issues are, have offered up solutions to help, but there has been no dialogue. If this review tells you anything it's this: It's fun at the start but the longer you dig your heels in, the less enjoyable it probably will become.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, honestly one of my top 5's. The immersion of playing as just one cog in a machine of thousands; working together, communicating, seeing honest-to-goodness supply-chains come together through team spirit, coordinated pushes into enemy bases, tanks rolling over enemy line while supported by infantry and artillery fire, the list goes on. Many real-world World War I and II tactics actually work in this game, and it makes for an experience like no other.
The proximity chat adds another layer of immersion and community that is sorely lacking in today's gaming industry. You'll hear allies calling out enemy positions and emerging threats as the battle evolves, injured soldiers shouting for medics, and machine gunners pleading for ammo as they use their last clip as sparingly as possible. The further away someone is, the quieter they are, so coms are (usually :P ) not too crowded. Another added bonus of the voice chat is that when you start playing and have no idea what you're doing, just ask. There's always someone eager to help out.
As you've probably seen already, every item and vehicle (and many structures) are created by players. There's something special about passing by a factory that's in full swing; cranes loading and unloading cargo ships, raw materials being fed to factories that spit out rifles, bullets, uniforms, diesel, petrol, gas masks, tanks, and a constant flow of trucks bringing the much needed gear to the front line.
Because of this, every battle matters. If your faction fails to defend a factory, then your side looses not just everything that wasn't evacuated in time, but the ability to effectively hold that region without straining surrounding territories. But again, you're just one cog in the machine, so fights manage to feel meaningful without also putting too much pressure on any one player. It can be a hard balance for a developer to achieve, and I think Foxhole's scale helped Siege Camp to nail this. (Not to subtract from their ability/achievement)

Foxhole is a unique game that offers a one-of-a-kind experience, and I could go on for quite some time about all the things I love about it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole i love you and i hate you.

One thousand hours within a single year and only now did my eyes open.

Lets begin at the start. You freshly bought the game. Installed it and fired it up.
Greeted by the option to join either one of the two factions Warden and Colonial.
It doesn't really matter which faction you choose and once selected you will enter a game unlike any other.

The posibillities are endless for it all is one huge sandbox in which you can do what you fancy at the time.
Front line warfare, gathering resources, weapon production, vehicle production, base building, trench digging, tank driving artillery firing, or just be good 'ol cannon fodder, you name it.

You can do anything you fancy for all those things have to be done by someone. Let me explain.
Every iten in the game has been build by either you or another player. Made by materials people gathered from the map.
Want a box of standard ammunition? The poduction process goes as follows
1. Raw scrap material is farmed at the scrap field.
2. Transported by truck towards the refinery.
3. Processed into basic materials.
4. Basic materials are then transported towards a factory.
5. Factory turns the basic materials into what ever product you so desire, this time we choose 7.62 rifle ammo.
6. Receive the crated ammo which is then driven by truck to where ever it is needed.

As you can see this process is lengthy and very time consuming and requires effort.
Players spending hours upon hours driving back and forth making items and hauling them to the front lines to be used either by you or your fellow faction players.

Yet within the system there lies a problem.

Public stockpiles.
As the name would suggest. Public stockpiles are places in which crated materials and vehicles can be put for later use.
This is either in a storage depot or a seaport. Both physical buildings within the game of which there are many.
Simply drive your logistics truck up to it. Click upon the desired items the front lines require and and drive it back for players to use. Anyone of the same faction has access to it. Which also means those who bought the game with ill intent.

And out of all the hours i have played have i neved seen as much ill intent combined as this latest war.
The problem? Alt accounts. Freshly created steam accounts, which have purchased a copy of the game to actively sabotage the opposite faction. Is your main faction losing? Then the alts wreak havoc by gathering as much valuable things they can from the public stockpiles only to destroy them by dumping it all into the sea. An act done in less than a blink of an eye with hours and hours of time and effort wasted of the player who created it. Countless of video evidence has been submitted of acts such as these to the developers. Tanks which take hours to build, Containers filled to the brim with stuff and pallets of artillery shells dumped into the water by non ranked ( level 0 ) players. Accounts that have been reported time and time again for such deeds still free to do the things they do without being perma banned. The problem goes completely ignored.

As an all round player doing front line warfare and logistics this is infuriating. Knowing that all the time spend can be just wasted away or handed out to the opposite faction for their own advantage in a matter of minutes when no one else is looking.

Until the developers come up with a working solution for these problems my review will remain negative, of which i had no joy of writing this.

For the game is so nice if only the developers cared more about it.\t\t\t\t\t
