\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will preface this by saying I do not like MMO's, never really been a fan of them and clearly not my thing. I can understand why some people like them but I usually prefer the closed multiplayer experience with just my friends. I bought this game on a whim because my friend who plays more MMO's than I, wanted someone to play with on it and it wasn't too expensive. My only reason for hesitance is that this game is like Rust where the server runs even when you're not online meaning all your progress can be destroyed without any real way of you doing anything about it. Plus you also have to log in everyday for a couple of hours cause if you don't it's likely your base is just gonna be destroyed.

With that out of the way, I can firmly tell you this is currently one of my favorite games in my steam library right now. I have since more than doubled my friend's play time and I can only find myself enjoying this game more and more. It's a good mix of serious and casual fun in my opinion where everyone puts a lot of effort into fighting but voice comms is filled with people acting out getting shot and it's hilarious. 90% of the people I have interacted with, have been kind and as helpful as they can be to someone who just got the game (especially the dude who explained how being a medic works to me whilst we were being rained with mortars). On top of that, coordinating a plan with the more experienced players is fun and easy too, often times they will call for people and you just have to say you're willing to follow their plan. I can't think of how many times I've been called over to help perform a push or help get ammo for a mortar barrage.

Even though my side in the current war is losing, it's still great fun and even more rewarding when you actually claim land back or even hold the line against an enemy push. Logistics is pretty fun as well in my opinion, it's like seeing a place that really needs supplies and trying to create a map to avoid backline enemies that will try to intercept your supplies. However I think my most enjoyable part of playing this game for me is building trenches and playing medic. Building trenches is fun because you're usually thinking of how to layout trenches in a way that allows people to circulate through without getting bottlenecked but with enough angles so that enemies can't shoot straight down them, all whilst being fired upon. Medic is fun because you're running around in the trenches with a basic gun and medical supplies reviving and healing people that either get gassed, shot or exploded by a grenade. You also get to hear a lot of people act being injured which as noted before usually ends up being pretty funny.

This game does a good job of making you understand that one person can't win a war alone but one person is part of the effort to win the war. What I mean is that there are a lot of big tasks that one person can't do alone reasonably or even at all however the little tasks that make up the big tasks feel just as rewarding. In the case of a medic, I might have rushed out in the open during an enemy push to pick up an ally and then get him to a trench to revive him. From that revival he might have anti-tank grenades on him that we use against the enemy tank to halt the push with the assistance from the rest of our allies. In that context, I wasn't powerful enough as a medic to destroy the tank and the guy with anti-tank gear was caught in the open when trying to change position, neither of us would have had the shot to survive the tank without each other's actions. The small action of reviving one player lead to that one player being able to take another shot at eliminating the tank which effectively halted the enemy push. One small one person action lead to another small action which lead to a team effort of halting a push. Which as a whole makes me feel that I am not completely useless on my own but that I need to rely on my team if we wish to get the big things done.

This game also does a good job of making my time feel valuable, unlike Rust where I feel as though I have to spend 4 hours everyday to actually make some sort of sense of progress (only for it to be destroyed a couple of hours later), I feel like I can get a fair bit done in only one hour of logging into this game. There's usually more than enough stuff that needs to be built or logistics to be run that you can get done in under an hour. The destruction of buildings and constructions thing is still there (and arguably worse than Rust in that it can be destroyed within minutes of it being built by mortars and tanks) but it's easy enough with the help of your team to build up quickly. It's punishing to lose it, but it's not punishing to the point where I feel as though there is no reason to try to rebuild or remake even. Perhaps it is not the best to compare this game to Rust in this way but it's the closest game I got to this mechanic.

Anyway, long story short, I usually don't like MMO's because communities tend to be very questionable but I like this game's community from what I experienced. I usually don't like games that require a time commitment from me everyday but I like it in this game because even a small amount of time feels valuable. I usually hate needing to rebuild a lot in games but I don't mind it in this game because it has a good balance of punishment but also leniency. I usually don't like games that force team based cooperation by under powering the player unless it's purely with my friends but I like it in this game because I can tell I am under powered but I don't feel fully helpless alone.

Moral of the story, I like this game and I think you should get it too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a straight 10/10 game. It feels both simple & complex at the same time. Simple because you need to choose what you want to do. You can be a Logi or Builder or Infantry or Partisan or Artillery or something else. You have to research a bit on the net on how to play any role. It is not too hard to learn any one role but the game becomes complex when you realize that there are just so many different things that you can do in Foxhole. It is definitely much better when you play in a clan or with friends although I haven't tried that yet.

The game kinda has a VALVE Source Engine feel. The way it looks like it was made by VALVE themselves when they were at their best. Graphics also feels Source Engine like to me, which is a very good thing. This is like the Dota 2 of war. There is no better game than this in the genre, not that I knew any others though. If you ask me, this is a much better way to spend your time than with PlanetSide 2. So far I've only played Logi because I think it is the best role to start with. It feels so good to bring crates to the frontlines and then receive commends. After all that work of farming, refining, waiting for the factory, you just bring those crates to the frontline and people reward you.

Now I will try to be on the battlefield and kill Colonials so I can realize what goods the frontline most needs and become a better Warden Logi. This game is very research-intensive in that you need to watch some YouTube tutorials first before playing it. In my first deployment I created a truck and ran out of diesel. After some searching I found some. On my 2nd day I freezed to death in a snowstorm.

Here are two good tips for beginner Logistics that most tutorials fail to mention.

#1 If you bring anything to the frontline ALWAYS turn them into crates first! Basically you gather salvage or any other resource, then you go refine it in a refinery, then you use those refined materials in a factory to produce crates of weapons and other things. Only things that come out of a factory will be crates. However, if you want to provide any other resource to the frontline such as bmats and rmats, you simply need to go into the menu of a Storage Depot, Seaport, or Shipping Container, with the materials in your inventory and right click on them and select turn into crates. That way you can transport crates exclusively to the frontlines which is MUCH better than just bringing raw bmats and rmats.

#2 If you arrive to the frontline e.g. a Bunker Base then drive your truck to it and press E and then do NOT click on the crates in your inventory manually and hit submit. If you do this other players can hit submit and steal your commends. If you want to do it right, Shift+Right Click on any crate in your own truck inventory and hit submit all crates to stockpile. That way the crates inside your truck will all be submitted to the bunker base and everyone will know it was you who did the work.

NOTE that this game has a lore too and most players are very passionate about it. I'd suggest to actually stay with one army forever and research which army you like better. You don't really have to because you are allowed to change army after every war but in my case it's just so much cooler to always play COLONIAL or WARDEN all the time, never switching to the enemy team and becoming a traitor. I want to stay loyal. WARDEN FOREVER!\t\t\t\t\t
