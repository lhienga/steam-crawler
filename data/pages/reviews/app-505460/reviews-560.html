\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe videos of this game that you will find make it seem really fun, with big teamwork action and an expansive choice of gameplay.
- You can either fight on the frontline with a plethora of weapons that requires teamwork to achieve good results with.
- Or you can go to the backline to make the factories and equipment that the frontliners will need to push the line.
- Or you can build large bunkers to secure terrain against enemy invasion.
- You will need to organise with hundreds of players to achieve shared goals.
- The two available factions have different playstyles that you will need to adapt to.

The game's concept is amazing. The execution however...

- Game balance has been terrible since the introduction of asymmetry. It is probably worse now than it was during early access. These problems are easily fixable, but for some reason they remain unfixed. One faction has a definite upper hand over the other faction balance wise, which leads to a pop imbalance (who wants to play on the always losing team?) and bigger queues.
- Factories are unintuitive. Logi is unintuitive. You spend a lot of time asking questions because the interface does a terrible job telling you what the gameplay loop actually is. You spend a lot of time grinding just to work around the game's clunky mechanics. You will have a hard time learning anything here unless you join a clan or you are lucky enough to come across an individual that is up for spending hours with you telling you how the system works.
- Good bunkers require the use of game bugs to build. The bunker meta is something that only the vets understand. Bad balance in terms of anti-structure weaponry means people who build bunkers can lose a week's worth of work in 1 hour or less.
- Ingame organisation is awful. If you want to organise anything, it will be all done on discord. The game's squad system and map marks alone are wholly inadequate to deal with the task of organising offensives involving hundreds of people, and yet that's all you have to access. Casuals just do whatever on the front, only clans ever actually properly push something nowadays. This is not "skill issue", it is simply because organising for people who do not live on discord is terrible, and at worst impossible when the ingame VC system goes down.

You can have fun in this game. But on balance you will spend more time being frustrated with the myriad of issues that it has, than having fun. The more you play, the more you will notice the glaring issues in the execution of its design. The more you will become annoyed about the thousands of issues that would take a tiny amount of time to fix that the devs never seem to fix.

You might think "haha look, another guy who has played 2k+ hours leaving a negative review". Yes I play this game even now. Would I recommend it to others in its current state? Absolutely not. Save yourself, don't get involved, don't chug on the hopium/copium that the devs will fix the game. Just wait and see from a distance. I still hope that it will become competitive again, but in its current state it is not. If the devs do manage to fix it in the next few months, I will change this review.
