\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly this game does not do a good job of advertising all the good things about it and I 100% recommend picking this up. Even if you aren't a big wargames person there's something for you in Foxhole.

This game is one of those with an incredibly supportive base which is why I recommend it. My second day on, me and my friends had the opportunity to get educated on a load of things at base by a commander who was willing to stay back and help the new guys; along with everyone on the front too helping aswell.

Another reason this game is so worth it other than the price is how in depth everything is. EVERYTHING in this game is player driven. Not one thing that happens in the game (other than AI turrets) is driven by the game at all. Which can be a downside for sure, but in this game it motivates everyone to constantly be learning something and doing something to help the effort. Which really shows with the influx of new players (such as myself).

Something that supports my last point is that this game isn't like all of the other popular war games like Squad or Hell Let Loose. This game is one persistent online war instead of round based gameplay. The reason this supports motivating people to be better is because they actually see room to learn. Unlike round-based gameplay; this war lasts until one side wins or the reset happens. So there's no ceiling for improvement. You can always take the time to learn to do something for your team while someone else fights the front and there's never anything wrong with that which is a great thing for this game and for you.

I wrote this review on an impulse so I have nothing else to say right now but i'll come back if I think of something. Feel free to leave a comment; I'll get back as soon as possible.\t\t\t\t\t
