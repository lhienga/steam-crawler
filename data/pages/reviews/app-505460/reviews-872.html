A long one, no TL;DR

Got me hooked immediately, I played 13 hours in two days.

Relaxing and fun game to play, lots of different things to do to help the war effort and fellow soldiers. Like scrapping resources, producing weapons, ammo, building materials and vehicles. Treating the injured and/or shooting the enemies.

There are no classes, but players can roleplay different roles depending on what equipment they pick up at the base and how they use their imagination. You can be a logistic pioneer and build defenses as the army pushes forward. A scrapper and manufacturer (backbone of the army and most important role, imo) to produce armaments, gear, medical supplies and building materials. A medic to heal the injured or a normal frontline soldier equipped with rifles, smgs, Hmgs and such.

There are thirteen different maps and they are huge, it takes a long time to reach the frontlines from base if you're on foot, so logistics drivers are usually waiting for players and drive them to the frontlines.

It's a persistent war simulation, it takes hours, days, weeks and months of real time to research and produce tanks and high tier weapons, such as V2 rockets and the newly added five manned battle tanks.

There is a rank system, you'll gain rank when other players commend you when you do well. Being a medic or a logistics pioneer is a good way to gain commends, since you're always helping and under the eyes of hundreds of players daily. If you like to shoot people and gain rank, do well during operations made by higher ranked players you'll have a good chance of being commended by them.

The community is friendly and very welcoming, at least on the Wardens side. When I spawned for the first time, there were experienced players who gathered groups of newbies and taught them all the basics they needed to know. Trolls and griefers and pretty fast dealt with by kicking and nobody tolerates them.

Some great and memorable experiences I've had.

I spawned to the warfront, I asked where men are needed mostly and I was asked to join a bridge defense operation in Callahan's Boot. The operation already consisted of five other players. I walked there and asked how things are, they told me to sit tight and wait for the enemy. Once night came, they tried to push the bridge with a 15.7mm machine gun, but we fought back and succesfully defended the bridge couple of times. When we ran out of frags they took the bridge from us, forcing us to retreat to the town. We were losing SS = soldier supplies (spawn points) on the HQ we had in the town.

We defended the town over an hour, until we finally got logistics people to bring us supplies, such as grenades, gas masks, SS and building supplies so we could push the enemy back, take control of the bridge and build more defenses.

Over time we got more people to join the operation from other towns as the enemy tried to flank us from the East and six soldiers were not enough to hold the bridge and the East flank. In four hours, our numbers had grown from six players to around fifty. We failed to push the East flank so the enemy pushed into the town and destroyed our HQ and we couldn't spawn there anymore.

We were fighting a losing battle, one by one we lost men and our ammunitions and medic supplies were dwindling quite fast. Around six hour mark we lost Callahan's Boot to the overwhelming and well supplied enemy forces. I quit playing, but the next day I logged in, we had already taken Callahan's Boot back. Presumably that happened when I was sleeping and working (Persistent warfare).

On the next day, I joined an operation called, "Guirrella boys" in a different warfront. We were a small team of players who used a barge to go behind the enemy lines via ocean routes, to kill enemy logi players and blow up their supply trucks on the roads, so our fellow players could push the front more easily.

We had to be very careful, since our closest HQ (spawn point) was a long distance away, so dying was really bad for the team. We were hunted down pretty fast by a armored car and I was one of the first ones to fall. It was a very exciting experience and I was left crawing more, so I waited until the rest of the team died and spawned to the HQ. We prepared for another querrilla operation, which, sadly ended at the beach we went ashore. Because the enemy players had build a pillbox (wooden bunker) equipped with HMG to protect the shore line and they slaughtered us like it was D-Day in Normandy.

I warmly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the few hours i've spent on the game. Arguably i can say that it is a GRIND. The persistent war really takes some time and effort. Logistics have to be given attention while fighting the opposing faction. However with all that said, this is a diamond in the rough. Even with the small player count it has never felt dead or unpopulated to me. Fights are always a fun to be part in.

What makes this game excellent is that it allows it's player to openly communicate and coordinate whatever operation they wish to do. Want to have a convoy of armored cars? Build them and Bring them to the frontline! Want to attack a certain outpost with a couple of friends or even randoms? Go ahead! This game offers so much freedom and "sense of achievement" that it breaths a fresh air into gaming.

Yes, it's not for everyone frankly. If you're the typical new generation teen that prefers twitch shooters and instant gratification this isn't for you. But if you're the type of person that wants to contribute to something big, to meet new people and be immersed in a WW2-ish setting. This is the game for you.

10/10 Would recommend to friends.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are many games that I have been back to from shorter or longer breaks, but no game has EVER made me write a review, which is the highest praise I can give. This game is more than worth the cost, not like the "bestselling" shooters we see so commonly nowadays ...

This game has everything that I enjoy - The hectic battlefronts that bring total strangers together in a common struggle, the highly strategic moves to upgrade towns & facilities, the labor to ensure the success of the men fighting at the front, long planned offensive operations and the satisfaction of defending a stronghold you built. You can be one man with a rifle rushing into hail of bullets to show your worth or someone who takes the charge of many to make that cruzial bit of difference between utter defeat or glorious victory.

The game gives you a good perspective of what war is really about - the mix of resources, weaponry, men, time and determination. You can work for hours on a project and in hours, days or weeks see it paying off. It might take a bit to get into, but it's all worth it once you grasp it fully and hear the cheers when the truckload of supplies reach the front with men in dire need.

The community is very mature, considerate and cooperative. It has the ability to make individual effort feel worthwhile while showing the power of large groups working together toward common goal. There is nothing more fulfilling than holding a position to the bitter end only to realize you have beaten the odds with simple wits and determination.

The graphics are something from my childhood, but for me it never was a barrier, it rather enables one to enjoy the content to the fullest and not get dazzled by magically overpowered players overpowering others. Everyone is equal, even though some mastery with a rifle does still make a difference and pushes players to improve their skills.

The developers are a charm after all these years still striving to improve the game and give the best possible experience. Coming back from a break of months one can find a game utterly changed and I can say, never for the worse. Starting from a small battles turning into weeks or months long struggles of hundreds with new content free of charge regularly.

EDIT after 1000 further hours and 2 years:
The game continues to evolve and grow, developers continue to try out new and bold things. Of course there are hits and misses in the process, yet the fact that they are still fully engaged in developing the game is highly commendable. There are also so many ways they could go into the micro-transaction world with possibility to buy skins or resources or whatever, yet at this point I do not believe it'll ever happen and would certainly ruin the game only for profit.

This is an extremely unique game that I must admit is not for everyone, yet still seems to be highly unappreciated for what it is (everything above is still valid) - hardcore war simulator. I recommend you try it out, if you like the feel it's certainly worth the low price tag for the hours you get and if not then you can always refund.\t\t\t\t\t
