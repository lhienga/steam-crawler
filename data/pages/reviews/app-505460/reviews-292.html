\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is both a fascinating and frustrating MMO. One the one hand, a massive player-driven war economy that produces and harvests all material used in the war to come. Also, a player-base large enough to sustain the game while also being small enough to be friendly and non-toxic.

On the other hand, the game can often feel like it swallows you up in the larger war. Players without vehicles are often given few, if any, anti-vehicle options that don't involve suicidal rushes or ambushes. Tank battles are often decided by who has more material and patience rather than skill or coordination. Infantry pushes are often stopped by AI with perfect aim, which is often far more punishing than it needs to be. Maps can also feel incredibly bland after a while, with fights either taking place in either an open field, a small city, a bridge, or a narrow mountain pass. A final criticism is that you cannot manually aim up and down, which often leads to fighting your own cursor to get a decent shot.\t\t\t\t\t
