\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has an amazing idea at heart, and it could be great, like an EVE Online set in WW1/2. But it's quite buggy (rubberbanding, getting extremely stuck in mud). And the player base skews toward the young, male, toxic gamer stereotype.

The UI, tutorials, and movement feel janky and unpolished. It was a strange choice making people move around the starting island instead of simply having a menu to deploy: this gets old. They desperately need to hire a UX person and a networking code specialist. If you're lacking a vehicle, it's agonizingly slow to cross long stretches on foot (needs an autorun key like PUBG). Likewise, holding down the right mouse button to look into the distance cramps your hand after a while; a toggle would be nice.

What could save the game is fascinating emergent gameplay moments (of which I've had several!). If the game is supported and fixed by the devs, it *could* join the likes of Rimworld, Dwarf Fortress, and EVE in the epic and funny stories it could generate. The world is highly player-driven and dynamic. Buildings and frontlines that existed a day ago might be rather different when you login again. Everything is built by players, right down to the guns and ammo you use -- someone crafted them and brought them to the frontlines.

The down-thumb is because I'm concerned about the toxicity of the player base, and the devs' lack of communication with customers. I wanted to refund the game and check back in in 6+ months, but I had hit 2.1 hours played, just above the no-questions-asked refund period.
