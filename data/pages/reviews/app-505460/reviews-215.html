Initially I was extremely excited for the 1.0 release. The facilities system and trains, and other additions in 1.0 looked amazing. I thought the game was about to enter into an awesome new era.

However, the way facilities have been implemented in the game has only made the problem of "regiments hoarding stuff" even worse. It's become nearly impossible for a small regiment to actually contribute to the new logistics systems. It has created more infighting, more greifing, and more frustration. I honestly feel bad for the devs. They put so much work into the 1.0 release, but the results on the live servers have nearly ruined the game. Player retention has fallen off a cliff.

I very reluctantly uninstalled the game, and have moved on to other games. I thought 1.0 was going to lead to me playing this game even more, but instead it has killed my desire to play the game at all.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR:
Beware: this game does not respect your time and most of it will be dedicated to farming.
"Foxhole"? More like "Farmhole".
The typical game that the more you get to know its mechanics, the more you realise how badly it is made.
And don't expect developers to be able to balance the game or not develop it in such a way that it is not a full-time job.
My conclusions:
And here we are, finally reviewing Farmhole: excellent idea but very disappointing management.
Right now the game is driven by amateurish fancy surmised as "hey, it'd be cool if our gamehadthese!"
Developers are focusing too much on new features while not prioritizing overall health of the game as well as the community.
This has led to a recent ongoing situation with a mass exodus of players and an unsettled community that is unbalanced in faction population and completely divergent in sentiment towards gamebalance.

It has been in my wish list for years, always uncertain whether to buy it or not given the nature of the game.
After the first hundred hours inside this title I still feel like a complete newbie who is only now beginning to see every thing that is hidden by the exaggerated amount of unnecessary farming.
Most of the time the battles end due to exhaustion and not because one side is better than the other: you spend hours making little to no progress, then you get distracted for a minute and everything evolves at a mind-blowing speed and I think that's what drives people to play it... not wanting to miss the moment when the enemy front reaches the breaking point in order to finally advance and get that dopamine rush.
And so you're willing to put aside the hours you've spent being bored with an auto clicker on to farm and having to be a postman in order to get that achievement, in order to push the front line by a few metres and be able to say: I was there.

Would I recommend Foxhole to new players, especially those who don't like being part of large groups like myself?
Absolutely not, if you really want to do this to yourself, wait for the discounts so you don't pay more than the title's worth.
Am I gonna still play with it despite all the s*it I've thrown at it? Yes, probably.
But only because I have nothing better to play with at the moment and, despite everything, I'm masochistic enough to want to play a war from start to finish to savour its various facets.

PROS
  • A good amount of the community is willing tohelp new players.

  • The lore, although hidden and not at all easy to understand, can be nice if one takes the time to search through the various wikis and read the dedicated Discord channel.

  • The graphics and models of the game are nice and manage to express and surround you quite a bit in the game environment you find yourself in: namely a dystopian World War II.

  • It is addictive and frankly I don't know if this is a good thing or not, especially if played in a group you will inevitably start to sympathise with what is going on given the immense amount of time spent even just to gain or defend few meters of front.

  • The game is unique, needless to say.
    As bad as it may have developers, you play it for the idea it tries to express (while not fully succeeding) and for the way it amalgamates various categories of video games including strategic, management and shooters.
CONS
  • So many QoL features are missing, even basic things like being able to adjust MOUSE SENSITIVITY in game. Ridiculous.

  • As far as the optimization of the game is concerned, it could have been done better especially because a lot of random disconnections areencountered.

  • Collisions are unsatisfactory:
    Heavy vehicles that get stuck just because you graze a pebble, tanks that can't get over a fence, and the climbing system, especially on rocks, is flimsy and badly done.

  • Being a game based on teamwork, I partly understand (although I do not agree with) the fact that alone you can do practically nothing.
    The problem is you need a huge amount of people to achieve a minimum of results.

  • The game's voice system (which is crucial since queues prevent organised forces from amassing easily/quickly in a hex) is unreliable and often breaks down without warning or notification.
    But after all, this is not surprising since it is 'Vivox'.

  • It is by no means newbie friendly in any form and the tutorial, even tho it's present, messes with your previous knowledge instead of taking you hand by hand through the first few hours of gameplay.
    For such a title, a clear and neat introduction is mandatory.

  • Communication from the developers is almost non-existent and updates are far too slow and often only make the game worse.
    Like, for instance, the addition of facilities that makes the game even more farm-based.
    A war game that forces you to spend a good 70% of your time in the back lines farming doesn't seem like a great idea to me... I didn't know I bought Factorio.

  • PvE defences (bunkers, AT stations and so on) are worth more and are extremely more efficient than the single person or small group of saboteurs.
    This is why people often prefer to wait for times when there are few people in the server to attack, so that they almost only have to deal with the AI. And I don't blame them.
    It only takes a single person with a hammer to BLOCK the advance of a dozen people.

  • About the shooting, although I am actually trying to get used to the isometric view, I find it lacking:
    It feels inconsistent and too much RNG-based for both tanks and rifles. (which, at least as far as shooting is concerned, I repudiate in every single game when excessive).
    Personally, another thing that detracts from the infantry experience is the fact that defenders and camping are extremely advantaged.
    The game rewards standing still in a trench for whole minutes staring at nothing instead of initiative or those who dare to make an encirclement manoeuvre thus promoting a flat playing style and increasing the already high timesinking caused by farming.

  • Besides the excessive circle j*rking within the community, this one is full of redditors and people who instead of playing prefer to cry and GO ON STRIKES even for months.
    Too attached to the faction they belong to for no logical reason.
    People who have arrived at a certain rank may be elitist just because they have been in the back line semi-AFK farming for more hours than you, when they may not even know the basics of the game.
    This isn't always the case, but there's no point in denying that the commend system falls apart since it's totally at the discretion of the player to give or not to give people commends.
    You might have done the most heroic deed of your life and helped capture an important city but no one will notice, so you won't get commends.
    On the other hand, driving a truck from one side to the other to deliver some crap to the front it's gonna make the commends rain on you even if you didn't make all that stuff but you just took it from the warehouse, in defiance of those who spent hours producing it instead.
