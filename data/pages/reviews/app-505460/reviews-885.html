\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, nothing beats jumping on Foxhole and fighting against the Warden scum. This is a great game with an incredible community. When you jump online and arrive at the front line you'll be greeted by familiar faces each and every time. That's what really make Foxhole special. Apart from that the game is evolving and only getting better as time goes on. The devs have updated the map by adding multiple regions, new vehicles, and weapons (although some weapons can be a bit too powerful *cough cough* the shotgun).

The only real complaint I have at the moment is that the game world can sometimes be a bit too big for the current player base. I love the community we have in Foxhole but if you're not fighting on the front line you don't really run into many people. This isn't a real issue though and actually works out well for the logistic guys who run supplies.

The game is a great top down shooter with a wonderful community and ever improving features. You can fight on the front line, run supplies to the FOB's for the fighting men, or build defences and outposts. Well worth the $19.99 price tag. I hope to see new faces soon!

P.S. If you decide to buy the game, don't be a dirty Warden. Join the Colonial Army today!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredible game, nothing else like it on Steam or elsewhere.

World Conquests, the main feature of the game, take weeks to complete and are actual drawn-out wars. Think Planetside 2 but isometric with much more depth, and a much stronger focus on building defences and logistics.

What makes the game is unique is the amount of player agency in well, everything. Every rifle you grab, every piece of ammunition you take, all of it was made by players. It's up to the players to decide where to concentrate their factories and material refineries to make resources, its up to the players on how to progress in the games faction-wide tech tree, its up to the players to build defences and make sure their towns stay secure.

However, this game is not for everyone. It can be fairly slow-paced compared to your usual shooter. You won't be able to drop in and be fighting the enemy in seconds. However, at it's peak the action in this game is top-notch. And the addition of knowing that what you're fighting for actively impacts a enourmous, game-wide world war is a feeling like no other.

Every enemy you kill is another step towards capturing that town, every town you capture is another step to taking that region, every region you take is another step towards winning the war. Everything matters in Foxhole and everyone can make a difference.

9/10 game for me, but again it's not for everyone. I would definately reccomend you watch some videos, read up more about the game, and be aware of your 2 hour refund period if you're looking to try it out. Also, do not be afraid to ask for help from higher-ranked players. The community is very friendly towards newbies who ask for help, and you will find many people eager to help you learn the ropes of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
