\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole offers a unique way of playing simulated war games in a MMO fashion. Most war games only lets you experience the forefront battle and nothing behind the scenes as your always just play as the soldier with their gun shooting enemies until one side has more points than the other then restart and go again. With these games there is no constant struggle for dwindling of supply's and equipment while trying to push the forefront on scarce resources.

Well Foxhole has another thing coming. In Foxhole your whole team of 100's of players will have to work together to supply your team with resources then build, buildings, warships, trains, tanks, weapons, first aid etc, with those collected resources. Then the other side of the team has to push the enemy back with that built equipment. Truly every roll in Foxhole is really important and no one is left out. Another thing I enjoy about this game is that the world is your oyster and you get to design and build defences like trenches, and bunkers like the pillbox bunker and a lot more.

Honestly I can fall in love with this game... although there one issue that i want to bring to the table.
That is: You can not not remove your allies buildings: In Foxhole there will definitely be moments where you will run into ally constructions and say to yourself "w*f is this s#*t" because the buildings that are placed by your team are placed in such a horrible manner that you cant even squeeze you player model through it. Then the worst part is, is that you want to remove it and revamp it, yknow make it look better, make it function better. But no, no such thing as removing buildings in foxhole, you cant even remove it with explosives because then the game will ban you from using weapons for a short moment for 'friendly fire'???
I get it, its to reduce trolling and griefing but then what about the players who really want to make good logistics and defences. I just hope the devs will come up with something.

I like to play foxhole with logistics with me building tanks and shipping them on a train to the front line... idk that s#*t just cool to me but fr i cant even support my team like that because there are so many ridiculous team buildings in the way.

But other than that i give it a 6/10 just because i cant really achieve what i want to do but still fun non the less and I think you will enjoy this game too.\t\t\t\t\t

Foxhole - A Tactical Warfare Odyssey Review (7 Hours Played)

Review:

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Foxhole, with 7 hours of intense gameplay under my belt, is a tactical warfare odyssey that demands strategy, coordination, and unwavering determination. This persistent world war experience offers a unique take on multiplayer warfare.

Pros:

Persistent World:
The game's persistent, player-driven world sets it apart. Battles have real consequences, and the evolving map reflects the outcome of each engagement.

Teamwork and Coordination:
Foxhole thrives on teamwork. Coordinating with fellow soldiers to achieve objectives is not just rewarding but essential for success.

Player-Crafted Economy:
The player-driven economy adds depth. From manufacturing weapons to delivering supplies, every role contributes to the war effort.

Strategy and Tactics:
The game encourages strategic thinking. Planning and executing successful offensives or holding crucial defensive lines are immensely satisfying.

Community and Communication:
The Foxhole community is dedicated, and communication within the game is vital. It fosters a sense of camaraderie among players.

Cons:

Steep Learning Curve:
Foxhole is not for the faint of heart. Newcomers may find it challenging to grasp the complexities of war and logistics.

Time-Intensive:
The game's persistent nature means that significant time investments are often required to make a substantial impact on the war effort.

Occasional Server Issues:
Some server instability issues can disrupt gameplay, although they're typically addressed promptly.

Verdict:

With 7 hours played, Foxhole proves itself as a unique and demanding multiplayer warfare experience. Its persistent world, heavy emphasis on teamwork, and player-driven economy make it a standout title in the genre. While the learning curve can be steep and the commitment substantial, the rewards of meaningful battles and a dedicated player community are well worth it.

Recommended for those seeking a hardcore multiplayer war game, fans of strategy and coordination, and players looking for an immersive and persistent online world!
