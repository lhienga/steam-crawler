\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHad I known what this game really was, I would have been playing it for years.

First things first, this game plays like a fully-fledged MMO, but with server wipe that happen every 10-30 days. It is different from many MMOs, as this one doesn't have levels or skill trees or any real character progression. But you DO level up, just like a real soldier becomes more experienced.

You see, 99% of the 'stuff' in this game is produced by the players. trenches, bunkers, weapons, ammunition, vehicles, recon, and honestly everything is pulled out of the ground, refined and shaped into resources for the war effort. Supply lines play a big role of the game, as well as intel and military infastructure, so the communication tends to be a bit deeper than simply 'lets go here and kill bad guys!' it will take a (small) amount of effort to get hip to what people are talking about, but i felt largely aware of what's going on in a broad sense after about 10 hours of playing.

So as you learn the map that has been created, learn weak spots and the architecture of your encampments, you become a more potent soldier because you shift from being guided to being a natural leader. "I dont know whats going on" becomes "don't go that way, that's a deathtrap. They are much softer on the east side"

And then suddenly, you see them fortifying their east side. Time to gather a strike force and stop their expansion before things get much, much harder for your regiment.

You will push and pull several hundred feet with your enemy for hours on end, until something gives - just like real war. You will make friends and some of them you will keep, and others will just be a funny gentleman you spent 25 minutes in a trench with before he died to a grenade and logged off.

Don't let the unusual PoV and 'simplistic' seeming visuals fool you.

There is NO other game on this planet that imparts the deeper feelings of war more than this game.\t\t\t\t\t
