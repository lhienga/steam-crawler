\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI said I'd recommend it, but it really depends on the reader.

I find the good things (for the most part) are in the game design: You don't stay still often when grinding, so that makes the grinding more bearable. You're not locked into anything, you can do logistics one day, combat the next day, and a combination the third day. You can jump in and start helping from the moment you enter.

I find the bad things (for the most part) are the community: People will often call for support and it won't be answered. Logistic questions won't be answered. Lots of whining and entitlement, but that's normal in a lot of games, so take that however you want to. People will steal your crap. The combat isn't really made for solo-minded players, there's a lot of coordination that matters.

tl;dr:

While it has some problems with game design, Foxhole is basically what it markets itself as (you also have to remember it is Early Access). The problem I think for people will be the community. It can be very difficult to deal with. So if you want to deal with a good community, this game probably isn't for you.

PS: There are plenty of great/helpful people in this game, I fear this post came off as "community bad and toxic".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, Foxhole is a fun, great top down shooter that gets you involved in many aspects of war such as combat, logistics, intelligence, armoured units, naval units etc etc.

Lets start by naming all of the positives about Foxhole
1.) Unique top down perspective
2.) Large community (Regiments/clans)
3.) There's always something to be done (keeps you busy for hours, even for days!)
4.) Different roles within the war zone such as Naval and land combat, different soldier roles such as machine gunner, medic, Anti-tank, Anti-infantry. etc.
5.) Massive map
6.) Logistical deliveries/runs make good use of kicking back and relaxing as you drive to different delivery drop off zones (Helps if you aren't feeling like combating).

A bit of history first
