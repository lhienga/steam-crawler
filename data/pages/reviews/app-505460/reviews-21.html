\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole encompasses everything you want out of a war game, and strikes a nice ​balance between an easy to start but hard to master playstyle. This top down war ​game put’s players on the same giant server, where each side battles it out to gain ​territory and ultimately win the war. The main selling point of this game however is the ​sense of scale and purpose achieved within the game.

Foxhole’s design philosophy is to create a large map with a grid system, and instances ​are created for individual grids which contain every player nearby. This allows the ​entire player base to feel a sense of comradery as they are all fighting the same war, ​while the actual game itself is able to save a lot of processing power due to this ​system.

The gameplay of Foxhole is so much more than your regular Battlefield or Call of Duty ​game however. Combat aside the addition of a company based system allows players ​to create large groups on third party sites like discord, and coordinate large ​operations together. The fully imagined logistics mechanic within the game provides ​different play styles to just being on the front line, as every weapon, vehicle, bullet is ​created and transported to the battlefield by players. The sense of depth they have ​achieved through this system makes for a large scale strategy that is unmatched in the ​space, as the biggest reason for losing territory is it’s neglect for logistics.

The combat however is the area which needs some work. The top down aim system ​could do with more fine tuning, as often you will find yourself aiming at nothing or ​firing in completely different directions to intended despite how long you gave the ​weapon to aim accurately. Despite this, there is such a unique form of enjoyment that ​you get by pushing a bridge or objective for hours on end, and the proximity chat ​allows you to develop temporary friendships with players that have a common goal. ​Jokes and strategies can be shared across the mic, and teamwork can be achieved to ​such a higher level once people have heard the voice of their fellow players, and don’t ​see them as just a pixel on the screen. One slight disadvantage worth mentioning is ​that the community has created a merit based system, which blocks new players ​behind a 100 hour wall before they are given access to some of the late game content. ​While there are tanks and artillery in the game, aswell as sniper rifles etc; they are ​often allocated by more experienced players to the most deserving on the team, ​restricting newcomers to a basic rifleman. This experience can ultimately disway many ​players from sticking around for more than one battle (which tend to last around a ​month) as it often leads to constant corpse runs back to the front line after a gas ​grenade or a machine gun caught you off-guard in an uncounterable way.

Foxhole is definitely a must-play for diehard fans of war games, and offers many new ​systems to the table which feel fresh within the genre, but it’s the moment to moment ​gameplay which will keep this game as a neesh community of experienced players, ​and only those who want to really dig deep into it will ever experience everything it ​has to offer.
