\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is with regret that I leave this review.

While I have had many a good time with this game its current state is unhealthy.
The devs and moderators are unwilling to permanently ban griefers. So when some guy dumps dozens of man hours, worth of logistics work onto the ground where it despawns, blows up key structures, or vehicles, he gets a nice light slap on the wrists, and then comes back a few days later to do the same crap again. Repeat offenders are common. Some of these people also have multiple alternate accounts, where one gets a temporary ban, and they switch to another, go someplace else, and carry on with screwing things up.

Logistics, the heart and soul of this game, has grown too tedious, with too few people willing to pick up the slack and keep the supplies going to keep your team in the fight.

Too much time spent driving, whether to get resources, or bring manufactured items to the fight, or bring newly manufactured vehicles to the fight.

Resource scarcity, might sound like a good idea on paper, but in practice it was and is horrible. There are never enough materials to go around, and get done the things that need doing. Previously resources were too abundant, and the flow of machines, equipment and structures was un-ending, going on so long as players had the will to produce and build. Whereas now, they swung too far in the opposite direction.

Too much land for the current player base to defend effectively.

The current state of the game has driven away a lot of the older playerbase.

We came in understanding that this was a niche game, and having a fully player driven war economy was going to take some work, but its really gotten out of hand. This is more of a second job than a game.\t\t\t\t\t
