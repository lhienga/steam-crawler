\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*I want to point out first that many of the in-game images on the store page seem to be panned out a good deal and you will not have that same field of view while playing the game. Some of them are also angled and present a perspective you can't actually see from while playing the game.

I dig how involved the logistics and strategy aspects of the game can be, but the infantry combat mechanics are clunky and frustrating. Movement is also clumsy and rewards jerking back and forth and spinning around like mad when closing distance or being shot at because of the poor shooting mechanics. I think the idea behind the gunplay is sound but the implementation leaves a lot to be desired. Constantly being killed from offscreen in an isometric game is also very frustrating, and poor design in my opinion.

So the game itself does need some work still in some critical systems I believe. That, combined with the overwhelming amount of purposeful in-team griefing I've seen in the game, makes Foxhole a "wait and see" at full release rather than a game I can recommend buying now in Early Access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan't recommend Foxhole in it's current state.

The core gameplay loop is miserable. There was an interesting thought in implementing a logistics system, but since .26 it's just went downhill and is now equivalent to a second job. I'd compare it to Elite:Dangerous' space trucking, but at least in E:D you can autopilot and look at the pretty skyboxes. The few people who actively participate in logistics are hard-pressed to carry out the utter chore that is trying to make the game fun for others by supplying equipment nonstop. Developers have brushed this to the side.

New players are left pretty much blind when they join, as the devs rely on veteran players to teach them. However, the treatment of older players leaves much to be desired and most aren't willing to engage with new players on a teaching level because they're being leaned on to provide a function that should be a proper tutorial. This was an alright attitude when there weren't so many things to do, but now the game has layers of complicated/time consuming activities which aren't feasible to teach.

Developers have muted people on devstreams who bring up questions about a recent incident with a streamer who caused problems for other players by boasting immunity to punishment because he's getting paid, has threatened volunteer game mods if they punish him for his behaviour, and has advocated teamkilling which is against the rules. There has so far been no answer from them as to how they're approaching this, and volunteer mods don't want to get involved which is their right.

Early access is being used as an excuse for bad choices and dismissal of issues. The game has effectively been in the same state for three years with no real changes to gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
