But in order to play this game and enjoy it, you have to understand that it isn't about YOU. It's about your FACTION. You have to think broadly, and in the long term, like you would in an actual war. The moment to moment gameplay won't always be exciting, but fire fights and long winded battles are intensely engaging. If you want to chill and relax for a few hours, but still help out your faction and friends, you can do logistical work (and I love logistical work regardless, it's a very easy way to get commendations and to get people to recognize who you are, which in turn gives you respect). If you prefer to push out and gain more ground you can join the front lines as a medic or artillery officer, or be a combat engineer and help shore up defenses to prevent pushes. My point is, there's always something for you to do in this game, and whatever you do, you're a welcome addition to your faction. Veterans are more than happy to show new players the ropes, and if you have a silly recognizable name, you may even find yourself becoming a beacon of morale for the front lines as everyone chants and screams your name as they charge into the battle lines. If you ever get bored of performing one role, you can always change it up and do something else. And you can do it immediately on the fly using your own judgement.

I guess if there is one word to sum up Foxhole perfectly, it would be "COORDINATION".\t\t\t\t\t
