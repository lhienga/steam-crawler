A game where some players must sacrifice their own enjoyment so that others may enjoy the game as it was intended.

Foxhole is fun, and Foxhole is boring. It all depends if you want to consume the resources other people have made for you, or produce those resources yourself.

The combat in Foxhole is exciting. Bullets and weapons that have been forged be either team are used on the frontline and are desperately fought for. Resources have meaning, and all combat has a cost. This weight makes everything feel more meaningful in the game, and every action more satisfying.

The logistics in Foxhole is boring. Everything that is created in the game is produced by a real person, and while that sounds cool on paper, some poor player is working away doing some incredibly mundane task to make it all possible. While it would make sense to have these boring tasks automated in some way, the developers of Foxhole deliberately want players to perform these tasks. It's like playing Factorio without any factories or conveyor belts. As a logistics player, you are a simple assembly line worker, and you aren't even getting paid for it.

If the game had more automation, with less time spent doing incredibly boring ♥♥♥♥ like clicking piles of scrap, then this could easily be an 8-9/10 game. But as the game is currently, I'd give it a 6/10. It's the reason there are only hundreds of players on Foxhole, rather than thousands. I hope the devs revamp logistics, and get more players on the frontline.

Ideally the player split should be:
10% Logistics
90% Combat

Currently the player split is more like:
40% Logistics
60% Combat\t\t\t\t\t
