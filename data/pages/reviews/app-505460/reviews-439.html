\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole's gravest offense is the in-game culture; not the game's lore itself, but the regular interactions that have developed between members of the player base. For example, one faction likes to spam the word "♥♥♥' in the chat (rhymes with dumb, which is what censorship is steam). As one might imagine, this is endemic of the kind of stereotypical 'frat-boy' culture that video game culture's public image is often tainted with.

The same faction has something called 'techmaids,' which made me think of cool science-nuns, and seems like a really interesting concept. In practice, most are beat down guys who do a thankless job, and are rewarded by a constant barrage of abuse and transphobic slurs in the chat. They are also typically incredibly rude and standoffish, but one can almost understand their passive-aggression when one sees what they have to deal with constantly. The same faction has this bizarre anti-chinese sentiment; apparently they have political problems with the Chinese government, so they think the best way to express that is to scream at random citizens. They also have both literal nazis and nazi 'cosplayers,'
despite the game taking place in a fictional universe. I thought those were baseless rumors, and then I joined up with that faction and saw it for myself.

Moles are also a huge problem. Alternate accounts are constantly taking resources other players put hours and hours of work into gathering and refining, and driving them into the oceans. Some people try to start drama with their alts specifically hoping to get others banned. Absolutely nothing is done about any of this, and during my time playing I saw a limit imposed on the number of reports a player can issue per war, which actively discourages trying to address these problems.

There are some kind, creative and generally wonderful people who play this game, but they are the pieces of corn in a cesspit, and absolutely no action is being taken to clean the game up.

The game itself ranges from fun to overly tedious. I love the top-down shooting mechanics, and would flock to a similar title minus the casual racism. I hate the production mechanics, it feels like the devs have gone out of their way to make them as boring as possible, hoping to simulate the mind-numbing grind of a factory job, something that nobody wants. The only reasons people actually do that part of the game is for gratitude and praise from the other players, and it's easy for someone to come and undo all of your work, or swoop in and steal the credit from under you.

All in all, even after putting in 400+ hours I regret the purchase. I never would've made it that far if I didn't have a dedicated friend who was really into the game. It's a mediocre top-down shooter, with a community that sucks any fun that could be had right out of the experience. I have a sense of humor, I'm not a gamer-gater, I like to believe most people are decent, most inflammatory remarks are all in good fun, and the mouth-breathing, basement-dwelling, cheeto-dusted stereotype that gamers are painted as is just that; but Foxhole makes me reconsider, and confronts me with a very real, very ugly part of my sub-culture.

Don't play video games with actual, real-life nazis, or by devs who do nothing to stop them. Don't buy this game. Lots of people who are into this are also into company of heros, go get that instead.\t\t\t\t\t
