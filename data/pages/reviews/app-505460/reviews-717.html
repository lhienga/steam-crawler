\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate 2022-10-19:
Tl;dr for 1.0:
- it's a meme
- more unfun grinding than ever before
- still one faction has a huge advantage terrain/infantry weapon/tankwise.
-> this is by design, these developers made a campaign out of the multiplayer
- simple vehicles like the better armored transporter or the tier2 LUV are gated behind "factories", before they could be created in garages
- it's clanhole now; heavy focus shift away from solo players to clan players
- trains just adding more complexity to logistics
- with fire introduced now nobody wants to build bases anymore, it's pointless

First of all, I like the idea and the concept of this game.

It's like a war sandbox game. The goal in this game is to control a certain amount of victory towns.

Two factions fighting each other in a big open world, divided into different maps/regions.
Each of these maps having different terrains, one faction is starting each new war with some southern maps and the other faction gets an equal amount in the north.

But here is the first problem: terrain imbalance.
High ground advantage is a thing in this game. The regions are not symmetrical, means one side has mountains, giant rocks, and cliffs surrounding their important towns/map parts, the other faction has plain fields with little to none chokepoints. The southern faction needs to push through narrow, easy defendable chokepoints, the other faction through open fields, and a stone wall, which only protects a minor part of the southern regions.

Starting with some basic equipment, the teams have to collect salvage material to craft all sorts of war-related items: weapons, ammo, vehicles, equipment like radios, shovels, etc.
Then all of these items have to be moved to the frontlines by vehicles like trucks, flatbeds, and boats/freighters.

Here lies the next issue: tedious, boring scrapping and logistics work (applies to both factions).
It's just click-click-click. No "gamification" of otherwise boring tasks, like in other games. Hours of holding the mouse button (click-lock is a must-have as a salvager) to get some salvage. The same goes for refining the materials and crafting.
You need to use external "tools" like mouse click-lock or you may get serious carpal-tunnel problems.

The next thing is the vehicle asymmetry.
One faction gets an early game advantage, the other one a late-game advantage. In short, better vehicles at the beginning of the war means nothing, because the northern faction gets better tanks/heavier armor later in the game, which impacts the gameplay much more.

All of these problems adding up to the next issue: population.
The team with all the advantages written above is also the team with the most players, which results in a) underpopulation of the other team and b) a global queue time. There is no faction ratio, no auto-balancing, nothing.

Non-faction related issues:
- vehicle handling is a hot mess since the last update

- terrains bugs: getting stuck at rocks, in trenches, in bunkers, at houses, at bridges, on corners all around the map. Sometimes you fall through invisible holes in the map. You mostly can't get out and have to relog or use the unstuck command. This is the most annoying problem for me. I don't get it why there're still so many terrain bugs while being in development for 3 years!

- alt accounts are still a problem. Players from the other faction joining the enemy team and causing teamkills, wasting materials/resources, destroying defenses and vehicles, stealing tanks, etc.

- Devs don't really care about the community it seems. No response on really great suggestions on Reddit or their Discord, ignoring feedback and constructive criticism. They just add more complexity to the game instead of fixing urgent problems. Their "vision" of the game and the added "lore" matters more than fixing imbalance issues and bugs. I don't give a crap on lore or vision, I just want a fun gameplay experience.

Tl;dr: Could be such a fun game, but in the current state, it's just frustrating to play.

If you want to try it out, buy it only at a discount / on sale.

Update 2021-01-16:
Still the same problems.
Not you shape the outcome of the war, the devs do - by creating funny "waiting rooms":
- global queue
- border queue
- queues of frontline maps
- queues of backline maps
- spawn timer for respawn
- "progress bars" for
- pulling equipment out of town halls, bunkers etc.
- crafting/"cooking" things
- building things
- salvaging materials
Most of this only because they can't balance the two factions properly.

It's basically a waiting simulator with some MMO elements to it.

- terrains bugs are still are thing, every playsession I get stuck somewhere and have to suicide or use my beloved !unstuck command.
- still no moderation, griefers, alt accounts, multiboxing, exploits, cheating; the whole package is here, and it's getting worse. Reporting them is useless, devs don't care much.\t\t\t\t\t
