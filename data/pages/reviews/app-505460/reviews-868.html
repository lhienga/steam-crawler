Some issues that will effect your experience, and/or will annoy the crap out of you:

1) Too much defensive building, building, not enough maneuver warfare. Defensive buildings too tanky, and too cheap, vehicles too expensive. This was not as bad back in the day, now 80% of the game is building defenses and supporting it with logistics. Is this a wargame or minecraft? Holy cow ...
2) Too much "running around" too little fighting. Even with people trucking you around, you spend most of your time running to the fight, IF you are not spending it building or doing logistics. They really need to figure this one out. Make maps smaller or increase walk/run speed. Something, anything FFS.
3) Can still kill half your own team with a truck, running over teammates is a retarded "mechanic", especially when all the noobs are bunched up at the front of the building loading up. Make friendly vic damage 0 or near 0. Enemy jeeps can simply run over 20 dudes in the open who try to play "legit". They need to reduce enemy vehicle "run-you-over" damage.
4) Line of sight system and spotting enemies and friendly still bugged and/or just does not make sense, in particular in 3D space. Can't see my own dudes righ in front of sandbags at times. Enemies can sneak up to you when you stack sandbags and you can't "look down" and shoot them. Defeats the whole point of the sandbags.
5) In a heavily team-based coordination/coorperation style game, there are still no useful systems of command & control for your squads. No overall map commander that could be voted on and receive some special abilities to direct the fight. No robust tools for squad leaders other than put a stupid icon on the map. No way to quickly tell who your squad mates are, where they are on the map etc. They have an "operation" feature which is nothing more than another voice channel to spam you with chat. Too much voice & text chat "chatter", leads to overload and confusion.
6) Basic aiming system still not quite fleshed out. Hard to know what exactly you are aiming at and if you are hitting anything.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlready a solid and extremely addictive game, packed with action, teamwork and completely hilarious situations you will be telling stories about to your friends later. Once you enter the warzone, you can easily spend 12 hours in it - you won't even notice where the time went.
It's got stuff to do for any kind of player:
Those who like logistics can produce resources and weapons and supply the front line. It's always rewarding to hear the excited cheers of soldiers when you provide them with a new means to advance on the enemy. No other game I know made grinding for resources so enjoyable.

Builders can enjoy taking over towns, fortifying and expanding the network of bases with a nice variety of defenses. The high points of this faction's gameplay is fortification in an active combat zone and defending a base that's under siege.

Strategists are completely unlimited to design the best ways to approach every combat mission. The matter of success relies solely on who's plan works best and how well it's executed.

Fighters have a nice arsenal ranging from handguns and carbines to mortars, tanks and howitzers. I absolutely love the artillery gameplay.

There are some occasional disconnects, vehicle movement and physics are kinda clunky and some key gameplay mechanics (like disassembly of friendly structures) are not yet added, but for an early access game it's a blast! All it needs is polish and more powerful servers. There's voice chat, which works perfectly and makes the experience all the more immersive.

Overall, Foxhole very much feels like StarCraft in WWII setting, where each unit is a real player and you can move to any faction whenever you feel like it. I mostly play building and logistics, but every now and then I grab a mortar and feel like I'm a Siege Tank) I strongly believe this is a legend in the making! And a breath of fresh air in the microtransaction-plagued gaming industry. Keep up the good work!\t\t\t\t\t
