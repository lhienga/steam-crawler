\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, so this is my first ever review, I'm not the best at writing so bear with me.

Foxhole is a supremely unique game, allowing you to contribute to multiple wars in the game. Every person you see running around is a player and they all have their jobs and things they need to do. I highly recommend that you work together with other players by joining one of the many clans within the game or get some friends to join you. There's so much you can do in this game. Whether it's getting materials to make supplies for the frontlines, or be at the frontlines in combat with the enemy, or healing the wounded and getting them back in combat. Foxhole's greatest feat is allowing you to choose what role you want. You're not forced into any role you don't want to be apart of.

Pros:
  • Foxhole gives their players a choice of what they want to do and how they contribute to the war, which is more than most games give, especially how many roles you can play as in Foxhole.

  • Foxhole gives you a choice of what faction you like to join and every faction has different weapons as well as vehicles you can choose from.

  • Any amount of cooperation with other players can change the tied of the battlefield. If you enjoy playing with other players and teamwork you would enjoy this.

  • Foxhole's price is perfect for early access and for what it gives you. I never had any issues with the price, at least to me anyway.

  • If you like lore, Foxhole has a massive amount of unexplored lore that the devs placed in wanting players to find. They also have a wiki talking about the lore and factions about Foxhole. So if you like having a background story during the wars this is also a fun thing they have.

  • One last pro is the community is awesome, there's so much that the community does and makes up, yes it's a bit toxic here and there but in the end, the community is pretty cool and the devs are giving out some awesome upcoming updates.
Cons:
  • Foxhole is a game that requires a massive amount of cooperation. I know I said this is a pro but to people who like to work alone this is a massive con. You can't do much on your own. All you can do on your own is send supplies to the front and just be 1 guy on the front but you'll end up dying more than anything.

  • Foxhole is still in early access so there's still a bunch of bugs here and there, but I wouldn't let it ruin your experience being in a war-torn world.

  • In Foxhole, there are sometimes massive queues in certain regions, so much that sometimes you'll be waiting for 10-15 minutes to get into it, there are ways around it but still a massive issue if Foxhole continues to grow. Queues happen when there are more members of a faction than the enemy side. It happens due to the server size as well. Only handing a certain amount of players.

  • Another con to Foxhole is how you rank up, there's a commends system. So basically with what you do and how you do in the war, some other players will commend you which you need a certain amount to get a rank up. This is a very long process, so if people play this casually don't expect to get ranked up fast with this system. Some people will even ignore you if your rank is too small. When I was a Corporal in-game I was telling a 2ndLt and some other people that we were getting flanked but they refused to listen to me due to my low rank and they ended up getting flanked and killed by the enemy. Some people can be ignorant when it comes to those things.

Overall, Foxhole is a fantastic game that gives you so many possibilities and roles you can be, especially with every new update the devs do that makes Foxhole so much more interesting. With the cooperation of other players, you can do anything to change the tide of the war. I highly recommend you try this game and if you don't like it you can always refund, but give it a chance, also try joining one of the many clans to get the full war experience Foxhole can truly be.

Edit/Note, New things are being added every season, so there's so much to look forward to in Foxhole. Best of all, the Community recommends things and the devs listen. - Edit Naval Update was added and brought me back to it a bit, Always loved Naval combat and beach landing in Foxhole, now even more it's possible to hit your targets by using battleships. Highly recommended\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole's a gem in the rough. The premise is essentially WW1 without planes. Wars are played on a selection of hexes the developers "unlock" at the start of a new war. The objective is to capture enemy victory locations scattered around the map. To do this requires logistical, tactical and strategic level decision making. You will have to build supply lines by processing and shipping goods to front locations. Protect those supply lines. The fronts will use those goods to fight the fight. The ground taken or lost will create some new disposition either advantageous or disadvantageous. Eventually a side will break the back of the other. The entire process takes roughly two to three weeks by current mechanics. When a side wins, the map is reset, new hexes unlocked and the technology trees wiped.

What have the developers done well...

1. Harmony between PvE and PvP. These are two different gaming demographics that often DO NOT get along. They want different experiences. In Foxhole, you will be valued. Hardcore PvE'rs are treated like royalty by the PvP community. A war game that makes care bears feel valued, that's not normal.

2. Combat phenomena's extremely difficult to translate into a video game. Players operate avatars that don't feel pain and can respawn. That invalidates large chunks of real world theory. Concepts like suppression, maneuver, and the limitations of economics often being watered down to irrelevancy. In Foxhole, like real life, these things matter.

-Suppression; In real life, suppression is the attempt to create a specific phenomena. There's a subtle difference between someone shooting at you, and someone shooting in your general direction. Often the person that gets shot or nearly shot, isn't the person that was shot at. Often the round/mortar/rocket that lands by you, was not post marked for you. However in combat, it all often feels terribly personal. The natural response is to take cover, especially if it IS actually at you. To stay alive as your buddies work the issue. Obviously real life lacks respawns. So behavior changes in the video game world when a player lacks the gravity of their mortality. Foxhole addresses this by the effect of suppression. Depending how suppressed you are, your aiming will become terrible or you'll be unable to shoot at all.

-Maneuver; Shoot, Move, Communicate are the three essential tasks of any war fighter. Move in video games has a tendency to create two extremes. The channeled meat-grinder where there isnt much travel but also not a lot of options to move and the one hour bold flanking maneuvers over thousands of meters of CPU grinding terrain. This is the genius about their essentric use of camera. Instead of rendering 1000Ms of assets which then creates more travel time, the overhead camera condenses the need for empty asset generation while facilitating the environment for scouting, flanking, and other general maneuver concepts. Furthermore, players not in natural line of sight of your avatar, can't be seen. No god mode for corners.

-Economic Limitations; Every item used at the front was crafted and shipped by a player. The right items at the right place, at the right time, can devastate the enemy. You have gas grenades and they lack gas filters? The enemy trenches just got melted. They have ammunition for mortars and you're stuck in a trench waiting for resupply? Hope you're lucky. Ground was taken because you got tank support? Right on. But lacking building supply's to fortify means you probably wont have it for long.

Minor Mentions:

-Day/Night cycle affects the battlefield. Vision is diminished at night. Sight improving items like binos will not spot dismounts at night. All you will see is the fog of war. During the day, if you have good line of sight, you can see roughly two to three screens further than normal with binos. It's a minor mention because they didn't really nail the gravity of lunar or sun cycles on operations. When the sun rises, you dont want to fighting towards the east. When it sets, you dont want to fight towards the west. When the moon is empty, you can't see anything. When the moon is full, it's time to go to work.

-Weapon stability is addressed by your firing position, rate of fire, and level of suppression. Your recital may be directly on an enemy but if your stability is garbage, you'll be spraying and praying. This walked the line between minor and major mention. They paired this effects really well.

-Weight system for goods makes picking equipment meaningful. This is pretty standard in most games with options.

-Various hardpoints. Vehicles take different damages and have different effects depending where you hit them. Got that tank in the tail? It's spitting fuel now and needs repaired or it wont be going anywhere soon. Got it in the side? It's tracked, a huge mobility loss allowing the Infantry to swarm it like ants. Got an ambush on a supply truck? Likely gonna kill that engine. It's a minor mention because it feels too subtle. Players like booms, and I respect that. However it's Hollywood. Mobility kills and burning is typical. Explosions are not. Call me a stickler.

3. PVE'rs are called "Logi". And Logi (logistics) has a TON of depth. The entire pipeline starts with collecting raw resources, processing them into refined resources, taking those refined resources to factorys and creating goods, then shipping those goods to various distribution centers, and finally shipping from distribution centers to the various front lines. You can reasonably play entire wars as Logi, and never pvp if play conservative. Or you can ship to the hottest most unsecure areas and likely have to fight your way in. And if you pay attention, you can watch how your goods can change the face of a front. Which is very cool.

4. It's price and development. I looked at this game a year ago and it looked like garbage. Like a mobile game hashing the same PVE concepts beaten to death by every other lack of vision developer. Now it has enough depth to facilitate tryhard play and allow players choices that will butterfly throughout the game. The amount of content and replayability for $20 dollars and no sub or micro-trasaction, feels like a steal. Seriously, I find myself in the awkward position of wishing there was more I could do to support this game. Because it has amazing potential.


What the developers need to improve..

1. Technology grinding. Technology is unlocked throughout the war which enables the creation of new items and vehicles for the fronts. This means the wars develop. The issue is the method for unlocking technology is farming resource nodes hoping for special extra drops which then are spent on tech. In practice, this means teams will spend hours smacking objects for a chance at drop of a reskin object to stay technologically relevant. Miserable gameplay and extremely disappointing from developers that have shown a knack for clever solutions.

2. Lack of Auto Functions. No auto forward or auto smack. No channel functions. This means all your actions require white knuckling keys or your mouse. In 2021, how is this still pretending to be content? You're shipping a freighter worth of goods to a seaport a few hexes away? Gonna press that key for a solid 15 minutes. I hope you didnt want to ride a motorcycle or grip a coffee cup in your 50s. Which makes it a necessity for players to get autoclickers or keypressers to protect their cartlage. Thankfully, these are not considered cheating. However, it should be organic to the game!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Amazing game made by wonderful developers. Yes, there are problems that are kind of hard to be fixed, but the good things outweigh the bad ones. There's many things you can do, from joining an assault on a fortress-of-a-town knowing you're sent to your hero-like death, being a meatshield for the tank destroyer while they repair, to chilling in the back lines listening to Press Corps radio jazz music waiting for your basic materials to cook.

Do know before you buy it that it is a bit time consuming. If you can afford that, I don't see a reason on why you shouldn't buy it. Here's a story from my journey so far, while cutting logistics to an active frontline:

>be me
>wanna cut logistics to The Latch cause wardens am I right
>link up with 2 other chill guys and go hide in bushes
>chat for a bit, kill a truck, hide the stuff, go try and partisan another route
>no trucks around there, go to original position
>truck comes, gun it down in seconds, check what's inside
>1.5k basic materials
>grab a hammer, start building a fort
>plant mines and put up a sign saying "PAPERS, PLEASE!"
>destroy 2 other trucks
>after 10 minutes
>"hey there's a guy coming from the east"
>he just stays there for a while, looking at us through binos until night time
>"it's kinda quiet, I'm surprised they didn't send QR-"
>LE MORTAR SHELL APPEARS
>we go take out the mortar crew that destroyed our watch tower
>QRF(quick responding force, A.K.A the guys that act quickly to liberate a logistics route) appears surrounding us
>all of us die
>go back to see what happened and see that they destroyed everything and bailed
>we all log off after like 1 hour of holding off supplies to the latch

Sometimes it can be boring, but I'm telling you, it's just a preparation for the epic victory royale moments. 9/10 pretty solid\t\t\t\t\t
