\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThink you have what it takes to be turned into a flying ragdoll every twenty minutes? hmph, alright.

First ya gotta ask yourself, 'Am I ready to get shot, blown up, gassed, run over, or just killed through the sheer stupidity of both my enemies and my allies?' If you answered yes, then you have a chance of these occurrences taking place less often. Since, I mean, if you're prepared it's probably less likely to happen...right?

Next you have to decide on what your faction you want to be part of, the Colonials or the Wardens. Doesn't really matter which you choose, but it's a good idea to at least try both factions out. I will say that from what I've heard, Colonials are far more... shall we say, forward thinking, while the wardens are more disciplined, for lack of better term. Like I said, doesn't really matter in the long run so long as you have fun.

After that, it's a less simple question. Who do you want to be? There's quite a lot of options, assisted by the fact there's a massive group dedicated purely to logistics and the numerous roles involved in that (which I will roughly explain later).The only actual class is the one you spawn as is a simple soldier armed with a pistol and hammer. there's a bunch of sub classes as well, like medics, tankers, artillery, shadowdancers , scouts, partisans, and who knows how many more. Ultimately, it doesn't matter what type of player you decide to be, so long as you can help your faction out.

Ah, Logistics. Such a simple term for such a nightmare.
I'll start off by saying that while some players may dedicate their entire playstyle to this, most don't. The majority do it when they just don't feel like getting constantly shot at (though chances are you'll still be shot at occasionally). There are outliers of course, such as those who spend their day dedicated to the HQ as a 'quartermaster', and keep the entire frontline supplied. 'But Bucket!' I hear you cry, 'Why do the frontlines need to worry about that?' Because without logistics, the entire front falls apart in the span of a day. Every single item, and I mean every item, was made by someone in logistics. That grenade you just threw into the enemy trench? Crafted and shipped out by logistics. That lovely rifle you just used to take off that medics head? Logistics made it, probably at the same time as the rifle the guy that just got shot next to you was using. That tank that's currently running over your trench and/or running over the enemies trench? Logistics made that, filled it up with some diesel, and drove it over to the frontlines. You just died and have to respawn at the nearest base? You just wasted a logistics well made set of soldier supplies which includes your uniform, a hammer, a pistol, and two magazines for said pistol. That's right, every time you respawn, you consume one soldier supplies, or 'shirt' as the players have taken to referring to them as. If a base runs out, no one can respawn there, and have to head back to home base and redeploy at the next closest base. The only way to keep respawning is to have a logistics player get into a truck, load it up with shirts, and drive it to your base. Logistics may not have the most exciting or adrenaline pumping job, but it's certainly one of the most important.

This game is chaotic. Wars can be won and lost in a matter of days, or dragged on for months. A warden push can take half of the Colonials territory in a week, and then end up pushed back all the way to their back line in a day. Most of the time, it's more of a steady tug of war, but anything can happen. Each soldier, no matter their role, is important in their own way. a single private armed with a backpack full of grenades can turn the tide of a days long battle, and an artilleryman can fire so many shells the enemy is forced to pull back or take excessive losses, conceding their frontlines to the enemy. However you do it, whether it's by fighting or supplying, you will help end the war, one way or another. Good luck soldier.\t\t\t\t\t
