The key thing you will want to know going into it however is that teamwork is essential to a good experience. If you try to do things on your own in this game you will have a very hard time. I haven't looked at the new tutorials since they updated things, but if you ask, chances are members will try and teach you the things you will need to know about the game, and there are plenty of videos online (I recommend anything by 'I Saw a Bear' on youtube) that can give you some of the basics.

I also know for many of you this game being in early access is a turn off. The devs continue to work on the game to this day, and frequently engage with the community to try and make improvements on a fairly regular basis.

All in all I would at least give the game a try to see if its your thing. I started having fun literally the moment I was walking to the front after I had been trained. Got in with a group of experienced vets as the new private. I was scared, ill prepared for the horrors of war, and the moment I got shot and was dying on the battlefield my CO came to my rescue. We all died of course, but its the thought that counts. Now I am quite skilled at this game, or so I would like to think, and there are times where I get into the thick of combat as a total badass, and other times where I get popped by some noob who just so happens to have the drop on me.
