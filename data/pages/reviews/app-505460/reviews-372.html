\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe current state of game balance leaves much to be desired.

To make a long story short, both sides have a distinct doctrine - The Wardens favor armor and specialized tools (and tend to have better armor and worse infantry) while the Colonials are meant to have a strong infantry doctrine but slower and more generalist tanks.

The last three wars have had significant nerfs to Colonial anti-tank infantry equipment while strengthening the warden armor arsenal. The tech tree (Both sides have a tech tree set from the beginning of each war) has also favored earlier Warden anti-base armor while the Colonials tend to find their anti-tank later and later in the game.

While I'd like to think this is a temporary issue that will be worked out, it getting worse across multiple wars is starting to drive Colonial players to the Warden side, making the issue worse.

Given this is a two-faction game based entirely around PVP, balance issues aren't really something that can be ignored - In it's current state, I can't recommend new players get into it.

03-05-2023 Edit: Given I haven't played the game for months, I can't speak of whether the above problems of game balance have been rectified, but given what I've heard from friends still playing, I don't have any interest in going back to Foxhole for a few reasons.

- Poor Dev communications with players, and seeming disinterest in what the players actually think of balance changes and features.

- Little to no testing with rolling out significant changes to both frontline and rearline balance.

- Questionable development roadmap in general.

In short, whatever the store page may say, you're going to be treated to the Early Access experience of paying for the pleasure of constantly testing new changes in general, with a devteam that's indifferent to your opinion, unless you happen to be an influential streamer.

I don't want to be overly negative on Foxhole - Conceptually, it's an amazing idea, and I did get a fair bit of enjoyment out of it, I dig the WWI-II setting vibes that are inspired without being straight ripoffs of the real world, but at the end of the day, for something that people pay for, you'd expect the devs to pay more attention to their community - Especially since it's that same community that gives their player-driven game any playability at all.\t\t\t\t\t
