\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWars are entirely player run. From logistics, to front line combat, every bunker, magazine, vehicle, gun, ship, is made by players. The game has a very consistent and active player base (1.5 - 2k+ players online at any give moment higher during peak hours) Steep learning curve (fun once you get into it). Regular updates, fun community. I recommend you play this game with a friend but, you can always play solo or if you are looking for organized combat join one of the many clans.

Be prepared to sink alot of time into the game though. If you like top down games such as MOWAS or COH this is the game for you. Foxhole has alot of content for its price tag.
And lastly, do not be afraid to ask for help plenty of people are willing to teach you how to play the game.
There is no game like foxhole and once you get hooked you can't stop playing it is a one of its kind game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I was looking for a tactical large scale war game that really hooked my interest for quite some time now, this definitely hits the spot if you enjoy combat games with more depth and freedom than standard twitch shooters.

The community is overall surprisingly inviting, friendly, and even helpful with new players. The mechanics work incredibly smoothly with minimal bugs and glitches in its current stat. It also has relatively regular fixes and content updates despite the developers being a very small group from my understanding which is great. The devs are active, friendly, and upfront. Also it has a surprisingly well built sandbox tutorial zone where there no team damage. It has multiple types of training activity areas to play around with and get a feel for from basic to vehicle to artillery and even base building training.

A word of caution however, if you have a hard time ignoring toxic players, avoid playing on the Warden faction. While the majority tend to still be nice people that are fun to play along side with, there is still a very loud minority who will spam chat with intentionally offensive derogatory bs, spam ban reports for giggles or perceived offenses, and also even team kill or at least make a heartfelt attempt to if they decide they don't like something you're doing such as building a truck for yourself which they suddenly demand is theirs and to fork it over.

A fix for at least the last issue however if you have to play Wardens due to either their visual aesthetics or in game lore striking your fancy, simply point an rpg launcher or similar heavy ordinance weapon at them and they suddenly become very polite when threatened with an explosive death. :)\t\t\t\t\t
