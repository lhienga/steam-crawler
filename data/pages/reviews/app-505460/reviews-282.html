\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExtremely Fun and Unique Game.

I've played Squad, Post Scriptum, Verdun, Tannenberg, etc. I'm not sure I've ever had such a consistently fun experience as do with Foxhole. These "mil-sim" games are tough. I love the concepts, but often the games degenerate quickly due to small player bases and bad/no communication. Foxhole on the other hand has been a much better experience.

Players talk frequently and are super helpful. There is ALWAYS something to do, and a plethora of duties and roles to fulfill. The combat is difficult to get used to at first, but is not infuriating (you can actually see the guy who killed you, unlike Squad lol). Plus, the huge player numbers per shard is awesome, you never feel alone. The 24/7 persistent wars gets me super invested in my team, and I am constantly checking how the war is going online when I'm at work.

It is not perfect, but if you like this genre/style of games, I can't recommend this game more. Try this before anything else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI see someone with more than 10x my play time saying
#Discussions_QuoteBlock_Author
"Another great game ruined by bad decision making development wise"

This is basically true. It's 10 pm EST, the servers are barely up under the load. Teleporting is ridiculous and makes the game completely unplayable. There are LONG queues literally everywhere. If you are a solo player and not interested in being in a "clan" then it's mostly not worth it. Being frontlines fodder is not unique enough to warrant the ridiculous $30 price tag. You can see the in thumbnail trains and flame throwers. You will NEVER see any as a solo player. In fact, I've only seen half built train tracks because apparently it's so unbelievably arduous there are none.

There are some good experiences to be had but it's more like 20%/80% heavily in favor of being frustrated that you can't do a damn thing on your own. There are vets on the forum bemoaning how unfriendly the production elements have become compared to how they used to be towards solo players. I've been playing a week, I'm not a vet, but I can feel their frustration as someone who has had some very naive goals like "I'll make a boat and load it with medical supplies for the front" only to find out the logistics chain for this is simply not attainable alone at all. Even going looking for vehicles to take to try to do things like this is tedious and buggy.

Example from my last session, I found some haulers parked unused. They were locked. Got a wrench and trucked one across two whole tiles, loading it with salvage like half way, find the only refinery in multiple tiles which was like trying to drive in to an Amazon fulfillment center because there are so few this is like the beating heart of your faction. Refined some things to bmats and fuel - probably wrongly because they were not in crates (same concept but more items, maybe in less space? not sure). I tried to leave, following the 80 player made signs only to have my truck slide half way down a hill and get stuck in some rocks where it absolutely would not move. That was over an hour, wasted, for nothing.

I'm not a CoD kid or anything like that, but it would be nice to see some statistics at least of your impact on the game - not even asking for a kill board or anything, just private stats. You get nothing at all for doing the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of jobs that support your faction. You will very rarely know if it was you that got a kill or not. A lot of combat is done blind either from lack of visibility or horrendous server performance.

Anyway, Steam has only their binary system and, again, while there are some good experiences to be had, most aren't. I can't recommend this to anyone but masochists. The ideas are great, the implementation is mostly underwhelming. If you enjoy full loot pvp games where everything is player created (ala EVE) you *might* like this, maybe.

EDIT: 12/13/22 - Well it finally happened to me. I got permabanned while standing in game at Eidolo trying to decide what I wanted to do next. I had just delivered some wreckage salvage made rmats to the seaport and decided I was done in the back line and was going to go to home region to redeploy. Home region failed but no error message, I tried again it failed again no error message. I went to log out and it said I was "banned indefinitely for playing an alt account" or something like that. I have only ever played from this 19 year old Steam account.

EDIT: 11/6/22 - The game play improves based on your knowledge of game mechanics, many of which are not explained at all in the game. The wiki made by fans is invaluable but also not fully comprehensive. There are mechanical quirks which are clearly the result of how the game is programmed regarding objects such as all vehicles being treated the same - there are is no collision damage, and vehicles ignore things like barbed wire. This means you can ride a bicycle across barbed wire without even slowing you down and also that you can, say, park a truck or a tank in with one because it's treated the same as any other vehicle. Anyway, the developers have still made some bad decisions design wise, the game is VERY time consuming to do anything worthwhile, but, your knowledge of mechanics steadily increases the options laid out before you. Unfortunately random newbies will ignore your map pins and chat - but that's not the developers fault.

The main design decision is still enforced communism where there is no real ownership of anything. I heard someone describe this as a system of use, not a system of ownership. You have regiments that go to great lengths trying to subvert this, but eventually, all things end up in public - which is this most unique, interesting, and controversial aspect of the game, and also what makes it worth playing.

There are still hilarious experiences to be had like getting behind enemy lines and dying in a duel where the enemy who hunted you down with a radio and watchtowers dies on top of you because they bled to death and then you have a brief exchange in local chat because yes, you can talk to enemy and they can talk to you, which is awesome and super rare in any games. All of the negatives stand, and there are more than are listed here but I'm going to change my review, because for me at least, it's worth it, although I still can't recommend this to most people. If you're in to full loot pvp sandboxes though, this is for you.

EDIT: 10/17/22 - The developers actively ban and remove outspoken critics and their posts in the forum and lock threads that have overwhelmingly negative feedback in them. The game itself is still a mixed bag, but my knowledge improving has helped the experience somewhat, it's, say 25%/75% ♥♥♥♥/non-♥♥♥♥ now. Still enough to log out frequently from frustration either from bad developer decisions or your "teammates".

My game play has mostly been trying to find something that I can do alone that doesn't feel like an absolute waste of my time. This also has to be tempered by how much time I can actually play in a session. Last night I killed two partisans by myself and partially rebuilt an observatory that they had damaged and then I stole their boat. That was actually fun, and doable because it was nowhere near a front line that's nothing but lag with people teleporting everywhere.

But, then later on, I tried to get shirts for some people who took a bunch of tanks and captured a forward base but then had no logistical plan or support at all. All the seaports in a long chain were out and I gave up and logged out because it was apparent there was nothing I could about it on my own and it was late enough I didn't care anymore.

2 nights ago I tried to get a truck to drive a single haul of bmats because it was all I would potentially have time for. I got to a seaport only to find out, from a vet, that I had the wrong kind of hauler and it wouldn't let me put any bmats in the inventory - ♥♥♥♥ design, thanks devs. It could only hold salvage, so I took a huge load of salvage to the refinery, queued all of it in to bmats parked the truck and logged out for dinner IIRC.

This game is an extremely mixed bag, like Harry Potter Every Flavor Jelly Beans. Sometimes you get vomit, sometimes butterscotch.
