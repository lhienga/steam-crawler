\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is war, plain and simple.

With war comes all its joys and horrors. The fear of running from hordes of the enemy, the satisfaction of pulling of a suicidal banzai charge, the frustration of logistics traffic jams, etc. You will experience first hand what its like to play as an infantryman with a life expectancy of mere seconds, the pressure and comradery of tanking, the monotonous nature of loading an artillery gun, the high risk, high reward of partisaning, and lastly, resource gathering and logistics. This is the experience and war.

Top-down shooting in this game takes time to get used to. You very often have to use the corner of your screen for maximum reach and vision. There are no tactical reloads, and you will die a lot if you're on the frontline. Still, it "simulates" the narrow view you get when aiming down the sights, and can very often lead to tunnel vision - something useful for stealthy maneuvers.

Playing on and off is advised. Wars last about 1 month in real time, but this is where tech progression makes it interesting. The tech progression has you experience early WWI style trench attrition in the opening days of a war, only to be later followed up with hoards of tanks trading shots at each other in disciplined lines some two to three weeks later. It might be the same map, same players, and same guns, but the engagements, the strategies, and the frontlines always differ one way or another.

There is lots of salt in this game. Be warned, some veterans might team kill you, steal your truck, or straight up insult you. Others might encourage you, and mentor you to the ways of war. But if you can find some buds to meme your way along the battlefield with, have the patience to experience battles of attrition for days on end (fr, some battles of attrition last several irl days), and prefer to play on and off, then this game might just be for you.\t\t\t\t\t
