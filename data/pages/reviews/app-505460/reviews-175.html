\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelcome to Foxhole, the dream game that never was and never will be. As one of my friends put it, “They try to sell you this idea of a player driven game with all this crazy stuff happening around you, but really it’s just Euro Truck Simulator and Running With Rifles put together.”

I could rant for hours about the issues with this game, but I’d rather focus on just one issue, the main issue and the reason why this game will never improve: the developers don’t believe in fun. Now what do I mean by that?

Well, first let’s talk about all the different roles in the game and how the developers are slowly but surely making them awful miserable to play. No, you are not allowed to enjoy the game. You are expected to play the game like it’s a miserable job that you hate but are incapable of getting away from.

So let’s say you want to build. Boy doesn’t that sound cool? You could build anything in the whole wide world. Freedom. Creativity. That sounds like a great time. Well, get rid of those lofty notions. The reality is that you will install an auto clicker and sit your character around for hours holding mouse 1. Gotta shovel out the dirt to make your tier 1 bunker. Then you have to upgrade to tier 2 and then to tier 3, but all this entails is sitting there holding mouse effing 1 for your entire play session. This is your best case scenario mind you. If you happen to mess up part of your building or have to demolish something for other reasons, now you get to track people down and have them flag the buildings in question. Then you all get to take turns getting locked from using weapons for 20 minutes. This is the reality of building in Foxhole. And this is a game you pay money to play.

Okay well maybe I just won’t build. I’ll do a fun thing. Surely, there are other roles that I will enjoy. I know, let’s do logistics! Logistics is suffering, and it sure as hell isn’t fun. The only reason anyone does it is because they want to help their team and win, same as building, but by no means is it enjoyable. Doing logi means driving around endlessly, going to resource fields to turn your auto clicker on while you semi afk, sitting at a comp mine while your autoclicker collects resources for you, waiting on emat or factory queues, and many other boring mindless tasks. For every 10 logi players you ask, 9 of them will tell you they do it to help the faction, not because it’s enjoyable.

What about facility work? That sounds like the more fun part of logistics. What if you could just play Factorio inside of Foxhole? If only facilities were actually that cool. No, the reality of running a facility in this game is that you have an annoying baby who hates you that you have to babysit all the time. Having a facility means farming multiple fields of scrap a day to upkeep it. And you get to do this every day for the entirety of a war (about 35-40 days long). That’s just the farm to upkeep it. Additionally, you have to farm to run it and you have to constantly fight off thieves as well.

Fair enough, you think. Logistics is kind of boring sounding anyway. What about combat? That’s got to be a blast. Let’s start at the front line infantry. It’s fun at first, but it truly is draining when you realize that it is an endless back and forth. Any gains you made last session will be undone by the next time you log in. Over time, that realization really does crush any passion you’ll feel for frontlining, but if the combat was fun, it could at least make up for it. The infantry combat is pretty janky and mostly just RNG rolls on top of RNG rolls. That’s not to say there isn’t any skill involved, but the way skill factors in is in the most uninteresting and janky kinds of ways. It’s fun for a moment, but it’s not enough to actually keep you around for very long.

Well, sure, infantry is just infantry. How about the vehicles? What about the tanks? I will say that the vic combat is pretty fun in this game, or at least it was. This upcoming update will place really low limits on the amount of ammo vics can carry. Limits like 25 shells in a gallant, 20 shells in a FAT, 30 shells in a spatha. These shell numbers won’t even last you a full day cycle on an active front. This is the developers telling you that they don’t play the game without saying that they don’t play the game. Not only do you have less shells, but now they are going to remove the very convenient method of pulling them (more on that later).

These super low shell limits are hell for tank players, but it’s emblematic of a much bigger issue with the developers. Their answer is always to side on less freedom for players. The best moments in this game are when players get creative, when they pull off unorthodox moves, when they risk it for the biscuit. Sure, running 200 shells in a tank is usually a really bad idea, but sometimes it’s the best ♥♥♥♥ you’ve seen all week. The devs try to sell you on a vision of this game being player driven and built on emergent gameplay, yet when anything actually emerges it gets killed off. This upcoming update is even going to prevent people from shooting from on top of catwalks as if that is some kind of issue. The trend is always less freedom for players.

In the last update, the devs wanted to tackle the issue of alting. One such method of alting was having your alt account drive your main account into an enemy base in a friendly vehicle. Because it was a vehicle being driven by a friendly, the AI wouldn’t shoot it. The smart solution is to make AI shoot at any vehicle with an enemy inside it. Instead, the devs decided to remove the ability to enter a vehicle with an enemy inside it. So, back in the day, if you forgot to lock your tank, an enemy would hop inside and take over a slot. You’d get punished for it, and it made for some of the funniest clips I’ve ever seen. It was a good part of the gameplay. Now it’s just gone because the devs can’t think of any other way to deal with issues other than reducing player freedom.

Another form of emergent gameplay was people realizing that small trains can pull items really fast. You see, if you only have an hour of time for your play session and you wanted to run a tank, you’d normally spend half your time pulling shells from a base and loading them. It’s an awful part of the gameplay, but with small trains you could pull faster. This is a feature we’ve had for 10 months now and has become a core part of the gameplay. Sure, it’s not “realistic” but so what? It’s convenient. We don’t like wasting our time arbitrarily. We want to play the game.

The devs, in their infinite wisdom, have decided this must go. And no, they aren’t going to remove it and replace it with something that makes more sense. They don’t care about you and your quality of life. They don’t care about your time. Your fun is literally not a priority to them. Even though the community is universally and unanimously in support of keeping the baby train, the devs are going to kill it. It’s such a big middle finger to the entire community. You like this thing that is saving you time and making the game more enjoyable? Too bad. The game is apparently meant to be full of down time, cumbersome, boring, and miserable.
