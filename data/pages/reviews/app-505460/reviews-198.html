\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought the game and got really enthralled for the first 50 hours. I spent most of those 50 hours on the front line. Amazing game, such a thrill to do things tactically and have fun. I met a squad we coordinated had adventures and fun. Other players who kept popping up in the same areas I was fighting started to say hello to me and we would chat when we saw each other in the fight! That was amazing and truly unique experience I've only see in foxhole. The community on the front lines is amazing! 50 hours of enjoyment for an indie game is amazing!

What went wrong? Why is this review negative after all that praise? Then after fighting I decided to move to the back lines and do logistics. The logistics has some of the worst game play mechanics I have ever seen in a game. Collecting the basic material for all creation amounts to standing still on a mining node and left clicking. Not for a few hours, but ENDLESSLY, like doing logi for a week this could be 30-70% of your gameplay time just sitting there and left clicking on a mining node for 20-30 hours a week. Moving items to and from big factory storage facilities is a convoluted pain of button pressing, not explained clearly really anywhere. You do the basic tutorials and they DO not prepare you for how convoluted the real system is. Tasks that would be simple in any other like putting resources on a boat are now 3-5 step convoluted processes. "Get this specific truck for those specific resources, crate them up on that truck. Don't use the wrong type of truck." "Make sure to take these steps to load onto a boat and dont do it wrong" The gameplay for logistics is completely uninspired and dry.

It's clear devs focused on front line fighting and making the gameplay AMAZING as a community effort there, but what ended up happening from their lack of attention on logistics created one of the most tedious and unfun systems I've seen in a game.

Eventually I got griefed doing logistics by my own team and quit. I walked up to components node to harvest and some of my own faction said "this is our node". After spending the last 3-4 hours researching and building the RIGHT type of car to haul these SPECIFIC resources, then hours collecting resources with other people at component nodes I told them no. These are everyone's public nodes and Im gathering(mindlessly left clicking) here too. Everyone else gathered at the same nodes I was already at why did this gang of veterans think they own this one. They blocked in my car and blew it up wasting basically hours of my time, and all my resources for both the car and the collecting. I decided I was done and probably wont ever play again. The resource gathering system is super tedious and awful already. I can see why some of the veteran players would want their own private "public resource node". But it was the single most toxic thing I've had happen to me in a game, and let me tell you I have 4.7k hours in dota 2. Yes it was more toxic, and memorable than all that Dota 2.

I don't even know if I blame the players at this point either. Sure they were veterans bullying new players saying "hey these public resources are for us and if you dont leave we will team kill you". However the system itself is so mind numbingly awful I can see why veterans would want to skip as much of it as they can.

I had some real fun fighting on the front lines, ignoring logistics when I was new, but I cannot recommend this game to anyone because it has one of the worst collecting, gathering, and logistics systems I've seen in a game ever. It is not fun from a gameplay stand point. It's designs fosters toxicity like you saw from those veteran players. And the worst part? Logistics, gathering, and collecting probably take up 50-70% of the biggest gameplay impact and time you will spend.

Why play a game where 40% of it is amazing fun, but the other 60% is an awful no fun experience. I did think front line logistics was fun taking trucks to the front, but when you go behind the front line to the back line logistics(where you build the important stuff yourself), you will face an awful gameplay experience, and a chance at meeting some truly toxic players fostered by that no fun system.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly Encouraged to at least give this game a shot if you like games like Helldiver, any large scale Military games, or anything adjacent to those.

It won't treat you well, you'll probably die huddled in a trench with your buddies getting gassed, shot, or run over by oncoming enemy armor. But you'll die and laugh about it when you respawn.

The community is pretty solid, despite other reviews stating otherwise, sadly I've only really had experience with the boys in green, I however have been told it's pretty much the same amount of good to bad when it comes to interacting with players on the Warden side. Maybe a bit more hostility when it comes to clans/regiments, but that's only if you have a dedicated group swapping between factions.

But it's something to play, and is especially fun if you can land a spot in a casual regiment or just grab some friends and fight as a (somewhat) coordinated group. And to top it off, it's always fun watching the border war on Foxholestats.com.

If you have anyone who plays this game and has more then 50 hours, especially ask them for where to start or advice. Otherwise, there's plenty of old vets willing to be a guiding hand if you ask, or so happen to find one doing a course grabbing every new player they can in the tutorial zone like I did.

The only bad I can really name is that sometimes the devs are bias from one side to the other, but it's never one side rules over the other with impunity (despite what professional reddit posters claim). That being said, I wouldn't go on the official discord unless you're willing to bring buckets to harvest the amount of salt that gathers from the diehard European players.

Frenchmen are a colorful bunch of people, but I can't say I like how upset they get when I shoot them in a video game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo you wanna play foxhole, Imma fill you in on all the joys this game brings.

Positives:
1. Combat is enjoyable when the servers aren't having an aneurysm so its essentially watching teleportation for all.
2. The community can be amazing at times
3. Some portions of logi are well implemented

Negatives:
1. The entire community is full of toxic ass people that will wreck your entire game if they suspect your regiment did something, we're talking about weeks worth of work that can easily be griefed
2. The amount of work required to maintain a logi network is unreasonable for small groups to preform so when the large portions of the community simply cant be bothered the war will go sideways really fast.
3. The burn out rate is through the roof in this game for multiple reasons, a great one to look up is "human pipe" which is when pipes aren't unlocked until the metal works factory (about 1 week of game time) so you have to manually transfer all fluids.
4. Their is no way to appeal bans, now why does this matter you may ask well thats a great question. If you are a solo or smaller group you can be mass reported until you are auto banned from the game, now why would this happen. You see when you play the game you sometimes interact with large regiments that may not be so friendly to the team effort and are under the opinion an area is owned by them so if you "trespass" or they believe for whatever reason you have done something they will mass report until the problem is resolved 9 times out of 10. unfortunately this is such a problem that you are guaranteed to have someone banned (for any length of time based on previous bans or amount of reports} within the first couple hours of every war due to fights between regiments over locations to build bases.
5. The game is a time sink. Now I got into this game and continue to play this game because of the relaxing fights with wardens and the struggle, this hardly matters anymore. If I want do anything from building guns to tanks it takes so much time it is honestly ridiculous. An example would be the construction time on a train engine, its 6 hours just for it to be built. Now I think this does help balance some aspects of this game but if you look at the pull speeds for anything in this game it starts to get nauseating. If I wanted to take a train to the front lines normally I would take 12 storage containers seems simple, now to fill each one of those containers it requires crates of supplies which can be guns , ammo, or other supplies. To fill those containers from public storage at a port or depot it takes 30 mins per container (so times 12) because of the pull speeds, there are entire systems to try and avoid these pull speeds but randoms don't use the systems so someone has to eventually empty a port or depot.
6. Finally its the allusion the devs think is happening which is 24/7 fighting and logi to supply that fighting. THIS DOESN'T EXIST. If you want the hard reality logi makes up maybe 20% of the player base but even then not all logi is equal since trains transport more than trucks obviously. Now when we discuss the front line combat its comical to watch every war a bunch of people play for a bit then get bored of a stalemate since you usually need the higher tech items to push through defenses that both sides build. In the end the game just isnt balanced with the player base especially when 70% of players are in NA or EU so there are times where the servers are dead.

TL/DR
The game is massively hemorrhaging players because the devs have set their eyes on a vision that simply wont exist, if you buy this game expect disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t
