\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDay 38 of the 87th conflict between the legion and the so called Warden empire... my fellow soldiers are charging the Deadlands, the echo of their hurrahs could be heard throughout the world. It was my first time driving so close to the front. I had just finished my training.

I was driving my truck on a country road to our capital, filled with the "spice" of war, our seaport my was goal. Behind the curtain that is the frontline, I was not prepared for the shock.
I loaded my spice on an Ironship and set sail while aiming for one of our most vital outposts. Some shot were taken but the fear of loosing everything carried me to the objective. It is upon arrival that I understood, I had yet to see war.

There was a welcoming party screaming and laughing, how have they longed for the spice. They took me in their group and with their help we organized a convoy. 5 people who were total strangers to each other would protect it with their lives for they knew; their brothers are in the dense bloodstorm and they had been out of spice for days now.

For the safety of the delivery, we cut through fields and forests, then we arrived at the destination against all odds, untouched by bombardements or partisans. We got close to the bunker base and that is when I saw the first shell hit. The land was shaking, dozens of soldiers were running around panicked hoping to dodge the mortal blasts that were making the local landscape a flat no man's land.

2 of the 5 trucks from the convoy actually made it to the bunker, I was in the second. Upon arrival, we did magic. I had never seen so many people unload crates so fast. With our armory full, one of our officer organized a team. At least 15 soldiers regrouped and organized a counter offensive. The way was shown to us by the veterans, we knew where and how to kill their artillery.

We joined in a trench half way to our objective, at the hotspot of our frontline. A whistle started whisthling, Hurrahs were chanted in harmony, without realising, my own battlecry was fueling a rush of sudden adrenaline. Then an officer jumped out the trench and ran, 2 followed and I was immediately behind and we poured between the warden's bullets, some fell, some passed through. All I remember is the fiery explosions and the colors mixing together: blue, green and red were making one.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun game WHEN TAKEN IN SMALL DOSES
Cons:
If your expecting any kind of interaction from the devs your looking at the wrong game.
Bugs that are known by the devs are not fixed.
Alts and hackers have to be dealt with by players because the ingame moderation system is broken and as mentioned above, devs never interact with the player base.
The logistics is often frustrating and time consuming often leading to player burnouts and even ingame strikes by players!
Balancing issues as Anti tank weapons for instance are more expensive that tanks, and other weapons being too strong compared to the other teams.
Queues in frontline regions often cripple player experience since depending on the day or time you will have to wait potentially hours in order to start fighting in the region.
Siege camp is in a state where it's much happier to sell the game often times more than once to hackers and alters who ruin the gameplay for many many players rather than fix the issues.

Pros:
The community is very active especially on reddit or discord.
Playing casually for small periods at a time allows you to enjoy the game and the realism of fighting a war for weeks at a time.
The game creates a large sense of community and teamwork.
(Mostly) Very friendly people to talk to and fight alongside.
Quite appealing graphics and gameplay when it works.
Could run on a potato machine

My personal advice to those who want to buy the game is to join a regiment and have fun but do not invest days and nights into the game. I'm sure maybe in the future Siege camp will eventually fix the moderation and balance issues but for now i'd recommend waiting for a sale before you pick this game up.
