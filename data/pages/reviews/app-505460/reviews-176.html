Without a solid group of friends, this game is absolutely miserable.

Cheaters, Griefers, and Alt users continue to destroy any potential enjoyment the playerbase may have.

The fact remains I may play this game more after this review, because friends that I enjoy spending time with still play it. However, I will never under any circumstance recommend you give this company a single cent for this game or ANY OTHER they produce. Don't let the name change fool you, this is still the same awful Dev Team that will produce "Anvil" or whatever they name it.

The only good Developer is Julian, the Art Dev. The rest are garbage.\t\t\t\t\t

I made many friends because of it, it gave me much joy, and a lot of stress as well.
There are so many things to do and learn, even with 2k hours I didn't experience all of them.


The game is unique, fascinating and immersive, even with it's isometric camera. A treat for a military game enjoyer.

Until you have to deal with it's bad side...

And for me, these are:

- The game is an unpaid job.

To be really effective, you have to work really, really hard.

And spend a lot of time and effort on organizing and communicating, since everything is made by players and it is a massive war.

It is just not healthy for the average player. It does not feel like a game, but a job.


- Only large organized clans will experience the game in its entirety.

1.0 changed the game with the "Facilities", those huge Logistics Hubs with pipes, cranes, trains and all.

And that limits solo players and small groups playing with subpar equipment, aka. Base Vehicles.

Now the good stuff is locked behind these facilities.


- Unbalance causing unfair battles.

"Power creeps" are common because of asymmetrical gameplay, turning the game into faction loyalist ego fights on internet forums and players crying for balance all throughout the war.


- Unfixed bugs and instability.

Some regions are barely playable, the engine itself can't handle too many structures, which is part of the gameplay to defend a territory, some bugs are still being exploited despite players reporting them for a long time.


- Long queues at peak hours. (Not so much as the game declined in player numbers)

Maps have limited player capacity, causing very long waiting times. Some players called the game "Queue Simulator" before quitting for good. The game does not have a system to kick AFK players from active frontlines, which is unacceptable for an MMO.


- Game is infested with "Alts".

Cowardly cheating players that buy secondary accounts on the opposite team to unfairly disrupt and sabotage.

Plus, the devs do not perma-ban these obvious alt accounts. They only get punished with time suspension.


- It has no anti-cheat protection.

Believe it or not.
