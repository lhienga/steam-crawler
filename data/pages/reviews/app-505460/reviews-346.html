\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Foxhole can definitely be fun to play at times (I wouldn't have gotten up to 757 hours of total play time at the time of this review if it didn't have something fun going for it), for right now I would recommend against buying this game, at least until it leaves early access.

Despite the claim of this game being "stable" and "balanced", I'm afraid that's not really the case. The game servers do have a bad tendency to cause players to disconnect at times, which can cause significant frustration if said players happen to be carrying many items on them to transport to a base or depot and especially if those same players are driving expensive vehicle, such a tank (Which can take actual hours to build thanks to the time it takes to refine the materials necessary even be able to build it). The voice chat servers are even worse in this game. I've had many times where my VC would randomly disconnects from the VC servers. Despite there being a in-game button to reconnect to the VC servers, I've found that the button, more often then not, simply create a looping error reconnect errir, requiring you to close and reopen the game to actually fix the VC. If you happen to be in a region (Often called "Hexes" or "Hex") that's at capacity and you can't reconnect though the in-game options, get ready to wait the edge of a hex for a long period of time just to get back in.

As for game balance, due to the fact that this game is in early access and the developers want to focus on getting this game fully released, balance is simply a secondary issue to them. Sometimes, game-breaking bugs can be found but rather than fix them immediately to minimize the effect they'll have in a war, the devs will wait until a war is finished to fix it (War in this game usually last 3-5 weeks). Even in the absence of bugs, if something happen to be clearly overpowered, usually nothing will be changed regarding the overpowered equipment, even in subsequent wars. Only in updates, which usually occur once every 3-6 months, will there be changes to stuff.

Chances are, a lot of these problems will be fixed in 1.0 update and this review will change and most likely be a recommendation to buy, but for right now, I suggest waiting till the game gets out of early access.\t\t\t\t\t
