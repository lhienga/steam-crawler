\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike most of the multiplayer warfare simulators on the market, Foxhole suffers from its poor design of the player feedback loops. You want to win the war, but you are meant to figure out the short-term goals, motivations and rewards on your own, as the only systems the game offers for that are 'commendations' and the 'ranks', neither of which serve any mechanical purpose.
It also, like most of the multiplayer warfare simulators on the market, requires unrealistic amounts of coordination from mostly random strangers, and the only systems the game offers for that are 'map markers', 'squads' and 'regiments', all of which are as primitive as you can imagine.
It is naive to expect every player to be able to, to know how to, and to want to communicate with other players verbally all the time, and it is very sad that Foxhole's devteam chooses to add more weapons, more vehicles and more complex features, instead of bringing the basic player experience to an enjoyable level.
The game is IMMENSELY fun when played as a large group of friends though, but so is setting homeless people on fire.

P.S.
Logistics are boring and un-rewarding, infantry combat is wonky and frustrating, and there are also no plans to add EU or Asian servers that I heard of, so you are stuck with a Canadian server, in a game with one-hit-kills and full friendly fire.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played about 56.4 hours in my first 2 weeks of owning the game and I can say I've about caught up to speed. I haven't seen all of it yet but I've seen enough to know how the game goes. The combat is a bit strange, it's got a bit different mechanics than anything else, but that doesn't mean it's bad. Took a little getting used to but it's pretty fun. It seems like it would be a combat game but overall I played much much more logistics. I just tried to write a review and messed up like 6 paragraphs worth so I'll keep it short. Most trucks cost basic materials. Flatbeds cost rare materials tanks cost 3 times the price of flatbeds. I fought as a soldier to get a feel for what the front would need then wanted to do logistics. I started scrapping and then gathering components. After finally making the flatbed I was able to gathe resources a much faster and transport larger cargo. Little did i know, everything needs to be squad locked or else they get stolen. Someone stole my firzt flatbed. So i had to start from scratch and build a new one. Eventually our team researched tanks so flatbeds were easy to come by and not getting atolen as much but now it was tanks. I made 2 by hand then transported them while my 3 crates were producing. Got 2 to the front for me to use. But that had taken all day. Next day I was able to come back to my tanks and use them and I picked my other 3 tanks up and put them in my boat. Along with a shipping container with all my tank ammo I made overnight. It took hours for them to produce, and much muchlonher to save the materials to build the 3 I built. The ship was relatively cheap, costing as much as 5 basic trucks but not requiring rare materials. Someone took my ship. It had 3 tanks and a container with ammo. No one said anything. No one admitted. The commend system is designed in a way that you're rewarded for delivering things by a little yes or no box on the corner of everyone's screen on if they will commend you or give you xp pretty much .. so teammates are motivated to crouch up to your belongings and wrench them open for them to deliver for their own xp. Is that them "just playing the game" or "helping the team" or is it just unorganized chaos. The majority of time was spent bumper car-ing around the factory refinery places just for someone to steal 75% of all of my acquired work. I only managed to get 2 of the 5 tanks I made and none of the extra ammunition. The gameplay isn't bad, I enjoyed it. The commend/xp system needs work, or the private stockpile of combat vehicles or squad lock timer needs extra work. I've never experienced something so ungratifying in any video game I've ever played. I've played games such as Rust for example where you will gather everything you can starting from nothing for the enemies to raid you and steal it all.. but even that is better than teammates stealing it. Even if I would've died in my boat full of tanks because I ran into enemies I still would've been less upset. Atleast it would've been my fault. But friendlies stealing you're hard earned equipment?! That's unacceptable. I would give it a 6 out of 10. Might play again\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is going to be a buyer-be-aware review.

First the positives:
- If you like RTS games, you like the old Alterac Valley battleground from vanilla WoW and you like rewarding teamwork, you will like this game.
- Easy to get into, but never repetitive, as your enemies and team mates are other human players.
- Stable, never had any crashes.

Then the be aware:
- You will hit a wall if you play solo; Certain systems require more than one person to operate and if you don't have friends to play this with and for one reason or another don't want to play with randoms, plenty of stuff will be out of your reach. This is not necessarily a bad thing, the game is by design teamwork oriented, but if your access to team mates is limited, tough luck.
- You will hit a wall if you play with friends; With friends, the game gets much better. Now you can operate a tank, an artillery gun or even a gunboat. You can take on pretty much of the enemy with confidence, but if you run into an organised enemy guild operating more artillery/tanks/etc than you do, it is time to join a guild yourself to stand a chance. Again, not a bad thing, teamwork for the win, just something to be aware of.

Then the bad:
- The community can be anything between friendly and incredibly toxic.
- The game rules are enforced by the devs with a roll of a dice.

- My personal experience with the combination of these two comes with the following story: I went to a garage, a building in which you build vehicles, with an intention of building myself a truck to deliver goods. While I was browsing the trucks to build, I was team-killed by a fellow faction member with Chinese symbols in his name. First I thought I had been blocking his access to the garage and he got frustrated because he couldn't get to build his vehicle, but that wasn't the case, he just went to his other friends, also with Chinese symbols in their name, who were now blocking the access to the garage with their armored vehicles.
I respawned, was pissed that my no-deaths playthrough of the war was ruined, told them not to act like CCP during Tiananmen Square Massacre, they teamkilled me 4 more times.
Now I thought teamkilling in teambased war game was punishable behaviour, but it appears stating a historical fact of Chinese Communist Party killing unarmed civilians is more punishable behaviour. These guys were not suspended, but I got 2 week suspension on my account.
Mind you, they could always have muted me if they don't like something I say, but I can't make myself unkillable by toxic faction members.
I heard later it is possible for big groups to use the in-game reporting function to mass-report individuals they don't like, which almost always leads to suspension of the account of the individual who they didn't like.

After that I decided to apply for a refund. If one can play the game for less than 5 days, accidentally state a historical fact which would give you a shrug at worst in other online games, and then get a 14 day suspension, well, why pay for a game you can't play.\t\t\t\t\t
