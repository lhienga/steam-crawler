\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan't say I'd recommend this. It's a very absorbing game and very fun to play but the combat is genuinely bad. Gun aim is terrible (half of your bullets miss despite aiming and clicking on the target) and you can't see anywhere past ten or twenty feet unless you use binocs or a sniper rifle, yet.. Somehow.. Other players can (even when you don't shoot a gun) and use this to instantly kill you with their guns which aim with near perfect precision each time and are NOT sniper rifles. Call me bad, say I suck, I don't care.

I play video games to have fun, call me a casual say I'm lame, I really don't care what some people chuck at me for disliking the state of this game. I was told this game was fun and sadly and this isn't the case. This game painfully reminds me of CoD and the constant aim hackers, general try hards that would always ruin the fun with their "hardcore gaming" skills and the bad attitude that the game breeds onto everyone. Will I keep playing it? Sure, maybe. Will I eventually lose interest due to griefing, hacking, dirty tactics such as flanking the front line, midnight raids on all your outposts and just general balancing and community issues? Yes, I will. This game really is worth the 12 dollars I paid to buy it since it's basically a vietnam simulator and either side can turn into the vietcong in almost every situation where a clan is involved.

When a side starts to lose all of the "hardcore" clans show up and outnumber you during twelve AM in the morning, storming every outpost they can and crapping on all of the defences you've built over the day with their mortar and howtizer shells. There is no winning, there is no losing. Instead it's a painful drag towards an inevitable loss as more and more people become more and more desperate to win and resort to griefing and shady tactics to do it.

Edit:

I'll define the type of griefing that can occur right here and how the devs can't and won't do anything about it:

The number one method of griefing clans use is they'll get an alt account on a VPN and join the opposing side, they'll then proceed to go to the front lines and steal ALL of the supplies in the enemy outposts. They do this slowly as to not arouse suspicion, usually five or ten supplies at a time and the person doing it usually brings them to the opposing outpost without anyone noticing.

The thing about this tactic is people who pay attention to supply lines such as logi players (me) DO notice it but are entirely powerless to stop it as there's no way to know who's stealing supplies. I've seen dozens of supplies vanish and they do it in such a way as to make it look like people are just dying and respawning.

It'd be easy for someone to get in a truck, lock it and then rush to the FOB to loot all of the supplies while everyone is pushing the enemy FOB and while it'd be painfully obvious, the person's throwaway alt would have accomplished their goal. Keep in mind that twenty dollars (13 since it's on sale) is nothing to some people and cheaters will have many many throwaway accounts to do this on. One fine example of griefers having dozens of throwaway alts would be minecraft where you can literally buy packages of 500 accounts from Chinese hackers.

When I spoke about this happening in-game someone silenced me saying I'd be banned just for talking about how easy it is to grief in this game and how everyone is probably doing it. I doubt what the person said is true and actually think they're one of the griefing alts. Afterall, bringing light to this happening would mean the devs would be forced to do something, but since there's no real reporting system in this game it's difficult to report people let alone prove anything when it comes down to hacking, cheating and griefing.

This brings us to the next method of griefing, which some of you will think is fairplay and not griefing at all:

Midnight raiding, this is when all of the European and American players (western players) are gone to sleep and the eastern clans (Russians, Chinese, etc) all log on. They scatter around the map like cockroaches and start griefing and nuking all of your FOB's before then fortifying the small amount of land they've just stolen from you.

This makes it so that by the time you get back from work, school or whatever they've pushed you all the way back across a tile or have just outright stolen the tile from you. You can say this is due to faction imbalance but since this is a clan focused game eastern players will want to join eastern clans. If those eastern clans are on a specific faction they will go to said specific faction.

The fix for this is easy but they don't want to do it. They have to lock specific regions and make it so no one can enter them during specific times while giving everyone a headstart to retaking anything that was griefed. This would force everyone to go to one or two tiles and focus all of their logi and fighting there, ruining any possibility of midnight raiding. Will they do this? Ofcourse not, that's not what the clans and players would want right? it wouldn't be realistic right? Well, sometimes sacrifices must be made to improve gameplay, regardless of whether or not players like it.

This brings us to the next tier of griefing, the most insidious of them all and the one type I haven't seen yet but that people will likely begin to see fairly quickly if they pay attention and try to expose it.

Spies, yes when people get desperate (Ie, one team starts to control more then half of the map) they will literally spend hours grinding in the opposite faction for those commends and will then start lying and deceiving players into traps.

These spies are in the opposite faction's clan(s) and will cooperate on discord with the enemy clan to lead players into horrific situations that coincidentally look like failed pushes or skillful gameplay on the enemy's end (ie, knowing where to flank, where to rush in with shotguns, etc). This is why they can shoot you when you're out of range sometimes as well, they are legit screensharing with someone on your team who's sitting back with some bino's or just telling them where you are. There's no way to prove or disprove this either, you have to play with the knowledge that anyone could be leaking intel to them from your side on discord.

The devs can't know who's doing this as the only way to know would be to infiltrate the hardcore clans and have players spill the beans on this. Even then, they'll know it's happening but won't know how to deal with it as how do you know who's doing it for sure? How do you prove their guilty? How can you link their discord logs to their steam accounts? There's a lot of shady tactics people can use to grief.\t\t\t\t\t
