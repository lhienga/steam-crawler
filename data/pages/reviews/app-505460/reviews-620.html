\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Buy the game, but ask for help when and where you can. Don't be afraid to speak up and ask a question.

This game just hooks you in. There's barely any RNG, it's just pure gameplay, teamwork, and tactics, and just the unspoken rule that you should always help whoever you can.

Fight on the frontlines to push or hold the line. Drive logistics trucks or freighters and make sure the supply line isn't cut. Go into the backlines and help in the effort to harvest and create materials to be used to make the supply for logistics, which is then driven to the frontlines, which will help win the war.

There are so many layers to this, that every action that anyone does has a ripple effect. You delivering rifles to a base could mean they hold the line just long enough until reinforcements arrive. Someone taking your truck full of supplies out with an ambush could lead to the loss of a whole region. Storing your nuclear rocket parts inside tank could lead to it getting stolen (this happened, it led to a town getting nuked).

You will have a blast playing this. The game has no mechanics which try to make it addicting (daily challenges, FOMO events, etc.), however, it does have one downside.

Big clans tend to be pushy or demanding, and will most definitely abuse the Comms Rating System and Report Systemn. If you ever get downvoted while speaking in the text-chat in-game, your Comms Rating will go down, prohibiting you from speaking or even hearing people. Usually, this would stop spammers, but what has happened is that people abuse this to mute players entirely (Research about GASHeimdall, an important player who got muted).
Clans also abuse the report system, where these clans can get you banned for days, weeks, months, maybe permanently, just for some petty reason or a misunderstanding. They essentially get all members of the clan to report you or someone. It sucks, it absolutely sucks. The Devs have tried their best in everything, though I have yet to see them address this properly.

Overall this game is a blast, I would give it 10/10, but the abuse of the reporting system and comms rating system ruins the game for some players, especially new ones who don't know what to do. Though, I've only encountered this problem with those who play during the day in American and European timezones. Perhaps it's a cultural thing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Fun, worth your time *especially* on sale. Best played with others, but not necessarily your friends. There are usually enough people who are more than happy to work as a team with you. It's as intricate as you make it, get it!

What Planetside has been struggling to perfect, Foxhole has found a unique way to change up the first person shooter MMO treatment of these games and even gave it what most of games like it fail to obtain; a sense of unity. Players gladly keep the front line supplied. There is quick and dangerous traffic at cities that focus on actually MAKING vehicles and weapons. There are even buildings that people build to contribute to a faction-wide tech tree. Just today the defense I was with said tanks were about to be finished researching; I can't wait.

The proximity chat is wonderful (text or voice) and you can ask people for help and they WILL help you! It's incredibly charming to go into combat and helping people with medical supplies. They go down, calling for help, and you go charging in to pick them up and carry them away. Five seconds of healing and they're back up and ready to fight.

If I were to organize its gameplay, I'd say Foxhole covers about 4 styles? I recommend mixing them up.

1. Ask people what they need and bring them it. Use a huge variety of transports for different amounts and different items.
2. Get materials and make stuff. Gather up mats to make buildings and weapons for the delivery people or for your own.
3. Go frontline. Help injured, mow down people with turrets, throw devastating grenades. Fire where the tracers come from!
4. On-field planning. Get cranes to position turrets best you can, hammers to build barricades and structures. Barb wire.

There *are* some things that remind me this has the Early Access slap on it. Small things that I can't rightly remember because they were small.
