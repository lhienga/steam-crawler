\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDear Devs, If you are reading this, Please skip to the Devs section written in caps below. Thank you very much.

If you are a person curious in buying this game then here is my story.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
I saw this game on youtube

Started playing it

Have been playing it ever since

Now the reason I'm writing this review instead of playing it right now is because after grinding junk into materials for 3 hours and turning those materials into weapons and ammo in order to give said weapons and ammo to a bunch of strangers I don't even know, I crashed and now all those 3 hours I spent are now thrown to the wind and wasted.

Am I disappointed? Yes. Will I return to the game? Absolutely.

The game is still in it's beta. The bugs are few but notable. There are a couple of features I would like to add but that's written in the dev portion.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Whether you are a casual player or a hardcore no-lifer, this game is suitable for you.

Experience a world where the entire playerbase is divided into two. Where you will be fighting a war that will last REAL WORLD days, weeks, even months. Enter into a world with an ever changing sceneries that are affected by weather and the ever constant war that takes place.

Watch as buildings that used to be there slowly crumble and deteriorate into permanent ruins.

Be a rifleman, shooting the bullets to the enemy,
Or be a driver, who delivers the bullets to the rifleman
or be a gasman, who fuels up the vehicles that the drivers use
or be a refiner, who uses vehicles to refine scrap into basic materials and fuel
or be a scrapper, who takes the scrap out of the scrapfields and gives them to the refiners
or be all of them, I don't care, take a load off sheesh.

Grinding
the grinding can depend. You can grind crazily for hours making the guns, ammo, grenades, and misc stuff to bring them to the front so you can have fun with them while the other players freeload off your hard work.

or you can be said freeloader and enjoy the fun at the front
(pro tip: if you want your favourite machinegun or ammo but don't have it at your assigned base, just post a map marker and whine like a ♥♥♥♥♥ along with your fellow soldiers on the front. Instant delivery)

Gunplay
gunplay is easy to grasp, easy to master, but very very very situational. You are no hero that can just go rushing shooting others. you find cover, stick to cover, and stay there for a good 5 minutes (let that sink in). You stay in that same spot for a good 5 minutes before you move deeper into enemy territory. That's hoping that your dear precious cover doesn't get fragged by a gasnade or a fragnade.

I wouldn't call it fully realistic but the devs took a good shot at it. I'll explain in the dev section.

you want a tip to earn you kills right away? take a grenade with you.
want a tip to stay alive? take a bandage
wanna get good with a gun? hah, go find out yourself I sure ain't telling you :P

Graphics
Low spec is good.

Price
worth the price

you want fast paced shooters, go play valorant
you want to grind for yourself alone? go play warframe

but if you want to feel the satisfaction of every kill that you make that you witness with your eyes, or establish friendly connections with strangers, or be part of an organized team of camaraderie and brotherhood

this is for you.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEVS, THIS IS MY FEEDBACK FOR THE GAME
(if you haven't played this game you probably won't understand this, so just skip this section. if you played this game a bit then you will know what I mean)

a couple of issues

in the refinery. please let us control the number of bmats that we take out of it. Literally, I just hate it when I want to take only a hundred bmats and end up taking all of my inventory space and 1000 bmats just so i can build a truck.

You can run people over with not much speed but cannot dent a bicycle when ramming at it with full speed.
pHyS1cS

your aiming reticle shrinks just as fast as aiming far away and aiming point blank. If our crosshair is near the player, which MAKES THE GUNPLAY UNREALISTIC. It would be easier to aim at things that are near you accurately than to aim at things far away. Imagine taking 3 full seconds just to land an accurate shot at an object 2 meters away from you, then taking 3 full seconds to land the same accurate shot at an object 22 meters away from you. Make it so when aiming at things near you, the reticle shrinks faster. When it's far from you, take the full 3 seconds.

Movable HUD. Let us move the compass and the vehicle and the fuel and all the other stuff. It's annoying enough that we have to play on the corners of our screen. At least don't block the way.

OR you could make it so that when aiming on the top side of your screen it gives you just the same amount of distance as aiming to the sides of your screen.

More complex driving. Gear shifts, drifting, brakes, acceleration. Make the driving entertaining at least.

When driving, the vehicle rotates, the camera doesn't. Place an option where we can keep the camera angle "locked" at the same stance of the car. If we place the road we are driving to on the vertical center of our screen, then when we turn the car, also automatically rotate the camera angle. A feature like that would be interesting, in case anyone would be partaking in races with the car and road improvements.

Make the scrap salvaging more entertaining as well, or make it less "attention needing". No one pays to play a game where doing stuff feels like a chore, and scrap salvaging feels like a chore. In order to get the playerbase more divided from frontline to logi, make the logi more entertaining. Stop giving the frontline all the fun and attention.

A form of currency would be nice. Let's say like, for every 2000 scrap you refine, you get one "Logi Token". You can give this token to other players in exchange for services, temporary authority and power, or even items. If the player is given a token but refuses to do said service, like, escort a logi on a shipment, the logi can report the violation and get the "Logi Token" back.

Preferable if the Logi token is not a "material thing"

let us build houses, or repair them. and let us place chests inside buildings or houses. Let us sit on chairs.

chests inside bunkers would be nice too tbh

farms, and food. eating food would take twice as long as self applying a bandage.

being hungry would apply debuffs like slower running speed or certain foods would apply certain buffs like extra hp, or extra stamina, or longer fov, etc.


cheers. love your work devs, wonderful work, but chin up, the end is nowhere near
