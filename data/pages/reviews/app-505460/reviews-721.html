\r\n\t\t\t\t\tits a really good and strong concept but in my honest opinion it needs a few things.

on a MACRO army level its great. there is drive and goals to meet and tech to gain. It feels as if your army is leveling up giving a sense of cohesion. Also there is real feel for change and contribution to the effort in meaningful ways.

on a MICRO level... its harder to say. There is no sense of personal or squad development in the same way. the personal drive to stay on and keep going dwindles much faster than that of a game that has some sort of RPG element where your character improves or changes. I dont think the devs are currently interested in change cosmetically because i believe the vision that they have is to visually form a cohesion between players and make it feel like your just another soldier in the squad. that makes perfect sense to me. What I would like to see is some sort of development as a soldier.

my idea for that.
So without making something so overt that would override balance but feel rewarding is a simple stat system. Maybe something like GTAV online does to maintain balance. The more you do something, the more efficient you get at that specific thing. EX. the more you harvest materials the faster your collect. the more you heal units, the faster you are at it and so on. Also on the balance side, have the stats deteriorate over time so one cant just have perm great stats all the time because he decided to stat farm one day. It would also motivate players to get on more often and stay on longer.

also just something on my personal level, take it or leave it... Id like to see some sort of automotive storage system. it gets obnoxious to see some town just littered with abandoned tanks, jeeps, and trucks. a "rabbit hole" method might be too OP and can be exploited but maybe something in the way of a parking lot logistics system where you can custom build a parking lot? like in the way that the trench system is done. like have a tile for one type of vehicle then tiles for others. and players can just connect them in however they feel they need to keep it organized and keep the towns from getting so clogged up.

Just a thought. I like this game and where its heading and i think it has truly great potential. keep at it Clapfoot. its a cool vision you have.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttldr: Recent 'Arms Race' update a welcome improvement, but the game only seems worthwhile if you're in a clan and even then logistics, team organisation and moderation tools are sorely needed.

===

Purchased this game when it first came out and quickly refunded it, as there was a clear lack of direction in regards to gameplay and infrastructure to allow the required teamplay. I re-purchased the game after the recent massive'Arms Race' update, and quickly got hooked! There is now a clear gameplay direction for logistics, and the combat itself is fun, if a little comical at times.

This all being said, I soon reached a "ceiling" as a individual player that loathes being apart of clans. The logistics aspect of the game needs some serious attention to prevent it being an exercise in frustration, let alone even basic tools to allow different languages to effectively communicate. These frustrations apply to both the individual and for larger groups/squads, so that both can't work together for the team as a whole.

Sadly these frustrations are also spilling-over into un-moderated toxicity within a team which thankfully I've only experienced in passing but still has now completely deterred me from the game. This is also combined with the lack of general moderation tools for racism, hate speech, etc.

In short, development needs to focus less on "cool vehicle/weapons" and more on:
1) Introducing more tools to help team coordination, especially logistics
2) Improving the integration between individual players and larger squads/groups
3) Allowing the individual player to have fun with the breadth of gameplay without feeling like they need to join a larger group
4) Adding more tools for moderation and language barriers

It's a pity. This game now has great potential, but I won't be coming back for a while until the above issues are addressed.

====
2022:

After leaving this game to hibernate for 2 years, I am re-reviewing it and the issues I initially stated above. I have also joined a regiment which has exposed me to the large depth of the game that was previously hidden to me as a solo player.

However this depth is still off-limits to most solo players, or even those playes part of smaller squad with 10 or less players. Thus it seems it is still up to the commnuity to provide not only this organisation to allow access to this depth, in addition to the communication required, with little assistance from the developers. One example is the pseudo-requirement to utilise Discord and it's in-built community and voice communication, whilst long-standing GUI bugs still exist e.g. FPS bug when a squad list is open with large nuber of players. Given that Foxhole gameplay reqlies heavily on teamwork much like other games such as Squad, this is a large oversight byt he developers to not foster this aspect better, as per my points (1) and (2) above.

As also per my previous review, the gap for solo players still exists in regards to this gameplay depth as per points (3) and (4). Again, Foxhole relies heavily on the community to provide the instruction and training, with little being provide from the GUI intuitively, nor from the tutorial system. Entering a regiment of veteran players is daunting and very much "sink or swim", which could very easily deter new player if not fostered correctly.

As before, this is all unforunate. I'm changing my review to "positive" but there's some really large opportunities being missed here. Development needs to focus more on helping the community coordinate in and out of the game better, and thus 80% of the gameplay, rather than the 20% of content creation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fantastic War MMO and a hallmark example of why more War/Army MMOs need to be made more. Before I throw in my own opinion specific opinion on this game, i'll break down three ways to play this game. Mainly because I feel like a mini-guide can help someone on the fence of continue playing or even buying get a broader decision on those acts.

-Combat
The upside to combat is it's subjectively pure enjoyment. I say subjective since I don't know if theirs any more fun isometric shooters out there than this since i'm not really into shooter games, but adding that you're almost always fighting in a new location every time you log on and use the area around you while you have limited resources to work with giving of how the game is formatted gives an exciting rush of managing your skill level with your resources. The cherry on top is an army simulator so you can lead/guide players into tactical missions to black your opponent.

The downside is, despite my rambling of how fun the game is, independent combat provides little productivity upon pushing into newer territories. If anything, independent combat just primarily helps with defense. The only way to genuinely push your army into your opponent's territories if hundreds of people in your faction decide to attack a certain region or a dedicated clan decides to bombard a poorly guarded region. Personally that's where the flaw of the game comes, but i'll get to that later

-Logistics
Basically how well you're about to push into a location or defend their location is primarily dependent on how much logistics is funded into your front-lines. You essentially have to make stuff and transport them somewhere to be used properly. How vague i'm explaining it is due to how theirs so many ways Logi can be performed, which may sound overwhelming but it's not and any form a Logistics is welcome by your team. On top of that, it's one of the easiest ways to get commends.

Although Logistics does feel nice helping out your fellow soldiers, it is a very time consuming task to build/transport materials to a base. I recommend multi-tasking with music or something else or even doing Logi with a friend, but overall it's dependent on what you want to do.

-Fortification
This is technically also Logistics, but the game is called Foxhole and ironically building Foxholes are a great defense from invaders trying to take your base. The reason I put it in a category on it's own is because building a good base is just as time consuming as transport logistics and even long-term combat. A well fortified base can make a base survive a full frontal assault, even if it's only guarded by 15-30 people. Just like Logi, it can range from many ways to do it, but dependent on your location.

Final Thoughts:
Most importantly, this game requires teamwork, which is why I have issue with the clan system somewhat. I feel like it does break the immersion in this game a bit by making clans more important than ranks. The game is about 3 or maybe more years old and all Ranks really do is show how long you played. I do think they have a potential to do more or at least restrict new players from wasting bmats(basic materials) or high-tier vehicles. I'm not asking for silly cosmetics or stuff that gives higher rank players advantage over other players, but giving players an incentive to aim for something aside from winning the war. Or hey, maybe boost up the rank exp each war dependent on how long that person contributed in the war.

Regardless, I do think it's a fun game to hop on for either a short-term or long-term play, just jumping on and shooting the opposing side down is fun by itself.\t\t\t\t\t
