\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has issues if you no-life and take it super seriously. It's not perfectly balanced and there are a slew of updates that shift things around constantly.

That said, the game is perfect as an, "I have an hour or two and that's it. I need all of my gaming time to be as fun as humanly possible" title. You can log in at any time and find tons of action. Quick and easy thrill ride whenever you want it. Learning is easy, skill cap is very very high. You will have massive adrenaline rushes and some unforgettable memories the likes of which haven't really been present in gaming for the last 10 years or so. I guess I'm saying you have a legitimate shot at reclaiming some of the joy you used to have when gaming.

TLDR: Pure fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first played back in War 93, I spent hours just driving trucks and barges. Didn't hear a shot fired the whole time. I drove bmats to Buckler Sound when the siege broke. I brought wrenches to the Battle of Ulster Falls during the dismantling of the Fuhrerbunker. I hauled artillery up the hills in Reaching Trails to destroy Fort Mousetrap. I delivered shells to the recently-liberated Blemish to help destroy what can only be described as an obscenely large fortress to the south. I was present for every major engagement in what was by all accounts a historic war in the role of logistics grunt and it was some of the most fun I've ever had in a multiplayer game.

This game is a highly effective autism diagnosis tool, and continues to improve as an experience over time. Cross-faction voice chat makes it easier to discuss things like the fates of prisoners, whose jingoistic nationalism for a fictional country is the strongest, the physical and personal characteristics of someone's mother, etc. 8/10, balance issues are commonplace between patches but aren't as bad as people make them out to be. Game has an adequate number of boats at present with more on the way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first tried this game back in 2016 and something about it made me buy it 2017, after that I played on and off for few years and by the time of fall 2020, I had only 130 hours. Then I found my place in the foxhole, a group I have spent almost 2000 hours with and learnt/tried most of the things in Foxhole with that group. In these past few years I have seen best and the worst of the foxhole, the community playing part on both.

In Foxhole you can hop on and hit the frontline to test your skills against all sorts of opponents and use both your factions weapons and those which have been taken from the enemy, or go to backlines to do some logistics and chat about the most random things while scrapping with some people you have never met before.
Ofc us veterans can be rather short-tempered at times and we can be rather harsh towards new players making a mistake that we have seen few hundred times already. That is on us, but I have been also trying to help wherever I can and try to teach new players that seem to need help. There are some unspoken rules within the community which some have had to learn the hard way. Also the fact that the home island's training courses are obsolete makes the player to player interactions even more important.

Foxhole still has that little something that makes it unique and has kept it's charm over the years. Player driven logistics, true persistent warfare, after the war starts it's goes on 24/7 till either side wins. Game where everyone can find their place in the war machine and help the team with their efforts.

Now that devs have been teasing us about the future Naval update and have addressed the issues with alting, building, facilities and partly logistics I am looking forward to see what future holds for this game.\t\t\t\t\t
