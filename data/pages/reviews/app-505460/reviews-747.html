\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this in a heartbeat.
Why?
I played this for over 100 hour and I think it's the best of it's kind.
Please don't listen to the children complaining that the logistics or build up is too long.
What are you expecting from a warfare game in this dimension or this kind?
Definitely not to get resources and equipment spawned in.

Game got a lot of tactic and possibilities, but you also rely heavily on each other. If Defence doesn't do it's part building and fortyfying = You lose. If Logistic stops farming and producing equipment = You lose.
Yes, sometimes the teams are unbalances, but I also had a lot of times where good clans and organized frontlines made that more than equal. Lots of good players with a great understanding of the mechanics.

This Game is for you if:
You like to work for your win. Build up logistics, farm basic resources, refine them, reinforce frontlines and build vital buildings like watchtower and defences to keep your cities and production safe.
If you like to tactically plan everything. From the equipment you need, where you strike, with what you strike (amphibian, sneak in with snipe support, bombard the enemy position with artillery) All of that is possible.
You like to work in a team and know that if you or someone else don't do your part, it's getting difficult.
You know that some parts of this warfare can be grindy. The way to some resources, technology and equipment is long.


DON'T PLAY IF
You are a whiny child that is just in for the quick action. You can take a rifle, amunition and just storm forward, but in most cases it won't help anyone but waste resources.
If you expect an easy play, easy win game.
If grinding and logistics horror you.


This Game is a bit unrefined in some spots, but very well playable.
Some trolls try to ruin it - be better.
Play for and with your team, not against it.
Seriously: Read the guides before buying or starting the Game to get a feel of the size. Don't rush in.
While it got an "starter-island" to learn, some things should be explained more thoroughly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't accomplish much in the 6+ hours I attempted at this game and it wasn't a jump-in-and-play type game like it advertised - so overly kinda lame. There seemed to be a lot of poor developments going on; both with graphics and frame-rate for the when this game came out that was unexcusable, and for executing game-play; nothing productive, team based or design to funnel the fun into anything enjoyable and crafty. The map was huge; massive really for its player count of 160-180+ odd game occupants.

The vehicles all locked and/or a pain in the in ass to navigate the interface to spawn - not to mention the scatter of voice chats with no direction, where player would ban new players to taking a truck or jeep because they'd bump poorly organized troops as they drove down the road or they'd get shot up the minute you came across a group of npc? players? bots? ... I say ? because they just seemed to deploy outside of range and would trash you at every instance with no npc, player or bots of your own to support your movement. Not to mention at times they seem to have about as much natural fluidity in gameplay as a degregate aimbot drone - so no idea of they were "actual players" and the voice chats were "real time". Did I mention the map is huge, and again no idea how "enemies" got that close to spawn points at times, let alone how the game mechanics of a persistent war worked with servers who clearly "meta-game" static defences at every turn - including these massive airstrike pushes and infantry rushes that seemed very server-side scripted.

What I did enjoy was the sense of scale and artistic elements. Everything else, the clunky weapon UI, the mindless running and aiming using the mouse pointer, the poor driving scope and the abundance of international players, all with a hate on (sorry PTSD) for self-segregating over the mic, made it kind of pointless to attempt coordinating transport, map area attacks and anything remotely class/weapon based in wave attacks over the voip.\t\t\t\t\t
