\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my first 4 1/2 hours of playing the game (these numbers will skyrocket) I ran around with a rag-tag group of French players, having one guy, Nashaga, to translate for me. We ran back and forth delivering Basic Materials to one camp after the other. Then we're ambushed by enemy soldiers out from a brush, we clamber out of the truck and deal with the soldiers. They were dealt with, but it wasn't a fight without a price, several of our men had been shot and died as we realized we didn't have the medical supplies to heal them.

We carried onto the next camp, everyone in a quieter tone, almost somber.

This game is NOT for everyone, it's a slow paced war sim, where you play a cog in a world war, taking thousands, potentially millions of lives. You won't be a hero, you probably won't accomplish a strategic plan, you may even not win the war. But that's not all this game is about, it's about the people you meet, the teamwork that's put into completing an objective, and it's about feeling as though you're putting a single brick into an ever expansive and for boding wall of a conflict.

You will encounter terrible people, not often, but can happen, but from most of what I've seen everyone is trying to work together to pull their weight, I thoroughly recommend this to people if you're looking for an experience that makes you feel tiny and insignificant, but nontheless important.


9/10\t\t\t\t\t
