\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very fun game where every bullet you shoot deserves a "thanks" to your logistics players. Your combat success is highly dependent on controlling yourself in a chaotic situation, and no one is superman. You can charge in with a bayonett, wreck the enemy artillery, get back to your allies, only to get killed by a stray rifle shot from a jumpy ally in the bushes.

No one player is all-powerful, but your actions still have weight:
* You can take a barge on a coastal map and steal or destroy the enemy's entire afternoon of logistics because no-one was guarding the port.
* You can single-handedly supply an entire forward base long enough for the enemy to run out of supplies, and for your allies to storm the town.
* You can build a chain of defenses along the entire front and irritate enemy partisans to no end.
* You can target for a mortar team and wreck the enemy defenses with your partners
* You can play a combat medic who runs out with a medical kit and a gas-mask and never lets a team mate go without a fight

All of these are valuable and personally rewarding ways to help the team win. If action isn't your style, grab a hammer and some music and do logistics. No one hates a person willing to supply the front line, especially if you put the time in to bring all the nice toys to the front!

That said, at this point there are some things to watch out for:
- semi-frequent glitches (occasionally irritating, mostly humorous)
- logistics is, admittedly, not the most exciting thing (they are working on it!)
- gun-play can feel ridiculous at first (but you get used to it)
- occasional arguments in chat (being polite rarely steers you wrong)

and a few other things.

Overall the game is fully playable, and seems to only be getting better.\t\t\t\t\t
