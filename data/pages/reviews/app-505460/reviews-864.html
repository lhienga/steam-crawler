If you have an extremely large amount of free time (like, 40 hours a week), 2 to 3 friends who can commit to always playing with you, or want to deal with being in a clan, then you could possibly have some interesting times with this game, but for anyone else there really isn't anything here for you beyond a very mediocre top-down infantry shooter. If you're really into military sims, I suppose you could find that aspect interesting.

This is a game with features that sound good on paper and some impressive technical achievements, but it's all ultimately overshadowed by poor game design choices that value the developer's idealistic vision over the practical realities of the players' experience.

Much of this game is just hours of tedium to create the possibility of some interesting moments, a possibility that often does not pan out and when it does, it's usually pretty mundane. This game has downright contempt for the player's time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have only had the game for 5 days, but I don't think I've ever had so much fun in a game in so short a time span.
The experience is new, refreshing, and it looks like it's here to stay.

Here are my favorite parts of it:

> Player-built Structures; this wall of sandbags you're building right now could save the lives of literally dozens of other people just a few hours from now.
> Similarly, the structures you build now could be gone tomorrow... which makes you want to play just fortify it even more and make sure the enemy doesn't destroy your hard work.
> Spending your time driving around carrying supplies and/or men is actually fun and fulfilling (Look at first point).
> Actually use real math to calculate for Azimuth for artillery bombardment; There's a google sheet online somewhere for this, but before I learned of it I actually used a piece of paper and a pen to calculate accurate fire while using Artillery.
> Very open and welcoming community base; Plenty of teacher-type players who can guide new players with different aspects of the game.
> Amazing rendition of war through beautiful beaches and towns that will someday have emplacements and other structures or debris.


Overall, this game has a great incentive-reward system, an awesome player community, and A-okay graphics that can please most graphics junkies.

Only thing I don't like are the occasional bugs but as it is in Beta as of the writing of this review, that is fine with me.

9/10 watch out for lag spikes\t\t\t\t\t

TL:DR
Persistent warfare, lots of logistical support, and a lot of frontline soldiers yelling for a medic.

The Good Stuff
+So the most common thing I hear from those who dislike this game is the logistical support involved and the grinding needed to do that. It does indeed involve the step of gathering some materials for a short bit such as scrap or fuel, and then refining that into better materials and then making an item from a big list of things needed for everyone else to properly fight. There are those of us that don't like that at all (I myself hate grinding but I do a little bit of logistics when its needed) but there are those who absolutely love it and enjoy acting as support instead of actually fighting. I think of the logistics support people as those people who grind to get 99 woodcutting in RuneScape, or play RPGs to death just to max all levels out. So my point is this: If you hate logistics and grinding... don't do it... someone else gladly will
+A massive world map that consists of the whole war. Within it are several regions to fight in (which may have a short queue to join them to prevent overpopulating). Within those regions is a detailed map showing lots of information on how the battle is going and where points of interest are
+A wide variety of weapons, tools and items to use on the battlefield to fit your specific style of playing
+This game rewards the patient and conservative player more than the very aggressive sort. Although it's hundreds of small players on one map there is still a ton of strategy involved on how to approach situations
+The environment is dynamic and shifts in real time. Time of day, weather and player built structures will happen constantly therefore changing the scope of the battle at hand nonstop
+The dedicated community in this game; those that play the game seriously and for fun, are very helpful. Yes this game has trolls, noobs and other jack-offs like every other multiplayer does but they are easily overshadowed by those who are genuinely playing the game. I learned most of this game by asking my friendly players how to do stuff. In return you can commend fellow soldiers (Press F1 to see the menu for it) which gives them a public rank to show how trustworthy of a player they are. You can also kick and mute players in this menu, in which the kicking system starts by banning the player from weapons for 20 minutes after a few reports and then inevitably a swift kick/ban from the region
+People roleplay in this game, giving it a very realistic feel to it despite the top-down cartoonish look. Riding in the back of a truck heading to the front line, training with a fellow stranger, or banter between the enemy and you. It's all very enjoyable and adds to the experience of the game heavily

The Ok Stuff
=In-game voice chat works fine, but it can be a bit hard to tell who is talking due to their voice being mono and not stereo, meaning they sound the same regardless of their position to you. Also the VOIP icon next to their heads will not appear half of the time signaling that they are talking
=Feel free to correct me on this for I have not tried using a wrench or breaking into someone's vehicle; friend or foe. There is the concept of locking vehicles. Most people who run logisitics do this to prevent trolls and more often than not someone running by and snatching what they have. It's an interesting concept but you can also break into others vehicles by using a wrench. Most people do this on enemy vehicles that were recently vacated or dismounted but the fact that a friendly could easily break into your own vehicle is a bit annoying.
=This game has quite the learning curve, moreso the logistics part of the game but it's still a fairly complex game requiring a bit of time to get used to mechanics. I still fumble controls after 15 hours of playtime up to this review

The Bad Stuff
-The chat window involves all chat in the game, such as Region (All), Region (Team), World (All), Local (All) and more. All combined into one window which becomes very cluttered and sometimes hard to read as some people don't know how to use it. It'd be nicer if this was divided into separate tabs in the bottom right instead of one solid window (and if that's a thing and I missed it then my apologies... someone tell me how to fix that XD)
-Vehicles aren't the smoothest but they do indeed work, you just have to be very careful with them. Roads are your best friend but the less traveled roads are a pain and usually don't work. If you're an infantryman, prepare to get run over a lot by speeding vehicles that don't have a good viewing distance
