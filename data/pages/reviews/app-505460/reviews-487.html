\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 3k hours, update on review:

Foxhole in general is a great game, you wont find any other even close to it, which is why I give it a thumbs up (I still enjoy playing it, and still play it as of today).
Its does have its cons and problems tough, and ill mention them here:

1) Problems with voice chat, constant bugs, not being able to hear local vc.
2) Problems with returning to home region, this happens frequently. Having to spend more time that it would be necessary otherwise.
3) Warden arsenal much, much stronger compared to Colonial arsenal:
- Blakerow, Fiddler, Sampo - 3 most overpowered guns as infnatry - they are OP when it comes to shadow dancing, or stopping shadow dancing. Colonials only have the Argenti that is actually decent, especially in terms of shadow dancing gun. Other guns are simply mediocre.
- Fiddler - the most broken infantry gun in the game - it has very steady aim when moving the mouse cursor fast; the recticle shrinks very fast, and stays that way even when moving; the precisions is hilarious, shoots like a laser gun acually; and the distance it can shoot is also crazy far.

- White Ash - AT weapon that is superior to anything that Colonials have in their arsenal - easy to track enemy armour even with splash damage, quite far from the tracks. Its range is crazy, you can throw over obstacles with its long arc and range. Can be used on barges, autoequips so you can throw multiple without going into inventory. Colonial counterpart, which is the ignifist is so much worse - does not autoequip, almost bounces everytime, even 5 times in a row from fresh armour.

- Warden tanks in general are move powerful, whats more, 3 basic variants can be made in a normal garage, without them being locked behind facilities (Outlaw, Silverhand, HTD).
- Warden tanks have so many variants - the Bonelaw and Highwayman - two distinct tanks which collonials do not necessarily have a counterpart.
- The Highwayman one of the best tanks in the game, shreds armour, has high chance to track or turret, again Colonials have no counterpart, not even slightly resembling the attributes of a Highwayman.

I couldnt care much for the game lore in general, but seems like Wardens have a more evolved lore, where more work had gone into it (which again is a sign developers contribute more to Warden faction).

All in all, develpoers cater more to the Warden faction, by making certain things easier to do, and in general a stronger weapon arsenal.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHenlo, this is my experience with my current time in the game as a Colonial.

There are some small buggy bits, but nothing that can utterly shatter a game experience, now that that's aside...

So... the game is DEFINITELY worth the money they're charging for it, even at its Early Access stage.
The gameplay at face value is typical top-down shooter-esque, but once you get into it there's a multitude of levels across all of the branches of the military force, even down to Logistics.

The game has something for everyone, even if you want to be a non-combatant you too can help the war effort by supplying the front with supplies, weapons, etc. Can even build, or help reinforce existing bases! If you want to be super sneaky you can do partisan work and sneak behind enemy lines. If you wanna focus on artillery, or even tanking, there's a regiment or group of people there to do those.

Even with the game at its current stage, there is no shortage of people willing to help if you're new and willing to learn, all of my experiences with the general community have been SUPER positive for the most part with very few bumps, but for the most part, everyone finds their comfy setting and helps the front in their own ways.

Oh, and for the people saying the mass report thing is abusable... As a colonial, I've come to realize, people only resort to this once an immediate threat or an outright recognized hindrance is apparent to more than just a singular regiment. This isn't to simply wish away people they don't like but to stop people who deliberately choose to go against the war effort in some odd way, like despawning things, friendly fire of particular units, stealing supplies, abusing intel since they're actually on the other side. Heck, even if someone ELSE is being harassed, people will go to the "victim" and ask wtf happened and if they want the "abuser" reported.

All that being said, I look forward to the continued development of the game and what else is to come, especially after hearing that they have seasonal events for this game and that they're actually quite fun!\t\t\t\t\t
