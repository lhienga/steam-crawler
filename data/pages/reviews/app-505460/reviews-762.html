\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTakes some time to learn the mechanics (as you've probably seen its a top down isometric camera; but there are 3D objects on the map that obstruct your vision, obscuring what you can see (and shoot) on map.); but after a while it is a very rewarding (albeit frustrating system).

The initial cycle of prepping at your spawn point, killing Collies and looting their bodies is pretty fun. But if you're not willing to play at least 10 hours in a few days, you wont really get a 'rewarding' experience.

Wars; the game you're going to play, last multiple days, even several weeks. They require an obscene amount of supply and grind to win, and the longer the war drags on, the more resources MUST be consumed. You realize that for you to spawn on the battlefield, Scrap had to be harvested, shipped, unloaded, refined and transported to a factory to be turned into shirts. Those shirts were in turn shipped to your spawn point, and 1 was consumed when you spawned.

The sheer need to gather materials, the manner in which they are moved around and refined and ultimately consumed vastly differentiates the game from any other. The Devs seem to have a love for the game, as there are continuous updates (played on and off since war 22; the current war (49) is MUCH better.), with more updates on the way.

For $20 its 7/10 ; For any sale its a solid 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
