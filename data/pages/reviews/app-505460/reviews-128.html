\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried it once and returned it. The top down myoptic view gives me a headache.

I was convinced by a long time game guild member to give it a try again. So I bought it again.

Well again I say that now I feel like I am hitting my head against the wall playing it. It takes hours to move products. The entire game is akin to a bunch of ants moving helter skelter around the map some obviously doing good but the vast majority going around in circles doing little to help the over all war effort.

All everyone does is run out as a fighter or drive around blowing your horn as a trucker trying to deliver 20 guns and 100 rounds of ammo. There are so many abandoned trucks around it seems the war could have been won if just one less truck per minute was produced and one more tank or even bullet.

But then a squad buddy told me the game was a Real Time Strategy Game and the players were like the little men. You know that you manipulate in those type of games that say "Yes Sir, Right Away, Sir" when you click on them to do some task like build a base, a bunker a factory.

Then suddenly the game became fun. Sure you can go to the front line and fire you gun with the same top down view. That gun someone brought you from a factory way back behind lines. That gun that has bullets because someone drove a truck and gathered raw materials then drove it to a refinery, factory and put it in storage. Even your ability to spawn some player had to create and move to the front line base you are fighting for and that allows you to spawn and get another hand delivered gun and ammo.

So if you enjoy being a bit player, If you enjoy base building if you enjoy strategy then this game is for you. Maybe you will build the Maginot Line or the Siegfried Line, who knows. No matter you will make a impact little by little. I recommend it to you.

But the truth be known the combat visuals are myopic top down crap with the need for constant camera angle adjustment. If you get in a crown you cant tell what your character is doing.\t\t\t\t\t
