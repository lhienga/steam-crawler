\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine an MMO version of Cannon Fodder (a game nearly 30 years old now), but with shoddy controls / gameplay.

You have a non-zoomable, mostly top down view centred on your character, with a slight amount of wiggle room around that centre point. This would make sense on square monitors / resolutions, but with everyone widescreen these days, you're constantly rotating your camera to try and see / shoot in the corners of your monitor where you get farthest view.

Gun play is plagued by RNG and LOS issues from undulating terrain you can't see from above. Couple that with having to try and spot people in the top corner of the screen and it doesn't make for fun gun play. Stance affects accuracy as one would expect, but RNG comes along and shots will regularly go completely out of your crosshair's cone of fire. It basically makes hitting people more chance than skill, or a case of shooting automatics into that damn top corner of your monitor hoping that stray shots catch someone off-screen.

There seems to be a rank and levelling system, but there doesn't seem to be much point to any of it. You jump into a server where every square meter is rammed full bunkers and defences (built pretty much physically overlapping each other for hundreds of meters at a time), often filled with top end equipment, so I don't know what we're supposed to be working towards. So I'm here to go and fight through a stalemate for no actual gain?

Local voice chat is filled with people screaming over one another so working out who is saying what is nigh on impossible when things get active, or other people shouting at you or team killing for trying to build defences in a way that they don't think is right.

The logistics and crafting system is well though out but very tedious. If you like spending hours watching various progress bars, this aspect of the game is for you.

The most "fun" I had was riding bicycles with two other guys for 20 minutes trying to find an enemy bridge to blow up with satchel charges. It didn't work though as we were killed by AI controlled defences before we could see them.

One good thing I will say though is being able to pick up wounded players (friend or foe) and run off with them like a kidnapper is funny.

Foxhole is a good idea but executed poorly and for me it really misses the mark.\t\t\t\t\t
