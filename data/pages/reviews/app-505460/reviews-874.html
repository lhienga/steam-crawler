\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game since it's very first days in early access and watched it grow,but sadly, I'm not too fond of the game as of yet. The game has a lot of promise in my eyes seeing as I'm a huge fan of "Running with Rifles" which is where this game came from, and it adds it's own system for logistics and such, which is awesome. But sadly, the game falls behind in player-base. What I mean is that the amount of players that are usually active is too low for the size of the maps and for the game to be the slightest bit interesting. It also relies too much on it's logistics system where you basically play hit the scrap pile and walk your over-encumbered ass to a factory to start an item queue that takes forever to complete. And back to the players, the ranking system is weird(in my opinion) and the players in a match are incredibly disorganized running around every-which way until they get mowed down by a turret which would be fine in RWR, but not here since items have to be created or looted off of corpes.

In the end, I can't really recommend this just yet since it's lacking some important components.

EDIT:

After playing this game more recently I've noticed that the issues that I complained about previously have been somewhat resolved, but other issues have also come up.

The player base has increased by a lot. This pretty much resolves my previous gripes with the game. This is because the map is now more populated, more players are now playing logistics, and larger, more organized clans are active.

The increase in player counts does have a drawback though, and that is that parts of the map now need to be separated into separate server zones. This is the new issue and, while it does serve to keep latency and frame lag to a minimum, it does tend to heavily inconvenience players, specifically the logistics players. Every server zone(Hex) has a max population which is nearly always maxed in the central fronts, which means that logistics players can't get in and deliver supplies to the front line bases, causing unnecessary attrition. This is an expected drawback considering the intended scale of the game, and while it is a major inconvenience at times, it is currently an unavoidable drawback.

The game is still quite fun, the community quite active, and the scale quite big, I currently recommend the game at a discounted price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a top-down MMO shooter that seems to tell war stories better than any other game, ever. It's something really different.

The premise is war. Not pathetic video game battles that last 30 minutes where people stand in circles to make tickets drop down, or a safe playground where your base is forever safe (ahem Planetside 2 ahem) - war. Every town must be cleared, constructed, and built, hammer by hammer by real players. All the materials are farmed by players, sent to a refinery by players, made into weapons and ammo by players, and delivered to the frontlines by players. The result is that every battle is an epic effort of balancing logistics and combat as two teams try to seize strategic locations day and night. Skirmishes last days. The once-in-a-blue-moon World Conquest can last weeks on 10 different maps. One may log out in the morning, and find out in the afternoon that the balance of power has shifted. People will have stories about how one squad ambushed and stole a truckload of supplies from the enemy, or how they valliantly held a town with nothing but rifles and wit. These stories feel significant because they come with consequences, as there will be a real winner or loser in the end, and it sure as hell ain't gonna be us.

The basic controls and shooter mechanics of the game isn't revolutionary. You've seen it - you can crouch and prone, aim to shoot, you got a primary, secondary, and teritary slot for guns, medkits and grenades. Yet the same mechanics evoke a different feeling, as it is both slow and lethal. Accuracy is highly penalized on the move, and just a few shots from any gun will put down a soldier. It encourages consolidated offenses and ambush, while discouraging one-man-army scenarios. All your ammo and weapons also have to be built by another player, and respawning takes resources as well. Even if one isn't good at gunplay, they can contribute by farming materials in the backline or building defenses. The game encourages players to fall into different roles to support a common cause.

As with multiplayer games, the community surrounding the game can impact one's feelings for the game highly. As with these experiences, your mileage will vary, but I have found my time with people mostly positive. Aside from a few bad apples (That you can votekick), the majority of players are friendly or at least supportive. After all, the hate is concentrated on the other team, and with ~60 enemies on the map, there really aren't that much room for individual toxicity.

All in all, if you feel capivated by the idea of large scale war and camaraderie among fellow players, Foxhole may be worth a shot. There isn't really anything that does anything similar (Yes, not even Planetside 2, the poor bastard).\t\t\t\t\t
