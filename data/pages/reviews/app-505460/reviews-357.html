\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good, fun game but absolutely not for everyone who likes shooters, really, this is not a shooter but a war simulator, purchase it with that in mind. I am new and have mainly soloed because I like to learn mechanics before I join up. Having said that, probably the most team/squad oriented war game on the market. I have not encountered what some posters claim about being ordered or being mass reported&banned. I have been asked to do reasonable things 3 times over many hours, with 1 exception which was someone ordered me to do something that made no sense so I just kept fighting as I was.

Next, IMO fighting well obviously helps, you'll see company's coordinate attacks which is definitely needed, but! Logistics&supplies win this war simulator, just like in real life. Without supplies you don't have the equipment needed when the enemy comes testing your defenses, especially now that tanks are roaming. No anti-tank weapons or grenades to hold off the attack until your side can get some tanks rolling in for support? You are done, they'll steamroll the squads that deploy in and desperately try to hold em off. Plus all the BMs needed for repairs and building. Logistics&supplies win the war.

Ok cons. My main con so far is how aiming/shooting works. It is so different than anything I have ever played that it really needs a starting tutorial that takes you through all the mechanics of how to aim to hit people in the open (easiest to get the hang of but even it has nuances), into bunkers, how height affect shots/aiming, how machine guns are different, when moving about how to aim, how to use dagger/bayonets.... the infantry combat has a lot of nuance that will put some players off before they get a feel for it. A tutorial that drags you through various battle encounters would really be ideal. The youtube stuff and stuff on reddit helps but it is rarely comprehensive and does not capture how the game feels because of how different this is than, well, anything else. One tip, different weapons for different jobs. Usually the case in these games but so much moreso in Foxhole.

Anyway, worth it if you watch some videos and think you'd like it. The ongoing war really is always ongoing and fun to be a part of.\t\t\t\t\t
