\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEver since WW2 Online died, I was looking for a similar game. That game was Planetside 2.

Planetside 2 was amazing: large scale battles where thousands of players could fight for territory and resources, action combat akin to Battlefield, customizable weapons and characters, etc.

Yet I felt like something was missing.

These games were too focused on just the combat side of things. War is more than just combat: it's logistics, intelligence, sabotage, morale and propaganda. Those games didn't have those things, but want to know what game does?

Foxhole.

Foxhole is the ONLY game out right now that realistically simulates war strategies. Even the 'Total War' series have some kind of balance to them to make battles somewhat equal. The reality of war is that one side will always be inherently stronger than the other, either because they own more land, have more people, have better weapons or have better luck in general.

In the game, you choose a side to fight on for the entire length of the war; either the Wardens (with styles akin to the Germans in WW2), or the Colonials (with styles akin to the Canadian/American/British troops).
Regardless of whether or not you are losing, you cannot switch teams. You are there until the very end. That is where the game truly shines. You see, morale plays a HUGE part in this game. There is something about having your territories taken and fighting with literal sticks and stones as a guerilla unit against an enemy with more tanks, guns and people. The rush you get from a successful sabotage mission behind the lines is unparalleled. I'll give you an example.

I played as the Wardens and our intelligence was severely outdated. High ranked players weren't updating the map fast enough and units would be sent to towns/outposts that were already captured. We would walk into a Colonial town, confused as to why we weren't hearing gunfire/artillery in the distance. Meanwhile, the enemy were steamrolling small 20 man squads kilometres away. My squad (about 7 men strong) were wiped by 2 light machine guns and an artillery strike, leaving me alive along with a random guy.
"Aria, you wounded?" the player asked,
"Yes." I said, "But I'm good. Let's get to the battle before they take more territory."
We bobbed and weaved along the cliffside, taking care to keep an eye out for enemy troops.
We eventually found a derelict foxhole alongside a road and jumped inside. He had a light machine gun with barely any rounds in it, and I had a pistol with 32 bullets.
We crouched and waited for the next wave of troops to come in. As soon as we heard tank tracks driving by, we both popped up from the foxhole and killed about 3 troops before eventually being mowed down by machine gun fire.
As we both lay dying in the foxhole, bleeding out, the guy said one more thing to me.
"It was an honour fighting with you, bro."
And then we both spawned, probably in different outposts because I never saw him again after that.

The experience of seeing the empire you fought so hard to build crumbling before you is so morale reducing, yet it makes you want to fight harder. The creative tactics us Wardens came up with as our Empire was being wiped definitely taught me many things that I could use for the next war.

We are currently fighting war # 746 and I am certain the enemy will have their hands full in the future wars from the experience we all gained as soldiers.

The game gets a 10/10 from me -- there is nothing like it on the market right now and you would be foolish to rob yourself of this experience. CAD$22 is nothing for the experience of fighting in a proper WW2-style war.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has potential, but quality of life issues and scaling isn't there.

Your going to run into queue times in almost everything you do, some of this is by design ( waiting for factories, resources respawns, ect), these type of waits I understand, but when queue times are horrible on the servers, the game is pretty much unplayable. Any hotzone full of battles tends to have queue times, these get bad enough were logistics players already spawn in game will be stuck on the border of map tile ( the servers are not dynamic instancing). So though its funny to see 10 tanks , and that many logistics trucks stuck on the edge of the map waiting for 15 -30 minutes plus to get in, it gets tiresome and frustrating really fast. This had become such a big issue that they split the server into two live wars ( so they aren't really one persistent server anymore).

Naval combat is kind of a joke, a barge loaded with guys with nades is one of the strongest naval units in this game, and the oceans feel more like lakes.

90% of fighting is done on bridges on the front lines, which means running into a choke or camping a choke most of the time your on.

They also removed all talk, it would be cool to talk to the enemy when your only a trench over, I can only image the fun you could have with that, but no more.

Logistics is fun until you burn out on playing a Truck driver sim,

There is a tech tree, it changes on the wims of the dev's with no reason other than them thinking it shakes up the gameplay, which it doesn't and makes their attempt at game balancing even a tougher job, it like they are shooting themselves in the foot.

Partisans( sneaking into the enemy logistics lines), is great for a thrill, and actually makes you feel like your helping the effort out. That being said, I didn't buy the game to play metal gear solid.

Joining a clan is something that I feel is almost required, as being part of an organized operation does give you that good mil sim tickle but even that is fleeting.

So with all this said, why did I dump 386 hours into this? Found a clan of cool people, got in their discord, shared memes, had a great time just chilling with em. So basically this, Foxhole is a top down VR chat room, were you sit on a bridge with your friends, doing memie ♥♥♥♥, while being blown up by the enemy.

This is a terrible game but you will find some fun characters along the way, and maybe like 2 or 3 good battles that dont involve a bridge.

I just played New world for 300 plus hours, waste your time in this game, at least they don't patch in game breaking bugs.\t\t\t\t\t
