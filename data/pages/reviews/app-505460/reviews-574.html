\r\n\t\t\t\t\tpros:
crazy large operations with loads of people.
makes you feel like you've contributed despite playing with hundreds of people at a time.
cool people (for the most part) all around.
cool game culture.
awesome art design.
regiments (clans) allow for finding friends to play with.
all aspects of play (building, vehicles, infantry, artillery, logisitics) are well done and feel great.

cons:
need internet to play.

not really any personal player progression/customization, just ranking up and changing gender and race. not necessarily a con, depends on the person. game is really gameplay oriented and does gameplay really well.

occasionally toxic veteran players/regiments that think they're the greatest thing to happen to this game.

Can't recommend solo play, as what you can do alone is somewhat limited. you almost need to join a regiment to get access to the big operations and giant bases, and the big ops and colossal forts and big war efforts are what Foxhole does best. You probably will not be playing long if you play alone. find a group.

you may hit a certain point like i did where the shock and awe of how good and deep the game is wears off and you figure out all of the mechanics in the game and it becomes a little bit hard to just keep throwing yourself into the game because it loses a bit of your interest. the game can seem monotonous sometimes since the war ends and everything to starts over every couple weeks, so its hard to stay really active in this game when it kind of feels like a hamster wheel. in reality it depends on the kind of player you are. some people love the h*ll out of this game and will literally play 24/7. youll have those guys who run logistics all the time and create enough tanks for an army because they love to do it (btw Logi players literally carry this game, saying this as an artillery man). then there are people like me, who like to play other games and like this game for the big operations and big moments, but not for the nonstop-frontline-run-at-your-enemies gameplay. the great thing about foxhole is that it supports both kinds of players; the 24/7 players get... well... they get to play 24/7. the "big moment" players like me get to use the vehicles, weapons, and boats that those 24/7 players crank out to make big moves and giant pushes, whether it be huge tanks columns or massive sea invasions. I know i put this text wall under con, but Foxhole's ability to match any kind of player's style of play is in reality a pro, and it highlights the mastery this game achieves in its depth and fun.

Main server is full a lot of the time, so the devs opened a second server while they try to increase capacity; can be seen as a con since queue times to get into main server, but is a pro overall since it implies that this game is healthy and growing

Occasional trouble getting into servers and bugs that cause big disconnects, but it happens rarely enough that its not a problem, and you can often just join back into the game in like 2 minutes after the crash.

conclusion
devs are working their butts off and haven't lost any steam. despite this game having early access designation, it plays better than a triple-A game. Really impressive developer team, i love what they are doing here and think this game deserves a buttload more attention.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI underestimated how fun this game would be. There are some definite drawbacks to the game in its current state, and there are some toxic players for sure... BUT there's a lot you can do as a player other than being cannon fodder on the front lines of a territory and this game has one of the best developed (albeit complex) logistical game play loops I have seen in a game yet. If the polished/finished version of the game can figure out a few things (IE. ownership of bases by squads/regiments so random players cannot access the cache) this will be an amazing game.

At only 25-hours played and still in my first war, I cannot speak to if there's that burnout/fatigue - though I imagine if I only played as infantry on the front line that fatigue (PTSD lol) could be the case. I enjoy the logistical game play loop and the building game play loop... it satisfies my itch to design defenses and build a strategy with my regiment.

My advice would be to find a regiment to join and not play solo... working together with a group is where this game shines its brightest.\t\t\t\t\t
