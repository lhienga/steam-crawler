\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent game. It has a lot to do, It feels like a true war and it is more player driven that dev directed.
There are tons of things to do in regards to combat. you can sneak around with small arms or shotguns, you can snipe people far off, you can use mortars, artillery, vehicles, boats and even nukes. you can run legistical support and farm to your hearts content, and that is actuallly rewarded perfectly with the commends and levelling system.
when you hop into the home region, you check the maps and you enter any region it feels like a true war. you can spawn at the way back, create vehicles and run powerful supplies up to the front or you can spawn directly in a frontline region and make your way to the frontline from there.(make sure to set a spawnpoint further in to save some running). Every kill, item stolen and item lost either helps or hurts your team in real time. Overnight the tides of war can be changed and that outpost you were fighting out of last night is now in ennemy hands and you have been pushed back.
You rank up using in game commends. These commends are given to players as in game rewards to then be use on other players in praise of their actions. The players control who ranks up. They also control the social system. teamwork and playing together goes a long way. 90% of the player base are willing to answer your questions and help you out, give you rides, run other players through the tutorials, etc. T
Take it slow at the beginning, When you get to the first region try to link up with a random group running around. they are following someone who is giving a quick lesson on the game. this is immensely helpful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game about cooperating with an army made up of individual soldiers, all controlled by a player. The only AI used in combat is for defensive structures, and this makes for some great combat interations with the enemy, as they behave like people, because they are people. Logistics is a big part of Foxhole, and if you spend any extended time in a major base you can see the trucks full of supplies going in and out of the production facilities and off to the front. The major draw of Foxhole is it's persistent war: No matter what, the war marches on. The wars continue day and night, between two ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ identical factions (Colonials VS. Wardens). Combat is fun, especially with friends. However, if you do not have great control of the callout keys or own a microphone, you end up at a pretty big disadvantage. Communication is a big part of foxhole, and it's kind of hard to yell "GRENADE!" fast enough using only text chat. Otherwise the gameplay will suck you in, and the feeling of seeing your advances with your buddies make an impact on the world map is very satisfying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's many problems with Foxhole

Ping:

Players will be playing with each other from across the globe, resulting in horrible ping. The developers have said that they have deticated servers in separate areas of the world, then why am I playing with Europeans, Asians, Americans, and Australians? Bad matchmaking? Well theres another issue then. The ping issue makes firefights, and really the whole game, not feel very satisfying. The game does a good job at trying to mask it, but when it takes a second for your gunshot to register, it gets pretty old. That's not fun.

Grinding:

One of the most essential roles in the game, Logistics, yet there's no fun to be found. You grind the same pile of scraps over and over, never ending because the front lines will always need more. There's no reward in it either. That's not fun.

Monotonous:

It's impossible to not die. Pushing against the enemy on the front, you are probally going to die. Then you respawn. And you do it again. And again. And again. Taking ground is so slow, that it can take literal days of fighting at the same location with no actual ground being taken on either side. That's not fun.

Hours to build something, minutes to lose it:

You can spend hours grinding the materials, then waiting for the blueprints, to finally be able to build your own armoured car, just for it to get blown up with one lucky mine. Hours of work, gone just like that. Your entire group of half tracks gone in a blink of an eye. That's not fun.

At first, the game is interesting and the developers had a good idea going, but you quickly realize how boring it really is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game had good reviews so I gave it a chance. Here's my experiance in the few minutes that I cared enough to play it before hating it. You spawn in some place, you go to another place. There are buildings. You don't have a gun. There are vehicles, but they are all locked. You can't kill your teammates or you get warned of being banned. There's no enemies anywhere near the base. There's no weapons anywhere. You have to drive like 5 mins away to shoot at anything.

It kind of just spawns you without explaining anything about where you are, who these two armies are, why their fighting, your purpose here, really anything. I don't think that's how most armies have been run historically. There did'nt seem like there was any reason to ever play this again. Unless you like top-down army base simulators.
