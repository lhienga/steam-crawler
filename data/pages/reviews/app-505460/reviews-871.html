\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing on a rant here. I used to play the crap out of this game early last year. I LOVED this game starting out. Literally had 40 hours in the game within my first 3 days. It feels old to me already. A lot of the added features don't seem to add a ton of enjoyment. The prioritization is horrible. There are things from back then that I didn't like that still exist. If clapfoot wants to appeal to the masses, they need to do a visual overhaul (better textures everywhere, more visual effects, more biome/zone variety type, nicer animations, etc), make combat funner (better shooting mechanics, bigger explosions, better sound quality, better animations, better field artillery system, etc.), and chill out on the building decay so people don't feel like the dozen hours of item grinding are for no reason because their hard work will just decay and crumble making all of their work pointless.

Okay, to start with the toxicity/moderator issue -

The rank feature is BS and contributes to unnecessary bullying. It's a social score that is managed largely by clans and people who fight for social status. If there's going to be commending, there needs to be a condemn feature as well so large clans can't vote some jerk to general who will just play and yell at new players for no reason. I got up to LT, but only because I helped people a lot doing a lot of Logi. People make COL because they manage clans and people suck up to them. Peoples fake rank gets to their head and it's a large factor in bullying in this game.

Also not cool with the group kicking. Group votes should alert a game moderator, not kick someone off immediately. There also needs to be more moderators that actively suspends people for harassment. Mods think that this already exists, but it doesn't on the scale that it needs to be. I tried to get in as a moderator due to my exceptional applicable experience in real life. I'm 32 years old with experience in military leadership/administrative(7 years military), armed federal security management for homeland security in extremely important facilities such as federal courthouses with a DHS security clearance/federal adjudication, physical security administration for a data center, management positions to include president of a non-profit organization to help veterans, tech/programming jobs for large tech companies, years and years of community management experience in very large groups, etc.. And guess what? My mod application was denied. They said my 220 hours of experience in the game wasn't enough to help provide for basic game policing and basic help. I just wanted to help stop the bullying factor and help players where possible. This is exactly the kind of reason why this game is plagued with toxicity issues. I figured with my background in real world positions of trust and a decent resume that shows professional experience in people management/communication, they would oblige. I was told that their newest moderators had been in the community for like two years, that they were already a friend prior to being accepted, and they won't recruit anyone newer onto the team. Then I saw a video where they hired on a dev who had never even heard of the game before. So they will hire someone who has ZERO prior knowledge of foxhole to work on and develop the game, but won't hire someone with exceptional real world experience to do basic game policing because they don't have 2+ years experience and aren't friends with the dev team for free/voluntarily? Then, I log into the game and players are bullying people in mass, throwing rank around, etc., and I hear about moderators not being professional all the time and doing things they shouldn't be doing. I go on steam and there are hundreds of reviews and comments about moderator issues and community toxicity. It kind of upset me because I liked the game and made friends in the game. I genuinely wanted to help for the simple fact that I enjoy helping others, plus I wanted to improve the experience for other players. I admin a jobs group with 16,000+ members simply because I want to stop scammers (which I do daily). I don't post or comment in that group to show my status or anything. I do it because it's a nice hobby to have. I enjoy helping people. I outlined this during my application to them. They didn't care to do a video or audio interview because they simply want all of their mod team to be their friends. Just a group of friends (whether or not they're qualified) to run the community. The mod team is a friendship clique that will not recruit solely on the basis of peoples ability to moderate which in turn effects the quality of moderators and their ability to actively control their community 24/7. I say this with confidence because of some of the horror stories I have heard in regards to mods in the past that were witnessed by many people and had identical matching stories. I haven't played the game in a little while, but I check on the new features once in a while. My theory is that the more eyes on ground in the game, the better. The more qualified people with mod powers who see what is going on, the better it can be managed. My goal was not to become game mod and tell everyone or for social status. I actually wanted to keep it secret and switch invisible to help only when I see something wrong. The game structure and community structure is plagued with issues. That's why the steam rating is only 76%. Most of the negative reviews, to include the con's listed in the positive reviews, almost all say the same exact things with toxicity being a huge talking point. The devs want to create the game that THEY want and what THEY enjoy playing, not the game that players want. That's why in the last 12 months, popularity of this game has not changed a single bit. Player count has literally stayed the same and negative reviews (despite all matching in some way or fashion) have gone ignored. Clapfoot is more interested in creating more weapons and etc. instead of fixing the core problems with this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game that reaally struck home for me. To give a brief rundown foxhole is a massive multiplayer where everyone is coordinated towards a massive war effort. Every person you see ingame is a player. But instead of most games where they will give you a it and launch you into battle you can decide what you want to do to push the enemy back. From fighting on the frontlines to creating and delivering supplies to building to running spec ops and much MUCH more. One thing that also puts this game out from others is that every material you use, every bullet you fire, every vehicle you drive had to have been built/harvested by a player. The community also gets an A+. The griefers in foxhole are few and far between and most people are nice and willing to help you out. The devs of foxhole are very close with the community and listen to feedback as well as fix bugs. They also keep themselves busy and release new content frequently for the playerbase to use. Getting into foxhole can be hard but if you ask around for help and listen to others when they give advice you will learn. Even after almost a year of owning this game I can always come back and play for 4 hours strieght and still have fun. This game has landed it spot as my favorite game of all time and I would recommend checking it out.\t\t\t\t\t
