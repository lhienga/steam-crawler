\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game that doesn't reward you for what you do. It even punishes you for doing it, even when you do with your best intentions. Your greatest feats will be forgotten in a matter of few hours. Your contribution, unnoticed. Your story, untold. You feel limited by your sanity even when you have the greatest of ambitions. You feel betrayed and forgotten by the very allies you are helping. And at times you doubt whether you are doing the right things.
Here are some scenarios:

1) When you spent hours fortifying a base, only to have it completely wiped out because your main force is busy fighting somewhere else. You ask for help for hours but not enough will come to your aid. And eventually you know its time to let go.

2) When you sacrifice some of your sleep to push a map, go to sleep, and wake up to the map completely occupied by the enemy.

3) When you try and organise something in the frontlines but your allies do not have the awareness to listen even when it will help them.

4) When the tank you spent an hour building: "scrapping resources for a truck > scrapping resources for a tank, complete with ammo, fuel and gear > driving around recruiting your crew > drive to frontlines" , gets destroyed by a friendly rocket (i was once the guy firing the rocket)

5) When the rate at which you bring weapons from the frontlines back to base pales in comparison to the rate your allies are taking them out of the base.

6) When you respond to a logi request for ammo. You drive all the way to the front to find there's an oversupply.

7) When the base you supplied an hour ago was conquered by the enemy, and you wonder if you should be hanged for treason on crimes of supplying the enemy.

8)When you are grateful for the logi bringing supplies to you but secretly want to tell them to stop because the situation in the front is not looking great.

9) When the front screams for supplies but no one will secure your logi route

1O) When you are so close to the enemy tank but you don't have the explosives to destroy it.

11)When you see people firing the howitzer with no binoculars or spotter.

12) When you loot a fallen ally to find them carrying 10 magazines when you only really need 2.

13) When you live in SEA so your ping is ♥♥♥♥ and Americans are sleeping when you are on.

14) When new guys are asking for guidance but you're too in the moment to help.

15) When you're new and asking questions that no one will answer.

This game is frustrating as hell, but why do people still play it?
Because its an accurate representation of life and society as a whole.
Everyone has their own personalities, opinions and goals, and its hard to get us working together. And you get to observe individuals and their character.

Enthusiastic people who don't mind living and dying in the front.
Strategic people that organise special ops.
Productive people who spent hours scrapping away like a zombie.
Criminals who purposely grief their own faction and ought to be hanged for treason.
Committed people who would sacrifice sleep to fight back, knowing what they do might not amount to anything.
Free people playing minecraft in little corners of the map.

Want to understand life a bit more? Play Foxhole. Want to play community driven RPG games but not attracted to exp, attack damage and artificial stats? Play Foxhole. Want your kids to grow up and become mature when they like to play games? Introduce them to Foxhole.

When you play foxhole, you are living another life other than real-life . And you can understand reality a little better and accept life for what it is, by playing foxhole.\t\t\t\t\t
