\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is not a sustainable game.

In every war since the mid-70s the mood swings in the community hive mind have gotten more violent. Developers are in a catch 22 where if they ignore the playerbase it whips people up into screaming hysterics, and if they do communicate with the playerbase then people will go out of their way to whip themselves up into hysterics anyway. The shortest, most banal messages from the developers are gathered, with both sides employing troops of specialist schitzos to over-analyse, decrypt through 14 dimensions of possible subtext, and eventually use as evidence in echochambers to claim that developers favor the other side.

The devs aren't little golden angels either though. They sit in their ivory towers adding piles of powercrept equipment while the fundamental problems of the game remain. The devs seem to exist in a reality where they're developing a game for the player base they *want*, rather than the playerbase they *have*. Discussions and accusations of cheating will get you banned from the forums while the cheaters themselves, even when caught and removed from the game dozens of times(!) are given a temporary time out before they can play again. You'll have the same account come back again and again and again.

A few dozen wars ago these people were few and had the discretion to cheat sneakily but these days they will openly and brazenly cheat in full view, despawn equipment and drive vehicles into water in a busy logistics town or sabotage a big base a few moments before a big clan operation. They expect to be caught and votebanned and don't care, they're just trying to do as much damage before that happens and their cheating account goes on a 48h cooldown.

A significant section of the game relies on what is essentially slave labor. Playing logistics is a full time job and you can spend literally fourteen hours a day waiting for production queues to finish so you can drive X to Y or gather more resources. The timers for production is set up to be short enough to be inconvenient to set it as afk in the background and long enough that it's boring to wait in your truck. About 300 of my 550 hours were used to do logi full time until I realized what a total sucker I was being played for. Literally unpaid labor and the game relies on convincing enough no-lifes to do it so that everyone else in the community can have fun (or dump the production into a lake - see above). Without these people, btw, there would be no game. I quit when I realised I was one of a significant number of people who were feeling compelled to do this task because nobody else was doing it and it needed to be done for the game to function.

At its best, Foxhole could be one of the best MMOs out there. It truly breaks new ground and the emergent gameplay can blow you away. But it's in the process of collapsing. Until some huge, systematic changes are made, pass this game over for now.

Currently the war is 100, the longest game on record. People are trying their hardest to win the special number war. A huge section of the playerbase is exhausted, frustrated and burned out but it giving it one last hurrah for war 100. War 101 may break the longest game record because there'll be nobody to play it...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played almost 500 hours with this game in a relatively short term, so it is obvious that I like it. The atmosphere is amazing, it gives back the vulnerability of a person in war. I like the idea, that you must work together with your faction selflessly, from gathering resources to maintaining the bunkers, and facilities, doing the logistics etc. So overall the game has great potential.
A reason why I give negative feedback is based on the developer's attitudes toward their players.

Despite the community requests, the developers seemingly only focus on adding newer and bigger guns instead of polishing what we already have. One of the biggest problems is Alting, which means a player buys the second game for his/herself, joins the enemy faction, and starts do sabotage action within.
Factions are completely vulnerable to this. There is no in-game mechanics that you can defend against this, and even if you catch an Alt account and reports it, it will get a maximum of 3 days ban. On Reddit, you can find violation logs, which go back years, and have 3 days of bans one after another.

When your faction loses a hex within a day, what you fought for weeks just because all of your tanks and artillery equipment ended in the bottom of the ocean and you weren't able to fight back, you might question that your hours, days of hard work would have been more effective by doing something else..

We have seen many games killed by hackers, and cheaters I really hope devs will pull out their heads from their lower back parts and start to listen to their players and focus on the real problems.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game like no other. The concept of it has not been done before on such a scale. When you first get the game, you will be amazed. Every single day you learn new things and the game's systems show themselves to be deeper than you thought before. Meeting the same people in a persistent war day after day really creates a unique experience as a war sim MMO.

But the game is in an absolutely broken state. As you become hooked you also notice the cracks, everywhere you look. Certain parts of the game require massive time sinks each day (upward of five hours per day) in order to work. And as you're now hooked, you'll spend more and more time on the game, despite it becoming a detriment over time. The game has lots of bugs, exploits, broken systems... but it especially suffers from a complete and total lack of interest or moderation on the developer's part. The sporadic (3x a year) update stream shows what the team has been working on, and is guaranteed to generate a new stream of players being drawn to the game. But the developers will ignore each and every single part of player feedback that does not have to do with fixing simple bugs. There are no moderators anywhere in this game. Breaking rules, cheating, griefing... It's not only ignored, you will be banned by the devs on all platforms for speaking out about these issues. You'll sometimes sink literal IRL days into a project such as an operation or base building. It's a common occurence to wake up, completely without being able to protect your work from griefers while you slept, and see that people 'alting' (playing on your team but actually helping the enemy by griefing hard) have completely wiped away all your hard work. And when you have video evidence of it happening, the developers will never do anything about it, even banning you from speaking out in the process.

This practice by the developers is decidedly anti-consumer, and the load of recent negative reviews reflect the community finally being fed up with the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. Despite this being a great game and loving the community, and despite me sinking countless hours into this game, I recommend that you save yourself the trouble, and not buy this game. It will frustrate yourself to no end, and become an actual source of irl stress and frustration. Do not buy.

Foxhole can be very fun, but also a bit overwhelming for new players. And a massive time sink and grind. It's a bit better if you are in a regiment, but it's still much grinding for supplies and vehicles. And the new msupps system for maintenance of facilities is really bad, even worse than the old system in early stage of a war.
