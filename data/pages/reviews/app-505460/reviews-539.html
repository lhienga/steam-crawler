\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe theoretical yield is the maximum possible mass of a product that can be made in a chemical reaction.

It can be calculated from:

the balanced chemical equation
the mass and relative formula mass of the limiting reactant, and
the relative formula mass of the product
An actual yield is the mass of a product actually obtained from the reaction. It is usually less than the theoretical yield. The reasons for this include:

incomplete reactions, in which some of the reactants do not react to form the product
practical losses during the experiment, such as during pouring or filtering
side reactions (unwanted reactions that compete with the desired reaction)
reversible reactions
impurities in reactants
Calculating theoretical yield
Reacting masses may be used to calculate the theoretical yield. Theoretical yield can also be worked out using a mole calculation.

Worked example
If heated, calcium oxide decomposes to form calcium oxide and carbon dioxide.

Calculate the maximum theoretical yield of calcium oxide that can be produced from 250 g of calcium carbonate.

Write down the balanced chemical equation:
CaCO3 CaO + CO2
Calculate the relative formula masses of the substances in the question:
Mr of CaCO3 = 40.1 + 12.0 + (3 × 16.0) = 100.1
Mr of CaO = 40.1 + 16.0 = 56.1
Use the balanced chemical equation to work out the reacting masses based on the relative formula masses:
100.1 g CaCO3 produces 56.1 g of CaO
Work out the reacting masses for 1 g (or 1 kg or 1 tonne if different mass units are used):
= 1 g of CaCO3 produces g of CaO
Scale up the reacting masses to match the given reacting mass of the reactant:
250 g of CaCO3 produces x 250
= 140 g of CaO\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA absolute blast to play, graphics are nice and the top down view makes visibility so much easier. The community gives every new player a warm welcome to the game and some high level players will walk you through the games tutorial and show you some of the tricks that help them stay alive. The game itself is simple enough to understand and the controls are smooth and well made so that everything you'll ever need to press is easily reachable. The community is also very extensive and players are devoted to the game. Both the factions have unique weapons and lore. The game receives constant updates and new content with any bug being found getting fixed very quickly. The devs are active in the community and encourage some the fan-art and story's the players make a write. Since the games learning curve is very shallow, you'll find yourself getting kills very easily and since the game is so player driven, other players won't bar you from joining their squad or clan even if your new, because every man or woman counts when the enemy is pushing you pack. Players give commendations to level each other up and high level players will have a lot of these which means if you play in populated regions then you'll get ranks very quickly. Don't be sad if you lose a war, because there's always the other war that you can join on the other faction if you want to experience what its like to fight for them. In all, this is a player driven coop game that is extremely easy to learn but difficult to master, and the finer details of the game truly make it on of the best games of this genre that you could possibly consider purchasing right now.\t\t\t\t\t

We ended up finding a unguarded relic base in The Lost Grove with two unlocked 120mm field guns. We ended up stealing one of the guns and we came back with a few other players and a 3 truckloads of 120mm ammo and we destroyed the Relic base. After that me and my buddy thought we should cap the base, but had no idea how. I got to googling and doing logi work to get the materials and construction equipment behind enemy lines while my buddy held the base and ended up destroying a tank and a few squads of infantry with a different group of partisans he brought with him. Out of all the crazy missions my friend group has gone on this is my favorite because two idiots wandering around the map managed to capture an enemy base and create a new front to the war because someone left 2 big guns unattended and unlocked.
