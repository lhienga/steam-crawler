\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game itself is really good and is the best persistent war MMO on the market. However, the community is plagued by "alts" - players who buy an additional copy of the game, join the faction opposite of their main account, and grief. The developers have no moderation doctrine to stop this activity and, although it happens to both factions, there are dedicated groups which specifically target the Wardens. Most of the developers play the opposing faction, Colonials, and they don't seem to care about how alts affect the Warden playerbase.

There are many other problems with the game - huge clans controlling limited resources and being douchey about it, infantry being nothing but a meat grinder with no tactical use or significance, streamers breaking the rules but getting preferential treatment, and the fact that the developers really do seem to favor the Colonials and their players who mob the official Discord and Reddit; however, the fact that active cheating is not stopped makes this title hard to recommend.

After 155 hours and reaching officer ranks, I'm done with this game. The developers spend all their time adding new, unbalanced toys to the game instead of addressing alts or adding much-needed quality of life improvements to base building and logistics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I absolutely recommend it. The atmosphere is fantastic, you get in and its all quiet. As you walk along the road to the front, you slowly hear the sounds of rifle shots approaching, then more shots, then you walk past sappers building defenses, then you can hear mortars in the distance, as well as machine gun fires from all sides. Every rifle, ammo, field guns, trucks, tanks, sandbags, everything was crafted from logistic towns by players, from scraps/components collected by players, sent to the front via trucks and ships manually by players to the front, to be used by player combatants at the front. The players who dedicate themselves to logistic plays a vital role, logistics problem is actually a thing, if there's no logistic route, guns starts to be in low supply, ammo quickly ticks down from use, and soldier supplies(in-game item that allows you to respawn in a forward base) starts running out and with no supply being delivered, the only thing in abundance is the starting pistol. Play the game long enough, and you will encounter these desperate situations. One time, me and the few soldiers that makes up Oceanwatch(region in-game) had to rush in and recover supplies from destroyed trucks against armored cars, all the while only armed with basic rifles, pistols, and whatever we salvage from dead comrades, and those few HE grenades the trucks were carrying meant everything to us as those were practically the only supplies we were getting after being encircled.

The community is also fantastic, being a player-based mmo/war simulator, the game have one of the most helpful community Ive stumbled across. You could ask anyone around on how to do it, and they'll likely teach you how, or sometimes, force you to learn on the spot because they really need someone on that field gun and you're the closest guy the have. The only thing you might want to watch out for is to make sure you never steps out of bound, because while I praise the community, others with opposing views on the community do have some merits in their arguments. The community is VERY on edge when it comes to new players using specialized equipment without supervision. As this is a team based game, every wasted specialized equipment harms everyone on the team, you lose that tank, you hampers the entire front because you got obliterated by a single suicidal veteran with anti-tank sticky nades, so you can probably see why the vets are groaning at such a waste, not to mention the logistic crews who would probably be out for blood if they found out what you did to that tank they built and supplied. God help if it actually get stolen by the enemy instead of just destroyed. Even if you made it yourself, people would probably still be on edge against you manning the tank, because they probably don't know you made it for one and just assumed you took it somewhere. Even new builders get flak, because again, they can actually harm the team, this time by building bad defenses that passively or hell even actively hinders the team like bad barbed wire preventing allies from flanking instead of the other way around, bad sandbags that just becomes walls with no cover advantage, or a trench that is a gas trap. But that's not to say you are forbidden from the cool stuff forever. Again, the community is helpful, ask around and most vet would be happy to have someone help him man a tank, crew a mortar, help build, or man a field gun. Very few would object at seeing a captain driving a tank guiding a private gunner, but a tank manned entirely out of privates with the exception of a single corporal manning the gun would probably alert every player of their possible doom. You are not playing your standard mainstream fps game or war thunder or rainbow six, you are not the super special ops and neither can you spawn in with a tank. You cant just play around with cool stuff because unlike those games I listed, the cool stuff is not the default, the default is that tiny pistol and the standard gear is that bolt action rifle the logis player deliver regularly. For every tanks you lose, hell for every shells you wasted on a single soldier(because tank shells are as/more expensive than the soldier supplies the enemy use to spawn), a single logistic player dies on the inside. Remember, this is a team based game that is themed around a large scale war, you are not the hero, you start as the soldier and for a large chunk of the game your character would probably die as that bog standard soldier. Now, you can play this solo, in fact Ive playing solo as Ive not been in a regiment/clan, but player interaction is a must. By that I mean, people will come over to you and ask for help, you WILL interact with someone. And Im not saying this as a bad thing, it means this game is perfect even if you have no friends that want to play the game with. While this game is not a traditional MMOs, this game have the most random player interaction Ive seen from all the MMOs ive tried out. And nothing is forced either, you are not making a raid party out of actual boss requirement or being forced into a guild like other MMOs, every interaction is organic because people genuinely need help with the war effort, a war effort you are also involved in. Now, downsides toward not joining a regiment/clan? Well you likely wont have the resources or the reputation of a clan that would allow you to man the cool stuff I rant about before. You probably would get to do them eventually, but you wont be guaranteed the chance to play with them and have to pray on random opportunities(spare equipment, lack of crews, people enlisting random help) to get on the specialized gear. Also get to high enough rank, people probably wont question what the hell you are doing even if you're not in a clan.

Now for the bad part, the truly bad part of the game is definitely the logistic burden. So remember those logistic mechanics I praised about? Well, on the flip side, the devs have truly up logistic. Essentially, for the past few updates, the devs have kept adding more and more items and mechanics that add logistical burdens, while doing nothing to alleviate the player burn out that have resulted from logistic players having to spent hours for practically no progress. The salt on wound is in this new update, when players requested more mines, they added more mines but also significantly nerf the production of said mines, resulting in the patch not only redundant, but perhaps even more annoying to deal with. The devs kept hinting at railroads but at this point knowing the devs they will just make it a railway gun instead of actual supply rails just to troll the logis into actually killing themselves. Theres also the alt problem, but Ive never seen those personally, only third hand accounts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuffers from the same problems as Rust and Ark where you end up gathering resources for 8 hours for the chance for an hour or two of fun.

It's the most fun massive war game I've played in. You really feel apart of a small ecosystem making improvements and group skirmishes to accomplish a small goal in a humongous war that lasts weeks and weeks. Spending the day finding a nice regiment and then fighting over a bridge and maybe organizing a tank squad or a motor team and then going to sleep and waking up wondering what the outcome eventually was is a really unique feeling that makes you feel INVESTED in the game.

I will say it's incredibly difficult to do anything meaningful on your own. This isn't a lone wolf friendly game which sucks for people like me. But you can find incredibly welcoming, fun, mature, and level headed mates to play with. Some people are quite toxic and some ambitions like building are incredibly difficult to do off the bat without someone yelling at you and being incredibly rude but if you're fine with learning the meta and following the status quo then you're good.\t\t\t\t\t
