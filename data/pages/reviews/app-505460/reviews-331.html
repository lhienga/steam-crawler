Horrific balancing issues

Update for 1.0: As I predicted, shiny new features but *continued* extremely poor balancing by the developers. My recommendation to stay away from this game still stands, and as I type this the Foxhole subreddit is awash with people decrying what the devs are doing.

If you head on over to r/Foxholegame you'll see that the player-base is seriously unhappy with the current state of the game's balance, and many new players find an extremely hostile community waiting for them when they jump-in for the first time. As is so often the case with the Steam review system, the "83% Positive" review score in no way actually represents the current feelings players and ex-players have about the current state of the game.

The "Colonials", the green faction in the game, are currently extremely under-powered. This has lead to glut of players from said faction, meaning that Colonial clans are struggling to stage any kind of decent in-game operations, and players in the opposing "Wardens" faction see eye-watering in-game timers at peak playing times. Whilst the meta-status of the factions has swung in the past, Siege Camp have been making the in-game experience for Colonial players worse for a good while now.

Logistics players from both factions formed a union in the past, and gained attention from the press for the sheer insanity of the concept, as it was the only way in which they felt that they could get immediate attention from Siege Camp- lo and behold just like in real-life, people coming together and downing tools for a better quality of life actually got a response from the powers that be- though as is to be expected of Siege Camp that response was half-♥♥♥♥♥♥ and merely buried the loudest voices.

All of this negativity is exacerbated by the extreme toxicity offered up by the community. r/Foxholegame has been an absolute mess of childish de-constructive gas-lighting for a good while now, and it's taken the game entering one of it's darkest phases ever for the subreddit's community to finally start to, albeit at an excruciatingly slow pace, improve. Many experienced players will more than happily team-kill you if they disapprove of what you're doing in-game, which can quite literally be any form of conduct they disagree with. Whilst the player-base has done their best to induce amnesia around the subject, there have been a number of cases where players have been banned for totally innocuous in-game behaviour, which makes the developers' lack of response to people playing multiple copies of the game at the same time, or griefing in-game friendlies as part of "espionage", all the more agitating.

Sure, the game fills a niche that frankly no other does, or is likely to for a long time, but you deserve better than to sink your priceless time into a game that charges ~$30 for entry, when what awaits you is a broken toxic mess. It doesn't matter if 1.0 brings a game that's hugely improved in both it's feature-set and it's balance; you'll be whiling away the hours knowing that Siege Camp could completely screw the pooch for the umpteenth time in the very next update.\t\t\t\t\t
