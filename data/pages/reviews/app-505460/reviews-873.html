We were established in Mooring County pretty big time, but it was getting night time for most of the players so the servers were dying down. All that was left were the dumb partisan dudes. Well, I, the classic Partisan, would wander around stealing whatever enemy supplies I could.

So, I managed to sneak my way to the enemy homebase. There, I managed to sneak past defences and steal an enemy tank. It was a pretty good snag, and I knew how to get myself out without dying (if I wasn't caught by players), so I began making my way to our base.

While driving this newly acquired tank back to base, I took a shortcut across a scarred old battlefield. It wasn't the normal route, but I figured, must not be much there anyway.
In the middle of the battlefield, I saw something in the corner of my vision. A truck. Huh. Not like you see those every day in the middle of nowhere, may as well check it out. Turn toward it, drive forward. Wait, that's two trucks. No, four trucks. And is that a tank? Oh, no, thats SIX TANKS.

Well, to cut the long story short, tallying up the grab : Seven Tanks (Including the one I had), Five of them filled with tank shells and all fueled. The four trucks, two are filled with building materials, two are filled to the brim with Tank Shell boxes (Amounting to roughly 550+ Tank shells).

Basically, by the end of it all, a small partisan force just became a full armored division in a matter of one lucky find. By the time I woke up the next day, I would learn we used the tanks to capture roughly half the map already during the day when everyone got back on.

I guess there are a few lessons from this story. First, god this game is fun. One person can change the outcome of an entire region. Second, supplies are God. Our outbreak from our small outpost wouldn't have been possible without those supplies. Third... Uhh... I guess don't leave a lot of supplies unattended in the middle of nowhere easy to take?

tl;dr it's fun and you can have a substantial impact even if you just want to play on your own\t\t\t\t\t
