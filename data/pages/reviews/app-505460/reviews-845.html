\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExtremely cinematic game, forcing you to socialize and cooperate with teammates. The gameplay feels (imo) pretty fair, and there are always multiple ways to handle a situation. When you boot the game, you're told to choose a side, either the Colonial scum, or the chad Wardens. Once you've made your choice, you can't change until the war is over. A war can persist for multiple weeks, so it's important that you and your friends choose the same side if you want to play together.

The game seems to be updated fairly often, and the devs seems to be open to criticism.

Biggest flaw I feel is that the game only has two factions, making the general population pretty unbalanced. The times I've played there's always been one team stomping the other, and I feel like having a third, rebel team, would solve this.

Very good game nontheless\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a game for everyone, and it's definitely not a Super Soldier Simulator where the individual takes precedent like an FPS. The individual tends to get in the way in Foxhole, and tribalists that emphasize their clan's individual accomplishments over the good of the Wardens or the Colonials also hamper their own chances to win the total war, and everyone else on their faction.

Gonna have to buck up and submit to only one of two teams in sum: Colonials or Wardens and be a total team player to be a welcome part of either side. It's a collectivist's game. The people that matter most are the roles that are currently being played less: Logistics, more days than not. We need you to fill a slot. We're not demanding it, but slots NEED to be filled or we all suffer. Someone is going to have to put their ego aside and drive a truck. Someone is going to have to buck up and bring up some gas masks. Someone is going to have run back a region and tell the Port Base Logi to bring up the Soldier Supplies for respawn to fill out the next region up's bases.

Foxhole's like actual war: It's more mundane time doing mundane work than rooty-tooty scoot-and-shooty time. But it's all important work that all needs doing, and the importance of each individual's work depends on how badly we need those individuals to fill slots and submit to work.

To emphasize again: It's not a game for everyone. Takes a certain mentality to be Logi that isn't typically found in Rogues in World of Warcraft, or the bunny-hopping twitchkiddies in Battlefield or Call of Duty, and we need a LOT of Logis.
