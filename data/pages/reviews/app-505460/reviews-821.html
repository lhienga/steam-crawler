\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good game that's recieving frequent updates that usually always adds new content or adds something tangible to some aspect of the game. Even though its an early access game and I try to stay clear of those titles, this one is a safe bet as the devs are devoted to the game and its success. They have proven this multiple times and where I do not agree with every decision they have made it is undeniable they have created a game that has kept me hooked longer than most other games could.

This game is centered around teamwork and coordination so it goes without saying playing with other people is a good idea for the most memorable experiences, but isnt always necessary. When I first started playing I did everything myself and had a reasonable good time doing so. But it wasnt until I joined a clan that I really started to have fun and enjoy the game on a new level. With some organized teamwork and a clear goal in mind just a few players can make a significant difference on the front lines of a battle, of which there are many going on at once in this huge world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATED UPDATE: After not playing for a year I recently returned. It is still the same game, although there have been some new things added. However it now has a major issue- Player Alts/ Sabotage.

There exists, on both sides, a number of players who have a separate version of the game, either with a completely different Steam account or via Family Share, and they use this account to join the other side during a war. These players gather intelligence via the map and chat, empty supplies from stockpiles and bases, drive resource-laden trucks into the water, load up on high-value items and run towards enemy (in their case friendly) lines and are killed, and destroy vehicles and facilities. Unfortunately the in-game Moderator position was removed by the Developers due to those selected for the position from the playerbase used their power to help their friends and clans. Even with an in-game reporting system, many times the sabotage is unseen or the player logs off before they can be reported.

Player Alts/ Sabotage reports/ memes/ videos on the subreddit are deleted by the developers because they are in charge of it, silencing the playerbase and trying to sweep the issue under the rug as they near an update and eventual release of the full game. The actions of the developers to not address this issue head on, censor subreddit posts, and act like nothing is wrong is a slap in the face to the players who have stayed with the game from the start, those who have joined in the interum, and future players as well. Therefore I must change my review again to Not Recommend, and hope that I may return once again to Recommend once Clapfoot/ Siege Camp fixes this issue.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've gone back and forth on this game..but I feel like there are still too many key pieces missing, and the community still has issues. I was there was a neutral review choice, but it doesn't exist.

Things this needs:
1. A more robust ping system, like Apex legends. Especially for those without a mic.
2. An overview system for commanders. Allow people to get a more RTS view and control and track logistics from there. Issuing orders via radio tower/systems.
3. A better system than the current vehicle lock/claim thing. If the squad is offline, or they haven't touched a vehicle in X time it shouldn't still be claimed. Nothing worse than finally find an unlocked vehicle rushing to pick something up and finding out you can't put anything in it because it's claimed.
4. Better kick/mute/etc management. You get these people running around piping music and other garbage through their comms. It can be hard to tag them in the middle of a battle, or they die before you can tag them, only to come back later. Let us pull up a list of people in range (or who were recently in range) so that we can easily mute them, or report enemies. I've been on the receiving end of some fairly profane abuse just for shooting someone.

Community wise:

More moderation, I don't know how it works when you report someone swearing up a storm in chat. I don't know if they get a clip of their last X seconds of audio or nothing at all.
Also get some more professional moderators. I've only ever seen one in game, and he was talking to someone and I honestly wondered if he was up past his bedtime. He seemed completely ill-suited to what he was doing and I'm glad it didn't involve me because there is no way I could have taken that person seriously. Not only that, he way he was handling the problem I walked by seemed very poor.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on a whim to try with my mates. Admittedly bought this when it was in the early stages of development. Played it for a bit and most of my mates got a refund on the game, with it being to laggy, or just not their type of game. But now fast forward this around one year or so, we all thought that it was a good idea to give it another go. Right now the game servers feel good, with little to no lag at all, fun game play, as well as a fantastic community.

The whole premise for the game is that there are 2 teams, The Colonials and The Wardens, fighting over different territories. You are a soldier fighting for one of teams, helping to claim territories against the other team.

It's not your everyday fast paced shooters where you just run in and just mow people down. It's more on the side of tactical positioning and knowing when to seize opportunities. Different weapons in the game, will amount to different play styles and doing different things. Weapons varying from SMGs to Rifles to Mounted LMGs to Sniper Rifles and many more. To add to this, if you are not a fan of just fighting, they also have the ability to become a medic and go around treating wounds and also reviving downed soldiers. If you want a game to compare this to would be games like "Company of Heroes" crossed with "Men At War".

The fun and unique thing about this game is that you are not restricted to just shooting people and killing. The developers have made it so that you can experience the other side of war as well, which includes building and placing sandbags for the troops to use as defense, construct gates and walls to section areas off, deploying "foxholes" for your allies to garrison and defend locations. To that, I will add that you can also be a supply runner, where you can use trucks to transport supplies from one location to another, or even resupply forward bases.

The one thing that I definitely will have to commend is that the people/players/community in this is great as everyone in a sense does try their best to role play to further enhance the experience. The community is very helping and more than happy to guide newbies into the game and helping them with the experience.

Really the only downside to this game that I personally thought was annoying, is that when you squad up with your friends, you do not get any indication of them from other players not in your squad. I understand they try and give it a bit of realism, but partying up with your friends should have some indication to help with where your squad is on or in that territory.

Overall, so far, with the limited amount of playing, my friends and I are enjoying the game and the unique style that it comes with. Definitely worth a try, even if you want to jump in by yourself you can still get a considerable amount of enjoyment.

Hope this review helps and good luck!\t\t\t\t\t
