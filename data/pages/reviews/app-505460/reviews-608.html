\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played MMOs and top-down games before but this one is one of a kind. I started playing this game with some mild expectations but continued to do so for hours, just because it was so fun and addicting. If you enjoy multiplayer, simulation, management and strategy games then this game will be perfect for you.

This game keeps you hooked in an endless battle between two factions. You will never run out of things to do. There are so many resources to mine, items to craft, bases to supply, people to help and you will (mostly) love every moment of it. The adrenaline rush of driving a logistical truck and supplying a base under heavy attack or destroying an enemy tank all by yourself is great.

However, in order to fully experience all this game has to offer i do definitely recommend playing this game with some friends or joining a regiment and their Discord (or other voice chat app) and partaking in large coordinated operations. That's where this game really shines.

Two thumbs up!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this game has the opportunity to be great, I seriously doubt it ever will be. While I enjoy many aspects especially the fact that you have to resource everything in the game, every tank, bullet and gun is made by a player (Don't get me started on Border bases). So many games gloss over this, and just take a lazy way out by having gear just magically spawn into the world.

Where the game falls on its face comes in the fact that you spend a great amount of your time fighting your own side. The rare resources that are required to build tanks and other advanced weapons are also used to make concrete, so the resource field fights start. In addition, there is no way to properly secure a vehicle from your own side. Especially if you have spent serious time getting that tank ready to go and having a random just walk up with a wrench and drive off just because they want to see what it's like.

The game suffers by usual game bugs like glitchy vehicles, invisible walls, frustrating game menus, no real tutorial, etc. But it also punishes players that can't make the game a second job.

The biggest issue I see with this game is the abuse of ALT accounts. Nothing is more heart breaking is to see a member of your own side (supposedly) drive off into the country side and supply the enemy with resources, tech and equipment. In addition, using these alt account to spot for enemy artillery or worse, the Sabotage of bunkers and bases by these supposedly friendly forces.

I have quite a few hours in at this point. If I don't recommend it, why am I playing? Mostly I have made some friends and I enjoy spending time with them. We all get frustrated, struggle and sometimes overcome the game's short comings. Without that, what's the point of wasting time in here.\t\t\t\t\t
