\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere should I start...?

I have never enjoyed a game as much as Foxhole in my 20+ years of gaming history, no wonder I put 3k hours into it in less than 2 years (~125 days). The idea behind the game is just wonderful, you can do whatever you want: everything you do has an impact on the outcome of the war, including scrapping, logistics, building, fighting, partisaning and maintaining.

Let me share my 3k hours experience with you:
-However the amount of joy coming from these activities are not equally provided: builders get zero acknowledgements whereas if you transport some goods to a frontline with full pop, you can just easily climb the rank ladder, no matter whether your resources were useful or not. Ranks don't give any advantages of course. Have you ever tried rebuilding a frontline that moved? Well, good luck mammoning the tier 2 trenches with a total of 66 mammons for a single trench completely dehusked, filling in craters alone, or simply destroying unwanted/badly placed friendly structures to get weapon restricted every 20 minutes.

- Alts (secondary accounts) are a serious problem indeed. Other games issue hardware bans, here they get a couple of hours timeout and can continue their behaviour the same as before, probably unnoticed. They drive free tanks, loaded freighters and trucks to the enemy or sink them, destroy friendly structures (like bridges or generator rooms for Storm Cannons) or modify garrison modifications so it stops teching overnight. They get access to regiment stockpiles and dump them into water, enemy hands or release them to public. You may ask why public goods are bad? Pulling from public takes years compared to private stockpiles, so good luck pulling thousands of crates. (Basically alts are partisans, but you can't really catch and punish them..)

- Mid/Frontline resource fields become useless over time. It is completely a waste of time to gather resources in the midline and to transport them to backline refineries even with the help of trains.

- Factories were hyped so much when they came out that everyone started building facilities, which eventually decayed in 2-5 days since the lack of maintenance and high Garrison Supply consumption. So many resources and time wasted on facilities even as of today.

- In the beginning, I spent countless hours of trying to understand some of the game logics by reading the unofficial Foxhole wiki, maintained by some volunteers. The game lacks of ingame instructions for new players. There is a training field in the home region, but for example how to claim a border base is not explained AT ALL, which should be compulsory... I could mention dozens of others like the above mentioned.

- Nevertheless, the main issue is the balance of the game. Developers promised to keep the game assymmetric which in theory sounds a good idea, but very hard to maintain to avoid being one technology/side OP then balancing (= completely nerfing) so the other faction gets the advantage.
- Although the technology tree got a final shape by the War 100 so it is much easier to follow and understand (no tech skip anymore, no tech choices in a single tier anymore, Iron and Alu requirements were cut off by a 0 digit, and proto vehicle kits can now be built with bmats and repairable with hammer unlike many wars before when proto tanks cost rmat and could be repaired only in garages and not by hammer). Unfortunately most new players don't even know what alu and iron/copper are used for because of the lack of instructions. Especially with the facility update, you can't directly build/MPF (Mass Produce) all types of tanks.
- It is extremely hard to stream Foxhole because of streamsnipers and intel-leak. When 1.0 came out, 8 out of 10 supported streamers were on Colonial side, even nowadays it is still more preferred than the Warden side. Simps and followers and even other faction's alts would go to say hi/kill the streamer for 1 minute fame.
- Every region has a counterpart in this assymmetric world, which are very often unbalanced. Some parts of the map should be reworked. Normal North vs South layout, Wardens start with Loggerhead with 3 bridges to be attacked from, while its counterpart Fort Duncan can be attacked only from 1 bridge. Lockheed can be captured from south with 1 single tank because with the rapid decay zone, you literally can't build any defenses south of the TH, compared to its counterpart Mercy's Wish which can be defended from all sides. Also, there is the bulwark (the huge wall) in the south with only a few gaps (usually covered with AI/ghouses) to cross that makes very hard for partisans from north side to cross. In the north, there is nothing stopping you to cross to any of the northern hexagons. Camp Omicron and Anchor Beach are also in my top5 list to be reworked due to their closeness to the border/rapid decay zone.
- Satchels (Alligator Charge) in lategame just ruin the game. You get 1 person with a Heavy Machine Gun to suppress rifle/MG garrisons while the other destroys in less than 5 mins the structures that were built/teched by hard work of lots of people in the past couple of real time days.
- And one of the most crucial problem of the game: the population. People lose interest over time, structures are not planned to upkeep for 40+ days (like War 100), nightcap can't be QRF-ed (especially satchel charges overnight), etc. This map is not designed for 1200 players at low-peak hours for sure, but 2000 players on a typical weekday is not enough either. It affects both the winning and the losing side as well: obviously it is always demoralizing to be on the losing/defending side, but the long queues (30-40 players with 1 hour+ queue) on the winning side are a pain as well.
- Finally of course lots of bugs, glitches, server lags, etc. are on my list.

The game in its current state is not recommended: devs aren't listening to player feedback and always favorize only one faction. I lost interest, I don't enjoy playing it anymore as much as I have done in the last 2 years. Ah yeah, devs want to release Anvil, the Foxhole copy in medieval times so probably the playerbase from Foxhole will shift to Anvil eventually. Don't support these devs!

PS: You can call me a salty vet, but before you do, read other "salty vet" reviews on steam as well. ;) Thank you!\t\t\t\t\t

-Alts and cheaters have been an "old problem" even when I joined back in 2019. They did nothing to fix the issue to this day, even tho we told them a million times. Good luck reporting them.

-Balance has been broken ever since the asymmetry update. They clearly dont know how to balance units, just throw one overpowered vehicle to one faction, then the other when they complain on discord or reddit(LTD+AP mortarHT, 3x more costly HE rocket vs ISG, broken sniper meta etc.) - just bring back symmetry already. Not to mention adding vehicles that are downright useless and only there to bloat the vehicle lineup, but never get used. Also the devs practically told us on discord that they dont give a damn about suggestions, they will select the ones they personally like, not the ones that are needed.

-Laggy servers, frequent disconnects. Complained about this back in early 2020 when there were only around 300 to 500 players here. They did nothing, now its even worse. ♥♥♥♥♥♥ performance all round. Not to mention the game breaking bugs and bugs in general that have been in the game for ages. Bad code is not an excuse for lack of action.

-Its so grindy you might just mistake it for War Thunder. Good luck spending 6 hours mining and refining resources, building the vehicle, the ammo, getting crew if you want to do tank combat. Then flip a coin and either do marginally good or die in the first minute, because driver Bobby drove onto a mine and you got ganged by the enemy. If you get killed by a clan because you try to take away a parked vehicle, now you understand why.

-Infantry combat is dead. Good luck getting even close with all the arty, the grenade spam, machine guns and launchers around. Also I hope you like trenches, because you gonna spend a lot of time in those. I hate trenches....

-Good luck finding out what stats your guns have, since the wiki is outdated and you have to datamine the game to find out anything about your equipment. Not to mention that dev team is not transparent, they dont communicate and sometimes outright shadow update the game without any mention in the logs.

-Moderation is clearly biased. Point out a problem they dont like and you might just get banned or muted on forums. Also if you have the connection, you can get away with a lot more things.

-Devs keep telling us that they dont want to push us into clans, but in reality you either have to be in a clan or find really good players in order to do most of the things in the game. Previously you could hop in and do infantry, arty, and even logi alone, but today infantry combat is dead, arty needs at least 3 people to do any damage and bigger logi hubs need more people to make sure the damn site does not decay and die. Dont even talk about tank combat.

-Dont even mention logi, if you like getting carpal tunnel while mining or like driving trucks for hours, you gonna love this. Also I hope you like waiting, those mats will be cooking in the refinery for a while. Hope you dont get partizaned while going to the front! Enjoy your stay in Yugoslavia.

-I'm not the building type, but if you are, you're ♥♥♥♥ out of luck. Some buildings are just too weak to be worth it to build. If you want to build a bunker complex, I pity you. You'll have to ask people to vote for the upgrades and essential stuff like AT and arty bunkers take irl days to get.

-Cross faction mic is gone. Why?

Thats the end of my rambling, I might just edit this if something else comes to my mind to add.
I dont care if you are a Collie, a Warden or take no sides or someone new who did not get this game yet, this game does not respect you, the devs dont care, so you should not too unless they change stuff. I highly doubt that tho, since 1.0 came out, they already announced their new game in the works, so I wont be surprised if they just abandoned this game and move on.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter spending at least 700 hours on this game, I’ll straight up say that I both love and hate this game. The game is definitely a unique experience, but its potential is tainted by half-baked mechanics and awful decisions from the devs. If you don’t want to read the full review just keep in mind that I’m not recommending this game because of its serious alting problem, the devs’ incompetence in communicating with their playerbase and addressing issues that have plagued the game for years, and the mixed bag gameplay.

Alts, griefers, moderation
The biggest reason why people are currently leaving negative reviews is due to the game’s horrible alt issues. Some of the alt/grief tactics include dumping supplies in the ocean, mass TKing, giving tanks and supplies to the opposite faction, etc. Unfortunately, alting is so stupidly easy and poorly enforced that it’s still a thing after all these years. This leads to paranoid vets who would suspect or harass any player below OCDT of alting just because of their low ranks, regardless if they’re just new players learning the game or actual alts (Cough cough BadManLarry). The devs do nothing about it and that brings us to today where people have had it.

They also don’t enforce their code of conduct, like at all. There’s no in-game moderation and the forums thread talking about the code of conduct was made fun of so much that they locked it last year.

Devs
Now you might think, why not contact the developers? Surely they’ll listen, right? Well, here’s the thing about Siege Camp: there is no communication with the player base whatsoever. The only “communication” you have from them is that one guy on the forums who locks a necroed thread after leaving a copy pasted reply or sometimes locks a thread without saying anything. They don’t participate in any argument, they don’t explain what they can, can’t, will and won’t do. You just have to wait until the next update and hope that it won’t just feature new tanks to play with.

The only way to make the devs listen to your issues is if you scream on top of the roof of every single platform, mainly Reddit, Discord and Steam, enough times until they finally listen. I mean for christ’s sake, it required a full-fledged logi strike to make Siege Camp add basic QoL features for logistics that not only should have been in the game in the first place, but they barely scratched the surface of what the people who arranged the strike have demanded. I’m sorry, but if you have to whine like a five year old on the forums or start a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ strike in order to have your issues at least addressed, then you definitely ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up the formula for transparency.

This lack of communications will bleed through the next points I’ll make down below. Why? Because the problems I’ll mention have been there since the dawn of time!

Bugs and half-baked gameplay
I haven’t encountered a lot of bugs lately, but a few have burned into my mind. First, you have the climbing that only works half the time. Sometimes I had to mash the space bar just to vault over a bunker right in front of me. Made even worse when you try to climb through rocks where the collisions are all over the place. Second, the rock collisions are garbage. They’re hard to climb through and you sometimes get stuck until you spam space bar and find the one pixel where the character can vault over. Third, getting “area obstructed” while digging trenches and bunkers even through there’s literally nothing in the way. Good way to have your defenses poorly designed because the game itself gets in the way. https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2824661520

Then you have the QoL features that are either missing or half-assed, such as not being able to change stance while cooking a grenade or reloading, not being able to check your inventory while inside a vehicle, not being able to shoot from the window while on a passenger seat, not being able to pull or store supplies in a vehicle because some idiot decided to squad lock an empty logi truck, wasting your mag completely when you reload even if you only fired one round, not being able to climb out the trench while carrying a wounded (You can’t even put him on the edge and climb up, you have to abandon him if there are no medics around), having vehicles freeze completely during a snowstorm, AKA can’t be used at all without wasting basic materials and gasoline to build a campfire next to it, after leaving it for only five minutes (This also includes mobile artillery, which makes no ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sense), setting up queues for regions in a way that population on one side doesn’t surpass the other side by more than five players (easily exploited by griefers who afk in spawn so that logis can’t enter the region), etc.

The Combat
Oh god, the combat. As a main infantry like me, the gunplay is some of the worst I’ve ever experienced. Let me sit you through why that is.

When you aim, you have a crosshair as big as your character. The accuracy is completely RNG, so you need to hold still for the crosshair to shrink, AKA make your bullets more likely to hit. If you turn only slightly, the crosshair gets big again and you need to wait another five years to shrink it.

Seems fine. After all, not everyone’s a navy seal, so it’s realistic, right? Well no, because the RNG is inconsistent. The biggest issue is that the crosshair’s size is not distance-based, which means you still have a chance to miss someone at point blank. SMGs are especially affected by this as the RNG can sometimes lead you to spray someone at point blank and miss every single shot, and then get killed in one hit by the guy’s bolt action. Sometimes I’m able to quick draw someone far away despite the wide crosshair and other times I miss every single shot against someone right in front of me. This is infuriating because you can always get screwed over by the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ RNG no matter how good your strategy is. Because of this awful RNG based gunplay, you have shadow dancers, which are players who cheese the mechanic by zig-zagging through no man’s land and then quick shot three guys with a bolt action rifle if their RNG is good. Sure, it’s effective… At breaking immersion!

And the cover system? Incredibly artificial. If the enemy is inside the trench, it’s impossible to hit him unless you get really close to the edge of the trench and shoot him from above, because your character locks his aim on the ground next to the trench. This applies to any cover. Cover=impossible to hit. Elevation sometimes doesn’t even matter. Oh, and don’t get me started on bushes, which make you invisible and impossible to hit without someone charging you with a bayonet or spamming grenades at you.

♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ grind
If there’s something the game likes to do, it’s to waste your time. Wait in queue before entering a region, which can range from 30 minutes to an hour (That’s right, do nothing on the bare bone home island for a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hour). Sometimes wait a full minute to respawn because steamrolling the enemy too quickly would hurt their feelings. Wait 5 minutes to dig one trench line. Wait 20 minutes to cook scraps and components in the refinery. Build a base for 4 hours because no one else is doing it, only to have it leveled overnight either from decay, a clan op or a couple of idiots with satchel charges.

The grind is so bad that a lot of people refer to Foxhole as a second job. And what’s the incentive into doing all this? Uuuuh, you can get commends from other players in order to get promoted, even though the rank system is functionally useless.

Conclusion (6/10)
Foxhole is the game that both gave me the best and worst experience. It has the potential to be a great game, but it’s plagued by awful decisions and barely thought out mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t
