\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I recommend this game for players who are will to join online clans/organizaton.
You could go running aimlessly on the battlefield for half an hour. Now the map does indicate where the colonials begin and the wardens end (or vice versa) so will have a general idea how to get to the front.

Logistics, and players whom take up logistics roles, forms the backbone of gameplay. All weapons, vehicles, tanks, and even the shirts you wear had to be made by some guy grinding on the home front.
Most of the fun lies in the combat in this game though none of that would be possible if there were players dedicated to providing supplies.
In general you really really should join some group/division because then you can contribute to a cohesive plan (this allows more efficient use of materials).

Anyway, I like it. Some times you do nothing, but once in a while this game has moments that make it shine like no other game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a highly interesting real-time war that pitches two sides with a lot of lore of tense hate toward each-other. It is a top-down game that takes the Running With Rifles approach and that Planetside 2 open-world feel, except in both games, numerous vehicles are supplied to your teams. In Foxhole, though, you have to work for every last thing you get, or at least, someone does.

The developers are constantly making sure, with each new update, that logistics is always as fun as the combat; if you're looking to go on the front lines, you'll hope the logistics have supplied the town or forward operations you spawned it with the necessary guns and ammo to supply an ongoing war, getting out there and constantly pushing up, or being pushed on; while on the logistics side, you have numerous ways to get things to the frontline. From trucks, flatbeds, and fuel tankers to barges, freighters, and landing ships! And all of it has to be built by hand (not a silly crafting system or anything) using the required amount of resources at the appropriate building zone (Vehicle factory for land vehicles. Labeled dock for seacraft).

The combat uses a line-of sight and stability system, meaning you have to stand still to get any sort of accuracy, and your crosshairs close in faster if you're crouched, and especially if you're prone. Additionally you have a line that draws from your player when aiming to help tell you where exactly you're aiming, and also a yellow line from the ground to the impact point to tell you how high you're automatically aiming. Affixing a supplied bayonet to a rifle or carbine (the only two weapons that can use it) is basically like equipping a lightsaber. If you get close and charge in, using this bayonet (you cannot shoot while it is affixed) you will instantly kill anyone you come into contact with, except for teammates of course.

I put hours and hours of my life into the game. It's like a second job for me. I'm not angry when my dude dies. I always had trouble playing war games like this in the FPS genre because I got angry when I died. I know it wasn't good to lose a life there, but at least I'm not angry about it. I'm already thinking of what I should've done differently. Every facet of Foxhole is madly interesting to me, from that industrialism feel of logistics, to the epic battles of the frontline. It has never got boring, and I've been playing for years!\t\t\t\t\t
