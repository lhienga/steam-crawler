Dotyczy to zarówno zwykłych szarych żołnierzy jak, logistyków którzy wiozą niezbędne wyposażenie na linie frontu, a także i sił specjalnych które przeprowadzają zmasowany rajd na tyły wroga, które musza przemieszczać się szybko i niespodziewanie by uniknąć wykrycia. Co z tego że barki desantowe dotrą do celu skoro statki z wyposażeniem utkną na granicy regionów w oczekiwaniu na kolejkę i zostaną podczas tego oczekiwania przechwycone przez jednostki wroga? Co z tego, ze skoordynowana akcja 50 czy 100 graczy, którą przygotowywali czasem i kilka dni zostanie przez to pozbawiona szans na powodzenie. Przecież w grze wprowadzono w zamian kolejny rodzaj czołgu, którego produkcja zajmie kilkanaście roboczogodzin, którego w żaden sposób nie można skutecznie ochronić przed złodziejami z własnej frakcji, którzy bardzo często są tak naprawdę graczami przeciwnej frakcji którzy kupili sobie drugie konto tylko po to by sabotować swego przeciwnika. Mam dość.

I believe that after exactly 25 months after purchasing the game and almost 3,200 hours played, the time has come to say Enough.This game has eaten its tail. Developers focused on new military equipment and new mechanics, which were probably intended to attract players in practice, however, they became cumbersome and led to the need for even more grind, ignoring the fundamental problems.
Developers have no ideas on how to solve the fundamental problems with server queues. This is important because the game is divided into about 30-40 contiguous regions, with battles taking place in a maximum of 6-7 of them at the same time. And each region has its own small pool of slots. On the one hand, the developers expect players to cooperate what's more they encourage it during developer streams. However, on the other hand, they do nothing about the problem of overcrowded servers, maintaining an opaque queuing system, priorities, unexpected reduction of server bandwidth, which often leads to a situation where in order to join a region that is at the front, you have to wait even more than an hour at the border. This applies to both ordinary gray soldiers and logisticians who carry the necessary equipment to the front lines, as well as special forces who carry out a massive raid on the enemy's rear, which must move quickly and unexpectedly to avoid detection. So what if the landing barges reach their destination, if the ships with equipment are stuck at the border of the regions waiting for a turn and are intercepted by enemy units while waiting? What about the fact that a coordinated action of 50 or 100 players, which they prepared sometimes for several days, will be deprived of any chance of success. After all, the game instead introduced another type of tank, the production of which will take several man-hours, which in no way can be effectively protected from thieves from their own faction, who very often are actually players of the opposite faction who bought themselves a second account just to sabotage their opponent. I have had enough.

The Good
The game is for now unique in of massive multiplayers, a lot of mechanics involved through multiple types of gameplay & usefulness to the war. The lack of cosmetics is good, everyone looks the same because nobody is here to boost your own ego with non-necessary good looking.
I like how the game looks too. There is a lore which progress (okay it's a bit inconsistent but it's fine now) and has an integration ingame in a smart way.
The top down view is refreshing and makes the game a bit more tactical and cinematic.
The constant influx of new content from the devs team.
Logistics, well sort of, having to craft stuff is fun, knowing you can win by cutting the enemy logistics is fun oo.
The Bad
If you don't speak english which is the lingua Franca on foxhole currently you will not appreciate a good part of the massive multiplayer and will cause trouble to your surrounding, i cannot tell how many times i did a mistake and someone in the VC alerted me or how many times i could succesfully explain something i was doing. But if you tell someone he's doing a mistakes and he doesn't understand or have its ingame VC off, it's gonna make you angry and you will think he's a spy.
Performances issues: There is definitvely some.
Censoring: The chat is censored and you cannot opt in or out, it sucks, even stupid words like coom is censored, want to do the welcome to the coom zone meme? Thrust a 68mm into your posterior this is a christian server. And this despite this game being crude on a lot aspects with a mature community.
Th
Growing servers issues: Lag spike(i think it's client side but not sure), rubber banding, ghosts, glitched nukes. not everyday but when it happens it will ruins your day.
The Ugly
Years old unfixed bugs, you can't do a gameing sessions without seing some bugs anywhere.
The maps are great and are overall upgraded overtime (slowy) but there is tons of invisible trees, bushes, bushes that are rocks, rocks that are bushes, flying trees, invisible moutains, moutains you can cross through like it's an illusions, holes where you're stuck until someone end your misery (or if you can use the !unstuck command).
At the current state building is qualifiable of psychological torture with the introduction of fire, game balance problems with productions of maintenance for bases you build.
Communications tools are...lacking, there is a VC, you can put map post (with extremely brief words), there is a chat with multiple tabs, well there is in fact 4 chats. There is 4 supported alphabets, western(english), cyrrilic(russian), korean and chinese. And it cause trouble because personally i would like to have a way to communicate about partisans in my logi map to other people which don't read my alphabet.
Devs communications plummeted to a less-than-bare-minimum, there is no community manager anymore, i don't know the reasons but i can guess the community must have badly treated them.
The community toxicity is a point you might heard a lot about. First there is the outside of the game, Reddit: do not. Then there is the Foxhole Discord Server which you should be careful because recently a good and well known content creator (mainly doing drawings) got doxxed for reasons? And i read devs talking about how they received death threat on a common basis. That said usually regiments/clans don't have such problems from my experience.
And the ingame toxicity where you get yelled at because you did something wrong, and this part is caused a part by me, i don't know how but the game turned me into a toxic vet. That said, i'm overall not a majority and most of people are nice.
Queues and Queues bugs: Queues are here because you cannot overpopulate the ennemy or because servers have hits their generous limits. It's painfull, sadening sometimes because you can't play where you want and with too much of your friends and fellow regiment members but great... when it works fine. Officialy devs never talked about such things as queue bugs and when asked on reddit, the answer is :there is no queue bugs. But when your side is queued, there is 35 people in it and there is 50 people and not queued in front, you know there is a bug.
Too much content directly: The 1.0 update introduced a huge tons of content, i was really into it, until i tried the dev branch, which lasted 2 weeks (extremely short for this amount of content). Where there was obvious issues that despite the community trying to communicate were left ignored and later on the release costed a lot of potential new players to quit. Devs finally reached out with some hot fixes but it was already a bad giveaway and some people already left. And still to this day, half a year after the release, some major issues pointed by the community have still not being considered by the dev team as a problem. It will be fixed by a way or another, but when? that's the irritating part, maybe some qualities of life improvement will never come or in a year. Maybe devs will throw off foxhole to focus into Anvil. I've seen devs stopping serving a game i loved in the past or just abandoned it unbalanced until the community left and then moved out to new projects. And i feel like with the new game they are about to pre-release, it might be the case. Foxhole is there from 2017, only less than 2K players on average at a time playing it.

More devs communications.
Less tedious logistical tasks, less soul eating driving, less assembling waiting time, more communication tools.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was a great deal of fun in alpha

- minimal grind logistics driven by communication and collaboration (If anyone remembers Quarter Masters, instant refining)
- player-driven technology system (offered factions a strategic choice between teching weapons, equipment or armor)
- cross-faction voice chat (for some people it was half the fun)
- fast-paced, decisive combat (with cheaper equipment, people weren't scared to charge into battle with tanks or high-tier weaponry)
- balanced factions & maps (differences were cosmetic)

Since alpha, its been a slow and steady downhill. Even though many new and amazing features were added, previously thought impossible, and at times the game felt like it improved a lot, every iteration ended being an overall downgrade in terms of fun. The larger the game world got and the more features were added, the more time each war sucked out of your life, to the point that unless you are a time millionare don't expect to actually get anything done in foxhole.

Sure, you can still experience the same amazing moments in foxhole as you could back in the alpha days, as well as many new things that were the result of the recent updates, but these short periods of having actual fun are now so far in between the moments of grind, waiting and general boredom that its hardly worth your time.

I have this formula to summarize the problem:

Rate of Fun = Total Fun / Time investment

While each update added to how much fun you could have playing foxhole, the time investment required to have said fun has been increasing way much more. This has gotten so bad with the recent updates that many players including myself have had the final straw and decided to quit and also drop a bad review on the way out.

I've been dreaming of the day trains would be added to logistics. Sure you can now drive a trainload of supplies and munitions to the front, if you happen to have a couple days to spare to first mine and build everything needed to drive and supply said train, and that's with a clan, of course. Sure, you can drive epic armored vehicles (flame tanks, multi-turret heavy tanks, landships) into battle, if you have say a week to spare to build up to the point you can produce/upgrade them, and then maybe have a little bit of fun before you lose your tank to something stupid like rng, noobs/alts, or realize that the game is so poorly balanced that most types of armor are total garbage.

And don't get me started on the faction asymmetry. Unlike in most asymmetric games where thousands of games are played for designers to find right balance between the factions, foxhole's developers test out their ideas for asymmetry precisely 0 times, hoping to simply guess the balance right. Since the developers do not play their game very often and don't really understand how valuable range, hp, damage, cost, speed or ammo capacity is, they have no chance of getting it right. Often I will see imbalanced items where one side has a range advantage or damage while the other has extra speed or ammo capacity (range being the single most important aspect in most situations, being difficult to compensate with any other buff). The end result is that each side has equipment and vehicles where the other side has a flat out better option which makes everyone unhappy. And since the asymmetry currently favors one side towards the endgame, the outcome of each war is decided in advance.

Finally, there is the problem with alt accounts, cheaters and population swings. Foxhole was built on the idea that no one man should be able to single-handedly decide the fate of the war. But with how much grind is required to build certain things, should those certain things fall into the wrong hands weeks worth of effort could be lost. Entire clan facilities go up in flames, or whole regions lost because of the work of one alt. If you are working to sabotage your team you can have a massive impact on the war. Another major problem is, certain times of the day/week will see more players for one faction then the other. This is primarily the result of discordification / twitch effect / national clans resulting in times where it can be extremely unfun to play the game. Players on one side facing massive queues preventing players from joining a front while players on the other side is getting stomped on all fronts by sheer numbers. I'm not sure what's the solution to this problem, but it needs to be addressed somehow.

If I can have one advice for the developers, I would say it would be best to take a step back and re-evaluate everything. The technology behind foxhole is amazing, but the actual game is (now) terrible. I wouldn't mind paying again for foxhole 2.0 with the exact same technology but better design. Maybe a new map and different factions without asymmetry or carefully tested asymmetry (you could easily find many volunteers among the community). Regions could be smaller but built with more care. This would reduce lag, improve combat and make logi less of a grind. Wars could be designed to be shorter too. People need to experience victory more often, not less, and nobody likes waiting weeks to unlock something they really want to build.

That's All

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is one of a kind and if you're looking for an mmo war game to sink an unhealthy amount of hours into then you've come to the right place.

Very unique MMO war game that I think some people get way too emotionally invested in because that's what it calls for to "win" at times. I don't really see the imbalances some others say are at play here, there is some asymmetry to the factions but from what I can tell there are pretty clear strengths and weaknesses that can be exploited for each side. In my view, it comes down to getting others healthily invested in the results and, if the review bombing is any indication, that's no small feat.

I will say that I think the Logi and Building roles do need some love. I'm not into Factorio or base building games typically but the time it takes to farm resources and get supplies to the frontlines is frankly cripplingly boring. I'm not sure if it's a matter of better understanding the mechanics at play here or needing to coordinate better, but that seems to be a general consensus of the community and something as important as supply lines and town maintenance for a faction need to feel more rewarding than they are in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
