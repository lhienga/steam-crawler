\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't remember when I picked up Foxhole or why I did. But I cannot thank my past self more for the purchase. Foxhole has been a thrilling adventure every time I play and the combat is nothing less than exciting. If you are considering picking up the game I recommend doing so.

Being in a world where every building, rifle, and vehicle are created by players is something I have never gotten to experience before. And yes it is just that! Every single tool you see in the game and many of the buildings were created by players and you will often see players utilizing factories and scrap yards if you are in the production zones. But it doesn't stop there. You will also find a variety of players creating fortifications on the front lines!

But if you choose to be just a soldier which is what I have spent a majority of my time doing then you will fit right at home in the trenches holding off one of the two factions of the game the Colonials and the Wardens. Something I will say about this game is that you are not usually going to be a war hero. This game isn't about that. You are a part of the wider whole and a member of a large and diverse team. Rarely does going at it alone do anything more than get yourself killed. But that doesn't mean you can't try it out as a partisan and disrupting enemy supply lines.

With all that being said the truth of Foxhole is that our achievements and victories are part of a larger war. We may have felt that we won an adrenaline fueled battle the likes of which could never be topped only to remember that the enemy has another base over the next hill. But that is the beauty of Foxhole. No two fights are the same and the experiences you have over VOIP are game changing.

This review was rather lackluster but truly this is a great game that has constant updates. I recommend you look into it. And should you decide to join the Wardens I hope to see you on the front lines or perhaps alongside me in the mighty fleet.\t\t\t\t\t
