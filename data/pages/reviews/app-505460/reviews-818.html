\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the game is "not there yet" the core game was improved by leaps and bounds with the last update. Right now the game is still at a stage where things such as ressource cost and facility placement are finetuned, but the game has come a long way from the skirmishes on one small map back in the day.

I will not lie there are still some issues with the game, both big and small, and for me this is not a game I can play for a long time non stop since it can be quite frustrating at times and there are no "personal" goals for players. So if you like somewhat tactical gameplay, with logistics operations spanning the whole map and battles that could be part of a hollywood movie give this game a try, it might be a bit hard to get into it at the start but if you get the hang of it you will always be back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole, a game that differs substantially from the hundreds of indie titles on Steam. Imagine Company of Heroes, but with each individual soldier as an actual player. You are but a small part of a complex and inter-connected network of players within your chosen faction (Wardens or Colonials). Nearly all aspects of the game is heavily driven by the player. Every rifle, bullet, tank, and bunker are manufactured, supplied, driven, and built by the players themselves. Both sides vie for control of an entire continent in a WW2/WW1 style 'line' based combat. The thing that makes this game truly enjoyable, is the community. The players control the game, they determine its narrative. Everyone has their moment within the game where they can reflect upon their actions and say 'this was made possible because I did that.' Everyone gets to be the hero in this game. Everyone's actions matter, its not just drowned out by some guy who has thousands of hours invested into the game. The power of change is truly in your hands within Foxhole. And the developers strive to make that even more possible via monthly updates. That is why I myself have found that I absolutely love it. That is why I would recommend it to anyone who favors team-based warfare games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy experience of Foxhole is divided into pros and cons for a simple explenation as of why I have mixed feelings for the game but at the end don't suggest you buy it.


The Pros

The pros for the game is that it's under constant development, being updated with new vehicles and equipment. The maps are massive, and you get to experience a war which is determined by cooperation and a logistical system to aid the fronts with equipment.

It is truly a fun game if you have a few friends to play with and want to play for a few hours together.


The Cons

The cons for the game is a relatively toxic community... Within the team (Warden & Colonials) it's relatively peaceful, contrasting to you and the opposite team. The toxicity is within the community, which has gotten worse between the years. The second con is the logistical system in which the game is dependent on. For the ones who hasn't played, it's a system to supply the fronts in the war with equipment (Guns, explosives & equipment) which is created by resources gathered to craft them. The problem is the a lot of new players who are able to withdraw the rarest equipment (Satchel charges, RPG's & Heavy Explosives) and waste them immidietly, not understanding the rarity of it. Concluding the fact that most work done by logistics is for nothing due to the uninformed/bad players.

In conclusion, it's a great game in terms of being a developed game. However, the community & game mechanics being dependent on the community is what makes the game bad, as it's hard to make progress, forcing people to to get into clans hoarding resources.\t\t\t\t\t
