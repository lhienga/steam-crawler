\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I forgot just how fantastic this game is. I bought and played it for under twenty hours before forgetting the game existed. I remember having good fun, and I always wanted to get back into it but never found the time.

I play solo, which can be a bit of a frustrating experience. But soloing as a medic and doing the best you can to make sure your teammates don't have to respawn (and spend the required respawn resources) is honestly very rewarding. Yelling "it's okay, I got you" as someone is RP screaming down the mic while you're patching them up and saving their life leaves a feeling that can't be described.

As of writing this, War 107 has ended in a Warden victory. I was lucky enough to have logged in today. But for my faction to have also won makes me want to fight in the next war even harder. Sitting in a trench, gunning down Collies as they try to push our position and praying to whomever that you don't get shredded by an artillery strike is a hell of an experience. And it's only made sweeter by the victory screen.

If you're debating whether to get this because you have no one to play it with - don't. Trust me, you'll feel a sense of belonging just from fighting alongside countless fellow soldiers. And you'll find a play-style you like.\t\t\t\t\t

I've actually had a few nice interactions so far even when im doing something unorthodox. I've mostly been fighting on the front-lines and have yet to dip my toes in any of the more technical aspects of the war. But the combat is rather fun and tricky to learn. Once i got the hang of it ive been enjoying even more than i already was. I think the main fear of getting into this game is the community and to be honest its scarier then it looks but i can assure you there are way more nice and engaging comrades then there are bad ones.

I also think that if you find games where you're doing a lot of hard work and getting little in return to be not as fun then this might not be a game for you. Attacks and killing other players can be rewarding in itself but if you're expecting to hop on a push the front line even further then you're going to find yourself mentally exhausted because more often then not you will be fighting in the same spot for hours or even days. The other day i was fighting in one area and decided to get off and take a break. When i returned we were still in the same spot doing almost the same thing. Fighting in the same trench as before. But it does feel nice when you get back on and you see that the area that you fought for hours and days for was finally captured.

Good Game. Worth a try!\t\t\t\t\t
