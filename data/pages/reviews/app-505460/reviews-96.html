\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf there was an option to leave a mixed review, I certainly would. Foxhole is one of those rare gems that I've been seeking for many years and yet I cannot help but find myself rather... disappointed in several regards. My review will detail what I love, and utterly despise about this enjoyable, but headache inducing game, and why I give it a tentative thumbs up.


What I love:
Teamwork the good:
The game glorifies teamwork. Teamwork is literally the name of the game and without it nothing would get done. Half the time, everything runs smoothly. Logistics is on point, delivering ammo, B-mats, weapons, uniforms, medical supplies, explosives, ect to the active frontlines where they're sorely needed. Players are working together to fortify a heavily contested location; building up intricate bunker and trench lines that are nigh impossible to crack without a heavy armor spearhead and countless artillery bombardments, coordinating combined arms of artillery, tanks, light scout vehicles and infantry into a beautiful blend of overwhelming firepower and morale crushing speed. Medics are playing their roles well keeping the frontline alive and active with their invaluable work, ensuring each squad is back up to full fighting strength without too much downtime. Often going out of their way to pull victory from the jaws of defeat with well timed and well coordinated triage efforts. Teamwork makes the entire game run, and without teamwork Foxhole just wouldn't be possible. By that point alone it's the main selling point that hooked me without question.


The Community the good:
Regardless what side of the coin you fall into; Colonials, Wardens, it doesn't much matter. Foxhole offers you a rich and diverse group of players who can move whole armies, shatter impregnable fortresses, craft endless tides of weapons and vehicles for war, aiding and guiding newer players on how to play well, while providing an experience unlike anything I've seen in an MMO before. I've met many a wonderful player who has made the game all that more engaging and wonderful, forging strong bonds of friendship that last even to this day for me. Foxhole has time and time again shown it's an incredible game with everything I could ask for. It's like a blend of RTS and third person shooters, and I adore it beyond words. As cliche as it sounds, when you're hunkered down in a foxhole with three other players with artillery shells exploding all around you, you feel a kinship with those players. Especially when someone breaks out into a shanty while explosions tear the land asunder with everyone else joining in.


The Wars the good:
Foxhole lives up to the promise in crafting an all encompassing war that spans days, weeks, months at a timeWars that can swing from one side having the initiative and advantage, to it all being lost by one massive breakthrough by the other team. The high stakes of every engagement, the thrill of being part in some grand operation to push the enemy out of territory you need, and the white knuckle firefights where one mistake could cost you hours of progress. It what really helps the game shine, and makes it feel like the most authentic large scale persistent war I've had the pleasure of playing in.


What I hate:
Teamwork the bad:
While teamwork is the name of the game... you're not always going to find it in Foxhole. Logistics ignores priority zones in favor of hording resources for specific clan bases, which can lead to massive swathes of territory being lost, and the war tipping firmly into the favor of the enemy. Players run around like chickens without heads grabbing as much ammo and material as possible only to die by throwing themselves haphazardly into the enemy lines losing both shirts and vital resources. Or just as often kamikaze a vehicle into the enemy base in some vain effort to feel like the main character of the game. Medics are screamed at for not running out into the line of fire to save some fool who chose to run headlong into the enemy machine guns or just as often not doing their jobs and healing those who are dying. And the absolute nightmare that is haphazardly constructed bases that are more of a detriment to the builders than to the enemy. Teamwork is the all important thing that makes Foxhole run smoothly, and unfortunately it's a mixed bag which result you'll get. Half the time it's a deeply engaging and breath taking example of what the players can accomplish by working together, and other times it's an utter cesspool of stupidity that there is no real escape from.


The Community the bad:
This is where the majority of my criticism lays. In this large scale persistent war you will find the absolute worst aspects of humanity on full display. Granted, my complaints are not focused upon the majority of the community, but the deafening minority who unleash slur after slur at each other for no other reason than to get a rise out of the other. Attacking people based upon the faction they choose to play. Sending death threats and calls to 'remove themselves from living' for daring to play a faction other than their own, harassing and degrading new players for simply trying to learn the game or just outright shooting them repeatedly for the laughs, and countless other examples. The game is supposed to glorify players bonding and playing together for a shared goal, and yet a vocal minority ruins the fun for the rest of the players by being xenophbic asshats who deify their precious faction to an unhealthy degree.


The Wars the bad:
In a game about persistent war, there are going to be agonizingly long stretches where you find yourself with nothing to really do. You missed the massive breakthrough into enemy territory and there's no trucks around to give you a lift? Better start running and hope you don't run into partisans. Get killed without setting your spawn point at the Battle Bunker? Best hope you don't get left behind when the frontline charges forward. The wars can be a great deal of fun when you get to take part in the actual fighting, but just as often you can get left behind without warning, or get drafted into resource consolidation by some higher ranking player who can and will kill you for disobeying them. Or just as often staring out across no-man's-land for hours on end waiting for the enemy to attack or your own team to attack. Or, worst of all, being on the receiving end of a total rout. Being steamrolled day in day out with no real way of fighting back, it becomes discouraging, and frankly not much fun. I could go on much more about it all, but I think my point is fairly clear.


Conclusion:
At the end of the day I give the game a solid 3 out of 5. Foxhole has it's goods, it's bads, and it's ugly. In short, you should at least give the game a try and see if it's up your alley, though I highly suggest getting it while it's on sale. The game may not be for you, but there's no real harm in giving it a try as long as you keep in mind that like any game there will be toxic ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, and frustrating lapses in action. I have much more to say about the game, but this review is already long enough as it is, and in the end only you can formulate your own opinion on this rough but undeniably gorgeous gem. A tentative thumbs up.
Signed: Tavi, the solo Medic Main.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for a shooter where you can get a pentakill after 5 seconds you spawned in, this game is not for you. If you really want a WWII shooter experience without feeling like the main character and unfair death is the norm, this game is for you.

First off, your actions have to be slow and deliberate in order play well. You can't just rush into the front lines. I can not tell you how many times in my first few hours in the game how I took a bunch of gear just to go and die because I thought the trenches were just aesthetic. Only poke your head out for short periods of time. Stabilize your gun before you fire. Wait until night to rush across the battlefield. Even tanks can get demolished by going a bit too far into enemy territory.

If you want a more chill experience, you don't have to be in the front lines. The equipment in a base is constructed and transported by far more people in the back lines than in the front. If you just want to collect coal for 8 hours strait while listening to music, you can do that.

The world makes you feel so inconsequential in the whole scheme of things. Even if you are in a tank or manning artillery or get behind enemy lines with a bayonet, your actions can not hope to change the tide of war. A few feet of ground can be an entire region lost tomorrow. Even if you do win, a new war will just start up anew again. If you feel like you let your faction down, just know it's not your fault.

Anyways, Foxhole is fun even when you get pounded by infinite artillery/tanks, the game can be very intense or not if you so feel like it, and the Wardens can eat my Colonial ass.\t\t\t\t\t
