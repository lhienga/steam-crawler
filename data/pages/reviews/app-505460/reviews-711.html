\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI purchased Foxhole when it became available on steam as an early access game and I have found this game to be the most enjoyable multi-player game I have played across genres in recent times.

I like the realism that requires the opposing sides to build everything that is used in the game such as ammo, medical supplies and even vehicles. Although, if you are an action orientated person, again you can hitch a ride straight to the front and battle it out in huge PVP battles with a third person perspective. I have spent most of my 200+ hours scrapping salvage, making medical supplies and hauling urgent supplies to the front lines. Every now and then I feel brave and head into the battle armed with a pistol and a rifle and I have enjoyed the battle dynamics that this game provides.

The game offers you a choice between going it alone and doing what you want, to joining up with a team or a squad of your choice and working together. The best thing is, it doesn't matter what you do, it all contributes to your faction's advancement to victory.

I would highly recommend this game as I will be playing this for many years to come.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's be straight, this isn't a game for everyone. As of Novemeber 2020 though it has 100k registered soldiers in the current war and it's been fun for me coming back. If you're not sure what the game is, read on. The map is made of large hexagons, probably the size of a decent IRL town. You can spawn in friendly town halls and other points in the zone and go fight. If the enemy overwhelm you and destroy/rebuild the town hall you lose ground, the first team to reach the other side of the map wins (I think?) The top-down style is hard to get used to, as are line-of-sight mechanics (Eg if you're crouched behind a sandbag even though the camera shows the terrain ahead you will not see any enemy soldiers or vehicles because your character's "eyes" are below the sandbags and thus they wouldn't be able to see soldiers either). The most critical part is that guns (aside from a pistol), grenades and building materials, even respawn tickets ("Shirts") must be created elsewhere and trucked in. This means you can't go yolo and take all the guns and try to be Rambo, your team will hate you and you will probably die and lose it all.

On a negative side I find the comms to be lacking. Voice can only be heard in a small radius which makes sense until your commander (some random guy who has a plan) runs out of sight and now you're lost and alone, The community is also a little haphazard, I was expecting disciplined "guilds" to form out-of-game with their own mumble/discords and third party websites. This allows them to set up, for example a hauling company which runs logistics efficiently. I haven't seen anything like that so far and it all feels a bit random.

Last negative is that the wars are extremely developer-led. They decide the zone layout and this means that when a war is won/lost the game is unplayable until the devs go in and "reset" the war. I think this is a risk going forward and could mean you buy the game and can't play for a couple of days. Maybe some sort of random/pre-programmed rotation could be pre-prepared allowing the "reset" to take an hour or so. Wars seem to last weeks so it's not a massive deal but seems strange to me.

Overall, recommend if you like a sort of RTS/teamwork/topdown/strategic sort of game where success is measured in how well your faction is doing rather than how many kills you have individually.\t\t\t\t\t
