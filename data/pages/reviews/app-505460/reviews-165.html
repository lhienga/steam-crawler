I've played it more than 1000 hours, I've involved myself with clans, random pick up operations, frontline, tanking, logi, backline saboteur, partisan and more. I've seen more of this game than anyone can really be expected to, and some parts are absolutely amazing, completely unlike any other game out there.

But I just can't recommend it. It's not in a good state right now. There are bad actors acting in bad faith, sabotaging their own team. There are systems that encourage hoarding of resources, that divide the team into factions instead of uniting them against a common foe. There are extremely obtuse, confusing and counter-intuitive mechanics that are difficult to learn even with help, and basically impossible without the organization of a clan. Which is all fine for a game if it just needed a little polish. But it's more than that now.

This game is supposed to be finished. It's past 1.0 release, and there is little to no communication from the devs, new content that is actively counter to their stated goals, and still no effective moderation system. Neither in game moderators that can ban bad actors, nor in-game systems to help the community police themselves.

It pains me to write this, because I am very passionate about this game, and it could be a 10/10 yes play this game recommendation. But it just isn't, because this game isn't what you'd expect from a finished game, and the developers don't seem to be doing anything to make it better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my most played game ever on Steam and the first beyond 1000 hours.

Foxhole is one of the most unique games I've ever had the pleasure to experience and never again in my life will I find one so well cared for by its developers or community. I bought this game I believe in 2021 for $20.00 and in that time have received what would be the equivalent of hundreds of dollars in DLC for free with the game's continued development.

This game is a 24/7 persistent MMO war game -> this leads to some of the most intense combined arms combat situations you'll experience in any modern game. This coupled with one fo the greatest (in-game) communities in gaming and you're literally buying one of the greatest purchases of all time.

This is an INTENSE & peaceful game. Players mine, refine, craft, transport, use and build nearly everything and a lot of nuance to game mechanics that otherwise aren't very obvious.

Pros:
In-depth game mechanics
Great in-game community
Constant updates
Logistically focused
Solid Combat
Continuous Improvements
Best value for your money you'll ever spend on Steam
Players get locked to a single faction per War (which can last 30+ days)
Developers honestly love this game

Cons:
Server stability can be wonky (often)
Reddit is filled with..... *passionate* players.
Game is niche, so high turnover of players.
Game is niche, friends you bring in can and will leave game quickly
Game is experiencing (at least a perceived) an increased rate of griefing, bug exploits and 'alts' (players that purchase two accounts instead of one to cheat on one side). This causing a high rate of player attrition and review bombing with a hope to get developer attention of issues.

You will meet great friends in this game across the world and I welcome you to join us! Both factions are filled with really fun people and you can't go wrong picking a side (I play both and switch between Wars)!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt closing in on 1800 hours (most actual play time, not afk to combat AI mechanics get to that later) I find myself continually hoping the dev's will fix the glaringly broken mechanics in their game. I cannot recommend anyone buy this game right now in its current state and have to assume their next game will suffer the same issue's. This game has changed dramatically over the few years ive owned it. On the surface these changes always seemed positive and in the right direction. For instance 1.0 introduced trains and facilities partially to address the LOGI strike that happened and as a QOL improvment to gameplay loops in Logi and production. What was learned quickly was a half backed system that more than doubled the workload in these loops as effectively you needed to to both now. Not an either or. "Dev vision" has become meme in the community to the point where many of us joke that this game is solely created as a psychological experiment testing peoples breaking limits. This game has NO moderators, appears to have no anti cheat, and the only two report mechanics are atrocious and appear to result in no permanent fixes. The report to dev's system is entirely external, and warns you in advance you will never hear anything further regarding your complaint. The internal system is an easily exploitable system where a small group of people can soft ban you without cause, reason, justification, proof etc. All these players have to do is click a button saying reported and you get first weapons locked, and then booted from server from some time (never longer than a few days in extreme cases).

Ultimately this leads to the swarm of down voting clearly going on. Every war sees the odd cheater / hacker / alt / griefer abusing the game and ruining it for everyone. past examples have been limited to a single account or two and have likely had little impact on the greater war. This "WAR 100" has seen coordinated strikes across multiple maps to a level never seen before. Previous examples have been someone "alting" a single tank or singularly strategic thing. In one instance alone this war, a clan (with proof) caught someone who eventually drove over 50 end game tanks into the water moments before a full scale invasion, leaving the defenders without a chance to fair play. I've very actively played over the past 1.5 years and the trend from the dev's is clear they simply do not care about their community. I've personally seen the communicate less and less, and continue to push out "fixes" no one asks for or worse yet, bastardised versions of that which the community is screaming for. Im happy to change my review once actual positive actions take place but to be honest, i dont even foresee lies from the dev's, that would require them to communicate with us.\t\t\t\t\t
