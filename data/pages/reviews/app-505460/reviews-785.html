Labor battalion, working hard, mining materials to be processed into weapons for the troops. A hard job, but a satisfying one. Salvaging the piles was the only thing that mattered to us.

Then the first report came in. The front line had broken, the trucks full of men began to stream from our base, trucks that at one time had been used to carry materials now carry men.

A NCO aproached our salvage team, asking for volunteers for some "Unorthadox tactics". Wanting a bit of fun and excitment, I said yes.

As the truck sped past the barely guarded Warden outpost, a full stray shots hit our driver. Being the one given the medic kit, when we finally stopped once we were sure we hadn't been chased, I healed the man. As we got closer to our "objective", I soon realize the plan.

There sat a colonial tank, slightly damaged but still in working order. As we all chipped in to repair it, we soon had it up and running. We got a report that a push was being planned for the ould outpost.

Full speed the tank rumbled towards the enemy lines. At first, we heard yells of joy and delight, thinking it was their own. As I followed in the truck behind, it soon took a different scene.

Howitzer shells rained down on their bunkers, bodies flied from where they sat. The gunner in the tank opened up, shell after shell into any building.

Gunfire started to light up my truck, pistol fire from a few medics they had missed. Quickly I drived back towards our line, being met with an advancing wave of our own men in trucks and in halftracks.

I don't know what happened to the tank crew, but god I hope they made it.

Foxhole allows you to either be a soldier on the front line or a logistician running supplies from factories to the front. There is a lot to do in between those two roles, but if you have a decent head on your shoulders you will have fun. There is no matchmaking, and big battles happen where players make them happen. Fighting for an hour in Linn of Mercy just to eventually have supply cut off and face defeat sucks, but that hour was not a bad time. The shooting is fun, though a little hard to get used to. Once you master the different weapons and know how moving and stances affect your accuracy, you start to get decent and score kills. Promotion is completely based on player recognition, so performing great feats in battle is a nice way to get promoted. Higher rank does nothing besides display your rank next to your name, but lower ranking players tend to listen to higher ranking ones. Ranks such as Major can often get multiple players to band together to work toward a common objective, and you start to see that people don't get to higher ranks for no reason. I recommend the game to people who like strategy, but if you want something that is action packed ALL the time you won't find that experience here. The action you will face has the potential to be way more exciting than a traditional first person shooter, you just have to find it first.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of those rare games that leaves you with distinct memories of interactions, encounters, etc. Other players are the content, and participating in the larger operations is an experience.

A fresh push in a rarely touched genre, a mashup of Planetside meets top down shooter like an inverted RTS. Everybody is one flimsy solider so cooperation and working in groups is necessary to be effective. Hundreds of players across a map so large it's split into dozens of servers with connected borders. Content ranges from logistics to the meat-and-potatoes combat, with no fixed roles in a sandbox-like engine. They've changed downtime between wars, and done smaller events, but the core game of World Conquest is like a single massive RTS match as seen from one soldier. The World Conquest lasts literal weeks, with online tools to watch the overmap progress like checking in on the score of a sports game.

Updates usually have major changes. A lot of wars, and the way they play out, are tied to whatever big features are added or changed. I like that the developers are always trying to innovate, but some people don't like going through changes that get rolled back or adjusted later.

Worth trying just to experience the scope of it, but a long learning curve before you're more help than nuisance. Shadow people and ask questions.\t\t\t\t\t
