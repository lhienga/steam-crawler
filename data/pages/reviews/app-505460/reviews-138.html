\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the most sophisticated MMORPGs I have ever played. Any player can take on any role, e.g. infantry, logistics driver, medic, tankmen, gunboat driver, artillery gunner, bunker builder. Infantry combat is only a small part of the gameplay for many, but core to the game. Where else can you experience being under artillery fire for 8 hours while you desperately try to hold out for counter artillery? In what other world can you attempt to burn down weeks of another players' work?

To new player, I would recommend watching YouTube videos or having a friendly player walk you through the basics at the tutorial zone. I also recommend joining the faction and regiment discords to find one of the most collaborative, engaging communities. If you are a builder, enjoy that you built it, but expect that it will go away.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game of impeccable quality. I have made several good friends playing as the game naturally gears players toward teamwork. For many players during the pandemic and those separated from in person table top gaming communities it is a great alternative. After my first dozen or so hours I was amazed at the value per dollar I had spent. Not only did I feel like I had a great entertainment value on my investment. I felt that it was heavily under-priced for what it is.

Key to the games amazing experience are four key elements. Some of them the development team has continued to improve over the time I have played;
1. Foxholes communications: Communications in Foxhole is easy to understand and to master. This is the first game I purchased a microphone for and it works wonderfully.
2. Fictional Geography: From my first battle at Outwitch Ranch to my fully veteran experience defending the Great Warden Dam the geography of Foxhole is second to non. Each bullet fired, each bandage used on a bleeding comrade, and each bunker built endears players to a location more and more until certain locations become places.
3. Moral: This is the first game I have ever played where battle psychology can be used to devastating affect. A single man with a bayonet can cause a whole trench line to flee in terror.
4. Equalizer: The games lack of achievements and war to war accomplishment inspires players. High school students play next to lawyers. Programmers next to sewage plant workers. It doesn't matter who you are or where you come from. Skill and competence are all players care for. A welcome escape from class structures.

Try Foxhole... Please. You should try at least once. :)\t\t\t\t\t

So I have clocked almost 100 hours in this game over the past week and I have had a lot of fun and I think I will keep playing it for some time. If I could give the game a neutral rating I would have done so, but for reasons that I will get into I chose to give a negative rating over a positive one.

Like I said the game is actually fun, but as you get more and more into it, it's flaws start to become more and more obvious.

First off, there are cheaters in the game and there is no anti-cheat system in place outside of a reporting system. Reporting systems can be abused and I have heard other people claim, that the large clans are mass reporting people they don't like or agree with. I have no personal experience with this so I can't say if it is true or not, but most games that has clans and a reporting system seem to have this problem, so I guess it is reasonable to assume that the rumors are true.

There are cheaters that use auto-aim bots and hacks that increase their health, viewing distance, build speed, run speed and much more. I have my self seen many people flying above the ground while using a set of binoculars to scout. If you are far above the ground you can see more. There are so many ways to cheat this game, just go and make a quick search and see for yourself, what kinds of hacking software you can get for this game. You will see what I mean.

Another way of cheating is to buy a second copy of the game and make an alt account. This account is meant to be set to the opposing teams faction, so that you can move in their territory freely while sabotaging as much as possible. These people destroy vehicles and supplies, they basically grief the other players.

I have had a lot of connection issues. This is not a problem for me in any other game.

There are at certain times at the day a lot of queue time. When crossing from one border to another you are essentially going from one server to another. These servers are called regions. Each region has a max population and when that has been reached you must wait until someone leaves the region before you can enter. At peak times that wait time can be up to 20-30 minutes EVERY time you need to go into that region, which makes it unreasonable to run supplies to those regions.

I have clipped through the ground multiple time while trying climb a trench.

Often times when climbing rocks, you will find invisible walls that will glitch out you player character making him make a sort of weird dance. If he does that while the enemy is near you, you will die because you can't move or shoot. This has happened to me many times.

I don't know what the solution to the cheating problem is. I don't think it is a development issue. It is a player issue. However, the clipping, the queue times and the weird dancing glitch that happens when you climb rocks I feel like are issues that the developers should have fixed before a 1.0 release.

These are just some of my thoughts about the game. The game really is a lot of fun, but I am left with a bitter taste only because it could be so much better.\t\t\t\t\t
