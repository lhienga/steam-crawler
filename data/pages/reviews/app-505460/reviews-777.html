\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a mixed-bag. I spent 40 minutes in the training grounds before I was happy to try deploying to the frontline with some idea of what I was doing.

I then found that my inventory was gone when I arrived at the front, and there was no ammunition for primary weapons. We got steamrolled by a group of players that obviously didn't have a shortage of weaponry.

The same thing happened again and again, either having no ammunition, or the only weaponry my side had were some rifles. Trying to take ground was difficult because rifles seemed useless against foxholes.

A lot of the game is dull, crafting or running around, or just waiting around other players wondering if something's going to happen. The game often feels unbalanced, and the friendly-fire leads to frequent teamkilling.

But then there are moments when you're among a group of players, led by an officer that's clearly got experience and passion, and you feel involved in something interesting.

I wish the weaponry was less clunky, I wish it was more intuitive, and had shorter respawn times/travel distances, and a higher framerate. I'd love to see this game in first person view.

As it is with the top-down view and large map it's rarely exciting, but it can be engaging. It just feels like a free web-browser game. At the end of the day I'd much rather play a game more skill-based (and one that challenges my graphics card).\t\t\t\t\t
