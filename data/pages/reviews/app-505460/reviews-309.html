\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust because I keep hearing many people say that the community is toxic and how this is causing people to not even give it a chance, I have decided write up a review.

The first thing I want to say is that in my near 60 hours of playing, I have not encountered such a situation where a clan will attempt to ban someone whatsoever. The community from my experience are helpful and I have not yet met any player who would harrass or otherwise grief anyone for the lolz.

But admittedly, this might also differ based on the shard (server) you're in. They have what are essentially different personalities. The one I mainly play on is shard-1 (Alpha server). This shard highly values tactical gameplay while I believe shard-2 (Baker) is much more casual, though I could be wrong as I have only ever played on 2 once. server Charlie is brand new, so I don't know much about it.

My experience might also be different from others due to how I immediately joined a clan after my second session (shameless SOM clan plug here). Playing solo is not the Foxhole experience so to speak. It's a game that centers around cooperation and working together to win the war. So I would highly recommend joining one.

You can either find one through clan advertisments at bases or going to the Foxhole Discord server, where there is a recruitment channel dedicated for clan advertisements. From that point your clan can help you train and find the role you want to play as, whether it be tank driver, artilleryman, or another grunt capturing the base that your support groups have oh so graciously softened up.

But just because it is vitally important to know this: YOU WORK AS A TEAM, not the other way around. If you think you can take on the enemy by yourself, your time playing won't be particularly enjoyable.

But to quickly just talk about gameplay, I personally think it's fun, though it acts more as a meat grinder for the run of the mill infantry. If you drive tanks it's even encouraged that you stay in it so as to prevent the enemy from capturing it. So it very much is a tactical game.

But yeah, I think it's a fun game and while I won't say I know every player is good person and that griefers are nonexistent, I have not seen much of any throughout my playtime and the players I have encountered don't engage in that or have harrassed players. So while I think the toxic community does exist, it is overblown and you likely won't have issues from these types of people. I also highly recommend joining a clan as I personally think that if those people are around they will likely be more hesitant dealing with someone who is a part of one for the whole "mess with one of us yadda yadda" reason. Oh, and joining a clan essentially a ticket for fun in this game.

Alright that's my spiel, go buy it or decide not to. It's neither my money nor my choice after all.\t\t\t\t\t
