\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: This was originally a negative review posted 15 Nov, 2022 during the community review bombing to show our discontent towards the developers. While my criticisms are very much still valid, these are the sort of problems you only encounter after playing for hundreds of hours. If you are on the fence about the game, I wholeheartedly encourage you to pick it up. You will not regret it.



Foxhole is not in a good place right now.

1) Wars last too long.
As of writing, war 100 is wrapping up with a length of roughly 55 days, making it the longest war by a wide margin. The previous record was from War 95 taking 42 days, which was so long it was referred to as the "forever war" at the time. The next in line where 36 days and 35 days respectively. In my opinion, wars should never take more than 30 days, as any longer just causes immense amounts of burnout on both sides.

2) Logi and Facilities suck.
The logi system, which has always sucked, was meant to be fixed by facilities. However, they do nothing to address the array of problems that logi has, and instead introduced a whole slew of new problems.
- They require massive amounts of materials to construct.
- They requires massive amounts of upkeep just to be maintained.
- Automation is practically non-existent, meaning everything needs to be done through manual labour.
- The massive amounts of hours that need to go into running a facility makes it impossible for solo logi and small clans to achieve, as well as still being a massive burden to all but the biggest clans.
- They are really hard to defend, requiring either a massive base to be constructed and maintained, or hoping no partisan comes near your facility.
- They get significantly more complex if you want to build the late game stuff, exacerbating the previous issues.
- All of this is extremely tedious and unfun.
- The best items are locked behind facilities, meaning those who don't want to deal with this are screwed.
The fact that the dev team saw the massive outcry from the L.O.G.I Union, and decided that this was an acceptable solution is a very sad joke.

3) Complete lack of moderation.
This isn't a situation of the devs not moderating enough, but rather the devs refusing to moderate at all. They promote a system of self moderation by the community as a way of not having to pay for moderators themselves, with has just left alts and toxic players getting off with almost anything scot-free. This then leads to a situation where systems like the report player button cannot be significantly changed despite how easily abuseable they are by clans because its the only form of moderation that is available. Better a broken tool than none at all.

4) Complete lack of developer communication.
A signature move of siege camp is a refusal to properly engage with the community. They can have a literal strike by a large section of the community and they won't even give a "we are working on it", let alone seriously engage with the issues presented. The only time they will communicate is when they push a massive update, before going back into hibernation for half a year. Its a big game, they can afford to hire a community manager.

If you haven't touched this game before, you may think its weird I'm writing this after getting 700+ hours out of it, with many other reviews coming from other players with hundreds or thousands of hours, but that's because the more you play this game, the more you are exposed to the deep seeded problems imbedded in this game. Out of all the games I've played, this is the only one where it feels like its actively getting worse with no real hope of change without a significant shakeup at siege camp, which seems improbable at best. I feel powerless to meaningfully change the game in a positive direction, or to address the array of problems not outlined in this post, so the only real thing I can do is post this, and hope the devs take note.

- A Colonial Second Lieutenant\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a unique blend of an MMO-Style game. I mainly play Logistics, which most people find unfun, but I do occasionally join the frontlines to fight.

TLDR: Very good, some small things I'd love to see improved. 8/10 Game

Logistics has a couple of layers, being easy for the most needed tasks while being complex for those that want to build giant supply chains coordinating with others. It's where I spend most of my time (solo) and I feel great when I deliver supplies to the frontline.

Combat is tricky, as the aiming system is unique and I haven't grasped it fully. It's also deadly, it doesn't take much for your character to die. Grenades, gas, bombs, tank blasts, and rockets usually kill you. Bullets from small arms will sometimes just wound you so a medic can heal you up. Medics are always in short supply though, so those that like to be more supporting in combat can be fulfilled with the medic role.

It's not perfect. I wish there was more to do on the Regiment (equivalent of clans/guilds) side of things, as the game is geared towards forcing the playbase to work together to win. With that in mind, bunker-bases are the closest to some way to have a face as a regiment but you can't deploy straight to any player made structures.

Loading and unloading of logistics takes some time to get use to, as it's sometimes weird. It takes time to learn what items can be packaged, where they can be packaged at, how to transport things, and overall learning how to do anything logistically that doesn't involve hitting material piles and loading them into trucks. Some guides in-game or some playable demo to help ease new players into logistics would do wonders to help boost new players into logistics.

The new player experience could be worked on to ease a player in, as most will be lost unless they watch some Youtube video or have a friend walk them through how to play, both for logistics and for frontline.

I look forward to see where this game goes! Their early-access to launch was an amazing transition and I can't wait to see how they improve/add to the game.\t\t\t\t\t
