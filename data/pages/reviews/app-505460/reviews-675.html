\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFew things about this game. I really like the idea of the game, but the execution of the idea is uninteresting at best. When playing this game you are given a few options on what you want to do.

Firstly, you have logistics which consists of mindlessly hitting a hammer against an item for 5 minutes driving back to a building where you turn it into more useful materials. Once you have done this you have to drive to give materials to the front line. For me this was a 5 minute drive each way. This leads to the tasks of getting equipment in this game to just feel like you are working a job or doing an extra chore.

Next up you have people who are shooting guns at people on the front lines. This is by far the most interesting of this game. However, the aiming in the game feels clunky and is not very fun to work with.

The last part I played was a medic on the front line. Which consists of just walking around in trenches until you find someone who is injured and you click on them. Playing as a medic in this game is just remedial.

The problem with this game isn't the concept it is that every part of this game that I have played in uninteresting or isn't fun to deal with. Playing a game shouldn't feel like you are working a job. Overall if it played any better I might play it once a month or so, but I simply do not enjoy playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a really good time to jump into Foxhole, population seems to have hit its all time peak and every front seems to be bustling with people. It should be noted that there are some issues with the game, but mind you it is early access. If you're like me you're probably looking for reasons not to buy the game, so I will list the negatives first, but keep in mind I recommend the game.

Bugs exist, but they don't really interfere with the experience, there are ongoing balancing issues as new content is added to the game especially because the two factions have now been made asymmetric in the weapons they use and vehicles they field, and the largest issue (or at least what I think is the largest) has to be how boring filling the logistics role can be, but you honestly never have to play logi. Some people love it, but as of current it is very menial and not at all exciting, although there is some satisfaction knowing the scrap you mined became the guns at the front and the shells that obliterate the enemy.

I suppose the community should be mentioned as it is very important that you communicate with others and it is guaranteed that you will be spoken to. Overall it is a positive community and there are tons of people to meet, but there will always be people telling others what to do, which can be aggravating when you are new. As a new player you may make mistakes, sometimes big ones and you have to prepare for experienced player to yell at you or kill you when you mess up by parking your truck sideways somehow blocking the entire road on a bridge where the front is. That's a bit extreme, but you can end up getting in the way or being wasteful by mistake. DISCLAIMER: I have only played the Colonial faction, and from what I understand there can be differences in the two faction's communities. Also stay off reddit, they're a bunch of apes

Despite the negatives you are bound to have fun once you learn how the mechanics work. They aren't very complex, but there also isn't really a tutorial, so you gotta ask others and learn yourself. Once you get invested in a war its hard not to play a dozen hours or actual days, but if you don't have the time you can go to an active front and still have a bunch of fun defending or pushing for just an hour or two. I would totally recommend it, especially if you have a lot of time.\t\t\t\t\t
