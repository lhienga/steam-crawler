\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTake this review with a grain of salt, as I have about 5 hours of gameplay in update 25 (the newest as of this date.) Anything I say may have been changed already, just bear in mind. I love the idea of the game, in fact I heard about it and just 'bout jumped out of my seat to go play it. From my experience (however limited in this title) tech grinding, takes hours and hours of just sitting there beating at broken down tanks with a sledgehammer just to get a handful of "tech parts" aka spark plugs. These are what allows the game to progress, otherwise you'll all just continue fighting with a rifle and a few other things here and there, but no tanks, no boats, no artillery, HMG, LMG, HMG gun defensive system, nothing. What me and my friend used to do was to spend about and hours and a half, which in my opinion was way to damned long, and in that time we would build 4 of the twin 14.5mm two man gun systems. We usually ran with five or more people per gun system, two people manning the gun, a dedicated spotter that would go in really light, just binos and an m1 carbine. And at least two men per gun that are bogged down with ammo. Occasionally we would also pair this with small mortar crews and end up running four guns, and 8+ mortars. Great times, but the setup, and the research still took way to long, and for that it's getting a thumbs down. I'll change that when it's not infuriatingly long.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't bother with this game. The game itself is a lot of fun, well designed and on the road to success. However because so much of the game's experience depends on the players around you, this is NOT A FUN GAME AT ALL. The clans/groups are incredibly elitist and will cut out players simply because of their inexperience. Groups are super cliquey and you'd be pretty lucky to be able to do ANYTHING with an established group.

I spent the better part of 2 months trying to get into groups on both sides of the war. The entire time it felt effectively like I was playing by myself. They only spoke to one another, didn't respond to anything I said, and more than anything: made the entire experience EXTREMELY unfun.

I really like Foxhole but I can't in good conscience suggest anyone buy it simply because of the lack of matchmaking tools that are in the game combined with how cliquey/elitist the groups can be. Unless you have an already sizable group of friends to play this with, stay the hell away from it.\t\t\t\t\t
