\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo keep this pending wall of text from becoming one, this "game" mostly involves people wasting precious hours of their lives gathering vital materials of many kinds, all for them to be lost in the least fun and most irritating ways. Without more people taking on such boring and clearly not meant to be engaging tasks (even Runescape has always done this type of gameplay better), there will ultimately be zero means to counter what such literal hours will bring out - things like artillery pieces and tanks, as well as fortifications.
So, in theory, the combat might be fun because you can get your hands on such things eventually, right? Surely there's a payoff to all of that invested time and effort. Unfortunately, if you're not the one steamrolling the enemy, they're deleting your literal hours of work in seconds, even if the skill and supply train to do these activities is rather uncommon - because on all levels, the combat is incredibly janky and requires fighting the game itself, with concepts like corner shooting and three dimensional environments including multiple floors on a top down shooter - elements rife for getting killed without sane means of retaliation. To put this in perspective, the most commonly accepted means of preparing for infantry combat is taking a mere rifle with only one clip, because you'll die before you can even reload.
So if it takes hours of time to get to the front with things like rifles and other required war materials (supplies needed to actually spawn at said front, or the various kinds of highly expensive explosives needed to engage previously mentioned armor or artillery - or gas masks and their ammunition to defend against actual warcrimes), and said equipment is for all intents and purposes intentionally hard to acquire and use, with multiple years of patches and updates intended to drag this process on longer and more grindy and war material intensive. Clearly, it can't possibly get any worse, and is the kind of war simulation a niche group of people desire.
At least, that's what one COULD think, but then you have to see just how the in game environment and greater community is handled, and then there, in its Reddit infused glory, ANY REASONABLE SOUL can see just how hopeless it is to even interact with anyone in a potentially constructive way and infuse the game and its culture with any sort of sanity in tactics beyond simple World War One trench warfare or throwing bodies into entrenched emplacements. Its to the point where even basic communication beyond any preset functions the game has can potentially get you banned from the game. I would not be surprised if this negative review gets me banned from the game.

The worst part about all of this is that this game was truly better in previous versions, with less blatant timesinks and more ability to engage in the greater community in game, with an all chat feature reasonable people could have used to taunt and insult the enemy team like normal human souls playing a videogame - so that logistics based actions took minutes, not hours, and so therefore dying in janky top down combat was far less irritating. There clearly is a good game somewhere IN all of this, but this development team, plainly stating that any proper criticism that any normal person would state is bannable, is clearly aiming to not make a good game or even a good experience from the game. There is definitely A community of this game, but don't expect it to be a magic bullet to ignoring the game's flaws and having a good time in spite of them.

tl;dr Foxhole is and strictly always will be a terrible game that used to be better when it had both a smaller scale and far less blatant timesinks intentionally forced into its gameplay to intentionally remove any fun the game has. While it technically has potential to be a good game, with at least one rather clear and proven path to fun and good gameplay people can talk about, it will instead be developed in ways that only people with severe masochistic tendencies will enjoy, in what seems to be a clearly unethical social experiment. You will probably manage to experience everything novel about the game if you DO attempt to try it in about the two hour refund window, even if that might be a little tight due to the relative quality of the tutorial, and how long it takes to even get started with playing the game.

Seriously, do yourself a favor and EXPLICITLY DO NOT PLAY THIS GAME. If you want a game in the "blue vs green team" genre, play what is now called Build and Shoot instead. You'll thank yourself later.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, I recommend this game, this game has a very broad range of roles and play styles that can appeal to many players.
In a nutshell, this is game is a perfect blend of GTA, Truck Simulator and Factorio all in the visual art style of the first World War.
Those interested in action, will be at the front lines were all the obvious fighting is taking place.
Here you will find you trigger happy lads and lasses, but also the combat medic’s keeping the troops a life and combat engineers building trenches and battlement positions to either hold the line or push forward.
For the slightly more peaceful roles that take place away from the front lines gathering resources and setting up production line to fuel the war machine and front line with whatever is needs to win this war. All what is being gathered, constructed and build here, is to benefit the war machine and keep the fighting troops fueled with guns, ammo and a supply of troops (aka shirts, a respawning mechanic)

In between you have you logistical players or truck simulator, hauling foods from where it’s produced to where it is needed at the front lines and their is more too it than just hauling freight back and forth.

During Early Access I didn't recommend the game, this wasn't due to the game being early access and your typical bugs and performance issues.
The short and simple version of it, those who had the screen advantage, could spot their opponents sooner and act before the opponent could react. At the front lines where all the actions were, that made the difference between a fair fight and both players having equal reaction time. Or the person with the advantage having additional time to take actions.

Which in my book reads 'exploiting' which breaks the game balance. Some attempts were made to fix this, but through custom resolutions, one could still achieve that advantage. This has been fixed now and I’m pleased to say, I’m enjoying the front lines once more.
