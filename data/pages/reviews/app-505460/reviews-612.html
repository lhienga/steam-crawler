\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic game with tons on offer for many different styles of play. A great community of players. Outstanding work.

Do not buy.

Disconnects and network hangs can happen every few minutes. Connections are lost while deploying from the starting region and the game client resets. Deployment attempts are rejected due to lost server connections. Your primary enemy is the connectivity. The opposing team is only a secondary enemy.

The state of the art for connection reliability in these multiplayer games is far, far beyond what the Foxhole team is offering. The connection reliability problems seem to be years old. Given that, I recommend buying only when it becomes clear the developer can deliver baseline connection reliability. Otherwise you risk entering an abusive, love/hate addiction with a great game.

Other than connectivity, the game feels very well coded. Other multiplayer games with more maturity, and even some long past early access, feel far "glitchier". This gives me high hopes that in the unlikely event the network and/or operations folks can't get their act together, the rest of the Foxhole team is savvy enough to replace them with those who can.

The in-game community deserves a special mention. It's awesome. It has the usual suspects: the loudly immature, the griefer, the elitist jerk, and so on. But their frequency is far lower than any other multiplayer game I've experienced. They're easy to leave behind during play. I've found the community welcoming of new players, forgiving of dumb mistakes, patiently answering dumb questions mid-fire fight. I've found clans happy to fight alongside solo players, groups of solo players organically organizing around goals without clans, and more.

In terms of play, the only major drawback is the new player experience. Holy cow is that a miss. It's as if the person in charge of that design had a grudge against the rest of the team. Had a strong desire to the see them all fail. If you do buy, know there are awesome players organizing their own very helpful, fun, and often front line training sessions for new players. After you find them, which can take some effort, join them!

Other weakish areas include the feedback system for aiming, contextual information presentation (how am I contributing to the war, how is the war going, which units are fighting where, and other things a soldier in a war tends to have a rough awareness of), the UI interaction design, e.g., quickly finding items in long and seemingly unordered lists of unlabeled icons, reading the names of players in motion. Of those, none stand out as areas where the developers have doubled-down on bad designs and decisions. They all feel like areas where a weak solution was put forward as a way to discover the best solution.

There's evidence for optimism around all weak areas. Even connection reliability.


Net Notes:

Foxhole's disconnects tend to happen instantly and without warning. They result in one of three outcomes. You can instantly reconnect and be returned to where you were. You can instantly reconnect, be returned to the starting area, redeploy, and then be returned to where you were. You can instantly reconnect, be returned to the starting area, redeploy and find yourself starting fresh as if you had just launched the game. I've not found any hint as to what causes which outcome.

Foxhole's network hangs seem to last from about three to thirty seconds. During this time you are frozen on the server, but your local game looks as if you are still playing. You can continue using the in-game voice comms. Sound effects and animations all enter a continuous loop, which is brutal during a loud firefight. If you, miraculously, do not die while hanging, usually you can carry on playing. But these hangs do sometimes end in disconnects.

Foxholes servers, each hosting a specific area of the map, seem to go offline for hours at a stretch. While offline, information is not provided by the Foxhole team warning players not to attempt to connect, what is wrong, or when to expect regions to return online. As you might imagine, in a game where strategy is important, having regions stop working for even brief periods, never mind hours, is at best frustrating. Insult is added to injury by the the client poorly handling server rejections, e.g., resetting on failed travel attempts and requiring players return anew through the starter area, losing queue positions in hours-long waiting queues, etc.

Balance Notes:

You'll read complaints about the two sides being unbalanced. About the Foxhole team meddling mid-war. I would take those with a barrel of salt. The barrels the authors of those complaints left you should do nicely.

First, I feel there is some imbalance but its likely all or nearly all due to the shenanigans developers have to pull during early access to gather the data to truly understand how to balance a game long-term. Second, I wish it were intentionally unbalanced! Perfectly balanced, perfectly "fair" is just plain boring. War (gaming) is interesting because it is unfair.\t\t\t\t\t
