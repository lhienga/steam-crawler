\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore i start. I like to adress that this game is really really fun to play for a long period of time. But after getting some sessions. you slowly going to see the same patern in the game mechanics and you have to use the experiences you get from previous wars and put them in good use in the next up coming battles. that being said let be point out the Pros and the Cons of the game.

PROS

- It's a very simple looking game, but with a huge in-dept cooperation mechanic. (Simple put. It's like an ARMA game.)
- Very huge maps with it's own uniqe feature in maps. (one map has a Swamp, the other are islands and some have huge options in flanking.)

- Interesting way of telling how old Warfare during WorldWar was carried out. (from Logistics to Frontlines. Every department has to help each other out in order to achieve victory.)

- Price. For what is delivers it is worth it for just 20$ as a just gonna help a team out in the weekends or just every day. (and with the SteamSales the prices are even more worth while!)

CONS

- Huge flaws in teamwork can quickly be a bad game expierence. (players that start from the beginning instantly go to the Frontlines without proper training on what to do and will get very frustrated when the enemies can overwelm them in better logistics arms.)

- Un-even maps, can make some area's of the map unfair. (even tho the Devs wanted this way, i do see that some of those maps form a huge issue in order to make an orginized assault possible)

- Cheating. (Yes... even in this game cheating happends, and while the Devs are aware of this, they have not found a perfect
solution to find and stop the hacking toolkit. They are thinking about an Anti-cheat system in the near future.)

- The Disconnect between Logistics and Frontline. (Logistics are the foundation of the war. They deliver Weapons, Vehicles, Building Materials and more to the frontlines. most of the time a lot of newcomers will ignore this and go straight to the front. and that leaves more empty hands in order to get supplies to the Front.)


Well then. I hope if someone is reading this. it will give them a better understanding of the sistuation of the game and if it is worth you're try. My personal opion is. Yes. In the in between moments i had a lot of fun with playing with Random players that just charge towards the enemy while also falling off a cliff and instantly explodes on impact.... \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing this game since 2018. Suffice to say, with almost 4,000 hours, I've got a fair amount of experience with it. I paid a lot less than $30 for it and feel like I've ripped off the developers with how much time I've spent on it. I've no clue how they aren't bankrupt, because most games of this format are subscription services for a reason.

HOWEVER, I can't in my right mind recommend this game to any of my friends, because while the concept is unique, 85% of the game-play is holding W or LMB.

Back in 2018, you could hop on, grab a tank or some artillery shells, and be on the front line in 15 minutes. If there were no tanks, you did about 15 minutes of work, and came back 12-24 hours later to collect your tank, and be on the front line in 15 minutes. If there were no artillery shells, you could make artillery shells for about 30 minutes, and be on the front line in another 15. Logistics was a factor, but it was an interesting and unique step no other game had.

Fast forward to 2023, and none of that is even remotely possible. There are very few additional steps, but timers upon timers. You want a tank? Wait 6 hours for a component node to re-spawn, mine for a couple hours, not get enough because there's not actually enough to go around, repeat, refine the components for another 2-3 hours, build the tank, and then after spending another 30-40 minutes loading and getting the equipment you need, you spend another 30 driving to the front line where you're likely to get one or two shot killed by someone who spent more time slaving away than you did.

I can't lie to you and say that there isn't plenty of good fun to be had. Once you get over all of that logistics the combat can be fairly enjoyable. But unless you're willing to commit 6-8 hours a day on this game with at least 3 other people over the course of a month, all you're going to be is fodder for the people who are.

I would stay away from this one until the fun toys are actually accessible. As is, it fosters an incredibly toxic environment where players compete with their own team more than the enemy. Some because they want to win that badly and feel the need to control everything, others because they want the big toys and don't want to share. Without a friend group big enough to compete with 15-20 people for resources, you're never going to get anywhere near half the content in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
