Original review.
Had a little bit of fun but overall felt like the game is too much of a chore.

You wanna run logistics to win the war? In order to get resources, you have to farm mats for them and this is really boring with no personal satisfaction whatsoever. You just hold down M1 button to farm or go drive around the map going to auto-miners.

Maybe you'd like to be a driver? But your vehicle gets disabled after a few bullets and your supply may end up going into enemy hands after that. Worst still, griefers will come and steal your vehicle full of supplies meant for your home base and rid of your inventory or drive it down to the enemy camp for them to steal. Many times, these griefers are from your own team.

Good luck getting people to vote-kick the griefers if you're a nobody. Much less luck if you are without a clan and don't have any group pull. If you're not in a clan, you belong to a group of nobodies and bad eggs from big clans/groups have pretty much their way to either bully you in game by screaming vulgarities or treating you poorly.

Maybe you want to be a builder? It might be fun to build walls and establish defences around your base wont it? Yes it would, BUT in game all you really do is laydown a blue print and hold your M1 button between 50-100 times and painfully construct your walls or foxholes with a hammer. Repeat that 3-4 more times to upgrade their tier.

Developers need to sieve out these poor game design choices and make them more engaging and worthwhile for all parties. Ass of now, being a builder/logistics/farmer/driver is really too much of a chore.

I honestly believe the potential for the most fun and strategy in this game belongs to being in the frontline. You get to scream at logistics for resources, you can plan your attack and flanking options, you even get to go for missions to steal enemy tanks and whatnot. That is what makes a fun game, not pressing M1 button for hours simply to get resources.

Do yourself a favor and join the frontline. Forget about being a logistics, especially so if you're alone or without friends or a clan. Griefers will ruin your gameplay and you'll have no place to address your grievances because you're a nobody. As of now, I wouldn't recommend this game till devs make the game better for all parties involved, and make this less of a chore when it's supposed to be a game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredibly dense, but can be very fun for those that are willing to get past the learning curve and have the time to invest into making a dent in any frontline.

You are just a cog in the machine; alone you do very little, and just have to accept that. You need to work with your team and use a lot of coordination to successfully win any battles. That really appealed to me and I enjoyed the game for the first few hundred hours.

It's so satisfying to put hours into preparing and organizing the supplies and people for a massive assault into enemy territory. So exciting to finally bust through the first line of defenses after hours and gain a little tiny foothold, that adrenaline slowly fills the brain as you start to wake up and put your brain to trying to figure out how to make sure all that time pushing wasn't in vain.

I've put over 1,000 hours into this game and I don't recommend it.
The reason I don't recommend this game is because half of the game (logistics) has been neglected in favor of the other half (combat), and both halves suffer as a result.

Most of my hours in the game were spent in logistics. One of the main draws of this game is that everything is player-made; the guns, the ammo, the vehicles, the fuel, the defenses. You can't shoot the enemy unless you or someone else makes that equipment for you. Every bunker you blow up is hours of another players labor gone. This logistics, as the developers have said, is kind of the "big idea" of the game. Over the course of the past few years I've played they've added more and more vehicles and guns and buildings, overhauled the logistics, building, and combat systems several times.

1. They've added a few tools or changes here and there to make things easier to do logistics like the harvester and the barge and the minimap. It says who stockpiles a base and what they submitted and that's nice that sometimes people who deliver things get a few commends.
But overall logistics has become more and more of a grind. Its taken longer and longer to produce the same amount of supplies which have gotten less and less valuable in the name of balance (like gas masks aren't as useful now because they also need filters to mean anything).

2. Balancing issues for combat have been offloaded to logistics by way of increasing respawn times for resources, upping the costs to build vehicles and manufacture equipment, making a new fuel type that's harder to get and making a bunch of vehicles require it. Buildings decay in more situations, making more materials go to waste. If anything in battle is too overpowered the solution is to either make it take longer for logistics to make it, or make it weaker, causing people to demand logistics make more of it. Rarely is a balancing solution to make an item easier for logi to make.

3. As the maps have gotten bigger, the war gets more epic, the delivery system has gotten more complex. One could load up a regular truck full of crates and drive straight to a front and drop it off like before, but the capacity of the trucks has not gotten any bigger or faster. It can take upwards of 40 minutes to drive from a main logistics hub to a front (plus the time it takes to make everything). The frontlines have gotten more demanding for resources. Before you know it, all the supplies you spent over an hour producing will be spent, or the situation of the front has changed and your supplies are made less useful.

To realistically keep a front supplied decently you now need an ever increasing team of people to work the various points of the logistics chain. Gotta have the people mining the resources, the people turning those resources into usable supplies, and the people delivering partway to the front with flatbeds carrying shipping containers, where a warehouse will have cranes necessary to offload the containers (the crane/container system is really cool imo), at which point a regular truck could drive a more reasonable distance to the actual frontline.

This delivery system is not spelled out to people and a potential solution could lie somewhere in educating people on how to do logistics properly, but the overall issue here still persists; it's not fun.

4. There's nothing to keep you entertained between the mundane clicking and tapping of the WASD keys except for conversation with other people (not so easy to find on logistics, as most people are just watching youtube or something in the background). You need full focus at all times, as there is no auto-drive. You can't set waypoints or draw a course for yourself, and auto-clickers for mining are "frowned upon" by the developers. Multi-tasking does not exist for logistics. If you want to plan ahead you'll have to pause what you're doing to look at inventories or the map. The minimap hardly helps when the map is so big, you have to open up the big map anyways to see where to turn on the upcoming fork, causing you to stop your truck anyways. If you hate yourself and keep playing you could potentially memorize the map, but you still have to check in case of partisans.

The best way to stop yourself from going crazy on logistics is to distract yourself. Watch a show or youtube or something while you absentmindedly "play" the game. The people on the front won't really talk to you because they're busy fighting and talking to each other in proxy chat. If the most fun way to play half the game is to *not play* then what kind of game is this?

5. It's different from other grindy games like survival/crafting games or mmos, because with those there's an actual payoff. There's a sense of satisfaction in seeing your hard work in terms of supplies in your base or stats on your character. With Foxhole logistics all your supplies just get poofed away into a stockpile. You don't get to see the impact of your hard work unless you immediately quit logistics after you deliver something (if you're even the one delivering the stuff). The recognition for doing logistics only happens if you deliver the supplies (you get a few commends and that's it); the logistics system has required more and more people, and still hardly get any credit until that rare person in chat tells everyone to "commend X because they've been scrapping for hours" or something.

6. This system of logistics has created another cyclical issue of logi player shortages. You don't have enough people with the energy and willpower to work together on logistics so people just resign themselves to doing one task completely uncoordinated with others (people decide to just mute themselves and mine scrap/build vehicles/deliver stuff while they watch youtube), or even try to do the entire supply chain by themselves. Since it takes so long for any single person to do these tasks effectively, they get burnt out before long and quit before anyone else hops on to coordinate with them.

7. Also partisans. You also have to pay attention to partisans and watch out for these people when you're delivering supplies, to ensure hours of work aren't thrown away to some dipsh*t with an SMG sitting in a bush at a border. It takes a fair amount of manpower and resources to make all the roads covered by watchtowers, and especially a lot more to put actual defenses down; it takes a lot more resources per stretch of road versus the amount of prep a few partisans need to tear down the defenses and make that road unusable for a good amount of time.

This is all on top of partisans camping the border, where defenses can't be placed. If there's an opening for the enemy to walk to a border road, one person could potentially draw several fighters from the front, and often after they've ruined at least one truck full of supplies and a players night.

------------------------------------------------
TL;DR the "point" of the game has been made unfun in favor of making the more conventional part of the gameplay more balanced.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t TL;DR
If you like strategic games and enjoy communicating with teams, get this game.

+Fun top down game
+Rewarding game play
+Generally positive players/communication
+Easy to make friends
+Roleplaying?
+Low requirements to play
+Graphics are good
+Choose your dynamic role
+Developers seem to listen

-Confusing at beginning
-Weird lag occasionally
-Griefers occasionally
-Hard to play by yourself

The Good
This game will give you plenty to learn. There are mutliple things to do and no real specific way to do them. I generally don't like the term sandbox since that usually means the developers give you a large map and tell you to have at it without a clear objective. This game gives you a very simple objective. Destroy your enemies. It also give you a variety of tools to learn and quite the player base to get it done. Honestly, I can see myself spending many more hours roleplaying my character and working with my teams to get small tasks done. Even as a solo player it can be rewarding to just run logistics to the front lines, give people rides, or help build the base. It requires good communication and people seem very willing to healp.

The Bad
This game is super difficult to learn at first. They toss you in a "training grounds" with little directions. They give you small signs to read to uderstand the controls of the game but its really just not enough. Luckily they do link you to some videos but the online videos still don't give you a real understanding of what to do as well as it can be. Also seeing some outdated videos makes it a little difficult to understand some of the new content.

Overall, for the price and the amount of gametime i have already put into it and how much I know i will continue to put into it. I highly recommend this game to players who like the strategy games and enjoy communicating with teamates.\t\t\t\t\t
