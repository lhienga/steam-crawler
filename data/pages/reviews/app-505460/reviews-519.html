\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMen of War/Company of Heroes/Planetside 2 hybrid without pay to win (or any pay mechanics besides buying the game). Every single fortification, vehicle, and weapon you see in the game is made by players.

Real-time persistent war that can concurrently go on for a couple of real-life weeks to a month. Wars regularly end with millions of casualties on both sides.

Logistics is the most major feature in this game. Besides basic infantry logistics that entail guns, ammo, fuel, medic supplies, garrison supplies, and defense supplies, and shirts (Spawn points) there is artillery, water, tank, and truck logistics.

Some players spend hundreds to thousands of hours JUST making artillery shells, establishing fuel nodes, scrapping, and tech running (Getting special materials to structures called Engineering Centers that can give the entire player faction another tech level).

Entire clans of dozens to a hundred people are dedicated to each of these single tasks including mass producing hard-to-produce vehicles like tanks. A lot solo mass produce these things its insane.

In an elongated war you will see an overstock of these materials and vehicles because as you are fighting, sleeping, and eating, people are scrapping, producing, and constructing.

Constructing is an entire third major job to logistics and fighting. People can make the Maginot Line look like a French Bakery in comparison to the insane fortifications that ring the ENTIRE front to the rear.

And the war goes on as this game has players in ALL timezones, from the Americas, Europe, to Asia.


EVERY SINGLE PIECE OF HARDWARE IS MADE BY THE PLAYERS. From the tanks, trucks, mobile cranes, and boats, to simple rifles and bandages.

For the BIG pieces of artillery you have to use actual triangulation and there is an artillery calculator for this game on the web. The largest artillery pieces can take DAYS to build even with major clan help.

Every single hexagon on the larger map is in itself its own large map, and yes, with real logistics comes real players trying to stop these logistics.

The battle is fought over the entire map. With partisans on both sides attempting to cut the other logistics off with counter partisans trying to fight them off.

Even if an entire map is filled with friendly players, if you fail to stop enemy partisans cutting off logistics YOU WILL LOSE EVERY TIME. Entire regions are lost over just a few dozen partisans cutting supplies off.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is intense, fighting on the frontlines is heavy and every person you see has an effect on the war. The war is well designed by the devs, the teams are evenly balanced with the amount of new people who grab a rifle and head off to war, and the pros who are either running logistics from the back of the map, keeping the war going, or the pros at the front in large squads taking charge of defence and offence, making a breakthrough into enemy lines for their team.

Logistics is a much slower part of the game but just as important, without it the frontlines wouldn't be supplied and the war would be over in a couple of hours. Trucks and boats with equipment and cargo for the war effort can be seen travelling around everywhere and if you're new to it there's always someone willing to show you the ropes and give you a hand.

Speaking of, the player base. This community is amazing, from random interactions loading gear up at a base, all the logi players who crash into each other and quickly apologise and every last bit of conversation on the frontline, holding morale up, providing support to comrades and most importantly, calling the other side cowards.

Long story short, great game, takes some time to get into, worth the effort\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is just about one of a kind, so if it sounds like something you might be interested in then you should try it- you are not going to find anything else like it. It is perhaps slightly similar to the no longer existing Planetside 1 in terms of being a strategic war, but obviously the combat system is completely different and much less twitchy, and there is more emphasis on logistics/manufacturing and player built structures. There is also a specific technological progression during the course of a war, which resets once a side won

It can be an incredible time-sink if you want/let it to be, but it is possible to enjoy it more casually, particularly if you jump into a squad where probably someone already laid the groundwork for whatever they are doing and knows what's going on.

Be warned, while many roles are possible to do solo or without any deeper level of coordination, for the game to really shine you should join a Regiment (Guild) or an open squad, or at least listen to the players near you.
The game also has quite a learning curve, another reason why having seasoned players to talk to will be beneficial

The game is still actively being developed. New stuff keeps getting added and large changes and overhauls are likely to occur - for example the entire logistic layout of the strategic map has been overhauled just a week ago\t\t\t\t\t
