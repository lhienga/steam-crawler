\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun game. I picked colonials as my faction, and got the basics down. I'd had already seen gameplay and tutorials before, so I was able to get into the gameplay of it fairly quickly.

Spawned in Lockheed, which was the front line at the time, and around 15 other guys were holding the major bridge connecting us to the wardens. We held for around an hour and a half before they pushed us back with a tank driving through the bridge. We continued to hold them back under constant artillery and machine gun fire, before 3 tanks had flanked us and had destroyed the main base in the town. We then spent another hour holding what was left of the city, before the wardens finally pushed us back from the town. I had managed to escape to Old Jack Tar where I informed our allies that Lockheed had capitulated. I returned to lockheed later attempting to pick off whatever wardens I could, before I was shot and taken prisoner by them. I was interrogated by them but never gave any secrets. They then drove over me with a C.A.T.
The amazing part is through the hundreds of dead on both sides, that specific town, the struggle we had against them, and they against us, was only a tiny blip on the entire map. It was a small segment of a region, in which the map has quite a few regions. The fact that we only contributed a small amount to the entire war, was very awe inspiring.

It really felt like you were fighting for a cause, and then showed either how small, or large you actually contributed. It just shows that everyone counts, but teamwork is the most effective way to win a war. A soldier placed behind supply lines cuts off vital ammunition needed to supply machine guns, soldiers, and artillery pieces. The logistic players handling getting supplies to everyone, and at the same time gathering resources, so the Frontline players can either hold, or push. And of course, the partisans, the ones wrecking supply lines and cutting the head off the snake. It felt like a living world, where everyone had to pull their own weight.

Most fun I've had in a very long time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Foxhole is fun, and unforgivingly brutal, there will be three hour back and forths over a piece of dirt, there will be people getting frustrated, grime, and blood. But if you can manage to power through, it's a unique game with a far grander scale than it lets on.

We had been fighting for a bridge for two hours, each push, each body, each rifle crack was a reminder that we had made both 0 and a lot of progress. The bridge was destroyed when I got in, but after the insistence of our Mortar Truck, we repaired it, only for them to die immediately afterwards.
So we kept up the assault, we even managed to push inward quite a bit, but we were dogged by logistical issues, too may dead, too few shirts. We pulled back and were about to fall back entirely, but then we realized something.

Tanks.

Logistics had delivered a few tanks, about five to our location, none of them were manned and the bridge could only support one or two at most, across it, more AT than you could count. But the problem is, we couldn't afford for them to take it. So, with limited people and bloodied noses, we held in a tense night of gunfire and blind charges. All because one Logi Man managed to make a suicide run with limited supplies were we able to even hold.

But we held.

After three hours I needed to stop, I was tired and wanted something to drink. We had gained 0 ground, we had lost 0 ground. However, that's not "The Point" of Foxhole, the "Point" is that you're in a war, a terrible and bloody thing that involves hundreds of moving parts and people to try and win it.

That is Foxhole.
A grinding butcher shop of blood, sweat, and lead. The little moments is what makes the game, the moment of shouting for a medic as you pull out of a bloodbath, the moment when a man charges your position with a grenade and blind faith, the moment when you argue and bicker, trying to figure out how to win such a microscopic battle in the grand scheme of things. The people you meet and the men you die with, the jokes, the somber moments, the desperation, and even the fear of running out of supplies.

If you can't "get into it," if you can't deal with the fact that you won't be getting an instant reward or any award at all for that matter. If you can't deal with a learning curve both completely sheer and horribly basic. If you can't dear with the bantz of very tired people trying to larp.
If you can't be immersed in that bloody conflict over nothing. Then I wouldn't recommend you get Foxhole, at all, but if you can, then it's something special.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played this for 26 hours and its already in my top 10 games of all time. It's an MMO where everyone plays one unit in an RTS. Good community, most people are pretty friendly.

---------- Edit (Feb 24th 2023) (~1500 hours in game)------------
Foxhole is a fantastic concept for a game and there is a lot of fun to be had playing with friends. However the game appears to be designed for attracting new players with shiny toys rather than making a quality experience once the game is already purchased. The developers clearly either do not play the game themselves to have an understanding of the games problems, of which there are many, or they don't care. Why should they care? the devs make money when the game is purchased and do not have a subscription. It doesn't matter if a player plays for 5 hours or 5000 hours the devs make the same amount and this shows in the game's QoL updates, of which there are none or at least no meaningful ones.

One major issue is with the building mechanics. Pre 1.0 update the game's engine couldn't handle many structures in the world at once so the devs try to limit building by making maintaining buildings as time intensive and annoying as possible. If you have a build to upkeep its like having a second job just to keep it from undergoing decay. Trying to keep a build alive for 4+ weeks is hell. In the 1.0 update however all this changed and not for the better. The devs added facilities which are industrial bases players have to build to get access to many of the games vehicles, many of which were previously available for far less effort. Unfortunately the game engine still can't handle building in large amounts so maintenance hell is still in place but with twice as much stuff that needs to be maintained. This just ends up with laggy servers and miserable players trying to keep a base alive so their clan can have more than basic tanks. Facilities could have been a great feature if the game engine could handle building but it can't so maintenance hell continues as the servers desperately try to cull the number of built structures. The devs added a feature their engine STRUGGLES to support as the main feature of the 1.0 update. I can say with absolute certainty not one dev of this game ever built and maintained a base, facility or otherwise, for the duration of a foxhole war.

Oh also it has a lot of griefers / alts (players who buy and account to play on the other team and grief) who don't get banned.

---------- Edit: (March 20th) ----------
The devs have addressed some of the issues listed above. Changing review to positive.\t\t\t\t\t
