\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a very... love and hate relationship with this game, something that has put me off from writing about it for quite some time. But, I will set that aside and talk about it as a proper review, instead of describing more personal and subjective reasons. Curse you, Steam, for not having an informative review rating.

On one hand, the combat system is - for the most parts - enjoyable, and something that I haven't seen done personally at this scale of play since Running with Rifles. The skill curve is rather high, although recently some new systems and combat changes have lowered the ceiling somewhat at the cost of making individual plays less important / satisfying and team-based ones more so. Either way, triumphs are usually memorable and mean something, and you might be able to recollect moments of gameplay late in the future. Logistics and building might put some people off at first, but I know players who spend day after day in it and seem to enjoy it rather well, so it may be for you more than it is for me. I'm looking at you, Factorio types.

On the other hand, this is a very team-focused game: it requires you to find a sizeable group to work with and fast if you want to make a noticeable difference in the war. Back in the day, it was a lot more solo and I dare say new player friendly, but as systems get more complex and centralised a project that might've taken someone thirty minutes could take hours now. This has had the side-effect of rendering independent players non-competitive (I.E. Clans with solid logistic bases can make and more importantly man scores of flatbeds + harvesters and dominate the resource fields of entire sectors), and like other MMOs it's rather necessary to find a clan of some sorts once you get tired of the gameplay loop (Which you most likely will, given enough wars).

If you are a new player, systems can often be confusing and opaque to grasp. Common criticism is that veterans are often dismissive, and while true in some respects this is an overgeneralization based on limited subjective experiences. An example is the following: The in-game tutorial runs you through the basics, but is next to worthless in describing the team-based aspects of the game. The best way to go about it is to participate in Regiment (clan) run training exercises at your home island; It might take some fishing around to find a date, and if you're willing to put the time into it you can find one. Follow this up with guides that are available for reading or watching. Alternatively, find (or know) a good teacher and form a master apprentice relationship with them for a play session or two. I haven't seen this done in a while (and it's rare), but nowadays a lot of the people that I have had the honour of being the master of find themselves with far more game time and familiarity than I will probably ever have.

--

You may have gotten this by now, but Note to all of this: The game is not new player friendly and is progressively becoming less so, and may take some time and willingness to learn.

If you are an older player returning to the game, I suggest that you familiarise yourself with the changelogs quite fondly, as many things have changed. This is advice that even I have learned the hard way.

If I sound pessimistic, note that this is a positive review because I believe that players that are interested in it can find some hundred hours of fun with it. Once, I might've have said that you might have fun with it (for that long) even as a solo player, but now I am not so sure. If you are willing to dedicate time, familiarize yourself with the game and join a decent clan. If you are not, you may have to look somewhere else. I myself had not have the time, although I do find some time to mess around every update or two.

Cheers, and either way, have fun.\t\t\t\t\t
