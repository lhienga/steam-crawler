\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think that at its core, foxhole is a great game concept. The persistent world and player driven supply and logistics concepts are unique and fairly well executed. The combat mechanics are challenging and varied- the strategy of the game's operations is complex. I think that while these are all positive aspects, the game is still somewhat unbalanced and that many aspects are not fully fleshed out and refined. They are close, however, and in my opinion the player experience is unique. It is the first game that I've played where the freedom to provide logistics and transport of goods to the front line, the options of combat styles and now the introduction of armored vehicles and tanks is creating a very interesting situation for players to work together and achieve their objectives.

The most negative things I experienced were related to player population in unbalanced incursions, maps and server connection, as well as the difficulty in teaming up with friends and finding your squad during battle. The lack of ability to easily navigate the map and even just read the map to find locations was initially a big hurtle for myself and my teammates. There are many quality of life improvements that could be implemented to streamline player interaction and teamwork. I myself did not experience many server issues but quite often my friends and I would not be able to join the same regions and team up to fight on the frontlines due to lengthy queues and confusing orientations of the map locations and layout.

I think that if the team can focus on addressing these issues, work towards improving the player experience and doubling down on the frenetic experience of combat on a larger, more streamlined scale they could have something really great here. It's already a great game despite some occasionally janky mechanics.
