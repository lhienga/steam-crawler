\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: This game is can offer an experience that no other game out there can. If you are interested in supporting a month long effort to defeat an enemy team across a huge map then this is the game for you. Large group battles where it takes a team effort to win. Plenty of roles to fill and things to do.

The major issues are a distant developer team, Queues to get into front line battles during most days, Tedious building and harvesting mechanics. This game WILL eat your time and life if you let it, especially if you try to play solo.

However, This game is fantastic and creates great experiences. More Info Below: 8/10

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Front-line gameplay gives you the opportunity to use a large variety of tools to fight or disrupt the enemy team. Use a rifle to kill opponents, Machine guns to kill and suppress enemy defenses, Mortars/Tanks/Artillery to destroy enemy fortifications. Play as a medic to heal your allies, Recon and build watchtowers for map awareness. There is so much, and that's just the surface level. Hopefully you can get in before a Queue hits during peak time!

If you don't want to do that then join mid or back lines to supply the war effort. Build fortifications or vehicles. Harvest resources that a player will need to transport to the factories to produce equipment which in turn, needs to be transported to the front.But, Most of the actual "work" here is driving with WASD and left-clicking.

Be aware, This game attracts very hardcore players who take things way to seriously. Team-killing and verbal abuse can be an issue. But these experiences are few and far between. On the flip side, these hardcore players are a major part of the war effort and can be so kind as to offer free powerful vehicles and invite you to their "Operations" with all their provided gear.

If you are more casual then there are still plenty of things to do. Spawn in to a region and shoot some enemy for a bit, quick harvests in the back. try to sneak around enemy front-lines to stop their harvests/transportation. Be prepared to usually spend ATLEAST hour to start and finish something.

All in all, great game with some limitations. You cant find an experience like this anywhere else. Buy it already.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game that got out of early access and into 1.0, this game is NOT 1.0 ready now don't get me wrong, this game is absolutely amazing in terms of design and concept but there are glaring issues that have yet to be addressed that have been a problem since the early access ever since I started playing back in 2017/2018.

The biggest elephant in the room is alting, where players buy another copy of the game to grief and steal the opposing faction that their main account is on, this has been a problem on both teams for such a long time that there has been a major detriment and contributes a level of paranoia that any new or low rank player could be someone on one of these alt accounts. This has caused a negative experience to those who are in the crosshairs of being accused of an alt and is more than likely to stop playing due to this experience. Cheating is also an issue as you could be walking in the middle of the night and be shot right out of nowhere with pinpoint accuracy, you can see in the store page it says "Valve Anti-Cheat enabled" but that is just a joke there is no such thing and cheaters can just go without a care in the world.

The second problem that has arose in the past couple of years is game balancing and testing, this has been brought up into the spotlight ever since the developers went away from symmetrical equipment for both sides and instead went down an asymmetrical tech tree for both factions with each vehicle and equipment having their pros and cons in using them. The developers have done such poor job in working out a balance for these equipment, often to one side curb stomping the other until they either release a hot-fix to balance equipment or wait out 1-2 months for their next update.

The last and final problem that I have with this game is the developer communication between the playerbase, they have live streams to help showcase THE VISION for a major update that they have been working on. These updates usually try to remedy a problem for a certain team or certain aspect in the game that in the end either causes more problems or is just poorly designed. The facilities that been added in the newest update gave new depth to logistics but at the cost of more time investment, this game can be extremely grindy especially into building/maintaining these new facilities or bunker bases.

This game that I have played for the past couple of years are some of the most fun I had in a video game and the experiences that can't be achievable in any other game. Foxhole is an absolute gem when I first started playing but since then it slowly start going into a downwards spiral, the community at its best is one of the best communities out there, but factionalism and paranoia of cheating/alting has already left its mark on this community and the mishandling of this game has not helped it since.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun for the first few hours, but once you scratch the surface it becomes an extreme grind. Moderation is nearly nonexistent, resulting in excessive griefing that is absurdly apparent when one war (a "round" for those unfamiliar with the game) often lasts longer than a month in recent times. That's 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, until victory is achieved. Critical moments are frequently ruined for both teams by trolls that go unpunished by Siege Camp.

The game has also become extremely grindy, and while I respect the lack of a "pay to win" model, not everyone can spend 12+ hours per day in game to experience all that the game has to offer. Building literally takes days of effort, and the more recent addition of facilities is even worse. When you consider that all of this effort can be ruined by a bored griefer on the internet in a few minutes you can see why the community is burnt out and fed up. The game needs a huge overhaul in terms of moderation, quality of life for players, and communication from Siege Camp to overcome the current hurdles. Until then, I simply cannot recommend Foxhole to anyone who is curious. You may be impressed at the chaos short term, but will be extremely let down if you survive longer than a few hours to see what lies beneath the surface.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is unlike any other MMO I've ever played. You really feel like you are one very small part of a large war. I know some of the recent reviews have been negative, but I think many long time players of this game lose sight of the unique fun parts of the game after awhile. Yes the logistics can be grindy at times, but getting supplies to a crucial front at the right time to see your side push or successfully defend feels very rewarding. Front line combat can sometimes be frustrating because it's hard to see the impact you have as one of dozens of soldiers on a front, but it's fun get to know and work together with the other people in your faction even when things are not going your way. People do complain about toxic players, griefers and alts ruining the fun, but I've found the vast majority of Foxhole veterans are positive and welcoming to new players because the realise that teaching new players the ropes help their team win.\t\t\t\t\t
