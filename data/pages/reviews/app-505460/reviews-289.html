\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBottom line up front: The game is very enjoyable, the community not so much.

Foxhole does an excellent job of crafting an interesting world, with plenty of options to be on the frontlines, behind enemy lines, or in the backfield doing logistics support. Want to craft and harvest resources? You can do that. Want to be a truck driver? Check. Go shoot people? No problem. It does all these things and does them pretty well.

The downside that I personally can't get past is the community. Don't get me wrong, there are a lot of people who are super helpful and very friendly, both in text and voice. But for every one of those helpful people, there are dozens of really toxic, disgusting people out there. And these are people on YOUR side - there is no cross-faction voice chat.

There doesn't appear to be any real moderation in text chat, and no way to moderate voice chat other than trying to figure out what lovely person is screaming about having an orgasm and muting them.

I want to like this game, but the community just isn't my cup of tea. If you don't think that would be a problem for you though, give it a look, it's a great game behind all the grime.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; If you are 10-17 years old don't bother. It's probably not your speed.
If you are 18-25 you might find some enjoyment in the front lines.
If you are 26-35 you NEED this game, trust me.
If you are 36-50 this game will bring back fond memories of early RTS games and you'll find enjoyment in the building and the fighting.
If you are 50+ Do whatever you want.

The Good:
Foxhole has something for everyone EXCEPT the squeakers. If you are a die hard FPS person this game is not for you. There is nothing fast paced about Foxhole, not even the fighting. Everything has to be cooperative and FPS people generally prefer solo play.

You can spend days (real days) just setting up your base and logistic lines. Every detail, every chunk of concrete, ever gate.

Divide your time between helping the back line support the war or dive into the trenches and shoot someone in the face. Be a medic to revive fallen soldiers or be a machine gunner and lay down suppressive fire. Be a tanker but watch your ammo supply, that crap ain't free and someone had to make each and every round you fire!

Be a bast@rd (partisan) and go behind enemy lines and blow their stuff up, destroy logistic lines and cripple your enemies ability to wage war!

The Bad:
This game could REALLY use some minor QOL improvements. Instead of clicking a million times to fill a truck just click fill... it takes as long as it takes but we shouldn't have to click literally 30 times to fill the inventory.

Chase camera. Using middle mouse to rotate your view is fine but there should be a setting that allows your view to center on you and chase you depending on the direction you are pointed. You lose a lot of play-ability bu constantly having to rotate your view.

Make characters upgrade based on merit. This will encourage more people to play. The ranking and commend system is garbage and no one cares about it passed SSGT. If you spend 10 hours a day digging why not make the character dig faster? If you spend dozens of hours a day driving why not make the vehicle more responsive to you? If you kill all day long why not make the rifle sight better for you.? There is literally 0 character growth and no reason for anyone to do anything long term because it doesn't benefit them at all.

All in all, great game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't really suggest this game in good faith right now. I'll keep playing it, because I enjoy it, but the developers don't understand what they've made.

For instance:

+Fire is incredibly over-tuned, as all know and are aware; corrective nerfs have made flame tanks and flame-throwers useless
+The Colonial faction in general is meant to feel like an NPC faction, compared to the OP Warden faction
+Queues on servers are long, because the server hardware can't support the requisite number of people per hex; this leads to major problems when logistics players have to enter into zones briefly to drop off supplies, but can't because of queues, leading to critically under-supplied front line areas
+Voice chat is hit-or-miss, despite being integral to the game
+The communications rating system is broken for a simple reason of logic; the system encourages people to down-vote unhelpful map posts, but never up-vote useful ones, so even the comms rating of a vet player will only deteriorate over time
+Train lines should be baked into the map like roads, but at present are player-built and consume huge amounts of resources
+The new rail-mounted super gun can fire across regions, without players being able to mount any effective counterattack against it because of aforementioned problems with the queues
+Did I mention the queues were ridiculous? They're ridiculous. End-game tanks require 4-5 crew, meaning any time a major player-organized regiment runs an op in a region, the region is effectively soft-locked to anybody else
+Oh, and the servers crap out regularly so that they report queues when there are none; such a bug caused one faction to lose three hard-contested regions in hours when they couldn't spawn in, wiping out weeks of work

In short: The devs don't seem to play the wars at length, just test things internally under very constrained circumstances. This leads to major persistent design flaws, especially when their Q&A is so anemic.\t\t\t\t\t
