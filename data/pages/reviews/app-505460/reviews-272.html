\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Foxhole, an Isometric 3rd person shooter where almost every bullet, rifle, grenade, truck, tank, train, and gunboat has been built by a player. You can Join a squad or a clan and organize attacks upon player built fortifications against players in real time. The game has a great experience the horrors of the front-line or the deadly raids on player managed logistics. Entire player made factories can churn out death and destruction that can bring your side to victory." This is the quote that brought me to the game and has kept me in the game playing. I find the very concept of the game incredibly novel and even with a confusing learning curve, incredibly engaging. Knowing that every tank we lose or kill will set us or the enemy back potential hours or days of material collection really brings the effects of a front-line soldier or a truck driver into scale.

That being said, this game does have it issues. At the time of writing this there is a review bomb that has been loosely organized through reddit going on. I personally disagree with the actions being taken by the players here, but there is a point to it.
There has been several longstanding issues with the game primarily with the bugs and the griefing. It is important to note that has been a couple "Forever wars" here in the past few months, Wars usually last a week or two but this current one is approaching two months. This war has been plagued by a massive amount of Alt accounts and if you dont know, "alting" is a form of griefing by having a second account to destroy supplies, teamkill, or otherwise be a hinderance to the opposing side. Siege Camp has been silent on the issue and the punishments in place do little to discourage it. It has been incredibly demoralizing for all players.
The Bugs have been a different issue entirely, its not uncommon to run into a minor bug, "ghostly soldier just walking across no man's land" is my favorite and is a harmless bug. There are however much more harmful bugs in play. The late game nuke missiles require a massive amount of planning, and a dedicated spotter on the ground to guild the nuke into position. Normally when the spotter dies all targeting of the nuke is lost, unless it bugs and continues to target the nuke. This is major issue that hasn't been addressed for almost 20 wars. Many players are tired of the unfixed bugs and the lack of any meaningful attempts to address the griefing.

I feel that i must recommend the game though in spite of it's flaws, even though the game can be very hard to learn, it feel so satisfying to figure out. If im pounding my head against a wall of lead on the frontlines i can go mine salvage on the backlines to vent my frustrations while still trying to assist in the fight. You dont get that experience in any other game.

My last note is to the review bombers: I get your point, but this entire "cancel the game!" mentality will not benefit this game. Your actions here speak volumes on why players get the impression that the community is a toxic heap. Shame on you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far i have enjoyed this game as a time killer, i personally dont get involved much with the community and play solo, I first started spawning at the front and looting battlegrounds to play with new toys and get a feel for the game.
After that for a few hours i actually found a lot of enjoyment in the logistics of the game however, its actually pretty impressive how the war effort is broken down into so many small pieces coming together, delivering ammo and weapons to the front and now they have things like trains to make it all that much better.
Sometimes i get on and collect scrap and whatnot for a bit, let it refine and then go turn it into weapons and ammo and explosives for the boys, once i have a good stockpile i start dropping it off at the nearest and most important frontlines, i dont know that it does a lot but it does feel like you are helping and people are appreciative of it, so thats a nice touch even if you dont feel like jumping into the community and feeling responsible for the outcome of a battle.
Overall i would say this game is worth checking out, it almost feels like playing over the head planetside but in more of a risk format.\t\t\t\t\t
