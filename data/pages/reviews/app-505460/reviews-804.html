\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole, it's a game you want to like, but really can't, and I'll give a listing of pros and cons before I lead into more nuanced issues of the game.
Pros:
+A different take on live action top down warfare that ISN'T an RTS!
+The gunplay is fairly solid, with easy to learn mechanics.
+Easy to learn and use vehicle controls.
+A half decent community despite the more competitive, almost e-sports like nature of the some of the high ranking players.
Neutral, Neither good nor bad:
~Passable graphics.
~Passable sound.
~Passable integration of surround sound.
~Passable vehicle controlability. They're not bricks!
Cons:
-Woefully imbalanced teams, by numbers and skill.
-Woefully unbalanced game mechanics.
-Highly exploitable game mechanics.
-Hard to judge terrain.
-Vehicles can and will get stuck and be completely unuseable. This bug has existed since day one, it still isn't fixed.

While there's a lot to love about this game, it is, at the core selling point, flawed. There has been no attempts to actively mitigate the issues with the imbalances with how many players there are, and how often one or more of the top ten clans wind up on the same side. While you can't fault a clan for wanting to play one side and only one side. You can make a point of saying that it's a bit odd when a majority of the top ten clans are on one team. You can even say it's a bit odd that one side has enough people on it at any given time to have an assault of twenty people in three different places actively ongoing with no stop until their goal is met. No interruption of supply lines, and with enough back end support to prevent any forces from cutting those lines with no need to pull from the frontal assault force.

And all of this? All of this has been an "issue" since day one. I hesitate to call it an issue because you can't fault the clans that genuinely do want to play only one faction, be it wardens or colonials, their entire game career, but I don't think that's always the case. I do think that some clans wait and see, or perhaps even actively ask which side certain clans have chosen before joining the next match. Again, I hesitate to call it an issue as some might be rivals, but some of them might just be trying to gang up with those more experienced clans. Yet, to top it off, even if one side outnumbers the other 3-1, there is absolutely no attempts to auto-balance the active players, or at least, none that I've ever seen.

Now we move onto the game mechanic abusing. It is possible to use the limited night time view range, and a rifle or carbine, to peak at the edge of the night view range, and thus remain almost entirely invisible while being able to fire on and kill those out of their sight. This seems clever, until after a couple of hours of it happening to you, you finally get how to do it, and quickly find out that it's actually quite abusive of the limited mechanics of the game. Then there's the ability to glitch onto certain rocks, and thus climb something that couldn't otherwise be climbed, by doing what I assume to amount to confusing the engine to get you higher than you should be. This is another issue that has existed since the early days that just hasn't changed.

Finally, we move into gas. Gas is a brilliant mechanic, but the real issue is that it wasn't actually *as* deadly as portrayed in this game. In fact, many soldiers survived exposure to the deadly chemical fumes simply because there was either a light drizzle, or even a slight breeze. Neither of these are actually simulated in the game, though given, nor is the true deadliness of the gas simulated either. In your average forest, deadly amounts of chlorine gas could linger in pockets for upwards of 24 hours if there wasn't any breeze or rain, yet in a clear area with a mild breeze, could dissipate in under a minute. Furthermore, very brief contact with the gas, under half a minute, wasn't deadly. Yes, the long term effects would lead to cancer and life long skin irritation, but it wouldn't kill you right then even there, and after a few moments in clear air, you'd be able to shrug it off, more or less, and gas masks and their filters were more than just a one use then toss item. These masks and their filters often saw use in multiple assaults before needing to be replaced, and this is once again due to how unreliable the gasses were due to environmental effects.

Essentially, you'd cause far more lasting and useful damage with a man portable flame thrower than you would chemical shells. I'll even show the numbers.
40 million total casualties of WWI.
1.3 Million to gas.
That's about a 30-1 other casualties to gas, but that's NOT fatalities. Fatalities of gas to other fatalities comes out to a WHOPPING 170 other deaths to every 1 death to chemical weapons. Lets narrow it a bit to combat fatalities to gas, and it's still approx. 100 to 1. In this game, it's more like every 3 deaths from other means are to one death from gas. Basically, what I'm saying is, we need simulated weather effects on the various gas grenades and artillery before I will even begin to consider this game passable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter many years, I felt it was time to type out a review for this game.

Foxhole has been many things for me. I've made great friends, i've pulled all-nighters, I've built clans and communities around this game, and will never regret a single second I put into it.

Foxhole is frustrating. It is grindy, sometimes tedious. It requires an amount of patience that frankly most members of the gaming community don't have, and I understand that. In a world of fortnites and instant gratification its hard for some to appreciate the long drawn out "Big Picture" and to not be the hero where everything revolves around you and your actions. It's even harder when you can play this game for hours, build bases, tanks, outfit entire regiments, lead said regiments into a front, fight for 3 days straight, and still lose. That's not a good feeling for anybody.

Here comes the "But"...

But, You can do all of those things. You can start from nothing, coordinate entire networks of logistical players, assign squads of real players to objectives, spend hours upon hours in strategy rooms drawing battle lines and writing up extensive operation plans. Where strategy and teamwork are paramount more than your ability to shoot someone.

So why does this game get my thumbs up? Because it is not for everyone. I dare say that this game is for the minority. The ones who read my But arguments and thought that sounds like a dream of a game to play. It sucks because it requires more players than are willing to deal with the games problems, and the game does have major problems, I'm not here to deny that. There are things that could be different that would allow a bigger percentage of gamers to enjoy it, and hopefully by the end of EA we will see it.

If you're willing to give this game a shot, don't do it alone. Don't try to come be the hero. Join the community. Be a part of the big picture, because that is where this game will shine for you.\t\t\t\t\t
