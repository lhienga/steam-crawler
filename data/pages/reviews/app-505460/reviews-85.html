\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game that checks all the boxes I could want from a game, from back-line logistics to front-line fighting. I am one to prefer the routine of back-line logistics, going through the motions of mining resources that are needed and turning them into supplies, weapons or vehicles that are needed throughout various stages of the war. My greatest sense of pride is seeing the crates I made from the factories being shipped out by truck, train or ship.

I have experienced front-line fighting in my first hundred hours or so, half of that on foot and the other half as armor crew before being recruited by an armor regiment (clan) and introduced into facilities to make some of the facility-locked tanks and upgrades for vehicles/tanks. I fell in love with the ability to build your own facility and produce all sorts of items needed for other facilities, and it does get complex at times with facilities and it is rather easy to get lost into it.

This game is heavily based on finding a group of players to work together towards goals for the war, finding a Regiment in this game goes a long way in helping you understand the game and have the manpower needed to help you in various tasks, whether logistics or front-line operations. Seems to be the most difficult part is finding group of players to work towards certain goals, not too hard to get into the big regiments that are around but I prefer to avoid the bigger regiments as I personally steer clear of the bigger clans and prefer to keep to a smaller regiment size as it's easier to manage and a little less chaotic. I highly recommend newer players to join the bigger regiments just to help get a feel for how the game play is as they'll have more clear cut goals and more people to interact with during their ops to understand how operations run for the most part.

For logistics, spawn in the back line and ask in the regional/logi/world chat about needing help understanding logistics and someone will be more than happy to help. I always do my best to help players that ask questions to help them understand what is going on, when I first started playing and I opened the map I was completely overwhelmed and had no idea what to do or where to go. Always ask questions!

Now on to the bad of the game.. This game if you "enjoy" logistics is super grindy, you can spend all day working on a facility and getting resources needed for the facility just for you to log back in the next day and it all be gone due to the power running, other players taking all they can get to reduce their own grind or partisans coming along and blowing it all up while you are asleep. In this game it feels like you are punished more for actively wanting to do logistics and especially so if you want to run a facility. You have to keep in mind decay, in which your facility (depending on the quality of supply is) can decay completely in 24 hours, so you are forced to log in everyday and grind for X amount of hours to ensure your maintenance supplies are where they need to be for X amount of hours you will be away.

There is toxicity present in the game, but I tend to avoid it. Not as bad as League of Legends but it can get toxic or crazy in world chat sometimes, but there is an option to minimize the chat so it's out of sight and out of mind. As long as you find the right people in the game, and avoid the crazies you can have a fantastic time in Foxhole!\t\t\t\t\t
