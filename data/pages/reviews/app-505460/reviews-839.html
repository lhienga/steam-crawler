There's tanks, ships, guard towers, bunkers, various AT and equipment you can use as long as you or someone else makes it. Some people complained at the lack of weapons in the starting area, but all it takes is a bit of common sense and perhaps a cold heart.

Watched as one of my team mates was shot and ran to me asking for a bandage, he dropped, and slowly started to bleed out. I kept the bandage for myself, and then took his rifle and ammo as soon as his body ceased convulsing.

Overall Foxhole is a fun and engaging top down shooter with am art style that's both gritty but cute as the same time.\t\t\t\t\t

PRO's:


- teamwork wins the day
- location based voice chat - only your closest teammates (and enemies) can hear you
- beautiful, highly detailed graphics
- great aesthetic
- top down perspective levels the playing field; veteran players can easily be taken out by new players
- great community
- passionate developers
- no lag
- HUGE, detailed world where the front line is constantly changing
- classless play; switch from being a medic to engineer in a heart beat
- your buildings and fortifications actually stay in the game world and has real impact on game play
- EVERY vehicle, weapon and even bullets were crafted by actual people
- huge focus on logistics
- huge player base
- room for everyone; you're making a difference just by showing up with your rifle

FUN
- run around trying to keep your team alive and have an actual impact
- deliver a truck full of ammunition and supplies to an outpost under siege
- go behind enemy lines on a raid
- coordinate a huge push through enemy territory
- seeing orders being carried out and leading to victory

ROOM FOR IMPROVEMENT
- Logistics can be very intimidating and complex at first
- More specific ways to commend great players would help encourage certain play styles
- Please stamp everything with a "made by' description so logi can be commended more
- gathering scrap is still a slog, but the new depleting salvage fields will lead to more strategic game play. Excited!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to begin.... this is my most played game ever, over even Skyrim, that's right, Skyrim. I wanted to put a review in awhile back but I wanted to wait and see what they would do. I must say, waiting did the trick, I've had this game for awhile and for some reason I've always come back to it. There's so much I could tell you about what I love about this game and hate, but i'll leave that for you to decide, so i'll give you the main overall features that make this my favorite game.The factors that always brought me back over and over is the player base (Collies are toxic, go Warden). I can't get enough of it, true i've been a Warden 99% of the time (Collie when i first started) but the players are truely remarkable. And no I don't mean the mass genocide of teammates or purposefully placing tank traps on roads to screw with logi. What I mean is the players, clans included, try so hard to not just win the war, but work together, yes I know impossible, no one ever trys to work with other players. This game brings out the absolute best and absolute worst in people, I can't express to you how often i've wanted to curse out a fellow teammate for stealing my vehicle. But at the end of the day you can just make a new one, except if it's a relic, then you gotta search forever for another. The Foxhole Devs have been trying their hardest too create a game that provides not just a realistic war simulation but a game of cooperation. How are they accomplishing a goal such as cooperation? Well they've been introducing new campaign maps that have regions (the most recent being the hexagon campaign map) that allows players to interact with each other by traveling through these regions almost fluidly, this allows for players to plan out strategies for either combat, logistics or even just meeting up with friends resulting in not just a region to region experience but a faction wide co-op experience that players can move around without any trouble... except if you run into partisans. Team building is the foundation of this game, I cannot tell you how many times i've seen communication completely an utterly fail when a teammate doesn't see a tank rolling down the road straight at your halftrack. It's absolutely infuriating... but it keeps bringing me back, because I know that whenever I come back, there is ALWAYS going to be something crazy going on, either night or day, someone is always doing something crazy. The developers have been working tirelessly too introduce ways of bringing more cooperation and team building into the game (such as the latest patch with the new logistics system) to bring a fuller experience to this massive online multiplayer game, because that's what this is, not a WW2 war simulation game, but a ever expanding team building game designed to give you a realistic experience. I won't spoil anything for you if you decide to purchase this 40% off game, but always remember that even though someone steals your truck full of bmats, you can always make another and spend 45 minutes scavenging for scrap :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole.... by far and away one of the best war simulators I have ever played. Everything from the bullet that comes out of your gun to the vehicle it was delivered in is made by the players. Every swing of your hammer for a resource is converted into something usable by either yourself or your fellow players.

The game is broken up into wars, each war ending when one team has captured and upgraded the majority of the town hall locations on the maps. This could take days, weeks or even months depending upon how the conflict is panning out.

Besides the amazing depth that this game reaches with its capacity for war my favorite part of this game is the community. A bunch of non-toxic teammates spanning from all the regions of the globe. Join the game, get greeted by an experienced player, they show you the ropes and set you off on your way. Like.... what game does that?

Also the development team.... so much love for these guys. All of the devs play their game, and are constantly working to improve it. This most recent update 0.26 IS HUGE!!!! so much work went into it. Whats more is they actually listen to player feedback. With all of the cash grabs happening these days with skummy dev teams Foxhole is a breath of fresh air in an otherwise stale market.

Now to the real stuff: This game is player population dependent......... so BUY IT! ESPECIALLY IF IT IS ON SALE!!!! 10/10 would spend the 12$ for this game... hell I've bought multiple copies just for my friends to join me.

One more thing.... whatever you do.... don't join the Colonials they smell funny (seriously)\t\t\t\t\t
