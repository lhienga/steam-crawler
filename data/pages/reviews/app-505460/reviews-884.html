At the time of edit I have closing in on 250 hours now (I would play more but this year is way to ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ busy for me)
I will just start by saying I love this game as a whole, the concept anyway, the others keep getting broken with ever update that follows.

Pros:
- Top down camera is great and works really well with the game
- The persistant warfare is an intresting factor and allows for much creativity when it comes to building of frontlines and fallback lines and even the very logistic routes that supply the war, owever due to the refusal of the devs to listen to the playerbaser I would negate this point as they just keep ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ things despite literal riots and then they just mute everyone who took part in it.
- Gunplay is fun all round now, however it still suffers with the prone meta
- Easy basics to grasp but not so simple that you learn it all within a few hours, for example I would consider myself a competant player but even now I would learn something new, whether that be that it takes 3 satchel bombs to destroy a bridge or 6 hits to finish a scrap pile
- I love the playerbase and faction related stuff. for example some people get very patriotic about their faction which leads to much fun outside and inside the game

Cons:
- Frontlines can be a nightmare due to on going issues with decay in the game and general population balance, meaning while one night you might hold the southern part of the map the next you fight you are struggling to hold the northern most edges on the middle maps overnight. (Recent updates have helps calm this problem but it still remains, hopfully as the popualtion of the game increases there will be less night capping and this should stem the tide of maps swapping hands overnight)(Edit here, playerbase is dropping as the devs refuse to listen to the players and just mute anyone who goes against them)
- Logistics can be hard to understand and get a hold of when just starting the game, and the devs continue to ruin it.
- as stated above logistics can be hard to understand at first but this is mostly due to lack of either a enforced tutorial or a tutorial that you will find naturally. the current one is there but is on the side part of the home island and is very very often not even glanced at by those new to the game causing not only confusion but also general salt as people new to the game due to lack of a good tutorial run off into battle with half the ammo for the entire army or crash trucks bound for the frontline right into the enemy's hands possibly costing you the battle depending on the situation. (you may think I am over overexaggerating the point of new players running out with half an armys worth of ammo but it is true and not too uncommon for me to find an enemy or friendly body with well over 10 clips but I have found as high as 50 clips on a body before)
-The moderation team could easily be compared to a facist dictatorship, lapdogs for the devs who continue to suppress any negative or constructive critism while also believing there is nothing wrong. They label anyone who goes against them as "Trolls" and "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥"
-The Devs couldnt care less for the playerbase, the community manager if you could really call him tat is hardly fit for the job.
-Ironicly the devs dont know the game at all.
-The mods dont even play the game they just abuse their power, but even with eveidence of this the devs refuse to accept it.

As a conclusion I like this game and will maybe play it but it is not without its many flaws. I could not recommend this game being honest, at this point its a waste of your money. Price wise I would say it is not worth the money as it is now but if on sale I would maybe consider it at best.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI normally don't write Steam reviews, least of all for Early Access games, but I felt like I should speak up as a result of the mixed reviews it's been getting recently.

This game is pure magic. I've never played anything like it. Forget MMOs where you grind for hundreds of hours for rare drops or high levels. In Foxhole you grind for hundreds of hours because you know what you're doing is urgently needed by other players. Menial tasks take on surprising meaning when you can see the outcomes in front of you: bandaging fellow soldiers means your raiding party lives on instead of fizzling out, a package of artillery shells holds off an enemy assault for minutes, a just-in-time delivery of spawn supplies stops a frontline base from starving completely. There are so many moving parts to Foxhole's battlefield, so many links in the chain, that it feels like there's tons of things you can do at any given time to make a difference. This game takes me back 15 years to when I was a kid in the schoolyard, building snow forts with my buddies, stockpiling snowballs, pretending I was a part of something big and important. I've never played a game where something as basic as scavenging supplies off the dead and carrying them back to base still felt meaningful.

It's true that the combat isn't super exciting, and many tasks in this game are repetitive and tedious. That's not the point. Foxhole is the only game I've seen people do those tasks without personal reward, for extended periods of time. It's the larger context that gives those tasks meaning. I will warn the reader that Foxhole is not the kind of game that you can duck into for some quick fun. It sucks up afternoons and weekends. The appeal is not in grinding a progress bar, but in watching the game state evolve over hours, days, in seeing your actions slowly change a persistent battlefield.

Foxhole is still a work in progress. Features and mechanics are being added or changed all the time. I don't know when the game will hit 1.0, or what it'll look like by then, or even if it'll still be alive by that time. All I know is, it's been a real privilege to have played this game in its current state.\t\t\t\t\t
