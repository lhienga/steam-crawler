\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThings that I experienced in the first 10 hours of playing:
- I thought on the front line
- I was part of an Artillery crew
- I drove supply Trucks to the front
- I got ambushed by Partisans
- I became part of a Squad
- I drove a Train
- I am helping building a base

If you have a friendly attitude and aren't too shy using the mic it s definitely a recommended game!
The team play comes naturally, as people have to work together to win the war.
Everyone is a cog in the machine doing their part and you can choose between front line and logistical activities depending on your mood. There is no need to spend hours grinding either, you just jump in and do your thing, whether it is 12 hours straight or 2 hours a week is up to you.

The game doesn't track kills, you don't even get a notification if you killed someone. This game is not about K/D, but about Team play. The "rank" system play a part in this as well, as commendations of other players increase your rank, therefore it is an indicator of how well you play with others. It is probably not a perfect system, but it does it's job. There are no loot boxes or other micro transactions that plague modern games.

I recommend watching some youtube videos from streamers about the game to better get a picture of the game play. There is a lot of fun to be had, even when you are loosing.

If you love the types of games where you can make friends or just have fun with strangers, then I can only highly recommend this game. Keep an open mind, stay relaxed and avoid joining the Colonials, I've heard they eat babies.

PS: Additional info for Linux users: Works great with Proton Experimental, haven't noticed any issues so far. Tho I did read on ProtonDB that some people had some issues with it.
I recommend checking the DB first before buying.\t\t\t\t\t
