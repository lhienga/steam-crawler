\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpent nearly 200 hours into the game, 3 out of 12 paid-leaves this year, and 4 days hospitalized after playing a devilishly long sessions of the game.

This is the game for you if you enjoy base building and strategic combat. This game is also for you if you simply enjoy doing good things to complete strangers and witnessing others do the same for you. Also if you enjoy an almost unjustified sense of belonging to a big team, achieving common goal of winning.

You will roughly spend 1/4 of the time building defenses, another 1/4 carrying stuffs around, and rest is the actual action. The weapons have a bit of learning curve to it, but mostly rewarding to master. The vehicles are well-balanced, especially after mines reintroduced, they aren't able to do too much without infantry support.

Currently has balance issues, glitches here and there, but it's currently in a very enjoyable state to play. Invest in a good set of headphone/mic for most enjoyable time.

10/10 -- Most enjoyable $10 I've ever spent on a game\t\t\t\t\t

On Wednesday December 26, 2018, I was delivering supplies and men to the frontlines along the west coast. I was making my supply runs out of Callahan's Passage, as the fighting along the entire front line had become extremely fierce. The roads out from Mooring County weren't safe, you see, too many partisans and Colonial forces patrolling and ambushing any stray supply convoy not heavily guarded, so I decided that discretion was the better part of valor, and opted to take the detour instead of risking my supplies falling into enemy hands.

We were losing, you see. We were losing the war bit by bit, inch by inch. When the attack on Umbral Woods far into the southeast, deep into their Heartlands failed, we simply couldn't organize and ferry supplies down to the breach fast enough to stop them from plugging the breach, and soon after, all the middle ground we'd fought so hard for was lost in the daily grind.

It was the "20th" war. 20 times this meatgrinder, this machine which consumes the spirit and flesh of men sprang to life, and it was a long one. The men grew tired and restless, they wished to return to their homes, their loved ones, and their lives, and they sought to end the war by any means necessary. I saw, what can only be described as "utter madness" take hold of many of my comrades and commanders. In a matter of days, ideas we would have never entertained seemed to infest the channels, each man proposing new and ever more insane methods by which we could wholesale slaughter the enemy and end the war, once and for all.

It was in this madness, this lost of humanity that they were found, up in the cold north, relics from a time long ago when men became demons and drowned the world in fire.

The ICBM launch sites, the bringers of Armageddon. It took us nearly two months to uncover ancient design plans which detailed the payloads, fuel mixtures, alloys, and thrust vectoring algorithms, and weeks to gather the necessary requirements, the refined fuels, the basic and advanced alloys, the specific launch angles, and the explosives required to bring these monsters to life. I wanted to cry, I wanted to ask them "look! Look what you do! These machines killed our forefathers! These rockets made our lands cold and barren, and you want to use them?" With each passing day I grew more and more fearful, fearful for myself, fearful for my wife, and by god, I even became fearful for the Colonials. These were not weapons men were ever meant to use.

I had hope, however. I hoped that, amongst this slaughter and carnage, cooler heads would prevail, and for a time, they did.


And then... well.. they nuked Jade Cove.


I tried, by god I tried. I loaded truckloads and truckloads of men and supplies and rushed them to the next town over, but it wasn't enough. The sky turned red, as if the wrath of god was upon us, a great rushing and roaring could be heard, drowning even the whine of artillery shells, and then the bomb dropped. The were no screams, there was no time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, it's been a year. And somehow I found 400 more hours since my last, highly critical review.

I think I can safely say that I find this game fun, but I wouldn't recommend it. This is the point where you say "What do you mean?"

Well, it's a good game, for sure. I enjoyed it so much that I racked up over a thousand hours in the past few years plus the playtime I had on the original pre alpha test client. But I don't think it's ready to release quite yet, and more than a few features are missing. It's enough to say that you should hold off until at least Christmas to see what the devs do.

For one, the logistics system is in a far better state than it was a year ago. I can comfortably say that I can gather enough materials to make a harvester in a few hours, and then I can use that harvester for a couple hours to make enough materials to create an armored fighting vehicle. And on the production side, the devs have added in a new factory that can mass produce materials, so you'll hopefully never be lacking in your rifles, RPGs, and respawn materials if your team is doing a good job. Of course, getting drivers to move those materials up to the front is always a big struggle, but I'm confident that with a more consistent population base, this problem would go away, as it does during the update war population spikes. But there are a few issues. First, the mass production factory should be able to make garrison supplies in order to help alleviate a lot of the grind that base builders have to do. Second, the AI voting system is a bit, well, questionable. The reason why this is filed under logistics is because it is far too easy to reset a town's ability to provide AI, which makes truck drivers a lot more vulnerable to guerrillas. (In general, it's too hard to get permanent AI and tech certain structures on BOBs, but you probably aren't too concerned about this if you're new to the game.) The change that removed small arms damage to certain structures is good (I think), but it should be refined a little further. Perhaps only allowing small arms immunity after being powered by AI would be a good change to stop people from building foxholes on the border to kill partisans. But overall, logistics is (mostly) complete. Some small changes and the 2020 vehicle™ will go a long way to polishing up logistics. Personally I've found there's so much supplies lying around in rearline towns that most of the time you just need to make the armored fighting vehicle and most of the time the ammo and fuel is already there.

Onto combat. It's a bit of a hot mess right now, and I don't know much about infantry, so I'll stick with artillery and armor. Artillery is kind of strange, since it's prohibitively expensive earlygame but lategame it becomes the deciding factor in most fights. I think the new howitzer garrisons that retaliate against indirect fire are really useful in preventing quick caps, but it would be nice if it unlocked a little earlier. As is, top tier artillery can most of the time flatten bases, and it's really hard to unlock howitzer garrisons for your bunker bases. But on the other hand, earlygame artillery is too expensive and most of the time the first artillery you see comes in the form of self-propelled mortars. Personally, I enjoy the midgame artillery experience where the kill times are long but the shells are plentiful. It leads to a lot of back-and-forth, which makes the fighting feel tense. Overwhelming firepower during lategame is boring, and with how fragile top tier artillery is, there is a lot of waiting and browsing Reddit on my phone. Armor, on the other hand, is still very unbalanced. Lategame armor fights are slugging matches, like before, but the balance keeps shifting back and forth with the only constant being that armor meta is unbalanced. Forcing players to choose between multiple armor types may keep each war slightly different, but it too often forces a faction into a rock and hard place where they must choose between a pushing tank and an anti-tank vehicle. This isn't fun for the faction being forced to choose, and it isn't fun for the opposing faction as oftentimes the use of choices will force players to choose one powerful vehicle or another, meaning that for the opposing faction they will still have to fight an obscenely strong vehicle, only it is now "balanced" under a decision system. This also works the other way too; the "Ballista" siege mortar, for example, has incredibly short range with extremely expensive shells that cost more and do less than satchel charges. Instead of increasing its range to keep it safe, the developers have simply moved it up the tech tree, meaning it is still bad, it's just that it comes earlier. The dedication to the "vision" when it comes to vehicles also hampers the situation further. Colonial vehicles are almost always open top, meaning players can be shot or grenaded out (in fact, I played a couple hours ago the Pelekys, and although the tank never died and we killed a few enemy tanks, it wasn't fun dodging grenades and being mortared out every fifteen minutes). Some Warden vehicles are shorter ranged, which makes the crews feel helpless against longer ranged vehicles. Overall, if you're thinking about buying for the combat, I would wait until December and then see what people think.

The community situation is strange, and I don't want to get into it. I also exclusively play Colonial, so I only know half the story.

Overall, is it a good game? Absolutely. But I don't think I can recommend it just yet. Give it a few months and come back to this review. If it has changed, I might change the negative review.\t\t\t\t\t
