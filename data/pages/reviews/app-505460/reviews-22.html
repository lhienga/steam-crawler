\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a very unique game that has a ton of persisting flaws that unfortunately makes it very, very hard to recommend.

The game is a mechanical mess, balanced terribly with minimal playtesting (this isn't pre-alpha anymore ffs) and is only held together by the incredibly dedicated playerbase who invest all their time and energy into keeping this persistant war going.

Without the playerbase, this game would literally not work and from my perspective the dev team are not doing enough to make them keep playing (QoL is the most requested thing by far). Siege Camp loves to drop more content for the game, but below the surface all it does is to increase the amount of investment the average player has to put into to gain access to it. Burnout players are unfortunately the norm because this game is grindy as hell, and that's by design.

This game features only a barebone tutorial and there's no dedicated test enviroment apart from a very short time period when a new major patch is implemented, which only purpose is to iron out the biggest bugs and the most controversal of balance changes. The rest will just go live and the entire war becomes basically a testing ground with the playerbase as playtesters. The latest Update pretty much was so bad they had to implement an emergency patch it right after the first update war ended. That's how little confidence they got in their game, and they make the players work through that.

Mechanically, this game is janky and unforgiving.
Building is time-intensive, unintuitive and a big investment with little return.
Double that for facility buildings, which are a very controversal addition that would blow the scope of this review.
Maintainance mechanics which are poorly explained and commucated.
Couple that with the most barebones of commucation tools and you got a toxic cocktail going that causes a majority to fail and quit the game.

Speaking of lacking communicational tools, Siege camps offloads that onto the playerbase too which resulted in each faction having several hubs on 3rd party apps such as discord. Which is frankly ridiculous as we're expected to communicate in-game too as people have to claim territory for building. All of this results in unneeded toxic conflicts (especially at the start of a war) but the dev team has put exactly ZERO effort at improving this for crying out loud.

The dev's focus on adding more content rather than fixing this game underlying problems hasn't shifted in the 2 years I've played this game. The game was never even once in a polished state, It's unacceptable at the point of this review as it's been over a year since this game went out of early access.

If you're only going for casual infantry/tank gameplay and join a clan to leech for their resources, you'll likely have a good time with Foxhole. If you intend to spend any more time/effort into foxhole to go deeper into the mechanics...

...stay the hell away from this game, for your own sake.\t\t\t\t\t
