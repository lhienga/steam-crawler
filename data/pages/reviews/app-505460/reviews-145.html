\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has so much potential, but after many hours of playing, I caution you not to waste your time.

It takes hours to days to weeks (real time) to get anything satisfying done, and there is very little reward for the hours to days long grind that logistics guys and builders are subjected to. You cannot manufactured goods sitting or they will disappear in crazy short times compared to how long it takes to amass resources.

it is insane that they make people do nothing but click a mouse for hours to days on end to produce resources. Making things does not require clicking but often takes hours to days longer. It is boring, unrewarding, and receives the least recognition in the game despite having the most impact on gameplay. Most players won't be bothered so shortages exist almost always.

Fighting is difficult and unsatisfying unless you have equipment you cannot even build yourself in most cases. there is no official organization so logi doesn't get done effectively and you fight on a front with mismatched ammo more often than not. Players are not friendly for the most part and will yell at you before they help you.

Everyone has an opinion on what should be done, nobody agrees and things fall apart rather quickly. This exists among solos, opposing clans, and within clans themselves. People are petty AF.

You will spend 2 hours grinding or waiting for 15 minutes of thrill.

It is all way too tedious and rewarding this type of game format

And finally, you will pay money for this game only to find that you can never actually play it how or when you want to because of the stupid balance system. Anywhere that matters will have a queue at any respectable time, and it takes 30 minutes to an hour to get in a hex with fighting in some cases. You can get into other hexes, but you will find that either they are not supplied at all or there are no soldiers there. you get kicked if you sit too long in a vehicle waiting even though you are in a queue. once you are in you cannot get back in easily if you are a logi. resources are often waiting at the border while the soldiers starve on the front. Stupid.

Bottom line. This game can be a great experience. It has some amazing moments. You are rarely going to experience the best this game has to offer, and ever more rarely will amazing moments happen to you personally. You will essentially just bear witness to large clan achievements where each clan member plays a significant but minuscule part. You shell a base to death, there's got to be a guy who does nothing but pack and unpack ammo which takes forever. You will be packing and unpacking everything you make longer than you will be fighting with it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed since early 2018 with relatively frequent updates. Very good game.. From things I love to things I hate -
Many comments say the dev team locks themselves away - at least in the personal experience of me and people I talk to, they have been very interactive and their dev streams are frequent. They're open about plans in the game and have decent long-term goals for the health of the game. Updates won't generally happen mid-war (unless it is truly gamebreaking) but thankfully there's no commitment to any given war.
There's a decent variety of roles to fulfill with no real expectations - you won't get locked into a certain playstyle. Combat feels intuitive - generally you don't want to run into a fight by yourself without knowing the area. I would compare gameplay to some older MMOs, there are "grinding" parts that are more of a social experience and there are "active" parts that require a bit more awareness. You can have fun with either depending on your outlook but obviously there's no reason to do things you aren't enjoying. Frontline roles include medical teams, infantry, tank divisions, and mortar teams. Backline roles include resource collection, production, research, and distribution. So in essence... Real war frontline and logistics. No air support (and to my knowledge no plans for it), navy exists but is plain, but land options are plentiful.
The map has a lot of terrain variety that I think works in its favor. Every area feels like a unique fighting experience with different problems to solve. Snow freezes vehicles and people, so you need fires. Mountains block mortars and create more restricted pathways for fights. Open areas are more vulnerable from sneak attacks. Cities have upper floors that people can shoot from. Bridges... act as a vortex for players that don't know better. I am a sucker for dynamic terrain and opportunities to use it to your advantage are plentiful.
Some minor community issues that I think can be ignored but could be a dealbreaker - some groups can be aggressive, especially to newer players, but on the other hand there are many more groups that are welcoming. I would not look at Reddit or 4chan or any other out-of-game group as a metric for what in-game is like. 90% of my interactions have been either positive or neutral-leaning-positive, but that isn't to say I'm a representative of the average.
The two teams are technically balanced though not identical - asymmetrically. There are waves of strength bursts on either side and occasionally it feels like it's just a research race, especially with high value technologies like some of the late game vehicles. No strong sense of "snowballing" present, though. Colonial weapons and tools are generally cheaper but slightly worse, while Warden weapons are usually stronger but take a lot more resources. This works well for some things but does not account for the relatively even number of players, that will grow and shrink over the day - having fewer weapons is a much bigger issue at larger populations, and having weaker weapons becomes an issue in a small defense. You can always steal from the enemy if fortune permits.. but there are better ways luck as a mechanic could be used. This imbalance also applies to the aforementioned terrain, though I personally feel like the challenges presented there make for a more interesting battle.
Barring actual gameplay and social aspects... At least as of reviewing there are some server issues that I think could be resolved - the game is segmented into hexagons, and travelling between hexagons involves switching servers which creates problems in more populated areas. Some glitches go in your favor, some out of your favor, but I think at least they are predictable enough that there are no surprises after the first time something happens.
I love this game very much and I can recognize why some people have issues - some issues moreso than others - but I would nonetheless recommend the game. Don't go in with wrong expectations, and show some discretion thinking about what you want from video games. You'll know pretty quickly if you don't like it so if you're on the fence you could always buy it and refund it after discovering your feelings. Or you could just not buy it too... I only have $2 on steam and $0 in real life so if I had to choose between buying it right now and not buying it right now well I guess I already made my choice.\t\t\t\t\t
