The game will not be around much longer and is most likely not the best investment as once the game's community dies and the servers are shut down there will be no way to play it unless the devs release private servers.

Now that my compact review is finished let me explain in detail the fall of foxhole. My first exposure to foxhole was in 2015 to 2016. At the time clapfoot had released a combat beta for foxhole that consisted of a third person isometric deathmatch game that soon evolved into a more tactical combined arms game with the addition of larger maps and vehicles. To put it plainly I am essentially one of the first to play this game, and have played it off and on for years since that first combat beta.

The real problem with the game is pretty much summed up in my last sentence. I have played the game off and on for years. Early on it was not hard to lose long stretches of time playing foxhole as in the early days the community was generally agreeable and easy to joke around with. The type of guys you would go online, and play a match with and crack jokes the entire time.

For some reason the community at large changed shortly after the game went free to play. As suddenly a much less fun community developed around the game, and my main theory for this is simple. Foxhole is a simple game consisting for the most part of very simple gameplay loops much like a mobile game. Certainly when it comes to the logistics portion of the gameplay. Evidently most people who bought this game early on were really only interested in those simple gameplay loops.

The core portion of this game's player base is made up primarily of whales, and generally are people who will come home from work (if they have a job), and log onto foxhole to start their second job. They don't communicate. They don't participate in activities outside of the gameplay loop they focus on, and they hammer away at repetitive tasks day in day out, and most importantly they are aggressively hostile to anything that interrupts their loop.

Furthermore the devs have gone so far as to add even more of an incentive for whales already stuck into the loops by offering them essentially unmerited praise by having an entire portion of the UI spam you with the option to reward people performing these loops regardless of the impact their actions actually have on gameplay.

So this leads us to foxhole today. A game on the edge, and a game with a community that will not die because of lack of interest, but because the developers fostered a core community composed of the type of person who would play world of warcraft for twelve hours a day, not bathe for a full week, and all the while not saying a single word to another human being for months on end as they use a bunch of pixles on a screen to slowly increase the dopamine drip, but hey at least the community Isn't "toxic". You can't have a toxic personality if you don't have a personality.

Regardless of updates, regardless of how much of a discount you can get this game for it is not worth it. Foxhole as an idea may sound fun, but the reality of foxhole has not been fun for at least three years. If you are really desperate to play foxhole do yourself a favor, and put together some sort of time machine, and take yourself back three years to before it was being sold on steam, back when you could play with people who realized $20 was too much for a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ less funny version of runescape with guns, and back when the game's community at least had a soul.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a honest and detailed review about this game and my personal opinion that most foxhole veterans think and feel.
Also as a foxhole veteran my self and player that has been playing this game from BETA, i still recommend this game and come back after couple of months of taking a break. but i just had to write this review in hopes that one day devs will improve couple if not all of the things i mentioned but many more that i didnt mention but every player will encounter them once they start playing.


POSITIVE
There are many positive things about this game, but ill mention only 2 that i feel are most important because i am doing a negative review and there are already many positive reviews you can read.
1.Regular updates and game improvements - Every couple months there is something new/different/changed, and its worth coming back and checking it out for couple of hours and then again taking a break for couple of months.
2.Everything is player controlled - Meaning you want something done, you gotta ask someone to do it or do it yourself. and yes, EVERYTHING IS PLAYER CONTROLLED, defenses, outposts, town halls, city defense building, bridges, vehicles and fuel for them. i am just waiting for a moment when they will make us repair roads, paint buildings and even implement a food and drink system, so youll no more need to go play farming simulator and instead can do farming stuff and supply food to real players. after all, this game offers everything and you can be anything you want: Medic, builder, miner, driver, engineer, architect, even roleplayer that can take enemy players prisoners, but of course you gotta find an enemy willing to roleplay.



NEGATIVE
-Corner shooting - 1. With rifles you can see further when corner aiming(i believe about 40-50% more).
2. Big size monitor give you extra 10-20% view distance boost that honestly doesnt sound much, but trust me, its ALLOT in this game.
3. If you add all that and go to a hill, youll gain about 30-50% more view range. so there is no surprise if you see a player base/outpost that your friendly team decided to just go around it and ignore it the entire war.

-Logistics is fun idea but badly done - There are 20+ regions and you gotta drive and teleport in each one to come to the front line. on top of that, front lines sometimes have 10+ queued players and you gotta wait till a spot becomes free, the more players the longer queue, sometimes even 20-30+ minutes of waiting.

-Day night cycle - Entire cycle is 60 minute long, meaning 30 minutes day and 30 minutes night. at night you cant do anything(especially if you in a vehicle because enemies can see your car headlights at all times, but you cannot see them unless they come in range of them being able to throw a anti vehicle grenade and you are GONE), the night should be reduced to 10-15 minutes, because right now, as soon night falls you go afk, unless there is some special "spec ops" mission that you and your friends/clan doing, but those are very rare moments.

-Ranking system - Each war big groups of 20-50+ people switch sides and war is always won if you follow what side they join and fight along side with them. you may think "well 1 or 2 groups is nothing compared to thousand of players" and you are right, but when 10+ groups of 20-50+ members change war sides, and all of them are veterans or at least know what they are doing. well you do the math.

-Leveling system - Levels dont really give you any benefits or advantages, but its always nice to see when someone is a high level, in most games it means that player knows what he is doing. but sadly in this game high rank/level only means you are saving those leveling points to trade them with your friends/clan mates and other way around.

-Player system - If you plan to go afk for short or even longer time(for example go sleep), just hide somewhere and leave your game ON, the next day youll find yourself still in game and ready to go(unless someone killed you and you went to main home page). many players do this because they are to lazy to wait to load into a game and wait for very long queues.

-Steam names and road signs - There are many players that take advantage of this, because its very easy to set your steam name and that name shows in game, and there isnt anything you can do about it, even devs dont care cause there is no "report" system that you can report that.
Second thing is the signs that anyone can build anywhere on the map, you build a nice sign that says something nice, 2-3 people upvote it, then you change that sign into something very bad/rude/rac!st and not even 100 players that downvote it cannot destroy it.

-No anticheat - This game doesnt really need an anticheat because even if you had aimbot, the aiming/shooting mechanic is so "Broken" that even aimbot would not help you to hit the target xD also second thing is you cannot even see a player name that killed you(unless you are downed and enemy player comes in melee range), and in order to report him you gotta know what he is using, what cheat and on top of all that video evidence of him committing what you state he is doing wrong. so there is not much point of going thru all that trouble.



CONCLUSION
All the things written in this review(and many more) have been mentioned before and even discussed with devs in multiple occasions. but nothing has been done about many of this things and no plans on changing them. even devs them self said couple of this things i mentioned will never be changed/improved. or one of their popular quotes they like to say is "thats not our priority". if "Unbalanced teams", "Tedious logistics", "Ghost shots" and most important "Corner aiming" is not a priority. then i dont know what is. but we are still left with a hope they will maybe one day think about improving/changing how some of this things work or think about them if/when they decide to create foxhole 2 or a similar project.\t\t\t\t\t
