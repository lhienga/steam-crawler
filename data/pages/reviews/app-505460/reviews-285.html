\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game a week ago and have been playing it significantly for the past week. I learned the ropes, joined a regiment and was having a blast until recently when a new tech was unlocked on the opposite side. The game's systems and weapons upgrade over time as each side researches new technology. The enemy recently gained access to rockets that generate fire on impact. This fire can be put out with buckets of water. The initial issue was that it took a MASSIVE number of buckets to put out the fire, and little effort to start it up again from their end. Bases burned. It was hell.

Then there was a patch. The patch increased the ability of the water bucket to put out fires. It still takes effort and energy to go grab buckets and pour water on the fire. However, there are still major issues. Not every base is close to a water supply. My understanding is that there are supposed to be wells, but I've been told they don't exist (yet). So, if the base on fire is near a river or lake, you can run there to get water, and run back to help put out the fire. This takes numerous people off the front lines and leads to the 2nd issue. The enemy often has a firing line in range of the only place to get water (often near a bridge).

So, 1) Enemy techs into fire rockets, 2) enemy builds the hell out of said rockets and continuously fires upon bases to set aflame then 3) the flame pulls some important percentage of people off the front to put out the fire (if you're near water) or the base burns to the ground since there's no water nearby.

Fun times.

What used to be a tug of war back and forth every day has turned into one side rapidly taking down section by section of the map.

In the state the game is in, I would advice against playing this game. You are either join the side with the overpowered tech and overrun the enemy too easily (where's the fun in that) or you go through hell for nothing as the balance is not there.

Wait on the devs to fix things and balance the game out further before giving it a try.\t\t\t\t\t

Foxhole is for the most part - the military sandbox MMO I'd always dreamed of that the gaming industry had lacked the tech or interest to achieve. Foxhole excitingly achieves this military simulation with an isometric view somewhat limiting your range of vision. This surprisingly creates quite a fun immersive experience, as you begin to notice how insignificant your combat efforts are compared to the larger war machine. Or vice versa, as hero soldiers, covert partisans & tireless engineers change the tide.

Been a persistent faction war demands persistent players and balance on what a single player can achieve. If communication here is not your type of game you may find yourself extremely limited in what you can do. Grab a rifle, a couple of clips & repeat on the front-line.
