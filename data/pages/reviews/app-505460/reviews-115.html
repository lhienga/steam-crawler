\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort: This game is clunky and lacks a tutorial, but it's a unique concept and a very interesting take on war shooters. Despite there being about a hundred players per area, a single player can make a significant difference, which is uncommon for games like these. 3/5


LONG:

The aiming system sucks. Im perfectly capable of putting my mouse cursor on the enemy and clicking, but even on enemies who are standing out in the open the game decides i didnt click hard enough or something and refuses to lock on, instead sending the bullet way over his head or straight into the dirt 5 feet away from me. There is also not alot of visual or audio feedback for when you shoot someone or get shot. No hitsound, no screen effect, you have to squint your eyes and look for the subtle blood splatters and wait for either you or the enemy to fall over dead.

Navigating the environment can be difficult for stupid reasons. Ive fallen through the floor multiple times, cliff edges sometimes push you out into the open to get shot, sometimes when walking ill get caught on the weird geometry and get stuck until i either get pushed out or die. The climbing system is broken too; sometimes i can climb shoulder-high objects easily, other times i have to shuffle in place and hold the spacebar for several seconds before the game allows me to climb over the shin-high plank of wood blocking my path.

The social and strategic aspects are really fun though. A single soldier can easily make a difference even in a 50v50 with the right strategy, and there are quite a few playstyles for infantry to keep the game fresh. The community is about what youd expect for a world war style military simulator but it was suprisingly friendly, I havent heard a single racial slur or homophobic rant over voice or text chat. Which is EXEEDINGLY rare for a game like this.

UNRELATED: Once i got caught in the open reloading; an enemy ran up to me and tried to shoot me at point blank range, moving around between each shot. He missed 5 times in a row and said "What the ♥♥♥♥!!", as if he somehow wasnt expecting this, like the game wasnt working properly. This epic meta strategy he probably learned about on reddit or youtube had failed him, something about it was so demotivating i logged off for the night. I genuinely felt bad for him, he had given in to the meta, he was no longer a gamer but a collection of strategies carefully crafted by nerds who are allergic to fun and whimsy.\t\t\t\t\t
