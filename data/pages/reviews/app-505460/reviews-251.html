\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Timesink with little fun, complexity without depth.

Pros:
1. The main idea is supercool. MMO warsim with logistics and freeform building, month-long games, reasonable sysrequirements to allow many ppl play.
2. Not much bugs.
3. Inexpensive, often on sale.

Cons:
1. Moment-to-moment gameplay, game flow, controls, interface are piss poor. Nothing is explained well, nothing is indicated clearly. Even world map is less readable than actual military paper map, there is no indication of verticality, 1 km long huge fortress wall is drawn like normal road, most building icons are nearly the same, only 3 colours are used. You can't see what is your character doing or what is his condition. Wounds are visualised only as blood on uniform, you can't check your secondary equipment without opening inventory window, you dont know if you character can shoot in this moment or not. Simply driving a truck is a pain, because you can only see like 20 meters ahead and even touching grass near road drops your speed to a pedestrian walk. There are invisible walls, loading times and queues between parts of the map.

2. Time sinks and busy work are main gameplay mechanics, there is no respect for player's time. Getting to do things after login might take like 20+ minutes. Hours of work can be lost to a troll or second account sabotage. It is expected for certain %% of players to do same monotonouse work all the time every day, to fill logistics quota. Somebody gotta drive that truck with salvage for 5mins to reach Refinery, then 10 mins to reach Factory, then 15 mins to reach frontline, then lose truck with all the cargo to 2 random partisans. Yes, trucks are kept fragile on purpose, to give opportunity for SpecOps. Hope you like to waste hours of your grind just so someone can have fun "cutting logistics route". Organized groups can build whole factories and railways but again, it entirely relies on every member doing one or two repeatable tasks for long time. Some veterans spent hundreds of hours of holding left mouse button, watching ingame objects being slowly built. Maybe they wanted to, maybe they were pressed into that for the sake of faction victory.

3. The game was in early access for years and devs were only adding more of the same. More materials to grind, more weapons to spend materials on, the gameplay never progressed. Of course players are not that stupid, they cut corners and pick only top meta stuff, but then devs just make already existing things more complicated to waste even more player's time. For example, you can build automated armored machingun pillbox with just basic material from your pocket and a hammer but simple sandbag wall requires premade sandbags, produced at special facility from the very same basic material, then transported via truck, then carried on shoulder then built into wall. Can't have too accessible cover, I guess, would be too easy.

4. You got no time for grind? Good news, there is frontline gameplay. Which is like every other combative videogame but slow and clumsy. No srsly, why would I play Foxhole for pure frontline expirience, when Arma and Planetside exist? The whole selling point of the game is about logistics and building and devs got no guts to go beyond generic grind with them.

5. Teamkills, organized report spam and other predatory behaviour are not prevented in any way. If you are a member of tiny team and there are only 2 ppl to back you up, you better agree that big clan, teamkilling your whole outpost, did you a favour. Not that you can do much about it.\t\t\t\t\t
