\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith years of experience now behind their belts, newly named developer 'Siegecamp' has created a unique war game that offers countless hours of gameplay within the Unreal engine. Unfortunately, the game still lacks the proper polish and balance due lack of community engagement and feedback.

Pros
+Player built structures allow for diverse strategic options
+Worldwide 24/7 servers create unique and engaging experiences with other players
+Player-run economy allows for in-depth logistical planning and tactical choices
+Private squad/regiment system allowing you to group up with friends or other teammates

-No proper direct-communication channel with players/vets to improve the game
-Lacking proper moderation and tools to counter alts/cheaters
-Lacking an up-to-date and proper tutorial
-Lacking quality of life game features (notably in logistics; i.e. more automated systems to ease logistics burden in late-game when resource costs are high)
-Lacking planning / communication options in-game
-Best vehicles are locked behind the construction and maintenance of a facility (large base) requiring a massive time investment (making them unavailable to majority of player base)
-Developers removed useful features (i.e. XP progress bar, end-war leader-boards)


What Foxhole offers as a unique game experience is a valuable piece of content for anyone and is worth the price of admission if it's what you're looking for. You'll no doubt leave with some unique experiences and possibly friends, however, you'll undoubtedly be sucked into an endless grind that cannot be maintained due to the lack of supporting systems and player intentions. If combat is your goal, then be prepared to spend countless hours sitting in queues and driving to do so.

I have enjoyed my time with Foxhole, but I have also realized that the game has shifted in favour of relying on a greater time investment from the player. If they decide to improve the gameplay loop, perhaps I'll decide to improve my review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game! Definitly worth the money to buy it in my opinion.
Pros:
-120 v 120 (last time i checked, the server sizes have been increased about 3 times so far! who knows maybe they will be increased again!?)
-huge world space divided into 10 regions, each with they're own unique geography and landmarks, and you can travel
between each of the regions to reinforce, resupply, and invade
-has something for everyone! You can:
gathering supplies
construct defences(using supplies that have been gathered),
manufacture weapons, ammunition vehicles and building supplies for your team
destruction of enemy defenses,(using the weapons, ammunition and building supplies made by your team)
loads of clans to pick and choose from if thats your thing! (i made my own clan(the 2142nd colonial battalion))
-avoid having a crisis of existence!(everything you do whether it be building, supplying, destroying .etc has an impact on the game!)

-take part in planned operations that took days to prepare for! but remember no plan survives contact with the enemy!
-be part of d-day-esque beach landings that can turn out to be glorius hail marys or disasterous failures.

the main game mode, 'world conquest' isnt something that can be won in a single day, most wars can take anywhere from 4 days to 3 weeks(the 11th war was the longest one so far) which present numerous opurtunities to form and execute long term strategies that can require varying levels of planning, coordination and teamwork to pull off! Theres no better feeling than planning an assualt on a heavily entrenched enemy position, that needed days of work to collect the vehicles, ammunition and supplies needed to succed.

-be part of an arms race!( starting with nothing but rifles and the barest of equipment at the begining of the war, fight your way across 10 unique regions to secure the materials your team needs to manufacture the arsenal it needs to win the war!)


Cons:
-The game is still in early access so its not a finished product things can and will change as time goes on
-sometimes the clans become a bit to influential at times(a large clan gets on to play for a few hours, everything is going great! your pushing through the enemy's defensive lines like a hot knife cutting through butter! then the clan gets off for the night and your back to where you started in a few hours.)personally i view this a pro, but i know that alot of people may be turned away by this.

update:

its been a year since my last review of foxhole and i gota say im still in love.
the devs are constanly listening to player feedback and suggestions.
new features are added monthly sometimes even weekly based off of player suggestions and feedback.
clapfoot is an awesome developer, that makes awesome games. Keep it up team Clap Foot Im rooting for ya!\t\t\t\t\t
