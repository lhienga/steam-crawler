\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so it is pretty fun for the right kind of person. You need to be in the mindset that you are part of a team effort and that there will be annoying people. Multiplayer gets that way, and there are a fair amount of people who are particular about how to play. However, it is an extremely flexible game, and can certainly have its appeal for people who enjoy top down 3rd person shooters. There are other methods to play, and the decisions are down to you and your ability to either listen and learn or cooperate with others. There are toxic players, this is a reality, and not an easy one to fix. Players are people, therefore difficult to manage.

This said, the game does indeed have problems, sometimes trucks jump around on the roads, or glitch out, resulting in a logi/transport run getting scrapped in the water next to a bridge. Other times, its the question of how to balance the game to enjoy the most player flexibility without ruining their experience. From roughly 100 hours of experience I've learned a lot about combat, logi, and other things, but there is so much I can still learn. I feel green every day that I get on to play. However, despite this, some players have done so much more and have learned from those experiences. There are intelligent, creative, cunning players who can make or break pushes. Some of the clans are even particularly nice and helpful. Just know this is not always the norm and to be careful about who you play with and hang out with, as they are at the end of the day going to approach things in a unique manner. I do think some players take it all too seriously, but at the same time, if you want to win, you have to work for it. As someone who was Warden for the Relic War, war 69, 70, and now 71, I can confidently say that while pessimism may be a problem sometimes, others we are all too focused on fighting our opponents to be too bothered with our grumbles.

There are some other problems I did not cover, such as the aim and view system being a little rough on smaller computers, or the somewhat clunky learning curve (as without the assistance of other players to train you, you will fly blind). But, these are things the community actively seeks to improve upon and allow new players the chance to enjoy the game. It has a lot of work to do, but it has come a long way from the game I viewed coming into the last Relic War. The polish is coming on, and it seems to be like the fun will continue.

Soldier on, and hopefully have a good time. 7/10

In Callahan I trust,
DJazzyThorn
Isawa Guard Regiment
SSgt (as of posting)

P.S. Would recommend new players who would like to try out the Warden Faction. They're an interesting bunch, and we have our moments. Best regards, and good hunting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoy teamwork based games and I love the concept of Foxhole. I've had it sit in my steam wishlist for a long long time before I pulled the trigger. The community is great, from what I've seen. Kind of like the Holdfast community, if it were on Xanax and helpful.

I love the ideas behind Foxhole and the teamwork and logistics that go into helping your side win. But, I absolutely can't stand the combat. It just feels...off. It's clunky and forced, I just dislike it. It feels like an old flash game from ebaumsworld. I've only played two hours, but the honeymoon period wore off within the first 30 minutes. I play a lot of grindy, drawn out, games. But those two hours of game play felt like two long years of incredibly repetitive game play. No matter how much I like the idea behind a game, if I don't have fun actually playing it, I can't recommend it.

That said, I love the idea, and a lot of people enjoy the game. It just isn't a game for me.\t\t\t\t\t
