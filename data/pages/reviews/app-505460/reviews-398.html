\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWould not recommend

Fun at first, logistics is fun, front-line shenanigans is fun, artillery is fun, shooting is MEH, building is slow and boring but has fun elements to it and theory crafting the "perfect bunker".

Community is very, very toxic and will call new players alts easily, drama is meant to happen
Clans (Regiments) is necessary part to have a level of organization and some can be fun, but drama between regiments is always there in some aspects. (Of course, majority of encounters are positive, but there are huge spikes of toxicity daily)

Mistakes are not allowed, will be reminded of those until the end of times.

Faction balance is non-existent, some of this goes well with the "Mass produce, lower quality but higher quantity" idea mindset but not having any of the tools available that the other faction has is completely unforgivable for "World Conquests" that last for 20+ days.

Also the faction loyalism has gone too far, neither side can really come to an agreement on where balance should be, both see themselves as the weaker faction always, playing on the "Other side" will cause you to be some-what un-trusted member of your faction.

This game has future, but currently all devs are getting done is burning out their player-base.

Edit1: Also to note, I started this game on Shard 2, which was far more enjoyable and relaxed experience where you would make rag-tag groups of people in order to achieve some goals. Far more enjoyable experience than on Shard 1, where it un-ironically is salty vets and clanmans, and every new player is blamed to be a shard 2 player, even 5 wars after shard 2 closed.

Edit2. With the launch of 1.0 the devs have only made the issues of dev balance in all levels of development clearer and clearer. I strongly recommend not getting the game - and if you do play as Wardens as everything you do as colonials would be like throwing a water bucket to a building ablaze. Speaking of fire, Warden fire rockets which colonials can't use are the most broken ever. What a perfect analogy.\t\t\t\t\t
