\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love foxhole and some of my most memorable recent gaming experiences have come from it but all at a cost sadly. The concept is very unique and whilst niche, fills that niche perfectly. The art style is superb, top down view is really enjoyable even coming from someone who's always used to playing FPS's, the teamwork and comradery is nearly unmatched with some wonderful players in both factions but in its current state I wouldn't recommend it for people who aren't already deadset on buying it. As many other reviews have addressed, alting (the process of bypassing the faction lock by purchasing another account and then using that to destroy the opposing faction internally) has become a rampant problem and there is little to suggest any actual dev response to it. Spending thousands of collective hours to build a tank, base, operation setup ect can be very rewarding when it gets pulled off but its increasingly more common to find alts go and purposefully destroy all that prep in the span of 1 hour with no repercussion. Toxicity also exists typically with people who have sunk so many hours into the game that they feel entitled to do anything however this is at least personally less of something I have encountered. Most it comes down to a few individuals or clans which do everything from mass report friendlies for now following specific orders to exploiting. Then you have the balance which at the current point in time is very lackluster with some egregious cases of guns or vehicles being leagues better or far more effective to winning wars than others which results in a serious faction inbalance problem that will become more noticeable as one plays the game. Whilst this will always be a point of contention as very few people can see past their own faction and thus never acknowledging the problem, it never the less exists and has only seemed to get worse in recent updates. This isn't helped by the fact that dev communication on the topic is almost nonexistent and so people have just become really demotivated to do much. Bugs also plague many parts of the game from minor but annoying ones such as getting stuck in flat terrain for no reason to server desync resulting in you being thrown far off the road whilst driving or game disconnecting losing an entire days worth of work in a moment with the worst being the many nuke (an end game weapon that can change the tide of a war) bugs with some being stupidly simple exploits to others being completely overpowered bugs that people can literally just accidentally trigger by playing the game allowing for the course of a war to be changed simply by a lack of polish on one of the most game changing features present. As I am not the most proficient in logistics I will not comment on the state of that but many others have highlighted complaints with it that need to be addressed as logistics is the core backbone of the game with everything deriving from that working.

In the end foxhole is a great game with many potentials from people who want to do nothing but make goods and ship them around to frontline to artillery crew, firefighters, medics, tank commanders, train drivers, builders, snipers, grenadiers and the list goes on but despite all of its unique and compelling attributes, the current negative state of the game has overshadowed them making it a lot harder to enjoy the fun parts of the game without having to curse all the bugs, exploits, balancing issues and lack of communication that come with it really taking away from what is at its core a beautiful one of a kind experience. If you already like the look of foxhole then new players are always welcomed and I hope this review doesn't turn away aspiring players but more just informs them of what they are signing up to and maybe to hold off a bit until things hopefully get a bit better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love the idea of this game. I love quite a bit in general about this game. However with love, comes hatred. In my 1k+ hours playing this game I've developed some thoughts on it.
Some Pros:
+The massive battlefields will always have a special place in my heart. From the tidal waves of bodies hurling mammon grenades at structures to the tank lines 20 deep on both sides blowing everything in between to kingdom come.
+As with any game with clans, you can see a group of individuals truly accomplish the impossible in this game. The well oiled machines of the community have bore fruit on both sides of the battlefield in ever amazing ways.
+The fact I need to put in labor to see my efforts blossom is a reward that not everyone will understand. I personally love the idea of facilities and such that players can create and use. The same goes with the logistics as a whole.
+A fair bit of effort can be seen in certain hexes around the map. That effort should be recognized and applauded.
Some Cons:
-Most if not all hexes have what I refer to as wasted potential. Relic bases with seemingly no strategic point to them that simply serve as another place to capture so the map matches your factions color. They're rarely built up by the players and ultimately just become wasted space. I'm sure the developers will eventually address this issue. Another side of this is poorly designed areas that are vital to each faction. Yet serve as little more than meatgrinders at any given war. Mercy's Wish has to be the greatest example of poor design. The fact that no defenses can be built around the vital storage depot remains a mystery to me.
-Builder and logistics QoL improvements are a requirement at this point. Many players burn out when they take on the role of building a defensive structure that can withstand the onslaught of the enemy faction. A random invisible tree stump can ruin hours of planning as can the sudden inability to build a structure in an area previously able.
