\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is just addicting as all shizz. It's easy to pick up, what you don't understand can be easily learned via asking for help or Youtube videos, the community isn't bad at all as some reviewers say (I play in the Colonial faction, haven't done Wardens yet so that could also be why.), there's a role for everyone and something for everyone to do. Builders, medics, snipers, artillery, infantry, tanks, logistics (supply runs), intel, miners. The vast amount of tools and weapons at your disposal is just outstanding to from different rifles, submachine guns, lmgs, mounted hmgs, AA guns, mustard gas, grenades, gas masks, flak vests, shovels, etc. Like literally everything you've seen in other world war II games as decor that you could never use or build is useable and buildable in this game. Every structure you see (save maybe for the main town buildings) is built by the community and this game LIVES AND BREATHES from the community. This is not a game for strictly lone wolves, while you can do some things like getting intel or a few kills by yourself, you NEED your brothers in arms to help you significantly affect the battlefield enough to capture territory as you are not going to destroy a whole enemy base by yourself. Not a "probably won't destroy an enemy base by yourself", it's a definitive you are not going to.

Beware: This game is highly addicting and war is non-stop. If you can't handle it or know when to call it quits, then do not buy.

Otherwise get this game, there is no other like it and it's bloody ♥♥♥♥♥♥ awesome war!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne day, I'm gonna write an essay about this game - but for now this'll have to do.

Foxhole is one of the most unique and intriguing games I've ever played. Playing Foxhole is like being part of an ant colony - every player does what they want, but almost every player is working together to achieve the same goals. Down to its core, Foxhole is a cooperative game, and the mechanics work together in a beautiful - if at times flawed - concert to incentivise players to collaborate. You want to command a tank? Well, you better get a crew together - you'll need a driver and a gunner. You'll also need fuel, ammunition and materials to repair the tank when you get hit; you'll need a plan, and infantry support in case the enemy comes running at you with a sticky grenade.

All those things come from other players. And because those things come from players, you end up talking to people, making friends, even if they're buddies you only share a foxhole with for five minutes. Even if your faction is losing, you're having fun because you're talking to people, working with others toward a common goal.

Finding your place in this big world - as a soldier, medic, field engineer, scout, grenadier, gunner, driver, factory worker, artillery loader, quartermaster, whatever - might take some time, but you can try many different roles and have many different experiences. Eventually, you might find yourself leaning into a little light roleplay, too.
