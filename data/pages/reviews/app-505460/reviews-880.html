The game during this time was based on weekly basis that let the small playerbase to rest for week and play. The mechanics had players rely on corner aiming to get extra vision and shooting range, which is a bug. The weapons were a matched set to the other faction same for vehicles. Players manually produce a small variety of tools for others to use. This genre has buildings mechanics to setup defenses that relied on big walls and AI controlled tower-like emplacements. This time of the development not very intensive learning curve. However this was to change in the future.

After playing 1k ingame and additional 500 hours into the community based Teamspeaks and Discords. My perception of game has plummeted. The building mechanic begrudgingly difficult as slight mistakes change result in permanent structural health decrease and it can be freely edited by others that can permanently affect it, requiring players to completely destroy their installations. Weapons are now split into 2 factions with varying advantages and disadvantages that counteract each others. This applies to vehicles as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought that this game was an interesting concept so I bought it and spent some time learning what you're supposed to do. Watching some youtube tutorials and twitch streams and trying out the different roles. Ultimately I felt that it needed a lot of dedication to really get into it and you're going to have to do some grinding to do your part and it just felt a little... flat. The odd fun bit with some team battles and some helpful people around, but I wasn't seeing too much in the way of overall fun and excitment.

Other people who put lots of hours into it seem to enjoy it and maybe those that like a slower paced game within grinding will really like it. However, I was pretty torn whether to carry on playing or not.

I tried again and then experienced some griefing where the enemy was camping the home base spawn point from on the roof of buildings (major advantage) and being semi-abusive over the global chat. It doesn't make sense to me why people get pleasure from getting kills from a near invincible position - in any game, but some people do. A measure of intelligence usually.

So, anyway, I didn't mind spending the money for the devs who tried to make a game that's different, but I wouldn't really recommend it to my friends. Even though I've given it a negative review, it's different enough that if it sounds interesting to you, give it a go or maybe wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
