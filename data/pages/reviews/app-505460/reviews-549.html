Between the connections made with other players and the amazing design behind this game, it creates a community that not only supports newer players (with other players running "Training Camps" and etc.) and manages to make humor out of a traumatic experience like being stuck in a foxhole and being shot at. One of the greatest features of this game is having people run Logistics, or 'Logi', where a player farms resources to turn it into weapons for war. All in all, one of the greatest War games in general.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR AT END

I got this game some time ago, when it caught my eye. "Persistent World War" sounded cool, so I got it. I was immediately blown away. I played it for a bunch, then put it aside for a few months. Fast forward to now. I started playing it again one night, and next thing I knew, it was morning. I repeated this cycle for many days, losing large amounts of sleep. This game is fantastic! The concept, the execution, even the community (I play on the Colonial side) is incredible. Of course you will run into a few ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ here and there, but from my experience they are few and far between. The game has something for everyone. Whether you want to fight on the front lines, run logistics from hubs, or fire massive artillery pieces there is something for you. I personally prefer logistics, but grabbing a rifle on the front has led to some of my favorite memories from gaming. No matter what you choose, this game is a blast. I would recommend grabbing a few friends and forming a squad, but the community is so awesome that you might just find you leave the battlefield with a few new friends. The only problem I have found so far is that in the war I just participated in, the Wardens greatly outnumbered the Colonials leading to a Warden victory. Either way, the game was beyond fun to play, and I highly recommend it to anyone reading this (admittedly long) review. You will absolutely not regret it. See you on the front lines!
