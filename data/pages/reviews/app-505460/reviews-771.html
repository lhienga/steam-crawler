\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love this game and it paid for itself multiple times from the fun I've got from it.

It's a must have if you're a fan of war games.


Pros:
[+] Very immersive. You're sitting behind a sandbag, in the middle of the night, with your friend. You both see almost nothing and only hear sounds of grenades exploding nearby and Howitzers trying to hit your defenses. You try not to waste too much ammo as enemies storm your line of protection in an attempt to take over the factory. Gas is flying everywhere, you hear some other guy shouting for a medic. Just trying to hold the frontline for another day as your team on the other side of the map is taking over yet another region.

[+] Multiple playstyles - you can be a medic risking his life to save teammates, partisan storming deep supply lines of your enemy with your buddies with SMGs, artillery operator measuring distance with binoculars and then shooting a Howitzer from afar, engineer building defenses, logistics guy bringing supplies, barge captain bringing soldiers to flank the enemy, factory operator taking care of supplies production. You'll never be bored,

[+] Awesome, complex, player-driven mechanics - if you disrupt enemy supply line, they will eventually starve out of shirts (respawn points) and with few men to defend the outpost they will eventually fall. Logistics is a multiple stage process and if your team messes it up, everything reacts to it accordingly.

[+] Fortifications keep a good balance between camping and assaulting and provide realistic frontlines. It's hard to take over a trench where the enemies are comfortably sitting but when you do, you're the one with the advantage. And then as you go further down the enemy territory, you have to make your own bunkers and foxholes or it will be easily retaken.

[+] Big map, many different environments (city/hill/mountain/open combat), day and night cycle which makes every skirmish unique

[+] Realistic - you can die within seconds, gas kills almost instantly, you can die out of nowwhere from artillery, you don't see what your teammate see and at night you see almost nothing. It may sound harsh but it's actually great and allows you to make plays you never thought you would.

[+] Constant new updates, devs reading forums and Discord



Cons:
[-] First few hours are hard and you don't really know what's going on

[-] Reporting system abusing, if you get reported 3 times you get a timeout from using weapons or joining regions, so seriously don't piss off any Chinese squads or you'll have to rejoin after changing your name

[-] Region queues, not much of a problem during the week but at the weekend it's horrible, you have to skip it by making a long walk from the neighboring region or doing logistics

[-] A little buggy, you rarely find bugs but when you do they're very frustrating\t\t\t\t\t
