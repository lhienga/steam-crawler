Maybe after 1.0 it'll change. But as the things as they are right now, i would not recommend the game to anyone unless they are looking for something specific.

First of all. there is no personal progression/reward system. for a game that requires your investment, there is nothing personal reward that the game gives you. remember the french invasion? it fizzled out because there is nothing in place that helps the player be personally invested in the game.

Second. combat is lacking or not intuitive, handling and movement in this game just feels weird, maybe its because of a lack of a proper tutorial but i digress.

Third. there is a certain way that others get a advantage, is is through tweaking the resolution, this advantage should never exists in the first place. this just tells me that the developers never though about balancing the technical aspect of the game.

Fourth. doing the logistics part of the game is just plain boring, you literally just hammer away at a object to get scrap. that is not a fun game play, if you have no investment in the game then there is no personal reason for you to scrap anyway.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had a lot of fun with Foxhole the first night I played -- ever since then, I have regretted my purchase.

This "game" is very tedious. You will spend the vast majority of your time running back and forth from a spawn or being berated by other players. Everything takes way too long; just to get a basic rifle kit takes about ten clicks (with a wait in-between each click, no queue for you) and around 2 minutes of your life... repeat this 20 times in an hour, waiting 40+ seconds each time just to respawn, and then another 3-5 minutes running back to the front, and you will have just begun to scratch the surface of this exercise in tedium.

The various "roles" this game advertises are just a continuation of this exercise in tedium. Building, Scrapping, Logistics -- it's all incredibly bare bones, all of these different "roles" are just clicking left-mouse-button over, and over, and over again. There is a near-complete lack of functional UI (including a missing "friends list" for an MMO, truly head-scratching), and overall the game feels like it was designed to punish and impede the player. There is very little fun to be had "unless you're in a good clan", which is a terrible argument, as any terrible game can be made fun with a good group of friends. Also, the rubberbanding that is omni-present throughout the game makes enduring the tedium an exercise in tedium itself.

The whole game, from UI design to its various gameplay loops, feels so incredibly quarter-baked that I find it astounding that people enjoy and recommend this game as much as they do. There's no functional tutorial, many of the systems feel obtuse, there is a lack of fun gameplay but PLENTY of tedious waiting -- bring up any of these points in-game, and you best be prepared for a game community more toxic and judgmental than Rust.

Foxhole: Great concept, but poorly implemented as a fun game. This is supposed to be an immersive war / job sim, but instead it plays out like a old Clicker Game from Miniclip most of the time.
