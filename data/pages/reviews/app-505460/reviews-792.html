\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a very ambitious game. Not simply just a top-down shooter, it incorporates heavily the supply chain side and building. You can be a simply infantry, spawning at the front line and then fighting (and dying) the enemy directly. You can be the supply chain, driving materials like weapons and ammo to the front. You can be the engineer that builds defences, buildings, or at the rear echelon you manufacture the goods necessary for the effort. There's so much to learn, it's a little daunting. The world is persistent and enormous, and to me that's it's flaw. It's lonely. Unless you always spawn at the front and play as infantry, you're probably not going to engage with anyone.

Since the game is so large and so complicated for anything other than infantry, there's too many tasks and space to simply get lost in. What am I doing? Why? Should I dig here? What happens if I throw up a line of fences blocking the road that leads to the front and parked locked vehicles to stop my own team from advancing? Not so much as griefing, but there's a lot of players that are just wandering around doing random things, probably clueless or just trying to figure it out.

Perhaps when (if?) this massive project is built further, it'll be a better game. But, at this point, I cannot recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
