\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game with provisions.

If you're looking for a game that is going to hold your hand and tell you everything you need to know off of the bat to do everything, this isn't it. At least not at this point. There are many videos online to help you get your bearings, the Devs have linked to some of them, but ultimately you're going to have to learn many things on the fly, or by doing them, or by asking other players.

This game is one of the best large scale player-base War games I've ever seen. The whole premise of the game is one massive War set in a ficitious setting but is heavily based off of WW1, you pick one side either Wardens or Colonials, and you essentially are trying to help your team conquer the whole map across many different regions in a persisent war or singular matches on smaller scales (The results of which in and of themselves also can benefit the War).

I love how there are no inherent classes to the game, a player can do literally anything any other player can do, and the entire thing revolves around TEAMWORK and coordination. Everything from logistics to actual military manuvers within the game, you cannot accomplish things effectively in this game without coordinating with other people and making plans among groups of people.

A player has to harvest materials so that they or other players can then research and build gear, weapons, vehicles, or buildings and so that they and other players can then utilize them in the war. This method puts a tremendous amount of value behind lots of the late-game stuff because its not easy to field a whole brigade of Tanks or to construct a large outpost to help seige and capture a town or fortification. You need a lot of people all working in concert, and this game offers that in spades with its large player-size for each individual regional map.

The player is a cog in a larger series of cogs, so to speak. So if you're looking for the game where you're running around being Rambo or BJ Blascowitz and just shooting up people like you're in an 80s action movie, this isn't the game for you. This much more of game for those who care about working together with many others to accomplish a goal. You have to be prepared to listen to others as much as potentially lead when you become more confident. You need to be willing to be a worker as much as a soldier.

If you don't find it fun to potentially be working Logi for several hours and making stuff for the troops or the base, you may not like parts of this game. But nobody forces anyone to do anything, its entirely at the discertion of the player. (Though one's team can suffer from a lack of Logi)

Weapons varieties are good and allow for a variety of personal specializations for different situations and manuvers, but all of it is the operatives of the players, there is hardly any AI in this game at all.

Research has meaning with how long it can take to develop and then actually build the stuff, and thus are encouraged to be used purposefully rather than willy-nilly.

I also highly recommend one uses Discord, the in-game mics are decent, but they also have good Discord channels.

This game is also still in early access, so the devs are still improving and adapting and implementing things, but I think they've done a hell of a job so far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9/10 - Easily one of the best Steam purchases I've made. Worth the $20 asking price.

This game is built around team work, thus relies on its community. It has a very cordial and cooperative community which will quickly teach you how to play, regardless of whatever role you choose. Roles are not defined, therefore one may be an engineer and then immediately grab a rifle and seek combat at the front lines. As stated before, the game is based on team work. To build the fortifications, vehicles, or weaponry, one must supply materials. To supply materials, people have to gather the supplies from scrapping and scavenging. It all comes together to create a constantly changing battleground that ebbs and flows depending on not only the combat methods used on the front lines, but also the supplies and logistics.

I highly recommend this game and am looking forward to seeing how Foxhole progresses in the future. I only give it a 9/10 instead of 10/10 because it still has room to improve, therefore I cannot give it a perfect score - it is in early access after all. Bugs do exist.\t\t\t\t\t
