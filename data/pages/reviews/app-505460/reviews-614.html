\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I have come to love this game and this game's player economy implementation I recommend giving this game a pass unless one with even more love than me wants to fund this game. At least until full release if it comes close to addressing my many dislikes about this game that outweigh my few likes.
I got the game thanks to the summer sale and a youtube recommended video. Marketing done right or lucky free advertising, I don't know.

Likes:

1. The massive player economy where half are fighting and the other half are the resource gatherer and produces in a "typical RTS".
I personally have fallen in love with the logistics system and will rather certainly continue to explore it.

2. The vision system that the in-game player characters are given. You only see what that tiny character head sees.
I dream of this system being in 3rd person shooters and the end of 3rd person peeking without the use of character's head.

Dislikes:

1. The vehicle stats are imbalanced.
Example: the off-road truck is slower than the "pack mule" making it very useless in my logistics runs. It needs a speed buff, I would want to see a 2x off-road speed buff for it if speed on road remains slower than the "pack mule".
They will probably tweak these stats more until full release.
Have not manned many combat vehicles so can't comment on them.

2. The in-game vehicle physics and terrain can be buggy. Have personally died to such bugs. This needs work. On some bridges the game disables the player's steering.

3. I got harassed when I tried tech running even after giving up the resources.
The system needs more decentralisation in my opinion. Each person should have an individual and character saved tech progress bar towards prototype kits, next to the global tech progress. Lower point kits can be cashed out if not selected but needed. That way tech running is still possible, but those who want to can connect their personal logistics line straight to tech.
This progress may decay or be lost upon teching up when found to be abused.
This way players who have little time to play, next to work, can still, over several real life days, access the tech side of the game.

4. The game has so many mechanical numbers which I would want to be available in a in-game "foxholepedia" or a manual
This knowledge should be accessable in-game, not just on the internet
This way players have something to read when waiting around for something/someone and not just sitting behind the keyboard.

5. The tutorials on the home island need more detail fluidity, much more. They are not doing this game and it's depth any justice.
It is important not to overwhelm the player but in this game there is not much of a choice. KISS: keep it simple stupid, but provide players the details when one asks.
Some quick-travel between training sites would be nice. That way players can learn quick and jump in-game quicker.

6. My personal introvert dislike for the simplistic/cheap use of discord instead of building a proper in-game chain of command voice chat.
Also sound bugs in local player chat were a thing for me.

7. The AI defences are too spammable. There should be at least one average road width between AI mannable defences of one type.
It is easy to spam the AI without remorse. There needs to be more thought put in defences, not just AI spam.
Some bunker parts feel pretty unused, except for a standing platform, due to AI spam.
Right now only a big group with proper equipment has a chance when facing spammed AI defences.

8. The game world relies too much on bridges/bottlenecks to dictate the flow of battle.
Because players can build defences along the entire shoreline, battles mainly take place across bridges. Especially early war/tech. This is a meat grinder stalemate which is extremely unfun.
This could be mended very easily if indirect fire/artillery weapons were unlocked from the start/very early game. These weapons allow players to create opportunities to break through.
The other breakthrough option is the tank but that I think should stay for later in the war.
Even after all that I personally think rivers should be used more rarely and the map needs a rework, but I believe it's too late for that.

9. Various truck horns not satisfying enough. They need more powerful sound.
Also "pack mule" high rev engine sound and idle engine sound can be heard together when on a logistics run.

10. In-game map needs more detail. Some stuff blends in and only local recon gives a proper picture.
I recommend using real life topographical maps as inspiration.

Neutral comments:

1. Needs better point and statistics system which each player can view. This is mainly to motivate logistics players and help them min-max.

2. In game traffic flow should get tweaked to make things easier for logistics personnel.
All items that block the road should only be player made. There should be no indestructible world items on the roads.

3. The game needs a third faction. To make the things even more interesting.

Overall it is a typical early access game. If a player likes the concept, one will but this game, regardless of what I say. In my opinion this game's main draw is it's huge player war and economy. Numbers efficiency and their proper coordination wins. There is very little individual play here when it comes to combat.

Now I will go to pretend this game is top view Euro Truck Simulator with combat somewhere about.
What else do you expect? I'm a support player :)\t\t\t\t\t
