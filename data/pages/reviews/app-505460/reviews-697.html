\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat idea for a game, made real by a talented team with no vision for the end result. Backtracking on several selling points over the 4 years of early access development and with no end in sight the future for Foxhole looks bleak. It will release eventually, resembling nothing what made the game great when it was released to the public.
Don't buy it, unless its priced less than a sandwich.

The biggest failure of Foxhole comes from Clapfoot's inability to understand the basic concept about resource based warfare and the failure to integrate it into their beautiful open ended overworld map. Instead of having manageable groups of players doing various activities stretched from supporting logistic routes to skirmishing behind enemy lines, the patch/update trend is towards funneling all players into the 100-300m thick front line areas to fight in a massive deathball. As a result the world map feels empty, running logistics is for the vast majority tedious, lonely and uneventful. Fighting at the front line is mindless and grindy.
Its a shame as the groundwork is there for an interesting, player driven trench warfare game that gets buried beneath prioritising"anti-toxicity measures" (that cause more community infighting) social credit scoring systems and reliance on the tried and true MMO grind.
As is, Foxhole is just another skull for the early access abuse monument.\t\t\t\t\t
