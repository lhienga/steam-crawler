\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the game's massive war it creates for you to put yourself through its gauntlet, the community is the only thing holding this back from being a thumbs up for me. There's toxic communities everywhere you play which is simply the nature of gaming these days to expect, but then there's abusive ones like Foxhole.

Some of the worst community I ever seen and continue to experience with the level of abuse from all stages through early war and late war with a broken report/ban system that is consistently abused throughout all theaters of either Logi, Infantry, or Arty.

Regiments and clans are spotty and few, with ones that have great guys and fun fights even fewer as they are all nothing more than wannabe generals always scheming large scale ops that never work out, ending in a collapse of several folks blaming and cursing one another and enough team killing to end the war faster for the adversary.


If toxicity was all there was to thumb down this game, it would indeed be an insult to blame the game based off of community action alone. However, the developers continued refusal to address the issue of the abusing report system done to ban players with no legitimate reason because any attempts to contact the developers on this matter either in discord or email will swiftly be taken down and a reprimand will direct back to you, meaning contacting the devs in regards to this will get you targeted by them too.

Until this cycle is broken and we have a change to how we can prevent unauthorized bans onto players who don't deserve it, and punish those who abuse it, I cannot support this game by recommending it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine, if you would, a very funny top-down War MMO with logistics, factories, all that jazz. Very fun game on paper.

But then, you realize that games like this are a prime breeding ground for terminally online losers who require the validation of others in the form of clans. Simply put, piss off a collection of these losers and you're banned from the game, essentially. The moderation in this game is the form of mass voting people off, and the devs picking up the pieces in the forms of tickets submitted to their help desk. A clan can ban you for a few days, and your only recourse will be an unban that will occur in 1-3 business days.

However, this process has improved greatly as of late. Serial mass-false-reports tend to be banned more often now (See the case of "BadManLarry" in the Foxhole community), but there is still much room for improvement. New players tend to be targeted the most by clans because they are suspected of being alt accounts, and older players who tend to piss off clan men get mass reported the same as well. I would recommend this game under 2 conditions:

1. You're willing to join a clan, willing to be bossed around, and willing to be a punching bag for certain people who haven't found a way to manage their anger issues, and take it out on newer clan members. (Of course, you'll have clan guys who say "We aren't like other clans!!! we're different!!!!" but as someone who's spent hundreds of hours trying out various clans, they tend to be all the same.)

2. You're willing to deal with the fact that you may or may not eventually piss off a certain clan member, who will then go to his discord or squad chat with 10-20+ people to have you banned off the game, forcing you to make a ban appeal and wait a certain amount of time before you can return.

Otherwise, the community makes what could be a really fun game, an absolute cesspool.\t\t\t\t\t
