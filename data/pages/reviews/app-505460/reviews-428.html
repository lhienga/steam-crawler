\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a well-developed game that is unique to others that I have seen and played; with over 1000+ hours played, I can say this with confidence. Over the three years I have played, it has changed in some ways for the better and some ways for the worse. But overall, I believe that Siege Camp, formerly known as Clapfoot, has done a well-done job at improving the game and making it more fun for the community… or at least for me.

I would recommend this game to any person, and I have been recommending it to friends for a while now. I will continue to recommend this game to others, even if I stop playing it, as long as they know two things:
Firstly; the game is a work in progress, so it is not going to be exactly perfect. Some things are tedious to do or very “grindy,” mechanics such as Logistics. But other things are more fast-paced in the game, so there is a balance. You just have to find your balance to keep it interesting. I found mine was Logistics (slow) and Partisaning (fast); frontline was a medium.
Secondly, the community can be the greatest tool and the deadliest weapon used against yourself; it all depends on where you look and who you ask. I found that some people are willing to help you wherever you need, in-game and even outside of foxhole (like other games too).

One example of the community is a great tool (in the game) is the Colonial SIGIL, (Side Note: I have played, almost, equally as Warden and Colonial. And I have been in WUH, SIGIL, WON, WM. I just think SIGIL was the biggest impact of positivity for me), in there I found that there were a lot of players that helped newer and older players alike. SIGIL organized prewar meetings and even had some operations that were for “Randoms” and “Clan Members” to participate in. From my experience, I found I was not treated greater or lesser depending on whether I was in a clan or not, which is a big factor for me when choosing whether I should be a part of certain communities that are in the Foxhole Community. Another example (outside of the game) for me is being a part of the Foxhole - Hearts of Iron IV community, it has been wonderful with players helping with ideas, lore, etc (https://cdn.discordapp.com/attachments/750578543397961895/927458081019932702/no_info.png). This is something (somewhat) related to Foxhole, but not the game itself, yet some have helped in so many ways which show how the community can be a great tool for aid in other projects and even just for support.

In terms of the community being the greatest weapon used against yourself is if you do something like grabbing “techmats” and “going against the vote (https://cdn.discordapp.com/attachments/750578543397961895/927456270989017098/Crime_-_Copy.png );” in some cases, clans or groups may try to “witch-hunt” you or force you to join the other side because you disagree with them. I understand that this may be rare for players in the community, or that players try to deny this stuff happens, but it does. So anyone new should know that it happens out there, and most of the time it is for the stupidest stuff.

Anyways, overall I do recommend Foxhole as a good game that has a good community and good developers. I believe there is work to be done in terms of the game and the community with the Developers and the Players working together in these areas to make a better game. Side Note: I do not think players need to take over development or that the developers should take over the community, but I think some aid on both sides would be good; like, the players coming up with letters for the developers for improvements in the game (stuff already happening), and the developers communicating with the players on how things should work on the community side of things. Anyways, whether you agree or disagree, this is my opinion; and my experience from playing the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBackground:
I have over 500 hours logged into this game over the course of the last 3 wars. The first 2 wars were on Shard 2 and the current war is on Shard 1 after the devs folded Shard 2.
PROS:
1. The people you meet in the game are what drive the game. If you find the right people, you'll spend so much time in the game that the bonds you develop will span past the game itself. They're friends, 100%
2. There's a lot of variety to the game in terms of roles you can fill. It's an incredibly good macro simulation of war. You can't win without logistics providing gear. You can't win without artillery. You can't win without armor pushes. You can't win without infantry to hold ground. At the macro level all of these things are balanced and integral to the war effort. Add on top of that the small unit tactics and the grand strategy of inter-regiment operations and this game has a lot going for it.
3. Related to 2, your avatar is just some nameless soldier for your side. There's no skills to develop or levels to gain. As soon as you spawn in, you can immediately jump into combat.
4. There's enough difficulty in the player skills required that you will spend 100s of hours mastering the various crafts. Each weapon and vehicle has its own strengths and weaknesses to learn.
5. Everything you do makes a difference for the war effort whether you know it or not. Every person adds or takes from the war effort in their own way. I've seen offensives get halted completely by one well placed artillery barrage.
CONS:
1. The player base is absolute ♥♥♥♥ as a whole. I know I said the players you meet are a Pro, but the player base itself is toxic, especially on the older Shard 1. Regiments will camp the resource spots and actually SHOOT anyone who gets near it. The phrase "clan man bad" comes up a lot in the World chat. As you may have seen in other reviews, some regiments have zero interest in helping new people. They have zero interest in working with other regiments. You either get out of their way, or you get crushed. This absolutely kills any sort of inter-regiment operations at a minimum, and can even bring the strategy to a halt. Couple this with the clear population differences between the sides and it's no wonder that the smaller population side basically gives up on Day 3 of war that could last up to 30 days.
2. The game itself has almost no story at all. This isn't necessarily a bad thing as it's most similar to multiplayer PVP games, but this is a con because of #1. Siege Camp took all of the worst parts of MMOs (the grinding and the PVP) and made a game just out of that. Because there's no story, you're left wondering why you're here and what to do when you first drop into the game. Which leads to...
3. The learning curve on this game is STEEP. There is a basic training area on the "home island" that will take you through movement/shooting, artillery, vehicles, watercraft, building, and logistics. You can go through the basic movement and shooting training in about 10 minutes. That's enough to get you to the front line and shooting the enemy, but not enough to know you're too inexperienced to do that. The other training areas are woefully inadequate for your needs. You're basically required to watch Youtube videos or you might find helpful players if you're lucky. Couple this learning curve with the logistics requirements and it's no wonder that you have LTs (the game gives ranks based on player commends) yelling at Private's for trying to take a tank to the front lines. And no wonder. Logistics is both the most and least appreciated part of this game. Most appreciated because timely logistics deliveries to a besieged base makes you a hero, but least appreciated because the amount of days required to set up those logistics lines is unknown to the average player. I've seen people calling for an entire front worth of tanks and other vehicles in one order, which would take several days of building to get it. Logistics is a huge grind to the point that the logistics players are considering going on strike for quality of life improvements. Which leads to...
4. Remember when I said "clan man bad" shows up in World chat a lot? So does "dev man bad." The devs have a clear and obvious bias towards the Colonial faction with regard to tech. The Warden tech is supposed to be more expensive but higher quality while the Colonial tech is supposed to be cheaper but worse. It's a difference of quality vs quantity in this case. Except that's not how it works. There are certain combinations of weapons, vehicles and game bugs that make the map a PVE game during the low population times for Colonials. On top of these bugs, the tech tree and the map resources for the game changes with every war. Yes, every war. While this can make for some fun tactical challenges at the map level, the tech tree change is asinine. For example, there are 3 different levels of artillery pieces in the game, 120mm, 150mm, and bunker-based 300mm Siege Cannons. The tech tree as of this writing has 150mm cannons in the very last level of the tech tree. Wardens get the 300mm in Tier 8, while the 150mm comes in Tier 12. Why, devs? Why make the middle tech the last tech? Another example from this war is the Warden antitank rifle. It should generally show up somewhere in Tier 3-4. This war it showed up on Tier 7. In comparison, Colonial anti-tank weapons had been available since Tier 1-3. Why make the basic Warden anti-tank weapon 3 tiers past its use? This doesn't even get into comparisons between the newly released LMGs.
If you like grinds, play this game. Otherwise, find something else.\t\t\t\t\t
