But, keep that in mind if you plan to purchase Foxhole - there are players (most of the regulars, actually) that will look upon any new players with disdain and treat you with hostility. The "veterans" are a tight-knit group that HATE new players, they have very little patience and their general attitude is: "if you are new, and don't know what to do, then go play something else".

Oh, also be prepared to wait 10 minutes (sometimes more) for a 1.1MB update to actually install. It makes almost no sense, but I can only imagine it has something to do with how the game is compiled (I do not have this issue with ANY other game, just Foxhole - on BOTH my rigs).

All in all, your money is better spent elsewhere if you are looking for a game with a welcoming community and cohesive gameplay.

If you are looking to just drive around in a stolen truck, for an hour, so that you can bash scrap piles with a hammer, for an hour, then return with your hard-earned resources only to be shouted at by some idiot veteran who needed something else and "why didn't you miraculously smell his needs through the air" then by all means, buy this game.

Seriously though, you would be better off buying a piece of wood, sprinkling some sugar on it and watching ants run around.

Graphics - hgaf/10

Sounds - poorly mastered/10
Gunfights - stupid character controller camera angles/10
Developer interest in the community - Only YouTube vets count/10
Updates - wait for a different game to be made by different devs/10
