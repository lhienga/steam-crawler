\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, nothing quite like it. It's a war MMO with a focus on logistics - all weapons, ammo, vehicles, artillery, shells, spawns, buildings etc are created by players and brought to the frontlines to be used. Community is mostly great, lots of good vibes. It is an online game, so of course there's some nut jobs here and there.

Can be played solo - 90+% of my time in game has been a solo player. Even better with a couple of mates, as vehicles like tanks need 2-3 players to use effectively. Joining up with other randoms works just fine too.

Wars last around a week to a month, though the current war has been going about 40 days and nerves are fried for all, hence the current saltiness of recent reviews. It's also a pretty close knit community, so drama can get out of hand pretty quick, best to simply ignore it.
Very rarely see cheating or griefing, definitely less than any other online game i've played, but it does exist of course.

Mostly works well, can be slightly buggy here and there. Might lose connection when crossing to different zones for example, but it's (usually) quick and easy to get back in where you were.

Can also be pretty grindy if you let it be - some players do nothing but grind for resources for example and rarely if ever fight. Some players do no logistics at all, and only fight. Play it how you want to play it - if I get worn out fighting and dying in the meat grinder that is the front lines, I might do some logistics for a while or move to a different front, or perhaps sneak behind enemy lines and disrupt their logistics. Lots of things to do, make your own missions.

Worth the price, but watch some videos of it before committing, it's not a game that everyone will enjoy - there's no power fantasy here. Death is fast and brutal, which makes teamwork essential and victory sweet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is an exercise in wild and contrasting opposites.

It's a wonderful example of the glory and beauty of hundreds of people playing towards victory. It's riddled with griefers and alts who are capable of undoing the work that took you several days in less than a few minutes.

It has a level of complexity in warfare that can only be matched by factorio-like games (Yes, that's right, it's a factory game disguised as a war game). Yet all of that gameplay is accessed by less than half the playerbse, probably less than a third of the playerbase if I'm quite honest.

It's a slick and complex top-down shooter. And at the same time the devs seem to add new toys without rhyme or reason, while nerfing in a seemingly haphazard and careless way.

It's glorious and horrifying all at once, which I suppose is a philosophical statement about war in general. And I cannot go far enough in recommending you steer clear of this thing.
