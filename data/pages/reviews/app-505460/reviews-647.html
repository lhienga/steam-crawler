\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I have played for 16.5 hours and I like it.
First thing you should know is it is top- down play. You pick one of two factions, other than colors of uniforms and equipment the game seems to be balanced.
Second thing you should know is the "war" can go on for days or even weeks... real time. So you will not jump in and destroy the other team and secure victory in one play setting.
Third thing: Everything is player built. From guns and bullets up to the main line battle tanks and ships. It is all built by the other players in the game.
Forth thing: In order to build you need to harvest resources. This means going to the rear and pounding on piles of scrap or sulfur rocks or fueling a oil pump. That's not all you need to transport it to where it is needed. This role is called "Logi" and it time consuming but very important. Nothing worse than reading the chat and your team is getting wiped out because the don't have ammo- because no one has made any and brought it to them.
You spawn in as a fresh player to your "Home" area. This is a tutorial where you run around and try out all you can do in the game. It is very informative and lets you try everything out. After you log out you are returned here and this is where you deploy from when you are ready to enter the game.
You do this by going to the deploy area and pressing "E". This brings up the world map and you can see where all the action is. One side is blue and the other green shaded representing the areas each faction controls. You will see squares that are flashing and you zoom in and out for details about the areas. Just click on a square and you enter the war at that location.
If you are on the front lines, head to a forward command bunker and gear up (All this is covered in the tutorials)
You can also choose to help build the production side- your choice.
The game has a small feel but is huge in scope.
The Devs seem to be active and care about the game.
It has no store or micro transactions which is a plus.
You can chat to players in game or use the built in voice comms for nearby players.
If you enjoy games like Albion Online you may enjoy this one.
Lastly it is in early access and is 20 bucks but will cost more when released. Expect some bugs but other than getting stuck in the terrain once I can't think of any.
Only a few you tube channels feature "Foxhole" but are worth looking at before you buy it is kind of a niche game. Hope this helped- Enjoy!!!
