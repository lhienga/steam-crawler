\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game during the latest sale. Reviews far and wide said the game's fairly slow paced with an emphasis on logistics, and oddly enough that sounded right up my alley. I guess I'm one of those guys who's most comfortable playing support in games and doesn't like to clutch hectic situations. So, I had high hopes going in.

About ~2hr in I was bored to tears because there just wasn't much going on and not much I could do in the meantime. I guess after the latest update the play area has been expanded into a massive interconnected world map, so the player base is spread pretty thin to begin with. I did manage to find a skirmish after a while, but just one death later I was down to using a pistol because there was no more ammo for any of the other weapons.

No problem, I thought, this is where the logistics part comes in. I will take up that mantle and make sure my team is well supplied! A couple youtube videos later I figured out how to collect raw resources and turn them into building material. To turn that into ammo you need to build a factory, which requires even more material. To gather materials faster you can use a truck. To build a truck you need to build a vehicle factory. Screw it, I thought, I'll just gather it by hand, the resource node isn't THAT far away.

Well, turns out the resource node can be depleted, and apparently it can take OVER AN HOUR for it to regenerate. It's my understanding that this was a deliberate addition in the latest update to cause resource shortages. To slow things down even further, I guess.

This game does a good job of making war realistic. Not everything in war is quick and exciting combat, there's also the tedious, boring and mundane parts. They really captured the boring.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun with friends, but otherwise a real drag alone. You really feel left out as a "random" in the war effort and the big clans in foxhole make you out to be the worst type of player in foxhole. The fundamental issue is that they succeeded at making a war simulator with frontlines and logistics but when people realized how boring war simulation is they left the game or ruined the war effort by wasting materials constantly trying to fight the enemy instead of launching a coordinated attack. There is never enough people doing logistics(delivering materials to bases) or resource mining because its some of the most monotonous crap in this game. There are only a few people who bought this game to drive trucks around or press left click on a resource for 30 minutes but its one of the most central parts of the game. The learning environment is ok, there are some people that are willing to help you but most of the people in this game are toxic af and will run you over with a truck if you dont know what you are doing. The worst imo are the clan leaders who help you out and then endlessly harass you about joining their clan and sometimes getting pretty toxic if you reject conscription. Most of the clans arent looking for players and so it doesnt happen often but the clans that are and just want more members will pull this type of on you.

It is really fun to around with friends though and you also wont be damaging the war effort if your friends know what they are doing. 2 people can run a mortar crew or howitzer crew which can be very effective and also lots of fun. You can also make a tank and hop in with your mates and blast people provided you have a good driver that knows when to get out of a bad situation.

All in all, really fun with ur mates but as one player this game gets very boring and very toxic quickly.\t\t\t\t\t
