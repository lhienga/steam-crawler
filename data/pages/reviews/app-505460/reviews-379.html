\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't think this overhead isometric game was going to be for me. The idea of having to talk to other players ceaselessly to get anything done was just not particularly appealing. I finally decided to give it a go, since this genre has precious little coming out recently, and holy smokes, how wrong I was. All of those things are, in fact, required, but it's not bad. The community isn't pro gl0b0h0m0 (generally) so everyone is reasonably based, team oriented and it seriously reminds me of Ultima Online but without the gold economy. Every bullet, gallon of gas and vehicle is made by players all working together to not just farm the resources or make them, but to then deliver them, load them and fire them. It's kind of amazing that it works at all, to be honest with you. If Ultima Online "make your own fun" gameplay ever appealed to you, then somehow, this WW2 themed game is carrying the torch. I've head it described as a "sandbox" and "sandbox" in my opinion means "wastes your time with no permanence". Nothing turns me away from a game more to realize that the 16 hours of grinding I did in a base is wiped out next update or one corrupted save later, for no one to ever see or appreciate, but this... this is so much more than that. In fact, I am quite eager for the current war to end, and to restart with no tech, and see what the current generation of players can do. Give it a shot! Fair warning though: You'll spend the first week asking questions, and since it's not full of touchy feely NPC's, be prepared to have the crusty vets yell at you for basically everything. Grow a pair, however, and this game will put hair on em. Trust.\t\t\t\t\t
