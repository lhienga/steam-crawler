\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf Foxhole had a redemption arc, it would be this Arms Race update. The main problem that plagued Foxhole's war economy - petrol scarcity - has been solved by allowing vehicle to use either petrol or diesel (which was how it was originally; if it ain't broke, don't fix it!). As an icing to the cake, the update also gives unique vehicles to each factions coupled with a new researching system. All of the sudden, massed vehicle battles that were gone now came back, giving back life to Foxhole. Still, there are many kinks needing to be fixed. The building decay rate is still too resource and time consuming, the prototype and mining-based tech research rate still need to be ironed out (pun intended), and of course there's still raging debates about the unique faction vehicles' balancing, among other problems. But all in all, this update did the impossible and made me return to the game that I swore to throw away for real\t\t\t\t\t

---{Thoughts}---
This game is truly a great game. A ambitious game for sure, but they manage to pull off a good top down shooter with a massive amount of the game focoused not on the horror of war, or the 'epic guns blazing' style either. Rather it puts a focous on the logistics of war in a massive multi section map. Often the best team doesnt have the best shooters, or the most poeple (though it helps), often its the team with better/more logistics players, and while many might think that is a bad thing becuase 'nobody will want to play the boring logistics' thats actually not true. Entire clans have formed around being logistics and engineers. Simply mining materials to fuel the machine of war. Often these clans hord a pile of materials and then make a massive push over a bridge or make a massive naval invasion over sea. Not to even mention the fact that the devs constantly talk to the players in their officila discord chat and even take some suggestions. Honestly i love this game and a great game to play with friends.

9.5/10 Great quality for a small developer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTldr: It is what I expected of top-down world war mmo. It provides the intensity depicted in war movies during combined arms fights. Aside from combat, logistics is fun to certain people that enjoy the mechanics.

4.4khrs, still Col
With the return of cross faction voice chat, its gotten better but the tech is still wonky. I dont use a mic but appreciate the roleplayers and hope more people participate. The other game i am playing at the moment is Battlebit, I wish a mod team is present to moderate like the Battlebit mods.

4.2khrs, Col
With the recent surge of negative reviews, I do believe the game needs better updates as it is after all a 1.0 and out of early access. But i would still recommend this game as the first 100hrs is still an experience like no other atm.

2360hrs. still a Maj.
Now a neutral that plays the perceived losing side to join epic fights at end war without queue. Will stick to collies for update wars. I find myself enjoying the game more even if the team gets pessimistic while losing.

1715hrs, Maj.
Played several wars with wardens. I really like the weekend zergs that are the signature of the Warden faction. I even typed Callahan propaganda to convert collies. Several wardens i met are cool and i enjoyed playing with them. Will now switch factions after every major update.

1586hrs. Capt.
War 49, Saved a BT in Loch Mor (Day 371). Joined wardens for a couple of wars. To be honest, its because I am doing less Logi that I am willing to use resources. The flow of combat with many things to do and consider, made the resource-conserving mentality i had as a collie to using resources "efficiently" because its warden-made. Full-time scrapper to combat-proficient Capt, I believe i made some progression towards that goal. Its like the moment I solo-ed 2 ptes with a pistol that makes its a fundamental change.

1.2khrs. Capt.
Still hammering Salvage. The consistent updates are interesting to bring top-down logistic-based war to gamers. With new tactical equipment like Smoke Howie shells, I wonder if bayonet charges are now a thing. I look forward to a bunch of friendly kills amidst the smoke. I admit skipping updates that provide a singular item but I am always back for new chunky updates to avoid burnout.

1k Hours. Level 19, Rank: Lt.
Recognized most vets and some recognized my ign as well. Skipped 4 wars but War 30 is the most fun i had since Nukes were introduced. Cant wait for next war as the new players are getting more experienced and have the ability to help the vets out.

#Trains2020, #Planes2021, #Submarines2022.
With the newest update, featuring the Logi update, scarcity became an issue. Securing resources became the main game. However as with the surge of new players, they often die alot or unknowingly steal resources. Hostility from veterans rose and new players will feel outcasted. Resources are a real problem as people wanting to experiment with them but lack the experience aren't given the chance to.
The Huge world map made tactics interesting again as diverting regions away from chokepoints to assault other regions is observed by the major clans and vets utilize strategy.
I am excited about the possibilities of new transports thus the hashtags. (Update 26)

#NukeJadeCove Again!
16 Dec steam sales, with more new players than usual due to the awesome 0.20 update featuring Main Battle Tanks and Rockets, I joined Fisherman's Row. As a skirmish map that are not considered to be part of the main combat regions, no veterans are playing in it. I was the only OCDT with another inactive in team chat. So, i took charge after seeing how logi requests get ignored, portbase and its stockpile empty. It triggered me so bad that I HAD to do something about it. What if these new players join the combat regions? How huge will their impact be on them? After hours of screaming at them in team chat, they finally abandoned the Sandbag forts and build real defences. It was an experience being the only one to coordinate such an inexperienced group and I enjoyed it. (330hrs in)


At 33 hours of play, I can guarantee that what the Hypey steam reviews describe about Foxhole is true. Attack and defence is tense when I do not have a clear idea of the number of enemies and friendlies that are in a firefight which can be solved with watchtowers. With experience and time spent on this game, people recognize you for your efforts and give you a commend, which gives you the required levels and ranks up the military ladder. Higher ranks do not provide any additional perks or stats that skew combat to vets. Although with experience, as a Warrant Officer, I did 1v2 two Lcpls with a pistol once.

Winning two wars against Wardens is the best feeling ever. The push to regain territory while we were pushed to port bases was freaking awesome. The big clans took over and basically gathered all solos to join in the great operation of smashing wardens (4 Oct 2018).\t\t\t\t\t
