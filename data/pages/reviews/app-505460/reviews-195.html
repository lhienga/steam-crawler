\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWar experience in Agile* way.

You start as a soldier, you die like a soldier, you gain experience as a soldier. In Foxhole you play as a single man that contribute to your faction (Wardens or Colonials) to steadily gain land, victory points, bases (predefined or player-built) or any advantage - including manufacturing supplies or helping progress in technology.
The most basic experience is frontline-like - from the base you spawn you take gun (be is simple pistol, a rifle or more sofisticated ones like launchers or mines) and go to the front. You kill enemies that can give tactical advatage to overtake some base (which needs to be destroyed if opposing faction got it). If you die - you spawn after short time in chosen base (as long as supplies last).
Another 'job' is widely regarded as production - you take your hammer (or later - use a harvester) and gather basic materials. These are to be refined (yes - you are right - in a Refinery) and then used to produce all that this game holds - guns, ammo, building materials, mines, soldier supplies, medical supplies, even tanks, field guns or trains ('big stuff' has longer production chains tho).
Next one is 'Logi' - you take a car (a truck is most common) and deliver all this stuff that was produced by others - to the frontline. You need to communicate and gain some knowledge what and where to deliver to let your guys be supplied enough to repel enemy offensives. Logi guys have chat channel of their own! It's a very important role.
If you want to help in more 'peaceful' way - just try to be a medic. You will either patch wounded fellow soldiers or revive fallen ones. Medics are highly regarden on a battlefield - they let others continue whatever a frontliner is about to do, saves time and resources. This 'job' can easily be very addictive one and many players regard this as their specialty.
Some players tend to specialize as 'bulders' as the game has a variety of structures (both defensive and industrial) implemented. As easy as it may sound, this 'role' also needs experience and insight to plan the base to make it as best defensive-wise or industrial facility efficient-wise.
Last but not least - you can try to be a partisan. After you will learn about a game, map and mechanics enough, you can try to work behind enemy lines and disrupt their logistic routes, steal or destroy produced stuff or just make a trek to annoy enemies with your presence.

The game has some lore in it, but personally I do not bother. The unique thing is that everything in this game (apart some specific exceptions) is done by the players, for the players and used them to actually fight the other side. No one will tell you what to do - just try whatever fits you best and excel in it for the benefit of your brothers in arms.
Some specific concepts are:
- player decides how to contribute - based on his/her/.. preferences, needs of the moment, ... - that's something that reminds me working in Agile way - players choose 'tasks' and try to fit in roles
- graphics are not 'pink sweet' - this is all about war and you will find rather harsh-looking landscapes, reminding rather real life battle scenes
- third person view may recall some games like Commandos - for better or worse - personally I find it for the better as it does not affect negatively my emotions as FPP of being killed
- sound may seem 'modest', but - believe me - in this game you need to hear if there's vehicle coming or enemy reloads in nearby bushes - this is somewhat unique and gives nice coherence without distraction
- the game implements voice comm - something that is invaluable on a frontline; if you add several text chats you will get very good focus on communication - something that makes war going your way
- collaboration and communication is the key - even if you are partisaining, your intel means a lot for rest of players; you will surely find some friends there, I can assure you!
- as an ending note - player will soon experience that production, logistics, organization and medics are as of much value as frontline fights - even if we learn of 'brave soldiers' whatever war or period - nothing will come out of this if not an army or supportive echelons of people in the backline

*Agile is a methodology widely used in IT sector and may be trade mark of someone, not sure\t\t\t\t\t
