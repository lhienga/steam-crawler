\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a similar problem to ARK:Survival Evolved, but I'll get into that further in the review.

Despite all the fun concepts in this game, I cannot recommend it to any new player whatsoever. As stated many times in other reviews, the ban system is very broken. The community is self-policing and given the ability to literally vote ban anyone they choose, so, if you happen to catch the wrong guy at the wrong time (it happens more frequently than you'd think), he and his entire regiment can just report you until you're banned, and keep repeating the process until the ban is permanent or incredibly lengthy in time. Aside from the ban system being broken, there is also one major, glaring problem: Elitism and "vets". This game has rare moments of being welcoming to new players at times. But, just about every regiment that I know of basically uses new players for slaves and cannon fodder while they themselves get to enjoy the fruits of everyone's labor and use the more advanced weapons/tools. Joining regiments is a joke. This game suffers from the same exact problem that ARK:Survival Evolved does. You're joining into an elitist community of armchair general neckbeards who take this game WAY too seriously. A lot of the older regiments have zero upward mobility and a preset rank structure that, chances are, you won't be allowed into unless you brown nose everyone above you. I compare this to ARK because the same issue exists there as well. It cannot be overstated that this game is full of "vet" players who are mostly elitist and will not view new players as even remotely equal. Understand this: If you join as a new player, you are highly likely to end up playing solo, or, joining a regiment that doesn't respect you. I've seen few, if any, exceptions to this. I'll quickly add as well that trying to start a new regiment is virtually impossible. The older clans recruit everyone they can under the promise of equality, but use them for grunt work with no reward, which makes recruiting difficult. No one wants to join a new regiment and build it up, but they'll join an old regiment and be treated as a disrespected slave by "vet" players who need some sunshine and probably a bath.

Simply put, veteran players and their toxic elitism will hinder the enjoyment of a new player, and if you're really unlucky, you'll probably get banned instantly because you didn't follow instructions from some elitist armchair general neckbeard having a meltdown. These are not exaggerations by any means.

Another quick point I'll make: 2 factions to choose from. One faction is heavily favored by devs to the point of bias and is generally given some of the best weapons and vehicles in the game, while the other faction is insanely quick to mass vote ban people and generally rally together in some kind of weird, cliquish, clannish mentality of tribalism and have been known to attack each other in game out of greed and hatefulness.

So, gameplay wise, the game is great. But, community wise....yeah, unless you're the kind of person who enjoys being demeaned on a daily basis, talked over and silenced by "vet" players who think they're General Patton, and generally perpetually viewed as a new player despite the fact that you've got 100+ hours in the game, I cannot recommend this game to anyone.

Go play Running With Rifles or something else. This game isn't welcoming to new players, so I'd leave it to the salty armchair general neckbeard elitist types who run this game into the dirt.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHands down, One of the greatest video games I have ever played. This game is so deep and complex it is impossible to explain the many things that make it great, you just have to play it yourself. If you are looking for a war simulation where you can literally experience every facet of war that you so desire, this is it.

The game has greatly changed a handful of times due to developer design. As the maps and game grow in size, it becomes increasingly hard for a single player or small group to feel they are usefully contributing to the war effort. The scale of the map can be intimidating to new players or to old players looking to complete tasks that were, at one point, much easier to do.

Regardless, this game is very much worth it. The absolute perfect game for a lonely, boring night. I have sunk hundreds of hours into this game. A must play. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
