\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole features large scale combat/war in a small scale isometric package. It is one of those games that looks really cool, is really cool, but is really hard to learn how to play. It features a simple interface, basic 3d graphical look, and solid (if awkward at times) gameplay
Final Score: 7/10 - Overall, I enjoyed the game and intend to play more of it. I would also recommend it to others as it is an enjoyable experience once you watch a tutorial video to figure out the basics.
In the game two opposing armies face off for control of a large island/continent. They battle with guns, tanks, mortars, grenades and a plethora of other weapons and vehicles. Every item a player uses in the game is made by another player and the logistics of moving equipment and ammo around the map becomes a huge part of the gameplay. . The next closest game in terms of themes, concepts and feels to this that I’ve played is Planteside 2 in that it features a large map with players fighting over strongholds and points of interest.

Pros:
-solid game design
- simple clean graphics
- functional ui
- fun concept
- enjoyable combat/gameplay
Cons:
-The isometric design of the game has a rigid camera that limits your ability to look around the map. This creates a sensation that your character can only see 60 feet in front of his own face which is unfortunate in a game with guns.
-My biggest problem was actually figuring out how to play. Many elements of the game are far from intuitive and very difficult to grasp
-At times the movement was difficult to control (especially while crawling)
