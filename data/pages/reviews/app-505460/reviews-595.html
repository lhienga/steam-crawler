\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is fun and inviting during the first hours as there is so much to learn and do. It also has a great promise of the perpetual war and everyone having a place in it; from soldiers and tanks to building bunkers and detailing logistics. In practice though this is only true if you get in with the right group of people.

Playing solo after a few hours and it becomes a slogfest of repeatedly dying because the loudest squealer on mic says suicide rushing is the best plan. Thinking about running back-of-the-line logistics? Perhaps drive a tank? Well forget it as most anything outside of being infantry is controlled by the larger clans and the discord cartel. You will be accused of being a spy, have your work destroyed, and be team-killed if you are not approved.

It is a relatively true combat simulator of trench warfare: An hour of riveting gunfire, explosions, and bodies everywhere, followed by hours of boredom staring into the darkness looking for the enemy. This goes on for days and neither side has gained really any ground.

Add in really archaic and unintuitive camera/shooting controls as well as systems that literally need volunteers running in-game boot camps to try to explain them to newcomers.

I enjoyed the time I played and feel I got my money worth, but I can't honestly recommend this to someone unless they are fully aware of the caveats.\t\t\t\t\t
