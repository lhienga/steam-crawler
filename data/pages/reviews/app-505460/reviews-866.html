\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI pulled on my tin hat and joined the battle zone.
I quickly realised that I had no clue what I was doing there so I walked off to gather some supplies needed for the war effort.
After gathering materials and creating crates of rifles, ammo and medical supplies a voice asked me if I wanted to come over here and join them.
“This is how porn starts” I said
“No, no just come over here…..it wont hurt” the voice replied.
“This REALLY is how porn starts” I said.
After following them I found myself a driver in a halftrack, receiving many instructions on how the game works from other members of the operation.
We formed a convoy of halftracks and trucks and proceeded to the front line. This is where I claimed my first kill.
An allied soldier, for a reason I will never know, run out from the side of the road whilst we were travelling and ran straight under my left track. There was nothing I could do, no time to react, the track just chewed him up and spat out his lifeless corpse. It did cure the squeak though so a small bonus there.
As we drew closer to the front, I could hear the sounds of battle and I began to feel dread for the upcoming contact.
We formed a line with the halftracks and charged the enemy. With my gunner spitting deadly fire we continued the charge, cutting and sometimes even running the enemy down.
In the end an RPG took us out and the attack continued as the world faded out.

In this game everybody is an important cog in the war machine, be it front line soldier, logistics runner or material gatherer. All people work together to win the war. If we do not work together the enemy will.
My experience in this game is that there is a steep learning curve but there is a great community of players in the battles that will help you and give you guidance.
The controls are simple enough but not overly simplistic so that you can have an easier time picking up the basics. You will know enough to put a tin hat on and head towards the enemy and your first contact will tell you all you need to know.
The graphics are just about right for this type of game, I would say think Company of Heroes kind of style in the look of the game.
The sounds are just right to me and you will only hear voices of other players when you are close to them, excluding operational chats. The sounds of battle grow as you get closer to the source of the battle.
Before you scoff at those who are gathering supplies, running war materials to the front. That rifle in your hands, those bullets that you are firing and that medical kit that just saved you were all made by a fellow player.
War has never been so much fun for a very long time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do not recommend this game at this point because it was not enjoyable to me based on my experiences as a new player.
I have little play time in the game.
My comments and insights would only be applicable for new players.
If you have 100's of hours in game, I suspect you will have a different view on the game.
If you are considering purchasing the game consider the points listed below:

PROS:
1. The graphics seem ok and the game runs smoothly on older equipment.
2. The controls seem to function well enough although I question the use of wasd as the movement scheme.

CONS:
A. There is little or no explanation of any of the systems or how they work. I was offered the ability to repair a lot of things only to be told that I didn't have a thing to allow it to happen. What the required 'thing' missing was never identified. I was unable to get crafting to work.
B. 1-2 shots will kill you so you will die in 1 or so seconds after coming into range of another player. Players in this game can be effectively invisible by hiding in a random shub. So you walk along, hear/see 1-2 shots and die after spending 1-3 minutes slowly walking from the main base.
C. WALKING....You will have to walk from the main base to whereever you need to be. You walk because as a new player you don't have access to trucks. All trucks sitting still are locked. New players will spend a good amount of time not playing but rather walking.
D. It is like Planet Side 2 in that you will run into all skill levels. This is not wrong but you are at a huge disadvantage as a new player. Everyone has the same weapons, however, they have some idea of what is going on. Actually, It appears that there is no point to trying to get better weapons because you die and leave them on the field. Most people (virtually all) appear to take the 'storm' rifle. I saw one heavy machine gun and one rocket launcher. Everyone else was just a rifle or a tank.
E. You can't 'see' what is going on so that you can learn from others. There is no ability to zoom out. You will only be able to move the cursor to a screen edge to extend the view in that direction. The maximum viewable area is about the range of a gun. I got the feelling that the game is mostly blundering around with only a general sense of where actions were occurring because no one can really see any significant distance.
F. No minimap overlay....I didn't find a way to have a mini-map overlay so movement was a series of walk stop, check map, walk. Not efficient. A minimap overlay would be better.
G. There are no symbols for players on the map. You will only be able to see fortifications. You will have only a vague notion of where anyone is. This will mostly come from the running chat box which reports that this or that has occurred as a specific location. If you find the location on the map, bing, you know that somewhere around that point something is happening. Now you have to slowly walk there.
H. TASKS.....I joined a few 'events' or 'tasks'. I was supposed to hold a bridge. There were no arrow to show the way. Also, most of the country side was blocked by walls with gates. So the process was a series of wandering around looking for gates in walls to get to the place I was actually to support. Never made it because a shrub killed me. I then joined a mortor crew. Again, no way to know what to do. No information on screen or on the map. When I arrived at the location (this happended to be on the main road). There was nothing obvious happening in the area.
I. The developer said that one design choice was to make all of the larger weapons depend on more than one person to operate. This sounds good but I wondered how this would turn out based on my experience in Planet Side 2. In game, it turns out that most of the weapons just sit around because there are no teams. I walked past bunkers, machine gun pits, artillery, etc. sitting silent. Players ran right past because they were apparently pointless. It requires two random players to coordinate after finding each other and realizing they you both want to do the same thing.

The developers says they update the game on a monthly basis. I may try this game again when I am bored. Maybe some of the above will be resolve in an upcoming patch.\t\t\t\t\t

The voice chat makes communication very easy and fighting over a few cities gives you an emotional connection to them. This emotional connection and longing to win the war makes people recruit people for their front line, teach new player how to play, take roles such as a medic or builder or soldier on their own accord and make friends that they can coordinate operations with. This game is truly and very naturaly driven by the players.

Even though this game is in Early access there is a ton of content and with more updates coming I couldn't be more excited.
