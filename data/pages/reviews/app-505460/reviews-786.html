\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe cold, night air was filled with smoke, the sound of gunfire, and the desperation of my fellow squad members. The Mara encampment we captured on our own was destroyed and we were scattered among the ruins running low on ammo. Separated from the squad, I ran into the second floor of a house as I could hear their screaming in the distance.
"Medic!.. They're coming in!.. We're outnumbered!.."
I could hear my squad yelling. Yelling as each of them were cornered, letting out a final war cry followed by an exchange of bullets, then silence. I peeked out the large hole in the wall overlooking the street toward the smoldering encampment. I couldn't see anyone and laid prone hoping no one would see me.
Suddenly 2 figures appeared in the street. I took my finger off my carbine's trigger when I saw it was my squad leader and one other, running from right to left.
"Run! Run for the jeep!" he yelled as he fired his SMG behind him and bullets flew over their heads.
They ran until I couldn't see them anymore and their pursuers came into view. I held my fire knowing that doing so would give away my position and the soldiers would find me.
There was a distant "Drive! Go! Go! Go!" as the jeep came speeding back the way they came. They were laughing at how they just narrowly escaped death. It only lasted a split second as my squad leader noticed a a familiar silhouette in the second floor of a bombed out building, looking at them as they drove away in haste.
"Wait! We left DubMeister!" he shouted. His words full of guilt and remorse. He had just left his squad's newest recruit behind to the mercy of dozens.
The sound of the jeep's engine faded away. I closed my eyes. Our allied reinforcements from the West weren't coming. There was no coming home.
I could hear the footsteps, and see the figures of the enemy running around in the courtyard. Their voices started to fill the still burning air congratulating each other over a job well done. Trucks followed suite for reconstruction.
A voice echoed over theirs in my head as clear as day. The words of my drill instructor from the home region "If you surrender, you're a coward and I'll shoot you myself."
"This is it then." I said. "One last stand."
I reloaded my carbine with a full clip and took a deep breathe. With sheer instinct to fight, I crouched, took aim and fired twice.
1 down.
They panicked and ran for cover. I could their voices.
"Oh ♥♥♥♥! There's one more!"
"Carbine on the Second floor! Take cover!"
The gunshots made some of their words inaudible. Some were drawing out pistols and firing desperately up at the second floor whilst in the open.
I winced as a burning sensation hit my gut. Shrugging it off, I continued to fire, not relenting once on the trigger.
2 down.
Another sensation hit me from behind and I landed on my back. I saw one of them standing their. "Clever pig." I was bleeding out as they gathered around me, giving me praise for my brass.
"Clean kills." they said. "Nice."
"Worth it." I replied and smiled as my eyes closed.

I've seen alot of negative comments about the community but almost everyone I have played with has been super cool. This game is sort of like a better (IMHO) 3rd person planetside, set in a fantasy world somewhere between ww1 and ww2, but with no class restrictions, almost limitless base building options and a resource driven supply effort that truly captures the feel and cost of industrialized warfare. The team effort that goes into a big push leaves you with the feeling of having been part of something bigger then yourself, and lasting feelings of "I was there." Hats off to the DEV team for real, this is the game i have been looking for for quite some time, and its only getting better!

A brief story of my first encounters in fox hole
after a very costly advance across no mans land I found myself pinned in an enemy trench with 4 other players...the last survivors of a forlorn charge. We were running low on ammunition and gas mask filters... Every man that tried to reach us was cut down and our medic could only watch as they bled out within feet from us... yet we fought on... suddenly heavy artillery began raining down on the enemy position destroying their forward trenches... a number of our men finally made it to us... after a quick resupply, our squad leader organized us, let out a whistle blow over his mic, and over the top we went....

10/10 would die gloriously for the cause again.\t\t\t\t\t
