\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly I would recommend this game to everyone.
It is very fun.
You can switch your play style however you like.

You wanna have more chill gameplay? Do backline logistics - mine resources, build facilities with your friends, produce everything from bullets and weapons to tank.
You wanna have a bit more thrill? Do frontline logistic - haul produced goods from the backline to the frontline, look which regions is running low on what, check for partisans on your route and weigh your option between the faster or the more secure routes and get celebrated as you bring in the much needed ammo, medical suplies and materials to fix tanks and buildings.
You wanna have action and kill wardens... I mean enemies? Go to the frontlines, fight in the trenches, kill enemy tanks, or man your own tank with friends or random players.
You wanna have even more thrill? Be a partisan - sneak into enemy territory, disrupt their logistics, destroy equipment before it even reaches the frontline, destroy whole production facilities or just sabotage them sneakily and have the enemy look for hours why their production isn't running, because you destroyed one of their power lines.

Now you are wondering: "That sounds like a fun game for everyone - solo players, coop with friends, coop with strangers. Why the negative review?"
Well, let me tell you about alts and cheaters.

First the obvious - there are cheaters out there, which simply circumvent mechanics like limited sight at night, which conveniently lag every time you shoot at them or which are able to fly over or run through structures.
And then there are alts - players who have a second account on the enemy side and use it to grief, spy, give equipment to their side, screw with your fabrication, block logistics by putting stuff in the way or disable your massive defences by parking trucks in front of your automatic guns.

Let me paint a scenario:
You have built a small facility with your friends, gathered resources over multiple days, traded with other groups to finally build a big battle tank. What could go wrong?

Alts taking your resources along every step of the way and just load it into a truck and driving it into a river or into the frontlines.
You would have been okay if the occasional player uses your buildings to refines their resources or even take a bit for their own needs, but now you have to make private stockpiles, lock everything away and start guarding and shooting at your own ppl, because they could be griefers, but often you just kill a curious new player who was exploring.

You dealt with griefers, so what else can hinder you building the beauty of a battle tank and having fun with your friends killing some wardens... I mean enemies?

Well, innocent looking player from your faction with binoculars look at your facility and stalk out where you store your resources, where your important production buildings are, act as if they are newbies and look exactly at how much stuff you have in your stockpiles.
And a few minutes later a single partisan or a group of them sneak along the borders a few hexes into the backlines and make a bee line to your stockpile, destroy it and leave.

So now you build start building defences - place pill boxes that shoot at enemies, build trenches and bunkers, haul tons of resources just for building them and pay their upkeep and shoo away and shoot at every player that comes even near your facility.

Just so you can be finally save and have you facility survive just the last few hours until the battle tank rolls from your assembly pad.. and finally you are done. You see the majestic curves, the smell of fresh oil - there it is. Now you just need to call your friends back from mining resources, put ammo and fuel into it and drive it to the front.

As you do suddenly you notice a hauler parked right in front of your defence. You wrench it open to check which newbie parked it in the most inconvenient place ever and drive it a bit further down the road. But as you enter the truck you hear explosions coming from your facility, you run back and you see a single partisan, which ignored all the overfilled stockpiles and important production buildings and went straight to where your tank was waiting for it's first action since its birth.
And you only see a smoking husk, which just despawns as you arrive. The partisan is quickly dealt with and you can still hear their spiteful laughter as you think about how you have to tell your friends that your weekend of driving you snazzy new battle tank was ruined by a single person with 2 accounts.

Your friends and you are now close to rage-quitting the game, but you decide to give it another try, you schedule your vacation time, use another week to gather the resources and are organising guard shifts among yourselves and finally you are sitting sleep deprived, but happy on your way to the front in your new battle tank "Betty II".
You dodge the conveniently abandoned cars on the road, the 3 ambushes which totally random pop up exactly where you are driving, despite you first driving on smaller not often used roads and then even completely off-road over the ripening fields of corn, seeing the raging farmer in front of your inner eye.

You arrive at the front, talk to other tank crews, are celebrated as the reinforcements everyone was waiting for, form a tank line, barely survives the enemy infantry concentrating their hail of AT-RPGs and anti tank grenades on you and there it is. The sound every tank driver dreads... the ringing of the bell of a bicycle and just as you are pulling back to fix the holes in your formerly pristine battle tank, they park behind you and for some reason a single bike is even sturdier than a reinforced wall... No screaming and cursing at the lowly Private on his bike helps, the enemy pushes forward despite it being dusk and you, your friends and Betty II the battle tank explode, sucking in their last bit of fresh air - ultimately killed by a bicycle.

So now you find yourself typing an angry report of this player and his bike. Looking if you have any message about the reports of the previous 10 accounts of alts over the last 2 weeks. Looking through the forums to see dozens if not hundreds of stories similar to your own. And you decide enough is enough. You edit your previous positive steam review from an enthusiastic report about the helpful community and the many-faceted gameplay options to a report of your heart-crushing futile fight against players, who don't play by the rules and devs who don't seem to care, because every alt is just another sold copy of their game... and you leave defeated looking for a game without pvp, because human scum always find a way to kill the fun for everyone.
