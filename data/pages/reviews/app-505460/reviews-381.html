\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game with MANY problems. It's hectic and thrilling being on the front and I think any player can get enjoyment from this game but after being out for 5 years and still having huge issues is a bad sign. Are the devs caught in the current profit stream and feature paralysed by the game's early-access success? The current problems are:

- The lack of command and control is noticeable. The fronts are always chaotic since there is no system to marshal the players into cohesive offensive/defensive actions. There is nothing more satisfying than being part of a well coordinated operation - an aspect mostly missing from this game. The exception is being in a clan/regiment which is not the experience for most players.

- Too easy to get stuck in this game.

- Climbing is chronically broken.

- Cliffs/rocks are broken leaving too much open for exploit.

- The pointless, sprawling bases do not connect properly making it impossible to move through them. You have to go over the top and can easily get stuck.

- The quick turning of the player when moving means pistol wielding exploiters can charge in using a zig-zag pattern into your trenches, avoiding all the bullets. But try get to the ground quickly after taking fire and you feel like a creaky geriatric fool who decided to tie his shoelaces on the way down. Immersion breaking.

- To reiterate: A grand war game that lacks coordinated operations is a huge miss. The chaotic malaise of MMO's goes back decades and this game has not innovated towards command and control in any way. I played BF2 almost 20 years ago and even that had a command system.

All that being said It's still pretty novel and worth purchasing on heavy discount.\t\t\t\t\t

1. Balancing issues in every single patch without fail
2. Mechanics in the game that are both unfun and unrewarding despite being invaluable to help win (logi and building come to mind.
3. A dev team that never communicates under any circumstances
4. Extremely ambiguous roadmap that does not actually offer much information on where the game is going
5. An overly-large game map that has caused wars to last far longer than they should and contributes to more burnout without adding fun ways to change gameplay
6. Tech system is outright garbage
7. Devs dictate what the tech tree will be before a war's start and can utterly shaft a team based on which technologies they make mutually exclusive to research
8. Comms system is worse than the tech system. Good players are often targets of witch hunts via mass reports and can get their comms taken away or even be banned
9. The game is intensely clan-centric. If you do not found/join a clan with at least modest numbers you will only experience about half the game has to offer
10. The community is a hassle to deal with. Plenty of elitism and arrogance which puts Hollywood to shame
11. A small yet noticeable presence of bigots and racists (and due to the aforementioned dog ass comms and reporting system, these players won't get banned)
12. Hackers face no repercussions for clear and outright cheating
13. Gameplay is often static and unimaginative. Fights at bridges which last for days or even a full week where literally nothing happens and you just waste your time
14. A known and completely unaddressed issue of alt accounts (someone buying the game again and joining the opposing faction, sabotaging it from the inside) which makes the game extremely unfun for everyone
15. Lots of minor features accumulate that need to be updated as the game has gone through development
16. Development is heavily centered around adding new equipment rather than making desperately needed quality of life changes

There are more gripes I have with the game, though many of them are only really comprehensible if you put some hours into it. Overall, in its current state the game is not in good form. There are too many glaring faults approaching the 'official' release, coupled with the fact that I have seen EA address game issues more frequently than I have seen Siege Camp do so. The developers are responsible for a large majority of the game's failings, and worse yet they respond to criticism (and even basic questions) with a prissy attitude that makes you wonder why they even bothered founding a game dev in the first place. Many issues can be resolved simply by discussing with earnest members of the community who have a solid grasp on the game, yet they choose not to. As a result, the game suffers, and I can't help but wonder what is going to happen to this game in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

Teaches that wars are won with logistical support and proper base construction.

No kill feed.
No kill cam.
And no roster of players in the hex sorted by faction.

The only way to determine who the cheater is on the opposing faction:
- Make an alternate account (buy another copy of the game, with a separate Steam account)
- Join the same server (shard)
- Go to the same region you were killed
- Report the veteran cheater (whose been playing since launch) with your brand new account
- Watch as they continue to play cheat for weeks...months

In a game that already has a massive problem with alternate (alt) accounts griefing & cheating, this method of "reporting" only exacerbates the problem further.\t\t\t\t\t
