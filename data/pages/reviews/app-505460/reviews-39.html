\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGigantic battles are awesome. This game fits many genre's: Builder, Craft system, combat, logistic/driving, strategy, RTS, open world, sandbox. All the things. I really enjoy the chill builder vibe when you are constructing stuff behind the front line, then changing it up and running into the heat of battle just a km away. There are many many items all with their own uses/ purposes. Everything is player built, giving a purpose to people that just want to craft bunkers and foxholes. Ammo, weapons, medical and all building resources are created by players who farm resources in each zone. Those resources and equipment are moved across the world by players driving trucks, trains and ships.

This game has some bugs but in the 70 hours i have played they are few and far between. My main issue is with the aiming system which is a bit finicky. I'd like to see improvements in the organization of squads and regiments, with ways to issue orders from high command to individual units. I also think underground bunkers and tunnels could be a cool addition. Maybe we'll see those kinds of updates eventually.

Overall this a very entertaining game with lots of room for meta and replay ability. 9 out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t

Day 1 already got drafted by a lonely Iranian boat captain to game-changingly shell the frontline for thousands of damage

I say good
Every few weeks it cycles and resets the persistent map when one side wins though, and late-stage war seems like an impossible tank slog. One thing I don't like: all the man-portable AT involves either the RNG-based Armor Penetration Chance system or the RNG-based getting close enough system. And none of the successful hits can involve instant tank crippling to the point that you're safe from the return fire.

90% of the game systems are intuitive but extremely arcanely built and unexplained. Tutorial Island doesn't let you play with all the parts of the game, nor does it leave you tanks and boats to learn how those work (the thing killing you most often while learning the game) I believe it's possible to assemble them on Tutorial Island but you would have to craft it manually. You know, the thing where immense legions of Chinese and Russian gamers have literal human 24:00:00 days of crafting queue time to get the resources for.

Fast tracked education tidbits for new players:
There is PvE in the form of bunkers built by other players that auto-shoot you if they're within range of an upgraded enemy spawn base, and you can shoot them with a lot of bullets to suppress them
All weapons have damage spread (for some reason) so if you died in one shot there was an element of dice
BB is short for bunker base, which you must manually set your spawn at when you get to. It's the FOB of this game, since the actual FOB structure is a direct downgrade to the bunker base.
Run directly at the nearest player with a rank next to their name and ask questions with your mic to learn everything. They will see you have no rank next to your name and immediately know how to help you - which could mean pointing you at the higher ranked guy.
The fandom wiki (unsurprisingly) is full of holes, but helps you learn old game systems like what it takes to make a jeep. Spoiler alert it's 200 components.
Set your mic input volume to 50% and go up or down from there depending on what people around you say about it.\t\t\t\t\t
