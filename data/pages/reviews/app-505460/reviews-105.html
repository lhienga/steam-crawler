\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I thought it was great. Like a tower defense except my enemies were real people. Putting down barb wire and defenses then watching the enemy struggle into them while my allies picked them off was great. Then it all started to fall apart.

Whether or not you or your team gets to use certain weapons or tactics is entirely dependent on the clans that run that part of the world. In the current war there are parts of the world my team cannot go because the enemy Storm Cannons (Most powerful artillery in the game, extremely effective) will obliterate us if we try. If we are in range of those guns, they are firing. And they won't stop firing until all of us are dead.

Meanwhile take a trip to our side of the map? There are SC's one river away from our enemy that just sit dormant because the can that runs them wants to do something else. And because clans can LOCK these weapons, NO ONE gets to use them. SC's will sit fully charged, with 100+ rounds sitting next to it and remain completely unused. When you challenge the clans about this they say you're a noob and need to shut up. You can point out that our enemy isn't letting these extremely powerful weapons just sit there but it doesn't matter they run the world so no one gets to challenge them.

This goes for a lot of things like logistics, supply, who gets tanks, who gets any kind of support at all. Clans will come in destroy everything you made remake it the way they want it and then leave again and refuse to support you with materials to upkeep. And on top of ALL of this the game has 0 stat tracking. Which means you have no earthly idea if you're making a difference. You can place down 1000 land mines and kill 1000 people or, you could kill no one. You have no way of telling whether or not your contributions made a difference which makes the 3-5 minute slog back to the front lines feel absolutely pointless when you get run over by a friendly car and have to re-equip and walk all over again. Uncertain of if your efforts make a difference.

It was fantastic for the first few hours. Now I regret getting it, and every time I try to get into it I just find more glaring holes in the formula that make me feel exhausted. If the major clans don't immediately agree with you, you might as well not be playing because you won't be getting any help. Entire sections of the map fall because clans hoard their wealth weapons and resources.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen i was originally looking at THIS exact page, wondering if i should buy this game, i ran into a lot of the reviews complaining about large clans, and their overwhelming power over their own faction. Often people would talk about their bad experiences with a clan that dosent like them, mass reporting them, and them getting banned.

Before i say anything about anything...I would like to say that i have mainly only played casually and alone. That dose not mean i havent interacted with other players and the community as a whole! I have been a part of many small scale operations formed by smaller groups of people or even larger clans. But i am not directly a member of a clan/group, big or small. Now that we have clearly established my position in the player base, lets get into what MY experiences have told ME, about the community, and game as a whole.

I have fought in two wars (at the time of writing), War 105, and 106. In 105 i was a warden, in 106 i am a colonial. I have not gotten into any brawls/fights/arguments/"just a bit of banter"s or general disagreements with other players. Specifically in war 106, in which i started properly interacting with other players. During war 105 i was a lot less experienced and naturally intimidated by the idea of simply joining a tank crew for example. So i have more experience interacting with colonial players than wardens. I cant yet properly say if wardens are any better/worse than collies, but at the very least i can confidently say collies have been VERY noob friendly so far! Now these are only my experiences, which dont speak for everybody, but i personally think that at the current stage of the game (at least if you go collie) you most likely wont have to go through any...Bullying, insulting, mass reports, team kills to players or structures, or any kind of toxicity. But dont think you can get away with doing something legitimately dumb! You will be scolded, but not ruthlessly ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ on.

TLDR: i dont know about wardens, but collies are pretty friendly. Worth the price.
