I noticed this game about a year and a half ago, and was unsure about buying it. I recently rediscovered it and decided to give it

a play. With a huge world map to fight over, good art style, team play, vehicles, logistical chains, fort building ect.. you really can't

go wrong with this game! While the top-down camera takes a little getting used to, it really isn't a problem and you'll quickly

adapt. Learning the basics of the game were pretty easy/intuitive and in almost no time at all, I was fighting on the front-lines!

On the battlefield, I was surprised by the lack of voice spammers and most players I ran into were helpful and respectful.


Overall, if you like all out war games that have a large scope, light Role-play elements, and fun mechanics this is a good purchase; Grab the game while it's on sale and have fun!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really good. The developers update frequently, the servers are easy to connect to, it even fills a unique spot among games in general that has been very pleasant to watch develop over time.
What is not good is the players. Frankly the community around this game makes it almost unplayable. Your average teammate will be a 12 year old screaming into his mic various lines from his favorite action movie, blasting music into local chat, and screaming every time he's hit like its the opening scene from saving private ryan. Your leader figures will be no better, often simply urging the team to do a banzai rush when resources are too low to sustain heavy losses, loosing the front, and then blaming the logistics team for not resupplying fast enough and team killing their least favorite player.
Ranking up requires commendations from your squad mates. Good luck hopping in as a PUG as such are rarely distributed to randoms. Your experience will be improved greatly by convincing 3-5 of your friends to join you and using a separate voice chat to co-ordinate.
Honestly I do want to love this game. I do like the Idea behind it: A massive warscape populated and run entirely by humans. But that is it's inherent flaw: The human element. Players and community can make or break a game and unfortunately for me it has been an experience I cant recommend to others.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is, surprisingly for a war game, a game of quiet pleasures. Ultimately nothing you do is directly rewarding, except perhaps seeing an enemy go down to your gunfire. Very much what you see is frontlines shifting, supplies moving, and a war progressing. A personal victory might be as simple as defending a forward base and only losing 2 screens of ground despite being outnumbered and outgunned. It might be crates of supplies dropped off after a hazordous journey through enemy ambushes. Despite being able to jump in and quickly find a task, be it frontline cannon fodder or mining scrap to make supplies, foxhole is a game best enjoyed over 2+ hours of play. This is to help see your long term efforts have impact, and also time to find a pickup group to work with if you haven't joined (or don't want to join) an organized clan.

In all cases as a new pair of boots on the front line, ask more experienced players for guidance. It's easy to waste supplies that someone worked hard to make, so learning best practice first hand is a great thing. Thankfully there's usually someone in the community willing to take newbies for a spin.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Can be fun, some great community members, interesting emphasis on logistics, absolutely no sense of accomplishment. It all seems very pointless after a short while.

I want to like this game and in fairness it seemed better than I expected at first. There are a surprising number of helpful players. The top down view isn't as bad as I thought it would be although it can be very exploitable. I really enjoyed the emphasis on the logistics.

I can't recommend the game however because there is no positive feedback for your efforts. You can spend hours and hours helping your side but there is no sense that your efforts matter. With an emphasis on logistics the game wants you to spend time gathering mats, refining them, building stuff, and hauling to the front. When you do that you receive ... nothing. Your only reward is that you get to do it all over again.

When you log out, everything in your inventory is lost so every time you start you only have a building hammer and a pistol. That truck you built to help you haul stuff? Long gone. You can lock it but anyone with an easy to craft wrench can take it. I suppose that truck really belongs to your "team" but having to hunt around and acquire a truck every time you log on gets tedious.

Your team is an unorganized mob. There are the 12 year old generals yelling orders in world chat. There are the 30 year old virgins team killing and otherwise griefing just for sport. To be fair, I did have a number of random interactions with some very helpful players who offered advice and guidance but nothing that would make you feel like your efforts were making a difference.

And that's the problem. There is no sense that anything you do makes a difference. The game may satisfy an altruistic need to "help" others but its an otherwise pointless exercise, nothing you do appears to make any difference and every time you log on you start from scratch. Any successful game needs to have a positive reward system. This game has none.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Unless you have a group of friends to play with for dozens of hours a day, and are okay with some random "vets" yelling at you constantly on the mic, avoid this game like the plague.

After over 170+ hours of gameplay, I feel like sharing my impressions on the game.

PROS:
- Good concept, including all the aspects of warfare, from infantry, to armor, navy and artillery
- Everything is player made, every bullet, weapon, vehicle, which makes the war quite dinamic
- Logistics is a huge part of the game, from gathering materials, processing and manufacturing, to supplying the front lines.
- Graphics are somewhat nice

CONS:
- The game is early access and is quite unpolished
- Definitely NOT CASUAL FRIENDLY, the grind is real and you may need to sink tons of hours in this game to achieve anything meaningful
- NOT NOOB FRIENDLY. Prepare yourself to be yelled at by vets for making mistakes, not knowing something or simply because you don't do what they tell you to do.
- WORST COMMUNITY. As a solo player, you are completely on your own, no one is going to pay attention to you, or try to help you in any way. Clans are even worse, since they treat new players as brainless-expendable-cannon fodder.
- Top point view is an interesting choice but it is very poorly implemented. You may need a 16*9 resolution and point your top-right corner of the screen all the time, to have the largest line of sight possible, otherwise other player will see you first and kill you without noticing them.
- The aiming is simply crap. There can be an enemy shooting you in plain sight and still you will miss every single shot. Other times your aim will be unexplicably blocked by terrain, features and or buildings just because the top-down point of view.
- Sound doesn't even work properly.

Therefore I cannot recommend the game. I seriously doubt this will improve since the population keeps dropping (avg of 400 players on peak hour) and only the vets and clans remains, making it even more elitist of a game. Besides, updates are only increasing the grind and the ammount of time you have to dedicate to the game.\t\t\t\t\t
