The most insane battle I have been in yet with trucks, fully automatic guns, heavy moving machine gun that killed dozens of players within seconds.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t The Worst New Player Experience

Decided to try this game out as it looked interesting, and was pretty affordable - and to my horror. It's an awful game for new players starting out. The "tutorial" prepares for you for very little of what the actual game is like. You're better off watching a youtube video to see what you should or should not do.

The community is mainly made up already established regiments who don't have the time of day for you. As an experiment, I asked four different officers from different regiments if I could join their regiment to learn the game (because the tutorial is godawful). All of them said "No your rank is too low".

Imagine that, a brand new player having "too low a rank". So if you're thinking of starting out in Foxhole, not only is the tutorial complete garbage - the community at large is basically an old boy's club and you're not welcome.

An Incredibly Unrewarding Gameplay System

In order to advance in rank in Foxhole, other players must "commend you". This is an incredibly stupid system, because I've seen people who do nothing in-game that are a high rank. This is because they are in a regiment (the game's equivalent to a clan or guild) and they obviously trade commendations with each other so they can advance in rank faster. Likewise, I've seen tireless medics and supply drivers (incredibly important support roles) which are Private or even unranked. Not having a proper rank excludes you from many functions in the game, including leadership ones. It's a remarkably stupid and unfair system that rewards collusion amongst major "clans" and excludes new players or more solo-focused ones.

I met a guy who basically dug out an entire base by himself with a shovel who was a measly Lance Corporal and we were being barked around by some kid in a clan driving all the cool stuff. I decided to confirm this myself by spending about 45 minutes farming mats, converting them to basic materials, and then manufacturing soldier supplies and ammo to transport to a high-activity frontline safehouse. The drive involved two map loading changes, endless driving, and when I finally delivered my much needed payload (soldier supplies for a push) - I was given 0 commendations or thanks for my effort, not from my peers or from the game. Imagine getting no rewards for playing the game as intended - that's the Foxhole experience.

Do not believe in the "Logi Hero" myth they peddle on the steam boards or youtube guides. The only thing you are doing is wasting your own time to get nothing in return. Nobody cares that you are helping, and not least the game itself.

If Regiments are the issue, why not make your own?

Because you need to be a SSgt rank or higher to create your own Regiment. Which involves you getting 50 commendations from your peers. You're not getting to this, unless you are already part of an outside gaming community that is going to make your own circle of self-commending exchangers. Actually since we're on this topic, you can't even create a basic Squad unless you're ranked Cpl. or higher (which involves getting 8 commendations).

Stay well away if you're a new player

I am sure this game is fun if you're already apart of an established community/regiment that has enough rank to access the cooler parts of the game, but it's a miserable, unrewarding and unfulfilling experience for a new player - even those willing to go the extra mile to watch youtube guides, read their extensive wiki and engage with people in voice chat.

Glad the game was a rather cheap title, because it'll definitely take more than 2 hours to figure out the basics of the game and you're not likely to get a Steam Refund (which I couldn't get). So save your money and buy yourself something not completely garbage instead.\t\t\t\t\t
