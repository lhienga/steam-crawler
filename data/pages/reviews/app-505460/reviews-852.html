\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe "thumbs-up" is mainly just for the concept, which I think is great in theory: An organic war sandbox where groups of players gotta figure things out for themselves and get their act together, with everything from troop movements to supply chains, and the winner is defined by who is best at coordinating and communicating. Almost more of a strategy game than a shooter.

However, that means players are the bottleneck, and who you're playing with can give an entirely different experience (which can be said for pretty much any multiplayer game, but is especially true here). Sometimes things turn out just like they ought to, where you find yourself embroiled in intense, player-driven operations, make fast friends with your teammates, and - even if you lose - come away with some fun stories, and maybe a group you can play with again. Other times, your teammates are a petty, snippy lot who shoot you for straying too close to one of their crates, there's a clan hogging production and abusing votekick because they want to build a castle in some obscure corner of the map, someone's intentionally sabotaging your team to help the enemy or just for kicks, and team chat is filled with people asking where they can buy a gun.

When the concept works, the game really shines. But owing to the "organic" nature of the game, it doesn't take much to make it not work, and turn the game into an absolute chore. Whether or not it's worth a buy depends on how much you're willing to chance it.\t\t\t\t\t
