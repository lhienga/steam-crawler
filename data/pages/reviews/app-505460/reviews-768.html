\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo with the recent flood of reviews coming in, I thought I'd update mine, 4800 hours later.

So I've put in way too much more time, effort, and willpower... and the game still doesn't do it for me.

Foxhole still reinforces an unfun and boring central gameplay loop which relies on increasingly tedious and involved logistics and supply management. I dislike the fact that only large/organized clans even have the ability to put in the time and effort necessary to maintain fragile facilities which allow for the construction of BT's and SHT's (the two largest tank types in game). In the old, old days, any old group of friends could scrounge up the rmats, emats, and bmats needed for a tank and its ammo, even if petrol was a pain.

I'll probably be updating this soon, but there are my literal shower thoughts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the game has potential it does not quite live up to its trailers for the most part. My short lived exsperiance was one of constant annoyance; from people yelling for their resupplying of weapons only to later be annoyed when they changed their minds and you finished a hour long drive and endless clicking fest, to having a overall disdain for new players trying to ask questions and learn from their own failures, all culminating in a slow crawl campaign of attrition that while some people enjoyed, I did not.

On paper the game is a grand-scale war with a asthetic between WW1 and WW2, what I believe the plan was for player interactions was to have players making supplies in the back lines, moving them to a processing station, then from their to making weapons, and then lastly to the front-line troops... unfortunately this is often interrupted with petty arguements and griefing. A example of my times with such was, I was mining and refining for about 1 hour until I decided to take what I have made to a factory and make some weapons to give to a nearby base requesting them. I finished making the weapons and moving them to the front, on arrival I was told they weren't 'enough' and in response I had 3 men throw grenades at my truck and destroy my supplies.

I decided to try asking a question in region chat about if I made a mistake, and no one responded. I asked once more, stating I was a new player and was told in rather harsh language that I wasn't wanted in this game. So I've decided that I'd heed their advice and refund this game. I don't hate it, but It just wasn't fun for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game I in the past would have recommended on concept alone. If you are interested in the game I still highly suggest consuming some of the content and media around it like the jade cove documentary or content creators like I Saw a Bear. That is a bit of the thing though. The game nowadays if pretty officially more fun to watch or talk about then play.

A friend of mine put it best saying: "The devs do not communicate with the community, nor try to police it or cooperate with the community trying to police itself. Reporting people for transphobia, homophobia or outright Nazism is met with the devs ignoring the ticket, or responding to it and then not punishing the offender." Toxicity has been a problem that has been growing in foxhole for a long time, and the developers have pushed the game in a direction that it doesn't really feel immersive or fun anymore to just be a soldier in this massive MMO war. When I first played, things were less static and it felt like as an individual you could make a difference. Less so by just lone wolfing it as a solo player but by just working together as a whole, without the need for clan level organization for every single thing. Now any front I go to is going to be either a bridge battle or a slugging match between bunker bases. If you want good fun fights with urban combat or in open terrain like is fitting and immersive for the era and vibe foxhole goes for, you have to basically luck out and be on at the right time.

Not all of this is stuff that is able to be completely fixed. Not every moment you log into foxhole is gonna be a fun battle, toxcitiy is never going to be completely gone, and of course there is going to be a need for clans and their more organized nature in many aspects of the game. But frankly now it feels like the game can't really even be enjoyed all that well without a clan, without running into nazis and transphobes, and without having to slog first through likely weeks of static unfun fighting. I am aware of the nature of what kind of game foxhole is, where there very much has to be a carrot and a stick. The issue is that now there is no carrot. We are just told to chase sticks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, I can not recommend this game. Originally what was a somewhat smooth and enjoyable MMO war experience, has turned into an incredibly cumbersome and dragged out nightmare. In early versions, you could carry out and accomplish a plethora of many different things by yourself in maybe 5-10 minutes, but now it will now take upwards of 30 minutes to accomplish just a single meaningful goal, even with the help of several other players. This game makes you feel like a slave rather than a soldier!
Another huge problem is the diminishing player population which is already sparsely spread across several enormous map regions.

The developers destroyed any form of "casual" enjoyable gameplay and replaced it with an excruciatingly boring, unrewarding, and punishingly slow experience. The updates only seem to get worse; instead of fixing the existing problems, the devs are just adding more broken features to the pile (e.g. Trenches, which have been long requested by the community).
Frankly, I do not see this game ever being fixed.

If you value your time, it's best to just avoid this game.\t\t\t\t\t
