\r\n\t\t\t\t\tY'know what, I changed my mind, i still think the bots can be harmful but if you're attacking in a larger group and making an assault they matter way less. thus, i will leave a positive review, however, im not gonna delete what i said as i still do - to an extent - standby it. but it's still a good game and you should give it a go

Bots ruin the game for me, and progress is so slow. I don't think it's the worst game in the world or anything but there's just nothing going on half the time. You run for 20 minutes to get sniped by an AI, I've spent 10 hours playing this game at this point and the biggest thing I've ever seen happen is we took a bridge, only to then lose it again. Sure, you need to have it so a couple dudes can't just run behind enemy lines and take a base, but AI with autoaim that shoots on sight is **not** the way to go about that. I suggest having a high minimum player requirement to capture a base, along with only allowing bases on the very front line to be taken and finally having to hold it for a far longer period whilst alerting the enemy team. If it took an hour to take a base, and the enemy team actually had time to counter-attack, meaning both sides have some agency rather than just encountering bots the game would be much better. BTW, when I talk about bots, I'm not talking about some niche presence that only occurs every while that it would be difficult to filter out, I'm talking sentries manning the whole front line 24/7. The prevalence of these guys is just astounding. I spent an hour and a half with a squad of four people trying to work out how to outflank them and we couldn't do it. I would wager there are possibly as many as 40% bots, maybe a bit less but certainly, if you think you can play the game even 5 minutes on the actual front-line without encountering one you are sorely mistaken. This kind of incredible prevalence means the devs must not only tolerate but actively encourage or maybe even own them. It's awful and truly revoked any fun from the experience. When I play a game I do so to compete with other players, or AI in such a way that it is a far competition. This is not that. I'm happy to have some sentries, to make sure that gains are hard fought but (I can't believe I actually have to say this) in a game about tactical warfare, the players actually need to be able to take the damn enemy position. If it becomes too imbalanced fine, bots then to flesh out the enemy numbers I am not against but they need to act at least vaguely human, aimbot is hardly fair.

again, the game isn't awful but it sure as hell isn't good either, and it's not even the mechanics or the coding either - bar occasionally when your character gets stuck in rocks, I've not encountered any bugs at all, and I love the logistics system. It's just the bots and snail's pace change in position, *just get rid of the damn bots... please.*\t\t\t\t\t
