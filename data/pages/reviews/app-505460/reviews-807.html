\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a truly unique gaming experience, and I would encourage everyone to try it out at least once.

The game can broadly be divided into two sections - the frontline, and the logistics.

On the frontline, hundreds of soldiers on each side fight it out for control of a vast map. And there truly IS a frontline - map control is everything in this game, and players fight to shift the frontline forward bit by bit. Combat isn't ruled by gunplay, but by movement and tactics - you won't be getting anywhere without suppressing fire, flanking movements, and support from artillery and vehicles. It takes a true team effort to push through a defensive line - and then it's time to build up defences of your own and use the position as a springboard for another push.

This is only possible thanks to those doing logistics - a process of collecting resources, transporting them to refineries, then transport those materials to factories, then transporting that equipment to the bases of the frontline. Battles often turn on this - a force with logistics support can call on heavy weapons, armoured support, and can respawn at forward 'field bases'.

Don't go into Foxhole expecting to be a hero. This isn't a fast-paced shooter, nor does it lend itself to individual heroics. In this game, you're just one cog in the vast machine of war - and it's an addictive experience.\t\t\t\t\t
