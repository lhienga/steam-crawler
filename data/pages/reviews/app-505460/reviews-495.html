\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am blown away by the logistical depth of this game. Despite how deep the systems go, each step along the way is consistent and simple. To create a dynamic simulation without too much excess fat, while also delivering a really solid line infantry experience for the layman, is pretty awesome.

The issue with a lot of war games is they ask you to believe the fantasy. In games like Holdfast, they don't ask you to play realistically. In Foxhole, every system is built so that everything naturally folds together. Trenches are built because they're a great, easy defensive structure. Soon, you're in trench warfare. Artillery is brought in to bombard them. Tanks come out because they need something to break through the trenches. Anti-tank guns are brought to the link to counter them. It all happens very naturally and seamlessly without a developer holding your hand or artificial restrictions.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm an old time player(from early access days) and the game as a whole is not bad, but I'm down voting since the aspect I like the most (infantry gameplay) has become neglected imo.

The main problem is every battle in the game now is WW1 Verdun, which is epic, true. However it also creates a really ♥♥♥♥♥♥ environment for infantry with lots of taking pocket shots at each from trenches followed by staring contests.

It's also very hard to "play well" as an infantry soldier, since there is so much that kills you and so easily, It all comes down to suicide attacks on buildings/tanks and trying to out attrition the enemy.

Last, there's the aiming system that works by eldritch magic. Actually part of the reason why it feels so ♥♥♥♥♥♥ to play as an infantryman is probably because the aiming system doesn't fit the game anymore, after it started focusing on base building and trenches.

Just to finish, I'm mostly ok with the other aspects of the game, but it's a bit strange that the role 80% of the players will experience feels so scuffled and unfun. True be told I remember having more fun with this game on its older versions (before the trench stuff).\t\t\t\t\t
