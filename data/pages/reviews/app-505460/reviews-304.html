\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst impressions: There's absolutely nothing casual about this game.
I thought it would be fun to participate in a large-scale war and this might be the game. My experience was such that it took about 1 hour to read tutorials how to equip this and that equipment and what every little icon on the map means. Then I started playing and went into front lines. Let's just say if you're not getting shot to pieces immediately it's just because you're in the middle of friendly encampment. There's no way to tell because as far as I know there's no minimap. Camera isn't fixed to direction you're moving. Every time you die you have to get back to base and retrieve and equip supplies. Rifle, ammo, medkits. Equip, reload weapon, move through trenches then die on first bullet and lose it all. Not once did I feel like I was contributing positively to my team.

Thought you'd sneak around and disrupt the supply lines or flank the enemy? There's some pillboxes that promptly shoot you on sight.

Then there's the logistic side of things. But who knows what the function of every depot and pillbox and bunker is and the battlefield is littered full of them. There's dozens of different supplies, intermediate items, vehicles and guns. They add to the complexity but don't increase meaning or player agency. If I deliver some stuff from point A to point B am I contributing? Can this structure hold the stuff I'm bearing? Who knows. The game lacks basic player motivators like progress bars, stats, indicators, color codes, callouts when you've scored a kill. Say some place has 80 rifles. Is that a lot? How many are they using per hour? How many are being delivered? There's no indicators like that. As such I think developers have tried to make a SIMULATOR what it feels like to be a soldier in WW1. Exhausted, encumbered, wounded, bored and lost.

So this is not a game where you just hop on a tank and start shooting, no.\t\t\t\t\t
