\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell I've just hit 100 hours and it's safe to say I like this game.
Will you like it, too? Maybe - but it's really not like any other game, specifically.
That's one thing I like about it. I'll be honest, the learning curve with the aiming and how you can see some things and not others, how to use angles correctly, realigning your screen during a firefight, etc, all took a while for me to grasp. I still don't consider myself great at it, but it starts to get easier the more you play.

I typically play FPS shooters, but Foxhole has a great mix of teamwork, base building and warfare that when combined into persistent wars really makes it unique to me. There's a good community and while I haven't spent enough time to join a org/clan they're very prevalent in the game and it seems fun to be a part of.

So far I've been focusing on basic infantry skills, a bit of engineer, marksman and medic. Looking forward to dipping a toe into armor and logistics but that's the great thing about this game - you can just hop in for quick 10-20 min gaming session as a rifleman or medic, or choose to spend countless hours running logistics and listening to a podcast or something. There's really something to do every time you go in the game that makes it feel fresh and alive.

Memes are good too.\t\t\t\t\t
