\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to love Foxhole. There is so much to love about the game. The setting, the community, and most of the game mechanics make it a pleasure to play. The one thing ruining this game at the moment is the lacklustre approach to moderation by the Dev team. The in-game systems for reducing toxicity are a combination of vote kicks and a Reddit style voting system on the global chat. Vote kicks are somewhat hard to abuse but the global vote has been abused multiple times to silence multiple players including myself. The Dev team have refused to help with this issue in my case despite other players having appealed it and others having received help with it. At this point I can only surmise that a specific member of the Dev team is taking personal action which is highly unprofessional. I would not be surprised if this review gets flagged for just that. Aside from this disappointing experience, the rest of the game is solid as evidenced by over 3,900 hours spent playing it.

Pros:
- Good core loop,
- Engaging community,
- Hours worth of mechanics to learn.

Cons:
- Poor moderation,
- Still a few bugs need working out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI kept my review positive for a while, but that was because I didn't play the game for a long time.

I left my initial review in 2018 about two and a half years after the game came out. Now in 2022, almost 5 and a half years after its release, I simply can't recommend it anymore. I'd just want to say it's a great game that you shouldn't buy. Its not horrible, but it has had some very core issues and developments that have made me feel like saying it shouldn't be bought.

When I got back into Foxhole after a long hiatus, a lot of the mechanics were new and the game changed a lot. I was already familiar with a lot of the initial mechanics first shipped with the game and picked up the new ones with some trial-and-error, so I would say I'm quite familiar with the game's current state. For that reason, I would advice against getting this game.

There a reason that the trailer videos only show the transportation side of getting supplies (and no you won't be getting a tank escort, ever). Gathering basic things like scrap is a chore that only the most inexperienced are willing to learn or senior players are willing to put up with. Scrap, the most core resource, is abundant but tedious to gather. Any front-line player will lovingly kiss you for enduring the Herculean task required to make repeated equipment trips. Unless you're willing to invest at least a couple hours gathering this material or having someone gather it for you, it is a miserable task. You'll be watching YouTube more than actually playing the game. Transporting supply crates to the front lines is usually a one-way trip for anyone running logistics, because repeatedly making the journey is a task only the most mentally broken will take on. There isn't a way to get around any of this either: the logistics side of Foxhole will have you doing the same supply runs repeatedly for weeks. It is so bad that I almost want to contribute to society by working at a McDonald's, because logistics is the developers way of employing free child labor. Every gun you fire, reload, and lose was the blood, sweat, and now tears of a sweaty gamer.

Although it is an anti-griefing measure, the slow timer of taking resources/weapons out of stockpiles makes stocking anything of value (tanks, artillery, field guns, and medical supplies) time consuming and boring. Contributing things like basic materials to a public refinery just means that players would have to take 20 minutes taking them out of the refinery for them to even make anything. Anything that involves taking things out of a stockpile is annoying at the least. It will almost make you wish you were packing groceries at Walmart and getting paid unlike this.

Rifle combat can either be the jankiest or most rewarding feature of Foxhole. You'll find players that can barely hit the side of a barn (namely your team), but there are also players that will Chris Kyle you while your head pokes out of your trench (these are the people you will usually be fighting). Automatic weapons and bayonets somewhat relieve the numbing desire to touch grass, but they usually get out-ranged. If you don't have a machine gun, 10 gas grenades, or god aim (that the game provides almost not tutorial on), you're practically just cannon fodder for the Soviet war machine. You'll frequently find yourself getting rifled in three perfect shots by someone who suffered long enough to learn the janky aiming mechanics. And I seriously do want to emphasize how bad it is. You will spend hours of your life, days even, getting gas grenade spammed, cross-mapped, and sitting in the same hole you died weeks ago. The developers may think this is a good thing, but this may make you want to leave the server and finalize the divorce.

Do you love tanks? Cause' you're basically never going to get to drive or shoot one. Unless you want to join a clan and seriously commit to this mess of a game, you are on your feet. If you want to mine any materials that allow you to build a tank on your own, you'll usually have to cross halfway across the entire map to fill a truck with 1/4 of the components you'll need to build even one tank (and drive back and forth several times). Additionally, if you ever do get to drive a tank or any armored vehicle, you'll have have to operate it as if your life depends on it. Tank combat usually consists of sitting outside the range of an enemy tank for sometimes hours without doing any meaningful combat. Make no mistake, if you even mess up one time by getting too close as armor, your several hours of toiling in the fields for the materials that made it are wasted. Oh yeah, and you're never going to get close enough to hit armor with RPG's or anti-tank grenades cause' their range is so bad. So armor versus armor is the only way you can play and infantry can't beat them. Maybe you'll get lucky and get stuck giving it fuel.

Question: Do you one of the following?
A. A Sniper Rifle C. A Tank
B. Gas Grenades D. Someone Grinding 24/7 for Supplies
E. Someone Who Learned How to Aim

If you do not have A, B, C, D, or E, you lose the game!
Foxhole has no tolerance for deviating from the Meta. If you don't play the meta, you die to it and lose. Better luck next time :'(

Have you ever played a game that felt like it was designed for grinding? Welcome to Foxhole, where the entire point is the grind. Your grind is eternal on the front, in the scrapyards, and in the war. The map is too big, the factions have one-sided equipment, and the game has way too many core problems.

It used to be a really nice game. I have fond memories of fighting on Oarbreaker Isles before it got updated to be a part of the main map. It was a great feeling to do naval invasions and build stuff on site that you knew you'd use without all the big transport and disconnect to the front lines. Now, all there is is two things to do: build equipment and grind for days on end without any war progress, or keep spawning in the same grind you don't know how to beat cause the enemy is doing the exact same thing. You don't simply "lose" a war in Foxhole, you "lose" when the logistic teams get bored and play a different game they actually enjoy: it's just a matter of if their side quits first or yours does.

Game development usually makes things better, but sometimes it makes things worse and doesn't address the deepest issues a game has. Don't buy it, not now at least; it isn't as fun and enjoyable as it used to be.\t\t\t\t\t
