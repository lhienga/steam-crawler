\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJoined, did the Training, jumped into action, got shot, found a Major, he teached me stuff not shown in training, i shot back, and helped him dig & build the defences that saved the frontlines.

Would experience grandfather's ww2 traumas again, and i didn't touch vehicles yet, gotta keep coming back with friends to spice it up! :heart:

Edit: i've played for almost a week now, made a docen + of new friends, meet more on discord, we teamed up, made tactics, all like real war but better cause we could joke about getting blown up by our own friendly artillery who sometimes fires shells at random who knows why, and after playing a bunch more i've to say:

- i've not expected so many friendly players and supportive/good community in general, i was worried by some bad reviews who stated some of the community was toxic, but i've to say, from 100s of player i've come across, 99% were sweet, supportive and helpful exept for...

-1 random brit who drives on the left lane. who crashed against my truck, and was a bit of a douche, but i mean, WHO DRIVES ON THE LEFT LANE!? The real mad lad! he yelled some unintelligeble excuse of wannabe english words and honked his horn at me for driving, on the right lane of the road! we continued our journeys and it was nothing you don't see everyday when 2 vehicles collide IRL

Aside from a mad brit everyone was Sargeant Friendship, this is in the Coalition side of the war i'm talking about, idk about the Wardens, maybe they are more Strict/serious since they are all gray? the lack of colour may be a factor there (?

So, i'm not saying the guys who had a bad experiene lied, maybe thay came across some closed group of toxic wasted ppl, and made them go mad for some reason, but so far, i found only fun, friends and a lot of strategic big war planning action, it's the whole deal!

Edit 2: i've played a couple of wars, tryed both Factions Green(Goblins) and blue(BlueBerries)

I've managed to find a couple really on the edge of the seat, borderline toxic players, but 95% even on Wardens where chill, friendly, and also warm for being blue, but i've to admit i had a traumatic experience once:

I was on a factory who i thought was owned by a friend i made in-game called Mc Donalds (but he didn't serve burgers and fries sadly) which was in fact owned by a really Yakuza Asian Gang, who was literally making every player on the server ran away and demolish weeks of their progress because they were causing so much trouble, they fired rockets and artillery at FRIENDLY FACILITIES, Huge Factories were harmed in the process of that film, and a lot of reports and salt was flying by over the chat and over my head

Unlucky me, i found my self on the middle of a confrontation inside the warden faction, where a asian named player started shooting at another one for "turning on a power plant" i asked him to turn on since i needed power to produce some materials i mined, but he was using the petrol instead of the diesel, so the asian proceeded to murder him, and by accident blowing me up too since i happend to get close to see what was all the bangs about..

I managed to talk with the leader of that asian regiment, i'm not gonna lie i was scared after reading all the mass reports stuff, but i got lucky it all ended good for me, it was a confusion, and language barrier made it hard to understand why they blown up and destroyed us their own stuff, a bit over exaggerated of a reaction imo, could've put a sign or told the guy that he was using petrol instead of diesel, but the low ranked asian didn't speak english.

Maybe, having a HotKey for "quick universally translated communications" just for the sake of clicking on a "stop a second" button to let other language speaking players understand better without the need to open google translate or "blowing up friendlies because that's the easier faster way"

-

But, even if it was scary, it did have a happy ending for us at least, and 95% of people i've crossed paths on both collies and wardens were cool AF, good people, but, i felt the fear, dev's need to think of a better report system, or change/upgrade the actual one

Player should not play with fear of losing access to a game they love and payed to play if they just want to do the right thing, and be understanding.

Otherwise, amazing game, unique and one in a billon experience of a game, 100% recommended. :heart:\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far I've adored this game, it's quickly become one of my all time favourite games and the more I play Foxhole the more I love it. An MMO game that blends the World War themes perfectly with the always online interactivity create an incredibly visceral and engaging gameplay loop.

This is achieved with a strict adherenceto player driven warfare, with developers and AI almost entirely absent, making the war effort a combined effort of hundreds of players, each doing their own small tasks that help build into bigger operations and movements. Each player really is an invaluable cog in the vast war economies, leading to fully realised consequences that can have impacts on the entire war, even if seemingly minor.

Gameplay wise it's a bit janky; this is a consequence of the EVE Online model they've adopted in which all players from around the whole world play on two servers, each with global reach. For someone in Australia there's a noticeable delay with actions, but thanks to decent net-code and all players being at this equal disadvantage this rarely has any consequences on battles and the flow of gameplay. So far the biggest issue with it is climbing over things, it feels like you have a broken leg sometimes.

There's an excellent amount of depth implemented in each aspect of the game, with a singular tightly wound focus on warfare. There isn't any unecessary fluff, every single item has a purpose in the war, and no amount of effort to the war is ever wasted.

This extends from the back line (logi) all the way to fighting on the frontline, and even sneaking behind enemy lines to snoop on their intelligence and disrupt their own supply lines and logistics, each part operating entirely independently by players. Even if you have a minimal amount of time you can spend on games, that minimal amount is valuable to the whole team, every unit of resources harvested helps, every kill you can make helps, every trench you can dig helps.

There is no wastage with this, it feels incredibly concise and as if it's driven by a distinct and clear vision that all aspects of the game are unified in achieving. It's an incredibly rich experience and frankly I love Foxhole, absolutely worth the standard retail price if you're willing to splurge.\t\t\t\t\t
