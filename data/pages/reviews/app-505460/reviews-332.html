\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy overall feeling on the game now is that if you're willing to grind in Foxhole like I did clearing out scrap and sulfur mines, doing logi, even building stuff occasionally, you can play any other grindy game that exists right now and have more fun the whole way through that other game.

You won't be yelled at in a game that requires mandatory Loudness Normalization via WIndows, you won't be harassed, you won't have your vehicles stolen or your character griefed because a clan claimed a public resource as its own and don't want you on "their land". You won't even need to join a clan to enjoy anything in that other game most likely. In Foxhole you are essentially required to be in a clan or you're locked out of most of the game's content.

The frontline fighting in this game is fun, but it's nothing so special that I would go out of my way to recommend the game. The last time I reviewed this game I had a change of heart that it was actually alright under certain circumstances, but I see no reason to be so forgiving to the game when most of the frontline gameplay relies so heavily on what is essentially free digital labor and the people are exceptionally toxic in the backline sometimes where most of that digital labor takes place. Don't like my review? Sucks, for you.\t\t\t\t\t
