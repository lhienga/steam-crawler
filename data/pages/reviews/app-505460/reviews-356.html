\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I should recommend this game. I've put an ok amount of time into it, I think the concept is fun and unique, I had fun, and I met people. All in all, you'd call that a success, right?

Unfortunatly, due to the negligence of the devs In regard to improving logistics I really cant recommend this game. To give you a brief summary, there are 2 halves to the game: the front line and the back line. The front line is where the combat, base building, and intel gathering is mostly done. The back line is where the resources are obtained, weapons are crafted, and vehicles are assembled. The gameplay loop of the back line, aka "Logi", involves making supplies for the front line and then driving the supplies all the way to the front line.

This used to not be that much of a deal, as the front line wasn't ever that far from the back line. However, as of a couple months ago when they increased the size of the map its been a pain in the ass to drive anywhere. When increasing the size of the map, they moved the factories and resource nodes 30-100% further away from the front line. Its now much longer to make a delivery, and because sections of the map can only hold so many people, you might end up driving all the way to the front line only to have to wait 20 more minutes for enough people to leave that section to deliver your supplies. 1 trip might take up to 40 minutes, and that doesn't include navigation mistakes. For reasons of "authenticity" to the time period the game is set in, there is no navigation mode on the map. When you drive, you have to constantly open the map (which stops you from steering and pressing the gas) which gets old really fast. There is a minimap in the bottom corner, but it only covers a limited area immediatly around you and doesn't show sections you are not currently in. Even with constantly checking often you will take a wrong turn and add more time to the journey. Implementing something as simple as drawing the route you want to take on your map and having it show up on the minimap is apparently beyond the capabilities of the devs. Implementing anything for Logi is apparently beyond the capabilities of devs.

The front line gets update after update of new tanks, new weapons, new gear, new buildings, new intel gathering, new everything. Over the past year and a half logi has recieved 3 updates, all of which were minor quality of life changes that should have been in the game in the first place. They were: being able to rotate objects in a crane, being able to chose which seat you sat in when entering a vehicle, and being able to cancel the animation that locks you in place when grabbing something from a stockpile.

Thats it.

A year in a half and they did next to nothing for a large section of the player base. In fact, they made the experience EVEN WORSE by making delivery times longer, AS WELL AS implemented snowstorms and rainstorms which freeze cars, make it harder to see, and slow cars down.

There has been no gameplay changes, no updates, no big improvements. And its not for a lack of possibilities. The community has been giving suggestions and ideas for years! They could add:

-Trains in the late game to improve deliveries
-Materials/incentives that you collect from the front line to deliver back to a factory (currently, after making a delivery most Logi just leave their car and respawn back to the back line because there is NO incentive to make the 20 minute journey back by car, leading to lines upon lines of cars that are unused at the frontline)
-Faster trucks in the late game
-A ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ pen and paper map to follow
-Faster transfer times between cars and stockpiles
-Being able to load vehicles onto the back of flatbed trucks
-Towable vehicles

Playing logistics is tedious, annoying, and no longer satisfying. The experience has gotten worse and worse to the point where logistics players in the game WENT ON STRIKE because being Logi was just dozens of obnoxious things pilling up to one unbearable experience.

I recommend that you do yourself a favor and don't buy this game until the game gets a decent sized Logistics update that the players are happy about.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCo-operation oriented MMO with no levelling, no "classes", and very little place for one's own ego. Players get "better" at Foxhole by increasing their knowledge and skill*, not by some dull grind-based mechanism, and everyone can contribute from day 1.

The learning curve for the game as a whole is a little but like tackling a climbing wall blindfolded with various people shouting instructions. That said, a little common sense goes a remarkably long way, and most of the community seem pretty decent. The wiki is also fairly comprehensive and well worth a read.

It does suffer somewhat from the curse of all MMOs, namely that unless you're in with a group of mates (which I'm not) you've got to go through the process of finding an appropriate clanguildorporationsquad to join (which I am yet to do ... that's the next challenge) in order to gain meaningful access to the more complex content. However, the lack of characters with baked-in skillsets does at least mean that anyone can join in anything as required, so no "we need a medic and you're an artillery gunner so you're no use" rubbish here, thank goodness.

I probably ought to add something about the game mechanics but in all honesty it's hardly worth it. It's a more-or-less top down shooter that aims for a gamey-realism, which it largely achieves. There are some utterly surreal elements to it, but overall it works. Logistics has a few user-unfriendly aspects relating to removing Stuff from public stockpiles, but they're not the end of the world.

Graphics? Well, it's Marmite. Siege Camp have gone for a look that evokes the grubbiness and ruination of war, and IMHO it works brilliantly. Foxhole is not a "pretty" game by any stretch, but visually it's very atmospheric indeed.

TL;DR - Well worth the price of a delivery pizza and a beer, and much longer lasting.

* Horrible word, but you know what I mean. When I was at school in the 70s, skill was reputed to be a cow's posterior disease by those in the playground. Any beef / diary farmers able to confirm this?\t\t\t\t\t
