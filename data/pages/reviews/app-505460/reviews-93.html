\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried as a solo player for a bit, and with a small squad of friends, personally didn't find it very engaging.

Tutorials and training are poorly done, making it pretty unfriendly to people who aren't familiar with these types of games. - It gives you a vague grasp of the basics, for a game that has a huge amount of complication, I would've expected at least a better beginners' guide.

The camera is disorienting and makes it hard to fight in rapidly changing situations from a top-down perspective. - It's hard to view what's ahead more than 50 metres unless you're aiming down iron sights, making it hard to plan routes ahead. - I'm used to Total War top-down cameras and fast-paced action, but this took it to ridiculous levels when needing to push through multiple houses and alleys.

Terrain climbing on natural objects is janky and glitchy, moreso on rocky terrain.

Stepping into ankle-deep shallow water somehow turns you into the slowest man alive, and you put away your weapon, despite still being able to use it if you re-equip it, it makes it feel almost like a bug when it happens?

The few gunfights I had were fun when they were fixed positions or slow pushes, but the above just made the game unappealing.\t\t\t\t\t
