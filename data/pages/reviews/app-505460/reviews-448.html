\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelcome everyone to, Company Of Heroes the MMO.

Pros:
-you can be an engineer and dig trenches and build everything (really, everything in the game) or guide the technological progress towards a V2 and 'nuke' the enemies (not a real nuke, but still feels good);
-you can be a logistics guy and save your buddies with that bomb transport (of actual bombs) to the front lines and run them over at the same time;
-you can be a soldier and... repeatedly die after taking way too many items with you from base;
-you can be an artillery guy and do the truck to gun shuffle while loading shells;
-you can be a medic and heal all sorts of STDs and other organ trench related diseases;
-you can be a tank dude and slowly drive backwards and forwards until you blow up, but you do get to run over people and that's fun... even if most of the time its your own people.

Cons:
-you need a microphone.
-the French can play it.

Side effects may include:
-bleeding out;
-having your eardrums shattered by the voice chat's unholy loud default volume;
-artillery induced PTSD, trench foot, developing an irrational hate of the French;
-nightmares about having your bicycle stuck in a bomb crater or finding oneself between many locked vehicles with no wrenches left in the world;

Also, changing classes is just a different uniform (literally). Just equip a medic uniform and you're a medic. You can use all items regardless of uniform, just not as effective.

The game is also really good at instilling a sense of camaraderie like no other war game I played so far and even if you're playing alone, just get a mic and fun is guaranteed.

I love just sitting in a trench, crouching for my life while getting shelled all the way to Australia when suddenly a guy charges from the back blasting Erika on voice chat only to get mowed down by MG fire after missing his grenade throw. So yeah, the community is great.

12/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole. A grand war simulator that seeks to fill your every want of gameplay.

Foxhole is an open world PVP war game. Its world is split into hexagonal sectors each with its own subsectors. The game is a tug of war between the two forces: Colonials and Wardens. Each with vehicles, weapons, uniforms and equipment based of historical ones.

Every single unit you see on the field in the game is a player. Every tool, piece of equipment and so on is created by other players. The creation of each item is not quick, it takes time, and therefore every utility in the game is worth more than in other games, as you can end up without it if not taking care.

Each war in the game usually takes from 1week to 1month to finish. This is dependant on how the players play, what the new meta after each update is, and how many players are involved through the war. In most cases the wars last for the lesser amount. But in rare cases they go on for quite long.

The game has a heavy focus on voice chat, so without it you may be ignored often, or get negative feedback. But with many players this is not an issue, and they are understanding of it.

There is a couple classes which I've played with after 106.4hours in the game. The way I play them are as follows

Infantry soldier: These are the grunts who fight for ground control. While playing this role you usually attempt pushing together with a squad to attack, and defend the frontlines.

Squad leader: This is the player who takes control of a squad, and tells them what to do. They are usually equipped with a radio and binoculars as to be able to scout for the group and instruct them in where to maneuver and where to focus their attack, or defense.

Medic: The person who tries to avoid combat, stays close to the back of the squad or frontlines and works his way forward to where friendlies are wounded to heal them, or have died to ressurect them.

Engineer: You build defensive bases, and structures. You repair tanks, and place mines for enemy tanks. You maintain your bases, and make sure they are healthy.

Partisan: These are the people who sneak in behind enemy lines attempting not to be spotted. Once behind you will steal trucks / destroy trucks, steal equipment. Blow up bridges, place mines on heavily travelled logistics routes and gather intel of easy to flank areas.

Sniper: Simple enough, get down somewhere high up if possible. Now get some long range kills.

Tank crew: These are the players who focus on destroying enemy forward defensive structures and destroying other vehicles, or buildings. This allows the Infantry soldiers to push, and take ground.

Logistics: These are the backbone of the entire game. As logistics theres little fighting, but always work to do.
Logistics gather supplies, and create weapons, tools, vehicles, uniforms, etc. Without them every other player would be running around with only their starter pistol and fists.

Transport: These are the guys that transport infantry back and forth from the front. Mostly its just TO the front, as most die when there.

I would heavily suggest this game if you want a game which goes on for long, and has resets often, with new content most of the time. If you have friends its even better as its easier to communicate and coordinate your roles.\t\t\t\t\t
