\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMaybe I'm missing a mechanic, maybe I'm missing the point. Foxhole sounded intriguing when I first had some videos sent my way from fellow gamers. A top down, shooter where you're a soldier in an alternate universe WW1. What could go wrong !? In my opinion, some things did. The player driven economy is pretty much set up already, there's no specialty to shoot for. Do I want to be a gunsmith ? Work in an arsenal manufacturing arty ? What about designing ships for the fleet ? What of soldiering ? Do I want to be a rifle man , or an artillery man, what about a sniper or and engineer. There's really no real road map as to where to go. It seems like if you don't manufacture your own stuff or part of a regiment ( clan ) there's really no incentive to play. I felt like I was just wandering around waiting for something to happen or running towards the gunfire. Combat is fun, I admit, it just gets stale after awhile. I'll wait on this game for alittle bit, maybe future patches wills make it a bit more playable.\t\t\t\t\t

This a community driven game. There are multiple roles for you to play and many ways to help your team win. The wars last anywhere between days to a week plus depending on how hard everyone is fighting. Honestly, I'm not sure what took me to long to write a review for this game.

Highly recommend picking it up -- especially when its on sale.
