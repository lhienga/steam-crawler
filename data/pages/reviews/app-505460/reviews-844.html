\r\n\t\t\t\t\tREVISED REVIEW: Altough the fundamental design flaws are still present, they created a very good game out of it. But I still have to warn you about a very obvious thing, the thrill of battle is not always present here, most of the time you will try to manage your inventory. Which weapon to take? How much ammo to carry? Is there an enemy soldier awaiting in a bush to shoot me? Can I scavenge enough ammo out in the field? Why bridges are so fragile? Why people are suicide running with a bicycle? ...or why on earth they love to shoot people on bicycles with bazookas?!

OLD REVIEW: Beautiful thinking, poor utilization. Voted negative because problems outweight the better aspects of the title.

Concept of the game is one big battle over several instanced maps connected together with boundaries which allow you to carry your avatar and any vehicle you are controlling to any instance connected. And fight with other players in real time. Think of it like an isometric battlefield game. Almost all defensive buildings (Except town houses, garrisons and some natural details), walls, sandbags, artillery pieces, armor, working vehicles, weapons and ammunition are created by players with a rigorous effort, and wasted by same players without thinking. Camera is free to rotate (Which allows you to see farther than intended by using the upper corners of your screen) but in some situations it gets frustrating because you constantly need to rotate it to see the enemy. And if there is an obstacle between you and the hostile unit (Building or player) you can't see it. You have to have a clear visual. This feature allows players to utilize stealth effectively.

Sounds good right? Sorry but no. Because game allows players to change sides (Or use alt account to join other side) and spoil everything for that army (Empty stocks, waste resources, destroy vehicles, block routes, learn about strategic installations and report back) without any consequences. I know it is quite hard to solve this problem but IT IS a problem and it is STILL THERE.

If you want to join, know all these things. Your side may win, or lose but like the war in real life: "It is only fair for the winning side"\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe concept of Foxhole is what got me interested. I like the idea of having players themselves shape the outcome of a war that can last days, weeks or months, even when you're offline. There's nothing more surprising for you than to find out that the base you helped build up last night was overrun & taken by the enemy when you log on the next morning. Gives you a sense that its worth to play it.

Another good thing about the game is that you can be whoever you want to be. Want to be a medic? Sure. Be a tank gunner? Just ask. Be a spotter for artillery & lay down fire on enemies? Check. If you want to be your standard run-of-the-mill infantry then Foxhole's got you covered.

There's also the logistics side of things, usually supplies/weapons are made away from the fronts of battle but almost everytime you spawn in, you're going to be seeing trucks going back & forth. Its the essence of teamwork & can help you if used correctly.

Overall I find Foxhole to be a good game. Lots of players are super friendly & there's also the occasional guy that just likes to play music in voicechat for everyone to hear. There's a good amount of playstyles that fit everyone's taste & with the persistent wars going over long periods of time, it makes for some interesting stories. No battles in the wars are the same.
