\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a game built upon a very interesting concept with a perspective that is inherently clunky. Even with 300 hours played, it can be hard to tell what is going on a lot of times especially in logistical regions. The fairy tale idea the devs had for the conception of this game was that each player would work together like the peons in RTS games in perfect tandem, all playing off of each other.

In reality, the game is a huge ♥♥♥♥ measuring contest between every player to try and get as many commends and/or make dumb jokes in global chat as possible to get attention. Even when your side wins, it isn't a real heartfelt victory because most players are trying to say stuff in chat about "Look what I did! I hit one of the last grenades onto the enemy town hall!" Even between the larger clans of the army, they compete and have lots of drama against one another all war long about how one is better than the other.

The perspective is also a huge component to this negative review. The perspective played in this game makes it lend itself not to skill in one-on-one combat; but rather your ability to farm advanced resources for your team to put your army at a technical advantage over the other. This means spending hours of time investing into the success of your team, and VERY rarely seeing the actual result of it. The most satisfaction you can get from this game is going onto foxholestats.com and seeing that your army took enemy ground. If you have any goals to be productive in your day, I would recommend against this game.

Lastly, the developers (god bless their heart) are a small team and get quarterly updates out. The things they are adding are so fundamentally besides from what they should be focusing on, though, as their game is currently dying. One of the "biggest" features of this new update was "No-Man's-Land" mechanic, where-- get this-- artillery does damage to the environment over time. Oh, wow, something that should've been in the game already? Oh, and wait, the artillery cant even completely destroy trees? And the craters that the artillery makes are clearly 3D assets placed into the ground upon impact, and not actually morphologically changing the geometry of the world? Cool!

With this "No-Man's-Land" update, the biggest player influx that's been seen has just occurred. Mark my words, it will plummet again within 2 months when the influx of new players become fed up with the style of game that it is. This game can be fun in very short sessions, but it is really nothing more than a game based off the "Robbers Cave" experiment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t12 Hours in and I already want to write a review. I feel that in itself says alot. Anyway, lets get on with it!

Foxhole, the MMO Sandbox WWII Top Down Shooter Thing. Im not gonna delve too deeply into the games mechanics, as they are very broad. But I will cover some of the core mechanics.

We've seen the premise before in games such as Planetside 2. A consistent war between players lasting several IRL weeks or months. However Foxhole has taken this idea and gone full bore. Everything about the war is fueled by players. Every gun, uniform, ammo clips, grenade, bandage, can of fuel, tank, mortar shell... (you get it) is manufactured by players. Every trench dug by players. This is not shooter all in, this is an RPG.

Wanna fight in the trenches beside your friends, clawing for inches of ground in an onslaught of offensive and defensive gameplay? or would you rather take the often more peaceful (but not always) life of a backline soldier? will you mine resources for manufacturing? deliver much needed medical supplies to the frontline? will you set up an artillery encampment, a joint venture with your fellow soldiers to support those fighting on the frontlines? Thats really up to you, and it is up to you all the time. Your role will depend greatly on the gear you took from a friendly base, Foxhole relies on players understanding the equipment, and being frugal.

For example, a typical frontline infantry "roleplayer" will take a rifle, two clips for that rifle, and bandage to stop bleeding if they are shot. No one, including medics, can self-heal. Be nice to your medics, they chose to save your ass rather than shoot baddies. Oh you want to be medic? Well dont take a rifle then, you wont be using it, save it for a comrade. Take a pistol, and load up on medical supplies. Okay now your bored of being a medic, so just dont take medical, just grab a rifle, two clips, a bandage and get back on the frontline soldier!

And there is far more to do than I have mentioned here. 12 hours is not enough time to have experienced all aspects of Foxhole, and for that reason alone I say its great value. And with an active and involved development team, I feel reasonably certain we have more great content to sink our bayonets into.

Graphics are kind on the eyes, but nothing to write home about, so I wont. Same can be said of sound design, its authentic sounding and I have no complaints.

9/10 I wish linking up with my buddy was a little easier, but I personally cant think how to do that without messing with the core mechanics, so I will suck it up.

Bertie
