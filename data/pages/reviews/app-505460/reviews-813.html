\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are many reasons why I do and do not recommend this game, however, the negatives outweigh the positives. First, the queue times. The queue times are absolutely horrible. As I am writing this I have waited over one hour to join a region all for "balance".

Secondly, as a warden player, I feel like the Colonials are bailed out every time we beat them by the moderators. For example, in War 35 in Endless Shore the Colonials had lost everything except for a fort and the Wardens walled it off to prevent the Colonials from leaving the area. Then the mods, from what I have heard, removed the walls in some way and demolished the "Goblin Prison" as we Wardens call it.

Thirdly, the aiming system in the game is not the best. When an enemy player is behind a low wall or behind a high sandbag wall standing on a low one it is extremely hard to hit them. You can have your cursor right on them and shoot but every time it snaps to the sandbag. The only way to get them to move is to destroy the sandbag or throw grenades and this brings me into my next point.

Lastly, grenade spam. I know the devs have already removed HE grenade stacking but something also has to be done about Frag Grenade stacking or it has to take longer between throws of each grenade. Grenade spam annoys me because in early game you can pretty much do nothing against it other than run away and the enemy doesn't even have to be close to nade spam. They can be out of sight and range with grenade launchers and still spam you.

Overall, until these issues are fixed I would not recommend getting the game unless you want to be very annoyed and almost not be able to play the game because of the queue times.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredibly good cooperative large-scale game.

Play whatever role you want. More of a builder than a fighter? no worries, let the logistic guys know that you're building an advanced outpost at a location and they'll bring you the supplies you need to build it. Or maybe you're more of an action builder? Just go on the frontline, grab some building mats and start setting up foxholes, barricades, and artillery pieces while under fire to push the frontline forward.

Want to set up a sneak attack behind enemy lines? Motivate a few soldiers, get a vehicle, grab whatever gear you feel is going to be needed, and get working on it. You'll be able to do so by ground or by water.

More of a support guy? Harvest ressources, refine them, and supply your soldiers on the front with everything they need, weapons, gas masks, explosives, armored vehicles, and much much more! Or just grab a medkit and go save some lives!

To resume the game experience, you start by selecting the faction you want to play with during this war (wars can last up to multiple weeks) amongst two factions (basically the allies or the axis) and then select your zone of deployment amongst a variety of regions having their own particularities that will greatly affect the gameplay (bridges, islands, moutains, water, etc...) Once you've made your choice, you'll spawn into your team's main base in the region, where you'll have access to building (vehicles) and crafting facilities (materials, refined ressources, and weapons/gear) as well as stockpiles of different sorts, don't hesitate to communicate with your team to know where the front is, and what they need up there.

Pretty much everything is worth playing, from the gunfight to the building to the leadership to the logistic, it's all satisfying because you notice the effect of your efforts. A single man really can change the course of a battle in this game, and here's a few example where i ended up being that man:

Tempest is a part of the map where water reigns, and bridges are often the only route to reach a position by ground. The thing is, bridges can be destroyed, but when they're behind 2km of enemy bunkers, foxholes, and bloodthirsty men, well taking them down becomes slightly more complicated. But i had a plan, all i needed was a few brave souls willing to follow my lead, and i did find them. The plan was simple, grab a flatbed barge, slip in a few troopers at the back of it, fill the barge with HE grenades and use the night to get to the bridge from the water. We succeeded, took out the bridge, and then pulled back about a hundred meters away, just at the limit of the reach of our rifle's frag-grenade launcher, and denied repairs to any engineer closing in on the bridge or enemy soldier coming to defend them with a grenade barrage, effectivelly cutting short their whole supply of men less than a kilometer away from their main base. The front was dramatically changed and in a matter of minutes our men had reached the other side of the bridge, giving us overwhelming control over this tile of the world map for a whole day.

Another day, our men had well advanced on tempest, but the enemy armored units and the corridor-like terrain were both a serious issue that stopped our progress in its trail. I took the time to observe the terrain, that corridor was made by a mountain, which top was only accessible from a place that was under our control. I filled a truck with enough mats to build an artillery piece and some ammos, then went up there, placed that arty, and started peppering the area, razing every single structure and forcing the armor out either by blowing them up or just by the mere threat of taking an artillery hit. Once again, this broke the enemy's moral (when you controle most of the map you're in, enemies tend to choose another tile to connect to, giving you the number advantage), and allowed for a final push from our team.\t\t\t\t\t
