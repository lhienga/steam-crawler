\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is pretty fun. I love how they're creating on a new idea. Wether you play logistics or fight. You always feel the impact you make with fighting synergy, medic support, or delivering that vital piece of cargo that would help teammates turn the tide of a battle. Especially if you think strategically. The community has been decent so far. There are those smack talkers so I hear, but I personally havent run into any through voice chat or text chat at all.

Cons- Very hard yet short learning curve to brand new players. The bad side..the game is so deep that people don't play to optimal performance (unoptimized logi runs, armored vehicles not distrubuted, trucks not distributed, ect.). As in real life wars. The game lacks generals and thus relies on blind orders and player emotion to attack and defend. The game makes lots of space for large scale tactical engagment, but the game limits size to the squads. Even Battle feild 4 had commanders and its not very tactical at all. Last con is the team sizes. I don't know of a solution, but one side is always overpopulated than the other. This usually determines who wins.

All in all, its definitely worth a try. If you learn the game by watching youtube videos ect, then I think you'll have a blast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdated Review:

Game is fun and addictive but I can't recommend it in it's current state.

Review is written from a Warden only player;

The main positives are the community and the feeling of immersion in a persistent world game;
- Community is brilliant, provided you are a team player.
- Welcoming towards new players
- When things come together it is rewarding.
- Game play is vast enough to accommodate a lot of interests.

Why i cannot recommend it right now (understandably it is early access);
- Logistics is unrewarding (but essential).
- Fighting / shooting can be buggy and feel cheap at times.
- You need hours to properly set anything up (this is less relevant to those who can reliably spend 5+ hours a day on the game.)
- Community moderation is non transparant meaning issues are never really addressed.
- Game moderation is almost non existant unless you are friends with a moderator - even then repeat offenders are never penalised. Case in point - you could spend 24 real life hours on something just have a "alt" who will buy the game just to sabotage your team come along and blow up / toss your inventory into the ocean. Despite providing proof, there has been no action from the devs or community management to stop this - the issue has become worse in the 2 years since my previous review.

Final Note:

Great game - i have enjoyed playing it but in its current state i would tell new players to avoid. The devs and moderation seemingly have no ambition to support it - its essentially an EA cash grab, that despite being an MMO type game they have no intention to support it in a meaningful way post patch 1.0. This even includes communicating there plans going forward - you will get fun out of it, but unless the devs step up and combat the griefing and make logistics more tolerable, you may have a dead game in a year meaning your money is wasted.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought Foxhole quite a while ago. It has changed quite a lot since then and will continue to do so. So if you are looking for a "finished" game this ain't the one.

That said, Foxhole has been an absolute joy to play and has no comparison in my opinion.

I have met amazing people in this game and made some genuine friends along the way. The game is fun, collaborative and the vast majority of people are sound people willing to help you out when needed. Sure there are some people that just aren't nice but that's life. The moderation policies were a bit too relaxed in my opinion however in the last while I have noticed them taking a stronger stand and I have high hopes for it in the future.

The Devs are very passionate about this game and are making a real masterpiece. Yes they have made bad decisions in my opinion but that is MY OPINION. No decision is going to please everyone and if you are getting this game you have to be ok with that. The devs have demonstrated that they take into account feedback, but again, everyone wants different things and so not everyone's feedback is put into action which is completely understandable.

A major thing that I love about foxhole is I paid around 20 quid (euros) for this game and have gotten so much out of it! No DLC, no additional funding requested. It is one of the most cost effective purchases I have ever made no doubt.

To summarise, Foxhole ain't perfect but I cannot recommend this game enough. It has truly been an absolute pleasure to play.

And to the Devs, know that we appreciate what you are doing!\t\t\t\t\t
