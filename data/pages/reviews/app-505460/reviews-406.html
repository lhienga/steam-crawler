\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMost similar to: Planetside
Rating: 5/10, there's something really, *really* good in here but it's buried under a lot of crap
Recommended for: Eve Online logi players, Planetside veterans, Factorio enthusiasts, people on the spectrum.

update 7 mar '22, recent update addressed many logi painpoints but many other issues remain. still negative, but i intend to try again in ~6mo after next update. hopefully they make building/salvaging more engaging.

Good:
-Satisfying, in a 'contributing to team effort' kind of way. You're playing with the lads & working on a group project but the project is WAR
-Frontline gameplay can generate good stories (ex "that one time with the stolen tank")
-A complete war machine. Players mine resources, refine them into materials, manufacture those into ammo & explosives, truck those to the front, and shoot them at the enemy. You can have an hours-long play session where you did vital work for the team and never even held a gun.
-Combat engineering (ie digging trenches/building pillboxes at the front) is a gameplay niche not present in many other games.

Bad:
-Game is janky and unpolished, lots of little pain points in minute-to-minute gameplay. Many core gameplay areas need a polish pass to make the game less annoying, more consistent.
-Asymmetric faction 'balance' that isn't very balanced at all. Both greenteam and blueteam have several valid complaints.
-Player culture. It can get pretty toxic sometimes and the devs are completely uninterested in managing/curating their community. Never look at Global chat. It blew my mind when I saw I couldn't just right click-> report someone when they started going off about nazi ♥♥♥♥ in global; that's a standard feature for decades, why does Foxhole not have this?
-Cross-faction sabotage/'alting'. Players are locked to greenteam/blueteam for the duration of the war, but if you have another steam account you can get a 2nd account during a sale, put that on the other team, and start driving their trucks/tanks/etc. into the water. In extreme instances whole bases have been griefed. Devs don't care.
-Building is very unengaging. Hold materials in inventory, click structure with hammer. Tape down LMB and walk away from your PC. Come back in 3 minutes to move over to the next bunker piece then resume AFK.
-Resource gathering, see above complaint re: building. Stand in a scrap field, click scrap piles with your hammer. Buddy in my clan would just put a weight on his mouse button, weight on his W key, and afk-walk from one end of the field to the other, for hours, to get the scrap.

My final, most damning point, and the reason I'm (still) recommending you don't spend time playing this game:
-Devs lack the resources to realize Foxhole's true potential. Not enough people, not enough money, not enough skills. The game is mediocre, "good in spite of itself", and they don't have enough man-hours and the right skills to un♥♥♥♥ it. It's been available to the public for almost five years now and this tedious-yet-oddly-satisfying pile of jank is the state of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately logi is not very fun.

The way you do logi is bonking salvage nodes and filling up material containers or trucks. But that process takes a long while each trip and it's very mindnumbingly monotonous. I tend to do solo logi, which I focus on stocking up the local refinery with bmats and emats..

Don't get me started on fuel trucks. You can only pull 3 cans max at a time and a singular fuel truck can fit 200 bloody cans. Game's been out since 2017 but there still isn't a button to auto fill instead of wasting my time having to shift click every 10 seconds or so. (Do correct me if the pull time is wrong)

Some will say "why don't you make your own fuel then to pull out faster?".
Problem is refineries tend to accumulate fuel cans that number in the thousands. Seeing over 6k cans in refinery isn't rare and no one is going to bother pulling it due to having to pull it all yourself without respite. That isn't gameplay that's wasting someone's time.

As far as I've seen they want to make logi more clan reliant. Have people scroop for salvage, have some others ship them or truck them to refineries. Other people use the products from the refinery in factories and then move the complete product over to their clan stockpile or to a frontline BB.

Problem is that really screws over people doing solo logi. Even with a flatbed it can only carry 5k salvage which isn't much in the grand scheme of things. You have to go back and forth every single time and you only make about 2.5k bmats per trip. Not all that much considering that thousands can get used up on frontlines on an hourly basis or so. It also doesn't help that the distance between refineries and factories are soo bloody unnecessarily long. Sometimes you have to go to a whole different hex on the map just to get your salvage to a refinery.

Best town I ever did solo logi in was Ogmaran (Before the devs ♥♥♥♥♥♥ it over so hard that only the seaport or a garage or was it a different thing?).

I liked Ogmaran because it had a high number of resource nodes in 1 hex, it always had a nice ratio of salvage:components fields.
It had 1 refinery, 2 factories, 1 mass production factory, a garage and a thing where you built those immovable machine guns/AT guns unless you had a crane.

Even though trucking stuff from the nearby fields to the refinery took a bit longer that I would have liked, I still enjoyed it as I didn't have to through very awkward routes to get there.

Ogmaran was easy to deal with for solo logi and was also the first logi town I was in when I first started playing, now it is a husk of what it once was unfortunately.

Now yes, I can just ignore logi entirely and focus on the fighting aspect. But that's like ignoring an entirely crucial mechanic in a game. Without logi, people would have to walk all the way to the frontline armed with only pistols.

14/2/2023
In the last part of my review I rambled on about something that was not important in the review. I am also redoing this as certain things have changed.

So I still do not recommend this game unless you feel joy from not feeling like anything is an achievement. There are "alts" which are people that join team to sabotage it to help the other, it's like if teamkilling had no repercussions on the perpetrator. However these alts can destroy hours upon hours of work with next to no consequences for their actions AND THEY KNOW IT. There is zero moderation at all in this game.

Even if someone were mass reported for an allegation with video proof, nothing is done to ban them permanently. They just get maybe a week ban at most and then they can just get back right to destroying people's hard work. Sometimes they're not even doing it to help the other side, they just like seeing people' reactions and they know nothing permanent will be dealt unto them. This problem is rather far more worse than a blatant hacker as I don't even know of any alts that have been banned permanently.

The utter lack of moderation has caused a noticeable number of the community to be very paranoid about alts. Some fears were founded, but most of the time it just ended up being a newbie being accused of just having no clue what to do and not do. A worser case of this is a streamer that accused a new player whose only sin was trying to build a Bunker Base (think of a base with defenses or small drop off points) and not knowing how to do it because the in-game tutorial is ♥♥♥♥ and you only figure out from another player telling you about it, you can search for the video if you want but I won't link it because no need for more fuel to the fire.

Facilities, my my.
The devs really would like you to believe that Foxhole is definitely a 1.0 full release game (no bug guaranteed), yet still multiple glitches persist and balancing choices that seem to entirely ignore any of the feedback from the community. They used to do QnA sessions with questions from the community, now they don't anymore and are already making a new game.

So to actually talk about facilities, they are painful and I have never felt like it was an achievement or it was satisfying to "engage" with. Trying to deal with the bullshittery of the devs telling us to use facilities but putting heavy maintenance costs on them if you think of building anything bigger to build trains and the like while having defences because "too much strain on servers". Why add facilities to the game if you are going to limit us because the game can't handle it?

The introduction of facilities also marks the dreaded vehicle variants. If you look at the vehicle roster of Foxhole, you'll see quite a decent amount of vehicle types. However they have locked all but the "base" vehicles from being built in the vehicle garage. Now these variants require a facility vehicle pad in that's also upgraded with the right upgrade to built the variant you want. However, there is no easy way to tell where the right vehicle pad exists if you are a person going solo (with other random/friends in a tank). So you have to ask in game chat if there is a vehicle pad available for X vehicle, in-game map is useless in conveying any useful information of that type.

Another thing, facilities really kill off randoms joining together to supply things. Logistics is of great importance in Foxhole, and a decent chunk of it is just random players sending supplies to whatever front need it. With facilities though, there is no way for randoms to be able to help with running other players facilties on and off short of joining the group that owns the facility. In-game coordination tools are horrid, even the devs tell us to use Discord for better communication. I don't mean joining squads in Foxhole, I mean one-off communications with people you probably aren't going to see again coordinating just for the 1-4 hours you happen to play together with that isn't being right next to eachother.

The in-game "tutorial" is still a joke, if I didn't get abducted by a random dude on spawn island wrangling up newbies to teach, I would have refunded the game before it even hit an hour of playtime.

The game has so much potential being a one of a kind where you won't get large clans being the strongest by virtue of hoarding ♥♥♥♥ since time began. Sure there are still clans that hoard supplies en masse, but it ain't like EVE where you gotta play for 2 years before you can join an existing group. And with the end of a war comes with the GREAT RESET, where the technology tree randomizes and resets captured territory reverting to starting war state and we get to do it all over again.



TLDR; Devs don't even communicate at all with the community nor even tell us why they don't communicate anymore while implementing balance changes no one asked for certain things.\t\t\t\t\t
