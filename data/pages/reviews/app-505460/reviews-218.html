\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview is based off an initial 4 hours play.

I expect this game might be good once you work out how to play it, but it does an awful job of teaching you. I think joining a pre-existing group is a requirement, I don't mean you have to cooperate to win, I mean you need another person to learn how to play. I know some people like this "work it out yourself" game play. For me that's fine in a single player, but it's a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ MMO design choice.

For my first hours I jumped into an existing war, deployed where all the other people are at a contested area, killed a few people, died a few times. It was quite fun, but felt pointless. I know that I shot people because I saw them fall, but there's no 'score', confirmed kill, nothing like that. It felt like attrition warfare, throwing bodies against bullets until one of our supplies ran out. I took a break and came back, the choke point had moved... so, something happened. No idea what.

The next day, a new war was starting. This is good. Surely joining at the beginning would give a clearer idea of what's going on. Not really. Again I spawned where all the other people are. They're digging a base. I was asked to vote on something and told how. No idea what that's for exactly, but I hope my tick helps. The enemy attacked our base and so we had some combat, again this was quite fun, but once they stopped attacking... I stood around aimlessly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole is a high concept hot mess and is [not to be missed].

We stopped to refuel and get more ammo when some jerk on our team took the armored car we were using. I was mad but whatever, that's the internet.

Five minutes later an officer from this jerk's clan came and found us. He apologized, said they had reprimanded the private, and offered to have their logistics players build us another armored car. 11/10. You can't pay for moderation this good.

It can be a bit of a slow burn sometimes. There's a Dwarf Fortress-level learning curve, but also friendly players who will teach you how to play. There are some undeniable quality of life issues. But there's also nothing else like it. When it's at it's best, and you're at sunrise, charging an enemy position with 60 other infantry, a half dozen tanks, and artillery support from an offshore gunship -- this is one of the most rewarding and fun games I've ever played. There's an amazing feeling of being a cog in a giant war machine, but if you're the right balance of bold & patient you can still make a real impact on your own. I thought I wouldn't like this game at first -- my friend had to cajole me into checking it out -- and I'm happy to admit I was completely wrong, my buddy and I are now constantly texting each other about how the war is going when we're not playing.

This is an RTS game where you play as one of the basic units and it is just... it's just a treat. Also, amazing graphics & especially the SOUND DESIGN is really, really good. Listening carefully to hear distant engines, footsteps, or gunfire is a huge part of the game. The alternate history setting is tastefully done, and the vehicle designs are great. The maps are cleverly designed and change around each war.

Your dad will think this game is cool. Just check it out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this game. I recommend this game.

But I am not a social player. I am for the most part a solo player but one who enjoys seeing other players doing their thing. I have been in a corp for a year in Eve Online in the past and tried a couple WoW guilds but in the end I find them annoying. Haha

I did not join or ask to join a Clan in Foxhole. My weeklong experience was as a solo player. I salvaged about 450 thousand units of salvage and components, and delivered about 250 thousand value to the front. The rest went to public. I spent some time building a small bunker base with a diesel generator, materials factory, and a light vehicle upgrade station. I have heard it said that the logistics and building in this game is a pain. It was a bit difficult to get into it and I watched a few youtube vids and looked up what buttons to push and when a few times but in the end it was worth it. Once you start to get past the learning curve it was rewarding. I wouldnt build my own bunker again though as it served no purpose towards the group effort though I did drop off a few upgraded basic dunn trucks in town.

I can see that the real action in this game is as part of a clan and I think that you should join one if you play. But that does not suit me generally.

I did not pvp in this game other than two enemy kills. One I was making a delivery and came across a friendly and an enemly. It only took a small swerve of my Dunn transport to run the enemy over. The second I did not feel like driving my empty truck back so I drove it over the blown out bridge into the enemy lines, jumped out and managed to kill one before I died. So I do nit have much to say on the pvp but that it looks like it could be quite rewarding.

I do not regret buying it and if money is not an issue you should too even if you only play a hundred hours like me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a competently developed isometric shooter/strategic RTS. The community isn't too bad, just lots of whining. I liked it for the first few hours but the further I got into it the harder I bounced off. At the end of the day this game is primarily about two things: grinding logistics or fighting in the meat grinder. Either you're grinding on making or delivering supplies, or you're grinding on enemy troops and positions. Yeah you can sneak behind enemy lines or you can build infrastructure but it still all boils down to the two primary grinds. If you're willing to jump into a big clan of randos and participate in large scale ops then it has a lot going for it but if you're looking for something to play casually with a couple buddies or solo I'd recommend looking elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t
