rarely you can meet players who just came to grief you, but game has enough ways to prevent griefing, i have only met these maximum 5 times for my entire play so far. Mostly players just came to normal play, so dont listen to the "toxic player reports".

Conclusion: This game has updates almost every month or two, there is always interesting changes, improvements and new content, dev team communicates a lot with community on dev streams before major or regular updates. If you like these types of games then this is the best game in any perspective. There is a lot to try out, all it takes with enjoyment up to steady 400 hours.

Pros:
- Very well developed logistic part that maintain front line.
- Work you have done to public (to other players) that you decided important - feels very good.
- The roles are deep, any you choose for yourself next hour - you can do something original with many opportunities.
- Good in game get started tutorial.

Cons:
- 15% of all content is impossible for solo players, unless you find out someone in the game, who want to do something interesting together. There's no problems if you have at least one friend.
- If you overdone something you may feel like this game is about hard grindy job.
- Shooting from weapons (i.e. rifles, smgs) is mostly random then skill, there are no skill in this game, all you need is to pay attention to your cross and a position, but this is better for this up-down game.
(Every advise is based on my experience, it is up to you to decide, ofc thank you for paying attention)

Overall: 9 / 10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is fun, but it is a huge time sink for little reward. Any sense of accomplishment is easily erased the next time you log in and see the progress you made last night wipe off the map.

Biggest issue, in my opinion, with the game is with balancing. It is non-existent and what mechanisms there are, are ineffective and more detrimental than beneficial. After a prolonged slugfest, the game tends to swing in one faction's favor. That faction swarms the other with a snowballing effect due to a combination of "stacked" team and the more frustating to deal with, player count. It can become frustating to play when you are fighting 1v2 or 1 v 1.3 (for accuracy sake, I suppose). Regardless, once one faction loses player due to gameplay fatigue, boredom, or real life, the other faction quickly gains the upper hand which can further discourges players from the underpopulated faction from playing. Why play against a larger team you may wonder. Normally you would play for the "challenge", but in this game, in the long term, there is little that individual skill could change the course of the war.

Really the only way for a underpopulated team to win against a larger team, is to play more effectively and smarter than the other side, but as in all games with freedom of choice, getting people to work together for a long time is damn near impossible. The best way is to join a clan, which diminishes the importance of the individual non-clan player, other than being a meatbag with a gun or hammer who goes with the flow of the battle. As such, if you want to experience the "military" roleplaying aspect of the game, it is much better to join a clan otherwise people will refuse to listen to you.

The game can become fun, but you may have to invest hundreds of hours into it, so if you don't want to or have that time, don't buy the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI currently have 239 1014 hours played in this game and intend to increase that value. I remember trying out this game about a year years ago but it was in a completely different state than it is today. My first impression over a year ago was okay, but I could never really find where I needed to go. I felt lost and inefficient.

But now - in its current version, they have vastly improved the UI and feedback of the game. It is clear what the goals are and what is needed. The idea of the war being shared across regions on the same "master server" makes this effort easy to grasp as to where to pick up and go fight. They have a decent training area that covers the basic interactions of the game, the players are awesome to interact with, the fights are intense and there are so many different roles you can get into.

I have my days where I'm energized by a cup of coffee and I'm ready to rumble in the heat of battle. I can fight at the front lines using a wide variety of tools and weapons and change into any role I need, be it Medic, Rifleman, Grenadier, Sapper, Tank Crewman, scavenger (scoop up weapons/supplies at front line and bring them back), mortar crew...whatever ground troop role you can imagine.

There are days where I get home from work and I just want to chill and run logistics for the team. I can choose how complex I want to get with my logistic efforts for the war. If I want brainless work, I'll go gather materials and refine them so I can stockpile the main bases in the back. If I want to be a little more involved, I can do that, plus place orders for weapons and tools to stockpile the base. If I want to take it further than that - I can drive my logi truck around the front lines and communicate with the team to find out where shortages are, and create an operation with other people who want to do the same and all of a sudden I'll be involved in a large logistics operation involving multiple people coordinating their efforts to keep the boys at the front pushing.

, I've been involved in naval operations that raid the shores behind enemy lines and connected back to front line troops to cut off the enemy to win a region back.

People have the ability to commend based on your actions, which gives a bit of a "feel good" that someone recognizes your efforts. These commendations increase your rank.

I really enjoy following the war when I'm not playing. I'll spend a night doing logistics or building up defenses for a town we own - then wake up in the morning to login and see if anything made a difference. It feels good to see the front lines held, but you are left to wonder what happened when you lose territory. In any case, I really recommend this game. I've played lots of war games and I'm just surprised I'm into this game since its not a FPS game.

I feel like this game and its developers are really onto something here and I can't wait to see how they finish this product and what other types of games they might come up with. They design the game in such a way that it feels unique compared to any other game out there, and I like that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say, I bought this game on sale not expecting much due to the negative reviews and the onslaught of disappointing games in the last few years. After playing for a few hours, I had my frustrations like anyone does in a new game they're trying to learn, but success really felt rewarding. When you take people out in this game it really feels like an accomplishment, not some random stroke of luck. Of course, as in any battle, luck does play a role and it's usually hilarious.

For the people that are downing the gunplay/shooting mechanics, they are entitled to their opinion sure. But to say things that are along the lines of "there is no skill involved" or "its all luck;" they either don't understand how the mechanics work in the first place or they just have bad point/click accuracy. I personally love the shooting mechanics. At first it did feel random whether or not my shot would actually land but as I played the game my success went up exponentially because I learned from my mistakes and always tried something different until I was successful. In the current state of the game, put your reticle on people as close to you as possible between you and them. Dont aim beyond people as you'll be more likely to miss, and let your reticle focus. Then Fire. It's really as simple as that. Also, high ground really gives you a sight advantage in this game which plays into strategy. If you're shooting at someone that's behind cover and you're not, you're going to have a hard time (duh).

Unfortunately I have not delved much into logistics yet. So I can't write much of a review on it. I can say, to those who do logistics, they are very much appreciated and I prioritize keeping them alive as they build as much as possible. Without logistics, there'd be no way to win the war.

As far as my logistical experience, I did have a blast in the first 2 hrs of my gameplay rounding up a group of other noobs in a truck to repair a bridge that allowed us to take an opposing town. And that's a big part of what makes the game fun is a bunch of random people coming together and communicating over the voice chat to complete tasks.

This game is one of those that WILL challenge you, and you WILL need to EARN victory. If you're a casual gamer that wants everything handed to you and for winning to be mindless and easy, you won't have a good time.

In my current 45hrs of experience with the game I can actually say I would have paid full price for it which I didn't expect. It's been a long time since I've played an online multiplayer game that actually feels like a real breathing world with people that need to work together to succeed.\t\t\t\t\t
