\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to love this game, and I want to recommend it. But for all its uniqueness and the things that I love about the never ending world of WW1/2 style warfare there are two major issues at the moment that hold the game back.

The first and foremost is the problem it has with alters/griefers. These are players who have a second account for the game which they use to play on the opposite faction to bypass the account faction lock. This allows them to spy and record the locations of resource stockpiles, research information, minute to minute logistic requests, base locations, and strategic targets such as Stormcannons and feed that information back to outfits on their home faction. They have also been seen in both factions stealing vehicles, blocking roadways and ports, stealing research materials to hinder weapon upgrades and more such activities designed to hinder war efforts.

The second issue is no less serious however and it is in regards to how the game has treated logistics. While the logistics of the game is one of its core features, one may say even one of its selling points, little thought is given to the players (such as myself) who commit themselves to the role as Logi players, who fuel the war effort on both sides both figuratively and literally. Every clip of ammo, every artillery shell, every bandage is made by players for players to use and delivered to the frontlines at risk of life and limb. But the job has only gotten more and more burdensome as patches go on as new weapons and items are added, while quality of life for these players has been stripped away. And it was not an easy ride to start with, as a Warden main we often suffer from a mechanic due to the snowstorms that blanket our part of the map during parts of a war which can cause vehicles to freeze if left unattended making scrapping and logistics infinitely more time-consuming as you need to make sure vehicles are warmed and fires set up so you don't freeze to death while gathering materials. What is needed is more quality of life for Logistic players, not more weapons and infantry resources to juggle. More time efficient transport methods, better containers and Logistics UIs, better ways to coordinate logistic operations, ect. If your entire games selling point is going to be built around a player driven war economy, let the players who hold up that economy and supply the warfronts feel respected for their time and dedication.

If either of these issues get resolved in a meaningful way I would happily spend my days scrapping and hauling once more, and change my review to a recommend. Until then I'd suggest you give it a pass. Praise be to Callahan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReplayed this game again today on Christmas to give it another chance after I bought it about 3 months ago. There are redeemable aspects about it, namely the graphics/artstyle and the good experiences you can have out of teamwork and a communicating squad, but the negatives outweigh the positives for me to be able to recommend the game.

1. Time consuming and boring in a lot of ways. Most of the time, especially if you are playing logistics, you'll be simply travelling either on foot or in vehicle. A good portion of time is also spent trying to craft supplies when playing as logistics, where you need to wait for supplies to finish, pack supplies, put them into crates using cranes, close up the crates, etc. I know that some vehicles do not require attaching supply crates but it's still a lot of time for little payoff. Something to note as well is that if you encounter a spy while driving a logistics vehicle and do not react very quickly, they will blow up your vehicle (with small arms fire) and close to an hour of work may be wasted with all the supplies completely disappearing.
2. Lacking and frustrating combat. The way the game handles combat, largely due to the camera angle, leaves much to be desired. It's hard to aim shots up or down, so you'll basically never hit a target. Then for some reason every enemy has about twice the viewing distance that you have and manages to perfectly instant kill you, even if you haven't spotted a single enemy.
3. Encounters with toxic players. I have not encountered too many toxic players in this game but there have been a couple of experiences for me, with one of them occurring the first day I started playing and made me want to refund the game. In this instance, I was trying to do logistics for the first time and happened to not have a vehicle as I was not yet familiar with needing one. I was busy crafting some supplies, and an allied player comes up to me and shoots me for "being a spy" because I did not have a vehicle nearby. I sent them a message in chat explaining I was new, and they responded very rudely and did not seem to care. I posted a discussion post on this soon after it happened, and people there did help me on how to play logistics. I thank them, the community for this game can be helpful.
4. Odd controls. The way that the player jumps and climbs on ledges is inconsistent. Players cannot jump over small holes/trenches, instead needing to drop down and then climb out. Spaces that look like one may go through are actually blocked off by hitboxes and mean you spend time figuring out how to get around.
5. Locking vehicles and storing private supplies. Vehicle locks last only about 5 minutes I believe, and mean that players can steal your vehicle and run off with it even if you went away briefly to do something and were going to come back. I once built a tank for a friend and myself to use the next day on the frontline, and I left a sign saying to not take it, and the next day I checked and it was completely gone. All my time and effort building it was wasted. I understand that there are apparently private stockpiles, but as far as I can tell it is not explained for new players anywhere, and that leads to negative experiences.

I definitely understand the appeal of the game. A very realistic war/logistics simulator which makes it quite unique, but the game needs a lot of changes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI DO NOT RECOMMEND THIS GAME as of December 25, 2021:

If you are looking for a game that will take incredibly large amounts of time to perform the most menial tasks, this is the game for you.
If you want a developer team who says "they care about the game and community," but then spit in the faces of the community when the community asks them to correct the wrongs wrought by toxic players, then this is the game for you.
If you like to build things and spend your time creating defenses, this is not the game for you. The developers are out of the loop when it comes to the issues that builders have in the game. I have over 1k hours building in this game, and after constantly being promised fixes and updates to the building system the developers I have realized that the developers do not want building to be free of toxicity despite the fact that building in this game takes the most time, commitment, and foresight than any other aspect of the game.
I have decided to uninstall the game because, despite a perfectly useless reserve function in the game, the developers perpetuate the continuation of alternative accounts purposefully troll the building community and and then post about it on 4chan. The incident in question was a mass of alternative account ruining weeks of building players' work, on both factions within the game. These alternative accounts spent ~1 minute to waste weeks of player efforts into the game.
To put this into some context, building a base isn't just building a base. You have to build each piece (1 min 20s to 2 min for each piece), then you have to upgrade each piece (1 min 15s each) with a resource cost (that also takes time to make the resources), again you have to upgrade these pieces to a more expensive version (in time and in game resources, and these resources are time gated and everyone is after them). THEN you have to defend these bases from being actively destroyed, meaning setting up rotations between different players in different time zones to DEFEND these locations. THEN you have to spend EVEN MORE in game resources to prevent these bases from decaying, mind you these resources have to be produced at 'factories' that only have FOUR slots to produce them (this game has a few thousand players and each player gets 1 of those slots; did i also mention that they can take anywhere from 12-45 minutes to make?).
I would not recommend this game to any of my friends, they keep asking me about it, I have kept thinking that maybe the serious issues in this game will be fixed after every update, instead the developers make new vehicle toys for the players to test out, while completely ignoring OR giving band aids for wounds that need a tourniquet.
Additionally, there are serious issues on both factions concerning groups of players exerting control over many aspects of the game. The game is constantly exploited by the player base when it comes to technology (a major facet of the game concerning when vehicles and weapons will be available to be played with). Tech resources are horded on both sides because of a dev enabled slingshot method, meaning whoever is the first to develop a technology is actually spending more time and resources to learn a technology 'tier' while the other faction waits until the time is right, to exploit the catch-up mechanic to use the least amount of resources possible to research the same level 'tier' as their enemy and then sling shot into the next tier. This system was developed by the player base on the warden faction by 'Legacy Gaming' players known for exploiting survival games. Just check their website, they sure are proud of this 'legacy.'
DO NOT buy this game, and if you do, DO NOT play LOGISTICS, DO NOT play building/engineering, DO NOT play infantry (unless you love bridge fights that you can't win), DO NOT mine resources (known as scrooping).
Tanks are pretty cool, albeit each is useless in it's own way. Honestly, just go play War Thunder if you want to play in a tank.

Edited as of March 13th, 2022:

I have returned after a much needed break from this game and have found the game much in the same state as when I left.

For Building, Update 48 brought one positive change, the fact that you can now reserve all pieces to your bunkers with a button; this is good. However, building bases to the level of concrete is now heavily punished, by the simple fact that there is no benefit from making concrete bases anymore. With the change to how Concrete howitzers react to damage done by anything other than enemy artillery: it doesn't. Cutlers, ISGs, etc., they do not attract the attention of the howitzers you put on your base. You are better off completely ignoring the T3 level of base building and just focusing on spamming T2 bases everywhere. The interaction with broken defensive structures is still unacceptable. Construction time for these defenses remain the same and have inconsistencies throughout. Namely, To cost upgrade a T1 trench to a T2 trench vs. a T1 trench connector to a T2 trench connector is not the same. The Trench itself is longer than a trench connector and both serve the same purpose, yet the trench costs less to upgrade than the smaller connectors. This seemingly small detail is an example of the oversight that the game developers display within the 'building sphere.' Production time for Bsupp/Gsupp production is still too long considering that
the builder is already sinking massive amounts of time into building a base. Bsupp/Gsupp production is tied SOLELY to base building, these should not take 12 minutes to produce a batch, when considering the massive time sink for every other building task. The game should not punish players that are not within a massive regiment. Massive regiments should not be the basis for tuning in terms of building. When large regiments lose their base and leave the game, they leave the rest of the team to pick up the slack because developers tune the game toward the success large regiments can achieve. TLDR; Developers are still out of the loop with regard to building.
