\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLogistics gameplay: 2/10
Other gameplay: 8/10
Overall: 4/10
I really wish I could give this game a good review, but it just drains me to play. The actual fighting as well as building (to an extent) is really interesting. However, logistics is utter bullsh*t (steam wants me to censor it), and that's what really topples the game over. Without logistics, there is *nothing*. No guns, no ammo, no vehicles, no structures. And there is so rarely anything other than basic rifles and rifle ammo, *maybe* some anti-tank, that there's nothing to do other than blindly run at the enemy with a gun. This is not good tactics. If you want something more, if you want to help actually *win* a battle, you most likely need to do the logistics all by yourself. For the most basic supplies like rifles, rifle ammo, and respawns, this means:
1. Find a salvage truck (harder than it sounds)
2. Search on the map for a salvage yard that isn't empty (harder than it sounds)
3. Drive to the salvage yard (several minutes)
4. Sit around in the scrap yard holding down left click to gather salvage (tens of minutes)
5. Move over to the salvage truck and put the salvage away
6. Repeat from 4 until the truck is full
7. Drive the truck to the refinery (several minutes) and submit the salvage for refining
8. If you need more materials, repeat from 2
9. Put away your salvage truck in a storage depot (several minutes)
10. Wait for your materials to be refined (can take hours)
11. Find a supply truck (harder than it sounds)
12. Drive to the refinery and take the materials out (several minutes)
13. Drive to the factory and put the materials in (probably several seconds but could be minutes)
14. Wait for the supplies to be made (several minutes), take them out when they're done
15. Repeat from 12 if you need more
16. Drive to the front. (several minutes) Repeat from 1 if you are ambushed by partisans.
17. Unload the supplies, drive the truck back to a storage depot to put it away. (several minutes)
This is the most basic list of instructions I could think of for making basic supplies. All of these minutes add up to a huge amount of time. Trying to make anything else is even more complicated and tedious, *especially* if it has to be made in a facility rather than a public factory. Facilities are player-made factories that require constant and heavy logistical upkeep. Most of them are private, so you can't really use them. Worst of all is that they're nearly impossible to find on the map, and there is no indication of whether they're public or private.
It seems like some people like the routine of logistics, but I find it to be an anti-fun mechanic at the backbone of an otherwise very good game.\t\t\t\t\t
