\r\n\t\t\t\t\t A game of great quality with tremendous ups and downs. Foxhole, a birds eye view game where you join an online persistent war, as a single soldier, that can last up to weeks, is a fun game that tests your limits as well and allowing you to enjoy yourself. Although this game isn't perfect it's nothing to completely scorn. Within the game of "Foxhole" you gather resources for one of 2 teams, the Wardens or the Colonials. Both teams are the same besides looks. Whatever team you join you'll immediately join the war and it's chaotic state. Upon joining can be both confusing and scary being a new player not knowing what to do. There are many roles within "Foxhole" where you do your part to help push the war in your favor. What I've noticed there are 3 main roles in the game a "fighter", Who does what it sounds like and grabs a weapon and jumps into the front line to take out enemies and their encampments to hopefully push the enemy back far enough to take their base, "builder" is someone who uses the materials that Logistics runners bring to build defensive structures (Foxholes, walls, sandbags etc) to defend your FOB that is used to re-spawn and supplies new soldiers with weapons and medical supplies. "Logistics" is the most difficult job in the game (In my opinion). Logistics is a massive part of the game where you'll gather materials for your team to use at the front lines. Logistics is a huge importance to the game but can also be a huge bore. The process for logistics is a repetitive pattern that can bore a player easily. Firstly, you'll likely have to spawn somewhere in friendly territory that has an salvage mine, refinery and a factory close by or the job will take longer. Then you'll need to build a truck either by getting your own salvage from (A) a salvage field or (B) a salvage mine where then you'll take the salvage to a refinery to convert to basic materials (or b-mats is how players refer to them) or just by using the basic materials that are already in the base. You'll then have to fuel the truck and repeat the salvage process you did to build the truck but then either take a truck load of basic materials to the front lines or to a factory to produce crates of items (Such as guns, ammo, medical supplies, etc.). Within the game there are regions that have to been taken (by capturing a town hall) and logistics mostly operate way behind the front lines in friendly territory where you'll take your truck full of supplies and run it towards the front lines (which can a drive). Now take that process and repeat it over and over. Now there are ways to keep logistics exciting like by taking a friend with you or group of friends to create a convoy of trucks and take it to a new area a build a FOB.

Remember that the game is long term so the game takes some commitment. Commitment in the sense that you'll have to put numerous hours into the game to see change. The frustrating part is that hours of work of gathering supplies, fighting and planning can all be in vain. For example, you rally your team to an area that has enemies attempting to take one of your bases to only have no reinforcements or logistic runners to assist you. This can be extremely frustrating not only because did you lose the base when it could've been saved but that you had no help. This brings me to another point. Communication is key in a game like this. making sure there is a line of communication between the "fighters","builders" and "logistics runners" can mean the difference between winning and loosing the war. A lack of communication between the 3 can cause confusion and ultimately lead the war in a not favorable direction.

Combat in "Foxhole" can be both slow and quick. With the defenses built by the other team and those same defenses being "manned" by AI combat can be slow and a grueling process. When encountered with this issue it is best to have assistance because attempting to do anything in this game alone can be difficult and boring. Although if there are little defenses and more players than AI the combat can be quick and brutal. Seeing dozens of enemies running in your direction can give both a shock of fear and a rush of excitement. Combat in this game is more fun (in my opinion) when accompanied with friends so that you can coordinate strategies to defeat the opposing forces. Many times in combat you can't even see the enemy, you're just firing your weapon in the direction that you saw shots come from. This also is frustrating. That's why you need to play the game to understand the game. There is way too much content in this vast game to all be reviewed by me. I just wanted to cover the main parts of the game being the idea of the game, the roles and how they are played/useful, and combat.

The game has it's strengths and weaknesses but overall it is an exciting game if you make the correct decisions. Rotating between the 3 roles will help keep the game fresh and interesting. Although even if this is done enough the game can seem boring at points because a large portion is non combat (2 of the 3 roles don't really include combat) and the game can be repetitive. As said earlier seeing your hard logistics/building work or combat being wasted or useless can be a frustrating part of the game but that is also apart of the game, not everything works out like you wanted it too and that's kinda the idea of the game. A war that has it's up, downs, twists and turns. That's why you keep working, fighting and building. But also seeing the same things pay of is extremely rewarding. seeing your defenses kill a squad of soldiers, or hearing dozens of friendly soldiers thanking you for bringing much need supplies to the front lines or killing 3 enemies all by yourself. That's why I would recommend the game because there is so much to do in "Foxhole" and there is a role for everyone, every soldier is needed and can be useful in their own way and the payoff for hard work can be rewarding.\t\t\t\t\t
