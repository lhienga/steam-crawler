\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a confusing game, you don't know if you hit someone.
Okay, let's roll with it. It's hardcore, I get it. But it's too confusing, like for example -

- You have levels, but the game doesn't even teach you how you progress them. So I wasn't able to figure it out at all.
- Shooting is wonky, as you get a line of sight, but even when you're prone, it's hard to aim and get shots where you want, as a line of sight becomes red in every possible way. (I did basic training, where it explained how to shoot, move, etc. but it's still hard and needs a lot of practice I guess)
- Squads didn't even work for me, as I joined the most populated war area at that time, there were only two squads open with 4 people and nobody even spoke there or gave any commands. As all other squads were locked I could not join them at all. So I was roaming confused as to what to do. Was running in covers and to sounds of action, where I didn't even understand what to do or where to shoot, or run. You have only a compass on you, without any indicators of where to run or what to do. The big map was also confusing af.

I did watch a basic tutorial on this game which was on the main menu of the game. It helped a bit before I started to play. This game needs a lot of practice and has a learning curve, it is hard for newcomers. And I just didn't feel like digging all the tutorials for this game, to just learn and understand what to do there. Maybe for someone this game is exactly what he looked on to. But that's not me.

In my, own individual, opinion, this game is overpriced even for sale, where you can find more interesting multiplayer games for less than 17 euros, what I paid for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game sucks. Even more, the game is not like on YouTube. Even worse, this game is no Planetside. It is far, far from it.

I've done everything from fighting to construction and logistics. It is not fun.

Firstly, the controls suck. They made looking on the 2d plane occluded by 3d objects, making it a pain to target an enemy on the other side of a tree or rock and requiring crazy mouse movements to do so. You also don't see into buildings in the only plane that matters to you, which is the one on your elevation, unless you are a foot's way from a wall. Even then, you might just have to enter the building to actually see inside of it, but good luck with that. You're probably going to end up dead by enemy gunfire from people that can see and shoot you, but you can't see and shoot them.

You want to autorun?
Use an external program.
Build anything?
Use autoclicker lest you wring out your mouse.

UI Response to the events also sucks. If you get damaged, there is no indicator that you have been damaged unless you take a very close look at your character in a lit area, and even then it's hard to tell. Status effects don't tell you what to do, so have fun figuring that out on your own. You could try to ask in chat or vc, but you may or may not get an answer unless you find someone nice.

There ain't a good way to do logistics, much less even move around. Players are limited to existing in hexes instead of a better seamless transition between space because the game is not designed to properly handle players transitioning between areas in such a way, even going out of the way to prevent to many players of one side existing on the same hex.

On that point, everything is already priced in by already existing guilds. You're definitely not going to set up a factory somewhere since you won't get any space, and you're definitely not going to gather any meaningful resources to affect the war. God forbid someone claims an unused clan technical lest the grudge reports be loosed and you get suspended for a while. If you're not banned from the game from your teammates, you'll probably make a small base that's well defended by AIs. The only issue lies within a large enemy clan simply waltzing in with 80+ mammons (high explosive grenades) and twenty guys to trip up the AI and blow up your base immediately. It doesn't matter how many guns you have if they are unable to fire as the AI cycles through targets to kill, but ends up hurting none.

On that point, constructions. This also sucks. Because the terrain changes when trenches and bunkers are built, the system isn't tolerant enough and you have to 'blueprint' the entire base to make sure it can fit. The algorithm behind it is too rigid to be used without quite a lot of finagling. Additionally, you cannot delete or modify the tops of bunkers once built. You have to destroy them with mammons, and that'll end up with your weapons being locked. Any time you cycle through build tools, you'll also be kicked out of the build menu.

This also reminds me of the inability to choose what goes in your primary or secondary slot. Shovels and hammers take up the same slot, the primary one, as well as any form of weapon. If you want to dig a trench, you'll have to make do with a crappy sidearm. Any good weapon, such as submachine guns or rifles, will always take up the first slot. And you have to keep in mind, the one who shoots first wins in these engagements. You simply cannot compete a rifle with a sidearm.

This reminds me of another gripe, the lack of an explanation on anything. You'll get a tutorial on how to move and shoot, and that's it. Go talk to others to learn anything else. That'd be a good idea, if the other players even knew the ins and outs of the game. As I've heard from other players, most of the data known about the game, such as construction, is data-mined because zero information is provided about the stats of buildings. You'd think having a low structural integrity would make a bunker easier to destroy, but it fact it only makes the bunker only a little harder to destroy with each piece you talk on. This kind of game design is counter-intuitive and needs to be explained by the developers, not data-mined by the players.

The game is not even optimized, causing many lost frames and freezes when using binoculars and when environmental effects like rain come into play. Keep in mind, I'm on a Nvidia 1660 Ti GTX and this should not be happening. I develop on UE5 with this machine doing crazy things and I rarely lose frames in this manner. This of course, is only a small point and I digress.

Steam would not refund me for playing this game for 30 hours, which is barely any time spent in this game. If you do not fall in love with this game immediately, do not spend a single moment of your time more, lest you waste 30 dollars and 30 hours like I have.\t\t\t\t\t
