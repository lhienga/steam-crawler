\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA unique game, offers a mix of many different things. If anyone's old enough to have played an old f2p game on the "Sony Online" platform called Infantry - the "core" gameplay is similar - You are part of a (very) big faction, fighting over a persistant map. A lot of different equipment can be used, next to a very large selection of vehicles. On top of that, a very complex and complete building and logistics system fuels the whole thing - base building, resource extraction and conversion, actual supply chains complete with transport by land and sea, trucks trains and boats... It's very similar to EVE Online in that aspect too.
You can play solo or in a clan, both are viable however if you want to really experience the deeper aspects of the game (serious base building, tank warfare, artilery missions or the brand new naval combat) you really should get in a nice clan because these types of resources are not available unless you build them yourself - which you can't do all alone ;)
Free expansions usually add a lot of new features...

Next to the whole gameplay aspect of the game, there's the community - It's also similar to EVE Online, with a lot of "on the spectrum" and general "internet idioicy" all over the place. Imagine vanilla WoW Barrens chat, but multiplied by a ton. Basically if you've grown up with the 90s internet you'll be right at home. If you are a delicate Gen Z flower you'll get instantly offended and go cry to the sky in outrage.

So yeah, definatelly a recommended game! :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tyou need to immediately join a regiment or else the game is not fun. all the stockpiles are localized, which means that truck/train/boat drivers have to move stuff that has been manufactured from one side of the map to the other. regiments are clans that typically focus on either front line or logistics, possibly both if they are big enough. regiments have their own stockpiles that are invisible to public, which means you can be fighting a war and NOT have access to better equipment.

i recommend the game if you are the sort of person that enjoys team games but want to see a larger impact. with your actions. the map is a persistent battlefield of fronts that are hotly contested. because of how huge the map is, there is a ton of variation in the geography which can make for interesting battle scenarios. I recall being a lone surviving machine gunner and having to slay some 40 men before any backup would arrive and relieve me. I remember being a partisan and sabotaging enemy supply lines ( often unsuccessfully but great fun. basically legal griefing.) and being a trucker has its own rewards. taking critical supplies into a warzone feels like you are doing a huge favor for your comrades. I still havent touched the combat engineering, town building, or politics of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
