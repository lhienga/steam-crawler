\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoxhole goes to some lengths to present a realistic war campaign.
Like in reality, the actual frontline action is the smallest part. 95% of the game are about walking, driving and left-clicking for logistics. Otherwise you lose.
There is some potential for teamwork and close coordination, however there are not enough players and no big enough guilds or player groups that an actual structured warfare would be possible.
From what I have seen so far, logistics is horribly boring and SLOW, extremely SLOW. Developers even went and made it slower and slower, moving logistics building ever further from the front to force players to drive ages with their trucks to resupply frontline. Not fun. At all.
Apart from logistics, there's building defensive stuff which is SLOW, extremely SLOW. You hold LMB for ages to build a smol hole in the ground to take cover in. You take years to finish a bunker by yourself. Even with up to 2 more players it still takes much too long for any meaningful war feeling to occur.

As for the (very small) part that actually deals with assaulting or defending the frontline, it is very simplistic and mostly about who has the more patient campers in their team. Or using positively overpowered grenades.

Not sure if devs plan to change the gameplay more towards reasonably timed reaction and action on the frontline (would require major logistics improvements and speed-up), probably not.

So yeah, if you like listening to music or friends while doing nothing very much, give it a try. Otherwise stay away.
