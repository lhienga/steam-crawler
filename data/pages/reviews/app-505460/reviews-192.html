\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou'd have more fun digging a Foxhole in your backyard. Before playing I watched multiple tube vids and took notes (because it takes a notepad to remember which keys to press to effect everything in game). It looked appealing in a 1990's graphics sort of way, but I just couldn't get into the game and feel competent with it. After all the vids and notes, just two and a half hours in and I raised the flag of surrender. The controls are barbaric. The fog of war doesn't belong in this game, but... there it is. Don't get me wrong, it's kind of fun exploring and seeing what people have developed in game, but that's about it. Fights are awful. If the game had been free I might think more of it but, having to buy it, I expected much more than what it is. Yes, someone put a lot of work into the "behind the scenes" mechanics, but game play leaves a LOT to be desired. Now if you want a FREE game with a far better platform for taking ground with land and air battles, one that has excellent graphics and quick travel to the battles (no arduous travel unless you just want that in your game) and FAR more action, I'd highly recommend the FREE game: Planetside2. Watch a vid or two and you'll be competent and fighting in no time... unlike this game, where the only persistent world warfare is in the player's heart as he/she hopes and prays the game will somehow get better.\t\t\t\t\t
