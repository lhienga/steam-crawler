\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just spent the last four or six or something hours protecting a riverside command post near Ambrose as the Wardens tried to build a bridge that would allow them to push further south.

I arrived as a lowly Lance Corporal, managed to scrounge up a sniper rifle and ten rounds of ammunition, and looked for a decent vantage point to try and pick off flankers making their way across the river. I landed a few good shots and since we didn't have anyone in the area with binoculars I ended up functioning as a makeshift watchtower for the rifleman and heavy gunner that were posted up around the area. I was down to my last four rounds when someone nearby said "Dude do you need sniper ammo? I think we just got some at the stockpile." He brought me 20 magazines and said "Do you want me to spot for you? I have binos." We spent the next hour and a half moving flank to flank keeping the enemy pushed back. If too many pushed at once we'd swap to our sidearms and gun down whoever came close. We eventually attracted the attention of a mortar team and even though we repositioned a few times they managed to dial in on us.

My spotter soon had to leave and the Wardens got their bridge built. I still had eight or nine magazines left, so I laid down on top of an elevated pillbox and helped mow down the advance with the rest of the defenders. It starts to get close to nighttime, and our supplies were dwindling. The offensive had us down to our last 50 or so spawn supplies. I called out in World chat that Ambrose needed shirts, mortar rounds, grenades, and whatever else Logistics could get us quick. Out of nowhere, I see a Field Gun start approaching across the bridge. I let the rest of the team know and we take defensive positions, but the field gun, grenade spam, and a few RPG rounds took out our foxholes and pillboxes quicker than we expected. I down the Field Gun driver once or twice but someone else manages to hop on each time. Eventually enemy is almost pushed past the bridge. They're starting to try and build sandbags and foxholes on our side. Situation was getting rough.

I run forward with my revolver to try and get an angle on the field gun. I check one of the enemy corpses near the start of the bridge and see that he has 3 gas grenades on him. I chuck one behind the field gun and hear five or six cheers from my side of the line. The field gun starts pulling back, their gas mask filters burnt out. A lonely infantry or two chokes to death in the gas. Still, as soon as the gas fades, the field gun started pushing forward again. A few heavy gunners are directing fire toward my position now. I equip the second grenade and lay prone, waiting for the gun to get closer. It inches forward and I spring up and lob the grenade right behind the driver and gunner again. Both go down and our side of the line goes wild. Three or four of the veteran players charge forward and land grenades on the gun to destroy it (we didn't have a wrench to commandeer it). Someone calls out my name and says that gas killed their whole push.

Even with the push dead, we're down to our last 20 or so spawns. I realize the friendly town nearby has two hundred or so shirts, so I grab an unlocked truck and drive over. I take fifty or so of the spawn supplies and make it back to town right as day is breaking. I call out again in World chat that we're in dire need of supplies at Ambrose and head back out to the frontline with my last three or four magazines of sniper ammo. The enemy bridge is still up and their infantry is trying to push past hard. I grab a few of the defenders who showed up throughout the night and point out the firing positions on our flanks that I had found earlier.

Eventually I get taken out by another mortar and when I respawn at town I notice that a Logistics Corps arrived in a BIG way. Three halftracks and a few other trucks dropped supplies off at our command post. Heavy Explosive Grenades, hundreds of rounds of heavy gunner ammo, sixty or so sniper rifle magazines, a few hundred rifles, mortars and mortar tubes, and 400+ spawn supplies. Once again, we're all cheering. We help unpack the crates as our frontline holds the enemy back from the bridge, and over the course of the next night cycle end up destroying the bridge and pushing to the enemy side. I pair up with a mortar team and defend them from enemy snipers and flankers while they break down the enemy defensive structures across the river.

At the end of the night I've racked up countless kills, leveled up a few times, added four players to my friend list, and realize that I got commended so much that I was promoted to Sergeant. As someone with less than 20 hours in the game I've never felt more included by a community of players. Now's the right time to start playing without question. Plenty of drill sergeants are present in each faction's starting area waiting to take groups of Summer Sale players through the basic boot camp course, and then it's up to you to find your place on the frontlines. This game is really is a one-of-a-kind experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWithin two days I fought on two fronts and participated in two important operations that crumbled the enemy and culminated into more land taken for the Colonials. First the Western Front near The Linn of Mercy. Supplies were low and Logistics were needed elsewhere for the advance that was happening further west on the coast. This resulted in low ammo and minimal weaponry. Me along with a platoon sized element of infantry were in breach, charging the enemy and attempting to push their lines back. Foxhole after foxhole, we pushed through losing men along way with a russian style charge tactic. One man gets a rifle, the other has ammo. After hours of intense fighting along with artillery support we were able to break through their lines and push them back and establish a foothold in the region.

After this a small squad was forming to perform behind enemy line sabotage, their job which became mine was to push past the Warden defenses and work toward their back line to cut off Logistic runs to the region. This would starve the enemy of much needed supplies and allow us to win the long attrition game.

A small squad turned into a small platoon of 23 men. We all gathered our gear and pushed behind into enemy territory. Making our ways through the lines we lost 10 men in total through random engagements and enemy defenses set up. Once in the back line we made our way through the trenches and discovered hidden enemy caches. WE DESTROYED THEM! Shortly after doing so we heard men and logistic trucks coming our way, clearly to gather the supplies or stash some more away. We set up an ambush for them. Once they approached it was 3 men and a truck..... They were outgunned and outmatched. We wasted them, laying waste to even more precious supplies of the enemy. From here we continued guerilla tactics hiding in the bushes near roads and ambushing logistic trucks as they pass. This went on for hours till we commandeered an enemy truck to make our escape.

We lost more men on the Exfil and only 6 remain including myself... We reminisce on what had just transpired and say our goodbyes. I decide to travel to the Eastern Front of the battle. Base Foxcatcher in the Weathered Expanse is where I land. Once arriving the weather is frigid and sight is minimal due to the blizzard. Shortly after I get called to the front and assist in repelling the enemy further east. Supplies here are strong and no man is left without a rifle or ammo. We had a very defensive battle here which was much different to the bloody charges I experienced in the west. It seems there was a command structure here and it made all the difference.

To cut it short. This game is amazing, you will gain experiences in this game you cannot ascertain in any other game. At the surface level it is an over the top shooter but once you dive into the game it much more than that. it is genuine full scale war and everything is controlled by the player. Even the rifle you fire was made by another player....

Amazing.\t\t\t\t\t

I was then recommended a specific battle to go to and ended up running across a battlefield full of dead people scrounging for guns and ammo while getting shot at... a lot.
After 2 hours and a few honorable deaths, I was running toward the front line and noticed that rows of neatly spaced defenses had been built and were being constructed by players. This game is an experience. Overall I'd give it an 8/10 due to some of the mechanics being a bit tedious along with a lack of notable music.\t\t\t\t\t
