The game itself is slow-paced and easy to get accustomed to. You can easily continue playing, but it's noticeable that it's an early access game. Here are some observations:

- After acquiring 300 members in the gym, you'll notice the game starts lagging, becoming unplayable during 'working hours.'
- There's a cash chime sound whenever you generate any kind of cash. This constant dinging is highly annoying.
- The workers you hire seem to be on minimal pay. Eventually, you'll realize you're managing everything 24/7.

The game is still in early access, and more content is expected. I plan to return after more content has been released. The developers are active on Discord, take note of issues and feedback, and prioritize them in their backlog as they receive notifications.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper fun. I wish this game was further along, but it is still super early and hard to be too judgy.

CONS:
Not alot of personality from the sims, extremely limited equipment options, and even less branding variation. Customization options need to be expanded on for location buildout as well as paint options, decals, etc. Sales feel soulless. I think a roll system for upselling packages and higher tickets would be WILD! Like the speechcraft feature in Oblivion. You should also be able to take loans and choose from finance options.

PROS:
Exceptionally hypnotic. The features and designs that are currently available are plenty of fun for early access. Economy makes sense. The daily duties and staffing are both a nice touch. Every expansion genuinely feels pretty cool.

Looking forward to the new features in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
