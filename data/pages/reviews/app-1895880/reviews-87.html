Sargasso has a lot of places that you can reach that you probably shouldn't be able to with invisible walls everywhere.
In the previous games, they placed boarders in the water/swamp/etc. so you don't go out of bounds. There were several times I found myself reloading checkpoints because I'd get stranded without a Speedle spawn point nearby.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a good game and would recommend it!

I do have some comments and criticisms that maybe nobody will see, but I'll mention them anyways. It may seem like because there are more cons than pros, I don't like the game, but this isn't true. I just want the game to be the best it can be and a lot of the cons are pretty small, with the exception of the first two.

PROS
------
* Movement is much improved over previous Ratchet and Clank games. I feel more mobile in a way that I like. Being able to boost jump and being able to phase are great additions.
* Beautiful graphics. The team really outdid themselves, the game looks gorgeous.
* New characters and enemy design are great, especially Pierre and Junk Bot.
* New planets and levels look really cool.
* Weapons are fun to use.
* New upgrade system with raritanium is interesting and I like it.
* Weapon wheel felt like an improvement from the previous games. I especially liked the D-pad shortcuts.
* Easy to tell what weapon ammo was present (after breaking the box).
* Preview videos of weapons that you could buy were great.

CONS
------
* Exploration around the map isn't quite as fun as the previous games. For instance, looking for gold bolts used to be challenging and you had to look above and below where you normally would to find them. In this game, I didn't really need to look in unorthodox places because semi-out-of-reach places (not necessarily out of bounds) were typically blocked with a wall, and so I learned to not get too tricky where I was looking for collectibles.
* Finding collectibles didn't feel as satisfying as in the older games. Maybe it's because there's no sound when I collect one of these items in the same way that there is when I collect regular bolts so it doesn't feel as impactful.
* Spybot/Lorb/etc. extra stuff that you could look at (after you collected it) was hard to find in the menus. I didn't even know it existed until I beat the game. I think it would have been cool to have some in-game prompt after I found the stuff that I could press to show/read me whatever lore is associated with the collectible.
* Hard to tell where health was after breaking the box. In previous games, it's pretty easy, but in this game it was challenging.
* Unable/not sure how to see the purchased upgrades of a weapon from the Pause menu. It seems like I'm only able to see it when talking with the vendor.
* Box breaker radius seems pretty small.
* No bolt magnetizer item which would be fine, but I feel like the default radius is pretty small. Maybe that's just me.
* A lot of debris on the ground so hard to tell what stuff is uncollected bolts, and what stuff is debris. This wouldn't be a huge deal if the bolt collecting radius was bigger.

UNIMPORTANT CONS
-----------------------
* No flame/magma weapon to compliment the freeze weapon.
* No long jump like in previous games. It wasn't super important because the mobility in this game is so nice, but for some reason, I missed the old long jump.
* Didn't like Zurkon Jr., I thought he was annoying.
* I don't know why, but I missed the old space battle maps and being able to upgrade your ship from the older games.
* I also don't know why, but I missed the old "Find X number of gems in this desert/frozen tundra to get Y collectible."



I can't stress this enough, but overall, I think the team did a really good job on this game and hope to see more Ratchet and Clank!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, the biggest pro: This game is GORGEOUS, easily the best looking Playstation game brought to Steam. It has such fun and striking colors, and the details on things like fur or shininess on metals is insane, and I'm playing on slightly lower graphics settings too. The combat is simple and fun, with weapons having quick upgrade speed from both using the weapons and finding Raritanium, the game's main upgrade currency. From what I've seen there isn't a ton of stuff off the beaten path, but what is there is worthwhile.
Both the style and writing have strong Saturday morning cartoon vibes to them, having a fun wit, but also not being insanely deep, which fits nicely with said style. There are deeper themes towards the end of the game, but nothing insane comes up, and I wanna freaking hug Clank and Kit.

The biggest flaws I have with the game regard performance. I don't mind having multiple fans nearby to help cool my computer (I've been used to it), but my playtime still had notable lag/slowdown when traveling between dimensions (in the purple space) in the intro sequence, and the loading times have been a tiny bit slow for my tastes in the few times I've had them. The far worse things are that audio can become choppy during heavy combat, and I've or frozen multiple times.

Overall score: 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’ve been a devoted fan of the Ratchet and Clank Series, particularly the original trilogy (including Deadlocked or Gladiator, the fourth instalment) on the PlayStation 2. Growing up with these games, I cherish fond memories of playing through them. Even now, I continue to enjoy playing them through emulation. Clearly, I harbor a bias toward those games. When compared to the later entries and the Future Series, I find fault with how the game has become generic, catering to mass appeal rather than staying true to the roots of what defines Ratchet & Clank. The once “funky catchy” soundtrack, the symbolism of modern culture, and the witty, smart-ass humour all integral to the franchise, that seem to have nearly vanished from recent instalments. The 2016 remaster, if you can even call it that, left a rather unpleasant taste in the original fanbase’s mouths.

Onto the review; The game itself is definitely enjoyable! It’s refreshing to encounter a game with such meticulous attention to detail and craftsmanship. The subtle touches in this gem caught my eye. For example, the Marauder Armour from Deadlocked triggered a delightful flashback to those who played it years ago. As for the story, without diving too deep, I appreciated the delicate balance between playfulness and emotional tones that the main characters navigate. While not as well-written as the first game, it still held its own. Some moments felt a tad forced for the sake of progression, but the level design more than compensated. Those seamless transitions between levels? Mind-bending! They ditched the monotonous loading screen and brought back the ship actually flying off and landing on planets too!

The music in Rift Apart left me somewhat disappointed, a trend that began when the legendary David Bergeaud departed from the company years ago. While some may appreciate cinematic music, I implore you to listen to the Ratchet & Clank 3: Up Your Arsenal soundtrack for the Marcadia level—pure perfection! Overall, the music in Rift Apart did its part to fill the void, but halfway through, I muted it and queued up the old-school soundtrack in the background. A 100% recommendation, by the way.

The weapons in Rift Apart were indeed cool and colourful, but they lacked the diversity of their predecessors. I’m puzzled by the need for four different glove variants, especially when two of them seem rather useless. Perhaps a multifunctional glove with all abilities combined would have been a better approach, rather than individual weapons. On a positive note, the RYNO returned! The attack animation is fantastic, but the weapon design felt somewhat lacklustre compared to its firepower. It needed to be more imposing, perhaps even monstrous. As for the art style, it falls into the “okay” category. I’ve noticed that Ratchet appears puffier and furrier with each game instalment—possibly a marketing moves for merchandise. While I’m not a fan of that trend, it does suffice. Overall, the game was great, and I thoroughly enjoyed it. I’m eagerly anticipating where the series will go from here!

The Galactic Rangers will forever hold their iconic status from Ratchet & Clank 3. No arguments!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful graphics, great story and memorable/lovable characters, fun combat, and overall just good gameplay. This is a game that really hearkens back to what it means to be a GAME. Just pure fun the whole the way through, including 100% completing the game and beyond. There are extras, cheats, new game plus bonuses, and just so much fun stuff. The dialogue is pretty fun too. Normal difficulty is actually pretty easy, so if you want a bit more of a challenge then select the second hardest difficulty for your first playthrough. I didn't die a single time playing on the normal mode.

The ONLY gripe I have with this game is that the characters are VERY quick to point out what you need to do and where you need to go. It holds your hand a little too much in the puzzle/exploration sections of the game. I really dislike this trend in gaming these days, but if that's the only thing wrong with the game then I'm still willing to overlook something like that. There were also a myriad of random bugs and visual issues when the game launched, but it seems like those have been smoothed over for the most part now!

At the time of writing this review, I only need one more achievement to get all of them. This game is awesome. I played it through once really quick on my buddy's PS5 and was thrilled when it got announced for PC because it meant I could finally spend the time on the game that it deserves. I definitely think it's worth $60, which is not what one can say about many games these days. Play this game! It's awesome! Insomniac and Nixxes did a fantastic job.

I'd also like to point out that the vibration for the DualShock 4 is remarkably well done. I'm surprised at how close it got to the PS5 version of the game, actually.
