\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot the longest, just finished first playthrough at about 9-10 hours (plus leaving laptop on by mistake). Veterans will know replayability; for newcomers it will be enjoyable to the extent that the weapon-play is enjoyable. There's puzzles and the like but if you're not enjoying blowing up things in the first 90mins then refund.

Probably the best Ratchet and Clank game since the PS2--first one to make me go 'wow' for a long time in any case and get me properly excitable.
Weapons are varied including some familiar favourites. Nothing mindblowing (but that might be my familiarity with the series) and the combat is fluid.
All quite tight in terms of controls, few instances of feeling like the controls let me die.
Best graphics of the series but maintains the style--feels like a Ratchet and Clank game, just modern.
Story is good. Perhaps lacks that magic of the old games.
Enemies are not quite as varied as might be desired.
Boss battles are alright if not inspiring.

Overall: it's not the original games, but that's not necessarily a bad thing. Perhaps it has less soul, but certainly has the fun factor (besides it's certainly not without character). Would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best recent games I've played. Combat manages to feel like you're absolutely destroying everything with ease while actually providing a challenge. The multiverse aspect is integrated into the plot well, with counterpart characters and locations appearing frequently and the ability to swap between realities in some situations.

My one big gripe with this game is that, despite being "Verified" for the Steam Deck, the Deck struggles with it even at low settings. Most of the game comes in at 25-30 FPS, but there are frequent drops and audio stuttering during combat, and one of the worlds even tanks it to barely 15 FPS whenever you're there. Lowering the resolution alongside the graphics settings doesn't help much and just makes on-screen text hard to read. That said, I still enjoyed it on the Deck because it's still a very good game and is still at least playable.

Makes me want to give the rest of the R&C series a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
