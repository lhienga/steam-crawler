\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't get me wrong, it's a decent game. But it feels like a beta version, it is definitely not a completed product.

Invisible walls all over the place, falling through the ground, general lack of polish, i even found a placeholder graphic just left in there. Just doing a normal playthrough, actively avoiding situations one might expect to cause issues, will still see a fair bit of them happen.

But that's not the main issue here. The problem is frequent cases of "this entire thing should have been reworked"; stuff that repeat players might not even notice since they already know how it works, which shows that there was no playtesting. Let me give an example from my own playthrough: I get a flying mount, and am told to fly around and collect the food for this mount. I fly to the food, but can't interact with it no matter what i do. I can't even get off the mount and manually interact with the food. Now stuck on the mount and unable to do anything i fly around randomly, then suddenly i get sucked down onto the ground. It seems i got close enough to a landing pad to trigger landing, lucky me! Jumping back onto the mount now instantly gives it a new ability, which can interact with the food i was meant to collect.

Things like these can also cause bigger issues, such as running/hover boots on a toggle remaining active during, well, everything. So whenever a cutscene ends you better hope you're not falling to your death. In one case this will even cause you to permanently miss a collectible, locking out the ability to get the final reward weapon for gathering all of them.

Other things includes:
The camera zooming mid jump, making it impossible to judge distance.
The game explaining mechanics after you have needed to use them multiple times.
Really poor optimization, you won't be playing this without decent hardware.
Getting sucked into nearby walls when jumping, without trying to wall jump.
And probably a lot more things i can't think of just on the fly.

And for those who have come from the older ps2 games and finally get to try a modern ratchet and clank game: This is a lesser version. Less weapons, less upgrades, less story, less levels, less content, less everything. And what is there are also lesser versions, with less interesting weapons and worse level design. Still worth it for the nostalgia though, just temper your expectations and don't be afraid to search up some common issues so you can avoid them before they happen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRift Apart is a good game generally and good platformer in particular. It costs too much for the game like this (console curse, I guess), so I recommend to buy it on a sale, unless you are absolute fan of the genre/series.

From Ratchet and Clank franchise perspective, I would say that this game is exactly what I expected being the person who played few other games of the universe (R&K first game, Up your arsenal, Deadlocked, Size matters). The game does not amaze from any perspective, it is just Ratchet and Clank driven by modern engine, all aspects of the game either just GOOD or OK.
The only thing that is really unfortunate is lack of 'winking to adult auditory' in a story line and characters development. Bunch of previous games I played always had some jokes, scenes or cultural references clear for adult auditory (try youtube "Ratchet and clank without context"). I do not see such things in this game. Characters are very plain, simple and naive. Sure, it is strange to expect 4D jokes from 7+ rated games, but at least early games attempted to work it out. This game feels more like another one Disney movie.

Nonetheless, if you like platformers of PS2 era or Ratchet and clank franchise, I can recommend for sure. Otherwise, check some review/gameplay video to decide.

Btw, the game is played very well on a keyboard and nothing feels too casual or inconvenient.\t\t\t\t\t
