\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a technological marvel but still not the absolute best in the series when it could have been.
So the visuals and load times are legit witchcraft, probably the first game I've like 8 years that I'll say with no caveat feels next gen. I played it at 1440p medium on a 1080 and even without RTX and everything is was really mind blowing at points.
The load times were a big marketing gimmick for the PS5 but really it's a testament to Insomniacs insane technical talent. I played on a good quality M.2 SSD and the only hitches I had were split second on the most demanding and sudden loads, but funnily enough this was solved and performance was actually improved by deleting direct storage .dlls in the games directory due to nvidias DS implementation not being great.
What really drives this home is when you unlock the jet boots. The pace at which you can travel a pretty sizable level is already mind blowing, but being able to jump directly into your ship and get to another planet without a moment to check your phone and with no pop in or hitching never got old, this is easily the fastest game I've played in many ways.

Technical TL,DR: Great port, lots of options to tune your performance, reasonably demanding but looks great at most qualities

Gameplay wise Insomniac have been killing it with games that have a very addictive gameplay loop, in fact I don't think there's any Insomniac games I have played and not 100%ed. But like Ratchet and Clank 2016, a lot of the gameplay design feels like it's made for actual toddlers and the game feels a lot thinner in terms of exploration and puzzles compared to every game in the series pre 2016. All the Ratchet puzzles feel like tutorial areas from the original game (A max security prison is unlocked by yeeting your wrench at 2 15ft glowing green buttons next to the door) and the clank and glitch puzzles, while actually pretty good fun have an annoying skip option in the pause menu that I hit a couple times when attempting to just back out of the puzzle. I think this really undermines these areas being able to just skip large chunks of the game, especially when this option is available on the hardest difficulty. I'd get it on easy mode or something but I just found it a bit weird and indicative of a lot of design choices that made this and 2016 a bit dissatisfying.
Something else that echoes this "too easy" nature is how they made a really fun and fast play style that could have a high skill ceiling but it's either totally trivial dealing with enemies, or you'll have quite a few total BS moments. With better enemy balancing the dark souls style dodge could have been used make a really fun and challenging experience but it ends up being used mostly for traversal or just last second dodging poorly telegraphed BS attacks.
I was also disappointed at the lack of completionist awards and how secrets are just shown on your map but getting them was fun and max leveling everything never took any real grinding and was neatly done by the time I finished 2 play throughs. Also no Insomniac Museum blows but not as hard as 2016s.

Gameplay TL,DR: A little dissatisfying in the same way as 2016 but not as egregious since it's not just a worse remake of a better game. Very very fun gameplay loop that could have been better but was still a good time.

Finally the story was fine, the voice actors we're all very good and the new characters were all actually really enjoyable. A few cool callbacks to previous games and it felt more connected to the rest of the series than 2016. The writing and humor kinda falls off a cliff near the end, starting pretty strong and ending in a very eye rolling and contrived manner but it's not exactly why I'm playing it

All in all an amazing audio visual experience with good gameplay, great if you wanna flex a new GPU or TV. If you're a fan of the series you'll feel how it could be better though. I'd wait for a sale if you aren't already a fan since it's kinda short if you rush it, I was satisfied with my time having paid full price though and to me it's like a 7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a little biased from R&C being the reason I bought a PS3... and PS4... and PS5, but I'm glad to be able to sell the latter now, given that this game which people said couldn't be ported has been.

Playing on SteamDeck with visuals set to low, SteamDeck FPS cap set to 40, AMD FSR set to maintain 40. It will still drop frames during hectic firefights (and can look very blurry at other points), but it's mostly pretty good - and if I ever upgrade my GPU on desktop then it'll be neat to see the comparison.

The port is mostly pretty good, but there are some issues here and there - I've had a few cases of the ground failing to load correctly (one of which was during the intro, which wasn't a great first impression of the port), and a few hard crashes that required fully powering off the Deck. You may still want to wait a bit for more patches.

Most of these issues are related to the game's fancy dimension portal sequences or scene transitions which require a lot of loading - each issue happening either during the transition or immediately afterwards. When they work properly, on the Steam Deck's internal SSD, these do have a noticeable delay compared to PS5 where they're near seamless, but that's kind of expected with the hardware. It breaks up the flow a little, but it's still liveable - if you're still using a mechanical hard drive on desktop or relying on the Deck's SD card, then it might be a bit more of a problem.

For the game itself rather than the port, if you've not played a Ratchet & Clank game, they're fast paced shooter-platformers with a variety of ridiculous weapons, and this one is fairly accomodating to newcomers; it catches you up to what you need to know about the returning characters in the first few levels, but a lot of focus is on new characters for this game anyway.
(If you're here for the story, you may still wish to watch a recap of Tools of Destruction, Quest For Booty, and A Crack in Time for a few plot points)\t\t\t\t\t
