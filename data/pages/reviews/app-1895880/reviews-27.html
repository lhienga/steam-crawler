\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis feels like a game that gets almost everything perfect. The graphics, the gameplay, the story, the overall feel, all of it. It's the perfect length too, where it doesn't overstay its welcome but doesn't feel like it cut itself off too short either (my 13 hour playlog was at 93%, didn't do Challenge Mode, got most of the collectables and all of the side missions).

For one, the game's GORGEOUS--I ran this on an RTX 3060 so nearly every setting was at High to Very High save for a few ray tracing settings, and it's legit one of the best looking games I've seen. It's just breathtaking. In addition, it's just as fun to play as it is to look at, where nearly everything (I'll get back to that later) is ultra polished. It does a great job balancing out actual playable parts and really inventive setpieces. In addition, the story is pretty fun as well, along with tackling some surprising themes like self confidence, and it was far smoother of a storytelling experience than the 2016 remake. And yeah, newcomers Rivet and Kit are great.

There were only really two flaws that stood out. One was that nearly every boss used a similar template, to the point where a few of them felt like the same boss, just with a different enemy design. The other was that there were some minor performance issues--I had a few crashes here and there, and there was a bug where I tried to switch from one weapon to another, but got stuck on a previous weapon.

Overall, as someone whose only prior R&C experience was the 2016 game (it was good, but clunky with how it tied into the movie), this was outstanding and highly recommended, especially if it's on sale. I'm extremely thrilled that it was outed that they're working on another title in the series, because Insomniac nailed it with this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Oh the nostalgia! Growing up with a PS2, this was an iconic game along with the Jak series. This brought me back to that time. There were no issues jumping back into the world of Ratchet and Clank as it maintained a familiarity with the mechanics, UI, and level design.
The story was a great (though short?) sequel to its predecessors with entertaining and lighthearted banter as all the others have done. I find self aware jokes much more fun than a super dramatic story. Reoccurring characters also gave a warm feeling knowing there needed to be a smooth hand-off between this story and the last.
For those who also grew up with a PS2 and are very protective of childhood memories - I too was skeptical, to say the least, of another protagonist in the series. Insomniac did not disappoint. I'm super interested to see where the next story is headed.
Engine ran nice, though could definitely use a little more optimization in some parts. Landscapes were vivid, as expected in this series. And game-play stayed fluid for the most part. 9/10 overall and would love for Sony to port future iconic series like this to the PC going forward. Looking at you Jak and Daxter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaystation must really hate that they bought insomniac, because they put out the most unimpressive game they possibly could have.

Puzzles are a joke, your ability to solve them takes slightly more effort than it does to count to 5.
Enemies are mostly just ripped from previous games. and they aren't even fun to fight. they just made them bullet spungy to make them take a while to kill, giving the illusion of difficulty.
"bosses" end up being the same 4 units over and over.
Weapons feel incredibly weak.
"secrets" are hidden so poorly, all you have to do to find them is really be able to look up slightly.
Levels are incredibly short, and are just as boring as the the story.
I can't help but feel that this game was intended for the ages of 5-9, because there's basically no challenge or thought required to play it. They even made grindrail sequences boring and hand-holding.

Having played the first 6 or so ratchet and clank games, i'd have to say that this one is a massive dissapointment. They weren't even scraping the bottom of the barrel, they let the barrel sit and grow mold, then they scraped the old rotten scum out of the cracks. The quality and artistry that made the IP interesting and fun is completely lacking.\t\t\t\t\t
