\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game, but try to get it on a discount

Pros:
-Fun gameplay and gun play
-Tons of cool cinematic moments
-Great sound design and graphics
-The inter-dimensional conceit isn't amazing, but it's fun

Cons:
-Mediocre Disney-efied story and forgettable dialogue
-Performance is not the greatest. Strange, since God of War (arguably more graphically impressive) runs far better than this cartoon game. You'll need a beefy PC to enjoy this at higher settings and/or with ray tracing.
-Steam Deck performance is atrocious. Very stuttery even on low settings with the FSR cranked up. I struggle to call this a Deck Verified title in any sense.

All in all, worth the money and a great game for kids and adults alike. Ratchet and Clank fans may be disappointed with how it stacks up to the classics. Feels like maybe PS tried to play it safe on this one. Either way I hope they continue to make more next-gen R&C games that use the tech in fun ways like this.

Wait for a sale since I see no reason to buy this at the full $60\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you haven't paid much attention to the Playstation, you could be forgiven for never having heard of Ratchet & Clank before.
One of the most cherished IP's held by Sony, Ratchet & Clank games are some of the best 3:rd person shoot em up platformers around. While the story of each game has never been their strong side, the zany humor and flashy combat with loads of weapons to choose from has always been their true focus and more than enough makes up for any shortfalls.
So it does with this one as well, the 11:th in the series. Some say they story is worse now and the humor is more flat compared to before, with less sass, but honestly... I've played several of the previous ones and I didn't notice anything particular in that regard with this one except maybe Ratchet being a bit more grown up and a little bit tired of heroing. Which, as this is the 11:th installment, would make sense tbh.
The combat is still strong, the weapon are still fun to use, the villains are still funny, and the level design is still great. It's a worthy entry in the series and well worth your money.
It IS however a little bit buggy. The devs still need more practice in making PC ports, but it's nothing gamebreaking by any stretch. I only had to reload once the entire playthrough and there's little penalty for dying, so you'll probably be fine if you have a machine powerful enough to run it. Buying it on a sale is probably recommended as it has a couple of years on it.

I had a good time with it as would any veteran of the series, and if you're new to it and enjoy action shooters that have an emphasis on fun over challenge, you probably will too.\t\t\t\t\t
