Fails to hit the heights that the series has been at in the past, but it is still a pleasant experience for the $40 that I paid. The graphics and sound design are excellent, but the movement, weapons, and enemy variety are not up to the standards set by the PS3-era titles. It took me about 20 hours to complete the game with all achievements, and while it was fun, not much about the experience compels me to continue playing on Challenge Mode. Maybe I will, maybe I won't. Somewhere on 6 to 7 out of 10.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhenomenal game. It holds up extremely well compared to the older titles of the series. The graphics are mind blowing, the voice acting is top-notch, and the inter-dimension gameplay is really, really fun. Clank's little puzzle missions (if you wanna call it that) is actually not that bad and very creative.

Ratchet & Clank is amongst my favorite video game series of all time. I played the first three non-stop back in the day and still am often to this day. In this game comes arguably the greatest antagonist in the games history, Dr. Nefarious. Which I might add that his voice actor plays it extremely well. A wacky evil super villain trying to take over universes. The jokes are often cheesy but sometimes humorous, which isn't bad considering that's how the series was from the start. This game is definitely one of the best of the series and the Hero mode is extremely fun, going through my first playthrough as I'm typing this review.

Rivet is another topic to bring up here. A counter-part of Ratchet from another dimension. As you'd expect, they have a lot in common. She's a great character and I'm hoping to see more of Rivet in future titles.

9/10.

EDIT: Please bring the old trilogy to PC, Insomniac/Sony.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gotta say as much as I enjoyed this game, it still has problems, although most of them don't have a ton to do with gameplay. Gameplay wise this is one of the better Ratchet and Clank games. If you just want to have the best third person shooter experience of your life, buy this. Otherwise, I have several gripes that I will detail in later paragraphs. There will probably be spoilers, so read at your own risk. Also, my ideas are not organized, I'm just writing them down as I think of them. A lot of it will be negative, but I do have some pretty positive things to say as well towards the end, they mostly have to do with gameplay lol. But still, don't anything I say as me hating the game. I just love it and want it to be better.

My problem with the game mostly has to do with the characters and story. It has a great idea, with Dr Nefarious transporting everyone to a universe where he always wins, but it's wasted on the writing and character design of modern RaC games. For whatever reason Ratchet is no longer the hot headed renegade he was in the original games, even though he mellowed out as time went on, it's still nothing compared to the bland garbage that he is now. And Clank is somehow even worse; he never opens his mouth unless if it's to say he loves ratchet, apologize to someone, state the most obvious fact known to the player at any given moment, or ask a question about said fact with an answer so obvious you want to stab a fork in your eye to relieve the pain. Honestly, I don't know how you can mess up this badly. If it ain't broke, don't fix it. But I guess they thought that instead of a badass hero we wanted a ♥♥♥♥♥ and instead of an intellectual genius we wanted a dumbass.

A big problem with the way ratchet and clank currently are now that I think of it comes from the fact that this is a rebooted series. I remember first playing and during the intro, clank told ratchet he's his closest friend and wanted to give him the dimensionator as a gift to find his family as a token of his appreciation, i was nearly in tears. When I saw it again in NG+ I felt absolutely nothing, because I had realized that this just isn't the same series I grew up playing. Ratchet and clank don't grow together and form an inseparable bond like they do in the original trilogy, in the 2016 reboot, they're practically best friends as soon as they meet each other, whereas in the original games, they actually hated each other for most of the first game, insulting each other and fighting and bickering after nearly every mission. By the end they had learned to put their differences aside and were able to work together to achieve the impossible, forming an unbreakable bond that would last a lifetime. They had actual real character development. It's not just "lol i love you bro because both of our names are in the title of the game"

As for Rivet and Kit, they're kinda just boring. Rivet is kinda cool, but at the end of the day doesn't really have any personality traits that truly stand out or make her meaningfully different than ratchet, and as for Kit, I really liked her initially, and she has an adorable design, but the more I saw of her the more bored and disappointed I became. I just think both of these characters had potential that got wasted. To be honest, it's mostly a symptom of the rebooted series just being a neutered version of the old PS2 games, back when characters were actually characters.

Another big gripe I have is with the main villain, Emperor Nefarious. It's kind of ridiculous how at every possible turn you take the dude is always right there to stop you in your tracks, conveniently after you just finished completing your mission so he can steal everything you just worked for. This happens multiple times and it really gets old. He could easily just kill ratchet and rivet where they stand at any given moment but instead he spares them every single time, and eventually just gets his ass kicked. It's like this guy actually seemed like an intimidating enemy until he actually showed up in the game, and from then on it became impossible to see him as anything more spooky than Dr Nefarious himself, just a big old tom cat for jerry mouse to clobber the ♥♥♥♥ out of. And it's funny when it happens to Dr Nefarious, he's just a clown getting hit by the proverbial slapstick, but for Emperor Nefarious to be set up as this unbeatable competent villain who always stomps out Rivet's resistance, he does not deliver at all.

Now I don't want everything I say about the game to be negative, after all I do recommend it, So I'm going to end it on positive feedback.

First of all, I love all the side content. Outside of the arena challenges, the side quests on every planet you visit feel a lot like helping those random people who need it in spyro 2 and 3. W side quests, no complaints here, they absolutely nailed it.

Whoever designed the new charge boots needs a raise. By far my favorite mobility option in any Ratchet and Clank game.

I think overall, they nailed the weapons. Other than the bombadier and pixelizer, every single weapon is either fun to use, has absolutely stunning visuals, makes me happier every time I use it, or any combination of those. Probably the most balanced weapons of any Ratchet and Clank game too, there are a few outliers but most of them feel very fair and reasonable or the super powerful ones have a small enough amount of ammo to keep them from being a one trick solution to every fight, you have to actually swap to different weapons and use everything you buy.

One thing that I also find interesting is the attention to detail and visual design of this game, but don't take my word for it. I think dunkey's video on it does a good job of explaining how wonderfully crafted the details and environments are.

Overall, 8/10; the good gameplay really does come through. It could honestly be as high as a 9/10 if you never played the ps2 games.\t\t\t\t\t
