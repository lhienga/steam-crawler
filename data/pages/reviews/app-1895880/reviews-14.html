Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart
is a stunning and exhilarating adventure that showcases the power and potential of PC gaming. The game is a visual feast, with gorgeous and varied worlds, lifelike animations, and spectacular effects. The game also makes use of ray tracing to create realistic reflections, shadows, and ambient occlusion, enhancing the immersion and realism of the environments. The game runs smoothly and offers a range of graphics options to suit different preferences and hardware capabilities.

The game play
is fast-paced and fun, with a mix of platforming, combat, and puzzle-solving. The game features a diverse and creative arsenal of weapons and gadgets, each with their own unique functions and upgrades. The game also introduces a new mechanic of dimensional rifts, which allow the player to quickly switch between different locations and realities, creating dynamic and unpredictable situations. The game also has a variety of modes, such as story missions, dungeons, raids, PvP arenas, and world events, offering plenty of content and replay value.

The story
is engaging and humorous, with a charming cast of characters and witty dialogue. The game follows Ratchet and Clank, as well as a new character named Rivet, a female Lombax from another dimension, as they try to stop an evil emperor from conquering the multiverse. The game explores themes of friendship, identity, and heroism, and delivers a satisfying and emotional conclusion to the saga.

Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart is a must-play
for fans of the series and newcomers alike. It is a masterpiece of PC gaming that demonstrates the technical and artistic excellence of Insomniac Games and Nixxes Software. It is one of the best games of 2023 and a worthy addition to the PlayStation Studios franchise. I highly recommend it to anyone looking for a thrilling and enjoyable experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a mixed bag.
Get it on sale if you're a ratchet & clank fan.
Personally wont be playing this again unless if this gets some rly cool mods.

+ Optional rifts serving as bonus challenges was a nice touch
+ Nefarious makes a come back
+ Customizable armor's very nice. Also the fact that just picking up a piece of armor gives you a permanent buff was very nice indeed.
+ Weapon upgrades were kinda cool i guess. With each level you unlock perks for the gun that you can buy with raritanium. Sad part is that the game doesnt tell you what the final level upgrade does for the weapon, you just have to figure those things out yourself

+- The first set of guns are fun to use, but the further the game you get the more boring the weapons get.
A shotgun that works like a shield, but it only has 8 max ammo, sad trombone.
An icecube launcher that freezes enemies in place & makes them take extra damage, "cool" idea except there alrdy is a weapon that freezes enemies in place, has a chance to drop health on kills, has more max ammo than the icecube launcher & is a turret meaning you don't have to be constantly swapping weapons to take advantage of the freeze effect. Even sadder trombone.
The minigun was super cool to use tho & def deserves to be a late game unlock. Loads of ammo, good damage, longer you fire the faster it fires & to balance this out you have to let the barrels cool off occasionally.
Most of these weapons also have a "half-trigger pull" effects, but i didnt use any of these as nobodys gonna have the patience to carefully press the trigger when they could just hold / spam it during fire fights
+- The collectables were super easy to get. Most of them were in a predictable places & you can even set map markers that guide you to them if you don't feel like exploring.
+- The hacking challenges were stupidly easy. Just hold down the fire power, aim around & you wont lose. I never rly liked these hacking challenges in Ratchet & Clank games (except R&C 1) to begin with, but this is so easy its not even fun.

- The game really likes to hold your hand during the story bits. This ranges from characters constantly telling you what to do (backseat gaming in the game itself) to the gameplay slowing down to a crawl so that you can press the correct button in time.
- Not enough ammo. The max ammo in the last few weapons are in single digits, the only time I actually used these items were after I had picked up all of the gold bolts & unlocked the infinite ammo cheat.
- The audio makes noticable crackles when a lot of noices afe happening all at once. Had to turn down the sfx setting just so that my ears wouldnt bleed everytime I fired a shotgun at a crowd of enemies & props. Doubt itll get fixed.
- You'll be playing mostly as Rivet instead of the games main character. The game should be called "Rivet & Clank" 'cause thats how about 60-70% of the game was.
- Maps have areas that you can go to that have nothing in them. The first Rivet stage for example has islands you can go to that are completely empty, whats the point of these?? Edit: Only later did I find out that they were gonna become important later, but wouldn't it have made more sense to put these areas in places where the players cant get to before they're supposed to be there??
- Story is very cringe at times, luckily there's a skip button. Unluckily this doesnt save you from the ingame character dialog. I would try to kill the talking enemies as quickly as possible so that I wouldn't have to listen to their dialog, their lines are cringe & it's always the same 2 voice actors.
- Im fairly sure theres a memory leak somewhere, the game would freeze for about a minute everytime i opened the map after couple hours of play.\t\t\t\t\t
