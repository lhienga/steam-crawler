\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely a solid Ratchet and Clank game. But in my opinion, not one of the best. Perhaps I'm over this series now, or maybe this series is getting stale, but this wasn't quite as fun as it used to be. I was a huge fan of the series on the PS2, and Going Commando is still my favorite entry in the entire series. Maybe it's unfair, but I compare every single Ratchet and Clank game to Going Commando. That was the most perfect game in the series in my opinion. Plenty of different and varying worlds, lots of puzzles! and very interesting weapon selection. Not to mention, way more activities to keep the game fresh.

Rift Apart was great, I just felt like it focused more on being a beautiful game than a good one. It felt very repetitive at some point, and there wasn't enough to really mix up the activities you do throughout the story. Also, I kind of miss how imaginative the weapons used to be. A lot of the weapons now just feel like a copy + paste of something from an old one. I know there's only so much you can do to make new weapons, but part of what I liked about the older entries in the series is that the game didn't seem to take itself too seriously. Almost as if they had way more fun coming up with implementations for the game.

Another thing I miss is the old humor of the old games. The games had humor that tried to go for an older audience, and now, Ratchet and Clank games seem to be more kid-friendly. Again, this is still a solid game, but if there's a sequel, I hope they try to go back to the old days of the series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've played Ratchet & Clank before, this is basically that, but on PC. (You'll want to use a controller.) There's nothing really new. Ratchet has the same moves as always. The story is a mostly linear path, where sometimes you can choose to go to one area first before another, but you still need to clear both to continue. You collect bolts as currency which you use to buy ammo and new weapons. You level up Ratchet to get more health, and you level up Ratchet's weapons to unlock new upgrade slots. You can unlock those upgrade slots with Rareitanium. There are gold bolts. I guess technically the armor is new, which unlocks a bunch of small upgrades as you collect them but is otherwise entirely cosmetic.

The whole "dimensions" thing doesn't really add anything to the gameplay. Instead it's kind of like the part of Portal where you can't control the portal gun. There are pre-defined portals you go through. Yay. This also leads to a couple of gameplay segments where a slower SSD/HDD will definitely make the game "worse" in that it'll stop once you hit the portal to finish loading the next segment, but it won't stop you from completing the game, and while graphically neat, they're still essentially segments where you're mostly on rails, running through a predefined path.

But the main issue is whatever causes the game to lock up. I'm lucky, I've only hit one instance of the game locking up: it's still technically running, but you can't interact with anything. However, some people have gotten entirely stuck due to this. If your PC is on the higher end, you're probably fine. I think. Although I guess there are also issues with AMD GPUs. In any case, you might want to check out other reviews or the community pages to see if your PC might have issues with the game crashing entirely.\t\t\t\t\t
