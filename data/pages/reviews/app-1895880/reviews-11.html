\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a fan of the series since the beginning, this latest entry carries the same charm and fun as the rest of them.
Your arsenal of weapons in this game are plentiful and very well rounded, I didn't find myself disliking one weapon over the other once I upgraded them all. Of course there is a bit of a grind to get the resources to fully upgrade everything but by no means is it a requirement if you don't mind a bit of an added challenge.
The platforming & puzzles carry that classic Ratchet & Clank feel while not being stale, I did find one or two puzzles that racked my brain a little more than the others, but for the most part its friendly for both beginners and veterans like myself. Exploration is, again, on par with the previous titles, offering a bit more freedom out of mapped perimeters. I am one who enjoys testing the boundaries of maps and as I expected, there were very few ways to get yourself stuck in polygons/environment requiring a checkpoint restart. The enemies and boss fights do get a bit recycled towards the end but with a few visual changes that (in my opinion) still keep the game fun and relatively fresh.
This title played great on my Deck, with only a few frame drops in some of the busier cinematics and playable areas. Aside from very rare and very brief character t-poses when skipping cinematics, there were no game breaking issues I ran into.

Overall, I was just as pleased with this game as I have been with all the others after a solid 26 hours to get all the achievements. I absolutely plan on replaying this one just as I have with the other entries in the series.

Throw this one on your wishlist or grab it now, either way it's a fantastic addition to your library!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnly in a few hours in. Primarily bought the game for the special effects. I consider it an entry point to next gen or this current gen gaming, considering it was a launch title for PS5. The fur effects look brilliant. I left the config settings on default as changing them showed a blank screen when loading a save file. If you leave the config settings on default there are no frame drops or stutters.

The game itself, the shooting is fun, using a mouse and keyboard I felt caused less motion sickness, I also turned off motion blur and depth of field which helped.

Some parts reminded me of Invader Zim, the voice acting. One of the enemies fought early on is a huge dinosaur, the face of which reminded me of a character from Spawn, almost a direct copy with horns and teeth looking very similar, I felt the 3D character was well designed but unoriginal.

That's kind of how I feel about the game, it is a combination of various cartoon cultural troupes but lacks a unique feel in the game itself.

The sound on a 5.1 Surround speaker pc setup, the dialogue was not audible, only after switching to stereo on windows and setting headphones as an option in game with mono audio, could I make out what the character were saying during in-game cut scenes.

The community page shows that the devs are not updating the game, so this is what we get, I wonder how PS5 plays.

It is nice to have the game and be able to play it. The main draw for the game is the special effects and graphics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's another solid Ratchet and Clank game. If you've played one before, then you know what to expect. The story is fun and carries the game well. The weapons are great, for the most part, and leveling them up is satisfying as always. If you like fun, creative ways of blowing up bad guys, then absolutely get this game. Despite the good, I saw two downsides to this game, neither of which made this a bad game.

First, there wasn't any need to go back to most of the planets for collectibles. Pretty much everything can be found on the first run-through. This is because you start off with the mobility upgrades from previous games and there's no late-game upgrades to unlock previously unreachable areas.

The second downside is that the achievements are too simple. For instance, there's no incentive to upgrade all of the weapons. I like this kind of achievement because it makes me try new tactics to get the upgrades. There are (pretty much) no achievements after the first play-through. (I think) every Ratchet and Clank game has New Game+ with tougher enemies and bigger weapons, but in this one you only have to play it for about 10 minutes to get the last achievement. I'm still playing to upgrade everything because I love the series, but don't tell me I 100%ed the game when there's still plenty more game to play.\t\t\t\t\t

SUMMARY


GOOD

+ Excellent graphics and environments

+ Works well using PC and PlayStation controls alike

+ Good old Ratchet & Clank run-and-gun action

+ No shortage of nostalgia elements for the long-time fans, easy to learn for newcomers

+ Contains most of the key components and elements Ratchet & Clank is known for


NOT SO GOOD

- Rather repetitive mini-boss fights

- The enemies could have used a little more variety

- Story is decent, but a little too short


VERDICT


8,7/10


I say that even with some obvious shortcomings, Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart is a fun platformer and shooter. And easy to learn and enjoy even for newcomers, even if the large amounts of nostalgic details otherwise favor and are the most enjoyable for the long-time enjoyers of the series.



However, I may have a personal theory about the shortcomings. I learned that Rift Apart was first and foremost made as an attempt to test how well the game would function using newer technology. Especially PlayStation 5's custom-designed solid-state drive was especially utilized both for the Rift Tether mechanic and for the scenes during which Ratchet or Rivet get quickly thrown through dimensions from one planet to another, changing the scenery near instantaneously. This wouldn't have been possible without the extremely fast loading times of PS 5's SSD. Therefore, shorter story and slightly lacking variety with enemies could be a form of precaution – perhaps the developers at Insomniac Games didn't want to risk investing too much into the game, in the event the end result turned out to be a complete disaster on PS 5.


Luckily, the game turned out quite well if I say so myself. Definitely a must have for any die hard Ratchet & Clank fan. Although, personally I would recommend waiting for the game to be on sale at a discount – 60 euros might be a little too much for a game with a shorter story.

Some more personal notes. When I played through the game, I would occassionally use the mouse and the keyboard, occassionally a PC compatible DualShock controller. And I can confirm the game works well on both, despite Ratchet & Clank's history as a PlayStation exclusive franchise until now. I must say I consider that a positive accomplishment from the developers.

(Granted, it helps Ratchet & Clank doesn't rely on excessive button mashing unlike classic God of War titles or Asura's Wrath).\t\t\t\t\t
