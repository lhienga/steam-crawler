\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a fan of the series since I was first introduced to it on PS3. So I've been hanging out to play this on PC for a long a time.

I'll start with the specs. I've read some commentary about this game absolutely requiring an SSD to be playable. In my experience that's not exactly true. I ran this on both my laptop and my desktop. The laptop is a 7 year old Alienware with a Geforce 970M connected to a 4K TV. I installed the game on an *external* HDD (connected via USB). It did struggle with some of the cinematics and the graphics are a bit fuzzy but the game was perfectly playable outside of that. On my RTX 3050 at 1080p, installed on an SSD, the game was gorgeous and played flawlessly.

If you've played the previous games in the series then game play wise there's not really a lot new here. I mean there is the rift gimmick I guess. It does add a twist being transported to new settings instantly which is fun but ultimately you're still doing the same sorts of things in those locations. The combat isn't too hard but challenging enough in spots to keep you on your toes. There's a truck load of weapons to buy and use. The weapon levelling system is similar to previous games (from memory) and is good at encouraging you to try and use all the weapons at your disposal. For completionists it also adds incentive to play through the game a second time, as it's unlikely you'll have everything fully upgraded in one run.

The graphics in this game are stunning. It's easily one of the most beautiful games that I've played in recent memory. Possibly the best I've ever seen?

The sound effects are pretty much what you'd expect for this type of game. The music is good but very understated for the most part.

Finally, the story which is usually the most important piece of the puzzle for me. It was just okay. I like the way the story switched between the characters which was cool. Having said that, I think Ratchet and Clank kinda felt like they took a back seat to Rivet and Kit. That's not necessarily a bad thing and I look forward to seeing them in future games but did seem a little odd in a game called "Ratchet and Clank".

So to wrap up, I don't think there is anything really groundbreaking here but it's a great looking game that is just fun to play. Keep 'em coming Sony.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRatchet and Clank was my childhood and considering I'm never going to get a ps5 just for Rift Apart and I didn't care much for the ps3 titles, this is the most recent RAC game I have played in at least a decade (not counting replays of the old games). I heard good things about this particular entry and since it was released on steam I said ♥♥♥♥ it, sold some tf2 items I didn't care about and bought it.

Let me start positive: I really enjoyed the gameplay, which is good - because RAC has honestly been all about the gameplay and not so much the plot ever since its transition to PS3 and onward. I completed my first playthrough in 15 hours - hardest difficulty, all collectibles - and honestly had a lot of fun doing it. There are some neat new elements in Rift Apart like phase jumping which is essentially a dodge move. Enemies will actually rush you down and try to overwhelm you while allies give them fire support - it can be pretty hectic and you need to be pretty snappy with your dodges to get out untouched. I quite enjoyed the challenge and I never felt like anything was too artificially difficult (Except maybe the enforcer challenge, you kinda need to max out the gun completely or else you'll run out of ammo and probably just die trying to avoid the stacking enemies). The bosses and minibosses all seemed pretty fun too! Honestly the game was a bit short for a full-price title. But maybe I'm just a ratchet and clank expert.

There's really just one major, almost unforgivable aspect of this game that really needs to be brought to attention. The writing is honestly just.. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ awful - and I really am not expecting much. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne where I wish I could give a middling review.

Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart is an excellent "switch off your brain" game. Aside from freezing for about 10 seconds every 10 mins in the last third of play, extremely competent game in every respect.

It is everything you expect from a Ratchet & Clank game. Humorous adventure with a variety of creative guns, enemies, set-piece rail grinding, collectathons etc.

For me, given Insomniac created the excellent Spider-Man PS4 game, I think the story is a bit by the numbers. You keep jumping from planet to planet with little sense to the journey. You mine a crystal. It breaks. So instead of mining another you go to the "fixing things" planet. There you get attacked by the fixing things robot for not much reason, and clank says he's been fixed and the robot stops attacking you. What?

The story has the feel of being an excellent improv show. But this is written! It should fit together better than this. You wander into a place where your weapons are confiscated, and the big bad snatches your special device from you there. Then you fight him and you have your weapons back somehow. Then next time you come to the same place, you take *no precautions* to arm yourself, or in any other way protect yourself in case the big bad is back. You just sort of know that he won't be back this time.

Same for the worlds. There are a bunch of planets but half of them feel more like a collection of platforms than an environment that is actually used by the creatures inside it. They're high fidelity but they don't make sense as environments, don't have .e.g predator & prey creatures or distinct areas of farm & housing. It would be fine if they were more distinct in other ways but they're mostly just haphazard rocks & pipes. Some manage to be a bit repetitive even when they are small.

The gunplay is as always excellent, as is the movement, with a dodge and boost added. The moment to moment feel of the game is fantastic.

The amount of repetition is subjective but it *felt* like there was quite a bit of repeating bosses, even in a not particularly long game.

Sound design & music is exceptional.

Overall it feels very "paint by numbers". And they painted very well! If you want to relax, then this is a great distraction game.

But I doubt I'll remember much of this in two years' time.\t\t\t\t\t
