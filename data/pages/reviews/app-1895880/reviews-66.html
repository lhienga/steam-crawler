\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I am a bit biased. A bit very biased in fact. The PS2 and PS3 Ratchet & Clank games are my childhood. I enjoy the franchise a lot. Rift Apart has some mighty big shoes to fill for me. It has to live up to the games I loved in my childhood and that I remember through some serious rose tinted glasses.

It succeeds at this. It succeeds at this quite handily. Ratchet & Clank Rift Apart is every bit the Ratchet & Clank games I grew up with and loved. It's got the truly insane guns. The over the top universe and a smooth kind of action platforming. About a third through the game you get introduced to the hover boots and it brought me right back to the awesome high speed movement of A Crack In Time on the PS3. It felt great back then and it still feels great now.

Visually the game is absurd. Even without ray tracing. It looks like a Pixar movie. Except all of it is rendered in real time and at a very smooth framerate. The game never dropped below 60 on my rig. Good job on Nixxes handling the port to PC. And double good job for them actively taking Steam Deck and Linux into account. There might not be a native Linux release, but Proton is fully supported and the game runs like a charm with no issues.

There's only one downside to all of this. The game's length. I took my time and beat the story in about 12 hours. However, there are still armour pieces and golden bolts that I have not found. I will be going back to find those.\t\t\t\t\t
