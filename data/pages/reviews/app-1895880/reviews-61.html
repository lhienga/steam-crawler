\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long-time Ratchet & Clank fan, going all the way back to the first game in 2002, Rift Apart is a pure joy to behold. There's only nine or ten worlds, but they're immersive and very easy to get lost in, with their breathtaking vistas, wide range and backed with a grand orchestral score. You'll visit a few of them more than once in different phases of the game, and they will seem different. The enemy mix is a little thin, and you'll figure out how to tackle most of them in a few worlds, but the game mixes up the situations to keep it interesting.

And what Ratchet & Clank game is complete without an arena to battle in? There are also side missions for our robot friends, though these levels can be skipped if you wish, with no penalty or worry of missing out on a trophy.

There are a heartwarming story arc or two mixed in with all the enemy butt-kicking. Ratchet's matured since his last canon appearance in Into the Nexus, and we work with him through some self-doubt, even as his kind influence helps the others to overcome theirs.

The million-bolt question: Will he want to go find the other lombaxes once this adventure is over?

I'm now on my second New Game Plus playthrough, ratcheting (heh) the difficulty up a bit and leveling all my weapons to max. I will probably still play it again after that. I can't get enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a fan of the series since the very first installment, and this game is definitely no different in that regard. I played the PS5 release first, and then tried the PC release once it came out and I was actually amazed at how well things went. Yes there were a few launch bugs, but a grand majority of high profile releases recently have had difficult launches, it seems like, and this is an IP that has never before been ported to the PC. That being said, I think I prefer the PC release in its current state after numerous bugfixes and patches.

I didn't think it was going to feel right to play Ratchet and Clank with a mouse and keyboard, but the precision afforded to you makes some seriously fun acrobatic playstyles much more viable than using a controller. It's just all around a very fun game to play. There's so many different weapons, all beautifully animated and bedazzled with post-processing effects and all with a really hefty punchy sound to them. There's a relatively limited amount of enemy variety, especially on the maximum and minimum difficulties, but when they combine together the battlefield turns into a lightshow and they all compliment the shortcomings of the other, if allowed to. Each weapon has a niche that it fills perfectly, and as your situation changes you can hot-swap to whatever tool you need, and it's a great time. Standard fare for Ratchet and Clank: gameplay is the focus and Insomniac nailed it like they always do.

The humor is a little on the sanitized side compared to older titles - back then it seems like raunchy humor was easier to get away with - but there's still a few little winks and nods here and there to let the fans know they're trying. This game approaches the saturday-morning-cartoon style world with a bit more grit than I was expecting, and I really liked that. The characters feel mature and dynamic, Ratchet seems to have internalized all the lessons he's learned from the older games, lessons that Rivet didn't have the opportunity to learn, so you see the differences in who they are play out. Both of them are deeply flawed, but the focus is squarely on Rivet and Clank as far as character arcs go. It's definitely not Shakespeare, but when the story is delivered to you in quippy conversations between volleys of a minigun that shoots little black holes at people, or while grinding down a rail at 200mph in the rain from a mile up in the air, then keeping things simple is appropriate.

It's a great game, and the PC release makes the series accessible to anybody even those that don't have a playstation console, which is my favorite thing about the game right now. I'd love to see a remastered release of all the old games someday on PC, just like they did with Spyro, and I assume the success of Rift Apart will be related to the possibility of that. Even with that being considered, I think it's a very fun game all on its own and would be an alright introduction to the series.

If it's on sale, I think it's worth your money. I'd argue it's worth the full price tag, but I'm a longtime fan and that's bias. Use the refund feature if you don't like it, or can't run it, or whatever in the first two hours. The first two hours are pretty indicative of how the rest of the game is gonna go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know that Ratchet & Clank is a long running series, and jumping in on the latest game might not give the best of impressions of the series overall, but to me it is just a generic action shooter game with quirky characters. And there's nothing wrong with that.

The gameplay can be fun at times and it was refreshingly forgiving - theres very little consequence for dying and the game basically just lets you pick up where you left off after you met your demise. A lot of games I played recently can be rather punishing in this regard so it was a good change of pace. Combat feels good, but the enemies arent very memorable sadly. It basically boils down to shoot with whatever gun you have handy until it is dead. There are way too many weapons in the game - this is a bad thing because none of the guns seemingly have specific strengths or weaknesses. Mostly youll be switching around between the ones that do the most damage or the ones that can prevent your enemies from moving etc. Generally I just switched to the ones that still had ammo and used that until I had more ammo for another better suited gun. This over abundance of guns is exasperated by the fact that the game pauses when you attempt to use the gun selection ring. There are also more than one level to the selection ring which means to get to some weapons you need to shift the ring to the next page with Q or E. This just totally takes you out of the action and hinders more than it helps. When you scroll your mouse wheel youll also switch to the next or previous weapon which can really be anything as I never really memorized the order of usefulness. I tended to just scroll through until I found one that seemed appropriate and occasionally shooting some shots that obviously was useless for the situation at hand.

The platforming parts of the game were enjoyable and I had fun traversing the various areas in the game. I loved zooming around with the hover boots. Click Click Click! And youre off!!

The story is basically some multi-dimensional waffle about saving the universe from some evil see-through-forehead guy or some such. It didnt really make a strong impression on me, I will confess. This is likely because I had no prior experience with the series so I had no real attachment. Or just the fact that I didnt pay much attention.

Graphics is pretty good and it ran well for me. FSR tends to look pretty bad, but I used that along with dynamic resolution to get an FPS of around 60 - 80 (targeting 72fps.) It ran really well and looked fantastic all things considered. I barely noticed dips in resolution from 1440p while playing and I didn't have a single stutter. (This is with all eye candy turned on to the max :D)

Sound was good but kinda soft wrt volume. Couldnt really figure out what caused it, but turning things up on the amp solves it.

And yeah, thats basically it. Maybe a bit expensive at full price... my copy was technically a gift. I would say if you really want to play it hold off until the price comes down a bit.\t\t\t\t\t
