\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally fun for a casual first and second playthrough since you're still checking out all the guns, and an extremely pretty game...
But it crashed waaay too frequently on my computer, and it felt very "bite-sized" compared to previous Ratchet and Clank games that I've played years ago on the Playstation2. In 30 hours I'd completed 2 playthroughs and got 100% of the achievements, it's disappointingly short and there is no post-game content to keep you playing. No racing, no optional endgame bosses (unless you count the Gold cup at the arena), the reward for collecting all the Golden Bolts is lame, and there is no Insomniac-Museum-Equivalent to unlock for the completionists. It's worth a play, but it just made me want to go back and play the PS2 games... Shouldn't the goal be to make your player want to replay Rift Apart over and over? Instead you made me wish I could go back 3 generations.\t\t\t\t\t
