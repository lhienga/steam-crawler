\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a REALLY pretty game and it has nice and snappy gameplay, Insomniac have done amazing as usual. If you are an existing Ratchet and Clank fan, or are new to the series and enjoy 3D shooter-platformers, then you will enjoy this game.

The photo mode and level of HUD/accessibility customisation I thought was really cool and its not something you see in every game.

Now, this was an awesome game, and I've completed the main story and got all collectables in non-Challenge Mode, but there are various things where the newer Ratchet and Clank games don't live up to the originals, what I can think of right now I've listed below. But, PLEASE do not let this dissuade you from playing Rift Apart, it really is a fantastic game, and is easily one of the best in the Ratchet and Clank series.

My thoughts on Rift Apart vs the PS2 games:


- No classic David Bergeaud soundtrack. At least 50% of what made the PS2 games fantastic was the background music. Newer R&C games have had a more generic action/sci-fi/orchestral soundtrack. It sounds like there was some falling out between David and Insomniac, nice Reddit post about it here: https://www.reddit.com/r/RatchetAndClank/comments/v0wgg0/i_actually_contacted_david_bergeaud_about/

- Character writing. Ratchet and Clank themselves are much more generic heroes in the newer games compared to the PS2 games (especially compared to the very first game). The titular characters don't get anymore development in this game, which I think is in part due to the introduction of Rivet and Kit (which do come across as more interesting characters) and because this game is kinda a soft reboot (it is technically the next game in the 'main' continuity since 2013's Into The Nexus, so it has been a while since that story has been picked up). Character writing for other main characters isn't that interesting either (Qwark, Nefarious, etc).

- Key elements of Nefarious' character have been lost. In this game: he doesn't malfunction and broadcast Lance and Janice; Lawrence does not show up; Nefarious has become increasingly less menacing every time he is used again in a game (reminds me of the Borg in Star Trek) and he doesn't actually do much (in both A Crack In Time and Rift Apart he has a cool opening, but then just bums around in his space station/base until the end).

- Qwark hardly appears :( This might be due to Jim Ward not voice acting him anymore, but nevertheless he hardly shows up in this game.

- The humour is not edgy like the PS2 games. The games have become much more kid friendly, while in the PS2 games it did an excellent job of being a kids game while incorporating adult humour.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has been playing and enjoying the Ratchet and Clank games for nearly my entire life, I can safely say this game is among the best in the franchise and probably has my favourite "sequence" out of all the games (Torren IV).

Lots of fun weapons to use, and while a few do return from previous games, new weapons like the Void reactor are really fun to use. I was also surprised but the difficulty of the game. While I always play these games on the hardest difficulty, this typically hasn't meant much for previous Ratchet Games, but here I felt like I had to actually adapt and use my environment/arsenal or weapons more.

Getting to the later stages of the game and setting up a bunch of passive damage, zooming around with the hover boots/rift thing to flank the enemies and hit directly with the stronger weapons was a lot of fun. Definitely the most "intense" that the combat has felt in this series.

Music was often non-existent and the little that was there was very atmospheric. GOOD atmospheric music, but a far cry from the groovy tunes of the original games.

The dialogue is a bit rigid and often quite blunt/direct. I think for the robot characters this is mostly intentional, but sometimes it harms Ratchet/Rivet's characters a bit too. Some of the humour felt kinda "try hard", but the game did get a fair few genuine laughs out of me.

Overall easy recommend. You'll have a great time.\t\t\t\t\t
