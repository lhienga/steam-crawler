\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR for fans of the series, this game is not worth the time nor the high price tag; working but semi-questionable port for a game that feels like a Ratchet game for people who have never actually played Ratchet games

With all the talk about Rift Apart's PC port, I figured I would give it a go and ramble about it as a long-time Ratchet fan (PS2 through PS3 barring Into the Nexus). So might as well start with the technical quality of the port.

Port Quality
Running a 3070TI, Ryzen 9 5950X, 32 GB ram, and a Crucial MX500 SATA SSD (560 Mbps), I ran the game easily enough at 1080p with a mix of high and medium settings (leaving bloom, etc. on, no raytracing) for 60 FPS throughout. The only times I ever encountered frame drops was when using the Burst/Blast Pistol and the Enforcer/Executor, which seems to be really common amongst users (some pointing it out to be a Windows issue, but I'm unsure on that). There were two total times I crashed: once crashing to desktop when quitting to the main menu, and another time as a hard lock crash when spamming a mix of Cold Front and RYNO rounds, on a later visit to Sargasso.

Ultimately to me the port runs fine from an "it works" perspective. There were numerous smaller bugs I encountered playing the game (mostly audio, the audio triggers in this game SUCK), which I don't know if are related to the port or the actual final product, but it mostly works. I can't speak for higher end users who are trying to crank this game up to 4k with all the bells and whistles, as it seems like those features can cause issues (between crashing and not looking ideal). Nixxes is still patching things up as we speak, so things may continue to improve with more time. Buyer beware in this regard, and do your research on hardware specs for this game (there are several videos on Youtube to compare for this).

However, even if you do get this game running with optimal settings, is it really worth the time investment?

The Game Itself
I'm going to say no. While the game still looks incredible even when on lower settings, stuff like the gameplay, writing, and the pacing of it all don't add up for a good experience.

Gameplay itself is a wash. Playing on Renegade Legend (hardest) for basically all of my game time, the "challenge" presented feels not so much challenging as it is cheap. This game has a LOT of weaponry designed to distract or weaken + freeze up enemies, which in turn usually makes them not even a threat. And when you're not using those weapons, you will eventually get hit by a stray bullet or melee enemy from behind you because of two things: Phantom Dash and the camera.

The combat is mostly balanced around you having the Phantom Dash, which is an i-frame dash new to the series. So enemies will now lead shots as you're strafing, which more or less demands you change strafe direction and/or to use Phantom Dash as liberally as possible. And to top that off, the camera is now zoomed in behind your character at all times in combat for this game. Most combat encounters are wide open spaces where enemies have a lot of room to hide and shoot you. So while you're focused in on a small part of the fight, something off screen is liable to hit you for free and it ends being incredibly frustrating. Which is where you use stuff like the Topiary Sprinkler and Cold Snap stop enemies completely and shoot with everything else you have on hand. Not very fun either way.

As for the writing, I understand that the game was rewritten at some point in development, but it still doesn't feel cohesive enough for a 20+ year old series. For one, it's like Ratchet and Clank have both forgotten most of what's happened prior to this game, e.g. Ratchet acting like Rivet is the first other Lombax he's ever met (she's not). Anything that pertains to previous games is basically treated like a memberberry with no actual consequence (Ratchet 1 enemies reused all over, characters from old games returning for just brief moments during the intro just so they can have alternate dimension counterparts, etc.). I didn't mind Rivet and Kit, though even their own development doesn't seem complete with how the game flies by. Dr. Nefarious is back but only as mostly a coward for the sake of Emperor Nefarious existing now. I could probably go on, but all these small things just add up and it's quite annoying.

Probably the best part of the game are the graphics and the setpieces out of context. If you don't pay attention to anything else but those things, you will probably love this game, which is why I feel this game is designed for people who have never played Ratchet. It somehow hasn't refined its gameplay enough for the better, and continues to double down on modern Sony-isms of characters talking too much and having graphical setpieces that do nothing for meaningful gameplay.

So for anyone that actually cared about the series at any point, ignore this game. Otherwise wait for a sale if you're deathly curious.\t\t\t\t\t
