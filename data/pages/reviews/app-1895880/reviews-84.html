Phenomenal Game

As a die hard fan of the original games on PS2 and PS3, I went into this game with nostalgia glasses on expecting to be disappointed. However to my surprise, this game blew me away. Rift Apart is hands down one of the best RaC games ever made. A prime example of how a modern day sequel to a long running franchise should be. First of all, the game is absolutely gorgeous to look at. Especially with max ray tracing and HDR, this is easily one of the best looking games I've ever seen in my life. The gameplay is as smooth as ever and easy to pick up as all RaC games should be. Combat feels good, and there is some new in-game mechanics added to the game to give the player more movement and ability to dodge attacks. Another thing Insomniac absolutely nailed was the cutscenes. They are all visually stunning and the characters feel very real with the help of phenomenal voice acting. Lots of humor in the game as well which was very nice to see as it felt like a tribute to the originals. Lastly, the guns are the best they have ever been in RaC, Rift Aparts weapons are so much fun to use and make you feel godly. Especially on NG+ when you get the Omega versions of each. I also just wanted to comment on the direct storage thing. I did notice a small difference on my SATA then on my NVMe, but not too much. But this game for sure could not be ran on a hard drive.

List of negatives:
-Game is a little short
-Quite a few reused bosses and re-skins of enemies
-Battle arena (which is usually the best part of any RaC game) was very disappointing. It isn't very challenging at all and the boss fights were reused from the main story missions.
-Game is pretty demanding, I didn't have any issues personally but for someone with a mid range-low range PC I can see how this game could give you troubles.

Overall a 9/10, I highly recommend to anyone who has never played a RaC to pick it up.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite a few hiccups in the port to PC resulting in some stability and performance issues at launch, I had a truly wonderful time revisiting the universe of Ratchet & Clank. This is a brand of gameplay I can easily slip into and always find it immensely enjoyable.

The series' trademark sense of humor is on full display here and never feels like it's overstaying its welcome, and yet Rift Apart also has a way of tugging at the heart in a way the previous games were never able to really accomplish. Credit has to be given to Debra Wilson (voice of Kit) and Jennifer Hale (voice of Rivet) for their outstanding work here.

The weapon variety (another series staple) is outstanding... in previous games, there was always one or two weapons I'd completely ignore (looking at you, Tools of Destruction Tornado Launcher), but that didn't happen here. While some of them were effectively overlaps in terms of their mechanics (such as the Pixelizer being functionally another shotgun) I enjoyed leveling all of them up to max.

This also has to be one of the best looking games I've ever played... benefitted greatly by its cartoony artstyle. The Pixar comparisons have been made with this series since its PS3 days, but this is getting incredibly close to cinematic quality. There are a few visual flaws apparent in the PC version (some really bad looking water reflections, for instance, with some graphical settings), once you get the settings dialed in the game is stunningly beautiful and well animated.

I was also pleased to see the return of the series' battle arenas (one of my favorite parts of previous games), however I do wish these were a bit more fleshed out. I could spend hours on end making my way through progressively more difficult challenges in the arenas of previous R&C entries, but they seemed to be over in the blink of an eye here... with the only reason to revisit them being to level up weapons. Zurkon Jr's announcement lines during these do make them quite entertaining however.

As someone who has not (and probably will not) bought a PS5, the wait for this game to come to PC was well worth it. Some kinks to be ironed out, for sure, but I for one am just glad to have Ratchet back! Now if we could only get the rest of the series ported over...\t\t\t\t\t

Double glad because it's actually a phenomenal game, and a very good PC port on top of that. Sure, I've run into a few glitches here and there; but I can name every instance on one hand.

PROS
- Very punchy, fluid combat
- Mostly freeform travelling - You can travel to and from different planets to complete missions or go back and look for collectibles you may have missed
- Arena mode is a fun, if rather short tier challenge system
- Ratchet & Rivet, and of course by extension Clank & Kit, are wonderfully acted and animated characters who have kept me drawn in
- Every world you travel to introduced a new mechanic for you to use, some transferring to other worlds as well
- Lots of cosmetics- all of which are earned. HOW'S THAT FOR SOME PRIDE AND ACCOMPLISHMENTS?

CONS (a rather short list)

Seeing as I have only one con and it's not even a big deal, it's a 10/10 experience to me. Do yourself a favor and check it out!\t\t\t\t\t
