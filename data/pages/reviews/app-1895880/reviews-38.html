\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you want to jerk it to a fuzzy alien girl? You'll love this game. Are you a man-baby who just loves anything Ratchet & Clank regardless of quality? You'll love this game.

If you want a fun game...you will hate this, however.

First off, multiverses need to stop. The childish and pathetic notion of the human mind being so amazing and incredible and special that whole new universes are created based on our actions is genuinely embarassing. New realities being made whether we lean to the left to fart or the right any time we feel a rumbly in our tumbly is just tired and sad.

Second off, this is not Ratchet. I'd say Clank is fine, but MY Ratchet? The confident bad motha who has saved three different galaxies on several occaisions is not here. Instead is a timid fellow who isn't sure if he'll measure up to his species. Bro, what!? You. Have. Saved. Galaxies. You defeated the guy who ran your species into another dimension! Please act like it, my dude.

The game itself? It's pretty middling...when it works. There was nothing that stood out to me set piece wise. Wide open fields to explore that just have stuff to shoot and collectibles to find. Such innovation, Insomniac.

Instead, the innovation has been focused in bold new areas. Core components of the game don't function consistently...

Weapon wheel? Usually it equips the weapon I selected...but sometimes it doesn't! Oooooooh!

Platforming? Maybe my character will suddenly lose all momentum mid jump and fall to their death! Funsies!

Sure, usually I can pause the game to relieve my bladder and resume playing...but sometimes the game crashes in the time it takes to take a tinkle. Tee hee hee!

All of this on top of my personal buggiest of bugbears. Dodging. Ratchet & Clank now has a push button dodge feature...and just like the Arkham sequels or the Spider-Man games if you don't push it at the exact correct nano second then your opponents will slide 10-feet to still make contact. Oh, be still my heart!

Sure, the game looks pretty, when it runs...or when it loads in the level properly, but it just is unstable and glitchy in the extreme. Add to that the notion that it barely does anything of substance with the dimension hopping itself, just using it as an excuse to have more levels to maybe load correctly...

Insomniac. Sony. They just put out a garbage product, pure and simple.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHey, remember when games were fun?

The Ratchet & Clank series has been built around the core concept that using bigger and more bizarre guns to shoot things is fun, and that's a winning formula. Rift Apart does not stray from that principle. It's a welcome break from games whose identity can only be described as "will make you eat your controller in frustration because hard apparently equals good."

The controls are fluid and intuitive, the graphics are lush and vibrant, and the worlds are fun to navigate. Small side stages that require some prudent platforming skills are never unfair and feel rewarding to complete in and of themselves. Occasionally, Clank has to do puzzle-solving stages that are ever so slightly reminiscent of Zelda dungeons. They'll make your brain work a surprising amount for a game that is mostly about mowing down enemies with an arsenal of wacky weapons, including a gun that that turns your enemies into a bush, a gun that shoots dog rockets, and a gun that flings shuriken all over the place.

The whole game keeps up a good pace and is delightfully energetic. The various planets you visit are all unique and memorable. The game never tries to be srs bsns in a sea of games that play up cheap drama to try and be deeper than they actually are. Rift Apart strikes a balanced tone between lighthearted frivolity and ponderous brooding, managing to be wholesome but not emotionally manipulative. It's a game you can enjoy at any age and any time.

I heartily recommend Rift Apart if you like Ratchet & Clank games, if you like a good run-and-gun title, or if you just want something that never stops being a joy to play. A superb title with excellent presentation all around.

And I guess I recommend it if furry girls are your thing.\t\t\t\t\t

A solid foundation for the future of the game series.

There's a lot to say about Rift apart, but I'll let essayists on Youtube get the details to you.

The game is great fun. There's not that much in in gadget-wise or puzzle-wise, but the pacing is really tight. The characters are well-represented and likeable and I had a great time any interactions took place. The weapons are fun and level well.

The only drawback is that really the game is not very long. I don't think this particular story could be drawn out any more than it is, but it's great the whole way thru.\t\t\t\t\t
