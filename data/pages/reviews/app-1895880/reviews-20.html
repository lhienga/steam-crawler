\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent game if you're in the mood for something a bit more old-school.

Pro:
- Game is incredible to look at, both in sheer technical prowess and artstyle
- Packed to the brim with charm and genuinely funny jokes
- Decent length for the amount of variety it offers, doesn't overstay its welcome like so many other modern bloated games

Con:
- Platforming physics are seriously janky at times (not in the fun way, but just straight up broken way)
- Even on maximum difficulty the game is quite frankly way too easy once you are past the first stage
- Bosses are reused a bit too much

All in all, I'm just happy these kinds of 3D Action platformers are still getting made by a major studio and would recommend it for that reason alone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up playing the Ratchet and Clank games on PS2. I really enjoyed the PS3 future series as a departure from the classic PS2 era of Ratchet and Clank; they're both good in their own way.

The PS4 reboot had me scared a little, as it was the first Ratchet and Clank game I did not fully enjoy.

However, this game is an affectionate love letter to every moment from the past games; many of the maps are cleverly redesigned stages from previous games, with little nods and winks in nooks and crannies previous games held collectables or secrets. Don't get it twisted, though, this game is innovative and well-made and can absolutely stand on its own right without an appeal to nostalgia - if this was a players first introduction to Ratchet and Clank, I'm sure they'd enjoy it all the same, even if some of the story beats fell a bit flat, due to a lack of perspective on the previous games.

The core gameplay is satisfying; gunplay, melee, smashing loads of crates and the platforming roots of the series are still here. Many new interesting minigames and elements have been added as well. None hog too much of the run-time which keeps every new element novel and interesting, and if you truly hate a new addition, the pause menu has a helpful "skip puzzle" option. Personally, even when a puzzle didn't feel particularly engaging for me, I never skipped because the character dialogue is interesting enough for me to want to stick it out, and most of the puzzles are strong enough to motivate you to complete them without any reward in mind.

The story is actually very heartwarming and intriguing. Although the dialogue can feel a little hammy for the first half, this stops feeling the case towards the midpoint. Stellar performances from all of the voice talent. Insomniac really gives enough time for the new characters to be developed and feel naturally involved, and the new motivations and development for longstanding characters from the series feel seamless.

Visually, this game is beautiful; I've not been sold on the character design of Ratchet since the future series began (because he looked so different, imo) but this game finally made the design pop for me. Every level is rich and beautiful, with a scenic shot of the planet before you land, and a nice panning angle of the opening area of each new planet showcasing the stunningly gorgeous landscape of where ever you've just found yourself.

Additionally, the animations are phenomenal. Certain scenes would fall flat were it not for the amazing facial animations and body language of the characters portraying complex emotions.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is at the moment the very best looking game with cartoony graphics and characters on PC. The optimization is good and the performance (whether it was achieved by some workarounds or not) is impressive. The transitions are smooth even on gen 3 NVMe drive. Game has a lot of charm and likeable characters, although the general maturity of humor and dialogues have been dumbed down a bit compared to previous installments. The story is decent, it is reasonably put into context of the franchises lore (without actual need of playing previous games, watching the movie etc.), although the surprise factor of it falls flat at times with the development of plot being too obvious. Gameplay itself is the weakest part, as the combat feels repetitive, despite there being so many unique weapons. It's a pity that the game failed to showcase these, there is not really much of a reason (such as clever use of environment, variety of enemies) to not be using same weapons over and over. Game is also rather short – max 10 hours if played just for story and 15–20 hours for completion. I definitely do appreciate games that do not waste my time nowadays, but I have to admit that Rift Apart would feel more empty if I didn´t take my time admiring the worlds and character animations, collecting items or replaying some mini games. At the time of release on PS5 this would be a 9/10 game. As it stands, it is sold for full price on Steam even though a considerable amount of time has passed since the original release on PS5. Because its graphics are one of the main selling points, it will probably age a lot following years. Therefore I cannot give it such a high score. I am glad that Ratchet and Clank made it to the PC after all, so that those of us riding the waves of nostalgia have one more game to enjoy. General audience can pick this game as well, altough I would recommend waiting for a steep discount.

8/10
