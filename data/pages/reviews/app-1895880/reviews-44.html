Now for someone whose first game on PS2 Ratchet & Clank 2: Going Commando, I was expecting a more comic story with funny gags from here and then, but without entering in spoilers, the story on this game had a more serious tone which caught me off guard, and I will say that the story is good (won't deny) but quite honestly I missed some comic relief, and I felt that it could be longer.

If you ask me honestly if I recommend this game to anyone, I would recommend without hesitation, but I would advice you that the game is rather short (around 10 hours to finish the game with all achievements, and a few more hours to level all the weapons, omega included) and maybe you wanna pay when the game is on discount.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a huge Ratchet & Clank fan! I've played nearly every single one all the way through to completion throughout the years, so this review is based on an R&C Veteran.

First off, the game is quite easy. I played on the hardest difficulty all the way through. Literally didn't struggle one bit.

Second off, it feels more like a traditional 3d Action Platformer moreso than a R&C game. If you don't mind a more linear experience, then you'll enjoy this game. If you prefer the old exploration style of gameplay from the past games, you'll not find much of that here.

Third off, the weapons. They are moreso a best hits of past games. There is minor experimentation with new weapons. Wish they would break from the traditional archetypes already.

Fourth off, the story. It's just average. Rivet is quite likable, but Kit is just okay. I feel like they both can be shaped into neat characters in the future. Right now though, Rivet is just overall better written.

Finally, the most important part, combat. It's flashy and fun like usual, but makes many changes to the core formula. Nanotech, weapon leveling, and progression overall are tweaked in a way that feels quite different than what most are used to running through a traditional R&C game. Overall I don't mind this game, but it's definitely one of the weaker games in the series.

So how is it?
Great, in many ways it could even be considered the series' best entry so far.
Its graphics, art style, sound design, many weapons and the way it manages the bring many of its previous entry's gameplay mechanics into one game seamlessly with its unique new ones is in itself praiseworthy.

However, it does lack in many areas where the previous entries did some of them better. For example: The Gladiator arena used to have unique bosses, there's only a little room for exploration given that there aren't many large areas that aren't blocked by invisible walls to avoid sequence-breaking and also to bring it to my main area of critique which is the campaign.

While the scale of all things is huge, the story is quite simple which is not a bad thing, however coming from The Future Saga series of games which had a heavy emphasis on the story by the series standards it does leave a feeling of wanting for more. That is not to say the story is bad or anything its quite fun and Rivet is a nice new addition to the series catalog of fun characters, the actual length of the campaign while not the shortest in the series it's not the longest one either.

It can be beaten twice easily in under 25 hours and thanks to the new game+ it's actually encouraging to do so given the exclusive new weapons and the chance to collect any collectibles and other content you might have missed on your first run of the game.

All in all that is to say that I'm glad that Sony decided to bring the series to PC for the first time and I am extremely thankful to Imsoniac Games for keeping their fan-favorite series alive after all these years with an awesome new installment with implications that our heroes aren't exactly done with their adventures just yet, here's to hoping that it won't take as long this time.

8/10

2024 update: It seems like R&C wont be getting a new game for a while or atleast a one made by Insomniac Games, well it was a good run for a classic Playstation mascot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbout 50 hours into gameplay, while I do have some critiques, this is a great entry to the series.

As someone who's played the first three entries as well as the gladiator spin-off, all several times through. This game does not disappoint except for two arguable things that were missing.

1. the spaceship dogfights
2. the more challenging new game+ annihilation nation challenges.

The spaceship dogfights were pretty good in the first games, and while I was excited to see where they took it in this game, I wasn't too disappointed to see it wasn't there.

The annihilation nation challenges were much more sorely missed. I used to get so much fun out of managing my ammo well enough to beat the bosses at the end of the "endless" challenges in earlier entries, and you will not find that here. What you will find is much more engaging gameplay than earlier games, with a dodge that elevates it completely. It is now possible to survive on good timing which means positioning becomes more strategic and less "just get out of the danger zones".

Weapons are solid and the leveling and raritanium systems are a nice improvement on earlier games. New game+ is excluding the harder annihilation nation challenges about what earlier entries have done with omega versions of weapons now for purchase.

All in all, if you've been in the same situation as me: playing the earlier game remakes on ps3 etc. wondering if this would play as well on pc?

GET THIS GAME. You will not be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA semi-sweet balance between the absolutely brainless saccharine toothache that was the 2016 remake and the complex and contrived plots of the Future series, Rift brings the best of the new (albeit not very "cool") Ratchet and his stalwart tin-can sidekick to PCs the world over. Rivet's addition was welcome and her story and personality were engaging. Being a given for Insomniac: AAA+ graphics, controls, and effects, the whole shebang etc etc. A rock solid product for fans of all ages even if the dialogue between the good guys tends to be on the extremely safe and cuddly side...This development, being a person who has bought every game between the first entry all the way up to Nexus, was a pleasant and exciting surprise.
The team supporting the PC release was lightning fast to cover most of the major graphical issues that I had, and thankfully provided a robust array of video options to help tune the game to get the best player experience possible. The handshake between Valve and SCE alone warrant a positive review as, hopefully, more of Ratchet's galaxies find their way unto Steam over time! Outstanding show!

One nigle though, Insomniac... if your dev team really DOES read the slop on the internet... Next time, maybe a few less fire and forget weapons/glove weapons? Sure they're fun for the spectacle, but the visceral sensation of "shotgunning" a mook is a tried and true part of every great shooter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long-time fan of Ratchet and Clank starting from PS2 it's great to see them finally coming to PC with an entry that is mostly a return to form. The gameplay is fantastic as always. The story has a few holes, but they aren't really noticeable on the first playthrough thanks to the fun characters. The performance is alright, though can dip on certain levels, if this happens don't be afraid to turn the graphics to low, the game manages to keep most of its visual fidelity thanks to the art direction even on lower settings.

All in all, really glad the series is finally on PC with a decent port and if you are new don't worry about the previous games too much, you should still be able to keep up with the story.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is worth the asking price and then some, doesn't matter whether your young or old.

Gameplay is so fun, feels great to level your weapons up and it's just so cinematic. Don't get me wrong, it's no Cyberpunk 2077 when it comes to story depth, but sometimes explosions on a Michael Bay level are all we need relax.

That said, unless you have a good PC, this is probably best played on your PS5. All the graphical settings added to the PC port are worthless if you can't actually run the game. This is also best played with a PS5 controller (I hate using controllers typically, but this just feels right). Only reasons to get this on PC over PS5 is if you have a powerful PC setup, or just don't have a PS5.\t\t\t\t\t
