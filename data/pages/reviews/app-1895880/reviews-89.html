\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*I ran the game on my AokZoe A1pro. The game ran smoothly at 20 TDP. However, the game crashed alot! The game recommended new drivers for AmD 7840, but AMD hasn't officially released new drivers yet. Crashes happened regardless at the lowest settings and highest TDP

The game itself, it was fantastic. The particle effects, beautiful settings, fluidity of the combat and platforming was on point. Many different weapons (best weapons is still in the Crack in time), overall it's a great Ratchet and Clank game.

The story was well paced and thoroughly enjoyable. I had my consternation about playing as another lombax, but I liked her character arc. Well written story, but not as funny as past ratchet games. I don't know why people thought the game was too short, it was the perfect length for me. I don't want my linear games to be too long. There's also some side missions that I didn't complete, perfect for anyone who wants to extend their playtime.

I highly recommend the game, especially ratchet and clank fans.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing a huge Ratchet fan, I have waited so long to finally be able to play this game. And everything I've heard about it since it launched on PS5 made my expectations very high. That being said, I can most certainly say that there wasn't a single time I've felt disappointed in this title.

Starting with the most obvious thing - graphics. I never thought, that of all games, Ratchet & Clank would be the best looking game I've ever played. I remember being in awe of A Crack in Time's looks. This is simply on another level. There aren't many games in which I've stopped playing just to look around and appreciate my surroundings so often. This with the lack of loading screens made it feel more like a movie than anything.
Speaking (writing?) of load times, I'm amazed how smooth the game runs. Of course playing on PC, there were few noticable hiccups, but I played on high settings, so considering that and how rarely they appeared, there's nothing I can be mad about.
One thing I haven't really heard a lot about in this game is its OST and sound effects. I enjoyed the soundtrack a lot, it is very energetic and responsive. But what I was really surprised by was SFX. The weight they add to weapons and enemies is outstanding. Anything from destroying surroundings, firing weapons, hitting enemies, collecting stuff all of it felt so satisfying.
Level design is also worth mentioning. The Future saga lacked diversity in its levels. Most games were super linear compared to the OG trilogy. Rift Apart not only brought that diversity back, it might even exceeded it. Not only are there open spaces and multiple paths per level but also you can choose what level to go to next, which I personally really like.

Story is probably the best in entire franchise. I was a bit worried in the beginning that it might get a confusing with the whole multiple dimensions stuff. I feel like it isn't the easiest topic to pull off well, but for me, Insomniac nailed it. Having multiple protagonists was also a concern to me. After all it is Ratchet & Clank. But everything was balanced so well that the only thing it did, was open many paths for future games. Although I wish the plot would be more related to previous games, expanding on what was already told... I get that Insomniac had to appeal not only to long-time fans, but to new players as well. And that they definitely did.
With that I can comfortably say that I love all returning content from previous titles; from many RaC1 enemies, QfB pirates, ToD and ACiT planets and of course everyone's favourite antagonist Nefarious. As someone who played nearly all previous games, every encounter with something familiar brought a smile to my face.

Only thing that bothers me is the game's lenght. It isn't short or anything, its just that the game is so good I didn't want it to finish. Now I can only hope for previous titles to be added on Steam, just so I can have an excuse to play trough all of them again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart if you like the previous games. If you're new to the series, this is a good place to start as well.

This is a fantastic Ratchet & Clank game. If you played any of the PS3/PS4 era games, it's a more perfect R&C game. The story is really good and wraps up nicely, and the game play is really fun. There's 20 guns, and most of them are unique and well designed. Some of the guns are just copies of other guns. That's fine, the guns they have are good.

Compared to some of the PS2 era games, Rift Apart is kinda short. I 100%'d it in 1 play-through, and then I did a Challenge Mode play-through (essentially New Game+). The Challenge Mode adds the classic bolt multiplier and unlocks Omega variants of the guns you've max leveled. The Omega versions aren't especially interesting, they're fine.\t\t\t\t\t
