\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith this review I want to address this game and also Steam and their review system.

1) The steam review system is really anything but accurate. Only "yes" or "no" is not really helpful especially with games like this one. A 1-10 system or grades would be much more helpful.

I'm a huge fan of the franchise and as the reviews and the voices of players and professionals were both overwhlmingly positive, I was kind of hyped that I can finally play it. And yes, I had fun with the game.

2) The positive aspects of the game are the following: a really good graphic and graphic design with a really atmospheric soundtrack. The voice actors make a really good job and the the new added characters fit the universe pretty good. The highligt of this game is the gameplay. A fast paced jump & gun with focus on gunfights and cinematic jump passages. Frustrating jumps/ blind jumps do not exist. It is for both Jump & run veterans and newcomers. Although newcomer will have more fun with the game, cause it is not really difficult. I played directly on the highest difficulty and for someone who played one R&C game before this should not be much of a challenge. The planets are well crafted and they differ enough so everyone of them has something unique.

Now why I say, that I don't recommend the game?

1) The story: The story is as short cutted as possible. There is no real character development, as the characters stay as they are in the end. Yes Rivet's character stays the same, only Kit's character changes, but that happens offscreen. Their bonding is so bare minimum, that if the One Piece Live action series could not reduce it further. The story follows the event of shattered dimensions, but it does not really add something to the gameplay, most passages of dimension jumping are cinematic, only two levels really rely on the change of dimensions. Also the newly added characters have not much screen time, Captain Quantum on screen is maybe 5% of the game, but he is so "important" to be rescued. He starts the last part of the game, but as the counterpart of Qwark it would have been interesting to see him in the role of the ressisstance. The strangest part of the whole game is, that Nefarious knows what they plan and where they are going, but he won't do anything to stop them. No trap, he does not go there himself, nothing. Why the imperator is not shown watchhing Dr. Nefarious place, why they don't have any interesting cutscenes together, why he tolerates the doctor and so on is not explained or missing. There are many points left out, which mirrors on the levels, there are ten levels (eleven with the planet of intro/end) with some events happening again on the same planets. The content of the game is barely worth 40$ not more.

2) The weapons: The weapons consist of staples and altered variations of these staples. A bomb, a rocket launcher, a sharp shooting gun, a shot gun, buzz blades, a shooting companion, a turret, something that shoots electricity and a blaster. The new gimmick weapons are variations of older ones. So in the end you have 20 weapons that have no highlight. They function, they make the same fun as they did 15 years + before, but they don't add something remarkable. The new mega weapon is the only one outstanding, cause it makes funny references.

3) Dr. Nefarious: The main villain is reduced to a side character, that does nothing relevant to the story. He doesn't even cause the catastrophy of the dimensions. His role is to be there. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tA big one returns for me here.

A fully fledged Ratchet and Clank game. A port that has seen some issues at first, but then again, what playstation port to PC is perfect at first? Most problems seem to be fixed at this point. The only thing I encountered, was that I could not get the Hover Boots to activate their full speed potential first time (every time mind you), instead I had to press the allocated button, let go, repeat and THEN was able to propell myself.

This game really tickles my nostalgia since I owned R&C from the very first issue on playstation 2 back in the day. This game has a lot of that olden goodness and then some more! Sprinkled in amazingly beautiful graphics (the environments of the worlds you visit, the character designs, the animations when you go from one world to another seemlessly through rifts, etc.), littered with particle effects that make this game look like one of, if not THE best visual presentation of "recent" releases.
Of course R&C is nothing without its g(ame)(un)play, and boy is it satisfying. Again, visually very striking, but the audio team also did an amazing job here!

Story wise, this is a fun ride you'll be on for some 10-12 hours, closer to 20 if you try to 100% it like I did. Do not expect plot twists that blow you off your chair like Bioshock might have done back in the day. But that's not what R&C is about, it's about fun and fast paced gameplay really! However, this game still provides with some funny and captivating moments. Secrets are also not that hard to discover since the game helps you out quite a bit on its own, I think thats just how things have evolved (not looking at you FromSoft...)

All in all, this, actual finished game on release -hard to find these days..- was a really fun experience! I think both old fans of the series and newly introduced people will definitely enjoy this game for what it is, a thrill ride that makes you gawk at your screen at the stunning visuals and make you get that YEAH! feeling when you use a combination of weapons to completely obliterate your screen full of enemies!

A must buy for seekers of adventure and lovers of high octane gameplay where there is eye candy everywhere!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm ultimately gonna recommend this game..but it just barely makes the cut.

For starters, the game is mostly excellent. A friend of mine described it as "almost like you're playing a decent Pixar movie" and I can't help myself to somewhat agree with him. The art design for almost every single character is so bright, lively and charming, the same can be said about the game's environments. The graphics and textures are gorgeous, truly complementing the game's fantastic art design. The voice acting is great too, not to mention that the sound/music design is superb. The story is great and I thought it was a fun way to keep the series feeling fresh, as well as opening the way for potential sequels.

To get into the gameplay, the combat is easily the best part. The fast and fluid movement of the gunplay is incredibly fun as well as satisfying. The weapon variety in this game is also great, giving you a lot of great tools to choose from to blast your way through waves of enemies. When youre not blasting and strafing your way through enemies as Ratchet or Rivet, youre thinking your way through some pretty unique puzzles as Clank. Overall, despite some occasional weirdness, I thought the puzzles were pretty well crafted and fun to work through. Lastly, side quests were kinda meh, not good or bad just kinda there. The challenges were somewhat fun but all the other side missions were just okay.

So why does this game, which seemingly has a lot going for it, just barely make the cut?? Well for starters, the enemy and boss variety was really poor and uninspired. It felt like I was fighting the same groups of enemies and bosses in almost every level. Sure, there are a couple of cool boss fights, particularly the ones with Dr. Nefarious. However, most of the enemies and bosses that you fight in the first mission are the same enemies and bosses that you fight in the last mission. Despite having a lot of weapons to choose from, even cool giant enemies like dinosaurs and robots tend to lose their wow factor after fighting them 10-12 times.

Next, I want to talk about performance because it was abysmal. Once again, a PS5 game has been released on PC with really poor performance and FPS. Was it as bad as The Last of Us: Part 1 on PC? God no. Was it good enough that someone should spend $60? Absolutely not. The game isnt broken, but I would hardly call it optimized. The frame rate was all over the place for most of the game, with the end of the game dipping the frame rate into the low 20s for extended periods of time. There were no stability issues to speak of, but I sure as hell thought the game was gonna crash several times.

TL;DR

Ratchet and Clank is a decent enough game overall, but these seemingly rushed out PC ports of PS5 games are making me worried. Almost every single previously-PS5 exclusive game that has come to PC has arrived in a broken or piss poor state. I think modern PCs are more than enough to handle the weight of a PS5 game, I dont hold the opinion that you need an i9 and a 4090 to run these PS5 games well. Especially if theyre labeled as Steam Deck verified, which is supposed to signal to people that it is optimized so well on Steam that you can play it on a handheld. Clearly these games need more time in the oven for optimization and QA testing on PC. All of this on top of poor enemy and boss design made the game feel more lacking than it should have.

This game was probably the most playable PS5 game when it launched and has gotten a little better, but it only reaches that playable label by razor thin margins.

Game overall: 4/5

Performance: 3/5

Overall score: 3.5/5 - Get it on sale.
