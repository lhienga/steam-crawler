-Sigh- The sad part is I like this game. I've loved the series since the very first one and I think this entry is still good, though I wouldn't claim the best. Difficulty modes are good, the script is funny but heartwarming at times. Kit and Rivet are cool, but I personally felt they were stealing the show from the duo we know and love.

What kills this game for me is the crashing. My computer's not bad, really good even. I have on High-Ultra setting and the game runs at 144FPS at 2K, which sounds great. HOWEVER, I have no idea why but any time I rift jump or a rift opens to spawn enemies it feels like im dice rolling a 20% chance to crash the game. It kills my enthusiasm to load my save, move forward ONE ROOM and a rift opens up that crashes my game, only for it to happen again after I boot it up again. It's agonizing and then i just dont want to play for the rest of the day. I seriously hope they fix this, because everything else is a 7.5-8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

INSTALL THIS GAME TO A SSD!
Most of the issues with loadtime and even performance are resolved instantly. This game was built to run on a SSD, so even if you're savvy and have a RAID 1 configuration with 7200 HDDs attached together, you will shrimply have a bad time.

Most issues related to crashing and some performance and shadow-related bugs with RTX have been patched.
Comparing with launch footage to today, it's clear that Nixxes did do rapid-response on patching the major issues that were encountered. The port was already a great port before, exceeding expectations, but the work done to address the major issues is worth noting. There are some other issues/bugs/crashing, but they are with the game itself, and not the port.

Again, this was already a great port. I did not expect 21:9 (and greater!) support. This game has to be one of the best, visually, in 21:9. It feels like the game was made for it. It doesn't feel cropped, nor crammed. If anything, the game feels crammed on 16:9. Nor did I expect >60 FPS support that didn't fundamentally break the game like the modern Spyro and Crash ports. There are some issues with higher FPS, but they seem to be visual, NOT game-breaking.

Also, I'm not one to gush super hard about graphical fidelity. But damn. This game just looks really good. There's a lot going on here that I think can only really be achieved with the bleeding-edge technology that this game utilises. It plays really good as if it's the capstone of ambition Insomniac set out to achieve when they first released Ratchet and Clank in 2002.

As a Ratchet and Clank game, well it's on the average side that you'd expect from the series. It isn't as short as Into the Nexus or Quest for Booty (thank god). But it's not quite the epic that Going Commando was. I don't think this is a negative because Going Commando did have problems with backtracking and the need to grind. But shooting for completionist is very quick and is comparable to Into the Nexus. This is why "this is a short Ratchet and Clank game" gets cited so much.

You'll probably enjoy every minute of it, though. This is just straight up gun porn and that's the point of the series. To quote the ad campaigns that were a part of my childhood, "It's time to blow sh*t up!"

Though I find myself disappointed in how lacking even the highest difficulty is. Yea, it's "fun" difficult and it feels like the game was balanced for that difficulty, but please. Bring me back some Deadlocked/Gladiator "exterminator" difficulty. It was unfair and that was the point.

Welcome to PC, Ratchet and Clank. Please be a permanent stay.\t\t\t\t\t
