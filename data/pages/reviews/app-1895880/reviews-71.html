\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a disclaimer do NOT buy this game if you are running an RTX 2060 and an i5-8400 processor. This game will run absolutely terribly with those internal parts. An RTX 4070 and an i5 12th gen will run the game buttery smooth though.

Ratchet and Clank Rift Apart is yet another PS5 game that has made it's way to PC and I couldn't be happier. Even two years out from the original release it still holds up remarkably well in most every regard. Although I do have my issues with the game, the biggest being that it feels like a glorified tech demo for the PS5 which means the game doesn't have nearly the breadth of content that it could have.

Moving on to specifics about the game I'll start off with the most notable part; Graphics and performance. As far as the visuals of the game they're absolutely stunning. The level of detail on nearly every single texture, model, and background is a visual treat which is all the sweeter when it's backed up by a rock solid frame rate. The game visually and performance wise is one of the best examples of what "next gen" can look like. Additionally the load times are fantastic, even though I don't have the most powerful PC possible loading entirely new areas only took mere second or two and that's so damn impressive.

Talking about the gameplay I think it's really fun. The majority of the weapons in the game feel good to use and all have a great impact when firing them as well as hitting enemies. The controls are buttery smooth, platforming feels precise and rarely ever has any issues on it's own. And the pace of the game is great in that it rarely ever takes a second to stop as you're pretty much on a roller coaster the entire way through.

That being said while the gameplay is fantastic the variety in enemies isn't. You'll pretty be fighting the majority of the same enemies throughout the entire game. Not only from the perspective of archetypes (small enemies, big enemies, flying enemies etc.) but literally the exact same models. It's somewhat masked by the dimension aspect of the game but I wish there was more enemy variety throughout.

Now when it comes to dimension hopping it's honestly super fun ♥♥♥♥. It's great to go into pocket dimensions for fun side challenges as well as use dimensional tears to move from one side of the arena to the other.

As for the narrative and the story I have mixed feelings no them. Although while I like Rivet and Kit as dimensional counterparts of Ratchet and Clank as well as the arc they go through I can't say I was heavily invested into the overall narrative or the stories of each individual planet. They're not bad but they don't stand out as anything more than locations for the plot to take place, they just have little personality. And the writing is probably the weakest aspect of the game for me. When it comes to the main cast they're usually alright, but it's when side characters start talking is when I have a problem. Most of the time they have a "Disney/Marvel" issue where every serious situation has to be undercut with a joke afterwards. Or sometimes they're just straight up not funny like the Morts. The only joke about them is that they're all named Mort and they act Canadian.

All that said I would still recommend this game quite a bit even with my grievances about it\t\t\t\t\t
