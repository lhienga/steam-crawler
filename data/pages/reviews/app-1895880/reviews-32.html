\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun nostalgia trip, a bit more family friendly than I remember the older games being (not that they were super mature or dark before), but rather short overall and empty of side content (unfortunate) and grinds (fine). It was nice to see the characters again, in high quality. And the new characters too.

Wouldn't recommend this for full price. 30% off $60 USD retail is... getting there, but if you really want value for your dollar beyond some nice nostalgia, then probably wait for a better sale.

Easy 100% in ~12.5 hours. (You need to start NG+ to get all the weapons, just up until you reach the weapon vendor again, very quick.)
Game takes roughly 11 hours, maybe more if you slow down, I just ran and ran all the time.

--

This game definitely gives a closer feel to the older Ratchet & Clank games than the PS4 2016 movie game. Decent nostalgia. A common sentiment people express is that the characters are softer than the old games, but it's almost been two decades since I last played so I don't really feel it all that strongly. Characters are still a bit quippy, but yeah, probably lack a little bit of bite. Pretty sure we used to be meaner to Quark and I didn't see anything like that here. A few hidden collectibles but absolutely nothing near like, clearing out the sewer pipes or the frozen planet with the serpents popping out of their little crater things. Very little side activities.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonal notes at the bottom, otherwise here are the bullet points you probably want to read:

PROS:
1. simple story, easy to follow.
2. Weapons as usual feel fun to use (adaptive triggers will take some getting used to if you're not used to this but when you do, it becomes FUN)
3. hard to get lost in a level as they're pretty straightforward
4. Collectibles aren't that hard to find (save for CraiggerBears- there's one in each planet and you just get an achievement for finding all 9 IN A SINGLE PLAYTHROUGH/SAVE FILE), but the Map-o-Matic makes the light collectible aspect fun.
5. Enemy shots (this is weird to put as a pro/nice aspect but hear me out) have some rather deceptively precise amount of leading for some of their shots. If you look for the gunner Pirate robots and their yellow bullets specifically, you'll notice what I mean by dancing around their shots for some time (easiest way to point out what I'm referring to)
6. Cold Snap. Don't sleep on this weapon, especially the ice spikes upgrade that shoot when an enemy is shattered- this can chain very quickly into a Crowd Control and/or arena wipe if used right.


CONS:
1. Enemy variety is lacking. This doesn't sound too terribly bad at a glance, but your main enemies are robot pirates, Nefarious robots, Indiginous (like planet-specific mobs), and Goons. To make things even dumber, "Bone Goons", "Undead Sand Sharks", even "undead grunthor" (of course) are the other half of the enemy varieties involved. Amoeboids return, but aren't very prominent outside of the Alien (Juice) Planet.
2. CraiggerBears. I mentioned them in the Pros of collectibles, but they are not marked on the map, EVEN IF You have the Map-O-Matic. I was hoping to get something other than an achievement for collecting all nine, but nope. Not even a Craiggerbear armor set (no bonuses), Craiggerbear grenades mod (or cheat), or Craiggerbear Hammer for Rivet (or Ratchet if using Rivet's Hammer cheat).
3. Crashes. This game seems to be notorious with crashing, even with high end machines (I'm using an RTX 4060 OC, i7-13th gen, and 32GB RAM and I noticed a few crashes especially in Torren IV if I just speed past the initial enemies into the Junk Bazaar or so, or if I tried to pin the Pocket Dimension on my map with the Ray Tracing settings on) as reported in the Steam Forums for this game.
4. Glitch. There's nothing wrong with her specifically other than how few her stages are in the game (two are optional as they only provide access to Gold Bolts for Ratchet). That said, Glitch feels like an afterthought in terms of story and is just used to provide a break in between the gun shooting action segments. Should you do the two optional levels, you get to see a nice confidence boost in Glitch as she celebrates her victory, but that's where Glitch's place in the game ends. (tl;dr: "it's more that Glitch has too few sections and is more of a sidelined character and gameplay shift)
5. Raritanium "scarcity" and the Cell upgrade system. This is a two-parter as they fall into the same idea for upgrading weapons. Throughout your gameplay, you'll come across Raritanium deposits here and there and typically you'll only have enough to buy the specific upgrades you *need* to pick and choose your desired cells to fill in and spend Raritanium on. The "scarcity" is shattered when you can farm Raritanium in select locations super quickly and realize they respawn when you die. As a very good and prominent example, Zurkie's pocket dimension starts with 3 Raritanium right next to your spawn. Smack those 3, fall down into death, respawn, hit the raritanium, fall down, rinse and repeat for maybe an hour and you'll have all the Raritanium you'll probably EVER need in that playthrough or save file. 10 minutes of doing that got me about 100 raritanium and I never had to worry about affording upgrades as they became available to me. 30 minutes would have made sure I didn't need to grab any more along the way unless I was leveling up entirely new weapons. If you do this, your bottleneck would be weapon Levels, which case there's no getting around using them and leveling them up like normal (I used the 1st challenge Bronze cup in Zurkie's to level up my weapons early game and upgrade them with raritanium as needed)
6. Mini-Bosses. Compared to the main bosses of the game like Nefarious robot in the tutorial prologue or the Seekerpede in Sargasso, I've only ever *remembered* the "Kong Navel Laser Beam" hulking ones- you cannot and will not miss them. I didn't mind them the first two times, but they are in virtually every single planet, like "really? this is getting annoying" feeling every time I see them. There are Grunthors in Sargasso but I tend to welcome them when I see them. Actually I don't even remember many other Mini-Bosses besides Grunthors and Kong Bots. and that's a shame.
7. Zurkie's Battleplex. I loved the challenge fights, I am more disappointed that there are literally just 15 challenges (in groups of 3) that are story-gated. A lot of them feel same-y barring the 3 weapon-restricted ones (Warmonger, Enforcer, and HeadHunter) which were quite a challenge, especially if said weapons have minimal upgrades/levels. The Mangler (an invincible environmental hazard gunning for you) appears in two of the 15 challenges, Undead Grunthor (Sue) is the big boss of the Bronze challenges, and a tag-team of Seekerpede (Scolo) + Undead Grunthor (Sue) is the final challenge of the Gold battles. I just described the interesting half of the Battleplex stuff (the other half are unremarkable in honesty), and i preferred the variety of challenges in past games' arenas (especially Ratchet & Clank 2: Going Commando's Megacorp Games arenas with the Impossible Challenge or Ratchet & Clank 3's Qwarktastic challenge (you'll never guess who is credited as the first victor)).

Edit: 8: Combo multiplier in my opinion makes you able to rack up a lot of bolts way too quickly and a very low bolt cap of 10 million bolts makes it so that you just need a little over half of that to afford *ALL* the Omega weapons on New Game Plus, and ammo will be barely an issue to spend your bolts on.


Runtime is about 20 hours casually if just sprinting from start to finish without doing small tasks on the side like hunting gold bolts or leveling up weapons (1st playthrough), your mileage may vary.

Narratively, this game is the latest (as of posting this review) in the story of Ratchet & Clank games over the years- from the original PS2 quadrilogy (1, 2, 3, and Deadlocked), to the PS3 "Future" trilogy. It's more or less "another adventure the iconic duo go through" that begins after the plot points of the PS3 titles regarding "last lombax" mystery and where they went. However, this story does not continue through the PS3's narrative story to its conclusion- it feels more like a filler episode (NARRATIVELY SPEAKING to the wider Lombax story) than a continuation of what and where the Lombaxes went, though you can listen to the Sevali Lorbs for snippets of lore like that.
