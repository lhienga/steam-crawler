Very positively surprised

As someone who has been a fan of Ratchet and Clank ever since the first PS2 games, I was a bit worried, if Rift Apart could capture the essence of what’s so great about these games. Especially since the PS4 remake made a few questionable decisions, to say the least. I very much went into this game with a sense of caution and I was extremely surprised at how good it actually is.
Rift Apart feels like such a love letter to the entire franchise and it builds upon the best features of it. From big things like continuing the plot of the future saga in a satisfying way, to tiny details like helmets being automatically taken off in cutscenes to allow you to see the Ratchet’s and Rivet’s (wonderfully animated) facial expressions.

For those that are worried about the game’s performance, let me say, so was I . With Rift Apart being originally a PS5 game, I was terribly afraid that the performance would, at worst, make it unplayable on my gaming laptop, but that luckily wasn’t the case. There were a few technical hiccups here and there, mostly sound delays/bugs/glitches in combat when a lot was going on on-screen, a tiny fps drop here and there, but for the most part, it ran surprisingly smoothly. The PS5 can impress with no loading times at all, which isn’t really something the PC port proved capable of, but to me, it was more of a fun little extra to spot where they hid the loading zones than something that bothered me.

About the actual game:
-\tWeapon variety and designs are amazing, all of them felt interesting and unique to play with
-\tCharacters are dynamically animated and written with a certain level of depth that actually makes them feel like they have personality
-\tLevels felt a bit shorter than in other games, but there is certainly enough happening to make each one memorable and fun
-\tMini-Games weren’t super amazing to me, but didn’t impact the game in a bad way either
-\tSide quests aren’t super complex, but give you more reason to explore optional areas of a planet, similar to some of the older games
-\tIn terms of Gameplay and flow of the controls, this might just be the game that feels the most satisfying to play in the entire series.

A little heads-up though: while the game is surely also fun for a player who has never touched a Ratchet and Clank game in their life, I would very much recommend either playing the future games (Tools of Destruction, A Crack in Time and Into the Nexus) or at least watch a story summary before getting into this one, because the plot of Rift Apart absolutely assumes that you go into this with the knowledge of what happened in past games.

While the game is not terribly long to complete 100%, it definitely feels like a fully finished and lovingly-crafted experience that is worth your time. So yeah, it really is a very good game! :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm just glad the Ratchet & Clank saga finally exist on PC.
Is this one the best of the series? No.
It's not the worst, but the 5th or 6th worst (for a ~14 games saga, it's a low tier game).

Gameplay wise, it's a fun game with the classic since "A Crack In Time" : Hoverboots, some variety in the planets, some are large with secondary objectives, objects to find, others are more directive, with a bit of story. These planets are a bit too big, I miss the precisions of the PS2/PS3 levels.


Graphically it's stunning (it was on PS5, on my PC I will need to upgrade to last gen cards to enjoy RTX, etc, I will redo this game after this upgrade). I will said that it lack his own identity, a lot of presets are from the one from PS4, which I didn't enjoyed very much.

And where it get's worst, it's story wise. I really didn't like to get to fight Dr Nefarious, AGAIN, for the 4th time (RAC3, ACIT, PS4 and now this one).
I liked him a lot the first 2 times but after Drek was replaced on PS4 by Nefarious, I miss new bad guys (same goes for the Thug 4 Less who were fun basic ennemies from RAC2).

But the real problem is coming from the characters. They are very annoying, because they are very naive "too nice". No humor like before, no one is sometimes "not nice", they are all pure goodness reincarnate.

Ratchet is a mechanics, such as Rivet, and she doesn't fix Clank's arm for the whole game, only Ratchet get to do that in the end, and Clank got a yellow arm to match Rivet one? Why? It's a robot and he has lost parts before.

Honestly they are just flat and not interesting and I don't want to just "save my friends", RAC had interesting plots before, TJ Fixman was great during the PS3 era, I don't understand why it's becoming so flat.

So yeah, I recommend this game, because I will always recommend a Ratchet & Clank game, specially the first one on Steam. It was well ported aswell. It's just not the best game ever made and I hope for a brigther future.

Also, please dev, don't se*uallize Rivet, I've seen interviews from female dev who were forced to fight to NOT have Rivet more se*uallize, Ratchet is not, don't do it on his feminine counterpart.\t\t\t\t\t
