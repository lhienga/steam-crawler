\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying that I saved the game and paused just to write this review. I am new to the PC world (less than 1 year) and due to my OTR Truck driving, I play on a Ryzen 9 laptop. Prior to the release of the xbox 360 era, I was a big PS2 player. I have played just about every Ratchet and Clank excluding the PSP exclusive. So when I seen that they ported a version to PC I was a bit skeptical. Let me finish my Intro to this review by stating that if you are questioning spending the money to invest in this game, It is 110% worth every penny as it lives up to my expectations for the Insomniac Team. Some may not agree, but in my personal opinion even having played this game, Going commando and Up your arsenal will always and forever be master pieces deadlocked in time. See what i did there. They are a relic, and something to be cherished as many new released games beyond the PS2 era have fallen short of expectations with new age technology. That all being said this game is one of my new favorites thus far, and although it is a single player game, it is something that you can play through numerous times and it never gets old, just as you could all previous Ratchet and Clank titles.

Let me start with the bad seeing as there is only one thing I have found to be some what tedious. I personally myself enjoy a great story and this series has never failed to deliver. However, the only bad thing I have noticed is the outrageous amounts of cut scenes, being in about every 5-15 minutes of game play. I specifically remember a time where you could run through an entire planet with 2-3 cut scenes per planet, being just enough to follow a great story but not so over whelming that you feel the need to skip through early game play just begin. This all being said as-well, I am still fairly early in the game, so this could change, but I am 2-3 hours in and I have spent just as much time watching as I have been playing thus far.

Now for all of the good. I can honestly say the graphics will not let you down. I play on a laptop running AMD RX 6700s and while yes, some of the reviews will reflect poorly on the games initial quality, I advise playing around with the settings until you find what works best for your set up. I am running 1600 x 1200 resolution at 45 fps with all settings to high. On a built rig, you could easily run higher. But even with the way I currently have things set up I am experiencing no glitching, hot screening, and little to no input lag within the game from scene to scene or maneuvering. The in game controls are as originally set up, however I play on an xbox controller blue toothed to my laptop, and It is taking some getting used to, seeing as my muscle memory still remembers the general buttons however there are some new added maneuvers and in game mechanics. Very well thought out. Very vibrant colors per usual of the Insomniac Games team. Just as you would expect in terms of creativity with the weapon system, even giving a new (new to me) upgrade system, to allow for more goals within the game.

Again, It is everything you would expect of a Ratchet and Clank game and while i may say that I find it annoying to watch cut scenes (that you can skip through btw), I am early in the game and even being said, I would not allow that to deter myself were I to be the one reading this, from investing in such a wonderfully made game. Also, again...the cut scenes may pan out thinner once further into the game. Overall I find it worth every penny and would buy again. Hope all of my fellow Insomniac fans are doing well, I will probably surrender sleep to attempt to do a 1-2 first run through. Happy gaming.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm only about 45% through the game atm, but I'd give it 4.7 out of 5. It has a few flaws here and there, but the systems it has in place easily outweigh any cons. Combat is fluid, dodging is reactive (if not sometimes limiting), and the sprint having a building speed mechanic is A++. The story line has some semblance of seriousness with heavy silliness; Because of this, character development is unexpected yet quite good. It's odd to say that this game has some of the most human interactions of any game I've played in the passed decade considering many of them come from robots..

This game also does a great job of bouncing between play styles without feeling like a break in game play. 'Glitch' is one of my favorite characters based only off of small bits of dialogue during intermissions and her understanding/growth through the mini-games she's in. The developers clearly understood what they wanted for her and did a great job of putting her passive thoughts into words, giving her amazing character development despite very low screen time.

I can't say this game is a masterpiece based on my current play time, but I can easily say it is a standard that all game developers should strive for. Even in the wake of Baldur's Gate 3, I think this a gem release (among PC).\t\t\t\t\t
