\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIntroduction:
The R&C franchise was one of the very first games I ever played as a kid back on PS2 with RC2 being my favourite and Deadlocked being a close second. So when I saw that a R&C game came out on PC I immediately bought it. I will try to put aside nostalgia and love for the series aside, however I will be making comparisons between this game and the OG quadrilogy.

The Good:
> Better than the reboot - enough said.
> Graphics in this game are incredible - every planet was well detailed and breathtaking to explore.
> Great weapons - the weapons were pretty decent and stick to the same theme of 'creative, fun and crazy' theme that has made the franchise special.
> Solid story and writing - jokes were funny, story was actually decent. I say this as RC2's writing was very rushed with, arguably, the most important character Angela Cross of the game being dumped in future instalments. Rivet and Kit make fine additions to the roster and having new protagonists is just what the series needed seeing as their original audience has grown up and Ratchet and Clank's stories are pretty much concluded. It was also nice seeing heaps of nods to previous games of the series.
> The cut scenes - at times this game seemed more like a movie than an actual game. There was a huge emphasis on making cut scenes in this game feel alive and more emotive.
> True to the franchise gameplay - straight up third person shooter. Gameplay felt massively improved with movement and the new arsenal of weapons playing in well into each other. It is always mad satisfying playing with the new weapons. The addition of the rifts is pretty cool, kinda blows you away the first time with how whole different levels were kinda just loaded in an instant.
> Customability - I love when games give you the option to change how your dude looks.

The Bad
> The soundtrack - don't get me wrong, the music during the cut scenes and tense moments were well-placed but there was a lack of atmospheric music. There would be times where I'd be sitting maybe 5 minutes in silence. It came off a bit strange to me as RC2 would have the planet's music playing constantly, no matter the situation - it was constantly looped but was never tiring to listen to. RC2 had nice experimental electronic music that always fitted the planet you were on. I have a feeling that with the new cinematic-story driven element of the game, that they would cut down on the normal gameplay music. Not once during the game was I taken away by the soundtrack which is sad :(
> The length - it took me maybe 10-15 hours to fully complete the game. The game felt somewhat short with only 9 planets to explore, albeit very well detailed. To put that in perspective, RC2 had 17 planets, with extra dogfighting locations, 2 arenas, 2 hoverbike races and 2 bigger maps to find and collect some gems. Not to mention that game was developed in (I think) under a year! I'm not sure whether you would place this in the goods or bads list but all I know is that after completing the game, I was left wanting more.
> Lack of enemy variety - In RC and RC2 each planet would have it's own set of unique enemies (even though they would all follow the same archetype) which made every planet much more memorable. However, in this instalment, there are 3 main factions of enemies and the sprinkled in random enemies from previous games.
> Not challenging enough - There is way too much padding in this game, even on the hardest difficulty. This might be due to the weapons being crazy powerful or just the overall difficulty of the game. In RC2 there were noticeable difficulty spikes throughout the game in which the player would have to adapt and learn to conquer. Now it feels like the game learns and adapts for you which should not be the case especially on the hardest difficulty. The toughest segment of the game was the 'kill goons with shotgun' challenge in the arena, which felt too out of place for a tier 1 challenge.

Conclusion
I love this game despite my gripes. I feel as if with this game the series is on a good direction moving forward. The gameplay is great, possibly more than great, the story is solid, the graphics are incredible and the cut scenes had a whole new layer of character to the characters and the world as a whole. However, I'd love to see some more creative and experimental music that fit the planets better, as well as a longer-length game.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t
