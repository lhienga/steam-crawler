\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up with Ratchet and Clank, (mainly the PS2 games) and this does not feel like a Ratchet and Clank game. yes, the graphics are pretty amazing, the gameplay is smooth (until you crash or die from speeding into a ledge the wrong way), even the OST is pretty good. but everything else is..highly disappointing. and not what i have come to expect from Insomniac.

The weapon design is awful, they look like over sized dollar store dart guns (The Migraine), and the sounds for them are even worse, ESPECIALLY the Blast Pistol (thanks for dropping the combuster though). they all lack FEELING. there's not even a cool animation for equipping one anymore, apart from the Blast Pistol. with an arsenal of 20, and with weapons like Cold Snap, Drillhound, Mr. Fungi, Lightning Rod and worst of all in my opinion, Topiary Sprinkler (Mini Turrets would be a welcome replacement for this joke of a weapon), your selection feels even more limited and 'family friendly'. The R.Y.N.O. 8 most certainly does NOT Rip You a New One, with 7 shots max and very poor damage (if any at all) on bosses, not to mention the tinnitus from using it. for being the universes most powerful weapon, you get it for FREE after getting just a few infobots that are a breeze to obtain. although, the references that come from it are pretty great. and the alternate firing modes for the weapons (not present on PC) are an amazing touch.

The enemies are all copy and paste, nor do they look threatening with their overly exaggerated proportions (Thugs/Goons and Monks having roided torsos and pencil thin legs), and there are no unique mini bosses, as they are also copy paste. the Clank puzzles do the job but controls get taken away too often just to show you obvious information even after NG+ and turning off tutorials (using buttons and unlocking the door). but the Glitch mini games are terrible, she doesn't stop whining, it ruins the experience. Speaking of which, NOBODY SHUTS THE HELL UP! If you can have two long clips of dialog simultaneously playing over each other, simply because you're progressing faster than the game expects, then there is more than too much talking. especially to themselves to push hints in your face.

the controls for Trudi are absolutely appalling and clearly rushed, aim and steer being tied on one analog stick. The Arena is pretty weak, with only 15 challenges total and reused bosses. you only get to explore 10 planets. all of which have plenty of invisible walls and bugged surfaces ready damage/instakill you with just one touch. and optional pathways being very short, only ever leading to a Gold Bolt, a R.Y.N.O. Infobot or Armor. all the planets, while looking great, all feel the same; boring. Enemies don't even respawn after returning to planets.

ohhh and the missed opportunities, the potential this game had. Giant Kit should have been part of the controllable gameplay, not to mention we haven't seen Giant Clank in so long. bringing back drivable vehicles with weapons that control smoothly. Controllable turrets. Swing Shot paths that require timing and thought. True underwater segments that include weaving between mines, racing the timer before the path is blocked, and bumping bomb crates for some extra bolts. maybe 25+ weapons with some returning weapons because current weapon creativeness is obviously running low (examples: Rift Inducer, Suck Cannon, Alpha Disruptor, Razor Claws, Predator Launcher, Plasma Whip, Spiral of Death). a returning Insomniac Museum. more dogfights using your ship in space. and some quality of life things like a customizable quickselect, water that truly reacts with impacts (R&C 1 had this in 2002. with an amazing inside look on experimental water wave physics featured in the insomniac museum in R&C Going Commando). and a Map-O-Matic that highlights
secret areas and isn't just a map hack that pinpoints collectibles.

I bet if insomniac was given all the time they needed, with no censorship, we may have gotten a polished masterpiece, with real humor, and galactic badassery. but Sony pushed for this to be less "kickin' @$$ one planet at a time" and more "baby's first furry platform shooter." with cookie cutter characters being no longer in line with their already established personalities, more continuity errors that are pilling up, and gameplay that's so severely linear and easy that replaying the game feels mind numbing. This series once had depth, jokes, amazing level design and challenge (plus commentary on capitalism and consumerism). creating a cartoony, but believable approach, and it worked. all with ass kickin' soundtracks composed by David Bergeaud, that has become my inspiration for music creation later in life. these games weren't just for kids, they were games for everyone at any age to enjoy and come back to. To see Insomniac lose what this series was and meant, just to make it more appealing to children alone and play it painfully safe.. it almost kills my hope of ever seeing a true Ratchet and Clank game again. I wont stop believing, but something has to change.

However, all of that being said, with 55 hours back to back, I did enjoy playing. And looking into the history of the series development, and Insomniac Games, the company has gone far, and despite the issues I have with this game, I still have a lot of respect for Insomniac, and only hope for their success, and of course the success of this series.

PS. Please bring back David Bergeaud. Movie like scores are good and all, but you hit gold with him. he made the planets come to life, and harder to forget, because EVERY track of his matches the level. and still, without him the game is missing something BIG. I remember about 7 seconds each of 3 tracks in this game. Bringing him back would not only help liven up the series again, but could influence countless children to make amazing music that stands out when they get older. AND be a treat for returning players.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a R&C fan for decades, patiently waiting for the day one of the games might be playable on PC, I am incredibly happy for this. Seriously, thank you whoever made the call to make this port happen.

There are a few issues with the port, a few things don't function properly above 60FPS, but nothing game breaking and nothing that can't be patched in the future. Otherwise, the detailed graphics settings, uncapped framerate, and the option to play on a controller anyway should someone desire to makes this the best and most customizable R&C experience yet.

The precision of mouse aiming and direct weapon keybinds elevates the TPS combat to a whole new level of mastery for the series, and the new additions and convenience improvements to the moveset really brings everything up to date with modern standards. I have been having an absolute blast with this game, and I'm well into half a dozen save files now. I can wholeheartedly recommend this game to just about everyone.

I think Rift Apart is the perfect entry point for people new to the series, and a love letter to older fans. It's true what they say: it's Doom Eternal for kids, and the visuals and animation quality compete with the best animated movies out there.

My only problem is.. I feel like I used up my one wish for life, and I can only hope that if more R&C is to come, it will follow Rift Apart to the PC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn a technical side, I couldn't have been happier with the release of this port, SEI deserves credit for it. You're given an expansive list of graphics options to tune to your liking, and it has seamless graphics upscaling so that this game can be played on lower spec hardware. Heck, I have absolutely no issue playing this on my Steam Deck or my 6 year old computer on high graphics settings. The gameplay experience is just as good as on a PS5.

I'm really glad Ratchet and Clank is continuing on at this day and age and I've always loved how unique the platforming mechanics are throughout the series while staying true to its old school combat system. I've been a long time fan of the series and I'm happy to have played through this game, and so happy that the Ratchet and Clank franchise is still continuing to this day.

The only cons I have are difficulty scaling (too hard or too easy, even on higher or lower difficulties), but I imagine it's hard to balance with the combat system. The other is gameplay bugs, but there isn't any Ratchet game without one! They don't break your game or your save file thankfully.\t\t\t\t\t
