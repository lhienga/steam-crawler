\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Combat is pretty fun
- Good graphics
- The bad guy is british
- The thingy with guns having more options when you pull the trigger halfway is pretty cool. Works even with an xbox controller but I heard it's even better if you have a ps5 controller
- Finding upgrade materials for your guns is much more fun than the simple combat xp in the older games

Cons:
- Buggy. At one point I had to use speedrunning tactics to skip a gamebreaking bug. A lot of other smaller stuff like suddenly not being to change weapons before restarting from a checkpoint, getting stuck in the ground or walls etc.
- Very low enemy variety, which is even more dissapointing considering this is a 10 to 12 hour game to complete and only 1 or 2 hours more to 100%
- This is up to personal preference, but I would much rather prefer a larger amount of linear missions compared to the very small amount of bigger levels this game has.
- The story is a slop of tropes thrown into a blender. About what you would expect for a game like, which makes it less bad, but you won't be surprised by anything this game throws at you when it comes to writing.
- Nothing special when it comes to music. Normally I wouldn't expect much, but the older games had some bangers.
- Adding to the previous point, this game doesn't have as much of a funky feeling as the older games. Even ignoring nostalgia, the dialogue is tamer, the characters are less wacky and as previously mentioned I can't remember even a single memorable track of music.

This would be a mixed review if Steam would allow them. Also keep in mind I was having a 9/10 experience for the first half of the game. It falls of pretty badly towards the end in my opinion, but doesn't completely ruin the whole experience. I would recommend buying this at half price.\t\t\t\t\t
