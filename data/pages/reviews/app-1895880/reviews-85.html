\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was a lot less fun to play than the ps4 one; there were far fewer puzzles, and the few that exist are trivially easy, taking longer to run around and complete than to figure out. The puzzle style's minimal potential wasn't even met by the designers, so much more could have been done. The combat loop, while fun & challenging at first, quickly gets repetitive. I played the first 2 worlds entirely with melee on max difficulty and only died 10-15 times. After that, the hp bars got tankier and the damage increased, so while still possible the additional time it would take wasn't worthwhile. There are also much fewer gimmicks; the previous game had worlds dedicated to mechanics like swimming, jetpacking, sneaking around guards, etc. This one has... a couple rails and some wall running with only a few uses. Platforming was much easier and less rewarding. Finally, the game needed a lot more testing and bugfixes. I have died more times to getting stuck inside of hitboxes than from the actual gameplay. There are also plenty of locations where the game explicitly claims that you need to utilize an item (like speedles) to succeed, but it is perfectly possible to just jump across the gap using the excessive movement options they provided.

I did like the increased mobility provided to get across worlds more quickly like speedles and hover boots. Weapons were somewhat unique, and the storyline & dialogue (while cliche) wasn't bad.\t\t\t\t\t
