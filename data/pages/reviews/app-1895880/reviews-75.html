\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost everything about this game is nearly perfect. This game is now one of my all-time favorite games, and it is also the most technically excellent PC game I have ever played.

- The platforming is extremely technically well done; it's not hard to jump to pretty much exactly where you want to (which may seem like a given, but many platforming games screw this up). The story is fine and doesn't get in the way of the gameplay.

- I have an AMD 5950X and an Nvidia RTX 3090, and this game runs wonderfully in native 4K60 HDR, with no upscaling. And the game is BEAUTIFUL. And I have virtually no stutter or frame pacing issues.

- There is exploring, but not so much it gets boring. There's a little bit of "open-worldiness", but again not too much. There are lots of collectibles, but they're not excessively difficult, and they actually add cool features to the game (such as changing skins of weapons, adding new graphical modes, and minor gameplay tweaks).

- The auto-save system is essentially perfect, and I almost never have to think about saves.

- The game plays much better on the Steam Deck than I'd expect (just set the graphics down one notch from the default). The Steam Deck version also has gyro aiming enabled by default, however I disabled this as I don't like gyro aiming. Still, it's neat that this is supported.

- Accessibility is great. There are 5 difficulty modes, and it's perfect for people of any skill level. Knock the difficulty down and even a kid could play it and have a really good time.

- Glitches and graphical problems are very minimal. Other than noted below, I've had fewer issues than probably any other AAA game ever.

- Controller rumble/haptic feedback is fantastic, and it has multiple modes if you don't like the intensity. The highest setting will eat through batteries, but I love it; if controller battery life is important to you, just turn down the setting.

On to the very few negative things:
(UPDATE to this section: the game has been fixed to support partial trigger pulls, however on Steam Deck, there is still no support for partial trigger pulls. You can go into Steam Input and see that "soft pull" isn't mapped, and there is no command to map it to other than "Fire". Additionally, one of the weapons has 3 states: full pull, soft pull, and even softer pull, so even if there was a Steam Input command to map to "soft pull", I don't think this would fully fix it.)

- There is an annoying one, and it's analog triggers. Out of the box, Xbox controller triggers are not analog. And I haven't found any way to get analog triggers to work on the Steam Deck. It doesn't *really* affect the gameplay much, but it's frustrating. The game's controller support has integration into Steam Input, which I always love to see, however you have to disable Steam Input in order for Xbox controller triggers to be recognized as analog. Analog triggers are not strictly necessary, and I played the game for a while before even noticing. But if you want the ability to pull the trigger part way for the few weapons where that makes a (small) difference, you'll need to disable Steam Input (which doesn't seem to work on the Steam Deck). Additionally, the threshold for "half way" is very narrow--it's pretty hard to pull the trigger just "half way", and the game doesn't have a way to adjust that threshold (and since Steam Input must be disabled for this, you can't adjust it in Steam either, and since my Xbox controller isn't a Pro controller, the Xbox Accessories app also doesn't let me change this).

- There is no rumble/haptic feedback on Steam Deck :(

All in all, I LOVE this game and would recommend it to anyone.\t\t\t\t\t
