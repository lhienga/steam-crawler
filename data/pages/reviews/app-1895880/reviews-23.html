\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I enjoyed the gameplay, I really can't fully recommend this game in it's current state (especially at full price) with how buggy the game is. The performance was iffy for me, textures looked bad in some areas and I'd get some really noticeable framerate stutter in some scenes. I don't know if it's my hardware (RX 6650XT + Ryzen 5 5600) or the game not being optimised properly, but it was 100% something that was really off-putting that I had to mess around a ridiculous amount to get it to run properly when normally with other games it runs fine.

There's a lot of moments in the game where I'd just randomly clip into walls or the ground and be stuck and I'd forced to quit the game and resume. It's absolutely infuriating and they need to be patched ASAP.

If you're considering getting this game, either get it when it's on sale or wait for further updates and patches. It's not the smoothest PC port and it's a shame because it's a solid game on it's own.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start off I will say that I recommend this game. However that recommendation comes with quite a few caveats so I will list them as such.

This game while fun and having the game play feel of Ratchet and Clank it's story does not feel as such. It's missing much of the tongue in cheek critique of mega corporations from the first set of games and the new characters are overall not that interesting.

While the designs are visually appealing, especially Rivet and the new environments, there is no real meat to their stories and arcs. Rivet and Kit's arc parallels Ratchet and Clank's from the first game but does not go much more beyond that. Otherwise the story itself is predictable if serviceable and i have no real complaints and no real praises for it.

As for the length of the game it's rather short. Veterans will find themselves 100%ing the game in roughly 12-14 hours and while the challenge mode is available much like previous titles it wont hold the attention of many. This is not too unusual however as this is a similar length to said titles many will find that $60 is a bit steep for such a short game.

Now for my personal problems.
There are quite a few bugs in this game, both graphical and gameplay interrupting. While the graphical were mostly clipping issues with the various armor i did have a few where an object flickered and jittered around.
The bugs that people will care more about are the interrupting bugs though and I will describe them as such. I had 4 deaths where my character either fell through the floor or had their model clip to the point where it hit a death flag and died. And I had 2 points where some form of preset animation was supposed to play and it never happened. Most notably was in my first 30 min when the door to the train station is supposed to open and it never did. in both occasions i had to die to redo the section for it to work correctly.
These bugs while annoying were not a huge hurdle or any real setback as the checkpoints are plentiful and forgiving.

While this may seem like I did not like the game I did have fun with it and will be replaying it on challenge mode I would recommend getting it on sale.

And if anyone is still reading this DO NOT PLAY ON RESISTANCE LEADER, PLAY ON RENEGADE LEGEND.
While this is partly because I find it more enjoyable its also because Resistance Leader is actually harder. The enemies are only slightly less aggressive and healthy than Renegade Legend and there is significantly less health pickups.\t\t\t\t\t
