\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe hours I have logged with this game should be a testament to how enjoyable I found it--but instead, they are a reflection of how much I wanted to enjoy this game and how much troubleshooting I attempted in that effort.

First of all, the game is fun. The gameplay and worlds brought back fond memories of playing the other Ratchet & Clank games as a kid. It's the kind of game you want to play again to get everything and reexperience anew. And indeed, that is what I've done across several playthroughs.

The problem emerges when one considers the fact that a single 20-hour playthrough may require a time investment many times that just to make the game work properly. This game is RIDDLED with various bugs, from DirectStorage to Ray Tracing to texture issues. I was able to work around many of these bugs (again, with many hours of troubleshooting because I thought the game was worth the trouble), and as mentioned, enjoyed the game in fragmented pieces when it worked.

However, it seems there is simply no further desire to address these issues, as every new Windows update, Nvidia driver, etc. causes a new issue, most of which cause a full system shutdown. This happens, as other reviewers have noted, on a very high-end machine (i9-13900K processor, RTX 4090 GPU, 32 GB DDR5 RAM). Such issues have persisted, gotten worse, and have since become seemingly immune to all troubleshooting efforts, rendering the game essentially useless and unable to be played for more than 10 minutes at a time.

Again, what a great game this was back when it worked.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDO NOT BUY THIS GAME FOR FULL PRICE!

I really enjoyed this game and have almost gotten all of the achievements for it at 16 hours of gameplay (most were spent replaying trials and grinding for upgrade materials in the arena).

The game is fun and looks amazing graphically, but it is extremely buggy and pretty short. While trying to explore I would randomly die from jumping on a rock and my character not being able to land flatly on it, or would cruise around on the hover boots to also just randomly die from literally nothing. There's just random spots where you die for no reason what so ever.

Another bug I had was whenever I would earn a new piece of armor, the character would stand there showing off their outfit, without showing the new armor, which isn't a big deal or game breaking but by the 30th time and my character acts like they got something new and does an animation to show it off, it just seems odd and reminds me of the bugs.

Story wise, its pretty meh and the characters are very shallow. For a game called Ratchet and Clank you spend more time playing as the new character Rivet along with Kit, and there is an attempt to have some depth between the two but it feels pretty shallow, Ratchet and Clank's interactions are extremely shallow and almost non existent.

Another thing that has gotten a little annoying as of recent Ratchet and Clank games is the constant chatter in your ears from the villain screaming nonsense, Zurkon Jr repeating the same 3 lines in any arena challenge, it's almost like the attempt at humor in this game is just to be loud and zainy, I get that it's a kids game but it just seems like this aspect has been cranked up to 11 in the recent games.

Enough with the bad, some good things about this game is that it does look amazing, especially after you turn off the motion blur and if your computer can run the higher graphics. Something else that is cool is that you can decide what armor both Ratchet and Rivet wear and I was very happy to sport the Deadlock armor for the entire play through. There are some cool little Easter egg references to other games like sly cooper.

The actual game play isn't much of a challenge just some strafing and jumping to avoid damage, the occasional actual dodge. Most of the guns are fun to use, some much more than others but I would say once I had the whole arsenal of weapons to max level, I mostly used 3-5 guns out of 20 or so weapons.

There are some puzzles in the game but nothing too complex and not a whole lot of them, it certainly feels like a little mini game made to breakup the run and gun. This being said, the mini game puzzles aren't bad, they're just far from best part of the game, I actually enjoyed the mini game of playing as this tiny hacker robot that would destroy computer viruses and the wall crawling mechanics were more memorable than the Clank Puzzles.

Overall, this game is a lot of fun and I enjoyed it. It is aimed more towards a younger audience so it's easy for people who are new to video games to play. For more of a challenge turn up the difficulty to highest or 2nd highest. I would like to restate that it isn't worth the base price that is currently $60, but is totally worth it if the game is one sale for $20-$30, especially for how many bugs were in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA superb game that I highly recommend buying whether on sale or not.

If you have not played any other ratchet and clank games then this is an absolute must play. If you have played some of the previous titles then you might find some criticisms, but, overall the game is a huge step up from its predecessors and should not be missed.

A superb game series that I am so glad is finally on PC. Ratchet and Clank is an old game series which is why it has a unique feel in the current market. In summary, it isnt long and grindy, it plays with the fun and simplicity of a platformer from 20 years ago but has all of the technological improvements of the last 20 years.

I played with a controller and it was great, I cannot comment on mouse and keyboard but I cannot imagine them working well.\t\t\t\t\t
