\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay here felt exactly like playing a standard Ratchet & Clank game. I hope you understand, I mean this in the best way possible. The R&C gameplay formula has been more or less perfected since ~Up Your Arsenal, and I see very little reason to improve upon perfection.

In terms of graphics, I mean, the game runs great & Rift Apart has the latest graphics options that a person would want from the time it was released. All of the standard bells & whistles you'd want out of a PC game - including an unlocked frame rate & the all-important ray tracing options, as well as other buzzwords you'd want. Additionally, R&C:Rift Apart is bright & colorful, which is ALWAYS important in platforming games. I can deal with grey & brown color palettes in more serious shooter games like Resident Evil 4 & Fallout: New Vegas, but that kind of thing would look BAD in a platformer. Thankfully, the developers at Insomniac have understood this for decades now, and it should come as very little surprise that Ratchet & Clank is very pretty to look at.

In terms of story, again, it's the standard R&C fare, but I cannot stress that this isn't a bad thing. The humor is cheeky, the characters are lovable, Clank makes a great partner to Ratchet as always and the newcomer duo are pretty great together too. Standard though it may have been, I liked what I saw in terms of story, so not much else to say there.

Finally, there are the Achievements. I still like getting achievements in games & especially on my favorite platform Steam in particular. If you're looking to 100% all of the achievements in this game, trust me, you will. There seems to be a general rule that "kiddie platformers" must be easy to complete FOR A KID because somewhere there's going to be a child out there who will one day cherish the memory of THIS game being the 1st game that child ever 100% completed. (See also: Spongebob: Battle For Bikini Bottom: Rehydrated. I had no problem beating that game as an adult when it released on Steam a while back.) Yes, it is totally okay for adults to like & play so-called "kiddie platformers" and in fact I'm an adult who prefers the genre or style of gameplay over most others, but the fact is, although we can play whatever games we want without judgment these days, at the end of the day, R&C is made to be, at the very least, kid friendly & marketed towards a younger generation. There's nothing wrong with that, I'm just saying you'll probably 100% Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart without issue.

Please, please, for the love of all that is good & wonderful in this universe or any other, PLEASE Sony & Insomniac, PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE release more of your exclusive content onto Steam! Preferably some legacy stuff, too! I promise you'll make plenty of $$$, especially from me because I've totally bought this
& Spider-Man day one, and am likely to continue doing so with many of your franchises that interest me!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally good story. Any fan of Ratchet and Clank will love this game.

I will mention that for PC this game seems buggy with enemy hit-boxes not lining up with the character models, like aiming for the head only for the shot to pass through without hitting, even though the cross-hair indicated it would hit. Some of the movements lag behind for slingshots and upgraded hookshot. You can swing/launch yourself for the game to not register you doing so and causing you to just fall to your death without being able to stop.

Aside from the bugs mentioned above the gameplay is fine. The difficulties seem accurate to the descriptions. Played through the game on both "Rebel Agent" and "Renegade Legend" with each being clearly different with enemy AI.

Overall I would rate this game with the bugs 7/10 without the bugs I would rate 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[Disclaimer: This is my first Ratchet & Clank game, and thus this review will be made without regards to previous games in the series]
Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart is a 3D platformer / shoot-‘em-up where the player is given a growing arsenal of weapons to mow down hordes of enemies in extremely expressive combat. The game follows the titular Ratchet and Clank as they are tossed to an alternate dimension, meeting their dimensional counterparts, Rivet and Kit, and try to foil the schemes of Dr. and Emperor Nefarious and fix crumbling reality.
As implied, the combat in this game is very fun. The player is given new weapons to buy at a rapid pace, which are easy to buy if the player is fully interacting with the game’s levels (at least until the late game). Using a weapon gives it experience to level up, at which point the player can spend collectible currency to upgrade the weapon substantially. This makes it feel like combat is constantly evolving and giving you new options, while still allowing you to focus on improving the weapons you like once you’ve found them. The platforming also feels dynamic and seems like it’d be great to speedrun, with several level mechanics and player options letting the player go faster and faster as the game progresses. I enjoyed the game’s story, though it’s nothing revolutionary. It also features a heavier emphasis on the dimensional counterpart characters, especially Rivet and Kit, rather than the titular ones, which as a newcomer I didn’t mind, but veterans might be a bit disappointed.
I had a great time with this game, so my main complaint was that it was rather short, only taking me 14 hours to 100% my first save file. It’s a pretty tight experience, but I still would’ve liked to see at least a couple more levels. Additionally, while mowing down regular enemies never got old, there was a disappointing lack of variety in boss enemies. There were three repeating bosses that interchangeably were mini-bosses and level bosses, and only four unique bosses across the game’s twelve-ish levels. The game compensates with a few thrilling platforming sequences, but I still would have appreciated more, which can really sum up my issues with this game. Finally, at least on the PC version I played on (v1.922.0.0), there were a handful of bugs, mostly a problem I encountered frequently in the late game where my weapon would switch randomly but the weapon wheel still thought I was using my intended weapon, and a hard crash I got when platforming to an area in the early game I maybe wasn’t supposed to get to.
Overall, while I wanted more, what is there in the game is excellent and very fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust beat this game within 16 hours and am wanting to give my two-cents. So you know, I grew up on this series so I may be partial. I have not played through the new-game+, nor have I 100% the game yet.

Entertainment: 38/40
+ It feels like a Saturday morning cartoon and it makes me happy :)
+ It's a charming and endearing world where everything follows cartoon logic
- It is not a very long game so I could only play about two hours a day to savor it

Design: 14/20
+ The shooting in general is done very well
+ Guns are neat and mostly fun to use
+ Platforming is well designed and fun to do
+ The replaced the hacking mini-games with physics puzzles, and even a nod to the Land Master Missions from DEADLOCKED
- I played on the hardest difficulty, though it never felt like my skill was being tested, but it did feel like my patience was being tested during a select few arena matches
- Has one of, if not the worst feeling snipers I have ever used
- The sprint is sticky and has a bad habit of not letting you pull out a weapon without stopping first
- Some weapons confuse me as to why they are in the game
- One of the big selling points of the game, that being duel trigger systems, is not integrated to PC very well
- By far the biggest fault in the game is the enemy variety, or lack there of, which unfortunately extends to its bosses

Presentation: 18/20
+ Is one of the most visually appealing games I have played yet, both in terms of clarity and style
+ Enemies talk to you during the combat, and I'm happy to say there were very few overly repeated lines
+ Character design is very nice and distinct
+ Dialogue is nice and fun with no line at the top of my head that I could say I hated
- The game has lost the parody feeling and risque sub-tones the earlier games had, though it makes up in charm
- The music was in general very forgettable and boring, except for the ballad sung by the two Nefarious's during the credits. That was art and it was a crime to not have included it in the main game!
- This may be a nit-pick, but Kit's and Rivet's line delivery is very similar and can lead to confusion as to who is talking during combat

Story: 17/20
+ The characters are all incredibly nice to watch and listen to, though Kit may be the weakest of the main cast
+ It is charming through and through, allowing you to just play and smile
+ The villains are the perfect level of hammy
+ Rivet :)
+ Glitch :)
- There were a few story threads that were left unfinished, such as Kits backstory and the Mother Virus
- It felt as though somethings were happening for the sake of happening

Total: 87/100
Statement: A very fun game that I would spiritedly recommend to anyone, but I would also recommend you wait for a sale as $60 is a bit steep for how much game you would get.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who grew up with the game on the PS2, Ratchet and Clank holds a special place in my heart. And after the horrible release of the PS4 reboot, i was worried about this one releasing. However i would say its one of the best Ratchet and Clank games out there. It really refined the combat, adding elements from Doom's push forward combat, while having this fast portal system to grapple through reality to the other side of the room. Enemies aim ahead so youll really have to trick the enemies constanly and change directions mid side jump, dodging at the right times.

I loved the story. It did feel a bit family friendly at times, though a lot of it was really fun and other times heart warming moments. The game and animation feels like an interactive pixar movie!

I've experienced almost no bugs or lag. I had 1 lag spike once, and another time weird moments with the shading. But overall as I've played it, no bad experiences or lag. Though toaster computers will probably struggle at high settings.\t\t\t\t\t
