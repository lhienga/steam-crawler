\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts at times like this that I find myself wishing Steam had a “meh”, or “ok” rating rather than the binary “recommend” and “not recommend” system it currently has.

That statement alone probably already explains my feelings on this game but considering the “recommended” status I have given the game you at least know it is sitting above the 50% mark. A small consolation prize I suppose, but better than nothing.

So, why do I think the game is simply “ok”. For this a simple question needs to be asked, and that would be, “Who is this game for?”. Is the game meant for new individuals to the series who want to get into it? I would argue no, the game does a very poor job at explaining past concepts to the player meaning it is assuming you already know the lore and character dynamics that have been setup.

Is it for old-school fans? More so perhaps, but still not in my view. Old-school fans would know the lore and such better due to playing at least some of the old titles, but old school fans are also going to have the most problems with changes that have been made to the game.

Speaking as an old-school fan at a fundamental level the game captures the Rachet and Clank theme. Dynamic gun play combined with puzzle-platforming and exploration. This is the best part of the game, the fact that it plays so well, its just all that was built on top of it that I question.

The game also has a fair number of bugs. Nothing really game breaking, but T-posing NPCs, floating grass and getting caught on random objects makes the game feel unpolished. A large game such as this makes sense there would be things hat are overlooked, but I was not going out of my way to find these problems which, once again, has me questioning the games design.

Why play this game over one of the older titles? I found myself wanting to go play the original trilogy of games while playing this game and so the above question needs to be asked, if the fundamentals are there, but that is it, and the original games had it more why not just play those? Visuals? I mean the game does look good and modern sensibility has given rise to better dynamic and flowing combat which is a massive plus. The other aspect is accessibility. The original games were released on the PS2 and could be played on the PS3. None exist on the PC and the fact that this is the first game that does means if you only own a gaming PC now you are kind of out of luck, within the legal founds anyway.

In the end if you want to experience the old games again but with all the modern changes then you have my blessing. If you are looking for a Platform-shooter you can most definitely do worse, but if you are simply trying to relive all the good times of the past games I do not believe you will find it here.
