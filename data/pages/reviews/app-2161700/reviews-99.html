\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat works in persona games often really works, really well. Here you have this game that, as I say, has this central theme of death or ending that runs through almost all of its social links. Its social links explore the different relationships we can have with death, and some of them work really well. Akinari’s social link is a little forced in its opening but is one exploration of how you might find meaning in your life even if ur dying of a terminal disease. Bunkichi’s social link concerns mourning the death of his son. It’s no surprise that a party members dies too, as mourning someone is always an interaction with death. The story mandated social link with Pharos/Ryuji is also so important because he literally is death! All of this is made better by knowing that the ending of the game has our protagonist die. Personally I love it. In the final exploration of death as a theme what is the other way in which we can interact with death as a topic? It is to die ourselves. The protagonist closing their eyes in warmth and sunlight, surrounded by a loved friend is the perfect way to die isn’t it? It’s a happy ending, despite the fact that we are no more. Apparently people don’t like that the protagonist dies? Especially as it’s kind of only vaguely suggested that something is wrong, and not made explicit until The Answer that the protagonist closes dies when he closes his eyes. Still, I really like it.
Not all the social links are good! One of the problems with this game’s social links is the fact that we are really encouraged to be a yes-man in them to get the best social link points to level them up faster. Odagiri absolutely sucks. I hate his guts and I hate that to level it up I have to tell him that he’s right by being a complete piece of ♥♥♥♥ loser. Chihiro also blows chunks. I like that you can tell Maiko it’s probably her fault her parents are divorcing. It’s nuts that there are only two social links you can do at night because it really ends up feeling like there’s just not that much to be done at night? Thought Matatsu and Tanaka are two of the most fun social links because they’re not annoying school kids! Also coming off Persona 4 and 5 where there are social links with all party members available from the moment of getting the party member. Levelling them up is fun and rewarding. And in Persona 3 you can only social link your female party members? And only with a social stat maxed out? This is terrible! I get that P3R is a very faithful remake and that is cool, but this is something that feels like should’ve been modernised. Like you have all these hangouts at Iwatodai dorm, as well as the fact that each character has “link episodes” which function as social links basically, resulting in you being able to fuse special personas. So ATLUS kind of want to have their cake and eat it too, but I don’t feel like it works. It’s much harder to keep on top of these link episodes, especially when they only exist for the boys, because the girls have social links… make it make sense! Also, it’s really hard going back to P3 and P4 and doing social links where you don’t get tangible benefits for doing them. I don’t mean to sound like a baby, but at least in P5 if you did a social link where you didn’t like the character, you often at least got something interesting out of it. Not so in P3! I can’t believe they didn’t find a way to incorporate this into P3…
On the topic of social stats, I don’t mind there only being 3 stats in this game, but I think activity balancing was way off in this game. I had all three stats maxed out by October without using guides for them, which meant that they became completely arbitrary metrics for the last three and a half months of the game, which sucks! The pacing around social links also feels really off, because when I had the option to do Mitsuru’s one, no one else was available for like 2 weeks, so I just powered through it at one time, and same with Aigis. On one hand it’s kind of nice to be able to focus on them, but it feels really disconnected from the rest of the game.
I have some character beat issues too. Ryoji should’ve had a social link separate from Death? He’s so important to the latter part of the story but doesn’t actually have a particularly long part in the story, and it’s a little weird. I think Chidori and Junpei’s romance is kind of garbage, and Junpei ends up less endearing that say, P5’s Ryuji because he is much more intentionally pervy which sucks. I think Takaya has a really great look, and his voice actor is amazing, but it’s no surprise that people no remember Takaya over Persona’s other villains. Takaya’s motivations aren’t particularly inspiring or thought-provoking. There’s a scene where he talks about how car accidents cause loads of deaths every year, but we don’t try to stop them the way we try to stop the Dark Hour. Then we say “that logic makes no sense”, and that conversation is just sort of over. There’s no actual part of writing that seriously engages with Takaya’s point of view, because he doesn’t really have one, and it’s a shame because I see potential in him as a villain that we just don’t really get. I think he sort of fails as an example of someone who is embracing the theme of Death as a good thing to strive for in some ways? All he really does is kill Shinji, which is its own muddied murder/sacrifice. Ikutsuki ends up being one of the most frustrating villains ever because his place in the story beforehand is so underused. We’re told that he’s the chairman of the school, and this adult who can interact with the Dark Hour but doesn’t have a Persona. why is that? We should be asking questions about what Ikutsuki is doing and what he is doing to actually support SEES. His betrayal is disappointing because there’s no indication of who he was beforehand. I think one of the things that is so brilliant about Persona 4’s villain is that his social link is optional. And if you do it, you certainly don’t find out that he’s the villain, so the reveal later is still a shock. But the social link allows his mask to slip just a little bit. It allows you to look at him and go “this guy is actually a lot more jaded and bitter than I thought, that’s kind of weird”, then when the twist comes around, suddenly his social link makes so much more sense. Ikutsuki is missing this entirely, just being a nice guy who makes jokes then suddenly wants to kill you.
Each Persona game mixes its gameplay up a little bit, and I really enjoyed that the physical attack types were all different in this one. Changing light and dark skills to actual attacks was really needed in 4, so it’s nice to see that update in reload too. Theurgies are cool, if not a bit overpowered. When the final boss had 75% of his health I used Armaged don which does 9999 damage to the boss, and it just killed him instantly. Like, getting Armageddon wasn’t very hard so this was a bit of a disappointing end to the final battle hahaha
But actually fighting gate keepers was fun, and I liked mixing up my team and thinking about how I was going to use my Personas. Once I fused Beezlebub I didn’t need to fuse ever again. As my social links and social stats were all done, I took the lord of flies to the arcade every day and maxed out his strength, magic, and agility, and gave him two stacks of Dark Amp, so he did stupid damage. Great game lmao
Tartarus sucks, and I hear it’s massively improved from the original game. I’m sure it has! But also it’s not improved enough. One of the problems of remaking a game like P3 is that Tartatus is there so you have to do something with it. You can’t just scrap it, which if you were making a new Persona game you would. I don’t know what the option is but Tartarus is really boring, and knowing that the creepy Dark hour is out there somewhere but we just can’t see it because we’re in a tower just sucks. The Dark Hour honestly feels under utilised from a creative point of view, but whatever

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my all time favorite games. Persona 3 is a game that invokes many emotions throughout its story, and I'm here for all of them. The highs and lows in emotion conveyed by Persona 3 are matched only by a few games. Not to mention the amazing soundtrack. Persona 3 FES (the best version of Persona 3) kept all of that and added additional content to the game that enhances the original experience. While keeping the original vision for Persona 3 by not giving you full control over the party stressing the message that everyone is their own individual. The tactics systems from these games are one of my favorite features in a video game period and while I was disappointed to learn that Persona 3 Reload featured full party control I can understand that this was for accessibility of the new players who are used to this feature from Persona 4 onwards. (I do appreciate the option to turn on the old settings in the menu)

With this being said I still love Persona 3 Reload. While I do not believe this is the best version of Persona 3 it is definitely the best version that is on the Steam platform. I believe also that the new soundtrack has some issues with the vocals not evoking the same emotion and some of the instruments not sounding as good as they did before. However I do believe that there are definitely tracks that are superior to the original soundtrack. Persona 3 Reload also is a far easier game than its Persona 3 and FES counterparts. There was never a moment where I felt that I could actually lose a fight. The theurgy system was a unique way to incorporate the old fusion skills and add new skills to the party members, but also made most fights a cake walk. The new Linked Episodes system was quite nice. Also the removal of the jealousy system from the old social links is a nice quality of life upgrade to the original. I also appreciate the fact they did not change much of the story and we did not end up with a headache like Final Fantasy VII Remake and Rebirth that completely butchered the original story and themes of the original story.

Even with all of the quality of life upgrades and some of the new systems I still find Persona 3 FES to be the superior game. I feel as Reload changed the aesthetic too much and it doesn't seem to convey the same emotions as Persona 3 and FES. Persona 3 Reload also swapped some of the 2D animated cutscenes for in game rendered versions of them which I thought was a weird choice especially for the persona awakening and Shinji's death. Also the DLC stuff ATLUS and SEGA did was kind of weird with making the game $70 and the expansion pass $35. When the original Persona 3 FES was only $30 on the PS2 with the same amount of content.

I still find it to be worth the $70 I paid and it will probably stay as my favorite release of 2024. This is by no means a bad game and one I recommend that you play.

TLDR; Persona 3 FES is still superior but if all the new quality of life systems are mandatory for you to play a Persona game you won't be disappointed with the product you find here. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played most of the game on gamepass but decided to buy it on steam to own it permanently as I did not have any money at the time
I've been playing Megaten/Persona for many years now however Persona 3 Fes was always my favorite Persona Game and third favorite Megami Tensei game right behind SMT4 and Nocturne. I am pleased to say that this game surpassed my expectations. I was mostly expecting the gameplay to be a reskin of p5 but was surprised to find out how faithful this game was. The dungeon crawling was really addictive and had a blast revisiting old social links. I like fat chicks. Playing this game was like saying hello to a friend I haven't seen in a long time. I highly recomend this game not just to any previous player of the franchise but to fans of the genre itself. Any JRPG fan will fall in love with this game (with a few exeptions like people who prefer fes or portable for example). The soundtrack while still amazing felt a bit weaker to the original while still being really good (Changing Seasons, Mass Destruction, and Mass Destruction Final Battle is inferior just to name some examples) however songs such as Color Your Night and It's Going Down are absolute bangers and I got depressed seeing Color Your Night go when the songs changed late into the game. If you prefer fes just know The Answer is leaked and will come out soon. People are modding femc into the game. This truly is the best version to play if you're looking to getting into Persona 3. All in all 9/5/10.\t\t\t\t\t
