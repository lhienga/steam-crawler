I'm not typically a fan of traditional RPGs, but I found Reload to be a very pleasant surprise. I played it on hard and found it too easy, though, so I'm hoping a second playthrough will make up for that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving just finished the game, I figure now is the best time for a review.

The good:
The game is stunning. It looks and sounds fantastic like always. The 1 more system really shines in this game with the ability to string across party members or follow up with extra damage/buffs/heals. I like the change from the original to include other bless and curse skills than just insta-kills and it helps bring combat in-line across the series.

Social Links are great and the daily life mechanics are wonderful. There is a lack of things to do at night in general (if you aren't hanging out with party members) but this is balanced with tartarus exploration well.

In all, it's a persona game. They're always good and this is no exception.

The bad:
First, I'll start with the obvious. This game is NOT a definitive edition of Persona 3 and its story. The game is currently lacking The Answer (This is almost certainly coming as DLC) and also is lacking the FemC story/option (This is likely not going to be added). Due to both of these, I can't say that a definitive edition of Persona 3 exists yet. Once DLC is dropped for both The Answer and FemC then I would say this is the definitive edition.

Additionally, this may have just been my feeling but the dark tone of the original story seems to have been toned down. The very end of the game brings a return to the darker feeling of the original but the rest of the game is a little more upbeat. Again, this could just be my perception.

The ugly:
Atlus promised they wouldn't do an expanded version of this game. In the same breath they basically guaranteed DLC for this game. To me, that's a scummy business practice. A DLC for The Answer is almost certainly coming. Personally, I would rather have paid an extra $10-20 up front and had all the content from the beginning.

Additionally, the exclusion of FemC makes me skeptical of the claim an expanded edition of the game isn't coming. It seems like that exclusion would be the obvious choice for an expansion of the game. We'll see if Atlus holds true to that promise though.

Final thoughts:
This game is really good. Persona 5 was a game that had a big impact on me when I played it on Playstation 4. Personally, I think this game's story is even better. Just like Persona 5 I'd give this game a 9.5/10. Hopefully, Atlus will release DLC soon to make this game the true definitive edition of Persona 3.\t\t\t\t\t
