\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my favorite game of all time, and they remade it. Yay! I want to break this down into two parts, one for people who never played P3 and for people who have.

First for those of you who haven't played P3 prior. Just go into this knowing a couple of things, this game is not P4 or P5, which you may be more familiar with. This story is a good bit darker, and there's a lot more consistency to that darkness. This game is a roller coaster, it will have very high highs and very low lows, emotionally, in its story and characters. This game introduces some of the absolute best characters in the entire series, but there will be some hurdles. They are growing pains though, most of them have brilliant transformations. This game gets you started very quickly compared to 4&5 with you getting control of your time within an hour. I'd offer two main warnings, one is that this game is incredibly easy to spoil as it's 20 years old, I would resist the urge to look up any art or most questions really if you intend to avoid spoilers. 2nd warning is for the first social link you meet is the Magician, he's a school mate, I have no idea why this character was chosen as your first introduction to social links. He's kind of . No lies, but the vast majority are exceptional. Every installment has at least one dud, this one is just up front and center. Speaking of social links, they can reverse on you in 3, or even break. If reversed they will require time to mend before you can make progress again. If broken you can't fuse Personas of that Arcana, it's brutal, this game is brutal. Take your time, it's an incredible ride, and even though I've played through P3 countless times it never fails to leave an impact. The main dungeon in this game is Tartarus and single dungeon similar to Mementos in P5, but it's go in when you want, no deadlines. Checkpoints, blockers etc. That being said I really disliked Mementos, a lot, mainly because it didn't feel important to what was going on. In P3 Tartarus is the point, it is the goal, and it's significantly faster. You'll climb floors sometimes fighting only 1-2 battles, so you're covering ground quickly each major block changes in music (adding to the original theme), visual theme, floor layout etc. The combat is very fluid for a turn based rpg, and the music, as with all Persona games, just crushes it. As long as you are not put off by what you see in the trailers, mainly how Personas are summoned in this game, it's worth your time. Easily recommend to any of my friends or RPG lovers. Difficulty wise, it's a bit on the easier side, if you are looking for specifically challenging gameplay, try skipping enemies on a few floors, over leveling vs under leveling makes a huge difference here.

**********************************************************************************
Still no spoilers <3
Now for those that have played the original, whichever version you played lol. So, there is currently no FES or Answer chapter, that is coming in a later DLC pack this year. There is no FeMC unfortunately, although I understand the difficulty of that situation. They packed the original with all kinds of new events and quality of life improvements. All the original social links remain, although they all got completely voiced, which is excellent. They recast virtually every character except for Elizabeth, that being said they did a fantastic job filling those roles, Junpei, Akihiko, Mitsuru, are very good, surprisingly as I was pretty worried about Junpei as soon as I heard there was a recast. and Ikutski got full on 180s as their previous voice work was, well, poor. Yukari is a bit questionable as her new VA took some of the catty-ness out of her lines, which to some makes her more approachable and likeable. Yukari is my favorite character and although it's different I still enjoy it quite a bit.

You can control your teamates! No more turns lost to Marin ***king Karin or letting you die cause you didn't get a heal xD Even though your male teamates do not get social links they have social events, chapters or character growth that you can pursue and these are all well done. So far the story has lived up to the original with no changes, apart from the scene at the beach which alters the identity of the last girl you hit on. Nothing major. There are plenty of new minor scenes that don't change the story but flesh out a few things. The music has been remade as well with some top shelf bangers on the tracklist, only a couple that I prefer the original of, but none of them are misses imo. You can still reverse or break social links although it's not quite as easy as it used to be, people will no longer reverse if you don't hang out with them enough. Social stats are a bit easier to aquire, there are new part time jobs and all kinds of minor things to fill your time with. A lot went into this game, and it shows. While I haven't finished it yet, I have no reason to doubt it at this point, it's been absolutely brilliant thus far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was worried about the remake was gonna completly bottle it. My initinal thoughts from the trailers and leaks gave me the impresion that this was just gonna be a Persona 5 esque game with the story of persona 3. I was completely proven wrong and my expectations we're deffinetly exceeded. This was a more or less a completly faithful remake with quality of life additions and new gameplay mechanics that elevated the game to new heights. It's not a perfect game and it isn't 100% faithful but I think in general it did as best as it could and succeeded.

Gameplay: 8/10
The gameplay is the standard persona formula with social sim and rpg mixed together, and as a person who played the original persona 3 fes for the PS2, it was a definite upgrade. First of all the party is now directly commandable which really takes the cake in this category. The originals strategic command system was in my opinion pretty good but as a person who started out with persona 4, I can't really say I was a big fan of it at all. This change made it vastly more enjoyable for me to play without the constant headache that your party members were gonna waste their turn(if you didn't plan accordingly). The additions of shift(baton pass) also made interactions with the characters that much stronger and saved many greivances with the weaknesses. The social sim aspect didn't recive that many notable changes, it is mostly the same gameplay loop that you would expect for a persona game to have. One of the nice changes from the original was social link reverting was removed. That mechanic made time slot orginization hell and im glad its gone without much second thought, although I do get the point behind it being a more realistic depiction of relationships, if you don't hang out with them much your realtionship to them will deteriorate but it was just a hinderence in the original game. The night time activities giving you a lot more options is nice aswell. We also can't forget the addition of theurgy, which is very useful but a lot of the time it's just eyeball candy used to keep things more interesting to look at and the damage numbers up.


Music: 9/10
The music was very solid, bangers even. Keeping with the thematic of the original game, and remastered very well.
There were some very clear outliers here tho. Mass destruction -reload- was pretty botched, it wasn't bad but it definetlly wasn't that great sounding and considering this is probably what most people would associate persona 3 too it's a very subpar remaster, although you warm up to it the more you play through the game and the more you hear it. As far as the new tracks go they all go hard.


General look/cutscenes: 8/10
The game looks gourgeous and the new cinematics look great. The style was a bit persona fiveavfied but it was distinct enough. The menues definitelly give the persona aesthetic and look great aswell. The animation quality has drsactically improved in the anime cutscenes and brand new 3D cutscenes aswell. The cutscenes are missing a lot of the "edge" from the original game and a lot of them were flat out chnaged but in the grand scheme of things didn't hinder my enjyoment of the story at all.


Story: 9.5/10
Out of the modern perosona games, it doesn't get better than this. The story was left completely intact and delivered beautifully. The game portrayed it story the way it wanted it to without much bs. It is very anime still at it's core(wow no way, an anime game being anime?!'11'??) which might put some people of. It isn't perfect and it isn't peak fiction but godamn does it get pretty close.


Characters: 10/10
Main party: This is the category that persona excells at(except persona 5). All the characters are greatly written and likeable and the best part? NO ANNOYING MASCOT CHARACTERS BEING USELESS PEICES OF COMEDIC RELIEF RAAAAH. This game writes it's chracters to all be very relatable. It makes them feel human with human problems and the way the story develops them along with their social links is just incredible.
Non party: The characters you meet in the social sim part each have their own distinct charm to them (except fat kid, you fat kid). They all offer a diffrent flavour to the game with their own distinct personalities that you just can't help but like them.


Overall score: 9/10.
This remake did things well beyond my expectations and if you can't tell by the short story I have written I really like the game: I didn't play the original very thoroughly and it's been a while so some crticism from that may be factually incorrect and frankly to ignorant to look through again. But that does not change the fact that as a standalone game it is amazing from start to finish regardless of the original.


Side note: DO NOT buy the DLC
I have failed to mention that in the gameplay segemnt but I will address it now.
The 100 Euro edition is a waste of money and a headache to deal with. It offers you BGM, costumes and personas from previous games but for your first playthrough the extra personas will up your fusions.
Especially at lower levels where most of them lie in. if you want to craft a specific persona using the reload calculator it will mostlikely not work, since you will get captian kidd on so many occasions.
