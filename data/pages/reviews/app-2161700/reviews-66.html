\r\n\t\t\t\t\taigis... you'll find the answer one day as well.

persona 3 reload is a... hard game to review.

this IS persona 3 we're talking about, i love the main characters, the simple yet underlying complex story told all at once, and aigis might be my favorite character of the whole series.

(and yes, i really like the new soundtrack)

that said, it's still a game, and a remake nonetheless, so i have some things i wanna complain on:

1. while i did enjoy the new hangouts and mini social links, i think this remake is far too constrained on being faithful to it's source material rather than trying something more with it, this remake could've been a LOT more, but instead it chose to do the exact same thing as it's PS2 counterpart, and as a counterpart the game still feels like it has the PS2 limitations in mind. and the whole time i kept wondering "i spent $70 dollars on this, and i could've just played FES again..."

2. also, the majority of the social links... kinda suck. or i don't really care for.here's a tier list of how i feel about them

3. they could've done a LOT more with tartarus, the same way they did with mementos in P5 -> P5R (i miss you, ryuji's insta-kill) and it kinda frustrates me they chose to handle it like this.

4. it still has the same problem as FES, that when you max your social skills and your social links at night, you have NOTHING else to do on nights. this is a little mitigated by the dorm hangouts but it gets old really quickly

but when all is said and done, it's still a really meaningful experience and i had a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed Persona 3 Reload. It feels new and old as it upgrades the combat system of Persona 3, adds some helpful quality of life features, and new social content with friends. Persona 3 was the game that established the social links and general foundation of the series that is repeated and expanded upon in the next two numbered entries. Playing through Persona 3 Reload for the first time, really opened my eyes to how strictly the developers reused some of the same exact tropes in the following games; the horny and academically incompetent male best friend, the girl who wants to learn to cook and uses you as her taste tester, the extremely shy and insecure girl who learns her value... the list goes on. While that's not a knock on Persona 3 Reload, I do hope they get more adventurous with the next game, because it is getting a bit old. The other side of that is the more unique characters do really stand out. Sanada is a wonderful character who really stands out due to his maturity and history with other main characters. Some other characters and social links stand out in a similar way, and have really touching stories with great conclusions. Considering the original game was the first time they used this formula, it's amazing how much they got right that ends up standing above its successors.

I never played the original, but from what I've seen (and played of Persona 4 Golden which seemingly has similar combat based on videos I've seen) the combat is vastly improved. The ability to shift to an ally after hitting a weakness makes exploring Tartarus far less time-consuming, and you're rewarded for excelling in combat with shuffle time which ensures more progress from each encounter. They build so many elements into the game that are there purely to make Tartarus more enjoyable; including ways to quickly level party members, buff your personas, create personas above your level, and fully refill HP and SP just to name a few. The checkpoint events that happen on full moons in Persona 3 Reload feel more unique and interesting than in other Persona games I've played, and always felt like a nice treat after exploring Tartarus and reaching a new border floor.

The story of Persona 3 Reload is fantastic. While some events are predictable, especially to those who've experienced a Persona game before, they have great payoffs with colorful animated cutscenes and heartwarming interactions between your party members. There are some annoying moments that don't make much sense and actively work against the character growth they're been building towards, one example that stood out to me was Junpei confessing his love for Chidori as she dies in his arms, in what has been massive character growth from Junpei and Chidori as they've both realized the value they have and impact they can have on others, but then literally a week later he's back to being his horny self, hitting on Yukari, Mitsuru, and Yamagishi (Steam censored her first name lol) and freaking out over the thought of them having nothing on under their Kimonos... Moments like this are rare, but feel very out of place.

Ultimately I really enjoyed Persona 3 Reload and highly recommend it. It's up there with Persona 5 Royal as the absolute best the series has to offer. It differentiates itself tonally and with its dungeon crawling in ways that could easily leads people to preferring it over Royal. After reflecting on it, It's my personal favorite in the series, and I'm excited to play the newly announced DLC and the other rumored remakes and new entries in the series when they release.

Highly Recommended
