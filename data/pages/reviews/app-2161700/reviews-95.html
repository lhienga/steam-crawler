\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersona 3 FES is a game that was important to me back when I was a kid. It helped shape my aesthetics and was overall a game that changed my life, (albeit not dramatically). So it's only natural I'd pick up this up soon as I heard it was coming out.

I'll preface this by saying. I love what Reload does. It genuinely enhances the story in every way it can, From the new cutscenes to the new character link events that flesh the characters and world out further. All of these are very much welcome additions to the story and it's what makes me love this version the most. It's almost like I'm re-experiencing the story for the first time again with the amount of new story content they've made.

The combat is a good deal more fluid now, They bring in the baton pass from Persona 5 in the form of shifting and they introduce Theurgies which are basically limit breaks.

While this may make the combat a lot more fluid and in-depth, I do think this affects the difficulty, I can't say I've died much at all in comparison to FES. Though oddly, they didn't bring back the weapon swapping from FES, So you can't have your lead character use for example: Yukari's Bows or Ken's Spears instead of the default sword weapons. It's a minor thing but it's a feature I personally miss.

The biggest hit against this game for me is the music. A lot of tracks are just not as punchy as they were in the original games. The new tracks are great but most of the old iconic tracks just don't hit as hard as they did before. I had hoped we could change them back to their original forms without mods, but that doesn't seem the case unless they release future DLC for it. Of course, this is mostly my own tastes here and yours might vary on this.

If you were a fan of the original, I highly recommend you play Reload. If my complaints have clued you in, its faults are minor and really all amount to your own personal taste. If you think you'll like it, you most likely will.
