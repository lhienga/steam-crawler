\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor me it might be my favorite game in the series, and the remaster really shines. A shame that the FES content wasn't also remastered, feels a little incomplete in a sense, but I can't complain. This game has some of my favorite social links in the series. While its JRPG gameplay might not necessarily feel as fun, flashy and engaging as 5; new additions to the combat system like Theurgy are a welcome extra flavor to a JRPG formula I've always enjoyed.

As always, Persona games are a huge time sink, but if you're willing to give them a chance I guarantee you'll get hooked. Whether you find yourself loving the characters, laughing at some of the bizarre dialogue, or just enjoying your journey in this world, I think there is something here for everyone.

I also think that thematically this game stands out among all of the Persona games. It tackles legitimately difficult emotional topics and does not pull its punches like P4 or P5 do at times. This game is about death, grief, and loss, but also about how to move beyond those and find new meaning in life. P3 has always been the most emotionally resonate game in the series for me, so I give it an outstanding recommendation for anyone interested in the series.\t\t\t\t\t

GOOD:
- P3R adds in some of the more flashy style of the later games while maintaining the original simple, gloomy 2000s vibe of the original game. It becomes a dark ass story, definitely the darkest of the three. This is tearjerk material and you should play it even if you feel like you can't handle tearjerkers.

- The linked episodes, spending time with dorm-mates, and group study sessions make your fellow SEES members feel less like co-workers and much more like friends. You can kinda tell these scenes are new when they happen. Friendship is something they got a lot better at in later games and now we get more of that warmth here in Persona 3. To me, this is the biggest improvement in the game. This is easily my favorite cast.
- - You can go through the entire game without having to interact with the P3 Social Links of lesser quality because of all the new SEES activities. I never touched Kenji, Hayase, and Stripey c1u2n3t4. Spent a few afternoons with my track teammate but didn't have to take it any farther. The SEES integrate well with the dungeon crawling part of the game too.
- Junpei is such a bro. He is the best Persona NPC ever I reckon. It feels so good when your best friend snaps at you in a game where everyone treats you like a damn phenom. I know I can trust this young man.

- Tartarus is so well redone. There are so many little improvements and added busy work on top of a complete visual overhaul. They didn't change any of the fundamentals of Tartarus, once considered a 250-floor slog, but it feels completely fresh and fun. To me this is the best dungeon gameplay in the Persona series. It's bite-sized, low-pressure, and never feels like a chore. I'm amazed that they turned it around so starkly.

- Sooooo much of this is voice acted. Almost feels off when it isn't voiced. I can see why they made such a big deal about having the most lines in the series.

- You can really tell that the Social Link system was made specifically for this game. It's more in line with the game's themes than in other games.

- The timing of the Fool Arcana Maxing, when we find out WHY it's the Fool Arcana, is perfect. Probably the best moment in the game.

- I love that there's not so much flashy crap filling up the HUD. It's simple and informative, not a cluster♥♥♥♥ like P5.

- Yukari looks better and is as based as ever.

- I love that they put the Frenchman in Home Ec LMAO

---
BAD:
- The story takes a pretty long while to really get going, although you'll be having fun way earlier than that. While there is a lot of upfront dialogue and exposition in the first few hours, it's all very basic and in all honesty some of it is entirely skippable if you've played Persona before.
- It is a lot less cinematic than P5. I think this is the main reason why it might feel "old" or "outdated" to some. I love the simplicity,

- Remember when I said that everyone treats you like a phenom? Yeah well Persona in general suffers from Dragonborn syndrome, but it's all made much worse by how often everyone reminds you of your infinite capability. That is paired with some of the most anime writing I have ever encountered. "The power of friendship" is a real, repeated line from the game. Everyone has a backstory that totally defines them as a person until the protagonist comes in and saves them. They will never miss an opportunity to thank you for all you've done for them. Yes, it's totally in-theme and yes it still works well, but sometimes it feels like it works in spite of itself.
- The game severely lack subtlety. A big reason why the Fool Arcana Max moment is so great is because its unspoken, subtle. This is a stark contrast to almost every other moment in the game, when every character pours their heart out for 20 minutes about "staying true to myself," "fight for what I believe in," and other banal anime-isms. Look, I LOVE this game, but that stuff is so off-putting. It's the type of shoit you find out when you're like 10 years old. And they repeat the same thing over and over and over again. This is an M rated game so I would hope that the people playing this game aren't captivated by this dialogue.

- I think that the original animated cutscenes were way better than the in-engine stuff they did here. Makoto's awakening and Aegis's introduction come to mind.
- The iconic opening was changed, which is such a head-scratcher to me. The pacing is jacked way up, its detriment. The shot of Yukari's lip backlit by the streetlights through her window with the lens flare is just gone. There are a lot of lip shots in this game, which would look much better animated.

- The phrase "Dancing like ballerinas" should never be a lyric in a rapped verse. The rest of the song is great, but that line kills it. Definitely the worst moment in the Persona series' stellar record with score.

---
UGLY:
- There are some graphical inconsistencies. The Mall and Strip look INCREDIBLE and then you go back to your dorm hallways where you're reminded that this was a PS2 game. A few new decorations would have helped this.

- The mixing on Yukari's flavor audio for non-voiced dialogue is way off.

- This is an unreasonable demand, which is why its in UGLY, but I really wish this game had a legit transit system like P5 did, where you could go places without really having to interact with a menu that much. That part of P5 felt exactly like what Persona is supposed to play like. Nothing is taken away from the game by not having it, just a lot of map menu usage.


CONCLUSION:
This is not the "definitive" version that everyone was begging for, but it is the best version. For me that matters so much more. This is a great example of addition by subtraction. They took the 3 or 4 good things from the Answer, put it in the base game, and scrapped the rest of it because the rest of it wasn't that good. Now they have a game at max quality all the time, without a 30 hour Tartarus slog poorly bolted onto the end of the story. It's an all-time classic RPG faithfully remade with updated visuals and improvements that almost always make it a better game. This is definitely my favorite Persona release.\t\t\t\t\t
