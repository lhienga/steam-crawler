\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played Persona 4 Golden on release and Persona 5 on release, my only "modern" Persona that I hadn't had a chance to dip my toes into was Persona 3. Leading up to the release of Reload, all I heard from Persona fans was that this was the best story of the 3. Having dumped a ton of hours into Persona 4 and 5, I was absolutely ready to dive into another Persona game. The only problem: adjusting to the less "busy" feel of Persona 3.

Don't get me wrong, this game was beautiful, but when you come from Persona 5 to this, it feels like it's much less content packed so those early months were really hard to get gripped. I would stop playing after about an hour, which is unusual for me on Persona, and then would play another game etc.

After about 2 weeks of this pick-up and drop play style, I hit June and then this is when the game took off for me. The story started to take some big swings, characters were developing really intricate personalities and you started to feel for these characters. After that point, it was an addiction. I couldn't stop playing and kept dumping hours and hours into this game. Soon I was welcoming the less "busy" feel as it favored more social links which were the absolute BEST.

After spending so many hours with these characters, learning the mechanics, I was met with the ending credits and I just sat there in awe. I think Persona 3 tells the most incredible story that leaves you feeling so many emotions by the time the credits roll. I don't want to spoil too much, but I can proudly say this is not only my favorite Persona story, but also one of my favorite stories in all of gaming. It's a special experience and gives you a new perspective on life.

Persona 3 not only became my favorite of the series, it may be my favorite game of all-time. I absolutely recommend you give this a go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersona 3 Portable and FES were already one of my favourite games I've ever played. This remake, while lacking the FEMC content (SEGA probably didn't want to provide the budget since they would of had to remake the entire game again), is still a amazing remake and shows how good Atlus can be when it comes to updating games when they put in effort (I'm looking at you SMT Nocturne HD).

The game not only has all of the base vanilla FES content, but also adds new content for the male party members, fixes major pacing issues, and expands on the relatively weak human antagonists and makes them genuinely good. The gameplay is also the best in the series in my opinion, as it strikes that balance between SP and HP based skills.

I can FINALLY recommend Persona 3 as a first Persona game and with the upcoming DLC adding the effective "sequel"/"epilogue" of The Answer, will end up being jam packed with content if it gets the same treatment as the main game. (I would recommend waiting for when this goes on sale however, 35 bucks is a little TOO much Atlus.)

The only real issue I have with the game is that Tartarus, while vastly improved, still falls behind the other Persona titles when it comes to how "fun" it is to explore it. However with the additions of stuff like the Monad Doors (fun little challenges) and some random events, it can be pretty fun.

Either way, definitely a vast improvement over the original, in both story and gameplay. I highly recommend it if you have played or are interested in the Persona series. Remember, robot girl supremacy.\t\t\t\t\t
