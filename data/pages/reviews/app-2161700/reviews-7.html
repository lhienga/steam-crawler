Voice actors are killing it. Character interactions are greatly more detailed than the original P3. The biggest issue still with this game is the horrendous pacing of the story nearing the end of the game. Since everything major happens on a full moon, there's just two whole months of the game where absolutely nothing happens and the only actions you can take at night is to sleep like a normal high school student. Social links in this game consist of the most enabling behaviour we've seen from any other Persona protagonist. You want to date your teacher? Screw it, go for it. You want to run away from home? Go for it. You want to keep training even though you'll sustain a permanent injury that'll effectively destroy your running career? Go for it. No Yu Narukami or Ren Amamiya shenanigans here. All we got is Makoto Yuki's ♥♥♥♥ it we ball.

If you're a JRPG fan who's never touched a Persona game before, wondering if you should pick up P3R to try, the answer is a resounding yes! Just generally, the Persona franchise is a strong performer in story-driven games and P3R is no different. A beautifully designed story is packaged nicely with gorgeous new graphics and enthralling gameplay which has been enhanced with quality of life upgrades introduced to this revamped copy of the title. It makes it easy for anyone to pick up and roll, even if you've never touched a JRPG before. The nature of Persona games as thick packages of unique branching stories also means that no two runs of the game are the same. You can always come back to this title for a fresh and enchanting experience.

The Good:

Characters - Like P4 and P5, the cast of characters is heart-warming and easy to attach yourself to. Your main party is made up of an incredibly strong core that never feels stale, especially with how closely tied to the story they are. Junpei's entire character arc is probably my favourite in the series, beating out my previous favourite of P4's Kanji. Yukari's entire character is a powerful story of overcoming the pain of loss. The dynamic between Shinjiro, Mitsuru and Akihiko is really second to none, with how well the characters build off of each other within the narrative. And of course, Koromaru is the best mascot character in the entire series. Teddy and Morgana cannot compete. The weakest of the cast is certainly Ken, with how awkwardly he is shoe-horned into the story. But Ken still has a lot of powerful moments that makes his involvement a worthy investment of your time.

Combat - P3R's combat system has quickly become my favourite in the series. Although I was definitely missing the Psy and Nuclear elements from P5, the inclusion of three separate Physical attributes was a nice compensation. P3R's combat is largely a better version of P5R's. The Shift system occupies the same role as Baton Passes, although it lacks a cool sequence of Persona protagonists giving each other high-fives. Guns have been dropped from P3R, with attacks that shared the Gun-Type from P5 changing to the Pierce-Type physical skill. All-Out Attacks and One Mores make a return, allowing for a quick dusting of your usual rank-and-file enemies. All-Out Attacks have been improved in P3R, as not only can their damage be buffed from Shuffle Times, but they can also be used when untargetable enemies are present on the field. The presence of Theurgy skills further enhances the combat experience, allowing for devastating opening attacks against bosses, or a powerful final push to finish off a particularly nasty foe. Overall, if you're a fan of turn-based combat from other JRPGs like Dragon Quest, P3R's combat will really scratch that itch.

Music - Persona titles always have stellar music. Every time another franchise brings good music to their games, Persona comes out and moves the goal posts to the next galaxy, as if to taunt the rest industry. P3R is no different. If you believed P5R to have the best music of the franchise, P3R hands Royal its own ass on a silver platter. Lotus Juice has returned with this title, bringing out a group of new tracks that build off of P3's J-Pop base to narrate a beautiful story. There are few games where you're inclined to listen to their soundtracks on repeat while level grinding. There are even fewer where you're inclined to download their soundtrack on Spotify and listen to it while grinding on other games. Persona 3 Reload joins Persona 5 Royal on that vaunted pedestal.

The Story - Persona 3 Reload has a story that was originally crafted in 2006, so sometimes you have to meet the narrative where it's at. Certainly there are moments in it where it can be a little cringe-worthy. But the game constructs an amazing tale about coming to terms with loss, and the personal struggles involved. Each character brought in enhances that story, all dealing with some sort of personal loss that is for you to explore and root out. The game does a great job of casting a sharp contrast with your character through their social links; if you chose to be apathetic and uncaring about the people around you, you end up powerless and alone. But if you invest in those people, you're rewarded with beautiful stories, tear-jerking moments, and an incredibly powerful array of Personas. The main story has amazing payoff down the line, and once it sinks its claws into you it can be really hard to have it let go. HOWEVER, be aware that this game can take a little bit to get going. A lot of its best narrative moments are locked behind the second-half of the game. Remember to give yourself breaks so that you don't burn out on some of the monotony of the first-half of the game. And ALWAYS make sure you go back to the flower shop to buy Junpei white flowers.

The Bad:

Social Links - Clarifying, this is not to say that all the social links are bad. Rather, the social links are a mixed bag that can feel like a drag to complete if you're not fully invested in a character. Because P3R tries to be a faithful remake of the original P3, the bonuses that Confidants had in P5 are nowhere to be seen, meaning that the only benefit of maxing out a social link is the persona at the end of the link. While that's not necessarily a bad prize, it can make some social links feel grating, as you're not gaining anything from interacting with particularly annoying characters until the very end of the social link. What further frustrates social links in the game is that most of the better social links, including your teammates, can only be advanced while school is in session. This means that if you don't map out your social link plans, you can end up on a school vacation and not be able to interact with anybody.

Night Time - Again a flaw of P3R trying to be a faithful remake of its original copy, the activities that can be done at night are incredibly restricted. Much of the map gets closed off at night, with only two social links being made available at night. While the introduction of the hacker's relationship website certainly helps in the second half of the game, the fact remains that nighttime in P3R is largely a waste of time once you've maxed out your nighttime social links. Although you can just jump into Tartarus straight away, the simplification of the navigation of Tartarus means that only 2 - 3 trips into Tartarus are really required between the major story beats; 1 - 2 to scale to the levels and 1 to serve as an extra exp grind.

Lack of FEMC: Persona 3 Portable introduced a female lead option for Persona with unique social links, story beats, and other cool features. Unfortunately, Atlus decided not to pursue bringing the FEMC option up to P3R. It's a disappointing development, especially given how beautiful P3R is on the whole, but a dedicated modding community is working to make this feature a reality. Hopefully one day soon you'll be able to stand awkwardly between Akihiko and Ken as they bicker about whether or not you should be wearing battle panties in Tartarus.

The Ugly:

Your face after finishing Akinara's Social Link.

Overall P3R is an amazing addition to the Persona franchise. It is another solid JRPG that brings the stellar P3 experience into the modern age with incredible quality of life updates, superb music, and bonus story moments with party members that helps to flesh them out. It is the best edition of Persona 3 out there, and definitely deserves a place in the library of all who consider themselves JRPG fans.\t\t\t\t\t
