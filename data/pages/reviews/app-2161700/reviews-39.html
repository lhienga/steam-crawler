\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who loved the old P3, this remake is spectacular and absolutely what P3 needed to be played in 2024 without the annoying drawbacks of P3P or FES. It is a bit on the overpriced side but the game is very substantial and it is not a remaster like many of the re-released games its a completely remade game from the ground up that is very faithful to the original. I struggle to think of which persona game is my favorite 3 and 4 just occupy different parts in my mind, but P3 was the one persona game who's story blew me away.

This version while I would consider it to be the absolute definitive version of persona 3 is missing a couple things. One of the biggest omissions and one i was kinda confused about was Ryoji's social link. without spoiling anything, he is a really important character and I thought it was odd that he lost so much screen time. Many people were also annoyed at Femc not being in this game but I do understand that decision as the Femc route requires heavy reworking of the story and lots of new voice dialog, I would love to see it as a DLC maybe one day.

This version not only makes the game playable in more places but fixes a lot of the complaints about previous versions. One of the most annoying being in the original and FES, where you could not control companions and they sort of just acted on their own. P3P In my mind, though it has a ton of content with the Femc route is by far the worst version of the game. It does have all the quality of life improvements that it needed from later persona games and those are present in this version, but the horrible audio quality, bad interface, lack of cut scenes really makes it overall a downer.

So if you have never played P3, or even a persona game in general for that matter, and want to this is absolutely the way to do it in 2024. I can not recommend this game enough\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA faithful recreation of the base game with a few good bells and whistles added on, as someone that has played P3, P4, P5 and the Q games, I did enjoy this. Well worth the time and the investment, but like everyone has said, " The Answer " should have been base game instead of used to milk more money out of us. I am simply glad it is happening at all.

Anyway, onto the review.

Pros :

1. More Voiced Lines - Giving characters a voice that I had to imagine when playing Portable / Fes, was so good. I loved Junpei's new voice, Bebe's accent was > Chef's kiss! < and giving the other social links a voice made me love them more. Even the Moon social link. . .

The emotions I felt when hearing them sad or happy tear crying, it helped me connect with them in a way I never thought I could do like I did in P4.

2. Combat - I loved the super attacks, either getting me out of a jam or killing off a golden shadow that absorbed all elements / psychical skills. Bosses were meh, until you get to a certain point even on Merciless. One thing I wish was changed was the whole deal of needing Shuffle Time for any Yen gains, otherwiseI saw no real flaws in the combat.

3. Social Link Episodes - While I STILL want the FeMC's social links in Reload since she gets to hang out with the team, the episodes were a good compromise. And while I was at first annoyed that certain Personas were locked behind these, I ended up crumbling and enjoying them all.

[]

4. Remixes - Only two of them rubbed me the wrong way, but the rest were VERY GOOD. I definitely be adding a few to my work playlist.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAtlus finally did it. They made a (mostly) definitive version of Persona 3 in this remake. I've played both the original game and portable versions throughout the years leading up to this release. While I always enjoyed Persona 3 for the story it told, it's narrative moments, and it's characters; I had a hard time recommending it to people that haven't played it yet. The original game has some pretty serious flaws and design oversights. The most glaring ones being the uncontrollable party, the unbearable fatigue system, and there being no meaningful night activities for the whole second half of the game. Persona 3 portable fixed all these issues, and the fixes are returned in this remake, and expanded upon. The only complaint I have is no Female main character and her links, but most of the non link features made it in. This might bother purists, but the game has been Persona 5'd in terms of presentation and combat mechanics. Baton passes (shifts) and an improved version of showtime attacks/limit breaks from ff7, called theuragy, are wonderful additions to the combat IMO. The social aspect of the game also has persona 5 like additions. Players can now increase their social stats in many new ways, including: Hangouts, the net cafe, food spots. and other new activities. For new players who may have enjoyed 5 but couldn't get into the original P3 due to age and mechanical flaws, this game is perfect for them to experience a great narrative with excellent themes and character developments. For us old players, if you aren't opposed to minor changes, I think you will enjoy this as well. Nothing in the story is cut, and is in fact, expanded upon.
Overall, I heavily recommend to all demographics: series fans, new fans, and brand new players altogether. This is a very well put together and lengthy JRPG experience that anyone can enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t
