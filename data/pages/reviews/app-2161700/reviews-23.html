\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am truly baffled by this game. I assume this is more a money grab then anything. Sure as a remake I guess it’s pretty decent. However, I don’t know why anyone would buy this game. This has an extreme feeling of being a rough draft of Persona 5. It’s very hard to recommend a game that is just a worse version of Persona 5. I don’t doubt when it came out originally it was amazing…but now…I don’t see why you would play it unless you are so desperate for more Persona games that you are willing to play objectively inferior games.

When I say this game is a rough draft…I mean like literally. Character story arcs are literally identical, but worse versions, of story arcs in Persona 5. It’s kind of crazy…Mitsuru (P3)/Haru (P5), Kazushi (P3)/Ryuji (P5), ♥♥♥♥♥(P3)/Futaba (P5). You are also collecting all the same Persona’s as in Persona 5. The twists in the story are basically identical to the twists in Persona 5. Even the character designs are basically identical. It’s just painfully obvious when they made the masterpiece that is Persona 5 they were directly modeling off of this game. It’s not like the difference between different Final Fantasy games that might have similar themes and ideas…it’s just lifting entire characters and story beats.

The game also just doesn’t go far enough in fixing some just real stupid things. For one, you spend all your time in Tartarus…which is this extremely boring tower that doesn’t really change. You are just climbing this grinding levels in the most boring environment imaginable. Mementos in Persona 5 is a masterpiece in comparison. The enemy design is also some of the worst in any game…it’s just the same enemy design over and over and over again with just the color pallet changing. You are spending dozens of hours in this place and I think in total there are no more then maybe 10 enemy character models. It’s baffling…this game has like a hundred + persaons…but instead of having you fight models of those personas as enemies…they just refuse the same bland 10 enemy models.

While Persona 5 has the character dungeons which are all amazingly well designed, Persona 3 doesn’t. You only really level up in Tartarus. It’s like having to be stuck in Mementos for dozen of hours but it’s a worse version. The main game of Persona of walking around hanging out at school is at least fun and enjoyable…but unlike Persona 5 you really can’t hang out with everyone. Which I guess is ok, but I would say I ran out of time to complete probably half of all the character stories. In Persona 5 I think I missed 1. I just felt like the game was balanced pretty heavily against being able to level up character stories which is foolish as that is the only good part of this game.

I also felt the overall story had real problems, so Ikutsuki being the bad guy was pretty obvious from early on…you know…given that he is using children to fight monsters. However, putting that aside it is wild to me that as soon as he reveals himself as this evil mastermind…he is immediately killed. I mean sure I guess he was so stupid that I’m glad he didn’t stick around, but story wise it sure feels like an odd decision. As the game then tries to make Jesus man the central villain…but he’s pretty weak as a villain as well. The story also does not bother spending enough time explaining who these people are, what their motives are, and how they are apparently building this cult. While this is a rough draft of the story of Persona 5…it even feels like a rough draft of a rough draft. The story needed a lot more time to breathe and come together. The end result that death arrives to kill all of humanity for reasons and was apparently part of a science experiment just made very little sense even in the world of Persona.

One of the only things I liked was the ending with the main character dying. This game does handle loss extremely well, like it’s a central theme in all these stories. One of the social links is literally a dying man. I sure wish those stories were told in a better game…oh wait they are…it’s called Persona 5. Although Persona 5 basically goes with the optimistic conclusion for those stories instead of Persona 3 which is like nope you die, your friends die, basically most of your social links end up miserable.

Look, I had fun with this game…but I don’t think it’s a good game, and I certainly wouldn’t recommend it. The best parts of the game are hanging out with your friends…but the game puts up too many roadblocks to doing this and the rest of the game outside of hanging out with your friends is just extremely poorly handled. I find it absurd that this is a $75 game…it is absolutely not worth that, maybe $30…but you shouldn’t buy it you should just buy Persona 5 and if you already played that…well go play Baldur’s Gate 3 it’s far more worth your time than this.
