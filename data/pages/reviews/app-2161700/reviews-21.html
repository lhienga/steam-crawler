\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I say about Persona 3 that hasn't already been said? Probably not much, but we'll give it a shot.

Persona 3 is my favorite of the Persona games, and Reload is the best itteration of it. P3 isn't my favorite to play (that would be Persona 5 Royal) as even with the innovations P3R brings, it still has a number of gameplay issues that hold it back. But Persona 3's story, themes, and social links exist on a level that simply weren't replicated in future installments of the series, that more than make up for its other faults.

Reload adds some much needed changes to the original game: party member control, variance in Tartarus, and some more nighttime activities, but the core of the game remains the same: virtually all of your gameplay loop is social links during the day, and Tartarus at night. What's more, there are more levels of Tartarus than there are floors in all of Persona 5, so if you're not invested in the gameplay loop, the game can be a tough sell. If you're not interested in grinding through procedurally generated dungeon levels, I recommend watching the Persona 3 movies, though you won't get the same personal level of experience as playing through the game.

And in the end, that emotional core-about finding The Answer-is what is so essential to Persona 3 and what sets it above P4 and P5. If you're the type of player that doesn't really vibe with messages and themes-"games are games"-you may not get as enough out of P3 to make it worth your while. But if you're the kind of person who believe games can be art, then Persona 3 is a perfect example of how.\t\t\t\t\t
