\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI mean, just look at my play time, this game rules, just like the other persona games. I just finished 100% completing this game and I meant to write this on march 5th, as it is an important day for this game, no spoilers, just play the game to find out why. Ok, time for the review.

Persona is a series that blends social simulator elements with turn based rpg elements and does so masterfully. The gameplay loop of these games is always a joy to experience because if you're getting tired of the rpg aspect, just do some social activities to unwind.

Persona 3 reload, specifically, tackles the thematics of death, it's a very slow start, however once the plot gets going it's just one of the best, if not the best narratives the series has to offer.

I tried this game several times before it was remade, whether it be the FES version on PS2 or the Portable version on psp, I never was able to get into the game, however, with reload the main atraction of the game, the dungeon where you'll be participating in the rpg aspect, has been completely overhauled for the better. It now has breakable objects to help you make more money on your extracurricular escapades, the previous optional monad block has been overhauled to be optional monad doors that may appear from the 3rd block onwards and will give you extra helpful items that you can craft into weapons and armor and, lastly but certainly not the least, every block has it's own layout making it so that it is less boring to explore in general, once you start getting bored of a layout you'll probably be close to changing blocks and be met with a whole new way to explore tartarus. However, even though Tartarus this time around is much better than in the previous versions of the game, it still can't escape what it is, this is no persona 5 palace and, eventually, climbing the tower may become stale for some.

Good thing the game isn't only climbing Tartarus then. The social aspect of the game introduces you to many characters with different problems and backgrounds for you to explore and it's definitely the highlight of the persona games, to advance these you'll have to put yourselves in the characters' shoes and imagine what they want/need to hear in a certain situation to earn social points with them, earn enough social points by hanging out with one of your friends/classmates to advance towards the next rank. New to reload, however, are linked episodes, essentially social links for the male party members, and some other supporting characters that are story relevant, that weren't in the original, in these there are no answers that award you social points that help you further the link, instead these are time specific and offer you the characters' perspective to what is happening in the story, most of the time. These were, in my opinion, pretty well written and barely noticeable that they were new to this version.

To conclude, Persona 3 Reload gets a strong recommendation from me, once the plot picks up it's one of the hardest games to put down and you just want to see things through. For achievement hunters this game has a very satisfying completion journey, you can get every trophy in a single playthrough with enough planning and the only "hard" achievement to get is maxing out all social links in a single playthrough which is manageable to do if you see some guides on the internet and waste minimal time during daytime.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti really resonated with the original persona 3, since i played it during a really difficult time in my life. i'd just lost my mother from cancer, and my father was never the less distant towards me. i had no idea what i was doing, so i played on very easy and still managed to burn through my thirty free revives. i finished the game and only maxed like... maybe three social links? i was like, fourteen or something.

i played most of it again through P3P, girl's route maybe ~2016-2017 or so. resonated a lot more with it, having looked into the themes and overall message of the game. "don't waste a second of your life", "make every moment count", and i definitely vibe with that.

so, hearing P3 was going to get a full remake a few years ago, i couldn't have been more excited for this game. persona 4 and 5 are *great* games, by all means, but 3 was the one that was very special for me personally. and i will say with full certainty;

Persona 3 Reload is the best in the series. It lacks femc, and The Answer (for now teehee) and the new version of Mass Destruction isn't *great,* but in every other aspect it's a giant leap forward. If Xenoblade Chronicles 3 didn't exist, this would be my favorite JRPG of all time. This game is worth your $70, and 70 hours of your time. Persona 3 is a game that everyone struggling in life should experience, whether that be on the stomach ulcer-inducing Merciless mode, or on Peaceful mode just for the story and characters.

this game's message is a powerful one. we're all going to die one day, so it's up to us to make the most of every moment we have while we're still breathing. if you have someone special, whether that be a romantic partner, a friend, a family member, or even a pet, tell them what they mean to you.\t\t\t\t\t

I always wanted to play through Persona 3 FES, but just couldn't get into it purely because of the way the game played, having only played P5.
