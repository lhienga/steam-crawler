\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game never has a moment or in-game day or time to waste or that's a dull moment. Atlus once again delivers stellar presentation and while the core elements and story is the same there are some added features and gameplay to make it just different enough to not seem like a faithful remake. I do recommend playing this game on Normal or even Harder modes if you're a returning Persona Veteran because this game, once you've learned the ins and outs, can be quite cheesy and even trivialize battles. But at the same time, it does take some work and hard effort to reach that point.

I was indifferent about the the new VA but after getting through the stories and hearing them more, i've grown to like them, honestly even more than the originals. (Not saying either is flawed or bad) It's also worth noting that every single social link event-from level 1 to level MAX 10 in the game has fully voiced lines, making them a lot more engaging to hear them express themselves and become inherently more attached. I personally don't think there was a moment where I thought the voice acting was considered "bad". Please don't let the new cast put you off from enjoying the game amazing content; they're all great.Persona 3 Reload is also a really friendly game for newcomers wanting to try the Persona series.

Music overall is needless to say is exceptionally good. I enjoy faithful remixes of the original soundtrack and the added tracks while not that many are still absolute BANGERS. The sound mixing is also done just right where sound effect played during gameplay are just subtle enough to overtake the music and vice versa, especially in battle.

Story...I won't give anything away. Take your time and enjoy it. There's a lot and never a dull moment with it. Even coming from the original, expecting what I am supposed to see, it was amazing.\t\t\t\t\t
