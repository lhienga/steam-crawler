\r\n\t\t\t\t\tloved the absurdist themes in the game. if you dig deep enough, you'll be able to find plot holes you can poke through, but i think that takes away from the game a fair bit.

the use of death as a recurring motif works well with the message the game tries to bring across. the death arcana represents loss, death, and more importantly: renewal. the characters in this game experience loss, grief, and after the MC interacts with them via their social links, they have a newfound resolve to live.

death, in the practical sense, is finality. everything that lives is designed to end. with such a realisation, it is easy to take a defeatist approach and sink into despair. what is the point of living if it's going to end? the game says: life contains value exactly because it is finite.

it works, because existential themes are universal; they are a reflection of the human condition. there are many different responses, but the struggle to find meaning is shared. camus once said something along the lines of: there is but one serious philosophical problem, which is the decision to kill yourself or not. if anything, this game serves as a potent reminder of that.

my eyes feel heavy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tP3R is tonally and sonically inferior to the original. if you want to experience the story in its rawest form, go play P3FES. cutscenes, art direction, aesthetics, and visuals are way too "clean" or sanded off when compared to the original. many things, which made me tense or even made me cry in the original, had me cringing or laughing in P3R. the new instrumentals (idk music that well??) for the remixes in the soundtrack replace the old grungy MIDI sound, cool sampling, and whatever else the original had and make them boring and hard to listen to. however, the original songs for P3R are absolutely lovely, which confuses me. and then there's the hit-or-miss voice acting from the new main cast. although, the VA work from side characters like NPCs and social links are top-notch, which only adds to my confusion behind the quality control of certain things. despite all that, the gameplay itself is a huge improvement over the original in every aspect. navigating through tartarus remains enjoyable, theurgy's are a cool mechanic that should be a series mainstay, and the enhancements to battles make them arguably better than both the og P3 and P5. for story reasons, i do wish tactics were improved instead of being completely optional, but who cares. battling was still super fun on hard mode. i also loved the Link Episodes and the extra added content to flesh out characters that didn't have a SL. i don't think this game should've been $70, but since my grievances are "minor" and i loved the 100+ hours i put into this game, you unfortunately have secured my wallet for the eventual P1/P2 duology remakes

TLDR: music sucks, P3's edge is completely gone, but the base game + new additions were such a high point that i still had lots of fun and want to see more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing to keep this as vague as possible to avoid spoilers for absolutely everything, and just sharing my thoughts right after finishing the game.

P3R definitely feels more like a slow burn when comparing it to P4G, but what really sold it for me was the final arc of the game. Although it didn't have the silly goofy characters I hold so dear to my heart found in the Investigation Team, SEES was appropriately and amazingly written for the themes this game is going for, and has its own identity compared to my pre-established notion of what a Persona game is like.

Without getting into too many details, all I can say is Persona 3 Reload was a truly fantastic and possibly even life changing experience. The sound track is phenomenal and pairs exceptionally well to what is going on in the game, and I even listen to it regularly in my daily life. The characters, main cast, social links, and even random dialogues around town, are written to feel like genuine people, and it synergises with the darker themes shown. The gameplay is what you would expect from other Persona titles with some much needed quality of life improvements, so if you're a masochist like I am, and enjoy spending a ludicrous amount of time grinding, then the JRPG aspect of this game would be right up your alley.

By no means am I saying this game is without its flaws, but what masterpiece doesn't have flaws imposed on to them by the biased public? Kind of a pretentious thing to say in a Steam review of all things, but I wholeheartedly stand behind that. For instance, I absolutely hated every time I got insta-killed, having to regrind numerous sections of the game, but does that take away from the full experience? A little, but I can overlook it. It's negligible flaws like that which can potentially put some people off the game, but all I want to say with this rant is to STICK WITH IT. The payoff is well worth it if you want to experience the amazing story told in Persona 3.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSince it is Persona 3's Promised Day, I would like to write a review on this game.

"(Cause) we're living at the mercy of pain and fear, until we dead it, forget it and let it all disappear"

There is a timelessness in this game. Memento mori, as the game starts with, as it constantly reminds, as it provides us with other meanings through characters lenses, and as it tenderly closes with. I have grown to cherish the most sincere messages and the way they are framed through conversations that the protagonist has whether he's experiencing normal school life, wandering through the city during evenings, or between SEES teammates, or rather the "extracurricular" club.

The music is well-composed and timely placed, whether that be alleviating an awkward encounter, a mischievous deed, or even where words cannot be put together. I'm glad they brought Lotus Juice back in the studio and he really burned it down with the amazing-as-always performances.

In terms of graphics, Atlus took what they did well with Persona 5 Royal and perfected the craft even more in this as there are different shades through different school semesters. Once the game is finished, combining with the new loading music, the game provides you with a different loading screen and this alone plays and pulls some of my heart strings.

While old English VA's are presented in the game with other roles, the new voice cast has done an amazing job at delivering such unforgettable characters.

Persona 3 Reload delivers such a remarkable story that has withstood the test of time of the JRPG genre, for many remakes and many gaming console generations it has provided.

For my first 90-hour playthrough, sometimes, just like in life, the hardest part of ending is to start again.

March 6th, 2024 update: Atlus confirming the epilogue after Promised Day is one hell of a move.\t\t\t\t\t
