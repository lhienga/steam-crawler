\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo...I'm on about 9hrs now. So far I can say that it's pretty good. It's a remake from the original P3 game that came out eons ago so the questions are, how does it improve on the formula?

Well. Tbh I might actually like P5R a bit better in terms of gameplay. The UI is so overdone that it actually has screwed me over a few times. In P5R, i could navigate through every menu as fast as my fingers would allow me to. So I could do about 6 inputs in maybe 2 seconds. in P3R however, there's a severe delay between inputs so if you click 3 times in one ish seconds then the first command and the last command will be inputted but not the second which could result in a fatal misclick. I chose to play on hard difficulty and was punished cause my target switch command didn't input do to the UI's animation blocking further inputs until it was done whereas P5R conditioned me to just machinegun through menus. This UI delay has screwed me over several times. The game is basically telling you to play slowly regardless of how familiar with the UI you are. It drags out battles significantly and ofc, your other party members are totally worthless so if you die they just stand there with their thumbs up their asses, giving up on life and resigning themselves to death instead of...you know, chucking a revive at you like they can literally do at any other point while you're alive.

This outdated crap is severely annoying and it makes absolutely zero sense. Bad RNG basically can end a run which is exactly what happened. The game blocked my input due to the delay, i failed to switch targets and attacked the wrong enimy and then got one shot the turn after and my useless teammates committed seppuku.

In short, a lot of the major downsides are still in the game, maybe more so than before. The gameplay falls short of P5 just cause of that insane delay between actions.

Now, All of the original voice actors are here. Though bafflingly enough, Atlus gave them all the finger and told them they couldn't reprise their roles and instead got to play other characters close to each character instead. Now, this actually isn't a huge problem. All but one of the voice actors are really fantastic fits to replace the legacy characters so its not a major gripe...until Akihiko opens his mouth. It's incredibly jarring to hear. I'd understand and would be totally fine with it if Liam wasn't around to reprise his role...but he is. He's Kurosawa which just makes things infinitely more jarring. What is the thought process behind this baffling decision?

The music: 9/10. Lotus Juice is, ofc, still here. If they did what they did to him what they did to the voice cast, there would be blood im sure as he's basically synonymous with Persona 3.

Overall, so far, It's good. my problems with this game are nitpicky. The game is crazy expensive though. I'd advise waiting for a sale. All of its DLC should be base game adds since its just recycled content from previous games and its even poorly implemented in some cases. I don't think this game is worth it's asking price ASSUMING you've already played the original. If you haven't though, then its probably very worth it

This is my 9hr ted talk.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore P3Reload, I think the most of the Persona community would say the following when comparing the games or discussing which one is the 'best': "3 has the best story, 4 has the best characters, 5 has the best gameplay"; that was such a popular thing to say it outright reached community meme status. But jokes aside, it did have a lot of truth in it.

With P3R, I'm expecting the majority discourse to change into people just saying P3R is the best.
The story is the essentially the same as before, with only minor changes; it's about as faithful a Remake as you can make it in that regard.
The characters, especially the main ones from SEES, are now given more moments to shine in Linked Episodes and Dorm activities, and romance is no longer forced when hanging out with the female links.
The gameplay is slightly improved from P5R, and it's not even a contest between it and the original P3/P3FES/P3P.
P3R is just so, so wonderful. It's borderline perfect.

My main criticisms of it would be that sometimes it is too faithful to the original. The difference in the quality of writing between most of the old Social Links and new Linked Episodes is staggering, the new stuff is so much better that, without context, you would just assume they just didn't feel like putting in effort on those Social Links. I was also hoping they would take some inspiration from the movies in how to present some parts of the story, but that was not the case; not that any of it is bad, quite the contrary, I just think the movies had some neat ideas that could have made their way here too. Also, the pacing could have been improved, but it still has the same problems as the original in that regard. Furthermore, there are some events/Links which are very missable, and that can be a little frustrating, especially for new players.

Going back to the positives though, just to list a few:
- Theurgy is beyond awesome. I almost find it hard to describe how brilliant I think they really are. And it's not just in terms of power that I mean that, either. Sometimes, it can be hard for game developers to get players to "act" how they would want, in the sense that they had something in mind when designing something, but players just don't use it that way. Theurgy is an amazing way to reinforce what they expect each character to play like. Akihiko fills his Theurgy bar when he is buffed? Better buff him a lot then. Yukari fills her Theurgy bar when healing? She will be our dedicated savior. It rewards playing to each characters strength, and goes along perfectly with their personality. I could gush about how great this is for a very long time. Suffice to say, I find it brilliant, and hope to see more similar mechanics in the future.
- All Social Links and Linked Episodes are voice acted. This makes such a huge difference in my opinion. It really helps to convey the emotions of each character. Special shout-out to the best social link in the whole Persona series, the Sun.
- The voice acting is great. Persona is the only series I play dubbed, and everyone should be applauded for their performances here. Special shout-outs from my side to the VAs of Akihiko, Mitsuru, Junpei and the protag. They killed it. The moments that I was looking forward to the most in the game were the pivotal moments for these characters, and the VAs did not disappoint me in the slightest; their performances were masterful. Also, I have to point out that even secondary character had amazing performances. Yuko sounded incredible, just perfect for her character. The Sun Social Link, again, was also fantastic. I could keep going on.
- The songs are also all remade, and they're all either as good as or even better than the originals, which is saying a lot.
- The presentation is fantastic too. So stylish. My one complaint there would be that shops don't feel as good as they did in P5R, but still, that's very minor. The rest is all as you would expect from the same developers, and it works great.

I could keep going; point being, P3R is pretty accessible, and merges almost all the qualities from the previous games into one masterwork. I do think for newcomers P5R might still be my number 1 recommendation, but P3R is giving it a run for its money.
Objectively speaking, I don't believe that P4G needs a remake at all, but I have to say, if this is what we can expect from ATLUS remakes, can't say I wouldn't be happy to see it. But, of course, I still would rather have them work on P6.
Looking forward to what comes next!\t\t\t\t\t
