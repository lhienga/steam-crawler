\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved the game, but for me, does not outshine Persona 5 Royal. One thing I enjoyed about this game that wasn't apart of the other two was that it was more fast paced (but perhaps, that can also makes it less intense and impactful), you didn't have annoying cut scenes after establishing a new social link (like P5), you don't have to sit through 2-3 hours of dialogue before you get back into the action. It felt smooth and the flow was satisfying, but also made it go by so fast.

Compared to the old game, this newer addition made this game the easiest Persona game I've played (even on hard move) but perhaps I'm just a better Persona player now after playing all of their games. The final boss was honestly braindead easy but cool, initial reaper fight was harder. The graphics and music remain to be top tier, I enjoyed Color Your Night the best.

As for the story, it is wholesome and meaningful, characters are lovable and the ending makes the whole idea of school a beautiful time in life for them.\t\t\t\t\t

This game was extremely relevant in my formative years, and it held a special place in my heart ever since I payed it to completion. These are the core messages of Persona 3 Reload. Thank you Atlus, for remind us what it means to be alive.\t\t\t\t\t

and the social links are still this game's strongest point for me, especially now that all of them are voiced.
The way they are presented, the problems they are going through,
and how you help them live with their struggles is very down-to-earth and believable.
I also really like the fact that you don't just magically fix their lives,
instead you simply give them someone to be with them during their hardest times.

The voice acting overall is superb and really helps you to immerse yourself in the game.
Even though I knew the story, during the most critical moments it still gave me teary eyes,
because the VAs did such a good job.

I would recommend it to every fan of JRPG's, in particular if you want to play a game that isn't all happy-go-lucky
and is more on the darker/realistic side.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t 100+ Hours, 2 Playthroughs, 100% Achievements Completed

Persona 3 Reload is a magnificent RPG experience in its own right.
I started craving for JRPGs since December and after finishing Persona 5 Royal, this game just happened to be launching around the corner. In anticipation for P3R's release, I started familiarizing myself with the PS2 FES version of the game and after getting a sense of both directions of these games, I say P3R had definitely exceeded my expectations for a remake title of a beloved classic.

The remake took many aspects of the original title and basically enhanced it without trying to stray itself too far from the original. Most of these changes I would argue are improved and well implemented that made the game more newcomer friendly for those who are not familiar with the franchise. In a sense, the remake accomplished its job of trying to be a well crafted entry for newcomers and veterans.

However, I must object to say that P3R is one of the many examples of SEGA/ATLUS' taken advantage of the consumer. I dislike their business methodology of selling parts of the content such as The Answer to help garner further revenue streams. P3 FES, a PS2 title from '07 was a complete package and now we're expected to purchase a part of that content down the line if we want to have the full experience of the game.

Regardless, Persona 3 Reload is certainly worth the cost on its own. The narrative and its characters are uniquely interesting and no doubt you will find yourself deeply invested throughout the game. I never found myself bored nor dissatisfied during my two playthroughs and that in itself was a huge improvement after Persona 5 Royal. If you're already a fan of JRPGs and you haven't touch this game yet, I highly reckon you do so.\t\t\t\t\t
