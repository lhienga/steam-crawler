\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersona 3 already was my favorite game of all time and I can proudly say that this is one of, if not the best remake ever. I don't want to talk much about the original P3 content, it's peak. It has some of the greatest emotional scenes in video games and the feelings of hopelessness and dread is portrayed beautifully with the atmosphere. Great cast of characters with good writing (which was improved in the remake.)

But what I actually want to focus on is the remake itself. As a huge persona 3 fan myself, it's unbelievable to me that I'm saying it but they really improved on basically every aspect of the original game. Most notably of course Tartarus, it's a lot less "boring" now. While I always liked Tartarus, it does get extremely repetitive at points especially when grinding for xp. The OG visuals didn't really help this case either. Now Tartarus looks and feels much more lively and gets boring much slower. That being said, grinding is a part of this game (especially on harder difficulties) still and if you do want to do most of the content then yeah, Tartarus will still get a bit boring late-ish game. The addition of Theurgy makes combat more fluid and more exciting as well, helping the grinding a bit.

Outside of combat and beautiful visuals however, the day to day life and the story shines the most. While everyone expected the Tartarus to be better than the OG, I personally would have never guessed that they could make the characters better. The cast was already great but this remake helps characters that didn't have as much spotlight previously by a ton. And these added scenes and interactions are some of the best social interactions in any Persona game. Characters are very well written and once the game really kicks off, you'll feel attachment to all of them.

As for anyone who hasn't played P3 before, this still is the best starting point. Yes it doesn't have the Answer (Yet) but every special bit of interaction they've added more than makes up for it.

All in all this game is a love letter for old fans, every single detail clearly done with care. It's by far the best looking and feeling persona game. Everything that made the original is improved upon. By far, the best game in the series. Amazing remake.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing from someone who has played P4G, P5R, P5, and P4 and tried P3 FES and P3P. Also a minmaxer who tries to 100% the whole game in one run.
This is for people who are coming from Persona 5 and are kinda unfamiliar with Persona 3's systems
also includes a short rant about minor UI and OST stuff.

TLDR; If you don't mind the missing content (FEMC and The Answer) it's the best way to experience Persona 3, but it might not be your favorite Persona game.

Social life
It is definitely a lot more forgiving than the original, P4G, and in my opinion even P5 and P5R. While it is still not a game I would say is possible for a newbie w/o a guide to 100%, I can imagine someone who came from the P5 craze and who is familiar with the systems to have an easy or manageable time doing so.

There were some changes with dialogues in certain scenes but I think it's still overall faithful to the original. Yeah you can nitpick some Aigis or Akihiko lines or whatever, but overall the characters are still the same to me. (except Yukari; her VA is way less '♥♥♥♥♥♥' with her delivery of lines which made me stop hating her)

Tartarus
Ok honestly, without revamping it I do not know how you can beat the Palaces in P5. Yes there were quality of life reworks like your party members don't get too tired and then become unwilling to continue with the dungeon-ing and there are clocks around to replenish HP and SP for some fragments and of course controllable party members. All they did was make the climb up less stressful, not more fun. Also there's no Ryuji insta-kill passive equivalent here, so you will take longer to farm up.

Combat
THE CRIT BUFFS ARE INSANE JUST BUILD ONE PHYSICAL DMG MACHINE AND ONE ALMIGHTY NUKE.

Theurgies are a cool addition that (I think) add some more depth to the combat that isn't 'superficial.' What I mean is that it doesn't become obsolete late game like ailments or whatever because bosses are just immune. The party members get stuff like a partywide charge/focus/heal or a magical attack with 'severe' motion value of their element. Since lategame fights are more buff/debuff centric this mechanic ages rather well.

Velvet Room and Personas
The Velvet Room is basically the same thing with the P5 installments but stripped down (it just has dyad and special fusions and fusion search). The personas don't have traits anymore (I will miss Vitality of the Tree).

Others
I have nothing bad to say about the UI except the wallet in the pause menu. It just looks like an MS Word text box and looks so out of place, how did that happen?! Why is there an extra menu upon entering the Velvet Room before seeing the fusion screen???? Just put me straight there and make me exit by pressing C (on PC) and X or circle on Dualshock controllers. Why do the border floor teleporters give me no choice but to return to the first floor????????

The OST in my opinion is an overall upgrade from the original. The exceptions being Iwatodai Dorm and Mass Destruction. For both of these songs my main gripe is the vocals. In my honest opinion they were better off untouched, the unique tone of the Iwatodai Dorm vocals were part if its identity for me. The sharp vocals of the original Mass Destruction were part of the appeal to me as well.\t\t\t\t\t
