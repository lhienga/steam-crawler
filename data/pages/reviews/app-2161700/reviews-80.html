\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I start with anything here, I will make a note that this is my first full experience with persona 3. I've seen a few memes before, but I had essentially no connection to the game-play or story before playing reloaded.

Persona 3 Reloaded is a great experience, and more importantly an approachable one.

While I could have easily played p3p or emulated the superior p3 fes, I specifically waited for this remake as persona 5 was my introduction to the persona game-play. Going in without the quality of life adjustments they've made over the years would have been a nightmare, and I'm happy to report that this was an approachable experience that I only found a few gripes with.

Being the not very gamer person that I am, I played a majority of the game on peaceful mode while using a guide in order to 100% the game by the end without having to run through a second time. Other than a few mild spoilers from the guide, this did not detract from my experience whatsoever- though this is because I don't really value the combat intricacies as much as some other people I know. Peaceful mode made running through Tartarus a breeze, though I do highly recommend turning up boss difficulty back up to hard so you can at least experience their gimmick before steamrolling them. Perhaps persona 6 will have an adjustable difficulty setting, where I can make the bosses more difficult while making the grinding easier and faster- but that's probably asking for a lot. Bottom line is that I was able to tailor the game experience to what I wanted it to be, and was able to enjoy the full 90 hours that I put into it.

My few gripes and nitpicks

While I think the whole of persona 3 reloaded is great, I think the weakest point of it is definitely the main dungeon of the game- Tartarus. To me it definitely feels like the prototype dungeon that they would eventually refine into the curated palaces of persona 5. There's not much they could really do to change it here, as Tartarus was a pretty fundamental part of the whole experience,

Shuffle time and the major arcana system- more relics of the original game coming out when it did- are where I draw most of my ire. Going from the holdup system in p5 where I could see the personas available to me and choose who I want to add to my collection to being completely random was not a great experience. Part of that blame falls on me, where being on peaceful disincentives me to go for all out attacks which at least always give you a shuffle time, but that just makes the act of grinding significantly less interesting when most of the time you aren't rewarded for all the battles that you do. And the major arcana system just sucks. A few good buffs that are thrown into a pool of useless garbage, meaning you have to hope and pray you aren't forced to take cards you don't want before you get something like the Lovers card (which is a huge buff to your persona fusions in the mid game). You could probably shave off 5-10 hours of my grinding if I didn't have to fish for these dumb major aracana buffs. I'm glad p5 did without them and moved those buffs to your social links instead.

All in all Tartarus is acceptable, but not great. It's painful at the worst of times, and braindead at the best of times. The gatekeepers are fine, and I like the quips your navigator makes during the Monad passages, where the bosses use the stupidest gimmicks to the point even the characters are annoyed. Speaking of quips though, the notes your party make during exploration are pretty grating. They're always limited to 1 call and 1 response, so even if they were going to say something interesting, that thread just gets dropped before it can be elaborated on. And god forbid you bring the dog with you, they will never shut up. No option to turn that off, unless you want to play solo- which is definitely an option and cool that it is.

Social links are hit or miss

My last real gripe with persona 3 reloaded are the social links. Some of them are fairly good, but most of them are either lackluster or simply a nothing-burger. And since the only rewards you get for social links are fusion bonuses and a persona unlock at the end, it feels a lot less rewarding than the system in persona 5.

Without going in to too much detail, some of the standout links to me were Moon, Justice, and Sun. Some of the meh ones were Hermit, Star, Fortune, and pretty much all the other romance options. Emperor and Chariot ended up being my least favorite, with Emperor specifically feeling like a rushed mess. A lot of that is subjective and you might vibe with different characters in different ways, but I do still feel like the social links were not the greatest. I do think they achieve the goal of making you more connected to everyone in the end, and the wrap-ups you get after the final boss are all really touching.

The addition of linked episodes for your guy friends is also kind of cool, though I feel like many of them are so spread out that it can be hard to follow the plot of them. /Part of that might have been from the guide I was following, but most of the final episodes don't unlock until the final month anyway so that still spans basically the whole game. They probably should have just been social links, but that would limit your outside connections a bit so perhaps that is why they are not. Hard to tell given how afraid Atlus are of gay people, apparently/allegedly.

Definitely worth playing, if you are interested in Persona

At the end of the day, persona 3 was a turning point in the persona series. It was able to introduce the demon compendium and strategic gameplay to so many people when it released, and it holds a huge amount of nostalgia for those that played it when it came out. Having that story that touched the hearts of so many available to be experienced by so many new players who now don't have to deal with the chunkiness of a ps2 or psp game is fantastic. It's not a perfect game, it's not even a perfect persona game, but I don't think it needs to be. We still have persona 5 royale right here on the same platform now. What matters is that we can all feel that our hearts have been touched by the protagonist of persona 3, and get to see what the whole series evolved from. The story here is not perfect, in fact I think we spend way too much time in persona 3 not even really having a story. And even when we do each part of it feels like it gets put on the back-burner, until we realize that we only have so many back-burners available on our stove. But it all wraps up nicely in one of the most touching final moments of a persona game that I've experienced. Kino, as the kids these days seem to say.

So, I do recommend Persona 3 Reloaded. If you're on a budget, get it on gamepass while they have it available there, you can beat it in a month if you put your mind to it. I think $70 is a bit steep for a game these days, but you will get your money's worth out of this one.\t\t\t\t\t
