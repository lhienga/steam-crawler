\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL; DR: I've played the original game as well FES, and I can say for certain that Persona 3 Reload improves everything the original title got right.

__________________________________

Persona 3 is one of my favorite games ever, and to this day it's been the only game to ever make me cry like a little kid at the end. I have a strong emotional connection with this title, its characters and especially the music. It was THE game that put Persona's name in the map and it was the last game in the series that still had a bit of the Shin Megami Tensei flavor with its story and themes.

THE GOOD

✅ A faithful 1:1 remake of the original game in every single aspect.

✅ A simple, yet very engaging storyline with interesting, well written characters. In a weird way it reminds me a bit of Evangelion, somehow.

✅ Combat is also fundamentally simple but carries a lot of depth with the elemental affinities and Personas. The party members have been completely rebalanced and got new abilities, called Theurgies (essentially Specials), as well bonus passive skills. Pretty much every character is viable now (though sadly Mitsuru fell behind compared to the rest of the cast).

✅ Tons of quality-of-life changes from the original game across the board. Way too many to listen, but it makes the whole difference.

✅ The soundtrack is absolutely stellar, as you'd expect from Shoji Meguro. The re-arranged tracks sound as good as the original, and I dare to say this is Meguro's magnum opus; his use of leitmotivs for Persona 3's soundtrack is outstanding and unmatched.

✅ Both the EN and JP voices are great. The EN cast really nailed the role and they sound even better than the original cast from the PS2 era. Also, Tara Platt, Yuri Lowenthal and Liam O'brien return as support cast to voice secondary character (Platt reprises her role as Elizabeth).

✅ This game has an impressive attention to detail in the smallest things. Such as every electrical object being seen connected to an outlet in the dorm. Or how idle animations of the party members in the dorm are filled with personality.

✅ New activities and fully voiced events with party members.

✅ The stylization that Persona 5 brought to the series is reflected in this game beautifully. Menus and UI are gorgeous to look at and still inject the vibe of Persona 3 without looking too much like Persona 5.

✅ The celshading is pretty spot on to give the whole game this very anime feel.

✅ Koromaru best Persona mascot ever.


THE BAD

❌ Very limited, nearly non-existent graphics customization settings. Also, there is no way to remove motion blur, and turning on reflections will overheat your GPU if it doesn't support raytracing.

❌ Graphically speaking this game is a bit of a mixed bag. While the main character models and renders look really nice, many backgrounds lack detail, use low-res textures and look a bit bland. In addition many of the models used for the Personas look a bit unpolished. The simplification fits the artstyle, as it's not trying to achieve realistic graphics, and I'm perfectly fine with, but I can't help to think this game sometimes looks two generations older.

❌ As much as I adore this game I think it should've been 60 USD; it's not worth the 70 USD to be very honest.

❌ Paid DLC on top of the already high price tag is a bit insulting. Sure, the DLC consists of Personas and costumes from other games, but the fact those aren't instead unlockables leave a bad taste in your mouth. SEGA just being greedy.

❌ This game doesn't include The Answer, which was a "sequel" to the main story included in Persona 3 FES. Knowing SEGA, they'll probably make it as DLC and overprice it too, and then re-release the game 2 years later with The Answer bundled, Persona 5 Royal style. Edit: Atlus just revealed that The Answer (AKA Episode Aigis) is on the way as DLC for September 2024, as we guessed.

__________________________________

Overall, even with my peeves, I absolutely loved this remake from start to finish and got my money worth. The ending hit me as hard as it did playing the original game; I cried like a little kid.

I've seen a lot of people complaining about the removal the female protagonist, but I'm personally okay with that. Her exclusion doesn't hinder the experience and makes the male protagonist stand out a bit more compared to the ones from Persona 4 and Persona 5.

If you liked Persona 5 you'll definitely enjoy this game, even though it's simpler in execution. It's not as flashy and stylish but it has consistency and very relatable characters.\t\t\t\t\t
