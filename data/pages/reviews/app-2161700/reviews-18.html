\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished today! I thoroughly enjoyed this game; highlights include the OST (baby baby, dada da daaa da da da daaa) and the story, both of which packed a serious punch. However, I felt like the game was poorly paced, especially during December/January where you're left with nothing to do during evening segments. Another criticism I have is that there are no social links for the male members of the S.E.E.S. and there is thus little character development for, for example, Ken and Akihiko outside of the main storyline's exploration of their relationship with Shinjiro . I'd also like to mention in passing this game's continuation of the Persona series-wide problem with tasteless tropes that undermine the themes of the gamesâ€”if you know, you know.

I know it's somewhat trite at this point to compare this game to Persona 5, but I think that despite the excellent restyling of the UI, updated graphics, and addition of new mechanics like Theurgy , this game seems to only on the surface level possess the polish that I felt even the base version of Persona 5 did. The Shift mechanic feels inferior to the Baton Pass system not only because Baton Pass is more potent (one might argue, in Royal, game-breakingly so) but also because it feels less fluid and satisfying in a way that I'm not able to fully put my finger on. One more gripe I have with combat is that, unlike in Persona 5, when selecting a Shadow to target with an attack it doesn't say if the Shadow resists that attack type. Lastly, people who are used to Persona 5's heavily curated Palace experiences might be disappointed to find that in Reload fighting takes place exclusively in randomly generated spaces analogous to Mementos. This wasn't an issue for me but may prove to be for others.

Overall, though, I would recommend this game to players if it goes on sale for 50, maybe 40 dollars. Despite a heart-wrenching yet inspiring story, one agidyne tracklist, and a cast chock-full of believable characters that you'll love to root for, I can't help but feel like this JRPG is seriously, seriously hindered by its pacing (I don't know if I emphasized this enough, it's REALLY bad).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I can confidently say that this is the definitive way of experiencing this game. It is a masterpiece. Plain and simple. This is a game you will play and you will never forget it. The story, the characters, the message that this game tells. It's superb.

Practically every problem I had with P3P, the version I had played, has been addressed. To the fixing of the monotony that was exploring Tartarus, to the adjustment of the immense unnecessary difficulty that the early game had, to the new quality of life changes, beautiful visuals and so much more...

It's not perfect, of course, as there were a few things that... Aren't as good as they could've been. Like, some of the new animated cutscenes lack a bit of the impact that the originals had, 1 or 2 of the new VAs didn't hit quite as well as the original cast, some of bosses were just a bit too easy, along with some other nitpicks I had. But, by no means does that lessen the greatness of this game.

This is, in my opinion, a must play for anyone. It is incredible, and it's an experience with no low points that will keep you engaged until the very end and make you rethink many things about your life after getting through it.\t\t\t\t\t
