\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally finished this game, despite playing the literal minute it released. Reason I stopped early, was because of the music change. I played through persona 4, persona 5, and so persona 3 music hopped into my recommendations. I've heard some of it. So some things stuck out to me, like the new moon reaching out, mass destruction, and now the ending song. Felt worse than the original. Luckily modders have been working out it since launch, and through their efforts I was able to replace whichevers ones I wanted with the OG. But comparing them now, the majority of the new remixes in Reload I believe are better than the OG. One example, changing seasons, amazing. Master of shadow being another. The new songs are great too. Full moon is great. Color your night is amazing. Its going down now may be the best. Music is such a huge factor in my enjoyment of a game, and the Persona series has plenty of it.

Despite my need to mod the game, I think its worthy of its current overwhelmingly positive rating. I recommend it as well. The price is pretty high, and there are valid concerns about it. This is a remaster of an existing game. The story is the same, and therefore it can still feel outdated. I'm talking about stuff to do daily. Persona is all about time management and planning ahead. Do you spend more time grinding battles before you leave? Do you calculate how much social skill gain you will get passively so that you can stop working on it? I started playing this blind, but not so blind. I've seen a couple of comments. I read about how you want to focus on all school social links first. And it didnt hit me until a bit later to start doing that when I started to suspect I've been losing too much time. If I wanted to max out all social links, I'd have to dedicate every single social link hang out on getting the maximum amount of points. Having to pick the best answer. Even when being tempted with wrong answers or even troll answers. Reload introduces this new feature called Rewind where you are given the option to go back in time. Basically this is just for people who were too lazy to save. Because its just autosaves, ranging from same day to a week ago. The problem is that if I want to go back after I picked a funny answer I'd have to sit through or speed through the rest of the conversation, basically spoiling myself of the content for the next time I do it the best way.

There is the problem. Every day its the same thing. Check schedule. Carry persona of the social link im going to meet. Run to the shrine and grab my free skill card copy. THEN I can go do the hangout. Every night its the same thing. Which social skill am I lacking? Better go level that up. Did I grind plenty of battles to get money? I guess I can splurge a bit more of the more expensive options to work on social skill. Which was probably pointless in the end, as I maxed out all my social skills with like 50 days to spare. In the end, I was just able to finish my last social link on the second to last day. I was excited cause I'd be able fuse the ultimate persona. Only I couldnt, because there are hidden personas unlocked that you cannot get unless you may out the new but not actually social links of the male party members. I've done a couple, I got no stat rewards. I figured it was a just a bonus story episode. Didnt finish any so no persona for me I guess haha. The only way to have maxed out everything is if I followed a strict guide from the start to maximize time on everything, therefore spoiling myself and making the game less fun. I already had to do some of that for the right social link choices so it became tedious.

The gameplay itself, was pretty good I guess. Not much different from how Persona 4 and 5 went from what I remember. Basically you want to stat buff yourself, stat debuff the enemy, and go for weakness when you can. All the while preserving hp and sp when able. But that becomes irrelevant in the later months. Because you start to have access to hp and recovery passives. So then, you can technically go infinite by revisiting the 1st few levels and grinding weak shadows. My other problem is how once you decide to stick with your preferred team members, you get pushed into keeping them forever. The game has a new catchup mechanic for leveling but thats not the problem, the problem is the shuffle bonuses. Giving your current team members permanent hp and sp bonuses makes them stronger and helps grind faster. It becomes a loop, so then youre kinda stuck with your core 3/4 members. Anyone extra just gets left behind. I tried maxing out everyone else by reaper farming but in the end without the bonuses they all felt weaker and I just made myself overleveled by reaching 99. I set the game to hard which was all I could do since I am locked out of merciless. Didnt even bother creating a full resist/immune persona. Final boss wasnt an issue. But I was scared I'd get hit with an instant kill spell and would have to redo that whole slog.

I think that the earlier events/bosses were pretty fun. And the later bosses were just boring being either gimmics or stat checks. But thats all turn based boss battles isnt it? If its too strong you were underleveled or unprepared. Too weak you were overleveled or fully prepared. Still, I had fun. My ingame time is 90 hours to completion, but my steam time shows 150 hours. 60 hours of leaving the game running in the background when I'm doing something else, or just paused and sitting at the menu listening to the music.

If this remake hadnt come out, I wouldnt have played Persona 3. And I'm glad its out, and I did, because now I can understand all the references made to the other games in the series. And I can finally start getting into the p3 and p4 spinoff games for more. One last thing, the digital premium version says it includes a dlc pack, but it explicitly states all of that dlc. Its just a bunch of costumes and personas. It doesnt mention anything about the newly released expansion pass because that didnt exist at launch. The people being mad about this claiming they were scammed just can't read.\t\t\t\t\t
