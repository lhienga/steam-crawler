A beautiful experience that teaches you (over the course of sitting down and playing a video game in isolation for 100+ hours) the importance of connecting with others and to live your life to the fullest.

The Good
Jokes aside, this game is incredible. The story, characters, visuals, sound design and soundtrack (oh god the soundtrack) are all stellar. I don't get emotionally moved very often while playing story-based games but this game made me tear up multiple times.

A significant chunk of the main cast in this game make up my favorite characters between P3-P5 and the couple characters that I had a lukewarm opinion of during my first playthrough have shifted to be way more favorable on a 2nd playthrough.

The visuals are great, the character models look significantly better than 3 & 4's (which makes sense, this is the newest release after all) and the combat animations and visuals (especially those sweet, sweet cut-ins) combined with the sound design and the banger soundtrack elevate the combat a lot.

As a more controversial note, I play Dubbed and absolutely loved the English VA cast. I think they did a fantastic job and I can't really think of anyone that felt like a miscast. I do understand the perspective of the people that played the original versions of P3 and are upset about the recasts, but in spite of that, I still think the new VAs did the characters justice (This was my first time playing P3 so I don't have that same connection with the old voice cast, so feel free to dismiss my opinion on that if you so choose).

The Not So Good
The only pain points I have are that some of the Social Links are duds (which they were still figuring the system out with 3, so I can give it a pass), not being able to save or access your on-hand personas to dismiss them / rearrange skills in the Velvet Room is a slog and started to drive me insane later on, and that the way SEGA's handling the DLC is awful (shocker, I know).

Final Verdict
Basically, it gets a 9/10 with a badass seal of approval, and I will now be basing my personality around the soundtrack for the next 6 months.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTried Persona 3 Portable. Didn't really like it, especially the S.Link events and story felt bland and boring.
Gave Persona 3 Reload a chance anyways.
It was wonderful.

The combat was clean and stylish.
The difficulty was fair. I got my ass kicked many times in hard difficulty, and I actually had to manage my budget.
The S.Link events were much, much better in Reload. I actually enjoyed the S.Link stories in P3R more than P4G and P5R, especially when I loved the whole P4G and P5R cast.
The voice acting was perfectly executed.
The music is awesome. I had a blast jamming to the overworld themes and battle themes.
The story is beautiful. I used to think the story was inferior in P3, but that was prolly because of bad sound quality and voice acting that I had experienced in Portable. Also having proper cutscenes in Reload made me actually care for the characters.
The game's message and ending is much, much more powerful than P4G and P5R.

Is this the best game of all time?
No.
Is this my personal best game of all time?
Absolutely.
Call it recency bias if you want, but this game really left a heavy impact on me irl.

If you had never played a Persona game before, I actually highly suggest you start on Persona 5 Royal first, it'll help you get used to the unique style of Persona games.
If you have played a Persona game but not Persona 3, you should start with Reload.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first got into Persona series with 5, everything got a lot more stylish and a more mainstream audience enjoyed it while some older fans described it as style over substance. I later tried Persona 4 Golden, a very good story and dungeon crawl with only a few upgrades from it's previous release. Persona 3 has always been difficult for newer fans to get into with it's age, many different releases and difference in gameplay. Reloaded solves a lot of the problems though may also change things it didn't need to. The visuals are amazing, on par with P5 but maybe not as in your face along with more focus on character dialogue. Gameplay is tuned up but some mechanics like ally tiredness or how weapons work are completely left out and newer features like passing a weakness hit or Thergy special attacks can make the game much easier. Some may also argue the cutscenes don't capture the tone as well but as someone who just saw it as the third wheel of the series, the story is still fantastic and gripping and may have some of the best team members in the series. People familiar with P3 will see a lot different but can live with it for the added polish, and fans of 4 and 5 will more easily be able to get into the story.

Overall compared to the others I've checked out and have HD versions: Social Links range from touching with usually 1 important question that needs to be answered and some I just felt frustrated with them since I know the right course of action and it's not being a yes man to them. Combat is very similar to other Persona games where you're encouraged to hit weaknesses, the bosses are fairly difficult but even with a disadvantage on one party member they're possible with proper levels and clever play. Personas are varied and fun to collect or see old favorites. I've already said the story is very moving and is taken a bit more serious than P4, but for some it's still kind of edgy. Not saying the guns aren't on theme, but that doesn't change they still use guns to summon their powers. Social skills are still a pain to adapt to but luckily there's more party related things to do during night hours and it's fairly easy to get a majority of the Social Links. I am not in the group who would be annoyed at remembering things differently or having some interesting gameplay choices just dropped for another Persona 5, but even from that group I've heard they're happy to see everything get the polish it's needed. The only issue now is from here there's P4 Golden, a step back in graphics and more chummy tone, and P5R which dumps all the dlc at once and might come off as shallow at times.\t\t\t\t\t
