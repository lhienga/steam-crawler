\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersona 3 Reload is a game that takes the concept of the series and perfects those aspects to it's max. This game, I think, could be picked up by anyone, no matter their experience or taste in RPG's and still have a blast with it.

As any persona game, it makes you really care about their characters, you want to see them improve, and seeing them get out of their personal struggle is something quite satisphactory and wholesome most of the time. And to be honest most of the characters in this game are more fleshed out than most in other games in the series, also because they don't solely depend on you to become better, other people will help them or even they themselves will come to a realization, just like in real life. Oh and the new activities you can do with your team just improves what I already had said, it shows you their normal life, just acting like normal, real people. Oh and the animations of the characters are stunning, when they are eating you can see them actually eat, unlike the lifeless automatons most NPC's in videogames tend to be. Also the facial expressions are insane, I thought P5 was the peak of how characters could express themselves, but looking at the characters here, specially in battles, is amazing.

The graffics and UI are some of the things I most enjoyed in the game, and as a matter of fact is kinda what sold me on the game to begin with since the trailer first released. Many times I would just pause the game and watch the menu, just thinking how cool it was and how the managed to make something 3D look so much like 2D. And I think they perfected P5's aesthetic here; if you barely noticed it was 3D, here you have to actually stop and analyse if it's actually 2D or 3D. Also, the transitions between battles or the times of day are amazing, they improved compared to what we had in P5.

The music is also one of the best things of the game (As in any persona) That hip-hop-y feel the original had, mixed with the instruments that P5 uses made one of the best videogame OST's of all time. Also the new additions to the soundtrack are amazing, being "Full moon full life" and "Color your night" my favorites of the game and the series overall.

I might have to give it some time to cool down but, as far of games I've played of persona, overall my favorite would be this one. I enjoyed this game to the last single second and I'm already excited to begin my next playthrough. I would recommend this game to anyone who is interested wholeheartedly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Persona 3 itself is an amazing game, the remaster is not the best it could be with many great features being removed from the game. Obviously it is the overall best game for any newcomer to persona 3 to get their hands on BUT it feels very rushed and easily could've added so much more stuff to the game to make it a 11/10 game. The biggest fumble that the game had failed to achieve that the original had done was the atmosphere. Areas in the game such as Iwatodai dorms being too bright, the music remixes are worse (subjective opinion, the original songs were great), and not adding any of the movie details to the game. A lot of the anime cutscenes were removed in place of the persona 5 style pre-rendered cutscenes, initially I was confused why they did that but the more I played, it was more and more apparent that the game was rushed. The quality in them varied greatly with stuff like the opening and sees gearing up looks great. The other ones such as the school trip or the battle on the bridge looks fairly rushed. My favorite thing about the game was these linked episodes which is a compromise to not having social links for male party members, by far the best thing you will be getting out of the game as a veteran player. The combat is a mixed bag because the one-more system is homogenized and bland compared to how they did it in the original but being able to control your party members is a huge positive for most people. The theurgy skills and traits are a fantastic way to create an incentive to hang out with your buddies in the dorms during your free time instead of speedrunning your social stats and social links for tangible rewards for doing so. Overall if you really want the true persona 3 experience, play FES but other than that reload is just fine as a remake.\t\t\t\t\t
