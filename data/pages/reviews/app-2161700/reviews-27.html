\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is NOT something that has any business being released as a full-price game in 2024, nevermind the separate add-ons.

Sure, a LOT of work went into this. The graphics are OK (if you enable VSync), performance is fine, the soundtrack catchy, and the game does a good job of explaining all its mechanics from the ground up, which will not only help you with this title, but also all other games in the genre.

However, main gameplay is just plain repetitive and boring. Grind social stats day after day, grind a procedurally generated dungeon night after night, grind your way through combat with blandly generic enemies (even on the hardest level, it's always the same: figure out their weak spots, if any, employ the right attacks, heal a bit, rinse, repeat). That soundtrack? It's good, but waaaay too short to cover all the boredom.

The apparently highly-rated story unfolds very, very slowly, and, other than being PAINFULLY cringe at times, could have been told in 20% of the time. In 2009 (the year this game nominally takes place), you might have gotten away with that. But we've, like, progressed waaaay past the point where this title is worth your money, unless you're the SEVERELY nostalgic type.\t\t\t\t\t
