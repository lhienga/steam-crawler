p3 feels like what the old-school persona enjoyers wanted: a compelling story, overhauled visuals and remastered/new music, and optimizations to gameplay. I haven't played p3 before this, but it feels very much like p4.
its an easy 10/10 for me; people who've only played p5 might find themselves a little lost, as the games before it feature far less ways to ramp up damage. there's no technical mechanic in the game, and almost all enemies are just damage sponges.
due to the previous point, with the exception of boosting theurgies, every enemy(until you've scaled) and boss is a damage sponge. the difficulty with the older generations of persona games lie in not slipping up in your planning and execution, and letting the enemy get 1 mores and put Wiping All Out on your tombstone.
it heavily reminds me of p4, as that was the first persona game i played, and as such feels familiar to me. the only difference is in the physical split being a little weird, but i got used to it fast.
After playing all 3 games(i'm not technically done yet), p3 definitely has the best story, p4 the best characters/social links, and p5 the best gameplay. p5 no longer reigns supreme as the best game for visuals and music; p3r is comin in hot.\t\t\t\t\t
