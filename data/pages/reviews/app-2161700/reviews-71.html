\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've Been waiting for this!

positives
+++character rebalancing some have new skills. Overall they are pretty strong or at least good enough at what they do for me to consider throwing them in the line up. (My first being yukari aigis mitsuru.)
++social links have full voice acting.
+no social links for male team members but at least bonus voiced interactions with dorm members that give them a bonus skill and raise your academic,charm,courage.
++theurgys are super fun, however it is an easy win button for anything not a full moon boss.
+While i am nostalgic to the old voice acting/BGM and prefer it The new are great with some minor hiccups.
++The game is visually stunning.
+++Tartarus is even better with blocks being a bit differently shaped to move around. it feels like a true dungeon especially with the monad doors scattered throughout.
+strega has more dialogue and are allowed to be part of the story a bit instead of just getting stomped for the easiest levels in the game.

Negatives

Alot of the poor balancing from inflated item economy, theurgys, and dlc personas is what bothers me.

-- all the free Beads, somas, sp food the game is just way too easy. Not once did i need to go to the phamacey to buy items. i sold most of them to fuse more personas.

- Even with selling most of my items, with theurgys its still way too easy. normal shadows become ants, Guardians become a joke, Moon bosses become tanks you widdle down with theurgys.

---no fatigue system. In old persona you could become tired from exploring too much or studying. I liked it as it prevented you from grinding the whole block in one day at least not without some penalty's making it difficult.
taking away this system while also 3d printing soul drops, sodas, snuff souls etc your encouraged to stay in tartarus as much as possible with everyone running out of sp often being the time you return. grind 1 maybe 2 days in a month and your fine every time. This is optimal as it helps you raise your links and social stats more.

--- DLC personas suffer the same as they did in persona 5.Using them also feels like easy mode as many start with great skills and unlock ones just as great or unique to them having many resistances while also not needing to be fused just get it from the compendium for 0 yen.
I want to use them, but if i can just pick up a pre-buffed dlc persona for free and mass export their overpowered skills fusing starts to feel redundent. its already easier than ever to make strong personas with skill cards.
--- overall unimpressed with the dlc. I expected more songs to choose from between persona 4 persona 5 and some of the oldie of persona 3 like soul phase or burn my dread or just having access to the old BGM of persona 3 FES in general. Lack of songs lack of ability to have a track play while exploring tartarus.

-Not having the answer come back is a shame. While im not a huge fan of the female MC i think it is also disappointing for her to not be here. Persona 3 reload could have been massive with these additions though understandably would need a fair amount of manpower.

---No chance for combo team attacks after a down. You added theurgys but decided not to expand on team attacks?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my first Persona game. I hated it... but It also was one of the greatest experiences I've had playing a video game in my life. If you've never experienced it for yourself, no matter if you're a persona fan, no matter if you even like JRPGs. I was far from the target demographic for this game. Get it.

I'll start off with my grievances, as I feel it's necessary to properly express how I felt about this game. Gameplay aside because it seems it's fairly unanimous that Tartarus is a grindfest, some people love it some people hate it. I've always half-joked with my friends that Persona games are made for 15 year old kids with no friends. After finally playing the game, well, I wasn't THAT far off.... I found myself frustrated with this game for some time. I'm a 24 year old man, I'm lucky enough to not lack in people to share my life with, I had no interest to role play as a high school kid and insert myself into these situations like is obviously the original intention. Some of the writing decisions (especially with the social link episodes) felt frustrating, lazy, or worst of all, completely bastardize certain characters message and growth to either shoehorn in a bad joke, an annoying trope, or honestly, just make a character into a bad self insert harem side character for the romance route at the end of a social link.

That all being said. I'm someone who wears their emotions on their sleeve, I very often cry over works of fiction. I've never had a game make me weep like a child the way Persona 3 did. There will be some vague spoilers in this. No explicit events or moments, but I will discuss the central conflict of the game. If you want to go into this with absolutely no knowledge of this game's themes and conflicts, stop reading here. Despite my previously mentioned frustrations, Persona 3, it's story, and especially it's main cast, absolutely stole my heart and ran with it. So many of these characters, and of their struggles, are either parallels to my own life, or to the lives of people I hold dear. The central conflict of this game, the very concept of death itself, was something that in my late teens and early 20s, I had to come to terms with on my own. It was a terrifying process, full of panic attacks and late night calls to my partner for comfort. I say all of this, because as I mentioned previously. I had NO INTEREST in inserting myself into the life of a high school kid. But this game forces you to sit there and consider with your own mortality. To put yourself in these characters shoes, and understand the impossible task they've been faced with. To watch them find their own conviction, their own reason to keep going. Despite the loss they've faced, with the understanding that there is even more to lose head, they band together and press on. My critique on the writing decisions this game makes I believe is valid, but something I came to realize is. This game was not made for me today. This game was made for me when I was a kid. When I was lost, and scared, and lonely. This game perfectly, painstakingly, yet ever so gently, completely broke down every single emotion and thought process I experienced on my own path to coming to terms with my own mortality. Part of me wishes I found this game when I was at my lowest point in my life. I needed something to hold my hand or rather, give me somewhere to rest my head through that process. Regardless, to say I'm glad I played this game could not be more of an understatement. If you were on the fence, I hope I did a good job at persuading you lol. Buy the damn game !!! Burn your dread.
