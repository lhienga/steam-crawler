\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPrior to this game, the only other Persona game I had played (and loved) was Persona 5. While I knew what to expect from the series, this was an otherwise blind playthrough. I haven't reached the end just yet but I think I've seen enough to write a review.

Dark Hour:
The thing that disappointed me most in this game was Tartarus, the place where most of the combat and exploration takes place. In my Persona 5 playthrough, I found Mementos to be a grind and by far the weakest part of that game. Tartarus is just Mementos, a series of small levels constructed randomly using the same few blocks. This worked fine in Persona 5 because the main focus of the game was on well constructed palaces, which flowed room to room and were filled with tailored enemy placements, and Mementos was just a side area to get stronger for most of the game. In Persona 3, the whole game is the same dull grind up Tartarus, with a few, very brief sections which break the formula. These all show promise but are far too short to have any meaningful impact. I understand this game is much older than Persona 5, but it's still a massive step down and makes large chunks of the game feel like a meaningless grind.
The combat itself is less flashy than Persona 5, but also feels a little less random. I am yet to be one-shot by some random series of "one more" attacks from a boss, and it is a lot easier to plan several moved ahead. I think there are too many party members in this game, but there is still a fair chunk of the game left and I may change my mind on that.
Initially, I really liked engaging in battle by running up to an enemy and hitting them with my sword as it made not getting an ambush far more likely. As the game went on, however, it just left me craving a real time action game where swinging a sword has satisfying feedback. I prefer the grapple hook from Persona 5, which lets you ambush shadows with their back facing you from a distance, and I would often jump through some hoops just to get the grapple animation.

Social Life:
I was also really disappointed by the world of Persona 3. After experiencing the incredible depiction of Shibuya in Persona 5, Tatsumi Port Island feels very small and very artificial. Again, this is quite an old game, but I don't think giving the city an overhaul would have alienated many returning fans.
The writing quality of characters is generally good, with some in particular being far more interesting than anyone in Persona 5. This is the one part of the game I think really holds up, and I wish there were more.
Edit: The biggest improvement in Persona 5's social system is forcing you to choose between exploring the Metaverse and building social links, so there were far fewer times when nobody was available and there was no more exploration either. In Persona 3's late game, I've found myself increasingly bored and instead of excitement, the only emotion I feel when the game hints that there's more to come is exhaustion. The only reason I keep playing is I know if I put the game down now, I'll never finish it. I hope the story picks up for the conclusion.

Story:
I haven't finished the game yet, so I can't really talk about the story. However, the pacing for the first couple of hours was really slow, and I think if this had been my first Persona game I may have stopped playing there.
In Persona 5, the game begins by putting its best foot forward with Kamoshida's palace. While the game never reaches these heights again, the momentum never really lets up. Persona 3 took around 5 hours to hook me, and even then the issues with the Dark Hour often made it a slog to get through the game.
The end of Persona 5 (base, mostly) kinda went off the rails, and I'm curious how Persona 3 will compare, but for now I think Persona 5's story holds the game together far better than this one.

Despite my criticisms, I do still tentatively recommend this game. If you're trying out the Persona series for the first time, I'd recommend trying Persona 5 Royal on Game Pass first, but if you're willing to keep pushing through the first few hours you'll probably find a great experience here, even if the game struggles to keep it going throughout.

Edit 2:
I've decided to put the game down for now, and probably for good. There were some great moments, but I feel like this game could have been 10-20 hours shorter and better for it. For context, I got up to early January before giving up. I've heard the ending's good, but I play games to have fun. The combat and exploration is no different to the first few hours of the game, and the social life opportunities outside of the school semesters are a joke. I wish they implemented a system like in Persona 5 where each day you could hang out with a teammate if you had nothing better to do, or at least offer more daytime social links outside of the school.
I'll leave my review positive for now, since I had 20-30 hours of fun with this game, but it is a huge disappointment after Persona 5 gripped me for 83 hours and left me wanting more (blind playthrough, missed the Royal ending). I'm looking forward to trying Persona 5 Strikers and also getting the Royal ending, and I'm left regretting the time I wasted on this game. I hope too that Persona 4 isn't a slog like this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersona 3 Reload takes what made the original release fantastic, then gives it a huge quality of life makeover while keeping the core elements intact.

The father of modern Persona games gets such a big glowup with the models and animations, and while most of the original voice actors got replaced, (I think the only one who stayed the same was Aigis) the new cast is insanely talented. By the end of the game I preferred the new voice actors over the old ones! This is further emphasized with the voice acting of ALL RANKS for the social links.

The voice actors were not the only thing that got replaced, as many of the existing songs got the same treatment, though this time to varying degrees of success. For example, I much prefer the original version of Mass Destruction over the new remastered version. But as if that wasn't enough we also got a plethora of new tracks, the centerpiece of these being "It's Going Down Now." Thank you Lotus Juice for bringing your A-game

While the models are crisp and sharp, on the personas I just can't help but feel like I prefer how they looked in Persona 5 more. The animations however are fluid, and you don't need to look any further than Yukari's Theurgy to experience this.

Speaking of Theurgies, this mechanic is one of the quality of life changes that adds a new layer of options in the game. It's now also easier than ever to keep party members leveled and gear with the inclusion of Monad passages and the clock. The changes to mechanics are felt the hardest when it comes to Koromaru and Ken. But my favorite inclusion to this game are the brand new Linked episodes that let us spend time with the Members of SEES that aren't proper social links such as Junpei and Akihiko.

Disappointingly however, the fan favorite FeMC from the portable version of the game does not make a return, and "The Answer" From FES is going to be released as DLC. I understand that it would have required significant more recording time, but it's sad to see her get no love. Another thing that I wish could have made it into the game were more boss battles akin to Persona 5's challenges.

Overall I consider this game a must play for JRPG fans, and especially Persona fans. I give this game a 9/10 for sure and am looking forward to the Episode Aigis update.\t\t\t\t\t

The updated features (aside from getting pestered to save people from Tartarus for no real reward or benefit like in Portable) are godsends especially the rewind system (save state mulligans) and spend time events with party members. I like that it keeps the "memories of you" opening note motif that were apart of some FES songs in the game, its like a little reminder if you've played this before of how beautifully this game's ending concludes.

I really prefer the original version of the Burn my Dread -Last Battle- since the lyrics are basically completely different from Reload's version, plus they nixxed my favorite part. I understand wanting to change it up after 18 years of thinking it over though.

"No soul, robust

with no life"

This game will make you cry btw, especially if you accidentally finish it on March 5th like I did.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGenuinely one of the greatest persona experiences I have ever had. I've been a persona fan for quite a few years now have completed multiple play throughs of royal and also having 100% both P5R and Golden. I played portable when it released on steam and while it was fun most of the main messages of the game were lost on me. This remake gives the original a new chance to shine and have an artstyle worthy of the story it's trying to tell. The game play is amazing and fluid, exploring tarturus isn't a chore, and every single story point hit so much harder especially with the amazing voices that went into this game. My only complaint would be the lack of achievements for going the extra mile for things such as defeating the secret boss or completing the compendium. Although it is a remake, it is definitely worth the money and I will be buying The Answer when it releases in September as this may be my new favorite persona game.\t\t\t\t\t
