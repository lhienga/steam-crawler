\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen judging the overall quality of a remake it is important to question the fundamental basis of its existence. The question is: does this remake justify its existence? In other words, were there any major issues with the original game, and is the remake an objective improvement on those aspects? A remake that passes this test with flying colors is the Dead Space Remake, which stays entirely true to the original game's level design and overall aesthetic while improving upon the aspects that needed it(weapon balancing, etc). Unfortunately, Persona 3 Reload does not live up to this standard. To preface this review, let me first say that Persona 3 FES is my favorite game of all time, and I am willing to argue that it is the greatest game ever made. Persona 3 Reload is a misguided and poorly crafted recreation of P3 FES that loses most if not all of the aspects that made the original game so fantastic. There are three major aspects I want to cover: story, gameplay, and overall aesthetic.

Story:

The core story of Persona 3 goes pretty much unchanged in Reload, which is great. The new cast of voice actors ranges from good(Junpei) to really bad(Akihiko, Ken, Shinji), and the removal of animated cutscenes in favor of in-engine ones makes many scenes come off as lackluster and removes the artistic cinematography of FES. The social links are the strongest part of the game, as they remain mostly unchanged with a few additions that improve upon the original iteration. The casting for all of the social links is also very good. Overall, the core story is the best aspect of this game as it stays true to the original and makes improvements where necessary. This is the end of any merits that Reload has.

Gameplay:

The combat gameplay from P3 FES has been completely gutted in Reload, replaced by a tedious gameplay loop. In FES, exploring Tartarus was a well-designed balance of combat and resource management. You clear randomly generated floors by finding the stairway leading to the next floor while managing your health and sp. Every party member has a hidden fatigue meter which causes them to become “tired” after a certain amount of battles. When “tired”, party members do less damage and take more damage. As you level up, their fatigue meter increases. This mechanic is designed to force you into spacing your trips to Tartarus out over multiple in-game days. It also works hand in hand with the exp system. Enemies will be sized based on how much exp each encounter will give you, meaning you need to choose if you think fighting a smaller enemy is worth the exp when it may cause your party members to become tired. The floors are procedurally generated, so it's pretty easy to run past an enemy and find the exit quickly. Once you have completed a certain amount of floors you will come to a mini-boss floor with a teleporter that brings you back to the ground floor, restoring your party’s hp and sp and acting as a checkpoint. However, if your party members are tired, they will automatically leave when you return to the ground floor. This creates another decision between fighting the mini-boss while tired and possibly saving some in-game time, or playing it safe and coming back the next day. These mechanics, along with the standard persona fusion system made P3 FES have an amazing, engaging combat loop. All of this goes out the window when it comes to Reload. The tired mechanic is gone, meaning there is no reason to ever leave Tartarus. You can obtain so many HP and SP items that leaving and coming back the next day will never happen. Along with this, there is now also no reason to not fight every single enemy. Unless you want to be woefully underleveled, this is almost a necessity. These two facts alone draw out Tartarus visits quite a bit compared to P3 FES. These combat encounters are even more grating now that the tactics system has been removed in favor of direct party control. Now instead of managing the behavior of your party members and adapting to possibly unexpected combat situations like in FES, you just spam the same moves over and over again depending on the situation and what party members you have. The new Theurgy mechanic is also terrible, it only exists to make the game incredibly easy with literally no downside. You can drop four atomic bombs on a boss numerous times during 1 fight if you haven’t already instakilled your opponents. Exploring Tartarus has also been needlessly complicated, with irritating gimmick floors that simply exist to pad out the playtime. The introduction of skill cards damages the gameplay quite a bit as well. Being able to buy and then duplicate extremely broken abilities and then slot them into any persona you want is nothing short of gamebreaking. The fusion system is also much worse. The addition of being able to choose which skills personas inherit when fused makes the meta extremely boring, with the only viable strategy being to keep the same 5 support skills on every persona you fuse for the entire game. In FES, the fact that skill selection was random and that personas had very strict skills that they could and could not inherit meant that you had to think about your build and what you were using to fuse rather than just mindlessly selecting what you wanted from a massive list of skills. The time management gameplay is not much better. The “late night” period is now gone, meaning it's infinitely harder to level up academics. The addition of part-time jobs that are free to work at and payout with money and 2 social status points makes time management extremely one-dimensional. There is no space between social link unlocks like in FES, so you will always have to coin-flip between 4-5 characters every day. Social links are also, for the most part, available much more often. Not to mention the onslaught of useless mechanics that are introduced during the time management sections. In FES, there were very simple uses for each social stat and very simple ways to level them up. At max, there were 2-3 ways to level each one up. Reload clutters your screen and your schedule with way too many available social links and options to gain items/social stats, to the point that it is overwhelming and bloated.

Overall Aesthetic:

This category is easily the most offensive of the three. The subdued, bleak aesthetic of the original game has been destroyed, and replaced with Persona 5 Blue Edition. Everything is bright, colorful, and inviting. The town is alive and bustling with people. Everything looks all pretty and done up in the terrible Unreal 4 graphics style. You can fast-travel to any location in the game. All of these changes completely ruin the point of the original game. The oppressively bleak atmosphere of the original greatly contributes to the themes of death and loss of purpose that are present throughout all of FES’s facets. In Reload, the “stylish” presentation betrays the content of the stories and social links, creating a very polarizing environment and severely damaging the overall quality and impact of the game’s message.

Conclusion:


There are a bunch of other smaller things that I hate about this game, like the remixed music, but I can’t find a good place to put them. Since this is a review of the Steam version, if you haven’t played P3 FES before I implore that you try emulating it on PCSX2 before playing this vastly inferior version. To all of you who have played the original game, enjoy this mess if you can I guess. Ultimately, this game is not worth anyone’s time or money as it completely ruins all of the aspects that made the original game so great.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first Persona game was P4. Some time after playing that one, I tried this one but could not get hooked. Never been a fan of roguelike elements, so Tartarus (the game´s dungeon) felt like a chore. I wanted to slap some sense into a lot of the social links (characters with an ongoing narrative you can invest time on to get some gameplay bonuses). It generally felt like I was too late to enjoy the game, and the gameplay refinements the series had gone through had spoilt me to it.

Reload has been a godsend. Getting all the quality of life enhancements of P5 into the plot of P3 gets you an amazing game. Too many social links are still basket cases, but now I even enjoyed Tartarus and the main ensemble are consistently awesome characters.

The story is driven by characters and themes more than by the plot. The plot iself is strong, with some weak points here and there. It holds well even to the high bar set by the rest of the series. The SMT/Persona combat loop is so good it almost feels like a cheat.

Also don´t worry if you have seen spoilers. I had seen them too, and the story still hit me like a truck.\t\t\t\t\t
