\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersona 3 Reload brought me back to one of my favorite stories of all time, and I enjoyed every second of it. From the visuals to the gameplay, I can tell how much time and love was put into this remake. Overall I would give this remake an 8/10, and would recommend it to any Persona fan and really anyone in general open to playing it. Where it loses 2 points is the lack of the Female Protagonist and the egregious DLC prices versus content.

1) I understand completely why the Female Protagonist was not included in this remake. Including her would be the equivalent of making the game again, which would be far too high of an order for an already bustling experience. Still, it's something I have to consider when we take into account that there is no definitive version of Persona 3, and unfortunately this remake cannot be considered the "definitive," and rather the "best" version to play. Persona 3 Portable's steam port I cannot say in good faith is a good substitute due to the port quality and its many issues, as well as the stripped down gameplay due to hardware limitations at the time. Ideally the Female Protagonist would've been included, but I understand why she wasn't. Nevertheless, I will still dock a point for this.

2) Egregious pricing for DLC is nothing new for Atlus, and I was hoping it wouldn't have affected this game, but who was I kidding. The prices for this game's DLC is abhorrent, especially for what essentially bottles down to a few Persona's from future titles, BGM sets, and costumes. They are simply not worth the price for what is essentially cosmetics and things that can easily be modded in for free. The DLC on day 1 is simply not worth the price and you should NOT buy it if you're expecting to get anything huge out of it. The second part of the DLC which docks points is the (as of writing this) recently announced Expansion Pass, which is a whopping $35 for BGM sets, 1 costume set, and a story expansion called "Episode Aigis." After recently experiencing "The Answer" in the original Persona 3 FES, I cannot say it is worth even $20 unless Atlus majorly overhauls and improves not only its gameplay, but also its story. The Answer is a very controversial epilogue and I understand why now, because for how long and grindy it is there's not much story aside from adding more context to base game events and expanding on why a certain thing happened, and the answer's provided may or may not be satisfying to players. I am holding out hope that Atlus will remedy these issues in Episode Aigis, but if not then the negative reviews the Expansion Pass is currently garnering is completely deserved.
TL;DR: DLC is overpriced and not worth your money, taking another point off the overall score.

Now while I know this review has been mostly negative, aside from these two major issues I have with the game, everything else is amazing and I cannot recommend this game enough. If you're wanting to play Persona 3, this is the version you should play. If you'd like to experience the Female Protagonist, unfortunately you'll have to make due with the terrible Persona 3 Portable PC port. For Episode Aigis, if it ends up being just like the original The Answer, I would recommend just watching a video of all the cutscenes or reading a summary, because actually playing it at that point would not be worth your time and effort unless they fully rework it for Episode Aigis.

Overall, Persona 3 Reload is an 8/10 remake with worthwhile base game content and a story that I consider the best in the series. As of writing this, I'm aiming to 100% each game, which I might add is not too hard for this remake compared to past titles like P4G and the original P5.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersona 3 reload is what I believe all Remakes of games should strive to be. It follows the story of the original almost 1 to 1, adding some extra scenes that give more depth to side characters and even antagonists. The gameplay has been revamped to be more similar to the latest Persona entries, especially persona 5. This may be a welcome change for people who prefer a streamlined experience that isn't as difficult to play through, however this does come with the fact that it is much easier than the original game. Overall, this title takes everything good about the original game and gives it a modern polish, making it newly accessible to a wider audience; although, do keep in mind, this game does NOT feature the female protagonist route from Persona 3 portable, which changes a lot of the story and cutscenes. Finally, I would highly recommend this game to all persona fans who haven't gotten to play the third iteration yet, and to JRPG fans in general.\t\t\t\t\t
