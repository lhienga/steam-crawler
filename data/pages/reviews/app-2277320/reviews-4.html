\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic fun little game. Honestly all of the bad reviews need to get some perspective, this game is WELL worth the price. Haven't experienced a single bug and have thoroughly enjoyed it and plan to continue enjoying it. Super cute characterization, fun customization, I love being able to combine various parts of monsters to make various creatures. Really fun designs, with very clear artistic intent in the models. In fact the art style overall is adorable, super cohesive, and really brings so much charm to what might otherwise be a very gory and visceral game!

Unfortunately some people just have zero self awareness or ability to comprehend that a game not being an 80 hour long ~AAA~ title does not make it an early access bug filled nightmare. Like truly staggering, this game is EASILY worth the miniscule price, and while the AI can be a little janky at times, when raiding they do opt to just stand directly on top of the enemy base, again THIS GAME IS TEN DOLLARS. Honestly, if you can't find entertainment in this game for $10, then fair enough I hope you find a game that is fun for you. This game does not deserve the criticisms it is getting though.

It could stand to have some better/more sorting options for body parts, for example being able to select a single race would be good over just the part, and as above, a bit more work on the AI would help. I find that the 'defend tower' AI doesn't adequately defend, however the default 'defend' AI placed AT the tower works much better.

Having more clarity/specifity around the mechanics would be good for beginners too, not everything is quite as clear as it could be such as the costs or damage, numbers in general tbh seem to blend into the background and are easy to miss, perhaps some kind of stylized border or something to help the numbers stand out more than they currently do.

Also you should include the cat more. Just personal preference, but this game needs more of him. All games do tbh.\t\t\t\t\t

Resource Management TD Necromancy
Overall, it's a good game; I haven't played the first one, but both are rather inexpensive on their own. So I might go back and look into that one sometime. If you're like me, you enjoy any form of Tower Defense game, especially those that aim to be different from the conventional classics that include placing turrets and fortifications while surviving waves until you eventually fail or succeed.

In Necrosmith 2, you begin in a single tower with some basic materials to create and smith your army of the undead, which you then send and command out into the world to destroy lairs, capture objectives to help fund your army and defend yourselves from the constant flooding of monsters and beasts being sent after you.

Destroy the main lair to complete that level and continue on to the next if you wish. Staying is kind of worthless because it just keeps sending more and more bosses after you with much higher scaling difficulty while also losing a lot of your material funding when the map is 100% scouted thus there isn't much of a genuine "end game" for any level.

Conclusion
It feels more like an idler game at times, as you wait for the day and night cycles to rotate, and it can be very annoying when your minions do not path to the locations you want them to go to while idle, forcing you to manually control them as they slowly lumber towards the location 5 minutes away.

The ability to use the minions with commands similar to RTS games like AoE and such, where you click the map and send them to their location, would be a more controlled way of making it more satisfying and allowing us to focus on the other parts without having to again, manually hold down the bindings for the singular minion to head to the specific location you want them to go to, unless of course you use spells and such to take out the objectives far away getting in the way of your auto-play but you don't get that until a bit further into the gameplay, so the first few runs will be annoying.

It's fun, could use some adjustments, but it's possibly like this due to artificial difficulty?
A game speed changer would be nice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA bit short (only 3 worlds) but then I remembered the game was 6 bucks so its fine.

I didnt play Necrosmith 1 so I can't compare what they've improved on.

Assembling your army of dead is a lot of fun, legs, arms, head and body. As you progress through the worlds you get access to stronger monsters to piece together with.

The game loop is collect body parts and mana, then build your initial dudes with what you got and then as soon as possible create your titan, which is a giant minion featuring the most health and damage you can possibly create. But even your titans will eventually need improvement on later stages. And you get new titan parts by defeating boss waves, which spawns when the timer goes down to 0 and then resets.

how to win the game is exploring the current world until you find the objective. Which takes the form of a much tougher base (bases spawn enemies during daytime) than the ones you're used to. There's no real rush to get to it once you find it, especially if you find it early. But the game will kill you after a certain amount of time by spawning an unkillable enemy (so you need some tempo, and also puts a limit on how much you can grind one map for a single run).

The graphics are very charming and upgrading your meta progression is very rewarding. Both in power and in appearance as the apparatus representing the upgrade gets noticeable changes each time.

There's a bit of RTS skill involved in this game. You can pause whenever luckily to micro manage things (defending your tower with spells while you are controlling one of your dead exploring the map and destroying bases with it). As the tower can explode really fast on World 3 if you don't have proper guards. Otherwise the game is a bit of an auto game. Other than directly controlling one minion you have preset behaviours such as "Go destroy bases", "Go destroy enemies", "Guard the area you're currently in", "Guard the tower" and "Collect resources".

Storywise, its not a game that last very long as you can complete it as long as you get the higher tier damage (normally in the arms). I finished it still missing a lot of titan bits and a few missing upgrades and a lot of missing artefacts (equipment you can have up to 4 of that gives passive effects). Beating it with just the xeno arms on 2 titans.

A lot of fun and creativity and a nice distraction for like a week. I might play it some more just to see all the titan parts and what being fully upgraded is like. But I'm happy wrapping up Necrosmith 2 here and I'll pay attention to if any Necrosmith 3 will appear in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very much like the first, with some changes. If you liked the first Necrosmith, you will most likely enjoy the second game as well.

Necrosmith is a really fun and addictive game about building minions with multiple body parts and sending them to fight and loot for you. You can give them basic orders and personally control one of them at a time. There is also the possibility to build very strong but costly special units called titans.

In each run you have access to some offensive, defensive and utility spells and can also find upgrades that provide different bonuses for your units and tower or additional body parts.

After each run you can spend the gold you earn in permanent upgrades for your spells or base body part generation. There is also a “relic” system that allows you to select up to four modifiers/bonuses.

It’s a very enjoyable small game.\t\t\t\t\t

1) area attack weapons rule, esp if you get attack bonuses from a recipe or a building,
2) lairs miss you if you have enough speed and run around,
3) on the second disk the lizard dragonwing combines both a speed of 160 and an area attack of 200; it can autoexplore and destroy lairs single-handedly if controlled,

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought it after 100% the first game. The price is twice as much but also the quality! However it is very different than the first one.

What I Like
STEAM DECK Compatibility
First impression is that they know their target audience, its great that it has Steam Deck compatibility in its option. I don't see many games do this (specifically optimize their game for the deck; controls, UI, etc)

New and More Balanced Mechanics
It is very different than the first one in terms of mechanics. Older body parts is still playable with the right upgrade. While in the first game you need to play newer parts because they're vastly superior and the older parts is downright unplayable in end game (i haven't finished in this one but so far it's great)

There are now body types such as bones, flesh, etc. which emphasize previous point on utilizing older parts so every type has its own use > in short, I like this mechanics more balanced!

Better Control
One of the major improvements over the first game is the minion control. Now, you can start assigning minions to do a specific task. For example, to specifically attack enemies lair aside from exploring or defending base. In the first game you have to make your minions to stand still to defend your tower. But I think the Ai needs improvement as they die so easily (do not try to avoid projectiles). I trust the devs will address this in future updates.

Major Content Improvement
One thing that the first game lacks is content. All i say is, there are more than one map in this one and they delve into the lore further. More interactions too which I really love.

Better Sense of Improvement
This game like the first one creates a great sense of improvements from the upgrades! I love it so much with every new unlocks! Making the exploration part more exciting.

What I Don't Like
Too Grindy
I still like that you can Idle this game and relax as it will pause when the boss appear but to get into that part is kinda grindy and slow. To start, the map is bigger but the unit speed is much slower... So to be in a state where you can leave it be you need at least a titan (more to it later).

High Reliance on Titans
This game have Titans as a new mechanics. Its basically what the name suggest, a titanic minion that combines many parts into one powerful entity. But it creates too much of a gap. Normal zombies even with later part struggles to simply survive against enemy titans... The game FORCE you to play around your titan which I'm not really a fan of.

Imbalance Weapons
The imbalances between weapons is very noticeable in this game. For example, lizard part in this game have granades and pistols. While granades offer stagger to enemis, pistols is simply wayyy better as it melts not only enemies but buildings. Pistol pierces to multiple enemies multiple times while granades thrown only damages them once. The power gap is crazy between same tier weapons. They need to balance this ASAP.

Ai too Stupid
Keeps on dying since they do not know how to utilize their weapon effectively if they have incompatible weapon combination. They cannot dodge enemies attack too.

Overall user Interface and Experience
While i do love that they put Steam Deck compatibility, but the user interface is too complex... I need to double click a lot of times because idk what i'm trying to do and boom somehow i accidentally cast a spell or i creates the minion lmaooo. Idk if it's bugged but it's too complex for me. And also, it took longer now for controller users to burn the part... and the parts is arranged badly compared to the first game. I hope the devs find solution for this.

All in all, it is a great game! I love the major improvements but complex doesn't mean better. The first game with its simplicity offer a smoother gameplay experience! But overall this is absolutely a better game despite it flaws.

7.5/10 - I hope they improve things I listed above\t\t\t\t\t
