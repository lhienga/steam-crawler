\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an okay game, good ideas, poor execution.
Pros:
- Building your minions is very fun
- The art is awesome
- The attack animations are cool
Cons:
- Extremely unpolished, feels like a very early access
- Controls are wonky
- AI is stupid (EDIT an update has since come out improving the AI, they're still not perfect but it is much better now and is no longer a major issue)
- Missing many important quality of life features such as selecting groups of minons, giving specific orders without needing to micromanage, etc.
One of my biggest gripes is that the game provides practically zero information on the DPS of weapons... there's a flat damage number but nothing telling you attack speed, range, AOE etc. Impossible to create good builds without tedious trial and error.
At the end of the day it is a $10 game, but the developer just didn't implement so many easy improvements that would have dramatically increased the quality of the game, so I cannot recommend it in its current state.\t\t\t\t\t

However, there were a few things that missed the mark for me.

Firstly, I went into this game thinking I'd get to make some sort of demon/dragon titan, as depicted on the splash art. There's nothing like that. Just six tiers of titan parts, with some variance in arms. Bone, Flesh, Magic, Divine, Alien, Robot. There's a lot of design space here that was just left empty, and that was a mark against the game.

Second, on the final level it becomes frustrating trying to find rare divine parts to put together some of the unique recipes.
