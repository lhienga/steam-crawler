Ultimately if they do right by this game and fix glaring issues, i'd be open to see where necrosmith 3 goes and hope they focus on replayability and building a better core gameplay loop so they don't need to rely on the upgrades crutch so much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked necrosmith 1, you will very likely enjoy necrosmith 2. The game is a simple, but enjoyable take on a mix of unit-management strategy and tower defense, with a very satisfying blend of exploration, unit creation, and the addition of the colossal titan units is a fun change.

I will note, the game runs rather short, and I would have enjoyed a longer campaign, if possible, but for what it is, it's very enjoyable. Certain elements of AI are buggy, at certain progression milestones feel they entirely overwhelm the others, or even make large portions of the game prior to that point entirely obsolete, rather than feeling free to explore and augment your units for the best combinations.

If power scaling, and a short campaign aren't huge issues for you, you'll likely love the game. I hope they continue to add content to the game down the line, as my only real complaint for the game is how short it felt. I would say the final disc can be cleared a few hours from start, and most of the content can probably be cleared in ~10-15 hours. Probably. The further you get to playing the game for 100% recipes and achievements, the more tedious previously enjoyable elements will become, so I haven't done that just yet.

Tl;dr, it's a very fun game, but it's a short game unless you decide to milk it for content yourself, which makes it a little less fun the more you do it.\t\t\t\t\t
