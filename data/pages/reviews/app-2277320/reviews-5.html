This renders most units and parts useless and doesn't incentivize using different kinds of parts, you're just going to burn them for mana. The final map has enough that you'll never run out for your titans.

Overall, it's good and it's worth the price. It's just rough around the edges since there's no hotkeys, proper sorting, and only 1 way to play the game or you lose.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tfun and unique game with a few glaring flaws:

1. small minions can quickly outpower your "best" limited unit that takes a hefty investment
2. player spells seem to quickly outpower enemies bu dont get any more fun with upgrdades. youd figure youd get bigger aoes at some point, or be able to deploy the cat to specific portals
3. very little variety in enemies and enemy buildings. the buildings specifically seem to all become the same thing (and is massively punishing to your units that will stand in the center of the building every time)
4. lack of a speed up mechanic. the grind in this game is fairly heavy and its made worse by the lack of speed up
5. repetitive and long levels

its also got a weird difficulty curve. the first 2 sessions were kinda hard. after that, i beat 2 levels back to back without spending any gold. that said, i still havent seen any of the secret items that the game has achievements for.

overall, great game and i hope to see some changes in the future!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was released too early.
The hook is there. It is fun. This could have been a good game.
But it suffers a lot from artificial difficulty due to poorly implemented game mechanics.
As it stands now I can not recommend it, but perhaps I can in a few months.

Here's what grinded my gears specifically:
- Can't hold the left mouse button to smoothly look around the minimap
- When not controlling a unit you can use WASD to move the camera, except when you're hovering any button.
- When controlling a unit, you can not move the camera apart from the non-smooth mini map, since WASD are now used to control the unit, Arrow keys have a different function, holding right mouse button deselects the unit and iirc. you can't move the camera by swaying the cursor to the viewport edge.
- The camera randomly panning to the boss that spawned (and not panning back with an arrow indicating the direction while stopping time for example) has lost me multiple important units while I was microing on multiple occasions
- Units do not respect enemy attack range/kill potential. This is frustrating with the gather/scout order, where as described the units will ignore enemies, but also quite literally will ignore them to run into a group of enemies because one of them died and dropped a shiny. This game is not built around micro-management. AI needs to be better.
- Units do not respect their own attack range. Titans with more range than the enemy boss will run to hug them for some reason and die instantly. Since you can only control/move around one of them at a time, this becomes quite frustrating.
- Units do not properly prioritize enemies. I've lost multiple games where my titan could've easily killed the boss standing right in front of him, but chose to focus some straggler coming for a visit from the local lair.
- The importance of the units respawn menu is being disregarded. The game has no issue with random popups just closing the respawn menu. Since afaik you can't directly spawn units using either the prefab or titans menu, this can be very annoying and lose you a winnable round.
- Afaik you can not assign prefab units and titans orders, so you have to do so after they spawn. One by one

I reached the second disc and found myself incapable to make any progress due to the consistent bad choices the unit ai does.
Other commenters have noted that reaching the second disk the game spikes too much in difficulty and they feel the need to grind upgrades to make up for it, but I don't think balancing is quite the issue here.
So for now I'll stop playing and maybe come back in some time hoping for things to have improved.\t\t\t\t\t
