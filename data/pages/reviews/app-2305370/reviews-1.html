\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOut of all the impressive things about this game, I have to say that it's just so well balanced in so many ways. The sense of community that comes from a small village coming together is really refreshing.

A wholesome spoiler:
A UFO crash-lands in your home and the two occupying it feel incredibly guilty for causing so much damage. They insist on taking care of it after apologizing profusely. The next morning, you wake up expecting to gather materials for the repairs but Williams has already donated everything necessary, and the boss of the construction crew, Mung, is reaming out the UFO owners.
At the end of this, they go through the trouble of not only repairing the damage they had done, but also upgrade your home. John now has his own bed, Sam gets her own room, and everyone gets together to celebrate after. The only resource it cost you was patience. It doesn't interrupt the flow of anything, and everyone in the village comes to your aid like you did to theirs.

Everything comes at a price in farming sims, and there are rarely moments where you're not roped into a fetch quest for something that's supposed to be a favour to you. We love these farming sims regardless of these silly things, but it's just really sweet that everyone wanted the best for John and Sam, and were more than happy to go out of their way to make their friend's lives better.

Anyone, I am pretty zonkered, play this game, it is v good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPOSITIVES: Eastward was a lot of bittersweet moments, and honestly I was torn with how the story progressed, and how you're constantly shuffled off just as John and Sam settle in to every city really grinded my gears. With Octopia, they just do a complete 180, and you really get to settle down and take in the countryside with a select few of the most memorable Eastward NPCs. The visuals are on point. The UI is cute, the environment is bright and cozy, the music is really heartwarming, and the dialogue is sickeningly endearing. All these aspects coalesce into a very diabetes inducing experience that I highly recommend.

NEGATIVES: I only have two gripes. One is that the pacing is a bit off towards the late game, and you find yourself kinda just skipping the night and waiting for NPC visits if you're aiming for 100%. The second being that I wish they would have expanded on the town and the available NPCs a lot more than they have, or at the very least, perhaps let you visit neighbouring villages for other activities. Eastward had plenty of one-off minigames, I'm honestly disappointed they didn't use that as an excuse to make them more permanent. But that being said, all the positives outweigh the negatives, and I would recommend this DLC (that literally feels like a whole nother game).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely worth getting. Visuals are top-notch as usual, and make the game really easy on the eyes. I genuinely wish there were more farming games with this level of lighting and sprite quality. There isn't as much variety as the base game, but progression changes things up now and then.

Gameplay is pretty good. A standard farming/life sim where you farm, fish, and mine to unlock more of the town and bring it to life. If you're a savvy player, you might find yourself way ahead on progression and just waiting for things to happen, but that's only for a little bit. Collecting everything is quite doable too, but also kind of dependent on waiting around.

The story is quite minimal but quite good. Not earth-shaking, but enjoyable all the way through, and not as confusing as the main campaign. Seeing the return of familiar characters is a treat, especially because their characterisation isn't dependent on a confusing story; its generally a happier time for everyone from eastward. That said, even though the townsfolk are enjoyable, I do wish we got more out of these characters a la stardew valley. There are glimmers of interesting writing here and there, but Octopia can't quite shake the shenanigans of eastward's story. They ought to hire a writer for their next project.

So this DLC gets a tick on all three categories, and is actually somewhat more satisfying than the base game. I do miss the sense of adventure, but that's not the fault of the DLC. Regardless of whether you own the game already or not, do yourself a favour and get this. for the price they're selling this at, its quite the bargain.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, while this DLC plays like typical farming sim, but the focus here is actually collecting recipe and cooking dishes to attract more people to visit Octopia. Its a refreshing take on the genre.

As for the gameplay, there's something that Octopia does different from your average farming sim. There's no Season here so you can freely plant any crops you want. Also crops grows very quickly in this game.
There's also the typical fishing mini-games and its pretty fun. Mining is also plays in a mini-game form, which reminds me a lot of Bomberman. Overall, its a nice expansion to the main story.

Oh and, while this game does takes place in seperate multiverse from the base game, you can jump straight into the DLC right away without playing the base game, there are some references that you will miss if you didn't play Eastward first, so I highly recommend you play that first, instead of jumping into Octopia blindly\t\t\t\t\t
