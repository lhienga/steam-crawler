\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let the word 'DLC' fool you, this is a whole game on its own. It might be short and repetitive at times, but it's very much worth it for its price (I dare say it's underpriced!). Plus, who doesn't want to see their faves thrive and get the happy ending they deserve? Anything for little Sammy and John. ‚ô°

If you thought this was only a silly farming sim, then you were wrong! It has some lore that adds onto the main game (Eastward). A few things are finally explained too: who is Solomon and what's his deal; how the Miasma cycle started; Eastward's Mother.

Finished the game's story on day 65, but this can be different for everyone. There's still things to do once you finish the story if you want to 100% the game.

The game has a slow start but as you keep upgrading buildings, your tools and meeting new characters, everything becomes much easier. Don't get your hopes too high when it comes to the characters you meet, not all Eastward characters make a return! Octopia centers around food, our beloved Chef John is tasked to cook specific meals to complete characters' side quests. There are over 150 recipes for you to discover, so you are encouraged to forage, plant crops and fish to 100% that recipe book! The mining mini-game is very Bomberman-like and another mini-game makes a return... if you can find it.

There's a few visual glitches here and there, but they didn't affect my gameplay at all.

Tldr; I highly recommend this 'DLC'. Not only do our favorite characters get their happy ending, but there's an actual story and context added to Eastward's lore. Go have fun and cook yummy meals for everyone. ‚ô°\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn many ways, Eastward: Octopia is one of the best indie farming sims available on Steam. It cuts unnecessary hassles bound to annoy long-time players of the genre and delivers quite a surprising amount of content for its price tag. That's what makes the expansion worth recommending.

However, it also punishes you for planning too much and being too efficient. Earth Gems make things much more exciting, but they're way too easy to come by and may end up leading you to boost your progress to a point you're left with nothing to do on the late game but skip days until new tourists arrive. The result is a very poor endgame/post-game experience, especially combined with the Fishermen Trio Death loop bug that currently makes it impossible for players to reach 100%.

In other words, the content features some annoyances that could easily be fixed by minor bug fixes and balance changes. Hopefully future updates address these issues and fully round up the experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a cheap farming sim if you have the base game. The devs call it a DLC but it's a full game. So, the same writing issues that plagued Eastward are somewhat lessened but are still in the product. Yes, there's overwriting for some scenes which isn't an issue due to fast forward button which didn't exist during my playthrough of Eastward. If you enjoyed the overwritten scenes from Eastward then you'll be disappointed in this game.


The fetch quests that I found irritating at best to not respecting my time at worst are much better placed in the DLC. Then again I haven't progressed very far in the dlc due to hearing about the glitches and experience a glitch that doesn't allow me to change the bobber for my fishing rod. I can change seeds just fine but my bobber isn't changing to the newly purchased one. And there's allegedly a glitch that prevents you from getting a hundred percent for some reason.


Things that I'm not surprised by but merely disappointed.

As much as the devs promised stories between friends at dinner, there's like one scene and that's it. And making friends is pretty much cooking a dish one by one and giving it to them when they show up in town. Cooking is tedious in the game which was an issue in the original game which had you cooking each dish one by one as well. There's not speed boosts or anything for any dish, just restoring energystamina/whatever it's called.

John who was an almost non entity in the original game is the same here. Do I expect him to open his mouth? Nope. Expecting him to emote somewhat would be nice to see but that was an issue in the original game. What else does he have besides his love for Sam? I have no idea and the devs' writing isn't telling me anything.\t\t\t\t\t
