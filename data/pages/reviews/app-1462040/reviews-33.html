\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've journeyed through nearly every installment of the Final Fantasy series, but among them, FF8, FF7, FF9, FF6, and FF10 hold a special place in my heart, ranked in that precise order. Undoubtedly, FF7 stands as a titan within the franchise, deeply ingrained in the memories of many Millennials like myself, who first experienced its magic as children. I'm thrilled to declare my adoration for its Remake. This iteration delves even further into the intricate tapestry of FF7's narrative, pushing the boundaries of character development far beyond what the original achieved.

Certainly, the Remake doesn't replicate every aspect of its predecessor. There are bound to be purist fans out there, quick to critique every deviation from the original storyline. But for those individuals, perhaps it's best to cherish the original and let this new rendition thrive on its own merits. Personally, I relished the fresh perspective this retelling offered and appreciated the opportunity to rediscover the story in such a luxurious manner. The gameplay is truly remarkable, seamlessly integrating with the narrative's depth. The attention to detail is exceptional, ranging from the lively NPC dialogues to the breathtaking graphical enhancements. Although I utilised mods such as the FF7 HD Project, which significantly enhances textures with an additional 50 GB of data, and played the game at a smooth 120 FPS, the core experience remains truly commendable.

Having been an avid RPG player since childhood, my standards are high, but FF7 Remake easily meets them. The soundtrack transcends expectations, and the voice acting rivals that of Disney productions.

However, no game is without its flaws. Chief among them, in my opinion, are the side-quests, which lack the compelling motivation needed to fully engage players beyond the rewards they offer. Additionally, the unavoidable comparison to the original game inevitably diminishes the Remake's standing in some eyes - the original will always be the original. Another notable weakness lies in the inclusion of the whispers, a concept borrowed from the Kingdom Hearts series. While they introduce flexibility and intrigue to the narrative, their presence risks diluting the coherence of the story by introducing too much ambiguity (i.e., the feeling that "anything could happen"). Yet, it's premature to pass final judgement on this element; only the conclusion of the trilogy will reveal its true impact.

Overall, I would give FF7 Remake a stellar rating of 9.3 out of 10. I wholeheartedly recommend it. This remake is truly remarkable and revitalises the classic game in a way that is both nostalgic for those who played the original, like myself, and captivating for newcomers. FF7 stands as a pinnacle in gaming culture, akin to the Don Quixote or Hamlet of video games. Don't miss out on the opportunity to immerse yourself in this unforgettable experience!\t\t\t\t\t
