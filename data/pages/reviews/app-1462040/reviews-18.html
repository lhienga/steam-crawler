Great Game for FFVII Newbies - go Play it!
I am new to the Final Fantasy Universe and thoroughly enjoyed every aspect of the game: from Combat over Story/Cinemtaics and Worldbuilding. The Dialouge is well written and the dynamic between characters leads to an immersive and fun gaming experience.
The Game is divided into multiple Chapters, some of which offer a semi-open world with sidequests and places to discover.
The (rather short) DLC Intermission is fun to play with some new combat and gameplay mechanics and a lot of pop-culture references.
The game allows mods (mainly retextures and character outfits) but i would highly recommend playing the game without mods for the first time!

Gameplay and Mechanics
Combat is real time, but the game offers an "classic" option that lets you focus on giving commands to your partymembers, (apparently) closer to the original ffvii games.
While the easy mode shouldn't give you any trouble, playing on normal can get you into difficult encounters when not being equipped properly. Even then, the game lets you restart encounters to change gear and tactics.
The hard mode partially lives up to his name. Since it is only accessible after beating the game and works like new game+ the first few chapters were pretty easy. Despite that in the mid- to end game fights can get extremly difficult, near to impossible with suboptimal setups/gear/tactics, which makes for a nice challenge (or a lot frustration) Looking at you Weiss and Pride & Joy

Negative Aspects
Sadly some in-game sequences and cutscenes cause quite a lot of lag and sudden framedrops, which isn't ideal. A workaround including mods does however exists.
Normally the enemy targeting works quite well in the game, however one optional minigame in the Intermission DLC is so overfilled with targetable stuff that the target-systems gets a stroke and is just horrible. That minigame was more difficult than the hardest (optional) Boss in the entire Game.
Not necessarily a negative aspect, but a frustrating one: minor spoiler warning: One optional Boss in particular (Weiss) requires near perfect execution and team composition, which i would expect from a souls-like game, but not from a JRPG leading to potentially frustrating moments when you want to get 100% \t\t\t\t\t
