My major gripes with the game:
- side quests are boring and repetitive (fetch quests, kill 5 X)
- there are too many unnecessary fights against random mobs
- gameplay is mostly cool cutscenes that are followed by tedious puzzles and long walks to artifically extend your playtime
- why is there no jump button? And why are there so many obstacles while exploring that Cloud could easily jump over? I mean he can almost fly when fighting...

The story with its memorable characters, the great soundtrack and the overall cinematic feel are very engaging but for reasons stated above the game is not worth the full price imo.
Get it on sale!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo there's good and there's bad.

The good: intense nostalgia for a childhood classic that I've thought about parts of fairly regularly since playing the original when it came out. The graphics are amazing. The voice acting is perfect.

The okay: the active combat system takes some getting used to, and I think a lot of FF7 fans (myself included) would have preferred they kept the turn-based system. Or at least given us a toggle for it.

The bad: your save file from this game won't transfer to FF7 Rebirth when it comes out, so whatever hard work, stockpiled resources, and leveling you do here won't matter in the next game. Your save file WILL unlock a summon in Rebirth, but that's it. For those of us who think in terms of "the long haul" and save things like elixirs for later, that's a big bummer. And since there's no kind of "completion carry-over", it made a lot of the side quests a "meh who cares" for me personally.\t\t\t\t\t
