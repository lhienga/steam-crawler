\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is worth about 25 bucks. Not the 40 bucks I got it for (lowest squeenix will probably ever go) and absolutely not worth 80. It's.. fine but it feels more like FF13 in FF7's setting. You mostly just follow the only road in Midgar towards your objective, stopping 2 or 3 times for some lame filler quests to slow you down and backtrack a little. I can't imagine anyone liking characters like Johnny, or caring about Don Corneo's henchman's background story. I also highly doubt that more than 3% of people enjoy the unskippable flamboyant-homosexual rhythm-game dance scene with obscured, moving inputs that still have the colors of playstation controller buttons.

Combat isn't terrible but could've easily been much better. You only control 1 character at a time and your teammates are almost completely useless unless you assume direct control or until you acquire the ATB-assist materia (which locks you into controlling 1 character). There's a stagger mechanic that's fun and engaging on paper but normal fights only last a few seconds. The enemies either die before getting staggered or instantly after. Then there's all the annoying stun or frog or sleep etc status effects that just put the game on pause. I get that they want you to switch character but the enemy always targets the one you're playing, which means the one you want to play will always be targeted to be removed from the fight. That isn't engaging, it's annoying.
Then there's the boss fights: you slowly work towards staggering them for a chance to deal some proper damage. That is the moment that should make you feel excited and satisfied, when you can unload all your saved up ATB on a huge threat and hopefully deal enough damage before it recovers. Guess what! There's now a short cutscene and the fight goes into a new phase that resets the stagger bar. You just got cucked. Every single time. They barely let you have an actual fight.

I'm still gonna finish it but seriously, just play the original on an emulator or steam. It's so much better than the remake.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe combat is sort of fun, but can feel a little button-mashy at times. It doesn't help that ATB is really clunky - enemies often interrupt your abilities or spells the instant you use them, despite you not having any indication beforehand that they were going to use an attack. Similarly, the tracking on the abilities is really poor, so many times you'll find that you use an ability and then they move an inch to the side and you miss. New enemies can sometimes feel awful to encounter, since you aren't allowed to change materia mid combat - using the elemental damage on a weapon can totally hose you in a way you had no way to predict or prepare for, since they can be resistant or immune to that damage type. I also dislike assessing enemy stats being bound to a materia, since that makes it feel really awful to use when it's basically required in order to strategize for fights.

It feels as if much of the game is padded needlessly. There are sections where you have to slowly shimmy through a tiny crack in a wall, and a few are placed in the towns, meaning you need to frequently go through these gaps to access an area with a merchant or quest and spend 20 seconds watching Cloud scrape his face on a wall each time. There are a number of dungeons that feel totally needless, like the train graveyard. The "puzzles" they add are also awful as well, they're not fun or mentally engaging, or even difficult, just time consuming, boring drudgery. I understand the need for games like this to add periods of "decompression" between action, but that doesn't mean it needs to suck. The exploration in this padding is often not even that engaging, with you running down corridors or tunnels and fighting lots of generic enemies, with the occasional of the aforementioned awful puzzles.

Additionally, the story is interesting and emotionally engaging - at least for the first 80% of the game. Without spoiling things too much, it feels as if it's meant for returning players rather than somebody experiencing it fresh. I never played the original, so many of the ways they implemented plot points felt like they went totally over my head, which is not helped by the fact that this game fills what is apparently only the first 5 hours of the original. There are things that happen that are totally not explained whatsoever. Maybe that worked in original FF7 (Which apparently took many plot points much slower), where you left Midgar in the same game, but when I have to deal with those problems now in THIS game, it feels very weird, since it means I have almost no reason to care about the current plot point.

It's still undeniably fun, but I think if your first introduction to the game is this, then you should probably just play the original, which sucks considering all the various QoL changes and visual improvements you'd be missing out on. As to whether fans of the original will like it, I can't say, since I haven't played it yet, although I will say that the use of certain plot elements, such as the main antagonist, was done in a really clumsy and inexpert fashion, which is chiefly due to plot elements that are apparently totally new in the remake.\t\t\t\t\t
