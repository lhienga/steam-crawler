\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm normally a pretty "here's the good, here's the bad, trying to be fair" kind of reviewer, especially with games I don't recommend. Hard to do that with this game. This game is so boring and poorly designed, it's actually offensive. The game starts off with a lot of combat, which is the only good part, to convince you the game might be cool. I'm convinced they do this on purpose to get you past the refund window, so once they reveal what the game really is, you're screwed and can't refund. The fact that more than a third of players haven't even made it 4 hours into the game to get their first weapon ability (check steam achievements) is extremely telling; tons of players just get bored and stop playing.

Graphics and combat system are very good. I really enjoy the combat, reminds me of Star Ocean: Till The End of Time. But that's it.

Level design is literally non existent. Everything is corridor after corridor. No sense of exploration at ALL. Open spaces? Junk or rocks in the way to make you run down corridors. Town area? Corridor after corridor. Invisible walls every step you take. The game is so linear in its level design and story progression that it's borderline claustrophobic.
Unbelievable amount of wasted time from drawn out cut scenes, drawn out dialogue, etc...Every 2 minutes, the game takes control away from the player to do a mini cut scene, force the player to walk slowly while NPCs dump bad dialogue, shimmying between rocks like it's GoW or Tomb Raider. Absolutely everything is drawn out and slow, other than combat.
Quest design is abysmal. Here's a real one. You're told there are rats you need to kill, but are warned that fighting them will draw bigger rats. You go kill the rats. You must then go back to the quest giver, tell him you killed the rats, then run all the way back to the same exact spot to fight the bigger rats, then return to him for a worthless reward. Back and forth and back and forth. MMOs have better side quests. Literally worse than the endless, randomly-generated side quests from Elder Scrolls.
Gear is stripped down and character leveling and management is very streamlined. You had more control over your party and characters in Kingdom Hearts II in 2003(?).

I played and beat the original. I went into this knowing it was not going to be the same, I heard there was some filler content and things were slow but I was ok with some of that; I was drawn in by the combat system. Unfortunately, this game is so offensively bad and boring that even the stellar combat couldn't save it, and the other reviewers did not adequately describe just how awful the game really is, hence my review. I got it for half off, what a waste of 35 dollars. Avoid this game like the plague unless you are someone who loves every horrible, boring open world and AAA trope imaginable, loves poor level design with ZERO exploration, and enjoys 90% filler content in their games.

The fact that this has Very Positive reviews on Steam says an awful lot about us gamers. People that enjoy this game are either completely blinded by nostalgia, or are on the same level as people that religiously watch every installment of the Fast and the Furious and think they are good movies. I wouldn't trust their opinion on "good movies", and I was definitely misled by the reviews into thinking this was a good game. It's a complete failure in almost every conceivable way. What an insulting disappointment, and one of the most lazy and soulless projects Capcom has ever made. If the characters were literally anyone else other than already beloved characters, this game would've been universally panned. I'm gonna tell myself it's the power of nostalgia allowing this game to be successful; it's the only way I'll be able to sleep at night.

In a world where Taylor Swift, the creator of uninspired, by the numbers pop songs (many of which are offensively bad) is the most famous musician in the world, I guess it shouldn't surprise me that people will give a thumbs up to a game with practically no real substance or game play outside of combat that is locked within small rooms and corridors. Entertainment made for the lowest common denominator.

If you are reading this as a new player, I would highly suggest Googling "FF7 remake boring" and read all the people echoing the same sentiment as me. If you're someone who played and loved the game and you're mad at my review, you'd be better off screaming at a box of rocks; at least that way you can argue with something intellectually equivalent.\t\t\t\t\t
