\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFF13 made me loose all hope in my former favourite series. I expected this to be the same kind of badly written, terribly paced and cutscene-heavy cashgrab!

FF7 remake is thankfully not like that. It is a well made interpretation of an old story, catered to modern expectations. These modern expectations might not be for everybody - I don't particularly like the combat, the "forced walk&talk" and magic barriers, that prohibit exploration. I had to take a break from the game after walking aeris home.

On the other hand I understand why its being done and after almost 12 hours, I believe that it is the only way to bring the story of FF7 back in the 2020s in a meaningfull manner and reach more than the handfull of nostalgia-fans that still haven't lost all hope for squaresoft.

It might not be my kind of game but despite that I am being entertained and don't have the feeling like the devs took a piss on my childhood.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR:
Final Fantasy VII Remake presents a mixed experience, with a solid story and character dialogue but questionable additions like the Whispers. Enjoyable graphics, voice acting, and music, but fetch quests and tedious mechanics detract. Combat falls short, and certain segments feel artificially lengthened. Additionally, retroactive censorship of Tifa's character is noted. Despite flaws, it's an enjoyable journey, earning a 7/10 (Buy on sale)

Summary
Final Fantasy VII Remake presents a mixed experience, leaning heavily on nostalgia for fans of the original game. While the story and character dialogue shine, the addition of certain elements, like the Whispers, feels questionable. Despite enjoyable aspects like the likable character designs, the game suffers from fetch quests and tedious mechanics that detract from the overall experience. Combat attempts a blend of turn-based and real-time elements but falls short, and certain segments feel artificially lengthened. However, the game's graphics, voice acting, and music are commendable, contributing to an enjoyable if flawed experience. Whether its appeal lies in its faithfulness to the original or genuine quality remains uncertain. Overall, Final Fantasy VII Remake offers an enjoyable but imperfect journey, earning a 7/10

Positives:
+ The story is good
+ The dialog between characters is great
++ They managed to stretch 10 hours of gameplay into 30 without it being too different while respecting the original game
+ Cinematography is really good, with nice camera shots
++ Cinematographer should be promoted
+ Graphics look good
+ Voice acting was good
+ Music was good
+ Beat the game and all achievements and didn't experience any bugs
+ Optimization, no problems

Neutral:
- Not sure on the addition of the Whispers, so far it feel like a negative but we'll see
- Some mechanics you use once, hopefully used in rebirth more, Red's run, Barret's wall breaking

Negatives:
- Price is absolutely crazy, definitely only buy on sale
- Tifa is censored retro actively
- Fetch quests
-- But what's worse, the fetch quests are purposely made very tediously, they purposely make you back track to the farthest areas to complete
-- Quests are not interesting at all and none of the story from them is meaningful, feels forced into the game
-- Quest designer should be fired while the cinematographer should be promoted
- Combat is terrible
--They tried to keep turn base while also adding real time and failed horribly at both
- NPC's over all are terrible, clothing design wrong, don't fit atmosphere
-- I get the forced diversity in 2024, but these are so awkward
- Character movement gets clunky and times for no reason
- Feels like part of the game was just designed to add time to the game
-- Puzzles are boring and just add time
-- Quests are extremely tedious and just add time
-- Some of the chapters are even filler and just add time
- Not until the end of the game do you experience the part of Final Fantasy 7's story that made the game great
-- Cloud's memory loss and Sephiroth wanting to destroy the planet

Good:
