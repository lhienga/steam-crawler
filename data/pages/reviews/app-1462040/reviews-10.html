\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
Gameplay-wise, the combat system of this game is extremely fun and well thought out. Each character has their own strengths/weaknesses, and you will need to utilize them properly to make a character shine. Additionally, there is a weapon skill and meteria skill (magic equips) system that allows for further customization to your play style. Early on these systems shine, and you get some great fights.

I fell in love with the combat system on the first boss, where everything was balanced and a huge challenge. Later on though you can cheese the combat system with non-MP using abilities (like pray and chakra).

The game has incredible set pieces, and unique environments that really bring the world to life.

The bad:
The story is confusing and the game is obviously created for ff7 fans. I have not played the original games so I'm not picking up on the bread crumb trail that they are leaving behind.

Outside of the combat, this game does not have that much to offer. Most of the rest of the game is fighting easy cannon fodder monsters, or running around trying to figure out where the quest needs to be completed. Some of the quests were especially confusing. And sometimes the running around there are events that slow the game down and get old such as squeezing through a narrow coorridor, or crouching into a small passage way. They do build the world atmospherically, but the time it takes to traverse these obstacles is too long and becomes repetative and annoying.

The pacing. Sometimes this game slows down right before different climaxes which is a strange design decision.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust Finished. Non-Spoiler Small Subjective Review.

Thank you for an amazing game, it was a great experience as a person who's never completed any Final Fantasy game before. Despite being slightly spoiled already, along with my vague memories of such spoilers as it is an old game from 2020, I finally decided to buy the game and play it anyways, which by far was a very fun and awesome experience to play-through despite the vague memories I have. Final Fantasy remake is probably the first ever single-player game I have completed throughout my list of uncompleted games and I don't regret choosing this as my first game to complete.

Main Story:
At the time I started playing, I haven't played the Original Final Fantasy 7, so I wouldn't really know what goes deeper into the lore or any references pertaining to it, other than what the game tells and shows me, despite that, I still found the story immersive, and interesting to continue on in wanting to know more.

The character and story development still kept me attentive throughout the play-through and with a bit of research during some chapters out of curiosity, and after the main stories ending with plenty of videos online to watch to explain it further, it gives you that wow factor as I just really love the "multiple timeline" concepts in story lore, and damn I enjoyed everything what FF7 revolves around.

Intermission DLC:
The DLC introduces 2 new characters. Although, the DLC only adds 2 chapters which to its entirety is a 5 hours play-through if story is the only focus, apart from the added mini-game. The short story for the DLC to introduce these characters does extremely well in presenting these characters in such a time frame where you feel a connection to them, and I deeply enjoyed that part of the experience.

Gameplay and Animation:
The combat and the tactical gameplay of this game were very enjoyable, it never felt tiring or tedious despite even doing the boss battles. The slow motion in tactical mode when selecting the items, skill sets, and more kept me immersed in the combat. I love how party management works, switching between characters and having to tactically switch because it is needed to effectively beat your enemies. it was fun fudbnsap[fuo9bn.

I really enjoyed the quality of the animation in this game, it's insane. Most games I have seen on Youtube or have tried out usually feel like an interactive movie with alright gameplay for their combat to get you through that story-telling of the game. Somehow Final Fantasy 7 Remake feels like a movie but on top of it an actual entertaining game.

Not only you feel immersed in such amazing cinematic experiences from CGI to in-game cut scene animations, but also how fun and immersive the overall gameplay that makes it such a great combination. As a person who has tried multiple single-player games but never completed any, I could never really find myself procrastinating and delay completion of FF7 Remake, it was that fun and immersive I had to continue playing all throughout the week.

Replayability:
There is a hard mode and chapter selection after the first playthrough, which I am about to do so I can get all the outfits, different scenes, despite searching it on Youtube. There is the Wall Market Coliseum and Shinra Combat Simulator as well, there is quite the amount of replay value this game has which for me justifies the value of 40$ (on-sale) I spent.

----
Making FF7 Remake one of my very first completed single-player games was the best decision I have done. Hopefully, FF7 Rebirth comes to Steam eventually.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrankly i wish i could say its a good game.
The story is stellar. I mean, its really good and close to the OG.
But they tried to be action WHILE STILL remaining in the turn by turn.
So they created a clunky fighting style that is ACTION, but you have to WAIT YOUR TURN.
For examples, there are phases of boss, you just cant do nothing. When they attack you, you CANNOT DO ANYTHING. You have to wait and block, to mitigate damage as much as possible.
So it is counter intuitive. You are not really playing an action game. Its a turn by turn with the illusion its action.
I really dislike it. Also, you might want to TANK some monsters with one of your character, to set up a group attack let say, and you switch to another character to attack the enemy from behind.
Well the game is CHEAP and will AUTOMATICALLY aggro the character you are selecting.
Resulting in you GETTING HIT no matter what you do.
Remember when i told you its a turn by turn with the illusion of action?
Thats what i meant.
So by trying to appease to the old players with a turn by turn AND appease to newer player by being action, they landed in the middle succeeding in nothing.
Its just disagreeable, and frankly stupid.
Just embrace the action style 100%.
Make it so that MY SKILL are rewarded. And allow us to have an aggro table so we can set up our party combos.
Worst action game i have ever played.

Again, i am an OG fan, who wished they would have went full action. It would have suited the game better in how they present it.
I have to admit, the graphic are amazing, the flow of the story is very good. And if you have played Crisis core before its even better.
But the game play is abysmal. I hope they have made it better in Rebirth.
If its the same, i will not play it.
The game play was better in Crisis Core. Felt a lot LOT better. It felt like what you would expect.
But in remake? It feels cluncky and unrewarding.\t\t\t\t\t
