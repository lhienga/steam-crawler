\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview from someone who is playing a final fantasy game for the first time and generally new to JRPG so I have no bias or nostalgia:

1. Sequences outside combat are super slow. Cutscenes interrupt gameplay every 1-2 mins. Animations take away your control for a little too long, to the point where I was getting frustrated at how slow my character stands up from a bench. Virtually no actual exploration, game is extremely linear with simple puzzles- I may as well be watching a movie.

2. Story deals with serious issues but it's undermined by cartoonish characters, voice acting and writing. Story doesn't make sense in a lot of places, at least to me. Dude is caught on camera blowing up a reactor, has a very recognizable sword on his back and walks town unrecognized by citizens/guards? Maybe I'm missing something.

3. Combat is fun but the occasional difficulty is for all the wrong reasons. Some "bosses" ended up being a battle of chipping away their health while guzzling consumables rather than learning their moves and how to counter them. And even then the game is way too easy.

5/10 being generous, not sure how this game can get stellar reviews without nostalgia.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen FF7 originally released back in 1997, I got it for my 8th birthday and was completely absorbed by it. I revisited the game multiple times over the years and was really excited about the announcement of a remake. I am really late to the party, but finally got around to playing it myself. I love how not only the revamped music but also a good amount of the gameplay scenes from the original create a good mix of nostalgia and a new approach to the game from a different angle that fits newer titles. I am having a blast with it. If I had to nitpick something, it would be the slightly janky movements when you jump through terrain or climb stuff. Some cutscenes also seem to have almost no sound effects. Despite those minor inconveniences, they really did a good job of transforming one of my childhood's favorite games so a new audience that wouldn't engage with old graphics can enjoy the story as well.\t\t\t\t\t
