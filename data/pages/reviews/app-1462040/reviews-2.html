\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, now that I've completed FF7: Remake in most of it's entirety, I feel ready to post a review. Though, the game has been available for 4 years now in one way or another so...I can't imagine this review will be useful for anyone. At just over 100 hours I've beaten the main campaign, DLC campaign, and Hard mode main campaign.

So what can I say that hasn't been said already? Can't think of much really. Many people praise this game. And some have ridiculed it. I don't think either side is 100% spot on. There's definitely moments that make this game worth playing. It's actually quite fun at times. But there's also moments of frustration, some of which I overcame when I changed my strategy the second time around. Most complaints I've heard had to do with the story but I recon the gameplay is what deserves to be focused on when it comes to criticism.

It's a fairly interesting attempt to blend turn based elements of rpgs with action elements of action games. This can make for moments that do seem a little unfair. Granted, if you have the right strategy it's not insurmountable but it does seem a bit unbalanced when you have to wait for your meter to build to use an ability (like healing for example) and the boss enemies can just spam and spam their special abilities. But again, the second time through I developed a more refined strategy and everything worked out.

As for the story, I think the criticism is understandable to a degree. This isn't a 1:1 remake of Final Fantasy 7 and I'm not going to bother going too deep into all of it. All I'll say is I, among others I have to imagine, would've loved an M rated 1:1 remake of Final Fantasy 7 with all the dark elements and creepy vibes the original had. But I knew, the moment this game was announced, that was absolutely not what we were going to get. Things have changed over the past 25+ years. Hard stop.

So, if there was one thing I would tell someone thinking about playing this game, it would be this. Play the original first. You must play the original to understand some of the story segments of this game.

If I could tell Square Enix something, it would be this. You shouldn't have called this Final Fantasy Remake. It's hard to believe after all the marketing and showing off they did of this game before it launched but I've actually read remarks of other people who were down right angry they bought this game because they were expecting a full game. I've even seen people ask "Is this the full game or just part of it?" Yeah...the name leads you to believe it's a full remake when it is in fact only the first part of a trilogy.

Despite that, it still ends up feeling like a full game it's just the story isn't completely resolved in the end. And I wouldn't tell anyone not to play and wait for all the games to release because there really is some fun to be had with this game. I was on the fence after watching a playthrough years ago but picked it up on sale and I'd have to say it was a worthy purchase. I feel satisfied.\t\t\t\t\t
