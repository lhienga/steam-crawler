\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe OG FF7 was one of my favorite games growing up and I knew I was going to be highly critical of this going in, so I'll try to keep the rant short as possible (just kidding this will be a rant).

++++The Good++++
Map design - Arguably the main selling point. Experiencing Midgar up close in 4k alone almost makes it worth it (almost). Everything is in place, down to the last poster or pile of garbage. This part, at least, was flawlessly done.

Music - It's a Final Fantasy game, no ♥♥♥♥ it has good music. Even if you pass on the game it's still worth a listen.

Combat - I enjoyed it. Or at least I would have a lot more if Hard mode wasn't progress-locked. Do not be fooled, this is not an ARPG, this is a turn based ATB game that just so happens to let you swing your sword and dodge roll in between turns. If you go in wiggling your noodle like it's a Devil May Cry Game you'll probably end up getting filtered.

Most Original Characters - To my pleasant surprise, most of the OG characters act like they are supposed to act, with one unfortunate exception I'll get into later. We get schizoid-autistic merc Cloud, not sad emo Advent Children Cloud. Aerith and Tifa are the unattainably perfect feminine ideals they have always been (God bless them). Barret is the angry black guy with the heart of gold, etc etc. It's all as it should be, at least here in the first 'episode'.

++++The Meh++++
Filler - I personally am a fan of stupidly long jrpg grinds, so I don't mind this part. Everyone else complains about it so I guess you'll probably hate it too. It is what it is.

++++The Bad++++
Sanitized - All the gore and edgy jokes from the original have been thoroughly scrubbed from the game. They even took out the bleeped out F-bombs. Maybe that's cool to you if you're the emotionally sensitive type but it takes a lot of soul out of the game and even leads to narrative problems later.

Most New Characters - With one or two exceptions all of the new characters are unlikable and add nothing to the overall story. Roach is a tool. Johnny is unadulterated cringe in human form. Whoever wrote Andrea's lines should be on a watchlist. Don't even get me started on some of the absolute specimens who flag you down to do their fetch quest dirty work.

Sephiroth - In the original game, Sephiroth was a gnomic, enigmatic psychopath who could only be identified by the trail of gore he left in his wake and he barely even knows who Cloud is until around halfway through the game. They had the opportunity to make him into something actually terrifying in this remake. Instead, every time Cloud gets a headache, this dude shows up all like "AY GURL YOU REMEMBER THAT TIME WE WENT TO APPLEBEES THAT ♥♥♥♥ WAS SWEET AMITIRTE ANYWAY HERES SOME SPOILERS FROM DISC 2 LMAO". And since there is no Edgyboy Gorelord ♥♥♥♥ allowed anymore, I guess he plays with spooky multiverse ghosts instead so he can harness the power of cringe to enter the Kingdom Hearts dimension. Because ♥♥♥♥ you, that's why. To be quite honest I don't even know who this jackass is. This isn't Sephiroth. This is some hollywood sex pest in Sephiroth cosplay.

Whispers - What's that you say? Spirits who fly around forcing you back on track when you veer away from your destined course? That's a brilliant idea. They should have let a few loose in the Square Enix building so they could have stopped Nomura from taking a giant dump on the greatest story he ever had the privilege of working on in his life.

Bad port - Lazy lazy lazy. Stuttery, buggy, trashy mess. Practically no KB+M support (good luck on that bike minigame lololol), mismatched glyphs, etc etc etc. So basically, the usual from Square Enix.

++++The Ugly++++
NPCs - All the random NPCs around the world have had their designs heavily westernized to the point where they look like they belong more in a Tony Hawk game than Final Fantasy. They are dull, ugly, soulless and quite frankly reek of the cynical DEI tokenism that's been infecting the games industry lately. They look wildly out of place next to any of the original characters and they never, ever, ever shut up. And I mean EVER. (HEY YOU WANT SOME CHILI BEANS) It's a design choice that serves no purpose other than to make a beautiful game uglier.

The fanbase - "WOW I CANT BELEEBE YOU THINK TIFA SHOULD B VOLUPTUOUS JUST BECAUSE THAT'S BEEN HER CHARACTER DESIGN FOR ALMOST 30 YEARS UR SUCH A TOXIC PORN ADDICT LOL UWU" (replays honey bee inn dance scene for the 20th time while writing CloudxSephiroth slash fiction).

++++The Inexcusable++++
Episodic - I guess Square Enix was getting tired of FFXIV being their only cash cow, but taking your flagship game and chopping it up into three half-assed science experiments over the course of a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ decade was definitely not the way to go about it.

Price - SEVENTY(70) American Dollery-Doos for a third of the game you got in the 90s. If you buy this at full price I don't know what to tell you but I hope your life gets better.

Retroactive censorship - I do not give a single solitary ♥♥♥♥ what specifically it is they censored. Retroactive censorship is a very very dangerous and very very stupid idea. Today it's just putting a modest outfit on a female character, tomorrow they've changed the ending of your favorite game because some baby-eating ghoul in US congress thought your game had a problematic message. Ignore the warnings of schizo doom-sayers at your own peril.

++++The Conclusion++++

Despite all the flaws I still think the game might be worth it... in 2030... when a complete version comes out and you can pick it up on sale for under a hundred dollars.... maybe.... assuming they can salvage a decent ending from all the indulgent changes they've made to a once beloved story (Don't hold your breath on that last part though).

But on the other hand, and this is the worst part of it all, is that despite what a huge mess it is, it's STILL a standard deviation of quality above almost all the other AAA slop that gets churned out these days. And that's just sad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt’s rare for a game to wear me down so much that I mentally bail out of what’s happening. This was about 12 out of 25 hours into the game. This game has so many issues that it’ll be a challenge to fit all of it into the character limit. It was a real trial to get to the end.
First the part that I hate the most: The localisation. I hate it, it sucks, I’d rather play a game that was translated by AI. Whoever did this, damn you.
I sincerely believe this game is padding the runtime big time. If I could edit my playthrough I would’ve cut out every slow walk, every slow crawl, every slow walk over a beam, every slow walk in a tight space or on the edge, every meaningless “traversal” mini-game, every monkey bar area, every repetitive lever pulling BS. Then I’d also edit out every predictable cutscene that didn’t need to exist, that could’ve been summarised with some in-game dialogue “let’s go here?”, “Ok”. If I edited all of that out I’d be left with about 6-8 hours of gameplay where actual things happened, instead of time wasting activities with dialogue that doesn’t go anywhere, mean anything or set up anything for later. There are way too many cutscenes. It’s not even funny anymore. Sometimes it loads you in, lets you walk 2 steps and then starts a cutscene. That’s just taking the piss.
The next hard to swallow pill is that the 6-8 hours when things are “actually” happening aren’t very interesting or compelling either. A quarter of that is the plot, which is fanfiction level nonsense written by people who love Sephiroth way too much. The ghosts are actual in-game plot armor. Preserving the flow of destiny is total bs, considering how Sephiroth is subverting it and yet they either serve him or are just part of him. It’s nonsense. Why write a story so broken that you have to add plot armor ghosts? Backwards priorities. Every time the ghost event happens in the plot it’s random and annoying. Even worse when you also have to “fight” them, which is always an empty hassle, another time waster that does nothing.
The way characters are written is unappealing. Very shallow and surface level, borderline parody.
Oddly enough, the one that has something going for him is Barret, who is the most over the top out of all of them. Yet he feels alive, like a real person and even has a small character arc. Cloud changes a little bit as well. Just a tiny bit.
The way you progress through the game is akin to a theme park ride. Interaction is an illusion, exploration is an illusion, and choice is nonexistent. Everything is railroaded, with constant road blocks and interruptions. You can’t even skip a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ladder, you have to get into position and engage the arrows to put yourself on rails to do the ladder animation, like you’re playing Witcher 2 and can’t climb anything or get through an obstacle without a set queue. It reminds me a lot of Witcher 2 in everything related to exploration. But I’d rate W2 higher, because it was more fun and didn’t have that god awful 3x mechanical arm manipulation section. There’s so much slow walking, crawling, crouching, etc. All of these sections need a skip button, it’s horrific torture that lasts for the full 25 hours.
The locations are always sewers, slums, factories, broken buildings or literal garbage areas, with maybe 2-3 notable exceptions. Graphically they look good. But it’s still a graphically impressive ugly factory or garbage heap.
The other 3/4ths are the combat, which is frankly mediocre and undercooked. At least it gets the accolade of being mediocre at first, and then it goes really to sh1t later. Most boss fights are easy but awful and full of jank. Boss uses a move that makes a horn light up, like it’s a specific indicator. You destroy his horn, he still uses that attack. Why even bother designing it like it’s an indicator? Everything to do with flying enemies is jank to begin with, but when you factor in buildings, ceiling and stairways, the jank becomes next level.
The levelling system is irrelevant, just a tacked on busy work to pad out your stats on the weapon.
What’s praiseworthy? The graphics, character designs, animations and the music. The problem is that the graphics are so good that the way the two main heroines act is incredibly unnatural. It’s very uncanny valley, like I’m watching beautiful alien dolls try to mimic humans, but all they’ve ever had to learn about us is anime.
So my verdict is that this is a very disappointing piece of eye-candy. The padding seems to be an indication that they were aware that they didn’t have much of a story to work with.
