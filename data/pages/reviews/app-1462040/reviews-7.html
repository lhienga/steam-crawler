\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed the original back on the PSX and loved it.
If you played the original, I'd say skip this: it's not the game you remember.

The production quality in this title is absolutely fantastic - amazing graphics and it runs great on a mid/low-end rig (even on Steam Deck). But... that's only going to carry a game so far.

This version might be what the creators would have made if current technology existed back then but, for me, the extra storage and power that they have available to them now has been squandered on overly-long in-engine cut scenes, bad writing, and hammy voice-overs.

The game starts strong but the last 10 hours of it have been an absolute drag. Once you hit Shinra HQ the level design drops off a cliff and becomes a mundane, on-rails affair with zero opportunity for exploration of alternate routes. The pacing of the story is really odd: you're constantly on the verge of a finale, fighting a looong cadre of boss characters, with the feeling that the crescendo is just a battle away. But every encounter ends in an inevitable escape by said antagonist, rendering the battles wholly unsatisfying and a complete chore.
By this time in the game you have already maxed your characters out and so there is no progression to be gained from these battles - they are simply fluff and padding, extending your Time Played stat but not moving the plot or the gameplay forward in any way.
The highway battle section when leaving Midgar is particularly cynical: there is no skill to the combat, you can't cast magic or use any abilities - all you can do is slash left or slash right, and try to hit the boss robots as many times as possible between their attacks, slowly eroding their massive HP bars. Utterly dull.

I absolutely cannot wait for this grind to end, and I can't see myself buying the second instalment or even playing the Yuffie expansion missions that come with this title.\t\t\t\t\t
