\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a devoted fan of the original Final Fantasy 7, I approached the Remake with a mixture of excitement and trepidation. Could Square Enix possibly recreate the magic of the beloved classic while bringing it into the modern era? In short: yes, they absolutely could, and they did so with stunning finesse.

From the moment I booted up Final Fantasy 7 Remake, I was greeted with a breathtaking visual spectacle. The meticulously crafted world of Midgar, once a pixelated dream, now sprawled out before me in stunning detail. Every alleyway, every towering skyscraper, felt alive with a sense of history and purpose.

But visuals alone do not make a great game. Fortunately, Remake excels in every department. The gameplay, a delicate balance of real-time action and strategic decision-making, feels both fresh and faithful to the original. Combat is fluid and dynamic, with each character possessing their own unique abilities and playstyle. Whether I was controlling the swift and agile Cloud or the powerful magic user Aerith, battles were always exhilarating and satisfying.

Yet, it's not just the gameplay that captivates; it's the narrative. Final Fantasy 7's story has always been its crowning jewel, and Remake only serves to enhance it. By expanding upon the events of the original game and delving deeper into the motivations of its characters, Square Enix has crafted a narrative that feels both familiar and entirely new. Moments that I thought I knew by heart took on new significance, while new twists and turns kept me on the edge of my seat until the very end.

Of course, none of this would matter if it weren't for the game's stellar presentation. From the hauntingly beautiful score to the impeccable voice acting, every aspect of Final Fantasy 7 Remake oozes with polish and care. It's clear that this is a project that was made with love and reverence for the source material.

In the end, Final Fantasy 7 Remake is more than just a game; it's an experience. It's a journey through a world both familiar and new, filled with unforgettable characters and moments that will stay with me long after the credits roll. Whether you're a longtime fan of the series or someone experiencing it for the first time, Remake is a must-play masterpiece that sets a new standard for what a modern RPG can achieve. Square Enix has not only succeeded in recapturing the magic of the original but has elevated it to new heights, and for that, they deserve all the praise in the world.\t\t\t\t\t

Playing the remake feels like it was made for me, which in a way i suppose it was. Everything is here, the train graveyard, wall market, the materia system even the cinematics are shot like the originals. As far as changes, Cloud is probably the biggest departure for me so far, he's got a personality outside of being mopey but he is most definitely still mopey so same ol' Cloud but more fleshed out. It looks fantastic, the combat is smooth and actually really works, the game wants you to constantly switch characters in combat to which can take some getting used to but once it clicks you can really dominate when you figure out weaknesses and timing your characters attacks.

The port is good, it hasn't crashed on me once but the options are limited aside from the basics so do yourselves a favour and download the Flawless widescreen options for the game as well.

Plays very well on my rig @1440p - 3700x/2080ti

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
