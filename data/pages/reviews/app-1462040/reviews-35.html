\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing for a couple dozen hours I am finding that the repetition of areas (e.g. you have to run back through say the sewer 4 or 5 times on different missions) has kind of burned me out on this game. I understand maximizing your design hours, but this is a bit much. After the first 2 or 3 times through they introduce chocobo travel points, but they mainly just cut a few minute of walking off. The repetition really gets to me.

The combat is fun, until you get to the point where everything either knocks you down or staggers you. Making the main character the most annoying one to play as in any combat. Barrett is much more fun and I found myself switching to him constantly. However most the power of the group is in Cloud, and he wont do crap when controlled by the AI

And finally pulling out my "back in my day" card, the action combat of the modern FF games just blows. I know it to capture those with short attention spans for strategy, but it really just make the game feel clunky to me. No where near as interesting as the first iteration. Maybe if there was a true pause system while you were making selections in the menus it would be better. But frankly I hate action Final Fantasy, and I doubt I will buy any more of the games in the series. It has been done to death in other games, and much better implemented.

Overall its a meh story now with a lot of chud work. I don't feel like i am getting anywhere half the time and the maps are beyond confusing when you are trying to find your next objective. Tifa is cute, and the casual cussing is funny. That is about it.\t\t\t\t\t
