\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI beat this game when it first released in 2020, thoroughly enjoyed it then. Story holds up, I actually do enjoy the new content that was added (Though some story changes I don't entirely agree with). Definitely recommend playing if you are a fan to the FFVII franchise. I am very surprised by how well the controls translated to keyboard and the fact that by default full keybind customization is available. The game seems mostly optimized, it's definitely one of the more solid Square Enix ports. The new battle system feels even better on PC and the experience is vastly more fluid that it was on the base PS4 (Cannot comment much on the PS5 version). The JP voice acting is fantastic, the English cast is quite good as well, though definitely more 'anime-like,' though that is overall the setting for VII compared to more recent FFs like XIV or XVI. I STRONGLY recommend playing the original game first as well as Crisis Core. You'll feel vastly more connected to the characters this way.

My main complaints would be the timed epic games store exclusivity that this game once suffered, and some of the story changes are a little jarring at times. I also don't believe the game should be $69.99 despite the fact it comes with the INTERmission DLC. It's a 4 year old game now, and I believe the price should reflect that. Overall it's a great game, the story is touching, and adds so much more to the beginning of VII that was outstanding. Additionally the OST is a masterpiece, it was the right decision to bring along Masashi Hamauzu. 100% recommend to any FF enjoyer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first Final Fantasy I've ever beaten. While I know every character and most story beats from the original FF7, I was still pretty new to the world of this game.

The combat is amazing and feels hefty. The mix of turn based and action was implemented nicely and had me coming back for more after every session.

The visuals are probably the most impressive part of this game. There were many moments where i got into an area where I had never seen that much style and fidelity at once, not even in more modern games. Square Enix knows how to make good looking things, characters and all.

The story is awkward at times. I had a lot of issues with the way how so many characters reacted very cryptically to certain events, but that's just how Final Fantasy games be. If you turn your brain off for some parts and just look at the cool concepts it gets a bit better.
Also the fate ghost antics and all of Sephiroths encounters should have only happened towards the end when they're in the shinra lab. Every early encounter with them felt tacked on and kind of ruined my enjoyment of many story moments in the first 15 chapters.

My biggest issue with this game is its pacing. A lot of the time I felt like certain story moments could have ended 2 hours earlier, but because FF7 Remake consists of only the first few chapters of the original game, they padded many parts with unnecessary combat sections. The worst offender being the lost children train station part, which happened simultaneously while the plate was going to collapse and avalanche members were under attack. I didnt care for anything happening in the train station, I wanted it to be over the whole time.

Towards the end I got more accustomed to the storytelling and how characters behave, and the gigantic fights with over the top visuals also helped make the experience unlike anything I've played before. I will 100% play the trilogy.
