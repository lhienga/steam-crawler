\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my favorite iteration of Final Fantasy VII thus far. I much prefer this over the original. The years haven't really been kind to the original in my eyes and this shows how far we've come since then. The storytelling and pacing in this game is very good, as I've come to expect from Square Enix at this point in time. If you ignore side quests, the main story has great pacing and the characters have good character development. There are a couple sections in the game that drag a little, but overall, it doesn't drag down the experience.

I do like the combat in this game quite a bit. It may not be like the original game, but I enjoy the action combat that has become the norm for recent Final Fantasy games. It's executed well here, allowing for multiple playstyles to shine as well as for mastery to exist. Each character has a very distinct moveset and playstyle that can be molded further with personal customizations via the materia and weapon upgrade system. Mastery of the system can allow for very powerful combinations. I will say that there is a difficulty spike nearing end-game. Sometimes, I found myself in situations where I would just be juggled to death without the ability to defend myself. It is rare. So perhaps I just got unlucky.

Overall, the package is great. It is way up my list of favorite games of all time. I'd highly recommend this if you're new to Final Fantasy VII. If you're a fan of the old game, don't expect the old game coming into this as you'll be very disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is quite possible the most hokey game I have every played. If you can’t sit through more than 10 minutes of a Fast & Furious movie then for sure do not play this game.

The main character is apparently so cool that he can ignore or grunt at people and yet everyone will fawn over him. All the female characters act like 15 year old girls on their birthday at all times. The main character grunts at them and they continue to have this same demeanor because they are apparently all so cutesy and enthusiastic at all times and the main character is apparently so cool that his bad vibes are countered by hour cool he is.

The tone of the game is like the most hokey Hollywood action film you have every seen mixed with JRPG tropes.

The main characters have a motorcycle chase where they murder about 30 people and the main character jumps from his motorcycle to this battle’s main enemy’s motorcycle, ruins the engine, jumps back, all while each motorcycle is going about 100 miles an hour. And afterwards the main character is cool as a cucumber and the female characters continue being cutest and act as if they just went to a nice cafe, as opposed to having a 100 mile an hour motorcycle chase where they almost died multiple times and murdered about 30 people.

Is the combat good? It seems good to me. I’ve only played a few hours but I can’t take it anymore because the tone of the game and the tropes and the action movie presentation and feel is too much to bear any longer.
