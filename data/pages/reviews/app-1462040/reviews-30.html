\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say, this is a fantastic JRPG experience.
Where to begin? The worldbuilding is amazing, the game plunges you into a well thought out and visually stunning world. The attention to detail is really great. The characters are so well written and their interactions feel genuine. Their friendship is also a highlight, I love how Cloud slowly warms up, I love how Tifa and Aerith becomes great friends, I love how Barrett comes to respect Cloud. There's so many great moments, I got really attached to these characters. The combat is also a highlight, a great mix of turn based and action RPG, feels fluid and satisfying. Also really original, I've never played a game that had that mix of combat. Story wise wise it's very uneven, there's times when the story is really good and paced well and there's times where it's dragged on. Flaws wise, this is a remake I shouldn't have to play the original to understand the ending. You spend the whole game not knowing who the heck is the big baddie Sephiroth and the ending doesn't do anything. "Yes but in the original it's the same!" Yeah but this is a full game, the original still had 90% of the game left. What should've been done was ending with the Rebirth flashback sequence imo. The ending is too confusing for those who haven't played the original and yeah that's fine but don't call it remake then. There's also times when the gameplay really slows down like going through a small corridor and it's so slow, really messes with the flow. This is 10 hours stretched into 40 hours and it shows, which is a damn shame, I don't mind having 3 parts, I just wish the would've cut out the parts better. Also yes this a very linear game but I feel like it enables the game to truly focus on the world and the combat, and I was fine with it.

Summary: A fantastic game with an uneven story and ending that leaves to be desired but an all around amazing experience with strong worldbuilding and characters. I highly recommend, as I played it, I understood why FFVII is so highly revered. Also plays BEAUTIFULLY on SteamDeck!\t\t\t\t\t
