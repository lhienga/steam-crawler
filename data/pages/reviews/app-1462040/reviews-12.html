\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLeveldesign/Gameplay:
There is way to much handholding in this game, its more like a linear movie than a game. I dont mean that in a good way. The world is beautifully crafted but thats it. I guess that almost all the 90+ Gigabyte Data comes from level design. Yes you can look at it a little while and think wow looks nice, but they dont do anything with it, its just wasted space.

Fightsystem:
The new fighting system is ok, it works cant complain at least not about the functionality. But its also wasted because why have the fights if almost all of them feel lifeless. Again its like hey there are 3 areas go there and fight noname monster for a sidequest. Do you need their material? No. Not really. Do they challange you? No. Not really even on hard. So do you do it to get immersed? No. Its more annoying and distracting from the main quest you are on, the timing just does not fit.

Charakter:
Yeah they nailed most of them, but it isnt that hard if you have decades of source material.

In the end its a movie/handholding/tutorial type of game. After my 10 hours I thought when does the handholding end and tutorial stop? Maybe there is a wast World around the corner.
Thats when I googled to look it up and yeah now we are here. Sorry to dissapoint you this game is nothing more than a long Story/Demo/Tutorial. Do I buy the other part? Defenetly not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, gameplay is solid.
Story is kinda different compared to the original but I think both new and returning FF7 players will appreciate it nonetheless.

The title is misleading though.
This is specifically and exclusively the Midgar section of the game.
Anything afterwards is not in the game.

Seemingly, even in the second installment there's no ruby or sapphire weapon.
While I do like the game, I don't like how they split up the original story into 3 games.


Considering there's no open world in this game and only a limited amount of replayability, I suggest you get this game at a discount. It's a fun game, but once you're done, you're done.
Of course the DLC adds some more content, but it's nowhere NEAR the content the original game had to offer.
There's nowhere NEAR the amount of customizability the original game bolstered as well.
And I know there's better graphics and physics and all on top of a new battle system...but I'm not happy about how much good content was cut just for these improvements...

Is it fun?
Yes.
Worth a buy?
Yes.
Amd I happy?
60%
