\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's like Square didn't learn a damn thing from Final Fantasy 13; long drawn out linear hallways with literal zero input or impact from the player makes a very bad game. Ten hours into the game and I don't think I've made any decision yet. Every "major" combat encounter is scripted to a T. You can't damage a boss more than a certain amount before a cutscene happens, summons are only allowed when the game says they're allowed, and limit breaks seem to only be available when the game has decided that combat is wrapping up. It's not a game- it's just a walking simulator with a story attached. No, honestly, that's all it is. There's no random encounters, there's no deviating paths, there's... Nothing. It's so boring.

The thing is that this remake tries to take what the original game did in ten hours (at best) and turns it into a 40+ hour game. And that original ten hour experience was jam packed full of action and iconic moments- this game? It's just characters fawning over Cloud as if he's a literal god while winding through hallways and sometimes having combat...

Oh, and the combat, if you can call it that, is so bad. Everything is tied to a slowly generating ATB gauge, which is how the original was- but, the original was turn based, so it made sense. This? Why do I have to wait for the gauge to fill, choose a spell, wait for the spell to cast, wait for the spell to hit the target, while still having to expend MP? Like, what? Either have an MP cost on demand or have a gauge cost- having both just bogs everything down. And, jesus, didn't everyone collective agree that Final Fantasy 13 was bad, and yet the stagger mechanic is back. Doing no damage for awhile just to "stagger" a foe so you can have a burst window where you actually do damage is so tiring. Who thought that was fun? Oh, yeah, and every enemy has a stun-lock mechanic that is poorly telegraphed. The game is like, "Oh, you're in melee range? Here, let me stun-lock you while doing almost instant-kill damage." And there's no grinding to become more powerful, so it's just a question of wiping and slogging through the encounter again.

The writing is also really bad. The original game made caricatures of the characters, because the game was already spread across four discs so there wasn't a lot of space to flesh out characters. In this game? So much character "development," and yet everyone is still a caricature without much depth. Yeah, having characters dialogue at you while leading you by the hand to the next chapter isn't character development- it's Fi from Skyward Sword. "Oh, you found a keycard, what can that be used for, Cloud?" I don't know, maybe it will unlock the ability for me to punt you out of the party so I never have to hear your whiny nonsense again, Tifa and Barret. And it's so nice of Square to make Barret just Samuel L. Jackson cosplaying as Captain Planet- sooooo much depth and nuance in that.

There was so much Square could have done with FFVII, but this isn't it. They honestly should have just made a 1:1 remake with graphical upgrades and some quality of life improvements. This? Hot garbage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally wish I could give a review other than Yes/No.

I enjoy the game when I'm actually playing it. The story isn't bad either. Unfortunately, the gaps between the enemy fights and actual story progression is filled with some of the most unbelievably annoying content that could possibly exist. There's also a weird paradox where the transition areas are so beautiful and detailed, but they're also hella tedious to navigate.

I want to make it clear:
- Holding a button to do something is not gameplay.
- Climbing up and down ladders is not gameplay.
- Forcing players to walk back and forth to press buttons is not gameplay.

It's like the game developers had some mandatory quota for the above things for EVERY playable area, and it got to the point where I just got frustrated at the amount of "nothing" I was doing.

The epitome of my personal experience was one (spoiler-free, don't worry) part where the characters need to urgently head to a certain destination. After an hour of navigating the area, holding down buttons to open doors, climbing ladders, and moving obstacles, my boredom *slowly* turned into frustration. But you know what? I told myself: Midgar is a trash heap - there are going to be obstacles, understandably.

It was when the mini cut scenes started playing I had to turn the game off for a bit and chill. The characters would stop for 10-15 seconds, comment about something, then talk about how they're in a rush to their destination. Repeat this about 5 to 6 times. Maybe I'm less patient than I thought I was? Or maybe it was THAT egregious.

Please. Trim down the fat of the game. I'd rather have a better experience over 15-20 hours than a mediocre 30-35. And "mediocre" is generous for how god-awful some of the pacing is.\t\t\t\t\t
