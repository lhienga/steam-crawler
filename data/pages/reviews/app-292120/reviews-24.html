I've played it first when it came out and even finished it but I didn't remember anything about the game.
So, I thought, let's give it a second try despite the fact that I forgot everything and the bad review, no?
Well... Now, i know why I forgot everything...
The game is just empty and linear. And the story + character, while the lore and plot are interresting, feel like something for a younger audience? (10 to 15 yo, something like that)
What I mean by empty is that there is no "world" outside the linear path. No NPC to talk, no real sidequest until late game, nothing to really learn about the world, etc. Especially if you have played FFX, FFX-2, FFXII. You will be really disapointed if you played those game.
If not and if you can rush the main story and you really are not difficult with how the story is craft, well, maybe give it a try. It's still a FF.
Otherwise, you will be disapointed.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy experience may not match anyone else's. At the least, I've learnt it's a polarizing game for people.

There are bugs and issues, here and there. And crashes have occurred for me occassionally. The port wasn't great in that sense, but I don't find the bugs are major piece of this.

The larger thing I hear on this is the gameplay, and combat. This is a linear game, for a long while. It opens up only in the final stages, though those stages are quite long, and distinct. The combat, meanwhile, is a system that throws people off. It's a mix of real-time and turn-based, mostly real-time. It's hard to describe, and this is the biggest thing that can turn people off.

All of that said? This is one of my favorite games. It has many, many flaws, and many have enjoyed picking away at it's imperfections. But the characters, the music, the details...I love them more each time I've played. While I've beaten the game once on PC, my hours don't account for my having beat this game on the Xbox 360, or the PS3. Seems I beat the game every few years. I started up a new playthrough just recently running at 4K, and I'm blown away at the details that I'm only now seeing. There's so much that was put into this, it's inspiring.

I will defend this game to my final breath. Though I accept it's not for everyone.
I recommend giving it a try. I'm very lucky that I got the chance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is going to contain a few SPOILERS. Firstly, however, I'm going to mention about this PC version.

This game runs beautifully and I haven't run into any major problems. I did have to adjust a couple of settings, but that wasn't difficult. But then I ran into a larger problem - every couple of seconds, the game stuttered. I discovered, after looking online, that it's related to the game trying to find a controller. There is a workaround that can be a bit involved, but I would suggest not bothering to play this with mouse and keyboard. It runs much smoother with a controller. When I used my controller, the game immediately responded to it and the stuttering was gone. I hope other people solve the problem this way. The visuals are very crisp and all the controls have been responsive. No problems at all.

I personally love this game - characters, music, setting - and, for all its faults, it has many good things about it. Some of the party starts off annoying, but they mature and mellow over time; my personal faves are Sazh, Vanille and Hope. A lot of side characters are underused, but still steal the scenes they're in. The story is fascinating and I didn't find it at all confusing as I played through. I actually enjoy the datalog and getting background information that way (for anyone who criticises it, there are so many games where I could point fingers for also doing it that it's unfair to just pick on this one). The music is always spot on for the area; one of my favourites that isn't used enough I don't think is "the Vile Peaks". The other that's fantastic is "Desperate Struggle". The soundtrack is very powerful and it's worth hanging around in certain areas just to listen to it. The visuals are beautiful, although it's a shame there isn't enough opportunity to visit locations.

Which brings me to the game's flaws. As with many game series that migrated onto the PS3/Xbox 360 consoles, FFXIII suffers from being rushed through the development phase. It's clear from concept art and the detail in certain areas that it was meant to be an upgraded "open world" version of FFXII - which FFXV eventually became. Some of the writing (although should I blame the dubbing for this?) is a bit irritating when it comes to characters. Some of the characters are too aggressive or repetitive for the situation at hand until they, as I mentioned above, mellow out. Everyone calms down about halfway through the game. There is a little bit of story fault I have with how much is explained about the brands; it could have done with a great deal more about the time limit aspect and some of the urgency is lost (I know it's a game mechanic, but running around killing marks when you're going to become Cie'th soon brings me out of the story a bit). As for music, my only complaint is how too often when they dub Japanese games, they replace some songs. The gameplay, as others have pointed out, mostly consists of pushing the auto-attack button and occasionally using potions, healing and switching paradigms. It can get tedious. To counter-balance that, I am pleased they have visible enemies and no random battles (just a personal FF preference). One thing I do particularly miss is the airship, which isn't even in this game (or any of the Lightning Saga games, for that matter). I SHOULD mention what I consider the worst parts of the game, and one of those is the awful active, dynamic camera during battles; the abrupt zooms, swinging around the arena and getting caught on objects so that I can't see what I'm fighting or who is performing what ability. The second worst thing is being forced to change between slow/fast speeds for battles, and lowering the difficulty to easy and then back up to normal; this is NOT because I'm a bad gamer. Sometimes, the game is too easy even on Normal; other times, they under-prepare you for an enemy that isn't even a boss and you get one-shot killed. This is with battles where I've upgraded all my weapons, put on appropriate accessories and levelled up the Crystarium. This can lead to farming points where you're just trying to get more powerful at a tortoise pace in order to defeat the next enemy.

When it comes down to it and all games of what I call the "PS3 rush era", FFXIII has a lot of problems that overshadow its good points. I recommend this game, although mainly for its story, characters, visuals and music, not so much the gameplay. Some marks are fun to complete, and bosses entertaining, but that's about it. There isn't much freedom associated with past FF games and there are certain things that aren't to blame for the "rush". Still, that's not enough to put me off, personally.\t\t\t\t\t
