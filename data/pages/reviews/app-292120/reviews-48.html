\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAm I just being picky or has FF really become this bad?

I ended up uninstalling after less than 2 hours. You can call me an RPG snob, but this game really missed the mark when it came to grabbing my attention in the first moments.

The game starts with no explanation as to what is going on, who the characters are, or what world you are entering. The combat attempts to be so "active" yet it just seems like you are smashing the enter button and watching things happen instead of controlling the fight. You don't level up so there is no reward for battles, so it just becomes an obstacle in the way of the next cheesy cut scene. The characters are all anime cliches and don't feel unique in any sense so you could care less about any of them in regards to story progression. Perhaps this all changes after a few more hours into the game, but I honestly couldn't be bothered to stick around and see.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I finally finished playing through Final Fantasy XIII. I tried to give it a fair shake, I really did. The combat was pretty fun. The writing for the first half or so was engaging. But after 60 hours, I realized that I didn't enjoy most of it.

The biggest problem is the 30 hour long tutorial where they drip feed you game mechanics while making you run down narrow corridors. They don't even properly tutorialize you on important things like chain bonus vs chain time and ATB refilling on paradigm shift in those 30 hours. Then they dump you in their "open world", which is a big central area with four dead end areas leading out from it. There are then "side quests", which you'll be doing all of, probably in exact number order, to grind enough to progress the story. You get there at chapter 11/13.

The first half of the story presents an interesting world and characters, but the second half is just mind-bendingly silly. It's just the same points reiterated by the characters while they do nothing to act upon those points. Imagine that scene from Simpsons where Sidehsow Bob is stepping on all those rakes. The party is Sidehsow Bob, except the BBEG tells the party there's a rake there, and they should step on it. The party says that they will DEFY FATE and NOT STEP ON IT, and then step on the rake. Over. And over. And over. AND OVER.

The game feels clearly unfinished, without much to actually *do* for a JRPG. This is a theme from modern Final Fantasy. I'd say it started with XII, when they set up a weird plot about the world's gods at the last act and left it hanging, begging for a "real" final act to the game that never came. XII at least had the amount of content I expect from my JRPGs. XIII is considerably more barren. I cannot believe that they made two sequels to this game, and that XV was almost yet another XIII game. Absolutely deranged management decisions.

Anyways, I had decent fun in the middle of the game, but the game has way too many flaws to be able to recommend. Sorry, folks. It's just not good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery criticism you've heard about this game is true. But what they don't mention is that XIII is still better than any mid-tier JRPG (ie, tales of) and a few AAA games as well. It's a complete and fully developed game, unlike XV. Play it!

Unfortunately, the PC port is terrible. Even though this game is ten years old, it runs at about 12FPS until you install a fanmade performance patch that brings it up to almost 60FPS. Then halfway through the game the environments get bigger, you fight more enemies at once, and -aga spells create huge screen filling visual effects. Even with the performance patch, you'll be playing much of the second half in literal slow motion, and with frequent drops back down to 12FPS. The levelup screen also runs in extreme slowmo at this point.

For a game that's known as Square's peak era for putting 1000% effort into the visuals, it's mind-boggling why they can't just hire one person and say 'Go sit in the corner for the next five years and make graphics bugfixes for the broken FF13 PC port'.\t\t\t\t\t
