\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do not recommend this game.

Feels like "press A button" simulator with few extra steps.

I didn't have much hope for this game to begin with, but I did not expect it to be this much.
Honestly I prefer to go to YouTube and watch full walkthrough, because game itself is absolutely nothing much and story wise - even in short time I have played - it is weird.

Characters are not acting according to situation almost at all.
Battle system is way too simplified, good for people that are bad at gaming and strategy or children, maybe.

I wanted to give this game a try, but I am falling out. This is probably the worst Final Fantasy game I have played.

If you do not want a challange and enjoy just sitting pressing one button over and over again, this game is for you.

Other than what I described above:
Graphics are really good.
Audio sounds nice.
Subtitles do not match audio often.
Characters seem interesting, however their acting according to situation often leaves a lot to think about.
Interesting looking menu.
Lack of interaction with NPC.
Linear gameplay.

As a Final Fantasy fan I am disappointed in this release, but give it a go if you want, maybe you will like it.

Cheers and stay safe.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this is a game I've tried to revisit dozens of times over the years since first attempting to play it on the PS3 shortly after it came out. I had been a Final Fantasy fan for years before then, and was pretty excited about this new entry, and initially I was impressed by the presentation here, but found the gameplay, combat, characters and exploration incredibly shallow and nowhere near the standards I had come to expect from the franchise. This assessment has only gotten more negative as time has passed. Probably once a year or so, I think to myself, "I'd really like to play through this game and check out the sequels... maybe if I can just trudge through it". I drop it like a sack of potatoes every time.

Exploration in this game consists of navigating through some (admittedly very beautiful for 2009) linear corridors to reach the next objective. Combat is predominantly focused on having a minor influence over the *flow* of battle rather than exerting a high level of tactical input of your own... you *can* directly input some commands but doing so feels clunky, as if the game is *really* not designed for that.

As far as the story and characters go, they're... fine. I don't detest most of them, but nothing happening has kept me in suspense over what unfolds in the story. This is a pretty subjective thing, but I just feel nothing for anything happening in this game.

As a full disclaimer, I have not actually finished this game. I usually play to around the part where the game "opens up" on Pulse, then abandon it. I can just never bring myself to go on further. Somehow, the part of the game that is the least corridor-like is always the part where my interest drops to zero.

It's taken me a long time to realize this, but I have come to admit to myself that I'll likely never finish this game, and as of now have no plans to revisit it again. I really genuinely do not like this game.

... And as far as the PC port goes, I haven't had any major performance issues to speak of on a relatively high end PC, but obviously those have been a problem for a lot of people as well. Your mileage may vary, but I can't speak to it.\t\t\t\t\t
