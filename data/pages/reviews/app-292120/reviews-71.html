Unfortunately the PC port is kind of botched and requires a patch to run properly, otherwise the game is more prone to crashing. As long as you are ok with downloading a patch, I would recommend getting this if it looks interesting to you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a bit on the odd side. Being the first game in the Fabula Nova Chrystallis series from the Final Fantasy franchise, it has a lot of, well, oddities.

Story-wise, this game is on the better side. The whole game is based around the theme of going against fate, which when you look at it from that standpoint as a whole game, it's great. It's story is paced well with some albeit somewhat lackluster plot twists. The characters, however is a bit, poorly written. Character 1, Lightning, her story is just her learning to trust her sister and that's pretty much it. Character 2, Snow, he has almost no character arc at all. His whole story is him being to good in the face of evil, believing in every small bit of hope, which frankly to me got quite annoying. Character 3, Sazh, interesting character. His son got taken by the 'child protection services' and he wants to rescue him, the rest of his story is just a spoiler to the plot. Character 4, Hope, he is just a child (who is canonically 13 throughout the game), who has no hope, funny joke SE. Character 5, Vanille, well, not much to really say because every detail of her spoils the game. So does this last character. Overall the characters were poorly written in my opinion but the story structure was decently well paced and the worldbuilding was great.

Gameplay, oh boy. Turn-based combat like every Final Fantasy game. It uses the ATB gauge mechanic which can make the combat a bit slow. But the stagger point system is just a mess full of wasting time and a whole lot of bad decisions. The more you build up the stagger gauge, the more damage you can deal which is nice, except you also need to hit a stagger point that varies between enemies. Some enemies also require you to stagger them to do damage, and their stagger point is either low but they have high stagger resistance so you build your chain slower, or a stupidly high stagger point. Oh, and there's a boss that requires you to not stagger it so that he doesn't wipe your party. The paradigm system is rather cool. It lets you switch out the "classes" of the party mid-fight, allowing for a lot of improvising, quick decision making and unique strategies. Also the party system is jank. If your party leader dies, you just game over. The exploration section is quite neat but very streamlined and there's no random encounters.

Nothing much to say about the character design and map design except they're great. Cinematics, as per Final Fantasy tradition, are so good that it makes you wonder where the game budget even goes too. Same with the soundtrack. Graphics are jank but good considering the game came out in 2003 and this isn't a HD port.

Story - 6/10
Music - 7/10
Gameplay - 7/10
Graphics - 8/10

Overall - 7/10
