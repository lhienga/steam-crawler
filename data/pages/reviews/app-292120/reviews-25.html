\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI slept on Final Fantasy XIII when it was first released, but finally decided to pick the trilogy up during the the Summer Sale.
I've been on a Final Fantasy marathon that I started with I Pixel Remaster a couple months back. I decided to take a break from doing them in numerical order after V, so I completed VII, and am now trying to figure out which one to check off the list next. Now I went into this knowing XIII had a reputation, and that Square has dropped the ball left and right on the franchise's single player entries for the past decade. So... how bad could it be?

...I just had to ask.

This is not a video game. It's a glorified game engine tech demo. Two or three hours in and I keep asking what's the point of it?

You're plopped into the world with zero explanation of the setting, the factions at play, and who anybody is... unless you read entries from the Datalog. They are dry, boring digital encyclopedia entries. Reading a textbook on current events does not motivate me to emotionally invest in the characters. Early on a teenage boy who's apparently important to the story, because why not, witnesses his mother die, and decides he wants revenge on another character who was trying to protect her. A young girl, who's apparently another important character, for also no apparent reason, tries to be emotionally supportive to him in the most superficial, stilted, Japanese media trope-y way possible. Lot's of smiles and bubbly attitude as if she's totally oblivious to the fact that they are literally in the middle of a state sanctioned genocide.
What do the kids say these days? That's cringe bro?

Now for the battle system. So far as I can tell, there's no point to the battles. No EXP. No leveling up characters. No laying the foundation for builds. No progress. Nothing. They are there simply to give the player some form of interactivity between cut scenes. It's been said the game is linear hallway, cut scene, hallway. They have it absolutely right. There's a grading system for the battles. There's points to be earned. What are they for? Nothing in terms of substance as far as I can tell. I am ok with linearity in an RPG. FFVII's Midgar was exactly that. IX definitely dived into that from what I played. X, my third favorite in the series, was all linear. However, the battles you fought had a point: it gave you, the player, a sense of progression. That's completely gone here.

The music? It's there. This orchestrated ambiance has no identity of its own. No stand out track that stays in the mind, of course, how could there be when you're hours in, but still in the same general area, waiting for the game to begin, assuming it ever does.

The graphics? Well... it does look very nice even for its age now, but I'm here to play a video game. If I wanted to stare at works of art while occasionally interacting with an electronic device, I could Google "Tifa, Lulu, 2B rule 34" and scroll on my phone.

Even if I never experience the entire Final Fantasy XIII multimedia presentation from start to final credits roll, I can still say I beat every Final Fantasy game, because whatever this is, it is no game, there's nothing to play.\t\t\t\t\t
