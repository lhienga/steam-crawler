\r\n\t\t\t\t\tpros
-gorgeous graphics
-Awesome music

cons
-Dumb battle system
ff13 plays like an auto battle mobile game with AAA production. Your only control over a battle is what stances your 3 character take (eg: tank, buff,debuff etc) and then you press auto and the AI chains up the skills and executes them. There is little to no strategy. You want to debuf the bigger monster while two of your other characters target the fast low hp ones? too bad your debuffer will also target them. You want to remove a debuff from yourself while the healer heals you? too bad your auto will try to heal you before your health is high enough for it to change to a skill that allows you to remove it. Hey why don't you manually select it then? well i have over 15 skills so let me search for it.
All this happens in real time so monsters are free to attack you and it feels like you are browsing through menus.
Status effects? the game forgot to teach you about that so good luck. Your party leader dies it's game over even though you have other characters on the field who are up.
You want to change your party setup? well sorry but we forgot to add a loadout so set your stance setup all over again when you change your party.
-Terrible pacing
It takes 20 hours for you to have a full party to use. Before that the game is in tutorial mode and you don't have any control over your party setup. The monster drops are low and you need it to level up weapons and accessories. The game essentially becomes a grindfest once you enter the more open world areas. You are essentially going through corridors before that.
The world of ff13 doesn't feel alive. There are no npcs and no cities to explore. You cannot get invested in a world purely by pretty sights. FF13 expects you to read text exposition to truly explore and understand the world.
-Characters
None of the characters really stood out.
lightning - is emo and whiny for the initial part of the story and blames everything on snow.
snow - typical anime mc who wants to be a hero but has royally ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up every time he tried to be one.
hope - stumbled from being unsure to murderer to stoic.
The other 3 characters arcs are fine but didn't leave any deep impression.

overall FF13 is a terrible final fantasy game and offers a very different experience from what you would expect from a jrpg.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
The combat system gives you a reason to use status effect spells, to use all your roles, and to actively switch roles. The cinematics are dynamic if a bit shaky. The animations usually look good, and at least some of the characters were enjoyable. The difficulty balance through most of the game was a bit overtuned, but typically pretty close to perfect. For most of it.

Cons:
Not fond of the game from a design aspect. The story was fine, which was why I could even finish it, but running from cutscene to cutscene with no towns, NPCs, side quests, optional areas, reason or ability to backtrack, or anything was just mind numbing. This game is probably fine for someone looking for what it offers, but it isn't what I come to JRPGs to experience.

Setting specific jargon was a coinflip as to whether you would be told what it was about or have to dig into the datalog for important context. Wouldn't have been as big a deal, but combined with constantly hopping from perspective to perspective while characters move to new locations off camera gave a very disjointed feeling.

The battle system seemed designed to take as much control away from players as possible. Team mates were AI only, menus had animation locks that made manually selecting actions time consuming, and the combat punished wasted time. It actively encouraged you to lean on auto battle for the first 30+ hours until you get into the post game. There were times when I was stuck just trying to heal to keep up with enemies and knowing a perfect debuff that would turn the tide... but which the AI wouldn't cast until it had gotten half a dozen other debuffs on first, with nothing I could do to force the issue.

There were also fights that I would get into, and struggle, knowing a specific role combination that would make things much smoother, but with no ability to put it into play until I restarted the fight and set up ahead of time. I love fights that force you to adapt mid fight, but I hate fights that force you to try again because there was some preparation you couldn't have known would be important ahead of time.

Roughly half the side content is soft-locked behind progression that isn't unlocked until you actually complete the game, breaking a tradition of nearly all side content just before the final dungeon. This ensures a specific game balance for the final fight, but once again at the cost of player agency.


All in all it didn't feel as much like playing a final fantasy myself as watching a final fantasy movie and having to do combat challenges to unlock the next 5 minutes of runtime. Everything seemed to play exactly as intended, and it was designed very well to accomplish what it set out to do, but for all that it just plain felt bad to play.\t\t\t\t\t
