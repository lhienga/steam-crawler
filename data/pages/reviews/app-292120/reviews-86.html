\r\n\t\t\t\t\t13 is no one's favorite Final Fantasy. So, if you haven't played FF before, go get 6, 7, 8, or, 9. Or 3, 4, or 5 if can go that retro. There was a lot for fans to be bummed about when this game dropped. Its missing any semblance of a world map. You can't really grind for power. There are no towns with npc to make small talk with. Airships? Cid? 10 was the last game made by the original producer, 11 was an MMO, and 12 was, like 9, an odd attempt to placate players who were falling off the series as it was changing. So, 13 was meant to establish a new dynasty of Final Fantasy. A more cinematic experience (who's old enough to have gone to the see the movie in the theaters here?) while also touting a more technical and complicated active time battle system more akin to 7 and 8 than any of the others but moved several steps into the future. The downside on that is that the early part of the game is very easy and then makes a huge spike in difficulty about 30 hours in.

Nonetheless, I am enjoying my current playthru more than I expected, again. The characters are great, the story is cool, the cutscenes were absolutely state of the art at the time and some of the design work still looks excellent by modern standards. The system is fun to use and fast moving. Its a great casual game to just push the button and watch it go most of the time, but definitely has enough grit to challenge you when it wants to.

Regarding this port:
I played extensively on my 360 until it died. Sometimes it looks much better than it did on my 360 and sometimes it looks kind of jacked. The trouble is that the game itself is tuned to the older technology. The frame skips and drops you will get didn't exist on the 360 because it didn't run hot enough for them to be noticeable at the time. The clunkiness of the camera control is 100% original. The mouse conversion is predictably awful, but I find that using a keyboard is way better than using an xbox controller for this game.\t\t\t\t\t
