\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, I will start with some negative points (only to encourage you to look past them), but this is an absolutely fantastic game.

Though I absolutely love this game, Final Fantasy XIII's first hours suffer from a too slow pacing in terms of gameplay mechanics, and a bad onboarding:
It takes quite a long time, until the combat system gets fun and intriguing, which is mainly tied to the story progression. After the whole combat system unfolds, it will be a lot more than just "Attack, Attack, Attack". I daresay the late game is one of the most challening ones in Final Fantasy games. (My personal opinion only)

As for the onboarding, FFXIII is missing an unknowing character. Everybody knows everything about the world and nobody needs to get taught, therefore the player also misses out on getting taught. This leads to a bit of confusion in the beginning of the game and it doesn't really pick you up, as a lot of keywords are thrown around that you don't understand. (That being said, the game offers you to read up on everything in the menu)

Now for the positives:
Once you get past the initial hours, the story, as well as the gameplay mechanics start to unfold and it turns into something beautiful. The characters are profound, each of them with their own agenda. The story stays interesting and builds up to a typical Final Fantasy epicness.
Fighting gets very challenging and strategic and offers lots of different ways to optimize and improve their fighting style. Lots of optional challenges await in the open world (it won't always stay that linear).
The graphics are absolutely amazing, considering this game's age (inital release 2009).

If you tried this game before and didn't like it in the first few hours, I strongly encourage you to try again and get past the first chapters. The positives of this game far outweigh the suboptimal beginning.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot one of the best titles in the series, but this is probably like the 10th time I've played it. It's not horrible or anything, but it can be a bit slow. The open world isn't very large and takes awhile to actually get to, which is a point of controversy for this game seeing as that is normally a huge part of FF games. Aside from FF XIV, I think this is where the main titles started to become a bit lackluster. My biggest gripe is how 90% of the enemies are either cybernetic creatures or just have more of a robotic look. Though that's just the theme for this trilogy and more of a personal distaste than anything.

Despite the games shortcomings, it is still an FF game and I love me some FF. If you are willing to give it a shot and accept it for what it is, it's fun. If you expect the perfect FF experience, you might be disappointed. This is the first in a trilogy style story though, and I think it should also be judged based off the perspective that this is 1/3 of an entire story...which gets kinda cringey and then the 3rd game you gotta fight with a timer the entire game...

Look, this trilogy isn't amazing in comparison to it's predecessors, but it's fun. Just buy this and play the other 2 afterword when they're on sale and in order so you get the full experience.\t\t\t\t\t
