\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI should preface this review by saying I'm not the biggest fan of Final Fantasy X. In general, I see X as starting the trend of over-explaining the narrative in an FF game to the point of having a character exist almost purely for the sake of being dense enough that everything needs repetition in order for him to understand it.

When many criticize the story of FFXIII by claiming that you have a hard time understanding what happens and have to constantly check the index to comprehend the story, I get a little puzzled. When I went through and played this game both times (recently and when the game came out), I found the index a regurgitation of plot elements already introduced to us. The exposit also came through somewhat organically. I'd rather go into a story and brand new world having no idea what's going on and wanting to find out than sit and watch 10 hours of explanation before I get to play.

That said, explaining FFXIII's plot efficiently through immersion doesn't make the story actually good. There's a lot of potential there, and if this review contained spoilers, I'd break down a few tweaks that could have made it a great story. Alas, I have to say that a lot of problems with FFXIII exist because a concept is under-baked and more time in development to refine execution could have helped this game in storyline and gameplay elements. I really love the idea of FFXIII's story, but it needed a little more work to truly sing.

The Paradigm system in the gameplay suffers a similar fate. Setting up shifting roles and aiming for fast-paced cinematic combat works really well to make this game graphically beautiful and visually interesting, but at a certain point it just comes down to the timing you swap your paradigm, and after you understand the swing of the system, it can feel repetitive. Still, I would argue that the combat system is more challenging than your typical FF game, so I enjoyed it quite a bit.

What really sells this game for me, however, amounts to a defense of its linearity. The portion of the game that feels linear does so to mirror the story. I don't see this point covered enough, but from my perspective, the gameplay feels linear to mirror the narrative of the story and opens up when the story also offers more options for the characters. Other than FFVI, it's rare for a Final Fantasy to think a lot more about how narrative structure marries game design and to fully use the media other than making the game into a long film broken up by moving your character between each sequence. You may argue that it doesn't justify the linearity, but I find it a lot more refreshing than frustrating, especially considering that FFX attempted something similar with its structure and did a worse job.

If nothing else, the game itself is gorgeous to play. Walking around through corridors and over the more open area creates a truly magnificent feeling and the spectacles surprise and delight to such a high extent for a PS3 era game. Most fans of the game laud the visuals continuously, so I'll leave it at that.

Extra content also has a great concept and a few fun challenges and bosses, but to 100% the game, you find yourself fighting the same enemy repeatedly for 40 hours, and that's a major negative. Would have been nice to find more variety in farming gil efficiently in post game, but I digress.

As a fan or the older FF titles, I most frequently recommend playing FFXIII compared to any other game published post-PS1 era. I just wish it were a little bit more fleshed out.\t\t\t\t\t

100% Completion Review

Ultimately after playing this game for 100 hours I can understand why some players dislike this, but I also feel they gave up too early. If I had to give it a rating I'd say 8.5/10.

Positives
  • Paradigm system - this made the game for me! even after 100 hours this did not get boring and as you progress through the game more and more of it is unlocked and makes it feel fresh again.
  • The main story was great fun, not an amazing story but engaging enough. The bosses were all tough but beatable, even if it just meant retrying and changing strategy.
  • No grinding required for the story, unless you want 100% completion. I f you want to put 30-50 hours at your own pace into the story you can do it without grinding once which kept the pace of the game really nicely and you never felt like you were at a stand still.
  • Post game was an absolute blast, with the 100 hours spent it was 50/50 in terms of main story and post game. Cieth stone missions and Titan trials are a great challenge and now we can choose to grind to really get the most out of our characters.
  • Linear story - This is both a positive and a negative. The positive being that you never wandered off and wasted time searching for nothing, it was play a cut scene and then get to objective while fighting enemies, and it felt fast paced as a result.

Negatives
  • Camera movement! The true bane of the game for me. I swear the camera wanted to get snagged on anything possible and move as slowly as possible just to annoy me, by far the worst part of the game for me and no options to change camera settings.
  • Summons felt lacklustre for the most part. In the story part of the game you're not really strong enough to utilise them, so unless you grind post game and upgrade your characters the summons just feel like a let down. Using them on some bosses in main story and barely knocking 10% health off for what feels like a long time is poor.
  • Linear story - The negative for me is that it really feels like you're having your hand held for the first 20-30 hours. When you reach chapter 10 you can utilise the open world part of the game and post game is open world to a point (some areas from earlier chapters can't be revisited). I feel a lot of players will struggle with this coming from other Final Fantasy games.

A great experience and am so happy I persevered after I got over adapting to the linear story aspects of the game because even I after 15 hours was thinking this feels very odd and restrictive for a Final Fantasy game.

If you plan to 100% this game I would suggest looking at a guide for TWO parts, and they are regarding the growth egg and potential missable items, other than that just enjoy the game as it comes.\t\t\t\t\t

The part where all the skill trees are unlocked and you finally have all the characters so you can start piecing together a stable team and composition. While the game gets far more enjoyable to play, it does become rather grindy if you play on normal. I caved and cranked the easy mode on because I wasn't looking forward to adding another 10 hours of mindless grinding just to be strong enough to beat the story.

Speaking of, I really enjoyed the story. Quite messy in some parts and strangely explained, but it makes up for in style and substance I guess. The characters are amazing, some could use some more depth but this is a story where we're kind of on the clock so we're not really privy to a life story. But I managed to fall in love with each of the characters at some point and if your RPG does that, you know they did a good job in the writing department.

The combat is enjoyable when you finally have all the features unlocked. Combat turns into a puzzle game and its interesting to switch between comps in the heat of things to beat certain enemies. Far better than the conventional turn based Final Fantasy is known for in my opinion. While I enjoyed Final Fantasy 7 (the original), the combat is quite dated so this game's approach to turn based is a refreshing new take on the style. A style of which we won't see outside of this line of games sadly.

The music is nice, as per usual, Final Fantasy tends to nail it with this sort of thing.

The world is interesting, though marred with boring level design. It is to be recognised that this game is a two disc console game so it was quite beefy back then, yet the game is very linear. It goes to show you how many different areas you traverse and atmospheres/biomes you interact with. You're constantly going to new places and the game doesn't really skimp on showing off these worlds in both cinematics and world design. For a game released 10+ years ago, it stands very well.

I modded the game so the cinematics and textures were more fitting for my resolution, so it definitely looks better than stock. The cinematics are beautiful and is basically the trademark Final Fantasy engine cinematics that just make it look impressive to watch in action. Leaves you wondering if the game is a movie or a game.

It's both. The game can be sluggish in the beginning because you're getting waterboarded with story and setup. A waterboarding that lasts a very very long time before you're really able to enjoy the games unique combat instead of being forced with certain characters.

With all those in mind, I truly think this game is wonderful and am saddened that many hate it because it was trying something different. But if you're willing to sit through it like me, you're in for a treat, especially because there's 2 more games part of the FFXIII world.\t\t\t\t\t
