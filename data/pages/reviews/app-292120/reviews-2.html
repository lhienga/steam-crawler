\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTypical jrpg-style storytelling. And eventho the game is fairly long, the story itself isnt fully explored. I love the premise of its characters connection with each other, their conflict and interest. But, the execution and conclusion isnt really satisfying. Maybe because it's rather old game and limited by its time's tech?

Gameplay-wise, i love it. It has that weird gameplay-loop that can be played automatically but also require tactical thinking to tackle some of its challenges. I require some tips online tho, some mechanics isnt explained clearly in the game.

The game's linearity, and pretty lifeless world will not sit right for some people. And because of its lack of jrpg town/city, it lacks the main cast interaction with people outside of themselves, side character-sidequest and what not.

But the gameplay and character are enough for me to complete the game. No matter what i say, lightning's design is still badass.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've played this game, it's better than you remember. If you haven't, it's better than you were led to believe. FF13 has aged gracefully and it's worth giving a fair chance. The music is magical, the environments are beautiful, the worldbuilding is interesting, the story is engaging from beginning to end, the combat is fast-paced and the scoring system encourages reckless play. It takes a while for the paradigm system to pop off but once it does you'll be rapidly switching paradigms, cancelling ATB bars early, interrupting and staggering enemies and pushing for high ratings. The game's balance favours hyper-offense which does reduce the value of tanks and defensive equipment unfortunately, but it is what it is.

But above all else, the characters are the star of the show here. The main cast is well-realized, with relatable hopes and fears and frustrations. They bicker and banter with one another, take time to reveal their vulnerabilities to each other, and all of them grow immensely over the course of the story. There's real interpersonal drama and tension. They're normal people given a collective death sentence and they all cope with the despair and the stress in different ways. Facial animations are impressive for a 2009 game, they're more expressive and lifelike than a lot of modern games (lol), and the mocap/animation work is brilliant. With the exception of some comically lower-budget throwaway cutscenes in the open-world section of the game, clearly there was some kind of production issue that forced them to cheap out on those ones. Luckily the cheap cutscenes are the ones that don't matter anyway, so it's no big deal.

As for the PC port, I expected it to be much worse but I literally had no issues at all throughout my entire playtime. Your mileage may vary, so look the game up on PC Gaming Wiki first and be prepared to install some fixes if you have to (I didn't, the game runs perfectly out of the box for me and maybe it will for you too!). Also, the English translation is unfortunately not the best - occasionally it leaves out nuance from the Japanese script (I speak Japanese and played with JP audio), a lot of the time this missed nuance is irrelevant in the grand scheme of things but sometimes it's preeetty important and it's frustrating that they just decided to change lines for seemingly no reason. Your image of a character can change based on subtle differences in tone and phrasing, so it would've been nice if the localization team took more care there.

Also Sazh and Hope are the best and anyone who disagrees will be rounded up by PSICOM and purged\t\t\t\t\t
