But when it comes to game play, it is a fresh take on the battle system as well as exploration. The problem with FF13 is that it emphasizes a lot on the restricted passages than any other FF game in the past. But to be true to my opinion, I loved the game and it's unique feel. Although I am a huge fan of FFX, this game definitely did grow on me after investing the initial hours and it has become quite a favorite of mine. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most underrated Final Fantasy games in the entire franchise,

Yes this game has it's flaws, it is incredibly linear for 70% of the story, with the last 30% opening up into a more open world format. But this makes you appreciate the open world portion of the game and makes you realise the linear parts were actually incredibly well done.

Paradigm system is fun and makes you look like a badass when playing in front of friends or streaming because everything happens at a fast pace. But controlling it is actually super managable.

Had to physically turn the sound off when walking in some environments, footsteps are annoying AF in this game. The last achievement you will most likely get is called "Treasure Hunter" and is the single worst achievement i have ever had to grind for. Achievement Hunters beware this game is a grind.

Characters for the most part are forgettable, but the story and music are engaging enough that it is compensated.

I suggest this game if you want an RPG to sing your teeth into and don't care too much about character development but want a great story, an even better soundtrack and beautiful visuals.\t\t\t\t\t
