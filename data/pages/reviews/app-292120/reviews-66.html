\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I love the setting, characters, world, and cut-scenes. The game starts to get really really repetitive after a certain point of just, walk straight, touch enemy, fight enemy, spend 2-3 mins per battle. Do side quest, walk back where you came from, then go back to running to the main mission point. The game-play is what is slowly killing me in the inside. The battle system is unique and cool at the beginning but after you hit the open world, that's when things start to get in your nerves, at least for me. Find their weakness, do it again. I like this game, but not the combat system or the exploration. Like the community has said its 'Linear' and it's true. I've played numerous FF games in the past growing up. This game is starting to become a chore than being a game I find enjoyable for its game-play and exploration. I get it its an old game. But this game has a place in my heart for its charm and its soundtrack. 7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I say that can mitigate this game's already long established reputation? Not much, I don't think. Most people already have their minds made up about this entry in the series. Many decry it as the beginning of the end for the franchise, and I don't think there's anything I can say that will dissuade their already cemented opinions.

But if you haven't played this game, and are on the fence about getting it, then I urge you to give it a try for yourself. If it's on sale, I'd say it's a no-brainer. Of course you've heard about the hallways, and how the game is "just press X" (which is COMPLETELY untrue, but only after maybe 5 hours of playing). The problem is most people didn't give the game a chance and gave up after a few hours, and their complaints stuck and fed into a sort of negative zeitgeist in this game's history. It's unfortunate, because Final Fantasy XIII has a lot more to offer than people give it credit for. To be fair, it does require patience. But I promise you, once the game starts to open up and you're slowly drawn into its unique world, you'll realize that it's actually an incredible amount of fun to play. Once it's firing on all cylinders, the battle system is an absolute blast.

This is my 2nd time playing this. I first played it years ago on PS3 and sunk about 80 hours into it. Now I'm doing it again and loving it even more than before. This time I'm following the story closely and reading up on the data entries it feeds to you throughout the game, and I gotta be honest, I'm loving every second of it. There's a depth of lore waiting for anyone willing to dive into it. But if you're not, I will warn you that the story can be pretty confusing on its own. You'll still know the gist of what's going on, but you'll undoubtedly have some questions.

So, does the game have flaws? Yes, absolutely it does. But it also has some very strong positives. Even though I liked it the first time around, I have to say this time I'm really loving it. I grew up on this series -- Final Fantasy 6 is and will always be my favorite -- but this game's pretty great too. Just because it's a newer Final Fantasy doesn't mean it's automatically bad. Go into it with an open mind and you just might be surprised!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShocker I don't like FF13. I don't think it's AS bad as many people say but damn I did not have fun here, I have many common complaints here like the hallway design, mindless automated combat and generally unlikable characters like Hope and Vanille, though I do think Hope gets better. Actually I think the middle part of the game is amazing, when the characters arcs really get going like Hope and Lightning's relationship, Light learning to let her guard down a little, Sazh and Vanilles relationship starting off as cute but soon gets derailed by some plot twists only for them to maintain their friendship, Snow is fun too, he's a big stupid jock but I find him endearing. But after these arcs come to a head, after Palumpolum is where the game starts falling apart for me, it quickly becomes "listen to bad man talk, fight bad man, ok move on" repeated, and though Pulse is visually beautiful and much more open, it's quickly passed and we go back to boring hallways, by the time we fight Orphan I was bored and lost all interest. The final scene is pretty great though real sad I gotta play FF13-2 now and undo such a good ending, oh well THANKS SQUARE\t\t\t\t\t
