\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the game itself: Thumps Up
For the quality of the PC port here on Steam: Thumps Down

In order to make the port more playable, you need to use fan fixes and do some light modding. The pre-rendered cutscenes are the same quality as the Xbox version before they were improved with Xbox One/Series backwards compatibility. In other words, not good. Not the 1080p quality that the PS3 version had.

Overall, the PC port is very barebones. HUD, UI and other 2D elements are at still their original resolution, making them look pixelated or blurry at 1080p or higher, depending on how much MSAA you use.

Lastly some positives but not really since you expect these things to be included; PC port is 60fps (despite being improved, the Xbox version is still locked at 30fps), better load times and the option for Japanese voices.

Mods used: FF13Fix and PS3 button prompts as the game only has colourless Xbox button prompts by default.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first FF on 7th gen consoles, XIII brought us into its world in 2009.... and divided the Final Fantasy community overnight. It's nowhere near perfect and the direction is very cinematic (arguably more movie than game at times). But the core of the game is a refined turn-based battle system (something Square Enix has strayed away from since, it's confirmed that XVI will NOT be turn-based) and the PC (and series X) releases feature 4k60fps gameplay, meaning its aged well.

My main (and likely most's) critique for this game is long hallway-like levels for the first 11 chapters, about half the game. These would benefit being halved in length. A plus for this approach is more time to take in the world and appreciate bonus dialogue from characters featured. Obviously though, level design changed for the sequels because it gets tiring.

I love the world design and most of the main cast. The world makes them suffer and gameplay reflects that. We trod along winding hallways together, rarely have a choice of where to go but forwards. Until... I love getting to Gran Pulse with these characters to explore a whole different planet. The gameplay suddenly opens up and characters can be any class and walk anywhere and take sidequests and fast travel. Final Fantasy XIII delivers a cathartic feeling by having such a beautiful subworld that feels like a different game within the game. It can be a slog rather than a journey getting there though.

Replaying the game, I figured out how to avoid a lot of the battles this time around. Bait a movement, j-turn around them. Which was nice to advance quicker, but now I realize I'm avoiding playing how it was 'meant to be played'. So at the end of the day, no matter how much I love the characters, and how much lore I take in, the pacing just sucks.

7/10 but one of my favorite rpgs anyways.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never understood the dislike people have for this game. I am currently on my second playthrough and the Steam version actually is a measurable improvement over the console version I played on PS3 due to the upgrade to 60 FPS.

FF13 is a very linear game for the first 20 hours or so, which often gets a lot of criticism. In my mind, the linear nature of the game removes any unnecessary filler and really crafts a specific experience for you. I also think the "extended tutorial" is almost necessary to make sure you are acquainted with the battle system for the late game, where I think the game yields a wealth of strategic decision trees in every battle. The story is about characters who are on the run and are travelling through specific pathways to reach their destination, so it wouldn't make much sense for them to be wandering around an open world and chatting with townsfolk anyway.

If you are looking for a unique take on the classic turn based JRPG formula, strategic and interesting battles and a crafted experience where you don't have to worry about a ton of missables, minigames or side quests then this game is for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnyone who tells you FFXIII is bad are not good at video games and their incorrect opinion should be discarded accordingly.

Be patient with this one, give it some time to open up and be rewarded. The battle system can be extremely fun much later into the game and offers up a great deal of difficulty in some of the later trials. The music and environments are gorgeous. Story is pretty obscure at times and requires you to read through some datalogs that are constantly updating as you play through, but like, you bought a JRPG so...

As a vet to the series having played through most mainline titles (barring the NES games) this is actually in my Top 5. I beat this on Xbox 360 on launch and am playing through it again right now on PC. Make sure to check out some of the performance patches and it actually runs super well.

Hope this helps. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
