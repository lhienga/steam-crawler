\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not listen to all of the negative reviews on this game. It is truly one of the best I have played and yes I HAVE played good quality games i.e. The Last of Us, Doom (2016), Assassin's Creed 2, Mass Effect 2, etc. This game may not be on the same wavelength as the one's listed above but is still really good. The pacing of the game is my biggest complaint in that you don't get 3 party team until a little over half way through the game and that is when you can really start doing some fun builds with all seven characters. Mix and matching rolls creates a fun and unique play style in my opinion. This was my first ever FF game I have played and I have played 2 others in FF X and FF VII remake and can say that this isn't as good as the others but please go into it with an open mind. The game can feel a bit grindy at times like if you want to do the C'ieth stone missions it took me like 3-4 days to get all characters maxed with their ultima weapons to be able to beat the super boss. I will say though the super boss is a fun challenge and highly recommend that you try it at least once. I will say this much though, Vanille is kind of annoying and Hope is unbearable in the early game but both character have decent character development to make them decent towards the end. The cutscenes are gorgeous. The story can feel a little sporadic but kind of finds its identity towards chapter 9 with an interesting turn of events. Last but certainly not least, Lightning is Bae!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love FFXIII. I've played it to completion twice on console. So far, this is a solid port.

As for the game itself, I won't sit here and defend myself from the vocal group of those who dislike it. That said, it's not for everyone. The game really is a fancy hallway and combat can get tedious in the early game before the entire party comes together.

If that doesn't bother you, get this game for the world, music, and atmosphere. XIII makes it easy to see what the Fabula Nova Crystallis universe could have been, if it hadn't been dropped a few years later. To me, that ambition is what makes XIII shine. Even though the narrative can get ahead of itself and confuse players, I can't help but feel a childlike glee at the scale and atmosphere of some the areas in the game. It's a game that takes you from set to cinematic setpiece and wows you each time. Few other games have as successfully, for lack of better phrasing, transported me to another world.\t\t\t\t\t

Exceeded Expectations

spoilers for the entire games plot ahead

I went into XIII expecting a downright bad Final Fantasy title because of a very vocal subset of the community. So colour me surprised when I found a title that was just as good as its contemporaries.

The story was definitely this title's strongest suit, I found myself loving it from the beginning all the way to the end; I had the same feelings for almost all the characters as well (the exclusion from this would be Hope, He spends the first half of the game as an insufferable piece of ♥♥♥♥ (though I'm biased as I hate revenge plots)) They all have solid writing and if they aren't already liked they redeem themselves before the end. Actually the only character that I never cared about was Fang, she honestly just didn't have enough involvement in the plot and seems just kind of strangely placed there, though there weren't any particularly bad parts about her character.
I also want to note that something I really appreciated about the characters in XIII was that some of them have pre-existing complicated relationships; That and they don't all just "get along" as is usually the case.

The world building is also incredibly well done, I think this might be my favourite world out of the FF games so far in fact; the idea of the Kami like Fal'cie ruling over a population of sacrificial humans on an artificial shell-like moon to summon an absentee god is just so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ crazy and cool. This is further helped by the presence of optional lore readable from the main menu. I actually wish this system was present in all the games as I love being able to learn more about the world the game takes place in.

OH! and the soundtrack is consistently ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ incredible, It doesn't ever have peak moments like most FF titles but is really good around the board regardless.

Now the gameplay is a bit trickier; I immediately found myself loving the combat system. It felt like a solid evolution of X-2's version of ATB, and the Paradigm decks were a mostly good evolution of XII's Gambit system though its simplification creates some problems when combined with the fact that you no longer have full party control. where i'm more neutral to negative, is on some design choices; A big thing being that the game doesn't "open up" until about 20 full hours in, it definitely feels great when you eventually get full party control and Crystariums however. Another large issue is that the game is way too long, by about 20ish hours I no longer cared about the gameplay side of things and just wanted to see the rest of the story. This problem was exasperated by the game artificially padding out game time by buffing enemy health and damage during the latter chapters encouraging grinding, I HATE grinding.

I would TLDR by saying that I liked the gameplay but the game itself just goes on for so long that it becomes a chore.

Now a major thing I saw people complaining about was that XIII is a linear game... but so is X and in fact the two games use the EXACT same layout for progression, to be specific I mean it's strictly linear with an open segment nearing the end and then it returns to linearity for the rest of the game with the ability to head back to previous locations for grinding (the airship in X and a series of portals like was done in VIII and IX for XIII). I don't see a problem with this, and by going into the game expecting it I had a blast.

yeah so I definitely recommend Final Fantasy XIII, It's a really good albeit flawed game towards the latter half. And I can't wait to get into XIII-2 and XIII-3.

So some last points I had in my notes but didn't really fit into the review will be here.

- Game looks ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ phenomenal, best looking Final Fantasy title as far as i'm concerned. XV is a close second.
- The first Paradigm shift in a battle is bull, it takes 3 times longer than the subsequent ones and this got me killed a lot, really annoying.
- later parts of the Crystarium take too long to level up further padding out the playtime artificially.
- Encountered some issues with controller button prompts disappearing and requiring a restart to fix them.
- I needed to install a fan patch to make the game function properly though I never tried without it so I can't really speak on the vanilla experience.
- T I N Y C H O C O B O
- Stagger system is really REALLY good.
