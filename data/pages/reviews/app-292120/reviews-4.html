\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely the black sheep of the family. 13 has a few problems like all other games but this game is in my opinion 1 of the most under rated.

Simplistic, easy to understand. Paradigm just adds a little extra control to your character you're using at the givin moment vs needing to contend with 3 characters at once. Being able to switch paradigms in mid battle makes its tricky to know what to use at the givin moment, but if you have your paradigms set up in the menu appropiately then switching and maneuvering in fights should be easy. (always have a medic) The A.I system is great in the beginning and starts to fall off a little near mid/late game. Your team tends to not choose the best abilities/attacks to deal with enemies. This can be particularly frustrating as you have no control over the selections your A.I team mates do. It still gets the job done none-the-less tho.

Instead of leveling you now have to grind up and level your weapons and accessories and after playing for a while that tends to get fairly easy through the shop as it provides you materials to use for your gear to level with. Bosses can be hard, especially on those duo/solo chapters. Be mindful of how you play :) Just a heads up.

Game definitely feels like a hallway sim, linear in progression but the purpose is clear. You are supposed to be fast moving, storming through enemies because the highlight this game tries to portray isnt the battles or exploration. It wants to be a movie, as it gives a finite amount of cut scenes and amazing story telling. As you walk your paths, the characters interact and talk among themselves creating a immersion not many games give. Great examples, God of War 2018 and Ragnarok or any Anime game you can think of.

The phrase "It isn't about the journey that's important. It is about how you get there, and the friends you meet, and bring along the way that is." applies to this game. Enjoy it for what it is :)

Over all the game is a 6.5/10 It's above average, good, fun experience. Is it the best, of course not.. but is it worth playing and at least experiencing? Yes, I'd say it is.\t\t\t\t\t

All the maps are linear, basically infinite corridors, the paradigm system for me is extremely tiring, in the middle of the battle you have to change your spec just to be able to heal someone, and every time you do that you spend time watching animation! I saw people talking on sites like Gamefaqs when the game launched, how innovative and BETTER than FF12 this A.I. system was. But no... FF12 continues to be the most perfect one ever developed for me. Everything is very automatic, the characters separate all the time, and always in pairs, which makes it even more tiring because it tells the story of one small group, then another, after another, until they come together again. I don't know how they thought this would be cool, besides, Square was so afraid of people not understanding this paradigm system that they made 30 hours of tutorial gameplay... I swear... I want to like this game , I'm giving him all the chances in the world and I'm using the patience I don't have in him. But the simplistic plots compared to the previous ones, the bad AI system, and Vanille moaning 24 hours a day, make me want to tear my hair out several times...\t\t\t\t\t
