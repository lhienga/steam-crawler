\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFF13 is enjoyable. I played it when it first came out on PS3 and recently played with someone else on PC. I know that people have mixed feelings, but the gameplay is interesting and the graphics are still great even by today's standards.

One of the complaints is that the areas are extremely linear, and that's true. I think games were trending that way around that time. It's bad in that there's less exploration, but good in that you're less likely to miss treasure chests.

The beginning half of the game limits you to specific characters and classes, which is fine as it allows you to learn the unique roles. This opens up later in the game.

The story isn't explained too well in the dialogue. Read the story in the menu to understand everything that's going on.

The PC port itself is kind of messy because Square Enix didn't include in game settings for graphics and instead has a separate launcher that opens before playing the game. If your cutscenes all stutter, search for the patch that increases memory available to the game and enhances the launcher.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHot take in comparison to the other reviews I've seen on this game. The characters are great, story is confusing but makes sense about halfway in, and the combat is genuinely interesting. The game is super linear which is unlike any other game in this series. The linear gameplay doesn't allow for much downtime in the story and it can feel like a gauntlet of combat and cutscenes. If you enjoy a streamlined story to tell you'll really enjoy this one.

Long time Final Fantasy fan and I'll give this one a:
9/10 visuals
9/10 characters (Could've been lower if some characters didn't develop the way they did)
7/10 performance. I'm running a ryzen 7 with 5700x. Still get FPS stutters here and there.
8/10 combat - can be basic to begin with using only auto attacks. Gets more interesting using the paradigm system
Story 7/10 - Lightning is an awful sister but she has a lot of self growth. People compare her to a Cloud knock-off but I'll argue to say she offers much more personality than cloud does.

Overall 8/10. Not the best FF game but in my opinion it was worth the buy for less than $20 and I'm interested in seeing how FFXIII-2 goes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was better than expected. This game was met with lots of negative reviews when it first came out, and when I tried it back then I think I let the comments get to me. But I decided to get it on sale for about $10 and ultimately, I was quite impressed with the game.

It's still not perfect and there are better Final Fantasies out there. The world is not as interactive as other entries in the title, and the battle system takes some time to getting used to, but once you do the paradigm system is amazing and lots of fun. The story is lacking in some aspects, but I ended up appreciating all of the characters and their respective narratives. Furthermore, the graphics are so good that they still hold up today, and the music is excellent (though that was never in doubt).

So overall I give it a recommendation. Even though it's still not my favourite FF, it's not my least favourite either, and it's still one of the better video games I have had the privilege to play.\t\t\t\t\t
