\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've tried with this one. Goodness knows, I really did.

But the truth is: FF13 is a game only a mother could love.

It's not even the bloated, messy, pretentious story that made me throw in the towel. Nor the mostly obnoxious, barely indentifiable as humans, characters. It wasn't the 20 hours long tutorial, the horrible pacing, the hundreds of (substantially hollow) cinematics that interrupt your gameplay every two minutes, nor those seemingly endless narrow corridors one literally has to power through. And it wasn't the fact that this is a bad PC port that requires its user to tinker with it, either.

No...
Even though those things that were previously mentioned will definitely grind your patience down, bit by bit, ultimately what made me realize that I couldn't do this any longer was the combat system. Which is arguably the most important aspect of any J-RPG. The combat system of FF13 is bad, it is simply not fun. It seems promising at first, until you eventually realize how limited your own input is, and how utterly and awfully GIMMICKY that whole system is. It's all a gimmick, gimmicks are cheap, and there is very little game to it, therefore very little to play with and enjoy. The transition when you change formations isn't even smooth gameplay-wise: as the player the game actually "locks you out" for what I guess must be one second which feels like two (now try to imagine how this feels when repeated a hundred, a thousand times).

FF13 is the game that buried this franchise, as far as I am concerned. I guess FF really ended when Sakaguchi left (after having lost his mind on Spirits Within and having almost sunk the company, effectively bringing an end to Squaresoft, but ah well). This... corporate product, for in the end this is what it is, has neither soul nor charm.\t\t\t\t\t

So sad it was not possible to play its sequel due to crashing.. but Final Fantasy XIII still gave me a wonderful experience. The visuals were so beautiful and the music very lovely and sweet. It's very linear, up until 70% of the game you're basically running along a set script and you aren't really allowed to explore until chapter 11 (there are 13 chapters of this game) and that is a shame because had this freedom arrived sooner I think more people would have loved this game more ><

The journey til that moment happens is very long but the visuals will not disappoint you and the battle system brings a lot of thrill and fun to the otherwise classic turn-based system seen in previous, older FF games ^^

I beat Orphan on the second try, as the player it's over if the character you play as die in battle so equipping Cherub Crowns on your party before battling against Orphan is highly recommended. Also this game has a tendency to crash a lot. So be aware of that as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know I have 22 hours in this game, but honestly, after a while I had to ask myself why I was even playing anymore.
The combat, although a neat idea and unique, just gets boring. It's almost never as clever as it pretends to be and most of the time you just wish you were picking what you could do rather than watching an autobattler.
I know people love the "story" but calling it a "story" is being incredibly generous. It just strings you along from one scene to the next, interrupting gameplay with short skits that really don't have anything to do with each other, and relies on exposition, or worse, you reading a gigantic encyclopedia to understand wth is going on. L'cie? Falcie? I am supposed to know what those are? Oh, it's in the lore in the menu. Oh, it's not that interesting.
Lastly, the characters are so unlikeable and one dimensional. Snow is just a meathead chad with a hero complex. Lightning is the strong silent type I guess, but is more the boring quiet type. Vanille's voice makes me want to rip my ears out. Hope is a whiny child. etc etc I didn't have a favourite character as much as I had ones I hated less than the others.
The pros? It looks good and the music is good I guess.
So why did I play it so long? It honestly teeters on being a good game for a while. You think there's some payoff, or more to it, or that maybe the combat gets better and you're just doing it wrong, or something - but in actuality there's nothing here but a polished turd dipped in gold paint, but it still stinks like one.\t\t\t\t\t
