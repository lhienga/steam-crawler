\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you really love FF7 and think it would be fun to play it again with characters and setting just different enough that you'd feel like it's not identical, with no materia and battles you can just walk through, you are clearly the target audience for FF13 and you should buy it immediately. That statement contains no sarcasm whatsoever.

I asked around, and it seems that you have to get pretty deep into the story before the actual game shows up. I'm just not here for that. I might be if the story was engaging, or if the characters were... I was going to say "interesting" but I'm instead going to say "rational Human Beings." They're all just so tediously dumb.

Vanille is such a constant barrage of one-dimensional vapidity that I don't even care about anything else that's going on. Frankly, I might be able to tolerate this game if she wasn't in it. She makes me understand how Jar-Jar Binks ruins Star Wars for some people.

It's entirely possible I just haven't played far enough to see that Vanille is actually a really well-rounded, three-dimensional character (I really doubt it), and that Lightning is indeed more than just Cloud with a better haircut (likewise dubious). But I have better things to do than wade ten hours into a boring video game to see if it gets any better.

Overall the worst Final Fantasy game I have ever played, bar none. I've played them all except 12 (including Chrono Trigger, Mystic Quest (both of them), Crystal Chronicles, Tactics, Tactics Advance, Mario RPG, Kingdom Hearts, and probably a few other off-broadway titles I can't recall just now) This is the first Final Fantasy game that has failed to entertain me well-enough that I wanted to play it again the next day. It's the first Final Fantasy game I will never, ever wish I'd finished. I care more about writing this review than I will ever care about what happens to "Not Cloud" and "Two-Handed Barret."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimilar gameplay to Final Fantasy 10, but faster and more streamlined. Completely linear level design until you're about 30 hours into the story where you get to explore a much more open area that also contains all of the side quests (conveniently, you get to return here after you defeat the last boss where you can finish up all of the loose ends).

Every area looks incredibly unique and interesting. The combat is fluid and engaging. The music is excellent and the cutscenes look beautiful. The story however, is not very good. Aside from making it unnecessarily complicated, there are lots of aspects to this story that I could probably write entire essays about and go into detail regarding how badly written it is, but I won't because it's not worth my time. If you play Final Fantasy games because you enjoy great stories, skip this game, play Final Fantasy 10 or 15 instead. But hey, at least it was better than Final Fantasy 12.\t\t\t\t\t
