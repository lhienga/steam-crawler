\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI seriously enjoyed the story, the world building and the characters. Genuinely good stuff.

What I did not enjoy so much was the scuffedness of the combat animations and other combat weirdness (the combat system is very fun but I take issue with many of its quirks), randomly inserting different people into my party and entering combat without letting me level their crystarium trees/update their gear/update the paradigms (which by the way happens ONLY if you have to retry after dying, so it's not like it's impossible to let you do that), truly AWFULLY TERRIBLE level design, endless amount of long encounters in the last three chapters, random boss difficulty swings, intentionally positioning mobs in your way so that you cannot avoid them.

Seeing all that, you may ask, why the am I even recommending it?

This is a really good game, I genuinely loved the first nine chapters. It just became gradually almost unbearable to continue afterwards. It felt as if all the characters suddenly stopped developing and the game was only focused on massacring beasts to get more powerful and to complete "optional" content. Quotation marks because if you avoid all the optional things and avoidable encounters, you are bound to be too weak to continue and you will get stuck at some point. Then it's off to farming mobs to level those crystarium trees.

So beware of that. There is no grinding or farming in the first ten chapters at all, but it will definitely get VERY OBVIOUS in the following chapters if you skipped encounters. It sucks but it is what it is.\t\t\t\t\t
