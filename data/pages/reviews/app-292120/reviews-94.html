\r\n\t\t\t\t\t♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, i actually liked this game. don't get me wrong, ffxiii is not the best final fantasy by a long shot, but there's a lot to love about this game than what it seems at the start of the game.

now, there's something you need to know if you're gonna play the pc version. this port ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ SUCKS, literally the worst squaresoft port i've ever played. yes, even worse than ye olde EIDOS windows 98 releases, it's a disaster for 2014 standards. for example, this game's size is incomprehensible: 50 ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ gb, what the ♥♥♥♥. i've seen longer, much better looking games be much smaller, so wtf is going on there? well, basically squaresoft thought it was a good idea to pack the game full of heavily compressed fmvs, and then instead of just swapping the audio tracks for the various languages, they incluided two versions OF ALL VIDEOS ON THE GAME. yes, prepare to download at least 20gb of videos in japanese you'll probably not even use. and that is just the beggining, this port is riddled with issues. it doesn't even show the xbox360 gamepad mapping by default, it just shows the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ keyboard bindings, my god. at least it's fixable with a mod, but come on, was it that hard to get it working before releasing it? keep in mind this game originally came out in the 360, i'm not really asking for much, since this game does support xinput. what it doesn't support is controller vibration. yes really, no force feedback. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, it's not 1998 anymore, at least get your game working before releasing it.

and to make it all worse, this game performs like ♥♥♥♥. it chuggles along in my 1050, with constant slowdowns when you get to gran pulse. AND THIS IS WITH FF13FIX, i can't imagine how bad this is by default. it barely runs on my 2014 laptop, honestly i wouldn't recommend this if you don't have a dedicated gfx card or something. play the ps3 or xbx360 versions if you can, i heard those are much much better.

the game itself tho, it's good. there's some MAJOR issues tho, like the beggining is a disaster. that's why i've hated it so long, the game literally starts with an endless hallway with endless boring random battles with ♥♥♥♥♥♥ soldier-like enemies. not to mention there's this awful green tint everywhere, it's disgusting. plus, the story starts in media res, they just throw a bunch of buzzwords at you and never explain them at all, never in the entire game. to know what the ♥♥♥♥ is going on, you need to read the "datalog", aka a wall of text explaining what pulse, cocoon, l'cie and fal'cie even are. pacing just sucks, specially for the first 10 or 20 hours of the game. it's nearly incomprehensible, and the fact that most of the dialogue consists of cheesy one liners doesn't really help.

the world doesn't feel believeable at all, it's like a bunch of extremely linear, hallway-style maps until you get to late game (gran pulse), then the game gets REALLY good. they weren't kidding when they said that this game opens up, in fact it's much more open world sometimes than even ffx or ffx-2. cie'th stone missions are extremely fun, but then again pulse doesn't feel real. there's no villages, no people, nobody at all. like, add something at least. oerba sort of works because you can see there used to be people living there, some traces of civilization at least. by contrast, the rest of pulse barely has anything at all except grass, mountains and enemies.

the fact that it's so poorly written and structured is a shame, the ideas for the characters (except for snow, all he does is scream "I'LL PROTECT COCOOOOOON", or "SERAAAAAAAAAAH") and the storyline are genuenly pretty cool, like they already have an edge over most other jrpgs, but the way the story is paced and the non existant dialogue completely kill it. i swear there are more one liners here than in every 80s action movie.

but even considering all that, this game is a ton of fun. the battle system is an evolution of what we got in ffx-2. basically, you use magic to raise the enemies "stagger" gauge, once it's full, it takes a lot more damage and lowers it's elemental resistances. it sounds pretty easy and boring, and it seems like it is at the start of the game, but trust me, it gets challenging and fun fast. bosses are really fun in this game, also buffing and debuffing plays a huge role here. almost reminds me of smt combat, due to how fast it is and how hard it sometimes gets.

also, the music is REALLY good, dare i say it's among the best in modern ff. even the chocobo theme is great, specially when compared to whatever the ffx-2 one was. hamauzu really outdid himself with this one, most battle themes are amazing, and the "dungeon" themes are really good. the last dungeon theme, the cradle will fall, is amazing too, sort of reminds me of ff8's the castle. oddly atmospheric and almost creepy, fits the tone of the chapter perfectly. it's not perfect tho, the main theme sucks ass and ruins every scene it's one with it's cheesy and dull feel. otherwise, great ost.

art design is amazing as well, monsters look amazing, nomura's character designs are better than they ever were up to this point, dungeons look amazing, battle scenarios are amazing too, some resemble late game ffxii. i really like the design of the last dungeon as well, kinda reminds me of smt nocturne's kagatsuchi tower with that mostly red and white color scheme. all the places have their own unique feel, although it's a shame they reused a lot of enemy designs, or just recolored them for a lot of the levels.

in conclusion, ffxiii is a really weird game. it lacks the cohesion the older games had, and the story shares a lot of the same issues as ffx-2. i think that is because production sort of became a mess after sakaguchi left. ffxii was the exception, since it was made by another team from what i know. but otherwise, this game has a lot to love. i understand why it has it's fans honestly, if you're looking for yet another final fantasy game, this might as well do it for you. if you're new to the franchise, maybe it's gonna be for the best to start with... less weird games, like ff6, ff7 or ffx, but i wouldn't skip this one, it's a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn't nearly as bad as some people might make it sound it is. The world in which it's happening is, well, what you would expect from a Final Fantasy game. Slightly more like Final Fantasy 7 though (a bit futuristic, comparable but not entirely cyberpunk either, with magic).

Just don't expect this game to be a masterpiece like Final Fantasy 6, 7 or 10 for example. This is just your average Final Fantasy game (as in, you recognise their signature but it won't totally blow you away - pretty much how I felt when I played 4, 8 and 12 for example). The game is definitely playably enjoyable, but they mostly focused on the visual show aspect of it, I believe.

In a way, I find this game to be closer to a "Tales of" game than it does a Final Fantasy game (especially Tales of Berseria, even if that one came 8 years after), at least gameplay wise.

The more you play it, the more you realise it's going to be very linear, and there's only one road on most maps. You don't move from town to town like in practically all the other Final Fantasy, and there's no world map (much like Final Fantasy 10 and 12, you walk in a 3D world, so no world map is needed). There is also no town nor merchants, because all the stores can be accessed at a save point. It's only by the last 3 chapters of the game that you can roam more or less freely. In total there's 13 chapters, so that gives you an idea of how long it will remain linear.

The dialogues are sometimes awkward because of how the characters interact with each others, and more often than not I have the impression the main casts are on the far edge of their personalities (the pink haired sword wielding woman is way too cold and clumsy, and the blond guy with a beanie and a coat is also way too naive, cheerful and clumsy); luckily, they compensate each others into being realistic to some extent.

Some specific parts almost sound cheesy, but some other part sound morally interesting.

I also read a review mentioning that the dialogues are very awkward. After watching a small video on Youtube, I think that's because of the English voice acting. I've been playing in Japanese (with subtitles) and the voice acting is good. At least, I have nothing negative to say about it.

Graphically speaking, it's quite beautiful. I'm just worried that, nowadays, companies are investing so much resources into 3D. Although it's very pleasing to the eyes, it's also very time consuming, because of which the costs of development are much more important and they have less time to worry about actually working on the game itself, even if this game is nearly 10 years old.

Gameplay wise, the fighting is somewhat comparable to Final Fantasy 10-2 and 12, real time fighting and you only control the lead character. The other characters will act on their own.

You don't have levels in this game, but at some point you will be given the possibility to earn points to spend to increase your stats or unlock abilities (almost like in Final Fantasy 10, but not exactly the same). Initially, you can't spend much points. But the more you play and the more you can improve your characters after major story events, even if it's going to cost more points as you keep going. You start on stage 1 and you can unlock stage 9 before finishing the game. After finishing the game, you unlock stage 10 if you want to keep playing.

You won't earn money after winning a fight anymore. Instead, some enemies will drop some items, which you can either sell or keep for later.

Some items can be used for upgrading your equipements (weapons and accessories). The "raw materials" give little experience but increase the experience multiplier (up to *3). The "mechanical materials" (they have a screw icon in the menu) doesn't increase the multiplier but give more points. And you have "crystals" that allow you to upgrade the equipment after it reached the maximum upgrade level (star level).

You also have some "screw icon" items that barely give any experience, and which are marked as having a lot of value. Those can be sold for a good price.

In my opinion, it's much easier to farm the "machines" and "guards" to resell their "mechanical materials" than the beasts, because "raw materials" are not worth much when you sell them, and you can buy them later on in the shop with the money you gain from selling "mechanical materials".

Much later on, you might find some better options to farm points and money, but it's still going to be quite a pain in comparison to some other Final Fantasy games you might have played. It's not difficult, it's just going to be very slow.

Someone mentioned in a negative review that they didn't understand anything about the game and they had to pause every now and then to go read about the lore on the Internet. But the truth is, when you enter the menu, there's an option in which you can read about the chapters you played through, the characters, the places, the lores, etc. You have everything you need to understand the game within the game, if you want to immerse yourself that is.

Whenever you loose a fight, you are given the option to "Try again", which will reload the game to moment just before the fight that you lost (rather than sending you back to the main screen or the last save, like they usually do in older Final Fantasy games). You can also press start during a cinematic to have the option to skip it, especially if it's a cinematic that you already saw before losing a boss fight for example.

Unlike the other games, you start every fights at full health, so you don't have to worry about healing nor reviving any downed characters inbetween the fights. There is also no such thing as mana, so you can just spam any abilities you want.

Once you finish the game, you'll be given the option to save your game, so you can reload just before the last boss to keep playing for fun.\t\t\t\t\t
