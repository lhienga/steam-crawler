About
Developed and Published by Square Enix. It was originally released to PlayStation 3 and Xbox 360 in December 2009 in Japan and March 2010 Worldwide. It's a Role-playing game that was divisive between fans of the series.

Gameplay
A different approach to the ATB system, more focused on action and quick usage of your abilities.
The character you control as a main character gives all the options you would expect of a Final Fantasy game, except for Auto, which quickly chooses which abilities your character is going to make according to the paradigm you choose for each character. The character you are in control will have access to Summons, Items, etc. While the others use abilities/skills according to their paradigms. As example: if you choose the following paradigms: Commander for the character you are controlling, Synergist and Medic to the other two, the Synergist gonna focus on buffing while the Medic heals the party. And all this can be switched during combat. Meaning you can have many paradigms according to the situation you are in, including full damage (3 commanders, but they focus different targets) or 3 magic (ravager, which increases the staggering of enemies, making them receive more damage). Even if it's seem complicated, it's an extremely rewarding system with can be very full and addictive for many people. But it's not for everyone tastes, so I recommend trying, and if you don't like, refund the game.

Characters
Some are likeable... but mostly not. They are pretty normal. Lightning and Fang are cool, but that's it. They are simple characters, and it's really hard to care about any of them. While I like Sahz, he is full of stereotypes... the black guy that is goofy and is used as comic relief (but never funny... maybe to Japaneses, not sure). And then there's Snow and Vanille... which are super annoying. Hope seems annoying, but I can barely remember anything about him. I wasn't able to care enough about the characters.

Soundtrack
Good soundtrack. There's some that are extremely good... but for me, it's a little inferior, comparing to other Final Fantasy games. But still very good!

100%
Very hard. And can be tedious sometimes. It involves a lot of farm/grinding. Have many saves, specially in Chapters 12-13. You will probably want to return to a previous save after beating the game.

Pros & Cons
Pros
- Extremely good graphics, specially to the time it was originally released.
- Excellent gameplay, which will please most Final Fantasy players.
- Good soundtrack
- Good character design (they aren't memorable as characters, but you can remember how they are visually very well)
- Some excellent fights with bosses and optional bosses, that includes very unique mechanics that make you rethink how you play each fight.

Cons
- Confusing story.
- Most characters lack a good personality or are extremely hateable (Looking at you, Vanille).
- Summons aren't so cool looking as in previous titles.
- Optional bosses, while most (specially the last few) being good fights, were most (if not all) recolors of previous monsters. But they include cool fighting mechanics, which require other strategies to defeat.

Personal Score
Gameplay: 10/10
Story: 7/10
Characters: 6/10
Soundtrack: 8/10
Overall: 7.75/10

Linear and Grindy, but I can't put it down

There are two kinds of JRPG fans out there; Those who like FFXIII, and those who won't even give it a fair shot because of what they've heard online. Yes, the port is a bit of a mess with crashes, freezes, and the occasional weird hangs after a battle, and the frame drops are apparent even on newer machines, but with some community tweaks/mods and perseverance, this game still treats us pretty well. It's just fun.

If you've never played a post-Enix era Final Fantasy title, you might be put off by how different both the leveling and battle systems run. The leveling in this game relies heavily on plot points, and encourages the player to utilize the battle system to out-strategize their opponent instead of over leveling to brute force their way through the game. While both ways are rewarding in their own way, I found it a little refreshing to see the character growth implemented in this fashion. It's reminiscent of the sphere grid from FFX, or even the license grid in FFXII Zodiac Age.

I highly recommend graphic mods and community made fixes either off of the workshop, or from the Nexus to better your experience and minimize any issues that might come up since this game seems to have an aversion to being on PC.\t\t\t\t\t
