\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend FF13 but with caveats.

Is it as bad as the general public opinion of it? No. Do I get why it was met that way? Yeah, absolutely. It is very linear overall, with limited room for exploration that you get many hours in. The combat system is drastically different than other entries and trying to play it like them would be frustrating.

To start with, probably one of the bigger faults of the game is that it does a really poor job of selling itself in the beginning of the game. You can't level up for TWO HOURS, which also means you don't have classes, so the whole "this entire combat system is reliant on hot swapping classes constantly" thing isn't even something you are aware of for two hours. That means early game combat is just.. dull and pointless. If you don't already know that it gets better... I don't know why you would keep with it. Speaking of slow starts, I want to say it was about the 20 hour mark that you FINALLY have a full party that you can adjust. Giving you options is not what FF13 wants to do. There something to be said for using time to establish character dynamics but uh, it's a bit long.

The combat in FF13 is way less "I'm going to do this attack, then this attack, and then this attack" and much more "Right now I need a Physical attacker, a Magical attacker, and a Medic, so swap to that Paradigm". It's very much geared towards using the Auto button (auto attack, auto support, etc) and the main thing you are juggling is what roles you have active in your party. You still need to be mindful of the situation and make sure you get the enemy weakness information so your party members react accordingly, but it's a very different feel than more traditional Final Fantasy.

Probably the last big weakness of the game is just that it can turn into an absolutely grind heavy slog, especially for money. The whole weapon upgrade system is neat, but getting the components you need to actually do it is just padding on a game that really didn't need padding. The linear nature of the game makes this a bit worse, as there ARE enemies that are good sources of money, but if you passed that area before you realized you needed a lot of money, well you can't turn back. I have no qualms in saying that I cheated for components, I did it legit on the Xbox 360 and I did not want to do it again here. That goes for experience grinding too, namely a little boost in Chapter 11 where the difficulty spikes, and then for the last boss. I fell off of the side quest train and I think if you wanted to play it through without cheats you'd need to do them all to have a chance of staying on level. In general the game is balanced well enough that mixing up your strategy is all that is needed, but there are a few difficulty spikes that are just rough.

I guess while I'm already complaining, I'm not the biggest fan of most of the cast, with Hope being the main character I despise. The others are fine, but Sazh really carries it by having the most interesting anything going on.

Overall it is fine, it has aged very well visually, it has its own flavor gameplay-wise that I'm not sure was appreciated in its time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuick review:

Pros
  • Mostly gorgeous visuals and art design that still hold up to this day
  • Great and varied soundtrack from Masashi Hamauzu (Final Fantasy X, Dirge of Cerberus: Final Fantasy VII, Final Fantasy VII Remake)
  • Interesting and detailed world and lore
  • Good cast of main characters
  • Mostly solid English voice acting
  • Great timing-focused twist on the traditional ATB gameplay...

Cons
  • ... that takes way, way too long to fully open up to the player
  • Linear areas aren't broken up with larger or nonlinear levels often enough prior to Chapter 11, resulting in monotony in "exploration"
  • Non-party characters generally aren't fleshed out nearly well enough and are often forgettable
  • Story isn't bad, but isn't as interesting as the lore or main cast
  • Game relies too much on the Datalog in the main menu to relay important world/lore information to the player

Overall scores: 7/10 objectively, 8/10 subjectively\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper mixed feelings about this game, the first 18-20 hours are a huge slog, and you are basically just on rails fighting whatever monster is directly put in your walking path with little or no choice. The only thing that interrupts this path are cut scenes for a narrative that is extremely difficult to follow with all the techno babble specific to this FF universe.

Eventually the game opens up and the Gran Pulse area is where it really shines and is a lot of fun, before reverting right back to the linear fighting track. Despite how straightforward it is, everything still feels poorly tuned, requiring an annoying amount of level grinding just to make some of the later sequences passable without frustrating and prolonged (sometimes 30+ minute) battles.

In the end, I have an appreciation for this game as a long time Final Fantasy series fan, but would I recommend this game to anyone else? probably not.

Hardcore FF series fans will have something to appreciate with the trilogy, but anyone looking to break in to the franchise, I would much more easily recommend Final Fantasy XIV, Final Fantasy XV, or Final Fantasy VII REMAKE as modern entries.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood things:
·Great battle system, could even be the best in Final Fantasy saga.
·Challenging difficulty at many moments, during the playtrough and endgame
·Money in this game becomes an important asset as items, so you have to think about not misspending them.
·Music is good, not at the very best of FF but at a great level as usual.
·The usual SquareEnix amazing cinematics.

Ok / Meh things:
·It looks fairly good in some places.
·The endgame with all missions and challenges is good, but the equipment upgrading and material farming end becoming very grindy.

Bad things:
·The story and the characters don't have the slight interest or emotion, very bland in that regard, and more thinking that this is usually an important asset of Final Fantasy saga.
·An eternal hallway, other games like FFX could also be that but they achieve to "fake" that sensation and feel free. FFXIII gives you an extreme corridor feeling.

6.5/10 I put a score because I don't think it's a terrible game as some people say, but it's not at the level of the majority of FF games. Since steam doesn't let you mark it as abstained (to encourage positive recomendations and selling which is ok for them) I check it as not recomended, but at the end of the day I recomend it to Final Fantasy fans.\t\t\t\t\t
