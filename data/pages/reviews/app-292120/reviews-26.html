Final Fantasy XIII (later called as FFXIII) is an underrated game.

-Setup for a story:
The events of this game take place on an artifical moon called Cocoon, but also for a brief moment we visit a planet Gran Pulse. It starts off in the middle of a Purge - Goverment of this world wants to eliminate everyone who had a contact with a L'Cie - a human that had met a Fal'Cie, a demigod, which enslaved a human giving him a focus and a magic power. Focus is a mission that a L'Cie has to finish before his time expires. If he is successful then he gains eternal life in a crystal. If not then he becomes a mindless zombie-like being called Cie'th. Because of some unfortunate events our main party characters become L'Cies with the same focus - destroy the Cocoon, the place where every human lives. They fate is sealed - They either kill humanity and save themselves or become a bunch of Cie'ths

-Main Theme of FFXIII:
It might seem obvious - fighting against our own fate. However for me it seems like FFXIII's main theme is depression. Every main character has a moment of self-doubt, feeling of being powerless, simply giving up. Some try to cover it and act strong. The end of chapter 8 however shows the extreme situation. I won't go into details to not spoil this moment. Other themes we might see in FFXIII are: betrayal, revenge, friendship. I do not think that they are as important as the other two.

-Gameplay outside of battle:
"Uhh this game is linear and its bad"
I often hear things like that. However there is one thing we have to look at. There are games that are made to be open world and games that are made to be linear. SE wanted to change the formula from the previous FF games. All of them were Open World for most of the time (I would argue with FFX but it's up to you). The problem with FFXIII being an open world is the fact that... well... we are playing as "wanted criminals". Our party shouldn't visit too many cities so they won't be discovered (In the entire game we only visit 4 cities, one of which is a village called Oerba that was the place where L'Cie had lived). Being an open world just doesn't fit the story. I can agree that some locations could be made bigger (but not as big as Gran Pulse section. It should stay as the biggest one)

-Battles:
"Uhhh just press auto battle and win"
Yes. For 90% of the game I was pressing auto battle instead of manually choosing my attacks. However for me the main part of FFXIII combat is a Paradigm Shift. You have to focus on choosing correct roles in battle and act fast changing them. I had so much fun with this. Wanted to heal? One or two medics. Wanted to stagger an enemy? One or two ravagers with a commando or saboteur. For me the combat in this game is more fun than FFXII's Gambits

-Locations & Music:
I wanted to combine those two for a reason. I feel like locations and their music can tell us what our characters are feeling. The Gapra Whitewood shows us a struggle inside Hope and Lightning, while The Sunleth Waterscape is as colourful as our happy Vanille... until she starts to realise that she is the reason why Sazh suffers. Then everything gets darker. Music shows this as well. FFXIII unlike any other FF games uses a lot of vocal tracks in its soundtrack. And I love it. Easly the best music of any FF game. "Blinded by Light", "The Gapra Whitewood", "Fighting Fate" - those are just some of the titles.

-Characters:
I won't go in depth to not spoil anything. All I wanna say is that I love them all. Maybe with an exception of Snow. Snow is just... dumb... oh well. And I also wanna say that I think the main antagonist is bad. I understand his goal but... It's just too complicated. He could finish everything much easier.

For me FFXIII is the best in the series and easily in my Top 3 games of all time. It has its ups, it has its downs. But it had the biggest impact on me from every FF game I have played (Haven't play XVI yet so please no spoilers)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember watching my sister play this game on the PS3 back when I was a kid and despite what most FF fans say about the game its honestly not that bad and holds up pretty well even today albeit I'm not the biggest Final Fantasy fan myself, I'm more of a sucker for the Dragon Quest games with the classic adventure style in addition to it not taking itself too seriously. On that topic, the thing with Final Fantasy is I find myself laughing at some of the over the top "epic" scenes that happen throughout the game paired with the ridiculous dialogue that Ill get into later makes for a real Sloppy Joe of a story, its literally falling apart as you bite into it.

I am going to be a bit nit-picky but couldn't they have done a better job writing some of the characters? I mean this is FINAL FANTASY, a powerhouse of storytelling, the household name of role-playing games, the ultimate single-player experience, you get my point. I mean with that being said, I am currently on chapter 6 and couldn't they find a better character than this kid Hope that tags along I mean he is an insufferable addition to the party and what the hell are these scenarios between Serah, Snow and Lightning, they're trying to drive this deep and dramatic narrative but I swear it comes off like some reality TV show like Jersey Shore and its funny cause some of these scenes are on a beach. They could've put a bit more thought into it but hey, what do I know. I guess things will make more sense as the story continues to unfold through the chapters.

All the elements that make a Final Fantasy game are present, great soundtrack and decent battle system but my brotha they could of at least did some revision before releasing some of the final dialogue choices and this is bizarre to me as this is coming from the same writer who wrote FFX and FFXII, some of the most beloved in the entire franchise and even in video games as a whole.

Finally, since FF16 wont be out on steam for a while I decided to fulfill my want for some good ol' Final Fantasy gameplay with FF13 and I gotta say, its doing a good job. I would say for the new RPG/JRPG player to avoid this as it wouldn't be an enjoyable first time in 2023, I'd say wait till they port FF16 onto steam or wait for the new Dragon Quest or better yet go smoke some crack idk man\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOf the mainline, single-player Fantasies Final, this one is by far my favorite (and no, it wasn't my first onethat honor belongs to the grandmother of them all on the NES). If you've never given it a shot because of the harsh responses to it in the late '00's/early '10's, you ought to! The game is, in the end, not significantly more linear than most other titles in the series (if you played and enjoyed FFVII Remake, in particular, you should really give XIII a chance!), and its combat and advancement systems are some of my favorite in the series, with a hybrid, fast-paced but tactical approach (though some of the intricacies aren't well-explained by the game itself, unfortunately).

Throw in some of the best character writing in the series (including some of its best female protagonists and more or less its first lesbian couple), a great cast, a story dealing with themes of fate vs freedom, the bonds between families of both the found and biological variety, while meditating on how we decide who we are in a world that's often hostile to us in various ways, and gameplay that ambitiously tries to tie them all together, and you have a quintessentially Final Fantasy experience.

I'm new to the PC version, having played the game to death during the PS3 era, and coming back to it has only deepened my love for it. Go in sporting your good time hat and you're in for quite the ride.\t\t\t\t\t
