\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts okay. Its not bad. Its not great. Its only okay. At times, there seemed to be something lost in translation. I didn't quite understand the motive of some characters in a few spots. The battle system is completely different than the usual final fantasy formula. It requires a different mindset, so instead of training to level up, you figure out what the enemy is weak against and use that in the form of either magic or physical. I loved the concept.

There were some battles I felt really proud afterwards, because of how complex it was. Other battles, it was either way too easy or stupid difficult.

The story does drop you in as if you assume some type of knowledge. Its not a negative but it is a learning curve to the lore of the game.

The music was either inspired by Final Fantasy X or it just happened to coincidentally be that way. I don't know. Some of it was great. There were a few that I just absolutely hated.

All in all, will i finish this game? Yes
Would I play it again? No.
I'm only really playing this to finish the final fantasy games I haven't played.
I give it a 6.7/10

If you're planning on beating this game because you love final fantasy, don't be afraid to turn on easy mode, because it doesn't matter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wouldn't say this is a top Final Fantasy game, but it definitely doesn't deserve all the hate it gets. I enjoyed the game overall, and the combat system was more macro focused than micro which I thought was a good variation of the ATB system. The main difficulty comes from having the right paradigms set up rather than inputting commands. The story was pretty confusing at first, with characters throwing out terms like fal'Cie, l'Cie, Cie'th, etc. without any explanation but it made more sense as it went on. The characters felt pretty realistic as well, such as people complaining about Hope when in reality everyone would probably act like him if put in his situation. In the end I was satisfied with the conclusion.

The music was good, but I really couldn't tell you at all about any zone tracks that I liked. The only zone theme I remember is from the Sunleth Waterscape (which is one of my favorite songs in general from this game). The great songs were very good though, and probably up there on the most iconic songs of the series.

The endgame is really the only part dislike about the game, which sucks because I really enjoyed the combat. There's too much running around compared to how little movement options there are. The type of grinding you have to do didn't really feel like it fit with how the game was designed, such as enemies not respawning until you go pretty far, ending up in more time spent walking than fighting. The sense of progression after you maxed out the main 3 roles of each character also felt nonexistent. CP grinding and trapezohedron farming are too tedious for me. I might come back to it someday, but I would rather move onto XIII-2 than grind it out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, I know it is a divisive game, and the memes about "it plays itself" can be kind of true sometimes, lol. But I love this game. It was the first Final Fantasy I played which I felt required a moderate amount of skill. What I think people don't understand is that this game requires a mastery of manipulating the battle system, so the features like, being able to stop charging ATB and begin executing command strings, and paradigm shifting correctly and quickly, can make or break your attempt at the boss/mobs. It seems easy because a lot of what's being automated is the busy work; the meat and potatoes of reading and reacting to mechanics is here en force.

My biggest criticism is the crystarium. It is largely a joke until you get the major unlock, and even then, each character has 3 roles that they excel at and you will need to flesh those out before it makes sense to branch out. That's true to the meme of playing itself, lol.

The PC port is okay, although admittedly you need a decent amount of power to run it because it is not well-optimized and lacks graphics options to tweak. It's not TOO demanding, especially if you have at least last-gen hardware, but toasters will probably short-circuit at native resolution.

The best way to play this game is probably on Xbox One, this would be a distant second-best. Any which way you slice it, I love this game, and there's a reason my PC version keeps getting love.\t\t\t\t\t
