\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pleasantly surprised with this game. It is no secret that this game is regarded as one of the most controversial and mixed entries in the ff franchise, if not, the most divisive entry in the series, but, despite all of that, I found myself really enjoying this ff adventure.
Ever since I found out that this game had a lot of controversy, it gave me incentive and intrigue to play it. There aren't a lot of game that I dislike so I was willing to give this one a shot and see how I would think of it. I guess I'll start with a few highs and lows with this game.

The story: I found the story to be pretty good, I think its not its strongest quality but it did the job to keep me hooked until the end and see the outcome of everything. Its not the most complex story in the franchise and the dialogue did a solid job in telling the story, but one thing that did kinda bother me was how some information was kept in the datalog, and therefore, you wouldn't grasp the complete story until you read certain moments of the story through extra text and reading. There were also some pacing issues for me, there would be moments in the story that were thrilling but then it would be slow again, I found that some of these moments were due to areas overstaying their welcome, areas in the game would be too long and repetitive, slowing the pace of the story and exhausting the player from so much battling. I felt like some areas could be half the size they are, and the pacing would be better if they had managed it this way.

The characters: The characters were enjoyable, I don't really dislike any character, although I know that some people dislike Snow and Hope, to name a few, but, I think that they all had their well deserved development throughout the game, each one with their own personalities and motivations as to why they are going on this journey, and by the end you feel satisfied for investing time with these characters and seeing how they grew as a group. The side characters were okay, even though they didn't have a lot of spotlight since the main crew was always on the move. As for the villain, Barthandelus was interesting, even though his motivations had me a bit confused at moments, especially towards the end, and by going back to the datalog, this menu keeps lots of key information about him, but his character was still intriguing and menacing.

Battle system: I LOVE THE COMBAT. I know that the combat is divisive as well, but I ended up loving it, it is the strongest quality of this game and one of the main reasons as why I continued towards the end. Its a very rewarding and satisfying battle system, because once you learn how to fully use the paradigm system, battles are the absolute best. When I found myself losing battles, which happens quite often, I would learn from my mistakes and retry battles, knowing how to take down the enemy, and it would always be satisfying, especially when you earn 5 stars. I know the paradigm system isn't for everyone, but the difficulty of getting through a tough battle, succeeding and understanding the mechanics is very rewarding to me, and that made me enjoy my time with it. I would actually consider it as one of my favorite battle mechanics in the ff franchise.

Music: The soundtrack of this game is absolutely beautiful, some of the tracks in this game are some of my favorites now. The range of songs were wonderful, areas of the game felt more enjoyable thanks to the songs. Some highlights would be, The promise, Blinded by light, Saber's edge, Fighting fate, etc.

I thinks that's all of what I wanted to say, and I think I've rambled on long enough. Its pretty clear that I REALLY liked this game, there are flaws of course like every other game, but I enjoyed it for what it was and it was a really fun experience. Forgot to mention the hallway simulator aspect that everyone talks about, the linearity never really bothered me, it didn't add any issues so it was never annoying, plus when all the areas in the game look visually nice, it distracts you from the linearity. Also I didn't have any major issues with this port, the game never crashed on me and it ran pretty well on my pc. And with that, I guess I'll move on to the sequels and see how they improve on the gameplay and story.
If anyone has gotten to this point, I hope it was useful to you in any way and shared a different opinion on this game, cause I think it doesn't deserve the bad reputation it has, this ff has its unique qualities just as the other ff's have theirs, and this one delivered with the ones it had.\t\t\t\t\t
