\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome games are open-world, some games are more linear. This game focuses heavily on the story, the characters and the player's curiousity and patience to read some datalog entries to get a grasp for the complex but fascinating world and mythology of the Fabula Nova Crystallis.
You have to like cutscenes (which look incedible by even 2023 standards) and you have to enjoy watching things happen, as much as doing things yourself.
Don't get fooled by the incredibly "out-there" cast, once you are a few chapters in you will realize how the game plays with the whole "don't judge a book by it's cover" trope and turns stereotypical JRPG tropes (such as the hero, the cutesy girl, the always serious one) on their heads.
Every single character in this game is important for the progression of the story. There is not just 1 or 2 main ones (even though it seems like it at first) and the rest is just set dressing. Every character is heavily involved in how the story turns out.

Also, there is side-content once you get close to the end with many cool enemies and very challenging fights, not much NPC action however. It's cool content if you are into the battle system and like strategizing how to beat that enemy with crazy damage or infinite HP.

As for the PC port, I can't touch on that since I installed the FF13Fix patch the moment I started playing. I would recommend you do the same.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was eagerly anticipating the release of "Final Fantasy XIII", and I must say, it's a game that has both impressed and left me with some mixed feelings. Here's my review of this highly anticipated title:

The Good:
  • Visually stunning: The graphics in "Final Fantasy XIII" are breathtakingly beautiful, with stunning character models, detailed environments, and jaw-dropping special effects. The game's art direction is top-notch, creating a unique and visually captivating world that draws you in from the very beginning.
  • Compelling story: The game boasts a complex and emotionally-driven narrative that keeps you engaged and invested in the characters and their struggles. Following the journey of Lightning and her allies as they navigate a dangerous and unforgiving world is a thrilling experience filled with memorable moments and unexpected plot twists.
  • Exciting gameplay: The game's battle system is unique and dynamic, combining turn-based and real-time elements. The Paradigm System allows for strategic customization of character abilities and tactics, providing a fast-paced and challenging combat experience that requires careful planning and strategy.
  • Unforgettable highlights: The game's memorable characters, awe-inspiring soundtrack by Masashi Hamauzu, and breathtaking visuals come together to create an immersive experience that leaves a lasting impression. The world of "Final Fantasy XIII" is rich with depth and complexity, and the character development is a highlight, making you care deeply about their journey and growth.

The Sceptical:
  • Linear progression: One of the criticisms of "Final Fantasy XIII" is its linear structure, with limited exploration and a lack of open-world elements. The game's story is heavily scripted, and there is less freedom to explore and deviate from the main path, which may not appeal to players who prefer open-world RPGs.
  • Steep learning curve: The game's combat system can be complex and challenging to master, with a learning curve that may be steep for some players. It may take time to fully grasp the intricacies of the battle system, and this could potentially be a barrier for new players or those who are not accustomed to the Final Fantasy franchise.

Final Thoughts:

"Final Fantasy XIII" is a visually stunning, emotionally engaging, and strategically challenging RPG that pushes the boundaries of the genre. While it may have some divisive opinions due to its linear progression and steep learning curve, it's undeniably a game that offers a unique and memorable experience for fans of the franchise and RPG enthusiasts. Its compelling story, exciting gameplay, and unforgettable highlights make it a worthwhile addition to any Final Fantasy fan's collection.

Overall Score: 8/10 \t\t\t\t\t

The battle system does have some depth to it, but again, most of that depth is completely locked away until you're on the last chapter or two of the game. And, frustratingly, battles take place on separate screens from the map, with a separate reward screen after winning. Having played FFXII immediately after this game with its in-map battles, quick exp rewards, and gambit system, this feels like a step backwards for sure.

And I really do wish I could say that at least the story was good, but, well, no, not really. There are a lot of interesting ideas and themes on paper, for sure. Every character in the party has specific motivations, problems, etc, and I could walk through specific instances where the game explicitly develops each character and relationship. It all feels rather mechanical, though. Like they understood the parts that make a good story, but not how to glue them together.

Overall, I'm personally glad I played it, and it's a game that's fascinating to think and talk about. But I wouldn't recommend others play it unless you fit into one of the two categories I mentioned at the top. And even then, I would caution not to set your expectations high until you get to XIII-2.

Oh, and if you do decide to play it, I highly recommend using a controller. I went xbox, and that felt very natural.\t\t\t\t\t
