\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEpic story, mediocre gameplay. If you like FF stories, definitely for you. If you play turn-based RPGs for the gameplay, you may not enjoy it. I do not agree with the hate this game gets, I've found it thoroughly enjoyable.

The first 3/4 of the game you're locked into characters and leaders. You cannot change them. Your party is split for half the game and you don't get the opportunity to do anything aside from the story for like 15-25 hours, depending when you get to chapter 11. The crystarium (leveling system) is also slowly unlocked by chapter until you get to chapter 11, so it gates the amount of leveling you can do until then - not uncommon for this era of RPG - see Lost Odyssey. It strays from a typical FF in this fashion. Therefore, if you're approaching this game, for the first time, with the mentality that will be a traditional FF where you can explore everything, level to your heart's content, and then move forward when you're ready, you will be highly disappointed - which I'm assuming where a lot of the hate comes from.

It is probably the most linear game you'll play for the first 3/4 of it. It is very obvious SQE overcompensated from FFXII, which was basically a single-player MMORPG albeit with a much better story than your typical MMO.

IMO, the story made it all worth it to get to chapter 11, where the game opens up and you finally get the opportunity to explore a bit on your own and grind to actually get meaningful CP. If you grind early, sure you can max out the limited crystarium but you'll never get enough CP to meaningfully level with it later when it actually opens up. Thus, the first 15-25 hours are pushing you through hallways, fighting, advancing the story.

The game allows you to explore and build everything up in Gran Pulse post-game as well, so if you're just in it for the story, you can roll through and then later come back to complete many of your achievements.

The visuals are great for a PS3/X360 game. The combat is better than the hate it's getting. If you sit there and just use auto battle, you'll for sure get bored. But, to advance, especially in chapter 11 and beyond, you'll need to understand the paradigm system, your characters' strengths and weaknesses, who will make the best commando, ravager, medic, sentinel, etc. You'll need to switch paradigms throughout - the guy who said basic fights take 5 min is just doing it wrong and wanted to complain...for example sitting in a sentinel/commando/medic paradigm. Sure you may win, and that would be boring as hell and take 10 min. But most basic fights you can roll through in 30 sec or less if you use the right paradigms, understand enemy weaknesses/strengths, basically just understand what you're doing.

All in all, I think this game is better than people say it is and I really enjoyed it when it came out on PS3/X360 and am currently enjoying it again. 8/10 in my book.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent concept with TERRIBLE execution. Watch a playthrough on youtube if you are interested.

1) The Grind:

Everything is an absolute grind, and I mean everything. Enemies has absurd health that takes forever to get through normally, but towards the end of the game they also take half damage from everything. Basic fights can really drag on, up to 5mins, and these are the basic fights.

You can upgrade your various weapons and accessories to be more effective, but this too is a grind as most things require thousands of points to upgrade a decent way, and most things only give very little. The best bet is to use the weaker things to build a chain bonus and increase the potency of future melds by up to 200%, so build that chain then meld a good amount of your best materials, These materials are also rare, and kind of a pain in the ass to get, however, you can buy them from the store, which is great...except that gil can only be obtained by selling the things you are farming or finding a pitiful amount in the chests.

It takes a TON of time to get anywhere, with really long corridors, frequent (and often irrelevant) cutscenes, enemies placed in a way where you physically cannot bypass them, This wouldn't be too bad, however, the fights are grinds where you only really can use one tactic.

2) Battle Mechanics:

If your party leader (the one you control) goes down for any reason, it's game over. This sucks so much and is by far the worst thing in the game. Especially when battles tend to place your leader at the front, so they tend to take a ton of damage right off the bat unless you have a sentinel, which is slow and often results in a lower battle rating, which means less XP and items and my god it sucks.

Characters switching between roles is absolutely vital, but the first time you do it in battle each character goes through the animation separately. The game does not pause and enemies can freely take actions, which has lead to more game overs that I care for, and feels very cheap.

Another thing that is terrible is that you control only one of the three characters. The others are completely automated and if you need them to do something, you cannot do anything but pray to the AI gods, and those gods don't like listening.

As stated previously, there is only one way to do meaningful damage in this game, and that is "Staggering" the enemy. Every enemy, every fight, this is a tactic that you HAVE to do, and the only class that can effectively build the chain gauge is Ravager, so now that is also mandatory. Other classes can buff and debuff, but often enemies will just dispel your buffs with their standard attacks (especially towards the end). The buffs you want (Haste/Protect/Shell/Bravery/Faith), may or may not be used the AI before worse buffs are used, wasting a ton of time.

Oh and 99% of what you fight is some form of machine, so that got old fairly quickly.

3) Minigames

There are none. You have combat, combat, and more combat. I hope you like the combat.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Conclusion:
It had promise, but terrible execution on nearly every level. When I got to the last chapter I just didn't care anymore, and it just kept going on and on through forced fights and bosses, then those bosses as standard fights not 2 minutes later, it just pure pain.

Just...do yourself a favour and watch a video if you are interested. It might be for you, but for the average player, this ain't it.\t\t\t\t\t

The game

You're all crazy. Final Fantasy X is also linear as hell, but we all love it! Final Fantasy XIII has engaging, dynamic gameplay that feels vital in ways that others do not, and its chain bonus and staggering mechanics make the flow of battles turn on a dime. Preemptive strikes, auto-heal, battle ratings, it all contributes to a seamless gameplay loop that keeps it fresh and challenging. The music's iconic, the characters are all hot, and the locales are breathtaking. There's plenty of hidden items and doodads that make you want to pay attention.

Yes, it's a fantastical, melodramatic fever dream. L'Cie? Fal'Cie? The ♥♥♥♥ are you talking about? A lot of the plot and writing is like that, but its uniqueness grows on you. Emotive characters and their great interactions keep it mostly grounded in reality, and the lore is fun to read about when you get the chance.

Final Fantasy XIII is a game for true fetishists who are as obsessed with armpits as the game's director.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a mixed bag to end all mixed bags.

On the one hand, it was refreshing to play a Final Fantasy game where the protagonists' flaws and self-doubt were magnified to such a staggering degree that takes on a tone almost akin to Neon Genesis: Evangelion levels of depression. The main playable cast of this game all have deep, deep problems that some people critiquing the game's story fail to recognize make for a compelling cast you want to see grow and change. Lightning has rage that clouds her judgment, Snow has naivete that harms those around him, Hope has misplaced anger resulting from grief, Vanille is a complete coward, Sazh has crippling depression and hopelessness, and Fang has no sense of self-purpose. All six of them have been tasked with a gargantuan, damn-near insurmountable task of revolting against the very concept of fate itself, and the game is more or less about how they process those issues through a sense of camaraderie and chosen family. These are not characters that have only a single flaw, either, as the flaws I mentioned create a kaleidoscope of conflict between the party members that lead to interesting ethical and moral dilemmas I haven't yet seen from a Final Fantasy game. It's really spot-on stuff that I'm sad to see get ignored by fans of the series.

On a visual level, this game is GORGEOUS, especially for the time- it might very well be the best-looking game released in the 2000's. Shadow effects, facial detail, animation work, it's all really top-of-the-line stuff. The game's visual style cribs the sci-fi elements of Final Fantasy VII and blends it with the more traditional fantasy designs of IX, leading to a visually-striking blend of color and vibrance that always holds the player's visual attention.

The combat also ramps up the active-time battle system of the previous games to a whole different level of strategy using the Paradigm system, which adds a great level of depth and challenge to the combat that means even if you grind out the Crystarium and get maxed-out characters, if you don't strategize, you will die, plain and simple. This tension only reinforces the mortality of our main cast and their feelings of doubt and inability to conquer the unthinkable, only to reward the player with a feeling of genuine accomplishment not unlike solving a difficult puzzle, and in turn a greater appreciation of the characters' capabilities when sharing responsibilities.

The soundtrack is one of the most underrated in the whole series. While Uematsu's soundtracks are iconic and given the recognition they deserve, Masashi Hamauzu's work is simply elegant and breathtaking. The use of strings in the arrangements, the clever and appropriate blend of orchestral and rock music, it's all a sight to behold that only serves to elevate the game. When you think of this game you've probably got Blinded By Light playing in the back of your brain.

On the other hand, the complaints about this game's linearity are entirely valid, but I have a bigger bone to pick with the pacing. The game speeds you through 10 chapters of nonstop hallways after a way-too-long opening chapter that does very little to tutorialize the main feature of the game's combat or meaningfully explain what's happening in the story other than some vague symbolism, then at Chapter 11 when the plot has finally ramped up to what feels like a natural climax, everything halts and the game becomes a semi-open world with side quests from NPCs that you can't see and fanservice Final Fantasy mobs to clear just purely for the sake of your love of the combat. When it reaches Chapter 11, all story momentum that the game was building up just sinks like the Titanic.

This game's script and plot also suffer from Proper Noun Syndrome. There are three different and distinct terms that end with the suffix "-'Cie", and all three get used in the same sentence in such a way that winds up only confusing the player. Being reminded of Kingdom Hearts' construction of plot and worldbuilding is not a high mark of quality, that much is for sure.

The complaints about the PC port are also valid, but there is a pretty simple patch to apply that will solve most issues with the game's performance. Install FF13Fix and the 4GB patch installer, configure some settings in an .ini file and you'll mostly have all performance issues squared away. I just wish the game was ported better.

6/10, slight recommendation. If this is potentially your first Final Fantasy game, I'd say stray far away, but if you're either returning to the game after playing it a decade ago on consoles or you're just a fan of the series in general, I'd say to pick it up for completion's sake. I first played the game when I was 9 years old and begged my mom to pre-order it with money I saved up from yard work, and didn't really know what to expect from the game, as I had only ever played the first two Kingdom Hearts games but had a genuine interest in Final Fantasy. I wasn't mature enough to understand the beats the story was trying to hit, and much like many of the other players I see reviewing the game, I saw the main characters as idiots or inherently bad people. Now, at 23, a little more than XIII years later, while I'm not in love with the game, I definitely understand it. Maybe you'll pick something up playing it that others may have missed, and ultimately, that's what makes this medium such a special one.\t\t\t\t\t
