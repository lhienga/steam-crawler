An Amazing Experience Marred by an Intentionally-Slow and Linear First-Half

Disclaimer: I played with HD Texture and Cutscene mods + Tweaked settings via Nvidia Profile Inspector + Reshade so I cannot comment on the performance. Only issue I had was 4 crashes through my 100+-hour playthrough


Final Fantasy XIII is an overall amazing game with an amazingly-written and grounded cast of characters. However, the first few chapters are slow by design and it definitely hampers the fun-factor of the game. It is also by far the least Final Fantasy-feeling game that I have played. Lack of towns/NPCs, an extremely-linear level design, auto-battle mechanic in combat, plot takes a backseat in exchange of developing the characters, etc. but In an era of open-world games, Final Fantasy XIII definitely stands out and feels refreshing in 2023. It is one of those games that unfortunately "gets better in xx chapters" but depending on your level of investment with the party and the world it portrays, it might give you a reason to play through the slog and have a surprisingly fun experience like it did for me.


Pros:

+ Interesting Lore and World-building. Datalog is fun to read through

+ Amazing character writing for the main party

+ Gorgeous Level Design and Animations

+ Fun and Complex Battle Mechanics by the end of the game


Cons:

- Horrible villains and underdeveloped side-characters

- Didn't care for the plot most of the time

- Extremely slow pacing in the first half of the game

- Voice audio can be low at times

- Combat takes a long time until it gets really fun and complex

- side-quests and crafting are extremely grindy and may require looking-up a guide to properly do it

- Personal Gripe: Vanille's constant moaning


Amidst all the cons listed above, the characters, and later on - the combat, are what really made me invested enough to go through all that and it paid off well. By the time I rolled credits, I am in love with Final Fantasy XIII and glad I gave it the second chance it deserves.

116 hours and 100% Achievement later, I give Final Fantasy XIII a 7/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game gets a lot of hate but I don't agree with them. While it isn't the strongest entry in the Final Fantasy series, it's still a fun game. One of the largest complaints folks seem to have is that the game doesn't open up until closer to the end of the game but I'd argue that there's plenty of JRPGs that also do not, not everything needs to be a sandbox game.

The story is pretty good and the combat system is pretty fun too. The game can get to be pretty grindy if you're trying to get all of the achievements but all that is of course optional.

The story does do a pretty decent job of fleshing out the characters and often allows players to change their initial opinions about them as the plot unfolds. In my opinion it's worth your time and money to play through this game and as long as you enjoy it to move on to Final Fantasy XIII-2 and then Lightning Returns to complete the trilogy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game doesn't deserve intense hatred it gets.

Like, yes, the game does a poor job at introducing you to the world, a lot of important information just gets shoved into the datalog and you always kinda feel like an outsider, the characters are all doing their thing, you kind of understand what it is that they're doing but you're not really part of it. It's a shame because despite the story itself being pretty standard the world is really interesting so it's a bit of a missed opportunity.
I do like the characters though, their interpersonal dynamics are handled pretty well and I thought their relationships developed pretty naturally throughout the game.

The shining star of FF13 is really the combat. FF13 ditches the micromanaging of your average JRPG and has you focus more on controlling the flow of battle through the paradigm system and it really is a battle system like no other. Despite how much people harp on it being just press X to win (which is just not true and the same could be said about countless other highly praised JRPGs) the game is really, truly, spectacularly engaging. It does take a bit to let go of your hand, which is a shame but man, once it does it just goes hard.

It's quite challenging as well, especially towards the end, the last chapters kicked my ass, the post game is even harder but I didn't mess around with that too much.

The linearity didn't really bother me at all and I'd say that Gran Pulse is probably the weakest area in the game imo.

Also, those FMVs are incredible, and the game still looks beautiful.

Looking forward to getting into the rest of the trilogy.\t\t\t\t\t
