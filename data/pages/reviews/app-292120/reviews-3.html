\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've ever wanted to play a soap opera, this is the game for you. That's not to disparage the game any; I actually used to really enjoy soap operas. The interpersonal conflicts between the characters spur them on for a good deal of the game, and the characters must set their nuanced differences aside to band together and overcome the larger issue at hand. I haven't seen a party that initially despised each other this much since I played Final Fantasy Four Heroes of Light for the Nintendo DS.

Speaking of nuanced, the combat system. A lot of the time it feels like you're not playing the game, but you really are. You act more as a conductor, and the challenge lies heavily in correctly changing "paradigms" (the party's strategy) on the fly and watching as the situation changes. It also takes a lot of experimentation to really catch on--the game gives you enough ideas as a starting point, but later on it expects you to have mastered this, and then it really opens up when anybody can be anything. Eventually you'll never have enough Crystogen Points.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of people rag on 13, and while I don't think it's a perfect game I really enjoyed it.

I believe 13 has possibly the best cast in the whole series, really only tied with 9. The characters all have real motivations for moving forward, and while some of the interactions are a little forced, the party really develops into a team in a fun way.

A huge portion of 13 is a sort of introduction to the world and story of the trilogy, and while many people find the long introduction to 13 to be a negative, filtering out a lot of players, I found it to be the better part of the game. The early sections of 13 focus almost entirely on the characters and the world, and the pacing is great. Jump to chapter 11 when the game opens up, and it loses a lot of focus and meaningful progression, and imo, it never really gets going again.

The gameplay is pretty meh, the team system is interesting but feels a little restrictive in what you can really do, as there's really only one or two "correct" ways to play the game. Normal difficulty was also pretty tedious and at times frustrating, and I still scrapped by on easy a few times even though I grinded heavily.

Still, I think the positives of 13 outweigh the negatives, and if you can put up with the gameplay it has a great story to tell.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game from the 2010s era of very questionable PC ports so don't expect much in the way of options, YMMV if it even functions or not without community made tools to fix any issues you may have - I was luckily able to play without any major problems.

That being said FF13 is still a great game that stands the test of time with a great story, characters, visuals, sound track and though it has a lot of non-traditional FF elements to the gameplay it stands on it's own quite well especially if you avoid the habit of auto-selecting battle options and take time to figure out each boss as a puzzle to be solved.

I found the more linear experience to be a benefit to this game and the battles are very well designed that you never have to grind or fight every pack of monsters to stay relevant as long as you use the right strategy (the reason many people struggle is trying to brute force non-functional strategies and dying over and over).

It's not perfect but it hardly deserves all the hate it got in retrospect.
