\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been 11 years since I played this on Xbox 360 and 100%'d it, so does it hold up? Yes it does. It's a little slow to get off the ground, but once it does, it has a somewhat deep and intricate combat system, taking full advantage of dynamic party class changes (Paradigm Shifts) in battle, ensuring you can't just brute force your way through (regardless of what Game Journo mode players online may tell you. Just play on Normal).

Leveling up through Chrystarium and Weapon upgrades does eventually get a little grindy, and weapon upgrades will require a guide to know what you're doing. These won't be too big an issue if you're just looking to experience the main story, but post Endgame content will require the absolute maximum power, so it can be an issue when it comes time to grind turtles.

The cast is solid, and the voice performances are solid in both languages. Everyone's motivation seems real, and although they have their emo moments and occasional teenage angst, they definitely grow on you as they work together to change their fate; eventually finding out the meaning of words like "teamwork" and "friendship."

Overall, one of the best Final Fantasy games, though with a little less of the illusion of an open world that earlier titles had. 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t TL;DR: I like Final Fantasy 13 a lot and I got a lot of enjoyment from the game over the years.

But I recommend it more for either Final Fantasy fans, those who want a different and more linear JRPG experience, or to those who can overlook and forgive the various flaws with the game or with the Square-Enix “approved” PC port... and are maybe willing spend time using mods to fix it.

I recommend it less to first time players who have never played a Final Fantasy game before or to those who are unassuming about the game and don’t know what to expect.

I wish Steam had a mixed option separate from just “recommended” or “not recommended”. I like this game a lot but there are numerous problems within the game and with the PC port itself that could be barriers to entry for some players. But Steam forces me to pick one of them annoyingly and I chose the option that I was suggested to pick from friends based on their opinion of this review.

---------------------------------------------------

This was my first Final Fantasy game and I played this on my Xbox 360 when it first came out in English and like a lot of people who played it I didn’t enjoy it at first and dropped it. But I regretted that and came back years later and found an appreciation for the game for its linearity and for doing things in its own unique way. Most big budget games these days have to be open ended with massive worlds to explore to the point of suffocation, so it’s a genuine breath of fresh air regarding Final Fantasy 13’s linearity. I have replayed this game so many times, more so than most games and I enjoy the story (despite its issues) and the flexibility of the combat especially when it opens up later on. These days a lot of people have come around to FF13 but I feel like I was amongst the first to enjoy its renaissance.

But unfortunately, no matter how much I like and replay this game its quite a struggle to recommend to anyone who doesn’t know what they’re getting into:

  • The story is a mess and could have done with a re-write. Everything is there but its not presented well and additional information is contained within an in-game encyclopedia.

  • Some of the characters are annoying – Snow, and Vanille can get on some people’s nerves. (other characters like Fang and Sazh are good however)

  • Combat is done with menu selection and you cannot freely move your character around.

  • The game is quite linear until very late into the game. It does open up and expand with side-quests and additional features but this one is a deal breaker especially for those who like open-world design.

  • You cannot change or rearrange your party until mid-game.

  • Level progression is time consuming: The amount of Crystarium Points needed to level up stats and unlock moves and the amount of money and materials needed to level up weapons in the mid to late game is exorbitant and really slows down the game. It stifles combat flexibility. You will be spending hours, maybe days and weeks levelling up characters and equipment if you want to get those achievements – truly a completionist’s nightmare.

  • Tutorials still appear more than halfway through the game.

Part of the reason why I struggle to recommend this current version of Final Fantasy 13 on Steam is because this is yet another unfortunate and standard case of a terrible Square Enix PC port. The game crashes infrequently, the textures are not particularly scaled up for PC, the CG cutscenes are compressed, audio mixing during some cutscenes is poor, the settings are less than barebones, and there are a few bugs.

It is difficult to enjoy FF13 without fixing the game via the various mods that the community have made. There are mods that improve the visual quality of the game and character models (including swapping out the compressed CG cutscenes for full HD versions if you want at the cost of sacrificing your storage space), unofficial patches that fix bugs, and a 4GB patch that prevents crashing. I recommend researching these online so you can get a grasp on the improvements made from these mods. (There are also various mods that range from altering the Crystarium to making cosmetic changes to character models if that’s something you want to do).

Square Enix should have made a better port when they released it all those years ago. It shouldn’t have to be the responsibility of the modding community to fix something that S-E couldn’t be bothered with to begin with.

I enjoy this game quite a lot but an unassuming gamer who hasn’t played a Final Fantasy game before and those who don’t know what this game contains would be more dismissive and dismayed over its different design and the plentiful issues with the PC port. But for those who want something different from a JRPG, don’t mind spending time to use mods to fix the game to improve its quality, and are more open minded then I would recommend playing it. Otherwise there are other Final Fantasy games that I would recommend like Final Fantasy 12, Final Fantasy 6, or Final Fantasy 9 instead if you want to start somewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

Characters: Vanille is the only one I really find memorable and the one that has some depth

Story: poor at worst, tolerable at best.

Play only for 13-2 and lightening returns.\t\t\t\t\t
