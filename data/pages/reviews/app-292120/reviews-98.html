\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy favorite final fantasies are VI, IX and XII. If you also like those then this review might be useful to you.
This is the worst final fantasy I've ever played, possibly the worst RPG I've played to completion.
I'm not even going to get into the story, I think it has some problems but nothing too terrible. But this game blows because of how it's structured.
In this game you essentially walk through linear halways, with battles and cutscenes every once in a while. It's like FFX but much worse. This game has no cities, only 1 NPC, and there's nothing else to do besides some side missions that are just more fighting in between a lot of walking. The segment where you can get side missions is also linear, as far as the story goes. You can walk straight through it and not do side missions, but this is probably the most interesting part of the game, and even it is done weirdly. The first time I went through it I did a lot of missions and it was a pain in the ass. There's teleport waypoints you can use but you have to unlock them through missions, and that involves a lot of walking back and forth. There's a lot of times in this game when you're just walking long distances. You can get chocobos, but they're only usable in a small area, and they're very easy to miss (you have to do some specific quests to unlock them, and one of the quest markers is hard to see because it's under something else, I missed the chocobos until very late into the game). Another weird pacing issue is how the game handles items. When you start it gives you a ton of potions and phoenix downs. The potions become useless very fast. In the final dungeon you find the first (and I think only) chest with an elixir, then you find a chest with 5K Gil, at a point in the game where you will probably have over a million. Weapons are also weird, you're supposed to upgrade them but the upgrade system isn't explained well ingame, and it's not a good idea to upgrade stuff until late in the game. I had to use an external guide to not waste resources (after wasting some resources eary), and I'd imagine it's even possible to think upgrading things isn't that important because of how it's glanced over.
The way your character gets stronger is also weird, the crystarium UI is awful and powering up varies from not being too clear how strong you're getting to getting big power boosts after specific segments (I believe this is by design, getting much stronger after some story events, but it does not feel good at all, because I wanted to get bigger power boosts before bosses, not immediately after them).
When I initially wrote this review I said the combat was acceptable, but I think I was suffering from some kind of Stockholm syndrome, because after starting XIII-2, seeing how much better combat in that game is, and thinking a little more about it I realized how bad combat is here. You have too little control over it, and there's too little customization. The most annoying thing is how little control you have over team mates, compared with the gambit system from XII. For example, I frequently just wanted a synergist to cast attack buffs to finish normal encounters fast but he'd cast like 5 defensive buffs on each party member before casting the offensive ones, and there's no way to change this, as far as I know.
The one positive aspect is how good this game looks. That's it. This is like a cake made of just frosting.
Playing through this game was a slog, a miserable experience and I do not recommend it to anyone except completionists who want to go through every final fantasy or a lot of RPGs or something.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I had to use one word to describe this game, it is: Tedious.

I don't even know where to begin so I'll just fire off the issues with this game.

The story is poorly written and not conveyed through the in game cinematics well at all. You have to read the datalog lore pages to get an idea of what's going on and even then it's a bit hazy. The story and its motivations does explain itself later in the game, but I think this is a poor way to go about it. The villains aren't really setup well either. The characters are good though, albeit a bit stereotypical.

The weapon/accessory upgrade system is just plain confusing and there's simply not enough guidance given on how it all works.

You really need to grind CP (level system) to keep up with the enemy difficulty. I find myself needing to respawn already defeated mobs and farm them constantly when I near the end of an area just so my characters remain strong enough to survive the next area.

I like the paradigm system. Although it needed smoother transitions between paradigms mid fight. I also feel like characters should've been specialised into certain roles. If everyone can do everything, none of the characters feel unique in combat.

The camera in this game is just plain awful. Seriously, who designed the camera system. They need a slap in the face, it's terrible. For most the start of the game I was looking up in the sky and I had to hold the camera down key all the time just to see where I was going. It gets better later in the game. But what they've done here.... It ain't it chief.

Get the game when it's cheap. Very cheap.\t\t\t\t\t

Final Fantasy XIII

Final Fantasy XIII is one of the most controversial games in the mainline series, often criticized for its 20 hours tutorial, being one huge hallway and regarding this specific version, the quality of the PC port.

After Final Fantasy X-2 was a success Square Enix started to look towards expanded universes as to reuse assets and an existing fanbase already invested in said universe, with that came the Compilation of Final Fantasy VII, and eventually Fabula Nova Crystallis which would be a common mythos that would be the basis for the Final Fantasy XII Trilogy but also Final Fantasy Type-0 and Final Fantasy Versus XIII turned Final Fantasy XV.

While this game’s early linearity is bad it’s not so different from Final Fantasy X’s linearity, but the one thing that probably accentuates the linearity is the lack of random encounters, while often found to be a sour point in classic Final Fantasy those random encounters add a certain degree of uncertainty which probably helps to alleviate X’s linearity, sadly XII has set encounters, what this means is that enemies always appears and it’s up to the player to decide if they can fight or not when given a choice. This problem is something that would later be fixed in its sequel, XIII-2.

Diverging from classic Final Fantasy in this game you only control one of the three characters in battle and each character has one role active at the time, out of battle you can program 6 paradigm shifts (which are 6 different combinations of 3 roles). There are 6 roles in XII, Sentinel (tank), Medic, Commando (physical damage dealer), Ravager (magic damage dealer), Saboteur (to cast debuffs), Synergist (to cast buffs).

The combat has great depth if you get to Chapter 10 where you are free to change party members/paradigms which happens to be roughly at the same time you get to a semi open world area with different quests and zones to explore.

These quests happen to be the endgame of XII, you will do some of them during your stay in the semi open world area but most of them are to be done after the main storyline. Overall XII’s endgame grind might prove to be more annoying than classic Final Fantasy since the way to obtain each character’s best weapon either requires you to reload your game plenty of times or grind enough gil.

Now for the story and characters, the game tries to develop each character, and early on it splits the characters into little groups so we can see the dynamic between these small groups form and their conflicts, in general I feel like this approach works, but it does feel like Square Enix really developed the early story and characterization well but towards the end there seems to be a general lack of development both in the main party and the villain (s), not that it ruins the game but it definitely felt like more things could have been done while we have the whole party together.

Overall, I usually give this game more credit than maybe the Final Fantasy community at large, while most of their criticisms are warranted the game still ends up being a really enjoyable experience, but given the quality of this port I’d wait for a sale to buy it (and if you have an AMD graphics card, be sure to fully test it before your refund opportunity window runs out).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHello friends,

Welcome to my review of Final Fantasy 13. This game is the first part of a trilogy, featuring this game, Final Fantasy 13-2, and Lightning Returns. Due to this being the start of the trilogy, some people may not comprehend the story of the game. This is like the first chapter of a book. By only reading the later chapters, or in this case, the other games in the trilogy, you will understand the story better. In this game we are introduced to several characters that must do something, or be cursed. The game is quite beautiful and has some nice music too. There are also many side missions, which you will find halfway thru the game most of them. Anyways, here is my pro/con list:

PROS:

- A beautiful game world.
- ATB (Action Time Bar) is back, and some must be open using the skill upgrade on the Crystalieum.
- Features some nice characters that are continued into the next games you can play as in the series.
- Side areas and hidden places.
- Side quests.
- Super bosses and ultimate weapons.

CONS:

- The story can be a bit confusing unless you play the other parts of the game.
- Some people experience lag in the FPS on pc, or crashing. Personally, i did not experience either of these issues with this game when i played it.
- The music is ok, but not that great.
- The game is pretty linear until about halfway thru the game. Do not expect to be able to get back to area's you visited before until halfway thru the game.
- Side quests are halfway thru the game, as above, most of the game is pretty linear, and only starts to get open about halfway thru the game.
- Lots of grinding required for either opening all the Crystaleium or getting the ultimate weapons.

I recommend the game for a good start of a story and nice characters. If you experience issues, it looks like from the posts this tends to happen on newer pcs. My suggestion if you have older pc, try to play this game on it with windows 10 or 7.
