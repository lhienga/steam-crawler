If you want to play this game on PC, I would highly recommend playing from the windows store or via PC game pass to avoid the terrible launcher. I can defiantly agree that the story and the level design were not that great, you spend 20-30 hours of the game just walking from point A to point B, walking down linear pathways with no exploration or interaction in between. Expect for chapter 11 when you finally reach pulse and get to do side missions. Though I have to say that the battle system is pretty satisfying when you finally master it, there's a lot that you have to worry about and nearly all of the jobs were unique and useful in their own way. Might replay this game again to give it another chance someday, but much rather play the other games in the franchise. 6/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, it kinda deserves the fame it got.

FF XIII lacks any redeeming quality that could make it excellent, it's a mediocre game with graphics that were decent for the time, and that to be honest still keep up to date.

However, my praise ends here: the graphics can't carry an art direction that is confused and tries to pull the game in too many directions at once, so you have beautiful enviroment with creatures that feel out of place.

The story tries the in media res trope but fails because unfortunately, you don't have time to get attached to the character it wants you desperately to care about.

The gameplay it's maybe the worst offender: the game feels like a corridor up until late, and when you do reach the point when you can experiment a bit you're already fatigued. The character development suffers from the same plight, a far cry from the freedom of the previous 3 chapters (FFX, FF X-2 and XII).
The main battle gimmick is divisive. You might find it of your liking, but it gets stale pretty quickly, and frankly doesn't allow you to get creative as you will quickly figure out the most optimal way to use the handful of skills you have.

I honestly would only recommend this game for historic purpose, if you really are a FF fan and a completionist, but otherwise there are plenty of better experience already available on pc, like the FFX collection and FF XII.\t\t\t\t\t
