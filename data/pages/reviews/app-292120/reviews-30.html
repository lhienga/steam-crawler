\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried to play this on console release way back when. I then took a break.....and I guess my mom must've moved the disc somewhere so I just couldn't find it.

So here I am years later, an adult with a PC that played every other FF mainline game and decided to buy the PC version to FINALLY play it through!

- I personally enjoy linear "railroad" games, so this was more of a pro for me.
- Story is pretty barebones, but characters are likable enough to make me care about them. I find Vanille and Fang's roles in the story to be the most engaging
- I find the Paradigm system to be super fun, forcing me to switch paradigms on the fly to work through all the different boss mechanics. I REALLY wish we can save loadouts. Thank God XIII-2 has save/load layouts

(LIGHT SPOILER/HEADS UP CAUSE THERE IS A POINT WHERE THE GAME FEELS LIKE IT COMES TO A SUDDEN STOP)
When you reach Chapter 11, you honestly can't just go straight to the next story point. After you beat a very early boss upon entering Chapter 11, you end up in a very large area. At this point, expect a LOT of grinding. The difficulty level on the monsters suddenly jumps up, and if you try to go straight to the next area, you WILL get your butt kicked in. Its a really drastic stop from what you expect of the game, so I want to warning that there is a grindfest expected in Chapter 11

A lot of people really like to hate this game for the sake of hating it. In my opinion its not awful like others like to say. If you don't mind "railroad" games and are a FF fan, play it and develop your own opinion. I understand why some people dislike it, but I do think the hate is a little over-the-top. Do it for Vanille.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3/10. At best.
First, this is a terrible port: framerate drops below 30 at times, with an i5-8400 and a 3060ti. Graphics have woeful aliasing, even if you try to enhance it. Load times are longer than they should be for a game this old.
The battle system is the worst of any FF game I've played yet, and one of the worst of any RPG Ive come across: you can only control the party leader, and your allies make terrible decisions. Each char has 3 of the 6 roles available (you unlock the other 3 at chapter 10, but theyre crazy expensive to progress down). However, you cant choose roles for individual chars; you can only choose presets which have a fixed role for each char, and you dont get enough presets (6?). And remember, (with the exception of healers) your allies will be horribly inefficient at their roles. Due to the progression system, the characters dont have strong combat identities.
You can't choose your party for most of the game. This is mitigated by the lack of character identities, but thats not a good thing.
The only difficulty in battles is generally in winning within the time target (you can win even if you go over it), since healers can keep everyone at full health against pretty much everything. Of course, they dont tell you what the target is until after the battle. When there are difficult battles, theyre mostly binary: get blown up, or win. It never feels like you win or lose based on skill.
The stagger gauge mechanic (enemies take bonus damage if you hit them enough to stagger them, and some enemies lose their defense, and enemies can be perpetually stunned) means that, against strong enemies, your performance in a battle is just about your ability to stagger. That means you play in a very scripted way, and so choices feel mechanical, rather than intelligent.
Character progression is basically forced. Characters only have 3 main stats - hp, strength, and magic. Due to the computer controller chars' stupidity, it's often better to avoid unlocking skills, except that most are necessary nodes in the stupid progression trees.
Eidolon battles are dumb. Theyre not about skill, theyre about knowing which moves work within the battles unique rules.
The weapon progression system is poor, and effectively locks you in to your early choices.
There are stupid names for pretty much everything (l'cie, fal'cie, Pulse, Cocoon). You have no idea what's going on at the start because of this. They could spend 30 seconds explaining what their stupid terms mean, but they don't bother. Once you know what the words mean, it isnt actually much better.
Most chars are uncharasmatic. The good guys are fairly boring, and the bad guys are just quasi-satirical homocidal, and at times close to genocidal, maniacs. (Quasi-satirical because they're not funny.)
Action cutscenes are amazingly terribly directed, with shaky and unnatural camera movements to the point that the scenes are practically unintelligible. The worst Ive seen in any game.
The inevitable plot reveals are just silly and random.
I was trying to play through to the end, hoping it would get good, but I got too bored and frustrated with all the terrible everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying FF13 in 2023: It took more willpower for me to get to the end of this game than it does to get out of bed on a Monday morning.

There's a lot to like; the characters are likeable (more of a Yuna than a Vivi but still fine) and of course they're gorgeous to look at, and beautifully animated. The scenery is wild, untamed, breathtaking at times. The music is fantastic. The story loses a little in translation as FF stories often do, but it doesn't quite feel fully formed. It feels like perhaps a lot was cut.

Maybe it wasn't just the story that needed a trim. Do you like corridors? Enjoy aimlessly running towards event markers? Slapping beasts left and right by mindlessly tapping one button, only to fail spectacularly the moment you try to engage a strategy?

Corridors. Gorgeous yet pointless roads, thousands of wasted steps, some roads leading simply from one event marker to the other, other paths simply to test your resolve. The road is long indeed. And honestly, the journey is *boring*!

The battle system is inventive and for a few hours it's interesting - there's a sweet spot where you learn to time any paradigm (job/class) changes to maximise atb charge, and the forced grind feels fun. But then you realise that there's no need for strategy. The most economical way to take down mobs - particularly bosses - is to employ one strategy for the entire battle, swapping out solely to replenish HP.

It made me wonder why I felt so frustrated with this game, when I've been evangelical about other games that really, when you boil the matter down, are also running between event markers and slapping mobs along the way, and I still don't know exactly why this one felt so different. It's probably because there are few of the usual Final Fantasy distractions, the chocobo subquests in FF7, the card games in 8 and 9, the blitzball and the sort-of-sudoku in 10. You *can* fart about on a chocobo if you like (eventually) but it isn't the same. It's still walking from place to place, just a bit faster.

I've come to expect Final Fantasy games to takeover every free moment while I play them, but I think 13 is best played casually. You can't set aside a long weekend for this - you won't make it. - but an hour or two here and there, a couple of times a week, and it's probably pretty diverting, and that's why I've reluctantly recommended it.\t\t\t\t\t
