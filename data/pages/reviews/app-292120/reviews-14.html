\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played a lot of Final Fantasy games when I was younger, and I absolutely loved them. Recently I got the urge for playing JRPGs again, so I bought and played through FF15. I wasn't really satisfied with it, so I picked up FF13 afterwards for 5€ on sale. Let's see if it fares any better.
Let me start off by saying FF13 was ported to PC pretty badly. It's originally a PS3 game, so character textures and cut-scenes are all done in 720p. Needless to say, that low-resolution rendering looks terrible on PC. There are also various graphical glitches, but luckily FF13 has a strong modding community and everything can be fixed by installing some mods. After downloading and installing over 100GB of mods (HD textures and videos, gameplay fixes etc.), the game looked fine.
Story-wise, this has to be one of the worst Final Fantasy games. I won't spoil anything by going into the details, but let's just say the story is very cringe and doesn't make much sense at times. The atmosphere is also lacking in vibrancy we would expect from a Final Fantasy game. I guess the main reason behind the lacking atmosphere is the linearity - FF13 is incredibly linear and doesn't even feature a world map. From the beginning to the very end of the game, you'll be going from point A to point B, fighting monsters along the way. Another reason responsible for a weak atmosphere is dull world design, which is also something FF fans aren't used to.
Let's talk about mechanics a bit. The character development system is very basic and doesn't offer any real customization. The combat system (ATB) is better than in FF15, but worse than in older FF games. The game is now fully real-time and you'll only have a couple of seconds to issue orders for the next turn. Unfortunately, this practically forces you to use the auto-combat option instead of picking the actual attack or skill. You can only control the main character in combat because of this time pressure, the rest are controlled automatically through the use of roles. Each character has a couple of available roles, which represent a certain playing style (for instance, a character can only cast healing spells if he is currently assigned the role of Medic). You can change role combinations any time while fighting and different role combinations can be configured out of combat. Another thing I don't like is the weapon/accessory upgrade system. Basically, you can inject any loot you get from monsters into your gear. However, you don't know what will be the final result of the upgrade, nor how many materials you'll need in order to accomplish this. So you'll be either forced to look it up on the Internet, or spend countless hours in tinkering and wasting materials.
On the other hand, gameplay of FF13 is pretty good. This still feels like a Final Fantasy game - there are plenty of different enemies, items and secrets to discover. Despite being half-automated, combat is challenging and rewarding. There are plenty of things to grind and I find myself mildly amused in grinding them.

Final Fantasy 13 has a lot of deficiencies, but I consider it a solid JRPG. I guess Final Fantasy games aren't what they used to be and we'll just have to get used to it.

My score: 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal Fantasy 13 is a bit of a mixed bag. First, I'll note that I'm not one to desire playing hundreds of hours on a game just to beat it, but if I love playing it I'll put the extra time in.

This wasn't that game for me.

In short, this game was extremely linear/railroaded with the exception of one major area, and lacked a lot of the charm of other Final Fantasies. Here's my breakdown:

Pros - This game had excellent graphics for its time, and the protagonist (Lightning) was one I really wanted to root for. The combat system is interesting, and there's a story that contains nuance. Others say the soundtrack hits, but it admittedly didn't do it for me. The plot isn't too bad, and it does feature a varied and interesting cast of characters in the party.

Cons - If you're looking for character interaction with the world outside of just the party, this truly lacks it. That said, the biggest con with this game is that the game is extremely linear, and in order to finish it, it requires something of a grind. About Final Fantasy XIII:

Well, I got this game because I've always wanted to play a Final Fantasy game that isn't completely classic nor completely modern, and I felt like FF13 was a good start off. I also have a thing for Final Fantasy's art style, their sometimes visual kei and anime inspired hairstyles, their sometimes bizarre but unique world concept and iconic monsters, and something about Lightning Farron's character design just felt magnetic.You think you know her but you don't, shes hardcore until you realize her weakness, shes a balance of masculine and feminine energy. In the midst of their ordeals, her character development, along with other characters really opens up. The story is not just any typical jrpg theme, its what makes Final Fantasy different from others. Their complex lore unique to their brand only. Oh, and I almost forgot about the music, their soundtrack is unforgettable too.

About combat:

I did not like the combat system in the beginning, I was a bit lost on how it truly works, since this was my first FF game. I had trouble picking which abilities to use and also the ATB fills up so fast before I finish picking, it made me felt auto command was more time efficient, but it was only true for earlier chapters. Later on when I tried to find out why I was killing some monsters so slow, it became apparent that exploiting enemies weakness, smart planning of character roles (paradigm shift), staggering the enemy, buffs and debuffs, and a balance between manually choosing abilities and auto battle commands makes efficient battles. The combat becomes more fast paced later on in the games when you get used to it, and there are more challenging bosses. Also, auto commands are generally used after you already have enemy intel info (which can be obtained after using the technique called Libra), so the AI can be smarter in picking abilities for you. The combat system may not be for everyone, but I learned to adapt and it kept me engaged till the end.

About PC port:

I was skeptical about the PC port as I've read others faced issues with crashing for PC and stuff about Square Enix not having good PC ports for Final Fantasy series in general, but on my windows 10, with 16GB ram, SSD+HDD with lots of space, GTX 970 graphics card, it runs absolutely fine for me with zero crashes, no lagging, even when setting to highest quality. The keyboard and mouse settings feels intuitive too, you wont feel as if it is made only for consoles. So...reviews aren't always reliable I guess.

Overall I enjoyed my FFXIII experience, and I even managed to get 100% acheivement which I didn't think I would. I loved only the art aesthetics first, but then I came to appreciate what it offers as a whole :)\t\t\t\t\t
