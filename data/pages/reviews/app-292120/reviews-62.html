\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though I thoroughly enjoyed this game, and it's a contender for my favorite Final Fantasy game yet, I recognize there are plenty of positives and negatives, at least in my eyes.

Positive:
Beautiful. Absolutely wonderful to look at. Even the different cityscapes all feel unique and interesting, and somehow they managed to make an artificial planet have loads of unique 'ecosystems'.
Music. I'm thinking of one, Sunbath something or other, with Vanille and Sazh . It's got a great main theme, one that I can still hum after not playing the game for years.
Ability System. The crystarium is what really makes this game shine to me. Actually getting to choose which stats and abilities you grind towards makes every fight feel incredibly rewarding, and they took all the right aspects from the FF10 and FF12 systems to make a beautiful, thrilling progression.

Negative:
Combat. I know you don't have to play this as an autobattler, but it really feels like that's the only option. Besides pressing the "Auto Battle" button, the only real input you'll have on your characters is switching between "Paradigms," essentially this game's classes. You're free to ignore the button and input your own attacks and spells, but besides prioritizing certain buffs and debuffs, the game would probably make the same choices fast by just using auto battle.
Story. Be prepared to have no idea what the underlying story is. I had to read every log in the game just to have a grasp of what's going on. FF13-2 helps a bit by fleshing out some of the universe, but most of it you're going to want to just google at the end of your playthrough.

Neutral:
Linearity. There isn't really much choice until almost the very end of the game. When I'm in the mood to 'play' a game but not really think all too much or make decisions, I throw this on and play some of the early chapters. That's a draw for a lot of people, but I also don't always want to stress over decisions like I do in most other RPG's.\t\t\t\t\t
