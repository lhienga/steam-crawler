\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is by no means a bad game per se, but simply does not live up to the Final Fantasy name. This fact is well reflected both here and in metacritic ratings as being by far the least popular mainline FF game.

Pros:
Gorgeous graphics - Playing at 4k, I definitely found myself just staring at the screen from time to time
Excellent music - One of the better soundtracks of recent FF games, even if it can't live up to some of the classics
Combat system - The paradigm system is quite unique and takes some getting used to, but I found it quite fun and engrossing

Cons:
Combat system - There's a somewhat steep learning curve, and the game designers decided to handle this by making the first 20 hours of gameplay a tutorial. *facepalm*
Uninspiring story - The story isn't bad, but falls far short of what is expected for a Final Fantasy game. It doesn't help that the story is half buried in the datalog.
Bland characters - Their personalities range from bland to cringe-worthy. It's a shame because the character designs are awesome, especially Lightning
Buggy - I got a lot of random crashes at 4k, and the enemy datalog entries are bugged above 720p (see https://steamcommunity.com/app/292120/discussions/1/606068060836189580/). I usually don't like to harp on technical issues, but for a game where shininess is the major appeal this is a big problem.


A lot of reviews will mention linearity as a problem. Linearity by itself isn't an issue; FFX is quite linear and is one of my favorites. The problem is that the linearity in this game is very poorly disguised and there's none of the expected payoffs in terms of character or story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe main plot of this game is about deciding whether or not to kill an orphan. Ethics, morals, destiny, how to cope, and a bunch of other somewhat interesting themes are explored. A bit edgelord at times and a bit campy at other times. The story is rather hit or miss. It's serviceable IMO.

I've played this game before on the xbox 360 and forced myself to 100% it in about 2 weeks since I borrowed it from a friend. I was rather meh about it back then and the feeling is even worse now since I still kinda remember the story. Oddly enough, I probably had more fun with it back then since I didn't know how any of the systems worked and stumbled my way through the entire game but after taking a closer look at the game now, there are a lot of flaws in it.

It took a few tries for me to beat the game because I kept getting bored and stopping for a long time only to restart again. The problem is the pacing of the game is rather slow. U essentially fight a few enemies, get a cut scene, walk a little, cut scene, fight, and cut scene. The game is rather corridor-like but I will give the devs one thing. They are very pretty corridors. I'd say that's about 90% of the main story content but only about a third of the game. It doesn't help that the combat is very automated. There is quite a bit of strategic depth to the combat and a few hidden ways u can micromanage things but overall it's not the most engaging. There's also the problem that the AI used in combat isn't always the smartest and u don't have much input with anything they do outside of micromanaging the party leader. U essentially have to play around the fact that the ai is an idiot and learn how to indirectly influence it. U can't even directly control ur party's positioning and if u want to deal with it u have to figure out a bunch of really dumb ways to manipulate the ai into moving into positions u want, like how certain characters jump back while doing certain attacks. Luckily, in most situations it doesn't matter too much and u can coax through ignoring it but it does hurt at times. The strange thing is that by playing unoptimally and not min-maxing the game I actually did better in my 1st play through than the second one in terms of score. In my 1st playthrough all those years ago, I never upgraded my weapons or accessories, care about equipment stats or anything. I just threw on whatever I happened to have. In my current one I did all that and it turns out the difficulty of score scales with how high ur damage stat is. Eventually u will outscale and 5 star again but before that u'll always be stuck in that awkward in-between where u're powerful but not overpowered enough for a 5 star. Luckily, the only thing u really lose by not getting a 5 star is time (U actually get less Technique points, less chance of drops, etc), especially if u're planning to 100% the game.

Eventually, the game opens up into an open world. Boy oh boy does it sometimes feel like the devs suffocated us to the point where we begged them to give us freedom, only for them to suffocate us with a massive world that u have to spend a good chunk of time walking around in. By massive, I mean massive in terms of how much time it takes to traverse. It's not that big in compared to today's open world games or even open world games that were around back then. There are things like Chocbos and teleport stones that cut down the travelling a bit but they're super limited in number and scope. U will spend about 2/3s of the game in this open world just taking quests that boil down to travel to place and kill monster. There's a hell load of uninteresting backtracking to do with these quests. Most of the rewards kinda suck too. I remember the first time I played the game I did enjoy the exploration a little bit but I did feel like it was rather limited. At the same time, I was thankful the game wasn't any bigger due to how much back tracking there was. Outside of the fact that I didn't enjoy exploration as much my 2nd go around (for obvious reasons), my feelings remain the same about exploration.

In the end, I just got tired of trying to force myself to 100% the game since I just wasn't enjoying it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I rage quit this game back in 2009 on 360, even with me being a hugeeeeee Final Fantasy fan. However, I waited until I finished this game to write this review. I'll sum it up in a few points:

1. Graphics, music, story and art are top tier as to be expected, as far as the time was concerned.

2. The relationships in the game start off really shaky and brass, but oh my GOD do they give some thicc and juicy character development that I loved. With there only being six playable characters, this are really tight knit and I loved everyone.

3. The last three chapters are ungodly grindy at times but once you can expand your roles to the almost max, you get a lot of cool stuff that makes it worth it.

4. I cried at the ending per usual. Honestly didn't know what to expect but I just found myself bawling lmao.

10/10, one of my favorite Final Fantasy games at this point. Now time to tackle the two sequels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing. As you can see, I've clocked over 180 hours in this game. In the review section, i see quite a lot of "do not recommend", but to me, i really feel that there isn't anything to complain about for this game.

1) The Story Line.
- Storyline is generally linear (except for the side quests) and could be hard to understand initially as there are many new terms or rather, entities in the world and the development is rather foggy, but as you progress through the story, it will reveal the many aspect of the characters and the relationship between each entities in the world. This created a very interesting story different from any simplified fantasy world other games can offer.

2) Graphic
- The graphic in this game and the cinematic scenes are so damn gorgeous, especially if you know that this game comes out for quite some time already, and they are even consider to be really good in 2021 era.

3) Characters
- The character development and their backstory are very well done. And you get to play each character and allow you to customize which character you would like to play with. As the story progress, you will develop attachment to the characters which were brought forward to the sequel of Final Fantasy 13-2 and Lightning Return.
- Of course, for me, the heroine - Lightning, is my favourite character of the entire Final Fantasy 13 series.

4) Battle System
- Many people don't really like the battle system for some reason, but I totally love it. You are able to customize and strategize with the battle system with the characters you like to use. The many paradigms also allow for flexible and fun battle.

5) Side Quests
- Unlike the Main Quest, side quests can be massive and really showcase the massive open world that this game has to offer. There are some side quests with mini boss and major boss which link further complement and complete the storyline from main quest even more. To be frank, some quests could be tedious or even difficult to complete at times if without some grinding, but they allow players to enjoy the game even after the completion of main story.

6) Achievement
- In order to complete every achievement trophies, i would say that significant time investment will need to be put in to grind and upgrade the characters and weapon. which is a very tedious process.

7) Music
- FF13 OST music are incredibly out of this world good. Once you immerse yourself in the game and the music, it makes the game so complete, and the music is so amazing that you will never forget them.
- I decided to replay the whole FF13 series again a few times just because i happened to listen to the OST somewhere and it makes me so nostalgic that i just had to.

Overall, i feel that this game is very worth it for only $20+, even more so if it is on discount sales. I don't see any reason NOT to pick up this game.\t\t\t\t\t
