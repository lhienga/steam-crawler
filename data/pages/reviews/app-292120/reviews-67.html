\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor all the love i have for FF, this game is frustrating for a few main reasons. These shouldn't be a deal breaker, but it's a death by a thousand cuts. All these small things make up a somewhat frustrating experience.

First is not being allowed to control your character's movement in combat. Whoever thought this was a great idea should have been fired before it was put in the game. The number of times where you will get hit with an aoe attack is outrageous and there's nothing you can do about it.

Second is the paradigm (combat styles) system. The system is cool because you let your characters develop specific jobs and can switch them mid combat. The downside is there's a big animation the first time you do it for every fight. It eats up combat time and allows the enemies to keep attacking you. Again, whoever thought this was okay should have been fired before it was put in the game.

My last major complaint is the heavy hand that forces you to follow a path with little to no exploration. It took me just under 23 hours to get to the first area of free exploration.

There's also smaller complaints like lack of NPCs you can interact with, all shops are now combined to the floating save points scattered around the world. Save points are very infrequent. Port quality is a mixture of good and bad stuttering.

In the end I'm going to try and finish the game. But to those fans of the FF franchise I can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis ♥♥♥♥ is so boring.

Maybe I didn't reach the point of the gameplay becoming interesting, but I really doubt it.

The formula for delivering the story is tedious. The party kinda stands around and decides to split up, you control one group and fight some enemies in an area. After like two or three fights, you stop and talk to the other party member and they prompt some kind of emotional response for later. Then you fight some more enemies, talk again, and repeat. At the arbitrary end of an area you watch a cutscene from before the events of the main story and it either reveals a secret or explains some ♥♥♥♥ one of the characters said. This is used in every single area without fail and the most fun I had was predicting when the Bodhum cutscenes would happen lol.

The dialogue has weird delivery and doesn't really help the already stale characters. Even though I'm not a fan of this game, I thought I'd end up liking at least one character, but that is not the case. I like Fang I guess but I don't know anything about her so maybe that's why. Snow, Hope, Lightning, Vanille and Sazh are all just meh to me. Some of these characters are clearly intended to be disliked at the beginning, but I have no interest in seeing them grow, if they even do grow. Vanille is one of the most annoying characters I've ever had the misfortune of playing as. She butts into every single scene she's in and the line delivery by her voice actress is not doing her any favors. She's also clearly a really important character for the plot but that just makes me want to play less because I know she's going to be around the whole game.

Overall, I wouldn't recommend this game to any JRPG fan because there's just an infinite amount of better games to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been holding back on this one for a long time, thanks to people saying it's bad. The truth is, it's a good JRPG and worthy of carrying the Final Fantasy name.

A side note on Steam version though, I played using the FF13 fix patch and the 4gb memory patch and had no issues at all with the game. I didn't try without the patches so I don't know if it does have issues.

The visual is great for a PS3 game, the story is decent, the characters are lovable (although a bit corny sometimes), the cutscenes (the reason we all used to play FF games since PS1) are great.

The combat system is NOT turn based though. IMO this is the game that makes better use of the ATB system. Changing formation during battle (Paradigm system) makes it strategic and fun. You can develop the character's build the way you want, using the Crystarium (which is just a skill tree system).
There are some caveats about combat/character build that I wish the game would explain better, things that you only figure after your first playthrough but nothing that would make it impossible to beat the game. If you mess up, you can just grind a bit more and get more CP (the points used to unlock abilities) to fix your build.
All in all, I found really cool how customizable it is.

I think the reason why people complain about the corridor maps is because we don't get a world map in FF13 and rather just go from place A to place B, but the places we explore doesn't feel different from older entries in regards to it's shape. In some cases, thanks to the good visuals, the places resemble a lot of FF7R even having a 10 years difference.

I was searching for a JRPG with good story, characters and cutscenes (I love them), and I found it in FF13. It was enough to make me want to play FF13-2 and see what comes next. The game deserves a far better reputation than it has.

My suggestion is: try it without asking yourself if it's good or bad, just have a good time playing the game for what it is or put it back/return if you find it's not worth your time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t• Not as bad as people make it out the be, but still pretty freakin bad.

• Having played nearly all the Final Fantasy games, I came into this one with low expectations and an open-mind. Still ended up disliking it the longer I played.

• PROS: Fun combat (gets extremely repetitive though), gorgeous visuals, high production quality, (most) character designs, and an outstanding musical score.

• CONS: Only to be overshadowed by horrible scene/scenario writing, character development, and overall creative direction. Walking straight and fighting gets old quickly. When the world opens up, it's too little too late. Speaking on the games linearity, if you're going to take away an open-world option for the players to deliver a well-paced, tightly-woven story, then that story better deliver. Unfortunately, it's linear AND the story disappoints. So...huh?

• This Motomu Toriyama (game director) is....something else.

• Glad I played through it once for a full Final Fantasy series completion. Will probably never play again though.\t\t\t\t\t
