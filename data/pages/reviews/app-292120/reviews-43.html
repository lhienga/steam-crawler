\r\n\t\t\t\t\tafter a long (!) time i recently purchased FF13 for PC (steam sale)
now FF13 is a fantastic game, Final Fantasy entry and jrpg, but the PC port got a bad rep.
from my point of view the game looks (still) absolutly stunning, especialy running on a modern rig, high resolution and higher FPS (pretty stable 60 on my system); the game was already a looker back at it's original release, but on a modern system? brilliant...especialy for a game this "old". i had 0 crashes, and only a few instances with frame drops (but i'm running the highest possible AA and shadow settings), playing on a i9, 3070 and 64, with the game stored on a HDD. loading times, performance and quality: i'm more than happy. but with the amount of people complaining about issues it's still something to keep in mind (nevermind the fact that it's Square is Ehnix doing the porting).

the game itself is somehow slightly controversial? while i'm not realy a jrpg fan and after 9 no FF catched my interest, i still understood that some where not happe with 13. i'm quite the opposite. 13 might be my favorite in the series only topped by 7 (the original not the remake).
is more linear than other entries? for sure, but that also allows for more stunning set pieces, tighter design (looking at you 12) and better synergie between plot and gameplay. it's probably one of the most choreographed FFs out there, but it's not less, but actualy unique and very engaging because of it. it's a real "adventure" or journey, not some open-world-checkmark fest. you get still plenty of freedom, opern areas, side content and areas to explore. the opening hours are more like a hughe introduction (which is needed).
the combat is probably where to the game got the most flag (and some progression mechanics); but what initialy starts as "boring" as 12s combat was throughout the whole game, quickly turns into a very fast paced (probably the most action-oriented FF bar the remake), yet still very tactical combat. once the game gets going (which honestly doesn't take that long) you can just feel the work and thought put into this very unique combat approach. it feels tight, fluid, responsive and adaptive. it's just a joy to grind, but also to fight more complex encounters.
same goes for the progression mechanics: starting out you see 1 accessory slot and 1 weapon slot, but every mechanic in 13 synergizes with everything else, even the plot. throughout your "sphere-board" (kinda) you get access to everything: new equipment slots, new abilities, stat upgrades, etc. . the progression of the "leveling system" is also tied to story, but in a way that makes sense: when the party (or individual members) tackly obstacles, like major plot points, enemies or personal issues, they grow stronger.
what i'm still not the biggest fan of is the upgrading system for gear. yeah, it works, but it feels unneccessary and convelotued it's not a major issue in any way shape or form, and sorta rewards grinding (which is a good thing), but i'm not the biggest fan.
also the fact that your party gets wiped once your controlled MC dies in a fight is still strange to me. it's also never a major (or minor) issue, but just a strange design choice.

now all this is already pretty great and well worth your time, but what makes 13 standout and one of my favorites in the series is the plot, characters, audio-visual-design and general production quality. when i first played 13 i was reminded of back then when 7 was originaly released on PSX. everything just fits together and goes to show you how it feels when professionals design a game (with enough budget).
Sazh is probably my favorite example (black dude with the bird): what initial looked like a token-black-character and not a good one mind you, is probably one of the best characters in the franchise. he's mature, his voice actors nails all the lines, he's unqiue and realy stands out even when compared to characters like Barret.
other characters will need to grow on you, but grow they do. it's their interactions both with each other and the rest of the world that makes FF13 and it's general presentation so unqiue. it's hard to describe, but where other games do this stuff very game, it feels very natural here. there's still the usual anime/jrpg cringe, but it's offset by brilliant design and acting.
the OST is another example. fitting a sci-fi-adventure-ride, many of it's track are just incredibly unqiue. electronic beats, mixed with more classical compositions and even chorus mixed in. same goes for the art. the world art is stunning even by today's standarts and so is the character design and ingame presentation.

FF13 is an incredible game, even more so on PC (even if you suffer some of the port-issues, there are mods out there), and in my book one of the best and most unique FFs out there.

PS: just a basic, spoiler-free tipp: in combat, after 2 "turns" (two full atb-segment-gauges) are done, switch roles to get a turn for free (this instantly refills your atb-gauge-segments to full). you can time this even better to start shifting during the execution phase of the last 2 atb-segments in "turn" 2, but that's a tad complicated and overkill in most instances.
also don't worry to much about weapon upgrading early on. basicaly chapters 1-8/9 are the tutorial/beginning, even if it doesn't look like it.\t\t\t\t\t
