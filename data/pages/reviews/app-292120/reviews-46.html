\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnnecessarily hated by so-called "fans" of the FF series as a whole, but honestly? Not a bad game at all.

Not *perfect*, mind. For a game that spends the first more-than-half of its story actively discouraging grinding, the latter half to third of the game is all about getting that grind on, because you WILL want to have as much of your equipment and levels maxxed as you can for the final gauntlet of minibosses. Combine that with a few minor quality of life issues -- battles with forced party changes that won't let you customize your paradigms until after you've lost the battle, but give you random paradigms when they force the change that may or may not make good use of your team members' abilities, f'rinstance -- and you've got a game that starts strong, but that's gameplay flaws really start to show once you really dig your teeth in.

That said, the game is a beaut. Great visuals for its original time of release (on 360/PS3,) a FANTASTIC soundtrack that's honestly one of my favorites in the series, and probably the only heavily science-fantasy FF game that I think really does a good job with the aesthetic.

Give it a shot, and judge it for what it is, not what it isn't. As a JRPG it's a good, if flawed, time, with a lot of creativity in its design.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCharacters: Cringe
Story: Boring
Gameplay: Restrictive
Performance: Horrid
Positives: Fang

Horribly restrictive game forcing characters into paradigm roles which could be interesting but half the time it is too slow and annoying to utilise effectively which is not a restriction of the prior games. I appreciate they tried to make something different but this was a miss.

The game runs like ass on PC. My specs are not terrible and this is the only game I seem to have issues with and sometimes it just does not even load so you have to verify the integrity of the files just for it to load again.

The AI is terrible. Not being able to move your characters makes encounters a coin toss. You are hoping they don't decide to stand next to the Sentinel getting cleaved by the big enemies forcing a reset because you cant really move them outside of setting the Sentinel to Commando first to get close and pray your Ravager decides not to run in.

People complain about the "Corridors" which describe the gameplay and map design of the chapters. I do not see this as an issue. There is plenty of room to explore in these restricted regions. and then late game they slap you into a MASSIVE zone that is so much pain to get around at first and really slows the pace of the game. The last 3 chapters is the same length of the first 9 I swear.

Closing gripes: Weapon upgrade systems are weird and limited through the game, boss scaling is all over the places, music is fantastic, Gil is very limited, Fighting Eidolons is just plain stupid, and finally, Do not recommend.

Fang supremacy.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, the hate for this game is undeserved. The game is pretty fun. You just have to accept that it's a linear, level based JRPG. That was a pretty out there concept at the time and I think it threw a lot of people. But, it actually kind of works. The combat system gets good at Chapter 3 when the Paradigm System is revealed. The combat system is really about managing your Paradigm (see Job) composition and switching them up as needed to win the battles, rather than moment-to-moment tactics. Kind of like the Gambit system in FFXII. I played this back on the PS3 back in 2009. The steam release has been stable so far, but the PS4 controller layout is scuffed since it's made for a Xbox Controller. Also, you might want to mod in some HD character models. Still, it's pretty fun overall and it seems to go on sale often.\t\t\t\t\t
