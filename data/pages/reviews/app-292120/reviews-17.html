\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played through this game twice back in 2009. At the time I really didn't like it, and over the years I dunked on it quite a lot. However the past year or so I wanted to give the game another chance, and so the past few weeks I finally got around to it

After replaying it I can say this is prolly in my top 5 Final Fantasy's of all time. I, and the majority of folks since its release; have been extremely unfair to it. I streamed my playthrough along with some friends and we all really enjoyed seeing the game again, or others who hadn't seen it before at all enjoyed seeing it for the first time.

The game's battle system really stands out as one of my favorite battle systems I've ever gotten to mess with in a JRPG. The Paradigm System and the Chain Gauge really make battles feel involved and the skill expression comes from actually switching Paradigms and building up the Chain Gauge to stagger enemies rather than just spamming your highest damage moves. The game's OST's always been a favorite of mine regardless of my feelings towards the game itself. And I was able to appreciate the story and characters way more this time around as well, loving the whole cast.

There are some parts of the game I still don't like such as Gran Pulse, and late game Crystarium farming (some of the nodes become ludicrously expensive), and the final area kinda padding out the time before you can fight the final boss. Honestly just use a trainer or mod or something to generate CP and Gil if need be when you get to the end. You'll thank me afterwards--

I will point out and this is no fault of the game itself but the horrible job done porting it; the game can crash and it can get very intrusive. It makes it hard to recommend, but if you can work around it then I absolutely recommend give 13 a whirl again if you haven't played it since release, or were on the fence about trying it

Overall very happy to have given the game another chance. I really enjoyed it as did my friends and viewers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a lot better than people make it out to be. It's definitely different than any other FF game that came before it, but it still retains a lot of the same things that make the series great. If you're on the fence about whether or not to play it, I'll just list a few things that you might want to know before making a decision (spoiler free).

The gameplay is not as braindead as everyone will have you believe. It actually gets pretty interesting by the time you reach the late game chapters... but that's the biggest issue, in my opinion. It takes for too long for the combat system to get to the part where you're having fun and thinking through your decisions, and not just pressing auto battle for every fight. Keep that in mind if you end up buying the game and think the gameplay is too boring. It DOES open up... eventually.

The story is alright. I wouldn't it rank it among the best in the series, but it's not awful or boring. Personally, I didn't spend too much time delving into the lore (there's a glossary you can access that explains the different terms and names you come across in the story), because it just didn't hook me that much, but there is definitely a wider story than the game gives you upfront if you're willing to spend time reading about it. All in all, though, it's pretty much what you would expect out of a Final Fantasy game.

The characters are decent. I think Vanille and Fang were definitely the highlight of the game (runner up Sazh), with the rest being whatever. The character designs and the world setting in general does look very cool. The aesthetic is what made me want to play this game to begin with, so if that's also your thing then you'll probably like it too.

tl;dr - if you really like Final Fantasy games and haven't played this one then I would definitely recommend it, story is alright, visually looks very cool, the gameplay is fun but it won't open up until around Chapter 9 or so. Vanille and Sazh will make you smile irl (:

Also, download the FF13 Fix mod because the game runs like ♥♥♥♥ if you don't.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
+ Beautiful graphics even to this day.

+ Good JP VA (can't speak for ENG VA but I think it's also good after watching some videos).

+ Combat system is fun, fast and refreshing. I wish more games copied it.

+ Main character (Lightning) is good but there's a huge problem with her situation... We'll talk about it later.

+ Good soundtrack, nothing that would be called "S Tier" but still good on its own.

+ Interesting world and lore, but...

Cons:
- The fact that you can't explore the world AND you have to read info about it in a ♥♥♥♥♥ datalog it's laughable. This is a JRPG, we WANT exposure to the world and lore through cutscenes or in-game dialogue, but another problem comes to show...

- The lack of any town or inn (at least to the point where I played, chapter 5) shows us that extreme linearity in a JRPG DOESN'T work. Yeah yeah, FFX was linear af as well (and there's nothing wrong with it, imo) but the fact that the game organically introduces the player to the world and lore it's what every JRPG should aspire to (also, had minigames and sidequests, in this game there's only corridors and combat, nothing else).

- The character cast is... unsuferable, except for Sazh. The rest of it, which is composed by: Snow, a 30 something guy who still behaves like a teen, jeopardizing the team and his friends many times. Vanille, eye candy for PDF Files. And last but not least, Hope, basically kid Tidus all over again but worse. When I talked about how good Lightning was as a main character but wasn't in a good situation, this is what I was referring to. In a better cast, she could kill it, but thanks to the writers wanting her and Sazh to be basically babysitters makes me wish for a story where just the two of them are protagonists.

Summary: Basically, the game isn't terrible, not even bad, but for JRPG standards it indeed is. Checked all the "don't do" boxes and shot itself in the foot. What a pity, this could have been much MUCH better if it wasn't for so many dumb decisions. Overall, I only enjoyed the combat system but not the other things that SHOULD make a JRPG, a JRPG.

Final score: 4/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI initially played FINAL FANTASY XIII back on release day in 2010; fully completing it once before not touching it again, and harboring a multitude of thoughts and feelings towards it. Now, thirteen years later, I finished my 2nd playthrough of this title. And it is through age and maturity that I bring my views and opinions from my retrospective look at this game.

The question of "what is a FINAL FANTASY game" cropped up again this year with the release of the latest mainline title, and what a "true" FINAL FANTASY game is. There are many elements others will gravitate towards, yet the common thread I arrive at is this; how the world relates to the characters, and how those characters relate to that world.

The central focus of the game is about fighting back against those that would force you down a preordained path. This can be summed up with an iconic quote from the game; "It's not a question of can or can't. There are some things in life you just do". Unfortunately some key information regarding certain story beats are omitted from cutscenes and general gameplay, but can be found in the Datalog in the in-game menu to read through if you're a lore-nut!

And weaving through this theme are the individual stories of the unlikely cast of characters that we come to get to know and grow towards & with along their journey. My favourite dynamic between characters is the one present between Vanille & Sazh. As Sazh is the oldest party member & a father, pairing him with the lively young Vanille makes one a great foil for the other. Their character relationship is also on display during gameplay, as several abilities and Paradigm Shifts will produce voice lines specifically targeted towards characters.

I did not enjoy most of the cast back when I first played it, and I just did not know why they didn't make a positive lasting impression on me. Granted, this was before I knew of the opinions of others. And to be fair, the game does indeed make it difficult to like most of them at the beginning. You have the over-protective older sister, the loud & stubborn big man, the plucky young girl, the older father-figure as your comic relief, the indecisive young boy, and the hard-headed feisty woman. Though, I'm happy to say that in my most recent playthough, I have grown to appreciate them more; and would certainly rate them high as a collective. I loved Oerba Yun Fang back in the day, but Sazh Katzroy solidified himself for my top spot; given his character development, quality voice acting, and not to mention being the father-figure and comic relief of the group. The antagonists also grew on me, in Jihl Nabaat, Yaag Rosch, and Galenth Dysley; understanding them more than I initially did way back when.

The iteration of the ATB system featured in this game is a natural evolution of the battle system found in the series up until this point. The character you play as will be designated as 'Leader', and the other two slots will be occupied by the party members. If the Leader is KO'd, then that's game over! So strategy plays a big part there. The battles are aesthetically pleasing, alongside the menus. There is a welcomed "Auto-Attack" option, which is great for going through enemy encounters that are similar and lower than your stats; however, you will need to actually make use of character-specific abilities yourself if you are looking to efficiently & appropriately tackle various encounters. For example, the Auto-Battle command will cycle through every defensive buff onto all party members *before* applying Enfire to a character for them to hit an elemental weakness to fire during their attacks, when doing so manually would allow you to freely select the spells and abilities to deal with encounters more strategically.

One of the best additions and features of this battle system is the Paradigm System. This allows you to switch the roles of your party mid-battle to deal with various situations as you see fit. You are locked to a maximum of 6 builds. These are initially set, but as the game opens up, you will eventually be given the freedom to create and modify your Paradigms as you see fit across your party; which feature a growth-tree in the Crystarium, though more linear than a Sphere Grid, but that's not an issue. The roles that you make & mix your Paradigms with are this game's version of the Job System; with Commando for damage, Ravager for increasing the enemy Stagger Gauge, Medic for general healing, Sentinel for defense, Saboteur for debuffs, and Synergist for buffs. There are characters that excel in certain roles, so it's best not to sleep on them when given the chance! For example, Sazh gets Haste early as a Synergist; which is a must. Snow, as a Sentinel, draws the ire of enemies to specifically target him; so you are free with the other two characters to do whatever. Vanille's debuffing as a Saboteur is invaluable. And Hope as a Medic is phenomenal. There's a lot of fun to be had in mixing up your Paradigms, and experimenting with this stuff to see what works.

To this day, FINAL FANTASY XIII has some of the most beautiful visuals; which is a testament for the time it was produced. And while the scenery is nice to look at, some of the corridor sections do feel rather lengthy and monotonous; broken up with an encounter, before making the next turn. Linearity isn't the issue, but repetitive design is. The Gapra Whitewood, and The Palamecia for example.

The soundtrack is one of the best the series has ever produced, and every track finds their proper place in every scene and zone. The battle theme "Blinded By Light" & boss theme "Saber's Edge" are incredible, and stand with many of the iconic tracks in the series.

As for side content, there are various notorious monsters that you can hunt for rewards; which becomes accessible in the later part of the game. Difficulty varies across these challenging foes, so plan accordingly!

And also; performance-wise, you'd best install the ff13fix mod. Trust me, it's like a different game!

After my retrospective look at FINAL FANTASY XIII, I feel like I came to truly enjoy the game for what it was. I was reminded of the great narrative and characters that this game has to offer; of the amazingly immersive and reactive iteration of the battle system, and fantastic boss fight designs. And gorgeous visuals, coupled with the beautiful soundtrack to accompany it. Of course, there is a distinct lack of side content, but the game pushes itself first and foremost as a narrative to be experienced; which is something I appreciate. Not everything is black and white, and the game deserves a better reception from the fanbase; but at the end of the day, FINAL FANTASY XIII is a solid experience and should be enjoyed as such.\t\t\t\t\t
