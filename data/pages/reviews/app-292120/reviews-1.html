\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me preface this by saying - this is an excellent game. Strong story, great characters. Yes, it's more linear compared to past FF titles but that does not detract from enjoyment.

The reason I don't recommend this game is because it's an awful port. It's highly prone to crashes, poorly optimized for PC, and a whole range of issues that will make you feel like you got ripped off even if you received the game for free. There are several fixes that the community has provided which make the game playable but I do not feel that is a standard SquareEnix should be held to. So if you're willing to grab the community-made patches, the game is perfectly playable and very enjoyable. But otherwise, don't enable SquareEnix's lazy porting behavior.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it does have a couple of redeeming qualities, they all get buried under a long list of issues and poor design choices. I really wanted to like this game, but after 40 hours I 100% agree with the poor reviews.

World and Story

The world itself is great; futuristic with unique designs and ideas. The characters, however, are not great, some are okay, while others are just horrible (Vanille). The way they are presented just makes it really hard to relate to them. The story is decent on paper, but how it is told in-game is just confusing and hard to understand. I feel like the story is a reflection of how the game's developers felt working on it: they were lost and hopeless with no clear direction.

Gameplay and Pacing

The pacing of the game is probably the worst part: the first 13 hours are a tutorial with a huge info dumps into the story and characters with "mash X button" gameplay and long straight corridors. I guess it is meant to invest you into the story, but does a poor job of it due to the confusing story-telling. This mind-numbing section leaves players a bad impression of the unique combat system, which does have a few redeeming qualities.

The next section, about 13-25 hours in, is probably the best part about the game. It focuses on teaching the paradigm system, giving the player fixed party composition and fights that are designed to introduce the player into the advanced combat mechanics. The levels open up a bit, instead of long straight corridors you sometimes get branching paths and hidden items, but it unnecessarily long, and often you just wait for each section to be over already.

The last part of the game, about 26 hours in, is like playing a different game. You get thrown into a huge open area, where 99% of the encounters literally 1-shot your team. This is where the gameplay goes from "I need to think what's a good paradigm for this fight" to ""oh, you didnt have the perfect setup of 6 paradigms and characters for this fight? Too bad, you die". You will die, a lot. This is also where the issues with the combat system begin to show. Basically, every encounter has specific paradigms setups you are supposed to use. If you use the wrong paradigms, you will either A) die instantly B) fight he enemies for 7-10 minutes and win C) fight he enemies for 7-10 minutes, then die instantly to a mechanic that you have no idea about. Since you cannot chance padargims of characters mid-battle and there's a only 6 paradigms setups at a time, this results in gameplay where you basically "need to die" to change to the correct setups, which is not great. In short, instead of playing the game the way you want with the characters you like (the point of any RPG) you have pre-determined setups you are SUPPOSED to use, but no way of knowing what they are beforehand.

Difficulty

All these issues regarding the combat system are far too many to list here, but another major issue that stands out is the difficulty curve. The first section of the game is mind-numbingly boring, the second is decently challenging, the third is nuclear difficulty. In the third section, once you near the end of the game you catch up in levels and the fights become doable, but then towards the ending normal mob fights become nuclear difficulty again, unless you have grinded 30 extra hours to max all your characters. Additionally, the difficulty of encounters (regular and boss fights) varies greatly. This may be a "feature" of the PS3 era of games, but it was just painful seeing your player character get 1-shot with no way to resurrect them, ending in game over.

Conclusion

In short, the game has an interesting world, decent characters and an (on paper) good strategic combat system, that all gets buried under a mountain of issues ranging from development struggles to, poor gameplay design to technical issues on PC. I dont think even a full remake can save this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I am not terribly far in and I will continue playing, but this really was one of the biggest mainline Final Fantasy mistakes of all time. Everything that everyone says is spot on. Story, characters, dialog, battles, "exploration", the flow, the leveling system - all are some of the worst this series has ever seen. The game is running fine on Steam Deck minus a few very small frame drops here and there so that's nice. There is a bit more in common with FFX and FFXIII than there is with FFX and FFXII so maybe some FFXII haters could find something to enjoy here, but X is so much more gripping and rewarding. Many RPGs could just rip the exact same combat system out of X and put it into their game and have good results. XIII...not so much. There are a number of tutorial battles that are trial and error segments with poorly worded instructions. There is no chance this will be turned into a positive review, but I will continue playing until I give up on it entirely or I force myself to play through every FF game in existence. One positive of this game is it sucked so bad it got people to start discovering Tri-Ace, Atlus, Vanillaware, and Level 5 who have pumped out far better and more interesting games on a much lower budget. Square-Enix is still struggling to recover from the stain this game put on their reputation and it's hilarious to watch. Listen to your fans. Your actual fans that have supported you since long, long ago. This is the exact opposite of what we want.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really really like this game. The game still holds up really will visually (with help from some mods), the music is sublime, the story is good, the characters all end up really likeable (with Sazh being the stand out, and right now my favourite FF character) and the gameplay is fun.

However there are some negatives, the missions on Gran Pulse start to feel like a chore, running back and forth all the time. Thankfully you can teleport to different spots, and get a Chocobo fairly early on, but you can't call for the Chocobo, nor will it stay when you enter battles, so it's a double edged sword.

The worst part about this game is this port, even with the fan patches, I don't think I've ever played a game this unstable (not looking forward to XIII-2 for this alone). Constant crashes, and it only got worse the further in the game you got, being the absolute worst during Chapters 8-10, with random crashes here and there throughout.

I still really like the game, and the poor ass port doesn't detract how I feel overall about the game, still had a great time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts the worst part of the series, its even worse than Final Fantasy Mystic Quest from the SNES
The battle system is boring, you can only control 1 character the rest acts on their own and you cant even influence what they should do.
90% of the game you just walk through enemy infested boring levels almost always straight forward.
You are limited in how strong you can get by the chapter you are in since you only get more "levels" after you have beaten the last boss of the chapter.
There is no classic level system, every fight grants you a specific amount of points which can be used to unlock stats and new skills but as i said you are still limited so you cant reach the "max" level in chapter 1.
The boss fights are often very hectic and you need to constantly shift between the different combat styles.
Equiptment seems to be almost useless, you can level them up a bit by using materials on them but the increase in power is only small.
Most bosses need some specifc class combinations otherwise you have no chance and you have to find out which those are by losing again and again.
The amount of fun i had with this game is not very high, i made it a bit further than 8 years ago and slowly i see again why i quit playing it back then.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI guess I do recommend this game?
This is a weird one cause the game is both really good and really bad in some aspect.

The bad:
- Some characters have weird/no character development when there should be some.
- Story is a huge mess and hard to get into since they expect you to read the datalog for some reason... lmao
- Chapter 12 is extremely harder than previous chapters since they expect you to do side content in chapter 11 (Not a huge problem for me but I can see it being a deal breaker for some people).
- Chapter 11 was designed by an intern at Square Enix... The teleports, if you dismount a chocobo it disappears, the map is terrible and so on... It's a terrible open world.
- GIL is way too limited which results in lack of experimentation with the gear cause stuff is way too expensive to level up.
- The port.

The good:
- The world.
- Lightning, Fang and Sazh are cool characters.
- The combat system. Paradigm shift is so much fun and has a high skill ceiling which makes it fun to master.
- The music man... the music... Absolute bangers left and right.
- And lastly I just want to reiterate, the gameplay is REALLY good.

Bonus bad: If you want to play the next game in the series get ready to download 100 mods.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you know anything about FFXIII, then you probably already know it's a hallway simulator and won't open up until about Chapter 11.

That said, I still find it to be a chill and enjoyable game. The story is interesting despite some flaws. I played the game back on PS3 and never really liked the main protagonist Lightning all that much; I'm doing a new playthrough with this PC port and am curious to see if that changes. So far, I still find her to be a bit of an edge-lord, but you at least have plenty of other characters that are way more interesting! My personal favorite is Sazh.

13 has always had one of the best OSTs for an FF game which elevates the very cinematic feel of the game. Even though I wouldn't place 13 in even my top 5 FF games, I've listened to the OST more times then I can count.

I think one of the single most enjoyable qualities of this game has always been the combat system. It's a very interesting take on the ATB system of older entries with the addition of the Paradigm system. Once you learn it and become comfortable with your class combos and understanding when to shift your active Paradigm during the flow of battle, the whole experience feels very fluid, fast-paced, and entirely satisfying. You have your micro decision making with how to best fill your ATB bars with actions while you have the macro of deciding which Paradigm (class combinations) to have active.
For anybody who doesn't know, there are a variety of classes that characters can have; e.g. Commando, Ravager, Medic, Sentinel, etc. Some excel at damage, tanking, or healing/support. A "Paradigm" is a combo of these classes for your 3 active party members. You are given some starter Paradigms when the system is unlocked, but you can create and customize your own. So for instance, you can have a Paradigm that is heavy DPS-focused and then one that is very defensive with a tank and healer. During the course of battle you will find yourself shifting between periods where you want to deal a lot of damage or hunkering down when the enemy becomes very dangerous.

If you want a game that you can just relax and enjoy a sort of interactive story with really fun combat, then I would recommend 13 to you. If you want a really deep JRPG like some of the older entries, this isn't it.
Also, I highly recommend installing the FFXIII-Fix mod and the 4gig mod as they will help with any sort of stuttering or performance issues.\t\t\t\t\t
