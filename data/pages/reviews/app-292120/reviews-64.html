\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere is the maybe button? That's how I'd rate FFXIII, a maybe. TLDR is only on deep sale.

The game has good production values like most of what Square-Enix does with Final Fantasy and was an amazing looking game when it came out on PS3. Sadly for the PC port they use pretty low res textures and the cutscenes are also low res. Mods can fix this though, but downloads will take a long time unless you pay for Nexus to get better speeds. In general I'd rate the port as pretty low effort, typical Square-Enix for PC.

The story itself is pretty nonsensical in typical Japanese fashion. Generally Final Fantasy stories are not all that complex and are easy enough to follow. This ones though, it isn't, possibly because half (or more) is kept within the datalogs, which if you don't read you'll never know.

Gameplay was a step backwards for the series, going more towards a fully action oriented style that they eventually settled with on FFXV rather than sticking with the ATB system used prior that's closer to turn-based. In this game you only control a single character at a time in a party size of three. There are six playable characters in total. The game is almost entirely linear, only Chapter 11 contains a more open area to explore. This caused a lot of complaints by people, but honestly I didn't get it. FFX is extremely similar in how linear it was.

The first several hours of the game (about 16 or so) is a painfully extended tutorial where the game handicaps you heavily in character selection (there is none) and character growth. It isn't until Chapter 10 that you can freely select your party members, party leader, and assign any of the five roles to them.

In relation to the rest of the main line final fantasy series, I'm afraid I'd have to rate this one towards the bottom. Only FF XV and VIII rate worse for me, with XV at the very bottom. So why a recommend? Because when this is on deep discount (less than $10), for that price, it is still worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe port doesn't have many graphics options but it has a good story, fun-enough combat system, plenty of well-made cutscenes, and incredible English voice acting (I usually prefer Japanese voices but this one was worth it in English!).

== FIX for the lack of graphics options ==
  • Choose 720 resolution in the game launcher
  • Download the free tool "GeDoSaTo" and use it to scale up the game to your desired resolution (1080, 1440, 4k, etc).
  • GeDoSaTo can also be used to customize anti aliasing, shadow resolution, anisotropic filtering, etc

    Using GeDoSaTo with the launcher res at 720 prevents the issue where enemy info doesn't show at 1080 or higher res.

How to play in Borderless Fullscreen mode:
  • In the game launcher choose "Windowed Mode"
  • Then download the free tool "Borderless Gaming" and use it to make the game borderless fullscreen.

    I find that running the game in borderless fullscreen also stopped the game from crashing every now and then. The game would sometimes crash when Alt+Tabbing between the game and other apps but with borderless fullscreen that doesn't happen anymore.

The game runs well enough on my old Nvidia 1080 GPU and i7. It's usually a stable 60FPS and sometimes drops to 40-50 FPS for half a second but it's a really minor thing.

Note: I beat this game before I had it on Steam so playtime shown is not accurate. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm kind of torn on this because FFXIII does have some great individual pieces like the setting, narrative concept, character designs, battle system (Albeit slightly flawed), and the incredible OST.


Let's start with the positives first

For one I really thought the foundation of the Fabula Nova Crystallis universe or lore was incredible. I liked flipping the whole "Warriors of Light" motif on its head and how being branded a 'chosen' is more of a curse than a blessing. Also the melancholy of having one's fate decided upon you by a deity using the whole Fal'Cie and l'Cie 'relationship' in this way. It's a shame Square Enix doesn't do another game using this as a base because it's a great set-up for an RPG story. The various locations are also surprisingly really nice to see despite the game running 'on-rails'. A quick note on character designs, they are all fairly well done and mesh well with the overall art direction.

Okay moving onto the OST, Masashi Hamauzu did an impeccable job with the music. It's very different from Nobuo Uematsu, but I think in a good way. The use of horns and strings were epic and powerful especially in the battle theme 'Blinded by Light'. Plus the somber and melancholic piano piece that serves as the main theme in 'The Promise' is unforgettable. As well as many other great tracks that just fit the game really well overall with a plethora of great arrangements and performances.

Lastly there's the CSB system or Command Synergy Battle system. While not perfect, I believe was an interesting way to shake up the 'turn-based' formula that was used heavily from FFX and the games that came before. The ATB gauge this time around became this action point meter divided in various segments with certain abilities needing more of the gauge to use than others. And being able to split up actions as the meter filled up for various offensive or defensive strategies depending on the circumstances of battle. It has a lot more depth and micro-management than given credit for. It allowed for some interesting and intense combat in certain encounters especially with Eidolons or story bosses.


Okay so here we go... the bad
*Warning everything I say below is just my own opinion so take it with a grain of salt, it's okay to disagree with me if you think differently*

A lot of the execution is just not done very well. I'll start with the characters. The cast of characters here that make up our ragtag group of 'protagonists' as a whole are one of the worst in the entire FF franchise. The writing is super stiff and makes it feel hard to root for them at all. The only character I remotely like is Sazh out of the entire cast. Even then they all for the most part just come across as really one dimensional, uninspired, and tropey (And not the flattering kind of tropey). It feels like the writing team tried to recreate the magic of prior FF parties, but took the most bland parts of what made those legacy characters memorable and injected them here. It also doesn't help that the vocal performances are not that great either, I don't really blame the voice cast themselves, but whoever lead the voice direction did a poor job trying to get the most out of the talent they had.

The pacing of the story is inconsistent and just all over the place. While I get our party is on the run the entire time after being branded l'Cie, there isn't enough on the side between major story beats to allow the narrative to rise and fall at a comfortable rate as stakes and dramatic moments occur. It makes certain scenes ring hollow and not really hit with the emotional depth the story is trying to accomplish. Having more downtime here and there would allow our characters to show more nuanced sides of themselves to give the player more opportunities to connect with them if not sympathize with their dire situation.

From a gameplay perspective having more side activities placed at various points of progression could have helped a bit with some of the monotony. That alone would make it so the endless 'long hallways' meme not be a huge point of contention among split fans. As an example FFX another extremely linear game is not criticized for the same reason because of the way it's paced (Even if not perfect). For myself there's nothing wrong with a linear game especially a very "on-rails" experience if it is a well-rounded one with a balance of main story progression and an option to engage with fun side activities from time to time. Or at the very least not feel like it drags its feet between major plot developments. This just overall in itself feels like a missed opportunity that could have given the game a bit of a boost for people that may have quit in the middle of story progression.

Lastly the Crystarium leveling/skill system is very rigid and from a gameplay perspective once you go through the game once, unless you love FFXIII I don't see a reason to replay. At least with some of the prior entries you could shake up your party comp and build either via some sort of job system or free flowing skill tree like in FFX. I mean the Crystarium is serviceable enough, but just barely.


Conclusion?

Overall I feel conflicted, FFXIII is not the complete dumpster fire people make it out to be. But it's easily apparent how flawed this experience is especially stacked up against other series' games even among the "bad" ones. It feels like certain aspects were either overlooked or not given the attention it deserved to allow this game to reach its full potential. Because as I said at the beginning there are some nice pieces individually, but taken as a whole it's just a disjointed mess. FFXIII is basically the textbook definition for "mid" not completely terrible but definitely not amazing either.

For hardcore Final Fantasy fans if you love the franchise as a whole I still think this is worth the experience even if just for historical purposes out of respect of the series. But for your average JRPG fan, I'd proceed with caution and low expectations.

It's games like these that I wish there was a neutral option instead of just a pure "recommend" and "do not recommend" option for reviews on steam.

Personally I don't regret playing this, but it's definitely a slog and it took a lot for me to keep pushing onwards with the game.


Unrelated sidenote: It's interesting looking back at the time this came out because in the HD Era of games with the PS3 and Xbox 360, Japanese developers really struggled to make that transition. Aside from the technical aspects, just creatively it felt like a dark time where a majority of their games just weren't it. With some outliers like the Souls series at that time. Obviously nowadays it's the complete opposite which is great, but it's just fascinating thinking about the landscape of videogames especially JRPGs in the era FFXIII came out.\t\t\t\t\t
