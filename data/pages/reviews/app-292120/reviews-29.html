\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like running down hallways? Then have I got a game for you!

In all seriousness, although I had a blast with FFXIII, it's difficult to recommend this game without reservations. When the game shines, it REALLY shines - the battle system, the presentation, character development, and lots of eye candy. It's all gold. But these positives do get hampered by some pretty bizarre design decisions.

Unlike the Final Fantasy games that came before it, in FFXIII there is no overworld exploration, no towns with interactable NPCs, and extremely linear gameplay progression. The first 75% of the game is essentially played on rails, running from one hallway to another with cutscenes interspersed between the corridors. The world is beautiful, but largely lifeless. It feels incomplete.

The story and character development are great on paper, but that's the biggest problem - it's all on paper. Most of the plot gets revealed in datalogs - hundreds of pages of text that players are required to read to understand just what is going on and what the characters' motivations are. The game also leans heavily on dramatic irony - all the characters have secrets that they just can't quite spit out, and the revelation drags on forever, which gets tiring. And although the game does have a good story with compelling characters, this is an egregious case of telling and not showing. None of this is a deal-breaker, but it's a pretty big missed opportunity. I'd rather see the action happening on screen instead of reading about it after the fact.

When you reach the game's last chapters, you finally do get taken off the rails and are allowed to explore a beautifully rendered overworld. But it's empty. Apart from a few items to find and side missions to complete, there isn't anything to explore and nobody to talk to except one little robot. The overworld is just another corridor, albeit laid out inefficiently.

I personally don't mind reading datalogs, nor am I especially bothered by linear gameplay, so I enjoyed it, but these are big drawbacks for players used to well-executed stories and sandbox exploration. The battle system and character customization are this game's saving grace. It's a shame these elements weren't given a comprehensible plot or a fleshed-out world to support them.

If you play games for the exploration and the world building, then FFXIII is definitely NOT for you. But if you like to sink your teeth into complex battle systems and lots of equipment hunting, then you should give it a try. FFXIII is without question a good game. It's just a shame, because it could've been a great game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI ignored the reviews.. I ignored the warnings. I decided that I will give FFXIII an honest try with an open mind since I have played almost every other FF... and loved almost all of them. Surely I can handle it, right? I've 100% Dark Souls as well as numerous other titles... there's no way I can't make it through the 3 FFXIII games.

well...

I really wanted to at least experience the story but WOW the gameplay is AWFUL. I have no doubt in my mind this was originally supposed to be a movie and then at the last minute they decided to slap together some "gameplay" (if you wanna call it that.) I don't even have a problem with the map being literally a straight line.. I can deal with that.. but the combat... it's physically painful to play through.

I will say the premise of the story was interesting and the graphics have held the test of time and still looks great today, especially with HD cutscene mods. but the gameplay... I doubt it was EVER what you could consider "good.." even a decade ago.

This game also requires quite a bit of grinding.. which isn't normally an issue for me.. but grinding with this combat system? HAHAHA. Do you want to know true pain? I actually fell asleep mid-gameplay around half a dozen times in the 22 hours I played. The combat system is not engaging. You have (almost) no control over your party members positioning. Don't want them to walk into an AOE? Well that's just... too bad if they want to. Sounds fun right?

All jokes aside...I can not in good faith recommend XIII unless you are a superfan, and even then.. it's very difficult to recommend. It's not a bad game.. it's just not a fun game. To put this into perspective, I do not give out many negative reviews.. and I think I've given all positive reviews for all of the 8 other FF games I've reviewed on steam...

Honestly watching just the cutscenes of the game on youtube is probably a much better experience. I think I could recommend that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game when it first came out on console, though I never completed it. Under no circumstances should anyone purchase the PC version of this game. It is plagued by constant crashes, the in-game prompts don't work if you use a control (even when switching the associated setting), and it stutters and drops frames even on a machine that vastly outperforms the game's requirements. In order to continue past chapter 3, I had to download two fan made mods, (FF13Fix and a 4GB Patch) to make the game even function. The fact that the game requires 3rd party software to be manually downloaded and installed to function is shameful. Why neither Steam nor Square Enix has done anything to address this broken game is baffling. It should not be available for purchase, as I cannot stress enough, the game does not function on PC without 3rd party fixes. For a game that launched after FF12 and was able to see its gambit system, why in the name of all things good am I forced to depend on braindead AI. Please let me dictate the order in which they cast things. Fang would be much better off using Fog and Fogga on many enemies, but she will instead spam Curse and I can do absolutely nothing about it.

Technical gripes aside, a review of the actual game. 2, maybe 3 out of 10. The storyline is fed to you in a terrible fashion, and many of the end game reveals needed to be revealed in the first act, not the final fight. For most of the game, I was asking "Who is this? Why do I care about this? Is that supposed to make sense? What is even happening and why?" These questions are never answered. I had to use the wiki and knowledge from future sequels (both of which are far better than 13, but not by enough to justify and trilogy of this nonsense) to even know what half these things are. The game is extremely linear until you reach a certain point in the game, I won't harp on this too much as its a common thought, but it does feel terrible. The combat itself is not well balanced. I fought every single encounter and overleveled my party quite a bit, and would still routinely get game overs based entirely on chance. When I retried the fight and changed absolutely nothing, often times I would win. The difference between victory and defeat should NEVER be up to random chance. The paradigm system is actually one I enjoy quite a bit. It allows you to change jobs on the fly and make combat extremely dynamic and fast paced. However, the game ends if the character you are controlling is KO'd. That's bad design. The AI is perhaps one of the dumbest things I have ever seen. When the party is all at 10% hit points, your designated medic will use less than half their available ATB gauges on healing. This wouldn't be a problem if you could raise your defense high enough to not be damaged, but several enemies, including the final boss, have the ability to upwards of 80% of your current hit points with fractional damage. You cannot resist this in any way and are lift hoping that your medic will use more than 33% of the resources in this emergency situation. The last boss is bad. Its first form has an ability with a 50% chance of inflicting instant-death. If that happens to your party leader, you lose. Immediately. Do not pass go, do not collect $200. Reload and try again because RNG has dictated you lose. The second form is a gimmick fight. Why would you make the final boss a gimmick fight? The game was already anti-climactic from start to finish, but the last boss was genuinely terrible. The game opens up to a post game after defeating the final boss so you can finish side quests, but I can't imagine why anyone would continue. According to steam, only 16.6% (at the time of writing this review) of players have defeated the final boss. I can only assume the vast majority stopped at the constant crashes in chapters 3-7, and the remainder decided to practice self-care and uninstall this abomination.

If you truly love this game and want to play it, do yourself a favor: get it on console. It won't fix the battles or the story or the pacing, but at least the game will function.\t\t\t\t\t
