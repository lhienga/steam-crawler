\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is clearly overhated, but it isnt a masterpiece by any stretch of the imagination. It's a good game that is severely hindered by some design decisions.

One of the first things you'll hear about this game is how linear it is, and make no mistake, it is EXTREMELY LINEAR. You'll spend 99% of the game walking through a hallway. There's nothing wrong with the game being linear, the problem here is that during this part of the game you have no side quests, almost no NPCs to interact with and the ones you can "interact" with don't say or do anything interesting. When the game finally opens up, it throws you in an area that looks all the same, the enemies spawn only when they at a fingers length from you and the side quests are all: "go fight this enemie you've fought 1000 thousand times before". These quests would have been welcomed earlier in the game, not at end when I want so see what happens next in story and I'm almost at the end of the game.

The Areas are not the only thing that takes a while to get going, the combat also does. The first few hours make you feel very restricted and the next 20 hours only feel a little more open. Thing is, when it gets going, it really does. The paradigm and the stagger system are both great, the game is less about stats (althought they always help) and more about building the right strategy and timing, even a normal fight can kick you ass if you're not paying attention to it. The combat is really one the highlights of this game and it shows that there's a lot you can do with turn based combat (even though this isn't truly turn based). SAZH HAS HASTE BTW. XIII has some of the best boss fights in FF, but also some of the most frustrating because Chapter XI marks a difficulty jump that players haven't really been prepared for by the previous 20 hours of hand holding. Also the Crystarium shouldn't even exist. The abilities should have been given to you through leveling up since the crystarium is linear and allows no freedom unlike the Sphere Grid, it just feels like more unnecessary work just to level up.

As for the story, I feel like this is one of those games that leaves and dies by its characters. If you don't enjoy the characters, you'll probably won't enjoy the story very much, since most of the game is just the characters running away from PSICOM and going from point A to B, with not many big events happening outside of the characters. Personally, the only two character I cared about were Hope and Sazh. The others I either didn't care much or couldn't stand (Snow). The lore is interesting but it is deliverd in a way that makes more annoying than anything else. It is still at least a bit compelling and enjoyable but the last chapter fells like padding out a game that didnt need padding.

Useless abirtrary score:
6.5/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8.5/10 Loved it.
Revisited this game as part of a FF franchise revisit in modern day and I loved it. That said, I totally get why this game has some passionate haters and mixed opinions. It just happens to be a game that works wonderfully for my tastes.

I LOVE the first 9 chapters from a story, character and world building standpoint. The story isn't anything great but it does have WWE levels of drama peppered in that totally works for me in terms of maintaining interest.

After this point the game opens up but also changes in terms of the kind of game it actually is...you go from a story heavy linear game to an open world game whose story takes a massive step back. The game also really opens up in terms of combat options, progression and challenge. I would describe this point of the game right until the end to play a lot more like the bulk of FF12 where you do hours of combat with small snippets of story.

What helps make FF13 great in a modern day is the delineation of combat and story. Its a fantastic dual monitor RPG to play while watching a show or listening to a podcast.

The game holds up extremely well. Iconic characters. Fun world. An okay main story. Couple of good tracks and one of the most fun combat systems in the genre once the game finally lets you use paradigms.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPurchased this game back when it first launched on the PS3 and remember being very excited to play. I have played just ebout every Final Fantasy game since FF4 and love all their games, this one included. But I remember why I stopped playing after purchasing it again when it came on sale here in Steam.

The game is extremely linear, which isnt a huge problem (For me) since you're essentially playing a story in a way they wish to tell it.

CONS:
~The combat, though fun, feels like its too difficult for just trash mobs. I played around with the Paradigm system and found a fairy good way to cut through trash but even then it still feels like some of the trash mobs have too much health and resistances. I understand that they wanted you to take advantage of the buffing/debuffing system from switching around, but even with doing both I still feel some of the creatures still take too long to kill. Not to mention how frustrating it is when a Melee character back steps away from enemies during combat which can lead to the stagger chain being interrupted cause they dont make it to the enemy in time thanks to that and being knocked up/down.

~You MUST grind CP or you are going to have a bad time. I have always enjoyed the leveling systems of previous final fantasy games and found myself sticking in areas too long just to grind out levels/strength but combined with the issues above, Its far less fun then previous FF installments.

~Dialogue/Voice acting seems so awkward. I heard they had some issues with the translations, so it can be understandable. But Im literally afraid sometimes someone thinks I am watching porn if they ever heard the sounds Vanille seems to make from just BEING IN A SCENE. She is literally doing nothing and is constantly making weird noises. Not sure if its ment to be cutesy or what.. but its just weird.

~I havent finished the game but I am sure it has a good story, its just very difficult to get through it cause till now (And where I stopped playing back when it launched) it seems to be filled with a lot of nonsense to possibly add character development? Ill find out later I suppose.

~The game has you constantly swapping from one group to the next, which would be fine but sometimes you are literally walking what seems like ten feet just for another cinematic/in game cutscene. There are soo many of them (To the point I am at now) and as I stated before, a lot of the information seems unnecessary.

PROS:

~Going to seem like the cons will heavily outweigh the pros but I am legitimately going to finish this game. One way or another, even if it takes all year. Because Final Fantasy has (for the most part) always had amazing stories that I have highly enjoyed and I want to see them all.

~The combat is fun. I love being able to swap rolls in the middle of combat to change my strategy mid fight. If only it wasnt as necessary for trash fights.

~The lead character (Lightning) seems like an interesting character and she is cool. I cannot wait to unravel more of her story as the game plays and eventually play 13-2 (Return of Lightning).

Summary:

Its a Final Fantasy game, and the first to come out for this generation of consoles. There was some troubles that I read about between the game team and the engine team(?) in the process which made for a few hiccups but its my opinion the game is worth getting and playing if you can pick it up on sale or even at current Steam Price (Not sure what its at now but I dont believe its much)

Get the game, play it, beat it. Enjoy the story.\t\t\t\t\t
