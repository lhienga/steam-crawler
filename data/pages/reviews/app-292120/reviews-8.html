\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt´s an okay experience, all and all. Not the best Final Fantasy by a long shot, and the gameplay suffers from the extreme linearity for the first 20/25 hours, until you can get a relief at chapter 11. After that, more corridors until you beat the game.
The story is a bit convoluted and it´s easy to get lost if you´re not paying attention at every turn. Some things are not explained at all during the normal course of the gameplay, and require you to read tons and tons of text at the datalog (this isn´t a Souls game, come on). The cast is also okay, and I think all of them do get an adequate development throughout the game.
The battle system is not my cup of tea, but it has its merits, being clearly an experimental system. It´s very fast paced and at times it requires some strategical planning prior to the encounter with the paradigms you plan to use for the particular enemies you´re facing, but the game relies too much on the stagger system for it to feel like an authentic combat.
To end on a good note, I did fell in love with the visuals (I think that was the first HD FF we got in the series), as it has a very good art direction, with most scenarios worthy of praise, at least in concept. The musical score is also top notch, and it knows when to bring forth different emotions at the right times.\t\t\t\t\t
