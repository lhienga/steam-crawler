\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I finally bought the game on sale after playing mostly FF 7-10 in the past (on PS2) and gave it a try. I knew it already caused mixed feelings back when it came out.
I finished the game in around 48h (after partlyforcing myself to) and grinded just a bit longer. Frankly, I only enjoyed half my play time.

I want to mention just a few of the problems I had with it:

- The game crashed several times at random moments (loading after a cutscene, on the overworld while walking, mid-fight,..) and Alt-Tab also breaks the game completely. It’s the worst PC port I’ve played so far

- Battle system: It’s kind of a unique approach – not classic turn based, nor full action. It’s serviceable, but I’m not a fan of it. What I really grew to dislike was the game rating you after every battle on a 5 star ranking system. Like, I know the fight didn’t’ went smooth, game, no need to rub it in…

- The game has a ton of cutscenes to the point of interrupting you every few minutes to play one for 1-2 minutes. It really was too much for my taste

- The last chapter of the game dragged on way too long in my opinion. It was just monotonous gameplay...

- The story itself was ok. I’d say, I was more invested in it in the beginning than in the end. I definitely don’t feel the urge to play 13-2.

So yeah, what can I say. I realized I prefer the old PS1/PS2 era style of FF and not the newer one.
Still, if you’re interested in the game, grab in when it’s on sale - you definitely get your moneys’ worth out of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very hesitant yes. The characters and the story are enjoyable for the most part, but I just got backhanded so hard by chapters 12-13, and I don't have it in me to keep on going, lol.

It was a personal decision to run past Gran Pulse as I didn't feel like farming cp and the cieth quests. The first 20-25 hours of the game are pretty linear as you progress through the story, but then you suddenly hit a wall where you have to farm just to continue the main story??? Sure. I mean, you can definitely brute force your way through the game, but I didn't feel like it was worth the struggle after the ridiculous difficulty spike and the many, many, many retries.

Ultimately, I had fun. It's a beautiful game with a fun combat/combo system and some pretty cool cinematics, but...

Cid Raines had the right idea. Pew pew.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am about three quarters through the game at this point. The two positives for the game is it does have good graphics and the voice work is fairly good. That asides when I compare it to previous Final Fantasy games I have played it is a bit disappointing.

The first odd choice for the game is that thus far it is linear, there is no exploration or discovery. Second the combat system has become automated as it is real time instead of turn based, you are able to control one character while the other two function on auto-pilot in their designated roles. Third the story isn't particularly interesting and the characters are rather over emotional. Fourth the upgrade system for weapons and accessories is very grindy/slow, though I have read in chapter 11 there are more opportunities to acquire money and materials to facilitate it. Fifth Final Fantasy summons have usually been a highlight, but the robotic version of them in this iteration is lack luster.

For 8 dollars on sale it is alright, but not worth more than that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall this is one of my favourite games of all times; it is a cinematic experience first and a JRPG second.
I played on a GTX 970 and used the 4GB fan patch for the game but i don't know if it applied properly

The Good:
- The characters are varied and memorable
- Chocobo chick is the cutest character in gaming
- Locations are ALL gorgeous
- Music is very good
- The corridor gameplay in my view streamlines the game to its benefit but some dislike it
- I love the hunts just as they were in ff12
- So many QoL changes such as auto-heal after combat is a godsend
- Lots of superb cutscenes amounting to around 8 hours; the steam trailer is representative of the quality
- One of the best art directions have ever seen; It is timeless
- No problems with xbox controller

Mixed:
- Plot doesn't make the most sense. I strongly recommend reading the datalogs if confused.
- Weapon levelling is a pain in nearly every way but is unnecessary to complete the main story
- Enemies can be quite spongy but certain strategies can mitigate this; there is an easy mode that halves enemy health
- Simplified gameplay means you don't have as many creative solutions like in previous games ie using reflect an your own party members then bouncing spells off yourself
- Crystarium is a streamlined version of sphere grid of FFX; I do love the style of it though

The Negatives:
- The port didn't crash on me but did frequently stutter; this is especially apparent in more complex areas
- Port uses lower quality assets so you may want to use mods to help the experience
- Farming gil and CP in post game is down right horrible

Tips:
- Don't underestimate debuffing and buffing in fights, especially boss fights, it can turn a 10 min fight to like 3
- Haste is the best buff in the game, literally makes your characters do actions faster
- Poison is extremely strong on high health bosses
- Roles have like a multiplicative affect so switching from COM/RAV/RAV to triple RAVs can make stagger bars fill faster
- Multiple of the same type of equipment equipped may give synthesis bonuses eg random stagger
- Summons are unique and always revive party members
- Poison is extremely strong on high health bosses

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinal fantasy 13 is an RPG that uses a different style of combat than it’s previous games. The combat is an active style but you cannot control your character during combat but instead use a turn based menu where you can select your attacks and magic, and your character will perform those attacks. The system is completely dumbed down where you can use auto battle for the entirety of the game if you don’t want to select each and every attack/ spell. It does work well and I used it myself for the vast majority of the game as I found it faster to complete battles this way.

The story is really easy to follow. You have become basically cursed, known as a L’Cie and you have a task you must follow or you will be turned into a monster. Should you fulfill your quest you will be turned into crystal. Honestly both outcomes seem bleak. But the story was decent and very long. I believe I finished it at around 45hours or so and then went back as the game offers end game bosses and items.

The game is extremely linear with very little exploration, which is really odd for a final fantasy game. While the game does open up to a much wider explorable area, it’s very late into the game and there isn’t much there other than some treasures and a way to farm CP points to enhance your abilities.

There is no leveling system. Instead it uses a “skill tree” that you can unlock spells and attacks. Along the skill tree is also hit points, strength, and magic that increase your base stats. The game limits how much you can place into each tier so farming for thousands of CP while useful in the long run will be stone walled until the last few chapters of the game.

I honestly enjoyed the game and the only thing that really got on my nerves was Vanilles voice. It’s ear piecing.

Decent game, weird combat choice. No explorable areas. Just know what your getting into so you don’t end up disappointed.

7.5/10
