\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore talking about the game I'll talk about the port because most negative reviews for this game has to do with the poor port job of this game. And yeah it's bad. I have an RTX 3080 and a Ryzen 5900X and this game still managed to drop frames. I did mod the game and that helped greatly, and I also improved the visuals and general quality of the game with additional mods. With the game modded, graphically, it held up over a decade later, frame drops were very minimal happening only in a few areas of the game, and I didn't experience a single crash for my 90 hours of playtime. No, it's not okay that this game launched as it did, and did not get improved since its launch, but with the game modded it's good enough in terms of optimization.

It seems the the general sentiment of this game being the death of Final Fantasy has died and people generally respect a lot of aspects of this game even if they find it very flawed. Yes, this game is a series of hallways, yes the characters are very archetypal in many respects, and yes the story overloads the player at the beginning with so much in-game lingo that it can be tough to follow and might require some people to look the the "Dialogues" section of their menu to actually figure out what's going on... needless to say that's not a great way to tell a story in a story driven game.

It might sound like I don't like this game, but on the contrary I found it fantastic. The characters being archetypal helped me understand an know who they are right off the bat, and generally speaking they all develop is fun and interesting ways that made them one of my favorite Final Fantasy casts. The plot could have been told better, but I didn't really require to Dialogues section in the menu to know what was going on. I found that menu option more helpful as a refresher to help me remember what was going on after taking a break from this game.

As for the hallway aspect of this game... yeah chapter 1-10 and chapters 12 and 13 are all hallways. Note I did not talk about chapter 11 which changes this game to an open world game and finally makes this game shine. The way in which the plot is progressed, the characterization, the added side quests, and the gameplay efficiently being fully unlocked meant that this game went from good to fantastic. Chapter 11 is by far the longest chapter, and you can revisit all the areas in chapter 11 during chapter 13 which you can't do with any of the other areas in the game. This is because a large amount of the game was built around chapter 11. The problem is to get to this outstanding section of the game you need to have your hand held for about 20 hours before being given the opportunity to explore and do your own thing. Also although the combat system is super varied and fun, that mostly only happens again after 20 hours when you are in chapter 11. That's not to say that the first 10 chapters are unenjoyable, but they are all being held back by themselves.

I don't have too much to say about the graphics other than they are fantastic; not just for 2009, but for 2022, they hold up that well. I played this game at 4K 60fps and the visuals of this game are bliss. I said I modded this game to look even better, and my god this game really holds up with mods. I can't understate how visually amazing this game is.

So in short this game is flawed, it's pacing is problematic, and although it does get better it should and and probably could have been amazing from the very start. But in spite of all this game's short comings I really enjoyed it, enough the get every achievement at that. If I had to give it a rating I'd give it a high 8/10, it would have been higher if the whole game was more like chapter 11, but hey what can you do. I'd recommend this game to any FF or JRPG fan, but it would be understandable if a lot of aspects were not enjoyed by a lot of people.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis port has serious performance issues related to game controller support. The game will constantly check whether or not a controller is plugged in, causing a very large FPS drop whenever it does so. The only way around this is to install an unofficial patch or disable the controller port. Unacceptable that the game has been left on Steam in such a state. I cannot recommend that anyone spend money on a product that is broken. Surprising that Square Enix is okay with this.

Regarding the gameplay itself: Interesting premise let down by poor pacing, both in terms of storytelling and gameplay. The first half of the game has it presenting the worst of itself - the characters are moody and edgy and the combat is artificially limited to ease players into mechanics. If you can push through to the 2nd half of the game you will find a rather interesting combat system and characters that aren't quite as terrible as they first appeared (minus Snow, who stays as one dimensional as ever).

The game looks good even over a decade after release and there are some very pretty visuals, especially in the first half of the story.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a mixed bag for me. Play it for the story, but it's not a game that lets you explore like you are used to in FF games.

As a FF game it's not what I personally like. I want FF games to be mostly free roaming where I can go anywhere I like given the circumstances. But here I am walking on a winding narrow road that I can only move forward and back on. There is no sense of exploration except from what the narrative lets you explore. Which is section by section. It's not until the final 2-3 chapters where you are able to roam between the places you've explored. And since it is all a long corridor it is a LOT of backtracking if you want to finish of all the Cie'th Stone missions. You will spend hours running back and forth and you will get bored out of your mind.

But if I don't look at this game like a FF game I really like the story and narrative. I like the simplified battle mechanics where I focus on roles instead of the specific actions, and that I don't have to heal up after every battle. I like the environments, the graphics are nice and colorful. The characters fit nicely into the story, etc. So if they just made this game as a story focused game, gave me easier and plentiful travel options to any part I want in the end game, I would have really enjoyed this game and would probably finish the end game content. Instead I'm filled with a feeling that there were more to explore but it was just SO tedious that I couldn't get myself to play anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing a fan of the franchise and hearing for years the reputation of this game, I decided to buy it while on sale to form my own opinion.

Gameplay –

I would say that the criticisms against this game are mostly true. I didn’t mind the linear path early in the game as I felt it was going to set the groundwork for the story, characters, and mechanics. But as the hours grew, the path became a mind-numbing experience that had little to no variety. If the path did deviate, it was for a weak item or a small amount of Gil. The one aspect of the game I found myself enjoying was the Paradigm system. Once you get three to a party, I found the mid-fight shifts and strategic Paradigm sets interesting and the sole reason the gameplay isn’t entirely terrible. The Crystarium seemed cool in concept but the execution is an absolute joke. You never really see the effects of it and is a chore to go through. You pretty much have to manually do what the past FFs did automatically. Such a pain! If you’ve played other FFs before, you’ll find this game incredibly easy. The bosses can be tanky but once you know how to beat them, plus a little grinding, they're easy.

Story (No Spoilers)-

The story is the biggest factor I look for in a Final Fantasy game; it’s what got me into the series. By saying that I hope you can understand why I'll be so critical about this aspect of the game. The story of this game is a special kind of bad. The type of bad that almost feels intentional; like a parody or mockery of JRPGs. You're given no context to the world, people, or conflicts. Characters will ramble on about things you have no idea about, use terms that are so confusing that you will lose track, and to make sense of anything you're forced to read far too many Data Logs. I’ll admit, after a while you start to understand more but that is too much to ask for any player. I recommend going some homework if you really want to know the story. I can’t believe I have to say that. Game acts more like a bad novel than a video game.

Characters (Mild Spoilers)-

Lightning is pretty bland and never did much for me. She’s not my most hated protagonist, that honor belongs to that ♥♥♥♥♥ Squall, but she is defiantly bottom tier. Snow is trying to be the cool “hero” but feels more like a douche frat boy way over his head. Vanille was extremely annoying and I hated that they were trying to make her that “wise young person” with her narrations and backstory. Hope was fine, a bit whiny, but fine. Fang didn’t do anything for me nor did her story with Vanille. Sazh was the best character and should have been the main protagonist.

Other Stuff-

This game’s graphics and cut scenes are absolutely stunning, as per usual with Square. For a 2009 game, this looks incredible. I didn’t have any issues with the port but I know others have. Aside from the main and battle themes, the music is trash. An insult to the Uematsu era.

Overall Score – 4/10\t\t\t\t\t
