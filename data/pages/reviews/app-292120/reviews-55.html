\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan, Final Fantasy 13 is such a fun ride!
Which also happens to be inconsistent to what I've always read or heard about FF13, critics and players always bash it for being the worst game in the series - trust me, as someone who equally loves Final Fantasy 7, 10 & 12, this is not at all bad and is easily one of my favorites now.
Gameplay does become repetitive by the end of the game but that doesn't make FF13 a "horrible experience" type of game, I honestly can't call any Final Fantasy that I've played to be bad, they're all well made games, even Dirge of Cerberus was fun with it being a 3rd person shooter.
Final Fantasy 13 is very linear for a JRPG unlike most of the other games in the series - so that might be a negative aspect for many players who like the classic Final Fantasy feeling, but those who like story and cutscene centered games (Metal Gear Solid 4 fans) will appreciate FF13, the cast of characters are some of the best in the series - especially Vanille, Hope and Sazh, and there's some really good emotional moments in the story and the world building lore is really cool too in my opinion.
Game's visuals surprised me, for a 2009 game, it sure has aged well, texture quality might be off here and there but overall it looks good - almost like it was made in the last 2-3 years.

ONLY bad thing I can say about FF13 is this PC port, it's downright unplayable without mods, some players can atleast play the game without modding it but for me the game wouldn't even launch, so be prepared for 30 mins of modding, which isn't hard, there's some well written guides here on Steam.

Those that have never touched this, please give it a try, the story is really fun to go through.
My rating - 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFFXIII was one of those games I really had to force myself to finish. The world is often visually stunning and looks great still today. There are some repetitive locations where almost every scene looks the same like Gapra Whitewood but the game redeems itself with places like Nautilus, Hanging Edge and some places on Grand Pulse. The music is awesome as well and I liked almost every character (maybe Hope was a bit tedious and crybaby-like in the beginning). But oh boy the gameplay in this so-called JRPG. No real interesting side-missions, the levels are straightforward for about 40 hours+ until you reach an "open world" and especially in the late game, there is a need for some grinding. It would be okay if the battle system would be fun but for maybe 70-80% of the game, all you do is spam the A-Button so that your group attack with their best spells and weapons. Occasionally you swap to another paradigm for some quick healing or status effects. It never gets really deep. In addition to this, there are some real damage sponges in this game that suck the fun out of it in no time. All you do is attack, heal, and keep status effects up. Rinse and repeat for sometimes several minutes. And then there are bosses that can kill a character in an instance. There are other attacks that wipe away 95% of your HP. The problem is that there aren't three characters on the battlefield. There is ONE LEAD and two others on the battlefield. When your lead dies: Game Over. Although the other two are still standing and could revive the fallen comrade. I don't understand that design decision. And now add all of this together. The fight is going on for several minutes, you constantly switch heal and attack because your party has only 5% HP. You swap back to attack, and the boss's live bar loses about 5 millimetres (0,2 inches), it goes on and on. At some point, you even make it to 50% of the boss's HP. And then an instakill. After maybe 5-10 minutes. With no chance of rezzing. That my dear friends is some ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and made me almost watch the rest of the game on Youtube.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs this game bad? No.
Is it the best game ever made? Also no.

To start off, this game is VERY linear, to the point of restricting who can be in your party up until around the half way point, so if you go into it thinking you will be given free reign of cocoon, you will be disappointing. Later in the game it does open up and there is a lot of side quests to do and missable cutscenes that further explore the party's dynamics but I found it to be a major pacing killer, likewise with the final dungeon which will take about 4 times longer than you expect.

As for the positives, the combat, whilst relatively shallow (there is literally a button that chooses the most optimal attacks for the situation and you are unable to directly control the actions of the other 2 party members) is fast paced and quite fun and coupled with the music and the fact that enemies wander around the map similar to ffxii, while also retaining the almost turn-based combat of other ff games, makes for combat that seems less like a chore and more like a fun challenge. On the topic of the music the OST is honestly one of the best the series (and games in general) have to offer.

The core mechanic of combat is the "Paradigm Shift" which allows the player to change the jobs of the characters in the party mid way through a battle. this adds depth to the combat as you can have paradigms that focus on healing and defence (sen/syn/med) and paradigms that focus on offence (com/rav/sab), or you could go for a combo of the 2 (com/rav/med or my personal favourite, com/syn/sab). It does have to be noted though that not every character has access to each role, so for the first half of the game you will have to make do with the roles of the characters the game supplies you with at that time.

Despite the level design being linear, all of the areas are distinct and memorable (mostly for good reasons but 1 or 2 areas really fall flat). Some of the best looking ares are lake bresha and the gapra white wood, both of which have stunning visuals and amazing music. some of the worst areas are those in the tenth and thirteenth chapters. The issues with these areas is that they are just way too long! i am pretty sure a solid fifth of my play time was spent in those areas alone. Other than that though the areas are all amazing.

The story (minor spoilers ahead) centres around 6 characters that were essentially exiled from cocoon ("Purged" as the game calls it) for being near the discovery of a pulse artefact. A quick sidenote, you WILL need to refer to the in game data log to understand the meaning of all the mumbo jumbo like cocoon, pulse, l'cie, fal'cie, cie'th and psycom. It isn't really necessary that you look at it after chapter 3 as if you read most of it then you will be caught up to date with most of the story. for those wondering pulse is the planet that lies below cocoon and both places HATE each other and the people in power of cocoon have decided to exile anyone who has been in contact with anything pulse related. Anyway, after the purge went awry, the party ends up inside the pulse artefact and after witnessing Lighting's sister turn to crystal, they all decide to fight the fal'cie in the heart of the artefact. After defeating the fal'cie, the main party are all turned into l'cie which are given tasks to complete by the fal'cie via cryptic visions. The base premise for the story is that the party have to find the meaning of the task set for them by the fal'cie, while also being pursued by the forces of cocoon, not only for surviving the purge but also for being essentially supersoldiers from pulse and therefore enemies of cocoon. The story is good but one of its major flaws is the lack of a central antagonist of the likes of sephiroph from ff7. Instead the main threat is just the forces of cocoon and there leader (who gets almost no screentime and is only present in the laterhalf of the game)

Overall ff13 is a good game for the price, with good combat, stellar music and excellent visual design that is only brought down by it's linearity and the sometimes confused story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame combat is very outdated and boring. Story is mediocre and quite dull that provides you with little attachment to characters until much later into the game.
First few chapters of the game is very boring until you get Paradigm Shifts where you can change your team members' "stance" in battle. But even then, the combat is very dull. Most enemies are immune to certain types of attacks and the amount of variety in attacks are limited to 1 or 2 for each elemental type. You can only directly control one character in battle and it's game over when that person dies.
Another big issue is the stagger and tankiness of the enemies. They are so tanky and their stagger bars are take so long to take down, you might as well consider the stagger bar as extra HP bars (that you need to chip down before getting to the actual HP). Did I forget to mention that these are just the regular mobs too? Regular mobs can take 10-30 minutes to take down even with strats unless you grind exp a lot.

Overall, I wouldn't recommend this game. A remake of this game to fit the new final fantasy combat systems might actually make this game more enticing though.\t\t\t\t\t
