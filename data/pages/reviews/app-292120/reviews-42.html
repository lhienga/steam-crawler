From Neutral to Negative due to port

I don't want to give this game a negative rating because it doesn't necessarily deserve that. And I don't want to feed the notion that this is necessarily a bad game. But I had too many issues with the port to give this a positive rating. This is going to be a long review because there is a LOT to discuss in this game.

Now onto the actual game. I went into this hearing how its a horrible game and one of the worst FF games out there. Its hard to go into a game when you keep hearing that negative opinion. Which is ironic because so much of this games themes are about not judging a book by its cover. I came in with all those negative thoughts but left with a generally positive feeling about a lot of the game.

That being said there are parts of this game that aren't bad. In fact there are some parts here that are so good I would put it in a top 5 final fantasy list. The crowning achievement of this game is its presentation. The music in the FF series is fantastic, this game might have one of the best out of all games and that is saying a LOT. The character designs and world design are so original and fun, I love how futuristic everything looks and I would love to just spend time looking at the scenery in this game. The combat is really fun, there is a lot of experimentation going on but it has led to a really interesting combat system that is some of the best in the series.

The story can be hit or miss but some of the individual scenes are SO strong. They left a really strong impression on me and I really loved the animation, voice acting and incredible setups that happen throughout most of the story. There is 1 scene in particular early on with fire works that completely wiped away any negative thoughts I had of the game and made me try to look at this game from a fresh angle.

I love so many of the characters in this game, they have issues. especially the more you play the more you feel like they lose their personality, but early to mid game id say these are some of the most interesting and involved characters I have seen in any RPG. This isn't a natural party like in earlier FF games, all these character have their own motivation and they all deal with this insane situation in their own way. I love how hard it feels for some of them to talk to each other because I totally understand that, they are doing what they think is right but there are no obvious answers to guide them. I especially love Sazh, its rare to see a black guy in gaming in general, let alone one of his age, I wish I could talk more about him but I don't want to spoil anything. In general his backstory is one of my favorites and one that I could relate to in many different ways, something I haven't seen in any other game and haven't seen in many other mediums.

Sadly I think all of this positive stuff ends up having some problem or another:

* The story and characters just kind of fizzle out, similar but slightly different from ff 12. In 13 eventually you end up with a bunch of info dumping after some time and throwing out any development that had happened for like 80 % of the game. Same goes for the characters, all their personality is replaced. All their lines feel like they are coming from 1 character. Its all about morals and ethics and what is right or wrong. Gone is any of the interesting conversations between party members. Any potential for side characters is also thrown away as pretty much all of them end up getting almost no development.

* The combat is pretty unique. The best way to describe it is that instead of controlling your characters you are instead switching their classes and making them do their job for you. If you swap you also get more 'Turns' which means you are heavily incetivized to constantly judge a situation and swap on the fly. Enemys have a bar, the more damage you deal and the more attacks you chain the more the bar fills, a big bar means you will deal more damage and stun the enemy easier. I really like this system and for a majority of this game this system is honestly really fun. The problem I have is that once you figure it out there are too many flaws. You can practically handle most situations the same exact way, and if you want to change up your team you will have to re calibrate your team composition. The stagger bar mechanic means that most enemies need a LOT of hp to make up for the fact that you gradually do more and more damage, this means even simpler battles can take a while because you are building towards that bar break. You also can't tell party members what to do. Its extremely annoying and towards the end of the game I felt like I was playing on auto pilot. There is a lot of potential here but a lot of it feels unrealized.

* On paper its one of the most accesible RPGs I have seen, there are a ton of turtorials and the combat is simple enough to grasp. However at the same time there is a ton of stuff the game doesn't tell you about, for example how do you upgrade your weapon properly? For a majority of the game I felt like I was making 0 progress in upgrading my equipment, even when I figured it out I still felt it was horrible to upgrade stuff. Why do you end up dealing so little damage even when the enemy stagger bar is super filled. This kind of stuff isn't clear enough and if you aren't experienced in RPG's or willing to read guides to get an understanding of unexplained mechanics you will probably end up lost.

* That incredible scenery and world building is partially really sad. Because every time I enter an area I would wonder what I could do or what is going on. And yet the entire game is practically a straight line, there is no side exploration with 1 big exception and there are no side activities. Due to spoilers I can't talk about it but there is 1 area that just screamed: TIME FOR MINI GAMES! and yet nothing happens. Instead of actually getting to play those things you get to read about them. There is a ton of extra lore and information about areas, characters and other stuff which is really cool. But a lot of it also shows how gimped the actual world is in many ways. Another example is this cool forest area, the description states how people get hopelessly lost in there. However its practically impossible to get lost due to how straight forward the game is and due to all the markers showing where to go.

Lastly let me talk about the port. Square I love your games but for the love of god make your PC ports better. So many FF games have 1 issue or another. This might be one of the worst ports I have played yet from Square. The game consistently stutters, there are even mods to try and fix this but those didn't help. I have a pretty meaty mid range PC for modern standards. I can play God of war on PC maxed out without any big issues. How in gods name can this game be lagging and stuttering as much as it is. I even had the game freeze a couple of times for a couple of seconds during cutscenes. I have seen others even today mention it crashing. None of this was acceptable and it especially isn't acceptable today.

I am fairly neutral on the game in general. I love a lot of parts about this game and hate other parts of it. There is a LOT more I wish I could talk about, but this review is already long enough. I think if the positive stuff sounded interesting enough then it is absolutely worth a play. If you are willing to read up on the game mechanics then this is a super straight forward RPG with a lot to love. Some people call this the worst mainline FF game. Those people are insane and haven't experienced all this series has to offer yet. FF 13 does a lot right and does so by experimenting and creating something fresh. I can't recommend this due to my general neutral rating and the port. But I can definitely see a good amount of people enjoying this game. If you can separate your negative views on this game I promise there is a surprising amount of fantastic stuff here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR;

For those looking for something like FF 7 to 10, I would not recommend this game, it's more like an interactive movie (more cut-scenes and often longer than battles) and the battle system isn't flexible and a lot of it is automated. It's probably closer to what FF 12 offers but I dont remember FF 12 having so many cut scenes.

TL;DR;

Before I start, I wanted to cover that I have finished FFs from 7 to 10 and played a bit of 12. I'm not an expert in FFs but I have been playing game from this franchise a fair bit.

Also I'm aware that I'm posting in 2023 when this game was out a while ago. But this should actually help you decide whether or not you want to purchase this game.

Now to the review.

The game is not using the traditional turn base system that FF started with, the fights are somewhat real time but not really either, you chain a bunch of commands that they execute when they have enough gauge. the method of selecting the skills to execute is a bit clunky and doesnt allow you to select different targets in the same gauge. e.g.: you could be casting a skill against an enemy that is would be dead by the time you cast your 2nd skill, granted there is some smarts that it auto targets the next mob but that doesn't work well for magic spells, if the spell is out by the time the mob dies, you essentially missed.

The story line didn't really get me hooked, I didnt really get attached to any of the characters as it seemed they lacked a bit of personality and emotions. What made this worse is the fact that you have a cut-scene of a minute or two long every 5-7 fights. Making this game dreadfully long to play. Some might say that every FF is like this, but from my experience they are definitely not as many cut-scenes. The FFs I've played definitely had dialogues in them, where you would walk up and talk to people and press a button to see the next chat. This FF feels more like an interactive movie, where you watch a movie scene every several minutes to see the story unfold. Not really what I expected.

Last but not least comment, I've gone until chapter 7, the remaining chapters might be richer in content but just based on the total numbers of chapters, I'm about halfway there. During those 7 chapters, the battle party was never at max capacity 80% of the time, the characters always ended up splitting up resulting in a 2-member party. I find this a bit annoying as it makes it hard to develop any sort of feels of the "real" battle with a full party and I've already spent 13 hours on this game.

---

On a separate more technical topic, when I launched this game for the first time, it was dropping frames every second all the time, after researching, it seems to have been because of HID compliant devices that the game was constantly asking for inputs. I had to disable dozens of them before the game became playable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started Final Fantasy XIII after learning about it through some YouTube videos detailing the troubled development of Final Fantasy 15 or previously known as "Final Fantasy Vs XIII". My favorite game in the series was XV and after learning that it was originally part of a three title trilogy of sorts labeled the Fabula Nova Crystallis, I was curious and gave the game a shot when it was on sale for $5.

I started the game and played up to when lightning and crew just escaped Lake Bresha. I was finding the game terribly difficult to enjoy. It ran poorly at 30fps with constant stutters. The only places I could see the beauty of the game was by either standing still or initiating a battle sequence which would bump up the fps to 60, which was quite odd. Furthermore I was completely lost with the story. Fal'Cie, L'Cie, Cie'th, Coccon, Brand marks, Focus... all of these important story terms and sequences were piling up leaving a jumbled mess in my mind. I couldn't understand what was going on story wise and only could tell what was happening through the cut scenes and gameplay. Lightning is a solider wizard... maybe? Sazh was like her sidekick or something??? He had a baby Chocobo chick in his afro which was all that really mattered. Hope was some annoying Sasuke clone. Snow was an extremely unlikable cliche man. And finally Vanille was so infuriatingly optimistic I could barf. the game just wasn't clicking with me. So I put the game down and moved on with my life.

After half a year Final Fantasy XVI was announced and looked like a better version of XV. Better combat, characters, locals, story. It reignited my fire to play FF games again. Since I never uninstalled XIII it was kinda just on my PC, sitting there. I gave the game another go and pushed through. the only Character I connected with up to that point was Lightning but eventually the relationship between Light and Hope and also Sazh and Vanille grew on me. The chemistry between the characters really started to click with me and Vanilles overly optimistic personality started to crack showing sinister signs underneath. pulling me into the story even more. After I made it to the Gapra Whitewood I really couldn't get past the framrate and the stuttering so I decided to put the game down once more until I could afford a better PC to actually give the game a fair shot to really wow me.

Another six months went and I finally got my hands on a nice gaming PC. I could run Apex Legends at 60! GtaV at 60! And finally; Final Fantasy XIII at 60. I even downloaded a mod that made the game run smoother and fixed some bugs and issues. The game looked beautiful and ran even better. I was ecstatic. I played everyday for a week and made it quite far but suddenly just dropped the game a third time. I don't even really remember why I stopped. maybe it was the liner level design. Or maybe the introduction of fang felt forced and out of place. I don't know? I just dropped the game and moved on. Every once in a blue moon I'd sit down and play it for a little, slowly progressing through the story, slowly making my way through.

I don't really know why but I wanted to know if anyone else felt the same as me. Did anyone else have trouble really coming to play the game and find some excitement from it... I searched blogs and sites, YouTube, and google for other peoples experiences with the game. I came across many story's but non quite like mine. At that point I never really put it together how liner and one dimensional the exploration was until I read somewhere that the game was a "Hallway Simulator" and that the combat was "UnInspirational" which swayed my thoughts and made me think... yeah this game kinda sucks. And then while scrolling through YouTube one video popped up in my recommendations; "Need for Closure and Gaming" by Daryl Talks Games, with Lighting sitting in the lobby room in Eden as the thumbnail. It was literally that one thing where "the universe gives you the answer to your problems" things as cheesy as that sounds, you are probably thinking "hes definitely making this up" but believe it or not, it was literally one of those moments. I watched the video and it was exactly what I was going through. Playing XIII and constantly dropping it and trying to pick it back up again. then finally pushing through and finishing the game to close out that chapter in his life. I wanted that same experience. I wanted that need for closure. I wanted to Finish XIII. So I sat down and kept playing.

Even though it took me 7 months from that initial video to beat the game, playing it little at a time and telling myself "don't rush it, enjoy it for what it is". Now, after finishing the game I can say it was all worth it. The Characters, The Settings, The Story, and the music... Oh God The MUSIC!!! Vanille's redemption arc. Lightning opening up to her friends. Hope's Growth from a crybaby to the embodiment of his name. Snow learning to not bare the pain alone. Sazh learning to forgive and appreciate what he has. And Fangs realization that her found family is all that matters. All of it accumulates together to create a story that's extremely beautiful and worth the 2 years it took for me to finally beat it.

If anyone else is thinking about picking up this game I assuredly suggest you do. And take your time. This game is something that blooms along with you and is better the more your willing to accept it for what it is. Its definitely not perfect but I can proudly say its my Favorite Final Fantasy game, and probably my favorite single player story game EVER. If you read this far... Thank You. "Wishes can come true. But not if you just wait for miracles. Miracles are things we make for ourselves. Here, and now."


TLDR: FFXIII is an amazing game when you give it the chance it deserves; finally after 2 years I beat the game and now its my favorite single player story game EVER!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Call of Duty of JRPGs!

Seriously though, when you read this game is "too linear" know that's an understatement. 90% of the game is literally a straight hallway, and even the few open sections feel simple and boring because the game wasn't designed for it. This game really puts into question what RPG means since there's no towns, no exploration, no real quests, nothing...

The story is also pretty bad. The general concept is pretty good actually, a moon run by religious machines as a theocratic state who force humans to perform their will and fight the surface world. The twists and ending and stuff are good too. The problem is, it's so poorly told. No establishing shots, no exposition, no environmental storytelling, no real time with the villains. Something as simple as a Shinra broadcast in FF7 would do the setting wonders, but you don't get it.

So why did I (almost) finish it? Well it has a cool and unique combat system based on switching classic FF jobs in combat, basically. It has wonderful music and visuals, as FF games always do. It's also a fairly relaxing game to play as 99% of the time it's rather easy and the linearity can be soothing in a weird way. The final boss was a MASSIVE difficulty spike I gave up on though, and I watched the ending on Youtube. Seriously I never had trouble until then, and then suddenly it was controller-throwing frustration.

Anyway... the game sucks in any relative sense, thumbs down, but if you love JRPG music and visuals and you want to waste some time, it's probably worth playing. Maybe.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFFXIII is a great game if you accept and enjoy it for what it is, rather than becoming upset about what it is not.

FFXIII is not a traditional Final Fantasy game. While some JRPG elements, including an elaborate levelling system and the iconic FF summons are here; many standard JRPG staples like towns, npc characters and open maps, are all mostly absent from this game.

What FFXIII is, however, is a polished experience, that focuses on visuals and delivering the combat system that set the bar for all FF games following it.

The Paradigm Shift combat system is designed around having the player changing their team's roles and formations throughout combat in real time. Youâ€™ll be dealing with the changing conditions of battle including enemy haymakers, status effects, or attempting to stagger the enemy for the chance to deal high damage.

The system focuses more on strategy and tactics rather than spending time navigating menus. The default auto-battle option frees the player from repeated combat commands, and allows them to focus on the paradigm shifting strategy - keeping the battle moving at a brisk pace.

Defeating powerful enemies is less about overwhelming them with stats, and more about identifying the most efficient strategy. Experimenting with different roles and tactics to find a five star path to victory is the core of what makes the FFXIII battle system so engaging.

The one problem with the Paradigm Shift system is that its heavily gated. The player really doesnâ€™t get full use of the system until the last third of the game. It would have been better if the designers took off the training wheels a fair bit earlier.

Story and presentation are important to FF games. In FFXIII's case its a mixed bag.

The game looks amazing, however the simple truth is that the story in this game is one of the worst in the series. Both characters and writing are poor. Strange terms like Fal'Cie and l'Cie, lacking any context or bearing to real life, will leave players confused as to the game's direction or character motivations. To have any hope of understanding the narrative, the player will need to read about the game's story via the in-game data log since the game's events do a terrible job of showing what is going on.

While the game lacks an open world, the upside is that the limited scope let the designers focus on creating some stunning smaller environments to travel through. It's important to note the criticism regarding the gameâ€™s maps being a series of corridors is mostly true. While the game does open up in the last third, the first two thirds of the game is mostly on rails.

Playing this game on steam also allows you to take advantage of mods increasing the quality of the gameâ€™s assets to 4K. FFXIII was one of the best looking games on PS3 and it still holds up over 10 years later.

A final criticism goes to the gameâ€™s crafting system; which is deeply flawed. What I can say here is that itâ€™s not clear or intuitive at all. The best thing you can do is find an online guide to walk you through what to do, and what not to waste your time with. While the crafting system becomes available early in the game, you can't really use it in any significant way until, once again, that last third of the game.

FFXIII is a quality experience that can be completed in 50 hours, or 80 to 100 hours if you do all the achievements like I did.\t\t\t\t\t
