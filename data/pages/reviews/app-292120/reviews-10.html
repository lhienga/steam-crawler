At least the graphics are cool, and from what I remember the battles once you actually get the full party (which from most other reviews sounds like 20-25ish hours in) are actually fun and challenging.

Overall unless you're a hardcore FF fan dont buy this game, and even if you are wait til its on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

What really kills things is the gameplay. The amount of grinding for Crystarium Points is obscene, money is always tight given how little you get from enemies and how much you need to spend on upgrade materials for weapons and accessories, and far too many battles (especially to unlock Eidolons) require very specific tactics you have no way of knowing about beforehand. Even with a strategy guide to know where everything is and what to do, you'll still lose more often than not. But the absolute worst part is how overpowered enemies are if you haven't been farming gil like crazy to fully upgrade your gear, including grossly overpriced minerals to unlock their tiers.

Unless you use a trainer, expect to spend most of your time grinding and farming over and over again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gave this game a shot, heard it "wasn't that bad". So I bought it on sale and wanted to form my own opinion, and I was sorely disappointed. I got to chapter 6 in the game, around 13 hours in, and it was too repetitive for me to care anymore. I got to the point where I became really frustrated from how boring and how much it actually does feel like you're running down a hallway with the same enemies. You can pretty much face roll them too. The paradigm shift system is cool and kinda fun, but in most common fights you probably won't be needing to switch your roles very much. The boss fights are nice though, you'll have to shift paradigms pretty often to give shields and buffs to your allies and you'll need to pick a good strategy to win. The music is not bad, I actually really like some songs, but I probably haven't gotten far enough in the game to hear it all. I also really like the sci-fi world this game is set in, I find it pretty cool to look at (especially the cutscenes) and it was pretty interesting. But the story was kinda meh so far, and even if the story was really good, it still would've been hard to continue playing with how boring and un-fun the environment is to traverse. I'll probably watch a video about the story and move on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7 years ago I would've recommended this game. It used to be my favorite single-player Final Fantasy before XVI. What do I think now in 2024? This game kinda sucks. Lightning and Sazh carry this game HARD, but everything else besides the music is pretty lame. The combat feels clunky, most of the game is a hallway (A nice looking hallway at least) and it gets old, the story is bland, the villain is uninteresting, most of the party is one note, traversal is slow and the side quests are AWFUL. The haters were right.

My playtime isn't reflective of how long I've actually played (I alt-tabbed a lot to do other things and take breaks), but I have beaten the game twice. Once in 2017, and once in 2024.

If you DO play this game, check the community section for guides on how to get it to run on current PCs if yours crashes on launch. We are very fortunate that people made fixes years ago.\t\t\t\t\t
