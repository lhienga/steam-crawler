\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great example of a flawed masterpiece. I'd be lying if I said the game was perfect, but it's a game you have to experience if you're a fan of the "experimental" late-2000s-to-early-2010s era of JRPGs.

The beginning (and middle) is as linear as people tend to complain about and both the dialogue and plot writing are exceptionally poor, but the endgame is worth it if you can stick it out. The characters and their struggles manage to be compelling despite their awkward portrayals, the combat system is fun and surprisingly deep once your build and team composition options open up (which is unfortunately late in the game, admittedly), and the god-tier music and visuals alone carry the experience the rest of the way.

As always your mileage may vary, but I believe that Final Fantasy XIII is Square Enix's diamond in the rough, and still worth a play both for fans of other Final Fantasy games and newcomers to the series alike.\t\t\t\t\t
