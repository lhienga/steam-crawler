\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried to play this. Really. But this game is actually horrible. Here are just SOME reasons why.

Characters: Bland.
There is not a single interesting character in the bunch. Nobody has a real motivation, they change their minds every five seconds about what they're going to do, why they're going to do it, etc. The result is that it feels like none of them have their own personality, and the only distinguishing feature is, "Who is whining the most at the given moment?"


Pacing: Garbage.
The first 11 chapters of this game are a slog through identical sci-fi hallways, without any meaningful chances to diverge. Looking at other reviews, it doesn't seem like it gets any better after the point where I've decided to quit (random Alexander Eidolon addition because they have to wrap this up and some of the team didn't have summons yet, so let's just wedge this in at a point that doesn't make ANY sense).

Because the storyline is convoluted and feels like random strangers were constructing it but going, "But THEN... [insert whatever random nonsense comes to mind, even if it doesn't match up with anything that has happened so far.." you get a hurry-up-and-wait situation where you are often sitting through what feels like HOURS of FMVs of the characters having the SAME CONVERSATION OVER AND OVER.

Battle Mechanics: Meh.
Honestly, I wouldn't mind if THE GAME DIDN'T HIJACK MY BATTLE PARTY AND PARADIGMS EVERY FIVE SECONDS. Having to reset ALL my paradigms repeatedly, because the game will just decide to take your party and assign battle roles that don't remotely resemble settings YOU JUST ESTABLISHED FIVE SECONDS BEFOREHAND, is what finally broke me. That said, let's talk about Autobattle while we're at it. It sounds like a good idea on paper, but in practice it just makes the entire option of choosing your attacks pointless. Why do it at all when the computer will just choose the optimal moves and queue them up? It makes the player suddenly abundantly aware that they are just sitting tapping a button repeatedly, without anything close to engagement.

Anyway, before this game robs me of anymore minutes of my life, let me just reiterate:

This is not worth your time. Go play Chrono Trigger. Go play FF9. For the love of God, don't buy this.
