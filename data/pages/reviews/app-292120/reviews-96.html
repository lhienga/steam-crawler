\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArguably the best entry in the Final Fantasy series (it certainly is to me).

Final Fantasy XIII is linear in nature for most of the story, but this is generally a good thing and was intentionally designed that way: the story always maintains good tempo and suspense.

NOTE: This game does *not* run well without modification, as Square Enix did a very sloppy job porting it. Thankfully this is fixed by unofficial fixes and mods - you can find information on those here: https://docs.google.com/document/d/19DSne5vsSVdbf_gS-fnD377drWX4g4evCFUKy61sR_E/edit?usp=sharing .

Pros:
- Incredible graphics (this was released in 2009 and still looks better than most games made in 2021)
- Great story and soundtrack
- Awesome characters and battles

Cons:
- Be aware that much of this game is very linear in progression; while this is in many ways a good thing as it helps to maintain the tempo of the story, it doesn't always appeal to players who come in expecting a super open world style RPG.
- Square Enix did a very poor job porting this game; it does not run well at all out of the box from Steam. While this can be easily fixed with a few unofficial mods, it is worth mentioning firstly to make sure people are aware, and secondly to point out that there's really no excuse for this sloppy of a port.

Note that console versions of FFXIII are locked to 1080p and 30FPS, so playing it on Steam with mods and fixes is much better performance wise as you can run it at 4K with 60FPS (and optionally, improved textures and character models).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing soundtrack
Beautiful cutscenes
Fun battle system (even with the way they drip feed the mechanics/lvl caps)
Enemy/Boss/Summon designs are great even with a few palette swaps

The level design is where this game gets most of its hate.
No denying it, first half of the game(Took me 25 hours) is mostly hallways/corridors where there's barely any choice to go off on your own and explore. Usually there is a branch or two with some enemy guarding an item that's a bit stronger than the enemies on the MAIN path. Feels like a long drawn out tutorial (Frequently locking your leveling progress behind story bosses and such)
The world opens up a bit more later but do not expect any major "open world" gameplay as you play through the story.
Most of the stuff here is endgame/postgame challenges for achievement hunters/completionists. I'm looking forward to tackling some of them.
The locations look beautiful (especially some of those HALLWAY sections) for a decade old game.
The actual MAP menu screen/Minimap on HUD is trash tho, this thing can get you lost even in straight hallways -_-

Overall the fun stuff outweighed the level design/map menu issues for me (50 hours doing the main story and some of the sidestuff + getting lost sometimes)

Good game.
Great story/characters.
Try it out on a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
