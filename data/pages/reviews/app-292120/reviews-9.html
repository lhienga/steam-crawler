\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story is fine enough, the battle system makes me want to fall asleep. *SPOILERS* The game's super-bosses are giant turtles, and the only way to take them down is by using Vanille's death spell, which is RNG and has a 1% chance of landing. You NEED to farm these stupid turtles for a rare drop to upgrade your character's weapons to their final forms. You will NOT get Ultimate Weapons for your characters in a "normal" playthrough of this game. It heavily relies on the player grinding CP (this games form of EXP Currency) to level up the Sphere Grid; I mean, Chrystarium. I SPENT almost 150 hours with this game, I gave it a good try, but this is my second playthrough in a decade and those stupid turtles at the end; it all came flashing back to me. Why DID I put FFXIII down? That Turtle Farm. Nobody has enough hours to spam death at undying turtles only for them to drop nothing, time and time again.

Another thing that bothers me; although your characters stats all break the 9999 cap, you never feel truly powerful. You invest so much time and effort and CP into levelling up your characters strength, HP and magic, but it never feels satisfying. You're still doing crap damage, healing for too little, and dying to the same old enemies. Half your party has abysmal stats that cannot be fixed with equipment or levelling, and with the exception of boss fights that require a certain role, you never use half your roster.

Don't get me wrong, I like this game, I enjoy the characters and the writing, and I had FUN playing it. But I'm gonna be honest with you, you'll probably burn out on the turtle farm, and never hold an Ultimate Weapon.

If you wanna play it for the story and have no interest in the post game clean-up, then yeah, pick it up. If you think you can get all the achievements, you'll need 6 Trapezohedrons. You likely won't get even one in an honest playthrough. It's deliberate, gruelling, turtle farming.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPrefacing this review by saying that I played it with a the FF13fix mod, which resolved a lot of technical and performance issues.

Final Fantasy XIII is a game that is 75% masterpiece, in that every good thing it does has to be qualified somehow. The battle system is my favorite take on ATB short of FFVIIR, but it trades direct control of party members for indirect combat paradigms which take some getting used to. There's a lot of interesting tactical decisions regarding positioning in combat, but you have almost no control over your own team's positioning. The game is absolutely gorgeous, but it's definitely got that "early 7th gen" shininess on everything and the UI is stuck at an ugly looking 720p. The whole game is lavishly produced, but it often chooses to indulge in it rather than move on to the next plot beat. The game's story is phenomenal, but some of the characters can be annoying.

It's a game that breaks with series tradition somewhat. If you come in here expecting lots of side quests and exploring towns you'll likely be disappointed. But if you engage with what FFXIII is and is trying to be, rather than how it relates to similar titles, you're in for a treat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I've never understood the fandom's hate for this game.

I have my own problems with it: I absolutely hate the Gran Pulse section and have since this game released way back in 2009. I hate that the supporting cast is next to nonexistent (though, 13 was hardly the first FF game to do this). I hate that the writing is sometimes so ham-fisted that the characters basically straight up look into the camera and tell you their motivation. I hate that sometimes you leave a cutscene only to take two steps forward and enter another. I hate that when your party leader dies, you get a game over.

On that note, many of the boss fights in this game are cheap as hell. Major offenders are Barthandelus and Cid Raines, though they're hardly the only ones. Every time I think I want to play this game I just remember some of the eidolon fights. If you take too long to beat a boss they cast Death on your party leader, just for the lulz, I'm sure. Even some of the regular encounters can kill you pretty easily if you're just one second too slow switching paradigms, or if you have the wrong characters in your party (pre-GP, of course). I'm very glad the sequel got rid of the "leader dies, game over" thing. I imagine complaints of FF12 being too grindy and meat-gate-ish inspired the battle system of this game, which does not encourage grinding at all, in spite of the fact that it has an entire section devoted to grinding. Consequently, it is the worst part of the game, because the entire plot stalls out just so you can grind.

Have I mentioned I hate Gran Pulse?

Anyway, as a silly FF game, FF13 succeeds. I ain't gonna lie, this was one of my favorite games for a long time and I still love it. Yeah there's a lot it does wrong, but to me this is the last mainline game in the series that really captured the old FF feel. I know everyone complains about the linearity, but that assumes every other game in the series didn't also railroad you to your next objective - even faux-open-world FF12 did this. The illusion of freedom is not actually freedom. If you want freedom in a game, I don't know why tf you'd pick up a JRPG of all things.

I will not ever say that this game has great writing, as much as I enjoy the characters and story; but at the same time, it really had that FF pacing down pat during the Cocoon section. It never lets you take a break and that's something I really enjoy about earlier FF games. You never had room to breathe and process wtf you just witnessed, nor did you have time to heal up before a particularly grueling dungeon or boss fight. FF13 sort of mitigates this with an auto-heal feature after battles, but the frenetic pace remains.

It is also incredibly good at hitting emotional highs and many of the set-pieces are appropriately climactic and spectacular. The music is absolutely divine and imo is one of the best soundtracks in the series. I also think this was the last FF to have actual decent character writing, simplistic, hammy, and on-the-nose as it sometimes is; the core cast is bizarrely well-rounded for the cast of an FF game. I love everybody's subplots and character development, and the interactions between the characters feel very organic in a way the other games in the series never achieved. It helps that the English voice cast is amazing. The character chemistry is phenomenal, and I still love the dynamics between Sazh and Vanille, and Lightning and Hope.

I understand why people don't like this game, I really do. It's definitely not for everyone - no game really is. But Final Fantasy 13 is a game near and dear to my heart and I recommend it - if you can grab it on sale, of course.\t\t\t\t\t
