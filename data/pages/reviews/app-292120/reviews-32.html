\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my childhood game and first FF game that got me into JRPGs at the age of around 10. I have never completed this game when I first played this (I sucked and died alot), but playing through this game again nearly 12 years later is bringing so much nostalgia of discovering the JRPG genre.
---------------------------------------------------------------
completed game 2023-06-02

spoiler-free (story wise) review update!

While I was ecstatic to play this game again, nostalgia can only mean so much in determining if you genuinely enjoyed the game or not. FF13 has some problems and issues throughout the game that should be highlighted.

1. The games UI did not age very well, specifically the world map system.
There is no way of navigating the map for the location name, you physically have to be in that location or need to mentally remember the location names; yes there are guides or websites that have mapped all the locations (thank goodness), but this shouldn't be needed for traversing a map in game, especially for a "casual" playthrough.

2. Am I blind or is the map 99.9% transparent?
Who thought a transparent map was a good visual design choice? The use of "soft" colours were visually really hard to see, especially the mission marks.

3. Poor side mission introduction and integration.
Why are "missions" introduced half way through the story? It suddenly went to a linear story in the first half to a semi-open "area", it negatively affects the overall pace of the game, where suddenly there is side quests to complete, it feels it was added last minute because the majority of the side missions are not necessary except for endgame (post story) items.

That is all I can think of for now, I will add more points if I come up with any.

Thank you for reading this review!
