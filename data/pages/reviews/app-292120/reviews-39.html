\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFF XIII isn't technically difficult to beat, but it's such a painfully bad game in so many respects, that playing to the end feels nearly impossible.

There are no towns to explore in this game, no NPCs to interact with. There are no sprawling environments to wander through. There aren't even bad puzzles to get stuck on. No, there are only narrow corridors - you press forward on the joystick, your character moves forward, and you watch bad cutscenes and undergo braindead turn-based combat for 50 hours. This is the only game I've ever played that gave me a visceral sense of claustrophobia - just so many corridors.

Yes, true, there is one open area where you can hunt special monsters after the 25 hour mark. But after you realize that each mission marker you find requires you to traverse across the same open area for 5 minutes for each monster hunt, then backtrack again for another mission, you give up on the monster hunts. And go right back to the corridors.

As for the combat, wow, so many awful decisions. You only control one character in a party of 3, and the game gives you an "auto" button that chooses your moves for you (so you effectively control no one). And unlike in XII, you can't influence how your teammates prioritize actions, so they end up constantly making bad decisions (because the AI in this game, like almost everything else, is trash).

The only thing you can influence in combat is the role each character plays at any given time (healer, dmg mage, etc.). You can swap back and forth between roles, which is.... somewhat interesting sometimes? But even the mode-switching is undermined by the several seconds it takes to switch in-combat from one mode to another.

The kernal of the story is good and interesting, but the game hides important plot points and character motivations in text data logs that you have to read to understand why a "plot twist" remotely makes sense. Squenix has hours and hours of cutscene footage, yet still was incapable of creating a comprehensible plot without the need of... text data logs. And even if you do read the datalogs, the game's story becomes progressively worse and nonsensical.

The dialogue is the worst of any FF game. With the exception of Sazh and Fang, the protagonists range from uninteresting (Lightning) to worst-in-the-franchise (Hope). Meanwhile, everything about Snow's character design is a bad dream from the 360 era.

The few things XIII gets right are on-the-fringes improvements. It's nice to restart after a game over at the enemy you just died to, so you lose no progress (you can also manually "auto-lose" instead of waiting to die). Regaining full health after each fight is a good QOL improvement. Not futzing with mana resource management is a welcome change. And... the final boss looks cool?

In line with the other "dumbing down" aspects of XIII, your equipment options are limited to only a weapon and accessories. You use monster loot to level up items, but are given no indication of when you're hitting an item's max exp, so you end up either leveling up little-by-little (which is tedious), or using a ton of loot for a huge XP gain and risk wasting a ton of loot when you've already hit the item's cap (which is obnoxious).

And then there's the leveling system.... Unlike in XII (or to a lesser extent, X), future leveling nodes are hidden from you, so there's no way to plan out a character's leveling path. You just choose 3 roles and commit all XP to those roles. No thinking required.

Man, what an impressively bad game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was quite the spectacle when it launched, and is still amazing and has excellent choreography and action scenes. The story was great, and each of the characters were really well done.

The combat was a bit of a pain, mostly with the tank class taking aggro and not keeping distance from my DPS, dragging them into the AoEs. Was a pain during the last fight as I had to keep swapping the tank's class to force it to reposition itself away from the others. The class system was also a bit restrictive as it's clear what roles each character should be fulfilling, so there's not really a lot of freedom there.

The biggest issue I think was the end game. The game opens up a little and allows you to free roam a bit, but only in the blandest area imaginable.

An impressive game though and worth playing through for any final fantasy fan.
The sequels are interesting too and worth trying out.\t\t\t\t\t
