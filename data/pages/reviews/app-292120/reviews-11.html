\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually post reviews, only when I feel really strongly about a game either way and FFXIII is a game that makes me feel very strongly about it.
A little background, I'm a huge fan of rpgs of all kinds, been obsessed with them since I was 8 with FFXIV on the SNES and I've played most of the FF titles.

Story 8/10
Gameplay 0/10
Music 8/10

Tl;Dr - Design was very innovative, but execution was poor and gameplay suffered horribly.

I absolutely hated the gameplay for FFXIII. Why? Because it had so much potential but never quite got it right (for me).
I normally really love when an rpg comes out with some really interesting new mechanics and FXIII tried a LOT of new things which felt like they could have been SO good. Time and again in this game square had great ideas but dropped the ball on execution.

A small list, some with comments:
Paradigm system wowed me at first but a few hours in and it honestly just became spam 1 or 2 modes, they don't really encourage you to use it fully.

Constantly switching between characters as you play through the game till you hit the last few chapters was a great idea for telling the story but it came with so many frustrations such as having to set your paradigm grid back every swap or how you really need to carefully distribute your materials between them unless you're willing to grind.

The extremely linear nature of the game design isn't new but it just feels so bad and the world seems less alive to me than in FFIV when you get to explore the world, visit towns and talk to people. This game was more like watch the action-y rpg movie rathe rthan play it.

Super boring map design.

Focus character dying is game over - this is just silly when groups of enemies can dogpile one person so easily...combined with

Very little means of increasing defense - there's only a few ways to decrease damage, mostly accessories and buffs. There's no "defensive" stats to improve. All character improvement for survivability comes from a deep health pool which feels bad (at least it does to me).

Random battles do not drop gil - this makes resource management kind of important until end game, only human enemies are a source of gil from valuable item drops that you have to sell. This was a really weird decision.

Crystarium - the primary means of improving characters at first it seems really cool with lots of choices but it's really just get enough points to get everything which you can do very fast and it's all just illusion of choice.

Item upgrades- seemed really neat at first but it's really simple just keep feeding your items to make them grow nothing interesting ultimately.

In conclusion, I am really sad that the gameplay for this entry was so boring, or frustrating, it didn't live up to the potential it has. I really liked the story but it was painful to actually go through it. I wanted it to be better. Hopefully, part 2 and 3 are better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFFXIII trilogy is a mixed bag.

FFXIII is a really good final fantasy game on it's own. It also runs well on the steam deck and looks stunning for a 15 year old game, and also ends on a bittersweet high note, like most good stories. The gameplay is boring at times and the story gets dragged out far too often, but the game is easily beatable in 30+ hours on normal difficulty without rushing.

I would recommend people that are new to FFXIII to stop playing here. Kind of like the Matrix trilogy it's all downhill after the first one.


[The Rest of this Review is Spoilers!!!!]


FFXIII-2 retcons the ending of the first game in order to justify its existence, using time travel and alternate timelines as the McGuffin while simultaneously spending most of the game trying to explain the McGuffin. The whole game reeks of bad writing. There are only a handful of playfields in the game, but multiple versions of them in different timelines/time periods. That said, the second game is faster paced and doesn't have a lot of filler content, so at least it's got something going for it - but it ends on a contrived low note to justify the 3rd game: Lightning Returns.

Lightning Returns deserves to exist even less than the second game, and was a rushed messed with a recycled religious story about God's savior collecting souls for a new creation because the current reality is too broken to repair. It's the weakest Final Fantasy game I've ever played, to the point where I watched a condensed playthrough on Youtube rather than finish it myself, just so I'd never have to touch it again. I'd recommend you do the same, it's just not worth playing. The fighting is repetitive and button mashing, the voice acting is flat, the story is recycled/plagiarized religious dogma, the game world is 100% recycled assets. The cut scenes are the only thing good about Lightning Returns.

--------------------------

In conclusion, FFXIII-2 and Lightning Returns are the reason why many older fans of the franchise have a low view of FFXIII. If the sequels did not exist, FFXIII would be regarded as one of the best. The world building, the character development, the setting, the plot, the ending, etc in the first game are high quality and live up to the Final Fantasy name. I would consider FFXIII-2 and Lightning Returns to be alternate reality spin-offs, at best.

So if you haven't played the sequels and are enjoying FFXIII, just know that the sequels are not really worth it. Just pretend the story ends after the first game and be happier for it. Part 2 and 3 were phoned-in cash grabs.\t\t\t\t\t
