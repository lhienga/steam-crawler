\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor context, with the completion of this title, I've completed every mainline Final Fantasy (minus sequels). I think this game was judged far too harshly when it first came out. I think tastes in gaming (my own included) have changed a lot since 2009. The linearity was really frowned upon. Big open worlds were the new hot thing on the scene. But now, we've all gotten a little tired of bloated open worlds in the last 10 years. So coming back to this one over a decade later hits a bit different. I loved the combat personally, as divisive as it is. It was something different from the ones that came before and it acted as a smooth transition to XV and FF7Remake's combat. With HD mods and an Upscaled FMV replacer this game looks great and doesn't look or feel like it's 12 years old.

TL;DR
This game had a rough launch. Square Enix's new ingenuity? Or a sign they lost the plot?
Today the picture is more clear. They always push to innovate and try new things. Sometimes they fail, but they "take destiny into their own hands" with each title. FFXIII deserves another chance in any Final Fantasy nerd's library.\t\t\t\t\t
