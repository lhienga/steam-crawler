\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStyle over substance.

Between Final Fantasy IX, X, X-II and XII by far the weakest. The largest portion of the game is one overly long tutorial along linear corridors, slowly introducing and expanding mechanics while also failing to properly explain most of them (hidden modifiers for upgrades, weapon modifiers of unknown strength, hidden synergies).

When the game finally does pretend to open up, it just gives you large plains with corridors extending from it. Still requiring a specific order of things to be done to proceed while allowing you to walk the majority of the path before hitting a progress block and having to walk all the way back because you did not do the right thing to activate teleport points.

Then, when it's finally done adding things and tutorials, you would think the game actually starts and takes off - but that's where the poor story already ends. There is probably twice as much 'content' if you go for all the optional bounties and grind, but I could not be bothered.

The story moves at a glacial pace, repeating the same key points over and over and over while doing little to nothing to flesh out the characters. By the time the story reaches its climax I honestly thought I was halfway through the game, with everything being set up and the characters walking into a trap that would lead into the final, big act where things would actually happen. But it was actually the end.

Similar to FFXII the enemies are actual visible beings in the world that can thus be avoided - which is great and let's you decide when to fight. But this also causes the level design to be so needlessly grandiose and full of empty space, forcing you to walk vast lengths without adding anything and not feeling like it's part of the world building. Merely a requirement to make the exploration and combat go together.

Combat is by far the strongest part of the game (and you'll be spending the majority of the game doing just that), allowing you to focus on macromanaging the party instead of micromanaging like in earlier games. Switching combat paradigms on the fly preempting the enemy abilities is a lot of fun. But not enough to let me overlook the weak story, using countless words to say nothing while trying to sound epic and dramatic.\t\t\t\t\t
