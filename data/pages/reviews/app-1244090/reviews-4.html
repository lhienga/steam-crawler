Sea of Stars review
(I'll just start by saying, I got both endings and 100% completed all content (according to Steam achievements)

10/10 Overall graphics - Wow, just wow. The overall visuals (maps, sprites, animations) are top of the line, hands down the best in 2D games imo. That title was long held by Suikoden 2, congrats on becoming the visuals champ!

10/10 Map layout. It wasn't hard to know exactly where you were going. I don't think I got lost a single time in the entire playthrough, this is really really impressive.

10/10 Map graphics - Absolute best map graphics. No complaints.

9/10 Movie animations - These were very well done and very enjoyable to watch. The only thing that I would note is that some seem more polished than others in terms of line art and the drone view of brisk seemed like a 3D map screen shot which was weird.

10/10 Sprite animations and art - Again, just wow. Some of the best in the history of 2D JRPG's. One thing to note is a very sloppy mistake with Garls walking sprite. While facing forward he has 1 eye, but when facing left, he miraculously has 2. The artist just flipped the right facing frames to use as the left facing frames and forgot that little detail. It would take 5 minutes to fix. That's a bad mistake to make on a main character.

5/10 Gameplay - I though nearly all of the gameplay was engaging and just the right level of difficulty and complexity. Unfortunately, the gameplay that wasn't was really bad. To complete every thing you have to do 11 quizzes twice, find 60 conches, play a "board game" that I don't care about, and look for recipes all over the place... these things don't respect the players time and pad the game with mind numbing content that's not fun in the least. It would be bad enough if doing these things was just for completionists, but to see the real ending you have to do some of them. This is a huge mistake in the design of the game. Additionally, I bought a JRPG, not a shmup. I don't like shmups even remotely, adding mandatory shmup battles is aggravating and definitely not welcome. All these things can be a good part of a good game, but none should block progression, endings or lore. It's too bad, because the map and usual battle gameplay elements are extremely well done, yet they're in the shadow of some rookie game dev mistakes. It's almost bazaar to see both extremes in one game.

2/10 Story - Oof, this one's not good... It's sad too, because the base laid at the beginning of the game has some real potential. I would say the problem isn't with the story itself, but rather with the execution or lack thereof. Some of the most egregious misses are with Garl.

-I can see that Garl is supposed to be SamWise Gamgee, and in a lot of ways it works. But the writer forgot one crucial element. His fatal flaw. Garl is supposed to be Sam, but instead he winds up being some weird Jesus figure. Sam was amazing and kind and loyal and a cook, BUT! He couldn't find it in his heart to show Shmegal even a shred of compassion, despite pleas from Frodo. This rounds Sam's character and helps the viewer become attached to him. Garl on the other hand is just weird. No one is perfect like that, no one is perfect all the time. He's an odd character that just rubs you the wrong way. I spent most of the game until he died convinced he must the be main antagonist.

-Garl, again... His death arch is great, I love it... except for one crucial detail, the timing. He's killed off too early in the game. Like I said previously, his character is still (and unfortunately this is never resolved) underdeveloped. So the player has little attachment to him. By the time I got to bring him back, I had already forgotten about him and honestly didn't care if he stayed dead or not. He should have been in the party until close to the end, and given a flaw to make him relatable. Garl was a big miss unfortunately.

-Overall story arch. The story is good, a very good premise to work with. But it clumsly starts threads and then leaves most of them loose. You don't feel closure that the end of the game. I think what the writer was attempting here was to leave an air of mystery around each character, which I agree should be done, but it wasn't executed properly and it was overused. For instance, frequently, during a cutscene, a character will speak up, demand answers or be about to reveal something and another character will rudely interrupt them for seemingly no good reason at all and that's it, conversation has shut down. This is annoying and not pleasant to follow in itself, but to add to it, most of those cases have 0 resolution after words. So many things are left unexplained. Like a bunch a little started stories with no ending. It leaves the player feeling very unfulfilled at the end of the game.

-The ending. How could so little effort and care be put into an game that was clearly lovingly and painstakingly created? Did you guys run out of time? This is when you could have resolved some things so easily! I expected to see a lot of those swinging loose ends tied off, but that's not what happened. Not only did that not happen, but MORE questions are added (facepalm) Like what is Romeyra doing with the headmasters staff?! I feel like the game could have benefited from two main writers, so they could push back on each other and ensure the story flows well.

As a side note, the game is just too long. I had fun with the battles until about 85% of the way through, then I wished I could skip them. Too much of a good thing is too much. I'll be happy when more devs stop trying to make the 40 hour epic and instead put out good 10-15 hour experiences.

In conclusion. I think Sea of Stars is both a great game and a bad game all at the same time. I would recommend game devs play it for sure! It's a great game to take notes from and learn some things to do and some things not to do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not sure recommend this game to other players is the right thing to do. The pixel art is 5 stars and I like the story . But in order get the real ending , I will need to revisit all the area that I visited previously. If this is just another ordinary RPG which has the escape maze or fast travel spell/item, that would be fine. But there is no such thing in this game. If the item that you need locate in the middle of the area(maze) , after acquire that item , you either move backward back to the entrance or move forward to the exit. You can't just escape the area (maze) like other RPG.

I have the same feeling now, after playing almost to the end of the game only find out that in order get the real ending , I have to revisit every area. I feel I am stuck in the middle, If I want the real ending , then i have replay the boring area(maze) once again, which mean I have to spend double of time. Or I ignore the real ending which I will feel incomplete .

Although the pixel is 5 stars, but the portrait has huge room of improvement.

I can feel the developer spend a lot of time in the development , for example, the character has their unique action when they are idle or in the fire camp. Each area has it own unique art work.

Character has max level , so you can't just level up and pray that you can defeat the enemy. But you don't have a lot other option to increase your damage. Every level up , you have to choice which attribute to increase , this look like you can train your own monster. But the real fact is, the higher level you are , the less option you have. Which mean at the max level , the attributes are preset by the developer. Just which max out which attribute first is your choice.

My suggestion is have a walk through with you, make sure you collect everything in that area. So that you wouldn't need to rerun the whole game twice.

Conclusion:

Amazing pixel art , a lot of room for improvement for all other parts of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
