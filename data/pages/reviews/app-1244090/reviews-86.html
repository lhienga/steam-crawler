\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars

Score w/o Context: 17/20 Mistimed Button Presses

The Context:

Retro-inspired RPGs have an extra tough time justifying their existence in the realm of indie games. Trying to evoke similar feelings and emotions from a genre steeped in history inevitably invites comparisons to titans of the genre, fairly or not. Sea of Stars welcomes this, wearing its inspirations to Chrono Trigger, Secret of Mana, and others; much in the same vein that The Messenger flaunted its Castlevania and Ninja Gaiden roots. While I don't think it reaches those highs, it is still a delightful homage to that era of JRPG's.

Your call to action is the typical end of world/chosen one(s) dynamic, but the main characters don't do a lot to differentiate themselves from the background. This can still work! Figures like Cloud and Chrono act as blank slates for supporting characters to shine, but I found the duo protagonists to be more milquetoast than most. Thankfully, Garl exists. Garl is the cure-all to any character issues this game has, and the glue that carries this story with the help of a couple of other standouts.

The combat system apes on Mother 3, and Paper Mario with a timing based battle system where commands can reduce or increase damage if they are used at the correct time. While engaging, I found certain moves to be almost impossible to get correct without guessing at first, which led to some frustration on some moves. Still, the matching lock system which allows you to match damage types to stop enemies large attacks is particularly inspired, and allowed for fun mini puzzles within encounters, and no battle outside bosses took particularly long.

I would be remiss without mentioning the music and art-style, both of which are standouts. The pixel art consistently surprised me with its detail, and some of the OST will land on my playlist, even if it doesnt quite hit the highs of their previous OST. A solid sophomore effort that benefits from the framework left before them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first game I have been compelled to review, mostly in confusion as to the general tone of awe that the community has towards this game. This game is absolutely beautiful, to be sure. The environments are breathtaking, and the majority of the boss encounters are spectacles of the highest order. This game falls critically short in two areas, however - the character development and the combat. Many other reviewers have nailed that the two main characters are basically Mary Sue chosen one stand ins with no progression whatsoever throughout the entire game. The developers also clearly push Garl as the favorite character, but it is hard to maintain empathy for a character who calls an evil immortal a "loser" to his face just for the game to have a cool moment and set off one of the dumbest and most predictable story beats in this game. All of which pales in comparison to the true ending. I haven't been this mad at an ending since I finished reading the Mistborn series. We bait out the big bad, we beat the pozz out of him, and the Alchemist rescues him and smiles! The End! What the actual chocolate fudge?

The second giant elephant is the combat. Often, enemy locks will require uses of moves that require timing like Moonerang. I grew to dread moon locks because they would drag combat out a massive amount, and with food I never lost a single combat throughout the entire game except for a single arena encounter for the true ending.

All of this combines to mean that I finished this game thoroughly underwhelmed. Setting up a true ending after a lot of optional content sets the aftertaste for the game, and this game was completely ruined by its ending. I do not recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

Just take a moment whenever you can switch the time of day to take in shadows, the light effects, and switch of background between day and night versions to see the dedication to detail they had; or go to an in to hear how each song has an alternative version being played, and that talking to one of the pirates actually stops the instrument they play from sounding in the background.

The writing is... well, I don't think I have anything good to say about it, and I think it might be a serious deal breaker for some people (EDIT: To be honest, I am part of those "some people", and this point really made me question if I should give a negative review instead). The game simultaneously tells too much and too little. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely recommend. It's a fun, cozy, classic RPG with some elements you'll recognize from other RPGs as well as new elements. The gameplay is very solid -- Paper Mario fans will recognize the timed attack/defend options, which keep you engaged during combat, as well as the Mario & Luigi koopa shell hammer attack that one of the main character's possesses. There's a lot of fantastic attention to detail in the combat, too -- like being able to reposition enemies to make AoE attacks more effective, or environments affecting how attacks operate (for example, standing in water snuffs out a fire attacks' AoE capabilities, unlike standing on dry land). Battles happening in the world itself rather than cutting to a new place/background is a nice touch, too.

I really love the non-combat exploration elements, too, which have a flair of Metroidvania to them. You gain access to new items and abilities that help you solve puzzles and traverse the game world outside of the turn-based combat, like a wind gust and a grappling hook, which really scratches that Metroidvania itch and makes the game feel much more dynamic.

The in-game "Wheels" game is also pretty thought out and actually fun.I find myself looking forward to new opportunities to play the game when I enter a new area. It's a fun distraction, and helps lean into that aspect of the game alongside talking with NPCs, casually exploring, and hanging out around the campfire -- that the low moments are just as important as the high ones.

Speaking of that, I like the campsite feature, which feels like a big nod to SNES RPGs, too. Hearing what new thoughts your partymates have or what stories they have to tell is a nice treat.

The soundtrack is fantastic, as are the graphics. And I love that I can play the game with one hand on keyboard lol

The overall story is a typical fantasy story, but that seems purposeful. It appeals to fans of similar types of fantasy RPGs stories, but there's also a solid tongue-in-cheek/aware quality that gives the story more flavor. So far, I've been enjoying it, as well as the characters.

Ultimately, a great pick up, especially for this time of year. As a big fan of Chrono Trigger, Paper Mario, Mario & Luigi, Secret of Mana, and more, this has been a fantastic game so far, and one of my favorites that I've played recently. It's also not like $60 or $70 dollars so that was cool.

Looking forward to playing more!\t\t\t\t\t
