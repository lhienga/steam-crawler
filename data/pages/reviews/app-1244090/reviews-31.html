\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic JRPG Overall. The Story was surprisingly compelling. I normally dislike exposition and yet I found myself by the fire listening to the stories that Teaks would read me, adding worldbuilding and backstory to the world of Sea of Stars.
The combat is also surprisingly clever. It's always impressive to me when someone can put a fresh spin on turn-based combat, and Sea of Stars does just that. The Lock System to disrupt enemy casting makes you have to prioritize whether you want to prevent your team from being hit with a powerful spell, or focus on dealing damage, healing your team, etc. Plus add in a timing-based mechanic similar to Super Mario RPG, and overall It's a very good system.

And of course, I gotta talk about the art. It's just straight up phenomenal. The lighting engine is also mind-blowing for a 2d-rendered game, and I still can't wrap my head around the fact that there are boulder-shaped objects that you can roll around, and each one of those has to have like, 200 sprites each to be able to capture all the different angles that the ball can be at. The attention to detail in this game is insane. I can not praise it enough. There wasn't a single character or environment that felt half-baked or phoned in.

My only complaints are with the level progression and exploration, and they aren't dealbreakers by any means. Firstly, level progression. Stats and leveling don't seem to matter all that much. The level cap is 30, so don't expect to be able to grind extra levels, plus weapons and armor are given to you at a very steady pace that makes you feel like you don't really have much control over your team's level progression. Not only that, but by the end of the game there will still likely be certain enemies from the beginning of the game that you still won't be strong enough to 1HKO, even with endgame gear. It makes it feel like it was just tacked on to give you a feeling of progression, despite not having much control over it at all. My only piece of advice is to choose mana each time that you level up, since that stat is extremely restricted. As for exploration, you don't really get to go out and discover side quests until literally right before the end of the game. I wish that they gave you freedom earlier to be able to do more with the world, but it's very much a linear game despite having an open-ish world. I get why they didn't want to give you too much freedom, since traveling to certain areas too soon definitely could break the story progression, but older Final Fantasy tiles definitely gave you more of a sense of exploration.

Overall I'd give this game a solid 8.5 out of 10. Despite my complaints, I had a lot of fun. I finished in just under 50 hours, but most people will probably finish sooner. I really took my time with it, didn't quite 100% but explored naturally without guides and savored the game, because I knew I'd be sad when it's over.\t\t\t\t\t
