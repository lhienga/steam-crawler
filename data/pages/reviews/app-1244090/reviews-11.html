\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCount of Sabotage studios to give a good rep to spiritual successors.

Coming off from The Messenger which captured the hearts of Ninja Gaiden fans among many others for its gameplay, presentation but mostly attention to detail, this one for the Chrono Trigger ones does in essence the same thing. Basically, it's an RPG with turn based combat mechanics that utilizes rhythm to make attacks and defenses more effective much like titles in the vein of the Paper Mario series. This is considering the combo attacks that go in hand with a meter gauge that lets the player change its tactics accordingly. Also, changes in the day night cycle play their part as well but mostly in the overworld regarding exploration and puzzle platforming exploration.

Related to that the story follows the lines of Xenoblade Chronicles with its twists and characters that you get to know of as the story progresses. While the main ones may not leave the biggest mark on the player by the end, the ones that join your party as you go on certainly do. We get to witness their grievances, feelings, backstories and purpose before culminating in a satisfying manner as the last cutscene plays. Something of note is that this doesn't only apply to the ones on the good side, even though their representatives are way more fleshed out.

Regarding the presentation, it's no wonder the game had been hyping itself up from its early visuals. The pixel art is not only quite detailed but it immerses you into a fantasy setting by the way everything in the background moves according to the time of day. Every enemy has its well defined sprite as do the NPCs, dialogue boxes and different elements that you can interact with. But there are 2 points in particular where the visual aspect truly shines: the special attacks and the animated cutscenes dispersed here and there. It gives you enough glimpses of the way everything looks from the characters' perspective that it leaves you imagining the ones you don't.

And right with the presentation comes the auditive aspect both in sound effects and soundtrack. Those who have been fans of Eric Brown's earlier work in the ninja game will be pleased to know that this time the results really do this game's justice. While I'm not usually one for these RPG battle tracks I found myself getting won over by them up until the setting of the second half of the game kicked in and really grabbed me not only by surprise but in jam mode. And all of this accompanied by the sound effects mostly of attacks well timed and the way they land. Gives the player more of a way to tell how well they are performing.

Lastly, considering its earlier work, rest assured that you don't need to have played it before this one. That isn't to say I don't recommend doing so if you have the opportunity though as there are a lot of references to make of it, mostly in certain areas and regarding some lines spoken by the characters. There is even a really end game Easter egg that will probably lighten up your day should you choose to find it. That was certainly the way for me at the very least.

In the end, by capturing what made JRPGs so special back in the day and building up on what came after this one's certain for 2023's stacked GOTY list.\t\t\t\t\t
