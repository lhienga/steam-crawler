\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not super far into this game or anything, didn't even manage to beat the first dweller/just recruited Serai. Despite this, I just can't bring myself to keep playing.
The characters are bland, the story is mediocre and messy and the gameplay got stale about 2 hours in and isn't likely to get better going by the lack of new moves on the horizon or the weirdly designed combo system that almost doesn't allow for use of the combo moves outside of boss fights. Maybe more party members would improve on this, but I doubt it.

The music is fantastic and the areas are wonderfully designed (the waterfall area in particular is incredibly beautiful), there are also some genuinely awesome animations for the characters, not that it can salvage the writing of said characters, but it's still great. The puzzles you do throughout are also pretty decent. This sadly doesn't make up for the straight up bad combat system or the awful pacing of the story, which is coupled with characters that don't really have much personality.

I really wanted to like this game, having been excited for it ever since I heard that the makers of The Messenger were making a turn-based RPG, but what they've presented is a gorgeous game with almost nothing else going on. If you can stomach a battle system that practically never changes from hour 1 and don't mind a story that has nothing going on, then I'd say go for it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would like to start by saying that Sea of Stars is a beautiful game. For someone who loves pixel art and admires a dedication to create a living world, this is amazing.
Visuals, Sound, Story, and game length are all great.

However, after having played through about half the game, I find myself getting bored with the gameplay. I like the basic mechanics of the system. Having to match the right attacks to break your opponents cast works well. Where I get bored, is the lack of skill variation. Most of the game you only have 2-3 skills per character (later you have 4) and you don't upgrade/alter/affix these skills at all. You just increase your basic stats.
Early game, the game is difficult because of the lack of skills. However after the games 5-6 part, it gets very easy. I took out a main game boss without it ever getting to use its summoning mechanic. I didn't grind at all in the game either.
If there was some way to expand on the skills your character gets, or perhaps some freedom to build your character the way you want. The bonus stats at level up don't end up feeling like they make much of a difference.

Despite me complaining about the gameplay, I still think this is a good game, and money well spent. However I will struggle to complete this one. That is coming from someone who has beaten all the FF games, Lunar series, Chrono Trigger, ETC.

Buy it. I hope you don't get bored like I did.\t\t\t\t\t
