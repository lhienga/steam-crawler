\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is pretty great but go with the adequate mindset into it. This game does pretty much everything it tries to do very well, and I mean very well. However, due to games such as Baldur's Gate 3 showing the gaming community that player agency and expression and how the world reacts to it is very important, people might expect something like this from Sea of Stars. This isn't that type of experience. Everything is crafted in a very streamlined manner. Progression can be very linear and there isn't many room for creativity in this game. It's a great overall experience with plenty of content (and some great hidden content as well), just don't expect it to innovate any front or be a playground. It's a great tribute to older turn-based JRPGs and it does a surprisingly great job at it, despite a few hiccups. If you hop into this game expecting exactly that, you're going to have a great time probably. A very solid 7.5/10 game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Intro: Another new JRPG inspired by the classics, namely Chrono Trigger and Super Mario RPG. Nothing that there's anything wrong with that! Tis my favorite game genre, and this game has been on my radar and tickling my fancy since I saw the first preview!

The Good: Everything you like and expect is done very, very well. Beautiful graphics, flowing music, and a very clever twist added to the traditional JRPG combat system. It demands you to think about your actions carefully, lest you be dealt a lot of damage and die (I could explain it, but there's a demo, so you can check it out for free and experience it for yourself!)

The Bad: Like many new games in the classic JRPG style, the story starts nice, simple, and appealing. Naturally, it gets more convoluted the further you go, till you don't care about it. Still, you push on because the combat is great (like FF13, which I will always defend).

Overall: 8/10.
Outstanding graphics, sound, and gameplay.
Damn good game, and definitely worth my time and money. Check it out if you want a good ol' JRPG. Just don't expect a coherent story.\t\t\t\t\t

The characters are not mere avatars; they are vibrant, relatable beings with depth and personality. Each one, from the brave protagonists to the intriguing NPCs, contributes significantly to the immersive experience. I found myself emotionally invested in their fates, a testament to the game's exceptional storytelling.

Visually, Sea of Stars is a feast for the eyes. The meticulously crafted pixel art landscapes, rich in detail and color, create a world that feels alive. And the soundtrack! Exploring the world, solving puzzles, and uncovering hidden secrets felt rewarding and kept me thoroughly engaged. The game's progression system is well-designed, allowing for meaningful character growth and customization.

In conclusion, Sea of Stars has not only rekindled my love for the golden era of RPGs but has also set a new standard for the genre. If you're a fan of unforgettable characters, gripping stories, breathtaking visuals, and soul-stirring music, Sea of Stars is a must-play. It doesn't just revive nostalgia; it redefines it. An At a Glance Bullet List Review

What I liked:
  • Nostalgia-like graphics but with modern touches such as movement and fine details.
  • Turn based combat with plenty of time to agonize or decide what tactic and attack to use
  • Unique and vibrant settings/level/area designs that make each one memorable
  • Tutorial goes in-depth with controller/PC controls for battle and explains a bit about how to time attacks for bonus damage, how to combo, how to power up, and how to recover from a KO.
  • The Relic system which adds several fantastic options to customize game play how you like it. Love to go hard-mode? There's a relic for that. Want it more story-telling? Relic. Bad hand-eye coordination? Relic.


What I didn't like:
  • The beginning of this game's pace suffers a bit from too much narrative that gets tiring quickly. While setting up the world and story IS important, I feel like the beginning of this game some how missed the beat of the rest of the game. Once you're out of the tutorial/beginning, it's fast-paced and great, but the beginning might be a turn off for many who don't want to slog through pressing A/Enter to get through conversations.
  • I'm 45 years old and just not spry. On-top of that, twitch/responses/timing have never been great. So I need to use the Relic to always ensure I get that extra attack in, however, that lowers the bonus damage a significant amount. I think punishing players for being slower/hand mobility issues/motor control is a bit weird as they give out a relic to help but then makes it basically useless for bonus damage
  • Enemies begin to be a bit repetitive, but given I do not know the time crunch, how much $ they had to design them, etc, etc, I understand the reasoning to re-use the same skin, give it a different color and re-use attacks. Your mileage about how this does or doesn't annoy you may vary.


Do I recommend it?
Yes! YES, AGAIN, IN ALL CAPS. If nostalgia games are your jam, if you loved Chrono Trigger and Secret of Mana, yes. Even if you don't, if you want a good RPG with turn based battles and can get a demo/try it on someone elses' PC or console, I highly suggest going for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8 hours into gameplay
Ok to start of this story has a very clear plot line to follow, and I love that.

With the mix and aspiration of continuing to explore the areas you'll unlock, finding out all of your abilities and the fighting gameplay gives you a sense of thrill while slowly understanding the combat mechanics.

The fights are turn based, a simple yet very satisfying combat. I don't wish to spoil anything as this is a game you need to play to understand, but I will say it's been a while since I was this immersed into a game.

It's a little slow in the start, but it's good slow. Letting you warm up to the story and onto it's current ongoing path, introduced to the comrades you'll fight alongside. More I won't say

Get the game,immerse yourself and enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t
