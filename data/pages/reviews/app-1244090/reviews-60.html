\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs soon as I tried the demo for this game, I knew I needed to play it!
The game gave me the feeling of a modern SNES game, and I love it. I grew up with the SNES, so this hit home in a way I haven't experienced in a good while. The pixel graphics together with the sound design made for a lovely experience all the way through.
The gameplay had the sweet balance of being simple in concept and allowed for a higher skill ceiling for those who sought it. And for those who wanted an easier way through, the game offered a unique way to customize the specific areas you wanted to be easier in the way of relics collected in the game, instead of the usual set difficulty settings.
The story was a delight all way through. Characters felt unique, and all of them had their own time to shine. The game had its fair share of obvious twists just waiting to happen, but many of them were intentional and delivered in great fashion.
The fact that they also had some Easter eggs for those who had played their first game, The Messenger, was also a neat detail.
This is definitely one of, if not The best game I've played this year!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best RPG released in the last 5 years. the visuals are gorgeous and have quickly become the background not only on my personal computer, but my work one too. Every animation pops with personality, every island is teeming with life, every pixel feels alive. The combat is fluid, easy to understand and fun to master, with plenty of ways to modify it for the sake of accessibility witch I find great. The story is just as convoluted as you would expect from a pixel art JRPG, and I love it! But, by far the *Best* aspect of sea of stars is it's characters! everyone our protagonists meet is so multi-faceted and human, they all sport unique and distinguishable styles of speech and silhouettes, making every random NPC you encounter feel so much more real. My only real gripe with the game is that the main characters - Valarie and Zale - are a little white bread when it comes to personality, it can get a bit jarring to see all of our party members - just Breathing personality and uniqueness - and then there's just valarie and Zale sitting there like "let's save the world cuz it's the right thing to do!" y'know? Although there is a part of the game that tries to humanise them by adding a bit of tradgedy to their lives, but they don't dwell in the character's head space after that event for it to be anywhere near impactful as a character moment for Val and Zale as I feel it should've. Sabotage Studio, you definately have created a marvelous piece of art out of pure passion for the industry and genre, and it was a welcome breath of fresh air, I can't wait to see what other marvellous projects you have in store for us next!\t\t\t\t\t
