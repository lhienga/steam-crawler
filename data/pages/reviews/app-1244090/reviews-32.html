\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who typically doesn't enjoy turn-based RPGs, this game is fantastic. The pixel art is just beautiful. The soundtrack is full of catchy earworms (shout out to RainbowDragonEyes on his return after The Messenger.) The puzzles were well spaced and while maybe a little on the easy side, they never felt repetative and remained fun. The writing was a joy and the world expanded upon from The Messenger left me wondering what other plans Sabotage has in store for this universe.

The most common sticking point I see people talk about is how basic the combat is. Frankly it felt just right for me. Each character has 4 individual moves and a larger pool of combos shared with other party members. While there a few moves that I certainly favored over others (moonarang and disorient, namely) I felt every ability had good use cases. Turn-based combat in RPGs is usually rather boring to me but the timing and lock features were just the right thing to keep me engaged.

IMO Sabotage Studios is 2 for 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a phenomenal retro turned based RPG game. To me it seems like a mixed inspiration of FF Mystic Quest and Mario RPG for the SNES. Great soundtrack, captivating story line and fun gameplay mechanics. The game is relatively easy, even if you're not familiar with this style of game, you'll be eased into it. If you're an avid fan of retro RPGs, the beginning is a bit slow paced, but once you've cleared the starting zone and unlocked all of the fighting mechanics, it does pick up pace. The game does have some completionist elements to it if you're looking to play beyond the main story line. I highly recommend this game for both people who are newer to this genre and those who are veterans.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm in love with this game, easily one of my favorites ones ever.

Catchy music, beautiful art style, fair (and adjustable) difficulty and addictive combat. The story may be simple but it's very appealing and the narrative is flawless. It has some plot twists, a few funny moments and also really sad ones if you really get into the game's universe (I wasn't expecting to cry, but I miserably failed).

EDIT: After beating the game, I must say that the ending is good, but kinda disappointing. It just feels incomplete and your expectations will likely be broken. Also, the final dialogs/events are really rushed. This game has a really great story and it deserves a more detailed ending after the final boss.

The "true ending" doesn't really fix these problems, but you get a really cool and long boss fight. Unfortuantely, you'll find yourself discouraged to do it if you are not a completionist, because in order to get the true ending you must do a series of taks which WILL take you few hours to complete. Fortunately for me, I had already done all tasks beforehand, so I could do both endings one after the other.

I complained about the ending, but that's the only bad thing about the game. It's still a great experience and I enjoyed every hour of it.\t\t\t\t\t
