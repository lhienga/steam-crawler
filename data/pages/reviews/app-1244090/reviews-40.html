\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars deserves all the awards it has received. This game is something special & charming.

* An engaging story with unique characters all around. Lots of positivity from your main characters. The flashback shortly after you start isn't just a "here's some filler backstory" but is meaningful backstory and catches right up to the moment it flashed back from, making the start of the game proper feel meaningful for the characters.
* The sound effects and their use for little details are tops. Even the book-on-pedestal save game device has the sound of pages flipping in time to the book opening & flipping pages.
* The music is fantastic. Shades of Crosscode, Stardew Valley, Hollow Knight, and even Cirque du Soleil's Totem. Delightfully melodic, and yes the combat music "Battle On" song easily gets stuck in your head. The OST is available to purchase, 9 hours of music in 206 tracks.
* The skills & items aren't overwhelming. No bloat.
* The animations are top-notch.
* The graphics are excellent too, I love the gorgeous art style.
* Excellent use of 3-D space; from climbing up a ladder while passing behind a terrain bridge in the foreground (that you'll later walk across) to the background terrain seen in an area while you're up in a very high area.
* The Relics (items given & purchased, in your inventory menu) let you toggle gameplay features. You're given 3 when the system is introduced: one makes the combat easier, one makes combat harder, and the other gives visual feedback to your self-timed, bonus, melee attacks/blocks.
* The cooking system & UI are neat; crafting doesn't feel like a chore because the recipes you know are all in a list, and when you highlight one to see it then it shows each ingredient needed plus how many you currently have. This makes collecting resources in the world feel worthwhile.
* The fishing mini game at designated lakes is fun & engaging, without being annoying.

The turned-based combat's gimmicks are actually fun, enhance gameplay, and don't annoy.
1. When you do a basic melee attack, it will regenerate some of that character's mana (for the magic attacks of course) plus spill out life magic orbs.
2. Those life magic orbs "using magic without magic" any of your characters on their turn can press & hold 1 button to gather them up remotely (fun animation) and then another button to absorb them to buff their next attack. That process is quick, easy, fluid, and looks & feels good. (On a PS gamepad you hold R2 then press X to gather & absorb.)
3. When doing a melee (non-magical) attack, you can press a button when the attack connects to trigger a follow-up attack.
4. When about to take melee damage, you can press that same button just as the attack connects to block and mitigate some of the incoming damage.
3 & 4 are optional, the NPC trainers tell you so, and both feel good when you get the timing right. They also enhance turn-based combat by giving you a bonus fun feature to engage with and without punishing you for not getting it. There is a Relic early on that you're given, that if you toggle it on will give you an extra graphical effect when you get the timing right on a hit or block.
5. Like Slay the Spire & Into the Breach, you get information on some of your enemies' next attacks so you can plan accordingly.
5.a. One way is the stopwatch icon above an enemy, the number shown is how many turns until they attack.
5.b. Sometimes an enemy will show several types of attacks (sword, hammer, sun, moon crescent...) in a UI row with a Power % which lets you plan your next attacks to counter this. If you attack with a matching icon (for damage type), it will negate that icon over their head and lower the damage of their next attack on you by some percent.
6. Some attacks have you press the interact button to make things engaging -- for Zane his Fireball you press & hold the button to charge it up a few stages before actually firing it, and for Valere's Moonerang boomerang attack you'll press it each time the boomerang comes back to you in order to bounce it back another enemy (it gets faster each bounce) so you can keep doing damage if you're bouncing it back with good timing.
7. You get the opportunity to "go back" when selecting your attack & which enemy to hit, up until you actually confirm the attack.

Plays well on the Steam Deck, and with a gamepad.

The game is absolutely charming in every way, and I look forward to dedicating the time to play it through until the end.


Inconsequential Quirks:
1. Some of the non-walking animations (tip-toeing on a tight-rope, climbing up a wall) are just a little off in speed from the movement speed of the character. (I may only notice because I've drawn pixel art characters for my own prototype side-scroller and had to pay attention to animation speed in comparison to character speed.)
2. Apparently in its first months there was an NPC based off a YouTuber (ew) I'd never heard of who turned out to be a not-great person, so that NPC was removed from the game. That was before I started playing and I don't know the individual nor situation, and so nothing was ruined nor improved for me (yay).\t\t\t\t\t
