\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe sense of wonder as the world and story is revealed to you is incredible. Crucially, I think that the lack of explanation of some elements was intentional, and it benefited my experience. The game gives you enough where, if you're actively engaging with the script, you can fill in the blanks like a child would with a children's fantasy story. The writers understand that imagination makes the world broader than they alone could make it.

My only complaint is that the playable characters are flat, particularly the two leads, who have so few personality traits, including those that would distinguish them from *one another*, that the two of them might as well be collapsed into a single silent protagonist. The REAL protagonist, the endlessly cheerful Garl, is fun only if you buy into his constant sense of wonder. Which I did, thankfully, but other players might not.

I cheered when anything cool or dumb happened. I cried multiple times. I laughed at all the Messenger nods. Top 3 of my favorite games of last year for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed my time with this game overall. The visuals are the most obvious selling point. It is among the best of pixel art I've experienced in any game and little touches like reflections in the water that ripple ever so slightly really sticks out in my mind. The soundtrack is definitely in my top 3 of 2023. The battle themes in particular are energetic and loop very well so they can be enjoyed for both short and long fights. Some of the later game areas have tracks that are stuck in my head to this day.

The combat is very fun, but not perfect. Many of the times hits don't feel like they hit when they should, and others have another vague timing. But there are other timed hits that feel very satisfying to do. The lock system is neat, while it did feel like eventually the game pushes you towards one optimal strategy the more you play. Challenges that restricted the characters that achieve this were usually more enjoyable that the standard battles due to this. On the flip side to this, some early game enemies are way too overwhelming due to lack of skills, often getting overwhelmed or feeling a need to backtrack to heal more often than I'd like until I acquire more skills/food.

The story is sadly overall a miss for me. While I've seen far harsher opinions on this, I was more confused than any other feeling. Some characters are written very well and I wanted to see and know more about them and their philosophies, but never really did. There is a controversial party member that is very much the definition of "too perfect", and it really felt sad that we couldn't see the character change in any meaningful way, even when the narrative itself set up for it. I don't want to embellish much further for the sake of spoilers, but the ending also felt unsatisfying and way too sudden, feeling incomplete. One might even be skeptical and call it "DLC bait". Which would be sad, because one of the many positives on the game is despite those complaints, feeling overall complete as a game. I had the most fun not with the story, or the RPG elements as much as I did just exploring maps and finding secrets. The puzzles in this game are very fun and clever. There were a few that stumped me for a bit because of the scope of where I was supposed to find a solution. The amount of secrets is very abundant and was a huge plus for me, since I loved the environments and area music.

Overall though, I really enjoyed many aspects of this game and while I do have a few criticisms, if a you want a beautiful game with e great soundtrack and fun puzzles and secret finding, this might be up your alley. They have caught my attention plenty, so I have started the Messenger and will be keeping an eye out for their next game.\t\t\t\t\t
