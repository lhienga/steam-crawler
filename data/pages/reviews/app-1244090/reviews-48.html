\r\n\t\t\t\t\t While I'm giving this a no for recommendation, it's mainly because I feel the game is more of an "eh, it's okay I guess" kind of game. I don't think it's worth the price it's at, personal opinion, so I'd say if you're gonna grab it get it while it's on sale. I wish a steam had a review option for games that are more of a maybe rather than just saying yes/no for recommending. I don't think i'm going to keep playing this game at this point. I bought it on a whim and I kinda wish I hadn't but what's done is done. Don't take my review too harshly, not like anyone will see it anyways, and give the game a try if you want. After all, steam gives refunds for less than 2 hours of game play, or watch some youtube videos if you don't mind minor spoilers.

As for more proper review talk:

The graphics aren't anything super special but pixel art is always pleasant to look at. Nothing crazy or out of the ordinary just good ol' pixel art. Some of the background animations could use a pause in their loops or a slight variant but other than that it's all good. There are some moments where the placement of characters can cause a bit of confusion, or just straight up clip off the screen, but this doesn't happen too often.

The characters themselves are pretty one dimensional in my opinion. They fit a stereotype/archetype and never expand beyond that. Maybe if I had gotten deeper into the game there would be some sort of character development but from what I experienced everyone is basically as they seem. There are /some/ questionable actions/motives for characters that are either done poorly, set up as "we'll tell you later", or just don't make any sense and they'd throw me for a loop when I noticed them.

The music for the game is... interesting. Sometimes it fits the theme or atmosphere, sometimes it's playing a weird techno bop while you're doing something mundane or during a serious moment. I don't understand the reasoning behind this. Taking the music at face value though a lot of it is quiet enjoyable, I'd find myself bobbing my head to the beats on more than one occasion.

The Story is basically "the power of friendship" kinda story. It's not as bad as that though but it's relatively close. That said, the beginning of the game has weird pacing. You start off going straight into combat and exploring a small area, getting you used to the games mechanics, but then you're suddenly ripped away from it and given flash backs and dialogue galore. This is not done strictly as a cut scene but the type where you HAVE to interact. Go talk to X, cut scene, go get thing from Y, cut scene, go to your room to sleep, cut scene, go meet Z at A, cut scene. Really killed my initial fun for the game, don't give me combat and exploration just to suddenly railroad me into a lore/info dump.

As for the story it's self it what you'd expect from a game/genre like this. There are some minor "twists" that will happen but for the most part it's all predictable. The game leans more towards a lighthearted atmosphere and embraces the power of friendship, comradery and the bonds you make with people along the way. It has some darker undertones but nothing that is explored in great depth or detail, at least not that I noticed.
I don't want to spoil anything specific but I am not a big fan of games that have collectibles that turn out to be far more than just collectibles, that's just padding your game for more numbers.

Game mechanics are fairly simply and straight forward though also irritating. Starting with whats irritating; the game shows that you can time attacks/blocks for increased/decreased damage. It then tells you this is not a major game mechanic and not to worry about it if you can't pull it off. It will then turn around and have it where you can't properly utilize the other mechanic of the game, matching damage types, UNLESS you can pull off the timing mechanic. Granted, there is something called "Relics" which are just options you unlock by playing to make the game easier/harder, and auto-timing is one of them (at the cost of doing less damage). I don't get why this mechanic is executed/explained the way it is, is it important or is it not? Pick one, I shouldn't have to rely on a toggle system of easier or harder to enjoy the game.

Another irritating aspect is that you cannot develop muscle memory for many of the timing mechanics. Enemies/allies distancing isn't just visual it actually is factored into the time it takes for an ability/spell to connect. Some abilities that can hit multiple targets wont work because the game registers them as being too far apart even if they're right next to each other. The worst offender I found was a throwing mechanic for a certain character. The game says to time your input with the throw to throw again. This is a lie, you actually need to time your input with the climax of the JUMP. There is also no explanation on what abilities can be timed for increased damage. Charging the fire ball has a window where it will do the most damage, but over charging lowers that damage, but does timing the swing of the crescent strike thing do more damage or not? What about the pot? The multi slash? There is nothing to hint at or tell you if you're missing something.

On to the simple mechanic of matching damage types. If an enemy is about to use a spell/ability it will show what damage you need to deal to interrupt them and make them lose a turn. This works nicely and makes you think more about how you spend your mana and in what order you choose to attack. The are some encounters where the game rigs its self and makes it impossible to counter the enemy, which can be frustrating, but I can kinda understand why they do it.

All in all, I'd rate the game a solid 5/10. It's good enough to kill some time, but nothing worth writing home about.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall decent game. Great art directions and some great music in the OST. World building was also very solid (petered somewhat at the end IMO). Regardless it was a fun experience and to reach the true ending there was little difficulty to achieve it with the tools provided. I never felt like it was a collect-a-thon, which seems to be becoming a common trope in games with any amount of exploration. The battles were fun and I gradually fell into a rhythm that worked for me. It did get redundant however, and by the 3/4 in, there was not much to try in terms of abilities (I primarily ended up using 2-3 of the many combos because of its utility). In terms of negatives, it was mainly dialogue, as it felt flat and too positive at times. Some stuff in the ending still don't make too much sense to me, but that's subjective. Puzzle complexity was also too simple and did not require much thought. A greater variety and increasing complexity would have been nice. Also, I did not feel like there was an answer to Zale's prophecy (also subjective). Regardless, I had a great time and would encourage others to give it a try.
