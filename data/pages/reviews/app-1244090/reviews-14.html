\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game.

I was wary about trying it at first, because I saw people complaining that the writing was shallow. My takeaway is a little different. This story is simple and straightforward, a tale of friendship and good versus evil without any cynical grandstanding or soapboxing on real-world issues.

There are certainly plot holes, areas where the writing could have been tightened up or expanded upon. Characters jumping to wild, unsubstantiated conclusions about things that turn out to be right on the money. Obnoxious side characters that turn out to be more important than they have any right to be. Contrived payoffs that tie things up a little too neatly.

The game also has poignant moments with successes and failures, tragedies and victories, losses and betrayals, and brought me to manly tears on at least three separate occasions. The true ending satisfied me greatly, but though there is a diagetic hint system that you unlock late game, I still resorted to using a guide to find some of the things I needed in order to unlock it.

Mechanically, the system requires you to pay attention, and button-mashing will not get you far, even in battles you are seriously over-leveled and over-geared for. Some people see this as a negative, but I enjoyed it. Most character combat abilities and enemy attacks have a quick-time boost/guard mechanic that you will need to exploit unless you turn some of the difficulty options down.

The game also has diagetic difficulty settings, allowing you to adjust individual aspects of gameplay by enabling or disabling various 'relics' you find along the way. I played with most of these disabled, but the "quicktime confirmation" one seemed pretty indispensable to me, and considering how late in the game it is aquired, the "shop discount" one also seemed fair.

The game's power curve is shallow, and I finished the game at level 21 or so, feeling quite prepared.

Animations were nice, with some of the ultimate attacks being quite fun to watch (some more than others). Some of the characters really stand out as having been designed by separate people. I always felt like Teaks the historian, an early recurring character, looked like she was cut out of a different game and pasted here.

Environments are nicely done, though occasionally confusing in their design, where places you can or can't jump or walk being poorly defined. There were at least two things I missed because "I didn't realize that was a door."

Music is quite nice. Well-done with occasional standout tracks. There are light and dark versions of most themes, which is a nice touch considering the game's themes and how it is implemented.


My takeaway is this : Approach this game allowing it to be what it is. Surrender yourself to the story it is telling and don't expect it to step outside of itself. That's what I did, and I had a grand time.
