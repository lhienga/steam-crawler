Sea of Disappointment

Overview
Sea of Stars is a turn-based RPG that strives to hit notes of nostalgia with its graphics and various nods to Chrono Trigger, Paper Mario / Mario RPG, etc. While beautiful in presentation, it was so painfully average. It lacks in every other department as it failed to hook me in at all to actually want to continue playing the game.


Presentation
There is no denying Sea of Stars looks and sounds beautiful. Based on how it presents itself alone, it checked all the boxes for me. Gorgeous pixel art with great looking areas. Music and sound hit the right notes. Even the way the battles occurred on the map is designed to make you point at the screen “hey, just like Chrono Trigger!”

And here is the problem with drawing on nostalgia so plainly. As great as this game looks, I just thought, “Well, why wouldn’t I just play Chrono Trigger instead and probably have a better time?”

Story
So, I’m aware I am writing a review painfully early. I couldn’t really take much more, I was just not vibing with the game. Usually story or gameplay can carry a game for me if I’m not feeling one. Both let me down, and so I felt it was better to just put this game down. I then wondered, well maybe I was in a bad mood. Booted it up again today after I’ve been enjoying some other games lately. Nope, still actively disliked every second playing this game.

So – perhaps the story does pick up later. But it has been four hours and I’m bored out of my mind. Sure, sometimes setup is required. I loved Suikoden 5 – it had a long setup to establish the world and political climate present for the rest of the game. It was still interesting with some intriguing characters. Or take the obvious inspiration of Chrono Trigger for an example. Wow, it had you traveling back in time quite quickly, hooking you into the premise.

Now what does Sea of Stars do? Well, something, I guess. There is some kind of plot having to do with solstice warriors, I guess. But it sure takes its sweet time going anywhere. It’s very important to dedicate a lot of time to the two main protagonists time at school and sewing a very important cloth that I’m sure will eventually be relevant, right? Oh and there’s a super overly positive best friend that everybody loves so much that they can’t help but mope about him not being on screen when he’s away. For a side-kick character, he sure is more present than the actual main characters. A device that can work with silent protagonists but these wet blankets of main characters aren’t even mute! They’re just devoid of any kind of personality, apparently.

What about side characters? They exist, I guess. Can’t really tell you their names because they’re not memorable to me. Dialog is certainly something. Look, I love comedy and fourth wall breaking but whatever was going on here in Sea of Stars was just painful to read. The pirate character breaking the fourth wall by telling jokes about RPG tropes was just beating a dead horse of a joke into the ground, it was so self-aware that it was just not even slightly amusing. Again, I usually like self-aware humor. But something about it here just feels wrong.


Gameplay
Now, I was at least somewhat mildly curious about the story besides not really hooked into it. But then I realized I’d have to play this game for another 20+ hours just to get the rest of the story. And I thought to myself, “nah, I’m good” and quickly uninstalled it. So, what’s so unbearable about the gameplay?

It’s fine, for about four hours or so, I guess. The problem is you eventually realize combat is entirely meaningless. What you are doing now in the early game is still what you’re going to be doing in the end game. I was huffing some serious copium as I frantically researched the game. I eventually unlock a new skill right? There’s some kind of point to keep leveling up right? Chrono Trigger had all these techs and combos! Surely, Sea of Stars would follow suit.

Ha, ha, ha! Sure, combos exist, but they aren’t tied to level up. In fact you know those basic skills you start with and been using from the start like that moon boomerang? Hope you like that skill because you will be spamming that a lot. You’re not going to learn any other basic skills. Ultimate skills apparently become a thing later, but good luck using that outside of a boss fight. Combo attacks are nice at least, but seriously, I am so sick of that f*cking boomerang. Seeing it again all these months later pissed me off so much that I decided to finally leave a negative review and uninstall this.

Next time, try having your characters actually progress and become more interesting as the game goes on – both in story and gameplay. There is literally nothing to look forward too. Wow a whole stat increase to do 1 more damage! Amazing! Meanwhile, I would love some kind of new skill or something… sure there’s some new characters later (apparently) but I lost interest long ago and something tells me they will suffer the same problem of becoming dull with nothing new to learn as well.

Game runs fine.

Summary
Check out the demo. If you think you’d enjoy 30 hours of that combat without it ever getting any more interesting due to the utter lack of progression, then all the power to you. I really wish I could have liked this game but it was just not vibing with me. It definitely had potential but in the end I think I became a victim of nostalgic expectations and was expecting a product far better than what I got. It was just disappointingly average. It’s probably not a bad game, but I just rather spend my time playing something I’m actually excited to play. This one just feels like a chore trying to push myself forward to have the merit to review it. Well, I may not have finished it, but I reviewed it anyways as I think it also says something when I can’t bring myself to finish a game.\t\t\t\t\t
