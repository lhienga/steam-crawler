\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent RPG, fully worth the price of admission. Flavor wise this felt a lot like Golden Sun to me, and the Paper Mario style countdown turn based + timing battles is a great system. I like the lock system, and making sure you have enough combo / mana / character turns to beat the lock is a fun puzzle in the middle of combat. The art is beautiful and I really enjoyed most of the writing. For this type of art style, the atmosphere can get surprisingly creepy in places. The relic system is great for tuning the game the way you want to play it.

I have a few nitpicks, but nothing that would change my recommendation. The first ending isn't great, and tracking down everything for the true ending is tedious. There's a lot of focus on cooking food, but since it's just healing and doesn't provide bonuses, I didn't find it really useful until halfway through the game once you find a chest in an out of the way spot that makes it no longer take your action. Combat skills are pretty static and level ups / gear are just number go up. Felt like there was more room for worldbuilding - looking forward to DLC or a sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very beautiful and visually detailed game, but that's all it had going for it for the 3-ish hours I spent playing it. The characters were as deep as a puddle and the story was the platonic form of generic. The English translation was pretty bad - or was it the writing broadly? I can't tell tbh but it did seem like almost none of the dialogue had anything worthwhile to say, despite there being so very much of it.

While playing I would find myself wondering, "why do I care about these people? why does any of this matter? couldn't i be playing something else right now?" It's difficult to care about the characters when the game itself doesn't. Traumatic things happen to apparently main characters and the game just moves on, immediately, as if nothing's happened.

And yet, it's hard to believe how undefined the characters are given how much time they spend talking.

Anyway, there are plenty of good-faith critical reviews of this game, and they seem to agree on the main points of where it goes wrong. Search them out for details.

Sea of Stars has all the mechanics of a jrpg, but none of the soul. But it looks really, really good. \\_()_/\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars and Starfield competed for my attention back when both released just a few days apart and Starfield ultimately won the initial battle. That led me to not getting around to playing Sea of Stars until January, about 5 months after it's release. It's ironic how now after playing both, I ended up enjoying Sea of Stars way more.

In the past 5 months, I did keep my eye on how this game did critically and in the public's eye and heard a lot of mixed opinions; a lot of claims about poor writing as a result of a non-native English speaking writer, gameplay being dry and boring, and a general mediocrity. As a result I was more honed in on these aspects in my playthrough to see what the talk was about and at the end I can say I didn't resonate with them at all. The writing is fantastic and gameplay is perfectly fine.

Sea of Stars really shines in it's attention to detail; you can clearly tell this was a passion project from the developers. First, the game just looks gorgeous and the pixel art is some of the most stunning I've ever seen. The characters and animations are expressive and the environment are just bursting with details. To go with that, the zones are so well designed with short but fun puzzles and well thought out pathing and traversal. The game is also paced so well and it really just flows so nicely from story beat to story beat alongside your travels from zone to zone; it's an amazing adventure from start to finish and has some really nicely written characters.

The gameplay was fun and despite hearing how the mechanics were so front-loaded, I had a great time with it. The introduction of new party members was progression enough for me and the game's length didn't allow an opportunity for the gameplay to stick around longer than it was welcome. I was a bit lukewarm on the timing mechanics though, they never really resonate with me in any game. Healing wasn't my favorite especially early game; you heal for so little, have such low capacity for items, and mobs hit very hard especially when not timed correctly.

Overall a fantastic JRPG and love letter to the more standard, old-school games of the genre. Highly recommended especially coming in just over the 30 hour mark.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t
