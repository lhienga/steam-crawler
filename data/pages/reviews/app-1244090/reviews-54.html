\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay: 4 | Narrative: 3.5 | Graphics: 4 | Soundtrack: 3.5 | X-Factor: 3

A gameboy-inspired indie RPG that nails the nostalgic charm!

GAMEPLAY:
Sea of Stars' strongest points definitely lie within the gameplay. In general, the overworld design felt strong, and interesting. Whenever the game introduced a new concept paired with a new ability, the puzzles that came afterwards always felt intuitive and fun! Some of the most fun I had in the game was from the post-game puzzles I encountered in dungeons, at their most complex. There are plenty of secrets to be found, plenty of interesting side quests (each with their own set of lore), and side games like wheels and fishing. Wheels is a mini-game where you fight action figures by spinning a slot machine. Personally, it wasn't for me, as it took too long and was more luck based than strategy. But it still felt like a polished, well-thought out game nonetheless! As for fishing, admittedly, there was little purpose to it, and could've definitely been a bit more fun. It feels like fishing is a staple for a lot of games lately, and while I like the concept of fishing in a game, it certainly doesn't feel new or exciting anymore.

The combat was very nostalgic to game titles like the Mario & Luigi RPG series. You can time your attack inputs for extra damage, and can time blocks to reduce enemy damage. I'm personally a fan of this direction/style of turn-based combat, as it keeps the fight more engaging after your option select. However, the combat eventually became quite repetitive despite new playable characters and specials being steadily introduced. Some of the attack animations are simply too long to constantly deal with (e.g. Moonerang), and slowly become a nuisance to use. As well, there is no flee option for any battle. This was particularly annoying when you were backtracking in the game, and didn't care to battle any enemies. And speaking of backtracking... the fact that fast travel was only unlocked towards the end of the game was criminal.

NARRATIVE:
All things considered, the story here is decent. It starts out a bit slow, then feels on par with any other game in the rpg genre. However, towards the end, the story picked up a lot and had a lot of interesting twists and turns! I think I would've enjoyed the story a lot more if I was more attached to the characters. The character writing is more on the simple side, with many characters feeling flat and not changing much throughout the story. However, where the characters fall flat (pun intended), the world building is definitely noticeable! I very much appreciated Teaks' lore dumps, and there are 12 stories to boot! Also, it felt like the game tries really hard to make you like Garl, and that made me reserved about how I felt about him. :3

GRAPHICS:
Sabotage Studios definitely went for a classic look here, and it suits the game! It's reminiscent of an up-scaled older rpg gameboy title. This is completely separate from the review, but I didn't find any of the characters cute design-wise. Unlucky </3

SOUNDTRACK:
The music was a bit of a hit or miss for me. Sometimes, while I felt like it was fitting for the atmosphere, I didn't quite like it. I think in particular, the music for the more serious and somber tones for darker points in the story didn't do it for me. The music for said tones usually felt a bit forgettable, which is a shame. The space theme and sound effects design was amazing, though!

OVERALL: 3.5/5
What initially drew me to play this game was the very title itself - Sea of Stars. Such a cute title, and being my favourite game genre to boot, I had to give this game a try. While the game certainly has its pros and cons, it's well worth playing through, and I'm excited to see what's next in store for Sabotage Studio!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t첫 인상은 훌륭하지만 조금만 해봐도
'이 게임이 그런 평가를?'하는 의구심이 드는 게임.
크게 3가지 문제가 가장 두드러진다.


1. 반복되는 게임 플레이

전투는 물론 맵의 구성조차 특정 패턴을 반복한다.
마을-낚시포인트-던전-그리고 다시 마을로 반복되는 식.
좀 더 다양한 이벤트로 변주를 줄 수 있지 않나 아쉬운 부분.


2. 이상한 전투 벨런스

적의 약점을 찌르는게 주가 되는 반면
약점을 플레이어가 알아내기 힘들거나
아예 약점 공격이 불가능한 상황이 자주 발생한다.

전투 템포가 꽤 긴 편인데
변수가 적고 할 수 있는 행동 가짓수가 적어서
전투가 생각보다 굉장히 빨리 지루해진다.


3. 미묘한 캐릭터

주인공(?)의 캐릭터가 매우 미묘하다
성격도 알기 힘들고 목표 의식도 알 수 없다.
동료가 사실상 주인공이라 치면 그 캐릭터라도 살려야 할텐데
사이드킥 이상으로 강조되지 않아 확 끌리는 캐릭터가 없다.
스토리에 나오는 주변 인물들은 좋게 말해도 유치한 편...



그래픽과 음악은 훌륭하지만...
정작 장르에서 가장 중요한 탐험과 캐릭터
그리고 스토리는 좋게 말해도 평균수준?

모난곳 없는 게임이면 고평가를 주는 최근 리뷰 흐름이
꼭 좋은 게임을 고르는건 아니지 않나하는 고민을 하게 만드는 게임.
나쁜 게임은 아니지만 꼭 해야하거나 수작이라 치기엔 많이 부족하다.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tis sea of stars worth playing? i absolutely think so

the game has a handful of problems, and i mainly think that applies to the original cast. valere, zale, and garl all are kind of boring characters. they feel very stiff in their dialogue and among other things, feel like they lack characterization. this is a shame because they are the MAIN characters. i wont go into spoilers, but it feels like these three could literally be any one else and the story wont change

HOWEVER. that alone does not hurt the genuine joy it is to play this game. it has a slow start, and gameplay is a little samey for awhile, but once it gets going it really does good when keeping the pace and flow of the story. it has a very "not taking myself too seriously" charm that makes it just fun and sweet to play. yeah, some of the twists are predictable, but who cares?? you can tell theres so much love put into the game. the art is phenomenal, the rest of the cast is so colorful and interesting, the bosses are so cool, the gameplay is a little repetitive but its still solid. every single enemy and boss is designed with a lot of care, and it really shows. not only that, but we have genuinely amazing animated segments throughout the story. props to the whole team!

i dropped almost 40 hours on this game and with all my heart i think its worth playing. its memorable, its sweet, and by all means is it silly. you have to look past some of the faults for sure, but its a gem otherwise. i think you just have to commit and get past the first island, and things really start to look up. give it a chance to grow on you!

(but i hate garl, this guy is so. annoying. and boring. and i hate him.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful oldschool turn-based RPG with some heavy flaws - might be a dealbreaker for you

What I liked

\tBeautiful varied environments! Extremely great art team, just look at the screenshots
\tPretty Ost, seamless night day transitions sound great! Some great tracks
\tA *few* great supporting characters
\tHigh fantasy lore, with atmospheric storytelling if you like that stuff
\tFrequent party swapping is encouraged, everyone gains levels simultaneously so you don't have to neglect anyone
\tmultiple difficulty options unlocked while playing and can be toggled anytime

What I disliked

\tThe "main characters" are extremely bland despite having actual dialogue (not silent protagonists)
\tlinear level design - backtracking is rarely encouraged, this is not a metroidvania
\thaving to press a button for *every* platforming movement. No, there's no option for automatic movement
\tlinear equipment, no reason to keep old items because there are no special equipment effects (1 irrelevant exception)
\tno debuffs ("poison" doesn't actually poison, it's just an element. etc) among all skills and consumables
\tonly one buff among all skills and consumables (block one hit)
\tstory is run of the mill high fantasy without depth. frequent asspulls / deus ex. Probably worst offender in this list...
\tdialogue is not memorable, minimal humor (and then just simple fourth wall + immersion breaks)
\tno side quests, at best you have unnamed 1-minute-quests which just reward you with a collectible

Still a "recommend". It was fun, I guess! No reason to ever replay it (no choices, no really memorable scenes)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Audiovisually it's a fantastic experience. The pixel art and the music are beautiful
- Combat mechanics are pretty cool; boosting and lock breaking gives it a strategic feel

Cons:
- Not enough depth to combat and equipment systems. Each character gets like 3 skills; this also makes the combat feel very repetitive because you're using the exact same skills in every encounter from the beginning of the game until the end. Additionally, combat feels dragged out because some skills like moonarang, which is super useful so you use it in every encounter, take very long to execute. When it comes to equipment almost all of them are straight stat boosts with no interesting qualities whatsoever.

- The world-building is very poor and often doesn't make much sense. For example, there's the port town, Brisk. Visually, the town screams that it's a trading hub. Except if you progress far enough into the game to see the world map you'll find out they have no one to actually trade with! The other settlements either don't have ports or are otherwise inaccessible. The game is just filled with all these elements that don't really coexist with anything else in the same world and kind of do their own thing.

- The story, the characters, the writing in general. They're awful. There's no other way to describe it. The dialogue is boring, the jokes are painful, too many of the characters are bland and none of the interesting characters get any meaningful screentime. The story is pretty generic, save for one or two interesting moments it just falls flat. The pacing is awful, there's a part where you'll just spend hours trudging through these seemingly neverending dungeons just to get to another dungeon so the story can continue. There's a sign near the beginning of the game that reads "This way to the GOAT boat" that made me want to alt f4 immediately.


- But far and away my biggest gripe with this whole game is: Garl. ♥♥♥♥ this guy. He's supposed to be Joe Everyman, the guy with no special powers. So why does this sidekick best friend character get so much of the spotlight? He hogs every moment for himself; every bit of story-important dialogue is delivered by Garl, every important interaction with an elder or council or whatever it's Garl that does the talking. When the two chosen one main characters save some people, the people cheer for Garl. It's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ridiculous. And the worst part is all the characters love Garl. The entire main cast, all the side characters, just can't stop talking about how good of a guy he is. Hell the villians are probably categorised as evil in this world because they DON'T love Garl. The two main characters especially are so obsessed with Garl that he is all they talk about. When he's not there all they say is: "Oh how I miss Garl","I wish Garl was here","Garl would know what to do!","Remember that thing we did with Garl, best time of my life". And when he is there they go: "What would we do without you Garl?", "You're the best Garl!", "You're so right Garl", "Thank Garl you're here Garl". I'm ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sick of it. I was convinced partway through that Garl was going to turn out to be the evil wizard final boss, that he's been emitting spores or something to make everyone fall in love with him. All so he could manipulate the heroes and take their moments of growth away from them, so they could never grow powerful enough to defeat him. But no, he's just genuinely a Gary Stu that can do no wrong and everybody loves him because he's the best. ♥♥♥♥ you Garl.

I'm gonna go wash the taste of Garl's ♥♥♥♥ out of my mouth, and I suggest the writers do the same.

5/10 too much Garl\t\t\t\t\t
