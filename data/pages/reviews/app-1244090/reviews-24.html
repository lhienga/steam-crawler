But Sea of Stars definitely has the best story out of all of them, and I highly suggest *SPOILER ALERT* to get the true ending of the game.

Sadly, it was a bit to boring in the beginning, it takes too long for an animation to execute after you've decided on an action. I get that it's to cater to newer players of the genre, but damn, I fell asleep on the first few levels of the game. I wish it had the same game-feel and quickness such as modern JRPGs like Yakuza: LIke A Dragon, or even DragonQuest.

But not even that could hinder the poured effort on game level, world-building, lore, and story.

There's a goddmaned reason why this game is 2023 Indie Game of the Year.

And boy does it deserve it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who grew up playing (J)RPGs, Sea of Stars is missing virtually everything I enjoyed about those "retro" games despite marketing itself as such. Leveling up in Pokemon is fun because it's an opportunity to learn a new move, or turn into a new cooler form. Finding a new Djinn in Golden Sun was always super rewarding since it meant unlocking new abilities or even completely new classes. FF4's story blew me away when I played it as a teenager.

In comparison, SoS has no meaningful game play progression or decision making. Levels and equipment just make your numbers bigger, making exploration and side content more or less meaningless gameplay wise since enemies scale to your level. No unlockable skills/classes or meaningful customization, instead, characters are stuck with 3 moves the entire game.

Even if you ignore the bad writing, the story and characters are generic at best. Even if I didn't have a problem with the gameplay, the story certainly wouldn't motivate me to play on it's own.

I'm legitimately happy that people are enjoying the game, as it's obvious from the devs put a ton of heart and passion into the game. While the graphics and music is great, that doesn't change the fact that SoS was offensively boring during my playtime, so much that I uninstalled it at the 13 hour mark.

I've seen some people calling this a good entry level RPG, which I strongly disagree with because it just lacks what I consider to be the "fun parts" of RPG games in general. I'd recommend Paper Mario TTYD or Pokemon if you're looking for a proper entry RPG.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is expertly designed.

Gameplay is turn based with engagements happening when you run into enemies. It uses time based input similar to a Super Mario RPG & the Lock System from Octopath; in tandem they create some very compelling moment to moment gameplay.

There are no random battles and the player gets all the exp they need doing a typical play-through without additional grinding.

The narrative is well told and fun while delivering some pretty emotional scenes despite no voice acting.

Music is just top shelf around. David Wise's (Donkey Kong Country) & Yasunori Mitsuda (Chrono Trigger) influence is felt and this blended style is beautiful. Mitsuda himself composed some of the tracks while collaborating with this game's composer Eric Brown.

Length wise it's around 30 to 50 hours for a first play-through + secret ending.

Additionally, there's a New Game+ as well for added replay-ability.

While I have not done NG+ yet, I look forward to a second visit in the Sea of Stars ☺\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrue end done, 95% of achievements gotten, it is now time to leave a review. I had a fantastic time with this game, I felt the story wrapped itself up nicely (despite some reservations), and the gameplay mechanics were great to engage with (also with some reservations). So as usual let me start off with the story.

I was always excited to advance the plot whenever I booted up the game so I’d say that’s some mark of good story engagement. Everything felt nicely paced and there weren’t moments where I felt things were meandering or leading up to dead ends. You have your plot twists here and there but the story brings you up to speed on how they happened so you’re not scratching your head about things. The story provides enough understanding to character motivations to spice up the plot and keep it moving forward.

But with all that said, this brings me to the reservations I was talking about. This game’s story involves some prophecy weaving, time jumping, and alternate reality elements. While character motivations felt like they were clear enough for me, there are some questions that still linger unanswered even with the true ending. I suppose it’s better to catalogue these concerns as worldbuilding elements than anything involving the story, but I was definitely left wanting more clarity/answers with the alternate reality stuff. I also felt the true ending could have had a bit more time to elaborate how the Big Bad felt, as it feels like he just went “ok fine whatever” and then peaced out lmao

I think that’s the only pacing issue I really felt, and it was right at the end of the game’s story. I think maybe there could’ve been a bit more time for the aftermath of things, but even then it didn’t water down how I felt about the story overall. This all basically sums up how I felt about the story, so I’ll move on to the gameplay.

If you’ve played Super Mario RPG you will find yourself right at home with the game’s combat. Turn-based combat with a design on timed attacks and defending. Sea of Stars has 2 other mechanics where you regenerate MP with normal attacks, and a power boosting mechanic that you can empower your normal attacks or character spells with up to a maximum of 3 times. Overall, I had a great time with the combat mechanics, I personally love being engaged as much as possible when it comes to turn-based combat to keep things active, so this timed combat design was lovely!

However, like I mentioned earlier, there are also some reservations I had with the combat. There’s a first strike mechanic you can do since the enemies you encounter are interactable and roam around the world you travel. I’m not sure if I missed it, but you’re able to surprise enemies with a melee attack (which also nets you 1 orb of boosting power or 2 if you equip a certain accessory), and I was never able to figure out how to do it. There is a global accessory that helps you travel around, and once I got that it was the only thing I used for the first strike mechanic. I never bothered trying to figure out how to first strike enemies with a melee attack afterwards lmao

Additionally, I wasn’t quite sure what spells or abilities utilized the timing mechanic, and then sometimes I wasn’t quite sure WHERE the timing was on them. One character has a heal that you can time for bonus healing but I was never really sure where it was. The game tells you what to do on some spells but sometimes it won’t say anything on others, so I didn’t realize this character’s healing spell could be timed until near the end of the game LMAO

In the end it wasn’t a huge issue, and maybe I simply missed certain cues from when characters were talking so I have to also give things the benefit of the doubt. However, if this turns out to be a consistent issue with players then yeah, this might be something to keep an eye on with future games.

My other issue are the ultimate attacks. Every character has an ultimate attack, and they perform a very nice animation that looks really nice. But as a player, when you’ve gone through combat a lot, what happens is that these animations become irritating, and there is no way to skip these animations, you simply have to wait them out. I will say that the artwork in this game is GORGEOUS, from the idle animations to the background and level design, everything looks fantastic. But in combat, when I’m playing the numbers game and trying to beat the stuffing out of an egomaniacal boss, I personally feel that everything needs to be snappy. Honestly, a simple skip button for the ultimate animations would be nice, but regardless, I completely understand that a person’s mileage may vary on this. It was something that felt worth noting at the very least.

Alright so, like I’ve mentioned this game was a blast overall, and visually everything was a treat for the eyes. The music was nice and I still think of certain riffs from certain tracks when I’m not playing. But with any of my reviews, there will also be a section for The Gripes.

I’m just going to lay this out bluntly: I absolutely REVILE the Wheels game. Wheels is a minigame you can play throughout the world, and any place that has an inn has a person to play Wheels with you. In a Wheels match you and the opponent put down 2 statues, and you have this roulette system to empower your statues to attack the enemy’s little fort, which you can also reinforce to protect it from their attacks. Each statue has certain abilities, like for example the wizard statue can attack 2 times, and depending on the enemy’s fort, you can hurt it twice, or hurt the barricade while the second attack strikes the opponent’s fort directly.

I have played a dozen games and I could not win a single match. I will completely cop the L here, maybe I’m just absolutely terrible at figuring out this game, but it really doesn’t help that it feels like I was playing a game of chance with barely any player urgency. You get 3 rolls at the roulette system, and you can pick and choose what to keep. But if your luck is atrocious like mine, more than likely the opponent will always have a step up on you, and you will lose with their fort being at 1 life point (or, you will somehow TIE, and a tie gives you nothing).

There are champions you can beat, and every inn as 1 champion. Beating all these champions gives you access to the person who made the Wheels game and allows you to challenge her. If you are an achievement hunter, YOU WILL NEED TO DEFEAT HER, so I hope you enjoy games of chance, because that is ALSO a thing I really despise LMAO, I wish you all the luck in the world that you have a better time than I did! Yeah I really did not like this minigame, and honestly this is probably the biggest gripe that’s completely an opinion.

This next gripe is a bit weird, but if you have memory issues you might find yourself getting lost A LOT. THERE IS NO MAP IN THE GAME AND THERE IS PLENTY OF BACKTRACKING TO DO FOR SOME CONTENT. Throughout the game I found myself wondering why they didn’t implement a map, especially because plenty of the levels are elaborate, and many of the later levels utilize an acquired tool for navigating the levels. While I still managed to figure things out, I think the implementation of a map would be absolutely helpful to everyone.

After all that it’s finally time to offer my final notes. If you are impatient or worried, there is much faster travel you get access to that lets you fly around, so if you’re fine with it you can continue progressing through the game until you get to that before dealing with game content you missed. If you find the game too easy, or want to make it easier for yourself, you can readily do that from the settings menu at any time. Some of these challenge settings are locked behind merchants, and you will have to find access to them, but to make the game easier (or more accessible) the settings are there for you right at the beginning of the game).

Alright, that's it for me. Thanks for reading!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot going for it, but I feel the ending is a let down given the time invested.

The graphics are beautiful and the combat is fun. I essentially played with the 'hard mode' relic on for most of the game, and it offers a real challenge, but not impossible.

The music is great. and I enjoy the variations on the theme.

The weakest element of the game is the story. I find characters to be a little two dimensional. Garl is probably the most "complex" character, but even still this isn't a game for its story.

The biggest issue I have is how it ends. You get the 1st ending, with the option of getting the true ending by completing various side quests.

I completed all of them beforehand except collecting all 60 rainbow conchs.
Most of them are not hard to find, but some aren't so simple or obvious. Hiding the true ending behind a collect-a-thon feels a bit cheap to me. I'm not sure if I'm motivated enough to do so.


This is a fun game, and most people will enjoy. Just be mindful that you might miss the full experience if you dislike treasure hunts.\t\t\t\t\t
