\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: I love the game and recommend it to anyone that loves RPGS

While I do love the game and I at this moment have gotten close to 100% the game and intend to do so later down the line, it is not the best that I have played. The game banks on the nostalgia that players have from when they were kids and it shows. The game play can be finicky and there is no real indicator for when to tap for more damage or extra hits. The characters can be quite dull besides Garl who felt the most fleshed out, and I feel that there was not a lot of character growth or any memorable moments between the characters besides the beginning and end. Everything in between was a blur to me.
But enough of the negatives, I loved the world and the music it really made me feel like I was on an adventure with my friends and seeing things for the first time. I would love to see more of this world with a dlc if there is one in the making.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is incredibly charming and top-notch when it comes to its visuals and music and would make it a fantastic game by that alone, but there are other elements that I do think bring down the game.

Positives:
- Incredibly charming pixel art graphics that are a joy to look at. The environments are very vibrant, character designs are wonderful, and the sprite animations are excellent.
- Fantastic music by composer Eric W. Brown and featured music by Yasunori Mitsuda, composer of Chrono Trigger's score. The soundtrack has 3 different variations for some songs, like a night, day and a pirate version.
-No grinding necessary.
- Exploration is fun as you go through the beautiful environments to find treasure.
- The story has some surprising twists that were interesting.
- Decent post-game content with some additional bosses to fight and side quests to complete.
- Relic system. An in-game system that you can toggle on or off that gives options to lower the difficulty of the game through decreased damage, increased xp or by having an option of the timed attacks and blocks being automatic.

Negatives:
- The story is a pretty typical hero's journey with your protagonists being destined to fight a great evil that includes some nice twists and turns, but it stumbles along the way. There are some great moments in the story, especially in the second half of the game where it begins to really pick up and there are plot points that are introduced that could have been explored more. However, these points are rushed, or quickly abandoned for reasons that you will find out after beating the game and looking into it. Both endings are pretty disappointing.
- The characters I thought were well-designed and had some charming characteristics, but besides that, there isn't a whole lot of depth or growth from them, outside of a few who show some promise. The two protagonists, Valere and Zale, are the blandest characters I've experienced in a long time and I think they would have been better off being completely silent. Itâ€™s frustrating because from the beginning of the game weâ€™re introduced to Garl, who has the qualities of a main character and is better suited for the role.
- Combat is simple and repetitive. There is a severe lack of abilities and variety, with each character getting 3 abilities and 1 ultimate. The abilities consist of a single target, multi target, and utility ability like a stun or a heal. Some ultimate abilities are clearly much better than others. Once you get to the final stretch of the game and have collected your party members and have a clear understanding of the combat, you'll notice there are specific characters with clearly broken abilities that you'll spam, which trivializes the game. The timed attacks and blocks, although interesting at first, get repetitive and boring.
- The true ending is locked behind 100%.

Not a masterpiece by any means in its narrative, and its combat, but the game knocks it out of the park with its pixel art and designs that are so charmingly crafted that you can't help but admire it as you explore the world. I think this is a fun, casual JRPG that some more seasoned players of the genre can find enjoyment in. I think it serves as a decent entry point for players that want to get into the genre with a more modern JRPG.\t\t\t\t\t
