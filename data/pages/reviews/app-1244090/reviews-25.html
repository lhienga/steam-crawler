\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game to anyone. The story was fantastic, the dialogue was fun and kept me engaged, and the gameplay was a ton of fun. If I had to give it a number, I'd put this at a 8.5/10. There is something in here for long time RPG players and there is something in here for people completely new to the genre.

The game isn't overly difficult and it has different things in the game to ramp the difficulty to your liking. The battles are pretty easy to grasp, but take some management to master as you go throughout the game. One of the drawbacks for me is the lack of complexity in the fights. There really isn't any customization in your abilities or armor, as everything is gained pretty linearly throughout the game, as you ramp up in power. This isn't a bad things necessarily, but it wasn't for me as I'm used to having a lot more customization options for my playstyle. For a new player to these kinds of games, I could see it being a good stepping stone to bring them in.

The movement in this game is just overall fun. They do a great job of giving you tools to move around the map and to solve puzzles. The maps are so well designed for if you need to backtrack, to make it less tedious too.

My only other gripe with the game would be the two protagonists Zale and Valere. While the entire cast is colorful and has such a wide range of emotion, it feels like the main characters don't. It's not terrible and it doesn't take away from the game much for me, but they just feel so much less vibrant in comparison to the cast that is in the game. All of your party members and side characters have interesting stories and interactions, but not as much for Valere and Zale.

All in all, buy the game, you will enjoy it. Garl is the best Bean\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs everyone else points out, this game is a lovingly crafted and well polished ode to the SNES era of RPGS. However, it's important to note that the developer has merely taken inspiration from that era, building on the storytelling and gameplay pieces that made that decade so endearing so that Sea of Stars is its own unique creation that stands on its own merits, not merely getting by on nostalgia.

In the terms of aesthetic, tone, and story telling it clearly draws from Chrono Trigger and early Final Fantasy.
With the tonal trend of "dark and gritty" story telling being extremely popular right now it's incredibly refreshing to play a game where the developer clearly wanted to tell an old style adventure epic with a heavy dose of magic and charm.

In terms of gameplay and mechanics there's a very strong influence from games like Super Mario RPG. Each battle plays like a strategic puzzle more than a standard RPG, where your choice of skills has a great deal of weight and your skill with quicktime events can give you an edge, but isn't integral to your success.

All-in-all, highly recommended and greatly appreciated that some devs are doing their own thing rather than following the popular trends.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's difficult to fully encapsulate all the ways in which this work of art resonated with me. I can only endeavor to try:

I am a rather fond proponent of "Golden Age" JRPGs, and I could exhaust the reader's patience with endless perceived subtle (and less subtle, but never unwelcome) references to those games in this one. The 90's and 2000's were a dark and troublesome period of my life, and I often found escape in the music and innovative number systems delivered from the Land of the Rising Sun. There was a much purer clarity of vision in those creations; by volume, there were fewer men standing between the authors and us, the players.

There is a deep welling joy that this game evoked in me; a reminder that beneath the pummeled, blasted, and imposing rock face that is my existing reality, there is yet a wholesome, stubborn and rebellious spark of a lad itching to don helm and walk the land righting wrongs merely for its own sake.

This haphazard and off-the-cuff metaphor is the best gratitude I can offer to those creators who had the courage to continually bare their own souls through the creation of this masterwork, and the fortitude to see it through to the end.

To the author individually: I have a great appreciation for your technique and wit, as well as your honesty and candor. Thank you for what you've given us; I will cherish it for a long time.\t\t\t\t\t
