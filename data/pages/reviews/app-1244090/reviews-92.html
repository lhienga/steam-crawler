\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Disclaimer Note: It took me around 30 hrs instead of the 22 hrs on my playtime to finish the game and unlock all endings.

As a casual fan of JRPGs, I can say that Sea of Stars was a wonderful game and would recommend any casual fans who would like to try a modern JRPG experience. The battle system kept me engaged with the timing system similar to the Super Mario RPG and made each encounter fun since I wanted to master the timing of blocking enemies attack and my own. I also enjoyed the "Lock" system where enemies charging their attacks can be stopped by hitting them with certain types of attacks. The boss battles were a highlight since it promotes players be aggressive in boss battles especially with the "Lock" system and the satisfaction of stopping & blocking their attacks. My only complaint is that I wish there was a dedicated "Run Away" command since fighting weaker enemies was a drag.

The story was a grand adventure throughout the amazing world that developers made. From the world building, characters, and story, I enjoyed the journey from beginning to end. Though my only complaints are that there are some plot lines that are unresolved so hopefully there's a sequel or DLC being made to close it out. The music was a highlight for me especially my favorite being Glacial Peak and the standard Encounter theme.

I recommend this game for full price for anyone interested since the game offers such a complete package for both hardcore and casual JRPG fans.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is excellent.

It's not perfect. The story feels forced and too much lore gets left to interpretation. Things don't seem to be explained well and are left to be told as a story by Teaks rather than acted out. Characters aren't explained well either and too many characters either get eliminated too quickly or they are far too powerful with no explanation why they are as powerful as they are.

The game is very linear and only at the end does it open up enough to backtrack. There is a lot of backtracking at the end. You end up collecting a bunch of rainbow conches for no reason, only to find out, vaguely, what you are collecting them for. If you look up the reason for the rainbow conches you find out they are there to get a proper ending to the game.

I know I am giving the game a lot of negativity. It's still a really good game but it's not the best. It really pushes the similarity to Chrono Trigger and even places some conveniently similar names in save files or things to find in the game. It is trying very hard to be like Chrono Trigger but it falls short.

The game has a good story though it takes some time to get going. The characters are very shallow and tend to be too mysterious. Garl feels like the main character and also has the most personality of the game. The two main characters you choose from just feel like they are along for the ride and are utilized as overpowered pieces to advance the story. They gain power for no real reason to push things along. Even characters that come later suddenly use items or powers they never really eluded to having just to end a boss fight or get through a door.

I wish the story did more though. I wish some characters weren't one offs and provided more. I wish it was longer... it needs more time to develop characters and establish lore.

It's worth the money, that's why I recommend it. The game is beautiful graphically, the music is spot on and I catch myself whistling to some of the areas, the combos and skills are engaging and interesting and the enemies are varied and have interesting moves to keep things different and challenging. Sea of Stars is the closest thing we have to old RPGs of similar fashion. My hope is it opens the door for other creators to push these types of games and improve this genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely wonderful love letter to the RPG genre, taking all the lessons learned over the past 3 decades or RPGs and combined into one amazing piece of art. The games visuals and soundtrack are as anyone can see absolutely stunning, so I wont bother going in depth on those.

Engaging combat, which just keeps getting better and better the longer you play, up until the very late game if you discover a sadly game breaking skill rotation, but its frankly not a big deal. Overall gameplay-wise this game is still a masterpiece.

While the first dozen hours might feel pretty uninspired story-wise, it really ramp up past that point and it becomes clear why the first few hours are the way they are. It's nice when characters lie or only tell you what they actually know, its realistic, but the first impression of the game suffer due to it drastically. The story from then on out drastically improves the deeper you get into it, becoming even mindblowingly good towards the middle and end. If you're not into it at first just take your time and give it a chance, this game is much more expansive than it may initially appear. The True Ending of the game is especially worth going after and odds are that you'll have already completed most of its conditions during your initial adventure.

Overall I'd rate the game a solid 9/10, nigh perfect if not for its slow start. Definitely worth a try, especially if you're a fan of Chrono Trigger and / or the Mario RPGs.\t\t\t\t\t
