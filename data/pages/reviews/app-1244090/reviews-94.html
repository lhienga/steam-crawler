\r\n\t\t\t\t\t--SPOILERS--

Amazing experience. Always had a soft spot for sprite based RPGs, and Sea of Stars delivers in every way for me.

This was easily the best indie game I have played in years, however there are only two things that keep this from getting a 10/10 from me.

The story has a pretty slow first few hours. It's only after a critical moment in the story (you'll know when it happens, trust me) where things finally start getting really good. I wish it was like that the entire time, and not reserved until the later half.

The only other gripe I have is the backtracking. The backtracking for the majority of the game is honestly REALLY bad. I'm sorry to those who do like it, but I couldn't stand it. In short, the backtracking for 90% of the game has you traversing through older settings for the vast majority of the world to get to where you need to be.

There is no on the spot fast travel. Way later in the story, you will get the ability to freely fly around and land anywhere on any island you want , and that makes traveling much more tolerable, but for the whole time before then, it's going to be a lot of back and forth.

Other than that, Sea of Stars is a fantastic RPG that pays tribute to the classics. DEFINITELY recommend at least trying the demo, but if you love old school style RPGs (especially action commands), you'll fall in love instantly like I did.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, it's time for my review. (SPOILERS AHEAD)

I believed that The Messenger would've been my game of the year for years on end. It was for a bit of time, maybe 2-3 years since I had first played it. When I heard Sea of Stars was dropping in 2023, a prequel that takes place in the same universe as The Messenger.. you know I had to play. I wasn't entirely sure if I would enjoy it, I was never a Chrono Trigger or Final Fantasy kid. My biggest exposure to a true RPG was Mario RPG, and even then I stopped playing throughout it. The Mario & Luigi games as well, when I was younger.

So I got the game. It was a bit slower paced at first, but as I kept playing.. it slowly dawned on me that this was my new favorite game of all time. Without a doubt.

The story I've seen a lot of people complaining about, the ending was underwhelming to a lot of people. And it seemed like there were a lot of loose ends left untouched. Although of course, as someone who played The Messenger prior to Sea of Stars, it only made the story feel better. There was.. SO MUCH to uncover. Although I won't cover all of it in this review, I'll go through quite a lot. Anything with spoiler text is pretty heavy, so uncover at your own risk.

My favorite section of the game was from the battle of the dweller of strife, to after Garl's death. Seeing that the Demon King was those four bird people was wild, and that Brugaves was the Demon General as well!? Absolutely mind blowing! Seeing the Artificer and what he was before The Messenger too was pretty mind blowing as well! There's no point in between the game that feels too slow paced, besides the beginning. Which is okay! It's nice to have them slowly ramp you into things.

I loved the characters in this game! Garl, Teaks, Serai, the pirate crew, all those folks! Becoming attached to the characters throughout the game, the personality attached to them (maybe besides Valere and Zale; a lot of people complain they felt void of life in this game, but I believe that's because they didn't want to differentiate the two too much, or else it'd skew people liking one or the other more. It didn't bother me though. Regardless), all of that good stuff! Garl's death legitimately made me cry. A game hadn't made me cry like that in a while. I was bawling like a baby. The way it was implemented was done amazingly, and if I could experience it that same way again, I would.


The locations in the game too were quite fun! Of course, I mostly enjoyed the Mesa Island area. Seeing Autumn Hills, Glacial Peak, what Quillshroom Marsh was before The Messenger - Songshroom Marsh!? Some of my favorite areas to explore purely based off that nostalgia factor. I liked most of the other areas as well, none really stuck out as bad. Maybe minor flaws, but none off the top of my head. They're all unique!

And the music? THE FREAKING MUSIC!? GLACIAL PEAK, SONGSHROOM MARSH, ELITE ENCOUNTER, MOUNTAIN TRAILS, DANCE OF 1,000 SUNS, THE MUSIC PLAYED IN TAVERNS!?! This game has some ABSOLUTE banger soundtrack choices! Arguably one of my favorite things about Sea of Stars.

The combat system also wasn't too bad! Some people complained it was too easy, some abilities being way too easy to use. I agree to a certain extent, although I would only really saying that about Re'shans Ultimate. It's the only one that fully heals the team which is strange. People complained about Serai's stalling kunai attack, but I feel as if it is necessary to stop enemy attacks completely. It didn't feel too easy for me personally. Still a challenge in the right parts! And if you want to ramp up the difficulty at any point, you have the option to.

So if you're reading this and you haven't bought it? BUY IT. ABSOLUTE BUY IT. If you a fan of RPG's like Chrono Trigger and such, you will get a kick out of this game. It's amazing how well crafted this game is.

Also nomiate this game for Outstanding Visual Style in the steam awards!!\t\t\t\t\t
