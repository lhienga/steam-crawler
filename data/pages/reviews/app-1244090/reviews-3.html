CONTEXT
I've played games for 25 years and this was the first game I played with pixel art or turn based combat.

POSITIVES
+ Stunning pixel art graphics with a modern twist
+ Intricately detailed environments begging to be explored
+ Fantasy setting with fantastical visual elements (glowing trees, floating platforms etc)
+ Accessible for beginners
+ I don't like puzzles and I'm happy to report 90% are easy to solve. Actually enjoyed them.
+ Solid 30 hour campaign that keeps things fresh with a variety of locations and characters
+ Music score is very good
+ Boss designs are unique and some are genuinely striking


NEGATIVES
- Some poor writing leads to out of place modern dialogue that doesn't fit in the world
- Character interactions can be very melodramatic
- No way-point option for when you get lost
- No quest log and lore guide to help track the story and characters
- No voices for character dialogue
- Desperately needed more cut-scenes to help connect to the world
- Auto-save system sucks
- Character 'Garl' is relentlessly irritating and does not warrant the attention he gets. Cringe
- I feel the story dragged a little and the pacing was off (the story would climax multiple times)
- I found the sci-fi ending very polarising

Definitely buy this. 8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA JRPG made by the Canadians, and it shows. Everything a JRPG should have is there, except for the grind. There is no need to grind heaps of monsters, really. No boss has more than two phases too.

The combat is turn-based, but with timings - aligning with attack animations, you can hit space to block/add another blow. The game says you don't have to do this. The game is lying. By the final bosses you won't be able to win without it. On the other hoof, by then you will have already mastered this.

The plot is linear (JRPG), but with a true ending reachable after you load from the post-final save and play some more. A lot of exploring and climbing into different holes, behind the back walls of buildings, etc., etc. The plot, the humor, slight pokes at the fourth wall, this all is good. Tools that help you find more collectibles are available. Super-secret ultra-meta scene after the credits exists, if you don't mind turning every stone.

Metroidvania elements and backtracking exist but mostly in the interval between the first ending and the true one, when, with all the tools at your disposal, you go through past locations once again. Wow-moments and being genuinely surprised, here too, both in terms of plot and game mechanics.

Due to the lack of grinding, it takes 25-40 hours, I think.

P. S. The post-ending, and the first ending for that matter, is all bosses well, technically not all, but "a boss, a boss, a cutscene, another boss with two phases, a cutscene, a short walk, a boss" - that's how it feels. I suspect that this is also normal for jrpg.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pleasantly surprised with the duration of the main narrative and the layered approach to it. The game doesn't really lay out, in an obvious way at least, the "main goal" of the entire campaign right out of the gate. This allows for you to achieve major milestones and begin to understand how that accomplishment leads you to the next necessary step. I also really appreciated the relic system letting you sort of "tweak" your difficulty in specific ways which is appreciated more than a "easy normal hard" approach, and this also flowed into rewarding players with more skill in timing things during turn based combat. I hadn't really experienced much of that previously and felt this to be a big boon.

Overall I would have to say I recommend this game, especially on sale. You can comfortably clock 25-30 hours with a normal playthrough that includes minimal "collection" activities.

Pros:
*Pleasant artwork and music
*Rewarding and substantive narrative
*Minigame (wheels) is a nice way to change of pace, if at times frustrating
*Skill rewarded during combat
*Nuanced reveals, some more obvious than others, that feel satisfying

Cons
*Fishing minigame adds little
*"True Ending" hidden behind some pretty boring time-wasting activities
*Cooking/food adds little. Could have been managed differently but did tie in narratively well.
*Combat lock RNG sometimes frustrating
*Trial and error necessary to learn proper timings of abilities, even with help of relic\t\t\t\t\t

Play Chrono Trigger. Play the original Final Fantasy 6 on SNES. The games that inspired this one still stand the test of time today, and are better investments of your money and effort.\t\t\t\t\t
