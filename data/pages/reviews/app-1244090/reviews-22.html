\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9/10

A fantastic throwback to old JRPG games and a great homage to Chrono Trigger.

Sea of Stars is a JRPG that tells the tale of Zale and Valere, two children brought by the Great Eagle to be trained into Solstice Warriors and fight the Fleshmancer's minions. The game takes heavy inspiration from Chrono Trigger, also with some inspiration from Super Mario RPG in the combat mechanics (with timed button presses to reduce damage from incoming attacks or increase damage when attacking enemies). It's a great game that looks fantastic, sounds fantastic, is very well animated and has great combat mechanics.

The combat involves each character having 3 special skills that use MP with different effects and timed commands, in addition to a basic attack. Combo attacks can also be performed with other characters after the combo meter is built and using combo attacks also fills the ultimate meter, for one of the characters to unleash an extremely powerful attack. The game is also more focused on resource management instead of item usage: while items can be used to recover HP and MP, basic attacks recover MP and skills can be used to recover HP. And in addition to equipment that increases each character's base stats and grant other advantages, gold rings can be equipped to provide advantages to the entire team like regenerating MP on a perfect defense or reducing the number of turns a character needs to get back up after they're knocked out.

The game is also very strategic with how combat works: during combat, enemies will sometimes start to cast skills and, during this process, there are locks of each damage type that can be broken by using the corresponding attacks, which will then reduce the effectiveness of that skill or, if all locks are broken, interrupt the skill entirely. This, in combination with all the different damage types that each character can deal and all the different mechanics you can use in combat, often gets players to think of their next move so that they can break an enemy's attack or at least reduce its power.

Story-wise, the game mostly tells a great story, with only a few moments that don't click too well and a few things left unresolved (which maybe will be addressed in the DLC?), but for the most part it's an enjoyable story with a few very heartwarming or heartwrenching moments.

It's also worth noting that the game has an incredible soundtrack, some fantastic animations that play during certain moments of the game and the game makes an amazing use of color, with everything looking bright and colorful without feeling overwhelming.

The biggest problem I can think with the story is that the main characters feel less interesting compared to other characters. Their story develops further on, but it feels like they never really escape from the "chosen one" tropes and their personalities also never really development too much beyond the hero stereotype. Other characters, meanwhile, are much more developed and feel a lot more interesting, with special mentions to Garl and Seraï, who easily earn the title of best characters in the game.

Combat is very fun and the game is not too challenging for the most part (but relics can be used to adjust the game's difficulty, either making it a lot easier or a lot harder depending on relics used), though there are a few difficulty spikes here and there. Surprisingly, with the exception of a few bosses, normal enemies tend to be more dangerous than bosses - at least at the start of the game, getting into a fight with enemies instantly casting skills right from the start can potentially lead to a game over, which is something I feel should be balanced - at the very least in the early game, as later on it's a lot more manageable once you have more characters and more skills to use.

Also, some of the timed commands feel off. While it's very easy to perform them for some attacks, for others it feels very weird - it feels like the moment when you should press the button for the timed command to trigger is not actually the one the game wants. There was one skill in specific that I could never get the timing for right and some enemy attacks also feel way too specific and with way too limited timing for when the game wants you to press the button.

The only other thing I can think of in terms of criticism is that I'd like to see more variety of skills and equipment. Only 3 skills (and an ultimate) for each character feels very limited and while there are some trinkets that grant certain advantages to each character (like extra damage to undead enemies or timed commands when attacking/defending getting a 10% bonus), I'd also like to see more variety of effects during battle, maybe some status effects included. Then again, this is entirely a personal opinion of mine and I can see that maybe the developers chose to not include more effects in combat so it doesn't feel overwhelming when combined with other elements already present, mostly the mechanic with breaking locks when enemies are casting.

All in all, this was an amazing experience and I can't wait for the DLC. Easily recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

As I will show below, it has beautiful pixel art, mechanics that refresh the genre and a story that captivates you. Not in vain has it won the award for best Indie game of the year at the Game Awards.

In a JRPG, movement is not the most important factor, but the level design -which I will talk about later- makes it very dynamic. As seems to be dictated by the rules of the genre, we will also have a couple of vehicles as the plot progresses that will facilitate our passage and access to new areas. Some jumps that give a touch of platforms (although automated).

The camera is static, a traditional top view in the style of Final Fantasy or classic Pokémon. The most interesting point is that there is no transition between free play and combat, making it more dynamic.

The game, outside of combat, does not use UI to let the artistic section shine with its own light. The combat menus are very intuitive. In addition, there are some sections that are introduced into the narrative, such as orbs to enhance attacks, in true Dead Space style. The pause menu is also very intuitive and clear, making it easy to navigate.

Sea of ​​Stars draws on the mechanics of previous games like Mario RPG or Chrono Trigger. He does not hide when doing so, he recognizes it and refreshes and improves them by adapting them to our time. It also introduces -again following the instruction manual of good JRPGs- a very elaborate minigame.

The navigation at a narrative level is excellent, revealing elements on the map that you cannot use and for which you will have to return later, giving the player the possibility of going to optional areas and with very elaborate maps.

The enemy design is very good. The area bosses present certain puzzles and strategies that, although simple to understand, complicate the game a little. On an artistic level they are fantastic, with the most careful designs, from the first monster you face to the final boss (the first one and the one in the postgame).

This is perhaps one of the negative points of the game. Getting gold is more complicated than in real life. The only way to get money fast is to play spinner and it's not really that fast. The monsters only drop ten gold coins at most and in the end the player feels like they fall a little short. In my case, I have farmed a lot and even then I have had to spend hours fishing to get one or two gold coins per fish.

This is one of the points in which Sea of ​​Stars and, actually, the work of Sabotage Studios stands out. Each level presents several paths, some optional, others that open after completing certain tasks. In general its levels are a master class on how it should always be done.

JRPGs are not particularly replayable except, perhaps, Pokémon. However, this video game has a system of relics that can greatly transform the mechanics and ultimately the video game being played. Therefore, it could be replayed several times trying different relics.

The game performance is quite good. Without being too demanding, sometimes many elements are put on the screen and, in general, it runs quite smoothly. Only on one occasion did it drop below 60fps.

The only bug I have found is already fixed in the new update. It was in the sea of ​​​​nightmare, that the game could crash and you could not continue the adventure.

The blocking and critical system is a delight. Everything is very well measured. Some enemies and abilities are difficult to catch but it always feels fair because there is always the possibility of finding when the right moment is.

Regarding the difficulty, I must say that it is not very demanding, but it also allows itself to be a challenge at times. Not even the optional postgame bosses are a big problem.

Regarding the soundtrack, Yasunori Mitsuda has simply participated. They create incredible themes with variations depending on the time of day the protagonists are. In addition, they remastered some songs from The Messenger and it looks great. 10/10.

Another high point of the video game: the art design of the stage, that of the characters, the animations, the skills, the combat. There's not much to say when what you do is simply excellent.

The plot is very good. It has certain twists that the player does not expect. Others that are expected but are treated with such grace that they look very good. The characters are wonderful and you manage to empathize a lot with them, especially with some like Garl or Seraï. They all have a lot of charisma.

The Spanish translation (which is the one I played) is not particularly good, and has quite big errors such as calling Cunaluna fish pie “pie de pescado de Cunaluna”. There are many translation errors that hinder the enjoyment of such a brilliant game.

This game is literally based on taking RPG tropes and twisting them, parodying them or emulating them, as the writer feels like it. It is noticeable that before writing the game they have dusted off their old JRPGs to play them and give us the best possible experience, with many breaks of the fourth wall. Literally.

In conclusion, Sea of ​​Stars is a very good game that, if you are a JRPG lover, you should take a look at. Everything he does he does well, and in some things he even excels. The small mistakes it has do not even make a shadow in the light of its successes.
