\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this bored one evening on a whim cause I happened upon a thread comparing it to chrono trigger and other oldschool games very favorably as a MUST PLAY.

I just finished the game. It gets extremely tedious after hour 5-8. Battles are a bit of a slog, you can't skip long animations, you are forced to break locks on even the most tedious normal battles, the leveling system is very unrewarding, the sound track doesn't really hum along, and the story is not only fairly boring, but it doesn't really make a lot of sense -- I read later that the indy team didn't have any staff writers - it shows in a bad way as a good story could have kept me engaged past the initial charm of the world 5ish hours in.

The game had some definite charm, the mix of old-new styles of visuals and gameplay was well done, animations are pretty cool. But the characters lacked any soul, and I finished the last 15 hours of the game fast-scrolling through most text while watching other stuff in split screen.

There's not a lot here, and its not worth the $35 tag.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShoots for the Moons, lands among the Stars:

I was so excited for this game when I heard about it only a year ago. I immediately wishlisted and bought it day one. The art style and promise of classic rpg story telling was only half of my excitement though. While Sea of Stars definitely delivered on those fronts, there were still major elements that it lacked which always left me a little unsatisfied. In fact almost every aspect of the game is about 80% amazing, but not quite satisfying for one reason or another.

Before the negative, I do want to stress the positives! The art is truly impressive. Character portraits, animations, lighting, depth and detail in the world. I could hardly ask for more! The attack animations alone are masterful. The way some characters move really add to their personality. The lighting and detail of the environments are also something to behold. It manages to have dynamic, glowy lighting without looking silly like other "HD pixel" games. It all looks very natural and fluid. There is one minor gripe I have with the animations though. There are not really any notable particle or impact effects. Even in the hit timing system, strong hits don't give any cool visual effects like you might assume. There is an option to have a little flare come up when you successfully time something, but it is quite literally a little star shaped single particle flare; it's not artistic at all, it's purely functioning as an indicator. Just feels like a missed opportunity considering how beautiful everything else is.

Another aspect for which I have minor complaints is the story. The story is fairly nice, it has more depth than any generic JRPG, but at the same time it isn't very thrilling or emotional for me. There are indeed twists (sort of) and implications to a greater events outside the scope of the game, as well as optional lore, but the story just feels too grounded. There aren't really any themes or a greater message to dig into or think about. The story happens and you watch it, you don't think about it too deeply. It is a nice story, but not a very memorable one. The settings also leave a bit to be desired, Most of the locations are outdoor environments with a nice mix of darker areas and even *BIG SPOILERS************** robotic areas, but for a game called "Sea of Stars" I expected a lot more deep space sci-fi elements (similar to Star Ocean which I assume inspired this game). Again, it is visually stunning, I just wish there was a bit more depth in the way I expected.

Now the biggest miss in my opinion; the combat. The combat is fun, it's so fun that it actually saddens me a great deal that there was so much not put into it. Personally, I love rpg combat that allows the player to be creative and try different things. This game allows almost none of that. It feels like you are always just as strong as they would like you to be, it never feels like you have any strategic ability. The only real challenge of combat is timing the button presses and knowing which element to use to break spells/attacks charges on enemies. I believe this is because of 2 major things:

There are NO status effects:
It kind of blows my mind, but this game has a heavy emphasis on elements, but have ZERO status conditions. Because of this, elements don't really feel much different at all. A poison hit and a moon hit are the exact same, only difference is what the spell charger on an enemy needs to be broken (or a specific weakness for SMALL amounts of extra dmg). It's really weird too, because you can cook with all these different ingredients, but they do nothing but either heal hp or heal mana, so the variety seems pointless.

There is ONE build because stats don't matter:
There are a good amount of different characters you can use in your party, but they really all essentially build the same thing. There is no variety in your build at all. You get linear item upgrades which feel like they do nothing but keep you at the exact same level as enemies the entire game. The saddest part for me is they added one of my absolute favorite features: choosing stats upon level. However, it doesn't matter what you pick at all. Ideally this system would allow you to try different things for different characters, it would allow you to customize the role of each of your party members, whether that's min maxing for dmg or creating an ultra effective tank or being more balanced. First problem is the stats you can level are RANDOM??? so you can't hard min max, but ultimately it doesn't matter because even if you consistently spam one stat as much as possible, you barely affect the play-style of that character. I spammed magic attack on Zale and physical attack on the Moon girl and they basically did the same dmg on both attacks and spells, same for the assassin. That and the lack of a speed stat, crit stat or anything else to add variety makes all characters feel like just a set of skills.

Overall, I did enjoy Sea of Stars a lot, but the whole time I really just wished the devs had allowed their game to be less stream lined. I understand that leaves it open to be broken, but at its current state it is an easy 8/10 with 0 replayability. I do recommend it though. You can really tell they skimped on nothing and put a lot of work into it with amazing results. It is truly a joy to play, there are just some choices that boggle my mind and really hurt the experience for me. It could have been a perfect 10/10 for sure, but it still manages to be a great rpg.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wouldn't say it's "bad" per se but it's lacking in a lot of departments that I wouldn't expect a JRPG in this price range this release year to have. Instead of using the games it's supposed to be inspired by to elevate itself, it only takes the superficial similarities for more of a marketing strategy than anything.

After so many other JRPGs since Chrono Trigger try to add to the genre and explore all kinda of unique twists or even trying to just better the already established mechanics of JRPGs, comes a game that doesn't really try anything new. It sits comfortably on it's "Chrono Trigger inspired" seat without an ounce of effort towards being anything unique.

Combat doesn't really change after the first hour or so. You'll be doing the exact same thing up until you finish the game. Level-ups don't mean anything. You get some increased stats and a choice of an additional increase (like the one you can see in the steam store page) that seems to only be there because otherwise level ups would be even more boring.

The story actually went backwards in terms of quality and interest. In contrast to the very cool concepts Chrono Trigger has, this one takes the safe road and doesn't tell anything new or exciting. You are the chosen ones, you are stronger than all of your predecessors because you are, and only you can save the world. Not to mention the mid game I'm about to die so let me just know everything that needs to be doing just because which throws any suspense of disbelief out the window.

Character-wise, the same as the story. Not even having anything close to characters like the Magus from Chrono Trigger for example.

TL;DR: a step backwards from things like Chrono Trigger which released almost 20 years ago. "Nothing ventured nothing gained" is a sentence that is quite fitting for this game, considering how few unique things they have. Maybe get it on a big discount. No way this is worth $30+.\t\t\t\t\t

Not just "nostalgia bait"

I really love how Sabotages design ideas take the best parts of old-school games and create something even better. It's like they're finally making the game they've always dreamed of.

In Sea of Stars, they've added a more active turn-based combat system, inspired by Super Mario RPG. You can time your attacks just right for increased damage and use blocks against attacks for reducing incoming damage. And from Chrono Trigger they added lot of things they liked, including combat happening in world rather than other screen after swirly transition-screen. There is almost no "random encounters" outside few "ambushes"

I haven't been this obsessed with a game in a long time. Decided straight away to aim for 100% the game, outside one last achievement meant for new game+ I'm almost there too (41/42)

I get that some people might find the block and attack timing a bit tiring after a while. But don't worry, you can turn on relics to make it happen automatic with block being 35% chance and attacks always (with less dmg so its not too op) and that way have a more traditional turn-based experience. It won't affect your achievements, and no one will even know you're using them. Plus you can turn them off again once you feel like doing active gameplay.

What I will also mention as sort of con, depending what you expect, is that the story is full of JRPG tropes in both good and bad. Like power of friendship being "massive almost-god-killing power". Or city NPCs talking almost non-sense or small random things like "I really like the inn" no one would actually say in 'real life' to random passer-by. I personally found that part amusing and very nostalgic.
If you take those as "Heh, of course they would add that trope from the genre" you will have bit more enjoyable time. Don't expect too much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game takes the best elements from so many classic rpgs and puts them all together cohesively. The games greatest strength is that it looks beautiful, There is also a ton to do with collecting chests and doing side quests and finding the rainbow conches.

Combat is pretty straightforward and becomes a bit more strategic when the locks (which if unlocked before the enemies turn could cancel their move and save your party from taking some big damage). start coming into play in fights which will keep you on your toes if you already grow accustomed to the games combat system which borrows heavily from Mario RPG with timed inputs for additional damage and reduction.

The only thing that moved a bit slow for me was the story it was difficult to stay into initially, but if you ride it out it does get better the further in you get.

good game overall i definitely recommend it\t\t\t\t\t
