\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game fails in everything but aesthetics. I don't often come across games that I would consider juvenile but this is one of them. This year I've been graced with playing a lot of wonderful games among those was Chained Echoes. A somewhat flawed but largely brilliant approach on the JRPG method that had depth in story and in combat. It also boasted similar aesthetics to this game (and used all of these things better).

Now Sea of Stars both fails to be enjoyable or inspired. The naming conventions used in this game are dreadful. 'The Fleshmancer' 'The Dweller of Woe' 'The Necromancer', 'The Solstice Warriors'. The names chosen are SO painfully generic, especially in a fantasy setting. It's like they didn't hire a writer and just decided that generic enemy names would work great as titles in a long form RPG.

Characters are flat and perhaps that's even being too kind. Neither of the Solstice Warriors boast any form of meaningful growth or attitude, their responses to virtually every situation are trite and predictable. Garl while initially lovable quickly becomes tiresome. NPC's and side characters are past the point of being uninteresting and well into the real of actively abrasive (Looking and you, Yolande and crew. Yes I hate you).

The combat is bumbling in the extreme. A handful of well thought out mechanics are hamstrung by overtaxed action economy, which leaves the player with a handful of potential moves but the answer you need is almost always just attack. Enemy breaks in most cases are either outright impossible or a mad rush with how quick action counters run down. I wish I had enough words to detail all the ways the combat kept running into itself.

Just go play Chained Echoes instead, its better in all regards.\t\t\t\t\t
