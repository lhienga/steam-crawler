\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVisually it looks pretty nice but that's about the only good thing this game has. It's a turn based RPG with action timings, which by default means you need to give battles your attention rather than a less engaged system. However the action timings don't really have much feedback - a little sound, a slightly different animation, but there's no weight to them. By the time I stopped playing the most engaging ones were the "hold A and release at a certain point" and "press A to bounce a boomerang" abilities that you start with - the latter of which just uses the same animation as the block.

The result is that battles end up feeling about as interesting as a standard turn-based RPG but require your full attention to make sure you are hitting that block/attack input for what is a pretty minor boost in damage/defense. Sure, the enemies can do big attacks that you want to hit them with certain damage types to stop, but even early on I found that these would fairly often result in patterns I could not do whether for lack of MP or lack of turns - such as an enemy needing 3 turns worth of different damage types, but set to attack in 2. Party members who are knocked out DO get back up after a couple rounds, but what this ultimately meant was that I didn't care too much about engaging with this mechanic. As other reviews mention, there are also no buffs/debuffs of any sort so everything you do is either attacking or healing - FF1 was more engaging than this.

As boring as the actual gameplay is, the writing might be worse. Dialogue ranges from acceptable to eye-rolling, and the plot tries to lay its cards out pretty clearly at the start. I'm sure there's more to it but it really sucked just having possible small reveals, tension, or questions about what the main task at hand was going to be more or less spoiled by multiple heavy-handed attempts at "foreshadowing." It feels like an early draft that they just shrugged and shipped. I also couldn't find any reason to care about the characters or what they were doing.

Overall I would say this game isn't really worth it at any pricepoint. It's boring, miserable to read, and you can get all the good stuff from it by just looking at the pretty screenshots on the store page.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first JRPG I've played. I liked how they set up / treated a character death, it set up a kind of dread I hadn't felt before. Besides that, the story is pretty nice. It's a bit childish, but the art made up for the mid-story.

I got kind of bored with combat after a while and felt like I had to push through to see how the story would end. I liked the puzzles though. Some of the puzzles felt unreasonable, but I may have missed something because I was getting burned out from the combat.

The level design is great. I was worried about not having a mini-map at the beginning, but the game was really good at hinting where I should go next. But, it wasn't always clear what were surfaces I could interact with vs what I couldn't, so there were some frustrating invisible walls I had to deal with.

The amount of effort needed for the true ending makes this....hard to recommend unfortunately. I can tell a lot of love and effort went into this game. I loved the art / vibes, and the character designs. A few of the NPCs, mostly Serai, Garl, and Hortace, were great.

But the regular ending was...such a letdown. The regular ending's boss fight was actually easier and less satisfying than the boss fight in new game +. The ending itself was also kind of an unsatisfying letdown with a monster and odd minigame that came out of nowhere. It looks like the true ending would have a much better boss fight and satisfying ending. But one of the requirements is such a hassle, requires so much backtracking, and involves so many time-wasting minigames that I'm debating just deleting that new game + save file, because I get so upset whenever I try to make some progress on it.

I'd say this was a 4/10 for me, so I can't really recommend this. But if someone really wanted to play because of the art, I wouldn't try to talk them out of it.

Just try that gain one more time!" - etc- etc- etc. An extremely quotable game not to mention the cast and story. Cloud, Yuffie, Tifa, Barret, Aerith, even Red XIII, even the bad guys had good dialogue

Sea of Stars (2023): quote from 'Villager': "Some Docarri prefer to remain in their inner chambers most of the time. As a tribe we learned not to take offense at such behavior, and to celebrate each community memeber's freedom to engage and associate to the extent that they desire."

OOH MAN. First off, people do NOT talk like this. Especially if they don't know you! In Kingdom Hearts two, in Traverse town, a random NPC might comment on the weather, or something unique about the town, or anything. Damn it, the NPCs in Final Fantasy 1 give more brief information about the location or events of wherever you are instead of giving you crap like that.

I wanted to make a folder of the worst dialogue in this game but go play any part of it and you will sigh with frustration at how stupid the writing is. I remember dialogue from this game not because it's funny, or interesting, or cool- but because of how stupid it can be "Nothing quite like Bardcore!", and oh my god- the worst character in the game (SO FAR) is Yolande. If that was your writer's attempt to be funny then they should be fired. .

The dialogue in this game is so unbelievably stupid that the only way I'm going to finish this game is I stop talking to NPC's- the main characters are bad enough.The story would be passable if not every word out of everyone's mouth (especially Garl who's the dumbest character I've seen in years) "WOW GUYS! AWESOME! THAT'S SO COOL! EVERYONE IS AMAZING! XDXDXD". "WE DID IT WITH THE POWER OF FRIENDSHIP!"

This game has some good locations, a few decent characters, good but not really challenging combat, and the art style (except for the characters) is really good. Actually, wait- the combat is too easy by far. What's the challege in this game other than listening to the characters talk about literally nothing.

I would not recommend this game to people I don't even like.
I am never buying a Sabotage game again.\t\t\t\t\t
