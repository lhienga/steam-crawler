\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my first purchase of an indie studio game I enjoyed my time with this game and recommend it to anyone with a soft spot for classic JRPG's as long as you realize what you are buying. The art, animations, and music are all great but majority of the rest of the game feels very disjointed, incohesive, or in some cases elements just thrown in for no reason. The game implements many RPG mechanics and staples of the genre but seemed like they had "too many cooks in the kitchen" or maybe they let the kickstarters drive development decisions too much. It's a shame because this game could have been great if they had spent more time fleshing out some of the core aspects of the game (plot, storytelling, combat, skills/abilities) instead of trying to cram as much as possible into the game.

Overall a solid 6/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is one of those RPGs that really stood out in 2023, probably because of its marketing (and even more so that it won best Indie Game Award at TGA recently), and while I would absolutely recommend it to any RPG lover (especially JRPGs), I thought it is just a good game; not bad, but not great either. With that said, it is very clear that the developers of this game really made this game with love, and there was definitely a lot of potential in this game that I hope improves (much more) and carries on to their future works.

Moving on to my criticisms of the game, there are parts of the game that are absolutely exceptional and even more so, however there are also parts of the game that were really frustrating for me, and I actually had to put down the game for a bit due to some of these things. However, after picking it back up, I was able to complete the game and reach the true ending as well as the easter eggs. I think the amount of stuff I had to do to reach that point though didn’t really feel rewarding as I thought it should have.

I believe that forcing optional content is a bad thing to have in RPGs and having to complete pretty much all the side content to get the true ending took a big chunk of my time and sucked a bit of enjoyment out of my play through. I can appreciate the simple, more commonly familiar mini-games such as fishing, which was never frustrating to do, but forcing other mini-games that I actually thought was unique and fun, such as the Wheels Game, became a point of frustration when it shouldn’t be.

Perhaps the biggest point of frustration and block for me was the start of the game to the mid game. The game just failed to pique my interest in the story and the characters at all, and the gameplay got very repetitive especially with what you had to work with in the beginning, such as the lack of party variation, and lack of skill variety. About 10 hours in, I actually had to drop the game for a bit because I would have burned myself out trying to force myself to play through it. For any Falcom enjoyers, keep in mind that this was the year that I (finally) fell down the Trails rabbit hole, and had finished the first 5 games (Sky Trilogy, Crossbell Duology), so coming from THAT to THIS contributed a bit to my dropping the game for a bit.

The story and the character development are probably the weakest points of the game for me. The game couldn’t get me invested in most of the characters that even the emotional parts of the game had me feeling little to nothing. Additionally, I felt that there was more character development for specific characters (that I won’t name) than the main 2 protagonists. The world building was overall kinda weak, only getting more interesting toward the end game but I was left with too many questions. This was absolutely a slow burn game that could not hit the points I look for in an RPG (mainly the overarching story, then being invested in the characters, the world building, the artistic direction and finally gameplay).

While it did not hit in the emotional department, I will say that some of the dialogue and actions/sprite animations of certain characters did made me laugh (thanks Teaks!). Overall, I just didn’t think the story itself felt interesting, and it also didn’t feel serious enough when it needed to be, but the laughs I had here and there helped to balance my issues with the story and character development JUST a little bit. Laughter really is the best medicine!

In terms of gameplay, I think they had good ideas inspired from other RPGs and it has a lot of potential, but the execution was sloppy. It was very repetitive in the early to mid game and it only started to feel more complete toward the end game (though still a little repetitive). I actually REALLY enjoyed the final few boss fights (especially a particular last one), since by then you have your complete party and skill options available that you could fully utilize. Having your full party and skill options also kinda made the game a little too easy at that point though. Related to that, I felt like certain relics that make the game harder actually tied in pretty well with the gameplay mechanics, as it helped make them feel more complete in my opinion, but having them available at specific points of the game was an odd decision especially when a particular “hard mode” relic is linked to an achievement. I think there is a lack of meaningful skill progression in the game, and while it does pick up in the late game, there is still very little skill variety and it is a little too late for it to happen when it only felt like it was starting to gain traction.

I think a notable mention is the mini-games and the puzzles. I felt like they were “just right”, as in it didn’t feel like a chore to complete when thinking about it on its own. The puzzles weren’t too difficult, but interesting and simple enough to solve that even the most anti-puzzle gamers wouldn’t have too much trouble with it. I thought the Wheels game was also quite unique, and it was very easy to understand. It is just unfortunate that to get the true ending, you felt forced to do them all.

With the story and gameplay out of the way, I felt like the overall visuals and music were INCREDIBLE, and they were definitely the strongest and most outstanding points of the game. The colour of the world and the sprite animations were superb and I can honestly say the game really captured the beauty of what made the retro RPGs of old so great and timeless. When I see the different skill animations, the actions of the characters, their facial/body reactions and the map design, the first thing I thought of was actually Chrono Trigger. I think Sea of Stars really raised the bar for retro style RPGs and really brings this style to a modern audience. Definitely master class stuff right there. Also, while I think the game was a little too inspired by Chrono Trigger in particular (noticeable references here and there, even in the map and sprite character visuals), I do not think this is a bad thing, and it definitely hit that nostalgia bug.

I absolutely loved the music. Usually when everything else is subpar, the music is what will help me decide whether I can keep going with the game or not. The soundtrack really delivered in Sea of Stars. I personally think that not EVERY track needs to be amazing; it just needs to fit the vibe. Having different variations of tracks is a wonderful decision that really fit the vibes of certain characters, places and moments and I think it deserves a lot of praise. Also, Yasunori Mitsuda as a guest composer was a wonderful surprise as his music really defined the gaming in my childhood. Off the top of my head, some soundtracks that really stood out to me were “Melisma of the Waters”, "The Frozen Peak (both versions)", “Sky Base”, “Skirmish for Glory!”, “Duplicitous Wrath (both parts)”, and “Fury of the Fleshmancer: The Final Stand”. They definitely made it onto my gaming playlist that’s for sure!

Overall, I’m glad I got to experience this game to its fullest. You can tell that with was definitely made with a lot of love from the developers that truly enjoyed the old retro RPGs of old. Even with its flaws, I think it is definitely a game worth experiencing especially if you are particularly fond of old retro style RPG greats such as Chrono Trigger, Super Mario RPG, and Secret of Mana to name a few.\t\t\t\t\t
