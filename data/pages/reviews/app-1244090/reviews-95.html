objectively good game, with beautiful art and excellent music.
but i still don't know whether i like this game or not.
The story so far has been rather... lukewarm. it's so tropey and bland, that i honestly just don't care for any of the characters nor the world. The interaction between characters feels so bland and surface level, devoid of emotions.
To be very clear, I don't hate the game, it's been rather fun playing it so far. the bossfights are enjoyable, there's no grind to speak of, the perfect timing mechanic is genuinely well executed and rewarding, but i'd be lying if i say the battle system alone can carry the game. I'm currently at the beginning of a new chapter just after the 'cataclysm', so i'm still hoping hope that the story or at least the characters will get better. Will report back later.

However, it falters in the gameplay department, as it feels monotonous with mechanical and overly simplistic combat. Many combos seem irrelevant, and the overlong animations for Ultimate attacks can be a drag.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved The Messenger and was incredibly excited for this game thanks to the gorgeous trailers. Unfortunately, the trailers show basically the only part of the game that excels in any way - the visual presentation. While I don't expect the stories and characters of western-made indie JRPGs to match up to the classics, I do expect them to be decent enough that I don't physically cringe from the writing. Garl is one of the most insufferable characters of any RPG I've ever played and the overarching story is bad at best. The battle system barely gains any new options (one extra spell and one ultimate move per character) from the beginning to the end of the game, the game bombards you with repetitive, story-irrelevant dungeons between nebulous plot points, and somehow even great songs from The Messenger are remixed into far inferior versions. Overall, there is no reason to play this game other than admiring the admittedly fantastic pixel art and/or morbid curiosity.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boy, where to start.
I really REALLY wanted to like this game. After Octopath 2 I've been dying for a good JRPG, and this one... this one isn't it.

The story is made for children... and I mean, it's really soft. From consequences of messing up to fighting a boss, telling him, hey, u do you, and then him turing his whole life around, it's just so bland. There's no consequences for any action. The story feels like it was written for one of those little kid shows, it's really bad.

Then the combat is just odd. At first, it feels good, but level ups and upgrades are extremely underwhelming. Say u have an armor of 10 and u get an upgrade that puts it at 36. Enemies that where dealing before 19 damage are now dealing... 17? Why? I upgraded my armor by 26 points and that took off 2 point of damage?? In level ups, u get 3 points (six if u bonus upgrade) so if 26 takes away 2 points, 6 is useless. This is the same with attack moves, where the timing is just stupidly forgiving (very easy) and the spells are incredibly easy to pull off.

Overall, the game is just easy and bland. I'm not sure if the audience for this game was elementary kids and younger, but I'm definitely not the target audience for this game.\t\t\t\t\t
