\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars - 6 out of 10 -

I really liked the story. It was kinda similar to the feeling I got when I played games such as Tales of the Abyss or Legend of Legaia (both great games with awesome stories). I will not spoil you about it.

In regards to the battle system I felt a little bit bored from time to time, and I continued playing because I was hooked with the story. The game lacked that essence of joyness compared to games such as Valkyrie Profile, for example. I mean, hitting an enemy twice or more just by tapping the button on the right time is nice but not so fun at all. Combos should have been more fluid to make this game even more exciting.

Talking about the characters I would say that I really liked them but I found something fishy about three of them. Resh'an reminds me to Gogo from Final Fantasy or maybe Bard from League of Legends. All of them share some mysteries not revealed to the player, ensuring a kind of enigma to be deciphered.
Serai is definitely Akali from League of Legends. She presents all the same traits: assassin, green clothes, ninja-style, uses kunais and even has a very similar hair style... I mean, seriously?
And we also have B'st. He is really similar to Zac from League of Legends also. He presents a body which can adapt to the situation transforming into a ball bouncing everywhere leaving bloblets on the floor. Plus, he is really tanky.
Zale is too similar to Jaster from Rogue Galaxy. Valere could be like Lenneth but i'm exaggerating here.

If we have to mention the music in the game, it was great. Some songs are highly recommended such as Derelict Factory, Songshroom's Wonders (day), final boss battle theme or the main battle theme.

All in all, I liked the game with its Steven Universe concept art imbued with some maturity. I know that it is difficult to create something new or different from everything already invented but maybe, developers had to be a little bit more original at some point.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game, not very good, not perfect! Was really exited for it, so much so I preordered it (something I ususally don't do) but after finishing it, I felt unsatisfied.

So, first of all, this game is absolutely gorgeous, from graphic style and animations to biom variety and creature design. The music is charming albeit not memorable, with some nice throwback tunes from The Messenger.

The plot is nice, not great, but definetly had its moments. I also liked the many nods to The Messeger.

Now, this game markets itself as a JRPG, and while it IS turn-based, it has nothing to do with a RPG, it's more of a turn based action game, due to the RPG elements being so shallow, i.e. everything boils down to slight increase of attributes (of which there are only 4 sadly) on level up, and newer gear means higher stats. There are no buffs/debuffs, and elemental weaknesses/strength hardly affect anything.

The battles are generally centred around using specific actions to interrupt enemies, and while this mechanic was refreshing for the first third of the game, it began to wear down on me, especially since each playable character only learns 4 abilities (yes really) throughout the game, which makes leveling up extremely underwhelming, and battles repetitive and formulaic.

I really appreciate that the dungeons are more than going from point A to B, and actually have a puzzle element to them, which, imo, is a very important component of any dungeon, a component that seems to be getting forgotten by the majority of game developers. The problem though is that at times there are too much dungeons, one after the other, which hurts the pacing.

I know I've almost writtern an entire PhD dissertation regarding this game, but it's just because I was really rooting for it, and hoping it would turn out to be the perfect modern day JRPG.

Despite the negatives, i'd still recommend the game, because it's a journey worth experiencing, but keep in mind that mechanically it's pretty basic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHailed by a lot of people as a spiritual successor to Chrono Trigger, this game fails to measure up to its predecessor. Combat is easy and filled with button tapping mini-games that quickly become boring and repetitive. Boss fights feel like a joke, especially with no functional debuff and buff management. The lock system feels like it could have been fun but falls short of the promise it might have shown due to either being overly easy to break or so tedious to break that it isn't worth attempting. Not that it matters with how easily you can trivialize the damage enemies deal through massive healing.

Moving around the world feels slow with "puzzles" that take almost no thought to complete. Ultimately it's a linear turn based RPG with tedious combat that fails to challenge and over world puzzles that also fall short. The only positive with this game is it is one of the most beautiful sprite graphic games you can find.\t\t\t\t\t
