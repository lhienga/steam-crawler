Luckily, these kind of games have seen a renaissance in recent years with more to come. And the love and effort for the genre and predecessors is evident in every aspect ‚Äî the look, the feel, the sound. The developers went above and beyond to do it justice.

Makes it all the more frustrating that I cannot enjoy this game.

Well, I could give a TL;DR, but I really want this to be taken with context and this review is more about ‚Äúgetting it off my chest‚Äù and for the developers (if they care) than anyone else. Wall of text and potential (minor) spoilers incoming, you have been warned!

There are two primary reasons that prevent me from continuing after about 25 hours, which brought me just to the cusp of the main plot to actually kicking off. I have not spoilered myself, but I can take an educated guess at how the game will proceed. And I‚Äôm all for it, this is not one of my problems.

1) The main protagonists are boring

This is mostly personal taste and a matter of perspective, not something I would objectively see as a crucial flaw. Valere and Zale are by no means bad protagonists, they are just not interesting. Both are likable enough and I do not mind playing and following them. At the same time, they could also suddenly die and be replaced and I honestly would not care.

While I applaud having protagonists who are not motivated by avenging their family or town or whatever, and their status as ‚Äúchosen warriors‚Äù still makes them neither unique nor quintessential to the universe (at large). But they also lack any conflict or goal beyond the destiny they were handed at birth ‚Äî which they embrace. Again, glad you avoided that rebellion trope for those two. Yet they lack any intrinsic motivation beyond duty and their personal prophecy quest. Good save, but only a stopgap solution.

Of course, they might develop further down the line, I cannot tell from my game state. I certainly hope so. But I need them to be interesting from the start and not just after 50 hours playtime. So far, they are eclipsed by almost every other character that is more than a two-line NPC.

Garl has his passion for adventure and cooking. Serai seems to have some edgy dark past going on‚Ä¶ which is not particularly novel but more than our two heroes have. Did not meet the other playable characters, but I can guess they got their personal quests as well. Heck, my favorite character is Teaks, and she is not even playable! Her quest to collect stories and document history, bringing back her ancient order of historians is so damn cool that I would love to have a full game just about that.

I just‚Ä¶ do not care about Valere and Zale. I wish I could play Teaks' story instead.

2) The combat system is a chore

I could have lived with the previous issue, but this is what ultimately made me drop the game (for now). And again, this is a matter of taste and preference. But when I play a turn-based game, I do not want to have my reflexes tested with timing puzzles. I want to sit back on my couch and relax, idly grinding through scores of trash mobs until I‚Äôm overleveled before continue the plot. If your gameplay is primarily about combat, your combat system must be fun. Players must look forward to the next engagement. But the longer I played and grinded, as is my MO, the more annoyed I grew. Until the thought of suffering another round of combat prevented me from booting up the game. And I hate that I hate the combat. I want to love this game so much; I appreciate the work, I do not regret paying full price for it and hope others enjoy and support it. But I cannot enjoy it.

I would probably not have played beyond the first combat tutorial if a quick Internet search did not assure me I could automate the double-hit mechanic for a damage penalty. Whoever came up with that relic, you are the real MVP. Because the game blatantly lies to you when it claims the double-hit to be an optional bonus ‚Äî in a combat system focused on breaking an enemy‚Äôs stride by hitting affinity combos, thus making the double-hit extremely important.

I never figured out when to press to defend or attack either, because there is no visual clue. You have to hit the split-second of the right animation frame. I just surrendered myself to the automatic attack and getting my head bashed in for full damage, because every successful crit or counter was just pure luck. I did not care to practice either, because I frigging hate the mechanic. I want to relax, not be stressed out by every single action in a turn-based combat system.

This alone could still be forgiven. Thanks to Adamant Shard, you can mostly ignore the issue at the cost of reduced damage dealt and more taken. Fair tradeoff. Unfortunately, these are not the only timing puzzles. Not even remotely, as the whole combat challenge is built around the things.

Now, Valere‚Äôs Moonerang or Serai‚Äôs Venom Flurry start with generous reaction times. But they are not difficult to pull off and maximize, I even got the achievement for the Moonerang ‚Äî by button mashing against a single enemy. Others, like the Sunball, are a joke to pull off perfectly but technically less powerful. Compared to Phase Shiv, which I only ever managed to time right once.

So this is not a skill issue; you would need a severe dexterity impairment to completely fail here. But that is not my point. I do not enjoy having to watch every action and continuously test my reflexes. I‚Äôm not having fun doing that, I‚Äôm stressed out. I do not feel rewarded for pulling off a 30+ combo, I feel my time wasted.

It‚Äôs like playing a tabletop role-playing game, like Savage Worlds or D&D, and every time my character does something, I get handed a sheet of 4th grade math equations to solve by my gamemaster, which I need to do in 10 seconds and the more I solve, the stronger my attack becomes. Eminently doable, but not fun. And this is what Sea of Star‚Äôs combat feels like to me. It feels like a chore.

And this is not something you could just fix with another relic, either. Because combat is balanced around this mechanic, so if you remove it or average the hits, you would make many encounters unchallenging. But as it stands, I cannot bring myself to continue the game with this. At that sucks.

I‚Äôm also not the only one with this particular issue. I‚Äôm not mad or disappointed, and I see my money spent well despite my grievances. But I‚Äôm curious how many people quit like for this reason.

I don‚Äôt want to be mean and it does not change anything, but I would really like to know.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: I put about 25 hours in to this game and decided to uninstall it. This game is just not fun. Quite frankly it was a test of will to get to 25 hours. I found myself getting bored about 10-15 hours in but decided to see if it gets interesting or better. It does not. The story is so dumb. At one point you bake bread for a long-sleeping dragon who wakes up and decides to become a..caterer? That's not even quirky or funny, it's just bizarre. The whole story is like this. It is all rushed and incoherent.

I can forgive the story because I can see why people would like it. What I cannot forgive is the battle system. If you want to do a special or magic attack, you hit space bar. That's it. That's the battle system. Hitting space bar a hundred times. Sure, there's a little strategy decided what "element" to use, but there are so many save points and bonfire resting points that there is little to no challenge.

The best parts about this game is the sound and art. That's it. If you want the Sea of Stars experience, download a few pixel art GIFs and listen to the soundtrack on youtube.

Original: This is a middle thumb game at best. The story is painfully simple, the combat is painfully basic, and the map is painfully boring. It LOOKS like a great game, it SOUNDS like a great game, but lacks charm. The best I can say for this is it is generic. I would much rather play Epic Battle Fantasy on those old Flash websites.

SoS is clearly inspired by the old RPGs like Secret of Mana and Chrono Trigger, but somehow misses the fun and I think it is because of the lack of depth. Sure, the game makes you think it's got an open landscape, but this is just a trick of level design. Yeah, you can jump off cliffs and grapple across gaps, but they all are designed to take you on a linear path. There is no sense of exploration or danger or excitement.

The characters are boring as hell, too. Partly because the story is rushed along, and partly because the writing is just bad. The humor is not very good, and most everyone you meet is forgettable.

The sound design and artwork are great, for sure but those don't make up for how NOT FUN this game is.

I would recommend Epic Battle Fantasy 1-4, Legend of Heroes Trails in the Sky, Final Fantasy Tactics, Tactics Ogre, Final Fantasy IX, or any of the original, classic SNES RPGs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou've probably heard that it's a gorgeous game with (at least mostly) great music that you will hum to your spouse and look up remixes on youtube for.

The bits you can expect on the worldbuilding and story front: Plenty of creativity on display and some really unexpected, but appreciated elements in the story (light spoiler: I really liked the outright lovecraftian elements).

But they had the autacity to pull a Bioshock: The game continues about a third of its' runtime after the emotional and dramatic peak. I can explain in the spoiler section.

The story had me hooked for a good while (after the admittedly slow start) and became even a bit challenging (and not only charming) when the devs introduced a relic for increased difficulty.

But as a famous game critic who has basically joined breadtube after 17 years in the industry said in a recent video: The ending of a story is essential.

And while the setup and the little touches (let alone: the polish) of the game are great and it's perfect if you're high and nostalgic: It just doesn't have anything to say and by the end left me... just annoyed. Plus: It's even more up its' own ass as The Messenger. Instead of trying to actually say something, the writers seemed too much up their own asses by playing Disney and hyping up their own wish for an SLU ("Sabotage's Lutomatic Universe"). Guess Hollywood and Nintendo aren't the only one who are after that sweeeet nostalgia money.

Spoiler section. If you haven't gotten at least to the sea of stars, this will potentially ruin the emotional core of the story for you. You have been warned

The game should have ended after Garl's funeral. It was the emotional core of the whole game. While the end left me mostly shrugging and saying "Yay, saved the world... again... would have rather played Disco Elysium instead". Finally, while being pretty and subverting a few expectations that are physically possible now (because it's 30 years later... who'd've thunk...), it just doesn't have anything to say.

They could've even set up how you get to the sea of stars, cross it and all of a sudden: We're in a PS1-era low-poly RPG like Final Fantasy, or Chrono Cross for the first 3 minutes, followed by a "To be Continued". That would have said something about nostalgia and would have also been a statement abount RPGs always being about Teenagers that use the powers of friendship to kill god. (which is what the story in the end pans out to)

Also, it would have been a baller move by the devs to shadowannounce their next try on Final Fantasy VII. That game at least had something to say about the world we're living in. (mega-capitalism, colonialism, climate catastrophe, ...)

Spoiler for the "good" ending: And in the end, they ruin it all by pulling an Ori and the blind forest, by reviving the mothertrucker? I was THIS close to cry on that damn funeral and then they decide to ruin that whole scene by bringing back their darling? That ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• will make me go misery on your ass.\t\t\t\t\t

But unfortunately, I couldn't bring myself to enjoy Sea of Stars. Below, a (not so) quick review on the game. So, short answer/ TLDR... Is SoS worth it? Should you buy it? I mean, yeah, sure, but only when it gets on sale though. This game definitely isn't worth 40 bucks on Steam and the other remaining platforms with so many other options out there being sold for a much lower price.

PROS:

-Beautiful looking graphics, reminiscent of the old-school era of RPGs from SNES. Even if you didn't grow up during the "Golden Age" of the genre back in the 90's/00's (I know I didn't. I'm a late 00's kid, after all), the game is an absolutely delight. Kudos to the art direction team;

-The overall presentation is fantastic. There are many types of cut-scenes, with the animated ones (in 2D) being the highlight during most of the intense moments.The team went above and beyond with the production. This gotta be one of the prettiest indie games ever;

-(Mostly) Fun and engaging combat mechanics;

-Great soundtrack. They have nailed it. I'm constantly humming the main battle theme while I study time/ work/ do house chores;

-Serai. I love Serai. She's arguably the best character in the game, don't @ me on that. Garl who???;

-The cooking system is alright. It's nice having to cook my own healing items. Resource/ ingredient management is something I wish more RPGs did more often, instead of resorting to buy the usual potions and concoctions;

-Exploration can be kind of relaxing. It never gets tiring to do things such as climbing up walls, walking on ropes, swimming in lakes... It just feels nice;

-QOL compared to older titles from the genre were also welcome.


CONS-

-The story is lackluster. It took nearly 10 hours for something interesting to actually take place. Most things were quite predictable and boring. Almost nothing feels like there's an actual weight to it. And the one moment the story decides to take a more risky, bold direction, you can just... "revert" that. By fulfilling the requirements for the real ending, everything will be "undone", and the previous impact evoked by that moment will simply vanish. The story clearly didn't have the same amount of care and attention that went into making the game look good, and that doesn't tend to be a good sign in a story focused RPG;

-The characters. Jesus Christ. Valere and Zale have to be the blandest duo of protagonists I have ever come across, it's unbelievable. Embarrassing even! They have NOTHING going on! All they care about is their duties as the Solstice Warriors and their best friend, Garl. They don't talk and behave like actual a curious-about-the-world teenager/ young adult would. They don't FEEL human, and controlling one or the other makes absolutely no difference at all. Gameplay wise, they only have different skills, and that's it.

Also this game wants you to like Garl. Like really, REALLY like him. I don't mind the side characters having some time to shine (that's good even), but this dude feels like the actual protagonist of the story and that's never a good sign, especially if we consider the fact the Valere and Zale have NO PERSONALITY WHATSOEVER. He's perfect. He steals the spotlight every single time, the story is all about him. I feel more like Garl is the true chosen one rather than Valere and Zale. Even when he messes up, the game treats him like he's a flawless human being, and our protagonists don't call him out for his wrongdoings.

Anyway... There's also Serai, but she's actually hella cool. The reason why I like Serai is because she feels like an actual character. She has motivations, reacts to things happening to her like an actual person, goes through an arc that tells us why she's doing what she's doing and, by the time you get to the end, she gets a conclusion to her story and resolution to her struggles. She finds inner peace. It's almost feels like she was written by someone else entirely.

The pirates are annoying and overstay their welcome. They have ONE JOKE, and will make sure to have everyone listen to it. Every. Single. Time. Everyone else is just as forgettable. They're bland and one-note;

-While the combat may be fun, it can get repetitive pretty fast, especially early~mid game, in which your options are quite limited. The characters have such a tiny skill pool that doesn't allow players to experiment with builds and whatnot. Heck, I'm mostly a CASUAL player who doesn't usually care about builds at all, and even so, I felt restrained somehow. I shouldn't even mind this aspect yet I do. The combat is similar to that of Mario RPG's: You time your attacks to deal more damage with the proper input, while also paying attention to the enemy attack in order to block/ parry, so you can receive less damage and survive the battle. There are combos and ultimate attacks, but that's about it;

-The world feels... Empty? Like, okay, sure. The devs wanted to create this world full of different things to explore, but the locations feels so dislocated from each that it doesn't really feels like it's real. It's almost like I'm exploring "mini-worlds"? Also, almost every single boss battle is grandiose so when you have to fight a big guy, the impact is lost, because you just fought against another big guy an hour ago. It's full big guys, but their impact to the narrative and to the player is... quite little, to be honest.

-The requirements for the true ending are RIDICULOUS. You have to do nearly every single side quest AND collect a bunch of useless shells??? These are SIDE activities, they're optional! They weren't supposed to get in my way to beat the game through the end! I wouldn't be complaining about this if the story and characters were engaging, but that's definitely is not the case here. I'm sorry, but I couldn't bother getting to the true ending. It just wasn't worth my time.

That's all I can say about SoS without getting into spoiler territory. I was expecting more than this, and the devs let me down. Please do better next time, Sabotage, I know you can do it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust... Ahhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh.

Pure RPG comfort food, playing this game is so refreshing. It's complete, it's fun, the story is passable even if not exceptional, and everything just ticks all of the boxes that I've been yearning for.

The Gameplay is sublime, the timings felt good, even map traversal felt good with the grappling hook.

The characters are fun, even if not exceptional, there were concepts that I found to be exceedingly interesting, the way that the story is told in the part of the game is interesting too, with that man telling the story as though it were history.

The art, I wish I had more words synonymous with magnificent in my vocabulary because the pixel art in this game is so freaking gorgeous. I would die happy if every single game I played was done in this style with this quality, everything is so smooth and charming.

The music. It starts out with the classic retro style, but as the game goes on it steadily sheds that styling and moves towards being its own thing, and I loved the entire thing. I hum the songs at times and I'm unashamed.

If you have any love for RPGs, old or new, play this game, it's so fun.\t\t\t\t\t
