\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game here is one of the best games i've played in my life so far.
If you are a fan of the Golden Sun games, the Mario&Luigi RPG's or Chrono Trigger this game is a must play.

The Artstyle and Art direction is superb, including the music (from the mellow track "Broken World" to the super upbeat "Dance of 1000 suns" there is lots o variety here.

The Combat is a cool combination of Puzzle and Timing (Some people call it QTE Combat, but i personaly found it more like rhythm parts to give the puzzles more action). Also there are options to customize the difficulty of combt encounters, ranging from full heal after each combat to autoblock, but also things like more damage recieved after mistimed blocks. I love the options, but there will be people who design their experience too hard/easy for themselves (looking at you, people who play games with guides exclusively)

The writing has its charm, but i could understand if it's too immature for some people. Again, personlly i really enjoyed it. The characters have all strenghts and flaws. Furthermore i realy love that the "Main Caracters" are not good with social stuff because of the things you play in the beginning. Which is cool because it gives other characters places to shine outside of combat.

The worst thing about the game is probably the demo tbh. I tried the demo, finished it, and didnt like the game at first. But the demo realy does a bad job with explaining the combat or setting anything up with the charcters you dont even know at this point.
The second worst thing is the minigame you can play at the Inns. I dont like chance based stuff so much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is a breath of fresh air, especially after the fiasco that was Chained Echoes. The art is vibrant, the world surprisingly rich, the characters are fun, and the game play is reasonably engaging, and the music is spectacular. A lot of peopled hyped up Sea of Stars to be the next Chrono Trigger which it is definitely not, but it's a good game in its own right and is well worth your time. There are some things that keep Sea of Stars from being a masterpiece but the pros far far outweigh the cons.

Pros:
- The character design is really well done with a lot of them getting a decent amount of development as the story progresses. You get a diverse lineup each with their own goals and backgrounds that made them feel complex and human.

- The battle system is the perfect amount of engaging for most people. The combat is turn based with timed hits and enemies come with a lock system that involves using specific types of attacks to break them. It's just enough depth to deter button mashing but not so complex that it would turn players off.

- The relics which are the game's way of allowing players to customize and adjust the game's difficulty is something more game developers should pick up on and implement. It makes the game so much more accessible and I absolutely loved that this was a feature.

- Like Chrono Trigger and other retro 2D RPG's there is a healthy amount of side content and secrets. Backtracking isn't super tedious and it was fun spending a few hours exploring optional bosses, dungeons, and finding hidden chests. Even better is the fact that the hint system is actually very easy to use and helpful.

- Wheels is a very fun minigame that I spent quite a few hours on. The fishing minigame was too easy and not all that fun but Wheels more than made up for that.

- There is a healthy amount of puzzle solving in Sea of Stars that is reminiscent of titles like Golden Sun and Lufia. This may be a turn off for some people but I found them to be a welcome addition that made the dungeons and towns a lot more engaging and fun.

- The art really felt like the classic 2D JRPG of the 90's got a modern day glow up with more vibrant color palettes and richer and more detailed backgrounds and sprites. As for the music, Yasunori Mitsuda was brought on as a guest composer so enough said.

Cons:
- A lot of people like Garl and I do too; he's funny, bright, and chivalrous, all the hallmarks of a likable character. The problem is that the story is ultimately all about Garl. The near silent protagonists have the spotlight stolen from them by Garl at the beginning of the game and it pretty much stays that way throughout the game. In comparison to Garl the other characters felt a bit empty and shallow and I would have preferred if the story and character development was a little more balanced.

- At the beginning of the game you have the option of picking Valere or Zale as your main protagonist and this does absolutely nothing. Literally nothing is different about the game so this choice does not matter and it's a shame as I was expecting there to be subtle but noticeable differences in the story that would have added replay value.

- The ending and true ending of the game was a bit of a letdown. The game spends a lot of time building up the villains of the game only for there to be no real climax and resolution at the end. This was especially the case with Brugraves and Erlina who I felt were underutilized and had their story fizzle out completely. Having the game end the way it did left a bittersweet taste in my mouth. Hopefully some DLC down the line will flesh things out and round things out better.

- The game is a bit too short. The second half of the game felt a bit rushed and I really wish there was more to do and more to explore. I'm sure the development team had bigger plans for the game that were cut short due to a lack of time but it did feel like the world building and overall game could have been a lot bigger and grander. It took me around 30ish hours to complete everything but had this been a 45+ hour game it would have been more successful.

While not a masterpiece, Sea of Stars is a great game that has a lot going for it. It borrows elements from a lot of titles such as Chrono Trigger, Super Mario RPG, Golden Sun, and Lufia but has enough substance and identity to stand on its own two feet. As far as 2D RPG's are concerned, Sea of Stars is my hands down favorite of 2023. Also Cartoon Network needs to pick this up for a Avatar The Last Airbender style show.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you have nostalgia for those SNES RPGs of old? If you do Sabotage Studio has a game for you. It will transport you back to those Chrono Trigger times with the chunky pixel graphics, turn based battles, snappy dialog, unique characters, and more. Even if you aren't an old person who played the SNES as a child there is still a lot to recommend about this game with the tight level design and varied game modes.

That said, there are some areas where the indie game rough edges show through. For example, every character starts out with a couple of starter spells. You expect to swap them out as the game goes on, but nope, they unlock one other spell and an ultimate ability but that's it. The wimpy single target heal is what you get. There is decent enemy variety but generally it's two or three types per area. The fishing minigame feels like it was added at gunpoint.

One neat thing is that fairly early on you pick up the ability to push blocks on the map, and a bit later you get a grappling hook. Usually you'd expect those things to be used in like one or two places and otherwise left forgotten, but most maps end up using both after you find them. Plus you will need to backtrack to hit up a couple of previously inaccessible areas that are unlocked by those abilities.

The difficulty level is roughly in line with those old SNES games as well. This was after the "Nintendo Hard" era of games when designers didn't feel the need to stretch games out to 80 hours by just adding extra 0s to the ends of stats. You can play straight through without doing all of the side content in a very adult friendly length of time. Some of the sidequests (mostly collection quests) can be a bit of a pain and require a lot of backtracking, but they're only necessary for the "good" alternate ending.

All in all I found it to be a delightful romp and well worth picking up.\t\t\t\t\t
