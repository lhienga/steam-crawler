\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: 4/5 stars, quality story-line and characters, interactive but repetitive combat, and a almost forgettable food and gear system.

I think this game is the perfect intro JRPG for someone who has never played JRPG's, or doesn't want anything too difficult. The game, while not being overly difficult, managed to really pull me in with the story and environment. If you let yourself ignore some of the redundant fights and forgettable gear upgrades, you might even be impressed by the different number of abilities and combo's this game has to offer.

Each fight felt like a puzzle I was trying to solve, which is a huge win for the lock system. I also appreciated the interactivity of fights. Being able to get additional damage on input timing was a fun mechanic. I also appreciated the different optional relics which could allow you to change the game and be more accessible to all people.

Overall, it was a great experience in itself. However, I was left wanting more variation in the combat and gear upgrades you get along the journey. The food system was also something easily forgettable, as I found it almost pointless throughout the game. Also, at times, some of the random encounters were more difficult than the boss fights because of how the lock system worked out.

After beating the game, I didn't really have the itch to do much of the side-content I left behind, however I do appreciate that it gives you that ability.

Glad I stuck it out to the end though, it's always great to support companies who make quality JRPG's that fill you with nostalgia.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArguably one of the better, if not the best RPG in a long time.

The story is pretty straightforward and is a good mix of not taking itself too seriously at times, to being pretty serious and brutal. Artstyle and music is this game's strong sides. Absolutely gorgeous and unique.

The game is not hard by any means and the unique combat system allows for some interesting combos and quite a few ways to tackle enemies and their special attacks.

Just like most other RPGs the game allows quite a bit of freedom to explore the world, grind levels and do side content. However levels don't mean a whole lot in this game and the game tries to keep you within certain level ranges depending on how far you've progressed the main story. Each level requires exponentially more XP than the previous and monsters in higher level zones provide many times more XP than the ones in previous. Which gives, on a surface level, the illusion of being able to grind levels, but in the grand scheme of things you really don't end up doing that and it's not really worth the time invested.

The amount of items you can equip and use is not bad, but the way you upgrade from one item to another feels very linear. There's barely any side-grades and most of them, with the exception of a few items, don't really offer anything unique.

I'd rate this 8 out of 10.
Overall the game is very very solid and it's a must play for any RPG player out there. I'd put this at 9 or even a 10 if they took some of the most treasured tropes of the older RPGs which this game is evidently trying to mimic. Like expanding the actual roster like in Chrono Chross/Trigger and less linear item progression. Biggest reason why someone wouldn't like this game would be the first 1-2 hours of the game which acts as a tutorial and building the foundation for the entire story/game, which can feel pretty slow.\t\t\t\t\t
