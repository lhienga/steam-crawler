\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is... alright. I'll recommend it because it's a pleasant diversion, but it's a far cry from the masterpieces it emulates. I'm a big fan of turn-based JRPGs, but this game doesn't really scratch the itch as much as I would like.

Graphics:
From a visual standpoint, this game is nothing short of stunning. The pixel art is beautiful, the lighting effects are impressive, and there's a fair amount of variety in everything.

Sound:
It's good enough to get the job done. There aren't any real bangers like Chrono Trigger or some of the more classic Final Fantasy titles, but it's good enough. I didn't hate it by the end.

Story:
The story is pretty bland. It has some moments that should be shocking and emotional, but the characters just aren't interesting. The protagonists have about as much personality as a vacuum cleaner, and the happy-go-lucky Garl is just a plot device. Serai has a dark, secret backstory which the game is interested in exploring for all of ten minutes, and then she's back to being generic fantasy assassin #9. The time guy has the potential to be really interesting, too, but he literally replaces himself with a doll and walks off for half the game. There's plenty of story to play through, but much of it is unremarkable and ultimately forgettable, and it feels more like the characters are watching a movie about the story than being personally affected by the story.

Gameplay:
The gameplay is alright. I didn't mind the timing mechanics as much as some other players, and there were a lot of interesting ideas going on. I do think the combat was too easy. By the end of the game, it's possible to stun-block the enemy in nearly every battle of consequence, which has the unfortunate side effect that some of the generic encounters with several enemies can be more challenging than bosses. That being said, I typically enjoy turn-based combat, and Sea of Stars has one of the more enjoyable systems I've encountered.

Verdict:
The game is the epitome of "good enough". Other than the graphics, there's nothing really remarkable about it. It helps fill a void in the market for classic JRPGs, but I personally found Chained Echoes to be a much more compelling title. It's worth a playthrough, but it's definitely not a game I'm going to log hundreds of hours on in replays.\t\t\t\t\t
