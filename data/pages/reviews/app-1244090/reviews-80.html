\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLemme start off with, amazing experience while you're high. Funniest plot I've ever gone through, and all around a really good time. I'm also extremely trash at games and even I can play this one.

By far one of my favorite games, I've ever played. Got introduced to Sabotage by one of my friends because he played the Messenger and loves Sea of Stars more than anyone I've ever seen. I got interested cause I thought the art was kinda cute for indie, and I haven't done a turn-based RPG in a while, now I'm addicted. They even give you items in the game to make it an easier or harder experience, like my dog could play this if I really wanted her too.

The sound track is amazing, I literally play it so my partner and I can go to sleep with noise. The characters personalities are amazing. Garl is the main character no one can tell me otherwise. The art is to die for as an animation major. Sure you've got minor bugs here and there, like being able to walk through a random rock on the wall, but that makes it even more enjoyable. Now it's my mission to find any secrets or bugs I can. Nothing breaks the game or the experience it's just funny watching your team get stuck on a clam for 20 minutes while you complete a puzzle by yourself.

I will absolutely be selling my soul to this game and getting all of the achievements, no matter how long it takes me. It's absolutely worth it for the memories and the bonding moments I get to have with my friends.

I warn anyone on PC, though it might just be my game, but there's an area where you use a crane machine. I swear never ever hold 2 controls at the same time unless you want the most demonic cries of machinery. The noise is so bugged and so loud but way to funny. I held W and A at the same time for 10 minutes straight just to hear the cries of that poor machine.

At the end of the day;
Story: 10/10
Music: 100/10
Artwork/Graphics: 10/10
Gameplay: 10/10
Replayable: 1000/10
Garl: Best Boy/10\t\t\t\t\t

While this game does have a fun battle system that makes boss battles enjoyable, along with a solid soundtrack that is where the praise stops. The characters are less than one dimensional not to mention just poorly designed overall, the dialogue is either extremely flat or extremely cringe. The story, all of it's lore and direction are all trash and seem to have been written by a 3rd grader. While I understand stories aren't the main element to a game in many cases I feel like RPGs are different. This narrative may have worked for a simple side scrolling game, Maybe going for an RPG was a bit too ambitious for the dev group. I'll give it a 5/10 to be nice but it really feels to be barely less than half of a game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: A boring JRPG that doesn't excel in anything but pixel graphics. If your looking for the a great old-school like JRPG (e.g. FF 4/6, Chrono Trigger, Breath of Fire, Dragon Quest 1-6 and etc). Look elsewhere this is mediocre at best. You will struggle just finishing this game due to the boring storyline/characters and repetitive combat.

Story - Truly forgettable. It took about 20 hours in before the game got interesting but at that point it was too late. This would be acceptable if they had interesting characters but the main protagonists are bland. All I would say is that one early game character should have never been in the game and more focus should have been spent on the others.

Graphics - Top-notch pixel graphics. This is one department this game truly excels at.

Game play - This title needed to focus on a few things and do it extremely well and it could have been here. Exploration = non-existent. The game pretty much forces you into one direction with no side-quests. Combat in this game initially was interesting but just became a huge chore by mid-game. Normal fights just take too long, the developers should have focused more on difficult boss fights that required the players to carefully use the game's mechanic of preventing enemy actions. In addition, all the characters learn their entire move-set relatively early in the game and there's nothing to look forward to in leveling your character and NO picking a stat upgrade is not what I look forward to.

Sound - Meh, some tracks were great but overall forgettable.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game that hits all the right notes but fails to produce a beautiful melody.

You guys, I wanted so badly to love this game. Really, I did. When I first booted it up and started to play, the nostalgia hit me so hard that I started tearing up! But the more I played, the more I found the that the mechanics that were implemented were shallow and basic.
The game is 1 part Chrono Trigger, 1 part Super Mario RPG and 1 part Zelda.
It takes the best parts of each game and implements it into its own. The issue that arises, however, is that these mechanics are not properly fleshed out and expanded upon.
I will give you an example. From the very start of the game, one of your characters has a skill called the Moonarang. It is essentially a skill where you need to time your hits and the better you are at getting the timing right, the more damage you do. This at first was extremely fun and engaging but as time went on, the fact that this skill did not evolve, change or get better or more complex made combat feel very dull, boring and repetitive. Worse yet is that this one skill is essentially the strongest DPS your team has if you get good at it, meaning that you NEED to use this constantly or else your damage output goes down significantly.
It just feels like the devs had a great idea, they started it, but did not build upon it.

Other things that bugged me:
1) Remember when in games like Final Fantasy or Chrono Trigger, you first get your Airship? How cool was that? You finally had the chance to visit anywhere on the map and do anything you wanted right? How freeing was that? How cool did that make you feel? Well, in Sea of Stars, you get a similar mode of transportation but guess what? All the cool new places you wanted to visit, well you can't access it now or you need a special item etc. They give you the Illusion of freedom, meanwhile, you are 100% still on tracks and must go to this one location for the next story beat.
2) For the amount of hype the devs touted about the musical score, I was expecting a lot more than what I got. The only score I loved was the one that plays in the Archivist's home. This also was the first song that greets you when you start the game. Maaaaaaan did that get me PUMPED!!! But slowly, I realised that the rest of the musical score was serviceable at best.
3) Story was basic. I felt nothing about my characters or their plight in the world they lived in.

Now for things I enjoyed:
1) The art design and colour palette used are fantastic. The incorporation of pixel art combined with small anime clips was a huge hit!
2) The Chrono Trigger combat style
3) There is a mini game in Sea of Stars that is very well made. It is complex enough to want to engage with it regularly and I found myself looking forward to playing it in every single town I visited. Excellent job here devs !

I wanted to conclude by saying this. This is a great first attempt from the Devs for a game like this. It has the potential to be Amazing (and believe me, I really wanted it to be) but it lost a lot of steam once you past 10 hours of play.
I wish I could give this a Neutral Rating because I want Thierry Boulanger and his team to make more games like this but I also want them to understand that just because they found a diamond does not automatically mean that it has a mirror shine. Keep at it guys.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
-Solid (but safe) game if you are in the mood for an older style rpg
-Twists on the combat system make it more engaging than a typical turn based rpg
-World is very pretty and areas are themed well
-Characters are likable and decently written
-The dialogue in this game has personality
-Music is great in general, especially the boss themes
-Didn't mind the plot as much as other reviewers, it's actually decent for the genre

Cons:
-Though I liked the innovations, I felt they could have done a lot more with the combat system they created; definitely some wasted potential
-The conclusion of the main story did not feel very satisfying
-I wish there was an option to use the "A" button less when traversing terrain; sometimes it's a pain to require both hands when you are simply trying to move from one place to another
-Immersion is sometimes ruined by stuff like hookshot targets or blocks in many areas which have no in-universe reason for being there
-About 90% of the puzzles felt overwhelmingly easy, to the point where they were more busywork/content padding rather than actual challenges. The late game solstice shrines are examples of the potential this game has, but was not shown off nearly enough
-Perhaps my biggest gripe, the game doesn't always respect your time when it comes to backtracking, especially early on where you will have to run through levels for several minutes to complete sidequests

Overall I'd give Sea of Stars a 7/10. It was worth the money but I can't really say this game is a masterpiece or that everyone necessarily has to play it. If you like games such as Chrono Trigger and you have an itch to experience a modern take on the genre, then go for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars I heard was a successor to Chrono Trigger. I have not done all the side quest, but I believe I have a solid enough opinion to form my review of this game… no this gem. Because I am going to call this game exactly what it is.
First off, a successor to Chrono Trigger, that’s still out there so I’ll hold off on if it is or not, but it does pay respect to it and in all the right ways. Story, Humor, Characters, and yes combat. However, I don’t think it’s a successor because the game is just too damn good and unique. Sea of Star steps up to the plate of a classic SNES JRPG and holds its own. It then looks at you says hold my beer, everything from sprites, graphics, music, combat, and story. It is solid with no frame rate issues and easy to use and very rewarding combat. It then looks at you and says just wait, you haven’t even seen anything yet. It takes advantage of the story and blows you away. The music for this game is too damn good. But when you have such a legend helping you compose music, what can you say but hell yes.
\tMy favorite part of this game is the music, the story is both compelling and will make you feel things. I won’t say what but your be in for a treat. It also has the guts to be as bold as it was and bravo! I want more, I would very much love to see more Sea of Star Games. My only regret was not backing such a S tier game on kick starter because I learn it was on there to late.
I would also love to see more games with Eric W. Brown and Yasunori Mitsuda doing music because yeah. Just wait, that moment above your know it when it hits. I highly recommend this gem.
