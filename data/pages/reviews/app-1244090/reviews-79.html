The sound is crisp, fits the tone, and seamlessly fills in the atmosphere of this interesting world. I've been playing it with my controller and I can say I don't have lag, stutter, or missed inputs with it. Wonderful game that brings back memories of Saturdays spent breaking all my RPG games out and binge gaming on them. Well done developers, scriptwriters, artists, and everyone who put their mark on this game.
Caution! Nerd Out Imminent!!
If you like 16bit Zelda, if you had the fortune of playing Eastward, Crono Trigger, Secret of Mana, Dragon Quest(Warrior) or ANY Final Fantasy(SNES/Famicom era), I could also go on and on about Breath of Fire, Lufia ect ect but I digress... you will love this addition to your library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA love letter to Chrono Trigger with some Mario RPG mechanics thrown in, and it manages to stand right alongside those two with excellent writing, likable characters, stellar world building and beautiful 16-bit art.
The overall story is amazing, and there are a few points that elevate it alongside some of the greatest RPGs ever made.

2023 has been a great year for gaming and because of that I think that some people have overlooked this one, due to some AAA games taking the spotlight and it would be a shame if this one was forgotten.
This game is an absolute masterpiece. Definitely worth picking up at full price.

I highly recommend a controller, as there is some realtime actions in combat and it just feels better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBasic game, extremely well done. Like going home and eating your mom's food after decades on the road.

Editing the review to mention some of the flaws of the game:
- the level design in general is not that great, with every tiny room requiring some mindlessly easy puzzle to be solved first. Eventually it just feels like a chore.
- not sure if it's something with my steam deck, but I had to put both the game's and the screen's brightness to the max to be able to see things; before that I was constantly missing objects and having trouble with moving around because some soil path looked like a hole in the ground or something.
- while the game is 2d, there's a lot of different layers of height and depth that get mixed in together and may be hard to tell apart. You often don't know if some platform can be climbed onto or jumped out of until you try to do it.
- battle system gets repetitive after a while.

Despite those problems, the game is still great and more than make up for those things in other areas.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a wonderful game. It's like a classic you hold dear: great music, charming characters and an unforgettable story.

The mechanics are familiar but fresh, especially when it relates to combat. The story takes an hour or so to really kick off. A fair amount of exposition is to be expected when setting up a good story though. So be patient. It pays off.

Speaking of the story, with absolutely no spoilers: The twists and the turns! The secrets you'll find! The items that will take you to places not explored in this type of game.

This isn't Chronotrigger, but it IS a time machine of sorts. If you enjoyed the JRPGs of the 90s, then make space for this one.

I had a 4-day weekend recently and I couldn't put it down. I unraveled every secret I could. I may have neglected my Honey Do list until Sunday night because of it.

It's got such heart. It's such a good time. The Developers gave this time and attention and it shows.

I'm probably riding off the cloud of just wrapping up the game, but I can't think of a weakness. It's top tier and it has earned its place among similar games that trail blazed the way so that it could exist.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only recommend this game for those that are new to JRPGs, that need a place to start. For seasoned fans of the genre, I say you can get way better elsewhere. Sea of Stars is not a bad game. It plays well, has no bugs, and is well paced. The graphics are beautiful, with mostly memorable music. Where I have problems, lie with it's story and characters, along with it's gameplay.

Sea of Stars has a pretty predictable story line, that ends with many plot strings unfinished. It has an abruptly poor ending, leaving you really questioning how it could really end the way it does. With even it's True Ending somehow being worse, and a total waste of time. It has characters that are one dimensional, that never grow from when their introduced.

The gameplay functions fine, but it's little quirks is where some of my issues land. One of the game's biggest inspirations gameplay-wise, I see come from the Mario and Paper Mario RPG series. Being able to time your attacks, to give more damage, and later to gain MP doing so. On the defense side, you would think timing your blocks would result in taking less damage. No, you gain more MP. This results in many deaths in the party, regardless of if you can time a block. With Party members being able to revive themselves after a couple turns, the entire party sharing the same Exp pool, which can end with battles finishing with dead party members still leveling up, gives us a game where death truly doesn't matter.

This is why I say if you're new to JRPGs, this is a good place to start. But as fans of the genre, we're left with a less than challenging experience, that we'll soon forget.\t\t\t\t\t
