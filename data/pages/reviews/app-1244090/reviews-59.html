\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis has taken many elements from retro games to come out with a enjoyable game.

No random battle encounters, enemies either patrol a given area or wait for an ambush. Attacking an enemy to start the battle gives an advantage.

Battles are turned based with timed prompts to do extra damage or guard an attack.
One highlight is the enemy lock casting system where they create a "lock" of sorts before casting an ability. Hitting enemies with all the corresponding attack attributes will break the lock and stop that ability from being activating.

Healing is generous with campfires for resting with no costs

I found the game's difficulty on the easier side and there are relics to make the game easier, add QoL features or add additional challenge.\t\t\t\t\t

Story? Linear. But that's not a bad thing.
Progression of areas? Linear. But that's not a bad thing.
Obtaining items Linear. But that's not a bad thing.
and abilities?

A lot of the ways this game handles how you interact with it is fairly linear, but introduces segments where you're (somewhat) free to explore each and every map, but must do the objective in a straight line.

This isn't necessarily a bad thing. It streamlines things for the player; it makes a lot of abilities, key items, ingredients very easy to find, and it's an enjoyable romp from start to (almost) finish.

I will say, the ending does feel a bit like a cop-out for a boss fight. I won't spoil, but it could have been done with a bit more of a bombastic finish. If they plan to make a sequel, I'd gladly support it.\t\t\t\t\t
