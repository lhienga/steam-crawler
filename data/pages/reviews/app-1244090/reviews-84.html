\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably one of my favorite RPGs, now that I've finished it.

+ Combat has timed mechanics akin to many Mario RPGs, and stresses that they aren't necessary to do damage; even so, there's features that improve feedback on timing, and some that activate them automatically, but with a proc chance or minor downside. Also present are "locks", a turn-timed match game with damage types that lets you interrupt some moves. There's a handful of abilities that manipulate them further down the line that make them demand more strategic choices as well. Kind of like Octopath's battle systems, except I actually wanted to finish this game!
+ "No grinding" is a point Sabotage pitched the game with, and they followed through. No XP grinding is required at all. Even money and drops have alternate sources.
+ The graphics and music are also huge selling points for this game, and they deliver; the environments and set pieces are cool, and the character design and music can both go unexpectedly hard.
+- Looking at other reviews, the story seems to be a point of contention. I agree with what most people are saying, but overall, I don't think the story's all that bad.
Carrying over from The Messenger is Sabotage's sense of humor, which sets up some pretty great jokes. They can range from references to in-character gags, and somehow there's a character that does both. The humor in the writing is one of the high points of the story for me.
Despite that, I agree the character writing isn't exactly the best; Zale and Valere, the player characters, have very little personality (as an unfortunately expected tradeoff for being player inserts), yet are narratively and mechanically the most important characters in the game; Garl- my boy, the GOAT, a king among men- carries the plot, probably next to his endless supply of apples and sandwiches, but his character trait really is just being the greatest friend in existence (Granted, I don't really mind, and I doubt you will either); other characters are refreshing, but don't often get as much spotlight as the main three. I do really love the fifth party member, though- they turned out to be very interesting.
However, what really carried the story for me was how every touch of foreshadowing pays off. Some plot points are easier to figure out than others, but I always felt rewarded when I accurately saw something coming, and everything I didn't expect was an awesome or interesting surprise. I highly recommend reading every lore entry Teaks has as you unlock them: very few aren't somehow relevant to the plot.
- To get the most satisfying ending, you do need to complete some side quests, including a plot-irrelevant fetch quest. I didn't mind most of them- the majority of them end with the harder bosses in the game, and reward you with the best weapons for each character- but it's a little painful to be locked out of an ending by fetching all 100 eggs or whatever. Thankfully it's nothing internet guides can't help with- I personally recommend IGN's, as the fan wiki isn't completely accurate.

Sea of Stars took me a bit over 35 hours to complete, and I still wish there was a bit more. I generally want to get at least one hour of gameplay out of every dollar I pay for something, and Sea of Stars delivered! If you get this game on sale in the future, it'll be extra worth it.

tl;dr: The combat is good, the art and music are good, and the story isn't perfect but has a lot of fun and good things about it. My only complaints with the game are with the story, and even those become minor when you approach it with the right attitude; don't expect narrative perfection and you'll have a fun time.

Oh, and if you played their other game, The Messenger- both games are connected. Keep an eye out for subtle and not-so-subtle nods to the other game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you loved Chrono Trigger and Super Mario RPG, you'll probably love this game. It's an incredible homage to/parody of those titles, and there is a staggering amount of love and detail in this game. And this came from a Kickstarter?!

Sea of Stars is clearly designed to be super-accessible and playable by anyone - veteran gamers will not find much depth or challenge here. Despite the wonderful freedom of movement and traversal mechanics, it's a nearly-linear experience - you're basically in for a joyride filled with crazy plot, characters, names, and places. The art and environments are absolutely gorgeous, and the dynamic lighting elevates the experience. The lock system adds a lot to the aging genre of turn-based combat, but tuning leaves something to be desired - once I had a decent grasp on the game, every fight became an absolute cakewalk. The ending also leaves a lot to be desired - you have to go back and find everything and do every side quest to unlock the True Ending, which was significantly more satisfying.

Sea of Stars is a beautiful evolution of an old genre, but somehow also feels like it doesn't do enough with its new mechanics. It's hard to lay criticism at the feet of a game that has clearly had so much put into it.\t\t\t\t\t

Those are the only two I would upgrade for most of the game after a certain point since they were the most helpful.
The Story is stupid, and everything anyone does is followed up with, "That's impossible!" , "Its never been done!". The dialogue is atrocious. I also absolutely cannot stand the names they made for things. Overly complex nouns. I couldn't understand that. Garl's character was also abysmal.

The good stuff is the visuals. I was ecstatic watching this game play out, honestly. It really is the best retro looking game out there with it's dynamic lighting and effects.
Sound design is also fantastic. I was wearing headphones most of the time and it was impressive. The music is also really stellar on occasion and made me feel like I was playing Golden Sun.

The saving grace is the visuals, sound design and the occasionally the combat. Later in the game when you master some of the timing with each hit it can be really satisfying as it does require skill, as well as using the correct weakness to cancel out any power moves the enemies have. I enjoyed that design.

Overall, I recommend it. Just don't expect good writing, pacing, and story direction.\t\t\t\t\t

-If you want a mature story with character development, this isn't it. I'd recommend this as a gateway to the JRPG genre for those starting out.
-Combat system gets tedious mid to late game. I got so tired of bouncing moonrangs and timing hits at the end that I turned on all the buff relics just to breeze through the last chapters.

All in all: 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pretty hyped for Sea of Stars because it came from The Messenger devs and it looked very similar to Chrono Trigger with the art style and overworld. I will say, for a 16 bit style JRPG, it's absolutely gorgeous. Some of the animations are really neat and the lighting is amazing for a 16 bit game, particularly during some of the magic abilities. The soundtrack is also awesome.

The story is great but I do have some gripes with it. Spoilers obviously:

Garl is such an awesome character and I'm not necessarily mad that he died but I feel like he died too early. I think the game would have flowed a lot better if he dies, you fight the final boss, and the ending is a tribute to Garl as a bittersweet triumph in saving the world. You save the world but lose a close friend. In addition to that, I feel like the game kind of goes off the rails a bit. You play the whole game in a setting prior to technology, you lose Garl, and immediately go into a world of robots and machines. It was a really weird shift where the whole setting changed abruptly.

B'st was a cool character to add to your party but I just wasn't expecting it. Every character that joins your party has sort of a build up and then B'st just shows up and joins your party. I feel like the devs wanted to give you something since you lost Garl and I just never really cared enough for B'st like I did Garl, Serai, or Resh'an because of it. You also don't get the true ending. I looked up a guide for it and there's a lot of work to be done. I couldn't be bothered to grind all that extra and decided to just watch a Youtube video. I like the true ending more than the ending we got but I would have been very disappointed if I went through all that grinding just for a different ending that to be honest was about 2-3 minutes of different dialogue.

I was a bit disappointed in the direction of the story and it made me knock some points off but overall, the game was still very enjoyable. Even with my issues with the story, I'd still recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
