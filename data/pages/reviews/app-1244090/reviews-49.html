\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust got past where the demo ends and have just hit Wraith Island - First and foremost give the demo a whirl, hopefully it has you wanting to play more like I did by the end!

The story kicks off about our characters Valere and Zale wrapping up their trials to become Solstice Warriors ready to set sail in to the open world to be part of the effort to stop the final threat to the world known as "Dwellers". One remains and after many years its your turn to bring this age of turmoil to an end.

You will venture forth as either character but they will be in your party utilising 4 different damage types to begin with, Blunt/Bladed/Lunar/Solar which you will need to utilise to exploit weaknesses of your foes and even stop their attacks in turn. Its a pretty generic turn based combat system with some reactionary bonuses if you're able to pull them off for additional damage/healing/mitigation. Its simple but fun and satisfying to pull off as not every enemy is the same and you have to see their patterns/abilities to get the upper hand. Its not hard and the gameplay is a little forgiving with its systems, if you were new to turn-base RPGs, I'd say this is actually a good one to start out with as there's even modifiers with Relics if you truly need them!

When you're not fighting, you'll be exploring, climbing, falling and puzzling your way through the world and its denizens for ingredients for cooking, new recipes, skill scrolls, treasure and getting past obstacles in your path to the objective. There's even fishing and a tavern game, Wheels! There's plenty to see, do and its not overwhelming or overly complicated :)

Quick-fire: The characters are good (Garl is a true friend!), their story and goal simple to understand, gameplay rewarding, the world vibrant and a pleasure to run through. The music is great and will get you vibing, I had heard the soundtrack a little before but in the moment, I feel its better!

As someone who struggles to properly enjoy games, (I had a bad on-and-off addiction to Playstation trophies for quite sometime and burned out) Coming back with Sea of Stars, I'm smiling, laughing, saying out loud how much I enjoy and appreciate this game. Truly this is fun, brilliant and a nice reminder for what games should be- not a chore or a 2nd job but genuinely something to look forward to and have a good time!

I can't wait to delve back in to Wraith Island, its creepy atmosphere and story background has me eager to see why such a place exists! It's residents are already a little strange but understandably so.
I'm having a blast and on the Steam Deck OLED, this thing thrives as any other RPG does on this device.

Newbies and RPG veterans alike, definitely check this out, there's a free demo! :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn an era of gaming where every game seems like it needs to be bigger than the rest with a million side quests, or hours of grind, or a difficulty so great that you have to play 5 hours a day to be any good at it, this game is none of that.

It's concise, it tells a linear story with some nice ups and downs to it, it's got a simple but satisfying combat system, and as long as you fight every battle that naturally comes your way you'll never need to grind. There are some side quests and mini-games, but they are easy to grasp and they never overstay their welcome. Instead they're mostly completed pretty naturally along the way of the main quest. If you grew up in the late 80s/early 90s playing RPGs on SNES, then this is gonna be familiar-but-fresh territory.

I paid full price for this game and it was worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
