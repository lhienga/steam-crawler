\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou have no idea how excited I was for this game when it was announced. I ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ adore Chrono Trigger; it's a game deserving of the accolades it gets. That's why it pains me to say Sea of Stars is the most disappointing game I've ever played.

**I want to preface the rest of this by saying Sea of Stars is not a bad game, not remotely. Hell, I'm aware I'm in the minority here and I'm happy for the people who enjoy this game. I wish I could.**

My first point of contention is the core gameplay. The battle system is very well put together, and I like that the game forces you to diverisfy your attacks between normal attacks (to regen MP and build up Live Mana) and special attacks. The game is mechanically solid, no doubt. The issue is... the lack of attacks really. All that praise I had to having to diversify your attacks is almost entirely undone by just how few special attacks you get. You'll be using stuff Sunball and Moonerang almost exclusive for far too much of the game. Buffs and Debuffs are almost entirely absent, and this game I feel sorely needed them. Combat becomes pretty mind-numbing because of the overly simple movesets. After playing the demo, I was concerned that bosses would regularly be HP sponges. This is somewhat the case, the aforementioned limited movesets compounds this, but it never got to an unbearable point admittedly. You also go a huge chunk of the game with the same 3-4 party members, and I know I'm a broken record, but the lack of variety in attack effects makes some of them feel alike. Combos are also horribly underutilized, you'll be using Zale and Valere's Solstice Strike for most of the game because the other Combos take too long to build up in normal encounters, and this one is just so practical since they both regen MP from it (they both perform a normal attack simultaneously). Every character combination should've had a a Normal Attack Combo like this imo. Rq, felt I should mention the only Game Over I suffered was this one point in the game's latter half where the part gets split up into 3 groups, and the enemy numbers in following area kinda just tore my ass inside out. The only part of the game I thought was remotely cheap.

The Relic system is the one thing I don't get. The only one I used was the one to make a star appear when timing an attack correctly (though sometimes the sweet spot felt really vague for certain moves). So, most of these seem to be Easy Mode modifications. Alright, doesn't bug me since I can just leave them off. What I don't get is that you have to buy them... Why? Also iirc Rainbow Conchs are only used for purchasing more of these, so if you want to play mostly vanilla then any time your reward for exploring is a Rainbow Conch, it's entirely unsatisfying. But yeah, why are these, again what I presume to be Easy Mode Modifications, something you have to buy. I don't see how that remotely benefits people looking for an easier time. And you toggle Relics on or off, not equip them to a specific party member. This system makes no sense to me.

A quick positive, this game is absolutely breathtaking visually. Genuinely, one of the best looking games I've ever played. The sprite quality is absolutely phenomenal, characters are well animated, and there's a real sense of scope.

The soundtrack is also very good, Eric W. Brown did a solid job. I'm not certain to the extent of Yasunori Mitsuda's contributions to the OST (I tried looking up lists and get some conflicting reports), with exception to Coral Cascades since the demo flat out said he composed it.

This game is also dripping with attention to detail. I got big, dumb grin on my face listening to the Pirate versions of the OST, but also seeing that each player in the band would start and stop playing in accordance to if they had a part in the track or if you spoke to them. I don't need these little details to enjoy a game, but I'm always pleased to see them.

I cannot give much praise to the writing, this game's story is a big load of *nothing*. The story is a lot of "Go here, do this, suddenly a boss fight against a dude we'll never see again". The characters are far too basic, though not insufferably bad at least. What we're left is some characters who clearly have potential, but are beyond underused and left undercooked. I think I liked Garl the most, if only because of how positive he is. There's also a point where he basically tells the main villain to ♥♥♥♥ off and I thought that was pretty funny. To give some credit, the game does have some decently funny moments. But they're just that, moments.

Without spoiling to much, this game does have a True Ending and I just could not be bothered to see it through. I looked it up and yeah I don't feel like I missed a damn thing. It also ruins one story moment I thought was genuinely good, like damn I thought the devs were really going through with a certain story moment but nope. Anyway another reason I didn't bother was because I felt the final portion of the game was just completely necessary and flat out bad. Like, I didn't care about the story yeah, but all the ♥♥♥♥ aside from the final dungeon was stuff I'd classify as "jumping the shark", it was that intrusive to me.

One last minor positive I have is that I think the game's dungeon design is overall better than most other RPGs's dungeon design. Hell, there was one dungeon that reminded me straight up of a Zelda Water Temple (Great Bay specifically).

Anyway, I'm done complaining. I wanted to love this game, genuinely, but just couldn't. I see the passion, I really do, but the game just feels like a proof of concept for me. Something a sequel could improve upon big time. I think the best case scenario is you haven't played Chrono Trigger, or are just a casual turn based RPG enjoyer. I do want to say that this game doesn't feel derivative of Chrono Trigger, it absolutely stands out. I just feel the game underutilized its battle system. This isn't like Bravely Default II scenario for me, a game I feel is just a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ mess design wise (not to mention not remotely holding a candle in comparison to its predecessors, but I'm getting off topic. Ngl I just wanted an excuse to bash BD2 real quick). Sea of Stars is mechanically solid, I just feel it doesn't use those mechanics anywhere close to their full potential. I'm starting to repeat myself so I'll wrap this up. This game only took me about 25 hours, but the lack of variety, and my distaste for the finale, made it feel so much longer. Very much a one and done game for me.

I know I just spent like 20 minutes writing a review bashing the game, but I genuinely wish Sabotage Studio the best in their next endeavor. And again, more power to the people who do like this game. I'm glad y'all find enjoyment in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
