\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is a love letter to 90s JRPGs. Conveniently, the Super Mario RPG remake also came out in 2023 and is a good reference point, though the biggest influence here is clearly Chrono Trigger. The visual design is as close as you could reasonably get without bringing on Akira Toriyama to do the character designs. And they actually got composer Yasunori Mitsuda to do some guest tracks. That said, under the nostalgic veneer the game has clearly availed itself of modern amounts of disk space as it's packed full of custom sprite work and far more music than those old JRPGs could ever have hoped to fit in a SNES cartridge.

Mechanically, the game does preserve a couple of Chrono Trigger conventions, but ultimately seems to take most of its lessons from Square-Enix's more recent efforts in the genre, particularly Bravely Default and the HD-2D series. They then inject a few of its own ideas into the mix and the result eventually settles into a fun rhythm, though I wish they'd shaken things up a little in the late game as the rhythm had become just a little too comfortable by then. Or perhaps I should've tried tightening the difficulty options.

Story-wise, I found Sea of Stars to be most effective for me when it was ushering its protagonists between memorable set-piece fights and surprising plot twists while foreshadowing more to come. Having so much more room for art and music than those old JRPGs really helps here, as the game can leverage them to make each place feel new and unique. This helps bring atmosphere to individual dungeons and those boss fights set pieces, and the contrast between them as we switch from place to place builds a strong sense of forward momentum.

Conversely, I felt the story was at its weakest when it didn't have that momentum. It takes a little while to build up from the start of the game, and the side-quests in the endgame feel very much like side-content and removed that sense of momentum. After them, the final dungeon isn't long enough to re-build that momentum before hurling you into an ending that can feel a little abrupt. That said, given the light adventure tone the game strikes there's only so much you can expect.

Overall, it's a fun JRPG with some great set-pieces that generally moves at a good clip. Genre fans will almost certainly enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 26 hours into the Game, here's what I think. Loved the game's story, soundtrack (amazing) animations and characters. The puzzle where good. The combat was ok, but honestly it needs more variety of base skills to make it less repetitive. The only think that I really didn't like was the last part, it feels rushed and the ending feels unsatisfying, at least for me. The characters background is ignored and there's this really sad feeling that the great plot twists, background story and lore for all the characters (even the enemies) is wasted. You dont even get to fight the "real" final boss per se. I don't want to spoil anything so Im trying to give the least amount of information, but darn, that ending really ruined the Game for me. It could have been so much better and could have been such a longer Game, it makes me really sad for the potential and lore wasted into the endgame. TL;DR: A fun and emotional gameplay, ruined by a lazy ending of what could have been a perfect RPG.
8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVisually gorgeous. Fun music. Great mechanics that encourage me to push buttons a lot, but not too complicated. My only complaint is the writing is a little too sweet for my tastes. Wasn't expecting it to be dark or anything, but I was hoping it wouldn't be so quick to use over-enthusiasm as a trope to cut off conversations that felt important. I also have a hard time getting into a story where there are people hinting at knowing something but not giving anything to latch onto until we can learn more information. Both of these examples are forms of teasing information without giving any substance and I quickly no longer expect any information to be given. It takes away from my excitement because I've been teased too much with empty potential rather than given small amounts of information. It's the opposite of info dumping making a reader bored. It instead makes me apathetic to any world information. But bright lights go bounce and I like that.\t\t\t\t\t
