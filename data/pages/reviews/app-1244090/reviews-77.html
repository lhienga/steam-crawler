\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame started out great. Beautiful pixel art, good soundtrack, and an interesting combat mechanic with timing button presses.

But after 20 hours or so of combat encounters, the game started to get really boring. Story hasn't gotten me hooked, characters were bland and uninteresting, and combat remained the same throughout the game with the same few skills. It didn't help that the timed input mechanic were pretty much necessary. Otherwise your skills would do piss damage and random fights would last 5 minutes long.

That was the biggest deal breaker for me. That bore me and I started actively avoiding enemies. I would have actually finished the game otherwise if combat was not so tedious.

Overall, had high expectations for the game. Started really good. But man does the combat really turn me off. I really tried to push through the game, but unfortunately I can't stand boring games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good, but my interest in it is waning.

The implementation of action commands (i.e. timing 'A' to do more/receive less damage) adds a bonus level of engagement to the already intriguing turn-based combat. New features that keep the battles fresh can take a while to get introduced, but the strategic cycle that happens between using magic, mana returning on hit, live mana, enemies' locks, combo attacks, and more results in a varied and unique combat system. The comparison to Chrono Trigger does exist in quite a few areas, but the blend of all those above gameplay elements makes Sea of Stars feel fresh, for a while.

It's the story where the Sea of Stars suffers the most. There are a lot of cutscenes and I don't really have an attachment to any of the characters. Only one party member is given any sort of personality, the rest default to 'gruff warrior without interests or thoughts beyond defeating evil'. And that one character is sort of tossed around as the sympathy baby. I caught myself mashing A to get through conversations pretty early on. To be fair the plot is far from awful and it certainly takes you on a grand journey full of strange places and people. I just can’t help but wish that more was done to prevent the narrative from feeling drawn out as it shifts between grandiose objectives.

Another piece of why I’m beginning to lose the motivation to complete Sea of Stars is that the time outside of combat/cutscenes is spent walking around pressing ‘A’ to scale cliffs or do basic, unfailable platforming. Oh, and don’t forget about all the extremely simple box/switch puzzles. Thankfully, the world is extremely visually pleasing. There only exists criminally good pixel art that’s made all the better with the lighting effects. (Seriously. The pixel art/animation in Sea of Stars deserves heaps of praise. However, interacting with the world in all these trivial ways devalues your relationship with the lovely spaces in which the game takes place.

Now that gameplay is starting to even out and the story is becoming the focus, I'm starting to feel like the best times I've had with Sea of Stars are already behind me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars had such potential, and it's a shame as a fellow gamer I can't recommend it. The game has some of the most gorgeous art I've ever seen in a pixel art game, and the OST is filled to the brim with absolute bangers. Unfortunately, the story is one of the worst I've ever encountered in a JRPG before. While other game genres can get by with lackluster stories, an interesting story and deep chracters are the cornerstones to what makes a JRPG a JRPG.

Throughout the story, the motivations of characters make less and less sense as the story progessess. The game ends up turning into a myriad of sequential fetch quests from place to place without ever giving the characters time to reflect on what would be naturally pivotal moments, or new revelations have little-to-no impact on the characters themselves. Moreover, the main protangonists have almost no personality themselves. Everything just keeps trucking along and near the end, you go from place to place at such breakneck speeds that the story writers forgot that they're not writing for a 2D side scrolling video game anymore, and chose to make a JRPG. This is nowhere near the likes of chrono trigger, it's a bit of an insult to say so. I would stay away from this if you're looking for a JRPG to sink your teeth into; it's simply not worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very good game that has very few ideas that haven't been done before, but does none of them poorly. You will either be fondly reminded of things you like, or feel like its derivative of better titles.

Gameplay is very good but has a couple of minor annoyances where some moves feel frustrating to time.
Music is never distracting but a good chunk of it is forgettable ("stars align on the assembly line" goes super hard though)
Story is solid but annoyingly requires knowledge of the dev's previous game to fully follow and lacks a lot of depth in the main characters.
Visuals are basically the only thing I have no grievances with. This game is pretty as hell.

Easy recommend, but I can understand why people have issues with it.\t\t\t\t\t

The somewhat repetitive combat. While there are many new mechanics introduced, there isn't as much diversity as other RPG's. Overall, the combat's great in my opinion, but I can definitely see where others are coming from.

Next, the story is decent. A large amount of reviews completely ♥♥♥♥ on this game's story, but I don't think it's that bad. Sure, it's not an award-winning story, but I've definitely seen better. I haven't seen some parts of the game, but there are interesting twists.

Finally, the exploration aspects could have been fleshed out. While there are quite a couple of complaints from others, I specifically thought a lot of the 'puzzles' were pretty meh. For most of them, you don't really have to think, at least in the main story anyways. There is side content, however, that provides more difficult challenges (in the form of puzzles AND combat), which was nice to see.

In terms of pros, this game has so many. The soundtrack is fantastic, having around 200 pieces (including the variants). There are day and night songs for most, if not all environments, and some tracks having specific variants. There's also many banger themes. Even the regular boss theme absolutely slaps (it's probably my favourite out of the sound track).

The art style is constantly beautiful. Honestly, I could probably take a screenshot anywhere in the game, and ihen use it as a wallpaper (slight exaggeration, but you get the idea). There's also cinematic sometimes, which have a very nice animation to them, fitting well with the rest of the game.

While I haven't played the messenger, I can attest to there being many references it. As openly stated, this game is somewhat of a prequel to the messenger, so if you've ever played it, Sea of Stars might just be the game for you.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be brief: I spent 40 hours playing this game to nearly 100% completion on stream. If I didn't have an audience and a co-pilot to goof with, that time would have been mostly boring slog.

Sea of Stars paints a beautiful world with delicious sound design, and it absolutely nails the nostalgia-bait aesthetic it's going for, but aesthetic is pretty much all it has. The combat system is paper thin and falls apart pretty early; in the second half of the game, most of the bosses never got an action, even with all of the hardest difficulty options available enabled, and there's basically no mechanical meat to interact with. The dialogue is vapid (behold a storm of "..." and "!" and "!?") and most of the characters never develop a personality (Garl's fine, though- he's the real protagonist, anyway). The overall narrative is pretty uninspired and absolutely loaded with references to The Messenger that feel really, really intrusive.

If you've got Game Pass or can nab it on a deep discount and know what you're getting yourself into, you might have a good enough time, but you really need to temper your expectations. If you're looking to play something like Chrono Trigger or Super Mario RPG, you'll have more fun re-playing those games instead.\t\t\t\t\t
