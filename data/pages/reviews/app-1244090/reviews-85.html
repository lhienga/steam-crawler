\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very enjoyable game, coming from others that are similar such as Chrono trigger, Final fantasy, Crosscode, or Octopath, I had a great time playing. The gameplay specifically is smooth and responsive, making actions feel crisp and precise. Moving around the world feels good, which makes traveling between areas less burdensome overall. There was plenty for me to explore, collect, read, and engage with, so much so that I always had something to do besides the main story.

Battles flowed in and out so seamlessly that I never really felt the need to avoid combat, and giving that it was so easy to engage and disengage, leveling never felt as restricting or grindy like others in the same category. There isn't much to complain about realistically besides some story and character motivations that seem strange, or out of place... but despite my misgivings, I loved the game. It has character and charm, which unfortunately is absent in many games in recent times.

Overall a I would say an 8.5/10 but my enjoyment placed me at a 9/10. Good and fun game. Might do NG+ for some added challenge in the future.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have now finished the game and analysed it step by step. As a game designer and as a lover of the genre, but above all as someone who has been waiting for its release since the first moments I saw the trailer; "finally a new jrpg! the best graphics I've ever seen."

And, indeed, so it is. SoS has the best pixelart graphics I've ever seen in the traditional jrpg genre. The music is outstanding, the realisation of the worldbuilding and how the settings intertwine with the soundtrack is a masterpiece. The art direction of this game is -without question- superlative.

HOWEVER: the game does not shine AT ALL in the plot nor on gameplay. While initially both these elements are nice and indeed seem to have interesting ideas, the longer the game goes on the more inadequate and ineffective they become, especially if we take into consideration the length of the title itself. The depth of the game system is... shallow. This can be considered a 'feature' rather than a flaw, but the game is so simple that I only happened to lose once -due to an error outside the game- and not against a bossfight. The bosses are -to put it bluntly- puppets. Especially when you unlock the characters later in the story, fighting becomes a piece of cake. Some skills (basic, not 'secret' or whatever) break the game into a thousand pieces. Anyone with half a brain can take this game and r@pe every enemy on screen during his first run, Final boss included (I have the blindrun recorded on my channel to prove it).

!!!WARNING: MINOR SPOILER!!!

If the game system is "rescuable" by attributing the simplicity to the desire to create an 'entry level' jrpg game, the drafting of the plot has no way of being safeguarded.
Leaving aside:
The lack of personality (or even some differences) of the two main characters, who have NOTHING to tell. At all.
The presence of a couple of secondary characters who I still struggle to justify their appearance within the story (Reshan presence is poorly written and generally he is a "deus ex machina" with legs who solves anything, but only if he feels like it)
Characters that appear late in the game and that you don't even have time to delve into (so why show them?)
The plot in general is a fairy tale that needs TONS of suspension of disbelief to be appreciated.
Even so, it draws its own resounding coughs at times.

The only thing that can be said about the story is that it very -very often- genuinely makes you smile, and at some points even manages to move you (i.e. when it does NOT talk about the -unfortunately- real protagonists of the game, i.e. Reshan and Aephroul)

If I can give Sabotage a piece of advice, I'd tell him to be more careful about narrative direction and writing, because it doesn't keep up with the graphics and music.

All in all, my 2 cent opinion is that it's worth playing if you're willing to overlook these flaws.\t\t\t\t\t

The levels look gorgeous too. Going to a swamp? Better believe that there will be pink, purple, and orange accents like mushrooms or flowers. Going into a cave? Better believe there will be blue and green crystals or whatever that glow.

This game is a lovely homage to Chrono Trigger with some of the best parts of Mario RPG thrown in, then mixed with decades of good game design. Even traversing the world is enjoyable.

Should you stop what you're doing and play this game immediately? Maybe not. But if this game looks even remotely interesting to you, you should definitely play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs it worth playing? Short answer is: Yes
If you liked classic RPGs, especially Chrono Trigger, this game with tickle that bone.

However, it merely tickles it.
Playing this game for more than a couple hours reveals a lot of its shortcomings.

In terms of presentation, Art, Music and so on, there's no question that this game does extremely well.
But it falls short in terms of gameplay and story.

Starting with Gameplay: It has a very good base, but it's very very easy. Maybe it's my old veteran bones talking, but I only game over'd once my entire playthrough, which included every secret boss. There are just waaay too many checkpoints and rest areas. I barely needed to use items up until halfway through since you basically start with two healers, which is a shame since the cooking mechanic is very thought out. Just not correctly balanced. Items are basically almost always full heals for the entire party. I would have loved to see items that boost stats, making me choose which item slots to allocate to them and what I should cook.
Which leads to the core of the issue of why the game is so easy. There's no player agency. The variance between skills is small, there's a heal around every corner, and most upgrades are linear. So that means that as a player you will never make hard choices. You will never regret those choices. In combat, and out of combat. fewer player choices lead to less player expression and leads to a less interesting game. Once you have combat figured out, it's just going through the motions.
Fortunately, the game's combat system is *just* good enough to carry you forward. It's still fun enough. but it could have been so much better.

My main gripe is the story. The main characters are about as interesting as a pair of sandals, and contribute about as much to the story. In the same way that you wouldn't want to go out barefoot, you wouldn't want to have a JRPG story without a "main character stronk chosen one". And the two main characters fulfill the bare minimum to that role. They are barely built up, and the writers knew that. Which is why I think they doubled down on Garl so much. The main characters are *boring* except for their relation to Garl. They almost never have anything interesting to say during any cutscenes. Garl is basically the main character. And you can easily see that in most other reviews here. In fact, every single side character is more interesting than the main duo.

This just makes me even more impressed with how much of a better protagonist Chrono is, despite being literally mute. He does much more and contributes more to the story. But I suppose I shouldn't directly compare the two.
Although it's hard not to, because for the duration of this game's playthrough I honestly just kept wishing Chrono Trigger would have gotten better treatment over the years.

Still, this is a fine tribute, and I give the developers all the credit and more. I'm sure this wasn't easy to pull off.\t\t\t\t\t
