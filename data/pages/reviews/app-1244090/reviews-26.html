\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to 100% the game first before giving a review, but last achievement is complete bullsh*t, so screw that

So, what can I say about Sea of Stars? It ain't Chrono Trigger, despite it desperately trying to be, not even close. But it is still a decent and enjoyable game.

Presentation of the game is great: graphics are just gorgeous, animated cutscenes are just breathtaking, music is great, locations are vibrant and memorable, level and puzzle design is fun. Minigames and sidequests are neat, I actually enjoyed Wheels, and fishing.
Battle system.... is okay. It starts really promising and fun, but then it just doesn't deliver on the promise. It stops evolving way too quickly, about 50% into the game you already seen almost everything game can show you in that regard. Each character has only three skills, and also double techs combos with each other, and one Ultimate each - and honestly, that's not enough for variety and synergy. Not the worst, but not really something to write home about
And for writing... Uuh... Yet again, starts promising, but the more you play, the more issues you notice. Bar few exceptions, characters are pretty bland, story relies on prophesies, visions and emotional power ups a bit too much, some plot threads are outright abandoned, ending is not really satisfying, and I think there might be even some plot holes, but I might be wrong on that one.

But on the other hand, while lows are eeeeh, highs are freakin' high. This game both made me cry and also say "Holy sh*t" multiple times. And that enough is worth it. It ain't Chrono Trigger, but so are most of the games ever. Despite flaws, it is still fun and emotional game, that has some tricks up its sleeve

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game, great game engine. Really feels like a modern SNES classic JRPG.

* Beautiful art/music
* Fun combat system

It's weakness is writing, sadly extremely important for an RPG:

* You're never made to really "feel" for any of the characters.
* Too many deux ex machinas (looking at you, Alchemist).
* Any dire situation is usually resolved shortly after; nothing is there to ramp up the intensity or your anxiety as a player.
* Nothing feels earned, just outright given to you. Attack combos, story-progressing information, etc.

Biggest complaint (story-wise): Our heroes never really lose / there are no consequences for failure.

The best old JRPG stories usually have one of:
- the heroes are utterly overwhelmed
- their actions have unintended consequences
- "they just don't make it in time".

In Sea of Stars, you're always on a winning streak. When you do "lose", there's no real consequences. The world barely even takes notice.

The old JRPGs I loved as a child felt more serious than this one. They treated me more as an adult. People die, true evil is done, etc.

This is the saturday-morning-cartoons edition of old SNES JRPGs.

That being said, I would absolutely play their next RPG release. I'm just hoping for a more compelling story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, I’ll offer the tldr; Sea of Stars is a captivating experience. It has a stunning hand crafted feel to the world, fun gameplay, and an engaging narrative.

Visually, the game is a triumph. It’s a beautifully vibrant pixel world filled with charm. It particularly pops on the Steam Deck OLED, which I played the entirety of the game on. While I appreciate the bigger story moments and character introductions having a cartoon-style cutscene, I wasn’t necessarily a fan of this art style. It felt at slight odds with the rest of the game. Outside of those, the game offers a great sense of scale, with beautiful landscapes, over world and vistas. This is no easy feat to accomplish with their chosen art style.

The gameplay is delightful, albeit standard, RPG fare, with a blend of exploration, platforming and turn based-combat that can depend on timing button presses correctly to attack and defend. Navigating through the landscapes feels intuitive, and the constant introduction of new traversal mechanics keeps things fresh.

In terms of the story, Sea of Stars doesn't disappoint. The narrative is genuinely heartwarming, weaving together a globe trotting, time-traversing tale of cosmic adventure, friendship and discovery. I felt the characters are well-developed. With the exception of the two main characters and the main villain, I felt each contributed to the overarching plot in meaningful ways. One character in particular is head and shoulders above the rest (you’ll know when you play) - with a really amazing arc that offers up more than one tear-jerk moment.

Sea of Stars managed to deliver an enchanting experience that kept me hooked from start to finish. It’s a great little homage to the RPGs of old. Whether you're a fan of RPGs or just looking for a simple yet immersive little adventure, Sea of Stars is a great choice.\t\t\t\t\t
