\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall Enjoyment: 9.5/10

LIKED:
- The overall experience. It looks great, it sounds great, it feels great, just an all around treat for the senses.
- The modernisation of classic rpg mechanics. It's got all the best parts streamlined without sacrificing anything. It feels like the game respects you, if that makes sense. A game made by gamers, for gamers.
- WHEELS. I love a good game within a game, and Wheels is deffs one of my favs now. Independent wheels adventure when?
- I like how they handled movement/exploration. Like they know just walking around is boring, so they mix it up as much as possible with the terrain, and in turn are able to hide things within the environment in unique ways. That is until you have to go back somewhere, see dislikes.
- Combat. I love the combat, not only because I'm a sucker for timed button pressing, but because it consistently presents you with micro puzzles and just never gets old. I do feel like by the end I sorta had it "solved" which is disappointing, but then again there are relics to fix that if I felt so inclined.
- Accessibility. This game does everything within its power to let you play it the way that works best for you. Hard? Easy? Normal? That's old news, you can make this game as easy or as difficult as you want on a sliding scale, not some rigid preset mode.
- Story/Characters. It wasn't anything revolutionary, but it was charming, silly, and fun. And they handled the humour much better this time imo.
- The Flimsy Hammer secret. Not the room, but the story from the cloaked figure. I cried. He warned me he was about to get way too real, but nothing could have prepared me. The love poured into this game is unreal, and it shows.

DISLIKED:
- Backtracking. The movement in this game isn't interesting enough to warrant going back through an area. It's so tedius with the limited entry points for most areas, and no way to escape combat. You're forced to fight enemies that aren't a threat and provide nothing of value when defeated. Granted, this is half resolved AT THE VERY END OF THE GAME. Had the improved travel abilities been introduced earlier I wouldn't be half as mad. It's so weird to me too, since most of the game respects your time in a way RPG's often don't, just this one thing seems to have slipped through.
- As mentioned above, the complete lack of tools for avoiding combat. Enemies should avoid you or something once you're capable of one-shotting them. So annoying.
- Sticking the true ending behind something as tedious as the conchs. That should have been for a Flimsy Hammer or some such.
- Honestly, I wasn't overly satisfied with either ending. I'm pretty sure they're leaving things open for future games, but for me it made the ending quite hollow.\t\t\t\t\t
