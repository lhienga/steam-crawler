\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's rare for me to sit down and just be MOTIVATED to play a game from start to finish anymore. I don't think I've been sucked into anything like Sea of Stars since maybe Subnautica. And I don't even really like JRPG's that much. But this game was just a rollercoaster from start to finish and skipping that first boring ending and doing the ~1-2hrs of extra gameplay required to unlock the true ending (mandatory, holy crap is it mandatory, and you don't need to worry about missing anything until you've beaten it the first time, because you can't even unlock it until you've beaten it the first time) should've just been the way the game was supposed to finish anyways, because it finishes STRONG.

I finished the game about a month ago and still just keep finding myself thinking about it. That's how I knew I needed to write a review.

This game comes out SWINGING with stunning art and an even better soundtrack. No kidding, this is up there with the very best soundtracks of all time. Undertale, subnautica, final fantasy (pick one), chrono trigger - this game's soundtrack SLAPS. I'm oddly picky about game music, and this one leapt out and grabbed me. One of only a select few games whose soundtracks make it into my playlist. And there's even an alternate list of almost the entire soundtrack, but played by pirates. Two versions of Garl's theme? Sign me the f*** UP.

The combat is serviceable and works well - I do enjoy the combo system to interrupt, but sometimes the skills required can be a hair too fiddly to reliably nail.

The plot is fun and engaging. I did not see the big twist(s) about halfway through. It ends very satisfyingly, though it does shoot itself in the foot because you have to do the last section twice in order to get the true ending - and doing so just kinda cheapens the true ending, which should have just stood on it's own as the main ending. It isn't even like the work required to get the true ending wasn't just as fun or engaging as the rest of the plot. You have to get the true ending. It's mandatory (and only like an hour or two of more great gameplay)

The two protagonists are a bit boring and generic. But they are carried HARD by the supporting cast. One of the best secondary casts I've seen in an RPG. Hell, maybe the best. They're flawed, they're vulnerable, they grow, they define themselves as important parts of the game not only as characters but in their utility in gameplay.

And Garl is simply the true protagonist of the game. Every character brings a crucial part of the plot, but without spoiling anything, Garl is one of the best written characters in a game and they give him the room to really do something with it. Giving a 2D sprite an infectious (but not annoying) positivity, an iron will, and a warmth/friendliness that I'm nowhere near talented enough to explain was impressive as hell. And his theme song may be my #1 song in a video game. Garl is the GOAT. Every time he does something, it's awesome.

If the game had to get by on the strength of its main two characters alone and the first ending was all it had, it'd be a good, if unremarkable 7/10. But Garl, the true ending, and the rest of the supporting cast drag it up to a 9/10. Play this game. I'm a picky gamer, and this gets an overwhelming thumbs-up from me. I'm gonna go listen to the soundtrack now.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVisuals? Top notch. The lighting in particular is where it's at. Never seen the 16 bit style graphics look so wonderful before.

Music? Oh mannn. Sooooooo good. About 10 tracks stand out to me and I will ALWAYS love listening to them.

Game play? Very good. And I say this as someone who hates turn based rpg game play but they did this really well. 4 things to master. Button timing, the lock system, the magic system, the combo system. Learn all 4 and it becomes clear that this is a very balanced way to approach combat. Mindlessly spamming your best attacks will get you killed in this game. Sorry guys but thinking is very important with this one lol. The other part of the gameplay is puzzle solving and platforming which is my fav part of the game play. There's also some side stuff like a silly board game and fishing which is a nice touch to spend a few hours on : )

Story? It's really good. My fav part of the game honestly. It starts off pretty by the numbers... But make no mistake. They are setting you up for a emotional rollercoaster once the fleshmancer villain character finally makes a appearance. And yes, he's a horrible monster person. A very sick character. A true villain. And a certain character who you would not think would be so important, turns out to be the most lovable character there is. It's not a story about super heros rising up to stop a villain. That's too simple. It's about the super heros friends. How they can matter just as much if not more than the main heros of this story. And that's just a wonderful message for anyone. Have you ever felt like you were not good enough? That you wish you could do more but can't for whatever reason? This game says that YOU CAN be good enough : )

I got next to nothing to say about this game that's negative. If you want the true ending you gotta find all these 60 hidden items throughout the game world and they can be a pain in the azzz to find. That was kind of dumb fetch questy stuff and the creators clearly wanted you to go back and see all the wonderful world that they created so that's fair. Still a chore though so I thought that part of the game was a lil lame. Luckily I play on pc and used we mod to cheat that part of the game haha. So I guess this part of the game isn't a issue for me haha.

No game is perfect so you wont see me drop a 10 out of 10. But it's a high af 9 that's for sure!

One thing I'm effing tired of seeing though is people hating the two mains being bland. Bro... That's on purpose! The mains being fairly blank slates is so you can easily imprint yourself onto them. That way whenever emotional story parts happen, it feels like it's happening to you. YOU! The human playing the game! You're the one losing friends in battle. You're the one becoming depressed and sad that you got screwed over! The story telling hits harder this way. Halo, half life, gta3 etc etc. This is by design so you get sucked into the character and BECOME the character. These fools will praise chrono trigger but hate this game which makes NO SENSE. I think some a holes just like to be elitist and act like any games from the past are the best, and new games all suck. Which is mostly true but not in this case haha. Just wish those idiots would get the obvious point here...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is an emotional experience of a game. colorful characters, concise and breathtaking storytelling that is full of heart and adoration it will take you to the edge of all your emotions. you will cry, you will laugh. Sea of Stars is truly a timeless classic. The developers have shown that time, effort and above all else love and dedication makes this game truly a masterpiece.

Kowloon and Sabotage have truly outdone themselves and me typing this review does NOT give this game justice. for it is in the players eyes and in the players emotions this game can truly reveal its beauty in everything it has to offer. The OST and the game are well worth the money for you will have tunes playing in your head for days, and the memories you will make on this grand adventure will leave you hungry for more. I myself am going for a full 100% playthrough. for it truly deserves the attention and the time and the effort.

The game has a slower pace, however, that does NOT negate the fact that throught out ALL that time, you are battling, crafting, adventuring, most importantly you are making memories. for this game hits you in the mind, heart, and soul.

the battle mechanics are very satisfying, it may take some getting used to, but fans of older, more retro style games like Paper Mario or Chrono Trigger will find themselves in a home away from home. for the style of the world mimics it to a T, while also having a distinct flavor of its own that resonates beautifully.

I cannot recommend this game enough. please support this studio, please support indie developers.

Final Rating: 10/10 would do it all over again.\t\t\t\t\t
