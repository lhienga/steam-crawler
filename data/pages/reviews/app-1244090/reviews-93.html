\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is a solidly designed game. The battle system is simple but fun. The story and worldbuilding is not elaborate or complex, but effective. Visually it's amazing, and the music has a few bangers.

But the problem is none of this evolves as you progress through the game. The story stays kind of one note, the music variety isn't there, and the gameplay doesn't evolve into something that can justify a game longer than 15 hours.

I don't want to say the game is bad, because it clearly isn't. In fact, in many ways it's awesome! It just needs trimming or fleshing out. Right now it inhabits an unfortunate space of being a shorter game that wants to be a longer game, and all you get from playing it is the feeling your time isn't being valued.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is a lot to love about this game. It's certainly not perfect, but I'd describe it as a worthwhile experience for those who enjoy RPGs but don't enjoy common trappings of the genre such as being 50+ hours long and required or highly encouraged xp grinding. Sea of Stars is a game that, for me, never overstayed its welcome, moving the plot and mechanics along at a solid pace for the entire duration of the experience. The plot itself is middling to good, with the characters carrying an otherwise pretty standard "chosen one(s)" plot with strong settings and reasonably satisfying worldbuilding. That is more than I would expect out of a 30 hour experience, and it definitely gains points for me as a result. It did not fall to bloat as RPGs often can, and that 30 hour experience includes playing a reasonable chunk of the side content. The side content is just as satisfying as the main game, and provides a slight additional challenge to an otherwise easy experience. My main criticism of the game is along this line: I did have a relic increasing my xp gain by 20%, but I was also taking 40% additional damage and didn't really find more ways to make the game harder (which is supposed to be part of the point of the relic system) and as a result encountered, truly, one point of actual difficulty in the game. The environment design is largely good, but there were multiple points where it was difficult to tell where I was supposed to go, which is a little frustrating. The music, as well, was slightly lacking in variety, though it largely makes up for that with quality. Each environment does have its own theme, but the battle theme is repeated throughout almost the entire game. It is an excellent battle theme, so it didn't end up growing old for me, but a little more variety than slight differences in the same theme would have been nice. The actual lock battle system is great, but I would have preferred more depth. It is a smaller experience, so this is pretty forgivable though. All in all, this game has such a solid foundation that it's kind of a shame that it wasn't expanded slightly more, though it makes sense to avoid that as it can so easily lead to bloat. Would love to see a sequel that builds on the original.

TL;DR Buy it, play it, love it. Wish there was more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeverely underwhelmed by the ending. This game does some fun and interesting things, but the story over 50 hours of gameplay doesn't come close to what you get in 30 hours of the classic JRPGs they were inspired by. If you're wanting an RPG with a satisfying conclusion, avoid this, especially since imo The "true ending" isn't even worth going for. Aside from the major character surprise, it ends up being just a simple end boss substitution without any real outcome difference. The big bad guy still escapes to who knows where, because we never actually get to see or go there. The mystery protagonist overseer escapes with him and all the high level talk of saving multiple timelines/worlds is basically thrown to the wind. An entire game worth of build up just to peter out at the end, makes me feel as if they were winging the plot as they developed and suddenly ran out of time before release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is a great "turn-based" RPG that pays tribute to classics from the same genre (especially Chrono Trigger) while also trying to be "its own thing". The game has a decent story but most importantly GREAT soundtrack to go with its fun combat and all area explorations. However, having said that, there are A LOT of aspects of this game that could've been "done better" and while most of these didn't really affect my "overall enjoyment" of the game, I still feel like I need to list them and give my own "solutions" to them in hopes that the developers might read these points and hopefully implement them in a future DLC or sequel if they decide to do one. These are as follows:

1) Combat: No Clear Indicator of WHEN to Press the Button for Extra Benefits During Skill Animations

This is, by far, the BIGGEST issue I have with the game. You see, the game tells you that timing your attacks is "optional/not mandatory", however, as you progress through the game and fight harder foes/bosses, you will eventually realize that timing skills will become CRUCIAL/MANDATORY in order to deal/receive maximum benefits and especially if you want to finish this game UNDEFEATED (i.e. without a Game Over) as I successfully did during my playthrough of the game.

And so, the problem is that the game does NOT attempt to help in (or at least hint at) identifying when to press the button to get these extra skill benefits during combat and so I eventually had to resort to websites/videos (which ALWAYS disappointing when a game forces me to look for external help outside the game) to figure out the timing for MANY skills for different characters.

Eventually, once you learn the timing for all moves, you just have to memorize them and keep timing them properly until you are able to master their timing or at least become good enough to nail them often times than not. It is not the worst problem ever, but I really think the developers could've added a SMALL improvement that would've easily solved this issue without much hassle (see below).

Solution to Problem #1:

Add a subtle glow/spark on the character during the skill animation at the moment I'm suppose to press the button to make easier to time these presses, rather than "shooting in the dark" or resorting to online guides to figure them out.

2) Combat: Not Being Able to See the Boss Health Bar:

This is probably the 2nd biggest problem I have with this game (especially with how many AWESOME boss fights this game has throughout its storyline). There is absolutely NO REASON whatsoever to not reveal the boss health bar using existing character equipment. As a result of this baffling decision by the developers, I had to play VERY DEFENSIVELY (i.e. by conserving live mana, MP and usable items) throughout the ENTIRE GAME because there was no way to tell whether I'm dealing good damage and nearing the defeat of the encountered boss (which would've encouraged me to be more aggressive and use all available resources to hasten my victory) or that I'm dealing minimal damage to the boss (and so that would definitely signal to me that I need to be more careful when using the current resources I have).

If the developers thought that showing the boss health would make the game easier, then I regret to inform you that it would have NOT affected the "difficulty" of the boss in any way (not even making it "harder" by not showing the boss's HP) as the only thing that has done was CONFINING ME INTO USING THE SAME STRATEGY FOR THE ENTIRE GAME (i.e. being conservative/defensive) because I couldn't see/analyze the damage inflicted on the boss's health to better adjust/alter my strategy as I see fit during the boss battle.

Solution to Problem #2:

Simply patch the game so that items likes "Eye of Yomara" can FULLY reveal all enemies' HPs/weaknesses, INCLUDING bosses' HPs/weaknesses.

3) No Side Maps for Towns/Dungeons:

While the developers did put a lot of care and effort into crafting this beautiful world, it was really surprising to see them neglect adding a side map specifically for towns and dungeons. As a result, traversing both of these can be VERY annoying, especially when wanting to explore every room in towns or finding hidden paths within towns/dungeons (I missed a lot of houses/rooms because of this and even had to resort to online guides AGAIN to get missed items in the post game).

Solution to Problem #3:

Obviously consider adding side maps to towns/dungeons whether in a future patch (which is too late for someone like me sadly) or at least consider it for future DLCs/sequels if we ever get there.

4) No Viewable Dialogue/Conversation Logs for Past Conversations:

As someone who struggles to maintain a "long attention span", oftentimes I end-up missing on key parts of the conversations (especially in a game that has no active voice-acting) and that is why I wish this game had a dialogue log that can be accessed at any time to view past conversations again so that I can either re-read what I didn't notice the first time or even remind myself of key story elements or "to-do quests" when needed.

Solution to Problem #4:

Add text logs that can be accessed by pressing a specific button at any time to view old conversations just like how it is implemented in other modern games like The Legend of Heroes or Fire Emblem Engage.


5) No Alternative Button Layout for Other Types of Controllers:

I personally used a PlayStation 4 controller (aka Dualshock 4) during my playthrough of this game and while this game ALREADY has been released on the recent PlayStation consoles, there is no option in the settings menu to change the button layout to the PlayStation button layout.

Solution to Problem #5:

Simply patch the game to include other controller layouts (PlayStation and Nintendo layouts) since the work for those has already been done on those consoles and so all that is needed is to include them in the PC version as an extra option in the settings menu.

6) Leveling-up Does NOT Heal Your Party Members:

Most others game usually HEAL you completely when you level-up, but for some reason in this game characters don't heal upon leveling-up (which really ruins the "I just got stronger" moment that you usually feel after leveling-up) and there isn't really any valid reasoning on why this isn't a thing from the very beginning. As a result, I usually either do 1-2 more battles to heal using character healing skills or go ALL THE WAY back to nearest camping spot to heal my party (yes because I'm that CHEAP on using items, EVEN IF I can make multiples of them whenever I want).

Solution to Problem #6:

Simply patch the game to heal your entire party completely when leveling-up.

7) No Run/Escape from Battle Option:

This can be annoying at times, especially later on when returning to earlier locations to find all missed items.

Solution to Problem #7:

Patch the game to include an option to "Run from Battle" for non-boss encounters.

8) Usable Items Given to You Other Than Through Chests DISAPPEAR If You Have Full Inventory

Multiple times I had a "full inventory" for usable items and then whenever an NPC gives me an extra item (like food), the item PERMANENTLY DISAPPEARS and is NEVER GIVEN TO YOU despite the game saying otherwise.

Solution to Problem #8:

Patch the game by adding a "Drop & Swap" option when given an item if you already have a full inventory.

(I have other things to say, but I'm reaching the word limit unfortunately)

Summary:

Despite all points mentioned above, Sea of Stars is a great game and definitely worth recommending for all turn-based/RPG lovers. If you're not sure yet, try the free demo and make your decision afterwards.

Final Rating: 9/10. \t\t\t\t\t
