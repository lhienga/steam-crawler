\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful graphics and sound design. OK Story and Bad Gameplay. (JRPGs and RPGs are my favorite kind of game btw)

Combat is very boring. It does not develop or change at all and leveling is just increase stats. New abilities dont change game-play. Overall just the worst part of this game by far.

The story seems OK when its happening the issue is the story happens for like 30 minutes then you're set for like 6 hours of what feels like side content. The story also doesn't make sense. Certain characters behave in ways that just don't achieve the goals they say they have.

The game feels like it was made by artists and feels like it wasn't actually played from start to finish by a dev because its a slog and its slow. I would love to see the rest of the story or explore the world without having to play abysmal RPG battles with 0 depth.

Character development doesnt exist btw. Go play Chained Echoes it has its faults but I actually had fun with that game even with its faults. This game I did not enjoy and really tried to give it a good shot.\t\t\t\t\t

Shooting For The Stars

That's not even an exaggeration. Sea of Stars is an indie title very much inspired by Chrono Trigger, a legend of a game and consider by many to be the quintessential JRPG. That is a high bar of quality to reach for. Honestly, I don't think Sea of Stars is trying to compete with the champ, instead it comes from a place of love and passion.

The two main characters of Valere and Zale are your run of the mill blank protagonist types, the sort you can project yourself onto. This works for some people, but not for others, however, the rest of the cast are quirky and fun, as they carry you throughout the story with its many twists and turns.

Like it's two protagonists the story will work for some, but probably be not as deep or involving for others. Wherever you're on one of the extremes or fall somewhere in the middle, I'd say Sea of Stars is worth a shot. You might just find a quaint adventure that proves worthy your time.

Pros
  • Great pixel art style that will dig deep into those nostalgic vibes
  • Variety of activities like fishing, cooking, even a tabletop battler to keep you occupied between battles
  • Encounter a fun cast of characters along your adventure

Cons
  • Character progression systems may feel a bit lacking if you really want to customise your characters
  • Main characters and story might come of as a bit cliched and boring to some
