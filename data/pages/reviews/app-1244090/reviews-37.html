\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally fun call back to what made the old top down turn based RPGs fun, with (almost) everything that was annoying about them refined out. No grinding for XP or rare drops. No running in circles trying to generate encounters. Travel is pretty quick and light with *minimal* backtracking across old territory.

Combat is fun, interesting, and occasionally challenging. Overall it's hard enough to be fun, but not to call it HARD. I didn't crank it up with any available relics though. The story was lighthearted, a little dark and sad at times, but as close to Rated G as you can get with combat (and pixelated gore) I think.

I don't feel compelled to try NG+ or play through the story again. This was a once and done for me, but well worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun homage to 90s turn based JRPGs heavily inspired by games like Chrono Trigger & Super Mario RPG with some great pixel art and fun environments to explore. The only real gripes I have are with the combat system and the two protagonists.

The combat system is simply... okay. The characters don't have much in action variety with some characters having far better options than the rest of the group and timing some of the defensive hits is rather spotty due to unclear visuals. Ultimately however, the game isn't difficult and there are options to tune it as you see fit if you struggle with some aspects.

Zale and Valerie are sadly fairly bland carbon copies of each other. The dialogue between the two could be completely interchanged and it would make no difference at all to their interactions, plot or relationships to the other characters. Instead of leaning into the admittedly tropey yin-and-yang personalities they could have had by being polar opposites (as represented by being born during the summer and winter solstices respectively), we're left with 2 near blank slates.\t\t\t\t\t
