\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Stars is an ode to the golden age of SNES JRPGs, brought in line to modern standards. The game features about a 25-hour main quest, beautiful scenery and area design filled with simple puzzles, a relatively simple combat system where enemy design provides depth, and a fantastic reward for those who achieve the relatively straightforward goal of clearing the game with all side content completed.

The visuals are absolutely gorgeous, the sound design and OST are fantastic, and the gameplay is fun, engaging, and flexible. The story is a bit simple, and the characters don't have the most depth, but the setting of Sea of Stars is whimsical and enthralling. Nonetheless, the gameplay might feel a bit too simplistic at times, and some segments of the main quest can drag out for a bit too long, especially if you've never played The Messenger. Regardless, I would absolutely recommend Sea of Stars if you're looking for a mid-length JRPG to get immersed in, especially if you have nostalgia for the SNES JRPGs.

Note: You don't need to have played The Messenger to enjoy Sea of Stars. However, your experience is enhanced so much more if you've played it.\t\t\t\t\t

also, forgot to mention, the game is piss easy. if you do not stack on every difficulty modifier the game allows you to, i do not imagine anyone would die in this game. and if you do die? you get all of your health and mp refilled. this turns every first attempt at a fight into a "test run" as, unless you saw the fight coming, it will be your only attempt where you do not have full health/mp to start. in a game with a hundred modifiers that make the game easier and like 5 that make the game harder, i really really wish the base difficulty of the game was higher.

overall, i'm still really enjoying this game. but these two things just make the game feel cheaper than it needs to be.\t\t\t\t\t
