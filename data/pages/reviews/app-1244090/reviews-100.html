\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo.. I write this just after the credit roll.

The save file shows just under 30 hours while Steam recorded 33 hours.

I will tell what this game is about(spoiler), skip down for my opinion/view for this game
You play as two children of unknown origin who go on duty, fulfilling your destiny. There are friends made along the way. First start clueless, then see the worlds far and wide. Worlds: Land, sea, sky, abyss, marine floor, another land, another sea, another sky, another marine floor, space-dimension, and others. Compelling, memorable stories made, cherished, archived.
Story of friendship, adventure, loss, betrayal, ultimate vengeance, goal. I got to keep this with minor spoiler ;)

Highlighted pros
:- Old JRPG style with mix
- Turn based combat
- Long, Engaging story
- Possibility to make progress even with short play session
- No tedious grind, battle, side quests, exploration(backtracking*)
- No random encounter (skippable by walking around, able to have preemptive strike, advantage in battle)
- Good bgms
- Visions of gamer's instinct
- DANG-COOL artstyle
- Engaging puzzles, a bunch of it
- Have many secret places to explore and things that are quite easy to collect
- Seamless transition for battle and exploration

As most of these are better to be left for surprises, I will not explain most of them.
DANG-COOL artstyle : for the characters, places, travels, and more. It is mixed too.
Visions of gamer's instinct : you know what these are. They know too. And they really use these well.
Those mentioned, combined with the story and storytelling.... top tier.

Combat, is a unique one. Turn-based, but not just "attack, buff, debuff, take damage". It incorporate "perfect timing" for attacks(normal attack, skill, combo, ultimate) and blocks(block in enemies turn, so it is not a skill the character have to spend 1 turn to cast), damage types, target range, and elements(with imbue to normal attacks and some combos(cross-attacks done by 2 character but only spend 1 of the character's turn)), locks(break these to interrupt enemies' skill cast), dynamic battle(the enemies take turn based on count down, so they may attack in between your character's turn(have up to 3 units on field at once, can be swapped anytime even during the character's turn in combat)). It lacks the debuff part. The enemies have their own weaknesses and resistances. The attack animations are at "standard" to "flashy". The BEST thing for the animation is: it is not long. Exception, for ultimates and two skills. Cool skills for your characters and enemies.

Exploration, you can climb small hightened land, walls, grapple to grappling points and climbable walls, swim(this part amaze me at the demo, which lead me to eye this game's launch), go fishing, harvest crops for cooking consumables(not farming, harvest), drive pirate ship and do drift, zoom through skies, talk to mole rats, kappas, a woman who ignore you and just want you to go away(for real, in the game), old man with bald head, and more. Beautiful environment with changeable lighting. Yes, you can change day/night time, at will, in this game. This used for puzzles too. Along with lasers, reflections, timed switch for platforms.

*All very straight forward yet unpredictable and keeps its show for ~30 hours of playtime. I did not side track much, possibly 2-3 hours and I still do not complete everything. Side tracking before the end is rather useless. Just remember some places may hold secrets, can be visited later.

Highlighted cons
:- Unskippable cutscenes*
- 1 instance of bug where I cannot progress because there was no collision. Use boy main and it is fine.
- No fast travel in regions that have value for backtracking. Between region, there are giants for fast travel. In region, have to walk the entire thing to go where we want. Want to back track somewhere in the middle of the region? Ready to walk.
- No region map. Though per region is not very big, but it is big enough. I think it is worthy of some map.
- (what else? '-')

*The cutscenes without dialogue lasts 10-15 secs max. The ones with dialogue may lasts for, maybe 3-5 minutes max but these tell the story and the thought process. Keep in mind that the whole game's story is not told solely with text in the dialogues. Some things are just there, and it tells.

End

Overall, it is 9/10 from me. I enjoy it. Not much demanding the players attention, even in battle. We have ways to recover the characters from critical health state and turn the tide. It is possible to just sit and relax while playing this game. Very good experience. Story, storytelling, and visual are the things I give most thumbs up to. This game does not demand long play session on any story progress, even with 30 mins each session, story progression is noticable. And it last for about 30 hours without backtracking or "move slowly", just forward, moderate pace for story.

*I just realize most things I mentioned are in the game's description in the store page. -__- my bad, hope you who read this do not think I waste your time.

----ON SECOND THOUGHT----
After more hours into the game and getting all there is... Wow. I would put this game at 9.3/10. Things considered, minor inconvenience but a lot of enjoyment I get from this game. Definitely one of the S-Tier story games. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFew games have given me as much fun as Sea of Stars has. While the moment to moment dialogue suffers at parts, the overall story is genuinely really interesting to dive into. There is one part of the game where the motivations for some characters wont make any sense, but trust me when I say the game clears it up later. Still, the combat system was genuinely exciting to play with, the balancing of the game is near perfect, and it's one of the few games where 100% completion wasn't a chore, but rather something I was looking forward to. Whether you want world building, surprisingly dynamic strategic combat, or just some generally fantastic visuals, then you'll have a ball playing this game. Fair warning though, the start is fairly slow, and unless you're willing to play about 3 hours of boring gameplay before the really great stuff starts to show up, I'd recommend not getting the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: this game's writing is trash.

So I was going along having an okay time playing the game. I was loving the presentation, but I wasn't in love with the combat. It's kinda slow and the mechanic of "Press 'A' to do more damage" is not implemented the best. So I thought, "Let's see how the story goes." I had made it to the end of the third dungeon and could tell the story that had started slow was about to pick up.

Spoilers Ahead

So you defeat the 'Dweller of Woe' (this game is full of super generic names btw). The Dweller's core pops out on the floor. Your two younger mentors then betray the ♥♥♥♥ out of you and your older mentor by erecting a barrier between you and them. The core is on their side of the barrier. Some baddies that had been in the background of the story show up and start talking about resurrecting another different Dweller. At this point, I'm sitting there like "Oooooh, there's gotta be some juicy stuff here. Maybe the old guy did some dubious things in the past." Unfortunately, no. The younger mentors just wanted to live a life of their own. Keep in mind the "Dweller of Woe" was the last Dweller being left in the world, so after destroying the core, they could have just ♥♥♥♥♥♥ off to the beach town you just went through. When asked why they wouldn't just destroy the core and, I don't know, bring about world ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ peace, they said, "You don't know that [it would change anything]." ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. You've killed a few of these things at this point, we just saw this Dweller mind control someone and presumably eat them, and no one is able to leave this island as long as this thing is alive. HOW IS THE WORLD BETTER OFF BY NOT KILLING IT?!?!

Seeing as this lazy writing was all I had to look forward to in the story going forward, I immediately quit the game and uninstalled. It may look like Chrono Trigger, but it's most certainly nowhere near that caliber of game.\t\t\t\t\t
