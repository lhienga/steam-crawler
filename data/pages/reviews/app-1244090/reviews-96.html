\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm very happy to recommend this game, it is a well crafted experience. It lives rent-free in my head and is another home run by Sabotage Studio. Following the triumph of The Messenger, Sabotage has not let down with its foray into the JRPG genre, with it's number of homages and call-backs. I can say with confidence that few JRPGs have captured me, and many other players, as Sea of Stars has. Eric W. Brown (AKA Rainbowdragoneyes) once again leads a masterclass in retro-inspired music, with the inclusion of Chrono Trigger's Mitsuda a real treat - the music invokes a sense of nostalgia - especially for players familiar with The Messenger.

The true magic of the story may be a little lost on people who haven't played "The Messenger" and interacted with the community, however, it is endearing and encourages the player to learn more about Sabotage's overarching universe and story. Stop reading this and play the game already!

If you get a chance, and have the funds, Sea of Stars is well worth your time - and I think it is my game of the year.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game that aims to scratch that nostalgia style but with modern quality of life improvements. If I had to sum it up in one word it would probably be "cozy." I got it at full price and consider it well worth the money.

Game play wise, nothing you probably haven't seen before. But was well executed for the most part. Enjoyed the lock system and attack timing. Both helped add some thought and reason to pay attention to battles. Rather than blindly spamming a few attacks over and over.

World building and story were fresh. Not much too far out there, but enough different from your standard fantasy RPG to be refreshing and interesting. Like focusing on the sun and moon as the source of the main characters power. Were a few plot twists I didn't see coming and mild denial on one that they'd follow through with. But they did and you'll know the one I'm talking about when you come to it. Applaud the devs for doing so and handing it very well, much better than how most games with similar twists.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was in love the first time the battle music kicked in and the little spikey snail dude was bobbing his head to the beat. Nuf Said.

(but i'll say more...)
I hadn't played a 2D JRPG all the way through since "Miracle Warriors" on the Sega Master System. My 2 favorite 3D JRPGs are "FFX" and "Lost Odyssey". I was REPEATEDLY surprised and impressed with the amount of care and love put into every little detail if this game. Any time I had a thought of "I hope I can do that" at some point, BOOM they did it! Want to travel faster? DONE! Want to buy, equip, and sell your old equipment all at once? DONE! Want a shortcut to the exit? DONE! The story, animations, characters, combat, design, music, (OMG THE MUSIC). *Chef's Kiss*

Just finished getting the final achievement. Even though it took 7 extra hours of New Game + time, it was worth it just to express my love of this game. The quality of life this game has ON RELEASE truly shows why we gamers turn away from the AAA studios and flock to these small passionate lovable teams. I bought the bundle with "The Messenger" and i'll be playing that next! I fully expect it will be a banger all the same! I already know i'm going to be buying every game this company puts out in the future in full support of their passion and love of games. 10/10 - Keep making games and show the world what quality means! <3<3<3\t\t\t\t\t
