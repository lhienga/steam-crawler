\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen loving this game from the very start. It very much brings me back to Chrono Trigger, but more modern.
- Beautiful art style and music
- Likable characters
- Doesn't take itself too seriously
- Feels very retro, but without those clunky things that we are glad to have as a thing of a past

Yes, it's "yet another pixel-art RPG," but beautifully done. The animations are very smooth, and varied. It shows a lot of love and attention to detail. There's some nice effects applied that make the world feel more real, despite being 2D. For example, if a character walks behind a crystal, their image will become warped as if refracted through the crystal.

The game respects your time. That includes things like:
- Save points happen often
- If you get a game over, you don't lose tons of progress
- There's no need to grind for levels (at least not up to where I'm at in the game). If you just go through the main story, your level would perfectly match the intended difficulty.
- There's Darksouls style shortcuts; things like ladders that you can push down to loop back to earlier parts of a dungeon.
- The dungeons aren't super long, and you won't have to fight the same monsters for very long, preventing things from getting stale and boring quickly

Some people seem to not care for the pirates because of their silly 4th wall breaking. I actually enjoyed it. Yes, it was a bit too on-the-nose at times, but I appreciate that the game didn't try to be so serious all the time.

The combat mechanics are enjoyable. Timing button presses with your hits to increase damage (or decrease when defending) makes things more impactful. It also puts some risk into certain skills; are you good at timing? Then perhaps a Moonarang might be a good choice here, if not, then try something else. There's also a bit of strategy - should you slurp up the "live mana" to try and take out this enemy quickly, or save it

I do like the characters. They all feel unique and have their own flair. Also - Serai is _so cool_.

I'll definitely buy a sequel, if one is in the works. I wouldn't be mad at having a whole crew of characters that were magical instead of just two.

Good game, but there's some things that could be adjusted:
- Valere and Zale seem OP and must-haves in your party at all times; it would be nice to have all characters viable. Unfortunately, these two characters are the only ones that have the special magic power that can hurt many enemies.
- Magic should be more impactful. Usually you only use it to break locks, and not for the damage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally a throw-back to the Super NES golden age of JRPGs. Battle system is very simple and adds an element of interaction that will keep most people involved in each encounter. There's a ton to do if you are looking to complete the true ending and the story is very rewarding (beware the mid-game SUPER sad moment).
Not much replay after you get the true ending, but nonetheless a good 40 hours for a game that pays homage to an era of games that probably is the reason many of us are JRPG fans nowadays.\t\t\t\t\t
