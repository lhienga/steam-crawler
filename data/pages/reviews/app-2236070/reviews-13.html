\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent climbing game with some minor stumbles towards the end. Has a fantastic sense of atmosphere and scope, with some of the most enthralling vistas I've ever seen in a game, let alone an indie title. Strong climbing mechanics with some really ingenious design makes for an experience with a gentle, but considerate, learning curve. Consumables and a really great rope checkpoint system make for many different ways to tackle each peak. A calm atmosphere, consistent rewards, and some unique challenges keep the game going for a long while. This game has a ton of content and makes it really worth playing.

The game's biggest hurdle is the jump between the beginner/medium to advanced and especially the expert peaks. Beginner peaks can take 20 minutes, only for advanced peaks to take upwards of an hour, and the final two levels taking upwards of five hours to complete. I really appreciate the game offering this challenge actually, as it brings a level of genuine challenge that few games lack! Clearing the final levels in this game is a genuine feat of strength, patience, and skill. However, I think that the game could have done a better job preparing you for those levels. Going from a fall causing twenty minutes of lost progress, to a fall costing two hours of lost progress, is just demoralising. Either the early levels should have been longer to better prepare you for the challenge, or the final peaks should be a bit easier to accommodate for their absurd length. Again, it isn't really an issue that these levels are so hard but rather that their intense length really makes it difficult to want to complete them.

Overall, I'd really recommend this game! It's a fantastic time with some really unique gameplay challenges you won't find elsewhere. Just bear in mind that this game asks a lot for completion, and that some might be genuinely unable to give what it asks.\t\t\t\t\t
