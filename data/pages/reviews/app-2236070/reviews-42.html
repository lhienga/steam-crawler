\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs other people have said, please do not go into this looking for a realistic climbing experience.

It is definitely not that, and is more of a physics-based climbing game, with rage game elements. This is especially obvious later on when the climbs get really long and more hazards introduced, but the game is rarely ever unfair. Every hazard has a way to be dealt with, but it is up to you to figure that out.

Do not expect to climb everything on the first try, it will likely take you many times to summit each one of the peaks.
However, the payoff of finally finishing a climb and seeing that view at the end makes it all worth it.

As a word of advice: Stay calm and Patient.
The moment you start tilting, you are going to make mistakes, which makes you even more tilted, and you make more mistakes.
There is no shame in popping down a rope and walking away for a couple minutes to calm down, I have had to do it several times on the later peaks to calm myself.

Happy climbing!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best game i've played this years, peaks of yore is a challenging but well paced climbing game making you think, how is this not vr. The control are excellent, with a lots of little detail in it, the feeling when jumping from a hold to another is unreal, your chest tighten and you release a sigh of relief when you manage to grab the hold.

The level design is insanely well made, teaching you new mechanics along while making it harder and harder, but never unfair, until you manage to master all mechanics and are left with the real mountain.

There are collectible to find which also prove as a challenge, rope to help you make hard jump without being punishing, or help you climb back up to it when you fall near it, a in-game speedrun timer that reset automatically once you touch the ground, other usefull item that i wont spoil, its just insane.

When i got into this game, i though it would be a 5-10 hours experience max, but here i am, reaching 22 hour, and still not done with the game. If you are a fan of climbing, just get this game.

You might think this is a rage game based on some review or current thread of gaming like only up, but i assume you that it is not. The dev have mastered the art of his craft, and all fundamental mountain can be climbed in 2-5 min max once you mastered the game, and learned the route.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very cool little game. Genuinely just fun.

I was worried about the amount of content, but here I'd say "hours played/Cost" doesn't really work.
At my pace a 17 hour playtime doesn't seem likely, but I don't really mind for the first time in a long while.
At the time of writing I'm around halfway done with the levels, but I foresee the actually hard climbs to take more time.

Talking about climbs, every climb is an actual brain scratching experience in the best way.

You learn a little something every climb.
Yes, the controls aren't perfect since you're working with physics grabbing, but they aren't exactly lacking either.
Adjusting your grip, reaching for the next grab and leaping all feel nice.
Sometimes your hands don't work the exact way you'd want them to.
But then you go again and do it faster.

I guess this is what some folks felt like with Getting Over It. This time I get it.

The story you get is fitting while not overdone, the gameplay is interesting and the game is challenging in a good way.

Cant help but to recommend.

Happy climbing :)\t\t\t\t\t
