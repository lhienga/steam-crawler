\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave you ever stumbled upon a video of Alex Honnold free-soloing a mountain and thought there's no way I would ever do this even with protection but were still kinda intrigued by it? How about randomly seeing a collab between STORROR's Toby Segar and Climbers Crag and were like this is fun? I would highly recommend this game then. (NOTE: Maybe this game is good for people who actually are climbers as well, would not know as I am not one)

-- GAMEPLAY --

Gameplay is presented in challenges of various level - what I have discovered so far are 'intro' mountains, which are challenging on their own anyways, 'intermediate' boulders which are shorter but more precise climbing experiences and lastly 'advanced' mountains which I have only finished the first one so far.

Positives are:
  • Challenges are well-thought out and designed to prepare you for the next ones. Just like in life, you keep learning and things keep getting harder
  • Moves and challenges fit the real life, at least from my limited experience through yt videos - here you also have crimps, cruxes and so forth
  • Some mountains do have options to 'partner up' with an npc climber which is a nice change to mainly soloing
  • There are various types of ways you can 'conquer' these peaks - you can use various gear such as chalk, ropes to keep you safe and coffee to give you temporary jump boost, you can free solo without any equipment, do a time trial or create a new route with partner. A lot of challenges also have certain collectibles you can find if you go a bit out of the way which prove to be useful for the most part (more ropes, more coffee)

Negatives are:
  • Sometimes the hands move in a mysterious way and when you think you are right there to grab the next stone you fall down to your doom
  • Partnering up with npc shows them climbing on a surface that is not even remotely accessible to you which is kind of grating
  • Seeing some speedruns shows that it is possible for immersion of realism to be broken but I am nowhere near good enough for this

-- STORY --

There is a story there. I would not really consider it important nor a reason to get this game but mainly as a means to provide more resources for climbing.

-- CONCLUSION --

I usually play this while listening to a podcast or an audio book, so it is a good, fun without getting too invested kinda game. It is INCREDIBLY challenging but it is also fun and gives you a sense of freedom. Most of the time you are dangling at a random place on the mountain, thinking about where to go or if you should put safety harness at that spot or risk it so you have more further up. As annoying the falls are, peaks are just as satisfying and you do feel a real sense of accomplishment. Fully recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
