\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game, this isn't a rage game with a gimmick, it has levels, items and different mechanics you are introduced slowly throughout.
It can be difficult, some frustration can be felt, but you can always pick another level and try again later, everything is always in reach.
Now, it's true that the LATER levels crank the difficulty very high. I still have not climbed up the last level. But I know I can.

Gameplay wise, this is a physics based climbing, you grab onto a hold ( a rock protruding from the wall usually) with a click, you hold that click, then swing your other arm with the other mouse button. Very similar to Jusant if you played it, but in first person, and more freedom, as you can release both hands while swinging to hurl yourself at a direction (hopefully toward a grab...)
You also use your feet once you unlock crampons, it allows you to make a jump, or you can plant your feet on the wall to manage some holds that drrain your stamina.
There are more stuff to learn, but it's best to experience yourself. Also I suck at explaining.

Aside from gameplay, there is no "plot" per se, but there is a setting, a fictional "I can't believe it's not scotland' feel in the 1880s. You meet voiced characters that gives you tips, items and sometimes a hand. It's nice. Cosy. Atmospheric. Only music in the cabin, which is where you pick your levels. During climbing you only hear ambience.

The game has a pleasant discord server, with the dev active in it. There is a small speedrun community for people who likes that. I am not fond of difficulty myself, and yet I keep playing.
So yeah it's worth your time and money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game that has it's own type of "skill ceiling".
Will go fairly quick IMO in terms of basic skill to intermediate in terms of how good you get, but from there, it's all about thinking outside the box and learning things your own way!

Gameplay itself is great. Lot's of wholesome meetings with different characters with story'ish content, rewards to earn, different routes to explore/master and so forth.
Graphics are amazing if you like this kind of style. You can change between the old school black and white filter, or you can go colorful though I prefered colors in my playthrough.

In terms of maps, there are TONS. And they get big, I mean BIIIIIG, and I could only imagine what this would be like in VR. There are no other climbing games like it, especially when it comes to the design, layout and previously mentioned, size.
I have never been a fan of rage games like "getting over it", because I don't have the patience, so take my word for it that this game is not like that. You will know once you play it.

If you like what you see in the trailers, then I highly recommend you to get it as it's most likely the kind of game you will enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t
