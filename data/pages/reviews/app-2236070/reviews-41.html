\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeaks of Yore is one of the most unusual and unfriendly games of its time.
In it, your task is to simply drag yourself up a mountain with your bare hands.

The act of climbing, in the digital world or in real life, has certain essential properties that gives the game its flavor.
No amount of forward progress is guaranteed; some cliffs are too sheer or too slippery.
And the player is constantly, unremittingly in danger of falling and losing everything.

There's a sense of truth in that lack of compromise.
Most obstacles in videogames are fake—you can be completely confident in your ability to get through them, once you have the correct method or the correct equipment, or just by spending enough time.

In that sense, every polygonal obstacle in Peaks of Yore is real.
They are unyielding, and that makes the game uniquely frustrating.
But I'm not sure Anders Grube Jensen intended to make a frustrating game—the frustration is just essential to the act of climbing and it's authentic to the process of building a game about climbing.

Imaginary mountains build themselves from our efforts to climb them, and it's our repeated attempts to reach the summit that turns those mountains into something real.\t\t\t\t\t

Peaks of Yore - The tumble is part of the climb

Peaks of Yore offers a nerve-racking climbing experience like no other game I have played. Set in 1887 it is practically dripping with old-school charm and style for the old souls out there. It takes you from the simple beginner peaks that all ambitious climbers must conqure before setting out for greater heights, to the towering mountains that few have ever set foot atop of. It gives the player a number of satisfying mountaineering tools aiding the player in conquering The Gales' more precarious climbs. These tools also become permanent unlocks, so you will still be able to use these in previous maps where you didn't the first time around. These climbs are incredibly tense as you desperately swing from one handhold to another, trying not to loose your grip, and grasping the mouse just that little bit too hard while playing. Everything from the sound design to the satisfaction of overcoming a peak that previously had you pulling your hair out is exilirating! The process of failing, trying, failing again ad libitum is as much a part of the process as succeeding in your goal, and it does this through its simple yet immersive mechanics! If any game deserved the title of easy and fun to learn, hard but rewarding to master, it would be Peaks of Yore!
IT'S NOT PERFECT!!! Although I have praised the game a lot, there are issues I have that have been pissing me off to no end! Physics are very weird sometimes, and I have found myself being pushed away from a cliffs face without any reason to, causing literal hours of wasted ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ time. The pause does not actually pause the game, but just toggle certain holds so you don't fall, but it does not apply to the crampons, which led to another 3 hours of my time wasted because of poor design. Although these things may seem small, when you are climbing the last map in the game, these things will piss you off to no end, and I seriously would not fault anyone who would cheat to get to the top, because I feel like I have been screwed over multiple times to utter h o r s e s h i t! With that being said, the game is fun, but mark my words, if the game screws you over like it has done for me, then save yourself the trouble and just download a debugger or cheat to the top, because if the game is going to cheat, then so should you.\t\t\t\t\t
