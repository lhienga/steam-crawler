\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game that does a solid job of teaching you mechanics as you go while ramping up the challenge. Successfully climbing the later stages really feels like an accomplishment, and with the mostly tight controls falling always feels like it's a mistake that YOU made as opposed to the game just deciding to say "no u".

I say "mostly" regarding the controls because every once in a while you'll have a moment where you swear you'll see your guy's hand touch a hold and not grab it, but that could just as easily be jank as it could be user error on my end. There's a certain level of stress involved when your mouse hand is sweating hard and you're at a tricky part of a climb.

Music is sparse but fitting where it shows up, the plot is lighthearted and charming, and the process of grabbing collectables from the levels is actually relatively painless (once you've gotten something you've gotten it, you don't have to finish the climb to keep it). Grabbing the collectables is also tangibly rewarded with more climbing resources, which I personally like having as an incentive.

I don't know if I'll go for the harder challenges because some of them sound pretty brutal, but I'll definitely come back to this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly recommend. Intensely challenging and satisfying climbing sim with varied gameplay mechanics and hours upon hours of content.

Its a lot more than just a challenge game though. It feels like a love letter to the art of climbing irl and early mountaineering culture, there's all sorts of lingo and related terms used constantly by the colorful and humorous npc mountaineers you meet along the way. I had no idea what a peakbagger was and i didn't realize "bouldering" was an actual term until i googled it later, it's educational lol.

The game has a ton of different peaks with their own challenges and mechanics, and views at the top. collectible items and easy/hard modes. And a built in timer feature. It starts you off slow from tiny tutorial hills no longer than a minute, and slowly builds you up until you're ready to climb hours long mountains. The satisfaction of getting to the top of them, after a long exhausting climb that sweats and tires your hands making each next hold all the more stressful and harder...its an experience of immersion that is hard to describe.

I can't recommend the game enough, criminally underrated.\t\t\t\t\t
