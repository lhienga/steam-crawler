\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeaks of Yore is an absolute sleeper and possibly one of the best games I've played. I've never found a game that's so comfy yet so sweaty, so relaxing yet so challenging. It manages to simultaneously be a game that leaves my palms actually dripping with sweat, but also one of the chillest games to vibe to up there with the likes of casual farming simulators.

It does have some similarities with "ragebait" precision platformers like Jump King and Getting Over It, but it manages to keep the stakes high while also giving you plenty of ways to keep yourself safe and avoid losing progress. Falling resets you, but you can rope it an almost any time, tethering you to the wall. For the true chill climbing experience, there's an optional safety harness that lets you leapfrog your ropes so that you never have to be untethered, just enjoying the climb all the way up.

There are collectibles and loads of unlocks for hitting different achievements, and watching your little shack and empty belt grow is so gratifying. It feels truly rewarding to hit those achievements and get new gear, or get upgrades, or more uses, or unlock a new set of peaks to summit.

My only real complaint is that some of the achievements are so unbelievably, punishingly difficult that there's about a 0% chance you'll 100% game, if you're an achievement hunter. The final peak of the game can take hours to summit, and there are multiple achievements for completing it on permadeath modes and without any of your tools. Keep in mind that this is the last peak of the game, which means you need to do everything before it, and you're expected to do it without ever falling a single time.

Utterly fantastic game otherwise and I cannot recommend it to anyone enough. This is a game you get on full price because it is just that good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Peaks of Yore, upon first glance, seems to be another Getting Over It style rage game, but it is so much more than that. In reality, this game is a beautiful climbing experience about proving to yourself that you can do it. There is no major incentive or goal other than "climb the mountain", yet i always find myself wanting to continue.
The physics are fair, with every fall feeling like it's your own fault. The characters and minimalist approach on storytelling are amazing (i only wish we saw more of the characters). The gimmicks are difficult in the best way, making each climb feel unique. Even when you complete climbs, there is reason to go back, in the form of time trials, which then leads me to the fact that this game makes moving fast feel so good (to the point where i haven't even fully completed the second book because i keep going back the first for faster times).
last things i want to say are that i have not fully completed the game yet (as explained in spoiler), i found the game through spiff, i'm probably gonna get the DLC day of, and this is a 10/10 from me. that's all, back to slapping birds for me, happy climbing!
