\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an amazing game. If you have been mislead to believe this is just a rage game, you have been lied to. This game is an amazing, addictive challenge with a great atmosphere. As soon as I started this game I knew I'd love it, hesitantly planning each move as I inched my way up the small rocks in fear.

I'll start off by talking about the length. It's, LONG. There are 20 beginner climbs, 10 intermediate climbs, 5 (lengthy) advanced climbs, and two unconquered climbs of 2300m and 7500m (almost as tall as mt everest). There are also collectibles too keep you preoccupied if that's not enough, but trust me... it is. You WILL not finish this game in under 20 hours, meaning that you are paying less than a DOLLAR per hour of content.

Aesthetic. It's perfect. There is a sense of accomplishment after each climb, as you look down and see how far you've climbed. There is a sense of fear as you near the top, with wind howling. The whole theme of 1800s and the lack of self-preservation is great. You'll meet familiar faces on your climbs, and they are always so welcoming and even helpful at times. It makes the mountains feel less lonely.

Difficulty. I can't lie, it's a tough game. Mountains aren't forgiving or fair, they are simply mountains. The game understands this very well. That is what makes it a triumph, that is what makes it so tense and so satisfying to complete each climb. The game does do a good job of easing the player into it for the most part, though I must admit there are two mountains that I feel are just frustrating and unfun. Old skerry and the beginner mountain with all the falling boulders are just annoying. Otherwise, very well balanced game.

Gameplay. It's solid, very solid. The physics feel a bit odd at first, but once you get the hang of them they feel natural and well thought out. Sort of in the same way that the source engine is janky in a good way, this game too has jank that is consistent and useful to the player. It can be learned and mastered and it adds a lot of depth to this game.

Variety. Each mountain is unique, almost all of them offering a different gimmick for the player to learn. In the advanced mountains, you'll find yourself using everything you've learned.

Overall, I think I'd give this game a 9.4/10. It's really really good and gives you a lot of content for the price. I'd HIGHLY recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t
