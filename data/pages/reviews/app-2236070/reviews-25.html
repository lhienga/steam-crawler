\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a simplistic but exciting game to play. I have to keep playing after I fall from a hold. It is an addiction. This game is so good that, even if I tried, I would not be able to leave if I leave a peak unconquered. The idea of a excruciatingly hard game that requires you to climb (Only Up, Getting Over It etc.) is not new. Peaks of Yore builds up on the idea by having a clear progression, that does not contain barely any skill gaps. The later into the game you get, you naturally get better really quick as you practice and progress. There is also a mental aspect of the game. It is unnerving when you look down hanging from a ledge super high up, or when you barely make a jump 300 feet in the air. The replayability of the climbing in this game is something I have personally never seen or felt before. The art style is also something that is very refreshing. It really fits the old Scottish feel of the game. It just feels like the game is was made with precious time, passion and love.

As of writing, I have 28 hours on this game. I have finally started my journey to beat the Solemn Tempest, and I expect my hours on this game may double. Wish me luck. 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
