\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: A interesting game that needs some Quality of Life adjustments

The game is pretty fun, honestly. It scratches an itch I haven't had scratched since playing The Climb on Quests - and I don't have to sell my soul to Facebook to get it! Characters are nice, the accents are quaint and the views are... well, a bit lackluster. If you like trees that expand into an endless skybox, than I suppose you might find it scenic.

The biggest con of the game is that you won't be hearing a lot of the dialogue unless you're flawless at your climb. If you fall while a character is talking, you just don't get to hear them say anything. Lord help you if you have to replay the map - something I had to do at a few peaks, due to rope issues - because then they will just not be there anymore, meaning you have missed out on dialogue not only THERE, but also in the future!

If I were to give a suggestion, I would say the characters should remain if you redo the map. I don't see why they should just vanish unless you wanted to portray some "passing of time" mechanic, but there really isn't a reason for that.Being able to repeat dialogue would be perfect.

Another big problem is the tips...namely that they come and go so quickly that you won't be able to see some of them. When I managed to get on one of the ropes, it said I could ascend with % which was very much not the case, it was Q, Q is not %! I had to check the keybindings a few times just to be able to make sure I got everything.\t\t\t\t\t
