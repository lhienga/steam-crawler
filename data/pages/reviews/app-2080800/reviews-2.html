\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeath Mark 2 is a fantastic sequel to the original Death Mark. For people who enjoyed the original game read no further, pick up this game when it hits a price you're comfortable with.

If you haven't played Death Mark I'd suggest tackling that first as this is very much a direct sequel with multiple references to the previous game.

I've been overjoyed by the atmosphere of this game including the clean artwork and unsettling music. The central location of Konoehara high school allows a cohesive feel to the exploration while offering new areas to for later chapters.

I'm about 11 hours in at the moment and am enjoying unravelling the game's mystery.

For a full price game the one drawback I've found is the lack of voice acting for the majority of dialogue, though the occasional phrases sprinkled in, particularly on character bio screens is appreciated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a big and long-time fan of the Spirit Hunter series, I was really happy with this addition overall, but I do have some complaints.

I will now break down my thoughts into different categories in my review which will contain a lot of comparisons with the previous games in this series (Death Mark 1 and NG). And as this is my first-ever review I hope this is helpful to both old and new fans who are considering this game!

Gameplay
I personally really liked the VN, point-and-click format in the previous two games and was slightly skeptical about the addition of the side scroll element in this one at first as I thought it would lessen the sense of dread and immersion during investigations, but I ended up liking it. Although what I feared did occur, the overall atmosphere is still as tense as ever, and adding the side scroll makes the exploration feel much more fun.

They fixed up a lot of gameplay issues from the previous two games. From my memory, there were many times when I felt frustrated (especially with the decision-making logic) and found navigation difficult in the old games, but I didnt feel any of that in this one! The exploration felt straightforward and clear, with a much more understandable logic in its choices.

As for the system of battling spirits, this new approach is a little strange. It is cool to add the RNG element, but also annoying at the same time because what do you mean that the MC who is a teacher has a low success rate of reading out a passage? On top of that, sometimes you could still fail certain actions when the success rate is very high. The fact that you can keep retrying before your health runs out also makes the encounters feel less challenging. This format is a little silly to me, and although I got used to it later in the game, I preferred the old system where if you pick something wrong, then its an instant Game Over.

Story
I think the story was good overall with how engaging it was the whole way. It is a little unfortunate how the twist wasnt that surprising, but at least they kept me second-guessing the whole way.

As for the tales of the spirits, since the location is more strictly related to high school this time, I found some pretty cliche since this setting is overused. I understand it was a challenge to make them interesting, which they managed to do in most cases, but it didnt quite hit for me. Although I still enjoyed them, I think that the spirits and their tales were more creative and memorable in the previous games (especially NG), so this was a slight letdown.

Characters
They were a huge improvement from Death Mark 1. I really loved the original cast in Death Mark 1 but will admit that they suffered from the lack of characterization due to how many characters there are and how they didnt have much time to shine due to the storys structure. Although minimal as it is not the focus of the game, the old crew had so much more characterization this time around through their little comments during exploration and interactions with one another, making them feel more alive despite still not having a lot of screen time.

As for the new characters, they were fun and quirky, but I honestly didnt really care for them, which might be intentional for the player to focus more on the OG crew (and if that is so, it was successful). I wish we had a little more time with some of them, but considering how many characters there are, it is understandable that some, among the new characters, didnt get as much spotlight as the others.

Art
As some may know, this game was crowdfunded and probably gave them way more budget to work with. The artwork in this series has always been one of its highlights, at least for an artist like me, and this game did not disappoint. I was really happy with the quality of the CGs and their consistency throughout the game, regardless of their significance level, which was most likely achieved by the extra budget they had this time around.

The death CGs you get when you fail to bring a spirit to complete salvation were great! Death Mark 1 did not have any, so it was cool to see these CGs of the original crew. Of course, they are also exceptionally well composed and rendered; however, conceptually speaking, they are a step down from the ones in NG in my opinion. Maybe it is due to this games spirits nature, but I wish the CGs were as gruesome and violent as NGs.

As for the spirits designs, I preferred the ones in the previous games, but this could be due to my personal taste. In my opinion, I think this games direction went for more of a weird and bizarre vibe, whereas the previous games were more grotesque and disturbing. I was certainly more terrified of the previous spirits designs compared to the one in this game.

Sound/Voice Acting
Delicious. This series has always been really good with their sounds but I think this is another step up from the previous games and I am very impressed. Really kept up the suspense and tension all the way through each investigation. Again, bless the budget!!

This game does not have a lot of voice acting, most of it being grunts, pants, and short voiced lines here and there, but considering how there was close to no voice acting in previous games, I am happy with it.

Comments on partial nudity
Now this is something I never liked about this series. There are a number of CGs with partial nudity of underaged girls in suggestive poses. I was a little glad with NG where they reduced that from Death Mark 1, but Death Mark 2 seemed to go back to its roots. I think there are fewer in Death Mark 2 compared to 1, but I still cant help but feel uncomfortable seeing them.

----------------

TLDR; Despite all its faults, I would recommend it! A huge improvement from the previous titles (especially Death Mark 1) in many aspects, although there are certain things I still prefer in NG. The game is not as difficult or scary as the previous ones but I think it is still worth a shot. To a lot of people, I would say $50 is a bit much but as someone who appreciates the art in this game a lot along with the fact how this includes 2 DLCs (if I am not mistaken), I didn't mind.

For newcomers, I think it is ok to hop into Spirit Hunter with this game, although I would still recommend playing Death Mark 1 first since this game is a direct sequel to that.

7.5/10 as a game, 9/10 for my heart\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's just get this out of the way right off the bat: I really liked this game.
That said, I had a lot of problems with it.

It felt much more like DLC for the original than its own game deserving $50. The story is fun, the art is beautiful, and I enjoyed most of the puzzles, but there's just not that much going on. Much of the exploration is very repetitive, and feels like padding, especially considering the only proper location to explore is the school. I kept expecting to be lead somewhere new, to really get that sense of adventure I got from the first two games, only to be a bit disappointed that none (except one) of the promising new areas seen in the story were fleshed out at all.

One of my biggest gripes was how little each of the "spirit of the week" stories was fleshed out. I kept finding myself thinking "that's it?" at the end of each chapter after the second. Chapters one and two were very long and fleshed out, and I felt satisfied by the information I discovered on the spirit's past, and the way they were "saved." Unfortunately, every chapter after was much shorter, and much less detailed, which is a crying shame, considering how fun uncovering those mysteries can be.

The last big thing that bothered be was the collectibles. I love collectibles. I'm obsessed with them. If I know they exist I can't keep playing until I know I've found them all. Make sure you also play like this, because despite how optional the game makes them sound, I'd argue at least half are completely necessary to finish the game without relying on RNG to make it through the final scenarios. It's not a huge deal, but it certainly does upset the tension of certain scenes when you feel the need to backtrack to find every collectible before doing what the story wants you to do, which is usually a supposedly time-sensitive issue.

I know I had a lot of pretty specific complaints, but I really do think anyone who enjoyed the first two Spirit Hunter games would also enjoy this one. That said, wait for it to go on sale. It's not worth $50.\t\t\t\t\t

Redundant and Time Wasting Mechanics
In the first game and NG you really had to pay attention to what order you explored the rooms, as you were rewarded or punished for searching every room and interacting with most objects.

This game outright tells you where to go next in bold colored text, while also littering the map with empty locations to explore and needless items to click on that point out the obvious, like that chairs in the room are stacked. In a game where you are suppose to search for clues, having dozens of pointless interactions bogs the game down.

It's one thing to punish a player for exploring, but it's ill advised to have the player backtrack previously explored empty rooms for minor (clues) the player already knows from very obvious dialogue and story points in a earlier act.

As an Example:

In the school on the second day you need to go to the student council room as the dialogue states. Though gives you the option to search 5 other rooms which state "no one is here right now," which is a needless choice. If the choice does not have a consequence (good or bad) then the story should just be continued VN style with scene transitions instead of forcing the player to run around to find the one something or someone the game forces them to find in the first place.

Wasting the players time with needless mechanics does not enhance the experience, it's just tiring. Do I need to really search 9 different class rooms to learn that a character was bullied when it was literally written on the walls and even stated by the ghost that you were not a bully?

Also, when deciding which clue to use during encounter there is a chance that using the clue will fail based on RNG. Not because you chose the wrong person, or wrong clue, but just because the RNG decided there should have to repeat the clue selection options to choose the same clue again for another RNG roll.

The game is shallow despite trying to be something more than the previous titles.

Inferior Art Direction
The game introduces a side scrolling element that makes use of low detail anime style 2D art. If I had to guess, it was a cost cutting design choice. As in the first game and NG every frame was very detailed since you saw it in the first person point of view.

Furthermore, in NG and DM1 all of the monsters were either frightening or disturbing in appearance with a few I did not want to look at due to their more disturbing features. Though DM2 went in a more PG direction with its monsters, where they have some "horror" elements, but also look cartoon-ish enough not to really be frightening when they walk toward you. The first monster made me think "a water balloon for a head, what a weird choice" and the second "The face isn't realistic enough to be scary, it's more like an alien," and the same ends up being true for all of the spirits as CH4's was just silly looking. They just are not scary when you see the full reveal.

In this way the game is grossly overpriced.

Post Game
Having finished the game, I feel as though the story was underwhelming compared to the first game in the series. As the original starts with nothing but a mystery, which comes to a climactic conclusion with a plot twist that only really started to reveal itself toward the end.

In SH 2, the plot twist is practically given away at the start. So the ending will not surprise at all. Not to mention the way some characters behave will just leave you wondering, "why did they even include this" because there are a fair few glaring moments in the story where content feels like it would have been better off cut from the game. This includes characters, dialogue, and even one of the spirits.

The weakest point of the story is Ch1
(The game has a very rough start.)

Though it might just be my personal taste, so I will just leave it at that. Anything more would just end up being spoilers.

Suggestions [Minor-Spoilers]
1. Cut out the RNG mechanics. The game would need a lot more game-play to justify having RPG mechanics, as adding RNG to choosing clues was an awful idea.

2. Make the monsters frightening again, the spirits were the least frightening in the series. Though I will say, the scissors tearing out of the skin curse was praiseworthy. Otherwise, all of the spirits were a disappointment visually.

3. The cut-scenes should have been cut from the game, they did not even provide a good intro for the spirits.

4. Heed all of the critiques made in this review.

5. While returning characters can be nice, the game would have been better off focusing on the new cast like NG did. The returning characters are hardly more than interchangeable companions with flavor text, while the only returning character that had a meaningful part to play in the story was the detective. Instead the focus got split between a needlessly expanded cast of characters.

All of the other returning characters just took away opportunities from the new characters to shine. As it is better to have a few meaningful interactions than to have many meaningless ones.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this series. I consider myself lucky to have stumbled across it and I'm definitely going to be helping Kickstart any future games. Compared to the first game and NG, this feels like a more forgiving entry, as a lot of encounters feel less difficult and you have a way bigger margin of error making it pretty feasible to trial and error your way through at times. The mystery and tension didn't feel as high as the first game either, but from a mechanics standpoint, I enjoyed finding the collectibles and it was easier to navigate.

If we end up with a game that has the stakes/tension of Death Mark 1, the vibrant characters of NG, and the mechanics of Death Mark 2...that would be one hell of an experience.

As a side note: the game is a direct sequel to Death Mark 1, so you should definitely play that first. NG is not really referenced outside of one character in a bonus chapter, and even then it doesn't really spoil anything, so you don't need to play NG to enjoy this game. Do it anyone though, NG is great.
