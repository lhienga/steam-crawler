\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love these games so much. I wish that they could go on forever. I love this game as much as the first two, but I do have some critiques.

As always, the art is my favorite aspect of the series. I've always loved the skill of the artist with their rendering and the variety of people that they can design and draw. The monster designs have always been my absolute favorite part. That said, some of the monster designs in this game were either relatively dull compared to the rest of the spirits across all games, or the designs didn't make sense within the context of the spirit and its story.

The good and true endings (I don't have the bad one yet) overstay their welcome. The need to tie everything up into such a neat package and/or give exposition on every single detail of the case is unnecessary in a game like this, and I believe that it's clumsy and awkward.

The RPG-esque elements were interesting, but why the success/fail percentage? Aside from adding tension, it doesn't make sense and is only frustrating when you know you've chosen the right answer but it doesn't work.

Even with those critiques, the only aspect of the game that truly disappoints me is the absence of Hyakki Yagyo. That's been a looming threat that's been mentioned in the first two games, and even if it wasn't the focal point of this game, I was hoping that there would at least be more build-up to it for a future game. Sadly, that wasn't the case. I hope that the Hyakki Yagyo plot hasn't been abandoned. I was/am very excited for it! I hope that there is a next game and that Hyakki Yagyo will be the focus! Also it would be amazing if Yashiki and Kijima team up like NG hinted!!!

That said, the mysteries were very interesting, I was happy to see the Mark Bearers again, it was really cool to be in a single location instead of moving around and become familiar with it. The new traversal system is great (I liked the old way too, but this is certainly easier to navigate and the animated characters are more dynamic to watch), and THANK YOU for the quality-of-life gameplay design choices like [Q] to return to the Infirmary. The game would have been very tedious without that. It was SO COOL to have little animated movies at key moments! I loved it!

Thank you very much for another marvelous game!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game, and the series, but I get the feeling this'll be the last one.
But I feel like it's missing something.

The story here is great, some magnificent psychological build up (the protagonist this time has character!) and it has some real great character moments. However, because of those character moments, a lot of other characters get sidelined, and the same applies to places in the school. If you think that setting looked nice, you'll never see it again. except for old school building and fox forest

As someone who loved the art of the older games, Kazuhiro Oya's take was incredible. But I feel the design of the spirits have sunken in contrast to what we have here. The older spirits were horrifying, looked like a real grudge given form, like something I wouldn't even dare go near let alone want to see! yet here we have spirits that are like 'yea im a girl who hung herself and my neck long asf'
Also, i would like to comment on the brutality of the cgs. In the older games, we saw the most gruesome death you could imagine, genuine nightmare fuel that put any horror you've ever seen to shame, but now we get something that looks just a lil' spooky and doesn't feel like it fits in a horror game.
Don't even get me started on the sexual cgs those are weird as hell.
I want to be scared, I don't want to be horny.

But the music is magnificent, something truly beautiful. The deduction them, and the theme of the school, both really capture the atmosphere, and it's super wondrous how it works, I genuinely feel oppressed when I feel like I'm being talked to in the school, thanks to the music. (the composer is Naoaki Jimbo and he is THE BEST composer, im just sayin' if he needs a kidney or two)

And overall?
I really enjoyed this game, I was there since the announcement trailer. I waited several years (since the release of NG) for another game, and they've delivered a great piece of art. (also the side scrolling mechanic is cool)
I only bought the game because I'm a nut for this series, but if you're on the fence, just wait till it goes on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpirit Hunter: Death Mark II is a top-tier VN horror game. I would recommend it 100%, with a 1000% for horror-enthusiast.

The ghost stories are deeply engaging, complex and well-thought out with the main theme being LOVE BEYOND DEATH (twisted love, pure love, you name it the game has it all). I was constantly on the edge and glued from beginning to end, and given the nature of the game, I'd say they did exceptionally well in this regard. Obviously, I feel like the character interactions, puzzles and multiple choices are the biggest contributions as to why the guessing game in here is so damn good. Also, having played the previous entry (Spirit Hunter: Death Mark), I appreciate all the little details, references and the passion the devs poured into the game, which enriches the experience more. I believe in order to fully enjoy this outstanding sequel, playing the first game is highly recommended to understand the world of Spirit Hunter in regards to cursed dolls, spirits and events that lead to Death Mark II.

To begin, the suspenseful, mysterious and tragic storytelling of Death Mark II coupled with our 'precious sensei' or Spirit Doctor, Kazuo Yashiki the MC is a prime example of how horror stories should be told. Yashiki ability to mesh with the rest of the cast and bring about their full quirky personality, as well as the way his relationships with the main heroines develop deserves praise. His reactions between hope and despair are priceless, yet despite the impossible odds, his determination and resourcefulness to save the living and the dead are what makes him truly unique (yes you need to save both to achieve the True Ending). This element of individuality adds weight and depth to his character, allowing the story to progress as it meant to be told. Adding to that, I really dig the 'feel' of the art designs for the ghosts, not to mention the eerie tracks on some areas which are the main reasons why I keep coming back to play Experience.Inc's games. And I really like the new characters (playful and teasing Michiho, shy and timid Douryou, yandere waifu The Departed, strict and stern Sakamoto, weird but helpful Abe) as well as returning ones (Satoru, Yasuoka, Daimon and Hiroo). Furthermore, the game has short movies in some chapters and I think its rare to see this in horror games, which is a delight to experience.

Though, the 2D-side scrolling feels like a downgrade due to the fact that it made the horror sections i.e spirit interactions much 'cheaper' of an experience compared to the usual exploration in the previous entries, however it did manage to create a sense of adventure when you're collecting items to level your spirits and receiving area damage. That being said, the rest of the game are dreadfully fun since it focuses more on character stats and sacred objects to help you in your cases. As for jump-scares, you can turn it on or off in the settings, but having it enabled feels much more immersive imo since it can appear in a variety of ways even when you're chilling in the corner :). Now for the fan-service on the main heroines (which are likely made for the Japanese audience), they are tastefully done (in a weird way) and doesn't clash with the ghastly, macabre atmosphere at all, rather it serves to further entrap you with a sense of bizarreness and anxiety where death is your constant companion.

In terms of technical issues, I believe there are some text errors here and there i.e Nakamura instead of Nakamatsu, Stoppping instead of Stopping etc. The controller support follows the Xbox control layout but so far I've faced no problems playing with a controller.

Overall, I had massive fun and enjoyment playing Spirit Hunter: Death Mark II. The game is worth it (only when it's on sale, if ever). I consider it an unforgettable 9/10. It's just that good, I hope Experience.Inc will cook up another Spirit Hunter game and keep up their Japanese spirit, maybe focusing on Lovecraftian horrors next (fishing hamlet, otherworldly city and fish people in Japan) or European settings (vampires, witch-hunt, castle ruins) because I know they can make it happen. The sky's the limit, there are so many ways for this series to branch out and I'm excited to play its sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
