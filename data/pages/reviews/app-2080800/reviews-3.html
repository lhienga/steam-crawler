\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was waiting for Death Mark II release and I'm glad that I'm here to experience it now!

Only finished Chapter 1 for now and it's fun to see side-scrolling investigation (I hope I won't have to run and hide from dangers in Clock Tower-style haha), 10 running old men grunts out of 10. Previous games pixel hunting somehow gave more spooks and immersion tho, but I won't complain much about it, it's nice to have fresh mechanics.
Also glad to see that now stats and items can actually have weight in gameplay, as well as success system gives more intrigue (but sometimes it feels a bit silly, I wish it could look more spooky).

Will update the review after finishing the game! For now, I'm gonna reflect on cases and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and add some sugar and drink coffee



UPD after finishing game thoughs! Can contain minor spoilers so proceed at your risk :>

Some of things didn't turn out as I expected both mechanic- and story-wise, but overall I still enjoyed the game.

I wish there were more engagements and surprises in side-scrolling part. Although I'm glad no one chased my ass, but something like hiding spooky spirits or someone slowly approaching you in the hallway so you have to run to the destination would be nice for extra pressure. Or more changes in enviroment as curses growing stronger or a bit more puzzles, perhaps?

Speaking about jumpscapes, in DMII they feels cheap, wacky and more annoying rather than really spooky :( I love the way they were done in prequel, when you are scared to open a closet or move flashlight to a certain spot because you may find otherworldly presence silently staring at you and then disappearing in a blink of eye, as if making you question your own sanity.

The spirits didn't feel so grotesque and disturbing as I expected and failed to make feel uneasy. Most time I laughed at their goofiness (still would not want to meet those guy IRL tho). I think the designs from previous games did a greater job in horror terms.

The fights became much easier to go through and I wish they were much more challenging (random is kinda fun at first but overall this element feels wacky and doesn't make me tense my butt on the seat) and unforgiving for mistakes.

And uuuh chapter 4 was totally unnecessary, in my humble opinion..........
(I require more sexy scary fanservice with male characters as a moral damage compensation after this cringe moment)

And yet, I enjoyed the story and the growing tension despite the answer to a mystery being more obvious than I could expect. I also love to meet old crew and see more developments to their characters as well as engagements with each other. Also enjoyed bits of everyone's voices :)

I would give a solid 7/10 because it was good, but it could be much better. Perhaps if there wasn't the unfortunate incident of losing a lot of development progress, the game could have all the things that it is lacking now :(

Meeoowww.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a huge fan of previous Spirit Hunter Death Mark and NG, I was looking forward to this for a very long time, and somehow still was NOT disappointed.
They knew not to change a winning formula, only to expand on it. As such, the story structure is very similar, old characters of Death Mark 1 return or are at least mentioned (Sorry, NG fans - and that includes me). The exploration is now more engaging in a Clock Tower like 2D sidescrolling way, I guess some may like it, others not.. character stats now matter more, even though spirit encounters seem a bit more streamlined. I have no problem with that.
Writing and character art is the same as we're used to. The price tag I was hoping to be lower (Yeah I am delusional like that) but it seems to be shaping up to be bigger game overall.

TL;DR for players who don't know the series: Top tier Japanese horrror, Visual-Novel with gameplay style (think Ace Attorney).
There is almost no voice acting except some character grunts, coughs, catch phrases.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally enjoying it thus far!

Liked the original, loved NG (bloodmetry and the trio goated), have high hopes for the rest of this one.

As always great character portraits, updated tracks from the first game sound great, the mystery is in full swing and I'm gripped! Fast travel is a nice quality of life update and I felt like the first boss fight (what I'm up to) wasn't too obtuse which was a little frustrating in the first game.

Not a big fan of the sidescrolling exploration/2D animation. I think they look a little goofy and there was a sense of dread in the first game with loading each screen as you progressed, even if it was a bit tedious at times. I would love to see an Etrian Odyssey style 3D overworld as you walk around instead.

Looking forward to playing more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed playing the game, but it did have its some issues. While there's a lot to delve into about this game in comparison to its prequel, I'll keep it short.

Major Drawbacks compared to the prequel:

1.The mystery element was somewhat disappointing â€“ I practically figured out the culprits early on, which weakened the overall plot. The prequel's mystery element, in my opinion, was much more engaging.

2.The gameplay got a bit messy at times, and the encounters with spirits lacked the challenge I was anticipating.

3.The playtime was relatively short, although I believe the prequel had a similar playtime. However, considering the price tag, a slightly longer gameplay experience would have been better.

Major Improvements that I noticed over the prequel:

1.The 2D side scrolling navigation was a notable improvement over the navigation system in the prequel.

2.The main character (MC) is more engaging compared to the somewhat bland portrayal in the prequel.

3.The characters, CG art, and overall aesthetics truly stood out, which was somewhat expected given the price tag.

Despite some problems, I would still recommend the game and I rate this game 7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
