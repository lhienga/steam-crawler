\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy review will have nothing to do with the original story itself, as so far what I've read of that is fantastic. My review will focus on the disgusting translation practices, and will hopefully dissuade Shiravune from doing the same for the 2nd game which they've already announced they are picking up.

Going over the amount of dated internet slang in this translation would take hours, it's honestly insulting. And considering I saw the translator themself say "I'll just say it now, none of that was me; Any complaints about my original translation were that it was too literal, so this is the far opposite end of the spectrum." proves to me that the editors were at fault, and I think they deserve to be named for this abomination of a product they've released. Chris Melchin & Puffketeer are the 2 listed as editors for this work on vndb, and I pray to every god there is that they never get the opportunity to work on a professionally release visual novel ever again. My hope is that they were with the Sol Press team back before they lost the license to the work, and don't have any affiliation with Shiravune themselves. If I see that there is any sign that the same types of tl practices used in this release are used in the future sequel release, there is absolutely no chance I will be spending money on it, and I will actively dissuade every friend of mine who may also be tempted to do so to not support those who would butcher a work like this. It would also be much better if honorifics were brought back for the 2nd game, as there have been many instances where the charm of a scene was lost due to poor alternatives to honorifics in english. I really do want to support these releases as I've been waiting to play these games since they released in JP 5 years back, but I simply can't if this is what I'm to expect. Shiravune, I implore you to get better editors who aren't going to butcher a translators work like this. Otherwise, you will see no monetary support on my end and I will not be able to recommend your work in good faith to friends who also have the ability to do so. The fact that machine translation can produce a better result to many lines in this work than what we got in a professional release should not be something that ever happens.

EDIT: Finished the game yesterday, and my god is it an incredible work. Easily one of my favorite vns ever. I just wish it wasn't plagued with these modern lingo bad tl embarrassments all throughout it's runtime. Even in huge climaxes there were characters calling eachother boomers and millennials and such, it was genuinely insulting. Thankfully they didnt completely ruin my experience, but I can see how they could for other people and that shouldn't ever be a possibility with a vn this amazing.

Also thanks to the comment from etch, good to keep in mind that it appears Puffketeer is the main problem with regards to the editors, assuming what they said is true then they should never be allowed to edit a vn release ever again. I also recall seeing them in screenshots talking about how they actively commit malpractice and tl things just to have bad online lingo, if you look up "nukitashi tl" on google it'll be one of the first reddit links to discord screenshots of them doing so\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe title of NUKITASHI should tell you everything. It is, after all, one of them Japanese shortenings for "Nukige Mitai na Shima ni Sunderu Watashi (Small Breasts) wa Dou Surya Iii Desu ka? Is this Island Utopia or Dystopia?" or my rough translation of "Living on an Island Straight Outta a Fap Game, What am I to do? Is this Island Utopia or Dystopia?" The "small breasts" part is Japanese wordplay lost in translation. You take the perspective of Junnosuke "Virginity is Cool, Stay in School" Tachibana as he attempts to eke out a "normal life" for his sister and his principles on Seiran Island, a uniquely-shaped island brimming with the three S's of tropical paradise: Sun, Sea, and Sex. Looooooooooots of Sex. That's right. On Seiran Island as far as the eye can see, locals and tourists alike are encouraged, BY LAW, to do the hanky-panky ANYTIME. ANYWHERE. With ANYONE (with a few exceptions). As much as possible, and in ANY WAY you desire. Now I'm sure you're wondering, "How did this island or even the premise for this island even come into fruition?" Why, it was to solve an ongoing crisis taking hold of Japan, of course: Declining Birthrates!

I was sold on NUKITASHI not because it won some awards within the visual novel-sphere a few years back, not because of its ridiculous and quite literally sex-charged premise and humor (though that is a big contributing factor), but because the developers did such a bang-up job advertising this game back when it first released. Well... the second game. Basically on the official Qruppo YouTube channel, you'll find a bunch of videos labeled "ボツシナリオ" or "rejected scenarios" that were cut from the game... except some of them are a little too meta to believe they would be implemented into the game. I assume they were all repurposed for advertising if not written solely for promotion. There's all sorts of scenarios from a "master-debation" parody machine of Taiko no Tatsujin, to a discussion among the glasses-wearing characters rejecting the idea of new characters wearing glasses -- only for the new glasses-wearing characters to show up and introduce themselves, to a Smash Bros. reveal trailer complete with an "audience" reaction. And of course, you can't forget the small community circling around the game, clipping various bits of the game to showcase any potential references within it or leaving it raw for some truly out-of-context gold. Now of course, all of these videos are in Japanese, so that's probably why it sounds like I'm talking out of my Ketsuno Ana. The point I want to make is that the developers have a sense of humor and it shows. NUKITASHI. IS. FUNNY.

If you are a person who enjoys dirty humor, but more in the Shakespearean double entendre sort of manner, maybe you might not like this game. That's not to say there might not be some legitimate flowery and risqué prose, but more than likely it will be overshadowed by what this game is -- a stream of no-filter, sex-charged, horny dialogue voiced normally as if moaning and ragged breathing were the most natural human functions. (Well, as natural as putting golf balls in holes is). You'll either be laughing your ass off or double-taking at the ridiculosity of the script and scenario. Most of the time it won't even take a minute or two before you hear 'F**k', 'Sh*t', '♥♥♥♥♥cat' without the cat, '♥♥♥♥'s Sporting Goods' without the Sporting Goods, 'Come' but its spelled like a Cyberpunk 2077 meme, men and women (mostly women) moaning in pleasure, suggestive liquid noises, slang or colloquial phrases, and/or any combination of all the mentioned and their variations. By the time you're done reading NUKITASHI, you will have learned at least a few more ways to describe shoving a pipe through a hole (read: sexual intercourse). Now if you're like me and you like good voice work ( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°), dirty jokes/puns, and sex humor laid bare, then you will revel in NUKITASHI's pages of quotable dialogue. Here are some of my favorites in the approximate 6 hours I've played.

  • "God nuts in mysterious ways."
  • "Perverted sex truly will save the world...!"
  • "There's no need to worry. After all, they always say 'Nothing diminishes anxiety faster than f***ing,' right?"
  • "It's called a cocksicle. They put flavored ice into a condom, and you suck on it. Seiran Island specialty."
  • "As the class representative, I cannot overlook delinquency in my classmates. Please make sure to f**k within the school as well. There is so much p***y to go around."
  • "Huuuh!? No I cant' take this anymore this does not frickin' compute these bazongas in front of me are maxing out my happiness gauge oh no let's f***in' go I'm literally deceased aaaaaa."
  • "I swear, this entire island needs Jesus..."

And my personal favorite so far...

"MORE F***ING GARLIC BREAD! CHOP-CHOP!!!"


Regarding more technical aspects... It's a visual novel. Yeah. Despite having the word "Nukige" in the title, I've heard that NUKITASHI is only really "nukige" in setting and premise because of how many background non-important characters are screwing their brains out. If you consider the amount of H-Scenes, it rounds out closer to a regular eroge than an actual nukige. This is hearsay, of course, though I'm sure some research on the Internet can easily confirm that, spoilers beware. On the topic, as far as I'm concerned, there's no choice nor two ways about it -- the R18 patch is mandatory. It just IS the NUKITASHI experience. Now unfortunately, the patch is only officially available (for free, but requires an account) via Johren. But that's a small price to pay to see NUKITASHI finally released, in English (and a few other languages) no less, after about 5 years of business nightmares (do your taxes, kids).

This game is definitely not for everyone. In fact, I can imagine quite a lot of people being offended by this game and/or even find its attempts at humor repetitive and appalling. But I guarantee those that resonate with it -- those that find non-filtered, sex-charged, spicy and salacious humor hilarious -- will absolutely love reading it, laughing all the way. I'm only five hours in with one part of the story under my belt, but I know I'll be eager to read through the rest. And when the second game drops, you'll be sure I'll be one of the first to become a citizen of the UNITED STATES OF DOSUKEBE! As they say on Seiran Island:


「良いドスケベライフを！」or in the translation, "HAVE A PERVERTED STAAAY!"\t\t\t\t\t
