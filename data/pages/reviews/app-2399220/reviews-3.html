\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the most consistently funny AND engaging visual novels I read in a long time.

I think the people who try to sell this as a plotge are not selling it correctly. To me it's a plotge in the same vein as the agave route from Majikoi. It has its serious themes but the action is very goofy shounen and there's still all of the over-the-top comedy especially with the whole unique parodying nukige thing.

But considering that I just compared it to Agave from Majikoi, which is my favorite visual novel, obviously means I still put this visual novel in high praise.

I actually quite liked all the routes. Some people have said that Misaki route is their least favorite but honestly outside a few rushed developments, it was easily the funniest route reminding me of a Majikoi or Asa Project route with how ridiculously funny it got. Nanase, Hinami, and True route for all good in different ways, I would say Nanases route is only my least favorite for minor reasons.

I think by the end I found every single major character likable or interesting. Including the antagonists, with some who you are not supposed to like it first. (Which does go into one of the interesting themes of the VN of you eventually coming around to things you initially dislike)

Junnosuke was a decent entertaining main Character mostly brought down by him being a hypocritical douche at times.

My main girl ranking goes something like this:

Misaki - No surprise she's my favorite considering she's the very quirky hilarious girl. But she also has a nice wholesome side (in fact, I liked how every single heroine with a route are all distinctive variations of nice girls)

Fumino - A very wholesome character with surprising depth. Maybe went slightly far on her being willingly subservient.

Hinami - An amusing character with some funny if occasionally Repetitive loli/senpai jokes. Definitely the least deep character but makes up for with some of the most wholesome scenes in the whole game.

Asane - the only character I would say got slightly worse as the story went on. Her jokes got slightly more repetitive and they made her slightly bitchier than I was expecting to non-Nanase girls. That said, her funny jokes were still really funny.

Touka - She was easily the most interesting antagonist to me so I was kind of disappointed they sequel baited her depth.

Nanase - Likeable but just doesn't stick out as much as everybody else above her.

Rei - Definitely got the most love in terms of giving proper backstory and depth. Her base personality just isn't quite as good as everybody else's.

Ikuko - She's okay, but definitely my overall least favorite. Her base personality is too OneNote and her depth and development is way too rushed when it shows. (The opposite problem of Touka)

Just a little aside on the translation. I do think there were a bunch of lines where they went a little too far on the localization memes/references/political jabs but to be honest, they felt more like mild to moderate annoyances at most. I would say the complaints are kind of overblown but if it really bothers you, you can wait for that supposedly upcoming improvement patch.

I'm still stewing on my final score, but with how consistently I enjoyed just about everything about it. I would not be surprised if I settled on a 10/10. It's been forever since I've given one to a full length visual novel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing two routes, Misaki & Hinami, I can confidently say I don't recommend this game.


I'll be using "MC" instead of main character / Junnosuke. I've also censored some of the words used here since I'm not sure what the filters are like for these posts.


Good
• The music fits the silliness of the island very well, and it also has solid tracks for the serious parts of the story.
• Although there's not many, the sex scenes themselves are solid and a welcome contrast to the constant over-the-top sex voice-over from the generic characters.


Mixed
• The humor
• The writing

Most of the ridiculous quips & one-liners from the main cast had me chuckling or outright laughing. That being said, there's definitely a significant amount of lines that were odd A lot of the vocabulary used for the quips & insults in the story are the sort of things you'd expect to find on forums like 4chan. Some examples for oddities & immersion-breaking include the following:


There's casual and quite liberal usage of "fa*", "fa**ot", and variations of the latter even though it doesn't seem to match what's being said in Japanese. In addition, there's inconsistency in the usage of it. There's many instances of "virginfa*" for example, but instead of "normalfa*", the word used is "normie".


In terms of the writing, there's a lot of funny and over-the-top situations that make reading through it enjoyable. That being said, there's also some very immersion-breaking scenes in the story which spoil the experience.


There's a scene in Hinami's route where Rei explicitly compares the MC's impotency & his admission to FS despite the fact as affirmative action. Shortly thereafter in the following scenes Rei directs insults at the MC with xenophobic undertones, and yet somehow after, Rei shows sympathy for the MC's impotency. Even though there's an explanation a very long time afterwards, scenes like this felt very immersion-breaking.


Bad
• The values the MC claims to have, in comparison to his actual values.
• The MC's unwillingness to reject his biases.
• The MC's actions in relation to his values.
• The way the story simply hand-waves issues or cognitive dissonance regarding the MC's actions and values.

I'm going to freely include spoilers in this section, since it greatly helps explain why I have issues with the above.


From the get-go, the MC makes it clear that he doesn't like the law of the island. He doesn't like the fact that he & his sister will be forced to have sex against their wills, and wants to change the law in order to prevent that. The problem presents itself when it becomes clear that not only does the MC want to prevent people from being forced to have sex, but rather wants to impose his arbitrary, restrictive views of sexual openness onto the people as well. Even though not explicitly stated, he seems to be aiming for a scenario in which most people act prudish, and abstain from sex unless they've, according to him, already been in a relationship for about 2 years.


• In Misaki's route, Misaki is admonished simply for saying the word "sex".

• Throughout the story, the MC continuously calls Nanase a slut at every chance he gets because of her perceived sexual openness.


The MC relentlessly pursues an "Us vs. Them" mentality, and is unwilling to co-operate or understand the non-heroine cast unless forced to for the sake of advancing the plot.


• Throughout the story, the MC insults the generic characters who are willingly engaging in sex, and always categorizes any of their positive emotions as a result of "brain rot" or being "brainwashed".

• Even when members of the "evil" rival faction, FS, show that they're willing to act of their own accord and not just blindly follow rules, the MC simply dismisses it, and immediately goes back to harping on about how he can never trust them and that they're evil.


To further exacerbate the issue of his values, the MC himself actually acts contradictory to them many times.


• In Misaki's route, the MC willingly has sex with what seems like the entire island when fighting his way through the final section of that route.

• In both Misaki & Hinami's route, the MC volunteers to have sex with the respective heroine in order to help them get out of bad situations.

• The MC has many run-ins with the enemy factions, and willingly has sex with them in order to help keep his team out of trouble.


Finally, when it comes to actually engaging the MC's contradictory actions & values, the story takes an awful approach. Rather than use the scenarios in order to promote meaningful growth, most contradictions are simply ignored or hand-waved. It would actually be better if the characters weren't self-aware, rather than bring up inconsistencies and then proceed to ignore them.


• Even after saving Nanase from being sexually assaulted, the MC makes absolutely no change in how relentless he is in calling her a slut. Despite the fact that Nanase being fearful of someone's sexual advances directly contradicts the stories about her having sex with anyone that asked.

• In Hinami's route, rather than acknowledge that he & the FS actually share common goals and it's feasible that they can work together, the MC dismisses any idea that they're not "evil". This despite having already seen FS members as "normal people". Furthermore, rather than attempt to build genuine rapport with Rei for his cause, the MC instead chooses to ignore all the positive signs he's seen from her and betray her. The story then deems it fit to shoehorn in a tragic backstory for Rei which the MC then uses when emotionally blackmailing her. All of this in order to reach the same outcome that could've been reached by simply attempting to work together from the get-go, which the MC himself on multiple occasions had in his mind.

• In Misaki's route, after admonishing Misaki on her sexual openness so much that she starts becoming prudish, the MC ends up having sex with her only 4 days after having started dating her. This is a stark contrast to the MC's own established 2 year rule, and he himself admits this. Despite this, there's absolutely no initiative taken to update his values to reflect how he's living. Instead, the admonishment of Misaki's sexual openness is kept and the MC alone is allowed to break his own restrictive rules and simply "oops" them away.

• In Hinami's route, in a Main Menu-only scene, the MC has sex with both Rei & Hinami. Despite being a swinger in that scene, there's zero attention given to how it affects the law of the island or the MC's view of other people's sexual openness.


To give a real-life comparison, given the amount of politics involved in the story, the MC's actions are reminiscent of someone who would decry abortion as unconditionally evil. After being presented with a scenario in which they have to get an abortion though, they'll be quick to bring up excuses to justify why their specific case is different and is acceptable. Afterwards, they go right back to harping on about how abortion is unconditionally evil, while conveniently ignoring the fact that they've acted in direct opposition to their values.


After skipping through the routes for Nanase & Fumino, I did notice that in Fumino's epilogue there's still sex being had on the island. Since I didn't actually read through it I don't know if there was a real compromise achieved between the island's original forced sex law, and the MC's restrictiveness on sexual openness. Unfortunately, it makes no sense that the game expect me to play through all four routes completely before giving a conclusion regarding the law change that actually makes sense, rather than somehow everyone being OK with going from non-stop sex everywhere to essentially having no sex at all.\t\t\t\t\t
