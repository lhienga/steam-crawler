\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYea, the doormat route of the game feels like how my life would have gone if my Mom had given me the "Just be a doormat for everyone" advice instead of just dropping a book in my lap of "how to make friends".

Nice part about the irl route of her just dropping the book in my lap... I wasn't suddenly compulsed to overwork myself to make friends with everyone like Nicole was. It was just a "Huh... thanks mom." Read it over, basically became better friends with my equivalent of Jecka. It went well until my Jecka equivalent was hit by a semi on the highway and ripped off their head. I wish that was just a dark joke, still stings. Anyway, it was right before graduation, and college was a lot better for me than my Jecka. They'd appreciate that dark joke though.

Game did an excellent job reminding me why I became a feminist in college though (and not the fake kind like the principle.) Lots of dudes are actually literally just as bad as presented in here. Shouldn't have had to deal with toxic BS as a kid, those still kids shouldn't have to either.

Game's good for girls who want to revisit their high school traumas and remind them why they learned certain lessons (or learn lessons they should have learned) or for guys who need a clue on why girls keep rejecting them and maybe get a clue on how to avoid being douches. Non-binary & intersexes? I guess you can enjoy it just to laugh at the messed-up binary world. Sure, you've got your own problems, but at least you don't have all of these problems... unless you did, in which case I guess that double-sucks, you got my sympathy.\t\t\t\t\t
