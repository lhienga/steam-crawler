\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very good game that, through hyperbole, represents high school as if it was ridiculously lawless with no moral considerations. Everything is over the top, but that's what makes this game interesting. The fact that the MC, Nicole, is a sociopath after her forlorn past had me empathize with her. I don't blame her for acting the way she does after everything that happened. I understand why so many people who played this game mention PTSD. Perhaps a lot of people have similar experiences; constantly moving from one school to another, constantly restarting, always the new kid.

On the other hand, the comedy in this game is amazing. Every character is unlikable, but that's what hooks people. If I received a dollar for every suicide, drug, r*pe, and pedo joke, I'd have between 30-100 dollars. Seriously, this game doesn't hold back, there is no filter and I love it. The only thing I worry about is when people take this game seriously. You're okay to disagree with these characters, but that's just the way they see the world. Nicole, for example, sees all men as rapists and pedophiles, and from her experience in the game, that is completely justified. But that doesn't mean that all men are rapists and pedophiles! This game was made by a man! Ultimately, the comedy is top notch and each ending ranged from hilarious to straight up depressing.

I highly recommend everyone to empty their wallet and play this game, then go outside and touch this green thing called grass.\t\t\t\t\t

This is a meme game, in case it isn't abundantly obvious, so that's where all of the Overwhelmingly Positive ratings are coming from. A lot of the humor is "edge-y" and crass, but the narrative commits to it so hard that it rockets into the cringe floor and breaks through to the other side, where manic absurdity lives and thrives.

It shouldn't work, and honestly if the game went on for much longer than it does then it wouldn't work. Instead, this game ends up being pretty hilarious and painfully accurate.

The only actual criticism I have is that the tone of many of the endings just doesn't vibe with the rest of the game. The story needed to pick a lane and stay in it.

Not sure I'll be visiting the sequel anytime soon, though.\t\t\t\t\t
