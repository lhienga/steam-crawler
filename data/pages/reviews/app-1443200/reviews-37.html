\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, so... my first time through the game, I got an ending that kinda unnerved me, because I wasn't expecting the game to go as far as it did. Then on the second and third times through the game, I got even worse endings, and I began to suspect there weren't any good endings to be had. And that would have really sucked....

It doesn't take long to get through the game, especially since you can Skip the intro after the first time, and can click through the scenes really fast if you want, especially those you've seen before. You can also save at any point in the game, so saving on a screen where you have to make a choice allows you to jump right back there and select a different choice, allowing you to quickly get to different endings.

I did finally manage to get some decent endings, and I appreciated that. It would have been a bummer to only have bad endings. Although the game is referred to as a rejection sim, manipulation is often a better tactic in the game than outright rejection.

There were a few places where I felt the story veered into a direction not well-aligned with choices I made earlier in the game, and in some places it felt like some choices should have been offered but weren't. But overall I enjoyed the game. The voice acting was great.

Lastly, this is my first time writing a review of a game on Steam. This game struck that kind of emotional response in me, to urge me to write a review. That in itself should say something. I give it 8/10, deducting one point for the items mentioned above about choice selections and the lack thereof, and one point because I'd have liked for each run-through of the game to have been 2 to 3 times longer. Was the price fair for what I got from the game? Put it this way... I'm not unhappy that I bought it, so it was worth the price for me.\t\t\t\t\t
