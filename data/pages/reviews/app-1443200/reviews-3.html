\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUsually, I am not a fan of visual novels. To me, they're boring most of the time, and the only real fun thing about is that you make choices and READ. There are a few of visual novels I like, such as Danganronpa (but that game had other gameplay elements to it which made it funny), and Doki Doki Literature Club which was of course genre defying. This game is one of those genre defying games, quite literally since it advertises itself as the ''anti-visual novel''. The game is basically that too, since you're not some quirky dork who somehow manages to get some sweet sexy, sexy, anime girls out of nowhere, but an anime girl who can basically get with anyone she likes, but chooses to cause chaos instead. Anyway, let's just get into the review.

Gameplay

The gameplay is pretty simple, even more so than most visual novels. You don't have to click to progress the dialogues since the game already does that for you, you don't have to read that much either, since the game has voice acting. The true gameplay of the game is making choices which lead you to get different endings in the game. Simple, and straight forward.

Characters

The characters in this game are fun. We have our protagonist Nicole, unlike most protagonists you might see, she's incredibly sociopathic, and a lot of the times; if you wanna play this game, forget your morals. You don't get a choice to be ''nice'' in this game, and the nicest choices you do have is usually Nicole being manipulative, or it's the least bad decision you can make with her. There's Jecka, who's Nicole's best friend. And of course side characters like Jeffery, who's a weeb, Kylar who's a generic stupid jock, and of course the pedophile counselor. The characters in themselves can be triggering if you're sensitive to certain topics, like a 1-3 characters are pedophiles, 1 is a rapist, while the other is a White Nationalist. Which to understandably to some, might be triggering, and might make some uncomfortable. However, for those who aren't they'll probably find these characters funny.

Comedy

First, and foremost: if you are NOT into crass-humor or are sensitive to certain topics being joked about or treated ''lightly'' ( I.E. rape, murder, pedophilia, necrophilia, suicide, and other dark topics ) than this game is not for you. The game's whole premise is it's humor and comedy, and if it's something you can't handle, I recommend not buying or playing this game. Since it's not one of those things you could ignore, since again, the point of the game is it's comedy.

That being said, I don't mind the crass humor that much. At worst it gets repetitive after a while, but I enjoy it. I like how the game feels like it has certain levels of realism to it as well. A lot of the jokes made in this game, could be made by real people in real life (though of course the game exaggerates it a little). The way Nicole and Jecka converse throughout the game is something you could see real teenage girls doing. The game's humor reminds me of South Park a little (though, not as indiscriminate and ''apolitical'' as South Park when it comes to it jokes' since there's certain political jokes made in the game that sort of tells you the political alignment of the creators unlike South Park which mocks everyone) with how offensive it is and how it mocks almost everything.

Conclusion

If you're not sensitive to dark topics, or dark topics being joked about and have a liking for crass humor, this game is for you. Since this game holds no punches when it comes to it's jokes and tone. It's a fun game to try, and will be a great breathe of fresh air for those just tired of how stupid some visual novels tend to be.
