\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is lacking substance and any sort of depth. Look, I am totally cool with a 2 hour story if that story is impactful and well fleshed out. Class of 09 just isn't that. It is hardly a story at all. Just brief interactions that lead to endings. That is all it is. 90% of the 2 hour playtime is just going back and choosing a different option to get a different ending. It takes about 10 minutes to achieve each ending. Characters don't get fleshed out and there is no plot at all. Yes the themes are edgy and dark but it's hard to feel sad when there is no emotional attachment to the protagonist.

I want to end this with a comparison to another game that I recently played called The Coffin of Andy and Leyley. I feel tcoaal is a similar game in how it dialogue heavy, features terrible people as protagonists, and very dark themes and humor. The difference is, tcoaal hits the mark with flying colors and ends up being a far more memorable experience, and that game is not even complete. It has a playtime of around 4 hours, 2 for part 1 and 2 for part 2, and in that time the game manages to tell a far richer and impactful story, where choices lead you down different story branches that actually matter, and not just to hamfisted endings.\t\t\t\t\t
