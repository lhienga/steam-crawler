\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClass of 09 review:

Warning: There will be spoilers.
Pros:
1. Female main character who is evil, on purpose, at least in most routes. Those are rare. What’s more, this one is plausible. Nicolle may be the best visual novel protagonist I have ever seen.
2. Great artwork.
3. Great dialogue. For instance, a white nationalist photography teacher explaining why its so easy to recruit his students to his organization says “Most art girls are terrified of black people. Not that there is anything wrong with that.” Nicolle responds, “You thought that I was a quirky art girl?”
4. The approach to sexual harassment and sexual assault. I have seen treatments about discreet instances of sexual harassment in fiction, like Marge Simpson being fired for being married. I have seen lots of fiction about rape, most of it is porn. But this is the first treatment of sexual harassment that shows it as endemic, because most of the male adults in this story are ephebophiles. It is also the first treatment of rape that shows it as a constant background fear in a young woman’s life, to the point that it doesn’t need to happen to her for it to shape who she is.

Cons:
1.In a story that prides itself on accuracy and claims to have been based on real events and personalities, it strains credibility in some places, like the ending where Nicolle gets “statewide expulsion.” I don’t think that that is a thing in America. Then there is the ending where Jeffery ends up pumping gas despite graduating valedictorian because he didn’t get into college, because has no extra curricular activities, because his fellow students wouldn’t let him into any clubs. Most universities don’t require extra curricular activities to get in. Also, an extracurricular activity doesn’t need to be done with one’s fellow students to count when applying to university. Jeffery does have an extra curricular activity. He runs a Youtube channel. So, this story doesn’t add up. Its possible that Jeffery just really messed up his academic career by only applying to selective schools and not mentioning anything he does besides schoolwork on his application. But there isn't anything about his character that indicates that he would mess up that badly and just give up, because again, high school valedictorian in that route. More importantly, this isn’t Jeffery’s story. It’s Nicolle’s story. Jeffery’s downfall has nothing to do with her. So, it doesn’t belong here. Maybe its supposed to illustrate that if nobody likes you, then they will treat you accordingly and your life will suck, regardless of how smart you are. That is true. But if I acted the way any of these characters do, then nobody would like me. So they should all end up pumping gas if that is the case. None are without sin. There is also Jefferey getting lynched, seemingly just for liking anime. I was homeschooled, so maybe it was different for other kids. But I remember anime being relatively mainstream among young people back then. So I can't believe that someone would get lynched just for liking anime. Finally, a human being, principal Lynn, saying out loud “We protest for fashion, not for change.” I have never seen anyone admit that their politics is just for show.
2. There are some inconsistencies in the writing. In one ending, Nicolle’s mom can’t afford a lawyer. In another ending, she has a lawyer good enough to sue the school successfully. It’s not clear how those things can both be true.
3. to get one ending, the one where you get the principal fired, you need to choose the option “yes, on one condition” when it comes up, without knowing what the “one condition” is. Even if you knew what that condition is, destroying the dark room, you can only know to pick that if you have ready played through the game before and know that Jeffery is going to take a picture of you undressing and try to develop it in the school, or using a walkthrough. I really shouldn’t have to rely on knowledge from other playthroughs or blind guessing to get any singular ending in a visual novel.
4. No option to take revenge on Jeffery for taking a picture of you undressing. What is the point of an openly unsympathetic main character if not revenge?

5. There are multiple points where the characters reference stuff that we the audience never get to see, like Nicole getting stalked by a pizza deliveryman, or Jeffery getting hanged, or Kyler raping another guy. That puts distance between the characters and the audience. Good fiction does the opposite. Good fiction brings the characters and the audience closer together.


Final rating: 7/10. Its not perfect. But the only other thing that fills this niche that I know if is class of 09 re-up, which won't make any sense if you don't play the original class of 09. So I recommend it to anyone who likes visual novels and has a high tolerance for bad people and bad things happening to those bad people, for reasons that may or may not be their fault, in fiction.\t\t\t\t\t
