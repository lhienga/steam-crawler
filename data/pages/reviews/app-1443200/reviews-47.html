Hard Pass
TLDR: Imagine an American public school filled with all the terrible and atrocious types of situations, tropes, backgrounds, and behaviors you can think of, and that is the theme of this game - it feels like writer took all of these only to see what would happen.

I have never played a dating sim before and this seemed edgy and ironic, so I thought it would be worth a try. I completed every ending in hope that it would eventually redeem itself, but it fell hard. The highs were meh and the lows were real low.

The characters are typical tropes in public high school during the mid-2000's, and probably other or every generation(s) but I only have the one experience for context; you have the jock, the stoner, the person no one likes, the people that make fun of everyone else, the creepy teachers, and the guys only see women as objects. The main character has had it rough and, for the most part, I empathize with all of the students. I get it, life sucks and people treat you like trash and I have been where some of the characters have, but everyone is completely unbearable and the endings are more or less the same; pedophilia, white nationalism, mass murder, suicide, and SO much sexual assault and harassment. There was no decent story or character to be found, the story didn't feel like it was going anywhere other than just surviving until the end, and maybe that was the point or its edge.

Maybe it's bias because I hated high school and relate to the main character, and I don't like seeing people continue to make terrible decisions. It was interesting the first play through (it took like 20 minutes) with the edgy humor and commentary of the dirty laundry of public school, but I wanted to see what else the game had to offer, and after a few hours of being bombarded with sexual assault commentary and all of the abuse between the characters, it just felt exhausting. There is no substance, resolution, meaning, or regard for those around them, and maybe half of the paths were completely meaningless (I did take each one); I can't even call this satire or comedy, it's just sad.\t\t\t\t\t
