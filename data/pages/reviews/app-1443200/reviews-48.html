\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is very hard for me to review. In the description, it says, "The choices you make reveal your personality, not ours." That's not quite right. The game is meant to be completed - after all, like a lot of visual novels, getting every ending unlocks a true end. But if someone tells you in detail their reaction to this game, I guarantee you that they would end up telling you more about their lived experiences than about the game itself.

The production values are top notch. Even if you end up not liking the story very much, the voice acting is top notch for an English VN - indeed, for a video game period. A lot of the writing is, tonally, like a more serious South Park - the meme "its the Boondocks for white woman [people]" doesn't seem to far off. I think whether or not Class of '09's writing lands with you will end up depending on your lived experiences.

Thematically, its hard for me to talk about Class of '09, because - as I implied earlier - I end up feeling like I'm looking at the game from my perspective. I think this is because, pretty undeniably, relationship dynamics between young men and women are a big part of Class of '09, and its hard to get out of your own bias's on that topic.

Overall, I very strongly recommend the Class of '09. It'll probably end up somewhere on my top 10 favorite VNs, and I suspect it'll stay there for quite a while.
