\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI graduated in 2007 and this really captures the "middle class white suburban kids high school" atmosphere of the era pretty well. Everything is very quippy and sardonic and abrasive, which was the cool way to be and pretty much exactly how we spoke (and honestly still probably speak to some extent). The situations and characters all seem hyperbolic at a glance but then I look back and one of my teachers was probably a nazi and two more were fired for inappropriate contact with students. Also the jock dudes were damn near actually literally this bad to just be around, it seems like a joke but if my memory serves me well it actually isn't. I can't imagine the minefield that being that age is now, ♥♥♥♥ that.

As far as being a graphic novel, I don't think this is very good. It has very little overall content and becomes increasingly annoying with subsequent runs to see the "endings" which are the only real goals. Each run takes like 15-20min unless you skip a bunch of text which you'll end up doing because it starts getting extremely repetitive. Also I felt like some of the branches of the story flow oddly, and like I was funneled into a specific path at the end that I'd already seen that didn't feel like it was really in line with my very different choices this time. But honestly who cares, the point of this isn't to be an amazing graphic novel from a technical perspective. Buy it and spend a couple of hours with a ♥♥♥♥ eating grin on your face, there's your $10.

Also based on the reviews I was expecting Nicole to be more, evil? I guess? Don't get me wrong, she's a turbo ♥♥♥♥♥, but everyone in this thing is awful so I suppose that dampens the impact of her actions a bit?\t\t\t\t\t
