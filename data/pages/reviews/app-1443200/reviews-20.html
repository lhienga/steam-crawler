\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game... WITH SOME CAVEATS. With all the glowing reviews you may be about to jump right in, but if you scroll through the reviews you'lll find a little bit of "I bought this game due to shining reviews and ultimately felt tricked" as well. So despite giving a thumbs up, I feel a few notes are warranted here. So, I'm going to begin my review with the caveats, and then roll into the review.

*First caveat, get it on sale. It is not worth ten bucks. I bought it on sale for $6, and feel $4-6 would be a fair price range. Maybe if you're just dying to ogle an animated 16 yo girl in her underwear, but even given the pretty solid dark humor, this is just too short and not deep enough (more details on that one below) to warrant paying a full 10. Even though I'm trying to hit all the endings and I have hit about 2/3 of them, I've only logged 3 hours.. and a certain portion of that is just skipping my way through dialogue I've already been through, trying to take the alternate paths. Each alternate path you can take in the story is VERY short.

*The story just isn't that engaging. Like even though this is primarily a humor game, I just don't find myself connecting with the characters or getting 'into' the story. When a character dies, I feel nothing. When one succeeds, I feel nothing. I laugh at the funny parts (which, granted, is most of it) and that's it. Even in the 'funny' story games I've played, often there's at least an ATTEMPT to get you attached to the characters.

*The constant depiction of underage females as s-x objects (I really don't know if Steam is going to flag this review if I don't edit LOL) makes me a little uncomfortable. And I GET it - again, it's all about humor, it's all about really trying to capture that feeling of being in high school, and it's all animated and the girls are drawn in an adult fashion rather than a kind of 'kid' fashion, you as the player know their ages. It doesn't reach a horrifying level, but it may occasionally strike you, 'this girl is supposed to be 16.'

*The animations are NOT in line with the trailer. This is not a moving animation style game. These are still images moving around the screen. I don't think this is the biggest deal in the world, but the trailer was a little misleading.

*...No Steam achievements? Really? Come on.

So why do I recommend? Because at the end of the day, it IS pretty freaking funny. Things escalate from zero to [self-termination] in a heartbeat. You as the player will be given choices (such as the one featured in the teaser pics between going to a concert and self-terminating) and the story will very often escalate at a break-neck pace into full absurdity. The characters are absolutely crazy, and it wasn't lost on me that LITERALLY EVER ONE of the school staff has some kind of major issue. Half of them are ped0s, (Or hebe's, for those of you who draw a distinction.)

The choices are also often absolutely crazy, as evidenced by the one mentioned above. You'll often find yourself being given a choice between a bad thing and a worse thing, sometimes neither of which you want to do. You quickly abandon all sense of morality and go on a wicked streak.

As a big fan of dark humor, this game has a lot of it You can watch the trailer and quickly realize this game is absolutely willing to 'go there.' I enjoy a game with plenty of dark humor.

So overall, if you're expecting a solid story with engaging characters, this isn't the one. If you just kinda want to have a little fun and enjoy some dark humor, this is it. Just get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was sold on this game due to the animated short. I thought the writing was whip smart and the voice acting superb, so I really wanted to check it out.

What I didn't expect was the game to actually kind of be a bit a thought provoking critique on the patriarchy.

I'm not saying this game is Citizen Kane or the The Second Sex, and it isn't trying to be. There's a lot of routes in the game that are just ridiculous farce for the sake of ridiculous farce, all dressed in the Mean-Girls-on-Steroids late 2000's american aesthetic and vibes.

In the context of it being a visual novel drawn in the style of anime and manga, I thought it was really cool how the female characters are depicted as Subjects rather than Objects. All the female characters are very grounded and well-drawn compared to the male counterparts, and the protag aggressively call out pedophilic and rape culture multiple times (almost to make sure that no matter what route you choose, you're almost always likely to see it). Sure, the situations are often over the top, but within the context of the game being styled like anime/manga, a genre that's pretty well known for it's objectification of women, it was extremely cathartic for me. There's also some critiques of racism in the game, which I can't speak to how well it handles, but I think it's also pretty cutting considering, again, the kind of demographics anime tends to attract, and being very good at pointing out that often people join these types of hate groups out of loneliness and a sense of community (which is still bad, obviously).

I like how the game forces you in Nicole shoes. She's not a role model, she's selfish and pretty much every choice you make will always involve her either trying to get the upper hand, or lamenting over how she's being too much of a doormat for people she isn't invested in. What's interesting is her perspective on the world is never challenged or proven wrong, and it's a very interesting look into a female, albeit a very femcel-y and doomerish, perspective. I was always pleasantly surprised at how sympathetic the game was to Nicole's problems, and even though she has an extreme worldview, the game makes clear why she is how she is due to her home life and the sexist culture that's ground her down over the years.

While I imagine most people playing this were closer to Jeffrey than Nicole on the school social ladder, at least for me, playing as Nicole and watching her roast people and say whatever was on her mind was extremely cathartic and left my jaw agape between laughter.

If you want a ludicrously funny game with some hits of social commentary here and there, definitely give this one a look.
