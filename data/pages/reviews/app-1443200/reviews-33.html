\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is honestly a crazy experience to put into words if I am being honest, but I will try.

Now, to start off this review I wouldn't recommend this to everybody. If you are cool with having no morals for a bit and constantly wondering why everybody is so horrible, then I would recommend this game. If you can't stand very morally grey characters or just morally evil characters, I wouldn't recommend this to you. Now I will go into the actual game itself (contains spoilers of what's in the game and I would recommend going in blind just like I did).

First of all, I love how literally everybody is a horrible person in their own way. No one is portrayed as a good person which is honestly a shock, especially with Jeffery. Usually people who are bullied are usually portrayed as completely innocent, which is fair as someone who got bullied for literally existing, but it's gotten to the point where the audience thinks that all people who have been bullied are complete angels which isn't always true. In Jeffery's case, you feel bad for him because he gets bullied but then you revoke that pity when he takes pictures of Nicole in her undergarments to sell to creeps who like her. Here he is portrayed as a horrible person because what good person would do something like that, even to someone you hate?? It's not something I see portrayed a lot unless it's a bully's backstory. I would make an analysis to more characters, but this section is getting long so I'll move onto more things I like which is the dialogue.

The dialogue is honestly pretty accurate to how high school kids talk like, but more satirical. I swear I could hear the kids at my high school say some of the things that these kids say which is honestly a breath of fresh air from the bad portrayals of teens in the media. Also, the dialogue is super quotable and funny which is a plus.

Lastly, I love the choices and how there are multiple endings. With the choices, there isn't a "good choice" in this game. It's always "uhhh, which one is less horrible?", except you still pick a horrible choice. I remember first playing and my first ending was when Nicole hung herself and I had to take a minute thinking "what the heck??". It's an interesting experience trying to navigate through this game trying to be morally good when the character your playing is an actual sociopath. Now for the multiple endings, the game said it best. It really goes into Nicole's thinking and it's introspective to why and how she thinks the way she thinks.

Anyways, I need to end this before it gets longer than it already is. Long and short of it is, I really like this game and how it depicts people who grew up in a horrible environment being raised in horrible environments. I'd recommend this game even if I don't think it's for everybody.\t\t\t\t\t
