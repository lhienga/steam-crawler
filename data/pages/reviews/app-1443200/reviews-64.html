\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti remember high school like it was yesterday. probably because i just graduated some months ago.

i was never able to find my people. having transferred after freshman year and having to spend my sophomore year online, by the time i actually went to classes full-time, i struggled to make friends. everyone already had their people, and the one or two girls i related to all transferred months later. i remember grade school, when i wanted this picturesque, coming of age fantasy that ended in my long-term friends coming up to hug me during graduation. i didn't have that. i didn't even go to prom; what was the point if there was nobody to go with?

i feel like class of '09, in a weird way, helps me heal my broken down inner child. it shows the hilariously bad and the sickeningly ugly sides of high school and the traumatic experiences of a teenage girl. nicole put it best when she conveyed her experiences with chronic depression: you can laugh, you can have a good time, but after it fades, you still wanna die. it's rare for me to find a game that gets me to laugh AND cry, and it's even more shocking that i was so absorbed, i played through every single ending in the span of nearly 4 hours and one session.

i, too, wonder what life would be like if i swapped places with the most popular girl in my school. i, too, wonder what life would be like if i ever stepped out of my adolescent shell.

i guess i'll never know.\t\t\t\t\t
