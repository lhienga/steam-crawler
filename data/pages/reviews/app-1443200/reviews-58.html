\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis really caught me by surprise. Beneath all the satirical humor, the game depicts a competent story with a cast of characters so flawed and often times vile that in contrast, it humanizes the game's sociopathic protagonist, Nicole. As an unreliable narrator who portrays her world in a negative light, she is both an irredeemably awful manipulator and a victim of circumstances you can sympathize with, which makes her an intriguing protagonist to observe. She is not someone to be idolized, but a cautionary tale of humanity's worst, which goes for both her actions and those of others.

The writing and voice lines are some of the strongest I've ever experienced, and a shining example of what well-spoken English dialogue can sound like. It hits the right amount of swearing involved without being unnecessarily immature, and despite all of its NSFW mentions, it maintains a degree of professionalism by largely staying away from depicting these and leaving it up to the readers' imagination.

The game does an excellent job at being authentic with well-written natural dialogue and notably, by not incorporating any meta elements or fourth-wall breaks. It manages to stick to a consistent tone of dark humor as promised without jumping the shark, ensuring there is sufficient build-up for some of its more outlandish scenarios such that they remain believable enough. Almost every given choice is meaningful with stark differences in how each event plays out, even if some lead you to identical endings, and while this can nudge Nicole towards new paths, the story remains entertaining as she stays in character with little change to her established morality, or lack thereof.

10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
