\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game because it does a lot of interesting things but it just doesn't stick the landing most of the time. It's way over the top, it's dark, it's depraved, and then it also seems completely flippant with the themes it's trying to address. I just didn't get anything from it. I chuckled a couple of times, but the humor also didn't hit most of the time, so there isn't even that to keep you in.

Pros:
-Voice acting. By far the best part of the game.
-Dialogue is snappy and well-written.
-Jecka and Nicole are a great duo, and very interesting characters. Very unique for a VN.
-How they handled sexual assault. It really gives you perspective on what life might be like for a girl like Nicole, and it isn't always unrealistic either. The message comes across pretty well, at times. The social commentary in general is pretty great; it brings up a lot of good points.
-The final cutscene when you get all of the endings. It's good, and it makes Nicole seem sympathetic while maintaining the status quo. It's like what the game should have been like for all of the endings, except you only get one after suffering through the other ones.

Cons:
-Game Length. I think this deserves 5 bucks for how short it is.
-Dialogue. Yes, it's decent, but it's so one note I spent most of the game rolling my eyes.
-Characters. NONE OF THEM ARE LIKEABLE. None of the side characters even get enough screen time (before being murdered or arrested) so it doesn't even matter. And most importantly: Nicole needs Jecka. She's probably the only half-likeable character in the game, so I want to watch their banter. I thought that was the best part of the dialogue, by far. Watching Nicole absolutely destroy someone's life and then move on like nothing happened or listening to her joke about killing herself or getting molested over and over and over again gets old, really fast. They really needed to give her some more depth in general. And don't get me wrong, I do feel sympathy for her because she lives in a messed up world and she doesn't deserve some of the ♥♥♥♥ she gets. But the game doesn't communicate its ideas well enough; she's just an obnoxious wench all the way till she gets killed or something. I'm engaging with the game because I want to have a good time (or feel something), and she's all negative nancy and shock value. None of her actions throughout the story really mean anything, and it makes you not want to connect with her at all. Which isn't cool because that's kinda the whole point of a visual novel, I think. Wow, subversive art! So deep!
-The endings. I think this is the worst part. They are so perfectly fitting for someone like Nicole, but they are dark and edgy for no reason and they barely resolve anything. Most of them go like this: "I went on a date with this dude. Anyway, he murdered and raped me hahaha I guess i'll see my dad in hell" or "I caused a guy to kill himself for me. Lmao! I'm gonna go become a vlogger." Sometimes it's such an extreme curveball that you didn't see coming and you're just supposed to laugh at the absurdity of it all, but every single one is too serious to really be funny. So you're just left wondering why you even bothered going that route in the first place.
-Plot contrivances. Actually GETTING the endings is annoying because most of the game goes through the same exact scenes regardless of what choices you make, to the point that the story can get a bit confused. In one plotline, Jecka and Nicole are friends. In the next scenes, Jecka gives her an ultimatum, then it never gets brought up again. In one scene, Jeffrey does something pretty mean to Nicole, then you don't get to act on it and it just disappears until later on (where basically nothing happens). In one scene, you absolutely dunk on Crispin and in the next he's hanging out with you at your house. That whole plot point in general makes no sense; why does Nicole bother hanging out with people she doesn't like? And on top of that you have to skip through dialogue a ton to find any of the triggers for the routes, which are often completely unexpected; you might think you're choosing a negative option, but it turns out positive. I want to feel like I'm making a choice, but instead I get to skip through tons and tons of dialogue I've already seen in order to maybe get the next ending (oh hooray! everyone died again!).


This game is a viral clip generator and that's about it. Substance is more or less thrown out the window in favor of over-the-top edginess. It makes the game unfun to play. Just watch some clips on youtube before you decide to buy. I can't wait to go overdose on percocet so I can forget I ever played this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing the Re-up and the original game I wanted to make a proper analysis review of it someday. Although, there's probably way too many things to cover. So, I just decided to write some brief general thoughts over here on steam reviews.

So, if you do have the time to listen to me whine about nothing and everything all at once feel more than free to continue reading.

The experience that I had with this game at first exposure was mostly negative, I disliked a lot of the characters (I still do for a lot of the cast) and I still have some problems with the writing, but overall I feel like this works perfectly well for a parody of early 2000's American culture.

It's a strange feeling, I'm not from the US myself, but it does make me nostalgic for the most part in relation to a lot of the events that got covered in the news back in the day along with a lot of the movies and music from back then. Mostly because this game has a lot of easter eggs about it too if you look at it closely on the background.

A lot of the subjects tackled on it hit like tidal waves that rip right through you. A lot of the underlying themes and stories are exaggerated to some degree, but never stray too far from reality. Which I do respect it.

However, it does make a lot of the humor on it be kind hit or miss for me. I feel like it handles heavy handed humor well for the most part, but it does tend to get too real at points for me to really laugh at some of the jokes. That is mostly likely intentional, but it does ruin the moment when the game is trying to balance being funny while keeping up it's edgy teenager facade.

Although, I'm still debating if that's a bad thing or not since High School humor tends to do that to you sometimes.

Regardless, I put my trust in this, and I pushed as far as I can go and for all this I ended up having this odd attachment to this game along with it's main character Nicole. It is clear from the get-go that she's not supposed to be someone you root for, but part of me can't help but find some catharsis in the little moments of realization and very rare sparks of human decency she displays at points. At least in her own way.

This is something that is a lot more prevalent I feel on Re-up since (At least from my vision) they make the characters like Nicole, Jecka and Ari feel way more humane, as well. Which is good, since a lot of the cast can feel one-dimensional at points.

Overall, solid visual novel for what its initial proposal is. It feels less like a parody on dating sims and more a commentary on a lot of the troubles that come with growing up during your teenage years along more heavy subjects like depression, suicide, and oppressive school culture. It has a lot of things that can hit you hard, but it doesn't try too hard to take itself seriously.
