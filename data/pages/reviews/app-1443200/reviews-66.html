\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games that is really hard to review. One of those games that completely goes against what you expected, but doesn't at the same time? I don't know, it's hard to put into words how this game makes me feel, but I still feel compelled to give it my best shot.

I'm sure that many of the people on this store page found it the same way I did. I found out about it through Lyle Rath, who I found out about through OneyPlays. With that basic association in mind, I just assumed this game was going to be goofy fun, with a signature dose of dark comedy in there. And while yes, there are a lot of funny moments in the game, we've both had the out of context scenes show up in our YouTube recommendations, but there are a lot of moments that just aren't... and they nail it. But honestly, I wasn't really ready for that. This game tackles a lot of things, self harm, racism, abuse of all kinds, but it doesn't handle them the same way everything else does. It's not a empty virtue signalling "these are bad, don't do them!" kind of game, but it's not just irreverent shock content either, these are stories and jokes with weight, you can tell the people behind the game have a lot of experience with these topics, and the humorous tone feels like "coping humour" for lack of a better term. To risk sounding like a pretentious person trying to sound smarter than they are, this feels like one of the most real games I've played in a long time.

Anyway, touchy feely crap out of the way, what about the overall quality? The art is pretty fun, and the voice acting is great. The only complaints I really have are nitpicks at best, the main one being when character sprites flip, they didn't draw different poses for facing a new direction, and the text on their shirts ends up backwards. It never happens for more than a few moments, but it can be kind of distracting. The game lacks music outside of its menus, but its also fully voice acted, and the game is permanently set to auto, so its not really something you notice, unless you're going back to sweep up endings on similar routes. I've mentioned the voice acting a few times, but its absolutely worth the mention, they got some pretty big names here, and everyone does an excellent job. Every scene is greatly heightened by the vocal deliveries, and it makes the comedy and the dark moments hit that much harder.

This game doesn't pull its punches, and its definitely not for everyone, but as someone who copes with trauma by using ♥♥♥♥♥♥ edgy humour, this game kinda feels like it was made for me. Its offensive for sure, but its brimming with charm, and underneath the layer of irony is a lot of heart. Reading this back, I kinda feel like a huge dork for writing a big sappy essay on something banding itself as a rejection sim, but despite that, it feels right. I don't know, its hard to review ♥♥♥♥ like this without it getting a little vulnerable. It only takes about 3 hours to get all of the endings, and as of the time of writing this, its half off. Its absolutely worth checking out, but make sure you're doing okay before you boot it up, it hits a lot harder than you'd expect. 9/10.

(also, by completely coincidence, the developers are currently running a kickstarter to make a Class of '09 Anime, I have no reach on this platform but hey, it looks cool, check it out.)\t\t\t\t\t
