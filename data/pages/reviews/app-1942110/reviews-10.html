\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou can see subsurface scattering on bat wings when they fly close to fire. This is the kind of attention to detail I wish to see in a VR game.

Overall, I loved it. In truth, I was starting to get a bit fatigued from all those Oculus Quest ports to PC, where you can feel that the gameplay simulation depth was optimized for a mobile processor. This one is different. From the start of the game, you can see dozens of physically interactable objects. If you see something on the ground, chances are that you can pick it up and do something with it. It had a bit of a childlike wonder of experimentation and discovering all sorts of interaction possibilities. There were a couple of moments where I was "hmm, I wonder if I can just do this," and when I tried, it worked. Things like these make the world more believable, as you try to mimic behaviors that you would also do in real life. This kind of stuff is what VR is really all about. I think that the devs did a really good job blurring the line between gameplay and simulation. At least for me personally, I did not encounter many "it's a gameplay limitation - you have to do it a certain way" moments. Want to pour all the gunpowder into a gun? Go ahead, no one restricts you to do that, no artificial fill-up bars; you just blow up your gun. Don't have a place to put your bullets? Take a coconut shell and place them in there. I love stuff like that, where the game gives you enough freedom. It's like saying, hey - here are your problems, here are the simulated systems. Go figure it out yourself and try to stay alive as long as possible. The more you learn about the systems, the better and faster decisions you will make down the line.

Of course, it's not all perfect. There are some janks in interactions, but that just comes with the territory when you are dealing with such a heavy physics-based game. But it was surprisingly limited. In my first hour, I encountered two problems. My hand got stuck to the chest, and the seashell was violently rotating on the ground for some reason (maybe it was some kind of evil island spirit trying to creep me out).

I am also in that camp who would wish for longer days. It is because I really enjoy the atmosphere and interaction the game has. There were a couple of times where I wished that I could just chill out and experiment with all the stuff the game offers. I wanted to build myself a shooting range, coconuts on the rock so I could practice my skill, and then just enjoy the sunset while cooking a crab.

The game also has amazing graphics. Usually, when I see VR game screenshots, I am skeptical. They are usually fine-tuned with super high resolution, and inside the headset, they look much worse. But with this game, this seems not to be an issue. On maxed-out graphics, it really indeed looks like on those screenshots (also depends on what resolution headset you have), and when the golden hour hits the island, oh my god, the vistas. Just amazingly beautiful. Another reason why I wish these moments would last a bit longer.

As for gameplay, I have a major problem. I can't get past those lizards; I just nope out from the headset. I don't get scared easily in VR, but when those lizards come, they look a bit like snakes in the dark, and I hate snakes. I can't get past them. Whenever I play, I just hope the island will be kind to me and send me only these wolves and flying rats. Also, I do have to agree that this part of the game gets repetitive quickly. It is probably by design, and since I have very limited experience in roguelike games, I don't know how valuable my feedback would be. But just to be an armchair critic, I think maybe mixing up those creatures could help. Especially down the line when more enemy types will appear, you could create unique attack wave combinations out of them so it's not "oh today is gonna be a lizard night" moments. Also, maybe make the beach area a safe place. So the beginners can take their time to prepare their stock, chill out, and experiment with systems. And when ready, they can go deep into the island where real dangers will come.

The island itself is really cool. I have no idea how they managed to create such a big, playable, and detailed area to run so smooth. There is really a lot to explore, and I got lost all the time. Even if that volcano hill was my main navigation point, I still got lost because there are parts of a lush jungle where you can see only a couple of meters ahead.

Can't wait to get back into the game and explore this world more.\t\t\t\t\t

UPDATE #1 Fixed Day Length! Game Is Now Fun! Developers Listen To Feedback & Are Fairly Quick To Release Patches

After the initial update after release, I can now say this game is balanced in terms of day length and difficulty. Days now last roughly around 8 minutes from dusk to dawn compared to the abysmal 4-5 minutes. In my run, I was able to survive 15 days, however during those days I did face some challenge while I was exploring the island, but the challenge was actually quite thrilling and fun. Such a small change made this game enjoyable. There is lots of areas to explore and the basics of the gameplay is pretty damn solid for an early access game.

What is this game?

Bootstrap Island is a rogulite survival game set on a remote island. You play as a crew member of a ship wreck. You start with literally nothing. You have to use your wits to survive. Just like any survival game you must eat and drink. You will also need to build fires to survive night fall. The way you build fires is how you would in real-life, bundle up some driftwood, put in some tinder and actually hit two flint rocks to make a spark and set the tinder on fire. During the night, you are hunted by creatures (wolves, lizard beasts, gargoyles, and more).

When you finally meet your demise during a run, you are graded on how long you have survived. Survive long enough and you unlock items for future runs that you find in chests. In the beginning, you can find a flintlock pistol and torch. You can unlock items like a musket, water bottle, and a knife just to name a few.

Graphics & VR Interactions

This game is quite good looking. In fact, the graphics are some of the best I have seen in a VR game. The lighting, especially inside a OLED headset such as the Bigscreen Beyond, is truely marvelous. I especially love the use of sun-shafts giving a sense of volumetric lighting/shadows.

VR Interactions

Interactions are quite satisfying, especially the coconut pealing. Also, the fire mechanics are very cool and definitely shows off what VR gaming can do in terms of interactions. Speaking of fires, I have got to say, the fire effects are truly next-gen. I have not seen fire building in a survival game so realistic. Kudos to the devs making such realistic fire effects. Plus the liquid simulation is VERY good (coconut milk and water in a bottle). The game also has a flintlock pistol and musket. These weapons are VERY good in VR. In fact, I have to say, you could not make these guns interesting in a flat-game. These are definitely weapons VR could only do correctly without it just being a tedious and long animation. You have to do everything: load the powder, drop the ball in, then pull the hammer back. So satisfying, but hard to master in stressful moments.

Grabbing Issues

Also, I have started to get used to the hop-up mechanic for grab, however, I still think there needs to be some polish to this mechanic to make it a bit easier to grab items. Aiming the hop-up sometimes just does not work.

Virtual Crouching Feedback

Another complaint I have is the virtual crouching. I play on a VR Treadmill. I am strapped in so bending down is a bit limiting for me so I have to use some sort of virtual crouching in VR games. Some games have better crouching than others. However, this game, I feel the crouching is too low. Perhaps have an adjustment bar in the game's options to adjust how low the virtual crouch goes.

Ending Thoughts

I have to say, after the update, I am now impressed. The game does show promise and I cannot wait to see what future content we get for this game. As of right now, it is quite a thrilling survival roguelite. Challenging, but its fair challenge.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is Awesome! I will definitely be spending more time in this one. you really feel alone out there. would be nice to have some friends able to join in with ya on your quest. you can explore and do things together. Maybe, someone could of warned me about my mistakes...
