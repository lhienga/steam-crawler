\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI lasted up to day 5 on my first playthrough before the game crashed. So I'm gonna be sour if I don't get my upgrade after all that. Anyways, it's a fun game but very very very far from realistic. The demon bats have to go. Given it's a tropical environment, it's one of the easiest places to survive if you had to. Not enough coconuts inland. There should be tons everywhere with all the palm trees, even on the floor. I should be able to climb palm trees. Nighttime wild life is the big realism breaker for me. Maybe have one or two wolves come and investigate you in the middle of the night. They shouldn't be circling you like some satanic cult. Maybe they just want to steal scraps you left around, which would entice players to not leave trash where they plan on sleeping, which sleeping should be a thing. There are tons of changes I would make to make it a more realistic experience. So far my biggest complaint is the night time just being attacked constantly. It don't make sense. Maybe a wild puma lurking in the dark once in a blue moon. Fire will help deter animals and provide warmth. In the game, we can't just kill a crab with a rock.... or even our bare hands. Not every coconut needs to split into 4 husks, add a variety. Fire physics are nice. Physics are nice overall. Graphics look great. Playing seated with seated settings didn't appear to work because I could not reach far enough to pick up items on the floor so I ended up playing standing mode just crouching everywhere. I'll probably add more critiques later. My favorite survival game has got to be Stranded Deep.

Update from the next day: Demon bats fly under water, should be able to scrape fibers from coconut husks with rock or knife as tinder.
I made it pretty deep into the island then it seemed like it just led nowhere, My unlocked knife never spawned after several days anywhere that I could see. More foliage needs to be interactable and utilized. Everything just feels useless and baron. For example. Scraping a rock or clam shell against a boulder to sharpen it down into a sharp point, grabbing vines off of trees and tying it to a stick for a handmade knife or spear. On the shrine, further inland, you should be able to salvage that stuff. So many uses for that bone, vines, post, all that should be able to be used. I wouldn't be mad if you made the days a lot longer too. It's just tedious moving like a football fields distance then having to prepare for night or auto death. Then your stuck somewhere inland with 0 coconuts or food of any sort.
Tides would be nice. Foraging in tide pools during low tide is a pretty big part of surviving on a deserted tropical island.
Also even the most remote and uninhabited places on the planet have human trash all over. I know it's 17th century but maybe add some stuff we can utilize like a washed up cup, buckets, ropes, cloth, anything. That makes more sense than having random chests everywhere with guns and torches.
I would also appreciate achievements. Cause right now I don't have much draw to keep playing. I know it's early access but I feel like I've seen everything already?

Update Number 2:
Lasted 3 days then my guy stopped eating whenever I put food up to his face and I thought I was going to die from hunger after starving for a couple of days but instead my game crashed again. Knife didn't cut fish that I caught with a pointed stick.

Would I recommend this game? Yes.
Would I advertise this game as realistic on it's store page? No.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my short time with the game, I have to say I'm having fun and there is a ton of potential here. What is here is a beautiful looking island with great ambient audio, VR interactions, and pretty fun and great fire mechanics. I think my biggest complaint at the moment the day/night cycle being too short.

My assumption is that being that it's early access, they probably have it shorter than planned to prevent you exploring everything there is too soon. There is clearly a large island here, but all of the content within is less clear. Right now, you're dumped on the shores with nothing but a narrator to vaguely guide you to your "objectives" . I haven't gotten to the end of of what currently exists in the game, so I can at least say there's more than a few hrs currently playable. I've traveled from the shores inland a bit and have survived against 3 different types of enemies so far.

All in all, what I see and experienced here is a very solid foundation of what can become an incredibly fun and engaging VR native survival exploration game. I'm looking forward to seeing this game grow! At the current price tag of $20, I'd recommend. It's pretty, it'll keep you distracted for at least a few hrs, and you'll get future updates as well.\t\t\t\t\t
