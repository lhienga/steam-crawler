This game is truly amazing - but also deeply frustrating. It does not - at all - hold your hands. The game is not perfect, there are many bugs, but it works well enough to keep you deeply immersed.

The game will take all the hardware you have and ask for more. The recommended RTX 3080 is no joke. Fortunately, the game uses OpenXR and will provide optimal performance no matter if you use Index and Meta hmds. I tested both Valve Index and Rift CV1, both work flawlessly.
My rig: Asus Strix OC RTX 3090 (24GB Vram), i9 10900K (10cores/20threads, 5.3HGHz), 32GB 3200 MHz, Win10 Pro. The game is stable on my rig, only had 1 crash in 4 hours, and that was before the game got patched.
I usually get solid 80 fps in 80 Hz using Index res 300%, DLSS Balanced, EPIC graphics settings and 4xMSAA. Rift CV1 ss 2.5 did look great too due to oled, and DLSS using TAA (temporal antialiasing) looks sharp with Rift CV1. Don't feel sad and left behind if you have Vive or Rift CV1, you're still getting a premium experience with oled and sharp DLSS. With Index you need high res to reduce the DLSS blur - so res 300% or even higher is by far not wasted.

The game is pure survival - and you will die by predators without a fire at night:
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3166212175
There's plenty of food, wood and drink, if you know where to find it - and that may take some practice and several deaths. This truly is Groundhog Day as Robinson Crusoe - you'll die many times. When you die, you start all over - but you can save the game. An example: quit the game on Day 5, and you'll start at the morning of Day 5 when you start the game again. So this game saves your days as long as you're alive. This also means, if you do not want to start the game all over, and you're close to dying on let's say Day 28, hurry and close the game, and you'll be able to restart Day 28 ;-)
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3169017653
There is much to explore - you can find the shipwreck and investigate - it took me 4 days to get there :-)

Should you buy the game now or wait? It is early access, it's not perfect. If Valve made Alyx 2 as early access, would you buy it? Bootstrap Island is a massive experience, I recommend buying it now. This way we can also help the devs. Valve had enough money to hire many play-testers for Alyx, but devs behind Bootstrap Island have nowhere near that kind of funding. Together we can all help make this VR experience a truly grand one. A gem. So join in - I doubt you'll regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust got to what I imagine is the current "end" of the game and I can say that I overall enjoyed my time! One suggestion I'd like to make, however, is the same thing that everyone else is saying, and that is that the days are quite short. I love taking my time in games and especially survival games. The shorter days constantly make me feel like I need to keep moving at all times under threat of death. I do, however, love making the campfires every night. It's a fun task and it being necessary to survive the darkness is cool and different.

I see a lot of people suggest adding a peaceful mode but I'd much rather have a system where you get attacked every few days or so instead of every night. Having attacks every few nights could allow you to take your time and make camps that you can return to every night while still needing to deal with some threats. Adding stuff that you can craft to make a camp worth returning to could be fun, as well. I'm excited to see the future of this game! We need more VR Survival games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeveloper Does respond so that's what I'm hoping for. The game would be fun if it didn't have immediately enough issues for me to just stop playing. I wanted to love this game I wanted to enjoy this game has a high potential to be good. I will now go into detail with the issues I've had.

This game is just made for quest with 0 consideration for other controllers. I use an index controller and it feels clunky and awful to use with the index, any finger grabs even the trigger, it doesn't show the drag to hand option unless you're hovering above it and it just doesn't feel play-tested with an index controller

The game has zero options for left handed people, you want the controllers reversed? they don't care about you

The game has no tutorial or explanation of mechanics, there is an attempted at environmental tutorials (a rock in front of a chest with a lock) however even those can be clunky it took maybe 7 hits to get through the padlock I almost considered looking for a key. it's not explained in-game if there's a holster system or anything, the gun controls aren't explained and how to get into a coconut isn't explained. I was able to work it out however it would have been better for a visual so I knew I was doing the right thing, the game desperately needs a basic tutorial to teach you the mechanics of this specific VR game as every game is different.

I played for 10mins before saying F this it's too bare bones and seems to be pushed out to try and get money. I deeply want to recommend this game I want to support new VR developers I want to help them but please. get to a more playable and accessible place or you won't get the success you deserve. There is potential there is. I was too afraid to keep playing as I may have to refund this game and probably will unless something is addressed.

Small edit: I'm aware the game says "no handholding" I do think there's a difference between being user unfriendly and "handholding"\t\t\t\t\t
