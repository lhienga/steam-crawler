\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is an absolutely great game, I must admit, and the developer(s) clearly put a lot of effort and thought into it. However I wouldn't recommend buying the game in its current state other than to support the people making it.

Your gameplay pretty much just consists of this loop:
New day
Possibly find new area with more resources
Scramble to find as many coconuts as possible for food/water
Run around to find as many planks and sticks to make a campfire
Night sets in, you build your fire and hope you have enough fuel to keep it going
Repeat

The game currently feels too stressful and the gameplay too basic to really explore and enjoy the beauty that went into making the game and its realistic mechanics. If the developers intended for this stressful experience then that's fine, however I personally won't see myself playing much in this current state, perhaps revisiting the game in a month or two.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: no content, feels janky, looks pretty and there seems to be a solid foundation for a good game if they decide to continue working on it.

Apparently days used to be even shorter than they are now? That blows my mind. The amount of time it takes to get food/water and cover some distance then set up a camp site is still too short. There need to be longer days or more sources of clean water. It's crazy your best bet is to stumble upon coconuts. it would be nice to see lakes/rivers. Using index controllers and it feels janky interacting with things. Especially the "gravity gloves". So many times the item just bounces off my hands I end up just crouching to pick things up because it's faster and reliable.

I got this game after watching the video on their youtube channel and a couple streamers. They would wander around then point at locations saying "I'll let you discover that" or "I'll let you find out whats there". Not sure if they are just paying these streamers to say that without actually showing anything because there is nothing to show. I went to the top of the mountain just to hear a voiceline confirming I am on an island and the name of the game floating in the sky. That was it. Then I ran out of time on the way down and couldnt get a campfire in time, died, whatever. I then go to the shipwreck. Just a bunch of copy/paste crates you cannot interact with and 1 chest with a torch. I already got a torch next to the spawn area.. Exploration just feels pointless without anything in the world. Even if there is anything in the chest you have no way of carrying it with you. They show gameplay with a holster and storage slots on their belt but you don't start with any of that so chests are actually pointless. After my first run I unlocked a knife. No idea how I did and no indication on how to unlock anything else. But you have to hold the knife the hole time or just drop it. You only have 2 hands and to use the gun you need to carry 3 things.. That's just wow.. I hope the upcoming inventory solves things because there is no point for any of the items without some way to carry them around with you.

Night doesn't even feel tense after your first one. It just becomes this annoying thing that haults all progress so you can dance around a campfire with random creatures for a few minutes. Plus night gets wayyyy to dark. You can't even see stars and it's just the creature's eyes and the light from the campfire. After a while you can see the stars again but it's just weird.

After running around the island for 2 hours the only cool thing I found was a post with human skulls. It made me exited that there was going to be something but no. There was nothing in the area around it at all.

At least the Maru are responding the customer feedback and do seem to care. Definitely looking forward to a casual mode. Taking a moment to actually watch the sun set over the ocean is amazing. I play on the aero and this is easily one of the best looking games I've seen. I'm tentatively excited for this one. As soon as the controls are polished up a bit and there's actually things to do on the island this could be one of the greater VR titles.
That's early access for you, I guess. Can't recommend at the moment but I will revisit this review once more content drops.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tok for context, i have so far logged 2 attempts, lasting 4 days and then 3, here are my thoughts
-the game can get brutal, the day/night cycle is QUICK, leaving you little time to explore and get loot
-you have to stick near the beach because otherwise there just arent enough chests or loot at all
-the progression system is very early and not quite clear as to what is really needed of me to progress
-the narrator tries to point me places but i never listen, nor could i really even tell where i was supposed to go?
-the grabbing system is... alright? it kinda feels natural but its still janky when trying to grab stuff, ESPECIALLY a rock
-right now, there isnt a whole lot of content, its pretty much just survive and then when you die, you unlock stuff, on that, i got a knife but its unclear if it has any more purpose than stabbing, alot of game aspects are unclear so im sometimes left wondering "can i do this?" and inevitably "i dont know if i can even do this, am i doing it wrong?" there definitely needs to be some form of loose guide system to give me a slight push in the right direction
-the enemies are pretty basic, decently easy to cheese/kite around without even using a torch but the darkness will kill you (weird ikr)

overall, for a game this early, its pretty good though it really has a long way to go, heres what id like to see in the near future, mostly content-wise
-a player body, i need to know why i dont have the ability to even store ONE item, do i even have any clothes? surely i could fit something, even if its just the pistol+gunpowder+bullets
-more passive animals in the wild for me to hunt (or tame??)
-full customization of the difficulty of the game, from chest abundance to day-night time, id like to tailor my own experience (difficulty presets would also help for those who dont want to sift through many settings)
-definitely refinement of the grab system, its rough around the edges but its pretty good at its core
-toggle crouch, that simple, i hate holding down the right thumbstick while banging a rock against a lock, gets annoying quick

TLDR: game has a long way to go but at its core its a good game. 6.7/10, wait for updates or a sale (not worth $25 at the time of writing)\t\t\t\t\t
