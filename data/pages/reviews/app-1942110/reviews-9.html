\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful Survival Game that is not too hard to jump into. Developers increased the amount of Day time you get before night sets in and you are surrounded by creatures. I feel like it's just about right now with the change. I've been playing via Virtual Desktop on Quest 3 just FYI.

But I do have a couple of complaints. Movement speed - walk and run speed needs to be increased. Even running feels slow...I'm jamming on the joysticks way harder than I should be when playing just trying to get my character to just MOVE. If I'm worried about damaging my controller then something isn't right.

The movement Teleport mechanic works when it wants to, but I guess not when carrying objects...and sometimes teleport seems like it breaks and doesn't work at all. But with some improvement they can hopefully make it 'just work' because I do like having the option even if it is secondary.

Secondly, to grab objects off the ground they tell you to press Trigger (grip works too) to "jump" objects up into your hand. Well...I couldn't get it to work for awhile as objects weren't highlighting properly.

After a bit I just started waving my hand over objects until they highlighted in white so I could grab them...which wasn't nearly as accurate as I'd hoped that mechanic would be. I'd like to see them improve this aspect of the game.

I still have a ways to go and haven't survived more than four Nights so far...so this is an early impressions review for an Early Access game for what it's worth. I feel like the game is worth $20 for sure...I'm enjoying it despite any shortcomings. I do give it bonus points for great graphics, and the use of Nvidia DLSS.\t\t\t\t\t

It needs a difficulty setting that changes the day night cycle to be longer, let the hard core fans have the ridiculous defaults and let your casual plays have a longer cycle "easy" mode. Quest 3 controller. I can not pickup the little bullets out of the chest. I can't get the white highlight to reliably show upon objects to "jump" the up to my hand. No toggle for hold or crouch. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot of potential if they can iron out some of the VR interactivity - the graphics are amazing and the island, lighting, trees and plants etc all look great. I did play the demo so not sure how much has changed but I found the lack of a toggle to hold, toggle to crouch made it really fiddly and frustrating to grab objects and move them where you want them to be - it is tricky trying to hold the right thumbstick down to crouch and then use the grip button to grab or position something. Using the distant grab also seem tricky to line up accurately etc. Just these two options would allow me to actually start to enjoy the game. I realise it is still in early access, but these are two essential features in any VR game, especially as there will be some players with physical disabilities who won't be able to hold buttons or thumbsticks down for extended periods of time. I still can't find a way to save my progress, although there are save slots and there seems to be a save file on my hard drive, but every time I play I have to start from scratch - is my progress supposed to auto-save when I exit the game? I recognise it is still in early access and I am sure many features will be added as it progresses - It could be a really good and original survival experience if they can iron out some of these issues. The day/night cycle is also still very short even after the update - just enough time to move a hundred yards, collect a coconut and start a fire etc...\t\t\t\t\t

Can recommend, but there is one big issue.

Great physics, great perfomance. I like the idea of no HUD or any interface (except hidden one with hydration, nutrition and health). Really like how this game just throw you in the wilds so you can discover the island and learn how to survive by yourself.

But!

Days are still very short. Can't really explore as much as i want. Is there any chance of day/night cycle settings? The gameplay is solid as the graphics are, but short days just don't let me feel any exploration. At the moment one day feels like 10 or less minutes, i barely can eat a little and find some place to settle for a night.

So if you wanna play this game:
As a roguelike with short, intence sessions - YES.
As a survival game - probably NOT.
