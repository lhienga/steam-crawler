\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst things first, this game is pretty good as is, the developers have shown they've got what it takes to make a good game so I do not regret this purchase. With that said, I cannot recommend in it's current form, BUT.... this is still Early Access I will update my recommendation accordingly.

The Good

So, the game is clearly a survival game set in a really nostalgic swashbuckling setting, it reminds me of Sunday afternoon pirate adventure films when I was a kid. It has the usual stuff, hunger, thirst, combat, starting fires etc. The twist is that each night you have to defend yourself against a progressively stronger horde of enemies.

1. It is visually stunning.
2. Even this early into development the game play is solid, there's some VR jank but nothing worrying.
3. The horde based combat really takes it in a different direction, it is an interesting layer to the genre.
4. The fantasy style island they have created is begging to be explored, it has pirates, treasure chests, a volcano, muzzle loaded pistols...

The Frustrating Aspects (for me)

i figured out how to cheese the game, not because I wanted to but because I died if not. The day lengths are not long enough to make progress into the island, find food, find water, and then prepare your defences.

Here is my average day (assuming the "night" goes from 22:00 - 05:00)

05:00 - Mad dash as fast as I can into the island, picking food and water up if you find any.
14:00 - You have 1 - 2 (real world) minutes to pick your defensive position.
14:02 - Start building your fire kingdom.
22:00 - Sit behind your fire kingdom walls wafting a fire stick about until morning.

It seems like you have loads of time, but you don't and that is the crux of my problem here. Playing this for the first time will see you die multiple times trying to get a decent fire going. Then you'll just chuck a bunch of logs on the ground and you'll survive the first night. After a while you realise the absolute best way to stay alive allowing the maximum exploration time is to find a a corner next to a large boulder or cliff, preferably with an overhang for when the flying beasties come. Then dump a two layer ring of the thickest logs you can find around your exposed sides. I further optimised this by doing my coconut munching just before it goes dark then I can directly place the husk on the logs as tinder. Don't forget your rocks otherwise you are not starting a fire.

As soon as you hear a change in tone of the music it means the combat is over, even if the enemies are still in front of you, you can just drop your weapons and get moving. Getting moving as early as possible is essential.

I'm sure in the final release there will be options to increase day/night length and even disable the hordes, just for me at the moment every minute in game is either finding the fastest food/water I can while looking for the next defensible position with enough logs to build the mandatory fire.

Verdict

If you don't mind taking chances on Early Access, get this game and support the developers, it is good as is but will be excellent based on the quality I've seen so far. However, I cannot recommend this to the average gamer in the current form, if you know what you are buying into then fine. It is still very early in development and there is a little of the ol' VR jank hands. My hand got stuck in a treasure chest once and I couldn't open the lid to retrieve it. I died later that evening.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the potential to be absolutely incredible. The graphics are some of the best I have seen in VR. I am honestly blown away.

I was so excited when I first arrived on the island and couldn't wait to explore. Then, the sun set after maybe 8-10 minutes. I fended off the wild life and made it through the night. Once again I set off on my travels, excited to see more of the island. then the sun set again, and again and AGAIN. all my initial excitement faded and I felt frustrated. I decided to push on. Surely this wouldn't go on much longer. I was wrong.

The day/night cycle is ruining the game. The days are far, far too short and after around 3 hours of play time I feel like uninstalling it and playing something more fun. Don't get me wrong. This is a very impressive game and has great potential. I just find myself becoming easily bored with the repetitive game play, constantly having to gather resources in order to survive the night. it's just not fun. I want to explore the beautiful world the devs have created, but I have to almost instantly worry about the impending sunset . Not to mention worry about my hunger and thirst. I get that this is a survival game and it is supposed to be brutal, but not at the expense of fun.

I hope the devs introduce a more casual mode for people who want to explore, something akin to Green Hell. Apparently there was a recent patch to amend the length of days. If this is the case I dread to think how bad it was before.

I just can't comprehend why the devs created such a beautiful place just to rob the players of experiencing it.

Also, I'm not sure if I am doing it wrong but the climbing and swimming mechanics are abysmal.

I can't recommend this game right now, however, I am optimistic that future updates will drastically improve the issues I, and many others have mentioned.

5/10 (if it wasn't for the issues I mentioned it would be an easy 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so I am updating my review. I would say that this game is shaping up to be something with some serious potential but at the moment there just isn't enough there. I have only had two play sessions in this game and there is nothing left for me to do or even explore.

I think this game could become something really special but for now I wouldn't recommend it. The mechanics in the game are really great. The graphics are amazing. Sadly the days in the game are still too short but once you get used to that you can get by as long as you start making a fire as soon as you notice the sunlight fading.

The main reason that I wouldn't recommend the game is that there simply isn't enough to do. In fact there is only really one thing to do. Make your way to the wrecked ship in order to get a holster for the flintlock pistol, bullets and gun powder. Once you have them though, then you might as well quit as there is literally nothing else to do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been waiting to write this review. I wanted to after the first time I played this game but I wanted to give myself time with it and make sure I fully understand it. I must say I love this game so much and it is not only my favorite vr survival game but my favorite survival game of all time. I am someone who normally hates survival games but I love camping and the real outdoors and this game is the first survival game I played that isn't littered with menus and overcomplicated perk and status systems this game is brutal while having a very minimalistic hud and beautiful stunning graphics which will allow you to complete immerse yourself into this world. This is the first survival game I played that feels and makes since just like it would in real life is it getting dark? You need to gather supplys and build a fire thirsty? Crack a coconut open and drink the coconut water from it. My only negative to this game is the lack of content as currently its basically just gather and explore during the day and then fight for you life at night and repeat but this is an early access game so I'm sure that will change in the future. I wanted to mention a couple other good things this game has going for it that don't really have to do with the game. Firstly the developer is amazing and very responsive, he has fixed all issues I encountered insanely quickly and added fishing into the game in just a couple of days which is crazy! Another thing is the price I got this game on sale for like 20$ which is a bargain for a gem of a game like this. Lastly I will be making videos on this game and have already posted my first two if you want to check them out my channel name is: Sgt.Ketchup\t\t\t\t\t
