\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNormally I'm not the type to play rhythm games. Usually they're a little too fast-paced for me to keep up with, and require players to remember two or four or eight buttons to press between at once. I tend to be pretty awful with split-second QTEs, so usually I just avoid the genre on that basis. But I heard that Trombone Champ was a bit on the more forgiving side, so I decided to give it a shot. Boy, I'm sure glad I did! Trombone Champ is very much an "easy to learn, difficult to master" type title that leans towards the easy side when it comes to actual unlocks, which is frankly my favorite approach when it comes to games. It's played using just one button alongside simple up and down movements of your computer's mouse, which is easy enough for my soup brain to remember. And the sweet serenade of the trombone certainly catches the player's attention!

Even aside from the gameplay itself, of course, there's still plenty on offer. A free play mode, some mild player customization, collectible cards. And the lore! Not to spoil too much, but the short but sweet campaign was certainly something to behold. There's an eccentric sense of humor dripping from nearly every aspect of the game, and it never ceases to crop up in the most unexpected ways. Best of all, the game is continuing to get steady updates, so I can feel pretty secure in my purchase even well into the future.

That said, one downside is that you can't expect to get a 100 hour playtime out of Trombone Champ. Expect somewhere closer to 5 hours of gameplay if you're not actively seeking out S ranks and achievements or participating in the (impressively lively) modding community. If such a short playtime is a dealbreaker for you, then Trombone Champ might be better worth skipping.

But if you're not too worried about how many hours the game lasts as long as every single one of them is a blast? If you want to learn the secrets of prophecy long foretold? If you just wanna doot on the funny trombone? Then I give Trombone Champ five hot dogs out of five. Mozart would have been proud.\t\t\t\t\t

It's quite short in terms of seeing the credits and achievements wise, but to be fair you can spend lot of time trying for better scores on the songs, so the replayablity is there.

My main problem - which to be fair I am not great at the game - is how it handles lot of short notes at high speeds. Even at low speeds I struggle to toot manually (I've resorted to making it automatic after 22nd song S'ranked to just get 30 s ranks out of way oops) but other than that it's fun rhymth game.

Would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
