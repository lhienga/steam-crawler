\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA true power fantasy for anyone who ever played lower brass. Imagine, a trombone getting the melody? Preposterous.

So here's the thing: this game is better the worse you are at it.
Okay, that's not quite true, but hear me out. If you've ever played a Guitar Hero/Rock Band style of game, you know that there's a satisfaction in getting things right. But in those games, when you flub a note all you'll hear is a little twang and your score meter goes dim from a broken combo. In Trombone Champ, as in playing the actual trombone, you'll be flailing for notes and missing them by just enough to make the results hilarious to anyone within earshot.

There are a handful of original songs, but the majority of tracks are classic standby tunes, which is where the game shines. You know how these songs go, which is why it's so funny when you bungle them so badly; and YOU WILL. There's a full band behind you playing a song perfectly, and there you are in center stage tripping over your own underwear and landing in a heap on the floor, showing your ass to everyone. Metaphorically speaking. The exuberance of the presentation is a capstone to it all, constantly silly and encouraging you with reggaeton horns and explosions of enthusiasm.

When you do start getting--well, I won't say "good," but "acceptable"--at the game, you'll find out that it goes a little deeper than just getting high scores. Not a whole lot deeper, sure, but there's a reason to keep playing and it's not just to watch that number go up. May the True Tootmaster be with you.

Edit: And should you say that it's not really a trombone simulation, because it has no embrasure control, well, that's a fair point. But if you complain about not being able to adjust your volume, I have to say: have you met a trombone player?

Addendum, after 300% more tooting: If you find yourself lacking a challenge after having played the songs you like as well as you could, turn on Turbo Mode! You'll get sent right back to that "hilariously bad" level again.\t\t\t\t\t
