A silly good time that turns out to be a surprisingly solid Rhythm Game! (9/10)

This game is a perfect blend of fun and comedy. Everything from the character design, to the inherent humor of playing trombones and failing terribly on your first time, or the comically absurd "huge plot" of becoming a trombone legend, it all feels well designed and earnest. It's almost impossible to not be surprised by the little silly things the game ends up throwing at you, and while the sudden background changes and sprite additions on certain songs might feel like a cheap attempt at "jumpscaring" you into missing a note, it never personally affected me much other than amusing me and putting a smile on my face. It's all very lively, and all that emotion is present just from the fact that your Tromboner, if Jubilant, essentially also dances in the background whenever you move your mouse.

It's very easy to see why or how this game basically exploded on the indie gaming scene, and why a year ago, I saw PCGamer or some other gaming news site tweet out a video that shows how fun and silly it is to play Beethoven on a trombone. The whole thing is simply fun to listen to and to look at! And for those who don't just intend to play it casually, there's a smooth learning curve to it that ended up being a blast to experience and end up mastering.

Information:
(•)Playtime: Estimated 15-25 hours. I sadly didn't take note of how long it took me to get 100% achievements and/or all songs on S, but I think my estimation isn't too far off. I keep coming back to replay some songs just to relax and it's surprisingly still quite fun for me personally at the 40 hour mark!
(•)Relatively easy Achievements. The arguably hardest one is to have "S rank songs" for 30 tracks, and honestly, if you just trust the difficulty sorting in-game, that only means getting S on "Long Tail Limbo" and all easier songs that precede it. I think it's absolutely manageable. The amount of song updates over the months probably help gave us more easy ones.
(•)Has decent accessibility options.

Positives:
(+)Fun main mechanics. Tooting along the bar up and down is quite interesting. I'm not a rhythm gamer, but I'm only ever familiar Guitar-Hero style note presentation in rhythm games where you just tap specific buttons in time to songs. This game is more akin to OSU in that your movement also matters, but confining it to a single axis makes it infinitely more accessible in comparison. It also feels fair in how it grades your pitch and score you, which may have felt slightly worse in earlier versions of the game, but with enough updates it now feels perfect for most songs imo.
(+)Relatively frequent updates. Every month or three we get like two songs, which is good enough for me. I don't know how long they'll keep this up, but it sure feels nice!
(+)Songs for everyone! Some songs are very easy and casual gamers will definitely clear them, but the harder ones provide a worthy challenge that might take 4 hours worth of tries to master. I feel like there's a good variety and range of songs here simply built-in. Although if that fails, there is most definitely a modding community that seems relatively alive from afar!
(+)Has some real bangers. Have you ever wanted to listen to Happy Birthday, but in Ska? Jazzy Jingle Bells? EDM remixes of classical music? You've definitely come to the right place to listen to these odd masterpieces and more come to live.
(+)Silly Goofy Jokes. Yes, there are fart jokes and poop jokes and totally-real-trombone-trivia-facts. Which isn't for everyone and definitely far from being a knee-slapper, but it is refreshing to see in games regardless.

Criticisms:
(-) Some of the "beatmaps" felt off to me. Specifically, there is a section in Eine Kleine at around the 40 second mark that suddenly has you no longer following the main violin's melody (the high one) and you play the less noticeable Cello or something. Either that or the tunes that you have to play get super simplified and/or syncopated with the main one, that I somehow keep making mistakes when trying to play this part.
The same can be said about Barber of Seville at around the 38 second mark. The part with 6 short notes in "3 pairs of two notes" feels oddly simplified enough that it doesn't feel right to me; I have the urge to TAP more often than the game wants me to, basically me wanting to follow the main high melody; that I end up always missing some notes.
As of the latest update, I have achieved S rank in all songs (which was made "slightly harder to achieve" in an update somewhere along the line), and I also have done an "OK or Higher only" song play on every single one except maybe Commander Tokyo, Jarabe Tapatio, and Champ Mix of Eine Kleine. So I think this isn't an issue of skill and those particular maps need to be re-examined.
(-) No Workshop support yet. A game like this should have it, but nothing official. Of course, I know there's a lively modding community out there, and I can understand the developer's concerns regarding potential copyright issues with content, but it would've simply been nice to have.
(-) Limited Trombone Customization. They recently added an update that lets us tune the color, patterns, and different hues and shading for our Trombone, which is nice, but you can't even save it as an extra preset to have multiple custom trombones to switch between. It only remembers the ONE configuration you last have. So once you make a good one, you're basically stuck to sticking with it, if you want to keep using it. I really wish they'll let us have multiple custom trombones, visually. But also, I still think the game could have more trombone noises to unlock, too. If anything, maybe NOT sticking to trombones would be an interesting idea.
(-) Slightly off Difficulty ratings. Very minor nitpick but the "difficulty" rating they give to each song might need to be re-examined, since I feel like some songs should have less stars or more stars on them, just based on the fact that if you compare a song to all other songs within the same difficulty rating, it's quite apparent some songs are definitely harder.

Overall, I think Trombone Champ is still worth playing for everyone. You might just discover you love Rhythm Games more than you initially thought!

Trombone Champ\t
Guitar Hero if it was good.\t

Pros:\t
+ Great set of classical pieces in the catalog.
+ As a trombone player myself, the sounds were very accurate when you're off tune.

Cons:\t
- Lacking Workshop support, would instantly boost the game's song variety.
- It's a little too "memey" for my taste and can come off as annoying than "obvious distraction"

It's definitely not in my top 10 of rhythm games, but it's fun for at least 4 hours. Buy it on sale.\t

Overall Rating:\t5.8/10 (MIXED)\t\t\t\t\t
