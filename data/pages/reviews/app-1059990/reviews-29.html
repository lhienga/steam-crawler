\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really fun to get started granted you find the terrible music you're creating funny. I would highly recommend this game for anyone who loves music, and is interested in the game's almost escape-room style story development. You start to unlock things in the game, find the secret story stuff, and things start to get a little more serious. At this point, the game's scoring system becomes a bit of a drag. It's nice that it uses an average, but the way that the champ bonus is calculated leads to some of the worst feeling gameplay I have ever experienced.

Basically, the way it works is that anything over a 79% accuracy will build your champ score, and anything under that will reduce it. So as long as you are playing good on average, the bonus builds. As soon as the bonus activates though, anything under 79% accuracy immediately removes the bonus. Every aspect of this games scoring system relies on an average, except for the champ bonus which is lost fully and immediately when you play a poor note. This leads to a lot of situations where you build the champ bonus through an easy section, then a hard section hit's and as soon as it activates it's gone. Unless you have mastered a song, or the song is on the easier side, it feels like a useless mechanic. Personally, if it was a situation where you lose champ power based on how poor your notes are, but it doesn't build back up until it's gone, that would be much more in line with the rest of the game's scoring system. Such that the bonus activates, and maybe you are allowed three "-". or one "--". Which of course means if you get a "-" then a "--" it's all gone, but at least you have a chance to mess up a bit and still keep the champ bonus.

Anyway, the game is great fun and I would highly recommend it. Just be prepared to feel a little miserable about the champ bonus once the humor wears off.\t\t\t\t\t
