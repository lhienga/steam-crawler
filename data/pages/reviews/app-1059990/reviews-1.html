\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun little rhythm game that I found through watching streamers. I expected it to be very challenging judging by their admittedly ear splitting playing haha, but surprisingly, as a musician(not trombone player), I picked it up very quickly. I was in the top 200 in the leaderboards first try for most songs, so i think most musicians will enjoy this game **once they get the finnicky latency and stuttering under control** . Yes, you will lose combos at a high level of play from the game's latency issues. BUT it is best not to take the game too seriously, and if you play well you can really make some nice sounding music, especially with the bass trombone which sounds pretty warm, and the trombone leads are written nicely especially in songs with a jazz or classical arrangement. The humor is pretty funny and original as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrombone Champ is an amazing game, a satisfying rhythm game and one of the best games of recent memory.

To preface this whole thing, I don't love rhythm games. They just usually aren't my jam. I've tried some of the ones that are supposed to be good, but whenever I'd quit playing them the only thing I felt like I was getting, or, rather, would ever get from them was carpal tunnel. Trombone Champ surpassed this prejudice, transcended it, even.

It's just brilliant. It's challenging but it is easy enough to feel actual progression (unlike many other rhythm games, which sometimes feel like you are ramming your head against a wall of arrows). For some reason, this format (in which you move your mouse up and down a slider and press a key or click to play notes) just feels natural. It just flows. For the first time, I could play a rhythm game without feeling like I would need to have a cybersecurity job at the NSA to even play the first song.

Plus, the game is ridiculously corny and caught me off guard a few times throughout the experience. It's all a part of the charm. Plus, at the end of each song, your score shoots up a bar which will then give you a grade. Even if you only get a B, the dopamine you get from the score rushing up that track is enough to make you want to "try just one more time", which ends up turning into hours of fun.

There is also quite a large modding community, although I regret to say that installing mods requires third-party software (for some reason there is no Steam Workshop support, probably something to do with music copyrights (I would assume)). That inflow of content will keep you coming back, to play anything from Jingle Bell Rock to the Star Wars theme.

I'd also like to touch on one final thing before I give my final thoughts, and that is my own personal experience with the game. I have more Steam games then I can manage right now, and I'd like to play most of them more (except for the ones I wasn't a fan of). Even with all of these games vying for my attention, to at least progress through the story, or earn a few achievements, I have 100% completed this game, and it is now one of my few "perfect games" on Steam. I didn't play it all to get another perfect game. I played it all because it was an amazing experience.

I can't stress enough just how good this game is. It runs on pretty much everything, the actual gameplay itself feels satisfying, and it was one of the first rhythm games I've actually enjoyed. I highly recommend you pick up this game, a gem like this doesn't come out every other day.\t\t\t\t\t
