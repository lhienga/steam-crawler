You have to use LESS dinos because of the increased quality, but the INDIVIDUAL dinos are far more detailed and have much more interaction with their environments.
The park is less streamlined and feels more like a zoo than a theme park, but for those of us ( including myself ) who cared more about the nature aspect got a lot to enjoy with all the different biome themes.

The biggest thing is that there is so much to do. Instead of just the 5 deaths, you actually play out the 6 movies and on top of that get to make your own story in the Challenge mode. The new dino models are high quality, and they have a lot of fun and interesting behaviors to enjoy. I did not know I needed to see a Trex couple greeting each other like dogs, but I can say I am a better person as a whole afterwards.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have yet to play more in order to make a fair review. But I can tell you why I will still keep playing JW Evolution 1.
First of all graphics. The graphics are obviously technically better here. But they are way too bright, I think they did that because this its supposed to be a ''Dominion'' game instead of just Jurassic World or Jurassic Park, so it has this extremely bright aesthetic. Which I think does not fit the theme and feel of Jurassic Park as a whole at all. While the first game has a darker tone which fits Jurassic Park just right.
2:Animation changes. Some animals from the first game had a animation rework, some animations have been completely cut. Some hadrosaurs don't walk on their fours anymore and constantly walk upright, I don't mind making them switch up between all fours and up right for variety's sake, but just making them move the ''new way'' is lazy and stupid. There used to be some animation differences between Troodon and the other ''raptor types'' dinos, these animation differences are gone now, they all move the same manner. Nigersaurus had his own animation set, now these animations have been cut and he moves exactly how the other hunched down sauropods move. Also the new hatch animations for the most part are not as cool and dramatic as the ones from the first game, there's a charm to the hatch animations of JWE1 that are still worth seeing.
3: Combat system, new combat is weird, I can't exactly say its a improvement over the first one which was bad already, I feel like the combat system here is just different and more buggy if anything. Now dinos will kinda circle around each other, sometimes they charge and from my experience, when they charge it gets very glitchy, most of the time they will just bump and clip over each other and run away afterwards without any fighting actually happening. And when they do fight, the big carnivores fight so fast its ridiculous, seeing giants like the T-Rex, Spino and Giga moving around like they weight the same as raptors is just cringe. Also guess what? same death animation for every carnivore except spino that gets a special animation over all the other ones. No especial animation for T-Rex, Indominous and Giga. Just spino, because the jp3 fanbase is the loud minority of the community that for the most part don't even like Jurassic Park at all I guess.
4: I.A, the I.A seens somehow even dumber than the one from the first game, the dinos here struggle way more to find food and water, and they mostly hang out around the same spot of the hatch. If you don't like to put food and water right in the face of your dinos because you assume they are going act like wild animals and use their sense of smell and eye sight to find food and water be prepared for a lot of deaths caused by starvation and thirst. Oh yeah forgot to mention, no feeders for herbivores in this one, gotta place the plants and trees they like, I don't mind this except that feeders would still be really helpful to deal with starvation being that most of my carnivores don't starve for the most part. Its really irritating and stupid.
5:Decorations, alot of missing decorations from the first game here, specially some cool trees that were present in the movies. That really pissed me off, not even the mods can't make up for the lack of those.
6:Mods, the original game still has some cool unique mods that game 2 hasn't replaced yet, I think the Ultimasaurus mod from JWE1 looks a thousand times better and scarier for example.
7:Layout and gameplay. Gameplay on game 1 is much more relaxing and so is the hud, the hud its pretty to look at, its easy to manage and understand everything while having enough time to enjoy your dinosaurs and park. The sequel has way too much micro management, to the point where you don't even have time to enjoy your park and dinosaurs because you are always too busy keep your park from bankruptcy.
8:The campaign mission from game 1 are actually fun to do. Very stupid sometimes, but they are comfy to play. While campaign missions on the sequel are just stupid and frustating to do.
So yeah, I will still be playing the first game since there's still reasons for doing so. JWE2 isn't a replacement, not by a long shot. Though will be playing more of JWE2 yet for a fair review. Its a good game and I'm glad it exists, its something to jump into after I finish dissecting JWE1, but just because JWE2 has more features doesn't mean the first one has become irrelevant.\t\t\t\t\t

After 43 hours, I'm very glad I did. My favorite part is they kept the need to directly control the vehicles to a minimum. That aspect of the game turned me off as I only wanted the sim experience. For the most part, I never had to do it. In the times I did have to do it, it was quick. But there are reasons to do it more if someone so chooses.

The only issue I had that's worth mentioning is the pathing of the vehicles. There wasn't one scenario or challenge where MVUs and Response teams didn't lose their path to a cage, so I had to manually respond to events that should have been automated. It was really annoying on some maps but overall not something that changes my Yes recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t
