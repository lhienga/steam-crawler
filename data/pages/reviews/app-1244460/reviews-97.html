\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing. It is basically the perfect blend if you're a fan of both Jurassic Park and them park builder games. I am also really impressed with the amount of campaign content. Many of these types of games only use the campaign to essentially serve as the player's tutorial, and that is the case here as well in the FIRST campaign, but there are also a lot of others that allow you to delve into the worlds of all 6 movies in the franchise, and they seem quite lengthy. The art style is great considering you have different styles for the movies and factions/businesses in the stories. Graphics are pretty for a game like this, which you spend most of the time zoomed out anyway, and the game works well on the highest settings if you have even somewhat modern hardware.

My one critique in some of the voice acting for characters. Most of them are done well, and the John Hammond voice actor sounds exactly like him, but the person they got to voice Owen Grady sounds absolutely NOTHING like Chris Pratt -- and it's so bad in fact, that I wish they had just left him out of the story completely because it completely removes the immersion. I have no idea if Bryce Dallas Howard is voicing Claire, but she sounds just like her, which is also jarring by comparison to the Owen voice actor.

Overall though, this is a great game, and if you can get the game with all the DLC on sale, it is a really great value.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving completed the first Jurassic World: Evolution and now having completed the majority of Jurassic World: Evolution 2, I can confidently say this sequel is a massive step up from the first game. Especially for its time, the first Evolution was a downgrade from everything else the tycoon genre had to offer. Although I had no complaints about the main campaign, that was just about all it had to offer and the DLC add-ons such as Claire's Sanctuary and Secrets of Dr. Wu were very hit-and-miss. I actually left Claire's Sanctuary a very scathing review at the time because it was such a frustrating experience. Evolution's biggest setbacks were the lack of a true sandbox mode and limitations in its code preventing the implementation of aquatic creatures. Seriously, what kind of tycoon game doesn't have a sandbox mode?! The developers may have eventually added a patch that offered some sandbox features, I don't remember.

Evolution 2 fixes all of these issues. The campaign is enjoyable; even the DLC add-ons, which in this case are based on Jurassic World: Dominion. Evolution 2 also offers an incredibly robust sandbox mode with great customization settings. Unfortunately there are some things I miss from the first game such as the hybrid dinosaurs from Secrets of Dr. Wu. Hopefully the developers plan to add these before support for Evolution 2 ends. (EDIT 3/13/24: They just did, about f***ing time.)

As a massive fan of Jurassic Park, I do have my own wishlist of things to be added as well. The first is a DLC based on the old Chaos Theory toy line that would introduce several hybrid dinosaurs such as Ultimasaurus, Tanaconda, and Amargospinus. The second is a DLC based on Evolution's spiritual predecessor Jurassic Park: Operation Genesis that would add structures based on those in that game as well as cosmetics for all the dinosaurs that appear in both games, especially the Operation Genesis Carcharodontosaurus and Acrocanthosaurus. Thirdly, I'd love to see a creature pack adding Cenozoic animals starting with the Smilodon from Camp Cretaceous, and maybe also a mammoth, Basilosaurus, and terror bird. I know this wishlist sounds pretty demanding and may be an unrealistic vision to have for the game, but it's just coming from a place of passion for the franchise.\t\t\t\t\t
