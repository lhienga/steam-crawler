The game wants for some fairly obvious features found in other Zoo simulators (by the same company), like animal enrichment, or the ability for guests to see things with their own eyes from the path unless you build them a dedicated viewing gallery. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the game, and wish I could love it more, but there are a few things that get in the way of that.

For one, the overall direction of the game just feels really confused. The first JWE was focused on the story mode gameplay with sandbox mode being more of an afterthought. This frustrated a lot of players, as sandbox creativity was negatively impacted by gameplay decisions made for the sake of campaign play; the biggest offender being decoration placement hitboxes. Hitboxes in JWE were large and very restrictive - this was to prevent players from trivializing the enclosure gameplay by just using unbreakable decorations to hard lock dinosaurs into their enclosures instead of using the breakable fences. For players such as myself that prefer story mode gameplay, I didn't mind this, as I understood this was a necessary concession for the sake of gameplay mechanics not being trivialized. For sandbox focused players that just wanted to build cool parks and let their imagination run wild, this was a very annoying aspect of the game. The simple solution if you ask me would have been to just have an option to disable decor hitboxes in sandbox, but I digress; onto JWE2!

In JWE2, they have seemingly attempted to satisfy both the challenge seekers and the sandboxers - and of course, as with any "good" compromise, neither party is really satisfied. The campaign mode is completely worthless, in that the game just handholds you the ENTIRE way through the game. It not only tells you WHAT to build, but precisely WHERE to place it, giving the player absolutely ZERO freedom to play the game how they want. I have never in my life seen a park builder/tycoon style game be so insultingly linear as the JWE2 story mode. The original JWE would give you broader goals to achieve, and then give you free reign to achieve those goals how you see fit. Sure, there were a few tutorial sequences here and there to introduce new mechanics, but they were reasonably few and far between. In JWE2, the ENTIRE story mode is an insultingly trivial tutorial that unforgivably overstays its welcome. The entire time I was playing the story mode, I was so incredibly bored by the mind numbing gameplay, and the whole time I was thinking, "Oh my god, when does the ACTUAL game start?? How many of these tutorial missions are there seriously gonna be??" And then the campaign ended. And I realized, "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥... That WAS the actual campaign..." It was utterly worthless.

Now the story does get better for sandboxers; rejoice, for hitboxes are much more forgiving than before! And yes, rocks and such can now be used to cheese the entirety of the enclosure system, making fences obsolete if you have the patience to place them properly, but whatever, it is what it is. I'm glad the sandboxers get what they want, because honestly that's the better side of this game anyway. But not all is well; in the interest of adding more "gameplay" (read: "tedium"), they introduced micromanagement of staff. This god awful system slows down the gameplay significantly, and is an absolute chore for both story gameplay and sandboxers alike. Fortunately they have added the option to at least disable scientist rest needs in sandbox, but you still have to go through the trouble of assigning the scientists to complete tasks instead of just paying for it to be done like in the first game. The scientist system is hands down the WORST new "feature" of JWE2, and I desperately hope that the idea is cut from future JWE games (if they are even making more in the future).

Chaos theory is more of the same when it comes to story gameplay; constant hand holding and tutorial style gameplay. Challenge mode is a bit more like the story of the first game, where it allows you to operate more freely to achieve your main goals - but the main goal is always the same: reach five stars by earning X income. I'm not even going to get into the mind boggling bad idea that park rating is directly tied to income JWE2, as that's a whole other travesty unto itself. The Challenge mode also lacks the feeling of connection that the first game had between its islands. There is no jumping back and forth and updating your parks as you go; each challenge is a one-and-done experience, which is kind of sad. The newly added Malta campaign tries to bring back some of that magic from the first game, but it's too little too late in my opinion, and suffers from the same tutorial style game design as the rest of the story experiences in the game.

I like the game, but man it could be so much better.\t\t\t\t\t
