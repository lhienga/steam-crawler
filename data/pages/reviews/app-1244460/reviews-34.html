\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game, but with some reservations. Like its predecessor, JWE1, it involves a lot of micromanaging and repetitive gameplay, which definitely are not for everyone. The campaign was pretty much just a tutorial to the game. Some of the Chaos Theory Mode campaigns were kind of fun, though. The Challenge Mode is indeed challenging, with it's varying conditions.
The sandbox mode is in my opinion the best part of JWE2, with tons of creative possibilities. Have noticed creating dinosaur enclosures and taking care of their environmental needs to be very suitable for a fun activity with my pre-school age kids, too.
Furthermore, JWE2 is actually an exceptionally customer-friendly AAA game compared to many of it's contemporaries: New stuff and quality of life -features have been added to the game regularly via free updates, and although I have bought many of the DLCs, the base game itself doesn't really lack anything I would see as essential. For example, the Jurassic Park -era buildings and maps are already available in the base game.\t\t\t\t\t
