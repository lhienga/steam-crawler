\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll begin with the good. Unlike the first game, the sandbox mode is available right away, although you have to complete the first mission in campaign mode to unlock various locations around the word to build your ideal park. The Chaos Theory missions are great fun, taking you through all of the Park and World movies (although the last one in the list is DLC). As expected, the music and graphics are beautiful, and the voice acting is great. The controls have been fine-tuned and it's easier to create pens for dinosaurs and other buildings.

Now the meh and bad. The campaign mode consists of a list of, frankly, boring missions involving building pens and capturing dinosaurs. It teaches you very basic mechanics and that's it. It's not like the first game's park-building mode with extra challenges. I'm not enjoying this game's campaign and I'm more completing it just to unlock more things. I'm on the fence with how the hatchery has been upgraded; you can now create a batch of eggs and pick certain traits there for dinosaurs, such as more or less aggressive, but it feels like it's overcomplicating the process a bit. The other complaint I do have is some of the banter between characters is actually annoying (although this is ONLY when I've played the campaign mode). The banter between John Hammond and others for the Chaos Theory original park is good fun.

I recommend this game if you enjoyed the first one and think it's worth owning both for their differences. However, as with the first, get Evolution 2 on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's OK. The campaign and chaos theory are BORING; the first game's campaign felt more like a grind and that you were actually building a park and now it feels more rushed and arcade like. I couldn't wait to finish it and move on. Like each island in the first game had it's own set-backs and challenges compared to this game where it's like it's on easy mode, they literally give you free dinos, and not boring ones literally they give you a freaking T-Rex, TWICE. Don't fret though, if you're looking for a challenge you can go into sanbox mode and start your own park with about $300,000, I find it fun and actually rewarding to witness the Dino's that I worked for.

Also, and this is a more personal opinion, but the realism is just not enough for me. Specifically regarding the dino's comfort: I'll have a small ass cage because I'm tight on park space that house like some Carnos and Majungosauros and it just doesen't seem right. In the first Jurassic Park movie when they get to the T-Rex enclosure or the Dilophosrous enclosure there's suspense there because you obviously can't see them in the Brush and the enclosure is so large they could be anywhere, whereas in these games you got like 1 or 2 trees and a small pond of water and the rest is open space. It just seems wrong, there's no way they'd be OK with that. Obviously you can customize your enclosure to have more brush but the space of the enclosure just ins't enough unless you dedicate an entire map to one dino. I would have like the maps to be bigger. Like MILES bigger.

Other than that, like I said, the games OK. I personally prefer the first one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an Okay game. I loved the first JWE due to the simplicity and fun build style, and this game super improved on the mechanics, design and new content. But other than the mechanics, buildings and parks, this game lacks a variety of content and the fun of the first game. First off the campaign takes itself too serious and feels draining to complete with locations that have no variety except the snow or sand. I will add how I liked the change in trees/rocks at different locations, and the different terrain options based on location. The locations were not fun and it felt like an extended tutorial, so when I finished the campaign I was excited to hop into chaos theory, I fully played through the Jurassic Park Chaos Theory and had a fun time, it felt a bit worse than the DLC for JWE (lacked JP decor and buildings that JWE DLC had), but I didn't complain since it was free part of the game. Then I went and headed into my second Chaos Theory mode, the San Diego one. It felt draining to play the same game again, pretty much no different with (I would say) and easier play through than the JP Chaos Theory. I haven't tried the other modes, but this game feels like an Overwatch 2 Scenario, No good campaign, the main gameplay is in Chaos theory which really lacks content and ins't hard. I do like the game but am disappointing in the product, could be a lot better.\t\t\t\t\t
