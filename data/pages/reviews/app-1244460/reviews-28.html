\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you wanna play this game to look at cool dinosaurs doing stuff and there's nothing else you are interested about, then buy it on a discount or something (I'm begging you, don't pay the full price, it's not worth it...). That's definiately the coolest aspect of the game, which overall doesn't seem to be too good imho (more below). Don't get me wrong, I absolutely love the models and animations for the dinosaurs there, and I could spend hours just going on a jeep tour to stare at these animals. But it's not the point of the entire game.

First of all, apart from sandbox and timed challenges, there are basically two types of campaigns: first one being actually called straight-up campaign, in reality a very short tutorial, set inbetween Fallen Kingdom and Dominion. This one is very boring, full of Jurassic World characters (which I personally dislike and find as simply pretentious) and fast to complete, and may leave you still a little bit confused about some of the mechanics, even after completing every part of it. The second one, which is definiately more interesting, is Chaos Theory, in which you (mostly, but not always) create parks from certain parts of the movies. It's still lacking tho. For example, I don't really understand why do we get a voiced cutscene at the beginning of each of them, get voiced briefings with franchaise characters after completing certain objectives, and then, when the story ends, you don't really get anything. No cutscene, no voiced dialogue going like 'congrats, you did well with Jurassic Park/Jurassic Park: San Diego/Jurassic World', nothing. Only a pop-up informing you about the completion of the final objective and getting 100% at certain CT scenario, inviting you to either continue with current park or get back to the main menu. Seemed very weird to me, as if it was done in a hurry or without really caring about anything. Didn't really make me feel like I achieved anything with completing the final objectives, either.

Mechanics may be a little bit confusing at first, even after completing the tutorial. I didn't play the previous Jurassic World Evolution, but I played another Frontier's game about managing a park full of animals, which was Planet Zoo. And dare I say, comparing these two titles, JWE 2's interface isn't very intuitive, and some aspects of appealing to the guests are a bit weird - especially how quickly people start complaining about the lack of transportation in your park, if you didn't think early enough to add it. Basically you HAVE TO add either a tour or a monorail in your park, and you need to keep it in mind while planning the infrastructure. Even in fairly small park people are quick to complain they have to walk their lazy butts around. This kind of stuff is the aspect I'm a bit salty about, because I don't feel like this kind of needs was addressed in the tutorial... Rather you were doing repetitive, basic stuff like catching the dinosaurs and creating habitats. If you wanna learn how to play the game, there's no point in doing the tutorial campaign, it's better to spend all those hours on fan-made tutorials on Youtube etc., tbh.

I also find it a shame how little diversity there is in the buildings. Yeah, you can change the colors and all, cool, but overall architecture mostly remains the same. I wish there were more options for that, a bit more creative than 'let's directly copy and paste the designs from the movies', 'cause man, how cool would it be to create Jurassic Park with theme of different parts and geological periods from the history of the world? For example, in Planet Zoo, if I wanted to make a zoo full of East-Asian animals and make the aesthetics of the region a main theme, I was allowed to do so. JWE 2 doesn't allow you to go very creative with anything, either leaving you with generic Jurassic Park, Jurassic World or governmental buildings. No real theme possible, even if you wanted to focus the aesthetics on, for example, certain geological period (let's be honest, dino stans, Jurassic and Cretaceous periods did differ geologically and in environement... why can't we take it in as a theme? Or maybe a purely marine theme? Anything?). Sure, it would require some more work to blend it nicely with JW's aesthetics, but I do think it could be possible. Instead, the lack of diversity in buildings made me bored with park designs very early-on, because I started feeling like I was creating essentially the very same park over and over again, just with different layouts and dinosaurs... Also, not sure if I couldn't find how to turn such option on or if it's simply non-existent, but there seems to be no option to show you a net or anything to adjust paths, buildings, fences etc. nicely to each other. If you have OCD-like issues or simply are a perfectionist (me), and NEED stuff to be matched just on point (also me), then it WILL be an issue. Again, compared to Planet Zoo, I found it as very, very disappointing.

I have no idea what should we be paying for with the full price, really, especially considering all the people saying openly it's almost the same as the previous Jurassic World Evolution game. I'm simply glad I bought it on winter sale. Game itself becomes boring very easily, campaigns are mostly lame, without 3rd party tutorials you probably won't get half of the mechanics right at the beginning of the gameplay. I'd probably call it a typical kind of cash-grab using popular IP, which is pretty sad tbh. For me it's easy to feel it being like 'let's make a management simulator out of Jurassic World' rather than 'let's make a management simulator settled in the Jurassic Park/World universe'. IP was the main focus, not the gameplay itself imho. A shame, because overall the models are GREAT and man, I just wish the rest of the game was as good as the animals in it. The variety is amazing, the option to modify genomes is cool and allows you to customize the dinosaurs a little bit (including colors!), and the animations are either epic or straight-up cute. Seeing the dinosaurs fight was always SICK, artists creating these models and animations deserved a raise-up in salary solely because of that!

IN SHORT: If it was a walking or a tour simulator with dinosaurs, I would recommend the game, seriously. But because it's a park building and management simulator with dinosaurs, I simply find it as disappointing in how it turned out to be.\t\t\t\t\t
