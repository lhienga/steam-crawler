\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI find this game to fit the exact niche I was hoping for, and its important to mention that I think its niche. This game is a flawed management game. Many of its systems are extremely buggy in ways that are extremely distracting and make the game harder for no reason. My biggest gripe is when common actions simply stop working such as when placing paths or fences. Sometimes clicking your mouse will quite literally do nothing when it should definitely be doing something. My other biggest gripe is when staff you will use to automate various tasks are unable to pathfind to their objective making it extremely tedious and sometimes very time consuming to aid them in finding their objective.

Performance wise the game is not too much better. I find that even with medium sized parks you can sometimes experience micro stuttering. I'm overall fine with this since its a management game and I find that frame rate is less important compared to an FPS for example. I experience framedrops and micro stuttering despite having a relatively high end system (I run the game at native 1440p).

Specs:
- rx 7900 XT
- r7 5800X3D
- 32GB of ddr4 3600mhz RAM
- and the game is installed on an m.2 SSD

Despite all of these issues I find the game to be extremely enjoyable because it absolutely fulfills the fantasy of building a "Jurassic Park". I don't plan on delving into specifics as my goal here is to warn people of the biggest problems I faced, and to take note that despite these concerns I still recommend this game (on sale).\t\t\t\t\t
