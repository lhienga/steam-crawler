\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt should be an amazing game, and maybe still is if you're willing to play on sandbox mode and you like dinosaurs. I got 100% achievements on the first game (on Xbox), but that was a bit of a slog, and I hoped part 2 would fix all some of the glaring flaws of that game. It did not.

The game is so much more tedious than it needs to be. Basic tasks like doing a welfare check on a new dino feel frustrating at times, especially once the park gets a little bigger. A ranger team is supposed to use an outpost to check on the dinos in that enclosure, but I found that I had to unassign my ranger's tasks to get it to check a new enclosure first, even if I changed its priority settings. Using the outposts just prevented me from making new dinos comfortable as fast as I needed to. You can't trust the automation to do anything well. If your park has an outbreak of a disease, you are going to be manually clicking every dino playing a game of whack-a-mole. I should be able to trust that my staff can do the task if I give them the tools and a nice park layout to get the job done efficiently. My medical teams should be able to do the job if I set them up with enough teams and research the medicine I need. When a dino dies, I shouldn't need to manually click it to get it be removed; there's not even any design aspect built around removing them. You just click them or you get punished. That's not an interesting mechanic because there's nothing in the park setup that involves making it function well. Dino dies -> click transport button in menu, click on dino. Every time. Why can't stuff like this be automated?

My biggest issue is how the game works when you try to add difficulty. In the first game, beating the highest difficulty level meant excessive amounts of micromanaging from the poor automation, and, even worse, hours of reloading for better contracts to make money off of, because there was no reasonable way to keep your park afloat without doing both of these things. It is exactly the same way in part 2. You want to stay out of bankruptcy, you have to be spending all your time completing contract after contract after contract, flying around taking pictures of dinos, praying that you don't get a disease before you can get a medical facility, praying that a months-long storm doesn't bring your park rating to 0 and cause you to go deep into debt from something you can't control. The contracts are RNG, too, so if you get an unreasonable one, you have to pay to reroll it. Essentially you might as well save scum it because losing that money is not tolerable on Jurassic difficulty. How about making the park managing itself the challenge, guys? Can I just have my win or failure determined by how my park was designed or something? RNG plays far too much into it. I get that you want each play to be different, but it's not the right kind of different where one outcome is literally unplayable and you just reload until you don't get the unplayable outcome.

Bottom line: I want to put the park management skills to the test and maybe handle a few fun things like dinos escaping. This game leans too hard on aspects of management that are tedious instead of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
