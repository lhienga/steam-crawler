\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is honestly art. It's innovative, beautiful, and rewarding with an excellent gameplay loop. This may be one of my favorite games.

JWE2 vs JWE1: [Note: I have a similar amount of time sunk into JWE1 as I do this game] This game is an improvement in all aspects from the first. I loved the first game, and didn't know how they'd top it when 2 first came out, but I hardly touch the original now. JWE1 is still an excellent game, but this one renders it relatively obsolete in my opinion. If you are looking for an incredibly simplified version of this game that has less to manage (and maybe a little less stress for those who don't enjoy all the little things) the first is the better pick, but this game from the variety to the graphics to the AI is a huge step up from its predecessor.

It has been amazing watching this game develop from launch. It's one of the few games I readily and happily spend money on DLC for. You can tell the developers really care about the product they're making and the new content they produce, and when I put my money in it I can see that money being put to good use in subsequent updates. That's a lot more than I can say for other games with DLC content. If you take your time to really enjoy each pack, the cost is worth it. Especially if you've sunk a lot of time into vanilla and want to explore a larger variety.

The game does have a few persistent bugs that can be frustrating, especially because of the damage they can do to your park ratings. Sometimes dinosaurs will randomly forget their territory borders and freak out until they draw new ones, sometimes dinosaurs randomly forget how to eat and you have to sell them before they starve because there's no fix for it. I've had this issue with the dilophosaurs especially, even back in the first game. I don't know why. But these issues haven't largely hindered my ability to enjoy the game, and sometimes just exiting and reentering the game fixes it.

Managing a park of dinosaurs where disaster lurks around many corners has somehow become a way to unwind for me. There will likely never be a JP sim like this again, and this is everything I had wanted from a JP game as a child. I look forward to seeing what future updates are in store.\t\t\t\t\t
