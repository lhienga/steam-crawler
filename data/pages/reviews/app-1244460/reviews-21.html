\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only play in sandbox, so I can't comment on the challenge mode elements, which seem to be popular. Mostly my enjoyment comes from building a giant nature reserve, terraforming it and managing populations while I listen to youtube videos or podcasts in the background (it sounds silly, but it is an excellent way to relax after work). Here are my pros and cons:

Pros
- The environment paint tools make for a much more enjoyable casual experience. Unlike PZ you don't have to place every plant and have it look nice; you can just paint a biome and hold over an area more if you want the forest to be thicker.
- The dinosaurs have unique social interaction, sleeping, hunting and fighting animations. I just tab around dinos and watch them walk around all day. The character each dino has is what would make me recommend this to players especially anyone who just likes dinosaurs in general, since it's simpler than other park sim games.
- I really like the territory and pack alpha features as someone who builds a nature reserve. The dinos won't murder each other instantly like some park games and seem to group up when given a large amount of room. Carnivores like Dilophosaurus and medium size raptors can hunt in groups.

Cons
- The actual park building elements are kinda meh to me. I don't like the pathing and the basic terraforming. I would like bodies of water that aren't all shallow puddles. Maybe I just haven't figured it out yet? The game is definitely less about creative building and more about park management.
- I felt like a lot of the carnivorous dinosaurs kept escaping to the point it was annoying. The "intelligent" trait makes the dinos try to break out if they are able to see anything moving outside their exhibit.
- Many DLCs don't add much enjoyment. They are all additional dinosaur species with on occasion some other scenery or challenge stuff, leaving more of the building aspect lacking. Still, there are some really great dinos added like Gigantoraptor, Therizinosaurus and Yutyrannus. Gigantoraptor is adorable and my favorite species in the game.
- The lagoon feature is sorely lacking. I hope in a future title they remedy this. The lagoons are by default barren and you have to place decorations individually while it's difficult to see anything in the water. I don't really use it because I don't build parks anymore. The aviary feature is a bit better since you can zoom into it and see what you're placing. There isn't much variation in the lagoon or aviary base game dino roster. Neither have many unique animations like the terrestrial species, although Quetzalcoatlus and Pteranodon can hunt medium to large land dinos and it's really entertaining to watch.

(For anyone who's played Operation Genesis:) Yes, but...

Out of all the Jurassic Park building games, I have only played Jurassic Park Operation Genesis (OG) about a decade ago, and this was a huge nostalgia bringer.

With all the DLCs included, It has loads more dinosaurs (114, compared to just 25 from OG), from which are a couple aquatic (13), aerial (11) , and Hybrids (3). However, I wish there were more aquatic and aerial dinosaurs, as it feels a bit hard to have much variety of dinosaurs in the same enclosure without conflicts or cohabitation issues.

Also, I recommend playing with mods like Kai's Wee Toolbox because of a lot of terrain & building limitations.
