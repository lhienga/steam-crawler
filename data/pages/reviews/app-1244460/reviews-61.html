\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Okay so steam doesn't give me the meh option.

Tldr: cool concept but an overall lazy design, Also game is INCREDIBLY expensive for its total content unless you're an average sim player so only buy on a sale

Pros- You get to build a literal Jurassic Park of your dreams

Fun(ish) sim-like qualities and gameplay buffers to give a challenging aspect while still not being too difficult

Different game-modes including a campaign that sorta rein-visions some aspects of the story, as well as cool challenge game-modes that take the older movies and does a what if scenario where things didn't go haywire and what those parks would have looked like

Cons- Very little cohesion with the world building aspect that this game could have

Somewhat buggy (at least from my experience) system for placing structures and somethings just stop working like the expedition center (normally pretty easy fix but still to keep in mind)

Limiting content outside of the sandbox mode in terms of freedom and decision making when building and laying out your parks

Voice dialogue that no matter how many times you hear it, cant be skipped aside from the few cut-scenes

The most egregious sin this game makes is not allowing for multiple saves in the sandbox mode especially given the 9 different locations you can build your parks. Even from a real world prospective, there would be multiple parks in real life if not that would be the biggest wasted opportunity in the world. As somebody who doesn't play sim games from the occasional boot up of sim city when I'm hanging out irl with friends and we get bored and wanna mess with the sims, the total amount of replay value drops instantly for somebody like me.

A fairly steep price range IF you want all the game has to offer plus a few nitpicks but not deal breaking, (no option to turn off auto-save, The need for a decent amount of ram just to make sure things run smoothly once the park starts growing in size (tho this is purely because of it being a sim game), The fact that I put this game on an m.2 and it still takes a hot second to load a save file even at a relatively small size park with few dinos.)
