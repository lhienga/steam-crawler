\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCons
- Guests are way too needy, should not need a bathroom, shelter, hotel, and shopping center/food court every 10 feet. Takes focus away from the dinosaurs which are supposed to be the point of this game.
- Configuring shopping, food, drinks is just busy work and a lazy add on by the devs, should be a fully automated process or completely removed. You just click through the same menu 50 times and select the highest profit number without reading anything, plus nothing - visible even changes and you don't even have a say of what configurations to pick as the guest demand randomly determines it for you.
- Feels like over half my play time is just clicking buttons on the management screen for research, expeditions, and fossils. This should be an automated or a more streamlined process so I get more time looking around my park in real time.
- Very limited customization options for buildings and habitats.
- Dinosaurs have almost no management compared to guests. A couple clicks to optimiaze their enclosure once built and you never even need to look at the dinosaur again. In stark contrast to the tediousness and boring visitor management.
- If dinosaurs attack and eat guests, your park just continues like nothing happened with maybe a slightly lowered appeal rating (while their corpses are still there in broad daylight!) instead of being forced to close or otherwise set-back in some meaningful way.
- Storms feel like a terrible mechanic. Every 15-20 minutes, a tornado ravages your park and breaks your fences and there is nothing you can do to prevent it aside from buying Storm Defense on your key infrastructure buildings. You have to manually go capture them all and repair everything. There should be a way to shelter the dinos in preperation for this since everyone else gets a warning.
- It is so tedious and frustrating to have to optimize every square inch of guest configurations to get the last little bit of a star rating needed to advance your park.
- The dialogue is terrbibly written, forcibly portrayed, and always says the same thing, make more money.
- Like all of Frontier's simulation games, money quickly becomes an obselte factor once you are forced by the game to optimize everything in order to advance.
- The campaign sucks and is just a long-winded tutorial, while Chaos Theory is the actual campaign.
- Chaos Theory also sucks as a campaign, the gameplay is just paywall after paywall after paywall. I seriously just sat on my phone and let the game run for 3 hours while clicking a few buttons. Recruit a scientist, train that scientist to the needed level, pay for access to a fossil site, pay to acces that fossil site, pay to extract the genome from that fossil, repeat 2 or 3 more times to complete the genome for every wanted species, pay to synthesize that genome, pay to incubate. There are also different levels you have to research to allow your scientists to reach the next tier of training to get the dinosaurs you need to complete the missions which, surprise, you have to pay for. Oh, and you have to do this exhausting loop for every. single. dinosaur. species.
- Another note on fossils and completing genomes, even if you have a genome at 98% complete, the game will still charge you $2 million to go on the expedition to get the last 2%.
- Also really anticlimactic ending for Chaos Theory Jurassic World mission, there isn't even a cutscene or victory line from the voice actors after you do everything.

Pros
- The dinosaur models and skins look great.
- Aviaries and lagoons are awesome and add much needed variety.
- The variety of viewing galleries/attractions and where you can place them are pretty good (but wish you could further customize).

I would recommend this game only if you can buy it for a hefty sale, or if you are the most diehard Jurassic Park/World fan. Otherwise I would definitely pass as there are much better options to spend your money on.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played this game already on Xbox Series X and wanted to play it again portably on Steam Deck. It's a good game but the story falls short of being any sort of improvement over the first game. Love the dinosaurs and behaviour patterns but I found keeping them happy/less prone to breaking out in storms more challenging in the first game. Whilst the game is a success in showcasing these dinosaurs, it is rather shallow in every other aspect. Park management and building is not much more in depth from the previous game and doesn't spark much creativity from one mission to the next. Keeping visitors happy isn't difficult and again, doesn't go in depth like other park management games out there. Overall, the appeal is all about the dinosaurs and not much else. I love watching the dinosaurs and making nice habitats for them which is mainly why I bought this game a second time (much reduced in sale) but I was hoping for so much more improvement from the first game.

And as a big aside, as of writing this in January 2024, DO NOT BUY ON STEAM DECK until the hideous graphical bug on dinosaurs and humans is fixed. This has been going on for months, has affected a lot of people, and Frontier don't seem to be interested in fixing it at all. Poor support from the developer so far, and at the moment it is completely unplayable on Steam Deck.\t\t\t\t\t
