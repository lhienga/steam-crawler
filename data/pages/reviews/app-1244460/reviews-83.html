\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR fun if you like Jurassic Park, City Builders or Park Builders, get this especially on a steam sale.

This is a vast improvement over the first game, it's basically a city builder strategy type game, or park builder you can call it, which is just a spin-off of city builder focusing on building theme parks.

I say this is an improvement because the first one was really not fun, boring, and lacking. This one took everything good with the first one, kept it, and improved on all the blah aspects making for a good sequel. I would rather the first one have been updated and improved upon but hey I will take a sequel over nothing.

I can't say I would not spend full price on this game but if it comes up in a steam sale and you like strategy games, especially park builders, city builders you will enjoy this one. Also helps if your a Jurassic Park universe fan.

It does have a learning curve, so be willing to go through some games to trial and error learn, some things are clearly spelled out.

A con I can point out is how difficult it is to get a park to 5 stars. It's not well balanced or intuitive how to get or keep stars to achieve five stars and sometimes you can spend hours with no progress. Devs definitely need to balance this out better or make it more easily identifiable what you need to get a better rating.

You also some times feel like you're managing the scientists more so than the part which can be a bit annoying since the focus is a park builder. Not a scientist manager. But these two con's are more learning curve and minor annoyance, which hopefully will be improved upon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot really a game review, more of a comparison with the first one.

Played this game 3 times on each platform and its really good but I don't know if I'd say an upgrade for the first. I loved the first game, most notably the campaign, this game's campaign, well there's no nice way to put it. It sucks. Like really bad, its basically just a tutorial, JWE1 was a tutorial on the first island, then a campaign as you move forward, this was just completing missions, no park building at all for campaign.

Challenge mode is also not as much of an upgrade from the first, it works in nearly the same way but the park building is different for this game, overall I'd say the island selection is better, but aside from that not really an upgrade.

Sandbox is a lot better for JWE2, the selection of decorations and paths and the huge amount of tools and resources at your disposal make this a lot more creative of a game.

DLCs are arguably the same as the first game, some DLCs have their own campaign, while others are just more dinosaurs. In my opinion I preferred the Dr. Wu DLC (best JWE1 DLC) over Biosyn DLC (best JWE2 DLC), but they're again pretty similar.

The main thing saving this game is Chaos Theory, say what you want but I LOVED JP San Diego and Jurassic World Chaos theories, tons of fun and in my opinion the best parts about the game. Some of the levels just felt like mission completion with no park building unfortunately but its a nice change of pace.

There are some "upgrades" to this game which are very polarizing, scientists, comfort levels, cohabitation, research, lack of contracts for divisions, all of which I personally don't like but they make it its own game and different from the first game. Overall, its a really good game to pick up, I'd say that its a must buy when it drops on sale ($15-20), DLCs aren't really a must except the Biosyn one (again get it on sale for $10).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJurassic World Evolution but the better sequel. A bit like when the second sibling arrives, more advanced, higher quality, more dinosaurs etc.

The first 2 hours are kinda slow, but it's getting you used to all of the new features n stuff so it's alright cause there's a load of things to get your head around.

Once you do the above tutorial-type stuff, you start to progress and get all of the stuff to build an epic Jurassic park. The sandbox mode is available straight away so you can mess around from the get go which is nice.

When you unlock the T-Rex and other iconic dinos its pretty cool. They have MUCH cooler cinematics when they leave the incubation thing compared to the first game.

Sandbox mode is also much better, all of the research is unlocked straight away, so no grinding the story mode using hard earned in-game cash to unlock scale patterns and genetic stuff. Deffo worth the £15 on the 70% discount

I'd say the only issue is that when you start creating your park, you will not stop until it is perfect, which kills alot of time and then when you find it is completed, you have spent hours, days, weeks, months on something that no one else will see or care about. And then you ponder in you own head about the inevitability of death and that we all have limited time on this earth and then you know that whilst you spent all of this time on a trivial game, you could have been doing something significant more valuable with your time, and then you will realise that you aren't a 13 year old playing games anymore, you will know that we are all going nowhere and once we are dead that is it, there will be no one to remember us, because all we did was play a video game making a theme park full of dinosaurs that only the creator knows about. We may try to make it so it isn't a huge waste of time, however by screenshotting the park does not make people think you are interesting, they wont care, and neither do you. We are all going to die.

Other than that, would recommend :)\t\t\t\t\t
