\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of my thoughts from the previous game carry over to this one, so I'll be using this space mostly to talk about what's changed. For the purpose of a recommendation, if you're on the fence about buying this, go check out the first Class of '09 instead. It's cheaper, shorter, and I can't imagine you'd like this game but not that one (and vice versa).


All that being said, I do think that The Re-Up is the significantly better game of the two. The different routes are more balanced, more characters are given something to do, the artwork is generally less awkward, and a lot of the sudden "shocking" scenes of the previous book have been replaced by slow, tortuous buildups that really allow Nicole to shine as a protagonist. The voice cast and dialogue are just as good as ever, and you can overall tell the author just generally had better stories they wanted to tell.


Gone are a lot of the silly "half-endings" of the original game. The Re-Up structures its story completely differently as a two-act drama. You're guaranteed to get a full Act 1 based on your choices, and you're guaranteed to get a satisfying ending in Act 2 based on some new choices. This guarantees that regardless of what you clicked, you're seeing a complete and compelling ~45 minute story as well as a super-satisfying ending that is relevant to the choices you've made up until now.... at least in Act 2.

The caveat here is that Acts 1 and 2 are mostly disjoint, joined by a single vertex halfway in your playthough; there is no overlap between the two. This can lead to major, status-quo events (like your mom dying) being literally unacknowledged in the latter half the game. I understand the difficulty in writing around this, and the first game had some of this too, but it does ruin the immersion a little bit.


I'd argue Nicole is less morally-divisive in this game, I'd also argue that this is a good thing. With the exception of one ending, Nicole is undeniably more actively malicious in the Re-Up. Her revenge here is fueled almost exclusively out of petty annoyance rather than legitimate self-defense, and this just really polishes where the first game already shined: the attempt to discredit the natural empathy the player has for the protagonist. Because as much my conscience wants me to hate her, man do I like Nicole as a character.

Nicole's friends do a lot of the heavy lifting as far as characterization is concerned. All of the throw-away girl characters from the first game become their own person and even the stars of some routes. It's great seeing so much of the talent from the first game come back to play a bigger role! A personal favourite of mine is Emily in her route.

SPOILER FOR EMILY ROUTE
She makes a great contrast for Nicole; her actions are more evil and she is more unhinged, but her mental struggles also introduce an added layer of culpability for Nicole as the "saner" one of the two and an instigator. The speech Emily gives before the ending does come off as "I'm 17 and this is deep". But the passion she delivers it with and her conviction in her belief (and subsequent suicide) leads to such a hollow, ugly feeling in the reader that is both deeply disturbing and morbidly fascinating to sink into. Ms. Ames is who I first agree with here as a "mature" adult, but the game criticizes me for forgetting what it meant to be a troubled teenager and becoming cushy. Wasn't I once 17, and wasn't I once deep? Are someone's thoughts discreditable just because of their age or academic achievements? Doesn't my own fascination with this ending inherently prove Emily's point? Where am I going with this........?

Jecka probably got the biggest glow-up out of the cast. No longer is she just Nicole's yes-woman, but instead plays the partially morally bankrupt voice of reason to the completely morally bankrupt protagonist. This combined with the expert writing gives Jecka the opportunity to deliver some of the filthiest clapbacks in human history; I have never seen someone's entire ideology fall apart as totally as when Jecka graced us with her most iconic two words: "You're white".


The final "secret video message" of this game just kind of sucks. It's like a Doki-Doki style horror ARG thing (?) that breaks the 4th wall for no reason and falls super flat. I'm purposefully spoiling it here so you can prepare yourself the disappointment that comes.


I'll leave with a hot take: I don't think the mall cop scene is that funny. I think it falls into the "shock" elements I didn't particularly care for from the first game. The slow unraveling of Nicole's sociopathic schemes across the various second acts of this game is where the real good ♥♥♥♥ is. It's watching a car crash in slow motion,

but you're the driver.\t\t\t\t\t
