\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite not being a "sequel" and the first game ending with graduation, both games co-exist as Re-Up references the first game, and assumes you've played it as it sidesteps a lot of the character introductions. So if you're looking at this game and haven't played the first, definitely play the first.

Class of 09: The Re-Up is a much improved and larger game than the first, and gets an A+ for being fully voiced with crazy dialogue and memorable characters. My only complaint is how they took Nicole from someone with a little bit of nuance in the first game, someone who you could tell wasn't quite the a-hole she was making herself out to be (though only barely), to full on crazy trashbag sociopath of a person in the second with zero remorse for anyone or anything. That said, I can't entirely complain about it since Nicole is still my second favorite after Jecka, but the third game could add a little of the nuance of her character back from the first game and I wouldn't complain.

This game is a literal example of a visual novel: you listen to some insanely fantastic and superb voice acting for about 5-6 hours and every now and then the game gives you a choice to make. That's it. No navigating the game world, no inventory; the only clicking is making said choice. Although one route did have me click "Google Search" for Nicole when she was learning how to make crack in her ploy to get her community service hours. And honestly? Not even the craziest route in the game.

Truth be told, I could use more visual novels where all I have to do is watch and listen, without navigating a superficial game world or fiddle with a lame inventory system. Maybe I should get into audiobooks. Regardless, Class of 09: The Re-Up (and the first game) were fantastic, cannot recommend them enough.\t\t\t\t\t
