\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompared to the first one, Re-Up has fewer choices and less outcomes, but the game is still longer overall. The animation is still fairly simplistic and the humor hasn't changed to much, so if the original didn't click with you, then Re-Up probably won't either. One minor criticism I have is that often the second half of a playthrough has almost no choices, and you're just watching a story play out. I didn't mind this lack of interactivity too much, as the writing and voice acting are excellent and more than make up it. One of the endings had me on the verge of tears, something I didn't experience while playing the original. Still, if you want to feel like you're actually shaping the protagonist's path, this isn't the game for you.

All in all, this game feels more mature in it's writing and the voice acting is even better than before, so I can easily recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
