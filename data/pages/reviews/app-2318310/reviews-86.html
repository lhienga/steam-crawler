\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is ludicrously well written for what it is. Surface level, it's funny as ♥♥♥♥, and a beautiful subversion of more things I can count. It's also one of the heaviest things I've ever read (listened to?)

I'm saying this without a single shred of irony: this might be the best writing I've read in years. It's on the same level as 'Less Than Zero' by Bret Easton Ellis - writing about such absurdly dark topics while never coming off once as just edgy, angsty, or emo for the sake of it is one of the hardest things to accomplish. It never feels grim-dark or gratuitous once - so far, it hasn't covered anything without a solid reason behind it.

I have no idea what team of nutters put this out, and how it executes one of the nichest concepts I've ever seen to a ridiculous level of quality. I bought it this morning after seeing that one cop scene, thinking I was just in for a dumb silly take on visual novel tropes; what I found was an actual work of art that has emotionally resonated with me and my own life more than... almost anything, to be honest.

Reminder, this is a game where a girl (spoilers) starts peddling crack to avoid doing her school's community service hours. And, in another route, becomes homeless and starts selling herself on MySpace for drugs, with one of the most accurate depictions of the reality of 'escort work' I've ever seen, coming from someone who has personally seen how that work corrodes people. And, generally, just has more edginess than I could ever quantify. I don't know how anyone could've created this, while still making a genuinely affecting piece of art.

It's bewildering and brilliant, and has made me laugh out loud several times. Highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
