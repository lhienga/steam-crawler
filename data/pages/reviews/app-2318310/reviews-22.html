\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy review mainly echoes the same thoughts I had about the original game. The main problem I have with this sequel is that it fixes none of the blatant issues the first game had (sibilance, awful interface), and commands a far higher price point - though presumably that's because it's just been released. Because of this lack of care about quality, it makes me far more hesistant to recommend the game at this price (I'd wait for a sale below £5), and comes across as the developer wanting to double dip on the popularity of the first game, instead of making a bigger & better sequel

What I do like more about The Re-Up compared to the original is that the storylines are more linear and less abrupt. The games take about the same time to complete, but The Re-Up has far fewer endings than the original game, which I'm considering a good thing. Endings come several minutes after your final choice, giving the writing & story plenty of time to explore the fate you've chosen for Nicole

For more feelings on the game in general, here's my original review of the first game, all of which still applies to this one:
