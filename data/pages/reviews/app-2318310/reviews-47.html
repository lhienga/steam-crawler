\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked the original Class of '09, then you have no reason to skip The Re-Up.

Think of it as an expansion pack that adds a whole slew of new characters, stories, and endings while also giving you more of the original cast that you've come to know and loathe.

The only real downside, if there is one, is that The Re-Up somehow manages to feel even more bitter and mean-spirited than the original. Nicole's always been a scheming ♥♥♥♥♥ surrounded by freaks and creeps, but she really ramps it up in this one. There were a few moments even I started to feel bad about what Nicole and the rest of the chuckle♥♥♥♥s were doing, but then the next killer line would come along and have me laughing my ass off again.

If cringe comedy or shock humor isn't your thing, then you might want to skip this one. But if that kind of thing is going to scare you off, I have to wonder why you're even on a page for a Class of '09 game in the first place. These games love to make you laugh and squirm.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very short experience, this game is a satirical piece at its most ridiculous and a genuine commentary on society at its most serious. As a head up, you will NOT identify with the main character, and if you do, please seek mental care.
While you play as a genuine sociopath, the game does very good at stopping you from getting attached to the characters, allowing you an overview of things happening and letting you see the commentary of this game.
The game makes use of various very graphic topics while never actually showcasing much gore, sexuality or other. These topics are all either implied, or used as jokes.
The Re-Up does also use topics some might not be comfortable with, however, such as Nazism, Overdosing and overt manipulation. If even hearing about those topics feels like too much for you, please, do yourself a favour, and do not play this game. If you are, however, someone who can endure these and can separate themselves from the character, those topics are used sparingly, and in ways that do not make you feel like a worst person much. Don't worry, this game won't make you role play being a Nazi for real.

Overall, I give this game a 7.5/10.
The Re-Up has very good parts, and parts that may make you laugh, or think ''Yeah maybe I can't fix her'', but where it's problems lie are in the very short experience, for a pretty decent pricing. You can do all 7 endings in about 5 hours, and even shorter if you read the text instead of listening to it. Additionally, you really have to detach yourself from the characters to play this game, and while this may be a personal gripe, I prefer really feeling as my character.
Game is still a short and sweet blast, and if you feel you can take it, I encourage you to do it\t\t\t\t\t
