\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Re-Up is EXACTLY what Class of '09 needed. The trailers and store page may say that this isn't a sequel, but it's 100% new content, so if nothing else, this is simply more Class of '09.

I wrote a review not recommending Class of '09 due to its poor use of mature content and lack of consequence in the story, its numerous routes railroading you into the same scenes over and over, and complete avoidance of aughts culture (in a game placed specifically in the aughts).

Re-Up has fixed all of these problems. The subject matter is still dark and in some cases deeply troubling, but characters are confronted for and face the consequences of their actions more often, including Nicole. While there are fewer endings, this means that finding them requires fewer treks through the content you've already seen, making this game way more interesting to complete.

The new dialogue (once again MASTERFULLY voiced by the entire cast) is now dripping with references of the era, finally adding some in-world evidence that this game actually takes place in the 2000s. On top of everything, new character art and some neat new scene styles add visual variety. The humor provided is top-notch (once again, massive shoutout to Kylar), the stories are fun to navigate, and overall this experience was MUCH better than the 'enhanced with some new content' version of the first game I was expecting to pay for. Great work you four-eyed, double ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWithin the first 5 minutes, I was already laughing harder than I have this entire week.

The art style is nice to look at, the characters are interesting, and the voice acting is absolutely amazing.

Seriously, I can't remember the last game I've played with voice acting This good. It's phenomenal.

Edit: I've now finished the game and reached all of the endings, including the secret ending. Here's my final thoughts.

This is one of my favorite VNs I've played. It... reminds me of the Panty and Stocking dub in the sheer crass, rude hilarity. If you know, you know.

I can't particularly think of any VN, or frankly another character, who portrays a high school girl more accurately than Nicole. The amount of abuse, self-hate, and just general rudeness is something pretty much everybody who's gone to a public high school has experienced before, whether it was direct at you, at a friend, as a witness, or you were friends with someone like that.

But now you get to play as that abusive, self-hating rude girl, and it can feel very nice.

I highly recommend this VN to anybody who's ever been to an American public high school. You might recognize some of the personalities the characters exhibit.\t\t\t\t\t
