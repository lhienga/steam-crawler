\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game with somewhat low expectations despite being a very long time fan of Lyle Rath's content, but I genuinely don't know why now because this is one of the best visual novels I've ever played - and I mean that without a hint of irony. The writing and characters blur the line between extremely hilarious to soberingly dark and serious, and it just works throughout.

Nicole and Jecka are the funniest and most toxic duo I've ever seen, but they're not one-dimensional like I expected them to be for a game like this, they feel like REAL highschoolers with some of the most scathing and razor-edged insults I've ever heard. Some of the other characters are purposefully one-dimensional, while some are their own distinct and likable (or unlikable) personalities. I can say with the utmost confidence that this has THE most realistic high school dialogue I've seen in any piece of media.

The only criticisms I have are that I wish there was a skip read text option, when I'd load the game from a save the text would start skipping on its own all of a sudden, and maybe implement some indicator for which choices the player has and hasn't chosen. But above all else, I wish there was more; and that's the best problem a game can have.\t\t\t\t\t
