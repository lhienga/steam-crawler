\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a little rough and buggy, I'm no fan of the always online as a mostly singleplayer/small group of rl friends type of player, and there's a lot of content to be added to make this what it clearly wants to be.

But there's really nothing like it. the setting itself makes it worth a look.

It's a lot of fun and at higher levels as your abilities grow it goes increasingly from "You're a steampunk refugee" to "you're a heroic Victorian adventurer" to "you are literally Johnathan Strange, with all the powers of the fae at your command and also a very dapper jacket." Firearms and later magic instantly become gamechangers and really feel worth the pain of unlocking them and then making sure you have the needed ammo and resource chains.

The crafting is fun if a little unintuitive at first, especially since a good deal of the gear progression only unlocks well into mid-game when tier 2 materials that aren't metals show up. But that's kind of a good thing as it encourages exploration and testing. The one gripe here is that that testing is often expensive, since if you make the wrong gun there's no way to break it back down and recover the resources used easily.

The crafting system also really heavily leans into the base building, and the requirement of furniture as "augments" to unlock crafting means there's a reason to furnish your house in a logical manner, especially once you start multiple crafting benches of the same type to streamline production.


Some things I'd like to see.

*better resource recovery to encourage exploring different materials

*slightly better tutorials for new players to introduced advanced mechanics before they're needed to progress

*slight improvements to combat, traversal, and UI, though these are currently ok

*more content accross the board, especially more poi loops, since the current 4-5 get old fast

*Materials to change costume colour as well as weapon colour

*more historically accurate clothing, especially woman's. We're at the tail end of the second bustle era here and there are everything from the old style shelf bustle to the new proto edwardian style, off the shoulder, square neck, and puff sleeves co-exist so there's a lot of room to move.

With men, the overall style is fairly static in these decades, but since the fae often dress in anachronistic styles, there's no restriction on making the more tailored 1830s-40s looks, or going back into georgian/stuart/elizabethan fashion. Especially in high end gear where magic is more apparent.

Anyway. great game, having a blast, keep at it devs! If you're willing to acknowledge this is an early access game and not a final beta test of a complete game, absolutely give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
