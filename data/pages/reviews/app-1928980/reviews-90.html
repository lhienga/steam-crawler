\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far I really do like the game, but as is to be expected in early access there are also problems.

Tldr/c. : It's an EA for <30€ with all the perks and flaws you'd expect.
If you want a finished Survival hit - wait for the full release.
If you don't like survival - this game propably won't win you over either.
If you really like survival and want something new that is rather good looking - go get it.
The Devs and Company are very active on the Discord server and pretty transparent regarding a lot of topics.

Let's start with the positives :
- The art and style of the Game is suprep. I love the design ideas for the different build pallets and crafting stations. The enemies and creatures are interesting takes. The world and skybox are beautiful.

- for Buildings so far there are seven different pallets, each with their own charm and unique style compared to the others.
- The crafting is no new invention of the wheel, different materials with diffrent stats that you can mix and match to your needs or liking. So even if two people wear the same set of tools or armor. They could have 'wildly' differing stats depending on the materials used.

- for now the story is not finished but the ideas presented are intriguing. The VO for Puck, a fae NPC, is very well done.
- the progression, with the major and minor cards is a good idea with promising potential.

Now for the negatives :

- Loading times ... for a game that is centred around moving through portals into other worlds and stuff, having to wait minutes every time you switch realms makes it kind of a slug. So far there seems to be no differenc between having the game on a SSD or a HDD, so gonna have to wait on optimization from the devs there.

- Combat is okay-ish, some enemies have very wierd movments and at least for now some varaites go for quantity over quality. Why fight two or three strong ones if could also fight 10 medicores ?

- Item and inventory management is a slight nightmare at the moment. I would love to be able to manage stacks and inventories myself thank you ♥

- the AI of NPC companions is also something that needs work. Giving them anything that is not a mele weapon WILL BE a mele weapon xD.
Also for the love of god stop using my high end materials to finish build blueprints you dumb ♥♥♥♥.

I will Update (hopefully) those points as major updates are rolled out.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, for an early access game this is incredibly well put together. I have only had one crash in 35hrs+ and no other issues.

To me it seems to combine the beauty of Fallout76, the base building of Arc and 7DaysToDie along with the exploration of the elder scrolls games (Oblivion/Skyrim etc). Coming across abandoned camps with small story snippets slowly fleshes out the world, secret portals to forgotten bases and varied puzzles. There is a bit of repetition in some areas right now but i fully expect this to disappear as the game develops and builds out its core asset library it is drawing from.

It doesn't hold your hand though and a few things are missed in the tutorials right now such as where to unlock specific newer benches etc. The guidebook allows you to see what each realm and difficulty combination will unlock for essence traders so it is not 'random'...more a carefully crafted progression path. It really brings home the feeling of a average person thrown into the fae realms and having to find their feet, see how everything works, crafting new equipment and learning how best to combine the resources they have access to.

I am very pleased with this game and cannot wait to see how it grows. The developers are responsive and are working on both QoL issues and engine feedback. I feel it already has its own unique look and feel and with time will grow into something quite spectacular with the obvious love that has gone into this art.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn inspired crafting survival game that falls short of delivering something truly new.

I enjoy playing it and I wouldn't say that it's boring but its easy to tell that the combat system was definitely not a very high concern in terms of gameplay. For me, this is a big deal because if the combat isn't fun, that means the grind isn't very fun.

For me, personally, the grind itself needs to be fun in games like this because that's where you spend the most time. I dont enjoy just looking forward to the gear I'm after, I want to enjoy getting there too.

The lore that's scattered throughout the world is cool and fun to find and the characters are interesting enough as well as the dialog and writing.

The realm system is cool and the world's are interesting but you can definitely tell that they are randomly generated.

The pathing for the AI is atrocious and rips me right out of the immersion which is a shame. Very unnatural and robotic how everything walks around. As specially your AI companion.

The dungeons feel like you gave a computer a math equation and told it to just generate a simple path with a beginning and end.

Graphics are 5/10 which is shocking because there are much bigger survival games with much better graphics.

Gameplay is also 5/10 because, besides the realm crafting, it's average in terms of fun and creativity. I'd like to say that the building system is way cooler than any other game I've played but that would be a lie. You get essence, build your work station and upgrade your equipment with special enchantments and things of the sort. I think they tried to do something different here but definitely fell short.

5/10. An average survival crafting game that needs alot of work to really draw me in completely. I'd rather play something like valheim at the moment but I will continue playing this to see where the end game sits.\t\t\t\t\t
