\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3/11/2024 - Initial Review - Negative

Come have a seat. Lets talk about Nightingale, a new online open world survival craft game in Early Access.

Now some might notice the qualifier of "online" was used where normally it isn't.

This is but one of the factors that will eventually level this game. Its not that you can play online...No... Its HOW they went about it. As has been seen already there have been some major bugs, long wait times, etc. etc. when it comes to games that require connecting to online servers. Nightingale is no different. Players complained and the devs heard and.... well promptly went all or nothing and said okay we can make an offline mode. This still doesn't fix anything except for that small minority that wish to truly never play with anyone. First, lets take a look at how this works. Nightingale uses an online play model not to dissimilar from an MMO, yet its not an MMO. You always have to be online, yet as far as we can see there are no microtransactions, no reason for it other than the multiplayer aspect. Well why not use P2P or a direct connection and the STEAM API to connect players? That's a great question and one I really wish I knew the answer too. The only reasoning I can make for why this is a thing is because Nightingale allows your friends to connect to your Respite while you are offline and the other options would require you to be in game for your friends to visit your cooperative area. I think this is going be something that is both unique and wonderful and something that could potentially sink this game very very quickly.
The concept of losing your progress or not being allowed to play because of server maintenance is really not acceptable anymore. Its the reason most games of this nature have gone direct connect or single player. It also means that when this game is End of Life... anyone that still wants to play it... well they wont be able to or at least not with friends once the offline mod is initiated. So lets just say its multiplayer with some serious caveats.

Next lets break down the simple stuff and give our brains a rest shall we. The graphics are great the environments are amazing as far as I have been able to see. The only thing here is that they feel limited. It doesn't really feel open world but more like open tile, as that's kind of how the card system works. Its not a bad thing but its not a great thing either. It just is how it is. The sounds and music are great and really help the world feel more alive.

Okay so lets get back to the beast topics again... Building limitations. Yea we don't even have to go into this one really. Its a non-PVP game where you are forced to play on servers that have building limits, and they don't always reflect the accurate numbers they should. For those of you that don't know that means with the limitation on building of say 300 items that you can only build 300 items. Seems straight forward. Except that if you need to build something that is required for the augmentation of your stations, you will obviously think that you can delete an item and it will lower the count. Not always. This limit is also really harsh to consider when you have players that play solo with an item limit and then say you have the max in Nightingale which is 6 players, they have the same building restrictions. So you are technically handicapped by having more players because that's up to 6 times the loot and 1/6 the building capacity, unless you all of course go back to your own perspective Respites, then meet up again at someone in your groups, the proceed to go to the next place... I think its still kinda foggy how that works. Overall, this is not a great idea and goes back towards the online play aspects of having everything be server based. I get why they do it, but it doesn't help the gameplay mechanics at all and actually severely hinders long term play. As for the rest of the building system. Its really just the same standard building that a lot of these types of games have. Nothing great, new, or amazing. The mechanics of the benches is neat but given the building restrictions it ends up being a headache more than a blessing for most people playing in groups.

Difficulties... This is just a broken concept. I can start a game on easy while my friend can start a game on extreme. We can still play together. Everything about this concept is just broken and there is a really good post
about it by a user on the forums under a thread titled "Development Priorities and Message from the LORD" along with some other interesting topics. So we will leave that one right there as its already been discussed at length.

Crafting in this game while complex just does not feel very rewarding. Users have reported on several posts that crafting needs a preview of whats going to be crafted because materials used in the crafting are supposed to effect the outcome of the crafted item. Although those same users not that some end game gear is not always better than items they crafted early or mid-game as well. Making complex crafting systems like this is tricky and require a lot of balancing. There is definitely a learning curve to a lot of the crafting systems, augments, etc that might take some users a bit to get the hang of.

Development. This is whats really hurting this game. The prioritization of some aspects over others. They drop everything when players Respite areas reset but put off updates that were blocking others from progressing past the tutorial areas. Server locations are constantly bugged or broken. Again this all points back to the online server model. The developers did put out a post on the development priorities. It honestly feels really lack luster and underwhelming given what players have had to go through since the games release. Personally I went through 9 characters before the update came out allowing me to get past the tutorial areas. Even from the time I reported the issue I found that players had the same issue since day 1 and it was 13 days later and they announced that they would be pushing the update back to come out with the regular update. Way to keep players from playing your game. Honestly the development priorities differ from player priorities, players want to play the game simple enough and the developers... I have no idea what they are thinking. They give explanations that sound good on paper. But play the game, read the reviews that are in depth, and you will find that there are some major game breaking aspects of this game that really make it not feel rewarding or worthwhile.

I do have hope that this game will turn around but given the fluff responses and mismanagement of the prioritization of the issues... I wait and weep for what this game could have been.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCons:
- it is permanently online and has ATM no offline feature
Why? I have two waiting periods just to get to another realm
(Portal building 30s - 5min and then 2min - 30min the transportation itself)
I am using an NVME and have a 40Mbit network connection (works fine in all other games)

- quite repetitive (the Fae-Towers and "puzzles" are interesting at first but quickly turn boring)
The first time I saw the towers and the puzzles I was amazed (so much potential and it was quite well made)
After about 2h in the game I sadly noticed that I have seen all the challenges

- the way you have to handle resources is just too much
Why are there so many types of the same resource like stone or ore?
I can refine these base materials and get the same number in refined materials only with the addition that it is based on
XYZ-base material (i.e. Ingot (Copper) or Ingot (Tin) ....).
But they do not mix when I am making some tools, I can only mix them when I am building something.

- sometimes it is a plain walking simulator
Walk there to get something .. solve the same puzzle / tower challenge you just completed 20min ago.
Visit person XYZ on that Realm .. go to your portal and open the realm .. wait .. wait some more .. walk to person.


Pro:
- wonderful world and interesting effects with the realm cards
The world is wonderful, nice graphics and solid FPS, the world is open and differ from realm to realm

- relaxing

- base building is awesome
the base building is straight forward and works really well

- combat system just feels right (at least for me)
it works nice and has the right feeling for me

Conclusion:
At the current state of the game it has a long way to go.
The core of the game was made quite well and now it just needs some fine-tuning, maybe some more diversity on the puzzles or a offline mod would go nicely.

Please look into the offline mod, in a game where I have to wait for the portal to open only to wait for the actual loading screen for the realm .. only to get some ore that is not on my realm .. I mean .. I hope you see the flaw of that yourself.
Offline or the ability to create own hosted worlds would boost the comfort of this game enormously.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is at about a 7/10 right now with the potential to be a 9/10 if they keep working on it. Crafting is complicated AF, which I am ok with, but I could see how it would not be everyone's cup of tea.

Absolutely the best combat I have seen in one of these 1st person survival games. The weapons have weight to them and you will knock enemies over. It is incredibly satisfying to slam your axe in the head of a bound enemy and then bring it down on them while they are scrambling to get back up. I normally hate combat in these games, but it's a blast in this game.

Realm hopping is cool but get's a little samey after a while. They need to add more modifier cards to mix things up. Maybe some more biomes too.

Great game but you may want to wait for some patches. I made it to the watch and have decided to hang up my umbrella until they fix and add some stuff, but I look forward to the continued development of this game and I plan on coming back to continue the ridiculous crafting grind in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

Cool ideas and neat systems, hidden under layers of clay
Preface
I like the early access journey. I think it's neat to see how games change and evolve. If you don't like this journey, this game is probably not for you as of the time of writing. I've had a few small bugs (mostly connection blips and Nvidia related graphics crashes). Some folks have had worse. In spite of that, this game has hooked me really hard. It's got working systems! It's fairly stable! I like the core loop. But it's also $30 for an early access game. That might not be your cup of tea and that's fine.

Also, I've been fairly impressed by the communication from Nightingale's dev team. This is under active development

The Core Loop
The world has ended, kind of. You've found yourself in the Fae Realms with no resources and a Fae by the name of Puck who has decided to help you out. And by help you out, he tells you to build a new life and that you gotta run.

So after realm hopping for a bit, you find yourself in your Abeyance realm, building a home base that serves as refuge, crafting hub, and resupply point. Pretty soon, you've made your base gear. You start realm-hopping to get new recipes, new resources, and an in-game currency called Essence. Harder realms have rarer resources and recipes, but are more challenging.

The core distinction with Nightingale is gear doesn't drop and your character doesn't level. And that feels weird at first! To survive at each new level, you'll need to constantly upgrade your gear. You rapidly realize that you need to be a pack-rat, storing materials so that you can make that next-level thing to make you a bit stronger. So you are incentivized to go from a small basic cabin to a proper house with multiple crafting stations and lots of storage to support your crafting needs.

Crafting
Really, the crafting is more akin to Minecraft or Factorio, where you start with raw materials, like hides, plant fibers, logs, ore, and stone. You refine materials and build intermediate parts to assemble the final thing.

So if you build a rifle, you start with ore which you transform into ingots, which then get transformed into the action and the barrel. Separately you turn logs into lumber into the stock. You take fiber or hide to make a wrap. You then assemble the wrap, the stock, the barrel, and the action.

But it's more complex than that. Every material has a stat distribution. So, for your rifle, it's about figuring out which materials give you the best range and ranged damage. For a pickaxe, maybe melee damage, durability, and strength. The point is that what you build is your own from mining ore to the finished product.

At first, this sucks. But as you learn the systems, you have a moment where you build something that is significantly better than what you built before. And suddenly combat and resource gathering are much easier.

Combat
Think Skyrim. You swing a weapon. It connects with an enemy. If you hit the head or heart, you'll do more damage.

Most significant combat happens outside of your Abeyance realm. You hop into a place, kill some animals / humanoids, take their resources, and continue.

Realm-hopping
There are three Biomes (Forest, Swamp, and Desert). Then there are Major cards that determine the version and look of the Biome, as well as the difficulty. Finally, there are Minor Cards. These generally have 2-3 net positive benefits and 1 slightly negative benefit. You play the Biome and Major cards to procedurally generate a realm. You can then play a Minor card to augment the realm.

Realms of a particular biome are fairly similar at the moment. Each realm has little challenges that unlock recipes and Essence. To get new Major cards, you progress through dungeons in your Abeyance realm, locked behind gear-score barriers.

Quests / Challenges
Realms have little challenges as well as dungeons that contain multiple challenges. At the moment, these are pretty basic. But, since things are procedural, there's plenty of room for these worlds to get richer and more complex.

Additionally, there's a bit of a story to pull you through the game. I've enjoyed the writing.

The barriers to enjoying the game
- At the moment, the UI is not great. It's hard to grok the systems that make the crafting a joy.
- Missing some QoL features like crafting from storage. At the moment, you need to pull each ingredient out of storage, take it to the correct work station, and then select it.
- Repetition: The game needs 4-5x more challenges and POIs. It feels a little shallow at the moment. That being said, the nice thing with procedural generation is adding variety means that variety could happen everywhere.
- Connection issues

What the game gets right
The core systems were hard to build. I like them! There's still plenty to do, but the core is good and can be built upon.

I think I'm ok with the game's promise to say that if you're willing to watch this grow, that this will be a game that develops over the next year or two into something that is a great live-service.\t\t\t\t\t
