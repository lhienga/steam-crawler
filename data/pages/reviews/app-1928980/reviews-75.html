\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game, but I've been stuck with primitive tools the whole time, even though I unlocked smelting and woodworking. I don't have the recipes to make better tools. I built augmentations to put near the crafting stations, which say they unlock the recipes, but they only upgrade the stations to be able to make them Meaning I still need to unlock the recipes so my character can make them.

I got frustrated and looked it up, only to find that you have to complete the main quest line to unlock better tools, which takes about 25 hours to finish. This means another 15 hours or so for me, since I've been playing it like a survival game.

Additionally, there is no multiplayer until that 25-hour quest line is completed. This means likely a month or so before my best friend, with whom I play survival games, could play with me, as he has much less time than I to play games these days. Instead we will just play other games instead.

On top of that almost every play session has had or ended on a crash to desktop, while frustrating that I could have forgiven in an early access game. The 25 hour progression freeze and multiplayer lockout while being forced to play on laggy servers with long load times instead of locally strikes me as something that will not be quickly fixed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe ambience is superb, gorgeous ocean water and rain. Love the blueprint system that lets you add materials from any part of the entire build.

Progression is dull and repetitive. The core gameplay loop is opening new realms, completing all the map POIs, and trashing the realm to repeat the cycle. Repeatedly doing the same puzzles or finding the Essence trader isn’t actually fun. If that is the goal for the devs, this game needs a mount to ride, because the Thin Veil card isn’t enough to carry all the traversing required.

Building feels awful only because some materials are insanely heavy. The game gives you quests to complete a NPC’s base, I don’t ever, way too much work, and I don’t need the essence that badly.

The seamless multiplayer worlds are a great, but none of my friends actually play anymore because of how boring the game is. We’ve only been playing for 10 hours and it feels like we accomplished everything. The devs have a great idea and great game engine, but it’s far from being a great game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is a diamond in the rough in its early access phase. After logging 135 hours of play, I think it offers great value for its price point. And the game will only get more content as time goes on.

Visual Appeal: Obviously the game shines with its beautiful graphics, which is likely a major draw for many players. The procedural generated landscapes in sprawling worlds are eye candy. If you’re someone who enjoys exploring visually stunning worlds and building a home with epic backdrops, Nightingale will not disappoint.

Combat System: Combat is straightforward yet satisfying. It rewards skilled players who leverage the mechanics more than those who simply button mash. The variety of interesting creatures, each with unique behaviours and weaknesses that adds to the combat experience. Some of the bosses are quite strong, with challenging mechanics.

Resource Harvesting and Building: The core game play loop of resource harvesting, crafting, and building is addictive. The need to acquire specific resources from different realms keeps the game engaging. The building system is really user-friendly. It has no issues with 'clipping', so you won't see that annoying red outline when trying to drop the last piece of your epic mansion because it's an inch too close to something else.

World Manipulation: You can influence realms on a global scale, altering their look and mechanics. This adds depth to the game play, allowing for varied experiences in different realms. That has huge potential, but I'd like to see them do much more with this mechanic in the future. Pro tip: This ability allows you to tailor your home world. If you plan to harvest a lot of lumber for a building project, modify your realm to increase lumber production. There's a lot of power there if you leverage it.

Challenges and Areas for Improvement:

The game is not hand-holding, requiring players to figure out some aspects of the game. The crafting system, particularly the use of 'augments,' could be explained better. Being unfinished, some icons are placeholders, which can be a nuisance. And the UI could use a quality of life rework. A lot of bad reviews I'm seeing are that the game requires an online connection, but the developers are already working on an offline mode. The devs responsiveness to community feedback is a promising sign for the future development of the game. Also, the game needs the ability to craft from chest inventory, which the devs are working on as well.

Conclusion:
In its current state, I'd recommend Nightingale - it's more than worth the investment for those who enjoy crafting, building, and exploring beautiful and dangerous landscapes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have 30+ hours in the game at this point and would recommend it as a purchase. I have played a number of survival-based games and find this to be the most unique experience by far. Of course there are going to be some rocky points, we're in early access overall. I do feel as though many of the negative reviews seemed to forget this point. I waited for some time before I actually purchased and began playing the game given some of the reviews up until then, but I'm very pleased with my purchase. The progression is unique and varied and so far I enjoy the complexity and unique crafting system. There are a few areas that need some love such as the AI on the companions and those moments when the game decides it should abruptly become night. All in all, I really look forward to seeing what the devs bring to the game as time goes on - its beautiful and unique and certainly worth the investment.\t\t\t\t\t
