\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really, really want to like this game. It's got some amazing potential, especially the crafting system. But it has some major issues that are killing it for me. 1st off, and most important to me, is the fact that you have to be online to play. I would love that to be an option, but the fact that its required causes problems. Any slight drop in connection boots you back to the main menu. Loading times can be kinda atrocious. The second big issue I have is the absolute garbage that is the NPC companions. Their only real use is as an easy revive if you go down. They can't use ranged weapons, and its a toss up whether they hit anything with whatever melee weapon you give them. They arent effective as extra carrying capacity because they throw resources into every unfinished structure you pass, regardless of whether its yours. Theres no prompt for it either, they do it automatically without even a notification. Last big issue I have is the fact that any base you build has a solid chance of just up and disappearing. I've been lucky so far, and only had a chunk of my base disappear rather than the whole thing, but now I don't want to play and risk losing 80 hours worth of progress. Again, I really want to like and enjoy this game, there's definitely a lot I do like as is, and I understand its early access. But if the game is so unstable I can't rely on my stuff still being there when I come back to my base, I'm not gonna play it. If the devs fix these issues, I would be more than happy to come back to the game, but until then I can't recommend it.

Edit: 30 hours later, I'm at the endgame. The devs seem to have solved the base issue for the most part, and they get extra points for being able to get the lost bases back for people. Still no update on an offline mode, other than that its coming, and no update at all on the npc problems. That being said, I'm changing my review to positive because after some more thought, for what is essentially an open beta, the game is solid, and quite a lot of fun up until the endgame. There's not much content to be found there yet, but the potential is there, and I can't wait to see more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I saw the trailer, I was hyped.
I was so excited to explore, go between realms and have a great time.

I bought Nightingale with alot of hope.
Within the first hour of gameplay, I realized my expectations and hopes fell very, very short.

The graphics were fantastic for the realms themselves.

The gameplay was pretty flat.
There wasn't much direction, complete quests given, jump into another portal, complete next quests, jump into another portal. Rinse and repeat.

The trailer had mentioned that you can unlock the biome and realm cards. There was no unlocking needed during my time playing. Perhaps it was in the tutorial stage. However, it would have been nice to have a sense of purpose WHY i'm in the realm even from the get go. Is the purpose to explore an abandoned building with enemies all over the place? Is it finding it in random corpses? No! you walk up to a realm machine that has them already unlocked.

What is the point of that?

Hey - you approach the enemy for the first time and ALL IT DOES is bounce around. Doesn't attack you, (game doesn't teach you how to fight which is fine) but there's no reason why I should care about collecting meat, bones and skinned flesh if all i'm doing is jumping to another portal..

If I were the devs, in the first hour, it should give you the full picture so that you can make an accurate judgement of whether or not you like their game. I should know within the first hour of gameplay if it's good s**t or not. Past two hours, it's too late to refund and I'm told by some sources THEN it ramps up. That's far too long to spend aimlessly collecting stuff for no purpose other than to build simple, simple things.

Are there inventory limits? Nope.
Do items stack? Yes. No need to be careful of how many items you're holding even though you can hold only about 50 individual items.

The only thing "survival" about Nightingale is the fact that the weather will try and sometimes succeed to kill you. Hot deserts have a condition where you can overheat. Making being in the shade almost all the time necessary.

Every time you go into a portal, you are given little to no information about the biome you're entering. Enemies? N/A. Biome weather: Hot/Cold. Even past the first hour, how are you to prepare yourself with weapons, ammo and such with NO background. You might as well scrap the realm and biome cards and throw me head first in a portal.

I refunded it because I'm disappointed this wasn't better.
They should have worked on this more and maybe waited to launch this in early access later.

Do not go through the portal, keep your $43 CAD.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a bad game for the money. Even at this early stage. Everything worked fine on my system, I have run into one incident of frame loss that lasted a few minutes and I have crashed to desktop about 3 times. Other than that, the game functions very well.

There is a ton to do in the game. I thought I would regret choosing the desert biome for my abeyance realm but there is still plenty to keep me busy.

The game has a very long introductory phase that I am still working on after all these hours. During this time I don't think you can meet other players. Still, It is not like your standard tutorial. I disklike most tutorials and still find this game to be very fun and challenging so far (I set my difficulty to 'Hard'). And thanks to the companion system you are not totally alone which really helps during the toughest parts of the game.

It is very cool travelling through this game with all of the victorian-era high tech and mysterious fae beings and workings. I'm still having fun and it will only get better when I can play with other people after the introductory phase.

I recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
