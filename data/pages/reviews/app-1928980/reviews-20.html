\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an excellent, currently very under-rated, Survival/Crafting game.
I feel that it's currently under-rated because a lot of people who have no experience with the genre but jumped into it with Palworld and/or Enshrouded are giving their reviews (neither of those games really being survival/crafting in the first place).

The Pros of Nightingale:
  • Beautiful and engrossing atmosphere
  • Great music and sound design that really helps the immersion.
  • The combination of the Major/Minor Card system and procedural generation really works well, and extends playability.
  • One of the best crafting systems in the genre that I've seen in quite a while. Though the complexity this creates may be a downside to people who like things to be simple.
  • Really solid building, and it's not voxel-based (sorry Enshrouded, I really don't like voxel-based building. Rotating pieces is something I don't just expect, I require it).
    Of special note is the sheer number of different building tilesets (styles). Nightingale has more variety in that area than any other survival/crafting/building game I've ever played.
  • Interesting Lore - The whole fairy realms and Fae lore of the game which you uncover as you progress is quite interesting, if you take the time to read it.
  • Highly responsive development team - The team has responded very quickly to critical issues and also does a very good job of communicating with the community, listening to community feedback, and and providing insight into their priorities and roadmap.
  • Good enemy variety - For the genre, Nightingale has a really decent spread of different enemy types, including fabled and elite enemies. Considering there are more biomes to come, thus even more enemy types, it stands out compared to many other games of this genre.
  • Engaging combat dynamics - In the early game, it might seem like all there is to do is attack, dodge, and block, but once you get into all of the various options for magical charms and enchantments (castable spells, which are unlocked as the game progresses, there is a really huge amount of options for engaging in combat.
    It's a lot of fun, provided you give the game a chance, seeing as the majority of these are unlocked through progression.

The bad:
  • Very little voice acting, at least currently, so you have to do a lot of reading.
  • Online-only - Offline mode in the works, but for now you are required to connect to their servers.
  • Complexity of the crafting system may turn some people off (personally, to me it's a good thing).
  • Most outfits look like arse, and color/pattern is determined by material used. The game needs dyes.
  • Tedious inventory management - Some QoL features to help with this are on the roadmap and they can't come soon enough for me.

Overall, I am having a real blast with this game, and the potential it has to be downright amazing when it is out of Early access and released is massive. Don't let the haters scare you away if you like this genre. Half of the negative reviews have complaints that aren't valid, the people just didn't learn much of the game at all before posting knee-jerk reactions.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not ready, don't bother with it for at least another year. While the footage looks promising and all the promotional videos from the studio, this game needed another year of play testing and development before being released as early access.

Bugs: oh they're bad, bad enough to make you just not want to play anymore after the first few hours. Constant disconnects, UI not responding, crashes...them having to take stuff down for emergency maintenance, losing all your stuff and having to wait a week for a restore...it's bad....

The combat is just dumb, you stand around with your companions and just bash stuff until it dies. Mobs that charge you will run into you then shoot past you half a mile, making you chase them down in order to hit them again, but by the time you get to them they've charged at you and shoot past you half a mile...rinse, repeat. The combat is clunky, the animations worse.

The accuracy of ranged weapons is abysmal, you have a little dot in the middle of the screen where you expect to be aiming while in 3rd person, and in first person have aim down sights. Well, the projectile will be off to the left significantly from where you are actually aiming. The same problem exists with melee and third person. The hit lands wildly far from where the reticle is on the screen, forcing you to sort of guess and estimate where the hit will land.

Graphics are horrible, not sure how people think any of it is any good. The animations are horrendous, the character models are pixelated and very dated looking. I guess if you're just looking at the sky or at stuff from a very far distance the graphics might look ok, but overall it's disappointing.

Mechanically, there are so many bugs and so many systems that don't make any sense, nor are they explained. For instance build limits will display at the bottom of the screen when in build mode (one of 2 build modes) - the numbers showing the limits are wildly off, and it's not explained that one set of limits is based on objects on connected foundations and the then other limit is the number of connected foundations forming a "building". None of the counts towards the limits are accurate and will fluctuate drastically depending on what you're doing.

The crafting system is probably the worst thought out I've ever seen. I've been playing MMOs and survival games for 20 years now and the crafting is just horrible. Each workbench can have more recipes unlocked by adding other augmentation tables next to the station. The workstations can only be affected by a certain number of augmentation stations and there's no way to tell which ones near by are affecting the station. Also, these augmentation stations will affect workbenches that make no sense, for instance, why would a grinding wheel augment a sewing station to add recipes to it?

Another note on crafting, you have to make all the sub components for each finished item, but there's no way to keep track of what you need to make and where to make it unless you write it down. Also, since you have to make each sub component, it ties into the ridiculous system of adding recipes to each station mentioned above. You will have to sit there and move augmentations around through trial and error to get a recipe to show up on a workstation so you can get the sub components created. Not only that, but there are so many different variables that go into it, you have to amass and refine so much material then sort through all that material to figure out what stats will be added to the final product based on the stats added to the sub components. It's overly complicated and really a turn off to have to spend that much time and energy to make something that will end up obsolete fairly quickly.

There's no indication on how to get higher "level" gear or make it, there's nothing in any of the descriptions on any of the raw materials that will suggest that you are actually making something content appropriate for your progression. You end up wasting a ton of time and resources finding out that you're making lower "level" equipment.

The good/ok:
The concepts for realms and traveling to them is very unique and I really liked the idea, even being able to reset a realm to get different things like mobs or resources. You can also customize different aspects of each realm, such as being able to gather more resources, hunting, combat tweaks, survival tweaks, etc.

Building is fairly decent, it's not as bad as Valheim especially in terms of performance. Once you get to a certain number of objects close by in Valheim performance tanks, not so with Nightingale. It's fairly easy to build and you can put something decent together without spending ungodly amounts of time building and tearing down like Valheim just to get it right.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was excited to try Nightingale when it came out in early access, but it didn't live up to my expectations.

First off, the game doesn't explain itself very well. There's no good tutorial, so you're left figuring things out on your own, which can be frustrating. Plus, when you're trying to craft stuff, it's hard to know what you're making because there are no pictures to help you. This is especially tough for people like me who have trouble with reading or spelling. It feels like the developers could've easily fixed this.

The font they chose to use in the game is also really hard to read, and there are too many story notes cluttering up the screen. It's confusing trying to figure out what you're supposed to be doing in the game when there's so much going on.

It seems like the developers tried to overscope too much into the game without focusing on what makes it fun. And making the game always online when it's supposed to be a co-op game is just annoying. I haven't tried playing with others yet, so I can't say how well that works.

Overall, Nightingale has potential, but it needs a lot of work before it's worth playing. I'll keep an eye on it and maybe try again when it's finished, but for now, I'm not impressed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good: it's a mix of Minecraft, Roblox, Valheim, and probably Raft all blended into a Steampunk world with portals and a really interesting and inventive system of cards that decide the "realms" that you play within.

The bad: I've played lots of early access games and none had/HAVE the (1) constant network issues, crashes, game lags, and drops. The (2) weird hitbox for enemies, which sometimes sees you being hit by what should be out of reach enemies, and hitting enemies after you have passed through them. (3) your sidekick wanders off and sometimes revives you and other times gets stuck behind enemies and can't or won't come help. (4) Very repetitive bosses and huge scale up of hit points. There is a boss [Fabled Automaton Knight] that guards Herbarium Site of Power. I have been playing on hard mode since the beginning and have enjoyed the hard lessons and I've figured out techniques to fight, but this one seems quite ridiculous. I would go into detail, but really it's not important. I've wasted probably 4 hours to no avail on this thing. (5) getting into the Sites of Power was also ridiculous. You get reject by a power field, but it took me heading to the Steam blogs for advice on a feature that apparently was there and now has been removed, which told you that you had insufficient gear score. Really, y'all? I hate having to search outside of a game to progress. (6) Quite a bit of grinding to create a pyramid scheme of developing lower level resources to desired end product.

Overall: I will definitely keep playing this game as I am a sucker for grinding towards a goal and I enjoy open world concepts that let me build cool homes and estates. The repetitiveness of the game will keep me staying up late at night and being tired for work in the morning. I know I can't be too harsh as this is an "early access," but frankly the mechanics that I have seen will likely NOT change much over time. So I give it a solid 6 out of 10 for recommending to others. However, if you like grinding, are fine with weird hit boxes, love open worlds, and don't mind network glitches, I would say it is more of an 8.5 out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t
