\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really tried to give it a fair chance but think it's essentially a bit of bait and switch.
- All those fancy outfits in the trailers...you won't be wearing those until you reach The Watch. The mall in the sky.
- "Realms don't correlate to Earth biomes"... really? Yes, yes they do and they aren't that interesting.
- Once you visit a realm (Forest+Herb for instance), that's the only Forest+Herb realm you'll ever see. There's no regeneration and pretty much no reason to return after you did the landmarks in it.
- Endless waiting to have the server do what exactly? Makes for a sluggish experience when you're having to bounce back to previous realms or drop materials off at your Respite/base.
- Companions are totally generic and except for picking up some slack in fights or carrying some extra stuff (which they sometimes lose or put in campfires or building projects) aren't any fun to be around. They do teleport around a lot rather than get stuck but it's disconcerting.
- Augmentations...what a pile of crap. Who designed this mechanism should have their ass kicked. It's confusing as hell and makes no sense. It doesn't help that most of the icons are missing but even if they were there, can anyone explain how putting a trout on the wall adds a couple of food recipes or having a set of bellows adds a few clothing recipes to the sewing table?
- Recipes...I have unlocked everything from all vendors up to Gloom and 90% of the recipes are still unknown. I guess I have to go to the mall in the sky for those. Including most of the food, most clothes, most non-basic tools. Why can't you learn a recipe when you pick up a material?
- Endless minor variations of simple things, none of which can stack together of course. Hide: tiers 1, 2 and 3 of prey, predator, named creatures, bound creatures, leather crafted from each of the hide variations, felt from each of the hide variations, 10 kinds of thread from each of the 10 kinds of fiber, endless and pointless variations so you can craft endless and pointless variations of basic clothing with tiny stats differences. Same with ores, wood, meat, glass, paper, inks, potions, every damn thing.
- Combat...flail around with an axe and maybe you'll whack something. Oh yes, enemies have "weak spots" but basically you're using blunt tools to flail away just like in Skyrim from 2015 so good luck with hitting "weak spots". Sure you can use a gun but reloading time is long so you'll shoot it once and then switch to an axe anyway.
- Tasks need re-balancing - for instance, avoid aiding survivors when their project is building something because they always need 300 logs and 100 sticks and you'll get 25 essence...about the same as you get from cutting down that many trees.
- NPCs...I cannot remember the last time I saw so many wooden NPCs. The ones that do move are like robots. The rest just stand there. Where there is dialog, it's verbose and not especially useful. It's a drag to interact with them honestly.
- Enemy "AI"...it's about the level of Zombies and Potholes. Sorry, but it is.
- Rock-climbing...jeez. Needs work, like a lot of things.
- Even the procedural generation of realms needs more variation in the land forms. They look like they pull from the same set of a few maps and scatter landmarks on top. You've seen one forest, you seen them all.
- for all the "steam-punkiness", there's no building your own contraptions. Now THAT would be a cool game where you can jank together a steampunk airship or factory.

What's good about this game?
- I think the variation in generated buildings is great.
- Some of the animals show some imagination (looking at you, Icarus)
- Landscapes are pretty nice.
- Resource regeneration is a nice touch. I wish it wouldn't regenerate inside my base though.

Fundamentally, there's just too much wrong with the game to make it really fun. I don't expect these issues to be improved that much during early access because they took the game in a few wrong directions and those kinds of fundamental choices will never get corrected.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an interesting game but not a particularly fun game at this point. The realm card system makes the game a different take on the survival, exploration game. Not sure if Zelzany's "Nine Princes in Amber" was a starting point but if you are familiar with the premise of that series you will see the parallels.
Reasons you might like it:
1. The lore and background and graphics are spot on. No complaints there. Graphics and writing team nailed it.
2. Sound design is pretty good. Only one NPC is voice acted, Puck - your guide, and they nailed the delivery on that.
Reasons to avoid:
1. Whomever designed the UI apparently thought their task was to make the UI as intrusive and complex for no reason as possible in order to hinder the player at every opportunity. I have seen this more and more in games as companies in order to cut costs drop UI/UX development on the game code developers and let them flounder at it.
2. You have a building game and the building system is really, really poor. It is locked, seems about 22.5 degrees, to a grid rotation and to compound that the building grid doesn't align with the world grid so trying to dovetail stuff together is an exercise in frustration. Exactly the same issues that are in other recent games, Return to Moria comes to mind. My experience is that after building my base, it randomly and spontaneously decides to disassemble portions of itself.
3. The animation for your character is pretty poor and not fluid at all.
4. Combat could be described at best as mediocre especially with the disconnect between the aiming point presented to the player and where the game actually thinks it is. Even worse if you try to use 3rd person view. NPC AI is pretty much straight line attack with no real intelligence demonstrated.
5. While they did add a keybind system after the alpha testing it still doesn't always work and the hint interface doesn't present accurate information to the player. For instance the building option to raise or lower objects.
6. No accessibility options, other than the keybinds mentioned above, just a placeholder saying it is coming. Which is a not so subtle way of saying that gamers with disabilities are second class citizens and instead of building in support from the start they might get around to it - which would not surprise me at all if that just got dropped eventually.
7. You character is apparently a teen-aged male shrew that must eat their body weight in food or starve to death. Literally minutes after eating something your character starts mewling and you are warned you are starving and their health starts to decrease. It can be somewhat tanked at higher levels but at lower levels it is fatal. Pretty sure that people don't die from not eating a snack every 15 mins.
8. Inventory management is pretty much non-existent. They couldn't even implement something as simple as sort by...adding to it that the same icons are used for items and some are just a generic N icon (I would give them a pass on that since it is early access if not for the fact they have should no real work in coming up with an effective inventory system).
9. It is pretty easy to get into a "Red Queen Hypothesis" scenario where all you time is taken up with just maintaining your stuff and you make no actual progress. especially if you only have a limited time to play. The really high cost to repair your stuff almost makes it less expensive to make replacements instead of repair stuff. Not sure if that is a subtle dig by the developers at our planned obsolescence industrial society or if they can't do math.

Great graphics, intriguing lore and world generation absolutely crippled by the gaming UI and gaming systems they developed. For not I would recommend taking a pass.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I had reviewed this yesterday, it would have been a thumbs up. I only get disconnected once every couple hours and in 27 hours of play have only had two critical issues (companion lost with all gear/just gone vanished all investment nullified).

For early access? I woulda still given it a yes. Its fun, there's a lot here to enjoy, and while there was certainly some work in progess stuff, it seemed to be going in the right direction.

Today's patch however has made the game unplayable for a stupid, easily avoidable reason if the people patching it had done even 30 seconds of testing.

Swinging a tool to harvest something damages your tool more than whatever you've harvested is worth (in terms of essence dust) meaning you can't actually keep your tools in repair anymore.

Apparently this doesn't matter for those players who have already blitzed to end game content, there's a "use your high tier essence" workaround. If anything this makes the situation worse.

This sort of short sightedness for something this basic kinda ruins my confidence in this team for the long haul. There's game breaking bugs at the moment, connectivity issues, progression blockers, etc. (which is fine, its early access!) but instead of addressing these serious issues they decide let's just crank up the repair cost to about 100x what it needs to be to make players struggle/grind more. And since there's no private servers, offline play, etc. you can't even tweak off the nonsense punishment mechanics (seriously, who wants to do a corpse run in a single player game? what's the point of that?). I tolerated it because the game was fun, but running around picking up grass with my bare hands to repair my tools is just stupid. It is no longer fun.

Its a real shame, because this game has lots of potential. But priorities do not seem aligned. I think this is a non-starter until there's offline mode and/or mod support to adjust some of the completely unfounded economic decision making here. Punishing everyone because some players found an exploit has to happen in games like MMOs but I am playing this game on my own, if I don't want to spend 50% of my playtime chafing against arbitrary costs I shouldn't have to. If this complete nonsense had been in from the start I'd have refunded it in a heartbeat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts ok. I was honestly disapointed. Ill deep dive a bit

GRAPHICS
Some parts good, some parts not. The grass and some of the textures dont look great compared to other games (enchanted has grass that looks amazing). However, the water is amazing. Best looking waves Ive seen in a game, even despite them not having foam. The ui also doesnt scale well on different screen sizes and seems clunky.

COMBAT
Havent gotten to guns yet but melee is ok. Enemy AI seems dumb and something about the combat feels off in a way I cant put my finger on. Maybe its the camera movement or stiffness of the animations idk.

CRAFTING
The UI is clunky so getting around took a second. Didnt seem like there was a way to pick up and move things last time played.

EXPLORATION
So far havent done a bunch. I feel like Im mostly trying to manage my energy and food and you die easily so havent really explored a ton. The lack of objectives isnt helping my ADD lol.

FINAL THOUGHTS
I feel like its missing something. Maybe feels unpolished more so then other recent survival games. The realm jumping is cool but it has no real progression or objectives. The setting is cool but without much in the way of NPCs or towns, its easy to forget. Ill update again later if I play more but having a hard time getting g into it so far. Its hard to recommend this over enshrouded, palworld and Ark.
