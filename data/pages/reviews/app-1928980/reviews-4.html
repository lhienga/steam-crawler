\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to really like this game--my wife had been looking forward to it for some time, and I haven't had anything to play with her in several years. So I really want it to be good.

It just isn't.

Don't get me wrong--I don't hate the game and it's not a bad game; it just isn't very good.

It's a survival and crafting game with some combat and story. But it doesn't do any of those 4 things well. The survival is very light, and I appreciate that because I'm not a huge fan of the genre, just a small one; but all you do is occasionally cook a few mushrooms and eat once or twice a day, plus sit on your bed once in the middle of the day to restore the rest bar. Truthfully, you could remove the survival mechanics altogether and no one would notice.

The harvesting portion of the crafting is terrible. It has a floaty feel that I just can't get into. You can hack at a tree for 5 minutes and make no progress, then change your aim point up or down by about 3 pixels and suddenly the tree falls over. But the wood doesn't go to your inventory--you have to go pick up each separate log. Rocks you hit once and a couple of bundles of stone pop out, but you can't see them because of the animated large chunks of rock obscuring your vision that you have to wait until they clear. You have a hireling who can go harvest wood or stone, and they will chop at trees or rocks...but then they don't pick up any of the logs or stone, so you still have to get those yourself. And then they weigh so much, you can barely move, so you talk to your hireling and learn they can carry so much more than you can--just give them everything that they wouldn't pick up. Crafting on the other hand requires the use of several different stations, and several different ingredients. But the ingredients are made from things that are made from things. The game doesn't tell you how to get the ingredients you need, you have to look at each of the crafting stations and work out "I make this over here, then I move the output over there, then that gets me the piece I need."

Combat feels like someone once described combat to someone else and that someone else tried to approximate it. Click your mouse to swing that weapon! Don't worry, the animation will eventually start! Your enemy has probably hit you twice by the time it does, but it'll go eventually! Fire that sling bow! Yep, you pissed off a wolf, now it and its 2-4 buddies will run up to you and jump on you! Don't worry about not having a reload button--just press the fire button again and we'll start the reloading animation. I'm sure you'll be fine while you wait!

And then there's the story. I love games with stories in them--my favorite studio is BioWare. But I just couldn't make myself care enough to read all the pages and pages of lore dumping. And what happened to NPCs with voices? I get that games with real acting cost more to make, but I sure miss having NPCs speak to me rather than hand me a piece of paper with their dialogue written on it. Except for your narrator, Puck. While I do enjoy the flowery language as a piece of the setting, and while the actor does a good job with delivery, I can't help but wish you could put that on 2x speed because it takes so long.

So I really want to like this game; and maybe these systems will be improved by the time they get to full release. But 12.9 hours in, I finally had to pull the plug and go play another survival game named for a guy who flew too close to the sun to wash out the taste of bad harvesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game, while not being perfect, is very much enjoyable. I am reviewing while keeping in mind that this is still early access and that things might change as updates are released. The potential here is insane. Is it perfect? No it is not. Is it still worth playing in my opinion? Yes absolutely.

Pros:

- The game is beautiful. The style, the art and the overall vibe is simply stunning. Visually it is impressive and really unique
in its steampunk/vicotrian style.
- It has decent combat with various weaponry to choose from, as well as magical abilities that can enhance your combat
efficiency. As somene who normally does not care about combat at all, I am actually having so much fun in this game. To
me it does not feel like a chore anymore.
- The story is interesting. It is not overwhelming. You can choose to read it all, or only read what you find interesting.
- Puck is my ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ favourite and he alone makes this game worth playing to me and I refuse to budge on this. Sue me.
- There is a lot of things to see and explore. Every world is based on your realm cards and effectively making the
exploration new in each realm type you make. It makes me WANT to explore, rather than making me feel like I have to.
- There is a ton of things to craft (not talking about building) and I enjoy that freedom of having a lot of items at my
disposal. The nitty gritty crafting of having to make individual component parts to larger recipies makes it feel very
impactful when you do craft something.
- The building is very detailed. Its got some decent variety already with some larger style types on main buildings. Furniture
has a ton of variety and options once you manage to unlock them through exploration or buying them from vendors in
game.
- It is early access. It has the chance to improve alot.
- The price is good. I think it is a fair price and I did not feel overcharged like I have with many other games I have bought.
- I dont feel like its an insane grind and that I can do things very much at my own pace.

Cons

- Some parts of the UI looks a bit.... lacking? It does not fit entirely with the rest of the style of the game just yet.
- The lack of voicelines makes the NPCs feel well dead. I am only invested in Puck (I love him) as his voice acting is
supreme. If they add voice acting like Puck has the other NPCs will really really improve the game I think.
- There are some very sudden frame drops on occasion that seem to appear for no obvious reason to me.
- It is a bit unclear sometimes where and how to find higher tier plants/trees. It simply says "found in the swamp" but no
other specifics such as if there are different colours to what you are looking for.
- Certain kinds of building styles are very hard to get and if you have in plan a specific house style you might have to
search a while to get everything you want. There might be style merchants but I haven't found any yet (please let me
know if I am wrong). The merchants I have found sell maybe one or two pieces of furniture or styled building. I want to
buy more than that.
- Sometimes rotating things in building mode is not functional. I have noticed walls and floors are an issue to rotate/flip.
- I there is a lottttttttttt of story and lore written down in text. Like a ton. You can choose to read it yes, but it would be nice
if there was a way to find perhaps records of these stories that you could play in your base (old time record player?) and
listen to while you craft and build.

Conclusion

I really enjoy this game. It is my favourite survival game as of now. The whole idea of the realms and the cards is just incredibly cool and well done in my opinion. The game is simply stunning and I usually never take screenshots of games but in this one I keep taking them and sending to my friends. There is so much cool ♥♥♥♥ to see and experience and every single new realm I make feels new and interesting to me. In my opinion, all my issues with the game so far stem from the fact that its not a finished game yet. If youre unsure, wait for full release. I understand that some players have experienced performance issues but I have not experienced any major ones apart from 3 crashes in about 60 hours of playtime. This is to me completely fine as I personally lost no progress from this, and again, this is less than I have had with some recent full release games. If you like survival games with a twist, this is definetly and interesting game to keep an eye on.
