\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs Early Access goes, this has been good so far. The foundations of a _really_ solid game are very much visible here. There's been an emphasis on QoL work so far that has been good, and the core mechanics for the construction and crafting elements of gameplay are doing well in terms of immersion. There is definitely room for improvement, and that improvement is _happening_, rather than being vaguely gestured at as a "roadmap for development."
The narrative interactions need more work, and do feel more than a little outdated, harking back to text-driven NPC conversations from the mid-2000's. I know that some people feel that cutscenes for NPC conversations slow gameplay and are distracting, but as a narrative tool, they would help with getting players invested in their characters, and the narrative taking place. Emotional investment and attachment, you know?
All that aside, this game has already put it's core mechanics in place, and promises to get better going forward. Absolutely worth the comparatively small investment, compared to other games in the space right now.
8/10, would bang.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're on the fence about diving into the world of Nightingale, let me assure you: don't let the early access status or occasional bugs deter you. This game is an absolute gem for fans of survival builders, steampunk aesthetics, and base-building enthusiasts.

I'll admit, I hesitated to purchase Nightingale despite hearing rave reviews. But one tipsy night, I found myself drawn into its universe, and I haven't looked back since. It's a testament to the game's immersive qualities that even in an inebriated state, I was captivated by its charm.

But perhaps the most compelling aspect of Nightingale is its sense of immersion. Whether you're crafting tools in your workshop or embarking on daring expeditions into uncharted territory, you'll feel completely immersed in the world around you. It's the kind of game that lingers in your thoughts long after you stop.

In conclusion, if you're a fan of survival builders or steampunk aesthetics, Nightingale is an absolute must-play. Yes, it's in early access, and yes, there may be bugs, but trust me when I say that the journey it offers is more than worth it.
