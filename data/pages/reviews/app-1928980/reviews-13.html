\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat concept and I'd love to recommend it, but can't in good conscience for now.
The biggest flaw is - as all the other reviews have pointed out as well - the servers and the need to always be online.

There are some great mechanics here - though occasionally ruined by a lack of good inventory system.
- How is there not an auto stash in nearby chests feature?
- How do like half the items have identical icons or lack items all together?
- The crafting screen could be designed better but isn't terrible.
- A search function in chests is severly needed - especially with the icon issue.
- I'm sure there is more, but those seem to be the biggest flaws imo. Sorting stuff shouldn't be 10% of your playtime.

Server issues also include exceedingly long loading times. I'm pretty sure they sometimes exceed 10 minutes.
Which furthermore makes traveling to other realms you have enchanced with specific cards a pain.

The graphical optimization seems to be a bit lacking and I'd like them to focus on that before a full release.

All those criticism are however fixable. And if they do get fixed, Nightingale might end up becoming a real hit.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3/10. The only redeeming feature of the game was a Developer's mistake which got patched out. What had the potential to be an incredibly rich and in-depth crafting system is now just like all the others. The portals only lead to the same boring maps so it matters not how we get there. Portal, door, loading screen. IDC. We have 3 maps, with variance to pretend like it's something new. (Minor Cards) The end game...like most games, consists of grinding for 'better gear' that you don't really get to use anywhere...as it's....The End Game. Oh, wow...I unlocked a Dauntless Pick that I can use running the same three vaults and show off at the Watch......eeeeww. Shinny. Get real. It's a boring grind with no purpose. There is nothing about this game that is truly 'original' enough, nor intriguing enough to keep the mind of an intelligent gamer.
If you want the Gaslamp experience...BUY IT. There is no other game that will do what it does for you. If, however, you thought this was 'something unique and different'.....it's not. It's grindy, and repetitive and the building is sub-par and limited....not to mention hid behind near-end-game. By the time you unlock them, there is not much of the game left and unlike other games...it's not fun to play for building as they have horrible building limits. After this game...I think I am done with EA. I'll wait till the dev's can figure out what they are doing so I don't waste another 200 hours like I did with this game, or others. Game Development has really tanked over the years and it's sad. So much lost potential.\t\t\t\t\t
