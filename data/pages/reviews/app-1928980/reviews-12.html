\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am playing through on an Xbox Controller to see how it goes. It is frustrating in the expected places like building and menu navigation. I am going very slow on purpose and have about 40hr played. This is a Grandpa-speed review.

Good: This game is fun. This game is beautiful. I like the theme and art style. I like being able to take my time and play at the speed I want. The crafting system feels endless. The difficulty is controllable and stays fun.

Bad: The Xbox Controller makes cycle on the quick items, ineffective to unusable during combat. A major point in the design and concept for combat. The gameplay is shaped to reward and drive fast progression and not grinding at low levels. I choose to hunker down in each world I opened and build to test as much and clear the map. This was very difficult. If you never stop moving forward you unlock materials and tools so advanced that the lower levels you left behind hold no value. If you stop to enjoy the world, do not use tools or weapons from more advanced levels because you can't keep up with repair. The goals that pop up to help teach you the crafting system need much more instruction. "Craft An Infusion" would be benefited by naming the workbench, or items needed on a checklist. With so many crafting stations and materials, more instruction is needed. Navigating the menus with the Xbox Controller is frustrating and convoluted such that it discourages one from spending the time to search for this information on your own. A final improvement could come from better notification from errors or server issues. "Server Not Available" serves to irritate players, whereas "Server Not Available - Planned Maintenance/ Update" give enough reason to keep players happier.

Early Release: Yep. There are some special moments. The followers need so refining...and finding. Some of mine have vanished for long periods. I have 4 copies of "Sam" at my base and each time my current follower vanishes and I return through a portal, I find a new "Sam," recruitable, milling around my base.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the premise, but the actual gameplay is full of systems that work against each other.

Your progression is gated based on gear score but gear score doesn't determine an item's effectiveness. Crafting gear forces you to choose between the appearance you want and the stats you need to be effective in combat. There are hundreds of different crafting materials but the vast majority of them are completely useless. You can decorate your house, but those same decorations determine which items you can craft at the various stations (which are limited in range and number of decorations they are affected by) requiring multiple copies of crafting stations and decorations and making it difficult to create an aesthetic.

Building is similar. You can build 100 different small houses in one realm with 350 pieces of furniture or decorations each, but you can't build one larger house with more furniture. There are a very limited number of building styles and they cost in-game currency to learn forcing you to choose between buying building pieces and gear recipes. By the time I did the grind for the gear recipes I wanted, I found I had zero desire to repeat it for the building recipes.

The game offers nothing that other games haven't already done better, and while it's Early Access, the utter lack of a cohesive vision or basic understanding of what people want from survival crafting games doesn't give me hope that the developers can turn this around.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is from 138 hours of play, and made during launch + first patch. This game has crazy potential. Overall, it's a good game, similar in many ways to Valheim with the capability to add more biomes, more enemy types, and endless portals to realms. That system alone should be applauded.

Pros:
- Solid mechanics base which is being improved with community input

- Beautiful graphics & eclectic style

- Interesting enemies & puzzles

- Crafting is nuanced and thought out

- Runs on systems pretty decently down to minimum specs

- Runs well on better systems

- Buiding is ok at current, and can be great with tweaks like better snatching, unlocking

- Devs are responsive and post often on the Discord about roadmap and updates, including hotfixes

Cons:
- Unfinished icons - this is especially rough, 30-40% of the items don't have icons. This ended up appearing lazy at best.

- Grind-y "Endgame" - the game is certainly in early access, but the progression gets extra grind-y towards the end. After getting access to the Watch, the best way to get T3 essence is to grind through the instanced dungeons (5-10 min per run in multi with a good group) to get 400ish essence, for which you'll need around 1100-1300 per item/item set, let alone final upgrades to crafting build items, so you'll need around 10000 to get a decent setup, and probably around 30K for almost everything + upgrades.

- Online only

Let's see where the devs take this. In about 3-6 months, this will be a game worth buying and playing with friends.
