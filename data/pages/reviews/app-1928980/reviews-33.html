\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is a weird game. It's sold as a solo/coop online survival game but it feels more like it's been modelled on MMO. Everything in the game is designed to take up as much time as possible, because there isn't actually very much to do otherwise. You grind away to get better gear so you can make better gear. That's the game loop. Crafting isn't fun. It's laborious, tedious, boring.

Combat is unsatisfying to say the least, just run is circles or spam a spell and repeat at the next fight.

Building? A very limited system with no room for placing things where you'd like, nope you have to use designated areas on the walls and ceiling.

At times the game looks beautiful. Amazing. Other times it's let down immensely due to the fact they treat the players point of view like a camera, and not a pair of eyes. This means there is often blowouts and blackness in situations where the human eye would not behave like that.

There is a lot that could go right in the development of this game, but as it stands just now there is a massive amount of work to get there and as such I cannot in good faith reccomend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really impressed with the concept of this game. I love survival games. But survival lover, don't get fooled: this game is not good or ready yet to be enjoyable.
I have been trying hard to play this game, about 35 hours on log, and my comments:

PROS
- A really unique setting and a wonderful artwork;
- Guns are awesome for survival games;
- Interesting concepts such as interchangeable realm cards.

CONS
- While the concept of realm cards for opening portals is great, waiting 2 to 4 minutes just to open a portal to gather some materials is horrible. Don't forget you have to come back to your "base realm" so add another 2 - 4 minutes of waiting!
- Also, because of the card system, you think there will be a lot of variations, but sooner than later it starts to feel extremely repetitive - open the portal to the "more difficult realm", gather similar resources, do the same easy puzzles over and over again.
- Combat feels like this game is really from the 1800s; is poor executed, dull, especially on melee.
- When enemies appear, your FPS will drop dramatically, making the combat even worse;
- Poor implemented combat and gravity effects: I fly away from the Gloom tower every time the maul monster hits me;
- Crafting system concept is interesting, but at the current state badly executed and overly complex: too many augments with too many outcomes makes you lost about what can be crafted or not and where to craft; also, the concept that you can use any ingot to build but the outcome on items will be different base on source material is interesting, but at the current state too complex and a drag to manage so many items made from so many variants.
- Mining and lumbering effects are weird and dull.

So, at the current state, I wouldn't recommend purchasing this game. I regret my decision to purchase it. And I am worried looking forward, because there are fixable things like load waiting times and poor FPS management, but the combat system and level design are core mechanics and to fix that, I'm afraid they would have to start from scratch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is super beautiful- the environments are stunning but I find it hard to recommend on the basis of the game play. The build aspect motivates progress, but anytime you build something you're unable to move it. This presents an issue as you're constantly expanding your space to accommodate the intricate crafting mechanisms. For whatever reason, when you break down an item to rebuild it elsewhere, you don't always get all the materials back and some of the materials require multiple levels of crafting to reach the final item. So for instance, if you want to move a work table, you'll need to break it down and re-craft the necessary materials, just to move it to another location? Why?

The realms are pretty to look at but it's a little bare bones in terms of exploration and npcs. You can't really interact with most npc's in the world other than to hire them and the people that do talk to you give you so much convoluted lore that it's difficult to follow. After nearly 50 hours of game play I still don't understand what the hope echoes are. And on that topic, when you release the hope echoes it gives you a full page of lore with no protection from whatever's happening in the environment, so many times you have to close the hope echo without reading it all to fight/ survive.

Bottom line Nightingale has a lot of potential, but I don't think it's been fully realized. There are still many bugs like, when planting seeds, only some of them grow, others never do. Some aspects are amazing like the realm inside a realm puzzles, and some are terrible like hired npc's terrorizing your builds. I cautiously recommend it as I enjoy playing co-op more than alone lol.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a complete disappointment! I was so excited for this when it was announced. Joined the discord, counted the days. then got slapped in the face. This game is so poorly optimized that nothing functions correctly. Server ping is so high that marbles and other projectiles take over 2 sec, to even collide with something you aimed at due to lag. The game becomes instantly frustrating as soon as you end the tutorial due to quality issues in the game worlds. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most ambitious games i played in a while.
Nightingale is classic open world survival in which you can craft and modify your own worlds. The crafting system itself is highly complicated and complex. Almost every piece of furniture matters. For example, if your sewing bench is in the vincinity of a candle (light), a fire (warmth), is build on a wooden floor and protected by a roof AND maybe there is a treasure map hanging right above it, you can not only work faster, it will produce higher quality products AND you will beable to craft explorer clothes because of the treasure map. Material itself is also taken into consideration. Products can look and behave slightly different depending on which items you used to make them.

There are items to loot and upgrade, infuse with passive powers or charms. There are magic spells to learn.
Most of the tools not only are for gathering stuff, but also serve multiple functions. A sickle can not only do slashing damage but also lets you do a dodge jump, the umbrella will let you float, the climbing hooks can let you reach high places etc...

Nighingale is a procedually generated infinite sandbox filled to the brim with convoluted systems and stuff that can be learned or upgraded. The game often time doesnt care if you figure out how to do something or if you are able to find the dungeon exit which makes it a special but often times frustrating experience. In that regard it reminds me of the late Elite:Dangerous. Lots of grinding, lots of discovery, all guided by the missions the player sets for her/himself.

That said the game is in "early" early access right now. There is network issues, bugs and glitches and almost everything in the game feels like its only the foundation for things to come.
Will be interesting to see where this game is in a year from now.
