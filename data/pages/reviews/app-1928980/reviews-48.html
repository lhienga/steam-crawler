\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis recommendation comes with great reservations. 100+ hrs into EA from day 1 so it's early EA with all the warts one might expect and maybe a few more. Was it ready to come out of the cooker, maybe not so much. Can it recover from the extremely unfinished state and some of the devs gameplay decisions? Remains to be seen.
PROS: This is a gorgeous game. What they have built, the ruins, that which flufffs out the story line, very nice. No lag for me but am only solo. The concept is novel and the story interesting. Puck's a hoot and well done. Critters so far are pretty nice looking, don't act goofy and do silly stuff like spin on their heads or fall over and otherwise look unprofessional. The music is nice if forgettable.
ACCEPTABLE: Combat is adequate if not a bit chaotic. It's a lot of whack-a-mole, dead, next? Some of the speed animations could use a little work but crazy fast hippos are a hoot. The fact enemies seem to have no collision is on the edge of cons. Puzzles though novel idea are boring after the third one and you will do dozens if not hundreds and that neatly brings us to ...
CONS: Don't get me wrong, I love survival games, all the things that happen between dropped naked and afraid to well developed strong survivor, lord over them all. NG though made/led by a team of pros with some real money behind them from Tencent just feels like they don't quite know what they're doing and who their audience is. If this was 4 guys in a garage then a lot more could be shrugged off and more slack given, but it's not and I worry that with its rough start they may not have the heart for the long haul. And it will be a long haul.
Rewards in this game are stingily given out. You meander around far too long in rags, living in a hovel. No need for immediate gratification, but a bit more would be nice. Building is interesting but their is a hard limit that makes it difficult to even make what you need solo, much less in a group. It might end up being good but right now (and even with a LOT of complaints) not a peep from the devs. It's a killer.
Crafting: Someone went to a lot of work to set up their system but then they just went ham and made the most confusing, overbloated, goofy system I have seen in 20 years. There are a few who love it to death but they are from the old days of OA and really get off on spreadsheets, lists, and do not want it to change. Everyone else suffers through it in various stages of exhaustion. It's mostly the augments but secondarily it's piles of mats that don't stack with infinitesimal differences and make for inventory nightmareville. Crafting from chests is coming, which will be of some small help. But it's just too much. Watch Skill Ups review. He speaks well to the game and its issues.
Last but not least is the actual game loop itself. It's EA so it's not complete, but it's pretty repetitive and if you have seen a biome once, you have seen it forever, X 8 levels X 3 biomes atm. That little squirrel on the wheel, that will be you.
Lastly, if you watch the trailer and think that is the game you will play you might be pretty disappointed. Since it started as an MMO and morphed into a survival game, that might be the cause. It feels more like live service and upcoming dlcs and cash shop kind of game than maybe a survival game as we know it. Is that bad? Well it does feel a little bait and switchy at times as in hour 40 I was still in rags hanging out in my hovel. Even now, being old and slow, am still dressed in better clothes that are only minimally holey. Where is that red dress with matching parasol and hat?
If money is no object and you understand where this game is, how EA works, go for it. If times are tough or your expectations can't be controlled, wait 6 months to a year.\t\t\t\t\t
