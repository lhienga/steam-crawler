\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLeaving my review to balance the negativity, as people either can't understand early access or like to buy into something that isn't what they're looking for and then complain.

This game has a unique setting which is what is likely the first thing to attract players, and the crafting is probably one of the most appealing parts of the game. A lot of people seem to be complaining about the complexity but I love it, although it is early access and there are ways to abuse the systems to make godly overpowered materials.

The combat at this point in time is honestly a little stale. There are some fun spells that can be added to equipment but after a while the game only challenges you by increasing the number of enemies you have to fight. There are some pretty entertaining puzzles though, and even after 100 hours I still come across points of interest that I haven't seen before, but you will see a lot of repeats in that time.

Performance wise the game runs very well for being early access. I played one of the playtests a few months before launch and there was some stuttering issues back then, but haven't had any really lag issues aside from when chopping down like 10 trees simultaneously or something like that.

I could probably complain about lack of content as I'm now at a point where there doesn't seem to be much reason for me to continue playing, but I've put a shameful amount of hours in since release and enjoyed all of them so I've gotten my moneys worth and I'm excited to see what is coming. Just hoping the bandwagon negativity doesn't kill off a potentially good game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is a crafting-survival game stuck in the Victorian era of the genre.
Where "modern" games of the genre has alleviated the loop of this game with QoL features like : unlocking recipes and 'technology' with unlockable tiers alongside world exploration, being able to craft & build from resources in your chest, to simply have a good balance of gathering time vs gameplay, etc...
Sadly, Nightingale has none of it and stumbles upon its mediocre loop.
If you can even get past the opening tutorial that is so tedious, long and boring; you will find a game that requires way too much gathering, with you being consistently overweighted, for so little outcome.
Truthfully, compared to Valheim or Enshrouded; Nightingale falls flat in the crafting / survival side of things, with a model that is 10-20 years old for the genre.
Then what about the story, theme and the ideas of portals that we see in the trailers ?
Secondary.. Or even thirdly I should say. And that is even if you have the patience to get there; which I will be honest; I didn't have.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this game is in early access and there are a few things about it that I would like to say. it is a good game and has a lot of potential! But there have been some rather interesting bugs and issues that I have found so far. I will get to that in a moment though. One of the issues that I have with the game however seems to be the lack the feeling that there are many realms out there. A lot of the in game text including one of the loading screen tips says that if you leave a realm, then it is very likely you will not be able to find your way back to it. i have found this to not be the case. In fact I feel like that for each of the realm cards there is only 3 maps. One forest, one desert and one swamp. And this is for each of the astrolab, antiquarian, gloom, provisioner etc cards. I say this because I was able to go back to the exact same antiquarian forest realm that i went to before, at a much higher item level. I knew it was the same because it had the same quest guy at the portal and everything in the realm was listed as completed. I was kind of hoping that the realm creation was random but it dosent appear to be the case. Something else that annoyed me was that when I went to the power sites to get new realm cards, the automaton bishops/knights feel very much over powered for the item level that is required to go in. One of them i had to just keep hiding behind a pillar while my hired help whittled its health away, coming out to revive him every so often. I am not saying this is a bad thing but it just feels counter productive. Some of the systems in the game do not actually give you much direction, like how to build a gun. I can make all the parts, and according to the schematic i should be able to make it at my workbench however the plans do not show up. even though i can clearly see them in my crafting log.

Now some of the glitches and bugs I have seen. Well the biggest one so far is the gloom desert drop tables seem to be all messed up. Trees/sticks/grass dropping meat, bones and leather instead of logs/sticks/fibers, animals dropping ore and rocks. It made me think I was in a SCP dimension. Some of the housing walls and structures can not actually be destroyed, such as the crude stone round window and crude stone stairs. they get to 1 hp but then never get destroyed.

Overall this game is good and I do recommend it dispite the abnormal occurances. As I sad above this game is in early access and I am interested in seeing what else comes out.\t\t\t\t\t
