Speaking of the vaults, these are super fun and almost give an MMORPG dungeon feel. I just WISH WISH WISH there was in-game text chat for the realm/vault you're in. Voice-only kinda sucks, and you do get text chat, but with party members only... which is great... but like... how do you ask people if they want to party up WHEN YOU CANT CHAT BEFORE HAND??? Common online game etiquette taken away by design.. so weird. I'd also like to see some more variety in the vault generation. It gets kinda predictable after a while.

I could say a lot more about the game, but overall I love this game and can't wait to see where they take it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this is a review from someone that has a few hours into the game but has not reached endgame content.

First off I want to list the things that attracted me to the game at the start.

1. The ability to share a realm with friends and not have to be online to host the realm for them to play. This has been an issue with a lot of the negative reviews but for me it is a benefit since the dev is providing servers for us to use.
2. The random map realms is a big plus for me since I like to explore and fixed maps for me just get boring since you always know where to find what you need or want in a fixed map.
3. The crafting is so intricate and detailed it can be daunting to some but for me I enjoy the assembly of the right materials and the right realm cards to boost the stats on the items I create.
4. The ability to create boons or banes to the realms to increase or decrease the difficulty or create better loot for each realm you create is a great feature and allow you to customize the realms to your individual play style.
5. Now this is a borderline one for me since the building in the game really takes time to unlock and I have not seen all the designs. Some have complained about the building but from what I have seen most of their complaints are due to the early building being so rudimentary. With mid game building options I have unlocked the building becomes much more aesthetically pleasing but also much more resource intensive, this is why this is difficult for me since I feel as you expand the building should not become so resource intensive and feel like a grind to make the materials for a nice place since you already had to work your way to a level of finding the schematics.
6. The world is beautiful. I mean really beautiful. Some of the vistas you come to see are really wonderful.

I think that cover the key points for me that attracted me to the game. So here are the things that I find unfinished, I think that is a good term.

1. The most infuriating thing for me is the NPC follower. They are an absolute nuisance for any other use than a pack mule. They will burn anything you give them in a fire in any realm you go to and if you give them high grade stone they will use it to construct some random building you come across. The NPC follower needs a lot of work. Oh if you do not change their weapon out they will chop trees or mine whatever is near, now the mining is not horrible the trees can be detrimental if you want to keep trees around your home. I have learned to keep my NPC follower equipped with a maul since that will make them effective in combat yet not destroy trees or other resources. Yet they will still destroy anything you store on them that is burnable or can be used for building without your permission.
2. Their feels to be a gap from early game to end game. I jumps from 100 to 200 and it does not seem to be an easy jump. The leveling is not fluid, is probably a better way to put it.
3. Character creation, sorry I just find it sad that dude looks like a lady and visa-versa. You can make a family tree of all males and have a female character, sorry but this just make no biological sense to me.
4. Character backgrounds. Why? They do not make sense to have a 78 year old character have the same physical ability as the 16 year old while both being a military officer? How do you become a military officer at 16? And how are you still living at 78 as a military officer in 1830? These things need to make sense for immersion.

Not sure that I have more than those Items. There are a lot of people that complain about the lack of an offline mode or single player but I feel this is actually a boon for players like me that want to share a realm with friends but we do not always have schedules that allow us to play together. Inflexion hosting the servers takes the stress of us finding a host server, and the cost, to be able to work together with friends even when you are not online together. I feel that the game really needed about another 3 months of development before release to reach a 6-9 month EA timeline. Although many of the issues have been brought to light with the EA that they may not have realized if it was postponed.

All in all I think Nightingale is a game that still needs work but I believe it has a ton of potential to become one of the top games in its class. I just hope the devs do not try to pander to the trolls and change their vision, I see great things in the future.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI REALLY want to recommend this game... but I can't.

First off this game was incorrectly advertised as a co-op survival. It's actually an MMO Lite. The core game loop is: grind gear up to max available "Power Level", kill boss who is above your "Power Level", unlock next zone, farm new gear up to new max "Power Level", rinse and repeat. They literally copied the Destiny game loop. And for end-game you get: RAIDS! YAY! (Seriously, I'm not joking the end-game is literally just grinding the same 3 lack-luster raids on repeat.)

So how about the in-between, the farming resources to get better gear. Well, it would be fun except this game is was CLEARLY made by artists who don't really play that many games. The world is FULL of points of interest. But 80% of them are just random art pieces with literally no game function. Some of them have a single food item placed on the ground in front of them, but THAT'S IT. Why is there an abandoned train car in the middle of the forest? There's no reason other than because it's a Steam Punk train. It has zero story or plot threads tied to it, and serves zero game mechanic function. It's literally just there to break up the monotonous scenery.

And worse yet the game looks really average. It's horribly optimized, so good luck running on max graphics and still being able to fight, but even on max graphics it looks 5 years out of date. It's not even a stylized art that can get away with not being realistic. Ark: Survival Evolved (the one that released in 2015) looks better than this game, and runs better too.

Combat... yeah it sucks. They (like everyone else) wanted hard core, Dark Souls style dodging and blocking. However I don't think anyone on the design team ever so much as watched game play of Dark Souls. The dodge is REALLY bad. If you dodge into a small raised portion of ground it fails to move you. The block barely blocks anything. Enemies are CONSTANTLY clipping into you forcing you to always be backing up just to see where they are. And the "bosses" are just health sponges that can kill you in a couple hits (I am playing on difficulty 3 / 4). They can be beaten, but not by blocking or dodging. And as icing on this cake, bosses have a resistance - weakness mechanic. They're resistant to some weapons and weak to others. To the point that if you use the wrong weapon type you're just going to loose. However the game only mentions this briefly 4 hours before it's relevant buried among one of their DOZENS of forced (sometimes mid-combat) pop-up text boxes. That is fundamentally bad game design on so many levels. I only found out after going to Reddit to figure out why the boss wasn't taking any damage.

This game has a repetitiveness and lack of clever design issue. The only puzzle is literally baby's first Simon Says. It just repeats the same sequence of 1, 2, 3, at you endlessly until you repeat it back. And then they make you do it dozens of times because they couldn't be bothered to invent a 2nd puzzle. The trailers show you ALL of the cool enemies. And I mean ALL. There are more but they are: blue wolf, green bear, giant grasshopper, giant lady bug, giant spider, rabbit, you get the idea. It's like they ran out of creative juice 2 years ago and forgot to stop by the grocery store to buy more.

The always online server even in multiplayer is complete BS. With it being an MMO lite I understand the desire, but if No Man's Sky can do let players switch back and forth then so can you. And any claims it's to prevent cheating are now proven false because the game has balance is already ruined from exploits that the devs still haven't dealt with.

Okay the good: this game has literally the best character creator I've ever seen. I get really annoyed that 90% of games only offer straight white people hair. This game doesn't just offer curly hair. It has FANTASY CURLY HAIR STYLES. The concept of taking a non-white hair and extrapolating it into a never before seen fantasy style is AMAZING. The depth on this character creator is insane. I could literally spend an entire day just playing with all the details.

If you like steam punk this game has lots of it, and it looks fantastic.

The game is mostly bug free. Mostly.

The sound design is... okay. I don't think the game has music. If it does it's so forgettable I literally can't remember if it exists or not.

Complex Crafting is really where this game shines. I LOVE the crafting in this game. The fact that different resources give different stats is AMAZING. I Just wish the auto-add would stop depositing my valuable resources into building walls for my house instead of taking the low level ones I don't care about. I know some people think it's a bit much, but honestly once they fix the abysmal inventory management (it takes SOOOOO many click to do anything), and the ability to craft without having the resources in your inventory then crafting won't be a chore. I would go so far as to say this is one of the best crafting games I've ever played.

I like how the weather effects impact game-play. The first time it hailed I was happy. The second time I didn't have my umbrella out and started taking damage and I was surprised. It honestly is nice to have actually survival style mechanics like that.

I will change my review to positive in a few months once the game is made less of a chore to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a lover of survival games I purchased this game 3 days after its release. It had LOTS of mixed reviews. I read only the negative reviews for hours. Most of the negative reviews expressed problems that almost every survival game has. So after 249.6 hours in game as of today I thought a review would help some people.

THIS GAME IS FUN !!! The fresh ideas of realm travel and steam punk is really great to me. I love that its a gear based game and there are SO MANY different ways to make your gear. Lots of different building designs you can use , not just wood , stone , or metal ... The game offers several different difficulty levels for each new realm you make with your own portals , easy , med, hard , extreme. Each increase also offers more rewards. There are quests that guide you to the watch. A public realm with vendors to teach you many many new things. Anyways I'm rambling. SO ..

Pros: FINALLY Not a 60$ game.
Great graphics. Draw distance is amazing.
Weather lol. Amazing but it can kill you.
Really supports group play , or solo play very well.
In game voice is pretty good. Even has echo while in caves.
Weapon building with different mats is amazing for min max building.
Magic is a thing and is really fun and fluid.
After 249.6 hours of game time i have only crashed once.
The load times are not as bad as some people have stated. Dont play new games on toasters people.
You get a companion and they look great when they stand in fires . no they dont take damage from it lol.
** This game keeps me involved and wanting to play more and get more people to enjoy they game with. **

CONS:
Standing at a crafting station will get you afk kicked after about 15 minutes. Kind of no big surprise but still. I'm crafting here.

Weather lol. Amazing but can kill you.

You will need a lot of storage boxes if you are a pack rat (hoarder) of every resource you find. Dont do this. You only need some of the resources you find, not all of them.

Tier 0 starter stuff stinks, Make new stuff as soon as you can.

REPAIRING. Costs essence. Essence is collected by extracting plant fiber, rocks, wood, hide, or basically anything you find. Its easy to get around. Just pick up tons of plants and sticks and extract lol. I recommend the harvesting enhancement that gives you about 400% more plants on harvest.

Companions AI needs some customization. MAYBE something like, dont put my best resources in the bonfire.

People have tips and tricks videos all over youtube , i recommend starting there. MAKE SURE they are newer videos. The game released Feb 20,2024 .. dont use the videos from alpha in 2022 ..

No mounts for faster travel. This doesnt bother me much because of all the puzzles on every map.

Google Nightingale Game for best search results. Just using Nightingale comes up with mostly birds.

Anyways I think I am done with this review of my play time. With out straight bashing other survival games by name to compare this game to. I have to say out of all the survival games I have played this one is REALLY FUN to me and one of my favorites for sure.

Hope you find this helpful.
Cheers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the more engaging Survival Crafting games I've played. To be fair, I'm also a big fan of the Fae legends :)

Keep in mind, this is early access. However, the development team has been very interested in implementing player suggestions (where applicable), and fixing bugs and exploits quickly.

I start with what I feel to be the weak points, then the strong points, and then personal things I'd like to see.

Weak points (not bug or exploit related):

1. Single shot firearms can feel pointless when there's more than one creature, or when one has frame rate issues.
2. Vaulting is okay, but could use some work. I know this is already being looked at.
3. Companions need command options. Sometimes they feel more like an obstacle or annoyance than helpful. This is also being looked at.
4. The swimming seems quite pointless and the characters swim so slow that it's almost too risky to go out and get the one or two available oceanic resources. Perhaps this is going to be fleshed out at a later time.
5. The building system can be a little challenging at times, but this is something they are working on fixing.
6. Most of the character face options are... not the most visually appealing.

Strong Points:
1. The Lore! I'm super stoked to see it get fleshed out :)
2. The game is beautiful, even if the characters faces and models leave a bit to be desired. The environments are unique and gorgeous, the animations are pretty solid, and the effects are often unique and engaging.
3. The crafting system is fairly diverse and engaging.
4. The creature diversity is promising.
5. The team play is fun, even if the rolls are a bit limited.

What I'd like to see:
1. More Fae! The mythical Fae world is quite diverse and engaging. It would be great to have side quest options for building relationships with various Fae and elementals, and having them assist in certain ways or even team up as support characters. Fairies could help with healing, making the base area grow certain native crops, increasing glider effectiveness, lighting up an area, etc. Tree Fae could produce specialized wood without having to cut down trees. Elemental Fae of different types can help create different boons, magical abilities, base augmentations, or help passively produce certain resources. There's so much potential with exploring the Fae/Human relationship dynamics!

2. More melee weapon options. I know this is being looked at.

3. More effective stealth tactics.

4. Magic specific tools that allow for unique combat spells, can't be used for anything else, and need to be repaired more regularly.

5. Rapid transit options across maps. Perhaps it's specific mounts you can develop relationships with in specific Biomes, and they require essence for transit time? Zip lines could be fun. You could also have Biome specific teleporters that can be built which are limited in the amount that can exist in a Biome.

6. After reaching the Watch, players should be able to build their bases in most Biome power levels. Maybe even set up to three Respites. Only being able to build the main base in an Abeyance Realm gets kind of boring.

All said, I'm really excited about the direction this game is going, and am looking forward to how it unfolds! Great job devs!
