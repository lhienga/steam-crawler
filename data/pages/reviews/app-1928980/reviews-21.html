First Survival eating up my time since ARK
I love this game in its current state and see high potential
Please keep in mind that Early Access is used to improve development with community feedback and do not expect a feature-complete game.


Yes, there are things in the game mechanic that are not (yet) perfect.
Yes, there are some bugs in the full range from entertaining to frustrating.
But in my opinion, Inflexion has already proved that they intend to make Nightingale a suvival game of high quality by reacting to players feedback and working on major bugs as soon as they get them reported.
But: It's still early access. So by definition, it is neither meant to be feature complete nor bug-free (Is there even such a thing as bug-free software?) In my opinion, Inflexion already proved that they listen to their community for the future development of Nightingale.
That is exactly what I expect after launching a game in early access.


This being said, I really love, what Nightingale already has to offer in this moment.
Not only one, but a endless amount of atmospheric and breathtaking beautiful worlds worth exploring. There are so many lovely details to explore. Huge, like mysterious floating ruins recaptured by nature or small like an old mossy bathtub lying somewhere in the wilds.
I further like the idea of mankind using an ancient portal network until it collapsed and myself being a survivor stranded somewhere in an unknown realm between unknown worlds.
Victorian inspired characters and ruins on one hand, phantastic creatures and mysteries in every newly opened realm on the other.
If I go through this new portal, what will it be that waits for me on the other side?


I am really looking forward to the future development of nightingale. I assume, I will spend at least an equal amount of time in the faewilds as I spent in ARK some years ago (At least in relation. I was a student when ARK started in early access)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have really enjoyed this game. I know it is early access so I am not expecting perfection. The development team seems to be taking player feedback seriously. I like that while the realms are procedurally generated, you can use various cards to change things up or tweak the environment to suit your needs. I like to take my time base building and wandering around in survival games and hate it when I quickly out level an area.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFeel like the game has missed so many opportunities in regards to its potential.
Pvp could be done similar to Darksouls where a player or groups could invade the realm you are in and steal what you have farmed etc. could make it like a setting so people still can choose to not pvp.
Allow for players to live with friends or others and form sort of clans within the community like a shared home realm which would allow for the solo grind that this game really is to be alleviated because that's all it is currently a slow boring grind despite stating its a co-op game.
The crafting and inventory system could use tweaks like search bars and sorting options.
Home realm does'nt upgrade as you do so your stuck with basic resources meaning you need to go to other realms to farm the resources that should be available in your selected realm despite your gear constantly getting better and out growing basic farmed materials.
Range on the augments and the benches for proximity is way to short it means your required to either put all benches and augments in a small area or build multiples of every augment to receive all buffs and recipes to crafting.
Having to constantly spam E to pick everything up when it really should just require you to run over it and when it comes to fiber, berries etc i can understand you being require to pick it up but basically you just kill enemies and spam e for every part of the game.
Creatures and bosses just constantly spamming one ability rather then have multiple attacks that they cycle through makes the combat rather boring (yes bosses do use 3 or 4 abilities generally but sometimes they just spam one skill dependant on how your play which causes a combat lock where you generally will die due to them being stronger then the player.
Creature damage balancing somethings hit way to hard at the lower levels which cause fights against bosses to become a chore rather then fun especially if your playing alone in a group you can sort of mitigate it but generally the boss still stays on the first person to hit it.
I was definitely looking forward to this game prior to playing it but now that i have i realise how many issues and features it is missing that most basic games have in general.
In its current state I dont recommend the game but to each there own some may enjoy it a grindy solo game with no real goal\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly access review.

Nightingale shows a lot of potential but in its current state it's quite dull.
The cons
Building:
- No wedge blocks.
- Only purpose for a base is crafting and storage.
- Very limited options on building blocks leads to less creative and fun bases.
- The augmentation system is very anti QoL.
Exploration:
- Bit slow, some PoI's are well.. not interesting.
- Very repetitive locations
- Card system is cool but could be much better if it changed more than just the look of the realm and some minor things.
Combat:
- I found combat to be very very boring. Had to play it in a harder difficulty for it to feel a little challenging.
- Repetitive enemies and combat styles.
The pros
I honestly didn't think Nightingale introduces anything that stirs excitement for me. A good games makes you wish you were done with work and at home ready to play and see what may happen. This game felt more like a chore. The card system is interesting but it's essentially a buff with few positives and some negatives. The augmentation system is tedious but has potential, essentially unlocking new recipes while allowing you to sort of decorate your base with objects that have some purpose.
The one thing I really thought was cool was character creation. But sadly for me it was pretty much downhill from there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful game filled with adventure and the horrors of the feywild. Here is my greatest opinions on this game.

_POSITIVES_

-The game is cheaper than its value.
-Beautiful graphics and animations.
-The voice acting (Puck) is incredible.
-The ambience sounds doesn't steer you wrong, you'll know exactly where the snarls of a way wolf is to the cry of a harpy.
-You wont get tired of playing in my opinion.
-Each new game is randomized.
- The character creation is stunning and actually makes you think about how to design your character. (I made Red Ridinghood ^-^)

_NEGATIVES_

-The game will crash at times, no worries tho, it typically saves where you left off.
-They could add more fae to the realm.
-Long loading screens that make Sonic The Hedgehog 2006 look like a fevered dream.
-The game will lag and I'd be scared to play with others unless you got a thousand dollars worth of internet speed and coverage and a gaming pc thats better the an HP Pavillon. (Which is the laptop I am playing on)
-Only 4 save slots.
-The game is networked to their network, which wouldn't be an issue if it wasn't feeling like when the nintendo switch put kindom hearts as a cloud net game.
-A better arachniphobia setting. Just becuase you dont add the looks doesnt mean the arachniphobia isnt there. Making it where the arachnids are replaced in arachniphobia mode would be a better course for those who are arachniphobic. As a person who has archniphobia to the point I become peralyzed in fear, I can safely say that thats a better course of action.


Over all the game is really good for Early Access and as a player I for one appreciate the game updates and bug fixes going on. I especially like that when the game crashes I'm actually able to report what I did before the crash to help make a difference and to let the creators know what happened in order to make the game more playable and enjoyable. I can see that the publishers, producers, and game developers put a lot of work and dedication to the game and I hope to see what becomes of this game with all its new updates and ideas fleshed out.
