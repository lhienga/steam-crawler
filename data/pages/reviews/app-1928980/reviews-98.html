\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have very much enjoyed my time with Nightingale, and am very hopeful for future development. The endgame crafting of materials, despite being a chunk of peoples dislikes, is actually the biggest aspect of Nightingale I enjoy.
So you have to find the raw materials, all with different stats of their own, to then refine with other raw materials, to refine further, until putting all of your materials into the final product (say a knife or clothes etc.) This allows for massive diversity and range in how a single piece of gears stats will look. It actually reminds me more of Diablo or looter shooters, but instead of getting the gear with the stats you craft it yourself. I really hope they keep this structure in place because a very unique game could come out of it the further they go.
Sure it's slow, and a lot of work, but that's the point. I understand the criticisms fully, but I also think of the possibilities with this mechanic and think it sounds far better than a Victorian era Conan Exiles.
I imagine if they went full Diablo and made the ascended realms (the harder versions currently in game) go higher and higher with increasingly better resources with diverse stats on each one.
Or if they went more MMO and improved the Vaults to be more of an epic raid or even go Warframe with the Vaults and make them a way to fuel a power fantasy with the overpowered gear you crafted.
Or they could go more No Man's Sky and make the randomly generated realms have more to them and be even more unique and fascinating.
The thing I fear though is that they backtrack and appease rather than focus on whatever their vision was, more than any game I have played in EA or tested Prerelease in probably near a decade I am excited about the potential for Nightingale, not just how fun it can/will be, or how well received it can be, but the potential to be something grand, possibly larger than itself.
Sure, it mashes a lot of other game mechanics together, but that's what games have done since the early 90's. That's how new genres are born.

With that said I would recommend this game currently, because it is fully playable and enjoyable, but only to people that enjoy slow paced, grindy, big number big damage type games - I would say 50 hours for a typical player currently and a couple hundred if you're like me and want to experience everything you can to offer feedback/personal fascination/whatever

TLDR: Nightingale currently is a beautiful art piece with interesting mechanics, rough around the edges, and currently would only recommend to a niche set of people but has the potential to pave it's own niche.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tId like to start off by saying, if you have less than 20hrs in the game your feedback is obsolete. There is no way you experienced what the game has to offer in the small time frame you played. If your a gamer who likes to bulldoze your way through a game then this game will not be for you. Now if your experiencing crashing/ lag or lost/no connection- your frustration is completely understandable.
Do everyone a favor and continuously report it.
Moving on....
I love survival games, I love all aspects of them. I've waited a long time for this game to come out and it has not disappointed. The realms, the clothes, the building, crafting system, collecting resources and character creation is amazing. My only complaint would be the blue print system. I don't mind the grind or collecting the resources necessary for crafts and While i think its a good idea for some "rare" gear, a lot of the good/ interesting gear is offered at the end game (so why would i need it then, the game is pretty much over?) I think a lot of blueprints/recipes should be offered near mid game or the augmentations should unlock much more than they do when you finally achieve crafting them. It should give you the chance to craft the item, then if you want it as a higher tier you can re-craft it with the better resources. I also think all base building styles should be offered much earlier than they are for the same reasons as the gear (end game??). I think making things like building styles and crafts more accessible would keep more people interested and feeling less burnt out. I still have much to do and learn in game so i'm hoping there is a reason for their choices or if it really is something that should be revamped. Over all i really enjoy this game and all it entails and i'm excited to see what becomes of it.\t\t\t\t\t
