\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI see a lot of negative reviews due to things such as there not being enough polish, not enough variety of weapons/armor/gear, complaints of it being online only (it's not a secret people should pay attention and understand what they're buying),folks having network connection loss ( in 117 hours I lost connection from the network 3 or 4 times, and for an early access game that's impressive to me). When you get down to it most of the complaints or negative reviews are things folks should expect when they purchase a game in EARLY ACCESS.

I personally overall had a blast with the game, I ground myself up to gear-score 276 which is damn near the maximum from what I can tell. my experience was mostly smooth and the game maintained my interest and I cannot wait for more content. (side-note: a snow biome would be so much fun!)

CRAFTING - 8/10 I enjoy the wide variety of materials that can be used in crafting, I like that each type of material effects various stats. What I am not the biggest fan of is the fact that it's a bit complicated to know exactly what certain things do such as augmentations. Sure, you can go through each of the 57 augmentations and set aside 5-10 minutes of your time doing so to figure out exactly what you need. I personally hope they link each augmentation to each type of crafting bench in the build menu so you can quickly assess which augment goes to which crafting station and exactly what it does. Also my other caveat is there isn't cloud storage in the game yet, so it gets rather tedious moving stuff around to craft.

EXPLORATION - 10/10 I loved the sense of exploration in each of the type of biomes as well as the various realm cards and how it altered everything from the sky, space, fauna and even creatures. I strongly enjoyed the bits of lore that can be found in various places throughout the map. I would love to see more variety in the puzzle mini-games when it comes to getting essence.

COMBAT - 8/10 I enjoy the combat, the melee feels very fluid however the ranged with firearms can be a bit frustrating when you miss your first shot and suddenly it's taking an eternity to have follow-up shots.

GEAR/WEAPONS 6/10 - It's early access so I can't complain much but I really am looking forward to seeing what other weapons they add because currently it's quite limited. If I can get a sword, dagger and bow combo I'd be in heaven. The weapons we have now have some fantastic detail to them, I enjoy how the crafting materials effect the appearance of the gear. My personal dislike at the moment is the lack of variety in clothing/armor, there just isn't much.

I also find it annoying how you cannot see what base item level gear will be until you put materials in. Which means to check any of the 6-8 hat recipes I have to see which is the highest base gear-score I need to go haul all my mats out of their chests just to put them into a bench and see what I'm working with. Also, in my opinion there is a real lack of clothing/armor for male character types.

ENDGAME - 7/10 I'm one of those weirdos who loves the endgame grind in games like this. I enjoy grinding dungeons for more essence just to get myself a nice new piece of gear. Looking forward to more variety in these dungeons/vaults in terms of location, enemies and mechanics. My biggest gripe about the endgame is how the vendors work, the clothing vendor for instance - I can buy bundles for druidic armor but I would love to see a breakdown of exactly what I am buying in that bundle. I would also like to have some type of gear stat breakdown so I can determine which bundle of gear is going to be better for my playstyle.

In conclusion, for a game in early access this game feels absolutely wonderful, the developer seems to really be focused on further improving the game and has created a gorgeous detail-rich game. I do hope they don't "dumb" it down too much because I love the complexity of the crafting system, just needs a handful of tweaks in my opinion. I look forward to upcoming updates, and hopefully a road-map for future content/changes. I HIGHLY recommend this game to fellow survival/crafting/RPG lovers like myself, just give it a chance and realize it is an early access game (also for $30, that fact I got over 100 hours of fun out of it is WELL worth it to me!).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're wondering why this game has a low review score, it's pretty simple to say: this is a tried and true early access game, and people are not used to "early access" actually meaning "early access." It's a game in its early stages, and there is going to be a lot of unpolished portions of the game. I know some games use the "early access" tag for funsies (looking at you Ark Survival), but this isn't one of those games.

With that said, is this game good? Absolutely. This game is fantastic. The foundation of this game is insane, and will absolutely lead you to know that its future - as long as they continue on this path - is going to be something even greater.

So what's so good about it?

The world: the environment, the story, the aesthetics... they're immaculate. The gaslamp/victorian/edwardian-vibe mixed with a magical twist has created something breathtaking. Look at screenshots & gameplay, and you'll see it in the skybox, in the enemy art. It's beautiful, and it's just the beginning.

The gameplay loop: the loop for this survival game is unique and interesting. You go through procedurally generated realms to unlock & obtain what would otherwise be limited resources. The combination of the type of realms that you can enter and raze are many, and they've already stated they'll continue to add more and more overtime.

The combat: the combat could use some polish (as magic is experiencing some limitations at the moment) but it is satisfying. You can get heart shots, which are even more potent than head shots. You can use magic, guns, and melee weapons, and will need to switch to better deal with enemy strengths/weaknesses.

The story: this game has, perhaps, some of the best writing I have seen in a long time. The story is profound, and has many twists and turns that keeps you on your toes. The fantasy it sets is a refreshing take on steampunk, and you'll see some familiar names from history riddled throughout. You'll be able to read journal entries that tell the tale of the final days of its writer, the beauty of a marriage between Fae & Human, the strife of the Fae Courts, the horrors of the unknown, and so much more. The writing casts such a wide net so quickly, that I worried it may have been too much too quickly. But they do so well in explaining and keeping things together. Pay attention, you'll find sometimes that not everything is as it seems.

THE DEVS: These are the most quick-to-address devs I have seen in an early access. They listen to the population and have consistently said "We've heard you, and now we're working on it." They're active, they're doing what they said they'd do, and I give them so many props for that.

Alright, so what needs help?

Polish. It doesn't have it, and it desperately needs it. From things like wonky traders, who may trade varying sets of the same armor, to trees that can lag you to high hell if you're not careful... The game has fantastic features, but they're rigid and definitely need polishing.

Building. It looks like they're testing their servers strength by first imposing a building limit. For people who are into massive builds, you'll find that you may have to employ some tricky strats in order to get around limitations on building. To quickly tl;dr the situation with building, you can make hundreds of midsized buildings, but if you're looking to go Minecraft Mega Builder with a single building you'll need to get clever.

And finally: it simply needs MORE. There is plenty to do now, but we need more! The early access gives you a great breadth of the game that leaves you wanting so much more, and they tease you with all the soon-to-come things, that you'll be begging for all of it now. This is where the early access part comes to bite us all.

I could go on forever about this game, but overall: I love it, and I can't wait to see Inflexion grow this beautiful game into something enormous. I wish them all the success they deserve.\t\t\t\t\t
