Build menu is simple and I'm enjoying how it takes you back to basics for gathering and crafting. However, the PVE is lacking the same Ark/Valheim chaos. As this game is still in Early Access, it is my hope that this will be improved over time. The creatures are in the same spots in groups of three and present no real challenge at present. I feel like it will get tedious very quickly though if there isnt more content added. I found that searching "ruins" etc was literally just empty passages with the occasional box. Kinda meh. Will wait and see if things improve.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUntil they figure out how to either :

A. make their servers stable so you are not kicked between worlds
or
B. allow us to host our own servers

This game gets a full thumbs down. Half my trips through the portal (if not more) end in me having to reload into my game because I've been kicked.

There are a ton of other bugs, as well as hundreds of reasons why i LIKE this game. But as it stands, forcing us to use their servers and not having them stable defeats the whole purpose of realmwalking. I'm very unhappy. I don't only have a couple hours as of this review either, i see great potential here but based on this enormous flaw, it is not worth paying the price.

Edit: There was an update today that was supposed to help with this specific error. It is about 60% better but still a major issue.


EDIT 2:

TLDR;Playing on their servers can suck. Big time.
Being forced online is not very fun.
Network lag and disconnections can ruin your experience.
The crafting and building is very much over complicated, but if you are into that, than it's a bonus not a negative.
The game can be beautiful, and scaling difficulty can make it ferociously dangerous, or painstakingly simple.

Personally i'm enjoying EA and will sink many more hours into it.

That being said, here are my more in-depth thoughts about the game:

They have stabilized many of the portaling, and I am finally able to feel confident jumping through towards another realm. Here are my current thoughts on the game, however i'm seeing others are still having issues. If my issues return my review will go back negative.:

The lore is fun, but the quests don't really feel too engaging, until Puck joins in, his appearences are always fun and quite stimulating.

The crafting system is extremely complicated, but there are very many QOL items that are never explained, such as being able to add base items from anywhere on your connected base (just look at the wall or the floor or a pillar with the items in your inventory), this helps enormously with schlepping items across base to change your stairs. Also, if you put up a new blueprint, and then destroy something the items will try to fill the new blueprint before dropping, which is very nice.

I personally love how involved the crafting system is, and its complexity has many arrows within it, you can look up full recipies easily in the crafting menus, and you can see directly from the item in the bench you want to craft, what augments you might need.

The building system is very nice, but again not well explained, there is the "build" menu and a "quickbuild" menu, both of which you must familiarize yourself with, but once you have the copy paste, and build menu features figured out its quite nice.

Also in regards to building, there are a few limitations to your structure builds, but they can be reviewed and you can reset the structure limits by building non-connected structures.
The build item limit is somewhat a bummer, i know they are trying to keep server lag down, and it helps that you don't need all the augments at once, but it would be nice to not have to worry about how many sawhorses i leave strewn about, or how many kitchen knives are spread through the base... kinda silly. Again, with my earlier comments, if you are worried about server strain.... LET US RUN OUR OWN SERVERS. LET US HAVE OFFLINE GAMES. LET OUR EXTREMELY EXPENSIVE GAMING PC's DO THE HEAVY LIFTING. It's so absurd, especially since i believe this is a thorough attempt to keep the game code hidden from the community. (Pure speculation.) I will give it a recomended thumb but with a huge warning:

Playing on their servers can suck. Big time.
Being forced online is not very fun.
Network lag and disconnections can ruin your experience.
The crafting and building is very much over complicated, but if you are into that, than it's a bonus not a negative.
The game can be beautiful, and scaling difficulty can make it ferociously dangerous, or painstakingly simple.

Personally i'm enjoying EA and will sink many more hours into it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of now I would not recommend this game. Nightingale is a game with a design that mistakes tedium for content and difficulty, it is neither. Base crafting is okay, it is the type where the structure pieces are prefab and you do your best to make something interesting out of it, to me it is a lot like Pal World in that sense. The realms are to damn big with no sensible way to cross them except to run so I hope you like running simulators.

The gear score system feels so out of place in a survival/base building/crafting game that for me it was sort of jarring. Here is the loop you will run multiple times over multiple difficulties that unlock once you "complete" the lower one...you craft or buy a card. You put that and another card of your choosing in the portal. The realm opens, you run immediately to the fae tower and complete some silly, elementary school level puzzle and then the whole map opens. You need these things called "essence" that come in tiers so you are sort of forced to RUN across the entire map doing more trivial puzzles, building someone else's house or mopping up remedial reading level AI.

The crafting is probably the part I dislike most, it is just raw tedium for the sake of it. Want to craft something? Well you better get ready because you won't be doing it anytime soon if it is the first time. You will have to gather mats, buy the recipe and then craft several other benches so that you can then gather the mats to craft other mats that you need to make the mats that you need to make your item...no I am not kidding.

I will watch this game closely as I love the STEAMPUNK(wtf even is magic(k) <--- there is no K in magic core?) ascetic and the idea of playing in the Fae but right now it just feels like my time is not respected and a bunch of low effort crap was jammed together thinking people would be to stupid to notice it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been waiting for this game to release for well over a year now with great anticipation. And with about 60 hours in, I must say I'm thrilled with my experience of it. Is it perfect? No, it has several things to be desired and some necessary fixes to be made. However, I do not see the "redundancy" that many here complain of. Yes, you grind, you take on higher level encounters, you upgrade your gear, and you carry on to greater heights. I see no difference in many other crafting/survival games. Certainly some allow you to jump right in and bite off way more than you can chew from the start, and maybe those who are disgruntled would be happier if Nightingale allowed that as well, but it is far from tedious to get there.

The worlds are phenomenal and fascinating. The Minor Realm cards that can be utilized in any realm allow for personal preferences to prevail in the realms. You want more of a challenge? Blood Moon card; you cannot walk 5 feet without encountering a new horde of monsters, both Bound and realm-specific. You want something to make travel and exploration easier? Thinly-veiled card; something to allow you to defy the laws of gravity, without suffering fall damage! And all of them result in stunning visual changes as well! My only wish here is that I do yearn for a greater variation of Major Realm card combinations, and hope to see that in future updates.

Additionally, and perhaps my FAVORITE thing thus far, is the nuance of crafting! You do not simply upgrade your weapons/tools from stone to copper, then iron, etc. No, all the different ores have different characteristics and statistics to them, so you can choose what to use to create your weapons/tools based on what you are looking for. Better ranged damage? There's a certain ore for that! Better durability or melee damage? There's one for that! And this extends into clothing crafting as well! It is remarkably nuanced and the limits to what you can do in personalizing and optimizing your gear seems excitingly endless to me.

If you do not have the patience, wait for some of the updates, because this game has much more in store for us all and I cannot wait to see it. Some of the communities biggest grievances are already in the devs sights and are being worked on presently. Like: offline mode, crafting from chests, and queued crafting to name a few.\t\t\t\t\t
