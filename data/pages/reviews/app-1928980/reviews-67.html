\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10 Hours. I am not purposely trying to offset the games mixed or negative reviews, these are just my thoughts.

Tutorial, a little too long.
Crafting, 10/10.
UI/UX? Could use some major work. But I personally can look past it having gotten used to it.
World building/mystique, 10/10.
Character creation, 10/10
Character models 5/10, this could use a little work.
Gameplay loop: upgrade, fight, upgrade, fight, boss, repeat. Some of the level scaling is janky I will admit.
Creature designs are sick as hell, love the steampunk/victorian era ♥♥♥♥. Now all it needs is some knight armor.
I am fortunate enough to have gigabit internet, so playing solo on a server isn't an issue for me. But I 100% agree the game should have had offline mode from the start. Is it not more expensive to host servers than to do so? Not sure.
Graphics are top notch - though not optimized very well. I've got a 3070, and 10700k w/ 32gb of ram and I have to play on low/medium at 60fps - that does dip a lot.

This all comes with the early access name plate. For what they are charging and what the time sink this is gonna be for me, I would recommend. I wouldn't pay more than what cost of the game is currently. That being said there more to be had here and with the updates planned I think the game has some really amazing potential, and I think the value proposition is going to greatly improve.

At 10 hours I have felt like I've done at lot, and nothing at the same time. I have not unlocked guns or magic yet, but I have gone through multiple realms and well.... I am hooked. Also I love Puck, really good character design IMO.

I may update this review as time goes on - the longer I play / the more fixes, updates etc etc. Hope this helps.\t\t\t\t\t

Con:

Please fix the networking.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game I really enjoyed playing and even though it does have some issues I still would recommend it because I had fun playing it and I feel it has potential and could get better as it gets some things worked on.
Even though the game is in a pretty bad state at the moment and needs a lot of things completed or adjusted, I still had fun with what is available. I do look forward to what they have in store with the game but I feel they should really focus on core elements and functionality.
My game literally breaks when I chop trees which is a core function of building/crafting and I believe early on it wasn't as noticeable because it takes some time to chop trees down but once you have better gear and your axe one shots trees I am noticing the game locking up and my ping skyrockets. I don't know why cutting a tree would be connected to the server but maybe this is something that will be addressed in the future hopefully.
Also, right now people are using exploits to boost and alter gear that is completely unnecessary. The content that is available right now can be ran solo on the hardest difficulties without using exploits. That being said though, people should be wary of getting gear from other players and should craft their own gear if they are looking for a more legitimate experience.
My conclusion is it's a very fun game with a lot of potential but needs some TLC in the right areas such as; balancing of weapons and enemies, armor/resistances, QOL spell casting button assignments instead of having to hold one button to swap spells, expanding charms and enchantments for more variety of builds to run, ability to see your gear's appearance as your crafting so you know what the end product will look like as well as being able to see your items/clothing base stats before adding different material attributes thus removing the unnecessary trial and error of item/clothing outcome. Filters for storage to easily find specific attributes you desire when crafting thus shortening the time you go through each individual item in various storage containers. Optimization and QoL improvements are needed and with a lot of these issues fixed, come full release, I think this game can be one of the best of its kind.\t\t\t\t\t

I can not recommend this game at this point in development, it is just a very convoluted crafting simulator. The tutorial needs a complete do over as it leaves way too much out of how the game actually works and is just plain weird. The augmentations for the various crafting benches are way overboard, it has to be the only survival/crafting game I have ever played that you have more augments than the benches can accommodate(Absolutely too much!). Recipes are over complicated so much that more than not you have to go outside the game to get help figuring out how to craft an item and even then it is not a sure thing that you are going to be successful(Go back to statement above about too many augments).

Having to go back and reset and rerun T1 essence levels in order to upgrade is a bad and boring grind, need to allow us to extract items from the different levels to essence of that same level. Or a conversion even if it cost more lower essence to upgrade to the higher one.
I have seen other games crucified for loading screens and they had nowhere near the amount or the time waited that Nightingale has. You have to wait for the server, you have to wait for the map, you have to wait for the portal, you have to wait to go to your respite, and on and on. I believe this is due to it being always online and procedural generation of the maps, needs to be fixed.

Didn't even get into the farming or the building systems due to just trying to figure out crafting and leveling.


I will say that the graphics are good and the style is for sure different.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like open world survival crafting/building this game is quite good. Some of the mixed reviews are mad about not being able to play offline but I feel like those people haven't made it to the area of the game where multiplayer actually comes into it's own. Does it need work? Absolutely. Is it a great game for early access? Absolutely. But, it's well worth the $30 it costs.

Many people have issues with the crafting system. I totally disagree because the complication is kind of what makes it good. You don't level up in the traditional sense, you do so by advancing your gear. HOWEVER, I will admit there should be a much better tutorial system that describes the attributes materials give and what portion of those attributes are applied to what armor pieces and weapons. It takes a keen eye to spot that some of the attributes just don't apply to some pieces when it comes to the end craft. This would also be useful for infusions labeling what items they can be applied to, especially since you need infusions on almost every item to even make it to certain unlocks without using the Twitch Prime gear.

I also agree with other reviews that there needs to be triangle foundation and ceiling support. I'm assuming this has to come with time because without it you're just stuck building many designs you simply abhor. I do find myself wondering how they are going to monetize things in the long run since it is a live service game. Working in IT it's quite easy to tell that your specific abeyance realm and all others outside of The Watch are on-demand which is awesome, but it's still gotta be hell on the server costs.

Overall, as long as the developers don't quit on us and keep providing new content and biomes, I'll continue to play. I do think we need animal taming/raising/ranching eventually, and much better procedural generation when it comes to dungeons and islands, the more you visit the more you realize many are the same as others. Also: Let us interact and give followers orders, tell them to stay home or stop using my rare materials to build the aid structure in the wild randomly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all this was my most hyped game of the past year and while it has its moments and can be fun, it is littered with bugs and imperfections. Where to even begin... The most annoying bug is being DC'd about 80% of the time I try to travel between realms or to the watch/my respite. It takes me about 2-5 tries just to get back into the game, then another few tries just to spawn into the realm I'm trying to go to. The enemies are extremely choppy which makes them hard to hit, not to mention some of the animals you need to hunt run so fast they are impossible to catch unless you want to run around aimlessly. I spent about 45min tracking a legend tier animal in a 160 ascended realm, finally killed it and then it despawned within about 5-6 seconds with no chance to farm the resources. The building aspect has a lot of cool looking structures but the snap function is awful. I've definitely seen worse in other games but it is no where close to Valheim's level of building. The progression is a bit wonky. The game expects you to make every realm in order of difficulty. My group skipped some of the earlier realms and realized we missed all kinds of quests that help with progression, not to mention tons of items that would have helped along the way that are now obsolete because we have already farmed better gear. The vaults seem like a cop out. The idea is cool but the execution is not great. They consist of a few small puzzles and waves of enemies just like the occupations and skill challenges you find in each realm but with a boss at the end that isn't anything new if you have done the quest for Nellie Bly. It's basically an essence grind, boring and unoriginal. There are a lot of other minor things missing like quality of life options. Like why is there an autorun feature but you can't use it while moving forward, you have to stop moving then hit the button. There's toggle crouch but no toggle sprint? It just feels like a very unpolished game vs what the trailers showed before release. I am hoping some major updates come out soon because it could be a much better game. For now though I can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
