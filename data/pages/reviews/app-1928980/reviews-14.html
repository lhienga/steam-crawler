\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an interesting variation on the classic survival game. If you're sick of survival games don't get it. If you enjoy survival games this is a good one and I recommend it. It's still in early access so go into it with that in mind. There are bugs of course and it needs some fixes, additions, and improvements but the developers are very involved with the community and are working on a lot. The game is gorgeous and like many indie early access games recently feels more fully developed than a lot of AAA games which is probably why the reviews are being harsh on it. People treat it like a fully developed game and get upset.
Compared to many survival games the realm jumping is unique and interesting. The writing is solid and the plot is decent. The building mechanic could use some improvements but is no worse than existing games like ASE. The ability to mass pickup all drops around you by holding one button is really nice. Crafting is interesting if a little complicated. Overall it is a really good game - especially for early access money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is nothing like this in gaming today. The main draws for me are the world building, setting, and exploration on the level of an open world RPG. I still see new dungeons and artifacts 130 hours in. The vistas and environments are stunning on my recommended spec system. The crafting system is very deep, comparable to Star Wars Galaxies. Combat is better than most survival games. It’s at least on par with Conan exiles with more modern ranged options.

That said, It’s very much early access. There are bugs, there are placeholder graphics for inventory items. This is version 0.12 and there is plenty of balancing and content to come.

So it's not for everyone. The online requirement and bugs will be addressed over time, so wait a bit if those are deal breakers. Players who don’t care about story & setting, hate reading in games, and skip all dialogue are better off elsewhere.

For me, Nightingale's well developed setting and amazing art are more than compelling enough to overcome the rough spots of Early Access. It will appeal to other sandbox MMO and open world RPG players like nothing else. Also good for survival players who are looking for something different. I feel I’ve already gotten my money's worth and am excited to see it develop over Early Access.

I love this game, I've been playing both solo and as a large group of friends, and It brings me so much joy. It's not perfect, and I never expected it to be. The most frustrating bugs are the ones that the devs have put high on their list which gives me a lot of hope for the longevity of this game.

If you're looking for something polished with no hiccups wait till they've patched it more. If you don't mind a few crashes and love to laugh at some of the silly bugs give it a shot!\t\t\t\t\t
