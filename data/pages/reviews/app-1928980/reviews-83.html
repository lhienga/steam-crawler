\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a neutral/mixed score review.

In Nightingale you teleport to a biome buy what you can from traders and pickup a few resources and leave. Compare this to Valheim where you feel like you are exploring the unknown, you travel vast distances by boat and make camps in different biomes.

You use two cards to determine the biome and type/difficulty in Nightingale, because you are jumping between multiple realms I never really feel connected. Compare this to Valheim, Enshrouded, Grounded, Smalland etc.

The biomes are scattered with challenges which are just cut and pasted. You don't really feel like an explorer. It's basically a randomly generated map with POIs and no real dungeons.

Unlocking blueprints is a chore and not interesting at all, you buy them from traders which only carry a few at a time. Buy a chair from one a shelf recipe from another very boring. No dungeons with interesting loot/recipe unlocks. I would have preferred to unlock recipes by finding resources and defeating creatures.

There is far too much inventory clutter, meat for example has different types and tiers, this goes for wood as well etc. It's insane.

Making charms, tools weapons and mixing different types of ingredients gets old really fast, they need to streamline this.

Some of the regions are really nice to look at but the game just falls apart when you look at the crafting, exploring etc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't say I recommend this game yet, but I do think it has potential. I'm about 10 hours in and have completed the tutorial and killed the first "boss".

There are some neat things about the game- character customization is decent, the environment is very pretty, and I like the idea of crafting and using cards to create different realms. But this game is really lacking in a few things and I don't think I can slog my way through any more at this point.

My criticisms of the game, starting with my biggest one:

1. Combat. The combat feels absolutely atrocious in this game to me, like it was an afterthought that no one put much effort into. You can just sort of wildly swing your weapon and hit stuff and everything feels very slow and janky. The first boss had no animations except one directional movement; when he got in range of me he just stood there and I took damage. I have no idea what his actual mechanic was so I just beat him down with a maul.

2. World feels empty. Now maybe this improves the further you progress into the game but the realm in which I built my respite feels extremely empty. Hardly any enemies, wildlife, etc. I ended up building very close to the trader and just farming rocks and wood to trade for the pelts and stuff I needed to make in order to craft the gear needed for the first boss gear check. It was easier than running around the whole island looking for something to kill.

3. Quality of life stuff: some basic improvements in this area would go a long way. Crafting from storage would be an immense improvement.

4. Performance issues: overall the game ran well (on both my desktop and laptop) but when first starting up the game it takes forever to load in and actually start playing. Hopefully once offline mode is introduced this will be a bit faster. Also, several times while playing I would get disconnected and unable to get back into the game for several minutes. Not the biggest deal but it does get a bit annoying.

Overall, the impression I get is that the devs spent a lot of time crafting a nice looking world with an interesting back story but everything else sort of took the back seat to those things. Idk if that was actually the case but that's what it feels like playing the game. Hopefully they will make some improvements, honestly I could deal with everything else if the combat was just better, but for now I would say this is probably a pass unless it's on sale for a steep discount.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK. I don't do reviews often, but here is my tuppence on Nightingale.

Pros: Very nice graphics (characters .. not so much), great story (what's in it so far), systems in game so far seem fairly robust. It's fun to play .... until it's not. Yes, I recommend it ... so long as the devs keep on the track they are on.

Cons:
-- Crafting menus are workable, but really really clunky and hard to use; especially with them auto-scrolling up or down when you click on something.
-- The online-only thing is really, really, REALLY annoying. Working on a big batch of ingots or oil at your Respite base? BAM, kicked for inactivity. 5 minutes AFTER you're back and moving around.
-- Zoning is really, really, REALLY long .. like alt+tab and read an article or two while the online-only thing generates the Realm on some server somewhere, sends it back to your PC, blah blah blah.
-- Online-Only with Early Access servers means lag spikes out of nowhere, network errors, and truly experience breaking zoning times. Either eliminate the online only req for non-public realms or really beef up the server support.

Most of the negatives revolve around just overall polish that I'm sure will come if they keep working on it like they have been, and the online-only BS that the devs have said they've made a priority.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely cannot recommend this at this time. I gave it a chance but couldn't help but compare this to a mutated survival game which grew additional legs just to shoot itself in more than two feet. I will likely try it after about half a year since I cannot envision them fixing all the problems earlier and I doubt the design problems are going away at all..

Graphics
- are good but in this instance overshadowed by the game running like crap

Performance
- horrible even when surpassing HW requirements
- game performance goes down with the amnount of entities in vicinity
- the only redeeming quality here is that the game goes down in the fps but does not stutter, however playing at 20 fps if the game spawns more than 5 entities (which is quite common) is just not acceptable

Design
- very confusing and it takes a very long time for the game to actually open up so expect a very linear experience at the start
- game has survival mechanics that simply do not make sense and only serve to annoy the user
- unless you look at the wiki and other sources the building mechanic will be confusing at best
- game does a very bad job at explaining how to unlock recipes and progress other than through the main quest
- there are areas which are locked until an item level is reached and the only indication is if you go directly to them and check if you reach the requirements

Combat
- enemies are often unable to deal with you strafing their attacks
- before you get ranged weaponry you truly realize how bare-bones the meele combat is
- the hitboxes often do not line up and enemy animations often go through you serving for a very clunky gameplay especially during group combat
- the projectiles just feel very weird, hitboxes on structure are often much bigger than you would think from their visual representation therefore shots that are close to floor/ceiling/wall often get trapped in it even if visual line between you and target exists

Game has many survival mechanics which allow you to modify the sandbox map severely which is a very good. It does give you the option to modify your experience just the way you like. But the above mentioned problems just outshadow the good qualities of the game.

Overall the game just needs a lot more work for me to consider downloading it again...\t\t\t\t\t
