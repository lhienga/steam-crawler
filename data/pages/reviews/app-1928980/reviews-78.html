\r\n\t\t\t\t\t**Game Review: Nightingale**

*Genre: Early Access Survival Crafting, Base Building*
*Developer: Inflexion Games*
*Release Date: Feb 20, 2024*
*Platform: PC*

**Introduction:**
Venturing into the realm of early access, Nightingale brings a refreshing twist to the survival crafting and base-building genre. Developed by [Developer Name], this Victorian-era-inspired game introduces players to a world of Gaslamp Fantasy, where the crafting of materials and building of bases takes center stage.

**Unique Features:**
Nightingale stands out with its Gaslamp Fantasy style, a rare departure from the typical survival game aesthetic. The game's unique selling point lies in its realm cards, allowing players to dynamically generate new maps set in distinct biomes such as Desert, Forest, and Swamp. These cards also come with challenge modifiers, adding an extra layer of complexity to the gameplay.

**Aesthetic and Firearms:**
The choice of Victorian-era clothing and building pieces is a bold and effective decision, rarely seen in survival crafting games. Firearms follow suit, drawing inspiration from real-world counterparts like the 4-barrel Lancaster pistols of England and the Chassepot Rifle of France. While mostly historically grounded, the inclusion of a smoothbore shotgun raises eyebrows, seemingly blending cap and ball muzzle-loader with a breech-loading shotgun.

**Crafting Depth:**
Nightingale adheres to the familiar progression system of survival crafting games but takes it a step further. Crafting items with different materials significantly impacts their performance. For example, a Lancaster Pistol made from tin and basic wood pales in comparison to one made from Pursuit metal and hardwood. This intricate crafting system extends to tools, clothing, food, potions, enchantments, charms, and even magic.

**Realm Cards and Exploration:**
The game's realm cards play a crucial role in creating a dynamic and ever-changing world. Choosing a biome and challenge card influences the map's layout and points of interest, ranging from traders and fae towers to vaults. Fae towers, in particular, serve as vital hubs, unlocking the use of minor realm cards with diverse effects, providing both challenge and reward.

**Magic and Combat:**
Magic makes a significant appearance in Nightingale, adding an extra layer of strategy to combat. Spells are crafted using different materials and can be slotted into weapons, offering a variety of effects. The higher the tier of the weapon, the more spells it can accommodate, providing players with versatile combat options.

**Improvement Areas:**
While Nightingale delivers an engaging experience, there is room for improvement. Quality-of-life features like pulling from chests during crafting, upgrading structures without rebuilding, and a crafting queue would enhance the overall gameplay experience.

**Final Verdict:**
Priced at $30, Nightingale presents a compelling early access experience, combining a unique aesthetic with intricate crafting and dynamic realm generation. Although there are areas for improvement, the game's potential is evident, making it a worthwhile investment for those eager to explore the evolving world of Gaslamp Fantasy survival crafting.

**Rating: A Promising Journey**

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off let me begin by saying I love Survival sandbox games like this so there is a bias
as an early access and with that in mind I give is a 9/10 as things get added and tuned ect I have no doubt it will be an amazing game. well worth the price
Pros: very indepth and easy to understand tutorial level that teaches you what you need to know for not only game play but for setting up the world as a whole.
It plays like other similar games like Valheim, and so if you're familiar with that game this one is pretty easy to pick up.
Fun atmosphere, the whimsical feel of the Faewilds really sets the mood, and the little structures I've taken to calling 'easter eggs' from the mortal plane really make me smile.
I have yet to run into any game breaking bugs.

Cons:
Its still early access so at least for now the models and other visuals are few in number so there is a bit of repetition and can get old fairly quickly, (however a change in realms helps with this some.)
The hostile enemies seem to be able to see you and lock on to you even if you try to do something like break line of sight and hide, so stealth play is a waste in its current state
Your 'recruitable follower" is almost more annoyance than they are worth, picking up resources you harvest, walking in front of you when you're fighting or in some cases completely blocking you from hitting enemies or picking up resources in tight places like hallways. There is no way to tell them to give you some distance, or what resources you want them to pick up, (unless you fill their inventory so they can't pick up certain things) They will 1000% cut down any and every tree in a 100 foot radius of you, many times one you're using for shade in deserts (the umbrella helps with the heat, but some times is a pain to have equipped all the time) you can prevent them from chopping trees by making them hold a different tool, but i've yet to see them use other tools for actually harvesting.

My personal hopes
Follower commands
more player character customizations
more outfits or a transmog system so you can wear what you want not what happens to have better stats
More animals (I can only slaughter the same pack of wolves for so long)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay lets be honest still in early access so its not a completed game as of yet, however I am eager for its completion. On my PC, a mid tier gaming computer at BEST, it will crash on occasion particularly when portaling to a new domain. That being said I really enjoy at lot of mechanics that they implement to give the game a steampunk/1920s explorer genre(think Allan Quartermain kinda setting which funnily enough the reference the character in the game). I very much like their resource and tech tree and the progression of following the primary quest is planned and encourages new players to insure that they have proper gear requirements for the different tiers of domains. Modifying the domains adds an additional layer of mayhem or benefits as you would see fit which can be done at a fae tower. So far I've yet to progress very far and have really only used the melee weapons in combat I particularly like the dash that the knife and sickle offers and tying it with the umbrella very fun highly mobile combo. For some more of the down sides your follower will use *any* material they have on them if there is a build project or even the objects that need fuel to burn for different crafting projects. So I would suggest being careful with what you give them. Seriously though its an all in all good game and well on its way to be an absolute favorite of mine!\t\t\t\t\t
