\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's fun.. BUT.

It needs a LOT more time to cook.
There are some building elements that are completely broken. And annoying. Some things won't snap. And plants grow back where you settled in. Not to mention that building cost is way too high and getting mats can be such a tedious task. Especially since you could also use them for many other things. But guess what will cost you more? Not weapons or armor. But building your base.

The endgame has potential though. The hub is neat. But either no one there is talking in chat or it's broken. But the idea of a drop in dungeon co-op romp is very fun. Kinda like an open world instance.
But that's just that. Currently, you're better off getting better mats in harder, ascended worlds.

I can't recommend the game now because I was more frustrated playing than I had having fun.
But I will come back to it once more updates hit and might flip my opinion around.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is definitely rough around the edges. It is in early access but I have dropped enough hours in that I think I have a good grasp of what it is.

I am into it. Its a pretty in depth survival game. Material, the stations you use, the augmentations, the enchantments, the charms, all make a difference in what you are building. While to some this may be cumbersome, to me, this makes for a great game. The exploration has been pretty good so far and even with only 3 biomes right now there is enough variation to make it interesting. I am only playing on hard but the game give enough push back. To push back you need to optimize what you are able to build. It rewards those that work to find the best materials.

AI needs some work but its also nice to have my purely hetero sexual adventure NPC Patrick. He has been with me for a while and is a pretty nice pack mule. Its also nice that he will build the stuff in my base as long I give him the materials he needs.

I feel like I have gotten my money's worth and will most likely play till I finish the current story, take a break till 1.0 and dive back in. Can't wait to see what they add.\t\t\t\t\t

I get very few network connection failures now. The game is lot more fun to play.

Review 2 (10-MAR-24):
Still can't recommend this game. Just lost everything in my Abeyance realm due to a patch update. They provided a ticket process to restore it, but I can't play until that happens.

I still get 2-4 connection errors every time I go through a portal or return to my (now deleted) Abeyance realm. They supposedly increased servers but there's no real improvement. All these realms need to be created locally without the need for the network.

Crafting is interesting in that it's very flexible. You can make (e.g.) thread with a dozen different characteristics which confers those characteristics into the materials you create from the thread. The downside is that those dozen thread types don't stack in inventory so your base is filled with storage.

The realms are quite detailed and picturesque. That's about the only positive feedback I can give right now. But, again, the game has very high potential so I'm not giving up just yet.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would heartily recommend this game if you like survival crafting games, and simple but satisfying combat. It's a little bit unrefined, but it's also very young in its development cycle. The systems are simple and satisfying, and anything you don't know you can google by now.

Devs are responsive and in tune, online only right now can be a little bit slow, but it's not too bad. They're already introducing QoL updates like crafting from chests in the next update.

I'm 13 hours in and will probably get 100 hours out of this game *in its current state*. They haven't even built the final city yet! Which to some is cause for concern, but for me, is cause for excitement.

This is a fun game. Exploring is fun, your companion has infinite weight but limited slots (a good compromise). Crafting is fun, and there are definitely lifehacks abundant in game, but even without them it's still fun.\t\t\t\t\t
