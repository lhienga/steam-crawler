\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Nightingale," developed by Inflexion Games, stands out as a breathtaking exploration into the boundless potential of procedural generation, crafting worlds that are as varied as they are mesmerizing. The game enchants with its Gaslamp aesthetic, teeming with tesla coils, zeppelins, and a roster of historical figures that bring its unique adventure to life. The allure of hopping into a dirigible and soaring through these meticulously designed realms from a bird's-eye view is a tantalizing prospect that fans, myself included, ardently wish for.

While "Nightingale" wins hearts with its innovative concept and visual splendor, it's not without its hitches, particularly noted in the realms of server connectivity. The anticipation of crossing into new realms is somewhat dampened by slower server responses, a small price to pay for the vastness it offers. Furthermore, the game, still in its early access phase, exhibits a few rough edges that need polishing. Notably, the AI, especially concerning NPC followers, sometimes detracts from the immersive experience, leading one to wonder about the decisions behind their programming.

Despite these imperfections, the core of "Nightingale" lies in its encouragement of exploration over haste. It is a game that invites players to pause, to appreciate the beauty and intricacies of its worlds. The puzzles, mazes, and combat, while present, feel secondary to the sheer joy of discovery that permeates the game. This pace may not suit every gamer, but for those who relish in taking their time, the game offers a refreshing departure from the norm.

In its current state, "Nightingale" is a gem with some rough edges, a testament to the game's ongoing development and the promise it holds. My recommendation comes with a caveat: patience is required, as the game is not yet fully polished. But for those willing to embrace its early access quirks, "Nightingale" offers an unparalleled aesthetic and exploratory experience that is hard to find elsewhere in the gaming landscape. With eager anticipation, I look forward to the game's continued development and the eventual realization of its full potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as I can tell, Nightingale is different from other survival games in one singular way: combat. There is no secret about that- sure the world is creative, full of dangers, and functionally massive, but so is every other survival game. The main page of the game, and the gameplay trailer "show off" the combat but not much else because there is not much else to be seen if you are not already familiar with the survival game grind and base building. There are "Towers" which are basically just buildings where they put a ♥♥♥♥ ton of enemies inside (and usually some kind of devastating boss enemy). The goal of the game is to accrue crafting blueprints and special abilities. There is no moment, other than the tutorial, and gifts from NPCs, you are going to find an actual crafted item, especially not after completing a Tower. Everything useful needs to be crafted. Which is fine, it is a post apocalypse survival game after all. And the crafting system actually compels me prefer this, but where is all the cool ♥♥♥♥ (nowhere to be seen apparently).

What I want to say is that the main point of interest and presentation that makes this game different (combat) isn't all that special. I feel like I'm playing Skyrim with more fluid animations and clunkier hit boxes. The latter is likely a result of early access nonsense, but the former is quite honestly worth ignoring if you take the combat system at face value; swing weapon while circling enemy, shoot ranged weapon while running away, and take damage without a clear, or operational, way to move out of the way of enemy attacks (other than circling them). There is a dash mechanic, but it's as clunky as the hitboxes in the current state of the game.

I have been playing for 8 hours when posting this. I've completed a singular Tower and been able to make it to an Antiquarian world, and have completed the first trial. After seeing the next trial be about again attacking a tower, I stopped playing. The combat, which again is the main focus of the game, is not in the place where it is to be worth $30. I know it is early access, I know it is unfinished, but I would rather them divert all resources to development rather than slow development for attention with an early access version. This may ruin the game's reputation, which I actually don't want because it has a lot of potential.

Submitting a review for an early access game is frivolous and silly, but so is playing one. So just wait for it to come out, or to be on sale, then play it. I truly believe it will be fun when finished, but not now. Unless the negative things I have said in this review have not swayed you so.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLets set this up. I typically like to look at a few categories for specific games. This being a survival we will set them like this.

Survival Mechanics (food sleep ect)
Building/crafting
PVE Combat
Exploration
Story

Survival Mechanics:
The survival mechanics in this are not horrid, Food and rest. The ability to eat a large array of raw foods and to place a sleeping bag anywhere (see building) would make this an almost tedious non issue except that the food not only replenishes you but gives buffs letting you choose specific foods for specific things. All in all a good Implementation of food and Rest.

Building/Crafting: Building itself is fairly light with only a handful of set pieces to choose from (stone and crude wood structures for a lot of the game). However this is offset with the ability to have 100 different building sights and each sight to have up to 300 parts. These can be combined in creative ways to make bigger bases and to change the way things look. Id like to see larger limits to the structures say 500, but not a deal breaker.

Crafting is where its at in this game, Harvesting different materials gives different stat bonuses to the items crafted with it. Like wise processing is not always necessary and some times just allows better bonuses. This is not overwhelming though. You quickly find that its simple yet robust enough to allow for tailoring of items. Added to this is the Enhancement system for equipment and you can get a lot of variety in equipment and items. In addition workbenches using decor for bonuses and unlocking recopies is a great way to add functionality to an other wise simple build bench make stuff system. Though this means also a lot of resource gathering. In this I would like the AI to be a bit better as they tend to be slow to do anything and even when they do they seem confused. Also it seems the AI can be a lumber jack or a Miner... would like for them to harvest plants and even be able to dress the animals we kill for hides and meat.

PVE Combat: PVE in this game is at times good and at times meh. the combat system is defiantly a needs work. The ability for a player to move versus that for the enemy leaves a lot of times where you have the "where did he go" situation as enemies will move suddenly or rapidly. Puzzles and challenges in the game amount to Simon says, find the mark, Hardcor parcor, and the NPC build my stuff. This is fine and some of them are interesting but Id like some more variety and maybe interaction. Perhaps stabilizing a ruin to get to the jumping part, or even riddles that need to be solved to tell you what to leave as an offering.

Exploration: This is where the game shines, with three primary biomes and a host of cards to alter and or level up the biomes. Movement through these "realms" is fun and even challenging, the ruins and little story blurbs are spread out enough to always make you look for more. Combined this with the Various different moving types that have been taken into consideration when doing level design and you have a wonderful experience of running jumping vaulting and finding new beautiful ruin areas and vistas. And its not like oh go in do 5 things and leave. Each realm is a new map with 2 dozen POIs and countless non listed areas to explore. The materials being dispersed through these makes it even more likely your going to spend a good bit of time in each.

Story: I haven't gotten far in the story, however my besti puck is guiding me well and between his mercurial talks and the logs and other story elements in the realms this is a fun little trip into some fairly in-depth world building (realm building?). Id like to see some more of the fey intractable, perhaps I'll find this in later game play.

TLDR: fun game for those that like building and exploring, Beautiful and deep. Could use some work in some areas but great so far\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far this game has been "terrible" to play because it doesn't really Explain or Demonstrate how to Do ANYTHING. Most games will have a simple tutorial to help walk you through- this game has many small windows, with small text that pop up that try to explain, however the text is cryptic at best... a game SHOULD explain in simple, well-written and explaining text how to do what you are trying to do.

I bought the game because I thought it looked neat... it certainly has "flavor" and a vibe to it... but having died several times without accomplishing much... it's a droll slog through looking stuff up on Google... the Key-mappings screen don't tell you everything... like how to change from 1st to 3rd person, nor do they tell you how to pick stuff up after you build/put it down.

Again- the game "looks cool"... however so much effort went into "looking cool" that they missed the very basics of getting you into the game... the first few "areas", that would be the Tutorial Section... doesn't clue you in to what you should be doing... usually a game will have you learn how to Walk, Run, Crouch, Jump... not this game- at least not at the current moment... I get the impression that the game has not been Play-Tested by folks that haven't worked on it... or they didn't listen.

The "guide" when you start the game, Puck... speaks in riddles... which I get is "Thematic"... but a Guide to get you through learning the controls should not be so Cryptic. I imagine once I learn how to move, how to interact with the environment- on a re-play things will be much cooler... right now I feel like I'm walking in a maze blind-folded... and I learn by hitting a wall and dying. The game pauses for 3 seconds and you respawn with none of your stuff... meanwhile whatever just killed you can see you when spawn in- so before you can react... boom, you are dead again.

In any case... I give the game 10 out of 10 for "Flavor"... but 1 out of 10 for being "New Player Friendly".\t\t\t\t\t
