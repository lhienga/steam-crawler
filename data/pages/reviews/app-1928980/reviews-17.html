Have you ever wondered what it would be like to live in a post-apoc version of Victorian society where humans survive by portal-hopping through Fae dimensions, all while flying with an umbrella like Mary Poppins??

Wonder no more! Nightingale is exactly that!

Well, kinda...

See, it's almost exactly that, except you're actually the steampunk lovechild of Mary Poppins and John Wayne so you also have a revolver, a shotgun, and a rifle to choose from as you zip around with your umbrealla shooting... checks notes uhhhh.... zombies?

But wait, there's more! You also get to be friends with Grendels, chill with Victor Frankenstein and Puck from Shakespeare's A Midsummer Night's Dream , and dress yourself in the finest modern fashion in cutting-edge 1800's ultramodern high-society style - complete with tails and a tophat ...but sadly, no monacle as of yet. sigh

OH! And you also ALSO get to hunt a Bandersnach from Wonderland, meet a Giant, build an enormous house full of lovely (and useful!) decorations needed to craft everything from a basic hunting knife or fishing pole to a Winchester gold-plated rifle, with exactly the stats you want them to have. Or even just the way you want them to look.

I know, I know.

You think I've lost my mind, but I promise I am not describing a fever dream. Whatever your expectations of Nightingale are, whatever you've experienced in the survivalcraft genre in the past... this isn't that. It's a refreshing, unique, and wholly indescribable experience that has left me in awe, giggling with glee, and giddy to see what the next realm has to offer every minute of the 150 hours I have played this game so far.

Nightingale just launched in EA less than a month ago, and already the expansion potential for this game is literally infinite. I, for one, cannot WAIT to see what the devs have in store for future content updates, let alone the 1.0 launch!

I also feel the need to help offset some bad reviews left here, for such a wonderfully complex, rich, and textured game:

ESPECIALLY for such an early EA indie game, but even for a full launch 1.0 AAA game (I'm looking at you, Bathesda), Nightingale has surprisingly few major bugs for a game THIS complex. Yes, you need a continuous internet connection to play (but they're working on that!) and yes, server drops happen from time to time, but jeebus chrity people! Look at everything these devs have packed into one game! That's a lot to carry on a server, and yet somehow they are managing it with minimal disruptions (and you never lose progress as it saves continuously, it seems.)

Some whiny people just can't seem to wait ten seconds to respawn maybe once or twice a day if you're on a stable connection (like you clicked "agree to when you installed it, people) - and the truth is they're mad and they can't wait that long because this game is that good and they want to keep playing.

Period.

Or some just don't want to put the work into learning the incredibly rich and fully-developed crafting system, and instead just want items handed to them without thought (or customization potential), so they've come here complaining and trashing the best game I've seen in AT LEAST 5 years in this genre - maybe ever, actually.

The TL;DR is this - Nightingale is a ridiculously intricate, outrageous, unique, and breathtakingly beautiful twist that is LIGHTYEARS beyond any game in this genre.

If you don't have the patience to learn how to craft quality products by reading stats so you can create infinite iterations of your gear and goods based completely on your own preference and playstyle - or if you're a gamer who just wants to go shoot boring stuff/other people mindlessly with the same "elite" gun everyone else has - go play Halo or COD or some other brainless and unintelligent shooter, because this is not your game (or frankly your genre) and that is not the devs' fault.

But - If you want a raging good time that will surprise you at every turn, in a breathtaking landscape with an immense amount of detail and customization to everything (including the current realm you're in at any given moment) - give Nightingale a shot. Pun intended.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe current state of the game isn't something I'd recommend. It's a pretty game and has an interesting concept behind it but the execution is poorly done.

When playing the game on a pretty beefy system that exceeds the recommended specifications there is an extraordinary amount of load time in the game. Yes, even with a gaming SSD. I'd normally forgive load times if there were a lot of assets to load or a big play space but Nightingale isn't pushing any boundaries with asset count or play space. The foliage and harvestables are all copies repeated over and over, the enemies are just duplicates throughout the whole game, and the POI assets are the same across all realms. Even with so many duplicate and carry over assets there is anywhere up to 3 minutes load time to do any realm switching. Even when you return to your Abeyance Realm (home base) you think nothing is happening because clicking the button only triggers the load time behind the scenes. When you finally give up because you think it doesn't work, as soon as you start doing something else the game swaps to another load screen.

Once you get into the game and put the loading screens behind you, there's really not much to do. That's not strictly true but it feels like there's not much to do because progression is so slow. You're spending so much time doing the same thing that nothing really happens. Harvesting is slow because you need so much stuff to do anything. There several points early on that you'll end up doing something just because you have no choice but to farm. For instance, T1 Essence is needed to upgrade your gear so you can complete the quest that requires a set gear score. By following the story, you won't have enough to do the quest so you're left to your own devices but you don't have any objective or reason to farm Bound enemies or further harvest other than hoping you get a rare T1 Essence drop. This problem is further exasperated if you make the mistake of taking the quest advice to purchase recipes from the vendor. You're asked to buy 3 recipes but of course you have some left over to buy the other 3 crafting machine recipes. Now you've set yourself even further back and have to do more grinding for T1 Essence later.

After 21 hours of play, I haven't made it past crude building pieces and still have on the first crafted armor. Having looked at a wiki in search of better building pieces it dawned on me that just to make a hut that doesn't look like pebbles it'll take about 60-80 hours, assuming I don't hit anymore random progression walls. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game having looked forward to it for quite a while. I've put 200+ hours into it and finally have to admit it's just a repetitive grind with far too many problems to have been released to early access. It's more like a beta version. I had hoped I would feel differently after having given it a thorough try and reached the endgame content, but I have to admit I was just being stubborn. $30 isn't a lot for that many hours, so that's a plus, but I wish I'd have spent the time elsewhere. It's possible they'll be able to turn things around if they listen to the many recommendations made by the community on their bug/comments site, but they'll have to scrap many of their existing mechanics wholesale and replace them with better thought out and well-tested ones.
Some of the most glaring problems are the extremely primitive combat, the very limited building capabilities (compared with any of the other major survival games), and the lack of cause-and-effect as well as final result information provided in crafting menus/graphics given the very complicated crafting mechanics. The complication of the crafting isn't the problem - I'd generally consider that a plus - but the lack of information and simple quality of life items (such as not being able to access your hot bar items while accessing a storage container) combine to make it very unrewarding.
So, until Nightingale is overhauled sufficiently to turn around their user ratings I'd give it a pass.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game reminds me of playing RUST as PVE, but with a quest line. Maybe the developers will start adding in some event based multiplayer down the road, but overall pleased with the game. I found the crafting to be well thought out for the most part. Augmentations need a little help such as allowing player to select which ones effect a crafting bench. Also the in game help and descriptions needs a little help, You can figure it out, but a little less Reddit digging for answers would be nice.

Inventory is tough to manage without a dozen or two large storage boxes, dressers. I like that refined items get unique characteristics but would like for flexibility when crafting with 2 different types of lumber, just take the lowest stats from the 2 and let me craft it.

Game play can get repetitive but I find it fun and by endgame its not to grindy since you can just teleport around and change it up. Love how many different worlds there are to go to and everything does look great.
