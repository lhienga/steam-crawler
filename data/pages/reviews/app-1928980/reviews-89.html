\r\n\t\t\t\t\tmy thoughts after 70 hours:

Bare in mind it IS early access at the time of this review.
------------------------------------------------------------

**I will not explain character creation... apart from... you create a character (all of which have been struck by the *YOU SO UGLY HEY! YOU SO UGLY HEY HEY!* Scanlan meme!!! and you can change the starting difficulty and name it)**

As a survival addict (its in the name) its *one* of the best i have ever experienced... and a VERY great try in being something almost completely different.

But... in true early access fashion, things could be better, some horrendous and also random bugs (which is to be allowed considering), however, Just over a week and no updates yet (apart from *we added a new server* stuff).

The bugs should actually be the priority as they are in MOST games - it is so annoying to have *random bug #1837* on character 12!!!! (this is what i mean by RANDOM bugs - example... one of my characters had his inventory eaten, none of my others did - in the Forest Antiquarian realm, the Realmic Transmuter is broken *on ONE of my characters* in the sense of, add a card to it, it does the GFX *i am changing this realm for you!* however, it doesn't take affect and you have no buffs/debuffs listed on the hot bar - as i say COMPLETELY RANDOM as it hasn't affected my 11 other characters)

Difficulty?
TO be expected... easier with friends... but a few of my friends express *the game play loop is currently too boring for me to play again currently, sorry dude, you are on your own!* - i have to agree in MANY ways that it does get extremely boring - i want SO MUCH to make it to The Watch, but i cant, because the bosses become IMPOSSIBLE on medium difficulty solo (in my abeyance), and now i am stuck on *medium* with impossible bosses... why am i stuck? Because if i want to make the abeyance realm easier i have to create an entirely new one and leave all my stuff behind!!!!

Content? I personally haven't seen much of it at all beyond the Provisioner Realm (as i cannot complete the Herbarium SOLO) so i have no clue! However, many streamers and youtubers have reached end game within 50 hours (but they at least, have friends who are bothered to play at all!)

Personal current outlook and feelings of where i am in the game? - Could have a LOT more content for us Soloists, mechanics SHOULD be explained a lot better! (how do i upgrade X bench with X? how do i get gear level 70? how do i get past my stale mate with the Formidable Bishop in the Herbarium? etc

GREAT AND AMAZING **try**.. yes.. its early access... yes it is being actively worked on (however, we still NEED updates!!! because now it is getting a bit boring having to create new character after new character, trying to figure out what my 6th character did wrong... and trying to find a *load* that ISN'T hit by random bug #937!!!!)

I am however marking as RECOMMENDED because for all its faults, it IS a very fun game (although gets boring fast) and they have tried so damn hard to make it such! BUY IT!!! ***If you dare!!!***
---------------------------------------------------

TL:DR:

Fun concept
Trying to be something new in an old skin
Difficult as a solo player
Boring game play loop
A few bugs, many of them random
Worth the low price if you understand that it is early access

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy initial reaction to this game was not good. I wanted to like it so much but was thrown off by the beginning. I was prepared to write a bad recommendation, but walked away for a few days to let my head clear. Then I gave it another try and muscled my way through the tutorial, making some gear, going through portals and setting up a base. I had frustration in trying to find and farm the T1 essence that is need for everything like gear upgrades and recipes, but I worked may way through it.

Then it hit me, if you have played one survival game you have played them all. The formula is the same and we have gotten spoiled expecting our cookie cutter mechanics and interface. But deep down in our survival bones we wanted something different and more challenging a little bit outside of the "survival" box that we have become so comfortable with. Nightengale is different - everything is different and it makes you think. Its challenging. Once you get over the mental hurdle that its not going to be as easy as all the other survival game formulas, then you will find yourself enjoying it more.

I agree there are some things clunky about it. I dont like my character all hunched over when she runs, I dont like waiting on the portals, I dont like all the written details on all the resources, and the menu system could be smoother. But the game concept, and the things about it that are different from all the other survival games is just what I wanted. Im sticking with it and cant wait for the fixes and upgrades and new content.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-PROS-
+ Crafting : Expansive and rewarding, but it is a bit of a grind fest with a steep learning curve, Some materials can be a damn pain to locate, but when you find them you feel it's a reward for your hard effort!
+ Survival : BE! PREPARED! You will probably die many times, and it is to be expected, so be sure to have your right gear, potions, charms and consumables in check.
+ Exploration : Super fun! It feels like there's always something to do in the different realms. Sometimes you stumble upon something you didn't expect, or find the material you've been searching like a madman for. Take your time to enjoy the diversity of the various realms and have fun!
+ Visuals : Gorgeous visuals, but with a few FPS drops here and there, and some stuttering from time to time. Could perhaps be a little more optimized. However, in general, I love the visuals and the various designs.
+ Audio : Wonderful audio, tho sometimes it feels a little off. It can happen in small spaces where the sounds get a little hollow and metallic sounding.

-CONS-
- Combat : Repetitive, and can sometimes also be frustrating as hell, especially when it comes to bosses and some automatons. It can feel a little heavy and unresponsive.
- Progression : As mentioned before, it's a grind fest. Be prepared to grind for the materials you need, and to go visit essence traders and sites all over the various reals to unlock the different weapons, outfits, augmentations and upgrades.
- Animation: truth be told, the animation seems a little lacking to me. I often find that both myself and NPCs around the world just look like dead husks standing around, with the bare minimum of animation. Blinking, what is that? Just the smallest animations on items, props and NPC/PCs would make a whole lot of difference!
- Still Early Release: I do feel it could have been far more polished. While I have enjoyed my time in the game so far, I am frustrated over the lack of icons, which are either replaced by a seal or a card with the game logo. I feel this is something that should have been done from the start, so the game at least felt a little more put together.
- Survivors: You will end up getting frustrated with them. They tend to be everywhere where they shouldn't be, and when you need to talk to them they walk away, while in other cases, they're standing uncomfortably close to you. You can't do much with them, except give them a tool and they'll just start doing tasks automatically. You can't command them in any way and there doesn't seem to be any benefit to which type of survivor you take with you. They're good as pack-mules as they don't suffer from the same disadvantages as you do when over-cumbered.
- Building is a bit wonky. I know we've been told why this is, but I still think it's idiotic. You can only place a chandelier if the roof above you is a roof and not a second floor. Plus, it's not too easy to build with the Tudor set. I have several roof edges sticking through the ceiling on my top floor because roofing with the Tudor set doesn't seem to be fully optimized. Hopefully, it will change in the future. Also, when you build things and want to undo what you just placed, it's not enough to just point the curser at the object and then press V. If you're not close enough, it will (for some stupid reason) delete the foundation/floor underneath you instead.
- Cursor placement: You can't always count on the cursor placement. I you chop down logs on the ground, I have found that sometimes you need to focus on where your character is chopping, rather than where the cursor is. It means you sometimes have to dance around a log to find the spot where your axe will actually hit the damn thing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: The game has good bones and enjoyable core gameplay, bugs and balancing are an annoyance though.

A couple things out of the way first.
The game is currently always online, something which I personally do not mind but a lot of people are upset about. In response, the devs made development of the offline mode a priority target. In their own words, it had always been planned but they prioritised the online mode for Early Access as they'd considered that to cost more resources and they wanted to get it done to have more capacities.

There are a lot of Network Errors happening across the board, the majority of which are seemingly fixable with just a File Verification or restarting Steam/PC, but some have tried pretty much everything and can only wait for bug fixes to hopefully drop soon.


Now, for the game itself, the grind gives me flashbacks to my Black Desert Online days. I spent 20 hours with the first set of tools, because I spent time exploring the realms and the current progression system actively disincentivises this, which is a shame.
Recipes for new gear can either be gained by doing puzzles and clearing NPC camps OR by grinding Essence, running to a realm's trader, buying all the Recipes because there are none that can only be found through drops and some that seemingly only the traders have and then immediately leaving the realm and going to the next to repeat this all over.

Different Biomes have different recipes, meaning whenever you get to a new realm tier you are actively hurting yourself by not rushing through them for the recipes and then only returning once you've upgraded your gear.


The minor cards are not always clear on what they do, and often tend to be buggy.
As an example for the first, there is a card called Maleficiate, which can drop as early as the tutorial. Maleficiate's text states this:

"Play this card to plunge a Realm into eternal night and increase the yield of the Bound's resources. Blackest ink seems to call out to the Bound beyond, urging them forth. Once alerted to your presence, they will gladly oblige."

What it doesn't tell you until you've put it into the transmuter and scrolled down all the way to the bottom of the card text, is that it has a permanent damage over time effect . If a newbie gets this card and puts it into the transmuter without fully reading it, it can soft-lock them because they often do not have the equipment to survive the effect and sometimes do not even have other cards to replace this effect with.

For the second thing, there are a couple cards that state they increase resource yield and refinement yield, but only the latter works.

Once you get to the (current) endgame area you will get to do a thing called Vaults. These are public dungeons where you will be matchmade with other players. There's a couple of issues with them.

For one the matchmaking is not based on GS so people who just got to them will be matchmade with BiS players that just rush through them for mats.
You are not grouped into a party together, meaning you cannot chat or voice chat with each other which is an absolute PITA when you have to do puzzles where a wrong input spawns enemies.
The worst part personally though, is that apart from the boss chest, loot is NOT instanced but a free-for-all, leading to leeches standing on the sidelines and just collecting loot while others fight.

You can eventually get a card that lets you open private instances of these, but the state of the public ones is not great.


So, with all that you may be asking, why do I still recommend it?

Because I've put 100 hours into it and genuinely enjoyed myself for most of them. The world is beautiful, the way minor cards can change the environment is breathtaking and fun to play around with for different purposes. There are cards that reduce or increase gravity, make it permanent night or day or change the weather with other effects such as boni for crafting, fighting or loot.

The combat may look bland at first but with charms, infusions and enchantments it has a deceptive amount of depth.

Need to kill insects but don't want to use a blunt or piercing weapon? Set your axe on fire and roast them.
Groups keep overwhelming you? Stack Strength on your weapon watch as they ragdoll around, pop a Quake spell down for extra fun.
Want to play a sniper? Memorise where Heartshot weakspots are on enemies and pop on charms and spells for one big BANG.

The puzzles start out simple but get more in-depth as you go on, later ones requiring the usage of ranged weapons to activate pylons you can't reach or climbing picks and clever parkouring to get to hidden loot.

The crafting system lets you personalise your equipment to a large degree with resources having different stats your equipment can inherit. Tin, Zinc or Nickel are all the same Tier, but have different stats resulting in different uses. One might be better for a melee weapon, while the other has stats that are more beneficial to a gun or protective gear.

The setting isn't necessarily my cup of tea and I definitely raised my eyebrows at the amount of characters that mention they were actually hailing from an oppressed minority group in the colonies (but only french or spanish ones), all while the implied-to-be english characters stay suspiciously quiet on these topics. It's also a bit hand-wavey with the history since there's mention of a Regency meddling with things, but the game plays a good 60 years after the end of the historical Regency period.

If you're a fan of victorian England and steampunk aesthetics though, I think you will be happy enough with it.


All in all, I've found this game extremely enjoyable and would recommend it because I think it has a very solid foundation and most of my grievances are down to bugs or balancing and not an issue with the core of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
