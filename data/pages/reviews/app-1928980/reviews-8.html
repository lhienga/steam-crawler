\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's very pretty, sometimes confusing and still early access so has a few bugs and missing items.

BUT

It's fun once you get going. The overall concept and setting are clever, probably can be developed further, but surprisingly deep. It's rare to see an entirely new IP, and here we have one. Sure, steampunk isn't new and survival games are not new, neither is having portals to other worlds new, But the way this is done is new.
Using the cards to affect the whole of the current world is a touch I really like, you can mess about to min-max to your heart's content in conjunction with augmentations (more on that below)
The lore is interesting, if that floats your boat, and the backstory is epistolary via you finding journals and notes from previous passers-by as you travel around.

The creatures you meet are named as if by Lewis Carroll, and while they are in the main variations on earth creatures they behave differently. Some are new in concept, which is fun.

Once you get past the lengthy tutorial it bites a bit more. This is useful to do as it shows basic mechanics and gives you access to some later game materials if you explore the tutorial realms to the max. Once done, as with all survival games, you're on your own and can explore endless worlds. Well, not really endless. There are several basic maps that use procedural generation to place points of interest. You get to set the difficulty for each world so you can work up to the top level. You can reset worlds as you wish, and you can change each play-through by playing a different world minor card. Much is hidden behind breakable walls or on top of seemingly un-climbable cliffs or floating buildings and landscape.

Downsides...

Nothing is clearly explained. This may be because the devs want you to discover how to do stuff, it may be added later, or it may be an oversight. Some mechanics are quite opaque in game, such as the augmentation system. When you look at an augmentation it does not tell you specifically which crafting station it affects to do what, it gives you an activity that can be on several stations. Some augmentations are really upgrades to old ones, so you should lose the old one as there are limited augmentation slots for each station. As some augmentations affect several stations and have a significant range you can end up with slots filled by accident, so you need either a duplicate station or a re-arrangement of everything. I've ended up making augmentations, putting them on a shelf and dragging them over to the item I wish to augment. Not ideal.
This could be better presented, such as telling the player that (say) the meat grinder has all the effects of the spit roast, or even having icons on the recipe cards they unlock showing which other augmentations do the same thing.
Companions can be frustrating and difficult to control (such as putting valuable wood on fires, attacking NPCs when you really want to be peaceful etc)

There's no voice acting, yet. At least for most of it. The main story lead, Puck is voiced and is suitably creepy for a Robin Goodfellow and his script has some Shakespearean references, a half-mangled Midsummer Night's Dream is in there. There's an annoyance with the interactions though, you have to keep pressing 'next' after every clause rather than have the character work though until you need to give a response.

So, where am I? I'm 140 hours in and still working things out, I've been taking my time and yet to reach the Watch level. I've enjoyed it so far, despite the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I am enjoying the game.

Good:

It is visually appealing. Noting mind blowing but well done environments and creatures.
Crafting isn't to overbearing. Needed materials seem fair. Lots of stuff you can build.
Dealing with hunger isn't annoying. Food is plentiful and it takes a while before you need to address hunger.
The overall theme is something new.
Jumping between different biomes through a portal rather than dealing with one huge map is easy to manage.
Maps are full of landmarks to explore. You aren't running through a bunch on nothing all the time.
It is more laid back and relaxing. No need to be sweaty.

Bad :

Haven't figured out why we have to be online for every biome. I know later in the game you go to co-op hubs. It seems like a waste of resources to need a server for solo play. Maybe it is their way to monitor cheating.
Lagging and rubber-banding in a solo maps is frustrating. Though restarting the game has fixed it for the few times it has happened to me.
Combat is a bit shallow. Plays like Elder Scrolls but nothing fancy. Swing/shoot, block, and dodge. Maybe some different attacks depending on the direction you move would add more depth. A lunge, strafe attack, jump attack, etc.

Overall I am enjoying the game. As I mentioned, it is more laid back and relaxing. It is fun to explore. Nothing seems overbearing and obnoxious. Just give yourself time to get through the tutorial and recruit your helper. They don't just gather and fight. If you are encumbered, just make them carry some of your stuff. I hope they don't nerf that. lol

It is worth the price in my opinion.
