\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: I love this game so far! If you enjoy survival crafting like Conan Exiles, this'll be right up your alley.

It isn't perfect, but has been incredibly good for just launching in early access. I have had minimal crashes and the core gameplay loop is fun and engaging. Definitely needs QoL improvements and balancing.

There is a main story quest to follow, but outside of those objectives, the core gameplay loop is:

1. Open a portal to a new realm. This is done by combining 2 tarot cards and picking a difficulty. Usually you'll either be target farming some sort of resource or going somewhere for a quest.

2. Once inside the realm, you are free to run around doing whatever, but each realm has a fae tower. This is the common open world mechanic of climbing the tower to reveal points of interests (POIs), etc. So optimal gameplay is gunning it straight for that tower. The towers themselves are composed of several points of interest and are great sources of the game's main currency, essence.

3. You then run from POI to POI completing them. These range from pattern-copying simon-says puzzles, hidden object game, building structures, and platforming to a few types of combat encounters. These POIs make up a good chunk of the gameplay loop and are the main source of the main currency, essence. They are really going to need to polish and expand these. Currently, some are quite tedious and either need better rewards for the amount of time they need (aid POI, lookin at you) or need to be less time consuming. I just skip the ones I don't feel like doing because you can always make a new realm.

4. While running from POI to POI, you will come across the various resources scattered around as well as wildlife, monsters, etc. This is when most of the gathering occurs for me. Fairly standard chopping trees, hitting rocks. The wildlife and wandering monsters generally aren't much of a challenge. There are named animals/monsters you can hunt (and they appear on your map), but they also aren't much of a challenge.

5. Return to your home realm and do all your base stuff. Sorting inventory, crafting, etc. This aspect def needs some QoL improvements. I spend a lot of time putting stuff away when I get home, it is very time consuming, increasing the time between realm runs. I would love to see something like a "move items to stack" button. Terraria has a "move items to stack in any chest near you" button that absolutely ruined other survival games for me. Every game with box based inventory management needs this. Farming is also pretty lackluster right now (i believe I read it is not working as intended currently).

Outside of this core gameplay loop, you eventually unlock dungeons. These are usually 3 POIs in a trenchcoat with a boss at the end. They're fine. You can also randomly encounter a portal to one of these dungeons as a POI, but I haven't encountered one of these as a POI with a boss at the end (that would be cool, though).

Progression is based strongly on recipe unlocks. You unlock recipes by buying them with essence from vendors called essence traders. Each combination of tarot cards has their own set of recipes that have a chance to appear on these vendors. You also unlock these recipes as rewards for completing POIs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale isn't what I thought it was going to be. Realms are repetitive, activities in said realms are repetitive, enemies are repetitive, and so on. The sense of discovery isn't really there. The lack of diversity in realms is lackluster. The crafting needs simplified. Now with the negative out of the way.

I love this game. Shocking with how negative I was in the beginning, I know. I can't wait to unlock the next stage of weapons to see how intricate and cool they are going to be. I love pre planning, setting aside top tier materials to be used in my next crafting session. The amount of building options for player homes are great. The ability to pre plan out how a structure is going to look is fantastic. Taking on Apex creatures is a challenge. Using the spells on weapons later on is quite fun. This game is perfect for 2 or more people to work toward common goals in a shared realm.

It's early access, I suspect or rather hope, that a lot is going to change. My main disappointment lies in the fact that there are no cinematics, a lack of diverse biomes, and having to go back into realms I'm overpowered for to farm T1 essence needed to upgrade items is a slog.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: New great take on the genre. Huge potential for growth.
This is one of the first Early Access games I can actually recommend playing.

I've been looking forward to this game for 3+ years now and I was worried about it after trying the stress test, but I still wanted to try the game and support the devs regardless just because of the theme and their new concept on survival games as a whole. I am glad I did! Yes, the start of the game is slow and needs a bit of work, but once you get the hang of it and unlock more carry capacity (craft a backpack) and you start discovering all the amazing things that are in the game it really starts to shine.

I love the way my character looks, it's such a unique art style. I get why some people might think the character models are ugly, but just play around in the settings and I am sure you will find something you love! I really enjoyed customizing my character. They said they'll be adding more ways to customize your character as time goes on.

I am not saying the combat is perfect, it does need some work but I find it rewarding. Switching weapons and enchantments, and just finding out the best combo that you enjoy is a lot of fun. I cannot wait until they add more down the road.

The crafting has to be one of the most interesting, complicated (in a good way), and the most satisfying I have seen in a game. It has a high learning curve and you need to do some research or just try things and figure it out as you go. Example: It really matters what materials you use when crafting, not only does it change the appearance of your items you craft but also every material has different stats. Figure out what works the best for you!

The portal system is such a great idea lore-wise and gameplay wise. You will have endless possibilities with your biome, major, and minor cards, especially as they add more over time. There is a unique feeling of immersion and excitement when opening a new realm, exploring and discovering it with my friends.

The graphics are beautiful! I haven't played a game this pretty in a long time. I actually stop and take screenshots constantly and I am not even on max graphics.

I really enjoyed building a huge Tudor mansion with my friend and we already have ideas for another base in a different biome. The building does need some QOL updates and a few changes but that's what EA is for.

I had a good time doing the main story(that's available so far) and learning more about the lore from the NPCS I have encountered. Obviously it's not RPG level of story, but the writing is very well done and if you like reading lore there's a lot out there to discover via the "echoes" you find randomly throughout the realms.

Lastly having a hub, like the Watch, where you can see and meet other players is what other survival games are missing: a sense of community through survival and working together.\t\t\t\t\t
