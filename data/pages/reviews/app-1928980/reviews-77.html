\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReleased too early for early access.

Combat - tedious. Either don’t do any damage to enemies or kill them in a couple of hits. Doesn’t feel balanced or meaningful. A level of jeopardy should be present when exploring and gathering

Farming - typical for a survival game. But there are far too many slight variances of the same resource. Tons of different woods, rocks etc these in turn bring with them different benefits when using that resource to craft something. But it makes for a chaotic storage process as there’s are way to many things to craft to make something. You need to turn wood to lumber to this, then that is then making something with that then combine that with this etc etc before you can craft. Massively needs consolidation.

Worlds - sort of generated worlds where non are the same….but there not really different either. Feels a bit rinse and repeat with not much by meaningful purpose of why bother doing any of it….just to incrementally increase your gear score.

Multiplayer - yeah…it’s present…sort of. Who won’t run across random people unless toggle that when going to a new zone….

Building - best thing about the game. The blueprint system is cool where it you can set out your base in blueprint before having the materials and it will auto build pulled from your inventory. Thank god it at least had this at launch…feel like you can do something with purpose.

I basically broke my rule of not buying ‘new’ releases as the meta seems to be release a barely passable game and patch it over the next year or so for it to be playable. Should have followed my gut and waited.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit on 30 March 2024: I've run out of fun things to do. I was having a blast folding metal, but turns out it was a bug and they fixed it. I've bought every recipe and blueprint, killed every boss. So it looks like the game has 255 hours or less of content.

Original Review: Kinda fun, but the crafting system is about as much fun as watching paint dry. If your players choose to tab out rather than sit for twenty minutes looking at the crafting panel then you are wrong. It's not so much a crafting system as a hostage situation. Not only is there no dopamine in farming crops and crafting, but if you had dopamine to begin with those tasks will drain them off and leave you dry. It's bad.

In addition to an obnoxious amount of resources, the crafting system suffers from too many augments for the crafting stations. Augments are things you attach to a station to gain new recipes. The problem is that there are a LOT of them and some work for multiple stations. Worse, you cannot simply click a station augment slot to see which augment can go there, and what recipes it offers. Instead, one must sort through the recipes and click each one to see what it does and where it goes. Be warned though, it doesn't do anything so useful as name the station it goes on, but instead gives a symbol that you must figure out because NONE of the stations have symbols, just names.

Speaking of the crafting system, this thing is massive and bloated with a ton of similar items that do not stack. Crafting is complicated because there are far too many options. It's really not fun at all. You'll spend more time trying to keep your inventory under control then doing anything fun due to how awful and bloated the crafting system is. This should be called an inventory management game.

You can get NPCs to "help" you, but they will also dump all of your best stuff into any station that consumes fuel, or whatever you are building. This often results in losing your best wood, stone, etc, to building quests and crafting stations with nothing useful to show for it.

The repair system is broken AF. If you hit repair all and you don't have enough to repair your most broken gear, it will use all of your essence and not repair anything. Do not give a repair hammer to your NPCs though, because they will use it to disassemble your base. No cap.

This game punishes you for not eating or sleeping, which is obnoxious. You do gain benefits from doing those things, but the punishment sux.

In a recent patch the return to base feature gained a wait period where before the patch it did not have one. There is speculation that this is a harbinger of PvP in the future.

This is the kind of game that is wildly popular on release, but slowly loses players due to bad design and the fact that it offers nothing we haven't seen before. That having been said, it's still in pre-release, so maybe they'll rework and streamline it into something that's more fun.

I wish there was a "Meh" button. Here's hoping they fix that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter about 40 hours I'm revising the review a bit.

I still recommend it, and I'd say for the most part it's basically the closest thing my wife and I can find to a PvE version of Rust that doesn't also have horrible graphics (sorry 7 Days To Die)

It's definitely a lot of fun, especially as you can effectively choose your challenge level based on the new "realms" you dial up (like a Stargate) as you progress through.

It's Early Access, and it shows however (although certainly far more polished at least as far as graphics goes).

That's not to say the game is perfect. It's pretty unstable and can crash a bit. Interestingly it crashes far more often on my wife's PC than mine - why is that interesting? Our rigs are almost identical. Although I do admit she was on Windows 10, I was on 11, and things sorted themselves out when we both went through and duplicated my graphics settings on her game.

So with this in mind, the graphics settings can cause you a LOT of grief it would seem if not set correctly.

Crafting is awesome, but the way "augmentations" work for the crafting stations is infuriating and confusing, this definitely needs work in my opinion.

Considering this is Early Access though - the game is definitely playable and worth a try; but I imagine it would be less interesting if playing solo. Definitely one to play with a friend.\t\t\t\t\t
