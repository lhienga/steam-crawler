\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm giving this a thumbs up because it will probably keep you entertained around 30 hours and $1/hr of entertainment seems fair.

Pros:
- quite beautiful. Sometimes reminded me of walking around in skyrim and just enjoying the ambience.

- the climbing picks are really cool, but also very buggy. They allow you to scale pretty much any vertical surface.

- a few interesting dungeon templates. I mainly liked the one with the extra portal at the bottom of the dungeon that led to another dungeon. More 15-30 minute content like that would be great.

- complex crafting system that takes material quality, crafting station, and crafting station augmentations into account when determining the outcome of the craft.

- the building system works well

- music is good and fits the theme well

- weapon hit feel is pretty good

- sound design is pretty good

- having both headshot and heartshot as critical weakspots is cool. enemies have vulnerabilities to different weapon types and damage types.

- they have a framework to create an interesting combat system that takes some inspiration from skyrim, vermintide 2, and destiny 2

- the realm card system is really interesting even in this game's early days. Lots of room to expand.

- can name portals, chests, and some other things which is nice for management

Cons:
- Combat. While the foundation is there, currently, combat lacks complexity and it seems you can get by through chugging health potions and lmb spamming enemies down.

- The application of procedural generation here is immersion breaking at some point. You will see the same structures over and over. I can't help but think each "level" and "type" of realm should have a bespoke and unique dungeon with greater rewards. Sprinkling the same towers/dungeons all over the map just makes me feel like i'm wasting my time and doesn't encourage me to try other realms.

- QoL is severely lacking in a lot of aspects. The developers, at the time of writing, are actively working on this.

- This game is currently online only and has pretty long load times as I assume every time you enter a realm they are reactivating a dormant server via a cloud service.

- Very grindy. I think this could be addressed by lowering the amount of resources required for some of the early and midgame crafts. Issues related to grind were exacerbated by early encumbrance limits (later mitigated by magic backpacks), painful inventory management, and lack of QoL.

- Way too many items in the game. There isn't just one type of lumber. For example, lumber alone has multiple tiers and variants like tier 1 swamp lumber, tier 1 forest lumber, tier 1 desert lumber, tier 2 swamp lumber, etc. These items do not stack and makes the cumbersome inventory management of a survival game even worse.


Summary:
The game definitely drew me in, but once the shock of the pretty visuals and novel experiences wear off you are left with unfinished game systems, mid combat, and repetitive short-form content. They should focus on improving combat and reworking the fae towers/ruins/encounters (what I'll call "resource towers"). The resource towers sprinkled across the map would be more interesting if they were fewer, more rewarding, and longer form. Some other improvements are probably down to QoL changes and math/stat tweaks to make you feel like you're playing the game rather than playing an inventory management sim. This game is so close to being awesome and I'm looking forward to more updates.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a EA this game is great. It is not getting the recognition it deserves. I expect great things in the future for this game.

PRO - The realm cards and RNG based lands you explore using them is amazing.
- Steampunk mixed with fae realms goes really well together.
- The enemies while not having a large variety yet, does have some very flavorful and interesting choices. If they continue to expand on them it should give a rewarding challenge with a variety of threats. (What is there is very good but you need to learn their behaviors or they may annoy you.)

CON - As a survival game it is adequate for EA but needs ironing out of a few bugs/qol problems that have been acknowledged and are working towards fixing.
- They are removing online only play which is one of its biggest problems atm.
- My biggest complaint that seems to be a feature, is the not always clear goals on how to do things. I have had to google a couple things to move forward.

There is more pro's and con's, but those are the big ones. Give it a try if you are willing to give EA titles a try. If EA titles piss you off, don't.\t\t\t\t\t
