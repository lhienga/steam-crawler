\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhilst clearly a V1 in early access. This game has a huge amount of potential that I was not expecting.

Combining a similar exploratory vibe as No Mans Sky, with crafting and base building akin to Valhiem culminating in an MMO style Raid system at the end game. The foundation to build on here is amazing.

The crafting system currently needs a lot of QOL upgrades and feels intimidating to wrap around. But given the pedigree of the developers, they are clearly wanting to give players the full ability to sculpt and design their builds to match their play style. Some may say its "clunky" which it is, but the design principle behind it is solid. Cutting out some of the busywork will be ideal.

At first the game feels like it has tedious grinds, but after about 10 hours it clicked with the minor realm cards. Want to build a huge house with lots of decorations? Use a Use a lumbermill card to double the ammount of logs you get from trees and massively boost the quantity of refines wood parts from your saw bench. Then plug in a Settler card to make all of your building parts have a higher crafting yield.

Understanding these systems to bend the world your needs is intrinsic to the game and could be better explained but at V1 with no patches yet, we can expect refinements around this.

Overall, recommended but may not be for those who find games in development too fiddly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs this it? Somewhere around the 10-20 hr playtime mark you will probably get such a feeling. Persevere, the tutorial has almost ended...
Yup, whatever anyone tells you, you're not in the full game yet. You're in a an early very stretched out loop of finding marginally better equipment and unlocking cards by doing a boss in a dungeon. But that's not the game. The game starts when you get your real quest, get to the Watch. Now all that going around and all those cards you unlocked suddenly make sense. Those monsters you killed with weird drops, suddenly click, as when you use those materials with whatever you can find in the highest areas you can manage, you can make your game much easier (or harder) for yourself, when designing your own gear. At over 100 hours. It's a fun concept, with VERY granular and in-depth crafting system (which I suspect most WILL NOT like). Eg: Ccraft A & B to make C, combine C w/ C-2, to make D, then combine D with F, K, and J, (all created like C) to make a (late-mid to late-game) Axe. This system gives you quite a bit of control over what stat values go into your items (but not the stats themselves). However, it can be tedious or outright off-putting to the uninitiated.

The game could have easily been single-player, and DOES NOT naturally lend itself to multi-player without an existing friend-group, but still went into EA as online-only. I don't care what it launches as, but the inconsistency with what they intended in terms of MP is clear and present. The game presented itself as multiplayer but without an existing friend-group, you will see zero people before you get to The Watch, which can take some time unless you are speedrunning the "MSQ." After The Watch, without an existing party of 6 maximum, multiplayer is limited to whatever randoms end up in the same boss realm as you from one of the portals at the bottom of The Watch... If you can get through the portal.

They are now shifting to develop an offline mode; however, this leaves the online play struggling as you may spend upwards of 5-10 minutes just trying to not disconnect when you take a portal literally anywhere (including non-portal fast travel if you are changing servers like between The Watch/Respite)

Some basic QoL features do not exist (crafting directly from inventory; certain items do not deconstruct back into base materials, etc), your companion (which is optional to recruit, but tacitly required) has very poor AI and can't be manually controlled/given instructions. They also must be given full sets of gear unless you want them getting 1-shot by everything late game. Lastly, for as granular as the crafting is for literally everything, there is no transmog or appearance editing; Gear/weapons will always have a certain initial and (potentially) maximum gear-score even, if you pack it to the gills with the rarest, highest-spec materials.

TL;DR: I like the game and will continue playing, as I'm familiar with the growing pains of Alpha/EA/Beta phase games, but I do not recommend it for anyone looking for a feature-complete, polished, fully-released game. NG is the epitome of the "release it, charge for it, and fix it later," EA model. If it weren't for the in-depth crafting, different styles of structure building, and trying to see how far I can go with that system, I would have uninstalled after about 15-20h or so. Overall, I recommend it for those who know what they're getting into, and will probably be very enjoyable (ability to get into servers permitting) to build/craft-a-holics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel like it is time to address the review culture around this game, which is indeed a bit harsh and almost held me from playing a game i enjoy playing a lot. Also i have to add that i am not married to or related to a dev of this game in any way.

For starters i have to say that i am a Relic of a time where a game worth 30 bucks offered a campaign that lasted 25 hours and then just was over and the only thing you could do was to play it again. 10-15 years ago we considered this fine because 25 hours of entertainment were worth 30 bucks, when you go to the cinema you get 2 hours for 10 bucks and you would not complain about the value you get right there.

Today's Generation views things a lot different, i unfortunetaly read too many steam reviews recently and too often came over the fact that people nowadays play videogames for 50+ hours and then give them a thumbs down complaining about systems that are not convenient enough, the amount of content being limited and the pace of the game being to slow/ not rewarding fast enough. I am not very conform with this Culture.

For Nightingale special i really wanted to address this, because somewhere inside of me i knew this is exactly the type of game i would enjoy right now, while the reviews held me from buying it on release day.

And thats exactly the first point i will address: why do people bother to make a rant in a steam review of a game which is obviously not the type of game they are looking for/ enjoy playing as their comfort game if their opinion is not product of false expectations or even meant in a destructive rather than constructive way?

And then there is those who repeatedly start their arguments by saying something like ,,i know that this is a early access, but´´, and then in the rest of the argument completely ignore the fact that this is a early access...? Again i sort this a similar way as the first point, this type of argumentation seems not to meet realistic expectations of the product nor being meant constructive and only constructive.

Fortunately i am here to tell you what type of game this is, so you can build an idea if this is for you or not, and summarize some of the usefull criticisms to give you a realistic expectation of the product in its current state.

Please when considering to play this game note the fact that this is graphically a very advanced game and you will need to have a decent PC to get a smooth performance out of it. That said the graphics of this Game are stunning and the different seeds that are generated through cards you combine freely are stunning as well. The only thing i would critic about its visual immersion right now is that the creatures filling this world often move a bit clunky or even stutter through the world breaking the immersion of your surroundings a decent bit, This is something the devs can improve in the future.
Still the world and its immersion are beautiful.

This is a slow paced soft-core survival game centered around a crafting system with its strength being its complexity, while its weakness right now being its inconvenience in some points due to missing systems as well as a sometimes unhandy User Interface. Still the foundations of it being complex in a good way if you like and enjoy complex crafting systems.
You have to manage quiet a big amount of resources and different crafting recipes, this is something you need to be liking to be able to enjoy the fundamental part of Nightingale, i consider this a strength if you do so.

In its current state you have to open different menus sometimes a bit often too look after things you need to have in mind for one single recipe which can be addressed by the devs in upcoming patches to make the crafting experience a bit more convenient, as well as this game needs a system that draws items automaticly out of chests in the area from any crafting station for any recipe. I see these as the easier points to address for the devs in the future.

The amount of Content of this game is quiet surprising and a big pro for a game being in early access still testing its foundations. You come to the point where you build a portal into your base if you want so and freely explore worlds that are based on the cards you combine creating them, and there are a lot of combinations possible. You can sink a ton of hours of your lifetime in this game if you want so, on that front the 30 bucks pricing is easily justified.

All in all i can say if you like slow paced exploring and progressing through a game, this might be for you.
If you like complex crafting systems always taking you to read through a lot of stuff and hunting for specific resources then this might be for you.
If you are immersed by graphically stunning and and creative world building and enjoy even just walking around finding places that let you forget reality then this might just be for you.
And if none of that describes the type of game you enjoy playing then why in the world would i recommend this to you? Well i cant answer that...

Yes, this is a early access and there are things the devs can improve on almost every front, but that does not make it a bad game in general, at this point i have to contradict the reviews picturing this as a disappointing product in general.

Having an average score of 6 on steam right now i dont feel like the product is in the position it deserves, considering the argumentation against the review culture i just submitted. Still this is subjective in many ways.

I personally would give it a solid 7,5 out of 10, with the potential to climb to an even higher rating with small improvements on its Systems and UI, as well as through adding content in the future.
