\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wouldn't recommend this one its current state. There are some promising aspects to the game, but there are just so many issues right now that you may as well just wait to see how development progresses before jumping in.

1. Network issues and load times - Multiple times I've received errors when loading into my world, once i even lost progress due to this. Other times I've just become stuck in a connection screen and had to kill the game to get out.

2. Grind - It is a bit too heavy for specific items. I've played pretty much all of these survival/craft games, and this one is pretty heavy on the grind side of things. More so even than games like Icarus. Especially when you factor in that you will be required to go to very specific realms for certain items, and will be tied into that realm for a bit while gathering.

3. Quality of life items - A lot of items that have started to become standard in this type of game are absent here. Things like the ability to craft from nearby chests have become features that should be standard by now. I hope that this is added soon.

4. Always online - This is a dealbreaker for many, and is the main reason I decided to not recommend the game. I really have no interest in endgame hubworlds with random people in this type of game. To me, this is a net-negative.

5. tutorial/beginning - The intro to the game wasn't too bad, but there were multiple times where text tutorial boxes popped up while Puck was talking, or while something was attacking me, and I just had to rush through them to get them off the screen. I also feel like the estate building portion was not covered well at all. It also didn't give me much of an option as to where to build.

6. Character creation - I realize this one doesn't particularly impact gameplay, but boy are the character models hideous, lol. I ended up giving up and just going goofy-style since I couldn't make anything that didn't look like a neanderthal.

Overall, If this game had been released like 3 months back, I'd be recommending it, but with better games like Enshrouded & Palworld coming out so recently, I just don't see a reason to pick this one up at this point if you haven't already played through those. Just let it bake for awhile, and I think it will improve.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: I am actually shocked at how poor the reception and reviews have been. This is, by far, the best survival-craft game released to date in 2024. That includes Palworld and Enshrouded (which are both amazing too).

TL;DR: There are some valid criticisms for the game, but the unique aesthetic, crafting system, realm mechanics, and endgame content more than make up for it. Overall 9/10, but will jump to 10/10 when they polish some of the nuanced issues (companion AI, augment rework, always-online, etc.).

Here are the pros I've experienced:

1.) It has by far the best crafting system of any game in recent memory. Every single crafting material has unique stats. Every piece of wood. Every animal skin. Every bone. It all meshes incredibly well with the open-ended crafting system, and allows for some really complex builds & gear.

2.) It has an extremely unique aesthetic (Steampunk/Victorian). Don't let the early-game fool you into thinking this is another low-effort Rust clone. The mid-to-end game content will have you dripped out like a Victorian-era nobleman/noblewoman, with customizable guns to boot (Winchester rifle anyone?). For quite a while now, I have searched for a survival craft game with visuals that stray from the cliche "caveman" gear or military garbs, and now it exists!

3.) The realm card system as a whole offers a unique approach to progression. I found it extremely rewarding to drop a difficult minor card into a boring realm to spice it up, change loot drops, etc. Minor cards can affect things such as difficulty, mechanics, physics, loot tables, and visuals (pop a minor card and watch the sky). It's quite varied, and I can say I haven't really explored all of the current option during my 120+ hours in-game.

4.) The endgame is a wildly better demonstration of this game than anything in the first 10-12 hours. You can expect: co-op matchmaking for dungeons with puzzles, raid bosses, and much more. Gear progression also blossoms around this time, allowing you to customize powerful weapons and armor to your liking. Want to rock a golden Winchester and dump lead like Arthur Morgan? You got it. You want to fan-fire a six shooter with the grace of a cowboy? Say less. I cannot overstate how much better my experience got around this point.

On to the cons I experienced:

1.) The companion AI is actually terrible right now. It is optional to bring a companion, so this issue can be avoided at least. In short, the companions will actively put the powerful materials you give them into fires as fuel. They also commonly chop trees down around you and/or your house, causing damage to you and your buildings.

2.) The always-online system is obviously not ideal. The company has responded and is working on an offline mode, so this issue will be eventually resolved. Most of my problems with this are due to disconnects, rubberbanding/lag, and of course, dependence on a server outside of my control to play the game.

3.) The very early game experience is actually pretty poor. I was optimistic going in, so I managed to get through it, but they really need to rework this. For the first ~10-12 hours, I can say I was not feeling very impressed. Beyond this point, however, the game really comes to life. YMMV of course.

In summary, I think the reviews for this game have been much too harsh. I can confirm having played Nightingale, Enshrouded, and Palworld for around 120-140+ hours each, I really believe this is the best of the three. Even at the time of this review, there is still miles of content I haven't touched, and the game just launched in early access 9 days ago. I have no doubt this game is a 9/10 with an almost guaranteed boost to 10/10 when the add an offline mode and fix companion AI.\t\t\t\t\t

UPDATED REVIEW
After playing deeper in the game, I'm back to add that this game tackles what every other survival game assumes is standard operating procedure. And yes, there are a group of gamers who love survival titles and complain about wanting something different but not THIS different. You can't use your S.O.P. in this game to meta your way through. It's colorful, creative and in early access with a fully flushed out gameplay that leaves too many things I look forward to experiencing still, even after 16 days of gameplay. Best of all, this game is built for nothing BUT continued expansion, something that some of us are always complaining about when we're 8 years in to a game like conan and love it but bored of the same maps over and over.

I read the negative reviews and keep feeling like these people are disappointed that their meta ways aren't getting them to the end game fast enough. This game is built to remove the "king of the hill" gameplay that's too disruptive in survival games today. Instead you find yourself going at your own pace, with your own interests and enjoying the creativity of others that you know and trust enough to invite into the realms of your making.

Yes, this game moves slower than many, but in a way that requires you to stretch outside your "beat the game" process and just explore. This is not a game for people who need to get to the end game in a day so they can start dominating.

The guidebook process is magnificent. You don't need youtube videos of someone who's figured things out and it's not because the quests aren't hard enough. The developers give us a crafting menu that allows you to not only see HOW to craft whatever you need but also to track it on your questbar AND they give you a journal that lists your current quests and deeper insights on how to fullfill each step of said quests. It's a lovely knowledge base that's been missing from survival games for a decade.

I use a controller because i'm left handed, and as I complain in most games, there's no left hand flip button anymore so keybinding reversals will come in time once I get my footing. But having the controller option is nice so I can relax and enjoy the landscape. It works well and adds to the relaxed intensity of the game.

Someone wrote about essence collection and how hard it was at a certain level to get lower tier essence but that just tells me they're trying to tank the game and not get the point. farming can be converted to essence. hunting can be converted to essence. Cards are played on a world to drastically improve essence. Charms and perks added to self, armor and tools/weapons all create whatever type of collection/effect you can want. It's a game of pieces where you collect all the pieces and then create the world and environment you want or need in that moment. There is no ONE way to play the game and yet there are also no predetermined meta builds or classes trees that you must choose and stay in. It's YOUR game to make and it's only limited by you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t💠 Early Access Review 💠

I hesitate to recommend this game... because, while there is a lot that I do really like about it, the main idea (and promise) of the game -- was the possibility of "infinite realms" to explore -- and that just isn't fully realized yet.

I want to walk through a new portal and be utterly surprised and in awe by the "infinite" possibilities that I might see when exploring a new realm. As of the time of this review... there is no surprise or major awe.

I want to explore lots of *unique* ("infinite") REALMS! -- Right now, each "Realm" just feel like a set "biome" that is generated slightly differently each time. Therefore, this does not lead to that feeling of wanting to explore each nook-&-cranny of every new realm I enter, because there just isn't enough difference and variety (per biome) to make me feel like I'm seeing things I haven't seen before.

That said...
1️⃣ The overall *look* (or "vibe") of the environments, as well as the music, are both stunning and quite enjoyable! No complaints. ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

2️⃣ The crafting system is amazing! It's one of the best crafting systems I've seen (recently) in a survival game. And other than not being able to craft items from the resources in our chests, I have no other major gripes about the crafting system! ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

3️⃣ The building system is serviceable, and can allow for some really neat looking structures, but ultimately could be better. We need to be able to build at angles, and not be so locked into the cookie-cutter boxes and placement locations -- especially for the decorations! We need more building pieces and decorations. But I do love that you can pre-plan your buildings (and can get 100% resources back when removing things)! If they can improve these things, I'll be quite happy! ⭐⭐⭐

4️⃣ The combat system is also serviceable, and is decently engaging, but it could also be better. We need more enemy types (always!), and I feel like the combat mechanics could be tightened up a bit -- especially in terms of enemy AI! But I don't hate the combat outright... there is a decent tactile feeling to swinging and landing melee hits (or firing weapons and landing shots) -- this is a definite plus! So, if they can improve some of the combat, in certain ways, it will be amazing! ⭐⭐⭐

5️⃣ Finally, the Realm system is such an amazing concept, but, as stated above, is only *barely* serviceable as of the time of this review. We need at *least* 3 more biome cards -- ASAP! And, if you ask me, the game could uses a system for the POI's, where the procedural AI, that generates each realm, would pull from a pool of hand-crafted POI's and other realm-specific landmarks, etc. -- and would not just use the same POI's again and again. Because, as it stands, once you've seen each biome (Forest, Desert, Swamp), you've basically seen them all. And the major & minor cards just aren't making me feel like I'm witnessing the possibility of "infinite realms!" ⭐⭐

▫ The Major cards just feel like a linear story-progression / gear-score check type of system -- with some NPC/narrative elements (and blueprints) locked behind each tier of major card. Thus far, I haven't seen any Major card *radically* change any of the biomes to the point of being surprised by an "infinite" possibilities of unique realms.

▫ And the minor cards are merely for a realm-wide buff / de-buff system, that usually comes with a change in the environment's color pallet and other minor effects.

📌 In summary...
I'm 60 hrs in (and I'm only Gear Score 70 -- out of 200+)! I definitely do plan to keep playing, because I'm a sucker for a good crafting system! Averaging my star ratings, for the 5 categories above, I would give the game = 3.6 / 5 stars.

At this time though, the gameplay-loop (and realm system) does feel a bit stale to me -- the game needs even more "shock & awe" added in (new enemies, new biomes, etc etc etc). I want to explore "infinite realms" of "infinite possibilities!"

However, I can see the *vast* potential this game has, and I'm totally here for it, because there is enough that I do like, that I can overlook the things I don't love just yet.

So, the reason I'm recommending the game, is because I do believe there are individuals who will love this type of game, and should definitely try it out, especially while it's cheaper (during Early Access). It's not a perfect game (yet), but it has so much going for it, and has a stellar team of devs working on it -- and they appear (so far) to be quite responsive to their community! So, I feel like that should be praised.

That said... this game, in its current state, will not be for everyone. Some people hate the always-online element. I personally don't, but its an understandable gripe. Some may find the game boring, because some elements of it are! But the Devs have already shown they have every intention of fixing a lot of these issues, and adding more content.... So, I'm recommending the game, because I do believe this will be a pristine gem -- in time!\t\t\t\t\t

++ Visuals: Using the UE5 engine quite well

However, it is very much an Early Access game, so take that into consideration before buying. At the price point though, I've spent my nearly 101 hours in the game having a lot of fun, and I am looking forward to revisiting later on.\t\t\t\t\t
