\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel the majority of the negative reviews are people who lack the capacity to look deeper than top surface level for survival games especially EARLY ACCESS.

That being said this game is one of the most innovative and new survival games I've come across in a while. Once you find all the little details you can tell just how much work went into said details.

Read ahead for a very in depth review.
My CONS in part 2

-----------------------------------

CHARACTER CREATION / TUTORIAL
Not going to lie, get ready for some uglier preset characters in the main screen, but they don't look nearly that bad in game. You can also edit facial features pretty in depth. They have an interesting concept of "ancestry" to make your face look like past relatives if you chose to go down that path (not necessary).

Tutorial is nice and straight forward.

-----------------------------------

GRAPHICS
This game is simply very very beautiful. Sun-rays, rain, fog, lots of things that give an immersive feeling of being in a real world. Stunning in every biome, very well made worlds.

-----------------------------------

QUESTING
There is a main quest line to follow, and some side quests you can find. Not too many side quests yet but they do have special rewards. Main quest is pretty interesting if you enjoy some game lore, and there is a point where you can complete an objective in very different ways. (one is a quick method, the other is longer but more "lore" intensive)

-----------------------------------

EXPLORATION / WORLDS
There is a combination of 13 default realms, each which have 3 biomes to choose, with 4 available difficulties. You choose whether its a private or public realm (default is private).
Doing that math that's 156 combinations of worlds you can do. Most overlap in what they provide, but simply put, there's 156 combinations to put in a portal. (all of which can be manually reset as well, to generate a new procedural generated map). On top of this 156, there are also things called "MINOR REALM CARDS" that can be played on the world for a general world "buff" or "effect". Jump higher and further or craft more ingots at the smith or put the world in forever darkness and take constant damage for bonus loot for example. There are tons of those minor realm cards to choose from.

There are a set number of types of challenges that each world has, which honestly can be fairly repetitive, but I personally have felt each new world I've opened is a completely different setup due to the procedural generation aspect for some of these challenges. Exploring feels fun, and when you complete the Fae tower for that world it adds markers in the map to go complete. There are "fabled" creatures that show on the map that give special resources which are harder versions of normal creatures.

The difficulty and combination of biome matters for what type of materials you find. For example on an easy difficulty world you find tin (ore), but on a much higher difficulty you find titanium (ore) but only on that higher difficulty and biome. There is a glossary for how to target these resources.

You craft an "estate" which is a fast travel back to "home base". You can craft and share a card from your world to allow friends to portal travel to your world (even when you are offline)

-----------------------------------

COMBAT (long section)
Combat is a combination of damage types and "styles". There are a combination of melee, and range weapons each with their own damage TYPE. Slashing, piercing, bludgeoning, missile, and finally magic. Each have their place in being effective against different enemies resistances.

MELEE
Pretty straight forward, smack things, chop things, do the damage in their face. Obviously different types of weapons have different bonuses.
Want to be a "tank" with crowd control? Use the maul (bludgeoning damage) for high strength (higher chance to knock down enemies) at the cost of not being able to hit crits.
Want to do high single target damage while things are distracted? Use a knife (piercing damage) for high crit damage to backstabs on enemies (or their head) at the cost of lower base damage elsewhere.
Want a tool that can also do multiple types of damage but cant block? Use the axepick to do slashing and piercing depending on how you use it.

RANGE
Also straight forward, shoot things with bullets. All of these do "missile" damage
There are 4 different main range weapons, each with different variants.
Shotgun, Pistol, Rifle, crossbow (shoots marbles, not bolts - interesting...)
Each have their trade-off like range vs crit damage vs fire rate, etc. There are special bullets you can craft for elemental damage with special effects instead of simple normal bullets.

MAGIC
The way magic is implements is certainly different from a typical magic. There are no staves or wands.
You craft/find and apply what are called "enchantments" to weapons such as a maul and you can "cast" from that maul. The "mana" is essentially durability of that maul. Each time you cast, it takes some durability away, and a little stamina to cast.
You can do quite a bit with magic. (not everything available is listed here)
Crowd control with the "quake/shockwave" spells and taunt with "Jana's roar".
Support allies with the "ring of empowerment" for more damage to allies, and group heal with "salvation".
Be more offensive with the "summon swarm", or the "blastminer's trap".
Give yourself personal buffs with "assassin's sight" for more crit damage or "arborist's shield" for more defense.
With this being early access, there's plenty more sure to come.

Combat is fulfilling enough for survivalcraft, there are a decent amount of enemies and different type of enemies. Mainly a force of melee enemies with some ranged (more like magic) enemies sprinkled in there (actual enemies, not animals). Once you get to end game gear, it does become a little easier, but that's because the end game gear score is just higher than the current highest level of enemies, so that's expected.

-----------------------------------

CRAFTING

ARMOR / WEAPONS
Crafting is very in depth for this game in the sense of STATS. Each material has stats on it for specific things it will buff.
For example, making a rifle out of pursuit ore is going to be better than copper ore because pursuit ore has a "ranged damage" increase. Copper does not have any ranged damage bonus, so it would end up with less overall damage if you made the rifle with copper ingots. This does make storage a little painful due to so many different stats on items, but just keep a chest for each type and you'll be fine.

There are stats for melee, stats for magic power, durability, resistances etc.
With some planning you can maximize your stats for a Maul to get the best melee damage you can get. Or make armor that has some bonuses in ranged damage to focus range. Combinations of stats are limitless, though some are clearly better than others.

Armor sets have base set stats, and whatever materials are used are what increase these base stats. There is armor for more health to be tankier, there is armor for more stamina and higher sneak %, there is armor for more magick power. Sets do have meaning rather than just "higher tier" than before. Lower "tiers" do not always fall out of usefulness. Weapons and armor have gear score but gear score is not always indicative of its usefulness.

STATIONS
Crafting stations currently have 3 tiers to them and you build "augments" next to stations to unlock the recipes you have found in the world. Augments can affect multiple stations, so you can "double up" with augments. Different augments give a list of different unlocks, but some can unlock the same recipes. (Ex. I need to craft the ammo crate OR the cannon augment to be able to craft firearms.) Some augments purely provide a decrease in crafting/refining time.

-----------------------------------

Overall I highly recommend this game to anyone potentially interested.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't say I enjoyed this game much. I didn't like the building at all. Crafting was way too complicated with all the different augmentation. Was having to grind way too much to get materials. Not to mention the crafting didn't make a lot of sense. For example, you made broth at the mortar station. Why wouldn't you cook that on the fire?? No time to just go out and explore, had to farm materials all the time. You had so many stacks of the same materials but were different levels or from different Biomes. Stupid. Made bag storage fill up because you had stuff that didnt stack even though it was the same thing. Every map was basically the same. Just added effects but it was still the same map. Got boring really quick. I really wanted to like the game but I honestly didnt from the very moment I started playing. I bought the game on launch and been playing it for a total of 142 hours, but I think today is my last day. Just not enjoyable, gave it a good shot.\t\t\t\t\t
