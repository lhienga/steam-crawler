\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAllow me to begin by stating that this is a revision of my initial review. If you wish to read that scroll down and have a look. Otherwise, I would not recommend this game to anyone in its current state. Overall the main idea behind the game is fairly decent. However, the implementation is poor. Poor is an understatement. This game is touted as a survival game but, it does not have the proper feel for progression that makes survival games resonate with the player. PROGRESSION is CRITICAL in a survival game; especially the feel of said progression. This game does not have it. Progression feels random and gifted rather than planned for and earned. It feels like spinning wheels and getting nowhere. I'm sure it's all tied up in the essence system and a plan for selling progress (high level - essence) to frustrated players who like the aesthetics of the game. Check my profile if you like. I have thousands of hours racked up in survival titles. Not this one.
Survival gamers go elsewhere, there's nothing here for us. Ill check back in down the road and see if they change course.

It's a bit sad but, let's be honest, the problem is that the 'game' completely lacks compelling game play. I spent all my game time working to get the portal going with the hope that the game would suddenly open up and show me something. Sadly, the next world was basically a cut and paste of the previous world with a new color pallet. There are no survival systems in place. The creatures were all basically the same creatures in both worlds. You walk far enough away for a long enough time and the very same creatures spawn back into the very same location in the very same configuration.

And, when I say survival systems, I mean survival systems that build upon each other that progress you towards a better situation. In other words, you depend on yourself to create your way out of starvation or exposure, etc. In this 'game' the work you are doing is to create a bigger number on a stat card. It completely lacks the survival feel. The only way to get new stuff is to purchase it from a vendor or maybe get a lucky reward. Its gated progression controlled completely by the developer rather than an interconnected system influenced by character build and player agency.

I imagine they are going to argue that there is agency but there is not. Every single player has to do the exact same things in nearly the exact same order to achieve the exact same result. Gear score 20. Then, build some portal cards. There are no choices. The game even tells you the portal card you are going to build. It's all so very boring. They couldn't even be bothered to put some effort into the games beginning and engineer a system that would have you choosing from three tasks that would lead to the portal card you wanted to start with. Or, create a system that had you crafting your way to the portal card you wanted. Either way, they choose it all for you then feed you more of the same.

It's just a bad game. Maybe they'll fix it but, I think their foundational system is junk. It's going to take a complete overhaul to turn it into a worthy survival game. I'm not sure that they know what a survival game is.


Ps. Did I mention that the maps are tinie tiny things you can run across in about 60 seconds? ..2 minutes tops if the generator dumps impassable cliffs in your way. I think the word vast was used in connection with the word realms.. must have been marketing buzz ( aka, lies ). Sad.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t35 hours in:
* The game is generally pretty fun, especially in the early game
* Music, SFX and graphics are great but there should be potato mode for people with lower and mid-range PCs, I have pretty good rig with 4080 super and it works pretty good for me but the amount of streamers I've seen playing the game and having fun while the game barely hits 30fps is a bit staggering.
* There is wayyyyyy too much grind and way toooooo early, especially if the materials you need are quite rare.
* Tutorials for certain systems are very lacking
* Ease of use for an interface like tooltips for items crafted are missing
* Can't assign npc helpers to do what you want and AFK farm
* Game feels like you are playing on 0.3 times multiplier for items when compared to amount of resources you need.
* As you can play the game fully solo then allow server changes for rates such as resource gain etc. ( besides cards that have hard cap and can't mix them up)


General info:
* It shows that it's early access pretty fast with missing icons for items that you will use A LOT, which makes inventory management quite annoying
* systems for enhancements for building need work, recipes don't unlock sometimes, and other times you need to have benches crafted very close to each other to get the perks creating a pretty bad housing experience.

Specific needed changes:
* Cards system for realm-wide boosts needs a bit of a rework, it should be easy to tell what card does maybe with a tooltip on mouseover.
* Rewards through challenges should be claimed automatically or show you easier that you can claim them instead of going to the submenu of the submenu
* Repairing items should be possible with different tier essences and the higher tier you use the less it takes- repairing blue gear is an absolute nightmare and you need an insane amount of Tier 1 essences to repair your items that are tier 2 and Tier 3, which forces you to revisit tier 1 realms and farm essences with pretty bad drop rates even with cards, and that's on top of farming for other resources that also deplete your durability making whole experience gruesomely grindy for no apparent reason- usually it's only done in live service games that want you to keep staying online and pay for stuff.
* There should be a system to downgrade higher-tier items to lower-tier to avoid going to lower-tier realms for no reason, especially as realms don't reset if you use the same cards combo.

A lot of other things need fixes and it's pretty apparent as well, but generally game has decent direction so far.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy husband and I have been waiting for this game forever. It is rare to find an open world survival game that allows you to do all the things you can do in Nightingale -- but in full co-op mode. It's a beautiful thing. Building, crafting, upgrading, exploring, combat to suit your play style, cooking, and meeting other NPCs. It's all in there. Some early issues we've noticed that we hope the devs will address in future patches include:

1. When playing with a partner, there is no shared loot system. Not 100% sure, but if I recall correctly, it's the same issue with quests. If you're completing a quest together from the same NPC, you each have to talk to the NPC separately to complete and collect rewards. When in co-op and grouped, the quest completion and looting system should be set to equitably distribute across players. (Ref: Tom Clancy's The Division 1/2)

2. Sometimes when sprinting, my character will blip backwards or forwards on the map. This is not a RAM or buffering issue, as I'm running on 64MB RAM which is more than sufficient for this game.

3. Would love a creative option. Sure this is coming, but for this kind of genre, you're going to attract a lot of the creative builder type players, and we like creative options to see what we can come up with! The grind is fine to experience the game and learn the mechanics initially, but this really needs a creative option for final release.

4. Need an option to play offline or on LAN instead of online 100%. (Ref: 7 Days to Die)

Character customization is lovely, and we are excited to see what else comes down the road as this game evolves. Definitely worth a play! And even better with a friend....\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am annoyed by the developers communication. It's ironic that everything revolves around the CEO, but the execution of the game is one of the worst I have ever seen by an industry veteran.

It riddles me why they released the game with the broken controller implementation. Nightingale is obviously designed with keyboard+mouse in mind, relying a lot on an UI with collapsed menus and hovertips. To make it worse in the current state, throw in a bizarre amount of placeholder icons or indistinguishable items.

In an otherwise stunning visual presentation, the missing UI images are a bad omen for the future development. I don't care about more content, I am more interested in finished content.

They claim that they pulled controller support last minute, but it doesn't seem it was ever there in a playable state or ever will be. Hence, don't even waste your time trying this thing on a Steam Deck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked the setting of the game, its style and the initial plot, and it's clear that the developers care about the game and plan to develop it further, improving on the Early Access.

And while their short and long-term plans are worthwhile and will certainly improve the game, for me the thing that put me off was not the absence of the offline mode (everywhere I play the internet connection is available and I was not planning to play from the beach or hospital), but rather that the NPCs (both friendly and hostile) move in an unnatural way, similar to the games from '00s. They just walk around mindlessly, from one direction to the other or (in the case of a hired companion) start mining rocks in an impulsive-looking way while walking past them. One of the first vendors I met was just
standing facing a building wall, and just staring at it without moving. I have seen modern games where NPCs act a lot more convincingly and in a natural-looking way, but here it killed the immersion for me.

It's sad because I liked the game's style and setting, and I am not sure this could be easily fixed.\t\t\t\t\t
