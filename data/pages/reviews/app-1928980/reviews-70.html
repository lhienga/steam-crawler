\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game needs a lot of help. Like post-traumatic therapy level of help. At first, it needs to reduce the scope of what it wants to be. Its MMO mechanics are from another decade and already feel antiquated. Its survival aspect is fraught with a bloated and disconnected crafting system, large biomes that primarily act as resource node containers with a bare-bones exploration aspect. Combat, UI, and responsiveness feel janky.

Every system in this game needs to be marie-kondo'd, if it doesn't spark joy, then get rid of it or rework it until it does, and if the whole game is at stake then so be it. Otherwise, I don't see a clear vision of what the developers want the game to be. If procedurally generated mystic Fae realms are supposed to be the focus point, then work on it. The whole idea of having Fae realms that can be tuned and tweaked with cards sounds interesting, but the current implementation absolutely isn't. Put the idea of multiplayer at rest for now and work on the systems that revolve around the single-player aspect for now, and make them work. Begin with the crafting, work your way up to the discovery and exploration aspect, then combat and boss battles. Right now, it's sad to see promising ideas handled so badly, and if nothing changes, this will be at best a single-play through experience.

EDIT: Yes, I am still playing it. My review isn't a statement of hatred but of concern. I personally like the game's concept, which is why I will still be play-testing it. If you are like me and want to see a good product in the end — despite my criticism — then beware that the game is pretty barebones and in Alpha stage. Things will be very rough and have a placeholder feeling to them, to the point of being infuriating, and there may be a chance that this game will in the end go nowhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT:Wow...just....wow....After all the comments about how terrible of a resource grind this game has been, in the latest patch the devs have cut the essence value of marbles in half, and tripled the cost of basic resources. The one thing that made this game tolerable was being able to factory farm the basic resources, and they have now cut that down to 1/6 of the power. I will *NEVER* do another aid mission now. They were already woefully under-rewarded, but now I can't even buy the stuff from the trader? seriously? You want me to spend an hour chopping down trees for 17 t1 essence? HARD. PASS.

AND!!! as if waiting for the gates to open, and then having to wait for it to connect to the server to prepare your realm wasn't bad enough, now my respite realm has to be prepared every time I want to use it?!

This game was pretty bad to start with and is now going in the absolute opposite direction that it needed to be going. Stay FAR away from this until the devs prove they actually listen to input

....

I legitimately cannot understand what they were thinking with that crafting system. I don't so much have a problem with the chaining of things together...it sucks, and it's low QOL, but whatever.

The problem is augmentations. I'm not especially far into the game, and I currently have 28 augmentations. If you are not familiar with the mechanic, they need to be within a certain distance of certain benches. The problem is: you have no idea ahead of time, what augmentations you are going to have, and which benches they will have to be near. The game does a...let's call it 'acceptable' job of telling you which benches the augs will impact, but there is just no way to build a workshop with all of these unknowns. I started making a spread sheet, but it is just an unmitigated PITA to try and get your head around all of it at the same time, to build out your space.

Equally bad- it can be a long damn time before you get any kind of basic furniture, so all of these augmentations just sit on the floor, in big pile, in the middle of the room. Furthermore, I still only have the most basic container and the item management in this game is just Out. Of. Control.

Finally, it is just unacceptable to have to wait 5+ mins IN THE MIDDLE OF THE NIGHT, for it to contact the server and prepare my realm, every. time. I. want. to. make. a. jump.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, I want to remind everyone that Nightingale is an EARLY ACCESS game. It is not finished and not 100% polished and the devs know it. Furthermore, it is an early access game that just dropped, not something like BG3 that was in early access for almost 3 years before its full release. And Valheim has been in "early access" for 4 years and counting. Nightingale is intentionally unfinished. It is quite playable but the devs know it's rough around the edges. This is not the full release of a AAA title from a huge studio. Please adjust your expectations accordingly. In exchange for supporting a game that is not quite fully baked, you get to participate and provide your feedback to the devs before the game is set in stone. See UI improvements that need to be made? Hop on the discord and chat about it! Found a glitch? Share while there's plenty of flexibility to fix it. If you're looking for a game that is 100% complete and perfectly polished (at least according to the devs. Plenty AAA games launch with huge bugs), Nightingale, and early access games on the whole, might not be for you. And that's ok! Just be realistic about your expectations. Nobody is tricking you about the state of this game. All that said, on to a review of the game itself!

I love the concept of Nightingale. The steampunk-meets-magic "gaslamp fantasy" setting is really cool and the graphics are stunning. As previously stated, it is an early access game that just dropped and is subsequently rough around the edges, but also full of promise! Unlike a lot of survival/crafting games, I'm hopeful that it will have a compelling narrative that comes together. I've already run into/heard from NPCs about/read about several prominent historical and fictional figures that are mainstays of the aesthetic. Victor Frankenstein, Nellie Bly, Charles Babbage, Alan Quatermain, and Ada Lovelace have all been referenced in the early part of the game that I've played and I expect to run into at least a couple of them. There are several interesting NPC factions that all have Opinions about each other and I'm excited to see how those dynamics unfold. On the faery side, there's talk of Oberon and courts, and Puck himself leads you through the tutorial and taunts you like the trickster he is from time to time.

Holy moly, there is So. Much. Crafting! Housing, crafting stations, guns, melee, magic, armor, clothes, modifications to all of the above. If you wanna get lost in resource gathering and building ever cooler stuff, this game will deliver.

I've played a touch of multiplayer and it was fun to run around together and goof around, protect each other while gathering resources, taking on enemies. It was neat! I'm excited about building a victorian sci-fi village with my buddies.

The realm cards are a fascinating mechanic! Use them and a realm portal to travel between difficulty levels and biomes. And there are locations in each realm where you can play minor realm cards to alter the behavior/mechanics of the realm you're in, and many of those alterations have drastic and stunning effects on the scenery. No realm is the same, because procedural generation, which makes the graphics even more impressive.

Ok, let's address the elephant in the room: Yes, the UI is clunky and frustrating at times, especially when it comes to inventory management and crafting. The building UI is actually pretty decent. And the two-step process of laying out your plan and then feeding resources into it seems counter-intuitive at first but starts making sense pretty quickly. But all of these minor annoyances are quite fixable, and not unmanageable for the time being. The character creation scheme is missing some features. There's bits of missing art here and there. It glitches from time to time. (though I've never had it crash on me. ) But nothing that makes the game unplayable.

All in all, Nightingale is a rad concept. It has good bones and is full of promise. If the devs follow through on polishing the obvious rough spots, it's gonna be a heck of a game. If you're looking for a fully complete, polished game from a big AAA studio, Nightingale is not for you. Yet. But if you want to help a diamond in the rough reach its full potential, plunk down your 30 bucks and become a realmwalker with us!\t\t\t\t\t
