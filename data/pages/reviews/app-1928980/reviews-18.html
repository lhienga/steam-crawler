\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is an early access product, no doubt.

For me, it was also an enchanting experience, wandering through shades of reality toward a center that holds. The way, the realms of the fae wilds progress and also can be changed by the player reminded me of walking through the shadows of Amber in Roger Zelazny's books.

My experience started with only minor if any preconceptions, as I had only seen a few trailers in the past years and a glimpse into the gameplay watching streams during the stress test. But I did not follow its development or learned about its premises and setting.

Starting my foray into the faewilds with just my underwear, as I had caroused the night before the calamity, I soon found myself gathering and crafting the necessities of life while establishing a little place to call home. If you choose to prep for the end-times ahead, you will start your adventure quite better geared.

Difficulty is entirely in your own hands, as you can at any given time tune the challenge of your environment to a level you are comfortable with.

The gathering systems are engaging, if occasionally a little janky (I am looking at you, tree of frame stutter). With lots of different stats on basic resources, the final product of a crafting process can vary greatly depending on the input. Depending on the difficulty you play at, you find yourself really looking into the depths of the crafting systems or you can pretty much ignore it on easier modes.

Your gear can be buffed through various means, fully under your control.
*) starting with the components you use in the process of creating it
*) followed by increases in selected stats through infusions
*) adding charms for additional mechanics (additional gathering, movement increases on certain conditions, short term damage buffs and more)
*) enchanting your weapons to add a wide range of active abilities (healing, damage, weak-point information, boss-tracking, ease of gathering and more)

All this allows you to tailor your gear to your preferred playstyle, or change your rhythm by using something completely different.

Using the mechanic of minor realm cards, you can influence the mechanics of your current realm in various degrees, enhancing movement, changing weather, food mechanics, gathering and crafting multipliers and other aspects of the game.

The combat system starts out with a heavy focus on melee and it takes a while until you get good options for ranged or magic combat. Combat feels a bit janky at times, but I have the impression this may be due to connectivity-issues/server response times.

Overall, you will need some patience, as various crafting options for weapons/items improvements/spells take a few hours of gameplay to become available.

Inventory management and item input for crafting is currently in need of improvement. The systems are perfectly workable, but are a few years behind what you would nowadays expect as quality of life. The team is aware of this and actively working on it as short term points on their roadmap.

You will experience a progressing story, which seems only a fraction of the final experience, but again, its early access and not a final product. In my opinion, the english voice acting of the prime narrator is quite good.

For me, the game was very engaging on my first playthrough of the currently available story on hard difficulty realms only and after that a short foray into the current endgame. I did not rush, used the available information in-game only, learned the various complex systems currently in place and finished after about 70 hours.

I was intrigued enough to start a second run on extreme difficulty only, just to see how the scaling works and if I could actually make it. I found it necessary to engage with a lot more systems the game currently provides to overcome that challenge, but I finished it after about 50 more hours.

Overall, I am looking forward to the future development and will revisit this game in a few months.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game, it has the aesthetic I would love to play with.

Issues I have with the game:
1) The grind: This game is a pretty horrible gear and consumable grind. You have massive amounts of ingredient qualities that don't stack, and recipe upgrades so you're always laddering your gear over and over again.
2) the sluggish difficulty ramp: I was running on hard and didn't feel any pressure until the 4th level of portal. The weird jump in difficulty when you got to the first robot boss was also bizarre. You go from no challenge and being able to use your combat skills to two shot deaths where you just have to circle pillars and no real readable complexity to the fight. I can't tell you how boring the robots are, they suck the combat sucks and they aren't interesting, but look forward to having them be your main bad guys for a while.
3) Feels procedural: Yes the worlds are procedural generated but there are good ways to break up monotony by having crafted experiences. Maybe this gets better later but I didn't see anything that felt like compelling gameplay.
4) locked off encounters: You run into a lot of locked off in your home world with no indicator on what will help you unlock them later. I just felt like the game was holding me back from all of the interesting content. Don't worry if you feel like you're in the same situation, that content isn't interesting either.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is open world crafting light-survival. The gaslamp fantasy style is wonderfully crafted and the game looks beautiful! There are some wonderful details in Nightingale; although, the game is very rough and doesn't do much to really stand out in the landscape of the survival genre. This is a game that I have been watching and anticipating for quite a while and am kinda sad to give a no recommendation on this one. This is a lot of rough edges; many aspects of the game don't seem completely thought out and come across as tacked on. There is a tediousness and arduousness to much of the game. You can get good Playtime Value out of this game, easily getting more than 1 hours per dollar spent on the game. I personally like Nightingale,; I like the gas lamp aesthetic and love how I can generate and shape realms with realm cards. Personally love the umbrella a lot, lol. But still cannot recommend even though I personally like Nightingale.

I have an A Day In The Life review of Nightingale on YouTube. I played for 26 hours for that review.
https://youtu.be/-Ayk7wlCQBM?si=3K-EWeUOwPUinv2H

Now...I couldn't give up on Nightingale. I created a new character and played for about another 27 hours on this character. I had the thought that maybe playing Nightingale for content might have caused me to inadvertently play in a way that decreased my over all enjoyment of Nightingale and that maybe if I played at my own pace and not for content then I would enjoy Nightingale as much as I anticipated.

My recommendation does not change.
With more time on another character with some advanced knowledge, I understand why some of the tedium and arduousness is present in crafting and inventory management with resources but it does not mitigate or lessen the tedium and arduousness that make those systems not enjoyable to begin with. There are things that I do like that result from some trial and error in crafting and resource management but again the payout is not holistically worth it, mainly allowing you to skip some of the gear score grind a bit.

Again I personally like, but do not recommend. I will probably still play some more Nightingale as well because I want to know what this Watch is in the achievements...but many of Nightingale's competitors are doing much more to stand out and be more mechanically unique than Nightingale. I will revisit this review with Nightingale's 1.0 launch, maybe before. This is one that needed more time in the oven before hitting the wild, even for early access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale, a game as of me writing this, has almost 14 hours of play time from myself. (8.6\\10)

To start let's talk about the only negative I really have. This game has a tendency to become confusing. After the tutorial your kinda pushed off on your own with a goal. The goals are labeled clearly on the right side of the HUD, but some of them require multipart steps to complete. I had found myself a few times creating or doing the wrong thing to achieve the goal. But that's truly the only bad part of this game, and with it being in early access and open beta, not much to complain about.

Now for the good, to start this game is what survival-crafting SHOULD BE! It blows games like Raft, The Forest and other open survivals I have played. The way you can control the environment, build and upgrade is amazing and shows clear thought. Secondly, the idea of this game is amazing, from the art style of the Fey to the sketches you get of "other worlds" is stunning. The idea of being lost in a 'madness' of fey realms and other dimensions was beautiful, and the ability to travel them felt unique and easy to achieve. (Don't worry it makes you work for it, but in a fun and earning way) The creature\\hostile mob creation reminds me of a Souls like style, you have to take a moment to think before you act in fast manners. Take your time exploring and seeing the world, this is not a blow through game. Lastly is the beauty of it, I don't run the most specked PC, and I can run this fine with stunning graphics. It does have that classic indi\\rust like character look but to me that's part of the joy of this game.

Nightingale is a 8.6\\10, It has made it's way into my heart and my personal top 10 list! I would highly recommend this game to others (and I have, and they have proceeded to love it). I have been following this game since its announcement on TikTok and other platforms!

If you have questions that other reviews don't answer leave them here, I'll answer them all. Hope to see you soon Realm Walker.\t\t\t\t\t
