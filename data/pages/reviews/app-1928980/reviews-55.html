\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisappointed and bewildered best describe my experience with Nightgale.

Launching in early access prematurely, I fear, may detrimentally impact its future. It kicks off with a lengthy, mandatory tutorial that's not only single-player but filled with verbose dialogue that often feels irrelevant. Post-tutorial, the game swiftly strips away your character's charming Victorian attire for basic rags.

Forced online play has drawn widespread critique, and though I initially overlooked this, it became a significant issue. Realm creation was marred by excessive loading times, eventually halting progress and forcing me to forfeit realms along with all my gathered resources, leading to lost progression.

After an exasperating 11+ hours, reflecting on recent titles like Palworld and Enshrouded, I can't help but feel this game suffers by comparison. Would my criticisms be as harsh without their context? Perhaps not, but as it stands, Nightgale was a frustrating journey from start to finish.

I had high hopes for this game and might revisit it after some time, hoping for improvements. As it stands, there's a significant journey ahead for it to meet expectations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are a lot of reviews and opinion pieces written by people that say this game is bad simply because it's more complex (or convoluted) than they felt like dealing with.

Not understanding a game, particularly one that's in EA, doesn't make it bad. This is literally the 'ironing out the kinks' phase of development. Is it grindy? A little, but less so than many other games I've played. Once you figure out how augmenting crafting stations works, all of the "crafting is too complex" tears go right out the window. Pressing 'C' brings up a window that tells you everything you know how to make and what crafting station + augment is required for it. There's lots of overlap. It's really not that hard. Combat is definitely a weak point, and my poor GTX3060TI struggles once there are more than 6 or so enemies coming at me, along with a forest, and skybox, and my 'helper' (okay they suck the most the hireling scripting is atrocious), and it's raining and you're next to a body of water with fish in it.... it's rough on the GPU.

One thing I DO hope they get to sooner rather than later is making sure all the items are properly named and tagged with whatever type of thing they are. ie Bound Bristle should be Plant Fibre (Bound Bristle) to keep it in line with everything else. I'm sure it's on their list. Just like items that don't yet have thumbnails and have a generic icon in crafting menus and your inventory.

It will get there. Give them time to work on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe TLDR: At it’s core it is pretty bad, save your hard-earned money. Maybe in 3-4 years it will improve but there will undoubtedly be better titles released between now and then.

I really wanted to like this game but sadly after 13 hours, along with several friends, I can’t bring myself to continue any further. With so many other good survival/building games out there it became increasingly challenging to slog through more of this title.

The good:
-Graphically, the biome is pretty nice. Foliage is bright and lighting is decent. Settings like the forest were nice to travel through.
-The ability to plan structures in their entirety and then add resources as you get them needs to be in more games.

The Bad:
-Quality of life is virtually non existent. From building to menus to interaction, it’s all tedious and slow.
-Loading screens can be painfully long.
-No offline mode so if you don’t have an internet connection or just a poor connection you’re out of luck.
-For a steampunk/Victorian setting there is virtually no steampunk/Victorian setting oddly enough. You actually ditch the Victorian look you start with almost immediately after beginning the title.
-The character creation is just creepy. From blank stares to weird poses, it feels pretty weird but could just be personal preference.
-NPC’s are stiff blocks of wood. Same clunky movements with the same blank stare. Your follower NPC will literally teleport just ahead of you as you move around. At one location I opened a door and my follower was already standing inside the room.
-The realmwalking mechanic of going from biome to biome is incredibly tedious. Basically, choose a realm as a home zone, then travel to others to gather a given resource and never return. The game even warns you that you cannot return.
-Animations are quite bad. Mined rocks just explode and disintegrate, chopped trees just spontaneously turn to logs, plants just disappear when harvested.
-AI is probably the worst I’ve seen in a long time. Every creature and ‘monster’ just runs in a straight line at you and leaps at the last minute allowing you to literally just slowly step six inches to the left or right and they fly on by. Additionally, you can physically attack a target 3-4 times before they even turn around and acknowledge the hits.
-Dialogue is just cringe. Only 1 NPC is voice acted (extremely slowly). From others I've played with you either like it or hate it.
-Quests are very bad. One quest is literally to raise your gear score, which leads to..
-Gear score in a builder/survivor game. The title uses a gear score system as gating to prevent you from entering certain areas or progressing the game further.
-In certain situations you will need multiples of each crafting station leading to crowding and more clunkiness.
-Recipes are purchased not earned or found. You can pretty much just gather a bunch of sticks, convert them to ‘essence’ at 1:1 and then buy every recipe instantly.
-Repetition, repetition, repetition. After about 2 hours you will have done everything that you will be doing going forward making the remainder of your time simply rinse and repeat.
-Virtually no reason to explore or move about the world to discover things.
-Almost no variety. I cant give an exact number but it felt as if there was only about half a dozen different creatures to fight.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn it's current state, I'd say wait and see. I feel like I just licked the batter of the chocolate chip cookies and got salmonella poisoning. It's not their fault, it's mine for not waiting for waiting for the cookies to bake in the oven first. But I'm on the toilet clearing out my colon with a stomachache. You can interpret that closing line however you want. Point is this game needs a lot of time in the oven.

There is a great foundation here for the game. The gunplay is definitely good. I can see the ex-Mass Effect dev veterans here at work. The melee needs a bit of work. It's floaty and the hits are iffy. Half the time I feel the hit isn't registering where I want it to. And when it does it is rarely on center of where the crosshairs are. They are trying for the Vermintide-style combat but it will definitely need to be a lot tighter to succeed. I'll be honest I cannot tell if it is on my end or a consequence of their always online therefore lag. Talking about the lag, I'm lucky enough to be on the same continent and I can feel the lag and connection issues. I definitely feel for those outside of North America.

Art direction is amazing. I am a sucker for steampunk aesthetics and I'll admit it. But you realise that with the random generation you still get a lot of reused assets and a handful of them at that. So the novelty wears off after you've seen the same things in world one be in world 10. Character design is questionable, the joke about fetal alcohol syndrome characters or opium addicts play very well into the Neo-Victorian era. Clothing designs are not as plentiful as I want.

Crafting is tedious but nuanced. I get what the devs are going for as there are many layers to creating gear but they are not well explained or proper contextual information provided. They would have Tier provided for most crafting materials but not for ore and food. Sometimes tier crafting mattered, some times it did not. It isn't explained clearly when it does, which stats do, etc etc. A lot of it is play and figure it out as you go, but it isn't done well and intuitively like let's say Terraria. Most of my play time was just figuring this stuff out making the game tedious to play. The nuanced part of crafting for example is crafting Desert/Forest (Desert fibre + forest thread) cloth will have different stats compared to a Forest/Desert (Forest fibre + desert thread) Cloth. So there's a lot of combinations to build your gear. It's fun but time consuming and kills you're inventory management as everything is it's own item.

And talking about time consuming there is a lot, a lot of things that just eat time for no reason, causing my internal entertainment gauge "fun" slowly ticking over to "chore". Crafting takes time, starting portal takes time, traversing is mostly by foot, cutting tree takes an oddly long time for it to fall, certain enemies require you to kill them for them to drop it's core (if it didn't fall through the floor 80% of the time) which you would need to break with your pickaxe to drop it's loot. Why can it not just drop it's loot. bound enemy spawns take time. Crafting recipes are spread out between between the 3 biomes across every major realm (read higher tier world). ie Shotgun in desert provisioner realm, pistol in forest provisioner and rifle is in swamp provisioner realm. You want all the firearms, go through all 3 realms. Every little thing is tedious and time gated making the game feel like a time sink. God forbid you are a parent with children and an hour to yourself everyday. This game is not for you.

Early access games so bugs galore and incompleteness (but it's EA you know what you're getting into). Nothing too game breaking but it does get annoying at times. Like the enemy or enemy loot falling through the floor mentioned before. Alt+tabbing and tabbing back in disables running until you open your menu and close it again. Lots of placeholder assets in the menu so at the end game I can't tell if my components are in my inventory or not at a glance. Some nonsensical things like Vegetable (Onion) and Onion (Onion) being separate items (they have different stats but why?). I need to scroll over all the individual items that don't have assets to figure out what it is.

End game sounded enticing, but it ended up being what you've experienced out in the game but on a slightly bigger scale with other players. It's the same little mini-missions/puzzles to unlock recipes outside of buying from npc vendors when you are in the realm and then you finish with a boss fight. The netcode needs work. Definitely experienced lag, rubber banding and disconnects when playing this mode. Voice chat is disabled currently but there is a greyed out scroll bar leading me to believe it's available later.

I want to reiterate, there is a good decent game under all of this. I don't think it's truly worth playing right now. Perhaps a few updates or maybe on release we'll have something much more polished.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I’ve been playing survival games for many moons. My first MMO was EverQuest which should approximate my age as a gamer. I remember the times of dialup modems and having to edit boot up files to make games run.

This game has captivated me, and has lured in all of the friends that I game with, in a way that hasn’t happened in literal years. Possibly a decade. It isn’t perfect but it is beautiful, well wrought, and remarkably stable for early access.

There are glitches issues and growing pains. Some inventory things still need icons. Sometimes a tree I chop disappear into the earth and results in a log eruption moments later. But the hitches are minimal, the story is compelling and it has so much opportunity to expand and continue to grow. Other fey courts. (Hello Winter. spring. Autumn. A court of blood and bone, for a dark fey Unseelie moment? So much opportunity to add biomes or other major cards and continue to iterate and make something entirely new.

I agree that I’d love voiceover for more characters. I’m sad to be in what is currently the endgame (though Puck set the groundwork that more is to come, yay). I've also experienced responsive and responsible dev activity working hard to make the game iteratively better with minor and major release pushes since the launch.

Nightingale has me on potential, theming, and beauty. Perfect? Not at all. But it’s great as it is and has so many paths to make it even better.\t\t\t\t\t
