\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 33 hours, I can say that this game is fun. Fun for me. I have enough experience in other survival craft games that I can manage to play Nightingale without too many issues. And thus begins my review.

Nightingale is not for inexperienced gamers or those who have little experience with survival craft. There are too many concepts and very little guidance in what you need to do next. This game has been frustrating, since plenty of research is also needed. Reading the in-game notes and online wiki help somewhat. A game shouldn't require so much research to understand how it works. Perhaps this is my situation, I cannot account for all the others who have played Nightingale.

The game's concept and visuals are phenomenal. No complaints. There are plenty of bugs at the time of writing this that need corrections. The enemies out in the world, particularly the animals, have the weirdest attack animations that fling themselves or myself far away. Hunting deer may as well be an adventure in itself. If you're not able to one shot kill "The Hunt", its going to run across the ENTIRE map. It can sense you a mile away. So it runs some more. It'll run for ages without respite.

Managing structure upgrades also takes finesse and talent. If you're OCD or an extremely well organized person, you'll enjoy the challenge. Otherwise, trying to make your work area look practical is a headache. Once I understand what structures should be paired up, and how they wont affect other structures, I'll start creating specific area for each set of structures. For now, I'm stuck putting everything near each other in an attempt to not miss out on bonuses.

I'll give the game a 5 out of 10, would play again. It's good, if you're willing to overlook certain elements and do plenty of research. I'll update my review later with more play time and game updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think there is a good game to be had, but not yet. Lots of potential for variety and longevity of gameplay. Right now though the realms are a bit samey, puzzles lack variety and realm uniqueness. I don’t mind grind but with it still being a bit buggy, hours of grind can be lost in an instant, soul destroying.

The Station/augmentation system is a nice idea but everything needs to be so close to everything else and even then the results of what you can build seem erratic. Even if you build them within a few virtual feet of each other the results on what you can build at that station varies with no obvious reason why. As a result I cannot build the aesthetically pleasing base I want.

Recruits disappearing and never returning, with all their gear did it for me. Not just once but several times.

I don’t regret buying and will definitely return when the whole thing is a bit more stable.
Early access I know, maybe released a little too early for a game like this. Keep working on it, I think you’ve got something worth pursuing. I’ll be back!\t\t\t\t\t
