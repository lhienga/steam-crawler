\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely in b love with this craft system. The table augments having different items that provide access to the same recipes (like the spit roast and tea kettle) is a nice touch, so you don't have to have duplicates in order to have it affect two tables that are not next to each other.

And the depth and customization in the craft system is fantastic. Want a new wood axe? Well you can just slap one together with whatever supplies you have on hand! Oh but you want a magic wood axe? Well pick and choose the types of ore, wood, and hides that you use to craft the components. Now you have the exact stat boosts you chose, be that damage, magic, stealth, defense, durability, whatever you selected in your material choices.

Oh and if you have a grindstone near your work bench when you assemble the axe, it will also get a permanent damage buff to the blade. And if you REALLY want to make the best axe you can, play a card in the transmuter (or build a workshop in a realm where one has been played) to improve crafted tools, and get even more permanent buffs when you assemble the axe. You could even take that to the absolute extreme and make the individual components in different realms with cards played for wood or metal working.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhilst have 60 hours plus in the game and I am enjoying it. I can't recommend it, at least in its current state.
The game suffers with major quality of life issues;
- There is no point to farming as you are always getting deminishing returns.

- There is no clear instruction when it comes to farming or way to remove seeds from planter other then destroying them. A lot of plants don't seem to want to grow in them and there is no info if I can only plant them in a certain Biomes or they just take longer.

- Its horrible trying to find plants required for cooking in the realms (I have been playing for 60 hours plus and only have found 1 wheat)

- Crafting times are horrendous

- A lot of crafting recipes are locked behind Augments which are decor items but you can only apply so many to a station and its hard to keep track of which is doing what. This leads to having to make multiple of the same crafting stations and with a structure limit that is not helpful.

- Realms are not very well explained

- Resetting a Realm does not delete the realm which can lead to losing important structures if you forgot to build an respite point and try to fast travel.

- You can only build Respite points (fast travel) in a certain realm, meaning when in the Biomes you HAVE to walk from point A to B and making setting up a base in Biomes a waste of resources.

- Crafting isn't very well explained and its a lot of trail and error trying to make gear with good states

- there is a structure limit and building limit, which with the building limit if you built anything in a lost realm you have wasted a building slot with no way of removing it unless you by dumb luck manage to back travel through various portals.

- there is not way to show the path between realms so you can get lost easily

-demolishing building or items is all manual, meaning it is very easy to accidentally destroy a wall when you are trying to remove a wall decoration

-climbing thing is horrible and since there is quite a bit of vertical in the game it just makes it even more laborious

Sadly if a lot of this is not address I can see the game dying pretty soon. I know its early access so there is hope for it to get better but I feel the game needed to definitely cook a little longer before their early access release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a stunning game. So much to look forward to as well. Giving me a bit of the ol' Skyrim feeling, but instead I'm dungeon delving and exploring the many different Faewilds like a dapper Georgian/Victorian Indiana Jones. I've seen a few bad reviews on YouTubes, but they seem to all be from the sort of people who didn't realise an axe does more damage than a knife, or that eating increases your stamina. It was painful to watch. Sure, the tutorial isn't fully sorted yet, but I'm pretty sure if you take the time to read the explanations, it's there. Remember to farm all the interim starting realms for resources, and you'll be fine. However, if you need the ultimate hand-holding and sign posted railroading to survive, this game might not be for you yet. (Just follow the main quest and it does work) I'm almost 60 hours in and I've been challenged at every level in a great scale of progression and have constantly seen new creatures and new environments. No server lag or crashes for me. Crafting can be frustrating and confusing, but that is likely to get sorted soon and it's rather satisfying when you realise you can infuse all kinds of different attributes into your gear, giving you a reason to actually hunt for a big variety of resources. I hate it when games just set you on a boring single track road of progression. The survival mechanics are meaningful, but not too punishing and the cards are there to tweak them to your hearts content. Tired of eating constantly while you slowly build at your base? There's a few cards for that. Wanna steam through one realm to farm resources? There's several cards for that. Need better rewards, resources and/or a challenge? You're covered and then some. The Character customisation is super detailed (with lineages!) and okay people don't like the presets... so what? Doesn't actually take that much time to alter nicely. Pretty decent for an early access game I think. Looking forward to it's development.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWild how I only managed to play an hour, but I'm going to give an honest review. Please keep in mind the play time I've put into it, and remember, my review may not fit your own tastes!

Let me start by congratulating Inflexion Games for their first official release. I've been a fan of the writers since I first picked up Dragon Age, and I'll continue to be a fan of their to-the-point story telling and world-building. Veterans like this in the gaming industry deserve more of a showcase in our community, and our praise should reflect how we treat them. From handling the Blight to handling Reapers, I've been a fan for a while, and will continue to look forward to their developments. Once again, congratulations.

Now, onto the game.

Nightingale, from the second you load in, is an immersive experience full of whimsy and wonder. The story-telling, as always, is top notch, and worth the time you'll be investing into it. Compared to other games of this similar genre, the story is easier to grasp, and much more a part of things than a side-objective. As someone who loves to be immersed in my games, this definitely adds points in Nightingale's favor.

The Good:

- So far, the Graphics, when rendered, are gorgeous.
- The Voice acting, as always, leads me into wanting to play more.
- The Story, as stated, is immersing, and wonderful for the people who like to lose themselves in a fantasy. Puck is an excellent leading man and has already stolen the show for me.
- Crafting System is Straight Forward.
- Materials and Resources are easy to discern from non-materials.
- The Creatures are a mix of Terrifying and Beautiful all at once, which really drives home that you're somewhere outside of the laws of reality.
- The Tutorial is Great at giving you an idea of what you're getting into without wasting too much time. (You'll probably spend about 30 - 45 minutes deciding if this is your style.)

And finally, onto my criticisms. I won't call these by any means 'bad' features, but rather see things that need polishing and offer a bit of advise.

- Combat is strange, and in my opinion, kind of boring. To rely on stamina for a basic attacking feature is certainly a choice, and helps you plan your actions a bit better, but in a game where 3+ mobs may be attacking you, I don't see why a shiv eats up as much stamina as it does. With as much time as I spend running away to replenish stamina, I would hope that combat was just a bit more engaging.
- Rain / Weather effects attribute to damage, another thing I don't understand. Intentional or not, I've never personally taken damage from heavy rain, but I've never been pelted. If that's what the team is going for, maybe stages of rain may help drive this home?
- The HUD for crafting is a little cluttered. Moving to a windows-esque folder HUD over a scrolling HUD might make things easier in my opinion, but, this criticism isn't the weightiest one here, so take it with a grain of salt.
- Server-Side Saves. Perhaps this is a beta feature, or maybe a prevention from people accessing too much / editing saves, but I don't understand it. I don't think I've played a game that hosted my data 'online' but, I digress, this is a minor complaint that will hopefully see patch.
- World Settings should be a thing for the more RPG-seeking fans. Realistically, I believe every game under the survival-craft genre should allow players to customize their experience to their own likings, but again, this will likely be addressed post-beta.
- The Swamp doesn't really feel 'swamp-y'. Maybe it's the swamps I've been in (Florida), but I think the area would benefit from more foliage such as mangroves or even willows.

With that, I'd like the readers of this review to keep in mind some core tenants before making a decision SOLELY on what I say.
1.) This is a Beta, of course there will be hiccups. At full release, I may have different opinions, and you might too!
2.) Take it with a grain of salt, but even with my criticism of online saves, this game still plays more sanely than that OTHER leading survival-craft. (You know the one I'm talking about.)
3.) There is so much potential here. I advise you to look over the videos presented along with potentially finding a streamer you can handle and seeing if the game is worth it to you.

All in all, I would rate Nightingale, in its current beta release, a solid 7.8/10. With a lot of room to grow, I know Inflexion won't let me down, and I know the feedback of the community will only help the game grow to become the survival-craft game I wanted out of the aforementioned 'other' one.

I'm looking forward to growing alongside the community, and sincerely hope some of the bugs / features are fixed for the games longevity. I've waited 3 years for the games release, and I'd wait another 3 just to see what Flynn and his team get up to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has potential, it is still early acces and very ragged round the edges but the potential is there.
I am playing as a single player and on a Steam Deck so experience may vary for others.

It is not a polished fully formed game, it's equally frustrating and fascinating.
The controls are a bit clunky changing items on mainhand or offhand using a controller is slow and when your weapon doesn't work on the creature attacking you and its necessary to change the attacks don't pause, death is a common event.
There is not a lot of hand holding in this game you finish the tutorial and its like of now go do stuff you have a vague direction you need to go but how you get there and how long it takes is up to you.
I like this personally I have limited time I can play and I like to explore the nooks and crannies and finding something new is rewarding.

OK the GOOD
The Realm cards a great original idea and with three options to create a realm the possibilities to explore are huge.
The Umbrella I love the concept its so silly and fun in equal measure (a bit like the leap of faith in Assassins Creed)
The later outfits, I've not got that far yet.
The finding of ruined buildings with occasional treasure, the buried statues, the roaming animals just doing what they do, the ghost Elk ( I think its an Elk) is just gorgeous
Puck, voice acting very good.
Building is simple imo though I'm still at the basic stone and wood buildings.
The initial tutorial is a really good introduction to the game and gives the player a taste of the three available biomes at the start of the game. You have to choose one as your home base.
Combat so far I find ok though a little disorganised, though that may be just me...
You come across these little stories of others that went before you filling in details of other people's lives.
There is no conventional levelling up as in you get points to allocate to a skill tree its more the level of your gear aloows you access to new locations and some places are not accessible until your gear level is sufficient.
You can recruit a NPC companion to help carry, harvest and fight with you, good for solo players

The BAD
The you can recruit cannot be ordered to perform a task they just stand round or if you give them an axe chop trees often on you or your house.....
The map you cannot put down a marker on the map easily to track to someplace as it works in say Enshrouded which I find frustrating. There are beacons I have used so again a partial work round
You can delete your companion if you click to put down a marker on the map on top of their marker and this deletes them completely.
The Steam Deck controller (I believe other controllers are the same) has problems on some of the menus I had to connect a mouse for the Essence Trader however its a useful work around.

The UGLY
The character creation I struggle to create someone who looks even remotely attractive there are plenty of options for eyes, head, lips etc and they are all yuck. I am not looking to create a gorgeous person but someone who doesn't look like they've been hit by the back of a bus would be nice.

Overal lots of potential and I am enjoying myself immensely, as fixes appear I expect this game to get better and better.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful and strange, you have to jump through the usual early-stage survival-game hoops, but as you get used to the systems, the game starts to open out.

It's still in Early Access, so there are some things that can/should change: The number of crafting benches need to be cut down. Also, despite the elegant clothing in the trailers, I still look like a caveman after 15 hours. After 20 hours, I started to look a bit smarter.

What the game does well, it does fantastically. The game looks absolutely gorgeous. The sound design is also wonderfully atmospheric. This is the kind of game you can lose dozens (hundreds?) of hours in, once you get past the initial hump.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow this is a game you can sink your teeth into!

I understand peoples concerns about the "online only" part of the game, but there is a reason for that if you simply persevere. A lot of this game is hidden, which matches the entire premise of the world; exploration and discovery. The game starts off feeling simple but gradually increases in complexity and difficulty. It is, however, a LONG game. Do not expect to see everything in the first couple hours, which I believe has contributed to the game's almost immediate poor reception.

The world(s) is(are) beautiful, the lore is actually very interesting, combat is meaty, exploration is endless. Crafting is a little on the complex side, but once you understand it, it's very in-depth and gives you a lot of opportunity to tinker. Base building also has a plethora of options available to you, AS. YOU. PROGRESS. You can build some beautiful houses once you have unlocked all of the recipes.

Do I recommend this game? Yes, wholeheartedly, with the exception that you need to spend TIME with this game to enjoy it. I'm not saying it's one of those "oh it gets good after 10 hours" - its simply a game that takes its time. I enjoyed the game from the moment I started, but there is a ton to do, so you will need a fair bit of time to dedicate to playing to get the most out of it. Almost like an MMO.\t\t\t\t\t
