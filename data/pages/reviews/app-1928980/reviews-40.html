\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this one. This has to be one of my very few negative reviews. I tried all the way through the initial grind, built a base, made it all the way past hunt realms and into the next phase of the game (avoiding spoilers as much as I can).

It's early access, but the basic game loop is fairly dull. You figure out quickly that random generated variety of realms ends up being a cheap gimmick that reskins the same content repeatedly. This would be fine except the content is fairly uninspired. Combat is clunky and doesn't feel great. The AI is simple. The character builds are basic. The large variety of augmentations for your crafting tables winds up being more of a cluttered mess and inconvenience than a fun mechanic.

Again, I really did try to like this one. I can't recommend it. I'm really hoping I come back and change this review in another year. Buyer beware! Watch some honest gameplay footage before you support this one in its current state. It is altogether an interesting distraction, but not very fun and the story/content fall quite flat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played to a high or end-level and completely solo at that, as I tend to do. Within that context, my opinions are, as follows:
The game is an excellent framework for play, like a SteamPunk Starfield: A set of semi-randomized sandboxes littered with mob groups, occasional quests and POIs, rendered beautifully with spectacular lighting and smooth animations.
Progression seems to be oriented towards increasing numbers (item level, etc.), allowing you to handle higher context, get better crafting materials, and building up your home and equipment to a higher level.
I've experienced a little of the story, met a few nice NPCs, and discovered some lore that held my interest. The hireling system reminds me of Dragon's Dogma 1's. The NPC follows me around, likes to chop trees and attacks things that attack you. Odd that my most recent anti-tree, anti-wolf psychopath NPC was labeled as a "Druid". I've been sadly misinformed about them.
There are rough edges, sure, UI problems and performance issues, but those are all more than expected for an Early Access Title. Nothing to complain of there.
The framework also allows the ability to introduce new stories and quests with special cards that can be used to unlock handcrafted content. That would be nice.

IDEA: They could even have something nice like a monthly randomization of pathways to Nightingale that must be followed to make it there.

IDEA: From Nightingale, you could theoretically have further branching out once you reach there and it could become a new quest hub... all in all the base is a fine stage to build your own story.

It just needs more compelling content, but I'm certain that's coming. As to the extent they will add live content remains to be seen.

Definitely worth a pickup at this stage. The team deserves your support.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tnightingale is very cool and I'll definitely give it another chance later in its development. I'm sure that the issues I have with this game are due to it being early access and they can definitely be resolved by the full release. The core of the gameplay loop lies in exploring new realms, gathering resources exclusive to those realms, and bringing them back home realm where all of your crafting and building is happening. My biggest issues are with just how long it takes these realms to open, even if they are realms that you have already generated and visited previously. My other issue with the game so far lies in the gear progression. It feels like upgrading to new gear just takes a bit too long. I really like the idea of finding different rare materials to craft your gear with different stats that the game has implemented, and experimenting with different material combinations is enjoyable; but at the same time it can also cause your inventory to become clogged very quickly, causing you to have to go through the long realm loading times again to drop everything back off at home.

I'll definitely check the game out again in a couple months to see how its changed. I do really love the things the devs are going for, its just not in a state I'm currently enjoying very much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's still "Early access" at the time of my writing this, though, I would say it would be more accurate to call that "Everyone can buy and play it, but we need you guys to help us work out all the bugs."

I have mixed feelings about that. I'm not sure how seriously they are taking player feedback at this point. I've only submitted a couple of bug reports myself, but in the recent large patch, the horrible issue with crashing seems to have gotten significantly better. Was it a major problem? Yes. Did they fix it? It appears they did.

I'm running in 4k, with a 4090, and an OLED LG C2. This game can be insanely gorgeous. Very good.

I play regularly with a friend. We've hit out build limit in my Respite realm. Not good. Of course, there's plenty of others we can go to and he certainly has his own respite realm, but that is beside the point.

The combat handling could be better and early ranged weapons are especially bad, but it isn't totally abominable and that isn't 100% the point of the game.

I've already mentioned how gorgeous it is, so add on top of it a pretty nice story and setting. The gathering and crafting is...... actually a surprisingly easy way to waste a lot of time. I do wish they'd improve upon the fishing, though. It could end up being its own mini game if they did.

It has a lot of promise and they ARE still working on it. If you're into the genre I don't think you'll regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
