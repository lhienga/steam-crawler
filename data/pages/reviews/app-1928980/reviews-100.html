\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk where to start..

Pros:

- I like the theory of the game, jumping from realm to realm to explore.
- The base building system is better then most games. I like the fact you can build the whole base then get items to after create the game.
- I appreciate the puzzle, that encounter ideas... but its has its draw backs...

now to the cons... Ooo boy..

-Keybinding system is completely broken, you either have to play the game on the default settings or your keybindings will break constantly. for example I like swapping strafe keys (a and d) for my q and e buttons. Well everytime I die my e button goes back to interact and I can no longer stafe to the right. I either have to completely log out of the game or die again to reset it. I have sent in a ticket as a major bug, cause honestly its game breaking.

- The crafting system is waaaaay too overcomplicated with way to many materials.. this is made 100x worse cause like 1/2 the materials in the game have literally the same icon or a place holder icon, so it takes forever for you to find what you are looking for in chests.. then you literally in the mid came have to Craft something > to craft something > to craft something > to craft something... to make the item you are trying to get. T2 resources are hard to find consistently and you essentially have to wander around realms until you find what you need and even with realm boost cards up get a fraction of what you need.

- UI - its clunky to use and it feels 1000 times worse when you cannot change key binds cause the binding either doesn't work or will stop working on you. Not having a way to scroll threw your off hand items instead of manually clicking them just seems like a really terrible decision and make me feel like the devs have never play any game at all that has a 1-8 hot bar..

-combat.... I haven't made it to late game, but the early game gun play is terrible... i don't know what would possess the devs to make everything literally hip fire, but it feels terrible. The melee weapons feel alright, but the the combat is pretty bland and repetitive. Creature attacks are very choreographed and easy to dodge once you get their pattern down.

- Progression - The game does a pretty bad job at giving you guidance on how to do things in the game. it refers you to the guide book, but its vague and you have to do a ton of searching/reading yourself to remotely find what you are looking for. some pretty critical things that should be blatantly obvious are not

Thats my intial feedback. I am trying to press through to see if it gets better, but at the moment I struggle logging on everytime my brother plays.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI understand it is early access, and I don't have 200 hours in the game, but it's not fun, so far. Here are my gripes:

(20 Hour Update in comments.
tldr; synopsis: Hard Pass.
This game hates you and wants to waste as much of your time as possible for some reason.)

-When any weather event occurs, you lag. And by lag, I mean it stutters like crazy, and actually doing anything becomes problematic. Feels bad man.

-Encumbrance is a mystery. Getting the backpack doubled my weight limit, but it seems that all the crap I have to carry around to function weighs 40 pounds anyway... so that sucks. The fistfuls of berries and meat you shove in your face all weigh about a pound a piece and if you don't have several of each, you have no health or stamina. See below for more info on Food Buffs.

-The Survival mechanics are the same essential "Sleep and Eat or Suck" nonsense. Apparently you can die from not sleeping in this, but you'll die from nine other things first, when low energy fatigue sets your stamina at 4 points, which is barely enough to swing a weapon once before a long recharge. So any enemy will wreck you in a matter of mere hours while you try kill it and it ineffectively mauls you over and over. OR you may just get caught in a hailstorm, and die from rocks falling on your stupid, tired face.

-The progression system is built around a Destiny-esque Gear-Score system. All of your progress in this game is tied to gear. You do not gain levels, you don't get abilities, and there is no "experience".

-The primary currency in the game is "Essence". This is gained from breaking down objects of various tiers into the constituent "being-ness". It also occasionally drops when you gather any kind of resource in a world of that tier.
Once you start needing higher tier essences, the game grinds to a halt, and it becomes a repetitive and unrewarding process of trying to find that ever more valuable "stuffiness" of the stuff you are smashing.

-Progress is also tied to crafting "Realm Cards" that let you do one of two things: Select the type and level of the world you are trying to go to... or Alter the world you are already in, often with dramatic downsides. For instance, one such Card I got thrust the realm into permanent darkness, and perpetually hurt me, all for a slight increase in loot from the "Bound", the zombie like denizens of the realms you traverse. It sucked and I immediately had to craft a "Cleanse" card to undo my horrific mistake. In the dark. While I got pinged by the existential nothingness of the realm I had wrought... or some .

-There does not seem to be any real use for the metal ore I gathered and refined. I have progressed up to the next tier, and still am locked out of crafting anything more interesting than a stone axe or a leather drape for my butt. 8 hours in, and I'm still stuck in noobie rags that I have to spend resources upgrading. Usually you at least have a vision of when you can get alloyed armor or tools, but I see no such indication that I will be unlocking that any time soon. Perhaps the very next thing I do will unlock it, but I don't really care anymore.

-You cannot move crafting stations, containers, or anything once it is built, and the only way to remove it, is to smash it with your stone -weapons. You lose resources in this process. And sometimes hit the floor or wall behind the thing you are trying to smash.

-8 hours in and the only ranged weapon available to me is a "Sling Bow" that shoots marbles. It sucks, and cannot take down a deer without four or five very precise, and lucky crit-hits.
This means your only solution to getting their skin and meat is to chase them.

-Running, fighting, or chasing sucks. Everything costs stamina, and you only get a little bit without using expensive, time-consuming, and heavy food buffs. I found that you can stack these pretty deep, since it tracks every food buff based on the item it grants, and if the items don't stack, the buffs do, so you can do some neat things, but the buffs only last about 12 minutes, (Without the corresponding realm card that increases buff length, "Feast"). So actually fighting becomes a chore of juggling buffs and food items in your inventory.

If you have enough of the food buffs, thought, combat becomes trivial, and the only real problem is how much hammer the enemies' faces can actually sponge up before getting crushed. Holy crap... I just smashed an overgrown Fae-Goblin in the face with a stone maul, and he took 26 damage. Cool. Lets do it five more times.

-Building is okay, but as stated before, there are no happy accidents in Nightingale. You will be smashing walls to expand or fix errors. On top of that, everything is too expensive, and materials are heavy and sparse. It takes far too long to gather wood and stone, and you will be sick of it in a matter of minutes.

Overall, its not great. It is a generic Survival-Craft game with the same uninspired "Eat and sleep or suck" mechanics, with a slightly worse progression system and a sortof "Steam-punk/Victorian Cave-man feel" to it.
Also, the goat man's magic words do not make me feel good. He sounds like a more condescending mixture of V for vendetta and Edgar Allen Poe. Ick.
The trailer had guns in it, but you will smashing rocks with a stone pick for nineteen (edit: No... more like forty) hours before you see your first musket.\t\t\t\t\t

Another drawback is the seevvvveraallll types of crafting materials - which in itself isn't bad, but the bad part is that it produces SEVERAL different types of products. Also, there are many missing icons that are replaced with the generic "N wax seal stamp" icon, making it awful to navigate through your bags and chests looking for that one piece that you "just know you crafted" at one point. Overall it's a pretty good game and a good start at something that could be worth while in the future. 6.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t
