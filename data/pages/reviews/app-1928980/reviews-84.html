\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's still very much an early access title, with some aspects being incredibly polished and a few others feeling like they got prototyped yesterday.

Environments are incredibly beautiful. You can spot landmarks in the distance, enticing you to explore them and get lost in a dozen smaller encounters along the way. While the stamina system get's a little tedious at times, combat is pretty satisfying (Seriously, try giving someone a pickaxe to the face)

Creature designs are incredibly inspired. It's not always clear wether something is hostile or not, but i think that's just great. It leaves you open to explore how the wildlife reacts to your presence and encourages you to remain cautious whenever you encounter a new beast.

The crafting system has some great ideas and some not-so-great ones. I love that for each recipe you can provide different versions of the same material, which then provide different effects. For example, when crafting a new set of clothing, the recipe merely calls for the hide of an animal. Which type and quality of hide is for you to decide. I really enjoy in-depth crafting systems like this, and leaves a lot of room for experimentation.

Unfortunately, the game also bogs you down with a dozen of different crafting stations. While this is common in moden survival games, i feel like Nightingale introduces too many at once. a mere 2 hours into the game and i already had my little blockhut filled with 8 different crafting stations. I don't mind having a lot of stations as long as they serve as progression milestones and i actually have to work towards them; Now they just making crafting a little tedious.

Managing your storage deserves a lot of extra love and care. I am sorely missing the option to merge existing stacks of a chest and my inventory, as well as being able to pull from nearby inventories when crafting. I would say this is something that requires the most attention right now, as we all know how much time we spend managing inventories in these games.

The UI, while gorgeous, is very unpleasant to navigate. You cant easily navigate them through keys; having to click on everything gets tedious when all the buttons are quite far apart.

Loading times are another thing that are breaking my immersion a bit. I downloaded the game onto my SSD and i'm still looking at 30+ second loading screens.

Character creation is an absolute horror-show. I had a LOT of difficulty getting my character to even resemble a human. All baseline presets look very similar with little variation, and worst of all, they resemble half-melted wax sculptures who stuck their heads in a hive of angry bees. The lineage system is an interesting concept but so underdeveloped i feel like it shouldn't have been included in the playable build yet.
Since character creation is the first thing you encounter as a player, I hope that it gets some major improvements very soon.

Overall I am enjoying the game a lot, but if you're expecting a fleshed out experience i would wait a year or so and come back to it. This game has extraordinary potential but it needs some time to get there.

Dearest devs, if you would like an extensive bulleted list of painpoints and general feedback, feel free to contact me. I really look forward to future updates!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an early access game that you should avoid for the next six months. There are far too few variants in the basic gameplay loop and far, far too many fiddly bits in the crafting and inventory management.

There are six primary materials (stone, ore, wood, fiber, hide, meat), three variants of each material per tier--discounting legendary variants--and processed versions of all those materials. There are five tiers. This results in hundreds of different primary components to juggle. Each takes up an extremely limited inventory slot, crafting requires that you have them in your inventory, and crafting itself is a player stall with completely unnecessary wait time for each step of the assembly process. If you have an ideal workstation and all the necessary materials, it will take several minutes to create a new gun, as you create ingots to make a shaft to make a barrel, lumber to make a stock, assemble that with all the other secondary components, then add infusions, add charms, and create ammo for it. Doing it right takes careful attention to material bonuses, on top of far too much patience. The game designer's love for unnecessary hurdles and details is psychopathic if not actively misanthropic in its attempts to waste the time of players.

In comparison, there are only six things to do on a map (help build, help defend, agility shrines, insight shrines, intelligence shrines, beat up monsters), with an optional tower and an optional dungeon that strings three of those things together. Aside from an extremely sparse number of quests, that's the grind. You repeat those six activities indefinitely for the resources necessary to spend hours managing inventory and making equipment back at your base.

The combat is simplistic and unrewarding. The game has a single martial melee weapon--the maul--and that's honestly already one too many, as you have limited space on your one and only hotbar, with far too many other tools to make room for a dedicated weapon. Seriously, they make you open inventory and juggle items in and out of your backpack just to harvest resources and navigate the world (Five slots for: Pick, Axe, Knife, Scythe, Gun, Maul, and the hated Climbing Picks, the worst navigation aid in any game I've ever played. Those not equipped in your five hotbar slots will be dropped upon death, so here's hoping you don't die on top of a mountain with your Climbing Picks in your pack.)

Building in other games, such as Valheim and Enshrouded, is a genuine delight that justifies the grind. The thought of putting together a base in Early Access Nightingale fills me with dread. I already have enough problems managing seven trunks worth of trash, I don't need to go harvest a ton of stone and lumber to make something nice on top of all of that.

I've played video games for thirty years, and never before has a game demanded that I learn and juggle so many fiddly bits for the sake of mastering such sparse and uninspired gameplay. There's some gorgeous artwork and craftsmanship here, but there isn't a game worth playing. Maybe there will be later.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo to start I play on Extreme difficulty and started out with an Extreme character the first beefs up the enemies on your starting realm, the second means you start out with no clothes other then your knickers. I've looked at several negative reviews and will also be commenting on them.

Combat: It's to whatever simple, it's not difficult enough. Playing on the hardest difficulty I find it to be at first difficult the first quest area especially took me a bit, as did the boss at the end. I had to plan ahead, and actually think about what I was doing and how to approach enemies as indeed the combat system at the moment is simple. You have swing, block and or dodge depending on the weapon/tool your using. There are also minor realm cards you can play that buff, and debuff you at the same time. Get more melee damage something else is impacted negatively. So makes you think what you want or need to do, and effects how you should prepare. At lower difficulties I don't find it's needed as much, but on Extreme you do need some strategy, and planning.

That said you'll find yourself on Extreme mode dying easily then not dying easily as you enter new Realms, and gather together essences needed to upgrade your equipment. The combat can be tedious, and boring at lower difficulties not fully tried it out on Hard the next one down from Extreme, but on lower difficulties it was rather boring.

Crafting: Seen alot of people complaining about can't do more then one type of whatever, there are to many benches, etc. Heres my question, if you have superior leather (as you can get lets say tier 1 hide to turn into tier 1 leather, or you can get Fabled tier 1 hide with better stats, why are you not using the Fabled with better stats? Then of course you have tier 2 etc with Fabled. Again why are you using inferior hide if you have superior hide? If you don't have superior hide then whats the problem. Simply create the leather you can, do other things while your waiting as you can make food, do the quests, explore etc while your waiting. Also you can pull out finished products as it's finished. As you don't need everything all at once the limit on stacking isn't really that impactful to the overall game. At first it might seem so, but honestly it's rather easy to make what you need right then, then start stockpiling what you might need in the future.

Material gathering mostly it's simple and easy after 5-6 hours of gameplay (including tut area, and exploration) I easily had enough to get myself geared up, had resources left over to upgrade, or make new gear (for my companion) a base, and most things I need without needing to continue resource gathering, or to stockpile to heavily. After the initial 5-6 hours I can easily gather what I need if anything in a few moments. Base is a 2 story 8 by 4 roughly thats rather empty even with all the benches, and upgrades I have available. There are of course higher tier hides and stuff i could get, and am working to get as i progress, but the have to, must get these isn't really needed. As I can easily aquire them while exploring and doing quests.

Survival: At this point doesn't seem excessively difficult to survive in this game, in a desert where I started an umbrella can easily stave off heat, same for wet when it rains. Food is plentiful though at this point not very varied, there I know are other things such as falling, breaking an arm, leg, spraining an ankle (far more possible if you play certain cards and get debuffs that make the climbing and even minor falls into things of nightmares.)

Story: Some seem to dislike Puck, and it's cool, as he's most assuredly rather shady, after all he is a Fae creature that isn't spose to be helping you but is for whatever reason. So trying to treat him like a human is simply absurd and they do a good job of showing he's not in simple ways such as appearance, voice (love the voice really rough and gritty adds to the ambience).

Overall: Game is on it's way to satisfy the survival crafting itch I have, though imo the cards debuff at times are far to much. Going from a fall not doing anything to it turning into death is a bit much. The visuals are great yet the world feels empty at times, and at other times like there is to much going on. Going from one nest of enemies to another, and getting attacked by aggessive prey species does get old, then other places theres nothing. Combat between the difficulties is very steep, but doesn't feel like the enemies are sponges as they are in many other games. On extreme you do less damage overall, and the enemies are higher level depending on where you go. I started out with a low gear score, and by end of tut was at 20 on gear score, with enemies around 40 or 50. Which this has stayed about the difference in our power as I go to new areas, and work on unlocking more.

The music to me isn't very impactful and I barely notice it most times, the companion or recruit you get can come in very handy for solo players and even at this early stage you can notice a difference in combat, and gathering with and without a companion.

Loading screens are meh, I know why they are their but imo it just slows things down as the gates take time to open, then you have a loading screen. Unless your set on exploring the realm your going to this can quickly become tiresome.

Overall I'd wait a little while longer, let it bake a little more, as some bugs, glitches, etc can make it difficult to play for some. For example my weapons are breaking rapidly which is cause for concern, as a single hit I can go from freshly repaired to broken weapon which can leave me relying even more on my companion for damage and running from a fight to repair my tools. Though usually the number of enemies are small enough that I can switch to a different tool.

Magic: Not seen much in the way of magic but a healing spell, which uses stamina as well and is tied to a single weapon. With above bug makes it difficult to use magic, and even without bug forces you to change weapons for casting magic which is odd to me, though much like in other games switching to different spells it's still an odd choice to make imo.
