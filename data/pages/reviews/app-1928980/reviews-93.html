\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am no stranger to survival/crafting games. If you are also no stranger to the genre, you can skip this title and continue playing your favorite one. I'm no pro at Nightingale as I've only played for a little over 10 hours, but that said, I felt a review for anyone that is like me and is someone who knows enough about a game after 10 hours to decide whether or not a game deserves any more of his precious time.
Here's my advice; give players a small taste of what you advertised in the promo videos (Guns, Armors, Enchanted Items and Action) right at the start of the game and then TAKE IT ALL AWAY. It will allow the player to "feel" what the Pale has stolen from them and give them hope that with some hard work and time, they can do more than just survive, they can get back to that level or even stronger and maybe even stand a chance at stopping the pale's advance. Have that be the start and then drop them into your post-apocalyptic Fae'ntasy world tutorial sequence. Or not [Shrug].

PROS
- The price @ $30 is a steal
- The world biomes are very different from one another and all are absolutely BEAUTIFUL
- Professional voice acting (Hope more will be added)
- Dialog script and overall writing (essence stories)

CONS
- The movement mechanics (First person and Third Person)
- The combat mechanics (Clunky and AI is frustrating)
- Another for movement mechanics (Navigating rocky terrain and Climbing stamina drain)
- Crafting system is overly complicated
- There are too many items that are the same but have infinite different qualities (Not one single item looted ever made me think I had found something valuable or special)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t33 hours in, I'm in love with this game.

I love the atmosphere and the idea of controlling what kind of world I can visit. It plays like an open-world survival crafter, but it 'feels' more like the MYST games.

I've played a lot of open world survival crafting games, I've sunk hundreds of hours into Ark, Conan, Empyrion, et al, but Nightingale has been the best of these so far and has the most potential.

What makes it so good is the level of creativity and sheer effort from the devs.

The visuals are stunning and the excellent sound design and music create a lonely, distant, otherworldly vibe that other games haven't achieved.

I chosen to play at a relaxed pace, rather than smashing through objectives. I'm taking my time to explore. The sounds and sights combined with the pace I've chosen to play has me very relaxed after a stressful day, my mental health has improved while playing this game.

There are a strangely high number of negative reviews for this game ( I really can't fathom why ) I've encountered only a few bugs - no crashes or game-breakers. In my opinion it's quite polished for am Early Access game. Most complaints just seem to be nit-picking or an issue where it's just "not their kind of game."

I encourage you to check out some gameplay streams/videos and make up your own mind on wether or not to purchase, but from my point of view, I'm happy to give it a SOLID "highly recommended."

The flaws:

1) The requirement to be online - the developers have said they will add an offline mode - but for now it is only a promise.
2) Possibly a consequence of point 1, but I didn't love the graphics, in fact I felt the texture quality was lacklustre for a 2024 game. I would like to see a more HD texture pack released, especially for the landscapes.
3) The early game didn't quite hook me, not like Ark, Sons of the Forest or Grounded - they all presented a mystery that I wanted to uncover. I haven't quite felt that with this.

Points 2 and 3 are clearly subjective, but I doubt I am alone in my opinions.

I have my fingers crossed that with some TLC I can change this to a 'buy recommendation because I want to like this game.\t\t\t\t\t
