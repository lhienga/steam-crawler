\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is difficult to word.
The game is a lot of fun, its well made, so why the negative review?
Its simple really, this game has 3 limits when it comes to building, and building is one of the main draw to survival games like this.
1) 100 buildings per realm (a limit that seems ok)
2) 300 structual pieces per building (annoying but combined with the 100 buildings you can work around that)
3) 350 items per realm (thats: crafting stations, doors, lights, augments, growing plots, strage crates, etc)

You can make a functional base with that limit... but thats what it will be... functional.

Keep hoping they will up the limit, its the unreal engine and the limit is insane for that engine (even more so given how small the maps are) Then add in that 6 players can share a realm and you can see the issue.

Was told "were looking into it" but then patch notes come out...sorting inventory is coming, offline is coming, craft from storage is coming, nice things to have sure...but what about the limit?

Latest devblog mentions structual limit being looked at...thats not the issue its the item limit that is. I do not understand it.
Ark has 60 players and huge maps, there is a limit but youll never reach it.
Connan, 30 player can be more, same as above.
Satisfactory, 4 players, massive maps and their would be riots if they had 350 item limit.
All the same engine (in the case of ark and satisfactory the same version of the engine)

Yet I have had people in Nightingale discord "limits fine for me" or "its not the engine thats the issue its to protect the servers"... The former, well good for them, the latter is insanity to anyone who has developed with the unreal engine.

In short, if you like to make huge bases with your mates that have content in them, decorated, built to produce items at good speeds etc.... then know that the trailers for this mislead you. DONT GET IT.

If you dont care about building, if you want great lore, good story line and a fun exploring, ignore my review and read other ones.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is definitely not a game that can be understood or progressed in 10-15 hours. I loved the game's crafting and building system. For example, to produce a rifle, you need to produce materials in three or four different stations. Of course, in order to produce these materials, you need to collect different resources beforehand. And with these materials you can produce the rifle. Honestly, I really liked this detailed production phase. For Building, you need to learn recipes and you find or buy these recipes in different realms. The building models are very detailed and nice. I hope the crafting and building system will not be changed.

Let's talk about the realm system... You travel to different realms with the combination of biome and major cards and you do this through portals. With minor cards, you can change the properties of the realm you are in (such as production advantages, strength bonus, food consumption) and when you do this, the environmental conditions (such as colors, weather conditions) also change. In short, you create alternate realms in the game through portals.

All this process of traveling, crafting and building between realms serves a purpose. You are trying to find Nightingale City, which is very important for humanity. You try to do this by constantly following quests, rumors and glimmers of hope.

In short, I loved the game. This is the best survival, building, crafting game I have ever played. Even though it's still in early access, I think it's doing a great job.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are a number of good and bad things in this game, but overall, I really enjoyed playing it through. It took me about 280 hours to complete everything defeat all bosses, including open world bosses, and get all achievements.

I took my time with the game because the world was a consistent creation, I enjoyed adding new things to build with, and getting through the "ladder" of realms until I reached the very last ones.

The difficulty is not bad, but varies. overall game play can be customised between "wuss" and "not bad". I played mostly on hard, with the last few ascended realms pushed up to Extreme. Extreme difficulty turned out to be the proper one for me. it was challenging enough. The only place I saw the balance broken was in the Solo vaults where I was dying and chipping away on a boss 1 bullet at a time, it took me 2+ hours to get through a solo vault. the public ones are set to easy I think, because with other players, it's laughably easy,

Another point that I didn't like were the entirety of the vault system. It was the same as the realm vaults just 3 times longer I understand the system but found it really really boring. You are basically either piss blood in solo vaults, or breeze through them in a group without the need to shoot a single shot..

I really liked the skyboxes, I wish the game would have more variety in that regard.

The building was a bit awkward at first, but it worked. The only thing bad about it was that the pacing in which the building pieces were distributed across the realms was way off.. I expected a new building set after my 2nd portal but I had to wait waaaaaay longer, to the point that I was borderline bored with the game.. I liked building and make an interesting house that I upgrade over time, so the lack of building pieces was frustrating. The devs confirmed to be working on the "replace" function for the building elements so I'm not giving them flack for it.

One system I found greatly confusing was the crafting one. I see that it's robust, but it's very convoluted. You need to unlock the station, the augment and the recipe separately.. Why? Why can't the augments just unlock the recipe?This needs to be 1 step shorter or better organised since this was one of the things I was considering giving the game a not recommended for, even though it's a brilliant system. In my opinion, the augments should unlock the new recipes. There are 2 ways to go at it. 1: the augments are cosmetic only, they are the "decoration" category, and you unlock stations and recipes from the realms. The other, perhaps better version is when you build an augment, they give the "exrta" recipes, and the workstation can build any item you unlocked without augments.
The problem is the workstation / augment attachments. one augment can enhance more than 1 station, and 1 station can get limited augments in range This leads to me loose track of what workstation is effected by what augment, and missing out of recipes, because there are no more augment slots on the station. Then I look up what is needed and I see that workstation and augments are next to each other yet I still cannot build something. I have to offload the table for example, them move it to an empty area, then move that specific augment in range to unlock crafting of what I need, then put everything back after done.. This is tedious and confusing.

I want to mention a thing I really liked, but was probably not intended: Hunting for companions. I really liked the idea that there can be randomly named people out there that you can recruit (although a bit awkwardly). A significant portion of my gametime was devoted to always looking at the NPC PIOs in a realms to see if I can find one that looked better or called better. Most of the NPCs were ugly a bit, and I knew I wanted a female companion. It didn't help that my companions kept disappearing between travels with all the high level gear I gave to them.. But regardless, it was a good minigame to find one that I liked. I eventually settled on one that was called Alice and looked like the youtuber ShoeOnHead. A bit.

Getting NPCs in realms was interesting, and good idea but some parts are very bad. For example, 50%of the time, you need to help them build something before you can recruit them. WHY? Now we built a house halfway in water with crafting stations, that nobody will use, because I took the NPC,, I think they should have a way to offer 2 things when you approach an NPC builder site: a quest to protect them while they pack and a quest to help them build, if you don't want to recruit them. That way you can decide based on name and look if you want to take them or help them settle. Much better system.

Multiplayer was more or less working, but it was a bit awkward to experiment with what was their loot and what was mine, what we could loot both and what we couldn't. It shouldn't have to be an experiment..

Building limits are assss.. Especially if you want to build on the same realm.. The reason me and my friends were not building on the same realm was that we all wanted our own house but kept hitting the build limit which was quite bad.

The story of the game was quite good, and void of all agenda, which I've not expected just by looking at the gamedevs. Thinking about the game overall ,the largest contributor for why I recommend it it's because it's focusing on the game and the world, and not on agendas and quotas. It just want to tell a good an interesting story. I'm recommending it because it's a game / fun first, and not a MTS / agenda first game.

An additional thing I wanted to mention was the voice acting. The voice actor for Pluck was extraordinary, I hope that the rest of the dialogues and notes will also be narrated with this quality in the future. I'll probably come back when new biomes will be added. That and new building pieces are the only thing that can pull be back in, since I played this game nearly for 300 hours at this point, I've seen enough, and the endgame currently is quite lacklustre

Overall good, game, time well spent, some misfires, lots more to develop but what's already in there is worth recommendation. Plus you can have a dog in-game.\t\t\t\t\t
