\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off I normally love survival crafting games and I've played more than I care to admit, but Nightingale makes crafting TEDIOUS and time consuming. Plus, you don't know the stats of what you're about to craft. Is it better, or worse than what you have? Who knows!? Just waste the materials which took hours to obtain to find out! (It doesn't show any stats on the crafting menus).

I hope you like crafting tables, because you're going to have an abundance of them and they require some item from another crafting table that required another item from yet another one and they all require the same types of materials.. so back out you go to forage, chop and mine - rinse and repeat. Also, the resources to keep some of the tables active (unless you shut them off every single time and then restart them when you need to use them) such as sticks....guess what? The tables/camp fire rip through them too quickly - so yet another journey to get yet more materials. Then there's the having to eat every 12 mins +-, don't you dare walk away from the game without logging out... the timer is ticking and most likely you'll come back to a character who's died and now you get to go get your inventory back where you died. I am beyond tired of feeding my character.. it feels like she has an eating disorder and is always stuffing food in her mouth.

Secondly, the combat lacks a LOT. Its clunky and boring...just keep mashing the same button over and over. The keys at times don't respond, so you keep pushing to use your climbing picks/axe/skinning knife etc. only to keep using your previous weapon/tool over and over. Need to heal? Keep mashing that button..perhaps it will switch over to use your healing kit/food.. maybe I'm just not pushing hard enough.. maybe I don't have any left..Nooo.. keep pushing the button hoping.. need to exit? Keep pushing ESC eventually it will activate and allow you to leave.

I want to be fair; it is a pretty game with some beautiful terrain and I enjoy building but....

Also, for some reason it won't allow me to add the final two sections of roof, I've tried so many different combos and I still have two gaping holes in my roof. Want to upgrade from a stick/wood house to stone? You have to dismantle EVERY single wall, ceiling..floor individually without knowing as you're hitting it if you're actually hitting the wall you want to remove or perhaps one of your crafting tables until it destroys (you can't just move something) - the fun continues though.. you need to remake that crafting table but for some reason the amount of materials you recovered when you accidentally dismantled it aren't the same as you now need to rebuild it.. time to hoof it again to get yet more materials.

Finally, the world lacks something... the animals clump in the same spots every single time.. no roaming unless you run through them..no worries though.. turn around and come back and they're back in the same exact spot! I wish I could explain what its lacking, I understand its after the worlds changed/died off..but it feels empty despite all the beautiful sunsets/rises and purple/pink trees.. I think its partly that you're going through portals to new realms yet they all pretty much look the same, at least so far.

I really want to like this game and perhaps eventually I will after some more work is done on it. Unfortunately Steam doesn't have a maybe or a wait and see option - just thumbs up or down..for now its down.

I would like to finish by commenting on the character creation, I know they're still working on it..but dang.. the current choices are a big bulbous nose or... I know a big bulbous nose with a narrow bridge? The presets are atrocious looking. Now, don't get me wrong I don't need a beauty queen..but come on! This game has some ugly female creation.. I don't know about the males since I haven't tried creating one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, this is a early access game.
There are going to be some things STILL being worked on. Like for example, some icons representing things like "carrots" appear as red circles with the Nightingale logo on it. Very simple, you KNOW what it is from the name. For those illiterate, I can see where this can be a problem. In that case, wait for the game to mature first so you get little pictures and can understand what that item is.

Second this game has a tremendous amount of features similar to Valheim. When you eat food, you get a HP/Stamina buff that lasts for a short time (15 mins to 30 mins). You can eat 3 different types of food to stack this buff.

There are Tiers of Crafting stations. Each Tier can only make its limited range of items. Once you "evolve" your character past that Teir, you move to the next Teir of crafting stations. Just like Valheim.

Also like in Valheim, you need warmth of fire to sleep. This game has that too. In valheim you can "augment" or improve items you make by adding these decorations around the crafting station. Sometimes this unlocks more recipies. Very simple. So for those who enjoy making a "kitchen" there are lots of decorations/augments to make that look nice but also further unlock items you can cook.

This is a crafting game mainly with some story/quests. Like Valheim.


I have played many games like this (Dark and Light - 500 hrs, ARK - 1600 hrs, Valheim 500 hrs, Palworld -- 155 hrs until it killed my 3080, Conan - 1200 hrs, 7 Days to Die - 2k hrs). This one is unique because itstead of having to travel far and figure out where the X biome is, you can just create that biome and go there. Generated on the fly. Its a nice change.

Building is similiar style to ARK but works better.\t\t\t\t\t
