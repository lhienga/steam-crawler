\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdited review after 26 more hours of play. I will mark edits with the word edit after each section. TLDR: I will recommend this game with A LOT of caveats IF they can address the server disconnect issues. Otherwise, this is still a downvote for me.

This game is incredibly rough. Server issues have been noted by others (constant failures to connect due to network issues).
Game crashes are too frequent on a new high-end PC. - edit: this was fixed by undervolting CPU and removing overclock on RAM. I should not need to do this to play a game, but this reduced a lot of frustration.

Combat is a main part of the game and sucks. Enemies often run on all 4s and are too low to hit effectively. Combat consists of a swarm of enemies, so there's not much in the way of tactics. There are no interesting mechanics to learn. It's the same hack and slash and potion and run every fight. - edit: combat gets more enjoyable after provisioner. I started on "hard" and it's just not enjoyable at early levels. The combat seems oriented toward the mid/later-game gear such as guns and axepicks or mauls. I ended up going back to "hard" realms about Gloom level. The sun giant was a fun fight. I have done it several times just for the enjoyment of it. Most fight are too straightforward with swarm tactics, but if they make more fights like the sun giant, I would enjoy the game even with all its issues.

Crafting is a mess. The complexity is interesting, but it needs a lot of help to be useful. Right now, you need a lot of different mid-level ingredients to make a new item, but the only way to figure out where to make that mid-tier ingredient is to run to the 12 other crafting stations and check or look in your guidebook. There's very little logic to crafting. The building is done well, but you're extremely limited (350 blocks) which means we'll never see beautiful builds like in Valheim or Enshrouded. - edit: I've spent a lot of time with crafting since this review, and my overall impression is the same, but I also now appreciate the nuances. I really like the augment system, but it is entirely unclear how everything works together without reviewing offline guides + spending time working on it yourself. I devoted like 4 hours just to figuring out the augments and what I wanted to use, and even with that, the game doesn't give enough detail for me to have been successful without looking up details in forums. I like the complexity of the crafting system, but it needs A LOT of focus to get it clean and clear.

Also, this game was launched before they had images for about 40% of the materials in the game. I could understand not having images for some difficult things, but they are missing images for coal, chitin, and other very common items. It's absolutely ridiculous.

All in all, this game looks beautiful, and it has a very interesting story. I enjoy most of the characters (though the companions need some sort of way to give them commands). I am going to keep playing this game with the hope they will fix many of the issues I've shared here.\t\t\t\t\t

Although there is a need to 'grind for levels' a bit, because there is so much else going on in the mean time and levels = new gear, it doesn't feel like a grind. Part of that is the nuanced crafting system and the ability to create and play new realms. So it's always a little bit of discovery and newness.

It IS still early access as I write this... so while there is definitely enough content to keep one busy already, I can see the edges of where things stop. Companions exist... but their behavior and capabilities leave something to be desired. There are realms of many combinations, but there could be more. You can build a base but it's limited in how many pieces you can use. My hope is that I'm seeing the bones of something that will be expanded in all directions. It has the capacity to be something pretty great longer term.\t\t\t\t\t
