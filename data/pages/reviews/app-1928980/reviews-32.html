\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was very hopeful for this game. Beautiful looking, but the game play is pretty bad. Movement is so sluggish. Combat is terrible. Targeting is bad. Trying to skin dead animals is a chore. Crafting system is a nightnamre. Nothing intuitive about it.

Grindy. So grindy. Every time I log in, I am resigned to spending a full 30 minutes of harvesting resources, just to build up my resources for repair, during a short bit of exploration.

Followers are sometimes helpful, like a quest full of agro. Your stamina won't get you througgh a battle in one piece, unless you have a follower to take some agro, so you can sit there and rest in the middle of it.

Personal storage is so painful in the first few hours. Unreasonably so. Once you have a follower, it gets better, since they have no weight limits. Why is that? What magic power do they have? Can I get that please? Sadly, you can't really give them commands, like "don't move in front of me every time I want to chop a tree", or "please come here when I want to unload your inventory", rather than stopping 200 feet away from my house, so I have to do the snail pace walk of shame, to carry your load to my storage baskets.

I've just become too frustrated with this game to enjoy. I'm only in 12 hours, but 13 is not a goal I wish to achieve at this point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm rather sad that I can't recommend this game, but there's just so much wrong with it.
Let's start with some basics:
1. It's boring. The pacing is terrible. The first realms have very little interesting content. They are there for forced farming to unlock the next realm of forced farming. It does pick up a bit when you hit the Herbarium realm, but I doubt most folks will really make it that far before getting bored. Even then, it's still a lot of lather, rinse, repeat. The puzzles are the same, the realm cards are the same (forest, desert, swamp), and the enemies are really nothing you haven't seen done before. You'd expect more from fae realms, but nope.
2. The AI is terrible. Mobs and bosses do the same thing...they rush directly at you at top speed. You can get a NPC helper who will assist you in battle, just be sure not to give them any high-end crafting materials or they will gladly chuck them into any random campfire or forge you walk by. Learning that part was fun. The rest of the NPCs, like quest givers and essence traders, are just placeholders standing there.
3. Speaking of quest-givers...I'm not sure why they have them. I've encountered 4 in 80 odd hours of gameplay that are just there to make unlocking something a bit more difficult and time wasting. The reward is something that you've previously unlocked through normal play, like a realm card or weapon, that has extra hoops to jump through to get just the next one in line.
4. Building. I think they took the basic UE5 system and just threw it in. It's just sad by today's standards. Do not buy this game if you enjoy building nice places. You'll be very disappointed. Due to the way the crafting system works, decorating is not gonna be fun either, Why do you have a wheelbarrow in the middle of the room? Because crafting. Why is there a frying and and a pot over here instead of in the kitchen? Because crafting.
When you realize Bethesda has better building than a game built on UE5...you get sad.
5. Crafting. Probably the best part of the game, and the most confusing. Get out your Excel so you can start making spreadsheets of what each station does, what items enhance which stations, which ones cancel out other enhancements, which components do what, what color they produce (yeah, no dye station here), and a host of other things...all pretty poorly explained in a barely usable menu system. It's like alien technology. It's probably awesome if you can figure it all out.

You likely already know about server issues, slow loading and realm changing, disconnects, falling through places forever (luckily, never encountered this one), and a host of more issues,
It's going to take a lot of work to fix this. I'm hopeful that I can change my review in the future as it really is a cool concept, and there are glimmers of a good game peeking out from what is currently a bit of a mess.
But right now, it's a nope for me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm giving this a thumb up. Here's why.
I haven't had any expectations before buying the game. I just hopped in and boarded the for now not-so-well-rated hype train.
The game is well playable, no major bugs discovered, just minor ones. No crashes in 50+ hours, no server connection issues.
Graphic looks beautiful, the stiff NPC not so much. Faces are hideous but you can create something nice if you give it some time.
Realm card combinations are decent but I hope more different biomes will be added with different topology as the realms are pretty generic. I think more creatures and tweaked AI will be also needed.
The game mechanics. I can see the frustration some people might have here. In pursuit for original content, this game has introduced some design and UI/UX decisions that can be a real unintuitive pain. Please don't. It's really bad. Like divided action/tool bar that makes no sense and consuming food by pressing F instead of LMB or no info showing on realm address cards when I want to use them to open portal (need to drop them on ground). Or so many crafting station additions I don't even remember which goes where and why my workbench doesn't let make me ammo when the saddle is right next to it. I can't even store realm cards properly, they're taking significant amount of inventory slots now. Why? And there's more, things that need to be explained more clearly.
Bugs. There are some. As for now, crops are buggy, sunflower grows on, then it doesn't at all, the whole planter gets stuck... constructions and items have all mats needed but they still show blueprint...
Building is decent but I think some things need to be re-considered like how some blocks fit together. And maybe more building blocks as the current set is very basic and thus limiting. The building blocks and decorations are very nice looking.
Quests are basic and the whole progress is slow-paced thanks to the crafting system which someone might not like but I personally don't mind. I like to know there's still content ahead of me. There are puzzles and they're quite repetitive but... ok. Early access.
Also the most peculiar seems the language choice here. Puck's voice acting is amazing. But the dialogues or lore are written to mimic historical language to fit the era. Which can be quite an issue for non-native speakers.
tl;dr
Many people complain about state of this early access game. However there's no clear definition of how such early access game title should look like and so I'm not going to judge from this point of view. Yes, yes, yes, Enshrouded is in better state and ASA seems like it's a forever early acces thanks to its bugs... maybe some of you also remember No Man's Sky and it's very bad beginnings and look where it's now.
I will keep an eye on the game as for me it's still a way to relax after a long day and hope the Inflexion team will keep up the good work. I want to believe this will be really good one day, it's not THAT bad but I can see a looong way ahead.
Soo... if you don't mind the challenge of discovering and slow progress, it's ok, yes. If you're just looking a place to chill and build, I'd say it's ok. It's not the best start of them all but it's a solid start.\t\t\t\t\t
