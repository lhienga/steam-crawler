\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has great potential but still has several flaws which for early access can be expected. The biggest problem is the online requirement. I get where the developer was coming from with tackling the hardest part first but then early access should have been delayed until it also had an offline component. For me, and I believe a large percentage of players, survival/ crafting games are something I prefer to play solo. I can play at my own pace and every new recipe or discovery I make on my own is more rewarding. It's so annoying that every time I go through a portal or start the game I am sitting there waiting, not for the world to load, but for a server to respond. I've experienced issues where I mined a large node and got disconnected from the server, only to get back in and find not only did I not have a chance to pick the ore up, but the node I mined has disappeared so I just lost everything from that. I also have had problems with some of the combats as the enemies or my character suddenly teleport around the screen making the enemies almost impossible to hit and sometimes resulting in my needless death and corpse run. I blame this more on the server side than my network as everything else is not experiencing any connection issues during those times. I usually see messages cycle saying awaiting server, everything is ready and then going back to awaiting server, etc. almost like the server really didn't have the capacity to deal with me but it responded anyhow and then said sorry I forgot I'm full.

As far as crafting goes, I've seen a lot of negative reviews complaining about the granularity of the recipes. For example, to make a gun, you just can't take ingots and wood to a crafting bench. You need to turn the wood into lumber, the lumber into a stock, the ingots into a shaft, the shaft into a barrel, etc. then take those components to a crafting bench. While I agree it does add extra steps, I think it adds to the reward in the end. I think another issue players have is there is usually a natural progression in the refinement of raw components. In other games once you can make leather, then you just take all of your hides and throw them into the tanning station and create leather because there is no other use for hides after that. In this game that raw component can also be used to make straps or felt so you need to be more thoughtful on refining things. One complaint I do have is with some of the labeling preventing you from using items together. For instance, animal pelts are labeled as predator and prey. If I make felt out of some of them that label will follow them (ex. Felt Predator tier 1 or Felt Prey tier 1). If I have one of each, I can't go to the sewing bench and make a garment that requires 2 felt because I need 2 of the same label (predator or prey). However the end garments attributes are not impacted by which one I used. Maybe in later game this makes a difference, but at least in the early recipes this attribute should've been ignored or given the ability to add multiple types to the recipes as the requirement is 2 felt not 2 felt prey.

Overall, I am torn about recommending this game. I'm really enjoying it, but if your intention is like mine to mainly play solo then I can't recommend it right now. If the developer had made offline a high priority instead of a medium I would say it is totally worth jumping in now as some of the complexities may mean that even if you have to start over for offline mode you will have learned a great deal from mistakes you made. If you intend to only play with friends then it is a pretty solid game for an early release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMarch.10/2024
The very first and most important thing you need to know about Nightengale actually isn't about the contents of the game, but rather the studio that developed it.
Headed and populated largely by ex-Bioware Dev's from back when Bioware made great games, Inflexion is delivering a unique game that is being moulded by community feedback.
You know that tiny little game (sarcasm) called Baldurs Gate 3 by Larion studios? There game was developed with the same approach, in that it was majorly transformed and developed by community feedback in it's Early Access state.
This is a rather rare approach to development in today's industry where most commonly you see investor-focused, creatively stifled, new IP's or predictable rinse and repeat sequels.

Nightengale is a game that just released into Early Access, and understandably so, in it's current state, it has been greeted with mixed reviews. There are some bugs to be found, as you might imagine, but I have been surprised with how great this game looks and runs on the medium and low end computers that my wife and I run it on.
At it's core, Nightengale is a survival and crafting game. If that is not a genre you enjoy then this is likely not the game for you. There are indeed quests in the game, and a TON of lore for you to discover and read up on, but understand that many of these quests merely direct your crafting journeys or provide alternative approaches to acquiring critical components.
To fully appreciate Nightengale for the special gem that it is, you need to switch from the speed-run, min/max, mentality of other games, and understand that it's a game that encourages exploration, experimentation, and overall an enjoyment of the truly beautiful and special PLACES that you uncover.

My trust and hopes are immensely high for Inflexion studios based on the legacy of Aaryn Flynn's pedigree for impeccable games. I believe that Inflexion will become one of my favourite studios quite quickly, and if you can see the potential of this truly special game, and the speed at which the game is improving and being added upon, it is hard not to justify a 10 out of 10 (despite the bugs).
