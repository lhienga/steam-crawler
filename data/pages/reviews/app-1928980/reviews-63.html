\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a player who has put 90 hours into the game and achieved item level 275, I have some thoughts. First of all, the game is absolutely worth buying in it's current state. The story line isnt finished, but its engaging as it is and will keep you busy for at least 60 hours. The crafting system in this game is VERY IN DEPTH. If you want something where the crafting is mindless, this isnt it. The crafting is not in anyway hard, but there is a lot that goes into it. Thankfully, you will get all of the understanding you need as you play the game. It starts really easy and gets complex as it goes along. The combat is a bit lacking, in my opinion. The game is still early, and im sure the AI will get better as it goes along. That's not to say its not fun, because it is, and the bosses can be challenging. Just saying that AI is kinda dumb right now. All in all its a really fun game, great building system, tons of options, lots to explore, and I really look forward to every update that comes out until it's finished.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, anyone who is complaining about this game and has less than 10+ hours into it does not deserve to give this game a thumbs down. With Nightingale, you're in it for the long haul. Think of something like Ark. Very long progression with a lot of exploration.

Graphics are fantastic. The realms are vibrant and visually attractive (even the swamps). Been running on ultra with a 5800XT and not having any issues.

The always online feature is a plus for me. My friends and I don't have to rent a server! Any of us can connect in and work on our world whenever we want! I built a mansion while me friends were offline and they got to come back on to a brand new home. It works. We have suffered a couple network connection issues, but a game restart fixed it right up. Everything is running very smoothly.

The crafting in this game is VERY extensive and realistic. For all the people crying that it's too much: How do you think the real world works with making stuff? The crafting system has multiple stations that each provide basic materials and refined materials. All which you use in combination to build your gear and whatever else needs crafting. I have reached tier 3 crafting stations and it seems like they do begin to combine some of the lower tier station features into one, which makes it easier. But as a recommendation... Set yourself up a system to make it efficient. You can move and name containers, so place your basic mats next to your basic stations and place your refined mats next to your refinement stations. It's not a hard concept. The one recommendation I would have for crafting is that smelting ingots takes an exceptionally long time compared to cutting lumber or spinning fiber into thread.

Similar to crafting is building. The building system works great. You can blueprint out the entire building before you start building. The roofs all line up well. No seams in the walls and floors. It all blends nicely. Looks beautiful. My one critique is the indicators of structural load. They are there, which is nice. But I can't seem to figure them out and if they are for the collective structure or per piece. So some clarification on that would be nice. Perhaps someone smarter than me will post a YouTube video on it.

One cool aspect of this game are the survivors you can recruit. They follow you around and fight, but don't say "Hey, look, a cave. I wonder what's inside." Can't have everything I guess... But, the cool thing about survivors is they will keep all your fires going at home and will build your blueprints as long as they have access to your containers. Recommendation: Do not give them access to all your containers, just certain ones. They tend to just move ♥♥♥♥ around too for some reason. And they will put anything burnable in the fires, so be mindful of your lumber and wood bundles.

Next thing are the realms. They are procedurally generated, but overall, they look great. We got one dud that was pretty darn empty in terms of points of interest and things to do. But that's okay. Just delete that portal and go to another realm! In the higher tier realms, there are harder jumping puzzles where you have to apply different realm effects via the realm cards you craft. It's an awesome system. I'm loving it. Every tier of realms has unique character to it.

Overall, this game rocks! I'm absolutely loving it. I just made it to the Gloom realms and am having a blast. The progression is paced well. It definitely doesn't feel like the slog that Ark and Valheim are. And the worlds at least have things to do. There are traders, points of interest, puzzles, and tons of things to hunt. It breaks up the monotonous chore of farming mats. If you want to "beat" a game, Nightingale isn't for you. There are a lot of realm tiers and you're in it for the long haul as you craft your way up from sticks and stones to whatever the devs have in store for us. For Early Access, I'll give it a 10/10.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInitially, I was drawn to Nightingale's captivating premise and the pedigree of its developers. However, upon playing the game, I encountered several issues that hindered my enjoyment.

Inventory and Crafting

The lack of integrated storage for crafting is a significant inconvenience. Having to constantly transfer items between storage and crafting tables disrupts the gameplay flow. Additionally, the proliferation of crafting tables for different items adds unnecessary complexity.

Limited Biomes

The game currently offers only three biomes, which limits the variety of gameplay experiences. While the biomes are visually stunning, they lack the depth and diversity to sustain long-term engagement.

Early Release Concerns

It's evident that Nightingale was released prematurely. The core gameplay mechanics are solid, but the game lacks polish and content. More time in development would have allowed for a more refined and enjoyable experience.

Potential and Hope

Despite its current shortcomings, Nightingale has the potential to be an exceptional game. The developers have a proven track record, and the game's unique blend of fantasy, magic, and steampunk elements is intriguing. I eagerly anticipate future updates and expansions that address the current issues and enhance the overall gameplay experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a huge disappointment.

I don't usually bother writing a review but since i have nothing better to do while this game has already taken 10 minutes to load up a portal to the next realm i figure why not.

It's a survival game with very little freedom to play your way. I wanted to explore realms before finding a place to build but no i have to build a house up in the beginner area in order to get gear good enough to unlock the next area. The game also forces you to constantly engage in some of the most boring combat i have seen to date in order for a small chance to get better quality resources to craft better quality gear.

It requires a lot of patience to figure things out because you get almost no explanation on the mechanics of the game.

I'm hoping the delay between firing the sling bow and actually hitting something can be explained because of the weapon type and that delay won't exist with proper firearms later in the game otherwise that is just horrible to use.

Remember when everyone was making jokes about how expressionless the NPCs were in starfield? I've seen more emotion on bare drywall than the NPCs in this game.

FYI since i opened up a portal with the astrolabe/forest card combo i have killed all the enemies that spawned, harvested every single resource in the area around the portal sat there watching the portal for about 5 mins and written this review and the portal still has not finished opening. the game has literally completed a full night cycle and i still cant go anywhere else.

Edit: so it turns out that portal is problem is actually a glitch, so resetting it and putting the cards back in fixes that, annoying but not the end of the world.

Some other things to be aware of though the astrolabe/forest biome seems to be exactly the same as the previous forest combo just harder enemies so I'm now having doubts about interesting the exploration is going to be if there is only 3 biomes available just in different configurations for each person.

The repetitiveness of this game is no joke you are going to continually do 1 of 4 things to progress. attack the same boring enemies with a combat system which is as exciting as just side stepping and swinging your weapon of choice.
Defending some NPCs while a machine does something which is the same as the previous task but with the added annoyance of defending a weak machine that the enemies just charge at and destroy before you even realise they have spawned.
Building a random structure for an NPC so just farm resources and just give them to the NPC and finally repeatedly just watch some pyramids play a note and then repeat that note back in a specific order to unlock another recipe... that's it that is the entire game.

Edit 2: oh i missed one. One other task is looking for hidden glyphs and clicking on them to unlock your reward...

To summerise its a poorly assembled collection of the basic bones of every other survival RPG which admitadly has some very minor interesting changes to the crafting system but the flesh around the bones is nothing more than a rinse and repeat cycle of bland simple tasks that are honestly far more annoying than challenging in an environment that looks good in the pictures but you will never find anything new if you continue to explore. if you have seen it ounce you have seen it a million times.\t\t\t\t\t
