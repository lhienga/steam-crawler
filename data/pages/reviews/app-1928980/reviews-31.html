\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Nightingale is currently in its Early Access stage, my experience with the game has left me skeptical about its potential improvement. One glaring issue is the limited variety of enemies, and disappointingly, every encountered boss shares an identical appearance.

The combat mechanics contribute significantly to the overall dissatisfaction. Despite being presented in a First-Person style, the combat lacks the visceral impact one would expect. The strikes feel unconvincing, and the overall experience comes across as clunky. Comparing it to Skyrim, a game released more than a decade ago, highlights the stark contrast in the quality of combat. Nightingale falls short in this regard.

Perhaps the most concerning aspect is the game's claim to be Triple-A, a status it fails to live up to. The current state does not align with the standards set by true Triple-A titles. From my perspective, the investment in Nightingale has been regrettable, as the promised quality is yet to manifest. As the game stands, it feels like a considerable waste of money, and I hope future updates can address these issues to salvage the overall gaming experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally Awesome Things in this Game but Currently Unplayable.

I have about 40 hours (I have put hundreds of hours each in games like valheim, minecraft, enshrouded) in this game and I would love to add a few hundred but error after error, freeze after freeze, half of my playtime feels like troubleshooting not playing. I was getting a game freeze a few times an hour. that finally stopped about 10 hours in an then at about 37 hours I keep getting the network error on trying to sign in at character select (error "dratted").

I love what this game is trying to do and the time I have spent actually playing has been fun and rewarding. the climbing picks, the dash-to-umbrella movement combo, the build material effects and min-maxing tools and equipment, and the magic, are refreshingly fun and new in the survival craft genre, but I would wait until the game is more stable.

The game is complex but not needlessly so. Hopefully they add better quest and crafting direction but other than the technical difficulties I really enjoy this game, but again, the technical difficulties are literally unplayable right now (can't log into servers)

I will definitely be getting back in as I have faith the studio will be able to make progress, and I understand that this is "early access" but many games are in much better states from a functional standpoint (Enshrouded, Valheim, Palworld to name a few) so I will be enjoying those until I can really invest in this one.

I simply can't recommend a game I can't play.

It takes time to get into the game. its complex and to understand this game and how to play it you have to put the time in. I personally started getting really into it around the 25 hour mark (I am slow so you'll probably understand things quicker), but unfortunately the more I got into it, it seemed the more issues that I had. I REALLY like this game but only if I can play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs the game perfect? No. But it scratches that survival game itch I've been searching for. The atmosphere and overall concept of the game is astounding. But it definitely needs some work.

Things I would recommend to the developers:
- UI cleanup, I know a lot of the art is missing as its in early development but this def needs to be uploaded!
- Character Revamp - IMO, the character creation is definitely a bit atrocious (happy that it's 1st person for 95% of the time).
- "Spells" being more accessible and easier to use. Maybe having a book of some sort that revolves around a different type of resource (since some of the spells are a bit broken and op).
- Load times - like GEEEEEEEEZE easy 2 min time to load my base and I'm, wired in with a decent processor.
- Unless I havent discovered it, being able to move base items around - which if I haven't discovered it, there wasn't a notable tutorial.
- A few of the animations are a bit.... eh. Like pulling out your 1st pistol, the game "constructs" it in your hand.
- Climbing in this game is atrocious - and some quests are blocked by climbing. PLEASE FIX THIS

This game is definitely not a "No Man's Sky release" - but it's not too far from it either. The uniqueness of this game saves and overshadows the flaws which makes the game SUPER playable and fun - but the items I listed above might need to be prioritized.

Regardless,

Excellent game concept and I cannot wait to see this game grow while I continue to play. This has the potential to be one hell of a survival game if done right.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight now, I would not recommend the game as is. There are some really cool ideas behind it, but some things that need to be reworked like having to buy every blueprint for most of the main "crafting tables". Speaking of which, do we really need that many different crafting tables/ tanners/ etc... The boss towers are cool, but the whole walled off thing till your strong enough to enter is annoying. The first one is told to you as part of the story, and I don't know if the others will be told later in the quest as you progress, but I discovered 4 more of these boss areas and have no idea what level of gear i have to achieve to get in them. This game has too much holding your hand kinda quests and not enough of letting you pave your own path.
Like I said there are some cool ideas, like the different realms to go to, but it just doesn't play right right now, maybe it'll get better as early access goes on. I will be keeping my eye on it to see what happens with the game.\t\t\t\t\t
