\r\n\t\t\t\t\tListen this game is in early access, it's not shocking it plays like it.
TLDR: It has everything for a good game, needs polish.
I have been following this game since I saw the first trailer randomly a few years ago and I tried my best to not watch any gameplay footage. I wanted a truly blind experience.
After 8 hours I can say it has so much potential.

Going in I thought it would be like a survival Victorian Destiny style of game, where you grind up weapons and gear to open a portal to a raid.
The actual game is different than what I had expected, this is truly a survival game first before everything else. The world is incredibly abundant in most resources and getting said resources for crafting feels reasonably challenging. Exploring the world that is made for you to find what you need to progress is relaxing. One thing I kept thinking about would be some sort of skill tree or some other way to make resource gathering feel a little more player-interactive than what it is now. The system of crafting and having augmentations for it feel so fun, like seeing all the moving pieces in a clock. Eventually I'd like to see some decorative things having effects on crafting but time will tell. Clothing in the game is a little... interesting? You have the choice of drip or drown and if you want to look like a Victorian bad ass you have to grind for it. Which is a choice that I dislike. The overall theme of the game oozes that bustling city look and for a game with magical creatures and portals that lead to different realms, you would assume you could fashion a better looking starter gear than the Orange Peel set.

Hunting feels fun but leads to my first REAL complaint about the game being the combat.
The combat in this game is not bad but I feel at times it can get really - boring? At least what I have seen in early game there isn't much variety on what the best weapon is to use. Without a doubt the crossbow is just incredibly strong, making other weapons feel redundant.
Fighting your first overleveled boss will make you realize just how insane a power level difference can be, and it is frustrating when you feel like you are shooting paper bullets at a higher level enemy. You can't fell a giant beast until you are at that level which makes sense but still frustrating as a player who likes the occasional challenge
The final complaint from me is the speed of enemies; if you stray too far away from lets say the hippo enemy they will activate their turbo boost and ram into you at light speed. It's funny but a bit frustrating at times.

I want to see this game blossom into something amazing and all it needs is a little more time in the oven. Until then I will still recommend picking the game up as with most games in early development it is cheaper than when the 1.0 update drops and it supports the developers that make this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, a tip; the difficulty of your home realm (chosen at the start) has a massive impact on the difficulty of the boss fights to progress in the game. If those fights aren't fun, just open up an easy abeyance realm to get over those hurdles.

Great exploration game, first time since Skyrim I've enjoyed just running around to see what I find. The environments are gorgeous -- even the expansive views. Way better than any other survival game on this front.

It's an OK crafting & survival game. Too grindy for it's own good. But the mix & match system of materials is neat. With a heap more recipes and a little number tuning it could be amazing.

Building is a good system. Nice to be able to lay out blueprints before putting in materials. Augmentations make good decorations and also have an actual purpose. The game could use a ton more furniture, however, but that's the kind of thing that's easy to add.

Combat is OK. Dodging and blocking aren't very effective. I really wish I didn't have to go through a menu to switch out spells. Firearms are surprisingly well balanced (by being slow to reload). You can aim your melee shots a little bit, and it is fun to sweep enemies by axing their legs. But most of the difficult fights end up feeling the same because the enemies just swarm you. I see potential here, but if you primarily want a game with good combat, I'd wait on this title.

The game needs a lot of work on its quality of life issues though. Inventory and recipe management is pretty bad just because you will gather so many recipes and items by mid-game that the lack of sorting, filtering, etc. is a glaring omission.

Overall, I've gotten a lot of enjoyment out of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHello All,

FIrst in-depth review after 13,000 hours of gaming on steam.

The good

The game is great to put in early access 9.5/10. A well solid build for an early access game. For an overall game a 6/10. I say this because the foundation of the game is fantastic. This is the first survival game I actually got into and I have played all the big titles (Ark, Conan, Subnatica, enshroud, no man sky.... etc). This game actually has a story pushing you forward instead of the insensitive of surviving to survive. not saying others don't, but this one actually grabbed my attention. It is interesting to say the least. The crafting can be overwhelming sometimes but very little compared to the thought that this is the time of the industrial revolution and that building and crafting where so huge during that period of time. Building is good not great, but good. I have seen better but also seen a lot worse across the board. The game has nice mechanics (except for jumping this can be improved). Combat is enjoyable and not overly difficult, I play on easy and like it like that hitting monster is responsive and feels rewarding. Gun game play is nice and getting ammo is not super inconvenient, so I do not feel like I am scarce for bullets. The realm system gives you a sense of a home with enough material to to create and prepare yourself for the next realm that you come across, and it all randomly generated based on cards such as biome and difficulty. You can even if you want keep the realm you made or reset it this was a nice touch for the developers to keep in mind. The tutorial in place does a good job of holding your hand, but giving you freedom to grow and develop like any good parent should do. Last the graphics, I feel like with the people at least I love. I know some people will say it looks outdated, but I "think the developers were going for a Victorian style of art that depicts people. If that was the vision they hit the mark. Mostly everything looks good to great no complaints for this day and age of graphics. It is a well rounded game with plenty of content.

The Bad

It being in early access so I take it with a grain of salt. Though some things the developers kind of just completely missed. The biggest one for me is bricks..... You can make bricks, but not brick structures it is the time of the industrial revolution I mean come on. There is even brick structures in the realms, but I can not craft a factory base that would look awesome. Climbing like I mentioned before could use a little TLC, but not a game deal breaker. Missing asset pictures and some realm cards are pretty useless, not a complaint, but the developers could have spent time else where. Now this is my last complaint and a personal one. From a development team that was once on the BioWare team the game lacks RPG elements in terms of leveling up. I know you level up based on gear, but that is not really an RPG in my opinion. You don't get better at something just because you have better gear, you get better at it because you have been doing it and have learned. The better gear helps and that is what they should implement here....

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took me [looks at the steam counter] 13hrs to hit the breaking point on this game.

It has potential. It should have stayed cooking for another year, but even then I think the designers have their eyes on the wrong targets so I am not sure it would have helped.

It can be pretty.

It has an interesting visual premise with the Victorian/steam-punk type of vibe they have in all the advertising merchandise.

And you get to experience none of it, because you get to dress in rags and sport the latest in "they hit me with an ugly stick" fashion for a looooooooooooong time. And they want you to keep upgrading junk and making junk for way too long.

They could have had you start with a piece or two of victorian-esque gear. They could have had all the right look with low levels ... but, no, they went with: cave man.

Seriously.

But I could have survived through that. The thing that kills this game is the game loop, and that what I am concerned about overall.

Grind stuff to unlock gear to level up your gear level to open the dungeon to kill the boss to open the next level of grind stuff to unlock gear to level up your gear level to open the dungeon to kill the boss to .....

THAT IS THE LOOP. That's it.

You do it for about 8hrs and you think "okay, this is fun" and then suddenly ... it's not.

The game is precariously thin and tedious. It's built on lovely worlds, but the mobs are the same, the bosses are the same, the gameplay loop is numbingly repetitive, the random-gen worlds are pretty ... but FEEL like procedurally generated content: "ooh, there's a tower to climb, they had stuff in the tutorial missi-EMPTY"

"What about this on-EMPTY"

"Mayb-EMPTY"

It's just VACUOUS and I am not sure the designers realize that because they got so caught up with their portals, worlds, and - apparently -end game visuals you don't get to experience until well beyond your boredom levels.

It feels like Starfield in all the wrong ways.

Elephant in the room:
Presently no offline mode and online has connection issues. This is the case but offline is actively being worked on as a primary goal. Server connectivity may be helped as more servers come online but that is a wait and see and your personal success with online may vary based on your own particulars.
Visual and Audio:
Procedural generated worlds look great and the skyboxes are varied and pleasant. Animations are smooth though they could benefit from additional attention. Character models are not going to be pretty but we can all take solace in knowing our character is the norm and not the ugly duckling (though you could make your such if you choose!). Creatures in the world are creative and I think they fit the story. NPCs are wooden (Puck is an exception) presently and could use some animation or behavior. The sound effects are good and seem to fit. I barely notice the soundtrack which is fitting for a survival game and enjoy it when I do hear it.
Gameplay/loop:
The story is good if you care to read all the dialog. I do not recommend skipping the story as I tried to do as you will find yourself far more gated and may end up grinding to acquire items that would otherwise have been handed to you. Keep watch on your journal as it tells you what quests you are on and who to find.

The difficulty is yours to chose and can be altered at each new portal you go through! The Food / rest mechanics only punish in that you will be borderline useless if you ignore them but do not seem to kill you by themselves which I like as well as the environment effects not being deadly but instead a debuff to your stats.

Mining / lumber both feel good doing (as much as they can) though I would like to see the nodes regenerate over time (even slowly). This is offset by spells and creatures that can regenerate parts of what you have used up but they are small in scale and not reliable. I think the idea right now is to travel to other worlds to farm resources and mostly leave your home base pristine (portals to worlds can be reset which refreshes them).

The gearing could use attention. I found myself unintentionally skipping a LOT of potential upgrades simply because I could not figure out how to get them or did not know they existed leaving me in some very unpleasant looking early gear until I got near the end game. Would like to see some additional offhand options that are not food or an umbrella. Having gear that can stave off cold and possibly heat would also be a plus though there are some means to do this in game as-is.

Partner AI could benefit from attention. Put an axe in my followers hand and it took 3 real world minutes before she understood to chop at the tree she had been standing next to. Also if you leave said axe in their hands do not be surprised when you suddenly have a tree crash down on your head while talking to an NPC.

Endgame loop is a touch..... loopy..... There are multiplayer dungeon runs and High End worlds available. Right now the former is by far more efficient for farming the games primary currency making it unlikely many will rush off to the latter. The dungeon runs themselves can be frustrating at first as you WILL be woefully undergeared and relying heavily on the rest of the team to pull you through but are fun 15-20ish minute runs once you start upgrading your gear. Only 3 available right now so yes they do become "samey" over time.

The ability to upgrade your home/base is very fun and has a lot of freedom for creativity and is to me the primary reason to keep coming back. This and gear are likely the two points there can never be enough of and hope the devs keep adding more to them over time.

Overall:
Definiltely recommend this one. It has lots of room to grow but is absolutely fun in its current Early Access form. If offline is a must for you then add this to your wishlist and check back as it is coming.\t\t\t\t\t
