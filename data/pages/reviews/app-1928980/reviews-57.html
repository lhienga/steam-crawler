\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this game IS in Early Access and DOES have some issues, I can't rave about it enough! The building is pretty standard but the Realm system and the insanely large crafting system make this a must-play for survival game aficionados.

Exploring the Realms can feel a tad repetitive while grinding for certain materials but no where near as bad as most other survival games with their fixed maps or procedurally generated biomes. The ability to make new Realms adds both a sense of control over the game world and a huge breath of fresh air whenever your current Realms get stale, too easy, or emptied of worthwhile resources. With 4 difficulty settings, 3 biome types, and 7 world modifiers there are 84 different Realms a player can create that are then each able to have a Minor Modifier added. With 30+ Minor Modifiers available, the Realm combos offer hundreds of hours of interesting exploration opportunities.

The crafting system may be the single most intricate I have seen. With hundreds of materials that have multiple variations and levels of rarity there can be dozens of ways to build just one tool or piece of equipment.

In the endgame you gain access to a multiplayer HUB with some endgame vendors and a conclusion to the current leg of the story, leaving much anticipation for more content as the game moves out of Early Access. The Vault Raids that can be done with other players add a fun little social aspect to the late game and the prospect of using Estate Address Cards to connect your main base and Realm to other players' Realms also provides another level of expansion to the number of unique Realms a player can access. Though much of the content in a Realm will be used up by the player who created it there are still resources and creatures that will respawn over time and some of the named Fabled creatures can be hard to find in your own limited number of Realms.

Overall this game offers hundreds of hours of driving content and weeks of possible gear combinations to try out while also having a well-rounded story and well developed lore. As the game moves toward full release the experience promises to improve and flesh out dramatically. I highly recommend this game for fans of survival, crafting, or even just a good FPS.\t\t\t\t\t

Nightingale Early Access Review

At the time of writing I'm approaching 40 hours of playtime but haven't finished the game. It's seems very unfair for this game to be getting a 'MIXED' rating when it has so much to offer in terms of originality, visuals and gameplay.

Nightingale is a survival-crafting game set in Victorian styled Fae realms As Inflextion games aptly put it: "Your goal: become a skilled Realmwalker, and navigate the web of transdimensional portals. Only then can you discover your way to the magical city of Nightingale, the last known bastion of humanity."

You start with a pretty robust character creator, although the character you create won't be that pretty, in fact, I don't think you'd describe any character in this game as pretty. Characters are somewhat stylised whereas the realms are more realistic and beautiful.

There is currently only one speaking character in the game - Puck, your tutorial guide. He's superbly voiced by Marc Warren with a script so compelling you relish the time spent with him. Other NPCs use text dialogue.

You choose your starting realm (Forest, Swamp or Desert) and you eventually get to an Essence Trader and an NPC who facilitates a companion. Essence is the main currency in the game. You collect it by dismantling items, killing enemies and completing quests, towers and sites of power. Soon you'll be able to craft an estate cairn which acts as a respawn and fast travel point to your estate.

But be careful what tool you give your new companion. I decided to build my first estate around a large tree, but part way through the process my companion felled the tree which demolishing half my house. Tip: if you don't want your house destroyed, either don't give your companion an axe (if you give them a pick they will gather stone instead) or don't build your house within tree-felling range!

You'll be given a quest to enter your first Site of Power which requires a gear score of 20. You then go about building crafting benches, some of which are bought from the Essence Trader, to gradually upgrade your equipment.

Crafting is one of the key elements in this game and you'll be doing a lot of it. I do enjoy this system as it seems to strike a balance between simplicity and depth. For instance, to craft a gun you need to craft the different components, e.g. a barrel. To craft the barrel you need metal ingots, to craft the ingots you need ore which you need to mine. For the most part this process is quite compelling. There are things that could be added to make the process smoother though. At the time of writing, you have to have the materials needed in you inventory, they can't be used directly from your storage, which means lots of "now where did I put that..." I currently have six large chests and it's an idea to keep certain items together for convenience.

Weapons and gear can be augmented by adding Enchantments, Infusions and charms. Enchantments can imbue your weapons with powers and can even regrow felled trees, as long as you leave the stump intact, whereas Infusions and charms can be applied to gear to enhance your abilities. These can be found in the realms or crafted if you have the blueprints.

Each realm, except your home abeyance realm, has a Fae Tower. These have three main functions;
1. Like Assassin's Creed towers, they reveal all the locations on the map.
2. They're a good source of essence and hope echoes.
3. They contain the device for customising that realm with minor realm cards.


As you complete sites of power, Fae towers and open chests you'll collect realm cards both major and minor. Major realm cards are used to travel to another realm but you need two cards. One determines the type or realm and the other determines the difficulty. This makes for a pretty unique way of customising your experience. You can further customise chosen realm with minor realm cards which affect your realm by, for instance, reducing the gravity so you can jump huge distances (useful in the swamp realm) at the cost of your tools breaking quicker.

You'll also find 'hope echoes' around the realms that give you a well written insight into the backstory.

As well as crafting gear you'll need to craft provisions. Meat-based food boosts health and plant-based food boosts stamina. You will also get blueprints for planters and be able to grow your own plants. These have to be maintained with water. If your health, stamina or rest level get low your character starts sounding weary, which is a good way of indicating you need to rectify the situation.

If you die (succumbing) and your companion is still active, they can revive you, but if they have also succumbed you will respawn at your estate. Which can be a pain if it's a long way from where you died, because you have a limited amount of time to get back to your death chest containing anything you were carrying at the time. But you can build a Faerie ring - a respawn point, if you remember to take the necessary materials with you to build it. It's best not to build this in the area where to boss is because it can easily be destroyed. Note that if you're using a firearm, you will lose the ammunition to the chest and have to collect it before you can continue fighting.

One interesting aspect of the menu is you can see what all the traders in all the realms are selling but you need to be in front of them to buy anything. So if you see something you want to buy and it's in a 'hard' realm you have to decide if you want to risk entering that realm to get to the trader as they could be right next to the portal or the other side of the realm. As all realms are procedurally generated you don't know until you go there.

I'm at the stage now where the sites of power have very hard bosses, two at once in some cases, and I've succumbed several times. I also found a site of power 'dungeon within a dungeon'. There was a portal in the 'dungeon' (site of power). That was interesting and fun because I had no idea what to expect and what would happen if I died, which I did, but respawned near the entrance (without a Faery ring) and thankfully not back at my estate.

There were a few times I found I wasn't sure how to progress, and went to the community to ask. They pointed out that if you read all the info you have and follow the quests you'll get there and I found that to be sound advice.

There are a few things I'd change, such as being able to instruct your companion to collect certain materials and more clothing variety would be nice but that may come later in the game. Combat is okay, but because you're swinging, say, an axe against a sometimes fast moving enemy, by the time the swing finishes the enemy has moved, so you miss. I needs a lock-on system really.

So far I've found the game very compelling and find myself thinking about it when I'm not playing. A big part of that is the excellent music score by Todd Baker which beautifully compliments the magical realms. Sound design in general is also very good, I especially like the sounds of entering a realm through a portal.

But the visuals are spectacular, particularly in the forest and swamp realms. I read some reviews that say some of the areas don't look good but I'm yet to find them. Also they mention bugs but again, I've yet to experience any apart from a couple of crashes and my companion walking over a fire and catching alight. There are also several items that have placeholder images but for my, nearly forty hours in an Early Access game is pretty good. I should say I have only played solo so far, so some of the issues people are experiencing may be in co-op.

The 'DLC' art book alone, may compel you to play the game. It's substantial and some of the best game art I've ever seen.

If you're someone who rushes to get to end game, this is probably not for you but if you're someone who likes to savour the realms and takes time to stop and smell the ... essence, then I would highly recommend Nightingale.\t\t\t\t\t
