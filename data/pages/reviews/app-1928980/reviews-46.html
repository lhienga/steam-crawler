Review:

Summary:
While the game boasts stunning skyboxes and generally impressive graphics, its repetitive nature leaves much to be desired. Despite good performance, there are noticeable drops, especially in certain areas. The building system, while promising, suffers from some structural quirks. However, it's worth noting that the game is still in early access, and improvements are likely to be made.

Graphics:
Visually, the game is a treat, with stunning skyboxes stealing the show. However, the overall aesthetic feels a bit repetitive. Performance: While decent, there are occasional frame rate drops regardless of the hardware used. Using mods (FSR2FSR3) helps alleviate this to some extent, but tweaking remains an issue, especially in certain zones.

Building system:
The building system shows promise, but falls short in execution. Some structures have odd overlaps, and the absence of certain features such as triangular tiles is noticeable. Still, these shortcomings are understandable given the game's early access status, and one can hope for improvements in the future.

Story:
Unfortunately, the game's narrative fails to captivate. Despite being a supposed focal point, the story comes across as lackluster and fails to generate much interest. It's disappointing to see such potential wasted on uninspired storytelling. For those curious, a detailed summary of the story can be found in this YouTube Video (not my video).

Problems:
A major drawback of the game is its connectivity issues. The recurring SSL read timeouts disrupt the gameplay experience and point to underlying network problems that need urgent attention from the developers. Addressing these technical issues would greatly enhance the overall enjoyment of the game for players.

The next concern relates to the forced nature of structure building within the game. This coercion is manifested in the requirement to build certain objects in order to unlock recipes for nearby workstations. The problem lies in the restriction it places on the freedom of base building. Players are forced to build either large or separated spaces to prevent augmentations from inadvertently interfering with adjacent workstations. In addition, the limited number of augmentations allowed and their potential to override nearby stations exacerbates the problem. As a result, players are forced to incorporate decorative items (augmentations) into their builds, disrupting the aesthetic potential of the game. While this mechanic serves as a means of hiding upgrades behind specific recipes, its implementation lacks the flexibility found in other games where upgrades build upon a base model. Instead, players are forced to build large, obtrusive objects such as cannons in close proximity to certain stations, which detracts from the visual appeal of the game.

TLDR:
The game impresses with stunning skyboxes and decent graphics, but suffers from repetitive visuals and inconsistent performance, especially in certain areas. The building system, while promising, has structural quirks and restricts base construction freedom due to the enforced placement of augmentation items. The story fails to engage, and connectivity issues disrupt gameplay. Despite these drawbacks, the game shows potential with ongoing development.

Can't recommend the game at this time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not basing my review because the game is early access. I would recommend this game if you are into and have time to grind. The bugs are minor for an early access game so if this is your type of game you'd love it and the bugs didn't affect my gameplay to a point that it annoyed me or even frustrated me, they have done a good job. The whole lore is very well done. They put a lot of time and effort into bringing the RPG side into this but also not making it overwhelming, the maps are very well made and the atmosphere of the game is really something I like. The building is amazing. I played the game with the minimal external help. I wanted to experience and find out everything myself.

Minor things that are not bugs that bothered me.

Recruited NPCs
When they are standing in your face when they are not in combat (but there are 5 bound running behind them attacking just you), this made me die a few times and feels overall awkward when you are crafting something and when you exit the bench they stand nose to nose with you, I built walls and stood face to face to the walls to avoid this.

Benches
Different tier benches look the same and it's hard to determine which is what. My problem with this is, I didn’t want to dismantle lower tier benches because the resources are only easily obtainable if you grind. You have to have the resources in your inventory and you can queue your crafting. However the way everything is made (using wide range of different resources to create something specific) fits in well with the other survival games I have played,so no complaints from me on the chain of resources.

Bastilles - Puzzles
I love puzzles but sometimes it was just a bit too much puzzling for my liking and no puzzle is the same and there’s no clue to finding out how to solve it, at least not what I could find looking only in the game.You can spend hours trying to finish just one. Don’t get me wrong this was fun the first 5 times but then it gets too much. The puzzles makes complete sense when you take the lore into account and this is something that I like but no I avoid the big puzzles

Gear Score
I believe Gear Score can be explained in more detail. I only understood it close to the end nearing the Watch. I didn’t exactly read every single thing Puck said. I ran around in mustard yellow clothing up until right before I could enter the watch and then discovered I didn’t have too. Cosmetics for me is a big deal in games so I was sad to find out I didn’t have to look weird all the time in yellow mustard clothing with a weird towel on my head.. I miss a concrete attribute and xp system. Don’t get me wrong it’s built in there, it’s just not explained at all. It’s a live and learn situation.

Questing and Building
The fact that you can only have your home in one of the starter realms was sad for me, it’s grinding to find the materials to make a simple house in your home realm (I mean if you want to make it look like something) but then you have to waste any other precious materials on helping NPCs built theirs, this I found overwhelming. I never went past a 6 x 6 wood house. Any resources I kept for essences and helping npcs. The chests could use some work.

Overall feeling.
It’s a pretty game, I love the setting and I love the lore.
