\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiving this game a tentative thumbs up, but it needs a lot of work. I have played a lot of survival crafting and this game shows a lot of promise. I really like the setting and the world. The crafting and base building are good with a lot of potential for improvement.

The bad is the godawful network lag. Combat against the tougher enemies is almost impossible when lagging. Try doing a jumping puzzle when you are not sure where your character is. Half the time it feels like I'm beating my head against a brick wall. In addition there are some bizarre design choices, like having a limited number of upgrade slots for crafting tables, meaning you have to create several versions of the same crafting table to make all of the recipes.

The devs have said they are working on a single player version of this game and that is the only reason I am giving this a positive review. Not playing it again until they do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this game has some amazing things going for it. The worlds are interesting there is tons of space crafting is on the verge of being interesting. I like the monsters and I wish there were more monsters and types of encounters and biomes.

The real problem with this game as it stand in early access is 2 things. 1: This game is chocked full of jumping, mantling, and climbing puzzles. You even get a special tool to climb. The problem is that the jumping, mantling, and climbing all feel and perform horrendously. Whoever the Dev is working on these systems has a loooooong way to go to even have a playable version of the game with these systems in place. They tried to put in some interesting level design that is counter to the tools you have and that just adds to the pain.

2: The gameplay loop is extremely shallow. You get a few different versions of the same items from the same things that are reskinned or a different mob or plant but the same idea and you just repeat this for tiers.

That all being extreme negatives The quests are fun many times and the interaction with a few of the non repetitive npcs is interesting. I hope they expand and give this some more depth because it really is an awesome idea and i feel the surface has only been scratched with what they have done with the worlds. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros
Nightingale has lots of charm with it's gaslamp fantasy theme. The Victorian era styles bring a lot of aesthetic value to the game.
The game stands out with its own uniqueness with the portal system.
The building is pretty basic but the few structure options they do have are really cool.
The game is lore rich.
The tier system in crafting is neat and brings some refreshing complexity to the survival genre.

Cons
Too few furniture options. I built this massive house and realized I have hardly anything to fill it up with.
The augmentation system is ROUGH. Most people don't understand it. There could be lots of changes to this to make it better.
I dislike that I can't place objects on any flat surface. Why do you give us these massive window sills and not let us put some candles or other lighting options on them??
Building system is pretty basic. I don't like that there has to be a wall downstairs directly below an upstairs wall, forcing me to mimic the exact layout from downstairs to upstairs. This feels really limiting. Also... I don't like that we can only rotate things at 90 degree angles.
The roadmap doesn't excite me with content coming up. It leaves me feeling like there isn't a big focus on feature updates. I know they're focused on stability but with end game being so repetitive, I'd like a little assurance that there's more to be working for otherwise the grind feels... pointless.

In summary, I loved playing Nightingale and I am really excited to see it progress. No one should expect perfection from an early access game. I hope the Inflexion team is taking the most valuable feedback and iterating on their product. I've been looking forward to this game for 2 years and I feel like it delivered what I was expecting.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is a beautiful game and has an interesting story line but is woefully lacking.

The servers disconnects at random times, several times during a play session- loading screen to get in server takes several minutes even on good pcs.

This game needs a offline mode badly, not everyone wants to dungeon delve while everyone else afk at the chest.

The crafting system needs love badly. Being able to pull for storage is a must that is not there. Also the craft is clunky, I understand the premise of it and it is a lovely idea. I cannot mix and match the same pieces of lining because they have different stat combo that has no indicator of all the pieces in it. So many materials are being wasted and takes up room in the storage.

Bases needs the number of structures increased, there is no reason for it to be so small when you can only have one base to port to. Multiple portals and buildings are necessary just for farming essence and materials alone. Multiple benches of each kind have to be used as well to produce anything in a timely manner and there are at least 8 types of benches needed for crafting.

Combat mechanics are the same at every event.

Events need to be balanced. The aid events are trash and a waste of time and resources. The Intelligence Bastions take way to much time for the small reward. The rest of the Bastions are fun though.
Grinding for essence to buy gear gets old quick. Four days of grinding for recipes only to spend two more just trying to get the right mats for craft the gear. Sadly, I still was not satisfied with the gear stats or appearance. Worst, you are literally doing the same maps that you have already completed over and over before you hit endgame.

Recruit goes missing! You put gear and weapons on recruits to help aid you and then they go missing with all the equipment you crafted for them.

There is no ticket system to help. Like the aforementioned recruit disappearing with gear, nope you have to make a post on a forum to identify the bug but no one cares that you lost the gear. The only ticket seen so far is the when they released a bug that made players lose their respite realm.

This game needs some tender loving care if it is going to succeed. Maybe in 6 months when they finally have time to solve their server issues and make an offline mode (since they care more right now about ingot exploits they created instead of people being able to connect or stay connected) I will give it another try.\t\t\t\t\t
