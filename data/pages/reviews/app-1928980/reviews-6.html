\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to keep it short and sweet.

I like it. It's an incredibly aesthetically pleasing game. The initial wonder of the whole setting is fantastic and the narrative and environmental storytelling is incredible.

Once that initial wonder and fantasy wears off, the game fails to keep pulling you in. Perhaps there's plans in the future to change this, but higher levels of similar realms just have different monsters. The environments are the same no matter what the lore on the card says about new fantastic gardens, doom and gloom, or mountains of industry existing in it which really was a buzzkill on my expectations.

It's a great game. Lots of clunky or poorly implemented concepts that I'm hoping are placeholders that are being improved upon. It's an Early Access review. Take it with a grain of salt.

PS: Stopped playing the game because my wife ran into an issue where cutting trees crashed her game to the desktop consistently every single time, which is apparently not the first game-breaking but this game has had recently. I hope they patch it out! She was obsessed with the aesthetics.

The game's initial experience feels like that Willy Wonka AI art attraction. It pulls you in with incredible covers and with shock & awe at the very beginning and turns into a huge let-down as you realize that your initial experience is basically all you get with small variations for the entire experience.. At this time because of what feels like clunky or unfinished progression mechanics and bland environmental progression, I can't say the game is worth the full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood God don't try to play this game with a controller! The menus will have you saying WTF enough to fill the swear jar in a NewYork minute. Even with the mouse and keyboard it's broken in many places, c'mon devs I know it's early days but give us something to work with here.
Initially I was blown away with the lush vistas and THE most realistic waves lapping at the shoreline any game has ever attempted, putting a huge smile on my face and conjouring up a colossal eagerness to explore the "proceedurally generated" landscape, however this was mired by the near identical repetition of said generation (are all forest biomes exactly the same just different colours, really?) and the woeful menu system.
Less said about the building and combat the better. That said it's early days so the potential is there for an absolute storming success of a game, not now though... No where near ready for players to embark upon their journeys. Sorry Devs but you dropped the ball on releasing this in it's current form, I'm not touching this until you're finished and I urge others to do the same, don't waste your money on a such a scribbled note of a game, not yet at least.\t\t\t\t\t
