\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would not recommend the game at this point. It is a great premise hindered by the poor incessant combat and lack of interesting variety. The big draw to the game is the proc gen realms which was fun at first but unfortunately, like all proc gen, gets stale and boring quickly. To make it worse, once you summon a world card pair into your world, that iteration is the final iteration for that pair of cards - if you do a forest antiquarian card, the world you enter will be the same world the next time you summon those cards. While it's nice because you can come back and finish what you were doing, you can never click a reset button to go to a fresh one which I always thought was the draw of proc gen - endless adventure. That can make the world feel a little more real but seriously limits the endless sense of adventure I was hoping for coming in.

Because of the procedural generation, I am pretty sure I've seen everything the game has to offer at this point and I have only entered into two of the available major world cards. There are the puzzle areas, where you find the music towers and hit them in the right order; the occupation, where you just kill a wave or two of boring enemies; or agility, which often feels mislabeled. That is pretty much it.

To top it off, a lot of this game revolves around travel and its really boring in this game - the worlds, because they are proc gen, do not have any interesting things going on between points of interest, so you end up just walking everywhere without much care for things between your destinations, and every time you go through a portal - which is very frequently, you have a very long loading screen. Mine is usually 2+ minutes. Now let's say you are playing coop, went into a world with them and now want to portal back to their home world - nope, there is no easy way to do that, you have to warp all the way back to your home world with a long loading screen, and then have them re-invite you with another long loading screen only to not spawn anywhere near them and have to walk all the way back in the uninteresting world.

I was very excited for the traders, but as it turns out, traders in specifc areas sell the same things no matter what world you are in. I was ready to keep exploring new worlds in the endless push to discover all the crafting recipes, but one you go to antiquarian forest, you can buy these certain blueprints and that's that. It just doesn't feel exciting.

The crafting and building really don't feel satisfying to me, the crafting is wildly complex and without decent quality of life inventory improvements it becomes such a slog to do anything.

I generally really enjoy survival games but there is not really anything in this game pushing me to keep playing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter spending 50 hours in the game, I'm not sure if I was having fun or not.

Crafting, this game's focus, I think, is centered around the player running around, grabbing mats from different realms and crafting unneccesary mid-stage items that only exist as ingredients for another recipes. You're gonna spend a lot of time running from chest to chest, as crafting stations (which are plentiful) don't automatically pull items from them, to craft useless ingredients just to make the item you actually want.
On the positive side, you can mix and match different variants of mats within a category to customize the end product to your liking. You can, for example, make an axe that'll deal more damage, be more stamina efficient or add some sort of magical element to it. This part of the crafting mechanic is actually pretty nice. Again, it'd be nice to know the stats of the base item without prepping any mats, but eh.

Exploration is very mid. Enviroments are indeed very good looking, but as the game has you traveling between alternative, randomly generated Realms, early on you're going to start seeing the same things over and over again. As you go to a higher tier Realm, you will mostly meet new enemy types and MIGHT find something interesting if you're lucky. Then again, after about a mid-point, you visit realms just to find a trader, get all the stuff you need to progress and bail.
As much as the game's trying to sell itself on exploration, I never felt so uninterested to see what's new in the 5th almost identical Swamp I'm going to visit. Travelling between Realms is something I'll get back to later.

Progression is weird. Instead of unlocking crafting recipes by picking things up, defeating bosses or making higher thier crafting stations, you have to hop from realm to realm and either buy stuff you want or complete points of interests to unlock a random item from this realm's pool.
Some recipes require augmentations, that you also need to unlock and may or may not connect to multiple crafting stations at once, which results in you moving them around in or outside reach of a crafting station so you can free up a slot so you can place another augmentation you need to craft this one very specific item. Why?

Resource gathering, despite the aforementioned apparent focus on crafting, can be significantly sped up with Minor cards. I never found a need to use those, but they're there if you plan on building something bigger than a bare-minimum box. That is, assuming the building limit will allow you to build what you want.
One thing I have to highlight is the fact that the game features a built-on "wiki" that tells you where you can find every item in the game. You don't have to google where the hell is this ore you're missing. This is a very good feature more games should implement.

Combat is a mixed bag. The feedback from using your weapons/guns and how enemies respond to getting hit is actually very respectable for a survival game. But the engagements usually boil down to running in circles and chipping away the health from enemies that just blob up and run in your general direction. And oh, you'll be stuck with the same three basic guns for the majority of the game. The cool guns you see in the trailers are locked behind end-game grinding. And the basic pistol's particle effects block the middle of the screen as you fire, so I hope you like not seeing what you're aiming at.

UI is a little bit on the "form over function" side. It's gonna take some time to get used to navigating various menus that might not want to cooperate at times, or lack key shortcuts. Apparently the UI is complex enough that it filtered a decent amount of players.

Base building is another mixed bag. On the one hand, you don't need any dedicated building tool, you place walls, foundations and pretty much everything else as a ghost and the game (very conveniently I might add) displays a total amount of mats you need to complete the entire build. You can either put the mats in gradually, or you can grab everything you need and finish the entire build with a single tap of the USE key, which is very nice. It'd be nice if I could pin this requirement to HUD, like I can during crafting tho.
Anyway, once you built the basic stuff like foundations, walls and roofs, there's no snapping to grid for most buildables. The collision during placement is very generous, allowing you to clip stuff into other stuff. For whatever reason, all the stuff you can hang on the walls has limited predefined snapping points.

One more thing worth mentioning is that the game is (for now) always online. I've lost progress a few times because the realm I was in decided to die and kick me back to the main menu. I don't know if the game decided to put my worlds/saves outside of my region, but the entire time I played it felt like I was playing on high ping. Walk into water, the slow down kicked in a second too late. Hit an enemy and they react with a delay. Incredible.
Be prepaired for a lot of waiting and staring at loading screens. As I've mentioned before, portals take about a minute to open and they close themselves after some time, seemingly for no reason. After you go through a portal, you're rewarded with more waiting, as the game does something in the background and this something may take from 20 seconds to multiple minutes, depending on something. All this waiting is probably tied to their server infrastructure prepairing assets or something, hard to tell.
Another "benefit" of the game being always online is The Watch, the game's online social hub that allows players to enter late game dungeons and craft late game gear. These dungeons are designed around having multiple people in them, cooperating to complete challenges and eventually defeat the area's boss, so if you've been playing solo up to that point, have fun relying on other players to progress further at a reasonable pace.

Other noteworthy stuff:
- Performance is all over the place. If you get stable 60 fps in the open world, you're very likely to drop during combat, especially against larger groups of enemies. With my rig that exceeds recommended specs, I almost always dropped to low-mid 40s in these scenarios, no matter what graphics settings I used.
- Hotbar is split into 5 slots for mainhand tools and 5 slots for offhand tools. Why do we need 5 offhand slots? I'd like to know myself. What I do know however, is that these 5 main hand slots, considering the amount of tools and weapons in the game, aren't enough. I had to constantly swap tools on my hotbar, while only two of my off hand slots were used.
- There's no quick stacking to chests or quick restocking from chests. More running from box to box to store the crap you brought and restock on items you need to not die. Even with the chest inventory opened, there's no quick stacking/restocking option.
- Achievements are a bit buggy. I've got an achievement for entering the post-tutorial realm like 30 hrs in and I did not get the achievement for reaching The Watch, despite being there multiple times.
- Maybe I'm just bad at navigating, but a minimap would be nice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an innovative take on the survival genre and a breath of fresh air. I find most survival games use multiplayer as an excuse to avoid building a proper story or providing a solid single player experience. Nightengale has its launch issue with the always online status but this hasn't bothered me one bit so far. Its odd, but I haven't had a single server disconnect or real lag issue. For me at least its just a mildly extended load time. The rest of the game elements more than make up for it and it sounds like they plan to allow offline play soon.

If there is anything annoying about the game so far its the odd progression of crafting. It feels like the game is a little too well balanced, almost on rails. It would be nice to be able to clever your way past some of the missions to get items early. I was able to jump passed one gate to get herbrarium access early but then still needed to follow the proper core mission progression to unlock the table to build the recipe I had. At the same time, the progression can be wonky. I could build a revolver pistol before I could build an iron axe or hammer.

No other complaints, its good, get it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs this game worth playing? Yes. There is a lot here to do even in early access. The environments look great. The story is decent and gives you a reason to keep hopping into other realms. I think the Realm Card system is very cool.

Here are the two things I would fix in the game.

The crafting is robust, but TOO robust. First you have to farm this, then you make this one thing, then you use that to make the thing you actually need. It's all over the crafting system like this. They need to cut out some of the middle stuff. And they need to make crafting faster if they aren't going to cut out the middle stuff. Example: Farm ore, smelt ore into ingots, make two metal plates, then use those to make a metal point. Way too much and way too much time.

Essence conversion. They need to either have a crafting station or a vendor you can use to convert essence from regular to T1 or reverse if needed. Then at least you don't have to go back into a different realm to farm certain essence.

For early access, I have had minimal issues. I think maybe a total of 5 disconnects out of game to title screen. As I said, there is a lot to do and I think they've done a nice job putting enough in here for early access to make it worth playing. A lot of building options as well. If they fix the crafting system, it will be even better.\t\t\t\t\t
