\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLongtime open-world/survival/crafter gamer here. I'm 116 hours into this game, and I am still as engaged as I was the first few hours of playing. This is a refreshingly different survival game compared to any other I've played (I've played Valheim, 7Days, Ark, Icarus, Enshrouded, Rust, No Man's Sky, Fallout, Conan, and I think I'm forgetting a few still). Biggest issues I see right now are with some quality of life pieces like fast traveling within realms (not the abeyance realm) and scaling difficulty. The game is in early access still so not a surprise, but most of the bosses are only able to be defeated via exploits rather than real strategy (at least as far as I can tell). It's a beautiful game, visually as well, and I really appreciate the references to famous scientists/historical figures as well. There is enough typical genre familiarity at the start mixed with some pretty unique mechanics that can take a minute to understand but once you do, you'll find yourself hopping from realm to realm in no time. It's definitely worth buying and playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is different... yet familiar. All of the expected survival crafting mechanics are present, albeit they might seem a bit complex to the uninitiated. The complexity of the crafting gives rise to a very deep system of augmentations and traits you can impart to crafted items.

But most importantly are the world(s), this is where Nightingale really makes it's mark on the genre. Using a card / portal system you can expect a new variant of a desired world every time you venture forth. Not entirely random, but offering enough variation to motivate exploration.

TLDR: A slow, sometimes confusing burn, but ultimately worth the time investment.\t\t\t\t\t
