\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is a grindy, fun and visually pleasing game.

Pros:
-Value (80 hr game for 30$)
-Graphics aren't too bad (However I don't think they utilised Nanite much.)
-The music/soundtrack corresponds and give s a good atmosphere.
-I didn't experience any game breaking bugs.

Cons:
-Grindy, very repetitive
-The farming doesn't work
-The higher level realms aren't as polished and you'll always have to go back to lower level realms to get mats.
-Your base structure have no health points, so if you accidentally hit your mouse button, there goes you foundation...


The game has potential and is decent, I would recommend it at this price point, but don't expect the best game or a long lasting game. There is a somewhat "multiplayer" called The Watch. It's basically the Nexus from No Man's Sky. There's only 3 dungeons out there to do with other players. It wasn't bad, but the end was a little sour. There's a lot of simple fixes that the developers should of thought of imo.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game does nothing...special. Its crafting survival with ARPG elements. Let me explain a bit better.

In your Typical ARPG you have a top down, birds eye view with usually a fixed camera angle. You run around killing hordes of enemies that drop gear and maybe some crafting materials to modify or RNG craft your own stuff for a chance at something awesome.

Nightingale takes away the gear drop and replaces it with material drops. You chop down trees, hit mining nodes, murder endless creatures that while not charging at you for blood, exist and thus need 'acquiring' (read as mindless slaughter) for your own material benefit. It takes away a lot of the RNG element by restricting what gear you get to 95% of it being crafted by you with materials you gather and refine that have set attribute boosts you desire.

But there is a catch. When you are crafting your armor or weapons, they have set attributes they can get. Your hat is a damage attribute holder, your coat is resistances, your back(pack) is your maximum weight, your shirt/pants are regen, boots and gloves also have damage attributes and boots have movement speed. So its not like you can min/max excessively when your stats are spread out and limited in places you can put them.

Now, lets talk about the In-House work benches a little. This is where you craft things, cooking, weapons, armor, materials for these things, Realm Cards (how to make a portal to teleport to places for material harvesting) and potions. One crafting bench can get X amount of Augments. X changes based on the tier of bench it is, and im not at endgame with the best benches (I think?) so I cannot tell you what it maxes out at. What I will tell you is you will be overrun with Augment benches. As it stands, I have enough Augment benches just for the Work Bench itself that I could probably cap out 3-4 work benches with unique Augments. Now keep in mind you have an item limit of 350 (benches, augments, chests, decor, whatever) that you can place. My Augment tab in my crafting menu has..45? I want to say, dont feel like logging in to check atm, and I still have more to collect. Now for those that played Valheim, think about the workbenches and how you got upgrades for em, thats pretty much what Augments are, but instead of just getting 1 Upgrade for half a tier or so, you need about 10 and they have to be within a limited range of the bench to effect it, just like in Valheim.

Combat- Its basic. You have a dodge or a block based on what weapon is in your hands. 1h weapons tend to dodge, 2h block. There are 1-2 weak points on enemies, Head is like 100% there and Heart, but sometimes the Heart is replaced by another thing. You have melee weapons, firearms and magick skills you can use. Magick come in the flavor of Enchantments you place on your MELEE weapons/tools.

Let me backtrack a bit here for the sake of argument. Some might say that most Crafting Survivals are kinda similar to an ARPG. You run around, get loot and materials, craft things, upgrade things, rinse and repeat in a new area after upgrading. The problem I have with Nightingale's way of doing it is its very very "Hey, you could go play an ARPG and get almost the same feeling!" The method they chose to introduce the meta grind is you make Realm Cards (or find em) then you place them in a Portal generator, you can set the difficulty easy/medium/hard/extreme (Power Creep rules). The higher the Realm Difficulty (represented in a numeric value at the top of the Realm Card Selection screen) the higher the tier of materials you can get from it. The maps are randomly generated with some preset Points of Interest hand crafted and scattered about. Thats it. Diablo, Path of Exile, Last Epoch and other ARPGs have been using that as a way to recycle their maps for a decade or longer. Okay okay yes you can even add MORE modifiers with Minor Realm Cards at a Realmic Transmutor while in a realm, oh wait, dont the three examples ALSO let you do things that modify the difficulty for increased reward besides just tankier mobs, squishier player? Yeah, pretty sure most of them do, maybe not Diablo, but Blizzard only gets one lightning in a bottle per franchise anyways and D2 was it for Diablo.

Anyways if you like looking at the same three biomes (probably more to come later) of Desert, Swamp, and Forest with maybe a slight color palette shift and spongier mobs, have at it, this game might just be for you!

Either way, I played almost 90hrs, didnt get into T3 quality gear (used T3 materials to make some T2 upgraded gear, you'll understand if you play) and started on the endgame treadmill for Purple Essence (In T0, the recipe purchases were like 10-20 essence TOPS, T1 was like 15 essence, T2 was like 25 essence but in T3 the recipe prices jump to a minimum of like 50, average 100, bundles of recipes for like 1,300). The jump in prices just turned me off. This game is completely single player playable, I only ever grouped up in the 'Apex Vault Realm' or w/e it was called where you have to kill some boss and can get some good crafting material from it. Its also probably the best Purple Essence Farm as long as you get quick groups (people actively trying to finish objectives to get to the boss and kill it). Each stage nets you like 15-30 Essence, completion nets you another like 100~ Essence as well as those nice crafting materials. Also, dont worry about trying to loot, its FFA loot rules, not shared or individualized loot but its nothing super special from the random mobs.\t\t\t\t\t

However, I've never encountered a game that is so incredibly adamant that I don't play it. I have a high-end rig, most games are played breezy on Ultra. I have a dedicated connection and yet, the game won't connect to the realm with any sort of consistency. Multiple crashes back to the lobby are in store for me any time I try to log in. This is an EA game, I'm aware, so I tried to be patient with it because I've been following it for close to a year. Multiple disconnects wouldn't be a big issue if that was the only thing: the game encounters game breaking bugs immediately following a disconnect, such as the escape key no longer functioning to exit menus. So, if you encounter a disconnect, it might as well drop you onto the desktop because the bugs require a full reset to fix.

That's one issue on top of others, the primary ones being that all of the cool stuff in the trailers doesn't exist until you invest a ton of hours gritting through the bugs, disconnects and low-content walking simulator that happens. I'm 44 hours in and because the crafting is a convoluted mess of 15 crafting benches with 15 upgrade benches, my character looks like a 1800's vagabond that lives in a stone prison by the river that has, I kid you not, a marble shooting crossbow and an upgraded stone knife to protect myself. 44 hours in and I've managed to do nothing cool, really.

And this is on top of the absence of commentary from the devs for anything reasonable as an update. The last one was them adding servers to South America.

Anyway, I'm gonna circle back on this one. Twice now I've been burned by cool ideas and premise from Bioware devs. It sucks, I'd love to thumbs up this one, but Enshrouded is a much better game in every way that this hot mess isn't, despite its own issues.\t\t\t\t\t
