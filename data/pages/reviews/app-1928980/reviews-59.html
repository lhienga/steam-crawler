\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been so excited for this game for almost a year, since I first saw the ads for it, and I have not been disappointed. Coming into it in early access and expecting it to actually BE in an unfinished state, is important.

It's early access so there's still some bugs with enemies and other things, and it does need to be refined, but honestly I can't stop playing, and even those bugs have yet to cause me issues with progressing in the game. As far as leveling, it's just grindy enough to keep you having to work a little to keep progressing, while also not being ungodly tedious. And it's a very interesting and unique storyline as well. NPCs are a small bit annoying sometimes, the AI for them isn't the best, for the recruits, but their use for carrying stuff that would be far too heavy for you to carry yourself makes it worth it. I've come back with far more ores and stone and other materials than I would have ever been able to bring back without them. The building options early on too actually look semi nice, and the system for it isn't the worst, though it's a tiny bit awkward at first. As stated, it is early access so it will likely be refined further as the game continues updating.

Now. One thing I will say is the third person view, the camera movement is.. laggy and janky and weird. It is still experimental, apparently, but I do hope they manage to fix that, because I have friends and my own partner has issues with first person views in games making them sick because of the camera movement, and in the current state, the third person camera is not great. But I think this game 100% needs it and I hope they refine it further soon.

All things said, as someone who plays survival games almost exclusively, I LOVE this game and I am so excited to see how it continues to unfold.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen you look at the description of the word shame, you would find this game. Its somewhat good looking, the story hypes you up at the start but then it is as if the developers abandoned all hope for the game. Aside from shooting some trailers, I doubt that the developers played this game at all. Sure, forge your own path but also take inspiration from some other survival/sandbox games out there which have pioneered the genre into what it has to become. Spending 100 years going through chests, especially when some items don't even have an icon yet is beyond annoying. Some enchantments don't work. Mostly because the game is online only and you play on private servers for some reason like its a brown stained metaverse.

Either the game designer was using recreational goodies while coming up with the systems of this game or he/she is a secret genious whose talents are better suited towards making puzzles that piss people off intentionally. The crafting bench system is beyond annoying, with hundreds of augments that you have to place down but guess what? Some of them do the same thing. Now go find out in the annoying guide book. A gear score system was implemented like this is some kind of looter shooter. You go to higher and higher realms after beating certain challenges. God I can't wait to hit 999 gearscore to beat Cthulhu!

The bugs are plenty. Loading times when entering realms are abysmal. My game is on an SSD. The problem is that the realms are servers. So the game tries to create a private server each time you enter a realm. I have had many crashes. The graphic settings game me bunch of trouble. But the most important bugs of all were about TREES! What the hell is wrong with the tree mechanics? Have you tested the bloody game at all? The tree cutting animations suck and are not satisfying. The trees fly around and even disappear sometimes into the ground. They almost allways fall onto you and hurt you. There is a monster that makes natural resources respawn but guess what? The monster can also fell trees. So if you build in a forest you are doomed! Bushes and trees will respawn inside your base and your NPC companion or the respawn monster will demolish the whole base.

Speaking of NPC companions, what the hell! You have a companion that you can recruit and I kid you not, they are the most annoying and empty looking companions that I have ever seen in a game. Their only purpose is that they can carry your burdens like good little pack mules.

See? The game was made by some mad man to piss people or troll them. I'm sure of it. Still I am willing to let them cook for a while. Then I might change my review. Good luck devs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tbuilding is great. Crafting is SUPER deep (to the point of borderline over complication tbh) HOWEVER the AI of your follower has completely destroyed this game for me in it's current state. The lack of a whistle or something to summon them to you in order to give/take things from them makes banking materials from a hunting run SOO much more frustrating - IF you can even get your materials back to the base.

I spent 2 hours running around collecting higher tier materials in the forest after finally unlocking it. It's a struggle, the bears are hitting like a truck as I'm admittedly under levelled, but I want the boost to my gear score from the better mats. I know for a fact that I had collected over 300 of the new wood and passed it to my guy, only for him to randomly walk up to every damn fire pit or smelter randomly placed around the map and refill them with my precious wood. And when you break the crafting station with fuel in it you only get half back, and if I take it out he just replaces it, so you need to wait for idiotstick to come close to you, take all its wood, break the damn station, THEN transfer everything back to them 2 minutes later when they wander back near you since you're soo weighed down you can't move. This is a complete joke, but at least there's a workaround. Until I get into a fight with a random bear, and while I'm skinning it my idiot runs over to some NPC I didn't see around a rock and dump like 200 of my logs into their blueprint that have sitting there. ANNNDDD you can't pull those back out. AT BEST you can complete it, break it, and get some of your mats back but not all. Absolutely unacceptable for your follower to hand over 2 hours of work collecting materials to progress your game. Until they update this I'm going back to older survival games. Which is tragic because I was really enjoying this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is... alright at best.

Its pretty, neat aesthetic with a not bad combat system... however..
The crafting system is an absolute chore.
-They have several tiers of 1 item, so based on what tier of item you use, changes the outcome of the thing you are crafting.. So you think this would be good right? But eventually it turns everything into a grinding chore.. I don't have a problem with grinding, I've played my share of MMORPGs and Survival games.. but its just too much. With so many tiers of one item and inventory space is a joke at best even for chests.

Unlocking new recipes is practically RNG based
They want you to reroll worlds and explore them again, but this just creates a lazy game. Where instead of crafting legitimate worlds to explore and find loot ( which btw the looting system is a joke too ). You just reroll a world and hope it gives you what you want.

I spent most the game just using an Wood Chopping axe because either my RNG is bad, or there was just nothing really better.

The building is decent. But not as good as Conan, Valheim, or Enshrouded. Its slightly better than Arks.
You also play on their servers, so if they go down, it all goes down. instead of playing on your own servers which would be better with even maybe shared progress.

The game honestly at the end of the day is a disappointment.
