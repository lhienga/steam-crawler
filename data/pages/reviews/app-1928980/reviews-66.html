\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn its current state, I would not recommend this game.

The ideas are all there for it to be a solid craft/survival game, but their execution is all over the place.

My biggest gripe so far is its guidance. I don't need my hand held when playing games, but telling me to just "Do this" and have absolutely no guidance past that is just poor design. Im a newcomer to this game and its world, I have no idea what I can and cant do yet, "Do this" is just a stupid way to go about it. When my patience finally broke I was already past the refund window.

There is also a reliance on Gear score, which Im not too crazy about either. You literally cannot progress the game further without crafting higher tier items because it will actually block you off entirely from entering the required dungeons needed to get more world cards/recipes and such if your level isnt high enough. It also wont tell you that either, just a magic door yeeting you backwards the moment you touch it with no reason as to why. For that, just have a normal leveling system.

Its also always online, which i rarely take issue with like others do. However, whenever you start up the game it takes between 3-5 minutes for the servers to get ready and load into the game. This also applies when you realm travel, and with other realms being a core part of the game its crazy I can go heat up a meal as I wait. They've mentioned creating an offline mode, but who knows when that will happen.

This should have waited another 6 months to a year before dropping into early access. The majority of its current issues arent related to player preference, they're just incomplete and half baked.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game. To discuss the negatives first: in my opinion the animations could be better NPC AI is clunky, and im looking forward to the planned offline mode as the always online is frustrating (although ive only been kicked for being inactive twice. and i WAS inactive both times.)

There are some unfinished bits to the game like item icons and character creation choices, but they will be added in future patches and this is to be expected for an Early Access game.

On a positive note, the game is really fun to play. It brings a nice mix of new ideas and has kept some well proven old favourites as well. i like the more creative and complex crafting mechanic and i find the game world and the items in it beautiful and nicely detailed.

In short, its a game that i currently love playing and i am excited to see what polish and new ideas the developers can share with us in the future.

I do think you get a lot of game for your $45 AUD.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good: I love the theme, and it's differentiation from the rest of the genera. The crafting system is deep and complex (and needs some more explaining and hand holding from the developer...). The idea of portals to adjustable realms which can be private or friends only, versus the public realms is great too. Graphically it's beautiful. Sound and music supports the theme and feeling.

The bad: Honestly, I'm at my wit's end because the bad really overshadows any fun I could have.
The "network error" and "disconnect due to inactivity" disconnections are extremely frustrating. I'll be crafting and get "disconnected due to inactivity" or just be playing in a vault or dungeon and get "network error". This happens multiple times a day and, due to extensive login time (an average of 5 - 8 minutes to go from launching the game to being playable. I have a 1 GB fiber connection, which I've tested and have no network issues with, so I know this isn't on my side.
My respite home keeps getting destroyed by random enemies. This is, quite frankly, the main reason I will not be playing Nightingale anymore. I spent the majority of my 147 hours seeking out specific materials to build the large house, and all the "excellent" quality work benches (crafting), and all the interior decorations. Many of the resources required multiple different locations of searching to find obscure resources. While crafting in one part of my respite house, on at least 6 different occasions, enemy NPCs will attack the other side of the house and destroy a foundation piece (or many pieces). This causes all the walls, placed objects (storage, crafting benches, etc) to be destroyed. The house is a 3 story house, so that's foundation, 3-6 walls, 2 floors, all the roof parts, crafting benches, storage and anything else to be lost. In most cases the resources did not drop off the destroyed parts, nor from the storage chests. Occasionally it did, but due to the nature of the materials, I couldn't afford to rebuild and was forced to spend hours out grinding yet again instead of enjoying the game's beautiful world.
NPC Followers don't act intelligently. You can give them tools like an axe, pickaxe, or battle hammer and they are supposed to use the tool for that job. However, most of the time they will just stand there doing nothing, or will use the tool (axe to chop a tree) then walk away from the resource instead of picking it up. In combat, if they actually engage the enemy instead of walking away, they can be quite useful and do a good job keeping an engaged enemy down. But they really, REALLY need to have a higher aggravation rating then the player, to keep mobs on them. Otherwise, the player is forced to drag enemies onto the follower to get the follower to actually hit them. Not fun.
Repairs are WAY too costly. You are expected to use the same resource (essence) to repair your equipment as you are to craft enchantments, upgrade equipment, buy recipes, etc... This makes the player have to choose what to prioritize. Repairs really should be essence OR materials used to craft the item (wood pole for axe handle, for example). It should be simple and easy and not something the player has to grind for or decide if they should spend on a new recipe from a ticket merchant.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been watching this game for almost 2 years, and I was pretty let down. I really hope they can pull it back to a good place. It's a loop like many people have said. Grind to get gear to run something higher. So you can do it again. Once you get to the watch, which seems to be end game at this point, you have to run 5 or 6 vault runs, to unlock one thing. That all they let you do, is run those same vaults over again.
Building was a nice change from say Conan. Prebuild everything so can gather and help friends. But if you have a follower with an axe, they will cut down a tree and smash it to the ground. The realms all have the same 'quests'. Kill the bad guys, click the totems in order, and find the hidden lines.
The crafting is bloated. There are like 15 types, and they all do the same thing, but you can't mix them. There are just so many things. And finding them can be a pain if you HAVE to have one. Upgrading items is boring, you have to have t1, t2, and t3 essences to upgrade. So if you don't have tier 1, you have to go back to the low-level realms to farm it, which feels like a waste of time. To unlock things, it seems to be random. Or you have to farm essence to take to a trader. Crafting sometimes feels like a time waster. 20 minutes to make ingots. I ran my 5 or 6 vaults, and unlocked t3 clothes and sent and hour making them, just for them to be 10 points higher than my old gear...
There are a few bugs, I've had issues where every time I tried to go to home realm I'd get kicked offline and when I logged back in, I was in the same place. I often load into home realm and fall past the mesh and just free fall for 30 or 40 seconds. Some say "but its EA" but these
In short, After getting to what is now the end game, there is nothing here to make me wanna play...\t\t\t\t\t

+ Graphics
+ Crafting system (selec carefully the materials to fabricate items)
+ Cool buildings
+ Create random worlds
+ Nice interface

- Loooots of bugs
- You must be connected to the internet
- THE FOLLOWER IS ANNOYING AS F... AND AN AS...LE (using very high tier item like wood to ligh up the furnace and etc... its like taking care of a baby who is constantly drawing on the walls). Constantly teleporting everywhere which makes very dificult to depend on it.
- Basic mechanics are no implemented (like staking manually the same item in the same item slot)
- Even though the game creates worlds randomly they are empty with the same challenges all the time. The only thing that changes is the seller guy, its the only reason to go to all of them to get all the recipies.
- Multiplayer is annoying and makes it even unnecessary and unpealing, loot is everyone up for grabs which is annoying when you have to farm essences. Also the dungeon where nothing is share and everything is "fastest grabs everything". They need to revamp group loot and maybe make it that everyone can grab everything.
- Farming Essences is annoying since you have to farm lower ones too, can't there be an Essence trader? 10 G1 = 1 G2 & 10 G2 = 1 G3? Something like this and vice versa.
- House building is very bugged and for some reason it doesn't let you build anymore (even though it says you still haven't reach the limit)
- Dungeons are just the same challenges that you would find "outside" but now they are inside... just the last dungeons have bosses in the end,,, Could be more awesome
- Combat needs to be revamped, it sucks. There is no juice to it. Its cool to have spells but it is very boring.
- You can loose items very easily (by bugs)
- End game is farming the lasts 3 dungeons over and over again.

This game needs a lot of work but could possibly become an amazing game. Work on the bugs, offline mode, better server, better follower, rework crafting and home crafting, create maps with more life and variation with better and more types of challenges, rework dungeons and loot system, rework "money system", make multiplayer more appealing because right now there is no value for it.

Thumbs down until improvement, but I believe it will become an amazing game if done properly.\t\t\t\t\t
