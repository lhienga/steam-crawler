I love that I get to go into realms and kick some butt... go go go... and then can go back and relax in my home. The progression is designed so well. You need to develop all skills to move forward...like cooking and metalwork and everything.

You can tell its in early release as there are some issues. The realms get a little repetitive. They need more realm types. I've had about 5 crashes in the hours shown, which isn't terrible. Skyrim still crashes on me the odd time and I'm barely at the minimum for specs for this game. The fact that you get bombarded by Bound when its night is lame. Would like to move around at night without that issue. Also the treasure returns need some balancing. Sometimes you think you've done something amazing and you get crap.

Overall, I would recommend this game. If you just want action it might not be for you, but if you want a thoughtful, beautifully rendered, sometimes relaxing, sometimes frustrating, go of a game, this is for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomething is missing to keep the player engaged and wanting to play more... The game is wonderfully beautiful and shows off some of the UE5 features well. Puck, while voice acted well, seems out of place and doesn't lend to an organic experience. Combat is rough. I like that the player can headshot enemies even with melee weapons and that different types of weapons are better suited for certain enemies. It is shallow as that's the depth i experienced. That being said, pacing may be an issue if the player enjoys building and decorating their base. I looted ammo, but have not made it to firearms yet in nearly 38 hours of gameplay. There is also so many items and quality types that storage is a pain to manage. QOL additions like hot dropping and benches pulling crafting materials from surrounding chests similar to games like grounded is a smart idea here, but i can see how pulling from chest may cause issues when considering material quality. I don't particularly like item level gated content as a gamer who generally likes their games to be difficult. That should be decided by the player based on how well they can handle the content before they decide they need better equipment before tackling it again. I do like the portal system for sharing your world with friends and i generally don't mind online gaming in 2024 considering nearly everyone that plays on a computer also has a internet connection and no one is buying physical copies of games from steam. Controller support is nice to have. It does need more development as its hard to navigate dialog and menus sometimes. I think with more time this game can be good. I wouldn't recommend as it is now though because it's just not very engaging.

TLDR
- Beautiful
- Boring combat
- Pacing needs looked at
- Needs QOL for storage badly
- Not engaging
- Partial controller support
- Offline sharing of your world with friends

Verdict - Wait to pick this game up unless you just want to support until it has more time to develop or the player may be off put by its current state and not want to come back to it.
