\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off as an early access game this is not horrible, but would I recommend you purchase it? I would say if you enjoy grinding for an extremely long time and can see past taking forever to move from the absolute basic wardrobe initially available then you will find that this game can be enjoyable and even slightly addictive while your hunting for progress. But there are at least 3 things they need to fix/implement,

1. They need to implement an essence conversion system. period. I'm sure some would say but that will break the game yadda yadda, no it wont there are ways to do it without breaking anything, i.e. 1000 to 1 up ratio or not allowing lower to be converted to higher depending on gameplay difficulty.

2. The game looks Beautiful, please make some good looking low tier outfits, because players are already sick of running around in the same rags for 10+ hours.

3. Allow buildings to be upgraded without having the need to tear them down first.

Outside of these issues there are bugs which as an early access they are doing a great job working on, and several other Q.O.L. upgrades they could implement {like leaving a build-able beacon in a realm so you can come back when you finish a quest that your simply not able to complete in said realm.} but I'm sure they will figure it out as the game progresses towards full release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that I have about 20ish hours into the game I think thats enough time to talk about some of the things that i like and dont like.

Pros --
The crafting system is pretty nice, i am loving having alot of benches and tons of different things to create and farm for i have been BUSY every time i log in
LOTS AND LOTS to do ! there are so many different worlds, biomes, modifiers, and so forth.
I like how to get new crafting benches and repair refining stuff you HAVE to visit new zones
Quest system is functional and makes sense with the lore and story. im digging it
but puck is a douche nozzle

Cons--
The survivors are pointless except for in fights ill explain. They might farm but they dont pick up anything they farm that system is broken. I have to run behind this heffer nonstop and pick up what shes farming.
If you had your survivor an axe near your base they will cut down trees that will surely land on your structure and BREAK the damn thing.
I had to take away access from the storage (might be why they dont pick up anything) BECAUSE this dumb chick keeps on putting high end matts into the damn fire as fuel. i had over 50 carved wood which is a longish grind to get and what does she do puts it in the fire thank god i noticed. The NPC survivors need a full look by the DEVs.

Climbing with the climbing picks SUCKS i have spent literal hours trying to use these things and i cant climb up a 5 foot wall without running out of stamina in 3 seconds. This needs a rework.

Now performance this is my biggest problem with this game. It is not optimized AT ALL. I have a 13900k, lowish latency DDr5 6400mhz memory, gen 5 SSD 10,000 read/write and a 4090, I have to put everything on balanced at 4k with DLSS on performance to get more than 70 FPS. and even then its still stuttering nonstop. No other game i have plays like this, first game ive played since the 4090 came out that is this bad with performance. They HAVE to optimize the game better. Now when i put everything on ultra quality the highest setting i get around 50-60 frames but fights are impossible i lag wayy to much and stutter.

Overall I am really loving the game i hope they can fix the few things that i am not a fan of. It would be real nice to have a better survivor intelligence thats for sure.

Finally YES I KNOW ITS EARLY ACCESS.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale provides something a great majority of survival games don't: hope. There is a reason to survive. There are people in the world all moving toward a common goal. Survival games that do not have any direction or reason (hope) for survival totally miss the mark. Nightingale gives players a reason to survive, a path to a better future; without this, there's no reason to survive. The concept of Nightingale is refreshing in a world of pointless and hopeless survival games.

I am almost 60 hours into the game and feel like I have just finished the tutorial. The placement of augmentations is a little wonky, as items need to be close to other items for them to work properly. Forced item placement messes up how I want to build my base. The game was somewhat confusing for the first 20 or 30 hours, but I feel like it was kind of a huge tutorial on how to play the game. I wish the Nightingale could be played offline; I have no interest at all in playing the game with other people. Item upgrades are a little confusing at first. You can upgrade items with multiple augmentations, but I still have no idea why some augmentations will only fit on certain items and not others.

Overall, the game is amazing. It's refreshing that some parts of it are confusing. I get to figure things out on my own, and it's not cookie-cutter. I guess if you are the type of person who watches other people play games on YouTube, some of the game concepts would be easier to figure out. I am extremely happy with the amount of content for an early-access game. I can see the early-access part of the game having hundreds of hours of gameplay, and lack of content is usually the death of early-access games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is a game full of potential. I've logged a little over 100 hours at this point, which is some of the most time I've put into a game in nearly a decade.

Do I run into some minor/major bugs and get frustrated by janky mechanics? Sure. Do I get frustrated by the inconsistent server lag? Absolutely. But I bought the game fully knowing that the title was an early release and was fully prepared for all the downsides that come with early release titles.

That being said, this game was well worth the $30 I paid for it.

Combat is a cross-breed between Skyrim and Bioshock. Landing hits on enemies feels impactful and satisying. The various melee and ranged weapons, with a magic system nestled somewhere between Skyrims's spells/enchantments and Bioshocks Plasmids, helps give variety and cater to different combat styles.

Visuals are gorgeous. With great lighting, shadows, skyboxes, water, everything. The graphics, art style, and landscapes are never a letdown when traversing across the various realms. Sometimes even being pretty enough to get me to stop and soak it all in.

Survival mechanics are good. Perhaps one of the areas of the game that needs the most tweaking, polishing, and ironing out. I personally enjoy the depth, where the stats and quality of the objects being crafted are dependent on the materials you craft it with. Base building is good, with a blueprint system that lets you layout the building before you collect and expend materials. Some of the of these mechanics could benefit from being improved upon more and could use maybe a bit more clarity.

Story and related mechanics are fun. The world building and lore is immersive and keeps things interesting. While the portal system that uses biome cards, and the minor realm cards that alter existing realms to varying extent helps keep the game fresh and separate it from other survival games. While the Apex and Legendary creature hunts are a fun addition, eliciting a shadow of a comparison to Monster Hunter-type games.

All-in-all, it's a fun early access game with plenty to keep me occupied and entertained either by myself, or playing co-op with friends.

My biggest takeaway is that the foundation of this game is real solid, The existing story, framework, and mechanics hold incredible potential for some real spectacular updates and additions. The game is fun enough as-is to keep me drawn-in, even in early access. But the potential for how this game could build upon an already solid base is what keeps me excited about it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderrated game. I've played many survival crafts in my time and this one is the only one in recent time I've felt goes the extra mile to distinguish itself from others.

Negative reviews tend to lean toward either always online requirement issues or lack of polish or content.

Always online when I want to play solo is an odd thing to madate, but in terms of polish and content, this game is ahead of any early access launch I've played. It actually has a bit of a story to follow, although many characters not being voiced is a small letdown, I enjoyed the amount of care that went into fleshing out a compelling world. That is a rarity in the survival genre. You can tell the devs want to create a world that feels unique and I'd say they've succeeded.

The crafting system is also my favorite of any game. It is indeed complex and can be overwhelming, but I'd rather have that than a shallow system that feels unrewarding.

I've completed the majority of what the game has to offer currently at around 65 hours, but plan to stick around much longer to build in my home realm and then start a second character and do it all again.

The only thing I want from this game is more. More biomes, more vaults for end game, more weapons and armor to unlock, more story content.

The only real complaints I have is server related crashing. Some days it feels common, other days it never happens.

Can't wait to see what the future looks like for this game. My only hope is that this one can see the same kind of future content releases as a game like Icarus. I want to see this game supported for years to come and it'll continue to be my go-to survival crafting game.
