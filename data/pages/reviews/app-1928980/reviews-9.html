Your work is subject to roll-backs and many other issues which seasoned survival builders can often mitigate by managing their own saves and hosting their own servers. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonest review because there seems to be a lot of mixed stuff on here, short answer: This is a good fun game.

Long answer: With nearly 90 hours gametime in this so far playing with my boyfriend we genuinely are having a good time with this exploring and building and stuff. We've played a lot of survival games in the past and out of all of them this is one of the only few that stand out.

Its theme and setting is awesome, victorian steampunk era delving through the infinite faewild realms, the concepts are there and the execution is good, not fantastic or anything but still commendable.
This game is still early access but compared to many other games that release in early access (or full releases in recent times) it still feels like a full game that is in the stage of ironing out bugs and implementations. There are rough spots but the devs have good credibility with me that they will be fixed in the future.

Music, atmosphere and visuals are all pretty good and immersive, the gameplay has some kinks to work out but its passable for me. The survival aspect is easier than other games which helps me enjoy it more as a casual gamer, it can be challenging but honestly I enjoy it more not being difficult with making you die a hundred times.

There are some aspects I find lacking however, being set inside the faewilds I would expect more strangeness and unique setting but so far the main difference between realms is different colour swatches for plants and animals and a rotation of types of ruins based on what realm is activated.

The crafting system is a bit too complex to get my head around as every material gives different stat bonuses and there's little to figure out what does what, theres also different colour swatches for outfits based on what materials you use with zero preview or indication what it will be which I find annoying. Additionally an annoying aspect of the game is the augmentation system which requires certain items of furniture to be placed in proximity of workbenches in order to craft certain things, this creates cluttered areas quickly which gets complicated by the small build limits and the fact that theres a maximum number that you can have.

All in all this game has rough edges but it doesn't impact the game enough for me not to recommend it, I do wish for several things to be fixed and more content added but it is only a month after than release and the devs have given a schedule for planned updates so I'm fine to give it time.
I'm still playing and still having fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not a terrible game. Floating around like Mary Poppins with a shotgun is wonderful. The realm card system has some intrinsic sense of whimsy to it. Want to turn the fall damage off? Sure! Want to make it night all the time? Go for it. It's actually rad.

However, the complaints you may have seen in the other negative views are absolutely true. This game is extremely fiddly and not in a good way. Its fiddly in a way that feels like the devs aren't respecting your time. The micromanagement is not at all rewarding and you're just grinding early game mats to make early game parts to make mid game parts to make end game gear.

It is actually exhausting.

The UX experience is pretty bad with the item stacking issues, the weird menus, and the 18 million tables and augmentations that each need to be cross referenced with pages and pages of other mats (see other issue of mat grinding).

Oh and if you're planning on building a pretty house? Do that again each time you want to move your house up a tech tree.
That said the tools they DO have for building a base fail in comparison to games like Valheim and Enshrouded or even games as old as 7 Days to Die. The snap points feel weird, digging around for basic build pieces in the early tech trees is a chore, and the results feel sub par even after watching youtubers do their thing with the tools currently available.

This game has a ton of potential, but for right now? Pass. Not wait for game pass, just pass.\t\t\t\t\t
