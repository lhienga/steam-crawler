\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: After twice as many hours, my criticism stands, but I'm still playing it. I would recommend with the caveats below. I changed to a like because I'm excited about this one. Base building has been a blast and the materials aren't horrible to get once you start throwing minor realm cards around.

Solid foundation here-- I can totally see the potential for this to be incredible. The card system helps you fine tune your experience to what you're looking for moment to moment. The setting is unique and interesting and points of interest on the map are very cool to see jutting out in the distance.

Unfortunately there are just some baffling decisions here:

- The hotbar is wildly inconvenient and split between "two hands" in a way that makes me feel like it would be easier to carry all my tools with my actual two hands while playing the game.

- Resource drops don't seem balanced for solo play with no option to make them so.

- Furniture placement is often off kilter and can be impossible to line up with your walls. Imagine spending a Saturday collecting for and building a big mansion only to discover none of your furniture can be rotated to an angle that actually lines up with your walls.

- Realms and their explanations can be obtuse. You'll get it after awhile, but just build in your Abeyance realm and forget the rest unless you need a place to sleep in the new realm.

- Loading screens are a buzzkill. Be prepared to wait a couple minutes before jumping realms. I suggest setting up the portal, then doing some chores around your base while you wait. In most survival craft games you play (Palworld, Enshrouded, Valheim, etc.) the world is mostly seamless. This is a bunch of little worlds connected by seams.

- Crafting is inconvenient in all the ways it could be. You don't pull items from chests, crafting stations don't feel properly optimized, there are tons of little things to craft but no way to queue up multiple projects, and there is no inventory sorting. You will spend an absurd amount of time sorting, running back and forth, fiddling with menus, and realizing you have nowhere near the amount of materials you need.

I am SURE these things will be addressed over the course of EA-- in most cases, it seems like a no brainer when you look at the competition. I look forward to returning to this and having a blast. I'm just not having much of a blast after 30 some hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review was written at 44 hours played. I will update it later on as well if my opinion of the game changes.

I am not a survival game player at all. I watch a friend play various survival crafting games but I have never felt that the game play would be something I would enjoy. Then I saw Nightingale. This is the first time the idea of such a game has ever appealed to me. It has an RPG element to it and a story that doesn't bore me to tears. So yay, there is story!

Next we can talk about the look and feel of the world(s) your character steps into. It is beautifully designed and your character is very cosmetically changeable. There is more to come in the character design feature so I think that is great for those of you who really like to tweak your character's look. The world itself is just gorgeous. I find myself sometimes stopping what I am doing in game just to look at the landscape around me and the creatures that inhabit the world. It is all really interesting and there is a plethora of things to craft with.

Speaking of crafting, it is quite robust with how it is set up and I haven't even really dug deep into the whole crafting thing. Heck, I have only created one base of operations! Well I guess that would be two because my original building was so bad but that was on me, not on the game developers.

With the crafting there is fishing. I do have a complaint about the fishing, I wish there was an actual how to for it because playing on the controller (yes, this game has controller support!!) it isn't very intuitive. I also use my keyboard and mouse in combination with my controller when I need to but I like that most things I do can be controlled via my controller. But again, fishing! Why are you so hard? Oh well, there are plenty of other critters to kill for food but fishing is always one of my favorite things in games that offer it.

Game play wise, Nightingale is fun for me. I like wandering around with my companion (yes, I play solo and! you can hire a minion to go on your travels with you) and discovering POIs and random encounters. It certainly makes me happy to discover something I haven't seen before and figure out the puzzle of it or beat up the monsters that spawn because of it or what have you. As a mainly RPG player, the combat isn't fabulous but it is still fun. I find myself cursing out the enemies as I battle them and sometimes myself for some random dumb move. It's definitely entertaining to me.

There are lots of bugs in the game but that should be expected because it is an early access game. The great thing about that is that the dev team has been very good about listening to their players and doing what they can in a timely manner to address any issues or even quality of life things people suggest. In fact they have a discord for the game and they interact with the community when they can. I find that quite nice. I've never been one of those people that hung out in the games official forums to get all the latest info but I do find myself hopping into that discord server to read up on things or check out what people are saying about the game.

All in all, I really think this game is fun as it is. I also believe that the possibilities of improvement is there and I think from what I have seen so far that the devs plan to see it through.

So, if you the reader have no problems with playing a game that is still seeing updates and changes then this game could be for you.

If you get upset because game play isn't perfect or exactly like you expect it to be then this might not be the game for you.

If you don't mind making constructive suggestions and explaining the issues you are having to a responsive dev team, this game might be fore you.\t\t\t\t\t
