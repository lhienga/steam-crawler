\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am struggling to enjoy this game. I really want to love this game, the premise is amazing, the environmental art is beautiful, the effects are great. Even the story line is good.

This is still early access, but, there are so many issues that need to be fixed.
* Character creation is very interesting, but the customization needs work badly.
* Always online is on the list of issues to be addressed which is lovely and will probably help quite a lot with the constant disconnects and lag.
* Building system is a hot mess and needs a complete overhaul.
* The controls are not intuitive at all.
* The goals to reach the next portal are stated, but it's rather like telling someone who never cooked to make a beef wellington then offering no further direction or recipe.
* The follower needs a MUCH better AI and it would be lovely if we could give them even basic commands.

These issues are all things that can be worked out in early access, and I hope they are because, I do truly want to love this game. Alas, at this stage I struggle to even tolerate it overall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale was a game I was looking forward to since it was first announced. The game trailers looked more promising than the actual game itself. However, this game is still early access so by no means does this review solidify anything until it is fully released. I may change my opinion after but that depends.
So really this game atm just weighs out in pros and cons so

Pros:

The game is beautiful. The graphics looked pretty solid and the realms were very detailed even down to the very sound of the environment. Honestly that transition from day to night is outstanding; the night sky is beautifully surreal. The swamp area was swampy: you could hear the mosquitoes and you could feel the stickiness of the muddy waters. The forest was relaxing: you could hear the wisps of the trees and really embrace nature especially when youre surrounded by soothing bodies of water. The desert was dry and unforgiving. I could feel the draining heat of the hot sun and the sands were its only ocean with the actual oasis being the only form of relief. You can definitely see the life between the trees and water vs. how dead and empty the desert really was.

I appreciate that the only thing your character needs to worry about in terms of survival is sleep and hunger. Which can easily be done.

Building was easy, hunting was easy and even farming for stuff was pretty easy.
To me it was a relaxing game and if I wanted more of a challenge, I was allowed to change the difficulty upon opening a portal to get to a different realm.

The character design as far as the enemies go were pretty good. The creatures that would spawn were terrifying especially at night. They kind of gave a silent hill vibe (Im thinking pyramid head and the undead/warped nurses).

Boss challenges were pretty difficult, as it should be, and for the most part their mechanics were pretty simple to learn.

I didnt mind the grind for building materials or other items.

I enjoyed reading all the lore that I got to find throughout doing the runs to farm. You also get to find recipes to craft new materials doing so.

The main story was interesting and the concept was definitely there.

I also really like that you can have some buddies to play in your realm. It makes farming and combat much smoother to navigate.

Cons:

Although I do enjoy a chance to create my own character in detail, I feel like the character design was just not it for me. Ive never seen anything more embarrassing lmao. When it comes to create your own character I am a definition of snob. I appreciate you can choose your own face, skin, hair and control how and where you place things (eyes, nose,etc) but overall it just made my character look really weird. The hair design was so unsatisfying. I hated it. It could be because I was so spoiled being super detailed like in Baulders gate or Wild Heartshell in the Sims but I guess this isnt those type of games. So really I feel like character customization was so unnecessary. There are no cut scenes to even see your design. This whole game is in first person by default. The only time you can see your character is when you set it to third person or when you get into your inventory but even then the details in the design look distorted.

The story itself seemed a little hard to grasp at first if youre not actually reading anything.

The whole portal system was so confusing to me at first. At some point I wasnt sure how to get back to my original base. I thought wherever you place a respite in whatever realm you go to, you can fast travel by selecting it in the map but instead it takes you back where the game first starts you. The frustration to get back to the point where you wanted to bejfc.

In general I didnt like the travel concept. Opening a portal took 10 years to load and imagine having to go back in forth between realms..
I wish there was fast travel within each realm site at least from mid map to main portal. I get that the point of no fast travel is to get the player to explore but a lot of exploration was met by empty space and rewards in random spots were pretty unsatisfactory.

Puzzles and occupation sites were pretty repetitive and nothing really changed other than the puzzles seemingly being a little longer but again repetitive.

Building/craftingI dont even know where to start. I LOVE building. I love having the free range to do whatever I want. This game just barely made that exceptional. There are certain things I cant do to build and overall had me have to redesign how I wanted it to be. Like walls and stairs were a hot mess to connect sometimes. Crafting was just annoying. I guess I can admit that I was spoiled from previously playing palworld and creating a base allowed me to use all my items without having to actually have said items in my inventory. So i know Ive read that its a possibility to introduce that but man is it a pain at this moment.

I hate that I cant even organize my chest inventory in a certain order. It just places the item in whatever spot is available. Same goes for my character inventory or whatever is in my backpack, I can't organize it a certain way that I want. I have OCD about that kind of stuff. Nothing has made my brain more itchy than that lol.

Combat was so clunky. It felt so chaotic and inaccurate. I could be right in front of an enemy and I would still miss a hit or a shot. The magic definitely helps with crowd control but when youre first starting, its honestly a nightmare.

Theres a certain point in the game where you get to play with other players online and although its easier to go through portal runs and boss fights, its still pretty bizarre that there is absolutely no way to communicate with them. Not even a chat bar to let anyone know where to go or what needs to be done. I literally had to do a crouch motion to thank a person for helping me get back up and staying with me after being taken down and breaking a limb lmao (thank you Cara99 or whoever you are!)

Overall I think this game had a good concept and I appreciate the design of it all. Theres just a few things I wish I could change. The server lag is frustrating but I do enjoy playing with friends. Survival crafting has always been one of my favorite types of games and for the most part this game did its thing. However, at this moment I dont think this game is ready to be played thoroughly and I dont recommend it. Id definitely wait until it is fully released and updated. Again, my review isnt set in stone until it is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove the crafting and building system - reminds me of the old, Star Wars Galaxies crafting system. Character customization is great - down to picking your own genealogy and incorporating that into the look of your character. Building is fun thus far, though material requirements, even in the beginning stages are pretty steep. The story line is fun and definitely challenging. I've died several times trying to get through the Herbarium haha. Crafting and upgrading your gear along the way definitely helps. *Finding* some of the materials is a bit challenging and requires a bit of research, as there really are minimal "hints" in the game as to where to acquire recipes and / or materials. Minor bugs, as expected, but the devs definitely respond to the community, and patch issues quickly, in comparison with other publishers as far as I have seen. Look out for Twitch drops as they come available - you will love the goodies you can get from them. Overall, a very fun game with minimal frustration points.\t\t\t\t\t
