\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; The game is fantastic. The item crafting is second to none. Combat feels great. However, can be tricky starting out at first seeming there's not a lot of hand-holding. 100% worth the buy. My only complaint (and its been fixed with the recent update) was being booted to the menu intermittently when chopping trees on a populated realm with friends. The game has been stable since the latest update. Played on 4090/7900x/64gb@6000mhz.

I wanted to wait til I had a lot of solid playtime before I wrote this review. First impressions weren't very promising. The game is beautiful but It felt like it started out slow with not much of an introduction to the world. Finding out exactly how to progress in the beginning was a bit of a pain, but after 6-8 hours or so, I got the hang of things. Crafting in the beginning seemed great. Every item has multiple purposes and finding that right piece of loot to craft your armor/weapon/etc can make all the difference. What you make your gear out of has a huge impact on how it looks and performs. There are nearly endless combinations. After about the 60-70 hour mark, I got to what I thought was completing the early access area, and I was kinda bummed. Thankfully, it wasn't. When you finish and reach the end point of what the game initially sets you off to do, you get to a point where everything just opens up. Everything that you've experienced in your play-through so far while trying to reach the primary goal now feels like a prerequisite or even an extended tutorial. It is fantastic. As of writing this, I have made it to the extreme end of the early access campaign at about 140 hours. I have made all the best items and gear that I can make, and I am eagerly awaiting the first expansion. Even with having all the quests completed, and top level equipment, I still have desire to play. This is a great time-killer, and a great long-term game to sink some time into. If you're on the fence, I promise it will be the best 30$ you've spent.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove this game. It's early access so there's still things to work out but the dev team has been responsive and clearly care about the game. I actually have lots of issues with going between realms but that's something I know they are going to work on and fix. Even with that frustration of network errors, the building, crafting, and progression make it worth it for me. I like how they don't just unlock magic right away - after all we're supposed to be humans in a fae's world, not fae themselves, so in my opinion it should take time to discover those fae magic secrets. It was a little overwhelming at first when it came to the stats on all the different types of materials (and I didnt even realize the stats mattered at first), but now that I've played a bit more it's actually fun to search for the best combinations of materials to get the weapon or armor or tool with the stats you want. Not to mention it's awesome how the ore and wood you use to craft the weapons/tools actually matter to the appearance! Love my metallic green "pursuit" metal guns! The aesthetics are gorgeous and once you unlock the Watch you get into a more mmo vibe where you're playing alongside other realmwalkers, defeating instanced fae dungeons with bosses. Overall, very excited to see where the game goes in the future!! I would like some more customization with the character creation in terms of body types, but its understandable that they haven't added that in yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale is less than what it could've been at this point.

The crafting is unnecessarily convoluted, the materials gathered can have different properties (for example different kinds of hides from animals) and still use the same image, which makes hovering over every single one a necessity.
Adding to this, having to carry everything you want to craft with in your small inventory is another bummer, when it could just be retrieving the materials from your stash in the same house instead.

Battles are rather braindead swinging for the most part, with no need to consider the weapon you'll be using since everything works as one. The enemy AI isn't much better either, with only a few moves to each enemy and a simple "approach and hit" tactic each and every time.

The environments are beautiful, but exploration isn't too rewarding.

In general I couldn't shake a sense of "what am I doing this for?" during every minute I played, there's no grander motivation but to survive and keep going, no goal to reach, just a long, grindy way.
Sad, but I cannot recommend Nightingale at this point in time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been EAGERLY anticipating the release of Nightingale, drawn in by promises of a captivating fantasy world filled with magic and adventure. However, after sinking (only) over 30 hours into the game, I find myself deeply disappointed and frustrated by the experience.

One of the most glaring issues plaguing Nightingale is its abysmal server stability. It's utterly disheartening to invest time and effort into a game only to be constantly met with server crashes, disconnects, and lag spikes. This instability not only disrupts gameplay but also undermines any sense of immersion or enjoyment.

Furthermore, despite being in early access, Nightingale feels alarmingly incomplete and lacking in direction. Progression is sluggish, with essential mechanics such as magic locked behind tedious grinds and unclear objectives. Crafting, touted as a core feature, proves to be a mind-numbingly dull chore rather than an engaging gameplay element.

The narrative of Nightingale is another sore point. Promised a rich and immersive story, I instead found myself struggling to connect with the world and its characters. The supposed fae, a central aspect of the game's lore, are disappointingly underutilized, with only one notable presence in the form of Puck. This lack of depth and diversity robs the game of its potential for the intrigue and wonder I was hoping for.

To add insult to injury, recent attempts by the developers to patch the game have only exacerbated the problems. Entire player bases being reset due to egregious errors is simply unacceptable, showcasing a worrying lack of quality control and foresight on the part of the development team.

Concluding... Nightingale falls far short of its promises and potential. With its unfortunate server stability, lackluster gameplay mechanics, and underwhelming storytelling, it comes across as a product rushed to market before it was ready. As it stands, I cannot in good conscience recommend Nightingale, as it offers little more than frustration and disappointment. I really look forward to seeing some significant development on this front as there's still a glimmer of hope that this game isn't DOA.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tListen, I've been too busy playing Nightingale to write this review, but I'm crafting some large quantities of food right now so I have a few minutes. I'll say a nice thing about an aspect of the game and then point out room for improvement until I get distracted. TL;DR- 9/10 EA (there are some bugs)

Gameplay itself has been great. Combat is satisfying and movement FEELS pretty good. When you hit an enemy, there's weight to it, they recoil appropriately.

Combat could be improved by shaking up enemy AI a bit. They do have patterns to learn, just not very complex right now. And while movement feels good, it looks kinda silly in multiplayer/ 3rd person. Added visual weight to movement would go a long way here.

Crafting... I don't know where to begin. It's not the crafting system it appears to be at first glance. In the beginning you use sticks and rocks to make tools to get better materials as expected, but then you get to a point where there is so much variation in the materials that there's no longer a linear progression for upgrading, and experimentation is beneficial. I've experimented extensively with a couple of guns and hunting knives trying to find a combination of materials that suits my play style. I use magic a lot, so my knife needed to be high in Magic without dropping the Melee damage or critical hit multiplier, while also not neglecting stealth....or the strength stat that determines how hard an enemy staggers when you hit. The answer was combing different materials through several stages of crafting component pieces to make my perfect knife. The end result is visually represented, which is a really satisfying experience. My favorite weapon right now is a purple gun with green inlay that combines three of the best materials for ranged weapons. It can be pretty big brain if you want to go deep in the crafting, but you definitely don't have to.

Honestly I don't have much negative to say about the crafting. It's one of the game's strongest systems. Explaining early on that you have the ability to customize to that degree might be a good idea though, as plenty of people seem to craft tin items and think it's the same thing they've seen a hundred times before.

Building can produce some very nice structures. Decorating goes hand in hand with crafting, as the items you have scattered around your home determine which of your unlocked recipes you can craft in your work benches at a given time. An example: To craft an Axepick of any kind, you have to unlock the recipe, and also have a drinking horn near your crafting station. For a pistol: Saddle rack. This results in a pretty eclectic collection of items on display at your home. This is great in my opinion. Instead of following a specific theme (though you kind of can), it results in a home that looks like a well traveled adventurer lives there.

The downside of this system is you're limited on how many decorations can augment a crafting bench at once. Example: I need to make an axepick, but the teaset that allows me to make a shotgun is taking up a slot near the work bench. I don't make shotguns on a regular basis, so that teaset will be better off in the kitchen where it will let me make tea and crumpets. You'll end up moving things around, which can be tedious...but to be fair it adds another kind of life to your base if you want to think about it like that.

Farming isn't there yet. Some of the plants aren't working, but they have already started fixing this. Wheat wasn't an option for growing when I started playing, so a lot of food recipes were off the table. After the last patch, I have access to flour and a whole assortment of food items.

Progression is tough if you prefer games that provide instant reward. Nightingale does make you work for what you have. There's a curve in survival games, where you struggle for a bit, just trying to scrape by with little resources, and then you break through and seem to have everything in the game all at once. That's normally where I lose interest. Nightingale extends that period of struggle, then when you break through, you see that you're only just getting started. They do this a couple of times before you get to endgame, and it's one of the reasons I have nearly 130 hours so far. Thriving in the Fae wilds ain't easy, but it's satisfying because you have to earn it.

There's so much to do in the game already. I have a laundry list of things that I need to do even during endgame: grow specific fiber to make better clothes, cook more food, track down specific animals, upgrade some of my new equipment, unlock more recipes and build a new base with the building tiles I recently unlocked. And I still have quests I'm supposed to do that I've been sleeping on.

Most of those things are multi-step processes. Some people probably find that tedious, but as daunting as it seems when planning it all out, I always have fun doing it.

Realm cards change the world you're in in different ways. Need to grow crops or be a better wizard? Play a Tempest card to make it rain constantly. Want a horror experience? Go to a gloom realm and play a bloodmoon card. Want to feel like a super hero? Play Thinned Veil and leap around the battlefield wielding your pistol from the air. Lots of potential for a good time here.

My food is done cooking. 30 high tier meals that should boost my health and stamina enough for extreme Ascended difficulty.

Nightingale does have a lot to offer. Best $30 I've spent maybe ever. And it looks like it's going to keep on giving. Pay attention to the narrative and the bits of lore scattered around the world. It's really good. Approach this like Valheim (or other survival/exploration game) and not a "live service multiplayer" and you'll have a great time. Most of the criticism I see out there seems misplaced to me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe launch of this game has, quite sadly, been rough for a lot of players. I cannot speak much on that, because i simply haven't experienced most of those issues.

Nightingale has a quite fresh and fun take on the exploration sandbox genre, in the way that you directly have the ability to change the way the world around you functions. I am a veteran of this type of game, and have thousands of combined hours in them, and anything that gives the genre a new look is good.

There are certain aspects of Nightingale that i currently see as issues that need to be expanded upon, or directly need to be changed to make good, but i am confident that it will become a very meaty game down the line. As of right now, it feels like the game is an expansive demo, and that is completely fine and understandable.

The crafting system is fun, if quite confusing early on. Weapons and gear is crafted using a basic recipe, but can be crafted with various tiers of ingredients, but it is quite confusing to figure out exactly which things you can use for which purposes. I like the idea of it, but the data you can read off of the screen while crafting is much too limited, and the screen where you craft is pretty poorly designed. Its like using a factory management screen, not a single crafting station.\t\t\t\t\t
