\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHello Fellow Gamers!

To begin with I highly recommend this game. Even though it's in early access this game feels more fleshed out that other full fledge games I've played. Yes, there are still bugs (though not nearly as many as skyrim STILL) and there are still some collision issues that need to be smoothed out. However, that just takes time and people finding those generated issues. Also, there are some elements/items that still have placeholder images for the artwork. Artwork assets are time consuming and honestly the lest bang for the buck when it comes to game develop. You interact with the code which is pretty darn smooth.

However, YOU WILL NEED DECENT INTERNET. (not fiber or anything but steady at least 5-6 GB internet). Your machine will also need the specs they provide and lastly be up-to-date in your windows updates. I swear that most of the problems people are having with this game can be contributed to these two things. You can expect a standard computer to run an online, heavy graphic game.

The game play reminds me of Valhiem but a lot more quest structured and steampunk magic (where Valhiem is all Norse). It also reminds me of Ark in it's crafting system and how things function. Again, this game is in development but so far it has blown my expectations out of the water and the fact that they are continuing to work on it only makes me more excited. People are NITPICKING this game to find flaws. Not sure why but I assume it's because they didn't quite understand the genre. It's a grinding craft game, kill the monsters, trees, plants and build stuff, level and repeat. :D It's fun!!!!\t\t\t\t\t

(Hint: When the narrator of any game tells you..."Fine play it your way." hide your wallet. That game will be a hodge podge of half completed systems, too many ideas and not enough thought provided to tie everything together.) After playing that game for 5 wasted hours I found a universal truth. Throwing things together without thought or vision gives you a muddled mess. Unlike the 10 hours I have already spent in Nightingale which like the Faewild planes you get to visit is a breath of fresh air! A clear vision, a concise story, with only enough guidance to get you going. Exploration, Crafting, and Light survival are the names of this game and they are front and center. 10 hours in the game and I still haven't felt compelled to leave my own realm. There is still a ton of realms I have yet to visit many things yet to do, but my one little pocket realm has already given me hours of enjoyment and entertainment. Few minor bugs and glitches but the team seems to be on top of things and so I have no doubt they will be addressed. Conclusion: If you are on the fence between Nightingale and Enshrouded, don't be. Nightingale is the *FAR* superior game!\t\t\t\t\t

Combat is better than most are saying, there's a tangible heft to each swing, ranged needs the most work but again, the foundations are there.

My biggest gripe is the opening few hours are slow and tedious. You need to make significant progress for the game to fully open up and draw you in. I dare say it's this initial period that most of the negative reviews are coming from.

If you're a survival fan who values exploration and adventuring, give Nightingale a go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game feels like an huge tutorial but without the actual tutoring. There is way too much time spent on basics and the game's tech tree (if it can even be called that) is far from intuitive. When I started buying recipes for crafting stations from an essence trader alarm bells immediately went off. I don't know any survival/crafting fans who want to learn their core stations and upgrades from a vendor. There is no experience level, so it's all about item level, which appears to mean it's all about finding the right vendors. So kill, explore, and solve all you want, but if you can't acquire the recipes for the stations to improve your gear, you'll just be farming mats.

After playing for 40 hours I've got stacks of mats that I'm completely incapable of using. And if I unlocked new stations today I would be totally overwhelmed because the variety of mats is dizzying. I recruited a friend to play and he looked in my hide, bone and meat chest and just said, "Yeah, I'm not dealing with all that," and asked for a refund. Good thing he didn't look in the ore and minerals chest. The irony, is I would actual love all this variety and complexity if it was progressing in a logical way. I'm running around with a tiny knife with a stone blade and a slingshot but sitting on an array of metal ingots, fabled hides, etc., that I don't have the knowledge or capability to use. And, yes, I know some players are already in the end game.

Most of what I've learned about the game has come from other players' posts as opposed to actually playing. Some people are already cheesing the game so good they don't know if the crafting (tricks) they discovered were even intended. As for me, I keep hoping that eventually I'll find a tipping point where the game isn't tedious, but it hasn't happened yet. The sense of danger and adventure while progressing in Valheim isn't something I've felt at all in Nightingale. And the smooth combat and flight of Enshrouded feels a lot more physical and real than the strange movements while running around slashing in Nightingale. But the biggest difference is figuring out what to do next and where and how. Nightingale has plenty of struggling to do that, but not in a fun way.

One of the things I love about survival crafting games is the replay value. If you get bored of fighting, build instead. Got bored of building, explore. But one consistent complaint about Nightingale is the need to build and remove your home before finding a place to call your forever home. At this point I'm on dwelling number three and I've kept it basic because I know I'm not going to stay. What I wouldn't give to be working on making the best home I could with the plans and materials available to me thus far.

Alas, I find the game to be a bit more like work than play. It's not too difficult. It may be too easy. I've accomplished a lot with my little stone knife. But I keep feeling that when the progression finally does come, if I stick with it, I'll be sitting on so many materials I'll quickly jump to the end. That's weird. And it's not much fun.\t\t\t\t\t
