\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it's a game with interesting concept, Nightingale suffers from many issues on two major fronts.

First of them is Early Access. The game feels extremely incomplete, rough, and full of "placeholders" and "temporary solutions", even for an Early Access game. Whatever is happening on server-side is equally unfinished; realms take up to several minutes to be brought online and a huge percent of players have been reporting constant disconnects, timeouts, and network errors. This can be extremely frustrating for a game who's entire premise is the ability to transition between disposable realms. The inability to reliably transfer essentially locks you down in one place and hinders your progress, yet there was absolutely no response from the development team about this critical issue. Are they looking into it? Have they given up on it? Is it ever going to be fixed? Nobody knows, because there is no communication from the devs.

Second major issue is the developers' expectation that players are going to spend hours grinding for the most basic materials. This problem is aggravated by recent change in "repair mechanic", where just plain maintenance of your equipment becomes unreasonably expensive and you're forced to grind even more just to be able to afford to repair.

I cannot recommend Nightingale at the moment. Maybe in three to six months from now it would be "Early Access". Right now it's not even that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNightingale the adventure begins...

So you want to know about what it is like to survive in a world disconnected from Earth? Not knowing anything about what you are going to do to survive? You came to the right place.

As you embark on your journey you will visit beautiful yet treacherous lands filled with exotic looking animals and some that look familiar. You will learn to harvest the resources around you to help you survive the elements and make housing and weapons to protect yourself. Those weapons will even reflect the materials you used in the way they look.

You will come across others to help guide you in this new land of realms you are in. They will show you tips to survive out there or how to impress the locals.

This is all in the vain of trying to get back home to Earth.

Do remember this game is early access and there will be issues that come up. At the core this game is survival at its finest, but it adds a RPG element with quests and makes the survival grind less well grindy.

Crafting is complex, but is really fun. Material matter for the final product. What you put in matters on how good your item will be. It also changes the looks. You make a barrel for a gun out of copper. Guess what you have a copper looking gun. Brings back some of that old dare I say SWG crafting feel to me.

As for those fussing about offline play that is coming. If you don't want to play solo online and get a feel for the game just wait.

The game has a great start and should only get better from here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, normally, I don't write any reviews, but this experience has been more than frustrating, and I hope this review will help people gain some insights before making a purchase. One of the core gameplay features of this game is traveling between realms. It seems like stepping into another realm opens a new server or instance, but the game fails to connect and throws you back into the starting screen. This is a really, really, really bad development issue, as it makes the game practically unplayable.

Early access should reflect a stage where features are not fully developed but still functionable, gameplay mechanics need some polish, or placeholders need to be replaced with new elements during the development process. However, the current state of the game, especially the constant disconnection from the server, is just unacceptable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnjoy a game for min-maxers, tinkerers and gatherers!

Yes it is Early Access and yes it has some flaws. The fact that it is online-only is a decision that is already justified and will perhaps be better understood in the full release.

The graphics with Unreal Engine 5 is awesome, the 3 biomes offer good variety for an EA in terms of resources, enemies, bosses and challenges. If that's too easy for you, you can adjust the difficulty levels and minor cards! the fight mechanics is something i was looking in other games so far.

Maximizing crafting to get the best out of the items requires planning and an overview. You don't have to play the game like this, but it gives you the opportunity.

The endgame consists of farming the best resources for the best items and the best food. To do this, you travel to new and old worlds and visit challenging realms (no spoilers here). For an EA, I am very satisfied and look forward to further patches, updates and content.
