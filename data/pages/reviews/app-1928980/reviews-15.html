\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was holding out hope of improvements, but instead the company has been causing more issues and making their already tedious as hell crafting even worse.

Crafting has a ton of different in-between steps. craft 8 different items that all come from INGOT just to combine them. Just ask me to put 16 ingots and call it a day, wow. Then each material gives completely different stats and there are also tiers of these materials. You can understand now just how complex it was already getting. The mobs also don't drop the base materials, they drop all sorts of intermediate items (like mechanical gears instead of ore or ingots). Which used to be annoying but still useful. HOWEVER, because they are incapable of thinking of more elegant solutions, they have now completely removed the ability to reclaim base materials from any of the intermediary items in order to stop an exploit (but they left the exploiters with their OP items!). So now you have to be paranoid crafting exactly X amount of each dumb intermediary step and cannot recover base materials from all those mobs that drop the intermediary steps!
Gee, thanx! There's EA and then there's bad decisions.
And the icing on the cake??? You have to go and grab each of the materials you need from your 15+ containers because it can't possibly see what the heck you have in the containers! Honestly this last change just made their already convoluted crafting system the worst thing I've seen. Way to go wasting this beautiful aesthetic on terrible mechanics and decisions.

Game is buggy as heck. Expects you to go to different realms, but opening the portal takes 2-3 mins and moving between realms is glitchy as all hell. Ends up generating copies of your helper that you can't get rid of, they just walk around your estate like an uninvited guest.
The game tries to hook you on aesthetics but the colors you get from crafting anything useful stat wise are mostly UGLY and the special creatures give horrific patchy two-color fabrics/leather that look horrific (no, I can't even consider those patterns, the clothes just look like you threw in bleach with the wash and it destroyed your clothes). The game has DYES that are used just to make realm cards and buildings. LOL what???
The game has a ton of crafting benches and augmentations that are capped so good luck figuring out how to make sure you can craft everything... And then throws a 350 items limit PER REALM. I can build 100 castles but 350 ITEMS to fill them all? I'm at 200 just with storage and crafting needs.
The crafting is tedious and also nonsensical. 30s per ingot and lumber and you have to craft so many of them, but everything else takes just 3 seconds? Somehow turning an ingot into a complex filigree or ornamental barrel takes 3 seconds but making a basic ingot is 30s?

UGH. Such disappointment. Stay away until 1.0 at least because this EA is a buggy drown-out mess.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game worth playing, if you're into mild survival/gathering/basebuilding.

Things I like:
Resource choice in what you make your items out of matters - which adds a bit of a min-max element to the game, and flexibility.
The server stability and game performance has gotten better what feels like every day since I started playing, which is great to see.
The building is headache-free from my experience, and there are a lot of blocks and decorations to use.
The world looks pretty good, has interesting biomes, creatures and points of interest.

Things I don't like:
The current pool of weapons might be a little shallow, but devs stated they are aiming to change that.
Travelling around the map - sure wish I could build waypoints so base location wasn't super important, would be nice to go between realms and have a reason to build bases in different realms other than the Abeyance.
There have been a few disconnects, and minor bugs. Nothing gamebreaking and they are noticeably fewer and fewer every patch.

Overall, it's one of my favorite games of the year so far, and I feel like it's a hidden gem.\t\t\t\t\t
