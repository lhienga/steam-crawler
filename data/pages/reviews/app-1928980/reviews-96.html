\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played most survival games and Nightingale is quickly becoming one of my favourites. I actually love the complexity of crafting and navigating the realms, and the fact that I have to seek out information and solutions myself. The game feels unexplored and as though there is lots of opportunity to discover new things, new ways to craft gear, and new unlocks. I've seen that some players have complained about onboarding and lacking information on what to do, but I kind of love that. I also haven't had a problem understanding anything after taking a quick look at the Glossary in game.

For an early access game I think Nightingale has done a great job at capturing my curiosity and keeping things interesting for at least 100 hours and probably more as I discover more depth to the game I didn't know existed - like Tier 5 Swamp Wood. Scaling gear score as much as possible and forging new gear with better materials feels really rewarding. I like the fact that there are "big numbers" - currently gear score 260 with over 2000 health and landing huge crits on Sun Giants! This feels a lot more rewarding than reaching a low cap.

Excited to see what else gets added to the game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think people are a bit harsh on the game right now. The game is okay. The foundation is there and it has big potential! But content-wise it's very barebones.

The big pull the game has is realm jumping. While the concept is really cool it's underwhelming right now. There's three types of biomes you can travel to, swamp, desert and forest. When playing one of these three biome cards you can add a modifier card that changes the difficulty of the realm you end up getting. But that's really all it does, it adds some different colors to things. but ultimately there's only three different realms you can travel to (with some minor procedural generation to it). I assumed before the game released that the amount of flavour, wonder and mysticism was going to be drastically different between a lot of the same biome realms you travel to. But alas, it's mostly just copy paste with the procedural generation being barely noticeable.

The building system is wonderful though! And art style has to be praised! Well done to the artists for the game!

The game will be an easy recommendation once realm jumping actually becomes fun and engaging! I want to be excited to see a new realm, I want to be surprised. But that went away after only a couple of realm jumps.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very good and in depth survival crafting game.
Each type of craft you do has its own work bench so for example you go to the forge for ingots and metal work, the tanner for leather, the sewing bench to make clothes and more, you start with simple work stations and then as you progress through the realms you unlock higher tiered versions of the workstations so you can craft higher tiered items you will also augment the benches with extra build-able objects.

You progress through the realms as you go making them more difficult as the story goes on, each time you enter a new realm you complete fae towers to unlock the map and then you complete some of the POI's this rewards you with essence the higher the realm the higher the Tier essence you get, you use this essence at the Essence traders to unlock your blueprints that you craft in the different crafting benches and the buildings you can build.

Once you reach The Watch you will find yourself in a town where you can unlock further blueprints and you can have access to doing Vault runs which again reward you with essence to spend at the traders in the Watch, you will also unlock further Ascendant versions of the realms you have previously gone to which again give you higher tiered items.

you are not handheld with this game and it leaves it up to the player to figure out what you have to do which can sometimes be confusing but then you can look in the menus and figure out if you are missing items from traders or if you are missing a quest or if you have a workbench built but need to also build an augment for the bench which will unlock a recipe.

as in any early access game there are bugs and things that could be fleshed out (more verity of enemies for an example) but given time this game has the potential for those things.

even though this game requires an internet connection to play you can still play this game SINGLEPLAYER without anyone else, you can set your realms to private which is the setting by default, you will see other players when in The Watch but you do not have to interact with them, when running vaults you are grouped up with random people but you don't have to talk to any of them you just go in and fight the enemy's and solve the puzzles in the rooms to move to the next, there are currently no roles like Tank, healer and damage which would require more in depth grouping.

improvements can be made like more main realm cards currently there are only 3 (forest, desert and swamp) more varieties of fae creatures and enemies, loading times, farming, but again this is all things that can be worked on\t\t\t\t\t
