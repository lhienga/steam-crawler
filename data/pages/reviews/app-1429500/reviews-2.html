\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good game, at the current point. The game is 78% percent complete, so waiting for the next expansion. Compared to the first, a lot was improved on. Has an entertaining progression, that also expands on some lore from the first game, as well as giving it its own story. The characters are colorful and enjoyable as well.

The combat is smooth, the graphics are great. The battles are fast paced, but also requires some quick thinking. There is eventually an arena mode, as well as some stat grinding. It also has quite a range of customization, with multiple weapons, equipment to the weapons, accessories. There are some puzzles in the game, the platforming was done great, etc.

You have three characters, and each has a unique play style. In short, you can build them in whichever direction you want.

It also has good music to it, and all around was a great experience.

Some additional information:

Story:

It revolves around Erza traveling to a distant world where her goal was for years to find her mother. This links into from the first game, the reason why she was determined to get her license. It also expands on Diablo, how she got stuck with him. While the story is simple, it is entertaining and will keep attention on it.

Battle System:

I'll expand more, there were improvements made to it so that is a plus. To give a general run down, each character has like five weapons they can use. Each weapon is unlocked after finding them at a certain point. With these weapons, you can chain together many combos, and these helps to make battles easier. The system itself is more fast paced, and requires some quick thinking at times as stated. Your performance will improve based on your attachments to your weapons and whatnot. As these can give you passives that massively changes the battle. I have a boss build and a PVE build. Each plays a purpose, the boss build really helps in the arena.

Smaller Things:

The arena mode is more for fun, but you get some things for doing it. Each has five stages, and there are a lot of bosses. There are also side quests, and some backtracking to do once you get the necessary enhancements.

Overall, this game had a lot of effort put into it. And while there were some issues, the devs worked to correct those problems and enhanced the experience. At the point I am writing this, I am waiting on the last expansion of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEzra's Wheel of Fortune takes quite a bit of inspiration from Bloodstained: RotN and similar metroidvanias. Move around the map killing enemies, mark down where you're stuck so you can return when you find the proper technique, farm enemies to get their special abilities and enough resources to upgrade your weapons or craft new ones. The bones of a memorable game are there, the graphics and music are excellent, the sound is ok, but a bit odd. Some of the sound effects seem like they are for a cartoon and not a game. The comedy doesn't really work at all and is unnecessary.

The main difference in this title is the special moves that were completely forgettable in other games are made much simpler and more important here. Bosses are damage sponges until you can break their shield, then they are stunned so that you can unleash a flurry of combos. The special moves do much more damage and the bosses have such huge attacks that the only way to get through them is to stun them and then do as much damage as possible with special moves before their shield refills. While the move system is interesting, it requires MP to use, the same as your equipment with elemental abilities, so the effect is you quickly run out of the ability to do said moves. There needs to be a drop that allows you to use those attacks without using MP or equipment that greatly increases the refresh rate, maybe tie that to its own bar instead of sharing special moves and equipment abilities.

At the moment in early access, the maps aren't completed. It appears that just finishing the maps and story are the current priority, along with prepping outfits to help boost sales. it feels like several mechanics just aren't there. For example the souls/gems/whatever you pickup from enemies all have their abilities at level 1, but there seems to be no way to get them to level 2. There are a few characters that send you on quests to find certain drops, but don't actually tell you what and there doesn't seem to be a way to figure it out. You just keep moving through the map and eventually get told you found everything you were looking for.

All in all, on the high end of good, not great, but has potential depending on how many updates it gets. Definitely worth checking out on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn average 2d metroidvania in anime style.

Graphics and animation is quite good, especially for not finished game. Loading of new zone is a bit longer then I wan't to wait but acceptable.

Player can switch between 3 characters with own battle style. Unfortunately I don't see reasons to use anyone except first one. Second character is required for some puzzles and can be helpful in battles with bosses to keep the first one. The last character specialized on close combats and almost useless with combat physic. Characters feels heavy and inert.

Other issue is enemies, especially bosses. They are dumb and have a lot of hp. So you lock them in a corner and spam with hits for a few minutes.

Height of platforms and height of jumps have strange balance. A lot of platform close to the limit of usual jump. But double jump is a little bit higher. So it's make jumping more comfortable but don't add a lot of new space on level as player expect in metroid-like games.

Also there are a complex mechanics for upgrades. You look for blueprints, buy new weapon from blueprints, upgrade weapon with elements of enemies(and there are a lot of different enemies, but not kind of enemies) placing them in limited space, every element has shape, also you can increase space for upgrade in weapon or armor. For me it's too much.

So it is good demo of developers skills. They know how to make long game and implement a lot of mechanics. But balance and common game design need more work. Not great, not bad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-- First impressions review --

Pros:
- Beautiful graphics and visuals
- Polished controls and responsive combat
- Good music and sounds/ambiance
- Jiggle physics and a general inclination for fan service

Cons:
- Only Japanese voice acting (so far in the early access)
- Protagonist is a cringe variety streamer, difficult to relate to or care about, does not match the rest of the setting imho

A solid option with good potential for enjoyers of metroidvanias like Castlevania: Symphony of the Night and Bloodstained: Ritual of the Night, though one can never know with early access titles. And the "teen streamer" character vibe should be pointed out as a possible deal breaker to many who care about waifu x setting consistency.\t\t\t\t\t
