Level design is OK for a Metroidvania game, but the rewards and normal bosses are a lackluster as they are too easy, and the rewards do not compensate too much before you reach Stage bosses.

Stage bosses is a boss rush mode from 1 to 5 adding a lot more difficulty, it is necessary for you to have a weapon that can parry attacks otherwise it is IMPOSSIBLE.
I was able to platinum the game within 14 hours and I recommend this game because the combat is creative, maps are ok, character design is bad, items are mixed feelings, and a lot more QoL can be added to the game like synthesis to items to make ingots into ore and disassemble what you do not use.

Give the game a chance if you are looking for a Metroidvania.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll give this game thumbs up, even though this is still Early Access. I think the game would have lots of potentials about the gameplay system, challenges and more interesting stuffs, etc...
As a keyboard player, I can say the game can play smoothly, even though there are some actions which need the mouse to move the pointer.
*** Graphic: The art design is nice, with distinct and beautiful environments, the characters are quite beauty. However, the NPCs and cutscenes, the characters models are lacking facial expressions. This is unfortunate from the perspective of a Metroidvania gamer. I hope devs will improve these weaknesses.
*** Gameplay:
- Basic: quite good, with many actions like closed-ranged attack, long-ranged attacks, magics, techniques and ultimate skills. Also, pretty nice of dodging, dashing systems, which the player should know when will have to dodge or move quickly. There are 3 switchable main characters who have their own skills, techniques, weapons. The idea of Metroidvanias with RPG elements and more complex, robust combat systems, these make the game have various ideas to play advanced styles.
- Advance: This is what I love this part cuz there are many ways to play with cancel technique. If not considering some scenes or actions that require a mouse to play, I can say this game is pretty good at advanced styles with each main character:
+ Ezra can play with Swordmaster style combines with Royal Guards and Trickster styles (I mean dodging and dashing). She can play with various styles, with her stylish combos and can finish with a very strong attacks.
+ Ciara can play with Kickmaster style combines with Gunslinger Styles. She can have good combos attacks combines with kick's techniques and can finish with gun. However, cuz of not many kick's techniques, she doesn't have many ideas to play as Ezra. I hope in final release of this game. She would have more interesting kick's techniques so she have more ideas to play.
+ Nia can play with Royal Guard, pretty nice Punchmaster and Knife. She can break the blue gauge quicker than even Ezra with her strong, quick punching attacks.
All 3 of them, if you guys can play with cancel techs, advanced styles, they can be very strong with stylish combos, that makes the game more interesting to enjoy.
Talk about techniques, compare to my favorite Metroidvania game, Bloodstained Ritual of the Night, the commands are simpler. However, the speed of this game are faster and the challenges are harder than Bloodstained RotN, these, make me have to focus and make decisions of playing various skills, techniques and advanced styles quicker.
- Overall of Gameplay: Pretty nice, can give more ideas of advanced plays. If devs keep improving the gameplay for smoother, that would be nicer.
*** Audio and Music: Quite Good.
*** Story: Fine and enjoyable.
*** Challenge: Balanced for normal play. This game would have the potential to have more challenges so that hardcore players can enjoy this game a lots. This game even need to have the level 1 only challenge, too.

I hope that in the final release of this game, everything will be better and nicer so that everyone can enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, I blew through my 2 hours' test period with this pretty quickly.

It's obviously still in EA and it shows, but I'm really surprised where it DOESN'T. Yeah, localization is still refining, obviously, and the text format in dialog boxes and its layout in menus can get a little screwy, but I can read everything just fine. I am never unable to do something I want to do because of poor design (so far), and honestly, this game has made me say "all right, VIDEO GAME" in a 'pump your brakes' meaning because they don't have to go this hard. The presentation and animation of this game are *beautiful*

Big Bloodstained vibes, but I don't mind, because I love Bloodstained and Igavania in general (Symphony ride or die; I'm a basic ♥♥♥♥♥; sorry), so more that's done WELL is very good. And honestly, this game seems to do very well. Bloodstained didn't have combo strings for its weapons, as a for instance, and I can even swap between them. It's cool getting to do a katana/rapier or gun/kick combo where each time you press one weapon's attack, it continues its string as if you had been attacking with it the whole time. It's really cool.

Voice acting is surprisingly good, even if writing is simple. I know the game is Chinese-made, or I'm pretty sure anyway, and as a Mandarin speaker, I've always always always wanted more Mandarin-dialog games (ANNO: Mutationem whaaaaaaat!). I'm disappointed this game doesn't have one (yet?), but the Japanese is quite good.

Too soon to be sure, obviously, but for an EA game at .5, it's really gripped me, and even if it maybe falls flat, I'm not sorry I took a chance on it. I'll be watching this one very very interestedly.
