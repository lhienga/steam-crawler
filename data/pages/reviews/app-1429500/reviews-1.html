\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will say that I enjoyed this game, and I would recommend it, but it has some glaring flaws that are quite annoying.

The pros:
The game is beautiful, I love the creature designs and the attacks of the different characters. The weapons are also pretty cool, and I like the uniqueness of each character. I love the metroidvania aspect of the game. The spells are pretty fun too.

The cons:

There's definitely some obvious early access bugs. There is some massive sound dissonance, like you can have the game quiet, then summon the bird spell and it blows out your ear drums. The same with the spinning holy orb things that spin around you. That sound is so loud compared to other spells. Also hit detection is pretty bad. Ciara's missile launcher really likes to just go through the enemy and not collide at all. Not only that but the lifesteal mod really seems buggy and doesn't work with Ciara's guns. I can fire the machine gun a million times and never get a single proc of lifesteal. Perhaps it's tooltip isn't accurate? Does it work less often for certain weapons? It doesn't say that at all. Also does it not work at all if you use it with your armor? I just can't seem to get it to work most of the time, and while it's not super great or anything, if I equip something I expect it to work. Though I'll admit a lot of the mods don't really make much sense or seem to work. Or maybe I'm just unlucky I dunno. Just my two cents.

Neutral:

The story is okay, I just am not a fan of the creepy old man hitting on the sixteen year old trope. But other than that it's kinda interesting. There's a few parts where I just was so confused because it seemed like it was going to go a different direction. I am eager to see it's ending though, I worked hard for it and i intend to finish it when it's time.

All in all, I still had a good time, despite it's flaws. And I think anyone else can too, just be wary of these issues, which hopefully will be fixed at a later date\t\t\t\t\t

I looked for a metroid like game and thought I found it here... to some degree yes, but actualy not realy. The rooms are very small and give no room for any maneuvrig and when you reach the edge, it shifts to the next "screen sized" room, there is no smooth sidescrolling. Funny part is, when you kill all mobs in one screen, change to the next room and immediately return, the mobs are also respawned.
The sound is about the same, story is worse, gfx much worse... actualy wonder how something for a nintendo switch can have better graphics then a pc... gues Frontier Hunter was developed for smartphones.
What also annoys me, there is no learning curve. Alredy the first mobs and especialy the first boss are pretty tough. It is worse, since the rooms are so small and there is no real place for maneuvring.
Your chars in Frontier Hunter are also not as maneuvrable as Samus in Metroid, which makes fighting even more difficult.
The first boss dont have one health bar, but a dozen. With the maneuvrability in this game, that is especialy fun.
When shooting... this game seems to only know 45°.... you either shoot left, or diagonal left, up, diagonal right or right. Aiming like with Samus? Nope.
Also a difficulty setting does not exist. You get better and want a harder challenge? Nope. You suck and want it a bit easier for now? Nope.
The controls are unprecise. As already said a few times the maneuvrability pretty much non existant. The combos are awful (like left left up down left A... yah sure, I remember when combos were AABA and the arrow keys only for movement...)
Whatever, I learned Metroid Dread, here, no chance.\t\t\t\t\t
