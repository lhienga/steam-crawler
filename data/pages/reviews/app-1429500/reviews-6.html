\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm gonna give this game a thumbs up with several caveats. This being an early access, I'm gonna split my review into 2 parts. One directed at other people that might be interested, and another at the devs.

To other players:

The idea of metroidvanias with more complex and robust combat systems is one that appeals to me, and this is the niche this game is trying to cater to. There's 3 playable characters that you can switch between on the fly, and even call fourth to assist during a brief period. Each character can hold 2 weapons and 2 spells at once, and perform a variety of special moves using fighting game style inputs. You can launch enemies into the air for combos, cast spells mid attack, and so on. There's a lot of variety here, but the game doesn't push you to do any of it. Doing the cool stuff is up to the player, so if you're one of those that call any game that does fast melee combat a "button masher", than this might not be for you.

For the story, thankfully, you don't have to play the original (Tower Hunter) to understand what's going on, as that game is very light on story anyway. There is a LOT of japanese cheese here, and the game doesn't take itself seriously at all. Of course, the story doesnt conclude in this build, but I don't expect things to become much more interesting on the final release.

The visuals are sadly on the weak side. The art design is nice, with distinct and colorful environments, but the dated 3D graphics really don't help. Things are at their worst during cutscenes, with character models lacking facial expressions. With so many beautiful indie metroidvanias on the market, this game sadly falls well below average.

In terms of design, its a typical metroidvania that doesnt reinvent the wheel, but its put together nicely. Some clever puzzles are sprinkled in which is nice. There's a robust RPG element, with crafting and customization systems.

The main issue with this game so far is a general lack of polish. Problems registering inputs and collision, and occasional bugs and slowdowns. Hopefully all these issues are solved before the final release, but as of right now, theres a "jank" feel to this game. Its a nice game with cool ideas, but I can't really recommend this over the more popular indie metroidvanias avaliable. But if you have already played those, and the idea of a "character-action" metroidvania entices you, keep an eye out for this one.

To the devs:

Here's some of my feedback on things I feel could be improved upon the current build in order of importance:

1: Clean up the directional input reading. Playing on a PS5 controller, in several occasions, the game would eat my inputs or register incorrect ones. Even trying to choose a teleport room in the map, the cursor would jump all over the place. There seems to be problems with how the game handles analog sticks, at least for the ps5 controller.

2. Work on the collision detection. Sometimes my hits simply wouldnt register even when I was hitting the enemy.

3. Work on partner AI when calling them. When calling an assist, the AI will often not follow you.

4: Don't split the words in the english subtitles. I'm not a native english speaker, but it's impossible to not notice the words getting chopped up in the subtitles, completely disregarding grammar.

5: The water controls shouldn't be the same as the air controls. Currently, trying to go down is the water is very awkward. Make it so we can dash in all 8 directions in the water.

6: Work on facial animations during cutscenes, specially for NPCs.\t\t\t\t\t

Starting off, I'm a huge fan of Iga style metroidvanias. It's among my favorite genres. This game is clearly taking a lot from Bloodstained and it does a very good job aping it's mechanics and gameplay. That isn't to say that I like every clone out there. To me, one of these games has to get a number of things right:

-exploration
-hidden rooms
-lots of weapons and skills
-an xp system
-crafting
-enemy drops
-minor puzzles
-modest difficulty and secrets
-fluid combat and movement

So far in my few hours of play this game hits most of those things very well and I'll speak to some things where it's lacking. Fist, it's movement and jumping feels a little ropey. When you jump into a wall I stops your upward movement and pauses there for a split second before falling. It might be intentional but it feels like a collision problem and doesn't feel right. It's minor and so far doesn't really affect game play much at all. Occasionally I'll miss a platform but it's rare and it seems like something that'll get fixed. Other than that jumps feel "right". This game has a ledge gripping mechanic for platforms that you can't quite make. This game has a little trouble with this at times. It feels related to the wall collision problem. Your character pauses in air when it reaches the edge of a platform but occasionally won't climb up. It's not major but feels unrefined.

Combat is very good. Each weapon I've found has a combo and special move that's proved useful though it spamming attack is all that's needed for most mobs. Bosses require dodging and dashing to avoid their staggering attacks that interrupt your own. There's a variety of spells and your mana pool is low enough and recharges slow enough where you just can't spam it to kill a boss. It's great to use in between attacks. So far weapons are only acquired through crafting and gated through recipes that use monster drops and items found in chests, ore loads scattered through the world.

Exploration so far is great. Lots of hidden rooms, doors that require a spell or item to open. I'm constantly running into rooms that I can't yet access. but there's still a lot of paths that are open so it doesn't feel handholdy or like these gates are funneling you into one path. So far these paths are long and often looping back so I haven't had to backtrack at a bunch of dead ends. It's solid level design.

I highly recommend this game for fans of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn exciting adventure in a new frontier!

Picking up after Tower Hunter and departing from the grind-heavy rogue-lite formula, we have here a lovely and promising entry. It gives a strong vibe of being inspired by the "Trine" series and the critically acclaimed "Bloodstained - Ritual of the night" but adds its own flavor mostly with combat much better than not only in its sources of inspiration but worthy of an action game. The variety of weapons and techniques is an improvement over "Tower Hunter". What I personally particularly liked about it is that both enemies and the player character actually react to being hit. Enemies (bosses included) don't just act like brick walls when being hit, the impression that they are taking damage isn't just represented by their HP bar decreasing, the heavy hits make even the toughest enemies reel. Huge as some of them are, taking a missile hit should look like it hurt and in this game it DOES.

Visually, the game is gorgeous. Though sometimes, the background can play tricks on the eyes. More than once did I try to jump on something which LOOKED like a surface to stand on, only to find out it's in the background.
The soundtrack didn't captivate me initially, but after listening to it outside of the game, I found it to be particularly pleasing. It really adds to the atmosphere of the game. The initial tracks are soothing or cheerful, reflecting the initial mood of the game but the music turns gradually more dramatic and foreboding as the scale and nature of the real threat are being unveiled.

Story-wise, it's rather interesting so far, can't give a detailed verdict on that until the whole thing's out. But the most amusing part is definitely the banter between Erza, Ciara and Diablo.
As expected with an early access release, bugs and glitches happen but they are being squashed. Some sooner, others later.
Eagerly anticipating the full release!\t\t\t\t\t
