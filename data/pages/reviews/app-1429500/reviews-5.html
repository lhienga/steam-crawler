\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought this game was fine. It was very similar to Bloodstained: Ritual of the Night, except probably more special move focused/combo-driven combat. It has a lot of charm, especially in the cut scenes which are interspersed throughout the game. You play as three different characters that each use different weapon types, although from what I can tell, you don't really ever need to switch characters except for when the one you are using dies in combat and you are not at a save point. I personally would have preferred them to just use one character throughout the game and have that character have the ability to use the different weapon types because managing armor and weapons in this game is somewhat of a hassle, especially considering each weapon, armor, and amulet for each character has multiple modification slots. There is a also cooking in the game, although the degree to which it had a noticeable effect on the actual game play was not obvious to me. I mean, it bolsters stats, but given how combo focused the combat is, and how easily your character can get stun-locked, etc, the meals don't seem to have that much of an effect. Another issue I ran into with this game was money, or lack thereof. You never make that much money in the game, or at least it wasn't obvious to me how to, so I would go through a lot of my in-game saving just buying basic health potions, which I thought was strange. I also thought that parrying in the game was kind of difficult, from what I can tell, you can only do it with the katana and there is no standard block button so it seems like you have to dodge attacks, which to me wasn't super intuitive. Another strange decision on the part of the developers was having you find special moves for the weapons hidden throughout the game in chests. I think giving you these moves from the get-go might have been a better decision so that you can formulate combos and get used to the weapons while you play rather than springing new moves on you late in the game. Overall, I think that there are a lot of people that will like the combat in the this game is it seems more involved than that of other "metroidvania" type games, but for me, I never really fully got used to it, so boss fights ended up feeling kind of cheesy and I didn't feel like I was learning the moves of the bosses so much as I was repeating strong combos over and over so that they're health bar went down faster than mine. I don't think this was a bad game but also not my favorite of this type I would say it is about a 4/5. Thank you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is still in Early Access at the time of this review. Even in an incomplete state, there is a lot of fun to be had here. The crafting, RPG elements and classic Metroidvania feel all make for a solid game with a good balance of fun and varying difficulty, depending on how willing you are to dig into the guts to break the game with its customization mechanics, or play things the hard way and go in with minimal preparation and attempt to take down bosses with sheer skill.

Traversal is good. It feels solid, and fast. The sprites are larger than a lot of Metroidvania's though, and with enemy collision in place, it can cause some enemies to form organic roadblocks if the AI paths them in just the right way, grinding the dashing to a halt and forcing you to shred the mobs. If you like to go fast, sometimes this game does hinder that and a lot of it seems to be somewhat random.

Spells are various and fun to use and the way you can customize your characters' individual builds allows for experimentation for different combinations of magic loadouts.

Graphics are solid, and they did take the risk of having fully-3D cutscenes instead of just voice text boxes for every story scene as most Metroidvanias do. I commend this effort. The 3D anime models do, unfortunately, have the dead-eyed doll look, however. This makes some of the scenes a little off-putting. Also, the sexual undertones are an effort to add humor to the mix, but come off as more immature and fan-fiction-lite than anything else.

Overall, this is a solid game and I look forward to seeing what is added as development continues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tindie metroidvania lacks AAA polish but is still great fun, enjoyed the first game as well(not required to understand story). Music and graphics are solid. Controls lack dodge canceling but overall are good.

-other thoughts.
-upgrade system lacks purpose. better weapons are available around the corner negating any benefit from upgrading. mats required to upgrade are too lategame/rare, further negating benefit of upgrading gear. There is no post game content.
-fixed map is great for navigating but lacks replayability of random-generated maps like the first Tower Hunter games roguelike-lite nature.
-multiple characters is cool, but I never switched and just used Ezra. Late game her stats were twice that of her partners from stat upgrade items further reducing their purpose. Also the third character doesnt "unlock" till pretty much the end of the early access content. not sure how much more game will be added on full release to make investing in that char worthwhile if they happen to be the one that catches your fancy.

-early access is a good 5-10 hours depending on your style of exploration and grinding for upgrade mats and specific souls to slot into your gear.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA little rough around the edges, but the core gameplay is solid. It's a Metroidvania with decent exploration and combat. Having 3 characters is a neat feature that gives you a variety of playstales, but I still favored Erza due to her range and flexibility. Ciara's lack of range outside of generally slow guns makes flying enemies pretty annoying. Nia's also got low range being mostly melee. In Boss fights, the range difference isn't as much of an issue, however. I do like the emphasis on skills and combos, but having some sort of MP Regen speed boost could be nice since later Magic costs a ton of Mana.

The equipment system is pretty straightforward, but each piece has sockets to enhance capabilities. This can get a little overwhelming at the start when you get a bunch of drops, but it's cool to have even if a bit tedious having to remove and re-socket each time you get a new piece of equipment.

The story is kind of run-of-the-mill, but it's serviceable. It's a little tropey. Translation / localization and typesetting could use some work, but I'm giving it a pass for now. Hopefully it gets cleaned up in the future. The music in the game is also pretty good. Right now, there's not a lot of tracks since it's still Early Access, but I generally liked what I heard when playing through the game.

I would recommend this game if you are looking for a Metroidvania that's anime-inspired with decent exploration and gameplay. Pick it up on a sale and wait for updates even.\t\t\t\t\t
