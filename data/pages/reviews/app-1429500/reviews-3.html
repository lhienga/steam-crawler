\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've not played this game for long but I've got some gripes I want to share.

So far as a metroidvania title, it falls short in terms of movement. There are no wall jumps, the only upgrade I've seen so far to improve movement is double jump. I'm not asking to wall jump off a single wall like in super metroid but it just doesn't feel that good. To put in a descriptive word, it feels sluggish.

Also The gatling gun aiming, only 8 ways? it's not a 16 bit game. I would ask to be able to improve aiming to a full 360 degrees if possible to improve that form of combat. These are my areas of improvement the devs could do.

The plus side is that it's a very pretty game, some decent combat abilities from the first main character you play and I like the melee combat from the second character you play.

got a nice bit of JRPG level up system as well to make yourself stronger so I wouldn't complain about that.

Would I recommend this game as a metroidvania to other people? Not really, not when there's Metroid, Hollow Knight and Ori and the blind Forest and Ori and the will of the wisps. Those are far superior games to this. The only thing this game really has going for it is the story, combat and the overall aesthetics that it advertises.

I would recommend this more as a Japanese Action RPG platformer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying as 0.52-ish early access, and considering discount. So far played and discovered fully the jungle stage.

Great game, polished over the previous title in almost every regard, maybe only a bit worst in it being much easier, too much easier in the beginning. I'll go over it in topics:

More metroidvania instead of the previous roguelike system.

Progression is completely different to fit the new genre, and works well so far.

The level design feels nice, remembers a bit of Megaman X8 in art style for me. As in it's detailed but not so much as to make it hard to play.

Graphics is pleasing and won't need you a expensive GPU.

Optimization is good so far, very rare frame drops.

Exploration is nice, you still have access to many kinds of weapons and in the first stage there are many hidden places and some connections to other places. Has teleporter rooms, works just like Hollow Knight's stations.

Combat is... a bit too easy, yet feels weird that dash doesn't bypass enemies. Maybe as to not make it too easy? Well, now you have to jump over them. I didn't like that much but OK. Also, animations could be more smooth a bit.

Characters were overhauled from previous game and their personalities have changed. So it's also a good thing in the way that not having played the previous game won't affect it much, or at all. Also, now they sometimes talk in stages, either with puns or some of their background. They're much better fleshed out, still ways to go but much better already.

So I'd give this a 8/10 considering it's 0.52 on early access and assuming you're also getting it discounted, although I find the base price a bit too steep, it really has a great overall quality so far so it's not unfair either.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGet this game if you like this type of metroidvania:
- Castlevania: Harmony of Dissonance & Aria of Sorrow
- Bloodstained Ritual of the Night.

Pros:
- Multiple weapon types to get per character.
- Hotswap anytime between multiple characters.
- Different skillset per weapon + per character.
- Each enemies drop unique "cores" which basically are hotswap-able gear runes.
- Dodge mechanic is instant, so you can use it anytime during combo.
- There are more story content & dialogue than the 3 titles I mentioned above. Which is kinda refreshing for the genre.

Cons:
- Currently still in early access, map completion is at 50%. It'll be 65%-80%-100% on each big update before official release later on (several months in between).
- Devs stated they require 3000+ reviews to commit into giving more content after official release.
- It's on the easier side of the genre with plenty of exploitable strats (for now), it's much more forgiving than classic metroidvania.

Conclusion:
- Try the game, it's worth it. The 2 hours refund window is plenty enough to see whether you'll like it or not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn easy recommend if you love your metroidvanias.

The combat is really quite good too, as it allow you combos. You get 3 characters who have different weapons which allow you to mix up your style too. It's fun to adventure and unlock abilities. It is what you'd expect from a metroidvania.

Right now it's early access so you don't get everything yet. There is a nice boss rush added to the end where you can fight more challenging versions of bosses. I do think there is still room for improvement but ultimately the game is just fun. I put 13 hours in so far just to get to where the early access content stops. I still have yet to beat all the boss rush versions. They are very hard.

I hope they will make it easier to upgrade gear and unlock slots, as they allow you to put certain abilities into your rings, armor, weapons and such.

Highly recommend this. I will make a video once the game fully releases.\t\t\t\t\t
