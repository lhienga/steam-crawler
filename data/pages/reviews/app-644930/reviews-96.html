The Positive
-Real-time strategy combined with tower defense survival mechanics just feels right.
-A neat steampunk theme.
-Interesting sprites.
-Fun and engaging base building with a variety of different buildings and units.
-A deep and varied tech tree in the campaign.
-Tight control of your units.
-A fun gameplay loop of scouting the map and killing zombies to clear new or more resources and building space for more housing, industry, or research buildings.

The Negative
-The difficulty curve feels nonsensical most of the time. At the start of the game, it only takes one zombie to get through your defenses to end the game. This is especially hard for beginners. Whereas in the campaign's mid-to-late game, the difficulty drops the moment you unlock any type of tower (balista towers, especially shocking towers).
-Even the biggest attack waves crumble before even just a couple of shocking towers. I don't like the hero missions. They feel completely disconnected from the whole game. In survival maps, heroes cannot be created, and vice versa.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCampaign wise I wouldn't say its the most engaging. It can be time consuming and a bit of a slog. I tend to do the "skirmish" or whatever you want to call it where its just a randomly generated map and you start from the beginning (before the campaign was released) and just go. I bought this game way early on and obviously don't have a ton of hours on it, but I find myself coming back to it periodically.

I would recommend the game if you are a huge RTS fan, enjoy city builders and are okay with dedicating time to a round or are able to pick up from your last save easily. As many have said, the groundwork is absolutely there but there are unfortunately some spots lacking. I've found my biggest issue coming back has always been time management. For me it sucks me in like Civ and somehow hours have passed, but that's me with almost any RTS.

TL;DR - If you love RTS, get it on sale but not for the campaign. If not, probably not for you.\t\t\t\t\t

Great art style, great sound direction, good pacing through the game, and lots of ways to tailor the challenge.

I never bothered playing the campaign - just the sandbox mode. My brother clocked the entire campaign and really, really enjoyed it, so I feel safe recommending that, too. But honestly whether you're a veteran of this genre or a newcomer, you'll enjoy your time with this game greatly.

The most important tactical advice I'll give is be prepared to spent a lot of time on 'pause', figuring out what you're going to do. Because there, as David Brent might say, 'noooooo a lot of margin for error, no.'\t\t\t\t\t
