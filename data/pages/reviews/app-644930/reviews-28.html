\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe overworld missions where you actually build are fun, but the game is full of long and horrible solo missions where you control just one crappy unit. I have no idea why these exist. You really have to do the solo missions to be able to research structures, but it's such a PITA I would rather urge you to not buy the game and spare yourself the pain of the game's solo missions.

EDIT: After 100+ hours of gameplay, I've decided to leave my rating at a thumbs down. However- I will tell you there is no other "zombie defense steampunk classic rts" game. TAB has that much going for it.

TAB is a game full of potential. All the framework is there- we could've had customized/make-your-own mayors, should've been able to add the train to custom maps, should've had more buildings and units like reinforced walls or suicide bombers. To put it in a nutshell, TAB has the problem of only giving you:
* Stone or Wood walls
* Three basic infantry
* (not much else)

You *do* have a lot of cool defensive fortifications, like barbed wire and landmines. The problem is that they aren't good enough to justify giving up the land you'd need to build them. It is more effective to just lay down lots of walls and put as many shock towers as you can behind them. No matter if you are playing survival or campaign, shock towers are the only thing that works. They're better than everything else, but I won't say they're broken. Why?

TAB gives you too few ways to scale into the lategame. Snipers in towers? Cool, but not functional. Wire and land mine or wood spike perimeters? That's nifty, but irrelevant. A carefully organized and diverse perimeter of Executor towers, snipers and soldiers in towers, wasp guns, special units and basically everything but shock towers? Nope, you're going to lose...

There is no reason to use anything besides shock towers because everything else sucks and has no way to keep up. Thanatos rockets? You won't have the oil to mass them. Executor fire will just choke when zombie numbers get really high. Land mines are one-and-done, and because they damage your buildings, require a lot of land around them. Barbed wire is effective when put behind your outer perimeter + turrets, but even then, you could pretend it doesn't exist- then buy more shock towers- and actually do better than you would with well-placed razor wire.

The "best" unit in the game, the Mutant, is completely worthless. An expensive low-damage melee unit that is just a fast, mild tank, unable to soak significant damage. They cost oil that you would need for rocket launcher men and titans, your go-to elite soldiers.

Titans are good elite troops for using up your oil, but realistically, you never need them. I have far more success rushing a perimeter of shock towers than I do with an army of titans.

Basically, all the fun things in TAB are only useful if you are already winning the game- and you only win the game by building lots of shock towers because (1) there's no other decent source of AoE and (2) nothing scales well enough into the lategame to be worth more than AoE.

Speaking of acronyms, there was a little game called Age of Empires where you could upgrade your otherwise-irrelevant troops through research... it came out 20+ years ago and has been a hit forever. Why TAB would place itself in the same genre- yet go without basic RTS innovations- is just bad. There's pretty simple ways to fix the balance problem:
* Troop upgrades, like giving soldiers different guns
* Adding fire to nearby ranger arrows with a building or something
* A type of turret/tower that gets stronger as it kills more enemies
* More types of infantry
* The ability to upgrade INDIVIDUAL mutants to actually make them useful and distinct from other elites
* More wall upgrades; detonating walls, electrified walls, reinforced walls, etc.


In general, TAB is a decent game in an uncontested genre with no competition. It has all the basics of an RTS game- except troop upgrade research- but everything you can build sucks compared to shock towers, there aren't enough upgrades for your walls, troops or buildings, and every match comes down to a stale rut of greedily capturing lots of territory to build the same things in over and over: x amount of farms, x amount of houses, make 2 to 3 barracks pumping out basic soldiers, put a bank and market next to the houses, build windmills and power plants, then surround everything in walls and shock towers. THAT'S IT. Any other strategy you could use- like rangers or snipers, or some other kind of tower like ballistae plus wasps and wire- is only going to gimp you. There is nothing worth using besides shock towers and basic soldiers. The elites aren't worth their resources.

That said, the developers of TAB are somewhat active, so there's hope for the game's distant future if they can take some of this critique to heart.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHero missions are some of the worst game design I have seen. Ruined what could have been a fun campaign. Don’t bother with the campaign.

Otherwise, the RTS aspects of this game with survival elements is fantastic. Did encounter a few bugs with unit visibility, i.e. my units and towers wouldnt fire enemies right in front of them due to an tree partially blocking view for example. This could cause a game loss. I also don’t like that buildings eat resources and that you don’t have good visibility or data on how much resources they are taking, making it unintuitive to expand in certain ways later in the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA well made Real Time Strategy horde survival game. The resource types, buildings and different troop types give the game variety and longevity. I spent a few fun afternoons seeing how far I could get. I think the complaint that gets this game the 77 it has on Metacritic is that I remember there being some balancing issues. You get pretty far and then bam you get demolished by a particular wave or more accurately by a particular simultaneous occurrence of waves. There's some unenjoyable luck to where your weak side is that you have to keep your sides as strong as possible. Yes you can have a mobile army of troops that can rotate around but if the zombies get through a wall and into your interior you're pretty much done for. Something that can be improved would be more building hitpoints because if they make it to your houses it spawns more zombies and the houses go down pretty fast.
There's also an exploration aspect where you can scout the map for resources and treasures but be wary if you upset too many zombies and drag them back to your base creating an unscheduled wave. The game is resource intensive with there being a literal building you have to build in order to build buildings. The pressing shapes, the conflicting sizes, the am I able to fit that other lumbermill in or is my first layer of walls in the way? These imperfections of building placement in an every resource counts difficult game all come to the forefront when you get run over and are set back to the drawing board. All in all there's room for improvement for this game but that's because the framework of the game is quality. What's a single player game without a challenge?\t\t\t\t\t
