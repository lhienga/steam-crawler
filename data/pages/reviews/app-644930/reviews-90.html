\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is basically a zombie tower defense but with base building, its quite fun but man does it have pacing issues. Its one of the few RTS I have played that has a pause mechanic to give you a bit of breathing room when too many things are happening on screen. This game is set on permanent IRON MAN MODE so no save scumming for you, you can take that as the devs wanting you actually learn from your mistakes and repeat the entire process, or them just not respecting your time. Personally I would have loved it as a option but I digress.

The campaign is where the meat of the content is in this game, to which there are two mission types are pretty straight forward (1) you build a base and try to outlast periodic zombie attacks with a mass attack at the end (2) hero missions where you control one "hero" unit.

The first mission type your most precious resource is SPACE, like seriously the only way to win in this game is expand expand expand. if you don't expand you don't get access to resources and you don't get a better defensive layout. You would think that an RTS about building a base and defending it is what this game is centered on, but man I spent most of the time in this game trying to kill every single zombie on the map to get more space and more resource coz at the last wave EVERY single zombie you leave alone with come for your ASS so you have to deal with them now or you will regret it later. If you manage to secure the map before the final wave it basically devolves into you waiting for 20-30 mins for the game to just bring it and at that point its just get stale coz you end up sitting around building up and expanding with nothing to fight except for the periodic waves. There are some missions where you start with no zombies and you just do nothing but build for 40 mins with nothing else to do.

This second mission type was quite disappointing, you control one hero and your hero does not have any special abilities or equipment or anything like that. Instead you pick up items like grenades and medkits and it feels like you are just clearing the map with one guy instead of an army. Furthermore in this mode you can pick up items for Empire points and Research points (which you really need) and once you cleared the map. It devolves into trying to find that one tiny item somewhere on the entire map that you missed for a good 10 to 20 mins or you give up and move on. They highlight them randomly by making them "shine" sometimes but even with that I could not bear to waste so much time trying to get everything.

After each mission you get to choose which technologies you can unlock, and its here that the game really kicks you down. Coz some techs are REALLY necessary to overcome the zombie waves especially at higher difficulty, and once you choose you can't take it back. It would have been nice to have some option to roll back your tech choices coz one wrong tech upgrade and your entire campaign could be massively ♥♥♥♥♥♥.

Overall I still recommend the game even with the shortcomings I still enjoyed it, but play this game is sessions. If you try to finish the campaign in one go you will go insane. A big thing that alot of people don't realize is you can change the difficulty of the missions if it gets too hard for you. Alot of people complain about wasted time, and I was about to be one of those people but then I realized I had been playing the game on hard up until the last few missions without realizing it and I was tearing my hair off why this game was so freaking hard. Some people can't handle swallowing their pride that they can't handle something on normal difficulty when they make a mistake, but is what the difficulty options are for. But as I've said maybe if the devs allowed for more options like an alternate save or optional iron man mode this would be a nonissue.\t\t\t\t\t
