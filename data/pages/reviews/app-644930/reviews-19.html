\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe idea of the game is really good, but there are a few aspects here that bring the fun to a complete standstill.

First of all, there is no option to load previous game saves. The developer justifies this by saying that the nature of the game requires this. That wouldn't even be so bad if failing/restarting a mission didn't lead to a permanent point deduction in the campaign, which also affects achievements. in other words, if you ♥♥♥♥ up in any way in the beginning of a mission you are forced to go with it.

The next point is the tech tree. As in many games of this type, you earn points in missions, which you then use to unlock new buildings and units. The only catch is that there are certain buildings that are essential in certain missions in order to complete them on higher difficulty levels. The higher difficulty levels are in turn essential for certain achievements. However, there is no way for beginners to recognize in advance which buildings and units are useful. If you make the wrong decision, you basically only have the option of restarting the entire campaign, because you can't reset the points. I'm currently in this situation, so my savegame is basically softlocked.

The third point are the hero missions. These missions are extremely boring on any difficulty level and require you to play the game "Where's Waldo" to find things on the map, with the catch that you have no idea what the things you are looking for actually look like. So you move your mouse pointer blindly over the map until a cogwheel appears somewhere, move your ridiculously slow-moving hero there, and repeat the whole thing 15 to 20 times. To be honest, that's not the kind of gameplay I expected from a game like this.
You can leave things lying around, but these earn the most points for the tech tree out of all missions, so not collecting them makes the previous point in this review even more challenging.

The other game modes besides the campaign couldn't really keep me interested for long, as the total number of buildings and units, and therefore the possibilities, are simply far too small.\t\t\t\t\t

'They Are Billions: A Symphony of Strategic Snafus and Zombie Shenanigans'

'They Are Billions' is not just a game; it's a test of patience, strategy, and the ability to keep your cool while an ocean of zombies tries to eat your brains. This game takes the tower defense genre, injects it with a dose of apocalyptic steroids, and then sits back to watch as you desperately try to keep the undead at bay.

Imagine playing chess, but for every pawn you have, your opponent has a thousand zombies. That's 'They Are Billions.' Your task is to build and fortify colonies amidst a world swarming with the undead, and let me tell you, these zombies aren’t the slow, shuffling kind. They're more like the 'Oh no, they're at the gates, and I forgot to build the second layer of walls' kind.

The graphics are a steampunk dream turned nightmare. The game beautifully renders your colonies, steampunk towers, and the teeming masses of undead with exquisite detail. The ambiance is completed by a soundtrack that's both haunting and invigorating, perfectly capturing the 'end of the world but still time for a cup of tea' vibe.

But beware: 'They Are Billions' is as unforgiving as a tax auditor. Every little mistake is punished not just by failure, but by the spectacle of your hard work being overrun by a zombie conga line. The game demands strategic planning, quick thinking, and the ability to not throw your keyboard across the room in frustration.

The difficulty curve here is more of a difficulty vertical wall. The game starts hard and gets harder, making Dark Souls feel like a casual stroll in the park. But, the sense of achievement when you survive a wave is unparalleled. It’s like winning an Olympic gold in mental gymnastics while zombies cheer you on.

In conclusion, 'They Are Billions' is an exhilarating, nerve-wracking, and brain-teasing adventure in a world overrun by zombies. It’s perfect for those who love strategy, creativity, and have always wanted to see if they could survive a zombie apocalypse (spoiler: most of us can't). So, gather your wits, plan your defenses, and get ready to tell billions of zombies that today is not the day!"\t\t\t\t\t
