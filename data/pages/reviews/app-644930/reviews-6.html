\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey Are Billions (TAB) is one of my favorite RTS games. I have logged hundreds and hundreds of hours playing this game over the years. The game play and overall idea of the game is superb. If you love RTS, base builders, survival or tower defense games, you will enjoy this one.

As for the negatives...

TAB has felt like an outdated game since it's release in terms of interface and controls. Very, very little has been changed since it was released in that regard. You will be doing a lot of 'clicking' on interface menus, because there is no way to bind certain things to your keyboard.

The game is very unforgiving, which is a good and a bad thing. 1 simple mistake can spell disaster for your play through of a map. I personally enjoy the challenge, but know that some players do not enjoy the frustration during the learning process. You will be tested on your game knowledge, as well as at the mercy of randomly generated maps in survival.

Lastly, and my biggest complaint about this game: INFECTED MUTANTS.

One of the absolute WORST implementations I could have ever dreamed of. These were not part of the game when it was released, and as the game developers added maps and increased difficulty options, they added an enemy unit called "Infected Mutants."

These infected mutants spawn randomly across the map, and have an obnoxiously large trigger radius. You will spend 30 minutes setting up your base, clearing, dealing with doom town waves. Then on day 25 or so, an infected mutant will randomly start running from half way across the map to the opposite side of your base. He will bring half of the zombies on the map with him and your run is over just like that. Whose idea this was? I do not know. Why hasn't it been fixed? Beats me. There are ways to try preventing this from happening as you learn, but they are minimal. Too much of it is RNG based (the map layout, where the mutants spawn, where they wander, etc.) NOBODY likes to lose games to RNG.

Overall, you will get your money's worth with this game. Many hours of enjoyment and hopefully we will continue to see improvements in the game over time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game, but its insistence on being all Iron Man, all the time holds it back. The first thing you should do after installing is go into the options and make sure Local Alerts is enabled; the game is *punishingly* difficult with it off. TL;DR: this determines whether the game will ping you because a zombie deals damage if it's on the screen you're currently looking at. Towns get pretty busy, and not realizing that one guy is slapping a house until it explodes into a dozen more zombies is usually game over.

The campaign is a delight, mixing up gimmick maps and Hero missions that are a love letter to the Installation missions in StarCraft 1.

I enjoy the game, but it would have been a better one if there was an option for manual saving and loading instead of killing the process in task manager.\t\t\t\t\t
