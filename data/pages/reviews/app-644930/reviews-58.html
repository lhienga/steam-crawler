\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCONSULT A WALKTHROUGH OR GUIDE BEFORE PLAYING UNLESS YOU ENJOY TRIAL AND ERRORThat's not a criticism, but it is probably the thing that I feel most needs to be said immediately after "I recommend this game." The game feels like old school Starcraft, featuring a tightly designed single-player campaign mode. The campaign features 3 different types of missions, and the player can climb the tech tree between missions with the rewards from the preceding maps.That's the rub, the reason you may want to check some other people's notes first. Spent tech points for the most part can't be reset or swapped around, so if you spend points inefficiently it is very, very possible to make maps harder than they need to be. With such tight design, I suspect that some element of that is intentional by the designers- the game already allows you to fail and retry missions but tracks your losses and lowers your final score for each failure. Realizing that you left a valuable tech option on the table doesn't make the maps unplayable or unbeatable- usually. I do believe that it may be possible to hard-wall yourself, but the game also allows the player to adjust the difficulty up or down before starting each map. You're given maximum flexibility to address your own sub-optimal builds.That said, getting stuck after sinking hours into an attempt and realizing that you either need to drop the difficulty significantly or restart in order to build differently can be painful. If you're like me, a first excursion into the game on your own will help you learn the basics and experiment with tech options. If you're lucky or just really understand the game well, you may stumble on a great build and have no problems. But, if you do get stuck, a quick trip to the forums here and on Reddit will arm you well for a second attempt at the campaign with a fresh set of ideas as to how to prioritize your builds. I've been having a blast comparing my first run to my second and seeing how much better I've gotten with practice, especially since for the most part there are multiple strategies that will work, but they don't all work in the same ways.If you're a fan of deeply analytical RTS games that allow the player to pause and think, you'll love this. A rare gem that manages to tickle the nostalgia notes without feeling even slightly dated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is brutal. In a genre where its usually ok to let one or two enemies slip through the cracks, if a single zombie manages to make it into your base, it's over. Absolutely unforgiving, no save scrubs, no checkpoints, and the game keeps track of your failures.

And yet, even when I'm frustrated and rage quit, I find myself coming back to try again. There's so much to do in terms of upgrades and progression in the campaign that I know if I can just beat this level, I wont have x problem anymore. The art style is easy on the eyes, and the music provides adequate ambiance; together they create an eerie atmosphere that keeps me engaged the entire time.

Some of the mechanics in the campaign are kinda bogus, like surprise not-really-a-full-wave waves coming in unannounced before the official wave, certain enemies being allowed free roam of the map from the beginning of the round, and occasionally misleading layouts (i once failed a map because I spent all my resources beefing up defenses around an obvious route in to the map but no waves ever came from there, and instead they overwhelmed my defenses elsewhere) but from what I can tell there is no reward for attempting the levels on a harder difficulty ( except for "Victory Points" :read "Bragging Points" that don't affect gameplay at all) so just do a run on the lowest difficulty. Trust me, easy mode is far from easy, and it gives you a little more time to get your settlement up and running.

Would definitely recommend to fans of iron man games, hardcore rts fans, and masochists, especially when it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
