\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review should be read after you have read the negative review of JaylewAF11, which makes very interesting points on how this game can be ruined for you if you mismanaged some things while doing the campaign of this game. There are probably guides around the internet, but I decided I'd leave a review because why not.

Major flaws of the campaign :

The game allows you to spend points into a research tree to allow you to unlock new perks for your civilization. At the beginning, you will only have access to archers and wood technology, afterward you'll be able to unlock stone masonry and oil refining/foundry technology for better defenses/offense. Points are earned when successfully completed a map.

However, once you've completed a map after selecting a technology to unlock, you can't undo your decision, so if you waste points to enhance wood technology instead of rushing stone masonry and upgrading stone walls, you will be left at a fatal disadvantage as you progress in your campaign. Saying that it is a soft game over would be no understatement, so I HIGHLY recommend you unlock technologies with great care.

if you fail a mission, the technology that you wanted to unlock will not be permanent, so if you fail a map and realize that another technology would help you complete the map (like an upgrade on soldiers DPS instead of an economy upgrade, for example), you can change the technology, provided you had enough points to unlock the other technology you're after.

The map campaign is split in between RTS map (over 2/3 of the campaign) to some solo mission that you do with a hero (hero is never present in RTS map, only in solo mission). The solo mission will require you to do a lot of kiting and hit&run tactics, carefully pulling ennemies and will take a LOT of time, so be advised that you can't rush those maps. However, those missions should always be completed first when you unlock one as you progress on the map campaign, since they also provide points to boost your tech tree.

yes, I agree with a lot of negative reviews, you are doomed to fail some maps that have some severe ''gotcha mechanics'', and yes I felt frustrated to waste so much time building my base. You can be tempted to dump the game for a few days, but now that you know the gotcha mechanics, you might feel like me a need to take revenge and build better.

There was a mention that failing a map will provide you with less points to spend on your tech tree. I'm not sure if it's true or not, because I've never noticed a strong shortage of points to spend on my tech tree. Maybe it's because I've focused my techtree in such a ways that I didn't spend on useless techs, such as the inn or spending on wood technology,

but if I can reassure some of you, I've failed maybe 75% of the maps 1 to 3 times, and I unlocked everything I wanted to unlock, and before finishing the campaign. I still have 2 roaming waves and 2 RTS maps to finish the campaign, and all the points I will be earning is really just bonus.

Now I redid all the maps at max difficulty (800%), and because I plan my base-building to always have some sort of backup everywhere at all time, I can do them without much trouble.

Don't hesitate to comment on this review if you want some tips, I'd be glad to help out. Some quick tips are as followed :

- Rush the farm and quarry technology

- Rush stone masonry workshop to then be able to unlock soldiers and better walls afterward.

- There are 2 technologies that give you ''starter'' soldiers in addition to your starting archers. Make the archer patrol around your base while using the soldiers to clear points of interest (such as stone, iron, a cluster of wood).

- Always wall in your most exposed building with wood walls. They're not designed to prevent your building from being destroyed by zombies, it's designed to give you some additionnal precious seconds to rapidly plan a counter if it's a little wave being sent by infected towns, and also to tank while your archers/soldiers kill the zombies.

-Don't hesitate to use a single archer/soldier (archers move quicker) to lure zombies into a circle while the rest of your army shoot them down.

- Rush the technologies that give you supplies each time the train comes to your base. they will allow you to build a barrack quicker and being producing soldiers. (Be careful to not make a too large army in the beginning, however. Soldiers requires a salary that will drain your gold income. Making ten soldiers and use walls to tank for them is better than making 40 soldiers that will drain your entire economy.) Only train soldiers a couple days before an annouced wave, and then you can refund them when you put them beside a barracks once you don't need them anymore.

- The tesla tower is hands down the most useful defensive tower against large waves of weak mobs. Coupled with soldiers/snipers in towers or some wasp turret (a late tech so sniper/soldier first) that are ordered to only target the strongest zombies will take care of almost anything the game throws at you. Making 2-3 layers of stone walls will ensure that you can survive.


However, always expect that your first line of defense may fall, so don't hesitate to build a second line of defense if you see that your first line of defense will fall, construction is quick enough that by the time your first line of defense completely fall, the construction of the 2nd line of defense will be finished. Be sure to ungarrison your soldiers/snipers to allow them to reach the second line of defense before it's too late, though. I'd also advise to make your first line of defense a certain distance from your base so you don't get overrun easily.

Good luck!\t\t\t\t\t
