Personal Notes:

After playing the game more, it does grow on you however the forced Iron mode seems like something I will just always hate. I enjoy being able to go back and learn how I could've avoided a situation and having to go through a lot of hoops to revert a save is not really acceptable. I feel both an Iron man mode and normal should be added. It does at least allow you to save upon quitting so you can come back to a game. The game itself has little issues, no bugs to be seen really and the games art style is quite interesting and looks great. The game play loop is pretty simple and easy to keep up with and the building loop is nice and simple with no much complexity so easy to learn. The overall gameplay is great once you master what you should do and sadly it looks like if you don't follow specific paths when it comes to what you build and when it is likely you will fail, something I am not a fan of. You always need to be on alert as originally mentioned 1 zombie can be the end of you if you do not plan for it. The modding community can help alleviate these issues with mods that can make adjustments.

Overall I would now recommend the game, but be prepared to fail and have to fully restart unless you set up a method of saving save files in real time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrutal, unforgiving and relentless. You will die, over and over again.

Game play is great with only minor hiccups that are easily worked around or avoidable. If you love city builders and/or defense games this one is for you. There is a pretty basic tactic that once you figure it out, works in most situations. However, there is always the one zombie that throws a wrench in your whole match.

One zombie can ruin hours of game time in seconds. One crack in your defense or hole in your perimeter will absolutely be taken advantage of by the stray zombie or random zombie wave. They will find the holes, they will slowly walk through them and obliterate your town before you can even get back to defend it. Once your residence blocks are infected, there's almost no chance, get ready to start over.

There are no saves, there is no going back, just starting over... and the survival mode has no time warp, and the matches take several hours. 1 day in the game takes 1 minute 30 seconds and the general time for survival is 120 days. so three hours. There are 80, 100 and 150 day games, but the shorter times are much harder. You get the same number of waves, just in less time.

There are some bugs. Sometimes your troops can't reach a certain spot on the map that has zombies, or they get stuck getting there and can't get back. I wish there were squads or some way to keep units together while they hunt zombies. If you use the hunt hotkey, they will all split up and you have to clear the map manually, which is usually fine since there is often down time. The game is limited with maps and modes, still enough for me to log 244 hours at the time of writing this.

Overall, it's a great game, get it. Just be prepared for many hours of frustration, but they makes the wins that much more sweet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA base building, zombie killing, RTS game? Yes please!

The game is fun and polished, well worth the money for the hours of entertainment I had even with its flaws. One of the games I will no doubt come back to time and time again. I would recommend playing on easy at first, but even then, surviving hordes of the undead is not an easy task.
Mastery of this game requires total planning, you would think the ability to pause the game and issue commands during the pause would make it easy mode, but no. It has that ability because you really need to plan it out in slow motion in order to succeed. Nice delivery of a cool mechanic.
My biggest complaint is there isn't any DLC content, and no real updates in quite some time. Wanting more of this game being the biggest complaint is hardly a complaint though.
If you like RTS games and dig steampunk or zombie genres, definitely give this a shot!\t\t\t\t\t
