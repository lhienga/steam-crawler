\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've enjoyed this game a lot but cannot recommend it because of the solo missions they force you to do during the campaign. Imagine the Starcraft II missions where you play as a hero and don't have to build anything just basically kill stuff and reach the end. The solo missions are like an extremely watered down version of that. 95% of the time you only have your hero to kill stuff. It is extremely slow and tedious. You have to go around picking up collectibles that are often indistinguishable from other objects placed on the map which just adds to the overall frustration of completing the objectives. I was able to AFK several times while my hero just sat there killing a room of zombies so I could eventually progress. Catching even the very edge of an explosion, even from your own grenades, will instantly kill you. At any point a new room could contain a very fast and powerful unit that will kill you in less than two seconds. Dying of course meant having to start the entire level from the beginning, which is absolutely heartbreaking when you've spent twenty minutes killing everything in your way and hovering your mouse over every single object that might be a collectible. Overall just a truly awful experience.

The RTS portion of the game is fun though.\t\t\t\t\t

Didn't take long for it to become my favourite game made in the past 20 years. Would have been happy with this purchase if I paid $90 for it. Very fun, strategic game that has no cheap mechanics and no bugs thus far. The game only punishes you when you mess up not based on bad AI or something. The upgrade system is very unique for an RTS as well and is extremely well done just like the rest of the game. The game is very well optimised as well as I can pull 300 fps on a 1660 6gb (I have an Ryzen R9 paired with it though but not sure how cpu intensive the game is) on maxed settings. Game looks beautiful and sounds beautiful as well.

Don't listen to any of the negative reviews. Should have been in the conversation for game of the year.

100% recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

Fail to build a certain wall set up in time? Your screw
Fail to build up your army to protect your people? Your screw

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey Are Billions.

RTS hidden gem.

Now I've been into the whole zombie craze since before zombies became popular again around the release of Zack Snyder's 2004 Dawn of the Dead remake, Followed FrankieOnPcIn1080p since he made Battlefield 3 videos and played DayZ before it was even thought of as a standalone game.
To say I enjoy them as a concept would be an understatement, but in recent years, devs drive to diversify and evolve the genre I often feel something has been lost from the overall impact our creepy rotting friends have on us when they ampere on our screens. The Walking Dead focused more on the horrors other humans inflicted on one another (the comics are WAY better imho) - newer films such as The Army of the Dead, see zombies who have evolved into something else and are sentient, and a myriad of other films and games have been formed as off shoots featuring zombie like creatures who have been pumped up one way or another to make them more horrific, or predatory.
Quite literally leaving our good old shambles for dust - even the Resident Evil franchise have veered into creepy waifu territory with the big lass and her BDSM tendencies.

TAB on the other hand keeps quite nicely to my ideals of zombism. The fresh ones run at you - the old one's shamble and the rotten ones...rot at you? Yes they have their "specials" - which are actually very similar to Left4Deads special zombies, although I'm going to gloss over that comparison and offer my defence to the special classes of zomble we see in TAB.

Fatties - big hard to kill slow. Easy - that's McDonalds client-ell covered.
Harpy - my ex-girlfriends
Spitters - also my ex-girlfriends - the bile they spit is literal radioactive waste.

There are two other classes but I'll let the game keep those for you to find your self - i'll give you a hint though,WFF wrestlers aren't immune to zombie bites.

So what exactly is this game?

Well its a single player RTS where you are charged with the job of re-taking the world from the zombie hordes. There are weekly/monthly community challenges, there is a Story mode, there are several other free play modes and its still in development. I think I paid something along the lines of £14 when I picked the game up but it often goes on sale and can be had for around £20 - or $30 if you work in dirty colonial traitor money ....uh I mean freedom units of course.
There is obviously a difficulty curve, and you can set the game to be relatively as hard or as difficulty as you like - but even on the lowest settings, this thing will kick your ass and keep you coming back for more.


TAB sees you in the usual isometric RTS view directing your little pixel people around - it certainly looks a lot smoother today than on initial release, but I having always been a fan of this art style since the C&C days, so a little fuzziness didn't bother me. That being said, if you pick it up - zoom in, the detail is there to be had. I am led to believe the whole game is either set - "in the far future" or on an alternate timeline altogether - there's plenty of lore available in game for you to answer this one your self - I tend to gloss over that stuff while looking at the nice pictures and trying to avoid having my face bitten off.
Tech wise you start off with the common "wooden tier" move to stone and then on to metal/steel and eventually oil. All pretty standard RTS kind of stuff and all the common type of buildings.
All with a little bit of a steam punk vibe to them, as are the units, some of them become really rather awkward to produce/maintain despite their usefulness, be warned though, some of them are more of a hindrance at times, especially when zombies react to sound! Take Heed!

Story mode lets you choose from 2 hero's - one, a large imposing male character with a slow firing explosive weapon, the other a small faster paced female character with dual pistols - akin to Tanya Adams from the Red Alert era. These are used in intel and wealth gathering missions where you can infiltrate a long abandoned military instillation now over-un by the undead, so you can use these assets to boost your tech level for base building and unit cap for when you need to fight a horde.
Nicely giving you 3 different game modes in one leading to you needing to develop 3 different skill sets and keeping the pace of the game off beat so as not to grind your interest into the floor after hour upon hour of the same game play style.

Which is my first bug bear with the game. Not only is the difficulty of this game hard - I actually like that bit - but the progress can be woefully slow at times. Spending hours in a map building up your forces only to lose your entire town due to one weak wall or miss placed emplacement which you didn't realise was actually behind a tree, is crippling. The game actually punishes you for failing - so you receive a detriment to your loot when you do complete the area you are in, meaning the next one is harder still as your resources needed to advance are throttled. Reducing the difficulty has the same effect, either making the next area completely inaccessible to you or reducing your takeout making further progress more difficult.

Of this mechanic I am not a fan, I understand it, I just don't like it. I like that the game is difficult and doesn't hold your hand that much and is punishing, in a way that only the likes of FrostPunk and Tarkov seem to get, but I can't help but feel it squeeze's the player too much? More than a few times I've found myself abandoning a play through as I either don't have enough tech to unlock what I need for the next map, or I've unlocked the wrong tech accidentally and have no way to re-skill my tech tree. A somewhat minor gripe in terms of game mechanics that others will probably overlook.

My other gripe really is the AI path finding. It has improved from its original inexplicable dumb route finding somewhat , but it often fails spectacularly , causing the inattentive to lose neumours valuable units on even simple straight-line movements. Let me explain a few of these issues so perhaps if you pick this up you might be able to avoid them.

Despite it looking like there are paths around tents/houses most buildings, there most certainly are not. If you pin units in, they won't move. They won't tell you they can't move, and most likely you will forget they are stood behind said building and just be a useless recourse drain, possibly even hanging other units out to dry that were expecting backup that never comes because it's got its thumb wedged up its ass. They need, at least, 1 free square to move through, and they need to be joined, diagonals won't wash. Sometimes it can be infuriating.

Units will, and often do regardless of what you tell them, exit a guard tower on the wrong side of a wall if the route is shorter - running slap bang into zombles and getting nommed so keep an eye out for where they exit - sometimes they pin them self's between the wall next to your tower and the tower - then, as above, just go to sleep and butt fondle. Helpful.

Thirdly and most importantly, to me at least - their actual route finding when other units are around - or even zombies for that matter, just sucks. Sorry to whom ever created the path finding, but would it hurt you to put an AI loop in to stop the little bastards if they run into somthing mobile?
I'll expand. If you have 1+ units , and send them from point A to point B - and on route, they encounter a zombie or haven forbid a horde in their path. Rather than stopping and fighting, they will continue to run on the spot while the zombie disembowels them like some sort of kamikazi crazed mental patient intent on murdering them self's by way of zombie bite.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played the first few missions of the campaign and it's just really disappointing. From what I've played only about a third of the campaign missions are designed around the core design of the game, i.e. grow, expand, and defend your base. Another third seem to be a combination of a weird tower defence thing except with all the fun of a tower defence game removed. And the last, and by far worst, third is missions where you micro one of 2 heroes you select at the start of the campaign around a facility filled with zombies, very slowly picking up a group of 6 or so and then kiting them back as you kill them and then repeat ad nauseam. And while you're there you're also going to be playing "guess which of these background objects are the one you need to click on to get the currency to unlock stuff for the rest of the campaign". And boy I hope you don't die right at the end because there's no checkpoints so you're going trough it all over again YAAAAAAY! There's other quality of life issues that I experienced as well such as not receiving notifications when certain buildings are under attack, or an individual zombie being too small to easily see on the mini map while I'm looking somewhere other than my base, or not being able to easily re-start the mission when you've accidentally messed up; so then you can either live with your mistake, in my case accidentally sending a starting archer off to her death early on, or quit the mission and get a lovely permanent mark on the mission screen saying that you failed once.

This is all just really frustrating because the core RTS, base defence game-play is really fun and for all I know the quick game option on the main menu could be just what I want. But when I start a new RTS I'm going straight to the campaign to learn what building produces what resource / unit, and what each unit is good for. And quite frankly this campaign just feels like a really cheap sausage, one third meat and two thirds filler.\t\t\t\t\t
