\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey Are Billions is a game I've had a fair amount of fun with, but also a fair amount of gripes.

On the positive sides, its art style and direction is on point. It's a visual feast, varied enough for different locations to stand out while consistent enough to help immerse you in its world. The soundtrack is also perfectly placed, full of anvils and brass, with added ticking sounds which ramp up the tension as a horde arrives

The minute-to-minute gameplay is also pretty good. Edging your units forward to clear new land and often act as a living, shooting wall while you try and tetris your buildings into the available space while trying to take advantage of the most optimal positions keeps you active, and bracing for the next swarm - often followed by having to rebuild or, in the worst cases, having to reclaim parts of your colony - is enrapturing. Once you've settled into the controls and the general flow, it can hold you for hours, whether you want it to or not.

However, on a less positive note, I find that the voice barks from your units get quite repetitive and annoying after a while, especially if you're clicking around a lot. The tone of their voices is what irks me, especially the Rangers - the unit you'll most likely be using to scout and clear pockets of the undead - as their constant moaning about having to follow your orders does get under my skin. The same goes for some of the Soldiers' lines - yes, I am sure I want you to go there, now do it.

More pressingly, though, when it comes to the campaign, there are three things that I end up disliking. The first are the much bemoaned hero missions. In theory, they're a nice break from the standard colony building and swarm surviving, but in practice, they're overly long, quite frustrating, and full of hard to see items that you need to pick up. The second is that, after a while, the campaign does start to feel repetitive. The new and somewhat varied maps certainly help maintain a level of freshness, but aside from the occasional gimmick, the gameplay is very samey throughout, and while the minute-to-minute gameplay is solid, that doesn't necessarily prevent it from getting repetitive.

The third is arguably the most annoying of them. It's the lack of quicksaving/reloading, or to put it another way, the fact that the game forces you into an ironman mode, without any options to turn it off. To an extent, I understand the mindset behind this, but to a much greater extent, when you've been working on a colony for upwards of two or three hours, only to lose it right at the final wave and thus have to start again from minute zero, it is incredibly annoying.

Ironman modes should always be optional for a singleplayer campaign; they should not be compulsory. If you want to hide an achievement or two behind one, then fine, but forcing a player to play the campaign that way is not ideal. I understand the desire to prevent save scumming and cheesing, but not making ironman mode optional isn't the right way to go about it.

At the end of the day, They Are Billions is a game I've had a lot of fun with. Its aesthetics - both visual and audio - are excellent, and its gameplay is solid and fun. While certain aspects such as unit barks, hero missions, and compulsory ironman mode do annoy me - and in the case of the latter, lead to me occasionally putting the game down for the day before I'd really want to - I still recommend it to fans of colony sims, city managers, RTS, and zombies.\t\t\t\t\t
