\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like building up your base for an hour straight, only for a few zombies to ninja your way through and colossally screw up your game experience, then this is the right game for you.

This game has a major con - the auto saving mechanics. For some reason if you're base is screwed, there is NO loading an earlier save to pick up where you were perfectly fine. This is because the auto-save overwrites the previous auto save created. With that said, just starting up your base for 1 hour straight only for it to go to shambles because of a zombie that slipped through, or a huge horde that decided to accumulate on one side of the base, makes the game extremely unforgiving. Not to mention each Survival map is randomized so you cannot reset with the original map you played so you can take a different direction. How is the player supposed to learn how to play better if all the minuscule mistakes delivers you a roundhouse kick straight to the heart? There are a few people in the world who are into that sort of brute-force learning environment, but either way, the player ends up burning through 1-2 hours of his time only to be faced with a game-over scenario.

This game isn't a waste of money, don't get me wrong. But in the hands of the WRONG player, it is a HUGE waste of time. As months/years go by I revisit this game to try and beat the next Survival game for once, but 2 hours later I remember what made me quit in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginally I didn't enjoy this. I found it slow and very frustrating to lose an hour or several hours play because of one tiny mistake.

I had a video about this game pop up on my YouTube the other day and I remembered thinking how much I wanted to enjoy this game, so I gave it a second chance after watching someone else play it first. It completely changed my mindset on how to approach this game. I moved from being defensive and afraid of the zombies to a play style where I dominant the map and the main threat comes from me overextending myself too early, forgetting to patrol a new Tesla tower or being lazy and not setting up a wall. Yes, one zombie can totally destroy your settlement, however it is normally avoidable or you can take drastic measures and demolish nearby buildings, swoop in your nearby troops and try to contain it. That has worked for me a couple of times. In the early game though, you are probably a gonner.

There really is an amazing game under here once you learn how to play it. If you tried it and didn't like it, do what I did. Have some time away, then approach it again from a different point of view.

I'm about a quarter way through the campaign and really enjoying it.

Word of warning though, maps can take several hours, so it's not for the casual gamer. Recommended
