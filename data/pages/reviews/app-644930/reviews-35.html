\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game can be kinda unforgiving and cruel, but thats ok in the right doses.
I tried to get into this game a few times and it just didn't really grab me as I thought the graphics were a bit ugly and at the time of release it was a pretty bare bones experience. A few years later I tried it again and got hooked. I enjoyed myself and finally understood the mechanics and strategies. Feeling happy, I started the campaign mode, and kept trying it. This is where the game fell apart for me. I really started to enjoy it but the worse you do in the campaign, the more difficult it gets. So... the better you are at it, the easier it is. This is very backwards and discouraging. It punishes making a mistake and rewards already being a pro.

What I mean is that if you start losing missions you might as well start over the entire campaign, every single time. If you want to play and just have fun, do that, but if you want a sort of C&C campaign experience, look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

The first of which being the most egregious: No Autosaving/Manual Saving Feature, so every mission you play (which can often run well north of an hour+) is meant to be done in a single go and with no mistakes, otherwise if you die, you simply have to start all over again, and hope you don't screw up. The other, which is more of a personal discrepancy, but one that directly ties into the prior issue, is the fact that even on the easiest difficulty, the Campaign itself from the very beginning is overwhelmingly unforgiving, giving you little to no room for errors. I'll let your own imagination do the rest and fill in the blanks with everything I've previously mentioned. To put it bluntly: For as fun as this game could be and I want it to be? It ends up being more like a powderkeg of unrelenting rage, and unrealized hopes & dreams. I wish I could recommend this, but I can't in good conscience.\t\t\t\t\t
