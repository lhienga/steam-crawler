\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan I recommend this game after 1000+ hours play? I prefer to choose between Neutral and No, but if you are a dark soul type of player, then this game is for you. This is a rogue like RTS zombie apocalypse defense building game, you cannot save gameplay on survival mode, and when your base is overrun by zombie its over. Almost two hours for one survival game (80 days).

GAMEPLAY "The micro management in this game was insane"
Every building destroyed will create snowball effect of mess infection, even when you realized it and want to destroy it beforehand, most of the time its already too late, because you cannot even destroy structure if there is any zombie nearby which is stupid. Not to mention the warning system failed most of the time, combined with tofu like structure of the housing building make it even worse. I understand the housing was not supposed to be some kind of a tanky wall, but 2-3 hit on a tent and boom is a joke. The AI zombie in this game have 1 common goal: march toward the command center and destroy everything on its path, either through random or constant wave. The dev seem to be done and not making regular update anymore and I have to say that the control system was a bit clunky for advanced player, for example when you need to put 200+ or 1000+ sniper on towers it will be a pain in the ass. The zombie wave direction is so tricky to predict but generally it target the weakest choke point, sometimes the main wave will split like a cancer. Even so, I hate when people complained and blamed the RNG for their lose, its more like a skill issue in this game.

GRAPHIC "Nice selection of map with different difficulties, but not polished"
There are 6 main map on survival, but most of the unique map design only available for hero campaign mission which felt like a search object game with very slow, dubious and monotone gameplay. My favorite map design are peaceful lands and frozen land, so wonderful. But, the map design on some map felt crumpled and have problem with color, for example the caustic lands is too blue greenish and dark, make my eye uncomfortable for playing it too long, while the caustic land is too orangey and bright. The generated map condition on survival vary a lot sometime it inaccessible on some part or appeared to be broken and unbalance, which make every map unique and the only way to save or store it is to manually copy paste the save file. Blessed the game for allowing custom map design for the community to submit their creation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFlaws:

Game lacks save/load for no reason? Typically, "Ironman" games like XCOM forbid save/load because they want you to press forward with your losses rather than reload and try to do it perfectly. But They Are Billions restarts the mission as a whole, so you're not pressing forward with an imperfect record, you're just... replaying the entire mission from the beginning instead of from the point where you made a mistake. The first thirty minutes of the mission are not fun enough to replay over and over.

Hero missions are incredibly tedious. The sheer number of shots your hero has to fire to cut their way through the map is absurd, and the game of eye-spy required to find all the upgrades is miserable. These missions are not really optional, since you need research/empire to progress.

Overall, this looks and plays like a game released in the 90s, with all the attendant frustrations made sharper by the fact that it should know better by now.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Challenging and Addictive Survival Experience with Room for Improvement

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐¾

We Are Billions offers a unique and captivating take on the survival genre, delivering a challenging experience that will test your strategic thinking and resource management skills. While it has many strengths, there are some areas where the game falls short, preventing it from reaching its full potential.

One of the standout features of We Are Billions is its atmospheric post-apocalyptic setting. The game effectively captures the grim and desolate world overrun by hordes of infected creatures. The art style and sound design work in tandem to create a tense and immersive atmosphere that keeps players on edge throughout their playthrough.

The gameplay mechanics of We Are Billions are where the game truly shines. The core gameplay loop revolves around building and managing a thriving colony while fending off waves of infected attacks. The strategic decisions you make, from city planning to resource allocation, have a direct impact on your chances of survival. The game excels at creating a sense of tension and urgency as you try to balance expansion with defense, making every decision feel crucial.

The difficulty level of We Are Billions is a double-edged sword. On one hand, the steep learning curve and relentless waves of infected create an intense and rewarding experience for players who enjoy a challenge. However, this high difficulty can also be a source of frustration for those seeking a more relaxed or accessible gameplay experience. The lack of adjustable difficulty settings may deter some players from fully enjoying the game's potential.

The campaign mode in We Are Billions provides an engaging narrative and a structured progression system. The story-driven missions offer a sense of purpose and provide varied challenges to overcome. However, the campaign's pacing can feel uneven, with some missions dragging on while others feel rushed. Additionally, the lack of mission variety and repetitive objectives may diminish the replay value for some players.

While the core gameplay loop is addictive, We Are Billions suffers from a lack of variety in content and gameplay mechanics. Once you become familiar with the strategies and tactics that work, subsequent playthroughs can feel somewhat repetitive. Additional scenarios, maps, or gameplay modes could greatly enhance the game's longevity and replayability.

The user interface and controls in We Are Billions could benefit from further refinement. While the game is playable and functional, some aspects of the interface can be clunky and unintuitive. Certain actions and commands can be cumbersome to execute, and the lack of detailed information on certain mechanics may leave players feeling uncertain or uninformed.

In conclusion, We Are Billions offers a challenging and addictive survival experience with a captivating post-apocalyptic setting. The gameplay mechanics and atmosphere excel at creating a tense and strategic gameplay experience. However, the lack of adjustable difficulty settings, limited content variety, and interface issues hold the game back from achieving its full potential. If you enjoy demanding survival games and can overlook its shortcomings, We Are Billions will provide hours of intense and satisfying gameplay.
