\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood concept but the game is by far too unforgiving to allow itself to have a mediocre unit control system and unreliable warning system.

Lose a building, lose the game.

Health bars are, by design, only visible when pressing a key making micro-managing units in combat a pain.
Units are difficult to select when packed together forcing you to pause to move them around.
Fog of war is very restrictive early in the game (when you are the most vulnerable).
The warning system that is supposed to tell you when a unit is under attack is completely unreliable.

As a result of those poor design choices/bugs, you end up having to zoom in to manually control units in combat on one side of the map while constantly checking the mini-map to make sure no enemy entered the view range of a unit on the other side of the map.

A few seconds of inattention (literally) and you've lost. Game is playable but only enjoyable if you like to tear your hair out.

Only giving it a thumbs-up because, even though those issues really suck the fun out of this game for me, it is a decently made game with fun potential for others.\t\t\t\t\t
