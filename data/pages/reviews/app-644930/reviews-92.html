\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrustratingly close to good, but I can't say it respects the player's time. If you really want to give this game a try, maybe pick it up on sale (but don't say I didn't warn you about how much of a time sink this is).

I bought this on sale for $15, and though I think there's a good game trapped inside, I've found that only about 15% of my time playing the game has been interesting, while the rest is just going through the motions. I feel like I paid money to have my time wasted.

TLDR:
1. This game doesn't respect your time. Its slow pace makes you slog through your build order, only to be rewarded at the end with a very brief, minimally-interactive siege, over and over again.
2. This game is missing quality of life features which have been standard fare since 2010
3. The developers have publicly stated an anti-modding position, and actively deter the community from implementing said quality-of-life fixes, or any other improvements

----

The core problem with They Are Billions is that victory is passive and defeat is anticlimactic. Gameplay can be divided into two sections: preparing your defenses and defending. Preparing your defenses is always a going through the motions process. You move through your build order, maybe scout a little bit, and press out to the nearest resource nodes. This simple gameplay loop can take tens of minutes on some missions.

After your opening build order, you get to the waves of enemies. If you successfully fend them off, your reward is more of the same gameplay loop. If you fail to fend them off, you have to start over - and go through more of the same gameplay loop. Because of the density the game encourages you to build at with population-based win conditions, there's no incentive - or room, really - to build fallback positions and tiered defenses. Your best bet is always to build one set of thick walls and crowd as much DPS behind them as possible. This makes sieges highly uninteractive.

----

That said, there is some enjoyment to be had in this simple gameplay loop, even if it takes more of your time than it has any right to, but there's not much variety. It gets stale quickly. The individual moving parts are all good, but together, combined with the pacing, none of them feel fun to interact with.

----

Additionally, this game is missing some basic quality of life features:
- There are no nested hotkeys for unit selection, so you have to use the mouse
- Not all functions are rebindable and not all keys can be bound
- Unit pathing needs polish
- Actions can only be queued homogenously (e.g. you can't queue "move" and then "patrol." you have to wait for the "move" command to resolve and then go to your unit and order "patrol")
- Patrol paths can't form closed loops
- The tech tree nodes and unit cards omit important information\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrilliant game. The standard campaign missions are great, there is a memorable one with a single bridge choke-point and the biggest wave I have ever seen comes at you. A massive river of zombies comes at you and it is glorious when you defeat it.

There is one glaring issue with this game: the solo hero missions.
This is a horde game and you get missions with one troop with a slow pistol and you have to search the entire map for items a few pixels in size because you need them for research points.

Playing one character is the worst, here is what you do for 25 minutes:
Shoot Shoot Shoot
Move back
Shoot Shoot Shoot
Move back
Shoot Shoot Shoot
One zombie down

Now do that for the other 20 zombies in the room and you have cleared ONE ROOM.
And these missions are buggy too, enemies will hit you through the wall and you are very fragile with nothing to help if you screw up. And if you do screw up, you have to start all the way from the beginning.
"What were they thinking?"

Anyways the rest of the campaign is great. This is one of those games in which you will often lose the first try but then you are able to build defenses and troops to counter that specific mission.

But the worst thing is that I want more and there isn't any!
I want more scenarios, more maps, more enemies, more units, more defenses, more tech progress, more game modes.
There are other games copying TAB and mostly they are doing a terrible job.\t\t\t\t\t
