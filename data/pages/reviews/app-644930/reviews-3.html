\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe load game button and delete game buttons are right next to each other. It doesn't ask for conformation. I got near the end of the game twice and deleted my game by accident twice. Making back up saves is difficult bordering on impossible. I'm not the only one with this issue, when I googled I found lots of people with the same problem.

It comes down to bad game design and philosophy. It seems like the creator wants to control how you play the game, probably doesn't want people save scumming. I feel like I wasted a bunch of my life trying to play a game where the creator is more interested in being hostile towards you and controlling how you play the game than they are interested in making something that's fun, enjoyable, and feels worth the money.

I know some people love games where it feels like the creator is out to get you and control you. If you like souls-like games and rogue-likes and you've always wished for a real time strategy game that uses the same philosophy, then this game is for you, as long as you don't mind potentially loosing all your progress due to poor design choices. If you don't meet those specific criteria then this isn't worth your time or money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis COULD be an amazing game. I really appreciate the difficulty and love tower defense style games. My biggest issue is the process it uses to bait you into building up defenses in areas where it says they are coming from, then ACTUALLY having them attack from quite literally any other direction. It is quite frustrating. I am not certain why this advance warning system exists if it is only in place to force you to waste all your early game resources only to have the enemy come from a nautical direction that isn't the one being shouted by the voice-over. This feels like the game creator is simply lying to create difficulty instead of making something that feels natural. I personally don't appreciate being blatantly lied to in my every day life. Surely i shouldn't be expected to give it a pass while trying to relax. I just wish for once someone would use intelligence instead of deceit to solve an issue. I wish this game like MANY others would stop calling broken game designs a feature. If you can get past the irritation of having HOURS of your precious game time wasted playing the same map over and over to learn where the enemies ACTUALLY come from or want to waste any surprise for yourself and watch videos of where they come from before hand, I HIGHLY recommend this game. It is at its core a decent game. Most everything works as intended. There is a REALLY bad glitch where enemies can literally WALK through the walls and closed doors in the missions where you play as the hero. I am also not referring to Harpies. I am sure this will be picked apart but luckily I never intend on looking at this again. Just wanted to leave an honest review.

Long story short. If you have patience for losing a LOT of hours because of a broken game mechanic then this game CAN be a very fun use of your time. It makes your heart race and never really gives you enough information to ever be really comfortable until you have a big beefy base. It allows for may different ways of getting the same job done for a simple layout.
If you value your time and get irked when you do as the game instructs and then are punished catastrophically for it, then play this game at your peril. It WILL make you wonder why you are playing it. I am LITERALLY only playing it at this point because I cannot return it and am a completionist. By the time you realize that the mechanic you have to trust the most is the thing that will get you killed it is far beyond 2 hours of your life gone.\t\t\t\t\t
