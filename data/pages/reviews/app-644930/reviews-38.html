The only way for me to make sense of the people who complain about the difficulty is that they set the game's difficulty on hardcore, and then complain that it's hard. Just put it on easy and it's manageable. You can still have to redo an entire mission every now and then because of a slight oversight, or because you didn't prepare for the specific enemy/wave that was coming (no way to know until they're there), but since the process is enjoyable I don't think it's that much of an issue.

The absolute worst decision was the pixel hunting in the hero mission. It's very poor design and never should have made it into the game.

Other than that the progression is well designed, with an interesting tech tree and something new in almost every mission.
Very warm recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan't recommend this game mainly due to the horrible campaign mode.

I really don't enjoy spending 2-3 hours of slug paced mission design (RTS, Swarm and Hero missions) which are not hard, but over punishing. Did your rangers patrol happen to be 2-3 pixels off and a runner sneaked through? Well thats 30 minutes wasted as you will not recover from the swarm, and now your campaign points are reduced because of a mistake you had no control over, which pretty much means you either go with a flawed save, or you have to retry the ENTIRE CAMPAIGN FROM SCRATCH just because of that 1 mistake.

You wont be able to get 100% of the tech tree complete, more like 70-80% roughly, and theres no refunding the points if you start a campaign with what you locked in, meaning that if you pick something from the tech tree that is not useful in the next mission, or is generally not meta, some missions later on will become near impossible to beat. Its not hard or a challenge at that point anymore, its a pointless chore.

Hero missions are slow paced Attack command dancing. They are not hard enough so you have to think a bit about how to move and aggro enemies, but not easy enough where you can just brainlessly run through them while watching a show on the side. They're the perfect pace where you can not do anything else on the side to properly spend your time, nor are the missions hard or even engaging enough to keep you awake.

Swarms are a mix of both in terms of fun and difficulty. You don't have nearly enough information on how the swarms are going to attack, and a single small mistake in placing walls and units means that your whole campaign score is gonna be flawed and you are gonna get punished. Fail once, replay the whole campaign from the start.

The campaign punishes you for no reason without keeping you engaged in any of the missions. The fun in each mission starts around 15-20 minutes in, and if you ♥♥♥♥ up a single micro or macro, its either a mission restart, or entire campaign restart.

Don't play the campaign, play the side modes. Thanks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is kinda terrible, on a lot of levels. The audio is the WORST. The voice acting is atrocious, especially the female hero. She sounds like somebody sat their sister down at the computer and said "Here, read this. . . ok, now make it SEXY." There's no unit balance. You're supposed to use different units for different roles, but the Rangers you should scout with are useless after day 5, even if you get them promoted. Soldiers don't have the range and damage necessary to put up with any of the later game infected. You end up just spamming snipers because the "end game" units take forever to get to and are too expensive to use en masse. Then you get to the part where the story doesn't even begin to make sense! You're retaking the world, but nothing that you've learned from any of the previous missions is available and you start from zero with your thumb up your butt EVERY TIME?! None of the units, research, or equipment you just fought so hard to master are even accessible? I still have to build ALL the tech buildings and then do the research AGAIN? C'mon, use that damned train for something other than screwing up my unit placement! The pathfinding is non-existent, you'd better be watching your groups like a hawk or they'll just fall apart. Units can't walk around each other even when given explicit movement commands, and will push through your other units rather than going around and will create actual clogs in your gates, which is super helpful when you're trying to respond to an incoming attack. This also makes it really difficult to try to rotate the guys off the front to try and keep them alive, since they'll just turn around, slam into the guy behind them, and then just walk in place while getting murdered. Want to queue an attack order because the "Chase" function seems prescripted and you don't want your entire platoon going that direction? Good luck, if you don't keep redoing the order every 20 yards the guys at the back will just forget what they're supposed to be doing and stand there until something runs up behind your army to pick them off.

All of that could be forgiven as simply choices made by the developer for gameplay reasons, but then it turns out the gameplay is crap. There are several mechanics regarding aggro but when you get to the later levels they all go completely out the window. Playing the last couple of levels is a complete crapshoot since when the swarms hit they may or may not drag along on of the biggest nasties in the game, and if they do and you're still early on you may as well just bend over and kiss your ass goodbye. The first time you meet mutants it's anybody's guess as to what links the mutant that's "guarding" the forest above where the only iron accessible to your starting location is, some fights he'd charge me 30 seconds in as soon as I started clearing the infected from around my Command Center, 2 or 3 times I had to go hunt him down around day 40, but I never did figure out what the hell he triggered off of. Want to build some defensive towers to give your units a little protection? HAH! You're screwed now, for some reason that alerted/pulled mobs from half a screen away. A single infected can wipe out your entire colony? Your colonists outnumber the little twit 100 to 1 and are all carrying hammers, pickaxes, axes, or other tools, not to mention in this would why the HELL wouldn't you start training people to use a weapon right about the same time they're learning to walk? Nope, they run and hide in their houses/buildings while the infected whack away at them, without so much as poking a sharp stick at them through the door, until the infected get through the "barriers" (since every single structure in this stupid thing can be knocked over by tapping on it gently, because after all it's not like you knew they were coming or anything) and BAM! your colonists are also now instantly infected, oddly even the ones that you watched scream and hightail it off the map. Also, I get that you're trying to build in "consequence" but having no capacity for a mid-level save/restore when you can spend hours getting to a particular point on a level just to find out that you don't know what's coming and haven't a snowballs chance in hell of even slowing down that final swarm, all so you can go back and completely start over. . . even though you're going to spend the first 45 minutes rebuilding your base exactly like you had to the last 4 times because the start never varies (except for when you magically pull mobs that you're not within 5000 yards of and they wipe your entire colony and you get to start over again).

Put it all together and this game just isn't fun, it's frustrating, I kept at it because I refused to let it beat me, even when it was straight up cheating (build too fast and suddenly there are extra mobs? the hell? swarm spawn is "random" but always seems to magically come from whatever direction you're least defended, I was actually able to manipulate this based on where I built walls and defenses) and I'm more stubborn than that, but this just ends up being another one of those games where there were great ideas but really poor execution. I'm so pissed I actually took the time to type all of this >.< AND PROOF READ IT so I don't look like an idiot.\t\t\t\t\t
