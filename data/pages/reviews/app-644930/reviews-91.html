\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is your SIM-city meets Left for dead..

Well.. Kinda?

If you're a fan of Starcraft/Warcraft/Command and Conquer style RTS games it is pretty reminiscent of those types of games. Now you just get a splash of ZOMBIES included to deal with.. Seems easy right?

Positives:
+ City Management is cool and fun in it's steampunk style.
+ You get to try out different types of units to protect your city
+ Main Campaign is neat with it's unique hero style.
+ Price tag is reasonable (in my opinion) for a game of such quality.

Negatives (if any)
- It can take quite some time for you to get through the survival maps.
- Game is unforgiving if you make mistakes so you need to learn from the different environments and adapt on the go.
- For lower end systems it can cause significant lag when the horde comes for you at the end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was on the fence about buying this game due to the negative reviews. After buying it, I thought they were exaggarting but I slowly come to the realization that they are not. The core game play is somewhat interesting and fun but the game design, of the campaign at least is just terrible.

1. Are hero missions that bad? Yes they are. Non-build missions are a common way to RTS to improve the pacing of the campaign- but it doesn't work here. Here they are boring, time wasting and doesn't even change the pace. You are doing the same thing you did in the normal game except you only have one soldier to move now, and also no buildings.

2. Siege missions are even worse in term of game design. It's hard to believe such a stupid game mode made it to the final version. The only reason they don't get as much hate as the hero missions are that they are shorter and don't actually require any interaction from the player so you can go do something else until they are done. The only thing that gets tested is your build, which you can't change anyway... this is so dumb.

3. Save/Load are missing for a reason. The goal is clearly to make the player anxious about the zombies, because how much you will lose if they got in. Basically, the game use the threat of removing your progress as incentive. And it works, like it or not.

4. The problem with that is that the game (The campagin) is also extremely unfair. Yeah, I initially had games when I was careless, didn't take the threat seriously enough, and I lost big because of that. When you the threat comes once in 45 minutes, it's easy to forget how dire the situation is. I figured that the people who complained were just like that, failing to take the risk seriously. This isn't the case though. Some missions that you will never suspect to be harder than others will reveal themself, after 2+ hours, to be impossible with the build you have (And don't give me that link to the youtuber who did it. I was coming in blind). There will be no warning, no tips, the game is just that disresepctful of your time.

5. Survival mode is not a rushed, untested mess like the campaign (which I assume the developers never wanted to do and where obliged to, not caring that this is the experience new players will get). It's far from being smart though. The key problem with the base game is the pacing. You may get tricked by the periodic waves, but the game is really only about the the final wave-- it's ramping up the difficuly few times over. So you play several hours of slow, low paced game with occasional wave for distraction just to experience a 5-10 minutes of the actual challange.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis current zombie fad has lasted a long time indeed, but in recent years it has grown increasingly stale. The presentation of zombies has become contrived and tired. The decline is likely terminal, but every now and again we will see a zombie game that does something fresh with its rotting protagonists. They Are Billions is one such example. It is an RTS, base-building and tower defense game, which makes it a fairly unusual combination for a zombie game. The world has ended, humanity has mostly turned and only one fortified city remains alive. The new emperor of this last bastion of humanity dreams of reclaiming the world. The player, a general in his employ, is tasked with leading this offensive. Let's see how this plays out.

The core of the game is its survival mode. The player needs to develop a colony, while exploring and clearing the zombie infested environs and resisting the swarms of zombies that constantly approach this little hub of life. The game offers some interesting units and various fortifications and automated defenses to see the task through. The struggle is to explore and claim the area around the colony fast enough, to be able to afford the necessary defenses before the zombies attack. It makes building up a base an extremely engaging activity. The player is constantly on the lookout for ways to expand, maximize growth and optimise defenses. It is not an easy task. This game can be brutally difficult and very unforgiving. It's not just the big zombie swarms. Simple mistakes can cost a run just as easily. Say a lone straggler passes through your defenses unnoticed and infects a house, cue in the entire colony getting overrun by infected colonists shortly thereafter. The maps are semi-random and there is, thankfully, enough variance to allow for different tactics and approaches, keeping things fresh across the numerous restarts.

In addition, the game offers a fairly long campaign mode where you can play the story of the empire's general. The campaign consists of three types of missions. There are Survival missions that play largely like the survival mode described earlier, though no longer semi-random. Swarm missions, where you are defending a fort against an attacking swarm. Finally, Hero missions. Those offer a more tactical experience where you control a hero unit and search various locations for artifacts. Unfortunately, the campaign is a rather uneven experience. Swarm and Hero missions simply needed more work and some fresher ideas to make them interesting. Thankfully they are rather short. As such, it is Survival missions that end up overstaying their welcome. Initially, they are pretty great, but as they get harder, longer and remain frustratingly faithful to the survival mode's rules, including the lack of proper saves, they eventually become tiresome. Having to replay the exact same mission from scratch several times, when there is dozens more ahead of you, is not the most engaging of experiences. It's a slog and a grind.

Moving on to the presentation of the game, things return to decisively more positive territory. The game doesn't really justify its steampunk aesthetic narratively, but it sure does work great anyway. Combined with its bold, cartoony colours the game creates a beautiful, visually satisfying experience. The music is also fitting and generally enhances the atmosphere. This atmosphere, the mood the game generates, is one of its strongest elements. The high stakes involved are palpably felt at all times. Everything synergises to increase the feeling of urgency and imminent threat and keep the player on edge. The greatest star here is arguably the game's antagonists. The zombies. The zombie swarms in particular are presented like a force of nature, as an actual wave of rotting flesh smashing against your defenses, threatening to consume all in its path. When the swarms get near, you will feel the unease and fear of this impeding doom. The game constantly reminds you that they are coming and that they are... well not quite billions, but it will sure feel that way.

They Are Billions was a very interesting experience. As someone who is generally tired of the zombie genre I didn't expect to like it as much as I did. Thanks to an engaging primary gameplay loop and an excellent presentation though, the game won me over. It is a shame that the campaign ended up getting a bit tedious. I would have liked to see what this game could do if it could shed away its survival mode's trappings and transformed into a more diverse RTS. Several missions in the campaign tried to freshen things up, it just wasn't enough. Either way, I do recommend this game to all fans of survival, tower defense, RTS and, unsurprisingly, zombie games. I would recommend to take smaller sips though, a few maps per week. Attempting to binge this game may end up backfiring.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTemporary review

I'm 7h, so I feel I can give a good opinion until I finish my review.
TaB is your standard base building isometric fare with a twist - billions of enemies.

Is it good?

So far, the gameplay has varied a lot. Levels have a 'retry' counter and that's an indicator that the game is a bit too "puzzle based" for me. If you HAVE to build x, then y, then z in that very specific order at specific times, it's no real fun. Sadly, so far the game has just thrown puzzle after puzzle at me, outside of the exploration levels.

Tech

Nothing to write home about. GFX are mediocre, animations not much better, I like the reference to SC and C&C but it only goes so far. The use of animation tools instead of animators is noticeable in the cutscenes, and the art is 'oh-hum' - just look at the store page banner for an idea of what lays ahead.

There are no hiccups and after countless patches during EA the game is well developed, but you can see where they had to go in and fix this or that - it's not that seamless. I get this clear sense they had an idea for 'something cool' - went with it, got successful, did their best to deliver despite their lack of experience. It's honorable, but it's not going to blow minds.

Conclusion

It's really nice to finally play a game that's not just a 'banished' clone. Sadly, I'd have liked to see a lot more options in terms of base building flexibility - as ultimately some levels WILL require to have a certain wall to the left and a certain unit type to the right. The tech tree is just there to progressively unlock structures.

In conclusion, a very tentative thumbs up until I dig more into this one, or just give up entirely. There are dozens of new games everyday and your time is limited - might as well play the best of the best. This feels - in between.
