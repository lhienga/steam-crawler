\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game relies on precognition because it does not supply enough information to make informed decisions, then requiring restarting from the very beginning wasting hours of time. It lacks many common quality of life features a modern RTS should have. Units require constant micromanagement, lacking any semblance of intelligence (are we sure they're not the zombies?).

The campaign research tree, despite looking open ended, has a set of predefined technologies you need to research to successfully complete each mission and you only get a limited number of points that you can't respec once set, forcing replay of the entire campaign if you weren't already an active informed player of Survival maps. Many technologies are traps: a small bonus to an ultimately inconsequential stat or an extra early unit when instead you could have spent points toward unlocking a required building or unit.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Good concept, but lacks a lot of QoL feature, which make it annoying in a while. I really wanted to clear it all, but can't stand it anymore.
Pros:
- lot's of zombies;
- hordes of zombies;
Cons:
- 3h into a match an some stupid ♥♥♥♥♥♥ make you do it from the start (no saving mechanism, like wtf?).
- Super annoying "hero"missions, why do they even exist? They are not story, but like a point-click adventure without the adventure part.
- Random selection of all building globally - double click selection made me destroy my walls in the whole map multiple times.
- Building stuff is annoying in general. There's not that many buildings, but they are scattered through multiple tabs, which are hidden in 'command center'. Why not just do a building tab right away? Why make me click 2x more to build than neccessary? Sounds like not a serious thing, but when you reach 2k population and need to build 100 more houses and 20 farms, all that clicking starts to build up.
- pathing is bad/weird. some clear spaces cannot be walked through - killed my units multiple times. Units constantly collide with each other and try to shove one another around without any success of reaching the destination. Simply bad experience :(
- Glitchy sound. Game has to be muted permanently, because sound keeps looping randomly and repeating itself. Some game features are lost without sound - announcements that something happens (though according to others those are often too late anyway)
- Very limited way to automate units (barely any), in a game of the numbers like TAB, i'd expect to have squad formations, evade/avoid behavior, expantion planning; well you don't get any of those; instead you will spend hours micro-ing every unit to scatter (because in group clumps they can barely move cause of colliding with each other), running around with faster units to juke/kite zombies;\t\t\t\t\t
