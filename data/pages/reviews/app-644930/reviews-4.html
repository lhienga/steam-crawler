\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLot of good with this game, lots of bad as well.
Very punishing colony building RTS that pulls no punches on any of the difficulties.
The campaign has its issues.
You arent able to respec your tech tree that you develop through it. This can lead to minor or major softlocking.
The "Hero" style missions are VERY tedious. Pulling one or two mobs out of a room at a time 10-25 times is brutal.
There are alternate routes and traps for you to use but on a first playthrough, i could see them all being missed.
But the game's core is very rewarding and engaging.
Discover unique maps, uncover resources, strategic points and race against the clock.
Zombies are all over the map and they all need to be dealt with the correct way and not let inside your colony.
The game is over if a single zombie sneaks in, unless you have a gargantuan military.
Some of the later levels can take about 1-4 hours depending on how well you play and hit the pause button.

7.5/10
Great core, but could have benefited from WAY more updates. Id recommend survival mode before heading into campaign.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell used frustration as part of game design can make for a fun experience, but this crosses a line for me. When you combine the lack of mid mission save with the game's apparent zero tolerance for mistakes without descending into a cascading fail state, you just end up losing hours of time replaying mission and hoping this time you don't miss one tiny red dot on the minimap. I'm hitting a point with some levels where I'm just repeating the same memorized build order over and over as I restart.

Additionally I don't think I've ever played an rts that makes you jump through hoops to start making basic units quite like this. You have to find and gather notable amounts of stone before you can make more than the handful of free units it gives you. You'll likely need iron to make units capable of defending against the first major waves. With above zero margin of error and a pressing time limit, most often this means I needed to replay levels just to get the map knowledge of where these were located so I could just immediately rush them.

The game is fast turning into the game equivalent of hate-watching. The combo of an rts and town builder pushes a lot of dopamine buttons for me, and a love a challenging game, but at this point I feel like I'm beating the game out of spite. Doubtless I will get better at managing the threats, but the first 10 hours has been miserable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are a lot of oversights that makes this game tedious and kind of frustrating to play. For one, there is no ability to restart a mission in the middle, so if you mess up and want to restart, you have to either wait to lose or try to recover from the mistake. Second, there is no ability to speed up time, and when you have several missions that require you to simply do the same thing over and over (build buildings to increase colony size), it becomes very tedious to just wait it out. Third, there are many missions where you have a main task, and the secondary task is to bring your colony size to a high number, but oftentimes you finish the main task while your colony size is very small, so you're just spending days (in the game) just making your colony bigger, which is very tedious and boring).

There are positives though, the game has a very neat mix of mission types (some you're building a colony and defending it against the hordes of zombies, others you're playing as the character and exploring areas), it's got a good story and art style, I just hope that if they make a sequel that they address the issues the game has. It has a lot of potential, it just comes up a bit short.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI suppose after 2,000+ hours the Devs deserve a review.

The campaign is okay. As others mention, the single player missions are not a good time. The other levels are a fun way to learn the game.

The game truly shines when you dig into the community maps. There are some very talented folks out there making maps so the fun never ends. The ability of mapmakers to adjust game setting like upgrading units makes re-playability endless.

Just a warning you have seen in other reviews: This game is HARD. The hardcore-style of no saves is part of what makes the game so fun. I probably have a win percentage of well under 5% but I am always excited to begin again and fix what went wrong.\t\t\t\t\t
