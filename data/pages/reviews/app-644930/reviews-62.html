I took BlackOut's advice from their comment, gave it another shot, and I take back a lot of what I said.

The campaign is actually a lot of fun once it gets going, and I found myself missing some of the things you get in the campaign that aren't in survival.

However, running the Hero missions on Easy still wasn't quite enough to make them tolerable.
So, I downloaded a trainer and used it to max out the Hero Experience.
Now, a fully capped out Lady Hero was much more my speed.

I still recommend the game, but I recommend more of it now.


"Only play the Survival Mode. I do not recommend the Campaign.

After 170 hours of Survival mode, I decided to play the campaign. The RPG style, spec out your Colonies/Armies thing sounded fun.

The first two missions were fine, general intro levels.

Then I got to my first "Hero Mission."

Oh Boy. Just what every RTS player wants, a tedious pixel hunt for resources while having to micro every. single. attack. of your "Hero," or they get merc'd by a few runners.

And the best part: it's forced Iron Man; no saves, no checkpoints, only pain. ?"

Just spent 30 minutes clicking your way through every. single. zombie. only to get blown up by a mine that only gets triggered by zombies? Too bad! Suck on that noob trap.
I'm the dumb one here. Fine.

Another 30 minutes. Have the hero search a case for resources. They get stuck in a wall. Soft locked. Wonderful.

Third try, another 30 minutes, back to the mines. Release the chubbies, run far enough that they hit the first mine, don't realiz there's still a chubby hurtling at you behind the explosion graphic, and get blown up once it triggers the second mine.

I am uninstalling this game.

At least the RT+P parts are fun. So when you fail, you learn, and you don't feel that bad about starting over cause it's still fun to play.

This Hero Mission, though. Tedious nonsense.

If you get this game, stick to Survival. The "Campaign" has a superfluous "g."\t\t\t\t\t
