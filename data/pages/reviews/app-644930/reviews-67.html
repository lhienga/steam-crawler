\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite my hours played I do not think this is a good game. At first it seems pretty good but the more I played the more I realized that the designers made, in my opinion, some very poor design decisions.

The good:
-Interesting design making the actual space you put your buildings a resource
-Campaign research system was interesting
-Art style, while cheesy, is pretty well executed

The bad:
-Voice work and writing are very bad, and I don't mean so bad its good, I just mean bad.
-Units are sluggish to respond and have pretty poor path finding. Controlling your units is often frustrating.
-Lots of gotcha moments and ambush style difficulty spikes. There where many times I lost a mission due to a wave being much, much bigger than previous waves, or from a direction I had no time to prep for, which wouldn't be so bad if not for...
-The mission length is waaay to long. Hour plus missions that could end because of a cheep shot the game did not prepare you for. Even after playing for over a 100 hours and adapting to, shall we say, the games unorthodox design choices, after an hour plus long mission it was still a roll of the dice as to whether or not I lost because of something I couldn't have anticipated.

In short, good idea, middling execution, but absolutely abysmal level design and poor difficulty balancing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first PC games were RTS games like Age of Empires II and Starcraft. Needless to say I burned out on RTS games by my mid 20s, and aside from 4x games, strategy isn't really my thing. The genre has become vast and deep with ideas, but They Are Billions takes all the best aspects of classic RTS and boils it down to what matters. One or two infected buildings could mean the game is over and you have to start over. You cannot load a game after you're wiped out. This game is hard. But once you get the hang of it, you realize your only true enemy are the hordes. Like so many online matches of Age of Empires decades ago, it's all about the eventual rush of enemies into your personal space like a house cat that just downed an energy drink. Simple, but effective.

Did I mention you'll actually want to do the campaign in this game?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the few games where every decision you make feels consequential. You have to juggle space, different types of resources, build orders, wandering enemies and unit micro in a match, and a satisfying tech tree between missions (in the campaign at least). Definitely recommend for those who enjoy management and strategy.

But be aware mistakes, even small ones, can kill you and end potentially a long attempt at a mission. The game's tension is high, so if you're looking to relax while making a pretty town, you may not like this. I find half of the campaign missions, which are either place units and watch them fight a wave, or controlling a hero character through a map, to be tedious. So even if you lower the difficulty of those ones to easy, they will take up some time here and there. Also the tech tree is rather opaque. While the game doesn't tell you this, you HAVE to tech up to farms and better houses, or certain missions become literally impossible to complete.\t\t\t\t\t

It feels more like puzzle game than strategy game. But only way to find solutions is to fail and waste hours of your time to play the same mission again.\t\t\t\t\t
