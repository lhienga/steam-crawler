\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to lead with stating that this is not a bad game.

The art's good, and it's got several really neat mechanics, like the intermittent 3/4 perspective heroic adventure sections which are a nice change of pace from an RTS. The execution, however, is lacking.

My biggest frustration is the timing. Missions with a stated length, such as most of the campaign missions and all of the survival missions, are not 'complete within this time period' but rather 'you must play this long'. Combined with no way of speeding up the in-game clock, it rapidly got boring once I'd sorted out solid resource management and had a good defense. The mission clock's tagged to real-time too, so you can't just speed up the gameclock to hurry it up. Adding an extra half hour to a map that I've already solved is just frustrating, and leads to 'playing' the game while alt-tabbed into browser windows entertaining myself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give this a "meh" instead of thumbs up or down, but I would say overall this game is too poorly designed and unpolished to get a positive. Just know what you're getting into before you buy it, I guess.

-No mod support whatsoever. All the glaring issues with the game could be fixed through 3rd party hacks and stuff, but as far as the devs go they don't give a , in fact they actively made it difficult to even try to do so. Huge middle finger to the community.

-The campaign is the most underdeveloped, horribly implemented, godawful piece of garbage I have experienced in any recent memory. It's like they didn't even playtest the thing. Why is it bad? Take a sneak peak:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k7Ii_3JBnh8&list=PLH39NVNVJEDt0S0_aWTZi_fQhAAyMUTKZ&index=26

Super fun and engaging gameplay as you can see. Go make a sandwich while the game plays itself. Absolute disgrace. The hero missions are an absolute boring slog as well. +The free play mode where you play randomly generated maps is decent. That is really the only selling point of this game, and YMMV but it gets pretty boring pretty quick. It's like if Starcraft had no campaign or multiplayer and was just scenario maps. If this single gameplay mode interests you, then go ahead and buy the game, it's enjoyable. If you're looking for literally anything else, steer clear. They abandoned this game long ago.

Just as a note, my 329 hours is nowhere near legit, I often afk or leave games running for days at a time so this is heavily inflated. I think I played this maybe 40 hours total, and a lot of this was just grinding my teeth through the bad parts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly believe that this is one of the worst games I have ever played in my life. I've had more fun playing flash games online, that's how bad this game is.

Here is a Dark Souls analogy for people who are familiar with the series.

Imagine that while exploring an area in Dark Souls, there are only 3 enemy types, and they're copied and pasted into every map.

Now imagine that every enemy in the area can instantly kill you, so you're rewarded for pausing the game and slightly changing the angle of your character every 3 seconds.

Now imagine that there's a shallow resource-management minigame that you're forced to play in the background while dodging enemies, and if you lose focus on a particular resource, you will silently fail, only realizing the consequences of your failure up to an hour later.

Now imagine that you have spent 10 hours losing to these basic enemies because your attention slipped a few times and you got one-shot, but now you have finally gotten the rhythm down, and you're able to get to the final boss.
Maybe it was all worth it? Does an epic test of skill await you?

No, not in this game it doesn't. Unless you have played it before and seen what the boss wave consists of, you have absolutely zero chance of winning. The game throws 3 new enemy types at you, and about 10 times the number of zombies as were in previous waves. To top it all off, you get 10 fps on this final wave, so you'll be watching a slideshow and struggling to control your soldiers.

A skilled game designer would give their players hints on what to expect, and slowly train them to have a chance of success. They Are Billions' game designer is, sadly, not skilled. The rest of the game has been teaching you that you can rely on your walls to protect your soldiers, but then on the final wave, they throw harpies at you that can jump over walls. The game has been teaching you that the zombies' weakness is their lack of a ranged attack, but then they throw spitters at you. The game has been teaching you that soldiers are the best unit and that you should mass them, but then they throw giants at you, which are impractical to kill without hundreds of snipers. The fact that the devs thought it was a good idea to throw these 3 enemies at you TOGETHER, when even a single new enemy type might break your defences, is absolutely mind-boggling. I cannot fathom what they were thinking.

Save yourself the money and frustration, and go play literally anything else. I guarantee you will have more fun.\t\t\t\t\t
