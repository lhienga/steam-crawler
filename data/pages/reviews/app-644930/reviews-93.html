\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a great theme, controls well, and the concept is cool. The campaign isn't good though and it drags the whole thing down for me.

If you want any kind of story more than 'we have to retake the world from the undead' then you won't find it here. The campaign is an excuse to string together a bunch of custom missions more or less. This makes sense because the game was in early access for so long and the main focus for most of that was the Survival mode. The problem is it's worse than just playing a custom Survival game because these missions are designed to ease you into the tech trees, units, etc but they're *SO* long that you're playing very boring versions of a normal survival game.

A big chunk of the missions are tedious pixel hunt hero missions that most of the negative reviews on Steam complain about. These complaints are justified because the research points you get from this missions is crucial on higher difficulties and finding all of them without a guide is annoying to say the least.

"Swarm" missions are missions where you get to place units/walls on the map and defend a tower until all the zombies are dead. The walls on these missions end up being entirely pointless and the real strategy is to just spam units and do nothing else and let the game play itself.

The game uses an ironman save mode by default where it constantly saves and if you mess up at all there's no possibility of save scumming or resetting to a prior point in the mission. There is apparently a mod tool that will allow you to save scum and if you're serious about playing I would highly recommend that. It's super demoralizing to spend 45 minutes building your base only to have a zombie horde RNG spawn in a direction you were slightly less prepared for thus ending all of your progress and forcing you to replay the slow beginning 45 minutes again.

There's very little variance in builds or strategy and that exacerbates the problem of having to restart due to the ironman difficulty.

The voice acting in this game is monotonous to the point that it's offensive: "On my way!", "For The Empire!", "On my way!", "For the Empire!". You aren't going to get memorable Starcraft 1 liners from this one.

If you do play this game, skip the campaign and just play Survival.

Cool idea but too tedious to be fun.\t\t\t\t\t
