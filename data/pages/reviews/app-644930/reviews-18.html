\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is basically a tower defense and not an RTS. It has no manual saving, so every game is a forced iron-man mode (I.E you have one life per save and if you lose, that save is dead). Very shallow resource mechanics. The way the game works, is basically if a single zombie sneaks past a wall and destroys a single building, the game is over 9/10 times. Because the game is so shallow as an actual RTS, it seems the game design was pushed in a direction to make the game as artificially difficult as possible by creating "special mobs" that are basically just damage soaks and there is basically only one way to play the game. I wanted to play the game and complete a level just to say I gave it a real shot, but I just can't get into it.

Forced Ironman mode. Not fun. Not relaxing. Unnecessarily stressful. Very shallow gameplay mechanics. Artificial difficulty leaves very little room for creative player-driven strategies.

E: If you aren't a die hard strategy game player like AOE, Total War, SC, Age of Mythology, etc you might not mind the loose use of the word "strategy" to describe this game, provided you like exceptionally difficult games. I don't like games that make difficulty party of their identity, so I haven't been lured into bashing my head on the survival scenarios over and over again until I perfect the pre-defined meta. I feel like I was catfished, but I gave it a shot. not for me.\t\t\t\t\t
