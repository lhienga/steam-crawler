\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is almost quite nice, but a couple extremally poor decisions hold it back and turn it into an exercise in frustration.

The non RTS portions of the campaign where you control a single unit through a facility could be nice RP fluff, but it's quite boring and lacking in interesting choices. Your tactical input is basically meaningless for these clunky characters and if you die you have to do the whole 30-40 minute exercise over again from scratch. No checkpoint system really turns these bores into a chore.

The campaign itself is ruined by it's tech tree system, that sees you making extremally important choices blind with no way to reset it. You'll constantly discover a tech you thought would be great is actually garbage because of some hidden drawback, and you'll sit there frustrated you wasted your points on garbage with no way to fix for the entire rest of the campaign. Unless you're the kind of person that enjoys restarting the campaign over and over again you're going to hate this.

The final very poor choice that ruins this game is the lack of mod support. Not just lack of support, but actively hostile to mods, the game developers make it impossible for wiser heads in the community to to roll back their poor choices and turn the game into a fun experience.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game so, so much. For strategy gamers who are turtle strategists at heart, this is by far one of the best games in its genre. The base game is extremely solid in its gameplay and gaming mechanics with its own unique additions that enhance and add flavor to the gameplay - for example, no other RTS game will let you experience the horror of seeing 1 invading zombie that evaded your watch start a mass conversion of your precious, hard fought-for city.

Add to that charming, well done graphics and a challenging difficulty level; in the end it's a downright shame that the developers seem to have abandoned the game, and didn't go the DLC route like other strategy games have benefited from like Stellaris and Total War: Warhammer. So much unused potential and DLC opportunities, it's just saddening.

I would've gladly paid for additional DLC content for this great game - or for a 'They Are Billions' part 2 sequel - but it looks like the devs have moved on completely to their other games in development. Maybe the actual launch of the full game was disappointing to them in number of sales, I don't know. Still, a shame.
