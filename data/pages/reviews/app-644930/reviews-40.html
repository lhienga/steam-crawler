\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's something in this game that makes me want to like it, but there's a lot that's just.. bad.

The hero missions in particular don't seem to add anything meaningful to the game. Imagine playing a match of a MOBA, but your character has no abilities and gains no levels, you just have to auto-attack and kite creeps for 30 minutes.

The game is somehow too fast and too slow at the same time. Resource generation is slow, and if you're not actively microing your units, it feels like there isn't much to do other than sit and wait for the train to come by and give you some gold so you can continue building. But if a single zombie makes physical contact with a house, he can infect everyone in the building in about 2 seconds, which spreads like wildfire even if you have attacking units nearby actively trying to kill the zombies. It feels like it takes 30 minutes just to get out of the early-game slog of a mission, but it can end in the blink of an eye. It goes from "nothing is happening" to "the world is ending" at the drop of a hat.

The game is also severely lacking in hotkeys. The building categories have hotkeys, but the buildings themselves do not for some reason, so even if you want to get familiar and quick with building, you're always going to be slowed down by having to mouse-over and click a button to build each building. Want to keep enemy health bars on? Gotta use a workaround of hotkeying health bars to a key on your number pad and turn off num lock to emulate the key being held down. There is no simple "toggle" key. Playing without health bars does not seem plausible at all to me. I don't know why they're not always on by default. There also doesn't seem to be a way to add units to an existing control group. You have to constantly select the entire group and re-hotkey the entire army if you want to add more units to it. Maybe I'm just spoiled by SC2 (which is nearly a DECADE older than this game), but the hotkey system in this game feels rushed and unfinished.

In terms of voice acting or story, it's completely laughable. The intro cut-scene made me want to reconsider even giving the game a chance because the monologue was so cheesy and poorly written. It feels like it could be from a kids' cartoon or a mobile game that was poorly translated.

The only part of this game that is fun is mowing down legions of zombies with your army, but the game tries its hardest to prevent you from experiencing that. Take a look at the achievements for the game. See how even the most common achievements have around 50% or less unlock rate? It's because people play the first level or 2 and realize this game is more stress-inducing than fun. Can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest way to describe this game is an abusive relationship. I really want to love it but it is really hard to love it. I’d say it’s the best zombie RTS game out there that has great gameplay, difficulty, progression and art style. It does not pull back punches and will wipe out your entire base if given the chance. It also gives a strong sense of just trying to hang on and keep working so you're never bored. However, there are quite a few issues that keep tripping me up and get me frustrated. Firstly, why are there no speed up buttons? It feels like it’s mandatory to have in RTS games, I know I said that the game can wipe out your base in a second. But having to restart over and over again because of 1 zombie that got through a crack and then having to grind my way back is just annoying. Secondly the troops range is not displayed correctly, for some reasons it gives you a number of the units range but I don’t know how to measure that. Is it by view? Or blocks? I’ve seen countless times archers just letting walkers pass because they were 1 dot away from their view and have to restart again. Thirdly the AI is not amazing, it feels like I have to hold their hand to where I want them to go the more my base gets bigger and bigger. It makes sense to give them room to move around in but even when there’s a clear and open gap they will not take it. Sometimes when I tell them to move they’ll move an inch before stopping to a halt. Fourthly, the tech tree is a pain. In my first playthrough I decided to have a soldier on start and extra food gathering and defense. This was a massive mistake as I’ve soft locked myself, because I always kept having food issues and ended up getting overwhelmed. The best route was to go into woodshop and spec into farming because farms will give you constant food whilst hunting cottages and fishing docks will not. This would be fine to experiment if you had a reset points option. In the hard difficulty that isn’t the case, you either do it the right way or die. Overall it comes off negative but once you get your head around the game and do a bit of research it becomes more bearable and fun.\t\t\t\t\t
