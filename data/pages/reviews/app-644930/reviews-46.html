\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInitial review after 8.3 hours:

I really wanted to like this game, but it has some of the most godawful design choices I have ever seen on an RTS.
As far as RTSs go, the game is pretty basic, but the developers tried to cover this up by adding metric tons of fake difficulty:

Having one zombie slip by your base and wreck your base by destroying one house in three hits, resulting in a snowball of zombies? Check

Having not only a metric ton of zombies in each map, but making new zombies pop in from thin air at previously cleaned up zones, resulting in the aforementioned result? Check

Having some horrible exploration missions, in which you have to clear a whole complex with a single unit, thus having a glacial pace? Check (Seriously, Starcraft did these missions better back in 1998 for Pete’s sake)

Having all these issues compounded by a forced ironman mode, meaning that if you lose a mission due to all this arbitrary BS, you have to spend one, two or more hours redoing it again? Absolutely check

And this just scratches the surface:
-\tThere’s no formations nor any hero abilities.
-\tMission objectives are seriously uninspired (Gather X amount of resources, have X amount of people, kill all the zombies).
-\tEvery mission has to be played in one specific way (i.e turtle hard, mass soldiers, mop up the map, complete the actual mission)
-\tPathfinding is all over the place, as well as targeting priorities (If the units are instructed to target the closest enemy, they will prioritize hitting an enemy a meter away, but on the other side of a wall, instead of an enemy on the same side that’s slightly further away)
-\tThere’s no way to highlight the relevant items that yield Empire/Research points in exploration missions, so you have to pixel hunt each box, barrel or wardrobe to make sure you’re not missing anything.

All in all, the game could be pretty good, but it’s hamstrung by some baffling design choices. Really do not recommend.


Addendum at 130 hours:

After spending 100+ hours on this heap, finishing its excuse of a campaign and getting the top achievement for score, I can safely say that I got good, but this game sure didn’t.
This campaign is now my golden standard for horrible game design.

-\tThe regular missions are mind-numbingly boring, since literally all of them have the same objectives: Get a certain population, hold against all the hordes and kill all the infected. EVERY. SINGLE. TIME.
-\tThe exploration missions manage to be even worse. Playing these missions on Apocalypse difficulty and having to clear hundreds of zombies in each level with a couple units at most (Half the missions don’t even give you that, you can only use your hero unit) has redefined tedium for me.
-\tThe horde missions are unironically the least strategic missions of them all. Literally all of them, except for a couple, can be solved by just making a unit ball around your outpost and letting the infected come. Even on the highest difficulty (Hell, it’s even worse in higher difficulties, since the infected count is so high that those missions can take 10+ minutes. You can go get a drink, browse your phone or read a book; these missions require zero input).
-\tThere’s a lot of poor design choices masquerading as difficulty: Research is pretty badly designed, since A) You have no idea how the units or attack towers perform before unlocking them, so you’re going blind if you haven’t played Survival yet. B) There are only enough tech points for about 70% of the techs (Assuming you find every item in every exploration mission) and several techs are basically bait. This, combined with C) Being unable to respect the tech tree, means you’re pretty likely to kneecap yourself by choosing suboptimal techs on your first playthroughs and kill your progress unless you turn the difficulty way down. And if you’re playing on the highest difficulties, you pretty much have to adhere to a very specific sequence if you want to have a fighting chance.
Also, you have no idea how big hordes are going to be until the last moment, so any kind of long-term planning is impossible unless you have prior knowledge of the level. This is showcased on some levels that throw a much larger horde than what you would expect for the first horde on other levels, just to get a cheap loss (Narrow Pass, I’m looking at you).
-\tAs a whole, while the setting is pretty interesting, the game does next to nothing with it. The lore is borderline non-existant, there’s only two characters to speak of (The Emperor and your hero unit), there’s only 4 “cinematic sequences” (And that’s counting the one for the ending) and the blurbs describing each mission might as well be interchangeable.
The campaign mode is basically a stretched-out Survival Mode with a tech system tacked on top. There’s absolutely no love behind its design.

The Survival mode is somewhat acceptable, but it’s nowhere near as good to carry the game nor justify its pricetag.

Can’t stress this enough: Don’t get anywhere near this game. If you want a hardcore strategy game, get Frostpunk (Sure, it’s a city builder instead of an RTS, but even then, it’s light years ahead of this).\t\t\t\t\t
