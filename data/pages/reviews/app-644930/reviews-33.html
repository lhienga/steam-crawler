\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the condensed version of playing a strategy game and pitting yourself against multiple unfairly difficult AI for funsies. It's a test of optimal build orders, expanding your base, some micro skills, exploiting the dumb AI, and a little bit of tower defense. While this is by no means a 10/10 game, there's just no standalone game quite like this to compare to, so this gets a thumbs-up.

The game is difficult but fair, and RNG is almost never something that will screw you over to the point that you can't beat the game. While there is some imbalance with units/defenses, the game is ultimately about self-challenge (with difficulty modes very flexibly changing every aspect of the game like enemy count, game duration, and terrain types) so there's no particular issue.

It's a shame that newbies will usually jump into the campaign first expecting the likes of Warcraft's singleplayer campaign, but beware: while those games have a focused campaign with AI 'skirmish' fights being more of a bonus, They Are Billions provides a focused skirmish game with the campaign as the afterthought. Don't play the campaign until you've played around with the normal gameplay. Actually, just don't touch the campaign.\t\t\t\t\t
