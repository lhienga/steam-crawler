\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally beat the full campaign at the highest difficulty, so its time to write a review! In short, a good RTS that's definitely worth the asking price. I can highly recommend it, and even better if it's on sale.

A bit more in depth, They Are Billions is a phenomenal game with gameplay reminiscent of old school RTS's. There's a good challenge for you, your units have personality, the buildings add to the steampunk theme, the music is good, you get to adjust your playstyle with the built-in tech tree, and there's a full campaign for you to play with lots of missions challenging you in unique ways, or giving you buffs in others.

A regular campaign mission (and the general gameplay loop) will usually start with you clearing out infected around your base, slowly making room for buildings, building up an army, and when carefully managed, a snowball to clear the map of enemies and prepare against/deal with incoming waves. While there's a lot more to it, such as tech buildings, different resources, and managing your limited space, the campaign tends to follow this rule. If any of that sounds interesting to you, this game might be for you!

And a special shoutout to Cape Storm, Narrow Pass, Lowlands and the final level: these missions were very difficult to me starting out on normal difficulty, and a whole different beast on the highest one. If you end up getting the game, I wish you good luck with these!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just want to preface this review with saying the colony builder part of the game is pretty alright, punishing but not in a bad way, generally if you fail its because you screwed up and the skirmish game is great fun.

The campaign though, my word I've never played anything more frustrating/ un-fun in my life.

What on earth were the devs thinking with the hero missions?

I'd read reviews before saying how bad the hero missions were, I thought to myself I loved the hero missions in equivalent games like warcraft 3 and C&C but this game seemed to take all the bad parts of such games and ignore all the good.
Your choice of hero is speedy girl or chunky man, you have two abilities "grenade" and "med kit" and that's it. Your hero is only usable in these god awful hero missions not in the actual game at any point.

Have you ever tried doing a "wheres wally" where you have no idea what Wally looks like, the page is covered in black ink and you uncover the page by inching forward while listening to your character yell "for the humans" 700 times? Its not as fun as it sounds, honestly.
Not to mention the devs putting these items in deliberately stupid area's off the main track of the map (looking at you painting on the radar map)

It wouldn't be so bad if these were optional but nope, you are required to do them every time they pop up otherwise you wont have enough "empire points" to do the swarm missions to progress to the actual fun part of the game.

Speaking of the swarm missions... again what the hell were the devs thinking? They're essentially just there to make sure you do the hero missions to earn "empire points" to be able to buy units in the swarm missions, the game of course doesn't tell you this when the first one opens up so you try it a couple of times and think... there is literally no way I can do this, Once you've done the hero missions and have enough points just buy a bunch of units and leave them in a square to kill the horde of zombies running at you until the level completes. So fun.

I really really wanted to love this game but I'm getting to the point where I cant actually be bothered to load it up knowing I'll have to do another hero mission. Such a shame the devs don't want to let modders attempt to fix the bits that are currently bad as I feel under all the rubbish there is a superb game here somewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent RTS, solid recommend. The save system people are talking about is you basically can't save/load the way you would in aoe single player, you can only save and continue from that point each "run" kind of like a rogue-lite. This may seem off-putting but you might appreciate it as a design choice consistent with the way a survival game flows. You have a stake in each game because it's possible to lose and your decisions matter since there are no re-do's, but at the same time the pace is pretty slow and you can pause to look around any time, save at any point and pick up from where you left off. I didn't feel that I wasted time if I lost a game, it was still enjoyable and I learned something for next try. Looking back at your base and seeing half the buildings zombified and no lights on is the game's way of telling you to build some walls. Same for getting a bad spawn or a good spawn, you'll start to get better after each loss. Game slaps, if you're curious about it you won't be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
