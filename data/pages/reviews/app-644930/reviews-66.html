\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe people who made this game made a decision for you. They decided it will be more fun for you if you are not allowed to save and reload games. It's not. The campaign is a boring, tedious, frustrating slog. There are 3 different mission types. Defense missions, where you plop down some soldiers on a map, go AFK for 10 minutes and continue the campaign, Hero missions, which are so horrendously unfun that I actually looked for mods to see if i could just skip them (there are no mods), and the normal RTS style missions.

The RTS style missions are almost fun but there are 3 main issues keeping them from being fun. 1) The game just isn't polished enough. A lot of quality of life mechanics that typical RTS games have are missing and the pathing/AI is just bad. You'll click attack move and then see that your soldiers are just running in place, not attacking anything because of a pathing issue. 2) If an enemy is able to smack 1 of your buildings it is an automatic game over. Buildings are infected so quickly an they produce so many additional enemies that if a zombie is able to punch a single building that ends your game. 3) You can't save/reload or speed up time. Oh you just spent 45 minutes on a game and encountered a new enemy that sprinted past you and punched one of your tents for an automatic game over? Well, replay that entire 45 minutes just to prepare for the new enemy type. Oh you're an hour into a map with and already prepared for the final wave that's coming later? Well, you just let the game run for another hour while you wait for that wave. Oh ,you're playing one of those terrible hero misisons and got a new ally and it turns out it's the only unit in the game that has friendly fire and it insta-kills your hero, lol, you need to replay the entire last terrible slog of an hour now that you know 1 unit has friendly fire.

Then there are the normal survival maps. It would be one thing if it were a give and take, loss and recovery, 1 step back, 2 steps forward type game, that would actually be fun. The thing is you can pause the game and any single one mistake is an automatic game over. There is no coming back from anything. So it's not really playing a game, it is laboriously and tediously doing step by step by step by step by step by step, pause, check pause, check, pause, check, pause, check, pause, check. because you cannot ever do 1 thing wrong. If you do it's just game over.

The game feels like work.\t\t\t\t\t

The game was pretty good until the devs gave up on smart design and added poorly conceived gimmicks into an otherwise brilliant mix.

You are told you have tools to mitigate this behavior but, again, when even your whisper-silent archers pull said Deus Ex Machinas from multiple screens away ... Out of the blue, for no reason (and you've adhered to strict noise management the entire time) every other attempt you make ... you'll lose faith.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want zombies and INTENSE, sweaty RTS gameplay, then this game is for you! This RTS doesnt just make you hurt, it PUNISHES you. If you start a colony and it gets overrun, there is no going back to before the zombies won. You are a colony commander, its your JOB to make sure that never happens. The game mechanics are balanced and beautiful. The enemy is destructive and endless (not really... but they are on the map initially and come in waves that feel endless). If even ONE zombie gets past your outer walls, you will suffer. A single zombie can completely infect your base (quite dramatically too). This is the ONLY RTS that makes zombies feel like a real threat. You WILL need to expand and you WILL need to balance exploration and expansion so that you don't bring down the horde on your defenseless cottages before you are ready. The campaign is difficult beyond even seasoned RTS players. I grew up playing command and conquer, Starcraft, Warcraft 3, and Total Annihilation and this game is on a whole 'nother level. I never even finished the game but I enjoyed it nonetheless. The steampunk artstyle is refreshing from realism or Sci-fi RTS (I suppose technically steampunk is Sci-fi but you get what I mean) and is executed very well. The learning curve is pretty steep and it requires more thought and planning than most RTS games.
The story: The zombie apocalypse has happened and the zombies won. Now you need to take back humanity's lands so that we can recolonize the Earth.
Each unit has a place depending on how you want to play. Each defense unit equally so. How well you learn the ins and outs of each will make or break your colony
NOT for the easily frustrated or the story seeker. Its hard and the story is "zombies!" but watching your base cull hordes upon hordes of zombies is pretty satisfying once you get a working defense in place.
7.8/10\t\t\t\t\t
