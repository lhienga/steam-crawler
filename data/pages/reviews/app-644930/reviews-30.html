\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game does a lot of things right - it really does. But:
1. The campaign's difficulty comes from very weird design decisions.
2. The hero missions are straight out boring - you are just kiting till your brain melts.
3. The permanent save combined with the fact that you cannot redo missions to get a better score (and tech) is just disrespectful of the players time.

After kiting the Weapons Factory for 20 minutes I died twice close to victory because of some intuitive range of mines and barrel explosions. On the third approach (really bored) I went straight for victory without gathering all the technology points. After being blocked in the strategy missions due to lack of technology, I went back to the Weapons Factory to max it out, sunk another boring 45 minutes into it just to learn that... you cannot redo a mission once you've successfully finished it. So I'm stuck on a campaign lacking the 300 technology points which would allow me to actually win the strategic levels. The only option is to restart the whole campaign and redo all the boring stuff again (especially the hero missions, without which you cannot progress)!

The strategic levels are a ton of fun, but the initial waves of zombies are very small compared to the last one. This means that you usually fail a level at the very end of it and have to restart the whole map from scratch doing the repetitive stuff to check if a different approach to the last wave will be better. This means you sink another 2 hours of repeating the same things between each last wave trial for a given map. And there is no option to speed up the time!

Why? Why? Why?!
This would be a 10/10 game if you shortened the hero missions (boring kiting) and allowed at least some form of reloading a mission (like a checkpoint after each zombie wave).
Until that happens, if you value your time - I do not recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
