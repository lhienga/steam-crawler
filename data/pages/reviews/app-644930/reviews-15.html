\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game but I just can't bring myself to write a positive review. The graphical style is beautiful, so is the orchestrated soundtrack. I'm a big fan of tower defense mechanics, and combining that with city building seemed like the perfect fit.

Unfortunately...

The game feels shallow. Very shallow.

The city builder aspect is extremely bare boned. There is almost no thinking whatsoever when it comes to resource management.
If energy is low, just spam more energy plants. If food is low, just spam more farms. If stone is low, just spam quarries. You get the idea. In the meantime, you spam houses to generate gold.

For most of the game, there are basically only one enemy, because different zombie "classes" offer no varying gameplay or strategies to beat them. Basically the only exception are the harpy-zombies that have the ability to jump over walls. The rest of the zombies are just bullet sponges that will go down with enough shooting.

Because of this, I feel like my decisions and strategy doesn't matter very much. Once you have figured out the optimal late game strategy, (which seems to be to spam Thanatos, at least in moderate difficulty), you almost can't lose.

I also thought the tower defense aspect would be more interesting most of the stationary defenses seem to accomplish the same that a powerful group of units could do with more flexibility.

In higher difficulties, the game becomes endless kiting and micromanaging your units back and forth. The infection mechanic is not fun, since the player has almost no chance of recovering the game after a single building has been infected.

Even though the main structure has a health bar, the game is practically over anyway if the zombies even reach that far, so you basically only have one life in the game.

Overall, a game with good potential if it introduced more enemy variation and actual strategy instead of bullet sponges and "gotcha" mechanics.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wrote a whole review without describing the gameplay. You are base building of course, but the game moves at such a break neck speed, you literally pause the game, set-up your buildings, then unpause and wait from more resources and explore a bit, then re-pause, repeat... I get a big kick out of it, but in the end, this will not likely be a 300-500hr game like some I've done. Below is my initial rant.





This is an odd kind of game. Became an instant cult classic, dedicated fan base who wanted nothing but more. Game came out in 2017 and for the past 7 years, nothing has been done with the franchise.
  • No DLC
  • Has no mod support
  • Zero talk about They Are Billions 2

Devs have responded to forum posts saying they are still working on TAB, but no ETA. Not even if it's actually TAB 2, or TAB 1 update, or TAB 1 DLC. The odd defense they've given that they aren't going radio silence is only met with 'We're working on stuff...' then more radio silence. I feel like they've lost some steam over the years.

Regardless, here's my review;
  • Beautiful aesthetic
  • Game play is fun and addictive
  • Story is above average
  • Zero bugs found

Cons;
  • No ability to make the mini-map bigger
  • No ability to delete troops, so if you make too many higher tier soldiers, you can't delete them in exchange for workers.
  • Again, no mod support. The community would've doubled the sales or more with mod support.

    Not that many cons TBH. For 24$, this was well worth the buy. 8/10 if I were being objective. But for the uniqueness, that special "it" factor, I rate it 9/10.
