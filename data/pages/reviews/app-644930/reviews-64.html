\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like wasting several hours of your day? Is your time not valuable to you? If so, this game is for you!

TLDR: Solid RTS and base building gameplay ruined by gotcha mechanics and the lack of saving.

Things I liked:
-Nice balance between base building and RTS gameplay. Very rich research tree.
-Game mode variety. Very difficult to get bored. A lot of strategy variety as well.
-Cool setting and story with great character voice acting
-Easy to run on slower computers

Things I disliked:
-The difficulty of the game is largely based on "gotcha" mechanics. People say this game is hard. Well, it is hard, but not because the AI out-smarted you, or even because you have to defeat a lot of enemies. It's hard because if you don't know exactly what's going to happen, there is almost no way to adequately prepare for what's going happen. For example, you will get a 24 hr early warning of a wave of zombies coming, but you have no idea how big the wave is or how long it will last. Do I need one wall or twenty? When you go exploring, will you find a few slow zombies or a huge wave or runners that will follow you across the map? In the campaign, once you know what you are going to find, the game becomes way easier. Which might be okay except...
-There is no in-match save point mechanic. Spend two hours building up your base only to realize 1 zombie snuck through a gap you couldn't see in your defenses? Too bad, you have to restart the entire match. Oh and the second time you go through that same mission, now you can only get 80% of the original rewards for completing the match. The third time it's 60%, etc. And you need those rewards to upgrade your research to make the end-game playable. The entire game is built around a snow-balling concept and save mechanic ensures you get punishes you for not being perfect.
-Erasing hours of progress is NOT a fun "punishing" mechanic. Difficult games can be fun because they feel rewarding when you do finally win. This is not like that at all. Imagine playing Elden Ring or a Dark Souls game where every time you loose to a boss you get a 10% permanent reduction in damage output. Oh and you can't save the game right before you face the boss. You can only save two hours prior to reaching the boss and even then it's a manual save, not automatic. That's what playing the campaign feels like.

Conclusion: I really wanted to like this game. It's well polished, runs nicely on older computers, and has really unique feel to it. But after restarting the campaign several times, often erasing several days of play, it's just not fun to play anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI spent a lot of time, but In the end, I give it a thumbs down.
Its the mind-crushing frustration that got me in the end. I get that roguelike mechanics up the tension and suspense by making success or failure really count. But, even on easy mode, the waves are so unforgiving, that by the time you've realized that you've underestimated the attack, it is too late to do anything about it and you've wasted 5-6 hours.
It's the final level of the campaign that set me off the most. The massive wave of harpies that simply jump over all the layered defenses I had placed specifically for that purpose, zoomed right past my group of helpless titans and go right for the command center ripping it to shreds in seconds.
Some might tell me that I'm just not good enough a player and should play it again another 20-30 times before I get it right. I just don't have that kind of time or patience. I'm the one that pays for the game, I should not be one that looks like a fool.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've changed my mind on this game, as even though it has inspired eye-watering rage from me at times, I keep finding myself wanting to play it, to try again, to figure out why I failed (again). If it can make me this angry but keep me coming back for more punishment, then I think a thumbs-up is in order.

I do think having some sort of manual-save option, perhaps hitting a milestone in a mission (getting past a certain pop or tech) gives a separate slot auto-save file that allows for even one spot to resume a mission without losing hours of progress. I get that this is a super-hard RTS, but having an ironman mode that autosaves over the same save slot seems very punishing when, combined with certain mechanics in the game, you can make an extremely tiny mistake/miscalculation and easily snowball your way to mission failure.

Perhaps the issue is that this game is marketed as a tower-defense RTS, and aims at enticing a broader audience, while in reality it is a hard-as-nails colony sim that takes place in extremely hostile territory. As one lone zombie can sneak in, attack a building and quickly infect it to produce several more zombies which then spiral out to attack other buildings, you can quickly lose a mission even on the cusp of winning it. Since there's no save, you lose all progress, and that's a tough pill to swallow when the pace of the game is very slow, unable to be sped up, and some missions can take hours to complete.

Some of the issue could be mitigated if the game explained a few things to you, like how zombies don't spawn in the map itself but continue to spawn from the open map edges (not ones bordered by woods or mountains but open spots leading to map edge), thereby posing a threat from directions you cleared out moments ago. Having better audio cues to signal your defenses are being attacked, as they often don't go off, leaving you to "hopefully" hear zombie noises nearby to alert you a wall is being attacked. (Zombies chew right through wooden wall defenses too, fast enough that a single zombie getting past your wall can spell doom for you in a matter of moments if you aren't patroling). The AI is adept at throwing these buggers at you in small enough numbers that you don't even see them, (often at a corner of your walls), and they are small enough graphically to be tough to spot behind your buildings.

Having better tool-tips, or more in-depth tutorial would help, (noise level isn't even mentioned, and it's HUGE in the way this game works), and it would give players who weren't aware of the frustration level this game can bring a better idea of what they are dealing with. For instance, back to the noise level, there is no mention or real indication of how much this affects your game. If you're the type of player who bought this and jumped right in thinking it was some sort of Starcraft/tower-defense hybrid for instance, and you need to spam buildings and get your stuff out without a plan, you will literally get eaten alive. Not only do you need a plan, you need to keep your noise/activity in-check while carefully expanding outward, as everything you do generates noise and draws more and more zombies. Maybe some tips on how different weapons attract more aggro, or even zombies attacking your walls makes noise that draws more zombies, would have been helpful at the start.

As it is, you are left to figure out yourself why things keep going wrong. I don't like looking up guides, but playing the first few times definitely felt like I was missing out on core-concepts. I don't mind losing, but losing that hard and fast with little idea of what I did wrong is tough. (I had troops, I had buildings, my economy was booming; in most RTS games this spells time to attack and win. In this game, it can mean just as you start to feel like you are on top of things, the game kicks you in the groin and says "Oops, did we forget to check our walls?" before you lose everything.

The problem is, I can't stop playing it! I have felt sheer and utter rage at all of the above, turned the game off, and come right back. So I can't fault the game for being tough as hell, or not giving a damn about whether I'm understanding what I did wrong; I can just wince and wish maybe some of this stuff had been made clear at the start. :D

End-take: Fun, addicting colony-sim/rts management that is super difficult and a bit obtuse on mechanics. Unforgiving single auto-save slot may turn off a lot of people, as it does really hurt to lose 3 hours of mission progress to a single mistake. A few improvements could have made it a big easier, but maybe that is the point. Just know what your signing up for, good luck, and always remember to listen for the tell-tale sounds of zombie shenanigans! :)\t\t\t\t\t
