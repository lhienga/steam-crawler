\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDifficulty is more about you getting cheesed than something like a Souls game. You can be an hour into the game, and a single zombie sneaks in, hits a tent, and game over, back to zero.

There are a lot of fans out there telling people "keep trying" and "the fun is that you 'learn' from losing" -- it is not fun to waste 20 hours memorizing a map setup just to get through to the next stage, where you again need to waste a bunch of hours just to again memorize another build.

You can't save in the middle of any stage, and the stages take hours. So one hiccup, one misclick, and you are dead, back to zero. This is artificial difficulty via deliberate opportunities for you to die by cheese. Spend your time and money elsewhere, especially your time. And to beat a dead horse -- when you die in a truly "difficult" game that you can get better at (again, like a Souls game), the death feels like something you can overcome. When you die in They Are Billions, every single death I've seen and had was cheese - so don't listen to people saying it is planning and getting better, there's zero skill curve if you die because a 1-tile space on the other side of the map let one single zombie sneak in and immediately infect a single tent, resulting in an unstoppable spawn of zombies. That wasn't lack of skill, that's just pure cheese - aside from the fact that you just spend 40 minutes or more than an hour on your build so far, and now back to zero. If you could go back 10, 20, hell 30 minutes to try again, without losing 100% of your progress, it would go a long way to feeling like you are learning/building/growing. Every game I ever see that Steam recommends as similar to this one, I'll skip.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of fun.

One of my favorite city builder/defense games. You will lost a lot, you will kill five thousand enemies just to lose to 1 zombie that made its way through some trees and attacked a camp causing an outbreak you can't make it back to and salvage quick enough.

I haven't touched the sandbox yet, and I KNOW that's the supposed main feature of the game, but I'm not great at RTS and have spent all my time in the 'tutorial' campaign. Easily gotten my money's worth just trying to complete it, I'm on the final mission and got a bit burnt out losing hours of progress multiple times. At this point I doubt I'll ever finish that last missions but the game is solid and I recommend it to anyone even slightly interested in RTS and roguelike city builders.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery disappointing.

I expected high quality game, but there are many annoying features that brake the game. One of the most important is that units are constantly getting stuck on buildings, scenery or other units. So when you send them to reposition, some will go to position, stop and start shooting, while other units will keep running on spot, because the shooting units are now blocking them. Other situations are, when you send units across the map and some units won't arrive, only to find out they are stuck running into a stone. The pathfinding is BAD.

These bugs are not forgivable in a roguelike game where you can't save progress!!

Other are just bad controls, where you don't have many keyboard shortcuts and have to click with mouse a lot.
You also can't de-select anything, which leads to mistakes.
Selecting individual units in a crowd is difficult.

Overall, there are lots of things that could have been made way better.

There is also not much progress. You do not have many units or defensive structures to choose from, so you end up using 3-4 types of units for everything...

The game is difficult, which I like, however, it is also oversimplified and got boring very qucikly.

I tried to refund it, but I played more than 2 hours, so it was denied.
If you like these kind of strategies, the Diplomacy Is Not An Option is made waaay better.\t\t\t\t\t
