\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it differs in many ways from the previous Dead Rising games, DR3 continues the DR tradition of fun mindless zombie slaughter. It lacks the bright colorful mall/casino environments in favor of a dark apocalyptic fictional spoof of L.A., but the zombie killing is still here. This game changes the whole "saving" survivors mechanic and is not the same as DR1/DR2/DR2OTR. The story is straight forward and enjoyable enough, but I feel DR3 lacks the charming characters of the first two games. The protagonist, Nick Ramos, feels a lot less enjoyable compared to Frank West and Chuck Greene, and he just comes off as kind of generic. I have roughly 45 hrs on the game and have only come across 2 glitches that have made me needing to restart. At the same time, while maybe a minor problem to some people, a certain few achievements are bugged and I’d rather not start messing with the saves files and restarting my progress to unlock it. Regardless of these minor issues, at the end of the day, I play Dead Rising games to have fun killing zombies, and DR3 still does its job of doing that for me. If you’re fans of the first few games, I highly recommend playing the end to this trilogy (I know DR4 exists, but let’s not talk about that one).

7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDead Rising 3 is a terrible video game. it has more, ' I guess you could call 'mini-games', which are very hit or miss ,while staying faithful to the show. It brings back great memories of the earlier seasons of Dead Rising. The characters play and control really well, giving you the feeling of being a real ninja game. The game has a load of content to experience with each mission having bonus objectives for a bit of challenge if that's something you're into. Some of the challenges however can be a little annoying but for the most part these are finished within 2 or 3 tries. Each missions is ranked by difficulty from D to S with D being the easiest and S being hardest, providing a well balanced experience. Fun boss fights providing different variations in size and scale, making for some epic immerse boss fights as seen in the Anime. All and all I'd say Gunfire Reborn is a mild improvement over the first game and worth playing if you're a real dead rising fan. Solid 2/10 Better Than Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice\t\t\t\t\t
