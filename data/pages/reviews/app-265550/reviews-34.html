\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Overall Score: 7.5/10

Pros:
Satisfying progression system
Getting to kill zombies in a million different ways
Lots of funny outfits
Campy, entertaining story
Tons of weapon and vehicle combinations

Cons
Bad camera
Clunky map layout

Subjective Cons
Main character talks to himself a lot
A little on the easy side
"Hidden" Collectibles show up on the map when you're close
Gritty look, but goofy tone
Modding required to unlock the framerate and fix the camera


Though many consider Dead Rising 2 to be the best in the series, Dead Rising 3 is a creative departure from the first two main games in ways that seek to improve the overall experience. The game still runs on a timer, albeit a much more generous one than in previous installments. Most players should have no problem leveling up and playing through the campaign without having to bank their progress and start from the beginning. In addition to weapon blueprints, there are numerous collectibles if that's your thing, though their location is shown on the map when you get close so they practically find themselves.
Another new mechanic is the introduction of killstreaks, which allow you to rack up some serious PP if you can keep them going. In addition to combining weapons, the player can now create vehicle combinations, which are just as absurd as you'd expect from Dead Rising. Killing zombies in this game is very satisfying, and there's a million ways to do it -- many of which are quite silly. The skill progression system is also a huge improvement over previous games, allowing the player to choose which attributes to improve next, and tailoring the game to fit their playstyle.

In terms of tone, the game maintains most of the goofy vibe that made the first two so lovable, but with an overall grittier look that, while appearing serious, is anything but. Some may find this an unappealing combination, but I personally think it works.

I really only have two main issues with the game, and those are the camera and the level design. I find these things tolerable because I otherwise really enjoy DR3, but they are annoying until you get used to them. That said, the camera is just not good. The field of view is way too narrow. The camera is also lazy, and you will get into the habit of using the recenter button. This can make some indoor spaces uncomfortable to navigate, even though there's nothing wrong with their layout. When the player sprints for too long and starts to get tired, a shaky-cam effect is introduced, and this can't be turned off. The follow cam can be disabled for manual control, but the shaky cam can't be disabled. Flawless Widescreen (a free mod) can completely fix the field of view, and makes the shaky-cam a little less annoying, but it's still there. For players wanting a high FPS, you will need Flawless Widescreen. The framerate is softcapped at 30 and hardcapped at 120.

As far as the map design goes, it's clunky. There are four main districts in the city. Despite being an open world game, you will find yourself checking the map to move from one to the other. It's a nuisance when you have to travel to the other side of the city, but I normally spend enough time in the same area that it doesn't bother me that much.

Overall I really enjoy this game. It has its flaws, but it's still a lot of fun and definitely worth playing. If you want a goofy open-world zombie game, Dead Rising 3 is a solid choice.\t\t\t\t\t
