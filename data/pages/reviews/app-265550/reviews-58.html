\r\n\t\t\t\t\tdanganronpa v3: killing harmony is very entertaining, with the high concept this time around being a satirical take on the zombie apocalypse going to the city of los perdidos (los angeles), california in 2021 (of all years)

don't be too stressed over the time limits - you're nick ramos, #3 mechanic/boy toy/orphan hustling to find the truth and combo blueprints out of the latest zombie outbreak after five days of surviving

if you do fail, you can always do the following:
plan ahead
abuse weapon lockers and garages
play the game normally since there's no real time crunch

/////

Danganronpa v3 is what I would call "it's what the devs wanted for themselves"-core. It's very similar to Saints Row IV in that the developers wanted to make a game for themselves and for a wider audience, not for the core audience they gained through Keiji Inafune's DR1, 2, and Off the Record.

So a lot has changed from how the controls are mapped, how the weapons are handled, and how survivors work now. Of course, the game introduces vehicles (and their combo variants) along with the rework of the psychopaths. Instead of being a rogue's gallery of normal people driven insane by the apocalypse, they're now just flanderized versions of the Seven Deadly Sins. The most drastic change is how the cases and clock works. You can practically put off two days in the normal mode with a partner, only God knows how many days I can waste in single player alone.

It's a bit soulless, but what soul there remains is carried heavily by its original soundtrack and occasional spark of narrative, aesthetic, or gameplay brilliance.

It's not a game for DR1 and 2 fans, but it's a good starting point for the uninitiated who may feel that DR1 and 2's time systems are too much. At least there's some fanservice for the fans of the earlier entry of the series.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFAN-tastic. Dead Rising 3 is the last good Dead Rising game, another true successor to Dead Rising, and now Dead Rising 2 and OTR, Dead Rising 3 features a large map and new systems, while still managing to retain that Dead Rising charm and gameplay feel, despite some modernisation, it's not quite as good as it's predecessors, but it's a very good game with a strong lore tie to the other games. If someone were to modernise a Dead Rising game, this is how it should be done, keeping what makes Dead Rising, Dead Rising, but giving it a much more open world feel and adding in plenty of new systems.. And of course, plenty of new combo weapons, vehicle combos, survivors and psychopaths. The game is also quite stable, being great for coop play and generally a bug free experience.

The game does not feature a sandbox mode, sadly, however progress carries over - IE, zombie kills - through your numerous playthroughs and DLC stories, which makes getting some of the zombie slaying achievements much easier and less tedious, as well as giving the ability to keep track of your true zombie killing numbers. The DLCs stories are quite good, and worth getting the Apocalypse edition for, and make up for the lack of Sandbox, as Dead Rising 3's time limits are much more lenient than the other titles.\t\t\t\t\t
