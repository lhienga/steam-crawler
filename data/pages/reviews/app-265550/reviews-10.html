\r\n\t\t\t\t\ta definite successful predecessor, in my opinion. 7.5/10 game, definitely worth it either on full price or on sale.

the story isn't bad at all, in fact I liked it a lot. there's occasional plot twists, a decent cast of characters to like and bond with, and the amount of time you'd be spending going through it all is relatively big; about as long as the other games if not a bit longer.

Nick is a great character who feels like a regular guy to play as, and the voice actor did an amazing job. he's no bigshot, just a mechanic who uses his knowledge of tools to put things together and puts them to use. we lose the traditional camera system, but that's not too big of a loss with what this game offers in return regarding new content.

for starters, this is the first game you get to craft on-the-go. this is incredibly useful and strongly encourages using combo weapons even more than 2/OTR since items that are used for crafting combos are incredibly abundant. plus certain recipes only need half of the recipe to be a specific item, while the other half can be any item that belongs in the category needed to craft said combo item. also, there are super combo weapons that are on a whole different level beyond what we ever got so far. and holy hell, we got a damn great selection of combo weapons in this game.

combo vehicles are now a thing as well. you can take two ordinary vehicles and make one massive super weapon, which can be very useful to get around and clear most of the zombie trash that roams the streets. some are less practical than others, but all of them can be fun and interesting to use when you get the chance.

the locker system is a very handy mechanic that acts like an item dispenser. it keeps track of every item you pick up throughout your journey and saves them in a record for you to grab out of the locker for free. this includes combo weapons, psycho weapons (the best part ngl), anything you can get your hands on. the main caveat is, the locker has "charges" that need time to refill as you play the game. regular items cost the least amount of charge, while combo and super combo weapons take much more of the charge meter, so it's not entirely abusable.

finally, this is the first and ONLY Dead Rising game to have voiced dialogue with survivors, and lets you resummon them to wander and fight with you whenever you want. this alone adds so much character to the survivors you come across in the city and makes them all the worthwhile saving. that isn't to say that the previous games were wrong to not have these features with survivors. this is just a really nice touch that I appreciated.

moving onto the main character; what's great about Nick is not only his personality, but the fact that he starts off as this guy who's absolutely terrified of the zombies. walk by a horde and he'll output voice lines showing his fear. finally we play as a main character who's suddenly prone to zombies as if they're an every day occurrence.

as you progress through the main game, Nick eventually loses that fear and gets pumped to fight a horde or even flat out irritated toward them when they're in his way. he doesn't force cheesy humor into his dialogue, but will occasionally say a funny line for the sake of comedy. thankfully this isn't overdone, unlike 4 where it's everything and 4 was outright the absolute worst with dialogue.

lastly, psychos in this game are... honestly pretty cool half the time but the other half don't really compete with what we got in DR1/2. I won't spoil which ones are good and which ones I didn't particularly enjoy, but they're all unique in their own ways that it's hard to complain.

LONG STORY SHORT, game is great. definitely better than Dead Rising 4, and will always be better than 4, without a damn contest.\t\t\t\t\t
