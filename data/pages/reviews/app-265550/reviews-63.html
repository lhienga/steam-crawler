\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReviewing in progress as I play but I didn't want to lose track of my thoughts. So far:

TL;DR: It's fun zombie killing on sale. I recommend getting it to just kill zombies if nothing else.

Pros:
- Great detail on zombies.
- The combat is where this game shines. It feels pretty visceral and you have to make sure zombies stay down (at least when traversing on foot and not mowing everything down in some wacky combo car).
They did a great job instilling more horror in this entry. The zombies feel scarier than before, and fighting them is where this game really shined.
- Swinging zombies off your car is fun.
Cons:
The map is atrocious. None of the stores have names on them which was a huge con to me.
- Some of the quest goals are pretty boring. "Bring X of Y" gather quests should be left to MMOs.
- This game refuses to remember your settings for some reason. Gamma, subtitles, online or offline..
- Why don't map marker disappear when you reach your destination?
- The roadblocks!! Why are there so many?? The map design is rough.
Both?
- Size of the world. It's pretty cool to drive through an entire city at first where the first two games were in a mall, but this game is a bit big for its britches. Traversing the world just feels kind of cumbersome after just driving through hordes of zombies.
- DR3 is where the franchise really turned. This game is markedly different overall and they couldn't quite nail down the feeling. At first, the game is really really heavy on horror and I was excited that they turned this into a real survival horror game, but they also kept the comedic effect and I couldn't quite tell the direction they were going for.
- The survivors become little objectives where you save them from impending zombie doom for them to just run away into sudden safety, or followers that you can summon at safe houses. I liked leading them to safety before.
