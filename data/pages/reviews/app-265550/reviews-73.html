\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not buy this. Do not play it a second time just to see if it was as bad as you remember. It was as bad as you remember.

COOL STUFF :)

The weapon locker is the best new feature because you can just load up on Split Shots once you get bored of walking through the endless hordes of minor inconveniences.

MAJOR PROBLEMS

The Map: It's huge feeling but also incredibly boring. There are tons of blocked roads and barricades because they don't want vehicles to actually get you from point A to point B quickly. There's not a huge amount of incentive to even explore since the map is always littered with weapons so why bother going through every shop when I know I can just keep walking and I'll find something if not an entire weapon cache like there is on the bridge. Then, during the late game, you're likely far too big for your britches so you're walking from point A to point B without any challenge which is absolutely thrilling and the scale of the map only exacerbates this.

Combo Weapon Rewards: No one wants your trash highly specific highly recipe for a bear with a machine gun. No, I don't want an explosive. Stop, I don't want your drone. STOP. I DON'T WANT THAT. Oh, the Split Shot? Now the game is too easy.
The garbage rewards really kills a lot of incentive to do side quests or explore when it's 99% trash outside of story missions.

Survivors: Too slow. Too weak. Too expendable. There's no reward for them surviving or even a reward for bringing them along. They're going to lag behind you so they're not great meat shields outside of bosses which they're likely not to survive the loooooong trip to.

Boss Difficulty: There is too little. I beat the cargo crane on accident before I even figured out what I was supposed to do.

Perks: The game blue-balls you with overpowered lvl 50 perks that you really, really, don't need. The worst part is that the later perks are WORSE than the early ones. Extra ammo and ranged damage is way better than piercing bullets. I'm going to be honest, I didn't even notice the difference.

Story and Tone: This game takes itself way, way too seriously. While the other Dead Rising games just used the natural mall ambiance for the most part this game loves to use generic stock-tier music. When they try, the music can be really good but typically they just use dull generic zombie music. The story is also incredibly dull without a whole lot of humor or intrigue. However, there is one death during the main story that was a pleasant surprise though.

tl;dr Get the splitshot then uninstall. It's just as hard as beating the game normally.
