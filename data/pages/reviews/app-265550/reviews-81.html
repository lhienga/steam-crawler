\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDead Rising 3 introduces another new character, Nick Ramos, He is a mechanic with skills.

The city of Los Perdidos, a fictional riff on Los Angeles, has a serious zombie problem. Everywhere you look they're there, shuffling, shambling, and groaning. Must have been one hell of a party.

Nick is trapped in Los Perdidos with a group of other survivors. The government will be dropping a bomb on the city in five days, and you have to escape before they do. Dead Rising's other thing is giving you a time limit, but this is the most generous one yet—which actually works against it. unless you aim to be a completionist - and can stand the constant and very frequent crashes (more on that later)

There was a real sense of urgency in the first two games, but this is 'time rich'. You have plenty of time to explore the city, completing side quests, rescuing survivors, and hunting for weapons.

But back to hitting zombies. This is what makes Dead Rising special. Every weapon feels different, whether you're carving through the hordes with a chainsaw, lopping heads off with a broadsword, or mowing them down with a minigun. The sheer number of ways to massacre and toy with the zombies is impressive, and it always feels great thanks to the tactile, weighty combat.

Los Perdidos is, although smaller than a lot of open-world type games is a pleasantly rich space. A huge amount of buildings can be entered, and their detailed interiors all contain unique weapons. If you played the first 2 and the off the record version of DR2 you will recognise the names of the shops

To make up for the increased size of the world, vehicles are littered generously around the map, including sports cars, bikes, vans, and, brilliantly, steamrollers and other construction vehicles. Nick, being a mechanic, can combine vehicles to create new, more deadly ones, (as long as you have the blueprint) like sticking the front of a steamroller onto a motorcycle. - personally, a favourite of this reviewer The combo weapon system is back too, allowing you to tape otherwise innocuous objects together to create monstrous, slapstick weapons.

Combine any two objects as long as you have the 'blueprint' for it you can make it, for instance, a handgun and a torch you get a 'tactical handgun'

Unlike the previous 2 games, Nick doesn't have to find a workbench to create combo weapons. You can do it anywhere, but it leaves you open to attack. Then there are 'super combo 'weapons this takes the insanity up a level. Again as long as you have the 'blueprint' you can make it a little more insane. start with a scythe and a katana and you get the 'Grim Reaper' (no not the guy in the cloak - unfortunately.) This is a massive bladed weapon with a wide sweep and long reach. You then find a 'gasoline canister (petrol to us brits) to create the Fire Reaper, which sets enemies ablaze, there is a further upgrade to this but I won't spoil it for you.

Dead Rising 3 is a game that just wants you to have fun. It revels in its silliness, even when it's being serious. There's a cutscene where, after killing a human for the first time, Nick has a solemn moment of reflection.

While most of the best bits have carried over—the persistent XP system, the combo weapons, its sense of humour to name two, some of the worst have too, namely the boss battles. They're long-winded, somewhat boring slogs, and unfortunately, they're no fun, repetitive and easily exploitable patterns. These, and the lazy sidequests, (in my opinion after multiple playthroughs) sour the experience.

Dead Rising 3 is a game that just wants you to have fun. This fun is ruined by the 'boss battles' and then the constant and frequent crashes. The crashes are I suspect down to the laziness of Capcom as from further research, it seems it was an Xbox One 'exclusive'. Which means that the PC wasn't the primary target device. This isn't one of Capcom's best ports. It's a system hog, (even on a decent dedicated games pc - Ryzen 7, and a 3080 GPU etc). Out the box, it's locked at a poor 30 fps, but you can create a file to force 60fps (instructions are widely available)

Every solution I found to solve the crashing didn't work for me, and that unfortunately ruined this game for me\t\t\t\t\t
