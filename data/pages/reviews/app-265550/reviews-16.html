\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Pre-review WARNING!!!: if you start the game and it asks you for your gamma, exit the game and play the exe from the file explorer. I tried to start the DLCs and it reset all my progress so I had to use a full-save from the internet. might be some steam cloud save kerfuffery but idk. good game doe, play it)

hella fun!

storyline is teetering between ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and believable
costumes and side quests are wacky and sometimes tragic. nick has nice facial expressions so try not to put on a huge mascot mask, unless you don't care about that
gameplay is supa fun!!! I love hack n slashing!!! I also love collectibles which this game has a lot of
on the topic of collectibles, pick up every item and clothing you see and get in every car you see, even if you hop right back out or drop the items or change the clothing back. you can spawn/choose anything from the locker/cabinet/workbench as long as you've picked it up before. I didn't realize you could spawn cars at car garages until the very last mission
highlight weapons: flame sword, chopper, ultimate grim reaper, laser sword, ZAR, and any shout weapon. most of the gloves weapons are fun, aside from the party gloves which are only pretend fun (ironically enough).
I didn't like the drivable or sentry type weapons because they were hard to make and uncomfortable to use
the map was small enough to make transport timely and still fulla zombie-killin
the time limit is lenient, enough for me to have gotten to lv48 by the time I finished chapter 7 and to have done every side quest I came across
I haven't played nightmare mode or the DLCs yet, but I've heard that the time limit is much tighter in nightmare mode and the DLCs have no time limit. not like it really matters because you can beat the main story really quick (2-3hrs?), I just imagine it's much harder if you don't have the skills unlocked from an earlier run on normal.
I hear that dead rising 1 and 2 have much tighter time limits and actually require you to chapter restart if you want to do everything, which sounds neat but the fact that you can beat this game nigh-fully in one run is appealing in and of itself

fun fact: in overtime, the timer doesn't actually start ticking. so spend that final night gathering your last collectibles and grinding PP, unless you want to restart chapter and lose your survivors/side quests :p there's no stake in losing them though, in my experience they weren't too helpful and all they really matter for are the achievements
fun fact 2: the hardest side quests for me involved collecting things, like hunks of meat or food/guns or a combo vehicle. of course over time it's obvious that food is in restaurants and guns are near military outposts or gun shops but I almost ran out of time for that quest because I was scrambling around the map to look for stuff

if you like killing zombies and racking up combos while dressed like a lunatic, this game is solid. just like me\t\t\t\t\t
