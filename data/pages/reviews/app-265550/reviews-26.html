\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDead Rising 3 is a mixed bag. This is another entry in a franchise I've loved since it's inception. Dead Rising 3 gives us an open world city crawling with the undead in numbers you haven't seen before - the amount of zombies onscreen at once is truly astonishing. Still, this technical feat is dragged onto its knees by some really crippling performance issues.

First and foremost, the game is locked at 30fps. The locked frame rate truly didn't bother me as much as it should but the real technical problem that couldn't be ignored was the extreme level of texture pop in and delayed environments. Throughout my entire play through, cars and buildings would take upwards of 4-5 seconds to fully load in even at point blank distances when moving at any pace faster than walking. This issue even got my character stuck behind walls which forced me to restart my game to fix. My character would often outpace the loading of interiors which would result in me being stuck in areas I wasn't supposed to be in. This is an abysmal PC port.

Nevertheless, the game is extremely fun (when it works as intended). Killing countless numbers of zombies has truly never felt this satisfying. The story leaves a lot to be desired but the special encounters and psychopaths the series is known for, are truly the highlight of the narrative.

If you want a mindless zombie killing game, this is it. If you are really adamant on having the best performance possible in a video game, you may want to skip this one. Overall, if the game hadn't suffered from extreme performance hiccups - I'd give this a solid 7, however; these issues are too severe to ignore. Dead Rising 3 gets a 6/10.\t\t\t\t\t
