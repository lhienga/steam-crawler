\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBuy this game on sale lol. Fun 4/5 Gameplay 3.5/5 Story (Dead Rising Quality) 4.5/5 Replay 2.5/5

The game fixed mostly everything that was broke about the previous games, like the bad AI posse. Then again they overfixed the posse and they dont take 'enough' damage from an explosion or friendly damage that in the first two games was higher. It was also easier to make you companions turn on you from too much friendly fire, its still possible, but way harder to make them turn on you. The not having to ferry them constantly back is a plus, but now there is no urgency to get to the next one. That's a good thing, since they WAY dialed up the time you have to save people. I ♥♥♥♥♥♥ around for nearly an hour getting blueprints and statues and still had time to grab 2 more survivors. There is no sense or urgency in this game, and now i know what feels like its missing. Its the constant struggle. I have defeated DR2 79 times now and I still look for ways to try and be faster while saving EVERYONE. This game, I don't feel like I even have to try. My first full playthrough , i got 24 of the 25 survivors. DR2? more like missed 12 on my first playthrough. Half the replay fun was the discovering people you missed, and exploring areas you never went. Like I said earlier, i ♥♥♥♥♥♥ around looking for collectibles, and still had time to nab 24/25 survivors first playthrough. Racking up Z's is way too easy now. To the point it isn't fun or as satisfying when you get the higher numbers. They made this game way too easy aimed at people new to the series. The bosses in DR3 are so easy it further helps to destroy the flow of the game. As much as I hate the Slappy boss fight, even after 70 plus playthroughs on DR2, they made this games' bosses way too easy. I died against bosses in DR1 even after knowing the game inside and out. I still die from bosses on DR2, but this game.............. I did not die ONCE during a boss fight in DR3. I only died once total the whole story because I set off a big bomb and was too close to it. No deaths by Zs or boss deaths. The city ' is bigger,' but most of its blacked out and cant be explored.

The game at its core though, IS DEAD RISING. Full of so much cheesy directing, cheesy action sequences, and cheesy cliches, it fits right in with the other games. Most of the game I could not stop laughing. This series continues to grow its cheese factor so much that soon they will have no choice but to form their own Slappy's pizza chain (get it, if you berate the staff you get what's coming to you). I really like though the 60s and 70s B and C movies feel, these games feel like homages to those old cheesy horror movies. Well honestly, the first one was a mall, and the second one was essentially an outdoor/indoor mall-casino.
The very beginning of DR3 I thought there were going a different direction. Started off tense, a little scary, and I thought, "wow, a dead rising game?" Minutes later though I laughed my ass off while saying "Yea, a dead rising game"

Doesn't have the seem feel as 1 and 2. Don't have the same need to come back at this again and again. More like 1 more time to get to 50 (at 42), and then one more time to do it in one perfect playthrough getting the one survivor I missed, which for me I didnt get that feeling in DR1 until the 43rd perfect playthrough, shaving off nearly a half hour from my best playthrough. I didnt get that feeling on DR2 until the 64th perfect playthrough shaving off another 22 min from my best game.
BTW. For those that said the game is full of aiming glitches, and is unplaybale, it IS and ISN'T.
Look at the reviews of most people that said that, even specifically their time. Most people quit right around chapter 2-3. Yes the game gets super laggy, aiming feels broke, and you'll fall through the world sometimes. This is only on you very first install playthrough. When the game first 'opens up,' the next few minutes to half hour will be marked by massive lag as the game uncompresses the world around you for the first time. I nearly agreed with some of the reviews, almost shut off the game and asked for a refund when I stopped a second and realized. I on purposely am playing on a system above min spec, and below rec spec. So I'm playing on Med High Visual Settings. I dialed down the graphics ( by default the game maxed it out to 1080p) to medium then settled on medium high on 720P. I put up with the lag because I suspected that like most games when you 'explored the rest of the map for the first time' the rest of the map's game files will finish uncompressing, 12 minutes later the game had finished uncompressing ( to help it along I stopped using vehicles and sprinting {which make rendering even harder until your rig can catch up} and walked around looking for blueprints and statues). After some truly funny rendering (or lack thereof) glitches, like where i was driving across the highway only to fall through the world and end back up in zen garden. I tested that the lag or glitchiness doesn't last. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tFOR THE ZOMBIE FIGHTER

Pros- A crazy fun zombie game, with many many combos to make weapons or super vehicles. The amount of chaos in this game is insane. A fun story line, with some extra character story lines in case you get bored of the default one. you can recruit survivors and have them fight with you or just send them back to the safe house. There is also a leveling system, which is pretty decent.

Cons- The quests and campaign missions are usually on the opposite side of the map each time. Its very hard to get around, since the vehicles break easily and the hordes of zombies crowding you constantly. Multiple roads are blocked off so there is usually only one way to get to the quest. The amount of zombies can get very annoying since they automatically re generate. Even if you took out 200, 2500 more zombies would spawn. It gets really repetitive when fighting zombies.

Overall- The game is a fun zombie/survival genre with creative ways to get through the night. If you have patience to get around and look forward to traveling for quests a lot then this game is for you.\t\t\t\t\t
