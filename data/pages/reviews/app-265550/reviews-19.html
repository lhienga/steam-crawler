\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDead Rising 3 is a great installment in the franchise that raises the bar set by Dead Rising 2 and Off The Record.

Gameplay is solid, with tons of blueprints to discover that let you combo even more than previous games. Finding what works or what doesn't seemed easy enough, and I never felt like I was in a losing position as long as I had a good combo in hand. The addition of vehicle combos was a welcome addition and I thoroughly enjoyed it however I do wish the vehicle health was a bit better. The hordes of zombies you would come across into are massive and can be challenging when you run into some of the unique zombies such as the football players, prisoners, or firemen. There are other special zombie types but I never saw them as a real threat especially the King Zombies they were a little over hyped via the cutscene introducing them.

Story is just a standard run of the mill Dead Rising story. It's not bad ,but its nothing to really brag about. I did enjoy returning characters that would pop up and seeing them tie the franchise closer together was nice. Side missions were nice and weren't repetitive. Psycho side missions were normally quick but enjoyable and gave you a nice slice of story as a reward.

The game looks fantastic and if you make a custom INI to improve the FOV and unlock the frame rate it makes everything so much better. With a map this big and crowds of zombies this big, I really expected textures to look worse, but was surprised at how well it looked.

If I had to give it a rating Dead Rising 3 would receive a 4 out 5.\t\t\t\t\t
