\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDifferent from previous Dead Rising games in the franchise this one is more of an open world than a big hub world, however in reality the game feels like 4 big hub-worlds separated by several bridges that may serve as loading zones to avoid having a screen when moving from one to another.

In the previous 2 games there were combo weapons and the ability to customize a motorcycle, by adding machine-guns or chainsaws, to make a more effective way of killing zombies. This game develops the concept by adding some combo weapons that can be further upgraded to make a more powerful weapons, as well as vehicle combos that will help you move trough the hordes of zombies with relative ease. However unlike the Dead Rising 2, you must find the blueprints in the world to be able to craft the combo weapons in the first place, but thankfully you can craft the weapons on the spot, without the need to take then to a special room.

Be aware if you attempt to get all the achievements in the game, since there are a few unpatched bugs that will make you start the game from zero, if you get them. I recommend reading on those bugs beforehand, if you are willing to go to the required grind that will be going for 100%. Since I didn't knew of them until I had beat the game once, and had to start a brand new save file.

In the end, the game is worth playing, it may be different from the other Dead Rising games, but there is still enough things similar to them that it does feel like on of them. Unlike Dead Rising 4 or the I Phone port of Dead Rising 1.\t\t\t\t\t
