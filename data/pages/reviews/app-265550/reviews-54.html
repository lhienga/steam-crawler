\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to love this game, but under a truckload of messy game design choices that are constantly at odds with each other, there is an excellent game here. Unfortunately, because of this truckload, this game is just barely good.
-
Dead Rising 3 tries to cater to every conceivable audience and playstyle, and the game suffers immensely because of it (much like another popular Capcom title), every potential challenge in this game is met with fail-safe after fail-safe after fail-safe.
In earlier Dead Risings, the most effective method of traversal is to avoid the zombies, weapons are generally a last resort to avoid being overwhelmed. In Dead Rising 3, you can run, go around, cut, and/or drive through the never-ending horde of zombies, and while this amount of agency sounds appealing on paper, this execution makes the game so mindless and unchallenging you start to feel like the zombies you are avoiding.

As if this wasn't bad enough, you are constantly given points toward a skill tree to become stronger, but it doesn't matter, protagonist Nick Ramos is about as competent at the start of the game as he is at the end
The story is incredibly bare bones, and as much as I want to love our anti-hero protagonist, his potential flaws are always ignored because of the narrative's need to progress.

So why do I still recommend this game despite it's many, many flaws? The most important mechanic in Dead Rising: The Timer.
Make no mistake, even the timer is bloated to give you more than enough time to complete everything in the game, but the sheer idea that this literal ticking time bomb might defeat you is enough to challenge the player, and encourage you to move as quickly as you can. Furthermore, the combo weapons in this game are just plain fun to discover and initially use, that is, before you realize almost every weapon is equally effective.

I'd love to talk more about this game, especially since it was the introduction to the franchise for me, and while it was certainly a divisive entry, I honestly think the game took steps forward, and as many backwards, for the franchise, but that's the meat and potatoes of my thoughts.
