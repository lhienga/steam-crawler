\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a weird one, it adds a lot of new content to the series, which is fun to play around with, at the same time it's strips a lot of the original content from the previous games. While I did enjoy the game when it focused on the fun of killing zombies with dumb and entertaining weapon and vehicle combinations, it did not feel like a Dead Rising game.

Dead Rising 3 strips a lot of that tension with the main story having a giant time limit that feels way too generous and side missions remaining the same, mostly due to the new open world environments it introduces and feeling more like a sandbox. The main problem with this change is that the map, even with some notable locations, feels forgettable, and the reliance of knowing item locations is less needed, as any item can be spawned at lockers in safe houses, even with it having a limit to recharge.

Other factors that I feel plague my enjoyment of the game is that the story was unmemorable compared to the previous games, most missions are fetch quests which feel like chores, and the difficulty spikes around from being fairly easy to painfully difficult. Although, I will give mention to the psychopaths of the game, which are memorable and different compared to the series, either with some fights focusing on a specific gimmick or the psychopath themselves having a unique personality.

Overall, the recommendation of this game depends on your expectations, if you're expecting another dead rising game, you might be a disappointed by the results. If you're coming into the series for the first time and just want to kill a lot of zombies, you will find a lot of enjoyment with this game.\t\t\t\t\t
