Dead Rising was a pretty interesting beast. It was rough, it was unfair, but there was something about it. I mean, we had many games about zombies before it. Way too many, actually. But somehow, Dead Rising's approach felt... I dunno, I can't say ‚Äúrealistic‚Äù, since the game was all about funny action and had some crazy parody elements, but still, it was closer than usual to how zombie apocalypse would probably look like. Long story short, together with some other survivors, we were trapped in a zombie infested shopping mall. And it's not like there was just a couple of zombies here and there. Like, you know, in those Resident Evil games. There were hordes of them. Seriously, there were so many that it was pretty much impossible to kill 'em all. Again and again you were supposed to break through huge crowds of zombies, while hoping none of them will grab you.

Talking 'bout killing, by the way. Another thing about Dead Rising was the fact that you were able to use pretty much everything as a weapon. You wanted to pick up the TV and beat the heck out of the nearby zombie with it? Dead Rising was glad to let you do that. Moreover, the sequel allowed us to combine items to create some crazy weapons. You wanted to combine stuffed bear with a light machine gun? Well, why not! That's what Dead Rising 2 was all about. Unfortunately, aside from that, Dead Rising 2 was pretty much more of exactly the same. We've got ourselves a new main character, gameplay revolved about more than just a mall, but all in all, it was the same exact experience. CAPCOM liked what they've achieved in the first game and were too afraid to experiment further. And it kinda... sucked. I mean, despite all the unique elements, Dead Rising wasn't one of those ‚Äúnext big thing‚Äù games that people were unable to get enough of. So, here comes Dead Rising 3 and, naturally, everybody wanted something new from it. The next big step for the series. Did we get something like that here? Well, yes. And no.

Like Dead Rising 2 before it, Dead Rising 3 is all about a brand new feature. And while it's a pretty cool and impressive thing, like combo weapons before it, the new feature in Dead Rising 3 is just that ‚Äì just a new feature. Long story short, in this game we'll be able... to combine vehicles. Bring two cars together and boom! You've got a crazy new something at your hands. Totally cool stuff (seriously, you won't forget the first time you'll do that any time soon), but... everything else in the game is pretty much the same. It's our usual Dead Rising thingy all over again. We're on time, we run around, doing optional quests (which should be completed within a certain time limit), we fight so-called psychopaths (optional bosses with totally crazy design that'll give you access to some crazy weapons), multiple endings included. In other words, even though technically, combo vehicles is an interesting feature, I can't help but seeing this game as a disappointment. I mean, come on. We already had more than enough of this. Why doing the same exact thing again?

It's not that simple, though. See, even though there were vehicles before (it's not something that was added in this game), those were never the main dish. While here? Here, it's all about cars. Which... creates a certain aftertaste. And I can't say it's a good one. For two reasons. First - it destroys the original feeling. You know, when it was actually scary to rush through the horde of deadly monsters. Unlike previous games, this one doesn't have that certain "zombie apocalypse simulator" thingy. You get into a car and you're like "Carmageddon time, b*tchez!". That's what this game is all about. And the second thing - focusing on cars inevitably made this game to feel GTA-ish. I mean, let's just admit it ‚Äì when it's about open-world games with cars, it's hard not to compare it with Rockstar's biggest franchise. And technically, that's just it. What we have here is pretty much GTA with zombies. Which isn't really a bad thing per se. The problem is - when you begin comparing Dead Rising 3 with GTA for real, it's hard not to notice how poor this game is.

And not only because Dead Rising 3 looks bad. Even though yes, it looks like crap. I mean, come on! 2013 was a year when games like Assassin's Creed IV: Black Flag, The Last of Us and Bioshock Infinite were released! And yet, CAPCOM gave us... this? OK, OK, this game had limited budget. But you know what? Even as a AA game, Dead Rising 3 looks bad. Look at Saints Row IV, for example. Limited budget, same year, way better visuals. But OK, whatever. We can deal with bad graphics. I can see how they had hard time dealing with performance issues due to zombie hordes, while visuals aren't the most important thing about video games anyway. Like I said, that's not the reason why Dead Rising 3 can't win any comparisons. The real problem is ‚Äì it's limited as F. The world map is way too small (you can cross it in only a few minutes as long as we're talking about driving), there's close to no activities (which is stupid, since hordes of zombies create a lot of opportunities for classic fun, similar to what we had in the first two GTA titles, which most of the people didn't even try playing for main missions), there are not enough optional objectives (there are less than thirty of those), collectibles, while not being completely useless (they'll reward you with the experience) are boring as heck, the story, while having some cute new characters (the love story with the lady called Rhonda that we'll get at the end is pretty touching) and also doing nice nods to previous games, is way too generic and uninspired, main missions are way too basic and straightforward, you can beat the game in only ten hours and so on.

Like I said, when you compare this game with pretty much any GTA-like game, Dead Rising 3 has no way to win. And that's the tragedy of this game. Dead Rising 3 is a story 'bout how fresh and daring idea led to a very strange result. Instead of becoming the next big thing in Dead Rising franchise, this game became more like a crappy GTA clone with zombies. And with so many GTA clones on market, wasting your time on this game? I don't think it's a good idea. Especially since, like all previous Dead Rising games, it's still a rough and clunky experience. PC port is far from being good too. For example, there's a weird infinite loading glitch. Sure, it comes with all the DLC included (aside from other thing, there's an episodic expansion), but still, CAPCOM didn't care about its PC audience much. So... yeah. Even though I always had a special place in my heart for Dead Rising, just because of it not being afraid of experiments, this game? In this game everything went wrong. And even though I can't say that Dead Rising 3 is completely unplayable... let's just say it began to lose what was used to be cool about the originals without being able to provide any proper alternatives. Dead Rising 3 was born because of cars and it died because of those. Nice example of how sometimes the ideas just... don't work the way they were supposed to. Dixi.\t\t\t\t\t
