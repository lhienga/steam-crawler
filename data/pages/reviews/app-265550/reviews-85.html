Ah man... How do I put this...

I'll be honest. By the end of this game, I was burned out. Similar to Dead Rising 1 and me trying to grind for the Real Mega Buster. Except that was optional. Here, it was necessary in order to complete the story/episodes. But here goes.

Gameplay: This game is a 3rd person zombie horror/action game. Unlike the previous games though, the camera is more focused behind the protagonist. Also, like the previous games in the series, you can fight optional psychopaths as well.

The cool part about the psychopaths in this game is that they represent the 7 deadly sins! Pretty cool concept.

You use weapons and vehicles to crush and slay zombies. As before, you can combine weapons together. But in this game, you can combine various vehicles together now. My favorite was Rollerhawg, bulldozer and motorbike put together, with a freakin' flamethrower at the front haha. But, I never used vehicles much to slaughter zombies. I went more for the handy combo weapons.

Unlike Dead Rising 2, you can actually combine two weapons ANYWHERE now, instead of just needing a maintenance room to do so, which I found super helpful. You can also combine the same weapon with different combos as well, not just needing specific items.

There's a Nightmare mode that makes the game "harder". If you want to experience Dead Rising 1 on your first playthrough, try this mode on level 1.

Now, for getting around the area...
This was my biggest problem for me.

Sometimes it felt like AGES traveling from one city to another. Traveling from one city just to talk to someone, then going to another city just to collect some item, then going BACK to the same city to talk to the person, was really time consuming, not to mention, really repetitive. Yes, we have vehicles, but we also have those zombies as well, stopping you, and making it more time consuming to get to another city. Not to mention the vehicles could break from crashing, zombies, mercenary gunners, the bikers throwing molotovs at you, and random explosive barrels all over the place on the ground.

Why couldn't there be a passage-like area shortcut that transports them from one city to another in a few seconds, like in the previous Dead Rising games? A zipline to take them from city to city? A helicopter that can easily and quickly take them from place to place? It's really the main thing that prevented me from enjoying the game much. Especially playing the DLC episodes.

Story:
Compared to the previous games, this game is a lot more serious. Even the graphics are a bit more darker in color. I didn't experience much comedic parts, but more memorable serious parts. The story in this game connects the 1st and 2nd games. It had actually a couple of interesting characters that I liked, such as the biker gang leader and Gary. It was a nice little story to conclude the series in my view.

Co-op:
The Co-op in this game is MUCH better compared to DR2, seeing as how you have a lot more freedom to do things like scavenging entire cities, saving a lot more things together with your buddy like collectibles, and it all feels easier to understand what's going on.

Haha, from my co-op experience, I've had a couple. From some dude kicking me from his online game while voicing and "expressing" himself, to another carrying me the whole game. One kept attacking me, and I wasn't even able to join another one's for some reason. Regardless, I had fun with it.

Music:
Not much to say. It had a lot of nice dubstep music for the main themes. Was a bit underwhelming that there weren't many rock music tracks here. It would of felt amazing for the bosses. Gives off that aggresive/tense feeling. Hence why the "Pride" boss theme is my absolute favorite. There's also a couple store/car soundtracks that I liked as well. And there's even referenced music from DR2 in this game. Not much though.

Achievements:
In order to 100% achievement complete this game, you need to finish a lot of things in the DLC episodes. I liked them a bit I guess, and was going to try to go for 100%, but it brings me back to traveling from city to city for all the DLC episodes. At least the Episodes give more playtime! It took me 1-3 hours to finish each.

I tried to speedrun the game on Nightmare mode after being level 50. Was pretty fun actually.

And finally....

All in all, it was an ok game. It was a Dead Rising game, but it didn't entirely feel like a Dead Rising game. If you aren't affected by long amounts of time spent from city to city, or do have the patience and want to experience this game, try it out. Me, I prefer this over the infamous Dead Rising 4, a game I'm not going to try out, I'm sorry haha. But it was a blast playing this trilogy!
