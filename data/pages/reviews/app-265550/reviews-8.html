\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as Dead Rising games go, Dead Rising 3 is alright. That's the problem, really--that it's just alright. It's not "good" like DR1 and especially not "great" like DR2. Yes I am going to be comparing this game a lot to its predecessors.

The first things that threw me off the Dead Rising vibe were the lack of colours and the overall dour tone of the game. From my understanding, they really wanted to sell an "apocalyptic" feel to the world; I get that Los Perdidos being a literal city and losing the entire population to a zombie outbreak is oh so tragic, but like...I don't want that in Dead Rising, I want to run around a brightly-lit mall styling all over the zombies on a skateboard while wearing mascot costumes, complete with mall music playing from the speakers; THAT'S Dead Rising to me. The overall darker tone was made evident in the first few minutes when I had to go through an abandoned zombie containment unit and proceeded to go into the graphics settings and turned the gamma way above the recommended level because I COULDN'T SEE ANYTHING.

The game is also stupid easy. The first few hours were actually pretty fun; run around, pick up objects, kill zombies and try to survive. Pretty tough, pretty challenging, standard Dead Rising fare. But like many games not exactly similar to this one, powercreep settled in and started ruining everything. DR2 introduced "maintenance rooms", where you could take two specific objects and combine them into a powerful combo weapon. DR3 took out the middle man and lets you make combo weapons anywhere, so now you're more incentivised to look for specific combine-able objects instead of relying on what you have, taking out that "survival" feel. If that wasn't enough, you can also spend skill points to unlock "combo categories", which allows you to make certain combo weapons using any object in the category. There's this one combo gun, called the Z.A.R., that turns boss fights into jokes; the blueprint for it calls for an assault rifle and a shotgun specifically, which aren't the most common of things to find so you can't just rely on it. Then you unlock the "firearm" combo category and gain the ability to make it using literally any two guns! And this game is set in suburban America which means you can find guns literally anywhere! Behind store counters, in people's houses, even just lying around out in the middle of the streets. I understand that this was meant to showcase Nick's(the protag's) engineering skills, but come on; don't do that at the expense of engagement.

"But you'd have to level up to unlock the category first! Surely that takes a while with Dead Rising's PP system." No. Firstly, combo categories aren't level-gated; you can get the firearm category right away. Secondly, that's another thing DR3 changed: you can grind PP. Every zombie you kill nets you a few PP, and using combo weapons nets you more. The game also keeps track of your killstreak, which gives you higher PP gain multipliers the higher it is. So here's what I did: grab a super combo weapon or two that has ridiculous AoE(for free from a weapon locker because this game wasn't easy enough), find one of the several densely-populated streets, and go to town. Within a minute or two, your killstreak will have reached 1000, earning you a x10 multiplier to your PP gain, and you'll probably have leveled up three times by that point. Rinse and repeat. I got to level 50 around the 3/4 point of the game, and then proceeded to overlevel multiple times and get a surplus of skill points by the end.

Time limits weren't a problem either. I know people dunked on DR4 because they removed time limits entirely, but I swear DR3 isn't much different. I took a good chunk of time just goofing around, finding collectibles, and exploring, and even then I never came close to running out of time on any mission.

As for Los Perdidos being a lot bigger than either the mall from DR1 or Fortune City from DR2: that's certainly the case, but it's also a case of quantity over quality. Los Perdidos just lacks character; it's a generic-looking city whose most overt characteristics are its desaturated colours and the freeway running through its proximity. The map isn't presented as a travel brochure like in the previous games; it's just a map. Shops aren't even marked anymore, and coupled with the blandness of the setting, they just leave even less of an impression; I think this is exemplified by the way your operator(i.e. the guy relaying mission info to you) says "the theatre" or "that all-you-can-eat restaurant" instead of using the names of whatever place he's referring to. I think having vehicles as your main travel method also doesn't help; you zip by everything so quickly that you hardly think about any given location.

Oh, and the writing is stinted and awkward. Dialogue lines don't so much "flow" into one another as they do "happen" one after another. The so-called "romance" between Nick and Annie, particularly, just kinda happened.

About the only thing that DR3 improved over the previous games is the scale and spectacle of zombie massacre, albeit at the cost of challenge. There's fun to be had here, that much I can say, but it's the bland, fills-your-stomach-not-your-heart kind of fun. I imagined myself being asked "Should I get Dead Rising 3?" and my immediate response was "Get either versions of Dead Rising 2 instead". If you want a good Dead Rising experience, get Dead Rising 2, and if you want a good mob-massacring romp, get one of the Dynasty Warriors games.\t\t\t\t\t

Just play Dead Rising 1

Pros:
+ Fun gameplay
+ Psychopaths

Cons:
- Bad port
- Insufferable padding
- Cringe story

Dead Rising 3 can be fun but it feels like the game tries it's best to ruin it. The port itself is embarrassing, as the framerate is locked at 30fps and will need the player to dig into the files to fix it. The padding between missions is the biggest thing that killed my vibe for the game. Literally after every mission you will need to drive to opposite sides of the map over and over and over.... The story isn't long but my god did they design the missions around wasting your time. I can't complain too much since I got this dirt cheap but I highly suggest playing the first Dead Rising instead. It's port runs much better and the missions and overall story are way more interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are better games than this, hell, there are games in this very franchise that came before that are objectively of higher quality... but holy smokes, i couldn't stop playing this one. What about Dead Rising 3 captivates me just a little more than any previous entry? Its not certainly not the plot, which i was half-way snoozing through most of the time. It certainly is not its cast, although Nick and along with some of the psychopaths tickled my fancy along with Gary. So what is it about Dead Rising 3, huh? What is this game's deal?

Everything else.

I don't think its up to debate that this is the best these games have ever played and felt. The movement, the weaponry and overall feel of the game is nop notch. Now, precise jumping to get some of the collectibles? That's where the movement has some issues. Aside? Big step up from 2. The environment is also really nice looking and detailed, though i would've preferred more color.

The addition of vehicles and combo vehicles was a great decision, in my opinion, even with all the roadblocks and crap in the way all the time. Its still quite great for navigating all the mayhem. Speaking of...
This game is complete chaos, it never slows down for anything. There's always zombies everywhere, you constantly have to keep moving, stuff is always breaking or blowing up. It really reinforces that feeling of helplessness and that things are completely D-O-N-E fakked.

However, amidst all this chaos and death, lies a very mediocre plot. I think where the story mostly falters, unfortunately, is in its ideas and execution. The main premise is coolio and what the writers came up with are interesting concepts. (The illegals plotline, Carlito's orphans, Chuck Greene, Isabela, Hemlock's main motivation and Hilde Schmittendorf to name a select few) However, most of these ideas are either rushed into the story or don't get enough time to develop into something interesting... or at worst, they are completely barren. The story picks up by the final hour, but by then, its far too little too late.

Not to mention, this game is shockingly easy, even on the supposed "hard mode". This affects all venues of the gameplay, especially the upgrade system and the Psychopaths who are laughably less of a threat than they should be and not much of a challenge to defeat. I believe i beat three Psychopaths without having my life drained from them directly. I don't mind a fun game i can breeze through, which this is, but it feels like Dead Rising 3 is too afraid of you not having fun. It gives you overpowered upgrades you can get any time that makes combo weapon creation far too easy (combining random materials together to create massive weapons in a way that is completely non-sensical), zombies don't do a lot of damage and psychopaths are as i mentioned, of very little threat to you. There were moments where the game clearly wanted me to engage with some of it's enemies that it had laid out for me to defeat, but i could walk right past them without any issue. Kind of depressing.

The side quests are as strong as they've ever been, even though there's a little less variation with different scenarios like there was in the games that came before, its still great. My favourite one was towards the end of the game, with Meryl in the "Memory Lane" side mission. The very topic the game touches on, our demise and our fear of it alongside appreciating older memories is something i've been thinking a lot about these past few weeks. As such, that really did resonate with me in a lot of ways. I needed that tonight.

In short, this is the best Dead Rising for everything except the story and characters.

It is rather a shame that this was the last game in the series they made, though. I wonder what a fourth game could look like. Maybe they could bring Frank back.

7.8/10\t\t\t\t\t
