\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun title for both seasoned veterans & newcomers.

It's got a healthy amount of action, with character building & customization, fun areas to explore, memorable characters, with a unique horror/zaniness that is distinctly Dead Rising.

For better or worse, it's very similar, but doesn't feel quite like Dead Rising. It's too different. It shines in its own ways & goes off in its own directions - in some ways very well. Personally, it feels more like a spiritual successor to the Dead Rising series, rather than a full, official title - but that isn't to say it isn't fun. It's a great time, but certain mechanics & changes will definitely put off some players.

I was very worried & skeptical, especially with how the game launched & given the reviews here about crashes, deleted saves, frame rate drops, etc. I bit the bullet during a sale however, & purchased it for both myself & my sister. We've had nothing worse than the rare light frame rate drops & the co-op has been a blast.

If you like zombie & action games, this game definitely warrants a look. Even if you don't like the older Dead Rising games, or if you don't like where this newer title goes, there's still plenty of fun to be had here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere is my Dead Rising 3 review or more like:
"Yo, remember how Dead Rising 1,2 and Off the Record was really good?"



1) game design, collectibles, map, minimap
===============
Yo, remember how Dead Rising 1,2 and Off the Record...

...had not tried so hard to be like Grand Theft Auto of Saints Row type game with big, empty open world and obnoxious long distances for everything?

...had not tried to be so serious and edgy?

...world was way more colorful and overall nice to look at?

...had toilets placed in good distances from each other while showing their locations on the map just in case if player did need to use them?

...did allow you to move 5 steps without need to kill 50 zombies?

...had no annoying roadblocks in every single corner?

...you did not need to travel 1000 metters to the next target location every single time?

...did allow you to close doors to prevent zombies from coming inside the room?

...had functionating mirrors?

...wasn't infested with collectible and didn't had "collectible nearby" notification every 30 seconds?

...did not had Tragic Ends that are the dumbest idea for ANY collectible EVER?
(you basically stand above a body of generic nobody and feel SADNESS
for 4 seconds while being unable to do anything as SAD music plays (and probably you will get hit by a zombie anyway during that considering placement of those collectibles)).

...had a well designed, multi layer map with area descriptions that did matter something?

...did not even need a minimap because how smaller, more detailed, good and memorable main map layout was?

...night cycle was not so annoying to deal with due ridiculous amount of zombie horde that exist now purely to waste your time on roads while driving?



2) main quests, side quests, game difficulty, endings
===============
Yo, remember how Dead Rising 1,2 and Off the Record...

...both main quests and sidequests was very unique from each other and fun to do?

...had perfect amount of sidequest to do (not too much yet not too little)?

...was not bloated by "kill all zombies in this area" or "reach this spot in X amount of time" or "collect X amount of Y item" quests?

...had you actually save survivors instead "kill all zombies in area to make puff out of existence"?

...difficulty and challenge actually did meant something and zombies was not such a joke to deal with?

...had multiple different and unique endings?



3) protagonist, RPG elements, weapons, vehicles
===============
Yo, remember how Dead Rising 1,2 and Off the Record...

...had interesting protagonist that was not acting like a big cry baby during zombie outbreak?

...player could deal with zombie horde by simple walking, rolling or jump kicking through them?

...player was not getting stuck on every single zombie due weird hitboxes and model ententities?

...you did not need to wait for crafting animation to finish every single time?

...did not had character movement so clunky as in Dead Rising 3, especially in term of running and turning left or right that takes FOREVER to do for some reason?

...had well designed RPG stat system that was making you feel excited with every single new level up which did unlock new skills, weapon combos and making your character overall stronger and more effective?

...did not suffer from Borderlands syndrome that was making generic enemies a damage sponges?

In short: in previous games no matter how low your ATTACK stat was - your combo weapons would still kill a generic zombie in one hit and generic human enemy in 2-3 swings.
Well, developers of Dead Rising 3 had a WAY more smart idea for fun - make your average zombie so tough that you need sometimes even 2-6 swings with your combo weapon to kill a single zombie. FUN!

...was not making you read a boring designed giant skill tree for 20 minutes?

...had player not hear "HUFF PUFF SO TIRED OH GOD NOOOOOO HUFF PUFF HUFF HERE WE HUFF GO AGAIN PUFF COME GET SOME F*CK F*CK AHHHHHHH YEAHHHH BABY GO HUFF PUFF" every single 5 seconds?

...did not make your character super overpower after reaching max level with infinite stamina or infinfite vehicle durability or health regeneration or being able to equip all magazines at once?"

...combo weapons actually did meant something while trying remember all possible combinations and what weapon was the the best tool for the right job?
Dead Rising 3 have so many generic or bad combo weapons that an average player will not gonna even see 1/3 of all in game weapons.

...player did not need to make a new combo vehicle every 2 minutes?

...did made using vehicles more special way to travel once in a while and not a constant requirement for reaching every single place?"

...did not had vehicle slowing down to comical speed after hitting a single zombie?

...had vehicles not exploding after hitting only couple of zombies?



4) story, characters, psychopaths, soundtrack
===============
Yo, remember how Dead Rising 1,2 and Off the Record...

...had am actual entertaining story from start to finish, a proper conclusion and great final boss fight?

...had memorable and important for a story cast of supporting characters?

...had many colorful and entertainingly NPC's, each with own name, carefully hand crafted unique face, clothes, design and voice?

...had many unique, memorable and fun to fight psychopaths each with own fighting style and song?

...had an actualy good and memorable soundtrack?



5) optimization
===============
Yo, remember how Dead Rising 1,2 and Off the Record...

...did run at smooth 60 frames per second?

...didnt had any sort of framerate slowdowns?

...didnt had absurdly long loading screens?

...didnt need to have equally long loading screens while going back to main menu?

...didnt had as horrible and agressive texture/model pop-ups everywhere as Grand Theft Auto 5?

...didnt had ginormous amount of bugs, glitches and ragdoll physics going crazy every few seconds?





6) downloadable content
===============
Yo, remember how Dead Rising 1,2 and Off the Record...

...didn't have sh*tty DLC's that last for 2 hours each where you played as generic looking nobody that did ruin whole protagonist shtick of being able to craft weapons and vehicles on a go due being a skilled mechanic?

...did not took place only during the night with triple the amount of annoying zombies on your path?

...not make a player go for a dull fetch quest after fetch quest after "kill everybody in this place" after fetch quest after "kill everybody
in that spot" after going ANOTHER 1000 METTERS TO THE NEXT TARGET LOCATION TO PICK UP A VERY SPECIFIC TRUCK THAT YOU HAVE TO BABYSIT FOR ANOTHER 15 MINUTES OF DRIVING 1500 METTERS AWAY?





7) last few words
===============
Yo, remember how Dead Rising 1,2 and Off the Record was REALLY good and WAY better video game than Dead Rising 3 and 4?
Because I do.

Yet despite being way weaker product than Dead Rising 1,2 and Off The Record - at least Dead Rising 3 still tries to innovate the gameplay while being a slightly above average video game.

Something that sadly you cannot say about a Fallout 76 of Dead Rising franchise that is Dead Rising 4.

Let's hope that Capcom will try to fix this franchise and next Dead Rising game will ACTUALLY go back this time to the roots with smaller but more dense detailed world,
good RPG system and less walking miles across galaxy to reach your destination.
Sigh, one can always dream...

If I could, I would give this game "neutral rating" but considering that there is no such option and that Dead Rising 3 is still an actually functionating product (unlike Dead Rising 4)
I am gonna give it a "thumb up" to this game.

Despite it's many flaws and issues, at the end it is still a Dead Rising game by both gameplay and soul (unlike Dead Rising 4).

This game does deserve to have mixed reviews tho.
Nothing more, nothing less.

Dead Rising 4 on the other hand - deserve to be send back to Hellish hole which it came from and forgotten FOREVER!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis Dead Rising is honestly my favorite. I think in Dead Rising 2, they played it safe and really didn't push the envelope, but with Dead Rising 3, they took the training wheels off and made the game CV wanted to make. It takes the best qualities of Dead Rising and puts them in an open world setting, while removing the poor qualities of the older titles. No more survivor escorts in favor of the posse system, which allows you to team up with survivors for some zombie butt kicking. I think this game has the best gameplay out of all of them as well and they take the combo weapons up a notch introducing tier 2, 3 and 4 combos with some amazing results. The story in this game is where everything pays off from the first two (only downside is it acknowledges Case West as Canon but you can't play it anywhere other than Xbox). One of my favorite design choices is how they went full send into terror, which was a wise choice. As many know, Dead Rising is a serious story with goofy moments. This game turns it into a horror game with a serious story and very goofy moments. It does everything right in my opinion and brings Dead Rising to the open world. Side quests are varied and enjoyable and the story is a nice 15 hour jaunt. This game also has very good collectibles imo. It puts them in places where you can explore new spots and really see the whole map. The map itself is pretty big, but not overwhelming and the seeing it change over the course of the story is awesome. My only complaint is the PC version is kinda wonky. Lots of texture pop-outs and a the inability to change the FOV really hurt the experience, but overall this is a great package and I really recommend this not only as a fan of Dead Rising, but zombie games in general. Here's hoping DR5 captures the same magic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Dead Rising series is unique to today's genre of horror in that it the games allow you to revert back to the start of the game, and start fresh, while retaining your character.

This feature is more of an essential, in the first title. A player could too easily find themselves backed into a corner, whether it be a boss fight that is too difficult without further character progression, or a main story mission that requires passing an impossible distance in a small time frame.

At these junctions, you would simply restart, and continue leveling your character, in hopes of achieving the ability needed to move further in the game. By Dead Rising 3, the game is a lot easier, so this mechanic is not stressed as much.

To clarify, you can revert back to any chapter you have completed, or simply restart the game. Your character retains his PP level and skills, a long with any held weapons and known blueprints. Saved survivors, defeated Psychos and cleared safe houses will all reset to the default state at the point in the game you have moved back too. These can all be revisited, whether you intend to correct a failure, or simply earn some more PP.

There are two important things to note:

- Survivors reset. You will need to save them all in a single run to earn the achievement for rescuing all survivors.
- Some side missions require previous actions. For example, there is a certain Psycho mission that appears in the final chapters, but only if you have previously rescued a certain survivor.

Never feel bad about missing side missions in favor of progressing the main story. The later part of the game provides far greater opportunity to level. It is undeniably easier to rescue all the survivors once you have a few super combo weapons and the level 50 skills.\t\t\t\t\t
