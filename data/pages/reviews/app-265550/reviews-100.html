\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played Dead Rising 1 and 2 to death and love them so it was about time to try out the 3:rd game also. I like this one also, but there was something nagging at me while playing that made me not enjoy it nearly as much as the previous two and I could not quite put my finger on it for a long time. It was not until I was about to put it down after beating it that I realized that the main fault with Dead Rising 3 and the thing that made it not quite stand up to its predecessors was also its main "feature" and that is the massive amount of zombies.

While it is very impressive and obvious that the developers want to show of this in the game with the great amount of zombies (I think I read that it is possible to render 1500 zombies on screen at one time) I also think this hurts the game. The game is just too frantic with too much going on at all times, there is never any downtime to create some sense of breathing room between the action. There is just zombies everywhere at all times and they are also much more aggressive than in the past games often throwing themself after you as you pass to grab you and mostly I got the feeling of seeing them as a sort of obstacle and irritation instead of a background threat. They are just in the way all the time between you and your goal and I found myself very frustrated and irritated many times when I tried to go somewhere and they would grab me or block my path while in a vehicle. A feeling I never had in the past games. The massive amount of them also make it feel quite pointless to actually fight them. Fighting and killing zombies is more of a thing you do here to get pp (xp). Even when you are in your garage to choose a vehicle the zombies is constantly at your neck and interrupting the process. There is just no pacing in the action.

One factor that might have contributed to my frustration was that the HUD clutter in this game is massive if you don't tweak some of the files and get rid of the HUD. They have a neat feature to hide and show the map and "quests" with the dpad, but for some strange reason you can't hide other elements ingame which is extremely annoying. I ended up tweaking the game to hide everything, including all the pickup icons (I found no way to just show the pickup icons and hide the "quest arrows" at the same time), this made it very frustrating to pick things up though as I would often pick up the wrong thing by mistake. I just wish it was standard in all games already to have an extensive HUD option panel in the options.

That said the game is still fun and it is interesting to see the game in a more open area like a city instead of a more closed of place like a mall or casino, though I actually think the more closed of spaces in the previous games work better and had more character (Overall I would say that this game has much less charm and individual style than the previous games)

There is also a really good sense of persistence in the game that I have rarely seen in other games. Often I would leave a vehicle in a spot in the game and return hours later to find it still parked in the same spot which is really cool and I would wish was a thing in all games of this type.

All in all it is a fun game, but not as good as its predecessors and it introduces some things that are making it less enjoyable and have less charm than the previous games in the series.

Some other small pros and cons:

Pros:

- All NPC:s have voices this time around
- The combo vehicles are really neat
- Much better NPC survivor AI than in previous games
- No loading times between areas like in the previous games


Cons:

- This game has some "special zombies", something I don't remember the previous games had and where more of a zombie puritan nature, which was very nice and refreshing in the myrriad of zombie games with all kinds of silly zombie types. I really hope future Dead Rising games will go back to the zombie puritan way.
- The timer was a bit novel in the first game, but it feels like it fits much worse here and just gives a sense of even more stress to this already too hectic and chaotic game
- I liked the simple resque missions of NPC:s better in the past games (even with the horrible survivor AI) instead of the often very nonsensical fetch quests here
- Quite clumsy inventory
- There is something "wrong" with the controls and camera, making everything much harder and frustrating to control than necessary. Probably it is mostly a factor of the complete chaos going on at all times where the camera and controls just don't "keep up" with the action
- Artificially "buffering" up games with pointless collectibles strewn around the map does not make any game more fun
- Probably a candidate for the most pointless and dragged out end-game last mission in
gaming history\t\t\t\t\t
