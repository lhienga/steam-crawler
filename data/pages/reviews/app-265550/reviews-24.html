\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing Dead Rising 3, I have to give it all up to Capcom, they are the originators of the survivor horror genre in video games. From Resident Evil to Dead Rising of course. I like that you don't have to play the previous games in the series since the 1st Dead Rising came out in '06, the sequel in 2010 to DR3: Apocalypse Edition because the graphics from the two games alone are dated, and rightfully so. In every Dead Rising game, you are introduced to a new protagonist with supporting characters, over-the-top, out-there, REALLY OUT-THERE boss characters, even ones based off the SEVEN DEADLY SINS, and the most obvious of adversaries, hordes upon hordes of zombies everywhere. And if you stay in the same place for too long, they will creep up and surround you to do you in. Since I am not a keyboard player, the PC controller I use suits me just fine, the controls are somewhat responsive but could use a little more improvement. Let's break Dead Rising 3: Apocalypse Edition down, shall we???

GRAPHICS & PRESENTATION:
On the surface, DR3:AE, though the game is nearly 9 yrs old, the graphics are improved from the previous titles, however, in the cutscenes, there is clipping in the in-game animations. I think this is due to my hardware specs, specifically, my video card (NVIDIA GeForce GTX 650). That's my cross to bear. I feel if and when I step up my video card game, those cutscenes would be more fluid. As far as the graphics go when doing real-time gameplay, it's a double-edged sword (no pun there). When the main character moves on foot, the animations are much smoother where as when the character gets behind the wheel of the various vehicles in-game, the regular, unmodded vehicles don't move as smooth as the modded, weaponized counterparts, such as the downloadable weaponized vehicles like the Rollerhawg that has flamethrowers on the sides and has a steamroller on the front. I also like the silver bike that has circular saws that cuts up zombies like a Chop-A-Matic food process only on wheels, plus I like how I'm able to do burnouts that literally burns zombies and non-zombies to a crisp. What's Not To Love About That? But when it comes to the thousands of thousands of zombies on screen at once, I get creeped out at their overall animations. Even after you sawed em up, sliced 'em up, they still keep coming! And IF YOU HAPPEN TO PLAY THIS GAME AT NIGHT LIKE I DO, IN THE DARK EVEN, IT'S EVEN MORE CREEPY!!! SO PLAY DURING THE DAY. Every corner is a zombie, more zombies, and worth noting, the zombies get even more aggressive at night even when their eyes light up bright red, so keep that in mind. And as far as the

GAMEPLAY:
DR3:AE's gameplay is everything you would expect from the previous titles, but with improvements and varieties. You the player have weapons combos and vehicle combos when fending off the endless hordes of the undead. Certain combo weapons are obvious, others are unique, and really weird. Take a huge teddy bear and an automatic weapon, and youve got the Freedom Bear, which will automatically fire at the zombies Rambo-style, or takes some gems and a simple flashlight, and you can make a lightsaber that slices zombies up many ways and many times. You are the McGuyver of the zombie apocalypse. As I mentioned before about the combo vehicles, you take two of the required vehicles, and mod them into death machines. Worth noting, you learn about the weapons and vehicle combos when you pick up the blueprints scattered all over the map. With the combo weapons, they're usually within reach after grab the blueprint, and you can make the combo weapons in seconds. But for the combo vehicles, even after you picked up the blueprint, you would have to drive the vehicles to nearest garage to turn them into those zombie killing machines. it helps if you have additional players in the co-op system. Let's talk about that. Anytime, you or another can enter in one's session so you don't go in on the zombie killing quest alone. You would need all the help you can get. My only complaint I have with the co-op feature of this game is since i have a mic, I would like to verbally communicate with live players in the session, because non-verbal communication just won't cut it. I'm not sure if Dead Rising 3 has the voice chat feature or not. That would be great and invaluable to me. However, most times, another player doesn't join in, so I help some random NPCs in -game. There are ones you meet along the way who will eventually join you after you do a favor for them. You also have to keep an eye on them if they don't have a weapon in hand to defend themselves and/or low on their health. There are times when I accidentally strike them when fighting off the zombies. The AI needs improvement though. Now for the

SOUND:
Dead Rising 3's in game sound and score is interactive, especially when the zombies gang up on you if you don't run and kill, so keep moving and keep slicing. Your best bet is to run to a working vehicle, weaponized preferred, and get to higher ground. The zombies have several variations in their design as well as their many forms of attack and defense. For example, there are some zombie football players who will make a gut-wrenching scream before they bum rush you to the ground, but they don't go down quick even when you attack them straightaway. So, you would have to use heavy weapon attacks or a CQC (Close Quarters Combat) grapple move that snaps them with a crunching sound. And the A. I. has its moments too.

ENEMY A.I.
The enemy A. I. in Dead Rising 3 is somewhat impressive, but also varies. The zombies become aggressive when night falls, and you have to mind your health, and move and slash your way to safety. The human enemy factions are more responsive and more formidable. They can eve dodge your attacks as if they are super human. You would have to go in on the using lethal attacks to take them down if you have.

FUN FACTOR:
Dead Rising 3: Apocalypse Edition seems to have a replay value. I find it deviously, satisfying when I cut through the zombies in half, quartering them, decapitating them. Gory as all get out. There is a variety of weapon and vehicle combos to discover. It like playing a George A. Romero-esque film, only you get to control the action.

So, give Dead Rising 3: Apocalypse Edition a try.\t\t\t\t\t
