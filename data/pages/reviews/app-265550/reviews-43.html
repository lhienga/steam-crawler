\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: Revisited after calming down

This game is unfinished and disappointing compared to Dead Rising 2.

0 story, 0 actual characters, 0 reasons to care about anything going on.
I know the game technically has a story at the final hours (Conclusion C6~C7) but its way too late by the time you arrive at it.

None of this game takes itself seriously and I know that people want DR to be this dumb shlocky b-movie horror series, but a lack of ANYONE caring or being invested in the plot makes this game extremely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ boring. The only characters who get anything are Rhonda and Gary, and most of the game isn't even about them meaning the most developed characters have zero time to speak with Nick or even scenes focusing on the player watching them by themselves. Combined with the fact that you can beat the game in about 2 hours because of how short the chapters are, there's so little to be invested in with DR3. Nicks closest friend since childhood barely gets any backstory other than they were close. It's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ absurd. Whats the point of all these characters when only TWO of them actually get development?

The gameplay is objectively better than DR2 with it having the series best combo weapons and combat systems like the combo meter and special moves that help you clear a crowd, but it also comes with over-emphasis on the world being bigger and driving. Driving itself would be okay but there are unbreakable roadblocks that force you to walk places in each area, and vehicles slow down when you bump zombies which means you cant just enjoy driving through enemies unless you have a combo vehicle which are also fun but most of them are bad other than the Rollerhawg. Speaking of driving, you cant flip vehicles so if the game flings your car because you hit a bump, you lose it. There are plot missions with vehicles and if they flip, you lose, reopen your last save.

There's so much ♥♥♥♥ that needs to be fixed in this game that I cant understand why they were left in. Zombie Swim now barely works and zombies are a complete ♥♥♥♥♥ to get past in groups. The FOV of the game is so bad I had to look up a guide on how to increase the distance so I could actually see everything around me. The performance is abysmal on current-day tech and the game constantly has rendering problems.

This game is also a bug filled nightmare in which achievements that take hours to do will suddenly bug out, erase your progress, and require you to reset your run. That or the game will just decide that you ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ suck and delete your entire save because you closing it for the night corrupted it. I shouldn't have to explain that an achievement that is noted as being extremely volatile in PC and CONSOLE ports is unacceptable and should have been immediately fixed instead of just left in the game. The fact this bug-ridden nightmare was just allowed to exist in this state rather than being pulled until it was fixed is extremely revealing to Capcom's ethics in game releases.

The story is pretty much just walk to a location to be told to go somewhere else, or waste time until the person you need to talk to is ready. You'll never have a problem with the time limit in this game so if thats what you wanted to hear, feel free to just waste 5 days ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ around and then rush the plot. Most of it is just one-line explanations or unfunny jokes that lead into the next unexplained or detailed plot development with characters that have hilariously little to say about the current situations going on.

Los Perdidos is an interesting and varied location as expected, but the distance between the 4 areas makes a lot of things annoying and time consuming. It's genuinely fun to explore each singular shop and see what new weapons and clothes you can discover as this game provides a lot more in each shop you enter. Its very satisfying and its one of the consistently good things about this game. Exploration is rewarded with rarer and more fun weapons!

The psychopaths are fun gameplay wise but very boring and underwhelming on a character basis. They're all obviously based around the 7 sins but in such uninspired and potential limiting ways. Since they all have to be based around a sin, none of them have any personality or reason for going insane other than portraying that sin and are hard to empathize with which makes them the most forgettable of the series. I assume they thought the spectacle of their sin being displayed would make up for their lack of personality?

Oh I just want to eat food! Oh I just want to work out! Oh Im just a lazy ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥! Oh im a sex maniac!

The only two I found myself really interested in was the biker who got a special story in the DLC that made him worthy of being a survivor rather than just some dude you kill, and the monk who lost his family because I could understand how he went off the deep end. The guy who wants to be you is kinda cool because you have to do his first survivor mission to make him a psychopath mission later on but thats really it for him. He was never really a bad guy in the first place, just a stupid loser, so having a boss fight for him is really underwhelming although again, kinda cool because you can choose to let him be a survivor or let him die.

The DLC is literally just the same map of the base game but w/ collectibles and poorly written stories of characters who arent even relevant in the base game, I have no clue what they were trying to do here. I will give the DLC the credit of saying the stories are more fleshed out than the actual game and the characters are more interesting than most of survivors or main cast so why are all the DLC MC's DEAD?? They should have been used in the actual plot rather than just random stories about characters that mean nothing because they never got actual screen time to be developed.

DR3 was disappointing and I can see why there isn't much press around this game. There's just nothing really here to attach to. Sure the gameplay is fun but when everything else is just a bunch of nothing, there's not much to say in general about the game other than that.\t\t\t\t\t
