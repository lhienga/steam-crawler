\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first Dead Rising is a classic video game and was a pioneer for many future zombie games, and Dead Rising 2 is one of my favorite games. Both of these games blend cooky moments with a surreal zombie experience. Pinning you with strange weapon choices to fight off hordes of zombies to save survivors. In an excellent environment of a mall/casino mall, where you are surrounded by bright lights and colors and happy music with this grueling environment of zombies. These are balanced exceptionally well in these first two games; gameplay is simple and complex within the weapons of choice, while the main and side missions add complexity to the situation. Making these games perfect, Dead Rising 2 slightly improves on this by having a more compelling plot and better AI navigation.
Dead Rising 3 almost gets these perfections right but fails in many ways and is sadly the fall of Dead Rising. The best parts of this game are the combo weapons, it is so much fun finding random items and slapping them together, and making a weapon of mass destruction. The story is sadly stupid in a wrong way; before, it was quirky stupid, "Bee's started the Zombie Apocolypse" when you keep rolling with that, it gets ridiculous, and it's always trying to be the MAIN STORY. This game would have been perfect to tangent away from the main plot. Survivors aren't a challenge anymore, it involves a random fetch quest, and you don't travel with them to the safe houses. The setting is drab and LAME; it's an excellent idea to have an entire city to travel around. Yet aesthetically, it is beyond ugly, a total flip from the charm of the previous games.
I genuinely HATE a lot of this game's parts, but as a fun game, I would still say this is fun to play. Multiplayer carries this game to the finish line of recommendations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I get into the actual review, let me preface this with: DO NOT PLAY THE DLC UNTIL YOU ARE POSITIVE YOU ARE DONE WITH THE MAIN GAME!!*

Moving on, this is an extremely fun game to play. The world building and story are both amazing. However, minus the fourth game, this is probably the least "Dead Rising" game. While there is quite a bit of world building, and lots of side quests, there IS a lot that goes on throughout the game and part of the "Dead Rising" novelty wears off a bit and causes this to be more of a zombie killing game that has a stellar story with Dead Rising elements.

*Now, the reason why I urge you not to play the DLC until total completion of the main game is because there is about a 75% (or more...) chance that ALL of your progress will be wiped due to a bug. I was level 47 back in 2021, and I only just recently started playing the game again because I was so incredibly angry and burnt out from the fact that I lost SO much progress.

However, all things considered, great game overall, and great for killing time. I like the references to past games, as for me personally, it keeps a little bit of the flair of the old games. As someone who grew up playing every DR game, this is probably my favorite because of the story and all the characters.\t\t\t\t\t
