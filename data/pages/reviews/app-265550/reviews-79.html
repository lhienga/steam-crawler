I've only gotten one hard crash and don't see any physics issues. Generally I maintain 60fps no matter how many zombies and gibs are on the screen on an rtx 5600xt

Before 4 came out this was the black sheep of the Dead Rising franchise but I think it's unironically a really good followup. Combat feels a little more on point to me, and some of the bosses have intentional design and though they're tanky they can still be easily dispatched if you bring some proper firearms or combo weapons. The outfits are more ridiculous and that was always one of my favourite parts.

Combat is obviously still jank but it feels just good enough to not be frustrating. psychos are still a pain but considerably less with the responsive control and movement options. the roll is instantaneous and cancels any action, and has a tremendous amount of i frames so it makes up for the sometimes unfair feeling attack patterns of the enemies. There are also decent crowd control options with the majority of worthwhile weapons having a decent attack radius.

Some aspects of the game undermine the dna of the series, such as being able to go home and spawn anything you've combined or found out in the world, but it also makes the game go at a more reasonable pace and considering the whole thing is on a (considerably less harsh) timer I think that's a fair compromise. That being said, I think the timer also undermines the exploration aspects of the open world, so it almost feels mildly held back by it being a dead rising game. There are modified game modes so I'm sure there is a sandbox type mode where the open world can flourish more. I don't think any of these games are fundamentally excellent designs but this probably "breaks" the intention of the game the most out of the first three but oftentimes is more fun and less stressful due to that.

I don't know how the game was on consoles on release but in this condition, definite recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

That's practaclliy what I can see about how it feels to play the game. That sucked for sure because camera motions were slow, sprinting felt dumb and also the format of the game since it was stuck at console like graphics and resolution. The combat itself how ever, was A LOT of fun. Creating new weapons that were mostly differnt from each other was just amazing and all the combos with weapon you could make was even better. It was a lot of fun and I enjoyed almost every weapon I picked up that could be combined. I am upset that it deletes your saved game when you complete the story but that was forgiven when I saw you kept all your skills from that last save. I haven't 100% this game yet but having all the locations of blueprints and collectibles already shown makes that a lot better for completion. Nick as a character was fine. I enjoyed his heroic aspect of life despite obviously being scared most of the time. All other characters were also pretty ok too. They were enjoyable and even some of the side quests for survivors were quite enjoyable too. I give this game a 7/10 just because it's basically a port from consoles but still fun if you can get over that and the graphics.\t\t\t\t\t
