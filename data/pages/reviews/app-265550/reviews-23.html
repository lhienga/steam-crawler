\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis takes everything good about the first two games and totally dumps on it. No real time issues and you can save anywhere removes any tension from what you're doing, just load from your last checkpoint if you die. DR has always walked the line when it comes to offensive stereotypes, but has usually stayed on the right line for the most part. Not this game -- the humour is incredibly childish, I don't care about any of the survivors and the psychos are forgettable and mostly easy. The city is sprawling, you have no real chance to learn it in the only one playthrough you will probably do (unlike the first two games where the maps were tight and you redid them over and over and therefore learned them).

This is what happens when you listen to people who don't like a franchise, you end up with a game like this. Avoid.

-Edit- I've played an additional 12 hours since my original scathing review, and while I still can't recommend it, once you've played through it once and don't need to sit through the excruciating cut scenes and can basically ignore the annoying side missions, it's better. Now playing on Nightmare mode and this is more of a true Dead Rising experience, but only slightly more.\t\t\t\t\t
