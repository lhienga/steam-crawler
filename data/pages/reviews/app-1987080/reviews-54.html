\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had an overall negative experience of this game, starting from the later half of level 2 and continuing through to the end. This is not to say that the game is bad however, the first level was very well made, the objectives of it were made clear and it was, overall, a very good level. Below you will find my dislikes.

My gripes begin with the transition of the parking lot into the office, the puzzles started to become convoluted and (at least to me and my friend) seemingly not thought out, notes began to be unreliable as they read very strangely or provided little to no actual hint on what to do, the levels begin to become quite large (and at this point far more difficult), enemy spawns in these levels worked well, to a degree but could be tweaked slightly.

The partygoer section was truly terrible you are given vague hints on what to do "find your gift" , you would assume that this meant there would be one for each player, there is not. Another reason why i claim this top be terrible is the time limit, when wandering through the maze it was incredibly hard to know where to go, finally the partygoer did not use its own rules (this is prevalent in the part where you need to blow out candles). After this the game only gets worse.

The sewer level. My god. what an abhorrent level. It was far too large for its own good, it was incredibly difficult to know where I was going, the puzzles were terrible, there were no hints to tell us what we needed to do or where we needed to go, for example, the medallions. You need three medallions in order to get a cog, a friend and I spent at least an hour wandering round just trying to find the final medallion all while avoiding as many enemies as possible (which was an incredibly difficult task considering how many hand dudes were wandering about). When we actually find the way to get it we felt it was incredibly stupid as there was no way we could've seen the door in the wall unless we were actually looking for it.

The terror hotel was all in all, a decent level. it was still convoluted and we found that it was a very very poor experience, it would be better to put the door number on the door instead of above it. The steam generator puzzle was aggravating as it had no actual effect on the moths. the gem puzzle was pretty silly, considering that there is no apparent way to pick up the fossils, (the only reason that we were able to do it was because i accidentally clicked on the fossil) due to the glass covering the fossil it would make more sense that you would need to break the glass, but no, you just need to reach through the glass instead. It would make more sense to remove the glass from the puzzle all together as it adds nothing to it. The puzzles were vague and didn't make a lot of sense.

Overall:
The first level is the undeniably the best,
The puzzles start of well but get progressively worse
The increase of map size, puzzles and entity amount per level do not equate to a good experience
The player models and enemy models were well made.

I do not think that it was worthwhile to purchase, it did however provide my friend and I a good experience for an hour or two. I only hope that you find it to be far better than I did.\t\t\t\t\t

now the negatives, the second half of the game (mainly the rat sewer level and also the "terror" hotel) were poorly designed, the sewer level was FARRRRRR to large with endless amounts of slow and tedious back tracking, with horrible key item placements in ridiculous random spots guarded by enemies with too big a hit box with horrible pathing in too cramped a space. actually figuring out what to do can take hours, but then actually doing the task can take twice as long, over half the play time was just for figuring out where only a select amount of the key item in the sewer were. it was near impossible to figure out everything wit the copious amounts of enemies with horrible pathing and conflicting game mechanics (having to sneak for the rat and hands, then having to run from the ugo dog). the "terror" hotel was a good map size BUT there were far too many enemies that were more annoying then anything in a really cramped space where it was really easy to run and get stuck on anything and everything. the ending moth section was weird and buggy and the game didnt make sense on how the steam would actually effect the moths and they just continued to attack me.
