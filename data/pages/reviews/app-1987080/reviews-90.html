\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game based on its premise, but there were a lot of things that kept me from enjoying the game as much as I could.

- The way the mouse seems to lag and then suddenly 'snap' when moving around can be very jarring. I've tried adjusting settings, but it doesn't seem to really addressing the issue with that sudden 'snap.'
- The blur and lighting is really hard on the eyes. Even turning film-grain off and tweaking the settings available, the lighting really leaves a lot to be desired. Being unable to see the people you are playing with who are standing close just makes it frustrating to work with.
- I understand the desire for immersion to have the player just dropped into the game where they have to figure stuff out, but that immersion is ruined when you have to bumble around for 15 - 20 minutes trying to figure out what you need to do. A better tutorial would help this, or some better design.
- It feels like the large bulk of the time is spent hiding in lockers, even with things like the screw driver to deter certain monsters. Having to deal with multiple entities who skulk about aggressively in the same spots (sometimes multiples at a time in an area) just kills the game's vibe for me.

There were some smaller issues that my group had when we played this game, but these seemed to be the biggest problems we all had. I would love to recommend this game, but I don't think I can until things such as these get some attention. The group I play with has played a variety of similar games (Dead by Daylight, Phasmophobia, Ghost Hunter Corp., Devour, Forewarned, etc) which all have their quirks, but the ones in this one seem a little more heavy-handed.

I wish the developers the best of luck, though! I can see a lot of potential in this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSaving system doesn't make any sense in this game. We got to the pool with some creatures swimming in office level, which required solving many puzzles and dealing with the skin-stealer, and escaping the b-day party stuff. The whole thing took a few hours to figure everything out and get to that point. However, the game didn't save any progress and next time we wanted to continue, we were back to the square one with everything needing to be redone.

I don't understand what is the point of this strict saving or checkpoint system. I'm not sure it makes the game more challenging in a meaningful way and there's no point in doing all the puzzles and going through the same struggles. The level design of the game does not make it replayable anyway. Also, we play easy, at least at that level saving or checkpoints could be more generous.

All that happened above turned my initially positive experience into a negative one and don't plan on going back to the game and do all the same stuff all over again.\t\t\t\t\t
