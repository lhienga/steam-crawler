\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Inside the Backrooms is a co-op horror game where you solve escape room-style puzzles while being chased by entities from the The Backrooms mythos; while the concept itself is intriguing, the actual gameplay is tedious and frustrating.

Gameplay:
The main gameplay loop revolves around solving escape room-style puzzles to progress through each level, with the added challenge of hostile entities from The Backrooms mythos lurking around every corner. While the concept sounds intriguing on paper, the gameplay becomes increasingly tedious the longer you play. The main problem is that the entities are simply way too oppressive, without there being any effective means of fending them off. While the game implies that each entity is attracted only to a specific action such as lights or radio communication, the only truly consistent way of avoiding an entity is by hiding in lockers. This is a huge problem as entities can sometimes camp an area you need to go to progress, and you are left simply hiding for prolonged periods waiting for it to leave. As a result, the gameplay experience very quickly devolves from horror to boredom or frustration.

Level 1: Lobby
Moving on to the individual levels, the first level of the game is a reference to the iconic Backrooms Level 0, also known as "The Lobby". The entities in this level are Entity #3 Smiler, Entity #8 Hound, and the unknown entity from Kane Pixels' Backrooms video series. This level consists of multiple escape room-style puzzles, where each room have clues for a puzzle in a different room, which then unlocks a different puzzle in another room, until you finally unlock the exit. While these puzzles are initially interesting, the presence of three entities in a single level often results in extended periods of hiding and waiting. Very often, multiple entities appear at the same time, blocking both our path forwards and our retreat backwards. This results in us being unable to do anything but sit in place until the entities disappear. And while navigation in this level is supposed to be confusing by design with every turn and corner looking identical, the combination with the three entities have the effect of making the level feel unnecessarily prolonged and dragged out.

Level 2: Parking
The second level of the game, Parking, most closely resembles The Backrooms Level 1, also known as the "Habitable Zone". For some reason, this level was extremely short with the puzzles all being relatively straightforward, and the only entity on this level is Entity #10 Skin-Stealer. Nevertheless, this level does provide a refreshing change of pace, as rather than simply hiding from the entity, you get an opportunity to instead "fight back" against it by locking it out of the room.

Level 3: Office
The third level, Office, combines elements from various levels in The Backrooms mythos. You start off in what looks like The Backrooms Level 5, also known as "Abandoned Office". The gameplay of this level is very similar to the first level of the game, where you need to go around the level solving escape room-style puzzles. One upside of this level was that each room looked distinctly different from each other, making navigation somewhat more bearable. Unfortunately, the Entity #10 Skin-Stealers on this level is somehow an even more overwhelming presence than the three entities in the first level combined.

Afterwards, you enter a small room which contains a few objects taken from The Backrooms "Level Fun", albeit the surrounding environment is still that of an office. Here you will be challenged to a series of life and death minigames by the Partygoers. While the first minigame was rather pleasant, it very quickly devolves into just solving mazes in a given amount of time. Unfortunately, the game fails at the horror element in this section, as the short timer results in repeated deaths that becomes progressively less scary each time you respawn. While the mazes in and of itself are decently challenging without being completely disorienting, the repeated deaths very quickly sours the experience.

Next, we enter into a room containing just a huge swimming pool, with Entity #5 Clump lurking beneath the waters. If anything, I think this was actually the most scary part of the game since you were unable to physically see the entities, and the only way to avoid them was by detecting their location using a proximity sensor. You could actually do this part without dying, which ironically made it more tense, especially when you see your friends in front of you get dragged underwater. However, there is one major design issue with this section. When you reach the end of the pool, the only way to proceed further is via a red keycard. Yet, there is no indication of such a requirement prior to going through the pool. Should this happen, it means that you're forced to backtrack through the entity infested pool, with it feeling more frustrating than scary the second time around.

Finally, you once again enter into a section that resembles The Backrooms Level 0: "The Lobby". Except this time, it is even more of a maze than the first level of the game. In this section, you need to find the correct door while avoiding the fake ones. The problem however, is that we didn't realize that if you enter a wrong door, an Entity #8 Hound is permanently unleashed into the maze. As there is no way to reset a level without starting from scratch, this mechanic feels unnecessarily punishing, because it means the more mistakes you make the harder the level gets. This makes the experience very frustrating, as you are constantly reminded of and regretting your mistake, while knowing that you wouldn't have had to deal with the entities if you did it correctly the first time.

Level 4 and Level 5
For some reason, these 2 levels remain locked even after completing the previous level. It turns out that in order to unlock them, you must replay Level 3: Office and backtrack into Level 2: Parking while having the red keycard on you. This is an extremely unsatisfying design choice, as the game is basically punishing us for finishing the game by keeping levels locked away from us, and forcing us to replay levels we've already beaten. At this point, I have not completed these levels, and should I end up playing them at a later date, it would only be due to my friends dragging me to play with them.

Technical:
Aside from gameplay and design issue, the game also suffers from a few technical issues. Considering that this game seems primarily designed for co-op play, it is inexcusable that players are unable to rejoin a session after disconnecting. Not everyone has consistent and stable internet connections, so the possibility of disconnects is absolutely something that the game should have prepared for, especially given that a single level can take at least an hour to complete. Currently, should a player disconnect, the only way to get them back is to restart the entire level from scratch. This not only disrupts the flow of gameplay, but it is also a highly discouraging and frustrating experience.

Additionally, players with higher ping lags when attempting to use the in-game voice chat. While we were able to resolve this issue by communicating via Discord, it does detract from the immersion that the game was going for.

Conclusion:
In conclusion, Inside the Backrooms presents an interesting concept, but numerous frustrating mechanics hinder the overall experience. While the game's puzzles and atmosphere initially captivate, it fails to strike the right balance between challenge and enjoyment. This serves as a reminder that less can be more, and that the absence of certain elements often evokes greater horror than their actual presence.

Rating: 5.0 / 10.0\t\t\t\t\t
