\r\n\t\t\t\t\tan objectively wrong interpretation of the backrooms. This game is basically a cheap knock off horror game that is flawed in so many areas.

problems:

Lighting. Its way too dark everywhere from what a normal version of the backrooms would be. It has that mystical black fog effect, so when you've got a flash light shining it really only shows you about twenty feet before dissipating into darkness. I'm guessing this was done to make it more spooky, fear of the dark and what not. But the specific horror the backrooms entails is not the claustrophobia, its the idea of huge spaces that are vastly empty and familiar. In other words, if the lighting was better and allowed you to see much further, it would add to that visual and psychological spook element the backrooms is known for.

Stamina: bad. So there are three movements. There's either walk at a tip toe pace, or there's crouch walk at an even slower tip toe pace, and then there's sprint which is drained quite fast. Essentially, you're best bet to not get murdered by an entity is to walk at a snails pace, and then only sprint once its spotted you and you have to run away. Basically to play smart you have to play painfully slow and boring. Ultimately, you're going to sprint a little bit to get places, which means that at some point you're going to be running from a monster, run out of sprint, and then you are simply forced to walk at a snails pace while an entity is whacking you. Simply put it is just not the way to do it. It feels absolutely wrong and wonky to imagine tip toeing away from a monster that's wacking your ass. the non sprint speed should be a little faster, fast enough to be reasonable. If necessary to add difficulty back, lower the sprint speed slightly but increase the walking speed. This would balance everything out and play with so much better flow, not having to tip toe all the time at a snails pace.

Entities: Neutral. Not factoring in the whole running out of sprint and having to tip toe away from an entity, the entities actually feel quite respectable. They look scary enough and behave in a way that I would say seems appropriate for the quality of this game. My only complaint is ive seen entities walk straight through doors which seems cheap. Overall I'd say entities are a neutral element in the game, they aren't excellent and they aren't poorly done either. SPOILER ALERT- there is one segment in the pool rooms where you have to navigate past a BUNCH of these arm spaghetti human meat ball looking things, and there's like a dozen if not more of them in one pool. You have to go around them and if you go to close they eat you. I think theres simply too many of them for it to be considered fair. END SPOILER.

Gameplay: Bad. It's pretty much entirely puzzle oriented. And its way too many puzzles and each one is unnecessarily difficult. Expect to look up multiple guides per level. For example on the second level i believe, you have to solve like four puzzles to progress which feels absolutely off. (there's two lock boxes that require inputting a simple code... why two lock boxes? theres already two other puzzles so just have one lock box, its already more than enough. Put both items in the same box). And they aren't easy either. They involve finding over half a dozen parts that are randomly scattered in lockers, so loot EVERYTHING. If anything this game becomes more of an intellectual puzzle game than a horror or backrooms game. It is just so off. Every level I had to look up multiple guides maybe except the parking lot, since that one doesn't really have puzzles. I'm assuming most of the backrooms audience members are kids, and I honestly cant imagine anyone below the age of 14 being a serious contender for being able to beat this game. I think its like 3 or 5 bucks, well personally I would pay another 5 bucks to have not played it and gotten my time back. It's difficult where it shouldn't be difficult and its easy where it shouldn't be easy. Probably the least fun game ive played in years.

final complaint. I beat the game, got through what was the old ending and even got to a game completion screen and woke up. I knew there was a sewers level so i was confused. I went to the menu to see if it had been unlocked since i "beat" the game allegedly. Nope. Sewers is still locked for me, despite progressing through everything prior to it. Thanks. so basically i put in all the effort to progress through the first 3 or 4 levels, just so i can be denied accessing another 2 levels that i presumably paid for. Theres some youtube guides on the sewer levels but they dont mention it having any quirks or weird pre requisites to unlock. im either guessing something was changed in an update or maybe it requires another purchase as some kind of DLC, either way some kind of notification or identification as to whats going on would be helpful, preferably in the menu where it can say whats going on, whether it needs an extra dlc purchase or what. Cant in good faith recommend this game to a single person.\t\t\t\t\t
