\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing this game I can say I enjoyed it and found it to be an overall good experience,
that being said a few things that could be done to make the game more immersive I will list the things in my opinion that could be done.

1. More immersive visuals, some of the game feels like it came out of a GMOD, and I only say that because the NPCs are very generic and so is the overall atmosphere of the levels, such as walls, furniture, bodies, entities, this is something that could be improved and would by far and would make the game so much more enjoyable

2. The mechanics, these for sure need to be improved, the walking, running, crouching, sprinting, and also their should be more options such as jumping and climbing, you could keep the challenge adding these options, simply make these much more physically demanding mechanics use more stamina, also the stamina should be set with he health, if you have low health why would you have normal stamina?
also swimming mechanics will definitely be a must because in backrooms lore, there is multiple backrooms levels with water, and that in itself is an endless possibilities of level;s and so and so forth, Poolrooms anyone?

3. This game is missing so much, I mean there are of course limits to everything, but this game has unlimited potential, some things that should be added is a more open world experience, now i know what your thinking, How the heck is the backrooms an open world experience? well you can No-Clip anywhere if you hit the wrong wall, this could expand into even more levels, and different possibilities, such as trading with other survivors, NPCs, and i mean an open world game, gets more players and is overall more enjoyable.

3. as i mentioned NPCs, this just needs to be improved overall, like i said the copy paste thing, it can take away from immersion really fast and make the game dull for some people, a game should grab the players attention and make them feel like their actually being challenged and actually making hard decisions, but nothing breaks the immersion more than seeing the same face or same corpse or same NPC over and over and over again, and NPC,s should definitely be more lifelike, for example the entities, it seems like they just go back and forth and back and forth and have no real chasing or hunting mechanic.

but this is just friendly criticisms, and I really hope that this game take a good direction and really grows and thrives, I of course know its still being developed and will take time to create, and build, and grow, but this has endless possibilities, and I really hope to see them come to light.
