This game has a lot of sandbox elements to it that make each world feel unique, with tons of different furniture and blocks to build with. Even though its 2D, it has a very charming art style with lots of small great details. Also, the 2D art style means there are hundreds of different weapons and items you can try. Some of which are devastatingly powerful and really awesome to use. some are just pure fun. Another thing that makes this game so stylish is the music. It is a really well made soundtrack and its 8 bit style adds to the charm of the game. Also despite being 2D the worlds still have a great deal of depth to it, because the game play is split between background and foreground and while the foreground can be quite basic, the backgrounds are always really pretty and detailed. With fun animations happening periodically to keep it feeling fresh and new. There are tons of monsters to hunt, and items and gear to loot, specially when you switch your world over to hard mode.


You are able to chose between 4 classes, melee, ranged, mage, and summoner. This gives the game more of an RPG feel to it, and makes it perfect to play with friends. While the progression feels more like an RPG the game play itself is more like a platforming, adventure game. A lot of the power-ups you find increase movement speed, or how much you can jump and move around. The boss fights are challenging but never feel unbeatable. This is also a sandbox game where you have tons of options to build and npc's even move into the houses you build. The building, and npcs make each world feel unique and lived in. Honestly, you can just load up a world and do nothing but build and have just as much fun as you would if you played the game play normally.

Despite all the pros there definitely still are some cons to this game. This game is PACKED full of stuff and content and if you want to experience everything/ get all the achievements you're going to need to use the wiki. If you want to 100% this game, there is tons and tons of grinding, even more grinding if you decide to go for the achievements. If you want to beat this game it is a commitment and not everyone has time and energy for that.


Overall this game is worth checking out, specially if you have couple of friends to cover all the classes. Its one of the most stylized games you can get if you love 8bit nostalgia. Beating it is really difficult and you likely can't beat it without the wiki. But its really gratifying when you actually do, specially if you are able to with friends.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTerraria in many ways is similar to Minecraft, only it's considerably more fast-paced in it's combat department. Enemies attack you the instant you spawn, and at night time enemies attack you regardless if you're behind walls or not. Line of sight kinda doesn't exist in Terraria since it's 2D, but don't let it fool you.

With it's vast collection of mods through TModLoader, Terraria is easily the best 2D survival crafting game. From building structures to using magic and fighting bosses, your imagination is the limit with it.

It can be overwhealming for new players who aren't accustomed to survival games or RPG styles, but I'd definitely suggest giving it a try. Wait for a deal if you wish, but it's worth the full price.\t\t\t\t\t
