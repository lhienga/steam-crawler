\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this is my under-two-hours Steam refund review. YMMV, it's not all bad, and this one might be good for you, but there are too many QOL issues and pointless chores in the gameplay and too many alternatives on the market to realistically believe that I'll be coming back to this one if it stays in my library.

First, I don't like the running around in 3D. There is zero gameplay gained by having 3D and camera rotation. I don't want a controllable camera. I want *one* camera angle where everything is crystal clear and I can easily see everything. Running around in close-up third-person is needlessly disorienting, since mostly it boils down to a grid with nodes at various distances where stuff actually happens. Running back and forth just makes it take longer. None of the gameplay elements that make Elder Scrolls or ARPGs or whatever interesting are present here, just pointless camera fiddling and endless running for its own sake.

There is a torch system inspired by Darkest Dungeon, which I also don't really love. Presumably bad things happen if number go down, but how much does number go down and when does it get how bad? You'd have to read the wiki. Does it burn while I stand still, or only while moving? It doesn't say up-front, and I don't want to use the current run as a lab rat to find out, so I'm literally discouraged from stopping to think in a Slay The Spire-style game, which is a really really bad look. You also have to walk into a certain halo of visibility in order to see what a node has in it, but stop short of actually triggering it and then mouse over it, while maybe worrying about the torch running out while you stop to think (or maybe it doesn't, who knows, but it's stressful to think that it might be). Just a total chore to do it in third person, and all the worse under time pressure. Again, just give me a graph with nodes and distances, make it turn-based, let me know exactly how far I'll be able to see if I light something up.

In battle, enemy attack order was not easy to discern, which matters a great deal as "block" is a mechanic that nullifies an entire hit per stack, and it would be great to know if 1 block means you're taking the 6 or taking the 40. Maybe it's left to right, but the first act boss spawns minions literally left, right, front, back, and center. You also don't get updated tooltips when hovering a single-target damage card over a target, reflecting armor/vulnerability/whatever, you think you don't have enough and somehow you do, or you don't, and you either lose sleep over it or you don't. At this point, you either love the game enough to read the whole Wiki and join the Discord or you don't, and I didn't, so I didn't and I didn't.

There is a lot of content that I'll never unearth and a lot of classes and upgrades I'll never unlock, but I found the user experience taxing in a lot of meaningless and unfun ways, and it just feels like trying to play a reasonably good StS clone while walking through a molasses swamp. I almost regret downvoting, and Tainted Grail might work for you, but this is where I'm at, and I am giving a lukewarm thumbs down.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really really love this game! It had me hooked and I still am! I love the card mechanics and I find myself loving certain characters. The storyline is pretty interesting too. I just don't like having to start all over again when you die and keep trying to collect cards from the ground up. The story keeps you hooked enough to make you want to keep trying. At least when you die, you still keep all the NPCs you discovered for your village so you dont lose too much when you have to start over.

I recommend giving deck building games like this one a try. I love the graphics and art and aesthetics! Oh and the best part, you get to keep Woolsey (your pet wolf) no matter how much you keep dying lol! He's definitely your saving grace and you always want to have him on your team so when you draw the card that lets you call him, play it!

As for the candles, they are kinda important otherwise you may run into situations that would be detrimental to your character/progress. But its not always bad. Its okay if you want to trying running around without a candle lit just to see what new characters you run into. But after the second run-in you meet you may want to light a candle because it does get worse the more you go without a candle! I do go without candles just to save one or two for the boss fights because you'll get bad cards in your deck that would hinder you.\t\t\t\t\t
