- Graphic: similar to Neoverse in camera and level of detail, but much better in visualization.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeckbuilding roguelike with good variety, fun mechanics and a strong theme.

3 major archetypes (Archer/Soldier/Summoner) split into 9 classes, each with a unique play-style and a good set of cards.

Relatively well balanced, probably on the easier side where even on the higher difficulty you win the vast majority of your runs. Although I will say that the summoner archetype does seem much weaker than the other two, maybe I'm just bad at playing it.

Most of the time the once you get past the first few fights the game gives you so many passives you start to snowball out of control. Which is both fun when you can play the yackety sax and do really broken things but it does kill a little of the challenge.

The surrounding story and lore is also pretty neat. I wont say much about it because I skimmed through most of it as I prefer gameplay.

Overall I enjoyed my time playing it and would recommend is to anyone who likes deckbuilding games.\t\t\t\t\t

One can hit bit of a plateau from time to time, where it feels you never will be good enough, and progress is slow, but the fantastic nature of this game pushes you forward to try again.

At least here in the beginning, I do find that your deck does have element of luck to it, because it's very hard to purge away cards that are not too useful. But runs are also very reasonably timed, so it is not much of an issue. In fact, variety in your runs keeps the game interesting.

i have not really played a deck-builder before, but I really love this game. I do highly recommend it! I look forward to learning new layers of the story, and trying to figure out different classes, great effort from development team.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSlay the Spire but better in almost every way. It really does an excellent job of blending roguelike card deck builders and RPG elements together.

Highlights:

- Slay the Spire styled combat and deck building but with 9 characters that have very unique traits and playstyles behind them.

- Compelling story, quests, NPCs and secrets that serve as great motivation factors to keep playing, particularly for RPG fans like myself.

- Dark gritty artstyle with pretty good textures and models, bringing the feeling of immersion into the twisted Arthurian universe of Tainted Grail.

- Permanent progression system. Every run, you gain permanent currencies that can unlock permanent upgrades for every character. It isn't limited to simple stats like HP and Armor, but also extends to effectiveness of potions and consumables, resting, special cards, cards drawn and even more.

- Cosmetic options for every character can be unlocked which are visually appealing. Outfits are be changed no matter what class you choose.

- Unique enemies and enemy compositions that require you to play strategically or risk taking too much damage, losing too much of your Wyrdcandle or straight up dying.

Issues:
- Visual feedback for damage, especially for multiple hits, can be misleading. I get hit for a lot more damage than expected sometimes.

- One particular enemy (Living Armor?) can suddenly switch to attacking for huge damage despite the icon showing it wants to buff. I got one shot out of nowhere a few times because of this bug.

- Missing audio for some lines of dialogue.\t\t\t\t\t
