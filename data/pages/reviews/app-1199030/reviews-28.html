\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWonderful game with a dark and fascinating world. It has a great aesthetic and is beautiful to look at and listen to. The story is very interesting and seamlessly blends with the games mechanics really allowing for an immersive experience.

Gameplay is fairly similar to Slay the Spire and other card battle roguelites but it has enough of its own mechanics that it feels fresh, most notably having a massive roster of extremely different classes to play.

It took me about 60 hours to get through its base content and 100% the game, but even after that there are still several fun endless modes, leader-board chasing, and even still an ever rising ascension style difficulty to the base game.

The games only major con is it runs extremely hot even if you have a top of the line PC, for whatever reason the source of its issue seems to be with its V-Sync setting, if you simply turn V-Sync off the games runs fairly well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game...
It is one of my most mislead frustration I've recently bought.
As you can clearly see on the store, the game was released in 2021, so it's not beta, not early access or anything, it is a finished game you can assume right?
Well, it is not. It has one of the worst walking controllers i've ever seen. You get used to it, but it felt as bad as early 90s 3d games bad.
The voice-over is clearly made by their own team, its not a bad team, but they can't act so all you hear is people reading text, and that turns anyone down.
The animations are so bad, awful. all of them skip to idle animation when they are supposed to finish, its not a smooth transition, its not polished as it should be.
The fights and enemies are always overwhelmingly hard. "But the game is supposed to be hard", don't shove that crap unto people...being hard is one thing, unfair, unplayable is another thing. YOUR FIRST FIGHT on the open world can be randomly against 3 monster which, buff themselves, debuff you (and the debuff you have to spend energy on that round or you will be ♥♥♥♥♥♥ on the next), damage you and almost leaving no way for you to retaliate them. So every fight feels like and endurance chore instead of being something challenging that you want to beat.
In all, the game feels like it is still in early access and has tons of things to polish and be revisited.
One last thing:
The videos and dialogues are just some [image.jpg] images with almost no animations and tons of pixels, it is horrible. The first video played was very hard to see due to the massive low quality of it, it was like 144p of any 90's game's intro.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a shell of a game. There are many stock animations still in the game, some of which are fairly bad, but that's not what I mean. What I mean is that ... the game is extremely easy. There are far too few encounters per 'level', and this seems to be because they don't want you to go back to town during a 'level'. That is no doubt because the game is already very easy.

The summoning character is *so easy* that one could waltz straight through all of the content without trying - on your first play through with that character. This, despite one of his 4 summons being virtually useless.

Thematically the game is very interesting. I would love for there to be about 10 times more content in the game, bringing it in line with an average amount of content, that was in-line with the content that we do have. Visually it's uninteresting, mostly because they don't want you to see anything. Lighting a candle lets you see, but is more tedious than anything and I've seen no reason, in the actual gameplay, to do so.

This game needs a massive overhaul and a huge increase in content to be worth full price. As it is, I paid half price and there's no way there was that much content in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
