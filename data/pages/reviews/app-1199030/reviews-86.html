\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing! As a long time rpg fan, I only recently discovered I like the "roguelike deckbuilder" formula a lot, and it was the excellent Slay the Spire that got me the message.
Tainted Grail is by no means inferior, on the contrary I find it far more charming, deeper and richer.
I never played the early access (I prefer playing games only post release) so I started directly from 1.0. The game has a diablo-esque atmosphere peppered with Witcher-y and Lovecraftian elements, with some Gothic roughness thrown in.
The game boasts a ton of classes (9!) divided into 3 archetypes (warriors, rangers and summoning-focused mages), with each sub-class covering a specific variety of playstyle.
I'm not going to spoil much about the lore, the classes etc. because part of the charm of the game is the joy of discoverying bits and pieces by yourself in a cyclic journey of challenging unearthly horrors (my only advice: if you unlock a class but it seems clunky and hard after trying it out, go watch 1 or 2 video guides about it, you'll be surprised...).
The game flow is divided among these things:
- the chosen class gives you a certain preferred playstyle and potential to grow;
- the (upgradable) hub is used to rest and recuperate and to strategize about how to spend your limited resources according to your situation;
- the nonlinear dungeons alternate between combat encounters and events/exploration, where you'll spill blood and make obscure decisions about all the many NPC you'll encounter;
- the combat relies on a bit of luck (which cards are selected from your deck and what card upgrades you'll be rewarded with) and a lot of strategy, there are MANY ways to tackle situations by combining enemy patterns (all attacks are telegraphed to allow strategy), cards of various types, passives, consumable items, permanent upgrades, helpers, runes, crafting, barriers, armor, summons, etc.
- there is no "stress" in building your deck because A) you can continously get new cards and everything can work all right (but you can also limit your deck and discard cards if you want) and B) you will accumulate a ton of passive abilities that will shape the way you play your cards, and as far as I know there is no upper limit, as many as you can get.
Really, if you like a dark atmosphere and are remotely intersted in deck building rpgs (there are so few of them yet!), THIS gem you just cannot miss!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, I usually don't like "slow" rogue-likes that takes too long to complete a run. This one certainly does but the biggest reason for that is that it has a lot of storytelling to do. The world of this game is quite nice, some kind of mad afterlife where all kind of monstrosities exist and I enjoy listening to all the voice-acting that brings this world to life.

On the gameplay side, this is a very good deckbuilder. There are 3 main factions with different set of cards. And each of these have 3 playable characters for a total of 9. All 3 characters of a faction have a different ability and passive and a different set of bonus perks to pick up when leveling up. And to be honest, it's kind of impressive how unique the 3 characters of a faction are while still using the same set of cards. Every character has to be built in a unique way and has a lot of potential to grow in power through new perks and cards.

My biggest pet peeve about this game is the camera in combat. Instead of being fixed in a position that allows you too see everything, you can move it around freely, and it never fits right. Zoom in and units UI will cover eachother if there are too many. Zoom out and your summons will be under your hand of cards making them hard to target.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot for me, Love Slay the Spire and Monster train but this game simply doesnt care about balance. No consistent way to get health back and everything is just very RNG heavy and random, also the Rogue "lite" systems are ridiculously grindy and uninteresting.

Best thing about the game is the music and voice acting which is pretty top notch + lots of NPC and random encounters I thought would keep things interesting but its kind of why I dont like the game; IMO the best Roguelites and Rougelites punish you for making bad decisions and then next run you think OK so I wont make that mistake again.
In Tainted Grail you really dont learn anything because everything is just so random you never know wtf you will get for interacting with NPC's (or at least I didnt understand after 11 hours of play) Some may love the randomness of that but I am not a fan, it detracts from actually having decision making elements that make Rouge games so fun for me

The difficulty spike from normal enemies to bosses are absurd and doesn't feel fun to literally wipe the entire map with ease just to then get killed by OP boss and all his minions in 3 turns.

Having single use cards that do 5000 damage that you save for bosses isnt very interesting either.

Can see why alot of people like it as good amount of content here with like 12 classes to play with and loads of variation, but its really just not for me.

TL DNR: Decent RPG Deckbuilder hybrid game , Bad Roguelite game\t\t\t\t\t
