\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail: Conquest is one of the top rouge-lite deck builders I've ever tried, and I've tried a few.

If you're new to the deck building it's all about starting with a decks of basic cards, and building it up with new cards you find along the way through leveling, events, or shopping. Each run new run, you start over mostly from square one, but there are some persistent unlocks and upgrades that give you slight edges and also provide a sense of progression, that each go adds to something more. In the beginning you really don't have much to work with an failure is to be expected, but over time with experience, luck, and those unlocks and upgrades, you will eventually be able to put together decks that have a chance to go all the way.

If you're a veteran fan of these kinds of games (Slay the Spire, Night of the Full Moon, etc), then I'm glad to say this one definitely stands out as of the genera's best in terms of design and polish. Balancing could still use a bit more rounding out, as some classes are feel very much easier than others. But as of writing this they're still actively working on that, and also given how much is on offer in terms of total classes, perks, items and what not that I find any small rough spots easy to look past. Only thing that kind of bums me out is that all the legendary neutral cards either limited, either per battle or total use. Which for balancing makes sense as they're OP game changing powerful, but as someone looking forward to a rare card to add on the regular, would mind some less powerful but reuseable legendary options.

Also Tainted Grail: Conquest does have some adventure to it, as in voiced NPCs, story quests, and even a small settlement that gets built up over the course of your efforts. I haven't personally seen that in this time of game yet and it's quite exciting. Defer back to my disclaimer.
This game is, in my experience, one that is punishing only if you fail to turn yourself into a raid boss through deck building, passive selection, and intelligent slotting of runes.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed playing this. The devs get a lot right:
* There are 3 schools of character-type, with 3 classes in each. Despite having 9 classes, they're each distinct and fun to play. Some are more intuitive than others, but I've seen people beat the game on very high difficulties with each, so they're all good. Balance seems pretty good among them from my experience.
* I haven't seen very many useless passives or cards. Certainly some are more generally useful than others, but most everything fits into some build somewhere.
* The story and setting is good - and the true ending is pretty neat.

There are a few rough edges, though:
* Some of the classes were unintuitive to me - but asking for help in the Discord was really useful
* Some of the quests are pretty vague with what to do next
* Most quests rely on RNG - you need to run into some event or character out in the world. You'll eventually find everything you need, but the progression can feel slow if you're trying to finish one specific quest

I got this on sale and would have been happy to pay full price. A really fun game if you enjoy this genre.\t\t\t\t\t
