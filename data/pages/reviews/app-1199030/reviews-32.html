\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a decent deck builder and rather immersive, but the pace is off.

Both the strategic layer where you can explore and slowly discover the lore of the world, and the actual battles, work very well. Tactically the game is sound with a genuine choice of feasible options, with the 3 tribes feeling very different to play (and each of the 3 heroes they have adding a little more variety still). The range of enemies is decent. The UI is clear and informative, except the odd grammatical slip ("fewer" vs "less" and similar). The aesthetics are compelling and the voice acting is, against all expectations for a small studio team, genuinely excellent.

The only real negative about the game is its pacing. Battles can drag on for too long, taking a dozen turns or more for some builds. There are too many battles per run, it's hardly a rogue-light if I have to invest a couple of hours or so per run as it puts me off experimenting. But most of all, the cost to upgrade things between runs is ridiculously high. In 16h of play I've found one rune stone fragment, while a single upgrade of the blacksmith requires ~20. Fully upgrading the blacksmith would probably add up to 1000s of fragments so 10,000+ hours of playing. I'm not a huge fan of rogue-lights compared to rogue-likes, but if you're going to do it, don't require insane amounts of grind to upgrade things.

Same thing for the story lines. Progression moves in tiny steps and requires who runs to make minuscule incremental changes to the character arc. I'd like to see how it develops (and if it ever becomes Arthurian as claimed), but not enough to keep fighting the same battles over and over again. But now at ~30h, I'm too bored by the gameplay for the story to motivate me to carry on. It's a shame that the pacing is off, but rather than having an excellent ~25h of story, they decided to stretch it out far too thin.

While this game is much darker in its aesthetics, I would rate it comparably to Roguebook in terms of recent releases in the genre. It's a worth a play if you're into narrative deck builders, but I'd recommend playing Inscryption and Griftlands first.\t\t\t\t\t
