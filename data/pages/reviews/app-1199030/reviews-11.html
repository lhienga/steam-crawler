\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gave it a lot of time but couldn’t get into it. I know I am in the minority and it's a bummer because I have played over a dozen of these types of games and loved each one.

The story, writing, and artwork on the cards and characters are fantastic. The rest of the graphics and presentation are rough (it looks like a PS2 game). There is also a weird rotatable battle map that is quite cumbersome and obscures my characters with my cards unless I maneuver it correctly. This means that sometimes my cards get in the way of the same characters I am trying to play them on. They should have ditched the whole 3D map altogether; it was a weird design choice. I also didn’t feel like information was always clearly conveyed during combat. The damage amounts also have a range, which I thought was a bit odd. Why add RNG at all in a game like this?

The classes felt unique enough, but there were certainly some that were just stronger than others. The nine classes share quite a bit of the card pool though, so it feels like there are only three real decks, and the variety comes in the form of the class passives. Sometimes the passives feel impactful, but often they do not. There was never a class that I felt was an absolute favorite or a blast to play.

Maybe I need to come back to this later and give it another chance but it feels quite rough around the edges and I just could not get into it.

For me, Tainted Grail is a really good deckbuilder game that I can highly recommend. The variety of different playstyles doesn't just come from different cards. There are 9 different characters with different basic abilities, plus a large variety of passive abilities that are completely different for each "family" of 3 character classes, and last but not least runestones that can sometimes change the game again very much. This variety and the really completely different gameplay of the individual classes appeals to me very much. Here also briefly to some comments on the Internet that there are classes that would be bad and hardly playable: I had no problems so far and do not feel this way. There are classes where you have to think and plan a lot more, but I find them all playable. However, you have to learn a new class each time and can't get away with one or two play styles. But that's what I find particularly appealing - having to stretch my mind and creativity.
What I didn't like so much are the lack of explanations of some of the rules and how cards work. It seemed to me that this was intentionally created by the creators, because even in the role-playing world you wander through, you encounter many encounters and decisions where it's not clear at first what's going to happen.
This is one role-playing element that I don't really like, but I realize that this is absolutely a matter of taste. Once you get past the beginning (or consult a wiki page about this game), this element no longer comes into play.
I also like the increasing difficulty of the game, which you have to set and not accept. Without this, the game would otherwise be too bland. The other challenge modes are also interesting and are good for many more hours of play.
My conclusion: If you like challenging deckbuilding and creativity, I can only absolutely recommend this game.

Für mich ist Tainted Grail ein wirklich gutes Deckbuilder-Game, das ich sehr weiterempfehlen kann. Dabei kommt die Vielfalt der verschiedenen Spielweisen nicht nur durch unterschiedliche Karten zustande. Es gibt 9 verschieden Charakter mit unterschiedlichen Grundfähigkeiten, dazu noch eine große Vielfalt an passiven Fähigkeiten, die für jede "Familie" von 3 Charakterklassen auch noch komplett unterschiedlich sind, und zu guter Letzt noch Runensteine, die teilweise das Spiel erneut sehr verändern können. Diese Vielfalt und das wirklich völlig andere Spielverhalten der einzelnen Klassen spricht mich sehr an. Hier auch kurz zu einigen Kommentaren im Internet, dass es Klassen gäbe, die schlecht und kaum spielbar wären: ich hatte bisher keine Probleme und empfinde dies nicht so. Es gibt Klassen, bei denen man sehr viel mehr nachdenken und planen muss, aber ich empfinde sie alle als gut spielbar. Man muss sich allerdings jedes Mal neu in eine Klasse einarbeiten und kann nicht mit einem oder zwei Spielstilen durchkommen. Gerade dies empfinde ich aber als besonderen Reiz - meinen Verstand und Kreativität anstrengen zu müssen.
Was mir nicht so sehr gefiel sind die mangelnden Erklärungen einiger Regeln und Funktionsweisen von Karten. Es erschien mir so, als wäre dies absichtlich von den Erschaffern so kreiert worden, denn auch in der Rollenspielwelt, durch die man wandert trifft man auf viele Begegnungen und Entscheidungen bei denen erst einmal nicht klar ist, was passiert. Man muss durch Probieren raus finden und Erfahrungen sammeln. Teilweise stirbt man auch durch solche Ereignisse oder das Deck bzw. das weiterspielen wird so sehr gestört, dass man neu anfangen muss. Dies ist ein Rollenspielelement, welches mir nicht so gefällt, aber mir ist klar, dass dies absolut Geschmackssache ist. Wenn man den Anfang überstanden hat (oder eine Wiki-Seite über dieses Spiel zu Rate zieht) kommt dieses Element nicht mehr zum Tragen.
Sehr gut finde ich noch den sich immer weiter erhöhenden Schwierigkeitsgrad des Spiels, den man einstellen und nicht hinnehmen muss. Ohne diesen wäre ansonsten das Spiel doch zu fade. Die weiteren Challenge-Modi sind ebenfalls interessant und sind für viele weitere Spielstunden gut.
Mein Fazit: Wer anspruchsvolles Deckbuilding und Kreativität mag für den kann ich dieses Spiel nur absolut empfehlen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot usually into these types of games but this one is pretty cool. But even after defeating the first Guardian, getting upgrades and exciting items still feels like a slog and I haven't seen any different weapons to choose from other than the default one your class gets. That would've been nice to see. Still a simple card battler that's easy to understand and satisfying when you pull off the right combos.

Editing this after killing all 4 Guardians:
This game is awesome but right after killing the last Guardian, the run just ends. It was extremely unsatisfying as I had become very powerful and wanted to further test my skills on what ever the last 3 bosses the goat mentioned were. But no, everything I had gotten up to the point was gone because I won the game. It would have been very nice after getting to that point, you'd be able to fight the last 3 bosses and if you lost to one, you'd just respawn in town again instead of having to work your way up to late-game again. It would have felt much better than just ending abruptly, kind of a turnoff for me.\t\t\t\t\t
