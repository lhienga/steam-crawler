\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR for the developers or people who don't want to read to read the whole thing. The game is a must buy for fans of the genre, and maybe not the best one for completely new people as this is one of the more complex deck builders in terms of cards in the deck you will come across. Blood mage and the summoner archtype in general needs work, its way too much effort for too little payoff, and blood mage could desperately benefit from a card that heals them but can only be used once per battle, overcharging golems costs too much for 10 hp. Wayfinder is maybe too strong? You can easily use like 20 cards in a turn with that deck and do like 1-2k damage in a turn late game. Voice acting was great, honestly superb work there.

I should by saying in the least arrogant way possible, i'm a veteran with deck building, rogue likes and turn based RPGs, so a game that combines all these is essentially something that was made for me as a consumer.

Tainted Grail mixes all these elements in good ways although it does very little unique to the genre, which isn't essentially a bad thing, it does all the main beats you would expect from the genres its mixing and refines them through researching its competition.

There are some balance issues and negatives with some of its mechanics but all in all Tainted grail is a game well worth your time.

I'll talk about the negatives before the positives, partly because there aren't many of them, and partly because that's what people are probably looking for the most here.

NEGATIVES

The biggest negative i have is the class system. when you play your first run you have one class available to you and you see 8 others locked out, you think to yourself, wow, the amount of variation is incredible. Sadly after unlocking all of them bar one at the time of this review, its 3 classes that each have 3 subclasses.

Theres summoner, warrior and archer. I have little problems with Warrior and archer, summoner however seems incredibly weak or at least to me. The amount of prep and planning the class requires for less pay off than the other two classes is pretty bad, and blood mage is almost unplayable. Half of the blood mages cards require a cost of 2% of your hp. You probably think to yourself, 'eh grandia that's no big deal' and at first glance you'd be right, but this game has very limited ways to regain health. It has one summon that when it uses its ultimate heals everyone for 10hp there are limited potions in a run due to shop limitations, and there's some runes that can give health back on combat win, but sadly runes are RNG to get the ones you want to you can't guarantee getting that rune. Blood mage took more cards and rounds to kill packs of mobs than any other class and used health as a resource to do so.

Wyrdcandle mechanic is also pretty redundant to be honest. It's their unique mechanic for the genre, essentially a fog of war exists and lighting candles increase the radius you can see in the fog of war. Things outside of your sight radius are unable to be identified, so npcs with mobs and what enemies are in the groups of mobs become unknown to you unless you use a wrydcandle. As you probably guessed this is another unique resource in the game, if you like to full clear zones like i do you'll find yourself with no candles by the second area, which isn't a super big deal but the game doesn't look all that pretty when wrydcandles are not lit.

POSITIVES

The game looks great for an indie game with very little budget, the main reason i picked this game up was because its a roguelike that isn't cartoony or has pixel art. It's art does look a little generic western fantasy but its supposed to its loosely set in the world of camelot with king arthur and his knights mentioned in conversations many times throughout the game.

It has an interesting world and story, i'm constantly wanting to know whats going on and the twists and turns with the plot are very interesting to say the least. I only have 8 hours on the game but because i'm not new to this genre i was able to win a few runs and experience some of the later story beats. A newer person to the genre might take 2-3x longer to get to where i am i would guess.

The world hub for this game is good, like truly. Many games have them, but not many do it well, enter the gungeon and tainted grail are probably the best i've seen of the genre so far. There's always something new to be unlocked or expand in both those games.

Difficulty is gradual in this, if you beat a run, the game gets more difficult, it continues to do this with each successful run, the loot gets better to match the difficulty increase.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredibly fun roguelike deck builder! It plays similarly to Slay the Spire, but with an enjoyable dark fantasy setting, interesting characters, and great 3D graphics. I only have about 20 hours in it and haven't played on the high difficulties, but it feels quite a bit easier than Slay the Spire. That said, there's still some challenge, particularly as the difficulty increases (kind of like Ascensions in Slay the Spire).

One of the biggest draws of this game is that it has 9 classes that are all fairly unique (although they are bucketed into 3 "factions," which gives them the same base cards as other classes in groups of 3). I still have 2 classes that I haven't tried, but all of the ones I have played have distinct play styles that make each run really fun and interesting to build around.

If slay the spire gameplay with a souls-esque dark fantasy setting sounds like a solid combination to you, I definitely recommend picking this up!\t\t\t\t\t
