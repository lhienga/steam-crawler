\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this one nearly 55 hours now and I played Monster Train for 300 hours (With more than one time first place on daily challenges) and Slay the Spire for 225 so I think I can give my two cents about this game. I will give this one the thumps up, it has a great price to time value even though I really do agree with others who pointed out that this game should’ve been cooked in early access for a little longer, there are some things to be done. (I won't mention every good and bad decision for gameplay though, I just wanted to make an overall review) All in all, I got my money's worth, buy it, I really think the developers will polish their game, as they majorly updated it just recently and there is hopefully more to come. Very nice people in their discord too.

First: There is a review with awards and 80 thumps up which says this game is too easy, I really can’t agree with that, I would assume after his 14 hours he did not even try difficulty 4. After 55 hours I am on difficulty 8 right now, so if you want a challenge, you will find a challenge. Of course, some of you will get there sooner. The only problem I see is in the run being way more difficult in the beginning than it is at the end. They have to tune this for a constant challenge. He further said the things you unlock for the classes (passives and cards) are not meaning- or useful, I strongly disagree with this too, many of them are worth it and they will let you progress further, and are indeed very meaningful, especially the upgrades you get from your village and the new passives you unlock for your classes. Please don’t let him scare you off from buying this game, seeking a challenge, especially for that price. This 80 or more people which got scared away really doesn’t help the developers making their game better. I also found some crap on google, which said this is the worst game ever, sure there is freedom of opinion but stuff like this is just damaging to the game. Please always remember the low-price tag.
There are many positive Aspects to the game. The most valuable point is the voice acting in it, I truly love it, I still got new voice lines after 50 hours. I loved the stories of the characters you save and get to add to your village. They are fun to talk and listen to. I loved the artworks. I also love the music in the game, even if two songs for elite and boss are not enough, and it is a little repetitive, I don’t know if you can listen to it hundreds of hours like in Monster Train, but it’s good. I really love the graphics and the enemies. There are certain things that are really neat, like when you unlock the bard for the village you can choose the music playing in it. You can find secret stuff and you have many quests to do, which are really engaging and finely crafted. Every new encounter was interesting, and I gladly listened to them and the female narrator. Progress in classes, as I mentioned before, really feels like getting something for your effort and the classes will get more diverse the longer you play. The card artworks are also wonderful. I still enjoy the fights and the gameplay loop.

Please be aware that you need up to 15 hours in this game to really feel at home. Which isn't to say it's bad in the beginning just that it picks up once you get accustomed to it. Also, if you compare it to Monster Train the card game itself is a little lacking, still very enjoyable though. But don’t forget you get an rpg too, and you have nothing like this in Monster Train.

You can customize your character and you can unlock dresses for him/her and can change them on the fly in the village. Some are neat, some a little less so.

I love the archer and melee classes, every one of them feels at least a little different and I like the overall mechanics and playstyles.

There are of course some negative points. Initial one being the performance, which was said to be fixed on 17. August but it never was. Sometimes it has only 50 fps or less which just isn't enough because the graphics while being nice are nowhere near AAA. Only thing you can do to get to 70 fps (which still is not much) is changing resolution to something crap, graphic details don’t change it. It is a known issue, and apparently, they don’t really know what to do about it. My system is still decent with a 1070 GTI and a 1600x Ryzen and I think I should get more fps.

Second thing is the monsters have “some" good animations, but you really don’t have many, there are three things you do, u clobber enemies with your weapon, you shoot enemies with your bow, your summons clobber enemies. Sometimes the animations of the attacks differ for example when you shoot multiple arrows, but that is not enough. It’s sad because it’s not corresponding with your cards at all which it could’ve been with the game being 3D and all.

Last point I will mention: Summoner classes are strong, but don’t feel the least bit good. I really hate Summoner playstyle and would’ve wished for mages, maybe fireballs and stuff. Something like you explode in your face for more damage like in Warhammer. Luckily the bring a class to level 20 achievements are quickly done on lower difficulties. Maybe necromancer is cool because of your ultimate, but still didn’t try it because of the summoner mechanic. Cards really are bland: summon, promote, activate, get some barrier. lame…
