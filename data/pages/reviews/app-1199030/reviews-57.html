\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the short time I've played this, I recommend it without a shred of doubt.

I was so addicted playing tainted grail when I first started it, I streamed it for 7+ hours thinknig I was just going to demo it for a couple hours. It is a combination of a hearthstone arena without trash rng & pay2win decks, coupled with classic RPG's battle styles (such as Final fantasy) with a strong Diablo/PoE flavor. It can be argued that you are primarily running the same map that shifts it form, similar to diablo, but I'de compare it more like playing an arena game. Where the same map is there, but every game has a different outcome. Plus, I really like how they slowly introduce new characters as you earn your general experience (beyond character level exp.) Grinding for experience to try new heroes keeps the game interesting, allows you to try all the heroes, and is not at all a grind if you're loving it.

I also really like how the game has two options after you die... "Start over," or "take a break." It is like they know you don't really want to quit the game, so they offer a "break" as the second option. xD xD

Truthfully, this game is different enough to be its own beast, and will really stimulate any strategy card game and/or rpg fans out there without a doubt. Plus, they play "Danheim" music as their primary score style. This is just the cherry on top with the whole feel of this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAm I enjoying this game? Yes. Would I recommend it to you? No way.

The combat and the deck building parts are fun. The voice acting is also good. Everything else is whatever. Out of the 9 classes available only 4 are fun. The rest are either boring as heck, underpowered, or both. Running around in 3rd person between encounters is a time consuming chore. Village upgrades are unexciting.

But the worst thing is the "final boss". The "real" one. You have to survive X turns, the amount of damage you do is relevant only for leaderboards, but not for the battle itself. And the "reward" is just a pat on the back. Seriously?!

So, yes, I'll probably do enough reruns to finish the story-line. But if you're looking to experience The Flow, entering the state of seamless oneness with the game, as you had before in Slay the Spire or Monster Train, you need to look elsewhere. Tainted Grail does not have it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn one hand, very nice looking game with fun classes and interesting combos! Very much a solid copy+paste rogue-lite card builder following so many before it!

On the other hand, the developers decided that because this is a "rogue-lite" game, you must grinding for endless hours replaying the game over and over and over in order to unlock more fun...

Ultimately, if they reduced the grind in many areas of the game I think it would be worth recommending. As it stands, I estimate I'd need to invest 30+ hours of grinding out the same few levels over and over and over in order to unlock any meaningful progression on the account.

For example, you unlock most of the buildings or "features" pretty quickly. And each of them effectively require a different currency to upgrade what they offer. The issue comes where the buildings you care about most and offer the more enjoyable changes to your runs, will maybe result in ~3 currency drops in a run... and you need 15-30-50+ for one upgrade. If a run takes you say ~15-30 minutes? You can do the math but likely see that you have to grind A LOT to unlock stuff.

Compare this to other rogue-lite games I've played where if you beat bosses, you straight up get new items to use (this game isn't like that from my experience). Those other games do have currency, but it's ONE currency that you choose to use wherever you want. This game has MULTIPLE currency types, one for each building, and you don't get equal drops of that currency every run. Some of the currency you have to actively and specifically grind for (often at the expense of others).\t\t\t\t\t
