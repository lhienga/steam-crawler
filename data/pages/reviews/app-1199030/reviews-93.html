\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIve got a lot of game time on Steam, but so few reviews. I tend to save them for real gems... Darkest Dungeon, Valheim...etc.

This, right here, is one of those gems.

This is something truly unique that I can't say I've ever played before. Some...strange mix of roguelite, deck builder, RPG, action, turn-based, and mobile all rolled up into one decadently sweet little treat. Truly infinite replayability...like Hades in the sense its constantly evolving, run over run. You're meeting new people, old people, minor quests (and major) all slowly progress, revealing bits of the story, new things to tinker with, new avenues to go down.

Each of the classes, though slightly similar in card choices, all vary wildly in playstyle, but are by no means difficult to pick up. Again, adding more to the uniqueness of each run (though I am definitely not a fan of the Archer classes, the mechanic is just cumbersome and plodding).

One particular callout, the voice acting, for a non-triple-A game, is actually truly fantastic. The woman who narrates, you can hear the care and precision she puts into her cadence and how she nimbly traverse the paragraph finding the right moment to accentuate a word or phrase.

All in all I am thoroughly enjoying this and would recommend it to anyone whos a fan of deck builders with a twist, Arthurian lore, and (to an extent) Diablo-style RPG elements (Baldurs Gate it is not, but it is just enough to make you feel like you're playing a role in the overarching story).

Now stop reading it and go burn that Steam gift card grandma gave you. Worth!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail follows a simple concept, yet is pretty fun. It takes a lot of elements from similar Deckbuilding Roguelikes such as Slay the Spire and puts its own Grim Fantasy twist on it to make it feel fresh.
Once you start, you'll have next to nothing to work with, as is the style of most Roguelikes, but on your journey you'll meet a lot of interesting characters (which have some really great voice acting) that'll be able to improve your skills, and have small, individual side stories about their past that you can explore to learn a bit more about them.
On your journey you'll also uncover the story about the island you're on, which is engulfed with a strange fog that makes people act in certain ways based on their regrets and guilt (from what I understand), and you'll encounter some not-so-human characters along the way. What's nice about the story is that it has a good aura of mystery to it and you'll be asking a lot of questions, most of which will be answered in creative ways as you keep playing.
If you're familiar with deck building roguelikes, the game is going to feel very easy for your first few runs, but every completed run unlocks a harder difficulty to play which will slowly begin to offer more of a challenge.
All in all though, this game is fun if you are into deck builders and it has a compelling story. It could still use some polishing here and there when it comes to balancing, but nevertheless it is worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA piecemeal story with decent voice acting and some Dark Souls inspired World Building, provides a backdrop for the roguelike gameplay loop that is fought with cards instead of a more traditional ARPG third person combat.

Throughout each session you'll come across random encounters that slowly chips away at the story of the world and its inhabitants, a session that sees you fighting your way past three areas and three bosses before fighting the Arthurian and repeating it all over again at a higher difficulty.

The game offers three archtype classes to which you can approach the gameplay loop, the Fighter, the Mage and the Archer, of which there are three variants in each, for a total of nine possible classes to approach the game with, all though a lot of the cards are shared within a given archtype so what ultimately separates the variants is their passive ability.

As you progress through the game you'll discover more cards for each Archtype, but there isn't much deckbuilding as you're provided a choice of 1 card out of 3 random at every level up. Further the deck can be supplemented with Quest and Story unlocked cards and on occasion some cards can be removed to reduce the random factor of the deck.

There's also the usual currencies to earn, that amounts to miniscule upgrades for the most part and a steep grind wall for most of them, making them of little importance before the game runs out of steam.

The price they are asking is more than fair for the amount of hours you can sink in to the game, offering on average around 20-30 hours before the gameplay gets too repetitive and stale.\t\t\t\t\t
