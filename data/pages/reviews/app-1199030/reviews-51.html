\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail is honestly something really special.

On the surface you've seen similar card battlers/deck builders, but what really sets it apart from others is the sheer amount of cards you can use, as well as an evolving story. Oh, did I forget to mention the soundtrack? Because it's absolutely incredible. Driving, dark beats that seem to crescendo at just the right moment and set the tone for the whole game.

You're not locked to any pathways and you're free to explore and upgrade your hub/town as you please. It feels like a mixture of Slay The Spire, and Diablo 2, but if the devs listened to a whole lot of Heilung while making the game.

If you enjoy rogue-light games that are also incredibly dark and brutal, this is an easy one to recommend. Full price or on sale, it's worth.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally worth checking out. The game is very easy to get into and gets pretty addictive. Music and visuals are amazing and there's also really good voiceover for the encounters. The worldbuilding and stories are engaging and worth experiencing.

Difficulty is fairly low. There's a bit of a bump when transferring to the second area, but if you've got your desk in order, it's smooth sailing from there. The deckbuilding is pretty entertaining, but don't expect a sophisticated balance. Some combos are just brutally overpowered and that's maybe part of the fun. You have a dozen different classes; some are fairly similar, some I managed to figure out and some are a bit over my head. The meta progression is kinda all over the place. I think the game expected me to keep playing a lot longer than it took me to beat the game enough times to get my fill and unlock most of the stories. Some cool perks can be unlocked really quick. Some will take far too long. Some don't feel worth the effort.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a `meh` thumbs up since the mechanics of this game is not my style. What are they?

- The game is very difficult and punishing. Even the times you think your character is well equipped the game hits you with a `nope` and you die.
- You die a lot! Actually that's one of the core mechanics of the game. To progress in the game you have to die.
- The game repeats itself too much despite the RNG mechanics. I find myself doing very similar things just to die and improve.
- The visual style is too dark and depressive. You want to give a break after a couple of hours later because of the melancholy. Nearly never anything good happens here. Pretty much like Dark Souls.

Why so many negative and thumbs up? Because it's innovative that tries something new. It has really beautiful visuals and it's obvious that there's a significant effort behind the development of this game.
