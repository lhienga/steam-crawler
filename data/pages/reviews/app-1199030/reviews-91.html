\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTG is the type of game i've been waiting for. It has features that break new ground and is the type of sleeper hit that people are going to look back to. Like Slay the Spire which gets mentioned a lot in comparison, TG is so much better in many ways, except maybe for the hardmode type challenge.

It really brings many things together in a flawless, fun and atmospheric way; a roguelite card builder rpg with many character archetypes set in a dark fantasy story world. Just amazing.

There are a couple downsides though. The character balance aspect is always going to be hard if not impossible to get exact but the heavy handed way it's been handled for Necromancer for example is unfortunate considering the way they changed Apostate, it just all seems arbitrary if not lazy.

The biggest downside for me is the fact that there could be more variety of areas and corresponding monster types. Adding dungeons for example would be great to keep your attention for longer without it seeming like the same thing over and over just with different backgrounds.

Another problem is that the difficulty should ramp up more as later areas are a faceroll if you build right and/or get lucky. I understand that this is a roguetype but this could have been avoided with less rng char progression.

Overall a much needed example of the creativeness that can be achieved, unfortunately pioneering work is never well known except maybe in hindsight.

So much value for what you pay, very highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
