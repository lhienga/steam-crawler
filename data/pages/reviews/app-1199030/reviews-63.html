\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest game I've played in a while. If you're a fan of rogue lite's, I feel confident saying you'll love this one.

I'm not normally into card games, usually playing more strategy/crafting/exploration stuff. Surprisingly, this has most of those elements. The rune process is super basic crafting, but the gameplay is highly strategic, both in the way you play cards and build your deck, as well as how you explore the world. The meta-game unlocked after each play-through is remarkably deep and unfolds both gameplay elements and story that are each exceptional.

If I had one criticism it's that it doesn't feel super balanced. But given the variety of characters and the huge variability in which they can each be played, it's not surprising that some avenues feel a tad OP. To that end I would suggest playing it soon, before the internet broadcasts all the min/max guides, although it stands to reason the balance will be improved with time as well.

Overall, even in it's current state, I'd rate this one 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail is a fun roguelite deck builder in a dark fantasy setting that has an Arthurian slant to it. The visuals and many of the characters you run into are very interesting and sometimes fantastically grotesque. The music and sound effects are very well done and go well with the setting.

You start out as one class and as you progress you can unlock other classes that each have their own strategy. I've got the Wyrdhunter and Pathfinder to 20 and am working on Barbarian now at a higher difficulty. The variety in classes and difficulty settings offer a great deal of replay value. It's one I can see myself coming back to every now and then over time.

The game is out of Early Access, but it could still use some polish. I'd love to see them fix some minor bugs and flesh out/add more story and characters, but I do enjoy the meta progression and the deck building aspect is pretty well done too (if kind of repetitive).

If this sort of game sounds like your thing, it's definitely worth the $20.\t\t\t\t\t
