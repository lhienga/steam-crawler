Now that you got the message, we can go a little deeper:
Tainted Grail: Conquest is a little gem and a surprise but with a few flaws. The first impression is striking because it has fully 3D graphics and high production value. Every character is fully voiced (shout-out to voice acting team because they did a hell of job). The grimdark tone of the setting is a nice change of pace from big daddy Slay the Spire and similar games who go for a more comic-bookish, stylized approach.

In my opinion the 3D overworld doesn't really add much and the graphics are not super optimized which can make the game unnecessarily taxing on older machines.
The overall roguelite progression is a bit grindy, but I understand the reason, in TG:C the permanent upgrades are really really powerful and if they made it easier you could crush the game before even exploring the whopping nine classes you can play with. Also the class you start with is possibly the most boring one of them all, so this and the general wyrdness of the 3D map can make the first few hours a little bit rough.

I might repeat myself but the game really clicked for me when I started exploring the various classes, they all feel unique and they feel broken in their very own way once you get deep in the deckbuilding and start figuring out possible archetypes.
20 hours in, I just finished the game with 2 of the 9 classes and I am just on the second of the 10+ (!) difficulty settings you can unlock. I can easily see myself sinking a hundred hours into Tainted Grail: Conquest without really getting bored.
This amount of variety and content makes it stand out and a really easy recommend if you are a fan of roguelites and deckbuilders.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game! Better than it looks!

I didn't much like the looks at first, or the arthurian/avalon setting, but the game is awesome! Good mechanics, lots of meta progression, amazing soundtrack, playable classes are different enough. Also the difficulty starts out kinda low, so it's easier to get into than Slay the Spire for example.

Best thing about it is that different classes will feel OP at different times and for different people. If you check the forums you can find posts like "X class sucks and Z class is OP", and you can find the exact opposite as well. Once a class clicks for you and you understand it properly, or you unlock a good card for it, it'll be amazing. And there are 9 of them to master.
Most classes are viable until a reasonable level.

The NPCs and different characters are also pretty good for such a game, interesting stories and myths.
You have a lot of quests you can do during runs. They offer different bonuses so often they are worth it, but they aren't mandatory.

Def worth the price, especially on sale!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo the game is kind of fun... for a bit. Then it's just a long, boring slog.

First, the good. The atmosphere is fantastic and I really like the artwork and the story lines of the different NPCs you meet, both those that join your village and those that do not. Certain classes are harder than others, which I kind of appreciate. Well, it's not that they're harder... they just have to be played in a different way. Most are equally playable

But the grind... the grind is terrible. Roguelikes are all the rage and I get that it's part of the "experience". But man, this one is rough. Lots of others have commented on it but really, 40 hours or more of what becomes an hour of completely repetitive gameplay is kind of like beating you head against the wall. I mean, I guess people like that kind of stuff but Griftlands, Slay the Spire, Monster Train... all way more fun than this.

The other thing I don't like is there are just a few classes and most of what you unlock are just slight variations on classes you already have... so there really isn't much variety in the game. To me, there are way better card roguelikes out there. It's an experience and the art and story are cool, but in the end, to me, it's just not fun after the first 10 or so hours, your village is full and you realize it's just a monumental time sink if you want to get anything useful out of them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played Star Realms, Slay the Spire, Monster Train, Vault of the Void, and Roguebook. Tainted Grail has replaced them all for me. I'm only a little over a dozen hours in and I am probably looking at 100 more easily. The quest system and npcs are interesting. The world building is unsettling and mysterious. I have no idea what's going on but I keep discovering little pieces at a time. You earn certain collectibles by performing certain actions. Killing monsters gets you blood, every card you don't pick becomes a card shred, you gather tallow with ever candle you use... and each of these can be given to upgrade different aspects of the game. And the best thing about it is the 3D graphics. While not perfect (they overdue it with the depth of field imo) it's great to watch in motion. My biggest complaint for the game would likely be the loading times and performance. I have a GTX3070ti. This game should not have the frame drops it does. Also loading into a new game takes about 30 seconds too long for me. Even installed on an SSD. Honestly, they get that load time down to sub 15 seconds and keep supporting it with DLC, I'll buy it all.\t\t\t\t\t
