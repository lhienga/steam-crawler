\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have recently gotten into paper Magic the Gathering (yes the trading card game in-person), and this game plays very similar to MTG. I have never played card focused turn based games before and this combat system is amazingly complex and wonderful for those who want to think out strategies. I recently bought a steam deck and this was my first purchase for the steam deck (I had+200 games in my library prior) and it works just fine. The combat is slow enough to use your finger to see the order of the cards you want to use and when not in combat the controller parts work just fine.

The voice acting is AAA+ and very haunting for the setting. I am only 4 hours in and just the first two classes I have unlocked are fun enough to just play those and I have all the other classes to enjoy. The card upgrades are very synergistic and makes you want to keep going with the class you are currently playing. I love how you do not have access to every class at first because that would be very intimidating and mind blowing to try to nail down the mechanics.

Dying in this game is part of the design and I almost quit the first time I died because I thought I would start over from scratch but dying is the best part of the game! You technically don't die but get to choose a boon and another class and redesign your passives and play a completely different style even if you wanted to choose the same class. To some this may seem repetitive but that is the infinite design of this game to grow in strength and eventually make a "strong deck" for your classes to progress forward.

This game will be my main focus and was a hidden gem I picked up 75% off last weekend on sale and would recommend it full price for anyone who likes MTG style of combat where you have to play cards with limited mana and get to deck build along the way.\t\t\t\t\t
