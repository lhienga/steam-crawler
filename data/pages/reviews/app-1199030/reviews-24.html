\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe short:

I would've gave this game a hard pass review at 4 hours. Gave it more time and discovered, despite some glaring issues, there is a decent game the longer you play. Try all the modes.

Long:

Game was a bit different than what I was anticipating, I wouldn't overly compare this to Slay the Spire. maybe a few slivers. I feel Tainted shares more concepts with Thea, Diablo 1, and crpg movement. GFX are decent but traversing the world can seem clunky and zones can appear similar even though each area is randomly generated. Lot of classes to unlock, which could give one more playstyle options. Most classes look and play, meh. Some are fun once you figure out how to use them.

I was so close to refunding but realized I hit over 4 hours. I thought, might as well keep trying maybe there is something im missing or at least I can leave a better informed No review. At 9 hours, things weren't going to well. I died then noticed there were two other game modes. I tried those two out. Wow, those modes at that time were better than the campaign. Plus, I could try other classes I had unlocked. I did that until hour 11 and decided to go back to the campaign. There is a repetitiveness but I gained more appreciation for the content...Now, I am at ~77 hours and still want to play in burst. Don't play this game with a StS mindset, only in the other modes, Tainted is its' own twisted monster. =)

Takes awhile but some portions of the game really shine later. Got to get content unlocked. Still feels lacking in some areas but not bad entertainment. I am glad it is turned based. Get on sale for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good, but for sure has it's problems. After a certain point of progress every interaction in the game is just a constant reorganizing of ruins. Like, they start out small, and take over the game. Plus, the game is built around the whole idea of being completionist, which if you are not a completionist, then you can't really beat the game. Also, some balance issues creep in because there are just so many characters to chose from.

However, the monsters look rad. The voice acting is pretty great. There are some fantastic combat moves, and tons of fun upgrades. It's got a tense feeling to it. I would say the game has about 35 hours of fun in it for a non-completionist like me, so measure that against the cost in whatever mental calculator you prefer.\t\t\t\t\t

TL;DR: 7.8/10

Very entertaining game with solid mechanics, a few of them quite novel and refreshing. One of the best in this "card-rpg" style. The lore, setting and presentation really hit the spot as well, also doesn't take itself too seriously but isn't cringe either.

However I MUST re-post a warning that other comments already mentioned: "There are dialogue choices you can make in your initial runs that PERMANENTLY affect all of your future runs. You have to start a completely new save file and lose all your progression if you make the wrong choice and want to maximize your potential for higher difficulties."
This can be a little frustrating since the game isn't a single run, you do multiple runs for every main run. Imagine you reincarnate as a new character every time you died in a choice-driven RPG and then your new game/character still has the choices made by the guy who supposedly just died instead of a fresh slate new game. It's one of the mechanics I mentioned.
Of course you can do a hard-reset, but you aren't just losing the progress made on one character, you are nuking various cycles of reincarnation (great for grind addicts I suppose).\t\t\t\t\t
