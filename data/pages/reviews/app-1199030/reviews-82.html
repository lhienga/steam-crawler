\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStarts slow, but once it gets going there's a ton of fun content built on top of a solid deck builder. One warning - the controller implementation is pretty bad, be ready for some serious frustration if you're not using mouse+key.

For the first couple of hours I felt frustrated and found unlocks to come trickling in too slowly, but after unlocking a handful of new cards and abilities I began unlocking more per run. Runs have been taking me about an hour each, which is a nice length imo, and you can save and exit any time (thank you!)

Deckbuilding is competent but a bit hit or miss in my limited experience. There's a ton of classes to unlock, the 2 I've tried have been nicely themed even if playstyles are a bit similar (mitigating incoming damage is key because healing is limited). Base decks are pretty boring and it takes a bit to pick up enough cards/passives for things to be interesting.

Story and atmosphere are fun, there's a ton of events and NPCs to encounter, I really like the concept of the world so far. There's a lot of upgrades to unlock but I've only earned enough currency for a couple.

Playing via controller (XBOX) has been super frustrating. Card selection is done via thumbstick instead of dpad, and there's no dead zone so it's way too easy to accidentally move up/down when you only want to move left/right. DPAD can spend consumables even if they're not shown on screen, cancel/confirm buttons are not consistent, etc etc. I've definitely had some rage inducing moments caused purely by the controller implementation.

If you like deckbuilders this is 100% worth a look, especially if you like the atmosphere. Prepare yourself for some frustrations if you use a controller. Overall I'm really enjoying this game and I'm looking forward to playing it more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll be honest, I was very mixed on this review, but it leans towards the positive. It's BIG (like, REALLY BIG) selling factor is the voice acting, atmosphere and art design. They are just on another level and give me some of the goosebumps I haven't gotten since I was 12 and played Diablo II. It is honestly what makes me want to see more of this world and to continue playing, it's the best feeling of "dark fantasy" I have seen in a game in awhile. It's how I imagine Shadowfell (DnD reference) being. I really cannot applaud it enough, I love every "wyrd" bit about it (terrible pun intended). I want the studio to get hired by one of the better quality AAA publishers and given the budget to make a game with this type of storywriting and ambience, it is the dark fantasy I have wanted to experience in games for so long and they have failed to, closest to in the last years being Bloodborne, but this one does it better, particularly the voice acting. It really is exemplary dark fantasy, fullstop.

Now, the negatives - progressing through this amazing story is tied to a very unrewarding -feeling- (stress on -feeling-) gaming loop. In around 10 hours of gameplay I have maybe succeeded in half of the objectives, according to the in game tracking system on progression, but I couldn't really -feel it-. I am aware that roguelike games are about the reset, and I actually enjoy that, but the whole progression in a run feels really vague and, save for a couple of more noticeable powers you can get, forgettable. Things feel just like they -happen- and not like I made them happen as much as I could, nor do I feel like I am making that much of an impact and that I have grown stronger after each run, consistently. And that is more of a -feeling- than actual fact, and it is hard to describe until you actually experience it. The same is reflected in the battles themselves - they are kinda fun, the cards being the abilities, but the way they are played has minimal visual effect on the screen: your character makes a vague movement, so do the enemies, you flinch when hit, they flinch when hit, it feels like there is a whole bunch of flinching with no contact, and suddenly either you or the enemy has lost a bunch of health in between the flinching, somehow feeling -extremely- quickly. I don't fully understand what happens on my enemies turn, like ever, I know what I do because I play the cards, but if I judged just the effects above them, I'd be confused as hell. I honestly found myself wishing for a log of the events more often than not, just to -read- what happened, because I couldn't really see it. This is a turn based games, which means that all the impact needs to be done and felt through the turn, and it really isn't.

To summarize - incredibly things all around the gameplay, the gameplay itself... not so much. If you want to get it for the story and ambiance, totally worth it, if you are in it for some good roguelike loops or turn based gameplay, well, not so much. Please note that this is still a positive review, because I went in for 50/50, and the story, atmosphere, voice and sound won me over so much more than anticipated, but if you are going in with a different ratio of expectation, you may be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail is a great game. Game play is like Slay the Spire, Monster Train, and other card battlers. But the loop is more like Hades in that the player is building up a village, there are quests, dialog, a story, new classes to unlock, new skills and cards, and progress carries over from each run. I like the dark aesthetic and the lore, but it's the battles that make me want to do another run--that and the feeling of constant progress. I find the difficulty to be about right. Deaths come from my own mistakes, and I feel better prepared for the next run. Graphics are nice, but there is slight stuttering and choppiness with loading when going into a battle but nothing that detracts from the game. Heavy metal horns up for Tainted Grail.\t\t\t\t\t
