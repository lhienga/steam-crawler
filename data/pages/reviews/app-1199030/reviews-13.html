\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the game is wonderful in world building with their stories, sounds and visually for a simple card battler, it is, sadly, nothing else.

Game offers 9 classes to choose from(actually 3 that have 3 versions) and then drags the player from mindless barely rising in the difficulty runs to accumulate upgrades and open new passives. Once you do that several times, it does become pretty bleak.

I was hoping that, at least, the endless modes are going to be fun, yet after 40 battles they stop giving options to affect the deck: remove or add new cards. So, next hundreds of battles will be with same deck. No added curses, no new mechanics on enemies, just a linear stat boost. Variety with how to handle such runs is barely existing and you simply brute force the only way, since all enemies are basically a threat of amount of attacks rather than their type or other conditions.

The first 10 hours were exciting. New game for me, great voiced quest lines, but after passing with every class it felt dull. It doesn't scale well, I honestly seen way more variety in deckbuilder games on the mobiles that come for free.

It doesn't get new updates, doesn't have any target lock, so, the only way to play cards is to drag them all. You can imagine how annoying it gets after moving 20 cards on your unit or wanted enemy just to pass through a turn.

Speaking of cards. Since the decks are limited in how much they can be affected from the starting variant, you will never be doing small synergy deck with fun mechanics or playing giga chad 200 cards cycle decks. As I said earlier, mobs never get more interesting than their base form, just more attack/hp. Same goes for the hero.

While I recommend trying it out, personally, wouldn't pay more than 5$ for it. Maybe even 10 if target lock was a thing and I felt generous.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great deckbuilder with some great ideas, very straightforward if you played a deckbuilder before and an overall great time waster. A few issues keep it from the being among the greats though.

Classes are overall boring and very similar. The mages all summon things, the archers rapid fire things, the melees all hit like trucks. Maybe your mileage will vary, but i found the first class to be the most consistent and quickest to win with. It seems the other classes reward long drawn out fights, while the Wyrd hunter is just gogogogogo.

Despite being a roguelike, it doesnt "feel" roguelikey. Cards are very similar in design regardless of class, and events get stale quick. The side missions are neat, but its not like you need to do anything special to complete them, just play and the occasional progression with a decent reward at the end.

Overall though, fun game. The lore is VERY interesting to me, and the story takes a neat turn after the first completed run. $20 is fair, and they seem to be expanding on the Wyrdness with an FPS game in early access currently. Only thing i actually want is workshop support, considering the animations are so limited and the exploration ripe with possibles, workshop support should have been a priority in my opinion.
