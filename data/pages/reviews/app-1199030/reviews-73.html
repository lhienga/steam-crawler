\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game grew on me. At first I was a bit iffy and didn't like the wyrd candle system, but after a while I've really grown to like it. The RPG aspect of this game is also really fun. All quests carry on even if you fail a run.

There are 9 playable classes (sort of). Each class has 3 sub classes that share cards, but their passives, abilities and play styles differ vastly. You have a main hub base that you find and upgrade vendors over time. The map system is exactly like Slay the Spire, but you travel between fights in 3rd person.

Once you unlock them, I highly recommend new players try the Sentinel or Summoner, Good classes for beginners.

The Berserker is my favourite class. The fun of dancing on low hp while dealing tonnes of damage is quite enjoyable.

tldr; if you enjoy RPG's and games like Slay the Spire or Griftlands you will most likely enjoy this as it's a mix of both genres with decent graphics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I got the game 2 days ago, and I've already put in over 12 hours. It had been on my radar as a deckbuilder game, and I decided to pick it up while it was on sale. Hadn't played a deckbuilder in a while, and hoped it would scratch that itch.....

For the past 2 days, I've GRUMPILY turned the game off, late at night, because I needed to go to sleep, because I had work in the morning. Both nights however, I seriously debated just calling in sick to keep playing, because I'm enjoying it that much.

I love deckbuilders, and this one has a really cool setup. I love that it's got so many different classes, that play significantly different to each other. I like how it's got a feeling of Diablo, and that I'm in the town of Tristram, running around doing missions for the various villagers. I like how they all have fairly deep quest lines, with multiple stages, that have some interesting writing to them. I enjoy the music in the boss fights, even though that isn't usually my jam, it does make the boss fights feel intense.

This game is a hybrid of deckbuilders with progression like StS, Darkest Dungeon with the Wyrdlight/Dark mechanic as you explore/fight, Diablo in it's aesthetic and tone. And it really works! I definitely feel I've already got my money's worth out of this purchase, and I'll keep playing it. I've technically "beaten" the content so far, or...at least the game actually broke the 4th wall to say "thanks for playing, keep playing if you want and we will be making more content later." But, there's still plenty of quests to do, permanent upgrades for my character to unlock through repeat playthroughs, new classes to try out. There is a lot in this game already, and it's quality content as far as I'm concerned.

Cons: There are some rough edges however, but it's a work in progress so that's kind of to be expected.

1. The voice/text REALLY don't match up half the time. Just, completely different words are being said, compared to what is being written. The context is the same, but totally different words will be on the screen. Like, the voice dialogue will say "Hey there traveler, how fare you this evening?" and the text on screen will say "Ho there stranger, how be ye this night?" It's a bit off putting, and derails my investment in the game a bit. Also, the voice won't match the tone of the dialogue sometimes either. For example, the text will say "You hear a panicked voice, SHRIEK at you from the shadows" but the voice actor, in a particularly bored sounding voice will say "Stay away stranger, don't come near me." Like the person is totally calm, and wasn't just described as SHRIEKING at me. So I think either a rework of the text to more accurately match the voice lines is in order, or redo the voice lines to more accurately reflect the tone of the script. If you tell me the woman is shrieking at me, then have the next line out of their mouths be a shriek please.

Also, some of the overworld textures for the various npcs are very place holder. Clearly not matching the actual image once you get into dialogue with them, and you see their picture. I hope this will change, and we will see the various npc's actually look like their portraits, when we see them on the overmap. Because I've seen several identical, shirtless bald dudes, who all look REALLY different when I go talk to them :D

But yeah, for the most part, my cons are only superficial things, and chunky writing here and there. Stuff that can be easily polished up as development continues. The core game loop, the combat, that is solid work, and really fun. I keep playing it for hours on end. I'm probably going to load it back up after posting this...though I guess I should probably go EAT something first. If you like deckbuilders like I do, this is a REALLY good addition to your library.\t\t\t\t\t
