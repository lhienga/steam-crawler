\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the price of this game, you really are getting a whole lot of value. This game is very very fun and has some really well thought out mechanics as far as deck building goes. I love how the passives and your deck can really build off of each other as well as the passive seem tailored to each class rather than them being generic passives. This has a lot of love poured into it and honestly I think it's worth more than $20.00. The only two complaints I have are: it really seems like you only have 3 classes rather than 9 (3 main classes and 3 sub classes) and some of the classes need some tuning. Again, for this game being $20, I think I got more than my money's worth of content but I would love to see the devs separate these classes more and make them more independent of each other rather than the same class with different passives (possible dlc?). The other issues is that some of these classes seem extremely weak compared to the others. Overall, wonderful game, would buy more of this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeath slough with reskined same-ish champs.

Three main stays of the classes
warrior, archer and magic user. each has flaw(s) and a theoretical way to be played.

warrior: cards can hit hard. Best built class towards new players. Stack Hp buffs and Buy potions. Put up with the death and get to bezerker, I had fun with the life drain it does fall off as overkill doesn't always give you the full Hp refill.

Archers: death by 1000 cuts, this archetype felt so action-to-damage starved. Plenty to do, nothing getting done. I suggest aiming your build toward on kill effects or towards stacking buffs and using free arrows. The lack of actions in the early game severely hampers set up builds.

magic user: you summon stuff and it fights/blocks/buffs/debuffs for you in one of three styles and requires you to manage the creatures with your action pool. Skulls and concentration oil are your friends. The necromancer felt the most fun, the druid did better against bosses and legends.

The Stagfather is a D*ck and hold onto your candles, using them before you go into fight can help change battle in your favor.

Over-all this isn't a game meant to be fun. It is a patience test in disguise. Some heroes have no early game viability but to get their stride you need to survive to late game or die multiple times, I suggest the later, just kill the first three battles and die, repeat till the cool and better abilities are unlocked and hope they appear in the current run. (the worst offenders being the way-finder, apostate and druid).

The action/energy pool you get is best described as stingy and makes you choose between damage and set up, often rewarding neither skill, memory or ability, only dumb Rng card luck and overwhelming cheese.

bosses are huge health pools that do allot of damage. most summon brutes or defenders to Debuff you. champs without a hard punch attack or an adequate action/expenditure scheme will find themselves struggling,

The Npcs are best ignored, they're supposed to help you make your deck stronger or allow you to use items. I think the idea was there, however the currency exchange system is paced very slowly and requires hard grinding for little reward.

perseverance, a good guide and the willingness to cheese everything without shame is needed to enjoy this game.

4/10 will update as I play more,

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter my first 2 hours of play I'm very pleased with this game. The writing and dialogue is really good. The voice acting is some of the best I've heard in a video game. The theme is appropriately dark. The core game play is very solid with a lot of depth. The music is great (certain tracks are some sort of neo-pagan Celtic/Scandinavian/Germanic/Saxon folk music sort of thing with ominous chanting, etc.), and adds a lot of gravity and flare to combat. The game is a rogueLITE so the meta game progression along with the enjoyable core game play loop makes it feel worthwhile. The enemy designs and behaviors are also quite nice. So far I'm very pleased with Tainted Grail: Conquest, and getting this game on sale (for 70% off) is frankly a steal. This is one of those rare instances where I actually feel bad for not buying a game at full price to support the devs. Ah well, I guess that's a good problem to have.

Edit: 15 hours in now and this game is awesome. Best soundtrack since Mechanicus too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSorry but... not for me.
The game is litterally based on the idea you need to die multiple times.
In game terms you don't actually die as... reasons.
Reasons that, based on how you took the game, make or doesn't make any sense.
I personally found the whole lore a bit too much out of touch and just not very engaging.

The voice acting is fantastic, as is the music.
The problem with the voice acting is that there is a lot of it and is bloody slow.
So... I ended up skipping it as it becomes quite boring quite fast.
In the two hours I've played I start skipping all those fantastic voice overs as they were just a drag.

Aside that, I am not sure I actually enjoy the whole mechanics within the game.
Resources are scarce, this means you need to die-revive-die to collect more resources.
At one point, after the first death, I actually had to "suicide" as I could not do anything else on the map and so I asked the Druid to... yeah. Send me the most powerful monsters, so I could die and grind more.

The wyerdcandle mechanic eh... well. Boring.
Essentially you have to carry these lit candles or you don't see anything around you and you get negative cards. Some of which are actually devastating (I.e. play the card of lose 75% HP...).

Refunding.
