\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJudgement -> An overthinker's dream.

Turn based strategy lover here - So what does this game offer that's different?
(to keep those of us that usually find a trick to beat the AI in these types of games)

1) It feels like a turn based dark souls. You die, you come back a bit stronger, wiser but your enemies also scale with you lol - Do not think they are the same everytime... Imagine playing dark souls, when you go back to earlier levels and you are buff....Enemies are all in the same place and do the same things, same combinations...Not here.

2) For a couple of fights you think you are unbeatable, this feeling happens quite a lot! The enemy combinations are excellent and will tear your "unbeatable deck" apart (in a pinhead voice) IF you don't think ahead. The bosses also create havoc and you must pay attention. One boss took me 2 hours because my strategy was slow and steady....

3) Every turn is a puzzle
To succeed you have to plan ahead by many turns. (but it's also fun just to go crazy and die a few runs in row just to explore etc)

4) Card combos and different characters. Being honest, my 18 hours so far have all been with the default character. I like to push it until I find that limit then move to the next. The cards really start popping later in the game once you learn to count. Yes count.
ie you have 10 perks that are on different counters - 3 hits for one to activate, 4 hits to make this enemy vulnerable, 2 turns before max damage, you ultimate counter is 4 hits from 300%, you deck draw has 10 cards left and you are waiting for a certain card or combo.....BOOOOOOOOOOM 1000 damage. Oops, I forgot about the other 2 enemies, uh oh! This game will make us overthinkers greedy.


What is different to slay the spire?
You have a base camp that you populate and upgrade. It is really tactical and not just for show! If you're like me and want to get the perfect strategy or die the least amount of times, this is a great addition to the genre. The story and creatures are so f'kin dark it's pure evil++++, I love it. The people you meet, the little stories are completely new - none of this "omg there's a girl who needs rescue, omg I found her, yay happy ending" .... It's all so dark that you actually start appreciating any good news for you or the people lol

What else?
EXPLORATION. Since you have a map and you move around it with your character, other stuff can happen in between fights. Be careful, plan ahead. Yes you can kill everyone and loop back before you fight the boss.


What would I like to see in a follow up? Fights in Tight spaces style of play - Where you have decks but also move around a map, so location matters too\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version: If you like roguelites or card battlers, this difficult and highly replayable game will satisfy.

Long Version: A grim and fascinating card battler / roguelite mashup, Tainted Grail tosses you face first into an unforgiving hellscape that offers quite a bit more story than most games in either genre. It’s difficult to progress in the beginning, but this is by design. You’ll need to make friends, collect currencies, unlock abilities, and get to know the boss fights before you can expect to move forward. As you explore farther into the game, new mechanics emerge, new cards unlock, and new classes become available. It’s a solid game that consistently presents meaningful choices, so even as your strength increases you might find yourself dying early in a run after making a boneheaded decision.

There are significant differences between the available classes, each offering unique playstyles and synergies. While it’s certainly true that some of the classes are simply more powerful than the others, finding this out is part of the fun. I’d recommend jumping in without reading a guide so that you can enjoy the different playstyles of each class. The reality is that you’re going to need to do a lot of runs before you can expect to take on the final boss, so checking out the different classes gives you a change to switch things up.

The art and story are dark and delicious, and the voice acting is professional. What perhaps surprised me even more was the score – it’s atmospheric and haunting in a way I’ve never heard.

Bottom line: If you like card battlers and/or incremental roguelite progress, and if you can handle an interesting if somewhat twisted story, Tainted Grail will satisfy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail is a dark fantasy Card Battler that's strongly inspired by Slay the Spire. If you didn't like StS (like me), you probably won't like this game. The atmosphere and game design changes make it enjoyable for me, but it still adheres too strongly to its inspiration for me to play it for another 100+ hours (as I have for similar games). There's fun to be had here, but you'll have to put your life into RNGesus's hands to find it.

The biggest improvements here over similar games are the class variety and the hub area. There's nine classes available here across three archetypes. Classes within in archetype share some of the same cards, so it's really interesting to see how flexible some of the more complex cards are. Cards get so complex in fact that this feels more like a traditional tabletop card game than a card battler at times. However, this results in higher difficulty fights being a race to dump your hand or dig for that combo that'll CC/kill everything before it can attack you. Whether this is a good thing or not is debatable, but it's another thing that hurts the longevity of the game for me. Another detractor for me was the fact that all of the mage classes are strictly pet classes... Usually I try to play something of each archetype in these games, but I detest the lack of control over the pets. This might sound like a nitpick, but I really, really don't like pet classes and the melee and ranged classes are much more diverse in gameplay options.

While the atmosphere and environments are wonderfully grim, they come at cost: TG is very unoptimized and graphically intensive. I wanted to play this on my Deck on vacation but I also didn't want to cook my lap trying to get 30 FPS out of a card game. My desktop runs it fine, but it's surprisingly intensive considering the genre and how unimpressive the character models are. Even if the game does run properly on your PC, there's still a lot of bugs and performance issues. I have a hard time telling if something is an intentional poor design choice or a bug in this game. For example, if a card is duplicated and the target dies, the duplicated card fizzles instead switching targets (or letting you choose). Bugs also range from graphical hiccups like stutters and framerate drops all the way up to fight-ending crashes. A certain run I had to hold back on the final boss (which is scored) because it was easy to create an infinite loop that would crash the game.

I'm on the fence about recommending this. I had fun with early wins but trying to climb the difficulty ladder was way too frustrating. I'm giving it a thumbs up because it just consumes hours for me; however, I am quite biased as a fan of the genre. Tainted Grail won't win over any new fans to card battlers, but it's definitely an above average game. If you can overlook its issues (and you're bored of Slay the Spire and Monster Train), then this is worth picking up.\t\t\t\t\t
