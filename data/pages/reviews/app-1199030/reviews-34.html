\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExceeded my expectations by a long shot. Definitely up there with my favourite deck builders right now.

Pros:
- Good voice acting and writing, paired with a story that fits a rogue-lite game. Worldbuilding is pretty solid for a deckbuilding game. The first time round I definitely enjoyed listening to the stories but future encounters are kept short for re-runs.
- Lots of freedom of choice. You are very frequently given the option between 2 or 3 things which makes tailoring your build easier. Rerolls are also fairly cheap.
- A large variety of enemies that have many different behaviors that allow for interesting interactions. They don't just feel like stat-bloated versions of weaker enemies.
- A large number of unlockables as you progress.
- Cards scale well (from cheaper weak hitting cards to more expensive heavy ones). Damage is based off of a % of your base damage (in contrast to say Slay the Spire where a 0 cost attack will scale with strength the same as a 3 cost attack).
- Well explained tool-tips on cards and hovering enemies.
- Decent quality models and artwork.
- No major bugs so far
- Cheap, considering the amount of content and replay-ability
- Interesting mechanics (leveling up, passives, runes etc.) that combine fairly fluidly without being overly complicated.

Cons:
- Definitely some issues with optimization. Game runs relatively smoothly most of the time but has frequent frame drops when transitioning or loading battles.
- Animations feel a little clunky and repetitive. A lot of cards use the same animation.
- UI is passable, but definitely lacking.
- The overworld feels rather bland and scarce. It's not particularly fun to explore, just walking from one battle to the next.

I would definitely recommend it at it's current price. Quite frankly, I'm surprised it isn't more expensive.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce again I am hunting for a great roguelite deckbuilder and this one came across my eye. My first impression was that it's very grotesque and gory, but after a few runs, you get used to it and there's a fun game in all this dreariness.

The story starts with your village getting destroyed but then upon you dying, you return to life but notice that time seems to be stopped or stuck in place. There's this strange stuff known as the Wyrdness that you are stuck in. People don't seem to notice that time has stopped and strange things are happening. In order to break out of this nonsense you have to defeat guardians, save people who have things they need to get done, and bring some of them back safely to your village. Can you break free of this cycle?

As stated before, the game is very gory and grotesque. It takes some getting used to, but it fits the theme and setting of the game. You start off as one class and slowly unlock more as you play more and more runs. Just like other roguelite deckbuilders, don't expect to get crazy far on your first few runs. Once you level up and unlock new cards and classes, then the fun really begins.

Each class has their own special cards and an ultimate ability. They each gain charges for the ultimate in different way. Some gain it by dealing damage, some get it by taking damage, etc. It's important to figure out the gimmick of each one so you can maximize their effectiveness and know what cards to add to your deck. Some classes are definitely more easier to play than others. Some take awhile before they get stronger and more viable like the Summoner class. It's nice that there is variety to keep you on your toes to increase the replayability.

If I can be completely honest however, I think what kept me hooked on this game was the storytelling and the worldbuilding. Throughout the game you will run into a lot of characters. Some will join you in your village so you can buy permanent upgrades to your character. Others will usually have a side quest for you to do to help them live out their lives in the Wyrdness, or escape it. A lot of the stories were really nice and kept me wanting to replay runs so I could see how the individual stories ended.

I unfortunately do have some gripes with the game. The map movement outside of combat was a lil clunky and really hard to see if you don't have the Wyrd candles to help you see better. I wish it had just some fog of war or something, but be mindful that it may strain your eyes a bit when you are exploring without the candle. While there are many classes to choose from, the majority of them are just "echo fighters". They are copies of another class just with a slight variation. Like one fighter class works by you dealing damage, while another fighter class is the same thing, except it works by you blocking and not getting hit. Only some of the cards and the passive abilities change so it makes the gameplay sorta stale after awhile. Lastly, there is a lot of stuff you have to get to get the "true ending" to the game. Unfortunately due to how same-y the game feels after a couple of runs, I didn't have the urge to push for the true ending anymore, but I did save a lot of villagers and completed a ton of runs before I stopped.

Final verdict? This was a super fun deckbuilder game. Despite the gameplay growing stale after awhile, the story and good writing kept me hooked for a lil longer. The aesthetic takes some getting used to at first but it's worth it for the fun combos and overall package you're getting. I think the asking price of 20 is fair for the amount of gameplay you get for this game, but if you're on the fence, getting it on sale is just as good. Thanks for reading!\t\t\t\t\t
