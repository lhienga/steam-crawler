\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail: Conquest is an enjoyable deckbuilding roguelike based on a board game.
It does have some cons but overall I still think it's a good game and I recommend it for fans of games like Slay the Spire.
Here's my honest opinion:

Pros:
+ 9 different classes that all play out in various ways
+ Many similarities to Slay the Spire in the way you see/predict how enemies will be attacking on their turn etc.
+ AMAZING music!
+ Card choices feel good and meaningful
+ Passive/Perk choices feel strong and impactful (same with 'Masteries' which are basically every 10th level)
+ Fairly decent amount of NPCs you can meet and interact with. With the usual permanent upgrades in roguelikes.


Neutral:
Fairly simple story, this can be both a pro and a con for some I guess. But what can you expect story-wise from a roguelike.
Game can get very easy but not before you have spent a good while on it. After that, once you master a class you might be able to oneshot many enemies.


Cons:
- Simple combat animations that I sometimes wished at least looked better and more flashy/epic.
- Some actions/animations play out extremely fast, this might confuse new players.
- Some re-used models that might break immersion a bit..
- Some of the NPCs look nothing like their pictures at all xD and two of them look exactly the same xD see my point above.




no but really.. I have seen several people say the game is worth it for the music alone and I agree :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSlay the Spire meet Diablo.

A diablo style town and questing environment merges with a slay the spire style card game to create an interesting card game with unique cards and a few twists on some classic types of characters.

The game in normal mode is a bit unforgiving at first. Once you get a few of the losses under your belt and have earned a town character or 2, things start to get easier. Once you've filled the town and beaten the big boss you find at the end of the 4 acts. The replay ability kicks in, you've unlocked a new character, and added some new skills and cards to the available list, or found a few quests you didn't complete.

Quest items and town environment are shared across all characters on the save slot. So have no fear of getting a quest with the starting character, and then moving to another character.

There are some minor ease of use issues but largely, the game feels polished and bug free.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like the game, but I just can't, for the main reason of I'm not having fun.

1) The decision to either progress the story or progress your run is silly. I get constantly gimped by not taking the reward offered, because there happens to be a quest to talk about. The game is not more fun just because you artificially extend its lifetime.

2) The game itself is super grindy on multiple aspects. There are like 5-6 currencies you gather during the run, each for different vendor and it takes multiple runs to collect enough to even upgrade once. There are many levels of several things.

3) Until you have unlocked at least a few cards for the class it is entirely luck based. You will die and die again unless you get a bit lucky with cards early on or died enough times to unlock the cards that can actually make some of the classes work. Why can't I influence the starting deck at all or something similar ?

4) Luck, again. My runs alternated between facerolling the rest of the game after 1st zone and getting frustrated with the random constantly screwing me.

5) I'm waiting for animations too long. Each run is about 2 hours, more than half of that is me just looking at screen not actually making a decision, yet they're short and frequent enough that I can't actually do anything else meanwhile.

If you have too much time to waste, go for it. It can be fun at times. I personally had about 5 hrs of fun and the rest was getting shafted by random. Decided to have no more of that.\t\t\t\t\t
