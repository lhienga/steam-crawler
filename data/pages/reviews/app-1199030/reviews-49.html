\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best indie card game I've played in ages, possibly ever.

I've been longing for a game that had the character of Hearthstone but had a good single player campaign. Despite the many bugs, Tainted Grail still scratches that itch perfectly. I wonder if the developers were fans of Blizzard games, as it feels very heavily influenced by them. It has the card mechanics of Hearthstone, the art-style of Diablo (this mental reference came up many times while in the home village), the character development of World of Warcraft, and some really good story-telling I didn't feel the urge to skip, like I do most games. This game is for adults.

On top of that, there is a huge amount of depth to each class. They are grouped into 3 archetypes - Melee, Ranged and Magic (summoners). Each archetype has 3 sub-classes, with a bunch of common cards you can gather during the course of a run. Beyond that, each sub-class has lots of cards and passives that are unique to each, as well as truly unique mechanics. Each class is worth playing and learning on its own, with only one or two feeling a bit awkward. Initially the thought of learning each class mechanic was a turn-off, however the game makes it really easy to feel like you're getting good at that class very quickly.

Right now, I'm stuck in the campaign with two quests I can't seem to complete, so it gets -5 points for making me look for editing-game-save hacks to fix it, but with so many other bonus points and everything else done so well, this game still gets a 95/100 from me, and is absolutely worth buying. I'm looking forward to more from this dev.\t\t\t\t\t
