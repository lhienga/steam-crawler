\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible. The pace is a bit slower than Slay the Spire or Monster Train, but it works because the atmosphere is just wonderful to soak in. One of the best game soundtracks I've heard in a while. There's a lot to take in, the gameplay is VERY Slay the Spire, but with quite a bit more depth and complexity. The first class was alright and enough to get me interested but the game has completely taken off for me since unlocking the Summoner. The flow of combat feels so good, very rewarding to plan out and execute turns. Personally I haven't even run into what I would consider atrocious balance issues like some people complain about. I feel like that's just how roguelites play out - you have good runs, you have bad ones, and there's all sorts of nonsense that can stop a run dead in its tracks.

Is the game perfect? No... The entire aspect of walking your character around and managing your inventory and equipment might be a bit tedious and pull you out of the game if you're just in it for the card game. If you want a super clean cut, straight to the point deckbuilder roguelite, there might be better options out there. But this game is all about the fine details and superfluous atmosphere and polish. It takes a really special game to capture and maintain my interest these days, and I haven't been able to stop thinking about this one, it's all I want to do in my spare time (I play offline sometimes so take my played time with a grain of salt). If you are at all interested because you like the way it looks and feels and breathes, and especially if you've enjoyed other deckbuilders, you will probably appreciate it for what it is without disappointment. You can't really spend $20 on a better game, if it's what you're into. If anything I expected less based on some of the negative review on here - I went in expecting it to have glaring flaws, but it's been nothing but enjoyable, only exceeding my expectations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: Unrealized potential, its a bit of a shame

There is a lot to like in this game but at the same time I nearly gave it a thumbs down. They did just enough 'great' that I couldn't do that but it is still a bit boring.

Pros:
Incredible art direction and voice acting
Lot's of replayability unlocks and classes to explore
Graphics are great

Cons:
Gameplay falls flat. I find the cards you unlock are not that meaningful as well as the skills you get when leveling up. Compared to a master piece like slay the spire for example where your deck makes or breaks you and affects how you play I felt like all the classes play the same and the cards rarely made much impact. There are some great ones but it feels like they put way too much content without thought into making the gameplay meaningful and fun.

Difficulty is all over the place but generally feels fairly easy out of the gate but I have played a ton of roguelikes so it's a great place to start for beginners.

If you are fan of the genre it's definitely worth checking out. I think the thing that is so frustrating is if they just spent some time coming up with GOOD cards that have some real variability on how you play said class it would have made this an instant classic.\t\t\t\t\t
