\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the game. It's NOT Dark Souls. That being said, do not expect some mass open world. There are 9 play able toons. But really there are like 3, the other six are just re-skined and a lil tweek here and there. I tend to think it mostly done for the player and their likes. I prefer the Neco over the summoner. I just like messing around with the undead. Either way they really are one in the same. It can be challenging but still fun. I like the npc's and the side quest. The voice acting is great! Sounds to me like the actors really tried to play the part. The music is on point to me and the graphics are not to shady at all! Again, this is not some huge dungeon crawler. It's a deck builder card game with some RPG spice. I Like to play this when I am on the road or staying in a hotel for a night or too. (I'm a truck driver). I recommend this game to people that like card deck types of games. I was not a fan of them until I played this. As well, for $20 bucks, not a bad deal. I have 7 hours into it now, so for me, it has half paid for it's self with the entertainment it has provided me!\t\t\t\t\t

I find the concept of the game cool ( an rpg card/deck building ), but as far as positive reviews go, that's all I got.

* 9 Playable characters...
- more like 3 playable characters and the other 6 six are just skins with a little ability reworked.. (instead of playing a summoner with living creatures, new class unlocked! they're now undead! Same minions, same skills, they're just undead!).

* Ever-changing maps
- Maps are really small and boring. No exploration. Not so much ever change.
- once you leave town, the only way back, is death, or defeat the guardian. Must defeat 4 guardians to beat the game, and all their maps look exactly the same. I feel like I'm playing an Assassin Creed game with their same, repeat, boring contract quests.
- If the map wasn't the size of a duck pond and offered more exploration/free will. it would be a great game, but it doesn't.


sorry, it's not for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a bad game for the price, but I'll recommend the devs to work a bit more on the optimization options (controlling the game on some of the menus was painful on my PC).

Gameplay is unique and eye catching, even if a bit grindy.

Story, characters and atmoshere are the key positive points of the game, every NPC makes it interesting and adds to the story (even if only occurs a bit a time) which progress accross several playthroughs, Hades style,

Classes give a bit more versatility, but the variety makes it hard to get the hang of them, since they use similar core mechanics but change them in a way that makes the learning curve from the previous classes be completely worthless As an example, summoner, blood mage and necromancer use similar core mechanics but not really, to the point that what you learnt in one of them will not work at all on the others or even help you understand how to use them.\t\t\t\t\t
