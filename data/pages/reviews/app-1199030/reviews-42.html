\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA nice addition to the spire-like genre, Tainted Grail is closer to a traditional (roguelite) RPG than a pure deck builder due to the importance of consumables, quests and meta progression. There is a lot to uncover in this game - new cards, classes, quests and difficulty levels will keep you coming back for more.

+ Plentiful class diversity, classes from the same faction share a card pool but play differently because of their passives and unique cards
+ Atmospheric dark fantasy experience with great sound and visuals
+ Quest chains are interesting and the story adds a lot to the game, instead of just being set dressing
+ Easy to learn, hard to master with Ascension style difficulty levels

- Optimization could be a bit better
- Genre purists might dislike heavy meta progression and RPG elements
- Not a lot of challenge until you hit Difficulty 5 and higher

Overall, I recommend the game and look forward to playing more of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe people who cooked up this game clearly tried to merge: 1. Dark Souls (all of em), 2. Slay the Spire, and 3. Diablo (all of em) into a single game. It kind of works, better than expected, but the end results are simply not as good as Slay the Spire or any of the Dark Souls games to its credit it does play better than the Diablos IMHO but that is the least of those games by far. If you ever wanted to play Dark Souls as a shorter time loop deck builder ala Slay the Spire, this is probably going to be the best offering in that mixed genre for quite some time.

In terms of the story and the setting, I feel it is a little overbaked and overwritten especially the dialogue. Tainted Grail wants to be Dark Souls, but both Dark Souls and even Slay the Spire know how to say more by saying less; too much bumbly dialogue and needless backstory is more of a burden then an immersion factor. That said the goat who helps orient you is generally done very well, which is important as especially at the beginning you get a lot of him. One significant and repeated writing failure: the incessant need to have major plot-twists largely explained by your allies with: well actually we either were lying to you or just decided not to tell you what you actually needed to do multiple times for no discernable reason or motivation is frankly terrible. One could counterargue that this game really doesnt need plot at all to work and be fun, and I would agree, but if one then bothers to put plot in it is going to be judged, and if it is bad it just shouldnt have been put in. The intro and setup are good, but the major twists from there are just random and unexplained in a way that only 12 year olds and certain Hollywood writers that have failed-upwards think is acceptable writing.

Tainted Grails devotion to maximizing grim dark occasionally moves into a slightly cringy trying too hard category. That said the art is really good IMHO and successfully fulfills their Grim Dark aspirations. This goes for the music too, though there is one legendary boss track with angry chanting that definitely has moments in the trying too hard category and was occasionally cracking me up as a result.

I have an army of little nitpicks, but Ill just mention the bigger ones. First it is pretty easy to get enough cards in hand that the cards cover your energy counter which can really ruin things at the most critical of moments. Second wandering around in the world on silly little hard-to-see jaunts between encounters is clearly a step back from the clean concise move directly to the next accessible point of interest from Slay the Spire that they are copying. It feels like the developers of Tainted Grail were starting with but trying to improve on the Slay the Spire paradigm there, but their approach actually makes the world feel smaller than it would via a Slay the Spire world traversal approach if done properly, and their approach also slows down the pace of the game as the action and interaction are now broken up with what feel like janky, awkward, slightly-slow walking interludes. Walking around in the mostly static tiny-feeling world has such a different feel from the rest of the game that it really is a bit of a set-back to the immersion and aspirations.

My whining and nitpicking aside, overall Tainted Grail is definitely fun, interesting, and well produced; the strategy and play I find immensely satisfying with very high replay value. I had to play it for a while before I fully appreciated the richness of diversity across the full range of different class play styles it is one of the few areas where this game has actually beaten Slay the Spire in design at its own game and this is quite commendable and key to its replayability.\t\t\t\t\t

100+ hours later: It's definitely not Disciples II :) - more like roguelite-ed Etherlords II in dark fantasy setting. And about music - there is one awesome track, one great track, and all other tracks are just good enough. But that single awesome track is so good that I was listening it outside of the game for quite a long time.

In general - great CCG rogue-lite game with a story, 9 absolutely different classes, nice visual and sound.
Kinda easy comparing to Slay the Spire or other similar games and classes are absolutely imbalanced - but all of them are fun to play (except necromancer).
