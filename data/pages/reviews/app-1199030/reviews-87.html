The world is dark, gloomy, and violent. Lots of strategy involved with the turn based combat system. You will die. Its brutal and will take some grinding to go further into the game. There's 8 (i think) classes you can unlock. Plus the first starting class you start off with. I've unlocked 2 so far and am enjoying the uniqueness of the characters so far. Some you'll be able to add to your village, and some will give you some useful techniques (cards) and equipment. If you're into turn based strategy, or card games, I would highly recommend. If you're looking for something a little different that's done well, give this game a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail is pretty much a clone of Slay the Spire. I debated on whether to choose Yes or No for recommending it, and settled on Yes because for the price it's an enjoyable experience that should give you may be a hundred hours of gaming, if not dozens and dozens. I'm at 25 hours, and I only unlocked everything for one class and on my way to the next one.

The Good Stuff
Fun CCG and character progression that keeps you starting a new game over and over again. Regardless of whether you choose to play at normal or easy difficulty, both are fun. And it's great that you can switch difficulty and keep the same progression. On easy, I think the pace is much better. It's not that normal is difficult, but rather that's it's just slow to progress.

Many, many classes. Of course some classes share the same sets of cards. Three different sets of cards among nine different classes. In other words, three classes to each card set. And it doesn't seem like you can unlock the entire card set by playing just one of the classes, which is a good thing.

Instead of a room-by-room progression map in Slay the Spire, Tainted Grail gives you a little more freedom to move about. Not too much, but enough to make it feel a little more open ended. For example, you can almost head straight to the boss if you want, but at the same time you want those other battles to gain cards, items, etc.

Music is awesome. Mysterious and playful sometimes, brutal other times, but always very dark. Sounds are pretty good too. Most voice acting is decent, though some is amateur.

Graphics and models of each mob is solid. Love the dark artistry that really sets the mood. Some of the stuff is even triple-A quality. Kudos to your 3D art/animation team!

The Disappointments
First and foremost, this is really just Slay the Spire with far better graphics and better story set in a gritty, dark environment. The gameplay and flow of progression is pretty much identical.

The story is interesting, but many parts feel amateur. Sometimes I feel like an experience writer wrote a section, then other times it sounds like something a school kid would write. But the worst is the entire basis for this whole world: "the wyrdness". They explain away everything unusual by attributing it to the wyrdness. Seriously, writers should be doing a better job here. Sure, I get it, the wyrdness is messing with everything, but at least dive a little deeper to give more meaning to each individual sub-story, etc. For example, boy eats a runestone, but the wyrdness was still responsible for his foster mother's advanced aging. Squire drove insane by the wyrdness, but his backstory as to his actions is almost nonexistent. And the Florist and Countess, which could be an awesome story, was otherwise pretty much empty and the wyrdness is to blame for how they behave.

Normal difficulty will yield some battles that just drag on and on until you've advanced stats and purchased unlocks. But the real issue is that unlocking things is slow in the beginning. I don't think the developer has the balance right for time investment on normal difficulty. Perhaps the developer wants it this way? Whatever the reason, I still think that most players who enjoy a decent progression pace will want to opt for easy. The developer could have spent more time tweaking things so that normal difficulty was more challenging than easy, but didn't drag on forever. This should be tweaked by mob damage to you, not mob health or your damage.

And that brings me to the cards themselves: balance sucks. High damage multiplication that is combined with draw speed and reducing card cost is always going to win out over other strategies. The Master-At-Arms' Tactic Mastery neutral card is a game-changer, and when it combos with cards to draw more cards, it lets you win fights in one round. It would be foolish to pass that up on each game. So yeah, balance sucks.

On that note, for the classes that use the same card sets, there is always going to be a better class. Sure, you could tweak decks for each class to play to their strengths, but why bother when one wins battles faster than the others?

I mentioned the large number of classes as a positive in this game, but there are only three card sets. And as such, that means strategies are not 9-fold, but rather 3-fold with some specializations. I'd really like to see nine distinct sets of cards, as that would greatly increase the replayability of this game.

Overall this game still feels like alpha. The poor balance, the missing assets, the repeated narration in multiple locations, and the tons of other bugs. Tainted Grail needs a LOT more polish. And that brings me to...

Some of the little things that suck in Tainted Grail:

- The main menu itself is confusing. For example, will "Continue" continue my progression in normal or easy mode after I switched? Will "Easy Mode" continue or start a new game? Of course I've determined what each one does now, but when I first started these were confusing. Add more detail to these buttons.

- The constant sound of the anvil in your village sucks. Please mute it or make it far less frequent.

- Not having all voice acting completed. Sometimes you're getting into the game and the experience, and then a huge chunk of text with no voice acting.

- Some this-or-that choices are almost the same thing, or just don't make sense for how a player would prefer to answer. It's a simpler matter of not giving us enough character agency. Don't c***block the player.

- Terrain that doesn't do a good job showing the edges of walk-able areas. I often have to have my mini map open all the time just to fine the shortest path to the next encounter, location, or NPC.

- Combat encounters are pretty much humping the damn NPCs and locations. I understand wanting to prevent players from doing nothing but NPCs and such, but this looks really silly out in the open. And that bard's dance moves out in the open looks like a cheap imitation of classic WoW human dancing.

- The overlay of buttons on top of other text is problematic.

- Why do I have to wait for the XP bar to fill slowly after each battle? I don't want to have to click on Continue twice: once just to fill it, and another time to ACTUALLY continue.

- I was used to removing one card at a time. When presented with the option to remove up to two cards for the first time, I accidentally clicked through to only remove one. Poor UI.

- Loading combat, leaving combat, etc. are rough transitions. The screen freezes for a split section, then continues half way through animations. It's really sloppy.

- The pet is worthless in combat. I have to spend 1 energy to summon it, then spent 1 energy each turn to boost its HP and near-worthless attack?

- Wyrdcards are not consistent. Using candles doesn't guarantee good Wyrdcards for both playing and not playing actions.

- Accept buttons should be grayed out if you can't immediately click on them.

- When you have a ton of things to collect after battle, including XP, items, cards, runestones, why do I have to click through each of them as some separate screen? Just put them all on one screen and let me continue. I don't want to click through a Power Point presentation at the end of each encounter. Either streamline it or give us a faster play option to disable all of that screen loading, animations, and clicking after every encounter.

This list of annoyances I could write is much longer, but reviews are limited in size. You should get the point I'm trying to make: many things in this game are annoying and could be improved. I recommended Tainted Grail because it's fun and worth the gameplay time. But it's going to be a one-play-through game for me and not a regular game that I'd ever come back and play again.\t\t\t\t\t
