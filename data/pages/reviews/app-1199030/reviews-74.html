\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeciding between a 'thumbs up' and 'thumbs down' was very hard for this, but I think the mechanics used for a deckbuilding rogue-like fell into too many of the same pitfalls that other games of the same genre do. I think the game can be fun for ~10 hours but once the grind starts there is no real reason to continue.

Pros:
Interesting story, atmosphere, and general feel for the world.
Multiple classes each with a different ultimate, way of generating it, ability you can reliably use every turn, and passive ability to start with.

Cons:
Too easy to build decks that combo out on turn one. In other games this can incidentally happen with luck or planning, but in this game in particular it feels like that's a mechanic built in. There are far too many passive abilities that can reliably give you more energy, cards, and power on Turn 1/2/3. It makes the game uninteresting when the game actively provides an incentive not to interact with the enemies/bosses. My first Boss 3/Boss 4 kill was done on turn 1 before they could have a turn.

There are many card options for what you can add to your deck, but many of my runs that are successful I add very few actual cards to my deck. Many times the cards you are offered are "OK" and side grades to your starting cards or very situational. But you don't take them because you don't want to reduce how often you draw your good cards. With that said, the cards that are good are... extremely good. Especially the 'quest' cards that after meeting a particular condition you get an extremely OP card you can use once a fight.

The archer classes don't feel particularly well thought out or rewarding. Having a common card type that actively reduces your ultimate charge feels particularly bad. If you are actively using these Quiver cards, the chances of actually using an impactful ultimate is very low.
