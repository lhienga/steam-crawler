\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off by stating to the developers a big thank you. This is exactly the game I have been looking for, and I didn't even know it.

For the rest of you, be warned this game might not be for everyone.

Who is going to like it: If you enjoy board games, more specifically deck building games with wonderful mechanics (think along the lines of Gloomhaven) this is probably a game you are going to want to check out. The best comparison I can come up with is if Diablo and Magic the Gathering got drunk one night and had a hybrid board game video game child. It combines the fast pace suck you in mentality of an action RPG with the let me think about this of a good strategy board game. The lore and story is given to you in such delicate little bites, that you don't feel that it is being rammed down your throat. But there is so much lore and story that it seduces you to playing into the wee hours of the night. The music is the perfect accent to fade the outside world out, without it being a distraction (usually I turn music off on games). Every run is different, and every run is rewarding (and frustrating when you die). This is a game that you can geek out for a couple weeks, put on the shelf, and then will crave to come back to a few month later.

Who is not going to like it: If you are looking for a straight action game, or a game that is a linear stream this is not for you. This game can be brutal to you and you have to be able to shrug it off and get ready to start over. This isn't done like a Souls like game where you have to hit a button at the right time, it's done by the cards were not in your favor or you just chose wrong. You die and start all over again, with the exception of a few unlocked perks and access to new possibilities your next run.

What to expect from the game: Part of the fun of the game is starting all over and trying to get farther the next time through. You will die, and die and die. But every time you go back there is something new, whether it be another NPC or different combinations of perks and cards. At 32 hours I have only scratched the surface of this game, and looking forward to what more is out there. More only regret was I did not kickstart the Physical board game when I had the chance (same company made both). Hope this helped someone out there.\t\t\t\t\t
