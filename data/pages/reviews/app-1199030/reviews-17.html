\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA terrific rogue lite deck building game. 9 classes is a lot for these types of games, Within each archetype: melee, archer and mage, the 3 classes play very differently from each other despite having access to the same cards. Cards that are good for one class are bad for another, which makes the deck building for each class very different.

While there certainly is randomness within the game, the more you develop your village the more tools you have to remove some of the randomness and gain some consistency. Thus unlike say Slay the Spire, where the trick is to make the most out of the cards the game gives you each run rather than working towards a predetermined build, in Tainted Grail you can very much work towards a predetermined build and develop your deck accordingly, which is better or worse depending on your personal preference.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR : recommend on sale not on full price!
If everything was on par with the quality of the storytelling this game would be an easy recommend. They really nailed the aesthetic of the dark and corrupted fantasy world where everything feels a little familiar but also wrong or uncanny.

The game itself is fun too, very reminiscent of slay the spire, however the balance is way less tight. I felt like I was breezing through the difficulties after understanding how the enemies and bosses work - and some classes seem to be strictly more powerful and/or easier to play than others.

The second issue is performance, the game looks okay on higher settings but it just has no right to put so much stress on a high end system as it does, the optimization was clearly not ironed out properly. So the best way is to play on the lowest settings, but on lowest the game honestly looks horrible, and even then it's not running proper smoothly.

Lastly, lack of content. I finished the game with 100% achievements after 30~ hours of playtime. It's really weird how they didn't put a lot more achievements for specific classes, difficulties and unlocking all the cards and collections and such. I have a lot more things to collect and I only got to difficulty 9 while playing so I'm not even sure how high it actually goes - but without corresponding achievements I just don't have an urge to keep playing, but that's more of a personal issue.

All in all good game with lots of missed potential, so I don't think it's worth it on full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very fun deck building auto battler card game. This game is a rare 3D game with beautiful graphics, but also means a beefier setup. There is a large variety of classes and cards, letting you have fun with a style you like. Everything you do will grant you experience / currency in some form, so just enjoy the game and farm if you want a specific currency. There is an overworld with a town in the centre (including shops) and a few areas surrounding it; after defeating the boss in each area you can exit and progress to the next area - but you can't go back! So make sure to clear everything out before you take the portal out. There is a miasma surrounding you that can decrease your stats and acts as a fog of war, blocking your vision and making it hard to move and see anything. This adds to the immersion, but I found it really annoying so I turned off the vision blocking darkness, which made the game a lot more playable. The miasma can be warded off with candles, which at the start is scarce and your "light" level starts off low. You will encounter events as well as static mobs on the map, making it easy to plan a route to navigate. Like normal maze puzzles, I worked backwards from the boss and plotted a path to my starting point. It's important to note that the candle only depletes when you make a step, meaning you don't need to light one until just before a battle. Shrines have an AoE aura that can pause your candle depletion, and also has an option to extend the aura. This is pointless however, as you can gain the much more important gold, or enhanced stats; I usually save shrines for a stat boost before fighting the boss.
