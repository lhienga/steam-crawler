In practice, however, so much of TG feels hollow or unfinished. The enemy designs are creative but the models look rushed and suffer from clunky animations. The game's tutorial is near nonexistent, so the player has to sus out what the game wants them to do, all the while introducing mechanics layered on top of each other that are rarely highlighted. At first I was concerned this would lead to difficulty and frustration, but I was surprised to find all this chaos was in the player's favor (for the most part) but lacked any sense of accomplishment. The helter skelter of the game's lack of balance didn't encourage me to continue, unfortunately, as it just began to devolve into a grinding sprint for permanent upgrades. Nothing really feels cohesive and it feels better suited as an Early Access title with room for fine tuning, IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurprisingly well-rounded and fun rogue-lite deck building game with a unique atmosphere delivered through rather good graphics and backed by a truly great soundtrack. Would recommend a look to anyone interested in PvE deck-builders at all. My main concern yet to be answered would be just how the difficulty scales and balance of the various classes, as I did complete the first run I set out on successfully. Looking forward to challenges ahead as the "mystery" unveils!

Shout out to Jorbs (and the devs for promoting through him), as that's how I heard of the game in the first place!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExtremely entertaining and the RPG part is actually suspenseful.

I think this game is better than Slay the Spire, Monster Train or Griftlands, not necessarily for the combat mechanics or card designs/interactions but because how the exploration part is presented (grim fantasy, 3d models, events, wyrmcandle mechanic etc). Cthulhu boss music freaks me out everytime. Classes may be unbalanced (melee/fighter seems too weak in comparison to summoner/minion based classes).

Roughly 10€ too cheap for the quality of the game imho.\t\t\t\t\t
