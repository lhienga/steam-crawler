\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game itself is OK. Not amazing, just OK. There are allegedly 9 classes, but in reality there is only 3 main class types (melee, ranged, summoner) and each class has 3 sub-classes which play all very similarly except for 1 niche small difference in game play. So, classes don't offer much variability and are also not well balanced in terms of strength.

Each play-through you are supposed to defeat the same 3 bosses and then your choice of an easy final boss or a harder one. It does get very repetitive, however there are many things to unlock which gives you a purpose to keep playing I suppose.

In fact, I would probably have liked to play more if it weren't for all the crashes I experienced. The game is very poorly optimized and crashes a lot. I turned everything to the lowest possible settings and turned off some of their more demanding features which helped me get more time without crashing but the game would still eventually crash without fail. If the game was not so poorly optimized and ran more smoothly I would probably have recommended to try it anyway. But as it stands do yourself a favor and stay away from this title.

TL;DR: A passable game which might have been an OK way to pass the time, but is too badly optimized and may crash too often to have any sort of enjoyment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic dark take on the Arhurian lore in a rogue-lite deck building manner.

At a glance, it's a challenging card-based combat mixed in with 3D world map exploration.
The learning curve is steep, but oh-so satisfying once one learn the ropes, allowing builds and optimizations.
For fans of the rogue-lite genre, game play is a mixture of darkest dungeon and monster train.

The replay value comes in the form of NPC stories and lore that spawns randomly per run. Narration and NPC's are fully voiced - a stellar job of voice acting no less.
