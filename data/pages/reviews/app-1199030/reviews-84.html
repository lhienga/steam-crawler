\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best roguelite deckbuilders, BUT...

I really liked this game. A roguelite deckbuilder that isn't just pure game mechanics, but has story and quests. And I was quite surprised that the story and quest progression works really well between runs.

BUT! Not all encounters show up in all runs, I assume it's randomly generated from the seed when the run starts. This would be OK if the quests wouldn't be tied to encounters. However when you need that one specific rare encounter to progress the story because you've already finished everything else and you need to repeat run after run after run in a hope that you can continue your ~90% finished story, it just become boring grinding. Not even grinding because improving my character(s) isn't interesting either, I'm way too powerful already.
I had to stop very close to the end because I would need to play who knows how many ours of boring repeated runs to find bump into that one encounter. Sad panda.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid deck builder who's primary issues stem from pointlessly employing a 3D map you walk around and navigate which adds nothing to the gameplay beyond revealing finnicky movement, poor navigating/pathing, interactable layering, inconsistent interactions, and the biggest pointless issue - your primary consumable, the candle, burning down by the step rather than by the encounter (which is compounded by the pathing issues.) The UI could use another pass but the majority of issues stem from the game's decision to require map movement in this way when few other games in the genre do this, even fewer do it outside of spaced blocks. If you find this appealing or can put up with it's frequent minor issues or just want to use WASD movement (which resolves many issues by its nature) then its a solid title with some quirks and admittedly a very frustrating introduction with a weak class and an uphill battle prior to many of the game's lasting upgrades.

I look forward to what the developers end up producing for the main game project this was spun out of.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty solid roguelike with a couple unique twists on the genre. The production value is notably high, it seems like. Pretty quality voice acting, decent Arthurian myth inspired story and lore, and way above average graphical fidelity for the par for the genre.

My main issues are the combat is often imbalanced and the pacing often doesn't feel right. Combat is usually way too easy or but then at times it surprises you out of nowhere with being insanely hard. Additionally some classes it feels satisfying to play the right cards at the right time, and other times it just feels like you have to go through the motions of playing the cards because that's just how the class works. Which leads to you not really feeling too in control of the playthrough, so much as just doing what needs to be done, without much decisions in how you influence your character build.

Pacing is kind of wonky throughout the experience. On your first couple runs you'll be figuring things out and taking a lot of new information in, and somewhat struggling for resources. But then with subsequent runs, think will feel boring at times because getting a couple key upgrades will make it feel drastically different with how hard it is to balance resources. Wyrd candles noticeably feel hard to balance early, then seem to become so easy later that it becomes an insignificant tacked-on feeling mechanic. A few of the mechanics, actually, feel kind of tacked on. Like, they feel okay in the sense of lore and the world, but gameplay wise they don't add anything meaningful other than pointless fluff (the various different items and consumable, notably).

Additionally it can feel pretty grindy at times. There is a decent handful of enemies for maybe several dozen hours of play, but after that, it starts to just feel repetitive with the enemies and their predictable attack and action patterns. So this makes the necessary fights to acquire resources to upgrade the town and your characters mostly like a chore.

Still giving this a positive review as there's a lot of things this game does that are fairly unique to the genre and do feel pretty satisfying. And checking the update history for the game you do see the developer does care a lot about the state of their game, making lots of quality of life and balance changes. I feel like with a little bit more attention and culling of non satisfying game mechanics, this could be a really stellar game. Main thing I'd like to see them do is to just work out some of the classes so they feel more fun to actually play (Apostate and Necromancer notably felt like a chore to keep going to finish the run).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t**Summary**
I struggle with this type of games, I think they're OK for 3h then I get bored and start thinking if there's something to listen to or watch on Netflix. You do a sequence of actions, Visit Town -> Kill monsters -> Get a torch, for some reason I'm not sure -> Kill a boss -> Visit Town and Repeat.

**Thoughts**
I kinda like the idea to build my own deck, but they make it so hard for me to actually decide how to build it and once I think I got a good deck I get an upgrade of a card and forced to replace my old cards.

The walk-around-in-an-area-for-fights could be dropped, just do as "Spire something something" game and remove the "innovative" walk-around thing, it's just wasting my time.

**Pros**
- Fun fights
- Semi-deck building

**Cons**
- Boring
- Repetitive
- Deck building is annoying and unnecessary complex\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only played few hours until now but really impressed by the game so far.

The game is similar to popular deckbuilding games in general, you have different classes which play so differently, you lose your character when you die but there is a meta progression at your base, which makes new runs a little more easy.

Compared to other deckbuilder games, the positives I noticed until now are,

+ The storylines: The game has some subplots for the encounters you have which progresses over different runs. They are interesting and the voice acting is quite cool.
+ Dark atmosphere + Tribal music
+Variety of classes: I only played with 3 of 9 until now. The reason I am impressed was I successfuly finished my third run with Zealo class. At the start it felt weaker compared to others but when you get used to the very different playstlye the characted got very strong. Now it makes me want to check all other classes and get my strategy for all.

- There is performance issue I am having and as far as I checked there are lots of users with similar issues. Loading times are long and general performance is not good.
- There are variety of enemies but in the few hour I played, I felt that when you optimize your strategy you do not change it according to enemies. I mean that building your character is very important and if you successfully do it you basically do the same actions on all encounters.

In general, liked the game very much and with this price tag it is a easy buy.\t\t\t\t\t
