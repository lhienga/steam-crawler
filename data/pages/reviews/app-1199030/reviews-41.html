\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a good card battler but not as good as I hoped.

I was about to give up on this game after a few runs with the starting character. I did well in my first run and then I got stuck. The starting character isn't very flexible, mostly just a damage giver. Worse, I couldn't make any progress and wasn't able to unlock anything significant.

I switched over to another class (summoner I think). Suddenly the game became much easier, maybe too easy. I think this is a significant flaw in the game - the starting class should be reasonably capable, not the best but not useless as it is in Tainted Grail.

The game is definitely designed for grinding. There are lots of permanent upgrades to unlock but in many cases they take an absurd amount of resources to upgrade. You would have to play dozens of runs to unlock some of them. Unfortunately there isn't enough variety in the game to make it interesting to do that many runs. Once you beaten the "true boss" you've pretty much seen it all.

A good card battler which I can recommend, but I was hoping for more given it popularity and high ratings.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA flawed diamond. Has a few flaws but a diamond nonetheless. Here we go:
It's a card game that is also an adventure game. There is a plot that is not that great and maybe a little too confusing at times. Overall the plot is not very engaging and could get a little polish through a better quest system or something that does a slightly better job, in helping you track down where's what. It's kinda like dark souls as far as the plot goes, meaning it seems really vague and nonexistent, but there is something.

And on that note what this game nails is the amazing exploration that dark souls has provided us with. Going out in the wyrdness where everything is blurry and you can see as far as your torch permits. Totally nailed the exploration part. Another game that really comes to mind playing this game is Slay The Spire.
Can you image Slay The Spire having amazing visuals, epic music, a grim atmosphere, grotesque monsters and a real map to go around and explore? You can now. Tainted Grail is like you got Slay The Spire and made it for adults.

The actual card game gameplay has a few bumps but depending on the class you play it may feel perfect or totally confusing. Cards seem pretty balanced, enemy mechanics are interesting and have a smooth learning curve, all in all it feels right as a card game. BUT.

There are classes that are more straightforward and feel more powerful and simple and other classes that are a real headache to grasp and understand their mechanics. The worse part is after playing about 20 hours I found out that the simpler, better and stronger classes get unlocked at later stages in the game. And this seems like a bad thing to me. I believe it should be the other way around, first get the simpler classes that feel more powerful, beat the game, crush it and then let's try something else for a change or even a challenge. Sadly the class problems do not stop here.

In an effort to provide diversity and a generous range of class options the game just does the old Mortal Kombat trick. Sub zero is the blue ninja, scorpion is the yellow ninja and reptile is the green ninja. Similarly in this game there are 3 classes of summoners that are almost identical. In conclusion this is so promising for the future of card games, i like so many things about this game and most of them could possibly get better through a DLC or expansion\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game to try to build my indie library, thinking I would play it a few times, like it, but probably not get heavily invested, as was I making my way through a multitude of titles.
How wrong I was. This game has captivated me on a number of levels, and I've kept coming back to it, trying to eek out one last victory, or start another run, or find out a little bit more about its strange setting.

For the review proper, I should start with the music; as a big fan of Nordic Folk, I really like it, and it fits the tone of the game wonderfully. I thought I heard Danheim a few times during a battle, come to find out it actually was them. Really cool.
The deckbuilding is loads of fun, the mechanics allow a ton of freedom to build a character how you want, exploit complimentary effects between cards, and tear it all down and start again to see if the next build will work better. The replayability is enormous here. That's not to say the game doesn't fall into a predictable rhythm after a few hours, but it never gets boring, and is always adding a new mechanic, loot, vendor, etc to keep things more varied than a randomly generated map alone could.
Everything mentioned up to this point would make only for a good game. Greatness here is won with the story, which is far better than it has any right to be for a deckbuilder. There's some logical hand-waving with "The Wyrdness" to tie up narrative loose ends and bind the cast with its many needs together as a community, but ultimately the struggles of these outcasts are compelling. They're all (protagonist included) gripped by the madness of a fraying reality, But despite the effects of this wyrdness, they all remain quite human. The attachment to these characters is helped tremendously by the voice-acting, whose actors bring them to life. You can't help but choose a favorite or two.

There's not much more to say here. TL;DR: Graphics good. Sound good. Music great. Mechanics great. Story great. Voiceacting awesome. I'm excited to see where the game/franchise progresses.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is easily the worst roguelike deckbuilder I have played. As someone chiefly interested in tactical gameplay and decisionmaking, basically nothing about this game was enjoyable.

Cards feature lots of RNG in the form or random damage spread on every attack (often REALLY wide spreads at that) and many include other nonsense such as random targeting as well. Defense is done mostly by blocking entire instances of damage or skipping enemy turns so you will very infrequently have to make interesting choices about how many block cards you should play vs dealing damage. Balance and difficulty is all over the place. Certain passive skills and cards are way stronger than they have any right to be and will carry runs if you get lucky and find them. Conversely, the unstructured map system means that, at any moment, you can very easily stumble into a fight that is way harder than it should be for how far into the run you are and just get obliterated with no chance.

The camera system is awkward at best and downright unusable at worst. Some combat encounters feature enemies which are too large to have entirely on screen, and others feature too many enemies so that it is literally impossible to have all enemy intents and health bars visible at once. The UI is also just abysmal. Lots of incredibly important stuff is super small for no reason, such as the menu button for accessing quests, and the draw pile during combat. On the topic of the draw pile, why is the discard pile on the same side of the screen? Literally every other deckbuiler I've played gets this right and puts them on opposite sides. It's like the devs of this game were going out of their way to make the UI unintuitive.

Oh, and the RNG isn't even seeded properly. If you ever have to save and quite mid-fight, when you load back in the fight will restart and your draws will be completely different.

This is a genuine 0/10 game for me and I fully regret my purchase.\t\t\t\t\t
