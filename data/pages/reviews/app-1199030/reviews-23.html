\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail: Conquest would have been a perfect game, if only the epilogue was handled better. 9/10

My thoughts:

I love Tainted Grail. Dark, atmospheric universe combined with Arthurian legend? I'm already there, baby. My excitement was already at its highest. And the music, oh, the music was throat singing? Absolutely fantastic. Bring it on, man. I'm not sure if I mentioned it in some of my earlier reviews, but I am huge fan of bands like Heilung and Wardruna. The gameplay is still unique to me even as I write this review. Tainted Grail was the only deck-building roguelite game where I had an avatar that I could directly control. This game is not exactly the exploration type, but I thought it was really cool to see my character walking around and interacting and stuff. Moving on, multiple classes that, though they might look a bit of the same, play different from one another (Berserker is best boi). Voiced lines were pretty okay, in general--I thought Goat and Death were meh, in contrast to the rest of the cast, but still serviceable. Animation, especially after Griftlands, was clunky. There's no getting around it; this is the game's weakest point was animation, but it was still serviceable. It didn't pull me out of the fantasy, so to speak.

And of course, Tainted Grail: Conquest's crowning glory: side quests. It sounds such a trivial thing, but I assure you, I have yet to play another deck-building roguelite game that comes close to this game's side quest system. More than the multiple classes, more than the deliciously dark universe and art of Tainted Grail, the side quests were the real force of replayability. "On the next run, I'll play as a Blood Mage and hit the story beats for this side quest." Of course there's a certain randomness in the game, so the item needed to continue the quest might not be available on this run. That happened to me a few times, but it never irritated me at all. The game was just so fun to play, with so many classes and a wide difficulty slider that I just got lost in the sauce. And completed side quests, in addition to telling more stories about the quest giver and in-game universe, will give buff or heal or a powerful useful card. The system was absolutely brilliant, and I'm surprised other deck-building games are not taking notes.

Now, clunky animations I could let go. Card artwork not actually reflecting its descripition/title I could let pass. But the ending, the true ending, was "actually, nihilism," and that one got my goat. I was hoping to see more Arthurian legend,
maybe a glimpse of King Arthur himself, but all I got was Death's whole, "Well, this is actually hell, so it doesn't really matter. But hey, you made it nice, so kudos." It was huge letdown, the narrative equivalent of shrug.

Tainted Grail: Conquest had everything: visual, music, universe, replayability. Unfortunately, it tripped just before it crossed the finish line, the ending coming across as tripe and edgy. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm playing Tainted Grail: Fall of Avalon (Board game) alongside this and there are no spoilers. Feel free to pick this up :)

I love this game and can strongly recommend

Pros:
- Really immersive world, lost of mystery and suspense. Great voice acting and interesting characters really add to this
- Classes feel unique and meaningful. There are 3 major classes if you like, Archer, Warrior, Summoner but they each have 3 sub classes. They all play differently and all feel rewarding. My personal favourite are Pathfinder and Summoner.
- Similar to Hades, dying is ok. You learn the different boss encounters, gain permanent currency that isn't lost on death and can be used to make you stronger for future runs as well as unlock new cards and passives. You're encouraged to experiment
- Combat feels great. It's turn based Card game which I'm really enjoying. You can really personalise your deck to meet your style of fighting
- Straight forward to pick up and understand, also easy to take a break and come back to and still know where you're up to

Cons:
- Turn based combat/Deck building games are not for everyone
- UI and graphics sometimes feel a little tacky/still Early Access
- Game can become repetitive, at its heart its' quite a shallow, simple game. This may not be for everyone
- RNG with drawing cards, deck building, NPCs and events which may frustrate people
- It is kinda difficult. There is an easy mode but I've not tried it but this may put people off

Overall strongly recommend. It's cheap too!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn amazing card game that adds so much to this genre. Recruit villagers to your cause, each with different stories and abilities that aid in your wanderings around the map. You unlock other classes with playtime, each one different from each other, which adds a lot of replayability. Encounters are moderately difficult offering a good challenge. I really hate games that are too easy where you can plant your forehead over the keyboard and faceroll to victory with ease. Tainted Grail is what every game of this genre should aspire toward and I cannot praise it strongly enough. So far only one bug found; a healing bug associated with heals on runestones but I am confident the devs will fix it soon. Overall a fantastic and enjoyable game that is well worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t

There are total 9 specialized heroes in 3 main classes which gives you 9 different experience and strategy. Moreover, a complex strategy is created with Passive Abilities, Cards, Runestones, Items, Enemies, Upgrades, Trades, Randomness, Wyrdness and damage ranges. Every moment you should make a choice considering also future events. Furthermore, game has not only high replayability but also necessity to play again to unlock tons of new items, abilities and cards. When you die and you start another campaign with all your previous upgrades and story continue where you left. In addition, game increase the level of difficulty according to your accomplisments to give you a balanced experience. You can also manually change the difficulty.
The philosophical and psychological content of the stories and structure of the new world is very good. Dark humor and jokes are fun. Sounds and musics are very good. Suprisingly first class game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThink of it like Diablo but instead of action RPG you draw and play cards in turn based battles. Every time you start a new run - a new instance of the world akin to entering a new game in Diablo, you start with a starter deck and improve it by collecting cards through leveling and encounters. Each subsequent run you start off a little stronger than before depending on the permanent upgrades you acquired from previous runs.

Be prepared to end a few runs early when you first play the game if you are fast and loose with the cards. As you familiarize yourself with skill effects, enemy abilities and resources in the world (and guides to Quests and NPC encounters on TGC wiki!) you will feel more and more in control.

The learning curve at the beginning may be high for those who are used to getting bountiful gold and potions as loot in common RPG. The game rewards you when you find that one class whose offensive style you really enjoy and lure you into a time pit with fun and sometimes crazy combinations from card synergy.

My best memory in game - finishing a run in the AMs while being cursed with all healing disabled since chapter 1 (at 36 health) and thinking to myself before the start of each of the last 5 fights that I was going to bed soon. XDXD

The game interface (on PC) as many have mentioned can be laggy sometimes but is largely manageable with a bit of patience.\t\t\t\t\t
