\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am enjoying the atmosphere of Tainted Grail immensely, and the game play loop and battle mechanics are satisfying. The incentives to complete the game to 100% are there, and the "magic card" deck management and battling system pairs well with the rogue-like nature of each play through. Every choice you make really matters, and many carry consequences over to your next run through the game. So, in addition to being Souls-like in atmosphere, the NG+ aspect of this title is really done well, and your first death will likely organically be your own fault, but you will learn. It's a great fusion of different genres, mixed in with some decent graphics and an addictive score of trance music to listen to while you play.

If I were to ask for one thing, I would ask for multiplayer. It would be great to have a way to duel a human opponent. I would also love the option to export my completed deck at the end of a run, somehow. I can only embrace ephemeral accomplishments so much. I want a mode where you can compete with other players using your exported decks. This would further incentive the NG+ gameplay loops, as each time you play, it's like you are interactively building a new deck for usage in future competitions.

9/10. Do that thing I said and it's 10/10.
TLDR: Great game. my butt went numb and day turned into night.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat voice acting. Impressive illustrative artwork. Interesting enemy models. Classes differ widely from one another. Classic deckbuilding roguelike powerfantasy executed fairly. It's got a dark tone that I like.

Unsatisfactory(Stock?) animations. UI is hard for the eyes. Narrative blatantly sways to "Old-School RPG" at times. No FPS limiter ingame. The class acquisition and progression system is very unsatisfactory.

You have to move your character in a 3D environment without restrictions. I mean torch diminishing as you travel mechanic would be much better if they implemented it without a 3D environment. I'd rather not use WASD to move my character in a card game.

Another thing about 3D environment in this game is that you can shuffle and skitter around a combat encounter zone to interact with the event triggers they are trying to blanket. First, It breaks immersion when that prideful knight is just beside some ghouls and does nothing about them, and when you activate the event Its as if you didn't just clear the area around him of monsters. I'd much rather kill the beasts or whatever, walk some more, find a decrepit old ruin, search the place and find the guy there. This experience can be enhanced by obscuring the event I will encounter in some way. Darkest Dungeon does this by twisting and turning the pathways and not letting you move your camera away from the caravan that's carrying you.

TLDR: satisfactory art content, good battle gameplay, stock animations, unsatisfactory progression loop, bad UI, could be better tenfold if it were a 2D game, devs love you please improve\t\t\t\t\t
