\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe pros:
--Nice graphics
--VERY nice audio
--Lots of classes
--Lots of customization options

The cons:
--Poor performance even on a beefy hardware
--Same enemies with same attack patterns over and over again
--Inconsistency regarding menus - say you want to interact with an NPC to buy something, you walk up to him, press "F", press on shop and buy the items that you want, to escape the shop you need to press "ESC" button (on your keyboard), while when you want to leave the "NPC dialogue menu" you have to use your mouse to press on "Leave" button.
Also, when you encounter a NPC "in the wild" and he gives you options such as:
1. Learn a skill
2. Get a card
3. Forget a card
were you to press on say "forget a card" option and were to realize you do not want to forget a card and would like to choose another option, there's no way of "going back" pressing cancel would just make the NPC disappear.
--Bad progression system, let us observe the progression system of other rogue-likes, such as Isaac and Risk of Rain 2.
As the game progresses, you unlock more and more OPTIONS, meaning you combine these new unlocked options with your old ones in order to "make a build" which would make you progress the game.
Now, Tainted Grail also has such a system - as progress the game more and more, you start unlocking more and more cards, talents and classes.
But here's the problem.
You also unlock "bigger stats" meaning, at some point you straight out start the game with 2x more HP, more damage, additional skills, OP runes, etc
This makes the game trivial even on the "biggest difficulty that the game lets you play on"\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tokay so now that i've finished the game ( won a run with every class, did all the missions and got to the screen "complete the game" which leads to the final bit of dialog ) I can give my opinion on this game :

- the story and world setting is great, it's well written, voice acting is good. it's a great grim-dark world. first time I see this kind of narrative content in a roguelite ! ( maybe hades tho, but it's different, the story in hades is still more of a side feature )

- the gameplay is great, it's a good card based roguelite. 9 classes to play as ( and yes, even though it's 3 archetypes divided in 3 subclasses each, all of the 9 classes are still pretty different and really play differently )

- 3D ! it's so nice to progress through the map and fight in 3D. First time I see 3D in a card based roguelite and I gotta say that 3D coupled with the great story / setting got me really immersed. it made the combats feel that much more visceral. add to that cool visual effects ( especially summoners having their minions grow as they promote them ) and you're a 100% into the fight ! :p

- "the true boss" ( optional last boss fight at the end of a run ) and the leaderboard ( total damage dealt to "the true boss" over 15 turns, without dying ! ) great idea IMO to test out your different builds and classes and see just how much punishment you can dish out !

now there are a few negatives that i'll be detailing but i don't think that they have a huge impact, i still had lots of fun anyways !

- min/maxing your savegame. maybe it's a common issue in lots of roguelikes idk, but it's quite annoying. even more so because there's a leaderboard of damage done to the "true boss" ( optional last boss fight at the end of a run ).
theoretically, you'd have to wipe your savegame where you "completed the game" and make a fresh one all over again ( knowing that permanent upgrades can be a grind to unlock ) to get a min/maxed savegame.
because you get different options during missions, different interaction options with NPCs and that does impact your savegame and the options you'll have in your runs on this savegame. also completing the game and having a min-maxed save are 2 incompatible things /:

- EDITED. I initially thought the "true boss" was getting tougher with each difficulty level which would've defeated the whole purpose of the leaderboard, but I verified this in game and I was mistaken.

- occasional crashes while trying to load a run, never happened while actually in game so it's less of a hassle. also didn't happen much ( 3 times i think in 56 hours ? )
EDIT : played an additional 50+ hours and never crashed again while trying to load a run, my guess is that they fixed it in one of the updates.

In the end, despite a few minor downsides it's been a blast to progress through the story, to get immersed in this world and doing runs is tons of fun ! I can only recommend this game !\t\t\t\t\t

Engaging (albeit lofty) plot, the classes are diverse enough to warrant thorough exploration of each of their builds (really lending to the infinite replayability its roguelite-ness provides), gorgeous setting, interesting characters and mobs, and I love the map exploration aspect of going from encounter to encounter. It was a breath of fresh air in this nascent but heavily congested genre to not click a room on a map to progress, but instead you chart the most efficient course to make fullest use of your candles. Plenty of unique aspects that you can invest into and grow stronger, like the card drafting feeling runestone system and the mastery trees as you level up.
And there's a doggo you can pet.
Highly recommend, some of the most fun roguelite action I've played lately!\t\t\t\t\t

The story is entertaining.
The voice acting is awesome.
The graphics are nice.
The gameplay is engaging (and not too infuriating).
The music is surprisingly good, and often gets me nodding my head in time with it while I'm fighting for survival.
There's a good amount of variety across the whole game - lots of character classes to try, and a tonne of build combinations.
The mix of RPG elements, rogue-lite gameplay, and deck-building is perfectly balanced.

I sat down to play this for "an hour or two". I just looked at the clock and realised I've been playing it for six hours straight and I forgot to eat lunch. So there's my review. Bought it on sale for 50% off, but honestly, I'd pay full price for it. It's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
