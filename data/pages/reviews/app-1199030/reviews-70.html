\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe mechanics of this game feel good.
When you die it doesn't feel like a waste of time you are rewarded with new strategies that you can play, sometimes dying feels good you can move on to something new or double down and correct where you went wrong last time.- (This is great because I die allot)
The loading screens between runs and combat aren't tedious and playing cards/chaining cards keep up with how fast I want to play them.
While I wouldn't say combat has a ton of depth its easy to pick up, the pace is great, there is a great variety in how you can play and decision making is important. Just because a certain type of deck is the strongest for the character you chose doesn't mean you will get the cards you need to play it that way on every run, taking advantage of the hand you are dealt is critical (you can add cards through encounters however what is offered is random).

Basically everything this game needs to do it does well, it doesn't cost much and its satisfying to play i think you should probably buy it.

Only con is that it doesn't look great, it looks like the developers did the best they could, like the funky Van Gogh style fog was a great decision, but I think I spend to much time thinking about why that was a good decision... Also while its great you can customise your character most outfits look bad.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen The Witcher and Dark Souls had a baby, it came out as a deck builder.

In general- Challenging, and unforgiving. Completely consuming in its macabre feel, between the lighting, soundtrack, and general dismay about you, you can't help but be drawn in.

The game play- Interesting process to actually building your deck. It's a marriage between RPG's and deck building. Some aspects could use work, but the dynamics check out, though some parts aren't self explanatory.

Story- Holy crap, there's an actual story! So far so good!

Replay value- Keanu stood there for a moment taking it all in. "Whoa."
From the get go, you see a massive variety of different characters. Between those and the options you face as you build your deck, it's not likely you'll ever have the same run. Some aspects don't change, you basically unlock one thing or another, then it becomes available from the start of your next run. It doesn't take away from the replay value, I feel. Mostly because you're gonna die. Then probably die again. And again. Have fun!

If you like deck building, dark fantasy, and a challenge, pick this one up.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow did this get above 90% positive reviews?

Alright, given I did not buy this for the story. Which might be cool, I do not know. But one thing is sure, no matter how good it might be, to unlock more characters, you need to get through the same story, again and again *Cough cough* that ****** suck. Right, that is not why I bought it, I bought it for the tags "roguelike/roguelite deckbuilder," right after patch 1.1 Shattered reality came about.

On paper this 1.1 Shattered reality, looked really good. Still do, great graphics, love the atmosphere, animations, even the sounds, but what really matters for a game with above named tags, is the meta game, combat, a synergistic item/card system, and a feeling of accomplishment when beating a big bad boss. On that, it fails.

1. You can't unlock new characters through these new two endless modes!? Roguelite progressive meta game

2. No paths to take. Funny enough the story mode, do actually kind of have paths to take like you would see in other deckbuilders, but the endless modes do not. But why not? Optional paths, leading to a more dynamic play through

3. The options you do get "rewards" after each combat, is as pointless as they are plain. 3 choices, between armor, max health and attack. At this point, you might as well just level me up automatically or maybe just find some more intriguing synergistic systems with items and gear, instead of a stat pat booster. Same goes for the card choices, where you very often see the same 1-2 cards come up in a row out of 3 choices. Uhhh choices choices, love me some choices

4. The power levels of the endless modes are either way to easy, or hard. The chill mode "endless power," is rather chill except from the first legendary fight, where you more then likely will die 9/10 times. But the 1 time, where you do get lucky with the limited choices you have from cards, and chose the right stats to go along with it, you will get past it ... and as the name suggest, go on an endless boring run. Yes, The endless boring run. I mean sure, I like the idea, but you will never get that feeling of accomplishment when 2 hitting big bad bosses. Or on the other hand, in endless misery, get beaten 99/100 times before you get to a boss. pacing and accomplishment is spot on

5. The only positive I'll take from this game, is that all of the above is something that CAN be fixed giving the developers enough time, but since the game got +90% positive reviews, and possibly a fan base that like everything I seem to hate, I doubt it will happen. I'll crawl back to Slay the Spire, Monster Train, Poker Quest & Griftlands ... that do, deserve their tags.
A new great roguelite deckbuilder\t\t\t\t\t

I liked the music, the gameplay loop, the voice acting, and the world design.

I disliked the User Interface / User Experience, some encounter design, some quest design, and some class design.

I've spent most of my runs trying to get rid of my starting cards, and skipping poor cards being offered. Unfortunately this only gets worse as you unlock more cards. I think this game could benefit from a card upgrade mechanic.

At the time of the review, I've finished the game and I'm only halfway through getting the one upgrade I want from the Master At Arms. The grind is real.\t\t\t\t\t
