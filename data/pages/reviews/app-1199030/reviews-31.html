\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has its flaws, but I would still qualify it a master piece, almost on par with Hades. While not as polished, it is more to my taste on some aspects so... yeah, probably a tie for me.
If you like Hand of Fate, you will most definitely like this.

124 hours is the time it took me to finish 100% of the story. I still have loads of skills and passives to unlock on all 8 classes, and I have yet to dive into the two endless modes and the weekly event one.

The story is captivating, and the voice actors are incredibly good. Well, most of them anyway : a few of them are, weirdly enough, so bad that they're good. A weird combination of the best and the dorkiest. x)
The world is so intriguing that it made me buy the board game. I'm not joking, I fell in love with it. It's unlike anything I've seen. It's dark, deep, rich, and the mystery is so engaging that questions regarding the story will swirl in your head. And most of all, some parts are so emotionally touching that... I will probably never forget them. Well I can't promise it's gonna be the same for you, but I think it's worth a try.

The gameplay is repetitive yet addictive, because, in my opinion, it really is everything a good roguelite should be. You keep unlocking things - many, many things - and you always have to make do with what the game gives you, while having to make constant choices. Two runs will never be the same because of the order in which you start different encounters and their interdependence, and because of the items, skills, passives, masteries and choices you made.

However, this game, despite looking rather good, definitely is not an eye candy in my opinion. The environment is dark, blurry (because of a fog called the wyrdness - that can be visually deactivated in the options if it bothers you), with visual assets that tend to repeat a lot. The opponents / bosses are not so varied either, but they do evolve as the difficulty ramps up. Also, you will find that some of them, you defeat easily in some situations, but are an extremely hard encounter when paired with complementary allies. In other words, I personally don't think it is an issue : the diversity comes from the team your opponents make, rather than the opponents individually.

I would say this game is more about strategizing than anything else. I don't think it will appeal much to people who need constant action, bright colors or loud sounds to catch and keep their attention. They will probably find it dull, repetitive and not engaging.
But I personnally think this game is a gem, and looking at how highly rated it is, I think that's quite a safe thing to say.

I hope this review helped in one way or another. :-)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail is a card game roguelike title similar to Slay the Spire. Like Slay the Spire there are multiple classes to unlock and play as through the multiple playthroughs you potentially might do. The campaign itself is about a 4 hour experience once you know what you are doing with the ability to make every go harder and harder through selectable difficulties. I beat the game in about four hours of experimentation with the summoner class once I figured out what to do and the mechanics of the game on the level 1 difficulty.

Tainted Grail is an addicting experience with a dark and twisted Arthurian theme. To keep things interesting while playing through the same areas over and over again, the game offers sidequests to complete for NPCs that populate the world that continue through each subsequent run that expand the world and lore. If deckbuilding and roguelike games are your thing than I would check this game out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think a lot of love has gone into this game but sadly I just don't find it very fun. I think the 3D world and battlefield is really unnecessary for what this game is. I think this game would greatly benefit from simplification. Even in town, I as a player do not gain anything from having to run from building to building. I wanted to try out all of the different classes but I didn't feel like grinding to unlock them all so sadly I only got to try 3 of the classes. The first class I liked but felt lacking depth, the summoner I didn't understand and the sentry I didn't like very much. In summary I guess if you really like these kinds of card games then this could be a good purchase for you but for me there is too much unnecessary bloat in the game and it would benefit instead on focusing more on core mechanics whilst stripping out the walking around and weird battlefield camera angles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool roguelite deckbuilder with some unique takes on the genre. Highly recommend for fans of Slay the Spire, Monster Train, etc. I have over 400 hours in STS across PC and mobile with I don't know how many A20H kills. Tainted Grail was worth the pickup. Folks who don't care for the genre won't be convinced, I don't think.

Note I've reached difficulty #11 in the main game mode, and haven't touched the other game modes. Can't speak for how it is later on.

Pros -
- Ton of build variance. Lots of classes with subclasses different enough to feel unique. Insane amount of cards, plus the powerups you pick up along the way.
- No other deckbuilder I've played handles combat like this one. Playing as a summoner feels like the fantasy Magic the Gathering always wanted of being a mage casting spells from their spellbook.
- Satisfying wombocombos
- Freedom to fight as much or as little as you want, rather than set amount of encounters.
- Great voice acting

Cons -
- Little bit janky. Stutters a lot even while running on a 3080. Sometimes need to click a few times to select an enemy. Sometimes controls don't always behave as expected, like pressing Escape doesn't get you out of an NPC interaction. Nothing so bad as to make it unplayable.
- Soundtrack is tiiiiiiiiny. The few songs they have are great, but they get old when looped so constantly.
- The overworld gimmick feels pointless after the first few runs. Torches are too easy to come by.
- Some encounters kind of feel like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ if you go into it not knowing that the enemy in the group is build specifically to punish you for whatever you may be lacking at the time. You'll definitely remember for next time though.

With a bit more polish this could be up there as one of the best in the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
