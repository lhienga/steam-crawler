\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail: Conquest is absolutely a stellar game experience and well worth the buy. This was my first "deck builder" type game, and it really appealed to me as a slower, more strategic challenge, especially on the higher difficulty settings. I really enjoyed the older King's Bounty games, and this really reminded me of that strategic game play except with cards instead of creatures to click each turn. The two games aren't a direct comparison, but I think gamers who enjoy those slower, more methodical thinking games will find this a hidden gem.

The core game loop hits the right run time for something you have to repeat a bunch, and the randomness of the encounters, cards, passive abilities, merchants, and bosses keeps each run relatively fresh. The rogue-like part where you continue to gain long term advantages even when you die is enjoyable and as long as you stick with the somewhat challenging start to the game, it becomes more and more interesting and strategic as you unlock more and more items and abilities. And there are a LOT to unlock. Each class feels fresh and challenging, some moreso than others, especially with the learning curve. The sentinel, for example, is straight forward and powerful, the Berserker takes much more nuance. The quests are well-written, interesting, and span several playthroughs, though your choices matter and will impact your character on all subsequent playthroughs. I found this game to be fantastic, and it looks like I'll 100% it in about 200 hours played. Hopefully, there is some continued development and expansion, but even if not, it was well worth the cost to buy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally fun game! I'm on lvl6 difficulty so not late game but beyond early game now. Beaten it with one of each of the main classes.

The aesthetics are superior to any deck builder I've ever played. The grim diabloesque style works really well here. The progression system is a lot more engaging here than in other similar games since you're slowly building you village. Also, there is actual voice acting which makes the game feel more involved. Overall look and feel of the game is top notch!

Gameplay itself is also fantastic! The classes feel varied enough to make it interesting and among the 3 mains classes are 3 subclasses each with their own nuances. I've played my fair share of card games, rogue-likes, and deckbuilders and I'd say in terms of fun I rank this one pretty high. I definately prefer it to Griftlands which is actually the game which got me into this genre.

This game isn't without its faults though. One thing I dislike is that a typical run in this game takes over 2 hours. As a rogue-like I don't want my runs to take that long, especially in later difficulties where I suspect you will die a lot. Also, there seem to be far too many cards that are utterly useless or simply worse versions of other cards. This results in a lot of skips for card selection which means my deck feels almost the same at the end as it was in the beginning of my run. Also, my decks seem to always end up the exact same every run.

Another aspect I don't really like is how the damage dealt is in a min-max range. Part of the appeal of card games is the precision of planning a deck and executing a specific plan per turn. This can be very difficult to do if you don't know if you're going to be able to finish off an enemy or not. It just adds a layer of RNG that doesn't play into the game well and the devs didn't really implement into the game in a meaningful way.

Overall I really enjoy the game and appreciate what the devs made here. You can really feel the production value and the art style and progression system are fantastic. From a deck-building gameplay purist perspective, I will say there are some redundancies that make it less fun than something like slay the spire but still way better than something like the negotiation rounds of Griftlands (bleh).

In the end, if you like deck-builders, you should definitely try it out! If you never tried any deckbuilders before, this should serve to be a great segway into them since it has the feel of traditional rpg.

8/10 from me!\t\t\t\t\t
