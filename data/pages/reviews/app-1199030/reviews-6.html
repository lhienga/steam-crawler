\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery nice surprise for a card game.

Dark, twisted and grim world and atmosphere.. remotely connected to King Arthur legend.

I really liked the story telling and writing, even though sometime it was unclear how to progress or it was RNG dependant so it was not possible to progress in following run..

Speaking of that the formula of game is roguelite card game with 12 different classes that are unlocked in time. At beginning I found those unbalanced and was not under very good impression but after few deaths and unlocking permanent upgrades I started to roll over with various characters and difficulties and found I was actually wrong (eg. at beginning summoner classes looked as only viable but once warrior or archer classes unlock their abilities and upgrades they are much more smooth to drive through game in particular on higher difficulties)

My only caveat to card game itself is that deck building and everything start from zero every run and one can not easily choose the synergy he wants from beginning of new run as you never know what type of cards, abilities, runes, etc.. will become available this particular run. Never felt exactly screwed but even with same character the victory formula that worked previous run was working no longer due this and new had to be developed.

Still had great time over +/-30 runs through game to reach true end and save all npc.

If you like roguelite rpg and/or card games it is well worthy to give this a try.\t\t\t\t\t
