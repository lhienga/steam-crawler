\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTainted Grail takes the gritty horror of old school Diablo in a new direction, by combining it with the quirky yet dynamic mechanics of a deck builder.

Most skill tree or level based skill RPG's tend to produce characters that are all the same within their class, meaning your mage or warrior will be the same as my mage or warrior. In TG, the random elements of available cards or skills available at each level change how the classes play out during each playthrough, so no two characters are ever exactly the same. Sometimes, you get just the right combination of cards and skills to dominate your foes. Other times... you make do with what you get.

Be aware the game is more of an "Arena Survival" style, which isn't for everybody. Hardcore RPG players might be a bit disappointed in the lack of non-combat content. I wish there were more locations, puzzles, quests, etc. not directly combat related, however the combat mechanics are so interesting, it does mostly make up for that.
The voice acting is fantastic, especially the narrator. Her accent is so compelling, it really makes you pay attention to what she's saying.

This game is definitely different and interesting, with a lot of replayability value across the many character classes and skill combinations.\t\t\t\t\t
