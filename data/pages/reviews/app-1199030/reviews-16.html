\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn summary: if you've never played with a rougelike deckbuilder and like dark fantasy settings, this is a really good game for you, and not especially hard.
If you really into the genre, then the some minor differences and the story are a good selling point for 40 hours of playtime.
If you wish for a 100+ hours playtime game or you usually have an hour of gametime on the evenings and want to play a quick match before dinner, this might be not a good choice for you.

Compared to other representatives of the genre (mostly Slay the Spire as it serves as a template each other game copies):
+ visuals & audio: has issues, like the drawings and the 3D models of the NPCs are vastly different, but it manages to create a dark fantasy setting. The monsters are chaotic and blighted, the atmosphere is grim, the villagers are suspicious. The handdrawn illustrations are fantastic, the general visuals are mediocre. I think with some handdrawn filters and different lightning settings it could have been much better, but serves the purpose.
+ battles: they look nice, feel nice, and grant you pretty of good options to strategize. I would even say that you need to pay more attention to the enemies and react to them than most of the competitors, even slay the spire.
+ story: now this is both positive and negative. I LOVED the NPC interactions, quests, and how the rougelite elements are assigned to them. On the other hand once you've completed every quest, the game sits down because the mechanics alone are not enough to run the show.
~ Mechanics: they would be awesome, but we've seen each one of them in previous rougelike deckbuilders. The positive is that its much easier to decide on your tactics and go that way, since you'll have a lot of control in your deck size and runes. The negative is that despite the game having 3x3 classes, the overall variety is not that great, and after halfway into the story the random encounters with npcs turn into expectend meetings you look for to increase your max hp or remove some garbage from your deck. The "true" final boss is just a "you can't really lose, try to get a highscore" which was seriously uninspired.
As I mentioned earlier there are 9 classes, but each 3 uses the same cards and skills, so the overall pool are basically distributed between their specials. For example berserker benefits from being damaged, so you only focus on reducing the hits with armor, while pathfinders special requires it to not get hit at all. This means the block/barrier cards are useless for a berserker (unless you get a random skill to use them), while armor and hp steal is much less useful for a pathfinder. I could go on forever, but the game would be better with 4-6 wastly different classes than 3x3.
~ rougelite elements: they are huge bonuses, and the difficulty levels expect progress in them too. For example in the beginning the consumables and runes are really bad. With some upgrades they become REALLY good, and the game compensates this with more and tougher enemies you'll have very hard time to beat without these. The problem is that the actual cards get less significant.
~ run time: most of the genre operates with 30-60 min run times, so you can play it when you have a little time regulalry. The average tainted grail run takes around 2-3 hours. This can be an issue for many people.
- wyrd candles : interesting idea, but you get so many that you'll basically always keep one lit, so the really risky additional cards turn into free boons.\t\t\t\t\t
