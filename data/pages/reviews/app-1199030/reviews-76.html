\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, this game is a mashup of a few different genres, at it's core it seems to be a CCG rpg. This game has come a LONG way from it's infancy. It was poorly optimized and ran pretty sluggishly for what it was. These things have been drastically improved. The camera angles are better, the choppiness is completely gone, and the difficulty seems to have been increased. (Very ok with me, the earlier versions had some deck builds that made the game seriously easy). From what I've seen, the devs planned on this being a big world rpg with CCG battles at it's core. The game we have now is merely a roguelike ccg game (like slay the spire), only you move around on a map instead of choosing locations or points on a map. One cool thing is the big open rpg they have planned is still coming, and if you buy this game (last i checked) you will get the big version for free upon release. All in All, I find this to be a pretty good game as it is, it's an original world with original characters, the story is interesting (told to you by a Faun), there's plenty of unlocks for current and future runs, you build a small village by finding lost citizens in the Wyrd (a strange blurriness that envelops the map, you can get torches to get rid of it, another decent game mechanic). There are several classes to unlock as of right now with a couple more still locked (I may be mistaken). My most recent play of the game was about 5 months before the last, and it had gotten so much better that i felt i should leave a comment. It's a pretty good CCG and i am happy to see where it goes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat Game. If you're a fan of Slay the Spire (in which I have hundreds of hours played) you'll probably enjoy this. It's similar in Act progression yet on a 3D set. You'll probably trounce the early game but much like StS there's Ascension-like levels to work up through. You only start with one class but there's a few base classes to learn (fighter, summoner, archer types). You unlock them and their alternate variations just like unlocks in StS, basically, and each plays differently enough, for a total of 9 classes. Appearance skins unlock once you've unlocked all the classes and can be changed at will any time you're in the village hub before and between Acts once you've met a certain npc. The rogue-lite hub is fun to build up. The hub, characters and story elements along the way are something StS doesn't have and brings... "life"... to the game. The graphics are some of the best I've seen in Unity and shows the developers know what's up. The atmosphere is dark and dreary just how I like and enemy design, both art and gameplay, is detailed and fitting. As of Aug.19, 2021 there's new game modes. I've only run into a few minor bugs and sometimes it feels like the UI was crammed in but I've not found anything to be broken. Glad my friend told me to check this out. I'll now be keeping an eye on their upcoming game, TG: The Fall of Avalon, as well.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
