\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game feels a little incomplete when it comes to balancing and some UI polishing, but I am finding it extremely fun and addicting.

I could play this for hours and hours. I have done a couple really good runs, and I expect to do more.

This is pretty much like a more hardcore version of Slay the Spire, and with some more work I think it will be better than StS.

Some recommendations for improvements for the devs:
- Make the quests clearer when you accept them.
- Make the stuff on the ground that you click after you kill an enemy a little more obvious.
- Make UI elements feel more responsive when you click or hover over them. Make things in the UI light up and make noise when you move them around. Right now the UI feels lifeless, even though the 2D art is great.
- Unlock the Summoner faster in the early game. I almost gave up because that first class is super gimped. The game became MUCH more fun when I got the Summoner.
- Don't just hide healing mechanics inside of combat. I found myself playing the summoner forcing combat, and waiting to get my healing cards before I could end my fights, because I needed to heal full up. It feel like that will be the only way to progress seriously every time -> Get some healing cards, and use them before ending each fight.
- Give some more reliable access to healing, or just remove all healing cards, and balance the game for no healing.
- I felt like I was not given enough opportunities to remove cards. Shortly after my deck started getting some really good synergies, I started getting too many cards and my deck got messy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all this game is such a visual and audio treat. The portraits of all the characters look great and the world and monsters are made with such a gloomy grim-dark style that I just love.
The voice acting is great as well. I don't know what counts as "good" voice acting but the only way I can phrase it is that the characters in this game sound and feel personal and that's a very good thing.

at first I wasn't 100% sure about the combat but as you unlock more interesting cards and passives along with getting to harder enemies it becomes very enjoyable and addictive. Whenever you get a good combo or a well built deck in a run it just feels amazing. The only other card battler or whatever you call this type of game I've played is slay the spire and while I did quite enjoy that. This game has all the build experimenting that game had plus it has a interesting narrative.

This was a bit of a ramble but I 100% recommend this game for anyone who sort of enjoy card battlers or roguelikes with good narratives pushing you along\t\t\t\t\t
