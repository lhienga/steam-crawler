It is a good card game, not great though. It's slower than competitors, cards have limited combos, and overall gameplay is not so great. The UI feels a bit clunky and rushed like they used default assets from Unreal Engine. Would like to see a unique interface, better cards UI (art is amazing though), and more animations. The game's strongest points are the music and the overall style and ambiance. Danheim and his Nordic folk songs single-handedly carry boss battles. Also I really liked the dark style and narrative of the game, which soak the player with emptiness and despair.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\thuh, ok, this is a weird one. Let me preface this by saying that I've played A LOT of roguelike deckbuilders and I love the genre, but this one is very, very unique and does a lot of things differently. In general, I would recommend it to veterans of the genre (probably not a good starting point for beginners), but I have a lot of thought, so it's easiest to group them into a pros/cons list

PROS:
- AMAZING atmosphere, lore and writing, hands down the best in the genre (unless we count Inscryption :))
- very solid graphics, music and sound
- the game is initially really hard, good for genre veterans
- it's an actual rogueLITE, which is pretty rare in the genre (the only other I can think of is Rougebook), not that I prefer one to another, just nice that it's doing something different
- some of the mechanics are very unique
- massive variety on player's side, decent enough in terms of enemies

CONS:
- just one, but a big one: balance. I feel like this game might have been a little too ambitious for its own good, meaning that the massive variety in terms of player characters left a lot of numbers and interactions being all over the place. Sure, Im only 15hrs in and havent even tried all the characters yet, but I can already tell that, for example, Pathfinder is a straight upgrade over Wyrdhunter and is super broken in general. Honestly, I feel like almost everything in the game could use some adjustments, starting with hero and card design, through enemy encounters (some are muuuch harder than the others from the same level) as well as metaprogression (feels a bit slow).

So, yeah, I definitely recommend this game, but I REALLY hope that the devs will keep working on it - the good news is that all it requires is some number tweaking and it will be absolutely amazing

edit:
ok, after playing some more (I'm actually enjoying the game more, which is easily explained by the below) I think I can pinpoint the balancing issue more precisly:
- the starting class, Wyrdhunter, is EXTREMELY underpowered. I approached the game the same way I approach other deckbuilders, trying to beat the game with the starting class first before moving on to others. This proved extremely challenging, I managed to make it to the final boss, but it destroyed me since the starting class has basically no way of scaling up. Once I finally tried other classes, they were all a complete breeze, not even comparable. This can seriously discourage players with approach similiar to mine
- cards vs passives: overall, your character progression is done mostly via passives, you can pick any cards that vaguely match you archetype as long as the passives line up. This takes away a lot of weight from the deckbuilding process. I would like to see most passives nerfed and most cards buffed to achieve the balance in the character progression\t\t\t\t\t
