\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Descriptive Narrative and Voice Acting is Wondrous!
A Rich World design, filled with Eccentric Characters that keep you enthralled in every twist the plot brings.
I purchased a gift copy of this game for my friend who is a writer, simply because the Narrative was so thrilling.

Game play is strong, with a mix of Hearthstone & Any Classic Turn Based RPG.
Monsters have predetermined moves sets which you can see in advance, you access your deck of cards to play counters to what the enemy is doing. Adding and Removing Cards from your deck is Key to being Efficient, and you constantly unlock new Cards, Abilities and Character Classes (New Starting Decks) which keep the game from becoming Stale.

The only Con I have for this game, which doesn't bother me personally, is that after every death in the game, you lose the deck you built. This can be frustrating, but is well mitigated by unlocking new cards to make your next deck even more power! Unlocking Character Classes (which have their own unlockable cards/abilities) to keep the game fresh. Unlocking New Narrative that progresses the Story. You see the story revolves around you dying a fair amount, so don't expect to keep that deck you built too long.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the end of the day, you're either going to enjoy this game or not. So let's get in to it!!

Let's address the main concern for a lot of people. Storytelling. If it's not storytelling, you're lying to yourself and me. The storytelling is amazing for this game. It keeps you interested to learn the lore of the game and what's going on with the Wyrdness in this Arthruian-landscape. The voice acting is very well done, in my opinion. There's emotion behind each character you talk to. It's not just dialogue being shoved in your face for the sake of progression. The soundtrack to this game matches the storytelling and the combat all too well. The way the music is selected is still a little random to me and I find myself hearing soundtrack's appropriate to the situation and other times wishing it was a more epic title for that specific encounter. However, it's not that big of a deal and I still love to hear the music that's in this game.

Honestly, this game combines A LOT of really good elements from several different game types. The card collecting system and combat style is well implemented. You start with a pretty standard array of cards and as you progress you collect more to use during your runs. Not quite open world but each area seems to randomly generate on each playthrough. Mix it all together with a little bit of RPG and decision making, leaves you plenty of options to see why this is a rougelite game and will keep you coming back for more after each run (fail or success).

The combat itself can be really fun and at times cause you to pause and think about how you want to execute your turn (several time's I've ignored what the enemy had planned next and paid the price for it..).

The tier difficulty system makes me think of Diablo 3's "Torment" difficulties. As you complete your runs, you move up in the "world difficulty". It doesn't come without it's pros and cons. Pros, you get more experience, loot, etc. Cons, for lack of a better way to put it, get's harder. Thankfully, as you unlock those new characters (Which there are 9, btw) the game allows you to lower the difficulty should you so choose to take a run to collect some more items for that new toon.

Speaking of new toon. Each character has their own cards. Meaning, unless they're neutral cards, you must play as that specific character to unlock it's new cards, passives, and other abilities. I find this gives more reason to switch up what character you play as. Though admittedly, you're probably going to find that character that fits you the best.

Check it out. If you're in to these types of games, totally worth the full price. If you're hesitant, grab it on sale and check it out for yourself. Happy hunting. Keep the candle burning bright! You never know what's lurking in the Wyrdness.\t\t\t\t\t
