\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was fairly buggy in the past, at times which has been its only real downside.
It's essentially a card game but I actually think it's a better 3D-Slay the Spire (if you enjoyed that, you should enjoy this), and unlike STS you actually make tangible progress between playthroughs using ingame items to level up your HP/Defence/Quantity or Quality of acquired runes (so it's not just run after run based on RNG). So if you have a bad run, you can work towards the next one being easier! (You have to 'find' NPCs to bring back to town who have separate currencies to upgrade your deck/self/map.)

The music is also pretty damn good (it was what first intrigued me to the game and builds a convincingly gritty and mysterious world. Think Arthurian and Norse/Celtic themes. The sound design and visuals are very cohesive. Danheim!)

Came for the music, stayed for the playstyles and the engrossing ministories/narration. One or two cheesy character voices like the ghost lol but otherwise captivating.

The classes are quite varied with totally different moves and styles (one is "take as much damage as possible to become stronger"; another "avoid getting hit and you do more damage"; "get stronger the more hits you land". There are 3 summoner style classes, again all with slightly different mechanics (one of which can turn into a lich as their special lol) I was surprised how much fun I was having between even 4 different roles, and selecting which new cards would best suit the approach.

You might really struggle one run and soon after upping your def/HP enjoy thrashing an enemy. You can up the difficulty to get more/better loot but beyond a certain point, even with max stats it can become hellishly difficult!

Intrigued to see what comes next for the series, as the story starts off and is kind of left open ended at the end of the game (even if you break the loop).\t\t\t\t\t

Additionally, there looks like there's a handful of classes - there isn't. Each of the 'classes' actually belongs to 1 of 3 main archtypes, which utilize the same starting cards and discoverable cards; the only difference within those different sub-archtype classes is their charge ability - and even then, they mechanically overlap a bunch still.

Like, it's not the worst game, but after having played it for a bit - I actively don't want to go back and continue playing. There isn't enough variety between runs/decks, and the core gameplay (map exploration, as well as combat itself) is such a slog at the end of the day. Sure, I like discovering the story in the repeated playthroughs/encounters, but that's not enough to make me actually want to keep playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6 hours in and I don't really have much of a desire to play any longer.

In one word, this game is meh. Adequately designed.

The setting, art, and voice acting are very well done, but the gameplay just isn't interesting. It seems like it's replaying the same few levels with the same few enemies over and over with the hope that you'll get a unique interaction and progress the story along. Everything except the core gameplay loop is executed extremely well, though. It runs well, looks nice, UI is solid, etc.
I picked the pathfinder, finished a run easily, and since then I haven't had much motivation to keep going. I'm fighting the exact same enemies as before, in the same places, with a weaker deck. Even if I complete multiple runs I'll still need to keep searching for interactions until I get them all.
The combat is mostly building up combos and unleashing them. You just delay until you get strong enough to win. Every fight drags on even when it's clear you will win, you can't just crush them as every combo takes ages.

In Slay the Spire, after you beat the game, that was it. You actually progressed and the gameplay and story prrogressed with you. You didn't have to do the same thing over and over hoping for a random interaction to spawn so you could progress. Not so much in this game. It feels like padding. I would like to see where the plot goes, but it's become too tedious to continue. Maybe I'll return in a few months.

Other things:
The wyrdness mechanic is cool, although it seems to only affect you in fights so you can safely walk around with the candles out and light one before you start a fight.
This game didn't really need to be 3d. It doesn't really add much.
The music is good, especially the boss music with the chanting.\t\t\t\t\t
