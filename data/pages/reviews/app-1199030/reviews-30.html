\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a cool game. It's a deck-builder with a great theme and cool graphics. It will have you on edge for a few runs.

Pros:
+ Cool theme and setting
+ Card combat works well. Good pace, nice animations
+ Really cool cards and passives to choose from, you can make awesome combinations.

Cons:
- I'm ramping up the difficulty with each completed run, but I do feel like perhaps its a tad too easy to make OP combinations. Especially the final boss is a pushover.
- I'm not a fan of the metaprogression system where you do all kinds of quest segments throughout your runs. There's too much going on divided over different runs. It doesn't interest me and I'm just clicking the 'Quest' dialogue option each time when I find someone, not remembering who it was or why I'm having a quest with them. By the time I will encounter the next quest segment I'll be on a different run, and it won't matter to me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, I know, I played 60+ hours on this game and I still review it badly. That's weird, but let me explain.

The pro :
- Graphically, this game is great
- Musically, I love the atmosphere
- The lore of this world feels rich. I want to live great stories within it
- A lot of class options (which is great, 9 classes if I remember)

But (The Negatives) :
- Given a class, I don't seem to have multiple ways of equal power for playing it. And therefore, when I level up, I don't feel like really having a choice. I just take the best option at my disposal
- A roguelite is about unlocking new options for your next run. You need to feel more powerful or eager to try that new thing you got... Here, I unlock new cards that don't feel like giving me a new way of playing and therefore don't renew my gaming experience
- When You wander in this world, it feels empty (would love a bit of decoration, broken items... some life in the Wyrd to justify lighting a candle)

Conclusion:
So That's why I don't recommend it. The world and the idea are great and I want to love it, but all I feel is a bit of deception and a waste of time I could have spent on a more engaging/thrilling game\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIve always enjoyed deckbuilders and this game seemed very promising when I first saw it, I was not disappointed at all. I absolutely enjoyed the entire journey through the game, even completed all achievements because I didnt get bored playing it. Every class feels Unique and creative and I love that each class has their own powers and not just their own cards. The story is lackluster and not that intriguing, I know a lot of games that gave a logical explanation to constantly dying and coming back in rogue-like fashion much better than Tainted Grail did, still it was appreciated for being there.
The voice acting is nice and the boss music sounds great and very fitting but all felt same-y and got repetitive.

TLDR;
Pros:
- Unique classes
- Replayability
- Fun Deckbuilder
- Good Music
Cons:
- Story is lackluster
- Not as smooth as it can be which causes a lot of misclicks if you try to play fast
- Camera angles are annoying and some large enemies cant even be seen fully
- While music is good theres not much variety, it feels like the same song over and over
- After getting true ending you must go through the dialogue again and again and again in subsequent finished runs. No way to skip.

Overall Score: 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
