\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be honest: I am no expert in these card games and i probably will never be. So i can't really judge how good the game is in terms of balancing, requirement for thinking all possibilities through and arranging your deck.
What i can say is, that is very atmospheric to me. The music, visual art, sounds and story-writing is well done including pretty good voice-acting (most of what you read is being read out loud). Most of that is done in sketch-like form, which isn't an issue for me. It fits the game very well.

So far i had my fun and not too much difficulty in proceeding. In the end it has rogue-lite elements anyway, so i guess you just have to play long enough to unlock options that make the game easier for you. And there is a lot to unlock. I'm yet not sure where it's going to in terms of difficulty and complexity. Up to now I completed one run (after a few game-loops and unlocking stuff) and now have the option to play the next diffculty. And I am still motivated to see what's coming next.
There are many, many passive abilities, cards, runes, helpful items. Maybe to much for some people who prefer to be limited in their possibilties to force them thinking stuff through, but as said I have yet to see where this is going to.
Also there are 9 different character-classes you can unlock. But I haven't tried any of these yet, instead sticking to the initial class for now. Should be worth to try out the others too though.

Considering the fact that i got the game on a discount for a few bucks, I can say that it's well worth the money to me. So if you are into this type of game, give it a try. I doubt that you will regret it unless you expected something totally different.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot sure how did I missed this game till now, it's an awesome RPG card game, bought this at 50% off and worth every penny. Can't really compare it with slay the spire imo, there are story / quest progressions, and you can unlock more NPCs and playable characters as you progress through the game, which unlocks more upgrades / cards for the player's character. This game feels more like a Diablos card game, there are runes that can be combined / equipped to give more upgrades, every level up of the character (per run) gives more passive skills, upgrades done via NPCs are permanent for the characters.

The game is straight forward enough to grasp, the info on what each enemy can do / buffs / debuffs are quite clear (though you can't tell whether the mob is going to attack you or your companion, and you can't who your companion is attacking as well, which might cause complications sometimes, but it's manageable). I think the usual problems I have with card games is I tend to overlook what debuffs / buffs the mobs are going to do and causes some miscalculations sometimes which results in game overs, but it occurs lesser here. The game difficulty is just nice to make it enjoyable, though I can see for players who prefer more difficult card games might not like it.

Anyways, definitely a good purchase, I usually dun really give reviews, but this game seems to be around for a while and feels like it goes under the radar for people (since it's not as hyped as Slay the spire / inscryption), so decided to write up a review for whoever it may help :)

Definitely worth a try!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA superb dark fantasy deck-builder!

Pros:
+ Even before the game mechanics I have to praise the absolutely fantastic art! Everything 2D - the cards first and foremost, but also the menu art and the characters portraits look absolutely stunning and create a coherent, dark and brooding atmosphere (in the vein of the Dark Souls series)
+ The game mechanics! There are several layers of complexity that overlap surprisingly well resulting in a brutal, yet fair, with a somewhat steep learning-curve gaming experience. There are different character classes that you can play as, abilities that you gain as you level up, items and inventory management, runes that can be slotted in both weapon and armor. On top of that there are over-arching quests, allies to recruit and a village to populate and upgrade in between runs
+ The writing / dialogue and lore are top tier and will keep you engaged, always looking to see how the story progresses (in a genre that usually doesn't pay much attention to such things)
+ The monsters and their abilities are very well presented and written into the narrative, are varied enough to justify the roguelite aspect of the game and transform each combat encounter into a mini-puzzle to be 'solved' by your currently constructed deck
+ The voice acting has to be mentioned as very good, in tone with the overall atmosphere of the game, even if in a few instances the voice itself does not seem to match the portrayed character that it's supposed to represent (i.e. too young a voice for a much older character)

Cons:
- The loading times are a bit too long both for the deck-builder genre and for what is actually being loaded (i.e. a tiny 3D map and the dozen or so monsters that populate it)
- The 3D player characters models and the map itself, while not ugly per se, stand out as the weakest parts in an otherwise excellent overall visual presentation\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Decent rougelike gameplay.
- Runs are mostly less that 2 hours so losing each run isn't so bad.
- You can mix and match the cards from the different classes to create quite lethal combos. My favorite was using Blood Mage (with its minion auto-level ability) with cards that increase attack and auto-delevel, making a loop that can increase the minion's attack to 1K+.

Cons:
- The candle burning mechanic is annoying and I'm still not sure what burning the candles even does, it's never properly explained. Because of this mechanic, you are incentivized to rush from mob to mob before the candle burns out without fully taking in the world and game atmosphere etc.
- Lacks replayability, probably because its so easy once you figure out the mechanics. Compared to the classics in this genre like "Ring of Pain", "Slay the Spire" or even "Monster Train", I don't feel any drive to do more runs to unlock more stuff etc.
- There seems to be a story here but all I was able to grasp were mostly undertones regarding King Arthur mythology. Kinda wish there was a more coherent plot to set this game apart from other roguelites.

Overall, 5/10. Not a bad game but not one I would recommend either. The aforementioned games: "Ring of Pain", "Slay the Spire" or "Monster Train" just do it much better and I would rather spend my time playing those games instead.\t\t\t\t\t
