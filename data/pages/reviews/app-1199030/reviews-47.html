\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery much like Slay the Spire, though easier and with currency system for permanent boosts.

I did enjoy my time in this game, but got burned out. Still, it's probably worth recommending.

At ~50 hours into the game I feel like I've seen enough to drop the game, even though I haven't finished the story, probably haven't seen most passives or cards. Quite a bit of currency-unlockables left as well. I did finish achievements though (so not much to achieve here).

The issue seems to be how easy it is to completely break the game, but at the same time how the design forces you to it (at least on higher difficulties). For example your max hp will be something like 100-200, while enemies will start hitting for thousands. You'll need to block everything or (much more often and easily achieved) kill everything in the first turn.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI so badly want to recommend this game because the story/lore, setting, music and especially the voice acting at all top-freaking-notch. But as rogue-lite "deck builder" this game fails really, really hard on progression and that bums me out.

5 hours in and here are my observations thus far:

Every single run starts with the same "perks" avail and you get to choose one: "no buff," +200 gold, +20 max health, +2 healing potions. That's it. You don't get to craft a "deck" before you head out so you start with the same few cards that mostly suck ass and you just have to hope that one of the early drops you get from leveling up is something that will help you. Often times, you're dead in the water after two fights because you didn't get anything worthwhile. You need something that is going to hit multiple enemies or you're absolutely done.

I've unlocked 5 classes so far but I'm still playing the first one because I haven't seen everything it has to offer. Or have I? 3 energy every round, sometimes I have cards that don't cost anything but sometimes they do. Sometimes the game gives me a random card that I HAVE to play or else I receive an absolutely ridiculous debuff...for example "Give all enemies 20 shield OR you lose 25% of your health on the next turn. That is just....stupid. This is "normal" level difficulty, why is this a thing?

You don't really get much stronger after finishing a level. You get a candle to keep darkness away for a couple of minutes. Beyond that, good cards are so rare so you'll likely be going into act 2 all beat up with few potions and the same cards you just barely scraped by the first act with. Have fun with that.
