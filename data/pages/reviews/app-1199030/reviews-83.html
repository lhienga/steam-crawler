\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a various amount of card games to date, mostly online card games as Gwent, Runeterra, Shadowverse. Also the well known games Slay the spire and loop hero, and a bit lesser known such as Hands of fate 2, Book of demons etc. So far I'm pleasantly surprised by this game, probably my favourite roguelite deckbuilding game I've played. It plays very much like Slay the Spire and the atmosphere feels a bit like 'Loop hero' meets Diablo. That said it distinguishes itself enough to feel fresh and to be a game with its own ideas.

First of this game feels much more like an RPG, which you could compare to 'Loop hero'. Between bosses you get back to your village and can buy upgrades, talk to NPC's you found in the 'Wyrdness', and a few other things. The story is continuous and every run evolves the story and world more, slowly you get to know more and more of the world you're in. One way it does this are the NPC's which you can save by doing various quests for them. The NPC's you will encounter on the map that you walk through and where you can 'pick' your fights.
I read some people find the map tedious and that it becomes repetitive, which can be true however I didn't mind it much, I actually enjoyed it. The developers listened however and they made 3 new game modes in the current beta branch which focuses more on the battles and not on the map/story itself.

The classes are diverse (9 classes) and distinctive enough, lots of cards (I believe around 300), passives (30-36 per class) , some masteries (6 per class), items you can use, and runestones you can equip. This gives a lot of room to make creative and unique decks which adds a lot of replayability to the game.

On a small note, since most models are 3D and it's a mix of a RPG and card game you will need higher specs on your laptop/pc than for the previous mentioned card games! They're still optimizing the game as well, so I wouldn't recommend playing this game on a potato..

So far have played it for around 20 hours and can see myself playing it for many many more hours! If you like roguelite cardbuilding games then you'll definitely like Tainted Grail: Conquest.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had been burned recently (like 12 hours ago recently) by a game that looked right up my alley. I wanted so bad to enjoy its unique initiative system and gothic horror aesthetic, and even played what became a grueling 5 hours, just to try to give it an honest chance. In the end, with a heavy heart, I had to leave my first ever negative review. So when I was scrolling through my library and remembered I'd purchased this a few months back, I was cautious. It too had looked like a fun time, "right up my alley," I'd said.

I am delighted to say I was right about this one.

A satisfying blend of Slay The Spire mechanics and grim mystery reminiscent of Darkwood, Tainted Grail exceeds my expectations in gameplay and aesthetic, but what surprised me most was how satisfying each run has felt, even in my eventual failure. Games like these can suffer early on because there's generally some amount of grinding expected, and building up your roster and bonuses can take a while before it starts to feel like you're effective. Not so with TG, which left me feeling impressively nasty right out the gate. Even with baseline equipment and abilities, I can make significant progress, which prevents the burnout that so many roguelikes invariably cause. Every aspect is clearly defined and explained in helpful tooltips, all of which feel very intuitive and natural (particularly if you've ever been a TCG player, it's like riding a bike). I am also fond of the way the story is revealed, in fragments and snippets, like moments of clarity hours after waking from a terrible dream.

At the time of this review, I've only put 6 hours into the game. For comparison, in that first game I mentioned, the first 5 hours were a slog that I begrudgingly dragged through. This was 6 hours gone in a flash, and I only stopped because I had to sleep sometime.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought Tainted Grail way back in early access and thought to myself I'd not play it until it's finished.

I'm glad I did. This is a very solid rogue-like, with elements of Hades (progressing elements of the story with each run) and a fluid combat and upgrade system flowing akin Slay the Spire. The atmosphere is gritty, dark, and reminds me a bit of experiencing Diablo 2 for the first time.

All in all, a well made game that made me very excited for other projects the devs have planned to release.\t\t\t\t\t
