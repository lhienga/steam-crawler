\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had never played a Fable game before, so I decided to see what all the fuss was about.
I realize that it's a remaster of a decade old game (at the time), but I will review it as-is for those that are not just counting on nostalgia to play this game.

- Story is a generic "zero to hero" (or villain) trope with bumbling British humor.
- Combat is rough and clunky.
- Level design is quite narrow.
- Art design and graphics definitely show it's true age, despite being a remaster.
- The way NPCs respond to your progress and attractiveness level is a nice touch.
- Non-combat mechanics (fishing, marriage, etc.) are very basic and barebones.

All in all, I cannot recommend it unless you played it as a kid. If you want a game with similar vibes, just play Kingdoms of Amalur.\t\t\t\t\t

This is definitely the game I remember, and it's just as fun and charming. This game has great controller support out of the box, and I love that. And the original voice acting is still there, which was a great feature of the original.

The one thing I don't like is the graphics upgrade. The update adds a lot more detail and much better lighting compared to the original. However, some of the updated models just look awful; the young hero looks like an old man. And one thing that is missing from the original is that everything had a slight haze to it, almost like you were in a dream.

The original game was bright and colorful while the new color palette looks darker and more muddy, but this is fixable using mods.

Still, it's a good game overall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's never been a game made since Fable: The Lost Chapters that hits quite as right as Fable Anniversary. It is incredible and instills the player with an atmosphere unlike any other game. The humor is there, the world feels lived-in, and every quest feels carefully constructed with fun in mind. Honestly there's nothing quite like it and that's what I love most about it.

I first picked up Fable on the original Xbox when my older brother rented it in 2004 at the ripe old age of 6. Even then I felt a sense of immense originality in the narrative, music, and art style in this game. The experience of defeating Twinblade for the first time was a heroic feeling. The ability to choose your own path and to have that path reflect on your character incrementally has kept me coming back about once a year for another play-through ever since 2004.

Coming back to the world of Albion after 10 years to a new art style and improved textures/graphics was a wild ride. I highly recommend this game for everyone honestly. Maybe not everyone will love it to the extent I do, but it's always worth trying out.\t\t\t\t\t
