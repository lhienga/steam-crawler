\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA charming, old-school game that I liked, but wanted to like more. The story is solid, though it can feel a bit dated at times. Characters are fun, if a bit forgettable to a new player. Voice acting and the script are endearing through their emphasis on a natural, colloquial style. But there are a few irks, pretty big ones at that. The UI is quite insufferable, especially having gotten used to great modern UIs. Most enemy types and melee combat end up being more annoying and dull than engaging and challenging. Getting around is a chore most of the time, largely thanks to the clumsy map.

Still, I'm glad I managed to experience Fable Anniversary, and I hope to see the remaining installments on PC. In hindsight, playing the game more spread out time-wise could've dampened the effects of the negative points.\t\t\t\t\t
