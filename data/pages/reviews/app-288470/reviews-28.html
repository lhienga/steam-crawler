\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(I played Fable prior to purchasing it on Steam)

Fable's gimmick is being able to play as the "good" or "bad" guy, with a nice scoop of quirkiness. Unfortunately it doesn't really do anything else well. This is a title that you play because you have played it before, not because it's interesting outside of the nostalgia.

Fable was promised to "revolutionize" the RPG scene - which was, at the time, taking on titles like Morrowind. In retrospect it's not very hard to see that such bold claims were a little... unearned. Fable stands as a pretty on-the-rails linear adventure game with a lot of binary RPG options. These options often involve taking one side in a conflict, then clearing the exact same mission with other enemies. NPCs will react to your choices through dialogue and, apparently, adjusting their prices in their shops. There definitely was ambition here, but it ultimately just doesn't really achieve much. The world feels flat and boring, subdivided in sections of diverging linear path.

Outside of aligning yourself with the appropriate end of the karma spectrum NPCs can be interacted with in the most hideously dull way. Your character is equipped with a set of emotes, such as farts (revolutionizing RPGs), a dance and a thumbs up. Some of these the characters will appreciate and react to, improving your relationship with them very slowly. They also react in more meaningful ways like appreciating getting "helped" (certain actions are tagged as such). Since having a vendor "like" you decreases their prices it is a mechanic that is best not overlooked. Unfortunately, like pretty much everything else in Fable, it's really bland and easy to exploit.

The combat is... unpleasant. There is no real state economy outside of health so enemies are mostly just a matter of "do that 1 thing that they can't react to". A bandit? Just block until they strike, then strike back a few times. A troll? Just want behind a piece of geometry and then hit them once they're done throwing a rock. There is no challenge. It's not dynamic in the slightest. The lock-on system is terrible and glue-y.

Fable released in 2004, while Morrowind relased in 2002. In 2006 we got Oblivion. Both Morrowind and Oblivion completely blow Fable out of the water as an RPG. Oblivion especially is just on a completely different plane of technicality with its complicated geometry and radiant AI. There is no reason to play Fable if it isn't for pure nostalgia nowadays.\t\t\t\t\t
