\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one is hard to review 17 years after release. Obviously in 2004 it set a lot of standards for RPGs, but playing it now reveals unfortunate shortcomings.

The most bothersome aspect is the vague, indefinite and uncertain nature of the game – a thing that is synonymous today with every RPG and open world game. It stays fresh for the first two thirds of the game as you gain power and knowledge of the world and story, but inevitably falls flat in the end as the story becomes predictable and the player’s powers become unmatched. There is no challenge.

And after the main story is over there is no reason to keep playing. The experience is over, dead.

Perhaps all RPGs are by their nature unsatisfactory in the end, and you should try to stay in the middle of the experience for as long as possible.\t\t\t\t\t
