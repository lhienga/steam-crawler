\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the infantile humour and dated graphics, I was enjoying this game up until the stupid "hobbe killing contest" quest. Basically, your character and his friendly rival from the adventurers' guild, a young lady named Whisper, have to help a farmer by clearing out all these little monsters called "hobbes" that have invaded his orchard. Since she's your rival, she challenges you to kill more hobbes than her. Apart from the fact that each of these things seems to take about 40 or 50 hits to kill (with no way to see how much health they have left), the game immediately brings up a "quest failed" message once all the hobbes have been defeated but she has killed more of them than you. You then have to either quit the game or reload from an earlier save.

Doesn't matter that you survived and helped the farmer with his hobbe problem, nope, the game just gives you the option to quit or reload at that point.

Totally idiotic game design. I would guess there are other quests like this further in the game where it forces you to either quit or reload if you don't meet some stupid secondary objective, and I don't like having to save scum my way through games like this, so I just uninstalled it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPreface to everything written, this game is nostalgic for me as I played the original on Xbox years ago. This game takes you from a kid with a stick to the strongest Hero ever. The story is decent but the combat and British humor is what makes the game fun. You will feel strong the entire game. The world doesn't have much to do aside from the missions given to you, so once you finish the missions - don't think there's much to do other than walk around and kill enemies.
The alignment system is pretty basic (either kill such a person and get gold or don't and be nice) and the world doesn't change that much despite what the description says. However I would say this game is worth two playthroughs, one for good and one for bad. Get the game on sale, and skip the DLC.
The biggest issue is there isn't a PC port for Fable 2 and Fable 3 is no longer on Steam.\t\t\t\t\t
