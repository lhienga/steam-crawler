\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo Fable is a classic RPG game that came out on xbox and then later got extra content in future versions. All of this and some graphical and UI changes were bundled together in the Fable Anniversary game here on steam. The rest of the Fable series is xbox exclusive.

That being said though I didn't play these games when they first came out. Kinda missed that boat. Now trying to play this game, for the second time, I'm running into some odd design choices. I have run into the scenario three separate times in my current playthrough where accepting a quest was a bad decision. If you choose to accept a quest there is no way to back out of said quest. This is alright, until you choose a quest that will prevent you from proceeding further in the main quest. This games loads the playable game areas into certain boxed regions which are very linear. However if there is an ongoing quest in that region, you must finish the quest and are not allowed to leave the area at all. If you attempt to leave, the game will ask you if you want to return back to the quest or reload a save before the quest. There is no just leaving a quest area and coming back later. You either complete a quest, or reload a save. And hopefully you did save, otherwise you're stuck until you beat that quest. And there are some very nasty and annoying quests in this game.

I am enjoying the general ease of combat and the world setting of Albion. There is a lot of freedom the player is allowed in the form of build, to marry or not, and certain key choices in the story. But some of these design choice just make my head spin. I understand that this is an "older" game. But these are just game design issues that really show how much further the gaming industry has progressed towards more polished experiences and makes this game just that much more harder to play through.

I am writing my (mostly negative) thoughts now as I've had a generally much better time this go around compared to my first. But I've had to take a break from the game since some of these quest designs and enemy encounters have just fully annoyed me. As I get further in the game and if my thoughts change, I'll update this review with an edit.\t\t\t\t\t
