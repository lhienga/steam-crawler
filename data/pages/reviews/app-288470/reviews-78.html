\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished my first play through of this game and I have to say, for someone who is a fan of RPGs this game will stand the test of time. Sure it shows its age quite apparently, but the dialogue is lovably funny and odd in the best kind of way, I haven't laughed at a game so much in awhile (in a good way). And that's really where this game shines in my opinion, in its ability to be vulgar to just the right point. Not to mention character development of being good or evil and the various level up paths were surprisingly fleshed out (there are SO many spells). If you enjoy an old school RPG this one will be a surefire winner for you, and despite it not being a super lengthy game I think it has just the right amount of content to satisfy you at journey's end.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Fable on release in 2004. At the time, I found it to be an enjoyable enough game, but a bit of a letdown after the immense hype and the developers over promising and under delivering on scope. The Anniversary version is mostly the same game, with the Lost Chapters update included and updated graphics.

In 2022, the game doesn't hold up that well. Combat and camera controls feel very dated and clunky, the voice acting is comically weak, and nothing you do feels very consequential. You can be good or evil, be a mage, or a fighter, but it ultimately doesn't change very much about the story or the linear gameplay and closed off environments with narrow paths you have to follow. You can do a bunch of terrible things and then decided eh okay, I'm going to be good now, and well, life goes on.

The graphical overhaul is pretty good, but I experienced a number of crashes when going to the Guild to level up your stats, and the FPS has a way going crazy if you minimize the game and then go back to it again.\t\t\t\t\t
