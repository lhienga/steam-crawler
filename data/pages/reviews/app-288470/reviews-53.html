\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFable Anniversary is basically the original game made available on steam. It is a remake of the Microsoft developed mini RPG just as you remember and loved.

It has a clean cut linear story line with the ability to upgrade your gear and weapons, along with some fun spells but by and large you either play as good or bad, and you change as you. It's a fun game and definitely worth the play through, especially if you never played the original.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was one great trip down memory lane and an experience I truly enjoyed until the very end.

I played the original once many years ago and remember enjoying the story and game quite a bit, but replaying it years later, I better understand why people keep mentioning this is one of the top classic adventure RPG games of all time.

In terms of game play, it's really as easy as it can get. The mechanics are simple, basic, but lots of fun nonetheless. Overall, very little is complicated about the game. Obtain quest, hack and slash targets, go to this location, gain gold and XP to buy items, level up and get stronger. There are still challenges to overcome, especially if you're achievement hunting, but figuring out how to deal with stronger enemies is as simple as experimenting with your arsenal, which is not limited to just playing one style only, the game actually encourages you to mix things up or even upgrading everything to be a full well-rounded character.

The story is your typical hero fantasy of becoming the best hero there ever was, whether it be a kind-hearted, charitable, protecting the underdog good kind of hero, or lust for great power, killing everything that stands in your way kind of villain, the choice is yours and both sides offer there stories, along with special suits and powers (at the very least looks wise). My first attempt at Anniversary, I played evil, but to be honest I got bored, because things just felt too easy and pointless. I forgot about the game for years, came back and made a new character playing good and enjoyed the whole experience, but..... still committed some evil along the way. Gotta get those achievements you know.

The game is not without its faults though and although it's an old game, there are a couple of things that made zero sense to me. First off, items... they are set in their locations and some of them are pretty useless by the time you reach them, making me wonder if they were just for gold sake or what, The Harbinger is a great example of this, but the worst was Avenger, which for all it's afford in obtaining is an absolutely useless weapon. You definitely want to have light weapons over heavy. Heavy weapons deal the most damage and the Bereaver is the most powerful, but since they're slow, you'll sometimes get demolished by fast attacking units like minions or high level bandits. You won't block in time and neither will you dodge quickly enough, you just have to take the hit and hope they stop for you to retaliate, which is uber frustrating.

In the end, I highly recommend playing this game. It's loads of fun to play and you'll spend hours doing quests, leveling up and completing the game.\t\t\t\t\t
