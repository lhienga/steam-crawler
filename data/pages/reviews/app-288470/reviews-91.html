\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemember when games were made to entertain you? To be fun? Remember when they were made for the sake of enjoyment? (peperidgefarms.jpg) If you've forgotten, this is a masterful time-capsule to such a time.

The original Fable was the last game I ever preordered, stood in line for my physical copy outside of EB Games back in the day only to get home and discover what a pathological liar Peter Molyneux was (is). Angry at the time, I still appreciated the sheer amount of fun the game was and played it for hours on end.

Decades later (f__k I'm old now) this game is still a masterpiece in its own right. I now have a greater appreciation for what it is, and not disappointment over what some a-hole claimed it would be.

Per the original design: to make the player feel like a mythological hero (or villain) of some grim's fairy-tale like world that fully owns its tongue in cheek setting, it's close to perfect. This remaster looks great, plays flawlessly, and feels better than the original (they improved the camera). Yes, I could complain about the menus but if you're playing with a controller (as you absolutely should) it was quick enough to learn and flowed just fine.

Do I wish there was a bit more of it? Yes. As I did decades ago. Does it still have some of it's half baked mechanics like lumps of flour in your otherwise perfect sweet-roll? Sure does and that's some of the charm: elements put in for the sake of fun. Does it hold up to contemporary games? Well no, and that's why it's great. Fable Anniversary preserves the fun and magic of the original, the whimsy, the silliness, the story where you always knew you were going to be the Hero (or villain) and that's what makes it special and a piece of gaming history.

If that's not for you, there are plenty of other games out there you can waste countless hours of your existence grinding, farming, and using as a contrived yardstick for your social worth as a "gamer."

Now... where's my Fable 4 already?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnless you are person who played this when you were 12 years old and want to relive that, this game is outdated trash.

* The worst control scheme ever in a first/third person game. Just try using spells and moving at the same time. WASD but to use spells you have to hold down left shift.
* Terrible movement controls, sluggish and imprecise.
* Really bad camera issues. The frequent use of really small play areas that cause the camera to zoom vary wildly if you back into any edge.
* The most loading screens I have ever seen in any game. Tiny locations, walk 100 feet, load a new zone, stare at same loading screen for 30-40 seconds. In some quests it is even worse, more from one room to another, loading screen time. In 15 minutes of game time, half of that is taken up by loading screens.
* On my PC various parts of the UI just don't display. Images on information cards that come up when you acquire something is sometimes blank, and sometimes has a picture. The sneak UI meter does not always display.
* The absolute worst thing about this game and what should be deal breaker for anyone but a fanatic is it crashes all over the place on Win 10. It was released in 2004 and in 2013 the engine and textures were upgraded, but the base code is still from the Windows 98 era which rarely run flawlessly on Win 10.\t\t\t\t\t
