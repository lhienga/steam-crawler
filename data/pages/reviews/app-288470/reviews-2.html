\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid 7/10 game from the mid 00s, Fable Anniversary is a run little hub based RPG where you get to run around being a Hero, whether Good or Evil (or even both), and solve all the world's problems.

Melee combat is fairly basic attack and block, with a build up to a block breaking heavy attack. The spells are what makes the combat interesting, as you can enhance your melee and ranged attacks to trigger multiple times, support yourself with several types of summons, slow time, flank an enemy, or just be a wizard and blow everything up. You're encouraged to combine all of the types of combat, which can flow together rather well.

The world is cozy and fun to be in for the short time that the game runs (about 25 hours for 100% for me). Several distinct towns with houses to purchase, pub games to play, and shops to visit. It's all very basic town life mechanics compared to today, but there is fun in the simplicity and how you can experience it all in a quick whirlwind theme park ride.

Overall it's a fun little blast from the past. It's good to go see, live in, and make your mark on the world of Albion for a short time for a visit back to the 00s.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFable Anniversary: A Nostalgic Revamp
Fable Anniversary is a 2014 remaster of the original Fable, a popular action RPG that originally released in 2004 for the Xbox. Let's see if this HD remake captures the magic of the original or falls flat.

Pros:

Enhanced Visuals and Audio: The most notable improvement in Anniversary is the graphical overhaul. The world of Albion is brought to life with stunning HD visuals, improved character models, and more detailed environments. The soundtrack also gets a refresh, with a remastered version of the original Fable's whimsical score.
The Core Fable Experience: Fable Anniversary retains the core gameplay and story that made the original so beloved. You'll still travel the land of Albion, completing quests, making moral choices that affect your character's appearance and alignment, and battling fantastical creatures.
Improved Controls: The controls are slightly tweaked to feel more modern and responsive compared to the original Xbox version.
Cons:

Limited Gameplay Changes: While the visuals are improved, the core gameplay remains largely unchanged. Some might find the combat mechanics and quest design a bit dated compared to modern RPGs.
Story Can Be Predictable: The story, while charming, can be predictable at times. The characters, while memorable, might not have the depth of more modern RPGs.
Technical Issues: Some players have reported technical issues with the remaster, such as frame rate drops and glitches.
Overall, Fable Anniversary is a nice nostalgia trip for fans of the original Fable. The HD visuals and remastered soundtrack bring the world of Albion to life in a new way. However, the core gameplay mechanics haven't aged as well, and the story might feel predictable to newcomers. If you're looking for a charming and visually-appealing RPG with a touch of whimsy, Fable Anniversary is worth considering. But if you're expecting a groundbreaking remaster with completely revamped gameplay, you might be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNostalgia is a huge drive for me in recommending this game.

This may sound cruel after all the work that was done to upscale the visuals and improve the character/creature models, but I kind of wish there was an option to switch back to the old-style graphics. I preferred the 'shinier' armour and weapons, what can I say?

In any case, if you want to play or replay the best Fable in the franchise, with the most interesting magic system and most satisfying combat, the Anniversary edition has you covered... Mostly.

What I still love about the game is how detailed each area is, despite being restricted to fixed paths through much of the world. The soundtrack (composed by Danny Elfman) fits beautifully. Every new region you explore has its own personality, as well as secrets to uncover with your trusty spade and fishing rod. You can build pretty much any kind of hero/villain you want to, or turn the knobs up to maximum on every purchasable ability and stat in the game.

All told, this is a remake that respects the gameplay and structure of the original. There's a fresh coat of paint and a few cheeky extras (weapons, outfits and the like). It's a no-brainer for all the fans who sunk a ton of hours into it on Xbox or on PC back in the day, and wouldn't mind running through Albion's charming, weird and distinctly British-humoured fantasy world all over again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFable emerges as a divisive title within the action RPG genre, marred significantly by its uninspired lore, blatant fourth wall breaches that disrupt immersion, and merely passable combat mechanics. The game's attempt at self-awareness, far from endearing, serves as a constant reminder of its artificiality, effectively severing any potential for immersion and leaving players acutely aware of their disconnection from the fantasy realm it seeks to portray. Coupled with a combat system that does little to stand out and a story that treads the worn path of generic narratives, Fable struggles to carve a distinctive identity. Its sole redeeming feature may lie in its nostalgia factor for those who have fond memories of playing it during their earlier gaming days. However, this nostalgia is a double-edged sword, offering a fleeting veil over the game's shortcomings and failing to render it compelling for newcomers or those seeking a rich, engaging fantasy experience. Thus, while Fable may hold a special place in the hearts of some, it ultimately falls short of delivering a captivating or immersive adventure in the eyes of many.\t\t\t\t\t
