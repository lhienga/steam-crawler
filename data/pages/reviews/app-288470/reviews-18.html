\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: I adore Fable and have beaten the original as well as TLC many, many times on xbox and PC.

Meh remaster. The graphics don't look *anywhere* near as good as in the screenshots even when standing still, and when the player is moving it really only feels like the lighting and post-processing effects like color grading and saturation are noticeably distinct from The Lost Chapters, and whether that's a good thing depends on the location in the game and your particular preference (of note is that some spells somehow look worse than their original counterparts). Even on max settings, many of the textures are very low quality and some of the new models change much of the original artistic style so whether they can be considered an improvement is controversial at best with many character models being technically superior but artistically inferior. This is even more jarring given that all of the animations are copy-pasted straight from TLC without any care paid whatsoever (which can lead to some weird stretching clothing textures when doing emotes).

It should be mentioned that none of the in-game art has been redone to suit the new models or graphics in general. For example, the Bowerstone barbershop has portraits all over the walls that use the Hero's likeness and in this remaster the Hero still resembles his original version. Another instance of this is seen in every single one of the clips that plays when selecting a given upgrade in that each of these clips is a very low resolution reused copy of the original with the original's graphics but like, compressed and grainy. While I personally enjoy TLC's art style more, it still feels jarring to be playing a "remaster" and be constantly confronted with relatively low quality versions of actually decent quality assets from the release of the original game.

Aside from the graphics themselves, the start/back menu UI has been heavily altered and it feels like sort of an arbitrary change given that the two biggest issues with the original Fable menu system, in my view, is that it's split between those two buttons instead of being condensed into a single menu and that some of the submenus get extremely dense later on without proper sorting. This game still retains the two separate menus but heavily alters the UI in a way that actually sort of makes them 'harder' to use by being a nested menu nightmare that involves using face buttons, dpad, shoulders, and triggers to navigate the back menu while the start menu is actually pretty straightforward and good. Of note is that Hero save has been removed and has been replaced by checkpointing at every level transition and the player is allowed to save freely (this makes some boasts 'incredibly' easy). Overall, the back menu ends up feeling very clunky later on when juggling a bunch of stuff because you're still doing a lot of scrolling along with needing to use the whole controller just to change a few things; the start menu, by contrast, feels a little more faithful and is much, much, much easier to navigate.

There isn't much to say about the gameplay as, with the animations being ported straight over, literally nothing feels noticeably different other than the difference in the spells' visual quality. That said, a new difficulty called "Hero Mode" was added which adjusted the game balance to be slightly more challenging for those that want it.

All this is to say, this is a 'fine' remaster if you can get it on a steep sale and the native controller support works great if you set it to "traditional", but if I had to recommend a version of Fable 1 for newcomers to it then I would say to just stick with The Lost Chapters. It doesn't go on sale as often but is only $10 and has a much more vibrant and charming art style and a better menu system. Otherwise there isn't enough of a difference to even warrant the release of this game since it, at least in my opinion, looks holistically inferior and less cohesive than the original.

So yeah, if you wanna play Fable, then I'd stick with TLC. This was *not* a bad experience, but the entire time I was playing it I wished I was just playing TLC instead.

EDIT: I forgot to mention that there doesn't appear to be any new content other than a Minecraft-inspired helmet in one of the shops. If you don't purchase any of the DLC (and I'd recommend that you don't) then there is literally nothing that this remaster adds to the original experience other than a new difficulty option. Not even a Jack of Blades voice toggle if you wanted his original voice before they pitched it down just for nostalgia's sake (I'm not entirely serious about wanting that but wouldn't it be kind of cool?). This game just feels incredibly cynical and like it was made strictly to minimize costs while maximizing profits.

EDIT 2: Also they removed the drunk effects which is a tiny detail but it bothered me. The Hero can now drink without ever becoming drunk but still vomits after a short period of time which is hilarious but also definitely not how it was made to be. No more drunken barfights in Bowerstone unfortunately.\t\t\t\t\t
