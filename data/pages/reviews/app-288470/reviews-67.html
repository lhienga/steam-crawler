7/10

The rating given concerns both the original game and the PC port.
Fable is something truly magical, charming, and kind of nostalgic, too, even though I have played it for the first time.
It has some significant flaws, however, and not everything in the game aged well. The PC port, in particular, is way far from perfect.
First of all, even though the game has lots of fun and perhaps groundbreaking features, some of them are rather for show: the relationships don't affect anything much, except for only that one; the economy (gold in general and real estate in particular) is useless unless you need the achievements--all the best stuff is gotten from the quests or chests, and it's not like you need the best of it anyway; the gear system is pretty straightforward and tedious; the leveling may seem diverse at first, but you end up having almost every skill maxed out (if you're careful enough), etc.
Nonetheless, although I would have obviously liked it more if all the features were perfectly polished, I also understand the limits of the production at the time. Fable is fun and great even with what it has to offer.
But here comes the port: clunky mouse support which will have you spend an hour adjusting the settings, an occasional menu/inventory bug that feels like a navigation key was stuck, the barely playable due to the said poor mouse support coin game... Wikipedia says it was in full development for 15 months. Really?\t\t\t\t\t
