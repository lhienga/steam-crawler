\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow...17 years since release of "Lost Chapters" and 8 years since "Anniversary" edition...😁
The same as when initially released, even now Fable can pack 20+ hours of fun.

Visuals, even though aged, remain colorful (like a true fable), followed by decent music.
Combat, although basic, in combination with different spells can make interesting combos.
Main story is nice and pretty straightforward, side quests are fun.
Upgrading your hero is simple, but fun.
Enemies can vary, but honestly, the biggest challenge is near endgame (and you still probably won't have to use resurrection vials)

You can search for epic weapons, buy houses and/or businesses, get married, go fishing and some other minor activities.

Overall, Fable is a little classic, and probably one of the best games former "Lionhead Studios" made (also worth mentioning "Black & White" and "The Movies").

Looking forward to "Fable 4" reaching PC...and in the meantime, will try to find "Fable 3" in digital form for PC...😅\t\t\t\t\t

The DLC is wonderfully kept to joke weapons and armor, adding to more of the lighthearted humor of the world, a shame the really funny ones are in Snowspire late in the game.
The game is overall more stable, and the graphics are a plus to the look and feel of Albion. They did a much better job in Unreal 3 avoiding that awful Fable 2 and 3 bloom, with only the sun being really 'gody' in it's lighting. Another shame that they didn't bother remaking meshes or models... The achievements are a welcome edition to the activities and quests found in the game. If you like Fable TLC and you just want a shinier feel and some included modding tools, wait for it to come on sale. The game delivered on remastering the graphics as they said they would, otherwise I'd hit thumbs down.\t\t\t\t\t
