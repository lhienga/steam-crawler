\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAaaah... Albion. How often do i need to return to you to get my peace at the end of the day...

You are searching for a simple, but fledged out RPG with gameplay worth of like 20-30h? A game that doesn't take itself to serious and has a cool karma system? A game that has a cool character appearence change based on your stat distribution and the path you chose? I could go on and on...

This game has a lot to do and to see... the story is simple but solid (it even has different endings depending on your choices!). It's old, i mean Fable - The lost chapters (the original version) came out in September 2005, nearly 18 years ago, but they still brought out the best of Fable - The lost chapters in this remastered version. They improved a lot on controls, especially fighting controls, they also improved a lot on the graphical site (more critique on that later) and they even improved the UI a bit and made it a bit more streamlined than it was before.

My biggest critique point of this version of the game are some of the graphical improvements. Yes, overall, they really improved on a lot of things... textures (for clothing, buildings etc.), lighting, effects, world assets and so on. So far this sounds good right? Well, yes and no. They changed the color tone of the shading quite a bit, wich sounds a bit nitpicky, i know... but still this gives the game quite a different feeling. I also didn't really mention "character models" as an improvement because well, they look a lot like plastic figures rather than the cartoonish and otherworldly characters of "The Lost Chapters". The fact that they also didn't remake the facial animations also didn't help, wich bugs me out quite a bit. So i still somewhat prefer the graphics of "Fable - The Lost Chapters" over "Fable - Anniversary".

But, i still totally recommend this version, especially for people who haven't played the original, since this version still has a lot of QoL changes that help the game to make the jump into a more modern era.\t\t\t\t\t
