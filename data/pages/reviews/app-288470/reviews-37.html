The only real downside is that it's from back in the day when gaming was still largely seen as exclusively for guys, so you don't have the option of playing a female character- and if you want to max out your stats, there's no way to avoid your character looking like the love child of the Hulk and Hulk Hogan.

On the other hand, it still managed to be pretty progressive for its time, in that gay male NPCs were voiced, and could be married- and from the second game on, you can play a male or female character and have whatever sort of relationship you like. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tA cornerstone in many gamer's introduction to the single player RPG games. While it does suffer from a not skill system that can be difficult to navigate and understand how it applies to interacting with the in game world, it is ultimately rewarding to those with patience. The story is a solid and refreshing experience for those who played it when it came out. Those who are coming to this game after it has been released or after playing its sequels may feel that it has many tropes that have become either staples or well worn components of fantasy RPGs. This anniversary version keeps much of the original while bringing much needed modernization to the game play and graphics. Long time or nostalgic fans will have an appreciation for what this iteration of the game brings and those who are first coming to it will find a endearing but some what rougher, in comparison to modern games of the same genre, classic fantasy RPG.

Returning Fans - 9/10
While a great update to the game there is little improvement to the game. There has been a lot of work bringing this game into modernity but outside of some cosmetic or dlc items there is little in the way of new content. For many, myself included, this is not a deal breaker but it would have been nice to have some additional content that could have connected or set up the following games even if they were simple or small.

New Fans - 8/10
A classic game with a clear old school feel. This is a solid choice in game but without the nostalgia of having playing this when you were younger or being a fan of the later sequels it may be a hard sell. The game itself is competent and makes improvements on what the original and the TLC version brought. If going in with the knowledge that this is a revamped game with much if not all the same bones of the original it will be a enjoyable if not very a comparatively complex RPG system compared to modern games of the same genre to delve into.
