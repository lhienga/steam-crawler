\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI may only have 10 hours when this review goes live, but to be completely fair. I have played this game to death on Xbox, Xbox 360 and PC.

Fable is just such a great game to go back to, especially after all the rough years pre-2022. The nostalgia hits hard and it doesn't let go.
Fable was one of the first ever RPGs that made it mainstream to a larger audience. Sure there was the ES series and marathon, but Fable was crafted for that home console life and it shows.
Sure the combat can be clunky, sure the story is very basic and absolutely the gameplay has aged. But it still PLAYS great. No bugs, no game-breaking quest bugs. Its just a simple hero-romp fest where you, the hero, decide whether or not you set unto the world pure evil, or angelic good. It's very basic in its morality system: Do good things = good boi points. Do bad things = bad boi points.

But I'd be fooling anyone if I said that the "good" playthrough is fun. Nah in fact, I had way more fun doing the opposite. Wanna lure people into a false sense of security? Have them follow the Hero, then lead them to a cult that sacrifices people to their God in order to become younger!
OR even just kill the peaceful villagers. Force their houses onto the market. Hell, buy their old house and rent it out to their children! Isn't capitalism great?

Want to kill your childhood rival? In an arena surrounded by cheering audiences? You can if you want to.
Fable gives you these absolutely ridiculous choices for no reasoning at all. You don't need to weigh the good and the bad. You're never truly locked to one side.

Then later, kill anyone you want!
Hell Marry the evil mayor of bowerstone who clearly is the embodiment of bad!

The game, even though basic. Can be an absolute blast. Especially with what you can get away with. Eventually the Power Fantasy sets in and you can just murder an entire town or hunt baverines and clear graveyards, because who can stop you? You can literally summon power from the gods to strike down your enemies. I don't think a town guard or skeledude is gonna stop you.

10/10 loved my descent from good boi to absolute nightmare of albion and would recommend to anyone\t\t\t\t\t

Odd they would make up a trilogy for Xbox360, but not Steam - when they had a windows release; maybe they would be faced with the decision of having support on iOS or Linux to expand upon their reach in the market, but who am I to speculate.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've attempted to finish this game several times over the years, mostly because a friend of mine says it's one of their favorite games. After finally beating it, I think I can safely say that I simply don't see the appeal beyond nostalgia. With clunky combat, a boring, predictable story and threadbare characters, it's tempting to say that this game hasn't aged well, but I don't think this game even holds up if you compare it to its contemporaries from the early-to-mid 2000s, either. Morrowind has a MUCH stronger open world design, KotOR has a better supporting cast and central narrative and it would be a struggle to adequately list the number of games with better combat and controls than this game. Comparing it to genuine classics might seem unfair, but it honestly feels like I'm being generous even comparing it to them. This game is a mess. Some people find it a charming mess, but the stuff that's truly unique about it (for the time) just isn't that compelling to me.\t\t\t\t\t
