\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played hundreds of hours of the original on Xbox classic, and played Fable Anniversary on Xbox 360. After happily owning this game one PC now, I can definitively say it has been one of my all time favorite games.

The story is simple, but definitely matched well for its' timeless writing and and decently interesting morality system. There are multiple story choices that affect the outcome of the story the player wants to tell, and feels like a really decent hero's journey that most modern games just miss the mark on for its' charm. It is an easy story to follow, and provides an simple sense of direction, while also offering a few avenues for players to be rewarded for sidequesting and exploring.

The gameplay is variable, and not all that hard to learn. You can reliably choose Strength (Melee), Skill (bows), and Will (magic) as a main class, or mix your hero between all 3 for the best overall experience. There's also a variety of unique weapons and spells to pick from, including legendary weapons, time-warping and arcane spells, and even exclusive bows that are exclusive to which abilities the players want to level up. Armors are also pretty interchangeable, and gives a variety of cool (and sometimes comical) choices for armors and clothes for the player to use.

I highly recommend this easy to pick up game for people looking for a simple and classic game to add to the collection.\t\t\t\t\t

I grew up with the likes of OG Diablo and Baldur's Gate. Admittedly Morrowind was my first 3D RPG intro game, but Fable TLC was a game that was a refreshing reprieve from the constants onslaught of tactical thinking and hardcore decision making, but still gave you that feel of a story and atmosphere. The combat is fairly simplistic, but it works and has a level of difficulty of its own. While I absolutely love Fable II and Fable III, Fable The Lost Chapters/Anniversary is my absolute favorite of the series. Comparatively, to other RPGs, the Fable series is fairly simplistic, but as a gamer that has a full time job and kids Albion is a wonderful world to hop into for a good thirty minute to hour span before the kids are calling for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, Fable. The game where you can be a hero, or a total jerk. What's not to love? Here are some reasons why you should play this game:

You can be the hero you've always wanted to be...or not. Want to save the world and be a shining beacon of hope? Go for it. Want to be the most evil, dastardly villain in all the land? You do you, my friend.

The world is your playground. Literally. You can explore a massive open world filled with all sorts of quests, secrets, and treasures. Just be sure to watch out for those pesky bandits.

It's hilarious. Fable doesn't take itself too seriously, and that's part of what makes it so much fun. The dialogue is witty and sarcastic, and the characters are delightfully absurd.

The combat is satisfying. Whether you prefer swords, axes, or magic, Fable has something for you. The combat system is simple enough to pick up quickly, but deep enough to keep you engaged for hours on end.

The moral choices actually matter. Unlike some other games where your choices don't really affect the outcome, Fable's choices have real consequences. Will you be a paragon of virtue or a total monster? The choice is yours, and it will affect how the world sees you.

The music is fantastic. Seriously, the soundtrack for this game is amazing. It perfectly captures the whimsical, fantastical nature of the game.

It's a classic. Fable may be old, but it's still a beloved game that has stood the test of time. Playing it now is like taking a trip back in time to the early days of Xbox.

So what are you waiting for? Pick up a copy of Fable and start your journey to heroism...or villainy.\t\t\t\t\t
