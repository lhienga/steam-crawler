\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort review: one of my favorite games. So much personality and a great sense of humor. I'd give it a 9/10

Longer thoughts: I'll fully disclose that I have some nostalgia goggles for Fable. I played it when it first came out, played it some more when The Lost Chapter expansion came out, and have played it even more since the Anniversary edition was released. It's part of my adolescence and I have a soft spot for it despite some of its dated aspects now.

Firstly, in regards to this particular edition of the game...it's kinda ugly compared to the original. The graphics are supposed to be "improved" but it honestly lost some of the game's story book vibe and replaced it with some out of place attempts at making the characters more realistic. It's not a huge gripe, but I prefer the original style. The DLC weapons are all sort of cool aesthetically, but overpowered when put into use. I'd say skip getting the extra items.

One major thing I like about this edition is it adds difficulty levels. The original Fable had none and was way too easy. The new hard mode disables resurrection potions, and ends the mission if your health bar gets depleted. It's actually challenging that way and a more fun way to play for experienced players.

As for the rest, it's the Fable: The Lost Chapters you remember. Fantastic soundtrack, quirky jokes, delightfully cartoonish sense of good and evil. It's all there and as fun as ever.

The downsides are also still there. Combat is still the game's biggest weakness. The world also feels lifeless as your character is one of the only people who actually ages in this world. It might have been beyond the ambition of the developers, but it feels weird how side characters who were middle-aged when you were a young man are still the same age by the time you are now an old man. It gives the game a weird frozen in time vibe by the end.

Those negatives aren't a huge deal, though. I play Fable for the atmosphere, which is top notch in my opinion. I don't know how many times I've played Fable in my life, but I know I will play it many more as the years go by. This is one of my comfort food games. I don't know if it would hold up great to someone who's never played it before, but I think it's worth it for the art style, music, and humor alone.\t\t\t\t\t
