How I Feel About Fable

"Ooooooh!"
"Aiiiiiiiie!"
"Ooooouaaagh!"

NPCs that are, putting it politely, terminally retarded sound off like Siren's the moment I approach them. People in the streets panic as my fat, mustachioed villain comes traipsing through town. Another slum full of morons to abuse.

The General Store owner panics the moment I set foot into his store and attempts to flee. I lock him in ruthless negotiations, where I buy 43 Emeralds from him. Naturally, he panics that he no longer has any Emeralds, so I generously sell them back to him immediately to remedy this problem. At a profit. The game is working as intended. I love it.

I repeat this as many times as I want. As many times as I deem necessary. I'm now the richest man in Albion, and the shopkeeper is bankrupt. He starts to scream again and repeatedly runs into a wall, stops and pauses, screams, runs back into the wall, ad infinitum. Sometimes he remembers I'm there, and turns to gasp in awe and praise me. Then back to the wall. Always the wall.

Bored, I go across the street to see what the Armourer has, but it might surprise you how quickly "shopping" can become "murder" in Fable. Naturally, the proto-gigachad guards come to apprehend me. I stand over the corpse of the proprietor, a man not murdered 3 seconds past, already purchasing the property from a realtor and renting it out.

Unfortunately, the consequences of all this evil is that you become ugly. In a lot of ways, Fable, the meta-gaming goal of Fable is to be true to who you are, but be the least ugly possible version of you. This means it's okay to beat the homeless man at the beginning of the game: because if you're too good, you lose. But it's the goal you chase. It's what keeps me going. To be the sexiest evil man in Albion, and to me that means remaining unscarred, and attempting to remain out of prison as long as I can. It's a game everybody loses.

But if there's one thing I learned from going to Church: you can buy your way to heaven. I run my ass over to the Temple of Avo and pay 200,000 tax-free gold into their little charity. "Oh my God, look at how huge his virtue is!"
Everyone loves me now. They clap. I get married. The ceremony is at the Temple of Skorm, tucked away in a little place called "Darkwood". We play a game, think "Wheel of Fortune" but with human sacrifice. Still, they clap and cheer. They love me. I can do no wrong. After all, I got Excalibur and I gave the Church 200,000 gold. I'm a saint. God basks me in a heavenly glow and I even have a little Halo above my head. "I am helping."

Fantasy-Satan is an excellent divorce lawyer, and for trading in my wife and a few of Albions annoying citizens, he gives me a bow..that'll I'll never use. But even in fantasy land, divorcing your wife by bringing her to Skorm's altar and playing the sociopathic version of "Wheel of Fortune" is regarded as "immoral". Thankfully, much like the real world, I can simply pay the Church to wipe away my sins, give me a clean slate, and a glowing halo. I *am* a good person.

I absolutely love Fable. It's absurd dichotomy of Good and Evil with truly radical decisions: take a childs doll from her or give it to her, beat a weaker child or defend him, beat a homeless man or defend him, sacrifice a child, or a bandit, or save them. You can be absolutely horrible and still be a saint. I think if you haven't experienced the glory of Fable, you are missing out on something pretty unique for it's time, and still just funny to play today. If this is how you're set on experiencing it, this is a good way to experience it, but I'd really urge you to go for The Lost Chapters instead.

How I feel about Fable: Anniversary

If you look up Phil Spencer, you'll see a man who associates himself with "top shelf content". I see a pale sock of a man who leeches the love out of everything he touches, as his production line spews out uninspired rehashes of beloved stories and destroys their legacy. He represents the era of Microsoft playing it Safe-Forever now, and being to stupid to even understand the magic of their own IPs so even the remasters disrespect them.
