\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to try to finish this game but at the end I could not find the power within myself to do so. There's nothing that is inherently broken within the game but it just feels terrible to play. The story is unmemorable with terrible and sometimes awkward presentation within the cuts-cenes. While I am aware that the "Anniversary Edition" is often seen as a visual downgrade from the original, I can't see the original being much better. There are quite a bit of things here that aged like absolute milk. The game allows you to explore the map but it doesn't really reward you for exploring off the predetermined path that the quests guide you with. I would find myself going into caves seeing what would happen if I reached the end and cleared it, only to find out that I wasn't really supposed to be there yet and the half the time there was a door at the end you couldn't go through without a mission prompt. This happened more times than once.

The combat becomes mindless once you unlock the "Time Slow" and "Double Attack" abilities. You will find yourself spamming dodge more than you would in something like Dark Souls because there is no punishment for doing so other than not being able to attack. I found no real challenge even if I was overrun by sheer numbers due to the fact the game gives you so many "revive potions". While mindless it doesn't change the fact that the game simply doesn't really feel all that good to play. Locking onto enemies can fail you and suddenly lock onto a friendly NPC. You have to hold down L3 to use the sneak mechanic. The menus are awkward to navigate. There are much more nitpicks really.

Cut-scenes are really awkward, having characters not fitting into the frame and having crucial lines delivered so stilted. I hardly cared about the fact that the protagonists village was invaded and burned to the ground because none of the characters were memorable and you're just kind of awkwardly thrown into this world as this kid who who everyone seems to awkwardly talk to in a terribly monotone fashion in the fashion of a Bethesda game aside from the odd moments where someone will yell and call my character "Chicken Chaser!".

I've seen this game throughout the years in passing. I've often heard of it's praises wondering if it was worth its salt, I decided to give the game a try. I believe I am more than halfway through the game and I can't really take much more of it. I can see where people can find it to be groundbreaking with it being the game that "revolutionized moral systems in games" but it really isn't worth playing through today in my opinion. I cannot recommend this game. There are older games that feel infinitely better to play than this.\t\t\t\t\t
