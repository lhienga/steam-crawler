\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and most importantly, bring Fable 2 to PC and bring back Fable 3 too.
If you're wondering which version you'll get: this version has controller support, but the other is more loyal to the traditional look (and resolution).
Fable deserves a good spot on my franchises list. It has witty dialogue, takes itself seriously while undermining itself with countless jokes and 4th wall breaks, a neat world, nice magic system and magic in worldbiulding.
Below I'll keep in mind comparisons specially under assumption you played fable 2 and 3 too, and buying on 75% off. Despite some heavy flaws, the core of the game is amazing and deserves a chance from the community.

Pros:
- Usually good combat;
- Witty lines and very fun overall. Even the achievements are funny;
- Great plot;
- Interesting quests, most of them aren't dull. Some have challenging choices;
- Gives you some solid 15h to clear, plus much more if you're after 100%;
- Armor does affect defense, attraction and scariness, plus moral standing. I really wish they'd kept defense in later Fables, but I can see why it'd "force" players to always use heavier armor;
- Strong brittish accent, for comedy reasons.

Cons:
- Extremely linear world;
- Unchallenging, never died or had to use a revive potion;
- Most of the events are non-repeating events, like the Arena;
- Combat can be very annoying, specially if trolls are around, and even less if there are two of them plus other baddies. Sometimes you're knocked down after being knocked down, but never gets to be a long cycle, at least;
- Moral system is mostly shallow and doesn't have that much of repercussion. I.E. you get choices but not many consequences.
- Limited real-state choices, only a few houses. Furniture is tied to upgrades, you can't place them freely;
- Some quests are bugged, and this edition didn't fix bugs, it just added more;
- Modding system is trash;
- Terrible social interaction menu, and lack of other NPCs profiles. I loved both of those in Fable 2, but here I didn't even bother flirting or whatsoever;
- Very limited selection of weapons;
- Very easy to max out all aspects as you get middle/end game;
- Armors don't have downsides, like heavy armors making you slower or worst to sneak. As far as I know at least, and if it's implemented it's not noted anywhere.

I'd say that, quality-wise, it's a middle ground between Fable 2 and Fable 3, but lacks more features than both. The plot is good, but the magical/mistery world setting is worst than Fable 2.

TL;DR Fable 2 made improvements over almost every aspect of this game, just isn't released on PC yet. Time to check Xenia progress... nope, not ripe yet.\t\t\t\t\t
