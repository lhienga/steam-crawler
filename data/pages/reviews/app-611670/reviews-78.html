-Skyrim is a good game
-VR is amazing
Skyrim VR though?
On one hand, skyrim VR is great. There is no feeling quite like exploring Skyrim in VR, and a classic fantasy open world game like skyrim is PERFECT for VR, standing on top of cliffs and looking down on Whiterun and slaying bandits is amazing in VR.
On the other hand, its kind of a bad VR game. First of all, it is ridiculously blurry for no reason, much like hitman VR. Making VR combat like this should be a crime, and the fact that Bethesda just did the bare minimum and then completely re-released the game is disgusting to me. The only redeeming quality is that Skyrim is Skyrim, which is ironic for a review of a game that isn't just skyrim.
I don't believe that the pros outweigh the cons. Buuuuuut, if your on PC, then you may know of this one mystical, amazing website known as, "Neksoos Mahds," which solves all of the other problems. Sharp eye reshade or whatever it's called, plus higgs, plus planck, plus ordinator, plus some other mods, and you've got yourself the absolute best VR game. If you have an aversion to relying on mods to make up for the downsides of a game, then Skyrim VR isn't for you, but if you don't, SVR has a lot of potential, and it is completely worth every second of modding, and every cent spent on the game itself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf anyone has wondered about if they should get Skyrim VR, i would not suggest it. The fact that the game is not built for VR is painfully obvious.

- Combat

Melee is far worse than playing normally, your strikes have no strength in them, so combat consists of you just slashing at the foe while it slashes back at you. It just isn't fun at all. It lacks all the possibilities and potential that it being an VR game gives. Bethesda didn't even try.

- Movement

Movment in vr feels extremely awkward and clumsy, in combat, you have to run after enemies, alike bowmen and wozards, normally not an issue, but because of the throughly unenjoyable chore that it is in VR, it is infuriating. The lack of buttons available, means that many actions are performed with the same button, which works about as well as you might imagine, it doesn't.

- Interaction with objects and the world

This is perhaps the only good side in playing this game in VR, and it isn't much either. the ability to look around by turning your head is nice and all, but it gets old very quickly, considering how much of an burden the gameplay otherwise is. Interacting with objects is, poor at best, even with some top mods available. Most of the time, when you try to grab an item from a shelf, and place it in your inventory, which is done by putting your hand behind your head, the detection fails and the item drops to the ground like an overglorified rock. INFURIATING. A feature that was supposed to improve gameplay, ends up infinitely worsening it.


- In conclusion

Skyrim in VR is not a game. it is a chore, unpleasant to play, that doesn't provide an experience that surpasses the base game, in any way. I have used several mods during my playtime, most designed to improve the experience, but this doesn't quite work out well, the base game just, is not fit for an enjoyable VR game. 1/5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t TLDR: crappy as-is, awesome with mods, use Wabbajack
This game can either be the worst, most basic VR game ever. Or the best, most immersive VR Experience currently available depending on whether you have mod or not. The modded Skyrim VR is probably the closest we currently have to a full-blown VR RPG game and I highly recommend it. On the other hand, A vanilla, unmodded Skyrim VR is one of the most barebone VR port lazily made by Bethesda to cash in on the VR genre.

If you want to experience this game fully but can't be bothered with spending time building a mod-list, I highly recommend you use Wabbajack, a mod-list installer. It will install a stable mod-list made by the community for you within 30 minutes - an hour or so (even faster if you buy a nexus mod subscription, just buy one month and cancel it afterward) No need to mess around much at all.\t\t\t\t\t
