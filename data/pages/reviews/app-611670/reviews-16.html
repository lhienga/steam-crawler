\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUtterly brilliant VR game, if you have a good headset and system, it's like "being there".

No, not in some "it's game, but you're there, in that same 3D" like it's "Lawnmower Man" from the 90s.

NO, no, no, no. The rendering, the depth perception, the 3D implementation is more like being in a Star Trek TNG Holodeck. It's the "3D graphics" but they look not 10, or 15, or 20, but 100 better, more organic. The detail of the textures comes out incredibly strong, noticing all the bells/whistles, small details, like rivets in the armor, the glossi-ness of the eyes, the remarkable iridescent of soul gems, or any gem you pick up and hold up for a closer look.

It's like being at Disneyland on some cool ride with animatronics, with all the sights/sounds perfectly crafted, but imagine if those Imagination animatronics had RPG-NPC-AI and get up and walk around doing whatever. Crazy good.

Most Skyrim SE mods work great (not all though and not all work at all). Just be sure to check for specific "VR" versions first. If it doesn't exist, then it's a dice-roll if it will work or not. Surprisingly though, many do, but do that due diligence first or your risk losing time when the game tries to load and crashes tons, trying to figure out which mod or mods are causing the problems. Ugh.

You see everything in a "true 3D" like you would see reality. Not some "flat 360-degree IMAX-like" version.
Like I said, it's like you're there.

Good mods are the one that lets you grab/pick up everything (including NPCs) or your gestures to easily interact with NPCs and they react (remember the 6-foot rule, if you grab/bump them too much they will aggro all "WTF BRO!") and the mod that renders your entire body (Skyrim VR just shows your wrists/hands, ugh). So whatever out you put on, you're literally wearing it and can look down at see yourself wearing it and all. Insane.

Enhanced blood and other type mods are cool. It's crazy swinging your weapons to pummel an enemy and seeing your weapon, hands, armor, and all covered in blood lol. Also, crazy to travel to here/there, it's like "oh wow, next I really need to go up the mountain right over there yonder to see the Greybeards?"

It's a whoooole other, different experience than the normal Skyrim game.

Again, it's not "you're there but "inside" and the graphics look the same".

Noooo, the graphics are 100x better, the depth perception and all renders it on such a level no screenshot or video can ever do it justice. They're out there but it looks nothing like that crap, it's 100X better and "real". Extremely immersive. You feel like you are "There-there"!

What a bummer, while I paid full price, the thing goes on sale dropping down from $60 to $15. Press F!!!!

Even so, the entertainment value IMO, the full price is justified.
That said, however, I have a pretty good, modern machine, and the Quest 3 that just came out a month ago which is higher quality than Quest 2 and others. For me, the game runs silky smooth, flawless, NO stutters/lag or anything (I don't link via wifi but to my system via wire so it renders off my system, not so much the VR itself).

If anyone has never tried VR before but loves Skryim and goes to play this with a good setup and decent VR, will crap their pants. I can't imagine going back to play normal Skyrim without itching all "OK but what if THIS was VR?" then seeing how most the mods I use work with VR, so then just go into the VR.

Also fun, the dual-equip "Shock" and go "POWER! UNLIIIMITED POWER!" at some fool that starts crap with me. Literally, lifting my hands for realz and going all dark lord of the Sith on a foe running at me with an axe, and I'm all "Well, I know magic, beeeoch!" and blast them.

The grabbing mod is great too, whereas in the game NPCs can be dumb and go into traps and get rekt, whereas I can walk up and grab them and pull out of danger with one hand, while healing them with my other hand. Then they go "Thank you!".
Incredible. The immersion is just insane.\t\t\t\t\t

Stabilizing it is NOT the same as stabilizing Skyrim SE, as you may find SteamVR itself actually tanks performance itself and need to use OpenComposite, which requires a lot more set up and unfortunately lists Skyrim VR as broken.

I must repeat, this is NOT the charming bugginess of your average Bethesda game, the six hours I apparently spent with the game so far was not spent playing it; I was trying to get it to run without freezing.

I will do my best to get the game to function, as I can't return it at this point, and I'll hopefully get it running and maybe even have fun with it, since I've been looking forward to being able to afford it for months-- but I definitely would not say the game is worth $60. Right now, it isn't even worth the $16 I paid.

Games should be playable as soon as you get them installed. Skyrim VR is not.\t\t\t\t\t
