\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing Half-life Alyx several times I found this game on discount so thought I'd give it a go.

I installed the game and loaded it up with my VR headset.
The Controls, The default settings did not work with my occulus hand grips, only 2 buttons worked and everything was using the trigger to move to the next option and not the A button like everything else does. After searching on google I discovered that it was set up for the Playstation beam controllers. Finally managed to get into the set up part of the game when you decide on what controls you want to use for movement - teleportation or walking. An error occurred and the game went back to the menu screen and I couldn't find a way of getting back into the in-game configuration to select which bits I wanted to use. Back to google, navigated through the menu's and finally found it.
With HL Alyx I used the teleportation method of moment, was intuative, accurate and easy to use. With this game it looked like something out of the 1980's with this horrible curved coloured line to the spot you wanted to move to and it was only for real short distances. Settled for the walking movement.

Finally started the game where you travel down in this horse drawn carriage with other convicts.
The talking between the characters was out of sync with their mouths and went on forever, there was no way to skip this so had to watch it all, your hands are bound so you cannot do anything with them.
After this section it started going with the people getting executed and the dragon appeared.
Oh this looks good, the dragon looks great. Started following the adventure, got my hands cut free and started following the character around the building,
I got handed my sword and started to practice but you could not move your hands to fight, you had to press a button. Your hands hover in front of you like you are playing "paper scissors stone" and that's the best they can do. You cannot cut with the sword you have to press a button. Completely defeats the idea of VR motion detecting hand grips. What is the point??

Kept positive and started running around the room checking out the chests and looting some items. Received several items of armor and got completely confused with the whole inventory system, It was just so confusing and difficult to use, trying to swap items without knowing the basic controls which took me 45 mins to set up in the first place I got completely annoyed with the game and had to take a break.

I had read somewhere that you could install mods to make the game better so looked them up and installed Skyrim Script Extender and Vortex. Opened up the game and there were some changes to the menu's but the controllers had defaulted to the beam type of system and the buttons on the screen did not match the controller buttons. After another 30 mins reading up on controller settings I managed to get things working again.
I installed the mod which improves the world graphics. Installed ok, started the game and was not impressed. Thought it might get better so decided to give it a chance.
Next, there was a mod that gives better hand control and another that shows your full body instead of just your hands. Nothing happened, this mod was at the top of the list, active, no conflicts all set to go. Loaded up the game and it was the same as before. I thought that maybe once we got past the horse/cart intro it would work as you could see your hands. Nothing happened.
I tried a few other mods which had dependencies on other mods for which I had the latest already installed but they kept coming up with incompatibily errors which I had to rectify but went round in circles installing/uninstallign them until I managed to get them all runing together but the end results were a total waste of time.

I first loaded this game up at 10am and by 3pm I had gotten nowhere with the mods, The game play was anoying and I lost total interest in the game and uninstalled it.
If I had paid £60 which was apparently the normal price I would have been very angry, but as I only had to pay £12.50 I didn't mind too much,

I've been playing RPG's for years and know how they are played, I've played half-life alyx 5 times to completion so know how the controls should work.

This game was so basic compared to what else is out there and would not recommend it to anyone.

It could have been so much better if the so-called mods were integrated into the latest release with the option to turn them on/off depending on your hardware by default instead of having to learn through the community boards and google to try and find compatible modules.
The menu system needs a total re-work as it's not easy to find anything.
The in-game control system needs ripping out and starting from fresh with today's technology and not something from 10 years ago. It needs to be able to detect your devices and mould itself around them instead of you having to spend hours trying to figure them out.
It needs to start of basic and introduce things one at a time and give you reasons and methods on how to use the controls and the environment.

If I didn't have any basic knowledge of games and was starting this as a new user I would be lost.
It's basically a 2D game converted to VR with basic controls and as you cannot see the keyboard and use that as a control method it's like playing a mobile phone game with oven-gloves on.

If they had integrated the half-life Alyx system of controls and graphics then this game could have been amazing. As it stands it was the biggest mistake of my life buying this game.

A whole day wasted.\t\t\t\t\t
