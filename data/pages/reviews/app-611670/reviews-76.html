\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStop telling the false story that "it will be the best VR game after mods". It is not.
Skyrim VR in Vanilla is a totally unplayable digital junk so much so that it needs to be questioned if it qualifies to be a game; Skyrim VR with mods barely becomes an averagely playable game - but at what cost?

First, you will have to spend hours, if not days, finding and downloading mods from different sources. You may save a little bit of life here if you modded the original Skyrim before, but modding the VR version adds even more frustration and confusion to the already exhausting chores. You need to filter out the mods that won't work in VR and those only theoretically work but in fact infeasible due to VR limitations, and carefully assemble your curated mod list together only to hope that it runs within the narrow happy zone between minecraft graphics and burning your machine to ashes.

Second, you will have to live with the stutters, screen tears and bugs that are not only annoying, but literally hazardous to your physical health. The very advantage of VR's immersion makes any glitches much more dangerous to overcome. There're too many mod glitches and performance issues that will transform your headset screen to powerpoint slides, a spinning tumble washer, or van gogh's gallery. You're likely to suffer symptoms from motion sickness to literally vision traumas. Welcome to the real Cyberpunk 2077, where your game has a good potential to kill you.

Lastly, you're a master mechanist and craftsman of Skyrim mods, and you're immune to any form of vision and neural disorders - congrats, you now can play your good old Skyrim... in VR. Admit it - there's nothing fancy or unique of Skyrim VR, aside from the VR itself. You play the same content (with much more clunky controls) and the enthusiasm quickly burns out after the initial VR freshness hype fades away.


Is a worth a try? Maybe if you enjoy VR games or basically enjoy the process of scrap engineering.
Is it worh $70, or even on sales? No. It has less quality than an average fan-made VR mod for other games and, being a player who only wants to pay for a fun game out of the box, you should probably invest your money and time elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVanilla game review: Complete garbage. Not worth the money even 99% off. Shame on Bethesda who care so little about Skyrim fans that they continue to shovel a different flavor of the same sh** into their mouths rather than creating a long-overdue sequel in the Elder Scrolls series.

Modded game review: The best single player experience on VR.

If you buy this game, learn how to use NexusMods and Vortex. I currently have around 60 active mods going on this game because the mods that work on Skyrim Special Edition also work on Skyrim VR, for the most part. The result? Many unofficial bug fixes, NPC and player models are improved dramatically, many of the statis objects are complete re-textured in HD, the level-up system is re-worked, you can see your own player model looking down (which is not in vanilla Skyrim VR, mind you), NPCs idle and day-to-day activities are re-worked to be more immersive, more weapons are added, more armor is added, more enemies are added, spells are re-worked, nightime skies are HD and cycle between galxies and northern lights, daytime looks privy to Minecraft HD sun shaders, and so, so, so much more.

The reviews of this game are painfully bad on Steam, but with a little trail and error with modding, you can turn this into one of the coolest, immersive VR experiences out there. This is only thanks to the community of people who love the Elder Scrolls games enough to create so many mods that the game is practically unrecognizable compared to how Bethesda left it.

Buy this game on sale (usually for $20) when the next big sale comes around, install Vortex, watch a few tutorials, and have a blast. It is crazy to me that so many years later, Skyrim can still be a fun game.

Special thanks to the community for making this possible.\t\t\t\t\t
