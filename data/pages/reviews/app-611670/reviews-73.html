\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed only on an Oculus Quest 2 at the time of writing. Your mileage may vary depending on your hardware.

Once again, Bethesda games' modding community proves itself to be better, more imaginative developers than the actual developers. Combining the VRIK, HIGGS, and PLANCK mods (with honorable mentions of Conduit, Weapon Throw VR, and Spell Wheel VR) fundamentally revolutionizes the experience by letting your character and equipment physically interact with the world. Seeing these mods demonstrated were what motivated me to finally take the plunge into VR.

Grab a potion and run to a dark corner before pocketing it. Shake hands with a newly met NPC. Grab someone's shoulder if they start to walk away before you're done talking to them. Chuck a tankard at someone to start a bar fight. Slit a bandit's throat and carry the body away over your shoulder before the others see something wrong. Throw a battleaxe and behead Nazeem from across the street, then use his corpse as a shield from the arrows of disgruntled guards. Suplex a dead dragon to add insult to injury. Play golf with a warhammer and a chaurus egg. These three mods make all of this possible and more, and it's embarrassing that Bethesda didn't develop this functionality on their own.

The only thing I don't like about this game is that we could've had this incredible gameplay experience four years sooner if this IP was in the hands of a more competent studio. I don't even mind the fact that I've now bought Skyrim 2¼ times.\t\t\t\t\t
