\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love Skyrim and I thought ignoring the negative reviews would work. I purchased installed VRIK and HIGGS, the game played and launched fine. I even added more mods up to around 200 mods on my Vortex. The game looked better but even with top end mods the controls feel so dated. A simple menu made the game so annoying since you had to switch constantly. Downloading a mod to prevent the use of menu's really didn't help at all either.

When you move your head there's this weird effect that makes the game unplayable. I had no issues with motion sickness but the game was just clunky. There's probably a way to get this game where you want it. However, after installing and adjusting mods for almost seven hours it wasn't worth it. There's plenty of other VR games that work out of the box and support mods. I understand it's Skyrim, I'm telling you though the mess isn't worth the purchase.

I understand Bethesda leans on the modding community and this is a older VR title. However, this shouldn't have been left behind by the developers and forgotten about. Trust what the others have said in their reviews and ignore this game until Bethesda releases Skyrim for the millionth time for modern VR. No level of mods can save this game, I'd buy normal Skyrim instead and mod that using the Nexus.\t\t\t\t\t

Seriously, I gave this Skyrim VR another shot, but no.

1. There's no weapon impact at all - you can't "feel" the weapon weight and punches feedback. Remember how it works in Balde & Sorcery or Boneworks? Not even close, forget it.

And no, I've spent in this game more than was recorded by steam since the modded version bypass its overlay.\t\t\t\t\t
