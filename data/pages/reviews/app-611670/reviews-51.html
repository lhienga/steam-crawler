\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: I play a heavily modded (but pretty darn stable) version of this game.

There aren't enough good things I can say about SkyrimVR. Once you get the correct mods in place (VRIK, HIGGS, PLANCK, SpellwheelVR), use weather mods (I love Obsidian or Azurite), lighting mods (Lux, Lux Orbis, Lux Via), an ENB, and more, this game becomes the most fulfilling experience imaginable. I have clocked over 1400 hours in this game and do not see myself stopping.

It's difficult to explain exactly why this game does so well once it's fully modded. The controls, while not as simple as Half-Life Alyx or Blade and Sorcery, are extremely customizable. The world lends itself to exploration and wonderment. The quests you can add and the new places you can visit make this game incredibly replayable. Even simple quests you may have done a thousand times on desktop, such as delivering Farengar's frost salts or chopping wood for Hulda, take on a whole new life. And imagine being able to actually slap Nazeem. That alone is worth the price of this game! :D

Real life is stressful, and after a day of streaming, developing, or anything else, I need to unwind. This is the game I do it in. I can live a second life, walk from Lakeview Manor down to Riverwood, camp in the mountains of Eastmarch, or chill in the Winking Skeever in Solitude. I can hunt with Aela, smith with Adrianne, or get into trouble with Erik the Slayer. Or... I can download new places and followers and experience something new.

While it's true that you can do all of this on desktop, doing it in VR, when the game is modded properly, is a whole other experience. It doesn't matter how many monitors or surround sound headphones you use, nothing beats that first time you put on a headset and wake up to Ralof saying "Hey, you. You're finally awake." (Or use Skyrim Unbound and skip the cart ride entirely...).

If you have a headset and you love open world RPGs, you owe to yourself to get this game and mod it. I can even post my mod list here if it will help. ;-)

Welcome to your new obsession. <3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the average gamer, out of the box this game is a mess with rudimentary VR tacked on.

I've actually put a lot of time in to modding Skyrim and Skyrim VR. The modding community for this game is amazing and if you're willing to chase that dragon, then you can potentially get to one of the best VR experiences there is.

Wabberjack is a tool that can help streamline the modding process, but there's still the potential for things to go wrong. And, if you don't want to use one of Wabberjack's lists, you can easily spend way more time modding the game than actually playing it. Mods like VRIK and Higgs really start to make Skyrim in VR sing, but the fact is that a lot of this stuff should have been supported by Bethesda. Despite all the amazing modders putting so much love and attention in to this, it's a massively distributed effort makes coming from outside the company, and so things become fickle. This is especially true when some people in that modding community don't see eye to eye, modders or don't want to support the VR version of Skyrim, or Bethesda does something that breaks modding for VR.

If you're going to buy it, look for a deep discount because you're very likely to put in the work that Bethesda didn't.\t\t\t\t\t
