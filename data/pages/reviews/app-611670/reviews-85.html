\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is fine, but for me, what this really game really showed me was it is not as important to have an immersive VR view and to be able to look around as it is to make the game mechanics and controls work well for VR and make you feel like you are using your body as much as possible to make it feel like you are in the game.

For example, the combat rules are the same as the non-vr version and parrying and attacking are done like flag signals so parrying is a position you hold your weapon. You do not perfrom a good deflection or counter blow specific to the enemy's action and your weapon swing and the collision with your opponent has little to no impact on damage. Also your shield only blocks arrows when held in a specific position so that you cannot block attacks and arrows coming from the side and you must turn first which means you can only defend against the guy in front of you.

I played this game a bit and mostly just toyed around before switching to other smaller VR games with less content and "inferior", but maybe more functional graphics that are simply more fun to actually play.

As I write this now there is an increasing number of "real" VR games in the RPG style that better show what VR can do and how much fun it can be.

Still giving it a thumbs up because it does show VR can work well with a full scale RPG setting and it has all the AAA stuff you can't get from a true VR game so it is a good transition in the early days of VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI give this a recommendation with two GIGANTIC caveats.

Caveat the first: This game NEEDS mods...
You're looking probably at least 5+ just to meet the barest of bones QoL to really make the game shine. Like having an actual avatar instead of being a set of disembodied hands or substantively interacting with the environment. Once you get through those it really starts to shine. It doesn't seem like there is a huge difference between looking at a floating hand vs being able to see your arm but there really is.

Caveat the second: The Price
IMHO this game is NOT worth the asking MSRP of $60, especially with all the tinkering that it needs under the hood. If you are going to buy it my advice is to buy it on sale.

At the time of this writing I think I've spent about as much time fighting with mods as I have playing the game, but I think I've finally hit a decent balance and can stop playing the introduction to the game on loop while trying to make sure things aren't going to explode.

There are a lot of other high fantasy VR games out there but from what I have seen of most of them at best they look like highly polished tech demos. While the Elder Scrolls VR Port certainly shows it's age it's the most complete world. It probably doesn't hurt that I've spent years avoiding the franchise so the experience is also completely new to me.

Even if I feel the need to tap on the Caveat sign one final time.\t\t\t\t\t
