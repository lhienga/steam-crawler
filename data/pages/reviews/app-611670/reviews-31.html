\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended, but with a serious disclaimer: The vanilla experience is one of the laziest VR implementations I've ever played and is nearly unplayable. Every single person at Bethesda involved with this release should be deeply ashamed on a personal and professional level for both the obvious contempt they have for their customers, and for the lack of passion they have for their craft. It's a staggering display of incompetence and apathy.

However, with the current mods available at time of writing this review, the gameplay and VR interactions can be fixed and fully transformed into what it should have been and puts it on par with other "real" VR games, which is nearly a miracle given the vanilla state of things. With the correct overhaul mods (HIGGS, VRIK, PLANCK are the big three, but there are a dozen others that I highly recommend and that all coalesce to further fix the experience) it becomes worth a buy, but only at the deepest possible discount to ensure Bethesda is rewarded as little as possible for what they've done here.

That aside, it's exactly what you think it is. It's Skyrim, but in VR. With the appropriate mods, you will be able to engage in fairly good physical combat, cast spells with gestures, pet dogs, pick up and throw/manipulate items, and so forth. Of course, there are also some great sights to see that look extra impressive or scary in VR. There's fun to be had here as long as you aren't already too burned out on flat Skyrim. As the VR version is built off of Skyrim SE, most SE-compatible mods also seem to work with it (Lorkhan, etc).\t\t\t\t\t
