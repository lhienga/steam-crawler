\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSkyrim VR is an alright VR port of what is overall a good Open World RPG. I feel every gamer should at some point escape Helgen, if for no other reason than because of how iconic the opening of this game is. If you have never played Skyrim, this is a great way to first experience it. You will be blown away by the absurd amount of things to be done in this game, but that's really where the biggest issue lies.

The issue is that Skyrim is an absolutely massive game with hundreds if not thousands of hours of content, and if you try to complete all of it, you will almost certainly get bored of the general feel of the game first. Typically, multiplayer would be a good solution to this boredom, but Skyrim doesn't have multiplayer. You are eternally alone in this game, and unlike other single-player games I've played, I often felt painfully alone when in Skyrim. Fun can certainly be had nonetheless, but it's likely going to decrease exponentially for the longer you play. It's for that reason that I promptly uninstalled the game once I had gotten every achievement.

I do still recommend the game, but not as heavily as I would recommend another single-player story-based VR game such as Budget Cuts or Half-Life: Alyx. Those games left me wanting more, but Skyrim VR does not.\t\t\t\t\t
