\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my first VR game ever ; as someone new to VR, I wanted a familiar world to explore. I played very little without mods and yes it was quite barebones. It is hard to believe a mod was required to just see my player avatar (VRIK). Still, I could see myself enjoying it enough that way for the sale price I found it on. It's a really great feeling to simply *be* in the world.

I would recommend this for Skyrim veterans looking for the most immersive experience possible.

If you want an awesome experience that can continue to grow and change as much as your RAM allows and are willing to put in the effort to learn how to mod, this is a great game for you.

If you are looking for a game to spend more time playing than time learning to mod and design, I would not recommend this. There is no shame in finding something more accessible. Both learning how to mod and endlessly browsing for additional mods can be quite fun, but not everyone has the time for that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnplayable and it's not just the VR:

Here's what happened the first two times I tried playing:
1. The first time the intro cart bounced around so violently that it somehow killed the horse pulling it. It sounds like a joke and something that would not be possible in a professional game, and it shouldn't be. So we were just stuck clipped into a tree and the intro never finished.
2. The second time the horse survived all the way into the town but the cart never stopped bouncing around, so I couldn't leave the cart.

I'm done, there will not be a third attempt. I'm motion sick from riding in a cart that is violently bouncing a meter or more in the air and spinning around 90 degrees or more in two axes.

My computer is much newer than the game, so I'm guessing the physics engine stability was tuned for older computers and that tuning is now broken. But I blame Bethesda for relying on the physics engine to work properly in order for the intro to complete. Bad move.

The other issues I had already read about, but I have clarifications:
1. The controls are a total nightmare of mislabeling, bad mapping, and just plain inconsistent design. The second level of the settings menu can't be accessed with Valve Index Knuckles controllers (there's no button which will activate items at the second level).
2. Their teleport and snap turn are not instant, they have a very noticeable linear (swooping) transition. The purpose of teleport and snap turn is to prevent motion sickness: Having any transition defeats the purpose completely and should never be on by default. I don't know if this can be turned off because the settings menus don't work.
3. You can supposedly install mods to these things, but the mods are not on the Steam Workshop (like they are for Alyx or Cities: Skylines), there is no Steam Workshop for this game. Have fun on the sketchtastic third (fourth?) party site, I will not be joining you.\t\t\t\t\t
