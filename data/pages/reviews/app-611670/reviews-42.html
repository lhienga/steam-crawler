\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome people take the view one should not give Bethesda credit for what Skyrim, especially the VR version, becomes when modded with community content. Fair enough.
I'm considering the fact that this "game", regardless of its faults in the released state, manages to inspire so much passion in so many, enough to drive such talented and vibrant community to create what Skyrim VR is today.
One really needs to experience in VR the current state of Skyrim VR with right mods and visuals to appreciate what it is. Every minute of my Skyrim VR experience is a rollercoaster of the mundane (relaxing at an inn, watching NPCs go about their business, as I sip my ale), awe (at the vista), terror (try venturing out into the woods in the dark), intrigue, and laughter (ok, I laugh easily). No wonder I simply wander where my fancy takes me, simply enjoying and still discovering the world - I have zero intention of ever "finishing" the game.
