\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome of these reviewers are feeling more generous than I am. Sure, you can fix this pile of poop and make it pretty nice(but not bug free) with mods. But the modders deserve that credit. Bethesda does not. This is the laziest imaginable VR port ever. Most of the controls were ported over from Skyrim special edition, other than the hand motion based combat that makes you feel like a little girl hitting a frog with a stick(there are combat mods for this). Same engine was ported over too, only without many of the bug fixes. Instead of adapting the engine and graphics to VR, they simply seriously reduced the detail and the view distance. And I know with an AMD processor and Windows 10 that the game crashes often within five minutes without the unofficial patch. This game is the epitome of why big studios should be hanging their heads in shame. I literally tried to figure out anyway they could have made this any lazier and more uninspired and I am coming up empty.

Buy it at the deepest discount you possibly can. Bethesda does not deserve any compensation for this. I wish I could give my money to the modding community that made this game a very worthwhile experience instead(I did buy a nexus mods subscription). But word of warning, by the time you have it modded to be a good experience, the system requirements increase exponentially. I have had to do a fair bit of optimization on my system in order to get this all running smoothly. But it is now the best open world game on VR currently. And aside of mods, go buy the natural locomotion plugin. It gives some exercise and brings many games, including this one, to life.\t\t\t\t\t
