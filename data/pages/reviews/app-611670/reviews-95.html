\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis port is so embarrassingly half-hearted I'll just move on to reviewing the state of modding it into something better as of January 2022:

The vast majority of SE mods work. USSEP notably doesn't work right, and the author's a petty manchild who hides all the compatible older versions and roams the Internet issuing takedowns on any reuploads for spite. There are clumsy workarounds for this. There are VR versions of SkyUI, SKSE, and ENB. SKSE mods work if they don't contain DLLs or have been specifically compiled for VR. VRIK gives you a body and holsters, HIGGS lets you interact with physics objects realistically with your hands. Spell Wheel VR reduces some of the dependence on jumping in and out of menus constantly. There are VR-specific sharpening and performance mods to make it run better.

There's currently no solution for the port's worst aspect, the melee combat. Combat in SkyrimVR amounts to standing five feet away from your opponent and waving your sword up and down repeatedly in the air over a certain velocity to register attacks because this roughly maps to how it worked in the original game. There is no physicality to it and it's not clear there's any path for the modding community to fix this. Don't expect Blade & Sorcery (or any other purposefully designed VR melee fighting game)

There's also not yet any mods for physical doors, containers, or activators (as in, levers/buttons/pull chains, etc). You stand in front of them and press a button like you would in the desktop version.

It's still pretty far away from the finish line but there have been some heroic efforts to turn Skyrim into an actual VR game. Bethesda can claim responsibility for about 20% of them.

Also if you haven't read between the lines yet, don't bother with this version if you're not going to mod it; the vanilla experience is disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t
