The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim VR is a lot of fun, but quite a lazy port from the base game to VR. With no hand interactions and just simple controller I/O from the base game, one could wonder if this game could simply have been released as a DLC of the base game, instead of a whole new release. Mods are the saviour of this game though, as with them you can make it a true VR experience.
The Story is exactly the same as the base game, with no changes whatsoever. Some of the perspectives have changes, you can look around while interacting with characters, but that is about it.
The Gameplay receives the most changes. The inventory now is managed through a fly-in menu that changes position based on which way you are looking when it was opened. Weapons can be swung much quicker than they should be, magic is fun and really good with the destruction school, and bows are made much more manual, requiring the loading of arrows. Bows clearly received the most attention with the VR port, with magic receiving the least, as not much needed changing. Melee weapons are weird, as clearly some work was done but dual handed weapons are clearly much better than single handed, and can actually be swung at a similar rate to single handed.
Overall, The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim VR is a fun port of Skyrim, but ultimately is a very lazy port. Mods can make it better, but essentially its the same Skyrim we know and love. It could have been so much more, or could have just been bundled as a DLC, but is overpriced in its current state.
The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim VR gets a 6/10.
