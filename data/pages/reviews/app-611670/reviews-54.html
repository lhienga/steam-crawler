\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a fan of Skyrim, but i am not a fan boy. That said Skyrim VR should have been the version that was released first. Yes, we all know, Skyrim has bugs, a lot of them and to get them all fixed, you need to spend a lot of time on modding the game. Yes, Skyrim has mod support, but if you are not willing to learn how to mod this game correctly, you can quickly have a very bad time with constant crashes or end up in state of the game where it becomes so unstable that you can't finish it.

But once you learned how to mod the game, what to do with loose files, fix incompatibilities between mods, write your own patches, solve conflicts etc. mods can and will transform your experience with the game. It can change the game from a great game to something outstanding. The VR version is no different.

And what comes on top of everything, is that you are now "in" in the game. You can become part of the story and it is darn immersive when you can bend a nose or two when you are asked if you go to the cloud district often. I can't answer the question if it is a good game. All i can answer in this instance is that it is Skyrim. Play it, enjoy it. I certainly do as i also do when i look under the hood.\t\t\t\t\t
