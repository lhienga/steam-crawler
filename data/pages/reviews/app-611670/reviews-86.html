\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA mostly barebones VR version of Skyrim that was quickly abandoned by the developers.

I'm hesitant to mark this as a positive review, as the game out of the box is just not worth it. You need mods to make it worthwhile.

The mechanics of the game are largely unchanged from the regular edition of Skyrim, with the addition of 3D projected menus and motion controller supported combat. Everything else remains mostly unchanged, including menu controls and minigames and all the tedium that brings.
The VR version was quickly abandoned after release, ceasing official support and updates. As such the engine the VR version is running on has lagged several years behind and is missing many new features that have since become exclusive to Skyrim: Special Edition, such as the Creation Club, along with the ESL (light) flag for plugins. This means the VR version has a hard cap of 255 plugins activated at a time, severely limiting how many mods you can have installed at once.


The only reason I'm leaving this as a positive review is because I find myself genuinely enjoying playing in VR with mods more than the flat-screen version (barring physical and technical limitations), the game feels so much more immersive and engrossing to play in VR.

Something as simple as being able to physically grab and pick up something in your hand, or wielding two-handed weapons with both hands (vanilla you use two-handed weapons with one hand) is missing from the vanilla experience and only accomplished through mods. (HIGGS)


I won't go into specifics about what mods to install, there are many guides and modlists available that can assist with this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Game itself: 10/10
It's a classic, pumped well over 1000 hours into it.

The VR Port of this 0/10.
You literally cannot even get passed the introduction cutscene 99% of the time because of how horribly ported it is. There's no way they actually QC'd or playtested this before just releasing it as you literally can't even start playing it without hitting a brick wall. For this alone I have to give it a negative review. That's not even bringing up the graphics issues.

The Modding Community: 10/10
The modding community has gone above and beyond making this from completely unplayable to not only playable actually fun and immersive. It's hard and a roundabout way to install the mods, but it was worth the couple hours i put into it to make it work. They're the only reason I haven't demanded a refund.\t\t\t\t\t
