\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisappointing cash-grab attempt to port Skyrim to VR. The game totally sucks without mods. Even with mods, the feel of combat and game world interaction doesn't come close to similar genre VR games like Blade and Sorcery. Until a better fantasy VR RPG comes out, I guess this is this best we have.

Cons
- Hands don't interact with environment (NPCs, items, anything)
- Missing body/arms means you can't easily see what you have equipped
- Melee combat feels much less intuitive. There's no clear indicator for when you're staggered, blocking is more cumbersome, and range is reduced for two handed weapons.
- the data graphics are even more apparent when you view the game from VR

Mixed
- With mods, things like magic and archery become much more fun - though melee still doesn't work well

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll say up-front that this is not a good VR game, in the state it comes. That being said, with a few mods, this becomes one of my favorite titles in VR. Without mods, I wouldn't bother. If you haven't modded Bethesda games before, this is where to start, because it's totally necessary to make the experience worthwhile. There are plenty of videos on it but I'd say the minimum are 3d sound, Vrik's player avatar, PLANCK and Higg's enhanced VR interaction. The list from there on depends on the person, but this is what I'd say is mandatory for it to feel like a real VR title and not a shoddy port.

The biggest positive of this release, mods or not, is the immersion of seeing Skyrim again in a VR perspective. Every big setpiece, every interesting location becomes amazing in VR. You'll find yourself looking around and generally looking up more, making memorable events like exploring the flourescent otherworld of Blackreach or the climb to High Hrothgar a much more profound experience. That's where the benefit of the base game in VR ends though. You'll need mods for it to feel like a VR release instead of an obvious port where the headset is the only benefit and your VR controllers are are poor stand-ins for a keyboard (the controls for which you can't seem to change, although I believe some mods might be around that remap the keys).

Now for the negatives (even with what mods I have). Some of the controls are pretty bad. The shouting key is mapped to what would ordinarily be the dominant hand's "grab" button - very easy to accidentally hit. Save often because apart from random crashes, you will be accidentally fus-ro-dahing shopkeepers and villagers more than you will your enemies, pissing off numerous guards. As for combat, regular melee combat isn't very fun in VR in my opinion. Some of the physics additions in PLANCK make weapons feel more weighty and realistic, but you're really just flailing your weapon at them - it's more affective to "bonk bonk bonk" hit them as fast as possible than pretend to swing a sword like a sword. Magic is much more satisfying as well has its own share of mods that add a lot of depth if desired. Beyond that, for me being able to aim the Dawnguard crossbow one-handed in VR is incredibly satisfying and archery itself is much more interesting in VR.

Overall, this is a terrible VR game without mods, and a great experience with mods. Skyrim is still Skyrim, for what it's worth (I personally don't feel the game or quests are anywhere nearly as memorable as Oblivion, and I probably wouldn't have played it again if it didn't get this VR release). That being said, being able to experience it from a VR perspective completely changes the game. You will notice details you hadn't before, discovering a room in a dungeon of slain explorers starts to actually look ominous and set some tension, and surprises like Dwarven spiders popping out of vents become borderline jumpscares. It's worth it, but only with a few mods that make a night-and-day difference.\t\t\t\t\t
