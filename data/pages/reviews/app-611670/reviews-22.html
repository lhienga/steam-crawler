\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, silly first impressions:

1. As a not-very-tall person of 5'5", I feel like I'm either the height of a 10 year old kid, or I'm floating 30 cm above the ground. Everyone in this world is like 2 metres tall. It's pretty sad for people like me :(

2. I swear the person who came up with the default control scheme for this game used a random generator to assign controls. Whoever decided that pressing forward on your right controller would make you jump instead of pressing A, grabbing made you go back, instead of pressing B; B and Y took you left and right in menus, instead of pressing the trigger buttons; and both rotating your left hand and rotating the hat switch made you change the direction you were walking, causing you to walk like a drunkard everywhere... must have been taking some serious drugs that day. I'm sure this can be fixed in settings, gad damn.


Next up, why I don't recommend this game:

This really should be a feature or a DLC rather than a whole new game. It's a pretty basic port where even most mods for the original Skyrim still work with it. Definitely not worth the full price at least.
It'a also annoying because I can't play with the accounts I have on my regular Skyrim in VR when I want. I either have to make an account that's 100% VR or 100% desktop. VR tends to be an effort to set up and I'd rather play desktop on some days, and sometimes I want to have an immersive experience and I play in VR mode. Having these as completely separate games prevents me from doing that with one playthrough. It's quite disappointing.
There's also the fact that Skyrim was originally meant to be played on desktop, and there's a lot of things in VR that don't work very well, such as weapon handling (how-fast-can-you-move-your-wrist-back-and-forth weapon speeds) plus some other bugs that come with this being a very basic port.
If you have a VR headset and you're wondering whether you should buy this version of the desktop version, I'll send you to the desktop version without second thoughts. If you already have Skyrim and you want a new experience with the game in VR, yeah, sure you can get this if you want, if you don't mind that you'll have to start over with a new playthrough, and the game is still basically exactly the same as the desktop version, only a bit worse and in VR, and it costs extra money.\t\t\t\t\t
