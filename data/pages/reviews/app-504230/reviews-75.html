\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt didn't blow me away like it did for some people but if you like twitchy platformers you can't really go wrong here. The art and music and story are all nice. Fans of animated children's movies on Twitter will be happy to learn that this game contains several realistic depictions of a panic attack.

The level design is pretty fun although it never really gets too hard until the C-sides and Chapter 9. The latter is the hardest platforming level I've played in my life, though requiring you learn speedrun tricks to beat it is a bit frustrating (I eventually got the hang of it by the end and then felt like a total badass finishing the last few screens, but the middle of the level was a huge bottleneck for me). If I have one complaint, it's the dash controls. This is partially on me because I can't use a D-pad to save my life and used analog sticks the entire time, but I'm pretty sure at least 30% of my deaths in the later levels were because the dash direction came out wrong. Honestly I think the game would have felt a lot better if it wasn't locked to 8 directions; this is literally programmed into the game too, but you don't unlock it as an option until you beat all C-sides, so it's pointless.

Lastly, if you complain about the main character being trans you're a loser.\t\t\t\t\t
