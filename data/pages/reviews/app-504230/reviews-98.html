Celeste is the best 2D platformer ever made.
Instead of continuing to parrot the other fans, I am going to talk about the things you may want to know before playing it yourself:

Difficulty
All chapters from prologue to epilogue are reasonably difficult and there is no section I would consider frustratingly hard. The bonus chapters are admittedly very challenging, but feasible for everyone who came this far. As such I find the difficulty exaggerated and sometimes dramatized in online discourse. Nonetheless, if the game proves inaccessible to you due to its difficulty, you can always turn on the Assist Mode which allows you to modify the game's rules to fit your specific needs. I recommend watching "What Makes Celeste's Assist Mode Special" by Game Maker's Toolkit for a full discussion on that.

Accessibility
In addition to the aforementioned Assist Mode, the options menu offers a Photosensitive Mode that "disables screen flashes and other visual effects that may bother people". Other accessibility options include turning off screen shake, remapping all controller inputs, and changing the Grab Mode from "hold" to "toggle", if holding down the grab button is strenuous. Unfortunately, the game in its current version (v1.4.0.0) is inaccessible to players with color blindness. Those players can make their experience more tolerable by installing the unofficial mod "Extended Variant Mode" that offers additional visual settings like blurring out the background or switching color grades.

Collectables
All hidden collectables in the chapters from prologue to epilogue can be found by actively searching for them and some even require you to solve a puzzle. I recommend resisting the urge to look anything up. However, there are some additional collectables after finishing the main story and you should definitely look those up in my opinion since it is neither realistic to find them nor fun to search for them.

Story
Celeste does feature story elements that are woven into the game, but they can be skipped through the pause menu, if you are just interested in the platforming. I personally enjoyed most of the story moments.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best platformers ever made. A brutal but encouraging game that introduces new mechanics, challenges, characters, and secrets at a dizzying pace. I've never been more happy to have sore thumbs. It's like if Super Meat Boy was made by Tumblr (whether you consider that a positive or a negative depends on how much you need to go to therapy). A colorful, brutal, heart-warming game. You'll probably cry, either from frustration or you finished the story.

Pros:
- A likable cast of characters in a story of overcoming adversity and accepting unpleasant parts of yourself.
- Tight, smooth platforming controls.
- Some truly devilish challenge (I died almost 1,000 times before I rolled credits).
- Ridiculous amount of optional content.

Cons:
- Base campaign runs a tad long, especially at the end.
- Easy to get lost, no map, especially when you're secret hunting.
- Multi-phase boss fights can go on for too long.
- Some mechanics are less fun to play against that others (such as wind and those bouncy clouds).\t\t\t\t\t
