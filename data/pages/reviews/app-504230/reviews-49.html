\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an enjoyable game. The sound design and music are magical as you climb up the mountain and is a mix of upbeat and therapeutic. I genuinely loved the whole hotel and library segments with the follow-up boss (Chapter 3), and I engaged well with the characters and found them quite charming as I enjoyed the narrative throughout until I decided to stop playing. Why? Later parts of the game become frustrating, and to me feels less like climbing a mountain and more of a unnecessary and unsatisfying chore of level design with extremely restrictive and unforgiving platforming where each pixel of movement and precision platforming was life of death. At one point I forgot I was even climbing a "mountain" and thought the developer had just thrown together levels needlessly. It got to a point where I felt it was redundant, and wondered why this precision platforming was even a choice. I enjoy difficult games from time to time as long as it's fair, and platformers here and there, but the punishing and extreme precision aspect of this platformer put me off entirely.

Maybe I will revisit this game in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
