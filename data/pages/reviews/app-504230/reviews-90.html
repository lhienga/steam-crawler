\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of challenge but super fun. There's 7 main chapters with 2 extras after, and each one introduces its own unique element that builds on the previous ones. Rather than being linear, there is a lot to explore and many of the rooms can be completed in multiple ways, which is what makes Celeste a flexible platformer. There are many collectibles to this game, which might be a good thing depending on whether you have OCD or not (I did so I collected everything). The story is also incredible and leaves a compelling message, covering anxiety and overcoming that as you climb the mountain. The graphics and animations are also excellent, and the movement mechanics are super fluid. The best part of this game is that you never stop learning and improving, even after dozens of hours. In the last chapter, the game teaches you advanced movement tricks that make you feel like you're practically flying. After the hours it takes for you to 100% the base game, its only the beginning because there are thousands of mods created by the community that add even more content (easier and harder) for you to play. 10/10 and would forget everything just to experience it again.\t\t\t\t\t
