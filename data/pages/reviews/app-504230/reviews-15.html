\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, I am blown away. There are so many, many good things about this game I could say, I could write an essay on how great it is and how much fun I've had, but, I'll just make it brief and talk just about the story, as if I were to appreciate everything this game has to offer in this one review, it'd look like a book.

Most time when I play a game, I don't pay attention to the story. I'm a gameplay first and story last kind of person. That's why I could never get into storybased games most times. Fortunately, the brillance of this game shines through by giving you both in a way that does not feel forced or impedes the enjoyment of this game, it actually amplifies it! It introduces you to the game first, how it's played, and continuously gets harder and more complex the more you go through it. I was already sold after playing just 30 minutes of it. And because I liked the game so much, I actually cared about the story and never skipped dialogue, and the dialogue is all done very naturally while still being fairly brief and having its themes be easy to understand while not being overbearingly obvious to the point were it feels spoon-fed. It is clear to me that every piece of dialogue was done with clear intent as there is little to no wasted words, making the story engaging, fun, heartbreaking, stressful, enjoyable, and easy to understand. I have rarely found any game which has ever displayed its story this well with the pacing and presentation, as even I, someone who usually does not care that much about story, got invested. That's also partially with the help of the animated sprites that appear next to the text boxes which allow you to not just understand but also feel how each character is feeling in each situation. I could go on and on about how all the art in this game is a treat to look at, but I'd just be wasting your time because you're not playing it yet. Go play this game!! Get to the top of that mountain with Madeline, it's a tough journey but it is absolutely worth it <3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPro tip for key board users: C starts the game, and the best key binds are using the arrow keys to move, shift for grab (don't use pinky), z for jump, x for dash, c for crouch dash.

So, originally I pirated it but I loved it and wanted it and it was deeply on sale so a friend bought it for me. let me tell you how it went with no spoilers.

So, when I was pirating it, i originally started playing it the way everyone does, but my key binds were really bad even though I tried to fix them, so it made the experience unbearable. I originally misunderstood and thought assist mode would make it so I couldn't see the ending or would be barred from getting anything I wanted out of the game, but after looking up what the point was (yes I did spoil things for myself, but not the journey you make), I turned on assist mode because I am climbing my own mountain in real life so to speak, and did NOT need another one. I blasted through to the summit, and I didn't get to try chapter 8 or 9 because I didn't even know what a crystal heart was. Honestly, this is good because i can't wait to actually play them myself. I really love how assist mode doesn't punish you for needing help, and you can either just get some assistance due to it being too hard or because you're disabled and need help, or you can just go full god mode and go get the dialogue. One would think "why not YouTube it?" and I'd say "because even if you use god mode to finish the game, you will still get fulfillment from it as long as you at least try to get the dialogue yourself."

the next day, i watched my friend attempt chapter 1 b side and felt a sort of determination and for some reason thought "I think i can do that". So I streamed to him on a new save to play legit, he gave me better key binds, and I began. I tried so hard to get every strawberry for practice using my new key binds, and after chapter 1, I opened up b side and got farther than he ever had. I have now completed chapter 1 and 2 b sides and I'm now dying on chapter 3 b side as we speak.

I have played everything legit and now only lack chapter 6-9 a sides, 3-9 b sides, and 1-9 c sides, as well as attempting those evil golden strawberries.

What had gone from "this is making me miserable" went to "this makes me feel good, actually". I feel REALLY great, actually. I swear my mental health has improved despite how much i hate chapter 3 with every burning passion on this earth. I feel like I can go accomplish something else in real life because i did this, basically. Like, go accomplish anything. I went into Celeste not a mountain climber, and came out feeling like I can conquer anything.

The game makes you think about a lot of things and you may even realize things about yourself. A lot of people see the story differently. Whether you see it as overcoming depression and anxiety, a transition story, or a DID story, if you connect with it and it helps you, it doesn't matter how you interpret what the intentions of the game were. You come out of it feeling like you can overcome the mental roadblocks that are keeping you from moving forward, and get a hit of dopamine every time you learn how to progress to the next room.

fair warning that the first three days were me not actually retaining what i learned and basically just learning only how to do each room separately, but now I am able to chain the knowledge together and make connections for how previous chapters have slight things that prepare you for up coming gimmicks. Every chapter is different.

The art is so pretty for a pixel art game, the music is great (I like the chapter 4 cassette room's music best, probably! it was so hard to get in there but the music was a hard banger), every time you think you learn how to do something a secret you need to get turns what you learn on its head. Though they reuse their own assets all the time, no two rooms look the same and everything looks like it was done by hand. They Madeline's hair procedural so every movement you make, her hair moves so fluidly. Tiny details in the game can easily be analyzed to find meaning in them to assess what something could mean. Sure, it could be a "the teacher is telling me to explain why this writer said the curtains were blue" situations and sure maybe the curtains are just blue, but they could actually be more than that if you think hard enough about it. At least when you analyze something you actually like it's fun so it doesn't matter.

Celeste has a lot of great messages on how to handle others and yourself that a lot of people have yet to learn even late in life. It's the kind of game that helps clear up static in your mind and make connections that make it possible to actually get out of the rut you're in so you're able to start climbing your own mountain in real life.

If you've never played it before, i hope you do. It's an extremely good game. You can also mod it to get even more content out of it! Probably the most recommended one to try would be Strawberry Jam, but you should finish the base game first. I'm not sure if you should complete chapter 9 before trying that or not because i haven't gotten that far myself, but i'm eager to play it. The mod apparently has even more character development, more plot, and the art is BEAUTIFUL. Really stays true to the vision of Celeste, honestly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a beautifully crafted platformer with very simple controls. I have had it in my library for over a year and finally got around to playing it, and I wish I had sooner. It is easily the most entertaining, frustrating, and downright exhilarating game I have played in years. It is such a unique experience wanting to rip someones head off out of rage whilst chilling to one of the best video game OST's I've ever heard. And even though it's brutally hard, I've pushed myself to climb the mountain simply because I knew I could. After playing this I feel motivated to climb a mountain of responsibilities I have in the real world, and am able to take on my problems one at a time. I look forward to the hundreds of hours of fun that is yet to come from this game. 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
