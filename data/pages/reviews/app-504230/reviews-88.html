\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've always taken a liking to games with tight controls and/or fast pace. VVVVVV, the F-ZERO series, Horizon: Zero Dawn, Super Mario Odyssey. These are all games that I find a joy to play due to their controls. However, none of them hold a candle to Celeste. This is, by leaps and bounds, the best tuned game that I have ever played, bar none. It's jumping, climbing, and dashing--no more, no less. The game takes such a simple concept, and stretches it to its absolute limit. Likewise, it will push *you* to your absolute limit.

The story is simple, non-verbose, but, regardless, thought provoking. It made me question Madeline's actions, but my *own* actions. In some aspects, the game itself feels like a mirror, helping you to find your true self, while also acting as a story element. Essentially, and in parallel, you *and* Madeline grow as a person. It's clear this game's story was crafted with delicate care and love. It's not one you see often. This part of the game has been covered quite extensively, and it goes to show how well made it is. I've had a few games hit me emotionally, but never one that made me think about *myself*. "What am I doing?" was always a question reserved for games that I felt were a waste of time, or for social media doom-scrolling, but it felt like the *game* was asking *me* what I'm doing with my life. In many regards, it was a real wake-up call, a sudden realisation of just how immature I was being towards others and myself. Likewise, this realisation gave me motivation to change. A story, literally, unlike anything else I have experienced. Less is more.

Less is more--level design in this game is completely unrivalled. Every single mechanic serves an important, distinct purpose. There's no filler, no annoyances, no nonsense. It is you, the game, and determination. Again--jump, dash, climb. The sky's the limit for this game's skill ceiling, but I've still decided to attempt to 100% this game, golden berries and all. The level design is, in every sense of the word, perfect. Extreme difficulty, quick respawns and deliberate level design lead to an incredibly gripping hook. I believe most people who play this game will want to do more than just reach the summit. This is truly a fully-realised set of levels.

Lena Raine's score backs this fully-realised world, and helps add to this place that this game is wanting to create. It's inspiring, motivating, and as memorable as a soundtrack can get. There are plenty of motifs, which always adds immense value to a soundtrack, telling a story in of itself. The music develops as you progress through chapters, a subtle effect which adds a great deal to the experience. The B-Sides are also very well done. All in all, I find myself coming back to the OST frequently, both for inspiration for my own work and just because it's such a joy to listen to.

Super Mario 64 is likely the most beloved platformer ever made, the DS version being one of my very first favourite games. VVVVVV is *my* favourite platformer that I've ever played, its impact on me being massive. But, Celeste is the *greatest* platformer of all time. The thought-provoking narrative, excellently designed levels and stellar soundtrack add up to an unmatchable experience. It's a landmark title that should be remembered for as long as gaming lives, and it sets a standard in what games should strive to accomplish. For once, a 10/10 just isn't enough.

I will 100% this game. If I can help it, I want to finish this game, front to back, with zero deaths. And, with how well this game is designed, I know I'll enjoy every moment of it. I could not recommend a game any more than this one.\t\t\t\t\t
