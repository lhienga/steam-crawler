\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt had been a long time since I cried while playing a game like i did with Celeste. I went through a terrible phase in my life not too long ago, where I developed depression and anxiety after my father's passing. About two years passed until I finally "overcame" that sense of emptiness. Well, that's what I thought.

Playing as Madeline, I realized her situation wasn't so different from mine. She thought she had overcome her problems, but in reality, she was just hiding it and deceiving herself. However, upon realizing this, she becomes willing to confront her "demons" and live with them.

In one part of the game, Madeline says, "I'm good at keeping up the appearances, but the truth is I'm barely holding it together." It was at that moment I cried and realized that I hadn't completely overcome depression. I was only hiding it from myself, trying to appear tough, and consequently, deceiving myself as well.

Of course, Celeste's message can be different for everyone, depending on their perspective while playing the game. In summary, Celeste is a game of overcoming challenges, where the "mountain" we climb represents life and its adversities. However, we must always stay focused and never give up, also seeking to understand ourselves along the way so that, in the end, we don't become our own enemy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't know where to begin...
At first, this game was charming, frustrating, and a little fascinating.
Then it was light and fun.
Then it was challenging.
Then it made me want to cry and throw my laptop off a mountain.
But after 100 failed attempts, the taste of success is like wild mountain strawberries.
So I cried for those sweet moments.
Then the game changed, and it blew my mind. It kept blowing my mind until it eventually blew my heart out of my chest, and I realized what this mountain of a game really is. With each new challenge came new skill, and new perseverance. The mountain became so much more than a mountain. I'm literally tearing up as I write this.
This game should be played in schools, churches, prisons, and anywhere a human exists. It is truly a wonder of this modern world, and my new favourite game of any genre, of all time. Believe the hype.
Thank you for opening up this part of my soul.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can see, why people consider it a must play. Rarely have seen such smooth controls, which are simple, but need to be mastered. The leveldesign is extremly well-crafted with clever "recycling" of ideas. Even if it is short, the optional stuff and endurance of the levels makes the game quite long. The checkpoints are in smart places, so you never get frustrated. And yes, this game is hard. Not insanely hard, but manageable. Unless you go to the optional stuff. It´s so much more brutal, than the base game. The soundtrack is tranquil and melancholic. Very relaxing. The story is also solid. Though I think, that there should have been more fleshing out of Madeline and her problems. Don`t get me wrong, the characters are really charming and the message of the game is great, but perhaps more quiet moments as well as metaphorical times would have benefited the story. So I`m not as in love with the game as others, but the game is still amazing. Definitely a game of immense quality and love. If this game helped you to stay motivated, find meaning or is one of your favorites, more power to you!\t\t\t\t\t
