I'm literally fighting myself the entire way.

I've never been into platformer games, especially the ones which can suck your soul out while you're struggling to complete every single screen without losing your last piece of patience. I hate challenges. I can't stand facing difficulties on my way. But the moment I saw a Celeste walkthrough (secretly peeping my boyfriend watching it on Youtube) something changed.

It was a hard period of my life: we moved abroad, I was weak and vulnerable. Every problem I had ever had popped up in front of me like I'd never tried to solve them before. I started playing and honestly I was crying all the way because it hit me TOO HARD.
For me, one of the most catchy points was the feeling of being understood. Yes, that's me, THAT'S HOW I FEEL EVERY FRICKING DAY RIGHT NOW. And at the same time Celeste gives you the opportunity to get over it in such a gentle manner that you inevitably start to believe in yourself not only in terms of the game.

Everyone talks about the psychological side of this game, and there is a reason why it works so well.
Our brain doesn't really care if it faces an obstacle in real life or in a videogame. While playing, your negative feelings won’t go away, but eventually the game leads you to the point where you're not afraid of them anymore.
I was sick of myself and I didn't even believe that I could actually finish this game, but at some point I got the feeling like "WOW. That screen was really hard, but I did it because I didn't give up!". And I didn't give up till the end of the main story, I didn't give up while gathering all the strawberries, I didn't give up finishing all B-sides in this game and WHO KNOWS what comes next!
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3038518298
So, Celeste basically proves to you that when you put your mind and soul into something, you can get it even if you thought that it would be impossible. You can heal in this game and it will give you the belief and courage to heal in real life. You can do much more than you think. And you are MUCH stronger than you think.

As for the technical side of the game: I LOVE it. As a person who's heading towards the scary number ~20000 deaths for the game (without C-sides and Farewell yep that's me) I really appreciate the quick respawn after death. I love the level design and style and last but definitely not least the MUSIC

So, I started playing it as a broken and insecure girl. Now it's been 6 months since the first time I tried it and I feel much better now. It was (and it is) a great support for me through the hard times, so I can't even express my gratitude for the fact this masterpiece exists.

Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
