\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game itself can satisfy you for about 20~ish hours. But the possibilities of the movement engine go much further than the game itself. If you like the game after beating it, and are hungry for more, I recommend modding the game IMMEDIATELY.

Back to the actual game, the story is brilliant, and helps us understand the struggles of not feeling good enough, and of losing someone close to us. The gameplay is smooth, and despite having only 4 actions, can become incredibly complex and interesting. Stuff like Supers, Hypers, Cornerkicks, Cornerboosts, Wavedashes, Wallbounces, Ultras and more are all real movement tech in the game, despite the only actions being Running, Climbing, Jumping and Dashing. Speedrunners can use this tech to drastically speed up their runs, and regular players can enjoy the fluid movement from this tech too.

That's about everything in the main game. But then again, most people that play Celeste for a long time have mostly played Modded Levels.

In my opinion, if you need a good start to Modded Celeste, get the Strawberry Jam Collab. Over 100 levels ranging from about as easy as the main game, to levels that need utmost precision and understanding of the tech that lobby uses. It allows you to slowly increase the difficulty over time as you complete more and more levels, and it is incredibly fun.

Overall, Celeste is my favourite platformer ever made, and I can assure you I will continue to play it for many years.\t\t\t\t\t
