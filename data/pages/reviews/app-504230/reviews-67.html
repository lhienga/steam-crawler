\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game as a fan of 2d platformers because of the precise controls, level design, and a surprisingly touching and heartwarming story. Dying thousands of times didn't really phase me (it did) because most of the time I knew that it was my own fault for dying, however some parts of the game definitely are frustrating, so 100%ing it is not for the faint of heart. The base game has a very good difficulty progression, and the b-sides for the most part offer a good challenge to people playing the game semi-casually, and you can do the c-sides if you're a masochist or have a lot of patience (or you're good at the game). I do have some gripes with some aspects of the level design, a lot of the later game is based off of memorization and the longer rooms for the b-sides and the late game levels can become very discouraging when you sink hours into it. But overall, the movement options and responsiveness of the controls is really good, and the levels are very tightly designed and no concept is ever just randomly thrown at you. its an easy recommendation for me to anyone who likes 2d platformers.\t\t\t\t\t
