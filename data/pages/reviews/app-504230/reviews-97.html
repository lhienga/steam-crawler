\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat story!
Great game mechanics!
Great pixel art and animation!
Great soundtrack!

I am not very good at platformers. I haven't really played/committed to any other games besides Minecraft. Meaning it took dying over 1,000 times on the first chapter for me to feel more accustomed to the controls. I was able to finish base game in 19.6 hours (according to Steam), and I was filled with an incredible sense of accomplishment as well as the satisfaction of "ending" such a great story.

Yes. This is a hard game. Yes, you will die a lot. Yes, you will feel like quitting because you've been stuck on a single screen for hours. Did I mention you will die a lot?

But all of that frustration and struggle, not only heightens your feeling of accomplishment when you DO finish the screen, but it also feeds into Madeline's (Main protagonist you play as) story beautifully. She doesn't NEED to climb this mountain. You don't NEED to finish this game. Why even attempt the climb at all?

I think if you give Celeste a try, you'll find out for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat struggle must it be, not to climb the mountain itself, but to find a good reason behind it? Is the dogged determination focused on the literal mountain or the proverbial one? Madeline made up her mind to climb one or the other or both. As did I. As do most of us. There is a canonical story behind Madeline's struggle and her decision to climb the titular mountain Celeste, but it's the open interpretation outside the official context that allows me to relate to her. None of us go through life with clear skies and sunshine over our heads at all times. A clouded sky and pouring rain will occur eventually and it was during such a period that I played the game Celeste. I greatly enjoyed the difficulty in a gameplay sense, even though I do not deliberately seek out challenging games. It helps that the game only gently punishes you as it sets you right back to the beginning of the screen after a failed attempt. There is no game over screen, no mocking sound and no negative feedback loop in the way of lost points, or lives, or money, or any such thing. You jump, you dash, you fail, you start again. You jump, you dash, you fail, you start again. Over and over again. Thousands of times, until you find your rhythm and overcome the challenge and the next one and the next one. Frustration never set in for me, even after a hundred tries on a single screen. Every slight victory was cathartic just as the story was cathartic for me as a whole.

I saw Madeline struggle with herself and her dark manifested other self, which is very much a part of herself and I knew what this was like. This part of us that scares us and sometimes seems so alien to us that we either ignore it, endure it, or even try to get rid of. Neither of which works at all. On the contrary, it's what set us on this darkened path to begin with. In the end, I believe it's necessary to say: "Hey, it is fine. As scary as some of those thoughts and feelings might be. They are not "a part" of me. They are me. I have to accept it and I will be better of with it." Believe in yourself as a whole human being, with the good and the bad.

So as I was walking through my clouded sky and the rain pelted my body, Celeste did not provide me with an umbrella or a raincoat to shield me from my elements. Instead it gave me the warmth and confidence to weather it all. Together with friends and family, and everyone else who is with me in the rain on our way up our mountain.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasily one of my favorite platformers. I've "100%" the game 3 times (not beating all levels deathless, which is not a steam achievement thankfully) and still love the game. It's super fun to play casually. Even if you aren't great at platformers you'll still find plenty of fun with this game.

If you like difficult platformers this definitely has you covered, and if you're super hardcore there is even an insane modded scene that has absurdly good players. The game also plays super fun as a speedrun and is the only game that has made me want to try speedrunning.

The only gripe I have with the game is that there is no native multiplayer. There is a free mod that allows multiplayer, but its a little laggy.

Overall, fantastic game and I would recommend to everyone. 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
