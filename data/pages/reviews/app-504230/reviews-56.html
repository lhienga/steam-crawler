\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCeleste is a weird game to me, personally. I know a lot of people like to joke around in Steam reviews regarding this game and also like to joke about melodramatic reviews in general. I generally try not to be melodramatic whenever I review a game on Steam. I don't think I've ever reviewed a game on this platform where I've been as sincere as now. So excuse me for my melodrama. I need to talk about something.

I've played through Celeste three times now. Once in 2018 or 2019. Once in 2021 or 2022 and now in 2024. First two times were on the Switch. This is the first time I'm playing it on PC. Somehow, I've learnt something new about myself each and every time I've played through Celeste. There's an almost magical grip that this game has. It's one of only two instances of a game being so personally touching to me that I refuse to interact with its community in any way just so that my love of it remains as pure as it can be. I don't really know if I can put my affection of this game into words. It has resonated with me on a level that I've never found any other game to even remotely come close to and I will always treasure it for that.

The gameplay, music, graphics, everything. It's all stellar and I think anyone could love this game because of that. But the story is what has pierced my heart each and every time I've played through it. If you've never played Celeste, don't read anything more about it and just play it. When I first played through it, I was completely blind and got absolutely devestated by the end. It was like a personal message to me specifically about my own life and what I had to do move forward. It's possible I genuinely own my life to this game.

I know this review might not be read by anyone. I know it's possible this review will be put in the backlog. But in the event that anyone affiliated with the development of this game reads this: Thank you. Thank you so much.\t\t\t\t\t
