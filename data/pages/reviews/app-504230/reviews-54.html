\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first played Celeste in 2018, I just grouped it with other hard as nails 2D platformers that I would never be good enough to ever get far in much less beat. I dropped the game and mostly forgot about it until I started to linger in trans communities when my egg cracked. I assumed it was just the "funny trans game" and didn't think much of it. For the past few months I have been suffering from severe anxiety and depression, the worst I have ever dealt with in my entire life. I was told by a friend to play this game all the way through, they said it would help me. So I did.

I played the game on my Switch (I bought it on steam later for mods), and started to get sucked into it. The writing and story started to really appeal to me, and I started to see parallels between myself and Madeline. The further into the game I got the more every single line of dialog resonated with me. Then came chapter 6. I had never seen a piece of media so perfectly portray my experiences with anxiety and how to work through it. I would consider this one level alone to be life changing to me. After beating the game, it felt like a weight had been lifted off of me.

The entire game is rooted into the central message of anxiety and mental health, even down to the gameplay. It will be hard, you will fail over and over again, but every time you do, you learn something new. You get closer and closer, until you finally do it. I am so glad I didn't get far into the game in 2018, I would not have understood it at the time. My depression and anxiety aren't gone obviously, but this game has helped me see my problems in a new light. No matter what, I can climb my own mountain. This game may become my favorite game of all time.

Also, STOP SAYING TRANS GIRLS CAN DOUBLE JUMP MADELINE HAS A DASH NOT A DOUBLE JUMP!!!!!! TRANS GIRLS CAN DASH!!!!!\t\t\t\t\t
