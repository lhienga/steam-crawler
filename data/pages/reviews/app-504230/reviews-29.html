\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCeleste not only is one of the best platformers to ever come out, but it's also one of the most rewarding games I've played.

The game has excellent pacing. It is able to progress the story, the level difficulty and introduce mechanics at the same time with impressive cohesion. Each level that you complete pushes your skill further, inch by inch, pixel by pixel, you and Madeline progress a little bit farther than before.

The controls are rock solid and very responsive. While difficult, the game is still very forgiving, and you can feel it was designed to help the player instead of punishing them. This makes the journey until the end of the game feel completely fair, and the sense of accomplishment from completing the game come from your own mastery of the controls.

There were times I was frustrated for sure, but the power of overcoming that, and going beyond was my favorite thing about this game.

I truly believe you can have any level of experience to pick this up, and still get a lot of mileage and enjoyment. From people who have never played a hard platformer to veterans of the genre, I could not recommend Celeste enough.\t\t\t\t\t
