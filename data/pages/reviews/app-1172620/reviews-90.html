\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm recommending this game with a massive caveat, which is if you do not want the threat of being ambushed or attacked by every single ship you encounter then this might not be the game for you.

I've been playing since the beta for the game, so I've had a chance to see its progression throughout the years even if I've taken extended breaks at times. They've added a lot of awesome and extremely fun content during its history but I feel that the direction it's gone recently has taken a lot of the magic out of the game. The community for this game used to be cool, I used to love meeting new ships and seeing whether they'd be friend or foe. Nowadays everyone is a foe, you can't trust anyone and everyone is out for your blood. Do not play this game on the High Seas if you have anxiety.

The game is amazingly fun gameplay wise; it's one of the best pirate simulator games on the market. But recent development decisions have encouraged the kinds of players who enjoy ruining other people’s day for kicks. I'm not saying PvP is bad or that the risk shouldn’t be integral to a game about pirates. I haven't shied away from PvP in the past, I even used to seek it out. But when it feels like every ship on the High Seas is out for blood, attacking first and asking questions never, then the game stops being fun and in fact becomes nothing but another source of real-life stress and anxiety.

Safer Seas does exist for those who don't like the idea of PvP, but I feel the implementation is an imperfect solution. As of the writing of this review, gold and reputation is earned at 30% the normal rate (in other words, you lose 70% of the gold and faction rep you could be earning), rep is capped at lvl 40 for all factions, and certain content is simply inaccessible in Safer Seas. So eventually you’ll be forced to decide whether or not having access to full progression and all of the content for Sea of Thieves is worth jumping into the free-for-all arena that is the High Seas. In the end if you're going to play this game and the idea of a constant, looming threat of PvP doesn't sound fun just play Safer Seas, do the story missions, have fun with your friends and do not go anywhere near High Seas.

I've made some truly amazing memories in this game, both with friends and with random strangers, so it’s sad to share how nowadays all of those strangers only seem interested in blowing me and my crew apart. But there was a moment recently that's stuck with me and captures exactly what bothers me about the current player climate. I was playing with another friend who wanted to do the currently active adventure, and while we were still gathering supplies for our sloop while we were still docked at our starting outpost a galleon sailed up to us, parked, then started unleashing all their cannons onto our ship. We tried to explain that we had nothing and that we were only interested in doing the adventure, and one of their crewmates responded with "**** you and your adventure!”\t\t\t\t\t
