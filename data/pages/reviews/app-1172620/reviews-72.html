\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so Sea of thieves is a great game. It has really good graphics, and only minor bugs. I love the combat system, there is a ton of missions and it is also impossible to get an advantage through paying. There are SOOOO many fun things to do, you can do missions for gold or enjoy your time at sea with some tall tales(story).

But there are some bad things that I need to point out. First of all you need friends to play this game. It is so exhausting and boring playing alone, but with friends I always have a great time. It is also difficult to get into the game, it has so many different mechanics, so it is really helpful if you have someone who can explain the game to you because otherwise it'll take a while to understand what you are actually doing. While there are many nice people out there, there are also a good amount of people trying to sink you and take the stuff you've been working for for the last hour. So the game can be very frustrating at times, e.g. when you get spawntrapped by a sweaty crew. I recommend trying to learn the PvP mechanics early and getting into some fights with your crew to improve coordination and mechanical skill to avoid being sunken to easy. You also have to play for long sessions for the best experience, meaning you should plan for at least 2-3 hours when starting a session with your friends.

To sum it up: The game offers you a ton of stuff to do, has a nice graphic and no pay to win at all. But it is very time consuming, you need friends and it can be difficult to learn especially the PvP mechanics. Overall definetly recommend to anyone looking for a fun experience with friends.

A little advice for PvP: Mechanical skill doesn't really matter especially on bigger ships, coordination with your crew is by far the most important thing.

Sea of Thieves invites you to hoist the Jolly Roger, grab your cutlass, and embark on a high-seas escapade. Developed by Rare, this shared-world online adventure game lets you live out your pirate fantasy with other players. But does it deliver the buried treasure or leave you stranded on a deserted island?

The Good:
Open-World Sandbox: Sea of Thieves offers an endless sea of exciting encounters. Whether you’re plundering rival ships, searching for lost vessels, or singing shanties with a pet monkey, the game’s impressive open-world sandbox gives you total freedom.
Immersive Sailing: Adjusting sails to shifting winds, using your compass, and inspecting distant land masses with a telescope—these mundane tasks become the main appeal of gameplay. Syncing musical instruments with your crew during downtime adds to the camaraderie.
Tall Tales: The game features fun and engaging Tall Tales—story-driven quests that unravel mysteries and legends. These quests inject depth into the pirate experience.
The Not-So-Good:
Lack of Progression: While Sea of Thieves resets everything except long-term progression goals each time you log in, the lack of meaningful progression can be frustrating.
Sparse Story: The game lacks a strong narrative. It’s up to players to create their own stories, which can be both liberating and limiting.
Repetitive Tasks: Some activities, like traveling between objectives or methodically searching for items, can become tedious over time.
Final Verdict:
Sea of Thieves is a game best enjoyed with friends. Its chaotic moments, camaraderie during downtime, and immersive sailing make it a unique experience. However, it’s not perfect. If you’re ready to embrace the pirate’s life, grab your grog and set sail—it’s time to become a legendary buccaneer! 🏴‍☠️
