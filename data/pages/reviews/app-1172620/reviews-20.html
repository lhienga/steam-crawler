\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow, I've almost played this game for 200 hours, so here's my truthful and honest review.

First off, let's talk about graphics and textures. I like the cartoony style Sea Of Thieves has going on; it's not overly detailed but still not overly simplistic either. Even though textures are quite simple, it doesn't mean the graphics aren't impressive. If you're in the right spot at the right moment, you might just catch yourself witnessing an amazing sunrise or sunset. In some ways, this game is really beautiful. If you're worrying about the water, don't be - it's not overly detailed either, but the physics in it are great. Your cannonballs and steering really depend on the wind and waves. It's very well done, and though not highly detailed, it's still amazing.

Modders and Hackers: In my 200 hours of playtime, I've only experienced this twice. Both times, they weren't breaking the game, just goofing around. Although it's completely unfair and should not be treated lightly, it's still fun to come across a flying ship or a ghostly figure floating around your ship. There's really not much cheating in this game, so that's the least you should worry about.

Sailing the Sea Of Thieves, there are unlimited things you can come across. Just sailing around on the open ocean, you can encounter the Kraken, skeleton ships, or a megalodon. Then there are world events. You might see a big skull or perhaps a ship in the sky (of course, clouds). That means there's a world event going on. Now, this is where it gets interesting because players don't take this lightly. There's a fight to the death when it comes to plundering an island. If you want to make a lot of money, go to these world events! Hundreds of thousands of coins can be made off of just one world event! Now, it's not just the world events that get you money. There are voyages and fortresses laying around, all very interesting and exciting adventures. You just buy a voyage and set off on an adventure to complete it, and you might even earn a buck or two. Once in a while, new Tall Tales come out - and that is really interesting stuff too! It's like a little story where you get to be the main character! Now, on a voyage, you don't necessarily make money. There are others where you can get curses. Now, curses might sound bad, but it's really not. With enough dedication, you can gain the following: Blessing of Athena's Fortune, Curse of Sunken Sorrow, Curse of the Order, Shores of Gold Curse, Skeleton Curse, The Ashen Curse, and Legendary Curse - all can be found in the vanity chest, where you can equip them to your avatar. It's really cool, so sparing some of your time for this is worth it.

Speaking of avatar equipment, there's one thing you should know before loading up the game: Pick your character wisely... You will not be able to change it unless you either save up for a long time or spend money on the game. Now that you know it, let's talk about equipment. There are tons of different stuff to equip to your avatar. I mean, there's something for everyone. The customization is unlimited, and there's surely something you'd like. The same goes for ship customization. A lot of different styles and furniture can be added to your ship. Some are really affordable, some are really expensive. And never forget the emissary.

There are a few different emissaries like Gold Hoarders, Order of Souls, Merchant Alliance, Athena's Fortune, and Reaper's Bones. Now, this may be a lot to take in, but don't worry... as a true pirate, you'll eventually come across all of them. Each individual provider takes in different types of loot, but you make more money off of it. Up to 150% more money can be made with the individual emissary.

Now for the ships. There are three different kinds of ships: Sloop (the smallest), Brigantine (the medium), and a galleon (the largest). All very effective depending on the players. Smallest = cheapest, largest = most expensive. As I said, all ships can be customized with both skins and furniture. PVP-wise, people say Brigantine is the most effective, but there are a lot of different individual opinions. Sloop sails faster than the other ships, as long as it's not sailing along the wind.

PVP: Both swords and guns can be used. Swords and guns are both fully customizable, and there must be something in there you'd like. There's a big variety of ice swords, lava swords, or just plain ones. There are different kinds of guns like a flintlock, blunderbuss, or eye of reach. Again, different opinions on this subject. Depending on the individual player, all guns are very amazing and effective. Now, there are also bombs. There are explosive ones and firebombs. And there are cannons too on your ship. Different types of cannonballs. There are curseballs that can lock the anchor or stop cannons, all very interesting.

And now for the goof. I've really only played with friends, as it's less interesting otherwise. I believe my friends and I have over a dozen clips of us just goofing around and killing each other. Shooting animals out of cannons, shooting people out of cannons, or even just exploding each other with huge gunpowder barrels. It's amazing and extremely funny and entertaining to play with friends. Doing it alone or with randoms just doesn't hit the same.

Bugs and glitches: It does happen that this stuff gets discovered. It's a very complex game, therefore there are only a few, but my friends and I too have clips of bugs, like our ship going flying, or just being launched. Don't worry about this, it's very well-completed, and only has a few bugs.

Loot and treasure: The variety of loot and treasure is great. There's a bunch of different types of loot to discover. There's still stuff I haven't come across yet. It's amazing, and there's always new and interesting loot to discover. The more you play, the more you encounter loot like castaway chests, seafarer's chests, marauder's chests, foul bounty skulls, disgraced bounty skulls, and hateful bounty skulls. All worthless loot that you will eventually lose interest in keeping. Some chests can even be worth up to 20K gold! It takes a lot of grinding...

Enemies: There's a HUGE variety of enemies. Weak ones like skeletons and ghosts to strong ones like an ashen lord or so. You'll always be surprised at what awaits you on the different adventures. It's incredible how many different types there are, and you'll always find yourself yelling, "WHAT THE HECK IS THAT?" Even with my 200 hours, I don't even think I've discovered half. Throughout time, I think the community has built up hatred towards the golden skeletons. To defeat them, you need to splash them with water almost INDIVIDUALLY, and when a horde of those comes towards you, that's actually annoying.

Overall, a 9/10 experience; I just feel it isn't a 10, but I really don't have any feedback... Amazing game, it's always fun to play shenanigans on your friends. (Mostly recommended for people with friends) GO BUY IT NOW!\t\t\t\t\t

Берем свободный день, швартуем Галлеон, флаг риперов, качелю в тиму который в соло может потопить вражеский корабль с бочкой в руках, чел который ловит рыбу и жарит (в том числе вражин) а так же по кд чинит низ, и конечно чела который помогает лутать лут и делает "работу с парусами" и можно отчаливать.

Конечно же не забываем и покупаем припасы в порту и плывем (корабль ходит если что) на ивент с корабликами или что там на карте заспавнилось у вас, без особой разницы.

Когда их у вас штуки 4-5 или можно даже больше (зависит от количества вашего свободного времени) идем делать ивент с черепком, каждый раз закапывая наградный сундук чтобы ивент обнулился и можно было начать сразу же тут же новый.

Один остается и сторожит ваш ShiP (мне понравился однажды случайно встреченный чел с ником Ship Happens)
Можно параллельно открывать смело какой нибудь вампайр сурвайворс во втором окне, время от времени сворачиваясь и проверяя обстановку чтоб вас не вжарили в спину (вас начнут жарить вероятно когда вы залутаете все 5 раз, или сколько у вас их по итогу будет, имею ввиду ивентов с ритуальных черепов.
