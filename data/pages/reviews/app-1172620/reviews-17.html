\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSea of Thieves is best played with friends, however with the "Safer Seas" option solo play is viable with a few caveats.
some of those being:
1. Nerfed gold and reputation gain.
2. can only get reputation with a faction up to 40.
3. certain features and "commendations" are locked, some of these can unlock more character customisation options.

with all that being said, this is a very good game, and while it is a payed for game with a micro-transaction shop, the only things purchasable with the premium currency are all cosmetic items that have no impact on gameplay.

in fact, anything you can buy with the free currencies are also all cosmetic, there is nothing you can buy in this game that will drastically alter how you play, your starter weapons are just as powerful as the cooler looking weapons, it's all about how you want your weapons and pirate to look like, and this is a very good thing because the only thing veteran players have over you is skill and experience, and for the most part the community is very friendly and welcoming, if you ask on any of the forums on how to "git gud" people will actually tell you strats and other things to help you, rather than just parroting "skill issue" at you... as long as you're polite, that is.

this game is HEAVILY PVP focused, so you shouldn't feel personally slighted if you get your loot plundered by other players, this is a game about pirates after all, a group of people not known for being overly friendly or caring for things like "ownership", so just relax and try to learn from any potential mistakes you may have made rather than blaming the game or your crewmates... unless it's hitreg, which still seems very finicky at the best of times.

overall, this is a good game if you have 1 to 3 friends all willing to pay 50 bucks (CDN) to be pirates\t\t\t\t\t
