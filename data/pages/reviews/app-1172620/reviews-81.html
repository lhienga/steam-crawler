\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA few friends of mine enjoy this game, and have been asking me to play with them for a minute. After installing the game and running a mission with them, the following occured:

Firstly, it took a long time to get the game going. There are multiple accounts that need to be set up and created in order to play.

Secondly, I was asked to choose a character. There is no customization beyond choosing which pirate you'd like to be.

I then joined my two friends and we did a sortie. This involved finding buried treasure on a couple of islands, followed by looting a vault.

Enemies and combat are basic and dated. This game feels like it's ten years old, when in reality it's only four years old.

The looting mechanic itself was tedious, as was the need to haul your treasure back.

Everything proceeded to become stressful when a player-based ship decided to chase us down. We were a player down at that point, and so the four-player ship that rolled up on us sunk us easily, ruining all of our hard work. We proceeded to disconnect, defeated.

I can deal with the mediocre gameplay, to be honest, but capping rewards so severely on private games forces a player to join public lobbies (and therefore be vulnerable to trolling and toxic players), which is the deal breaker. I play games after work to relax and decompress from the stresses of everyday life, meaning the last thing I need is to be worrying about other people rolling up to rock my world, burn my ship and steal everything I've earned. To be penalized for wanting to enjoy a private game by severely capping reward rates means they've made it clear how they feel about players like me.

All in all, it's tragic that this is the only pirate game that seems to be standing on its own two legs, because otherwise there could be a (mildly) fun time in here, if not for the other players. 2/5 stars, 3/5 if players weren't involved. I've requested my refund.\t\t\t\t\t
