\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried this game years ago and decided not to get it because it was clunky at the time. Fast forward to today, where a friend of mine wanted to play the game, so I thought I'd give it another go, bought it and waited for the very long download of 105GB.

Once the download finally completed, I start up the game and the first thing I see is ads for battle passes shoved into my face. I close those and continue ahead to the pirate creator. Now last time I played this, you couldn't customize your pirate, so I figured after so long, it'd be an option now-
nope. No, still the same.

Okay fair, maybe it's the style of the game. Let's just select a pirate, go from there. After a lot of cycling for a pirate that didn't look like they'd been slammed with a rusty, metal bat, I click select-
EAC detected local file integrity violation: Unknown file version (X)

Little did I know that a wide variety of errors would be stemming up from EAC. I've tried multiple online fixes from the website, from forums, from googling the specific errors and trying almost all of them (aside from a fresh OS install but I already did that not too long ago, so I know that's not the issue)

One thing I did notice was that I wasn't alone with this struggle since the EAC addition. Plus this isn't the first time I've experienced EAC more or less kill off a game by breaking it's core and making it unplayable.

Because after 53 minutes of trying to get this game to run on my beefy PC and having EAC gatekeep me from the game I paid for, I've decided to just get my money back and yeet this pirate game back into Davey Jones' locker.

You shouldn't have to do so much work just to get a video game to play. This isn't even going over the fact that I had to watch so many ads from having to look up the EAC error, try a fix, restart game, try again, only to have a different EAC error surface from the depths. I get it, you need to advertise new DLC for this pirate game but shoving it down people's throats certainly doesn't leave a good impression and I saw this complaint shared on different websites when I was looking for help.

Don't get me wrong, I genuinely really wanted to like this game. I honestly and truly want to but it's literally unplayable with the EAC killing it's code, so the ads shoved on top of a $50 (AUD) game just feels like a shameless slap in the face after waiting for so long for it to download, as well as the fact that you can't even properly customize the pirate and have to rely on basically continually throwing the dice to not get a smashed potato.

Again, I sincerely *wanted* to like this. I wanted to play the game and experience the Yo-Ho adventure of being a pirate;
Checking out the cosmetics, such as the Kameo-themed content, sailing with my online crew and just being harmless ratbags while harassing the spooky skeletons but this has just felt like an on-going punishment for picking up a game that just doesn't want anything to do with new players.

Maybe one day if they remove the EAC, then I'll revisit it and give it another try (third times the charm, as they say) but for now, I'm just going to get my refund and check out a different game that isn't dependent on an anti-cheat program that is notorious for killing off a game's ability to function.

I sincerely hope they fix this and remove the EAC because as fun as it does look, I can't recommend it in it's severely broken state.\t\t\t\t\t
