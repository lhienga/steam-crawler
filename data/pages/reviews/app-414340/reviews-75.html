\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recently bought a PS5 controller and needed a game to test it on PC. Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice looked like a perfect candidate, since it was selling for a bargain of 5€ on this Winter sale.
Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is an atmospheric adventure game where you take on a role of Senua, a lone Celtic warrior who is crazy as a bat. Senua has visions, hears voices in her head and is terrified of mysterious darkness that seems to follow her wherever she goes. The story takes place around 8th century, when Vikings first landed in Britannia and enslaved Celtic people. In an attempt to resurrect her dead lover, Senua travels to the depths of Hel (also known as the Underword in Norse mythology). Throughout her journey, she will learn Viking legends as well as her own troubled past, descending ever deeper into psychotic mental illness.
Hellblade is made on Unreal Engine 4 and has great graphics. The effects that were used to illustrate Senua's madness are disturbing and realistic, making the game's atmosphere pretty unique. Gameplay mostly involves walking around and solving puzzles, with the occasional battle with Senua's inner demons. The puzzles are mostly visual in nature and require focusing your attention on specific objects and shapes, which allows you to alter reality. There are several puzzle mechanics and none of them are too hard to master (I got stuck a couple of times during the game, but only for a short while each time). Combat is fairly basic and involves mashing buttons quickly. Hellblade is pretty interesting at start, but grows repetitive and boring pretty quickly. There isn't a single NPC in the game, there are no upgrades, optional content or collectibles of any kind (aside from the stones that tell stories of Norse mythology). So, I was fairly happy to finish the game in less than 10 hours. On the bright side, my PS5 controller is working flawlessly :)

I would recommend Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice to people looking for something different. It isn't a particulary good game, but it's worth playing solely for the unique psychotic atmosphere it offers.

My score: 6/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is trying to do two conflicting things:
1. Create a feeling of despair and paranoia. While cool, the positive reviews vastly overstate how successful the game is at this. Having multiple voices playing in your headphones at once is unnerving, but it's not some masterpiece representation of mental illness.

2. Present simple but annoying-to-complete puzzles that require you to (slowly) scour the landscape to find runes. These puzzles are so frequent and time-consuming that they destroy any pacing and immersion you might experience.

I was really enjoying the narrative, but every time I'd come to a new locked door that required me to find multiple runes, I just sighed in frustration.

The juice wasn't worth the squeeze.\t\t\t\t\t

Overall Score: 5/10

Sound Design: 10/10
Combat: 2/10
Story: 6/10
Graphics: 7/10
Gameplay: 3/10

As a Steam Deck OLED owner I looked through the list of compatible games at the end of 2023 with the aim of adding some games to my collection that would function well on the device. I also had a New Years resolution for 2024 to complete as many games in my backlog across all systems as possible.

Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice was the first game I played in 2024 to completion. However, it wasn't through enjoyment. It was through the fact I had set myself the challenge. I remember playing the game when it originally came out back in 2017 on the PS4. At the time I would have been 29 and was more interested in Dota 2, FPS games and things that were more cinematic. I remember I quit the game because the combat was just terrible.

Nothing has changed in 2024. Whilst the game does look amazing on the Steam Deck Oled, the main gameplay loop amounts to no more than a walking simulator. I was actually pretty bored of the game in the first hour and half so forcing myself through the whole 7.5 hours of the game was a slog. The sound design is excellent and whilst I can't speak to how accurate the portrayal of mental illness is, the game does a great job in its sound design to create atmosphere. It's unfortunate that everything else in the game breaks you out of that immersion / enjoyment.

Enemies randomly spawn behind you / off screen in the limited combat which is incredibly frustrating and more or less amounts to bad design. The main character is unlikable. Rather than drawing sympathy for her plight, she actually just got on my nerves. Giving up at the slightest challenge. Not a strong female character at all.

Unfortunately, I think the rave reviews for this were a product of the time. It was more or less a tech demo for what Unreal Engine 4 could do and graphically it still shines but even the most low budget games released nowadays have better gameplay than this.

If you are a fan of limited gameplay and visual novels, you may enjoy this. However, if you want something more engaging, modern and with a likeable character in the same setting I'd suggest you skip this and play God of War or even Assassins Creed Valhalla as both are a lot more enjoyable (despite the many flaws that Valhalla has).
