\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give this a thumbs-up, but I just can't.

TL;DR It's slow, boring, and also not a game.

While interesting in concept, I find that this game just falls flat. The visuals are lavishly detailed, but ultimately bland and boring. I get the Hel is supposed to be desolate, but since Senua herself is dressed in drab colors and the "enemies" are all drab shadowy figures, it all just blurs together. The story is simple, when you get right down to it, and nothing all that deep. The exploration of Senua as a person suffering from some serious mental illnesses is... fine, I guess? The directions it takes the character are generally trite, and for a game that wants to be Very Serious about mental illness, I think it too often falls back on cliches to provide its generally overwrought storytelling. The combat is the most token of token gameplay features - repetitive, boring, and actually quite at odds with the rest of the game... which is also repetitive and boring. Senua is often called a warrior and carries a sword, but nothing about her ever suggests a warrior's background, memory, or mindset. I suspect that this was a walking simulator / puzzle game, but someone said at the last minute "wait, what about some combat for the kiddies, then we can also say she was a warrior for more feminist cred?" Maybe that's too jaded a suspicion, but seeing as how she comports herself like a broken waif afraid of her own shadow until an enemy appears, and then right back when the enemy is defeated, it's one I can't shake. The puzzles are fine, again, in theory, but the controls are gluey and Senua walks so slowly (jogging is only an iota faster) that it takes forever to go through the motions. After an hour and a half of playing, I confess, I watched a YouTube longplay of the game at 2x speed, which still felt achingly slow.

If this hadn't been a game, but rather a short movie, cut to about 45 minutes, it could have been pretty okay.\t\t\t\t\t
