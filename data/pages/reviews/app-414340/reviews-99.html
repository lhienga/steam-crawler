\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImpressive visuals, sound is good. Physics/FX as well as some scripted destruction is well done.

Gameplay is boring at best, annoying at worst.
Half the playtime is spent searching for runes, which means matching faded symbols on your screen to some static objects in the environment. You will do this A LOT. It gets boring after the second time.
The rest of the time is spent either running to something, from something, or just to the next stage of the level.

Every now and then you get fights, which look very good, but feel terrible as the game seems to randomly decide, when your input is accepted and when not.
So dodging is sometimes the only way to escape shots from a boss, while it's not working at all against a minion right after.
The fight animations are few and get uninteresting quickly. Nothing is physics-based and the enemies look and feel very generic.

Overall this feels like an interactive experience with annoying chores.

I personally can not really recommend this game, although the graphics especially in the opening sequence were some nice eyecandy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been standoffish about playing this game for a while now, even purchasing it on a -85% discount for $5 was a stretch for me, but man was I wrong. This is the most compelling game told about psychosis I've ever experienced. The depiction of mental illness had me guessing the whole time.

The ambiance, the way they used the voices. I kept thinking about if this was all actually real or if everything was just in her head. And it'll keep you guessing past the ending of the game.

Senua is the most human heroine I've seen in any game ever, and while there have been more 'strong' female characters, there's no heroism like the heroism of the truly broken and crippled. She has to fight her demons, and it's all the more meaningful because unlike fem-Shepard who eats Reapers for breakfast, or forced feminine empowerment characters, you can see in the gorgeously motion captured performance of Senua's face, that she is truly terrified as she fights, but you can't be truly courageous without fear.

This game is short. About 7 hours short. This game probably has awful replay value. All the same, this game is a must play if you're a fan of good storytelling.\t\t\t\t\t
