I hear voices in my head

And anyone who plays the game will have heard them. Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is a game in which we play the role of Senua, a Celtic warrior who goes to hell to save the soul of her dead beloved. Of course, the way to the goal is not easy, and Senua has to face many puzzles, enemies, but especially voices that try to drive her crazy.

Starting with the combat system it is as good as it gets. We have several types of attacks, and killing more monsters on our way is satisfying. When we get a little more of them, of course, we can switch between them, but it's not very well done. There are also "bosses" in the game, but I'll leave that for you to discover on your own.

As for the puzzles, they are relatively simple, so we shouldn't get stuck in a particular location for too long, but they are done really solidly and logically.

As for the puzzles, they are relatively simple, so we shouldn't get stuck in a given location for too long, but they are done really solidly and logically. Admittedly, most of them are based on runes that we have to find in the environment, but it is done with pleasure.

Going back to the voices in our heroine's head it's also not that they are there and that's it. During combat, at least, the voices can tell us from which side an attack is coming, or if someone is lurking behind our back. While solving puzzles we can also hear subtle hints.

Graphically, the game is simply gorgeous, although we won't experience many diverse locations in the game, the ones we do get are done really nicely. The same is true of the sound design, the dubbing and music are very good and it's nice to listen to it throughout the game.

Despite the fact that I have an overall positive opinion of this game there is one thing that incredibly managed to irritate the gameplay for me. Namely, I think that this game is a bit of a running simulator. What I mean here is mainly that most of the game is spent walking. Admittedly, this doesn't make the game suddenly weak, but it is a slight disadvantage that can make some players discouraged from playing further at the beginning.

Summary

Overall Hellblade will stay in my memory for a long time because of the great storyline and the voices I hear even after finishing the game. Additionally, the low price and short but satisfying gameplay. I can't wait for the next part, maybe with more mechanics and longer gameplay.

✔️The storyline is really good
✔️The climate of the game can be felt in each successive location
✔️Sound and voices in the protagonist's head (and more)
✔️The graphics are beautiful
✔️Low price

❌Too much walking

Time of completion: 7-9 hours

Rating: 8/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade: Senua's Sacrifice features amazing visuals and sound quality. Intriguing story connected with puzzles and fairly decent combat system, to break it down we'll get something like: a puzzle game with acceptable combat, a few bugs and amazing story. I think this is more then just a game, the amount of work put into making Senua feel realistic. The emotion put into her facial expressions, voices guiding you and sometimes doubting Senua as if it was real thoughts of a real person, characters with their own backstories put together with checkpoint system...a well told story. It feels like an interactive movie. For me it is truly a work of art more then it is a game, because it still lacks some gameplay mechanics I would appreciate having and would make the game feel more like a game (if you know what i mean).

This gets us to the next part I want to talk about, the combat system. Yes it is repetitive, boring sometimes, and every battle feels the same most of the time, but it also has its own pros. First of all its very smooth and flows very well with the theme of the game, Senua's animations are amazing and connect very well, the dodge mechanic is very useful and saved me many times throughout the run. So overall it is decent,l it also depends on players preference but for me this game was never about combat.

Next is puzzles and i can't say much about them. Personally i never liked puzzles but i got lost into the games story and puzzles aren't that hard in this game so I didn't really mind doing them. Yes sometimes i got confused but i quickly found solutions.

Overall this game is good, I rarely finish any singleplayer game due to the loosing of interest, but this one has got me playing it for 5 hours nonstop without even noticing it. It's a rare find for me, It kept me interested, hungry for more, i have a feeling I'll replay this at some point in the future. I love it.

Note: as of writing this review, i have not finished the game yet.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWriting this at 3am after just finishing my playthrough in one sitting.

TLDR: Amazing story-driven game. Combat is ok (dark souls meets god of war). Has a few downfalls that keep coming back but a solid 8.5/10. If you like anything related to Celtic or Norse mythology. Send it.

Note: People have related this game to dark souls. I went in expecting dark souls. It's not. Yes, you have to use your brain in combat or you'll die....but you're not going to spend an hour on one boss.


Pros:

1. Story - The storytelling had me for most of the entire 8 hour playthrough. A few times I found myself drifting away but then something would happen and pull me right back.
2. Voice Acting - Not only do they do an amazing job depicting the scenario the game gives you (trying to give you an idea without spoilers), but the extra voices in the game are amazing.
3. Music - Can't go wrong with any Celtic music.

Cons:

1. One of the Core Gameplay Mechanics - (Gameplay mechanic/spoiler. If you want to go in blind don't read). - The game revolves around solving this "rune" puzzle system where you have to make shapes with the surrounding environment. Cool thought and concept the first couple times. After the 50th, it gets a little old. Don't get me wrong though. It's not game breaking and the story picks up the slack.
2. Running - simply put...if you're in combat, sick. If you're travelling around the world (which is more often than not)....someone in a Walmart mobility scooter goes faster than your pitiful jog.

Definitely recommend overall. Graphics are outstanding and for the few cons I've listed, the pros I haven't gone into detail about farrrrrrr outweigh them. Oh, and make sure you wear a pair of good headphones for full immersion.\t\t\t\t\t
