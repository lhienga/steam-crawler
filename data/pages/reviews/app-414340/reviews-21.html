\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI give this game a rank D- (35-40%)
Poor game - don't waste your time..
See my profile for more details about my reviews.

Well, I don't know if I am being generous by giving it a D- or if I am harsh by giving it a thumbs down... but I can not reasonably give Hellblade more than 40%... It is not an awful game, it is just... very poor. I don't think Ninja Theory ever did a good game, anyway. Or maybe we are just worlds apart, them as "game devs" and me as a "gamer". One thing I will say though, they made me laugh right at the end, when the credits started rolling: a dreadful song written in bad prose (no rhymes, terrible lyrics) in which the singer starts by saying "I know it's hard to tell how mixed up you feel right now...".
Indeed! I felt very mixed up during this game because A) the game is bad in my opinion and B) there are sadly decent things here and there, throughout the game... but only in potential.
Let's start with the good ones: the game is old, yet very pretty. The atmosphere is dark and well done. Some songs in the background (vikings/wardruna style) are decent. The combats... had the seed of potential for being interesting. And... that's about it. Oh, also, the game is short, done in less than 10 hours for sure.
The bad, though? present everywhere. First this "game" is not really a game, it is basically a walking movie mixed with ONE type of fights repeated throughout the whole thing, and ONE type of puzzles, also repeated all the way. Once you killed your first enemy, you know what to expect UNTIL THE FINAL BOSS. It is all the same! The puzzles? Fine for an indie game, and maybe for levels 1 to 5, as a tutorial? but for a "big" game like that? so repetitive and boring.
And "walking movie" is perhaps the worst of all. This "game" is not a game, it is once again one of these pretentious, blabbering arty experiences from people who might be smart but think they are way smarter than they are and who want to be acknowledged as such. Extremely pretentious, pseudo philosophical, soporifically boring... if you loved The Tree of Life and all this arty non sense at the movies, you might enjoy this one too, same junk. There are voices in your ears ALL THE WAY, sometimes 10 VOICES talking at the same time... all the "dialogues" are actually "monologues", with big words like darkness, hope and sacrifice every sentence, like a bad metal band's whiny song. Senua's motion capture breaks the immersion with her teeth sticking out and her always looking at the camera. The "CAMERA", like in a movie, flies here and there, carried like in these arty movies... The other NPCs are simply real actors filmed and delivering their boring soliloquy, dressed in animals skins and fake blood... the whole narration and story is told AFTERWARDS, meaning NOTHING happens in the game but Senua walking through Hel and suffering, crying, fearing...
Using the excuse of "depicting psychosis and other mental illnesses" to make it caring and concerned does not deter the fact that it is simply a very poor game. Some people might love it, I really did not, and I am glad I was able to finish it to say that.
Now I will not hide the fact that I am looking forward to Hellblade 2, as I still hope that Ninja Theory MIGHT do a better job, because the potential was there, for sure! Beside that? Don't spend money in that. There are way, way smarter and deeper, yet more genuine games out there. Hundreds on Steam! For me, Hellblade is not worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy good friend Alex Michael Hunter recommended this game to me. Him being the smartest and most humble man I know, I knew I had to try this one out. He explained the idea of the game to me somewhat, but after playing and achieving 100% of the game I would like to give an honest thought out review to anyone actually considering buying this game. This is of course completely my opinion which means nothing to anyone besides myself. I want to break this review up in a few simple sections.

-Story/Plot
-Game play/Atmosphere
-Overall Theme/Inspiration

*Story/Plot - Without spoiling too much of the story of the game. It is essentially about a woman with severe psychosis who is constantly hearing voices including her own and that of other characters in the game. To be completely honest I had a difficult time following the complete story of the game. It is a constant battle with her own mind and her weaknesses which is an awesome concept due to her dealing with psychosis, but there is also the overarching plot where you are supposed to be saving some sort of boyfriend from the grasp of Norse gods and goddesses. It seemed like it never committed to either of those fully. Just when you thought she was overcoming something in her own mind or body, it was actually just some random Norse god messing with her head. The ending of the game is also very confusing and did not make any sense. I personally feel like it did not wrap up either of the story lines mentioned above. Besides the criticism, the idea of having this woman battling with her mind constantly is a great concept. Battling of gods is also a winner from me every time.

*Game play/Atmosphere - There is quite a bit I could dive into when it comes to the game play portion of this review, but for readers sake I will keep it as brief as possible. This game has many pros and cons. One pro is actually being the combat for me. The battles I had with some of the monsters felt really good and action packed. On the flip side of this combat you have only two attacks to choose from with an evade and block button. Not too flashy but, slaying the enemies DOES feel somewhat good still. Con, there is truly only maybe four or five enemy types you encounter the entire game. It is complete copy and paste for every fight you have in this game beside the boss battles you have. They do a very cool feature with the voices in your head talking to you though when it comes to the combat. They tell you when enemies are surrounding you and when to dodge or block incoming attacks in a subtle yet useful way. Very HEAVY PUZZLE game. The flow of game play is going into an area, completing 30 minutes of puzzles, having a boss battle, and moving onto next area. Rinse and repeat. I was not a fan of the puzzles at all solely due to the amount of them in the game. If they had sprinkled them throughout the game instead of flooding the game with them, I would have enjoyed it quite a bit more. Very linear as well. So not much exploration or chances to find hidden places for some cool secrets. Very straightforward. The atmosphere of the game however was actually really good. The game honestly gets pretty dark at some points when it comes to mental health and having disturbing scenes which I was a fan of. Not many games dive into that side of things often so it was cool seeing one that did. It does it well too. There will be points of the game where the voices in her head have been talking so long that when you take off your headset it really is like getting them out of your own head. Also the areas which conveyed some of her darker thoughts and feelings were done really well in relation to the overall theme of the game which is next.

*Overall Theme/Inspiration - I am not a developer of this game so me saying that I believe I know what their theme or inspirations with the game are, is my complete opinion. With that being said the theme or inspiration of this game is obviously mental health and showing some awareness to it in a fun game. The theme of the Norse mythology is also a HUGE thing of the game. Which is something that I am personally a sucker for and I feel like a ton of people can really get behind it and enjoy it like I did. I have played the newer God of Wars and being inspired to learn more about that mythology I have read and done a little research on it. I found myself learning so much and getting reminded of some of the lore I already know. This game is amazing when it comes to teaching you so many stories and interesting traditions or customs with Norse mythology. One achievement of the game is activating all of these things in the game called Lorestones. Hence the name, with each one you discover you get a piece of lore/history lesson of Norse mythology with different stories relating to a certain point in the game you are at or just for entertainment purposes. I found myself many times replaying each of these Lorestones just to hear the amazing stories again. If you enjoy learning about different mythologies I would recommend the game solely just for that aspect. The mental health awareness idea of the game is also a really good one. Its said on the games loading screen that people who experience these conditions and the doctors that treat them have all collaborated on the game to give you the true feeling of what someone with one of these conditions would feel or act like during the game. It is a really good concept and I found it awesome that I could get a glimpse into their lives and sympathize with them on some of the things that they might feel.

My Honest Review
--------------------
Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is a short and sweet play through that is easy to 100% if you're into that. You learn quite a bit by playing the game. I personally did not find anything new/amazing about it however. From a very linear yet confusing story, to sometimes boring battles and puzzles, the thing that saved the game for me truly was the atmosphere and the history lesson of the game. I would wait for the game to go on sale before buying. I paid 2.99 USD for the game and I personally believe it is well worth that. Anything over however, I would save your money and wait for the sale.

5/10 = Average Game\t\t\t\t\t
