\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Northmen often tell of a woman riven by madness and seared by grief. They say that, so great was her loss - and so unwilling was she to accept it - she stood in terror, unyielding, before the realms of giants, spirits, and gods not her own in battle of mind, body, and soul.

In this, simply put, unmissable experience, you are that woman - Senua, a Celtic or Pictish warrior impelled to Viking lands in relentless pursuit of restitution for what their gods dared take from her.

Delve into scientifically informed replication of psychosis with unforgettable visual and auditory effects composited into a masterpiece of storytelling and character immersion.

The gameplay itself is on the shorter side, but encompasses visual and auditory puzzle elements, engaging if uncomplicated combat, and compelling structure. The art direction is superb and, despite being powered by last-gen Unreal 4, holds up beautifully against even brand new AAA titles as of 2024.

In my experience, high-quality headphones are an absolute must for Hellblade and the game is best played in an environment with minimal light. I am not fortunate enough to have one, but I imagine having an OLED monitor would also be a major plus for handling some of the darker moments. Also included is a VR edition of the game which I now can't wait to try and compare.

You don't want to miss this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA rather bizarre "experience" from start to finish.

This is a game that tries to expand upon the genre by blurring the lines between cinema and interactive medium and certainly achieves that but without the charm of a game like Death Stranding. While the production values, acting, and direction are all excellently polished from the kickoff of the game, the experience as a whole falls flat.

The game takes itself far too seriously and suffers from severe pacing issues. The blend of puzzles and action combat make this feel clunky. The combat itself is satisfying but it always feels predictable when it will hit and the types of encounters that will be presented. The puzzles are great... THE FIRST TIME. After the upteenth time they are obnoxious and after about hour 3 I felt myself wishing for the end of the game. The boss encounters felt samey other than Fenrir, which plays to the game's theme exceptionally well.

Overall I wouldn't recommend this to just about anyone, but any gamers who are also film buffs, this experience is worth going through (but only once -- assuming you get it on sale).\t\t\t\t\t
