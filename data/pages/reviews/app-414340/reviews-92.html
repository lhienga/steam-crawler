\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the game overall is good, but I am writing this review to focus on some of its fundemental problems, which are gameplay related.

- No tutorial? Really? The game doesn't have a hud, so it doesn't tell you that you what button you have to press to perform a specific action. I understand the "no hud because immersion blah blah blah" idea but opening the menu to learn the keybinds is not that immersive! At least the game is not complex enough for that to cause a massive problem.

- Combat is mediocre, leaning towards bad. First of all it is very basic. You have a light attack, a heavy attack, block (can also parry if timed correctly), kick (for shields), and running attacks. That's about it. There is only one weapon in the game and that's a sword. Enemies are also very basic, and there are like 7 or 8 unique enemy types in total. At least the parry animation is really nice. The main problem I have with combat though is that the game forcefully locks your camera to an enemy. You can't get rid of it, there is no in-game option. That's just bad game design, there is no excuse for not letting the player have control of their camera while in combat. Only thing you can do is switch the focus to other enemies. And the cherry on top is that you can't practise attacks if there are no enemies around.

- You can't interact with the world much. Your path as Senua is about as linear as it can be. There are no items to collect, no secret paths, no multiple ways to progress the story for the most part. Although I can definitely excuse the no items part based on how the game tries to immerse you, I can't really do that for the rest. Oh and there is no jump button or crouch button. The most interaction you have with the world is picking up a torch occasionally and lowering bridges to progress.

- Minor issues: Subtitles are a bit out of sync sometimes. Senua's "run" should be renamed to "light jog". It is pretty damn slow.

Most people will finish the game in 7-9 hours, and my point here is really simple: An 8 hour game with the issues above is not worth 30€. It definitely has other areas where it excels at such as story, immersion, audio, etc, but they can't carry all the weight. I would not pay more than 20€ for this game, and 15€ is in my opinion a very reasonable price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"My gods abandoned me. I am alone."

Such a beautiful experience it was, but Senua's journey can be too overwhelming to bear. I felt Senua's fear through me, breathed and wept with her, and carried her burden within myself. The voices were in my head, everything made me paranoid and I was full of anxiety every second I played it. Even though at some moments I found it overwhelming to continue I wanted to complete Senua's quest and I'm glad I did. The opening credits were too powerful and I was scared of the unknown lying ahead of me since I had no idea what the game was about. I'm sure I still would be as afraid as I was even if I knew what I was going to deal with. I believe it's one of the games that can deeply affect you if you're not ready for it.

As the game is all about the atmosphere one shouldn't expect too much of an action. The combats are pretty basic, you deal with the same type of enemies and there isn't much to do other than evading or dodging enemies and then attacking constantly. Character design, puzzles, and music were fascinating and the sound design and visuals felt very real. Despite all of its beauty, the game has lots of bugs, glitches, etc. I didn't countered anything game-breaking but it's better to be aware of that. Human characters other than Senua felt like they didn't belong to the game, it just felt like developers recorded simple videos and transferred them to the game, making it look weird when next to Senua. Even though I found the ending satisfying the song choice was disappointing and ruined the atmosphere for me. I believe the second game will not have any of these problems and serve as an even stronger experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a wild ride. Ninja theory has created a work of art with Hellblade IMO. It does an excellent job of not only portraying psychosis but also "commenting" on plenty of other mental health topics. It's definitely a game to play when you're in the mood for something very dark, a little creepy, but also one that will surely elicit an emotional reaction.

The puzzles are not difficult but I found them to be very clever and to fit in well with the overall experience and story. Admittedly the combat can be a little janky, but you don't play this game for the combat. I would highly recommend setting a difficultly that you're comfortable with up front. The game starts you off on "Auto" and I personally found the curve to be frustrating as I progressed near the end, not overly hard it just kept getting slowly but clearly harder with each encounter making it feel inconsistent. FWIW difficulty does not matter for any of the achievements and you can unlock them all by completing the story and collecting all of the Lorestones, which are easily found with a little exploration in each of the areas. They make it clear where you stand finding those throughout the game in an intuitive way, I wish other games would take note of how they did it here.

Overall Hellblade is certainly worth your time and money. It's on the short side (I completed it in about 6.5 hours) but I will take a dense 6 hour experience over an empty 40 hour one any day, especially at $30. Though it can often be had on sale for much less.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was a HUGE surprise to me. I knew going in that it was going to be kind of a trip, but I was in awe at how well they captured the disturbing feeling of the main character's psychosis. After the first couple hours, I actually had to take a break to come back to reality a bit... it's pretty solid in terms of immersion. They don't have tutorial icons/prompts, instead you have to listen to the multiple voices in your head which may or may not even tell you the correct thing to do.

Only criticism I have is it's a little optimized. Nothing crazy, but I also have a 3080. Sometimes the voices wouldn't sync with the characters facial movements and lips. Other than that, it's a pretty impressive cinematic presentation.

I tend to be into games with a bit of psychological horror, so if you're into the same, this one is definitely for you. It's on the shorter side, but I feel like it was just the right length to where it didn't overstay it's welcome.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor those interested in this. Play it. It is an extremely grueling and painful journey to bring back the one you love. There is a technical permadeath, but it's hard to get. The game was initially confused with being a God of War clone. I can confidently say that it is NOT. Nothing about this is like god of war. What this is is a souls-like PunchOut with mostly linear pathing but some fair exploration. Accurate and absolutely brutal creatures and mythos, etc. The game also lets you find Nordic stories and reads them to you.

When all seems lost, do not give up. Just because you fell on your ass, does not mean you have to stay there.

Storywise: 8.7/10 A grim, dark tough reality and tougher burden.
Gameplay: 7.5/10 Fair amount of puzzles and absolutely devious combat. You're constantly outnumbered and have ALL odds stacked against you.
Visuals: 11/10 ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ the detailing and all for a game released in 2017.

All in all: 8.8/10\t\t\t\t\t
