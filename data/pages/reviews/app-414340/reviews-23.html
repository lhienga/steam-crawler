\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you can get this cheap, it's a slow and interesting thing to experience.

Pros:
- Graphical detail can be VERY GOOD across the environments, effects, and characters. This game looks like it's made out of real stuff, more than most games.
- If you're really into bipolar conditions and schizophrenia as an interactive experience, this is for you!
- The main character is a particular woman, no deviation or watering down of that.
- I haven't really gotten too lost in this game so far.

Cons
- EXPLORATION IS SLOW. You can walk and run but even the run is kind of a slow jog.
- EXPLORATION IS SPARSE. Most places you can't really go, unable to climb up or over much of anything. There aren't many optional places to get to or rewards for doing so. Almost nothing is interactive.
- NO REPLENISHMENTS. No food, water, herbs, potions, bandages, or anything. Nothing is available to offer respite of any kind besides completing something and the lighting returns to normal. Something like that would be a nice break.
- MECHANICS ARE BASIC. The puzzles are just wandering around looking for shapes. The fighting is sword attacks and either blocking or evading. There are paths, ladders, doors, and ledges. So far there is nothing else.
- COMBAT IS SLUGGISH AND MUDDY. You can't move in any direction very far or direct your offense/defense very effectively. The timing feels weird. It seems like it's designed to make you strategize your movements but often the best way is to semi-spam the controls.
- CUT SCENES CAN'T BE SKIPPED. If you're in a hurry with this game, you're going to be frustrated. You have to just wait through all the dialogue and monologue this game has to offer.
- SAVE SYSTEM IS UNFORGIVING. No quick saves, only checkpoints. Every time you die, you're one step closer to losing all your progress, with no way of telling how likely it might be that you'll perma-die before finishing the game.
- DIALOGUE IS MEH. I don't really find most of the dramatic dialogue interesting, whether that's from the focus signposts that tell you lore, or the interruptions of visions that talk to you. The whispers during combat are useful for knowing better when to dodge but otherwise I'm not enjoying the voices in her head. Plus most of them seem to be guys that I don't know or care who is supposed to be who.\t\t\t\t\t
