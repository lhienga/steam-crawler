\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAllow me to share my thoughts on Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice, a game that, despite my limited playtime, has left a notable impression. While the gameplay doesn't stand out as exceptional, it holds its own ground. The battles unfold with a cinematic flair, akin to experiencing a grand spectacle in a movie theater. The whispered voices within the game are a standout feature, immersing players in an auditory journey that adds a unique layer to the experience.

However, despite these positive aspects, I find it challenging to wholeheartedly recommend this game. As an individual prone to motion sickness, Hellblade becomes an unsettling experience due to elements like motion blur and a limited field of view (FOV). Although attempts to manually modify configuration files were made, the lack of in-game options to address these issues remained a hurdle. For those without motion sickness concerns, Hellblade may offer a worthwhile experience. However, for individuals like me, the game's current configuration poses significant challenges. I remain hopeful that future updates may introduce additional in-game options to alleviate motion sickness, potentially making it a purchase-worthy title for a broader audience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember trying this game on Xbox back when it first released and I remember giving up at the second puzzle gate because the game did not explicitly tell me how to solve it. After reading a lot of positive reviews about this game, and with the second game coming up I decided to give this a go, since it is only 2.99.

This game sucks, it is extremely boring. It has you walk through bland environments at a snails pace, even when sprinting, and has you solve the most boring puzzles to have ever been created. The gate has a shape, and now you have to find this shape in the environment to open the gate. Some gates have more than 1 shape. It is the most boring thing I have ever had to do in a game. Another puzzle is where you find a shrine, touch it, and the environment starts to burn as Senua panicks as you try to find your way through the gate this fire has just opened for you. It is not fun.

In between, and sometimes during these puzzles, there might be some combat. It is the simplest combat you can imagine. There is a parry mechanic that is so forgiving I would be surprised if you'd ever time it incorrectly. And then there is a kick, a fast and a heavy attack. At least the animations during combat are fluid and look good.

The idea of the voices in Senua's head is very intriguing, but the gameplay is just so tedious and bad that it is just not worth it to experience the interesting narrative that is going on.

Graphically this is a mixed bag also, there are some stunning visuals but there are also ugly textures. With ray tracing on, the reflections in the water can look amazing, but take a closer look and the water looks like it is glue. Then there are also the performance issues that plague this game. I have experienced constant framerate drops throughout my 1 hour with this game. I play on a Ryzen 7 5800X3D+RTX4080 with 32GB of DDR4 ram at 3440x1440 resolution. So this is definitely not a hardware issue.

Lastly, for the ones interested, this game does support ultra wide. which is always a good thing.

As for me, even though this was 2.99, I have still refunded it.\t\t\t\t\t
