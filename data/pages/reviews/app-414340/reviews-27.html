\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade is a engrossing game, challenging in parts driven by game mechanics that make for tension inducing moments (Run speed, dodge speed, etc). As many have said, it is a genuine piece of art, a considerable effort has been taken to curate such a detailed hellscape, providing plenty of moments of horror, desolation and isolation. The sound underpins the entire game, providing a hauntingly constant audio track of what horror might be around the next corner. I also didn't know all that much about Norse mythology, especially it being so dark, destructive.

The game isn't without fault, I found it rather dull at times, slowly wandering around and some of the puzzles were a relief once completed, rather than satisfying. And the final song, its definitely marmite for sure, but it didn't work for me.

That said, the game will leave you with some profound thoughts on how death might affect you or maybe how death is affecting. I finished Hellblade and found myself speaking to my wife within minutes about what the game meant to me or might mean about how we process loss. Overall, the game didn't reach the tippie' top score heights for me, but its definitely a very strong 8/10.

"A life without loss, is a life without love"\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been sitting on these thoughts for a few weeks now, but the way this game has touched me is unbelievable. Think this is a game where you'll either absolutely love it or curse it with your whole existence; the gameplay consists of mostly puzzle-solving and some quite simple combat here and there. It's a lot of walking, too.
What I want to talk about however, is how quite simply genius I found the integration of the gameplay into the story and the mental state of the protagonist herself, as she's going through a psychotic episode. It was so damn smart to force the player to adapt to the logic she applies to the world around her, in order to proceed further into the game. And also call you out. Because if you believe Senua's reality is twisted, you must also accept yours might be too. I have played quite a few games, but seeing these aspects being so well-woven together in this specific game excited me so damn much. The immersion is unparalleled, thanks to what I already mentioned, the 3D audio and the voices in Senua's head, that will definitely affect you too as you are going through the game. The making-ofs are also exciting as heck, I am so happy seeing how much care and love the team put into the portrayal of something still-so-controversial.

Give this one a chance, it's short and sweet-- just in case you are also one of the many people that adore this game :)\t\t\t\t\t
