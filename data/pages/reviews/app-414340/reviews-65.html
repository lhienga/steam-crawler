\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIgnore the kids complaining about riddles and lack of action.

This is a journey for people with imagination and appreciation for a psychological and emotional experience which goes beyond running around and killing things. The graphics are great, as is the atmosphere. The game does not take you by the hand in regard to orientation but that is the only part that is really a bit hard.

For people complaining about the riddles: Seriously? It's not that much just a bit of perception and coordination and the game gives a lot of hints to solve them. You just have to listen and understand - oh, I see why that might be a challenge for some people...

All in all it is quite a short game but I prefer it to end when appropriate rather than dragging it out with endless running and fighting when it brings no additional flesh to the story.

Tip: Play in the dark with either headphones or a surround system and when you're alone. Not a shocker game but the atmosphere is just much better as you traverse the land of the dead.\t\t\t\t\t

The plot, while good and original, lacks emotional depth and drama. The game itself touches on a very rare, but no less interesting topic of mental disorders and uses it in a very unusual way in the narrative.

Most of the game consists of generic and boring (albeit with interesting conceptual roots) puzzles and controversial but generally passable combat. So, after the first 1-2 hours I sometimes caught myself thinking that I wanted to quickly get through the next puzzle and finally come to a boss battle that would at least a little dilute this despondency.\t\t\t\t\t

Accept It!
I got this 30K IDR on winter sale, after they announced the release date of sequel, the sale is now 20K IDR here. 30K or 20K IDR, I can say it's a good deal and I had an amazing experience yet it gave me eerie scary sheet. Especially the voices and her expressions & stare.

God, I love it but I'm scared. I recommend play with headphones on for best experience in hearing the narration and the voices. Those voices won't stop from the start till near the end. For example, in combat, indicators like arrows will show if there's enemy behind you. Instead, they use the voices, saying "Watch out!" or "Behind you" to alert you that there's enemy behind you ready to swing their sword and you have the chance to block or evade when the voices say "Evade!". Sometimes they tell you to use your Focus and they will say "Finish It!". When solving puzzles, like looking for runes to open the gate, they'll say "Don't you see it?", "Does she know?", or "It's right there" when you're near the area. The rest, whisper through your ears while exploring. But they are the guide for Senua, serve as thoughts, doubts, warnings, and sometimes encourage her. Yet, it still scares me. Big applause to those who worked on it, I can still hear some of the voices.

The game focuses on exploration, that's what I thought. The story is based on Norse mythology (Viking age), and you follow the story of a broken Celtic warrior named Senua as she embarks on an intense journey through a hellish underworld. But now I know why after I watched Hellblade's feature (short docu behind Hellblade). Those voices, delusions, hallucinations are from Senua herself. She suffers severe psychosis mental illness, like schizophrenia. It's really cool that psychologists and people who experience psychosis were part of the team. So that's why I saw Mental Health Advisors credited in the beginning and it feels like the game puts me in Senua's shoes, allowing me to experience everything she's going through.

Last, I can guarantee you can get 100% Achievements if you find all Lorestones (tombstones narrating different stories of Norse legend) for the "Stories From the North" achievement and an additional cutscene. And there's a photo mode to capture the moments, but the exposure sometimes doesn't work properly on me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not a recommendation. I did not finish it, nor will I.

This game is getting flak for being too little of a game. I wholeheartedly disagree. I wish the whole “game” would have been on rails, maybe with auto-scripted fighting scenes where you just mash a button, like Asura’s Wrath.

If I want a fighting game, I go back to Nioh 2. If I want a puzzle game, I go back to…well, forget about that. I hate puzzle games like that, and I wouldn't touch one to begin with.

I got lured into it by a positive review that said something like “All I ever did was walk forward”. This is just not true. When the third “Find a rune in the environment” puzzle turned up, I turned the game off. I just can’t muster the patience to do stuff like that in video games. To me, it’s just not fun.

Why the thumbs up? This game is between a rock and a hard place, with players either complaining about too much game and too little game. In that regard, they just can’t win, and I realize that.

More importantly: Right from the start, it’s apparent how much effort and passion went into this game. On a chunky rig, high quality earphones and a large OLED TV, this is nothing but a spectacular and unique experience that drags you in from the first second. It is striking how effective the game is with it's visuals and audio, I have not experienced something like that before.

For that reason alone, this is a thumbs up. I’m going to keep the game and not return it (at the very least, it’s an impressive tech demo) and “finish” it on YouTube in a severely degraded experience. But I’m not gonna watch YouTube videos on where to find the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ F in the landscape, sorry.

As a post scriptum: It’s effing cute how they trigger warn you for depicting psychosis and then proceed shoving the nastiest of violence in your face. I get it that everybody needs a trigger warning for something these days, I think it’s just a peculiarity of modern sensibilities that you ain’t getting one for stuff like “staking people and burning them alive”. Having said that, this game is rough in that way. It’s straight up horror and If you're not into that kind of stuff, stay well clear.
