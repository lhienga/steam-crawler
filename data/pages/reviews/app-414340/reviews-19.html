\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very curious game, for which I let myself fully embrace the inherent strangeness - in short, you play as a very apparently schizophrenic nordic girl during the viking age, having visual and auditory hallucinations, that seem to "help" her during a not less bizarre quest she has set for herself, wanting against all odds to find back a deceased loved one.

The artistic direction is close to flawless, and reminded me quite a lot in certain places of God of War, naturally, due to the ancient scandinavian context (but also the character's handling) ; there are some beeautiful sceneries to be seen here, but most of them will be dark and even quite horrific, which might unsettle some players - lots of corpses, and also many scenes of mental disturbances due to the mc's illness, which illusions serve as means to progress throughout the story. The world presented is then quite often rather suffocating, although with brief apparitions of light, and spectacular nordic sceneries.

The "action part" of the game is divided into puzzles, which were relatively easy but may require some research sometimes, and combat, which I found a bit annoying at times, although it's focus system was the start of something interesting. What made me like the game wasn't those aspects so much, but Senua's tortured personality, and the hope to find something good or worthwhile at the end of her path.

As for what goes on in Senua's mind, it is a constant feast of voices, visions, mixed with nordic mythology; I wouldn't say the experience was painful to play, as for Senua's delusions, but I was glad that the game had the length it has, because it got to be quite "intense", due to its proper and particular universe.

My words here may seem a bit mitigated about Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice, but fact is, I liked it quite much and will be very curious to play its followup. It was a short masterpiece of a different kind, that may provoke many questionings in the player's mind - as well relative to the ending, which was against all expectations, one of hope (or is it?) and preparing us for the next game. One word about the ending credits music, "Illusion' from VNV Nation, which warm and modern character surprised me so much, in regard to the overall "ancient" vibe of the game - would have expected at most a cheery folkloric ending music, but here we had a nonetheless very good *pop* song, fact that made me smile, but which in the end, fit in with the game's ending and overall thematic, just a bit more of a modern take than what I would have expected.

So, Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice, might be one of the "weirdest" (but "weird" is just a question of perception, isn't it) games I've played, due to its themes and ambience, but also carries depth and fragments of beauty - the kind that may haunt you and make you ask yourself many questions, even if they remain unanswered. And this might be the precise mark of a true masterpiece. Will be awaiting to play Hellblade 2, with no doubt. And a tingle of anguish. )\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most memorable gaming experiences I've ever had, from a game that often goes on sale for $5 or less.

Presentation (5/5) – Presentation is key for immersion when a key aspect of the game is to integrate psychosis into the gameplay and the narrative.
+ Visuals are among the best for a game from 2017 and hold up really well today.
+ Binaural audio to integrate voices and other audio produced impeccably – intended for high quality headphones/earbuds. Said to match experiences of sufferers really well.
+ Music is sparingly used, but used very well, especially during the story's climax
+ Excellent enemy design in line with atmosphere of game – add tension to the game
+ Character facial textures and animations of very high quality, helping bring empathy to protagonist.
+Visual distortions are varied and said to match experiences of sufferers.
+ Horror elements are terrifying – speaking to the excellent production values and art direction.
+ If you want further immersion, it can be played on Virtual Reality! (I have not done this)

No negatives


Story/Narrative (5/5)
+Simple to understand story slowly unwraps as you play, bringing motives and sympathy to complex characters.
+Protagonist is very likeble and easy to put yourself in her perspective, adding to narrative strength, emotional peaks, as well as tension during danger.
+Lots of metaphors and parallels to relatable and universal aspects of grief, denial, determination.
+Spectacular voice acting throughout
+protagonist being voiced by a non-actor with mental illness, brings authenticity and does a great job.
+Lorestones an optional aspect to gain background information on mythology associated with game

Gameplay (4.5/5)
+No hud, no RPG, no inventory, no experience, no grinding – it is stripped down to the basics of linear gamplay and accessible, simple combat.
+Combat is very satisfying in its simplicity: parrying, dodging, running, attacking in a few different ways, and having a special ability. You don't have a ton of buttons or combos adding confusion to the experience – makes me feel the game was heavily intended towards people that don't typically play videogames – it is very accessible.
+Voices around you can sometimes assist you, such as warning when you could get attacked from behind, or opportunities to use your special ability (focus)
+Psychosis integrated into puzzles, brings a positive side to it on viewing the world through a different lens to the point one without the condition couldn't solve them.
+An extended horror segment is among the most terrifying experiences in all media I have come across, yet it is still something I can pause and put down ... for some, this can be their reality. This is a brilliant move on drawing empathy to what is stigmatized.
+I'd say the highly inspired direction of the game helps cover some weaknesses in gameplay mechanics, making them detract less from the overall experience.
-A few of these puzzles can appear redundant or complicated, but not a major issue
-Lack of interaction with environment, unable to climb or jump over minor obstacles
-Lacking in enemy variety – usually fighting the same 3 or 4 enemy types throughout and just a few boss fights.
Not a negative to me but the limited combat mechanics may be a negative to some gamers, and can be noticeable during the 2nd half of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Hellblade is one of the best video game stories out there with some of the most interesting and experimental presentation I've ever seen.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be honest i don't even know where to begin. no game whether its RDR2, eldin ring, ghost of tsushima, tlou, uncharted, tomb raider, etc has ever had as much of an emotional impact like Hellblade has. once i finished the game i sat in my seat for a good minute just taking in everything i played through and how i got to that point. even though I've never lived with any of the disorders Senua has, I felt like i was experiencing them through the raw emotion and visual scenes in this game. it was almost like you could feel the absolute pain and torment Senua was facing barely holding on to herself throughout the entire game. from the voices in her head telling her to stop, go back, your going to die, to the voices making conversations among themselves. And the colorful atmosphere determined by what kind of situation you were in was always fitting. from blue caved scenes expressing how tired she was, to red flashes of her deepest fears. to yellow and orange fire burning everything around her showcasing the chaos thats happening in her head. it all was so fitting for the scenes that played. and the music that was played really gave the game its feeling of redemption and fit the nordic history. of course Hellblade still has some imperfections, for example the actual raw combat isn't great, the game doesnt make all the mechanics clear like the fact you have to lean left and right when going across a plank, (even though its common sense), the puzzles can get a little annoying sometimes. but Hellblade isn't like ghost of tsushima, rdr2, etc. and that's why Hellblade is a unique experience and with the prequel coming out in a few months i am happy to say this game will be my number 1 favorite story telling game, for now.\t\t\t\t\t
