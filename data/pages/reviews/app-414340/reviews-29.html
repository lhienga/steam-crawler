\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is a short story driven game with an interesting concept of a main character: Senua, as game shows the player what having a psychosis can be like or what can happen in a mind of a person that is experiencing this kind of mental health disorder.

The story of the game is set in Norse Mythology and tells us the story of Senua, and her journey to Hela to save her lover.

As this game is made my Ninja Theory, the very few combat segments of the game feels really good and fluid but it gets repetitive after a while, same with the puzzles, it is interesting at first but gets repetitive as there is little variation of the puzzles throughout the story.

Visuals of the game looks very pretty, soundtrack, narration, and voice acting of the characters are very good aswell.

All in all, this game is not for everyone, even for me, as almost 70% of the game is a walking simulator. But I enjoyed the game's unique concept of a main character and story telling and got me hooked after around 3 hours of playing the game. I was going to give this game a negative review, but I understand that for people that love this kinds of games, It's REALLY good.

Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is a solid experience for me.
Story is 9/10
Walking Simulator is a 2/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTime for an unpopular opinion.

I really wanted to like this “game,” I mean I REALLY did. I am very interested in the theme, concepts, and the difficult topics it challenges itself to convey. But… all of that really ends up sounding good on “paper,” and ends up making a game that should have been a movie or a show.

And that’s really the problem. If I remove the great voice acting, cool effects, and general story elements - this is a glorified walking simulator with phone-game quality puzzles and combat.

Literally everything is meant to pad the run time. I remember a section toward the end where you have to solve a puzzle, and I had to walk 10 feet out of the way to open a door that is supposed to be a shortcut, but the “shortcut” takes identical time to traverse. No cut scenes are skippable; which should be my choice, not the “games.” Even the credits have a 60 second unskippable black screen at the end.

6 hours in and I felt like they were running out of ideas. Oh no! What’s my next “hold right trigger at the right angle” puzzle going to be!! Let me guess! I have to walk to some absurd angle to view it that requires me to open 314 doors because Senua will break if she climbs a rock - but can get hit in the face by a broadsword 25 times and is totally fine.

This is not a game. This is a “staring” simulator. This is an amazing story with beautiful visuals and adept attention to detail someone crammed into a game because it was the only medium they were comfortable with.

I am now an expert in staring at Norse runes.

Definitely worth the 3$ I paid for it.\t\t\t\t\t
