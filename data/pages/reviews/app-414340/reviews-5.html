\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat experience all around. It's a fairly one dimensional story told in a linear fashion with environmental puzzles and a smattering of combat interspersed, but the way in which it's told sent shivers down my spine on numerous occasions.

Ambiance and mood are huge factors to what makes this special. Audio design was fabulous and I highly recommend wearing headphones. Personally, I had to stop playing after dark mostly because of the voices whispering in your ear.

None of the controls or mechanics are taught to you outright. I pulled up the Controls menu early on just to get an understanding of what moves were possible because it sort of just expects you to know. Minor gripe and easily solved by looking at the menu, but a little unusual for modern games.

For as much as this game goes on sale, an 8 hour 100% game, and the stellar narrative experience, Hellblade was well worth the time and money spent.\t\t\t\t\t

The combat is pretty one dimensional in that there isn't much variety to moves and enemy types. You get a light attack and a heavy attack and can chain things together fairly effectively. The parry system is cool but doesn't really feel all that rewarding when you land a good parry.
I also have to stress I strongly disliked that FOCUS mechanic in combat.... Why have it if certain enemies seem to be able to negate the effects of the ability altogether? Just seems kinda stupid.

IDK.... just felt tedious after the first few times and even more so after you realize they kinda took that mechanic and ran with it. Just got stale way too fast.

This title could've been something so much bigger and way more interesting than it was.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game, made by very talented people. I liked graphics, combat, some clever puzzles, sound design, minimalistic interface, the Norse setting, disturbing art direction, and the plot. However, I could not help feeling that I've played it all before. Hellblade follows the template of PS3-era single-player action adventure diligently: we have combat arenas, puzzle arenas, bosses, cutscenes, "interactive cutscenes" (where you move very slowly and listen to narration), collectables and audiologs (2-for-1), steatlh sequences, etc. Only the RPG elements seem missing. I would have hoped for innovations in gameplay, and voices in the head, although an interesting element, did not change the overall gameplay feeling significantly. I hope Ninja Theory will be bolder in that area in the sequel. 7/10.
Gameplay statistics: 7.5hrs, medium difficulty, 41/44 lorestones collected.\t\t\t\t\t
