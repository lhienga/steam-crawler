\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn incredible game. Equal parts harrowing, poignant, scary, touching, and epic. Very story driven with a simple yet satisfying combat mechanic. Some puzzles might frustrate but you'll get through them. The lack of any kind of HUD is also refreshingly welcome. Playing with headphones is also a must.

The combat seems simple at first, but the game has no button prompts or HUD so you sort of learn everything you can do halfway through the game as you figure out all the combos and moves you actually have as you start trying other buttons. I could spoil what it all is but figuring it out is part of the fun.

You may have heard of this referred to as the schizophrenia game and that's because Ninja Theory worked with doctor's patients and mental health professionals to create and accurate depiction of psychosis, and boy, does this game hit hard. They added intense binaural audio for the voices in your head that comment on and react to what you are doing. They might genuinely have you second guessing what to do at points and by the end will have you really sympathizing and understanding psychosis and how hard life is for those with it. There is a little movie you can watch from the main menu about the making of the game (they have a popup that recommends you beat the game before watching it) and it details all their efforts in researching lore, history and mental illness.

It was an amazing experience and I'm glad I got to play this game. It's roughly 7 to 10 hours long. I think it would be worth the $30 price. I got it on sale for under five bucks so I am definitely not complaining. Some of the best five bucks I ever spent.\t\t\t\t\t
