\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's my take on the game - its actually phenomenal on how much effort the devs have put into making cinematic / drama along with a hoard of narration and backstory via "lorestones".

Long story short, the game revolves around Senua (the warrior women) who goes on a huge psychosis and PTSD rant to oppose her father who claims her psychosis problem as a "curse" given by the Gods of the Norse era and all hell was let loose because Senua and her mother were still alive and thriving with so called "darkness". Senua is weirdly a badass character, if only there wasn't so much whisper crap from the voices that constantly gives you an ear ra*e. She then journeys with a decapitated head belonging to Dillon (her husband who was already dead) to try and find Dillon because apparently daddy had "hidden" him from Senua. Not sure if the intention was to "revive" him or was it because of Senua's illness but apparently thats the whole point of the game where she went on a murder spree to finally give up and face reality that her memories were altered / forgotten due to her psychosis problem.

The cool parts were she would go between dimensions (much like the portal game) to face off against the "darkness" and the parry was oddly satisfying. The HUD limitations really gave a in-depth experience to the combat system. I would have definitely enjoyed more variety combat if only it wasn't so braindead spam all attack buttons and then decide to either parry or evade. The "focus" part was quite interesting but OP, considering how you practically freeze time and exploit the system into killing bosses easily.

All things considered, the devs did not think much about skipping cinematics, custom save points, traversal being absurdly slow and constant noise pollution from all the whispering slapped onto narration. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tEver wonder if there are countless voices speak to you in your head?
Basically, Senua's Sacrifice is that experience and much more.

The story is kind of confusing and simple at the same time but you will want to experience it from begin to end (and it is possible to finish the story in one sitting too since the game is kinda short), the atmosphere is top notch with the eerie sound effect and music to match, and the actress did a helluva job bringing all the emotion of the character to life.

Game-play wise, combat is simple, satisfying and responsive, you have light and heavy attaches with block and evade for defensive; the game is linear, mostly you walk from point A to point B, have a combat section, find and scan the environment for rune (puzzle section) and proceed, sound boring but it's not.

The game's worth every penny I spent (which is not a lot since I got it on the winter sale) and I would recommend it.
