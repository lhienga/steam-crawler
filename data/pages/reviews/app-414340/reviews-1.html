\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is a difficult game for me to rate. Nearly every aspect of this game is done exceptionally well and the developers have achieved what they intended to, but unfortunately this game was just not a fun time for me. Due to the subject matter of mental illness and psychosis, its actually not intended to be a fun game, but rather a traumatic experience. The gameplay is simple. It mostly consists of walking around and solving puzzles. Most puzzles usually require you to find symbols within the environment by standing in the right spot and looking at things from a proper angle. There is some Dark Souls-esque combat from time to time in which you have quick attacks, strong attacks, dodges, and parries. Graphically this game is stunning, even after all these years. The environments are as graphically gorgeous as they are esthetically hideous. The sound design in this game is also incredible and headphones are highly recommended. Throughout the entire game you hear different voices and they come from all around you. This is part of the game design and its attempt to simulate a state of psychosis or maybe even schizophrenia, but it eventually became overwhelming. The game is loud and depressing. The constant barrage of voices, yelling, screaming, crying, pounding, etc. gave me a headache. In fact, this was my second time playing this game as I've beaten it before on PS4, and both times this game gave me a headache, and as I'm sure everyone can agree, headaches are not fun. Hellblade is an experience that I recommend everyone to try, but only once.\t\t\t\t\t
