\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best games set in the Viking-Age that I've played.

I originally played this on Xbox Series X over a year ago ( I know that was late to the party )

As a lover of Norse mythology and the Viking age this game hits all the right check-marks for the period its set in. there are tales of the gods and giants told through lore stones by a character called Druth and those tales are told fairly accurately from what I have read from books such as Neil Gaimen's Norse Mythology and Carolyne Larrington's The Norse Myths.

Combat is simple, easy to learn and fun there is nothing too overly complex or frustrating here.

The sound design is amazing and I recommend playing this with a semi-decent set of headphones, as you will hear all sorts of sounds that you will most likely miss out on if you do not. from creepy whispers and screams to the many voices inside Senua's head whispering and chatting away to eachother.

At the core of this story it's about the mental health of a character that has been through traumatic events Melina Juergen does an outstanding job portraying the struggles of a character suffering from mental health issues. Melina is perfect in the role of Senua and I can see why she won the game award for best performance in 2017.

It's also worth me noting that I did have some stuttering issues with the game even though it's a been out for 6 years this seemed to have been mostly caused by the raytracing options and tuning that off helped to improve my issues, as well as turning Nvidia DLSS on.

Replaying this on PC is a great experience and I'm very much looking forward to the highly anticipated sequel Senua's Saga: Hellblade II in 2024 and I would highly recommend this game to anyone interested in the sequel before its release.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasily one of the best games I have played in a while. I will say it is shorter than I like for games that are $30 (I got 100% in 7.2 hours), but I got it on sale anyway.

I'd say this game didn't lean very heavily into any one thing, besides the story, and its message. You can tell the real efforts went in to telling a really good story and having an amazing design, which some games have a hard time doing these days. While the combat is exciting, and the puzzles are cool, I don't think they wanted to put so much into those that it would slow down the story for you, which was their main focus.

I do hope with the second game that they start to lean more into the combat as well because it reminded me of Rise: Son of Rome and its combat structure, which I still love playing.

With the puzzles and fighting feeling limited, and it being shorter, I don't know about $30 for it. It does have VR though, so for me personally I'll be getting another play through of it at least. I would try to catch it on sale though.\t\t\t\t\t
