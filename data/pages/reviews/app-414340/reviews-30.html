\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs art, this game is masterful. Graphically, thematically, and aurally stunning. The setting is wonderfully explored, showing off the deceivingly complex culture of the age of Vikings. The game demonstrated great range, delving into both oppressive horror and tentative hopefulness alike. The actors showed out, all delivering excellent performances. The story was captivating, taking time to explore themes and issues rarely addressed elsewhere.

As a game, it lacked substance. Mostly consisting of brief puzzles and combat sections, the gameplay offers nothing else besides long walks between the two. The puzzles are relatively simple, and utilize the environment in an enjoyable way. I preferred them to the combat, but that's less of a compliment to the puzzles and more a slight against the combat. Enemy variety is nonexistent, with a grand total of 5 normal opponents and 3 bosses, 2 of which are re-used at separate points in the game. General combat is mediocre at best (it has weightiness in spades, which could be good or bad depending on the person), but at its worst it is irritating and claustrophobic, notably at the end of the game, where you get crammed in tiny spaces with too many enemies and too little room to maneuver. These problems are compounded by an incredibly narrow FoV, a poor targeting system, and an infuriating amount of visual noise when using the only combat ability that is provided to you.

Fortunately, I believe that the pro's outweigh the con's. The tale being told here and the visual feast provided are simply too great to be deterred by the weak gameplay elements, especially for such a reasonable price. I give it a 6.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t
