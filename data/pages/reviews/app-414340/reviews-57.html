\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the game has beautiful graphics, the boring story with the poor optimization and linear gameplay turned me off.

People need to stop pretending that this game is a masterpiece and accept the truth... that it's simply an over-glorified movie game. The voices at the start are f####g annoying, a lot of the gameplay is mostly walking, looking at walls with symbols, and spamming light attack on the repetitive enemies.

Now lets talk about the poor optimization. I have a powerful rig. It can run RDR2, Cyberpunk, and Call of Duty all at a stable 60fps with high graphics enabled. Hellblade ran at a constant 24 - 40 fps depending on which area I was in. I turned down the graphics to low, and it didn't seem to have any effect. Fighting enemies or walking in high folly areas dropped the fps to 15, making it super annoying to play.

Getting the game on sale ($3) is a steal, but cannot recommend you getting it for $30.\t\t\t\t\t

An awesome game with awesome LORE and MUSIC.
The combat was really good.
The graphics are exquisite.
The game duration is great considering it's a low paced gamed, it changes from playing some other FPS.
Damn, i even tought i was going crazy with all the voices around.

But even an awesome game has its negative traits.
- Walking like you are jogging, damn it takes time (Personally i liked the slow pace but I know some people wont like it)
- The puzzles (No hints on how to first do it OMG it was frustating)
Maybe there are more negative things for other people.

I would really recommend buying it when its on sale if you want to save some money.
If not, go right ahead and buy it.

I will definitely buy the sequel.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very interesting game worth recommending. The game quickly puts us in a state of permanent anxiety, even whem we feel as the player that the world around us is not fully real. All of this thanks to the good narration, cool music, voices in our head and ambient sounds. Our journey to the heart of Helheim draws heavily on Norse mythology and thanks to Lorestones we can explore it even. The game mechanics contain two interesting elements: when our MC is moving on a narrow wooden beam, we must take care of her balance so that she does not fall. Death, on the other hand, was presented as rebirth, but at the cost of progressive rotting of the MC body. The more often we die, the more rot spreads in her body. As for me, I had a lot of fun with this game, but IMO it is dedicated to people who can tolerate some heavy, dark reality.\t\t\t\t\t
