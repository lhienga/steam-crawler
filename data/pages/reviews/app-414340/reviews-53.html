\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game wrecked me inside out. I can't control my tears when I reached the finale. I mean... at first I thought this is just going to be a creepy, horror type of game but I was wrong. When the voices started it feels weird and creepy, feels like they are right here with me, beside me but then as I continued to the game the voices starts to sound soothing to me and sometimes they do say helpful words to Senua. Funny thing is when I came to the final battle where there are a lot of enemies to kill, I started to speak and cheer for Senua like those voices in her head.

I can say that in some ways I really relate to Senua. Her traumas, the shadow and darkness, and her love for Dillion as her only light in the dark. The blindness shard trial hits different to me. The way Dillion's voice is so calm, guiding her through the darkness until she finally reached that small light...it was a very good representation of how Dillion accepted her for who she is, as someone who lives with psychosis and traumas. I totally understand why she can't let go of her lover easily. He understands her pain and darkness. He was there when her father (and mother) weren't.

The way this game dealt with grief, depression, traumas, and other mental health issues...it is really something. This is an art, not just a game. A masterpiece. I have never been this emotionally attached to a game or character before. I wish I found out about this game sooner but I'll be waiting for the sequel for sure.
Worth every minute to play this!\t\t\t\t\t

The combat was engaging and fun, and there were moments when you pulled off combos against multiple opponents and felt untouchable. There were moments this game made me feel actual dread as I plunged my head underwater knowing my light would go out.

The game play isn't perfect, and there are times I wish I could run a lot faster than you can in game, and some portions I found too slow, but If you embrace this game as a JOURNEY into a deep compelling story beautifully and disturbingly told, not a sprint through content, then this is a MUST PLAY.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game, the visuals, audio, and story are very well done, You can see fantastic performances by actors involved in "Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice".
My main problem is with the gameplay. Not that it is not good, but it feels like it is in pieces. There are platformer/puzzle sections and there are fighting parts. They basically never get mixed. You are either solving a puzzle or you are fighting. When Senua takes up her sword, you are in a fighting pit and when she doesn't have a sword in her hand, you have to solve the puzzles. You have no control over when to sheath the sword or unsheathe it. I think it would have been more fun and challenging if these two parts were mixed on more occasions.
That's my only complaint about "Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice". I'm just giving a thumbs down because there are mostly positive reviews about the game, otherwise, I would have labeled it as recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
