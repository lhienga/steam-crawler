\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn intriguing, unique game that deals with psychosis, loss, suffering and trauma. While the game has some pretty simple mechanics, it more than makes up with story, visual and audio content. Bear in mind though, if you aren't one who can sit through large parts of a game being walking and internal dialogues, this isn't one for you.

The Norse mythology that is interlaced with Senua's story is well-done, and I actually felt motivated to search for the collectibles (the vehicle of delivery for said mythology) throughout the game. The sound-stage for this game is excellent as well (a good pair of headphones is highly recommended), you can really feel the "whispers" around you.

The game doesn't overstay its welcome either. While some may consider an 8-ish hour game to be short, in the context and setting of this game, that duration feels just right.

I do wish that there was a little more variety in the base enemy models, but since Ninja Theory developed this one independently, I can't blame them for economising on that front.

Still a solid recommend from me though. Looking forward to the second instalment!\t\t\t\t\t
