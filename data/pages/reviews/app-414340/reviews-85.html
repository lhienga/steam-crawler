\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7 hours..... 7 hours of trekking through hell and the underworld with Senua... and I have a lot to say.

First of all however, unlike most other games, the notes and cautions you get as you boot up the game are not just there for legal reasons. As much as it pains to say this, but if you had prior experiences with psychosis, especially schizophrenia... stay away from this game. Watch youtube videos explaining it instead if you're THAT curious.

Now, onto the real review...

1. Gameplay ( 8/10 )

This, in my opinion, is the most important aspect of any game. A game can have a story as simple as a line and a dot, but if the gameplay is fun, innovative, and unique? it'll still be a fun time. On the other hand, not even the most elaborate story can save terrible gameplay. And I'm glad to say that Senua's Sacrifice passes the test. There are some instances where the combat can be clunky, but it is saved by how satisfying and how fluid it is. Senua truly moves like a skilled fighter. Her stance, her footing, her swordsmanship, her parries and her counter attack, are all perfect. I find myself itching for the next battle right as the previous one ended. The combat is wonderful. The targeting and evading however... now those need some work, especially the targeting. Evading is a simple tap of the spacebar combined with the direction you are moving Senua in... a bit lackluster if anything... And evading has no further possibilities, it can't even be part of a combo, it simply lets Senua evade an attack and it resets her moveset. It's not bad, but there are definitely more that could've been done here. Targeting meanwhile is quite awful. I often got surrounded and then butchered from multiple angles thanks to this. First of all, you cannot release the targeting. Once a fight starts, Senua is always targeting an enemy. Which means you cannot reposition yourself which is a very important aspect in any fight. You "can" somewhat reposition yourself by mashing the evade button and moving her backwards, but it is absolutely not optimal at all. But we don't even have to go that far, because even just switching targets is a chore. By default, the key bindings to switch target leftways is left ctrl, and rightways is left alt. Who the hell thought of this? I quickly rebinded these to Q and T ( Because E is for focusing and I've rebinded R for blocking ) and it works much better although still difficult to use. Why can't I simply cycle? This terrible targeting system makes battles with multiples opponents more difficult than it should ever be. Boss fights however? works quite well.

2. Story ( 8/10 )

Senua's story is...... a brutal one. It's not brutal in the likes of God of War where YOU are the one punishing, no no no.... in this one, YOU are the one being punished. It is indeed a very grating story with an incredibly messed up ( or should I say fxxced up ) psychological horror elements. This game can drive you insane if you're not psychologically stable, and for the rest of us? it's a piece of absolute madness that we're lucky enough to never needing to deal with in real life. It is a story about love and hate, life and death, trust and betrayal. Senua was lost, and so were we as we started her journey with her. The writing is sublime and the delivery is Godsend! These are truly some of the best voice actors around and it shows. They might not be famous, but they sure as hell top notch. This story is an emotional and psychological rollercoaster taken as far to the maximum as it possibly can. But despite being a rollercoaster, its moment of calm and bright are rare. This is a dark story. One that kills hope and shatters light. Be ready to be plunged into the depths of hell, because you literally will be. Welcome to the underworld, child.... a place where no lights can shine, and no amount of suffering can pay your way out. If you're into this sort of storytelling, then this one is up your alley. It's not the deepest story around, but it is indeed complex and very emotional. It is written well, told well, and delivered exceptionally.


3. Graphics and Performance ( 7/10 )

This game looks gorgeous. It's a bit of a given considering it is running on Unreal engine 4, but you can clearly see the amount of passion, love, and effort that went into each aspect of this game. The special effects especially is the cream on top of this wonderful cake. It also plays with white balance cleverly, fitting the mood and delivering an atmosphere in a more seamless way than most. As for Performance.... I think this section needs an improvement. I am playing on a Razer Blade 15 Advanced with an Intel Core i7 8750H and an Nvidia GeForce GTX 1070 Max-Q, coupled with a fast NVME SSD and 16GB of DDR4 RAM and this game still refuses to run at 60fps on medium to high settings at 1080p. Don't let the minimum requirements fool you, this game won't run well on most setups, especially aged ones like mine, which is not supposed to happen on a game from 2017. Frame drops also come often and they are not subtle in any way shape or form. This game runs on my setup averaging 50fps but the drops can be as awful as 7fps and lasting up to a second. Not good Ninja Theory... not good. The game needs a lot more optimizations, and it clearly shows.

4. Audio ( 10/10 )

This truly is the ace up the sleeves that Ninja Theory has stored for us. The audio in this game is a serious masterpiece. From the voices inside of Senua's head, the sound of the nature around her, the clings and clangs as her blade meets iron, and the slish and slash as her blade meets flesh.... the roars of the demons, the whispers of the dead, the unsettling and frightening sounds of hell and the underworld, to the clanks and clunks of the thick metal doors.... Everything is perfect. Every single sound in this game leaves a strong impression. If you're going to play this game, I highly recommend you to wear the best pairs of headphones you have at your disposal. This game is insanely clever with its audio. It is not only there for amusement, because in certain parts of the game, you also need to utilize it as a tool... Playing this game with inadequate audio equipment is foolish to say the least. I played this game while wearing a pair of Grado Labs SR60e headphones connected to a Topping NX1s DAC+AMP combo and it was worth it. Senua was "blind" in one part of this game, and without these headphones, I'd be in big trouble. There is also a boss fight that requires you to listen closely to directions of noises, so pick a pair with good sound separation and imaging, because it will REALLY help. If anything, I think a fancier pair of headphones like the Sennheiser HD660s and the Beyerdynamic DT 1770 Pro will do a great justice. ( P.S. I don't recommend speakers unless you want your neighbors and/or family members to question your sanity )


Conclusion

Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is an insane experience.... but it doesn't just punish you, it also teaches you. It teaches you how to face your fears, and how to stand up again and again no matter how many failures and discouragement you face. How even in the darkest of moments, there will still be a way out as long as you seek it. I know many stories teaches you these things as well, but this game does it in such a different manner, that it is ineffable. This game forces you far outside your comfort zone and places you in the most uncomfortable situations possible and you must succeed. It also shows you a slice of how schizophrenia looks, sounds, and feels like... Which made me respect my friends who had to go through it in real life more than I have before. It is a game I very much recommend to anyone who is curious, adventurous, but also mentally and psychologically stable. It is not a game for the fainthearted. It is a one of a kind.... And I think it is better off being that way.

( P.S. To those who played the VR version of this? you are mad. INSANE!!!.... And I respect you for that. )\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is a game about grief and learning how to let go and accept the outcomes granted to you in a unique way. The game has you wondering what is real and what is not, who to believe and who can't you trust.

The game dabbles heavily with psychosis (with proper research behind their portrayal), which is something I wish to see more developers and story writers do in their games. I love games that talk about mental health in any aspect, but sometimes it's not researched properly, and it leads to inaccurate representation and stereotyping. Ninja Theory took time to do their research, which I give a huge thumbs up for (and also means that can be triggering for some!).

A story told in three parts/sections, the mechanics are a bit clunky but the combat is fun once you get into it. I very much enjoyed learning when to dodge versus when to parry, and using our special ability to have an upper hand. The puzzles in this game are fun and not overly complicated. While most of the Norse mythos went over my head, it was fun having Druth recite stories to us throughout our journey. The character designs were unique and well done, and I'm excited to play the Senua's Saga!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm torn because there seems to be a very strong story being told with an incredible art direction, but the game fundamentally is flawed, as a game.

The combat is quite frankly the worst part of this game, it adds very little in my opinion, but mainly with how limited and clunky the movement while in combat feels. I can't really move the camera and am forced to look at the most recent enemy to engage me which makes taking on more than one enemy very difficult for I cannot get in more than one swing in at a time.
I don't understand why it was designed like this. The game seems scared to embrace a more souls-like approach to combat. Being unable to swap targets is quite honestly the death of the system. Even allowing me to move my mouse to change the camera focus doesn't seem to do anything. The combat isn't difficult or anything, but simply tedious and boring.

I'm mainly marking this review as not recommended because despite my attempts to optimize the graphics, nothing seems to help. There are constant frame stutters in certain moments which really ruin the mood. Playing on the lowest settings and even optimizing the graphics card specifically for this game has done very little other than make the game look worse, but still lag. I don't know what's causing it. And I don't know how to fix it so I don't think I will, which is a shame.

The game mechanic of trying to find environmental silhouettes is interesting, especially because you're supposed to hear the wails of the damned when you get close, but I cannot for the life of me find the solution on the watch tower. The good 20 minutes has been sunk into trying to find this darn thing. I only get the overlay while I'm up there but I only hear the voices when I'm on the ground. Restarting the game doesn't make it clearer, and lining the icon with what looks like it should be the answer does nothing.

I'm really disappointing because I'm missing out on a very artsy game and I can't experience it like at all.\t\t\t\t\t
