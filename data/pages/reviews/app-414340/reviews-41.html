\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my review I just want to talk about the voices that are part of the game. As someone who had heard voices all day long for three years, I can only confirm how realistically such psychosis is portrayed here. It doesn't surprise me that psychologists are said to have worked on the game.

The voices differ like different people. They comment on your behavior, your movements - everything. They also talk about you among themselves, and of course mostly negatively. There are usually one or two main characters and others who come in and disappear again. Often it is babble that is difficult to understand from many people, sometimes it is suddenly very clear, direct and loud from just one person. Frequently recurring phrases in this game like ‚ÄùWe know what you're thinking.‚Äù and ‚ÄùWhat is she doing now?‚Äù or ‚ÄùYou're looking for us everywhere, but we're in your head‚Äù are some that you hear very often. Some sentences come up over and over again, sometimes hundreds of times in just one day. It is very difficult to concentrate in such a state. As if you could watch yourself gradually losing your mind. It is important not to forget who you are. You can't lose yourself.

All of this and much more was presented excellently in the game. I couldn't help but be amazed. I'm really looking forward to part two. Big praise to the development team, who, in addition to the extremely good depiction of such psychosis, also created a fantastic game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a big fan of most third-person action games of this style. I think they're mostly about the story, but then I'm distracted by the gameplay too much, and in this case that meant I'm facing puzzle after puzzle(and I hate puzzle games) wedged between random fights. I hate the fighting of games like this(although being able to block-counter things saved the playability for me,) so I was pretty much doing an endless stream of stuff I didn't enjoy only to see the story unfold.

A problem I have with games that are focused on story is typically that I don't feel a sense of reward until the ending. It's like I'm drawing out this stuff I don't care about for the sake of an ending that I only care about because of all the annoying stuff I had to do to get to that point. So what's all this mean?

I'm barely enjoying a story unfold because I'm distracted by puzzle hassles and tedious fights that make me wonder if I'm just going to give up suddenly because it's too difficult. This draws on all the way to the ending, and then I realize the game is over. This type of game IS NOT for me.

That said, the stylized effects were interesting/cool, and the story itself was okay, and I don't want to say it's a bad game. I know plenty of people who love games of this style, so I recommend it to them. I also got the game for like $3 or something, so I don't mind that cost for a pretty quick play-through. If you like this style of game, go for it. I don't like pretty much ANYTHING with Dark Souls-style fighting, but this game got a little closer to Assassin's Creed with the blocking/counter mechanics, so I didn't mind it as much. If you feel like me, get it on sale and play it when you're really bored and want a straight story game to battle through. Otherwise, people like me should probably stick to open worlds and whatnot.\t\t\t\t\t
