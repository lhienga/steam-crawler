\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an amazing, emotional and harrowing experience this game is.

Without spoiling the setup and story, the game is deeply rooted in Norse Mythology, you play as Senua, a warrior from the northern british isles who is trying to cross into Norse hel to reclaim the soul of her lover.

The thing is, apparently all her life she has "seen" and herd things differently than everyone: some sort of "seer" powers or was it actually mental health issues? This aspect is beautifull represented in the game, particularly where she constantly hears voices in her head, which can be helpful in the combat encounters, but their constant prattle amongst themselves not only make you wonder what is actually the truth and what is going on, also makes you wonder their motive, etc. Another great thing is the visual effects depicting illusions (or delusions?), visualizations of fears, dreams, etc, in a gorgeous way.

And that's not all, the game uses this kind of thing to constantly explore fears people may have in their minds, like fear of fire, of darkness, fear of doubting yourself, etc, etc, and during many points of the whole game this works as imersive and intense as the best horror game or movie you've experienced, thought it's not exactly the main focus of it.

Gameplay wise, it's mainly a 3rd person action game, but the combat system is more simplified than many other games: it's slower paced than like GoW, for instance, there's no HUD, no skill trees, no gear, etc - just you and your sword. You have light and heavy attacks, dodge and block/parry, and you have to use them well for the different enemy types: which there are like under 10 different types.

This may seem simple in theory, but it actually provides for very engrossing, intimate and tense combat situations - which is helped by the very good character and enemy animations which make you feel each blow and parry and kill. And when things get hard you feel the tension and the need to really perform so you don't get killed.

Apart from the regular enemies there are also 4 or 5 boss fights that are quite spectacular and epic.

The other part of the gameplay consists of traversing the environment and also solving some puzzles. This is the slower part of the game, and you'll have some long stretches of walking and figuring the puzzles which is where the whole "voices in your head" truly shines making it not boring but always pensieve and giving you glimples of lore and storytelling for you to work out in your head.

During the game you will uncover bits of pieces from her life that will paint the full picture in such a cinematic, poetic way. The games uses lots of moments where they mix in-engine 3D rendering with live action performance in such an engaging and visually striking way. It's hard to write about, best experienced. And all the performances from the actors are spectacular, no wonder in particular Melina Jurguens (main character) won the award for best performance in 2017.

Then, although the game was originally released in 2017, it has received an enhanced upgrade in 2021 and now features higher graphics fidelity options, RayTracing and DLSS. It looks absolutely gorgeous with everything set on high (including RT) on my 3080 Ti at 1440p around 100fps with DLSS quality. It rivals graphics from current games, honestly, and enhances the norse mythology and "mental" / supernatural elements.

TDLR:
-----
In the end, this game is about realizing an imersive and personal experience depicting an epic, emotional and harrowing journey. The mental health issues are depicted beautifully, interwoven seemlessly with the graphics and audio presentation in a way that I think is truly unique and impactful and engrossing to the player.

The only gripes one can have with this game is if you have no patience for slower, pensieve, segments of gameplay, or you absolutely need an open-world, tons of loot, gear, etc etc.

No: this is the journey of Senua, an 8h game, quality over quantity, but rewarding to the max in terms of a well crafted, unique, experience at any dimension you can thing of.

Highly recommended and cannot wait for the next game - Hellblade: Senua's Saga.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game, for all the effort that was put in the research of psychosis and turning it into a video game.
For starters, the combat in this game feels really good. It's simple and snappy. Cancelling attack inputs with a dodge saved me so many times. Senua's attack animations are beautiful and you truly feel like a warrior. Enemies' attacks are easy to read and respond to. Enemy variation is not great, but good enough for a shorter experience. Puzzles are simple and fun too. The sound design and binaural audio are top notch.
That being said, this game is a bad trip from start to finish. There are no happy moments. You only get voices in your head telling you how worthless you are and that you should stop trying and accept death. And darkness, literally. Towards the later chapters you are only walking through dark caverns, tunnels, often without being able to see much in front of you.
I played this game in quite a few sittings, because I felt I needed a break to catch my breath and do something that would cheer me up. I don't know if because of that, but I didn't get the story at all. Sometimes I felt the narration was going very fast and sometimes it was just too dense, with so many references to norse mythology names and stories that you get overwhelmed. Unfortunately, you cannot re-read any of the story in the menu. I was particularly frustrated in the final sequence, where you have to do something that the game always taught you not to do. I had to pause the game and check online for what the hell I was doing wrong.
Overall, I would recommend this game for the audio technology, combat, and the whole feel of simulated psychosis. Unfortunately the story didn't really connect to me, which is relevant for such a linear game\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't like to admit it but this game effed me up a bit. The voices are similar to what I have to deal with and Senua's journey hits close to home. So this game beat me up pretty hard.

The game itself is good. There are some gamebreaking bugs but if I could fix it, so can you. The puzzles are simple, slightly repetitive, but they are satisfying. The combat is a mixed bag being both dramatic, cinematic and rewarding, and also at times clunky and awkward.

As a whole, it is an experience worth having. It pulled at my heartstrings hard, more than what I would like to admit, and it touched me deeply. If you can overlook the bad, there is plenty of good to make up for it.

I think it is high time for me to consider seeking help more seriously.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to see the story, but if I have to go through 7 more hours of THIS to see it, I rather not.

Summary
This is a narrative experience that REALLY wants to be a movie.
What little of gameplay there is feels like an afterthought to pad the playtime. Both combat and puzzles are shallow and repetitive.
The walking speed might add to the atmosphere, but barely moving while NOTHING is going on is driving me crazy.


Combat
Enemies have a script they stick to. You as the player have
  • A dude! Dodge once and bash to death.
  • 2 of the same dude. Dodge in a way 2nd one doesn't reach you and bash
  • 2 dudes again? Bash them! WAIT! A 3rd ones spawns behind you (OUTSIDE OF YOUR FIELD OF VIEW) and attacks. Previous strat works.
  • Omg, a dude with a shield... Dodge, activate focus (slow time), bash.

Bosses have a few patters you might need to learn, but timings are generous.

Puzzles
The first format I encountered had me stand in correct spot to line up 3 symbols with a door.
The challenge is going through a linear puzzle solution (you can't perform a wrong action) to be able to stand in correct spot.

Why am I doing this? Are devs having me question my sanity by having me do ~6 hours of self-solving puzzles?

"But others rate it well"
Open up achievements for this game right now and look at 2nd most completed achievement. This is the percentage of players that beat the first boss (47% at the time of writing).
Most players didn't even beat the first boss which takes about ~1 hour.

It's not a difficult game or boss, it's just that the game is tedious.

Conclusion
This is a 3/10 game, but it might make a 8/10 movie or TV series.
Watch it on youtube and experience the 8/10 and not the 3/10\t\t\t\t\t

Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice - A Haunting Journey Through Madness
Graphics: ★★★★★
Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice boasts breathtaking visuals that seamlessly blend realism with a touch of the otherworldly. The character animations, facial expressions, and atmospheric design contribute to a hauntingly beautiful portrayal of the Viking-inspired world. The use of binaural audio enhances the immersion, creating an audio-visual experience that is both stunning and unsettling.

Gameplay: ★★★★☆
At its core, Hellblade is an action-adventure game with a strong emphasis on narrative and psychological horror. The combat is methodical, with a focus on timing and parrying. The puzzles are cleverly integrated into the narrative, providing a break from the intense combat sequences. The game successfully balances these elements, creating a cohesive and engaging gameplay experience.

Story: ★★★★★
The narrative is the heart of Hellblade, exploring themes of mental illness and loss. Senua's journey through a personal hell is both gripping and emotionally charged. The storytelling is masterfully done, weaving Norse mythology with Senua's struggles in a way that is both thought-provoking and deeply moving. The use of Senua's mental health challenges as a central theme is groundbreaking and adds layers of depth to the overall experience.

Soundtrack: ★★★★★
The haunting and evocative soundtrack of Hellblade perfectly complements the game's atmosphere. From subtle whispers to powerful orchestral arrangements, the music enhances the emotional impact of the narrative. The use of 3D audio technology further immerses players in Senua's world, creating an unforgettable auditory experience.

Level Design: ★★★★☆
The environments in Hellblade are intricately crafted, with a mix of eerie landscapes and nightmarish realms. The level design supports the narrative, creating a sense of unease and disorientation that mirrors Senua's mental state. While some areas may feel linear, the overall design enhances the psychological impact of the game.

Replayability: ★★★☆☆
Hellblade's relatively short runtime and linear progression may limit replayability for some players. However, the emotional impact of the story and the depth of its themes may encourage some to revisit the game to uncover additional details and nuances.
