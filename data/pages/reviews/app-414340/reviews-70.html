\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't a game. This is a slightly interactive GPU benchmarking software DISGUISED as a game. Admittedly, it is very pretty, and puts an ungodly amount of focus on the main character's terrified facial expressions. These are the most impressive facial graphics I've ever seen.

But, my God... Cookie Clicker has better combat. Just spam the left mouse button and proceed. No skill required, no thought required. And the game won't shut up. It CONSTANTLY has three different voices muttering garbage to you the WHOLE time, incessantly, with basically no quiet, music or breathing room. I get that's supposed to be part of the story for the main character, but constantly listening to garbage dialog is NOT FUN. All you do is press forward until the next time you are supposed to spam click for combat or click on a rock that gives you tidbits of Norse lore. This game is literally like walking through a museum pressing buttons to read canned dialog out loud for each exhibit. Seriously, there aren't even NPCs, there are rocks you interact with.

Do you rely on subtitles? I hope not, since they don't match the spoken dialog and are full of spelling and typographical errors. I also hope you enjoy trying to constantly read dialog from 3 different voices all talking over each other the ENTIRE time, written on different parts of the screen.

Do you want to know how the combat is supposed to work or what any of the controls are? I hope you enjoy constantly referencing the controls menu, because there is a total of 0 instruction, tutorial or intuitive control learning. No tooltips, nothing.

Do you want to know how much health an opponent has or that you have? How about how much damage each type of strike does? I hope you don't care about any of that, because the developers didn't. Combat is, again, clicking until the fight is over.

Do you care what the name of the protagonist is? They don't even know. Senwa? Senooah? Senyua? It's always written as Senua but in speech they switch in and out of each variation back and forth. I seriously don't know which one it is actually supposed to be.

Do you like Ralph Bakshi's 1978 animated Lord of the Rings, where he mixed animation with live action to create the most unhinged cringy feast for the eyes ever conceived? Well you'll love their use of real footage alongside the rendered character throughout the game. That's not awful at all...

I repeat, this is NOT a game. This is a UE4 tech demo that appeals to what hipsters think art is.\t\t\t\t\t
