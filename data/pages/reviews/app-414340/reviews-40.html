\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn experience as much as it is a game, Hellblade Senua's Sacrifice can be a way for people to get a better understanding of mental illnesses such as psychosis and what those suffering from them go through. As one of the main selling points, a lot of work went into making it as close to reality as possible and I found it very informative.
If you're not here for that, there's still a decent game with an interesting story that's worth playing here.

You can understand most of it yourself with what the game shows you, but some details could have been a little more explicit, or at least less hidden, given how important the story is. But hey, there's always the Internet if you missed something or want it explained.
As for the gameplay, you will be doing a lot of walking and a lot of looking. The different mechanics are never clearly explained but they're fun to discover and figure out for yourself, and most are intuitive. You could even say that it helps you relate to Senua and what she's going through. The puzzles will require more observation than reflexion and you will never be stuck for too long because the different zones are smartly designed.
The combat is simple, but I enjoyed it. Based around dodges and timing, it serves as a break between slower sections of gameplay and brings some short but welcome action. The few bosses are really well designed.

The game may be short ( 5 to 6 hours ) but it's pleasantly varied. Each section has a dedicated mechanic as well as its own visual identity, and given the Nordic mythology setting, they all offer stunning vistas. It's beautiful, and this goes for the character of Senua as well. Her facial expressions are really impressive and you can always tell what she's feeling. This comes at a cost however, as the frame rate can be a little uneven at times, but this really isn't that bad.

Hellblade Senua's Sacrifice won't be for everyone and that's perfectly fine. Those interested will be in it for the story as much as the actual game, and once I started playing that's all I thought about until I got to the end. Not the most innovative gameplay by any means, but a journey I'm glad I went on. A 7 that I will definitely play again !\t\t\t\t\t
