\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade: Senua's Sacrifice puts into perspective how the reality of people with psychosis into a game. However we see reality, it will always be different from people with this condition. Interestingly, it keeps the player engaged through Senua's experiences, telling her story through the psychosis episodes in the game.

Combat in the game is not the best, arguably the weakest point of the game. However, it is on-par with similar modern action games. It's not good, but it's not bad. There are almost no tutorial in the game. Small hints on where to go and on how to solve a puzzle through the voices in her head.

There is no UI throughout the game. An immersive game experience in exchange of telling the game's mechanics. For example, you don't know what key an action is until you progressed into the game further, you accidentally discovered it, or you looked it up on the controls.

For what it leaves out in exchange for the immersive experience of the game more than outweigh its shortcomings. Definitely a recommended game. A little notice though, this game HAS multiple elements of horror. Some are uncomfortable, some are fine, and most are fine. Notably, only one part of the game is definitely a horror segment. Take it with a grain of salt. What I consider horror parts of this game may not be horror for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeautiful game. Graphics, story, combat, acting, and lore are all quite amazing. Especially if you love Norse mythology. One of my favorite things about the game is the lack of hand-holding. You can look at the controls in the menu, but that's all you're getting. No HUD. No tips. No guidance apart from what is given to you by the narrative. I picked this up for dirt cheap, about 3 bucks if I remember correctly. And it was well worth it. It's probably 5-6 hours in length. There are lore runes constantly throughout the game that basically tell you the story of the Norse gods from the beginning through Ragnarok. I missed a few but at this time can't convince myself to play through again just to get them all and the achievement. Bet well worth at least a single play through. Excited for the sequel launching in a few months at the time of this review.\t\t\t\t\t
