\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA unique single player experience, Beautiful visuals (and distortions) and haunting audio that does a great job of depicting a protagonist in what could be described as a psychotic state. Playing with a headset is recommended and really augments the experience. The pacing was done well for most chapters and the Nordic lore is sprinkled in with easy to find lorestones (collectibles). Senua's facial expressions run the gamut and truly show us her sadness, guilt, rage, and confusion as we progress through the story. The voices that surround us as we play do a good job of pointing out things in the environment, combat and a plethora of other things. Since there are no tutorials or a HUD, the voices will give important information along the way, and I found this to be a special departure in terms of the storytelling that I haven't really experienced in other games. The combat is fairly simple and does get repetitive but it does the job well enough to move the story along. Everyone should give this game a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

I was sad to see it was nothing like Dragon's Dogma, but it quickly grew on me. The ASMR-like voices always guiding you etc. were a really unique concept. The combat was very unique and engaging. I wish there was a little more variation, but overall I really enjoyed the combat sequences; just keep in mind it certainly isn't the main point of the game.

The story was confusing, like all of these really in-depth stories, and in the end you will either understand it completely, not at all, or have your own interpretation of it. It isn't one of those "I will explain every single detail", you have to think really hard about what the message is that they're trying to get across. Some people like this, some don't.

People are mentioning the length being too short. I actually disagree; I think it was correct, if not a little TOO long. I am an impatient person, and I value my time a lot - so I found a huge gripe of mine was the run speed being too slow. I found some backtracking and exploration sequences feeling extremely bogged down and I was just holding forward hoping it would speed up somehow.

The scenery... I can't even begin to describe how good the world(s) look. I won't spoil anything, but I was blown away at how immersive it was and how creative the designers must have been to come up with some of this stuff.

Overall, it was a great game! Not really my cup of tea (as I thought it was going to be more of a Dragon's Dogma-type game), but I am glad I got to experience it.\t\t\t\t\t
