\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know the game states this, but please make sure, you are playing the game with the right mind set. It touches on heavy themes and depicts mental illness. Make sure you are healthy and well :)
With this out of the way, I think this game is absolutely amazing. For one, I like that the game touches on the themes and depictions I mentioned earlier. This is not a long game. I played 7 hours and finished it and got all achievements. But this game has the perfect length for its intensive immersion. This game took hold of me like few other games did. In a gaming-landscape of many games doing similar things, this was very different.
I see people's critical points, but they didn't really bother. Yes, the combat is simple and there are not many enemy types, but I don't think the game needs more. The mechanics are perfectly enough for its focus.
On the other hand, so many things are so amazing in this game. It is very beautiful, especially considering that is more than 6 years old. The music is amazing, the atmosphere is thick and immersive. The gameplay is smooth and supports the themes. The many puzzles are just the right level of hard where you have to think but are not overwhelmed. But the best things are the characters, the story and the way of storytelling itself. It is unconventional and non-linear but very well paced, and you can think along and ahead for yourself. Even the lorestones giving you Norse mythology is integrated into the storytelling and the feeling of the game.
As I said, I see where some people criticize some aspects of the game. But I had very special 7 hours. For me personally it's 10/10, no questions. I can't wait for the sequel!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had to take a day or two after finishing Hellblade to really digest it. I honestly think this game is seriously underrated. It's such an insanely immersive game that I finished it in almost one go, a type of immersion I don't think I've felt with a video game before.

Some advice; don't go into this game looking for a fast paced, high fantasy, action packed adventure. This game is the journey of a Celtic warrior grappling with the grief of losing her loved one. It's a slow burn, it's an 8 hour gritty & psychological experience. You won't be going around looking for collectables and upgrading skill trees.

The voices Senua hears really add to your paranoia. I remember going through a dark area with a torch protecting me from an enemy and one of the voices said something along the lines of; "The light's gonna go out." I suddenly started to panic that the light would actually go out. I won't pretend to know what psychosis is like, but I had a few moments like this that really made me feel like I almost could.

I think a gripe I might have & I could certainly understand is that this is a lot like an 8 hour movie. I don't think that's necessarily fair since you could say that about many linear story games, but the gameplay is sometimes lacking and adds some credence to that statement.

Personally love the combat in the game. It's slow-paced and simple. I believe it lends itself really well to the more realistic, gritty setting of the game. But I can also understand that it is repetitive. Like I said, there is no collectables, there is no skill trees. You get 3 attacks that you can somewhat combine, that's it. I can understand not everyone will like that. You can parry, block, roll too but after that there is no variety.

There's some really fun, unique puzzles with some creative mechanics to go with them, but a majority of them are about 2 or 3 of them repeated. Those are the not always fun ones.

Graphics are phenomenal and you can tell there's an extreme amount of polish on the game. I think the only kind of bug I experienced was a funny animation bug after respawning. Stayed at a constant 60 FPS with max settings bar raytracing. Seriously, the polish and lack of bugs makes it so much more immersive.

I can't say for certain most people will like the game, I loved it, but not everyone will enjoy a more metaphorical or psychological take on a story like this. If you do want a more literal story based on Nordic mythology with plenty of action, go play God of War 2018.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I understand that the creators of this game wanted to do something different, the end product ended up being way too focused on the mental health issues, while the gameplay and user experience really suffers. This game is really more like a kind of "schizophrenia simulator" that gets way too bogged down in the hallucinations and voices in the protagonist's head to be enjoyable.

Hellblade is fairly frustrating and annoying as an experience, which means that many people are just going to want to walk away rather than suffer through it as some kind of odd psychological challenge. To put it another way, if there was a game that was a realistic portrayal of a person who is crippled and elderly attempting to get through each day in a nursing home, does that sound like it would translate well into a video game? Probably not, but the creators of Hellblade basically did that with a mental disability instead of a physical one.\t\t\t\t\t
