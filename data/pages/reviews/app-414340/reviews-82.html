\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not one to post reviews usually. But this time it's a lot different.
Let's not beat it around the bush, this is a story game. If you want some action-filled, ADHD fast gameplay, this is not for you, straight up. It has fights and it has bosses, but they are really not the main focus in this game. They are a part of the game and, yes, you need to learn how to fight, otherwise the rot will spread more and more.

A good plus AND minus is the fact there are absolutely no tutorials. The voices in Senua's head will try to help you, for example if they say "Focus" they mean you should press E, if they say "Evade!" during a fight, they mean you should press Space bar to dodge, but the minus is that there is no where in the game itself where it explicitly tells you that and you would very likely need to open up the controls to see which keybind is which. There would've been no complaints for me if there was even slightly a small pop-up saying, I dunno, Press E to focus or something, BUT, the fact that there is no hand-holding is a big plus in my book.

Now, most of the gameplay consists of different puzzles by looking at different perspectives to see runes, illusionary walls or repair bridges by looking at the exact angle of the broken pieces. There are also from time to time encounters by the Northmen. What I found really annoying is that there are, I think, 4 different types of Northmen, i.e. enemies and you can pretty much get used to them pretty easily. It's not bad, per se, but more variety would've been appreciated. There are also 3, arguably 4 bosses in the game, as it is not a very long game. It can be completed in 4 hours and the only reason why I have 15 on it is because I was so encapsulated by it I repeated some chapters, then I repeated them again for missing Lorestones. The Lorestones are something like collectibles, as they are optional to collect, or in this case, look at, but every time you look at one, you would get an interesting story related to Norse Mythology.

Senua, in particular, is a very interesting character, suffering from psychosis, depicted by different hallucinations and the voices she hears throughout the entire game. She hear whispers and voices in her head, and I am going to say it right now, if you go in the settings and mute those voices in the audio settings, then please refund the game, as it is really not for you. Or don't refund it, but when you complain I would be the "I told you this wasn't for you", "I told you so" guy. The entire point of these voices is to explain the madness she goes through.

The game, the rot, the different enemies and environments and even this review are hostile to you. This is entirely on purpose. Reviewing every game that I 100% Achievement Hunt <3

This is a game that is unlike anything you have ever played before. It is less of a game and more a work of art. It is a journey you can only really experience once, and I highly recommend going in blind. Turn off your lights, pop on some headphones, and immerse yourself in Senua's world.

Pros: I had heard many things about this game before I actually bought it, and it exceeded my expectations every step of the way. From the sound design to the atmosphere, this game truly is a 9/10 for me. The story blew me away, and I could feel Senua's grief and understood her motivations for every action. The acting is off the charts, and the motion capture was really ahead of its time. Achievements were given practically for free except for one collectible based achievement making this a no-brainer for a 100%. The combat was simple yet satisfying, relying on simple attacks and parrying. After this experience, I can't wait for the sequel!!

Cons: While this game is a masterpiece, it isn't without flaws. The puzzles can sometimes feel a bit tedious, as I found that I either solved the puzzle instantly or walked around clueless for a while. I also found the loadstone lore to be a bit disconnected from the game, and I didn't really understand what the guy was talking about (which can be solved by a lore youtube video). Boiled down, this game is basically a glorified walking sim with a sprinkle of puzzles and simple combat, so know what you're walking into. The whole point of the game is to tell you a fantastic story through the video game medium, and if you don't like games like that then this isn't for you.


TLDR: A one time experience that I have yet to see recreated in any other game. If you loved A Plague Tale, then this game is for you. Be aware that most of the gameplay is walking with sprinkles of combat and puzzle solving.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an incredible balance of storytelling, scenery, combat, dialog, puzzles, exploration, art, and more.

The voiceovers are outright stunning in their quality and relevance, reacting like a chorus in countless unique events and player actions throughout the story. The progression of these voices throughout the story is gripping. While the motion-captured animations may seem unnatural in some scenes, they hardly put a dent in the overall experience.

The voices both instill a sense of sheer dread when called upon, subtly building it over time, or calming it down in the toughest of backdrops in a believable way that makes their optimism enchanting, contagious even. they allow the player to feel and share Senua's experience, her fears and hopes, down to her trembling lips and fingers.

It's impressive how commonly seen and seemingly basic mechanics are cleverly combined in different ways. Both in combat and in the imaginative, and more importantly, unique challenges each level presents.

The combat strikes a balance between fluidity and strategy. requiring good positioning while allowing one to recover from a bad one with some difficulty. The enemies adapt and get significantly smarter if you do well (on auto setting) learning to block better, dodge attacks, and use more varied attacks of their own, much akin to the player mastering their move set.
Damage taken feels impactful, slowing down movement and even changing some attacks to match the fragile state the character is in (no jumping strikes, sloppier swings, etc.) without prematurely ending the fight, giving a badly bruised Senua a chance to defy all odds and come out on top.
Enemies share this damage system, showing signs of the damage they took in the way they fight.
From start to finish, I never felt entirely safe, and just when I'd start getting confident with my abilities, a new enemy type was painfully introduced, with a new unique behavior that forced a new approach.
That last point never stopped me from defiantly proceeding, as the losses felt impactful, with a lasting visual mark deeply tied to the story and the possibility of just one more death being the last. Victories feel all the sweeter for it, all throughout the game.
Each boss fight is completely unique, engaging, hard, and well-structured with clear signs of the fight progressing without cluttering the screen with health bars.

I NEVER got bored of anything at all for all the time I played, which is impressive given the simple design, this game reminded me that levels shouldn't be judged by the amount of objects in them but by the puzzles they contain and their appropriateness to the story. This game absolutely mastered both aspects.

The mental health aspects are treated exceptionally well, it never felt forced or pushy, never too much or too little, never disrespectful, showing both strength and weakness without over embellishing either, it felt real.

The game forgoes combat encounters during certain sequences, giving the player a clear but subtle indication that no combat will take place, directing the attention to the emotional journey with a gentle, invisible hand, allowing those moments to resonate, and the players time to reflect.

The ending was one of the most well-earned cathartic experiences I've had in a game in recent memory, the end of a journey worth the time taken, a battle worth fighting, and a battle worth winning.
The tweaks made to the combat for the finale are simple, and subtle at first glance, but impactful, without invalidating or trivializing combat entirly. The fights last long enough for them to feel fitting and impactful, but not nearly enough to get stale, repetitive, or boring.

This is truly a masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat Storytelling, Good game, but I'm somewhat conflicted.

The puzzles can be kinda tedious. They're far to often "Walk around until you can "imagine" a rune made up from the surroundings", which isn't thaaaat bad, but sometimes (especially in the Vjalraven area) there's quite some walking/searching to do and Senua isn't exactly fast (even with "run" activated), making this more often a tedious walk that feels like a chore, rather than solving an interesting puzzles.

Combat feels quite good in principle - the flow between different attacks, dodges, blocks, seeking opening and knocking the enemies off guard feels very nice - but fighting against multiple enemies feels kinda wearying (and after the first fight, there's always multiple enemies).

This might be, what the devs had in mind (after all Senua's journey is exhausting her mentally and physically), but puzzles and combat both feel kinda wasted here, as if they're just filler to keep you occupied while listening to the voices in Senua's head.\t\t\t\t\t
