\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone living with dissociative disorder, I find most video games to be just a source of entertainment. However, "Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice" is an exception. This game is not just a game for me; it's a profound journey that resonates deeply with my experiences.

The game's portrayal of mental health, particularly psychosis, is done with a sensitivity and accuracy that I rarely see in media. Senua's journey, riddled with voices and visions, mirrors many aspects of my own reality. The developers' decision to consult mental health professionals and individuals with lived experiences has paid off, making the representation feel authentic and respectful.

The audio design is particularly striking. The use of binaural beats creates a hauntingly immersive experience that simulates auditory hallucinations. This feature alone has made me feel seen and understood in a way I didn't expect from a video game.

Graphically, the game is stunning. The landscapes are beautifully rendered, and Senua's character is portrayed with such detail and emotion that it's hard not to get drawn into her world. The combat system, while challenging, feels rewarding and integral to the narrative, symbolizing Senua's internal battles.

But it's the story that truly sets "Hellblade" apart. It's a tale of struggle, resilience, and the power of the human mind. As someone with dissociative disorder, I find Senua's journey to be a powerful metaphor for my own struggles with reality and identity.

In conclusion, "Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice" is more than just a game to me. It's a masterpiece that has touched my life profoundly, offering both entertainment and a deep sense of connection to Senua's experiences. I wholeheartedly give it 5 out of 5 stars and recommend it to anyone, whether they're interested in mental health, compelling storytelling, or just a uniquely immersive gaming experience.\t\t\t\t\t

Sure, great educational tool about what psychosis is like but I didn't find this particularly dark or interesting. Mostly felt sorry for the girl an entire time while being bored and frustrated simultaneously.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a story designed for the atmosphere, with some puzzles and combat to break up each section. Based on steam achievement percentages less than 50% of people haven't gotten passed the starting bosses, meaning they've missed a significant part of the game.

Fighting is good: There is a light and heavy attack, with a block that can parry on good timing, and a dodge button. There are combos that reward experimentation, running jump attacks, and a 'focus' mode which enhances attacks.

You only have 1 weapon and can't pick up any others even though there are many just laying around the place, and different enemies carry stuff of their own. There is not huge enemy variety, and you'll quickly learn to identify which enemies are easy or hard based on what they carry or how they look.

If you are interested in character driven stories, you might enjoy learning about Senua and what she's going through. Otherwise this might not be for you.

The second game is in development right now but no release date yet, so if you don't already have this then probably just wait for the next special.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like to rate games on features rather than a whole ? out of 10 type scoring. This game is brutal on your mind and senses especially if you use some form of Headset. I used a console PC connected to AR glasses and I found that the experience was nothing like I have ever experienced in any game before. I payed about $5 USD for the game because I am waiting for Baldur's Gate to go on sale. I heard about the game being very good and from a small development team. So Here goes my review of Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice.

I give it a 7.5 out of 10 for its combat system. Impact full at times but also very frustrating when the game pushes you into a corner and you are just unable to do anything but die.

8.7 to almost 9 out of 10 on the how much you feel you are in the world of Senua's mind. You hear voices coming from inside your head (With headphones). Using the AR glasses my eyes were stuck watching the gore and the virtigo inducing images and motion.

7 out of 10 for the puzzles in the game. I found that they were good and made you feel like you accomplished something but still there was some that really had to be done over and over again to pass them.

A solid 9 out of 10 for the Graphics and visuals. I had to turn most of the settings to medium due to the Console Computer I was using. Though I suspect lots of the lag was induced out of making the game world feel more unreal. Still I found that the images that assaulted me made me actually feel like the world was much more real than a game should be.

After playing about 18 or so hours, the last few just working on getting the lore achievement, I feel satisfied in purchasing the game. I feel that even at 20 bucks (or even the original price) this would be a fine game to immerse yourself in.\t\t\t\t\t
