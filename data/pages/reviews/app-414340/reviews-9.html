\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo preface this review, I bought the game for $3 and completed it in one extended sitting. Gameplay-wise there is a lot to be desired, as combat is "set pieced" and unnatural, splitting the game into 2 modes. There is the look around the environment and find the rune-shape to unlock the door mode, and then the look-lock seven (max) different enemies (which increase in difficulty for a short time until capping) mode. It flip flops between the two in every level and thats it for gameplay. I think there could possibly be a permadeath feature, where if you die too much you lose all progress. This wasn't a problem for me. Although I probably died in combat 3 or 4 times and once in a level, causing "the rot" to make its way up Senua's arm, giving some much needed tension. All the characters were likable and the dialogue often gave me chills, as well as the depictions of psychosis, which are very unique in games, especially in this setting. The story felt so original although pulling from mythos, the clash of celtic and norse mythology was personally so cool. At some points I really related to Senua and felt for her, but shes not a sniveling baby. Her motivations are actually very real and fleshed out, and shes a badass godkiller rescuing her love from hell eat your heart out Lara, Alloy, Ellie and Chell Chell catchin' strays mb Story slaps imo, can't wait for the next one! I think I got the bad ending but it was still kinda sweet so that's also a plus! \t\t\t\t\t
