\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is an outstanding action game with breathtaking visuals, great sound design, and compelling storytelling.

You play as Senua, a young Pictish warrior on a personal journey into the depths of Helheim, trying to save the soul of her lover, Dillion. As you start your journey as Senua, paddling along a river with a voice telling you a story, you are immediately captivated by how fantastic the game looks and sounds. Both combined create such an immersive experience.

As you progress through the game, more of Senua's story unfolds. The story is dark and about a constant struggle against self-doubt, with a focus on mental illness. The pacing of the story is good, containing twists and turns as Senua's mental state progressively deteriorates. The ending is very emotional and satisfying. Throughout the game, you have a handful of voices (known as the Furies) whispering in your ear. These voices constantly reflect on Senua's actions and her state of mind. They certainly add to the anxiety and tension, as they reflect Senua's mental illness and psychosis.

There are visual puzzles that you must figure out in order to progress, which involve finding Norse runes within the environment. Each puzzle solution is a simple matter of perception. They are easy enough to not upset the pacing of the game.

As a Pictish warrior, Senua certainly knows how to handle a sword. Combat revolves around dodging, blocking, and striking your enemies. Most of the time, combat is fun and smooth. Performing a well-timed block, dodging a big attack, and then striking the enemy behind you is very satisfying. There are a variety of enemies and boss fights to keep combat engaging.

Overall, the game is relatively short, but that is a testament to the fact that quality is better than quantity.

In conclusion, Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is outstanding, and it shows that a lot of passion went into creating the game.

Verdict: 8.5/10. A masterpiece!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games that I eyed for way too long without buying and when I finally did, I had no regrets. I am not one to shy from games that have heavy subjects or themes, and the way they confronted the subject of psychosis, and allowed you to see through the eyes of someone who lives with it, was an intense experience like no other. I have always been fascinated by viking lore and in my opinion this game submersed me into that world through intense visual art, sound and atmosphere that not even God of War reached, and I am a huge fan of that franchise.

The puzzles were unique, thought provoking, and only added to the richness of the story, focusing on perspective rather than logic. The combat was just challenging and engaging enough to keep me on my toes me between puzzles and story.

While I would warn against people playing who need constant action or are sensitive to the subject matter, I would recommend this to anyone who can appreciate a game for its captivating beauty, incredible story, brilliant acting, and perfect execution of a subject matter not many are willing to undertake. A true work of art in video game form. I cant wait for the sequel!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved this game. I know it's not everyone's cup of tea but it hit all the right marks for me and really pulled me in. The story was compelling, visuals beautiful and while it was unusual, I really enjoyed the different game play styles. The puzzle mechanics were interesting, not too easy as to be insulting but also challenging enough you felt something when you figured them out. I really enjoyed walking around finding rune stones and learning more about Norse mythology. Finally the combat: I know it gets a lot of flack for being somewhat simplistic (compared to a soulslike) but there are some fun combos to discover if you experiment and by the end I had Senua carving through hordes of demonic Norsemen with style. The boss fights were also really cinematic and satisfying. A great game exploring themes of loss, depression, despair, fear and anxiety and yet it managed to end on a hopeful note. Really glad I gave this one a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
