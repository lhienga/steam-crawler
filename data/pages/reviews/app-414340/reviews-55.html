A different approach

Gameplay
When we dive into this game, we find ourselves in front of either puzzles or combat. Starting with the puzzles, I will say they are very unique, especially when we look into specific features that really make them stand out: like portals, perspective construction, or even rune finding in very original ways. The only minor inconvenience I faced was that for puzzles that play a lot with perspective, the character's speed was a bit annoying. Sometimes it would take me more time getting from one spot to another than actually solving the puzzle; which is my second point, puzzles were a tad bit too easy and repetitive once you got used to the features that once were jaw-dropping. But since it is a relatively short game, you may not face this problem.

On the other hand, we have combat. I actually really enjoyed it. I generally like parry-heavy combat systems and this kind of fits this description (since dodging is only useful against a handful of enemies). My only problem is that enemies just spawn from thin air, making it hard to play with positioning, but again you can just parry (which really rocks because the voices warn you of incoming attacks). Enemy variety is enough, there are not a lot of different types, but some variations are present to keep it a tad more interesting. And personally, I really liked the boss fights, although some are trivialized because of the features you unlock.

Visuals and effects
I love how this game looks, it is astonishing and always managed to creep me out, or cause some sort of repulsion. And it is very well paired with good sound design, and the best of all, the voices. This feature was what originally attracted me to the game, and I will say I quite grew used to having my doubts and fears constantly augmented by murmuring voices. Thanks to the amazing voice actress, I really was transmitted a sense of panic or confusion even when nothing was happening, that helped me immerse further in the experience. I even missed them when they would briefly disappear but at the same time feel an immense sense of relief. This game really played with me.

Story
Throughout the game, we get to know more about the main character and the people that are important to her, which I honestly did not enjoy that much. I found it hard to create a connection with these characters since we only get to know them briefly. What I found myself really getting into, were the pieces of lore distributed all around the map.

Ending Statements
This game is an experience, really linear and cutscene heavy, it suirprises me that I enjoyed it so much. I would not recommend it if you enjoy more gameplay-heavy games or you don't enjoy puzzles, but if you can get over that I highly encourage you to give it a try. Living 7 hours from the perspective of the protagonist and living with her psychosis, was really a unique and worthwhile experience that has really impacted my view.Plus I never had a woman's voice so close to me LMAO. In all seriousness, it is worth a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

The sound, the themes explored (they literally hired psychiatrists and psychologists to ensure that Senua's disorders were accurately depicted), the soundtrack, the visuals... I also preferred the more simplistic yet more methodological combat of this game. The story was also way more up my alley. Preferring GoW is totally valid, but for the price difference, I don't see how GoW would be a better value.

*Though take into consideration that I'm a psychology student and I'm inherently biased towards games that explore themes of mental health in a great way.*

If one day there would be a psychology class asking the room to play a game and explore its themes instead of a book, that would be, to the best of my knowledge, the best game for it.\t\t\t\t\t
