\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had fun playing this! The story was interesting enough to keep me hooked. It takes some time to actually get what's going on but I think it's worth it. Senua is an interesting character and the actress' performance was really captivating. The graphics were really good and especially the character rendering is ahead of it's time. The combat was really fun and simple, which I appreciated, with some very impressive animations. Music was really good as well and felt fitting of the whole setting and story.

Now some of my problems with the game... The puzzles took me a bit to get used to and some of them were a little bit frustrating to solve. Even though the combat is great, there was not enough of it. There's definitely more of it towards the end, but there are some big sections of the game with no combat at all. Something that also bothered me was the movement speed, which could've been a little faster. Lastly, even though my game ran great on my PC (Ryzen 5 5600X, RTX 4070, 16GB RAM), there are a lots of stuttering when entering new areas, possibly because of shader compilation, but other than that the game was running great 80-100 fps @1080p on max settings with ray-tracing.

All in all, if you enjoy story-driven games, I highly recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: Owned here on Steam, but played on console.

The biggest thing I have to say is that Hellblade surprised me with its gameplay.

I went in expecting a heavily combat oriented skill check fest. Instead, I got several hours of an artfully crafted story about a woman on a journey of no return. Senua is on a pilgrimage, and it's conveyed so well.
There's no side content, no collectables, no currency OR EVEN GEAR, no NPCs but the ones in her head and memories.
Ninja theory dialed down the typical action-oriented gameplay, and focused all of their efforts on sound design, environment and boss fights, to convey the story of Senua and her people.

So, don't expect elaborate combat. It's there when the story requires it (and man, does it feel heavy, and important, and majestic), while most of the gameplay relies on puzzles, walking through scenery and listening to the story.
They want you to see the world through Senua's eyes, not get sidetracked with loot and killing off mobs.
That being said, some of the level design choices make the puzzling parts frustrating, or make your next step unclear. I got lost more than a few times, not knowing what to do next. So, having to rely on a guide to go through a few areas really broke the immersion for me.

Ultimately, Hellblade is a story-first, atmospheric game about loss, rejection, family trauma and love, all wrapped up in a nice Viking/Celtic mythos with a horror ribbon on top.

Oh, and absolutely, you'll have no idea if what's happening to Senua throughout the game is in her head or not. And that's kind of the best part. Ninja Theory really made one of the most iconic games of our time, paving the way for so many others we now have.\t\t\t\t\t
