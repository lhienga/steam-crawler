\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to give this game an indifferent review, but since STEAM only allows review with Like and Dislike, I decide to be the bad guy, give it a “Dislike”.

Let me explain:

Do you like fine art? Do you like modern art? Do you like behavior art? If your answer is yes, then this game is for you, and it's an absolutely astonishing masterpiece.
Because this game has the same vibe of modern art. Playing this game reminded me of my visit to an art exhibition, and there were many art short films playing, with artists doing dance, acting or monologue. This game is just like those art films.
I am not an art guy, when I watched those art films, I found it weird and not interesting at all, thus I have the same impression about this game, weird, not interesting.

This game is slooooow. To those people criticizing God Of War: Ragnarok being a walking simulator and movie game, wait until you play Hellblade, this game is the peak of walking simulator and movie game. Everything is slow, you walk slow, the pace is slow, the cut scene acting is slow. And the game often just halts completely to make you solve puzzle, and you walk slowly around the map to find the solution, yet this game tells you nothing helpful, resulting in me getting stuck for multiple times. It’s really boring.

Combat is ok, but as soon as you getting surrounded by enemies, the fight gets really annoying.

This game is 7-8 hours long. It’s more like an interactive movie than a game. I paid 3 USD for this game, so no complaints.

Graphics is good, but this game doesn’t look beautiful, because everything looks so miserable, which is in tune with the setting.

I heard many people praise this game, but personally I don’t like this game. I am just not that kind of person enjoying the overall “modern art” feeling. If I have to put a number on this game: 5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCreepy, atmospheric, just difficult enough. The game punishes your for mistakes, which I like, but not in a way that I got overly frustrated. Failure matters in this game and knowing that just made me work harder and made the perfect parries and flawless fights feel like real accomplishments. If I had one complaint it would be that some of the early lore stones I missed because they were hidden but there was nothing to cue me into that fact since most of them are in plain sight. I would recommend this to anyone that likes a creepy atmosphere, Norse mythology, vikings, or games like God of War. It is not super long (Steam shows I played 4 hours), and the quest is pretty straight forward. So if you are like me and grow weary of the open world overload that so many modern games have, and you just want to sit down to a challenging game with an excellent story then you will not be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
