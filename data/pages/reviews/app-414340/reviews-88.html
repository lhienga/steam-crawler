\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCategories of importance (to me):

1. Gameplay: 7/10. Solid but not groundbreaking.
2. Story: 8/10. Great story with excellent delivery. Can be slow at times and a little convoluted.
3. Originality: 8/10. Exceptional take on some things not a lot of games try to tackle and did so in not a preachy way.
4. Graphics: 9/10. Beautiful game. Some settings slow you down and make you want to just gaze into the artists hard work.
5. Stability: 8/10. Very solid game. Some hiccups here and there but nothing breaking.
6. Modern Wokeness: 9/10. The team that made this game created a game which does not break the immersion with modern political/Social garbage. They made a story and stuck to it while seemingly respecting the culture they were focusing on in the game (take notes, devs of God of War). Some controversy when the game came out was understandable. This small and frail woman is devouring these large and well equipped demonic entities. However, you are defeating her own demons through this journey. I understand why people would find issue with this. The "strong and can do anything woman character trope" we see all too often is a concern and a killer of immersion. The way the devs do it in this game is full of tact and class. It's nothing to worry about. The devs don't shove their political and social proclivities into this game nor down your throat. It is a story based game, it focuses on the story, it's a great story, and it fits well into the era, location, and cultures while taking a few creative "non woke" artistic freedoms.

Over all this game is great. Well worth the money and will be a gem in my collection for the rest of my life.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't had anyone close and near me in my family or friends with mental health issues like psychosis, so I had have no experience on this subject...However, I was so sucked into the story and it made me understand how difficult life like that can be. Ninja Theory constructed a game with the essence being psychosis, and it's just...insane how they depicted it. They said you should put on headphones to get the full experience...and my god, I was right in the middle.

The voices, the sounds, the music...the whispers are right there, As if somebody's just standing there next to me. It's eerie and scary. The visuals are crazy too...everything is alive, and suddnely it isn't. You feel like you're being followed while YOU'RE the one trying to hunt. The puzzles made me go a little annoyed, but It's all part of the process where you want to tie the loose ends and solve the problem, while it slowly drives you mad; It's all part of the game.

And everything you do wrong, every wrong choice Senua makes, you get reminded by it every step by those whispers. It's driving me crazy...just as it should. It's mind blowing.

As for combat, you're able to set the difficulty on automatic, so I feel it helps you a little when you're in a pinch. But even so, it's pretty straight forward.
Overal the game was fun and interesting....but I really had to stop every now and then to collect my mind. It's pretty heavy.

Can't wait for Hellblade 2! And I'm so happy Heilung creates music for these games<3\t\t\t\t\t
