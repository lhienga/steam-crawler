\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story definitely leaves a very solid impression. It does feel that a lot of work has been put into the psychological aspect. The spatial audio of the voices in _your_ has been recorded with care and is definitely high quality. Music is used somewhat sporadically but has been chosen carefully and fits the atmosphere well.

The things that I did not like fully were fighting (it was somewhat all over the place, certain enemies would randomly become harder, other times the game would throw multiple waves of enemies at you for no good reason other than to seemingly prolong the game?) and puzzles (somewhat repetetive, involving a lot of walking and running (which is faster than walking but still slow) and looking for the next thing to "focus" on. Also the game lacked some sort of a tutorial to explain what focus does etc, but I guess it wasn't too hard to pick up on it. Controls and target switching was sort of meh.

Overall I can definitely recommend this game, especially on sale. Solid story. Very good graphics even though DX12 does stutter regularly and this has never been fixed. Acceptable controls/fighting. Good music and great overall audio.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are three things to this game. Puzzles, combat and the sensual experience. It is unique in the way it portrays the story. It's almost ethereal. It's not so much a story that is told as it is a way to convey feelings connected to that story. It's confusing and beautiful. It's convoluted and focused. It's everything at once. And it is incredibly well done.

I'm not much of a fan of the combat. I never liked that twitch-reflex style where you have to learn patterns and push the right buttons at just the right time. I'm not good at it and it frustrates me more than it entertains me. But luckily the game does give me a difficulty setting for this which makes it bearable.

The puzzles are good. You'll be facing different kinds for different parts of the game. And even though they repeat a few times it doesn't grow stale. It is interwoven with Senua's experiences which makes it interesting enough to keep going.

Senua's character herself is pretty amazing. The actress portraying her does an astonishingly good job. And it combines perfectly with the peripheral characters who orbit Senua's consciousness. One is especially grueling to listen to. Quite literally. The developers of this game should pat themselves on the back. Some games are "just games". This is a combination of art and game. And it combines pretty damn well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Masterpiece of Emotional Depth and Gripping Gameplay

Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is an absolute gem that transcends the boundaries of traditional gaming, delivering an experience that is as emotionally profound as it is visually stunning. From the moment I stepped into Senua's hauntingly beautiful world, I was captivated by its unique blend of mythology, mental health exploration, and intense gameplay.

The narrative is an emotional rollercoaster, masterfully weaving together Senua's personal journey with Norse mythology. The developers at Ninja Theory have crafted a tale that explores mental illness with authenticity and sensitivity, creating a protagonist whose struggles resonate on a profound level. The attention to detail in portraying psychosis is not only groundbreaking but also a testament to the dedication of the development team.

Visually, Hellblade is a work of art. The meticulously designed landscapes, atmospheric lighting, and character animations create a world that feels both hauntingly realistic and otherworldly. The sound design deserves special mention, as it plays a crucial role in immersing players into Senua's mind. The binaural audio is nothing short of a masterpiece, providing an eerie and immersive experience that enhances the overall atmosphere.

Gameplay-wise, Hellblade strikes a perfect balance between exploration, puzzle-solving, and combat. The combat system is challenging yet rewarding, and it evolves seamlessly as you progress through the story. The puzzles are cleverly designed, serving as a narrative device that keeps you engaged and invested in Senua's quest.

What truly sets Hellblade apart is its commitment to raising awareness about mental health issues. The inclusion of a permadeath mechanic adds an extra layer of tension, forcing players to confront the fear and anxiety that Senua experiences. This unique approach not only makes the game more immersive but also fosters empathy and understanding for those dealing with mental health challenges.

In conclusion, Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice is a masterpiece that pushes the boundaries of what video games can achieve. It's a profound and emotionally resonant experience that leaves a lasting impact. Ninja Theory has not only delivered an exceptional game but also contributed to an important conversation about mental health. A must-play for anyone seeking a thought-provoking and unforgettable gaming experience.
