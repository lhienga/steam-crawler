My thoughts:
Hellblade is a narrative driven, third-person adventure game with some light puzzle solving elements. It has top notch presentation and sound design which combine together seamlessly along with a captivating performance by Milena Juergens (Senua) to produce an experience that is uniquely suited to the video game medium. If a piece of media can elicit a strong emotional reaction it is something special. And in this, Hellblade has done its job particularly well, leaving me still thinking about the game several days after finishing it.

However, there is one caveat to my recommendation. This game is unrelentingly depressing. As such, I wouldn't play it unless you are in the right mind frame. Mental illness features heavily as a theme and may also be a trigger for some people.

What I like:
  • The Story
    The story gets revealed to you in pieces throughout the game. There are a lot of emotionally heavy themes at play here and they are done respectfully. Mental illness and the stigma associated with it feature prominently. Another theme is coming to terms with the acceptance of the loss of a loved one.
  • Sound Design
    Ninja Theory used binaural audio capture to replicate the voices that Senua hears in her head. In addition to learning what it is like for her it also aids in some interesting gameplay design. For example, you might hear a voice during a fight to help you. Something like "Watch out!" or "You're surrounded!". The voices would also give you conflicting information during puzzles that made you question your decisions. It's a very unique and interesting experience that I haven't had in a game ever before.
  • The Soundtrack
    The soundtrack and score perfectly represent your lonesome journey through Hell. I remember being on a desolate beach at one point with shipwrecks all around me and hearing the waves lapping on the shore, the wind blowing through the wreckage, and the score adding to the atmosphere. It felt surreally immersive. The final boss battle music was epic and I've been playing it on Spotify a lot lately.
  • Presentation
    Having no HUD is interesting. It looks very clean. It tripped me up a couple of times though because I'm so used to seeing a button prompt to open a door or interact with an object.
  • Powerful Technology
    The technology to bring the player model to life in the game is amazing. They used some way of scanning the actress' face and body in real life then put her digitally into the game with amazing success. Using mocap on top of that for realistic animations and movement, the result is a very convincing portrayal of Senua as a real person. You get fidelity to see every emotion, every facial tick on her expression that helps sell her emotions.
  • Animations
    I'm not sure if they mocapped the combat animations but I found them to be very satisfying. Especially the parry move.
  • Combat
    I've read reviews that say it's too simplistic. I enjoyed it though without getting bored.
  • Bosses
    I really enjoyed the creature design, especially the bird dude and giant wolf.
  • Milena Juergens!
    She was Ninja Theory's video editor and ended up taking on the role of Senua. She never acted before but hits it out of the park. I don't think the game would have been the same with out her.
What I dislike:
  • Camera Issues
    Maybe related to my ultrawide resolution but sometimes my camera would clip through walls during tight areas.
