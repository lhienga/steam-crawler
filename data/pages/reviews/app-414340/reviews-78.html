\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was presented to me as an action horror game, but it's a walking sim with sloppy combat and some non-gamer's idea of puzzling tacked on. I love walking sims, so even if someone told me that, I'd have been down, but I'm telling you: this game is boring. I appreciate high concept depictions of psychosis, as someone who's experienced psychosis. But it's hard to care about the atmosphere when you're stuck in a tight, simple, repetitive game loop, and the camera is stuck right behind the protagonist's shoulder. Am I in a rich, immersive world, or on an arcade rail? I guess it's supposed to be both somehow. And I've never liked the over-the-shoulder 3rd person cam, but I'll put up with it in a shooter where I can adjust it. I guess I won't for close combat and world-gazing. Does the game loop get better later? Nothing left me curious enough to find out.

Anyway, I really wanted to like this game, but the first hour was so bland and cliched design-wise that I had to get up and do something else. I'm glad this game helped people but I'd be embarrassed to ask anyone to play it. Just have your apathetic parents watch A Beautiful Mind or something.\t\t\t\t\t
