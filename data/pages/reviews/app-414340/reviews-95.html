\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game definitely has its ups and downs but overall is a great experience especially when you can get it for around 4 USD on a sale nowadays I see no reason to pass up playing this at least once.
The story is great, the art design, the combat, the characters, everything feels very fluid and interesting this game has its fair share of interesting takes on story telling and combat and they all surprisingly work very well. This games biggest issue is its home to some of the most grating story parts I have played in so long, parts that when you do them you honestly are begging them to end before you put the game down, but stick it out and you'll be rewarded with a great story and ending.
I only hope the sequel can take the best parts of this game and keep going and make the worst parts a bit more tolerable. Overall a great game go in expecting some frustration but for the price and the quality its definitely worth it and worth playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom the story (and the very cheap price tag) alone I recommend playing it. However the useful life of this game is exactly 1 playthrough (6-8 hours). All other mechanics are mediocre. Combat is sparse, predictable, and so easy to be interesting (I played on hardest). Puzzles are repetitive (find runes, go through illusion portals, find more runes, align shapes, find even more runes, etc). There's a LOT of walking involved.

The story carries everything else for you to enjoy this game once, then there's no reason to play it again, which is fair, however trailers and whatnot like to showcase as if there is a lot of action involved, but there isn't. This is more like a movie game. Also the voices always whispering nonsense is VERY annoying. I get it's supposed to be accurately showing mental illness, but it makes me not touch it again once I went through the story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is game was heavy, brutal, full of gore, and yet, hauntingly beautiful.

Starting off with what i didn't like about the game, the combat and core gameplay itself is relatively weak, with very shallow mechanics and the worst camera auto-lock i've ever experienced. also playing the final encounter with Ray-Tracing on dropped my framerate from 160 to 20 because of all the particles XD.

But that's about it for negatives, now moving on to the positive aspects, this is a very deep and well thought story, not the kind that you easily forget in a few days after being done with the game. The world design shows that a lot of work went into it and does a great job in communicating the feeling of darkness and desolation that the game's atmosphere has.
it's a very short game (about 5.5h to complete for me), which is good because this allows you to fully experience the amazing story this title offers before getting completely burnt out by the very basic and repetitive combat encounters.

In conclusion, this is not a good action game, and if you're looking for that, then this title just isn't for you. What this game truly has to offer, is a beautiful cinematic experience with a deep and emotional story behind that will leave you with more questions than answers by the time you're done with it.

Solid 8.5/10 for me, definetely worth a buy, especially when on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
