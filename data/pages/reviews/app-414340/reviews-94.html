\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade: Senua's Sacrifice provides a new experience in the realm of gaming. Ninja Theory as a developer brings mental illness within the concept. They try to convey the message that mental illness has existed for a long time, which is the setting of this game during the Viking era.

If you think this is a hack & slash adventure game, no, you are wrong. The selling point that they want to show in Hellblade is the anxiety, confusion and doubt that exist within Senua.

I thought the 8 hour gameplay actually was short. It seems that this duration was created to minimize boredom and repetition for players when completing puzzles. The flow is like this:

Walking through new place-> solve puzzles -> defeat bosses -> progress story (and continues like that).

Nevertheless, this game is worth buying. Moreover, there will be a sequel in 2024!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade is a fun and pretty quick cinematic experience. Definitely worth the 5 bucks I bought it for. I wouldn't have wanted to spend to much more though. I don't have too much to say about it, overall it's a decent game. It has great audio, the combat does what it's supposed to even though it's pretty bland and I wasn't a fan of the forced semi 1v1 fights. It tells a story that kept me entertained throughout the whole game. The way the game was paced had me ready for it to be over. That's something I hope they tune for the second. The game is only 6.5-7 hours and by the end I was so ready to be done. I definitely recommend the game to anyone who is interested. Especially on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery much recommended, for a price of €10 or something like it ! Wait for a discount to EXPERIENCE this wonderful 8-ish hour adventure.

I don't usually write reviews (I made two in over a decade) but Hellblade is something special. It's full of mindtricks and weird little things that seem both normal and strange; The game makes you accept and pay attention to things that twist your brain, while it also tells you to ignore those and fight against them (quite literaly) at the same time.
Confused? Don't really understand how it's possible you understood that?...... Welcome to Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice.

This is your type of game if you want atmosphere and story; and don't shy away from psychological horrors. The way this game uses it's dark and uncommon subject (mental health) for storytelling, character- and worldbuilding and even as a means of progression and immersion, is truly incredible. (I don't think I'll use the word 'madness' again for some time, not after this experience). It's never really scary but the feeling of threat is everywhere and almost constant.
Also the fighting (a slow, deliberate pace and easy to master) and most game mechanics are very much enjoyable, even if they aren't perfect.

Two very noteworthy thing:
1) The mind♥♥♥♥s this game creates in my head, through the voices and visions.
You never know what to believe or what to expect. Even with the constant advices and oppinions and a quest that seems clear and linear, many twist and turns left me exclaiming a huge WTF.
A quick example, during the first minutes of the game: After playing many 'platforming games', I never expected to have a fear of hights during a videogame!
2) The mythology, stories and ways of explaining the world.
This all seems to be correct, as far as this enthousiast for Norse mythology and Celtic/Nordic history can tell. This game's lore is a nice introduction into the rich pantheon and worldviews of the old Celtic/Nordic people.

As a round-up: This is a beautiful, haunting and very immersive game; Hard to swallow sometimes, but accessible enough for most middle of the road players. A rough gem, but of the kind that doesn't need a polish to shine and be beautiful!\t\t\t\t\t
