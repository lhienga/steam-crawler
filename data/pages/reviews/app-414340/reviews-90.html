\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLess of a game and more of an interactive movie. It seems the glowing reviews are more about it standing as some sort of holy altar toward mental health rather than an objective gaming experience. The gameplay is as light as can be: combat with three attack buttons, a block, and a dodge with some puzzles. That's it. The feel of combat is actually good, but after a few battles, it's just repetitive and dull.

No doubt the visuals are excellent along with the voice acting, although all the whispering is obnoxious. Obviously it's going for a "psychosis sim", if you will, but it grates on your nerves. Play with headphones at your own peril.

If you want an actual game, this is not worth the money unless it's on a big sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHellblade is a game I can barely dignify with a proper review, because words don't come to me when I think of it, infact, not a single constructive thought does. Funny enough, I may call it a "game" but it's truly testing the definition, being that it's more of a seven hour exposition dump littered with riddles and other incoherent nonsense. And while it uses some admittedly impressive visual tricks to add to its nightmarish world and atmosphere, every time I catch myself thinking positively it's only but a moment before the pure hatred seeps back in due to some aspect of it's rather egregious design. The combat is heavy and deliberate but also desperately lacking in complexity and depth, and the encounters do nothing but accentuate it's repetitive, simple nature. The puzzles are, to put it bluntly, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. So what is this "game" then? If not the most boring seven hours of my game related year so far.

A grief stricken, warriors journey into the grips of hell as she faces inner demons and actual, very real ones. Sounds cool? I know, I thought so too. But this game does not match any expectation I had of it, and you wouldn't miss much if you watched a good recap video since it'd be a very similar experience.

6/10.

(Played on a R7 5700X/RTX 4070 combo at 1440p 'Very High' with RT set to 'High' and DLSS set to 'Quality', using an Xbox Series Controller.) \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA face painted with battle paint, anger and determination in the eyes, a heroic quest ahead. But don't be fooled. Senua, the protagonist from the Ninja Theory studio, shows war blue not only the will to kill, but also probably tries to hide her darkness behind it and banish the whispering demons. In vain. Hellblade is a properly psychotic action in which you will never be sure if you are facing a real threat or the fruits of the main character's twisted mind.

However, anyone expecting pure butchery would be wrong. Of course, you'll also see situations where soul-searching goes by the wayside and swords stuck in heads come to the fore, but Hellblade certainly can't be declared a concentrated action.
It is more of a psychological study. Which is a phrase that creates the impression of a dry monograph written in a professional style, the reading of which will not only give you information about psychosis, but will also cause it. But nothing could be further from the truth. Hellblade is a ballad about insanity dressed in a damn catchy jacket, which sometimes runs into the field of unadulterated horror.

Senua travels to the underworld of Norse mythology to save a loved one's soul. But the apparent main plot line is actually just a kind of cotton wool in the background. That is, a symbolism under which something completely different can easily be imagined. A story about dealing with one's own fear, struggling with declining mental health, and reflecting on how incredibly little it takes for the human mind to slide into a spiral of hopelessness and rot. In other words, Senua herself is the main plot line here.
Hellblade is an excellent, emotionally charged poem about the real and imagined darkness inside people. Unique, fresh, gorgeous and scary. Play it. 25% \t\t\t\t\t
