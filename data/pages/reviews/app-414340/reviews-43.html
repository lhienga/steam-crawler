The longer version is that I really was worried that this game would not take psychosis seriously, or use it as a crutch. I have experience working with individuals that suffer from Schizophrenia and treating them in therapy and in some training to simulate hearing voices they will have someone stand behind you with a funneled paper and whisper to you while you try to talk to someone. I felt like that was happening during this whole game. The combat is ok, and I do like that when you press pause it shows you the controls because there is no UI, no menu's and even the introduction of the "perma death" is a lie. I love that concept and I love that this game really makes you feel like you are going through hell and not just a "hell in your mind". That being said I really didn't like two of the Trials of Odin. The swamp one in particular I think I died three or four times and while I did figure it out rather quickly and avoid the monster I did feel like the range it had to one shot you was kind of bigger than it needed to be. Other than some moments like that I was blown away and I cannot recommend this game enough.\t\t\t\t\t
