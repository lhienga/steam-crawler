\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished Goddess of War 2017: Senua's Sacrifice.

I kind of liked it. Also kind of didn't.

I think the graphics look great, I liked especially the gods' / monsters' / creatures' design, in that they deviated a bit from the typical fantasy designs where everything looks super heroic and clean and really nice. They went with a more "natural" design, I think, everything is dirty, rotten, broken, and made of things a person from that era would use (rags, bones, sticks, animal carcasses, etc.). Also Hela doesn't look like Cate Blanchett.

I don't think the music is anything special, but the sound design is superb. Oh, and I know that it's kind of the point, but the voices were SUPER annoying. Also helpful, sometimes.

The story didn't click with me, it felt... meh. Didn't like or care much about the characters (more like THE character). Too much drama / suffering / grief for me, but that's my problem, not the story's. I knew that Senua had severe daddy issues (besides her mental condition) way before they tell you and that the "enemy" was his father, and I'm not the kind of guy who foresees what's gonna happen in a story.

Also the narrators felt or monotonous or exaggerated, depending on who was narrating. Mimir didn't speak much. A shame, I liked his stories in the other games.

The puzzles: ugh. Most were easy, I got stuck in only one or two. But every time I saw them coming I said "Not another one of those..." to myself. There are A LOT of puzzles, very repetitive, very boring, too many of them.

Combat: Okay-ish, not too bad, not too good, repetitive, predictable, always the same enemies except for the bosses. Also they repeat a few of the bosses, like games used to do in the 80s / 90s.

I think it can be a good experience (I don't know about "game", but experience) for those who like this type of story, appreciate good graphics and sound design, and don't care much about combat or LOTS of repetitive puzzles.

I don't know if I would play the sequel. I think maybe not.

Also it was only 2 dollars and change, can't complain much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolute masterpiece, in my opinion.

Gameplay is a mix of light-medium traversal puzzles that feel unique, and combat scenarios with focus on dodging side-to-side, blocking and light combo attacks with a very satisfying parry system. The fights are very personal and in your face. There are no visual indicators on the enemies attacks aside from their own arm movement, which forces you to really pay attention to their animations and what side of the body the swing is coming from to properly dodge, parry or block the correct side. The stripped down combat is enhanced and stands out by the sound design of the swords and Senua herself. Simple combat, but extremely visceral,grimy and intense. The final 'boss' being a massive highlight.

Senua suffers from schizophrenia. Gameplay wise it feels really unique relying on the voices in her\\your head (headphones absolutely a requirement) to dodge attacks from enemies out of your view. The voices serve more than just combat, though. They give Senua someone to interact with and talk to - fleshing out the story, world and character tenfold. The voices are ever present during puzzles or traversal, playing both as the devil and angel on your shoulder. It is a constant struggle Senua has to endure. It's really well done and unique.

The story says a lot. Senua is exiled from her community, and basically a normal life, because of her mental illness. People in that era weren't aware of schizophrenia, so it makes sense that they viewed her as a freak and an anomaly for having voices in her head. And Senua feels that way about herself too. The game focuses a lot on the journey of acceptance with herself and with her husband that she is so desperately trying to save from the cruel Norse Gods of her world.

The presentation of the game is very heavy. It's full of misery, blood, and darkness. You fight humanoid creatures and entities related to the world of Norse Mythology. It's a game where you always feel like you're losing for how much torment Senua goes through in her story.

A 10\\10, on all fronts, for me. I'm beyond excited for the sequel as the ambiance and grittiness seems to be increased from what has been shown.\t\t\t\t\t

Now about the combat. It's a really simple and widespread system with light/heavy attacks, dodge and block timing. Also you have an ability to slow down the world and enemies around you, but preserve your speed at the same time. It's not really something outstanding, but it's not bad and I had some good fights, so the game has +karma here. Also the combat is really easy, like I died, maybe, a few times and I played on the hard difficulty. Whether this is good or bad is up to.

Also I really dislike that the game has a heavy "railroading" feeling. What I mean is everything is very animation heavy. You can't just jump, if you want, you need to wait for animations to finish, sometimes they are really slow, etc. But I suppose that's a matter of a taste.

-I won't tell much about the story, but I can say that they wanted to go hard on the "mental illness" part and they did it well. Also I really liked Druth character, others were fine, I guess. My only problem is that it picks up around the second hour of the game.

-Artstyle is gorgeous. Landscapes are beautiful and final sequence is just out of this world. You can play the game just to see it, it's worth it.

-OST. It's good. I really liked the final sequence music. Won't say much about it, find it on the Internet, if you want.

-I can't tell much about the technical aspect, cause I play on the pretty old hardware. There weren't any serious bugs, all I can say.

In the conclusion, I can recommend it, but beware, that first 2-3 hours can be a real slog. Then the story picks up and it becomes actually good(I know it sounds like a meme). Also it's dirty cheap on sales, so why don't give it a try, but I think it's well worth the full price.

8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
