\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCutscene The Game
Challenge: take a shot every time you hear "darkness".

This is a visually stunning game. The visuals and sounds really sold the idea that you were in some gigantic lair of a god. A nice detail was that I could still see the bridge and other buildings later explored through the warrior trials, from Surtr/Valravn's domain, adding to the feel that it wasn't smaller areas loaded as you played them, but rather one large grey hell.

My favourite part of the game was the lorestones and hearing the stories from Druth; excellent casting. Overall, all the voice acting and binaural sound design with regards to the furies etc was exceptional as well.

I will admit that the beginning of the game was quite frustrating for a while. Finding those runes was a real killer, especially in Surtr's domain, because you're still learning how it works and they're not in consistent places (like I expected them all to be aerial, after the introduction, so the R on the ground took me forever to find). However, by the time I reached the warrior trials, they were a lot easier to identify and the new ways they were concealed in the mountain also kept the hunting for runes fresh.

My favourite area/chapter was the warrior trials of Odin. I think many will agree that the blindness shard was a novel, anxiety-inducing chapter in the game, in the best way.

The combat was mostly alright, involving a lot of timed dodges and parries, using focus to attack mist enemies etc. It didnt develop into something wild, with a wide range of attacks or something, but I think that was for the best. The combat would come at any time and ,with enemies spawning in outside your field of view, could feel quite claustrophobic. I don't have a problem with this though, as it was by design (listening to the furies about threats being unseen doesn't mean they aren't there and watching the feature showing the making of the game explains that the point is to put you in that place of anxiety and frustration). Honestly, my only actual criticism of the combat was the locking on of enemies. The switching of targets only seemed to work on those within your centre fov, making you vulnerable to the enemies just to the side that would attack. In time though, dodging mitigated that problem so I don't actually have any changes that I would make to the combat, just sharing my struggle.

Chapter selection to gather missed lorestones was also a really nice option.

The team that made this game can be proud of themselves as I feel it was perfectly made to meet the goal that was behind it. Involving members of the community in the mental health field and people living with psychosis to help shape Senua's experiences was a quality choice and it really shows the love and effort that went into making this game.

10/10 the game hits the target it was aiming for, even if not everyone understands the message behind the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is... interesting. But is it enjoyable? I wouldn't say so.

That being said, I don't think a game with the purpose of transcribing the experience of dementia should be enjoyable.

I believe it is good at what it does: the voices, the feeling of utter confusion and the bone-chilling moments when Senua looks you straight in the eyes are very effective. Even if I kinda wish the game would explore these feelings further.
However, the combat was lukewarm and the rune puzzle things were an absolute chore.

In the end, I am very glad this game exists, and I glad I played it. I think you should play it too. But I don't want to play it again.
