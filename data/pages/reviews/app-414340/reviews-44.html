\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an absolute ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ♥♥♥♥. I've watched the feature movie and it didn't help.
Representation aside, I had zero immersion for several reasons and at the end I was absolutely annoyed at the game (yeah, some dialogues were beautiful and charming, but anything else...).
Banal and boring perspective puzzles. Yeah, psychosis, yadda yadda, but everything barely requires thinking or creativity and will purely be replaced by walking, which comes to...
Senua is slow as hell. Walking, running, exploring is absolutely excruciating. I was getting older every minute of trying to get somewhere. There is plenty of backtracking and the game weirdly linear.
Fights are agonizingly boring. You have around 5 types of enemies (counting generously) with exactly same moveset and their only strategy is [bold]maybe[/bold] get behind your back (if they didn't spawn behind you) and only difference between fights is their combination and amount. So by the end of the game sometimes you will be flooded by enemies and your only strategy is dodge, hit and focus. Bossfights are almost nonexistent. There are... Three? Four? You'll fight weak copies at the end anyway, so let's say three and a half fights and only one of them feels unique/
Developers chose Celtic/Nordic theme and they didn't even try to put any effort into music. I think I liked the game before that one fight where I heard some Viking metal far away which stopped with the fight to be never heard again. Same goes with the general vibe. The game is either music-less or there is some off-the-wall ♥♥♥♥. I think I heard music thrice: that Viking metal bit, some post-rock during, ehhhh, trials and some indie during the [bold]unskippable and unscrollable[/bold] credits. UPD: Apparently there's supposed to be music, but it didn't work in my case. OST is okay. My point still stands.
And about voices. I was absolutely fascinated at the beginning, because that's some quality ASMR. But with the time it became somewhat annoying (well, that's probably the point) and I wouldn't mention it if the voices were properly scripted. Instead I've stuck listening to never ending blabbering of "narrator", trying to restart lore bits (which are stuck to the place you've activated them, so no continued exploration while you listening to this ♥♥♥♥) and triggering some waypoints which in turn played voices relevant to the time I passed that waypoint for the first time/
All in all it feels like half-assed adventure/movie where most effort was put into exploration of psychosis which could be set in any time/period (because outside of some attributes, "relevant details", and "lore" we aren't exploring anything historically relevant. It felt too long for what it was (and was very short overall) and I think I felt myself taken hostage by this game.
Play it if you like, but I won't go through it again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually don't play games like this (short and linear games) but I had this one on my radar for a long time and since it was really cheap, I finally bought it and gave it a try, and I was surprised at how much I enjoyed this game.

And it's funny that one of the things I liked most was the fact that it's linear and simple to follow.

The sound design is simply one of a kind and it gave me legit chills at times, the soundtrack perfectly represents the nordic vibes and Senua's loneliness in the journey and the story is amazing and I can't wait for the sequel.

While walking, the animations are really good and the facial expressions of Senua are amazing, you can truly feel her emotions through them. And in combat the animations are smooth and the combos are very well done.

The boss fights are really good aswell, but the one thing that I kinda dislike is the camera/lock-on system. It works fine, but if you have multiples enemies on the screen, switching between targets can be a little frustrating and the snap when switching can be a bit immersion breaking. But in general, the controlls and combat are good.

Lastly, the puzzles. They are pretty simple but really well made and are good moments to relax after intense fights.

I can't wait to see what Senua's Saga: Hellblade II has in store for us.

(Also, thank f'ing Odin for Chapter Select, I missed 4 lorestones and thankfully now you don't have to start all over again just to see the additional cutscene and receive the achievement.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game for story, graphics/ambiance, and the psychological/Norse mythology theme.

However, this is one of those games where you wonder if it would be better as a movie. I will say you do feel more invested in Senua's story playing as the character. I found a lot of the gameplay itself to be tedious, just trying to get through it to get to the next story part (which is usually not long in between). The gameplay has some basic light action RPG fighting, some puzzles, and walking or SLOWLY jogging to get to the next area (often walking back and forth in the same area to solve the environmental puzzles that can be quite clever but often become tedious). Maybe these drawbacks will be improved in the sequel, otherwise I feel like it would be better off as an Until Dawn or Telltale style game (although there are not any different choices to make in this game).
