\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played for 7 or 8 hours.

It's unique, and I'm glad it was made, but it's not fun and it doesn't mean anything to me. I personally feel like I'm wasting my time while playing it.

The game does not modulate tension very well. Games need sections of high tension, followed by sections of low tension. If you don't have these variations in tension, then the player gets used to it and it no longer becomes tense. This lack of variance in tension and lack of variance in tone causes the player to become bored and does not let them experience a range of emotions while playing the game.

This modulation of tension can be seen between sections of danger and sections of safety. The first half of Resident Evil 7 is a great example of a game that does this well. The sections of danger is when you travel out into the mansion and Jack's presence is very tense and scary, but as soon as you enter a safe room soothing music plays and the player knows that they have all the time that they need in order to take a break, plan their next move, manage their items, heal, whatever else they need to do. If the tension gets too high the player can make their way to a safe room to temporarily release some tension.

Games also need variations in "tone" between sections of the game. Silent Hill 2 is a good example of a game that does this well. The player goes from the very high tension, scary, dark Apartment section, to a comparatively low tension walk around town with Maria. In this section you have a companion. You're in a bright environment with lots of room to manoeuvre. Then you go to the bowling alley and get to experience the funny, goofy scene with Eddie eating pizza.

These two games do a great job of modulating the tension and the mood or tone between sections of the game.

Sennua's Sacrifice does not do this. It's always a dour affair with Senua constantly seeing visions or showing how fearful she is. And after a while the player learns that they really aren't in any danger outside of the combat sections. The non-combat sections are all smoke and mirrors. The player eventually learns that they aren't actually in any danger when the village is burning down or whatever.\t\t\t\t\t
