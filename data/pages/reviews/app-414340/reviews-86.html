\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was a linear but interesting 'story rich' game. The main premise is that it's a fairly standard third person slow paced hack-and-slash action game (not to different from modern God of War, minus the teen sidekick scrappy and plus a strong horror premise) where you are tasked with guiding a savage warrior woman on her quest to overcome her schizophrenia in order to resurrect her dead lover.

Senua is plagued by horrific mind-induced terrors and hallucinations both deadly and obstructive, all while accompanied by a cacophony of loud and often unhelpful (but occasional tactful) voices in her mind. There are also certain elements of the gameplay that are designed to freak out and fake out the player, such as ominous phrases like 'if the rot progresses too far, her story will be over', and having the voices whisper that Senua's torch is about to go out, leaving the player to figure out what is true and what is just misdirection (if you don't want to 'spoil' the experience by looking up a guide). As thriller horror genres go... you either like it or you don't or you have mixed feelings about it like I do.

Gameplay-wise, the combat challenges are serviceable while the puzzles are initially novel but quickly become tedious.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThanks to the 2023 Game Awards, I discovered "Hellblade." The trailer for the second part was so impressive that I felt compelled to try out the first.

About The Game

Simply amazing. I don't feel the need to add any other words. The story delves deeply into Norse mythology, and the atmosphere, visuals, sounds, everything is just mind-blowing.

The combat felt somewhat unusual at the beginning, as the game doesn't introduce you to the gameplay mechanics. Initially, I was concerned that the combat was overly simplistic. However, once I realized that it's not just about attacking but also includes parrying, dodging, and more, all my concerns about the combat dissipated.

Won't say anything more; I can't wait to play the second part.\t\t\t\t\t
