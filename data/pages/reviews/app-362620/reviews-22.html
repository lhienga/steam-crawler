\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 8 years in early access this game STILL has a utterly useless tutorial that actually tells you to do things that will actively make your first few custom built software projects a tedious, confusing mess.

Case in point, the tutorial tells you how important marketing is and when you should start certain marketing stages, but completely omits to that you absolutely should NOT market your first software yourself and instead use a publisher. Supposedly this is because you have minimal reputation and therefore any marketing effort will have almost no effect but will cost you money.

As a result I watched my first hire marketer spend a year attempting to just produce a simple text based press release and then failing by missing the recommended deadlines while continuing to cost the company precious money. After trawling through the Steam discussions for an hour or so I find multiple people with similar experiences are recommended to not hire marketers at all, and just pay a publisher only for the first few software. Why TF is this not even mentioned in the tutorial?

More generally the major problem is that while my marketer was typing away actually doing SFA for a year there was absolutely zero useful feedback about the effectiveness or otherwise of his marketing.

In real life even a braindead new marketer would be posting on forums, tweeting, setting up a discord, submitting an ad the relevant industry orgs etc AND THEN checking up on how many people noticed/read their efforts and keeping track of this. I cannot believe this game does not give some feedback on tasks like this so you can judge while marketing continues whether it is worthwhile or not. Instead you get to watch your idiot marketer spend months years supposedly working on something and then failing to complete it anyway!

I get that this is still early access and supposedly a single developer but there is just no excuse for having major game mechanics STILL so poorly explained and with so little feedback. It wasted my time as a player doing something that was not even slightly enjoyable.

If you have hours to spend failing at game tasks, reading forums to find out what was in the developers mind and then spending more hours trying again this time playing the game the way the developer wants, you might like this game. For anyone else I don't recommend the game at all.\t\t\t\t\t
