\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun little game with a lot of depth. The Developers have been actively working on this for the past few years that I’ve owned it, with consistent updates and a fair amount of added/expanded content. I genuinely believe this will be one of the few early access games that will make it to a full, and polished release state as it is fairly polished, and stable as is.
There is a small, but productive modding community behind it that have helped expand it, and add more hours of playtime. I definitely recommend this if you are into these types of games, but are hesitant because of the early access tag.

The game itself sets you up as a small time developer where you can develop all kinds of software including, but not limited to, games or a whole operating system (think Microsoft Windows, or the Macintosh), and slowly expand into a big time developer in your own right. You can have servers to host your internet-based products with bandwidth management. There is a lot-based building system where you can expand into neighbouring lots and slowly take over an entire town, or just move away from the town into the big city if that’s your desire. You can specialise products to fill niches in order to capitalise on an untapped section of a crowded market. There is, of course, staff and wage management. Overall, it has a lot of depth and I’ve thoroughly enjoyed my purchase, especially given the small asking price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
This game has done something that few managment games have done, and probably on accident. Software Inc. has overcome the exponential growth problem that plagues many managment games. In Software Inc. both the beginning and end are challenging to some level, and while the end is definitely easier, it's not too easy because automating stuff is clunky and inefficient. Automating software development in this game is a weird puzzle and you constantly have to adjust the parameters as the team changes. The beginning also is fun because you are not waiting endlessly for money. The games runs very smooth, especially in the beginning, so you can easily skip the downtime.

Some other small things: The game offers a lot of replayability, fun puzzle and management elements. it also offers a lot of creative freedom but only if you want to bother with that. Difficulty is high, especially when playing for the first time so I would advise to start on easy or medium difficulty and trying a few different things.

Cons:
The automating part might me a fun puzzle, you first need to understand it and that is not very easy to do. Staff like cleaning and maintenance is a nightmare to manage as your company grows.

One thing that really annoys me is that with a few features you already reach 100% of potential customers, and you can just release that same piece of software for eternity. I would like to see a little more in depth market simulation. It is already quite good in many ways, but this is still lacking. What I would like to see is customers that look for a specific set of features and have a maximum price. That way you can sell multiple versions of the same software. You might release a 2D editor focused on pixel art, one focused on video editing and one high end piece of software for the professionals. And maybe twenty years down the line the market has changed, and no one wants pixel art anymore, or perhaps customer expetations go up as the market grows. This would add a giant but fun aspact to the game and not much would have to change at all!

Conclusion:
Amazing game good for hours, altough sometimes a bit clunky and hard to understand.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough the game can be very satisfying and fun to play at times, the fun slowly progresses into frustration as I tediously attempt to make a distribution system.

The addition of conveyor belts sounds good in theory and I applaud such an effort, but in its current state I find it non-intuitive and barely enjoyable to play. I spend most of my time hopelessly battling the building system to do what I want. I long back to the times when all you needed was a printer and a pallet in the same room - done.

The building system definitely needs an overhaul. Why can't I put a room inside a room? Why can't I just place walls? Why do I have to fence an area in order to build outdoors? These are all non-intuitive bugs which just completely ruin the experience for me. Please fix these issues before adding another broken "feature".

The first few hours are the best, no complaints there. But as soon as I am required to expand, the experience degrades radically for me.\t\t\t\t\t
