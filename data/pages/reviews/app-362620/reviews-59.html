\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent Game-some notes (the game is very much still being worked on, so do not let these deter you, it's a very worthwhile purchase)
-Needs Researching Overhall. Game randomly cancels research when other companies file patents, yet when you check the patent screen, no one has the patent, so it makes no sense why research would be stopped. Also, please bring back dedicated research in place of the new by year generic!
-When it comes to HR and Project Management, it would be nice if 1.) we could set a team dedicated only to project management, meaning it can only be assigned to a single project, and 2.) if in the HR screen, we could have the recommended amount on the side, so we know which one to set it to without opening a separate window. Also, having the option to automatically hire the recommended amount of programmers
-HR again- separating the specialties for programmers, art, and design, as well as designating sub leads (perhaps up to 3 or 4) to be able to take over a project when the lead is out, say sick or on vacation.
-Being able to have headphones and productivity boosts on the same table; I want my guys to use a calculator and jam out at the same time
-For the hardcore players, maybe an option to have to have someone research tech before its possible release. New servers, computers, and other items should have to be invented before being purchased, and someone should be responsible for researching them and profiting, even if it is a competitor. Also, more increments on these would be nice and more continuous.
-Being able to switch into an in-depth view of a subsidairy would be nice, or even being able to ask them to design a certain product or research tech.
-Automatic porting would be great. If it could be set up as an option or say, any products still being supported get ported to a certain IP, that would save time, especially with product management.
-As an extra hazard, government taxes and anti-monopoly lawsuits should be fun! Floods, snow storms, and other natural disasters would be nice.
-Setting up the skill rate on hiring as more continous, up to say 100% or 95%. Diminishing returns on salary costs would be a balancing act.
-Effectiveness of teams on any given tasks, with breakdowns on negative and postive effectives. Too many people? Show it! Too many tasks? Let us know! Is that chair performing miracles? We want more!
-Team groups. I'd like to be able to sort my teams up by Support, Marketing, Project Management, etc. without scrolling through a list of thirty or more in the mid-late game.\t\t\t\t\t
