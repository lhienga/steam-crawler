\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat, amazing, terrific, and overwhelming. These are the words that come to mind during the first 4 hours of game play. After that? Well drop off the overwhelming and replace it with simply-complex. The game is quite simple once you get past the overbearing nature of so many menus and statistics. And overtly enjoyable. Whether I'm playing as a single dude printing off hundreds of thousands of copies of software, or playing as a 50 employee company developing AAA games, or even a couple of deadheads getting together to make game cheats (workshop mods) I always had a blast.

The sheer detail and scope of what this game is and can be is insanely impressive. I would like to give this game the best rating and review possible because it is simply my favorite micro-manager of all time. I get a feeling of accomplishment when I play this game that very few games can provide in this modern era of games like FO76, Cyberpunk 2077, Harvest Moon: One World, GTA Trilogy: DE, Evil Inside, Balan Wonderworld, ect. ect. ect.... It really makes me feel like I'm going back in time a little bit to the good old days of 1995-2012 when developers actually wanted you to try in their game to achieve something that was never intended with the game. That kind of open ended design philosophy and whole heartedly caring for the videogame (and not the money) is exactly what I get here and is one of the reasons I get such a sense of accomplishment and joy when my company releases it's first hit piece of software. I can not say this enough but the developer really really tried to make this game into something that you want to play for hours, days, and weeks on end.

However now that you know just how much I love this game I must also tell you that the game does have it's fair share of issues. A more streamlined tutorial would be a great place to start. I would suggest adding a tutorial mode in which I can select a specific aspect of the game to focus on and get a more detailed tutorial on that specific feature. I do like how the current tutorial system works but it can be annoying and at times hard to understand. Though all in all this is a very minor complaint. There are also a few glitches I've ran into that I'm sure the developer is more than aware of and working towards fixing as I'm writing this. (For instance the whole not wanting to use an unassigned chair so I have to delete and replace the chair just to get my employee to work. Can be very annoying when I'm working with 50 chairs.)

There are also balance issues in this case I would like to specifically point out the printing of software. This is an extremely easy and cheeky way to make money. Build big box, put printers and conveyors in box, build small office with everything to make one person happy in it and assign to ceo, hire couriers, profit, place more printers and conveyors, profit more. Now the simplicity isn't the problem it's the fact that it makes a stupid amount of money. When doing this or even simply using this to beef up income the game poses no challenge at all.

Now as a final thought I would like to give the dev an idea that makes complete sense to me and I have no idea why it's not a part of the game. Employee ID/key cards. I cannot count the amount of times a robber had broken into my office, made off with 10k worth of stuff only to make it ten feet outside, get caught by the cops, and then suddenly have magically lost half of the stuff they just stole. Infuriating simply infuriating. Especially because outside of having round the clock security posted all over the place there is no real way to impede those guys. So here's where employee key/ID cards come into play. Have doors that have varying levels of security (mag lock, key lock, bolt lock, ect.) and make it so that if they don't have the key card they can't get in/have a chance to not be able to break in. I mean really who doesn't lock the front door in all my companies? This is the exact reason companies have these employee ID/key cards IRL. The only way to get in is to have intimate knowledge of the locking system and electronics or to break windows. Which takes me to the next part of this suggestion. Alarm systems. Self explanatory imo.

Seriously this game is amazing. But I would love a security update to help at lease reduce the chances of those crooked cops taking half my stuff (where else does it go? does he drop it and nobody even bothers looking for it? I want my dang table plants back!). And to further impede the robbers from actually getting into the office. The best security doesn't discourage people from breaking in it prevents people from breaking in and that's discouragement enough.

I'm not sure if the dev will read this or if they will take my suggestions to thought but either way I simply want to thank them for this amazing experience!\t\t\t\t\t
