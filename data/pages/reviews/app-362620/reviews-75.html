\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first Purchased the game all the way back in 2017, I remember scanning through YouTube looking for a game that allowed me to live out all of my fantasies of becoming a developer. I used to do that a lot, sitting in front of a screen for hours on end creating concepts for what I believed to be the 'Perfect' game, only to be disappointed when nothing compared to what lay in my imagination existed or that the core idea was there, but I found the gameplay to be repetitive and within an hour or two boring, I'm thinking of a certain development tycoon. So imagine my surprise when I stumbled along this piece of genius.

I was inspired to make this review, my first ever in fact, after reading others opinions on the current state of the game, a lot of those other reviews are only made up of a few short words that provide no help whatsoever in helping a prospective buyer decide on whether they should part ways with they're spare 10.99 or not, a figure that in my books is an absolute bargain, especially when the value of this game can only increase as it gets further on in development.
So here's my take.

I thought the game had a lot of features back in 2017 and it was different, it was ambitious, most importantly it was fun. Piecing together a character and choosing their specialities I found at first to be incredibly difficult as I had no idea as to what I was getting my self into, Sure I had watched videos but this was my playthrough, did I want him to be an audio guy, specialist in 2d or did I just pick any random thing and throw myself head first into it. I'm sure you know. 15000 is plenty of money to become a software millionaire, I thought as I began development of a Computer OS. I couldn't have been more wrong and very quickly was my company; a one man band operating out of a 3x3 shed powered by the coffee machine that was stuffed in the corner, heading for bankruptcy. I barely made it past the first year.

Fast forward 3 and a bit years where I have just reached what I would consider to be the endgame, Taking approximately 16 hours with 1 billion in the bank, a retired founder and 230 employees that churn out three different pieces of software every 2-3 years. Even then I never really 'completed' the game. I could have had 8 different teams working on different software, a warehouse where I print my own products, and the largest digital distribution network. I haven't taken into account the multiple mods that add further software and furniture products into the game to enhance your experience even further.

I recommend that if you find yourself even remotely thinking about picking up this game, then do it, I could have easily spent 20 in its current state, and have payed way more for games that are a fraction of the quality.


Remember that this is my first review which could have easily taken over my entire day as I attempt to write a more detailed account of my every playthrough.
This was the first game I have ever purchased on steam and there is a clear bias towards it, However I truly do love this game and have enjoyed every hour spent playing it\t\t\t\t\t
