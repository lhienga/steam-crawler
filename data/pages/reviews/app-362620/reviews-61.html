\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPrice: The price is fair and worth it, but looking at the game objectively, it's worth it to wait for a sale.
Gameplay: The game itself is straightforward and repetitive. There's no real difference between making a 2D editor or an audio tool. 90% of the fun is building and the management aspects of stuff, which can last you a bit, but are not very in-depth and are also repetitive. The progression mostly focuses around making progressively bigger offices, and teams and managing them, which, again, is the funnest part, but progression outside of that (growing your fanbase, building a business reputation, acquiring businesses) is largely entirely irrelevant. I've never reached a point where you can have a hundred teams all automatically making new stuff, but you can reach that within a day's worth of gameplay, and there's basically nothing to do after that. So I hope you like replaying the game, or else you will probably not get more than 60 hours out of it.
Replayability: Assuming you like the gameplay enough to, well, replay it, there's tons of new stuff to explore and new ways to play the game. I'd recommend coming into it with somewhat of a roleplaying mindset like "I'm gonna pretend I'm Adobe and make and maintain all the products they make and spearhead 2D graphics research" or something like that. Makes it funner and gives you more of a mission/guidelines to follow, so to speak

Overall: A good game that's worth the money. I hope the dev gets a bigger team and makes a more complex and in-depth game next time around, because the singular dev made a damn good game all on their own.

Recommended Changes:
-Minor fixes to the building system are needed. You should be able to place lights above tables, for example.
-The 'interest' mechanics are weird I'm pretty sure they're static (don't hold me to this). I feel like there's no real reason to ever make a product that has all that many good features because you'll just be 'wasting' interest, which really isn't how it should work in the first place.
-The mechanics around research & patenting are bland and inconsequential. I really wish you could actually see what researching a new technology means and how it could give you a competitive edge or allow you to release software with a brand new feature that makes everyone want to buy it. Right now it's literally just seeing a number go up and you get some royalties for developing it first.
-This is a problem I see in many, many games, and I've never really seen a game that has solved it, but the process of developing software needs to be more complex than just seeing a bar go up and making a few decisions. I have no idea how it would be done, but if there could be some way that you could actually go through the process of developing something and facing the challenges and making decisions and structuring it in such a way that you have to be involved in the development process and your decisions matter and impact the final product? This game would be an instant 6 stars out of 5. I have the same problem with Game Dev Tycoon: Every single game is basically the same but you check a few different boxes or move a few sliders differently. It would be a monumental task, but it's a task no game I've played has ever succeeded in.\t\t\t\t\t
