\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would have rated this game neutral if it was possible.

It is kinda sad that the game has been in development for over 10 years and the developer has not yet figured out how to make the game feature complete.

The game is pretty fun until you realize how badly the research portion of the game is implemented.

There is a MASSIVE design flaw in the latest version that completely ruins the game.

tldr: The way research works in this game is completely broken and unfun. I think I remember the research being better like 5+ years ago and the game design has gone backwards ever since.

The workshop is dead, most modders and players have given up on this forever early access game as well, and the developer is probably just updating this in his spare time when he has the time to do some bug fixes.

-----

The long story on why this game is completely broken right now:

So the year is 1980 and you want to make a game.

When you develop software, such as a game, you need tooling such as 2D tool, 3D tool, audio tool etc.

Instead of using your competitors tools and pay licensing you simply create the worst possible tool you can, without any features and click "in house". You create a 2D tool and an audio tool with 1970 tech.

Then you start designing games and select your tools as dependencies, since the features of your tools does not matter for the game product, only the "tech level" does, you can use your own bad tools for free, which does not affect the quality of the final game.

The years go by and it is now 1990. All you have to do is update your 2D and audio tool to the next "tech level" which is 1990. But here is the bad design: the features of the tools are still 1970. You don't actually need to develop any more tools with better features, you just have to keep updating the "tech level" each year by clicking a button to upgrade the software.

It's like EA releasing Fifa with a new yearly number, but nothing under the hood was updated.

Now you can create your 1990 game with the latest features with your 1970 tools.

You can make an MMO with networking features, when your game engine is 1970 tech in the year 2000. Does your game engine need networking? No.

And in year 2000, guess what? You can just update your tools to year 2000, but have 1970 features and it will be "up2date".

This is such a massive design flaw, that I can't comprehend how the developer was thinking. Probably to simplify the game to the point it requires zero strategy and it just becomes an update simulator.

This problem creates three massive flaws:

1. You never have to use competitors tools, because you can always update your own bad tools to the latest year and avoid licensing fees

2. Mods such that add more software like "game engines" are useless, the same problem occurs where your game engine can be 1970 features in year 2000. All you have to do is update the year to stay in line with the tech level

3. It does not require you to focus on anything really, since you can just make the most barebones tools without any features and use them for the rest of the game while you focus on your main product


How it should be:

1. You should not be able to advance to the next yearly tech level, if the features of the tools are not complete.

2. A tool that requires 2000 features to be used in developing a year 2000 game should have those features, not features from the year 1970.

3. A tool which is rated "mediocre" should give mediocre results in the final product such as the game. You simply can't make AAA games in year 2000 with 1970 tools.


It's simply ridiculous.

Oh and don't get me started on the other insane glaring issues such as starting with 4 founders that work for free forever, aka starving for 0 salary while having the most insane stats (basically 4 A++ workers for free), and hosting business that prints money which requires 0 staff to maintain the infrastructure, or digital store that can't be marketed etc.

Another massive letdown was the inability to see inside other players structures in multiplayer. You kinda want to see what others have built.

Hopefully this game is fixed in another 10 years in forever early access.

I will revise this review if any progress of a better research system has been made. IMO the dev should roll back how it used to be, because this is just bad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6/10 for me, 4/10 for others.
This game is fine, for 20 dollars its pretty decent.

For this game i wish there was a different way to review, I guess i would recommend but this game shoots for a very unique audience which is pretty sparse.

Lots of micro management, and unlike other games it doesnt really stop. you really do have full control and that aspect is really really interesting and provides for lots of data and information.

But that also adds a skill gap making it pretty hard for new comers, but thats alright.

My problem is the micro management does NOT stop, at some points it just gets too far down into the deep of it for my taste, and there is an auto project tool but its kinda confusing and it ofc cant take into special circumstances making it unviable.

Other then that its pretty easy to get money early on and just coast for the rest of the game, so even though i dont really know whats going on im somehow making money?

Dont get me wrong ill be racking up hours on this game, but thats not for the gameplay, just because this game scratches an itch in my brain for graphs and monopolies which this game lets me do with ease. not for the gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
