\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a connoisseur of this "spreadsheet simulator" genre and, if you are into this kind of game, I would wholeheartedly recommend this one. It has many systems to interact with, many layers of approaching efficiency and many paths to the general objective of optimising and getting bigger green numbers.

If you are not into these, you may still like this game for its interesting approach to "tycoon" games. It is a later, better and more complex game dev tycoon, so much so that it barely resembles it. Do check it out. This is a good game. It is also a game that will feel a bit overwhelming for the first couple hours.

Do not be frightened by the apparent difficulty, which is not truly difficulty but
obscurity and complexity. It is not a hard game, but one with many systems that require timely explanation. Do not read every tutorial when you start playing. Let yourself learn how to do easy things: contracts, deals and simple software before you do other things. Game tutorial should explain this better.

If you play many of these, I can tell you in my 24 hours of gameplay so far, I've yet to delve deep into many of the features. This is a game you can get 20 to 40 hours easily. It is not a shallow game.

Difficulty: easy to moderate
Complexity: very complex
Features: complete game that does not feel early access
Optimisation: It runs perfectly in my somewhat dated 1060 6gb

(But has little decorations).\t\t\t\t\t

There's a lot of things to do, you can attempt to be a gaming-centric company. Whether that be a multi-million dollar AAA company, a console company, or you try to pull off a Nintendo. You can also try to be the equivelent of Apple or Microsoft, working on OSes all day, and swearing at your competitors when you realize your 4 years of work was all for mediocre sales, despite building an Outstanding + Visionary product.

I also like the simulation, but I think it's not perfect. The AI companies often acquire other companies or "inherit" IP from bankruptcies (from companies they invested in; yes I said this game has a lot of depth) and basically do nothing with that IP, as in never develop the next product in the franchise, just letting it rot and die (not even updating the software!).

Some software "genres" are unsustainable for the AI. Companies that solely focus on Computer OSes (which after the initial generated companies, I've seen all companies that develop Computer OSes only develop Computer OSes) Often can't recuperate enough profit from their OS products, dying a slow and painful death. Depending on how big of a hit their initial OS is, they usually only last for 3~5 iterations before going bankrupt (and their last OS product is usually a dumpster fire). Of course, when going bust, their IP is acquired but basically abandoned, which in many cases is weird and unrealistic because sometimes these can be hugely popular Computer OSes that just have to bite the dust because the original company had an (game wise) unsustainable business practice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally fun game, I always love building a new company and watching everything interact. With the latest update the building mechanics are even more fluid and easy to use. To me the building aspect is really the shining light of the game because once you figure out how to maximize profits, its not very challenging to make money. I usually end up trying to give my employees the best benefits while taking my time to give them a fun, healthy work environment.

While the economics of the game can be a little trivial at times there are many other management aspects to keep you busy. You can micro manage employees as much as you like, or instead hire a supervisor to task if you would rather focus on the larger day to day management. The real challenge comes from trying to grow your company to hundreds and hundreds of employees while still being able to provide competitive wages and benefits.

In the end I've always had a great time watching my company evolve from a garage to a huge corporation that is publicly traded. The only update I would really love to see going forwards is some sort of an awards mechanic at the end of the year. Something as simple as a newspaper article or even a full on expo to invest money and hardware into would be a really great fit and much appreciated.\t\t\t\t\t
