\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think I've played this game a little while (48.4 hrs) to form an opinion. At first the game is quite enjoyable, but mid game things start to unravel and it becomes (at least for me) rather grindy and not a very enjoyable experience because I'm stuck in this endless game loop for which there is no escape unless I completely shift gears.

Let's take a look at Sim City 4 which has hundreds of different options and is very complex. Not at any point do I feel stressed, rushed, or forced down a certain path, I can go at my own pace in my own time and it's so relaxing. When I'm playing this I just feel stressed and uncomfortable, somethings not quite right, whether that be the level of micro management of employees, or being forced to announce software and stick to release deadlines something is missing.

I want to create a great company, one that makes great software or great 'things' that everyone in the world wants. Not necessarily a huge company with thousands of employees in huge buildings but the CD Projekt Red's, the Raspberry Pi, the Industrial Light and Magic, the Pixar of old, the Skywalker Ranch. I can't seem to do that with this game, when I release a product I feel I have to instantly start the development of the next version, keep up with competitors, hire more employees, expand offices, grind through the development cycle, get reviews, announce a release date, issue press release, rinse and repeat.

I thought maybe the in-house Framework idea was the fix, but maybe that mechanic isn't explained very well. I know this title is from a single developer, so lots of respect and massive kudos, you do have a game here. Maybe the slow pace type of company I want to build is not possible in its current iteration, I will for sure check back in the future.

But for right now, it's not the game for me but it may be for you.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall this gets a thumbs up from me but there are some areas that need improvement. However, the game is fun and has that "one more day" feel that has led me to spend 57 hours and counting!

Pros
- Fun gameplay loop for managing a software company
- Management layer is good, building and managing teams, their workloads.
- Can build your own offices and buildings
- Various different ways to play and different areas to focus including contract work, development, printing, digital distribution, investment and taking over other companies.

Cons
- Very few items in build mode...for the last two years there have been ONE picture you can use to decorate rooms..you better hope you like dozens of pictures of a cactus. Overall lack of enviromental items to use in build mode to decorate offices. Very bare bones.
- Digital Distribution and publishing are extremly bare bones. For digital distribution you cannot see how many items you are selling per month! You have literally no info about how your company is doing in this area apart from money made (total) and server load. You can also make deals with other companies to sell their items via digital distribution but apart form % you take and their load on your servers there is no info. How many copies are being sold, is it increasing etc. No info at all. It's EA so I hope this area is expanded upon. Also, while you can print games yourself there is no way for your to publish your own titles or titles of other companies / subsidiaries apart from digital distribution. There is no option to digitally distribute subsidiary software so assume this happens automatically? No idea.
- Buying out companies is very bare bones as well. You can give them money and let them do their own thing, use them on some of your work items or shut them down, that's it. You cannot do things for them, ie. handle support, printing, distribution etc.

Overall the game is fun and makes a good gameplay loop that I have enjoyed alot but there are items that need improvement, hopefully they will be addressed as the game continues it's EA development. But as of now, there are a lot of hours of entertainment to be had.\t\t\t\t\t
