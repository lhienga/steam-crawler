\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst... I've played way more than 2hrs, but most of it was offline.

Second... I am terrible at this game, the pacing for software development is extremely slow and then when you have to research technologies it can take literal years where the techs are obsolete by the time you finish, even with a cheated max stat team of 40 people.

Third... this game has the best building and designing mechanics of any game I've ever played. I enjoyed the Sims back in the day mainly to build homes, but this makes The Sims building mechanics look like fingerpaint compared to fine art. This game has the ability to custom style nearly everything, save the style or just the color code (hexadecimal?) and paste it elsewhere, change the default style of furniture and rooms, copy just furniture or rooms, save room and building blueprints, etc. You can build curved walls and build 11-floors high (10 + basement). It has very few mechanical restrictions that impede creativity, the only four I can name are no internal walls that aren't connected to an external wall or another internal wall, no glass elevators, you can't plant trees in unfenced areas, and you can't hang external signs where something is connected to the inside of the wall (lamps or elevators).

Summary: I recommend this game for its awesome building mechanics and on the assumption that I'm just bad at the gameplay. Even if the gameplay is bad, the building mechanics still make it worth buying for that, you can always just console command however much money you need.\t\t\t\t\t
