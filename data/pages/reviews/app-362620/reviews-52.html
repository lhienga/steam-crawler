\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI keep intending to review this game with each new Alpha but I end up playing it so much I never have time. Now it is in Beta and I know I'm about to get sucked in again, so let me just drop a quick review here to help buff this game up on Steam Store a little more, because good grief does it ever deserve it.

It's a management/tycoon/design game and its just too incredibly awesome to ignore if you like the genre. It's getting better with every major release and there has been basically not a single backwards step during development so far. The scope is grand, the building and management tools are excellent, and there's so much joy to be had in creating an original, tiny software company in a garage and slowly growing it up until you dominate several markets with an accompanying headquarters the likes of which has never before been seen. I've built many fantastic, beautiful, and functional buildings in this game, but I had almost as much fun building my teams and products. Hardware manufacture being added, and finally now in Beta being extremely advanced, has broadened the product scope further.

The game supports mods and there are a few good ones but keep in mind the community is small and mods updated for the latest version always take time to appear. But you don't really need much in the way of mods for this game to be great. It's just simply great.

Just remember that engineering is the core focus. The meat of the game is building teams of compatible and skilled employees, putting them in the right environments with the right tools, ensuring that they do quality work on products the market wants to buy, and do it all with a big enough profit margin to pay bills, salaries, expand the size of the company, research, manufacture, etc.

It's just a joy to play for management and tycoon fans alike. I hope it gets several more years of development, because I can see it getting even better.

I'll write a better, more comprehensive review of the game after a couple Beta releases (first Beta just started and I haven't even played it yet, it is this good at the end of ALPHA!)

-- edit 4/15/22 --

I felt I had to add an extra note that you need, to a certain extent, to be able to make your own fun in the sandbox environment, to be able to enjoy Software Inc (at least at this stage of development). It's not structured and will not offer you challenges you don't set for yourself. The full scope of the difficulty has yet to be balanced. This is the only legitimate reason why anyone could honestly say they hate Software Inc, and that's on them. It also is, sadly, to their great loss, because there aren't that many games out there as good as Software Inc.\t\t\t\t\t
