\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game a couple years ago when it was still very early in development, and play it periodically in spurts every month or so.
The core game of developing software to make a profit and expand hasn't changed since I began, and is still very fun and interesting and ultimately what got me hooked in the first place. What keeps me coming back so often is that the content and depth of the game is constantly changing. The developers are constantly adding new features and changing old ones to keep the game fresh, and every single new feature is a welcome addition in my opinion. I especially like that they have never lost the core feel of the game, whilst still adding so much more content that makes that core feeling even better. Especially when the new features make it so if you are new it is still a relatively simple game, but more experienced players can really take advantage of all the features.
Although it is definitely overall a fun and positive game, it is still very clearly in early access. The tutorial is terrible, I hate to say it, but every time there is a new feature that I want to learn about and use, I try the tutorial and it just makes everything worse, forcing me to take steps that I don't want to, so I just turn to forums or figuring it out for myself. Also, the pacing of the game needs to be more varied, it seems that if I play on easy, my first release makes me bajillions of dollars, and on hard no matter what I do my products fail even though I know exactly what I should be doing to succeed. The difficulties should be either less extreme, or there should be more middle ground difficulties so that playing on medium isn't the only option.
Sometimes the new features are buggy or unbalanced, but I'm not too worried. For example, getting robbed happens like 6 times a year even with security, and the fire inspector just tells me I suck at fire safety despite having what seems like a million smoke detectors and sprinklers. Despite these complaints, I've had the game long enough to have confidence that most of my issues will be solved, and ultimately, they don't detract from my enjoyment of the game much.
If you like simulation games where you can either automate everything or micromanage everything, then this game is great and I definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played this for around 20 hours, I would definitely recommend it. Certainly the biggest Development Simulation out there and it's loads of fun to create software and compete for a top spot in the industry.
However, there are some gripes I have with the game as of yet (still not the full release). The controls are a big clunky. But the main downside is that your teams need too much micro-management. Each development needs a certain amount of programmers, designers etc to create the best product possible. At the same time, the people working for you cannot sit in a room together with people working on other projects. Which leads to you having to switch them around constantly. Also, their need for socialising is far too high, meaning that they'll lose around 2 hours of productivity every day either in meetings or at the water fountain. I'd love to see the developer making tweaks here to simplify things a bit. Or at least give clearer feedback to the player how I can improve. Also, the curve is a bit too steep. You go from having a small team to needing a lot more staff quite quickly, meaning you'll have to expand at a fairly ridiculous rate, which threw me off a bit.

But don't let those things take away the positives, which is mainly that this game - more than any other in the genre I've played - really gives you the feeling of actually running a development company. Great work so far, really looking forward to the full release and the updates to come!\t\t\t\t\t
