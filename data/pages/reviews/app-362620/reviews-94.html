Seriously though, this game may be the ultimate company management game? it has a *lot* of depth, with available technologies changing throughout the ages, stock markets and bonds and financial toys, dependencies between the products so you could make your own ecosystem, sims-like personal and interpersonal relationship status effects, lots of things to dress up your perfect office, and of course WAY MORE because it supports MODS which is truly my favorite feature in any game, there's no way you can match the amount of content an engaged community as a whole can put out, and the recent 'hardware' update was more or less already available for quite a while in the workshop (although the official implementation is way more extensive and has factorio-like belts and such for your own product manufacturing setups!) :)

All in all, could I recommend this? Certainly. The only downsides I can think of is that it runs a little heavy on CPU and GPU for a management game, but it still ran on any laptop I tried (although it did get quite warm). Even its price I'd consider almost low for what you get, this game is certainly very playable for early access, it always was and now after many updates I'd consider it to be almost a version 2, it really is very good.

If I had to recommend a company tycoon/management game, this would probably be the top of my list.\t\t\t\t\t
