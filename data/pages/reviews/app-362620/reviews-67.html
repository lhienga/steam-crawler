\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs you can see I've played this game for more than 60+ hours. So why the "Not recommend"?

Believe me, I really wanted to like this game and I played a lot with it thinking 1. I will better understand it and so it will be more enjoyable and 2. at one point it will come out of Early Access and will resemble something of a well-rounded product. Simply put, this is game is still unfinished. First of all, let me get this out of the way: I know it's a one-man dev team and I appreciate the effort but it doesn't change the fact that the game has big imperfections.

As you begin you set up your character who is basically your CEO and you start working in a rented office. Basically you grind contract work for several days and try to work on your own, original product at the same time. You can't do everything on your own as you need various skill sets so you start hiring people. Once your first (or maybe second) product hits the market you'll get plenty of money to get your own building and build a bigger team. Somehow this first part of the game seems unnecessary for me as you don't learn anything from it.

Mid-game can be interesting as you release more and more products and you build your teams. You need Designers (architects), Developers, Marketing people, Artists and Team Leaders. One really annoying thing is that you can't put different teams in the same room because they'll start complain so team are fairly rigid.

t gets pretty boring and not very creative as you progress your product through the design-dev-qa-release-support chain. A really annoying thing again that if your product suddenly becomes popular you run out of servers which you couldn't see before so there's no way to be prepared for it. Same thing with printing CD's. This is the part of the game when you start getting overwhelmed by manually assigning stuff. So you decide to try out Project Management. This is exactly the opposite of the manual work assignment: you set it up and then you wait. And then nothing happens. People are idling, while there's more work etc. And then suddenly something happens but you have no control over it so you just watch.

The Stock Exchange aspect of the game: I think this is absolutely unnecessary. It's well done and everything but it's very easy to abuse it. And by abuse I mean, get rich with it once you have some money. Basically you can maintain a basic level of operations and become a self-made stock broker and make millions off of it.

In summary:
Pros:
  • build your own building
  • randomly generated characters make it more interesting
  • you get to control a dev company
Cons:
  • clunky UI
  • no useful help - you have to figure out how to do lots of stuff like basic development or project management
  • you can build and decorate but there are only a few items so there's not much variety
  • grindy gameplay which gets boring after a while
  • too many unfinished features - less would be more
