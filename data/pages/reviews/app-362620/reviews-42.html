There's no clear difference between making an RPG vs. a Simulation in the Game section. There's nothing "special" about particular software categories except that you compete with other products in that category and that your employees could be especially capable in particular categories. If you make an antivirus software, there aren't any special events where your software failed to prevent an especially harmful malware attack, or vice versa, it just feels like a label without significance. However, it looks like the devs and modders are addressing this gradually, and given it's current beta state I think the game is in a good place despite the lack of meaningful distinction between software types.
Fundamentally, it looks like development started with a good idea, but didn't go all out immedietely trying to add every feature they wanted. They started with a good framework and are adding detail on top of that solid foundation; considering the other projects out there today that try to hit a home run right away this patient approach is encouraging and something I like to support and I'd recommend you do so as well if the subject matter appeals to you.\t\t\t\t\t
