\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the current state of the game, I cannot recommend this to anyone, except to those who like games where the challenge is only in the first hour or so. Like some of the other negative reviews, it is true that once you start to make a lot of money, the game is over. There really is no incentive to research new technology or even build up your company past this point. You can just sit there and watch the money roll in. Simple as that.

However, one thing I haven't seen from any reviews (at least the ones that I have read, since there are so many) is about progression. Even when you have all the money in the world, it is sluggish in my opinion. I get that slow progression is more realistic to an actual business, it does not make for a gratifying experience. As some others have said, this is basically a business simulator but with software and stuff, which is not original to begin with. When you compare it to other games like it, however, it falls flat for me, and makes it pretty boring.

Another thing I have to say, in-line with this game being boring at times, is the tutorials are just reading and nothing else. There is nothing wrong with that, and I hope they are just a placeholder for something a bit more engaging, but it really seems like reading was the order of the day for whoever was making the tutorials. I wish there was at least some form of image to help explaining what to do, or even a video, but instead we have black text on a white background.

I know that there are a lot of people that like this game, so I'm obviously not speaking to you. But for those who are considering buying this game, this information would be useful to help you decide whether this game is for you. I am still going to play it after all this, and I will update this review if these things change, and it is still in early access so I think some things will change in the coming versions.

It is also important to mention that this was and is still being developed by one guy, so it makes a lot of these things more forgivable, and more willing to support the project later down the line. However, there are still issues that I have, as I have previously stated, that take me out of the experience. I have no ill-will towards the developer, and in no way intend to discourage them from continuing to make games, I'm just here to give some honest feedback from a random consumer of the product.

Enough of the negativity, there are some good things in this game that I love. For one, simply being able to switch between software and hardware types is a must, as it gives you more opportunity to expand your company, instead of being locked in on video games. I also love the design options you have with hardware. Rarely do we see a game where you are given the option to fully customize the design of your hardware, so much that it was the thing that drew me towards this game in the first place (not the only thing I liked, more like the final push to buy it). The graphics, while simplistic, are very pleasing to the eye and aren't distracting from the core game. I also like the build mode, and that you can build an office or assembly/printing facility from scratch, nice touch.

All in all, I do like this game, but some issues prevent me from recommending the game to others. If I had wanted a game with sluggish progression, I would play GTA Online without buying shark cards. I do have hope that these things will change and the game will get better over time, and I will change this review when that happens. However, as it is right now, I don't recommend that you buy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSofware Inc. is an absolutely incredible work of art and a pleasure of a game to play!

If you like management style games such as Another Brick In The Mall you will love this game. Although it may look a little tedious and boring, I always find myself excited to develop a new product, market it and see how well it does. And with the brand new Beta 1.0 it doesn't just end there. You can update your products, create Ad-ons and plugins for them and of course still port them to new operating systems. The level of detail you can go into when developing products is immense honestly. You need to decide everything from the name to its features to even what 2D editor it will run on and who the lead designer will be!

Another amazing thing is this games building system. Although a little annoying at times with its incapability to have rooms inside of rooms, it makes up with hundreds of objects to place, parking, garage doors, helipads and even windmills and solar panels. You must heat and cool your building, design fire safety into it and add security cameras to stop thieves getting your items.

Theres so much detail I really cant put it all into this review. But what I can put here is that you should absolutely check this game out. Its being regularly updated with new features all the time and for just over £12 you really cant go wrong. So yeah, absolutely check it out and i'm sure you'll love it!\t\t\t\t\t
