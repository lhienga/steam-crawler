\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good and fun game. The early stage is quite tense balancing the ability to make software quickly to make money and having money to pay employee's and upgrades.

There are a few things that I find difficult in working with or bugs, these being
  • When using Game Creator Maps, the Reception Area is always dirty and you'll NEVER receive contract proposals. I don't know if this is intentional to cause the player to build their own buildings; if its intentional, its a bad way to implement that force.
  • The constant changes that cause all of the Software category workshop mods to stop working until a new version for that mod is released that works with that update. It does prove that there is work being done and that the work being done is worth while, but its a little frustrating.

Suggestions
ALSO, I would love some sort of system where you could buy out roads and permits and stuff so that you could build some of the awesome blueprints provided by the community. The way it is now, its literally rolling the dice with each seed hoping you have a plot big enough to build those blueprints.

  • The way training employees should be revamped (Perhaps having the employer choose the college for better stat increases, increased training speed at higher prices) and a tuition reimbursement system should be implemented making the employee locked into a contract for X years depending on the amount reimbursed.
  • Hacking and Hacking Software: Becoming a black hat hacking corporation/team should have high payout but high risk with it. Fines, arresting of the team members in the hacking teams and maybe even a game end in the event of the whole corporation is closed by the government. (These could all be fought by lawyers). Corporate Espionage for better software and designs then your own.
  • Stock Manipulation: You should be able to choose launch counter marketing campaigns against other software similar to the one you're making. Increasing your sales and either dropping or increasing the stock of a competitor. (Lawyers and Marketing team work together).

Overall, I love this game. 10000000% recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe small developer team adds pointless new objects to the game instead of improving anything. You never look at one of the desks close up. Usually you're trying to manage your projects and look at the zoomed out building/floor. So why does anyone care if you can move one of the pencils on one of the desks by one inch to one side? These are the kinds of useless new features that the dev wants to work on. They seem to be fully committed to recreating a version of Sims. This game's codename could have been "Project Dull House"...

UI is still trash. It is probably some point of pride for the small developer to keep the same old and broken UI they wrote at the start. None of the windows ever fit, you always have to scroll left/right/up/down to see the info you want. You cannot pick which columns you want to see in all of those tables. OMG are there lots of tables and other spreadsheet metaphors in this UI. The absolute worst part is that you can only resize windows by a single corner. You will miss being able to drag one of the 4 sides, no, you will have to hunt for that one little corner with your mouse. And you will want to do that a lot: each time you restart the game all windows get reset; also see my comments above about having to scroll, you will be trying to resize those windows regularly.

Gameplay itself is probably not what you want either. You will never be able to just pick it up and play as a newcomer. All the guides are outdated due to the dev making random changes. The codebase must be a jumbled sea of spaghetti, because any time they update something, every prev strat flies out the window. Lots of bugs being touted as features and 'realsm', but they are really just bugs. Many hidden features and mechanics that end up ruining your big plans.\t\t\t\t\t
