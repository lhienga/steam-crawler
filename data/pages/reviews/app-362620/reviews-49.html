\r\n\t\t\t\t\tApple Inc. is an American multinational technology company that specializes in consumer electronics, software and online services. Apple is the largest information technology company by revenue (totaling US$365.8 billion in 2021) and as of January 2021, it is the world's most valuable company, the fourth-largest personal computer vendor by unit sales and second-largest mobile phone manufacturer. It is one of the Big Five American information technology companies, alongside Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft.

Apple was founded as Apple Computer Company on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne to develop and sell Wozniak's Apple I personal computer. It was incorporated by Jobs and Wozniak as Apple Computer, Inc. in 1977 and the company's next computer, the Apple II became a best seller. Apple went public in 1980, to instant financial success. The company developed computers featuring innovative graphical user interfaces, including the original Macintosh, announced in a critically acclaimed advertisement, "1984", directed by Ridley Scott. By 1985, the high cost of its products and power struggles between executives caused problems. Wozniak stepped back from Apple amicably, while Jobs resigned to found NeXT, taking some Apple employees with him.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had it for a day, and I can say this:

It was a little confusing to start at first. A lot of games fall into the trap of a tutorial that's way too long, or one that doesn't give anywhere near enough information. This game does not do that. This game only gives you tutorials when you actually open the menus that you need. Meaning you won't get spammed with a bunch of tutorials in one go, forget everything and not even use some of them because it's a goal for a later date. The level of information they have is perfect. And they're easy to turn on and off whenever you like.

The actual gameplay can start off being a bit confusing, but with the help of the tutorials and some common sense, it's very easy to get going. It's a great management game for owning a company, and I've never seen anything like it.

If you're not into building, like myself, there are plenty of buildings you can get from the workshop. The only issue is that a lot of them are out of date, so check the last time a blueprint was updated. Even then you can do whatever you want to those buildings and make your own versions of them.

There is plenty of mod support to improve the gameplay and add to the game, with so many different mods available for customization or gameplay mechanics that you'll never get bored and there are so many ways to play the game even without them.

So far, I don't really have any complaints besides the stocks aspect of the game. You can buy stocks in other businesses in order to completely buy them out or make them a subsidiary. It's a bit hard to find where to check up on them after you close the first menu, and it would also be nice to see all the staff of that subsidiary and be able to transfer back and forth. The stocks are a bit confusing and for some reason hemorrhage money quite a lot seemingly randomly, but that is the only thing I haven't figured out about the game yet.

There is even a whole manufacturing option, which I haven't gotten into but it seems very easy to understand.

All in all, I really really think this is worth even the full price.
