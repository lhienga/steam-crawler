\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGraphics and Visuals
The graphics are great. The icons all make sense for ease, and I am a fan of the art style- it strikes a balance! It is a fun cartoon style with splashes of realism to make it more immersive.

Game Design
If you’re brand new to time/resource/simulator games, this may be a bit overwhelming. These are namely, the genre of my gameplays and it can be a bit much at times when trying to identify the “one” thing that needs to be clicked. The tutorial information certainly helps however, there are things that are not explicitly stated and need to be “trialed” to understand. Be inquisitive, click around, try things, “un-try” things, and see how it works. Sometimes I feel like I have to click multiple things to have the information I need (e.g. when selecting a contract- opening up the other employee information to check skills, etc.).

Bottom line- there is a LOT to do. If you get bored playing this game, you might not have explored enough. You can get into the details of actual contracts and deals, manufacturing, marketing, HR management and workforce development, workplace environment/equipment, investments, financials, etc. There are many things to monitor and remain aware of throughout your gameplay. HIGH replayability.

Gameplay Mechanics
The controls themselves work well and have good responsiveness. I haven’t had issues with selecting or moving anything within the game.

The roofing function- sucks for me. It never looks the way I think I am setting it up to look like lol. Personal problem but an annoying one because it doesn’t just auto roof when I am ready to put a roof on and if I have a rooftop ac unit- forget about it—but you can’t because you won’t get insurance when you fail your fire inspection. Point being- DETAILS matter and you’ll need to get a grasp on many different game mechanics to be successful.

Sound and Music
Hit or miss. Sometimes the music is playing, sometimes it isn’t. Effect music and ambient sound is fine and makes it feel like a real “office”.

Innovation
The game introduces new items and features through your gameplay, and you can build upon things so your company doesn’t get “stagnant”.

Performance
I’ve had a few crashes but no loss in-game data. Loading times are quick, things are “quick” and I don’t have a “gaming” computer.

Multiplayer Experience
It exists- I haven’t played it.

Single-Player Experience
I really think the single-player experience is top-notch. You can adjust the difficulty level as you get the hang of things.


Value for Money
I play a LOT of games within this genre. This is 100% A BANG FOR YOUR BUCK. I don't say that lightly. There is some complex rabbit holes you can find yourself within and that is a good thing for the realism of the game.

Community and Support
Patch notes galore which is great. There is an active community and some good resources to provide tutorial & walk through
