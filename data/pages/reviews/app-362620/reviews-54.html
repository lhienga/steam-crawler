\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: In depth tycoon game with a large learning curve.

Graphics:
Rather simplistic 3D, works well.

Storyline:
None

Price:
This game is EXTREMELY underpriced. It should be at least $30 - $50 with the amount of gameplay that anyone can get out of it.

Difficulty:
It's as hard as you make it (your choices effect the difficulty).

AI:
The only AI is a few random number generators disguised as competing companies.

Cutscenes:
None

Fun:
This game is a BLAST. It goes extremely in depth, allowing you to control every aspect of your company, while still providing great satisfaction. The building mode is also the best building mechanic I've played around with ever.

Sounds/Music:
Barely noticeable, not really remarkable.

Replayability:
You can always replay this game. Due to the sandbox nature there is always new things to try.

Controls:
The controls have to be the worst part of the game. Everything is over complicated and very daunting... I almost immediately quit because it didn't make sense. Please please please watch someone playing the game from the start for ~15 minutes before you play the game, it will help you much more than the miserable tutorials.

Performance:
It starts off seeming extremely optimized, slowly consuming more and more as you have more going on. In the current state of my company I play on the lowest graphics settings to achieve a stable framerate, which honestly isn't ideal (i7-7700, GTX 1060 6gb, usually ~5GB ram consumption in this game).

Modding:
Mods are one of the saving graces of this game. Coredumping has fully embraced the modding community (even helping modders out in their Discord server), leading to endless mods existing. It's not hard to find anything you wish to add to the game - or make it yourself.

Selling Out:
There is not a single thing in this game that would make me feel like its sold out. No DLC, no sequels, no merch, nothing.

Similar Games:
- Game Dev Tycoon

Bugs:
- Small visual issues with pillars
- "Computer assigned in room that employee can't access" pops up... even when you don't have any employees or computers\t\t\t\t\t
