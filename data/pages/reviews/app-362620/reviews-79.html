\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot yet.

The game have good ideas on software complexity / dependencies, development cycles, developer growth / productivity. Marketing is quite simple, but I like it, since the focus should be on development / management.

But I feel like I spend most of my time furnishing offices and I feel like a waste.
I want to play the game, not only play house. And given the rapid-growth nature of the game, you spend A LOT of time playing house.

At some point, when your company is 100-150 employees large, you don't fit in any of the vanilla buildings anymore, and you have to either:

a) find a community building in the workshop, weeding out buildings created in previous versions / different years / without parking / without bus / without adequate heating or ac / etc
b) spend a day or more to create your own building

This is a hard-blocker for me. On every run, I stop playing at this point, because the game shifts into a endless chore.

I would suggest simplifying all the building/furnishing logic so the player could focus on building products instead. Maybe one idea is to allow players to buy land plots on rental maps, so they can gradually grow.\t\t\t\t\t
