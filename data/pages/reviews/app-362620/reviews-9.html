\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a game, this should be second place for game of the year after Baldurs Gate 3. It is so incredibly addicting, way more than "one more turn" in Civ. More like "I just wanna see the sales for my project", you sit down for a quick session at lunch time, 20minutes later it's midnight and you're starving and your wife is upset because you ignored her all day (in my case I ignored her the whole bloody week haha).

Best thing since Game Dev Tyccoon. The amount of freedom in this game is nuts. You want to make games? You can. Wanna make software? You can. Design phones/consoles? You can. You want to play factorio and just print all the things for AI without doing any designing yourself? You can. Did you ever dream of running a 24/7 callcentre with uncomfortable chairs and outdated PC's for all 50 of your employees? You can. This game is so much more than just 'game developing' and I love it.

Multiplayer is stable and fun, you can see each other on the map and help/compete with each other.

UI is complicated.. it has so many buttons, I like most of it but it does take some time to get used to where things are. 50hours deep and I just learned you can directly replace all computers with the new version.. never thought to right click furniture before :D The active projects view is probably the thing that needs the most work, at the start of the game it's easy to keep track of stuff, but once you start taking on deals and developing several projects at a time it can get very clustered, the current "filtering/grouping" compacts things but then leaves some info out that I like to see constantly at a glance. Perhaps a menu button that opens a notepad with all active projects would be best, one that you could pin, resize and move around.

TL:DR If you like dev games/business management games, buy this. This game belongs to be up there with the greats.\t\t\t\t\t
