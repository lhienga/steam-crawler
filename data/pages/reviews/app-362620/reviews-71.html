\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game looks cool and fun and promising and sorta like the Sims but running an IT business.

But then you realise the tutorial is abysmal, there's no wiki, Steam guides are for advanced players only and don't provide any information on the interface or what you should actually be doing, and the game seems to be updated so fast and radically that a consistent guide CAN'T be developed.

And if you DO find a guide that you can squint at and nod your head, thinking it sorta makes sense at the start at least, you can get yourself as a solo contract worker, then a software developer where you slowly go into the red and say you had fun.

Your Player Character has no needs and is effectively an android, but your employees have so many needs it's like you are their therapist and babysitter, so it's like "well I might as well do everything myself then", except it takes forever and hence you end up in the red.

Tl;dr, this game is equivalent to expecting a monkey to know how to play chess in nature; it requires an encyclopaedia to play and instead the tutorial you're given is essentially "well here's like 1-2 things in this game.... see ya!".\t\t\t\t\t
