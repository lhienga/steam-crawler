\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is sort of an addendum to my DCS review, but specific to the F/A-18C module.

I grew up on a US Naval military base, and of all the planes I got to see flying everywhere all the time, the F/A-18 was always my favorite. I naturally gravitated to flight and flight combat sims when I got my first computer system, and picked up most sims available at the time - until Jane's F/A-18E Super Hornet was released (sorta shows my age). I played the heck out of that. Time marched on though and the sim became incompatible with later OS installs, yet I still thought about flying the F18 a lot.

Then this package was released. I admit that the price tag was intimidating at first. But I eventually plunked down the cash... and holy hell I was back flying the F/A-18.

The whole package is just awesome. The detail, the flight dynamics, the avionics systems, everything. As nearly everyone else has stated who has reviewed the product, it's a must buy if you like modern combat jets and play DCS - and yes, the price tag is worth it even at full price. The plane is easy to actually fly - but once you begin to dive into the systems and the weapons, you quickly find that it does have a learning curve that's fairly steep and requires a lot of practice / training. But with this plane, that is definitely not a bad thing. Despite that, however, the Hornet is not an overly complex plane to start up - even with a step by step checklist it is far easier to start the systems, fire up the engines, and taxi out than it is to do the same in the A-10C.

In addition, I fly exclusively in VR with a Rift. To sit in the virtual cockpit of this beauty is a complete joy. The close up details are amazing, and the cockpit doesn't cause a drastic frame rate impact in any way despite the systems or detail. Nearly everything in the cockpit actually works and engages / disengages a system and can be clicked or moved - even the pressure knob in the analog altimeter. The catapult launches off the carrier are cool, but coming in for a landing on the carrier in VR is intense the first few times (and try doing it at night in VR with hardly any reference points at all).

The plane is still being completed - there are some systems that aren't available as of this writing - but DCS is working hard to get the final systems pushed out as quickly as they can. As such, there are new things available all the time, like the AGM-65F recently, and in the next few days LGBs (laser guided bombs). As a multi-role air combat platform, there's little you cannot do in the F/A-18.

I highly, highly recommend this DCS module. Trust me, you will not regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Hornet is one of the very few truely multirole aircraft in DCS at the moment. It does, however come with some caveats; it's in essentially beta, the module will be feature complete in the future, but at the moment only the air to air (AtA) items are available and working. The Air to Ground (AtG) is pretty basic, with dumb bombs and rockets. However, mavricks (laser/IR/CCD) are being added and on the horizion is the HARM, targeting pod, laser bombs, and the AtG radar modes. When the HARM comes in, it will be the second blufor side mod (not counting the fan-made A-4) with anti-radar weapons.

It's also a study sim, you're expected to know what all the buttons do, and how to configure the displays for the situation at hand, but to assist you there is a large and helpful playerbase making video and text tutorials, but before you jump into multiplayer, people expect a minimum level of knowledge; startup, weapons choice, proper radio setup and use, taxiing, take off, navigation, proper weapons employment, flying the landing pattern, landing safely and taxiing to a safe spot away from the runway to rearm/refuel. Take it in steps,

So buyer beware -- if you're okay with a product that half done and getting better over the months, then good. If you're not okay with a unfinished product, wait a bit, it'll get there. But if you only buy one module, this can do AtA, and AtG compentently, if perhaps somewhat unexceptionally in either area.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe F/A-18C Hornet is one of my favorite aircraft, so of course I'm going to like the aircraft no matter what. However as of right now (November of 2018), the module is kind of lacking considering it's large price tag. Granted, they are starting to add more stuff such as the JHMCS, AGM-65E/G varients and the AIM-9X, with more on the horizon as time goes on. But if you're tight on cash and want a module that will give you enough to learn for a very long time, get the F-14 Tomcat when it comes out, or get something like the A-10C. Because the F/A-18C just isn't done yet, and is kind of boring once you learn what little there is. The only upside I'd say to buying it now, is the fact that, since most of the features are being released a month apart from each other, it kind of paces you on learning them, instead of you wanting to learn everything all at once (Like I did with the A-10C until I learned that I just had to take it one step at a time).

tl;dr
Great module but is kind of lacking in stuff to do at the momen. If you want something with a lot to do and a lot to learn, look elsewhere. If you're okay with everything not being out yet, and just want to get the Hornet, go right on ahead as this aircraft is still a joy to fly.\t\t\t\t\t
