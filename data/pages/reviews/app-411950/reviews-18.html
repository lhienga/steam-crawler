\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my first DLC for DCS. And it is completely worth it every cent. It comes with a lot of tutorials, a detailed user manual (236 pages for each language), many training missions, campaign missions, skins, wallpaper, soundtrack, etc.

The F/A-18C Hornet is the perfect bird in DCS – especially for beginners but also for people who have experience with other aircrafts. It can do everything you might think of in DCS. Deadly Air2Air capabilities, insane Air2Ground capabilities, great flight model, very easy to fly (fly-by-wire; the computer translates the joystick movement into manageable maneuvers that won’t rip your wings off the plane… srsly I tried the F14B and when you pull your stick a bit too hard the bird just falls apart – no joke), many advanced weapons/modules, and still getting better every month with updated functions and improvements.

One question I had when I bought it were the multiplayer capabilities. Will I have disadvantages against others when picking this bird?

In multiplayer the F/A-18C is very strong. I killed Mig29s, F14s, F15s, F16s, JF-17s, other Hornets and stuff. The Hornet can deal with them all. You won’t be a loser in multiplayer when you choose it. Just practice to fight against others. Do not always flank straightforward! This may often kill you.

Compared to other planes it might not be an F14B Tomcat that can shoot down enemies from 50nm ahead with IR guided long-range-missiles... true. The AIM-120 AMRAAMs of the Hornet are more like deadly within 15-25nm range. But you can learn how to get into the right position to even kill a Tomcat which was designed to be the deadliest bird in the air. And it’s not only about range. It’s also about situational awareness, tactical maneuvering and engaging in the right moment. You can always bring you into a better position, use the environment (fly low under the radar between the mountains) and let other birds come closer looking for you. When the Hornet is in AIM-120C-range, the other guys are basically toast. The F14B for example may be deadlier in an 1v1 long-range-fight. But the F/A-18C has many options to deal with an F14B. And the F14B is good in Air2Air but in Air2Ground it’s comparatively bad.
Check the tutorials from “Growling Sidewinder”, “Grim Reapers”, “RedKite” and DCS staff member “Matt Wagner” (as I call him the Bob Ross of DCS xD) how to use and fight with the Hornet. Everything is explained there and can be practiced.

The Hornet comes with insanely strong systems: Easy to use radar with many modes and functions. You can also softlock multiple targets which are then displayed in your HUD with several information about them. In your cockpit there are many displays which can give you all of the information you want to see right now and you can set everything where you want it to be. Also, the Hornet can carry the Lightening Targeting Pod which you can use in Air2Ground mode to easily spectate targets in insanely long ranges (basically as far as DCS is rendered in your options) and assign/guide your weapons on them. You can for example kill up to 8 targets from 60-70nm range with AGM-154 JSOWs in one strike. The F14B can’t do that.
There are so many possibilities to kill targets with the Hornet. After >200h I still have not tried everything yet.

Oh and one more thing. The F/A-18C Hornet can start and land from carriers and can be air-refueled which is very cool and can be easily done with some practice. (You will fail a lot and at some point it becomes like riding a bicycle.)

Buy this plane and learn one system after another. Can recommend it 100%.
Watch the YouTube tutorials and you will have a lot of fun!
Cheers.\t\t\t\t\t
