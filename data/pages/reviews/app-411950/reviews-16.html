\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played DCS World for about 325 hours, a lot of that time learning the Hornet as it was my first professional flight model aircraft. I picked it up on sale for 40.00 USD and it is definitely an amazing starter aircraft for anyone wanting to get into DCS. Once acquainted with the cockpit, which should not take very long, you'll be able to go from a cold and dark state to fully operational ready to taxi for take-off in 3-5 minutes top (if you manage your time well probably faster). The designation F stands for Fighter while A stands for attack meaning this module is capable of preforming Air to Air combat and Air to Ground. It's also carrier based meaning that you can launch and land on carrier decks.

I can promise that if you're a noobie to DCS and have grown out of the FC3 modules, this is the next step. It will take practice to learn how to navigate the various functions like IFF, and TACAN along with learning how to select and deploy the various weapon systems as the Digital Displays can be confusing but remember why you are purchasing this module, which is a study level simulation so you will spend plenty of hours just trying to figure out which Display to pull up and how to select, and deploy weapons. But this common of any of the fully fledged simulation modules, so it is all part of the experience.

Lastly I cannot go without saying some of the Hornet's real life counterparts are still in development and are not implemented. That being said Eagle Dynamics is fully dedicated to this module and update the module regularly to add these features. The current features however are more than enough for any combat situation and you will honestly not even notice the lack of these features unless you are already deeply familiar with the F/A-18C.

To wrap up this review, this module provides a cockpit for a new pilot to easily get acquainted with, while also providing a deep and complex weapon, radio, and navigation systems available for when you become profecient in basic flight systems.\t\t\t\t\t
