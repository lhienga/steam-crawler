\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I thought it was worth it for the menu music alone. It befits cowboys putting to sea.

Then I found out it's available on Youtube for free...

I guess now I have to learn flying it...Joke aside,

It seems like an underwhelming aircraft at first which is not as able to fight in the vertical plane as lighter aircraft such as the F-16 and Mirage-2000C as well as heavier birds of prey like the F-15C and Su-27S. It just feels a bit more limiting. The Hornet bleeds speed upon reaching a decent turn rate. An issue I haven't noticed or be troubled with in all other aircraft. However, it's not as bad as I thought it was. You can regain lost energy relatively quickly but this does put limits to its maneuverability in a dogfight (not that it needs to dogfight though as the chances of it dogfighting other aircraft with guns only are pretty slim thanks to its lavish missile-carrying capacity so it essentially only needs to at least point its nose toward the opposing aircraft to launch its missile. The AIM-9X makes this incredibly easier too).

Note that the F/A-18C in this module is the F/A-18C Lot 20 which batch was delivered between 1998 and 2000. The Lot 20 Hornets comprise the VERY LAST BATCH of F/A-18C Hornets produced before production shifted to its successor, the Super Hornets. Lot 20 F/A-18C Hornets were retrofitted in mid 2000's adding things like the AIM-9X. It's the MOST MODERN aircraft in DCS at the moment but beware its airframe is not as good in terms of flight performance. Although it may seem a bit underwhelming when compared with other twin-engine aircraft such as the F-15C, Su-27S, and J-11A, this is THE BEST HORNET VARIANT OF ALL IN TERMS OF FLIGHT PERFORMANCE (lot 18, 19, and 20 are the best dogfighting Hornets with more powerful engines than older Hornets, better avionics, AIM-9X operability, and smaller airframe than the Super Hornets). It's better than even the successor F-18E Super Hornet in performance. Mind you that the F/A-18C is a true multi-role aircraft. It's even a swing-role aircraft as you can cover both air-to-air and air-to-ground in the same mission and flight time. You wouldn't be terrific at either when you swing role but you can equip the aircraft for these simultaneous dual roles.

The good things about the F/A-18C are that it can carry more missiles than even the F-15C and its performance is not worse considering it has to be able to land on a carrier that requires many compromises (think strengthened undercarriage and fuselage, tail hook, folded wings, and low speed handling requirement), The level of rigor demanded of airframes to be able to land on a carrier on a frequent basis is not light after all as the very frequent endeavor would put a heavy toll on ordinary airframes. All aircraft can land on a carrier, ONCE. Only a few could take off from a carrier having landed on it and do this repeatedly by the hours over decades.


The Hornet can carry 12 missiles in total. The best combinations are either 10 AIM-120C + 2 AIM-9X OR 6 AIM-120C + 6 AIM-9X. You can still equip it with a single drop tank. For longer combat mission, you can equip the aircraft with 3 drop tanks at the cost of 4 missiles. But with 8 missiles you're still carrying as many missiles the F-15C air superiority fighter can carry.

The AIM-9X can be fired off boresight and thus, compensates for the aircraft's inferior maneuverability and power in a dogfight. The aircraft's airframe itself isn't that bad in terms of dogfighting capability. You can kill MiG-29, Su-27, F-15C, J-11A (half fuel), even the lightweight Mirage-2000C in guns only dogfight.

The F/A-18C would make a very good coop aircraft for online squadrons for its limiting performance forces people to cooperate better. The DLC immerses you in role playing an F/A-18C pilot as well. It's the most modern and versatile playable aircraft in DCS. In this regard alone, it's worth buying. It's almost like cheating. It's also ridiculously easy to learn. Even easier to learn than the already easy-to-learn Mirage-2000C which I also highly recommend.

I almost forgot to mention this but this aircraft is the first aircraft in DCS with full night operation capability. The cockpit is NVG compatible and you can use NVG now to look outside. I still shiver flying in the dead of night. It's dark...You have to try it to understand it. The NVG gives you an eerie tunnel vision that adds to suspense. I think it's either a second or third generation NVG.\t\t\t\t\t
