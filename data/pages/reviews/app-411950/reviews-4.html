\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot the fastest fighter, not the best ranged fighter. It doesn't carry the biggest bomb load. It does not have the most powerful radar. Probably not the prettiest or most majestic looking fighter. What it has going for it is much harder to see than the paper stats that might shine in some magazine cover. The Hornet is a jack of all trades and a master of almost none. But what it does, it can do very well. Air to air, dog-fighting, close-air-support, tactical strike, SEAD/DEAD (Killing SAMs), naval strike, carrier operations, night operations, land operations; yup the Hornet can do it and do it well. With the flick of a switch, the Hornet can change roles and be effective at self-escort.
The Hornet features one of the first 'glass cockpits' in a production fighter which grants it the ability to be flexible in-mission and presents you with IMO some of the easiest to find and concise data of any of the modern fighters in DCS. Common systems are clumped together in the cockpit and data entry is all done in one centralized area. This makes managing the fight and the flight systems including complex weapons less demanding to the pilot giving you more brain bandwidth to tackle other tasks. Many fighters which came later modeled themselves after the Hornet's cockpit.
Flying the Hornet is fairly easy, it's digital flight control system makes the aircraft behave very docile and stable and yet extremely agile. At low speeds and angle of attack is where the Hornet truly is a master. The airplane just hangs on and can continue to pull off maneuvers at speeds lower than 200kn. In a dogfight where the aircraft have been brought down to slow speeds by hard maneuvers, the Hornet is a truly deadly foe and will likely be the last plane flying. It's weapons load is not that of an A-10 but is nothing to dismiss.
It can carry a vast number of air to air missiles including the dreaded "Spamraam" configuration (yes I know, massive eyerolls from veteran DCS players). Air to ground the inner and outer wing pylons are capable of carrying the same weapon types including JDAMs. Some fighters such as the F-16 can only carry these smarter weapons on one station per wing. This gives the Hornet a lot of flexibility in weapon loads as well as rather hefty smart bomb loads for missions that do not require long range.
The Hornet also gets some specialty weapons such as the Harpoon, SLAM, SLAM ER which are only carried by a select few aircraft. They offer a fun dynamic to the game IMO. Carrier ops are rather intimidating and rightly so for a beginner but they really add to the experience once you get the hang of them. It is hard to go back to long boring runways sometimes.
Ultimately I find the Hornet is greater than the sum of its parts and my favorite modern fighter in DCS. It is a good fighter if you are a beginner and a craft that is layered enough to keep an expert pilot satisfied. It is also a good gateway into other more tricky fighters if you wish to specialize in those later.\t\t\t\t\t
