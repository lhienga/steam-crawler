\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you can own just one complex plane in DCS, make it this one.

Surprisingly, for a so-called 'complex' plane, the Hornet is much easier to fly than most low-complexity (Flaming Cliffs) planes. This is because of its benign, fly-by-wire assisted, low-speed handling, and its carrier-capable landing gear that make - ahem - marginal landings survivable. Compared to, for example, the Eagle, landing the Bug is much easier: simply place the 'spiky circle' on the runway, reduce speed to 150 knots and fly it down.

The Hornet offers lots of fun even for inexperienced pilots because of its forgiving flight characteristics and the fact that even though it can - if you choose so - offer lots of complexity, you can basically fly it only requiring control for stick, thrust, gear and flaps. This is not in small part because much of the highly varied complexity is controlled by the three multi-function screens that newcomers can ignore and focus on flying the plane. Being fly-by-wire, there is no need for constant trimming, your on-board computers take up that burden. As a result, the Hornet flies easier than, say a Cessna Skyhawk or Piper Cherokee - or, to stay in-game, the Flanker, Tiger, Frogfoot, or even the Hog.

The great thing about the Hornet is that it's *accessible*: start flying right away, and learn the more complex things later on, and on your own pace. There is very little up-front learning involved: start the engines, up-throttle, pull back on the stick. This is what sets the Bug apart from other high-complexity planes such as the A-10C (I and II), F-5E or Harrier (with the possible exception of the Jeff). Start flying right away, master landings quickly. The next step, learning how to navigate is actually fun with the Hornet (unlike other planes - i'm looking at *you* Hog!), as it only requires you to learn how to switch to the HSI screen. As you learn how to master that screen, more and more options open up almost naturally, and you soon discover the myriad of possibilities that these three screens offer.

This is in stark contrast to the undisputed king of complex models, the A-10C. The Hog *is* definitely the epitome of sim engineering, and a fantastic experience. But by comparison, learning the Hog is work, while learning the Hornet is fun. So if you are looking for fun, choose the Hornet. If you are looking for a challenge, give wings to the pig. And unlike the Hog, the Hornet can be your first paid-for module *and* be enjoyable. That's maybe the reason why for every hour I've spent flying the Hog, I spent three in the Bug's cockpit: fun versus accomplishment.

Recently, some more high complexity, highly accessible modules have become available, and they promise to be as much fun as the Hornet; namely the Viper (F-16) and Jeff (JF-17), with the Jeff surprisingly leap-frogging the Falcon in terms of sheer fun and maturity. Today (late 2020) both models require significantly more polish before they can compete with the Bug, though.

Finally, for VR players the Hornet offers an additional important boon: due to the heavy reliance on the buttons arrayed around the three MFD, operating the many, many functions merely requires you looking at at button and clicking it - much simpler than remembering a switch on your HOTAS. In VR, the Bug becomes much, much simpler to operate than most lo-complexity planes that rely on many (hardware) buttons on your stick.

If you've just installed the free base DCS game and now you're looking through the list of modules and thinking "Wow, these are quite expensive" and are wondering which ones you should buy? Then this plane right here is where you should start.
For a modern era multi-role war plane, chock full of (relatively) modern avionics, the F/A-18C is easy(ish) to fly.
The fly by wire flight control system will hide many sins, and will get you up and flying straight away, even if you have little to no flight experience either real or simulated.
In this plane you can carry out arguably the widest range of roles in the whole of DCS, as this plane will attack ground targets and airborne targets with equal aplomb. The range of munitions and equipment that can be carried on this bird is impressive, and the array of on board equipment, such as the multi-function air to air and air to ground radar, will allow you to put those munitions exactly where you want them.
The Hornet lacks power though, and cannot hope to climb and zoom with the likes of the F-16 and F-15, so can be out matched in beyond visual range engagements. However, it's low speed and high angle of attack performance is excellent which, with practice, will allow you to hold your own in close up ACM. You can really haul the nose of the Hornet around.
It's quite east to take off and land in the Hornet, and for the shear amount of usage, I can't think of another DCS plane module that gives as much bang for your bucks.\t\t\t\t\t

Stings like hornet and it's as hard to learn it as chasing the hornet in your room through the window

F/A-18C Hornet is definitely one of the best DLC's for DCS world. I heard a lot of praise and couldn't resist so i bought it when it was 50% off. This was my first full fidelity plane for DCS. I knew it's extremely complex so I will need a lot of time learn. Was not disappointed, still a lot to learn, feels like I barely scratched the surface. I'm not a hardcore “this is not a game hurrdurr” simmer so don't expect any in-depth look at systems from me. I just want it to feel real and be close to a real life plane.

It's one of the most modern planes for DCS, i'm into modern ones with lots of electronics, guided weapon systems and so on. Besides it's quite iconic and is used by many NATO and other countries.
Hornet is a multirole plane. This was also one of the reasons why I chose it. I love having some air-to-air action and bombing commies on the ground and this baby does it all pretty good.

I know this may sound quite contradicting, I mean getting one of the most complex planes as your first DLC. It definitely isn't noob friendly, it's the opposite. But why I love it and recommend it for noobs is that it's capable of so many things. You can learn air combat, ground bombing and carrier operations. Got tired of air combat? Then try bombing or carrier landings. It will teach you everything. You'll learn how to work with radar, how to track targets, launch rockets, distance of rockets, how to evade rockets, how to drop unguided bombs, how to drop guided bombs, litening pod and tracking the targets on the ground. Not even mentioning carrier ops. Oh and air refueling – hardest task in the whole game. I don't think I will ever be able to do that but you can try, maybe you will succeed.

Flying it is pretty easy (as in stay in the air and not crash) because of its fly-by-wire system and autotrim. There's a nice autopilot too which is very useful while you're flying to your target and setting up your weapons and other stuff. You can use it as a cheat for air refueling too.
Of course everything in this plane is working, all the switches and knobs, radar, weapon systems, it's as close as it gets to a real plane.

You'll be overwhelmed by stuff you can do, won't know anything if it's your first full fidelity DLC. But it's fun, the process of learning is very rewarding. The very first time you take out the target and return home, the very first time you land on a carrier – your hard work pays off.

I just wish it would come with few campaigns, for this price i'd expect it to have 2-3 long and immersive campaigns.
Also i'd expect to get a discount on supercarrier when buying this. Maybe make a bundle or something? Supercarrier and Hornet goes along very well and that would increase the sales of both.

Graphically it looks stunning. Exterior and interior are very well done, the tiniest details are there. Download yourself some cool liveries and go on a mission dropping bombs from anime themed plane.

Don't know how the thing sounds in real life so it's hard for me to judge, but I guess the sounds are good? Well it feels like a beast.

Overall a very good starting plane for noobs because it teaches you everything. It is extremely complex and it will take a lot of time, 50-100 hours I guess, but it's rewarding. If you're experienced then much less of course.
The price is crazy of course like any other DCS DLC so it's a risky buy without trying. Wait for 50% off at least.

Pros
[/list]
  • Deep systems&extremely complex and hard to learn
  • Great if you want to learn everything
  • Lots of modern stuff to help you out
  • Looks great
Cons
  • Price...
  • Few campaigns should be included
  • No discount for supercarrier

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is easily the best module for DCS and along with F-16, the easiest to enjoy.

The F-18 is a powerful jet that carries a lot of ordinance and fuel. It is one of the better jets - if not the best- to dogfight with in multiplayer.

F-18 can land on the ground or a carrier and has more forgiving landing gear than just about any other jet. It doesn’t tip as easily as the F-16 when taxiing too fast.

F-18 has far more ability than the A-10 or F-16, although the F-16 is easier to equip with AMRAAM missiles.
