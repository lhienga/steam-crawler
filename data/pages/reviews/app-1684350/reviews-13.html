\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll in all i can recommend this game, it has a lot of Love put into the World design and backgrounds, and the Devs obviously put tons of time into doing research on things "in ye olden Days" there are some caveats however.

First off it's sort of short for this sort of game, 30 hours and thats it, including doing every Sidequest and really reading everything you find, so someone rushing it is probably done in half that.

Second, on the technical aspect: it looks decent, but nothing to write home about, thats running it on a 4090. It has Bugs and random crashes, but in 30 hours it bricked itself twice and glitched out maybe 3 times. One Encounter was flat out broken and starting it would crash the game every time (Sailors in the Slum).

Third: If you expect a RPG with Loot and deep Skilltrees/Mechanics, look elsewhere. The game has some decent options and nice combos you can get going, but overall i ended up with mostly running Suffering and things that slap down Focus.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to love this game, I was counting down the hours for release. The setting looked gorgeous. It had fun spooky lads. A cool folky vibe. Instant wishlist.

But is it boring. Just follow the red line and click the thing it leads to for HOURS. The dialogue is repetitive if you speak to the same person more than once, some NPCs have no idea what I've already done (e.g. one TRIUMPHANTLY DEMANDED that I do something for her that I'd already kindly agreed to do 45 seconds earlier). It's a walking sim with reading breaks, I wanted a dark RPG.

It's like the inverse of Lethal Company. The dude who made that knew what was fun and put it together on an ugly (yet charming) shoestring. These guys know how to make a nice isometric market but have no idea how to make an enjoyable player experience.

Ofc this is all personal preference, and maybe having played BG3 as my last campaign game is casting a heavy shadow on this, but I'm pretty sure it is missing something.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPasst game compeltion:
I need a thumb that points left or right, instead of up and down. Sadly after finishing the game I have to say that it has many issues, it is buggy, the atmosphere does not get better at all, the music is bad and gets much worse over the time, since it likes to loop on itself very often and the track is quite short, in addition it also has pauses between loops which is an issue since there are only very few ambient sounds, especially indoors. Sentences get cut off at the end of recordings, echo gets worse the further you get in the game... Simply said, the sound engineer was either an amateur or phoned the whole job in. Terrible sound design, just absolutely awful, and since half of your gameplay is sound, this just drags the whole game down. They would have made a better job if they just played the whole time clown music, at least they would have had some atmosphere.

Combat:
And combat, My god the combat. It is not bad, do not get me wrong. It works like a puzzle. I played on challenge and it is actually very challenging.... sometimes... and sometimes if it laughably easy. The bad part is, that you can fight two groups of enemies, located 20 meters of each other in the same area, one group is pathetically easy, and the other is the most brutal combat you will ever face in any game... The issue is not that the combat is bad, the issue is that the battles are not well balanced.

Character builds:
The game would like you to believe that it has a talent tree, it does not. It works more like a battle pass for Talent Points. Since combat requieres you to have a certain amount of points in every "branch" you literally can not specialize, you have to progress in all branches at the same pace, especially on challenge difficulty. Since ever combat will have one trait of each type at the same level that you need to overcome that trait. So any talent choice is an illusion.

All in all, it is a double A tittle with reduced price. So it passes. But not with flying colors. It passes with a scratched butt.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAchievements bugged out. Almost finished second act. Caught all Salutors. Did every quest I could.

Game suffers from overabundance of ambition and lack of execution. It might try to convince you that there is a fun demonic combat to be had and adventure-like, detective type work to do. Neither is true.

Combat is your typical one-action-per-round turn based combat with very slight variation. All in all, because of how incredibly easy the combat is, it doesnt amount to much. Becomes very repetitive very fast.

There is no detective work to be done. You dont even have to read most of the text from the clues you keep picking up- the main character will outright tell you what happened when you collect all the clues in the set. Just pulse your batman vision like madman and collect all clues until the game gives you a solution. Well done detective!

The setting might be interesting if you are a Pole, like Polish history or like the year 1905. Otherwise it amounts to taking place in a random European city of the era, but with more alcohol consumption.

Audio and visuals are a mess. The voice actors try to affect some sort of Polish/Russian English accent early on, but most give up as the game goes on and just use plain English. The artists knew how to make cool looking Salutors, but apparently their artistic passion doesnt extend to pleasing human facial features. At least Wiktor and Ligia look ok.

Lastly, the game has a terminal lack of systems. Its billed as an RPG, but you have no real inventory, equipment, money and so on. The amount of actual quests (not errands that are all the same) is fairly small.

Hate to be so negative, as I liked the premise and the main character, but there are better ways of spending your money.\t\t\t\t\t

A Spellbinding Adventure Awaits: The Thaumaturge

Prepare to be whisked away into a world where magic reigns supreme and mysteries abound with "The Thaumaturge". As an avid gamer and fantasy enthusiast, I can confidently say that this game exceeded all my expectations.

From the moment I launched "The Thaumaturge", I was captivated by its stunning visuals and immersive soundtrack. The attention to detail in every aspect of the game world is truly commendable, from the intricate spellcasting animations to the lush landscapes teeming with fantastical creatures.

But what truly sets "The Thaumaturge" apart is its spellbinding gameplay. As a budding mage, players are thrust into a rich narrative filled with intriguing characters and intricate puzzles to solve. The spellcasting mechanics are both intuitive and incredibly satisfying, allowing for endless experimentation as you harness the elements to overcome obstacles and foes alike.

The game strikes a perfect balance between challenge and accessibility, ensuring that both seasoned wizards and newcomers to the genre can find enjoyment. The pacing is spot-on, with each new discovery and revelation propelling the story forward at a tantalizing pace.

Beyond its captivating gameplay, "The Thaumaturge" also boasts a wealth of content to explore. From hidden secrets tucked away in every corner of the map to side quests that offer unique rewards, there's always something new to discover, making each playthrough feel fresh and exciting.

In addition, the developers have shown a genuine commitment to their community, regularly releasing updates and patches to address player feedback and improve the overall experience. It's clear that they have poured their hearts and souls into crafting a game that not only entertains but also resonates with players on a deeper level.

In conclusion, "The Thaumaturge" is a must-play for anyone who has ever dreamed of wielding magic in a fantastical world. With its breathtaking visuals, engaging gameplay, and rich lore, it stands as a shining example of what a modern fantasy adventure should be. So don your robes, sharpen your wands, and embark on an unforgettable journey into the realm of "The Thaumaturge". You won't be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy play time is a bit inflated, I left it on for about 20 hours on accident.
The game is glitchy, and has gone to a non-responsive black screen twice.
The voice acting is stilted and awkward. I get the impression that they wanted authentic Polish and Russian voice actors, but had few options.
The combat is repetitive. I don't think I've ever come close to losing a fight, using the same formula every time.
The intrigue is buried among a deluge of inconsequential scraps of info.

Overall this game is just boring and slow. Maybe if you're very interested in early 1900's Polish history it will scratch an itch for you, but otherwise spend your money elsewhere.
