\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter receiving all the achievements, I can finally share my full impression.
The game is worth going through it at least once.

Of the big pros for me:
- It's an interesting detective story
- The mysticism associated with the salutors (I wanted to collect them all)
- The mechanics of the clues you collect to solve the case.
- The music in the game is awesome
- People who like to read a lot of notes will also like it (there are plenty of interesting notes).
- Super cool salutor designs

A big bonus for me was the buildings in the game, which are still there in Warsaw. As it was interesting for me to compare these places in the game with real today's places in Warsaw.

Now for a bit of cons:
- I didn't encounter any critical bugs, everything could be reloaded, or a good autosave always saved (good thing I only had crashes a couple of times in several playthroughs)
- battles with opponents will be boring, but this can be corrected by simply catching all the salutors and choosing different tactics
- had trouble understanding how to do some of the achievements

And a small minus for me, the fact that the Upyr didn't talk and didn't make jokes about my actions (but this is generally not a minus, because none of the salutors don't talk to you)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA surpisingly good game that came out of nowhere for me. I mean, I haven't been hyped for it, have barely heard about it. Visual quality is top-notch for an indie isometric RPG, and story is interesting. Combat system... Well, it's not very deep, and for the most of the game, you'll breeze through all encounters, but the final boss fight took me several tries.

I find a small fault with clue-seeking/secret solving mechanic, though. Running around finding clues was more or less fun, but then the game connected the dots for me. That was less fun. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, where to begin?

Story: The story takes place around the period of Russian Revolution in the Kingdom of Poland in alternate universe. In amidst of the worker strikes, spies, industrial revolution, there in lies demons/apparitions called salutors where only thaumaturges (people with sixth sense basically) can see and yield its powers.

Premise: You assist Wiktor solving his father's mysterious death and decide Warsaw's fate by finding clues and connect the dots. Sometimes you'll be in a fight. Sometimes you decide whether you should be deciding with the socialist or with the government. Sometimes you will search for new outfits and sometimes you just stand at the pavement watching people interacts with the environment.

Environment: Gritty, the colours suits the vibe the game is aiming for. Also the buildings are so charming to look at. Also everything looks crispy.

Core mechanics: You will right-click until you find every clues. Is this really a detective game if you only needed to right-click the areas until you find every clues? Yes.

Voice acting: It sounded stiff in some areas. Most of the voices are feel like in a big hall/echoes a lot. If you're easily bothered with how the voice actors act/say their lines, then maybe you'll have some trouble adjusting with it for the whole game.

Leveling up/EXP farming: If you want to level up and max out Wiktor's dimensions, you basically have to do all the side quests; from collecting all the clothes, the sketches, photographs, and side quests. At one point you'll barely feel you're making a progress as the EXP yield in these side quests are so small. This will get tedious; I think this is the part where it feels grindy and filler-ish.

Ending: You'll get one main ending and several mini endings/closure, depending on you choices, what quests you completed, the level of pride you have. I only got one ending and it shooketh me to my core lol

All in all, is this game for everyone? I'd say if you have extra cash and you wanted to have a good/bad time living in alternate universe of Warsaw 1905, then you can buy this game and enjoy every morsel of details being put in the game and leave it at that.

I'm going to keep playing the game for at least 20 more hours.

Also, please Fool's Theory; let me pet the cats, buy the jam doughnuts, have a toggle between running and walking, and have a camera mode. Thank you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompelling take on a choices-matter RPG with really interesting story beats and a reasonable amount of content to sink your teeth into - took about 25 hours to get to /an/ ending but I know there are more out there. A lot of the meat of the game is presented in little blurbs of info you can glean by 'reading' objects in the world so if you don't like reading this might not be the game for you.

The sound design and voice acting are fun and entertaining, and do a lot of work to keep you engaged with the story.

Combat was not the games strong point, with even the hard difficulty posing little problem except at certain difficulty spike points in the game. I found myself vastly outscaling a lot of the encounters just by playing the game normally, especially once you start unlocking more pieces of your powerset that can trivialize certain enemy types. But this is ultimately a minor gripe as the combat was still fun and strategic even if it wasn't particularly difficult.

Some elements of the UI seemed silly to me (both the combat and the level up screen in particular) but they definitely did a lot of work to make sure even the menus fit with the games overall aesthetic, so this really is a nitpick.

All in all, there's nothing bad I can really say about the game & would favorably compare it to games like BG3 and the Shadowrun series in the realm of choices/story/world design. Definitely had a bit of the TTRPG come to life in a video game vibe. So what are you waiting for, go play it!\t\t\t\t\t

Really loved the atmosphere and salutors' designs. Overall, its an interesting setting; kudos to developers for coming up with something fresh in our world of remakes and remasters. You are playing as a polish nobleman in 1905 who has certain paranormal powers, which allow him to sense memories and emotions connected with various personal items plus allowing you to influence peoples' decisions. It makes you a paranormal detective of sorts, which basically describes a huge chunk of the game. You are searching for items, tying them together into conclusions and confront people about their secrets. Also, you're gonna do a lot of fighting. One of the things I learnt from this game is that everyone in Poland wants to beat you, mog you or both :P Dont worry, remember salutors' I mentioned before? Those are the "demons" who are attracted by humans' flaws, which you can tame and use for your own benefit (both in fights and exploration )with a little help from a "certain man, who lived in Russia long ago". Fights are pretty straightforward, there are few tactics to pick from, some are easier than the others. If you'd pick a high difficulty you may struggle in the beginning but it will pass when you'll have more salutors' at your disposal. Still, similar to other reviewers I found the fighting rather repetitive by the end of the game, although I was not really bothered since it was simple.

There are few memorable characters and quite some interesting stories and sidequests, although sometimes it feels like the storytelling is a bit rushed and dialogues become rather flat, uninspired and linear. Luckily, the game is rather short, so you won't get bored, especially if you are not looking all over the place for collectibles. I can strongly recommend looking for sketches though, because these miniquests are rather interesting in terms of world building and its nice to see the effort developers put into crafting alternative Warsaw of early 20th century. Fair warning though, the game is rather heavy on the politics and some things that are included there might be debatable in terms of historical accuracy. I would suggest you to enjoy the personal stories of the characters and their relationships, because some of them are quite touching and heartwarming.

Overall Score: 5.5/10. Didnt really like the endings of the main quest, felt like it was not very well thought through. Otherwise game is worth playing imo. I would recommend taking it during discounts and finishing it once.\t\t\t\t\t
