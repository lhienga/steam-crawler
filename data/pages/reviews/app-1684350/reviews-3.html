\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Thaumaturge is a story based game first and foremost. If you came expecting a dark pokemon/persona style collect the monsters game its not here. In fact the games story will change based more on the relinquishment of said creatures to.

The setting of the story and dark and deals with some hate oriented conflicts. Some hate dealing with nationality, some with religion and being different. So if you aren't into the rhetoric or display of such might want to skip. The story however does explore on the consequences that such fears and prejudices have and handles the material in a very human way. The items that are used to be read and revel information to the player have an impact on dialog options and are used to open avenues of approach in the story. However it is best to read the information as pending on the choice you may get a vastly different outcome of the situation. Not all choices lead to a happy ending. The happy ending isn't always optimal. The setting and political intrigue present in the story is quite enjoyable at times especially with the context of the protagonist being and exile who was never really brought up into the political world. As the protagonist you'll be face with choices that deal with family obligations, old friends, promises made during the course of the story. Each slowly opening or locking later choices. Progression in the story will be from a isometric style view. Sub quest have a time indicator so that you are away if its going to be locked out soon or not which is helpful to place priories and the game does let you know when you are about to hit the point of no return.

Combat is handled in a turn based style with an action sequence bar to help predict or optimize moves. Combat offers a couple interesting was to approach it. Tried and true beat them up with quick weak attacks or slow harder ones. But also presents other options such as DoT style fighting with action delays or debilitating aliments that will lead to powerful finisher attacks. These are all viable and modifiable based on the progression of your Salutors and the path they follow. Progressing down their path opens dialog in story as well as adding side abilities to the style of combat you adapt in the for of equip able effects.

Overall I really enjoyed the game. As a non spoiler suggestion I would say to not go into the game planning on "catching them all". The actions you take effect the story and will lock you out of options if you makes choices that only benefit the protagonist. You also don't get enough experience to cap our the 4 different paths in the games level up system. I feel this is intentional as capping a path grants dialog options that are simply not possible to have otherwise and usually are tied to a particular outcome of the story. My play through involved trying to be a jack of all trades which though allowed me to complete the game I felt the story would've been far more enjoyable if I just focused as some of the missing options later seemed like they could've been critical to a different outcome.

In summery the game is a fantastic story. Characters feel real with presentation, concerns, and motives. Combat tried and true turn based yet satisfying. Collectibles have purpose and meaning. This game is played for the epilogue. It will vary heavily on your choices and is seemingly short enough to play thru multiple times with out a crazy long time sink.

Would recommend to those enjoying the above or detective style games where choices matter.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou get to be friends with Rasputin.

Good game overall. Well written story that is very critical and self-aware with some interesting characters. JRPG and point and click adventure are apt descriptors to keep in mind when going into this game, as the focus is on the narrative with some solid turn-based combat thrown in that showcases the unique Slavic (amongst other cultures) folklore.

Choices do matter, especially in the latter half where they affect multiple aspects of the story. Game drips with gritty, dark atmosphere and for this price I would recommend it. I only hope they expand on the concept and core gameplay in their next game.

A Polish dub with Russian in appropriate parts would be great.\t\t\t\t\t

This is a rather tedious slow pace game, where it doesn't really gives you much liberty on how you decide to play the game, and generally my action don't really seem to have any consequence until the very end of the game. And the games basically is telling the player where to go and what to do, so where is the mystery in exploring?

The bad:
-Side quests are boring, and it feels more like grinding to increase your level.
-Game play is dull and predictable.

The not so bad:
-The world design is nicely done.
-The story at times is interesting, but with the game play you just want to skip the dialogue most of the time.

Not Exactly What I Expected, But Still A Enjoyable Experience.

I was intrigued by this game when I played the demo during last year's Steam Next Fest. The dark fantasy settings and the subtle writing really caught my attention. The story and what's in store later down the line interest me greatly. I imagine the story being similar to that of Jim Butcher's Dresden Files but in Central Europe. Well, what I experience in the full game is not exactly what I anticipated it would be. The story centers more around human struggles and less supernatural stuff, and the gameplay also has some confusing designs. Yet, I still find the The Thaumaturge an interesting ride.

Thing I Like About The Thaumaturge:

Compliments
→The story isn't what I expected, but it's still interesting to see how a thaumaturge manages his way into society.
→I enjoy the writing of this game.
→ I love how the game recreates some of the well-known folklore creatures in its style, eerie but wonderful.
→I actually learned a lot about Polish history.


Things I Like And Dislike About The Thaumaturge:

Combat and Builds
Like
→Builds: Even though you only have four abilities throughout the game, the options to buff those abilities are quite rich. From simple upgrades like more damage and additional debuff, to interesting ones like granting your salutor faster moves or breaking enemies down faster for finishers. It is important to customize each ability to fit the salutor of your playstyle.
→Salutors: Probably the most interesting part of the combat. Salutors have their own abilities, though they are not upgradable. You can freely choose which one to use during battles. Each of them specializes in different aspects of combat: some are great debuffer/finisher, some focus on reducing Focus points, and some are hybrids. It is interesting to note that salutors synergize quite well with each other. With the right combination and your buffed abilities, it is not uncommon to instantly turn the tide in harder battles.
Dislike
→Confusing Design: What I don't like about combat is that you have to use your basic abilities first before you can use your upgraded ones. I genuinely don't know what the logic behind this design is. Not only does this design make the fights, even the easier ones, take longer than they should, but it also takes up space, which could potentially introduce new alternative abilities for true build variety.
→Encounters: I am disappointed to see how little variety the enemy roster contains, and there are no thaumaturge or actual salutor fights either. Enemies only hit you, make you bleed with different knives, and shoot you. That's it. There is no debuffer, no buffer, and no other class. Traits could increase the difficulty of the fight, except we can easily break them with one move from the right salutor.

Things I Dislike About The Thaumaturge:

1. The Whole "Detective" Thing
The other part of the game is to run around the map, spamming your perception button, to see if any red particles concentrate on one spot. This is how you "detect" clues. Once Wiktor collects all the clues, he will automatically conclude the solutions or ideas himself. This design may appeal to those who just want to get through the story as fast as possible, but to me, it makes the whole situation less interactive on the player's part. There is no secret component on items for you to interact with and discover, and there is certainly no need for you to actually piece the clues together.

2. Other complaints
→ I wish the whole "feed your Flaw" system could have more impact than dialogue choices. Other than Wiktor's downfall, Upyr could also benefit from the evolving Flaw and become stronger in combat. The same deal goes with all the other salutors.
