\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I like the game but there are two things I don't.:

First off, I am not a fan of turn based combat. I played through the game on the hardest difficulty and found the combat boring and monotonous. Probably even worse on the easier difficulties. Not to mention, I got all the way to the end boss having one stat a point too low. This made the fight exponentially more difficult because I lacked the stat level to remove certain buffs. The game also reloaded right into the boss fight every time so I had to keep changing my attack loadout each and every time I died. All the previous autosaves before that were cutscenes leading up to the last boss so there was no way out of it. Quite annoying.
Secondly, the dialogue can be annoying. There are times were you are often unable to complete a conversation and or progress in the story because you haven't wandered around the different areas of the map to find all the clues. Basically you will be stuck until you find everything they want you to find.

Besides those things, the game has an interesting story and good graphics. There is a lot of decisions you can make that will have interesting impacts on the story. It also runs pretty well with the latest patch. The initial release had some issues when loading in areas during travel. The combat is probably the worst part of the game but there is a lot more to enjoy besides that. If you are into turn based combat then I would recommend this game at the full price. If you aren't really a fan of turned based combat, I would probably wait for a sale for $20. Also, the game does not have modern ideologies in it. It is true to the time period so it is nice and easy to get immersed in this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite its many, many shortcomings the game has an atmosphere. The music score and the visuals are beautiful and well executed. Dark, sordid yet pleasantly calming somehow. That is the best and the only plus so far. Everything else is not so good. But atmosphere is a redeeming quality, so I'd give this game a thumbs up.

Major problem, however, the game keeps crashing. And crashing. And crashing. Making the game borderline unplayable and certainly disrupting the atmosphere.

Dialogs are stuporous and mechanical. Though voice acting is not too bad, the texts themselves are lifeless. NPCs too - they just sit or stand around, like they have nothing to do or worry about.

Combat is repetitive, and any originality is quickly snuffed out by boredom.

I keep trying to rotate the scene, but it just doesn't, and it is annoying since there is a feeling that it should be able to.

There are a few klyukvish moments (the names, the references, the tzar etc), but I must admit it could be much worse, especially due to the presence of Rasputin, the King of Klyukva himself. Yet, he is much less weird than one would expect in such a setting.

The story could be rather intriguing, but again it is full of cliches, rather generic dark fantasy in pseudo-historical setting. Not too bad, but far from such masterpieces as Vampyr, Planescape:Torment or VtM:Bloodlines.\t\t\t\t\t
