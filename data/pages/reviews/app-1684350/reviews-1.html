\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPROS:
-Very interesting combat system that combines Pokemon-like monsters with customizable attack sequences.
-Interesting worldbuilding without a slog of exposition.
-Decisions really do matter.

NEUTRAL:
-Right clicking for the Perception action means you're never in doubt about where to go, but it also means that there isn't much incentive to explore. You pick up almost every quest just by following the red line to the next point.
-I did experience one crash, but it was after playing for several hours in one sitting, and my save files were fine.

CONS:
-There is an autosave at the start of fights, but it doesn't allow you to save after you adjust your combat upgrades, meaning if you fail the combat (usually only during boss fights), you have to set up your combat upgrades again.
-During later parts, responses and reactions sometimes seem like non-sequiturs.
-There is some railroading at times, particularly related to your character's attitude toward certain characters (Wanda and Grigori being the worst offenders).

OVERALL:
Would recommend for anyone who likes Pokemon combat or wants to see it evolved, eastern European folklore and history, and/or story based games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked this game. It's well set up for a sequel. If you like detective games that requires some reading and carefully making dialogue choices you will probably like this. Combat could be better, I enjoy turn based combat systems but this is not for everyone, and even for those who like turn based it still needs some refinement. Main quests and the meaningful side quests were really engaging and i never found myself not invested in what was going on.

The only real gripe I had with the game is there are a ton of "quests" (not main quests, or side quests, but like optional exploration quests) which are almost necessary to have enough experience going into the final act to pass a non insignificant number of skill checks. If you're going to structure your game like this, the content has to be more engaging in my opinion. I did all of them and what it amounts to is find 3 clues, go to place, screen turns black, you get a sketch on your screen, and the quest is complete. I think it's cool to have small stuff like this in a game which encourages exploration and can add context to the world but maybe have the reward be something other than (almost necessary) experience.

The Salutor feature was really cool, honestly I loved it. I really felt like a thaumaturge. To me it sets this game apart as a unique experience and the roleplay element of it is fantastic which has a lot of potential for more.

7/10. Did enough that I would buy the sequel and am hopeful the next game will be more refined. I am the target demographic though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat unique game.

My first play through clocked in at roughly 35 hours, so plenty of content here, and I consider it to be a steal at the current price point. For games like this, if done right, the story is the reward, and I think that applies here. Top notch characters, quests, writing, and lore. I wanted to soak in all the details of Thaumaturgy as well as the historical setting of 19 aught 5 Poland.

As for the turn based combat, my advice is to approach it like a puzzle. What works on one difficulty will work on the others, so don't be afraid to crank it above what you normally would. When you get into a fight that seems insurmountable (typically the salutor acquiring ones), take a step back for a moment, consider the mechanics available to you (and used against you), tweak your build out, and experiment with different approaches. You will eventually find the trick to it and be victorious.

As for bugs, I encountered a few minor ones during my play through, but nothing game breaking. The main annoyance was periodically getting stuck on the environment while exploring. I found that opening and closing the menu would quickly fix this.

So great game, highly recommended, and well worth the asking price. I hope to see more of Wiktor Szulski in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

Anyone who claims to be bored during this game has to be suffering from TikTok brain rot because the story is captivating, characters are memorable, combat is fun, and the fantasy of being a Thaumaturge is almost exactly like the fantasy of being a Witcher. If you are on the fence, just bite the bullet and buy this awesome game!

You play as a Thaumaturge, basically a warlock that can use demons (Salutors) like goth pokemon. There was even a "I wanna be the very best, like no one ever was" dialogue option so that is S+ automatically. You have the ability to absolve people of flaws that draw these demons to them and influence them to commit heinous acts. In this game you will dive into that fantasy, dense historical intrigue with Rasputin, Tsar Nicholas, and many time period relevant factions. You will piece together the mystery of your estranged father's death and chart your own path through the political and occult circles in early 1900s Warsaw.

Overall the combat is fun and reminds me of roguelike combat systems, your choices have real consequences with multiple endings and changing relationships, and the spirit of the game is massive in the way that you can tell it was made with love. I will be singing the praises of this game for a long time.\t\t\t\t\t
