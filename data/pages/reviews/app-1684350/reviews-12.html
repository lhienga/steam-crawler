\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMain plot, side stories, choices and and random interactions were just awesome. From the mechanics perspective, I was actually looking for something, that wouldn't really bother my hands and more let me experience a good story.

I was playing on hard mode, but still only few fights were challenging to me (more about it later).

Pros and cons as same points fall into the same pit:
- The game offers a wealth of choices, but some lack immediate impact, with consequences only revealed during end game.
- Scavenging the world actually boosts your experience, but at the same time makes the game less challenging as you have full skill tree
- Love that turn-based system and all salutors, but I would speed up animations or let them skip and balance things more, because regular fights do not require all salutors


Minor cons
- Missing quest markers on the map
- When you travel to a district, you cannot choose a starting place, so sometimes you have to run on the other side of the district
- 100% GPU usage making my dusted 2080 to spin a lot
- Had few crashes due to upscaling (doesn't matter if that was dlss or any other option), but after turning it off, I didn't have any\t\t\t\t\t
