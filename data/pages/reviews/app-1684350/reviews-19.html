\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts sad I have to honestly put a thumbs down.

Obviously, the team had a creative vision, some good ideas for the atmosphere and setting, but did not have neither talent nor resources to create something interesting in a story-driven RPG genre.

The story is boring. Its not AWFUL or IDIOTIC but you can just see the low writing and directing skill. Noir detective style stories are a trap - they have to be really captivating, smart and stylish to avoid boredom, detachment and walls of dull text.

The "detective" part is trivial - you just click on an object of interest and solve all the mysteries. Hell, even old-school puzzles with levers and buttons are more engaging and actually require player attention.

The RPG system is simplistic and barebones.

The gameplay is painfully average, combat system is weak and reminds me of the tainted grail a little - even though there was a lot of emphasis on difficulty spikes, experimentation and deckbuilding, here we have like 1\\5 of good old rusty tainted grail in terms of tactical combat and builds.

Overall, its like... 5\\10 just for the effort of a small team, maybe? Honestly after a couple of hours feels like even 4.

Sorry guys, you tried!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game delves deep into the polish/slavic mythology and culture, reminiscent of other detective type games with an interactive environment full of clues, important figures, angry spirits, and a fight is always looming around the corner. Along with branching choices that make you want to go back and try the other route.

The highlight of this game is both the theme and the combat. The atmosphere is steeped with culture, while the combat breaks the information gathering monotony at just the right times. You "collect" spirits and use them against your enemies to create well orchestrated combos with a very unique fighting system featuring a timing mechanic. You alternate between using your own attacks and commanding spirits to take a chomp out of your enemies, and can even use them to influence people's minds.

Some of the investigation reminds me of The Witcher series, but so far I'm really enjoying my time as a Thamaturge!
