\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very climatic adventure game with tons of collectibles about historical Warsaw. The environment and storyline are pushing the overall score to 10/10, while character progress and fighting are dragging it down to 7/10. Side-quests were my favorite part of the game - nice writing there, mates!

Graphic is just enough. Nothing too bad, nothing too good. It's unique, which makes you forget about issues here and there.

...

Some notes after finishing the first playthrough:
- Please rework the character skill-tree to be more meaningful to the story and distinctive. Right now, I just dump my exp into some characteristics, which do not really impact anything. Having a high Heart gives access to better skills and some dialogue options, but so do others. Give me a real choice that describe my character's progress. Disco Elysium might be a good inspiration on how to make interesting character development even in a completely non-fighting game.
- Please, remove 90% of unnecessary fights. Even though they are dynamic, they are boring. This game looks like it is advertising a punch in the face as the best option to resolve any situation. I get the idea, but sometimes less is better.
- Majority of quests are tagged as time-limited, which makes the unnecessary feel of urgency. Please tone it down, or explain better how the time passes.
- Some QoL around finding clues on the streets, that not require using the 6th sense constantly?
- Do I really have to collect everything? Exp is necessary to get skills and then salutors, which are needed to get better salutors and some advantages during dialogues. It's the GET THAT DAMN POSTCARD all the way, isn't it?

Overall, the game lacks depth in its current state, but it is a perfect ground for improvement. I expect more from this world. DLC's with new engaging stories or new iterations of thaumaturges. Let me pick myself a salutor and a flaw, and put me in the middle of some interesting drama.
Thanks!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was pretty good. I enjoyed the story and setting a lot. It's fairly fast to beat, but based on the story choices, it may be worth a second play through making other choices. The choices in this game really do matter. I made some choices and still feel bad for some of them. That's the type of impact they may have.

The voice acting is excellent. Also, the combat is pretty entertaining. I'm not a big fan of turn based combat, but there was a decent amount of strategy in the fights.

Graphically, it's nice to look at. Most the game is played via a top down diablo-style view. But the conversations and such are more third person - and there are a lot of conversations.

The idea that you are doing detective work is interesting, but in reality you're just button mashing R2 until something turns red and looking at it. There's not any strategy in the detective part, but it does fill in lore and story.

I played the game in VR using UEVR. It ran well and was a lot of fun in VR.

I'd recommend this as a buy if you enjoy RPG games and good story. It took my 23 hours to beat on normal difficulty and I did as many side quests as I could find.\t\t\t\t\t
