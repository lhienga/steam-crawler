While some of the in-game models such as the "Salutors" were incredible, most of the NPCs were kind of phoned in. Also, some of the voice acting wasn't the greatest outside of the protagonist. This game also evolves heavily based on your actions, so much so that I'm thinking about playing this again at some point to see what other outcomes I can get. For a turn-based noob like me, this was very forgiving on "normal" difficulty. It had challenge, but it felt doable. It also incorporated some different mechanics than what I've seen from other turn-based games I've tried such as BG3 and Super Mario RPG.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game. It was a lot of fun. i beat it last night and stayed up way too late to do it. The story was very interesting. The characters were amazingly wrote. I enjoyed every tidbit of information i read, it was really immersive. It was exciting finding all the clues and exploring the areas. Each area having its own unique feel and charm alongside the people who inhabited them. I found all 8 salutors... do your sidequests ;). I had favorite moves for each. The turn based fights never got repetitive. I enjoyed the puzzleness to them and the animations that followed.
Wiktor had a lot of funny one liners and you just wanted to keep stroking his pride to hear them. I enjoyed the dialogue and its interesting as an English speaker to hear different terms and ways of saying things. It kept the feel of it being in the past. There were a lot of tough choices to pick through, some minor that you learned later on had consequences. The end fight was really fun and challenging. It definitely felt like the main boss fight.
This leads the game to be replayed to see it all especially with multiple endings. If you want a secret... you cant get every single thaum skill which i enjoyed learning a little too late.
This isnt to say there arent bugs.. there are but they are no big deal... some subtitles are off from whats being said, some animation issues like a weird head shake in cut scenes that goes away quickly, & props sometimes clipping... it happens. There was only one fight i couldnt do because it wouldnt transition from the conversation to the fight.. i just got a black screen (fight with some sailors that had ladies with them). That's really the only thing i couldnt do.
I would love to see the other flaws you take on have their own dialogue options. That would be really neat and immersive. It felt like they really had no consequences or meaning to them. Some gave you stat bonuses or buffs but they were just kinda there no matter how many you took on.. only your main one really mattered. I did enjoy the main flaw having its own invisible meter which locked out or made available dialogue options. That was a really neat feature.
I do highly recommend this game, it was very entertaining and fun. I was addicted and wanted to just keep coming back to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; Good game, not perfect, doesn't need to be. I am having a great time, but I would like to see a little bit more done with it.


While only about 12 hours in, and not sure how far into the main story I am, I do want to say that I think this game is great. Making a new IP is difficult, especially in our current age of sequelitis and reboots out the ass, and making something like this is no small feat. Games are hard, who knew?

Fool's Theory have done a really solid job making this city feel lived in. It's no Night City, but maybe that's for the best. All the little Points of Interest, the sketches and fashion magazines and incidental moments (like being ogled by women on the street), framed against this deeply intriguing story that blends history with fantasy, makes The Thaumaturge's depiction of Warsaw feel very much alive.

The detective mechanics, while simple and not exactly rewarding for the exceptional snoop (all the clues and conclusions being drawn for you), do provide meaningful interaction and the simple fact the game TELLS YOU when you have locked yourself out of certain choices makes it narratively satisfying, even if it's a little repetitive in execution. On that point, one thing I would've loved to see is more use of the Visions Upyr bestows upon you, perhaps featuring the other wild Salutors; giving us greater immersive context into the people we are investigating. Someone like Abaurycy or Vesna would have been perfectly used for this.

I think Rasputin's presence in the game, while entertaining and welcome, is a little shoehorned in. If he was a persistent accompaniment to Wiktor, instead of another location on the map of Warsaw, with more input on the story, i think I would buy his presence in the game much more. But again, I'm only 12 hours in and I've been distractedly running around looking for fashion magazines and laughing that the game has no clean-shaven options at the barber.

As far as combat, it's perfectly serviceable. I do think, however, that the thaumaturgy points are awarded MUCH too freely; even reading repeated advertisements or articles that do not provide anything for quest objectives give experience; I have maxed out my Heart and Deed trees (up until the second Salutor) and I only just acquired the third Salutor in the entire game. Given that there appears to only be a total of 8 Salutors, and I still feel rather early in the story, I find myself rather worried I will max out my skill tree and have nothing to try out next time I play this game. Plus, playing on the hardest difficulty I still find myself steamrolling most enemies simply through abusing Suffering and interruptions; enemies simply don't deal enough damage to be threatening enough, and certain combinations of attacks are simply far too powerful. I would like a future update that expands combat a little more, especially since the majority of the actual fight seems-- unwieldy. Mostly fisticuffs with the occasional revolver shot against people with knives, guns, and even full-ass swords.

Most of the music also feels relatively unremarkable as well; not that it doesn't serve it's purpose, it's very immersive, but even the main menu theme doesn't have anything that makes me want to whistle or hum with it. It's rather tragic because I think a game like this could really shine with a strong soundtrack. You have things like the Hurdy-Gurdy, the Balalaika, the fiddle, all featured in the game, I think using instruments like that more prominently for the music would do wonders.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was a marvelous, fresh experience. The Thaumaturge is a fascinating game.

It reminds me a lot of The Witcher 1, even though on the surface they are completely different games. They both have that slightly janky European style, a fascinating main character with a brilliant voice, a quasi-realistic setting with a mysterious, magical flavor, more morally ambiguous characters than you can count and clunky combat (each in their own way, of course). But most importantly, they both have an atmosphere and story in both main and side quests that motivated me to search every nook and cranny to find all about it. Is it coincidence that both stories come from Poland? Maybe, maybe not. All I know is that I am really glad that stories like these are being told and I can experience them.

The game is not without its minor flaws. While voice acting is generally superb, the spoken text sometimes does not match the subtitles, especially in the latter half of the game. Combat is a change of pace at best and a distraction at worst and feels unnecessary. Finding and taming salutors has not much other purpose than to improve combat option variety and open some dialogue options, but I feel they could have done more to make them feel more special and not like creepy pokemon. And finally, the game performance can be sporadically abysmal even on very high end hardware and the reason is unclear: I will a few more patches would do the game good and ensure that the legacy of The Thaumaturge will be of a cult classic.

The best moments of the game are its story and its fantastic characters and it is in the dialogue scenes where everything comes together almost perfectly. The music also improves the mysterious and creepy undertones of many conversations in "thaumaturgy dark land". There were a lot of great moments in this.

In summary, I very strongly recommend this game if you are interested in the premise and love a dark, mysterious atmosphere in your stories. Be warned that combat is not the main point of the game and that there is "Euro jank", but in a good way.\t\t\t\t\t
