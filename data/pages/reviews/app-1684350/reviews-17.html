\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has an interesting idea behind it. The idea of going around and catching monsters that inflate and feed off of people worst emotions and then using those same monsters to further your own goal is a lot of fun in my opinion.

The gameplay is entertaining, the story is fun so far, and the characters are memorable for the most part.

The game does have some issues from what ive experienced so far however. My main problem is with the dialogue. There have been times when ill be talking to an npc when they will say something that feels a little out of left field, or Wiktor will get asked a question and then no matter what option you pick he will just ignore whats asked of him. Its pretty easy to ignore for the most part and doesnt really ruin the experience at all, it just breaks the immersion a tiny bit.

The combat system is simple and easy to grasp, and feels satisfying when you are able to plan and combo your and your Salutors moves together to wipe an enemy out. The fights for capturing your soon-to-be companions are also really cool and thematic, especially after the build up of having to figure out who they are attached to.

All in all: Games fun, would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst things first: don't be confused by the turn based combat and the complex story with multiple sidequests: this is not your run-of-the-mill RPG. If anything, this game is something like a Sherlock Holmes game with a few combats sprinkled here and there. Something like if one of the Vampire The Masquerade visual novels had a child with Pokemon (I'm afraid you'll only get this last part if you play the game).

That does not mean, in any way, that the game is bad. On the contrary, this is one of the best games I've played lately, but if you come here expecting to skip every dialogue and jump right into the action, you are going to be very, very disappointed. If anything, the game has TOO MUCH combat for my taste. I often find myself trying to do the fights as fast as possible so I can go back to the mystery solving.

You will also be extremely disappointed if you expect to play with a character that speaks and acts like you: you will make many decisions and get to very different outcomes than other players, but the main character, Wiktor Szulski (yes, I had to google the name) has his own personality, and you will have to go through that.

On the technical aspect, I've had a few fps drops here and there, but still nothing too important, and I definitely didn't find any gamebreaking bugs. Many so called triple A's could learn a thing or two.

In summary, if you are looking for a game with a strong story component and some greatly captivating lore, this is for you. On the other hand, if you are looking for a Baldur's Gate type of RPG, you better find something else. But if you do, be advised: you are missing on a product with an amazing story.\t\t\t\t\t
