\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, The Thaumaturge has some performance jank. The animations can be a little stiff and there are some visual bugs at times. So far, I've only had one actual crash, though. And at the time of writing this review, I've played for 35 hours.

Some people have said this is a point and click walking simulator or even a pixel hunting kind of game. Which, I don't understand where that's coming from. But, I will say that if you're struggling to see the "Hey, look at this!" visual, then you may be somewhat colorblind. I don't mean that as a slight. The sparkly effect is mostly red. But it is noticeable and gets moreso when you snap and/or get closer to where the object is. Maybe tweak the colorblind mode and see if that helps.

This game is an RPG with turn-based combat. You don't change your stats via equipment as in some RPGs, but you do change your attack effects via your choice in salutor actions and adjusting things that modify the attack chains you have access to. An attack could inflict max damage every time. Or double damage (or more) based on other factors. Or, maybe an attack will have a chance to negate an enemy action when a status is applied.

There is a LOT of customization in how you set up your combat flow. So the reviews saying the combat is simple are apparently playing a different game. Or maybe they stopped before the more difficult effects and combat modifiers were introduced.

As for the flavor and story, this game has it in spades. This alternate 1905 Warsaw is rich in life. It feels very lived-in: with all the beauty and misery that comes with it. It is nearly equal parts lush and gritty. The textures in this game are just so good. The high-class areas feel like appropriately posh, with an edge of tension with the occupying Russian guards watching from a distance. And, the down and dirty poverty-stricken areas feel outright squelchy and you can almost smell them.

It may not feel like it as much early on, but your actions absolutely do have weight. There are several with potentially devastating outcomes. Allies may be made or lost depending on your choices. And, of course, how much Pride you cultivate throughout will also affect the story. You may get beat up a lot more if you're a smarmy, uh, jerk.

While I understand that this game won't sit well with everyone, I do think that its current "Mostly Positive" is a bit unfair. This game has a lot of charm and I definitely recommend checking it out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: Solid 9/10, most of my complaints are minor.

Pros:
+ Fascinating setting
+ Excellent writing in most places
+ Solid soundtrack
+ Graphics are (mostly) good
+ Combat is simple, but satisfying
+ Art direction on the Salutors is super cool

Cons:
- Awful voice acting most of the time, only characters with decent VAs are the main cast.
- Lots of reused assets and contrived ways to reuse previous areas. It is a AA game after all, but it is one of the primary ways the budget of the game rears its ugly head in the end product.
- Detective segments are for flavor more than anything else. Whether it's a contrivance or a solid mechanical inclusion depends on your personal opinion.
- Dialogue writing specifically sucks. The dialogue writing is worse than the writing you'll find in all of the pieces of writing and item descriptions you'll find. Don't know why.

Really interesting setting, you can tell the devs have a certain affection for Poland and an interest in the time period. It tackles some social issues of the time, but often in ways that you may not notice unless you're already familiar with the history of the setting. If you are familiar, though, it will be pretty hamfisted. The flavor text is always very good. The dark and seedy tone of the game resonates throughout. Only a few cons I can think of, and none of them are serious. 9/10, Great game, you should play it!

Story
Your decisions actually matter, and have a high impact on the story, makes you think about your every word.
And the story is AMAZING, I intentionally missed some side quests(which are great as well) because the main story was so interesting that I wanted to progress in that.

I really liked the fact that the focus was on the investigation throughout the game. You had to investigate different objects to find out different important things about the main story.

Combat
The combat system was OK. I haven't seen one like this so it was something new, which is always appreciated.
The animations of your salutors (demons, who help you during combat) were really well-done.

Movement
The movement was a bit annoying, many times move buttons wouldn't work after using a door.

Names

As some one who doesn't speak Polish it was a bit hard to memorize most of the names, but obviously they have to have Polish names since the whole game takes place in Warsaw.\t\t\t\t\t
