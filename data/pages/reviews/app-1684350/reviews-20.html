\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame has it's flaws. It crashes, has graphical artifacts and issues. May seem confined at times. But it is incredible atmospheric and unique. You are drowned in early XX century Poland, at the edge of incredible political and historical changes (Prior to WW I and partitioned between Russia, Austo-Hungarian empire and Germany). Beautiful work was done to depict the time, social complexity, many cultures and nationalities in one spot fighting for their influence, and Warsaw in its unique style specific for that time period. You can believe this was "The Paris of the eastern Europe". Nicely rooted in Slavic mythology and spirituality which makes it unique gem among other games of this kind. To be fair it is a detective story with magical arch and mechanic comparable to games like Disco Elysium in game play mechanics. I pity that there is no Polish audio in the game as this would add to the game style and feel especially that dialog written in Polish use specific for 1900's Warsaw street slang called 'gwara'.
Story is very nice and engaging. Based on family secrets in historical perspective.

For interested parties (Works well on SteamDeck - could be optimized better. Around solid 30 FPS)

+ Incredible feel of the period
+ Storytelling that is complex and not dumbed down.
+ Good mechanic
+ Rooted in Slavic mythos
+ Great choice of historic period which is not often depicted
+ Price to content ratio
+ Good flow with focus on story. Not too difficult making it easy to immerse yourself.
+ No woke BS overflowing even though respectful for all kinds of views and perspectives.

- Graphical glitches
- Crashes (3 in 6 hours of gameplay, hope to be solved after patches)
- Pity you have fixed camera angle and can't turn it around (it is 3D space after all)
- Graphic is nice but not stunning.
- May be too historically complex for people used to simple stories and good-bad narratives.

Not a masterpiece, but a nice little gem of a game.
