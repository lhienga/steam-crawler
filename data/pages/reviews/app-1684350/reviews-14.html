\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Thaumaturge is a text-heavy RPG set in an alternate history 1905 Warsaw. The detailed locales, abundance of lore notes, and compelling cast of side characters made this an easy recommend to anyone who enjoys pouring over the small things. Combat and investigations take a back seat to the expansive story, but every aspect of the game works together nicely.

It's been a mostly bug-free and smooth experience which is impressive for a game of this scale. A couple noticeable quirks were the slightly slow loading of textures when fast traveling to different areas (mainly Southern Śródmieście), during which Wiktor will appear half buried in the ground before immediately popping back up. Though I personally wasn't bothered by any of it since it comes and goes pretty quick, I do hope Fool's Theory polishes up the few rough spots the game has in the near future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA well-made isometric RPG. If you liked Disco Elysium, you'll definitely like this one as well.
What stood out to me were the different salutors, so so nicely designed!
The setting was great and the set-pieces/ districts felt very cared for.

Clicking your fingers while walking around places for exp/ collectibles can get a bit tedious, but it didn't take away from my overall experience. Animations can be a bit wacky, but this is a AA title, so I wasn't expecting to see motion capture or insanely well-rigged models. Other than that I have little to complain about, I have a positive aftertaste and I'm looking forward to do a new run where I try to do things differently.

Choices felt meaningful, I really enjoyed how giving in to your Pride takes away your agency of choice, locks dialogue options, but also helps out on occasion.
I've only experienced two of the different endings, but it was great to see how the different character relationships blossom, or fall apart.

Much recommended, you should try it out!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really think this game is interesting, fun, and fresh, but what's holding me back from enjoying it is the fact that I cannot take the narrative seriously due to the voice acting, as well as the strange writing.

Some of the voice actors are amazing, and sell the delivery. A lot of them, however, cause the story to come across as poorly paced, which just doesn't help when you go between the amount of world-building involved in the flavor text of the hints you seek, to the lack of audible narrative. I really think that either finding better voice actors, or keeping voice acting to a minimum in the game, might make for a better experience. It just makes an okay writing of a story feel worse than it actually is in-game.

I would love to see this in a more polished form.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid A tier game with good environments, great writing, acceptable voice acting... and a lot of bugs. Like hoo boy are there a lot of bugs. Crashes too. Every 10 minutes at the least. The prologue was good, but ever since I got to the main Warsaw area, it's been non stop memory leaks and C+ runtime errors. I can't recommend this game to anyone before they fix such basic things.

Setting: The setting is interesting and it's a fresh take on the occult horror mystery genre. The environments work as you might expect and the graphics are decent. Don't expect to be wowed by anything outside the beutiful sketches and photos your character can collect during the game. The game has a disclaimer at the start that ruffled some feathers, but it boils down to the devs saying "we are very diverse, but wanted to make a mostly historically accurate game set in the break of the 20th century so don't get your knickers in a bunch over modern day political issues" which I think both libs and cons took to be aimed at just them, so do be civil about it. From what I've seen it really strives for historical accuracy as well as possible... though the electric trams of Warsova are three years too early!

Writing: The writing style is interesting and doesn't hold your hand too much. Characters might intentionally leave things unsaid or avoid subjects. Some dialogue doesn't flow well into the next line and the character might go from one end of the emotional spectrum to the other between lines, which shows some late editing of dialogue, but it's not too jarring. Sometimes the characters are a bit superficial and wear their hearts on their sleeves which isn't quite typical of slavic people, who are usually far more restrained (when they don't get vodka at least). I would have preferred an even more mature approach where you have to work harder to unravel the Flaws of people, now it's just basically clicking on a few glowy bits on the ground and then using the dialogue that's clearly marked as "this moves the story forward". Maybe it'll get more complicated later on.

VA: Like I said, it is acceptable. There are some really bad deliveries (young Wiktor is atrocious) but for a small indie team I didn't expect anything more. People speak english everywhere and drop random russian/polish words now and then, which is an okay compromise between full english or full polish/russian dub which would have been very problematic for, again, politics. I just really wish people would understand that it's a small indie company and people are complaining about not having their localized subtitles for this and that language, the devs can't do everything and for the time being I'd rather they use their time on fixing bugs than making subtitles in italian, brazilian portuguese or laplandish samic.

Bugs: The game is both buggy and crashy, and that's the reason why my upvote turned into a downvote. Constant crashes and ubiquitous bugs makes this game difficult to play. I'm forced to stop playing until the game gets some patches. There are also a LOT of visual glitches, clothes billowing this way and that, texture pop-in, especially when loading the game. It's not a light game despite being an Indie title as it uses the UE5 engine and needs a better PC than you might expect, so do check the requirements.\t\t\t\t\t
