\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boy............
I really really really wanted to like this game but I have mixed feelings... I would not recommend, but still there are good aspects. I am at Chapter2 (dunno how long is the game) completed side quests, and clicked on everything.

The good:
If you are the type of guy who likes Persona like turned based combat.
You like story driven games with a few choices.
You really have to be interested in the world to push forward.
The atmosphere is fine
The salutors look nice
The Deluxe coat looks fancy :)

However....

The combat even tho I am playing on Challenging so basically the hardest difficulty still "easy" I mean I died a few times, but minimal thinking is enough , as you can't really customise your salutors, you only have limited choice in terms of the MCs abilities.

The combat is forced on you pretty much everywhere. Randomly walking on the street, and if you see a talkable npc , its a fight , no question, I have kicked half of Warsaw's a$$ at this point.
Other then the combat We have to gather clues and information from things, talk to people. Use the previously gathered info to you advantage.
Reading is key point.. or as it should be, but even when you don't you can just mash the Guidance button , and it will give you a trail to follow. It would be a great feature if the game would give enough information to not use such "cheap" follow the line solution, and let the player read and enjoy the work of a detective. ( I think many disagree here :D )

Technical side is awful however.. Animations jittering, faces morph when talking. The charm gets lost when we zoom in.
From far away all things look nice , as I said I like the atmosphere. I just don't like to talk with ppl when their eyes move weirdly or hair clips to stuff. Weird really...

Characters are all over the place for sure. I like some of them. I hate few of them. I tolerate the rest .
The MC is fine. I like these cocky who else if not me type of guys, the motivation of him is what I still don't know after 10 hours. Even if choices matter It will only show in conversations and it will show you missed a clue, or you choose something else before so you can't say number 4 :) Constantly reminding you that you are missing something :)
I would just cut that out so I don't even know that there's Number 4 missing cuz I was arrogant before :)

Anyway... Dont expect much. It's not an adrenalin rush. Its a calm runing around following the trail type of game, with mid combat. If you don't like reading it's not even worth it :D

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is hands down THE most boring walking/reading sim game I have EVER played. It started off interesting enough, the combat even felt fun. Then, a few hours in, you realize there actually isn't anything more. Every single quest, every single interaction, is just a tedious repeat of you first hour or two. You basically spend hours running through every city and every corridor while snapping your stupid fingers to make bland meaningless clues appear so that you can solve/get quests. As you advance, you'll need to revisit ALL OF THE CITY LOCATIONS and do THE SAME WALKING AROUND SNAPPING in places you've already endlessly snapped in, because new clues and quests appear and you'll miss them otherwise. Quests = EXP which = levels for your monsters. It's just an endless loop like that, running to places you've already been, snapping your fingers obsessively every few steps, and occasionally getting into combat that once felt fresh and new and now feels tiresome.

I've played a lot of games in this genre, there are definitely ways to make a game feel like the player matters, the player can solve mysteries, and the player choices matter. This is not that game. In this game your choices apparently only impact a small smattering of dialog options,. You don't use your wits or logic to solve puzzles, you just snap your fingers around and click on the things that show up and eventually the dialog option for whatever mystery solution/progression becomes unblocked.

If you're looking for any kind of challenge, this isn't the game for you. This is forced linear story, forced linear character development, forced linear combat, forced linear dialog.

Lastly, whoever put the RPG tag on this game can FRO. This is an interactive story, and a monotonous one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Thaumaturge is really just a dressed up Point&Click Noir adventure that fails at satifactorily implementing mechanics you see in other genres.

The Gameplay is terrible: Skill-Progession is completely linear, Combat is repetitive and tedious, finding clues consists of right-clicking your way across the maps. Story and dialogues are badly written. So what's good: the Atmosphere is believably 1905 Europe and the supernatural lore and themes are interesting. The best thing about this game is the ability to fit in unlocked upgrades into your basic attacks to modify them significantly with things like improved damage or passive buffs or chances to interrupt enemy attacks etc...but fights are too much tied into you having to switch your "companions" to disable enemy buffs so there is no real need to switch up your skill configuration, nor is there an easy way to do so.

I really can't recommend this at any price because of the tedious combat.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game so far, I am enjoying it the more I play. I disliked the combat system at first playing on "medium" difficulty, but when the first boss fight prompted me with the difficulty screen, I chose the hardest setting, and oh boy did I start loosing constantly. That's when I got the hang of the combat system and realized that I'm having way too much fun with it (that's my first time playing a game with this kind of a combat system).
I am loving the story and the setting so far, mixing history with magic (fantasy I guess?) is a cool and an intriguing touch, especially in setting that I haven't seen in other games before, Russian-controlled Congress of Poland, just before the "Russian Revolution of 1905". The story, lore (newspapers, letters, npc conversations and etc.) do make the setting more convincing, immersive and fun to play.

Negatives:
There are some bugs and crushes here in there, but nothing game-breaking.
I do think that leveling up is too fast, I currently have 7 unspent points before catching the third salutor.
I don't like that when roaming the world the NPC's just have subtitles on top of their head. For me, the world would be just a bit more immersive if they had voice acing.

You did read a lot of Dostojewski or are from Poland or Eastern Europe? You'll love this game. But if you didn't or are not... This will be the same, only the eastern european equivalent. Even the voiceacting seems strange, unless you willingly immerse yourself into the "Slavic side of things".

It is a good game that brings with it the vibes of "Witcher 3 detective work", Penny Dreadful (TV show), Cthulhu-Esque-Things, Vampyr, and a visual atmosphere that reminded me a lot of RDR2, and overall... well, it's wonderfully atmospheric.

The game isn't perfect. Too slow, to stumbling at points... but it is very intriguing and entertaining. A solid 7/10 for me.\t\t\t\t\t

Great music and SFX for items/NPCs etc.

Loading times are extremely quick (requires NVMe SSD for best experience).

The story and all dialogue is extremely well done. Voice acting sounds premium and the story is engaging. The side missions are very diverse and never get stale.

The User Interface is PC friendly and logical. No trash console port UI here.

Has a dynamic night and day cycle and includes weather such as rain.

Copious amounts of lore/notes and a compendium for all found lore.

Some of the best world building I have seen in a indie game to date.

Some woke themes. Example your sister being a feminist and potentially being LBGQT++.
