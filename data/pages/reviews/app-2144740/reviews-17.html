\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game sadly was a disappointment to me, as I absolutely loved the first game and count it among my favourite games ever. Long story short, the game feels very awkward to play, in numerous ways. Although I have only played the game for a bit over an hour, it was enough to convince me that this game was not worth my time or money.

Here are some of my reasons why I think this game is far inferior to the original Ghostrunner:

-The movement just feels a lot slower compared to GR1.
-The blocking mechanic heavily incentivises defensive gameplay, interrupting flow.
(for example Locking you into a 2 second finisher animation when you parry an enemy.)
-The Sound design is very weak, lacks impact and feedback and doesn't feel satisfying.
-The Level design in GR1 feels like an environmental puzzle to solve and optimize, whereas in GR2 it feels like a parkour playground with enemies randomly thrown in.
-Why do I have to talk to 3 badly animated NPCs in a boring hub world after every mission?
-Why is there a 3 minute cutscene in the first mission?
-Why won't the characters ever shut up during the mission? At least the dialouge in the first game was interesting and relevant to the story, but here it's just endless yapping about stuff i don't care about.
-The upgrade system feels very uninspired compared to GR1

And lastly, the soundtrack.
Daniel Deluxe absolutely killed it with the GR1 Soundtrack. It was gonna be hard to beat that.
And yeah, GR2 definitely does not. It's still good, don't get me wrong, but it doesn't match the Identity that Ghostrunner recieved through the first OST.

So, to conclude, this is a refund for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game with some lovely new additions, BUT I feel, in some ways, it is a step back from 1.

1 was non stop moving forward and speed and perfect execution. Little room for error. 2 has changed that in a few large ways which, IMO, hurt the game.

For some inexplicable reason, after many of the levels, you're transported to a headquarters to walk around and talk to people. It's very jarring and I don't care for it at all. Really brings a fast game to a screeching halt.

2 is definitely much easier combat wise. Enemy bullets are much slower, some of the more dangerous guys from 1 have been neutered and the Ultimate moves are overpowered. There is a larger enemy variety, which is great, but it's still easier.

The upgrade tree in 1 was amazing. An inoovative tetris like mini-game to prioritize your perks. That's been replaced with a more standard motherboard approach.

The motorcycle is absolutely a blast and amazing, but it clashes hard with the open world in the later half of the game. Since the bike feels and looks like it's meant for roads and tracks, riding in a desert area, you can get stuck awkwardly on geometry.

I imagine some of the changes were made to maybe cater to a less die hard audience and I understand that, but I still wish it had a bit more of the steeper difficulty of 1.

All that being said, the game is still very fun with amazing visuals, high speed and killer music!

I do think 2 really took the great parkour of 1 and just really kicked it up a notch with new moves and challenges so big kudos for that!

I look forward to the DLC and, hopefully, a 3rd entry to the series.\t\t\t\t\t
