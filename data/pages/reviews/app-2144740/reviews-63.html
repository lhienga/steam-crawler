\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved the first game, and while I don't mind this game, I'll say one huge gripe I have with this game. The parts in between levels where you're in the hideout are the most boring, stupid idea. The last game had non-stop action, with almost all the exposition being told to you while you play, which made the game constant action. Why would they change that in this? Sure, you might say, "They still tell you exposition while you play," and yeah, while that's true, I shouldn't have to dread ending the level due to boring slow-paced, mundane ♥♥♥♥. Oh, it is more than just slow, mundane, and boring; it also looks BAD. When you talk to characters, there are almost no animations to them. They strike one pose and stand in that same pose the entire time they talk to you, and their faces BARELY move at all when talking to you. It's mouth movements and maybe a slight eyebrow twitch, WOW! That being said, all the dialogue is skippable in those parts (thank god).

I'll also add that the big open levels are cool, but at the same time, things are far too spread out, making it slow-paced, which again doesn't feel good.

People have been complaining about glitchy things happening to them, and honestly, I've had that happen, too. Still, none of it is game-breaking, just sometimes awkward, funny or a minor inconvenience.

Besides that, it's still an excellent game, and I recommend it!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort answer: (no spoilers until the end, spoiler section marked)

Ghost Runner 2 is a proper sequel in my opinion. the gameplay has been expanded upon from GR 1 and Project_Hel. Though I did get quite a few frame drops in large set pieces even after cranking my graphics all the way down, it didn't distract from the greatly expanded combat, new and upgraded utilities, amazing level design, and quite enjoyable story the game has to offer. if performance is what you're worried about I would say maybe wait for a couple of patches before purchasing, but definitely play the demo. But the game is a solid sequel and I cant wait to see what they do next.
8/10


Long answer:
The combat has seen the greatest improvement. abilities blend together to make fun combos, the new enemies have very unique attacks that go alongside great with others causing you to not feel overwhelmed by everything going on. the new upgrade system, though lacking the charm of the first games tetris like system, allows you to form Jack to the ghost runner you want him to be, also aiding in exploration due to the upgrade chips being hidden on every level and there being a lot of them.

The parkour and movement are good but lacking compared to GR 1. The overall movement speed feels slush in comparison to the first. this is, from what i can tell, done so the combat could be more fluid. Movement in combat feels great (running speed, dash distance, bullet time movement), but the parkour segments outside of combat feel a little slow overall. the removal of multiple dashes is what hurts me the most. the new stamina bar the game bases dashes/blocking/parrying on limits jacks overall movement as now you have to decide between blocking, or dashing towards the enemy, chewing up the same bar. also meaning that if you block bullets you may not have enough stamina for that dash you though you could do, leading to your death. overall if the dash system from the previous game was re-introduced (while the stamina bar was used for only blocking and parrying as i do enjoy the limits on that) i would have no gripes with the how jack feels to control... besides for the games momentum

momentum conservation in this game is very strange. certain object give you ridiculous speed/height while dashing or jumping off of them, but as soon as you dash or slide for a second all that momentum is lost. bhopping feels bad in this game due to how slow it feels and that jack cant keep any of his speed doing so unlike GR 1. If the game allowed even just a wee bit more fluidity in jacks momentum it would do wonders.

The level design is wonderful. Inside Darma you are treated to bright neon-dystopian visuals like the first game but with so much more flare. Once you reach the outside, the post-apocalypse world took my breath away (as it also did to my frames). The games signature techno-style blended amazingly with the outside environments and the things you run into out there. the game doesn't recycle the same gimmicks too often and comes up with new and interesting ways to do the same thing you've done already. the new sections of the environment you can manipulate add a lot more options in combat and movement. my biggest issues come with the extreme frame rate drops in some sections, in one section i had to run passed all the enemies as my game was running at about 15 frames and i couldn't do much in the way of fighting. i had many issues with the bike jumps where i would either get too much/ not enough height and crash, or get glitched inside the floor after landing and have to reset. Launch pads are great fun but the hit box could be a little taller for them as i would attempt to jump into one and flight right on over it to my death. the layout of every level was great besides one level where you have to do long drives on your bike from one objective to another without much to do in between.

The bike is a great addition when used properly. every bike-only level was the most action packed, edge of my seat gaming experience I've had in a while. each level with the bike is even cooler than the last. shooting with the bike can be a little frustrating as the guns auto holster after a couple of seconds and over heating so fast you have to tap fire to get any real use out of it.

SPOILER SECTION:

the wing suit has to be the most amount of fun I've had in a long time with a movement based game. the open arenas and parkour segments cause me to almost forget about my issues i have with the game. but you only unlock this ability for the last few levels of the game. making a nice send off to the game but leaving me wishing they added a couple more levels with the wing suit in mind.

The story, though not amazing, its great fun to listen to as you're hacking up cyborgs. the main plot was compelling enough for me to be compelled to find hidden tapes to learn more about the lore. The extra world building put in with the collectibles helps paint a picture of what life is like in this world. the added touch of the descriptions being from jacks POV is a cute touch. the new characters are great fun to listen and chat with though Zoe is a massive step down from her character in the first. Though i didn't care much once Kira and Rahu took main stage as Jack's besties.

on top of that, more Rahu. PLEASE

Overall the game is a great sequel though the toning down of the movement is a disappointment. the new more open level design is great and is kept very fresh with unique arenas and enemy variety. the movement could use a couple adjustments but i am very pleased with this game and i cant wait to see what happens next.\t\t\t\t\t
