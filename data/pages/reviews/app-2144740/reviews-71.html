\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the few times you'll see a sequel do pretty much everything better than the original. Ghostrunner is a great game. Ghostrunner 2 is even better. I'll keep it simple: if you liked the first game, you'll love this. The mechanics are there and improved, the level design is terrific (best I've seen since Titanfall 2's campaign), the various game mechanics layer on top of one another beautifully and, by the end, it's a masterpiece.

The story is good too if a bit formulaic. I enjoyed the characters, there interactions were good. A little cliche but you care about the characters enough to still be invested. The ONLY issue I had with the story was there were two specific themes they explored but didn't finish. It felt about 75% of the way there and then the story ends which makes it... a little unsatisfying.

But don't let that deter you from buying the game. I'd still give it a 9/10. If the story was just a LITTLE more developed, it would have been a slam dunk 10. You'll still enjoy every minute you play this game. No doubt about it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a Mixed Review. The Game is currently rated Very Positive, so I am not recommending it to bring that down.

tl;dr. Do not believe the hype. The positive reviews are dramatic exaggerations.

GR2 is ok. It has improvements in some areas and flaws in others. It is not as replayable as GR1 and is - in a strange move - notably very anti-speedrun in it's level design compared to the haven that was GR1. For a game with timed leaderboards encouraging you to go fast, this is disappointing. But you'll only notice if you actively try to go faster than they want you to.

I wholeheartedly regret buying the full version at 70$. It is not even close to worth that price. The lowest price is much more fair. I wouldn't say wait for a Sale, unless you want to.




Long Version.

You'll note there's a difference in opinion here. A lot of people say that GR2 is better than GR1 in every way, I have to say I disagree and I believe I can easily and simply show both where and why now.

IF you are simply playing the game, GR2 is probably fine. It has new mechanics, and you probably won't notice a lot of the glaring flaws. There are some pretty big plotholes in the transition from GR1 to GR2, but it seems to me a lot of the issues here is that GR1 was Developed by OML *and* several supporting studios like 3D Realms. Take a quick glance at the Developer on both games, you'll notice what I mean.

IF you are trying to Speedrun, go fast, or use strats from GR1 - which still work in GR2 - then the entire game is a massive step back. This is most obvious if you were Speedrunning GR1 in the first place. However, this obviously doesn't reflect the majority of the userbase I think.

Anyone coming from GR1 with a slide-hopping twitchy quick habit will discover very quickly that the levels in GR2 almost always punish this behavior now. An incredibly painful fact to accept. It doesn't take a genius to figure out how this would rub the quicker people the wrong way. Excellent example: The first level out of the Tower - after the level that was in the Demo - there's a Cybervoid section. You are forced to wait for a platform to slowly trudge it's way through the area. If you go any faster than it, the game will kill you. GR1 had none of this, and it was better for it.

There are some very poor design choices in some areas. The Church area has these Holographic platforms that swap from intangible to tangible - not an uncommon thing in platforming - however they do so with 0 warning or pattern. No sound, no flicker, no color change. It just winks away. They then change how it works a total of 2 times. Another version that you control with an ability and another version that you control with a button press. They then rapidly flip-flop through these, actively preventing you from getting better at it.

Then then created an entire boss fight based on this idea.
- Small Rant -
The Second Boss in this game is the most confusing, infuriating, Frankenstein's Monster of a random amalgamation of concepts and ideas sloppily stitched into a gut-renching mess of Screen Confetti that I have ever seen in my life. Like they had 3 Different Boss ideas, couldn't decide which one they preferred, and just said "Whatever, let's do all of them and not do QA for it."

A lot of the platforming needed some fine-tuning. More time in the oven, some more considerations. The sad thing is it's glaringly obvious a lot of this could have been fixed. Sometimes, things *should* be cut, and it's clear they didn't do enough cutting here.


All in all. Usually, when a Sequel doesn't live up to expectations and feels like it's missing "Something essential" throughout. I find the culprit is usually a loss of Talent. Once I realized that OML made GR2 on their own this time, a lot of the game started to make much more sense. They lost a lot of essential talent, and it shows.

I don't hate this game. But I do wish I spent less and knew more of what to expect going in.\t\t\t\t\t

10/10 im already halfway through my second playthrough

I can't say I am a fan of GHOSTWALKER
My days of playing games like Prince of Persia have long passed. While I enjoy games that are fast-paced and full of bullet hell, I don't understand how this game appeals to those who want a mix of slow and semi-fast gaming. I mean, he's called the ghost runner, but he's so slow, the game seems clunky, and I ran into so many glitches and issues, most notably with the motorcycle, which caused my momentum to stop totally and destroy the experience.

Conclusion
Many individuals believe the game is good, and that is completely acceptable. But when can you genuinely trust Steam critics when the majority of them submit meme-worthy things like ASCII art and cat memes / trash postings rather than anything constructive? If you haven't played GhostRunner, you won't understand what the hell is going on here, so you should go check that one out first because the recap is extremely brief and you don't comprehend the background chatter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI LOVED the first Ghostrunner, but it seems like they completely missed the mark with this one. The missions seem way less cohesive and the story is SUPER bland with terrible characters that it just makes you wish there wasn't any at all. I felt like the first game was primarily focused on combat with some puzzling here and there but that's not the case with 2. It kinda feels like the whole game is puzzle after puzzle with combat sprinkled in. The bike was cool but then it came to a weird "open world" part where you have to get off the bike after every little section in order to progress further. I also ran into several bugs where I either clipped through the map or completely fell through it while playing with some performance issues and hitching when loading into a new area. I also for some reason feel like this game is WAY more annoying when dealing with enemies when I didn't necessarily feel that way with the first. So overall, I feel like they had something great going with the 1st game and then they just decided to try and over achieve with the 2nd. I do not plan to finish the game after waiting for it to come out if that tells you anything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tfantastic game. a straight upgrade from GR1. BUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUUT its a bit buggy and overall rough around the edges when it comes to performance and stability. theres been a few moments where ive been softlocked in arenas because i got to em too fast and thus enemies wouldnt spawn, and a good handful of moments where the next chunk of the level hasnt loaded yet and id be looking into a void waiting for the rest of the level to show up. putting up with those hiccups however is worth it imo. the game itself is fantastic. the bosses/set pieces are top notch, the combat is just as good if not better than GR1, the new mechanics and enemy types the introduce were nice, the new outside sections with the bike were so much fun, even when those were when the hiccups and bugs were at their peak. kinda came off like the devs were getting a bit over-ambitious, but still the outside levels themselves were still a blast to play.
also the story being more human focus is a bit mixed, sometimes it works well, other times it fell flat and came off a bit cringe. but the expansion on the other ghostrunners themselves and the new antagonist(s) were really cool and learning their lore (and by extension jack's) was engaging, at least more so than the humans.
overall its a fantastic follow-up to GR1, and more than worth the $40, especially if you like GR1, BUT if you dont have the patience to put up with the jank or bugs, hold off till the game gets a few patches and hotfixes before giving it a try.

tl;dr
ghostrunner 2 is fantastic when its not buggy
worth it

UPDATE: after a bit more research, the most of the bugs ive come across might be a result of my graphics card finally getting a bit too old or outdated, your experience may be more stable. i have a NVIDIA geforce gtx 1060 for reference\t\t\t\t\t
