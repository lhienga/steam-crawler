\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-Game is pretty short.

-The bike was a mistake and I hated it every time i had to use it I am not listing all the problems the bike had because it would simply make the game more fun if it were removed entirely.

-Level geometry can be wacky it is doubly so for the bike.

-Wing suit was fun, shame it was only for one level.

-Ultimates are underwhelming.

-The parkour is fun except when it wants you to jump through a small hole in a wall as there is a good chance you will just stand in the hole instead of going straight through.

-A lot of the arenas feel samey probably due to the donut design for most of them.

-there are a few enemies that are poorly designed in such a way that it makes your default tools for killing enemies insufficient for killing them, namely the flying rocket enemy and the wheel enemy. for the wheel enemy it requires them to charge into a wall before you can kill it this is difficult to do in areas where there isn't enough room to maneuver around them or in areas where there is a lack of walls, and for the flying enemy you can't reflect the rockets back at them so your only options are having your shuriken charged or the area having a easy way to launch yourself into them and hoping they don't move away while you are in the air.

-lack of proper boss fights the worm was just another bike level and Avatar was just another arena fight both were terrible leaving 3 proper bosses 1 at the very beginning and 2 at the very end it is horribly spaced out.

-blocking attacks from certain enemies can send you flying and as you are usually on small platforms surrounded by void getting sent flying usually means death making blocking these enemies unviable.

-The floating platforms that you pull to you using the grapple are fine when you are standing on the ground but when you have to do so while moving they suddenly become the hardest thing to land on and if you hit the side of it you very often start a wall run instead of climbing onto it.

The game is pretty fun to play but I don't think i can recommend it though with how short the game is and about a fifth of the levels requiring the bike there just isn't enough of the game to play.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 is definitely a well deserved sequel. You can tell the development team put a lot of care into designing the feature's. There isn't a big gap between the tools and abilities that you learned from the first game put into this game. Most of the abilities you learned from the first game are unlocked in the first level. This help paved the way to have "Jack" learn more abilities throughout the game. If you are looking for a story driven, SUPER HOT, runner type game this would be a definite buy. It is even a plus that the game isn't priced at 59.99 or even 69.99 as most games are nowadays.

There are some negative's that do need to be talked about but they may be ironed out over the games lifetime. At the moment of me writing this review, I have 100%'ed the story and achievements. There were some things that I experienced over the course of the game that weren't joyful. When you unlock the motorcycle parts of the game, I would occasionally hit random bumps on the road where there was nothing at instantly explode. Other times my motorcycle would spawn in weird positions if it had to be respawned. On the later levels, I experienced a lot of frame rate drops to the point that it made the game unplayable unless I somehow made it passed a certain area or restarted the game. My setup currently is running a 4080 so frame rates below 10 aren't expected. Especially that the next feature, RTX, seems to be missing from the game. It seems that Ray Tracing was removed from the release of the game because it was buggy and would cause people to crash. Finally, the Wing suit was a cool addition to the final stages of the game but it seems like the map wasn't properly finished or created for those levels. I was able to go around many corners accidently skipping the main path and skipping ahead in the story in certain areas.

Overall, this game is definitely a must buy. The Indie developers have done it extremely well and are always taking feedback from their community to make it better. Ghostrunner 2 also is much easier compared to the first game if anyone stayed away from the games because it was too difficult. The base game takes a little longer than the base game of Ghostrunner 1 + its DLC. I am really looking forward to see what the future holds for Ghostunner 2 and possibly a Ghostunner 3 coming down the line.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 1 but with more customization, new movement options, and new refreshed gameplay and enemies. Super fun to complete the story, achievement hunt, and speedrun, Solid 9.5/10 for me

A few things I'd like to see added though:
- Hard Mode like GR1 (added on 12/7/23, wasnt there at launch)
- An option to auto-skip cutsenes after seeing them the first time
- An option to disable highlighting of what to do (things like where to shoot to cut a box down or where to tempest to push a box)
- Ability to launch the challenges from the menu after completing them or getting gold in them (some are really fun to play, but it would be nice not having to run through half a level to get to it)

Otherwise everything was amazing, great gameplay and music. The addition of the motorcycle and training simulator is super fun and adds some more depth to the gameplay. I also think the rework of the skill tree, abilities, and ultimate gives the ability to create a play style unique to you, much more so than the GR1, since there is a greater ability to customize abilities to fit your playstyle as well as be able to try and beat levels in different ways, rather than being stuck to one option. The world hub is hit or miss, personally I think it was cool the first playthrough, but it can make speedrunning not as good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very different experience from Ghostrunner 1, but I'm honestly loving it. It feels more fleshed out and complete, the world has more purpose and, there's non-linear levels with more exploration, and different options.

I loved Ghostrunner 1 for how intense it was, and Ghostrunner 2 tones that down, but I think in a really good way. I disagree with a lot of the negative reviews saying they haven't built on anything.

There were several games I was looking forward to this year, and I was disappointed by many. Out of the 25 games I have bought this year, I have refunded 24. Ghostrunner 2 is not one of them.

//// REVIEW EDITED /////

I have now finished the game, after only 8 hours. And I died a lot. That's a bit sad.

I have 2 main criticisms.

The wingsuit was awesome, that should have been used for way longer.

The final boss battle is extremely dissatisfying. Simply parrying and dodging. At least Madhu required a crap ton of movement and still attacking him regularly. I'm not a fan of boss battles where I have to do certain things, let me slice the guy. Addendum to this, going into the cybervoid in the final boss battle is horrible. Why have you changed the gravity in here? It is not fun, and I died in there only twice to the boss, but about 30 times simply because I was moving how I had for the past 9 hours, and the game said, no no, don't do that now, that doesn't work. Not fun. Not the best ending.

Still reccommend, amazing game, superb graphics, but, feels like you gave up for the final boss and the ending.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is everything I wanted and hoped for.
The upgrade tree is better than the last game, but I would still like it to be so that you didn't have to pick and choose only *some* upgrades. I like the memory chip mechanic, as it is way to make you work for the progress, rather than just grinding XP.
The gameplay is somehow even better than the last game without it changing drastically. I especially like the block mechanic. Rather than timing a slash to block a single shot, you can block a whole bunch, which costs stamina.
The bike is an amazing way of travelling quickly in larger levels without changing the game's fast pace, and I love how they still involve some parkour aspects too.

There is so much more I'd love to say about this game, but that belongs in an essay, not a little review forum.
10/10\t\t\t\t\t
