\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I love GR1 and its mechanics.
For some reason, the devs decided a hub area to talk with NPCs was necessary for GR2. This causes a large break in the gameplay. Multiple others have also stated this flaw.

Secondly, the movement feels less satisfying and more clunky. I was extremely disappointed when I jumped and dashed on a sliding rail to go faster and then fell through the rail. The addition of a stamina system has potential, as multiple dashes can be used in quicker succession, but causes issues when moving for prolonged periods of time, such as between fights.

Thirdly, the difficulty is a large step down, even in the early parts of the game as a ranged attack (shuriken) is given very early. Personally, I loved the difficulty of the first game. When I played it, I decided not to use sensory boost the entire run except when it was required. This made me able to simply react and predict the bullets from enemies. In GR2, the player gets a block, which I think is stupid and slows down the pace of the game.

Fourthly, the upgrade system. In GR1 each upgrade had its own shape and you had to piece together all the upgrades that you wanted into the always too small space. I personally really enjoyed that puzzle element and the min maxing of which upgrades to use. In GR2, you spend your time scanning for memory upgrades to fit more upgrades into your motherboard. There is no puzzle aspect and the extra memory being locked behind collectibles slows down the game. I really appreciated GR1's style of rushing through a level and unlocking upgrades at the next cybervoid mainframe, meaning no upgrade was missed due to a lack of collectible searching.

TL;DR, buy the game if you enjoyed GR1 or are interested and you want an easier experience. The game is still decent, just disappointing when compared to GR1. (Also I heard that this runs poorly on weaker computers). I plan to play out my 2 hours then refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI overall enjoy the game more than the first game. The most annoying enemies from the first game are pretty much gone, save for small one-time miniboss encounters. The game is almost fully based on navigating the arena and finding ways to close the distance instead of chasing after RNG teleports or outrunning suicide bombers. This is accentuated by some really fun late game mobility options that really shake up the gameplay enough to avoid the repetitive feeling I got towards the end of the first game. The boss fights are also better, but still easy and simple. I beat most of the boss fights by running in and slashing, parrying whenever the boss made any kind of move, and dashing back in when they knocked me back. It was only ever the segments between the boss phases that gave me issue.

There are only a couple of negatives for me to mention. The most major one is the enemy having map hack. There were multiple moments where I rounded a corner to flank an enemy and they immediately opened fire as soon as I came into view, killing me before I could react. I don't know if something changed with the AI between games or if the first game's arena designs simply didn't have that many walls, but this happened enough times in this game to get me heated. I had to start rounding the corner while immediately holding block to survive.

Another one is the facial animations being genuinely really bad. There is now a hub level where you can walk around and talk to players. The writing is shallow but alright, and I enjoyed these moments. It lets you interact more with characters that don't make much of an appearance in the story, and highlights Jack's personality more through interactions with more normal people.

However, it's really distracting when you talk to the NPCs and their lips are the only thing moving, with their cheeks and eyes being completely static. It's animation from two generations ago and very disappointing.

The last negative to mention are the exploration levels with the bike. The linear levels with the bike are fine, if not a little bit unforgiving considering how imprecise the bike feels, but the open exploration levels just don't feel made for the bike at all. There are little bits of geometry dotted all around the map that my bike kept getting snagged on. It happened enough to affect the flow of the map and my enjoyment of them. These maps also had pretty much nothing going on, as once you're forced to properly get off, it just turns into another normal Ghostrunner level, which makes me question how necessary how necessary the massive open areas were.

All in all, a pretty good upgrade to the first game in my book.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll be honest, if you have lots of time in the original, it might be better to just stick with the original. But if you are playing Ghostrunner 2 as your first Ghostrunner game, you'll find fun in what Ghostrunner 2 improves on from Ghostrunner 1.

Here's how I would put it.
Ghostrunner 1: Hardcore, precise, close and methodical combat. Speed is important, and facing an enemy head-on is out of the question. All enemies are extreme threats that can take you out with one wrong move. Mistakes mean restarting from the checkpoint and fighting again. Planning and perfect execution is required.

Ghostrunner 2: Somewhat casual-friendly. Still a hard game, but with more tools to make it easier for yourself. It's a little less "Ghostrunner" and more "Ghostslaughterer". There's more enemies, most of them easier than the original, and there are plenty of ways to take a fight head on, without planning, panicking, and being sloppy. Which is great! For a casual audience.

Coming from a die-hard fan of the original, this game has some major upgrades from the original. That being said, it also has some serious downgrades, only... these downgrades are very apparent if you are an extremely fast-paced, hardcore gamer.

All in all, I enjoyed Ghostrunner 2 more than Ghostrunner 1, despite spending a lot of time on the original. It's an absolute steal for the price, it's just as adrenaline-inducing as the original, with twice the content.

The game suffers from too much padding in the Outside sections,which is not something I expected from Ghostrunner.

Tons of bugs,tons of performance issues,litearlly unbeatable final(?) boss due to constant crashes. Underbaked dialogue and cutscenes. Too much scope,too little time. Gameplay loop was still pretty fun though.

Scummy diff editions stuff with "early access" and "season pass" BS. Don't buy right now.

EDIT: After having to switch to DX11 to even progress in the game (it crashed mid bossfight) the game became more and more disjointed and less polished with more and more bugs. The whole final bossfight was the cheesiest,most 12 year old ♥♥♥♥ ever,along with the ending.

There was potential here,but this was released months early,if not a year or two. Jesus christ what a downgrade From the grappling hook,to the graphics even,to the options menu,to the horrible audio mixing (one of the enemies in later levels is literally 4X louder than anything in the game and just screams TARGET NEUTRALIZED) and just plain bad performance.

Do not buy,even if gets patched. This is a travesty compared to the last game. I'm baffled how people say this is good,this is genuinly terrible.\t\t\t\t\t
