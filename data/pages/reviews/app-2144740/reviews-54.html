\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort Answer: Improves over the first game, but the pacing is a bit weird, and sometimes FPS goes into the 20's which is unacceptable. (playing this game on Epic quality, no RT, 3060Ti 11700K 32gb of Ram, NVME SSD, well cooled) EDIT: Also, don't use DX12 cuz they didn't' optimize it for DX12. that is the major cause for low FPS.

Long Answer:
Ghost Runner 2 is less consistent with its quality, but it's still Ghostrunner; I like Ghostrunner. The biggest thing you'll see people angry about is the Hub level thingy, and I agree it does disrupt the pacing in this fast-paced game. The performance also isn't great. (Tip: Turn off ray-tracing, it barely does anything except destroy your FPS) I wouldn't mind the talking segments as much if the characters didn't emote worse than a Bethesda NPC. It's sad that the NPCs looks so awkward while talking because I can tell the devs put a lot of effort in to improve the character models compared the Ghost runner 1. Obviously the game play also is refined. I would have been perfectly happy if GR2 didn't bite off more than it could chew, and just focused more on refining than reinventing the wheel. But if they did that, some people would inevitably complain that GR2 is too much like GR1. Turn off Ray-tracing, try not to look at the NPCs talking, and most fans of GR1 will still have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid sequel to the first game, which was an incredible experience. This game is not perfect by any means, but hopefully will be improved after some patches. I can appreciate when developers go outside their norm and try new things with sequels. The new mechanics of the motorcycle and wingsuit were well done and interesting, even though the motorcycle needs some work (not uncommon that it gets stuck on the smallest of rocks which renders you unable to move backward or forward to get out of the spot you get stuck in, but loading the most recent checkpoint solves it). There is an 'open world' level and it was just okay. I personally prefer the linear formula that was perfected in the first game. All in all was a fun playthrough. I just REALLY hope that the insane frame drops in a few encounters are solved for ASAP. It's disheartening when a game that's released in 2023 has a few spots where frames drop to literally 10 FPS, which makes it extremely difficult to get through the encounter (I have a beastly 3090 BTW). I have faith in these devs to fix it with time though. As of 11/4/2023, these issues persist unfortunately. I assume there are QA teams that tested this game with various hardware combinations, and it's very possible that shareholders/investors pushed the release before certain things were fully flushed out (i.e. the Cyberpunk syndrome), but just wish small studios would not be subject to the same BS. Anyway, all in all this game is good and worth a playthrough but just please wait until a few more patches where the game will be more playable from the standpoint of a consumer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid sequel with some flaws.

If you have played Ghostrunner and don't want more of that, this isn't for you. This game iterates on the first, but it does not get too adventurous with that iterating.

Some of my personal pain points in the first game were addressed in this one:
- The Uplink system has been replaced, so you don't have quite so many timing sensitive checkpoints anymore.
- The upgrade system is no longer a puzzle. The new system still makes you weigh your options and try out different combinations, but you no longer have the interface hassle of the puzzle system on top of that. Overall a great improvement
- The cybervoid sections are now more in line with the real world levels instead of slow puzzles.

I would also say that the level design has improved over Ghostrunner 1, with some experimentation on non-linear levels that work quite well. But for me the bike section of the game was a bit too drawn out. The first bike level is awesome, but after that it gets a little stale as driving isn't particularly engaging for the most part. I respect that they tried this to mix up the formula, but 3 levels instead of 5 would have probably been a better choice. Either that or give the bike sections a bit more love playtest cycles.

Also like that we get to interact with the NPC cast a lot more. The writing also improved in that regard over the first game.

In terms of stability, I had some issues with enemies sometimes spawning inside the ground, which can make for some tricky situations, but aside from that I had no issues running the game in DX11 mode on Proton.

The only thing that really frustrates me as I'm trying to 100% is that some of the checkpoints put you in a spot where you can immediately die again if you reflexively hold forward as you respawn. Oh and also some of the enemy positioning is unfair in my opinion. You open a door and if you forget that an enemy is right there aiming at the door, you will probably die.\t\t\t\t\t
