\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- Story is worse than in GR1. The antagonists are flat and unimpactful. It feels uninspired.
- You'd expect the combat to be determinstic but it honestly isn't. The optimal way to go about ecounters is very linear but even then it feels random all the time. Often the same movement gets you killed while other times it gets you further (Even when respawning at checkpoints). The lack of consistency keeps be from even trying to no-hit a level. I remember GR1 to be better in that regard also.
- The open levels are a step backwards. When I play a ninja I want to go, go, go and slice enemies up as I go and move further through the tower.
- One of the powers somehow bugged out and the shuriken-blink stopped working for me with no way to fix it.
- The upgrade system is uninspired compared to GR1.
- The friendly characters are a waste of space. You wont care much about them or for the dialogue. It suffers a bit from millennial writing.
- Movement feels worse than in GR1. It feels more janky and it takes you out of the experience.
- While the bike was a fun idea, it's clunky and oftentimes horrible to control.
- Half of the abilities are a waste of space and because nobody would use them.

I was excited when I heard about GR2 but ultimately it was a big letdown\t\t\t\t\t

I love the first game, I love the dlc, I like the sequel.

In general it's not a bad game but I stand on mixed feelings for this one, when it's more true to the original it's great, when it tries to be bigger and better it results in a worse experience.

The main problems I have with this game are the open areas (even the open map), often arenas feel too big with enemies too far from each other to actually worry about (in the first game there were more tight spaces where you had to be either more creative with Jack's skills or with you skills as a player), the open spaces also feel a lot easier to deal with because of the block button which feels a bit too powerful.
My main complaints are in the level design but there are other small issues like the hub that feels a bit dead without a background music or some kind of environmental effect (apart from the radio) and talking to the npcs kinda slows down a lot the pace of the game, other than this I don't really like the ultimate skills especially the blink which feels clunky to control while the others are not really that useful, also another negative is the bike that often gets stuck on rocks or geometry in general in the "open map" mission (otherwise I did really enjoy the bike).

Enough with the negatives tho, let's list some great things starting with the cybervoid levels, they feel a lot better to navigate and show a general improvement in level design, the music is great (especially the first time you get in the bike) and the voice acting is spot on like the prior game, I also liked the kind of aesthetic change when you explore the outside (even if those levels are the ones I liked the least) and to end this section: when the game tries to have the tight level design, like the original, it's really great.

Overall is not a bad game but I expected something different, I feel like Project Hel did a better job at following the original game's vibe, speed and level design quality but this is more ghostrunner and I like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh yeah, I love this game. I loved the first one, too, and this scratched all the same itches. It's notably easier than the first one, and the reason why is, I think, because you have a more fleshed out kit as well as a block button. Cons? idk, there's a few glitches, a few invisible walls here and there, and sometimes I just feel like it's a bit too forgiving. But that's just me as a massive fan of the first one. It's not something you'll notice if you're new. It also seems way less focused on completion speed, which I appreciate, since "shaving off a few seconds" sounds more like homework than fun to me.

If you played GR1: Buy this game unless you were a snob about the ultradifficulty, but in my opinion you'll still find plenty that's hard here and plenty to love. If you played GR1 til you hit that lasertower and gave up, you're gonna love GR2. GR2 doesn't require meth to complete any of its levels.

If you haven't played 1: Start here. You're gonna love this. It's what the first should have been, in my opinion. Can't wait for the ever-based devs to put out content here and there so I have a reason to come back.

Hard recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
