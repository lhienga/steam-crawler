Let me start with this: This is a fantastic game, super fun and challenging. Definitely a worthy sequel to GR1. However, there are some things you need to be aware of before purchasing:

1. It's somewhat short. It took me 9 hours to complete. Now this is a bit longer than the first game (about 7 hours) but i was hoping for more. Especially for $40.

2. It starts out slow. Lots of boring NPC dialogue that could be removed entirely without really taking much away from the experience. However about halfway through the story you start having a lot of fun with new mechanics like the motorcycle, and wing suit. (personally I feel like they should have given you the wing suit earlier because I was having an absolute blast with it and wish I got to use it more.)

3. It has a lot more puzzles than the first game. Now this may just be my personal opinion, but if you're gonna drop me into a world where I'm a cyber ninja with a sword, all I'm gonna want to do is run around doing parkour and killing things, not solving puzzles. Now I will say the puzzles are never very difficult (There wasn't a single one I got stumped on), but they just feel like a tedious task i need to complete before I get to go back to being a badass samurai robot.

4. The God-awful NPCs. The little hub area you go back to between missions is definitely my biggest complaint about this game. I understand what they were going for, trying to add more story to the sequel, but the dialogue gets really boring really quick. It also just feels very disconnected from the rest of the world since you just kinda spawn away from it at the start of each mission, and spawn back there at the end. Not to mention the models for the characters feel very lifeless and almost out of place graphics wise compared to the rest of the world, when I'm there, it almost feels like I'm playing a fan game.

Despite all this, I really can't say that I don't recommend this game, because if someone would ask me if they should get it, I would most certainly say yes. The sheer fun you have playing this game definitely outweighs the flaws. The motorcycle, the wing suit, leaving the tower and traversing the world outside of Dharma tower, not to mention the bad ass chase from the giant robot worm, all great stuff. High hopes for a potential GR3!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is annoying, I wish Steam had an in between for thumbs up and down, because that's what I'd give this game, a mixed review. I'm editing my review because they added Hardcore Mode. Play Hardcore Mode if you have the game, it makes things better, but problems still exist. Apologies for the bad news.

This is a very good game, but the reason I'm giving a thumbs down is because I cannot recommend it when GR1 is just so good in comparison. I've seen a few people saying its better than GR1 and that's awesome, I'm glad people are having fun! These are all our own humble opinions and I respect that... but GR1 is 100% better.

If you've never played either I can't recommend this over the first sadly.
I saw someone say GR2 is more refined and I strongly disagree, its actually dumbed down. GR1 feels 100% more fluid, faster and difficult.
Hardcore mode makes things much better in GR2, but some of my biggest issues like the game's approach to enemies and abilities still exists and I know they won't go away. I can only hope for GR3.

Areas:
Many of us agree that the outdoor areas severely break the flow of the game. Also going back and forth through areas in this game is not a good move. I understand the necessity of reusing an area, but here it feels very cheap. GR1 you basically moved forwards the entire game, a true sense of progression and a "I'm coming for you" feeling that I absolutely loved.

Overcoming Enemy Types:
Now this might be subjective because this is how I like to play, but I make it a goal to only use the sword as much as I can help it. No shuriken or any ranged ability on enemies. It just doesn't feel right to me, cheesing it almost. I find it MUCH more satisfying to take down every enemy in the area by viciously dashing and slicing through each one to the next, getting right in their face in the blink of an eye. Shurikens being used to activate things in the environment is fine, but I want to tackle my enemies the way I choose to. GR1 lets you do that, player choice is a wonderful thing.

Now for GR2... There are certain enemies that you MUST attack in a SPECIFIC way. E.g. Shurkien first and then attack... no acceptations. I really don't like this. Enemies like that are boring because every encounter is now more predictable. I love GR1 because pretty much all the enemies you can attack at any time, so long as you can get close, and overcome in interesting ways that are discovered simply by playing the game a lot. E.g. For some enemy types not all attacks need to be avoided by going left or right, you can slide at the right moment or dash up into the air to get more height if your legs are about to be lazered off.

They also added a block button. This'll be the first time I've said this, but NO BLOCK BUTTON PLEEEEEASE! I know in a melee game a block button feels like a must, but the way blocking/parrying worked in GR1 feels SOOO MUCH BETTER. Its a click, cannot be held. Its way more risky and scarier, therefore IT FEELS BETTER. GR2 blocking is like, "oh, Im being shot at, hold button until stamina depletes....... ooooooo..."
GR1 blocking is like, "oh god, here it comes... aaaaaand NOW! BAM!" Way WAAAAAAAY more satisfying. Playing GR2 I literally never used the block button, disgusting lol I only parried which I'm happy you can still do, but get rid of the hold blocking I beg you.

One last note on enemies... I swear they made all the enemies easier or slower. Very sad. I feel like the leaping enemy takes an extra second now in the air. THATS A LOT for a game like this. Totally messes up my timing because I've gotten so used to that enemy that I dont slow time, I wait for him to leap at me then I step to the side and slice him in the air. Doing that in GR2 now I can feel the extra time it takes for him to get to me.

Devs, I love Ghostrunner, GR1 became of my all time favourites, but please do not hold back on your players. Do not make the game easier, we like how difficult GR1 was. Not everyone liked how tough GR1 was and that is a good thing!!! Its good that you have a community of dedicated players who want the thrill that comes with an intense game like this. What you DON'T want, and is what you're currently going for, is an easy experience that many casual players will play, but DROP so fast you won't believe. True fans of an amazing game stay with it FOR LIFE if they can help it.

Final note, stop offering me skills and abilities that make me invincible at any moment in the game. Stoooooooop. If I get hit by an enemy THEN I'M HIT and I am okay with this. I will adapt, do not make me "invincible while successfully blocking", "invincible when dashing in the air..." AHHHHH this was probably one of the biggest things I hated to see. Not only do you make the overall gameplay experience easier, but you give the player unlocks/skills that make the game EVEN EASIER. Don't do this next time please. Look back at what your fan base loved about the first and stick to that if you want GR3 to be the game. No unnecessary cheap Fallout dialog interaction with characters, no HQ, less annoying characters in your ear, no open world sections, no dumbing down the experience.

In GR1 not everyone is going to "feel" like a badass Ghostrunner because it's difficult and they give up... But those who stick with it TRULY FEEL like this deadly machine because they knew a section was hard and overcame the situation. The final kill is always so sweet... I get chills.
In GR2... everyone is a badass cyborg ninja... so nobody truly feels like one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am incredibly sad to write this review. 2 things to say before the review: 1, I haven't finished the game yet (I absolutely plan to) 2, Ghostrunner 1 is my favorite game of all time.

TL;DR: Amazing music and visuals, more story than GR1, Rougerunner mode is awesome. Movement feels clunky (when compared to GR1), bad platforming sections, and gameplay feels slow at times.

Some of the positives of GR2 is that the story is somewhat interesting and is much more engaging, I have enjoyed Rougerunner alot so far, as it feels much faster paced than the main game. The graphics are amazing, even on med-low settings, and DON'T GET ME STARTED ON THE MUSIC it is superb and some of my favorite video game music.

My main problem with this game is that it is too slow. Don't get me wrong it has some fun fast-paced sections, but overall it just feels slow, especially when compared to the 1st game. One of my favorite things about the 1st game was how fast paced it felt, how fluid the movement was, and most of all how rewarding it felt to play. This game just doesn't deliver on that. I played the demo before pre-ordering the brutal edition and I was SO excited for GR2 as the demo felt good, like the original and fast. But then on day 1 of being able to play it I boot up the game it it just fell short. Even of the demo. It felt clunky and unpolished, the levels where not good and felt like they had no direction to them, and most of all, the platforming sections where disappointing.


But even these super fun things dont make up for the downsides that are the clunky movement, bad level design, and bad platforming sections. Overall, I don't regret paying $70 for this game as the studio more than deserves it as GR1 is an absolute masterpiece and they have put so much effort into GR2. I hope that they improve upon the game and keep working as I think (and many others also think) that if the game had been worked on more, it would be better, kind of like Cyberpunk, No Man's Sky, etc.

Note for Devs: I love both GR games and hope that you continue work on them, this one just fell short of my expectations for it. Also I CANT REITERATE THIS ENOUGH: THE MUSIC IS ABSOLUTELY AMAZING OMG I LOVE IT.\t\t\t\t\t
