\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: This is an LSD trip that last for a day similar to Katana Zero and I dig it. This game is the direct sequel to Ghostrunner (2020) and it improves a lot of features while also introduce some new issues that kinda lower the overall quality of the product. Nevertheless, this is a solid game that fans of the first game and fans of hack-n-slash games will definitely enjoy, give it a shot.

*Positive:
+Graphic is nice, has clear visual style and very details in Dharma tower
+The combat is *chef kiss*, well-crafted in term of visually and sound design, every slash is brutal
+While some people may dislike the toned down difficulty, this is actually my positive point. The game is still hard, but not brutally hard anymore
+Extended RPG system with new skills, upgrades and ultimate skills, making the game highly customizable for any playstyle player wants. TL;DR making the game easier or harder on choices
+The soundtrack on its own is not very good, but for the pace of the game, it did a very good job

*Negative:
-While the game has many options for setting, one thing persists is the stuttering no matter your setting might be, which can be annoying
-The story is serviceable but not very memorable, most characters except Zoe and Kira are blank-slate boring, the theme and setup is very interesting but fall flat due to pacing issue
-The game is a little bit short, around 6-7 hours to finish a playthrough, recommend increase to 10 hours
-The open-world section is heavily under utilized, almost no feature to back it up due to most features are collectibles and chip upgrades. If they plan to expand this in the next game, recommend hiring someone with open-world and mission design expertise.

*Final score:
-Critic score: 6/10, a very flashy roller coaster with many bumps on rails that need fixing or improvement
-User score: 8/10, two step forward, one step backward, and leaves to desire in the end like the bittersweet of a bloody chocolate\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 is an excellent follow-up to the original and improves on it in many ways. You are given more mechanics in combat to utilize and fight enemies, you get a lot of interesting new enemies, and your abilities act as movement and combat tools that you can use. The soundtrack is fantastic, and the artstyle is great while also giving more of a variance in the environments that you encounter. I think the big issues come with the bike, which feels absolutely terrible, and the story. The story is pretty lame with a mediocre ending, and the characters you encounter aren't nearly interesting enough to actually care about. The sub-par facial animations looked really awkward, especially because there are so many robots it gets a little jarring. I didn't try the roguelite mode but if I do, I'll talk about it in my review here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMore Ghostrunner - if you enjoyed the first one this is just as good!

- Same base gameplay and combat loop
- More skills to mess around with
- Level design is still really good and makes you feel like a stealthy ninja. Plus the new motorbike and wingsuit are fun to use, and the vehicle levels allow you to make the most of what they offer.
- Boss levels are incredible both visually and mechanically, especially the sandworm!
- Runs very well on 1070 + ultrawide
- Still a bit buggy, had to reload the last checkpoint multiple times, and even had to restart my game once

Even though #2 has more stuff & more varied gameplay, I still prefer the first one as it really clicked for me - but Ghostrunner 2 is just as good, especially the massive boss fights\t\t\t\t\t
