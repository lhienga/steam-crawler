\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first Ghostrunner game is far better than this one.

Ghostrunner 2 is more focused with the maps than the combat. I've spent more time solving map puzzles than actually fighting enemies. That is, when the maps are loading properly. I've gotten stuck and fallen into the sky box multiple times. This is especially true on the desert maps, which are already extremely boring to play on because of the excessive size and dull gray color scheme.

It's no surprise so much time was put into the over-designing of maps because the combat itself is lacking. The block feature is by far the worst addition in Ghostrunner 2. Players can lazily press a button and block barrages of bullets. The original Ghostrunner player could only block one projectile at once and it required good timing. This made the gameplay more fast paced and exciting as dodging and dashing were more important. The second game also has a lot of emphasis on the secondary abilities as they charge way too quickly and are necessary to complete the endless map puzzles. This would be less of a problem if ultimate abilities weren't added in. All these extra abilities make the sword play seem less important, which takes away from what made the original combat interesting.

There is also too much emphasis on the story of the game. Ghostrunner was able to implement the story into the gameplay through in-game dialog. The story existed to enhance the gameplay, but Ghostrunner 2 feels like the opposite. The story takes priority, even if it negatively affects the gameplay. For example, the game is constantly being interrupted with safe hub scenes so the player can have boring conversations with boring NPCs. The deserts scenes are also proof of this. The writers really wanted Jack to leave the city without thinking about whether or not this would be good for gameplay. As a result, the game is now filled with extremely large, buggy, and dull desert maps which are not nearly as fun to play as the city maps.

Then there is the inclusion of the motorcycle. Let's just say driving this motorcycle makes driving in Cyberpunk feel easy in comparison.

Overall, Ghostrunner 2 is an alright game, but the original is much better. Overall, Ghostrunner is more exciting, more fast-paced, less buggy, and overall better designed than its sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
