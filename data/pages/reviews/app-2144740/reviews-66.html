\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes.

While the story is cool for most part of the game, the way some things are delivered or executed hurt it. We have a lot of conversations with the cast when we are at the "hub" area, but these chats feel lacking due to the "fallout/bethesda" way of talking with NPCs. Terminator Resistance also had this problem, but done by different people. I have some problems with the way the hub area and the cast is pretty much intact by the end of the game, and how Jack's resolution lacks (to me) proper build up.

But we are not really here for the story, are we? We are here for the AMAZING and FLUID and FAST gameplay!

-Combat feels great, an evolution of the first game. With better boss fights too!
-Soundtrack is also very damn good, fits perfectly with every level.
-Upgrades are really varied, create a lot of sinergies and different playstyles. It's also tied to exploration and completing hidden challenges because you have to find chips to unlock space for the upgrades, which are bought at the hub area or some stations in latter levels. Collectibles and stuff appear on your radar by default, so that's helpful.
-Cybervoid looks astonishing and is way more fun to navigate around, they really learned from Doom Eternal in terms of "keeping things fresh", not only here but in the rest of the levels and mechanics.
-The motorcycle sections are great, aside from 1 level which felt too long, but overall excellent.
-The intro and end cutscenes look phenomenal in terms of artsyle and presentation!

I do have a few negative comments, aside from the "narrative issues" already mentioned, the rest are more related to platforming inconsistency, performance stability, deflect/parry not always working as intended, enemy tracking sometimes being BS, and worst of all... geometry ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ up deathless runs or challenges. But nothing that can't be fixed.

9/10 in combat, 7/10 in story, and with the other issues I'd say it's an 8.5/10. Really solid improvement over an already great first game. Keep it up everyone!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy my what a treat this game is, there's many things this game does better than the original, but there's also some things that have been downgraded. Everything I write is of course my subjective opinion so take it with a grain of salt.

What I love in Ghostrunner: The movement, I absolutely love the movement of these games, I honestly don't know a game that does it better.
The combat, it feels oh so satisfying to slash and dash through your enemies in these games. When you flawlessly execute a room you're left with this feeling of godhood I swear.
The aesthetic, the cyberpunk world is personally one of my favorites.

Some of the good things that's done better than in the original:
The levels are more varied in the way that you play through them, the obstacles and the way to solve the level is more interesting and flows very well.
The enemies are also more interesting to fight, there's more type of enemies and they're more dynamic as they actually move in this game (Shocking 2023 AI technology)
Bosses are way better, the bosses is the thing I disliked most in the original and they're done much better here (except for one boss, but I will not spoil)
The bike, the bike is cool af. Sometimes.
Abilities, the shared energy system is really nice. You can mix and match your abilities and build your own play style, and the upgrade system is really nice also with many interesting upgrades that makes the ability actually feel different.
Cybervoid is done better, it doesn't lock you out of using your dash so you're free to move around as normal.

The bad:
Its as if something is trying to kill your momentum, I noticed this in the demo and found it horrible, in the release it seems to be happening less but its still present.
The open world, the open world if it can even be called that looks like its an after thought. Its not very interesting as there's nothing really to do there besides ride your bike and find a few collectibles.
The bike, the bike can easily get stuck on any edge of terrain or obstacle, sometimes to the point where you have to reset.
The hub, the hub is a neat idea and could have been executed better, there's nothing really to do in the hub.
I also think its a shame they changed the sword animation, in the original it made you feel like more of a cyber ninja but this is probably just personal preference.

Overall I think its better than the original and I would definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
