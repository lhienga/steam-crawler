\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely loved this game and the first Ghostrunner. On the original I've collected all collectibles, I've bought every DLC, I've completed all the bonus modes (Wave Mode, Kill Run, and Hardcore mode) more times than I can count. I've always thought ninjas were super cool and I've always wanted to be one, this series is the closest I'll ever get. I've just recently collected all collectibles for the second game and I've completed the main story as well as all 4 stages of Stage Mode, I've also unlocked all upgrades and maxed out my motherboard. I cannot get enough of Ghostrunner, I REALLY hope that the developers make a third game because I'd buy it without hesitation. If you like fast paced, action packed, and intense gameplay then the Ghostrunner series is exactly what your looking for. I've found this game frustrating at times and I'm very grateful for that, it's been a long time since I've found a game actually challenging. Great story, epic gameplay, hours of play time, and characters you can really get attached to, I can't recommend this game enough, be sure to play the first ghostrunner though. Ghostrunner 2 does offer a recap animation from the ending of the first game but its not the same as actually playing through it. Ghostrunner and Ghostrunner 2 have been the best games I've bought in the last few years. Thank you so much to the developers and artists who worked so hard to make this game a reality. I pre-ordered the Brutal edition of Ghostrunner 2 and I wasn't disappointed at all, there are some minor glitches but none of them are game breaking or overly effect gameplay, as a matter of fact some of them I found very helpful. Anyways now I gotta go dig into hardcore mode, thanks again to everyone who helped create this masterpiece...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who adored the first game (and it's OST), I wanted to pick this game up to try. There will be a lot of comparisons to the first game in this review.

I waited for a bit, as I heard that the performance was really bad (comparative to running the original Ghost Runner w/ Ray Tracing levels of bad).



The Good:
♥ Overall, I enjoyed the game, and it was certainly longer than the first one. It took me 8 ish hours to complete the story while partially looking for the collectables (part of that was due to performance issues, which is covered later on).

♥ I quite enjoyed the story actually, It's a direct sequel to the original Ghost Runner.

♥ The soundtrack. If you liked the first game's OST, I'm confident you'll fall in love w/ this game's OST just as much. It's fantastic. Most of the songs are done by We Are Magonia, with some appearances from Gost, Dan Terminus, Arkadiusz Reikowski, and Danial Deluxe (The OG Ghost Runners composer/writer). Songs are uploaded on Spotify by 505 Games themselves, if you wanted to take a listen there.



The alright:
♥ Controls wise, it's slightly more clunky than the first game in my opinion, but generally the mechanics are on par with the last game. Later on in the game a mechanic completely breaks ledge boosts though, so keep that in mind if you intend to speed run/like that movement tech.



The bad:
I'm gonna start this off w/ the performance. It's ATROCIOUS. There is constant VRAM and DRAM memory leaks, meaning restarting the game every now and then will drastically improve your performance (in my experience).

♥ I originally downloaded this onto my 1TB HDD (although to One More Level's credit, they did recommend that an SSD is to be used), and the stuttering made the game entirely unplayable. I'd be going from 140+ FPS to 10 FPS every half second.

♥ Moving the game to my NVMe M.2 SSD resolved only some of the stuttering, and improved my overall average FPS to around 180 FPS ish inside the Dharma Tower. (Which is kinda weird that storage would play that drastically into my performance)

♥ As I progressed through the game, the performance got astronomically worse. It was so bad to the point where one of the last few boss areas dropped my overall average FPS to 20... I had to drop my render resolution to 25% in order to achieve 60 FPS in this area.

♥ The stuttering is constant, and random. To my knowledge there is no fix for it (at least I'm aware of). DX11 and DX12 mode both stutter just as bad in my testing, and it also really, really hampers the game. I hear that the console versions are also EXTREMELY poor in performance too, so I don't think it's just an issue w/ the PC version.



Overall rating
These are my personal opinions, so keep that in mind*

♥ Story -> 10/10
I really enjoyed the story. Jack has a LOT more voice lines, is more vocal, and seems to be significantly more free thinking. His interactions w/ the climbers are also a lot more personal, and more human rather than his original self in the start of the original Ghost Runner.

♥ Performance -> 1/10
When the game isn't stuttering it runs actually reasonably well. But w/ significantly lower frame rates than the original game. Highly, highly recommend to install this on an SSD if you have one. Otherwise it'll likely be unplayable. (Especially later on towards the end of the game).

Starting the game off, I averaged roughly 140-180 FPS @ 1080p max settings w/ my setup. However, after the first 2 levels that quickly tanked to low 90's. So I tweaked some settings, and that only brought it to 100-110 or so. Halfway through the game I was struggling to maintain 60 FPS min settings, and towards the last quarter of the game I barely maintained 30-40 FPS consistently.

♥ Mechanics -> 7/10
Some of the controls were moved around to make room for new mechanics (i.e. right clock is now block instead of grapple), and dashing in this game feels a lot more janky than in the original. DSJs and Ledge boosts are extremely inconsistent from a mechanical standpoint. Fine for normal gameplay, but for someone looking for an OG Ghost Runner experience w/ movement tech, it's a bit lacking.

♥ Speed-running? -> I'm not sure what to rate this tbh. A lot of the OG's mechanics were tweaked or changed partially from what I can tell (though I wasn't a very amazing speed runner of the last game, I did put some effort into learning some tech for a day where I do decide to commit to a run),



Overall TL:DR -> 6.8/10
I would love to give this game an 8.5/10 or higher, as at the core of it all it's good.

But the performance issues actually made it nearly unplayable. I had to take frequent breaks due to the constant stuttering causing motion sickness/rage. Also the stutters would often lead me to die right before a checkpoint, so I'd have to do ALL of a room again in order to progress.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot as good as the first one. But still worth it IF you really loved the first one and want to see the story end.

TLDR: Story is a nice continuation, and has a nice ending. Soundtrack is great. Graphics are fine, performance is not. Movement is janky / unreliable now. Levels have less "flow", feel slower at times. Difficulty is all over the place.

Graphics look very similar to the first game, but at least on my machine, I couldn't even get stable 60fps everywhere with FSR enabled! No problems in GR1 without FSR... sad :(

The hub between missions destroys pacing, imo. Why chase after other games' trends?
Also levels have become much longer, at least they feel longer. And I'm not going back to that hour long level just to find that one sword I missed.
Difficulty is weird. Some arenas are really easy, and so are some bossfights... then there are arenas harder than bossfights... not quite sure.

The movement seems off, not reliable. Especially in cybervoid, sometimes you jump really high, or suddenly your dash is much faster than usually, making you miss your target.
Pulling yourself toward a hook has a strange velocity curve, becoming faster again after the pull is done(??). Movement abilities like pull and dash do not add, but cancel each other out. And many jumps are made to look very close, so use a dash? Nah, now you'll miss it because you cancelled the other effect...
Also sometimes wallrunning will cancel and other bugs like that. Not too often to completely destroy the experience, but enough to be annoying.

Motorbike is sometimes a bit janky, but you don't spend too much time with it. Also was a lot of fun seeing outside.

Also ♥♥♥♥ paid skins DLCs.\t\t\t\t\t

In GR 1 there usually felt like there was a flow whether it be just traversing the level, combat, or both in a majority of levels even in the arena rooms I remember just moving kill to kill felt good. But in GR 2 traversing felt inconsistent with the mid air dash distance, ledge grabbing, and the god foresaken swinging / monkey bars. There have been times where one attempt I would able to swing from 1 bar to the other bar by just holding W then I reset / die down the line then all of a sudden on those same set of bars I come up short? Okay sure so add a dash mid air for more distance now usually that's fine but the thing that confuses me is that somehow you can still come up short with a swing + air dash then other attempts either almost overshoot it with a dash or just hold W and it's fine.

Combat in GR 2 especially in the arena-like rooms felt like the devs made the arena then just sprinkled random enemies around without rhyme or reason. Not to the point where it difficult or challenging but monotonous like "ah yeah this arena is 3x3 the lets put 2 pistol guys in the far left corner platform, 1 machine gun guy in the top right platform, 1 hammer guy with 3 melee guys on the center left platform, 1 mech in the center. Oh and lets make the platforms various different elevations almost making each platform like its own little combat island that isolates them from getting any assistance from the other platforms."

Bosses in GR 1 I remember having a blast the first set of levels but before hitting my first wall which was the first boss T0M getting like 30-40 deaths on that boss alone on my first play through. Then when I eventually beat T0M it actually felt like I got better and overcame that wall feeling accomplished and same thing with the other bosses. Meanwhile most "boss" fights in GR 2 were very forgettable without spoilers I remember I the 2nd and 3rd bosses were cool / unique where it didn't just boil down to (maybe parry then) slap them around abit till they get to a health threshold > do their chore so you they they're no longer regening HP and or being invulnerable > repeat this process 3x.

Story for GR 2 was okay. I did like how it brought up more lore about the ghostrunners and other things but the hub idea did kind of ruin it for me. GR 1 you got the story / dialogue through comms during the levels while traversing or even during combat but it fit the flow then onto the next level for more fun and story. With GR 2 on the other hand would still have comms during the level which was still good but then the momentum of going from level > hub > checking your upgradeable chips > talking to all the NPCs exhausting all dialogue options like some NPC from oblivion > lastly talk to the main NPC to go to the next level then repeat. It would just kill the momentum for me really. Though I did enjoy the banter between the 2 guards even if it was only for like the first half of the game.

GR 1 upgrades actually felt unique and actually made me want to use my brain figure out the best way to set up my tetris blocks to get the skills I want to fit with skills that affect different abilities / are from different trees. GR 2 really didn't do it for me ... It's just "Okay select a skill from a category you want and put it on a column. Good... Now that that column can only be skills from that category (repeat 4 other times as you unlock more capacity on your motherboard)"

Ultimates were a new addition that GR 1 didn't have. They seemed cool but every time I unlocked a new ultimate I'd use it once then just move on and forget about it and play with out it. At least they weren't mandatory to beat the game I guess ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯\t\t\t\t\t
