\r\n\t\t\t\t\t++ Very fun gameplay
++ Beautiful visuals
++ Very cool parcour and movement
+ Good soundtrack
+ Interesting story
+ Nice levels
+ Extensive upgrade-system
+ Cool bike and wingsuit

* Tedious Bossfights

- Open Wold segment does not fit at all + Bike gets stuck everywhere
- Rough facial animations
- Stiff conversations
- Annoying, bad dialogue

Just like the previous game, I liked this one a lot too. Main points are the great combat, parcour and the cool electro-soundtrack, which make this addicting to play. The characters and story premise left over from the previous game are pretty interesting and the levels are pretty good as well, having good architecture and paths.
There are some negative points though, killing the pacing and the feeling, which the first game did not have: The biggest negative point for me was the Open-World chapter, which did not fit at all. I SORELY missed a map in this part, and that the bike gets stuck absolutely everywhere makes this a damn pain to play. The homebase was an unexpected addition, however the conversations are very stiff, with weird pauses. Add some ROUGH facial animations and you start to wonder why this was added at all. Add some annoying, marvel-esque dialogue during the missions, and all new additions detract from the game's strengths.
Overall I liked the tight pacing, interesting story and levels from the first game more. I still had a very good time with this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 is the definition of what a sequel should be. It improves upon all the tenants the first game set up and expands the world and gameplay in ways even fans weren't expecting.

Visuals in this games are a subtly better than the first intallment but unfortunatelly there is no ray tracing support at the time of writing, with promises of full support in the near future. The game already looks good without it, it can only improve with its addition.

Sound and music go harder than the original in almost every level, there are some supreme bangers in this game save for 1 or 2 stages you will be adding almost all of them to your music playlist no doubt.

The gameplay includes everything you already knew from rail grinding and wall running to dashes and slowmo but it also adds new trinkets such as a gliding suit, ways to create new jumping points for your gap jammer and much more.

There is also the element of story here which is greatly expanded. Characters are given time to breath and show their own personalities but this not being the focus of the game or having enough budget to allow for true engagement, you will find yourself yearning for the next "standard" level pretty quickly. Fortunately these stage are as short as can be so they function more like little add-ons rather than anything else.

9/10. Great enough to stand up on its own against behemoths like Zelda and Spider-man at the game awards.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing from an addict of the first game, the sequel is a massive disappointment. Everything feels like a downgrade from the first game.

Combat and abilities: Clunkier. The basic loop is fine, but everything else feels worse. Blink has been completely ruined, now you have to manually mark targets before activating it (which often gets you killed because it does not stop time), shinobi's atttacks land before the attack animation finishes, and the deflect animation stops the flow of combat for the sake of a mildly cool execution.

Movement and parkour: Movement is largely unchanged, which is fine, but for some reason the rails that you slide on pull you in like a black hole whenever you get near them, often leading to death. The wingsuit and motorcycle are neat gimmicks in normal mode, but their terrible controls and handling become obvious in hardcore where pinpoint precision is required. The motorcycle especially has inaccurate hitboxes (you will often barely miss an obstacle, only for it to kill you anyway), a camera angle that makes seeing where you're going an issue sometimes, and a unique affinity for getting stuck absolutely everywhere, forcing a restart.

Everything else: They got rid of the unique Tetris-like upgrade system from the first game in favor of a more typical tree in which you place upgrade nodes. The problem is that several upgrades and perks make certain sections of the game literally impossible, leading one to wonder why they were ever added in the first place. The mid-section of the game is an absolute slog, where the game turns into a pseudo-open world that doesn't service the gameplay nearly as well as the indoor sections. The boss fights are fun, except for the Dismantler, who has a move that kills you the second he starts his animation and is also not telegraphed, so if you're right up on him when he uses it (which you probably will be), there is absolutely no way to prevent a death. There are still annoying puzzle sections that kill the pacing, but they are usually paired with some platforming that makes them not quite as obnoxious.
Hardcore mode is also really lazy and feels like the devs didn't even play test it with how most encounters are either trivialized by cheese or made almost impossible due to clunky controls and hitboxes.

Idk man it's fine but really disappointing after the first game.
6/10\t\t\t\t\t

(For context Im playing on dx 12 most settings on high with a i5 12600KF and a RTX 3070ti)
Tons of frame drops
Low FPS depending on the environment
Very Buggy
Functionality of wall running is poor (Sometimes too responsive and sometimes not responsive enough)
Hit boxes are confusing (Especially when grappling the wall spiders )
Clipping through walls (By going to fast and glitching through them or wall running into them)
Cannot manually respawn motorcycle when it bugs out or clips through wall
Bosses can bug out when switching phases

For the record I have no completed the full game but here's my current appraisal of the game:
This game is fundamentally different from the original game. It's not the same linear slice em up to advance kind of story. Now there is a lot more parkour and interaction with characters along with changes in how some stuff works. I think that this game is definitely worth the story and gameplay it brings, however, that is if they patch all of the kinks and bugs this game has. I feel that the original ghost runner ran fairly well on my older, much less powerful computer and that the original definitely felt more refined. This game feels dysfunctional in certain areas and definitely could use some reworking for how the player interacts with the environment ( Specifically those robots you have to grapple towards you, I almost always slip off ).

The new stuff they added is pretty cool. There is a lot of new mechanics to explore and the combat is now slightly different if you want to take a stealthy approach or a fast paced approach. I can't really speak on all of the new changes to combat since I pretty much stick to the old fashion slash and dash but from what I've tinkered around with there are definitely cool tricks you can do and new movement mechanics.

In short, if you look past the bugs and know how to work the graphics tab so your computer doesn't become ash in 2 seconds, I think you will have a very enjoyable time learning the lore of the game and all the satisfying slicing, dashing, and motorcycling you get to do in the game.

This is my first real review and I'll hopefully update this review when I finish the game and if the game's performance ever changes.
