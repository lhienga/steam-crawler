\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen i played ghostrunner, i cried tears of joy at a game that did not lack in visual aesthetic, interesting narrative, tight game play and mechanics, AND an all banger soundtrack. it was and is a beautiful and near perfect gem that will stand for an extremely long time. Ghostrunner 2 is the same, plus more. As a game with no scaleable difficulty, this translates for some to simply being harder. In some ways this may be true, but what good sequel hasnt added new mechanics and environments? These new mechanics and environments may present new challenges but they are not shortcomings of the game. That was to address one complaint i have seen that i found rather invalid. That said, there is an area of valid complaint. The NPCs are...pretty lifeless. Granted it is a rather extreme change of pace from the first game, having casual and candid conversations with ambient characters between levels, and it shows. The voice acting is still fine, cutscenes are orchestrated well, but alot of the dialog and smalltalk between jack and the other characters has alot of awkward pauses and lines delivered by swaying manequins. It is worth noting that these lacking points are the smallest and least notable part of the game and are most certainly not meant to be the focus but are simply meant to offer some more spoon fed background lore on the world and its workings. To me the effort and inclusion of an option to get these voiced lines giving me background is a nice inclusion that i can forgive the oblivion npc style of delivery.
