\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 is a fun train ride. They traded precise speedrunning gameplay of the first game for more storytelling and even some larger, less linear levels. The smug Marvel-esque dialogue with Kira is cringe as hell, I wish game developers stopped doing that. The story is forgettable.

The parkour sections are still the star of the show and most levels look absolutely fantastic. There's lots of trial and error which can be annoying but there are plenty of check-points so you won't be backtracking for too long.

The combat sections are so-so, not very well thought out and pretty messy overall, same for the boss fights. Hardly used my ultimate abilities and upgrades are only somewhat useful.

Like I said, if you're looking for an on-rails Cyberpunk hack and slash parkour game, you'll have a blast with this one. There are a lot of cool ideas and mechanics like the motorcycle levels, but maybe they tried to add a little too much of it and should have stuck to the core parkour + ninja action.\t\t\t\t\t
