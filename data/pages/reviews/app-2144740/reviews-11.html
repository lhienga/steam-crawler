A REDEEMING SEQUEL TO A MASTERPIECE

Ghostrunner 2 is the perfect follow-up of it's predecessor with much more to offer. I managed to beat this game in under 8 hours (excluding the simulation side missions)

PROS:
-Fluid and fast paced combat
-Efficient Optimization even for lower gen cards
-More characters, dialogue and depth than in the previous game
-Awesome story with a superb plot
-Phenomenal OST
-Optional "simulation" missions to test your skills and earn rewards

CONS
-The movement feels less polished and slower than the previous game.
-Very few extremely tedious levels (example: some of the cybervoid levels)
-Effortless and undemanding boss fights (example: the final boss)
-Few repetitive puzzles along the way

Verdict
If you enjoyed the first game then you will absolutely, without question enjoy this one as well. With all the pros listed above regardless of the cons, this game is an absolute gem worth buying. (you can get this for less than 20$ on sale).


8.5/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked Ghostrunner 1, you most likely gonna enjoy this one too. 80% of it is an improvement, another 20 could be done better.

As a huge fan of the first game i really enjoyed:

- visual design
- level variety
- increased versatility of combat and movement system
- more upgrade options
- new enemy types
- existence of hardcore and survival mode
- new challenges scattered across the levels

Somewhat undercooked, bitter-sweet additions:

- open world section (a very welcomed change of scenery, but feels empty and boring at times, not enough content there)
- hub area (welcomed addition as well, but too little to do there, not very engaging, feels kinda unnecessary)
- bike sections (feels great when it works, but often it doesnt works that well. Problems include awkward camera positioning on sharp turns and no free camera in general, getting stuck in the most insignificant piece of rubble, disability to retrieve the bike from some places so you have to restart from checkpoint)
- music is decent, but doesn't slaps as hard this time (there are couple of tracks i really liked and the rest is just okay, while each track form the first game was a banger)

What i didnt like:

- game pace is significantly slower and everything is easier this time around (first game was faster and harder, but had an option to slow things down for anybody desiring more casual experience. Second game offers just casual experience, with no option to crank things up for the veterans. Hardcore mode is harder, but still as slow)
- amount of technical problems, which wasnt the case with Ghostrunner 1 (freezing enemies, occasional crashes, stutters, gamebreaking bug in the end of a level prevented me from finishing it, had to redo the entire level)

Overall it's a good game, worths it's money. I just wish it could be even better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Easy recommendation. It improves on the first game in many ways, but also falls short in others.

Longer explanation, I really REALLY like this game, but I don't love it like I loved the first one. It has more interesting abilities, more interesting enemies (although they are easier), and a far more ambitious story and scope. However, this leads to the feeling that it's unpolished in some areas.

There's a thousand reviews that mention the "open-world" level, which let me just be clear it is not open world. Instead of just one objective at a time, there's 3 different objectives that you can do in any order but it's very contained in how you actually get there, there's only one way there and back to each objective. Now, I think that it is certainly the weakest section of the game, but I also don't think that it's inherently bad. I see people complaining that it exists at all, but I don't think having an open-world in a parkour style game is a bad idea at all, and I don't think people should write off the idea entirely. It just wasn't implemented very well and ends up being a little...boring. However, I commend the developers for trying new things, and taking the chance on some new ideas. Sometimes ideas work, and sometimes they don't. I just hope they take it as a learning experience, don't get disheartened, and approach the idea again from some new angles.

I think the most frustrating part of the game is definitely bugs. Compared to the first, it feels...janky. Like sometimes I wouldn't stick to the wall when wallrunning and just fall off. Or I'd get stuck, or I'd dash in a direction and go hurtling way farther than I should have, or I'll dash in a direction and go straight up in the air for no reason. Or I would parry an enemy's attack, it would travel back and hit them and then I'd drop dead at the same time. It's just a little buggy, but it's not a AAA studio so I expect there to be a few bugs. I didn't have anything game-breaking, just annoying little things.

The story is much more ambitious than the first game, and I don't know if I like it more? I like parts of it more, the characters especially are much more fleshed out than the first game, but I also feel like it was a less focused, less tight story. I think this is going to be a personal preference thing, but again, I'm glad they're trying new things.

Overall the game is really very good, and if the first game was a 9/10, this one was an easy 8/10. It's a little short, I finished in only 7 hours, and I also was messing around collecting the purple memory slot things, but I would still say it's worth full price, and if you ever see it on sale absolutely buy it. If you've already beaten the first one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner (1) sweat here, 3 hours into GR2.

At first the gameplay felt a little off, like I was missing a limb. SDSJ's don't seem to work so well anymore, or maybe the timing has changed and I'm slow to adjust. Otherwise, the movement is just as fun, even better in some aspects. I like the new upgrade system (and the new upgrades) more than the last game and the BOSSES... the devs have massively outdone themselves. GR1's bosses were extremely mediocre but the second GR2 boss is already one of my favourites of all time (not difficult from a gameplay perspective but incredibly visually appealing)

The characters have certainly improved over GR1, now with physical presences in between levels rather than being disembodied voices. The dialogue in the Hub is a bit awkward with pauses between sets of lines, but this will likely be polished up in the future and it's still engaging for now. The dialogue in mission is pretty funny, I'm enjoying it so far.

I really like the setting and background of Ghostrunner. It's rare for me to be absorbed into lore but GR2's story so far is quite interesting.

Also, the soundtrack slaps on GR2. The tracks in the previous game weren't very memorable, but they were good and I did keep them on over playing my own music. However, I can see myself listening to this OST outside of the game.

Overall and thus far, GR2 improves on its' predecessor (which was already a favourite of mine) in every single way. I waited for a sale to buy it because I'm broke but if you can afford it, support the devs!\t\t\t\t\t
