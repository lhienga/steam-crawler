\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally, I felt like GR2 felt like the alpha of what the game should be. There was so much that was janky, missing, unpolished, or just a downgrade from the original.

The load times: This game has a major problem with its loading time, especially coupled with the fact that 90% of the time when I loaded into a level, the game was still rendering part of the environment. I would load into a level, and there would be missing sounds, textures, or entire objects for upwards of 30 seconds. I don't mind a longer loading time for how visually stunning and complex this game is, but when the loading screen didn't actually load everything, that's a problem.

The open world: Personally, I feel like the open world was unnecessary. Ghostrunner is a game revolving around smooth movement and utilizing the environment to your advantage, trying to traverse tight environments quickly. The open world throws that out the window and makes movement boring for a good chunk of it. The barren wasteland separating the various towers from each other just felt like empty space meant to give you something to do while the characters droned on about useless information and jokes that felt forced.

The frame-rate issues (in the open world): This game was clearly not optimized very well. Upon reaching the top of each of the towers, 99% of people are going to look out at the world below, especially due to how stunning Ghostrunner is visually. To my dismay, when I looked down at the open world below me, i went from 140 fps to a staggering 24. I don't have to be a game developer to know that's a problem. It seems as though the render distance is pretty much max at all times, or at the very least excessively large, so when you'd look at the level below you, you would be loading pretty much the entire level. This also happened in a few combat rooms, which made completing said rooms tedious.

The story: Now, I don't honestly expect or need much from the story in a game like Ghostrunner. It's the gameplay that matters. That being said, the story was forced down my throat so much that I just started to ignore it at some point. There was so much dialogue in this game i felt my ears bleeding. Almost every room has some long line of dialogue or conversation or attempt at a joke. The dialogue was so long on multiple occasions that I managed to complete the room and move into the next dialogue trigger, causing the characters to just stop talking mid sentence and move on to the next bit of dialogue. Honestly, I started to ignore the story after about the 3rd level because it just felt like they were constantly infobombing me. Show, not tell.

The bugs: The thing that ultimately caused me to write this review. During the Dismantler boss fight, he just stopped moving. I couldn't damage him, no dialogue was happening, he just stopped. During the various time trials, the environment doesn't reset itself entirely, so certain things (those platform drone things that you pull towards you) just stayed where they were, causing me to have to restart again. In a combat room in the open world section of the game (the same room in which there were massive frame-rate drops I mentioned earlier), I pushed enemies off the edge with tempest. Multiple times, they would get stuck on the wall and would not fall down, causing me to have to either reset or waste more energy on them. In that same room, i pushed an enemy into a wall, and he just got stuck in it. I tried to kill him, to no avail, until he shot me through the wall.

The motorbike: This one pains me, truly. I wanted to like the motorbike, I really did. All I have to say: please let us move the camera and look around. driving up the wall and not being able to see any of my surroundings does not work. Also, please less gun and sword combat, and more jumping off the bike and letting it run into enemies or bombs, its fun.

The boss fights: Most of the boss fights felt lifeless compared to the first game. The opening fight with Ahriman was almost interesting, except it really boiled down to "run around, hit him with the stun, then hit him a few times." I feel like more could have been done with the gap jammer in this fight. It could have been cool to use it to pull us closer, or to other platforms, or something like that. The Naga boss felt really boring. It really was just ride around on the motorbike and occasionally left click a few times. It was also just not animated half the time, honestly really disappointing for such an interesting idea. Bring back tom.



Now, that's quite a lot of negativity, but I do want to highlight some of the game's more positive aspects.

The motorbike: Yea, the motorbike is in both sections. It's cool. Felt kinda janky, but looked really cool. I do like the fact that you could jump off and it would send you fairly high.

The combat: when it works, the combat is really fun. Assuming no bugs are happening, it is an upgrade from the original. I do really like the ultimates, and I feel like they allow for interesting playstyles. Ultimately, its Ghostrunner but more.

The upgrades: I think a lot of the upgrades in this game were really cool. Every time I'd see a new upgrade unlocked, I always wanted to try it out, so I'd slap it on. I think the variety of upgrades and various types are pretty cool. I can't lie, I do miss the tetris, though. Overall, good upgrades. They felt impactful, but not like one was inherently better than another.

The parkour and movement: This is where this game truly shines. It's movement is the same as the first game, but there are more ways to interact with the environment, and I truly felt like GR2 tried to create unique arenas. Much like the first game, many of the combat rooms felt like a puzzle to solve, and it feels so good finally having it click. The movement, as always, feels fluid and fast. A couple of new interactables in the environment, but overall nothing too new, just a bit more of what I already loved.

The environment: Very unique environments, as always. There was so much going on, and so many cool things to look at in the background and foreground. Each of the different areas felt fresh and unique. The lighting was also really well done. Visually, a hit once again.


Edit: After finishing the game, the final boss fight was the most boring part of the game. It felt bland, slow, and uninteractive (as did most of the boss fights for that matter).

I think GR2 had potential, but it fell flat in a lot of areas for me. It felt like they spread themselves thin and tried to do a bit of everything, and ended up doing nothing particularly well.
I'd pay full price for the first game, but I'd wait for this one to be on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm kind of up in the air in this one as I really loved the original Ghostrunner. There are some strange bugs with custom controls where some of them won't let you rebind keys (mostly just the hub which I don't understand why it has a different set of controls in the first place) and every time you launch the game, some of the controls reset to the default controls. I've never seen a game like this, let alone a game released in 2023. Those were fortunately the only bugs I dealt with in my playthrough.

First, I'll go with the good. The gameplay is still fun. It's fun to run, dash, climb, etc as you traverse the level and take out enemies with your katana or other abilities. Most of the enemies will be familiar with what you faced in the original, there's no real change there. The graphics are quite a bit nicer here and the world is beautiful. It's mostly set in an apocalyptic wasteland rather than a cyberpunk world compared to the first.

There are also some neat vehicles like the motorcycle. It was pretty fun to use although it overstayed its welcome a bit in my opinion. The game is about traversal and finding cool ways to kill enemies, the motorcycle was pretty cool but it took away from the actual game play to kind of pad your time in the game. Late in the game you also get a wing suit which was fun to use for traversal as you use momentum to swing up and down into small openings. The mechanics for the wing suit were solid and I wouldn't mind playing a game focused on it to be honest.

That being said, the level design took a massive step back in comparison to the first. The original Ghostrunner felt just as much of a puzzle game as it was an action game and this one just feels like a straight up action game. In the original, every wall, ramp, opening, etc was intentionally placed. as you practiced clearing a room, you would find the optimal path to take and feel like a real ninja taking out all the enemies with precision and skill. In the sequel, every room kind of just feels like a big arena with some walls. It really takes a lot of fun out of the game.

I still enjoyed Ghostrunner 2 and I would still recommend it on sale but just know, while it's a pretty upgrade on visuals, the gameplay loop is not nearly as satisfying. Hopefully if they make a Ghostrunner 3, they'll focus more on clearly defined encounters rather than just large empty rooms with a few obstacles.\t\t\t\t\t
