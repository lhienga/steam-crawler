\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(The following opinion is rooted with the experience of Ghostrunner 1 [GR1])

If you like GR1 as a fast-pace challenging game, you might not like GR2. In general, I like GR1 more than GR2. I still thumb up for GR2 since I respect all the effort.

Major Changes to Previous Mechanics
GR2 changed two major mechanics compared to GR1, which are stamina and blocking. For stamina, in GR2 there is a general energy that allows players to dash and parry, which lets players dash multiple times by default unlike GR1. For blocking, in GR2 players can hold down the block button to keep blocking instead of pressing the block button just on time to block the attacks in GR1. These changes allowed players to dash more and defend from attacks easier, so the battle difficulty in GR2 was more forgiving but less challenging than GR1.

New Mechanics
Motorbike, wingsuit , and robot platforms . (spoiler as not shown in the trailers)

Both motorbike and wingsuit provide stimulating gameplay experience with some drawbacks. First, both of them need their own maps to work, especially wingsuit is unlocked really late and doesn't have much time to use. Second, motorbike can often be hung and stuck on an edge or a cliff if you don't die on the crash. Third, some motorbike missions kinda break the whole pace of the game, especially that one outside map mission for motorbike is comparably long due to the free-roaming level design.

Robot platforms are terribly implemented as they share the same UI with grapple points. During the first playthrough, players do not know about the map and when they see a big (E) in far away, they cannot easily figure out if it is a grapple point or robot platform . Thus, players may die at the first time unfairly since they are not clear if they are pulling themselves towards the item or pulling the item towards them.

Storytelling
Story in GR1 is concise but polished while GR2 tried to develop the world and characters but presentation is kinda boring. For example, the Hub acts as a transition between mission and it helps to tell background story but the dialogues were really flat. Also, the whole idea of the Hub slows down the game pace especially if you are looking for a fast-pace action game like GR1, which highly focuses on the missions.

Overall, GR1 is fast-paced with compact and challenging level design while GR2 is slow-paced with casual level design but extra mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPROS:
Those who say less is more and gr1 had more fun combat just have not given the game enough time, towards the endgame once you unlock all your perk slots the game has so much build crafting potential and the combat becomes so fun i am extremely satisfied, at first i was also disappointed i wont lie but it just keeps getting better and better, i cant say the brutal edition is worth it just yet but the base game is definitely worth.
Overall the levels and bosses are much more unique and fun, the visuals are unbelievable especially compared to the first, and the music is SIGNIFICANTLY better.

CONS: If you're used to gr1s combat then the beginning will definitely feel slow, the endgame activity imo is a fun but too short for the build potential and can be frustrating compared with limited lives compared to the infinite attempts you would get in wave mode. GR1 was also one of the best optimized games ive had in my whole library but this game can get subjected to a lot of stuttering at random. Finally the wheel enemies just don't feel like they belong in this game.

TLDR: the game definitely has some issues but if youre not easily frustrated the base game is very worth it especially towards the end\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tpros:
- I love the first game, so finally I can play the second one)
- bike!
- characters can be not only heard in radio calls but finally could be seen alive)
- bosses (but quite few)

cons:
- the game feels a bit easier than the previous one
- block ability destroyed concept of puzzle-battles like it was in the first game, where firstly you should've found a right route to complete a battle
- hub location and partly "open world" in some levels are looks a bit out of place for GR from my perspective
- in roguerunner.exe the is no possibility to start simulation with the 2nd level if 1st was completed before, which makes no sense to play it until all simulation levels will be unlocked. So this mode has no sense until almost the end of the game
- despite roguerunner.exe levels are available via game main menu, hidden challenges are not, so it's boring to rerun completed levels only to re-run hidden challenge for a higher grade even if it was unlocked previously
- it's annoying to wait 3 seconds before hidden challenge will be started when you make a lot of tries to complete it
- bike controls a bit irresponsive and it often stucks somewhere in the textures

couple of bugs I've faced with:
- "Buy all the upgrage chips" achievement was given when I've bought NOT ALL chips (1 chip remained unbought)
- sometimes during hidden challenges restart, the speed of GR from the previous run was stored. as a result, new challenge try GR could just fly away somewhere very high
- sometimes color effect of Sensory Boost ability remains after die/restart in the case if it was active at the die/restart moment\t\t\t\t\t
