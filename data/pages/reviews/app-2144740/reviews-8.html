When the second game was announced, I was not sure if I should buy it, and the €40 price is definitely too much.

After buying this game on sale, I had my doubts about how good it was going to be. The first 2-3 hours were kind of boring to me. However, after getting the bike, the game got really good. The story became more interesting, and the level design improved with each stage. In the end, I still think this game is too short for its price, but it did not leave me disappointed like the first one did. This time, I will definitely replay some stages and maybe even go for 100%.

If I must give it a rating: the first game is a 7/10, and this one an 8.5/10. If you played the first and liked it, or if you're a new player looking to buy the games, I recommend purchasing both when they are on sale and playing them back to back. That's probably the best way to experience these games.\t\t\t\t\t

-Better skill system, and better skills overall
-Exploration to find collectibles is handled better
-You now have an ability and an ultimate instead of just having an ultimate
-There aren't any bad levels
-More note worthy stuff to do
-You can go way faster in the game if you have the correct build for it
-Blocking is a good addition
-You can actually see what killed you, when you die
-Improved QoL over the first game

What it does worse:
-Wall climbing is super janky, some walls will just eject you midrun and cause you to die.
-Enemy readability is much worse (especially the gun enemies)
-The Stamina system is terrible for movement, but makes sense for blocking
-The outside levels aren't that fun to play, and are too long
-Boss fights are weaker
-Hardcore mode feels more like them throwing death planes everywhere, than an actual difficulty increase
-The story is mediocre, and I don't think we needed a hub just to not talk to npcs.
-Certain levels aren't connected, so it makes the game feel more disjointed (likely due to the hub)
-I had way more crashes, stutters, and bugs in this game over the first, hopefully they'll fix these issues down the line.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 handles some aspects of its predecessor better, and some worse. One thing I've noticed in GR2 that differs from GR1 is the way the movement and camera flow together. GR1 had more static, snappier movement, which created a better feeling of control over your character. GR2 does the opposite. The camera and movement are more fluid and shaky. The fluidity adds to the game in some ways, but can create some rather disorienting situations. GR2 really expands onto GR1's setting and story, and creates a better understanding of it. GR2's boss fights are absolutely outstanding though, compared to GR1. Some boss fights are more than just a "hit-dodge-hit" system, with some bosses having parkour sections and traversal sections in their levels.

To conclude, I would give Ghostrunner 2 a 10/10, same as GR1. The graphics, gameplay, lore, and soundtrack all stand out when compared to other games, and helps create a more unique experience.\t\t\t\t\t
