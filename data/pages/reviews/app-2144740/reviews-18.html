\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 is how you do a sequel right. Take everything we love about the first and add more. New enemy types, abilities, customization options, a roguelite mode, a motorcycle and a wingsuit. It all fits together so nicely. This is the best first person parkour game ever. Way better than Mirror's Edge and Dying Light. Those games suffer from missed potential imo. Ghostrunner fully realizes its concept and plays to its strengths. Fast, brutal combat and challenging platforming. No more, no less. There are a few more open ended levels with multiple objectives, but it's nonetheless a linear experience and that's where it shines.

The presentation is, once again, jaw dropping. The lighting from neon signs with an art style that blends bright colors with gritty cyberpunk architecture make for pure eye candy. The cyberspace sections are gorgeous. Very abstract and angular. Later in the game, you go outside the city and see the arid wasteland that's been left behind. There's good variety featured here from levels to enemy types. Arenas are brilliantly crafted with verticality, environmental hazards, platforms and carefully placed enemy types to make you use every tool at your disposal. Nothing feels unnecessary. I will say I only used one of the three ultimates exactly one time. You'll never need to use those.

Movement is still deep with a high skill ceiling. Controls are tight and responsive as expected. The way movement and combat are blended so seamlessly is a thing of beauty. It's like Hotline Miami mixed with Mirror's Edge. Death comes swiftly for you AND your enemies. All you need is that one swift strike to take an enemy down and it's immensely satisfying. The challenge comes from getting close enough to cut them down. There are no guns (aside from the bike) and most enemy types have guns or other ranged attacks. It takes quick, on the fly, thinking and reflexes to avoid any damage and carefully pick apart enemies one at a time. Shielded enemies are vulnerable to the bullet time dash. Mechs can be temporarily disabled with the shuriken. Bullets can be deflected back with a perfectly timed parry. It's surprisingly complex.

Boss fights are great and varied. The devs were nice enough to add checkpoints during the boss fights, so I don't throw my keyboard out the window in frustration. The Dismantler is incredibly designed. Three separate health bars that each need to be depleted along with three movesets. It's in a large arena. He can summon enemies and heal himself. It's the most dynamic fight demanding a different approach depending on what state he's in mid-fight. He should've been the final boss tbh.

The soundtrack slaps. I love that dark grimy synthwave. Nothing else could set the tone properly and match the intensity of the gameplay. The motorcycle inclusion had me curious, but concerned. Vehicles can ruin gameplay. The bike from Turbo Overkill triggers painful flashbacks. This surprisingly works well. Jumping off the bike to avoid lasers and grappling back midair while it's still moving is so immensely satisfying. I can't put it into words. However, the camera on the bike is atrocious at times since you have no control over it and it can just move on its own at time hiding what's ahead getting me killed immediately. Let's get into the issues.

Performance is not so great. This game likes to lockup and chug in the middle of the chaos getting me killed. Certain areas are just badly optimized. Most of the time I'm getting 90+, but it likes to plummet down to the 50s in certain areas and transitions. I'm also just indifferent to the writing. I get Kira is supposed to be sassy and cheeky, but she just became annoying with her constant need to drop quirky one liners like a bad superhero movie. It's hard to care about these cardboard cutouts masquerading as characters when almost all development is handled outside of the core gameplay in the HQ. The new skills system is boring in comparison to the Tetris like design of the first game's skill system. I like the variety of locations, but those outside desert levels look extremely bland and uninspired by comparison. Every texture is so washed out. Thankfully, the sandworm boss does not disappoint. The hub HQ is just unnecessary. It's only there for exposition. It never evolves into anything more than just a pace killer.

I loved the game regardless. I was hooked from beginning to end and look forward to wherever this series goes next. It took me 7 hours to complete for me, but with all of the challenges, unlockable hardcore difficulty mode, roguelite mode, and collectibles, you definitely get plenty of bang for your buck. I recommend it for the price tag.\t\t\t\t\t

~ FINAL OVERVIEW ~

GR2 is mostly the same as GR, introducing some mechanics and removing others.

UPGRADES:

The music. Oh, the music. We Are Magonia did a fantastic job on the soundtrack, as did Dan Terminus. 10/10 eargasm. Congrats.

The bike was a great addition, and the first bike level (I Won't Be Back Today) is a perfect introduction to the bike mechanics. The fast-paced music and gameplay made for easily my favourite level of the game.

Being able to leave the tower to explore was a great idea, and the Asura is a perfect antagonist for Jack.

The boss fights are no longer mindless dodging and slashing, and involve some extra mechanics that really improve gameplay, such as the towers that Madhu the Dismantler summons to shield himself. They require critical thinking and strategic positioning to quickly deal with.

DOWNGRADES:

The outdoor, openworld levels were lacking in entertainment.

The bike levels after I Won't Be Back Today were often rather dull, just riding monotonously through grey barren landscape with little to nothing to break it up.

The new shuriken mechanics are well done, and add a good way to quickly kill or reach enemies from distance, but removing the other well-known abilities, such as Blink, Surge and Overlord and mitigating them to Ultimates was not a good idea. They were great helps in the first game, and helped break up monotonous slashing and hacking.

Pillars Of Creation. Need I say more?

~ OVERALL RATING ~

9/10. Definitely recommend playing the first Ghostrunner first.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of the first game, getting this one wasn't even a decision. It's very good, but somewhat different from the first. At its core, it's still a parkour action game, with a cyberpunk visual and musical style.

The first one was a very straightforward game, get from point A to point B and try not to die. This one have some "semi" open world sections that might turn some players off. However, it really isn't the focus of the game and it doesn't overstay its welcome.

Controls are still very responsive, with some newer commands, like blocking. It feels more robust than the first one in this regard, and i have nothing but praise for this.

The visuals are really good, again, just like the first one. Even though most of the game is passing very fast because of your movement, you feel the ambient and the music really helps to make the experience more immersive.

There's a great deal of customization for your playstyle as well. There's more options than the first one, with some combinations turning the game into a complete ludicrous experience, where you're running around at lightning speed throwing skills that instakill people, or making your character better at slower, tactical fights.

My only real complaint is about the story. It feels weaker than the first one, and the character of the Ghostrunner loses some of its mystery, which is a shame. This time he's much more talkative, in the later stages he even feel like chatting during a crisis that might very well doom them all. Feels strange, but nothing that cripples the game.

Voice acting isn't bad by any means; most characters are convincing, especially Kira and Bakunin. What feels strange, for me, is that while you're in the hub area, the characters speak with long pauses, and their models are quite strange, so it breaks the immersion a bit.

But, these "problems" are just me nitpicking. It's a great game and it will give you some very good hours doing parkour and slicing people up. If you liked the first one, just go for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you really liked Ghostrunner and want some more here it is...

To start out with, this game does a lot right and good, but is overall a bit rougher around the edges than the first game.
The story while decent isn't nearly as tight knit as the first game was, no clear goal for a lot of it, and a lot more of an aimless one at that. Ghostrunner never had a great story, but it was straight forward and carried on characters, which is done only partially here.
As for gameplay it peters between the best the series has had, and just kind of boring. The excellent upgrade system has been replaced with points that make you prioritize challenges and secret finding in an otherwise linear game. The difficulty balance is a lot more wild, but generally being a bit to easy and less focused on actual parkour and mobility than the first game. Instead of having a fairly simple hit boss X number of times they have fully fledged health bars that makes them a lot more annoying to actually fight, rather than focusing on a single method to beat them. Most of the first games powers are now substantially weaker but have an awkward use in puzzles outside of that. Replacing powers from the first are a new set which are wildly unbalanced from each other, and both recharge too long, and are rarely interesting to use, typically being a kill X group button comparatively.
Contrary to what a lot of people seem to mention in other reviews I actually rather enjoyed the motorcycle segments and the outside. Finding it a breath of fresh air (pun there) and one I don't generally see as really overstaying it's welcome either. Cybervoid puzzles are far far better though in nearly all regards.
TLDR: The game is still Ghostrunner, its still good albeit a bit rougher. Struggles to really get in the groove though until the last act.\t\t\t\t\t
