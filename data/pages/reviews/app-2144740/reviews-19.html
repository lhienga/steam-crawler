\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was an enjoyable experience overall but it feels little more than an extension of the first game and falls short of deserving it's own title (and £35 price tag). Even though I had fun with it, it was hard not to compare it negatively with the first game in most scenarios. The upgrade system is seriously underwhelming and half of the upgrades are almost useless, although the way to piece them together and maximise their effectiveness, similar to the first game, is engaging and provokes some degree of thought. Though in reality it has very little affect.

The hub world was probably the worst inclusion that they introduced since the first game. Where Ghostrunner had a continuous flow where the events and plot flowed through conversations had whilst you were playing, 2 now stops you to listen to in a meaningless hub world which only contains (other than characters to talk to) a machine to buy, not even apply as that's done in a menu, upgrades and a machine to play a short mini-game.

There was one main inclusion however which I really enjoyed and that was the cyberbike! It provided a break to the core game-play loop of running around and slashing in a positive way which preserved the tempo of the game, unlike the hub world. It was something new to learn and fit the pace of the core game-play which then allowed them to be used together later on in the story.

Overall I think if you enjoyed the first game, this is a no brainer as they are almost the same game, although this installment is far less of a challenge. However, it is also for this reason that I cannot recommend enough that you wait for a sale. 100%ing all the achievements took me under 14 hours which for a £35 game, especially one that is a near identical sequel, is atrocious.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDifficult to review, while it was enjoyable to play, it feels like there's steps forwards and plenty of steps backwards.

I think if you enjoyed the 1st, you'll very likely enjoy this. I just personally had no desire to replay it more after completing the campaign, unlike how I did with the 1st.

For the good things. They've given you some of the stuff, like abilities and ultimates, a lot earlier. Felt good because it would have been silly for Jack to have just forgotten stuff like shuriken. I much prefer the skill tree in this one. The combat and movement is all crisp smooth, I never experienced anything bad with the flow of combat and movement while on foot. The roguerunner was pretty fun too and a cool way to add even more replayability.

For the things that were iffy to me. The story, it felt forced and pushed on me. In between missions, they'd be this hub with NPCs to talk to. Felt very unnecessary for a runner game like what the first one was. Voice overs during the missions are fine enough story, it's the gameplay that attracts people.
The gimmicks. It felt like I unlocked some weird gimmick every other level nearly, like the bike and an unlock on the almost last mission, which you use pretty much just for that one mission. The bike especially felt really janky to ride on during some parts.
Most of the levels just felt quite bland to go through, though I can't put my finger on why so take that with an extra grain of salt.
The bosses all seemed really quite easy, which goes back to the difficulty thing.
Bloated might be a good word for it, but you get so much stuff, then either use them and ditch them or use them just the one time for that specific level.


Before I got the bike, I was thinking it was really great, bar the forced story, that it felt like Ghostunner 1+++. I think the game tries to be way bigger and better than Ghostrunner 1 and it suffers because of it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummery!
If GR was part one, GR2 is part 2 a solid 7/10 would recomend if you enjoyed the first and or if u able manage some jank, wanting a basic but fun action game with some light puzzle elements!

Being a bit harsh on ghostrunner (ghost runner 1 and or GR) i have to say ghostrunner 2 (GR2) improved alot opon gameplay issues with GR
Alot of arenas have been opened up alot more bringing alot of freedom aswell as improving abilties like the shurikans and improving the variability of enemies that you encounter.
This together with the fact of missions being more variable in general makes the game feel alot more like a action game then a puzzle game which to each their own i believe its better this way!
I also felt like the bosses were much better but they still suffers from jank as it just doesnt really feel right when cutting them up but still a massive improvment from the first games bosses!

I did really miss the neon part of the first game and overall some of the mission design of the first one or rather the enviroment design.
Some the missions outside of the city are weird, it turns a cyberpunk game into a mad max title which isnt what i wanted and it kind of made me dissapointed. I did enjoy the boss encounter and the entry into the desert aka the scripted bikeriding but other then that disapointed, not sad just disapointed.

I do have to say that while i really like the music of GR2 being composed by "We are megonia" and similar artists, but me being desensitized to the artists that composed the music of GR2 made me wish a bit for the tracks from GR which in my opinion had a really distinct charm, a bit more mysterious vibe or how you want to put it.

The series still suffers from some jank in cutscenes and QTEs but im not sure if its better worse or the same as the first game.

Overall the game does alot of things right when it comes to the gameplay which should come first aswell as make the lore more intresting but i did feel like the plot of the first game while more predictable felt better, and sadly again the third act is just weird, it kind of feels like it comes out of no where and its just feels jank storywise. This together with the fact that the sounds and looks not as good as the first makes it a bit of a disapointment, just my opinon!

Sadly, like the first entry the game it just feels a bit too short like another hour of something would have improved my enjoyment but i understand why it ends when it does!
Dispite them setting up a cliffhanger inside its audiologs!

If GR was part one, GR2 is part 2 a 7/10 would recomend if you enjoyed the first and that u can manage some jank wanting a basic but fun action game with some light puzzle elements!\t\t\t\t\t
