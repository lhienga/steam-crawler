\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR
So, I have 125 hours in GR1 and it's my second favourite game besides DOOM Eternal.

I personally like the sequel more, it feels more enjoyable, with more experimentation and I'd say that it is even more addicting (In a good way) than GR1.

Let's start with good things:
-Bosses are a soild improvement. I'd say that only T-073-M holds up to the bosses from GR2. Rahu is a better Architect boss, Ahriman, Mitra and Mahdu are more engaging than whatever was that Mara boss fight in GR1. And Naga is iust pure adrenaline rush. Glad to see a boss while you are riding the bike

-OST is still god tier. I'd say it is equal to the GR1 OST. Daniel Deluxe, We are Magonia, Gost, Dan Terminus and Arkadiusz Rejkowski all have made a solid piece of art.

- Superabilities are a nice addition, although you'll use them max. once in a deathless run. Maybe with exception of the level Pilars Of Creation , because of how long this level is.

-I personally find the bike to be very fun. However it can be clunky due to strange collisions.

-Characters are all well made. Jack still is a badass. Zoe is the same as in GR1. Saul and Kira are nice additions. Bakunin really grew on me. Connor is meh, Mitra gave him a reality check tbh. As for the villains: Ahriman is specific, I like that he has a sense of honor and that he treats defeat as a disgrace, which his death confirms for me. However he's kinda underused. Mitra has an interesting backstory, you kinda feel bad for him. I like his transition between Cold and Ruthless to Rageful in the final boss. Yet, he's backstory in my opinion needed more time. Madhu has an interesting ideology and his Dismantler form was a surprise for me. Rahu is the G.O.A.T., that's all you need to know about him:P

-Graphics are beautiful. However keep in mind that if you play on low quality, characters faces can turn into a light bulb.

-They kept the Slide-Jump mechanic from GR1 <3

-The finishers are satisfying to do

- Wingsuit is something I didn't expect. Although I didn't like it at first, it grew on me after playing with it for a bit.

-Basically everything I liked about GR1 is in GR2

-Challenges in levels are also a welcomed addition, but man, F#ck the one in Licking the Wounds . Also I think that you should have a skin for completing all of challenges.

-The story is fine, I enjoyed it

--Roguerunner.exe. It's fun, but I prefer the Kill-Runs or Wave Mode from GR1

The mid:
- The level Pilars of Creation. It's way too long and you can easily get lost. I like that it's open and you can easily go for a long ride with the Bike, but it doesn't fit the game in my opinion. It was the only level where I raged a bit
-The hub world. Nice idea, but again it kinda doesn't fit the game. Also it should have more LOREEEEE in it. However: Victor and Butch........ Their interactions are f##king hilarious and they carry the Hub World. I wish they got more interactions
-You should be able to see if you did the challenge in levels. When you are grinding achievements you have to remember which one you did and which not. This can get kind of problematic.
-The ending is a good idea, mid execution. It should have been a cutscene, not a simple walk in the Hub

The bad:
-Random FPS drops. In some cases you can experience heavy FPS drop. I personally experienced it in the level A Price To Be Paid , however I'm one of the lucky ones



Do I recommend the game?
It's an obvious YES

Ghostrunner 2 is one of, if not the most fun game I've played:)
I hope to see more features to come in the future. It's just unfortunate that GR2 didn't caught the attention and it's not that popular. It deserves it.\t\t\t\t\t

Gameplay
If you're looking for an incredible, top tier gameplay experience from this game, you can't get any better than this. Vastly outperforms even the original in that regard. You're introduced to an impressive amount of new mechanics throughout the game at a perfect pace, the devs never let you get bored of a mechanic before introducing a new toy. Each mechanic feels incredible to utilize, leading to even more satisfying moments throughout the game as you get more and more mechanics to string together.

Story
While the story might not win any awards, it's much more engaging than the previous title. You're much more involved with a tangible threat that, when coupled with the gameplay innovations on offer, creates a great sense of progression throughout.

The Hub
There's a few aspects in particular that Ghostrunner 2's expanded scope does not stick the landing with. Luckily, every single one of these aspects is contained to a single, separate instance that only occurs for around 5 minutes periodically after certain levels. Nothing about the Hub worked for me. The animations were quite stiff here, and there's an attempt at dialogue options that never feel natural. Abrupt tonal shifts and pacing problems are constant. It's very jarring to go from the beautifly animated, perfectly paced package that is the rest of this game, to the EXACT opposite. It feels tacked on in the truest sense of the phrase.

TLDR
The developers exceeded even my highest expectations with the aspects that mattered the most. Gameplay is some of the best I've ever played in any video game, and the story is a step up from the original. The aspects that don't work for me, while they really dont work, are all self contained within the Hub world, which you never spend more than 5 minutes in at a time. 99% of your experience will be spent with an extremely fluid, satisfying gameplay formula that evolves meaningfully throughout\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I think the original ghostrunner is one of the best games of all time.

This game is very much the same with an excellent bike mechanic. But overall it can be quite buggy and the boss fights are VERY dumb. I'm also not a huge fan of the new upgrade/memory layout mechanics.


Epic:
- The soundtrack. Another 10/10 like the original.
- The movement is near identical to GR1 and it has the same overwhelmingly satisfying movement/violence as GR1.

Good:
- The bike missions; really great mechanic and they blend it with the regular gameplay to make a few levels really fun and engaging

Meh:
- Wallrunning OOB seems to have been reduced
- The stamina system makes dash-on-dashing harder and makes the movement a little bit less satisfying. it also doesn't really seem to serve a purpose
- The story; its there, but I didn't really care about it
- The wingsuit; why introduce it so late into the game? There are so many degrees of freedom to exploit and make the levels more interesting! But instead its all jammed into one level at the end of the game and its certainly not up to its potential. Also dashing from wingsuit makes you just fall which is frustrating.
- Enemies are pretty much the same as in GR1 with a few new ones and a little more variety.
- The new abilities are ... fine I guess. Basically the same as in the first game (+ shuriken) but not as powerful.
- Some levels in Dharma city are good, outside on the ground the world is kind of empty though. overall I liked the vibe though so I won't dock them too much

Bad:
- The upgrade system. Tetris system from past game was perfect, interesting, and original! This upgrade system in GR2 is dumb.
- Blocking. In theory this would be a great mechanic but in practice, along with the select ability keys, is just too much stuff to keep track of. I used block on the first level and after that never used it again.


Abysmal:
- Game breaking UE4 glitches occur very frequently, especially at the last boss fight
- The actual mechanics/gimmicks of sword v. sword/Gr v. gr boss fights are INCREDIBLY dumb. You basically have to (1) find the quirk to kill the boss and (2) repeat it ad infinitum. it does not feel like a cool boss sword fight at all. I thought the bosses in GR1 was underwhelming, but this is a whole other level of frustration and stupidity. You can't defeat the bosses by being evasive, but you can by moving around at 2mph and exploiting the one trick to defeat the bosses over and over. Truly some of the worst boss fights (besides the bike boss/tunnel "fight") compared to what they COULD be.\t\t\t\t\t

The upgrade system is changed up from the first game but with in my opinion more interesting upgrades while retaining the puzzle element of the first game (and some upgrades from the first game such as item radar and reflect projectiles not needing upgrades). The bike can be hit-or-miss, but I found it to hit more times than not.

My one caution, and this is a big one, is the performance. As of this review I encountered numerous stutters along with fairly long loading screens even on very low settings. Even moments where the game doesn't load things, halting progression. If you can run the demo fine, go ahead. If not, maybe wait until it is optimized more.\t\t\t\t\t
