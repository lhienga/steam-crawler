\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been hooked on Ghostrunner ever since it came out a few years ago, and after enjoying it and project Hel quite a lot the mere announcement of this game was enough to make it my most anticipated release this year, and certainly for me it has not disappointed. The gameplay is tweaked a little from the first, and whilst one or two things stick out to me as better in the original (I preferred it when deflections demanded a well-timed attack rather than a dedicated block ability, for instance) overall most of them dramatically spice up the gameplay and the game uses them to excellent effect in its new levels and throws in a lot of engaging new enemy types (alongside one or two carried over from project Hel). The story is also a step-up: though the first game managed to create a genuinely engaging story within the confines it set up for itself, 2 manages to take advantage both of the player's presumed familiarity with many characters and the new status quo set up by the previous game's ending to thrust you right in the action and goes some really interesting places with it! The boss fights are also a marked improvement, but the real stand out change here has to just be the graphics and presentation themselves, most of all the soundtrack, which is 100% bangers.\t\t\t\t\t
