\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love ghostrunner. LOVE! one of my favorite games for sure. but this? this isn't it.

right off the bat the game felt unpolished, something that would become all the more clear as I continued playing. this is a good example of a game going "release now, fix later".
a few example include, but are not limited to:
• killing enemies feel choppy compared to previous game.
• consistent crashes upon launching the game.
• missing audio/sound design.
• RTX will come in a later update.
maybe make a complete product and THEN release it? it's so sad to see this way of rushing releases are effecting even non-AAA devs.
without spoiling anything; the whole bike section was awful and dull, the ending unsatisfactory and storylines just dropped in favor of an "open ended" ending for a third game. the game in general feels too large and long, and you really start to feel that especially when the polish just isn't there.

again; I LOVE ghostrunner. I was really excited for a sequel! but I'm left incredibly disappointed and not at all looking forward to the next game that is undoubtedly to come. I don't hate this game, I just don't particularly like it either.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo me, there is not a single component of this game that is not an improvement over GR1.

The most noticeable improvement to me is the environments and set pieces. I love GR1 but a lot of the environments blend together; Dharma City, the Cybervoid, and TOM are probably the only distinctions I can make between areas looking back on it. GR2 fixes this wholesale. Environments are varied, unique, and memorable. This also reflects in the boss fights as well.

The story seems to be contentious between players, but I really enjoyed it and I still think it has a lot of those philosophical questions the first one asked: What is it to be alive? to be human? what if you're a copy? what is your purpose? what will you do now? Jack also has a lot more characterization and made him more distinct and, dare I say, just cool.

The soundtrack is just as good as ever but I LOVE the tracks by Dan Terminus and Gost. Discontinued is my absolute favorite.

The new upgrades system still holds some of the complexity of the last game without being a chore to work through, and really enables different play styles which is greatly appreciated. I know a few people have also complained about feeling sluggish early on, but I think this is perfectly fine to be slightly slower at the beginning to make way for the insane speeds you can achieve late in the game.

All in all, GR2 is amazing. If this was the last GR (though I doubt it) I'd be happy with this being the last outing. If it isn't, I eagerly await GR3. 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIMPORTANT DISCLAIMER: Since this review is trending on top, I want to add that this game is actually great for the people who couldn't beat Ghostrunner 1, or the people who found it to be too hard to enjoy. GR2 is a good game for newcommers or casual players. This review is from a perspective of a hardcore-difficulty enjoyer. And while flawed, it is still an good experience overall.

Good Game but a Major Step-Down in Combat from GR1.
Unfortunately they've made a mistake that Doom Eternal as a sequel has avoided. GR2 is too focused on the story instead of gameplay. And as a result pacing and combat become far less interesting compared to the first game.
- Combat arenas are too open with large gaps between enemies.It makes all encounters easy and same-y.
- Arenas do not involve using parkour and environment to your advantage. Parkour is used only to navigate to the next enemy.
- Arenas do not have any advanced level design. GR1 had a lot of great arenas that always challenged their player and required different approach. Like the 5 swordsmen moment. All GR2 arenas have the same approach, kill all enemies and hold block button between the kills.
- The Block button makes game too easy, and less fun a result. It acts like a crutch for a bad play-style. I think they should've kept as a part of easy mode and let hardcore players parry with left mouse button just like before.
- Enemies were replaced with grey, voiceless zombies that are hard to distinguish from a distance. This goes completely against game's formula, enemies should be easy to read, not memorized.

Platforming is fun but flawed
Overall combat is treated poorly with the game doubling down on platforming. Which has also a couple of flaws, but fun and engaging in general.
- The new robot platforms are a terrible design decision. They cannibalize the grappling hook point and often I just die because I can't distinguish them. You can also wall-run on a side of them which will result in an unfair death.
- A lot of of levels do not have proper death zones at the bottom of arenas. As a result I get stuck on geometry and don't get an insta-death to restart the arena.

Outside Sections were a mistake
- All outside sections are lifeless and boring. Replacing colorful charismatic city with green and orange desert was a huge mistake and a blow to the pacing of the game.
- The music choice didn't help as well it just adds to the boring feeling of the location.
- Bike gets stuck on a geometry all the time.
- Replacing charismatic colorful enemies with dull grey zombies that all look the same. It makes combat more punishing because you don't know what type of enemy is in front of you until it shoots.
- 3 of 4 levels you spend outside the Dharma Tower do not improve gameplay and only serve the story.

Final Thoughts
While it provides fun soundtrack, good story and amazing environment yet average level design, the game is too short. It's not designed for replayability like the first game and combat design went from advanced to basic. I am looking forward to more additions to this franchise but I feel like developers either do not understand what made the original fun or they've rushed the sequel and didn't experiment enough.

If you are a hardcore fan of GR1, replay the game on the hard mode and get the sequel on sale. It's fun but a step back from GR1.\t\t\t\t\t
