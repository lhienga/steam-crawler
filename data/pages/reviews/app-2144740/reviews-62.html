\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend Ghostrunner 2, it is a fantastic game with: amazing movement, intuitive game design, a wide range of difficulty in its levels, and fantastic map design.

That being said, I have many issues with Ghostrunner 2, and they are the following:

Ghostrunner 2 just like Ghostrunner is very short in length (it took me 9hrs to complete the main story).

Ghostrunner 2 seems to have taken a leap of faith in many aspects which may be hit or miss to various different players, the developers definitely far exceeded what was needed in order to bring new mechanics to the game, however, this seems to have backfired on them, because as a result the game now feels kind of clunky with many mechanics introduced just to be dropped at later stages in the game, this all makes the game come off as an experiment or a test as opposed to Ghostrunner which felt like a carefully tuned, enjoyable game. Ghostrunner 2 is not by any means bad or not fun, however, there are many aspects to it that just in my opinion don't fit the franchise, for example, the introduction of the motorbike in the game was at first a fun concept that could have played fantastically into one-off chase scenes or obstacle course levels, instead they decided to double down on the importance of the bike by applying an open world aspect to 2 levels in the game. This not only makes the game flow worse with the constant need to get off your bike to defeat enemies and unlock a gate to progress but it also makes the game an incredible pain to try and gather all the collectables. This game is definitely more suited to a linear level design, and the open world aspect should (at least in my opinion) be dropped as Ghostrunner at its core is designed for close-quarters high speed combat as opposed to slow and long distance encounters.

Another feature of Ghostrunner 2 that didn't play out as well as expected would be the wing suit introduced towards the end of the game, it really is only present for the last few missions and then isn't even required in the final boss fight, just like the bike after the few levels where it was present the wing suit as just dropped afterwards and not mentioned before, the wing suit once again just like the bike actually slows the gameplay down, does not allow it to flow well, and allows for a lot of annoying and bug-induced deaths.

The final boss for Ghostrunner 2 is also majorly disappointing, the final boss doesn't feel like a final boss at all, if anything the second last boss that we encounter in the game is by far a bigger challenge and also more fun to fight, as the final boss seems to be spoon-fed to the player, with constant assists to almost ensure that the player passes within a few attempts.

Now, don't get me wrong not all the features added in Ghostrunner 2 are bad, I for one really enjoyed the small breaks between levels where you got to go around the hideout and talk to your team, buy and select your abilities, and the new upgrade menu, however, overall Ghostrunner 2 has definitely bit off more than it could chew, and this is of course not on the developers as they did a fantastic job and managed to implement these features with minimal to no bugs at all, however, unfortunately the game just feels less like Ghostrunner and I fear with the ending of the game, that this may be a recurring theme in future instalments in the game.

I hope that this review is helpful to anyone debating buying this game, I would still highly recommend it as Ghostrunner is really an amazing game and experience, it is just important to keep in mind the shortcomings of this instalment when coming directly from the previous one, I hope that any future instalments manage to remove or atleast incorporate these new features in a way that allows for smoother and better gameplay.

The game is good, but not worth $60 (AUD)
gameplay wise this game feels the same as its predecessor, however i would even say the movement feels cleaner and faster which is great.
There is some performance issues, my PC specs are roughly equivalent to the recommended however i cant run the game at anything above minimum settings without having frame by frame stutters, perhaps its my hardware issue i wasn't bothered to look into it but i know others are having stutter issues.
The story is mediocre at best, but still a good sequel, only 7.4 hours to finish the main story however which was...disappointing - however i do plan on doing 100% achievements.
dialogue is not as good as ghost runner 1, nor is the the Upgrade UI
soundtrack is solid and there are some interesting new challenges within the story and enemy types. The parrying system is Terrible (although i just played sekiro which has the best combat system and parrying in gaming history so i shouldn't be surprised).
The boss fights are cool but kinda rng based, i do prefer the parkour type boss fights.
game lacks polish and seems rushed almost, but again its still solid. just not worth the full asking price.
7/10\t\t\t\t\t
