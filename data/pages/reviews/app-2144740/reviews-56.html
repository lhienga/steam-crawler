The game-play is a good extension of the first game with an increased difficulty to emphasize the fact that your fighting new enemies and for a different reason. But, the one thing I did notice and wish they would bring back is slide canceling from the first game. Then again, this game seems more grounded in reality compared to the first one. So, overall this is an amazing sequel that I will speed run for a long time!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed the game. If you enjoyed the first iteration you will enjoy this one it's as simple as that.
Things I enjoyed most:
- The "boss fight" where you ride the bike.
- The story.
- Collecting all the things.
- The puzzles.

My complaints:
- The restart mechanic is suited for linear progression but there are some open levels where it can be disorienting.
- The bike is not bad but not great either. I was getting stuck on bumps in the road and various objects I should have merely bumped into or flown over.
- I found it to be more frustrating than the first iteration. There were a lot more "WTF" moments. By the last 3 hours I couldn't wait until it was over.
- The character faces have very much detail and very little expression leaving the dialogue without much emotion.
- I didn't enjoy RogueRunner. Just because everyone else is doing it doesn't mean you have to.

I was hoping to find some sort of replay feature in this game. Seeing as it takes so much effort to do it just right it would be even more rewarding to be able to watch the successful run from start to finish without the countless failures.

Keep up the good work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhost Runner 1 was great, Ghost Runner 2 is good. I had a ton of fun.

If you only read this sentence, you should know that GR2 is worth your money if you liked GR1. It falls behind it's predecessor in some aspects, succeeds it in others. The game steps out of its comfort zone often and to varying success;
and in doing so justifies this game being a sequel instead of just a GR1 dlc.

Story:
I enjoyed the story. GR1 ended at a point where there wasn't any obvious direction for the story to naturally progress, which can lead to some shoe-horned plot in games. The studio did well in creating a believable-in-universe kickoff and plot for the story. The end (last 2 missions) did feel rushed narrative-wise.

Aesthetic:
Its Ghost Runner, it kicks ass.

Platforming:
Solid like the last game, much of what made that system work is here. GR2 also improves on the cyber-void environments as well, making them a tangible part of the story and missions - where in GR1 cyber-void was mostly just for annoying puzzles and tutorials for new powers.

Arenas:
GR2 falls short of its predecessor here, arguably the most important part of the game. Its not bad, but the combat of this game is much easier than before, and that creates a situation where you're flying through every fight. What made GR1 great was taking several minutes re-trying each room to find a good route to navigate the terrain and enemies. You're not going to find that here.

As far as the design of the arena's themselves, I couldn't tell you if they're any better or worse, because you simply don't see any of them more than once or twice. The game does not force or provide incentive for you to stick around anywhere.

Combat:
Combat still has the same bells and whistles of GR1. The problem is that with all these fancy toys, there's no need to ever use any of them. Game is simply too easy. You unlock 4 (maybe 5?) ultimates throughout the game, I don't know what any of them do because I didn't feel the need to use any of them a single time.

Bosses:
Just like in GR1, the best boss in GR2 is the environmental boss (it absolutely rocked). The other on-foot non-platforming bosses fall flat. The final boss was perhaps the worst boss in the series, sadly.

Enemies:
All the enemies that make an appearance from the previous title are great. New enemies are a mixed bag.

Hub:
There is a hub between levels where you can talk to the characters. I personally enjoyed all the dialogue the characters had to offer, and I think they were all well-written. Frankly though - I'm in the minority. Odds are you are going to think this is boring.

Upgrades:
The upgrade system is a downgrade. In GR1 we had this tetris-like game of its own to slot in our upgrades, which was fun. In GR2 we just have squares to drag our upgrades into. Some of the upgrades provide extra power for how you position, but the extra power is wholly unneeded. Ultimately we're dragging squares into squares with little to no care or thought required.

Conclusion:
I know, if you've read this far, you must be thinking that everything this game has to offer is inferior to the original. That is not the case. The game is still tons of fun, and the devs should be commended for trying new things. The first bike mission absolutely blew me away. Without the misses, you don't get the hits.

GR2 proves that a GR game can offer more than GR1's platforming and combat puzzles. Lets just hope that GR3 polishes up that core gameplay, makes you earn your progress, and keeps thinking outside the box.\t\t\t\t\t
