\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game, I really do. It's got solid mechanics, an alright soundtrack, and looks gorgeous, so what's wrong? Flow. Compared to it's predecessor, Ghostrunner 2 lacks the same flow that Ghostrunner had. In an effort to flesh out the story and world the developers chose to add a small hub area you return to between missions, chat with NPCs, upgrade, etc. This completely kills the flow that the former game had from level to level. I'm not sure the game really needed this, story is nice to have, but when the original game's story was painfully generic while also being kind of out of the way, it was tolerable. Now when they force you to engage with the story when it didn't really improve all that much and kills the flow of the gameplay, it just doesn't do it for me.

I also find myself getting lost more in this game, in the previous one where you were meant to go was very obvious, illuminated yellow was a good indication of where to go, but while they do try to stick to that formula here, it falls flat because of lighting in many areas not drawing enough attention to it. In cyberpunk games like these, you expect to see splotches of dull color off in the distance or in the corner of your eye, when it's illuminated properly, it attracts attention. They don't always follow that formula here. It's particularly bad in areas that are very bright and colorful as a baseline so navigation becomes confusing as the somewhat more dull yellow banners you're meant to be looking for are lost in the sea of bright neon colors.

Finally, I don't find the soundtrack to be the same kind of special the previous game had. It doesn't have the same catchiness to make me want to listen to it outside of the game that the original does.

Overall, I'm disappointed. I really enjoyed the first game, was hoping they would keep the feeling of flow from one level to the next, and the hub sections just kinda killed it for me. The gameplay is solid as before, so if you can make it past the giant brick walls you slam your face in to after every level, you'll probably like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have not played the first game:
This is a fast paced game with a series of linear levels filled with enemies that all go down in one hit, but also one tap you, and parkour challenges. Some are more focused on the parkour, some more on the enemies. With cyberpunk architecture designed to be almost terrifyingly massive, and a story that picks at the very basic question of the humanity of a machine. The story isn't fantastic, but it is a serviceable vessel for the stellar gameplay loop.

For those who have played the first game:
This one is worse. But not by much, and only for specific reasons. The visuals and visual spectacle especially are better, with much better looking cybervoid sections and many more varied regular world environments. But there are some problems. It's buggy! I fell through the map and glitched into walls and even some enemies got stuck in walls. The noncommittal nature of the frequent auto-saves means these glitches didn't amount to much, but they were incredibly annoying, especially in a game that is prized for its fluid and fast movement. The enemy types and enemy variety is mostly just the same as the first game, but I will admit, enemy design is not important in this game to begin with. The game also falls off a bit with some of its new mechanics. A lot of them are cool and fun, but some feel unfinished and others simply break the fluidity of movement (floating platforms I'm talking to you). You now have a stamina bar, as well as a dedicated block, and parrying is now just a perfectly timed block. Also, weirdly, every controller setup is the same as the first game, except bumper jumper alt is missing? Though you can fully and freely rebind every button-bind unlike the first game, so that's nice.\t\t\t\t\t
