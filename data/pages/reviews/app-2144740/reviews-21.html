\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh yeah baby, we are back. I loved Ghostrunner 1, and enjoyed it quite a bit even with it's flaws. Ghostrunner 2 doubles down on the cool factor, as well as the complexity, and you are left with a game with a lot more going on this time around. It has a few issues here and there, but I feel they are minor when compared to the sheer amount of love that was put into making this game feel as awesome as it does.

PROS:
- A bunch of new features added: They added blocking, side abilities, and new ultimates, and with a lot more versatility at that. Where Ghostrunner 1 was a lot more simplistic in nature, Ghostrunner 2 gives you a lot of freedom to try your own things. It's pretty rad to be honest. The later half of the game adds a bunch of really neat stuff that I absolutely was not expecting to be as cool as it is.
- Less puzzles, more hackin and slashin action: One thing I didn't like in Ghostrunner 1 is that some of the puzzles forced you to be at a standstill and involved standing around instead of harnessing the cool factor. I think Ghostrunner 2 made the non-combative areas feel more meaningful and fun, instead of just solving a simplified mobile game puzzle.
- You just feel cool: How do you explain this? Everything in this game makes you feel like an action movie star. Once you get the hang of it, you can do some crazy stuff which is the things of dreams. It's tough, but after getting through a difficult part after a few deaths is so satisfying.

NEUTRAL: Where Ghostrunner 1 was more of an arcade style consistent level to level format, Ghostrunner 2 took more of a story based aspect, giving you time to talk to other characters and really flesh them out. They feel a lot more human in this game, whereas in Ghostrunner 1 you just feel like they are just background noise. It's a nice change of pace, but perhaps not for everyone. It's nice that you can choose to talk to characters, but also can just go straight to the next mission.

CONS:
- PC Optimization. This is the biggest flaw in Ghostrunner 2, the optimization is not the best. There are some times when the game is running at a super crispy 100fps for me, but then there are other times when it drops to 30 frames (especially anything outside, especially on the zipline areas (minor spoiler), even when I turn the settings way down. I like to think I have a decent PC that can play most games unhindered, but this made me feel like I need a NASA supercomputer to play it without it dipping past 60fps.
- Bugs: Throughout the game I encountered a few annoying bugs, and in all it is not as perfect as it could be. Movement can come to an abrupt stop, hit detection can be a bit wonky, and sometimes it isn't clear if something is supposed to happen, or if it happened by chance. There was one time that I fell through the floor into a random empty space and could do nothing until I restarted from the last checkpoint. I'm not going to knock it too much, seeing as it is the first month of the game, and that it is more of an indie game, and not some massive triple A Rockstar Games title.
- Boss Battles: This one is minor, but the bosses really seem to spike the game's difficulty. The game goes from being hard but fair, to being bullet hell territory real quick. The final boss battle is probably the best example of this. It is WAY better than the boss battles in the first game though, which I felt were very much lacking in retrospect, but I still feel is not as fun as the normal combat.


IN CONCLUSION: If you liked Neon White, Mirror's Edge, Doom Eternal, or any parkour based games out there, you will love this game no doubt. The flexibility, creativeness, and atmosphere are so awesome in this game, and there is very few games out there that will be able to make you feel the same way. Even with its flaws it still remains my favorite recent playthrough, and I'm excited to see the new game mode coming out later this year.

8.5/10, such a badass experience. Just needs some touching up is all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMixed feelings about this game. I love the first Ghostrunner game, I don't believe I can translate my feelings for it in words. As excited as I was for the release of this game, It's definitely lacking in some aspects. One of the things that stood out to me during my play-through was how the game is divided. There's the half without the bike, and the half with the bike. The first half of the game flows smoothly, it's the similar linear story to the first game, just more intricate. However, once the bike is unlocked, the direction takes a weird turn. The game becomes open-world, and while this is a neat addition, it's not really executed well. I can name countless times where I had to forcefully restart from a checkpoint after being softlocked, and often because there was no direction on where to go. The game is also rather choppy, but once again, divided between the two halves of the game. The first half of the game runs perfectly fine, 120+ fps on max settings (DirectX12), but once you reach the open world area of the game you can expect your frames to be at least half that. Note that although I'm not rocking the latest specs, I have a 3060ti with a AMD ryzen 5 5600. And having levels where the frames sit consistently below 30fps is not the best. Various rooms of the game were practically unplayable, and even turning down settings did not fix. All that aside, I still absolutely loved this game. The bike levels feel so satisfying when done right, and the actual movement of the game is smooth. I'd still recommend people to play this game, even if they haven't played the first. But definitely not as much as I did for the first.

TLDR: New gameplay mechanics are fun when they work, albeit choppy in the latter half. Not exactly on par with the first, but overall a great game nonetheless and still had tons of fun playing.\t\t\t\t\t
