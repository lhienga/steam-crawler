The devs did the impossible - they made a sequel that totally surprises you in how much it was able to one-up the previous game. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood Game but i didn't love everything new.

Good:
- Addicting Gameplay
- Mostly Great Mission Design
- Awesome Soundtrack

Bad:
- The "Pillars of Creation" Mission felt like an Open World Chore that dragged on for too long
- Some Bugs led to some Deaths due to getting stuck on Walls or Rails not working as expected
- The "Story Hub" and interactions with other Characters felt like unnecessary padding. I prefer the story to happen during Gameplay since that is where the game shines.

Conclusion:
I like this game a lot and would recommend it, BUT I'd recommend you to play Ghostrunner 1 first because it feels like a more focused and more technically polished Game (and i enjoyed the story more).

If you played the first one and crave more Ghostrunner like i did, get it!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 is a fantastic sequel to a near-perfect original. Stepping back in to Ghostrunner was as easy as riding a bike, with several meaningful QoL updates to the base formula. The only real complaint I can make about Ghostrunner 2 is that it's a sequel.

Pretty much every minor complaint about the first game has been touched on. Levels are bigger, there are more bosses(and what bosses exist are more involved and fun), the cybervoid sections give you full movement, and ability options, there's more traversal options(including a cool future-bike. And yes, you do get to do an Akira style slide moment from first-person when beating a boss while riding it).

What's most important to remember is that Ghostrunner 2 is not DOOM or Shadow Warrior. This is a puzzle game like Hotline Miami where the solutions involve violence. Absolutely worth the price of admission, especially if you were a fan of the first one.

Also, the game ran perfectly OOTB on Linux for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome other reviewers go as far as not recommending the game for a reason that the game focuses more on the story than fighting arenas and other game mechanics. While this might be the case I do not feel it should result in not recommending the game as the game is amazing, just bit different than the original. It is very apparent that the devs want more open less linear experience and I feel like they nailed the balance between limited resources for an indie game and big plans and ideas. It is still very linear experience (~which is not a bad thing~), but inclusion of HQ and more open big locations with multiple tasks that can be done in any order makes it feel like a more living actual world. This is something that I missed in the original game, where the game aesthetically was amazing and as someone who loves cyberpunk I missed not being able to interact with the world more. This certainly helps. When I first entered HQ I sat in the office for few minutes just looking out of the windows at the world around.\t\t\t\t\t
