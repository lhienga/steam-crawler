\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Better than Ghostrunner 1. If you can put aside how bad this game runs in a select few areas, it is genuinely one of the best games I have played this year and is my contender for game of the year.

Jack is back for the second time in Ghostrunner 2.

The Good:

- The combat is very satisfying and fluid

- The movement is very satisfying and fluid

- Great OST

- Voice acting and cutscenes are good and the returning characters voice actors have returned

- The new ultimate abilities are fun to use

- The new enemies are fun to fight against and enemies from the original return

- The new motorcycle, while jank at times, is pretty fun to use

- While the story is admittedly nothing special, it is good

- The upgrade system is miles better then the original "Tetris" system

- Trying to beat your best time on a level is really fun and rewarding and arguably is what makes this game special

- Skippable cutscenes for repeat playthroughs and speedrunners

The Meh:

- There is a new "hub" world which was not present in the original where you can talk to your friends and get to know them a bit better. I personally didn't really like this and would only talk to them about the bare minimum needed to progress the story, but some players might like this. One More Games have allowed you to skip through dialogue if you don't care for it, which I appreciate

- The motorcycle can occasionally get stuck on random geometry, forcing a checkpoint reload or death. I had this happen a few times in my playthrough

- The ending wasn't bad, but it was kind of anticlimactic. The game should have ended after finishing the final level, and end with a cutscene of Jack telling everyone he was leaving instead of making the player talk to them

- The In Game Timer is not 100% accurate and can be off by a few seconds

- For those playing on controller, controller support for the original Ghostrunner was much better than in GR2. This will hopefully be addressed in an update

- The wingsuit in the level Too Close to the Sun should have its own dedicated bind in the controls menu


The Bad:

This game needs a performance patch.

MY RIG:

Windows 10
RTX 3070
I7-13700KF
16GB Ram
Loaded on an M.2 SSD


I'm not talking about stutters, though I will mention them briefly. I want to be very clear here, this is NOT shader compilation stutters, I know how Unreal Engine works. The demo DID contain shader comp stutters, but this seems to have been addressed in full release and it now compiles shaders on boot.

Unreal Engine 4 (Ghostrunner 2's Engine) has features built into that allows developers to start "preloading" areas in non gameplay intensive scenes before you reach them to help prevent stutters. It seems like this feature was used poorly or not at all in some levels as this game stutters for a brief moment when it attempts to load almost any new areas in a level.

This isn't that bad but in some levels it will happen multiple times, such as in Licking the Wounds (The demo level) in which it will happen 4 times in the same spot every time regardless of how many times you've played in a session. Certain levels such as Will Bushido Allow It?, I Won't Be Back Today , and The Monolith of Inhumanity don't contain any noticible stutters. I suspect the developers started running out of time to add the nessessary triggers and could only do it in some levels and this will be fixed in a later update, hopefully.

But onto the actual problem:

Some areas just have so many effects happening on the screen that not only will your FPS tank, the audio will become all garbled and will start to hurt your ears. I want to clarify and say most levels run fine, but some levels are really bad.

I played my playthrough on "High" graphics at 1080p on the DX11 version. Lowered down into Low and Medium to see if it was better, and it was, but I still had some issues.

This was especially noticeable on the following levels and areas:

A Price to be Paid, in the "Hostiles" boss fight. FPS will tank into the low 50s and maybe 40s here. When the lasers spawn, audio will become garbled due to so many effects.
Winds of the Desolate, in the open world sections
Pillars of Creation, in the open world sections
Can Anyone Hear Me?, in the outside biking section. This was one of the least bad, though.
Danse Macabre, when the boss uses his healing move. Audio can become garbled here
Elevator Maintenance, in the big room fight towards the middle of the level. FPS can dip down to the low 40s here on my hardware.
Too Close to the Sun, in the arena close to the end of the level. I get stutters between 30 - 100 FPS here on my hardware, even when most of the enemies are dead. I suspect its trying to load something in the middle of the fight.

Minor performance issues:

Shadow Quality on Low seems to break some characters faces, and they'll look really funny (heard someone say they look straight out of an episode of The Simpsons lol) so use Medium and not Low. I'm pretty sure this an Unreal issue and the developers can't fix it.\t\t\t\t\t

Early Access Review
Ghostrunner 2 lives up to the hype. It improves on every aspect of the first title; the combat is even better with the addition of the ability to block and perfect-parry and a bike that translates beautifully into the combat and more. The story took me 7,5h, which is about the same time the first game took me, but the satisfying combat adds to the replayability of the game + there is an additional roguelike gamemode I've yet to try out. A Hardcore mode that the first game had is not there (yet). The story is again not a masterpiece but keeps you engaged, at no moment did I ever feel bored while playing the game. The only bug I've encounetered was a minor visual error in the credits.
With nothing other to complain about, this is a clear 10/10 for me and high up on the list of my personal best games of 2023 in a year that had an exceptionally high amount of great game releases. A must play for movement FPS lovers.\t\t\t\t\t
