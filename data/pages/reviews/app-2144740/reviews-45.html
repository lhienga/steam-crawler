\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Bigger and bolder, but..."

The announcement of Ghostrunner 2 was just a matter of time, as the first part was surprisingly good game.
Obviously the sequel should have been bigger and bolder in every aspect of the game.
And it actually is!

I truly enjoyed almost every level of Ghostrunner 2, I like the music, I like the motorcycle levels on insane speed.
This game brings very satisfying challenge.
It's fast, gory and very stylish.
Just what I love.
Nothing much to add.
Good stuff.

But.
There are some serious "buts" as well.
What do you expect from game like Ghostrunner? Maybe more challenge? New mechanics? New weapons and the way to interact with enemies and world?
No, guys, here you are a hub, where you can JUST walk around, and talk to people. Almost every AFTER level.
Ruins the flow pretty much, feels very unnecessary.

But it's nothing comparing to last level before last bossfight.
This level almost ruined all impression of the game.
This level is HUGE. And it felt like something unfinished, something SO BROKEN, or something that was made by someone who have NO CLUE what he is doing.
Here you get new mechanic, that you use only this level a couple of times.
This level is complicated and unfairly hard.
Beating it feels like bug-abusing.


One more time - Ghostrunner 2 is a good game.
Cutting off penultimate level and unnecessary dialigues - and the game would be 9/10.

But minding everything you going through this game gets it's absolutely fair:

7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasily one of the most enjoyable games I've ever played...and I haven't played much of it yet, because I know it's not an intensely long game, but it's a speedrunner's wet dream, and I love the challenge. It's so fast paced, so when you do complete a level, you'll feel accomplished. Wallriding, sliding, grappling, hacking and slashing, and deflecting projectiles with your giant katana, feeling like an absolute badass by vanquishing everyone who has 1hp...but you also have 1hp. Get hit once, and you die. But...yeah....I feel like I'm going to actually submit results to official speedrun site for this game. Just, once I get better at it. I read reviews of people who died like 255 times during a single level when they first started out. It's not that difficult, especially if you've played the absolute crap out of any fps that has wall riding capabilities, or you played surf in counter-strike, or bhop maps in cs.

This is just more fun...in short bursts. I mean, it's very fast paced, so it will just drain your epinephrine from your body until you have to stop. At my age, that happens quickly. But...the game just makes you feel like a god when your movement is perfect. Just...so satisfying. Worth buying and keeping in your library for when you need to vent out some frustration. I mean, you could potentially get frustrated playing the levels, which I did once (one guy killed me with some sort of phaser rifle and then I got the tutorial tip of "press shift whilst in mid air to dodge"), so I got mad that it told me I could dodge after I died.

Still though....really good fun. If you liked the latest versions of Doom, you'll like this.\t\t\t\t\t

Normally this would be a good thing, but the tight, tactical, methodical, and surgical approach to the game feels lost.
