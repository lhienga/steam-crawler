\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really, really, REALLY wanted to like this game. Ghostrunner 1, despite its flaws, is one of my all time favourite games and this game seemed to be improving upon it. More enemies? Motorcycle sections? New abilities? Going outside of the Tower? Sign me up. I was rooting for this game, especially since I was underwhelmed by most of the 2023 releases I had played.

But... this just isn't it. General movement feels worse, there's a lot less momentum and certain actions just feel worse than the first game, like the shuriken and the grapple. Ghostrunner 1 felt super fluid and I got a hold of the game's feel very quickly. This just feels like they changed it for no reason.

Level design is significantly worse. It's just too easy. I don't understand what goes through people's heads to make sequels easier but then have the story rely upon you knowing the first game. Pick a route, either make a game for older players or don't and make a prequel.

Mechanics are overall worse too, some enemies are just completely annoying and the motorcycle sections in particular just play awfully. I get that trial and error is a key part of what makes these games work but foresight is also a big part in that. You have to see what you did wrong as a player and hoe you could improve. There's no improving to suddenly slamming right into a wall because you can't look up.

It's not technically a bad game, but it's a bad sequel for sure. I'm not finishing the game. Hoping that if they make another one, they rectify the series.\t\t\t\t\t
