\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhost Runner 2 is the prime example for why it is important to be aware of ‘less is more’. Game Developers creating sequels usually fall for the same trap of quantity over quality. It’s no surprise that once the baseline of the game is created, the developer would then expand vastly different mechanics into the game. This decision design, however, ended becoming more detrimental to the core fundamentals of the game, and instead, alienates what made the original GR1 great.

I don’t think I will be finishing the game for this review. In the future I might revisit, and it is a possibility that I would change my mind, but upon other users’ sentiments, the consensus appears to the same. The flow of the game is stalled through more than many cringe cut scenes, lore, chat bubble world hub, item pickups, and mixed level designs.

The interesting narrative for GR1 is left soulless in GR2. Many conversations during within the game do not feel engaging in anyway, and also clutters the screen.

The level design is hit or miss. There are parts of the game where there was absolutely no music playing. There appears to be more invisible walls and lack of exploration outside bounds. I also absolutely hate the amount of yellow coated paint to indicate where the player should go… I blame Resident Evil 4 Remake for my hatred. Transition from level to level has taken a hit with the world hub.

There’s more I could go on about, but with only few hours on this game, it’s whatever. I always have GR1 to fall back to.

I can’t wait for GR3 RPG UBISOFT EDITION.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the only game I have preordered in my life, and I'm glad I did, because it is amazing. Ghostrunner 2 improves on just about every issue that the first game suffered from. The combat flows extremely well and the new ultimate abilities are amazing. New sword inspect animations are incredible, and the boss system is much better.

In the previous game, boss fights were just about dodging or parrying the attacks and then slashing them when they're vulnerable. GR2 doesn't do that, and instead opts for a proper damage system, constantly striking your opponent while dodging their attacks. The levels flow very well, and the perfect parry takedowns are a little janky but still pretty cool. There's also a sort of home base that you can explore and interact with in between missions, talking to characters in the game, upgrading your skills, ect.

There's a bunch of new skills to experiment with and have fun with. The game looks graphically amazing as well. The cutscenes feel much better compared to GR1, although the facial expressions of some characters in game feel kind of lifeless and they just stare into your soul while talking to you, but other than that they're great.

And there's also the bike levels and the levels are kind of more open rather than directed. I won't spoil anything but I will say that the bike is a blast to use and is probably the most fun I've had in a game for a long time.

Yes, the game is difficult, just like the first one. There is no denying that. One hit and you restart at the last checkpoint. (there's alot of those thankfully) But if you can get the flow and movement down and learn to use your abilities in unison, Ghostrunner 2 is so much fun, and considering that most AAA games are $80+ these days, this game is definitely worth the price\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good, combat is way more enjoyable than the first game. Movement feels pretty good, especially the bike and other other movement option you unlock later in the game. The "open world" aspect of some of the biking section was interesting, but I am not sure how to feel about it just yet. The new upgrade system I feel was a good change of pace compared to the original, but I feel some abilities and ultimates greatly outshine the rest. The worst part about the game was having to return to the hub area and interact with the NPCs. It completely broke the flow of the game, and really dampened the ending of the game, since the final boss fight was followed by clunky dialogue with the other characters. Overall a very enjoyable experience, and I will be replaying it a few times to explore everything and get all the collectibles. 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
