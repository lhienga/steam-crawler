\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I saw the announcement for the sequel I was really excited. I really liked the first game, even though it was rough around the edges, but it was amazing. The edges though, you couldn't cut yourself with those. Where as the this entry, the edges are so rough that they will rip the skin right off.

The performance is not great. I get constant stuttering whenever traversing from arena to arena. Frames drop from 170 down to 16 after the slo-mo "executions" and will not go away until I change something in the video settings. That is DURING combat sequence, which just kills it completely. Audio in some sections cuts in and out, like someone is pulling out and plugging back in my headphones. I like to play on FOV between 95-105 and usually it's fine, but this game changes the perspective terribly. Not only the 90 FOV feels like 70, but when I change it to 100 everything looks like it's a good distance but in reality it isn't even close. I found myself swinging way too soon, missing the attacks.

The gameplay is not great either. There are a lot of bugs that kill your momentum, in a game about being a cyborg ninja, where the movement supposed to be great. The FOV makes you feel like you're a tank, either seeing normally but feel slow or see very narrow and feel slightly faster. Level design is not great either. Traversing doesn't feel smooth and finding the next path forward is not easy. I often just stand and look around to try and find a way.

There are a lot of great ideas in the game but none of them are executed well. Needs more time in the oven for polish LOTS of stuff up\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA disappointing sequel to one of the greatest Hotline Miami successors out there.

Ghostrunner 2 aimed to go bigger and jump higher, that much is clear. What's missing, however, is the initial sharpness of the formula that made the original click in the first place. It was tight and snappy, calibrated to strike right at the target, one adrenaline shot after the other.

Now, with vast levels, complicated enemy patterns, dozens of situational skills, perfunctory puzzles, and story beats that break the momentum, it's as if Ghostrunner no longer wants to be Ghostrunner. It strives to be many things at once, from Turbo Overkill and DOOM Eternal to even Half-Life 2 at times — which are all great comparisons really, just not for this game. A lot of new ideas simply don't go well with the core "live fast, act now, die, repeat" concept, eventually diluting it so much it gets annoying. And when replaying a fight feels like going through motions, you know something is very, very wrong.

There are some redeeming qualities, sure. The overall presentation is pretty solid (apart from some wonky-looking NPCs), fundamental combat and traversal mechanics can be fun in the more concise encounters, and the bike is the single best addition to the mix. So good, in fact, that it managed to make the worst level in the game somewhat enjoyable. Yeah, keep the bike, it's cool.

Still, I can't remember the last time a short adventure like that was such a chore. Ghostrunner 2 did scratch that very specific cybersamurai itch for a bit, but if I knew what it had in store for me, I would have replayed the first game instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've never played Ghostrunner 1, play that one! It's a great time, doesn't overstay its welcome, and the complexity of the combat encounters challenge you for the entire runtime.

Ghostrunner 2 is, like, a sequel that I think learned all the wrong lessons from the first game. It has way more narrative moments, they're delivered worse, and the first game being occasionally interrupted by boring puzzles seems quaint in comparison to a game that is frequently interrupted by a mixture of puzzles, forced narrative, and scripted sequences. There's, like, a social hub? Like there's a Normandy / Majula / home base area you go back to between missions where you need to talk to NPCs and have to walk up to a terminal to upgrade your abilities because ???

There's ambition here that is worth credit but this is, like, not the game I want that stuff in? The first game was a really great balance of Titanfall-like platforming and Meat Boy-esque precise execution and the opening hours have so little of the latter and so much of the former that I kinda have no interest in seeing this one through. You get to the good stuff in GR1 and it kinda doesn't let up until the game's over and even a few hours in, I never felt like GR2 had picked up steam yet.

Oh well!\t\t\t\t\t
