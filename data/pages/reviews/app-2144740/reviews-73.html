\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly this is a good game. very good one, but even then i would be inclined to give a mixed rating if i could. Just because first one did everything better.

The best part? The game runs damn well, it's a new release that can easily go 200+ FPS on high end rig. No stutter either.

Bike is cool as hell, but actual gameplay segments are just okay, and bike itself is janky. I probably got stuck on random terrain elements while driving more than actually dying in those missions!

Adding block is unnecessary and clutters the controls.
Removing counter from melee attack is also a bad decision honestly, same thing with automatic melee finisher on parry.

But the worst thing is the pacing. Adding intermissions between actual missions just doesn't fit the style of this game, and frankly - character animations in those sections are mediocre, so are their models. Even cybervoid got downgraded visually - not in actual graphical fidelity, but in terms of art style.

It kinda feels like first one was a passion project with tight controls and well designed sections. Second one feels like they threw ideas that sounded cool and then it got took over by corporate commitee - which had no idea what made the first game so good.

Bossfights are a downgrade - they are your typical hp sponges with non-demanding mechanics, that actually don't interact with most options given to player, whereas in first one they were basically puzzles to solve - just like all other levels.

On the other hand i like what they've done to powers, and splitting them between normal ones and ultimate was a good choice. Using them in exploration is nice, but it is too rare, and usually focused only on one of them at once.
You have great mechanics - try to stack them over time, pushing the players a bit.

Level design took a hit in some parts, feels less 'organic' than the first one, and more game'y.

Music is also more generic. It fits the theme, but it isn't as coordinated nor tied to the levels themselves as in the first one. There are some good tracks, but nothing reaches highs of the first one.

Despite all that it is still a solid game, it's just a downgrade over first one.

EDIT: Finished the game.. and i went back to first one to finish Hardcore levels. IT wasn't noticable without direct comparison but first game is VASTLY faster in movement and pace.\t\t\t\t\t

And what the result? Unexpectedly mediocre.

The pros are all the same as it were in the first game but with some bad additions:
- Some level in the tower have zero personalty and design. It's just some dark tunnels. The cyber space as bland as it can be.
- Some of the levels have little to NO MUSIC, LIKE AT ALL (just some weak ambient which hard to hear at all).
- Outside area is incredibly boring. It's just generic post-apoc with ZERO interesting explorations and battle levels. Feels like an early-mid PS3 stuff.
- Hub area was added just to annoy players by constantly ruining the pacing of gameplay. Who needs bethesda-like dialogues in their Hotline miami-like games???

And yes, this is only like a 7-10 hours of gameplay with more then half of it being very mediocre.

I will recommend the first one over this day and night. For this one just play the free demo (basically the best level in the game which was very misleading).
