\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA bit slow and tedious in many areas compared to the first game, with fewer/simpler combat puzzles, and far fewer examples of the game mixing enemies into the linear traversal sections between arenas, from what I can remember. The pacing is especially thrown off in the exterior bike level where you're just riding through barren, post-apocalyptic hellscape for 30 minutes, but almost all the levels reflect this change to greater or lesser degrees. Compare that to the first game where each level is a tightly honed string of set-piece arenas connected with some light platforming, basic puzzles, and parkour combat.

Hopefully the devs can release a new mode, as they did with the prior game, that remixes the encounters in each level to provide a more intense experience that aligns closer to what they already achieved.

I also don't understand why they felt the need to change the skill and upgrade system. I thought it was perfectly elegant the way it was, where you had to balance your upgrades spatially with the tetris grid and also your upgrade complexity vs. energy regen for your skills. The rows and columns system of this game just seem like an unnecessary over-complication. Why does blink require so many button presses now? I guess the capability to precisely select enemies to target over a wide area is interesting, but it felt more intuitive when you just took out everything in a straight line in front of you in two keystrokes.

I will say that there's a ton of variety in the mechanics and enemy units that you encounter, so that will mix things up when there IS something to do. That is, when you're not autopiloting through the long stretches of empty environments listening to VO.

Plus, why did they feel the need to add a dialogue system to Ghostrunner? Truly baffling.

Some significant performance issues, but what do you expect. Modern gaming, right? The days of getting a working product on launch day are long gone.

There's still no other game quite like it, if you like the first, it's still worth checking out. Hopefully in the future it can be made better by adding more enemy encounters and hazards throughout the levels, Ã  la Hardcore mode from 1, because the game has good bones.\t\t\t\t\t
