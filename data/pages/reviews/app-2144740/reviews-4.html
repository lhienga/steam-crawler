I LOVE THIS GAME SO MUCH!!!!!
This is quite frankly one of the best games I've ever played. I know that's saying a lot, but I mean it. This is a perfect sequel for already an amazing game.

Pros:

+ Minor improvements to gameplay from the first game. They did not ruin the experience with new stuff. The gameplay is seriously amazing!!!
+ The upgrade overhaul is a welcome feature
+ An amazing soundtrack from some of my favourite synthwave artists
+ Intriguing story. Not filled with generic clichés.
+ New gameplay elements such as new collectibles
+ The boss battles are amazing, and it's so rewarding to beat them
+ Amazing graphics
+ Aesthetically pleasing level design. The artists really did some amazing work with this game.
+ No microtransactions or battle passes or any of that ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥

There are so many things I could add to the list of things I like about this game. I hope they release a story DLC to this like they did with the first Ghostrunner.

Cons:

- Custom keybindings don't save after one session. You have to bind them again after relaunching the game. I hope this gets fixed soon
- Cannot upgrade the motorcycle
- The story is quite short, but not too short

This is overall just all around 10/10 single player gaming experience. I cannot recommend this enough!! ^^ Such an underrated gem of a game, seriously.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 is a very, very good sequel to the first game. It has the same lovely platforming, movement, setpieces, combat encounters, and cool swords, just like the one before it. A few new abilities, new enemies, and great new music. A motorcycle, a huge open level to explore at your leisure. There's tons to do with the achievements, and the newly added Hardcore mode and Roguerunner side mode for some extra skins (for free!) is such a lovely little cherry on top.

The highlight of this game is Jack. His voice comes through with so much more emotion than the first game. His voice actor knocked it way out of the park, far surpassing his role in the first game. It's honestly been half the reason I've loved the story, is Jack's voice actor. If anything, that might be the greatest upgrade to this sequel, second to everything else. In short, it's just more of what made the first game good, impossible to dislike it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnjoying your fast-paced, adrenaline pumping rhythmic game? Time to stop and go back to a hub that could have easily been a menu. The developers added dialogue options, but the only options are to listen to more boring and cringe dialogue — and the order you hear it in; or just leave. I opted to leave. All the dialogue could have been trimmed down and fed to you as radio communications instead.

This really leaves me puzzled, they took all the best elements of Ghostrunner, and slapped arbitrary elements on top of it. Open world segments (boring), stamina-based blocking, time trials, a tacked-on rogue-lite game, ultimate abilities... I'm shocked they left out romance options.

I'll give the devs props on the atmosphere and the music, but this game leaves me nostalgic for the first game with levels that are tightly designed, adrenaline pumping missions seamlessly leading into another with no downtime at all. Ghostrunner 1 felt like I was dancing a deadly ballet across a chessboard; Ghostrunner 2 feels like dancing the Macarena, fun for a minute, but you wont get 5 hours of enjoyment out of it.

I'll be cautiously optimistic for Ghostrunner 3.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great sequel with some few design issues that hold the game back.

I'm not a big fan of the hub area. It slows down the action and the face for the characters aren't the best. It's used to move the story along and set up missions but that can be done in the world of the game.

The bike game play was fun but most of it was used to get to a new area and to just get on foot. I wish there were more moments like when you first leave the tower but those are few and far in between.

The controls are fine for basic stuff but when you add in other tools then you are setting yourself up for arthritis. The bike controls don't really work with mouse and keyboard either. I had to switch to controller to make it work.

Other than that, great action, great bosses, great cyberpunk world, and great upgrades.

I need a 3rd one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally the only thing you need to know is that it's more Ghostrunner. What's not to like? The mechanics are remixed a little bit, but the idea is still the same. As such: the gameplay is spectacular. The story is... meh, but that isn't what you're here for. The dialogue hub is plain annoying but not any sort of issue. The bike segments are stupid fun and feel awesome.

There are two things I would say are inferior in this game compared to the first. First, the upgrade system, which went from this awesome, dynamic, puzzle to a boring grid (although the actual upgrades themselves are pretty cool).

Second, the gameplay in the first game feels sharper. You've got a lot more tools with a lot more uptime in this game, which sounds great (and can be), but in a one hit-one kill style game like this, it really just slows the combat down, makes it less smooth.\t\t\t\t\t
