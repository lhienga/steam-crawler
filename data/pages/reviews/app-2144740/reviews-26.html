\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fast paced 1-hit death cyberpunk slasher/platformer you won't forget!
Although it doesn't fit the [1 hr gameplay = 1 dollar spent] criteria, it's well worth the money for the standard edition at least (and an even better deal if you get the brutal edition on sale).

Gameplay
Similar to the first game, the fun lay within the wallrunning, jumping, and grappling introduced in the first game, now with added motorcycle! Slashing through enemies while avoiding certain 1-hit deaths is fun beyond compare, an experience tailored for high-skill gamers. This sequel adds new as well as heavily improved mechanics, notably the parry/deflect system, (endgame) flight suit, several motorcycle racing style levels, as well as cleaning up the old game mechanics like the abilities system. High skill, high reward!

Achievements
Short and sweet, half the achievements are challenging and fulfilling, requiring you to put your skill to the test. None of the achievements are particularly tedious if you keep in mind that you must search for collectibles before starting. This time, there's a built in radar for collectibles, so there's no excuse not to get them all! The most challenging achievement is finishing 5 levels without dying. Think it's easy? Think again.

Story
Massive improvements over the first game in several areas. First, you can actually see who you're talking with over the radio face-to-face this time! Voice acting has improved leaps and bounds with quality dialogue and meaningful content. While I don't feel particularly attached to any of the characters, there is the groundwork laid for future games. They certainly don't lack in personality, now they just need time to marinate on the player.

Hate
Very little to hate about this game, but the introduction of new mechanics will always pose issues/fixes that need to be made. As such, only 3 notable problems occurred;
1) The motorcycle feels extremely floaty, and the lerp applied to the camera makes it hard to take sharp turns which are sometimes necessary
2) You can't move the camera while driving, which also makes it difficult to see where you're going at times
3) On the motorcycle stage in which you are chased by a large entity, if you die around the beginning of the chase, then you become stuck in a loop in which you can't boost out and the slow speed causes you to repeatedly die. A simple solution might be having the boost accelerate in faster burst initially, or reduce the lag on the boost input.\t\t\t\t\t

Not because they haven't expanded their game play elements, they've added multiple new mechanics to the game to refresh the play style. However, the main issue arises the moment you go into your "hideout" for the first time and immediately break the action to try to have a direct area that expands story and lore. This is very counter intuitive for a game of this play style, the first game had me hooked as it was one continually fluid game. While this game is good, i would rather recommend to any of my friends to play the original.\t\t\t\t\t
