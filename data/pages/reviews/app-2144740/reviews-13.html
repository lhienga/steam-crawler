\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is exactly what I wanted from Ghostrunner's sequel. It expanded on every aspect of the first game while adding it's own unique things to make it even a better Ghostrunner. The addition of new abilities like Shurikens and Clonings or the new bike levels is so good IMO. The story stuff was also expanded which I really liked since there were more cutscenes and better transitions between the levels. Also now you have a base that u can hang out with your friends between missions which is awesome. The soundtrack and variety of level design and enemies was also phenomenal just like the first game. The boss fights in this game are amazing which is another positive note since GR1 didn't have many boss fights (they were more like 3 to 4 hit kills in GR1).
My only nitpick is that there are more collision problems and getting stuck in the environment due to the fact that some levels have multiple route options. Also the quality of NPC animations could've been better but that's not expected since this is not a AAA game.
Man I Hope this game sells well and we get a third game for Ghostrunner or at least get story DLCs for GR2 :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked the first Ghostrunner a lot, and Ghostrunner 2 is a good game as well. However, if I had to pick one out of the two to recommend to people, I would go for the first game rather than this sequel.

For the most part, Ghostrunner 2 still has the elements that made the first game great. There's the slick-as-butter movement, well-designed encounters that force you to really come to grips with the abilities of your Ghostrunner in order to survive, and a great cyberpunk aesthetic. However, I also agree with many of the other reviews I have seen here on Steam: Ghostrunner 2 feels like a less focused game than its predecessor, including elements which distract from the visceral joy delivered by the combat and movement.

It's something we see a lot with sequels in games. The first title in the series is good (or in the case of Ghostrunner 1, very good), and presumably does well enough to warrant a sequel. However, for that sequel the developers often feel pushed to expand the scope of the game. In the case of Ghostrunner 2, this is expressed via the addition of a more fleshed-out story that is experienced via conversations with other characters in a hub between levels, and open world-ish sections where you drive around on a bike.

Neither of these two elements are bad, but they also didn't feel like they were so excellent as to add a lot of value to the game. And the net result is that there is more downtime in Ghostrunner 2. With all due respect to the developers, I found it hard to feel engaged by the story, as it is fairly flat, and also simply isn't what I come to the Ghostrunner franchise for. The open world sections, on the other hand, are cool at times -- some of the parts where you drive the bike are genuinely fun and exciting --, but often also felt like useless padding that make the game grind to a screeching halt.

I can't be sure of the actual numbers, but due to these extra elements, it just felt that Ghostrunner 2 had less of the content that I actually want out of these games. My subjective perception of this sequel is that there simply were fewer combat encounters in this game as opposed to the last one. I also felt that perhaps there was a tinge less care and creativity that had gone into designing these encounters, in comparison to Ghostrunner 1. Some of the arenas were bland, lacking verticality and making you rely almost solely on using jump pads to avoid enemy fire and bounce from one victim to the next. This aspect was most strongly apparent in the second half of the game for me. In the first half, Ghostrunner 2 felt like it directly continued what the first game had offered, but in the second part the setting and gameplay changes. This variety would have been welcomed, if not for the fact that I could not help but feel the second half of the game was just a bit less well-designed than the first half.

Nonetheless, Ghostrunner 2 is still a great game which I can rather easily recommend. Just make sure you buy the first one over this one, if given a choice.\t\t\t\t\t
