\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2 is... Well debatable for me. It's not good, but its a WHOLE lot of fun. I'll be honest here, my review is going to focus on the bad. I don't want to drone on about what everyone else already has. The game is crazy fun. It's as simple as that, but these 3 issues for me got in the way SO much, I almost did not leave my review as recommended:

There are so many spots that are blatantly hidden areas with some secret inside, until you realize they actually aren't, and this area is just not meant to be explored. It feels like the developer just forget to place the collectible inside. I get its not that kind of game; exploration is NOT to be encouraged here, but it sure does look like it is in game often times. When there IS a collectible, it's in an area that is easily accessible, and not at all off the beaten path, AND marked on your map.

Dying sucks. Often times, falling off the path into the abyss takes WAY too long. Some levels also have you ascending upwards, making your fatal descent even longer. Even worse than this though, is how you can often just get completely stuck. You'll fall off the path, get yourself jammed between some of the bars, props, and meshes below the level, leaving it to you to find out how to hop around just right to escape it and finally face your actual death. Frustrating.

The upgrades are boring. There's only 1 in fact that genuinely gives you an ability of sorts. I imagined this upgrade list would be packed full of changes to the player's maneuverability (being that the game is entirely focused on movement), but instead they are just plain, often scenario-specific, boosts. This really hampers down the excitement I got from picking up all the not-so-secret collectibles and earning enough money to keep the upgrades coming. In fact, I dismissed the upgrades entirely less than halfway through my play through.

Level design (not visually) is... meant for maybe another game. This is not going back to the secret rooms with no secrets. This is something a bit worse. For just one example - there's SO many platforms (that you must run across to proceed) that are ridiculously large. The maps in general are just often WAY too large for their own good. I feel like we either need to crunch these down to remove all of the boring fluff, or make the player's base running speed WAY higher. Genuinely, there were so many times I stopped running across a huge bridge of sorts just to double check I couldn't actually run any faster, or there wasn't a "cooler" way to maneuver passed this section.

I think all in all, Ghostrunner 2 failed in level design and execution. There are several other points I could nitpick, but they're not big enough a deal to even mention, so level design and execution is what damages it for me. The REST of the game however is, as you'd expect, a heart pumping blast to play, and the addiction to running is just as strong as ever since Ghostrunner 1, even when it had me running when and where I shouldn't have been far too often.

6.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
