\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a rare regression from the first game, which IMHO was almost flawless. The first game was precise and very tight. The runs were fun, the neon cyberpunk atmosphere was amazing yet grim, and the story was interesting while being easy to follow. The soundtrack was also absolutely amazing. All in all the first game was very curated, and streamlined. But most importantly it was FUN, you felt like a ninja.

This game is just the first game but worse in almost every way. You spent about half of the game in a bleak, brown, colourless desert which means the game completely loses its cyberpunk theme and feel. Its also a bit more open meaning you lose alot of that curated and streamlined flow the original game had.

The other disadvantage to this open area approach is the game just cant render it well, the framerate completely tanks in these sections of the game to the point where its barely even playable. In the first game I was clearing 144 frames without an issue. In the open areas of this game 50 frames isnt uncommon, even with DLSS.

Then theirs the combat runs which frankly are a bit of a mess. Theirs several runs that have enemies wandering around like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ zombies, which means you go from calculating who to kill first and when, to occasionally mashing the trigger button to clear the run, its just far more loose than the first and no where near as tight. Its just not fun.

The story is uninteresting for the most part and its paced poorly, but its serviceable. Sadly the soundtrack is complete ♥♥♥♥ compared to the first.

All in all, just play the first game, its really brilliant, theirs a reason more people are playing Ghostrunner 1 than 2 on Steam currently.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game! It picks up where the first Ghostrunner one left off and expands it.
You'll get high adrenaline rush with exciting full-verticality traversal puzzles,
and fast-paced, split second decision-making combat, while you level up expanding your
skill tree. It's a pure skill based game. No walking simulator here, you'll to sweat to progress through the levels.
New movement mechanics are the bike and the wingsuit (which basically allows you to fly).
It's a shame that the wingsuit is introduced very late in the game. The music is phenomenal and
the perfect soundtrack for the non-stop action. Some downsides: Not very well polished at certain
occasions, like the bike rides: you may easliy get stuck in the geometry which is annoying,
of course you can always restart from the latest checkpoint, but it would be nice if the game
did the reset for you. The boss fights, I didn't like so much. It's good that the game is generous
when it comes to checkpoints, but some boss fights were annoying.
Overall a great game if you like skill based fast-paced action.\t\t\t\t\t
