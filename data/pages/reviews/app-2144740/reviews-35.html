\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJack is back and One More Level does it again!

Almost everything about Ghostrunner 2 exceeded my already high expectations. The combat is fluid as ever bosses feel more challenging and exciting and the story and environments feel more rich and fleshed out. Top it off with an awesome soundtrack I could listen to for hours and the recipe is *chef's kiss".

As nearly perfect this game feels there are few drawbacks some more glaring than others.
If you played the first Ghostrunner no doubt you know how mind numbly hard some areas can be with the fast movement and one-hit death system, well for good or for bad Ghostrunner 2 feels somewhat easier mostly due to the fact new abilities such as the ninja star make dealing with enemies much easier tacked on with environmental interaction to assist in your slaughter this can make things feel easier than the first game.
If you saw the trailer no doubt the motorcycle clips caught your attention, well unfortunately the bike sections were some of my least favorite (Minus one part in particular). Controls feel hard to figure out and the insane speed make the camera go bonkers sometimes. Once you get a grip on the controls it's not that bad but still I found myself wanting to simply return to Jack on foot not to mention once I really had a grip on the bike I was already back to being on foot anyways.
Finally performance, probably the only real negative thing that isn't me nitpicking. Near later half of the game you will leave the tower and enter an apocalyptic wasteland there is one particular worm enemy here that spits acid, perhaps this is just me but once the acid landed and the deadly green cloud lingered my performance TANKED, some points even to 10-15 FPS. This is such a small section of the game where these enemies create the issue but dang if it doesn't just kill the experience when it hit me hard the game was near unplayable. Such a terrible smudge on this near perfect work of art and I'm holding out OML with patch this soon.

All in all even with these issues my overall experience with GR2 was fantastic and recommend this to old and new players of the series. 8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR - An imperfect but solid successor to the first game, 7/10.

Pros:
- Same fluid movement and combat that made GR1 a great game
- New powers and enemy interactions allow for more creativity in approaching combat
- More flexible upgrade tree
- Boss fights have more variety and are generally more interesting than GR1
- Soundtrack still goes hard

Cons:
- Some new mechanics (e.g. the bike) aren't great and distract from core gameplay
- The greater flexibility in approaching combat makes some fights feel less polished than GR1
- Narrative and dialogue feel a bit rough/forced, though no more so than in GR1
- Performance is pretty rough in a few spots, particularly in the last third of the game. For context, I'm on an RTX 2080 Super with a Ryzen 7 3700x.

If you enjoyed the first game and are looking for more, I'd recommend this one. If you haven't played the first game, I'd start there first, rather than jumping in here.\t\t\t\t\t
