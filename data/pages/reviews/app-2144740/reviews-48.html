\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSlice and dice.

This is a review about 2 weeks after the game launched. So there are a few bugs that have caused me to restart checkpoints, such as knocking a enemies off the map but they didn't die from it. And another bug on one area in 1 mission that starting causing my game to stutter to 15FPS frequently. Only way it went away was reloading a checkpoint or getting past that area (on a 4090 FYI). But overall game was interesting and fun to play with constant reasons to use the skills in your arsenal.

The skill/motherboard system is way better than the tetris design in the first game. Makes your choices of skill more open and larger to play with, but also has reasonable limits like motherboard memory, debuffs for certain skills, and perks for skills with specific conditions met (like slotting it at the last slot in the column).

There's a lot of replayability in this game, and I aim to 100% it just because it's that enjoyable to go back to. Plus there's a "simulation" side game that challenges you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWill need to do replays for achievements and improving times and deaths, but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ this game is great! Loved the original and love this one too.

Note: This review contains unmarked spoilers, so read at your own risk if you haven't played!
Tl;Dr: Game is gud. Need more.

I did actually enjoy having a little more meat on the story this time. The breaks between some missions made since because unlike the first, this game's story isn't a non-stop charge up Dharma, and I honestly enjoyed some of the NPC dialogue even if certain characters got more attention than others.

Gameplay is very much improved. Slide-jumping is still around which is nice. The new upgrade system felt a whole lot more streamlined and enjoyable to use; dashes and blocking changes were also nice and the stamina system was much better than I initially assumed on hearing about it. Powers were much more fluid in the gameplay, and using multiple feels more encouraged compared to GR1. Ultimates were a nice way to include some more powerful abilities, but I think the Bullet Time and steroid boost ones quickly outpaced the rest in most scenarios (Overlord is also still bad in both games).

Bosses were honestly much better in my opinion. Hel didn't offer all that much variety in the first game, TOM sucks, and Mara was mostly alright. GR2 definitely did a better job overall, and the second to last boss was definitely my favorite with the three swapping forms.

The world was also spectacular. I rather liked spending some time outside Dharma tower. Level design was also great, and the freedom in each combat arena allowed for fun gameplay decision making.

Also, the Motorcycle and Wingsuit are an absolute blast. My main complaint on the Wingsuit is it's only introduced on the last mission, but that said it led to some wild gameplay. As for the Motorcycle, I enjoyed its segments, though the one problem is there weren't enough like the first segment. The chase across Dharma and the race down the side of the Tower were exhilarating, and I wish we got more of the bike in those Neon set pieces.

All in all, Ghostrunner 2 is a solid game all around. 8/10, will certainly play many times again.\t\t\t\t\t
