\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to start with this review by saying that I loved the game's story and new systems. This was a fun sequel, and I loved most of it. The level design was great. The motorcycle missions were a wonderful addition. It reminded me of the swoop bike racing that was in KOTOR. This game reminds me of KOTOR a lot and gave me a sense of nostalgia when I played it. I do think this game is worth the purchase if you enjoyed the first game.

My one and only problem with this game is the boss fights. With the exception of the second boss fight, which is mainly parkour, the boss fights weren't fun. The bosses are damage sponges. The idea of this game is everything dies in one hit, even you. I understand bosses needing more health. Hel in the last game had three chunks of health, and when you hit her it felt satisfying. When fighting the first boss, you have to keep swinging into him. It feels like I'm hitting him with a foam nerf sword. It would've been better that every time you parry or stun him that your hit takes a chunk out. Instead of hitting multiple times. The third and forth bosses have the same problem.

All in all, this game is really fun, minus the bosses. Despite my one complaint, I do think this game is worth the purchase. Thank you for reading, and have a nice day.

Edit: I've 100% completed the game. My opinion still stands. Fun game. Bad boss fights.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo Ghostrunner 2. Like many other studios, fell into the same trap of going too big in the sequel without really understanding what made the first game successful. Bigger is not always better. There are a total of 18 stages, of which 4 are very, very long stages that are semi open world that take about 3-4 hours to finish, which is something like 1/3 of the game. Not fun at all, half of the time will be traveling on the motorcycle from point A to point B. It feels like they added mini-tasks to prolong the game artificially unlike the first game which was very short and to the point.
The plot is unclear and boring, there is really no auditory twist. They have now added a hub world between the stages where you can talk to the NPCs who will comment on what is happening in the stages themselves which again the acting of the voice actors is mediocre -. I wouldn't really happen to even play these games to simply read text into the microphone.
I would happen to it even playing these simply read text into the microphone.
The graphics are much better but optimization for PC is bad. Especially in DX12, even in the first game there was the same problem that seems not to have been addressed by the developers. Full of frame drops, stuttering, the AI ​​doesn't always respond correctly, the game crashed at least 4-5 times during the 10 hours it took to finish it.
The mechanics themselves are more or less the same as the previous game, the enemies are identical to each other. The skills/progression does not feel significant at all, you can not touch the skill tree from the beginning to the end and the experience itself will be more or less the same. Because the game is mostly based on one hit death mechanics for both enemies and the player. The game starts with the Dash\\slow time skills and like the previous game you get 4-5 new skills that...surprise. It's exactly the same skills from the previous game!
All in all a good and fun game but for what I already played in the first one, I wouldn't pay full price but wait for a significant discount. They didn't take any risks with it and went for the safest and most boring thing that could be as a sequel.
5/10
The cost of the game is $40, which is apparently a standard price for AA, but Pierre, I wouldn't pay more than $15-20 for it.\t\t\t\t\t
