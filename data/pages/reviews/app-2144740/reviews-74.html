\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 1 was probably one of my favorite games when I played it. It was a phenomal, difficult, and rewarding game to get skilled at. I have beaten most of the ghostrunner 1 levels with zero deaths, unlocked every collectible, and unlocked every achievement (prior to the release of Project: HEL), and own every cosmetic DLC.

My main gripes with Ghostrunner 2 are as follows:

The new "block" mechanic is not fun or rewarding. Ghostrunner 1 felt like a puzzle game where you had to find a good route to approach certain enemies or get skilled at parrying. Ghostrunner 2 contrives this by giving players a sort of get-out-of-jail free card in the form of the blocking mechanic.

I VERY strongly dislike how the characters in this game interact, most notably their use of modern slang. Zoe and Bakunin are the only characters who seem even remotely serious about anything happening in the plot; Kira, Connor, and even Jack seem detached and have been infected with Marvel level quips and quotations. Slang and 21st century concepts like cosplaying, larping (the characters in the game actually say "larp"), the use of the phrase "bet" and other examples really pull me out of the game; this isn't 2020, it's the far future with cyborgs and a desolate earth. It would be nice if the characters felt like they were actually invested in the life-or-death circumstance they are in and if the dialog didn't feel like it was a fan fiction from a 17 year old set in the Ghostrunner universe.

I also feel like the pace at which upgrades are presented to the player is a bit too rapid, and the medium through which they are given isn't satisfying. In GR1, you had to earn them by completing certain parts of the game, and often by completing Cybervoid puzzles (if I recall correctly, I could be wrong). In GR2 most of these abilities are just given to the player, which makes it feel a little less like you earned it and more like someone gave it to you.

I'm still playing the game (haven't finished my first run yet) but these are my gripes so far.

All of that being said, the combat is fun and the music still bangs. I'd buy it, just expect to cringe at the dialog.\t\t\t\t\t
