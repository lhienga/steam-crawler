\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one I'd really like steam to have a maybe option for.
Ghostrunner 2 is very good but lacks is certain areas I can see being gamebreaking for some. The motorcycle is very buggy, and you wont encounter it until halfway through the game. The motorcycle can slam into objects and get stuck forcing a restart. The game in general can be lacking of polish at times. I dashed out of the environment 3 or 4 times to my death in the void because of collision detection errors. I noticed 3 or 4 instances of being able to see straight through the ground or walls into the void. For a game that's very hotline miami esc, it can be irritating to have to redo sections because of collision errors with the bike/player. Ghostrunner 2 continues the series' difficulty in a good way. I would say it was slightly easier than the first, but not a noticeable step down. The level design has taken a step down in quality. I mean there is a point where you are breaking colored crystals in line with what the game says to do for basically no reason. It's not hard, it's not fun, and it doesnt break up the gameplay cause it's inherently set to a 25 second timeframe. There are no moments of me admiring the levels for how interconnected and well made they are. Part of this comes down to the increased number of collectible items in missions and general gameplay's less involved nature. There is less than 5 new enemies. There's the wheel zombie thing that involves dashing to avoid and then you kill it. There is the sandworm that spits acid. The zombies are kind of new visually, but are mechanically just reskins of previous enemies. They replaced the warden with the shinobi. That's it. As far as cut enemies... They removed the wreck. They removed the turrets. They removed the sniper. They removed the splitter. So for every enemy they added, they removed one from the original that provided a unique gameplay challenge. This really is so hard to recommend with all of these elements, but I still do. The bosses and gameplay are fantastic. The music is excellent. The story was very mid, as expected. But the good parts of this game are so worth experiencing. So go into this game with hesitance, and just get ghostrunner 1 is you're curious about the series. And, I believe Ghostrunner 1 is longer as well.
TL;DR
1 is better, and this one lacks new enemies and the amazing level design we saw from the first game. If you dont have 1, get it instead. If you already have 1 and liked it. This one's good, with more problems than the original. But fun nonetheless.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWARNING!
Very poorly optimized at launch!

Consistent framerate stutters on anything other than the highest end systems (And even sometimes then!) that ramp up into becoming unplayable a couple of hours into the game at the open world segment, even on the absolute lowest settings.
This is conveniently far enough into the game to void the steam refund period.
If intentional this is an extremely scummy, manipulative practice that abuses the steam refund policy.


Is the game fun? Not if you are looking for more of the same high skill + precision execution gameplay from the previous title. Not even ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ close.

It's step away from what made Ghostrunner 1 unique in many ways and a step down in others, aside from the upgrade system. The slew of new mechanics, while a welcome addition are often not well thought out, polished or utilized.
Really, really disappointing.

The 'ramming your head against problems' approach works way too often, even when the game is chugging due to the leniency that comes with designing combat around ability usage and blocking. This isn't inherently a bad thing but as aforementioned, diehard fans of the requirement of finesse and reflexes stemming from the mix of brutality and fairness of the first title won't get that itch scratched in a way they were hoping for.

Most enemies no longer predict your movement so there's none of that intricate planning or juking out attacks only to finesse the other way at the last second.

Certain enemies REQUIRE the use of abilities instead of giving you the option to utilize skillful counter movement to bypass it's usage.

Certain enemy attacks seem to be randomized and/or not sufficiently telegraphed. This is a downright sin for a title like this.

Properly timing parrying is a joke now. You can now just block entire volleys of shots while bumrushing.

Bland, unclear enemy visual and sound design turns gameplay into a guessing or memorization game instead of rewarding reflexes.

Imprecise and buggy platforming at times. Unclear level geometry and color scheme to subtly direct the players attention makes it hard to tell which way progression is way too often.

Uninspired combat arenas. Enemies pop out of nowhere way too often.
Stupidly narrow corridors and poorly placed units behind doors allow enemies to initiate combat before you have even half a second to register what they are and react accordingly.

I don't care about how bad the writing and story is, but needing to go back to a hub in general breaks up the fast paced 'GO. GO. GO.' action the game is supposed to excel at. A minor gripe, but still valid.

I'm sure there is plenty more but I've only played seven hours at the time of this reviews conception and I'm unable to enjoy playing more due to framerate issues.

If you are wondering why it is so well received, it's likely because they made the game more accessible by lowering the skill requirement down to pander to a larger audience, hence the plethora of positive game reviews that lack an in depth analysis compared to the negative ones. Additionally, with how many positive ones are saying so little/inaccurate and the emphasis on financially investing in marketing I'd even go so far as to come to the conjecture that they have paid for many of these positive steam reviews.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGhostrunner 2, in comparison to Ghostrunner 1, is a much more complicated game. While it still feels just as smooth in a lot of places, the addition of new ways to approach combat and parkour can feel both exciting and frustrating. The level design is often amazing and of course the visuals are always top tier, but I can't help but mention that there are a few areas of the game that feel clunky (which is strange for me to say when talking about Ghostrunner). Clearly, the developers put a lot of heart and soul into the game, but their ambition for greater additions to Ghostunner 2 has led to certain mechanics feeling frustrating (with the main problem child being the bike). The boss battles are amazing, the combat is still as fluid as ever, the music is as fantastic as ever, but the story line... The story is a bit obvious and stereotypical and I often found myself wishing the writing took several different turns. Jack feels more human than ever as well. Of course, this is a GREAT evolution of his character. One of my biggest gripes with the game itself is its tendency to halt the flow of levels, combat, and parkour in favor of plopping you into the hub area where you can talk to NPCs. While this is great for lore, it feels strange in the high-speed environment of Ghostrunner. Additionally, poor choice of geometry and terrain throughout the game can cause issues not just with Jack himself (wall-running, jumping to other floor levels) but also his bike. This can become incredibly frustrating. On top of that, the game has a healthy helping of bugs (with one example being the bike not respawning on a level after I crashed), but honestly that is expected with any game nowadays and these issues will probably be addressed with time. The game sometimes also has frame dips when there are a lot of bullets and/or enemies on screen, but I managed to fix this by lowering all of my graphics settings from medium to low (RIP).

Overall, despite my gripes with the game, I recommend anyone who enjoys high-speed combat, parkour, and cyberpunk themes to play it.\t\t\t\t\t
