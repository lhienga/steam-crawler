\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game completely blew me away and easily became my favourite game of 2022 and 2023.

Instead of giving you a detailed review on the game, I just wanna share my story on how I discovered this masterpiece.

Back in August 2022 I attended Gamescom in Cologne, Germany with 2 friends. I saw the Raw Fury stand and watched someone play the Cassette Beasts demo, it kinda brought back old Pok√©mon vibes for me. Unfortunately I wasn't able to play the demo myself, but I wishlisted the game on Steam once I got back home and watched some teasers on YouTube which already made me fall in love with the concept and OST of the game. (I watched these on repeat btw because the OST was otherwise not available at that time, luckily Joel Baylis released some songs later on)

Sometime in November (I think) the demo was available on Steam for a week so I downloaded it immediately and started playing. It was a blast! At that time I was struggling with mental issues and stress and CB was a bit of an anchor and something to look forward to at work. And the memory of hearing "Wherever we are now" in Harbour Town for the first time... damn. That song hit so hard because it captured my current mood and lifestyle at that time perfectly, just floating around and living without a real direction. But anyways... I was playing the demo for hours of hours, and the demo only had content for about 20-30 minutes. But enjoying the cozy atmosphere of the game, listening to the music and farming bootlegs was still a lot of fun. And after the release date finally got announced, I surely had something to be excited about!

Fast forward to April 26th, the release date of Cassette Beasts. Of course I instantly purchased the game and also it's OST. I enjoyed playing it way too much, and I found myself listening to the OST way too much everywhere! In my first few hours of playing time I didn't achieve that much, most of the time I was just standing there, sucking the atmosphere in and enjoying every piece of music (I know I'm saying this a lot but I'm sorry, I just love Joel Baylis' work and it's 100% one of my personal highlights).

But apart from the overall feeling and the soundtrack, the gameplay also holds it's own, with a lot of cool areas to explore, some amazing characters and monsters, and an incredible combat system with a lot of depth and strategy (even though custom starter/random starter is straight up broken and far too powerful). The unique type interactions and the fusion system make this game truly stand out and make for a great experience. The archangel fights feel epic and rewarding, the coolest battles in my opinion are the Ranger fights though. One thing that's always been underwhelming for me in the Pok√©mon series were the gym leader fights, Cassette Beasts uses them in a better way: They teach you some of the strategies that are possible in this game! For a lot of them you'll actually need to plan ahead because if you're not prepared for them they will beat you with their setups and tricks, I enjoyed almost all of them a lot (looking at you Gladiola ._. ) The different forms of fusions are also really fun and help to prevent fusion from becoming stale and boring too quickly.
There's also a little bit of a friendship/romance system in the game, but that's honestly one of the weaker parts of the game. It helps to boost the fusion strength with you partner which is nice, but the dialogue options themselves and lack of interaction with the characters make it feel sort of incomplete and rushed to me personally, I guess it's fine though because it's not one of the game's main focal points anyways.

I think that pretty much concludes my little review of Cassette Beasts, it became a lot longer than intended. Overall it should be clear that I highly recommend this game to everyone with a love for monster-taming RPGs and a similar taste of music and ingame atmosphere. Thanks for everyone who's actually managed to read through all of it, I hope you have an amazing day, keep care of yourselves!

Greetings, ShuddersGist\t\t\t\t\t
