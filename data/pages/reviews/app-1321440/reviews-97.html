\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best Pokemon-alikes I've ever played, with an excellent soundtrack. If you've played Pokemon, you probably have an idea of the broad strokes, so here are the things that are different that sell me on it.

One: It's got a paper mario-esque partner system where you and your partner are turning into monsters. So your 'level' is the level of the characters; the monsters have a rank of 1 to 5 stars from getting levelled up. Levelling up gives them more slots for moves/passive abilities and gives them moves.
Two: Your moves in this game are stickers, which can be freely moved to different monsters, and which come in Uncommon and Rare varieties that have randomly generated extra benefits beyond their normal abilities.
Three: The type system is fun and varied and has way more than just 'certain attacks do extra damage'; For example, Fire attacks turn Plastic targets into Poison targets; Fire inflicts Burn on Poison targets; Poison increase AP regeneration on fire targets. This, combined with wide and varied type-changing effects, makes it really strategic, as does the fact that the game has an AI programmed to pay attention to this aspect.
Four: Shinies in this game are 'bootlegs', who are not merely of a different color; they are of a different type, and get different moves from being rank-upped, and tend to get more Uncommon and Rare stickers, making them very handy.
Five: The game has a Fusion system where your monsters are combined into one big super-monster which has a unique sprite for every possible combination.
Six: The game takes an incredibly hard turn into lovecraftian, existential horror approximately 10 minutes in.

This isn't like BotW/TotK where your default stamina might last a good 5 seconds or so. No, you start off with 2 seconds of running, and if the stamina bar is depleted, you __stop__ to catch your breath for a good 2 seconds. Now, I've read that your stamina increases by 0.5s(?) per boss (along with earning other movement options to unlock areas), but to me that sounds like a band-aid over an unnecessary wound, because you still have to pace your character's running.

Let's be clear, if Game Freak or Nintendo did this, they would be rightfully eviscerated, but the pendulum has swung so far in the opposite direction that players are just giving a pass on this. I put up with this in BotW/TotK, but I want to play this game for the 'mon catching and world exploration, not timing my inputs like a rhythm game just to move around at a decent pace.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best Pokemon style game to date.

Other good ones: Monster Sanctuary, Coromon.

You always fight with 2 pokemon on your side at once, but have a party of 6 like in Pokemon.

While RPG combat often does get repetitive, it got a little bit much for me at times.

The music is pretty great, as is the games visuals.

The dialogue is far easier to suffer than the Pokemon games, and amused me at times.

The Monster design is Pokemon to a fault, silly things like my bullet pokemon evolving into a rifle type Pokemon.. The images of both are the same size, why would it evolve into that outside of trying to be cute with its names? Dumb.

I wish your NPC partners were no-clip or at least could not bump into and push blocks around when you are trying to solve a puzzle.

Exploration is good, the world is very open and you gain abilities that open up the exploration (similar to Zelda and metroidvania games).

If you enjoyed Pokemon and games of that nature, this is absolutely essential. It rivals Pokemon in many ways, and does some things better.\t\t\t\t\t
