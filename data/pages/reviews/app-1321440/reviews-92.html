\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn amazing experience!
This is a spoiler free review.
I will break this down to different aspects:

Gameplay:
Solid mechanics and a good interpretation of classic Jrpg and Creature collection mechanics.
Creatures can't die while you are trying to catch them and the other intricacies if that system are perfectly crafted.
The game isn't afraid to allow the player to exploit its mechanics, allowing for skill expression and fun combos.
That being said balancing feels very good and progression is tied to your character and not your creatures so you will never loose progress if you want to swap the creatures you are using.
The the final puzzle in the game is unique in each playthrough, which guarantees that you actually have to solve it yourself (the internet might or might not be helpful).
The late game migth include quite a bit of grinding (after the first 30-40 hours of gameplay)
The post-game unlocks Nuzlock and Randomizer options for new runs.
Honestly 9/10 on that aspect alone. If you are a game designer you NEED to play this game.

Story and world building:
Solid with some existential commentary and good mystery hooks.
The concept and story hook is REALLY good. A spin on isekai (in another world) tropes that I find really cool and satisfying.
The world is full of people you don't have to talk to that often have interesting things to say. Every building in the towns has a "purpose" lore-wise and you can kind of tell who lives where if you pay attention.
The main riddle of the game is unique to each playthrough which keeps the mystery alive and makes it cool to keep track of what is happening.
Overall the story is not groundbreaking but it is serviceable and allows other aspects of the game like its writing and characters to shine!
Since I want to emphasize them I will tackle writing and characters in their own sections.

Writing and Characters:
The writing is fun and engaging, and characters (including the protagonist) are well written with a lot of personality.
Quips and Jokes are snappy and fun.
The characters have a lot of variety and you have some control on the way you interact with them. This is something that becomes more apparent later on.
Most characters have partial voice acting with some voice lines that people will not hear depending on the choices they make in the way they build their relationship with the supporting cast. Even less important characters have partial voice acting which keeps the world feeling alive and consistent.
This was the first rpg in a while that made me care so much about the characters I was interacting with (some more than others admittedly) and more importantly, about the relationship I was building with it.

Art and Music:
The game is beautiful!
The pixel art is good and leaves just enough to the imagination.
The character portrets are great (for humans and creatures) and the character designs are inspired and varied.
Without spoiling much, there are certain moments in the game that contradict its art-style. This is intentional and very effective in making those moments (and usually battles) stand out a lot!
MUSIC SLAPS!!! I don't care who you are this is REALLY good. If lyrics distract you then there is an option to turn the music instrumental in the sound options (still slaps).

Overall, I would recomend this. I would say it is already one of my favorite games of all time.

The game looks like if Pokemon Diamond and Pearl refined its style, soundtrack is fantastic. Gameplay invites you break it in so many different ways, by giving you massive variety in Uncommon/Rare stickers (attacks). Maybe you got a 30% chance of Confusion in a 0 cost attack, and you build in to spamming that. Or you opt to commit to turtle strats, by combining Leeching and regen effects, and spamming walls until you can one shot anything that's in front of you. Combine Ghost and Gambit statues, and become a powerhouse that can't be hit with damage. Drawback? There's no drawback if you're counting in Dog Years. And that's not counting your freedom to adjust your own stats once you've collected half of the Captain Stamps. Go for it, 200% Melee Power with only 50% Ranged Power, 75% for each Defence.

Bootlegs are also interesting. Different colour, but also different type, and movepool. Go ahead, collect 14 variants of every single monster. Go and collect and fuse in combat every possible combination of over 16 thousand possibilities. If you want to be a collector, you've got room to collect here.

Oh, and Custom Games are a thing! There's enough possible random elements that you can do randomized runs too. You could randomize the typing and movepools, the main quest location, even what partner starts with what beast. Complete with timer, if you wanted to race and master the game. Oh, and you can also scale the AI up so that even random encounters could have the logical AI that human encounters would have. If you want to have a harder experience, and prove yourself, you can do so.

This game was made with love. This isn't just a bi-yearly edition of Pokemon, with corners cut.
And on the sliding scale of Pokemon to Digimon, I'd probably put it closer to Digimon anyways. Especially with context wrong Archangels and transforming in to the beasts yourselves.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is going to be a short review because I just finished the game and want to get a few thoughts out - I may eventually come back and revise this to go more in-depth in the future.

Clocking in at finishing the Main Story at 34 hours, I feel this is a surprising and bittersweetly short game.

The story in this game is fantastic.
The music is fantastic.
The art is fantastic.
The aesthetic is fantastic.
And even the writing is quite enjoyable and fun. The characters are honestly extremely charming - both Allies and Villains alike. (Yes, even the "dating" option was quite strange but surprisingly charming.)

The mechanics - while very reminiscent of other popular MonGames - are also quite unique and are surprisingly easy to memorize after awhile in spite of their depth (hint: have the Tutorial help on until you "get it").

The short Main Story is the only downside to me. While there is replayability that gets unlocked in spades after the final boss (new modes for creating new games and new Ranger Quests if you continue your current save), the Main Story is just so enjoyable that it feels quite emotional and sad when it is over.

Overall this is a definite Recommend - but be warned: enjoy it while it lasts since the Main Story ends much too soon. But look forward to lots of replayability if that is your thing (I look forward to 100% my save :) ).

I look forward to future DLC/expansions for this game - I don't think I ever want to catch that last train off of New Wirral!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, I love it!

The monster-catching system is tastefully done and the 'upgrade-monster-to-evolve' portion scratches a Digimon-itch of mine. But honestly, what this game excels in is the **music**. I mean...just wow. I honestly wish the old Pokemon and Digimon games had arranged their score this way. The audio blends seamlessly into cutscenes, battle scenes, and the environment. Plus, the music is a delightful mix of rock, 90s synth, some electro and modern EDM arranged in a medley that kept me hooked for 4 hours straight (up to the point where I was doing random mob battles just to hear the fusion music more!).

The art is fun and the overworld puzzle mechanics are doubly so.

I believe that this game should be a contender for the Steam Labor of Love Award\t\t\t\t\t
