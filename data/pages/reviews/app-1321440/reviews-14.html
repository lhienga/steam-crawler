\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLately, I've been trying to pick up where I left off with the Pokémon series from when I was a kid, but it's been hard since they lack a lot of modern video game features like in-depth dialogue and quests, so whenever I try to get back into them, I quickly get bored because they feel stale to me and the sheer amount of new Pokémon is intimidating. It's not that those games are necessarily bad, just that they've from over a decade ago, and it's hard for me to go backwards.

I decided to pick up Cassette Beasts so that I can slowly get back into the creature collection genre and I've gotta say this game feels like a Pokémon game, but with updated mechanics and an system. It's not just a bad wannabe Pokémon game; it's its own thing inspired by Pokémon and it's really hitting me with the nostalgia vibes. It also uses a fusion system that I like. I always hated Pokémon fusion fan games since it always seemed like a lazy way to make fakemon, but this fuses monsters in a way that's not intrusive or jarring.

All in all, if you want an original Pokémon-esque game or want to get back into creature collecting, this game is great. It also has a great soundtrack and retro aesthetic in 2.5D that I really dig.

If I could describe it in two words, it would be "Modernized Nostalgia."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdating after pushing through a bit more:

I've mostly finished the game; however, I can't bring myself to grind through the notice board quests for the final last part of post-game content. All of my previous assessments stand with another added point that I did not find much enjoyment in:

The notice board quests are simply too repetitive and grindy. Requiring 100 fusion material for level up post lv 3 means that the majority of the quests you get at that point are "hey go grind X monsters" MMO type quests. If it was 5 or 10 batches of these fetch quests, I might have put up with it, but 100 is simply too much.

I really want to love this game: the music is so fantastic; the story and especially the final fusion trope ending is something I should like a lot (the conceptual monsters part too). And yet, the story just feels too sparse and too spread apart for me to truly engage with the characters and the world.

Maybe the zelda-esque exploration aspect is enough for others to fill in the huge narrative spaces in between, but I suppose it's not for me then.
---------------------------
I think I've played long enough to see what the game excels at and where the game doesn't work for me.

Here's why I would recommend this game for its strengths:
(1) Quirky breezy and inclusive atmosphere
(2) Encourages fun experimentation through the implementation of stickers and such
(3) Great art design overall, especially the archangels
(4) Fantastic exploration mechanics that progresses smoothly alongside your monster collection progress (Pokemon HM done right)
This is the perfect monster collecting game for people who are attracted to its aesthetics. Pokemon for liberal art students would be how I would describe it.

Here's why it doesn't work for me personally: I've realized that what I want out of a monster collecting game is perhaps the sense of companionship you get with your creatures. I find it difficult to establish that here when the sticker compatibility has a greater emphasis on creativity. Then again, these aren't monsters you are capturing, but vestiges you are recording. So perhaps I went in with the wrong impression. The plot also feel too scattered even compared to the pokemon mainline games for me to grow an attachment to the companions. They are quirky and fun banter camp dialogue generators and I like the writing for the characters. It's just a shame that it's so far and few in between.

I also can't bring myself to get past the sticker management UI which makes the process too much of a headache for me (why does the sticker pages have multiple pages while being scrollable???? Did I miss a toggle option or something??? And while there are filter options which seem to be added according to player feedback, I find myself making far less use of it than its existence would imply)

I don't think I can bring myself to enjoy and finish this game despite its unique artistic vision and good enough execution, but this is a genuinely good game that I know someone out there, the right person, would fall in love with it in an instant.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI initially purchased on switch, but this game has not only been my favorite game of 2023, but one of my top 3 games of all time. It's like a monster taming game but instead of taming monsters you transform into them with a cassette player!

This game really just wants the player to have fun. Fully customizable AI and scaling (even customizable mid-playthrough!), with perma-death and randomizer options after beating the game once, and the main puzzle of the game differs each time you make a new game. This and the other options allow for a unique experience even on your 2nd, 3rd, or 20th playthrough! So, even if I uh completely accidentally solved the main puzzle so early on that I was completely confused as to what happened and why (as I couldn't even do anything with solving it until later) it actually took me a while to solve on my second playthrough due to it being completely different!

There is a local co-op feature, but I haven't gotten to use it yet so I can't really comment on it. However, there is an online multplayer and PvP update being heavily worked on and planning to release early this year, so there's that to look forward to! As far as characters in the game, there are several characters and you can improve your relationship/bond with them. There are some cutscenes and light romantic events that can happen if you select the right options when you are close enough to a character, but they are entirely optional so if light romantic elements don't interest you, you can just avoid them. Or you can always just have Barkley as your companion!

It has a phenomenal soundtrack, fun and unique designs, complete customization, many huge Quality of Life features, (stickers are SO NICE and have INSANE competitive potential(for those who've played Pokemon, think of one-time-use tms but then you can take the move back off the creature and get the tm back to put on another one, or just take off a move that was part of its learnset and make it a tm)), and many unique and fun features! I mean, the typing system itself is actually really fun! They made a freaking typing system FUN. I didn't even know that was a thing I could enjoy so much until this!!!

I will absolutely be trying to get all the steam achievements, and while the Bestiary is able to be completed, I probably won't truly complete it as the fusions do have their own section. On top of that, it keeps track of all the bootlegs (shinies but the typing changes! one bootleg variant for each of the 14 types, allowing for 14 bootleg variants for each creature! thankfully it doesn't keep track of bootleg fusions though, so it is actually humanly possible!) so uh yeah good luck if you truly want to 100% this game!

So yeah, that's my semi-spoiler free review for this phenomenal game. I would pay $60 for this game if it was priced that high- I cannot recommend it enough for those who want a monster taming game suited for an older audience!\t\t\t\t\t

MY CONCLUSIONS?

Okay, I was truly sleeping on this title but honestly it was worth it, in terms of reviewing it rn and returning later. I feel like the climate for a "pocket-monster adjacent" has been perfect to play cassette beasts. CB is def more akin to the series it draws inspo from, in terms of the early 00's titles (think pre-crystal basically) except...it's not. I think this game deserves all the praise and hype for truly taking the core mechanics and improving upon them whilst still being a really unique story and model design. If there was any game N tendo should look to for "how to make a game that isn't a C/TM infringement and lawsuit waiting to happen...look at this game. Games such as Palworld are absolutely trying to improve the formula Legends A wanted to innovate in the series, while Cassette Beasts is out here bringing the original classic formulas and mechanics, into a refreshed and most of all, unique story and game. It's like they took my favorite series I grew up on and instead of ripping it off, they truly just kept what worked and everything that needed to change, was changed. Still fairly early into the game itself (sitting at 19 hrs and feel like I've barely scratched the surface of the story and hidden things) so that def earns a positive review. With AAAs being easily 70-100+ dollars before taxes these days. it's really great to have games like these still shining in the rough of their genres and price-points. Coming off of other game titles and having a soft spot for indie devs, time management, RPG survival sims and being a kid in the 90s. this game really is something I will be telling my peers to play when they ask for game recommendations next time.\t\t\t\t\t
