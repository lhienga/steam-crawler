\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts is probably my favorite creature collector game out there. The music is phenomenal in this game, and I listen to the soundtrack often, (please add Arrow of Time and Your Inception to Spotify I'm begging.) Like I would tell you to get this game for the music alone, and the way music is used to enhance scenes, areas, battles, characters is wonderful.

I love the design of both characters and creatures. And the fusion mechanic is so incredibly cool and actually useful (unlike another creature collector that attempted it that just padded out battles with bigger health, this game actually brings some strategy to its fusion mechanic.) Not to mention when a fusion takes place, the vocals of their songs kick in?! It really hypes up any battle honestly.

The story and world of New Wirral are so interesting with the eldritch lore of archangels and the designs of them being just chef's kiss. I adore the companions (Meredith is best girl) and their side quests too. Stories of people finding love and getting by in a new world, helping each other, as well as the conflicts with other people stranded are so interesting. The game feels very post-apocalyptic (though I'm sure there's a better term to describe it that I can't quite place) and gives it a cool atmosphere with things from different worlds being pulled in and appearing as wreckage (ships, stores, etc.)

I won't spoil the ending of the story, and while I think it was very good, I'd love for this game to have a couple more different endings or even variations of the same ending. The endgame is also great with some cool fights (including a boss that has my favorite song in the game tied to them). It can be pretty repetitive, but they mix in some interesting endgame quests here and there among the repetitive bounties. As well as the ritual candle grind for bootleg or "shiny" creatures. I don't think they really take away from the game though, and you can have fun just playing through the story and fights. I'm a completionist so I'll get all the achievements one day, may just take me a while.

If you've made it this far into my "review" of Cassette Beasts, thanks for reading! I feel like its more so just me gushing about how much I love this game. I look forward to also playing Lenna's Inception, and since your here I'll talk about just a couple more things. My favorite fight is against Ianthe, it was so difficult and I really had to come up with some ideas to beat that fight (i also went into every fight probably a little underleveled which I think added to the challenge. So I'm not sure how much overleveling takes away from the experience) I also played Piers of the Unknown, which I thought was a very cool little side story. The puzzles are amazing, but outside the final boss I think the other fights in it are a little meh. The creatures it added are so good though especially with the theme of the DLC. Like the creature designers in the whole game just go so hard. I love seeing what fusions of monsters come out to look like to. That's all though folks! Thanks again for listening to my spiel!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really don't do reviews often but this game made such an impression on me that I feel like I kind of have to get my words out. I am one of those people who wants Pokemon to do better by itself but doesn't play anything other than Pokemon, I am part of the problem, so I bought this game because I heard it was good and wanted to try other monster collectors.

I'm extremely glad I did. I have very little content left to do in the game but I can honestly say I really enjoyed my time with this game. As a game, it's really strong on a lot of different levels. Graphically the game is very beautiful and I'm a sucker for pixel stuff as a general rule, so this game was a breath of fresh air. It's stylized very nicely, each area has a distinct visual theming with only a couple of exceptions, and the boss design is really good. The soundtrack is amazing, and it took me a bit to catch on that going into Fusion causes a vocal version of the battle track to play, which is just peak. More games need vocal tracks.

Gameplay wise I found this game to hit a nice sweet spot for me. There's a lot of familiar systems here: you've got a large type chart, you've got AP management, and you've got a wide variety of tools to work with for making sets, and its all done in a way I really enjoy. Type advantage not just being a set of increased or decreased damage numbers (although that is a part), but having unique effects and additional AP recovery made having a wide team of monsters for story fights much more rewarding. AP managment is fun and makes you really think about your turn to turn choices as well as how you build your sets. There's a variety of good and interesting options to choose from with basically every monster, and the typeless stickers help to ensure that no type feels offensively (as in, damage) bad.

A lot of the fights are pretty difficult as well, although sometimes difficult enough that you do wonder a bit if the game is fudging a tad. I don't think it is, but you will have a lot of fights that come down to margins and getting a bad crit or miss can feel really debilitating. I like that the Ranger Captain fights all have a strategy in mind when you do them, and that success matters more on your ability to interact with the strategy rather than grinding past it. There's a really good learning curve to this game where you're getting used to the systems, you start realizing what works and how to counteract these strategies, and the general importance of the Fusion Gauge to your continued success in fights. I do have a minor gripe I would feel remiss not to bring up: the Puppetox fight gives a weird expectation about how Archangel fights are supposed to work. I got the idea that these fights were more along the lines of puzzle fights, so I spent a good amount of time at Hecahedron wondering what I was doing wrong (nothing, basically).

The monster collecting part is also very good. I greatly enjoy any system that doesn't feel like it wants to waste your time, and outside of a couple of really difficult to record monsters in the post-game, it feels really balanced. Remastering tapes being simple and having relatively few frills is also something I thought was a good idea. It vastly simplified a lot of what could be a headache inducing system.

The only part I find significant fault with is the story. The story isn't bad by any means, and I enjoyed it for what it is, but while playing it, I couldn't help but feel that it suffered from being a little too light. The pacing of plot points is very erratic, and you often really do not feel you're making any sort of progress on what the story is actually about. There are some reveals in the post-game that feels honestly more appropriate in the main game itself, and the primary point of conflict is revealed so late that it doesn't feel impactful.

This extends to a lot of the game as well. Characters who have art and feel like they should be important, such as the Doctor Pensby and Clemence immediately exit the story the minute the introduction is over. Very few of the Ranger Captains feel like they have much to them, and that's a shame because they have interesting roles on the island! On the whole, the Rangers don't really impact the story. They don't have any role in the conflict with the Archangels or with Aleph, which feels strange. On the whole, it makes the world feel a little bit less alive than it should be. This even sadly extends to the partner characters, and I was genuinely hoping that the post-game would bring some more characterization and character growth to them but was a bit disappointed to find none.

This isn't a deal breaker really, but it's more a bit of a disappointment. The story is still enjoyable, and even with all of this, I found the ending cutscene genuinely emotional, so there's some good writing chops here, but I found myself wanting less and less salad and more courses to my meal.

Overall, I think this game is very good. It's very well-crafted and thoughtfully designed. I would heartily recommend that people play this, at least in part, to formulate their own opinions on the game and even the stuff I personally found lacking. This game deserves a ton of attention and support. It's that good.\t\t\t\t\t
