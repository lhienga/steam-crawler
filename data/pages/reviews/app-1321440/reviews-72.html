\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery cheery and artful monster team building game. As of review, about to unlock the post-game content.

Aesthetically, I find the game perfect. Great music, monster designs, boss contrasts, and surprising amount of fusions that look great. The visual and sound effects of attacks go really well too. There are a few fusions I've seen look odd, and I dismissed some music at first, growing on me the more events occurred. It's all charming.

Mechanically, 9/10. For a team building game, the 8 move slots of each cassette are very customizable and accessible with the sticker stores. This creates a lot of possibilities in setups, and more so with Fusions and Mind Meld, which combine the two move pools together. This has led to a lot of enjoyment exploring, slowly building better iterations with balances of AP regen, move flexibility, status applications...

Speaking of, two notable differences from most other monster tamer rpgs. AP, you default get two of per turn, and these are spent to use moves. Naturally better moves cost more. The other is status effects are applied to the trainer, not the monster. So if you bury an npc in miss chances, every future monster they switch to will still have it.

This was great, until I discovered Custom Starter and Random Starter. Although there are a ton of ways to effectively use them, each way tends to be a different form of (near) OTK. And they also trigger if the users are switched into mid-battle. They completely derail the AP / Turn economy.

The same mostly goes for Pre-emptive strike, but I hilariously got hard countered by Jagged Edge in one fight. Along with other contact triggers. While concerning, this seems fine enough while still being great for auto-pilot farming normal encounters.\t\t\t\t\t
