\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good game that blends monster-tamer, turn-based battles, and open world into an enchanting and delightful gameplay experience. Though it presents familiar elements from the traditional Pokémon franchise, the refreshing ideas of “humans transforming into monsters”, monster fusion, and sticker customization do make this game stand alone from any other Pokemon-alikes.

Gameplay – Very decent 20–30 hour length. It is all based on a world where people record monsters onto cassette tapes and play those back with cassette players to transform. With the completely customizable sticker system and huge movepool of each monster, you can apply many creative strategies to your build. Those passive effect stickers, various status effects, and transformation switches bring so much fun and unexpected chaos to the fights. Random Starter + Headshot set kind of ruins such experience, but at least it’s quite helpful during post-game 5-star and bootleg grinding ;D Personally, I wouldn’t say I like the puzzle element across the map, but still within my acceptable range.

Graphics and Soundtrack – The pixel art and vibrant colors make the whole world alive, and I do love it. The monster design is a huge plus, creative and distinctive. I was quite fascinated when I first saw the fusion mechanics, and then I realized that YOU CAN FUSE ANY TWO MONSTERS OF THE WHOLE BESTIARY! That’s crazy, as they really sketched over 10,000 combinations! Another standout feature is the groovy retro-futuristic soundtrack that perfectly complements the game’s theme. Some battle music, particularly those with vocals, transient smoothly, making those battles special and immersive. Previously I tried this game from my friend’s library, but for the artbook and soundtrack, I purchased the complete bundle without hesitation XD

Overall, delightful, comfy, and unique gameplay with adorable creatures, innovative mechanics, and strategic depth. Highly recommended to anyone who wants to try a monster-taming game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game surprised the heck out of me with the demo (now unavailable) so much so that it's an easy day 1 purchase for me, and I played it straight to finish, which I rarely do now with games as an adult, maybe playing it on the Steam Deck helps hah.

The combat is turn-based and is interesting, you mostly have a partner with you and you can change the type of beasts that both characters could become leading to a dynamic mix-and-match strategies. Each cassette that holds the beast forms have a set of stickers, those are the aggressive, passive and support moves that the character could use and each stickers have their own rarity with additional effects on them, leading to the possibility of creating some very interesting (even borderline unfair for the enemies) strategies.

Main feature of this game is the fusion mechanic which combines both beasts that your character and your partner currently equipped into 1 mixed beast, combining both their stickers move pool and stats. Love every time this happens as the current soundtrack switches to vocal version and it just fills me with that feeling of "LETS GOOOOOO".

Enemies of various types like beasts, bosses and other characters can be very challenging and requires you to switch and try other strategies, especially the bosses and some character fights when they also fused with their partners. The bosses are some of the most unique designed characters I've seen especially when you first see them in your fight.

This the only game where I feel like I want to go out and record the bootlegs version of the beasts, they're like shinies in Pokemon but with a different type than their original version with a look that matches that new type, this leads to creating more interesting combat strategies and there are mechanics that makes the bootleg hunting/grinding easier.

There are end-game contents and even new game+ contents and gives you the ability to turn on some challenges like a cassette is permanently destroyed if it gets defeated and randomizing all of the beast locations.

All and all I completely love this game and I highly recommend people to at least give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game does some things that are amazing, but it also drops the ball in a ton of ways. First off, the monster designs are just top notch and I struggled to find a single one that I didn't like visually. The way they handled weaknesses and resistances was also really fun and I really preferred their method over other games. The combat was your basic turn-based junk but I did like how you could have over 5+ moves with multiple passives. Finally, the fusion mechanic was fun and I liked how they added to the combat (Even if they were absolutely op at the end)

Now it's time for the negatives which I will put in an easily digestible list.

1. The over-world is boring, small, and lacking any unique dungeons.
2. The designs for the human characters were almost entirely bad. Despite New Wirral being a dumpsite for different realities it looks like everyone came from the same Portland Antifa "protest". I don't mind a different variety of people with their own beliefs and sexuality, but the developers really need to learn about variety.
3. The story is stale, nothing you haven't experienced before.
4. Tons of materials but nothing to spend them on. Also most loot boxes contain anything exciting or useful.

I really had high hopes for this game, but after 40+ hours of playtime I've come to the conclusion that it's just not as good as it's potential.\t\t\t\t\t
