\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: So I just found out the publisher for this Casette Beasts, RawFury, are part of Sweet Baby Inc.'s clients on their main web page. That alone should be a major red flag of what this game is like.

Game is deceptively fun and has a unique take on monster collecting via old video cassettes (hence the title). You are thrown into a world with no escape among other people in the same exodus situation, and eventually go on a journey to find a way back home using the creatures that inhabit the land. But instead of just using them, you fuse with the beasts and use attacks arranged by music genres as damage types, with some genres more effective than others.

Suffice to say, I didn't get very far because I didn't want to support the company itself, given how proud they are to promote a game that is British and also "queer, anticapitalist and multicultural." It's fine to be queer and have the option to pick your pronouns (even though I personally find it replacing the gender option frustrating). But to keep mocking capitalism and even boast about being multicultural when it mocks and vilifies British themes (religion is bad because it makes you think about death, you fight Angels, evil incarnation of Arthur) is just spiteful. Even if one could make a case that a few instances of jabs and commentary on positive themes spun in a negative light, it's hard to ignore a whole laundry list of them, and whether it's deliberate or not.

Behind the fun and cute aesthetic is a mean-spirited game.\t\t\t\t\t
