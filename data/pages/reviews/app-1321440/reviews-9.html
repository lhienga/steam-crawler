\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts is a nice callback to the OG Pokemon monster taming formula except with it's own unique twists. There's more move flexibility and reusability with any move that's learned from your beasts/monsters which can be "peeled" from those monsters and transferred to any other compatible monster. Shiny hunting or "bootleg" hunting actually has an impact on fighting mechanics besides aesthetics which makes for some nice endgame content. And the designs of these monsters feel unique and lively, yet it don't feel like it's designs are copycatting any of the previous monster taming titles. Fusions are an additional layer of complexity to fights that function similar to the fusions in the cartoon network show, Steven Universe. However, I feel like there could've been more thought put into the designs of the fusions as most feel samey and unremarkable.
Every fight is a double battle as opposed to the usual single battles in Pokemon so team dynamics can have a lot of room for exploration on building beasts a certain way to best bring about battle harmony. Additionally, one person in the double battle can be catching or "recording" wild beasts while the other can distract the opposing side from targeting the vulnerable person.
All in all, this game, while still giving a callback to the old school monster taming formula, offers a fresh new take on it, and so I definitely recommend this game for anyone interested in this category even in the slightest.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought I'd love this but I got like 30 mins in the other day and couldn't handle it anymore. The weirdest part is that I can understand *why* a lot of these design decisions were made, I just can't stand them in this game.

- Moving is weirdly mushy/floaty compared to other games with the exact same movement (Played Crystal Project right after this, and it's much better)

- They put in BOTW stamina, but stamina usage is so high that you can only run for about a second before having to walk for 5. It's worse than not being able to run at all because it "feels" awful knowing i could be moving faster but can't. BOTW stamina works because the slow movement makes you plan your path through the world. This game is good looking, but with the top-down perspective i'm not seeing any beautiful vistas on the horizon to seek out anytime soon.

- The writing is marvel quality, almost verbatim from the first NPC and PC: "Wow I'm catching monsters! In cassette tapes!? That just happened!" and I absolutely hate it.

- The music has loud lyrics, like TWEWY, which is awesome, but idk for some reason i really hate it here. TWEWY has really simple areas so the music helps you zone out while moving around. This game has pretty areas and doesn't need lyrics, so it winds up being Too Much.

-On top of that, NPCs all have a loud one-word noise they make when you talk to them. It normally doesn't bother me in other games, and i can turn it off, but this along with the last two points added up to me dreading anytime I had to interact with anyone because the dialog/music/text is all a big jumbled mess. Text noises and lyrics need to be off by default.

I can't even talk about the monster battles because I've only done a couple of simple ones. The game is too annoying for me to wanna play to the point where it gets good 😕
Same with movement, I'm sure i'll eventually get some kinda movement abilities that make stamina more fun to play with, but at the start it feels terrible.

First impressions are important.\t\t\t\t\t
