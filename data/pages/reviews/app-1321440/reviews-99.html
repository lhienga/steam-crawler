\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is, first and foremost, a Pokemon-like that RESPECTS YOUR TIME.

Want to try a different moveset? Freely swap moves around between any/all of your monsters. At any time. For free.

Tired of hoofing it around? Fast-travel's enabled from the very start, go nuts.

The combat is also interested in more strategic depth than just plain STAB and type weaknesses. Strong type attacks against weak types inflict nominal maluses on the weak type, and weak type attacks inflict nominal bonuses on the strong type. I say "nominal" because type shifting moves are incredibly common and used by just about everyone, so what appears to be a bonus inflicted on you NOW can rapidly turn into a malus if you're not paying attention!

Also, the soundtrack SLAPS.

And the writing is good as hell.

And might also be linked to Lenna's Inception which is also fantastic in its own right??

Basically buy this game, you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best monster catching games I've ever played; it's a breath of fresh air in a genre filled with samey and unremarkable games. Whether it be the battle mechanics or story decisions, Cassette Beasts is filled to the brim with brilliant game design and world-building decisions that lead to an enjoyable experience from start to finish. No feature feels out of place, and the in-game settings allow you to tailor the difficulty perfectly to your tastes.

It absolutely helps that the monster designs themselves are memorable and fun as well, which is something that many other games of this genre miss the mark on. The team behind this game has managed to create a cohesive cast of monsters and characters in a world comprised of junk that has fallen through the cracks of universes.

I highly recommend this game to any fans of the Pokémon or Persona series, as it shares many elements with those franchises whilst also establishing its own concepts. The developers seem dedicated to supporting this game in the future, considering they have free updates, merchandise, and paid DLC in the pipeline. So, while the game is already jam-packed with content, there seems to be even more on the way.

Final consensus; 10/10, a must-play for fans of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite possibly one of the best monster-taming RPGs I have ever played. All the monster designs are great, the open world feels expansive and is genuinely fun to traverse, and the game itself is pretty impressive visually. Cassette Beasts also has stellar combat; running off the AP system, every ability a monster has is tied to an AP cost, so stronger moves tend to cost more AP and weaker moves less AP. This creates a very satisfying rhythm in combat, where you have to plan out your AP usage accordingly to utilized your strongest skills. Contributing to the fun of the combat, each monster skill is represented by a sticker, which can be freely removed and reallocated to any other compatible monster. This allows for some very fun build craft, and plenty of broken movesets. The story of the game is nothing crazy, but it's engaging enough to keep you moving. Story really shines, however, with the companions in the game (two in particular). And, of course, the game has a frankly incredible OST.
This game feels fresh and fun in a genre that is often more than content to fall into trappings of a particular monster taming RPG series. Cassette Beasts is a truly incredible game. I have fallen for the game, head over heels, and I cannot possibly recommend it enough.\t\t\t\t\t

As soon as you start the game, you are hit with a serenity of warm music, like the days of old before taxes, before work, before responsibility. The game's music direction is superb. The game mechanics rewards thoughtful play with types going above and beyond what Pokemon ever did. Every single type goes beyond type counters, and every type will react differently to every other type. As an example, resonance between metal and glass, your fire buffing your wind enemies, your poison hitting metal giving the enemy poison thornmail. This game brings you back to the old RPG days before all the shovel-ware low-effort games you see today, and evolves the monster-taming genre with its evolution of the old-outdated pokemon type system.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just finished this game and I just adored it! The themeing is SO good and I love that i keep finding new little details that really hammer in the theme they're going for. I think the mechanics are fun and the monsters have fun designs. The battling is really nice, theres just so much that can happen to change how you need to fight especially compared to other games in this genre. I loved that every fight could be completely unpredictable. The music is great. I liked all of the characters. The story was interesting enough. I think if you like monster taming games you're really going to like this one. The whole concept and theming is just too good to pass up on. I plan to go back and finish up some stuff that I didn't do before finishing the story, but I'm very satisfied with the game. I only have a few things that I personally would like to see changed and maybe these options exist and I just didnt see them. 1.) I find switching out your party to be really cumbersome. I think switching and adding monsters to your party should all be in the same menu. Instead you have to put a monster in storage, back out of that screen, and then go into your storage to add from there. 2.) Even after a monster dies all of the status effects keep taking place and sometimes it can go on for a long time if theres alot of status effects. I think if a monster is dead just dont play all of the status effect animations after. I know they also effect the trainers, so i get it from that perspective but the ones in the wild should just die when they're dead lol it does waste a lot of time. 3.) personally i just couldnt figure out the sorting system for the stickers. I hit sort but it doesnt really keep to do.. anything? Maybe I'm just missing something. I felt lost in that menu most of the time and was just attaching whatever to the monsters so i didnt have to keep scrolling forever through that menu. But these are all kind of just simple quality of life things and dont really have any effect on how much I enjoyed this game. I look forward to any future updates or sequels that come from this game.\t\t\t\t\t
