\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those times where Steam needs a neutral option.

I think Cassette Beasts is a game with a lot of potential. I liked the visual and audio style choices they made. The setting was OK, but I did find some of the choices a little jarring. The gameplay was OK. Although technically the gameplay would be better than Pokemon, I strangely found it less engaging.

Other people would probably enjoy it, but I gave up fairly early. Firstly, I found the game too hard. I ended-up turning all the difficulty settings to lowest, but was still not at a level I found enjoyable. Perhaps the systems were too opaque for me and I was not picking-up on how to play? Regardless, at least one full party wipe every play session was not fun. I also found myself lost and confused, often finding myself confused on where I was supposed to be going, or how I was supposed to get there.

This was a game I expected to, and wanted to, love. Unfortunately, it was not a game for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn a genre dominated by a singular series of games, Cassette Beasts is a massive breath of fresh air. Every other monster taming game more or less plays the same; Electric type move hits a Water type Creature expect some massive damage by way of super effective moves, but whats interesting for Cassette Beasts is that same interaction also applies differing buffs or de-buffs depending on what hit. The game is full of surprising game-play twists that gives new life to (in my opinion is) a stale genre

Lets take a look at that prior interaction, lighting vs water, in Cassette Beasts conductive is applied meaning whenever a future lightning move hits anything on the battlefield a small portion of lightning damage is applied to the affected creature. There are plenty effects some are similar in effect like Fire vs Plant (this should be obvious). Want to lower defenses on that earth type, you should use a metal type move. Dose your opponent like to use plant type attacks? Try using a poison type monster to gain a damage up buff.

Lets do just one more, Fire vs Plastic, what buff or de-buff is caused? Trick question, this interaction is a transmutation, the plastic type is melted by the fire and becomes a poison type. Sadly there are only 4 such transmutation interactions but there is a typing centered around transmutation, glitter, anything hit by it will become glitter type and any glitter type hit by any move will become the same type as that move.

I've only talked about type interaction up till now but rest assured there are more twists in the battle system to talk about like Fusion, Trainer KO's, expanded status effects, Recording (Catching), Coatings, Walls, Bootlegs, Stickers, and a probably bit more on top of that.

Overall I find the game to be charming but its not without its faults, for as fun and entertaining the battle system is it wont help when it comes to the slog that is post game. Overall Its a fantastic game with at least a 30 hour main campaign/story and satisfying fights sprinkled throughout, just be wary of post-game.

TLDR: Good game, good monster battles, slog postgame.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a masterclass addition to the monster tamer genre. One thing I can never get over with any monster tamer not named Pokémon or Digimon are forgettable or bland monster designs, which I feel Cassette Beasts gets right. The designs are delightful and memorable.

The combat and ancillary features are well designed, too. All combat is done with a partner of yours, which lends itself to interesting strategies a la Pokémon's double battles. On top of that, typing interactions go further than simple damage modifiers, deepening the strategy. Fusing is also really fun to use. Each monster combination has its own sprite, so look forward to 16k+ fusions in the game!

The music in this game slaps, too. The battle themes all have lyrical versions that come in at the most hype moments and go so damn hard.

I could keep going, but I'd rather end my review here so I can play some more. GET THE GAME.\t\t\t\t\t
