\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not that similar to Pocket Monsters™. There were definitely moments at the beginning where I thought, "well that's just [x] from Pokemon," but that went away pretty quickly. The core systems are very different and (for the most part) better. There's a lot of QOL improvements compared to Pokemon, like enemy level scaling, a built-in False Swipe mechanic, and fast travel that's available before the three-quarter mark of the game. I really enjoyed the soundtrack, particularly the battle themes. The visual style is yet another reminder that good 2/2.5D will always be better than mediocre 3D.

I did find the dialogue to be pretty bad at some points, such as in the (optional) romance scenes, which were definitely Fire Emblem: Awakening tier (i.e. not good). The main story was just okay, though I guess this is an open world game. The final boss fight was a bit of a headscratcher given how gimmicky (in a good way) the other boss fights were. The post-game is super grindy if you want to get every achievement, as there are only a few non-generic sidequests before you're just doing repetitive fetch and bounty quests for the rest of time.

Still, it's a nice game overall. It definitely reminded me of a happier time. A time before responsibilities, work, and Gen 7.\t\t\t\t\t

- Combat that demands more than the one brain cell normally required to remember type advantages.
