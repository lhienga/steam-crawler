\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think I've ever written a Steam review before, but this game is a must buy! I went in out of pure curiosity after seeing a recommendation online - with no idea what to expect - only to find myself completely obsessed.

The combat is intuitive and fun, with a level of customisability I haven't seen before. The characters have wonderful designs that you can tell came from a place of passion. All of the game's companions have interesting personalities that are really allowed to develop on account of the abundance of companion-unique dialogue. The story (which I still haven't even finished by the way, I just had to get a recommendation out) is super compelling, subverting your expectations and playing around with some really cool concepts.

I found that the difficulty of the puzzles was a tad low (at least so far), but that this didn't detract from the clever ideas and designs put into play; in fact, it felt like the devs struck a kind of perfect balance which always kept you from toiling for ages to find a solution, but still often made you actually think. Adding to this, due to the endless options for battle loadouts, combat difficulties can always be overcome with a little creativity, which is really rewarding. This game felt super friendly to all skill levels/levels of play!

I could keep listing pros, but really, I think it's best I hold my tongue and let you craft your own experience with the game. I honestly think the tiny Bytten Studio deserve some serious respect for achieving this with such a small team. I wish you guys the absolute best in any future projects - I'm definitely gonna stick around for them!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI saw a lot of people recommending this game as a Pokémon like. It was enough to sell me on the game, but now that I've played it, I actually take issue with this description. This game is far more than an indie clone, it is inspired by that genre of game but does more to be innovative and unique to the series than any main line Pokémon game has ever tried to do.

From charming sprites, great and well implemented dynamic music, interesting monster design, a refreshing take on type matchups, good characterisation and a story that isn't forgettable, this game would be worth the price of a triple A game that a certain, Nintendo Entity might produce and then some. So much so that I actually feel a little guilty for buying it in a sale!

Pros:
- Refreshing take on combat, Type Advantages and Disadvantages are far more 'strategic'
- Great sense of humour interwoven within the world, from funny monster names to the move 'Bad Joke'
- Music is well composed, catchy and dynamically adapts to whether there are any fusions up
- Sprite Work is well done
- Cassette/Retro theme is well executed, and fun to experience. Items and mechanics are appropriately named
- Remaster (Evolve) criteria are unique and interesting
- Metroidvania style world exploration with abilities allowing you to explore more and more

Cons:
- Game can't decide what type of achievements it wants to give you, you get 5 achievements in the space of 10 minutes for breathing, and then there's one that will require near enough 100 hours of gameplay
- End game options could be a little more diverse to make grinding 5* a little more invigorating
- Some movement bugs, one of the stations doesn't like you going right straight after exiting, one of the other stations sometimes won't let you leave it because the loading zone (ironically) doesn't load
- Dying feels very punishing, especially as a lot of the captains/archangels won't clue you into their gimmick until you fight them, leading you to be trapped in a very frustrating situation. It never looks good to see 8000 pieces of material disappear.

All in all, this game is amazing and I've been absolutely addicted to it since I picked it up, the game oozes love and effort from every orifice, and if you pick this up as an RPG fan, you will not be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is Pokemon, but the devs care about more than just money.

Actually, scratch that. Saying that this game is "Not Pokemon" would be a disservice to all the things that make this game great. It has so much more flexibility of builds than Pokemon ever did (even if a certain build is a bit too OP). It has an art style that is sharply distinct from anything Pokemon will ever use, and individual sprites for 14000+ fusions of any two Pokemon monsters, which is deeply astounding to me as someone who did a couple of sprites for Pokemon Infinite Fusion. It messes with the established formulas in clever, unique ways, such as a type chart that deals in status effects and a mana-esque AP system instead of the PP system of Pokemon.

Of course, all of this would be for naught if it didn't stick the landing. But it does. So much heart has clearly been put into every aspect of this game. The music exceeds even the standard set by the game's name, although I will say that the tracks could be a bit longer in some scenarios. Still - if only more games would have vocals kick in like this! The story and characters are actually a bit compelling - I don't think anyone expected the next Hades, etc. from this game's story, but I still think it's worth paying attention to. And what I mentioned about the gameplay and art is only the bits I could put into words. tldr: Play the damn game.

Did I mention this was mostly made by two people?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly can not suggest the game highly enough. It is FAR more than a Pokemon clone or anything of the sorts. It is truly it's own beast and a magnificent one at that. From a creative and well thought out type chart and matchups, along with far more interesting interactions between those types, to the utterly AMAZING soundtrack. Seriously, this is one of the best OSTs of any game PERIOD, indy or not. The world is fun and rather diverse to explore and the characters are just as sharp as the music.
The only real flaws I have with the game is that the platforming is jank at times, but never a deal breaker and the overall main plot is solid, but nothing super great, but the individual stories for your teammates are all damn good as well.
Seriously if you like monster tamer games, a soundtrack that SLAPS like no body else or just games in general, give Cassette Beasts a try. You will NOT regret it.\t\t\t\t\t
