\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette beats is essentially: what if we throw pokemon (base), digimon (in the landscapes and some creature design seems influenced), and monster rancher (nature of music media being connected to the creatures thematically) into a blender .

Make that an issekai.

Add a complicated type system that sounds like a child talking out loud (I.E. If you hit plastic type with a fire type move, it permanently becomes a poison type and begin doing poison damage).

Have a player character and follower characters who act as an ensemble cast to talk about the nature of making art and art taking a life of its own.

AND make pokemon a 3d platformer where you can glide, climb walls, and use other moves to traverse. THE ONLY COMPLAINT: sometimes platforming is can be janky or your followers knock a block of pressure switches. You can also get creative jank (i.e. glider with float upgrade + tapping the vine ball in air = practically flying until you run out of stamina. Which is fun by the time you get the peices for it)

Another thing the game does well is reward the natural grind that comes with these games by having plenty of rewards to improve you capture rate, customize you trainer character with basic skill stats.

The games map seems designed that there is always "1 more thing to do" in the map section next to you, so the game has a loop. I still havent beat the game or touched dlc, so there are still mechanics I haven't used yet. (havent unlocked the bulletin board for more rewards and endgame content.

This is a masterstroke in the creature collector rpg genre talking alot from the classics. It really is a full package where it seems everything in the game has been thought about, fleshed out, and interconnected properly and it was refreshing.

A Pokemon-like, Except You Become The Monsters You Collect/Record.

I didn't think much of Cassette Beasts initially. I picked it up during the sale simply because I like the artstyle and the beasts' design. I was really turned down by the whole "turning into the beast you collect" gimmick. I prefer my creatures-collecting games to have boi and gurls running around rather than me turning into them. Yet, the game gradually wins me over with its core mechanic and the world design. I would even argue the leading figure of this turn-based sub-genre Pokemon can learn a thing or two from this fun indie game. The skill mix-up, duo combination, elemental interaction, and the simple but effective world exploration, all intertwine perfectly to make Cassette Beasts a really competent game in the genre.

Things I Like About Cassette Beasts:

1. Level-up Mechanic
Instead of leveling up individual cassette beasts, you level up your character and the stat YOU carry. The cassette beast you transform into will inherit those numbers, combing with its own. This is a great and time-saving design that I am confused why haven't more games from this sub-genre. It solves a major problem I always have when I play through these type of games. Whenever I capture late-game monsters, I can't really use them because it has low level comparing to my current team comp. I have to reorganize my established team comp for the new monster and grind some mobs for experience. All of this is rather time-consuming and honestly, kills off the pacing for me. Of course Cassette Beasts has the same problem, but the method it provides drastically reduces the tediousness. Because the chosen beast inherits your character level, you can bring just about anyone into battles without worrying too much about being one-shot. This design makes me want to try out more team comps even after I finish the game.

2. The Open World
Think of Monster Sanctuary but in a 3D, top-down setting. This design has the same vibe of metroidvania which I enjoy a lot, and the world design really reflect on that idea. Each time you encounter an impossible roadblock, you make a mental note to remind yourself to come back here after you get new ability. And how exactly do you get new abilities? By collecting the right creature! This is a design I find fascinating about Cassette Beasts and Monster Sanctuary. It incentivizes you to collect as many creatures as possible, basically aligning with one theme of this sub-genre. The open world of Cassette Beasts is also full of little secrets and puzzles. Many obstacles encourage you to use the abilities you have. What's more interesting is that the game allows you to sequence-break a lot. Like a chest which is too high to reach. Obviously you need either the Vine Ball or Flight ability to get it, but you can also just take a box and use its tiny pixel to hang on the ledge and somehow jump up(don`t know why though).

3. Combat
Wow, where do I even begin with? The elemental interaction is quite detailed. It's not just simple "fire deals more damage to grass, but weaker to water". It is more like "fire burns grass causing burned status effect, while fire vaporizes water causing the steam to heal water." The skill mix-up is also quite rich. From passive skills like AP Starter(give you 1 AP at the start of battle) and Custom Starter(Use the next skill on the list for 0 AP at the start of battle), to active skills like Wonderful7(give random debuff to target multiple times) and Echolocation(give you Multishot, and is automatically used at the start of the battle), there are so many ways to build your ideal cassette beasts. Last but not least, your partner's role is important. Two damage source is the norm, but it is more interesting to customize his/her/its build. A support build focusing on healing and removing status is quite strong for tougher battle, a Glitter build to completely randomized everyone's element suitable to test your build flexibility, or a build designed to force other Rangers to change their cassettes all the while damaging them.

4. Other Compliments
The music and the general atmosphere are really nice, especially in the Cafe.
Bizarre design of all the Archangels.
I really like the design of all the cassette beasts.
Each partner has his/her/its own interesting quests.
Viola my beloved~

Things I Dislike About Cassette Beasts:

Other Complaints
Like I said, I would rather have a sentient cloud follow me around than become a sentient cloud.
Bootleg hunting is a bit grindy.
I wish some evolution condition can be clearer.
