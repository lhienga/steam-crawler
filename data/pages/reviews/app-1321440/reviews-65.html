\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm 6.3 hours in and this is great! It's story is really intriguing and it feels darker than any other games that play like it. Not darker like a 12 year old edgelord, but more like there's an undertone of sadness. I don't know how much is considered a spoiler so I'll stop there. Also this game fixes a big problem that games like Pokemon have, All battles here are 2v2. In Pokemon, the two builds that everyone has to use in single player is either a fast glass cannon or a beefy heavy hitter that can take a hit. In Cassette Beasts using a stat changing move is not detrimental and wasting damage because your other monster could be attacking that turn, not only that, your stat change could actually be comboing with your other monster. There's an exponential increase in depth because of things like this. Also typing isn't really bonus damage in a rock paper scissors way, it's special effects. A wind attack used on a fire type "blows them out" reducing the fire types damage output. There's a ton of interactions like that. It's very interesting and unique. I'm no where close to finishing the game but I can already tell it's amazing and definitely worth playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette beasts is an okay play. It isn't revolutionary, but it is charming and the basic mechanics are simple enough that if you've played any amount of Pokemon you already know what you're doing. While most of the designs aren't ultimately striking or memorable, they're pleasing, cohesive, and none of them seem out of place when put next to another. The amount of quests you're given makes it feel like you usually have something to do, and even though I never found myself remembering the lay of the land the terrain was still somewhat intuitive and never overly irritating or annoying to explore. The character arcs, while somewhat rushed, are fine: every character has something that someone else might like.

However, while not unsatisfying, Cassette Beasts isn't innovative. It isn't new, it doesn't introduce any new concepts besides the initially interesting archangels, which are shunted off as "human ideas" and not explained in any truly meaningful way besides offhand exposition. I won't find myself playing Cassette Beasts again after I 100% it. The 19K fusions are impressive, sure, but none of them are particularly fun or different once you've gotten around twelve. The deck movement is also annoying to go through, because you can't storage lead 'mons and if you want to shift to a different partner you have to shuffle your party around to make sure you don't leave behind one of your 'mons with them. Some fusions are also incredibly busted compared to others, so you might run into a fusion that flattens you if it has multi-hit moves (which are utterly cracked in conjunction with fusion power) or is simply faster than you and can hit you with several rounds of "desperation" before you can land anything. Similarly, the effects that come from type interactions are interesting, but they're difficult to remember in any capacity and it's basically just better to ignore them in favor of double-smacking, multi-shotting and multi-hitting everything in your way.
The story, while not the focus, was also boring. I got hit with the classic "you thought it was something we could interestingly explain in universe? SIKE! it's an allegory for the human mind, GET PRANK'D IDIOT!!!" which is like, fine. Whatever. But the game also makes no real effort to explain itself beyond "by believing it becomes real", but that isn't explored interestingly or explored really at all. The game would benefit in an unbelievable way from story-centric lore beyond "we're trapped, waaaahh", because its concept actually IS interesting if engaged correctly!

The game is also imperfect, if in an irritating way and not a reprehensible one. This game has no dialogue options- it was buttons to progress the current tree you are on and I would have found speaking to the characters much less annoying if any choice you were given actually mattered, which they did not. It has less interactivity than a Pokemon game itself. In the same conversation I got FIVE different dialogue choices that all lead to the same thing: the choices were basically "yes" "definitely" or "absolutely", except they weren't because I only got two choices and they were basically worded exactly the same. It was genuinely insulting, and I ended up hating the characters because of it.

TL;DR: Overall, Cassette Beasts is fine, and I'm just nitpicking. It's an acceptable time sink even if I wouldn't play it over fangames like Pokemon Reborn, it is worth the $20 price tag. The gameplay is engaging for someone who likes the typical Pokemon RPG-make, even if it can be grindy and annoying to other people. The designs are eye-catching even if not iconic, and I'd suggest it if you find yourself in want of a monster RPG and around 30 hours to kill. It's not a bad game at all, and the only reason I find myself so unhappy with it is because I feel that it could've been so much more.\t\t\t\t\t

Splendid
The elephant in the room is Pokemon. As a great lover of Pokemon I can't help but compare and contrast them.
Cassette Beasts has so many changes from Pokemon that it would be wrong to call it a clone. It clearly has its own design goals.

Experimentation is one of those design departures. With Pokemon it is quite expensive to experiment. Leveling up different Pokemon takes time. Learning a new move means an old one is (mostly) forever gone. TMs can only be used once, depending on the game. In Cassette Beasts your character and the beast's level are separate so even a fresh beast doesn't need to be grinded up to be at a reasonable power level. You never lose access to moves, when one is replaced it gets put in your inventory. Able to be put back on your beast or a different beast whenever you want. This means you can try different combinations to your hearts content, or at least try things out instead of being afraid of losing one of your moves to try something new out.
There is something lost with these changes. Pokemon making it harder to switch Pokemon gives you a greater connection with the ones you do stick with, so the value of this change will change from person to person.

Complexity is another design change. Pokemon is wary to add more complexity to its default game systems. Cassette beasts add several layers of complexity while also keeping clarity. These layers are numerous; double battles, action points, beast fusion, type coatings, player and beast health, and type advantages giving unique statuses instead of double damage. These all are more to keep track of, however it never felt overwhelming and the game always gives you the tools to quickly double check things so you don't need to memorize anything. As someone who has been hungering for more complexity in my monster catching game I welcome these changes.

Calling Cassette Beasts "better Pokemon" is kind of derivative as it isn't even trying to be Pokemon. However, for many lovers of the genre this is a breath of fresh air, and highly welcome and recommended.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI always see this game compared to Pokemon but really, it always had a more Digimon feel to me. You're a young adult sent to another world full of creatures some of which have branching evolution, the red head has the air type, the characters even wonder out loud whether only a few moments or the same amount of time has passed back home in their own worlds similar to how time worked in the original digimon.

For the game itself, its really fun. All my cons are really just nitpicks or way I feel the creators could build on the game if they ever do a sequel. It's an obvious love letter to Pokemon, Digimon and being a kid in the 80's and 90's. There's so many things it would be cool if they expanded on but over all I don't think there's a single real complaint I have about the game and would recommend it to anyone interested in creature collection games. The world is more interesting that anything I would have expected\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLets get the bad stuff outta the way first. The game is easy for the most part. Specifically the trainer AI battles are way to easy. Rival monster recorders don't use items or even switch out when presented with a type disadvantage, neither do they make good use of the fusion mechanic. Even the "Ranger Captains," which kinda act as gym leaders, don't make use of the item system the player has access too or even fusion with their partners until their last two beasts; Hell almost all of the enemy trainer's you battle will only have one or two monsters against the player's 6 monsters cap. I would LOVE to see the trainer battles take in account "repeated moves used by opponent" and have timely "switches," an example would be:

+ They are fighting a player with a creature that has hit him with certain type move repeatedly.
- Then the Ai should know that this is their "go-to move" and they should switch into a countering beast, as it will counter it, if available.
+ Or if a player has over buffed themselves with power, they should be able to switch into something to dispell it or apply accuracy/uni debuffs. or can buff their own dodge
+ In the very least, the Ai should know when the player has clearly used a move that it is weak too and switch to a beast that is atleast normally affected by it (obviously it shouldn't switch EVERY time a situation is brought up, just make it another option for the Ai so it doesn't just sit there taking it.)


Or

+ There are two "supportive" beasts out on the battle field on the Ai side.
- One of them should switch into a damage oriented beast.

And then you'll have creative scenario's like this:

+ Due to a switch, the Ai is in a good position to tank a hit based on the player's previous moves with their current creature. However a beast in the Ai's party is low on health.
- They should use an item to heal it or a rewind on a previously defeated beast.

The Ai should be able to defuse and shouldn't be so predictable with it always fusioning their last two beasts.

+ if they are getting hit with a sleep debuff they should "split it" and defuse like how a player would.

Different reasons for the Ai TO fuse would help make them more unpredictable as well:

+ if they are unfused, but BOTH have too many debuffs, they could fuse, and use new leaf on themselves, if one of them has the move.

+ if the player has commonly used a fire type move and the Ai has a wind type beast, They should fuse regardless of the other Ai's beast.

That being said, not every NPC needs to be advanced EVERYtime, some need to be dumber. They could code these behaviors to an intrinsic IQ value like some pokemon hacks do. The Higher the IQ, the higher the chance of these behaviors occuring in a given NPC battle.

That being said, this game is awesome so far. Unlike other "monster battlers" every single creature feels unique with different abilties that you earn from them and different amount of move slots and different Action point counters and skills.

This is straight up more fun then any mainline Pokemon game I've played lmao. The combination of every creature FEELING unique along with its own element chart separate from other games and with different stats from creature to creature, REALLY make this game be its own thing. Even the designs have left me thoroughly satisfied and it was hard to pick favorites out of them. Alot of the designs I liked more then current monster battler design. However it's not a very difficult game at all. I kinda wish the Ai was harder and did more things the player does to even it out. I've never had that goofy moment like I did in Pokemon soul-silver where I was spamming heals and Lance did the same thing with his dragonnite. Some other elite four members would have a small chance of switching into a better type of advantage as well. NPCs should be given the same tools as the players in places it makes sense. The Ranger Captains definitely could use items as storyline wise its kinda goofy that the player is the only one who thought to use them in battle. Alright NPC battles rant over, this game is superior in every way to every other monster battler I've played. I look forward to seeing what they do with this!

If you were convinced in not buying it because of anything I said here, your doing yourself a disservice. It is still an amazing game and its not like it doesn't get challenging here or there. Its a hundred percent better and more refreshing then any monster battler, mainline or otherwise.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo say this game is good is such a vast understatement..

Cassette Beasts made me feel a little emotional when I started playing it, a Creature Catcher that did not insult my age while still making me feel the Nostalgia I have craved from GameFreak since Pokemon Black 2. The work and effort the devs put into making this game shines through from the very start.

The highlights:

Combat is fluid, simple and new. Instead of 2x/4x weakness and resistance, the types affect each other via changing the other Beast's type or adding a buff/debuff that is vital in battle. The catching system is neat, it requires strategic thinking and instead of capturing and "enslaving" the beast you actually record it and it still stays on the field. Music is nothing but bangers and the maps are well designed. I love the romance options in the game and the Fusion system makes me excited to try new combinations.

Give this game a try, support these devs, this is better than anything the Billion dollar Titan has put out in years.\t\t\t\t\t
