\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game on a whim, following my love of similar JRPG games that shan't be mentioned in this review, because I feel that it would do the entire project a disservice. The innovation, heart, and soul woven into the experience that is Cassette Beasts isn't something that will soon leave me.

The story, while not especially standout, stands on its own and uses the mechanics of the world to build further mechanisms of storytelling (passive or active). Some of the Ranger Captains at first come off as atypical, but the more you delve into the nature of the world in which you've been dropped, the more sense of their station can be made. Their individual fusion typing grows with the taste of personality you get from each, as well as the ancillary characters come to flesh out the world around them.

I could gush about this game more and more, but it would be an absolute waste of review space if I didn't even dip my toes into the music of this game. Easily some of the best crafted pieces I've listened to in a long time all come from this game. Something that I read in regards to the music of this game that struck a chord with me is, "I didn't know that something I'd never listened to could evoke such a sense of nostalgia in me, but it did."

Treat yourself to this experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can not recommend this game enough. With Pokemon being a dominating force and setting the precedent for the "catch them all" category, this game truly shines through with the percentages speaking for itself. Despite the small size, there is a lot of content especially for a small indie game between the main portion of the game, collecting all 150ish cassettes, and a really engaging story before you include the 5 side stories from the main crew you'll meet throughout the game. The soundtrack is INCREDIBLE, so much so I find myself coming back just to listen to the cafe theme and the fusion theme from time to time. They even iron out a lot of problems that other monster catchers have struggled with, including the types which make sense and even offer an explanation (example being water corrodes metal, lowering both ranged and melee defense), and offering an explanation that helps it stick without referring to the type chart that is handily applied. My favorite part is the bootleg system, which allows you to find EVERY beast in another type, including implementing a method to force bootlegs. Not to mention it is difficult (but not impossible) to accidentally kill off a a bootleg because every battle is a double battle and recording (capture system) automatically preserves the beast at 1 health so you can keep trying to catch it.

Overall, this game is incredible and with pier of the unknown coming out (a small section that offers an additional 10 or so beasts, worth the price and accessible before end stage content) I am still excited for new updates, dlc, or another game from this company. If you like monster catchers, this should be on your bucket list

PS: Do not worry about the double battle feature, it is implemented nicely and flows with the story/game welll.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuch a fantastic game, solves all my issues with common design tropes in the monster taming genre, with some of the solutions being extremely fun twists, its like, subversive... and even when you think you know it all, it throws some new fun stuff at you. The monster designs and even the moves are honestly great, I'd suggest going in blind, since the game does a darn good job at not being a wiki-game.
More specific points:
-Say goodbye to grinding your individual creatures. They all inherit your level, and combine that with their star rating, which only needs to level up 5 times per evolution stage
-Good difficulty control options that can be changed any time.
-The sticker system is genius for moves, letting you really customize and experiment without fear of wasting anything
-THE TYPE CHART OH MY GOD THE TYPE CHART. the games combat is based more on status effects applied by chemical reactions (i.e dousing fire types with water, plastic types releasing toxic gas when melted by fire, plastic conducting electricity thus giving it the multitarget status letting it hit your whole team)
-The game doesn't have long as heck move animations (cough pkmn cough)
-Fantastic story and characters. like, as for the writing? Fantastic writing, storyline has some really fun premises where you'll kinda get the idea of where its going but not to the SUPER predictable kinda sense. its as open world as pokemon scarlet and violet tried to be, with several main questlines that eventually merge into one, but... the scaling is like, way better! The characters are quite lovely
-I'll say it again, the creatures are lovely to encounter. I love all of their designs...
NOTES:
-Do not turn off the music or the lyrical tracks, they're adaptive so you arent always hearing them, they give this game a lot of identity
-Yes, the game has a stamina bar... its a bit odd at first, but it makes more sense as you progress, and becomes a non-issue.
-The DLC isn't that amazing. Get it if you can, it fits perfectly fine, but its nothing to like, absolutely write home about. A new boss, a few new creatures. Solid DLC, but not very open.
-Online multiplayer has yet to come as im writing this review, but its in the plans
