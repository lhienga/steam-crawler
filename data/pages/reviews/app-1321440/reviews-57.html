\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a huge innovation on the monster catching/battling formula. I don't agree with all of the changes, but this game is such a huge step forward that it should be the new baseline. On top of that, the character designs are great, the soundtrack's nothing but bangers, and the story is engaging.

One example of a great mechanic: the player now has a character level that determines your monsters' overall strength. As you fight with a monster in your party, it also gains stars (individual experience), which unlocks new stickers (moves & passives) and allows it to evolve. With the traditional formula, you'd encounter a new monster you liked, but have to train it up 20 levels - and who was going to invest that kind of time without knowing if it's good? But it's really easy to grab a new monster here, get it enough stars that it's functional, and then try it out.

I have minor gripes, but they're things you wouldn't notice unless you were already deeply invested in the game. I hope there's a Cassette Beasts 2 in the works.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fun, but its not nearly perfect like many reviews would lead you to believe. The monster designs are almost always extremely mediocre, with a couple exceptions. The most interesting designs in the game are the archangels, since they all have different art styles and aesthetics.

When it comes to the battle system, I'm conflicted. It adds alot more elements to the fights with the elemental interactions, but sometimes it can get a bit overwhelming and make you want to ignore it entirely and just brute force your way through the game. My brain's probably been decayed due to Pokemon's easiness, though, so that might just be me. Speaking of that, the puzzles in this game are ridiculously easy. It's kind of annoying.

The characters are okay, the story is fine. One thing I will say is that the story elements all feel very loosely tied together, oftentimes seeming like multiple stories were just smashed together without care.

Also, you can get to higher level areas in the game earlier than you're supposed to very easily using the magnetism ability. Just stand on a magnetic box and use the ability, and you can fly on it.

Overall, it's a 6.8.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game blew me away in so many aspects.

Mechanically it feels like an evolution on the pokemon formula in every way:
- Type interactions are interesting and offer much more depth to the combat than simple +/- damage
- The ability to collect and swap moves out on all of your mons lets you customize them in very unique ways
- Companions not only add depth to the combat by making you use a team of 2 mons, but also connect the out of combat progression back to the combat in a very clean way
- The open world is neatly designed, and exploration provides a very meaningful axis of progress in multiple ways
- Shinies can be seen in the open world, and each mon has 13(?!) shiny variants that actually provide gameplay differences
- There's a decent amount of post game content, as well as "custom game options" that allow you to create challenge runs on subsequent playthroughs

On top of all of this, the OST is something else entirely. I think the songs themselves are wonderful, but also the audio design and implementation really helps them shine. Each song has an instrumental version and a version with lyrics that seamlessly transition between each other depending on certain conditions. For example: in town, the background track is instrumental when outdoors, and transitions to the lyrical version when you enter a building. It's a little touch that I think adds so much to the character of this game, and it's a great example of how much thought and care went in to the whole package.

I could go on but honestly if you have any interest in pokemon style games you owe it to yourself to give this one a shot. It's rare to find a game with so much heart put in to it.\t\t\t\t\t
