\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInevitably Cassette Beasts is going to be compared to a certain other video game series. You know the one. It's the most profitable IP ever? So putting aside that comparison, let's just get this out of the way: Cassette Beasts is really fun! It's got cool creatures, novel ideas in its battle system, fun characters, and of course the music slaps. If you like turn based RPGs, you'll have fun with this one, check it out.

Now, let's put that comparison back on the table. It's really cool to see a game so clearly inspired by one of the most iconic game series ever putting its own spin on things and succeeding. Pokemon is such a well-beloved series, you could just clone it and people will be happy (and rightly so, Pokemon is great). But the fact that Cassette Beasts goes so far above and beyond that is awesome. I tried this out because I thought it looked like Pokemon and was continually pleased and surprised every time it wasn't. I hope you enjoy it as much as I did, whether you're a long-time Pokemon fan or not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played a few creature collecting games in the past (Including that particularly popular franchise), I was drawn to this game, and then my eyes were opened.

It has an extra layer to the type advantages/disadvantages that can make you think more carefully when it comes to your next move which I really enjoyed, it wasn't as simple as "This is better than this, so just does extra damage".

The creatures are well designed ranging from cute and cuddly to weird and creepy, and I loved every single one, and the fusion system as well! Such an interesting mechanic that you don't always see. Can't forget the bootlegs either, essentially shinies that make them be any type! Think of all the colours!

The story and world is pretty neat as well, with some interesting puzzles that occasionally made me think every now and then.

I love the art-style and soundtrack of this game, probably one of my favourite soundtracks from a game for a good while, and fit the situations!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort summary: RPG good, buy game and enjoy!

Ok then, let's dive maybe not so deep:
I know what you think... "This is like Pokemon" but not really, the only thing they share is that they are "monster catcher" games and also RPGs, other than that it plays totally different and that makes it more approachable but also harder if you so wish to. How so? Lemme try to explain it
As I already mentioned before this is a "monster catcher" game where you copy monsters with your tapes and build teams for double battles (yeah, you'll always have a partner by your side) but this game dodges the sometimes frustrating grind of leveling up your monsters because you and your partners have levels of their own and the monsters scale of that level making it much better for trying out different monsters or strategies. Speaking of different strategies monsters have abilities called stickers, they get new ones for every "star level" and if your monster stickers are maxed up these are saved in your inventory, you can also replace those stickers with other compatible ones and there are even buffed up stickers that give you different bonuses!. You can go a level deeper and fuse your monsters (only when that specific partner has it unlocked) to make an even stronger if they are the right type for that battle as those fusions share stickers and types!. Types? Yeah, every monster has their specific type and only one type per monster (that type can change with remasters, which are evolved monsters), there are the usual water and fire but there's also weird types like astral and plastic! This concept gets really interesting as types not only are weak to other types but they also chemically react to them, if you hit an Ice monster it'll melt and become water type for 3 turns, how cool is that!? Oh and the game gives you an in-game chart of reactions, I love this diegetic UI thingies.
Ok, enough mechanics let's talk where this game leads you. You wake up in another world where monsters are everywhere and people have built a settlement to keep them out, you first learn that no one has found a way out yet but after you battle a certain being (won't spoil much) it tells you that there might actually be a way to get back to your world after all. In that journey you meet some interesting characters all with their feelings and desires, they even come from different eras! You'll get to know them better when completing their quests and going out into the world with them by your side as every time you rest at a bonfire they'll give you some backstory or talk to you about the current events, they even react to certain things when walking by or getting into places.
Don't know what else to put in this review, but yeah the game is sooo good, the characters are good, the story is good, the music is good and I haven't even played the DLC yet!.\t\t\t\t\t

I still imagine hanging out with them in my head, though~ And quite frankly, that disappointment probably comes from my own bias as a pokemon fan.
