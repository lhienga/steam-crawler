\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA top-notch open-world creature collector RPG with some fantastic iterations on the formula of its predecessors, a fun soundtrack, and a lovingly-animated collection of sprites.

Mechanics
Your Player Character and current Partner (of several you find throughout the game) can use one monster at a time (with six maximum in your party). Every single combatant uses AP to fuel their attacks, generated at a rate of 2 per-turn. Fusions combine your Monsters together for a single double-type (which are otherwise single-type) and pool your AP together. Notably, this means Fusing isn't always the smart play due to losing an action per-turn and potentially opening up new weaknesses.

Type Advantages are based on logical reactions between elements (Plastic emits toxic fumes when burned, becoming Poison) with a buff/debuff system in place of raw damage output. Notably, however, your human characters have health as well--any damage that goes beyond your monster's HP spills over to the character, potentially taking them out of the action for the rest of the fight.

Catching leaves one character vulnerable, and any damage dealt to the target monster or recording character will adjust the odds of success accordingly. No need for any False Swipe move, either--it only KOs the target if the recording is successful!

Moves are equipped to individual monsters as Stickers, and you may freely take them off and reapply them as you see fit. Leveling is simple enough, you gain your full learn-set from 5 levels and always have the same base stats (with your character leveling up being the source of improved stats). This means you can start using a monster immediately if you so choose! In addition, monsters continue to accrue experience to gain free Stickers. Evolutions are also always available when resting after five level-ups, making prep-work rather convenient.
Shiny monsters are present as Bootlegs, with every monster having a Bootleg of each type. These monsters gain better quality versions of moves on level-up in addition to having a different base-type.

As for open-world content, you can go wherever you want whenever you want provided you found certain skills by capturing specific monsters. Areas have a base level, sure, but you can also configure the passive scaling in case you want to grind or want more challenge. With several major objectives scattered across New Wirrel and many goodies to find, there's plenty of incentive to look around!

Story
You've wound up on a supernatural island known to its inhabitants as New Wirrel. You're certainly not the first castaway, either; countless things (people included) wash up on these shores, and most folks are pretty sure there's no way back to where you're from. Shortly after you arrive, however, you and Kayleigh (who is accompanying you on your first mission as a Ranger to help protect everyone from monsters) find that there is a moon-shot of a chance.

Unfortunately, it involves taking on Archangels; quasi-eldritch beings that dwell in areas beneath the island's surface, with the first of them you found now inhabiting your body to recover from her near-death state. Taking them down reveals more of Morgante's song, unveiling clues as to where you must go in order to find the one way out of this place. Notably, it's unclear as to what Archangels actually are until the endgame.
Meanwhile, each companion has a goal of their own. Kayleigh, for instance, wants to confront the leader of a cult she used to be part of. Meanwhile, Meredith is trying to hunt down a specific record from back home, and Eugene keeps picking fights with real estate agents that may or may not be vampires. You don't explicitly have to help them, just like you don't need to challenge each of the Ranger Captains to become an official Ranger. But it certainly helps your main goal.

There's more to uncover, of course, and I won't spoil any of it.

Content
In addition to many sidequests (and more in post-game areas), respawning encounters with both legendary monsters and Fused enemies, and a large roster of monsters, this game is well worth playing through the postgame of. We're also getting multiplayer, trading, and more!
You can fully customize your difficulty, as well, with sliders for enemy level scaling and individual AI competence settings for wild monsters, humans, and Archangels.
As for replay value, there's a built-in randomizer and several options conducive to Nuzlocke play if that's your jam. You either have to beat the game or input a code to unlock them, though. In addition, the content of Morgante's song (and thus, where you need to go) is randomized per-save!
Music
Numerous tracks (most notably all battle themes) have dynamic mixing that changes under certain conditions such as Fusing or entering buildings. I honestly feel like there's no bad track in the entire lineup, and bought the OST specifically to listen to them on the go. The vocals for "Same Old Story" in particular give me that The World Ends With You vibe and I really enjoy it!

Do I recommend this game?
You saw the thumbs-up already, you know my answer.
Cassette Beasts is one of the best creature collectors out there right now, and is perfect for scratching that itch if you need to. The combat is fun and technical, the difficulty is around the sweet spot without much tweaking and can be tweaked if need be, and the music is a blast!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool little monster collection game that can probably scratch your itch for a certain monster collecting franchise.

The gist of the game is you run around an island, collecting monsters and moves, exploring and battling monsters and bosses. Exploration was engaging but not too rewarding - it's an open level with metroid-like blockers that require new movement tools, and you can find mini-puzzles that unlock chests. Quests have grid markers that point you in the general direction, guiding you through the intended path but are very open-ended.

(Worth noting, you can go straight to the final boss but going through the story makes the final fight easier, a-la BOTW. Cool!)

As you see more of the island and defeat bosses, the plot unfolds. The setting is interesting and had me engaged at the end, and is a whole lot better than every pokemon story, but it's also a slow burn. Companions are likable, but you don't get too many interactions with them. The overall tone of the game is hopeful, earnest and endearingly cringe, and had a real charm for me, but YMMV.

The big draw of the game to me is how different mechanics wise it is to pokemon, and how easy it is to change your builds. Double battle is the default, which I really like. Type advantages provide buffs/debuffs instead of a damage multiplier, and type manipulation is common with moves and Bootlegs (type-specific shiny mon). Your level is independent from your monsters, and moves can be freely added and removed.

All of this makes for a great teambuilding and battling experience. I loved making crazy builds that can win battles before it starts, max out AP in a single turn, or abuse type interactions for massive advantages. Unfortunately the early game is a bit of a slow burn, since you don't have access to all the tools and the combat system doesn't really open up. The fusion system is also gimmicky, kind of like the generational gimmicks pokemon has.

In my opinion this is a worthwhile purchase at full price if you like monster collection games. This can take up days of your life if you're not careful.

As a final note, the only reason I bought it is because someone called it "liberal pokemon". I can, in fact, confirm this game is exactly liberal pokemon.\t\t\t\t\t
