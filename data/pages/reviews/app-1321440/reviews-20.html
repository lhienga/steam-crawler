Cassette, where did i end up geting myself this time?!

this is one of these games i wish it had a neutral option. i can recomend, but with a lot of caveats. its... \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am clearly one of the outliers here, but I cannot recommend this game. I am a huge fan of most creature collectors, from Dragon Quest Monsters to World of Final Fantasy, Digimon, and, of course, Pokemon. This game just seems kind of like a thematic mess to me. First and foremost, picking your starter tape basically explains nothing about it besides "Whats your style," In every creature collection game in existence this is the pinnacle moment of choosing your first partner, in Cassette Beasts in an afterthought. Unfortunately, for what I have played, that's what every character, emotion, or even combat experience feels like, an afterthought.

Combat: Slow animations, way to many abilities, having a partner in the first place?

Movement: Movement is janky as well, I know it gets better as you get further but its such a turn off to even attempt.

Creature designs: One thing creature collector games hammer into you in a digestible way repeatedly is creature types and what beats what. You don't need a novel explaining the mechanics. I have no idea what my first two tapes were until way later on in the battles. I had no idea what things were weak too etc.

Secondly the creature designs felt off. Games like Pokemon and Digimon do a really good job of things, starting off small in silhouettes or designs, even matching what spawns to the environment. Cassette Beasts has a giant ass Lunar New Years Lion dog spawn in one of the first areas. It's large and intimidating, doesnt fit the area and also offers no sense of a challenge.

Music: Finally the reason people are probably wanting to play this in the first place. Why is it soooooo slow. It's almost like a Minecraft vibe but that doesnt fit here. You are going out on an adventure warding off wild monsters while fighting archangels and you have this really slow lofi music. Going back and having a discussion after fusion and fighting what seems like some higher being with lofi in the background just isnt the vibe. The Hayley Williams-sounding girl lyrical tracks playing while you are listening to characters and reading slowly become grating as well. For a smaller team the game seems like it was an ambitious project, there is strong designs and concept foundations but they just did not land the mark for me. Too much was done without any sense of world cohesion or the way it feels to a player.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hate leaving a negative review for this game but I find this a hard game to like. May I just say before going into my review, this is a SOLID game which the developers have done a great job of so what I dislike is the actual spirit of the game, not the mechanics.

I don't know why but the more I play this game, the more I feel dead inside. A Pokemon game always made me feel alive and excited to go into a world to capture Pokemon; in this game, I don't know, I just don't feel it for some reason - I don't know what I want to feel. I know when I met Kayleigh and started interacting with the other people in the town, everyone felt really one-dimensional, even Kayleigh and some of the companions I befriended (didn't finish the full game) just didn't come across as 'people' to me. They are always so positive for some reason like nothing is wrong yet they have been transported to this strange land away from their family and friends and it's just not a problem whatsoever for some reason. As I mentioned, I didn't play the full game so I may have missed something but the development of the characters feel off to me.

I spent 7-8 hours in the same biome and I had only explored half of the map by then. This wasn't much of a problem but just going through the same type of environment all the time was a bit boring.

I think the absolute worse aspect of this game for me is the Cassette Beasts. I don't like the core idea where they aren't even living things but rather a music record you play - therefore feeling very impersonal. I also don't like the way a lot of them look - this is also compounded with their evolutions which I don't like either. Don't get me wrong, some were good but you like a certain one but when it evolves, it turns into something ugly. This will not affect most people but a person like me wants a good looking (don't take that the wrong way lol) monster which I can stare at all day while going through hours and hours of tedious battles (not the battles in this game but just in general with any creature collector game) and it feels worth it at the end of the day. When I play Pokemon or even Temtem, it was kind of a 'me and the boys' kind of game but with this game, I can't form this connection with my Cassette Beasts.

Please don't take this review the wrong way as implying it's a horrible game, it's not, it's just that it works with a different philosophy that I don't like. If you're reading this and expecting something like I was hoping to expect, I would recommend looking for a different game as this won't satisfy that desire.

I hope this review was helpful and have a good day.\t\t\t\t\t
