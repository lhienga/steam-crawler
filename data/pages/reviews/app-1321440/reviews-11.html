\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good game, but a game personally not for me. 6.5/10.

Pros:
- good, well thought out combat, move sets and difficulty
- cute art and characters
- did i say there were cute characters? they are all very cute
- bopping music
- cool open world exploration that's largely unhindered with tons of secrets
- creative and spooky bosses
- easy to collect all the monsters!
- made with godot engine! (i love godot)
- DOG THERE'S A DOG I LOVE THE DOG

cons:
- hard to navigate, clunky movement system combined with strange physics
- many characters without a lot of depth outside of their main quests
- not a lot to do other than repetitive tasks
- story is short and uncompelling to the point where i didn't feel the urge to finish the main story

maybe i'll finish the main game at some other time, but i really felt like this wasn't for me. i'm glad that there are people who do really like it, i feel like there is a lot of charm but ultimately it wasn't what i was expecting. if you can look past the cons then it's really good! i liked it well enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthe game is really solid and has a lot of strategic depth, some pretty good writing, a lot of wonderful creatures to collect, and some honestly pretty fun open world exploration, puzzles and platforming... i hate open worlds, puzzles, and platforming but this game is very much the exception to all of that because there is actually stuff to do between point A and B and movement is fun once you unlock the movement abilities, those abilities can trivialize a lot of the platforming, and if you cant find an element for a puzzle in the area you can just wander off and grab a similar element from another puzzle and it will still work removing a lot of the tedium)

this game also solves some of the major issues with pokemon and other similar games.
1.) the game is actually balanced for every member of your party gaining experience after every battle even if they never got used
2.) you can swap moves between your monsters freely outside of battle. for example trapwurm is one of 2 monsters that learns the move "custom starter" and there arent any other ways of getting it to teach to another monster. but you can remove it from trapwurm and give it to another monster once trapwurm has unlocked it. this can be a really good way to gain great moves throughout the game for no cost
3.) "shinies" are even more unique and special in this game because not only are they a different color but they are also often a different type and have a higher chance at learning uncommon and rare variants of moves (rarer moves have random bonus effects tacked on to them. for example i found a glitter bomb with a 26% chance of giving the caster a status that makes every moves targeting the caster miss

now for the bad
you cant pet the dog :(

physics objects in the world have way too much gravity applied to them- so much so that if you toss a box at a high enough wall with you are rubbing your face on it enough times in a short span without moving it will send you to the shadowrelm briefly. i discovered this within the first 10 minutes of playing for the first time (hadn't even seen a trailer for the game at this time let alone any gameplay or reviews or anything of the sort) with a buddy via remote play. he placed a box at a wall and jumped on it just right and was sent to the stratosphere and i placed it on my head just right and found my self drowning under ground.
still on the subject of physics objects your AI companions can interact with them while trying to path to you knocking them off buttons and ledges and just generally wasting your time

personally i just hate the music. say what you will but the singers voice is awful- literally cant understand a word of her vocal performance. and the backing track is the most "mid" thing i have ever had this displeasure of running through my studio monitors, and im not talking midrange i realize im in the minority here but i just hate the music- so much so that when im trying to manage my party i either have to get out of the coffee shop or turn the game audio off- it doesn't loop properly and it isn't at all in the background when it really needs to be.

and the big one: it crashes. a lot. after 28 hours across 2 devices its crashed 4 times. 3 on my steam deck and once on my over kill desktop PC just before i decided to write this review. im not sure whats going on under the hood but it shouldn't be crashing this much and i dont have such issues with other games. moreover this game kills my steamdecks battery harder than elden ring. that isn't hyperbole either i get around an hour of battery with this game and trying to do anything to get more out of it just results in choppy framerates and a blurry image. meanwhile elden is pushing 3 hours on low at 720p at 30 FPS. again- not sure whats going on under the hood but this game is very GPU hungry- way more than it has any right to be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPLEASE NOTE I am writing this review as a casual gamer and a very small streamer. I'm writing this review for people like me who hate it when games get these "overwhelmingly positive" reviews just because a lot of big streamers are playing them. The experience is so-so. It'll probably be someone's favorite, but definately not everyone's.

This game has a really strong opening. I felt like a kid when playing for the first couple of times.

Then the monotony started getting to me. The lack of variety in scoring. The abundance and not-at-all optimized cataloguization (is that how you spell that?) of stickers.

Like many games it's probably designed with great intentions and much love, but unfortunately it hasn't been playtested enough during the "monotonous" parts. Going through shops, inventory and stickers in the main town is awful because there's this one song that just repeats over and over in the background.

Overall, I'm playing and streaming this game for about 2-3 hours per session and I'd say a third or half of that time is just spent on trying to figure out which stickers do what, how to build my tapes and trying to find specific stickers.
Why can't we sort the stickers in a list by type, rarity, cost or new? If I don't immediately jot down which sticker I get from a ranger it gets lost in the clutter.

The map is a mess. It's easy to look something up on the grid, but when you try to navigate in the world everything looks the same in every "zone" and you get lost. It's often unclear if a chest or switch in a hard-to-reach space is meant to be reachable or skill-depending.
The switch-button puzzles are great, but some require a lot of debris which makes you want to look around the world for something to use, but as soon as you walk a pixel too far everything despawns and is gone from where you placed it.

Like I said. It's got a lot of heart, which makes it stand out from other half-assed games. The story holds up as far as I've gotten (I'd guess a fourth or third of the way?).
The characters are sweet, modeling is amazing. Don't even get me started on the beasts. They're original, fun and type-accurate in their appearance. The blurbs for each one is great. The description of the type-specific tapes are great. The Archangel-cutscenes scare the crap out of me.

There's so much good in this game that the fact that I'm considering to put it down when there's so much left for me to see is kind of depressing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(I don't want to go into great detail about Cassette Beasts because I think most folks would get more enjoyment from going in blind.)

I very much enjoyed everything this game has to offer. Cassette Beasts feels less like it's trying to one-up Pokemon and more like it's trying to occupy its own space in the Monster Collecting genre. The characters are just lovely and I loved learning more about every companion.

The writing is great. When you first boot up the game, you really feel the sense of the overarching mystery of the setting. This feeling is made much stronger by the superb soundtrack, which performs magnificently in every aspect of the game.

Of course the main reason to try a creature collector is the creatures themselves, and this game has no shortage of great monster designs. There's clearly a lot of love put into the designs and the spritework is extraordinary.

Type strengths/weaknesses play out much differently here. Instead of just doing more or less damage, different elemental moves cause different chemical reactions based on who gets hit by what. A metal type monster melts when hit by fire attacks and suffers weaker defenses. Electric monsters get more conductive when hit by air, water, or steel attacks so their attacks hit the whole enemy time. Conversely, they get insulated when hit by earth attacks so they can only hit one enemy at a time. There are dozens more reactions that can stack on top of each other and completely change the course of a fight.
Furthermore, your monsters' techniques can be taken off of them and put onto other monsters, giving you more reasons to try out other tapes and see what else they have to offer. Your battle stats are based on YOUR stats that get enhanced by the monsters you change into, so it's much less punishing to switch out monsters on the fly.

Anyway, please give this game a shot. This game deserves so much more attention, and the setting has so much potential. I really hope they revisit it in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts is one of the best creature collection games out there and even exceeds it's "Mon" influence. It's hard to explain what the game does so well but I'll try to summarise it.

- All double battles (the best format imo). The fusion mechanic is also neat.
- Levelling is based on your level rather than a creature level, meaning there is rarely a need to grind and any new creatures you collect are almost usable as soon as you get them
- Lots of settings for scaling a difficulty. You can fine tune the enemy and npc level scaling and also the intelligence of the enemy AI to suit the experience you want.
- An actual challenge. The bosses and late game rangers (gym leader equivalent) require actual thought and team planning going into them and all play in a different way. And again if you don't want a challenge (or want more of a challenge) you can adjust the difficulty as you see fit.
- Great creature design
- Moves are signified as stickers which you can freely put on and removed from creatures. This allows for easy customisation of builds
- A decent open world with free exploration. The world is a perfect size of not being too big but also having enough mystery to discover. Whilst there are quest markers you are left to go wherever you please and are usually rewarded with a secret puzzle or area. There is also a slight metroidvania aspect with abilities you unlock as you progress. The rewards for secrets aren't usually that interesting but the joy of finding a secret and solving it is a reward in itself.
- MUSIC. One of my favourite game soundtracks of 2023. So fun and I listen to regularly out of game.
- Indie game made by 3 people (2 devs 1 for music). All 14000 fusions are hand drawn by mostly one person.

My only major complaints are the story being pretty shallow and the platforming/movement around the world is quite often frustrating to control. Otherwise this is one of the best creature collection RPGs you can play out there.\t\t\t\t\t
