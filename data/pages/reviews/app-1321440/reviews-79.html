\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan, there's so MUCH about this game I don't even know where to start! May be slight spoilers ahead so be warned!

The first impressions made things really captivating being something akin to Pokemon but with a definite twist and interesting story. Transforming into monsters with the power of music (cassette tapes) with the schematic on how monster types and their strengths and weaknesses work is a really cool concept, and how it played out in game was definitely good to get a knack of as it presents certain effects that can take place before you can attack with a particular move on the battlefield. While there is the chart in-game you can view (and to that extent present on the wiki), just having that little know-how while in action helps a lot.

The challenges through the gameplay were pretty nice in terms of what you had to do to work around certain gimmicks or particular combinations of moves or team synergy, and I found this particularly with the rangers in the game that this worked well given each ranger and their companion pulled off a particular tactic and no major battle was the same. While I left it untouched, the "difficulty" settings were a nice thought to add in to either make the game a little more difficult or easier, which maybe I'll try in another play through.

I also like how akin to Pokemon's recent games battles allow all your monsters to level up as opposed to "only the ones fighting earn experience", as it does save the frustration against needing to level up all your monsters you want to use for the current experience or your dream team, and fortunately it's like in a way how much you have to level up creatures are based on their "evolutions" or just where they would stand in the "evolution" group (if they couldn't evolve like some late-game monsters). Especially post-game for bestiary records or evolving some late-comers to the team all it takes is about less than 10 tough battles to get "evo 1" monsters to 5-star.

The soundtrack is gorgeous with the instrumental tracks and then especially the battle themes with my favourites being the ones focusing on the major battles in the game, especially when fusing with your companion. But that's more for a review for the soundtrack itself and not for what's more in-game.

I can't wait for Pier of the Unknown to drop and the extra multiplayer update in the future, while I do like how there's more to do post-game after the story, I hope there'll be even more when that time comes soon. Cassette Beasts has a really nice charm to it with it's all it's monsters, challenges and music.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo preface, this game is not a Pokemon-like. It is indeed a creature collecting RPG with monster battling elements, but that is pretty much where the similarities end. I would probably say it's closer to Persona than much else, but it is very much something uniquely in its own right.

I honestly went into this expecting to just catch monsters and fight people, but was instantly slapped into a strange meta world with a highly intriguing set of circumstances. The mechanics take a little getting used to, as they use "reactions" rather than straight type advantages (a bit like elemental reactions in Genshin), but once you have those down, they're fairly easy to account for and even exploit. Additionally, rather than the usual ungabunga type gameplay that ends up happening in a lot of PKMN clones, Cassette Beasts allows for some extremely silly automation strategies that will allow you to take down even post-game bosses with hilarious ease.

I blasted through this game in less than a week, and I was honestly impressed at how well it kept my attention. My only minor gripe is how long the final post-game quest takes to unlock, but other than that, it was pretty smooth in terms of overall progression. While every area has set levels, most NPC opponents, fusions, and bosses scale very nicely with your current player level so that it never feels like you're too over- or under-powered. The music is also great, though admittedly I played with the lyrics off. I truly appreciate that you can turn lyrics off, since I find them incredibly distracting, but the lyrics themselves are nicely written.

Overall, very pleased with this purchase, and a strongly recommended play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked up this game expecting a half decent Pokemon clone. What I got instead was one of the best monster collectors I have ever played. This game may invoke ideas to compare it to Pokemon but make no mistake, this is it's own beast of a game.

The elemental system is was of the most fleshed out and well thought out I have ever encountered. Rather than a simple strong or weak, every element uniquely interacts with others to completely alter the tide of battle, like using fire on a plastic type melts the plastic turning it into a poison type, or hitting air with a fire move causing a surging updraft putting up a defensive wall around the air type. This system is fine tune, dense with customization, and just begging you to break it open with a hammer.

The monster designs are absolutely endearing with so many that I couldn't help falling in love with. Hell this is the first Monster Collector I've played where I DIDN'T feel compelled to keep my starter because of how many options there were, and I mean that in a good way. Combine that with the unique bootleg system, and this truly is a game just desperately begging for an online battle mode. I can see a meta forming behind the scenes already.

The bosses were an absolutely treat, with several of them being permanently stuck in my head. The side quests and partner characters are all deeply interesting with some very relatable and realistic worries. I felt compelled to do everything and meet everyone. I plan to get all the achievements now at this point.

The music is the real stand out here though. Every track in this OST is mesmerizing. Lyrics dropping in and out based on the context of the situation, a heavenly voice that draws you in... I found myself unconsciously singing along every time I went to the cafe to manage my team.

If I have to give anything negative about the game, it's that as it currently stands I do it a "journey is greater than the destination" type situation as the game itself including the final boss were absolutely fantastic, I did find the main story ending to be less than satisfying, especially given the game does have a romance system in place, but I feel like it didn't really contribute much once I chose my partner. All things that can be rectified with DLC, Updates, or anything else honestly.

9.5 / 10 from me. Recommending it to whoever I can.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wonderful Monster-Collection battler. The chemistry system is a fun spin on types and adds quite a bit to the depth and strategy. The Music is jamming, the sprite work great, and the world is fun to explore thanks to the abilities you unlock to progress through the world.

Some QoL features really need to be added to sticker (attacks can be freely moved between monsters) management thou, as having a large collection makes navigation a mess. A filter system needs to be added and the default sort option is just not a solution at all, there are mods to add more sort options and I HIGHLY recommend using it.

I really recommend this game to anyone looking for a good monster collection battler. Its not ground breaking IMO but just really fun and well worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t
