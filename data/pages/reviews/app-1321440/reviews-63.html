\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti first heard of this game by watching alpharad gold play it, thought the artstyle was super appealing and immediately bought it after watching that video, something i have never done before after watching a let's play. being a fan of pokemon my whole life, i was really drawn to this game, moreso than any other monster collectors besides pokemon (and maybe yo-kai watch). i'll list what i think are some really strong parts of the game and some parts i think are a bit weak (while trying to spoil as little as possible). i played this game completely alone so i can't comment on the coop though.

stuff i liked
+ a lot of focus on human characters. i love the human characters in pokemon so this made me really happy to see.
+ music is really good, none of the songs i actively dislike or got bored of. i couldn't find some of my favourites on youtube so i bought the ost just so i could go back to them any time i wanted
+ as i said above, the artstyle appeals to me a lot, as well as the monster design. the fact that there are 14k+ animated sprites with just two full time developers is nuts (a vast majority of them do seem to have been procedurally generated but what can you do. it fooled me otherwise a couple times at least)
+ the world feels massive, in a good way. in a similar way to how old pokemon games felt massive as a kid. new wirral feels as expansive as i thought unova and sinnoh were as a kid.
+ the gameplay took me a bit of getting used to but i still really like it and there's tons of really cool saucy stuff you can pull off. sometimes i'd go into a fight underleveled but with a creative strategy and come out on top and it made me feel really proud for coming up with it, a feeling that i've only gotten from pokemon in the past.
+ didn't mess with it too much since i don't like self inserting in rpgs but the character customizer gives like the perfect amount of options such that it doesn't feel too daunting. plus you can change anything at any time.
++++++++++++ the difficulty options are AWESOME. they're kind of tucked away but you should absolutely turn them up. scroll to the gameplay section of the settings tab. didn't feel the need to grind ever on the max setting and every battle was even. there is an order the game lays out certain tasks in, but on the max level scaling settings you can completely ignore it and it's so so so COOL.

stuff i didn't really like (might be a bit nitpicky but i do think they're worth airing out)
- the overworld puzzles have some janky physics which can be frustrating at times. sometimes i felt like i was supposed to use a moveable object as something to stand on top of but whenever i did the cube would convulse. multiple times during my playthrough movable cubes flipped over or sent me flying, which just became an annoyance whenever it happened. i'm sure these issues could absolutely be fixed in a patch, i hope this part of the review ages poorly.
- my game crashed once on an older build, but given the fact that this happened to me only one time and the game has since received new patches, it is likely that they fixed whatever caused me to crash.
- i found one of the partner's quests really frustrating, because you're locked out of using a core gameplay mechanic with them until pretty late into the game, especially for how long their quest is compared to other partners and how early you meet them. it's not the end of the world, it just means that using this specific partner is the hardest to use, so i'd suggest not using eugene unless it's to work through their quest.
+ these issues do not retract from all the positive things to be said about the game, so i really hope this doesn't come across that way.

overall for its price i think this game is a steal. every year or so i run into an indie game that knocks my socks off, and this is the most recent one to do so (it's also the first pokemon-like to do so too). i 100% recommend this game to anyone who likes pokemon or just any kind of jrpg. i'm writing this before i play the dlc so i'll write a review for that too when i'm done it. you *can* access the dlc any time after you buy it but i intentionally avoided it so i could just focus this review on the base game. i also tried to avoid being too specific about gameplay and characters and music because i think it's worth experiencing yourself, instead of having some person tell you about it. you can tell this game was made by people who get what makes pokemon work and what makes other jrpgs work, and put their own creative spin on it that also works.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tfirst off, wow where do I even start with this game,
have you ever played pokemon and went like "gee this is getting boring/repetitive very quick"
you ever play a SMT/Persona game and said "all i had to was spam weaknesses thats it"
well, strap yourselves in, cassette beast not only refuses to be boring, but also somehow manages to one-up just everything i've seen in ANY monster collector RPG i've ever seen.

you know how most of those monster games you just hit weakness and match types and win, well that just about HALF of what cassette beasts is about, the other half, and quite important, is the reactions, you might set someone on fire, you might make them change element completely (example, hit plastic enemy with fire, they become poison), or you might buff them!! (oops!)

and considering all fights involve you and your partner fighting together, you can build quite some interesting strategies.
basically imagine if pokemon was ALL double battles (i can't believe pokemon never did that btw)
the entire game can be played with 2 players, both exploring and in battles, player 2 takes control of the partner
there's 6 partners in the game, from the first kayleigh you start with, to various others, a painter, a emo rocker, a Shakespearean character, and a DOG! (cute!!)
regardless of what monster you or your partner use they will be whatever level you and your partner are, while they all have a default monster, they can switch freely as well
monsters themselves don't have levels but increase in rank to learn a new move (random, whatever they can use) and at rank 5 they can evolve (if they can) there's some branch off evolutions too (the game asks you, few have special condition like day/night time or having some specfic move equipped)

but way way up in the games setup is the one crucial thing that this game builds on a lot, FUSION
now, every beast in this game can fuse with any other beast, thats a ridiculous amount of combination that the game actually tracks, somehere in the 14,000 total fusions. (even the devs don't recommend trying to find every single one)
but what actually happens in fusion? a LOT, all your moves combine, all your STATS combine, very important, your power of attacks and defense increase exponentially! and you get more AP (used for all your commands) and also your elements combine, your buffs combine, and just about all the passive abilities stack
about the moves? well the number of slots a monster has depends on each monster, and evolution tier, and some just have more for unknown reasons, the most slots seems to be 12 (that i know of)
you can FREELY equip and remove moves, called stickers in game, at any time to any monster, you can mix and match moves all over, got a new favorite monster? move all the stickers to them (provided they can use it of course)
and the passive abilities are downright absurd, from simple "change the monsters element, defense to element, exp boost" to more complex things like "use attack when battle starts, chance to use same move again, faster AP recovery, counter attack" if you set up the moves JUST right you can just about murder everything before they ever move, but it takes some setup and luck to get all the moves and passives that just break the game, but even something as simple as "use this move twice in a row" can get massively absurd
this text block is getting lengthy i doubt most are still reading, but all i can say is, hands down, best monster collector RPG i've seen, and i've gone down all the pokemons, digimons, dragon quest monsters, and monster ranchers,
the only game i think of coming close to how good cassette beasts is, is nexomon extinction, thats MUCH more of a pokemon clone though
cassette beasts REALLY tries to set itself up as a whole different game, i can say maybe the only things similar to say pokemon is like catching monsters, and well... yeah the similarites prob end there!

also i should mention the game has optional level scaling, that goes from off ( default enemy levels) to slider of 1-20 values, either making enemies scale down to you or scale up to you. it changes the game a LOT,
at default (Middle) it works out decently (the highest default level is lv70)
protip, if you put the scale all the way up and fight the enemies right at the start of the game (beach) the trafficrabs, they're quite easy to beat even at higher levels, good way to level up but also to mass evolve monsters for collection purposes

final point, the game is really open-world, you can (and probably should) gain access to all abilties to traverse the entire map in about an hour of starting a new game, swim, climb, dash, glide, magnet, and flight,
the game will point it out as you progress, but not all at once
if you somehow figure out the location of the games final boss (it's randomized) theoretically you can beat them right off the start of the game, the game slowly reveals the location per each archangel boss you beat

in terms of game length, on default difficuly and scaling, about 20 hours you'd finish the main story, there's plenty of postgame stuff, and a dlc pack (about 2 hours long, and 12 monsters more) and of course you can go catch all 140 some monsters and bootlegs
and i believe an unlimited number of ranger quests

can't wait for the online update!!!!\t\t\t\t\t
