\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will never be able to tell if I love this game as much as I do because, or despite, of having spent a lifetime playing pokemon. I can't guarantee you'll squeal at the revelation of another layer of complicated mechanics like I did without it. I sure as hell can't guarantee that you won't find traversal too slow or the artstyle too messy, even with the prerequisite nostalgia.

What I can tell with absolute certainty, though, is that this game is earnest and charming and relaxing and cute beyond compare. It's also obtuse, slow, indulgent, even cringey at parts, and entirely unapologetic about all of these things. This combination might put you off it as much as it endeared me to it. In fact, you probably already know if it will.

I don't have much criticism of the exploration or the environment design. The map itself is fun to traverse, fun to revisit with new upgrades, and fun to blaze through once you've collected everything. The puzzles scattered throughout it are also fun if a little on the easy side. This is fine, they're there to keep you moving you through areas and there's enough complexity in the combat and team-building mechanics to make up the difference.

If there is one solid criticism to be levied against this game its about the creature designs. They're extremely hit or miss. Now this could be entirely personal preference but a lot of the first forms feel very bland. The trafficrab is fun, don't get me wrong, but the gag isn't strong enough for me to train it up just to see what it turns into. Unfortunately, I felt the same about many of the early-game monsters.

The Ice-9 tape nearly turned this review negative. How dare you remind me of Alice unprovoked. My trust in this developer is irreparably damaged, and I am constantly refreshing twitter for the inevitable apology. 0/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBravo. They have done a really good job and it honestly puts Pokemon to shame.

Pros:
+ The amount of options and things to do is great. The Blend of the 2D world with 3D mechanics.
+ The fusion feature is done so incredibly well. So many combinations!!!
+ The battle strategies are far superior to other Creature Capture Games. The Passive moves especially.
+ Quests - They just keep coming!!!! so many things to do.
+ Travel companions. Many options options.
+ Trainer level up. This is what I would have put in my Fabled Apps game.

Cons:
- Sometimes the U.I is a bit clunky.
Example: Some attacks give you buffs. But we get all the buff animations even though we've won the battle and no more beasts are coming. It should skip that stuff to make it a quicker and smoother experience. I'm surprised they haven't done this.
- Most menus are good, but navigating can sometimes take a while when checking your moves (TMs) takes a while scrolling to the bottom.
- Having to move beasts around a bit to keep the beast your team mate was holding before you swapped team mates.

The U.I is still pretty good! I just find myself having to work around the menus a bit.

Otherwise this game is superb.
The story is a bit relaxed so far. But the "bad guys" are pretty interesting.
I feel a bit of LGTBQxyz+ agenda in it, but it's not overkill.

I hope the Devs keep pushing this game.

Also maybe some level balancing, my beasts are pretty OP and I am only half way through.\t\t\t\t\t
