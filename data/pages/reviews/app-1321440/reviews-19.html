\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts was a fun game for awhile, but got stale for me before the end. The music is a big highlight and really sets the style for the game. I think the monster designs and artwork are really well made. The type interactions are unique. For instance hitting fire type with air type will lower the fire monster's attack. There is no double and half damage interaction with types. The story is an interesting concept with some fun moments. The Ranger challenge is an interesting alternative to Gym battles. It is an open world design which is fun but I found it easy to get over leveled.

There were a few design flaws that ultimately soured me on the game. The open world is somewhat dense with stuff to do, but once you've played for a bit, it quickly becomes stale. I think a smaller map is better than a large empty one, but it just feels tiny by the end. The next issue is battles. The game uses double battles and that paired with an AP system paired with no way to deal 2x damage leads to longer fights. After awhile battles became tedious as I was doing laps around the map trying to find objectives and constantly getting into random fights that took time even if I was significantly overleveled to them. Finally the ranger battles never challenged me. There was one or 2 that weren't push overs but they left me underwhelmed.

Another major issue was that half way through the game I found a team that worked and there was no reason for me to change it out. The problem was there are some superior skills like Echolocation (hit both enemies at once) and it is not easy to quickly see who all can learn the skills. So by switching out to different tapes I found myself much worse off. If you enjoy min maxing move sets on every monster/tape possible than this will probably be a positive for you. For me the sticker system opened up possibilies but I quickly found that most monsters couldn't use most stickers. There are so many sticker moves that it became tedious to redo movesets and look through them all. I feel like a refinement to this system could make it significantly better than the TM system of Pokemon.

Overall, I've focused on the negatives in this review, but for the price it's worth a look if you are interested. If you like Pokemon / monster taming turn based games than this is one of the better ones I've played on PC. It has it's issues but has enough going for it that it's worth checking out. I hope the dev team can refine the rough edges and create a sequel down the road because this concept and style has the makings of something great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOf all the pokemon-like monster collector games I've played none quite compare to Cassette Beasts.
It hits all the same gameplay notes you'd expect from the genre whilst also adding some truly unique and fun mechanics.

The story is fantastic and engaging. I could gush about the writing for hours but its worth experiencing it for yourself. While I won't divulge any details I will say that I am usually let down by the endings of these types of games, but that certainly wasn't the case for CB.

The art is gorgeous, especially during some of the more unique areas. Creature designs are excellent, silly and creative in equal measures. The world design evokes both a nostalgic and otherworldly appearance which really helps nail the mood and atmosphere the story aims for.

Then there's the music, which is quite possibly the highlight of what was already an amazing game. "Wherever We Are Now" is such a cozy and catchy song. I have listened to it for hours.

TL;DR: Fantastic monster collector game clearly made by a dev team who really care about, enjoy and understand the genre. Music so good you'll catch yourself humming it to yourself when you're cooking dinner and a stunning artistic direction and aesthetic.\t\t\t\t\t
