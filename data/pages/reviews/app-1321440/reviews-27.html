\r\n\t\t\t\t\t++TLDR: This is a fantastic monster taming game with fun exploration, an interesting story, and great combat mechanics. However, there are areas for improvement with art, odd filler content, and some combat mechanics.
PROS:
+The game is open world, though some of this doesn't open up until you learn certain abilities. This makes exploration quite interesting throughout the game. And, by the end of the game, since the abilities that enhance your exploration allow things like double-jumping and gliding, moving around is an enjoyable breeze.
+The game introduces mechanics to replace weakness and resistance, which is a breath of fresh air. Rather, type-advantages cause temporary buffs and debuffs, which, when explained by the in-game tutorials are both hilarious and logical at the same time.
+Combat is made more interesting by introducing a point system that allows different moves based on the available points. Higher hitting attacks cost more points to use, so players need to ramp up and balance their turns instead of spamming heavy hitting attacks every turn.
+The game introduces relationship strength to encourage players to pick combat partners and explore their storylines more extensively. By the end of the game, I had unlocked several interesting combat partners to fight with. SPOILER: Exploring multiple relationships and growing the partners and their beasts actually matters for the final boss fight.
+Puzzles exist in this game and sometimes offer a challenge. This was greatly missed from games like Pokemon that oddly have removed those in the last several generations.
+The story is quite interesting and has multiple, interesting twists.
CONS:
-Combat can be slow because of the points system the game introduces. This means that some fights against lower level monsters can drag on and cost HPs desired for a boss fight. By the end of the game, I was largely trying to avoid fights.
-Some of the anime art for the characters seemed very amateur. I would hope that if there's a sequel, more money is put into hiring a better artist that could channel a similar, but more professional vibe for the game.
-While exploring, the perspectives sometimes change. This is interesting when that reveals new, hidden, areas, but often just became frustrating having to change controls or watch the camera shift often just because I'm near the border of an area where the perspective shifts back and forth.
-Some of the characters had random interactions to reveal they were gay. Though, there really was no plot enhancement or story that this added and this never came up for more than 10-15 seconds. Rather, this seems shoehorned into the game for no apparent reason other than "inclusivity." I'd rather this be more fleshed-out if it's going to be in the game. In this case, I just groaned.\t\t\t\t\t
