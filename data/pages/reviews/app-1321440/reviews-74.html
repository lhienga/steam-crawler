\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts is a Pokémon game that treats you like an adult, both in its relatively mature themes and its gameplay. It respects your time - dialogue and menus are a breeze to cycle through, you can reference a type chart at any time, and the world map is intimate and easy to navigate with a great fast travel system.

This philosophy even carries over to the battle system, which features a replenishing per-battle SP mechanic that eliminates the migraine-inducing "run back to town just so I can use this one move again" loop. Grinding is also practically non-existent as levels are tied much more strongly to your base characters than the titular beasts, putting you in a great spot to take on the final boss as soon as the option is there.

Also, refreshingly, the playable characters are all grown adults who you can romance no matter your gender identity, and they all have interesting backstories you can unravel by spending time with them. This is backed by some great, though admittedly sparse, voice acting, a feature The Pokémon Company seems to write off as a mortal sin.

I'll admit the creature designs leave quite a bit to be desired, and that's really where Pokémon still reigns supreme. You're probably not going to be drawing any of these beasts in your notebook while you're bored in class or a meeting, but I'd overall describe most of them as "pretty cool."

And it's difficult to get completely invested in this world where every inhabitant seems really uncomfortable with the idea of Cassette Beasts existing in the first place; a far cry from the majority of Pokémon's population falling in love with - and often dedicating their entire lives to - a wide cast of cuddly companions.

If you're an adult who's felt disenfranchised by Pokémon's inefficient hand holding and laser focus on the younger market, definitely give Cassette Beasts a try. You'll get a short and sweet RPG monster-collecting experience that gives you a firm handshake and buys you a drink. Just don't expect a juggernaut universe that becomes your next personality like its grandfather before it may have done for you back when "2nd recess" was a regular part of the daily itinerary.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGAME OPINION:

Bought it on launch, making a monster tamer that is unique is a rocky gamble -- but this game blew me out the water! Fusing with your caught monsters has definitely been done before (Digimon) but you can't in good faith compare this game to anything out there. Not even Pokemon. I'm not articulate enough to go too into detail, but 'Cassette Beasts' shakes the table, I truly believe they're changing the standards for monster tamers with this one. Its right up there with Nexomon for me. They also don't fall victim to being too convoluted while trying to be different, the game is easy to catch on, and the battle system feels very fluid. Mechanics aside, The characters are very fun, the premise draws you in from the start. Stuck on this strange island, everyone is from a different time period, or alternate universe, maybe they're not even from a real place at all. But none of that matters, you all have each other now in this cozy little town. Honestly the world building is spectacular, I can't get into it without spoiling, just simply going to say 'Cassette Beasts' gets it right on all fronts. A+ sound track, A+ Story, A+ Design. I'm really happy I bought it on a whim. My only complaint is I wish there was more to do, some more time with our companion characters and the wonderful characters that make the world, I feel like I completed the story too fast, but we're getting a DLC soon so I'm looking forward to that!


PERSONAL REASONS WHY I LIKE THE GAME:
I love the 80's and all things vintage so being able to guess which songs are what and catch the references has made me so happy on top of all the cute and cool fusions. I'd pay close attention to the lyrics to see if I could name the song, and was also waiting for particular popular songs from the 80s to pop up (was very happy for the depeche mode reference!)

Anyways..this is long and scrambled, but just buy it. You won't regret it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best Pokemon-like creature collector I've ever played bar none.

This game just does so many things so right. It copies Pokemon in the ways you want - cute and varied creatures to collect, and a lighthearted and upbeat tone/setting. But it takes almost every aspect of the now stale Pokemon formula and re-works it in a way that breathes fresh life into it. I'll give you a few examples.

In this game you turn into the beasts you capture. They inherit their level from your character. What this means is that instead of capturing a wild beast at X level and now you have to raise it, you catch it and can immediately use it at your character's level. Its moveset won't be fleshed out yet but that's easy to fix. As a result it's really fun to run into new beasts. I found myself capturing and immediately using new beasts all the time. I'm legitimately excited to take the time to catch everything in the bestiary.

The type matchup system is significantly more involved and a lot more nuanced. Instead of being super effective and just doing more damage, type matchups confer buffs and debuffs. For example, attacking an earth type as a plant type adds a health leeching effect from you seeding them. Attacking a plastic type as a fire type turns the plastic type into a poison type cause you've just made them start giving off noxious gases. These matchups are pretty intuitive and it brings a totally different way of thinking about them.

There are a bunch of new types like the aforementioned plastic, astral, beast, glass, and others. None of them are super compelling on their own but it's fun to have the type chart be that much bigger.

The game also deserves props for its art and music. The pixel art is well done and has a really fun mix of kooky and cool. The music is very chill and quite relaxing. I may listen to some of this music offline, which is really saying something.

The story and world/setting are a little flat. They're not bad by any means, and when compared to Pokemon they're more than acceptable. But don't get this game expecting a compelling world and story, cause they're not here.

My biggest gripe with the game, frankly, is the starter options. You're given two options: a goth cow, and a candy demon. They're both pretty good in terms of their strength/usefulness, but only having two such strongly stylized beasts be your only options as you're first being introduced to the game was a really bad call. I found myself trying both and then being like, "Really? I have to use one of these?" This is a minor gripe though.

TLDR - great pokemon-like creature collector with a huge array of innovations and a personality of its own and it's absolutely worth picking up. I'll be raving about this game for a while.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the art. I like the sound. The music, characters, writing, gameplay, pretty much everything is good.

BUT!

And it hurts me to say this - because for an indie game this is a huge achievement of a game - there's too little to do and the pacing is off because of it. The "Ranger Board" should be unlocked far, far earlier, and a lot of the post-game content feels much better suited to be in the main-game, just to solve the weird pacing issue where you end up finishing the game too quickly. I spent about 30% of the game in post-game content, which is honestly, far too affected. Also, the sorting functions could use a lot of work.

BUT!

Everything else drips. The artwork is beautiful, and the direction for the archangels are fantastic. There's enough variety in elements, creatures, and skills to make things interesting. It's the perfect background game. Throw on a podcast and drop the audio to 20%, and it's the chillest hours you'll spend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPositives

+ Loads of monsters to discover and they're generally very well designed and fun with interesting themes
+ Evolutions hit the sweet spot of keeping in line with a monster's theme but being different enough to distinguish it i.e. it's not just a bigger buffer version of the previous one
+ Some monsters have multiple evolution paths which is interesting
+ Monsters have excellent sprites and animations and the battle backdrops are really nice
+ Game mechanics are really tight and leave lots of room for experimentation, especially with every battle being a 2 vs 2
+ Loads of flexibility in what moves each monster can have as you can switch abilities (Stickers) around freely (providing the monster can learn the ability)
+ A lot, if not all, of the monsters feel viable in combat
+ The difficulty is fairly well balanced - it isn't a complete cakewalk and there are some challenging fights
+ Minimal grind - you have your own character level that influences how strong all of your transformations are, and monsters have their own 1-5 star rating that you can level up and influences how strong they are - it cuts down on level grinding massively and in turn makes monsters viable a lot more quickly rather than having to grind up their levels a lot
+ Type effectiveness results in buffs and debuffs as opposed to massive "I win" damage, you can still be at an advantage / disadvantage but it doesn't mean you're completely guaranteed to win / doomed - additionally, whenever you select to use a move, you get a pop-up that tells you the buffs and/or debuffs you're about to apply which is really handy
+ You can fuse 2 monsters together and every single combo has its own sprite (not all are created equal but its impressive it works across all monsters)
+ You have the freedom to tackle quests and the map largely in whatever order you want
+ There's decent endgame material to progress if you want more to do after beating the main story
+ Music is nice and fitting with the game

Neutral

/ The story is decent but the characters and writing are just okay and not super memorable

Negatives

- Sometimes there are minor performance issues when there really shouldn't be considering it's a 2.5D game
- When you swap out partners, they keep the monster cassette that they had on them, sometimes making it frustrating to switch around to get it back
- Not made with any kind of competitive play in mind

Conclusion

My first game ever was Pokémon Yellow so it's hard not to compare it to Pokémon, especially considering how big and well-established that franchise is. I haven't been tempted to a monster-collecting-and-battling game until now because I didn't think anything else really looked that good or had much of its own identity beyond "PST hey wanna play a Pokémon knock-off on PC?"

Cassette Beasts definitely has its own identity and has plenty of innovation to stand out on its own - it has excellent mechanics and memorable monsters that made it a blast to play. If you have any kind of Pokémon itch this will definitely scratch it, but it's also just a great game even without the comparison. Hope to see more of it in the future!\t\t\t\t\t
