\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This is an interesting game in concept. There's the potential for something good here but the final product unfortunately comes off as shallow, uninspired and feels lackluster in a lot of aspects. 6/10

As a massive fan of creature-collecting games since early childhood, growing up with the original DQM and Pokémon on the GameBoy, this game is a massive disappointment. I might give it a very cautious recommendation but I simply can't give it a positive rating here on Steam.
The reason why I might come off as very negative and thoroughly critical is because I hold this type of game very dear to my heart and I initially saw a lot of potential in this game and had high hopes for it (mostly based on community feedback).
However, I feel this game has so many flaws that never get addressed by others in reviews (mostly because it's a cute indie game and nobody wants to be "mean") but the devs need to hear the negatives just as much as the positives, if not more.
Obviously this game draws heavy inspiration from (primarily) Pokémon so there will be a lot of direct comparisons here, if you don't like that leave now - they are necessary.

    CREATURES (MIXED / BAD)
  • Creature designs are very uninspired, the sprite work is fairly well done and there's no real "offensive" designs but generally speaking the large majority of these creatures look bland and don't have that wow-factor that classic Pokémon have. These designs get further worsened when using the fusion mechanic, making the resulting fusion look like AI generated deviantart OC.
  • Creatures baseline stats have very minimal differences between species', making most creatures very indistinct and boring.
  • Your captured creatures have absolutely zero "personality". This might be hard to convey properly but each creature feels like a complete carbon copy of their peers. Pokémon mixed this up with abilities, gene stats, trained stats, natures, Pokéball effects, etc, just to give a few examples.
  • The available creature types are alright and the majority of type-interactions make sense, however some types are just out-of-place or serve no real purpose
  • The fusion mechanic is completely useless, tacked on and adds nothing to the core gameplay. It gives access to one ultimate attack depending on the fused creatures but it doesn't add anything interesting that would mix up the gameplay (like Mega-Evolution in Pokémon). The game wouldn't lose any value if it got removed outright.
  • The idea of having skills/attacks be freely (mostly) interchangable between creatures is a neat idea in theory, however a huge majority of the available skills are functionally the same just with a different typing. The UI for assigning skills is also terribly cumbersome.
  • Most unique skills with interesting interactions are locked to a specific species or incredibly limited in regards to which creatures can learn it, also none of those creature-specific skills are marked as such, making it a pain of finding out who can learn what.

    PRESENTATION (BAD)
  • The visuals are a mixed bag at best. I can see the devs going for a certain universal, simple art style but I personally feel it's just too shallow, lacks a personal touch and often starts to look very muddy. Some of the sprite work is also just bad and could have been improved massively with a few retouches and a distinct art style.
  • The world is beyond bland, repetetive, and gamey. There are arbitrary obstacles and walls everywhere to gate you out of certain areas and there are no landscapes that stand out or even wow you. It genuinely looks like baby's first RPG Maker game in the absolute worst way possible.

    AUDIO (MIXED)
  • The soundtrack is mediocre and forgettable with a few standout tracks
  • The idea of having the soundtrack layered like in Metal Gear Rising with vocals kicking in during "high intensity" situations is always a nice touch
  • The constant looping vocal track in the main town gets annoying very fast
  • Creature cries sound pretty good for the most part, only a few sound distracting. It's a shame that your own creatures don't cry out when they enter combat though.
  • Ambience sounds are nice but get drowned out easily by the constant music and sound effects.

    GAMEPLAY SYSTEMS (MIXED)
  • Always having double-battles is great, it's always been an utter shame that Pokémon never made more use of it in the mainline games.
  • The game features really nice custom playthrough options including randomizers, Nuzlocke and ironman mode. However, these are all locked behind NG+, requiring you to do a full playthrough before you get access to them.
  • The game features adjustable difficulty options both for level scaling as well as how "good" the AI acts. Unfortunately, both options have very negligible effects and even on the highest settings you'll barely be challenged.
  • The way buffs/debuffs work in this game is just badly implemented. Buffs apply to your characters, not your creatures. So switching creatures won't make buffs/debuffs fall off and it's very easy to completely cripple an oppoenent or break the game by stacking insane buffs.
  • Combat is waaaayyy too slow. You're frequently punished for clever strategies and/or exploitation of type weaknesses by having to watch half a minute of debuffs being applied, taking effect and combat interactions taking place EACH TURN. There is no speed-up function or options in the menu (akin to Pokémon's "show battle-effects on/off") to alleviate this.
  • Catching creatures is more engaging and feels more based on your gameplay than in Pokémon or DQM. This is a double-edged sword though because you always have to go through the motions of setting up a capture, even if the creature is low-level and it's the 100th time you're catching one.
  • The late and end-game are capital B boring and grindy. There's no way to put it nicely, there's absolutely no value provided after you've finished the story.
  • Game focusses A LOT on jump-and-run "puzzles" or challenges with a jump-and-run system that is by all standards just bad. It's floaty, glitchy and often times just frustrating. The DLC is especially egregious in that regard and almost made me quit the game altogether.
  • Open world traversal isn't helped but hindered by the world design and how the different traversal skills behave.
  • In my 40 hour playthrough I had 2 random crashes, one losing me around 2 hours of gameplay. Bugs seemed fairly rare and most I've experienced were linked to the jump-and-run mechanics (clipping, glitch-jumps etc). I had two combat softlocks, one of them being technical (the game froze and didn't react to inputs anymore). Other than that the game feels polished and stable. Can't comment on the performance since my system outscales the requirements massively.

    STORY AND CHARACTERS (VERY BAD)
  • The story is incredibly forgettable, boring and repetitive. There are some neat concepts and details but those ultimately go nowhere. There was never a moment that resonated with me or an idea that left me thinking afterwards.
  • The characters are incredibly forgettable, boring and have little allure. Same as the story, the few interesting details the characters offer aren't elaborated on and go nowhere. They also ALL feel like really stale stereotypes, the only exception to that being a literal dog.
  • The character affection system is just grindwork that leads to nothing. Yes, you get stronger fusions and you can "date" your team mates but it's all just surface level and a waste of time.
  • By the halfway point of the game you've basically seen everything the game has to offer and the rest of the way is just a sluggish repetitive chore.
  • The final encounter was very disappointing, focussing almost entirely on a scripted series of events instead of a true test of your skill and team-building.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally not a fan, and it's mostly on the art end.

I can't get past the art style - it's very deliberately the way it is, and it's definitely competent in areas, but that way personally really doesn't do it for me. It's emulating B/W - B2/W2 era Pokemon and how that integrated pixel art and 3d, but just kind of falls short of the mark. Everything is just too mismatched. The trees especially I found distractingly poorly rendered. Anything that isn't 3d or a beast is very flat. Again, it's deliberate, but I really did not like it.

The 2D art portraits for the characters are adorable and polished but needed to be pixel art or not there at all. You just have mismatched pixels, 3d and 2d all layered on top of each other. I try not to let visuals bother me too much but in this case it spoiled the experience to the point I didn't feel motivated to play - and combined with a tutorial area and writing I didn't find gripping, it killed my motivation to continue.

My opinion is pretty subjective though. This game isn't for me but that doesn't mean it won't be for you - if you like the art style, give it a go. I wish the creators all the best and think the idea in concept is excellent - just not for me. :)\t\t\t\t\t

The amount of Monsters and their abilities are a bit limited, but well designed. The map is overall relativly large, but the starting town feels a bit empty when you go into the buildings.

The ending of the story is a bit disappointing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally fun monster tamer, lots of wholesome vibes. A nice take on the combat system that is slightly more focused on statuses and type match-ups and really opens up once you start getting a feel for it. I love the decision to reflect type advantage in status effects rather than damage - it both allows for creative strategies and gives you a chance to play around bad match-ups in a way that's not just 'coverage'.

Story-line is a little thin, but that's pretty typical in this genre - it's still more engaging than anything Game Freak has written in the last 15 years.

Definitely some broken stuff (ahem Paint Swipe), but it's not like there's a PvP meta-game to worry about, and some of the most fun I had was figuring out exactly how to kill everything on Turn 1.

All in all, a very cute and satisfying game that has reignited my love of a genre which had been losing my interest.\t\t\t\t\t
