\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne thing to be clear Cassette Beast is not trying to be Pokemon and that is a good thing as it stand out as its own unique monster “tamer” game.

The premise Cassette beast is that every beast in the world can be recorded with cassette with the exception of arch angel. You can then take the form of recorded beast. The game is heavily themed around cassette player, you don't tame beast you record their form and store it on a tape. You heal by rewinding with pencil, revive with spool, ect.

Each tapes have generous amount of sticker which are moves in this game. As result the move pool of each tape does not feel that limiting.

Because beast are just form stored on tape, there is no breeding in cassette beast which is a boon for people who dislike breeding in monster tamer.

One of the most outstanding feature is elemental catalyst. When a elemental attack hits a different element, an extra status effect can occur. Or even change the receiver type. Like if plastic gets hit with fire it will become poison.

Another example catalyst is a lightning element gets hit with a water element it will get the conductive status effect which grants it the ability to hit multiple target with otherwise single target ability.

There too many to list in a review, but the catalyst can result in many wacky and fun scenario. You can unintentionally screw yourself over or gain unexpected advantage due to this system.

Finally for the big one, FUSION! When you build enough meter you can fuse with your partner. You can literally fuse with any combination of beast. The resulting fusion is more powerful and the fusion still have its own idle and attack animation.

The game encourage you to further relation with your companion to boost your fusion power. If you choose to you can pursue romantic relationship with the human candidate.

When you fuse, the vocal kicks in. The vocal is amazing and really hypes the situation. The vocal kicking in when you fuse at very tense situation makes for some very memorable moments. I bought the soundtrack because it really is that good.

The arch angel are the bosses in the world, they are otherworldly being that don't look like they belong in the world of cassette. The boss fight and design are quite memorable. I quite enjoy the very distinct design which some time are quite unsettling.

Minor nitpick ,you'd think NPC would utilize cassette to help with task or labor. At most its seems cassette beast form are just used for traversal and battling.

Fusion material grind can be a bit much. Unfortunately this has resulted in people cheesing encounter to deal with the copius amount of fusion needed to progress the ranger board. Bit unfortunate as the enemy fusion combination can result in interesting fight and situation

Summary

A cassette themed monster tame game with its own unique and fresh take on the genre. Emphasis on fusion and catalytic effect of differing element to create a unique gameplay. Great sound track with vocal that kick in when you fuse, hyping up the situation.\t\t\t\t\t
