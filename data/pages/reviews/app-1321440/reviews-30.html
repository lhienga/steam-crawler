\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, I want to say, absolutely fantastic game. I love it to death. Elemental system is unique and fun, I enjoy the exploration, the combat is incredible. I strongly, strongly recommend this game. That being said, I do have a few minor gripes with it. It seems like almost every time I move out of one map square into the next, even in the same area like in Harbortown, I run into an invisible wall for several seconds, which has often resulted in me getting attacked by monsters while low on health because I couldn’t get away from them, and this also hinders my exploration and is moderately frustrating, though it’s not that big of a deal in the end. My other gripe is that the movement doesn’t always seem to work quite as smoothly as I would like it to, there seems to be just a bit of input lag, which makes some jumps a bit tedious. Both of these are rather minor, and other than that, I thoroughly love this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis game is a masterpiece, managing to capture all the fun of an old school pokemon game while also staying unique and different

If you're worried about this game being too close to pokemon, dont, this game manages to create its own unique way to play with fusions and type interactions. Instead of some types of monsters being weak to others, the type interactions instead inflict different debuffs or buffs based on type. Also changing your enemies type is an option to give yourself an advantage! playing with these interactions in mind is a lot of fun.

The form collecting is also fun, catching them all is actually attainable in this game but takes some effort and willingness to farm, if I had any complaints I would say its a bit simple but that really depends on your luck in getting some monsters to spawn. Doing the quests is also fun and the endgame just keeps going with more things to do to keep you coming back.

All in all I had a lot of fun and highly recommend this game to everyone who likes monster collecting games\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game honestly really surprised me! I picked it up following the demo months back, and while I thought it was okay in that opening hour or so, I have really warmed to it after picking it back up.

The mechanics are pretty chunky and meaty in terms of what you can do with any given creature, the designs and animation of every creature are fun and breath life into the turn-based combat, and really if all this game had was the battle and progression mechanics I'd be sold!

Beyond that though it also has a sizable assortment of quests and story lines to progress, many fun characters to meet, narratives to develop, and while there is a core main quest, there's a lot more you can choose to interact with or not which actually delivers on the appeal of side-content in an RPG in a substantial way. Being obviously compared to pokemon in most of its surface-level aspects, I was delighted to find their sort-of equivalence to gyms are one of these mostly-optional areas of the game! Rather than having to trod through each area 1 by 1, I can just find a leader whenever I see them, and focus on the more interesting narratives I'm here for. Great game, great atmosphere, highly recommend if you have any interest in this sub-genre of RPG.\t\t\t\t\t
