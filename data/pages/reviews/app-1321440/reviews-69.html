\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally nice game with a good amount on offer. Almost 20 hours just doing main and side quests with no completionism. The art's pretty good and while the design philosophy for the monsters will always be up to personal taste, there's definitely some winners in there. The general game flow is your standard RPG fare, but the combat brings in a lot of fresh ideas. The main one being that type advantages inflict unique status effects themed around that type instead of just dealing more damage. The bosses and other major npcs also use a lot of varied strategies that make you have to actually think about how to counter them instead of just pressing the big damage button.
It's fairly rough around the edges, but considering the price, time investment, and size of the dev team, I don't think there are any game breaking issues. Overall, it's very corny, and it's got a lot of heart because the developers clearly liked what they were making.\t\t\t\t\t
