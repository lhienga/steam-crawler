\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like monster taming games or similar I cannot recommend this game enough.
The visuals are good, the fusion mechanic is decent, the visuals are charming, the cassette and nostalgia feel is spot on, and the world is large enough to take time to explore with varied environments, without being overwhelming.
Also, your creatures level is not independent. You have a set level and stats yourself, and whichever beast you equip gives a boost on top of it, meaning when you get a new creature they are not immediately the weakest one you own in need of leveling up, they are already able to be used.
So, especially as AAA game prices climb to over $80, this little gem for $20 is well worth it.
I would also recommend getting it for steam deck or on switch, as it's a great game to play on the go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI must admit that I was hesitant to try Cassette Beasts initially. The art direction, utilizing diverse styles and tropes, was difficult to digest.

Upon entering the world of Cassette Beasts, my concerns regarding the artistic style immediately melted away as the player is immediately plunged into some of the most intuitive and original systems in the genre. The player has virtually infinite agency over how they want to play the game, as abilities can be swapped between each of the different "beasts"; this freedom allows players to experiment and create combinations from start to finish of the game.

There are so many different things that can be praised, the soundtrack in particular is especially captivating, as the songs have dynamic versions that add lyrics during more intense portions of the game.

I have not yet bought or played the DLC, Pier of the Unknown, so I am unable to speak on that, but I am looking forward to when I can eventually get around to it.

I am also excited about the prospect of the upcoming co-op update, which will allow players to freely trade items and "beasts", while also participating in game content.

If I was to be critical of the game, I would say that endgame is somewhat bland. While there is post-game content to complete, it feels like it is lacking certain systems that would help make it more engaging. For example, the player is able to collect "Stickers" (the abilities of the "beasts"), but by the end of the game, you most likely have encountered all the possible stickers and are left searching for higher rarity versions of them. Introducing a system that would allow the player to combine stickers to create a higher rarity version could create a better end-game resource sink that would keep players interested.

Overall, I cannot recommend this game enough. It has a reasonable entry cost and even if you're only interested in playing through the story, you'll get ~20 hours of game-play at least.\t\t\t\t\t
