\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI completed the main story and a couple of the side stories, and I can safely say my opinion on the game is a positive ‘okay’, maybe a solid 6/10.

The fundamental gameplay loop is a mix of Pokemon and Persona. You run around, catch dudes, fight dudes, and customize your dudes to fight stronger dudes. There’s a whole type chart and set of status effects and a lot of complexity under the hood. I never engaged with any of it and used the same 2 dudes without much trouble for the entire game. It’s cool that it’s there and maybe it’s intended for something harder than the recommended difficulty, but I never really felt challenged (but that’s probably because sleep is a completely busted effect).

The parts that the game does well are its exploration and character customization. It was a lot of fun trying out different sets of passives and moves and strategies, and it was fun trying to break into areas of the map I thought I wasn’t supposed to be yet. I will say there’s not much reward for exploration other than getting more areas to explore; I don’t think any of the ‘reward’ treasure chests I opened contained a single thing worth my time for the entire game. There’s some puzzles that are surprisingly fun to solve, and the movement options your character gets are quite fun to use around the map. These two facets kept me playing even when I felt like dropping the game out of boredom. HOWEVER, exploring is also kind of bad, because if you go off the beaten track rather than heading straight from quest marker to quest marker, you might end up a much higher level than you’re supposed to be. I didn’t do any grinding and was typically a full 10 levels higher than everything else (apart from some bosses which I think were level scaled).

In addition the art style is functional, even if it’s not fantastic. In particular the boss designs were great- I would have enjoy the game more if the aesthetic they used for the boss areas was more common. Unfortunately, most of the time you’re fighting very tedious, unchallenging battles where the same music drones on and on. It’s not a bad song but goodness does hearing it over and over get tiring. The battles also can go on WAY too long, some of the animations (especially when there’s a lot of statuses to tick through) take forever. I almost dozed off during a couple of fights.

The story is there. It’s not particularly badly written but not particularly interesting either. There are number of ‘partners’ you meet that serve almost no purpose except to steal your monster whenever you switch them out (you can get them back immediately, I just got cranky every time it happened). These are set up like Persona-style social links, where you do a little quest and they give you a little perk in battle. I almost never used these because the fusion mechanic (which is cool) was almost always terrible for how I customized my dudes, so I barely interacted with it. I also really didn’t care for any of these side characters. The game suffers from being excessively nice, where everyone is just super cool with your character and supportive and friendly and as a result I found absolutely none of the characters worth caring about. They have nice art though!

Overall the game is probably worth about 15 to 20 bucks. I did do all the extra challenge captain bosses to see if maybe the game has more challenging post game content but they were somehow easier than the first time I fought them, so I didn’t bother with any of the other post game stuff. 25 hours is pretty good for what I paid, so I can recommend it. It’s an occasionally interesting but largely mediocre experience. With better writing or a more challenging set of obstacles it’d be way cooler but as is it’s about what you can expect from a game with this price.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game, dollars per hour isn't a good way to review a game, but a team of 2 developing a game that gives at least one dollar per hour is impressive, and there's a large amount of content delivered on top of solid foundations. This feels like one of the only Pokemon-adjacent games that feels like it can compete for that playerbase. I'll preface this whole thing by saying that I'm a bit of an ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ who only likes to criticize games but know that I really enjoyed my time with this game, and have already recommended it to a few of my friends

The gameplay is solid, and the variety of types is good. The main difference between Cassette Beasts and Pokemon is that instead of super-effective types dealing more damage, they will apply a special buff to the opponent, which can be positive or negative depending on the matchup. I VASTLY prefer the traditional super-effective damage bonus from Pokemon and most of the debuffs don't affect your opponents as much as getting a debuff can hurt you. This mostly results in bogging down an already relatively slow combat experience because you just end up trying to avoid crippling debuffs while hitting your enemies for neutral damage and whittling them down. For reference, I had the A.I smartness increased to 1 past skynet, and default scaling because grinding is boring. Some of the fights in the game can drag on for far too long, and it never really has the satisfaction you would get from Pokemon by strategically setting up a team to blow through your opponents. This also causes you to have to rest a lot throughout the game, and later on, almost after every single battle for me. The fusion mechanic is awesome and while initially it might seem less useful than it actually is, the stat and type combination as well as receiving double the AP per turn combines to let you just pelt your enemies with extremely strong moves. The open world almost Metroidvania aspect of the game when out of combat is well put together as well, and I have little to no complaints there.

In terms of content, there are around 120 monsters, tens of thousands of fusion combinations, 12 Ranger Captains, 5 or so landlord hideouts, 8+ archangels, and much more that is too much to include in this review. The highlight here would have to be the captains and the archangels, all of which have unique gimmicks and strategies that differentiate themselves much more than just the simple type theme of Pokemon gym leaders. The map is around the size you would expect it to be, with plenty of little nooks and crannies to keep me alert while exploring it, although I would have preferred there to be more puzzles.]

In terms of the art and music, I've seen some people complaining about the music, and as a grade-A music snob, who isn't really a snob because I enjoy most of everything, the music is fine. The main issue is that most of the game will have the same track playing in the background, and I think the song mostly does an okay job of falling into the background, but man the vocals are present way more than they should be, talking with one of the characters, exploring peacefully, doing a battle- it can be annoying and not fit the mood at all. The battle themes are a little generic and dull, but when everything is put together it's adequate for an indie game. The art is obviously very subjective, but I will say that some of the monster and character designs really hit. Unfortunately, the majority of the monsters just end up looking a little bland and indistinct. I don't find myself gravitating towards almost all of the monsters in the game. In something like Pokemon, every generation has 4 or 5 really compelling designs that make me love the process of getting those Pokemon and having them on my team. Most of the monsters in Cassette Beasts aren't super cool, cute, unique, etc., and paired with the lack of move diversity most of the monsters end up feeling like a bunch of stats with a type.

The social-sim aspects of the game are the biggest letdown in my book, as most of them have limited interaction with the player and even less in between each other. Only being able to have one companion while the rest of the characters are sitting around doing nothing back in Harbortown sucks, and I really would have loved the companion limit to be purely for gameplay so that you get to see the gang interact. I like the little lines that characters have at the fireplace but I just think it could have been so much more (something like Tales of Arise was my mental image before playing this game).

Most importantly, even though it's a dev team of only 2, I would absolutely love to see a sequel that builds upon the already solid foundations this game has. I really feel like this has the potential to be something fantastic.

7.4 / 10\t\t\t\t\t
