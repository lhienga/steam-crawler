\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I go over the game I'd just like to say this: I never played any monster tamer before, pokémon included. This is my first entry into the genre. I do know a few bits and bobs here and there, but I don't have any actual first hand experience of any other monster tamer. Take of that what you will when reading this review.

 Overview 
I did not expect Cassette Beasts to be as good as it is; the gameplay is (for the most part) fun, innovative, and easy to understand, it has a great artstyle, and an AMAZING soundtrack. It is overall a VERY good game. If a non-pokémon player like me likes it, I'm sure anyone who DOES play other monster taming games will love Cassette Beasts. Easily an 8/10 for me, my 2nd favourite game of all time (so far).

Gameplay 
Overall, the gameplay is very good at the start, but after you beat the main game and get to the post-game content (yes, there is post-game content) it starts to lose a bit of it's magic.

As for specifics, I'll put them in bullet points here:
        
  • You only get to have 2 people on your side in battle; you and your companion.
        
  • There are 6 companions in total. You meet more and more of them as you go through the game. Each companion (bar 1) has their own special beast that have their own unique quirks and moves.
        
  • A 'fusion' system in the game let's you combine you and your buddy's beasts together to make a single, big and powerful one. They are pretty overpowered, however other people and even beasts can do it as well, so I suppose it balances out in a way.
  • Each beast has it's own type, e.g water, air, plastic, etc. Type advantages don't only grant you extra damage; they can also have special effects. For example, an electric beast hitting water/ice will conduct electricity on them, electrocuting them every turn. Or, for a more extreme example, a fire type can hit a plastic type, turning the plastic type into a poison one. It might be confusing to wrap your head around first, but it all comes naturally eventually.
  • The 'capture' mechanic in this game comes in the form of recording. When recording, the person recording will go back to the human state, leaving them vulnerable. Recording chance is shown to the player and is affected by a number of factors; the beasts health being a good example. When a beast is recorded, it isn't necessarily captured, but is essentially copied instead. It's a far more humane way of using beats instead of capturing them into little balls and essentially forcing them to fight for you. Speaking of which...
  • You get to BE the beast. You are an active participant in the fight. If your beast form goes down during the enemy's turn, you are revealed and can be actually hit yourself, and if you get downed, you are permanently gone for the fight. However, same goes for the human battlers; you can obviously use this to your advantage.
  • The game has a focus on exploration as well. The overworld map is big all things considered, with a few unique areas/biomes about, with unique beats inhabiting them. There are puzzles around which can be solved to open up paths, reveal treasure, or progress the story.
  • The bosses are mostly pretty easy. If you get their gimmick, you can easily work around it if you strategise well enough. This doesn't mean they aren't fun though.
  • The post game content is really the only place where the game loses it's shine. Without spoiling too much, it's like 80% random cookie cutter quests and 20% actually unique content. It's still fun; just not as fun as what I experienced before the post-game.
  • I haven't finished all what the game has to offer 40-ish hours in, but from what I know I have yet to play, the total playtime (unique post-game content included) is around 50, maybe 60 hours. Excluding post game it's around 25 hours. There is still collecting all the beasts and even seeing all the possible fusions however (which there are like tens of thousands of) so you might also get a few dozen/hundreds of hours from that.

That's all I'm gonna say; there is still a lot to the game, but I'd rather not spoil too much.

Story 
The story is pretty fun. Each character/companion has their own quest with their own storylines. The characters themsleves aren't one-note; they feel like real people, and are fun to listen and talk to. The ending is pretty underwhelming, but I suppose it's all about the journey, not the destination. Again, won't spoil too much, but rest assured that it's a good story that might even get a tear or two out from you.

Artstyle 
It's cute and very pleasant to look at, ranging from the character portraits to the pixel art. The team have done a phenomenal job on these.
The beast designs are both unique and random, ranging from a sad water tap to a cat with a flat screen for a head. If you like weird and fun designs, you'll like this game.

Music 
The soundtrack consists of 80's/90's synths and rock. Some of the tracks even include vocals, which slap REALLY hard and still give me goosebumps everytime I hear them. Think Persona 5; just instead of jazz it's electric guitars and synths. It's really good.

LGBTQ Representation
The game does have options for same-sex romance, along with the option for your character to have they/them pronouns. I'm indifferent on this, but maybe you might love/hate it. I'm not the one to really judge either way.

Community 
The community is small but welcoming. The people in the discord server are a great bunch of lads and are really supportive. The developers even also talk often in there. As far I see there is no toxicity at all.

That's all I got to say; great game, I highly recommend it if you like fun, innovative gameplay, or even for the artsyle/music alone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an incredible spin on the monster catching genre that I think really needs to be experienced.

While there are a couple things about the game that I've had issues with, such as somewhat clunky menus with regards to switching around party members, I cannot stress enough that the rest of this game is actually close to perfect.

The gameplay is satisfying, it's fun to come up with ridiculous strategies and watch them play out live, and I found myself getting giddy even when I lost, because it meant that I'd be able to come up with something stronger and come back twice as greater than they had thought.

I adore the characters. Every single one of them is a joy to adventure with, and the ones I can't take on adventures with me, I so desperately want to. (But that's what workshop mods are for. >:3)

The bosses in this game. Hoooooly crap. I'm going to restrain myself from talking too much about these because they are genuinely so much better experienced blind, but just know you're in for a treat. The concept is ridiculously cool to me, and the execution is FLAWLESS, with the caveat of a couple of them being pushovers, but they still come in swinging with incredible designs and just the general rush of being in the fight.

The soundtrack is flawless. There's not even much to say outside of "I HAVE BEEN LISTENING TO THESE SONGS ON LOOP SINCE I STARTED PLAYING THE GAME" and I think that honestly says what it needs to.

I have only had this game for around two weeks and it already means the world to me. I HIGHLY recommend it and would LOVE to see more people get into it. <3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was truly an unexpected gem.

Picked it up not really knowing what to expect after hearing some good reviews, but I was genuinely caught off guard by the level of quality present.

The turn based combat is fun and has some nice variations to it that help it stand out from the crowd such as the more unique type match-up system

The art is also a treat to look at, blending 2D sprites with mostly 3D environments and the Music is of equal quality.

Having completed the main campaign I can also say that the Post-game content provides players with plenty of reasons to hang around.

My only real complaint was that the non scripted enemy encounters in the overworld seemed to be a bit lopsided in terms of enemy variety and typing, with poison and metal enemies in particular seeming way more common than others in most of the larger areas.

That aside though, if your looking for something to match the older Pokemon games or are just looking for a fun turn based game with a solid story, fantastic music and visuals, I'd give this one a try for sure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be frank, I initially began my experience with Cassette Beasts on a Captain Jack Sparrow version of the game. After about 40 hours of game play, the pure love and care the developers poured into this game forced me to come to Steam to purchase it. I will also be getting the DLC in a few days once it is released.

Every second I have spent playing Cassette Beasts so far has been a perfect blend of nostalgia, joy, and wonder. As most who give these monster collecting games a look, I am a massive fan of the Pokemon series. And while your opinion of the newer games of that series may vary, I can say without a doubt, that if Game Freak spent the time and effort to polish their games as thoroughly as the developer for Cassette Beasts has, there would be little chance for an indie game like this to shine as much as it deserves to.

PLAYER CUSTOMIZATION
I'm glad that this aspect was included and that is has the detail that it does. I am definitely the type of person who can spend hours in a character creator if it's detailed enough, and while Cassette Beast's character creator is very sparse, it does allow for enough of a tailored appearance that you are able to bond with your avatar that much more.

EXPLORATION/ PROGRESSION
The entire map is well thought out and designed to funnel you down the intended path while giving you enough freedom to explore and discover on your own. During the course of the story, the island felt magical and expressive wherever I went. Clever mission based systems and plot lines allow you complete the game in nearly anyway you'd like. Your enemies also scale to your player's level, which keeps everything challenging as you continue through the game.

STORY/ CHARACTERS
The story line of the game is simple, but is expressed in such a way that the player makes far more of it than it actually is during gameplay. This is mostly done through incredible character design and small, but consistent player interactions with the cast. You have a selection of companions that accompany you through your adventure, there are quite a few other cast members who you interact with regularly, and even though you don't necessarily spend a large portion of your time with all of them, each character's personality is on full display. Your companions will frequently have flavor text, and small amounts of voice acting that really bring them to life. The addition of a relationship meter that positively affects combat, and the option to enter into a romantic relationship with your companions is a nice touch.

MONSTERS/ FUSION
Almost every Monster Collection game has left be with the thought that some of the creatures could have been better, some of them are subpar and should have been removed, and some of them just look like they don't belong and should not be a part of the game. THIS GAME IS A PERFECT EXCEPTION TO THAT RULE. I know that a lot of modern Pokemon fans have some Pokemon that they just believe "fit", but even if they don't quite work for every fan, they still have that "Pokemon feel," and eventually they end up growing on you to a certain extent. The monster designs in this game are, in my humble opinion, a masterpiece. Of the entire bestiary, every single creature "feels" like it belongs in this game. The cast of creatures is largely inspired by folklore and myth in a similar way to the Shin Megami Tensei/ Persona series, but with a unique stylized design that really makes the monsters feel like a part of this world. Between the Monster and character design, this developer could truly make a series to rival Pokemon if some additions were made.

COMBAT
The game is essentially 2v2 for it's entirety, with a very neat system of monster fusion in place that combines your stats and abilities into a single pool. Do you want one strong monster to fight your two opponents, or do you need the additional monster's turn? Your enemies can do it too, by the way. Instead of type advantages in the traditional/ bonus damage sense, there are instead chemical reactions that offer you various benefits or debilitation. Each reaction's core logic is explained to you in a tutorial bubble the first time you encounter it in order to make it easier to remember. A thoughtful and effective gesture. The combat AI and level scaling keep the battles interesting, and while with time you can definitely build a monster that will drastically overpower any opponent, the core game will make you earn your victories.

MODS
Fully Supported! There are many well made mods that add nice quality of life features, and most (NOT all) of them will not prohibit you from unlocking achievements. I recommend giving them a look and tailor your experience to your liking.

MULTIPLAYER
This is the only area where the game feels lacking. It actually has a form of couch co-op multiplayer that allows a friend to drop in and out at anytime and control your companion. The multiplayer almost feels like it was designed for a couple to play together now that I think about it. I would like to see some form of interactive online multiplayer, but the game in it's current incarnation, with as much mod support as there is, and the extreme variations of possible "stickers", makes this seem like an impossibility as it is now.

Whether you've been looking at this game and haven't been able to commit to it, or you're looking at it for the first time, I hope that this review has helped you make the choice to dive in. This game is a masterpiece. Somehow a comforting and cozy walk through the forest, and epic world-saving adventure all in one. Also, the soundtrack is actually worth every bit of the cost of entry by itself. Wherever We Are Now is on Spotify, and I've been listening to it on repeat for the last week. Bravo, Bytten Studio. I might play Lenna's Inception now just so I won't have to leave the worlds you've created.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start, don't let my hours here fool you. I also own this game on switch (where I have a LOT more hours in the game... like many times the hours), and it is easily one of my favorite games from the last decade. I have always been a fan of pokemon games, and this brought me all the joy of a pokemon game, with a wonderful breath of fresh air. It took everything I love about pokemon, and improved on so much of it. So much so that I played it here for just under 8 hours and decided that I needed to be able to take this game places with me and purchased it for switch.

First off, the story is really fun and well written. The characters are all interesting and would totally love to see more of them, and with that ending, it opens up a lot of potential even for future stuff (and if ever this game gets a sequel, I am buying that soon as I can. Heck, I am eagerly anticipating next weeks dlc release <3)

The game play is familiar, but unique. I loved collecting all the creatures, and the fact that rather than literally capturing them, you yourself get to be the creature. It was like all the best things about pokemon and digimon had a baby that surpassed their franchises in ways that hopefully they will see and adapt from. From the exploration abilities, the fact that you don't need to grind to make a tape worth using, and the variety in attacks, I adored playing this game.

The music is wonderful and I have spent a bit more than a reasonable amount of time just sitting in an area listening to the music playing. The energy of the cafe is so wonderfully chill, battle gets my blood pumping, all in all I wish there was more songs, but I enjoyed every song in the game.

Now, to avoid exclusively gushing (though, seriously, it is gush worthy. I do not regret at all getting 2 copies.) I will get into the negatives too. There are a good few creatures, but I do wish there were more, especially ones that have really cute/pretty final forms. (Mardiusa and Misamodeus are probably my favorite designs among final forms). Not really a complaint, considering that wanting more of something in a game is usually a good sign. I have a similar issue with most aspects of the game. I want more music, I want more creatures, I want more story, I also want more character customization. I just want to keep enjoying this fun world.
My only real complaint, and I am spoilering it because if you haven't finished the game you should not read this next part. I feel like everything involving the other world and the Amber lodge feel underutilised. Like, when I got sent to that desolate wasteland, I was hoping for something to happen. Something new that I could take back with me. I would have loved more in the other world before returning. Something unique that would need to be done first before being rescued. Even the lodge felt like it was going to be a lot more, and to just get dropped off with little to it beyond some exposition felt like something was missing from that whole segment. Despite this, I adore this game, and will for sure enjoy playing it for many many more hours to come.\t\t\t\t\t
