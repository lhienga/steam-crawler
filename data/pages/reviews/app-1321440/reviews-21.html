Definitely a unique take on the genre, an emphasis on status effects and there is the ability to make some pretty broken combos.

I will say this, the music is 10/10. Joel Baylis is an amazing musical artist. The music in this game is so impressive. I love it, and many of you may as well. Give the soundtrack a few listens. The story is alright, it's basically an isekai story, if you know what that means. The major themes are akin to American Gods by Neil Gaiman. There's a lot more deeper you can go with the monster catching than I chose to, as well. They did a good job with the flavor text for the monsters.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t**__Cassette Beasts:__**
Overall Objective Score: *8/10*
Personal Score: *9/10*

Cassette Beasts is a true love letter to those who love pokémon that Game Freak Head Honchos themselves could give less of a ♥♥♥♥ to give us. It is a really special game in my opinion and anyone that even remotely likes pokémon should definitely give it at least shot, at its default price I'd even say it's worth it.

**__Gameplay: 8.5/10__**
The gameplay in cassette beasts isn't revolutionary by any means for an RPG but for an monster catching game it has incredible innovation from Pokémon. From fusion to every battle being a double battle it is a true breath of fresh air. It's post game is pretty enjoyable but gets repetitive very fast.

**__Story:__** 8/10
Cassette Beasts story is mainly a mystery throughout 3/4's of the game with not many huge answers given until the last act. I found this to be a positive and a negative but mostly a positive, the characters are also very good.

**__Music & Graphics:__** 8.5/10
The battle music can get a tad bit repetitive but what RPG doesn't have that issue. The music here is almost all incredibly enjoyable chill music and all of the more action battle music kicks up even better to be an awesome experience when you fuse!

**__Performance:__** 7.5/10
I ran into a few issues/glitches with cassette beasts nothing by any means to be game breaking but slight annoyances/take you out of the game.

**__Challenge:__** 7.5/10
The game is definitely challenging when you don't know what your doing but, later on it becomes very easy when you learn about a certain firey bug and a funny starter. Of course you could just not use it but I found it made grinding within endgame much more fun.\t\t\t\t\t

One of the best turn-based combat systems in an RPG.
One of the best games, period.

This game's systems make it an extremely solid, delightful playthrough. It has a tactical depth that is easy enough to navigate and settle into as a new player, and the designs of the characters, the creatures, and the bosses are all extremely appealing. Exploring the world presented to us does not get old, especially after unlocking a new traversal ability. The story, especially the characters, has kept me engaged as I fell in love with each and every one of them.
The game's systems feeds into a great loop that makes this product really good.

The small details make this a masterpiece.
The music, with how the vocals fade in during certain moments, heighten the gameplay to an immeasurable degree.
The thought and care put into all the bosses, keeping them all fresh and unique not only in their aesthetic design, but in their strategy and tactics as well.
The little blurbs in the bestiary, the minor romance system-
It all makes this game unforgettable and genuinely really fun for me!

I can't recommend this game enough- I'm glad I purchased it when I did.\t\t\t\t\t
