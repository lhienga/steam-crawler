\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe real pokemon was inside you all along.

I love creature collecters, and this one definitely scratched the itch. The battle system is interesting with lots of unique synergies and strategies, and the character design of both the people and the monster are both unique and interesting. I love the cohesive theme of the creatures being tapes (with moves as stickers), with bootleg versions, remasters, and various other tape-themed items and moves.

I wish there were a couple QoL improvements, as I'd like to grind out 5 stars on each tape but without any option to speed up battle animations (not to mention the weird part where I can't directly swap between my tape storage and party and need to manually put each tape in storage that I want to trade out), I don't see myself putting in the time to. There are also a couple strategies that kind of invalidate the entire game (looking at you, Random Starter Headshot), but in the end it's your choice to use them so I don't necessarily think they need a nerf.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is everything pokemon should have been for a long time. the battle in the subway tunnel where the fusion mechanic was introduced completely hooked me, it was phenomenal. I love the characters and their individual story paths, from cults to landlords, everything was so well done. The music alone is worth purchasing the game. I suggest experimenting heavily with your party, even minor things like having certain stickers on your cassettes will completely change the playstyle of the monster, for example, I had geargrind on my velocirifle, and instead of becoming an artillerex which is a fire type, he became a gearyu, which is metal type. The bootlegs (shinies) are what really got me into collecting in a way even oldschool pokemon couldnt. They not only change the color of the beast, but they permanently have a different base type, including GLITTER! not only that, but there are bootlegs of every type, meaning there are TONS of unique color palettes for every monster, and glitter makes everybody shiny (temporarily)! Getting a glitter type bootleg has been nothing but a difficulty increase for me, he gets me in so much trouble when a fight should be easy, but he will never leave my party, I will die and itll be entirely my fault and I am okay with that. One piece of advice though, evolve your dominoth (you're forced to record one as a part of the tutorial) at night. In my opinion, lightning is utterly broken, and wind is insanely weak. If you evolve dominoth during the daytime, you'll probably toss him in storage, but I evolved him at night, and when combined with my ramtasm, most things just get decimated. In fact, I was shocked to find that my go-to monsters are dominoth's night final evolution Mothmanic, and traffikrabs evolution Lobstacle, not really things ive found much later in the game. I expected them to basically be pidgey and rattata, but instead they have hard carried through most fights along with ramtasm.

I could gush about this game forever so TLDR: It's better than pokemon. this game is built on passion, and the devs are very talented. Those are two things no one could honestly say about the pokemon company and their "games" lately. the biggest issue I have with the game is that it feels a little unbalanced, I cleared almost every single fight with just ramtasm and mothmanic. Even one fight that punishes you for attacking recklessly, I was able to tank my own double buffed damage and kill the boss without even swapping or using a healing item. but hey, its fun to feel powerful, so maybe its a feature, and not a bug.\t\t\t\t\t

Cassette Beasts isn't one of those.
This game is so full of charm and wholesome fun, not to mention a bangin soundtrack, I couldn't put it down. It stands on it's own two feet in the genre, with it's own distinct style and mechanics, and everything from the monster design to the delightful pixel-art island was fantastic. I loved it.
If you have any interest in monster collecting games, Cassette Beasts is an experience that will remind you why you liked the genre in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t
