Gameplay exceeds what I normally consider to be the maximum threshold for quality, like a kid getting 108 on a quiz at school. The amount of depth and complexity is huge, and there are admittedly some completely broken strategy combos that can 1 hit kill postgame bosses because of this. If this was all the game was, then it would have been an instant sell, but then there's the story, which I'll get to last. The battle system has serious balance issues as a result. Sadly, you will be stuck with some pretty pathetic monster designs. Sure, they have good ideas behind them, but the execution of them is crap. The beasts look like they were failed attempts at creating Digimon.

Music was fun and grew on me throughout the game. Tons of good tracks. At first I disliked it, but it went places. The catharsis of certain fights really shined thanks to the OST, even the narrative dribble fights that take place later that ruin the game. Again, this is not the problem, but if you analyze the lyrics, the songs are shallow and arrogant, and I don't want to go past the letter limit on this review to explain it, but it's bad to say the least.

When compared to Pokemon or Monster Sanctuary, this game falls flat in storytelling. Yes, most creature collector games are rather simple in story, but this is an extra special level of bad. The reason is that the game does tons of projecting. While I can't say it is overtly political, it is very clearly created by people who don't know ♥♥♥♥ from shinola. The creators are clearly projecting their bullcrap based on their political leanings and atheist angst towards their parents. Remember when lying preachers said that Pokemon was Satanic? Well, this game might actually be for story reasons. Some of my objections are easy to explain and some are harder to explain. Without much detail, the reason I said this game is Satanic is because you align yourself with the angelic manifestation of rebellion and betrayal to fight a being named for extremely high levels of divinity and who is not too loosely based on the God of the bible. Welcome to dribble writing. At least it isn't as ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ awful as Iconoclasts, which was atheist dribble projecting hatefully at religion AND a terribly boring game.

Sure, they are meant to look like vampires, but never cause any trouble and they mostly stick to their own land. To make matters worse, your character breaks into their buildings to attack them, which is really messed up. I am not joking about this, and am dead serious at how bad this part is in the game. At first, you might think this is just one tiny bit, but it always gets worse. Fighting the Landkeepers is also a fairly extensive sidequest in the game, taking you all over the island and having you break into several buildings. What provokes this random mayhem in the private property of other people? Assumptions that they are somehow evil. Honestly, you don't really get into fights with them unless you venture into their territory, so this is really dumb. At least the various Teams from Pokemon were involved in organized crime, terrorism, and so on, but the Landkeepers are minding their own business, quite literally, when the protag breaks in and attacks them. If you did that crap in Texas, you'd be shot dead and good riddance, so yeah the "heroes" of the story regularly take part in acts of trespassing, vandalism, and assault.

That's right, one of the characters gets you to commit crimes for your socialist "utopia".

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to preface this review by saying that I loved this game. I wouldn't play 90 hours of it and 100% it if I didn't really enjoy my time with this game. That being said, I'm an overly critical person and this game is certainly not perfect.

So the good: It's essentially a 2-dev game with custom artwork for all the combinations of all 128 pokemon cassette beasts and it's really neat that they put such care into the art. The art itself is also unique and distinct, and the tailored lyrics to the situation your character is in is really interesting to see a game do. The type mixes are confusing, but they're at least scientific, and hey - I liked that it was grounded in more realistic chemical reactions than arguably a lot of creature collector games.

Alongside all that, the story itself was engaging and compelling and I haven't enjoyed a creature collector game story in a good ten years. It was mysterious, and kept me wanting to learn more and play further. And it was never easy to guess what would happen. The archangel designs contrasting the game's aesthetic was also a really cool feature, and the bootleg versions of creature types and sticker TMs really gave you a lot of customisation for your teams, along with the fusion. This game was an enjoyable game.

But it was also incredibly frustrating, and here's why. I'm gonna be brief:
There's a lot of late-game crashes that increase the more you use your sprint/dash. The game cannot load in the world as quickly as you can sprint, and even the "loading in the rest of the world" jarring, out of place loading screens do nothing to help the loading issues. And if the game can't load it in, it either completely stops your character with an invisible wall or hard-crashes your game.

The character relationships are ok but ultimately disappointing - The characters are kind of boring, get better as you get to know them but even then, they're pretty mid, and spoilers: but none of the relationships are meaningful nor ultimately matter because you - and the characters themselves - leave it all behind, even with a romantic relationship. Which is weird, because you get the excuse "I can't go there" but then the dog can, so why can't anybody else? Another complaint is that the game heavily favours Kayleigh, and you lose out on a lot of early game lore, dialogue and flavour-text about your situation and even game mechanics if you ditch Kayleigh and go with Eugene or someone. Which is nuts, because the game is barely voice acted, so it wouldn't have taken them much to adjust early scenes for other companions. So the whole relationship/romance system is there, but ultimately feels hollow.

Speaking of hollow, the stickers/TMs and bootlegs somewhat trivialise the gameplay. It ended up being very min-max, with the "strat" being Glitter Kephri with Headshot and Random Starter, or a normal Kephri with "type-change" support that trivialises the types. I enjoyed the fact the game gave you that level of customisation because it gave me the freedom to choose beasts based on aesthetic choices, but kind of killed the incentive of trying out different beasts, because why would I bother when I could just change the sticker?

The end grind is also pretty insulting. I finished the main story in 50 hours, and only realised there was "more" after seeing the achievements. The "more" is a handful of quests hidden behind fetch&carry "townboard" quests that have you running across the map (that loads in really slowly!) doing busy work in what is essentially radiant quest padding between levels. I had very much checked out of the experience by the end of the grind, even with the ritual candle "exploit" to speed up the process. It felt like a really lazy way to create extra content when it started really strong with unique quests, especially as this grind was only accessible at the very end. If you'd let me do these missions throughout the entire game? It wouldn't have felt half as terrible. But I found myself struggling to boot up the game for such a grind, which sucked, because it was so strong before that and the cracks started to appear.

Anyway. It's not a bad game. It has flaws, but I enjoyed my time with it, and it's definitely worth the price, and an incredible job for a two-person team. I'll keep an eye on their future games for sure, and I don't regret my time with this game - but I'm content to uninstall this and never touch it again.\t\t\t\t\t
