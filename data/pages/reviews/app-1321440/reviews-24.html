This game was better than I ever imagined. I got it around $15.99, but I waited around before playing it. Once I started, I couldn't stop! I would also like to preface this by saying that 99.9% of the time, I DON'T LIKE OPEN WORLD GAMES, it's just that this is the rare 0.1%.

Gameplay:
The Gameplay loop of this game was perfect for me. Personally, it kinda reminded me of Persona 5. For people who haven't played Persona 5, the gameplay loop is explore, battle, capture, and talk. There is a decent amount of dialogue, and story options in this game, and you even get to romance a partner character of your choosing. The main story is also quite interesting. Sometimes you may get tired of the story though, and that's when the exploration, and battling come in. The double battle system offers a lot of chances the create unique, and powerful strategies, but the same applies to the enemies. Things rarely get boring! The only "problem" I have with the battle system is that it can get a bit too easy if you choose your monsters a bit too well, but the problem can be solved very quickly by just increasing the enemy's smartness, or pure levels to make the game harder. The catching part is also quite fun, as it's not just using catching items and praying. There is a whole catching system that I won't explain right now, but it's fun! After all, this battling, and catching the stories, and small cut scenes during resting periods can give you a nice break from the action. Oh yeah! The last thing is exploration. The physics in this game can be a little wonky, but that's a good thing. It allows you to get to places you weren't intended to go to if you can logic your way through the physics system, which can make you feel smart. You also get upgrades during the game that change your exploration (climbing, dashing, flying, etc). Overall, I would give the gameplay of this game a solid 9/10! The only reason I removed 1 point was because the puzzles in the game can be quite repetitive after a while. Btw, there is a monster called Triphinx, and it learns the move "Wonderful 7." If you want a challenge, don't pick up that monster, because it breaks the game with how easy it becomes once you get it.

Characters/Stories:
I adore the characters in this game. They are all written so well, and they are just so cute. Plus you get to date them. I don't have any problems, so that's all!

Art/music:
The art in this game is phenomenal! Shout out to @SmaiART (Twitter) because they made amazing art! The music in this game is also the second-best videogame soundtrack I have ever heard (Persona 5 is still num 1 in my heart). I bought the vinyl, and CD if that says anything!

This game is a 10/10! Even at full price, I would recommend you buy it! I will edit this review once I finish the DLC.

//Warning, I will discuss how I feel about the ending//
Ending feelings:
I felt kinda empty when I finished the game, but it wasn't in a bad way. The ending was good but just wasn't what I was expecting. It took a day or two for me to just accept it for what it was, and love it all the same.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts is basically a Pokemon game with some added twists. It's got cool monsters, good art, and catchy music. If you like the classic Pokemon games, you'll probably like this too.

I wasn't big into Pokemon as a kid, but I know lots of people who were. Played a bit of Fire Red and Leaf Green, and every now and then, I want something similar. This game hits that spot perfectly and without having to set up emulation.

It's got the usual stuff: pick a monster, build a team, evolve them, and beat leaders, here called captains. The world's fun to explore with some simple puzzles. One nice thing is that when you get abilities from certain beasts, like flying, and you don't need to keep them in your team to use them.

The game's pretty easy overall. If you're a hardcore Pokemon fan, you might want to crank up the difficulty. I played on normal and it was a breeze until the final battles. You can get strategic with team setups, but you don't really need to at the normal difficulty.

There's also this other big story in the game, which is pretty interesting, but I won't spoil it.

Overall, Cassette Beasts is great for anyone who wants to play a chill monster-catcher with a fresh twist. It's easy to get into and a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
