\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow is it? 10/10 for most aspects of the game. The story and narrative had given me chills at times. Especially when the lyrics to one of the songs was used by a character in the face of a fight against one of the bosses of the game. Bosses, or Archangels, are special as they embody a concept. The point of this game is centered around fusion and transformation into the many monsters this game provides, with archangels being the ones that through everything in the story just seemed out of place. Often using a different art style entirely and often they monologue over their general concept. This game also has some of the most interesting boss mechanics I have seen in a monster collector game in a long time. Sometimes I look at this and imagine how Pokemon as a franchise would have had better success if they made the game as engaging as this one is. Sure, Pokemon has a story and different regions, but none of the actual boss fights or challenges in the game really require the end user to strategically make decisions nor is there inherent challenge. Pokemon has a difficulty slider of simply "use worse things" but at some point it does get a little frustrating that the last time i could even worry about a battle coming up was in black and white with the extremely random Cynthia encounter which especially since you can access this fight right out the gate was a true challenge and i actually lost against them due to said surprise. This is why I feel the inherent challenge Cassette Beasts gives makes me want to play this even more. I also know that at some point the developers of this game plan on adding more content and an entire multiplayer function would prove to be a lot of fun. Overall, while I am excited for the dlc coming for Pokemon scarlet and violet story-wise I don't really look forward to it in any other way right now. This helps keep me busy and even challenges my play-style very often. Sure there may be OP combos and the like but even then if something shuts down your next move then you become an easy target that could lose all of your HP in almost a single hit from "the old 1-2" or even from something like a multi-targeted last rites! The soundtrack in the overworld could use a few more tracks but only because of the fact that a lot of preparation will use the same song over and over. Though the soundtrack is so good that I often don't mind. The songs with lyrics are especially nice, the first time i even heard lyrics in a song in a Pokemon game was gen 5 and village bridge. Later the same game came Colress. While I appreciated those tunes I would often find myself wishing I could play some songs at will. This game can technically do that also. Rematches with bosses are also a thing while some Pokemon games fail even at that. Often opting to only have the E4 be rematchable. In this game? Everything is rematchable and often adjusts itself to go about undermining your strategy, causing you to have to rethink or re-plan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic!

Listen, i was in the target demographic when Pokemon first came out. I love a good creature collector game and i don't think there are enough of them. Of the one's i've played however, this is by far one of the best ones. This game does not get enough love, more people need to see this gem.

I am not the kind of person to want to replay a game a second time. I don't like redoing things and even when i do try to replay something a second time, i usually only get about halfway through it before i get bored of it. This is one of the few games i did two play throughs on. The first one i did all the end game stuff and the second one i skipped the endgame but i still beat the main antagonist and got to the ending. It's that good.

There are so many improvements on the genre over other creature collectors that i could gush about the fine details for hours. Also the music has been stuck in my head for like a month now and i love it.\t\t\t\t\t
