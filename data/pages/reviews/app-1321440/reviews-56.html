\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts is an incredible game. Rather than trying to pointlessly reinvent the wheel, this game features a number of innovations to the standard Pokemon formula on both the large and small scales. For example, the reaction-based typing system creates much more dynamic gameplay than Pokemon's simple type effectiveness. Another example is the combination of a few mechanics in the game's levelling and move system. Levels are tied to the character, rather than the beasts, who instead have a separate level system that controls number of move slots, moves learnt, and this game's equivalent of evolution. Furthermore, once a move (sticker) is learnt, it can be "peeled", put into move storage, and given to any other beast that's compatible with it. There are a few other ways to get stickers and a high amount of universal stickers. Overall, this combination of mechanics allows the player to immediately integrate a beast into their team, since it inherits the player's level and has immediate access to their move storage.

As for other aspects of the game... The game has an okay story and characters. There are some nice moments to it, but overall it's nothing particularly exceptional. The graphics are nice, and the beasts have a pretty good design (do note that the design philosophy is a bit different to Pokemon's). Character customisation is unfortunately fairly limited, with a disappointingly low amount of clothing options. The music is pretty good, though.

You don't need to have played Pokemon to enjoy Cassette Beasts, but the comparisons are inevitable - not just because this game is a creature collector, but because it unashamedly improves upon Pokemon's gameplay systems.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is absolutely some initial clunk, but this game takes wild and unexpected swings for something that could've just stuck the formula and been a perfectly normal pokemonlike.

There's Shin Megami Tensei and Digimon DNA mixed in. There's a working physics engine. There's a vocal version of seemingly every song in the soundtrack. There's surprisingly deep lore. You can fuse every gad dang pokemon into every other gad dang pokemon. I don't... I don't actually know why they did that. That's a monumental amount of design work for a payoff that the average player is barely conscious of. And the whole game is like that.

Cassette Beasts is casual and cozy. It's got heaps of sidequests, Legend Of Zelda style puzzles, and mons to catch. Character customization is weirdly deep, and you can peel moves off of mons and put them on other compatible mons, and this includes passives and specials. The move list is also bonkers wide, and a *lot* of them aren't just +damage +type stuff. Every tenth move I see is metagame defining in its potential. Not to mention type interactions aren't just plus damage. There's an unhinged number of them, all with highly specific effects, and STAB bonuses don't seem to be much of a thing, so you can aim for really wide coverage with your movepools.

Or you can leave move slots open, because some moves scale their effects based on the number of open slots.

It's good.

Now, on the downside, the game does start a little slow. Before you've gotten a few mons and acclimated to the way combat works and gotten some traversal abilities, map exploration feels clunky. This improves, but you have to be willing to get over the initial hump. Also, the game's cozy tone is not always... completely cozy. There is *stuff* going on in the metaplot that is extremely compelling, but less nice than you'd expect from the genre.

Ultimately, I'd say Cassette Beasts feels like a weird DS monster catcher rpg that never quite took off, but developed a strong cult following ten years after launch. If you like specifically that type of game, you're going to have a *phenomenal* time here. If you're just looking for a pokemonlike, this is still a good one, but be prepared to spend the first few hours getting acclimated.

Edit: Rolled credits. Holy crap this was made by like three people, publisher and voice cast excluded. Also Yuri Lownethal is a voice in this? What a weird gem of a game. Definitely a bit neither-fish-nor-fowl with its pokemon color palette and SMT tone, but it takes some wild swings and hits more than it misses. Strong recommend if you like monster catchers and you're looking for something cozy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is easily one of my favorite titles of 2023. If you're a Pokemon fan like myself and have been disappointed with the vast majority of Pokemon games released in the past several years, you are going to love this game. If you don't care about Pokemon, it's still an enjoyable RPG in its own right. The turn-based combat is a bit more advanced than Pokemon, but not so much that you'll feel overwhelmed. The quest lines are actually intriguing and the characters are decently written especially the companions. It has some good boss fights with the Archangels. There's also a local co-op mode that lets another player take control of one of your companions, which is neat! All of this backed by a solid soundtrack.

As of this writing, the developers are working on an update that adds online multiplayer for you to explore the overworld together, fight rogue fusions, trade cassettes and battle with other players. This review will be updated once that goes live and I have a chance to try it out.

TLDR: This is one of the best non-Pokemon monster taming games yet.\t\t\t\t\t
