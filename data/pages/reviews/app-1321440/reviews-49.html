Like pokemon, but instead of capturing the creatures and using them, you record their tracks onto audio cassettes and transform into them yourself. You have a companion with you, for which there are several options, and if you progressed in a relationship with them (which for some can be rather tedious) you unlock the ability to fuse in battle - to combine your beast forms into one powerful combination. Pretty fun to use.

Visuals
5/5
Rather simple, but colorful and nice. A lot of cool beast designs and even more fusion forms.

Music
5/5
Gorgeous. Soundtrack is great. Sets the mood for battle and rest just right.

Story
4/5
Pretty simple to be fair. But that doesn't mean it's bad. I finished the main quest in ~20 hours without hurrying. There are also things to do apart from / after main quest and there's a DLC which is nice but also short.

Overall
4.5/5
It was fun to play. I plan to go back after some time to play through the DLC and maybe complete later-game quests. And to chill - Cassette Beats is great for relaxing and having a fun game session.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good monster taming game with many unique mechanics that keep you thinking, with a very 80's aesthetic to it.

Combat uses a buff/debuff system for the monster type system; fire sets poison on fire, but poison gives fire an AP Boost buff, but neither interaction directly increase/decrease the damage of the attack. While you can just attack without a direct damage penalty, the potential buff you're giving may require you to weigh your choices.

On top of that, moves use AP that regenerate during combat. Stronger moves require waiting or leveraging other mechanics to get them out faster.


The monster themselves have wide variety of moves, attacking,status and passive, and a large number of slots to put them in, so you can put together some devastating combos with some set-up.

With all these factors, even the weak basic moves a monster starts with can remain useful throughout the game.

On the recommended difficulty, unless you break the game in half, the fights that should be difficult will be, so you can't really power level your way through the tough encounters.

Combine all of this with an amazing cast of monsters and characters, with an amazing soundtrack, and you've got this monster collecting gem.
