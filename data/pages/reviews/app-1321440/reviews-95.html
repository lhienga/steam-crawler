As a person who has been following monster collector / creature collector games since Pokemon RBY, Cassette Beasts is not simply a clone but another monster collection turn based RPG that stands on it's own feet without relying on it's inspirations heavily.

I've dug my hands into other monster collection games but this one stands along side the big names in the genre like Shin Megami Tensei or Any of the Digimon games (have yet to play a DQM game, looking forward to playing those games).

The game does not play like pokemon aside from adopting the type advantage chart which works widely different in cassette beasts compared to it's counter part and the "ranger captains" which replace gym leaders but the way both are implemented are tweaked enough to be unique and not be a straight up rip off . The game's battle mechanics is well executed, love the sticker system for moves since it further incentives you to capture new creatures that you might never use but you still get "stickers" from them. Levels are human character based and not connected to the cassette beast so you wont have an issue capturing an under-leveled beast then wasting time grinding them up, you simply slap on compatible stickers and maybe remaster them (remastering is just the game's version of pokemon evolution) the beasts have their own levels indicated by stars, each star slightly buffs them up and give you a new sticker from it. There are even further small tweaks here and there that I wish other creature collectors rip off from cassette beasts and make them a standard in their own franchises *looking at you pokemon*

Every human character is interesting, the monster designs are great without looking like fakemons which alot of the indie monster collectors fall victim to. Love the monster designs with some standouts, the over world exploration is fun with some top town zelda-esque abilities which you gain as you progress through the game.

Based on how much the developers followed up with their updates on lenna's inception I can see this game having a healthy future. Great job Jay and Tom, liked lenna's inception but this game tops it easily.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my highlight this year!

It's a monster collector like Pokemon, but different enough to feel fresh.
There are 14 Types, but unlike in Pokemon there's no super effective/not very effective, instead it's debuff/buffs/type changes. Fire on Ice? It's effective, but turns the Ice monster into a Water Type which extingushes Fire lowering their offenses. Plants on Earth seeds it and puts leech seeds on every attack.

But my favourite part got to be the "shinys" in this game, called Bootlegs. Instead of every monster having one different coloured version, it's 14! And that's not all it does, it also changes the Type and the moves it learns.
For Example: There's a Fire-type dog but you found a rare yellow one? It's a Lightning-type now and learns Lightning-type moves.

The game also looks beautiful and runs smooth. It controls really well and once you get more movements abilities you'll zoom through the map.
There are also fusions who look different depending which two monsters you fused together. Oh and there's a built in randomizer.

Negatives:
- It crashes randomly, luckily it autosaves on every exit and fights and loads fast.
- Only 128 monsters. You'll see the same ones a lot, and because there's not a big pool it means you'll get spoiled what your evolution looks like before you get to it quite a few times. The Bootlegs do help here somewhat.
- Bad inventory management. You get so many stickers (This games TMs) but no way to sort them by Type or AP cost.
- The ending is too bittersweet for me, a 2nd choice would've helped here.


Overall probably one of the best games I've played the last 5 years, and I hope we get more monsters through updates and DLCs for years to come.\t\t\t\t\t
