\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti really didnt wanna give this a negative review cause its a pretty good indie title with a lot of love put into it, but its just not for me.
battling is cool but very slow and became pretty tedious for me only after a few hours, and while the battling system aims to be complex i dont quite think it hits its mark (but there are pretty cool ideas there, such as side-effects for type advantages)
the fusion mechanic i feel is misadvertised. i went into it thinking it would be a thing you could do at any time to give yourself a super customizable team and have each "monster" feed into each others strengths, but... nah. you cant even do it till you get closer to a party member, and you arent going to see a lot of fusions ever anyway, since team building doesnt really seem that useful at all (since everything is just tied to your player character)
the grind is also super annoying (and i know jrpgs and grinding go hand in hand but theres something about the way this game deals with it that annoys me)
the writing also kinda bothers me, its just weirdly meta and not in a charming or funny way. the characters arent too interesting either imo
that being said though this is objectively a good game with a lot of love poured into it, i love the monster designs and the music is alright, i do also like how open everything is, but i just dont really find it that fun in execution.
i WOULD recommend giving this game a shot just to see if you enjoy it, but this review is negative just to sum up my feelings. that being said, i love indie devs, keep doing what youre doing\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd say it's a pretty good 7/10 pokemon-like game.
It's just that nothing it does really stands out or sets it apart, and it has nothing special going for it.
The monsters design and art is ok but like 70% of them are pretty boring, and a lot of them are tbh pretty ugly, especially the fuses. The battle system is ok but a bit weird, the catching mechanic has WAY too many animations and the character health is barely relevant. Some items having the option to completely trivialize the combat also. The story and character were really not interesting, and a lot of the movement abilities in the overworld felt finnicky. But still I had a good time, capturing and evolving the monsters was cool, the game was challenging especially given how much you can customize it's difficulty.
I recommend it mainly for people that likes creature-collecting games like pokemon, as it does it's job well as a creature collector game, but less as a game as a whole.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPutt simply, this game is fantastic, and If you're into rpgs or monster collectors, this is a must play. The battle system is fun and deep, the elemental type system is intuitive and opens up many strategies. The designs of the creatures are among the best in the genre, and the special bosses, Archangels are... lets just say this game is best experienced spoiler free.

There are a few things that I hope the devs fix mainly:

-Tape/monster storage could be much better. Mainly the ability to sort tapes within categories and the ability to manage partner tapes from the tape storage screen.

-The Post game is decent, but very grindy. Grinding 150 fused material per level is absurd, and I think that it could be toned down, at least up to level 6.

-Some sort of post game battle facility would be nice. Even making the Infinite Railway mod official would be enough.

-Call for help and Treasure Dig are NOT good enough to be only 50% accurate

TLDR: Buy Cassette Beasts. Whether you're a fan of or frustrated with modern Pokemon, you wont regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts had been sitting in my wishlist for a while, mostly out of fear that another upcoming monster taming game would be underwhelming. IMO if a game can put a nice spin on the genre and actually attempt to face the BEHEMOTH that is Pokemon, hats off to them. Giving Cassette Beasts a chance was a great gamble

-Story: 7/10
The type of open world game that throws multiple objectives on the map and is like "go do all this to complete the game" which isn't my really my cup of tea, but really fun nonetheless

-Art: 9/10
All the character portrait art and promotional art is phenomenal along with the world and creature design, but the open world sprites felt... off, for lack of a better term. (only when exploring the map, not during battle. Those sprites are nice)
-Monster Design: 7/10
At the time of typing this I haven't completed my Bestiary, but all of the monsters I've encountered have really charming designs. The game kind of loses me at the fusion mechanic though (Granted I know for the sake of game creation they could give every possible fusion it's own hand made sprite, but some of the fusions look a tad goofy)

-Soundtrack 1000/10
This soundtrack goes HARD! and its only supported by the fact the the music gets more dynamic if you engage with the in-game mechanics. the same mechanic also applies to the mellow town theme. being instrumental as you walk around town, but entering a building ramps up with vocals for an even soother tone

-Combat: 9/10
Made me think more than in Pokemon. Instead of individual moves having a use amount, you build up spendable points each turn that you can use on said moves. Instead of types just doing more or less damage to each other, they have specific reactions with the games chemistry system, which makes battles less of a mash A simulator. There's also and essence of resource management since you can only hold a limited number of healing items. Now I can't just buy a million revives and beat my opponent via war of attrition

-Misc Notes
-The companion are cool and all have pretty compelling stories that are really grounded in the setting. Although some companion quests take you to late game before you can fully utilize them
-The mix of 2D sprites in a 3D environment could be jarring to some people, but I think that's what makes it charming
-OMG the boss designs are so cool and I wish I could ramble about them but its definitely something a new player should go in blind to
-The open world puzzles aren't the most attention grabbing, but they are a nice break from constant questing/leveling/farming and who doesn't like some free goodies\t\t\t\t\t
