When I first found Cassette Beasts I assumed it was just another game that was very similar to Pokmon, and since monster taming is one of my favorite genres I figured I might as well give it a shot. I was correct in the fact that I would enjoy the game, but it's extremely different to Pokmon. I'm going to go into more depth in this review but if you want a TL:DR, here's that. If you want a monster-taming game that really changes it up compared to the others out there then this game is perfect. The graphics are beautiful, music is probably one of the best I've heard in an indie game, it's full of interesting puzzles that use fun abilities you unlock throughout the game, super unique monster designs and fusions, and an actual open world that has *some* level scaling, meaning you have some freedom on what to do first. If Nintendo wants to improve their future games they should really take notes from these indie devs.


I actually had to shorten this review by 11,501 characters and I'm so salty about it lol. Pastebin link at the bottom if you for some reason want to read the rest!


Pros:
  • Double battles were always my least favorite type of battle in Pokmon, and also one reason why I don't want to go solo in Temtem, but I (surprisingly) enjoyed it a lot in this game. Not every trainer/boss battle is a 2v2 and may be a 2v1, and sometimes when its a 2v1 they'll bring out another monster when you defeat the first one so it's not totally overwhelming. Also if the trainers monster your fighting is at low HP and the move you use is high in power you have a chance to kill the monster AND the trainer, meaning you don't have to fight the rest of the team! Fusing, even though doesn't happen every battle, makes battles much easier and enjoyable. It's not exactly OP since it means you only get a single turn to use a move and your types are combined meaning there's a chance you get more debuffs from enemies. Also if you die as fusion both monsters die.
  • This game probably has one of the best soundtracks in any indie game. Tracks like "Face Down", "Like Chimeras", "Your Inception", "Deus Ex Calibur", and "Arrow of Time" are SO good. (BTW some of these may be spoilers so I don't recommend looking in the comments of these songs if you haven't played yet.) I think the only other game that could compete with the #1 indie game OST is Risk of Rain 2. My only complaint about the music is in the beginning of the cons section.
  • Having more then 4 choices for moves and being able to change them out easily is super nice. Also love that you can remove a move off your monster and you can reuse it later. I guess that doesn't really matter in Pokmon since most TMs are infinite.
  • The player abilities are really fun! The only one I don't like is the swimming ability . It's too slow. But the magnetism ability is so fun and there is so many unique puzzles around it.
  • Having the choice to switch out passives and have multiple passives is super nice.
  • There is no such thing as "super effective" or "not effective" moves. There is still match type ups, but its based on buffs and debuffs. This system is extremely unique and you really have to think about what your using instead of in the most recent Pokmon games you can literally use any damaging move and still win. In this game you can actually put buffs on enemies if you don't think about what your doing.
  • The fact that you don't "own" the monster and that you transform into it really brings a lot of variety into the battles. It's also super cool that you can land a debuff on a trainers monster, then when you defeat it and the next one comes out it still has it on.
  • Though it doesn't happen often, I absolutely love when the camera knows when to move to show around the corner. You can't manually change the camera but the places where its set feels appropriate.
  • The level up system is also super interesting, and I really enjoy it! With the catching system you don't "catch" the current one your battling, you just copy the monster and its moves over and you get your own. So the monsters you catch don't have levels. But instead you have a player level. And every monster is the same level since its your level. This means you can record a monster in the early game and be able to use it in late game without having to level it up at all! Also super cool that in the main town you can change your player stats (melee, ranged, etc) and monsters you recorded gets affected by it, but also has its own unique stats.
  • The game is basically perfect on the Steam Deck, and I assume on the Nintendo Switch too since similar controls but I can't say the same for the performance. I don't even wanna try the keyboard controls honestly. Though I will say I was constantly getting mixed up with the ABYX buttons. Especially since A is the interact button and X is talk. I feel like they should be the same.
  • I quite enjoy the randomly spawning Rogue Fusions. Adds that extra bosses to fight when you don't have anything else to do.
  • If you click the quit button it'll warn you when you last saved. Super handy and more games need this.




Cons:
  • I really wish you could turn vocals on for the music more often! They are locked behind being inside a building or being fused in battle. Also, hearing the vocals while reading text can be distracting. They should just implement a button where you can turn it on and off at will in post game.
  • There is way too many moves that are pretty much the same exact thing, like on Meredith's signature monster I had like 3-4 moves that did electric 60 point damage. It's just bloat.
  • I can't recall if Coromon and Temtem have this, I know Pokmon does, but I really wish when going to apply a new move to a monster it would show which can be applied on which BEFORE you click on it and press apply. Just a nice QOL. I know you can go to your party and change a move and see which ones work so maybe this isn't an issue but I dunno.
  • Going into a cave/room and opening a chest and it being a Rogue Fusion only annoys me more then anything. If they wanted it to be a mimic chest I wish they would at least make a small difference that I would notice before engaging. When I open these chests I'm not prepared for a big battle like that. Funnily enough, there actually is a post game legendary that IS a mimic. and luckily it does fix this issue because it will talk to you and ask if your ready to start the battle. But this only applies for this monster and not the rogue fusions like I said above...
  • Even though I really like the player abilities, it seems weird to lock them behind catching a specific monster. Luckily the game will give you hints on what to catch but at the same time it seems super easy to miss it and never get a hold of it. For example, the Bulletino speed boost ability is not necessary I think but it sucks that some people could miss out on this awesome upgrade!
  • I'm not really a fan when rogue fusions change up the map (adding lava/water) that much. It was much more manageable when I was in post game with all the abilities but before that I didn't even bother with them.
    Saving does not always put you in the exact place you saved. I saved in front of this one NPC, because I wasn't sure if it was a captain or just a trainer that wanted a fight, and when I found out it was just an NPC and couldn't be bothered at the time to fight him I restarted my game so I could start off where I was when I saved. But instead it loaded me in the train station. This isn't a huge issue, but is slightly annoying.



    Anyway, not even gonna bother writing a conclusion. No need to just rewrite everything I said but shorter. Just buy this game if you enjoy Pokmon/monster taming games. It's cheap and infinitely better thenPokmon Scarlet/Violet.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA monster collection game whose main story can be completed in tens of hours (w/o a guide) (I'm still nearing the end at 63 hours causing me to question what "nearing" means, but I've put some time into alternate runs already). There is stuff to do in post game, like finish your collection and compete with others. There is speedrun and nuzlocke support.
Unlike some, you don't confine the monsters, while fighting monsters you capture a recording and can later become them. Your avatar and companion level, and the monsters that you use gain moves and evolve. This allows you to be highly effective when you transform into a monster you just recorded. Your effectiveness is modified by your avatar's level, the move power and type and the target's type forming a substantial elemental matrix. There are also a couple of surprises.
There are 128 monsters, but you can also merge with your companion to create whatever 128 x 128 unique new and very powerful new monsters. You can also record "bootlegs" which have a different type than the original monster. The bootlegs can also affect the type of parts of the moveset. The mechanics are simple and deep.
The travel music has a lovely coffeehouse vibe, with some moderately exciting battle music. The tracks don't get in the way, they do enhance the experience.
The stories, each companion has a story and you are motivated to follow them and complete some stories of your own, are pleasing. Each companion has a unique and fun story.

Note: It has become clear that it is possible to play the game for many more hours than I "initially" thought before the final curtain.


I would highly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
