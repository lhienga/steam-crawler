If you wanna romance this game angles towards the cutesy side but this is no Fire Emblem romance it's just wholesome romance. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my fav game in years.

Pros:
- Cute pixel art graphics.
- Open world works great. The game can get easy as you become stronger than certain areas, but I did not care: I had creatures to evolve and the fights can be quick enough that It was not a problem.
- Exploration is fun. There are secrets to discover and map abilities to unlock.
- The story is also interesting and delves into some interesting existentialist questions.
- Great variety of creatures to evolve and develop.
- Creatures can be modified considerably. Lots of fun combos to try and put together
- So many types vs types effects. Looks overwhelming, but I learned them fairly quickly and they add a lot to the fights and strategies
- Not too long. I finished the game in 35 hours. I wanted more when I was done, which is rare for me. I usually rush to finish a game in order to start a new one. (There are so many interesting games to play)

Cons:
- hardcore gamers will find the game too easy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is another one of those games that preys on nostalgia but doesn't capture the charm of what it's trying to emulate. It has all the surface-level makings of Pokemon, plus some extra, and is well-polished but doesn't feel nearly as satisfying to play as Pokemon. Part of it is the aesthetic, which doesn't seem to have any cohesion around the creature designs while having this weird western indie aesthetic for the characters. Another is the battle system, which relies a lot on buffs/debuffs and feels really tedious to play, having all the features of an RPG battle system but without any of the strategy. There's the overworld, which seems like it wants to be filled with environmental puzzles but simultaneously does nothing besides serve as spawn point for enemies you will dodge constantly and take you from point A to point B.

I could go on, but if you've played games like Cross Code you probably understand what I mean. It's trying to capture the Pokemon audience, and maybe it does judging by the Overwhelmingly Positive tag, but Cross Code also used to have an Overwhelmingly Positive tag capturing the Mana/Alundra audience, but once you play it you see it's just the veneer of the games it's trying to imitate and what they add to make it their own feels out of place and worsens the experience. It feels very much just a western indie dev's version of Pokemon, and the elements they added I could do without.

There are a number of these types of games I've played. Sea of Stars, Cross Code and Cassette Beasts I would rank as the worst. Couple of the good ones:
- Symphony of War (Fire Emblem)
- Siralim Ultimate (Dragon Warrior Monsters/Monster Rancher)
- Unsighted (Boktai/Zelda Minish Cap)

I don't know what it is about western indie devs but they don't seem to understand how to tell an endearing or charming story. All of these types of games have the worst writing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts is phenomenal to the point where I'm having difficulties fully expressing it. It is easily the best Pokemon-like game on Steam and it's not even close. Hell, I would even go as far as to argue it's a lot better than anything the Pokemon franchise has wrung out in years.

The core gameplay is that of your standard creature-collecting RPG a la Pokemon, but has more mechanical depth beyond just the rock-paper-scissors style of battles; heavily emphasizing buffs/debuffs and status conditions, not to mention the fusion mechanic, too.

At times, it's reminded me a lot of the Persona games more than it has Pokemon - thanks in no small part to the game's stellar story, premise and themes - as well a jamming soundtrack and a bunch of challenging and tactical boss fights.

Loved my first playthrough. Still have to see what the post-game and the DLC have to offer, but based on what I've done already, Cassette Beasts is absolutely one of my favorite games of the year.\t\t\t\t\t
