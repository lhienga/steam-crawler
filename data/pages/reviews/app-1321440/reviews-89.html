\r\n\t\t\t\t\tY'all are inevitably going to compare this to pokemon, and I'm not even gonna pretend that my lifelong love of those games isn't exactly why I bought this. Which is a damned shame, because this game really isn't quite that. I mean, nothing much manages to have the sort of charming monster design and style that pokemon has, but this game feels like its more interested in making a name for itself on other strengths, namely being a really homey little JRPG, and I'm not sure how else to describe the vibe of this game, but I absolutely love it.
Like, you meet NPC companions, and they all just kinda give off this same, really charming vibe of "We're all in this together" that meshes well with the setting, and just gives the game cozy feels. It captures the emotions you're looking for in a game trying to capitalize on your nostalgia for older games in its genre without actually trying to be the same as those games in a really masterful way I've never seen before.
But it's polished and modern and just interesting in a way that alone makes it worth recommending. It's got good style and energy behind it, despite me coming in skeptical that the cassette thing would be more of a weird retro gimmick from a decade before I was born, but no, it's just good stuff, at least from the first few hours I've seen.
And the turn-based combat going on has strong pokemon vibes but is clearly trying to make for its own identity- the type chart (which the game hands you as an item early on, and you're free to check in battle, thank god) is mostly built around free status conditions, positive and negative, rather than big damage buffs, and it's a solid take. I'm having fun.
Strong recommend on a game I was half expecting to refund when I bought it, tbh. I've been disappointed in enough retro-styled games like this that want my nostalgic-for-old-pokemon money that I was going in skeptical, and was proven soundly incorrect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts offers a refreshing take on the monster-battling genre. While drawing inspiration from other popular games, it boasts a distinct and appealing identity.

Initially, I had reservations about the 2.5D design, as it was new to me. However, it worked really well with the art style and is a great showcase of what can be achieved with the Godot engine.

The monsters possessed unique characters, exuding a sense of originality. Similarly, the characters encountered throughout the journey were remarkably charming in their own unique ways. The subtle exploration of themes like the environment and urban development were really well done, without feeling "in-your-face". The incorporation of an evil team comprised of real-estate agents was a fun addition.

Undoubtedly, the highlight of the game for me was the soundtrack. The songs selected for different scenes matched the stakes perfectly.

The only criticism I have is that the game did crash once or twice in my play-through (losing some progress from lack of saving). It would usually happen when I tabbed out of the game, but otherwise it felt really robust.\t\t\t\t\t
