\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the best creature collectors. It's just solid in every category.

Good stuff:
  • Great music (which is rare in a creature collector)
  • 2v2 battles allows for support/attack specialization
  • Good open world exploration with lots of goodies to find
  • You can recover the stickers (moves) from your monsters to use on different tapes
  • Tons of strategy potential from move combos and passives
  • AP system makes you choose a mix of weak and strong moves
  • Innovations like fusion and bootleg monsters (different type than normal)
  • Monster name puns
Type advantage/disadvantage applies a debuff/buff instead of dealing more/less damage. I think this is more interesting than a damage multiplier, but I'm sure someone will have a problem with it. The buffs and debuffs are genuinely good too.
You level up your character rather than monsters. This means when you record a new monster, it's already your level and you don't need to train it forever.

Bad Stuff:
  • Even boss fights can be trivial once you make a half decent build
  • Random chance to record a monster can be quite frustrating
  • Random drops
The random chance to record monsters is the most glaring issue. I've spent dozens of tapes trying to record the same rare monster before finally getting it.
Recovery items can trivialize combat. Enemies just don't use items, but you can. Debuff removal items can be used without using your turn, which sounds nice but essentially makes you immune to debuffs. Remember the only penalty for type advantage hits are a debuff, so now type advantage doesn't matter to you.

Overall, the game does a great job of scratching that creature collector itch and trying new things, a few complaints aside.\t\t\t\t\t

This was a tough one for me.

One of the first things that hit me was in-game music with singing. This happened in the middle of dialog, and I couldn't pay attention to the text at all because the vocals were so loud. Thankfully, there's an option to turn vocals off in the settings, which helped a ton. Characters would also often say / shout / yell out random phrases at the beginning of their dialog, which irritated the f**k outta me. You can disable this by turning voice volume to zero in the settings, as well.

Speaking of, there's a lot of fluff dialog in this game to the point I found myself getting quickly annoyed just trying to get the information related to the story / quests. Additionally, there's often pop-ups that try to explain things, mechanics, etc which in themselves aren't bad but there's sooo much of them. Oftentimes found myself having to resist the urge to just skip through things so I could get back to the actual gameplay, and during the actual gameplay found myself wishing I could speed up some of it somehow (some animations just seem to drag and drag.)

I think the artwork is great, love the designs of the creatures, but the "evolutions" (remasters) didn't make much sense to me. I get that you can have different outcomes depending on the day/night, but there's a lot of humanoid designs and I guess that just isn't my taste.

There're various NPCs you have to bring around with you in your party, and are quest / story related. This was a huge turn-off for me, and you have to use them (as far as I know) or you will be lacking a slot for a creature in combat. Wish there was like a multi-deck or something where I could just throw creatures out and be solo, but I suppose that defeats the purpose of "transformations" (you transform into creatures to fight FYI.)

Mixed feelings on the combat. I really, really don't like the AP system. I want to be able to use the moves I've found / learned / unlocked without having to "waste" a turn doing a weak filler move. I like the sticker system (think HMs and TMs) because you can "peel" off moves and keep them to use on another creature. I like the different types of moves and types, but all the interactions between them were confusing at first. Creatures either feel very weak or very tanky; haven't found anything truly in-between.

The 2D / 3D is done mostly well, until the camera randomly flips on you when you turn a corner or go down a narrow path. It was super jarring for me and sometimes would get stuck flipping around because of the way it changes your perspective / controls.

Honestly shocked to see this game be Overwhelmingly Positive, but I'm glad people have been enjoying it. The genre definitely needs outliers like this trying to innovate and do better than their source material. This one just didn't feel too good for me.\t\t\t\t\t

Best monster tamer I've experienced
Cassette Beasts is such a unique monster tamer experience! The world is filled with numerous adventures, puzzles, beasts, and companions you can romance! Play through storylines at your own pace with whichever companion you want to bring along. This game is perfect for a more casual player like myself who doesn't enjoy punishing death mechanics. You can flee from any battle, even if your flee attempt fails you get the option to leave the battle at the cost of losing a few ressources which can easily be regained.

I cannot praise this games variety of storylines and puzzles enough! I am enthralled with the world of New Wirral and my passion for exploration has been renewed with this game.
While I usually rely on wikis to help me through difficult quests in games, Cassette Beasts has made me choose to actively avoid seeking out any guides. This game has made me love discovering things on my own, and I'd encourage others to do the same.

Fun battle mechanics
I love that we, the players, get to choose our companions moves in battle, allowing for more strategy and leaving less up to chance. While recording a beast, it cannot be knocked out, allowing you to try and try again should your recording attempt fail.

The type chart is accessible in game at any time, yes even during battle! Unlike a game like pokemon, in which types only effect is a damage increase or decrease, Cassette Beasts type matchups have meaningful effects. If you attack an air type beast with a fire type attack, the air beast will gain a damage absorbing air wall. Attack a metal type beast with a poison attack and it'll be coated, dealing more damage to your own beasts when either party uses a melee move on the coated beast. This makes strategy all the more fun!\t\t\t\t\t

I have only a couple nitpicks about the main game:

- The comedy is really hit or miss. For me it was more misses, but there were some good moments.
- The ending kinda sucks.
- I only really found myself interested in Felix's story, and the Shakespeare references with Viola seem comically out of place.

The post-game and 100% experience, on the other hand, soured me on it a bit.

-Once you get into a potential rhythm of how everything works, you realize just how ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ long each combat is. Pokemon, for all its faults, has an option to turn off battle animations for a reason. -The overworld dash's ability to start a combat with a Fire element attack is sometimes detrimental. It wouldn't be as bad if you could just avoid Air-type enemies in the overworld, but because you only see one monster of a potential encounter you can bump into something hoping to beat it quickly only to have an Air Wall suddenly block you. (This is, of course, compounded by the above issue.)
-Speaking of: the Ranger board quest to knock out certain types of enemies is really rough. The whole Ranger Noticeboard system is half-baked to begin with, but given that not every enemy on the board can appear on the overworld it can become a grind of 'run into enemy -> escape because the board target isn't there -> repeat ad nauseam'.
-Several of the fast travel points are in really awful spots. One of the mods I installed fixed this by allowing me to also fast travel to campfires, but before that if I wanted to go anywhere on the north end of the map it was a frustrating endeavour of dodging weak enemies to leave, for example, the Mall or the Titania.
-The overspill mechanic is cool in the main game, where you can coordinate your fusion and attacks to end combats with other humans early, but it's a concern if you leave a low-health monster out. The post-game removes that functionality entirely from enemies, but keeps it for you, so not only does human combat become less interesting, it feels like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ rules patch exclusively to screw you over.
-The post-game grind is so slow.. Seriously, you need 435 Fused Material to unlock Orb Fusions, which are the only way in the game to get more than one Fused Material at a time. Otherwise, it's static encounters and quests. Once again, this is compounded severely by how slow combat is. (This might be alleviated a bit by the Pier of the Unknown DLC, but I doubt it.)

Overall, like an 8/10. Don't 100% it unless you're a moron like me, though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best not-Pokemon "Pokemon Clone". I've played a lot of games inspired by Pokemon - TemTem, Coromon, Monster Sanctuary, you name it - and Casette Beasts blows them all out of the water. The two most common pitfalls for games like this are either that they're basically just Pokemon (in which case I'll go play a Pokemon game I like instead) or they introduce some original stuff but aren't engaging enough to keep me with them. Not so with Casette Beasts.

It's not only got a lot of engaging overworld gameplay, but it's also got a fun and unique aesthetic, some fantastic monster designs, and really interesting and unique type matchup effects that go far beyond just doubling and halving damage and will entertain both intense strategists and casual players alike. The writing is pretty good too!

My only advice is that if you're like me and you intend to stick with your starter for a while, look them up so you don't get stuck with one you don't like all that much, especially because it's gonna be a LONG time before you get the other one. I'm 7 hours in and coming to terms with the fact that I think I want to start over with the other starter. I'd also suggest you minimize what you look up online after that point - I've been having so much fun just grinding to turn my Beasts into their next form and discovering the weird and wonderful new monsters they turn into.

10/10, and I'm a harsh grader.\t\t\t\t\t
