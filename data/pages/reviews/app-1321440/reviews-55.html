\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been a very long time since I've come across a game in this genre that is difficult to put down once I start. Many days ended in literal hours of "one more sidequest". On top of that so many of the beast designs are incredible and inspired. The amount of work that was put into this aspect of the game is incredible and the devs deserve praise and support for this alone. But there are other reasons to praise what they've done too.

Aside from that the game includes features that should be the envy of other entries in the genre. The bootleg system as an alternative to rare/shinies is incredible and I can never look at other game's versions the same way again. The levelling system makes it less of a grind to add new beasts to your team and you can get a new beast to decent combat level within a few fights. As for type matchups the game's coating system gives it a chance to explore interactions that nobody else can offer - because of course an ice type beast will temporarily become a water type when you hit it with fire! It makes so much sense.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my weaknesses is gacha style games because I love seeing the different designs. CB does a great job with their creatures, making them all feel unique. There are very few you could compare to Pokemon, and even then, they still stand on their own. On that note, they do a cool job with bootlegs (this games version of shinies). I like how the typing changes offer advantages in battle and, when they level up, they create better moves. As for criticisms, I had hoped the fusion system was going to be more similar to something like Shin Megami Tensei, adding a deeper level of collecting, especially since the partner system seems to be drawing inspiration from Persona. Also, the puzzle solving never evolves beyond simple switch and button pressing and the end game is a huge grind. The noticeboard tacked on 13 hours of gameplay but I wouldn't say it was fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn conclusion, Cassette Beasts is the best Pokémon like game to braise the world in recent years, and that’s coming from a die-hard Digimon fan. I found myself falling in love with the story, the art style, the world, the beasts, the fusing mechanics and much more! The ability to take moves from one beast and apply it to another meant levelling up multiple beasts were actually worth it, as you could stockpile deadly moves. This is a game ultimately made by Pokémon fans for Pokémon fans, improving on ways that bring more customization and freedom to the player than Pokémon does. All this dressed in a lovely purple and green colour schemed UI that really pops with the cell shading art style.

Overall Rating: 10 – Masterpiece!

Let's start with what actually made me try out this game and won me over immediately: exploration and movement. Bytten Studio made travelling around entertaining rather than a chore: it's not just walking around and stocking up items between battles (those are still there, don't worry), it's actually exploring a well designed environment with puzzles and what not. These aspects are well thought and meshed together to provide an experience that hardly becomes repetitive, and managed to keep me hooked for a good while.

As if all that wasn't enough we have battle animations, extremely curated sprites, catchy themes/songs, a whole new take on the shiny system... It'd take a wall of text to go over all of that, so I'll keep it straight and simple: Cassette Beasts offers what Game Freak always refused to give us. A map that we actually interact with, curated animations, (much) less grinding, a hassle-free way of switching around party members and their loadouts.\t\t\t\t\t
