\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompleted, 8/10:

Pros:
-Music is great, and the way they activate or deactivate vocals depending on the situation works very well.
-Monsters have interesting designs, and don't feel generic in the way many other Pokemon style games do
-Fusion system is very interesting
-Monsters tend to (for the most part) be balanced, and even if they're strong in one way, they'll be weak in another
-Built in tools for special playthroughs unlocked after your first. (Tapes break permanently, defeat is game over, randomize monsters, types, and moves, and custom seeds.)
-Two main stories that you can play through at the same time, that being both the rangers, and the Archangels.
-Archangels tend to be actually dangerous, and can defeat your team if you're not careful, even with fusions.
-The elemental system involving debuffs rather than straight damage multipliers is quite interesting.
-Basically every monster is viable, since you can move the stickers (which are what gives each monster their moves) between tapes anytime you like.

Cons:
-Mobility system feels very weird, and doesn't tend to work that well. This mostly goes for the rolling ball and electric abilities
-There are a few very overpowered strategies that, once discovered, make the combat extremely easy (Glasstain + Gambit + Fusion + Glass Cannon = instant win 99% of the time)
-Fusion is made out to be something that's hard to pull off, but once you get a few upgrades, you can pretty much pop it whenever you want without worry of needing it again before you recharge.
-Rangers were very underwhelming, and could be defeated without much effort, even without fusion. (Except for the ghost one, that one was just annoying.)
-Battle animations can take forever, especially if there's multiple effects being applied to a monster, potentially even multiple times.\t\t\t\t\t
