\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCassette Beasts has all the makings of an amazing monster collector/battler. The story is simple, yet compelling. The characters, both the partners you hang out with and the opponents you end up stomping down along your adventure, are all well written with their amusing and quirky personalities. The monsters, for the vast majority, have cool and interesting designs, as do the Archangels. The Ranger Captains and Archangels both make for fantastic boss fights, each one bringing a different strategy or gimmick to the table. Taking them down isn't often easy, but you can turn the tide of battle with the power of Fusion. Fuse together with your partner to combine the stats and movesets of both of your monsters! Fusion is key for getting through tough battles, so unlocking the ability to fuse with the different partners you meet should be your top priority. Honestly, my one knock against this game is that Eugene can join you at the beginning of the game but you can't unlock his fusion until WAY later. Eugene is cool and he doesn't deserve to be put on the bench for that long. For those who are more obsessive about the collection aspect of the game will be happy (or driven crazy) by the inclusion of bootleg monsters. Not only are they a different color, they are an entirely different type! Whether an Ice Pombomb or an Air Carniviper, bootlegs can get you that much closer to building your perfect team. And the perfect place to hunt for bootlegs is within my favorite feature of the game, Rogue Fusions. Rogue Fusions are fights with two random monsters fused together with a higher chance of one being a rare monster or bootleg. This, coupled with their inherent difficulty, makes them for very exciting battles. Lastly, as many already know, the soundtrack is as amazing as you'd expect from a game that revolves around people putting on headphones and listening to cassette tapes. It is no exaggeration to say that the battle tracks in this game are the best of any game I have ever heard. The best part is how each track starts as the instrumental version, and when you fuse together the track transitions into the vocal version. Seriously, what is cooler than that?
Cassette Beasts is an amazing game and one of the easiest recommends I've played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay is amazing. If you're a fan of old-school Pokemon, this is a must-play; though the assortment of monsters is a bit on the smaller side. Move management allows you to turn your monsters' moves into TMs at any time. Some moves and statuses are also really interesting. For example, Chemical Imbalance is a passive that has a chance to proc a move of your choice whenever you land an advantage hit, and the Ghost status effect makes you invulnerable to direct damage until the timer runs out, at which point you die.

Exploration is similar to a Metroidvania, and it's good. You unlock abilities and go back to familiar places to unlock new paths.

Good pixel art, but somewhat repetitive animations. I was not a fan of vocals in background music, fortunately there is an option to turn it off. The story is its weakest point, in my opinion, but it was interesting enough to keep me hooked.\t\t\t\t\t

The fusion system is great and really adds to the drama of battles and decision making involved too. And on that note, the battle system has good changes when compared to Pokemon that make for more unique and refreshing combat. Overall a solid game, and a rewarding experience too.\t\t\t\t\t
