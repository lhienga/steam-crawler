\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeyond the Baltic Sea DLC for me is very underwhelming and this is the first piece of major content from SCS that I struggle to recommend. The studio may have increased the size of the map development team but this has clearly hit the end-quality. The map expansion boasts of 13,000 kilometers of new roads, 24 new cities and towns spanning 5 countries but on the whole, nothing feels new or unique. Major cities are far too bland and small and there's just too much copy & pasted elements outside of them. The game is also really beginning to show its age.

The most frustrating issue I found was the contstant fluctuating speed limits in some areas with constant sharp declines and random increases. It was impossible to get any driving momentum going - you can go from 50km/h up to 70, down to 60, back up to 80, then sharply down to 50km/h again in rapid succession. I had about 5 different speed limits in 15 seconds in one job and the unpredictable decreases cost me several tickets. Clearly the scaling is a problem but the amount of speed sectors and general errors with the speed limits all factor into the annoyance.

Overall, Beyond the Baltic Sea simply doesn't offer enough to justify the price of ¬£13.49 and what you do get is nothing we haven't seen before. Compare this to the Oregon DLC for American Truck Simulator; that was only ¬£8.99 and felt extremely well-crafted from scratch. Whether I'm in Russia or Lithuania, it still feels like France or Sweden with no new feel or stand-out assets. The frequent changes of speed limits and errors on top just leaves me feeling that the DLC is not worth it especially at its current price.\t\t\t\t\t
