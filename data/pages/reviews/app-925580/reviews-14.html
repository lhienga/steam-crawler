\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my All Time Favourite Computer Game Ever. What can I say. Over 750 hours and still enjoying it. Whether your focus is campaign (economy game, become the lord of a trucking empire!), or customising your truck fleet, or just exploring 100 percent of the enormous map, there's a whole world here for you to get into.

My personal approach was a little unconventional: I built a USB game controller out of a bicycle on a trainer stand, and now I pedal around Europe for exercise and sight seeing -- I have turned it into Euro Bike Simulator! But prior to the bike idea, I campaigned happily for hundreds of hours with a USB wheel and pedals, driving my favourite Mercedes truck all over Europe, Iceland, and Russia. The vehicle physics is good enough that ETS2 taught me how to back a (single) trailer.

Some of the modelling is superb, the recreation of national signage and roadside details is meticulous, and the sim is open enough that there are many, many community mods: custom vehicles, map expansions, cargoes and companies, weather and scenery hacks.

ETS2 is one of the best kept secrets of the gaming world, a simulator that stands head and shoulders above most others in the genre. The only other sim I could compare it to is XPLANE-10, a truly open world (the whole planet) but with lower-grade scenery. ETS2 can teach you the geography of Europe, introduce you to the architecture of different regions, and provide a cheap "vacation" when your local scene is dark, cold, or boring. A ride or drive through the vineyards of southern France on a gorgeous summer day is a great antidote for the winter blues! Seriously, get the core game and give it a try; it's cheap to get started. If you already like driving sims and have a wheel and/or pedals, so much the better.

If you ever manage to explore 100 percent of the map (I never have), you can always swich to American Truck Simulator and start exploring the US! One or two new states added every year. I own both sims and love both of them, but have spent way more time with ETS2.\t\t\t\t\t
