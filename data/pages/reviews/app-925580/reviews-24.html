\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been waiting so long for this expansion to be released and now I can not believe its finally here :) As an estonian, huge thumbs up from me. To the details... I really enjoyed the exact replica of the TLT-livery Tatra KT4 tram in Tallinn passing by Narva maantee. If the tram tracks in future developments would also go to Tallinn airport (line number 4) it would be extra cool, as Tallinn is currently one of those unique cities in the world where you can get straight from plane to tram. I also enjoyed seeing our railroad crossings, new Škodas used by the police and of course, the refrigerator-alike speed cameras :b The "cherry on top of the cake" for me was the opportunity to now virtually drive in Laagri (suburb of Tallinn), where I live. One funny thing also to end with: I saw an M62 loco passing on Maardu railway bridge, which went out of service in Estonia already a decade ago.

Huge respect to SCS for finally bringing the Baltics into the game!!!
Aitäh!!!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good DLC with a huge road network to explore which covers every corner of the new map.

Pros: The countries feel different and they have their own charm to it with unique buildings, landmarks and road signs.
Extra care is taken to create local vehicles such as cars and trains. Ladas and UAZ vans look very authentic!
There are also some new interesting cargoes to take.
Each country has their own railroad crossings and traffic lights.
They took their time to recreate all the rescue and police vehicles.
Russian border crossing is unique and has just the right amount of annoyingness to it.
The landscapes and vegetation are all top notch, and reflect reality quite well.

Cons: Too many russian vehicles in EST, LV, LT. (But it's ok, since they are great models)
Some buildings or areas appear to miss some detail props around them.
Lack of pedestrians (usual issue).
Russian backroads in perfect condition.
But those are the only issues I have and looking at the size of this map, it is impressive.

Recommended DLC so far! Extends the base map in a major and impactful way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing from St Petersburg, I can tell you I've been waiting for a DLC like this since day 1. And I'm glad to say that SCS didn't disappoint! This DLC brings in the Northeastern European countries of Latvia, Lithuania, Estonia, western Russia and southern Finland. I can say that St Petersburg was recreated in stunning detail considering the fact that they needed to shrink everything down, everything feels natural and the way it's supposed to. The road signs, advertisements, cities and roads look just like real ones. The new border feature is great and works well. The waiting time isn't too long but realistic enough, perfect for multiplayer environments with high player counts. Going from Russia to Finland feels very real, and the contrast between the two countries is very apparent just like it is in real life. I'd recommend this DLC to anyone who lives in Northeast Europe or is interested in these countries.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaven't seen too much of the new Baltic region, but so far, so good. Very impressive! Bought a new Garage (for me and my mod trucks) in Helsinki. Cities are big. Very big. I started the journey to the Baltic Sea region from my former garage in Stockholm, going to NS Chemicals for a load of Carbon Black Powder going to Saint Petersburg's Port. That city is huge as. Wish I could have seen it in the day. Maybe next time. Good Work and Kudos to SCS for this DLC. Quality is what I expect, which is nearly the best. Only Red Dead Redemption 2 is better environmentally, and that's a AAA game. Only thing I have to complain about is the near constant changing of Speed Limits on the rural roads, but that's trivial.\t\t\t\t\t
