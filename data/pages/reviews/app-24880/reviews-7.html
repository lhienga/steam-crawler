\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game needs ASAP a remake.

Imagine if GTA, Assassin's Creed, Hitman and Wolfenstein had a baby. That's the Saboteur in a nutshell.

So the game is about Sean, the Saboteur, that undertakes missions for the French Resistance in WWII. You will drive through the nazi occupied Paris, disguise and infiltrate buildings, bombing the F out of nazi targets, and shoot whatever is moving.

As an idea is absolutely great and that's why it needs a remake. The ambiance is spot on, and the black-white filter on the occupied areas make it like a film noir. The story is half-baked in a sense that it has some nice characters but terrible plot and story. It feels more like random side quests than a proper story. The pacing is atrocious. I quitted it in the beginning of act 3, as the second act felt like a whole game altogether. The world is a bit empty as all you have is a mechanic, a gun trader and a million (not kidding) towers, lookouts and antennas to bomb.

The worst is the gameplay and especially the controls. The game feels so clunky is baffling that got out of the QA. Jumping, stealth, parkour, climbing and movement in general feels so weird, it's game-breaking bad. Climbing a three-floored building takes an eternity, you will fall constantly because the game won't register the grasp, you will fail to draw a weapon since there is no animation cancelling and a ton of other problems with handling and controlling.

Still it's an interesting game and in a sale it is not a bad choice. But it needs a remake, so it can truly shine as it deserves.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed this gem a few times through as a PC game, but it's definitely not a PC game. It is all the cliches done well. You play a foul-mouthed, Irish race-car driver/mechanic turned resistance chad. You smoke, drink, womanise, race cars, shoot not-sees and blow stuff up. What more could you ask for (boobies?).
While I don't enjoy cut-scene games The Saboteur manages to get the balance just right. It has great voice acting, models and animations, a huge variety of NPC, side-missions and scenery.

I particularly enjoy the use of colour to reward your liberation of a B&W Not-see area. The recreation of 1940's Paris is spectacular. A basic HEAT game mechanic but the methods for escape are hilarious: run away, hide in a shed...or brothel, kiss a girl, go to a bar & order a drink or use a public urinal) so long as no one is looking). A bit grindy and repetitive and a completionist's nightmare. Otherwise a solid casual sandbox done well. Tons of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

So my first impressions upon configuring the game and starting was this is pretty good and this is horribly optimized for pc sluggish performance and letterbox bars on the sides of the display. After some tinkering with the settings I figured out that I can max out the resolution to my display without issue which is 2k 16:10 aspect ratio setting maxed out and everything runs fine with one catch being you have to have the games refresh rate no higher the 60hz. If you go beyond this you will encounter graphical and performance issues. with that said my limited time playing so far I am enjoying the game and would recommend it if you are interested in action adventure games with stealth mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t
