\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo much potential wasted.

The driving, parkour, gunplay and melee fighting are all surprisingly well polished and smooth by themselves and the setting also makes this pretty unique. It's kinda like a GTA or Just Cause in France during the Nazi occupation. So all of this is pretty good, why the negativ review then?

Well, first off the stealth sucks and it's a huge part of the missions. Enemies have an area around them that gets them alarmed when you enter, even if they don't see you. You can dress like a Nazi, but that makes you even more suspicious for some reason. If you climb too close to Nazis (even if they don't see you), you guessed it, they will hunt you right away.

I meantioned that the gunplay is good, but if you run 5 steps our little Irishman puts the gun away automatically and that got me killed a few times.

One of the worst aspects is the black and white filter. Areas you have not freed yet are black and white with some red details. It sure looks cool, but makes stealth and firefights unnecessary hard at times because you can't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ see the enemies.

None of this makes the game too hard to finish or anything like that, it just isn't fun to play for those reasons and I lost interest barely more than 4 hours in.

[The game runs really good on the Steam Deck with settings maxed out.]\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurprising how such an old game works so well on the newest hardware.

I have tried playing the Origin version many times and it was always ridden with crashing issues.
Here though I can set the game to 1440p no problem, Did have 1 crash initially but that was only when I alt tabbed.

So The Saboteur feels like a Mix between Just Cause and Assassins Creed.
You can destroy structures with explosives and climb and jump across rooftops.
Gunplay is nice and quite frantic.

Music is nothing to write home about but it gets the job done.

The thing I expected to enjoy the least was the story, But to my surprise it is all quite the opposite.
Like I said I do enjoy the gunplay but the story is fun to a point where I just want to focus that for the most part.

If you can get it cheap for like 5 to 10 bucks then this is a pretty good time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Saboteur is a really awesome open-world game. It takes place in a Nazi-occupied Paris. The areas of Paris are depicted as black and white or full color based on whether the area is liberated or not. I think a lot of the game being black and white is a fantastic touch. The protagonist is an Irish race car driver, Sean Devlin. Irish characters always make games better, so I immediately knew I was going to like this game. The Saboteur is a revenge-driven story, and our man Sean won't stop at anything to get it.

You can get plenty of time out of this game, even more than I did. The few problems I had with The Saboteur were entirely map-based. A lot of the time, the radar is super hard to read, and I relied on my memory to traverse the map more than I did the radar. I'm not sure if there's a keybind for opening the full map, but I know that when I pressed M, it didn't open. Traversing the menus to get to the full map is a bit tedious, but it could just be my mistake. I also thought the map was very oversaturated with points of interest. There aren't any achievements either, so I didn't really see any point in trying to 100% the map.

Overall, the characters were amazing, the setting was amazing, the story was amazing, and the gameplay was amazing. The Saboteur is definitely worth it. Now I'm just patiently waiting for EA to release the Mercenaries series on Steam.\t\t\t\t\t
