\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst up don't be deceived by the short amount of playtime I have with this game. I played it extensively on Origin and decided to buy it again to show my support. In fact when I launched the game my save data surprisingly carried over. If you are on the fence about buying this game then just stop reading this review now and go buy it, trust me it will be well worth your time. If you wanna know more then carry on reading.

Basically this game is a combination of 3 different games: GTA, Assassin's Creed and Wolfenstein. You play as Sean Devlin an Irish racer who was loosely inspired by the real life racer William Grover-Williams who worked as an agent for the British in France during WWII. In this game you are tasked with sabotaging the Nazis by destroying points of interest and killing high ranking officers. Apart from that you also have to resolve Sean's personal vendetta with Kurt Dierker, a Nazi colonel who killed his best friend.

Apart from the main campaign you can also freely explore and drive around the streets of Paris in a really expansive open world, especially for the time this game released. The way this open world is presented to the player is of course the biggest element of this game. As you know the game is in black and white, however once you liberate an area the color returns to it, an incredible effect that was never attempted before or since. You can blow up watchtowers, tanks, Nazi lookout spots and propaganda loudspeakers. By doing this you are rewarded with contraband, which acts as currency, and you also make traversing through the city easier since the spots will not respawn.

Lastly, the game has some really well made driving and parkour mechanics. You can drive and race around the entire map, and even fight the Nazis in drive by shootouts. You can climb almost anywhere you want, and usually you will be rewarded for doing this with extra contraband.

All in all this game is genuinely one of the greatest games ever made that's set in WWII and it was Pandemic Studios' masterpiece and swan song\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome this has finally come back to Steam, would have loved to have seen a remaster of this underrated gem of a game which is a real classic! Also works well on steam deck, set resolution to 720p as 800p is bugged/issue with it and to make it fill screen press quick menu button in game (3 dots) set scaling to stretch, then scaling filter to NIS have sharpness around 8/9 improves standard resolution quality and enjoy!

Also select gamepad with track pad support under controller settings in game, it works absolutely fine with this. Notice one or two reviews saying no controller support, this is incorrect as you just need to select it in the settings.

The Saboteur is a mix of GTA style open world with Just Cause style like elements destroying the Nazi War machine in occupied territories during World War 2, also has some stealth sections where you can disguise yourself to blend in and plenty of action! It's a truly unique game with it's distinct black & white with red accent noir colourings, also when you liberate Nazi occupied areas it brings back full colour to regions. Lots to do with plenty of main and side missions including freeplay elements and Sean has his own unique perks that can be upgraded after completing certain challenges, the music soundtrack is this game is perfect for the World War 2 setting.

Don't forget to climb the Eiffel Tower! You can also gain contraband to buy new weapons from destroying the Nazi war machine around the world map and finding famous landmark/beauty spots. When you find a landmark it does an Assassins Creed style 360 view of the area, if you haven't done so already add this absolutely classic gem to your steam collection, you won't regret it!

Highly recommend The Saboteur, 5 stars! 'Top o the morning to ya!' \t\t\t\t\t
