\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike going back home or seeing an old best friend again and it's like no time has passed. I fell in love with Le Saboteur back in the first decade of the new millennium, and I couldn't believe there was no sequel... and certainly couldn't believe that one of my fave publishing houses (at the time) would drive a stake through the heart of such a great development team. Farewell, Pandemic. We miss you!

I have not had a computer with a CD/DVD for a while, but the last one I owned, the disc that was in it was Le Saboteur. It created such an ambiance: period music from the car radios, Parisian cabarets, period weapons, everything you could ask for to support immersion in a 2009 title. There are so many things Le Saboteur brought to the table that other games have imitated. Driving crazy and decide to just run over bad guys in the streets or sidewalks like Saints Row 2, 3, 4.... yeah, it's here. Crazy parkour like Dying Light, unlocking a thousand places you couldn't get to without? Yeah, it's here. Great stalking and sneaking system, like... well Deus Ex, Thief, Far Cry, you know... it's here. Alarms going off and bad guys hot on your tail, and you're got to outrun that circle of awareness that's parked on your head as in Red Dead Redemption? Yeah, it's here. But one of the coolest features in the whole world is an idea I've never seen anywhere else. When an area of Paris or the surrounding region is controlled by the Nazis, the whole thing is pictured in Black and White like an old movie. Well... except for the Red... the red Swastikas, red lights, red whatever... But when you fulfill all the mission objectives in an area, which can include just some random murder of Nazis and other destruction (such as vehicles), then the area is transformed into full color expressing its new freedom from the tyranny of the bad guys.

The characters are well realized folks w ethnicity and backgrounds, with good voice acting--you get to play a hot-blooded Irishman named Sean Devlin, a race car driver and mechanic who's willing to take out his temper on the occupiers. You can interrupt them while their randomly abusing the locals--punch em or shoot em. And you get more points for nailing an officer than a grunt. Nice systems!

The one true silliness in Le Saboteur is the part of the game where you can earn endless experience and it's built right in. In some parts of Paris, there are anti-aircraft guns. If you climb the building and the platform, kill the troopers at their stations, you can take over the A-A and start shooting at zeppelins and other Nazi aircraft. You don't run out of ammo and the game will endlessly spawn new targets. Of course, the alarms will escalate and lots of bad guys and vehicles will begin to crowd the streets, but they don't climb like you do and you can shoot for a long time. You may die when you finally get tired of it and begin to escape--getting away from a Nazi company, AFVs, and maximally escalated alarms is not easy, but death is not the end in Le Saboteur. You will respawn, none the worse for wear--and all that new experience and unlocks remains. Pretty silly, but its as fun as an arcade shooter. Enjoy!
