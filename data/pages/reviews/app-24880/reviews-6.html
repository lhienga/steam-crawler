\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game for a lot of players, however it is also unfortunately far from perfect. To say that this game aged poorly might be unfair, but sadly true. The controls are clunky at best, vehicles are more like a second thought etc. I can see that a lot of heart was put into this game by the team, but there is just one huge miss that ultimately turned me away -
Why the hell can I pick up a STG44 in a German base in the year 1940? Did the dev team even bothered to look up what the 44 in STG44 stands for? It is also named MG44, which is not unhistorical, but it was named this way because their leader hated the idea of an assault rifle. Unsurprisingly, it then uses MG rounds in-game, and that is the final punchline. And no, this is not me being unreasonable or picky, because this game already features the MP40, which is an automatic weapon. The excuse that the STG44 is used for gameplay reasons is just that, an excuse.
I cannot in any way recommend this game to any player with a shred of knowledge and passion for history and historical arms. It is also a dated game with no modernisation nor optimisation to speak of, which always spells ill for modern PCs. This might be a great game for the collection, but the shelf is where it belongs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 15 years later, finally EA decided to port this gem to Steam. Back then, I never knew that this game existed until I saw a YouTuber that goes by "The Professional". He had done a play-through of this game, and the game has immediately intrigued me. I had never played this game before due to my Xbox 360 not working anymore, so I was unable to play this game until now.

A open world WW2 game is definitely very unique, and playing as the rebellion against Nazi Germany has never been fully explored, and I appreciate that this as games has never really explored this aspect during WW2. As most WW2 first person shooter games, or games in the genre as of whole, you were either a US Soldier, UK Soldier or a Soviet Soldier. Never have you been a resistance fighter.

The game has a very unique artistic style to where oppressed areas (Areas that have heavy Nazi occupation) is black and white. The only color in occupied areas is red, being from the flags, the armbands, the equipment, everything that has red will be shown to you very clearly, and the soldiers will have this aurora (Can you call it that?) around them. Areas that are more free will have color restored, from being black and white to full saturation color, and you can see the different colors clashing in the sky when you are around the boarders of areas that are in and not in oppression, which is a very nice and amazing touch for this game.

This is no generic WW2 shooter game that most of us would expect, but rather elements like parkour/climbing from Assassins Creed, third person, open world nature from GTA and you get this.

My only nit picks about The Saboteur is the automatic nature of reloading and going into cover. I've been used to having control over reloading and going into cover with other games, but I suppose this game never had such buttons to do. Nothing majorly bad when it comes to that, but it can be a bit awkward at times.

Very fun game, and may I mention, EA has not included their stupid, dumb Origin launcher. So that's a plus in my book.

Rest In Piece Pandemic Studios, you'll be missed, EA done you dirty, quite a shame.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI told myself when this game was finally put back on steam, that I would buy it. Well, EA actually did it. And I am so happy they did. This game is still just as fun as it was 15 years ago on the PS3. While this isn't an amazing game, there are not many games like this. I love this French resistance against the Nazi regime during WWII. Then add in the ambiance this game has, and the beautiful black and white to color when you free a certain zone, and you have a fun 3rd person shooter with some historical cues. The only two things that went wrong is the game crashed about 10 times while I was playing. Mostly in the menu, which is weird, but a couple times during a massive explosion. That, and the body movement in tight quarters felt like there is a dead zone in the controller movement. Nothing major, but I do want to mention it. Having said that, this is a great game from the ps3/360 era and it holds up well. I wish Pandemic survived so we got a sequel, but now this is on Steam, so it should be preserved for a little while longer.\t\t\t\t\t
