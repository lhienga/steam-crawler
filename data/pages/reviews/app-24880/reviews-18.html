\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played The Saboteur not long after the original release and had a blast. What a surprise to see it on Steam.

This Steam version seems to be the original version, not a remaster because I don't get to change the resolution anywhere and the graphic looks old. The game runs well on my Win11 laptop under 800x600 resolution, a compatible version for most current PC/laptops to run.

The most daring design of The Saboteur might be the color. That is, only until you liberate the area did the color return, otherwise it remains black and white. However the town is alive without color, NPC walks freely, they can dodge cars, buy newspapers at the newsstand, if you stand still your protagonist will smoke. I'm surprised to see this 15-year-old game with easy wall climbing and comfortable car driving, making this game so accessible. The shooting feels cheap though, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2 released in the same year, but you can't compare this action game with Activision's FPS. Besides, enemies are highlighted so you can see them well in b/w settings, and the way points are extremely clear.

The story is okay but can't reach today's standards, but at least they are not dumb. Some are errand missions that require you to travel half of the map, but those are not tedious when you can hijack cars, travel while enjoying views of atheists and landscapes in rural areas. It features some real places in Paris, you don't get to drive past Triumphal Arch in many games, also you'll get close to Eiffel Tower at the end, and I lament forgetting the strip club thing. BTW there's a switch for nudity in the option menu. 

I fast-played 3 hours and according to a full walkthrough, I'm halfway to the end. My goal is not to finish, rather, get to know the game I loved 15 years ago and 3 hours into it I have a good idea. You see, back in time it took me 30 hours to finish, as I climbed every tower and liberated every place, but now I consume contents x5 faster. It is inevitable that people consume content faster and require more nowadays, and the game industry strives with thousands of titles to choose from unlike when I was young had to stick with just a few. The Saboteur reminds me of the old days, it is also the few games that could survive 15 years of time and be made playable. Glad after all those years I still play.

New players might find this game outdated, but for a returning player it's all about nice memories.
