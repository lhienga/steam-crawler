\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on DVD when it first came out. Couldn't manage to get it to work and had to have my computer wizard friend come over and download 'who-knows-what' from the internet to finally get it functioning. Only last month I got rid of the game because I couldn't get it to work on my Windows 11 state of the art gaming system.
But hooray it was released again by EA and works like a charm. The graphics are dated but the story and missions themselves ease that discrepancy. You're Sean Devlin, the race car driving, smoking like a chimney, whiskey drinking, womanizing, wise cracking Irishman. Oh yeah...and you hate Nazis. You charge all over gay Paree shooting Nazis and blowing everything they have to kingdom come. Raise some hell and climb buildings a la Assassin's Creed to make good your escape.
You ally with the Resistance who give you bases of operations and in destroying things you're credited with cash to purchase newer and deadlier weaponry. Some of your missions are really tough so if you've never played you might want to start out on an easy mode.
Finally just listening to Sean's smart ass remarks and the double ententres from the games characters ("I have no taste for German cuisine." "Perhaps, but soon the women of your country will come to savor the taste of purebred German Knockwurst"). I mean, how can you not get a laugh out of that?
And be sure to allow nudity in the options menu. You'll thank me for it. Once I switched back to 'no nudity' and Sean's voice announced "Put the kids to bed already".

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think my words can do the game proper justice, but here it goes:
Long story short, you play as a member of the French Resistance during the Nazi Occupation of France.

The game does a good job of portraying life in France under the iron-jackboot of the Third Reich. Areas that are being directly suppressed by the Nazis are shown in black & white, basically having their color taken from them. However, when you start fighting back, and manage to take the Nazis down a peg, the color comes back into the world exactly the same as a bomb dropped from the sky.

The game has destruction elements that rival Just Cause's. It's also one of the way you earn contraband (the game's form of currency in this time), so there's extra incentive for that. Everything from loudspeakers, sniper towers, armored cars, there's dozen & dozens of things for you to blow up, earn contraband, and buy everything from guns, ammo, upgrades, etc. It also takes climbing & stealth elements like that seen in Assassin's Creed, except the stealth isn't nearly as annoying, and you don't automatically fail if you get spotted. Stealth is almost always option, but if you're like me, feel free to go in guns-blazing, if you think you've got the firepower.
Even your buddies in the French Resistance can help you directly in the world in places you've already liberated from heavy Nazi suppression, even allowing you to escape a hefty alarm level once you've progressed the early story enough.

This game is the last game made by the late, great, Pandemic Studios, known for also making the "Destroy All Humans" franchise. From what I'm told, they literally had to fight just to get this game released all the way back in 2009. Until now, GOG was the only means of playing this game on PC (not that I know of any emulation, either.)

TL;DR: This game is badass and has lets you punch, shoot, run over, and blow up Nazis & their stuff in occupied France years before the Americans came to liberate it once & for all. It's also got a pretty decent overarching story written into it. Much respect to Charles De Gaulle and the French Resistance; those guys & gals had serious BALLS to do what they did.\t\t\t\t\t
