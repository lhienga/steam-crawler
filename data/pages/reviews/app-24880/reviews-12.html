\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was so much fun for me its not very often that i get hooked on a older game unless i have a nostalgia to them. You can expect about 8-12 hours of gameplay if you are only focusing on story side of the game.
This game has way more personality than some of the AAA games released in the past year or two.
Just make sure to lock your fps to 60 game will otherwise stay at the lowest resolution.


Pros
+ Open world
+ Felt like i was actually in Paris
+ Movement from Assassin's creed climbing, running and jumping from roof to roof
+ Gunplay of an older Grand Theft Auto game as well as "wanted" levels
+ Disguise mechanic from Hitman
+ What an EA title that doesn't require you to use their launcher :O

Cons
- Story was a bit repetitive and predictable at times.
- Minor bugs on textures and sound likely caused by too new hardware/OS
- Game never told me who was In Paris

Easily the best 5€ i have spend in the last 3-4 years. This game is definitely a hidden gem.
I will give this game a score of 7.2/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn case you're wondering why I'm reviewing a game I have less than an hour playing it is simply this: This is one of my favorite games on XBOX360 (and one of the reasons I keep my console) and I have hundreds of hours logged into it. Then I got the game on GOG a few years ago and logged a few dozen more hours. The time I have on here was to make sure it works on my high-end PC (it does). But yea, I own this game three times for a reason - it's awesome. The plot is decent enough to carry the game forward, but really you play this game to blow up Nazis and liberate Paris and the Parisian countryside. This game is a fantastic sandbox for raising utter hell. The gameplay loop is an utter treat, the weapons all feel good to use (and each type caters to specific styles of engaging the sandbox), and once you unlock certain skills/perks? God does this game become legendary.

I miss when EA made games like this. Worth the $10 (or $5 on this release sale). It's one of my all-time favorites from that era.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI read that a lot of people had played this game when they were younger, but the game doesn't hold the nostalgia factor for me. Regardless, I really enjoyed this game.

A few things wrong with it, namely the clunkiness. Sean is fairly inconsistent when it comes to climbing and sometimes launches himself from a rooftop instead of climbing down, and headbutts the wall instead of catching the ledge. Other times, enemies will fall from the sky and cause suspicion which can mess up a well laid sabotage, or at one point, a magical floating sky Nazi spotted me trespassing and set off the alarm. Disappointing when you're trying to play stealthily. Checkpoints are a pain too. The AI will let you through, decide to close the gate on you then attack you because you broke the gate. Something unrelated is the tonal shift. It's jarring at times. The last mission was chaotic and grim. Then it goes back to being fairly comedic. The AI is pretty stupid, all things considered. From pedestrians running out into the road while I'm being chased by a convoy of Nazis (which I was penalised for) to Veronique just... dying. Repeatedly. Because she saw a tank and threw hands.

Other than that, It's a very fun game. Some of the weapons are a real step up (namely the Terror weapons and the Viper suppressed MG). and the chaos you can wreak is top notch. Granted, it's weird that you can blow up 3 tanks, 5 towers and a general and the enemy will still just look around for intruders quite casually instead of being alerted, but it's a small price to pay. Hijacking tanks and AA guns to cause mayhem and shoot down airships never got boring.

Sean himself is an interesting character, given that he's not your typical fearless American war hero, but a severely pissed off Irishman. And being from Belfast, a lot of the slang was nice to hear (except top of the morning, but I guess that's for comedic purposes). I couldn't help but laugh at him yelling, "That's for the Jews!" after tossing an enemy off of a building. His banter with other characters is great too.

All in all it's not a perfect game but for it's age, it was a blast to play. 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remeber when I first played this gem in 2009... such a great experience and, for its time, offered a very unique blend of action and stealth reminiscent of the Assassin's Creed games. They even implemented a disguise system from the Hitman, allowing you to take the uniform of any fallen Nazi soldier and use it to infiltrate enemy bases.

The game is also built in a way that allows you to approach objectives in multiple ways, thanks to the parkour system. While it can be a bit clunky at times, it remains fully functional for the game's map design. Additionally, there's a simple RPG system where you can unlock different perks based on your playstyle. These perks, for example, unlock new ways to stealthily eliminate enemies. It's almost identical to the perk system in Wolfenstein: The New Order.

Overall, it's an amazing experience with a really well-written story and memorable characters. Robin Atkin Downes with that fake Irish accent is just fantastic as the main bloke!

However, the game can be a bit problematic to run on newer hardware, especially with multicore CPUs. There is a patch available from the GOG version floating around the internet that fixes most of these issues.

Adding achievements or Steam Cloud support would be a great, but I'm not holding my breath for it.
No Origin launcher is a big plus
