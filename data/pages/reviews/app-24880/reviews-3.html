\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan this game brings me back.

What a wild time for gaming. In an age where licences come and go, more and more games now are seeing a massive resurgence in the industry. Games that seemingly disappear off the face of the earth without a trace. Games like Alpha Protocol, or Spec Ops, that get completely removed from physical and digital storefronts because of tiny little expiration and complications within the games licencing. I feel like this is a given but man it is so good to get these games back. Piracy is a such a complicated thing that because of today's climate with gaming (and even shows and movies), it's moreso encouraged to really keep those products alive. The fact that EA is now doing this with The Saboteur gives me hope that we'll one day see the full catalogue of delisted EA/Pandemic games. I mean they're all fantastic classic early 2000s games too.

The last I played this was when I was 7, I gave a copy to a family friend alongside physical copies of Mercenaries 2, and Flatout Ultimate Destruction. All super awesome games that unfortunately when I got them back, the discs have all been scratched to hell and no longer playable. It bummed me out for a long while and honestly I just had that memory in the back of my mind while moving onto bigger, better, bolder video games. Fast forward to now and not only do I get to experience all the Command and Conquer games on here, but now one of my childhood games is brought back to life. And it plays flawlessly, just as I remember.

There's some jank, but this is still such a solid video game. Honestly one of the best WW2 games ever. For a price tag of $14 I highly recommend picking this one up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolutely stellar game from 2009 finally freed from the clutches of the EA Launcher and GOG for the reasonable price of $10. The EAL version required some modification with the GOG patch in order to run at 1080p resolution however it's included with the steam release, as is the nudity DLC if that's you thing. The only thing I would hope to make it to the steam release are the achievements from the console versions but I wont hold my breath for it.

Others have had issues with crashes however with a 3090ti, 32gb ram, and a ryzen 7 2700x the game has run fairly stable for me with consistently high fps and only minor bugs on max settings and hasn't crashed on me once. The render and culling distance can be quite problematic and the cause of most of my issues while playing due to it's close proximity to the player character.

Misc bugs that have happened to me:
Cars and certain freeplay targets clip and slide around when popping in, sometimes destroying other objects or enemies/pedestrians
Sometimes Nazis punch and TK each other when popping in at close proximity
Sometimes the mouse cursor will randomly unfocus from the game, moving freely around my monitors while also controlling the game, can be fixed by alt tabbing
Nazi deuce-and-a-halves are constantly honking whenever rendered in.

It is still worth the 10$ its on steam, you should get it.\t\t\t\t\t
