This is the 2001 A Space Odyssey of open world 3rd person shooters and a work of PC art. If the Louvre ever puts video games in their art museum 100 years from now, this game will be in there. Tons of fun and replay value. I have played this game 10 times now and it still never gets old. Also, if you can't get the game to run in Windows 11, I got it to work with a simple change in 2 video settings. See my comment in the discussion forum. If you don't buy this, then you are stupid! LOL! Heck, I would compare this to the 1998 classic game Hardwar. Both are two classics with a ton of replay value. The funny thing is both of the developers went bankrupt but they both have cult following. Kind of like the typical starving artist problem going on here. Your art is worthless until you are dead. This is a Picasso and Hardwar is a Salvador Dali.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this game quite a bit. It's not a masterwork but it's quite entertaining and has a very unique style.

I never played any of the Mercenaries games, but this was kinda reminiscent of other sandbox games like Red Faction Guerrilla or Far Cry (3 and onwards). The style is basically the same and everything related to the liberation of Paris aspect also felt very similar to what you could find in those games.

The story itself is not great but was serviceable. I can see why a game like this went unnoticed back in 2009, when there were so many 3rd person sandbox games, specially having so many bugs and half-baked mechanaics but... sometimes it just works, despite all of that.
