\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn't bad as such and was probably a better experience back in 2009 but having played better games of similar ilk from that era this one doesn't quite satisfy. The style reminds me a bit of early GTA or Mafia but the map and gameplay feels bland and mediocre to me. The combat (and story) is cheesy and perhaps it's supposed to be. One of the first missions where you disguise yourself in a Nazi uniform is supposed to be a semi stealth mission but the soldiers have such a short patrol path they were frustrating to avoid. I teared up the base and went in guns blazing with no consequence. Maybe it gets trickier later on but from what I played it didn't feel much of a challenge. I quite like that you can go and discover areas off mission and punch a cow into giblets but I never found the map that interesting to explore. Shame 5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Saboteur was one of those games that I really enjoyed when I played it on the 360 but like with all hindsight seems like an experience that suffers from bloated content, dated and clunky controls, and a somewhat predictable story
made up of a bunch duochromatic (heh) characters. That being said it is quite an enjoyable sandbox game with lots of things to shoot, blow up, and wreak havoc in this fictional, Nazi-controlled France.
It has serviceable enough controls which really is impressive for a game originally released in 2009, but after climbing the 100th building the charm of climbing things and planting yet another bomb at the base of yet another tower starts to wear off and becomes a chore rather quickly. I resorted to stealing a tank to clear places out the ol' fashion way towards the end. You don't even earn achievements in this game anymore so the idea of collecting all the collectibles doesn't really factor into any kind of satisfying resolution and just feels like more fluff for the sake of fluff (but i guess that goes for almost all "achievements" in a way).
The idea of a game that combines the parkour aspects and assassinations seen in Assassin's Creed and the carjacking and racing of GTA is still something that is still fairly unique and I'm happy this game exists to scratch that itch.

6.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
