\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you look at the AppID number, you can see that this game has been in EA's backpocket since forever, and it's actually amazing that it ever even came out here on Steam at all after all these years.

It's pretty good considering its age. The setting is pretty interesting for an "Open World" type game, it has pretty good art direction, the mechanics are generally good , and it performs (mostly) stable for how cluncky old it is. The two things I'd really ding it for are the rather subpar "Assassin's Creed"-esque parkouring mechanics, and the absolutely excessive amount of meaningless "Free-Roam" "activities" (aka:Just things to blow up).


It's cheap though, so you'll definitely get your money's worth out of this game.\t\t\t\t\t

The Saboteur Finally Lands on Steam - A Classic is Here
I have very fond memories of The Saboteur on Xbox 360 playing it back over 13+ years ago and I honestly never thought I would have the chance to finally enjoy it again on PC, let alone Steam. So many hours playing this on console lol. This was a gem of a game from Pandemic Studios that really takes you back to a time when video games were just... made different? There's just something about this game that really allows it to stand on its own even in todays age. Even being a 15 year old game it handles well on PC and handles just as good as I remember.

Think a historic GTA V. Not as many car options or weapon options BUT great chaos, story, and voice actors. Graphics can be adjusted for your modern computer (which should be able to run everything on high, but if not it allows lower graphic options - you can do this when launching the game to edit resolution first before starting) and overall it's just been great nostalgia while, I believe, still being easy enough for people who have never played it to get into it in todays age of 2024. It's a blast and I am so excited to continue replaying it. This is genuinely one of my favorite games and a great addition to Steam.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that this is finally out on Steam I bought it as having played it to death on the Xbox I felt I needed to do myself the honour of having it in my library. I wasn't disappointed.

The GOG version had huge problems with bugs related to it not running on modern multi-core systems, the map was bugged and the saves where unreliable.

This Steam version has none of those problems. I don't usually have good words to say to EA but on this occasion someone who works there clearly liked the game enough to ensure this final release would be bug free.

The gameplay is extremely satisfying. Running and driving around Paris with the ability to climb all over the famous landmarks, blow up Nazi infrastructure and slaughter the 'master' race will keep you busy for a long time. Even after you complete it you can continue your personal one-man vendetta against the goose-stepping enthusiasts and thin their numbers even more, for no other reason than you like doing it.

The voice acting for the main character is great. His one-liners as he slaughters yet another truck full of SS are hilarious and it's good to see that modern attitudes haven't resulted in some of the more choice comments being removed. It's all in here exactly as the original.

For £5 you can't get much more of a bargain at the moment. Buy it whilst you can and turn the volume up, the music fits the game perfectly.\t\t\t\t\t
