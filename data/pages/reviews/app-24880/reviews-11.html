\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm enjoying The Saboteur. It's one of those EA games that never got a sequel and was kinda left forgotten about, but people who played it back in the day have really strong feelings about. I'd never tried it before, so decided to grab it now that they brought it to Steam and see what it's all about.

I'd never really realised what kind of game this is, so it was cool to discover that it is basically the third person, open-world mission structure, shooting, and driving of Grand Theft Auto, mixed with the climbing and stealth of Assassin's Creed, lightly borrowing some elements of the Hitman mission design playbook, adding in a healthy dose of Just Cause's freewheeling guerrilla warfare, and all served alongside little bit of Wolfenstein for the narrative tone.

It doesn't necessarily do any of these things better than its inspirations, but it's certainly doing them competently, and it's a fairly unique combination for a game to take on all at once, meaning the gameplay holds up reasonably well and offers some fun distraction that feels varied enough to stay interesting.

I'm 3-4 hours in and I think the game has settled into a fun rhythm, but I'm definitely curious to see if it shakes it up at all (and I'll update this review if it does anything crazy). It's not quite the mind-blowing game you might expect from the fan reviews, but it's definitely some nostalgic comfort food gaming and I can see why you'd love it back on release in 2009.\t\t\t\t\t
