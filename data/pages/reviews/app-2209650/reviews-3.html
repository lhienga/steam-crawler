\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have spent several hours exploring Oklahoma in ATS, visiting all cities and discovering most of the roads. I must say that I really had plenty of fun doing so! First, it is a relief to finally get to drive in a part of USA which doesn't feel like the West anymore: welcome to the Great Plains! I think that the feeling was captured pretty well, even if I would have preferred less tree walls in the flatter areas, allowing for uninterrupted views of the plains till the horizon. I get that on a 1:20 scaled map there certainly are limitations to this aspect, I hope Kansas will offer some properly flat landscapes.
I personally don't find ATS's representation of Oklahoma to be boring, certainly not more than the real thing. Some pretty amazing landscapes can be experienced in the Gloss Peak Mountain range between Enid and Woodward, as well as the beautifully represented Beaver's Bend park in the south-east.
I really appreciated the very realistic road signage, as well as the very mid-American feeling of the cities. My entrance into OK was thru US-385 coming from Lamar, CO. Getting into Boise City was certainly a jaw dropping experience! The city is really well mapped and feels like a proper Great Plains city.
SCS has adopted a new mapping style with OK, where many roads are hidden from the map and must be discovered by the player (usually just 1-2 roads per DLC were kept hidden). I think that it is a good idea!
My only gripe is that Oklahoma City doesn't feel particularly big, they chose to represent very suburban areas - and they did it very accurately - while Downtown is far on the background and can't be accessed due to the missing I-44 segment. At least, I-40 feels very dense and well mapped around Okla City.\t\t\t\t\t

Great, but expensive!!!

Oklahoma is an extremely welcome and fun expansion to ATS but has many minor issues that make it better to buy on sale or wait for it to be fixed.

Pros

1. There are many fun places to drive on this map

I loved driving around Oklahoma because of all the rapid driving responses needed by the scale of the game. You will be kept on your toes if you follow the traffic laws, even in TruckersMP. I also loved the Turnpikes, and I am totally okay with there being less or shorter turnpikes in the game if they don't go anywhere that would be important for the game. I also liked entering Oklahoma as well as leaving. The transition is not rough and the road bumps at the state lines feel realistic. This is the first DLC to do that correctly.

2. There are a ton of places to explore

As previously stated, the map feels immersive, especially in Tulsa and Oklahoma City, but the amount of hidden roads is insane. There are also depots that offer unique parking experiences.

3. The DLC makes you actually want to visit a state most people consider boring

SCS did a great job making Oklahoma feel alive. This state to most people who don't know it is boring, but it does not feel anything like a "flyover state."

Cons

1. Lack of detail and sloppy finish

A lot of the map felt like it had voids in it. The Panhandle felt incomplete and anything to central OK City was blocked without signage explaining why. (ik trucks aren't allowed there) There were multiple grammatical errors while playing in English on certain game messages (such as "Thankyou for using the Indian Nation Turnpike") and some messages displayed too shortly if the route advisor was hidden. ProMods does not have this issue, so why does SCS?

2. Like Wyoming without Montana

Wyoming was garbage without Montana, and Oklahoma also needs a companion state to make purchasing it worth the money. I love Oklahoma, but $11.99 is a lot for one of the smaller DLCs in a world financial crisis. SCS will need to start bundling states very soon.

In conclusion

If you want WOT stuff, buy now, but if not, wait for a deep sale. Even Texas at $18 is a better value. But this DLC is amazing and I totally recommend it if you really want it and have explored a ton of the current map.\t\t\t\t\t

An Exciting Journey Through the Oklahoma

Rating: ★★★★☆ (4/5)

Pros:
1. Immersive Landscapes
American Truck Simulator's Oklahoma DLC delivers an authentic and immersive experience as you traverse the diverse landscapes of the Sooner State. From the rolling plains and golden wheat fields to the bustling cities and iconic Route 66, the attention to detail in recreating the state's topography is truly commendable.

2. Route 66 Nostalgia
The inclusion of the historic Route 66 adds a touch of nostalgia to the DLC. Cruising down this iconic highway, you'll encounter charming roadside diners, quirky landmarks, and breathtaking vistas that capture the essence of a classic American road trip.

3. Variety of Cargo
Whether you're hauling goods through the scenic countryside or navigating the urban jungle of Oklahoma City, the DLC offers a diverse range of cargo types and delivery destinations. This variety keeps the gameplay fresh and engaging, ensuring you're never bored behind the wheel.

4. Impressive City Detailing
Major cities like Oklahoma City and Tulsa are intricately designed with meticulous attention to detail. The bustling urban environments, complete with realistic architecture, landmarks, and street layouts, make for an authentic driving experience within the city limits.

5. New Industries
The addition of new industries, such as oil drilling sites and wind farms, adds a layer of realism and economic depth to the gameplay. These locations not only offer unique challenges but also provide opportunities for lucrative deliveries and an expanded sense of exploration.

6. Hidden Roads
Surprisingly, this expansion offers a large number of hidden roads. After playing it for only a few hours, I found a lot of them, mainly between the cities of Tulsa and Idabel. I like the idea of hidden roads and hope the next DLC will include them as well.

Cons:
1. Limited Map Expansion
While the Oklahoma DLC offers an enjoyable experience within its boundaries, some players might find the map expansion relatively modest compared to previous DLCs. A larger playable area would have further enriched the overall gameplay experience.

2. Lack of Unique Mechanics
While the DLC introduces new cargo and industries, it doesn't introduce many unique gameplay mechanics that significantly differentiate it from other DLCs. Some players might have appreciated more distinctive features to set Oklahoma apart.

3. Traffic Flow Issues
In certain urban areas, players might encounter traffic flow issues or AI behavior quirks that can momentarily disrupt the immersion. These occasional hiccups can momentarily detract from an otherwise smooth driving experience.

4. Missing Landmarks
While the DLC captures many landmarks and points of interest, there are a few notable omissions that could have further enriched the environment. The inclusion of more iconic landmarks could have enhanced the overall sense of place.

5. High Price
From my point of view, €11.99 seems like an insanely high price for the size of this country.


But in the end Oklahoma DLC offers an enjoyable and diverse trucking experience, allowing players to explore the heartland of the United States with its distinctive landscapes, vibrant cities, and iconic Route 66. While the DLC shines with its immersive environments, cargo variety, and attention to detail, there's still room for improvement in terms of map expansion and the introduction of unique gameplay mechanics. Despite its minor shortcomings, the Oklahoma DLC remains a solid addition to the game, providing hours of engaging trucking adventures for both new and seasoned players alike.\t\t\t\t\t
