\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEditing my review after much more time spent in Oklahoma. There are definitely pros and cons, previously only seeing cons.

Starting with the pros, I think that they nailed the panhandle and Tulsa. I enjoyed driving through the panhandle with the marked cities and unmarked cities. I think that the panhandle was represented pretty well. The vegetation especially was done very well. Tulsa represented the industry there impeccably and I enjoyed taking and delivering there quite a bit.

The cons are long though which is why this is not recommended for now. First of all, the frame drops are atrocious, especially in Okla. City. Places like Portland that are much much more graphically demanding have no issues, but a lot of Oklahoma drops frames and freezes like crazy. My main problems are with Okla. City and southern Oklahoma. I've been to Okla. City a couple times and they didn't capture any of what was interesting about it. From missing highways to missing a lot of Route 66 culture, they completely missed the mark. I find Oklahoma actually pretty interesting being there a couple of times, but in this DLC it's presented as very lack-luster which I do not agree with. Like many have said, it just feels unfinished, and I'm sure they'll come back to it like they did Texas and improve where it was lacking, but why are states releasing like this anyway? Texas and Oklahoma have just not been there quality-wise like I expect from SCS. Another issue is the price relative, Montana and Colorado are the same exact price but have so much more to offer, I do not see how Oklahoma in its current state is worth more than 8$, especially with the lack of anything new to do gameplay-wise. My final gripe is with the AI that for some reason is worse than normal in this state. I turned off my traffic mod thinking that it just hadn't been correctly set for Oklahoma, but that was not the issue. The AI in Oklahoma just seem to be worse, I see multiple crashes and am hit myself on multiple runs. I'll have cars back into me, turn directly in front of me or hit my trailer, cut me off while I'm running 70 and cause me to wreck/flip. They'll merge into each other on on-ramps, crash, and sit there, for minutes, nothing happens, they never correct themselves and drive on, they just sit there. I don't understand what changed but there's definitely a difference. All I can say is I hope it improves from here because I love Kansas and Nebraska as well, it'd be a true shame if they disappoint on those states as well.\t\t\t\t\t
