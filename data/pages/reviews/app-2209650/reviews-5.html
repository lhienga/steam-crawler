\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will definitely agree with some of the comments here that are meant to give criticism for this DLC. I believe the road network in OKC and Tulsa were relatively poor for what it is, and cities and towns that play a significant role in the state of Oklahoma being rather forgotten. The suburbs of both Tulsa and Oklahoma City were almost made non-existent in this DLC, which I believe should have gotten some recognition. If they weren't their own cities, they should have been at least an exit or at least be noticed. I don't like how they did Oklahoma City's road network one bit. Completely chopping off 235, 444 and 44 and making Oklahoma City less than what it is, isn't a good look. Some things that had plenty of great potential to add value to the game weren't taken advantage of.

Onto the good, I think they did amazing with the panhandle. The panhandle has more roads than it can handle, and it's highly detailed. I think they did a good job illustrating how unexciting the state is, you're not going to get mountain ranges or an insane amount of stunning views in this state, and this is how it's going to be for the next several states they bring out. I've lived in the great plains myself, it's known for hauling goods from one side of the country to the other. I think they did well with the skylines and downtowns of main street America like they always do. I like how it opens up more space in the game for adventure and driving. Remember, this game is managed and made by a handful of foreign developers, and this was highly detailed for what the situation it is. I hope that they focus better on the future cities they make and try to work on more three digit interstates, along with better sights to see.

The cost for this DLC may have been pricey for what it is, considering Washington and Colorado came for the same price. I think Oklahoma should have been cut down a tad but you can't undersell it. These developers worked hard for a result that will add to, (*eventually in a few decades*) a game where you can drive coast to coast. I hope they rework this state a bit and try to improve the realism of the cities, that's what seems to bother me the most personally. I recommend this DLC to someone who wants to have everything, but this wouldn't be my first pick. Not because they did a bad job, but because the state isn't like Colorado or Washington, it's not going to stun you every second you're in the state. I hope they do better with Kansas and future DLC's. But they deserve credit for the effort and hard work they did for us.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, ok... (pun intended.) I'm coming at this from the point of view of a 16+ year CDL driver who just happens to live in Oklahoma City and drive all over the OKC metro area and as far west as Elk City, OK. So, bear with me, because I'm gonna critique and nerd out on several items. First up, I absolutely LOVE the rendition of the Petro Station at I-40 and MLK in Oklahoma City. Devs, if you're listening, it's perfect- never change it (unless you're going to make the hotel next to it a little more scuzzy.) Interesting choice to merge Meridian and MacArthur (It's MacArthur north of I-40, Meridian south of I-40.) It works though. Missed opportunity though by not putting a Freightliner and Western Star dealership in between the Walberts and the water park, because they're there in real life. The rotating, pedestaled cement truck would have been a nice touch too.

The scenery in OKC along I-40 is pretty great. Where the heck is Frontier City though??? It's a landmark and the first thing you see coming into town from N. I-35 or from Tulsa. Also missing is the entire southside.... but.... I'm OK with that. I biggest gripe though is I-44 and I-235! I realize I-235 just goes into Edmond and, pffft, you don't need Edmond, but not having I-44 there is just awkward. I would absolutely be grateful if I could exit onto I-44 from I-40 and take it all the way up to Tulsa. Likewise, I-235 could be added up until where it merges with I-44. (Ok full disclosure, I live off of one and work off the other and really just want to see if I could see my house and place of employment.)

Oh, and an absolute thank you to the devs for including the First Americans Museum!

Outside OKC, Clinton looks ok, but it'd be nice if the road Route 66 Museum is on would connect to the highway. The distance from Clinton to OKC seemed pretty short and there are a couple of small towns that should have at least gotten a shout-out of some sort. Kinda hoping when Kansas comes out they'll flesh out I-35 North and 75 North out of Tulsa. Tulsa looked great too, but I don't remember seeing the Cityplex Towers, or whatever they're calling them now. It was fun seeing Beavers Bend too.

I think that's all I've got to say so far. I'm loving Oklahoma and it's actually incredibly surreal to fuel up at some of the places I've actually fueled up before. Hoping it gets tweaked just a little bit, but otherwise, I'm having a blast reenacting my real life job.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmerican Truck Simulator is a great game, the game has constantly been getting better over the years.
This DLC however wasn't so great in my opinion. It feels relatively unfinished and arguably doesn't represent Oklahoma that well, it is very empty and boring. Many towns surrounding the cities included in the DLC could've been added and would've made a great addition to the DLC but were instead completely left out. Like Moore, Norman, Chandler, Bristow, Jenks, Broken Arrow, Sand Springs, and Stillwater to list a few. Not only were entire towns left out, but certain major highways that could make a great addition were left out like parts of Route 66 which could open up the opportunity to include the entirety of Stroud, Creek Turnpike, OK-99, OK-56, and Highway 169 to list a few. There are even some spots on the map that weren't captured correctly. Like north of Tulsa around the Rock Port, that whole area is very inaccurate. Entire blocks of Guymon are missing too. I'm sure there is more missing but those are things I've seen. SCS needs to give the missing towns some love and fix the issues in the towns that are already there.

This DLC is not worth the 12$ price tag for what it is providing. It feels rushed in some aspects, doesn't capture the state right, everything is squished together and a lot is missing. I hope there will be some updates to this DLC in the future that will add these things and more. Only time will tell.\t\t\t\t\t
