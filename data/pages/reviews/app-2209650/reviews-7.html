\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurprisingly I found this DLC to be a bit better than their previous Texas DLC. Sure, there isn't as many roads to drive on nor is the state very interesting to look at but I think SCS did their best in faithfully capturing the feel of the state. However, there are still issues that plague this map DLC from being great.

A major issue I have is that I-44 does not wrap around the northwestern side of Oklahoma City as it does IRL and I don't see any reason why it was not included. There's room for it to be added so it's not like they can't fit it but perhaps they will include it in a future update. An issue that still persists with each new map DLC release is hotels, yards, factories, fuel stations and farms being blocked off by either invisible barriers or a gate. I also noticed many prefabs and assets getting recycled from previous map DLCs, even old assets dating back to California. I understand that making new assets and prefabs takes time but I can't help but get deja vu at some points when I drive in Oklahoma.

Overall, I think this DLC is fine for what it is. I think the price of $12 is a bit much for what you get. $10 seems more reasonable to me considering what you get. Unless you really want to drive in Oklahoma, I'd just wait for this to be on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOklahoma is a great DLC, especially in junction with Texas. It's obnoxious to me to see people negatively reviewing it for fact of geography. Driving through the plains is a really great change of pace to the deserts and mountains that ATS had been dominated by for its life before slivers of Colorado/Wyoming were added and pushed deeper into by Montana/Texas, but regardless that's simply personal taste. If you had no interest driving through a plains state and yet bought Oklahoma anyway and proceeded to negatively review it *for* being a plains state, that's your decision.

Map detail only continues to get better as the DLCs progress and Oklahoma provides more hidden roads per amount of content than any state by a wide margin. US-259 through the Ouachita mountains is a fantastic edition and a good taste of what the Appalachians will later have to offer.

Perhaps there could've been another road in the Texas panhandle to help Woodward and Enid but it's still more than acceptable as is. Most ATS DLCs are now cheaper than a single meal at a restaurant except it provides content forever, it's absolutely a great deal.\t\t\t\t\t
