Compared to some of the previous expansions, this DLC is basically more of the same. If you expect a jump in quality, like there used to be with every new expansion, don't. Those years are well and truly behind us. And if you expect abundant spectacular scenery, also don't — although that might be due to realism.

Overall, Oklahoma feels slightly unpolished. Some minor nitpicks I encountered in just under 5 hours:
  • Many gas stations, especially towards the northwest, have buildings that you have to teleport on top of for them to turn yellow on the map. It's nice that unique assets were made, don't get me wrong, but the work has to be finished.
    Edit: Semi-fixed. With a fresh account, I checked 2 gas stations ans 1 business that I was certain had this issue earlier. 1 gas station was fixed entirely. The other one only had 1 building fixed, the rest I still had to teleport on top of. The business wasn't fixed at all.
  • Floating objects.
    Edit: I could only remember with certainty the location of one such object that I saw. It's still floating.
  • Every time there's a tollbooth, the game displays the following message: "Thankyou for using the ___ Turnpike" — as if no one did a spellcheck.
    Edit: Almost fixed. "Thank you" now has a space in the middle. With some tollbooths, however, the message still doesn't end with a full stop. The message can also be triggered slightly before the "Toll of $_ paid" one. The way it goes: the "Thank you" one is displayed for a fraction of a second, then the full "Toll paid," then a brief "Thank you" again. Come on, guys. How does it take you more than 2 tries to get a pop-up message right, what are you, Dovetail Games?
  • There are many small bits of road that cannot be explored due to incorrectly placed barriers. Admittedly, this isn't just an Oklahoma problem.
    Edit: As with the floating objects, I could only remember with certainty the location of one such bit. It's still inaccessible.
  • The Boise City (not to be confused with the city of Boise, Idaho) viewpoint says "ok_boise_city" instead of the correct name when viewed.
    Edit: Fixed.
  • When delivering cargo to the Central Civil Works company in Oklahoma City, the white outline that's supposed to show where the cargo must be precision-parked does not appear. A look with the freecam demonstrated that it's been placed below the ground.
    Edit: Not fixed.

Again, these are nitpicks. But there were just a bit too many of them in just a bit too short of a time period, and it's not like these are obscure logic/economy bugs or anything. Had it not been 8.5 months since Texas, I'd speculate that this DLC was rushed to release.

There is no "treasure hunt" to be had here, like there was with the historical markers of Texas. There are just 10 viewpoints and 10 Photo Trophies. All 4 achievements are rather boring and unimaginative. So, in those (more videogame-like) aspects, it's a small step backwards. A part that I did like quite a bit: plentiful secret roads. One of them is even genuinely challenging to navigate with a trailer attached.

And now some words regarding value, which is the main problem of the Oklahoma DLC. It is less than half the size of Montana, which was also released for €12. And it certainly doesn't compensate for it by having twice the content or twice the polish. We can also compare it to the similarly-sized Washington DLC, which has 16 cities, and upon release, has noticeably raised the bar in terms of scenery. Oklahoma has 10 cities, and it certainly doesn't raise any bars when compared to several previous expansions. And now the kicker: in many, if not most non-Western currencies (notably the Turkish lira, the Russian ruble, the Japanese yen, the Indian rupee, etc.) this DLC is either barely cheaper than Texas, or outright more expensive than it, despite being 26% the size. Ultimately, it's all peanuts, but the comparisons invite themselves and they aren't favourable at all.

Edit: 10 days after the release of Oklahoma, SCS have raised the prices of every other DLC to match it. Well, that's one way to address the issue ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯. Prices in the main Western currencies/English-speaking markets remain the same, by the way. They must not have experienced any inflation this past year, lol.

As the states further from the coasts are more flat/agricultural, this was the perfect moment to start bundling them. Kansas and Oklahoma together have the same land area as Montana, and just over half of the land area of Texas. Pricing them together at €12 would've made sense, especially considering their geography and the significant price hike in most non-Western markets. Then again, it did take SCS most of a year to put together the smallest map expansion yet. If we had to wait for Kansas to be finished as well... I'm not sure that would be sustainable either.

An aside: 6 years ago, I speculated that it would take SCS 10 years to finish the US map. A dev jumped in the comment section to say that it wouldn't, as it took them 1.5 years to do 15% of the US's area. This would put the estimate at 6 years, not 10. Well, it's been 7.5 years since ATS got released. That is more than 6 already, and we're only 13/50 states and 40.5% of the land area in. Even disregarding the East coast's increased road density, it's going to take SCS another 11 years to finish the map for a total time of nearly 19 years. That'll teach me to expect reasonable timeframes.

In summation: more of the same, except the portion is smaller, more bland, and in many markets (mine included — hence the thumbs down), more expensive than usual. I guess that's why this map expansion is the worst-rated one yet, and by quite a margin.\t\t\t\t\t

How they are doing it now is completely fine, as the states they have done, so far, are low in quantity; but, when they make more states, I do hope they create bundles, and even if release dates have to be postponed, I'd rather buy a bundle that contains, for example, SD, ND, MN, and IA, or something along the lines of that. Anyway, this is a great DLC in and of itself, and I actually love going between OK City and Tulsa, and I think that's the highlight of the DLC. Again, I understand the criticism for such a small map being expensive, but due to reasons previously stated, I resolve that this DLC is still worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDriving through the flat, green, empty state of Oklahoma gives you a great opportunity to let your mind wander.

Some thoughts that may run through your head:

- What's my favorite place in the Rocky Mountains? Hm, what's even my favorite state in the Rocky Mountains?
- When Arizona gets updated in the future, will it look as awesome as southwest Texas?
- Both Montana and Texas... Yeah, 2022 was a great year.
- The last update of Texas definitely made Dallas more attractive.
- Utah has grown on me. It wasn't my favorite state from day one, but now I always like driving there.

After about 6 hours of driving in Oklahoma, I have to admit that I might find it the least interesting state since... Well, since the start of ATS, actually. And I suppose the next couple of states also won't be on the top of my list.

Is Oklahoma a bad DLC? No, definitely not. Just like other recent states, SCS has done their best to portray the state as it is. I guess Oklahoma is mostly similar to regions in northern Texas (unsurprisingly), which are also boring... Boring in a good way. A long drive passing just fields and trees can be very relaxing.

If you liked ATS thus far, I definitely recommend buying Oklahoma. Drive around there, try to enjoy the cities and the scenery, but afterwards don't forget to drive back to a state like Idaho or Montana. I think the landscape will look even more impressive after you've come from Oklahoma.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually write reviews on Steam, but here, I must. This is the worst map DLC from SCS since Going East for ETS2 and that was their first, so I can excuse them a lot for that one. Oklahoma, however, is not their first. They already made a lot of great maps. I understand IRL Oklahoma is a small state and not as interesting to drive as the other states in the game, but that's more reason for SCS to either packing it with another state (Kansas preferable), like they do with countries in ETS2, or make it cheaper. But not everything in this DLC is the state's fault. Having only one repair shop in the whole eastern part of Oklahoma, and even then, on a small road you would hardly even drive, is totally the fault of SCS. Why not putting at least one other repair shop in, or around McAlester and another in, or around, Tulsa? And there's more of these annoyances that could be easily avoided with careful planning on SCS side.
Anyway, if you are big fan of this game, have other states and want to support SCS, go ahead and get it in the bundle that will give you some small savings. Otherwise, however, I can't recommend you to pay the full price. Wait for a sale and even then, think carefully if this is worth your money. In my opinion, there are better states for this game than Oklahoma.\t\t\t\t\t
