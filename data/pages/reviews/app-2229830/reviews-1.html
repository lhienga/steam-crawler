\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame itself is awesome, I am just leaving this review for technical state of the game. Never mind, Windows even on Steam Deck it works perfect after a single patch. I used KB+M. There is no EA launcher attached to this thing so there is that too.
Here are the instructions for how I got it up and running. With this setup, I think I played over 5 hours without a single crash or any kind of other problem. Go here and download the ZIP file https://github.com/FunkyFr3sh/cnc-ddraw. Afterwards follow the instructions, extract and override everything in your own game folder and override your own ddraw using the launcher like it says. Last thing to do after that is set launch options in your own properties using this command copy/paste without the outer quotes "WINEDLLOVERRIDES="ddraw=n,b" %command%". I am using GE-Proton8-3, there were other configurations that other users mentioned and worked with this but that was the last one I played around with so it's not a exact thing that you need to run the game but just keep in mind as a alternative.

Just in case anything changes I am leaving the proton page here so you can see what other users encounter.

Sometimes your team will try to go around the entire map in unknown territory because they think its faster than waiting for the bridge. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tA gaming relic.

Multiplayer didn't work, and I can't even find an option for a skirmish mode, so this just seems to have singleplayer missions. The missions guide you through an easy learning curve, although they might have obscurely specific ways to complete them, and it doesn't seem to save your progress, so after finishing a mission you have to go through the cutscene for the next one and then save the game.

Some of the CGI cutscenes were terrible, as they didn't relate to the story (or even the mission) but the technology was relatively new at the time, so it didn't matter if it was basic - it was just fun to watch them. I also felt it was fun to tinker with an RTS game based on modern warfare with a pinch of sci-fi, although there's some awkwardness to it.

The gameplay is a bit claustrophobic as your units are surrounded by the fog of war and have next to no visibility, and buildings have to be built against eachother. The poor unit pathing has your units run into danger, so they take a lot of micromanagement (and potentially keeping the gamespeed low to do all that clicking). The remastered version has a better resolution and a skirmish mode I think, although I got this version fairly cheap in a bundle, which has merit as a raw piece of gaming history.\t\t\t\t\t
