Where it all began and one of Westwoods finest hours

My first experience with C&C was the demo and it was a pleasant surprise as my experience with RTS was fairly limited back in the day, but I didn't fully understand what I missed out until I finally played The First Decade and it was a BLAST!

The gameplay is simple and janky, but it is easy to learn and unintrusive, still playable after all these years.
The units exhibit character and personality with how they look and behave.
The story and characters while not too complex are still engaging and always interesting to listen to.
The music is an absolute banger! Frank Klepacki nailed it! Some may say that other games in the series have better soundtrack, but to me the first game with all other add-ons offered a complete package.

It makes me glad and nostalgic to have this classic game available today after all these years, proving that they still have life in them.

Here's to another several decades of Command & Conquer.

Act on instinct and keep 'em coming!\t\t\t\t\t

Steam BlogReviews™. They say that you're most impressionable between the ages of 3 and 6. Well, I literally wouldn't be me without this game.
- Musical taste. Fond memories of innocently skipping home from school in the sunshine singing/humming Just Do It Up. For every artist/album that I enjoy, I enjoy because I first enjoyed Frank Klepacki's music in this game.
- Massive interest in politics, philosophy, religion and computers, especially the role of computers. 20th century history and religion teachers either loved or hated me. IT teachers were pointless as I usually ended up educating them.
- A general tendency to question leadership.
- Simplicity in game design trumps needless complexity, every time.

The remaster is objectively better in almost every single way, with my only complaint there being that they made certain missions easier for the zoomers, but... this specific collection of 0s and 1s is representative of half of my soul. My sincere thanks to everyone who was involved in creating especially this game, but also the rest of the 2D C&Cs (and Tiberium Wars). When school sucked, they were always waiting for me back home. I genuinely surprised myself with how quickly the muscle memory came back, and I was quite happily playing with mouse-only controls and no build queues after one level. Sometimes less really is more, and conveniences are just that - conveniences. It all works perfectly on W11, with no need to download some hacky over-modified exe from a certain anti-fun, literal institution-worthy somebody; we've got the original game as it was meant to be back!

So thank you EA, now please get on the phone to Petroglyph immediately about rehiring them to rectify your mistakes. With a budget the size of Jupiter. And with a remaster of TS/RA2 as a bonus. I swear, if that happens I'll never diss you ever again. Bygones would be bygones. Water under the ****ing bridge.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the original Command & Conquer from 1995, warts and all. An amazing game back in the day that helped popularize the real time strategy genre along with Warcraft. If you are interested in where RTS games or the C&C series started out, you owe it to yourself to try it out.

The standalone Steam release is in an odd spot. While EA did do some work to make it work better for modern systems, running the game off the original executable has been made almost completely obsolete. The Remastered Collection brings the game up to modern standards and adds extra content not found here. This is on top of the fact that the game itself is freeware and can be used with OpenRA or the v1.06 fan patch.

All that said, it's still good this version exists. It serves as a way to preserve as much of the original experience as possible, even if various elements haven't aged that well.

If you want to play the definitive version of this game, get Command & Conquer Remastered Collection

If you want to just play the game, get the freeware release and use the fan patch
