\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: After the latest update, I haven't encountered any major bugs, but still run into visual bugs here and there. The worst bug I've run into since the last update prevented me from opening any menus or screens (couldn't open the pause menu, map, or merchant menu). Force closing the game and restarting it solved that issue and haven't run into it since.

At this point, I'd say most of my complaints are w.r.t. balancing and the actual impact of traits and choices. I remember once hearing that "gamers will optimize the fun out of a game" and that feels like where a lot of the games problems lie. There are typically very obvious, if boring, traits to go with and as far as primary traits I don't think it's particularly difficult to just slowly keep raising all the stats of your goblins.

---


I've had a lot of fun playing, but it's really hard to recommend with the bugs it has currently. It would be one thing if they were minor/purely visual bugs, but the ones I've encountered have all been game breaking.

1. After losing a goblin to a dark alter, goblin randomly showed up in my party later in the adventure. After returning to base, the goblin had become duplicated with 1 having all 0 stats, no status and just stuck in my base and then the original goblin. Unfortunately, I was unable to dismiss either and when going to breed goblins they would break things further. Every time I scrolled up/down through the list of goblins, more and more goblins would randomly be marked as "dismissed" until every goblin was dismissed. I had to restart for it to reset things, but that was just temporary as they would eventually all end up "dismissed" again.

2. Random UI backgrounds getting stuck and blocking view of everything during adventures.

3. Saving is just entirely broken to a single state in time. No new saves are actually made. Like, at least give me an error message that saving failed, come on guys.

Overall, the bugs have left a pretty sour taste in my mouth, I'm sure I'll end playing more if the bugs are fixed, but definitely plan on avoiding this developer in the future\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game, The theme and mechanics are great. The artwork, sound design, and narration are all top tier. Sadly I cannot recommend this game at this time.

The game is FULL of bugs, some are minor and purely visual, others are game breaking and frustrating. On top of that the game is not balanced very well. It requires certain materials to upgrade your lair to progress in the game, but you'll quickly be overloaded with gold you can't spend, and you'll never find enough wood or fiber to upgrade important components quickly. This results in tons of unrewarding grinding to gain little progress. Moreover the game starts to become very monotonous and way too easy, especially when they ask you to repeat areas and bosses over and over to continue further. These issues could've been easily spotted and solved if the game was properly play tested, but clearly it wasn't.

All those problems aside, it seems the development team doesn't work very hard to fix these bugs and takes 3 day weekends every week when they have a nearly unplayable game.

I want this game to be good so badly (hence my 45 hours of gameplay). Unfortunately it just isn't, and they don't seem to care enough to fix it in a timely manner. Maybe give it another 3 years and it'll be better, but until then save your money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's quite a bit to like about this game, but also room for improvement. I wish there had been an early access mode so that the developers could get more feedback and implement it into the game for a polished release.

Pros:

-Unique sense of design, both for the characters and the environments. They're beautifully rendered by the artists, and that's by far the biggest 'draw' here. (Get it?)

-Interesting story also gives us a novel take on the classic RPG format, having us play as the fodder mob that we're so used to farming xp with.

-Emphasis on the synergy of your team, rather than the individual strength of a singular unit

-Several classes to play around with to accommodate for your play-style.

-Branched pathways/procedurally generated levels like Slay the Spire.

-Goated narrator

-Runs smoothly, small download size. I'm playing on PC and have noticed zero bugs since launch day. It seems to be a different experience for other people though.

Cons:

-My only real con, like many others have mentioned is how big a grind it is to get the proper resources to develop your base and to upgrade your team. Think 'Hades', but instead of new dialogue options, you run into the monotony of revisiting the same areas. (I think this could be alleviated by increasing the resource output per mission that you do, to decrease grindy feel). Additionally, the currency that you do receive in bulk (GOLD) quickly becomes useless, and you accumulate so much of it, without a significant way for it to transfer over to the resources that you actually need.

Comments:

-I've seen a lot of people commenting on how you don't feel a personal connection to the goblins, and I honestly don't see that as a con. Unlike games like Darkest Dungeon where you intentionally are meant to use the same mercenaries, goblins are the quintessential fodder mob. They reproduce like rabbits and are weak as hell. Those are essentially their two core traits. They overwhelm you with numbers. Some of these reviews make it seem like the players were expecting to get to know every single goblin as though they were going to watch them as they raised their families and coddle their grandkids...I just don't think that's a priority for me in this kind of rogue-like, and you get really good at min-maxing the breeding to generate your lil elite goblin lineages.


Bottom line, I'd still recommend this game to other players, but do make sure you understand what you're getting into. It's a slow-progressing roguelike where you're oftentimes grinding to ensure that your goblin team can keep up with the increasing difficulty of enemies. If you can make it past that, you're rewarded with some truly beautiful, hand-drawn art and a fun story to work your way through. At the time of this review, I can't attest to the replayability, but right now, due to the progression-locking of the game, it seems like the game follows a linear story.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really would like to recommend this game but I sadly am unable to do it in good faith.
While seems fun at first cutesy & bright kind of Darkest Dungeon the game quickly becomes... not fun & repetetive. I will not mention the bugs that are still out there (my main one is where I am unable to dismiss or hire new goblins thus making breeding new goblins impossible).
Additionally I feel that almost every thing out there is made to inconvenience the player. Whether it is UI stuff, moving on the map, animations, cutscenes, small icons you have to click with precision on the map.
Moving rooms, goblin management (Fixed) or merchant interactions are especially frustrating. The upgrades all need the same materials thus you have to repeat a mission multiple times for an upgrade. Gold while important at first quickly becomes thing you actually do not need, have heaps of and cannot actually do anything with.
The combat while interesting at first features many similar type of enemies (Halfling, Halfling Warrior; Purple Ooze, Yellow Ooze, Green Ooze) which do not have that much different strategies and feel like copy + paste with different colour palette. The enemies are almost the same so you do not need to think up of new strategies of dealing with them (though there are some strategies particularry effective against some types of enemies). The bosses either are easy as heck or very hard and usually very very long.
Whole content of this game when it comes to combat and exploration feels like unfinished product.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think this one is ready quite yet.

There are some bugs still but I'd imagine those will get fixed soon and they aren't that frequent.

The game loop is pretty simple and a little grindy, (36 hours with one restart and I just got into act 2) but I find it kind of fun. There's not a lot of meat. Pretty much what you see is what you get. I don't really think that's a bad thing.

The story is fun and I want to see where it goes. I particularly love the narrator.

My biggest problem comes from what you do when your at the goblin base. It's very much a chore navigating through everything. Navigating your base consists of a lot of zooming in and backing out. It's super jarring.
One of the big draws to this game is breeding the gobos. The big problem is you have no real way of organizing them. What you get is a single file list that takes up 10% of your screen with 1 single filter. Which means when you breed your goblins (something you are given incentive to do after every mission) You are stuck shifting through a lot of goblins 1 by 1 . It's just painful...

I'll probably come back and check this out later and see if navigating the base has gotten better. Until then I"m going to put this one down. Just know what you're getting into with this one. It'll probably get better with time but at the time. If you are interested I'd wishlist it for now and give them a little more time.\t\t\t\t\t

Oh yay I have created a Retirement Village for 122 ill used and mistreated goblins only for it to get instantly deleted upon completion.

Good = breeding, guilds and visual design

Drab = combat AI, base building and weapons & weapon upgrades

Outright Bad = bugs, crashes, economy, length of each chapter, resources for upgrades and combat difficulty being way to low.

Please play 300 hours to unlock a mode that may be challenging = BS\t\t\t\t\t
