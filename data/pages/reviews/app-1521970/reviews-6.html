\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to say pretty much the same things other reviews already say, and I know I only have 3 hours right now, but the game has some weird issues.

Here is what I can say for now :
- All goblins feel samey. The breeding aspect of the game pretends you can create goblins with specific stats and traits, but the stats seem limited by invisible hard caps, resulting in all goblins having pretty similar stats.
- The grind is real. I've done 3 missions in search of wood so I can build the last of the 3 first guilds (guilds are rooms that allow you to change the class of a peon goblin) and I got 2 woods. From a merchant. It's absolutely insane because I need 15 to build the room, and 20 per upgrade. You have to get extremely lucky to find harvest spot, or for the mission to reward said material at the end.
- The interfaces are absolutely awful. Some things straight up don't work, like scrolling, and some things work half the time, like clicking "outside" of the interface to go back. But that's not my main issue, the interfaces are slow and HUGE, but lack important informations like stats. You have to go one interface deeper to get the information you want about a goblin, and then go back and do it again for another goblin. it's especially annoying when breeding goblins.
- Some effects seem really weak. Like bleed. For example, you can apply & stack bleed that will deal 15 damage over 10 unit worth of time, or just deal 10 damage 2-3 times in that time. It's the same for healing, which is very weak (while enemies hit quite hard).
- Lots of bugs, mostly minor ones, like the narrator spamming "the inventory is full" when I pick up stuff that is stacked in my inventory.
- Some things are really convoluted and confusing. I have slots for goblins in my barracks, and slots for retired goblins, but also slots for the goblins in my team ? Meaning if I remove a goblin from my team but don't have space in my barracks, I can't do that. Why ?
- A LOT of "temporary stuff", like temporary weapons you keep for 1 run, temporary buffs, temporary ability upgrades, temporary weapon upgrades. It's not that much of an issue but it's really annoying to taste the power of a new weapon and it vanishes at the end of the run.
- You can find food to heal your goblins but once the mission ends it is instantly sold (I think) and you can't start a run with food in your inventory. Which is quite annoying considering how bad healing is and how hard enemies hit.

It's really a shame because the game looks really interesting, you can see combinations of abilities and perk that would make for a great team, and I really enjoy how the turn system works.
But oh my god the grind for materials.

(By the way, despite what the game's store page says, it cannot be shared with steam's family sharing)\t\t\t\t\t

Rogue-like? Chore-like!

UPDATED REVIEW
: I haven't played the game since I posted this review which was a good 4 or 5 days ago. During that time I have now realized what is "wrong" with this game. And it is not that the goblins are unmemorable and basicly just clones of each other with different genes/stats ,
It is not that the base building aspect is buggy, lacks depth,creativity or innovation like the fact that having goblins in the base will be just that, goblins doing nothing in the base and NOT "One goblin will defend or be the lookout, this goblin will do blacksmithing, this one will clean etc." is such a missed opportunity.

NO, IT IS THE FACT THAT THIS GAME IS BEING TAGGED AS ROGUE-LIKE
. What is the most important thing when playing Rogue-like games? It is the randomness of each game and encounter and all the things/upgrades/loots you get and it becomes a dopamine hit and release that you keep playing. It becomes a 'one-more'... until next thing you know its been 12 hours and the sun is almost back up. Don't get me wrong Goblin Stone does have rogue-like aspects to the game but it does so differently than most game. But do keep in mind that this game is also a turn-based RPG so to be honest it can do away with the whole Rogue-Like mission maps and it'd still be the same game. Repetetive and boring and here is why:

The upgrades you get are not permanent but all the deaths are. What makes a Rogue-like? It is the crazy upgrades you get that last until you face off with the final boss or you die and you start over again getting probably different set of crazy upgrades or whatever the gimmick of that rogue-like is.
In Goblin Stone Rogue-like upgrades only lasts for the duration of the mission and any good loot you get while in that mission is also only usable until that mission. Also you don't die a lot but your characters do. And I think some degree of attachment to your character is necessary specially for the RPG aspect of it. After I unlocked the next 3 rows of genes I've become desensitized with losing goblins and it just feels ehh.

So you find yourself playing this game with a bunch of characters you know won't be there the next day while getting upgrades you know won't be there 30 minutes later when you clear the Map. I usually don't care for grinding but this whole set up just really makes it feel like a big chore. [/b]

So there you have it why I can not recommend this game. Try if you must for the Story and the gameplay itself is not bad, I actually like the turn base mechanics of this game but like I mentioned it doesn't get any deeper or better 40 hours later.


Old review:

Follow the lives of 2 goblins as they build a base or until they die in the first hour or so (don't get attached to any of them) and gather more goblins, so that you can grind mats grow your base by going on adventures/missions with a party of 6. You will then have a routine of:
- start a mission to collect mats, to make base upgrades/class upgrades and increase goblin limits by killing off or retiring veteran goblins, to make room for more goblins so you can get more upgrades to build a bigger base, to get better, younger, gen z goblins by killing more goblins so you can build a bigger base to house more goblins, you get the point.

Started out fun and fresh during intro until it wasn't.
The game wants you to fall in love with your favorite goblins, or so they say but the game makes it hard to feel attached to any of them as you literally have to farm their souls, either thru death or forced early retirement to increase you base level and unlock more action skills.
At first I wanted to keep all my goblins as long as I can but this is pointless because pretty much all the goblins are the same per generation (per batch/set of genes you unlock). Once you get to the breeding part of the game which is an hour or 2 into it, you will find out that even when you grind for the next 3 hours for mats to max out your goblin capacity, there still isn't enough room for everyone or for more! Soon I find myself killing or retiring the very same minions I tried preserving because 1) that is how you upgrade your main HQ, 2) to make room to breed more gen z gobs because 3) all the goblins you have at the present will always be inferior to the next group you will get simply because you unlock more genes and better traits as you progress.

This game turned the missions/roguelike aspect and base building into a grind fest and is very limiting when it comes to collecting and breeding goblins.
This game have a lot of potential. It isn't a bad game, just a flawed one.
I would still recommend people to play this game but just make sure that when you do, don't fall for the sunk cost fallacy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great light-hearted game for those who like xcom and Darkest Dungeon. It's not hard, but it is creative, and the strategy is in eugenics of goblins over leveling up. I love the humor that sympathizes with the goblins being attacked by everyone else; so playing the underdogs in a fantasy RPG setting is fun. The goblins never level up. They are what they are, but if they survive they can continue to create legacies. It's both a fun version of the above games, and a slight nuance to the game play on how you progress. Highly recommend!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the Darkest Dungeon 2 I was hoping to get. The game is adorable and the fighting mechanics is pretty nice. I still can't fully understand goblin breeding, but it's kind of in that zone where you're eager to figure it out step by step. The only real annoying issue I have with the game is the UI. It feels like the developers have spent a huge portion of their art budget on fancy animated UI, but at the end of the day this only leads to frustration. It's cute to see for the first ~5 times, but after that it's just a waste of time. You might ask why is it such a big deal, and the reason is that there are no useful summaries or lists of anything you need - you gotta click on every building and then the upgrade button to figure out if you have resources to upgrade it. You will constantly be denied certain actions because you hit a limit of weapons, space in the storage, goblins in the barracks, etc. And none of this info is visible anywhere but in the view of the corresponding building. So, most of your base building gameplay is just this endless clicking and watching the UI animations. I hope the developers realize that no matter how much they are proud of it and no matter how pretty it is, they should work on improving the UX of the base building.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was looking forward to this release for a long time, but I have to say I'm disappointed with what I've experienced so far. The roguelite elements don't go past anything but random map generation, only being able to retain collected resources through the incredibly short runs leaves you frustrated at having to essentially restart over and over with your blank slate of a goblin squad. What's the point of finding a cool weapon or upgrade for your goblin if it's going to be gone in ten minutes, having to start the increasingly difficult missions with inferior weapons and cards EVERY single time? If nothing you do matters, you have to ask yourself, why even play the game? And for a roguelite there doesn't seem to be any randomness in the core way you can "create" new characters-- breeding. The stats of each parent are pretty much just rounded up to be the same or worse, you can't get ANY new traits through it, those are locked behind the odd goblin you may find, though I've never seen any traits other than the base three in the supposedly "large" pool of them. There's just no uniqueness in the goblins as individuals, even though they're supposed to be THE main characters! Just like the enemies, there are no variations in them. Heck, even the physical traits all look the same other than skin tone, a flabby goblin looks the same as a thin goblin, the curved ears have no visible difference to the straight ears, etc. It's just not the experience it was advertised to be, and while I understand it's made by an indie team, that doesn't feel like a valid excuse for the state the game is in. I just don't feel any love or care put into it, as I would expect from indie developers and their passion projects. I sincerely hope it improves because make no mistake, I WANT to love this game! I just can't at this moment in time.\t\t\t\t\t
