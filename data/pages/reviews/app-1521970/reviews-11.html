\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually write reviews until I am done with a game, but I want to offset some of these negative reviews. Yes, there are bugs and I have been lucky enough not to experience any game breaking ones, but this game is made by a small indie studio and they have been actively fixing issues every week.

There is a lot to love about this game: the beautiful art style, the combat mechanics, and the story. There's fun in trying to breed the perfect goblin with maximum stats and ideal genetic traits. I haven't even unlocked half of the possible traits, so I know there's a lot to look forward too. The combat is unique and the bosses in later zones feel different from each other and are rewarding to beat. The merchant sells new weapons every day, so try to buy ones with high power and gems when you see them.

I agree with some of the other reviews that the game can get a little grindy. It would be nice to have more variety in enemies for a particular run that usually has about 5-7 combats. As for your goblins: you shouldn't get too attached to them because you'll be retiring them and replacing them regularly. You can keep your favourite retired goblins in your Quarters and use them for breeding. For each run, you should prioritize harvesting materials to upgrade your rooms. You should also try to rescue goblins with new traits and higher stats, so you can breed them and unlock genes. You'll also find more variety in goblin appearances as you progress through the game. Soon you will find yourself with an entire army of blue goblins!

All in all, the game is fun and rewarding. I look forward to playing it more!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWake up babe. New Darkest Dungeon-like just dropped.

If you can avoid the allure of the trailer, I suggest go in completely blind, the narrative is a joy to unfold.

The hand-drawn backgrounds, storybook aesthetic, and lighthearted tone belie the unforgiving combat. You are frequently on the backfoot facing opponents far tougher than you. You will send your companions to die, and die again. You monster.

They do not sell their lives in vain. Their soul energy unlocks new combat abilities, and grows your X-Com style home base. Your lost friends will continue the fight on with you in spirit.

As Darkest Dungeon veterans will know, strategic use of deceptively weaker combat abilities that inflict status effects like stun and bleed is key. Each balanced character class has their roles to perform, and excel when you play to their strengths. Use your tanks to absorb hits intended for squishy glass-cannons. Combo your class abilities to pierce armored opponents, or inflict critical damage, and win the day.

One of my favourite wrinkles is if one of your allies are killed, their grieving friends will rally. Enraged, they gain a brief strength boost that may turn the tide against a difficult boss.

All in all, a soft calming fantasy game to unwind and vibe to after a long day.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game doesn't deserve alot of the hate it's getting in my opinion. There's been a few bugs but I can't remember the last time i played a game that didn't release these days with some launch day bugs. The gameplay loop is fun, fights provide a good challenge, it has some good humor and pokes fun at rpg tropes. I haven't really had a need to do any breeding as I get good enough new goblins from rescues and squatters. Some of the resources feel alot more scarce than others but not enough scarcity that I ever felt the need for grinding. One thing about this game that I love is that you do not need to stress much about your goblins dying in combat. the first fatal strike brings them to 1 health and they get a permanent debuff reducing their max life by half and if they do die, your remaining goblins get a massive buff and you still get the souls of the dead to help build up the base. I'd definitely recommend this game for anyone who likes rpgs in general.
