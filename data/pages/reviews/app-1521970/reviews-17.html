\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-Overview-

Looking past the bugs, I think the core mechanics and game play loop are really fun. ( 7.5/10 )

For me the game really picked up after chapter 3, which was about 8 hours in. I would say if you're not enjoying the game in the first hour or so then you probably won't enjoy the rest. So far I have enjoyed it a lot, even if the bugs make it hard to do so.

-My Opinion-

First of all it is frustrating to be soft locked once every 40 mins or so by mating goblins and them doing nothing, which forces you close the game and restart. Expect to have some patience if you're going to play the game as it is.

I don't mind the UI, although I agree with others that it could be optimised, especially in the base building part.

During dungeons I don't see the value of finding temporary weapons from chests that you can't take home, I'd rather they didn't exist or offered something actually valuable. Also there are encounters in the dungeons that often are the first thing you run into and require gold to participate in. You don't take any gold with you so they are pretty pointless and leave you feeling like you missed an interesting opportunity. These are some minor points that don't stop me from enjoying the game, but I hope they can be improved.

The most important thing for me, which I feel has been missed somewhat, is that the goblins aren't that gobliny. Also a lot of the potential for the RP in the RPG part of the game is lacking. Other than class, weapon and appearance there's not much else you can customise or control about the goblins. They don't talk or have much personality beyond what you interpret in their expressions. Add the fact that they are supposed to die or be retired means you can't really get attached to them. I would like for them to add someway of customising the goblins further, or immortalising you favourite ones with a statue or shrine in game.

I also think the world building in general could be improved. The overall lore/story is quite minimal/slow and I think would benefit from being sped up or introduced in other ways for example item/place descriptions that can be explored in the players own time. The narrator is an interesting way to introduce the narrative, however, for me it kind of breaks my immersion (similar to Biomutant). I found myself thinking "is this voice a part of the games world in any way? or just someone reading me a book?" I'd rather it be a character in game, or even an old goblin (like in overlord) that narrates.

Take everything I've said with a pinch of salt and if you're unsure, I recommend you play the game yourself, potentially after some updates :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanging my review to a recommend. First patch came out promptly and fix the issue I was facing. Always appreciate good dev support, and it should be given the credit it is due.

Further the game is a nice mix of aesthetic stylized fun; the goblins, narration, laid back game, and actual strategy for battles that can make a real difference in success or failure.

A few items that would be good to polish still is the general interface. For a game that has a very nice light flow, it interrupts that a lot with extra clicking and navigating, or unclear structures. If I can use A and D to go left and right, I'd like to use Enter by default to do interactions (climb, jump, gather, etc.). Keeping track of what i can equip to whom, with what gems and items, or bonuses last forever vs just a mission I still dont know how to properly do. Personally, I'd rather bonuses and items just be nerfed and all be permanent, seems a waste to fret over your paths and selections when most of them dont last more than 1 battle.

All in all though, I'm invested as long as the devs are too. I'm still playing it now, and with only some minor tweaks I can see myself playing for quite a while more.\t\t\t\t\t
