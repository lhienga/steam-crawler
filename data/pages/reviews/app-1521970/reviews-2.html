\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun little turn-based strategy game that borrows lots of its combat mechanics from Darkest Dungeon, and its base-building from XCOM. The game has a charming hand-drawn storybook aesthetic that fits the game well. Also the role reversal between the fantasy good guys and bad guys is heavily reminiscent of the Overlord game series from a decade ago.

Like its inspirations, Goblin Stone too has little mercy for your squad members, but that does not mean the game is necessarily difficult. Indeed due to the rage boon, the breeding mechanics, and the expendable nature of gear, a few deaths here and there usually make you more powerful instead of weaker. While survival is still broadly speaking preferable, these mechanics help ensure that you won't feel too sorry when your goblins eventually face their demise.

Some people have complained about bugs. For my part, I have experienced none so far.

Definitely consider this game if the idea of "Darkest Dungeon but not dark and also with adorable gobbos" sounds interesting to you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fine game and only a soft recommend. I will admit that it mostly gets by on its charm. I love myself an underdog storyline, cute visuals, and monstrous humanoids. The gameplay is ... good enough, which is why I would suggest buying it on sale. If the game was not pandering to me specifically, I would have probably quit it by now. Also still needs a lot of ironing out.

It's not particularly challenging nor particularly innovative. It's like DD but not as good in terms of gameplay loop, variety and basebuilding. The breeding and "deckbuilding" mechanics are really underbaked, but at least each of the classes are balanced. They all have well-defined roles and each of them justifies themselves in gameplay.

However, both in terms of abilities and genes, there really isn't much variety because there are always options that are very clearly the best. It's always just "max out the attribute scores and take genes that accentuate the class' purpose and further drive up its main attribute".

The game struggles with variety in general. Variety in enemies is the biggest culprit to me. For the first 20 or so hours of gameplay you just find different power levels of the same handful of enemies. Only in the third map do you encounter new enemies AND get variety in scenery, too.

Truly, the single saving grace, and it's a BIG saving grace, is that it's so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cute. The goblins are adorable. The orcs are adorable. The gnolls are adorable. The kobolds are adorable. Somehow, the trolls are adorable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Good:
1. Awesome Voice acting.
2. Great Story line, including a unique "start" of the campaign.
3. One of the best ways I have seen a game deal with "death" of characters.
4. It's very challenging.

The Bad:
1. There is a memory leak in the game, only thing I can think of, as this game will max out my 2090 TI - even though this game isn't some graphically intensive game. This happens mostly in the menus, even if I turn the graphics down to low and limit the FPS.
2. Practically no "loot" in the game. When you go out on the "small adventure" or "location" almost everything you find there is temporary. So if you find a weapon? Temporary, it's gone after the short adventure. Buffs don't follow after the adventure, you can upgrade your attack cards via a "battle guru, orc, well that is just for the small adventure as well. I think it's a terrible way to have a game played, as I have no real "reason" to want to go through these adventure locations, other than to move the story forward. I have been playing for a while and i still only have the weapons the game starts you out with.
3. The only thing that is permanent in the adventure locations are death and near death. If your goblin hits a really low HP, they get "mortally wounded" which is a death sentence. IF they survive, they get 50% max health forever, so it makes them kind of useless. At this point it's either let them die, dismiss them, or retire them, they are no long a "viable" team member.
4. There isn't any kind of experience or levels/any kind of progression of the goblins as well.
5. This just makes it feel like I am doing the same thing on repeat, even after the third location. It just makes it feel very disconnected in my opinion, I would rather get through the map as quickly as possible, knowing that unless i find a treasure chest that has materials in it, it's all just worthless after the run is done.
