\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fun game! I play it on the Steam Deck with no issues.

If you're a fan of goblins (gobbos) then this is an absolute must!

You start out with only two gobbos but slowly build a base that can house a gobbo city. Over time you bring more gobbo's with different traits and appearances. You can breed these gobbos to create new gobbos.

There's a lot of complaints about the game and some of the criticism is fair. However, the dev's are working to fix the game and actively engage with people in the discord channel. I highly advise buying this game, not only because you are supporting a dedicated indie game dev studio, but to also support the likelihood of more goblin based games appearing in the future.

Story --> 8/10
Gameplay --> 8/10
Art --> 9.8/10
Overall --> 8.9/10

Once the game goes through a few more patches it will likely become a 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow did this game win so many awards?

The premise is great but the game's optimization and overall performance affect how much fun can be had at any point in time. If you're playing on a high resolution, some things in the game become impossible to interact with and on top of all of that...the game loop is incomplete and boring.

There's Slay the Spire and Darkest Dungeon elements to it, but none of them actually work as you want them to.

- You have a finite number of cards each class can play. No further unlocks as you progress in the game.
- Interacting with your base between runs feels like more of a chore than a benefit.
- The "story choices that matter" only affect the rewards you get versus anything else fun. Gold, or some power, but you also have to play a very boring mini-game to see if your goblins have enough cumulative stat of that type to unlock it.

I really wanted to like this game and gave it a fair number of hours before realizing I couldn't continue playing it in the state its in. Maybe they'll turn it into a complete game one day. On the plus side, the graphics are really cool.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely write reviews, but this game has been such a letdown I just have to. It could be so great. The artwork is awesome, the premise and storytelling are enjoyable, but... it's just such a slog. Gold becomes pointless, there aren't enough persistent upgrades to make farming enjoyable in any way, your lair and the upgrades are nice, but it's so hard to farm for the upgrades (and again, farming is just a chore) and they made breeding for genes and stats boring somehow. I really really wanted to like this game. I got to the last area by pure persistence and I'm just so bored playing this game that I probably won't finish it, despite how much that will irk me.

If they tweak this game and add in more reasons to want to farm, add more persistent upgrades and change the ridiculous mechanic where you are locked out of upgrades and genes, this could be one of my favorites. Until then, it's just a big slog and a bummer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is just dull. 12 hours in and I have fought the same 10 enemies over and over. The equipment is boring. The base building is incredibly shallow. The base building UI is horrible. You get all kinds of resources and there is no way to see which room needs what kinds of materials without clicking in. The upgrades themselves are dull. The combat is easy and shallow. This game needed another year in the oven.\t\t\t\t\t

What I've seen so far is a whole lot of repetitive, generic enemies to grind through. The "darkest dungeon" danger is not there. Oh, and "My barracks are full" is annoying as hell and really prevents experimentation or breeding new goblins. Limiting the active roster to 12 (later 18) goblins is just a poor design decision. I don't think any of the lair updates allow me to build second barracks or improve the ones I have fast enough.\t\t\t\t\t
