\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoblin Stone is a grindy, repetitive roguelike strategy game. That isn't necesarily a bad thing, but it depends on what you're expecting from the games you play.

The game revolves entirely upon bands of goblins you find/breed and organize yourself to travel the world. I haven't finished the game completely, in fact I'm very much in the beginning of the game, but have enjoyed a lot building/leveling the base and breeding the little buggers to optimum stats/genes. I cannot stress enough how much grind this game has.

The art is nice to look at, the music however isn't that memorable and the narrator has a good voice but gets repetitive after a while.

Overall, if you want something to play while watching something in your computer, I think this game might be it. If you're looking for something that actually holds your attention, maybe look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI heard people compare this to Darkest Dungeon and while there are similarities, it's the differences that separate the 2 games.

For example, if you lose a character in DD, you lose all the time and effort into levelling up that character.
If you lose a goblin in GS, you get rewarded for it. In fact, you can't progress unless you retire your goblins, or lose them in the dungeon.

Another difference is the classes. In GS, some aren't unlocked until you get to certain points in the story. In DD, the classes are random stagecoach selections. In DD, you can stay with a single comp, but different areas will be more difficult (like a bleed team in the Cove) so you'd start looking for other strategies to use. In GS, once you have a good comp, you don't want to change it. I've been running 3x hunter and 3x shaman for the last 15 hours and the game is a breeze.

If you don't like grindy games, this isn't for you. The lack of wood and fibre will stop your base from improving. The game would be served better if they doubled the wood and fibre rewards. It's such a roadblock. I had entire row of inventory of leather and bones, but barely any fibre and a lot less wood.

People also mentioned bugs, but I have only had the game crash once and when I restarted the game, I only lost a round or two of combat, so it wasn't really game-breaking.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tToo many issues.
Somewhere in this release, there is a fun game... but it is ruined by so many annoyances, bugs, and bad game design.

--Bugs, can't trust the game will actually save your progress. Still true 18 days after release.
--Game performance is bad. Low framerates and laggy cursor. My computer is not the worst, with a 1080Ti video card.
--Character progression is weird. You never level up. All skills are shared among all goblins of one class. I don't like how you can't customize each goblin. Small amount of choices. Not many good skills.
--Narrator can't be turned off
--No fun loot in the game. Very rarely have good loot to buy at the merchant. Get only trash weapons from quests, and nothing from boss fights.
--Can't turn off loud ambient sound. (endless running water in base, flies buzzing on zombies, etc)
--Terrible UI
--Really annoying animations on UI that you can't turn off (every menu is slow to appear, and annoying to navigate)
--Hard to see the things you want to see about your gobin roster or the upgrade options and costs. You need to click 20 menus to see anything, and every click leads to a slowly animated window
--Same battles over and over again.
--You get stuck into your build. You get permanent buffs on goblins and then you don't want to get rid of them.
--The way you upgrade skills during a level, means you want to have as many goblins of one class as possible (so that the buffs apply to all of them) This makes your group more homogeneous and boring.

Everything leads to you fighting the same monsters over and over with the same exact party. Gets very boring. If you are bad at the game, you probably have goblins that die, and then you replace them with whatever... but my goblins never die and so I have the same party for every fight.\t\t\t\t\t
