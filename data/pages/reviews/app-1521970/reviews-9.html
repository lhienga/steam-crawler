\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm torn on this review.
The game-play and mechanics seem great. There are so many bugs. This should be an early access title.
So many quality of life features should be added as well.

The UI is TERRIBLE! They dropped the ball here. The UI will show some but not all the stats for a goblin when you are trying to breed. So you have to manually go through each and every goblins stats one by one until you find the perks you want. Once you find said goblin, you must remember it's name because you can't select it for breeding until you exit the stat screen. Oh and the stat boxes randomly break. You try to open stats and a wood panel never opens. This means that goblin is broken and you can't even equip their gear. I end up dismissing them because I can't even sacrifice them for souls.

You can't play with gamepads, even though they are supposed to be creating that feature in the future.
The mouse cursor is so small and dark in color, I it's nearly impossible to see. I have to wiggle the mouse around forever to locate it.
In a fight if you want to heal another team mate there is a giant stat box blocking health bars at times, which makes it really hard to heal the right goblin.

The game does not seem to be balanced. I'm in chapter 3 and only party wiped twice. The problem is you need to kill some goblins to upgrade your main building at your base. I never have enough souls.

So I'm giving this a negative review as the developer did not release a complete game. This game has a lot of potential and I have had quite a bit of fun with it despite all the flaws.\t\t\t\t\t

Amazing Art & Story, Tight Gameplay, but Buggy & Sometimes Repetitive
A solid attempt at turning traditional RPG on its head - but perhaps wait for more updates!
https://youtu.be/Fpt2TxyYezo

TLDR in case you don't want to watch the video:

1. Art, story, and voice acting is stellar - this is a story that is funny, heartwarming, and totally unexpected! This isnt the traditional RPG where you get attached to a few characters and level them up with experience - but instead you make their offspring stronger by breeding those with desirable attributes. Dont get too hung up on them dying - goblin souls are whats needed to upgrade your base!

2. Combat is tight and fun in many ways, but it gets repetitive at times battling the same packs over and over on some maps.

3. That said, the occasional unique enemy and boss fights are where its at - intense, tough, and requires a lot of thinking and prep to get through.

4. I wish there was more enemy and map variety - aside from the themed biomes, most maps are basically picking the right choices between resources, items and rescuing more goblins from captivity - this really is the main drawback of the game!

5. Some buggy gameplay and UI issues at the time of launch - but Im confident they can patch these out with time!

6. The base building is pretty basic to say the least - but the breeding of goblins and using of souls to make better variations is pretty interesting.

Im hoping the devs patch out the bugs and add more gameplay variety - both in maps and enemies, as this is a very charming and lovable game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like to give games as much of a chance as I can, so unfortunately with my time played I don't think I can get a refund. That being said, there is a good game here but its buried under bugs and poor optimization.

The concept of Goblin Stone is great. Its essentially a Darkest Dungeon kind of game with 2 major aspects. The first is base building. The base building takes more from Xcom than it does Darkest dungeon and involves building rooms underground that's viewed as a 2d slice. The second major part of the game is the missions which are very reminiscent of Darkest Dungeons missions and combat. Stack on top of these two major elements the theme of goblins being the good guys or underdogs, and it's already a good recipe.

But... the game released with two huge issues. One, the bugs. Crashes, things going missing, effects not applying, etc. I encountered a run ending bug where I was unable to build the guild halls for any new classes going forward from the Acolyte. I was stuck with 4 classes because I couldn't get access to these rooms. Some games I can endure the initial launch bugs. But if there are bugs that essentially destroy my run on a campaign based game I can't reload from? Its death to that game.

Two, poor optimization. I don't always have access to the best hardware. Over the years I have jumped up and down and played my games from everything from a laptop to a desktop. One of the best things about being a strategy game lover is that most strategy games run really well even on low specs. They tend to also be very optimized. Unfortunately Goblin Stone feels very un-optimized. There are constant stutters in everything you do. From closing menu's to moving in missions. Exiting in and out of various screens can be very slow. In strategy games this can be a killer for me. Because of how frequent you tend to pop in and out of various UI elements in a strategy game it needs to be pretty smooth and quick.

I wanted.... to really love this game. The art, the concept, the combat. So many things are fun and a good idea. It's just not where it should be for a 1.0 release. Kind of really sad about this one. Was looking forward to a good xcom/darkest-dungeon campaign strategy game. Hopefully the developers can turn it around and fix the issues.\t\t\t\t\t

Old review.

The game is super charming, and I really enjoyed it until I got save data corruption at 36 hours. I can't decide if I was lucky or unlucky for it to take this long to hit me.

Man, you can't have this much save corruption on a game that only has one save file that auto saves. Saving has to be 99.999% stable to do that. Something really got messed up on QA. Not sure if they didn't have any, QA was ignored, QA did a bad job, or someone through in some code before the game came out that QA didn't get to test, but you can't launch like this. Its honestly so hard to get a game that stable I don't recommend doing that kind of save system, I say this as someone who used to love doing such a mode in other games and now does it with normal saves but a rule that I can't save scum. Loading on bugs only.

I really like how they helped counter the deaths on a mission run by giving you the chance to pick up more goblins to replace them, an innovative way to deal with death spiral that happens in a lot of squad based games where your expected to have people die. Enjoyable story, lighthearted with a heart string pull here and there. Music is relaxing, atmosphere is very pleasant. Love the art style in general and especially on the gnolls.

Beyond the save issues, it does have some balance issues with resource income at this time, and the fact that I hit late game with still the basic squad of goblins and was wrecking everything. I was using the breeding mechanic, but man it doesn't feel like the first 3 classes alone should be this good this late.

It also has a number of decisions that make it far too much of a time sink. Animations that are cute the first time but annoying the 100th, some animations that take longer than others or could be sped up by a 1/2 second. Needs 2x and 3x speed options for combat. The constant load screen between sections of the map is very annoying, would be easy enough to ignore if it wasn't for all the other time sinks.

The merchant sucks, and the resources trade is laughable. You get so few from him even as your overflowing with gold. He either needs more, or they need to add in a 2 to 1 conversation rate for resources. That would help deal with the imbalanced resource income as well.

The base needs little goblins running around doing stuff in places except the retirement home, barracks, camp, and war room. Most of it is an empty ghost town that feels so much like their should be goblins that I swear its another bug in a long list.

I honestly think the "scouting" missions and the "story" missions should be condensed into the same mission. Sp remove scouting missions all together and just have the story missions do the scouting as well. This is a large time sink that doesn't bring anything interesting to the table. I'd also change the bounties you need to do in some areas from "5 of any" to "kill each of the three once time". I've accidentally avoided potentially interesting bosses because its just "kill 5 bosses", and needing to kill more than each boss once seems like a time wasting grind.

The only visual issue i can think of is its really weird how straight faced the allied races are. Its kind of uncanny running through an allies camp and having them all stare at you with one or two completely still faces. It doesn't help by how dynamic and good the art is in the cut-scenes is either, the contrast makes it worse. The goblins can get away with it because of their many different looks, and you honestly stop looking at them after a while.

This game has such charm in so many places, but just too many save breaking bugs and it doesn't feel like it respects my time. I say this as someone who has put 1000s of hours into the Xcom series, many 100s into other games similar to this, and enjoyed many hours into city builders, factory games, and 4x. I specifically like playing time sink games. I think part of the problem is some of the ways this game sinks time makes it feel like a grind, not like your progressing. How to fix that? Not sure. But it needs help.

I hope the devs are able to patch this boat up, bail out the water, get their reputation back and get lots of sales.\t\t\t\t\t
