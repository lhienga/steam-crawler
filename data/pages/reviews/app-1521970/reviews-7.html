\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is with a heavy heart I write this, but sadly I would not recommend Goblin Stone to other people in its current state. The game is a bug filled grind, with limited content. Which is a shame for such a visually beautiful game.

Resources are far to scarce, and concentrated only on specific types (mainly wood and even more so on fiber)
Progression is non existent. Combined with the fact that combat is painfully easy, means that the main mechanical theme (breeding goblins that are better) falls by the wayside, as there is no need or urgency for it.
The breeding mechanic itself is very lackluster in and of itself. The barracks is to small to really delve into any sort of customization of goblins and the traits are hard locks between story progression points.
I had initially thought you'd gain new traits or be able to breed higher stat goblins by, you know, mixing and matching types and breeding them. Nope, grind until you reach a story beat and bam! You just find better goblins. So whats the point in breeding them?

I could go on. But there isn't a need.

This game has a lot of potential to be the Darkest Dungeon successor it wants to be, but it still needs a ton of work, but that's just my opinion. I really hope the Devs continue to fix and update this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Do not buy. Besides the bugs everybody else is mentioning, the overall gameplay loop is simply too based on random numbers to feel productive, and too slow and cumbersome to work as a roguelike.

This is my first ever review and I'm heartbroken because this game has so much potential but it simply isn't there with execution I have tried and tried to find the fun and it simply does not deliver.

Gameplay itself is relatively slow, with goblins barely able to fight at best. All upgrades available during missions are temporary and are taken at the exclusion of permanent resources and money, but even those are randomized to the point that it's difficult to predict ahead of time whether the expedition will be worthwhile. These missions take about half an hour to complete and eventually feel samey because each individual goblin has so few abilities... and those are randomized, making it difficult to plan ahead even in battle.

There are theoretically permanent upgrades available via goblin breeding and improvements of the headquarters, but breeding is also completely randomized and can only happen once per day (so one time between 30 minute missions.) Base upgrades require resources which, because of random chance within missions, are impossible to plan ahead for and extremely slow to collect.

There is an extremely steep difficulty curve outside of the starting area, leading to all-but complete permadeath party wipes. Theoretically we could upgrade via breeding and resources... but in practice, that feels impossibly distant. I've played for 23 hours because that's simply the sort of time investment this frustration requires.

I wish I hadn't purchased this game. Please, save your money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Wishlist this game and check back in down the line. If they can fix the problems, it has sky high potential

Man, this is a hard one to write because I love a lot of parts of this game as a long time turn based/base builder/roguelite fan. I have almost 20 hours played and will probably grind it out to completion and have an overall good time, but the experience is significantly hampered by a terrible UI (ranging from everything to tool tips to menus, from moving buildings in your base to trying to figure out which Goblin has the most power when changing up your party) and a plethora of bugs. While only one of the bugs I've experienced has been "game breaking," the issue (my screen was stuck on a previous image even though that event was over) was thankfully fixed with a reload, although I had to redo the previous fight. Speaking of which, for a game that says it saves constantly and there's no going back, I've had to redo things every time I had to quit mid run.

Of course, that's not touching on the plethora of frustrating bugs that weren't completely game breaking. I've run into significant issues with Hunter traps activating but doing nothing, the Guard's intercept/guard abilities not triggering as other goblins get attacked, and a couple other abilities either not working as intended or seemingly not working as intended and I have no idea what the intended interaction is. Sometimes it does exactly what I think it will, sometimes it doesn't. Which really sums up the crux of my negative review: either these core mechanical interactions are fundamentally bugged, or there's something mechanically going on that is not explained anywhere in the game. For this latter, it is a common frustration. This game's UI almost never gives you the information you want or need to make informed decisions, and when it does, it's usually displayed in a way that is hard to find, too vague to be useful at a glance, or simply just a pain the butt to navigate to because you have to go to a different menu screen (in another room) to find out stuff you want for the screen you are currently on. This can be even more annoying with the clunky and slow-responding UI. And don't even get me started with how you view your goblins themselves/how terrible the sorting options are. For a game that focuses so much on the breeding of Goblins and the importance of items, they make it incredibly hard and frustrating to navigate those aspects. I dread growing my base and having to try to keep track of even more goblins even though that would normally be a management genre inclusion that I'd love.

And these are just the most glaring issues, I could write another 10 paragraphs on the UI design problems of this game. To say it's not a polished product is an understatement. All of that being said, from what I can tell it’s the first game from this studio and I imagine they are still learning the ropes. I sincerely hope they learn from the criticism and keep working on this game, because if they can address the wide of variety of problems, they have the potential for a top tier indie game.

To start, the style, narration, and overall humor has been very enjoyable. The combat system is super interesting too. It took a while to grow on me, but I wound up loving the turn-based system where “time” is your combat resource and turn order affects party positioning. It can be a little difficult to follow or predict, especially when various slows and hastes get introduced into the equation and the turn order display doesn’t seem to take that into account. Which again goes back to the game simply never giving you enough information for your choices, but the base of a cool system is there. Same can be said with the breeding system. It’s a great idea that fits in super well with the intended disposable nature of the goblins. Normally I would love spending time on a breeding system like this as sort of management style optimization, but it’s so clunkily done that I dread it as much as enjoy it. The more I play, the more of a time-sink my base becomes, and not in a productive way. It’s just so hard to navigate effectively that I waste time completing basic game play loop tasks. I can also start to feel the grindiness creeping in, although this doesn’t bother me as much as it does some other reviewers because I enjoy the adventure loop overall and its combination of roguelite elements in temporary buffs/weapons and enhancements and permanent buffs/recruiting new goblins to replace your deaths and increase your gene pool.

While I obviously could go on about the bad and the good, the bottom line is that this game is early access quality, and for that reason I can’t recommend it. I would highly, highly, highly, suggest that anyone interested in this title wishlist it and check back in down the line, though, because if the devs are able to fix even the most glaring bugs and UI/QoL problems, this review would turn into an easily recommend. If you are like me and a big hobbyist gamer who really likes the ideas of this game, it might be worth picking up now, but that's up to you\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou can watch my short 5 minute review on the game here:
https://youtu.be/8HPjnc4PcQc

Goblin Stone is a story driven turn based roguelite which sees us manage a goblin settlement by farming resources on expeditions through beautifully stylized levels, where we face off against a handful of copy pasted enemies by following the amazingly narrated story of likeable orcs and clumbsy goblins, while a few major issues plague the whole gaming experience.

We follow the journey of a few goblins who got driven out of their home by a third of the lord of the rings cast. Shelter- and homeless they find refuge in a settlement which we extend with additional rooms to stack the gathered loot, train new goblins into different archetypes, trade with a friendly orc merchant and give them a private room to breed new generations of goblins.

With new genetically evolved allies we start our expeditions in the war room picking a mission to gather resources or a story relevant episode on the beautiful table. Once we pick a destination we see our goblins tread on a path with a few twists and turns replacing the simple roguelike themed maps of other games and giving us a sense of exploration not to mention the breathtakingly stylized enviroments. Amidst the pretty scenery we encounter first and foremost enemies which we face in the neat combat system.

These battles see our leather skinned group take turns with our enemies. Each character has an aray of actions to choose from with different cooldown times. Using a powerful attack sees our goblin move back on the time bar with the next one stepping into the front line. Since most enemies attack the goblin closest to them the combat system gets far more strategic since we not only have to take into account when our goblin will react next but who he will receive the next attack from. This simple yet clever change to the typical turn based combat system gets further effected by the few unique effects our skills can have, by slowing down the enemies, speeding up our own allies, or entirely stopping them in their tracks and on the time bar.
The variety of abilities increases further with the different classes our goblins can become, with the guard shielding himself and others or the acolyte healing his allies or the hunter setting up a poop trap which blasts our enemies... thats some explosive diarrhea...

Apart from the combat encounters we can also find merchants, new equipment for our goblins, caged companions we set free, camps to regain our health or shrines and trainers to upgrade our stats and abilities for the ongoing expedition, to name a few examples. Furthermore we encounter well written events which challenge our team to amass a certain stat to successfully finish this encounter.

These stat points furthermore dictate the classes our goblins can become and are heavily influenced by the traits each goblin has from birth, but more on that in a minute.

These classes can be assigned to our companions through the integral rooms in our settlement. If we want to have an acolyte in our team we require the room. This however opens up the first issue the game shows since not only do we need to encounter these classes to unlock their room, but also are the building costs of these rooms quite expensive. The rarity of the classes amounts to me having only five of the seven classes available after over 8 hours of gametime, while the building cost requires us to venture out 2-3 times before we have enough resources to build a single room.
This grind is one of the more annoying hurdles of the game, since it blocks our path to creatively setting up a varied group composition and are forced to fill the team with the same class multiple times during the first couple of hours.

Apart from issue number one we get back to the variety of our companions and their love to each other, since breeding is a fundamental part of our settlements progress. Every goblin has three stat points and possible traits effecting their combat power, health, armor or crit chance. These traits can be adjusted when sending two goblins with the wanted traits into the love making room which results in two new goblins with a mixture of their parents traits, while the OG goblins die during the mating act (death by snu snu)

Even though death is constantly around the corner for the green skinned folk their deaths are actually an integral part of the game, since goblin progress is being built on their ancestors backs, meaning every killed or retired goblin rewards us with souls which we can use to upgrade the settlement as a whole and gather more companions with more traits.

As mentioned the traits heavily impact the stat points which are essential to the class system since a brute with a high body stat cannot become a shaman, while a goblin who has the religious trait cannot become a fighting class, which makes sense since religious people are never violent... okay bad example.

Entangled in the class system are also weapons each goblin can carry which often come with unique traits of their own but feel like a missed opportunity since we cannot upgrade them in the long term but either temporarily for an ongoing mission with the gem stones or the blacksmith, yeah drop your head its a shame I know.

The enjoyable combat, mixed with the great artstyle and nifty settlement system, sadly cannot outway the current issues with the grind, the weak customization system and the fact that many players even encountered gamebreaking bugs. Therefore Goblin Stone currently cannot be recommended but once they fix the main issues, it can become a great game for sure!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to enjoy this game, as it has a charming visual style and im a sucker for the games where you play from the perspective of the "monsters" in fantasy.
But its severely lacking overtly clunky....

The pros:
For the most part, relatively balanced combat where you frequently just barely scrape by
Interesting class mechanic and dynamics between them
Detailed info blurbs on mechanics
Charming art style

The cons:
The UI is extremely sluggish and tends to eat inputs.
Mouse over tooltips exist for everything but they tend to refuse to show up
Some of the non-damaging abilities are flatout useless vs just doing basic attacks
Effects and buffs dont last that long, making it feel like you have 2 groups of 3 goblins instead of a group of 6.
Armor is kinda pointless since things hit so dang hard the 2-5 damage less per attack doesnt mean anything in the end.
Difficulty spike is real...
Despite not having access to all 6 classes right away you still find gear for those classes for some reason, bloating your limited inventory
Base building is a massive grind. I am selling off bushels of leather or bone because i also need wood or stone to use them and i have basically none of the latter.
Confirmation bias perhaps, but the % for bonus effects and resistances is a lie. For only resisting Slow 20% of the time it seems to be resisted more than 2/3 of the attempts....and i dont think i ever had my raiders' slash 20% bleed go off. Ever.

The game has promise, but right now its more aggravating than enjoyable. Also the difficulty spike is ridiculous...you go from an "end of area boss" fight having 1 ~50hp tough target and 2-3 other mediums of about ~15-25hp to suddenly "Here face 5 halflings that have 2 armor, 50hp, fast as hell, and intentionally single out your goblins to kill them faster"
I wiped on that fight 3 times before beating it, and i beat it with 2 goblins at a thread of life left. Prior to this, i think had 3 goblins die due to bad timing rather than oppressive difficulty.

Things i'd like to see improved:
Fix the UI so it stops eating button inputs, also the scrolling in the base building section needs help. I keep finding myself hitting a key or clicking with the mouse 2-3 times to initiate something like selecting the desired point on the world map.

Make effects like "Intercept" last longer. Things like Haste is fine, but most others are pointless.

Bleed needs a huge buff. Its initially better since it does 130% damage and ignore armor, but very quickly i can kill the enemy faster by just attacking through the 3-4 armor anyway especially when i have a high crit chance (of which bleed has no benefit from). Know how fast i stopped using bleed? 4th area. Perhaps improve it to 150% and drop the duration to over 4 turns instead of 5, since the main issue is speed of the kill.

Make armor actually worth it. I notice that the better attack for your Raiders is a multi-attack that gets somewhat neutered by high armor but NONE of the enemies seem to do multi-attacks, only debilitating or heavy hitting ones. You know armor is bad when my guardians' are just shieldbashing trying to get the stun and doing 4-5 damage to PREVENT an attack rather than soak it for another goblin.

Beef up the Mechant so he actually has things to purchase. Since you dont lose weapons when goblins die (or even if you party wipe) you dont need to buy many weapons and he seems to only have weapons 90% of the time. When he does have crafting supplies its like 1-3 leather or bone and thats it. I have thousands of gold i cannot use because i dont have any materials i need to build with. Except leather, man do i have a lot of leather for some reason.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the time of writing this the game sits on mixed reviews, 51% positive, 318 reviews total. Even right now a lot of negative reviews praise the game but cant rate it positively because of bugs, so I am expecting the overall score to rise soon (and you can check if I was right, teehee)
The game is fun and I havent had any of the big bad bugs the reviews mention, some hotfixes already went out. There is some work to do regarding quality of life, for example if you wanna scroll in the merchants inventory by clicking and dragging, you have to click inbetween items instead of on them, which initially made me believe you couldnt scroll by click and drag at all on that screen. Scrolling in the base screen doesnt work with awsd but only with click and drag or by edgescrolling. Some important tooltips dont show up on mouse over while the character portraits still come to a standstill from scrolling further down, so you have to wait for them to be perfectly still again before you can see the stats of the goblin you wanted to look at, and everytime you open an item cache in a mission you get a narrator voiceline, which would be fine, but you also cant press space bar again to collect all loot immediately, but instead have to first wait 1.2 seconds for the narrators voiceline to be finished, the first button press just skips out of the lil oneliner, small unnecessary stuff like that. None of this is a big deal and should hopefully be fixed soon.

Underneath all of this small stuff, however, the game is genuinely good. Varied enemies and bosses, strategic breeding of desired traits, a fun core combat loop, I also really like how they worked permadeath into their player progression, if you dont lose any goblins you will end up retiring them instead because that yields souls that you need to upgrade your townhall - and goblins dont level up anyway, the only stat that increases through play is how many souls you get on retirement, so you have incentive to use the newer, stronger generations. A lot of love in this one, deserves a way higher rating than it currently has and it hurts me to see when I get a new, rather basic achievement (for example breed goblins 30 times) and then I see that im only one of 1.5% of players who got this achievement.

I suggest getting this game and seeing for yourself, they just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ their launch by having a few major bugs initially. Overall it's already way better and will be very good once they sort out the smaller quality of life stuff.\t\t\t\t\t
