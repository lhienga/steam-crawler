\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI write this review with a bit of a heavy heart.

I think this game isn't worth my time now, but I'm hopeful it will get development support and fix various minor issues and add more content.

Let's start with the good:
- The backgrounds and art style is very charming and cohesive.
- There's a solid loop here of gameplay that inches you steadily upward, but it doesn't feel exactly like a loot treadmill/
- Skills are bound to weapons and jobs, which creates interesting decisions in your party composition.
- The timeline turn system is excellent, it makes debuff skills interesting and valuable.
- Enemies feel bigger and meaner than you, so you have to have your goblins work together to defeat them.
- My initial impacts with the game did involve hitting a few wonky bugs. It looks like the developer was already getting on top of those (within release week, so good job!)

Onto the not-so-good.
- The narrator starts off interesting and a bit glib, but becomes fairly generic lacking personality. Overly present on nearly every interaction. Not enough dialogue variety.
- The animations of the characters feels a bit stilted, especially the orcs. They lack smoothness when they're on scene.
- By and large, most of the goblins don't feel like they have any personality at all. They don't have many idle animations, they all sort of do the same thing together regardless of their job, they don't banter or interact with each other. The enemy adventurers feel that way, but it's more forgivable.
- There's a lot of missing UX options and polish (like smoother faster transitions between screens, support for key rebinding)
- The level generation is not particularly large or impactful. A chunk of the time you'll get shops early before you've had a chance to gather money, or encounter buff effects after you've fought the combats.
- The game overuses temporary upgrades that wildly swing things. You can get big buffs to Power, or resistances, or temporary weapon or skill upgrades. But they feel fleeting to the point of not feeling like a thing to build or play around with. I wish some of the upgrades gave a residual item you could apply to a future level, so there'd be more decision making in building your party.
- There isn't quite enough enemy variety in the early zones, leading to gameplay feeling repetitive.
- The hub area feels fairly nonperformant and feels clunky to click or move around on.
- Every character of a particular class is conformed to the same skill set, meaning you can't have one crit focused character and another big chop focused characters.

I really want to love this game, I didn't return it because I want to support the developer and hope this game gets worth me revisiting and updating this review!\t\t\t\t\t

20 hours in and all i can say is... i don't feel compelled to finish the game. I really wanted to like this game as i've been following the development in discord for about a year now. here are some of my issues:
- I wish the narrator wouldn't speak every. single. time. i pick up some loot. It stops me from accessing my inventory until he finishes speaking and its frustrating. It would be okay (i guess) if he had more than 5 lines of dialogue when i picked up something but it gets repetitive.
- I succeeded at failing a 3% chance of failure and lost my best goblin right before a boss fight.
- The merge options for the main base are limited, i have LOADS of space for repeated buildings but whats the point???
- In my opinion it takes WAY too long to get access to the other units.
- Breeding can be a huge hit and miss, whats the point of showing 3 different potential spawns and not getting any of them, or even close for that matter.
- Traps DO NOT have a 50% chance of going off on hits.... i know it isn't impossible for these to fail 8 -10 times in a row... like i'm sure i can flip a coin to land on heads 8 times in a row... eventually...


- The art style is nice and i liked the writing.
- I haven't run into any save corruption issues like some others have in their comments...sounds like a bad time. So no issues for me there.
- The weapons felt punchy but there was definitely a lack of options in weapons and attacks that made an impact. There was really only 3 'attacks' i felt useful for a couple classes.... not to mention the fact that some cool weapons i found along the way i cannot bring back with me... like... what? Why? I already have WAY too much gold and the merchant never has any good weapons so why cant i keep my loot i found while exploring?

I just wish there was more reason to keep playing other than grind for materials to eventually get a new class to only be let down again by breeding over and over again. It just feels too slow. I dont know if this is supposed to be a long game or a short game and i don't feel like some of the devs did either :( Sorry.\t\t\t\t\t
