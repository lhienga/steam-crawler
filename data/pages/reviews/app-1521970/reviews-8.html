\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever thought it would be so much fun killing hobbitses, wizzies and men-folkses hunting XP.
With names like Slaghacker and Windranker, this game can leave you gobsmacked.
Especially if you're English!

EDIT:
Now that I have completed the game:
This game is Ironman mode only, great for XCOM players who enjoy this mode.
Upon completion, there are escalation settings for a replay that will make the game more challenging - I have not tried this yet.
The story and narration was good, I like to read books and the story plays out like one.
The RPG mechanics are quite simple and there are Crowd Control skills that can be utilised - I haven't seen streamers use them effectively yet but I found that they certainly reduce the amount of hits you will receive and made the tactics fun deciding which skill to use.
Deaths of your goblins are not that painful and are actually rewarding as you need to farm their souls anyway to upgrade your base and unlock skills.
The breeding of your goblins is intuitive, it just takes many attempts to get what you desire.
(HINT: build more than one breeding room and upgrade them)
Goblins do not level up, upgrading your guilds will upgrade their power, so new replacement goblins will be similar to the one that died. No more rage quitting a campaign like I used to do when losing captains on XCOM ironman mode!

My PC: 1080ti graphics, SSD drive, 7 years old. The game ran smoothly.
There are bugs and each time the game locked it required closing and reloading the game which did not take long. Fortunately I did not experience my game save ever being corrupted. It did lock during battles but upon reloading I was able to continue the battle with no problem.
It is understandable that anyone with a HDD drive will be frustrated because of load times; with SSD's its quick as the game is not huge.

Overall for the £18 I spent, this game is fun, well worth it and I believe they will fix the bugs over time.
For me none of the bugs were game breaking and this game has me hooked!

Now for my 2nd playthrough...\t\t\t\t\t

I fell victim to the bug where the base deletes a room if you move the stairs, but I had enough resources to replace that room so I wanted to play on. But then for some reason the game no longer let me build guilds. Seems unplayable from that point right?

So I decided I'd start again because I had been enjoying it. And then the game wouldn't let me skip the intro scene dungeon and just jump straight to the point where the base building starts again. The intro dungeon is cute and fun... unless you've been forced to restart due to a bug screwing up your save.

QoL improvement suggestions:
1) Ability to skip the intro dungeon.
2) Set save states at chapter completions, and ability to restart from the start of the chapter of your choice.
3) Ability to set combat speed at a higher rate - combats are a bit repetitive (as per any turn based thing) and they tend to go very slowly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoblin Stone is a neat game that immediately captivates with its charming aesthetic and engaging gameplay. Worth the full price and my 3 years wait.

Its very unique turn order mechanic adds a refreshing layer of strategy to the genre, keeping you on your toes and encouraging dynamic decision making.

And it offers a cute story that complements the gameplay!

However despite its strengths, Goblin Stone falters in its UI outside of battles. Literally the worst I've seen in a long time. And I play a freaking lot of games.

Honestly, how is it such a good indie game but makes Kojima look like an UI expert?

PROS

I like the hand-drawn visuals and the relaxing sound, plus the story of covering an underdog race. And the story-telling is nice.

I like the innovative battle, where the cost of using a skill is the number of turns you have to wait before acting again. However, this was not properly explained during the tutorial, so I had been scratching my head what the skill numbers meant.

CON/BUGS
Also, while I haven't encountered the bug where people where unable to play pass the tutorial, I did encounter some.

Some genes are not working as intended, like Alert and Defensive.

Spirit bonus after the base value of 4 is not working as intended.

Sometimes not being able to click on the newborns in the warren. (Need to close game and re-enter to fix)

Issue with selecting goblins. When you click/select on a goblin on the panel/list, another goblin will be shown in the list once the selected goblin is highlighted. This makes it a pain when sorting or finding a particular goblin for breeding.
