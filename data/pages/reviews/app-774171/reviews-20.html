\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe non-recommendation is more to highlight the buyer beware aspect of this review for late adopters of DLC content. Do recommend Muse game on sale. Understand DLC can no longer be all grabbed in an all in one DLC package via steam purchase anymore. Have to go through the in game store. Yes they have Muse Plus which gets you everything released up to October 2023, but after that is all purely in game store. Most of those usually run $8.99 each, so the price hike for those that don't catch the train does ride high for missing the train. Are they worth it? The assistance is worth it for learning players and the tracks themselves come with fun unique visuals and are crossover characters so licensing fees.

If you already got Just As Planned, Muse Dash has already grandfathered you in and you're riding good as long as you stay active during first week releases of DLC updates. Otherwise, you'll be in the same $8.99 train as non-owners of Just As Planned.


Muse Dash Rundown
Simple musical rhythm game with fun graphics (Only 2 buttons)
Prompt directions, visibility, and pace of what to grab or avoid is how difficulty gets adjusted between charts.

Content is absolutely geared towards teenagers and young adults with a taste for the upbeat aspect of Japanese & Chinese popular music culture.
Shows signs of being designed for Mobile devices and Nintendo Switch first but added in good convenience options for PC version, including controller support.
Large, mostly upbeat music selection
Crossover content open for unlimited time frame
Popular songs do get special chart effects and visuals
Original and crossover characters with various boosts and gameplay modifiers with the ability to shut off their modifies with a helper if you just want a skin swap.
Newer players will eat DLC price hike that early adopters will not have to.
As of review, still being updated with more songs after 6 years both free and DLC, with a mixture of crossover and original songs.
Many Easter Eggs and hidden charts, from Touhou to Cytus 2. Knowledge of what happens in those games may prove useful to unlocking those secerts without guides.


In speaking of Easter Eggs
For those who want to understand why Muse Dash is still special despite it's simple concept
Bad Apple has special visuals on their chart
the nyan cat song is best enjoyed after mastering the default songs
If you're familar with the hellohell animated music video, the chart visual fade effects match pretty close despite there was no need to do so without affecting game play.
Cytus 2 has their most famous song of their lore be given dual treatment of played straight and for those of us who know when the song played in the story, can find and play a special chart with unique visuals as well
Default songs from level 55 onwards will play around with player expectations for unique challenges

TL;DR
The chart designers didn't have to go those extra miles and this is by no means an exhaustive list, but if you know the pop or meme culture behind a song, the care the chart designers add to each song shows through the visuals. Some of them hidden through repeat clicking difficulty before starting a song.


General Vibe Of Music Runner
Muse Dash is the simple one to just pick up and get back into.
Good mood booster.
Mixed feelings on the management change but their handling of newer Muse Characters and their bonuses (i.e Rin and Len helping players doing duo notes & hold notes hit off hand prompts easier) aimed to be reaching out to both add in popular music based icons into the game that also help players still learning advanced difficult chart patterns, while not overshadowing challenge characters that reward higher skilled players who like to curse less than perfect game play for score boosts.
i.e. Little Devil Marija, who only really works for already fast paced charts, otherwise she straight up offs herself in under 30 seconds due to her HP drain.

If the Japanese and Chinese heavy song rooster isn't your thing, try out DJMax. It's a significantly more PC based title with a massive collection of charts and many more English songs to rock out to. Funny enough Muse Dash collabed with DJMax Respect V as well and Rin is a pretty good collab character for those who dislike timing fading prompts. Or just stick to Beat Saber.

Old Rockband 3 and Dance Dance Revolution fan who has been seeking out various music games ever since former musical rhythm titles faded from prevalence. Disappeared off Steam for several years when titles like Arcaea, Deemo, Cytus 2, Lanota, and Beat Saber played better on non-PC attached devices.
