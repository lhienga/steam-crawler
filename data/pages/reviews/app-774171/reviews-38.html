\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUsually I wait to finish a game before reviewing it but, uuuh, you can't. But I put in a fair bit of time to feel comfortable putting a review out. Muse Dash is a rather simple rhythm game to which is a great entry point into the genre. The music selection is quite healthy to help a newer player, or even a veteran, gain their footing while playing along.

The visuals themselves can be a bit distracting. Not even regarding the characters themselves, the visuals themselves can be a bit too much when trying to focus on the track itself. There've been a few times where I've found myself lost, not because of the rhythm, but because of the visual clutter on the screen.

But I do also want to bring up the tracks themselves. Overall, I found the gameplay simple, yet engaging. Usually, I feel the maps work well with the difficulty you are selecting and is mapped quite well to the song you're playing too. However, I did find some songs w/ harder difficulties to add more when it doesn't seem to fit quite right. Hitting multiple notes when maybe it should have been a simple hold. Or adding notes during a hold as well which makes me scratch my head. These don't come up often, but it feels like cracks that formed due to the simplicity of the game and the need for some songs to fit the harder difficulty.

Overall though, Muse Dash is quite stunning! I find myself playing it here-and-there for a little bit, passing time with a few tracks. It's perfectly good for killing some time and, with the simplicity of it's controls, is easy to pick up right away to start getting into the rhythm of things!\t\t\t\t\t
