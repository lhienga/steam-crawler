\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember getting this game on iOS when it first came out, and now moving over to PC, I've definitely gotten a lot better. I've played thousands of hours of other rhythm games like Geometry Dash, so getting full combos on level 8 maps and below with just my mouse was a piece of cake. If you've ever played Harmo Knight on the 3DS, this game has a similar gameplay loop. I think overall the basic concept is pretty fun, and definitely worth the price of the base game. DLC adds quite a bit of content, but I'd really only recommend it if you *actually* made it all the way through the base game and want to play more after that. I've gotten through all of the achievements in the game without the DLC (which would make a couple like getting a certain number of characters/illustrations unlocked easier), and now that I've gotten through it all, I think I'm ok with getting the DLC personally. I *did* wait for a sale though, since the asking price of >30 bucks is kinda steep for what is essentially more maps to play through.

Here's the part of the review where I move a little further from what it does well. The game does have content to come back for, but I think it'd be more compelling if it were more community driven (ie: you could create custom maps and play other people's insane custom maps). As it stands now, once I run through all of the songs in the game in S rank (or 100% if it's a level 7 or below song), then I'm done.

Anyways, if you're looking to do the achievements without the DLC, then the 20 illustrations will probably be the biggest hurdle time-wise, since I had to level up to over level 180 on my account before I got my last one. Seeing the number of people that have the achievement though compared to some ones that are easier, my guess is that a lot of people just ended up buying the DLC. So, if you do cave early, you are probably in good company.\t\t\t\t\t
