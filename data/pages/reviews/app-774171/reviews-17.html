\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not the target audience for this game. I do not watch anime, much less the "advanced" kind. That said, I do like rhythm games. And much like its characters, Muse Dash is one that is stripped down so that we can focus on the fun parts.

You play with only two buttons: a jump hit and a ground hit. You jump over saw blades and hit enemies to the beat of the music, and that's it. Charts are friendly to all skill levels-- ranging from tapping along to quarter notes all the way to pure chaos. The enemies (which act as "notes") and effects are a treat to look at, making the game feel incredibly alive compared to more traditional games in the genre. Songs are catchy, bouncy, and fun. Playing this feels like eating candy and is just as addictive.

If you like skimpy 2D girls, cool. But if you don't, do not be put off by the gimmick. At less than a dollar on sale, this game is a must-have in almost any library. If you're not convinced, you can play Unbeatable: White Label for free-- if you like that game, you'll LOVE this one.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t

Rhythm game with very little punishment.
Gameplay
Missing a note will break your combo, but not necessarily take your HP. You can literally clear a song by aiming for not hitting a single note, you only lose HP if you character collides with a note. You not only hit notes, but also avoid obstacles that would damage your character and break your combo, which gives an unique feel of variety to the game. Although most rhythm games are 4-lined, this one is 2-lined, which again is a little unique or unusual, especially for those used to playing rhythm games with 2 hands. You cannot change keybinds, only presets.
Maps (or as they call them here "charts")
To fully enjoy them, you will need the DLC. Not only that, but if you heard of or seen those modded maps, you will need the DLC to play those (although they're no supported by developers in any way). Think of the base game as a demo unless your favorite songs are included in it.
Characters
There are not many characters, but they have variations or skins with different skills or passive abilities. You can unlock most characters by playing and collecting "shards" but buying the DLC will unlock them instantly.
About the DLC
Buying the DLC (Muse Dash Plus) will unlock a lot of songs, characters and their skins. I recommend it, but even that doesn't unlock everything. You must play to unlock base game songs. There are a few micro-transaction style mini-DLCs (vocaloid packs) which I actually recommend if you like those songs.
Sexual Content?
Just a little, as the characters strike poses and show a little skin on victory screens and loading screens, but nothing wild, this isn't an eroge. Not all characters do that kind of suggestive poses so if you would like to avoid that kind of theme, there's no problem.
