PRETTY GOOD, JUST KIND OF EXPENSIVE IF YOU WANT THE WHOLE-ASS PACKAGE

I've seen people say this game is "like Taiko but with hot women" and I've never played Taiko but looking at gameplay of it... kind of, probably?

(first things first, yes, there's a considerable amount of titty in this game, which might scare you off, but the game itself's pretty solid too and you can individually turn off all the questionable loading/title screen art if you're worried about playing this in front of other people)



This is a two-button rhythm game (so sorta like taiko), with each button being separated into its own vertical "lane", and you use the buttons to hit enemies that come along both lanes.
(you can map multiple buttons to the same lane mind you, which is gonna be REALLY helpful later on as you have to hit more notes faster)

Has all the usual types of "notes", including held notes and "mash as fast as you can"-type notes. Plus a couple curveballs like "ghost" notes that fade into invisibility as they approach forcing you to measure when to hit them by ear.

There's also gravity since you're controlling a character running to the right so button taps on the higher path will have you falling back to the lower lane after a second. This gets used for the purpose of un-hittable obstacles that you have to jump over, or little note pickups that you just have to touch regardless of timing.


You can unlock a good bunch of characters as you play, via randomized "piece" drops you get as you level up, (though you can't get duplicates of ones you've already completely unlocked so it's not really a gacha thankfully), each with their own little passive effects and or touched up mechanics. You also get pets the same way, which have more minor but also more specific effects depending on which you equip.

It's pretty solid honestly, though starting from song difficulty 9+ or so it goes into the usual eastern rhythm game ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ where you're not going to get ANYWHERE close to clearing through the notespam unless you sink like 300 hours into the game to learn it in and out.


...Music variety is where it starts to suffer a bit though, considering most of the basegame music (hell, even most of the DLC stuff, especially the Otaku packs) are vocaloid, jpop, or adjacent types of music. Not all of it, obviously, but with 500+ songs and a general focus on these genres, a lot of it kind of starts to blend in, and that type of music really ain't my thing. Not exactly a super weeb.

That said though the DLC brings with it a ton of more varied stuff too, even if you might have to trudge through the samey stuff to find it. There's a favorite system and plenty of filtering options though, to make browsing a bit easier.

slic.hertz alone IMMEDIATELY kicked my rear end into next weekend and made me realize the game had way more going for it musically than I originally thought.


On that note, there's about 50-ish songs on the base game, which goes for really REALLY cheap. It's the $30+ DLC that brings the total up to 500+, including collabs with other rhythm (and even non-rhythm) games.



Now, uh... Originally "the DLC" consisted basically of just a single pack called "Just in time", like $30 or so? And you'd buy that and get permanent access to all the songs PLUS whatever they release in the future.

If you already had that purchased, like me? Great, you should still keep access to everything they add later (probably so they don't get sued).
If not, uh... It's a bit of a tougher sell. It's really expensive, even if it DOES still bring with it a ton of content.

It's a perfectly fine game, again, it's pretty fun, but you might wanna get DLC stuff on a steep sale if you didn't buy into it early already.
