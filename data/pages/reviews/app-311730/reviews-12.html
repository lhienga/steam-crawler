\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has solid gameplay but the DLC will drive you insane.

This game is nowhere near as bad as Street Fighter V where you have to pay money to unlock entire game modes and most of the characters, but it's still pretty bad. While all the DLC is cosmetic the majority of this games rewards happen to be cosmetic which makes having the vast majority of those options being paid extra content is another example of a company nickel-and-diming the consumer to death... especially once you add up how much the cost is to get ALL of the DLC outfits. Here's a hint, it's over $1000 US to completely unlock this game.

The story is as pitiful as it is in most fighter games and there isn't much to do aside from online play which is kind of a pain to figure out how to get into. After you figure that out you are treated with actual gameplay which is actually pretty fun. The fighting elements and styles are all very distinctive and the balance seems to be there as long as you've put the practice in. But even then you'll come across people with costumes you haven't been able to get because you didn't want to put the crazy amount of money into the game.

So if you don't mind hitting a pay wall when it comes to in-game rewards then this is fine, but if you are one of those who wants to get all the costumes for your incredibly well-rendered sexy characters then forget it unless you got some high numbers in your bank account. Oh, and the special characters like Mai Shiranui? You can't unlock them anymore for some reason so forget it. I can't suggest this game for anyone who doesn't like DLC that holds a primary aspect of the game back. You can get SOME good outfits, but overall it's disappointing and you'll get over the fun pretty fast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really mixed in my rating...The thumbs up here is strictly for the gameplay-you can download a free version first, it gets you 4 playable characters and every play mode except Story. The Full Game package gets you Story Mode, 30 additional characters (pretty much every prior DOA fighter ever and a couple crossovers from Sega's Virtua Fighter series; you have to buy Naotora Li separately, while as of this review-October 2019-Mai Shiranui is no longer available) and the DOA 2 & 3 soundtracks, and compared to individual purchases will save you roughly $100. The game looks awesome in HD and plays well although you need a high-end CPU and HD graphics card, it's easy to pick up but challenging to master as while it's a "pure" fighter (no weapons, fireballs, etc.) the real emphasis is on countering and technical attacks. And you have the option for the "DOA physics" for the female fighters if you like the extreme bouncy-bouncys.
The thumbs down is because of the DLC. If you want all the costumes that aren't included in the full version, prepare to spend roughly $1100. That is insanely overpriced, and this is coming from someone who unhesitantly dropped a couple hundred for every Xtreme Legends: Dynasty Warriors 8 DLC (I waited for sales though). If you want them that bad, get someone to buy or gift them for you or hold out for a sale, but even then I wouldn't even though there are some very fanservice-oriented ones. If you buy and DLC outside of the full game bundle, just get Naotara Li and regret that Mai is no longer available.
If you're thinking about this game, download the free version and try it out, and if you like what you see get the Full Game bundle and the Naotora DLC, then try and sucker people into buying you the rest of the DLC. Don't buy the costume DLCs unless you can catch at least a 75% off sale as they are ridiculously overpriced and thus not worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
