I gotta say i was UBER sceptical if i should buy this game having played it on PS4 (The superior version to many).
It was on sale with a bunch of DLC for 19.99 EUR so i dove in.
Mostly for the enhanced graphics PC would offer and mostly to be able to play it in 4K.

I got to say i was not dissapointed.
Being able to crank up the resolution made it, at least for me, for the extra 20 EUR to own it on PC.
Great controls, slick 60fps in 4K power by my GTX 1080, same great singleplayer component with a bunch of DLC included.

I recommend it on PC if your GPU isable to output at least 1440p in 60fps and if you wanna take part of the DLC that's bundled in right now.

I can't say anything about the multiplayer component because i wouldn't dare to try it.
Too many awesome players out there. Single player is my ♥♥♥♥.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally recomended this game, but after time has passed, I have to change that to not recomended. I can't believe this game is still $40 when it originally came out on ps3 and 360 in 2012. $40 for a six year old game with issues and missing features.

One of the biggest issues is just that online functions weird. Instead of joining a lobby or something, you just play arcade and wait for a notification to pop up that someone else might want to fight. So you hit select and it stops your fight against the computer and you have to wait for them to respond. In DOAU and DOA4 you could just join random lobbies around your level, and watch fights while you wait for your turn. The way they do it here just breaks up the action too much. The fights do seem really laggy as well, and in a really inconsistant way. In a game like this where timing is really important, it's frustrating when the game jumps between half speed and full speed every few seconds in an online match.

In others you could run through the story of each character in about the same time as arcade mode. In this one there's just one huge story. It was real neat the first time, but i don't really want to go back to it. So I spend most of my time playing arcade mode while looking for a game. But if you happen to change your steam name or some other things you lose all your save data. So you lose any costumes you unlocked by playing, any high score data from arcade and time attack and your online ranking. It seems other things besides changing your name can trigger this as well, but I'm not sure what. It just makes it feel extra pointless.

I'll still jump in and play a few rounds from time to time, but all in all, this is a weak version of a weak installment in the series. Hopefully DOA6 will be better because doa4 and doau aren't even backwards compatable on the xbone at this point. If you do have an xbox 360 somewhere doa4 is real cheap rn tho, so give it a shot. I think it's a lot better than this game.\t\t\t\t\t
