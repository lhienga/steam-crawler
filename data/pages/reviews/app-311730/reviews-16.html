\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most compressed and elegant 3D fighters you can play.

Essentially, it is a simplified and faster paced version of games like Virtua Fighter and Tekken. You get 4 different actions: punch, kick, throw, and counter. All of them are connected with a rock-paper-scissors relationship, and this forms the basis of the mind games and the general flow of the decisions in a fight. Of course, newer players will feel more compelled to just attack relentlessly, and yes, this game gives you a lot of different chains and approaches for rushdowns that will be enough to get yourself through a match or two. But against a more experienced player, more careful consideration must be taken, because they'll know what your character can do and will counter it every time. Once you're up against better players, matches become very intense, and moment to moment decisions become excillarating.

The character design and variety is solid, but it doesn't quite match its contemporaries. There's a good amount of characters here that feel completely unecessary, or are simply uninteresting. The animations are overall great, but characters are lacking in features that truly make them stand out. There's no flashy special moves or really standout signature identifiers. You could be forgiven for mistaking Leon with Bayman. There simply aren't enough instances where the game can differentiate the characters' personalities and such. An example of this is intros/outros. They have to be the most bland I've seen in a game like this. The English VA is also not great at all (but you can just set it to Japanese if you care).

There is a story mode, and it's laughably ridiculous, as is the way of Fighting Games. If you're into that, you'll get a kick out of this nonsensical plot they've thrown together. It's actually the only mode where they can actually flesh some of the characters out, and honestly it's a lot better than what other fighting games do these days (which is forgo a story mode entirely). Really, story modes like these should never be taken for granted.

There are a lot of different challenges to beat and whatnot. This is a game that can be greatly enjoyed with a podcast on, as you grind out each of the character's alt costumes in arcade mode. Try it, it's zen-like. The fluid gameplay that never gets old is perfect for it.

Yes, there is a mountain of DLC for this game. I find there is little value in reviewing something like a swimsuit pack. You already know if you want it or not. Suffice to say, if you're into stuff like that, you'll never find a deeper hole to sink into than this.

Overall, a stunningly well made game with fluid fighting, really nice graphics, and deeper-than-you-might-think mechanics. Give it a go with the free version if you're unsure, but to any fighting game enthusiast it should be an easy decision.\t\t\t\t\t
