Now sit down you little pervert, I am going to tell you a great, well maybe not great..more like sad..story..

Way back before Dead or Alive 5 released on PC I remembered how much fun I had had playing it on the 360, so I checked the forums and read the store page description - online added in 3 months, looked like decent support from KT and Team Ninja would follow release, and they replied on the forums that they would not be bowing to the SJW crowd with their character models, which earned my respect and a pre-order. Then, it all went down hill faster than you could say, "but bouncy boobs!"

It all started with the initial slap in the face - PC gamers did not get all the stages that the consoles received, nor even all of the stage SFX(we still have the stage-outs, though..oh joy!), and then the backhand- the game's graphics are serviceable, but nothing like they could be, or should have been. This is a port of the arcade version, not the newer, shinier, better looking, with better graphics and features the consoles received(one could argue no jaggies here, but still..). This will never receive an update to put it on par with the superior console version because it's not running on the same engine - which includes damaged outfits, better skin shading etc., Fine, I could live with it. I had bouncy boobs and slick moves to keep me entertained.

Then comes the 3 month mark, and lo! the online was not released. Instead, it came to us in the form of a beta. It was functional, but barely - plagued by crashes, a lack of options for region selection(players could select a region, but only on a very broad, very basic level - thus players would be paired up with people from EU despite picking NA etc.,) no cross-platform and no lobby system. They kept pushing its official non-beta release back, and kept pushing it back until it was finally released a significant period of time AFTER the date stated on the store page - and it is basically the same as beta with slightly better matchmaking and fewer crashes, but still none of the other noted options. It looks like we will never get those options, either. Moreover, there are not many people playing online anyway. While we're mentioning updates - Did I mention the significant lack of any substantial updates in any way, shape, or form? Yeah, that's right. None.

Just to beat us over the head even further, they release loads of costume DLC at a cost, on average, on par with the price of the game or double it. Ok, they're just cosmetic and some of them include a hefty amount of costumes but again - those costumes do not feature any of the destructibility or additional SFX given to those who buy them on console. Not only that, but they keep vomiting out these DLC costume packs without actually updating the core game. We have barely received any of the balance updates et al., that the consoles have received. Not only that, but they're also releasing a DLC character for the PC and consoles and, you guessed it! PC doesn't get the stage she comes with, but consoles do. We also don't get any of the destructibility of her outfit.

Then, to pour salt into the gaping wound and kick us while we're down, the rest of the game is ACTUALLY FUN. It runs smoothly for me fully maxed out- on both a gtx780 and the replacement gtx980ti. The animation is fluid, looks nice, and attacks look like they genuinely flow into one another so it's fun to pull off a fancy combo. It sounds nice, the character voices are also decent, and the roster is quite large with each character feeling satisfyingly unique in fighting style and animation. In addition, there are neat water effects (wet clothing after a fight from sweat, or when a character falls in water) and it's nice to see dirt on a character's skin with a bead of sweat trickling down his/her head after a fight.

Even better, no matter how experienced someone is in the fighting game genre - anyone can pick it up and play it - I recommend a controller or a fight stick. I did not try it with keyboard. A new player can still witness the flashy moves resulting from his/her inputs and cool combos just as much as an experienced player - granted the experienced player can pull off more combos etc., because the skill ceiling, I believe, is fairly high and the combat system is decently complex.

There's also a single player story mode that's, well, there...The story isn't really much to pay attention to and the only purpose it serves is to get players used to some of the characters' combos and such.

Oh and did I mention they even added the ability to choose how bouncy the boobs are?
( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°) HOW. BOUNCY. THEY. ARE! ( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°) Bless our adolescent perverted little hearts.

So to conclude, if you're someone who's really desperate for a fighting game to play offline with friends and for an excuse to slobber all over your keyboard as you watch the heaving of your favorite DOA5 waifu's breasts - then I guess you could spend the $40. For anyone who wants a fun, competitive, well-supported fighting game with responsive developers who actually care about their PC community -- I beg you, please look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDead or Alive 5 Last Round is a superb game just like the variants before it, but putting aside the nickel and diming there's too much wrong with the PC version(performance not being an issue, game runs nicely). The PC version lacks stages from the console versions and the graphics aren't as good as the PS4 and XBone versions. Also, no Steam cloud.

My major gripe with with this version though is the button bindings. Sure you can bind fighting controls to whatever but non fighting controls are messed. I have a custom arcade stick that's dual modded with a Mad Catz 360 controller and a ChImp SMD. I can use my stick in either ChImp or 360 mode. If I use it in ChImp mode, I have to use the first button on the top(or 1P) as the confirm button, the first button on the bottom(or 1K) becomes cancel. With every other fighter, 1K and 2K are confirm and cancel.

It gets worse with an Xinput controller. The pause button isn't start, it's bound to the right stick(which you have to press down). That may not be too bad if you're using an Xinput pad but if you're using an Xinput arcade stick you don't have a pause button.

This could easily be fixed if Team Ninja were to make a patch that allows you to bind everything like Ultra Street Fighter IV on PC allows, but they won't do that. And because of that I don't recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was asked what my most disappointing purchase on Steam was and at first I said Project Cars; Project Cars looks amazing, but the AI is so god awful, it's hardly a driving simulator.

But Dead or Alive 5 takes the cake as the purchase I regret the most. Not only did I preorder this game as soon as I built this PC, but it was going to be my most played game; my catalyst back into the fighting game realm which I had strayed from for too long. The only other game I owned on Steam was Resident Evil Revelations 2 and it was in episodic release so the entire game wasn't even out yet. And I was really looking forward to playing ranked and actually trying to climb that ladder...... But oh how Team Ninja failed on this title. I won't even go into all the garbage that comes with this game; just check out all the other negative reviews on Steam or do an internet search on why this game is so terrible for PC as opposed to PS4.

And just when I thought this game couldn't get any worse, I bought a new monitor; shiny ASUS RoG 27" 1440p 144hz monitor. So I loaded this game; does not support 1440p. Not only does it not support 1440p, but it doesn't support 1200p and DOES support 4k. WTF? I think this has to be the only game in the world that offers 1080p and 4k resolution support, but not the resolutions between the two!

I absolutely love Dead or Alive the fighting game franchise. The gameplay in DoA5 itself is really good. The port is a slap in the face to PC owners everywhere.\t\t\t\t\t
