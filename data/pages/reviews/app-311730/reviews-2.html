\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun fighting game, considerably better than 6. More characters, more (and better) stages, more costumes, no intrusive meter mechanics and (arguably) better visuals, since everything in 6 is overly shiny, making characters look like they're made out of plastic.

This port is terrible though. Barebones graphics options that are on a separate launcher entirely, unstable framerate even on a powerful rig, missing content - since some got removed (Mai) and some never got ported to begin with, and absolutely abysmal online (delay-based with no lobbies, you can only fight people in ranked and obviously there's no crossplay).

I'd say it's still worth to get it on sale because *it is a good fighting game* and it goes for cheap. Get some friends on Parsec to run matches and you'll have a fun time. If you really want a half-decent online experience, go for DOA6 instead or get this game on PS4. As a sidenote, you can still get Mai on PC if you know where to look, so this version has that going for it I guess.

As a bit of a rant though, why would you waste your time playing this over any other 3D fighting game? Tekken 8 is around the corner, get that instead. DOA is a really sleek fighting game series but it gets treated like absolute garbage by it's companies, so it's hard to stick around. The pervy gacha game is still being updated while DOA6's support got dropped in less than a year, which is very telling of KT's priorities.

The community is very helpful, shoutouts to them. But it's also extremely small and split in every way possible so getting matches and learning is a PAIN.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDead or Alive 5 is my favorite fighting game of all time. I also had plenty of hours sunk into the Playstation 4 version before my console sadly stopped working; but Dead or Alive 5 is a game I don't mind paying for twice. I have a strong attachment to this game, I used to play it fairly often with a group of friends and still do from time to time, but enough of a love letter to the game, and let us get into what makes this game fun.

In an age where big-budget fighting games are just now trying to balance accessibility vs depth, Dead or Alive 5 had that balance mastered years ago. DOA5 builds upon the three button control format of Virtua Fighter in such a way that anyone can pick the game up and bash out a cool looking combo, but there is also a great amount of variety and creativity in crafting advanced, high damage combos. Another core feature of the game's combat system is the inclusion of holds. This feature sets the Dead or Alive series apart from its peers in the genre by making both offense and defense more engaging and tactical; you cannot just throw out your biggest plus on block attacks over and over and fish for a combo like in other games, the aggressor has to get creative with their offensive approach and not become predictable, otherwise they will lose very quickly due to having their attacks held repeatedly. The holds system, combined with the approach the game takes with making command inputs slightly simpler than most games gets you right into the action and decision making of what a fighting game should be, without all the pointlessly obtuse, pretzel-twisting directional inputs of other games that put off many potential players from the genre.

Dead or Alive 5 also boasts my favorite roster in fighting game history. I am a fan of Virtua Fighter and the old KOF games as well, so having Akira, Jacky, Sarah, Pai and Mai Shiranui as playable characters, along with newcomers Momiji and Rachel to this entry from Dead or Alive's sister series, Ninja Gaiden on top of all the fan favorite characters from Dead or Alive's already iconic and colorful cast makes this game's roster the best in the genre by miles. In addition to the gratuitous amount of characters included, each character feels unique in their design and play style, so there is a character for every style of player among the cast.

One other thing I really like about this entry into the Dead or Alive series in particular is that it is the last fighting game that really feels like an arcade game to me, and not some silly, pandering, eSports nonsense. If you are like me, and enjoy the old, arcade-y, 3D fighter vibe of games such as the Virtua Fighter series or the first four Soul Calibur games, this game has it in spades. Dead or Alive 5 also comes with a plethora of game modes as well, so anyone interested in single-player content will feel like they got their money's worth out of the game.

I highly recommend this game to fans of fighting games, arcade games or anyone who is looking for a video game with engaging combat and a truly unique cast of characters.\t\t\t\t\t
