\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, here goes...

Primary Complaints About The Game I see:
1.) DLC is expensive.
Here's the thing though:
- The DLC are costume sets, most of which are 'mashups' that are cooperations with other companies to make costumes from their characters and include them, the only DLC that would actually affect gameplay by incorporating a new character is 5.99 which is pretty cheap for a DLC Character.
- That and, at least it's nowhere near as expensive as Train Simulator 2016 DLC.
2.) Lobby System Is Missing For Online Multiplayer
- Very true, I believe they need to patch this, desperately. That being said, my friends and I have no problems finding eachother through Throwdown Matches while playing Arcade Mode in between.

My Other Complaints:
- Xbox One Controller Button Layout is a bit whacky because it reads as an Xbox 360 Controller, so weird stuff happens, like for Throwdowns (which if you're using keyboards this same prompt appears and doesn't give you the correct button to hit) it displays as hitting the back button to accept or send a throwdown request, however on the Xbox One Controller it's the Left Analog Stick you need to press. Other than that, the only weird thing I tend to find mildly annoying is that it reads my Triggers as ALWAYS OUT which, in post battle, shoves the camera in the top left corner.

With the exception of the Lobby System being missing though, these are minor, very minor complaints. I've been a fan of DOA since my cousins got me into it back with DOA2 for the PS2 and it is still an extremely solid fighting game to date. DOA5 is a good installment, the story mode isn't anything to write home about but if you're looking for a great fighting game with solid, responsive controlls, plenty of additional content including a load of costumes, characters, and rediculous combos to learn and attempt/fail, look no further.

I give DOA5 a 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

Story Mode is pretty beefy. Story itself isn't but meh.
The game itself is pretty good. The fighting is fast and frenetic. There are a variety of modes and the online mode is finally here. The bar of entry is actually friendlier than most other fighting games.
Game is still getting support.

Cons: Depiction of female characters pisses me off. I came here to scrap not to objectify women...-_-
DLC is an attrocious cash grab that is grossly overpriced.
Online community isn't nearly that active and doesn't have a host of online options that one would expect.
PS4 additions weren't carried over.

I can't recommend the game. Between the female character design and the overall handling of the game in regards to DLC and the online, only if you're a diehard fan or it's on sale...
