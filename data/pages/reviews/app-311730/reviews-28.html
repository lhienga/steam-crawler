\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not really a fan of fighting games. Only ones I played before this was Soul Calibur IV for PS3 and Killer Instinct for Super Nintendo. Actually loved both those games... But that being said I had a lot of fun with this game too. I read all the reviews before playing this and I knew online play was limited and I also knew the console version is somewhat superior. Softer looking skin and online play don't matter to me. I just want to play the damn game. No nonesense. And I got what I wanted. So if online play doesn't matter to you either, then please try this game!

The basic mechanics of a fighting game are all there. You get solo play, you get survival arenas where you fight enemy after enemy, time trials and team arenas.

The thing I liked is some of the stages are dynamic. You can be fighting in a quiet old looking apartment, blowing through walls and smashing tables and then bust through another wall onto the yard outside. Or throw your opponent off a tower and jump down to the street below. The changing settings is fast paced and doesn't break the flow of the fight at all. I also appreciate the amount of detail the settings have and the amount of it that can be destroyed. Destroying the environment doesn't appear to alter the stages that much, other than asthetically. Fires bursting from the walls or sparks flying, lots of flashy effects and busted pieces of metal and other things. The amount of environment damage gets pretty intense in prolonged fights.

You only get to choose 4 characters which is a disappointment but it is a free game afterall. The amount of content in the F2P version is incredible. Lots of people complaining about lack of content but I found there was a lot of content especially for a free game. You get all game modes except story mode, you get to choose all stages. All beautifully crafted and high detailed stages, I might add.

I'm not sure if other Dead or Alive games were like this but I had to snicker about the age difference in genders. Female characters appear to range from 12 to 18 years old. Meanwhile male characters look 30-60. I think there's maybe 2 female characters that could be around 25 at the oldest. I'm talking about appearances only though. The actual ages of the characters, as designed by the devs could be different. I'm not sure. I was also surprised to see quite a few free outfits for the characters you can choose. The skimpy outfits for the girls are rediculous but amusing.

I really liked the music in this game. I don't know what it is. I usually turn game music off and play my own music but... I didn't turn the music off for this game so I guess that speaks for itself. Also the sound design of everything else is great too like sound effects etc.

All in all, this is only after maybe 5 hours straight of playtime. The other hours played were afk. But I rather enjoyed this game. There is a crazy amount of DLC with a super price tag but I think you can largely just ignore it and purchase just the main game which you can find here: http://store.steampowered.com/sub/46886/ It's only CDN$ 43.99 which is CDN$ 1,067.48 cheaper than adding all DLC to cart (CDN$ 1,111.47).

Pros:
All game modes unlocked.
All stages unlocked.
Fully destructible environments.
Dynamic stages.
Excellently designed characters.
Clear and vibrant sound design.
Good music.
Decent options for desired game length and difficulty.
Fun and challenging fighting gameplay.
Fast reacting controls. No clunkiness, at least none that I could find.
Gamepad support. (I'm not sure why it says partial controller support, my gamepad works for everything. never needed mouse or keyboard once in the couple hours of gameplay)
Cute outfits.
Cons:
Way too much DLC comprising mostly of costumes. Costumes only you or people who've purchased the same DLC can see.
Character selection is lacking key features. (no ability to view your character from a different angle)
Character details don't seem to do anything? Had me confused for 10 minutes switching between A-D and seeing no changes. Edit: Changes the colour of the panties.
Neutral:
Story mode requires a DLC purchase (CDN$ 16.99).
Limited online features.
Missing graphical details from the console version.

The really bouncy breasts in this game made me laugh.

Overall, great content and excellent value for a free-to-play game. I recommend it. 10/10
