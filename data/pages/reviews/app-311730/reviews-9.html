\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love DOA since the first game on PSX, and this one is fantastic, fun as hell and technical.
Sure, the Steam port lacks lobbies and other minor things, and that's really shameful, but the gameplay is here, and it's insanely good.

To sum up DOA compared to other fighting games: you can hold (=parry) when you are hit (hitstun) Strikes beat throws, throws beat holds and holds beat strikes.
Holding requires to do the right one for the right move, it takes about guessing your opponent moves or knowing which ones comes next.
You can also bet on your opponent to try to hold, and grab them, it can do insane damage!

The pluses:
+ Accessible: Mash! Try to hold! It's already fun for starters.
+ Technical: Grounded combos are actual mixups, counter hits and characters' weights provide different juggles, fool them to do a critical burst or a hi counter throw, stages offer different possibilities, etc.
+ Lots of different characters: Ninjas, wrestlers, drunken master, little girl who spins... it also has the missing characters in DOA6.
+ Virtua Fighter guest characters!! Akira Yuki, the man who is a tier by himself, is here!
+ Tag Mode!! The missing feature in DOA6 is here, and it allows you to do insane combos! Plus you can play it with 4 players.
+ Great stages with lots of interactions, offering different possibilities and situations: throwing them into a prop extends the combo, some walls trigger an event that changes the stage, go through the roof, etc.
+ Situational moments where your position in the stage allows you better combos or different throws, etc.
+ Netcode is pretty OK for playing people in the same continent.
+ Beautiful characters (obviously)
+ TRY IT FOR FREE: it has 4 free characters, then you can buy another one you feel like playing.

Now the minuses:
- The Steam version lacks lobbies. You must look for your friend in ranked match and it sucks.
- Mai is no longer available, because of licensing. But you can still play her in DOA6.
- Stages look grim, missing the usual colorful standards of the series.
- Costumes DLCs are bought in expensive packs and not individually.
- 1 or 2 missing stages in this version.
- Controller support. If it doesn't work, try Big Picture and Steam controller options. Also the right stick doesn't work anymore... sad for taking pictures.
- No exploding clothes! Get the PS4 version instead!
- Sexiness can be... too much. At least there are "decent" base costumes.
