\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've enjoyed many fighting games in the past; Mortal Kombat was the first, Street Fighter, Tekken, Battle Arena Toshinden, Tobal No.1, Ehrgeiz, Soul Calibur, Bloody Roar, and my first DoA game was DoA 2 Hardcore, which, I can say DoA 5 is a great improvement since 2 (haven't played any in-between).

Graphics are great and so are the physics (you know what I'm talking about...). There is a wide variety of techniques for each character that take some time to master, given the battle situation, so it's hard to get bored, somewhat easy to get frustrated though. Music is pretty good, really just background noise for me, I'm more focused on the fight, but I'm sure it helps get me pumped.

The level hazards and destruction are what make this game twice as interesting for me. Since Bloody Roar you've been able to launch opponents through walls, and that was awesome for it's time, besides that it's usually just been a 'ring-out'; DoA takes it to a whole new level, forget walls, forget floors, how about hitting your opponent with a runaway train, or kicking them off a 100ft ledge? Yes please!

I see griping about 'how bad the port is' and, 'where's the lobby function?'. As for the 'bad port' issue, I don't know where this comes from, I have a USB adapter for a PS2 controller, and I can play just fine, it's only slightly confusing at first if you want to remap the buttons, but two minutes of trial and error for a permanent fix is fine by me. 'Bad port' just seems like a lazy complaint; am I missing a component to that issue? Let me know... As for the online play, it's not something I do, and I understand the need or want for people to be able to play with their friend a thousand miles away, sure, in today's society we should never not be connected, however I don't see the problem not only because I don't do online matches, but also because I grew up sitting next to my opponent, using two controllers, and that wasn't always available, so I learned to like playing solo.

There's so much hate for Honoka, why?? Yes she borrows moves from some characters, but if you're into fighting you should remember Bruce Lee once said, "Absorb what is useful, discard what is useless, and add what is specifically your own"; Honoka captures the essence of what Bruce Lee was.

BIGGEST CON: Why the hell do the costume packs cost more than real clothes?? Well, sometimes more, but you get the point... $40 for the base game is reasonable, but then $55 or even up to $75 for extra content? Nooo, the extras should really be no more than half the cost of the game, at most. When I scroll down and see, "$1,081.39 - Add all DLC to cart", I have a mini friggin heart attack, what the hell guys??\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid 3D fighter with silky smooth animations, and a wide variety of characters with unique fighting styles and balances between each of them. These things are however, crippled by a lack of actual gameplay updates which are overshadowed by the developer's task of developing numerous paid DLC , which are clearly taking priority over key features like graphical limitations and the lack of private lobbies.
The story of the game isn't much to celebrate, giving mediocre voice acting, and a setting which seems to jump from location to location with little to no transition. This can leave many players confused as to how the characters even got there, and why they have encountered each other in these places, a fine example of which being a transition from a city setting to a snowy tundra.
Each character's moves and appearance feels very flushed out, and the player can quickly find the one which suits their playstle. Though some characters obviously recieve more superficial attention than others, with the wrestling queen Tina boasting over twenty five costumes, and some other characters falling to a variety as low as six different outfits, some of which may look similar to one-another.
In short, the fighting is wonderful and fullfilling, though the soul may need more work.\t\t\t\t\t
