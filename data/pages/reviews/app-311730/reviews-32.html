\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFighting game for PC (Thumbs Up)
Port to PC (Several Thumbs Down)


Positives
- The game itself is polished in terms of fighting games.
- The majority of moves are easy to execute with a controller
- Graphics are decent.Stages are varied/provide a good sense of depth.
- Lots of costume choices (DLCs)
- Break away areas and damage spots are placed well
- The music score is good. It’s a fighting game!
- There is a online fighting

Negative
- The lack of a SOFT ENGINE makes the game look not as polished and defined as the consoles.
- The control setup is terribly limited
- Missing several stages
- Costume effects that are in the console are not a part of this (even the DLCs note this)
- Price of DLCs. They cost more than the game itself.
- The online fighting

The DLCs are probably one of the most frustrating thing about this game. They have done a decent job at keeping up with the console release dates however they are highly expensive and do not have the same features. How do you charge the same price and give the consoles swappable/destructable clothing and completely remove that feature on PC.

Not sure what direction the developers are trying to go with this game, but making it "Free to Play" Then selling the "Core Fighters" for $4 as DLC is horrible. Fortunately I purchased this long before they did this and it was on sale.

If you are looking for a fighting game for the PC, I might recommend this game if it and and the Core Fights were on a HUGE sale.

If you are looking for a good or faithful DOA, I would tell you to stay far far away.

I am happy that they finally gave the PC world a DOA, but very heartbroken that it was a travesty of what it could have been. And somehow they find new ways to make it even worse!\t\t\t\t\t

I can't speak much for the more competitive side, but I can say that it would take a very considerable amount of patience to master characters based soley on the sheer number of moves and possible combos. Online mode is not very active and I'm told has some issues as well, but when I tried it the game worked fine.

The story is pretty dumb, but was fun to play through.

There's a lot of fanservice with costumes you can't turn off so keep that in mind when playing the game around friends.

Finally, the game is near impossible to play with a keyboard and controller support is iffy, which you'd think would be the priority in a fighting game of all things but ended up being by far my biggest gripe with the game. I'm not sure why or how this happened but be warned that you may need to jump through some technical hurdles to get the controls to feel proper, and may not be able get the buttons perfectly mapped even if you have a controller.

All things considered this was worth the price (ignoring the ridiculous DLC). I don't play a lot of fighters but I'm glad I bought this one. The newer console versions of this game are definitely better than this port though, so I'd reccomend those first over this version.\t\t\t\t\t
