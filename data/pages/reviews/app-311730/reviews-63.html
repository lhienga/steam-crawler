\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want Dead or Alive 5 just as a fighting game, and can wait patientily and practice for the next 3 months for the release of the online mode, then this is definitely worth getting as it now stands as probably the best 3D fighting game on Steam. We have no Tekken (yet), SoulCalibur, or Virtua Fighter, so as it stands the competition is near non existent. That is unless you count games like Naruto, which while good, are not really what I would call true competitive fighting games.

Dead or Alive 5 as a 3D fighting game is more than competent. It's fast. It's flashy. It's just fun to play. Nothing wrong with that.

Sure, this being Team Ninjas 2nd game to be ported to Steam, they are still showing they have a LOT to learn. I mean as far as ports go, this one is far from perfect. But it is not as broken as some say. It is missing some stuff. But the core gameplay is at this stage is excellent.


What is missing?
The Danger Zone and Crimson stages which are PS4/XBOX One exclusive
The Soft Engine
Some PS4 particle effects
Online mode (coming in the next 3 months)
Support for keyboard configuration

The only 2 things in that list I find a bit unacceptable would be the lack of online, which IS being patched in, and the keyboard configuration. Hopefully the KB configuration will be patched in later. Though I find for a 3D fighting game, that a controller works much better. And this game has controller support. But the other 3 things? I'm not going to whine and say this game sucks because of the lack of those things, like some people are doing. Why? Because I actually have enjoyed the game so far. And is that not what matters?

As for the game itself, Dead or Alive 5 is like a much faster and flashier Tekken and Virtua Fighter. A game that focuses mainly on hand to hand combat. It includes a basic fight system that consists of a punch, kick, grapple, and reversal button. It allows movement in all directions, with a basic side to side 2D movement as the primary, and an easy sidestepping as the alternate. Each of the 30+ characters has a wide array of moves and combos, and varying styles of fighting as to not have too many "duplicates" among characters. One of the big features of the DoA series is the reversal button, in which if you time and aim it right can reverse almost any move. But timing is the key. So people who master this move will obviously have a major advantage. The nice thing is that DoA very easy to learn. But DoA is also hard to master. It's a game you can play both casually and competitively and have fun either way.

Stages are the next big draw for the DoA series, as DoA is one of the first 3D fighting game seires to include multi-tiered fighting levels with breakable walls, or other hazards that knock players into new parts of the stage. And it looks absolutely fantastic when doing this. Like starting the fight on top of a balcony and knocking someone over the edge into a courtyard below, or smashing someone through a wall into the next room, and more like this.

And of course, the absolute biggest draw (though some will be ashamed to admit it) for DoA is the costumes. And there are tons of them. If you did not preorder the game, you still get a massive assortment of costumes to select from for the 30+ characters. Yes, the costume DLC at this point is ridiculously overpriced, but I guess fans are going to pay what fans are going to pay. But none of it is required to get the most enjoyment out of the fighting portion of the game.

Anyways, this is not really a review, but just me rambling, and looking for a way to give a thumbs up to this game. If you want a solid fighting game and prefer to game on your PC, and can wait a littloe bit for the online mode, get this. If you would rather have the soft engine, some better particles, and 2 extra stages (which are not even that great), by all means give the PS4/XBOX One versions a shot.

I'll probably do an actual review for this once the online mode is up.\t\t\t\t\t
