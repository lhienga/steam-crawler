\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGET THE FREE VERSION FIRST! One major issue with this game is antiquated controller programming. So get the free version, make sure you can set the controller up right, THEN think about buying the game. Now, on to the review...

If you're looking for a deep fighting game to play off line every now and again, the game will deliver. Every character has a huge list of moves you can learn and practicing will definitely improve your game. The graphics are good enough. The game is perfect for stereoscopic (e.g. 3D Vision) users with the action taking place right at the range where eye triangulation works best and a small modification will push the interface out of the screen to prevent most of the depth crossing.

A lot of folks are complaining about overpriced DLC. What they are missing is that the DLC is totally optional. There's a couple of fighters (and those DLCs are actually reasonably priced) but the rest are just costumes. What's more, the modding scene for this game has provided a huge list of additional costumes you can have for free.

That said, there are plenty of valid issues with this game. The controllers are the biggest issue but you can now dodge that bullet by trying the game out before you pay. If you're looking to play with your friends over the network, look elsewhere. There's no lobby system in the PC version. It also has stability issues. The game saves enough that crashing isn't too painful but still....

So yeah - if you want to have some fun with a single player fighting game and would rather fight with buxom babes than watch somebody's head get ripped off and the blood drank, this is a good one. If you've got 3D Vision, it's a really good one!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLets clear this out of the way first: Yes. I do recommend the game for what it is. No I don't like what Koei have done with the PC version. The DLC prices are absurd as well.

I will clear the negative first: This is a port of a sub-optimal version of the game.
The online has been a mess for a long time.
The DLC is absurd.

Despite the negatives being very clear. The game itself is very fun and I have spent a crazy amount of time with it in total. I owned 2 versions (on Xbox 360) of this and I spent little time in snapping a third up even despite knowing it was a less than ideal version being ported over.

I am not a fighting game player, not at all. I am terrible at everything there is to do in fighting games, I cannot play Tekken or Street Fighter to save my life but with Dead or Alive, the design choices mean that you can get into it really easily but still see where the rest of the game is beneath. Another thing is just how much weight is behind everything you do in this game in comparison to other fighters. 2d fighters are obviously flat, Tekken you're treated to a puff of red smoke on hit but with this, a good hit feels like a good hit.

The character designs are great also, though its as comedic as you might expect from Team Ninja. Yes, there really is an option for boob physics. Stick it on OMG mode for a couple of rounds, laugh, then stick it on whatver the basic setting is and forget about it and focus on the gameplay. I have some screenshots up, you can add in shaders and fiddle around with them to make the game more vibrant than it was originally.

The long and short of this is:
It's a sub-par version of the game that's ported, though it runs well and you can add shaders if you want.
The DLC is stupid.
If you aren't fussed about this and actually want a really fun, easy to get into fighter on Steam that you can pick up and put down when you want, this is your best option in my opinion. Just don't expect much from the online.

It's well worth sale price.\t\t\t\t\t
