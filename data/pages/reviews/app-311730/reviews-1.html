~Some random wise hobo at the intersection!


I played the free version for DOA 5 awhile. It surprised me I enjoyed the core fighting as much as I did. It was easy to string combos, do tag attacks,and knock opponents through floors,out windows,and into oncoming obstacles thus hearkened back to playing "goldie oldies" like Mortal Kombat Deception and Tekken Tag! However be forewarned.....

There is no more Mai in Last Round. The licensing agreement ran out so if you want Mrs. Shiranui you'll have to buy DOA 6 and also by her as separate DLC. As much as I love Tecmo Koei let's be real,their nickel diming tactics are beyond egregious!

Yet when I can get most of a great fighter for 19.99 on sale I'm alright with that arrangement. I came here for "Dead Or Alive" so Mai's absence as a playable cameo is disappointing but not deal breaking. Especially because I knew beforehand what I was getting into. If you are unsure you can always play the "core fighters" free version like I did! Admittedly this is a good non intrusive way to advertise a fighting game. Let gamers dip their feet into the metaphorical sauna water. If they like it they can drop money on more content and if they don't they can walk away with their wallets,sanity,and savings intact!

Purchasing DOA 5 for me is akin to preserving something essential from the past. Something that unfortunately could not be so easily replicated today. I say that on account even though the game is dated it still is spectacular to behold. DOA 5 proves you can have believable 3D models which also look enticing. Aka, "ESG-Endorsed-Plain-Jane-Beauty-Drain" is not always an indicator of peak realism. Exceptional above average beautiful people actually exist in our world and obviously if someone is training to be an MMA fighter, yokai hunter,boxer,mercenary,or a ninja they are going to be at max physical prowess. Never mind the fact there is no point adding realism into a game that already has ridiculous super powers and outrageous lore. This whole argument is disingenuous hog-wash as best. Western developers need to realize in most cases no one appreciates bland,unremarkable,out of shape,or ugly looking avatars!

Frankly, I don't need to see myself in a game to enjoy it. Sure, I get somewhat envious when I look at Ryu Hayabusa but simultaneously I am fond of him as a heroic archetype. Ryu is an old friend whom has taken me on many thrilling adventures I otherwise would have missed out on. I could never hate him or hate what he represents. Ryu and positive role-models similar to him are what all men should strive to be like at least in part! (Same with Momiji representing the best traits of womanhood)

Pros

+The fighting itself is peak.

+The interactive partially destructible multi-leveled environments had a lot of thought and care put into them.

+Decent roster

+Tag mode

+Is a stunner graphics wise. The way fabric,hair,and bodies flow,bounce,and move is a sight to behold. Years later this physics engine still impresses!

+Decent amount of unlockables already in game.

+Likable characters

+Neat references alluding to Japanese folklore and mythology (Big boobie tengu momma!)

+From a more idyllic era in gaming when fiction and entertainment were allowed to promote beauty,motivation,and hope. Yeah,I miss those days too!

+Free version allowing you to get accustomed to the game's mechanics and fun factor before you decide to put money down on a purchase.

+Most of the VA work is decent despite the overarching story being a little confusing if you don't know about canonical events from previous installments. (Ayane in particular sounds like Heather Mason from Silent-Hill-3 and if its' the same voice actress that is awesome!)

+Virtua Fighter cross-over (Sarah Bryant,Akira,etc.)

+Hypothetically DOA and Ninja Gaiden sowed the seeds for future games like Senran Kagura and Oneechanbara and for that I'm very grateful!


Cons

-Mai got banished into license agreement purgatory!

-Additional character from Samurai Warriors is also "locked".

-Even if you buy the "complete game" there's still a ridiculous amount of side DLC. If those outfits were real you would need a walk in closet the size of a mansion to hold everything! Do you know those people whom have 1,000 pairs of shoes and 1,000 bathrobes? This is very similar!

-It's one thing to have dlc or expired characters but teasing all that in game is a new low. The fact we can still fight Mai yet cannot purchase her anymore shows the flaws in this type of marketing strategy. It's a pity such a potentially top tier franchise is held back by copious amounts of corporate greed. (And if you look at other more recent fighters you'll notice these same tactics were adapted by many other game studios as well. DOA 5 as much as I love it was "ground zero" for the current season pass/dlc characters/dlc outfits shenanigans you now see springing up everywhere within this niche. Might as well be cancer spreading in real time!)

-Competitive scene is probably on life support by this point. Doesn't effect me personally but if you buy fighters to square off against real human opponents you may want to "ask around" before you break out your credit card.\t\t\t\t\t
