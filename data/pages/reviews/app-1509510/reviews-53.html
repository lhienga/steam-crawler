\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, this game is incredibly deep and a thoroughly satisfying challenge. Absolutely the spiritual successor to Banished. I have a number of minor complaints, but I expect most of them to be polished off by release. The only major complaint I have is one I also had of Banished, which is the lack of any kind of diagonal placement. It's frustrating to have a long stretch of perfectly straight river that would be ideal for riverside development in an early settlement go to waste because the river flows southeast instead of due south. The same is true of mountains, which could accommodate large mine complexes but are oriented on the map's diagonal. I'd appreciate if the devs enabled at least a 45 degree placement or else biased the procedural map generation toward producing mostly N/S and E/W features. The game does have a terrain re-working ability that allows you to level and dig terrain, but it is (rightfully) behind a tech and, I imagine, resource/labor barrier that relegates it to the mid-late game, though I confess I haven't explored that bit yet.\t\t\t\t\t
