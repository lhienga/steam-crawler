\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe games good, but it needs some serious tweaking.

On the negative side:

Terra-forming is locked behind the tech tree, and once unlocked is severely lacking. you CANNOT re-create mountains, adjust terrain depth, or re-work water tiles less than 3-4 tiles at a time.

Once your city reaches late game it becomes near impossible to sustain at populations over 1,000. (My peak was 1.3k). The primary issue is "City Stress" which is built into the game and primary based on city size (AKA the larger your city the lower the desire to have children)

Bluprints take AGES to find. Most hardcore players "Scumsave" just prior to the merchant arriving. This can take as many as 100+ re-loads to acquire them all. I'm on year 150ish and still only have less than a quarter of the available blueprints without scumsaving.

On the more positive side:

There's a wide variety of crops, animals, and advanced processing available. All of the buildings can be individually upgraded (from within the building by itself or done by batches once you have the tech). This allows you to make the city more personalized. There's tons of behind the scene controls such as material banning, reserves, building locations, max productions, limited storage locations, etc.

Most of the buildings can be completely upgraded to a newer better version of the same building (separate from above upgrades), You can trade with your "neighbors", and receive new tech from it, it's possible to acquire new seeds / animals directly from your environment, there's a varied amount of random events, natural disasters can be turned off, and almost every non-food resource is used for a construction even if only limited late game contributions (this forces the player to either focus on producing trade exclusive items (higher value) or producing versatile trade goods that can be used within the city itself (more versatility).\t\t\t\t\t
