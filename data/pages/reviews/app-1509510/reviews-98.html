\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game with a lot of potential and even more bugs at the moment.

It's Early Access. Holds a ton of promise. Far more pleasing game than Banished or Ostriv and feels like a nice town-builder. Banished and Ostriv have always focused on the survival aspect and almost feel like they are from the dark ages in terms of aesthetics to me. The mechanisms of Settlement Survival are decent at the level which they are in Dec 2021. You could potentially get a game play of at least a couple of hours.

The bad part - bugs, loading issues, mods that break the gameplay. In my gameplay, with all mods turned off, game files validated (from Steam > game properties), workers get stuck near the original marketplace. As workers stop, work piles up. Nothing gets moved, repaired, built or produced. As a player, we have zero control over this situation that can occur at any time in the game. If you have been blessed with Cholera or another disease initially, the clinics operate sub-optimally. For some strange reason, the people who are infected decide to quarantine themselves next to the marketplace (same spot where workers are loitering). So now you have idle workers next to infected patients. The number of patient increases exponentially. You would build 5 clinics and the patients would still use one and that too at random. The doctor is idling away too while patients infect others. You take in immigrants to increase the population to keep your economy moving. Then again, those immigrants arrive at the exact same spot where you have idle workers and patients. Lo and behold, now you have more patients to deal with. After trying this game for hours, I have given up. Maybe circle back once this game gets out of early access.
The game is named so generically that it's impossible to find any FAQs or decent work-arounds for these bugs online. Someone commented that it's the problem in the search query. So you'd have to prefix 'game' to 'settlement survival' so you don't get COVID or other pandemic related results. Renaming this game would be helpful.
Overall, loved the concept. The execution is lacking. It would be great if the developers could help fix these nasty bugs that render the game useless rather than launching Christmas trees as an update. What fun is the Christmas update, when your population just dies because of bugs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put a little over 5 hours into this game, and so far i love it.

So, to start off, the game play is relaxing. you don't feel rushed for anything and it gives you a wide range of control for most of the towns creation and survival. The tutorial is helpful and is well worth the hour or so that it takes to learn how to play the game. (Although, i could understand if you want to just jump right in and try to learn everything by yourself. it is your town, after all.)

But... I do unfortunately have some issues and gripes with the game, and some of its technical bits. For instance, why is it that so many maps have large mountains and hills? It makes building a town a little difficult. And then the settlers and citizens... 9 times out of 10 they have a lot of issues going on. Seems a bit too much, but that's less of an issue and more of a personal gripe. But constantly getting pinged that several settlers are thirsty, or hungry, even though i have 8 large wells and well over 10,000 buffalo meat is a little annoying. And then to top it off, the animal pens... This made no sense to me, but even though i have multiple animal pens, i can have two small or even medium sized pens with the same animal. i tried looking through the help tab but found nothing that could help me "Separate" the animals.

But, to sum it all up. if you want a relaxing game that does not make you feel rushed and is (For the most part) Very informative, and will have you wondering what you could do next, then this is surly the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t
