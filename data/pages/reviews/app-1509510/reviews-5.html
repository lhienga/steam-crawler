It shouldn't be difficult to place a building in a city builder and it shouldn't be hard to remove a tooltip that shouldn't be in the base game at all. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I don't do a lot of reviews but I felt this one needed one. This is long so I apologize but I really am writing this for the developers, I hope they read it. City Builders/Survival/Management games are usually the games genre I play and it has been that way for years. I was playing when SimCity originally came out and spent hours/months/years playing that game. I've read a lot of the comments and I always find it funny when people who play this genre that within 10 to 20 hours they can have a solid impression of the game, well in my opinion that is just not possible with this genre. So for awareness, I have 110 hours into this game. What makes a game great in this genre is how game play is when you are deep into the experience and this is where so many of them fall apart. I know it's tough for small studio's and indie developers to have a really complete polished game because they need to start making money but please stay in early access until you do have a solid game play experience.


Now for the game, this game has good game play with potential, but I think they rushed to version 1. This is why I'm giving it a thumbs down. I'm not saying don't buy, but make your own judgement after reading. When you go GA, you are basically saying we feel we have a solid experience for you, sure you will have bugs and other annoyance but the major game play should be good to go and should be playable deep and I mean deep into the game play. Game balance and AI should be solid. If some of the key con's are fixed I would change my recommendation

Pro's
  • For a City Builder/Survival type game it has a very deep and rich resource/product processing system. I would compare the resource choices and product processing you get to experience on par to the Anno games.
  • The fact I don't have to go buy a tile or land or whatever, just let me build on the map in the beginning, which this game gives you the freedom.
  • Not my favorite style of graphics but they have done a good job to make this game look really good
  • I like the factions and the fact you can get architectural style building from them and some of their enhanced production abilities.
  • The tourism part is a nice little added bonus, it expands the game play giving you something else to manage that is different just ffrom managing your settlers. Now I do think more could be added and maybe this could be a DLC
  • Open ended game play. What I mean by this, is there is no scoring system, no I win system or anything like that. You just get to build until you are either tired of it or you used up all the land. This is city building, why do you need to win anything. Now that being said I think adding goals/objectives throughout the game would be a nice touch, again look at Anno
  • The logistics system is pretty solid
  • Techtree is nice but could use some small improvements, like how each tech can impact other techs.

Con's
  • People - wholly molly, this should not be that hard to manage. Example - I have 200 laborer's, so I either decide not to except immigrants or I end up missing a cycle or two of them and then all hell breaks lose. My labor force drops quickly because they have to fill vacant jobs from people biting the dust and then I run out of laborers and then I run out of workforce and worst of all it's random because you don't know who died and if the developers say look at the bottom left hand corner to get updates, that horse crap. The aging/working/education system is in bad shape. how can I go from 200 to 0 when I have over 100 people that are getting educated and should go into the work force. Where are they going, to a 20 year school, this creates you having more people dying then that are coming in the workforce. Now you have a massive shortage everywhere and you have manage it all. NOT FUN! You don't need to be educated to work and if you forcing everyone to wait to get educated, I'm just going to say OH WOW! And death waves - aka City Skylines 1, that was a huge issue for them in the early stages of release. To me this is the number one issue of the game!
  • Management is OK but could use improvements - not enough flexibility to manage things in a table. Also could use some better reporting. Game speed needs to be addressed - things are moving to slow and then you need to speed things up to make the game play reasonable. I should need to be on 5X or 10X to make things happen and what happened to 3X, 4X, 6X, 7X, 8X and 9X
  • Getting seeds is OK but gathers really don't find a whole lot so know you either need to buy them or trade for them. YUCK! If you want to do this then lower the cost - these are seeds not gold nuggets
  • Farming production is a little off - too much or too little, too difficult to manage.
  • Some of the production chains seem a little unequal and not in a good way. Why is it do hard to keep up with drinks, like coffee, tea, whiskey. I think all my people must be spending most of there days getting to much caffeine or getting wasted. And of all things Soap should not be that hard to keep around.
  • Animals - Not sure what is going here. You can either get animals through a hunter(which is rare and you can't find all the animals this way) or through trade (however, trade rarely offers you an animal). This is the hardest resource to get. I still don't have all the animals after 109 hours. I can't make milk because I'm guessing I don't have Cows but not sure because I don't know if this is one of the animals. I have cattle, but apparently they are not the milk providing kind. Getting animals should not be this hard in life! Why don't factions have animals to buy?
  • Improve the trading system. Factions are great, but please make trading with the normal trader real, not that I have to match dollar for dollar. Sometime traders come to your port to buy products that they need, not just sell
  • I would like to see the housing system be approved upon, more choices, easier upgrading. And when you start working with different factions how about getting housing in their architectural style. Again maybe a DLC
  • I'm not sure what you where thinking with the beautification items but just really odd choices. Come up with some better items - more trees, bushes, flowers, statues, etc. Make a DLC that has more realistic beautification items.
  • This one is not a huge deal but could we get some larger maps please!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is another game where I would like to put "neutral" as evaluation. Game is passably ok, but there are enough drawbacks to make it less fun than expected.
Not in particular order:
No endgame tasks. Just grow your population and take care of them.
Growth of settlement is quite slow, I almost always ran 10x speed and paused when needed. Immigration would happen once every few years, and especially at the beginning when growth of people is very slow, you just roll on time.
Seeds for new plants and also getting new animals is very slow and very random chance. Again, you can buy what you need but it costs buildings and after that, such purchases are hella expensive.
That leads to next issue. If you did not get animals that make leather or furs, you are stuck with your people all going nude.
There is a bit of lacking reporting, so it leads to sometimes overproduction of food.
There are very few iron and stone deposits open on the map, so you have to rush quarries.

I did like tech tree, but you need very many parts of it to make a successful colony.
So if you can enjoy "average Banished clone", this game may be for you. But it is overall average in so many aspects.\t\t\t\t\t
