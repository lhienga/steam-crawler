\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really chill game that is easy to sit back and get lost in for hours. If you like Banished, it's almost certain you'll like Settlement Survival, because the idea and the gameplay are very similar. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredibly solid settlement game, I went into this not quite sure what to expect and found myself enjoying every moment as I grew out my town.

The art style is simple and consistent, each individual building is fairly plain but some of the larger, later game, structures are all the more impressive next to the simplicity of the basic structures. Once your settlement starts growing the town as a whole looks beautiful, The little extra visual flourishes from decorations and the fact a lot of upgrades are visually represented show time and care went into presentation.

Tech plays a large part in how you progress, both how you generate tech points and what you unlock can vary from game to game. Every tech feels like an important pickup and there is rarely a 'correct' or obvious option to go for first. What you unlock will shape how your settlement grows even if eventually you can unlock it all.

Trade is a massive part of the game, there are unique upgrades only available from trade, access to certain plants and animals is locked behind trade and each trade partner offers unique techs and upgrades for trading with them. This adds a lot of choice and options, something this game excels at with both this mechanic and the research tree helping each run feel very different.

The game does feature disasters but despite the survival in the name I've rarely had them be more than an annoyance, unless you really crank the difficulty it is not difficult to survive. However thriving and growing can be difficult and growing too fast is likely to be the cause of most of your failures, slow and steady is the name of the game. If you go in expecting something like Frostpunk where you're fighting for every inch you will be disappointed, instead think of it more like the classic Pharaoh where you build up slowly and steadily, managing increasingly complex production chains to keep your people happy.

There's a lot of depth and choice in the resources you gather and what you do with them. How you generate food, what you trade and with who. The basic mechanics are simple enough to grasp that the game is easy to play but the complexity emerging from the choices make it difficult to master. I heartily recommend this to anyone looking for a relaxing settlement builder that nonetheless can make you use your brain.\t\t\t\t\t
