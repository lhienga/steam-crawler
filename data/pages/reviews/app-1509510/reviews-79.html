\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is everything I wanted Banished to be, and then some. When I played Banished, I enjoyed the game but was endlessly frustrated with the bad villager AI and the limited options for growth and recovery from disaster. Villagers would die suddenly in "accidents" in one of the mines and I would entertain myself by assuming that one of the other miners was the murderer all along.

Enter Settlement Survival - where murderers are an actual mechanic, the AI isn't broken, and disaster recovery comes in a few different forms that don't involve scrapping the entire game and starting over again. Even in an early access stage, Settlement Survival feels more complete and more satisfying than Banished ever was, and even has tentatively added new elements like magic to the mix. I particularly enjoy the factions that have been added, where you must build up a level of reputation with each faction to have access to their high end goods. After all, you wouldn't give Random Joe #37 all your secrets for mining gemstones and obsidian just for the asking, right?\t\t\t\t\t

But, overall, the multitude of grammar errors notwithstanding, the game is progressing well. The Tech Tree: I would point out that the alignment of some of the techs are improperly placed. The glaring example is Herb Knowledge. This is placed after the Nursery tech which makes it pretty much obsolete and tech points used on it are wasted. It seems to me that the correct order of things is to have Herb seeds THEN figure out they grow best in a carefully tended Nursery.
The Pottery branch would be more appropriately placed in the Processing section.
Education needs more. For example, a tech which unlocks “College” or, even better, “University” which should be required to be built before you can build a Research Institute. Additionally, I believe the tech “Alloys” should be made available in the Education tree and be required before one can unlock the Smelter tech. Correspondingly, Remodel Trap and Remodel Gear should be combined.
Transfer Stations and Supply Stations (Supply Stations would be more appropriately named Supply Depots) should be customizable and used to transfer materiel to Trading Posts and Docks. There is really no need for them to be used for filling up warehouses and stock yards as the villagers already do this automatically. This will allow one to stockpile only items to be used for trade in the Supply Depots and will help make it easier for workers to locate the goods. Maybe require the tech Quick Preparation as a prerequisite for the tech Transfer Station?
The Faction System: This great addition to the game adds a level of unexpected difficulty especially with population management. The diversity it adds to the game is also magnificent. Having the ability to take ideas from other parts of the world and utilize them in the city can enhance the game greatly as well as shore up areas of weakness.
I also believe reputation should be improved upon. Maybe Carlos doesn’t like you because you put up a Mosque in your town instead of Templo Mayor. This may cause him to want to charge you more for goods. Maybe Rania likes you because you put up a Mosque in your town and in a show of friendship, she charges you a little less for purchases or is willing to pay a little more for your goods.
Also, it should be remembered that a person like Rania would NOT purchase pork or pork products. These items should be removed from the items list for her. Carlos, given the abundance of corn/maize in his area, would not purchase corn-based items or, at the very least, would not be willing to pay nearly as much for them as would the others. These kinds of nuances change the trading strategies significantly, and thus, the reputation as well. Reputations work in two directions. They go up and they go down. This is not currently reflected in the game where reputation only increases.
Now, I do have one major beef with the tools. Since when are Obsidian tools stronger and longer lasting than Iron or Steel tools? Obsidian is very weak by comparison. While it can be significantly sharper, it is also significantly weaker and less durable than iron or steel. And what about Bronze? It is also far stronger than Obsidian but seems to have no place in this game. Personally, I would get rid of the Obsidian and replace it with Bronze. Of course, this means you need a Tin Mine and a Copper Mine, but that shouldn’t be too hard to accomplish. It also allows you more options at the Smelter.
I have a gripe about the Dock. Why MUST I purchase something when the Merchant Ship arrives in order to sell my stockpiled items? Why can’t I just sell the items for Silver Coins and NOT buy something from them? Often the ship arrives without items I need or want. It would certainly help with keeping stock lower. I cannot tell you how many times I have had to build, yet another, storage facility because all my storage facilities are full even after remodeling.
Remodeling: What a wonderful feature! I love the idea of Remodeling a building in order to improve on some statistic or another. And, the fact that it is kept separate from Upgrading is one of the inspired nuances that makes this such a great game. Add to it that some items needed for Remodeling can only be obtained through Trade, adds a whole new level of strategy to the game.
Extras that make this game unique: There are a few things about this game that, though not all are unique, are cute nuances that keep the game from becoming boring or drudging. Tears, used for the construction of certain statuary and magical items, are one example of this. Also, the “mystery” items that often float down the river are a fun extra. So is the travelling merchant. But, the thing that has impressed me the most is the way the game looks during “night” time. This is where the game truly takes on its own persona. Other games have “night” mode but honestly, they are usually annoying and I almost always opt out of using it. With this game, though, the developers went to extraordinary lengths to make “night” time as pleasurable to view and experience as day. I have found myself changing how I design my towns in order to ensure I can build lanterns, lamps and other “night” features.
I mentioned earlier that it does not have as many resource management chains and those it does have could use a little refinement to make some items more difficult to manufacture and some a little easier. Corn, for example, can be used for corn flour which can make cornbread, tortillas and such. And, why do these games NEVER include Rabbit? Rabbit was a necessary staple during the era in which this game takes place. It provided meat and fur, though it can’t be used to obtain fat as rabbits have almost no body fat. And, you can use the feet at the jewelry shop to make lucky rabbit’s foot charms. The tails are very popular for making fishing lures even to this very day.
I know that I have mentioned a lot, and of course, it isn’t MY game and I am not involved with its development. After over 300 hours of playing this game, I have to say I not only recommend this game, I am also looking forward with great anticipation to its future development. I can see myself playing this game well into the future while in my rocking chair my legs covered with a blanket while my nurse complains that I need to be taking my afternoon nap.
I can also see sequels and a lot of DLC in the future for this game. I think you have hit on a gold mine here.
