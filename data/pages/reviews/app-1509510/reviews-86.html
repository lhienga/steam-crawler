\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think the game is fun and beautiful, but some minor tweaks are needed to cross into the next level.

The micro management is significant, especially for a larger towns. Want to build out your city while lumberjacks bring in the lumber? Nope, they over-harvested and now you have to stop them from felling trees while they plant only. Want to have 10 different animals and let the butcher harvest what they can? Nah, gotta have 10 different butchers only processing that exact type of meat.

The game forces specialization and does not feel like a "survival" game, where a small piece is part of a big picture. Instead, there are a ton of small pieces that need processed into other small pieces. Why wouldn't the butcher just process the meat that is available? Instead, you have to build out enough for every animal you are harvesting (and getting from hunters) as each type is a unique item. Butcher runs out of buffalo meat? Welp, the guy is just going to sit on his rump until he gets buffalo meat, regardless if there is an abundance of alpaca meat or not.

This is just an example of what happens in this game to force multiple buildings due to requiring specialization. It occurs for the sawmill, weaver, blacksmith, etc..

I really hope the devs take a look at how they are requiring specialization of each item and instead let there be options such as "process meat" and then it will work through what is available, regardless if it is buffalo, alpaca, chicken, turkey, fish, etc.. Let it auto-pilot a bit so other fun items can be accomplished (other fires to put out, realistically). If a tailor wants to make rough clothes, wouldn't they use what is available instead of requiring someone to tell them "use leather!"?\t\t\t\t\t

Exactly the same, it took me 20 years to solve this issue (mod didn't work) as there were not enough people all the time and i was dependant on illlegal immigration. However, it's more forgiving - there's no death marathon anymore (i call Banished "Dead Til Summer"). Hard to fail, hard to progress early.

Game is stable, with very few gimmicks. Well done and it's Early Access! So i'd say if you buy this instead of Banished, the value you get for the current price is indeed very good.

What I miss is CAMPAIGN or SHORT TASKS. that would increase game value dramatically. I don't want to stop playing once I finish the tech tree. That's why I played Banished only once - there was nothing to do anymore!

update:so i've finished the tech tree in 83 "years" of playing, halfa billion in cash. Playtime is 30hours, exactly like Banished which required 2 attempts tho (unplayable without mods). I never played it anymore because that was it. Now the good news is in SS there are already more themes and more will come. The game is ultra stable with very few minor bugs and defly worth of almost half price of Banished. But again, i exceptionally can afford spend so much time on single mission(!!), i would welcome short campaign and conquests and no other game than Train Valley understands it. Why every building game must take days and have no endgoal is puzzling!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first time I've been hooked on a city builder for longer than a few hours purely because there is so much to do! And right off the bat, I will say that if you aren't into micro-managing, this game isn't for you.


Managing Buildings and Resources:
Because there is so much to do, it does get a bit tedious when you have a lot of people and therefore a lot of buildings. Then somehow jobs keep going unfilled because there isn't enough workers to fill the spots, but honestly for me, that's the type of thing I can't help but try and solve and then end up spending way more time playing the game than I initially intended.

There are some things that I wish were more streamlined (such as new workers automatically filling the first slot if there is no worker at all instead of all going to the same place).

Seeds will be tedious to get and can be a bit frustrating because some which are necessary for advancing won't show up at all. To help solve this a bit, I think immigrants should have a chance of carrying seeds (a very low chance, but a chance nonetheless).


Navigation, Menus, etc:
There are some things that might be a bit frustrating to some people (like not knowing what type of material an item is (especially if the item is a seed or a plant) or knowing the actual population without having to go through a menu), but nothing like that is game breaking (for me), just a bit frustrating. The info menus are easy to open and close with a press of a button, and the game gives you the common options to navigate around the map.

Some of the building menus can get really cluttered when you research new buildings and then can be easily lost either because of the lack of separation between different types (example: cabins, water, mining which are under the tree icon) or because the "new" label disappears into the background the building previews. Also, the buildings will run off the screen and then be covered with the navigation arrow as the menu will turn into a scroll one. Again, for early access, this isn't really game breaking (for me), just a bit frustrating.


Overall really great game! The only thing that truly frustrated me was trying to find the Bedding Shop and then realising that I didn't have cotton.\t\t\t\t\t
