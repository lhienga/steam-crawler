\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThree major issues and a couple minor quibbles.

Major Issues:
1)\tThis game should have a content warning for su!c!de. There is a (as far as I can tell) random event that will kill one of your townsfolk with a pop-up informing you that they (paraphrasing) 'got depressed and committed su!c!de'.

2)\tThere are few, if any, accessibility options. No colorblind modes, no font options, no font scaling. It is, of course, possible options might be added in the future. However, if accessibility isn't included from the beginning, it's much harder to implement.

3)\tThe team released an update on January 15 that included a 'joke' about a woman developer needing to work overtime. From that update:

“You may ask whether cactus normally grows in the desert? And currently there isn’t a desert terrain on
map...
Well, that won’t be a problem for our art designer if she could do some OT work...（Wait, put down the
knife! Please! Someone! Stop her!!!)”

There is so much wrongness packed into those few lines, it’s almost stunning.

Minor quibbles:
Periodically a saucer-type UFO flies over the settlement. It feels very out-of-place in an otherwise medieval city-builder.
At least one of the events added in the Spring Festival Update includes outright supernatural elements. Not quite as jarring as the above but might be off-putting for those desiring a pure historic setting.

Otherwise, this seems a fairly standard medieval city-builder. The team is releasing updates at a good pace - which include both bug fixes and new content. I didn’t encounter any game-breaking bugs and it ran well on my good-but-old hardware.
