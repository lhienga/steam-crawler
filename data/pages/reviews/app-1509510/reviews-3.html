\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this genre of came— and complex economy management things are fun.

The biggest miss for me with this game is the dependency on randomness to make or break a game.

For example, clothes (at least early/mid game) tend to be fairly difficult to acquire. In a lot of games they are a major bottleneck. However— If you get one of a handful of seeds that make clothes easy, its almost a non issue. But if you don't get one of the specific ones, the game will be 2-3x harder.

In late game, you can easily buy the right seeds, for sure— But having the balance of the game so dramatically shifted by the completely random "Did you start with this seed or find it early" just makes the game unfun.

If you want to win at the harder difficulties, it becomes about fishing for the right starting conditions instead of just trying to play excellently to succeed.

I like some randomness, and its fun to have things that are random give minor buffs/challenges— but when a random event completely changes the difficulty level this dramatically, it makes it unfun.\t\t\t\t\t
