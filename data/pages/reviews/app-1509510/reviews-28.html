\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's okay for its price. There's a lot of complexity in the game that can keep you busy for hours, but it feels like sometimes it's complexity just for the sake of having more complexity.
I found that after a couple hours of playing, my activities mostly came down to securing enough food and housing, then slowly start implementing the latest tech I unlocked. The "wins" in this game are mostly based on having enough resources or being able to put one less person on a job because you upgraded to a more advanced version of a workshop.
It is a pretty enough game, which is good because the other key motivator is to watch over your settlement and kind of enjoy seeing how far you've come.
What I was kind of missing were some more memorable events. There's random events in the game, but they repeat often, and are usually some binary choice that might require some resource for one or the other option, and then a buff or resources, or the opposite of those, as an outcome. Besides those benefits, it's done and dealt with the moment the initial dialogue closes after picking your option. Would've liked to see a bit more, like how my choice actually plays out (instead of it being implied by the text or resource gains), or choices that lead to other events in the future.
I suspect the developer is a non native english speaker, because some of the grammar is a bit off, here and there. That's fine, it's still understandable, but it does irk me a little bit.
Finally, as mentioned at the start, the price of the game makes up a lot for its faults. It's cheap enough that I won't expect a perfect game. But there's plenty of room for improvements.\t\t\t\t\t
