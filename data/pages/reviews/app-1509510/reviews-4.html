Feels like an open-source game with a price tag

Did not get to the proper gameplay because the game feels clunky and not user-friendly to me.
I played all tutorials and started a new game. What was not good in my opinion:
- music is kind of boring and repetitive, had to turn it off and use Spotify in the background
- building sounds on click feel unnatural, their volume lvl is way to high and the sound itself is weird (several loud knocks for "builders" building gave me an impression of simply putting a random asset there without bothering much)
- map sounds, again are somewhat unbalanced to me, I don't feel present in one world, I feel like just someone turned on 10 different sound effects without bothering much
- user interface is too cluttered and small with a lot of text, so that you have to constantly strain your eyes and brain to get the complete understanding of it (makes sense to some extent, but with such effort I can learn a new programming language instead of learning to play this game)
- on the contrary, descriptions of buildings miss the main point, why to build it at all - e.g. there was a building that lets your create tools, but you don't see why you need those tools at all - having that stated in the description would make sense
- management is confusing, when started playing I could barely understand how many people I have, what they do, do I have any free labor, do I have enough food production, water production, what else I have to focus on etc.

From the good things I could highlight simplified but pleasant graphics.

Comparing to Frostpunk or Cities Skylines this game is a let down.
Sorry people who liked it, maybe it's just not for me.\t\t\t\t\t
