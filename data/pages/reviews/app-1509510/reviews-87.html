\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't expect to sink three days of my life into this game in one go - but here we are. It's a great town management sim and I can't wait to see where the devs take it next. I'm happy to see the trade system is getting an overhaul soon as it's very clunky right now. The tech tree can sometimes be a little confusing when trying to work out which tech is the best one to unlock next. Switching between the different tabs on the tech tree to work out what you need to unlock next to get to the tech that you actually need. I have seen some people saying that the tutorial is confusing, however I didn't find it so. If you have ever played Tropico it will feel familiar once you have the basics down.


I am having one issue however with murders. It seems that no matter how high I can get the population happiness after it dips, the murders will still occur at a high rate, and continue to do so. Are these murders already programmed in once the happiness dips? It seems that no matter how much you can recover the happiness it doesn't affect the murder rate until quite some time after (we are talking in-game years).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
*super, super, super micromanagey, which I'm into
*visually appealing
*tech tree
*excellent implication of logistics
*solid trade system, economics
*varied disasters
*challenging
*long play time
*yuuuuge diversity in resources, materials, and buildings
*the tech tree allows you to either specialize your settlement or be more broad-based

Cons:
*despite being micromanagey, the game does not allow individual directions to be given
*similar vein as above, you cannot queue production/tasks. If you have a bunch of buildings set to go up, you can prioritize one, but can't set an order for them to be done in
*mid and late game building and resource management is cumbersome as there is no single menu that lists every building, who's working there, etc
*immigration rate needs a buff, as child maturation/lifespan makes early game tougher than it needs to be
*you can only "research" one tech at a time
*no search function
*happiness function is a little wonky
*map seed process is vague

Neutral:
*no war/bandits/conflict/wild animal attacks

I'd recommend it, but if the devs read these, then I would also recommend a central hub/UI, as mid and late game it gets very difficult to keep track of which buildings aren't employing at full capacity, which ones are where, and which buildings are going to be build in what order.\t\t\t\t\t
