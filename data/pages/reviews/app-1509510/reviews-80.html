\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a straight up Banished clone.

As someone who loved Banished and played a lot of it, I see this as a good thing. The new game engine is obviously the biggest selling point here, as there is no signs of any slowdown even as your town grows to a large size.

The introduction of tech trees and 'research' is an interesting addition, and adds some variety to the gameplay. However I find it also gatekeeps progress a little bit, which can be a little frustrating at points.

Some parts of the game have good depth, others require more depth.

Overall, if you liked Banished (the vanilla version without mods), you'll like this game. Keen to see what sort of mods + additions get made to Settlement Survival to continue to flesh it out.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not that bad of game and I enjoyed my first few hours of play. It is similar to Banished, but I also got Anno 1404 vibes from it, and you could consider Songs of Syx a distant cousin to it. Ultimately, I'd rather play those games at this point than return to Settlement Survival.

One thing that irks me is the constant updates from the developer. Too much of a good thing is bad! Every day there seems to be a small patch with some petty little bugfix. It's annoying to constantly see Update Queued in my library. Roll these bugfixes into a weekly or monthly patch, or bundle them with feature releases.

The game is full of weird design decisions, bad interfaces, odd behaviour, and poor English translations. Having one or two of these issues isn't bad on it's own, but it really starts to build up and the game feels amateur. Pardon my laundry list of complaints, but here goes; Boats will travel down the river and go straight through your bridges as if they were never there. Citizens will call out in a speech bubble "It's the lunar new year" all the time, but you can't do anything about it, it's just a random callout that busies the screen constantly. You'll get gifted fireworks, but I haven't found a way to use them, so they clog up my warehouse. Tons of stuff will clog up your storage areas/warehouses and you can't dump them or get rid of it in any way.

The tech tree is really hard to read because the names of the techs scroll/marquee too fast and you can never read it (who thought of this? It's hilariously bad design!). The tech tree in general doesn't make any sense, you can build a fishing port and start fishing, but you don't need to research any of the fishing techs like nets or bait. But if you build a Clinic, you can't treat anything until you research like 5 different techs.

There's a HUGE tree that starts growing in your settlement as part of a "quest" and it takes up the whole screen, you can't play when this tree is growing. When you click on it, it presents you with the option to cut it down, or move it. If you cut it down, your whole settlement gets punished with a percentage decrease on lumber production efficiency. Pardon me for not wanting a giant ugly tree that completely blocks me from playing the game...

The sound effects are really amateur, they weren't mixed properly at all. Do not play with the volume turned up, certain sound effects (like clicking on the Reservoir building) seems to blow out the mids and lows on my headphones and it's really uncomfortable.

Honestly, I could go on. The game is just amateur in all respects. Even if they fixed all of that, the game has SO MANY resources to manage, you will get stuck just clicking around buildings trying to load your head with all these numbers to figure out what you need to balance/build next. The user interface doesn't present you much information about your settlement so you have to engage in this constant tedium to keep everything moving forward.\t\t\t\t\t
