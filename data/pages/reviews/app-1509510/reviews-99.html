\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteam asked if I would recommend this to other players, and as it stands, I'd say not yet. I still have to wait and see what the full release will be like.

I think the main thing I don't like is that the UI is a little sloppy. Certain text bubbles and status panes are always on top, and it gets in the way of text. You can't move the panes, so you have to move the map around to be able to read. I also hope they eventually hire a tech writer to rewrite the text because some of this is really hard to understand.

Housing around the marketplace is a cool idea early game, but it should have an upgrade that gives it double or triple radius at some point. All of my towns are starting to look the same since no matter how high your population gets, you can only place housing in a 32-ish block radius, and if you want to build out, you just start another clone of that in a new 32 block radius. I know there's a perk that gives you a 20% expansion, but it's still not enough to space out my maps and kind of build how I want, even if it's less efficient.

I also tried the Easter Island scenario, which I thought was cool, but there was no ferry. I think it was intended to have one though because the description says sea trade is slower, and there are trade perks for the ferry and sea trade.

That being said, I've been binging on it lately as a replacement to Banished. I don't think it's a bad game, and I like the upgrades and buildings. It's just not something I would tell someone to go buy just yet.\t\t\t\t\t

Some notes for the devs:
- I like to micromanage my resources so implementing an "allow" or "dis-allow" function on certain buildings
e.g. defining which fuel resource a home can use. I don't want the people taking my coal. It could be the same as the Storable Items list you have on the Warehouse. maybe also limiting how much someone can take so that we don't have one hoarder taking all the resources.

That's all I can think of at the moment :)
