\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought I was going to dislike this game due to the initial difficulty wall you hit really early on. I definitely quit more than a handful of times in frustration, assuming the game to be too unballanced.

Then I kept going with the tips I learned from restarting a few times. This game has a really interesting way of constantly hitting you with new challenges, skills, and resources to manage. I thought after a while that I'd hit that inevitable point of stability that all these games have. Where you basically can't lose due to having a strong enough foundation.

I was wrong.

I'm not going to say it's perfectly balanced, or paced, but it's fascinating how often you can feel secure, only to have that security be short lived and another new threat pops up. There's always a way to deal with it, but it's not always obvious.

If you like city builders, but get frustrated by how relaxed and simple the genre tends to be, then this is certainly worth trying. It's definitely going to cause it's own frustrations, but if you take it slow and build only what you need, you might end up liking this a lot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike others have said, it is very similar to Banished but with a few extra features like the skill tree and events. Also it is still being developed which is great.
I have been enjoying it quite a bit.
Some people were complaining about the skill tree moving a bit slow, and I can see where they are coming from. When I first started playing it I was getting a bit frustrated with how slow the tree was progressing. I had a wait a while for points to become available so I can unlock certain important buildings. That can sometimes really slow down the game play.
However, I did figure out a few things to do to make it go quicker.
1. Utilize the Forward button. Speeding it up x5 during stable times really helped move things along.
2. Unlocking the education skills and research institute allowed for faster progress.

So while I understand the complaints and I do agree there is room for improvement, it's really matter of being patient and not trying to just rush through the game all the time. Slow down and enjoy the city that is unfolding in front of you. It can be relaxing.\t\t\t\t\t
