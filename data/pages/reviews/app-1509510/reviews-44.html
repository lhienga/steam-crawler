I will say off the bat this game is feels very familiar as a game already out: "Kingdoms and Castles". Like it even has some of the same items and actions with the same names, to the point where I thought the company had just remade their game.

Wells: to provide water and put out fires
Forester's Huts: Produce woods
Orchards
Pastures
Marketplaces: provides closer resource location
Storage yard
Cemetery
Chapel
Clinic
Repair Shop

Like I understand that it is the same genre of game so it will be similar, but it just immediately put me off.

That said, it does improve on several areas compared to K & C. First, graphics are definitely better, with more visual aspects than just basic resources.

Second, there appears to be more progression and varied items in terms of resources.

It is also nice to have some builds at start for the difficulty level.

Cons:
When I try to tab out in border-less windowed mode, it really doesn't work nicely.

I will continue to write more as I go.


2 Hours In:

So I do enjoy the graphics quite well, and the item models are nice to look at.
I still and pretty early-game so I can't say for certain how it all meshes but I am enjoying it.

The tech tree is quite interesting, given research you are able to unlock technology and traits to further develop your settlement, from mining to trading to farming. I do wish there was a bit more detail on how some techs need others from different tech fields (agriculture, mining, processing, etc). Perhaps a tech tree would be better than tabs.

It is a bit frustrating when I try to destroy a building and I can't just click a quick button on it to deconstruct instead I have to go click the deconstruct tool from one of the bottom screen tabs then select it, but thats a minor gripe.

I also enjoy the nighttime lighting, I enjoy seeing my settlement lit up in the darkness compared to the rest of the map.\t\t\t\t\t
