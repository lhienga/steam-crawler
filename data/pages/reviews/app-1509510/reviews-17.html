\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally well done. Feels like a modern version of Banished, with some significant improvements.
  • Tech tree is a huge win, allowing for more indepth gameplay without feeling overwhelming.
  • Being able to get more seeds and animals naturally, while still needing to trade for all of them also feels great.
  • Caravan trading instead of just waiting for the ships was a fantastic improvement, which allowed for targetted (and efficient) resource purchasing/selling, while having to weight the citizen commitment and risk.
  • Being able to terraform was a really nice improvement.
  • Having an upgrade to infinite quarries/mines felt great, and their output feels much better than Banished.
  • The time feels loads better than banished. It can take some time for things to get done, but at 10x speed it feels like stuff is actually moving. I found myself pausing or reducing the passage of time while I planned out more infrastructure way more often than waiting for something to happens.

There are a few minor detractors, but overall they don't diminish the gameplay experience, just nitpicks.
  • Default map feels small, could only really have 3-5 housing hubs. The larger version is a significant improvement, but starting over after investing so much time in my initial settlement feels bad.
  • Some of the common citizen resources (soap, medical powder) don't properly track consumption, so it can be hard to understand what's happening.
  • There really needs to be some way to track aggregate population clothing/eating/shoes etc, you can go through each citizen, but it's hard to identify that 10% of your population is still using basic clothes you're keeping around as backup.
  • Also can be difficult to identify why your home stress reductions is like it is. Full population with garden villas and sheyuans (-25% stress), with puppies(-15% stress) and cats(-5% stress), but my average stress reduction was -27% instead of -45%.
  • The marketplaces could be handled better, even with max employees, wouldn't get over 40% capacity, and was storing items villagers don't need.
  • Being able to level mountains, fill in lakes and rivers, and dig new rivers, but not be able to remove old quarries or mines felt wrong. Also wasn't great that you couldn't add mountains, since some buildings require them.
