\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I started playing this game the week before finals (stupid, I know). I put in about 45 hours that week and an additional 40 the week after. I was addicted. I literally couldn't close my eyes without seeing the game. Absolutely loved it. Managed to do fine on my finals, but didn't sleep much because I wanted to keep playing so badly.

I haven't played a whole lot since then; kind of got burnt out. But, I anticipate picking it up again soon.

Favorite aspects: I love city-builders like Banished, and this game is like a new and improved version of Banished. The tech tree helps you gradually unlock new abilities, buildings, and improvements, which is super cool. I also love that there is a grid system to plan the city out on and I liked the trade system too. I haven't played since the trade update this morning, but I hope the new update doesn't make things overly-complicated. We will have to see. I also really like the ability to enable mods like larger maps--that makes the game even more exciting for me.

Least favorite aspects: I struggled a lot with population stress once my population got too big. It frustrated me so much that I ended up adjusting it in the game's code to turn it down. In early game-play, when struggling to find enough workers, I would normally turn my farm production off in the winter and move the new laborers into other open jobs, which can get very tedious. Once I have enough adults to work all the jobs, though, I normally just leave the farms on all the time, which isn't as productive, but is a lot easier when you have 20+ fields.

Hope this was helpful! Let me know if you have any questions I can potentially answer.

Also, keep in mind that there is an official Discord page for the game, as well as a Wiki that is in development, and a subreddit where other players can help you, too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended? Yes and no. The game looks nice, has good music, and is technically fun...IF ONLY it were balanced. It's an addictive game but after hours upon hours of playing on EASY MODE, I still can't get my village to survive. Imagine having 4 or 5 fuel factories for like 10 houses but somehow never having enough fuel. Imagine having a dozen fields, two pastures, and a fishery, but somehow your population of below 100 is starving?! Like do they not know how to eat??
I gather all the necessary resources and build roads, but they never seem to reach any of the target structures on time. And the technology tree is a heaping mess. In order to have any upgrade, I have to go through 3,4, or 5 upgrades prior, and it takes forever to generate experience (even with 10 research buildings which are upgraded). For example:

-Villagers need to eat meat.
1) Create pasture and dump a bunch of herdsmen (1-8).
2) Get food processing from the tech tree to unlock the butcher. Dump in a butcher (1-2).
3) Villagers won't eat the meat because it's raw. You need to have it cured.
4) To have the meat cured, you need the cureworks, for which you spend one tech point. And you need labourers (1-2).
5) The cureworks won't function without salt. You spend another tech point to get the salt works. And more labourers (1-2)
6) By now you have spent 3 tech points, which has taken several years to accumulate.
7) You have also used at least 6 labourers to get all of this to function at the bare minimum.
8) You never see the cured chops show up in your inventory. They apparently don't exist.

There is an ungodly amount of upgrades which all necessitate different buildings, which means you need more labourers. But you can't really have it all because if you have too many labourers, everyone dies since you lose your resources fast. But if you don't have enough labourers....everyone dies because you can't...procure...the resources....Incredulous. No matter how hard I try, and no matter how many different ways I've attempted to work around the tech tree, this game is flat out impossible. I've tried focusing on different areas every time. I've restarted about 50 times in the past few days. It's basically not possible to stabilise your village. As for monitoring your supplies, so far it's rough and unhelpful. Nothing seems to add up.

Bottomline- the game has a lot of potential but it's infuriatingly unbalanced. I'm hoping it will improve with future updates, but as it is right now, it's just a load of frustration.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSettlement Survivor doesn't deserve a thumbs down. It has so much promise. It looks great, and wants to provide a deeper, richer experience than it's spiritual predecessor 'Banished'. Not bad for an Indie game.

But it falls short in some significant ways.

The tech tree is overly complex, the rewards for new technology in many areas are way too costly to implement, or needless locked for little gain.

Just one example would be bread making. To make bread, you need a bread workshop (can be upgraded to a bakery to make more advanced baked goods, but requires a lot of technology investment and "advanced" materials such as tables??), and the processed food materials. To build a bread workshop you will need glass. To get glass, you need a sandpit that produces clay and sand, and a then a kiln factory to produce the glass. Why a bread workshop would need glass in the iron age, is perplexing, but okay. In order to bake bread you need flower, so you will need a least one of only two or three types of seeds (from a lucky starting map seed, or very expensive trade order), such as oats to farm, and then a mill to grind the oats after they're farmed into flower. Oh, and you'll need sugar or butter, which are two other technology lines, with multiple steps. and then something that produces sugar or milk and the processing workshops to create those products too.
That's a lot of technology research just to bake bread, not to mention all the material and labour costs, food and support required for the the workers, and critically, time. This compared to the blacksmith which is available at the beginning of the game, which makes just tools.

It's not this is necessarily terrible, per se, but it's seems some tech went needlessly deep, complicated, unnecessarily interdependent, while other's could use some obvious upgrades.

No queueing system for production, so the same workshop (say the tailor for instance), which can produce multiple items (clothes and shoes) has to be constantly monitored, and switched over. That get's tiring quickly.

Also, consumption and production levels needs rebalancing.

Finally, the information panel to monitor production, consumption, and population needs a significant upgrade. If the game want's you to go deep, then provide the feedback to really plumb the math in a detailed, and especially real time way. Yearly, historical data, is nearly useless, without a calculator on standby, and waiting a year to understand a shortfall is too long. A bad guess can spell disaster, when the information you need should be at your fingertips.

My 40+ hours of play time weren't without enjoyment, but in the end the game becomes needlessly grindy and illogical. 150(+) citizens and counting.\t\t\t\t\t
