\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my opinion it's far from the best in this genre, but it's not bottom of the barrel either. It's just kinda ok. I got in on a Steam sale for like $12 and I've put 8 hours in at the time of this review, so I can't complain too much.

One of my biggest gripes is that I really don't like the setup of the tech tree at all. You have different types/categories of tech, but if you hover over something on one tab, It'll say you need another tech from another category before you can use it. They should just make one branching tech tree with the different categories of tech different colors. Where things are "connected" to one another and kind of flow better. Think like Civilization when you view the overarching tech tree. I hate having to keep flipping through categories because I want a tech but find out I have to go to another category to get a prereq.

I don't turn the music on when I play this, I just listen to podcasts, so I can't speak to whether or not the music sucks like a lot of people are saying at the moment.

While it's not great or a game changer, I'd still recommend it, albeit on a slight sale. It is in EA after all, so here's hoping they get things a little more ironed out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's many games that appear to be like this one but to me none that come out the gate with so much polish and detail right off the bat. While playing I'm NOT sitting here thinking: "Boy this game is going to be good when they implement X, Y, and Z function into the game". Nope not at all because right now it's got more than enough depth to keep me going for the money they are asking.

There are so many items to craft and make and actually play this game like a true trader in a new land. Before I can even blink here comes factions which totally changes the trading aspect and makes the game even deeper with strategy on making the most money for your advancement. Love the deep tech tree which takes a lot of work just to get into the 2nd tier of the 4 tiers available across 8 or 9 different specialties.

The game also makes me appreciate the Chinese culture and some of the tidbits they put into their special events. Of course the developers are Chinese and they have not taken any shortcuts or rushed a product to market with false promises. Instead they've published a well done game with plans for much more, The translations are mostly perfect and I do enjoy having a bit of Chinese lore put into the game. It's never too late to learn more about our neighbors.

The graphics are fine, nothing special but I'm glad they spent more time on developing balance and great game mechanics instead of a poor running game hidden under fancy pixels. You can tell what they values more, game play over glitz.\t\t\t\t\t
