TL/DR: The Tech Tree makes no sense. Until it does the thumb stays down.

Really that's it for why the game gets a negative review, because honestly for the most part it's what Banished wanted to be. However...
The tech tree is bonkers. To give you a good idea of why it's silly, and not only because of the tree layout itself. Oh no...
Early game one of the most difficult aspects of your village is managing health. Not only because of sprains and bruises, but because pretty much EVERY. SINGLE. FOOD. ITEM. IS. POISONOUS.
That's right, for most of early and mid game you will likely incur a -20 health for every 10 food a citizens consumes.
And there's no real way to mitigate this either since every quality food product requires numerous research unlocks in different areas so unless you are ridiculously lucky with your starting animals AND seeds (cows + wheat or oats) you'll have a ways to go before making ... toast.

This is compounded by the absurdity of medicine. Herb, your basic go-to healing is only available from gathering for an inordinate amount of time. And the unlocks to start farming it? 4 points in an otherwise fairly unnecessary tree (forestry) and you need to send your villagers to gather plants to find the seed.

Now, you start the game with 5 research points which you can allocate between a plethora of semi-vital things. The herb thing might sound nice, but that's 4 points gone which could be used on many building unlocks that would be much more useful...

...however the technology progression makes no sense. An example of this, one that literally pushed me to the point of writing this review, is quality leather. I got my town to the point where I could develop some better clothing (even though my folks still largely ate basic foods)... Only to find out that you unlock the quality tailor BEFORE the leather processing building, one locked behind the other of course. Which means there is literally no way for you to progress this line of production. You either use 2 points or just sit on an unusable building.


And no, these aren't the only broken production lines.
Which sucks, because otherwise the game really is a total gem.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBase game... meh.

I would rate this neutral if I could, Vanilla is a thumbs down, steam workshop makes it a thumbs up. The game really just scratches that itch to install Banished again with a good mod.

I've been playing vanilla but the population growth is so freaking slow that it literally tries to doom your town before it takes off. You are literally developing your town faster with materials and technology than what you have with population to achieve better products to prevent your town from spiraling out of control. It results in micro-managing (not fun) in moving people around for different resources or turning off and on different buildings. The other annoying part is the fact that each building can perform multiple jobs, for instance the butcher, you'd think he would utilize any meat and butcher it but each meat is a focused job that you have to select, various meats coming in but not enough to satisfy a single butcher and small town? Get ready to constantly switch his butchering choice on a regular basis....

So the workshop fixes a lot of QoL and missing base game balances at the moment to let you enjoy the game but for Vanilla, the balancing feels way off to the point of micromanaging which takes away the depth of the game. There are a lot of product steps which is pretty nice. I think the size of some of the buildings can be reduced, especially in the beginning where the buildings size with your city, small butcher to a commercial butcher.

If this wasn't early access, it would be a no but I guess, lets give it time. It definitely has potential.\t\t\t\t\t

The core problem of this game is that the job assignment is a chore. You never have enough people to fill the positions, much because of the lacking QoL I describe in the next paragraph, but also because it hasn't been balanced properly. Farms require far too many workers. Foresters huts and gatherers huts provide far too little resources.
