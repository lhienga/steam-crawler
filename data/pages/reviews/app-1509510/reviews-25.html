\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti have been playing this game since early alpha, and overtime it has gotten only better.

it starts off tough, not knowing where to start on the map, or prioritising the wrong tech or running out of food, you know the usual stuff in a city builder.
however once you get some crucial technologies you can turn it all around, but rapid population growth can bring you back down to the ground. there is so much to balance that it keeps every minute of the game engaging. you may have great plans and you decide "oh this tech and this tech together will be good" but without proper people management you can cause more issues. you may think this is a complaint it's really not i love planning ahead but this game proves that worrying about the future instead of the present can slow all the city down!

there has been a few bugs over the time i've played but most of them get patched within a month roughly.

the one thing i will bash the game on however is the settlers ai's sometimes if you place a building that overlaps a current function, sometimes they break will just die as a result. for example

the boiler room placed during the winter has caused many of my settlements to end because ONCE its placed they stop using the fuel in their homes, the boiler worker then wont work cuase they are cold, which causes everyone to wait for the fuel that will never come. and ALL die. this last statement is a warning and a bit of helpful advice i feel.

overall a solid 8/10 for me would recommend wholeheartadly\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHard to recommend this game in it's current state. It has nice graphics, and a pleasing ascetic, but there are lot of questionable design choices in game. I would give it a neutral recommendation if possible.

I enjoy this style of game, and played tons of hours in similar ones like Banished, but this game feels like it lacks balance and/or direction. It is hard to pinpoint exactly what is wrong with the game, but the best way to describe it would probably be: Settlement Survival feels very linear, and somehow too open ended at the same time.

There are multiple strategies for survival, in terms of which goods to gather and which buildings to prioritise, but you almost have access to too many different buildings and technology options from the very beginning, making progression seem shallow and somewhat nonexistent.

The game doesn't feel very challenging, no matter how you set your starting parameters. Starting with less people isn't necessarily more challenging, as they use significantly less resources compared to a large settlement. It is fun and seems to have better pacing with less people, but it mostly just takes much longer to get anything accomplished. You only have the option to start out with 2, or 18+ citizens, and nothing in between. There is a significant difference in these two options, with no good middle ground. This forces you to play a very slow paced game, and one of the only ways to speed up a 2 person starting file, is to quickly build a Town Hall to encourage Immigrants to move into your settlement. It seems ridiculous having an enormous town hall for a 2 person village, but you can do that, and/or wait years and years for your population to become larger via producing children which take forever to be born, and even longer for them to grow into working adults.

You get way too many resources off the start as well, no matter how you choose the settings. You can't even choose an option to have no starting seeds, discouraging any sort of foraging style. You just start with tons of resources, seeds, and an annoying "tutorial" type objective thing pops up and somewhat forces you to build exactly the same initial buildings off the start, adding to the linear feel and not making it feel like a sandbox game, and also largely taking away from the survival aspect since you're so well equipped and railroaded into a certain build order.

You immediately have access to probably 90% of available buildings off the start of the game, and there isn't much urgency to constructing any of them, other than maybe Houses and the Chopping House for firewood. (Citizens need homes and will quickly freeze to death in winter if you have neither of these.)

Sure, you can fail a few times while learning the base mechanics of this style of game, or have a natural disaster ravage your town once in a blue moon if this option is enabled, but once you understand how this game functions at a base level, it becomes very easy, and the biggest challenge is dealing with the poor AI, and cumbersome menu system. Reassigning people to different jobs was easier in the beta, and for some reason you cannot lower the number of current active Builders to more important jobs, without manually reassigning them personally.

The technology tree is interesting, and could potentially add variety to your style, but it is poorly designed and not balanced very well. It isn't very intuitive, and a lot of the buildings, their functions, and what they require to produce are not well explained. You can choose upgrades in a somewhat nonsensical order, as some of the things you unlock are related to completely different buildings from a different technology tree. This kind of makes sense contextually, but not in the way the tech trees are balanced or laid out, often resulting in wasted points, or having to basically save up tech points to dump several into various different trees, just to get one thing to function properly, while having unlocked useless and unusable technology in the meantime. Some of the tech upgrades seem overpowered in terms of how game changing they are, like getting 100% increased production from certain things such as farms and stockyards. The progression is all over the place, and certain things will totally change the dynamic of the game, but not really in a good way. Certain resources become trivialised via these massive upgrades, and they also make the lesser upgrades basically not worth investing in, since everything only takes 1 tech point. Having certain upgrades go from something more moderate like 33/66/100% production via 3 tech points would be a lot better balanced, for example. You acquire technology points fairly quickly anyways, so some change like that wouldn't even really be a huge nerf to the current state of the game, especially since you often have no other good options to choose from anyways.

The tech trees are linear and it would be more interesting if you had branching paths, or had to weigh options within specific trees themselves. Other obviously overpowered technology examples are the very easy to get "-75% construction material" upgrade, which trivialises a lot of the resource management, especially compared to some of the literally useless upgrades which are available as other "equivalent" options early game. Obvious choices like that make the game seem linear, in terms of which upgrades you should get each play through, at least in the early stages of the game.

The Transfer Station doesn't seem to function properly, if at all. The Caravan seems bugged as well in some ways, as the chance of death seems to not actually be represented properly as an actual stat in the game. I have sent out dozens of caravans with the lowest chance of death possible, with varying amounts of people, and the death rate always seems much higher than what is advertised. Some can be accounted to bad luck, but I have had dozens of Caravans with 20+ people and a 4% death rate return often with 1/20+ survivors, with no logical explanation, and it is statistically improbable/impossible with the numbers presented in game, especially after your caravans are fully upgraded.

Without mods, the base maps are too small. The largest map available becomes filled fairly quickly, and I would definitely recommend getting some Larger Map mods if you plan on spending any significant amount of time on the same map. These mods are often buggy and usually aren't compatible with most other mods, so you have to choose which lifestyle improvements you actually want.

I really enjoyed some aspects of this game, and I know it only recently came out of beta, but I won't be sinking any more time into this at it's current stage of design. Hopefully there is a balance patch and overhaul on a lot of these issues, because the base game has tons of potential, but feels poorly designed from a game play perspective.\t\t\t\t\t

Settlement Survival
A very interesting and beautiful construction game. The graphics are easy on the eyes with a bright color palette, the constructions are beautifully and impressively designed. As for the gameplay, I don't rate it too highly. It has most of the basic elements of construction-themed games, which is good , but there's nothing particularly special about it.
Especially in the late stages of the game. It made me fall into a loop of building and expanding.
The game map size, even at the largest setting, is not big enough for me as I had to spam a lot of buildings in the later stages of the game.
Nevertheless, the game deserves a positive review.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first glance it looks like the beloved Banished improved and re-imagined, but once you play for a few hours you will start to realise its really just Banished at home.

In Banished, the villagers age increases each season, unlike in this game, where its by year, making it much slower paced than Banished, and frankly, just a bit unbearably slow. I admit, the villager AIs has orders of magnitude of higher IQ than the Banished AIs but that's not where the game falls short. One of the more concerning problem is that resources acquisition is extremely unbalanced, paired that with the pace of the game being slow, it makes for a good time to alt tab. Like for real, how much water does these villagers drink. For some reason I need minimum 20% of my population to gather water just to get by, and another 30% to make food. There was never a time where I had more than 3 spare labourers to do stuff after everything else that's required to survive.

Which leads to another problem, the tech tree makes this game unnecessarily convoluted. I understand that some people likes depth in games, but is locking something as basic as shoes behind 3 layers of tech tree really necessary, and is locking bridges behind 4 layers of unlocks really make the game more enjoyable.
Look, I get it, gitgud blah blah blah. But how about the devs gitgud too. The apiary never works, anyone that becomes the herbalist seems to go blind as its always low on raw material no matter how many gathers hut I have. The hunters might as well be napping all day as I have never seen textile produced more than single digit per year. There's a bug that makes some buildings in construction simply get ignored for some reason.

Man I hope they improve this game, I loved Banished and this game is the spiritual inheritor of it.\t\t\t\t\t
