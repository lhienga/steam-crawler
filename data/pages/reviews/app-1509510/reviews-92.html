\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs others have said, this game is undeniably beautiful, but for that reason I feel like the technological and civilizational progression of your colony is way less visible than in other games and at times feels forced. You start with those already very good-looking fancy buildings, which feels like you're managing an already well-established modern civilization simply relocating somewhere else rather than a modest early colony trying to prosper and survive. Banished and other games were very good at making you feel like your colony was truly starting from nothing and evolving through hard work, here I just felt like I had to unlock one pretty building after the other, with little effort. The town will not look that different from one generation to the other; it will simply expand.

Most of the things that you have to unlock feel very forced and unnecessary, and the devs seem to focus on the end-game with hard to achieve final goals in order to increase the longevity of the game, rather than making the early progression slower. No matter your difficulty settings, you will quickly get everything you need to have a properly working city. All in all, it did not feel like survival.

This does not necessarily make it a bad game, and others might enjoy it, it is indeed a relaxing city-builder, but should compare itself to Tropico or Simcity rather than Banished, as it was lacking the satisfying visible struggle and evolution of your colony, which is what I enjoy in those "settlement survival" games.\t\t\t\t\t
