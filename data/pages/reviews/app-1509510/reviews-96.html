\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game falls into an interesting spot simply because it is a blend of two other games. The classic Banished, that is an influence for so many others, and Life is Feudal Forest Village( LIF FV), which is almost a straight rip-off of Banished's core code and UI but with their own graphics and a few other mechanics and elements. Settlement Survival does expand on the Happiness and Health areas in a better way, which actually makes this game more challenging but cuts the knees off that mechanic with the technology and research element. And that is the primary difference between this game and Banished, the research and the technology tree. Delaying the use of buildings and technology for the sake of waiting for research points is dulling. It kills the game. I shouldn't need to research clay and sand or any other "resource" that is simply dug from the ground. It is a base resource, it is there. The fact that it requires a special building to produce it is enough. This goes with most of the technology tree, it should just be available. The resources required for it should be the limiting factor on most things. You need glass, so you need sand and a kiln of some sort. I shouldn't need to research, Clay and Sand, then a Kiln, then the building I'm wanting to build anyway. Especially when you average a single technology point a year.

In my opinion Banished and LIF FV are both much better games that will provide continued enjoyment. Yes, they have limitations once you grow to a certain point. Just consider that the end game, you won. You built to the limits that your PC can handle. Could Banished and LIF FV be better? Of course they could. This game doesn't do that though.

So, on it's own merit, is it worth it without the comparison to Banished and LIF? No. For the same reasons above, they kill the game with the required research and technology.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very cute town builder much like banished. It takes a few tries to get the hang of things, but once you do it's pretty fun to make a massive sprawling towns across the map. The events they added often give you a bit of a leg up at the start, which is nice. I highly recommend it.

My tips for the game would be to make sure you have your resource monitor up at all times (U on the keyboard.) If a number is red, that means it's going down, and that's bad. It's easier to put cap limits or sell/trade excess than it is to deal with running out of supplies. The other tip would be if you are unlocking technology, pay close attention to the right. It will show you what you need to produce or build that new technology. It doesn't stop you from buying technology you can't currently produce and then you'll be scrambling through the technology lists looking for what you need. So plan carefully.

My only gripe would be my normal city got to a point where I got a disaster every year, and that seemed a bit much. At first it was just droughts, or floods, etc. But then the tornadoes came. The first one went down the river so it wasn't too bad to rebuild. The second one had ripped through the heart of my town, it took most of the year to finish rebuilding all the homes lost. I was happy to see no tornado for next year, but a new icon. I hovered over it "Sandstorm, damages buildings..." So now I'm playing on easy mode for a bit...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA commendable successor to Banished.
Settlement Survival derives many of its mechanics from Banished.
In some regards, the game is a stark improvement on Banished.
In others, it is a bit of a mess.

The good:
The mechanics are quite well fleshed out. The trading system is a step up from Banished, which is much appreciated. The housing in SS is more realistic (in terms of families per household). The variety of crops & livestock to grow is more comprehensive than vanilla Banished and the uses of animal products are greater (I'm thinking of the Soap factory which uses animal fat from the butcher).
These are only a few examples, there are plenty more.

The bad:
Within a few minutes of playing the game, I could tell that the developers were not Anglophones. This in itself is not a bad thing at all, but sometimes the descriptions can be confusing or misleading.
The later game features require more sophisticated town planning, but when building & production descriptions don't make sense, it can become quite frustrating. (Yes I understand the game is in beta).
The graphics feel more cartoon-y and the music is pleasant at first, but becomes extremely repetitive. At least the music in Banished was catchy.
Finally, there are still quite a few bugs in the game. The most annoying one I noticed was that my hunters in one game would not collect anything. I tried many different locations and different employees, but nothing worked.

Overall, I believe this game is worth a purchase if you are wanting a newer version of Banished. Settlement Survival offers the player more incentive and opportunity to build a bigger and better town than you could ever accomplish in Banished. I really enjoyed playing it, but the confusing translations and mechanics can be a bit jarring and/or misleading.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent game. I've only played for under 40 hours but I've played so many games like it. Alot of the Impressions Games city builders: Zeus, Pharaoh, Caesar; all of the Sim Cities; Tropicos; Sid Meier Civilizations; alot of the Age of Empire releases; the like, etc. This game, Settlement Survivals, most resembles Banished. Where Banished failed in late-game engagement and challenge, Settlement Survival picks it right up. It has a pretty enriched economic component that Banished just lacked. There's dozens more raw materials, specialty structures, items, and specialized labor/jobs. There's even a technology tree to expend research points on. Quite simply, in theory: you can re-create the 18 and 19th industrial revolution transition from agriculture to factory with the crazy product value chains and supply chain capabilities. It's largest drawback is this same advanced concept which makes it such a great game. Right now it lacks significant planning tools for effective advance economy management. There's also a lack of good automation features. I found myself constantly handling supply shortages because of it. I didn't feel like a powerful governor or mayor but rather like a administrative desk jockey or bean counter.

Ultimately, it feels like a fairly complete game which makes it all that much more exciting for whatever improvements, updates, and add-ons that might filter into the finished release. I have a TON of comments and suggestions if anyone knows where to leave them. Overall 5/5 for an early access game and a probably 10/10 once released with improvements!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked Banished, you'll love this game. It's still in early access and there's times when it shows, but even in its current form it's extremely playable. Compared to Banished there's so much more to do and develop, so much more ability to micromanage how supplies are used, and new items and features are still being added. Like Banished, there are some quirks that you have to be mindful of when strategising, but as you get familiar with the game those are easy enough to account for, and the game is still in such early stages that there's no reason to think that things won't be tweaked over time where needed.

Tldr; Banished but better, with more stuff to do and more control over the nitty gritty. Needs a bit of polish in places but that's why it's in early access, and there's a steady stream of patches with bug fixes, new content, and fine tuning/rebalancing. Well worth the money in its current form, and has a lot of potential to be truly excellent when complete.\t\t\t\t\t
