\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game, but there is so much wrong with it that after 20 ish hours I'm getting more frustrated than having fun. I love me a city builder, and especially one with complex production chains, etc. But they have to make some sense, and be somewhat predictable, and you have or should at least be able to review things when they get more complex. Here's a non-exhaustive list of what drives me nuts:
-The stated output, time, and stamina numbers for a production building appear to be meaningless. Why do I say this? Because they don't change when you do anything that is supposed to affect them. Add a brick kiln? Add gears? Add compost? Numbers remain the same. Hell, upgrade a mine to a 'better, more efficient mine', and the numbers will still be the same. They might be having an effect, but who the heck knows?

-Logistics are entirely a mess. Why are there so many storage buildings? And why don't they make any sense at all? Also, god forbid you'd like to put an item in a delivery station or supply station and you don't actually HAVE any of that item. You can't reserve it for placement unless you actually have stock, which creates a stupid cycle of waiting, clicking, waiting, clicking. But overall, the transit, supply, delivery thing is overly convoluted for no reason and doesn't seem to work. Further, and maybe WORSE, the dang workers in the production shops just horde crap in their backpacks. For like a year! I've got smelters running all over the map with 70 units of steel just chilling in their packs while I can't build things because I need it. They must be into crossfit.

-Trade is just TEDIOUS. At least early game. You can't carry much. It's worthless. It takes half your colony and it takes FOREVER. I just forewent trade entirely at this point. Also, I pretty much only play the game at 10x, yet it still feels like everything takes forever.

-Things are just unclear in general. Are the building efficiency numbers per worker? Like, I can have like 7 pasture workers. Or 2. Can I raise alpaca somehow faster with more workers? You really can't tell. Couple this with the arbitrary 'production limits' which I assume are there to ensure you don't chew through all of your raw materials you might need for other facilities - but that means balance is just broken. Most default production numbers mean the workers are idle half the year.

Anyway, there's more, but you get the point. The game has some positives for sure, but I think it would simply benefit from some simplification, clarity and polish, and rebalancing. As it is, I can't keep playing it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat colony sim in the style of Banished. The type of game where you can tell yourself, "just 5 more minutes," and then look up to see that it's been 2 hours.

There are certainly a few UI improvements that could be made (I would love a "Demolish" button next to the upgrade buttons when a building is selected), but some great updates have already been implemented in the short time I've owned the game, so I have no doubts that those are in the works.

At the moment, I feel that I've played through most of the content the game has to offer, but the addition of more preset campaign options, like the Easter Island campaign, could easily balloon the amount of content without the implementation of new mechanics. I will likely come back to this game every once in a while for years to come, and I already feel that I've gotten more than my money's worth.\t\t\t\t\t
