\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played couple different games of similar styles but this one is the one I always come back to. They're still updating and making new contents, and improved a lot since I first played the game.

If the population is not growing fast enough and you're always out of labourers, build more houses. I always try to build enough to have vacant houses available and the population almost never decline.
If the happiness is low and no procreation is a problem, build cemeteries, give them shoes and alcohol.
Unlocking technology requires planning, build MANY research facilities to speed up the progress.

The newest save file I've made is only 40 years in with 550+ population and 70 workers to spare, green happiness and health (didn't even build candy factory yet). The first couple tries might be frustrating and not everything may be 100% straightforward, but the fun of it is to become better and knowing how to improve next time. The game is also designed to throw challenges at you even at the end game so you're still managing populations and happiness constantly. Which is a good thing because I've played so many games like this one where I was so rich and had surplus of resources that there was no reason for me to unlock anything else going forth.

It's a well designed game, encountered very little bugs, and has a nice community.
My only problem is that after unlocking everything and have a stable settlement, there's nothing else to do - aka i played too much.\t\t\t\t\t
