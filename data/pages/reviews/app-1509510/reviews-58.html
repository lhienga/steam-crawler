\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game looked great at first, nice graphics, quite a lot of tutorial, and a tech tree to give you something to do for a long time.

But it just doesn't match my play style. I like to set up a working, balanced, colony, then research a few more things, build new buildings, rebalance, expand, research some more, etc. But the game just won't let me. Stay at 40 colonists for a while (to the point where you're running out of laborers), then get 10 immigrants all at the same time. Can't accept only some of them either, you need to accept all. Which means many more houses to build as they won't move in together, and medical herbs becoming scarce. So, research getting herb seeds, which costs all of my tech points, as this is quite a way down the tech tree. But does this help? No, because you need more tech from other pages to build the buildings! Tech wasted, colony dying to diseases.

Yes, some city builders get boring after a while when there's nothing left to do, and adding some challenges may sound like a good idea. But I'm playing my first game, in easy mode, for a reason - I want to learn. Can't do that if the game forces you to crash your colony into a wall.

Unfortunately, I can't refund, as with all the tutorials you won't notice the bad parts until too late. Not too much damage as the game was on sale for €10, but still a disappointment as I won't play the game again.
