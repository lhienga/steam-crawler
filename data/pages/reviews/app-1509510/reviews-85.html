\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSettlement Survival is a great game, but does not really have something special.

Don't get me wrong; the game is well-made and certainly to be tried by other city builder gamers. Some mechanics are a little twitchy, but overall the game is very playable. There are plenty of supply chains, different approaches to upkeep your settlement and even thought-through upgrades of buildings.

The major thing that I miss in this game is something to add a little spice. There are no incoming attacks to be defended (e.g. like in Going Medieval), it is a grid-game (unlike games like Foundation) and there is no competition to build your settlement (e.g. like in the Anno franchise). It is just your town, its needs and nature to battle it out. It got pretty stale after stabilizing food sources.

Big disclaimer though; I have not tried the Easter island scenario or have I played on extreme difficulty. I expect these to be very challenging, but I don't feel the need to try them out.

To conclude, Settlement Survival is a great game, well designed and amusing to play. I have actually enjoyed playing it for a while. However, I do not recommend this game to players trying out this genre or players looking for a little action in a long-term save.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFoundation vs. Banished vs. Dawn of Man vs. Settlement Survival
Having played 40/110/130 hours of the first three I'm noticing a glaring few things that make this game come up short.
This game has a lot more resources than Foundation, a more complicated "Citizen statuses" than Banished, and more extensive technology tree than Dawn of Man, but these doesn't necessarily make the game better.
While I enjoy "complicated", there is something called too much. There are so many resources with so many buildings that take up so much space, you're hardly left with anything left to make room for your houses and their accommodations. Citizens want to live next to clinics and churches and have to live close to a market, so naturally you want to clump them together, but you need about 12 butcher shops just to handle all the different kinds of meats. One building can only produce one item, so one has to process mutton, one venison, one alpaca meat, chicken, turkey and so on. Apply this to some 10 different types of food producing buildings, with 5-10 bi-product process like leather, fat, feather etc.
Oh boy this game has simply TOO many resources, with a UI that has limited overview. Despite the few resources in Dawn of Man, I could always bring up a graph showing the resources over time (showing trends up and down) for each resource. This game has a single window to find the absolute values of each (~100 resources) and one with multiple tabs to find graphs for "all food over time" and other info. I'm struggling HARD to keep an overview over my amount of clothes (if they are going up or down), medicine, health, housing, happiness.
This game is simply too much.
Edit: Tried it again for 2 hours and remembered again what's wrong: You get tech points and you CAN spend them wrongly and end up killing yourself by freezing or poor health. This feels not as much "go a path and make it good", but "Find out the right path".\t\t\t\t\t
