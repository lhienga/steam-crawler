Subtle racism still is racism.

My great, great grandfather on my father's side migrated from Spain to Mexico in the late XIX Century. On my mother's side, my grandmother migrated from the north of the country and my grandfather's family came from the south and the border with Guatemala. My father migrated ten years ago to the USA, to take a high level academic position there. Therefore seeing the racist trope "migrants bring illnesses" is rather upsetting for me. It's not migrants who bring illnesses, is poverty, undernourishment, lack of hygiene and want what causes epidemics.

But if that trope wasn't bad enough, you can build special residences for your migrants, where they will be overcrowded and hence won't be able to reproduce. Think about that for a second, we can take in migrants, and use them as workers, but we won't let them reproduce. That's particularly upsetting knowing that places like Israel had policies where they would forcibly sterilize migrant Ethiopian Jews.

I find it appalling that the devs have interiorized racism to such degree that they didn't even stopped to think if implementing ethnically biased birth control policy was problematic. Double so since I was expecting a "cozy city builder with survival elements" and not "build your own apartheid village simulator".

tldr: The building and survival gameplay is good, but the use of the racist trope "migrants bring illnesses", and the gameplay mechanic of building special homes for migrants so they won't reproduce, kills it for me.
