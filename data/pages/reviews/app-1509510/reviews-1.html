\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis could be what you're looking for if you have been hungry for a Banished+ clone (it goes deeper than banished in places)--the thing is, it's not. It has a lot of good elements, on the surface, it feels like banished, but if you play a challenging game you have to micro extensively. Ok, you might be saying, that doesn't feel too bad.

Most of the micro is to correct horrible coding. Here's a simple example: workers carry around their production. There's no reason to this. They don't take their goods to the stockpile with urgency, sometimes they'll carry the products around for two to three days for no reason until eventually they drop it at the stockpile. It's just flat lazy stupid coding. The products could be left at the workshop and porters or delivery people could come get them, or any number of other solutions---workers could drop off on their way home each day. No, no way, just have the workers carry things around for days while another citizen starves to death, dies of thirst, or freezes to death. Now if you have 1000 citizens, and all your supply chains are on lockdown, this is fine and you might not notice. But at the start, or when you get a huge load of immigrants, or in a harder mode when you're pushing through and need goods to be in the stockpile, it's foolish.

I'll give you one more. Students in the school don't start as students until 9-10 years of age and often aren't done with school until age 20+. School takes forever to get the kids an education. You can micro this, kick the kids out of school and bump them into the laborer class, but then they don't have the educated efficiency bonus ever. kids do nothing, just consume resources and stand around. They don't have help with gathering or carrying. So again you have this stupid bottleneck due to horrible coding. This was brought up in a discussion forum and the developer response was essentially "git gud". There's a lot of indifference and lack of careful thinking by the dev team to smooth this out.

Game looks solid, has good features, but under the hood, there's very little attention to making it a smooth play, it's rough and the economic systems are chunky instead of granular, and if you like playing games with precision and a smooth feel to the economy at all, this is not the game for you and the devs don't care based on responses in forums. They've got your money already, play it like it is.\t\t\t\t\t
