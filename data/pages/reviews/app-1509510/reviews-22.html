\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8.5 / 10
If Banished & Frostpunk had a baby.

It's a great game, with a fair price tag, mod support and you can customise it from easy peasy up to f*in difficult.

There is hardly anything bad to say about Settlement Survival, so the few things that I personally did not like are just minor complaints in an otherwise great game which should suit nearly any gamer.
What's also worth pointing out is that this game actually has an endgame. So you can't just get to a certain point and whoop, you just snowball through from now on and all the challenge is gone.
Sure sooner or later you can have a perfect city but getting there really requires you to do all the things the game has to offer and do them properly.

So now to my minor complaints:
.) Performance does fall off a bit once you have a big city, like in the late-late game. The FPS & frametimes stay solid, not like in Frostpunk where you will drop into the 40's because single/duo core optimization, it's more that loading / saving takes long, even from/to an NVME drive, and that the UI gets a bit stuttery at times due to the sheer amount of content it has to load.

Also the game eats a bit too much GPU power for my taste compared to how it looks. But that's speaking for the ‚Äúbetter graphics‚Äù version on all maxed settings and once again only starts to show up in the late-late game.

.) I'm not a big fan of the RNG on when and what seeds or animals you will find. Same goes for merchant ships and what they sell. Especially when it comes to the unique blueprints.

.) Translation to English, especially in tooltips, leaves a bit to be desired at times.

.) UI for stock & production management could be better.

.) Mods have some interesting, negative, Impact on loading performance by spiking CPU usage, hard.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Good game with very few bugs. Basically Banished with improvements.This game may be regarded as a descendant to Banished. It improves on Banished in several ways (for example, processed foods being more filling, houses filling up all your citizens' needs) and incorporates some of the things I liked about the Mega Mod. I ran into some smaller issues like a citizen getting stuck standing at the market forever or buildings not consuming the items that they were supposed to consume (the clinic didn't use bandages and so the recovery of injured citizens took forever), but they seem to have been solved. Graphics are nice and detailed enough, rendering distance works fine. Animations of animals look a bit janky but I prefer that to the game struggling with the animations of many animals. I'd love to see some of the features we got for Banished via the Mega Mod, like these: - buildings using different designs (What about unlocking housing styles via faction reputation, so that with a good reputation, you are able to build the whole town in that style instead of only special buildings?)- a hot and dry climate setting, maybe other climates, too- more crops and products, maybe sensitive to different temperature ranges so that you really have to consider what to grow and what to buy (ties in nicely with different climates)... and the ability to turn off events completely. Being able to adjust difficulty is something I really enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game. There are so many good things in it. But it is so buggy, and the gameplay is uneven, and often illogical.

What is the boiler room? It makes no sense. It seems like they adapted an electrical system, like it's a power plant.

The way ressources are used is unclear, it often just suddenly goes from tens of thousands of units of a ressource to 0 in a second (flax or food or water for example). Often there are ressources in the warehouses but the production location says that it has no ressources. most of these city games have staff in the warehouses who also act as delivery and also the workers often deliver to the warehouses, like farmers delivering their crops. And so, when a ressource is not getting to the warehouse, it is easy to spot the problem in the chain. But in this game it is a mystery. I can have no builders at all and yet things still get built somehow. Their delivery system is ridiculous because the range is so small.

The range for worship, theatre, health care is insane as well. Why would a population of 250 need two hospitals and two theatres and two cathedrals!

The education system is not good, why would a farmer or a miner need an education bonus. If you are a miner with a phd, that is a useless phd! The Tropico series solved that problem (a lot of problems with Tropico, sure, but the education system works).

Why no granary?
The food quality system does not work. veggie salad?? gourmet shop? what? come on! Why not have restaurants? Or a tavern? or both? or set the system communist style, like in the old impressions games where everyone has a certain quality of food... or certain neighborhoods have better food and higher expectations (here the mention goes to Emperor from impressions, when you would read what was being served, it made you want to eat it!)

Indeed, as with all these new city-building games, there is A LOT of arrogance from these developers, in that they are trying to reinvent the wheel and are not humble enough to look at previous successful city building games from the early days of video games, like the impressions series, that fine-tuned all these issues between Caesar to Children of the Nile.

A few problems persisted in these city building games, definitely, the walker system was weak...but that could be fixed with city statistics and a certain automation, city-wide between citizen and ressource.

But here, it's like they are starting from scratch, and making all the same mistakes that have already been made in the early days of gaming, but slightly differently...why not learn from those older games? ARROGANCE! As a result, it is almost impossible to track ressources or figure out exactly how to build a successful city. The happiness system does not work either.

The size of the buildings makes no sense, the water system is fantastical. No irrigation. How does the bathhouse get water? (carried from the reservoir? what?) the latrines system is silly... and the range is also fantastical (so I need three hospitals for my large city, but just two latrines is fine for 300 people!!?? Why not make toilets upgrades to the houses? (from outhouse, to central flushing toilets, with a sewage system prereq)...no instead you can buy a cat or a dog for your house! or bedding? what? Why can't bedding be a luxury ressource like ceramics or wine?

Plus, the houses don't upgrade, so you must destroy the house to replace it with a luxury house. It is a bad system, built on the pay to buy tablet/phone game model and not based on solid gameplay data. It makes more sense to have the citizens upgrade their own houses based on the necessary ressources and luxury ressources and access to wealth.

No salaries either, no taxes? You start with no money. So I guess you make jewellery and sell it and EVERYONE wants your jewellery! right.
In fact, even in the old zeus style games, you could just sell hundreds of sculptures to become very wealthy, but at least in those games, foreign powers had ressources that they wanted and ressources that they didn't want. In this game, the economy is childish and poorly thought out. Again, it is like the "economy" of these tablet phone games! And again, there are lots of really strong economy based games in the past that solved all these problems. There was one that was just economy that was really good, from the mid aughts I think, where you ran a trading family (in China I think) but I can't remember the name of it. My point is the model exists and it works! Why not do that? Instead of reinventing a terrible economic model that does not work?

I so desperately wanted this game to be good and I have tried for months, coming back after every update, but it is just not a good game. Unbalanced, poorly executed and way way too buggy.
