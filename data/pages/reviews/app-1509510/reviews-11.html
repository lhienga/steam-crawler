+ Changing challenges early-mid-end game.
+ Big (well, for the settlement sim - factorio tech tree is bigger) interconnected tech tree in multiple areas.
+ Good clear interface.
++ Text search of items (- you need to know name of the item or look through strangely sorted list in one of 10+ categories).
+/- I do not know if I liked it or not, but relatively small benefit in crafting items. You get roughly 20% increase in total price, but often big decrease in number of resulting items.
- bug: Dock order menu replace Checkmark name from "Show unowned items" to "Hide owned items".
- Organizing storage is big part of the game, but low control. No filters to see only allowed/forbidden items, no way to copy settings. Dock order menu loses cart when you close window.
- Low control of citizen's stock. They manufactured items, you need that 10 tools right now, the smith went to rest with 20 tools in his backpack, Need to wait a couple months before they become available. Solution - include pocketed items (can be 100+ in one pocket!) in available items and make them unload pocket when items are requested.
Need better in-game documentation - with recipes of produced items and interlinks. I want to estimate production benefits before I invest into that tech tree and build the facility.
- Food, beverage items need to be listed in hunger/thirst points, not in amount of owned items.
- Can not expand variable size structures (fields, storage yards, etc) - rebuild only, lose upgrades.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: So I continued playing and I can now recommend this game as it's both actually playable - which it didn't feel like for the first few hours of stumbling around - and can be fun once you know the pattern.

However, I stand by my assertion that this game needs to still be in Early Release as several features of the game are not finished, not clear, or just plain broken.

Map generation is alpha level at best and the seed is irrelevant. Your options include adding rivers (1 or 2) and lakes (also just 1 or 2) and only on the largest map selection and the rivers will be uniform with the same width throughout the map and the lakes can generate on top of one another. Mountains will generate and lakes/rivers will carve them up but they don't look weathered, just not planned.

Building placement with a messed up map generator is obviously an issue. You can turn building 90 degrees per rotation and rivers may or may not play along with your plans and perpendicular rivers are useless as you can't building anything on them until you unlock terraforming.

Terraforming is one of those tools that sounds good but is actually a nightmare. Part way through the horrid research list - more on that later - is terraforming to make flat land and another unlock to make rivers. You can't make mountains, which is fine. However, terraforming to flat land is a selecter like placing a field and the land is suddenly flat. Great for absolutes but inexcusable for it's lack of finesse. It will place great land for building a riverside production center but won't have any flow or texture to it, so you try to use the "river terraforming" tool and find it's not in line at all with the flat land tool. The river tool has you place water as if you're actually creating a river flow which means if you are trying to just make a flowing river, no problem, but if you're trying to make a river run along a course through a city and want it to look natural but be useful, it'll break the game mechanics of bridges and buildings but you won't be told they aren't working. You'll see.

I'm not sure if this game was written in another language and poorly translated to English or if the devs just let the intern do it but in-game descriptions are both unclear and ignore many rules of grammar. The tech "tree" is my biggest complaint about the game and the language used to describe what each unlock allows just makes it worse. If you want to know what a relevant building does if you purchase a specific unlock, too bad, because the descriptions are very brief and glaze over the important details. Save scumming doesnt help either as you still have to construct the new building to see the recipes and find you may or may not be ready for this line of unlocks. For instance, bedding can be made with linen, not just cotton cloth, but the description only lists cotton cloth so I put off bedding until it was one of the last unlocks and only then discovered the omitted details that were game changing.

Below is most of my original review which includes more game issues.

> First thing, your people are absolutely deserving of their easily avoidable fate. They have no self-preservation instinct and will absolutely ignore your "requests" to prioritize farming before a famine or gathering drinking water before a drought or, my favorite, just getting some damn firewood before winter sets in! Three people can't even produce 200 pieces of firewood in a year so you use a third of your initial population just trying to produce firewood, but wait, you don't have enough logs! So you build a second forester hut and find you're already out of people due to the water, farms, firewood gathering, and having someone to flick items at your other peeps because they don't have time to do it themselves when they're busy freezing to death - or spraining their delicate ankles.
> Which brings me to my second thing, the "processing" centers the game offers, also known as buildings to everyone else. Don't bother looking for stats in-game on what a single person is expected to produce as a farmer, forester, or tailor (or anything else) as I'm 99% sure it's just an RNG and you get the shaft if you expect anything useful. You have "upgraded" versions of buildings that can be built along the river that state "higher efficiency" than their landlocked counterparts but has no corresponding information and ultimately seem to be detrimental as they take more resources and you can never get the workers to stay focused. You can prioritize a building but it's random chance if they'll complete it. If you're short of building materials the game makes no effort to have workers do better so you have to manually unassign workers from jobs that can't be completed and temporarily assign them to cutting down the trees that have been marked for chopping for three years, or gathering rocks or whatever else your people are ignoring.
> The third thing fits well here as Banished was very basic and you could reasonably expect how to get started after a couple playthroughs but this game shrugs off any notion of progress as you may or may not start with anything that allows you to produce a very necessary item; clothing. If you think in this game like you did in Banished - like building a trading port - you'll find the world doesn't care what you want and you need to be an oil-blessed prince to afford anything.
> Fourth thing is the research add-on, which is a nice way of saying it needs to be yanked out of the game, smacked until it gets its together, and put back in when it grows up. Every single unlock requires at least one other unlock, if not more, and you don't even have anyone available to man it. A few unlocks are crucial, but the game doesn't tell you that. A few unlocks are great, but you won't know that until you play long enough to gain enough points to unlock all five components, gather the resources, and squeeze out enough kids to man them. By which time your town has come down with the plaque...during a drought...and you're on fire.\t\t\t\t\t

as it stands, this is a fun and sometimes difficult city builder. in the early game it can take a while to get new citizens, especially if you build a school, so you will have to micromanage often who is working where to make sure you have enough food, heating, clothing and tools while you wait for your next emigrants.

after you survive the early stages you will be rewarded with a game that's far more complex than the art style might lead you to believe. the standouts are a deep skill tree that add more complexity to your city the deeper you go, and a trading system that grants you new resource systems and mega-structures as you develop a better relationship with the societies you choose to trade with. these innovations allow the game to compare favorably to banished and farthest frontier.\t\t\t\t\t
