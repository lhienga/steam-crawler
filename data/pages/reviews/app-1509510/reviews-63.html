\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDirect copy of Banished, which was desperately needed! A true survival city builder!

Survival City Builder
In Settlement Survival you have to guide people in the wilderness, build their homes, gather their food... Or rather said, assign buildings to be built by the people themselves and work the jobs. It's always a juggle of hunger, thirst, warmth, happiness and health. If one of these factors falls behind, the population will decimate. There are difficulty parameters to set beforehand to determine how strict these regimes are. In basis this is exactly Banished with some of its gamechanging mods.

The differences so far are a deep research tree, to drip feed new buildings with their mechanics. A lot of the mechanics of the Banished mods are incorporated, such as processing food into better food which still more hunger, and raw food straight up hurts people's health. There is also trade with factions which improves your relationship with them, which unlock powerful new buildings.

Early Access
The game in its core feels like some of the coding is copied from Banished. That game was such a huge success! I have played that one intensively, and returned a few times to the Steam page, hoping for a sequel. Now that game was developed by one person I think, and he self published as one of the first games ever! I always imagined that the guy bought an island somewhere from his Banished-money. Or I might have heard this rumour from Total Biscuit in his cooptional podcast. Anyway, I was hungry for a successor! If Settlement Survival did copy some of that game, it is what many people craved for.

The development of SS has frequent updates, which are massive improvements! Such as last update, which allowed buildings to be set an end product, and allowable resources to make that product. Before that patch you had to have multiple of the same buildings to craft with different resources. This new system allows for way better production chains, and ultimately for tightening the difficulty.

That said, my first experience with the game wasn't that good. The population kept on growing while a part of them didn't have any homes. I had to actively keep the people unhappy, in order to stabilise. The game has massively improved, and the full release should be soonish. The game is good enough to play in my opinion. There is a scenario for an Easter Islands experience, and the developers have more scenarios planned in the future. A good way to do multiple playthroughs.

The final point is that the game is a bit taxing for computers. There is a high res and a low res mode, but you have to pick on launch, and the settings aren't ported over. There are improvements possible in this portion.

Further conclusions
This game is what many people craved for! But at the same time, I remembered right away the limitations Banished had. The big one is that there is no height, or depth. Everything above the sea level plane is unworkable. Ten years ago this was okay, but in 2022 this feels outdated. The game desperately need building animations, there now isn't any. Every building has green grass beneath them, while it seems easy to make it at least brown.

So what one craves and reality is not always the same. I am very happy this game exists, and provides a basis for the genre to move forward! I know I know, at this moment there are more survival city builders to do this. The more the merrier!

79/100

I wish-listed this long ago, picked it up on a sale, and didn't realize until later that it's still early access!! I generally have been staying away from early access these days, but this game is in a pretty good place for EA; smooth and I encountered no bugs.

I enjoyed what little I played of Banished (maybe I need to go check it out again), and am enjoying this as well. It has some challenging supply chain management. I enjoy the game play loop of "solve one problem just to create two more" (Factorio, ONI, and Satisfactory are among my favorites) and this one seems to present that as well. The graphics are "cutsey," but don't let that deter you from the underlying game play. For those that might think this is like Cities: Skylines or those style of games...it's not. It operates at a different level - so many that like Cities might not like this one and vice versa; however, I tend to like both.\t\t\t\t\t
