\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this thinking "hey this looks like it could scratch my city builder itch" and next thing I know I've played 50+ hours in a week and my extreme difficulty mode run has 1.2k people living in it trucking along after several tornados, dust storms and plagues.Game's in a good state, although there's definitely things to be improved on, but it's a hearty recommend if you enjoy the genre - it looks nice and colorful if simple, there's great convenience features like 100% resources returned when demolishing a building which means it's not very punishing at all to remodel or rearrange things as you go (*note this costs a tech point investment, but very easily reached).

My main quibbles:

- The tech tree needs reworking. Some upgrades, most egregiously the one that allows you to craft traps, are relatively deep in the tree for something that is most useful in the very early game - by the time I have the tech points to spare, I've already been able to acquire all the animals from hunting, and it feels like ♥♥♥♥ to beeline for it when there's much more attractive options. Maybe if it was a level 1 tech and crafting required reeds or wood (you can justify wooden traps/snares surely) then it might be interesting, but as it is? Nah. There's a few others like this where the placement is awkward or just not very fun to work towards, so getting a tech point isn't "oh cool, I get a new thing" but "okay, I need one more before I can get [tech I actually could use right now that's deeper in]".

- The RNG can be real rough. Early game cubs capture is the big one since both fat and fur/leather are very useful for clothing/soap(->health), but a bad streak of lost caravans and/or natural disasters can ♥♥♥♥ up your whole thing. I had two flu plagues in two successive years before I got the tech upgrade to fix it, and let me tell you it was rough. This is arguably a feature though, some of us like suffering and you *can* adjust the frequency of disasters.

- The good old Banished-style population death wave when the old people all die en masse at the same time and you get an awkward lull while waiting for the kids to grow up but the lack of laborers/replacement for deaths throws a wrench in things. Less noticeable in the early game, but by the time I hit 250+ population and definitely now that I'm above 1k it's dearly felt.

Still a recommend. I'll be back playing it tonight and maybe I will finally, FINALLY figure out how to get my food supply chain in order.\t\t\t\t\t
