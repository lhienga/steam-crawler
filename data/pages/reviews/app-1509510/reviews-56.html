\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR
In general, I feel that the basic ideas are good but need balancing. The prerelease state resource tree is an excellent addition, and that alone, even if nothing else is added, justifies purchase. Ask the Banished dev if you can use his music/ambient sounds and give him credit/proceeds to cement this as the legacy and to fix the sound issues. Get the game now as it is fun (as of July 2022) and hopefully it will improve even more. Most of what you like in Banished is here already but there is a new resource tree model, and some ease of use additions like included mods built in.

Full review:
At first I didn't like the fact that they ripped off 90% of the game mechanics in Banished, a game which I adore and is made by a single dev. They are up front about this but that doesn't make it right to say 'inspired by' or big fan of, etc. in my book. Legally they are fine since only artwork and music can be copyrighted and game design can be copied (think of all the Minecraft clones and Doom clones). But In the spirit of game design I sincerely hoped they asked the developer of Banished for permission since the core buildings dependencies, resource mechanics, and user interaction are a cloned copy.

However, the banished dev made his game almost a decade ago and has not put much into it recently, so I believe the timing is fine with someone trying to improve on that format, if it is done with quality and purpose, honors the original, and adds enough to be necessary. Legacy questions aside, Is it fun and does it improve on Banished?

I do prefer the sound effects and music in the original. In Banished when you hovered over a group of buildings you would hear the combined sounds of all the shops together in that region. In Settlement you have to click on one building to hear it and then click off to turn it off. The original has several music tracks while this one has one that is good but it gets repetitive.

World generation is similar in both and you have the option to use seeds here too. You can pick your starting location here and also have the option later on to flatten mountains and create rivers which is a nice mod that is part of Settlement by default. You also have the option to allow more resources from mines and quarries so you only need one of each and can almost get by with that until you unlock the unlimited resource options for both. It is clear many popular Banised mod ideas are included in the core game here, which isn't surprising since the devs were also Banished mod authors. The artwork is good enough and has a cheerful tone to it, but not amazing, although it works fine. I do like the addition of a event bar that shows you when disasters and merchant ships are coming, as well as the seasons. Tornado disasters however are broken because they always go the same path (lower left to upper right) so I turned those off.

The resource discovery tree is best new actual improvement to the game, and allows you to see all the new ways to improve. You get discoveries with science points which are extremely slow to come by. I wish you could trade the merchant for some sort of research currency to speed this up. Some seeds and animals seem to be hard to get and I still havent figured how to get silver coins or explored trade. I feel the resource tree needs some work as there are too many paths and it isn't clear which ones give you new buildings vs giving you a resource boost. Perhaps scale it down to just buildings. The logistics tree is very confusing and overly complex for instance. Some items like reeds seem to be used early on but are hard to get, and domestic fuel is almost always out all the time which is frustrating. Food is extremely easy to have on hand though once you get a few crops and pastures, almost too easy.\t\t\t\t\t

If you liked Banished and want a great "2.0" version, Look no further. Really solid to play in its current state and haven't encountered a single bug so far while playing. Looking forward to watching this one develop and grow.\t\t\t\t\t
