\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like small portions of this, but there are some major flaws in the game that I doubt will ever be solved. I'll be posting my feedback in the discussions area about the major flaws but I'll go ahead and lay some things out now that point out why I will not recommend this game at this time.

Terraforming is free easy map editing with no checks or safeties. All you do is drag the river tool or the flatten tool around and bam, it creates a river or flatland, ignoring things like mines, quaries, or even structures. You can build rivers under your homes, you can flatten mines and quarries and they all still operate just fine. Its the most lazy feature in the entire game. Its instant and free too, completely breaks all immersion.

Inventory system appears to suck, terribly. Things get scrambled every time I load a save. I can have a mostly empty stockpile set to only accept Timber when I log out, then I load the save and suddenly there's a bunch of random resources in it like food or whatever. I have no idea how those things even got there. I think the game automatically just shoves everything into a random storage if something was over capacity somewhere. Not to mention the category of items sucks. Why would Ink be lumped in with Timber? Ink isn't wood. There's many items like this, placed in incorrect categories.

The cloud layer which at first was nice, became annoying, the same cloud over and over, going around in a circle. Then I discovered it was all clouds and their speed was based on how far from center they were. It turns out, its just a rotating image, the laziest method of clouds I have ever seen. Sure, its literally just a shadow generator but come on.

I purposefully decided to destroy my settlement, and turns out even after all the people die, nothing bad happens. You just wait years on in for immigrants to show up and start again. Oh and research keeps going up with no population at all.

There are certain other nuances that all suck, some people have mentioned them in reviews, and I've tried to comment on a few reviews that mention the wrong nuances that are actually accurate to some degree. But eh. One silly one is the latrines which claim to collect animal poop but if you don't have them, human poop shows up around your homes. These latrines are ridiculous too because of their wide range and worker count and other factors, its really, very silly how this game was designed. It feels to me like something that was rush created to get it out and make some quick cash. I dunno. If the developers would like to chat with me, I have all sorts of feedback about how they can improve things. But, who knows if I'm gonna keep going with this. I don't think there's any mods in this game that can fix some of this hilariously bad design stuff though. However, if they do want to improve things, I'll go ahead and post my various feedbacks in due time in the discussions, or wait to be contacted.\t\t\t\t\t

A borderline recommendation...
Pros: #1. It has a lot of depth. It's certainly not a casual-friendly game, since it has a rather large amount of specialization and upgrades and stuff that you can do via a tech-tree and various unlockables. This would be a con to casual players, but it's certainly a pro to me personally.
#2. When you start a game, you can generate a random seed for a map and you can randomize the bonuses on the map with a click of a button. This will appeal to those of us who like to min-max the starting position of a base.

Cons: #1. The realism of the game is out of whack eg. peas can survive up to a temperature of -2'C IRL yet in this game your entire pea crop is wiped out way before it reaches -2'C. This also has a knock-on effect which means that EVERY YEAR around winter, most of your crops will be wiped out completely, which is something that isn't made clear in the tooltips or tutorial of the game.
#2. There are various 'bonus spots' on the map, but the game doesn't tell you exactly how these spots function ie. does just one part of your building need to be touching the spot or does your entire building need to be inside the spot for the bonus to be active?
#3. It takes 9 real-life hours at 10x speed for a child to become an adult, which means it will take 90 real-life hours at normal gameplay speed... this is obviously hilariously broken. I've never played a colony sim where it takes 90 real-life hours for a child to become an adult.
#4. The game's tutorial is stupid and doesn't explain things properly and completely omits some very important information.
#5. The game has LOTS of grammatical mistakes and spelling errors.
#6. You can't change the resolution of the game at all on Borderless Fullscreen, and you can't even change the texture resolution either. So if your PC is too weak to run the game on Borderless Fullscreen, then your ONLY option is to quit the game, lower your desktop resolution to 1080p or 720p, and then start the game up again.
#7. The regional pricing of the game is really bad in multiple regions. The game is 400% too expensive in some regions.
#8. You can't cancel a prioritization of a building once you've set it to be prioritized.
#9. You can't set roads to be prioritized.
#10. You can't select ANY resources to see what they actually are (not wood, food, stone, iron, or ANYTHING).
#11. The food consumption of your settlers in the game is set way, way too high imo.
#12. Your buildings take about 5 years to get the state "Needs repair" but then less than 1 year after that to be completely broken and unlivable. This is stupid, as it gives you basically no time to respond to the "Needs repair" state.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSettlement Survival (SS) attempts to directly replicate Banished + Colonial Charter mod, right down to building chains and resources. This is not necessarily a bad thing, I would love a 'Banished II', but it has to be done right.

Several factors put me off SS in just over an hour. Worker animations are janky, world textures are often just blurry pastel colors and poorly translated English is present throughout the game and menus. The UI can sometimes bloat up to 1/3 the screen with info that was not requested or really necessary. Simple buildings are locked behind a tech tree, providing no real gameplay impact other than throttling what is already a very slow paced game.

Despite replicating the hugely popular Colonial Charter (CC) building chains almost directly, the complexity and variety of resources has actually been reduced. There are less crops, less produce variety, less craftable goods. This in turn lowers building complexity, for example in CC you cannot build town houses until you have glass, timber, bricks and building wares (think pewter cutlery & candlesticks) - you are stuck on log cabins at first. In SS you immediately build advanced looking buildings with no costs apart from timber. Maybe they import the glass and tiles, who knows?

This simplification of production chains and less resource variety is my biggest turn-off for the game. I want MORE staggering variety and complexity for a modern Banished-CC would-be successor, not less.

There are some bright spots in Settlement Survival. The Trade mechanic with other settlement leaders is very good, some of the building designs like the Hunters Cabin look great and in Winter snowfall the game looks genuinely pretty (the only time when the bright pastel colors worked for me).

Is this an upgrade over vanilla Banished? Err, I would say yes IF you enjoy the art style. It simply has more content than regular Banished. Is this a replacement for modded Banished? Sadly no, I could not recommend this to anyone who has ever enjoyed CC or the colossal MegaMod.\t\t\t\t\t
