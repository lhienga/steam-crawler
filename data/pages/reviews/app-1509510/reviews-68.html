\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a big fan of Banished and Anno this is really a fantastic game to add to the genre.
If you've played Banished you will slip into this game very very easily - but with more options and QoL tools and systems.
The trading system is really neat. You have the normal trading ships plus other factions you can trade and gain friendship with - this friendship is beneficial as it unlocks extra builds/resources otherwise unavailable. This is done via a caravan system which has its own risk/rewards system tied to it:
Add more staff to the convoy? Great! Its safer but slower
Less? Good as well - shortens your trip by a but the risk of death goes up.

The only minor criticism I have is the lack of minor events - they're there but not enough of them. But I'm sure this will be improved on as the devs are very active.

I advise playing the first few times without disasters as it can be challenging enough as is (even for folks who know the genre well).

Technically, I've come across no bugs or crashes as of the writing of this review so all good there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the least balanced city builders I've ever played. I genuinely wonder if the devs even play their game because it's just layers upon layers of broken mechanics, RNG, and tedium. It's a shame too, because this could be one of the best.

1) Essential components, like medicinal herbs, are buried in the tech tree.

2) Staffing buildings with even one citizen is impossible because right from the start the game forces dozens of production chains on you, far more than you're capable of handling even after 5 years.

3) The only way to get any degree of output from buildings is fully staffing them. Anything less and you're productivity is effectively non-existent. Upgrades to buildings only come after significant investments into the skill tree.

4) Population growth is abysmal. It's probably one of the most complained about mechanics of the game. Fertility rates are tied to happiness but to maintain happiness you have to give your people every possible luxury and goods as well as developed government services, but that requires a huge population. See the conundrum?

5) Very little is explained to you. Tech tree unlocks are confusing to figure out. One tech unlock allows you use a building, but another unlock in another tree unlocks the building. Half the time it's just trial and error figuring out what each unlock does further adding to frustrations when you society falls into a death spiral because you're missing a critical tech unlock that would keep you citizens alive.

6) Happiness if fickle. Any disruption in your settlement can send your citizens on a murderous rampage. Also, fertility is tied to stress, which, if you haven't guessed already, goes up if happiness is down. Stress also impacts worker performance. Which is worker performance is down your production chains are impacted which further drives stress. All this can be triggered by something as benign as bad RNG preventing you from unlocking a critical tech or discovering a plant or animal.
