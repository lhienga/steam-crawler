\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is relaxing. I am playing my first game on hard and found it adequately challenging. Had some crushes of food production although that can be solved with rotating dudes to farming in the fall. This is one annoying part of the game. In Dawn of Man for example, during harvest season, your whole village will automatically move everyone to harvest crops. This makes a lot of sense because whatever you don't harvest before winter starts gets wasted in this game (and also Dawn of Man). So when your field has max capacity of, lets say 2 workers, it is actually 1 worker for planting, and 2 for harvesting, and 0 for idle seasons (although here the worker automatically becomes a laborer so that's okay). So basically I have to half my farming workforce for spring, then double it for fall. So in the fall I empty out my processing/factory workers and force them into farmers, allowing me to max allocate a lot more farms than you usually could with static allocation. In a such a setup I get so much food I can have more than enough; and it also seems necessary since without this trick I was getting killed and starving half my town above 100 people. Anyway, I understand the point of this dynamic, but having to do it myself every year is a bit annoying that's all.

In summary, it's a better and more complicated version of Banished/Dawn of Man. I've played ~10 hours on the one town on hard, and there is plenty of new materials/tools, and tech for me to get, so that's interesting to look forward to. I might finish this run, or just do a new town on most severe difficulty. Either way it's fun.

One more thing I would prefer is more adult graphics, but I knew what I was getting into when I got the game so the cartoonish graphics were expected.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot bad, not that good either.

After 16 hours of gameplay I have hard time to come up with a reason to actually keep playing. The game itself is not bad, but it's about the same as every other settlement game out there. Decided to start this on the hardest difficulty and apart from the tough start (since I decided to skip tutorial and din't realize you can adjust the max worker count on each workplace), there was really no challenge to this what so ever. The biggest challenge is to keep up with having enough warehouses to store all your products. But as said, after a certain point (which comes very early in the game) there is just no way you can fail in this. I suppose you could keep playing to gain those few extra production buildings, but then again, whats the point... Yes you can start to product some extra goods, but then again, you don't really need them to thrive.

There is really nothing wrong with the game other than there really is nothing to play towards to.

If you can get it on sale, then maybe its worth it, but I really wouldn't pay the full price.\t\t\t\t\t
