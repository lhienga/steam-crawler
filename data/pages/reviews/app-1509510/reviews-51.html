\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love city building games and have quite a few, I wouldn't consider myself a hardcore expert but this is my take on the game.

What I liked:
- Friendly interface. Easy to find buildings given the categories.
- Buildings would require materials/production from other buildings. (this is true for most of these kind of games but it's REALLY important here)
- Random disasters were a good challenge, forced you to play in a diverse way (not having only farms/orchards)
- Natural regen. Trees and bushes regrow if there's space. Which fills up empty spots in your city and adds a nice touch.
- Terraforming option. You can more or less customize the map (add/remove rivers and lakes). You can remove mountains but you can't build them, makes sense.
- Seasons and day/night cycles. Made the game a little more dynamic
- Cute and simple graphics. Not heavy on the computer (until you fill the map with buildings).
- Trading option is fun. Trading is almost a must in this game to unlock seeds and obtain livestock that you wouldn't normally get. (very hard to find seeds without trading). Encourages players to set up a trade system where you gotta sell a lot of stuff (also perks depending on the faction you trade with, huge bonuses)

What I didn't like:
- Reinforcing buildings against disasters or adding upgrades/furniture was tedious mid-late game as you have to individually click on buildings to do so. (when you have over 100 buildings you can imagine)
- Can't upgrade most buildings. You have to destroy/remove them first then place the upgraded building (this messes with logistics and leaves people homeless or leaves you in a deficit for materials for a bit )
- Hard to manage logistics/people. Starting out is very simple and easy, but once you get past 500 people it becomes a nightmare trying to reassign people to work at nearby jobs. The delivery stations you have to manually add resources to it, which can get complicated if there are many buildings nearby (should be automatic based on nearby buildings)
- The tech tree isn't very expansive and if you spam schools/research stations then you fly through it.

Overall:
This game is fun while it lasts. After about 35 hours I feel like I've done everything there is to do, there is still sandbox mode and story mode but those don't interest me. This was one of those games I couldn't stop playing, but got bored (and a little frustrated with people management) towards the end of the game. I paid about $18CAD for the game and would say that it's worth it. The game is still in early access and I'm hoping that they add some more elements to make the game more fun, as well as improve the logistics management.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game appears to be Banished with low-poly graphics. Almost everything appears to be an exact duplicate of Banished, including all the problematic features. The upside of the low-poly graphics is that it likely enhances performance when your city gets big and crowded, the downside is a lot of the buildings aren't all that distinctive. That being said, the style is generally easier on the eyes than Banished, but at the cost of buildings not looking appropriate to the level of technology in their construction.
The game seems to want a lot of min-max from the player, but does not seem to supply him with the necessary data to do this in an organized way.
While I understand that this is a game, it IS a simulation game, and the devs would do well to ask themselves in what way their game might correspond to real-world rules and expectations rather than slavishly imitating Banished. As much as I like/d Banished, there were quite a few irritating things about the design, some of which were baked in the cake and could not be modded out. Settlement Survival seems to have copied many of these problems, but I have no idea if they are hard-coded or might be susceptible to third-party correction.
As of yet, there seem to be very few mods available for the game, but more than I would have expected for a game still in early access.
Overall, I'm not hating this game, and if you like survival/city-builders and you don't have Banished, I recommend this. If you do have Banished, there is no particularly good reason to buy this game at this time\t\t\t\t\t
