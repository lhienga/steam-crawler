The developer's response below has left me feeling optimistic, perhaps there's more engagement in other channels than steam I need to look for, but I will try again in a few weeks and hopefully the game will be up and running!

-----

In this review I'll try to keep in mind that this is an early access game. I don't mind buying & playing those at all, but there is a trade-off...either you play full price and get a playable game that as a community you can shape, or it's sold 'cheap' and you accept it may be buggy as you're part of the testing programme. Well, this game is buggy, crashes and because of how it works it can become downright unplayable...and it's not cheap at all!

I really like the idea of what the game is trying to be, but it quickly confuses itself. You don't really get to play with the bus network much, as this is (understandably) costly and you can't just buy & sell a bunch of buses willy nilly to create these different lines. I suppose you can in sandbox, but then where's the challenge of the game then? I could print off a map and get some crayons for far less money! No, your time is mostly spent managing your bus depot. It's really mostly a depot sim game, not that the advertising makes it look that way. Yes, there's the maps, the route design, timetable making - don't get me wrong, there's a lot of other stuff there - you just don't get to use it much.

To get a bus back on the road, once it arrives, you need to wait for it to park up, then tell someone to come and clean it, then ask someone to take it and fuel it, then once they've parked it back up, tell them to take it and run it through the wash, then once they've parked it again (and looked at you annoyed for making them drive up and down), you can tell them to take it to the workshop to repair the router. Yes, the router, that important engine part... I'm being a little over the top of course, the buses don't need to be in perfect condition every time, and the fuel tanks tend to last for a couple of days. But why not get them cleaned when the alternative is to sit there watching buses sit parked doing nothing.

Whilst I didn't expect this game to be a high-octane action-packed thriller, I did expect to be able to build on what I have. Perhaps a small bus company may operate like this, but you'd think you'd be able to research and develop new skills/people/facilities - for example hiring an "inspector" to check each bus as it comes in, and send a cleaner if needed, or a "shunter" to fuel and park buses, maybe "workshop parking" where a driver would leave a bus that needs fixing, or a "cleaning bay" on the way in so buses are cleaned before parking. Essentially, something to help develop and automate the processes, so you're planning/assessing/optimising as a player, not clicking the same frustrating processes, multiple times per bus per day.

Of course, this could be part of the future development that's built on the feedback from players. But we haven't had any announcement from the developers in a couple of weeks now, and whilst some update was downloaded, it's just made the game crash for me. Not too promising.

Putting the future aside, the game as it is now,is basically playable (for most players). There are a lot of bugs, a crash every couple of hours but (at least until the last update) you can use it at a basic level. Unfortunately it quickly becomes significantly more difficult as you progress. The infrequent bugs become more commonplace (e.g. a random parking glitch is a pain for a small depot, but essentially game-breaking in a large depot when it keeps reoccurring and jams everything up unavoidably) and the poor design of some aspects really shows through.

As an example, each vehicle type has it's own foibles - but the game doesn't tell you. For example, if you watch on the map, some clearly have different speeds than others. The amount of fuel used can also differ hugely, whilst this hasn't yet (for me) resulted in a bus running out of fuel, it's something that could be better shown at purchase - for example, MPG, Range, Speed, Acceleration, etc could help you choose the most suitable vehicles. Likewise, each shift has 2 values for the number of passengers, they can vary wildly, and there's no explanation of what each is. Even if you can work out more about each bus, you'd be clueless as to what size vehicle to use!

One of the most painful aspects of poor design, especially given how much time you spend in the depot, is how the vehicles drive about. It's bonkers, simply bonkers. Some of it, for example driving through walls and the back end of the buses flailing about like a fish just feels lazy, and is perhaps forgiven as "they'll neaten up the graphics for release". Other things just make no sense...the roads are wide enough for 2 buses, so why on earth do buses drive down the middle of the road, stop with the front ends a few feet apart, then slowly drive through each other in order to pass?!? It's not just a poor graphics issue, clearly it's intentional design as they do slow, stop then drive slowly. Someone decided this was the best way to do things...is that person's experience of buses limited to the Knight Bus from Harry Potter? Once you have a few dozen buses this poor design means your depot will go into meltdown when buses are moving about as gridlock quickly forms...which wouldn't be half as bad if the buses didn't need to keep adding to the traffic to get fuel, or for a wash, or to replace their routers.

The idea of the game is great, 'transport sim/tycoon' is a huge and popular genre and the ability to use real maps of anywhere in the world is brilliant. It also opens the game up to those outside the genre, who fancy being able to play with their local areas. Unfortunately the execution is poor and the game is not that reliable. The developers have been engaging with the community, although they seem to have gone quiet, but it's probably not meaningful engagement (e.g. running 'Which bus should we add' contests when there is some real problems with the base game).

I'd like the game to work, as overall I do enjoy it and I like the idea. But if/once it is made to work, in my opinion, there's a lot of improvements needed to make it anything more than a limited 'depot sim'. Perhaps at that point it would be worth the asking price, but for now - I think I'd rather have not bought it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun game to start with and has occupied me for 12 hours or so. I suspect in its current form I wont go much beyond 25 hours,as I'm reaching the "grind" point a lot of these simulations have.
The game is clearly divided into two parts, strategic (setting up routes, prices, and hiring staff) and micro (running the depot in excruciating detail).
Strategic: In a city I know well its good to see if I can outdo the local RL operators, but the ticket pricing is too simplistic and discourages expansion beyond city edges to other towns, which in RL make sa lot of money. Buying a bus is a blind decision,all you really know is the capacity and purchase cost not running costs.
Micro: The single Depot is completely down to you; size, layout etc, as is recruitment (also a bit simplistic). Having to manually instruct every aspect of bus cleaning washing, fuelling, repairing etc is Ok for ten buses, getting a chore at 20 and will bore me to tears at many more! As is instructing every shift for every line, sometimes several times a day
Summay: Good fun for now but I can see an end soon unless an update adds several new features.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPromising little management game that deliberately doesn't attempt to overreach itself and does well as a result. You build a bus company from scratch, setting up its depot and planning routes in real maps, buying and researching new buses to meet increasing demands, setting timetables to maximise economy while avoiding overcrowding, and sorting out basic maintenance for your vehicles so they stay clean and mobile.

While you can choose any city in the world to set up in, there's no real difference in appearance, bus availability (this does change across continents), or your tendered contract offers. You can't scroll outside of the depot area, because you can play on any map and it's unreasonable for a budget level game to be that ambitious in scope. It's not as if it makes no difference where you play - the map is populated according to actually-existing local businesses and houses, so sprawling suburbs can be hard to manage as you search for points of interest or heavier populated locations to place stops. Working out routes that navigate complicated one-way city centres is a nice puzzle, too.

It's in early access at time of writing but there's a full enough game to enjoy so far. There are some gripes, like overall data not being presented in single tables meaning it's quite hard to manage a large fleet - you can see overall condition but not whether the lower conditions are because of lower cleanliness, which can be ignored temporarily, or broken parts, which can't. There are a few little bugs here and there too, but in 25 hours I haven't encountered anything that broke the game.

Definitely worth a look if you like management and transport games.\t\t\t\t\t
