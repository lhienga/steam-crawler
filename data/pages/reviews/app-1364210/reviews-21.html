\r\n\t\t\t\t\thaving great fun with this game once you get the hang off it, played around 12h so far setting up Bus routes in Bristol. Polish needed in places and some of the micro could maybe be reduced, right now you have to clean every bus by manually assigning a cleaner and tell every bus to refuel,. these actions cannot be queued up, while 1 bus is fueling you can't send another so this can be tedious but something I am sure the developers will change this is early access.

You can micro-manage right down to which driver drives which bus or have it auto-select drivers and bus's for your bus routes which is nice (Note this requires the scheduler be researched)

The game is a little slow I may not have got the formula with routes/ bus's just right yet but a day lasts quite a while and the money comes in slowly, get occasional challenges such as sell 12000 tickets that give a cash boost on completion but it can take several hours to buy enough bus's to add another route, there is also a sandbox mode where you start with loads of cash, this is not a big deal.

Seen some comments about the price, £22 (currently 10% off for early access launch) is hardly AAA game price, what do people expect... Its well worth it even in its current state.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is actually really fun. i mean when i bought this , i was expecting i would be refunding it , but then i really enjoyed it.

Goods :
- You can literally play any map , ANYWHERE in the world you want.
- Good amount of bus's to choose from.
- You can build your company from the ground up.
- You have sandbox mode for those why just wanna play without earning money.
- You have a custom paint booth and you can even import decals from your computer via the in-game.
- Game has not crashed once yet. for a Early Access game , i am impressed on how stable it is.

Bads :
- Music is so annoying.
- Game NEEDS automation for mid-late game for Fueling, cleaning , and washing bus's.
- Zoom/tilt camera really sucks. it needs more freedom and/or needs to be able to zoom out more.
- Mid/Late game has a stutter like the game has trouble keeping up. (Depending on city , population , and how many bus's you have running)

Ugly :
- Fueling , Cleaning , And Washing buses , this is So bloody annoying. Each bus has 4 options that you need to service... Fuel - Clean interior - Clean Exterior - And vehicle condition. you'd think that one employee would be smart enough to service all needs of the bus with one click but no , you have to click each one , wait for a worker to service , click again , wait for worker , click again , wait for worker......OMFG !!! this alone makes me not wanna play the game.

Closing :
This game is a really fun game and if you're the business management type of player , you will love this one.

WISH LIST ITEMS :
- Automation system where Buses/Employee's just know to go to work on re-fueling , cleaning , and/or washing when the bus returns to the yard. There is so much to juggle and this takes up a HUGE part of your time and it's super annoying clicking over and over and over on every single bus.
After you have x-amount of routes , Buses , and tasks going on , it is super hard to keep up by clicking one bus over and over and over to get it ready for it's next shift.
- I wish the game had a Auction option for buying and selling buses. would be really cool.
- I wish there was a Buy multible buses at once option.
- Would be cool to have random Special employee's i could hire.
- Would be nice to edit bus routes without having to delete the whole route just to add a stop in the middle somewhere.

Is it worth a buy ?? YES
Game is easy to pickup and play. in about 20-30 minutes you'll be playing this game without issue.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEsse meio que dói no coração negativar, mas vamos aos fatos:

O Jogo é muito legal, você poder criar uma empresa de ônibus na sua própria cidade, você baixa o mapa do Brasil, e o seu bairro estará lá. Você pode criar rotas que você conhece, das empresas que você conhece e quem sabe gerenciar as coisas melhor do que a empresa verdadeira? Porém... o jogo meio que morre na proposta dele bem rápido.

Vamos começar pelo o preço... 66? Sério? Esse jogo deveria valer 30 pelo o que ele oferece. Acredite, não há muita coisa aqui, o que é justamente o outro ponto desse game: Não existe desafio, o progresso dele é bem rápido e você vai desbloquear as coisas mais importantes nas primeiras horas.

Não existem concorrentes no mapa, então você é livre pra ganhar rios de dinheiro, criando rotas atrás de rotas até você literalmente cansar (que é o que vai acontecer), depois que você automatiza a parte de "macro" gerenciamento, não resta muito pra você fazer além de ficar criando rotas novas, e clicando em todos os ônibus pra saber qual precisa de limpeza, combustível, ou reparos. E isso depois de um tempo... fica chato.

As "missões" do game não tem muito sentido e se você fazer elas ou não, não tem nenhum tipo de interferência no seu pseudo progresso.

Ai Krose, mas o jogo é um Early Access... Sim ele é, mas ele é menos que isso, ele atualmente é um pré-alpha, não me leve a mal, existe muito espaço para melhorar aqui. O Gerenciamento é muito simplificado, você sempre terá sucesso e sempre ganhará mais e mais dinheiro, sem nenhum tipo de risco, sem precisar pesar as suas escolhas, como por exemplo: Uma rota que não da dinheiro, ônibus que bate e precisa ficar encostado, fazendo que você quebre a cabeça em como arrumar a escala, ou motoristas que ficam doentes e não vão trabalhar. Você não precisa se preocupar com absolutamente nada disso, apenas crie a rota, e deixe o ônibus rodando para ver o dinheiro entrar.

Na situação que ele está hoje eu não recomendo, talvez daqui há 1 ano, depois de mais alguns patchs, se ele não for abandonado no meio do caminho.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have only played this for an hour, but it looks very promising! I never imagined that this kind of game would be made, it feels just the type of game I would've made if I'd be a game developer. I won't do a full review just yet, because it's still early access, but I'll point out a few bugs or suggestions:

- There's a big red exclamation mark over every chair or table I have placed. They're all facing the correct side so this may be a bug?
- There doesn't seem to be an option for a staff car park, I think this would be important because they can't all cycle to work.
- I think it would be good if there would be different sized roads, not just the one standard size.
- I would like if the rush hour time window could be adjusted. In the game the evening rush hour is between 16-19, but I live in Finland where the rush hour is more like 14-17, so I'd like to adjust that for my game.
- Why can't I place objects side by side? For example with the lockers, there's always a small gap between each locker, which looks stupid and takes up space.
- The smallest frequency for bus intervals should be less than 10 minutes, in my home town many lines run with intervals of 7-8 minutes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has a good idea, but the current implementation of it leaves a lot to be desired. The game hinges heavily on micromanaging a depot, and where I wanted to play a game where I create a fictional bus network and see how it does, I instead found myself micromanaging things I didn't want to manage in the first place for approximately 85% of the time spent playing the game.

The game starts with having to chose a location for the depot, and from there on is then permanently focused on a 3D view of said depot. The game wants you to build various kinds of rooms - A service center, a corridor, an office, a kitchen, a bathroom, and required amenities for each room. You also have to build the parking spaces for each bus you will buy, a gas station to refuel the buses, and a workshop to maintain them. You then need to hire the staff required to run your company. Finally, you can create bus routes on the map. But once a route is created, you get a list of shifts and you have to assign each shift to a bus and to a driver, for every day of the week. You can unlock a button to do this automatically later on but the game initially expects you to do all this by hand, for every shift, bus, driver and day of the week. You also need to provide each employee with their personal locker, and office staff need their own dedicated desk as well. In the end, the actual creation of bus routes seems like an after thought. You don't even see your buses run until you look at the map.

I encountered numerous gameplay issues while playing the game, and only time will tell if the developers will solve these in their game:
- The game has a tutorial in the form of a list of tasks to complete to build your depot, but it makes no effort in explaining the UI to you. The game just expects you to figure out how to do the things it asks you to do on your own.
- When building road pieces in the depot, the road pieces can only be built in a grid layout that's much larger than the grid used for all other objects. This quickly makes it difficult to build an efficient depot layout
- The starting area of the depot is rather small, and I was able to fit maybe 20 parking spaces in it if I really tried my best. The game has an option to expand the depot through research, but I still feel that the starting depot should be larger.
- The game's UI has no option for doing things in bulk. Need 6 buses for your route? you'll have to open the marketplace UI, click the bus you want, customize it, and finalize the purchase, 6 times. There's no option to just tell the game I want 6 buses of the same color and decoration. The game does have an option to save liveries, but to apply a livery, you have to click "load", then "browse local templates", then click the livery you want, then click apply. That's still 4 clicks to apply a livery, and 6 clicks for each additional bus I want to purchase, or 36 clicks just to buy 6 buses for a route.
- When creating a bus route, there's no insight into what drives demand. You just have to make a route, and at the end you'll see how many passengers are expected for each trip. This means there's no strategy for building good routes besides just trying something and seeing what the game says at the end.
- At the end of a shift, the buses need to be fuelled and cleaned. I have to click each bus to send them to the fuel station and get them cleaned myself, at the end of every day. This is not something I want to have to manage, in real life I'd hire employees to take care of this for me, as most managers would. This is something that staff is perfectly capable of handling themselves and it makes no sense for the game to expect anyone to put up with this.
- Employees have a friendliness and a reliability stat, but the game doesn't care to explain how those stats affect your game at all.
- When creating bus routes, the path finder seems to use the rules for private cars. Roads that are for buses only in real life cannot be used in the game. Road obstacles that only allow buses to pass cannot be routed across at all either. Even obstacles that only close during the night cannot be routed across. Playing in a city that makes heavy use of dedicated bus right of ways and sends private cars to the edge of the city, this made it practically impossible to create any sensible bus routes as my buses had to go around the city as well.
- When creating bus routes, it's possible to create a custom stop, but after plopping one down, there's no way to delete it. The stop can be removed from the bus route but the stop itself remains on the map forever.
- The entire gameplay loop is basically focused on building your depot and micromanaging your staff, their planning, and the maintenance of your vehicles. As a result the game is permanently focused on a 3D view of the depot and the map is only used for creating routes, and ocassionally for looking at where your buses are during the day. The actual premise of the game, creating and running bus routes feels like an afterthought.

All in, I think the game is currently not worth the asking price of €28 EUR. In quality I think the game is closer to a €10-.€15 game, and even then it still has fundamental gameplay issues that need to be ironed out.

To the developers of this game, if they are reading this review:
It's clear that a lot of time has gone into developing the depot features of the game, but harsh as it may be, I think this was the wrong focus for a game of this type as the game is promoted as "create and run your own bus network", while the gameplay feels like it's "Build your own bus depot and micromanage your employees, their planning, and the bus maintenance". I bought this game hoping to create fictional bus lines and see how the network would do, but instead i'm micromanaging all kinds of things I didn't want to manage in the first place.

It might be a better idea to make the depot gameplay optional or at least make it much easier and simpler to work with. For one It would be great if the game had prebuilt rooms that we can plop down to just get started quickly. And please reduce the amount of repetitive tasks. I think it makes no sense to have to fuel and clean each bus at the end of the day being the manager of the company. Staff can do take care of this themselves.

The planning bit of the game, assigning shifts to buses and drivers to shifts also gets repetitive rather quickly once I've got a few bus routes going or have a route with a high frequency and lots of buses. The "automatically assign everything" button shouldn't be something that is unlocked later on in the game. Ideally, I'd even say introduce an option to have staff be hired automatically and remove the need to place a locker for each member of staff. Not a single company I've worked for provided me a locker. Personal desks do make sense but to be honest I think even that's a bit much micromanagement for a game.

For some inspiration in how you might focus more on "create and run your own fictional bus network", please look at the game "Nimby Rails", which basically does this for rail networks. I think more focus on creating and running routes and less focus on micromanaging all the little things that a real life manager would need to manage would be a good thing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn't quite what I was expecting: it's more about managing the business than the bus routes. That said, I'm still enjoying it, just having to adapt my expectations.

The "bus route logistics" include planning the routes, the stops, and the service pattern. This tells you how many buses you'll need, and therefore how many drivers. There is some opportunity to customise the timetable, e.g. different shedules on weekends, and there are several different types of vehicle to choose from, which can be painted any colour you choose using an RGB colour palette. But that seems to be about all you can do.

The rest of the time I am spending buidling and designing my depot, hiring staff, setting their shift patterns, sending them on training courses, etc., and cleaning, refuelling, and repairing the buses. In the initial (early access) version of the game this needs to be done on a day-to-day basis however there has been some feedback to suggest it would be better if much of it could be automated, e.g. set a cleaning or maintenance rota for each vehicle.

I'm thinking about playing this as a day-to-day business management game at the moment, rather than a public transport game. I pause the game at the end of a game-day and then plan what I need to do with my staff and vehicles before the next day. So far I've only created two routes, and already have seven drivers whose development, happiness, wages, etc I have to think about, and six buses to maintain, when I'd rather be thinking about where I should be adding new routes.

The graphics are good for what they are supposed to be. There isn't the realism of Cities Skylines or Transport Fever 2, but those aren't played on real-world maps, of course. That said, it would be nice to be able to see the depot from street level, especially since managing the depot is such a big part of the game.

As others have said, there are a lot of minor issues still to be resolved, perhaps more than might be normal in an early access game from the bigger developers. But this is a small, independent team (3 brothers) and they've done an amazing job in putting this game together. It's a good game as it is, with the potential to be oustanding.\t\t\t\t\t

There are a lot of bugs in the game. The graphics and textures lean toward being rather simple; the price of the game would make most people tend to believe that there was more ambition in this area (pretty common for most simulation games), but I think it may be punching slightly above its weight when concerning the current price.

I'm still curious to see where this goes, but for the immediate future I think it will be something that sits in my library and I'll follow the patch notes to decide when to pick it up again. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has an interesting combination of micro- and macro-management. The macro side is the service planning: building routes and balancing service frequency with demand on a real-world map. I haven't tried a larger city yet, but the ~50,000 population town I started with has reasonably realistic ridership numbers and commuting patterns. It's fun to play virtual service planner in a location you know in real life!

The micro side consists of the depot management. You assign drivers and vehicles to shifts (manually at first, and later automatically) while balancing fleet, facility, and staffing needs against the service you've planned. This bit, in combination with the service planning, is where the magic happens. Want to increase frequency on a popular line to meet demand? You'll need new vehicles and staff, which come with a cascading set of requirements. You'll need more parking- oh, and now your fueling station is too busy so you'll need another, and now you need more mechanics, who need more office space... and so on. There's a lot of small goals to keep you engaged.

I'll also note the devs came up with a clever system regarding revenue- in real life, bus fares almost never cover the full cost of operations (the exact amount of "fare recovery" can vary, but in the US we're talking typical figures of 20% or less per operating hour). The remaining cost is typically subsidized by taxes. Most transit management games tend to ignore this nuance, but City Bus Manager has a clever solution- the city pays you extra money for each fare you collect. That keeps the tycoon-style gameplay intact while keeping things more realistic. Does this matter in practical terms? Well not really... but I thought it was a nice touch.

I didn't realize until writing this review that the devs also made the Busbetrieb (Bus Company) Simulator OMSI 2 add-on. That explains why this game feels more robust than a typical first effort.

If you like transit tycoon games and don't mind some micro, City Bus Manager's worth a look.\t\t\t\t\t
