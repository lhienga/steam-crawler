For me this game has too much micromanagement but that does not make this game bad and I still am playing it as I can mostly ignore the micromanagement most of the time.

Pros:
Great to be able to do this in a real city
Quite a fun concept
Good level of detail if you like micromanagement

Items for improvement:
Repetitive, For instance you cannot send 4 buses to get refuelled if you have less than four fuel pumps. There is no queuing of tasks.
It is hard to see if a bus needs something until it is really bad.
I do not understand why the office has be so many blocks large to hire so many people. Often a bus depot is small, This is perplexing.
Scheduling day by day and only a week in advance is a bit tedious.

For an early access game I really enjoy it. I see a huge amount of potential. For Macro management Sim fans this is going to frustrate you, for micro management fans this will probably be enjoyable especially if they add more features to the game.\t\t\t\t\t

I could probably forgive these issues and play in some random city that works, but the current pricetag is way too steep given the serious issues with map loading and a general absence of content. If you want to pay $30 to look at a map, there are better games out there.

--Stress management. When your drivers drive a shift, they accumulate stress. The only way to counter it is to have more drivers than you have shifts, that way they can relax between them.
