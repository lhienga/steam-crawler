\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow where to start! Well, I just happened upon this game by chance a few weeks ago and added it to my Wishlist. And since the only other game that I can compare it to is Nimby Rail I will use it as a comparison. It was right up my alley and I am use to playing similar games like Nimbi Rails that have the same concept and work off of Open maps. I pulled the trigger on it the other day and from the start I was hooked. Before I knew it 3hrs had passed. If you play Nimbi Rails you will have a good concept on how this game works. But unlike that game that has no major building restrictions you are limited to using roads for your routes which is logical because well you are using buses instead of trains. That is where the similarity ends. While the rail game does let you tweak a lot of things and is very immersive, this game allows a good bit of micro managing and let’s to tweak things but it more streamlined and you don’t become bogged down with so many tasks and it makes the game more enjoyable and lets you get down to business and that is building a profitable bus empire.

Pro’s

- I love that the game has a perfect amount of micro managing to keep the game fun. A few more features that let you micro manage a little more than what is game is welcome.

- I love that the game already has a wide selection of beautifully modeled buses.

- You can design your buses anyway you like with built in bus editor you have a great set of tools to design your fleet just the way you want and make it stand out. You can even upload your design directly to the workshop which is already up and going and has hundreds of cool bus designs to use. Each different model of bus will need to be manually created for that particular bus but once you do one you can simply add the paint scheme to all future buses of the same model. And if you feel like changing up your fleet design, simply send your bus to the paint shop that you build and give your fleet a fresh new look. ******My favorite feature about bus design is you can upload any Jpeg image to create a decal for your bus. So, if you want to make your own logo out of game using a logo maker and then put it on your fleet or an image from a real fleet bus you can and it is very easy*******

- You get to build your bus empire in real world cities! If you want to build your station on the road you live on you can! The map also has points of interest highlighted.

- You get to build your terminal and design it the way you want and you get a decent size plot to do it on and through research you can expand your property to facilitate more workers and buses.

- The game feels familiar and does not take long to learn what tabs are what and the building feature is fun and is similar to building a house like in the Sims.

Con’s

- The maps are constrained and although you can unlock and expand the map through research it is still very limited. I would like to have a globe like in Nimbi rails where you can zoom in to the map like in nimbi rails so there is no wait time or loading when you expand territory ***THIS WAS VERY ANOYING*** I would like to have the ability to switch between map layers Nibi Rails uses a Map that has far more buildings in it and also has population density. Now real satellite imagery would be nice and it is mentioned in the description of the game that would be amazing because it would let you see the area of where you are placing your stop.

- In building a line it seems like points of interest are the main way to get passengers. I would like to see people taking the bus to work. If I put a bus stop in game now in a neighborhood it is not used by commuters going to work etc.

- I don’t like the fact that if you put a bus stop down next to a but bank and the stop is named that and then you put down another stop on the same block Infront of a different business and it is also called bb&t. I know you can rename the stops but it would be awesome if the game would name the business or address where the stop is located instead of using the most prominent business, school, church etc. on the block

- You can only build one depot for your region so you cannot set up multiple terminals in different towns to service them and expand your business. If you set up a line in another city then your bus has to travel all the way from your depot. The game seems more aimed at a city-to-city bus which is cool I like the concept of a commuter bus like grey hound but I would like to see more importance shown to picking up passengers in town and moving them around town “Innercity” lines and then lines that take you from one place to another and a big thing I would like is to be able to have people buy tickets for a bus ride out of town even across state lines similar to grey hound and south Eastern coaches. You could then build a depot in big and small cities around the country where people can buy tickets to travel long distances.

- *******The description says real time but the game days run very fast at normal speed. I would like to have an option to have the game run in actual real time. So, the time it takes a bus to get from one point to another is how long it takes in game. Nimbi Rails has this feature and it would be so much fun. There is also a air transport game that when you log out your aircraft are still traveling so you can pop in from time to time and click depart etc. This would be a cool feature. ******** REAL TIME IN GAME IS MY #1 REQUEST DEVS*******

- The time table is very simple In nimby rails you get to choose how long a train stays at the station before it leaves. I like that the game calculates how many buses you will need based on how frequent the line runs but I would like to see a break down hour-by-hour min by min and the ability to add as many buses as I want to a line.

To sum it up this is a very well-rounded game exsp for an EA game. There are a ton of features in game and being an EA game, you know they are some great things to come. I like that the devs are interested in what we really think and are open to suggestions “there is a suggestions button just for that”. The developers are very active in discussions and take the time to respond to everyone. I have and I am sure all you have as well purchased many EA games that were just barely playable littered with bugs and the developers were silent and released a small update maybe once or twice before they ghost with our money. You know the ones I am talking about. lol Dang cash grabbers. So the fact that the game is being developed by 3 brothers in there newly formed business “WHICH IS COOL BTW” getting to work with your brothers and the fact they are very active made me feel good about buying this game. Something I look for before I buy is how active the devs are and how many bugs they fix and how quickly they answer our questions or address issues we have.

This game is very fun and it scratches an itch for games like this that I have. My number one request to the developers is to have the option for REAL TIME IN GAME! Nothing is wrong with the speed the game runs now and it prob works good for people who don’t have much time to play. But for retired veterans like me I have all the time in the world. And would like to be able to run the game in real time. I can’t wait to see where this already awesome game is headed! It has nowhere to go but UP! Guys you are going to love this game like I said I have played only 3hrs and out of all the hundreds of games I own it became my favorite game in just 3hrs. I have yet to run into any bugs! You don’t need a long tutorial to learn how to play it either the game is very intuitive and easy to navigate! So what are you waiting for? And why are you still reading this? Go buy the game already!! It is worth every penny so just pull the trigger already and click buy! Your still here? Go check out and play this awesome game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is certainly worth it for Early Access. I began the game at smooth performance with freezes, so set my settings to lowered ones (not a surprise there, considering I only had half the ram I usually had (4gb), I'm getting 16gb ram this christmas however). It overall worked out well, with glitches every now and then. After expanding my company and fleet, it feels it's a bit to easy. Everything is too fast faced I feel, I'm already on gold and only got it almost 3 weeks ago, albeit me playing several hours at a time may explain it. It is clear that the game has potential and I certainly am hoping for a good future, but don't recommend it should you not be happy with issues with performance. These have been reported a lot so the developers are aware. I also think there needs to be larger variety of vehicles for company, improved tools for management (like being able to select a bus for cleaning, whilst that bus is being fueled, for example), possible shut downs or downscaling if you go bankrupt (at the moment, it seems you can just make yourself out of it and that's the end of it), etc. But considering it's early access, this will all probably come in time and I can only wish the developers the best of luck with their project. Also when scheduling buses, it would be nice if we could put certain types of buses as priority for routes.

In short, if you have an interest in management, buses or both, and don't mind issues with performance every now and then, this is 100% worth it. It's different, it's fun, it's great and has a bold future. The developers are friendly and have acknowledged all bugs and made efforts to resolve them. If you have less an interest, and don't particular have patience for freezes or just overall waiting, perhaps this is a game to pass.
