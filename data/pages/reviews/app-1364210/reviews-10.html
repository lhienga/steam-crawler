\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm conflicted on whether I should give this game a recommendation or not. I felt a negative review was a bit harsh on the developer, but there are many improvements that could be made.

Currently as it stands, the game has good customisation and the real world map works really quite well. The early game is great and forms a good little lite business management sim - however the longer a company operates the more tedious and repetitive the game becomes as it expands. Here's a few things that, in my opinion, might improve the game overall.

1. Put less emphasis on the 3D bus depot. When you have a hundred staff members the game gets quite laggy, even on a good rig. That said the depot works well for early game play, however it eventually gets incredibly tedious when you have 30+ buses and 100+ staff to micromanage.
2. The map expansions take too long or don't expand far enough. If you choose a town out in the middle of nowhere like I did (Scottish Highlands) you have to make multiple unlocks before you get to the next town to link up, and since you can't make any more routes either, you're basically stuck playing hours with no changes to make.
3. I know the game is called 'City Bus Manager', so maybe this is the wrong thing to call for, but instead of the tedious late game bus depot gameplay, I think it might be better if you could sign off a region as completed, and then become a national planner where you have the option to start another depot somewhere else, or maybe link two cities together with a coach network. (In order to do this then the fuel values would need to become more realistic.)

Just my two cents.\t\t\t\t\t
