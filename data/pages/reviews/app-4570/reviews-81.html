\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy actual hours aren't reflected as I bought this game specifically to play for the Apocalypse mod.
I also would have given this a positive review, however in light of recent circumstances involving the content of this game, by the original IP holders, Gamesworkshop, I cannot recommend it. If you want a sincere review, as I cannot fault you for just looking up decent comprehension of the game, it'll be mentioned briefly after a break in the text.

At the time of writing, Gamesworkshop has issued an update for the IP regulations and management which retroactively created a blanket solution to a problem (people using 3D printers to replicate their pricey products) which put fanfiction writers, fan animators, and lore summary YouTube channels under fire. Without them, I wouldn't have consciously purchased this game as I actually loathe RTS titles. Seeing how space marines drop from the sky to rain fire onto an ork is why I bought this. Laughing late at night over the little quirks of this awesome universe is why I bought it. But hearing how this game still generates fan theories in lore summaries and discussions is why I bought this game. Without those entities, the time I've put into painting minis, buying lore books, and actively being a part of a greater community all would have not happened without finding those social media outlets. That's why I can't reccomend it.

But further, as I've mentioned in another Warhammer review, this IP clause could impair mod creators, especially those that accept donations for their time and effort making fan content to celebrate Warhammer. Honestly the Apocalypse Mod is sorta the only way to play since everything is taken to such an extreme in scope and complexity where it mimics the mental feeling of the table top. It would suck to see it go down.

********************************************************
For those who want a genuine review, considering that I don't like RTS's this game actually does something cool for me, I actually like it. The dialogue is a little cheesy, I'll admit, but nothing is more satisfying in a 40k game to see Space Marine drop pods, one after another. That's something even Space Marine (another game) fails to do at times, as much as I love it. The combat still feels chunky and satisfying and despite it being a replica of other RTS's it makes use of the IP to it's betterment vs being a one trick pony. Every faction feels unique and the Blood Ravens have been an excellent inclusion to the expanding lore of Warhammer.\t\t\t\t\t
