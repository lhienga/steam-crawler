\r\n\t\t\t\t\tListening to Gabriel Angelos brief and debrief me after every mission is music to my ears. The writing is really good and the delivery is just amazing.

Path finding for infantry units at max size as well as vehicles is not great. It is much better in future games but that does not mean it is a deal breaker. The game does not support modern resolutions like 1080p for Dark Crusade, yet oddly has a windowed option where as Dark Crusade does not.

The single player missions particularly against the Orcs is best described as a long maze of a map with your forces setting up secondary bases at the front. The maps open up more against the Eldar and Chaos.

My one complaint with Dark Crusade was that every map just felt like a skirmish map with some minor modifiers such as an enemy having elite units or what not. Or the enemy attacks you and almost every point is under your control from the start and you have 3 bases from your attack turn. The best levels in Dark Crusade are the Strongholds and in this game save the first 3 or so levels, it feels like that. Maps crafted for you to use specific units or sometimes have guardsmen and Leeman Russ tanks.

The game has aged well and I can't wait to revisit Winter Assault.

This game came out 2 years before Company of Heroes and while both games are very different, many of that games ideas had their genesis here. So its worth playing to see just where Relics great RTS formula got started.
