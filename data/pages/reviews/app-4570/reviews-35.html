\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuch a good game! I first played this game when I was a kid, and it was also my first introduction to Warhammer 40k. At the time I had no idea about the tabletop game, I thought it was just a RTS series like Starcraft lol. I even bought the Dark Crusade expansion as a kid and loved all of it(I missed Winter Assault, and never play Soulstorm either). Eventually I didnt have a computer anymore for a long time, playing on consoles only. Cut to about 15 years later and I get a laptop for Christmas and I pick up the Dawn of War Collection and Dawn of War II Collection from the Winter Sale that just passed in 2022-23. I just beat the Dawn of War Campaign, and have started Winter Assault, the 1st expansion, but I can most definitely recommend this game for and RTS or WH40k fan. Its so much fun and offers a bit different RTS gameplay than Starcraft or Age of Empires in the way you gather resources. Its much more combat heavy, as you need to capture and hold Requisition Points on the map to generate your main resource. This makes you constantly fighting and defending the enemy over these points. Also You rarely build a single unit when you make soldiers. Instead you get a squad of some. For Example Space Marines: When you build one Marine "Unit"(Squad)4 guys will pop out of the building,but are selected as one squad when you control them,and you can build add more soldiers to the squad and customize them with certain heavy weapons. This makes lots of soldiers running and gunning across the battlefield,and the battle are very fun to just watch. This game has 3 expansions for it:Winter Assault, Dark Crusade, and Soulstorm, and while I dont know much about the last one, the other 2 I would recommend as well. If you can get the Collection I suggest you do,as it is a very very fun series to get into, and you dont need to know anything about WH40K to enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI still think this game holds up, even if it is a little dated. I would recommend it with the expansions. The Soulstorm expansion is still one of my favorite comfort games that i have hundreds of hours both on Steam and CD. I still regularly play that version. But even at the base game, its still a well structured RTS thats still fun to play.

Graphically it is inbetween when 5:4 resolution was still king and 16:9 was just becoming mainstream. But there are patches and mods to easily fix widescreen issues. (The later expansions fix this.) That being said, i still think its pleasant to look at. It blew me away when i first played it. I used to sneak on my dad's laptop just to play it since my PC couldn't run it. The fact you could zoom in and see the detail of each model was crazy to me. Its dated to todays standards, but i still think it has its charm now.

Gameplay wise, its a fairly standard RTS. Closer to Starcraft style, but typically objective based. And it doesn't do anything too wild, but the formula it has is very well done and is very efficiant. I still compare many other RTS games to this series. And i honestly wish this series would make a return. Especially after seeing how DOWIII turned out. Anyway, overall the game is easy to get into. And, again, it's still enjoyable. I even replayed the entire campaign just for this review. The overall story is classic 40k. It isn't too in depth, but it still works well within the game and mechanics. And the rendition and world building is still one of the best in the 40k games i've played.

Overall, id recommend this with the expansions. But the base game still has its moments. It also would be a good intro to the 40k series, it's kinda what got me hooked. I'd also recommend this as a entry to RTS even if you don't play RTS games much. It won game of the year for a reason.\t\t\t\t\t
