\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like bleak dark settings set in a sci fi universe? Do you like playing as a race of arrogant space Elves? Do you like mowing down your enemies with several heavy machine gun like weapons as a bunch of super soldiers?
Do you like zooming right in to a battle to watch both sides preform brutal mortal kombat (Well more like mortal kombat lite) style finishing moves on each other?

If you answered yes to any of these questions then you will probably like dawn of war. The story campaign drops you in head first right into the action. Even if you know nothing about the universe you will soon understand that the big green angry men are the bad guys and your super soldiers and the regular soldiers are the good guys.
Now while I might have made some hardcore fans of the game froth at the mouth with that statement, trust me when I say you will want to take the lore of this game one step at a time. There is enough lore on the wiki to drown in several times over.
The story is written well enough that I was glued to the screen wanting to know what happened next. And this is before I became a massive fan of this universe btw.

How does the gameplay fare though? Its pretty good. Picture your classic base building RTS like age of empires and you're good to go. Only here loosing your town centre or command centre is a much bigger deal. Its harder to rebuild and might cost you the game. Does this mean the game is pretty hard then?

Well it can be. This is one of the best selling points about dawn of war. The A.I can be set to everything from "Toddler" to "Oh no. Oh help- it is coming to kill me- IDONTWANTTODIE!" So even if you have never played an RTS game before you should be able to get the hang of it after enough games. I'm pretty sure the game can be played in multiplayer without too many issues too.

In the past you had to use some sort of 3rd party matchmaking system like Hamachi (Oh man that was a piece of garbage) to set up a game. But now you find your friends on steam invite them and you should be good to go.
This game is great for CO-OP with friends by the way. It is so satisfying to each play a different side and coordinate against the A.I or another friend who knows the game more. I have had matches that were an all out slugfest that lasted less than 20 minutes, to spending a whole evening slowly creeping forwards over a war torn battlefield that no one could conquer.

This game somehow makes that fun btw. Due to each race having multiple options at your disposal, you can change up your tactics and come up with new strategies on the fly as long as you have the resources. It also helps that the largest maps aren't that big, so games never drag out to the point where you are playing "Hunt down the last enemy unit on the map"

It also helps with salty players who want the make the match drag on forever by hiding in a corner.

In case you can't tell I love this game. I recommend getting it when it goes on sale so you can get the multi pack letting you buy the game and all of the expansions in one go. Trust me, you will soon be aching to play a new faction, since there are only 4 of them in the base game.\t\t\t\t\t
