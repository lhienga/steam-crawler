\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a soft yes. Mainly for the reason the game has seen a fair lack of updates for 40K Dawn of War to run properly on newer systems and wider aspect ratios. On top of that, you may have to use unofficial patches or run at specific resolutions to get the game to function properly at all.

However, this is one of the best RTS games I've played. While I've only 24 hours logged on Steam, I played this game for hundreds of hours before this account. Great sound design and an okay story with a lot of fun missions. However, it hasn't aged too well, and that's not just the lack of updates from the developer. The graphics are in need of a light polish or the ability to upscale the resolution a bit. Words look fuzzy and you can see that this game was made for older monitors. But, as said, its fun.

At least the price reflects its age. And when it's on sale, it's dirt cheap. Along with that, any potato of a PC should have zero issues running this game at it's maximum settings. Unless you're using a PC built around 2005.

So. Is it good? Yes. Does it have problems on modern operating systems? Yes.
So if your a fan of the 40K series and universe, this is a must have. Otherwise, if you don't really want to have to do any kind of modifications or using community made mods from other sites, give this a pass and go for one of the newer games. But I have to note: The second and third DoW games are a very different kind of RTS. Far more squad based, whereas this title focused more on both squads and army building and movement, with a much larger emphasis on base building and resource management as well.\t\t\t\t\t
