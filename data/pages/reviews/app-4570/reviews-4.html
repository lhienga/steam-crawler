\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game very much of its time, and its time was a golden era of gaming. An era we got Dawn of War, Homeworld 2, Rome: Total War, Half Life 2, Company of Heroes, to name just a few classics, games that funnily enough all generally worked out of the box, rather than today's ethos of releasing half finished games and finishing them after they have your money.

The graphics are of an age but the gameplay is as fresh today as it was 20 years ago, with a large roster of well balanced units that compliment each other very well, and a story that has plenty of twists in it, especially the ending. For 40K fans there's a lot here to love. Whilst not entirely faithful to the Codex Astartes (which the Blood Ravens are supposed to be), it's captures the spirit of 40K, showing the comedic elements of the Orks whilst still presenting them as a threat.

Quite simply, this is one of the best RTS ever made and you can get it and all its expansions for less than the original release price now. If you're an RTS fan and/or a Warhammer 40K fan, you owe it to yourself to add this to your 40K gaming collection. You won't be dissapointed. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the game that got me into the warhammer 40k franchise.
When it first came out in the age of multi-disk CD boxes, the game allowed for amazing massive battles. It was the first time I found recruiting a squad of Space Marines gave me a full squad compliment. Put Star Craft to shame with the amount of Zerg Rush of Orks to Marines on a mission.

Didn't even need to mind ore or harvest gas, just capture objective markers by planting flags and building power generators. With no worry about mirco managing a resource tree like say Warcraft.
The combat is amazing for it's time too. Watching characters enter Melee combat was bad ass. Watching a Marine fury swing his chain sword into enemies like a final finishing blow or thrusting a knife into their collar bone before kicking their enemy to the dirt. Most of all watching a small Marine Libarian take down a towering giant of fire and lava.

The game gives you a multitude of factions to play, starting w/ 4 in teh first game. Then introduce new ones in each expansion. Factions all have their unique structures & unit types.

Dawn of War main has an epic campaign with amazing voice acting from iconic names: Paul Dobson, Scott McNeil, Nicole Oliver and more.

Winter Assault introduces a new winter theme map with the Imperial Guard as the next faction introduced.
The WA Campaign can be won w/ either of the 4 factions. Which subtly introduces the next big faction.

Dark Crusade is the biggest expansion, introducing 2 new factions: Tau & Necrons. As well as a campaign that is player driven, which grants choice of playing 1 of the 7 new factions.
This campaign has a subtle RPG element allowing players too upgrade their commander & his retinue as they progress in the conquest of the planet Kronus.

Soulstorm introduces a new factions, the sisters of battle & the Dark Eldar
The game was developed by a different studio, as apparent in the mechanics of the units, the environment & map designs.
It's not as good as Dark Crusade, though it tries for the same means of a player driven campaign by conquering a whole sector. Sadly, the game fails at this with poor writing, bad unit coding, and buggy game play.
