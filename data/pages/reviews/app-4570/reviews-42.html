\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe death of a non-believer is more nondescript than someone who believed in dying for something. For the Emperor or for Chaos. For the restoration of your former glory or for the WAAAGH!; believing in something seems to be the only thing this universe can offer as you die amongst millions of others in a never ending war that appears to be going nowhere.

I only picked up bits and pieces of the 40k lore throughout my life but only started reading more thoroughly once I bought this game. The game may be dated at the time of this review, the gameplay is similiar to other RTS games, and the resolution doesn't go any higher than what was available back then. But this is one of the best RTS game I've played; especially for a franchise I didn't know much about until I got into it. The campaign is linear compared to it's last two expansions but it does give a better understanding of the universe and what the Imperium of Man go through on a daily basis. It seems like alliances of convenience, betrayals both within and between factions, constant xenophobic mistrust between humans and actual aliens, constant suspicions of being a traitor or God-Emperor forbid "Heretic", fighting for the sake of fighting, planets being settled, lost and retaken again over the millennia and giant freak-champions of Gods stomping around and killing those who don't believe seems par for the course in many stories in Warhammer 40k.

I find the graphics pretty good considering when it came out, but the resolution is a different story, but if you can either work that out or get used to it (Like I did); then this is a must-play RTS game, even if you don't know anything about the lore but there's a good chance you'll want to know more after playing this.

One more thing: If you're only interested in the multiplayer aspect, you should get the soulstorm expansion as it contains all nine factions and is the most up-to-date for that experience and the best part is that you really don't need the base game itself to use it. In fact both expansions; Dark Crusade and Soulstorm don't require the base game and can be bought and installed separately without needing the others. (Winter Assault is the exception and requires the base game in order to play.).\t\t\t\t\t
