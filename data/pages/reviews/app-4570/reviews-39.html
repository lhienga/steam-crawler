\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI re-bought the Dawn of War collection. I remembered how good Dawn of War series was and I have the physical discs hidden away some where. About 20 years later it was time to revisit this series. It was time to dive back into greatness.

Sadly things did not go as well as I hoped they would. The first issue I ran into was I couldn't get the game to run at 1920 x 1080. That's unfortunate. But I still had a lot of fun with the story and the run through Dawn of War was a lot of fun. Entertaining story and the combat was great.

I then moved on to the DLC. Starting with the Winter Assault. At first I was having fun, I decided to play the bad guys and goodness, they have you jump through some strange balancing act hoops to complete missions. The story felt lacking. It wasn't as good as I remembered it. Maybe I played the good guys. The DLC was okay.

Then I came to the spiral... I must have mentally blocked the next 2 DLCs. I could have sworn they were good but.... Both seem to have little to no story and you fight on what appears to be just multiplayer maps. It was the start of the lazy developer phase. What was considered balancing was limiting unit types and everything seemed to come across as tanky. I mean, heavy bolters against guardsman should melt them right? Think again. Not even with tech maxed. It all felt spongy.

Dark Crusade and Soulstorm are just wash, rinse, repeat grind as you fight to secure the map for your faction. On normal the AI feels tanky, but not unbeatable. This made these 2 DLCs feel like a time sink and not as entertaining.

I would highly recommend the first Dawn of War. I wouldn't bother with the DLC. They just try to ruin the experience you had playing the first one.\t\t\t\t\t
