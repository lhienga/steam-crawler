General Thoughts/Overview:

Dawn of War (DoW) is an old school RTS and championed one of the supply mechanics that can be found in a raft of modern RTS games. I remember being disappointed with it, but I couldn’t remember why until I finished it and found it ended on a cliff hanger, something I consider to be malpractice in the gaming industry.

You find yourself in the shoes of a blood raven commander, tasked with clearing a planet of the orkish hordes that beset it, only to find yourself and your chapter embroiled in a sinister plot by the ‘great enemy’.

Pros:
  • Simple gameplay that stream lines a lot of RTS mechanics into an easy to play and quick to learn experience.
  • DoW really leans into the epic nonstop fighting, you can zoom in and watch your squads go to it as well, and it feels great to do so. Speaking of which, the squad based system itself is great for keeping control and resupplying on the fly.
  • The simple nature of the game and the easy campaign make this a good old power trip and stress reliever. If what you’re looking for is the whole unstoppable space marine army vibe, then this will have you covered and then some for some fairly mindless strategy.

Cons:
  • Several audio and visual bugs plagued this title, mostly found during the transitions between cut scene and gameplay. Audio can hang, or repeat, or ear rape you with a solid blast, the screen can freeze or units can be smeared across it (and not in the delightful way intended by, say, a dreadnaught).
  • The campaign is super easy. There is almost no challenge at all, with everything you face being significantly weaker than you. Ramping the difficulty also doesn’t make much of a difference, simply increasing the hp and resources available to the AI as opposed to making them utilise resources and strategic points more intelligently.
  • There isn’t much variety to the units or their upgrades, making the game fairly repetitive as you build up to your optimal composition and stomp the AI.

Suggested improvements:
  • Bigger zoom out.
  • More varied upgrades for the units.
  • More nuanced AI.


Overall Recommendation:

Ya know what? Sure.

In a binary format this just about wins out. Its and ancient game, and it really does show its age, but don’t let that put you off too much.

This game is an incredibly simple RTS, and as such I don’t recommend it to veterans of the genre looking for a challenge, but I do recommend it to newcomers.
DoW doesn’t have a decent story, or decent voice acting, or decent ambient sounds, so don’t be expecting a twisting and turning masterpiece. It does, however, provide a nice, concise, and well strung together simple good vs evil romp. Sometimes that’s all you need.

You really mustn’t get this when it’s not on offer though, it’s a massive rip off for what it is given its almost old enough to drink in the states, and you can probably find a CD key for it for the change you’ve got in the sofa. I’m pleased to say I added it to steam using actual physical CD keys… So if you do have it laying around in an attic somewhere and it’s been a while, or you found a cheap copy, then you might get a couple of evening’s entertainment.\t\t\t\t\t
