\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe early-mid 2000s were a time when there had been a real shift in online gaming, particular in the PC world. The RTS market had dominated since the late 90s with such timeless classics such as Star Craft, War Craft 2, Command and Conquer, and Age of Empires. However, with the rise of games like Ultima Online and Ever Quest, popularity for these games were rapidly increasing, even in their infancy.

Dawn of War might have been late to the party, but it was still successful enough to bring Warhammer 40k to the video game medium, with future titles in this series as well as branching into other genre. Whats great about DoW is how refreshing it still feels in the twilight years of the RTS heyday, especially with the mechanics that it brought to the table.

Though not as refined as previously mentioned titles, it's still game play stands out on its own and apart. You aren't bogged down with needing to create a factory of unit producing buildings and spamming hotkeys. The squad based system makes the game feel intuitive and fast, and resource management also lightens the hotkey load since you aren't mass producing gathering drones and scattering them across a map.

The campaign is the best part of the game, in my opinion. It captivates you by throwing you right into classic fantasy territory of Humans vs Orks, and introduces more prominent characters and races along the way. It's relatively short with only 11 Space Marine missions, but they're varied in their objectives and strategy's which makes for a more fresh play through, and is much less daunting compared to games like Star Craft and Age of Empires.

The other races are playable through the games Skirmish mode, and though there's 3 others, they all play pretty much the same. The paint mode is fun but limited as well, with only a few units to choose from.

Overall, if you're a fan of 40k, or a fan of RTS games in general, its absolutely worth picking up. It has aged well, which I find true for most RTS games. You might be better off investing in the sequels, as they also have expansions to add to the game, but for what this is and at the sub $15 price tag, I highly recommend.
