\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImportant notes before I get into reviewing this game: if you want the game to be widescreen in 1080p, try one of the options here: https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=205040803 (I've tried these solutions myself and they didn't work for me, but they might work for you). That's everything! Let's get into it.

Dawn of War is without a doubt a highly competent and enjoyable RTS game that has made me more interested in the WH40K universe than ever before. Every faction feels so unique and fun to play as (especially the Space Marines and Orks), the visuals are great for an early 2000's game, each unit looks well-modelled and has brilliant fighting animations that are incredibly enjoyable to watch (e.g. Dreadnoughts picking up and crushing enemy units with their hands, Squiggoths impaling enemy units with its tusks, Avatars impaling enemy units with their swords and watching them slide down etc.). As well as the good modelling and animation, the voice lines of each type of unit is very fitting for them. The music is very well-composed and good enough to listen to in your spare time, the sound design is passable but is overshadowed by the screams and war cries of the different factions' units. I should also mention that this game can be run on modern systems literally from the get-go with no patches required.

For Skirmish, there is a wide variety of maps to play on as well as a 4 difficulty modes, and various game types (e.g. Sudden Death, Annihilation, Assassinate etc.). There's also an online multiplayer mode but it's pretty much dead. The campaign is decent, nothing groundbreaking but I enjoyed it. It was reasonably long and managed to be a decent challenge from time to time despite me playing it through on the lowest difficulty (normal). WARNING: there are spoilers for the campaign's plot further on so if you want an unexpected experience then by all means don't read the rest of this paragraph. To summarise, it's basically a story of an Imperial planet being invaded by vicious Orks, but their invasion is a distraction for the Imperium that Chaos set up, in order to give them enough time to free a demon known as the Maledictum, capable of brainwashing masses into joining the foul legions of Chaos. As Gabriel Angelos, commander of the Blood Ravens 3rd Company, you must put an end to this heretical plot, for the Imperium, and for the Emperor.

Despite how good this game is, it still has its faults. For instance, the game doesn't let you zoom out enough, making it difficult to control large armies, as opposed to other RTS games like AOM where you can zoom out quite a lot, allowing you to select larger quantities of troops at a time and making it easier to see what's happening to all of them at once. Another issue is that the pathing in this game really shows its problems when trying to control a large army (AGAIN), when controlling quite a lot of big squads and vehicles, some of the squads and vehicles get lost and awkwardly start going in random directions before continuing their rightful path. Some may even stop and do nothing. I also don't like the lack of formation options, meaning that you can't organise your troops as much as you can in a different RTS game like AOM. Another problem I should point out is that whenever you load a campaign save, the music and ambience disappear, which takes away from the experience. At least I haven't had this issue with skirmish saves, but that makes the issue even stranger. Last problem I should point out is that there's no widescreen support for 1080p. Seriously, I tried changing the resolution in the .ini file, two big black bars on either side of my screen appeared. I tried using the solutions that I provided a link for in the "important notes" section above but they didn't fix the problem for me. Eventually I gave up and continued playing the game in its highest native resolution (1600x1200). Not a terrible resolution but it could be better. Oh, another issue I should point out is that there are these odd outline flashes from time to time. I had this same issue in Psychonauts and Warband. In Psychonauts I just ignored it, in Warband I fixed it by playing the game windowed with the Borderless Gaming program. I tried using the Warband solution for this game but it resulted in those annoying black bars again and so I just decided to put up with the odd flashes. One more problem I should add is the aforementioned dead online multiplayer. Fortunately, the pros outweigh the cons with this one.

So to conclude, Warhammer 40,000 Dawn of War is an excellent RTS experience that will likely make you fall in love with the WH40K universe and will provide you with thrilling, addictive, and strategic gameplay. It's let down by some problems such as odd outline flashes, flawed pathing, a zoom distance that is too restricted, no 1080p widescreen support, as well as a music and ambience bug for campaign saves and a dead online multiplayer.

Final verdict: 7/10 "Walk softly, and carry a big gun."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started looking into this game because of my history with Warhammer, but I'm pleased to say the experience went much deeper than just nostalgia. The game is engaging, with a decent dose of lore into the 40k universe. I've heard the 40k universe can be a convoluted mess to get into, but the writing here is considerate enough that I never felt lost or confused by the story of the campaign. It was all easy to digest, but a touch too straightforward and simple. However, I see there are expansion packs with unique campaigns to each of them, and I feel this may flesh out the stunted storyline of the base game well.

Despite the game's age, and because it benefits from being an RTS, it's easy to overlook the dated visuals - except for cut-scenes. Still, the camera's position can't be zoomed out enough to be comfortable, and gameplay does suffer a little for it.

Replayability is definitely one of the games better qualities. Each army plays a little differently - very differently for Orkz - and even though there are only 4 races to choose, I had hours of fun learning each of them. It was also loads of fun getting into the mindset of each race as I played through skirmishes with them. The snobby elitism of the Eldar, Chaos forces neurotic drive for domination, the righteous spirit of the Space Marines, and the dopey aggression o' da ork boyz were all compelling and contagious personalities to indulge in.

I can't really speak to the online community, I haven't really tried to jump into an online match since the game's age doesn't inspire any confidence that this would be likely...

Still, a really fun game, and great start to a no doubt great series of games!

7/10 - No painting required.\t\t\t\t\t
