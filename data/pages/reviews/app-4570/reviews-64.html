\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only played the campaign. The gameplay is solid. There's quite a learning curve. Normal was too easy, so I was playing on Hard. About midway through the campaign, I was starting to feel like the missions were impossible on Hard. Then I looked up some gameplay tips and realized I hadn't been strategic enough with my usage of units - I was mostly building ranged units, but you really do need melee units as well. I was also spreading myself too thin - the unit cap is pretty low and you can only defend so many control points.

That said, the difficulty is hit or miss. Some missions are much harder than others. The final mission was a joke, even on Hard, and I'm sure that wasn't just because I was getting better at the game. There were very few enemy units, and unlike other missions they didn't seem to be building anything and never sent waves to attack my control points.

The story is decent; the voice acting is mostly good, but the cut scenes are terribly choreographed and animated - probably a natural result of trying to make in-game cutscenes in an engine designed for RTS.

Being an older game, there's no widescreen support and no way to customize your hotkeys. The thing I found most frustrating is that you can issue multiple consecutive orders to the same unit(s) by holding Shift, but the game does not visually represent this. For example, like any RTS you can set a series of waypoints, but unlike every other RTS there is no line to show the waypoint path that the unit will take. The same applies for build orders, repair orders, attack orders, etc.

The campaign contains a paltry 11 missions. Just when you're starting to really get into it, it's over (and of course it ends on a cliffhanger).\t\t\t\t\t
