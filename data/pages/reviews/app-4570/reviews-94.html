\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWarhammer 40,000: Dawn of War is a RTS game set in a grimdark Warhammer 40,000 universe, where there is an eternal conflict between religious, xenophobic humans, bloodthirsty aliens, and demons from another dimension. This is probably one of the best 40k games out there, because of it's addictive gameplay, solid presentation for it's time, and fatefullness to the source material.

There is a singleplayer campaign with a solid storyline. You play as the Space Marines of the Blood Ravens chapter, led by captain Gabriel Angelos and his friend librarian Isador Akios.Space Marines were sent to the planet of Tartarus to help local Imperial Guard with the Ork invasion. The simple mission of cleansing the planet and eradicating Ork menace quickly bacomes something more menacing, since the Ork invasion was a ctually a distraction placed by the forces of Chaos, searching for a powerful artifact somewhere on Tartarus.
The story, like I said, is fine. The campaign itself mostly serves as the tutrial for the multiplayer and skirmish matches. The story is well voice acted, but the dated presentation makes it look a little goofy, when two low poly-models perform simple animations, while talking about matters of life and death. Still, it is a decent introduction into the game and into the world of Warhammer 40k.

The graphics are a little dated by todays standards. That's probably was made due to the optimisation, since there is a lot of stuff at the screen at once, with all the buildings and units fighting each other. The game was released in 2004, when we got such breakthrough titles like DOOM 3 and Half-Life 2, both of which are very impressive graphical achivements for their time, but this one kind of looks like a game from the late 90-s. There is also issue in which the game doesn't support modern widescreen resolutions, so you have to go out of your way to fix that, replacing config files, and even then the UI will be stretched from 4:3 to the 16:9, and it does not look pretty.
But the art of the game still holds up and it is a pretty good interpretation of the 40k. There is also great soundtrack, and brilliant voiceacting. So even if the game technically dated, it is not awful and still is enjoyable to look at.

Now, I'm not expert at RTS and the only reason that I decided to pick up this series because I was interested in Warhammer. This is apparently a very aggresive RTS compared to the other games in the genre. Main objective is to capture strategic points, that will accumulate resources for you, and build up your base to get most powerful units and crush your enemy. Campaign mode explains main loop really well, introducing new units one-by-one each new mission, but the main modes of the game are multiplayer and skirmish, where you have access to the other races that play differently from each other with their own unique units, buildings and mechanics. If i got into this game younger, I would probably lose hours in skirmish mode alone, but now I lack and interest playing that, and I too bad at RTS games to play multiplayer, but I thimk fans of the genre may enjoy this. The game still has somewhat of a cult following with all of it's expansions with more races, and, of course, modding scene, that introduce new units from the original tabletop and rebalances.
