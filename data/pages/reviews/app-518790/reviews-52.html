Nice visuals and exploration experience, but the weapons feel unsatisfying to use.

Note: Got this on sale. Initial experience included buying the QoL DLC, namely the tents and the ATV ones, so the distance and "saving" weren't a problem. They really should be included in the base game, though, as others have said. tl;dr at the end.

Disclaimer: I don't play games much, but I played Insurgency (2014) a lot before, and it had really satisfying and responsive weapons, so I'm mainly comparing theHunter:COTW to that game.

My main problem with the game is with the handling of the weapons, which is the focus of this review.
The gun handling in the game JUST doesn't feel right or good.

For example, on reloading. Using the revolvers, why does he reload so slowly? And of course, something like "reloading while running" is locked as a "perk". I wonder how many more perks are needed to get the character to a not-annoying level?

Moving on, the lever action shotgun has a similar painstakingly-slow reload sequence. Reloading the AR-style rifles also has this part where he seems to miss the mag well every time as he casually brings up a fresh mag at a glacial pace? Gets old fast.

Reload cancelling should be part of the game as well. Sometimes, you just need 2 shots on the revolver, so Lclick should make him stop reloading and get ready to fire. It's so annoying to wait for the whole reload sequence when "reloading to full" isn't the only realistic way to reload, if realism is one of the game's goals.

Overall, the reloading animations seem so artificially slowed down, with the .357 revolver reload for example, looking stiff and robotic, and not in a good way.

The round count per magazine is cursed as well. Based on my research, some hunting places do have regulations on magazine capacity. (The one I saw was 5 rounds for semi auto.)

And, for games, I'm not for 45-round extended magazines in a slow-paced hunting experience, but the M1 Garand style rifle has the en bloc clip with just 5 rounds. They couldn't even make it an even number and pretend that in the game universe, their M1 Garands had 6 rounds per clip.

And the AR-15 and AR-10 as well with the 5-round magazines. It seems like the hunter is nerfing his own hunting experience by not getting normal mags. Or are those locked behind a perk system as well?
Maybe the reserve rules on round count can be different per reserve, so there's more variety: one reserve can have 10 rounds, another can have 5, etc.

On the topic of aiming, the ADS scope wobble is insanely nasty as well. It looks like the player is drunk holding the gun. It even happens when in the prone position. Is there no way to set the rifle on your bag or a rock or something so it's more stable? That, or have a bipod attachment. Seems like a basic "realistic" thing to add as DLC or something to the base game.

Adding to that, the guy's aim gets all wobbly and tired (Elevated heart rate! Oh my!) after like 2-3 shots when ADSing. It does not seem realistic that the guy can hike miles and miles up and down mountains like in "Lone Survivor" carrying like 3 guns plus other gear no problem, but he can't shoot well after 2-3 shots.

Other suggestions:
  • Don't wait for the newly-selected item/weapon to fully come up if you select another item.
  • Have an option at the shooting range to retain infinite ammo, but have the round count per gun be respected, so reloads can be timed and included when practicing.
  • Better photography mode with changeable lenses, depth of field, focus distance, aperture, etc.
  • Hotkey to remove all the added things on screen for better screenshots without having to wait to pull out the camera.
  • Support for multiple save files
  • Higher animal render distance
  • More granular sound controls, like being able to turn the bike noise, wind, etc. down.

tl;dr Overall, after hunting and tracking the animal, getting to the shooting part is just so unsatisfying and kills the joy out of the game for me because of the underwhelming weapon handling. I really want to recommend this game otherwise, because of the scenery and soundscape, but the weapons could use some work. At least the guns sound good.

I hope to revisit this title if they keep on updating it, as I haven't tried bows yet.\t\t\t\t\t
