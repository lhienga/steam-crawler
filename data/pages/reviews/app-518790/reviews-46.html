each map is so unique and i love how free roam it can get, and also that it doesn't bind me to missions and i can turn them off or on depending on my mood. i've never hunted before IRL but it feels pretty realistic- until i'm shooting at a very specific bison like... 7 times and it gets bugged in game and stands still and when it finally drops it's still alive... i couldn't harvest the kill that day.

OR when i spotted a herd of caribou in Yukon Valley; only intended to shoot a bull bc of the rack and when i did, the whole herd literally stood in game, bugged, couldn't move, so i just took 'em all out. my Bloodhound had a easily good time that's for sure.

yes, i've dealt my fair share of bugs in this game, but it's not too bad, just not ideal when playing multiplayer w/ friends but even then we still find a way to enjoy our time.

my only biggest concerns really revolve around the DLCs; while i'm very glad that i got the Master Hunter Bundle when it was on sale during December, i still found it very confusing that if i hadn't, i would need the DLC to even get the 4x4s.... i agree with other players that say the vehicles should just be apart of the base game.

TL;DR: good game, should get for fun if you've never hunted IRL but like hunting sims, DLCs can be pretty arguable. breathtaking maps IMO, i even hallucinate seeing the in-game lit up animal tracks IRL when walking sometimes.\t\t\t\t\t
