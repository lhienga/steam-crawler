\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and most important: I recommend educating yourself thouroughly before you buy this game.
Now my experience and thoughts:
I really wanted to like this game and enjoy it to the fullest, however...
As you could guess, it's a hunting simulator. Relatively realistic actually, but after doing some research, base game maps are considered the worst. Sure, you can play MP with a player who has a DLC with specific map, but when streamers and youtubers even encourage you to buy DLCs, that should speak VOLUMES. Beginings are always tough in every game, but here, you really are at a huge disadvantage, as perks and skills for sneakiness and stealth are higher up. So, it will be a huge grind until you can even possibly be a proper hunter. Also, if your computer is mediocre, excpect it to sound like a washing machine. I really should have refunded it while I could, but it was my Bday so I was like ''why not try something new?''

I will give props to graphics, as they are stunning, and the map is huge, which is both + and - : reasons being missions (won't spoil it but traversing really long distances and a basically waiting game for dozens of minutes at times for anything just to pass by) and also being relatevly slow in speed, both walking and running. It also makes it repetitive exploring new areas and having such big maps may be one of the reasons why my PC hates it when I play it. Again, you are almost obliged to buy DLCs, vehicle one especially. If you have lots of time or you enjoy the scenery, then this isn't as bad for you then. Also, regarding the maps, water areas are, compared to DLC maps, in most cases, very horrible for spotting and tracking. Highly recomend checking for yourself and if you do decide to buy DLC, be sure which one you get.

As for gameplay, as I said, relatively realistic. It is good, as is the story and voice acting. My issue here may be that certain animals are almost unaproachable (this is where perks and skills at higher levels come at play, you really need them, but first ones aren't that good in my oppinion - look up a guide), or are scared for no reason. It also doesn't help the way animals move. Easily spooked even though they shouldn't be (there is a difference between rarer ones, as they spot you faster, but I doubt every fox and boar was a rare :D ) from distances greater than 100m-150m, even on low visibility and crouching. On the other hand, I got running like a maniac in certain parts and when I get into clearing, I spot a deer just a few dozen meters away, and sometimes it is rarer one. Inconsistent in my opinion.

Another very important thing - trophies. Yes, they exist, however, in base game (or was it free DLC, can't remember) you get a lodge, but you can only put 1 of EACH animal species in base game. So, if you caught cool Diamond ranked trophy of idk Roe Deer, and you have Albino Roe Deer, you can only put up one. You need one of 2 SEPARATE DLCs for the lodges that allows you to put around 200 (I think, definetly more than 100) trophies in total.

Also, if I recall corectly, in one of the videos of youtuber LadyLegendXO, base maps earlier had more spawns/animals. It seems they reduced it (purposfully?), while DLC weapons and animals are both in abundance and better.

Just to be fair, my bad experience (apart from fear of computer exploding ofc) may be because this game genre may not be my cup of tea, or I just couldn't figure it out. Then again, there are lists on youtube with titles ''best starting maps for begginers'' and base game maps are always put last/worst, so take that into consideration. It's definetly not a game that is unplayable, but you do need a lot of time and grind. Also, it kind of breaks the immersion and/or is pointless to people who like singleplayer experience if you need to constantly go into other people's worlds.\t\t\t\t\t
