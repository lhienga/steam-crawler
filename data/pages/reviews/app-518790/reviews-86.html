\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpent 3 days installing this 85GB game, only to discover that most of the content is locked behind DLC. Why make me download and take up 85GB on my harddrive, if 90% of the game isn't even playable without DLC??

The game itself is fairly boring, and doesn't even live up to the depth of some original XBox games. Any animal can hear you snap a twig from hundreds of yards away, and somehow automatically know it's a human and flees. Your crouch movement speed is a snail's pace, but since it's the only way to not be a danger beacon to the animals in the area, you'll be spending most of the game at that speed. There's enclosures in the game that allow you to "camp out" and wait for animals, but it's pointless because you'll most likely be stuck there for irl hours waiting for anything to show up. The few items that are available to buy without DLC are absurdly expensive. I killed about 12 animals my first playthrough, and ended up around 8k. That took about 4-5 hours of gameplay....the average equipment upgrade costs between 5k-20k.

What's unfortunate is the moments where I was actually hunting and tracking something were enjoyable, and a part of me still wants to play because of that...but spending the vast majority of the game quietly shuffling from A to B ruins the experience for me. I don't care if it's "realistic". If I wanted to waste hours of my life to not have fun, I would just go hunting irl.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: 9/10 - would recommend for hunting amateurs and pros. Quirky storytelling and a save-bug drained one point.

> Graphics: The game was released back in 2017 and playing it in 2024 it has really matured a whole lot. This game does not only sound nice, it also looks astonishing. From a graphics point of view I'd rate it a high AA-title.

> Mechanics: Ballistic curve, different calibers, different animal that need a specific approach each. It is nice. Tracking a wounded animal feels real but sometimes the animal despawn. That is the only uncool thing about hunting. This game is possible to be played as noob or pro alike.

> Storytelling: This is the only point I always found very annoying. Unskippable cutscenes and "calls" from someone I am not interested in listening to. If you want to completely immerse yourself, it is probably still a solid 7/10.

> Bugs: Saves. Saves is the biggest issue. Your guns and scores will be saved correctly but everytime you join a map everything is reset. You have to go and unlock all the houses - over and over again. There were none other bugs I came while playing this game.

Overall rating: 9/10 - besides the quirky storytelling and the save-bug I would highly recommend this game. You need some time and also a calm personality to play this game. If you are more a run and gun shooter guy - this game is probably not what you are looking for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo if you buy the base game you're buying a broken game. So they got me on 5 dollar sale a few weeks ago. And the game is pretty good. Nice visuals, has some nice quality of life controls like "G" auto walks. I have pretty bad arthritis from working in IT and playing lots of video games. But this new generation of we sell you demo version is something I don't like to support.

I did end up falling for the scam b/c its a pretty decent game, but I won't give it a thumbs up b/c the base game is basically a "bait"(Hunting pun).

The main issue with the base game is that you cannot buy tents, or vehicles without spending another $15 at the time I'm writing this. Also the weapon selection is pretty poor. I feel sad that I fell into the trap. but if you go into the game knowing you're going to be nickle and dimmed at every turn then its a pretty good game. I just hate that they had my 2 hours and my $5 dollars before I realized I had been taken for a ride.

So with that I would say if you don't want to be a rube like me move onto game makers who release complete games and not grindy BS to squeeze some dollars off you.\t\t\t\t\t
