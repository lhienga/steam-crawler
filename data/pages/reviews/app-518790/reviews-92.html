Short Game Description
theHunter: Call of the Wild is a realistic hunting game that features many huge reserves, most of which are behind a paywall but are worth it. The unique problem is the abusive DLCs and the little appreciation for the game that developers are giving.
  • Positives
    Discussing the positives, it has huge reserves based on real ones, which don't feel repetitive as you explore, and many unique animals that pertain to that area. Secondly, there are many weapons to pick from, and you can customise the loadout you will be using to hunt by creating some hunting areas, which the game forces you to not hunt all the time in the same area because there will be fewer and fewer animals if you hunt there so much. Thirdly, the animals are hard to encounter, and you will need to track them with footsteps, need zones, drinking zones, and sleep zones, which will be good spots to ambush the animals if you are sufficiently observant. Lastly, the game has some missions and spots with lore that make the game a bit more interesting to explore instead of just being a straightforward hunting game.
  • Negatives
    On the negative side, and the main complaint I have with the game, which is the reason I'm marking the game with a negative review, is that there are too many DLCs, most of which are quite essential, like some weapon packs, the ATV, the dog, tripods, and tents, that are not justified to be behind a paywall, as they are necessary, and some of them feature bugs, which makes the experience annoying and is not something uncommon; it was something I experienced through all the gameplay. The reserves deserve every euro spent, but most of the DLCs give a huge advance and don't take that much time to programme.
Conclusion
To conclude, the game has many good points, such as giving a realistic experience with many varieties and some interesting lore and quests in each reserve, but it is quite disappointing to see all the abusive DLCs that are not that hard to implement, some of which are essential to play, giving a huge advantage against players without them, and it is even worse when you pay for some of them and have constant bugs. The game, despite the abusive DLCs, is a 7.25/10.\t\t\t\t\t
