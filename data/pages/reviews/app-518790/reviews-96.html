\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Gonna post an actual review for this one.

Having grown up hunting and being drastically disappointed by nearly every hunting game, theHunter: CotW is the only hunting game I've played that makes me ACTUALLY feel like I'm hunting.

The gameplay is slow-paced and tactical, it requires you to either track animals stealthily and effectively, or familiarize yourself with their daily patterns and wait in a strategic spot/stand, and it relies on accurate vital shots to make kills.

The game is far from perfect, has a handful of minor and major bugs, but the devs are active with fixing bugs and improving features from what I've seen. It's also a bit of a gamble whether the game will run BEAUTIFULLY or TERRIBLY on your machine. I've had PCs that were quite a bit under what I thought would even play the game run it like a charm, and I've had PCs that should be able to run fine lag so much the animations of everything were slowed down and I was playing the game in slow-mo. Still got a trophy elk that way, though.

BUT when this game runs right, it is an absolute dream of an experience. The gameplay loop is paced nicely, there's a very dynamic skill curve to hone over time with the gameplay loop in general, and it has quite a lot of skill curves to master for each particular strategy and piece of equipment. Each successful kill is extremely satisfying, each bad shot comes with its own challenges and opportunities to learn, and you can spend hours in the game just enjoying the scenery and it's still a relaxing experience. Hell, I've spent whole evenings just tracking a single animal and feeling more than satisfied enough for the night when I finally get it. It really does feel like hunting beautiful tracks of land. One of the best games ever made for unwinding after a long day.

Tl;Dr Pros and Cons

Pros:
- Actually feels like hunting
- Nicely paced gameplay loop, not too rushed, not too slow
- Beautiful scenery and graphics
- Deep skill curve
- Lots of different animals to hunt, all with their own strategies required
- Extremely relaxing and rewarding, no matter how you play the game
- Updated and bug fixed regularly

Cons:
- Performance intensive
- Some machines just don't run it right
- Known bugs and issues. Some inconsequential, some hilarious, some awful
- DLC heavy, esp for duck hunting, displaying trophies, and additional locations
- Vitals system is extremely strict. Makes for great shot placement practice, but you can shoot something in the jugular and watch it just run away with a "No vitals hit" message bc the jugular does not count as a vital organ :|
- Shotguns shoot like 10-20 pellets max, so duck hunting is often a total joke.

8/10 Only hunting game where I've ever fallen asleep in a stand and still had a great time.\t\t\t\t\t
