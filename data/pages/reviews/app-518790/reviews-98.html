3. Demos or In-game strategy tutorials
4. Map improvements

6. Inventory improvements

Introduce a radar HUD screen or a separate animal tracker that clearly shows the location of all nearby animals i.e. where they are resting/feeding/drinking. Add difficulty levels to the game - Easy, Medium and Hard. This should resolve most of the problems mentioned.

2. Tutorials on how to hunt different animals. I know this is a big ask but it is definitely going to help new players even if we can cover one animal from each class.
I think the highest class is 9. So, nine tutorials with tips should be sufficient to get any new player familiarised with training and along with this you can introduce ranking, trophies, ammo selection and any advanced topics in the tutorials itself before pushing player towards the main game.

3. In-game tutorials need to be available. How to track deer steps? What is the approx. safe distance to maintain? Which scopes/guns or ammo to use for an effective kill?
It is unreasonable to expect from a player to experiment with all these just to analyse which is better or worse, that will be a waste of time.

4. In the map section, if you click Legend it will show all zones or other markings but the data is not organised in a way that could help the player. For instance, in the legend say 15 items are there (rest zone, drink zone, home, tent icons etc) and the player wants to have an ability to filter what they want to see on the map rather than seeing all junk that is available. Player may like to see only tents or bear tracks for example without having to see 100 icons on the map. A filter will be useful here.

5. Basically, there are very less number of animals corresponding to a mission. In the mission that I have mentioned earlier in step #1 harvest two blacktail ... it was way too difficult to spot any deers, not even during the time zone, not near any drink or feed zones. Guess this is the case with all the other missions. For instance, when you are on a coyote hunting mission, the game isn't showing you at least 10 coyotes wandering nearby the mission area for you to hunt.

6. I bought a lot of ammo worth over $1000.00, but I didn't realise it was incompatible with the guns that I already own. This rendered the newly purchased ammo to be useless and my cash wrongly spent. The worst thing is that I can't even trade ammo back for cash or for any other category of ammo. That's a shame! I expect the game to prevent me from buying any ammo which isn't compatible with the guns that I own, or at least it should show me a list of guns only that are compatible with the ammo. Either ways when you purchase a gun, the system should show you all the information about which ammo is compatible and which isn't; vice-versa should also be true i.e. when purchasing ammo (all compatible guns need to be displayed). A proper table with select-able options and columns is all what is needed here.

7. Why do you need to spend $250.00 at an outpost to sleep? Isn't that supposed to be free? A new player will keep losing whatever little cash that they had earned only to be spent for resting at outposts. In my opinion, resting needs to be made free as a means of going back to the saved time instead of restarting the whole mission again. I think that is the only chance one can get their time back without spending cash at the moment.

8. There should be a feature to allow saving game progress to a savepoint. The player doesn't have to fiddle with time going backward or forward. Just introduce a game save feature that will allow a player to go directly to that savepoint. If they had forgotten to do something, they can continue the game from there anytime.\t\t\t\t\t
