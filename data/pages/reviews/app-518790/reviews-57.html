\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is my first experience with a hunting simulator and has swiftly become one of my all-time favorites. The game demands patience, particularly in the early stages. I started in the expansive Layton Lake District reserve, where I continue to hunt and complete quests. My first successful hunt came after several hours of gameplay. Initially, I kept inadvertently scaring off animals without even seeing them. I then discovered that moose were the least timid creatures in the area. I set out to hunt a moose and managed to stealthily crawl within shooting range. However, due to either the insufficient power of my starter weapon or non-fatal shots, the animals would flee, wounded, and I couldn’t find them. My first successful harvest was a moose cow, which I stumbled upon accidentally and managed to take down with several point-blank shots from various weapons. She took me down in the process as well.
With time and practice, I’ve honed my skills and can now harvest animals with relative ease. However, certain animals still demand patience, a skillful approach, and a well-placed shot to a vital organ. Additionally, some quests present quite a challenge, requiring not only patience but also a dash of creativity for successful completion.

Overall, I really like the game. Even after more than 200 hours of playing, there is still a huge amount of content left. I appreciate that this isn’t an arcade game, but rather a game that closely resembles real hunting (as much as a game can). That being said, there are deliberate assumptions in the game to simplify certain aspects. For instance, I don’t believe that in real life, wild animals would allow you to get as close to them as you can in the game, or that they wouldn’t notice your flashlight at night or your dog barking. However, I think the developers have managed to strike a balance, making the game appealing to both supporters of realism in simulation games and casual players who might be put off by excessive realism.

Many people complain that most of the content of the game is locked in the DLCs. The DLCs do bring a lot of additional content. However, the base game alone has enough content for hundreds of hours, and the DLCs are not expensive, especially when they are on sale.
At present, the content offered by the two reserves in the base game sufficiently meets my needs. I have yet to explore the reserves introduced in the DLCs, as I intend to complete the quests in Layton Lake and Hirschfelden first.
Since the game has enough outposts for fast travel, it won’t take much time to get to any location on the map after you unlock the outposts (use the lookout towers to find them), and walking adds to realism. But if you’re not a fan of walking and want to make your life easier the ATV DLC and the Tents and Ground Blinds DLC should be the first ones to consider purchasing.

So, whether you’re an avid hunter, someone who has never hunted but wants to experience it, or simply a nature lover who doesn’t have the time or opportunity to venture outdoors, I recommend this game to you. By the way, the game’s depiction of nature is truly beautiful.
I don’t hunt in real life because I can’t bear to kill animals. However, this game allows me, and many others with a deep-seated hunter’s instinct, to fully engage in the thrill of hunting and tracking prey without harming a single real animal.\t\t\t\t\t
