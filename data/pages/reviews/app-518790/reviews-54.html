\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot to offer. For its strengths it's very well made and is a ton of fun and very immersive, but it has some really glaring deficiencies.

The biggest is there is no way to pause the game, and when you load a saved game you are placed at the nearest waypoint. If you haven't unlocked any additional outposts or placed any tents (you have to pay more for the tents), you have to walk (back) to where you want to hunt. Considering the movement speed is realistic and the sound punishing when rushing and extremely punishing when using the ATV, this means that if you have kids like me and I would guess most of the playerbase for a game in this style, you're going to be spending most of your time walking back to where you want to hunt instead of actually hunting. I personally could not stand this (it completely ruined the game for me), so I modded the game so my run speed is a lot faster (3x). Which comes with significant downsides in and of itself, like being able to abuse it and run as fast as animals that are fleeing. I choose not to play multiplayer, because mods like increased run speed are really bad for multiplayer.

Many players have requested pause and exact location saving to be added. But I don't think they'll add them because they're not something they could reasonably put in a DLC.

Other glaring deficiencies are things like:

The fact that clothing makes absolutely no difference on whether you are more easily seen, which is not reflective in the slightest of real life hunting.

Settings are stored in the saved games so moving from different computers absolutely jacks your experience without doing an initial graphics adjustment (like trying to play on PC and Steam Deck)

There only one saved game allowed--if you want to try a different build you have to start from scratch or reset your skills / perks (extremely expensive) every time you want to adjust anything.

Any map with mountain lions/ pumas/jaguars they're constantly chasing away your game. Populations need to be adjusted because irl you almost never see or hear these animals yet in game they're insanely common and ruining everything most the time.

Map markers -- you can add a single waypoint that is lost when you close the game. A HUGE QOL feature requested would be to be able to add multiple map markers or waypoints that are persistent play session to play session

Map filters -- being able to hide / show POIs

You can't swim--if an animal lands in the water, you have to sit there and wait for the animal to arrive. For birds this was a huge problem so they kind of solved it with the labrador DLC, but it would make far more sense to just add swimming or a way to traverse the water to the game.

There's no unit slider. Purchasing ammo you have to purchase in 10s no matter what.

You can't remove items from your inventory, if you bought the wrong ammo you have to literally shoot it to get rid of it. Significantly clutters your inventory.

Animal render distance. They disappear at 415m about. Which isn't super realistic, considering you're using the same rifle that has been used by snipers to hit targets several times that distance. Would be nice to try my hand at very long range hunting.


There are a lot when you get down to it. However, the game is a lot of fun to play. These are things that could easily be added if they took a month of development time away from making DLCs. I understand they're a relatively small team, but this game has a very active, very passionate player base and it's hard to imagine that they wouldn't be happy to pay for most of these as a QOL DLC. I know I would. Unlike almost every other game making team out there, I WANT to support the continued development of this game. I know that they aren't selling millions of copies, and I know this isn't an extremely widely played genre. So I get that they need to make DLCs to stay afloat, and I'm not against that. But please add some of these.\t\t\t\t\t
