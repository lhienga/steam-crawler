\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR, Keep an eye on this game if you like Runescape. It has flaws, but could go far.
--------------------
Decent alternative to Runescape, but missing a handful of the core elements that make Runescape so addicting.

My main gripe is the lack of meaningful or relevant quests. A Runescape quest will give you a goal, ie, "reach 25 mining and 25 smithing," which creates a milestone for you to reach and incentive for you to level those skills. Then to reward you for leveling those skills and completing the quest, Runescape quests often reward you with game-changing items that will unlock new shortcuts, make skilling easier, or give you access to something or other you never had before.

Quests in Black Grimoire are pretty standard sidequests. Kill 5 bears, receive 150g and some archery exp. I will give them the credit that some of the quests have multiple outcomes, but if anything, I would argue that hurts the experience in a game like this as that just incentivizes you to wiki the correct answer for the best reward.

I think with a reinvented quest system, some tweaks to balance a few of the less valuable skills, and continually added content, this game could be a real gem and could become a well-known title among Runescape fans. It's got a ways to go, but I'm hopeful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike others I jumped on the demo after watching Splattercats review of the game, also like others I feel this has a lot in common with old Runescape. I'm a few hours in to the full game now and I really like it.
Very simplistic mechanics, beautiful artwork with lots of little details, very much a casual game.
I feel like this would be a great game for kids, it's easy to grasp and play and I know my Grandkids would love it, so I hope that Olipa does well with it, would be nice to see it ported to other platforms so kids would have better access.
So far from what I've seen I think the game has serious potential but even after a year of EA going by I can still see it being a very short game, other than repeat plays using different weapon sets I can't see most people sticking with it for long.
Would be nice to see a procedural dungeon added at some point so after the main story is done you will have a reason to continue playing the game, maybe something similar to the old Fate games.
All said and done I really like whats here and I think the Dev team has done a great job thus far!
It's worth noting that the game runs very well for an EA title, I've not had a single issue with FPS or game breaking bugs.
Pretty good little RPG for $15 bucks just know ahead of time the content is very casual and won't last long.
Thumbs up!

*Edit at 9 hours in*
Just wanted tp follow up and say that after spending more time in game that there is far more content than many reviews might lead one to beleive.
I've also come across many paceholders and it looks like much more will be added.
The game does seem to have a lot of grind...but thats not a bad thing in this case.
Given the beauty of the artwork and the impressive music you will lose yourself in the world.
I would rate the game a 9/10 at the moment, 10/10 after more content arrives, solid buy!
