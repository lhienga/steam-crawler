\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game reminds me a lot of OSRS if it was a singleplayer game with an good story that I'm actually interested in. Currently about 7-ish hours in (Steam doesn't count the beta hours) and I'm loving it! You still get to see other players walk around in the world and you can talk with them. You just can't really do anything else together. But it does make the world feel more alive.

So far the only things that I got slightly bothered by are:
- The inventory is fairly small, especially at the start before you unlock a house or can use a bank. If you're a hoarder like me and you find it hard to throw stuff away, you're gonna be running to your storage a LOT.
- You can't zoom out the map for a full view of the whole map.
- As far as I know you can't lock you minimap or worldmap, so they keep rotating whenever you rotate the camera. I personally don't like that and I would like to have a way to lock them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked up the game after seeing it on Splattercat's channel. So far it's been a lot of fun! The game takes a lot of inspiration from Runescape - obviously - but it has it's own little charm. There's a lot of nicely animated little cut scenes and the story so far is fairly interesting. You'll find npcs prattling on to each other about various things which are fun to overhear. The combat is pretty simple, but you do need to keep an eye on your resources and it looks like there's plenty of buff/de-buff types of potions/spells to play around with as well.

Quick Tips for beginners:
-if you want to gather a lot of a specific resources early on choose the "trait" for it. For example, Woodworking gives you a satchel that'll carry 5 wood instead of each taking up a space in the inventory, Alchemy allows you to stack 20 empty vials instead of 10, etc.
-once you're past the graveyard bit you'll get access to a storage coffer. Just follow your Wizard Master into the woods.
-your first shop will be in the town after the graveyard.
*I'll add to this if I notice anything else that could be helpful to know

For the devs:
While the inventory is rather small I think just one more row of 4 spaces would really make it more manageable without it being "too much." Also, Thank You! for having a UI slider in your game. I play on a TV monitor and there's so many games that lack this basic feature so I can upscale the text to better see it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSober review:
Recommended but with a caveat, the game feels like runescape in many ways, in its skill leveling system, it's graphics and right down to it's map setup. This is both a good thing and a bad thing. If you enjoy old school style rpg's down to their quest systems and other foibles then this is the game for you. Otherwise this might irritate you before you make it past the first few tutorial levels. Will look forward to see how this game progresses, but the opening levels have me putting it down. For now.

Happy coding team!\t\t\t\t\t
