\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those cases where I feel Early Access games would benefit from a different set of "Recommend" options. This game has a lot of things going for it, but I'm giving it a thumbs down for now purely because realistically it's not something I would recommend to anyone I know in its current state. I will change to thumbs up in the future depending on how the development goes. For now I'll say only buy it if you want to help support the project, otherwise just try the demo and wait for full release.

First impressions after about 15 hours of game play:

Excellent foundation for a Runescape-like RPG experience but currently lacks any real depth. The full game somehow still feels like a demo to me. It sorely needs more interesting story/lore content added. It's marketed as "lore heavy" and "story driven", which is kind of true for like the first 45 minutes, but as soon as you're past the opening segment (essentially the tutorial) the narrative takes a hard nose dive into the dirt and the game basically turns into an endless series of bs fetch quests.

Oh the quests, the quests, the quests. All the quests I've encountered thus far are lame af. Maybe they get better further into game, but so far they are not doing anything for me. There does seem to be hints of interesting lore but it's been pretty sparse. My primary motivation to keep playing at this point is hope of finding more nuggets of this lore, and some diminishing optimism that the main quest will get interesting, sometime. Maybe after the crusty old necromancer stops jerking me around, eventually, at some point.... perhaps. Because lemme tell ya, the general game play, as it currently is, is not holding my interest much longer.

Inventory management is a problem. And by that, I mean it's just gawd-awful. It's fundamentally like Old School RS inventory system, only they somehow made it even worse, like way more tedious. Why? I have no idea, because there's no real economy in the game that I can tell, apart from a few simple shop supplies and some skill training costs. The only purpose the inventory design seems to serve is to make the game significantly less fun. You have 24 backpack slots and about 95% of items in the game do not stack. And playing the game involves picking up, carrying, and swapping out lots of items. This is a massive impairment to the game play enjoyment because game progression requires skill leveling, and skill leveling requires a good deal of grinding, and grinding requires traveling to/from the bank, and banks are kind of scarce in the world, similar again to RS. Just trying to do anything in the game feels more like a chore than a game purely because of how tedious inventory is. (there is however, a fast travel mechanic via horseback riding that's limited but welcome)

Unlike RS though, the skills are very, very basic. Combat skills only have 4 or 5 perks to unlock, and crafting/gathering seem to have even less. Leveling up also allows to gather/craft/equip better stuff, but it's all severely limited in variety. Things might open up a little more after progressing further through the main story but I'm not sure.

The game does have a lot charm to it. The simplified visual style is well done and I like the general world design. The environments are well crafted with a good amount of detail and ambiance. There are some solid bones here with tons of room for the developers to flesh out into an excellent game.

I guess to coin a phrase, "The game has potential." is appropriate here. The inventory needs to be completely reworked imo, but otherwise it's a solid start and just needs more content. Which is what Early Access is all about. So I'm hoping the devs keep the updates coming and am looking forward to the day I can give it the ol' "Thumbs Up".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played this Early Access for quite a few hours, I believe I can give an honest review.

This game will be attractive to Old School RuneScape players as well as who played RuneScape back in the day.

Game is in its early days. There are bugs that needs fixing and some optimisation issues in the current version of the game. As far as I am aware devs are listening to players feedback and working on fixing bugs, this can be seen by frequent updates and hotfixes.

About the game itself, there are quite a few side quests and a main story quest line. Some quests could be improved by giving more accurate directions, but taking into consideration that the game is in EA, it is decent, hopefully with much more content to come.

Skilling: Some skills could be improved to assist the player and encourage to skill. For example Woodsmanship, cutting spruce till level 20 is a tedious and long grind, whilst fishing has more options. For instance, you start off with level 1 fish, unlocking new species at level 10 and 15, which has increased xp, therefore not making the grind so tedious. On top of that, tailoring although the variety of different gear can be unlocked every couple of levels, acquiring materials can get slightly frustrating and unattractive.

Inventory: Even though the inventory is limited to 24 spaces and some materials stack only up to 5/10/15, experience scaling and inventory slots are balanced in my humble opinion. Most of the fuss I have seen in the reviews are from players who want to get the best possible gear without putting much effort in achieving that. Experience scaling - well done developers.

Overall, there are plenty of bugs and optimisation issues that needs to be addressed. Nevertheless, this game has a great potential. This small developers team has a done a great job, taking an inspiration from a legendary game that most of players have heard or played before. With the great inspiration and vision ahead, this could become a truly great game. I will be giving this game a few months break and head back in to see how this game has improved over time.\t\t\t\t\t
