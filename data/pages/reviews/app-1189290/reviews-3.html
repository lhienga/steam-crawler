A lot of buildings are empty and some areas cannot even be accessed. It is a very early stage of early access. As a warning for those who are interested in a fleshed out gameplay. You won't get it.

The worst is the inventory space. Trust me it kills all the fun of the game!

The Game advertises itself as:

Variety of quests with deep writing.
(Go get me 15 carrots! Dont ask questions! - is not a well written quest and they don't wary much either and there is no deep writing so far that i could tell)

Numerous weapons and spells to employ in strategic combat.
(There are few weapons and spells its just numbers go up, nothing special and there is no strategic combat)

Permanent choices that affect gameplay.
(Didnt see anything yet)

Large open world that rewards exploration.
(Large open empty world that wasn't rewarding in any point so far. Most are dead ends or very strong enemies you cannot beat)

Skills & Crafting systems that offer freedom of playstyle.
(This basically says grind a lot so you can craft a lot and go back and forth a lot)

Customizable player houses to rest your weary bones.
(There are indeed a lot of houses to buy can't say anything about that)

Social multiplayer - meet other players in towns, taverns etc.
(Nice idea for the future when the game has a playerbase i guess. I only saw 1 player so far)

It has real potential to be good but as it is right now its not enjoyable. Just FYI!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, I will say that this is definitely early access quality. It is relatively stable and complete - better than many production releases. But, there are a few rough edges to smooth off, still. Really, the only thing that adversely affects game play, for me, is that the keyboard/mouse control is a bit wonky. Many games, these days, use WASD for movement, and the mouse for camera control. This game does the opposite. Click-to-move is problematic because you also click to attack and interact.

There is an experimental setting that lets you use keyboard WASD for control, but apparently you can't control the camera with this setting enabled. Hopefully controls will get smoothed out.

But, with that said, this is among the best of games in this genre. The content/story is definitely richer and more interesting than most. The quests are pretty well considered and encourage you to explore. I do find that most quests are not straightforward - there are usually complications along the way. My complaint is that, frequently, these complications require you to go back and forth between locations you've already visited, rather than always sending you some place new. It makes the world feel smaller than it actually is.

In short, this game is not perfect, but it is excellent for an early access release. The potential is there for a really great game. However, rather than talk about the potential, I would rather talk about how the game is right now. And, right now, it is worth purchasing ... but just barely. I think I paid $15 - $20. If I had paid more, I would have been disappointed with its current level of development. As it is, I feel like I am getting my money's worth.\t\t\t\t\t

Extremely happy with this game overall.

Put a handful of hours in already and loving the aesthetic and feel. Lots of small quirks and bugs but nothing that has stopped a session or inconvenienced me.

Some things I'd hope to see are:
- option to bind SteamInput as well as mouse and keyboard. Don't worry about 100% pad support, but there's potential for analogue sticks in view & movement
- Cloud saving
- For people with mouse-accuracy issues or low framerate, similar to Ctrl-click for attacking nearest enemy
- - modifier-click to pick up nearest item
- - modifier-click to interact with nearest object

I think my only real complaint is that the users character has a name. In this style of RPG I would have expected to be allowed my own name. None-the-less the plot is interesting and I'm enjoying it thoroughly\t\t\t\t\t
