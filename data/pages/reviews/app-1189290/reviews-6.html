\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended, provided you like old-school RPGs, because the school of this one is decidedly old. I downloaded the free demo the other day and played the hell out of it. Came to the store page to wishlist it, and realized that it was scheduled to release in ~8 hours. Lucky me! Picked it up the second it was available.

I never played RuneScape, nor do I know anything about it, so I can't comment on the similarities this may have to that game. But I can tell you that this game does a good job of giving you simple, straightforward systems and then throwing you into the game's open world. Off the top of my head, I can offer a couple highlights:

  • You are given pretty free control of the camera, which I like. I stopped counting how many games I wanted to play, but gave up on because the camera/zoom/FOV was too restrictive. No danger of that here, for me.
  • The UI is very simple. I like simple things. One thing that will always bother me is when game devs (or anyone, really) try to re-invent the wheel for no reason. I've played games that had incredibly awful menu systems, just because they'd convinced themselves that they had to do something different. You don't. When things work, you allow them to work; you don't try to fix them, anyway. The dev of this game allows it to stick to its strengths.
  • The world feels well-built, mostly. There are a few small villages and medium-size towns, and generally they feel pretty populated and alive. While there are a number of buildings you can check out that end up having nothing (and I mean nothing) in them, the overall feel of the environment is that it's populous, active, and there is plenty happening around you.
  • There are a number of types of fast travel in the game. You'll find portals around that you can activate and jump between, but there are also portal scrolls that will take you to any house/property that you purchase. There's also a mount rental system whereby you pay a nominal fee to ride a horse to another nearby location, hands-free. So you can either go the long way if you're into taking the scenic route, but you aren't forced to if you're feeling impatient.

Like I said, the dev allows the game to stick to its strengths. Considering there's a free demo available, I'd say it's worth sinking a few hours into and see if you like it.\t\t\t\t\t
