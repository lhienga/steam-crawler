\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo from the get-go I want to mention that in terms of controls and language I had no issues. It booted up in English, and it can very easily switch from controller to keyboard. Keyboard controls ARE a bit wonky, but in my opinion they're easy to get used too, although I would very strongly suggest using a controller. If you played any of the Harvest Moon's on an emulator, the controls are pretty much the exact same.

I LOVE this game, I adore all the HM/SOS games, but when I heard a remake/remaster of A Wonderful Life was in the works, I was ecstatic!! The graphics are so cute, it brings more colour and life to the game, though there are some differing art styles that are noticeable in the game. Dialogue is very much updated, there are some new cutscenes that add new and different dimensions to existing characters, it's just such an amazing feeling to re-explore the game itself.

Now my minor dislikes lol: the animations seem to be the exact same as the original, so with the updated everything it felt janky at first, and there are certain pieces of dialogue that has errors (ex. Pui calling Coco Valley, Popo Valley). Some content was seemingly cut or not expanded on (ex. child's diary. It used to change from month to month, but so far I have only seen 1 diary entry per applicable year). This is what gives me a mixed feeling the game initially, even though I still love it, I wish I would have waited until there was a sale. $70 CAD, I did expect more, polished content.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a typical case of wishing there was a rating between 'recommended' and 'not recommended'.

As a fan of the original game, 'Harvest Moon: A Wonderful Life', I've been enjoying this a fair bit. Quality of life improvements are sparse, but appreciated. However I would find it difficult to recommend this remake to somebody who doesn't already have a fondness for the original game. This remake simply doesn't stack up against more modern farm sims in terms of features. Somebody playing this game with a totally fresh perspective for the first time today would most likely find it lacking and would be better off playing one of the numerous alternatives instead.

One aspect that I do find to be a downgrade from the original personally is the visual style. Seeing the valley at a higher resolution and frame rate is nice, but I do miss the more 'earthy' and desaturated look of the original game. It gave it a distinct melancholic and 'down to earth' vibe that fit the themes of the game and I feel this is lost to an extent with this version.

That isn't to say I think this is a bad game in its own right, although it's difficult to not see it through my own biased, nostalgic lens. It's a solid farm/life sim, with most of the fundamental stuff that you would expect, but I would suggest waiting for a sale if you don't have an attachment to this game already. It definitely feels like the developers were primarily focusing on appealing to people that do, down to the game using a lot of the original sound effects and such. It can certainly be described as a faithful remake, for better or worse depending on your perspective.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tadmittedly I've not played this version terribly long, but based what I've seen through videos, my current playtime on this game, and my previous experience playing AwL repetitiously as a kid.

It reminds me why I fell in love with farming sims back then, which I've not felt in a long while despite my best efforts. I even wanted to make the next best farming game for a long time, to make this experience something even the smalls in my life can share in.

The map has a new life to it, and brightening the colors is part of it, but it's also just me recalling that maps don't need a lot of space if you know how to fill it. It's big without being too big and notable spots are well spaced. The inclusion of the river path between the woods and archaeology site among my favorite additions.

knowing the SoS folks would have to rename many things is understandable, and I'll get used to it, but one thing I dislike most is that Forget Me Not Valley became Forgotten Valley. Even if the name must change, the name doesn't suite the theme of the game at all imo. This game was always about cherishing the life that has passed. Wish they kept that spirit of remembrance in how the town is referred to so it doesn't give off the vibe that the community is shriveling or dying. It's a pointless complaint, but it feels like they didn't put thought into the intent of the name and quickly picked an antonym when even a very different synonym would have been better.

Regardless of minor flaws and inconveniences, the quality of life additions balance it all out. I'm happy, and I would ask anyone who is a fan of farm games to give this one a try, and maybe in this way we can share a very happy part of my childhood.\t\t\t\t\t
