\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMany complaints about this game appear to come from individuals accustomed to modern farming games. This game is quite old and it was attempting something different upon its original release. While it may not necessarily justify its price tag, you do have the option to wait for a discount. It places a greater emphasis on farming and character events. Not everything needs to offer hundreds of different activities or an open world map. Sometimes, less can be more. This remake represents a significant improvement over the original and previous re-releases.

However, it commits the cardinal sin of altering the art. I strongly disagree with many of the new character models and textures, they aimed for a more colorful aesthetic but made the art appear overly childish and stripped away many of its defining features. Nevertheless, I'm content with the Bachelors looking younger, as it was somewhat disappointing for female players of the game.

Another issue this game encounters is the lack of improvements in critical areas. For instance, it lacks a proper display of where character events will occur, akin to how Rune Factory handles it. There is no comprehensive guide on how to raise children, which remains one of the most mysterious aspects of the game. Even in this remake, the community seems to have difficulty figuring it out. There was no need to maintain such obscurity in this regard. And why did they remove animal death? That was actually a very important and memorable mechanic. The seed crossbreed mechanic is also still very annoying, you can't give Tartan multiple seeds, you have to keep spamming controls until you get the amount you desire.

It does improve gameplay by making the game feel faster and less clunky. The farming portion of the game is also vastly improved, it used to be very slow and not fun at all. Now it is very easy to maintain a steady schedule without feeling overwhelmed.

If you're seeking a modern farming simulator, this game may not be the right choice for you. It caters to a very niche audience and was niche even when it initially released on the Gamecube. It's somewhat disheartening that contemporary games often shy away from introducing aging mechanics like A Wonderful Life did. One of the most captivating aspects of this game is witnessing your character age alongside the villagers and marrying different characters can offer replayability.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI not only played this game on gamecube growing up, but have played it within the last couple years as well, so I am very familiar with how the game plays, and am not only reviewing through nostalgia goggles.

While I do hate some of the visual changes, specifically saturation choices that almost make it look like a mobile game, I have found that this is a nonissue with a few mods. I don't feel like they upgraded anything, really, besides some dialogue and cutscenes; however the one thing I always wanted since I was a kid was to romance the male bachelors as a male character. The freedom with sexuality and gender choices, as well as character customization, is a big enough upgrade to keep me playing.

I like this game. I like the way it looks (with recolor mods). I certainly wish it would've been a full remake instead of a reskin, but all things considered, I'm just glad I can play the game the way I always wanted to without modding a gamecube ROM that crashes every few minutes. I'd like to see Magical Melody get the same treatment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend the game for any fan of the original. I still prefer the original over this version but do appreciate the subtle changes that were made. I like the game because I am a long term fan and needed a game to just sit back and relax. I haven't played long enough but do hope we can upgrade the watering can eventually to water just a bit faster.

CONS:
1. I was really upset when I went to the trail thinking I was about to visit another town or something, but instead just lost six hours of game world hours and did nothing at all... Come on devs... that was really lazy and a slap to the face to us HM fans. The least you could have done is add some kind of feature or a crappy message saying "oh, maybe I shouldn't go there right now". But instead you got my hopes up for nothing...

*Things I wish the game had or could be added in the future*: "Only played about an hour though at time of review"

1. An update that could add some of the Harvest Moon DS features: marriage candidates, the sprite casino, the mines, ability to marry the Harvest Goddess, etc.
2. A new area in the game that we could visit like an island to collect stuff or go on vacation with the family.
3. An update to make the game look better in general on PC. Trees and foliage could look a ton better, even the characters.

DEVS, THIS GAME IS A WISH COME TRUE, PLEASE UPDATE AND MAKE IT EVEN BETTER!\t\t\t\t\t
