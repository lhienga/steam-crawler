While I do feel some of the renaming is a little weird and it definitely has its quirks, so far I love this game about as much as I did its gamecube counterpart. The mechanics of the tool upgrades are modern and intuitive, the new storage system is nice, and the additional marriage candidates have had really fun cutscenes and development in the overall story of the town. I'd recommend this game if you loved the previous one, and I think it's the best of the "Story of Seasons" Harvest Moon IPs yet. If I had any complaints, it's that somehow I feel like I have much more freetime in this version than I did the GC version, so I spend more time fishing than anything else. Trips into "the far off town" are good for timeskips, but I just wish there was a little more to do in town with the updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been waiting for a remake of this for a long long time. Seems to run pretty well, and has the same general pacing as the original version. I never feel rushed to get things done like in other farming RPGs. Be warned that this game has a set time/storyline and it will progress whether you want it to. I don't know how mouse+keyboard controls feels because I'm playing on a controller, but even so the controls feels a bit clunky. I wish the settings gave more control over specific actions.

If you really love other farming sims there is a high chance you may not like this one because there is a lot less to do than other games. I like it because I find it easy to relax and just have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJuly 14 Edit: Steam Deck Note

Have logged in a few times and the game is unresponsive to the deck. Was able to fix this by verifying the integrity of game files.

Overall works excellently on the Steam Deck. Only issue has mostly been for text screens. Before you hit Steam + X, just make sure you touch the on-screen text box.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

THEY USED THE SAME BACKGROUND MUSIC AND AUDIO CLIPS FOR ACTIONS <3<3<3

Lots of quality of life fixes from the originals. Watering plants isn't so touchy, in-water fish icon during fishing, shipping bins and ledger are directly outside now, the main crop fields are a bit bigger, and you can skip character intros - just to name a few.

Still can't steal Romana's cats though. Maybe one day ;-; </3

I really like how they've kept how all the individual characters walked in the original. It's small, but noticeable. A lot of throwbacks to the original games just with better graphics and overall gameplay improvements. I feel like I'm literally just playing an improved version of the original.

The marriage candidates REALLY got some glam-ups. Marlin Matthew really has the brooding anime guy look going for him now. It'll be between him, Gordy, Nami or, and this shocks me to say considering I hated his original design - Gustafa. RIP Rock - I'm just not looking for a Backstreet Boy.

New players might get bored of this pretty fast. Any of the people playing for the nostalgia I can say the full price would be worth it. If this is your first time with Another/Wonderful Life, you might want to wait until it goes on sale. It can get monotonous, but for anyone who played the originals - it won't be an issue for you. Word of the wise for any new people, make sure you befriend Nina and Galen early!

They give you a bit of help when you first start. I don't remember being given like 2k and an apple tree seed in the originals? I'd survived purely on the Mugworts I was picking for almost the entire first season.

This remaster is everything I've ever wanted.\t\t\t\t\t

A remake with improved farming gameplay mechanics, but the story is nowhere near as realistic as the original game's was.

So I have way more hours for this game on another platform, but my review stands for the PC version too.

This is a remake of a much older game that you have probably already played before. If you haven't played the original then I highly recommend you do so before trying out the remake. If you're expecting new additional mechanics with this game then you may be disappointed; it feels like they pretty much took the original version of the game, enhanced it with modern graphics and improved on some bugs or gameplay mechanics that needed some fine tuning like frame rate issues. Unlike the original version, the remake actually smooths out the farming experience to where it's actually bearable to play as a farmer now; planting and watering crops is easier, shipping items is easier, so on and so forth. They did add a feature to where you could accept villagers' requests for items or dishes in exchange for money so that adds more to the list of things you can do in the game.

Asides from some character name changes and appearances (due to what I believe are legal reasons) as well as a shift in color scheme throughout the game, I do believe the remake does a good job of sticking to what the original game intended to portray to the audience...except for a few things that could be immersion breaking enough to ruin the experience of playing this game entirely.

Everything is happy-go-lucky in this remake which completely goes against the brutal, realistic nature of the original game

The beloved aspect of the original game was how realistic the progression of the story as well as its gameplay mechanics were. For one, animals could easily die in the original game if you did not consistently take good care of them. In the remake, animals don't die at all. That is...not how it works in reality which is a large component of what players liked so much about the original game. If A Wonderful Life is supposed to capture the realism of life and its brutal nature, why are animals unable to die...? It is immersion breaking to know that you could go days without acknowledging the existence of your barn animals and they still will not die. It also renders caretaking of the animals rather pointless. In the original game, if you did not feed your cow for just one day it would most likely get sick or something...kinda like in real life? Yes, this is just a game, but when you take away an aspect that was already in the original version of the game that aimed to portray reality then that point becomes meaningless to argue. It breaks the immersion that the developers claim they want us to experience and enjoy out of their product in the first place.

They also beautified the character of Murrey, a homeless man struggling to get himself situated in society. Though the brutal nature of homelessness for a man was once captured well in the original game, they decided to completely ignore the elephant in the room with the remake when it comes to how harsh life is for homeless men. They actually went as far as to change his look completely from wearing dirty clothes, having nappy hair with bugs flying around his head to having colorful garments, well kept hair and butterflies flying around him. Now tell me in exactly what reality do homeless people have it that good? It is not only immersion breaking but it is completely insulting to the people who have experienced that brutal reality, and it is also insulting to take that away from the audience to be able to familiarize themselves with that reality somewhat through the character like players could with the original game. You cannot sell to an audience the concept of a rural village in which its inhabitants live in for a myriad of reasons such as escaping the depressing and lonely nature of the bustling city life or the poor life choices they made coming back to get them, only to completely ignore how reality works and go as far as to insult the audience in order to do so.

You can select your character's pronouns but cannot adjust the sexual orientation of your character

It is no surprise that the remake includes same sex marriage and selection of pronouns unlike the original game. After all, the original game was created in a vastly different timeline years ago. However, I have an issue with the way its implementation fails to really curate a gameplay experience for everyone like the developers claimed. It really feels like the game caters towards a minority group of people with a selection of pronouns or are attracted to the same gender, and this is coming from a woman that likes women.

When creating a character I decided to play as a male character for nostalgic purposes, since the original game had you play as a male and that's that (unless you played the version in which you could only play as a female character). Right off the bat they have you type out your character's name as well as select your character's pronouns. The problem with this is that that's all you can adjust in the settings when it comes to gender-based preferences. Throughout my playthrough I had other male characters flirting with me in many instances of cutscenes and there was nothing I could select in the settings to prevent this. That immediately broke my immersion of a game that once did not include any of these features; what if I was a straight male and did not want male characters flirting with my male character during my playthrough but instead have friendly instances with them? Or what if I decided to play as a female and I did not want the female characters flirting with me because I was straight? The developers claimed to add these features and cater the storytelling and character design towards these new features to make the experience inclusive for everyone but failed to really get the inclusion part down. They should have implemented a feature in which you could select your orientation like how you can in Sims 4 and they should have created different cutscenes for this feature, otherwise you are creating a gameplay experience that is far from inclusive and immersive.

Should you buy this game? I say buy it after you have played the original first then buy the remake on sale.

All in all, I do not think this is a bad remake persay. I think the remake does a decent job at capturing the original game's message and emphasis on family, social interactions, and life experiences and choices. It is also nice that they added the villagers' requests feature which really adds more to your things to do in the daily life of a farmer. However, I think you would be doing yourself a disservice if you did not play the original game first. Though they improved upon a lot of farming mechanics and graphical issues that existed in the original version, I still believe the original version did a much better job at displaying real life through the world design, character design, music and story progression.\t\t\t\t\t
