\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was the first game that got me hooked into the Harvest Moon franchise all the way back in 2003. A game that has stuck with me ever since, and is one of two games I kept my original copies of from all the way back then because I was too nostalgic to get rid of them.

I am in the 3rd month of the first year still, so there is still a lot to do and see. But figured it's good enough spot to post a review :)

The bad:
- Keyboard/Mouse does suck but was to be expected (I am using a controller dw)
- Having to press confirm after selecting things is a bit redundant
- Having to to say "Yes I am done shopping" instead of just letting me buy whatever I want while in the shop menu seems like something they should've changed
- The square that shows where you're selected on is very finicky in the mines, and glitches out sometimes when watering your crops
- Probably the biggest issue is naming animals. While at my PC I can obviously use my keyboard real quick to enter a name, it becomes an issue with my ROG Ally (and I assume for those using a Steam Deck or similar)

The good:
- While this obvious due the size of the game, but it runs great
- So far besides the annoyances mentioned above it has been bug free
- Graphically while it's not impressive it tries to stick to the original art style while using the newer aesthetics we're use to with Story of Season games
- Getting to see Nami again, because best girl :)

Overall while it wont blow your mind away if you already play modern Story of Season games, and it does miss a lot of the modern features and QoL that said games do have. But I do recommend at least trying it out!

(Though I do think $50 is insanely pricey for a 20 year old game, but I did pay it and even got the silly $4 outfit DLC)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played a lot of Harvest Moon games, but never got to experience the PS2/Gamecube titles. Though the original crusty PS2 aesthetic of this game is gone, it still feels like a game straight from that era in the best ways possible. It's a very charming entry in the series and I can see why it has a cult following.

There are some bugs and optimization issues, but it is otherwise a good PC port. I am playing it on steam deck and can confirm it runs fine. It's a simple entry, even more so than Friends of Mineral Town, but it is still worth it.

Edit: So I nearly got 100% in almost 90 hours of playtime. My final verdict for this game is that it is simply the best Story of Seasons game I have played. It does become repetitive around chapter 4 or so but that is about the time I decided to attempt getting all the achievements that I could. The whole thing with you and the villagers aging and watching your child grow really added a depth to the game that I have not seen in other SoS/Harvest Moon games I have played. Though the writing was minimal overall, there were a lot of really charming and emotional scenes scattered throughout that made me care for my little virtual family. I loved the character interactions and personalities a lot in this.

I really, really hope they are able to bring these mechanics back in a future title and flesh all of this stuff out more. I absolutely adored this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, I want to say I played the original on GameCube and I loved it.
*I think this heavily gives me a bias towards the game and I am aware of this*

SOME PROS:
The game is a faithful remake of the original and adds some nice mechanics/customization that before you could not do.
It adds some useful QoL updates like the Map of where people are in the village and the encyclopedia.
The art style is cute and is more colorful than the original.
The update on some of the characters is welcomed but they still have that charm that they had in the old game.
The music and sounds of the game are lovely, I have had no issues with music randomly stopping.
The cut-scenes are welcomed and feel impactful to the game.
The festivals are a good way to get closer with some villagers and are a good addition.

SOME CONS:
I do think, especially if you did not play the original, it can seem as if there is not much to do.
The game includes: farming, fishing, mining, caring for animals, and some other small tasks.
These can seem extremely minimal especially in a farming sim type game.
There were some days I was done with tasks by 9 a.m. and was completely lost at what to do.
I wish there were friendship hearts with villagers to see how our relationships are with them.

I would recommend this game, especially if you played the original. However, I can see how $50 for this game could be high to someone who did not play the previous version. Maybe wait until it is on sale or if they ever make any updates.\t\t\t\t\t
