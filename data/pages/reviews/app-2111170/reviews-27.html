\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I'd like to mention that I am playing on PC and the controls are not even close to as bad as what other people are claiming. They are pretty normal controls for a PC game for the most part, and if you're not a fan of something, you can just change the controls. I didn't change the controls and I have no issues playing this game. It uses WASD like every other normal game, and you use your mouse. Pretty straight forward.

I'm loving the game. It seems almost exactly the same as the old game at the start, but as the years go on you notice more and more differences from the original game. I love the changes they made to it, except I wish they kept the names of the villagers the same and the layout of the farm the same. I noticed they kept Cows the same, but changed the chickens, sheep, and horse. Unfortunately, I am not a fan of the new horse model and would prefer the old model instead of the new one. I am also not much of a fan of the fact that they removed a lot of the buildings for the farm that was in the original game and instead replaced them with tools. It just makes less for the player to do and doesn't make the farming aspect of the game as fun as it used to be.

I wouldn't decline an update that lessens how long the horse sleeps when it sleeps, because I end up waiting quite a while sometimes for my horse to wake up. And as a new player, I didn't know you could call your horse so I ended up just waiting for it to wake up, which was real torturous.

If possible, it would be wonderful if modding could become a thing for this game.

Besides that, this game is very fun if you're a fan of harvest moon games and slow-paced games. Most of the time you are just living a simple farm life, and a lot of the appeal of the game comes from friending all the villagers and uncovering their stories and making your own by having a husband or wife and a child.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a remake / remastered version of "Harvest Moon - A wonderful life", which I loved to play as a kid on the GameCube. Unfortunately despite this being a great nostalgia trip, I can not recommend this game to other players, due to the lack of content in direct comparison to other modern farming games such as Stardew Valley, Olive Town, Roots of Pacha, Travellers Rest, ...

Pro
  • Game stays close to the original GameCube version
  • Beautiful graphics
  • Homosexual marriage now possible and Lumina is now a marriage candidate
  • Requests have been added to the game
  • 70+ new events
  • New family events with your spouse and child
  • Four seasonal festivals
  • Camera tool to take pictures and selfies
  • New animals
  • New, rare crops
  • Map that shows you where every NPC currently is, so no more searching the entire town
  • 5 Minigames

Contra
  • The gameplay is extremely slow, animations take way too long especially the dig animation in the mine
  • There is not much to do in the town, harvesting is limited to the forest in the north (in autumn you can also harvest items in the town), the beach is basically only there for cut-scenes and fishing, mining is boring and time-intensive and the town people don't talk a lot
  • There is not much to do on your farm either, there is no crafting, you can't decorate your house, you can't decorate your farm or move buildings, there are only limited item upgrade options and it takes quite some time to get the required building
  • You are forced into marriage at the beginning of year 2 (I know this is like it was in the original, but we live in 2023 now, if you add homosexual marriage why not remove forced marriage while you are at it?)
  • You get to know that there are other places outside the town, but just like in the original game you can't visit them
  • No museum, despite fossils being part of the game. There is a collection album, but you won't get any rewards for completing it.
  • Tool upgrades are simply bought from the traveling merchant, in other games you need to collect certain items or do quests to get tool upgrades.
  • Fishing system is boring and way too easy, you don't even have to look for fish it automatically spawns every time you use your fishing rod
  • You can open a shop, but it's kind of a waste of time, because you can also sell everything to the traveling merchant
  • The items you can put into the shipping bin on your farm are limited, probably to force you to use the shop system, but that feels more like a forced "feature" rather than adding something meaningful to the game

In my opinion the developers missed out the opportunity to recreate this game as an enhanced version of the original and instead decided to mainly just copy & past the original.

If you played the original game (like myself) and you want a nostalgia trip, then wait until the game is on sale. If you never played this game, don't spent 40‚Ç¨ on it, buy one of the more modern farming games instead.\t\t\t\t\t

'A Wonderful Life' being so close to my heart, I'd like to offer a high-level review and comprehensive info on what is new and what has changed.

For those who have never played any version of AWL such a detailed review will not be necessary, so here's my review/summary and we can go into the finer details afterwards:

Summary & Review
SoS: AWL is a farming simulator set in the cozy 'Forgotten Valley' - keep various animals whilst growing crops, befriending villagers, finding your future husband/wife and over the series of chapters raising a family all whilst discovering new events & expanding your farm.

This remake includes features from AWL & AWL Special Edition whilst offering updated graphics, better performance, a new spin on music, some new features and various quality of life changes. You can present masculine or feminine, choose your pronouns & marry any eligible candidates (regardless of sex).

I'd recommend this game simply because the GC version had two separate versions (for hetero m/f playthroughs), some design issues and a less sophisticated sound design, whilst the PS2 (Special Edition) had many improvements, it ran absolutely horribly. Therefore this remake is a necessary one and worth it.

They did more here than the infuriating trend of simply updating the graphics and calling it a day. This is refreshing.

So for those new, that's all you need to know really, there are some changes I'm ambivalent about, essentially approach this game as a 'casual experience' because there are more safety nets now.

If you'd rather discover what is new / has changed on your own STOP READING HERE.
That being said feel free scroll down to the comment section on this review. I have put a short history lesson about the series name ‚ô•

What is new/ has changed?
New Features
CHARACTER: Choose your appearance (skin tone, hair, hair colour, body shape, pronouns and can change your clothes)
RELATIONSHIPS: can marry ANY of the male/female bachelors. There is sadly no way to set a preference at the start of the game and all candidates can trigger events - a pity if you wanted a platonic relationship.
CHILD: You can freely choose if you want a boy or a girl, no need for Mr Pots this time.
BULLETIN REQUESTS: You can now do requests for villagers for rewards.
SENTIMENT TRACKER: You can now view your relationship with all of the villagers in the pause menu.

CAMERA: You can take photographs in game and they're stored in an Album you can view. Added side effect you can see interiors easier.
CORKBOARD: collectable photographs throughout the story, I guess.
ENCYCLOPEDIA - Records all kinds of items and when you first obtained them. This includes pretty much everything.
NEW RECIPES (automatically added to the stove when discovered)
WONDERS - Collected for doing various things in game - act as in-game achievements.

COOP/BARN Upgrade: You can double your barn/coop capacity to keep 16 of livestock & chickens each!
SHEEP/GOATS: These can now be bred.
PREGNANCY: You will now need to manually animals into isolation close to birth.
CHICKENS: Hens can naturally hatch eggs by laying on a fertilised one for a few days.
MILKING ROOM REMOVED: I am not sure why. It's implied there is a tool to milk all your cows at once that exists.

TOOLS: Upgrades are now offered over time via Van instead of by order book.
FERTILISER SPREADER UPGRADE: Will automatically fertilise a field.
FERTILISER MAKER: Now produces 5 fertiliser.
FLOWERS: Trick Blue flowers can now be spliced to create blue variants of crops

QoL Changes
Animal Care
GOOD: Brushing is now the same as washing - no need to push animals to the trough anymore.
GOOD: Milk is auto-collected now when using the milker.
GOOD: You can bring chickens outside using a bell now.
BAD: You can no longer feed your dog - your dog is now automatically fed by Takakura.
BAD: Cows now provide indefinite milk output makes it no longer imperative to breed cows. Output will reduce but never 'dry-up'.
VERY BAD: Animals can no longer get sick or die. The only consequence is there is a new 'C' type quality milk that cannot be used in cooking or shipped. WHY?!

CROPS
GOOD: Selection only allows watering dry/tilled tiles. Fertiliser cannot be used on a max quality crop. Useful!
GOOD: Planted trees auto-block surrounding tiles to prevent planting. Useful!

General
GOOD: Underwater bridge between Spring and Digsite improves ease-of-access.
GOOD: You can choose items to sell at your market stall now.
GOOD: Shipping bin/order form/collection bin/tool & food storage are all conveniently placed outside and close together.
GOOD: Picking up multiples of the same item (e.g. flowers) can be done and putting things into your bag is instant.
GOOD: Like SE you need to fertilise your grass - however a tile won't stay fertilised forever, instead grass spreads to surrounding tiles over time to compensate for this. Pretty cool!
MIXED: You can see objects in the dig-site as you dig them up - however Flora/Carter standing close by can make it hard to actually dig.
MIXED: Forced wake-up at 6AM. Takes away risk of oversleeping.
MEH: You can leave the valley to 'visit other towns' but all it does is pass 6 hours. Future feature?
BAD: Territory capture: It's very easy now - no time limit and Charlie has lost his skill.

Aesthetics
MOSTLY GOOD: Updated graphics - (a more cartoony colourful feel. Chickens in particular look very different and you are stuck with cotton-candy pink sheep). You may want to turn down the brightness a little.
GOOD: Revised music - a new spin on music. Pity we cannot switch between GC/PS2/SoS versions)
MIXED: Localisation - improved translation quality, however the updated names to be closer the literal/spiritual meaning of the Japanese will be jarring to those familiar with the previous localisation.
MIXED: Design Changes - Gordy (Cody) definitely had a questionable design originally, the brow ridge arching over his eyes, wide strong features & the weird addition of facepaint however...did we really have to turn my boy into a Pok√©mon trainer? With the frat-boy build and loss of the mohawk I find it hard to believe this man PUNCHES metal into beautiful art. A change was needed but I am sure I'm not the only one missing when he was built like a statue.
BAD: Design Changes - Pui (Murrey) the dirty homeless beggar with flies buzzing around trope is not necessarily a tasteful one but the transition of this beloved character into a forest gnome-like creature with a resplendent crown topped with a flower and two butterflies, who now sings happy songs because I am willing to throw him a coin... I don't know. Pui was originally a reminder that the Valley is set in a real world and poverty exists, I can understand tweaking this character but I think this was too far in a different direction.
BAD: Design Changes - The Blue Bar is now the Bluebird Caf√©. In the original the drinks were not explicitly alcoholic but it was implied. I guess there's more stuff you can order now, but that could have been done without sanitising.
BAD: Censored names in a single-player game - you cannot name something 'Cass' because that it has a bad word in it.
BAD: We can choose slightly longer names but restricted characters - there is no on-screen keyboard with extra symbols like stars, hearts and so on available to choose from. Only non-accented alphanumerical names allowed.

That about covers it. Loving the game so far even if it's over-sanitised in some areas. Hope all this info was helpful to new comers & devoted fans all the same. Thanks for reading and happy farming ‚ô•\t\t\t\t\t
