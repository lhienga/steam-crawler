TLDR; Game still feels very much like the original IMO, just some QoL changes that I think have made the game a lot more playable/less convoluted. If this game is nostalgic for you, get it!

I was super hyped for this game to come out, as HM:AWL was a childhood game for me! When I heard that you could select your pronouns and customize your body, it honestly made my day as someone who is queer/non-binary! :o)

Mechanics:
They still feel reminiscent of the original, but tweaked so that basic farming skills like gardening or taking care of animals is less work-heavy. Fishing was tweaked as well in a way that I enjoy (I love fishing in life sims!!) I think the challenging aspect of farming is still there from the original, so this isn't super easy-peasy especially if you're a beginner.

Graphics:
I've noticed a couple textural glitches here and there. Hugh's model has probably the most noticeable mouth texture issue. In some of the cut-scenes, I noticed the models mouths clip through here and there-- but it doesn't really bug me too much. I did not experience any lag/performance issues as others have mentioned!

Launch-day Issues:
I did experience an issue regarding what language the game launched in, but thankfully I had a friend who could read Japanese and made it a lot easier to navigate the menu. I can see how this may have put some players off, but overall it did not affect my game-play experience.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUptop, running this on Linux: Proton Experimental on release date, and there are absolutely no issues it runs perfect. It even started OOTB in English, which is apparently a problem some people had? Shoutout to the team for Proton compatibility, I don't know if you had to go through trouble to achieve this but I appreciate it.

It's been years since I played what I consider to be a real Harvest Moon game, no Exp points, no skill tree bs. And I loved Stardew but Harvest Moon: AWoL has always been about getting a 2-3 things done, and then wasting the rest of the day doing normal IRL ♥♥♥♥ like picking flowers that you are just going to have to throw away anyway, being unproductive because you were distracted by music, trying to see more than one wild animal in one day, and even running halfway to talk to a old friend, looking at the time, and then going home instead to sleep.

This game is about living a wonderful life, I don't think you should be rushing relationships or only planting the optimal crops. Play with your dog, water your plants far too often, talk to townsfolk that live inconveniently far or you don't have gifts for, spend the entire season collecting ingredients to cook a dish and then eat it for breakfast the next morning. I don't know how the TV channel about luck for the day works, but I don't even want to look it up and ruin the feeling I get when I'm doing my usual morning fish, land a big one and remember that Yes indeed, today IS my lucky day lol. Sure I could just rush fruit trees and google how to get ruby's spice (just kidding they probably patched that) crop fusion for easy tons of money and buy all the stuff...and I do like money, trust; but the pacing of this game is really nice when you don't know/use any optimal strategy for a little while and slowly improve your processes until after the first year when things start to change in Forget Me Not Valley

QoL improvements are sick and appreciated. And it's nice that I can be dark skinned now, honestly they could have called it a day after making the hairstyle customization but they they went the full distance, cudos for that. In summary this game is chill af 8.5 Aromatic Herbs/10

PS I wanted to address some issues I noticed from other comments:
  • You can control the camera by clicking and dragging with the mouse. Its not in the keybinds, but what is in the keybinds is that you can left-click instead of pressing enter to interact with ppl/objects. As a result you can play with left hand on WASD and right hand on mouse. With that I don't see how anyone would have trouble with controls or camera, but as I mentioned it's not an obvious thing within the first minutes of playing I just figured it out by being like "Hey wouldnt it be easier if..." and yeah it is easier lol .TBH this needs a "Takakura's Notes" entry (which serves as the unintrusive lore-friendly tutorial)
  • Dealing with the inventory selection controls is something you eventually get the hang of. Apparently you can move between items in the quick-select menu with the "</>" buttons, just found this out I'll rebind the keys later to z/c or something. (Another thing I would like to see a Takakura's notes entry for)
  • Digging in the Digsite but Carter stands in front of only spot you want to dig at? Position your character so your blue box would be on the spot, then angle the direction your character is facing so that youre sortof not making eye-contact/facing them and you can dig! Finally you can get those weird Cactaur looking things for Nami!!
  • If you want to give someone a gift and they have a request, you select that you just want to chat and you'll give the gift. Not sure if that was a patch but yeah.

The fat cow blob from Story of Seasons Pioneers of Olive Town didn't get put in this game and honestly, mixed feelings on that, fat cow blob my beloved but the animal models look nice enough so meh


In Comparison to SOS Pioneers of Olive town:
As a fan of the story of seasons series this game is very comfortable and fits most of what you'd expect from this series, however that being said after playing Story of Seasons Pioneers of Olive Town this game so far feels a little lacking? at least the early game but I'm giving it a benefit of the doubt that it's the usual early game drag of farm sims where you're broke and bored and don't have much to do.

The lack of mining kind of feels disappointing? I don't mind a more chilled out game compared to the overload that was Olive Town where you had everything to do and very little time to do so (at least in my opinion) I just hope the sprites do more in the later portion of the game instead of just standing there and being cute little guys and giving me recipes cause so help me god if I get more useless sprites like in Olive Town I'm eating the red one.


Cons:
An actual complaint about the game though and not just comparing it to another, maybe I just didn't read the notes properly enough for taking care of your animals but I wish it was a little more clear that you can interact with them numerous times a day aside from just milking. That personally caught me off guard cause I'm very used to the typical farming sim husbandry experience being:

you run in > you pet > you get their items > you leave (or left them outside)

I don't mind this mechanic, makes bonding with your animals actually matter and more integrated with the game just wish for my 2 brain cell head wished was a tiny bit more clear.

Having something more to do during the day besides just cuddle up with all you animals throughout the day, water your crops in the morning, fish and frantically dig up as gold forks are you can before Van visits so you aren't broke for the next couple of days... would be nice? I mean the daily early game chores aren't bad but a little more? variety? would maybe help break that up a bit

Pros:
Not being overloaded with a lot of npcs is kind of nice, lets me feel more connected to them and being able to bond with them

While not knowing that I could interact with my animals a whole bunch throughout the day was a little frustrating it is pretty nice and something that helps the animals feel more like they matter instead of being there to spit out items for you to just sell and move on with you day

some of the marriage candidates feel like their personalities really shine through with their walking animations, poses when talking to the player and responses to getting gifts

the sprites are just little guys, look at them they're so cute. They're all just little guys (still gonna eat the red one if they don't help on the farm)

The game being open world is pretty nice and the map is fairly simple and easily traversal


Overall rating:
I feel like I have a lot more negative to say about the game than full on positive but don't let me stop you if you want to try out this game for yourself, you may like things I personally don't or I may enjoy things later on that aren't your personal favorite

that being said I would give this game a solid 7/10 and very open to changing parts of this review when I put more hours into this game and progress farther and farther\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up on Harvest Moon: A/nother Wonderful Life, so I was very excited for this remake and followed it very closely. So far I'm up to day 10 of Spring in the first year, and there's a lot I like about the game, but unfortunately there's also a lot I don't like about it. Some of the things I don't like are nitpicks, admittedly.

1. Not a fan of the graphics; a lot of colors don't look good together. Most of the game's textures are washed out and very simplified, while some things are darker and richer in color and more detailed in texture. For example, Chris looks horrible because her hair has gone from a yellow-blond to muddy blond, while her pink suit isn't dark enough in color anymore to balance that out. It's hard to explain, but I hope people will know what I am talking about. Again, this is probably a nitpick.

2. Like a lot of Japanese games in this genre, it's clear that more thought was put into the console version when it comes to controls. The keyboard and mouse bindings are an embarrassing mess and it's difficult to rebind them. A lot of actions in the default settings overlap, so for example, when I am trying to switch tools with the mouse, I have to be sure I am not facing any animals or people or... really anything, because the game will think I am also trying to interact with it.

3. I love that most of the sound effects are exactly the same as the original, personally. But when compared to the newer sounds, (such as re-recorded voices and music) they sometimes stick out like a sore thumb. Some of the older sounds are still crunchy and lower-quality.

4. Fishing is a lot easier in this game, thank goodness, but it's also... too easy. I'm glad it doesn't take nearly as long as it did in the original, but now you're guaranteed a bite within one second after casting, every time.

5. The game does run as smooth as butter. I was honestly expecting it to run horribly, so this is a nice surprise.

6. The range for when music starts playing in certain areas is way too large. The songs you have going on the phonograph on your farm start playing way before you even approach the entrance and it's very distracting and annoying.

7. I love the inclusiveness with same-sex marriage and being able to be non-binary. It was always so strange that you had to buy an entirely separate game back in the day just to play as a woman if you wanted. I could understand being locked in with hetero relationships since it was the early 2000's, but separating the ability to pick your gender was just ridiculous.

It's a good game. I don't think it's worth $50 USD, though. More time could have been spent on the PC version and making those controls easier to navigate and understand. I'm enjoying my time with it, but they've also managed to flick away a lot of the charm and character of the original with washed-out, pastel graphics and more cutesy character designs. (Can't believe they've "yassified" so many people, like Gustafa, Cody (Gordy) and Marlin (Matthew.)) It's honestly kinda conflicting to click "Yes" on recommending it for that reason. So, make of that what you will, I guess.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been a long time Harvest Moon player, so I have been very eager for this release since they announced it. I feel that it will obviously be more enjoyable for those who played the original back in the day for the nostalgia kick, but it is still good enough obviously to have new fans. That being said, those who are comparing it to Stardew Valley, and saying it sucks, I do not feel are giving it a fair chance. They are completely different games.


Another thing: people are complaining about the characters names being changed. The reason for this is because Marvelous and Natsume split, due to Marvelous acquiring their own in-house localization team (X-Seed). Natsume still owns the "Harvest Moon" name, which made Marvelous have to rename to Story of Seasons, while Natsume continues to make absolutely trash ripoff Harvest Moon games. A lot of the characters names changed because X-Seed and Marvelous wanted to have the characters names as close as they could to their japanese names. Some of them were already close enough to english names, so those ones are as-is or had very little change.


As for having issues with controllers not working, I didn't have this issue. I am using an xbox controller, but obviously can't speak for everyone on that.


Now at first, I was actually really worried this game wouldn't be good at all or have the Harvest Moon feel to it. Especially since the color scheme is clearly different and doesn't have that gritty ambiance the GameCube version has. Personally, I enjoyed the "thicker" models of the characters more if that makes sense. But I so far really enjoy the game, so where it is lacking in that, I feel has been made up by the improvements they have made since the 2003 version. Some of the improvements/changes I have noticed so far that I specifically enjoy are:


*Added Requests that also help improve relationships with people. It is extra stuff to do when it gets slow.

* They actually explain stuff a little more in the game. I remember as a kid being frustrated, not knowing what to do or how to do certain things.

*Recipe list

*Map that shows villager locations

*Stamina bar

*I havent gotten to it yet, but in one of Takakura's notes he mentions he will tell us more about hybrid crops when he finds out, in other words progression. Tbh this always confused me as a kid haha.

*Oh yeah, and obviously the fact you can choose to be a boy or girl, and gay if you want.

*Plants actually sway, compared to the prior stationary models. Its just cute ❤️

*It is way easier to navigate when watering, planting, harvesting, ect.


Lastly, everyone is talking about how this game isn't worth the 49.99. Clearly, it is subjective, I've wanted this remake for forever, so personally I would have paid $100. I've ripped this game apart growing up replaying it so much, but even with the burnout that comes with that, I still really am enjoying the remake now. I will most likely end up with 100+hours. Also, I prefer the old chicken models haha.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I was small, I was obsessed with the Harvest Moon series on Gamecube. My first experience was playing the first title, A Wonderful Life. Muffy quickly became my favorite love interest. I spent many many hours playing the first game, and then got the second game, Another Wonderful Life where I could finally play as a girl. I was very excited but I still preferred the first one since Muffy was my favorite character. For AWL I picked Rock because I didn't like the other options lol. Also played Magical Melody but didn't like it as much.

Fast forward to the announcement of SoS:AWL I was SO happy. I could relive my childhood with updated graphics and everything. Now that I've gotten to play this game I am very happy it feels almost just like the old game and this time you can customize your character which is awesome! I have noticed many reviews mention the controls, and while I do agree they aren't the best, I've gotten used to it rather quickly and adjusted to playing with the PC controls. My only gripe is how annoying it is to switch held items. I like how we have so many options for love interests now too. Muffy(sorry, I guess she's Molly now?) forever holds my heart but dang I want to romance others too now just to see. Wish they kept the name Muffy though.\t\t\t\t\t
