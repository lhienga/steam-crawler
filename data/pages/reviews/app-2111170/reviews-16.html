\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the different take on farming sim that this game has:
1. The majority of the game is focused on life after finding a partner and starting a family
2. Creating new plant varieties by crossing different plants together.
3. Improving plant attributes: (improving quality, increasing which seasons and soil they can grow in).
4. More variety in livestock types and products

I have never seen another farming sim use some of these neat features. I was hooked on trying to breed the best of each type of plant. (Many of these things are not available until after the first year, since the first year is focused on finding a partner. Get through the first year and the game expands).

I didn't feel like there wasn't enough to do with the time available and I didn't feel like it was any more repetitive than other farm sim (I put a lot of time into Stardew Valley). The seasons are way shorter, so each day represents more time. A year is just 40 days instead of the typical 120 (30 days a season). However, I felt this fit with the activities available in this game. I think the flow of time was just right.

This game definitely focuses solely on farming and socializing, so there is no combat area or similar things that people have come to expect from farm sims.

One of the best Story of Seasons/Harvest Moon games. It is one of those games that I revisit every few years. (I played the original on Gamecube).

I thought most of the quality of life changes were great. One thing that I liked in the original better was that milk production was only available after a cow gave birth, and eventually the milk would go dry. So there was a focus on timing and continually breeding new livestock that I though made it more interesting. Now the cows just give more milk after giving birth, rather than ONLY after giving birth. I kind of wish the developer would make "Difficulty Settings" or something to be able to choose some of these features in the original.

Overall, still worth the time and I am glad they brought this classic back and gave it a new wonderful life :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm heavily biased because the original is my favourite game, but this game is amazing and I absolutely love it. The updates to character designs are well loved by me and especially a certain homeless individual who was portrayed very badly in the original.

One thing to note is that the keyboard settings are really weird and I actually had to look up how to exit the game the first time I played because it's not the esc key it's the backspace key that opens the exit and options menu. I have changed some of the key bindings to suit my needs but they are still a bit wonky.

This is definitely a welcome addition to the Story of Seasons series and I am so glad that I can now play on PC even with the keyboard wonkiness. It's also important to note that the game is quite demanding in terms of cpu/video card so you may have to turn them down. I run most other games at high and I have to run this one at medium without antialiasing.

Another thing to note is that when you're in the dig site it's sometimes really awkward to dig the spots because they won't activate if an NPC is standing too close and they wander all over the place so you're constantly trying to dig opposite the NPCs.

But, the graphics are beautiful and just as I remember them, the characters are the same ones I've known for more than half my life, and it's really a treat to be able to play it all over again. It's a bit buggy, but I am thoroughly enjoying myself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a solid remake with some nice quality of life updates...but I can only recommend it as a nostalgia purchase for those who grew up playing the Gamecube/PS2 original. I'm enjoying myself as I was obsessed with the original as a kid, but compared to other farming sims and even other Harvest Moon titles this game's age really shows and the core gameplay loop is pretty lacking.

If I wasn't watching a stream or listening to a podcast on my side monitor while playing I'd get bored fairly quickly to be honest. There just isn't enough to do and see in the extremely small world which seemed much bigger back in 2004. Once I water all my crops (which now takes way less time due to QOL updates) and take care of my animals it's only 9 AM in game and I'm left just wanting to sleep and move on to the next day. Unless you care about seeing every friendship cut scene for every character, Forget Me Not Valley really has close to ♥♥♥♥ all to do in it.

If you're in the mood to try out a Harvest Moon game just emulate Harvest Moon 64 instead of playing this. And if you want to try out the farming sim genre just pick up Stardew Valley as it is very much the modern spiritual successor to Harvest Moon and far more enjoyable than this remake.\t\t\t\t\t
