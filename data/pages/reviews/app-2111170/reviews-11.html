Starting off
❀ ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━❀
The original game was my first ever game I played from start to end. Hearing about the remake I was just waiting on the edge of my seat hoping it was just as good and kept the nostalgia. My standards, like so many others, were extremely high and reading the reviews before I purchased made me really nervous.

At the time of this review, I have put in 46 hours. I play this game on Mouse and Keyboard.

Getting into it
❀ ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━❀
This game gives you ALL the nostalgia from the original. Yes, names were changed and I will forever call them their original names but other than that, I think it's INCREDIBLE.
-The cut scenes are amazing, in the beginning it is a lot it seems, but honestly I loved every single one
-The controls are really easy to get used to for Mouse and Keyboard, you can change them as well if needed. I ended up only changing one key (the pick up key)
-You can build friendship extremely fast, for marriage candidates, you can see all the cut scenes pretty easy if you are in to that before picking.
-The map is so helpful to find people in the beginning, but you tend to find out their schedules quickly.
-You are able to pick up the same object without putting things in your bag first.
-Your bag is HUGE! By the end of year 2 is when I found I needed to start using the storage more due to my bag filling up quickly.
-The Hybrid crops can be tricky, but talking with a few people you can learn a bit from it or just experiment and when you understand it, it becomes so easy to just keep going.
-You can save at ANY TIME! There is no need
❀ ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━❀
Reading some reviews, a lot has been fixed for mechanical things. I never once had it pre-load in Japanese on PC. The only thing in the beginning that took me a bit is to learn how to exit the game, I didn't realize you just had to press ESC and you will pull up the menu. The camera controls took a bit to get used to, honestly got pretty dizzy at first learning them as they are so quick!
I do wish you get more things from villagers, like more unique tools and such. But it does make it easy to upgrade your tools since its all in one area besides "blessed" tools.
❀ ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━❀
All in all, I find myself always wanting to play this game. I absolutely love it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis ain't it. Basically, if you're on the fence about buying this, don't. Either wait for a sale or watch some gameplay before you commit 50 bucks to it. If you do get it for full price, know that what you're paying for is nostalgia, but beware. Your nostalgia is LYING. Do you remember how much of a craphole little town Forget-Me-Not Valley was? I didn't. This remake shows off every problem with the original that you don't remember. It's a featureless farming game with bare-minimum relationship and farming mechanics, all crammed into a tiny town with nothing to do. The fishing sucks, the digging sucks, and the music doesn't change between seasons.

Overall, the gameplay experience felt kind of like being physically at McDonald's, but ordering using one of those new touch screen kiosk things. There's no meaningful interaction, nothing more interesting than menu navigation, that annoying Maroon 5 song you hate is playing on the overhead speakers, and you're beginning to question why you even bothered to enter the place when you could've just gone through the drive-thru. At least eating your limp McDouble alone in your car is more outspokenly lonely than being physically in the building and avoiding eye contact with the homeless guy waiting for the bathroom.

It's not a 0/10, but if you want a good Harvest Moon remake, go play Friends of Mineral Town instead. Better yet, if you want a good, robust game that expands on A Wonderful Life's setting and characters, play Harvest Moon DS! No, I'm not kidding.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished my first playthrough, here are my thoughts.

Pros
- Beautiful story of growing up, starting a family, and growing old. So rare in this genre, and really well executed!
- Certain elements have been made easier/simpler, such as being able to milk cows all the time, not just after they give birth, and having the option to breed goats.
- A really interesting cross-breeding system for crops, with a BUNCH of hybrids to discover
- You can live out the queer fantasy of dating anyone you want without judgement, and also have a child with your partner regardless of gender
- While the tone and overall feel is much lighter than the original Harvest Moon title, I wouldn't consider that a bad thing. You never feel punished for failing to befriend a character early on, either.

Cons
- While each year is rather short compared to other SoS and farming sim games, finishing the entire game takes some commitment (~150hrs, allowing for time i spent paused doing other things in the background). There's a time skip feature that i used a lot during the last chapter, and that helps a bit when you feel like you ran out of things to do between chores.
- Eventually, it's easy to feel like there's nothing else to do. There's ranching, farming, fishing, mining, cooking, and talking to people. But the character dialogue can get stale after 100+ hours, and there aren't many special cutscenes after the first few years aside from with your family.
- Also, aside from the few facility upgrades, there isn't much at all to buy in this game. Van's shop has special stock during the first few chapters, but in later chapters he doesn't add anything new if you've already upgraded your tools. Having some reason to keep making money would help this game a surprising amount, even if it was just more outfits or cosmetic upgrades.

Overall exactly what i hoped for in this remake. I would love to see a child raising system incorporated into other HarvestCrossings, because there's tons of potential for generation mechanics and dynamic in-game community growth in this genre. Hopefully this remake sparks interest in that, so we can move beyond trying to make the "next stardew." It isn't perfect, and feels a bit like the meme of a detailed horse drawing eventually becoming a child's doodle at the end, but if you want a farming sim that allows you to actually *finish it*, it's well worth the money in playtime alone.

7/10 for nostalgia and making me cry, points lost mostly for sluggish pacing towards the end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: This entire review is just the outcome of someone who, as a kid, only got to game Saturday and Sunday between Noon and 9 while also having to split the console time with two other siblings. The only way I've known to play this game is while on the clock and intense.

I absolutely love this new release of AWL mainly because, like myself, this was their first intro to a slice of life farming game and for many still IS the only farming sim game that has such an advance aging system embedded in it. (which is amazing for how old the game is)
This is what I think would deter a lot of casual slice of life players. Your farm isn't forever.

\tFor many, this game can start out as a casual play through but the aging system kicks in immediately. If you want to see and get the most out of this game a.k.a make money, build your farm and raise your family; you as a player are going to have to actually think about where you put your assists.

\tThis is where I think the game's EASY and HARD mode activate (if that's hard to believe)
Unlike other farming slice of life you have to choose which path you want to focus on. This is because, AGAIN, the aging system makes you need to prioritize your resources. In my opinion, crops would be considered HARD mode due to the many hybrid combinations (HINT: its not just the crops but also the crop quality) but I'd prefer every gamer make their own experiences and conclusions.

You can 100% play this game very casually and still have a great experience but you'll honestly end up walking away feeling like the game could've been better (because I have) only to replay and realize how different the gaming experience can be when you focus on how you want your farm to be from the start.
Side note: All of this detail is only for building your farm so imagine how the game gets when you have a kid that also has its own growing system that is impacted by who you marry and how you interact with them.
Basically if you're looking for a game that will probably consume you for the rest of your life this game is wonderful however its not for everyone because its not one of those games that you can really get "lost in" and benefit from it.\t\t\t\t\t
