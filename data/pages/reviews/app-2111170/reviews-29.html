\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who played the original, it was so special being able to load up a new save of A Wonderful Life after all these years and fully throw myself into that nostalgia bubble. I'm only a few hours in so far, but I can say I'm having as much fun as I did at 10 now that I'm 27 (and the quality of life changes that they made are so nice.)

If you're looking for a similar game to Stawdew Valley, then this game might not be for you, and that's okay. It's a lot slower than Stardew. When they titled this game "A Wonderful Life" they meant it; there's more of an emphasis on getting to know the townspeople and befriending them (there are quite a few upgrades and items that you receive through expanding your relationships with NPCs). They remember what you gave them as presents and make comments on it. As their relationship grows, their dialogue changes. You get to see the residents grow up with your character and your character actually ages! They get wrinkles, gray hair, and will comment about their age if you have them look into a mirror. You also get to see your child grow up and can influence what kind of job they have.

This is definitely more of a laid back farming RPG than what is usually on the market these days. It takes about 25ish minutes to get through one day but there are so many things you can do: dig at the archaeology site, go fishing, play mini-games with an eccentric pair of twins, grow crops and raise animals, etc. You can explore new cooking options and take some psychedelic mushrooms and talk to the smallest of harvest sprites... I will say this game has less features than a game like Stardew since it's more-so focused on the stories of the townspeople. If you're not into the narrative experience I probably would recommend something different.

Control wise, this game probably wasn't optimized for a keyboard and mouse and it shows. You can change the control scheme but it did feel annoying and I prefer using a controller. So far I haven't noticed much grief in the way of controls other than not being able to dig certain spots at the archaeology site because Carter and Flora are standing in the way...

As far as performance goes I was a little apprehensive about getting the steam version since Marvelous doesn't usually optimize their PC releases but I haven't had any trouble running this game at the highest settings.

If you don't mind taking a step back, learning about the townspeople, and taking in the story, I would recommend this game. If you enjoy a more fast-paced farming RPG where the years go by a little faster, I don't think I could recommend this to you. As someone that grew up playing every Harvest Moon game I could get my hands on, I really think this remake does the original justice, and I'm looking forward to living A Wonderful Life.

Oh, and if you get this game... don't forget to give Nina a flower everyday.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you spent countless hours as a kid playing this game and burning through your summer breaks and after school hours in the process, I strongly recommend picking this faithful remaster up. I have no gripe with any of the changes made to the game and completely understand/appreciate those changes. The developers seem to be very transparent on these changes and if you find yourself upset about any alterations to the original, perhaps if you hear them out in the developer log you will be more understanding. On a side note, I watched a youtuber play the Japanese release of this game, (joshplays) and I feel as if he sold this game short. He did not nearly touch on the quality of certain things that I find in my playthrough, so I should definitely recommend people to try the game themselves and be careful not to buy into other peoples lackluster experience. I can guarantee anyone who played the original and has stayed true enough to their inner child, will thoroughly enjoy this game. As for newcomers to the series, I will endorse this game and have you know that this was basically the Life Sim Genre progenitor, so throw some respect on its name.\t\t\t\t\t
