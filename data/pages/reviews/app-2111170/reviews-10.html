\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA huge draw of this game is nostalgia, as it is a remake of Harvest Moon: A Wonderful Life, originally for the Nintendo Gamecube. A lot of the game play reflects that it is a remake of 20 year old technology. The graphics were overhauled and some scenes added, but it is meant to play like the original. Along with the updated graphics, the controls were made less "clunky" and caring for animals is made easier. Like all HM/SOS games it does get "boring" toward the end, especially if you are a seasoned player. This game is meant for everyone and is intended to be relaxing/cozy so it does leave a lot of extra time to get everything for players who are new to the franchise or go at a slower pace.

Cons:
-Glitch with chickens/eggs. Eggs randomly disappear/reappear during collection.
-Dialogue doesn't update after year 3/4 which makes later chapters boring
-Some cut-scenes are too specific with weather/time/schedule requirements to trigger
-Characters are bland and not aesthetically pleasing (Even with the updated graphics characters outside the original Bachelorettes are ugly and don't fit with the aesthetic of the Main Character; they are over-stylized and have niche personalities that aren't interesting.) IMO, this game has the worst cast of characters of any HM/SOS game.
-Not enough story for chapters 3+

Pros:
-Easier controls+saving compared to original
-Updated animal care (no need to push each cow to water spout to wash) and tools
-Additional dialogue compared to original
-Unique approach to farm sim
-Fun and rather addictive gameplay for the first few chapters

Overall, I recommend the game as I did have fun playing it and didn't get really bored until the last year. It could have used more cutscenes/events in later chapters as the first chapter had so much going on that the drop off was noticeable. I never finished the Gamecube version, but was able to finish this one. Not a lot of replay value for me, but I'm happy to have completed a play-through.\t\t\t\t\t

A Wonderful Life was originally a Harvest Moon game on the Gamecube, and though it retains the trappings of most farm games (crops, livestock, fishing, spelunking), there isn’t as much moment-to-moment pressure to maximise your yields.

Where games such as Stardew Valley or Rune Factory lock you into an absorbing cycle of equipment upgrades and farm expansion, A Wonderful Life is content to let you potter around town at a leisurely pace.

Each season lasts just 10 days.
Sounds short but listen there are 7 Chapters that contain different increasing years of playing time.
There is no game (so far I know) where you will see how your child turns out when it becomes an Adult!!!
Influenced by your actions and your friendships with the community around you, even the partner you have choosen to be yours take a bit part in it.

That’s because the game’s focus is as the title suggests on having a wonderful life.
There’s far more to do after you’ve built up your farm, chosen a partner and settled down.
Characters age and change over time! New faces move in and others depart.
Some Character are cheery on the outside but may reveal more once you get to know them, as continual heartbreak, unhappy marriages or the pressure of parental expectations are not uncommon themes!

I've tried replaying the original in the last few years, but the slow movements/animations of the character and loading times always drove me crazy, 'causing me to drop it. Now with QOL improvements, faster movements, and speedy loading times, I find myself having a blast in this remake. There's a few downsides - I'm not a fan of some of the localization changes (Forget-Me-Not Valley is much more charming than Forgotten Valley imo), and the bar being turned into a cafe makes little sense due to the fact people still go there at night and still act like they're at a bar rather than a cafe. Nitpicks aside, if you enjoyed the original or favor farming/life sims, I highly recommend giving it a try if you enjoy a peaceful and laid back experience.\t\t\t\t\t
