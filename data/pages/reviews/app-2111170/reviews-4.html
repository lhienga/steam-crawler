\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr Sorry, but as someone who has not played the original but has played several Rune Factory games and Story of Seasons games, I found this experience to be pretty barebones and lacking. Every other farming sim I've played has had more depth. Rune Factory 4/Trio of Towns are better experiences in my opinion.

I only got up to the start of Chapter 2 before getting so bored, I stopped, so maybe my opinion doesn't matter, but I go a little bit more into that and why... I'm a guy that ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ puts hundreds of hours into ♥♥♥♥ like Animal Crossing and Farm Sims so a game like this losing me at a dozen hours is a feat honestly.

I understand that it's a remake of an old game, but when it comes to remaking something "faithfully," I feel like some compromises should be made in adding QOL. As it stands I feel like RF4 and the SoS1 and SoS2 have multiple magnitudes more depth than this game. A lot of little things add up to make the experience a slog. Why do the two mining dudes block you from digging when you're on top of them? Why can you not put Fodder in the bin when your animal is on top of it? Why can't you interact with your animals without pissing them off if they randomly fall asleep? Why does the map not show the current location of villagers so I still have to track them down within the acre? All the other games previously mentioned did this. Why is moving around such a slog?

As far as depth goes, all other prior mentioned games have 4 seasons with 30-31 days. This game all seasons are 10 days. Crops and products start at B, and go up to S within one cycle. Gaining friendship with villagers takes no time at all - justifiably since a year is so short and that's how long you have to confirm a marriage partner, but still, it takes so little time to woo a candidate that I felt absolutely nothing. Nothing really feels like an achievement because everything happens so quickly.

Perhaps the appeal of this game is that there are chapters and time skips and you get to see the progress of time. That's why years are shorter and everything takes less time, but frankly speaking all the little things and lack of depth as a result made me just stop wanting to play before I even got past the start of Chapter 2. The problem is there's no real overarching goal or thing to aim for I guess? For a while in RF4 and SoS I'm raising friendships/crop levels/gaining money/accumulating resources and the process takes long enough that the progression feels rewarding. I don't get any of that in this game.

Last thing worth mentioning is that the villager dialogue during gameplay is droll and starts repeating more quickly than New Horizons which is a feat.

Rune Factory 4 has a lot of unique dialogue that makes talking to villagers every day a worthwhile prospect, and the script has the energy and personality to make them likable. It has more farming mechanical depth and it even has combat/dungeon exploring on top of that. More QOL than this game. There's always something to progress that doesn't take so little time it's unfulfilling.

Trio of Towns has more depth to basically every aspect of everything, not sure what else to say about that in particular...

I understand nostalgia and that this game probably meant a lot to other people in the past, but as someone without that experience this game is REALLY outdated. There are better experiences out there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will be entirely honest: if you didn't play this as a child, you most likely will not like this game. It's slow-paced, clunky, and unpolished in comparison with contemporary games in the genre. This is not Stardew Valley, Rune Factory, or even Story of Seasons: Pioneers of Olive Town. It is, objectively, tedious and repetitive.

But

I love this game. When I saw it was on sale, my heart fluttered. This was my favorite game of all time as a child. I would literally spend upwards of 8 hours (if I could dodge my parents for that long) playing it in the basement, enthralled. I learned Excel just so I could keep track of my crops and animals. I drew so much terrible fan-art that I filled boxes of it. I wrote fanfiction of it before I even knew what fanfiction was. Every secret, every interaction, every obtuse mechanic has been carefully cut into my brain. The world, the characters, they all felt so... promising. So open. So very much like real life yet so very safe. I felt like I was apart of this story. I laughed, I cried. This was my game.

I write this as someone who's drowning deeply, deeply in the depths of nostalgia here. If I hear "Breeze Song," I cry. It's that important to me. That's why I modded this game to hell and back. Was it for gameplay? No. Was it for ease of use features? No. It was to bring back the old terrible 2004 graphics, the poorly translated names, and the music. That's honestly all I needed to give this 10/10, best game of the year. They could've literally just ported the Gamecube version to PC, and I would buy it on release day with a smile.

So would I recommend this game? Not really, unless you're like me. The controls on PC are wonky, even with rebinding the keys. The graphical settings don't seem to do much of anything, and the textures are still somehow jagged and blurry all at the same time. The characters are one-note, their stories trite. But this is my favorite game of all time, and I think it is for other people too. This was a foundational experience for me, and it expanded the series (and the genre) in ways that I don't think have ever been fully realized - even today. I guess what I'm saying is, that if you are my clone, you will like this game. Otherwise, I think there's other games in the series and genre that have better QoL features, better characters, and better mechanics.

But it's my game.\t\t\t\t\t
