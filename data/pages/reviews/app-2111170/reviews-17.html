\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas really excited for this game after months looking forward to it. and even bought the DLC. Was immediately put off by how front-loaded the intro/tutorial is. All the farm mechanics (including soil fertility, etc which doesn't seem to actually follow what they say) is shoved at you as soon as you arrive at your farm. This is fine, but I couldn't find a way to properly recap what I couldn't remember which resulted in aimlessly wandering/clicking things.

The BIGGEST issue for me is the UI. I've played clunky games and poorly ported games before, but this one was unbearable. Imagine a typical 2-joystick type camera (like Halo or whatever) where left joystick is movement, and right joystick is orienting the camera. It's essentially that, except left joystick is WASD and to orient the camera you must be actively engaging a mouse button while turning it where you need. I think a setting where you can remove the need to be holding a mouse button and instead have a normal fps style camera/movement control would make the game actually playable.

Also, just to note, it took me about 45 min of play to realize that you could even change the camera view at all since it wasn't mentioned in the tutorial that I recall.

Other issues:
- The toolbars are not at all intuitive
- No obvious 'move stack' option or tooltip on transferring items from one place to another
- No pause (escape) menu - can go to inventory to pause the game though
- Weird pet peeve, but when exiting a building you don't always have the same camera orientation as when you entered
- Interacting with some items (ex. wild flowers) only gives a tiny prompt in the bottom right corner. I have a large monitor so it's very easy to miss
- Would love to be able to access game tips, save menu, and general settings without going back to my farm house
- Kinda weird that they tell you to get married quickly multiple times in the tutorial :-P
- Farm doesn't seem to be very customizable (aesthetic-wise)

Pros:
- Generally really pretty environment (especially the water!)
- Characters seem interesting
- Intrigued by breeding animals/crops
- Character creation is pronoun-inclusive (she/her, he/him, they/them)\t\t\t\t\t
