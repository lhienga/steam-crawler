\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you loved the original then this is definitely for you. The systems and mechanics hearken back to a more simple time in the farming sim genre with new quality of life features that make it easier to re-experience. The remake is beautifully redone and truly gives you the same "feels" that the original did all those years ago. This is a more life sim focused farming game compared to many of the others out there and even some within the Story of Seasons/Harvest Moon franchise. All the usual farming sim features are there but expect progression to feel a bit different in this one (which I personally feel like it makes it stand out from the rest of the pack in a good way). As a caveat though, if you really want your farming sim to be all about crafting out the layout of your farm and hurried schedules of this, then that, then this, and rinse and repeat it may feel underwhelming to you. This one encourages you to make friends with the towns folk more, get married, and not live like a hermit the first few years until you've "made it" like most farming sims cause us to want to do.

All in all from the perspective of playing the original and wanting to experience Forget-me-not Valley once again this is a for sure recommend.

If you come from the newer age of farming sims expect something different than what you have been used to. It's not necessarily a bad thing but maybe check out a video review or two and see how it makes you feel on it. Although beware as there are a lot of sites seemingly have reviewers that are either not fans of the genre or never played the original. It's worth a try if your curious and kinda want something a bit different from the modern farm sim.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish Steam had a star system rather than a black and white Yes or No option. Though I would recommend this game, I would give it 3 out of 5.
As someone who never played the original Harvest Moon: A Wonderful Life, i cannot make any comparisons. However, I have played several of the Story of Seasons games. My favorites being Trio of Towns and Pioneers of Olive Town in that order. Those games were fun and engaging from the very beginning and had a lot to do from the start.
This game, however, is a bit boring.
Pros:
Decent graphics
Good time consumer
OK character customization
Cons:
You have a set space for planting, so you can only plant so many crops/trees at a time
Fishing takes way too long
Currency system is a joke (you can only sell certain things from your box for a small amount and to buy any upgrades and/or additional animals is outrageously expensive)
When buying things like energy drinks from Van, you can only buy one at a time
Definitely NOT worth $50

Verdict:

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can guarantee most people in the market to pick up this game played it back when it was on GameCube, or maybe a very niche crowd playing it on PS2. For those people, I can say with certainty that it's everything you remember and more*. I'm putting an asterisk because the things they add to this game that are different from the original are small, besides the new coat of paint. I'll go further in depth in a little bit.

First of all, let's talk about farming. One really aggravating thing about the original game when growing up was that there was so many hidden intricacies in farming that I just didn't understand when I was a kid. I didn't understand why my cows would not produce more milk during the summers. I didn't understand why my animals were all sick if they were in rain. Why my crops were dying because you could only learn what season they grow in from the shop menu. This stuff made the game a frustrating grind for cash using the few things you COULD instead of an intricate balance of learning and doing, just because FINDING the places to learn this information is hard. Luckily in this version, all of that is changed. Information is given to you upfront and accessible at any time through "Takamura's Notes" on the settings screen. But more importantly, they don't give it to you as an info-dump, but rather unlocking chapters over time as you need them.

Farming also received significant upgrades in gameplay. Watering is much less of a hassle with the ability to snap to a new tile after watering one, so you're not trying to angle yourself for EVERY crop tile. Trees now create paths between them so you can't accidentally plant crops in ungrowable tiles. Seeds can be tossed as an AOE by holding down the plant action. It makes it much nicer to tend to your farm without taking too much of your day to do.

Livestock besides the face-lift to the UI and giving visual indicators of health and affection didn't receive a whole lot more, but being able to know when your livestock have products ready for you, the bells letting them in and out now being MUCH less confusing, and not having to push each one over to the water trough for brushing is very nice. I wish we received more animals, but that's more personal opinion.

Villagers, besides getting a few redesigns are almost entirely the same. Dialogue is very similar to the original, the bachelor and bachelorette heart events are almost entirely unchanged, and they all have the same schedules as before. Nik, Nak, and Flak, now Jack, Ace, and Hart, are now actually worth visiting, as they impart cooking recipes for you to use in your kitchen (I cannot understate how insane it used to be that you didn't have a recipe book). Most importantly, actually befriending every villager has been incentivized using a new mailbox system that villagers can send gifts to, along with a bulletin board that gives you quests to both raise their affection easier along with score you some free items. I think the things they added are nice, but I still wish that villagers didn't have only four things to say every day.

Speaking of which, all of the original bachelorettes from A Wonderful Life, AND the bachelors from Another Wonderful Life are dateable, with no locks on gender. Clothes also aren't locked on gender, but they've been changed slightly as to be more neutral for genders across the board. The biggest thing though that's new for Story of Seasons here is pronouns, which can be set as He/Him, She/Her, or They/Them in this game. It's polished, it's well-implemented, and most of all it is SUPER affirmed through all the other changes they've made. It doesn't seem like much, but as a programmer, I know that means they had to rewrite a LOT of dialogue to work for They/Them pronouns to work. If you look at other games that allow pronouns, especially like The Sims, it's much more rocky and unpolished. So big thumbs up for them in terms of making the game that much more inviting for the LGBT fans of this genre. (Which is like, a VERY large chunk of its fanbase)

In terms of the hobbies you can perform, fishing is not much different in this iteration, other than the fact you don't have to wait all day for catches, and archaeology now has visual indicators that something is inside of the dirt instead of just digging in the same spot ten times in a row hoping that you're doing something. Minigames are back, and not much different, but they're fun nevertheless. If there's one thing I wish they added more of, it's things to do in this category. I guess it's never too late with DLC, but I really doubt we'll ever get anything like that included in there. The biggest disappointment here is the hill to Mineral Town, which now functions as a 6 hour timeskip--which you could also do by literally just sleeping. It feels like wasted potential to not introduce a new area with Mineral Town characters (or new ones!) or new activities to do.

Overall, I've been having fun with the game. It's a little brighter and more saturated than before, which in my opinion kinda changes the cozy cottagecore vibe I used to get playing the game as a kid, but the quality of life more than makes up for its shortcomings. It's still the little farming sim that made me feel like I had something to run back to when I was stressed out or tired, and to that point, I think there's merit. But there's shortcomings too. This game is almost entirely unplayable on PC without a controller (and we know how well Steam handles controllers sometimes). The game defaults to Japanese if you try to use the config tool first, which can cause problems for people who aren't the foggiest familiar with 日本語. Maybe with this port there's some potential for modding, but only time will tell.\t\t\t\t\t
