\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI impulse bought this game, and don't regret it one bit. I, admittedly, never played Harvest Moon, I only ever watched my brother play it. I might've played it for five minutes, but I was too young and too dumb for it, so I gave up. With that, though, I'm coming into this game a little blind, so some of my complaints might just be mechanics carried over from Harvest Moon.

If you like Stardew Valley, this game will capture your heart for very similar reasons (the romance aspect, similar farming & animal maintenance, etc.), but you'll be sitting there missing Stardew when it comes to fishing or mining. Both are incredibly slow and mind numbing, so I've gotten to the point that I'll just rest my thumb on the button to fish and mindlessly scroll on my phone, just using the vibrations and noises to guide me on when to reel it in. It's just waiting, and not at all challenging in the slightest. It's a little true to actual fishing in that regard, but when it comes to video game fishing, you can do a lot better than the bare minimum. The mining is similarly mindnumbing, you just mash dig on a hole until you dig something up. You just can't look away very long, since some holes don't have anything at all and you have to move to the next hole, so you're sat there mindlessly spamming the button to try to get through it fast enough. I've gotten to the point that I don't bother fishing or mining anymore. Not enough reward for all of that nonsense.

All that being said, it's a genuinely good game. I'm not very far in, but I've been playing nonstop since I got it and I've enjoyed every moment, I just wish mining and fishing were a little bit more interesting. I've been playing it on Steamdeck and it works just fine, and once I bumped the resolution up to the highest setting all of my graphic issues were fixed! It's only crashed once, which is less than expected imo.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who is very biased toward Harvest Moon: A Wonderful Life, I came into this with very low expectations. I did not enjoy the character redesigns in Story of Seasons: Friends of Mineral Town. Heck, I already did not enjoy the decisions to modify character relationships, families, etc. Specifically for the port of Harvest Moon 64 that became its own game, Harvest Moon: Back to Nature. Gotz will always be Karin's father, Rick will always be Ann's cousin, etc. Though, don't confuse that with me not saying it is a good game, because BtN is great. I speak so articulate because I enjoy the game, but I digress. Lastly, I expected the graphics to be very muddy, tons of creative choices done to hinder rather than improve the core of the game. There was a lot I could have thought of.

Surprisingly, Story of Seasons: A Wonderful Life deviates almost entirely from my expectations. It is not without me saying it has any issues at all, but it has far fewer than I expected. I had already been making this list since I started playing, and I am not that far into the game so it will not be nearly as in-depth as many others on here.

But here goes:
Pros
+ New art style does not look like a smartphone game.
+ Town Introduction can now be skipped.
+ Gameplay is incredibly more streamlined.
+ Through testing out various inputs, it is indeed possible to play with the GameCube controller for all necessary inputs.

Cons
- Load times are very poor when entering buildings [Steam Deck].
- "Takakura's Notes" are shoved down your throat and sometimes spoil stuff that should have been a surprise, as they were in the original.
- Poor analog implementation.
- Renaming characters with established identities.

Neutral
° Player can move noticeably faster.
° Difficulty is noticeably increased in farming activities, requirements to retain energy, etc.
° Bulletin Board from The Tale of Two Towns returns.
° Load Screen should have an option to be a black circle transition to make it easier on the eyes.

Now, to summarize a couple. The analog is because I wish the game tried to have a speed between "walk normally" and "ludicrous speed" and it does not feel like there is. So if your joystick is sensitive, you will shoot to the moon right away. It is not like you ever need to walk, and when you are in Strafe Mode, it is very controllable. It just feels a bit imbalanced to me, but honestly does not take away too much from the game.

Other thing is, I do not like character names being changed at all. For instance, Maria from Harvest Moon 64 will always be how I refer to her (changed to "Mary" from BtN onward) to list a different game for example. I will always call Muffy by her first name too (changed to "Molly" from AWL remake onward). Stuff like this bothers me since they already have names tied to them and in a remake of a game that is most focused on its characters than anything else (well, up to the first Story of Seasons, anyway), it means a lot to me. You get attached to how a certain character is and this practice they sometimes do of modifying a character's title, origins, etc. This stuff bothers me a lot. I don't mind them adding more to the character, but taking away is where I put my foot down. Unlike the case with SoS: FoMT that was more grounded in names, art design and writing, once a text editor comes out for SoS: AWL, I can just change the names back. I am still waiting for a mod of BtN and FoMT that does that someday, but I digress.

That is the end of my criticisms. I actually enjoy playing this a lot, these aside. I know it is an old game in spirit, but it is the first time I have wanted to put time into a new release in forever. It is still very fun to play, same as the original. But it adds so much more. Lumina has a drastically better redesign than the effort of AWL Special Edition. Besides her, the male characters they redesigned to be better suited as bachelors are good too. Gustafa feels like a younger version of himself and has a very good voice for his appearance. Gordy feels exactly the same, on top of the emotional distress of his art being more in line with his age. Kind of wish Matthew took a bit more inspiration from his original design when he was Marlin, but it is not a bad take on him. I do wish Yasuhiro Wada was consulted to at least help with art design in both the FoMT and AWL remakes since that would have helped a lot, but I can at least say the AWL remake did a much better job at being faithful to the original than the FoMT remake.

I was also surprised the remade music was not unpleasant on the ears this time. SoS: FoMT has a very unbalanced soundtrack with a few good ideas, though I definitely just wanted to replace all of it with the BtN originals. Overall, a lot of the music in SoS: AWL is much better by comparison. I do miss the original intro portion of Breeze, but I can live with it. The new one sounds nice. I actually think some of the NPC House music sounds better in some cases. And I am very picky on music too, so the fact I can confidently say it catches the charm of the originals in a lot of them is very nice to admit.

I would like to go on even more, but sadly, I am only in the first week of Spring. Shocking to write this much in such an early part of the game, I know. But I will say I am here to stay for the journey with how optimistic I sound, beginning part aside. And if anybody does prefer things all the way in both remakes, godspeed. Nothing wrong with that. I just personally miss how some things used to be and I will always feel that way. But I have decided I want to invest a lot of time into this remake. It has been very fun to me. Also, this accidentally just turned into a double review since I brought up the FoMT remake a ton.

But, yeah. For those who enjoyed the original Harvest Moon: A Wonderful Life, please give Story of Seasons: A Wonderful Life a chance. It will be well worth your time.\t\t\t\t\t
