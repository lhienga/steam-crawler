\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs Someone who remembers playing this on console I had high hopes and expectations for the remake of such a classic and one of a kind game of it's time, even now the concept of ageing and watching your child come into adulthood ( and passing away) is still very rarely seen in these types of games.

I know a lot of people will dislike the fact that some of the things that made this game a challenge are gone like animals getting sick and die, which made sense with the entire tone of this game i.e the journey of life from the beginning to the very end and experiencing the highs and lows and the hard work paying off in the end . But honestly for me it was still a thoroughly enjoyable experience. a Harder difficulty option however would definitely be a welcomed addition for some :)

Despite the real challenge aspect of animal maintenance being absent you can still suffer from neglecting your animals by their products dropping to a grade that cannot be sold so ignoring them for too long will require you to spoon feed them Fodder+ and fuss them for over a month or two to fully recover from it so be sure to give them at least 1 interaction a day ( Especially your cattle animals)

I'm unsure if I'm the only person to notice this but fishing did feel like it got stunted compared to the original release, I used to be able to get my farm off the ground by catching Sharsharks so fishing really felt like unless you needed them for gifts, cooking recipes to do the 100 dishes or any other achievements it felt very lack lustre and wasn't great for making any money which i felt was a shame, the addition to multiple different fish and the option for Ocean fishing was nice (original had about 7 in total unique fish an 3 sizes)

All in all I enjoyed my return to Forgotten Valley and was sure to experience everything this time round to give it the fair justice it deserved, I also loved the option to pick Female in this version but would like some more girly outfits :3 ( Yes it's a farming game but we wanna look cute sometimes too!) As long as it doesn't look like 'I Love Bears' T_T

TLDR:
If you haven't played this before i recommend it as it is a unique farming experience and lovingly recreated closely to its original counterpart. If you have played before? Come back for nostalgia you won't be disappointed :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen this game was announced I was super excited. I played the original a lot on my GameCube and always had fun min maxing how much money I could make within the first year, be it legitimate or with the glitches that existed. That said, the localized translation of the original left... a lot to be desired... I don't think I ever uncovered every record or secret.

Now this one is in my hands and not only is there nice localization and some good QOL, I'm once again spending a lot of my time min maxing different files to see how much money I can make based on luck, choices and the like. I love the rest of the game, too, but the simplicity of that first year has always been the most enjoyable for me.

That's why despite my enjoyment of the game and raising my child with different spouses as I build up the farm, I was a bit sad and disappointed that the game puts you in a game over if you do not marry. It's the point of the game, yes, but I was hoping that would be changed with this new version, to like, some stasis where you can say 'not ready yet' and it just replays year 1 for you. That first year just goes so so fast and I'd really love more time to properly dedicate to not just befriending the potential spouses, but getting them genuine gifts they actually enjoy. Instead of, yanno, shoving flowers and herbal soup at them every day (what I keep doing every playthrough so far).

Or for an option to not marry anyone locally and wind up being the single parent of a kid anyway (a fling from someone in the city that went sour or... whatever), but that's an overly ambitious expectation to have for a remake game, so I'm just being wistful here.

The only other thing I think I wish was in-game was the ability to skip cutscenes. I replay year 1 over and over... so stumbling into all the events every time back to back sometimes makes me wheeze.

That said the rest of the game is about just as I remembered it and a lot more. I only had the original version, so just 3 spouses (Nami, Cecilia, Molly). Being able to see the male spouses from the other versions (Another Wonderful Life and Special Edition, iirc) was a treat and I do understand and appreciate the name changes to a number of the characters.

I think the only thing that throws me out of the loop in terms of localization in this game now is calling it "Forgotten" Valley. I think keeping it as "Forget Me Not" would have a lot more punch to Takakura's opening narration if he said "Forget Me Not Valley, a place that the world over time forgot about" or. Something. It just doesn't ring the same, but it's not a deal breaker. I also miss the alliterative/rhythmic appeal of Nic Nac and Flak.

Hybrids are just as overwhelming to me as ever and I'm glad that they kept cows looking the same as in the original! I always enjoyed a more realistic leaning kind of direction with the farm animals and it's why AWL was my favorite out of every game. I was a lil sad that the other animals are super stylized though (sheep are pink and have their afros, chickens and ducks are orbs, goats... well they are certainly goats, the horse is also certainly a horse). They're growing on me. I love my sheeps now. Wish there was a breed that was natural white colored though.

All that aside I do enjoy how this game is more or less a relationship with your kid as you raise them, and the farming has a deep system with a lot of stuff to explore in it but is not necessary to have a decent experience. Kids are cute and grow up so fast. :[ Again I'd love a perpetual mode to just 'stall' a year before progressing to experience some of it a little longer.

Anyone coming from a game like Stardew need to know that this game does have a specific goal and an end. It's a bit open ended in what you can specialize in to make a profit on your farm, but there are specific factors you have to take in when it comes to making money and how profits can be VERY fast if you're lucky (my file where I dug up 7 golden forks day 1 of spring...) or slow and steady if you're not. Some things can be very obscure and vague (like parts of raising your kid's interest and learning hybrid crops, but there are guides for those, for the most part).

It's all those little things that more or less show that this is a remake of a game from a previous console age. If you're OK with something a bit more linear goal wise and want something different I recommend this game. If you much prefer the absolute near timeless freedom Stardew provides, then perhaps not.

I'm generally mostly fixated on year 1 cause I know I am determined to have a specific profit before I get married and I've yet to reach that yet. I am stubborn, but I'm having fun while doing it. I'll eventually finish a full life on a file when I quit being stubborn about it but I'm in no rush for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight off the bat, the game integrating non-binary and queer marriage while being our nostalgic fun farm sim is enough to deserve all the support. Not enough games have those options, when they SHOULD be there, and no matter the reason, just the fact that they exist here should be the norm and I will happily support any dev teams supporting this.

For those who played the game in the past, yes it is def your oldie game with a fresh coat of paint! For those who never played it, I highly encourage to try it out.
I'll say it honestly I don't think I am even a fan of farm sim, but I'm a fan of being my self-assured queer self in games and making friends with fictional characters, and this is why I like this Story of Season in particular. As a kid, a lot of the Harvest Moon serie had characters I felt were pretty bland, and enjoyed the way Wonderful Life had unique type of characters.

That said, despite me being happy to find this game again, one of the biggest downside is how little was ADDED to it. I'm grateful for everything implemented. I'm grateful for the translation being redone to be more accurate. Grateful for the JP voices option too!
However, I always felt like Wonderful Life was somehow... lacking in arcs. You get introduced to a lot of characters and their story... and then that's it, just like the old one. It makes things feel very... static. Especially since it is one of the game where the bachelor(ette)s don't get with anybody else if you don't choose them. This is less about romance rivalry for me and more about no one getting any sort of growth outside your own character.
There's an argument to be made about the theme of the game being the cycle of life, it being a bit more gritty and that's how life is people don't go through story arcs, but the matter is friend let's face it that's copium and it's just the lack of content.
With this said, I'd 100% get my wallet out for any DLC if we could get more of this world.

Still, yes, it is worth it. The game is iconic for a reason, despite having had little additions outside quality of life adjustements!\t\t\t\t\t
