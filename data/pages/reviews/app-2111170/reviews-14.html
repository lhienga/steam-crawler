\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery slow-paced and probably more so enjoyed by those who played the original Harvest Moon: It's A Wonderful Life on the gamecube or playstation. I grew up playing the original on gamecube so being able to return to a newly polished remake was exciting. I kept getting hit with waves of nostalgia while playing, especially in the opening scene. I would say that if you like slow paced farming games set in small towns you'll likely enjoy the game, but because of the price, I would recommend possibly watching a play through of the game online before buying- or waiting for it to go on sale- just to make sure you really want the game.

Aside from my love for the original, which leads me to love this remake, I could understand why someone WOULD NOT like this game. The map is very small and days can seem repetitive if you have no requests or goals in mind. It sometimes just feels like an endless loop of: talk to your favorite villager, forage, water crops, tend to animals, end day, repeat. Which can cause some boring and stale gameplay. To solve this, I think it's best to have goals in mind and keep an online guide nearby as you play so that you can make goals and understand what you need to do to achieve those goals. The game doesn't really give you large objectives to work towards, like stardew valley and the community center, it just kinda tells you to start establishing a life in your new home. The only large goal that I have encountered so far is that the nature sprites briefly mention that they want to bring back the harvest goddess through the spring and wish to do that by making the area nice and caring for it, but after that cutscene, you aren't given any objectives or quests to work towards that goal which is a bit confusing.

Anyway, overall, I love the game even though I haven't finished it yet, but I can understand why someone would not like the game, or feel as if it's boring or aimless. Really, the point of the game is to live 'a wonderful life' by changing, growing, and making connections that will last you a lifetime. The messages within the game are very bittersweet, and for players of the original (from my experience), will feel very nostalgic not only for the original, but for the time in their life when they had first been playing the game. Really brought me back to sitting on the scratchy carpet floor of my shared bedroom with my two siblings and staring up at a TV as I played the game for hours on end until my neck started to ache. I remember the dig site by the waterfall was my absolute favorite (and still is!). I was constantly going up there just because I loved how secluded that area felt.

In summary, I would recommend this game to those who played the original and fans of other harvest moon games, stardew valley, and just anyone who enjoys small town farm games. The characters within the game are very likeable and have good dialogue that doesn't feel awkward or stale (sometimes can feel repetitive but what can you do? lol). They each have unique personalities and within your first season you'll feel yourself gravitating toward a specific "group" of villagers or a specific villager, which really helps with creating a sense of community in the game! The cutscenes you encounter are cute and enjoyable, with good back and forth dialogue that give really good insight into the characters and your role within the town. The farming system is easy to understand and doesn't feel overly complicated. The animals are easy enough to care for that they don't feel like an inconvenience, but they're important enough that you won't forget that they exist. Progress is VERY slow and takes some patience, so find something to occupy your time with. I recommend the dig site, talking to everyone in town, and fishing. Fishing can feel like a drag because it's slow and the fish don't get you a lot of money if you sell them, but if you keep most of what you catch they're great for substitutes in recipes that require a fish in them.

Anyway, I apologize if my review seemed very rant-like, haha. If you do buy the game, I hope you enjoy, and if you decide not to because of my review, but still are looking for a farm type game, I would suggest you check out stardew valley (an all time favorite of mine) or roots of pacha (love this game! got it last week, it's extremely fun and has an immersive plot-line).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShould you buy this game?
1. Did you enjoy the original?
"Yes!"---- go to #2.
"No"... ---- go to #3.
"I never played the original."---- go to #4.

2. BUY IT NOW!

3. Do NOT buy this game. You will not like it (most likely).

4. Are you looking for a slow-paced & relaxing game?
Yes. I am looking for a laid back gaming experience with more of a slow grind & I plan on putting many hours into a game over many hours and days irl. Games with a daily routine appeal to me and I don't get bored of that laid back experience easily.---- go to #2.
No. I'm looking for something more fast-paced or something that I can play all day for several days straight that oozes complexity and variety in gameplay. I'm not interested in a slow grind experience or a gaming experience with a chill & laid back daily routine. I'm prone to burnout in games quickly and easily, and need something more complex to hold my attention. Otherwise, to me it's not worth spending this much money on a game. ---- Go to #3.

Why I love this game etc... :
I'm 31, love farming sims, and Harvest Moon: Animal Parade is my favorite farming sim. The original of this game was my very 1st Harvest Moon game though, and has always held a special place in my heart. It's the 1st farming sim game to make me fall in love with farming sims. However, after picking up a new copy of the original game a few years ago, I felt that the replay-ability did not hold up for me because it felt so dated- mostly due to it feeling so tedious with the farming, picking up items etc, and slow movement compared to time & pacing of the game.
I can gladly say that the quirky charm of the original definitely holds up in this remake though, and has several quality of life improvements that have made the game feel much more modern! It's literally everything I could ask for in a "remake" of an old nostalgic fav of mine. And for me personally, most (maybe all) of the changes they made to the remake actually made the game even better than the original! The only thing different that I could see as a reason for feeling mixed about this game would be that the vibes are a bit different than the original. It feels more upbeat and colorful now than compared to the original. So it feels less raw and realistic (because now it's more colorful and also now animals can't... if you know, you know). For me, I enjoy this update to the overall vibes and color scheme. I can also see why others might prefer the original overall vibe, realism, and muted colors to the current vibes though.

This remake has the perfect amount of nostalgia mixed with quality of life improvements.
Imo, because of this, it is the best remake of any game that I have ever played.
I give it a 10/10!
I did not enjoy the recent Mineral Town remake or the PoOT game.
If you also did not enjoy those two recent games, I ask you to still consider giving this a try like I did.
I think you'll be pleasantly surprised with it.
Now, we can only hope that they do a remake of Harvest Moon: Animal Parade sometime soon like this, and stay as close to the original as this game did while just making some slight graphic and quality of life improvements. That would be AMAZING! *fingers crossed*\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Harvest Moon AWL Special Edition on my PS2 until it finally kicked the bucket. Finding out that this game was being remastered was a literal dream come true!
Bear in mind this is a remaster of a 20 year old game. It is not a remake. The full integrity of the game was maintained with only minor changes, such as character names and the ability to load a cheese maker more than one milk jug at a time.
This is mostly a nostalgia goldmine. While the game itself is still perfection in my opinion it may not be for everyone. It differs from similar games in the genre, there is no advanced crafting, there are very few town events, and there is very little "freedom" in how you may want your life to look (ie. you have to get married and have a kid or the game will end).
The game focuses primarily on relationship, story, the beauty of ageing, and living a full "wonderful life"; which I will be doing for many more hours to come.

PS: If you are on PC play this with a console controller. It was NOT designed for keyboard controls at all which is really its only true flaw.\t\t\t\t\t
