\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I get to the review, I think it’s important to mention a few things.
1. For those who don’t know, Story of Season: A Wonderful Life, is a remake of the 2003 Gamecube game Harvest Moon: A Wonderful life. From 1996-2013 Marvelous Inc had their games translated to English and localised by the company Natsume. In 2014 Marvelous Inc decided to have the series localised by their American Publishing brand Xseed Games. Natsume owned the rights to the name Harvest Moon so the English name of the series was changed to Story of Seasons.

2. Since this is a remake, I feel it's unfair to the game to compare to others in the farm sim genre. As many are aware, Harvest Moon (SOS) games paved the way to what we now know and love about the farm sim genre. They were a heavy inspiration for the many, many, many farm simulation games we have access to now, with many of its unique features being outright copied and further expanded on in the newer generation of games. In this review, I will only compare SoS:AWL to the original, HM:AWL, and speak of improvements or negatives.

3. At the time of posting this review, I have nearly 100 hours in the game, and completed most achievements, wonders and the encyclopedia. I have a 6/7 chapter farm, so some of my information may be from outside sources. I mention this only for the sake of showing my experience in the game. For context sake, I married Gordy and had an Academic daughter. I use an Xbox 360 controller and have no issues with game play. (I have heard negative things about mouse & keyboard but can’t personally speak to anything).

---

Pros:
- Huge bag space given from the start of the game (No need to spend $$ to upgrade, rarely will you ever find your bags completely full). There’s also a shelf & outdoor fridge to place extra items. In Year 2, you’ll gain access to an indoor fridge which has even more storage.
- Romance & Marry men or women, gender neutral name options.
- You can give more than 1 gift a day to build up friendship, sometimes up to 4-5 gifts depending on the person, and friendship only degrades with one specific person. This makes building friendships & relationships incredibly fast. If you’ve played as many farm sim games as I have, learning everyones likes, schedules etc gets old fast. I appreciate this very much.
- You can see everyone on the map from the start of the game.
- Profit re-balancing compared to the original. It’s much simpler to make money, a good early tip is sheep are a good profit.
- Movement speed and animations are faster compared to HM:AWL, you can also pick up multiples of the same item easily.
- Fishing has vastly improved! In the original it took up a LOT of time, you hardly got any fish for the time spent and it was VERY difficult. There’s an actual shadow now.
- The game is LONG! There are 10 days in a season, 4 seasons, and 7 chapters of story. Lots of replay value! Try different marriage candidates, have a different gendered child and try raising different skills for different story outcomes on 2nd, 3rd, play-throughs. There’s also 8 save file slots to accommodate this.

Cons:
- The game starts off on a poor note; launching the game will pre-load in Japanese, on PC. There are workarounds, as others have noted in guides/other reviews, but obviously, the game not being double-checked to be ready for launch day has to lose points in my book.
- The hybrid & rare crops system is poorly explained, poorly executed/improved on (can’t bulk make seeds), and in general is very confusing for beginners. If you want to do this part of the game you basically HAVE to either refer to a guide/spreadsheet or write stuff down, thankfully there are really wonderful guides in the community tabs for this game you can check out.
- Having chickens sucks. To get your eggs daily you must pick up the chicken, set it down somewhere else and pick up the egg from under its butt. Sometimes other chickens will race to warm up the exposed egg, making you have to pick up multiple chickens. It’s irritating. If not for achievements sake, I would not have upgraded the coop.
- In a couple of in-game years, once you have the bigger barn & coop you’ll spend most of your time on the farm. It can take a really long time to tend to your animals, they constantly fall asleep so you have to wait for them to be “available” in order to snuggle up to them or milk. When refilling fodder, if your animals are in the barn (ex. while it’s raining outside), it’s really annoying to refill fodder since they’re constantly in the way. Just minor irritants that add extra time to animal tending.
- Mechanics for influencing your child’s job/personality are not introduced to you in the game, to find all the information about this you’d have to look at a guide. This is good and bad, it allows you to explore your son/daughter more organically, but if you don’t get many cut-scenes it can feel like you’re missing out.
- As a result of the above, because I didn’t know what to do by Chapter 4 my kid felt unloved because she had no friends. Apparently, I was supposed to run around with her as a baby and talk to people in the town to help her build relationships. She would say horribly depressing things like “I’m too miserable of a human being to hang out with”, and “Nobody would ever love a girl like me”, which could have been avoided if the mechanics were introduced in-game.
- Carter & Flora standing in the dig site can prevent you from digging certain tiles, there’s no way to push them or move through them, sometimes you can lose out on items because of this.
- Not being able to pick specific body types. If you enjoy being femme, the clothing all comes on one basic body type which resembles a masculine frame.


-

I personally recommend this game to players looking for a farm-based game that has life sim elements, players who not only want to plant crops, trees and care for animals, but also to marry, have a child and grow old watching their child grow up in a small quaint town, with characters to meet, grow close to and fall in love with. I would acquaint this game to a more ‘traditional’ Animal Crossing vibe, in the sense that if you take it at a leisurely pace you’ll enjoy it. I think if you are looking for a fast-paced farming game, with an expansive map, abundance of characters and features you’re looking at the wrong game. This game requires you to slow down and many things are on the simpler side. If that’s up your alley, you’ll enjoy the change of pace.

I’ll happily give the game 4.3/5. The issues I mentioned are fairly minor grievances, and I overall had a very positive experience with the game. I mention them so that prospective purchasers can be aware if it’s something that bothers them.\t\t\t\t\t

There is slight changes between the original and this version - however I very much have enjoyed my time in Story of Seasons: A Wonderful Life.

This game feels like a fan service for long-time lovers of AWL. Anyone who knew and loved the original will have an easy time but new players might not understand right away. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been starving for a remake of the original from the Gamecube. This is an almost exact remake from the map, buildings, characters, gameplay, and soundtrack.

I literally almost cried when the main farm song started playing because it brought back so many good memories playing this with my sister and dad growing up.

If you played the original this is a mandatory buy, but keep in mind that since it is a faithful remake that the scale of the game and map are that of the original, which does feel small in comparison to what we are spoiled with in modern games. So, maybe wait for a sale if you can manage the patience, if not full price is still worth it for this game to the right person.

Nostalgia aside, if this will be your first time playing probably pick it up on sale, but overall compared to other farming games this one is incredibly relaxing, has great gameplay loops for progressing your farm, and has my personal favorite art style for farm games. It's nice to be properly 3D and be able to move your camera around compared to other games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrue to the original but with lots of small quality of life improvements. Better writing (or maybe just better localization). As others have said, this is not a modern farming sim but for me that's one of my favourite things about it. I've got about 200 hours in Stardew Valley, it's a game I like a lot. But I am also in a dopamine loop when I'm playing Stardew, there's never enough hours in the day, I'm min maxing my crops, etc.
AWL is not about any of that. It's about the town you're in and the people in it. The pace is slower and more relaxed. But the gameplay is also deep. Cooking is available from day 1, cows need to be bred to produce milk, crops can be made into hybrids.

For me, this game is hands down a 10/10 and I hope they remake it in VR one day so I can move to Forgotten Valley when I'm old.\t\t\t\t\t
