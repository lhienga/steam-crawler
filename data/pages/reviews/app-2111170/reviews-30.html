\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far looks like a good remaster of a much-beloved classic. I've just about finished Spring Year 1 and everything has been very smooth - no stuttering, no long loads, and everything looks very polished (albeit very pastel and bright. I remember the original being very realistic and earthy in color scheme).

As others have mentioned, keyboard play is terribly laid out and frustrating to use. Thankfully, you can go into options and rebind the most egregiously offensive default keys to something you like better. Ideal? No, but that's the solution we've got right now. Can't comment on controller play because I've yet to try it.

Additionally, on first launch, the game will opt to run in low resolution windowed mode, so that'll be your first stop in options to fix.

As for the actual game, a bunch of welcome QOL/modernizations:
+Tool use has been significantly sped up and also somewhat automated (ie you can repeatedly press the tool use button and you will auto-select the next tillable/waterable square in range and automatically move to/perform action on that square).
+Bells on the Barn/Coop to call all animals inside/outside.
+Character customization. Pretty basic set of faces, hairstyles, skin colors. You can change your hairstyle and color in-game any time (certain bachelor/ettes like frequent look changes..).
+The cooking interface has been overhauled and is much better.
+You can marry any bachelor/ette regardless of your gender selection. Except Rock, he's awful, no one should marry him.
+The addition of an achievements system for completionist types.
+More cutscenes
+Probably more but I'm not that far into the game yet.

Definitely an enjoyable return for those that enjoyed it back in the day. It's all here. A decent entry into a Story of Seasons/Harvest Moon type for a newcomer as well - though maybe not as full on features as a truly modern game might be expected to be. It is simple and small in scope, the story about a farmer growing their farm and family over the years in a tiny country village. A very relaxed experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was the first Harvest Moon game I played when I was younger and I remember being OBSESSED!! They kept pretty much everything the same, the ambience, the music and village all feel so nostalgic to me. It works great with the PS4 controller, so I can't comment on the keyboard controls. No bugs and good optimisation so far, stable fps and everything :D

There are still a few things that disappoint me though:

-Because there isn't all that much additional content or change, I don't think it's worth the current price, I'd suggest buying it during a price off so it's as if you're buying the original game.

-I don't like the fact that the seasons are extremely short just 10 days compared to the 30 days from the OG. I wish there was at least an option to make it longer or shorter (like when in the Sims when you can adjust the lifespan length).

-Not sure if it's because I havent played a Story of Season game in a while+the short seasons, but the days feels very quick compared to what I remember.

-I don't like the new cooking level system, I'd rather if we were able to just get all recipes but each time we make them we get a certain grade depending on the difficulty of the dish+our level

But basically, I'd only suggest you buy it if you were a big fan of the original game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory of Seasons: A Wonderful Life is essentially a remaster of Harvest Moon: A Wonderful Life from the PlayStation 2. If you played the old one the game is pretty true to the original, all the same characters are present with the addition of a couple more. The farm layout is almost the same and the town is not only the same layout, it's the same size as the old game's world, so don't be expecting a whole lot more from the original. There is some new things added into the game. I don't believe goats were in the old one, and there's a few more interactions with townsfolk. And Muffy's name was changed to Molly, which I'm actually kinda glad. The graphics are hard to rate, they are pretty bad that it looks like the PS2 version, but they are also crisper and clearer details on the crops, items and other things; it's better, but still looks old and cheesy.

All in all it's basically the same game as the PS2 version, with the notable exception that you can actually make a profit selling crops wholesale. People who owned the original will know what I'm talking about. When the game came over from Japan to NA and Europe it shipped with an error in its coding that inverted the seed/fruit price, so the fruit grown sold at half the price of buying the seed. The only way to make a profit was by either raising animals, using fruit trees that produced fruit yearly, or fishing with a fishing pole. Thankfully they fixed that lol.

It's a decent game if you want to relive your childhood again, but honestly I was a little disappointed. I guess I was expecting more added to expand the game to make it new and fresh. I'm going to give the game a positive recommended rating because it's cute and I used to love this game, but $50 is VERY STEEP for a game that is a 2000s era repaint.

Yes, I said repaint, not remake.

P.S. - The option to play as a male/female character is misleading. You don't really pick a male or female avatar. They give you face/hair/clothing options that are either masculine or feminine and then have you chose your pronouns - he/him she/her they/them - so yeah. That was almost grounds enough for a negative review.\t\t\t\t\t
