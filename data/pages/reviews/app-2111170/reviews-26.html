\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a Harvest Moon girly since I could pick up a controller, and HM AWL was my very first Harvest Moon game, so to say I was excited to play & had high hopes for this remaster would be an understatement! However, upon loading in the game, I was really surprised by how unpolished the game felt and ultimately, sadly, decided to request a refund for this game.

To start, my game booted in another language, and I had to Google how to change my language back to English. While that isn't a huge deal, it certainly sets the tone for a first impression. I also had a difficult time figuring out the controls right off the bat -- keyboard and mouse are an option, but I found that sometimes, clicking my mouse would change an option, and other times, it would serve to confirm an option. Character animations were very choppy and the movements seemed sharp and unnatural, rather than smooth and fluid. It felt like I was playing a HD hack of AWL, not a full remake. I was ready to put all of that aside for nostalgia's sake, however, as soon as I gained control of my character, I found that the quick, jolt-y movements of my character combined with the camera speed triggered my motion sickness and I was unable to continue playing, even after turning down the camera speed (which was really difficult to find -- why aren't settings mapped to ESC?) This was interesting, as I've only experience gaming-related motion sickness when trying to play VR, never from a non-VR game.

Being someone who loves farming simulation games, I've played a LOT of really wonderful games over the years. Many of them being worked on by developers from this studio! Having so many incredible games at my fingertips has really raised my expectations when it comes to farming simulators. With all of that experience in mind, it is really difficult to justify paying $50 for a remake that looks and plays like a game from ~2013, not 2023. If you really loved HM:AWL, you may appreciate this game at a lower price-point, like $20-$25, but I would encourage you to 1) take some Dramamine before you start up the game, if you suffer from motion sickness, and 2) lower your expectations.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a pure rush of nostalgia. I can't believe it's been 20 years since the original. How does that make you feel? Because it makes me feel pretty old.

Now I'm gonna start by saying this. If you're thinking of buying this game because you love Stardew Valley or Sun Haven and those were your introductions to farming Sims, I do not recommend this game for you. If you never played the original as a child, I do not think you will enjoy it. I wouldn't play this game if it wasn't for the fact that I LOVED this game when I was 5. It's a very simple game and is nothing like Stardew valley. I'm already seeing Stardew players flock to this game and saying it sucks. They just don't understand. They weren't there in 2003 playing this on GameCube. Now that I got that out of the way....

It's pretty much the exact same game but they made a few changes. Some changes I like and others I don't.

PROS:
The farming mechanics feel way smoother. Remember in the original how much of a nightmare trying to plant, water, etc was? Well they fixed that in this remake. It's so much faster and fluid, and you can change camera angles while in your fields which is really nice.

It runs really smooth. I have an i7 6700, 16GB of RAM, and a GTX 1070. i play the game at 1440p Ultra settings and I get a steady 60 FPS. I can even play it at 4k around 40 fps but I like the smooth 60 so I stick to 1440p Remember in the GameCube version it would stutter from time to time? And it was even worse on PS2. Yeah that's not an issue at all here. Which I would hope so, for a brand new game in 2023.

You can now fish at the beach. Which I know probably isn't a huge detail but it's just nice that you can do it now.

You can go to the city. Now what I mean by that is, you can go to the city and it skips time by 6 hours. so you can't actually go to the city and wonder around but it's a nice little feature for if you need the time to pass. (I just prefer to fish to pass the time to make that bread ;) )

They added a bridge over the river so you can get to Carter's dig site straight from your farm instead of having to go all the way around. I really like that.

You can customize your character how you want. Though I just made my character look the the OG because I just felt it wouldn't be the same.

You can actually sell your produce in the shipping bin instead of having to take them to van. I know you could do that in HM AWL SE but I never liked that version much.

You can save the game at any time by pausing it and "writing in your journal" which I really like. Now you don't have to wait to go to bed to save your progress.

You can see how much energy and stamina you have. In the original you were always left guessing how much energy you had left. You could open your pack and see your characters stance to see if they were tired or not, but with the stamina bar it's just much easier to keep up with my energy levels. Though if you do run out of stamina, the game will skip ahead by a few hours and you will regain some energy.

Van Still comes on the 3rd and 8th of each season and from what I can tell, he sells all the same items.

They added an automatic fertilizer machine you can put in your fields. They're kind of expensive but I'm definitely looking forward to using those. I think they added a few other new things but I'm not too sure.

There are four Bachelorettes and 4 Bachelors you can choose from. And it doesn't matter if you play as a guy or a girl. You can Mary any one of the 8. It's nice to see the developers being inclusive like that.

I think that's about all I can think of for the pros. Now here's the cons

CONS:
THEY CHANGED THE NAME OF THE VALLEY FROM FORGET ME NOT VALLEY TO FORGOTTON VALLEY! Forgotten is the complete opposite of Forget Me Not. Now they're just saying the valley is forgotten instead of not forgotten. This isn't a deal breaker for me, I'm just a little disappointed with this decision. But I'll live.

THEY CHANGED THE NAMES OF SOME OF THE CHARACTERS
Muffy is now Molly. Celia is now Cecilia. Marlin is now Matthew. And they changed a few other names. Nami is still Nami at least. And most of the other characters names were unchanged. But I still call them all by their OG names anyways. Ain't no way I'm calling Muffy "Molly" ick.

The controls could use some polishing up on keyboard but I'm using an Xbox controller. It took me a little bit to get used to the controls but once you get used to them they're pretty easy. If you can't use a controller and you don't like the keyboard controls, you can always change your key bindings. Which is a headache, I know, but it is what it is.

The backpack menu is different, like way different. I'm not really sure I'd call it a con BUT you have to get used to it. I hated it at first but now it's fine. It's just that initial shock of "okay, I wasn't expecting it to be like that." I never played animal crossing, but my friend said the menu reminds her more of animal crossing. So I guess if you play animal crossing, the menu will look fine to you

The graphics aren't really the best. I actually think the GameCube version had better lighting and shadows and stuff. This looks a little more flat and cartoony. But that's probably what they were going for and I do somewhat like the art style of the game. Not a deal breaker for me though. I think the game still looks nice in its own way.

It's $50. I think that's a bit steep for a game that's so simple. But nostalgia calls. So I had to buy it. That's probably why they made it so expensive bc they knew us harvest moon lovers would pay the price anyways. Oh well, it is what it is.

I think that's really the only cons I can think of right now.

IN CONCLUSION:

If you played the original when you were a kid, you will most likely love this game. It's just a huge hit of nostalgia and it feels really good.

If you are coming from Stardew valley, you probably will hate this game. It's nothing like Stardew and you will probably get really bored really fast. So keep that in mind. We don't need anymore Stardew players causing this game to have mixed reviews. Don't get me wrong, I love Stardew too, but it's a way different game.

So all in all, have fun farmers! And build a yourself a wonderful life!
