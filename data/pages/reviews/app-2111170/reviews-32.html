\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a first introduction to the game review after a few hours of game play.

I have played the original years ago back when Adam Sessler was yelling at it on X-Play that it "NEEDS MORE SEEDS!". Okay, let's begin...

## Core Game Mechanics ##
Good change/update. The game pacing is faster. You no longer run like a waddling penguin that takes six hours to cross the map. I appreciate this very much - you can actually get a lot of errands done in a single given day, unlike previously.

Controls are... Different with a mouse and keyboard but given enough time you learn them like any other game.

However, the Stamina mechanic I feel is too generous if it is being true to the origin of the game. At the beginning, per the old game, you had very little Stamina. By "working" the farm, as years go on, your Stamina increases significantly, allowing you to do more - it literally paced the game for you outside of pure monetary means in-game. This mechanic seems gone now. You're really only burdened by the clock of the day, and your wallet. I can complete the dig site, farm, and run around and barely hit half of my Stamina.

Graphics look good! Big improvement, if not at times stuttering. Clearly could use some performance enhancements to make the frames smoother in some locations, but overall a non-issue. No crashing so far!

Targeting your "boxes" for watering, etc., can (and still is, like the original game) a little jank. To overcome this, however, I realized by aiming your camera straight down your targeting is far more forgiving. Thankfully, the camera completely rotating when you walk into one of your fields is gone from the original game.

You can be rewarded by finding random recipies scattered throughout the place at various locations. Some exploration is rewarded, thankfully.

## The Dig Site ##
Dig site is the same, mostly. Only issue here is the character hit-boxes block your character. A result of this is you can easily miss-click your action in the dig site, and some squares can be a PIA to target, making this issue a rather consistent annoyance.

## Farming ##
Works the same as the last game, really. I do still agree with the past criticism of the game "NEEDS MORE SEEDS!" but, honestly, it is fine in the end.

Animals are the same, mainly.
However, they removed the Sheep and we got Goats. Sure? Take that for what you will.

You can pick-up a multi-stack of hay from the silo to fill your feed troughs in the barn. Not sure how I feel about this - QoL is better if you have a lot of animals but it is weird thinking you can grab a stack of 30x hay bales and just do everything in bulk. In a weird way, the manual labor is what appeals to some of us.

I wish there were more building upgrades available. (There may be more later on, but I doubt it. Felt like it was a missed opportunity).

Start of the game is FAR more forgiving - they even give you an apple tree seed to start! (Whaa?) EZ-Mode.

## NPCs ##
Interactions were improved a lot from what I remember. Cut-scenes and stories are more fleshed out and a LOT more common. Characters in the past that were hard to "find" consistently seem to be far more available.

You now have a set of "hearts" you can track for relationships as well as a sliding bar to see how much people like you. I wish there was a way for you to track what "people liked" for gifts through trial and error, seeing as everything has a journal entry, but now I'm nit-picking.

You can be a girl OR a boy!

I wish you could sell more via the bin in your farm. I'm STACKING collectibles from the fields, flowers, etc., and can't sell them until I see the traveling merchant which can be a bit of a drag.

## Conclusion ##

Overall, good game. I am still just starting so some of what I say may not be entirely true late-game. This is also a day-one release review so remarks about performance, etc., may not be valid even by EOD tomorrow - who knows. Keep an eye on patches.

The game in the end is true to the roots of the old game, with some added in extra material. However, I feel the extra material is still conservative to what they could do. They could truly expand a lot on this game and still not ruin the formula of the old one and I hope they do.

My only main gripe is the clear failure of the Stamina bar. It, weirdly enough, is a core game mechanic that truly helps dictate game pacing. It makes rainy days feel liberating, it makes the dig-site an all-day consuming activity, and makes you prioritize just HOW much you are willing to farm without being fully burdened by your own self-inflicted workload. It also means meals, and Stamina rejuvenating items lose all meaning to the game. In the original you wouldn't catch me without stuffing my face full of Mugwort (Now an Herb) every day for that little extra hit of Stamina. Now, it is a stack of 20x+ sitting in a cabinet waiting to be sold.

Oh, and the fishing -- Just get rid of the literal fish in my opinion and make it a shadow or something. It just looks... Weird. If you get the game, you'll see what I mean... Has no implications on the game... It is just... Yeah...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey understood the assignment and once again delivered on a remake of one of the classics. Like stepping back in time 20 years ago, but with a fresh look and feel. The sounds are nostalgic.. with some of the sound effects being identical to the ones used in the OG. The music is slightly different, but oooooh, it just gives my brain a huge dose of nostalgic serotonin.

Pretty much every mechanic that existed in the original is duplicated here almost exactly. If you've played this before, your brain might just take over and go into auto-pilot as this will feel in a lot of ways how it was to play the first time. This can be a double edged sword, however, as they haven't really added any content here to have more activities to use up your time. I had always hoped they might add more, as there is still a lack of things to do as the game progresses to later years in comparison to other SoS and Harvest Moon titles.

The game handles smoothly (playing with a controller) and I appreciate more control over the camera. The run speed is MUCH faster which is a huge improvement, and the hunger mechanic doesn't seem to be as aggressive here. You can sell items to Van in a stack rather than one at a time (HALLELUJAH)... and you can also save anywhere at any time. They've added a way to check your affection and friendship with people (rather than kind of guess, because it used to be obscure) and you can of course marry every person who was available previously without needing to switch between games to do so. In fact, gender has kind of been eliminated altogether - allowing you to customise your character however you see fit and then choosing pronouns. There's lots of customisation here, and you're even allowed to change your hair color and style on the fly.

Murrey's redesign is kind of atrocious, which is so far my biggest complaint. I can live with this, however, as I'm really enjoying the rest of the design work that was done here. I also feel it's pointless to not let people change eye color if they want, if customising the rest of your look is available at any time.

If you loved the original, I'd definitely recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn it's current state, the port needs work. The camera controls in particular need revamping. But the gameplay is smooth. It really feels like I've travelled back in time to experience one of the best games I ever played on my Gamecube. The characters and animals are as lively and interesting as ever, and the new love interests seamlessly integrate into the cast, so much so that I almost forgot that they were not in the original game at all!

HOWEVER!!!

In my humble opinion, Three things need to happen before I can 100% endorse this.

1. Work on keyboard controls. They just aren't very good right now. They lack polish and it's very awkward, plus I found no way of pausing the game to go to options while IN game to change the controls.

2. The camera needs adjusting. Make it move with your mouse naturally, not by holding down a button. This might be rebind-able, but as a default, it's clumsy.

3. Allow people to change their name and pronouns mid game if they wish. People's lives change, just as your life changes in this game. It would be simple enough to allow people to FULLY customise everything on a whim. Restricting that seems pointless.

This game is FANTASTIC, but it's not QUITE ready for the PC market yet. I would say if you love Wonderful Life, get it on a console, or wait for patches to the PC port.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA port that delivers on all counts - thank you so much!

chapter system covering around 30 years, well-paced story about raising a child
fun hybrid crops to make
lovely, warm, quirky characters
changes to characters over the years
changing seasons, nice romantic festivals
nice special wedding scene
fishing, dig site, exploration, collecting wonderfuls
it’s just enough to do to make things interesting, the type of game you enjoy spending time in and want to create a new character immediately after you finish


I missed Forgotten Valley. Glad to have it on PC and it has all the charm, with a few good changes from the original versions. Another Wonderful Life has always been one of my most favourite HM games. This release preserves that.

The game pulls together every feature from AWL, ANWL and the Special and makes a lovely work of it.

It’s one of the better paced HM games: has a plot spanning 30 years, divided into chapters, seasons are 10 days long but there is plenty to do to keep you going, like making fun hybrid plants and crop mutations – including blue varieties as well as 2nd and 3rd generation cross-breeds. Get sheep for good gold. The cast of colourful and heart-warming characters makes this one of my go-to HM games and am really glad it got ported and remade. It keeps the feeling I always loved about the original games. One of the standout features of this game is that as years go by – the characters change, age. It keeps the feeling that time flows, unlike in many similar titles.

The core thing here is to raise a child and go through the hoops of them growing up – so if you don’t marry by the end of chapter 1, it’s game over. You can propose earlier but I like waiting until the guy comes over and asks – it’s a form of reverse proposal I always liked in HM games – so yes, there is one and it’s worth waiting for it on 10 Winter, but make sure to raise those hearts. On the other hand, if you do wait for the reverse proposal, you will miss out on an extra (and lovely) engagement event that happens after you propose, so choose. You can marry any gender, of course. They also kept rival events from original games. That was always fun.

This release has been adapted and uses three genders properly – they aren’t a gimmick. They work, so I am consistently referred to as a she. You can be a they, if you want. It also makes slight but welcome changes to the original games, including graphical upgrades, and some changes to marriage candidates. It adds the artist Gordy as a bachelor (he was previously known as Cody). Gordy’s heart events are just as fun as those of other characters, and introduce his artistic process. Exploration, working at the archeological dig, fishing – everything is fun and very relaxed and charming in Forgotten Valley. In essence this is the Harvest Moon I love and remember and am enjoying it in the ported version very much so.

This game is nearly everything I loved avbout HM games – everything Olive Town nearly ruined for me. I’m glad to be back in Forgotten Valley. This game is definitely the kind of Story of Seasons I want to have and a meeting with an old friend. Comforting and nice to play, heart-warming. A fine addition to my favourites. Puts a smile on my face. I’m really grateful for this release. And hope to see Animal Parade one day too – the second of my most favourite HM games. The third and last were SOS and Trio of Towns. But A Wonderful life was always a stand-out HM game because it has this wholesome, warm feeling about it, a different storyline that is well-paced and fun to play. Has a heart.

Precisely my kind of game, because I seek games with a heart. This is one of them\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory of Seasons: A Wonderful Life is a farming simulation game that was first released in 2003 for the Nintendo. so literary when I was kid I played a lot on PS2. The game was developed by Marvelous Interactive and is the fifth game in the Harvest Moon series. Here is my review of the game:

Pros:
- The game has a charming and immersive storyline that will keep players engaged for hours.
- The farming mechanics are easy to understand and implement, making it accessible to players of all ages.
- The game allows players to interact with their surroundings and the people in their village, adding a sense of community to the game.
- The graphics of the game are bright and colorful, adding to the overall charm of the game.
- The game features a day-night cycle and weather system, making the game feel more realistic and dynamic.

Cons:
- The game can feel slow-paced at times, with some tasks taking longer than necessary to complete.
- The game can become repetitive after a while, with players having to perform the same tasks day after day.
- The game can feel limited in terms of customization options, with players having little control over the layout of their farm.

Overall, Story of Seasons: A Wonderful Life is a charming and immersive farming simulation game that is perfect for players who enjoy a slower-paced, more relaxed gaming experience. While the game can become repetitive at times and lacks some customization options, the overall experience is enjoyable and highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
