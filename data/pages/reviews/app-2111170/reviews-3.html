\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompared to other SoS games I'm honestly disappointed. I never played the original AWL, but the focus of this game is animals, not crops. That wouldn't be a problem, though I prefer focusing on crops, but animal husbandry in this game is an absolute logistical nightmare. Chickens can end up anywhere, including lost in fodder grass in the field, and lay eggs at seemingly random intervals. Inside the barn, meanwhile, your horse will eat any other animal's fodder, sheep are constantly falling asleep on top of fodder bails, and you can't do anything with a sleeping animal. I'm struggling to get anything done, and my coop and barn aren't even maxed out at tier 1, forget about tier two. I know, I know, "skill issue," but ultimately I think this game just failed to find that line between realistic and fun. It just feels more like work than playing compared to other farming sims.

On top of that, the game feels way more story focused, forcing you to marry by the end of the first year. I actually like that there's not logging/mining in this game, but they didn't offer any suitable alternatives (other than fighting to get your animals to behave), so you're pretty much stuck spending most of your day fishing and digging for relics once you've fulfilled the marriage requirement. All this also means that everything you get you either just pay for, or comes for free, like your house upgrade.

Ultimately, I was really excited about the premise, getting to actually raise a family through a storyline, but the pacing is all off, way too much control is taken away from the player on how to fulfill conditions, and the entire farming aspect of the game was poorly executed. If you loved the original AWL then knock yourself out with the nostalgia, but otherwise, there are way better SoS games to choose from than this one.

P.S. While I applaud the gender neutrality of the player, excuse me, but why does my wife get boobs and not me? Patently unfair, tyvm\t\t\t\t\t
