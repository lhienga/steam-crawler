\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Buy it when its on sale if you love the game, wait for the more of the road map to be released if you're uncertain about buying it because you will be disappointed.

The Long Dark has been one of my favorite games for a long time, with the ability to continuously draw me back into it repeatedly. The game is unforgiving and can be punishing to those that are unprepared for it, and its one of the reasons that so many people (myself included) enjoy playing.
This DLC sadly gives me mixed feelings.
I found myself excited to explore the new regions an delve into the new open world.
The views and atmosphere of the areas gave me a sense of wonder and intrigue, but after wandering around for over an hour searching for new and interesting locations, I was left still hungry for more.
The spaces feel large, but more empty and unused than open and vast.
The few areas of interest were so distantly separated that exploring them felt like a chore that i was simply completing because I felt I should, not because I was genuinely excited to do so.
The long trek between points of interest felt tedious and dull, and lacked the occasional distracting object in the distance that other areas have.

The road map for TLD promises cougars, alternate arrow types, more clothing options, traders, and generally more in-world objects which will make the world feel more real, but as for now, this DLC feel like a large sum of money to unlock a large area with comparatively little in it, and so sadly I am unable to recommend it for the price it is at.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary:
Maybe hold off if you're not sure atm. Obviously buy now if you love this game and want more.

Waited a week before buying, still in rocky shape rn. The studio is selling the DLC now, but releasing the content for the DLC as they finish different areas in regular updates, so this is a very early review, with not a lot available. I trust the studio will fix these bugs and get things in order before releasing future updates. Do more beta testing before general release.

Big bugs:
Crashes when opening inventory/storage inventory UI.
My performance has taken a big hit, and on fullscreen 4k the very top of the screen is shifted offscreen.
Also hitboxes are sometimes extremely small and/or only reachable from very specific angles.
I have had a few crashes in my 8 or so hours of play since this launched.

Small bugs:
Lots of floating objects, etc. It seems there were a lot of small changes to the older maps and a lot of tables got moved leaving the boxes on the tables floating in midair, etc.

Grips:
While forsaken airfield is a huge map, most of it is completely empty snowfield with no life, plants, or even rocks. I appreciate that the area is supposed to feel expansive and empty, it feels far to much so atm, probably because the rest of the DLC hasn't been released yet.

Cool stuff:
NEW AREAS! WOW!!!
The trip there was enjoyable, not particularly challenging, but long enough to be an adventure.
Love the small trainstation area.

...waiting on the rest of it atm.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing the main TLD game for well over 600 hours, and it is a great survival game experience. I purchased this DLC on launch day, and since I didn't play it for several hours after launch, I had none of the major bugs people had who tried to play it immediately after it was released. Overall, I would say the bugginess level of the DLC at this early stage in the year long roadmap is completely tolerable. I've had a few weird items floating above the ground texture, a few items that have misplaced hit boxes etc, but nothing game breaking.

The new region is astoundingly vast, and definitely beautiful. The feeling of wonder and anxiety when forging into the unknown is absolutely precious, and well worth the price of admission for anyone who has played the base game as much as I have. The new content released at launch is certainly enough to keep me busy for a while, and definitely a good first step on the path of this DLCs development. But you can tell that its not a complete product yet, and many of the most exciting features Hinterland has promised in the buildup are not implemented yet. All of this was made clear by the studio: if you buy the DLC early, you are buying a work in progress. I see it as an investment in insuring that this game I love will keep growing and evolving into the future. So if you want to follow the development of the Far Territory step by step, bug by bug, you should buy it now (and save a few bucks while you are at it). If you want to play the full DLC in its entirety, you are best off waiting till its finished.\t\t\t\t\t
