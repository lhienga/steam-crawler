Review revised:

I am currently experiencing massive lags when I open the inventory, especially in the clothing menu. This is a problem I did not have before the release of "Tales from the Far Territory".

I find Forsaken Airfield to be quite a nice map overall, as its openness offers variety from the previous maps. However, the map certainly needs to be reworked in some places. Not only is it almost impossible to survive there (as a starting area) on Interloper. I also find it very poor that there is only one workbench on an airfield with helicopters, planes, workshops, storerooms and hangars. And of course it is in the basement, where there are no light sources... I also don't like the lack of cooking facilities/fire barrels, which has been mentioned several times.

What concerns me more, however, is the evolution of The Long Dark's survival mode over the last few years:
The spray paint that was introduced for example. It's nice at best, but nothing more.
The bulletproof vest, more off the mark.
And now the Glimmer Fog.
Fog belongs in the game. It's natural, it makes orientation difficult, it makes for interesting and exasperating moments, and it adds to the challenge. It adds depth. But a different kind of fog, just more fog, does not automatically make the game better. On the contrary. Coupled with the Insomnia, a completely uninteresting game mechanic is introduced that just annoys me personally. The item variations are nice. But they are very rare (which is okay) but they don't affect the game experience at all at the moment.

I wonder what goal is being pursued to add more facets to the core aspects of the game - namely the struggle for warmth, water, food and sleep. Namely clothing, fire making, shelter building, cooking, fishing, hunting, item building, maintenance and repair, plant and animal life, weather effects and so on. These are the aspects that make up the game and that should be expanded. And not as the very last thing.
Starting locations and Loot Tables have been reworked. That's good in principle. I also think it's great that there are now far fewer weapons to be found. But I still find 10 cans of spray paint in just one location on Stalker...
I have the feeling that the game is being developed more and more without its very soul in mind. I would enjoy it more without spray paint, without a rather pointless body armor and without a totally annoying Insomnia effect.

I'm just looking forward to ptarmigan, cougar, more clothes, acorns, soup and other food, reworked ice fishing, more cooking recipes, travois, but most of all: Finally such a central element as a reworked, (and hopefully) really good mechanic for fire starting.

I don't think the game is worth its price at the moment.
But I am (still) a fan of Hinterland and The Long Dark. And I just hope that Tales from the Far Territory will somehow get its act together.

Only for this reason I give it a thumbs up...\t\t\t\t\t

The bug has been fixed! Here's an partial review for the DLC, as it has not been fully released yet. Tales from the Far Territory is a Pass-like DLC bringing more content over the next 12 or so months. The first batch involves a new area called The Forsaken Airfield, a new affliction (insomnia), and hidden stashes that you can find clues to in vehicles.

If you're a fan of wandering, the journey to the airport will already be a treat (though where to start is a bit confusing, as it was not clearly mentioned). Having not played The Long Dark in a while, it was a blast trying to remember how to survive in the cold, cold winter. So far, I've made it to the airfield and started exploring the first buildings. It took me around 11 hours to prepare and reach the airfield, though more experienced players will definitely do it faster.

Right now, I would only recommend you to buy this if you are craving for a new area to explore. You will already have enough entertainment in the base game, and waiting for more batches might be a better choice before purchasing. You will however save a little bit of money if you buy it early, as the price will increase by 5 USD/EUR at the midway and the end point of the DLC batches.\t\t\t\t\t
