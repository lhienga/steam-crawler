\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGonna start off by saying, I understand some criticisms with negative reviews, and that some people are encountering bugs that ruin their experience, but... I'm not encountering any bugs that are game breaking or even remotely severe, and although progress on this DLC is slow, I'm not sure why anyone expected anything different based on the development of the base game. For that exact reason, I held out on purchasing until about a week ago.

That said, I've been enjoying the additions from the DLC. To be honest, I bought it for one reason and one reason alone, the new cooking. However, the new Airfield map turned out to be a location I actually like setting up base, the glimmer fog is an interesting storm mechanic that adds some variety to the game, and I hope they give us an option to turn it on world wide at some point. The cooking has been nice, it makes the mundane task a little less boring by adding variety, the fire hardened arrows are appreciated for hunting small game, and the new items are good additions.

At this point, I still haven't repaired any radio towers to take part in tales, not sure when I will, it's not a major selling point for me, but I could see it adding a bit of life to the game for some.

In the end I'm happy with the content I got from this, gives me some new experiences, and looking forward to upcoming maps and content. To anyone who's had the game for long like me, it's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: I bought on release and was conflicted initially, but after giving the new areas several hours of playtime, I'm sold. Hinterland still has it. I'm loving the new content and the new systems integrate really well into the base game. Hinterland has my trust in this game and its DLC's future development. The studio had a lot of rocky patches during the longstanding development cycle of "The Long Dark" but they never backed out of promises made, even if delivered late.

More content and bugfixes are on the horizon so I'll go over some common gripes I've heard in the community to hopefully inform the potential buyer. Keep in mind the base game is still being updated (for free!) along with this DLC.

1. Bugs and no content! What happened?
When you buy this DLC, you are buying a season pass. Keep that in mind. I would even venture to say this should be tagged as "Early Access" on Steam. The content is coming but in stages instead of all at once. As far as the bugs go, Hinterland always had buggy releases. Someone on the forums mentioned bringing back public test releases on Steam. Gee, that's a fantastic idea that used to produce really stable builds for everyone else. (especially the console crowd) The bugs do get fixed, eventually. These past two weeks, I've seen some really rapid development on bugs that came with the "Part 3" content drop. In an ideal world, the initial content release would be bug free, but Hinterland has never had a good track record of achieving this. Keep your expectations low and buy the Steam version if you want the "bleeding edge" release. Every other platform gets updated slower.

2. New zones are a walking simulator! Booooooring.
- This DLC is not for you if this is a concern. While it is true that accessing the new region requires traversing two intermediate zones, this would be the same as spawning in Desolation Point and traveling to Mountain Town (Milton) when that was new content. I do not see a deterioration in the quality of the new zones from a development perspective. If anything, they are challenging in ways we have not seen before. Distance and open spaces have been used in other zones to enhance difficulty, such as the Coastal Highway coastline and most of Forlorn Muskeg, but not to the sheer scale that Forsaken Airfield has used these traits. I welcome the challenge.

3. What is in this DLC? What is in the free updates? Where is the changelog?
- Hinterland did not make figuring this out easy, especially on release. They have improved their communication since then, but sadly, this is yet another area where Hinterland has been weak historically. A good place to find out what is currently released, what is planned, and the differences between the DLC and base game updates is this website: TFTFT Expansion Website Otherwise, the Steam "news" updates now include a changelog, so you can look through those and piece it together yourself. Hinterland's official forums are also a good resource: Forums under "News and Updates"\t\t\t\t\t
