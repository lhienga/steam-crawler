A lot of other reviews have bashed the forsaken air field region and while I find myself agreeing with them, I have a feeling hinterland is going to push the envelope in some way. The first wave is admittedly bare in the star region, though they've added some elements to the game that are going to lead to bigger things; the gear variety, the memento caches, and the loot table refresh are those things. While not huge, their impact is felt across the game world making it at least fun to approach the game slightly differently. Variety is what the game needs, and hopefully we'll get more meta shifts and additions. Also, add an in-game update road map, so people can know what's in and isn't.

Buy it if you like the long dark, this dlc is certainly is palatable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Long Dark is a game of patience. I will not hide that as a long term player I have some sincere disappointment with the initial content release. However, I can see the effort. The new region is huge, and knowing the financial burden involved with developing regions for TLD I cannot fault them for struggling on the first content release. The loot refresh is a lot of work as well - especially given that Great Bear makes one of the largest sandbox environments in gaming at the moment. Even with all of that work we received music, new sound effects, and new item variants.

I will state that as an interloper player I receive very little from the initial release - but I will have patience. The new region gives little reason for an interloper player to journey there other than to test themselves.

I hope to see additional ways to travel slightly faster, and some of the item variants appearing in interloper runs. But I see how this lays the groundwork for more content. Just remember that in the Quiet Apocalypse that patience is a strength. This is only the first content release after almost a decade of free updates and it is my hope that this new revenue will allow Hinterland to develop more freely this beautiful game. I eagerly await the next content update, and will return to update my review for the next.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTales from the Far Territory? No. This DLC should be titled "The Long Dark: Tales from the Long Walk." To be fair, this game has a lot of walking anyway, but something about the trip to get to the "Forsaken Airfield" had about as much sense of satisfaction as walking across town to an empty parking lot.

The introduction of a new mechanic "Glimmer Fog" (insomnia risk) is yet another annoyance to add to the list of annoying mechanics such as intestinal parasites in cooked meat, or cabin fever to improve your odds of staying outside and dying from wolves or cold with the occasional bear thrown in the mix.

It takes forever to get there. It's big, it's new, and it's largely devoid of anything interesting. The ONLY redeeming aspect of the Forsaken Airfield (besides the "oh cool! Some new models and art assets!") is the potential for a custom game/escape scenario. "Walk 10K miles to the airport and escape in an airplane during a geomagnetic storm!" Maybe?

It's too big, too empty, too boring, and far too long of a walk to be worthwhile. It should also be an option as a starting location; I suspect it isn't simply to try and push up "DLC playtime average hours" metrics because it takes forever to access the location.

I love The Long Dark. I would have rather seen a DLC dedicated to "base customization" that was discussed many months back. Instead we ended up with a giant, slow, snow-covered sandbox without any meaningful sand in it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrankly, This DLC isn't worth the $20.00 you're paying for. Don't get me wrong, I love Hinterland and The Long Dark to death. Hinterland, although frustrating when it comes to mods, is a diligent studio who puts an incredible amount of detail in their game. I enjoy their work. If it goes on a 50-60% sale, that's a good price. Most people are down-voting it due to the fact that they didn't understand it was an expansion pack over the course of 2023.

However! This DLC (at this point) is essentially a pre-order for the majority of its content. I wish that Hinterland had released more content on Dec 5, as Forsaken Airfield & Its respective transition zone are somewhat bland. What this DLC is supposed to give you (By the end of the year) is two new animals, 3 new maps (plus the transition zone), a variety of "unique" gear, an NPC in survival mode, alongside new mechanics. So far Hinterland has delivered one map & the transition zone, unique rifles, and the glimmer fog mechanic without the shortwave; Which, was kind of the whole point of glimmer fog.

Forsaken Airfield is.... cool? Although there really isn't much reason as to going there. Most of the surrounding bodies of water are points of contention for wolves, alongside the fact that its one of the coldest regions in TLD. As said earlier, it would've been more ideal had hinterland released more content on Dec 5, giving Forsaken Airfield a little more excitement than it has currently. As of the moment that I'm writing this, only 2-4 mods are functioning due to the DLC update. Kinda sucks.

Again, I like Hinterland and what they do however $20 is far (not pun intended) too much for what you're getting At this moment. Wait until its on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
