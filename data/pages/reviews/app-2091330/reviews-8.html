\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a travesty of an expansion for an incredible base game.

The expansion has been mired with delays and bugs. The touted 8-10 week release schedule has been missed every time. Delays are not unusual in the industry, but despite them, the results have been infuriating.

The content introduced has been excessively ill conceived. The new region is a long, uneventful trek to what is essentially a wasteland of an area. New features appear to be bafflingly lacking in purpose. Fire Hardened arrows are a costly alternative that accomplish nothing that a stone cannot. Acorns are a cumbersome way to gain a tiny amount of food, but clutter the UI with redundant crafting options. Glitter Fog and Insomnia being little more than annoyances in their presence.

But worst of all to me is the buggy releases and the terrible handling of them. This DLC is an Early Access project. The releases are produced in such a state that you wonder if they were tested in any way. Fishing completely broken, rope climbs becoming deadly, animals phasing between life and death in a loud, jittering manner, sudden changes in the gender of the character as we play.

All this could be so much easier to digest if we could rollback our game versions to the most recent stable release, but no. The game has forced updates to play, and previous versions of TFTFT are not available, so if you are, say, in the middle of your long permadeath run and a latest release bugs out leaving you without access to food, or stranded at the bottom of a long rope climb, well you are at the mercy of an eventual release of a hotfix.

This DLC is a disaster. It has all the hallmarks of new and/or inexperienced developers not knowing how to use their codebase while overseen by similarly inexperienced or overly absent producers/directors. I abhor Early Access releases for games, and now it has seeped into DLC too.\t\t\t\t\t
