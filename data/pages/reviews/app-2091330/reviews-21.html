\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith Tales From The Far Territory we see the first paid DLC for The Long Dark. Upon release it's akin to a 'Season Pass', where you get some features up front but have to wait for the rest to be released periodically over the next twelve months. These features do not add anything to the Wintermute Story Mode, so if you are strictly a Story-Mode player, you should pass up on this release. If however you are very much into survival mode - and a lot of fans consider this to be the true core game of The Long Dark - there is a lot to be excited about. Let's break it down:

Paid Tales DLC Initial Release:

Two new maps: Far Range Branch Line & Transfer Pass, and the Forsaken Airfield.

New features: Glimmer Fog and Insomnia Affliction. Item Variants.


The above is what you can access now. Future updates will come periodically over the next twelve months. They are as follows:

Two more maps to come: Industrial Mine and Mountain Pass.

New features to come:

Handheld shortwave radio.
Enhanced cooking with recipes.
Travois for dragging supplies.
Ptarmigan - a bird that you can hunt.
Trader. You won't believe his low, low temperature - er prices!
Camera & Dark Room for photography obsessives like me.
Cougar. Of the animal variety, mercifully.
Safe House customisation, allowing you to organise and decorate the places you call home.

If you're a Survival mode veteran the above should excite. If you've just bought The Long Dark they may not seem like that big a deal, but together they strike me as a sizable update for the money. So far I'm enjoying the first instalment. The new maps have a compelling style of their own and are pretty vast, with the airfield being a big open affair akin to Pleasant Valley, only somewhat bleaker. Glimmer fog is a moody, eerie new update that perhaps edges a little bit too much into the 'magical realism' category for me in a game that otherwise grounds itself in real-life possibilities, but it's a neat mechanic. The item variants add quite a bit of spice to the survival player's regular looting sprees, but won't fuss the hardcore Interloper crowd that much.

Of the upcoming features it is the Safe House Customisation, trader and travois that excite me the most - with the camera and dark room coming up close. As for value for money, well I always thought that I'd be happy to pay a fiver for each new map as they offer so many hours of gameplay each. As I'm getting effectively four maps with this pass and a bunch of new features I'd say it's worth the money for me. But is it worth it to you? First you've got to ask yourself...

1: Am I here only for Story Mode?

If you're only interested in the adventures of Will MacKenzie and Astrid then no, you shouldn't. Stick with The Long Dark package you already have, or buy the standalone Wintermute Story release.

2: Have I just bought The Long Dark / The Long Dark Survival Mode Edition?

There's a lot packed into Survival Mode. It'll keep you entertained for hundreds of hours. You'll also be getting the following features released for free over the next twelve months:

Visor notes which reveal hidden caches.
Improvised crampons and fire hardened arrows.
Acorns and burdock.
Improved fishing.
New wildlife in the form of the doe.
Hunted - a new instalment of The Hunted Challenge.

As it stands I'd just enjoy the regular Survival Mode if you're new to the game, and buy this when you've explored all the maps and itch for more. As The Long Dark maps outside of this DLC currently span more than 55km squared, the base game will keep you going for a good long while. However be aware the opening 10% discount won't be in place later on, and you might find yourself itching for fun-with-safehouses, trading and expanded cooking later on. Naturally if money isn't too much of a concern I'd advise getting it, there's quite a bit of value in this bundle.

3: Am I a long-standing Survival veteran?

I can feel comfortable in recommending this one to long-standing survival players. This is a DLC that was requested by the community in a poll a while's back, a community largely built up of core survival mode players, and it addresses many feature requests the community have made over the years. Those who bought the Long Dark a long time ago should also reflect that upon release seven years back and for a long while after it generally cost less than ten bucks, and over the years has expanded its map size fifth-fold and added many new features, so that the base game is well worth the current asking price. So quite a few in the community who have sunk hundreds of hours into the game are happy to reward Hinterland with some more of the brass jingling in their pockets. I also don't feel upset that this has come before Wintermute was completed. The original Long Dark campaign was planned to be around ten to twelve hours in length, and so far it's clocking to about thirty-five hours of gameplay with the four episodes released so far, so the final vision of the game is significantly greater than what was promised, and all the better for it. Yes, Hinterland messed up with an undercooked initial story-mode release and had to waste a year fixing it, but overall I feel they've more than delivered.

I hope that answers those who are naturally dithering over whether to buy it or not. I think it's definitely worth getting, but the DLC isn't going anywhere and with so many great games knocking around at the moment you can easily choose to wait a while and see how it pans out. For Long Dark fanatics like me, which I consider one of the greatest games ever made, it's a must buy. For more information on what is contained in this release, and road-map updates, please visit: https://www.thelongdark.com/expansion/
