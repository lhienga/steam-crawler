The trek into the Far Territory can feel long in some ways, but it is not prohibitive (even on stalker). I'm glad the distances aren't any shorter. To me it adds realism that you have to plan a bit for the journey.

When I got to the airfield, I experienced a moment where I realized I could get very lost as the weather started to turn. I mean totally lost. In other maps you can go straight for a while and find the edge of the map or some landmark you can benefit from. The Forsaken Airfield has one giant expanse in the middle. You have to know where you are heading.

Each map has some special ways to try to kill you and this one will try to do it with its new affliction: zappy-vapor night ghosts.

You can't stay inside, you can't stay outside, and its a long way back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter spending all my food and firewood on a three day walk from Broken Railroad, I am now stuck inside a control tower, suffering from frostbite and insomnia. With no food, water or any means to make a fire, cabin fever is rapidly approaching as I look at all the delicious meat walking around among hundreds of cat tails on the frozen lakes and rivers outside. A kilometre away, in the snowy, aurora lit bear territory, I can spot a crashed helicopter, and it makes me wonder if there could possibly be any matches or flares to find there. Perhaps the cabin on the cliff on the other side of this vast, frozen hellhole of a region has a warm bed for lone survivors with no bedroll? If I could just make it across the tarmac to the huge hangar, there clearly must be some loot to be found. Maybe a candy bar, or at least some cloth to repair by severely damaged clothes? My options are limited. I could stay put and starve to death, or if I want to see the dawn of a new day, risk freezing to death by looking for some calories to keep me going.

In all honesty, I do not think this DLC is for everyone, but I do believe that most long-time survival mode players of The Long Dark will enjoy the new content. I still have a lot of fun with the base game, and wanted to support further development of survival mode by buying the DLC. Fully aware of the fact that the DLC is supposed to be a season pass with new content gradually made available over several updates the coming year, I am not the slightest disappointed by the first release. The new loot locations and the mementos alone makes the game fresh and exciting to me, so the release of a huge, new, challenging region is just a bonus that will keep me busy while waiting for further updates.

I can highly recommend this DLC to people who have already invested some time in survival mode, and thus are aware of what this game is all about, including the long, slow-paced walks, and frustrating moments eventually resulting in the death of their character. Impatient people who mostly enjoy fast-paced multiplayer action games, or those who are not prepared to lose their long-running saves and start over again, should probably be looking for some other games.

Personally I think The Long Dark is a masterpiece, and I am really looking forward to the new updates. I am also truly grateful for the flawless native Linux support, and hope Hinterland will keep supporting Linux in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

Like:

"Launch day! Today, we are launching part 1 of a year long of updates. What you can expect to find is the Forsaken Airfield, and it's transition zones, in addition to the introduction of:

Glimmer Fog,
Insomnia,
Three New .303 Rifles types in addition to the original
The Woodwright's Bow,
Spelunker's Lantern,
Doe variant for deer,
Craftable clampons,
Mementos where you can find hints to under vehicle visors and other places, that can lead to loot caches in the world

We also did a complete overhaul of the loot spawn tables, and changed up player spawn locations to help make survival mode feel like new again."

Seriously, if they just did the above, I think people would be a lot more chill. I know I would have been happier to know "what's new" in the game so I know exactly what I'm looking for in game.

The fault of the Road map, is they're showing all that is to come, rather then breaking it down to to the planned updates. Like "At launch, we plan to bring this to you. At the next DLC Drop, we plan on doing this. Drop three is going to be XYZ", etc. All subject to change depending on how Development is going."

This would help the community at large, by stabilizing expectations. Otherwise, yes, the Roadmap feels misleading.

I know I was looking for cougars and ptarmagins, etc.

The Last thing I want to know, is if I need to start new runs with the updates or not.

This whole DLC was an absolute disappointment.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off I love The Long Dark. Both story mode and survival mode are awesome if you love true survival games. That being said my 2 complaints about the DLC tales from the far territory is that it was hell getting here, seriously it's a long hard journey to get there. You can't just load in, you have to find it and getting to the abandoned airport is crazy long but that not my main complaint. The main problem is where is the rifle? I stared out in Milton and I went from there to mystery lake then to Forlorn Muskeg and into broken railroad and finally from broken railroad made the long track into the far territory but no where through any of those places have I found a rifle. Thank God I found a revolver in mystery lake but when I couldn't find the rifle anywhere I thought ok maybe they ate saving it for the far territory because I heard the DLC content would bring new loot and a new rifle its what it said. So I said ok I can be patient and wait. Well I got to little abandoned coal shoot and abandoned buildings and no rifle or gun anywhere well then I went all the way to the huge abandoned air field with several buildings and looked everywhere and there is no rifle to be found and all the "loot" is the same. As far as I can tell we paid for the DLC content and only got a new region with all the same loot and no rifle anywhere the only thing new and different about the game is the deer there are now doe and bucks instead of just bucks, there is a new region but no new items, and rifle is just gone. Not only that but everyone gave up their previous saves for this because the update wiped everyone survival play clean and we were promised a lot more. Honestly as much as I love the long dark this was a huge let down and needs to be fixed ASAP! Why make a new region and not put a rifle in it anywhere? Why tell us there will be new loot and new rifles and all this when there isn't? I'm very disappointed in Hinterland, you guys are so much better then this. If you pay for DLC content including a new rifle and new loot in a new location that rifle and loot should be waiting for you in that new location. I would settle for the old rifle being there at this point. I have like 60 bullets and can't find the rifle anywhere. Maybe there is an update coming or something so if things change I'll update my review but right now I'm not impressed and very disappointed as I'm sure a lot of others are as well. I feel like you should of waited until you had everything ready before you released the DLC because now I've already been there and looted the place so the rifle will not show up nor will the the new loot that coming unless I start over yet again. But despite all this I believe in you guys and I believe in this game and I would still recommend this game to others because it's truly a work of art and one of the best survival games out there but right now I can not recommend the DLC content. I say people should just wait till its ready or they will be just as disappointed. I know Hinterland did say it would be a six month process and have updates I just expected more and Honestly if you do get the DLC content don't come here yet. The harsh journey is not worth it and the loot is no different and there is no weapons here at all, only a place to forge arrow heads that's it. My advice is wait till it's finished so you don't have to start over and do it all over again to get the new content and you can be surprised the first time around but in a good way, kind of like Christmas morning, instead of in the very sad way I was surprised. Love you Hinterland but do better.\t\t\t\t\t
