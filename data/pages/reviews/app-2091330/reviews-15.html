\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’m a long time player of ‘The Long Dark’s survival mode (400+ hours across other platforms), and picked up the Steam version for the slightly earlier access into the Expansion Pass.
Playing on the Steam Deck for the first time, with Tales From The Far Territory installed, I can say that the experience has been very positive. No crashes or bugs, quick loading times. Everything seems good. It’s a shame that there were hiccups on launch day, but these were fixed quickly (and I think that voicing your complaints immediately in a negative Steam review, rather than in communication with the devs, is a bit harsh). The branding and information around ‘Tales From The Far Territory’ has made it clear that it is an Expansion Pass - ie, not the kind of DLC where ALL content will be added immediately upon purchase. You do not need to buy this pass to continue playing survival mode.
I’ve ventured a little ways into the new zones on a fresh voyageur difficulty save starting at Broken Railroad, and, after quite a lot of walking, managed to get to the air field and set up a base. The new areas are expansive, intriguing and in-keeping thematically with the rest of the game (I particularly like the Vacant Depot in the transfer pass, similar to the Mountaineer’s Hut in TWM).
I’m excited to see how the Expansion Pass progresses. Many of the updates on the proposed road map are things I’ve been eager to see in the game for a while. For now, it is obviously not something a brand new player should rush to buy, given the expanse of Great Bear Island already available to explore, but it is welcome new content for a well-worn survivor, and I appreciate the amount of free updates Hinterland managed to release before a paid DLC became necessary. Looking forward to the year of content ahead, and will update this review to match a little further down the line.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start this review off by saying that out of the truly mind boggling amount of survival games, on steam and elsewhere, I believe that The Long Dark is the best one, period. I love this game, and if I had enough time I would spend hours writing about every single little thing I love about it. So when I heard that TLD would be getting a large DLC package, I was extremely excited.

Unfortunately, it seems that Hinterland has botched this first part of the expansion in various ways I don't feel the need to get into here. I was not personally affected by any of the important issues other people ran into and Hinterland has already apologized for, except for a few strange bugs here and there. I found the three special rifle variants in a shack at the hydroelectric dam, which was an extremely bizarre thing to stumble upon and did throw me for a loop. Other than that, my experience with the new content has been solid.

With all that said, I think the new content this DLC brings is very interesting and excellent in many ways, but it also isn't quite worth the twenty dollar price tag. Yet.

I'll spend the rest of this review explaining that yet.

My favorite part of this DLC was just the presentation of it. I know that some people have complaints about the length of certain sections of the new region and it's connectors, namely the initial areas, but personally I love it. For me, that just made this place feel more real. As anyone who has been outside can tell you: the real world is huge and mostly empty. For a survival game with a heavy focus on the challenge of hostile weather and the immersion that goes along with that, I think the extended length of the initial sections works as an interesting challenge. It's also just extremely beautiful. That's subjective of course, but I'm ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ right.

For me, a major part of why TLD is so damn good is the beauty and danger of the natural world it manages to enjoyably recreate. Those initial areas are a solo hike through the wilderness, and I'd like to remind people that this is what the rest of the game is too. Just with less *stuff* all over the place to pick up.

Once you're past the initial areas, the rest of the map where you'll spend your time is huge. And it really feels that way too. Forsaken Airfield is a welcome departure from the other areas just in terms of how flat and open the terrain is once you're out of the mountains. When the weather is clear you're constantly treated to a vision of extreme beauty. It's genuinely awe inspiring. Purely in terms of being a pretty place to look at, Forsaken Airfield has become my favorite region, beating out my previous favorite Ash Canyon.

They absolutely knocked the visuals and terrain out of the park here, in my opinion. Unfortunately, though, the vastness of the map is at odds with how little there actually is to do, currently. It's one of those things where I was fine with that during the initial transit section, but after reaching the main area I was expecting to find many things to collect, investigate, and explore. I did not receive that to a fully satisfactory degree. Honestly, theres not that much to do or places to explore in this region yet. The places that *are* there are certainly interesting, but there just isn't enough stuff in this region to justify the journey it took to reach it.

I was surprised at how little stuff there was to find in the new areas past the transit zones. I expected to find some of the new equipment they were talking about, but found nothing I hadn't seen a million times before in the other regions. I don't think that's a total deal breaker because you really do need those items. But that loot is not commensurate with the journey it took to get there. Forsaken Airfield is probably the most difficult region to live in for those reasons: how long it takes to get between the main areas of activity, and the lack of loot you need to keep yourself alive. If you went into Forsaken Airfield with a very skilled, fully kitted out survivor, with a backpack full of advanced tools and other high end rare stuff, you *could* make that region your home. But I imagine the struggle to survive there is just too difficult to make it fun for long-term survival, and an important part of that struggle is, of course, the weather.

Forsaken Airfield's weather is *extremely* bad. It's very cold and windy as ♥♥♥♥, and white out conditions are incredibly common. You can expect to be dealing with lethally cold weather constantly during your stay. Surviving in this region is a real challenge, which is super fun. The most fun I had with this DLC was when I got stranded in a cabin on the very northern part of the map due to weather. I was almost literally snowed in by a never-ending storm, with my supplies dwindling as it went on and on. When the weather finally broke, I made a break for it, only for the crappy weather to return a few hours later. It's that kind of challenge that makes the game so fun! Nature does not give a ♥♥♥♥ about you. To remain in the lands of the living you have to bear nature's fury while making good survival decisions. I love that aspect of this game so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ much I could marry it.

But beyond that, there really isn't too much to do in this region, yet. Things to see, certainly. I could look at this area all day, but I can't help but feel a bit disappointed by it. I paid twenty dollars for this, but what I got was something that felt like it was worth five, maybe ten if I was feeling generous. But with that said, I'm fine with the twenty dollar price tag because I know that more content is coming soonish, and from what I've seen on the roadmap the new content does seem really awesome. I hope that with the next updates Forsaken Airfield gets a bit more love, a bit more stuff to find, and hopefully some encounters with those Cougars they've been teasing. Those narrow canyons you first walk through would be an absolutely amazing place to get ambushed by a mountain lion.

If you haven't bought this yet, I would strongly recommend you hold off on buying it until at least the next big content update drops in a few months.

I want to emphasize that what exists in this DLC in its current form is definitely good. I enjoyed it. There just isn't enough of it yet to justify the price tag.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisregard the negative reviews. People are being silly. Every single thing people are complaining about has been clearly communicated by the Devs several times over the last few months.

Does it have bugs? Yep. Name one game or DLC that didn't at launch. However, Hinterland fixed the most egregious bugs within a few HOURS of launch. That's impressive! As for the people who are complaining that it's 'unfinished'...um duh? It's not a DLC, it's an expansion pass for the next year or so. Again, click the Steam information pop up or follow the numerous links to get more information. If you can't be bothered to click a link or read a devlog to get more information in this day and age, that's on you.

Now don't get me wrong, losing saves because they made drastic changes to the game coding is unfortunate, but it was necessary to keep the game current and to further improve it in the future. Also, it was clearly communicated months ago. Hinterland also made it possible to continue your saves using the Steam Time Capsule feature, which required quite a bit of extra work on their end and they didn't have to do it. They did it for you.

I paid $10-20 when the original game launched and have gotten Skyrim level gaming hours out of it. However, I don't see any wolves flying around in the sky backwards. I would gladly support Hinterland considering every update since then has been COMPLETELY FREE.

Now for my initial experiences. The game feels completely different and fresh. Not knowing where every item spawns makes it feel like I'm starting the game all over again. The new variants are unique and really help expand the game. The new sound effects have made me stop in my tracks and just soak in the ambience (or try to find a way around because it doesn't sound friendly). Overall, I am very impressed with it so far.

TL;DR: If you love The Long Dark, buy the Expansion Pass.\t\t\t\t\t
