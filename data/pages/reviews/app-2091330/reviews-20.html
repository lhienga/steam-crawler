\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEXTENSIVE REVIEW:

This DLC had a bit of a rocky start with the major bug rendering the game unplayable, but that was fixed within a matter of 1-2 hours.

It would appear that most of the people complaining about this DLC's lack of content clearly did not pay enough attention to Dev Diaries and the like, as they have stated time and time again that this DLC is a 12-MONTH CAMPAIGN. Meaning all of the content listed to be included in the expansion is being drip-fed in bunches roughly 8-10 weeks apart.

That said, I did expect *more* content to be available right away given it's the first initial release. I figured they would go full-on with this very first release, then have smaller updates along the way. However, that's nothing but a minor disappointment--you're still getting your money's worth.

Included in this first patch of the DLC is:
Forsaken Airfield (Gigantic region, possibly the largest one yet, with lots of unique and interesting points of interest!)
A hub region which will connect the two coming regions, plus a transitional region in order to get to the hub region in the first place.
Glimmer fog & insomnia affliction.
Five (5) new weapon/item variants.
Improvized crampons (game-changing!)
Visor notes (hidden caches!)
Does! A deer, a deer, a female deer!
And new voice lines ???

Question marks at the end of that last point, as I haven't seen nor read anywhere this to be the case, but I swear I've heard voice lines in this DLC that I have never heard before, and I have way over 4000 hours of The Long Dark playtime.

If you don't think this "little" amount of content is enough to justify the price, then hold off on buying the DLC until more content is released, and buy it at any point where you feel the price is justified, instead of complaining about having bought it now and not having the content you hoped for.
NB: You'll actually save money by buying the DLC now than doing so later when the price(s) will go up, but you do you.

I'm personally content with the initial content given, especially considering we'll be getting another content update in late February/early March, judging by the time-table.

No, my main gripe with this update would have to be the bugs. It has only been three days since launch as of this review, so many of these issues are likely (and hopefully) to be fixed aptly, but I feel prospective buyers should be made aware of these problems.

1 - Menu stutters. Certain in-game menus--mainly the Clothing tab--takes way longer than any of the other menus to load, and sometimes having multiple articles of the same type of clothing (two pairs of gloves, for example) will render them invisible in the clothing equipping section. It can be fixed by clicking off of them and then back, but hopefully this will be investigated further by Hinterland.

2 - Hitboxes. Many of the game's hitboxes seem to have taken a hit with this update. Most noticeably the Old-Man's Beard lichen, in which you have about 2-4 pixels only that provide you the prompt to harvest. Other things include helicopter/plane doors when inside; the new harvestable barrels found in the Forsaken Airfield region; and a few other miscellaneous items. I'm hopeful this will be looked upon as well.

3 - Invisible items/corpses. Yup, certain items and corpses in the game are now invisible. I encountered an invisble frozen corpse inside of a cave in Broken Railroad, and inside of the Hunting Cabin there was an invisible bench.

4 - World holes. In certain locations of the new region there are areas where there are missing textures from the terrain, or where the terrain clips with other things.

5 - There is a snow shelter on "Drift Island" which spawns ~5 times inside of each other, meaning once you enter one, you can't leave. You click on the shelter and it gives you the prompt to "Use", "Repair", or "Dismantle". If you click "cancel", you get teleported beneath the terrain and you are unable to move. SO DO NOT USE THE SNOW SHELTER ON DRIFT ISLAND.

On my third visit to the shelter (after relogs), I decided to dismantle it. That's when I noticed after having done so, it still stood there. So I did this again 4-5 times until it finally vanished.

Update, Dec 9: After further research, there seems to be more than only one snow shelter in Forsaken Airfield that does this. So I suggest not using any pre-built snow shelters until this bug gets fixed. Also, these shelters seem to respawn somehow, so I guess we now finally have renewable cloth as well for the time being!


6 - Certain items in the world clip into the ground or hover in the air. Not a huge deal for most, I'm sure, but my immersion does break a bit when I see items floating above the ground or whatever object it's meant to be on, or when I see furniture lodged half-way inside of a wall or into the ground.

Conclusion: Whilst this review may seem quite negative, I'm overall content with and do recommend this DLC. I've played the game for well over 4k hours and enjoyed free updates for years--20 bucks for this DLC is well worth it, especially when taking into consideration what's to come over the course of next year.

Overall, my rating for this DLC so far(!) would have to be a 6/10. Above average, but slightly lackluster.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe fundamental issue behind the support or outrage of the current DLC all revolves around if you are willing to accept the concept of "Early Access DLC" or not. It's being marketed as a "Season Pass" but traditionally speaking, season passes contain multiple "Complete" DLC Packs that are coming soon or partly available currently. When a Season pass DLC is released, it is most always in a complete state with the rest coming soon or later in the year.

However, further complicating things was the lack of transparency on the release phases. Only until the day of launch did they produce a single video talking about the included features but in a way that could of easily left upon speculation that there was "More" to discover as I expected from any available for purchase DLC product. I figured, there must be more.

They're asking you to buy in at 60% of the final price point for about 15-20% or so of the actual content currently available which set the wrong expectations going in. The update consists of almost purely cosmetic features and new regions but none of the gameplay that made people interested in the product in the first place. Had the product been sold for closer to $10 and had they been more transparent about the first phase of features on the steam page and marketing materials none of this outrage would of occurred. But, they chose to keep their cards close to their chest.

I realize defenders of this company who love the game will point out the price point buys into a "Year long content stream" but I'm sorry, I don't pay for promises, I pay for content. The price of an "Early access DLC" needs to match the current phase of available content and properly scale as content is delivered. If you want to launch a DLC with 15% or so of the content, don't sell it at 60% because there's an assumption of current value connected to the "Early access" model.

All that being said, I love the long dark and I believe the team will be able to eventually execute a great product and I'm excited to see how it ends up but this decision, marketing campaign and abstract poorly executed communication was just a failure and I'm okay saying it. It's okay to be nuanced and say you love the long dark and simultaneously that the DLC's design and launch execution was terribly done. Both are true. When you sell a product, you're selling exactly that, "a product", not a promise. Content must justify the price and in my opinion it just didn't. I'm all for supporting your favorite companies but I'm quite sick of them taking advantage of their most die hard fans. I recommend waiting to pick this up until the value matches the asking price.

However I will change this review later after I see how well they manage the "Content release stream". =)\t\t\t\t\t
