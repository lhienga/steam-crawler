\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: I play a custom game with Interloper weather and needs, better loot quality that gives everything a chance to spawn (but rarely), and only (low) sleep healing and birch tea.

I've always enjoyed how sheer distance is used to create higher stakes in the game, but usually it's viable to just pop from safe house to safe house without having to do anything extra. I've wanted to see the game go further to require migration prep of a sort to get to (and stay in) some regions, and Forsaken Airfield leans in that direction. The journey there is long and requires more resources than normal. The airfield itself with long distances and harsh weather requires more improvisation such as emergency fires, warm tea buff, and torch use to get around the map without losing health to attrition. Food is scarce in a real way since the weather keeps you sheltering much of the time. On my first visit I left with my tail between my legs after 2 weeks or so because I wasn't sure I could keep things going much longer and the resources needed to get back to Broken Railroad isn't trivial. In short, I've enjoyed the challenge of this region, as well as the other content being added that gives more breadth to the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: I haven't completed the first tale 'Signal Void' , so some of the story I'm going to assume so far, and this is my opinion.

The Long Dark: Tales From The Far Territory so far is a good DLC. The introduction of the first "Tale" is good, letting on to a darker story than Wintermute has so far, about different medical experiments happening in the abandoned bunkers all around the Southern Great Bear Island area. I like the introduction of going place-to-place to restore the signal towers to find the bunkers or hidden caches presumably hidden by the different personnel or the scientists of the bunkers before or after The First Flare.

Why I like this DLC so far is because it makes you go to those regions, it gives you, the player, a purpose to find these bunkers, and to learn what happened in this world. It gives a silent, non-narrative survival game a hidden story, for you to find and learn what happened before the power went out in the Northern Canadian regions due to The First Flare.

What I don't like is the lack of the "New" clothing items and "item variants" added. 4 new clothing items, and 5 new item variants, 3 of which are about the Hunting Rifle, are a bit disappointing. I like diversity, more options to help your character survive in the winter wasteland while looking better. If they added 1 to 2 more clothing items, and item variants of... let's say, the hatchet, revolver, hunting knife or like a thicker, longer prybar that has more power, but a pound or two heavier, and take a bit longer to use.

I realize that Hinterland is a small game developer, and that it takes a bit to make these updates, so I respect them for their work for this game. I'm just a person who is used to more options and variety in games, and I want to say my piece for what I'd like for the game, and say my review for this DLC, Tales Of The Far Territory.\t\t\t\t\t
