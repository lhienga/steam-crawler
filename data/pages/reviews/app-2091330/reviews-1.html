\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have very mixed feelings on this DLC. On the one hand I really enjoy a lot of the things that the've added, especially radio questline, the airfield, pretty much everything up until the addition of the mine. One caveat being that the airfield's glimmer fog effect is an eyestrain that induced a couple headaches here and there.

I do not like the mine. At all. I expected I would, which made this surprising.
The map features annoying toxic waste areas that require a mask to deal with. This mask screws with your visibility and is *very* limited in its use. This would be fine if some of the areas where you need it weren't already really dark and hard to navigate. Which brings me to the biggest reason why I think this part of the DLC is just...not great.
The mine map being inside makes sense right? Of course it does, it's a mine. That's fine.
It's not just the mine though; there are a lot of interiors in this map, and they are MASSIVE.
IDK. I get that this area is supposed to be late game and tough to deal with, but even on a well stocked and long running Stalker game it mostly just frustrated me.

I still think thus far this is a good expansion on the base game and look forward to it's conclusion. I'm aware that for a positive review I mostly explained why I didn't like a certain aspect, but I genuinely just enjoyed all the other stuff. I got very good at using the bow and arrow from the island across the frozen lake in Forsaken Airfield :) it was a good practice location for a skill I have neglected in most of my playthroughs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I stand by my review for the base game, I can actually say this is a good DLC for a couple reasons;

First and foremost, if you had a long survival run going and are concerned about losing it to the DLC, you won't. Your game will continue but you may want to re-check places you have been for anything new. But I hope you have cooking level 5 already because chances are all the new cooking items are most likely already spoiled.

Second, you get a couple new large maps. I have been all over the new airfield and it has some scary moments like the first time I ran into glimmer fog and almost walked right into my good buddy Yogi the bear. I don't think our friendship is mutual though. Sometimes he tries to maul me and I put a high velocity flare into his face...oh good times.

There are some cons though, I am not a big fan of the cooking items. Some give decent timed benefits, but many of the items are heavy and the effects they counteract are easy enough to deal with so I don't find it all that worth hauling them to my many bases other than...it's something to do.

So I'd say if you already have the base game with a lot of hours into it and are looking for something new, give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give a "eh" review, but I can't.

For the price of $20, is this worth it? Are you getting enough that you aren't getting in the main game to be worth $20? I would say that depends on what you are looking for. Tales From the Far Territory adds a few new zones to the game, the two main ones being Forsaken Airfield, and The Zone of Contamination. These zones do provide interesting story content that has not existed in survival mode up until this point, where you unearth a conspiracy and a catastrophe that happened just before "the quiet apocalypse".

My main issue with these zones is just how long you have to walk to enter, or exit them. You really need to prepare for a long journey if you want to access them, which is fine in and of itself, but to complete the story, you have to make multiple trips there and back, and the amount of time it takes to move back and forth, and travel the entire old world, really takes the wind out of my sails. Undercuts the feeling of exploration and discovery for me.

Let's say you have finished all of the story, do you have a reason to trek to and back from the old zones? Do they offer exciting loot you can't get from anywhere else? I mean; maybe, the short wave radio is the only thing that is really worth going there for, because it unlocks caches around the map that you wouldn't be able to find otherwise. Even then, though, it's not really worth it given what I have been able to find with it so far. An MRE and some flares.

All the other new loot has been retroactively added to older zones. The only thing you are really getting is the story, which is cool, but I feel like they should work on retroactively adding survival story content to older zones, rather than creating new ones where you have to go on homer's odyssey to unlock the ability to find it (after you make the trek -again- and turn on all the radio towers).

I mean this, by the way. I hope the devs don't abandon the idea of adding story to survival. I love the idea of uncovering nuggets of lore that weren't there before. I just hope next time it's a little closer to the main zones, so that the trip isn't so grueling. Like I ended up pausing and putting on a podcast because of how long it took to get there and back.

Either way, I still love the studio, and I'm still going to keep paying for this stuff because I want to keep seeing updates. I don't think it's bad out of laziness or ill will, just a design hit-and-miss that a lot of people actually do enjoy. Just not me.\t\t\t\t\t
