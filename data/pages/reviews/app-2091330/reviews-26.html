\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey don't make games like The Long Dark. And I mean that both in the sense that there are not a lot of "they"s like Hinterland, who have been rolling out free, premium content to their cult-classic players on the regular for almost a decade; and also in the sense that the literary conflict of Man vs. Environment just isn't a genre that is explored in most media, let alone in gaming.

Every other "survival" game out there needs sharks or zombies or dinosaurs to make their game compelling because the devs don't fundamentally understand or prioritize internal drama, the aesthetic of contemplation or the value of suspense. Which is fine, those games are fun! But The Long Dark is something different. Hinterland nails its environment and art direction so well, and understands the triggers of dread so well, that they don't need anything to make the game gripping beyond the dispassionate, immutable facts that time passes and winter is cold.

This update is more of a season pass than it is a complete DLC package. It's a massive dump of new and intriguing maps that will keep you exploring for a few months while they roll out some of the new features and fan service that players have spent a few years salivating for. If you want all the new paid features at once, then sign up later. If you want it the minute it's ready, sign up now. It's not rocket science, guys.

I mean look, you sit over there and stare at the wall for a year so that you can buy all the content at once and get "good value". I'll start playing now. Two years from now, when we're both out twenty-five bucks, I'll have 50% more time with this content compared with your solid theory as to whether it's an eggshell or semi-gloss. Honest to God.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay.
The DLC did release with a rather significant bug. That got fixed within 2 hours.
Hotfixes were released multiple times through the afternoon and evening.

The first part of the DLC is good.
It feels like I am learning how to play the game again, after over 7000 hours on Steam online, offline, on XBox One, and an Switch.
That should say something.
The novelty of new "first times" for finding the new loot spawns.
The loot table refresh that has made the game more like it used to be back in 2016 when I first started playing.
The strategies and playstyle I had has to change, and I have to learn new travel routes, and new looting paths to get kitted out well enough to not freeze to death. I've been playing vanilla Stalker for the last 4 years, and got so comfy in the game that I haven't died unless I decided to kill my survivor off so I could start a new run. The early game struggle was always the most exciting part of the game for me. The struggle is real again. I feel like I did when I first started playing 6 1/2 years ago.
And I get to feel that again when the next part of the DLC drops and things change again.
And again, when the next part drops...

I am loving having that "new-ish player" feeling again, learning new strategies and not having things be where I got used to them being.
Yes, there are still some bugs that need to be fixed- nothing game-breaking for me on Linux or Windows 10. The game is running well, even if a few whacky things are happening at times (levitating loot- lol). If you love the game and want new content- buy now. If you just bought the game and aren't sure- you can wait to see if the game fits you well. I expect it to only get better as they add more things over the next 12 months.

Yes, I am a fangirl. And I don't regret it one bit.
