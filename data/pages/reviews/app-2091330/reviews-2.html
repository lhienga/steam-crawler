\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like The Long Dark. It is a thoughtful game full of bespoke beauty: in landscapes, in music, in gameplay, in feeling while playing at 3 AM in winter with the windows open to feel the cold. I've had the game for a long while now, having bought it for $10 back in 2016, but only in the past few years have I really played it. Since then, the developers have been adding features every holiday season. The value I get out of the base game far exceeds the price I paid for it.

Naturally as an open-world game there are always things that players want added (*cough* SEASONS PLEASE *cough*). Mods definitely add another layer of dimension and like with most modding communities make the official developers look unproductive. And there is merit to that. It has been many years now that the story mode is still unfinished. Luckily I wasn't too into the game for its whole history and haven't had to wait as long as others. But I've also not played the story mode at all, wanting to wait until its complete. To think that a good chunk of this timeline was the developers going back to improve the art and quality of what should have been up to par originally is laughable at best. Part of this may be due to the studio's lack of funding, something touched on by Hinterland themselves; in purchasing the DLC in early access, the studio would have more funds to put towards both completion of the DLC and the story mode, and expansion/improvement of the base game. Nevertheless I enjoy this game and the content that they do add, and had no qualms giving Hinterland money that they had mostly already earned, while gaining access to their DLC and hopefully investing in the games further future as well.

The controversy upon release of the DLC was quite unfounded in my opinion. It was quite clear to me (and anyone not absolutely blinded by their excitement) that the features teased were not all going to be available day one. And it seems that as features have been added this past year people have gotten over their convictions and are leaving more positive reviews. However, I will say that one issue I have is that the pre-release marketing did make it clear that people who purchased the DLC upon release would pay less for it than someone who purchased it after the originally-planned-to-be-one-year-long expansion pass was over. As the year progressed and updates were released, the cost of the DLC was said to increase. Obviously that model has changed a bit, but seeing the DLC listed now, one year and three updates later for not just the same base price ($20), but in fact cheaper with holiday sales ($14), than when I purchased it day one ($18, $20 base price with sale), is kind of insulting. I understand that most sales probably were within the first week of launch and the main point of this was to build capital for the studio moving forward but regardless they advertised that there would be benefits supporting the project early, specifically financial. Yet having the price still be the same (in fact cheaper) is a blatant false advertisement. On a similar note, the price of the DLC is the same as the base survival game, and with sales is about the same as the base game with story mode. While there is a good amount of content in the DLC and it is not yet finished, it still does not match the base game in terms of content. I hope that by the end of the DLC's development that the content included better reflects the price.

Nonetheless, I do think investing in this DLC has provided Hinterland with the opportunity for them to show their capacity for providing us with regular and substantial updates. The DLC has so far proven to be a decently large addition to the base game and I am hopeful that development for The Long Dark maintain an air of this focus going forward. I would like to see them maintain at least their previous yearly update schedule going forward for years to come. And I hope more people discover and enjoy The Long Dark and find my review at least helpful.\t\t\t\t\t
