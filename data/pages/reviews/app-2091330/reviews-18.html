\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the base game, to this day still one of my favorites and most played.
I was super excited for this DLC and the addition of a new map.

Unfortunately, this zone is not playable like the other zones. I have now tried 8 runs (10 if you include the 2 where I remade it to try and get a different spawn point that wasn't at the top of Mount Everest) on Custom Interloper (Passive Wildlife and Rifle availability)

The only spawn point is at the entrance to the zone (From Transfer Pass), the path down is super long with no buildings or containers. If you're lucky, by the time you reach the first building (a trailer) you are already at a third of your health just from freezing. Even tested one run with max starting gear setting, still froze almost immediately.

When I FINALLY managed to reach the airfield, I was greeted by buildings with apparently no fireplaces, no fire barrels (despite the dozens of barrels in the hanger), no furnaces, and no way to get warm inside if you don't have warm enough clothes to begin with. If you are lucky enough to not freeze to death before reaching the buildings, you will freeze IN the buildings.

I know they said the DLC zones would be more narrative/story zones, but with the zones being in survival mode and not story, I expected them to still have a chance at actually SURVIVING and playing in it like normal if you had started in other zones. Any other zone, no matter the difficulty, it is still feasible to survive and play in your starting zone; this is not the case for the Forsaken Airfield.
I am the type of player that chooses a zone to start in and I will hang in that zone until I've looted most (or all) of the resources and am established (Good gear/clothes) before venturing to the other zones. You CANNOT do that with this zone.

For all intents and purposes, this zone is NOT to be considered part of the normal survival zones. The only feasible way of surviving this zone is getting established in another zone and trekking over to Forsaken Airfield when you have good gear/food. i.e. Do not START your run here, it WILL fail, likely before you even reach that first trailer.

Again, I love the base game,. 100% recommend. However, this dlc is not fit/feasible for survival mode in the way it has been presented. I do not recommend it if you mainly play survival, especially if you are like me and prefer to centralize from the zone you want to play in as that is apparently not possible on this map.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoy this DLC. This is just the first part and the content we have is just a taste of what's to come.
Sure it was buggy, but I always expect game breaking bugs and many of them weren't even that bad.

The new regions are beautifully designed and also minimalistic which is equally beautiful. Forsaken Airfield is easily my favorite region. Many complain that it's too empty but I think that's the point. The empty and barren nature of the area is what makes it so great.

The new doe, improvised crampons and little features are good QOL additions. The new visor notes and mementos I haven't seen much of but I like the idea behind them. I just wish they were more varied and not static every time.

I haven't seen the new variant items yet but they look pretty nice. As for interloper players, the nature of interloper kinda prevents these items from spawning entirely. And interloper players would 100% be upset if rifles started spawning in their games. Although I'm sure they could make it work somehow.

I don't think this DLC is incomplete. It just feels that way to people who A) don't read and B) expect DLCs to be complete upon release. There are 5 more updates coming to this DLC. Just like how there have been many updates to survival mode AFTER the game left early access, there will be more updates to TFTFT after it releases. So I don't think it's fair to call it Early Access DLC. Since not having all the content on release is the point. Plus multiple updates will keep players invested over time rather than just once at release.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Long Dark is my favorite game ever by one of the best studios out there. Tales from the Far Territory gives the game new life, and I'm excited to see the story and sandbox continue to unfold as I force my sad, freezing survivor into ever more bleak circumstances! They're always moaning about how they can't feel their toes/can you eat trees I'm so hungry/please bandage my sprained ankle/I'm bleeding to death/and on and on. And I'm like: did we die yesterday? No. Did we die the week before? No. Month? No! Starving all day is fine. We have a jar of maple syrup we can guzzle before sleeping in some cave. Life is all about your attitude! And my attitude is positive! I say Yes to wrestling wolves naked with nothing but a hatchet and subsisting on shelf-fungus!

Having hiked out to the new Airfield territory and explored, some of the new mechanics are FUN. They make old reliable tactics defunct. I'm excited to have to think on my feet again!

For the concerns mentioned about loss of saved games... start a new run! I feel a certain peace that my 500-day survivor I started at the beginning of the Pandemic has moved on to a digital life somewhere else. She is finally at peace and at rest, with some soup.

UPDATE: With more play time in the "Tales from the Far Territory" maps, still a recommend! Some reviewers felt that the new maps are a big let down, with critiques being that the Airfield map is mostly just gigantic but lacking content. I can appreciate that, because it is the largest map and isn't as content-dense as the other Long Dark maps. However, it's an interesting challenge in itself and actually contains plenty of resources and locations once you get to know it. Another complaint was that you can't survive if starting on such a barren map, but that hasn't been my experience. It isn't barren, you just need to know what to look for. I appreciate the feeling of huge open spaces, although I hope that future Tales maps will return to the more dense design of the original maps. That does seem to work better for the game, although, again, the Airfield isn't broken at all, it's just different.

I also have enjoyed some of the houses and buildings that don't do a loading screen, with the transfer to a fullly stocked indoors being part of the main map - however, I do think that this design seems to give the sound system some challenges and possibly may contribute to bugs in the audio, which I hope get fixed. The Long Dark sound design is so good, and is such a core part of the whole experience that glitches in even the wind noise can really take away from the experience.

The new content that I've experienced in Part 2 (Signal Void) has been fun! When I encountered and figured out the tracking mechanic, it was a very cool experience, very thematic and very atmospheric. It gave me a reason to venture out into the open at night during an aurora, which I typically haven't had to do much, usually opting to bunker down and ensure survival. I like these new mechanics that shake up our survival habits or reward a more risky, active approach.

Overall, still very excited to see where else in the Far Range the team takes the game!\t\t\t\t\t

Value for Money?

As a long time fan of this game I am really excited about the addition of new features and new regions. I have just clocked over 3000 hrs in this game, a milestone I reached while playing in the new regions (Far Range Branch Line and Transfer Pass and the first major new addition Forsaken Airfield.)

I usually play on Interloper setting, but not wanting to suffer too much, I went in on Stalker setting, which would give me a good balance of loot spawns and some challenge while I explore and map Forsaken Airfield.
After about 25 hrs exploration I'd say the region is fantastic! I love the modelling of the the choppers and planes, and the buildings are really cool. There are so many electronic meters and monitors which come to life in an aurora and in the glimmer fog and the control tower gives a really neat view over the airfield when the weather is clear.
The new music is so atmospheric and the weather cycles create such an immersive feeling which is primarily what I play this game for.
The region is quite open and huge (it is an airfield after all) and this presents a challenge when navigating in blizzards and fog and glimmer fog, but there are refuges scattered about especially if as a seasoned player you're prepared and carry a heap of fuel wherever you go. A downed chopper for instance, can make a really nice bed with a protected fire burning throughout the night. As you explore further you realise actually there is a lot of contour and there are plenty of hidden areas and landmarks to navigate by.

So now for the elephant in the room. There were numerous bugs on release and players that were hyped for new things got a bit disappointed, but fixes have already been released for the more serious problems and I trust Hinterland to keep providing fixes and improvements. This is a team that really cares about the player's experience and their track record is stellar. I'm still encountering minor bugs, a floating book, a safe dial with no numbers, wonky hit boxes on some items, but these things are pretty insignificant given the scale of the update. Another issue players are having is not knowing what changes or additional features to expect and when, but for me this is adding to the mystery and voyage of discovery of the game.

Does the DLC provide value for money? Well, to me it definitely does. More value than the money I spent, much more. I recognize that different people see value in different things though and I'd say that if you're a thoughtful gamer and already a fan of the Long Dark you'll most likely find the DLC worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
