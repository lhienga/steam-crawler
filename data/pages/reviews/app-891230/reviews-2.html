\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought the vintage pack along with all the other packs in order to support Frontier in continued development of my all-time favorite game.

This pack seems a little weaker than the rest. It does feature 5 flat rides and two coasters but I'm not sure how useful and universal they will be across many different parks. It can be frustrating looking in my ride list and having to mentally block out unused rides which leaves me with the same ones as usual. If a mini cable car ride had been included it could be added to any of my parks but an old vintage fairground ride will mostly look out of place unless you are building a beachfront pier.

The scenery items are modest with a few items that I will keep coming back to but unfortunately I feel that I could have done almost just as well without this dlc.

I will still purchase the next dlc that Frontier come up with but I hope that they feature a more useful ride set.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I bought the base-game during the Summer Sale, quickly got hooked, and as a way of showing support, I bought the rest of the DLC a few days later, never noticing the message about the upcoming DLC released yesterday. I have spent the better part of the last two weeks creating my own park, and I'm still working on it. The theme of the entrance and initial park area was loosely based on Main St in Disney World, but I was/am creating a mood that was a hybrid between that, the World Dair expos in turn-of-the-century America, and Victorian-era England. Mind you, I didn't set out to do this, but that's what I was doing according to this latest DLC package, I suppose. Except I was painstakingly creating and adapting the blueprints I had from the game, as well as the countless items the workshop creators have posted :)

Anyway, this DLC is another great extension of the base-game, for those like myself that have truly been hooked by the creation-element of the game. If you just want to play a management simulation, you really don't NEED any of the DLC packages, as you can get many/most of what the DLCs offer through workshop-content (and please try to remember to give those folks a thumbs-up, at the very least, for sharing their work!).

Here's my only gripe with the game and all of the DLC packages so far -- are you reading this Frontier Developments -- where are the additional wall-mounted lights to suit this style/era? There are new Victorian-era STREET lamps (two different styles, actually) but still no lights pre-created in this style/era that will snap to a wall automatically. Please address this critical oversight immediately :P

Otherwise, job well done!\t\t\t\t\t
