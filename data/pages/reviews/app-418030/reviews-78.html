\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not a bad game, nor is it an experience that is not fun, but it has some serious flaws, most that I would say are, if not game breaking, then certainly either unfair or unfinished. The best part about that, however, is that this game is in Alpha! Which means a lot of these issues and more may be addressed sometime.

Issue 1: Hostile animal movement. These are part of the game, you should have very little problem with them overall. As long as you sneak around, grab what you need, and don't get in their way, no issues. The issues I have with them comes from the fact that they corner you 60% of the time if there's more than one. They will randomly both walk towards you, skipping the phase where they lift their heads to search and begin stalking you. This usually isn't lethal, as you're faster than both wolves and bears. However, if you're just trying to get home, or to your dead body, or to safety from something else chasing you, or you have no stamina, you're screwed.

Issue 2: Hostile animal population / spawn rates. Hostile animals are virtually everywhere. According to some quick research, The average black bear density is about 1-4 every 10 squared kilometres. The map of subsistence is not large, maybe somewhere short of 10 squared kilometres, There are bears and wolves dotting every hill, plane, beach, cliffside, forest and meadow. And they spawn back nearly every night. Your base, if built too near a bear spawn, will basically be smacked every time you step outside. Not to mention, getting around these animals is damn near impossible, if you want to collect loot, which brings me to point three:

Issue 3: Looting, and wandering. Looting feels good! There's just enough loot that you feel like you always need more, but you get enough to slowly accumulate. Looting is 90% of the game. This gets old very quickly. Fishing can help you survive, but ultimately, you'll need bait, which requires cordage, which requires plants, and grubs, which are found from chopping the biggest trees. You could go mining, I guess, but you're still doing the same thing, and ultimately, your inventory won't fill up if you bring a pick and chip away at all the ores you find while looking for crates. Hunting? Great. You're still looking around randomly for stuff, and liable to find loot. My issue comes from the fact that it's so random what you may or may not find. You really can't hone in on WHAT you want to find. Nails are a mid game crafting recipe, and you need to find them otherwise. This is being fixed, thankfully! Nails were once much harder to find, now they come in more than stacks of one. Still, the main gameplay is waiting for animals to maybe move to maybe get something you need to advance. and after that, when you eventually amass all the resources you need, you're stuck waiting for things like power and mass to accumulate, especially power. The core gameplay loop is still fun, but it's the same every time.

Issue 4: Aiming down sights. Sensitivity does not change when you aim down sights. The iron sights are horrid. Tiny, inaccurate, and they bob significantly unless standing still, which is a death sentence for anyone getting into a fire fight with a hunter. Having to put a pin on a pixel, all while any slight mouse movement throws your aim to the other side of the screen is not fun. Body shots are laughably ineffective, and headshots are instant kill for any unarmored human target, plus, ammo is hard to make. This all culminates in a nearly impossible to win firefight, for a very low amount of materials, but ones that are needed for making proper weapons, and can't be found elsewhere or crafted.

Issue 5: Hunter base locations and hunters. So, combine the above issue with the fact that hunters build bases surrounding you, which can't be removed, and they hunt you down over a massive distance once you're noticed. They also tend to have very good accuracy, near perfect in some cases, but usually either one or more of their shots will land. They can spawn in groups, and they respawn every night it seems. Even if you do manage to kill them off and want to claim your spoils, you'll be stuck breaking down the door, which takes a few axes, and 5-10 minutes. The loot is not worth it, and making a breaching charge is just throwing away resources. They have unlimited grenades and ammo, and break your structures like a drunk teenager drop kicking a sand castle. You can barely dent their door with an entire axe durability, and it is never worth it. What the honest . Not to mention attacks, but at least then You're on your own turf.

This is a good game, don't get me wrong, but these flaws suck, and make it hard to like. Hopefully, things change, or become better, and they just might, keep your eye on this game if you want to try it out, but be prepared for a lot of frustration, and be expected to have patience above all.\t\t\t\t\t
