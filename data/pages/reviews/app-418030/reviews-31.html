\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame has a good idea, but poor execution. Early game is boring and repetitive. Spend hours looking for boxes with RNG loot and little plants to build a house. No primitive crafting except for a campfire. Want a weapon? You can craft a bow or a modern rifle, nothing in between. No POIs of any kind, which would be okay if there was a sense of diversity to the flora/fauna but there isn't. Predator AI is horrible, you'll spend a lot of time dying to just jump into your house because 4 bears are walking in circles around the front of your base for no reason at all other than their pathing is stuck. Building system seems to have promise, but I honestly didn't even get far into it because the artificial grind loop was boring as hell.

The game isn't difficult as much as it is unbalanced. The resource grind is arranged pretty poorly. You need 3 wood logs to make ONE plank, and recipes require a LOT of planks. Each tree will give you roughly 3-6 logs. Add to this the fiber grind. Two pieces of fiber for one piece of cordage that is required in high amounts for every crafting recipe. The fiber spawns in as little branchy plants around the map, and while they're relatively common they're not common enough for every recipe to require you to pick roughly 20 of these things. But okay, you've fought through that nonsense and finally crafted a bow. Have fun hunting only chickens with it because feathers don't drop enough in boxes and bows only work viably against chickens and rabbits unless you're going to cheese the AI. Already you've caught yourself in 3 artificially difficult grind loops and you haven't even begun thinking about food.

You can sometimes find a single carrot or a single blueberry sitting around, but until you make a planter box (have fun farming for that) you'll be living off of kelp long enough to die inside of your base everyday. In between chasing chickens, chopping trees, hunting RNG boxes, hunting rare branches, you'll also find yourself almost drowning yourself looking for one piece of kelp. There are no other food sources besides these options and hunting. Given that it takes several rounds from a gun to kill a single wolf, hunting is not viable until late game unless you plan on cheesing the AI to get it done.

Usually all of these things would be relatively forgiveable in an early access game, but its been early access for 6 years and the devs seem to be more focused on adding ATVs and useless things rather than shaping up the base game with much needed QOL updates. That tells me that they consider themselves finished with the base game, and with that it's just a poorly made game.

I'm a big fan of hardcore survival games, and this ain't it. The game isn't "hardcore" its unbalanced, unnecessarily grindy, and artificially difficult. I only spent $10 on it and still don't really think it was worth it. If you do decide to buy it, beware that you only have 2 hours of playtime to refund it and 2 hours of playtime in this game doesn't do much, so probably just don't buy it unless devs do the needed and overdue work\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs i'm approaching 2000 hours of game play i thought it about time i wrote a realistic review of this game.
Subsistence starts you off with an axe and, depending on the difficulty level, maybe a water canteen, a pistol and a bandage. You must carefully move around collecting plants, chopping trees and finding loot crates which are randomly scattered around the wilderness whilst avoiding wolves, bears and later, cougars.
The weather changes and so do the seasons bringing new challenges as you progress. I've never played on 'easy' mode so i can't comment on that, i normally play on 'normal' mode and have tried 'hardcore' mode which is more like perma-death !
Get too close to a predator and they will growl or grunt at you as a warning, ignore the warning and they will start towards you, ignore their advance and they will chase you down - tread carefully !
I love this game and whilst the developer has made some questionable decisions during the development, the game continues to be a lot of fun with (i hope) a long way to go in it's development with regular three monthly updates.
I highly recommend Subsistence and have hundreds of hours of game play on my YouTube channel so if you are hesitating on purchase, go ahead and watch some.\t\t\t\t\t
