\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI give it a thumbs up but with a huge caution. I just don't understand the thinking behind this game. I picked it up on sale so no biggie but wow, I would have thought it would have way more in the game by now. The updates come VERY slowly.

It's a survival game with very little purpose. It's punishing in the silliest of manners and it makes the game more annoying than challenging. First off, you basically feel like you've been tossed over the fence into a nature reserve filled with predators and you are their fresh meat. Every direction you walk you will run straight into another predator just waiting for you like mobs in an MMO. No matter what direction you head you will run into another bear, wolf, cougar etc. Watch out for the boars, they will poison you somehow.

You get tossed into this world with little help and only a very sparse tutorial. You will get hungry and thirsty fast and if you don't immediately start preparing you will die due to the cold the first evening you are even in the game. There are no POI's such as houses, buildings etc. It is just an open world in which magical loot duffle bags and boxes get sprinkled around the world each morning like magic. It seems like pretty lazy development to go this route as opposed to providing interesting POI's to loot. It kind of makes exploring a chore as you are only hunting for boxes and all of the world looks the same. Inside these duffle bags are the resources you need to survive like building resources or ammo. Unfortunately you will find like one or two bullets in these bags and you need at least 5 to kill the most basic predator. You can mine rock and you can go into underwater caves but that will be much later in the game. Now you get to fight bats! You can hunt and fish which is fine.

Once you build your first little base during the tutorial you will now have enemy AI called "hunters", who will spawn into the world build their own base and eventually attack you. This is one of the better parts of the game except there are a grand total of TWO different models of AI which is pretty lame after all of these years of development. There is an ATV in the game now but I don't really know the purpose as there is nothing on the far side of the map that you can't get on the other side of the map. It's all the same loot sprinkled around the map of trees, rocks and water.

So what do you do in the game? You die. You die a lot and you get frustrated until you eventually get enough resources to stay alive. If you get poisoned, you will stay poisoned even after you die and respawn unless you finally get all the resources to make an antidote. You will respawn far away from your body and you will run into a whole string of predators on your way back, if you can even see because it gets dark quick. Then you will get cold again, even in summer, get eaten by a predator again or die of hunger or thirst and that's without having to deal with the AI eventually who are perfect shots.

I really hope the dev comes up with a better game loop because some of the mechanics are nice. The world itself looks nice and the game runs smooth. But holy crap it's annoying to play and I'm having a hard time even figuring out why I want to play as there doesn't seem to be much to the game loop. This needs far more to do in the game than just survive for the sake of surviving. It seems very similar to the Infected except at least the infected has some towns to loot. I love survival games, but I'm having trouble finding the point to this game.

Oh, and the game has a tendency to crash.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLiterally the hardest survival game I have ever played.
That is not saying it is Bad, because it is not, I have enjoyed my time playing this game.
It Can be fairly heavy on the resource grind but adds to the adventure, having you going everywhere looking for fibre and nails.
The game can get fairly frustrating with deaths, a wolf or bear will stay around your corpse making it difficult to get your gear back, however, some clever kiting will have you back at your gear and on your way in no time.
The season systems are pretty darn good and add an extra layer of difficulty(not that it needs it...lol)
Overall, I would, and do suggest this game to anyone who likes hardcore survival games\t\t\t\t\t
