\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, the Positive.

The game does deliver a survivalist feel as you play, especially if you are alone. You might even start thinking of writing some kind of weird Kaczynski style manifesto at night. The game can be a challenge, even on easy mode. Fishing and Hunting is kind of fun and if you enjoy gathering style games, you'll basically like this one, especially for the price.

The Negative.

The game really needs some balance to the wildlife spawn as it becomes quite difficult to gather certain mats to move the game forward without feeling like you're on a constant repeat cycle. It is quite grindy, so if you aren't into that you should pass. You can also wind up doing a large amount of very repetitive daily chores just to keep things going. The development of this game is also quite slow, which is understandable as this is a single dev working this game to fruition, so don't expect it to be finished any time soon. to me, it just feels like something is missing, although I can't quite put my finger on it. I've tried playing co-op and solo, and it takes a lot of time to meet your partner in crime in a co-op game.

While this game is very nice looking, my thumbs down is based primarily on it's playability/fun factor at this point.

Here are my recommendations to improve the game:

1) Balance wildlife spawn.
2) Increase animal husbandry options.
3) Add more tool upgrades, things that improve your ability to catch larger fish, etc...
4) Make the presence of bees less of a rarity and install beekeeping that could give your farming a boost.
5) When it rains, allow rainwater to soak up your soil.
6) Additional building skin options.
7) Put in more food sources, even if they don't provide a great deal of nutrition (see what the TV series Alone)
8) Provide an option in Co-op play to spawn in together at the same spot

I'll keep an eye on the updates and may change this review to a positive, since the price is quite good and I think it is almost there.\t\t\t\t\t
