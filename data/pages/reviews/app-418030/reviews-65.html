\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot for the feinthearted!
Note: I've played a few more hours, and am yet to get to fully explore all the expansion material since my last play, but thought it all worth and updated review.

The game is still a challenge, but it's visuals are beautiful, using the Unreal engine. Some of the animations are a little stiff, but not immersion breaking... There is a wonder here that keeps bring me back...

Here's a mixture of game mechanics, my observations and even tips:
- Get used to dying - a lot! Animals will tear you a new one (death animations are included) and hunters are able to headshot. Dying means loosing some item durability and some random items in your backpack. A small setback but part of the 'punishment' - so keep it to a minimum.
- Re-spawning in a random area of the map can present challenges, but the plus side is during the runs back to my backpack I am able to gather loot crates, bags, plants, etc.
- Base building is fun. A few quirky things, but nothing game breaking. Suspending a base off the side of a steep cliff can prove useful as it limits animal attacks and hunter damage.
- If the hunters slow down progress with consistent attacks, you can turn them off in the options and re-enable them once you have a better base ;-)
- Some materials require you scavenge to find them (like cotton seeds), others you need to kill hunters to get and some you can grow, mine, farm and manufacture. This means you can't hole up in your base if you want to progress. You will have to face scary beasts and hunters eventually... but its part of the experience!
- Great Day/night cycles with changing weather to mimic a full year of seasons. The seasons will change the landscape (snow!) and what plants are available. It also changes animal behaviour, like moose becoming aggressors!

All round a great experience - get it! \t\t\t\t\t
