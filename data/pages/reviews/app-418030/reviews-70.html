\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBig fan of survival games, and like all survival games this one comes with it's setbacks, but it seems to have more setbacks than good. Because of my love for the survival genre, I had quite a few people suggest this game to me. I skipped reading the reviews simply because it seems folks don't normally grasp what a survival game is supposed to be and will complain about things that don't truly matter. After reading a lot of the reviews though, I can see where most of them were coming from. It is disappointing to see that the dev left his full time job to work on this in 2018 and it is still where it is. A game that came out long after 7 Days To Die, yet lacks a lot of the basic fundamentals that a survival game should have. You have things such as a refinery and stoves, even air conditioning.... But you can't craft a throwing spear, or basic arrows just out of some cordage, sharpened sticks, and feathers. Coming across resources are difficult. Much of what you need can't be crafted, and when you do come across it you won't get or find much. The crafting recipes for much of the items are also wayyyyyyyy out of whack. I have played Subnautica, 7DTD, Green Hell, etc., and there was reward to the grinding. It is as though the balance between realism and a video game are constantly tossed around with some things being overly realistic, and somethings being so far fetched it makes no sense. Like having the stuff to build an air conditioner yet needing nails to make an arrow?? The crafting needs major refinement. The animal spawning is absurd. So many predators yet they wonder around and co-exist. Hunters come to attack your base and the Grisly bear out front just ignores them. The quest for nails is never ending it seems, when before there were metal nails, it was common to use wooden pegs to keep things together. Why can't we have that and then evolve to something sturdier built with the nails?

I understand that this game is Early Access, and it is admirable that it looks a lot better than 7DTD when they have a whole group of folks working on that game and it looks like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ still. Not to mention the mess with Tell Tale. But expecting folks to be tolerable with one Dev after so long with the amount of people who have bought the game you question what the money went towards if it didn't go towards hiring help to craft this game into the diamond it should be by now.

As for right now, I can't honestly suggest this game to anyone. There were quite a few times my buddies and I even thought about quitting and not coming back until we saw something major in an update. The endless grind to still be questing for nails, or ammo, or cotton seeds, it gets to be too much. Then when you finally get the items you need you go to craft what you wanted all along to find you need something that is even more rare. All the while the hunters have randomly everything you can't get. I also don't understand how you can loot the hunters and they have a bunch of random things, most of the time not useful, but you can't take weapons or clothing? What is the point of having them? At this point you just avoid them as there is no purpose for them. They don't help you, they make you waste resources repairing the damage they cause to your base. But there is no reward for engaging them.

The power and mass system is also a mess. You work hard to have the power and mass to craft these items so you have them at your base, just to find you need to grind even more for more mass and power to craft things within those items.

If you love grinding with little to no reward, then this game is for you. Be warned though, this game still has a very long way to go before it will reach the level of being a true survival game. The only thing at the moment that makes it a survival game is you are lucky if you even survive an hour playing it without wanting to un-install and get a refund. I really hope we see great things come from this title. Strangely enough I knew nothing about it until a week ago someone mentioned it. A month ago while playing Green Hell my friend and I were talking about a game like this and how it would completely turn the market for Survival games upside down if done right. This game just doesn't hit that mark quite yet. We can only hope that changes in time.

There are many bugs that need fixed. I hope it happens soon!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComplete (and final) Review Edit:

So I did some thinking as to how one could summarize this game up. At first I said it was realistic minecraft .....now? I would say this is equivalent to a "dark souls" of open world survival sandboxes. Not to mention this game is produced by a single person. Let me explain the pros n cons. This is coming from a HC mode player as well.

Cons:
- A tad bit outdated. Game came out in 2016 if he bumped up to unreal 5 (currently 4) I'd say it would look superb compared to now. Don't get me wrong I still think it looks good for now, but could use a lil razzle dazzle. Yes character/animal modules are kinda god awful (because once again outdated for a 2016 game)

- Resource grinding IS tedious. Then again this is just a literal bare bone pve setup. Now, there are people who complain "You get X amount of X from boxes, trees, etc." 9/10 times those are players playing easy/normal and cry about it. If they played HC mode they'd come to a realization that their comments are laughable. These players expect to be handed the goods without even realizing the literal name of the game (no seriously look up the definitive name of the game, it's self explanatory). Those players also did not evolve in the game as well. Remember I play this in HC mode and I do just fine. Why? Because I'm a minimalist, and learned the mechanics of "where to go for X" etc. People also complained about finding certain nodes, particularly fibers. At first, I as well, did complain cause it was slightly hard to "see them" especially in spring/summer times. I eventually just disregarded it cause I developed.....wait for it......"fiber optics" :troll face: Meaning I just got used to it and knew how to look for them catching my eyes.

- Maintaining your survival bars. People complain that they gotta eat/drink to sustain themselves constantly but that's kind of the literal point of the game right now. It's really not hard if you simply take time to evaluate the game data as you progress. I.e. at the start you're mostly eating chickens but by end game you're eating roasted ribs (some sort of "meal") which 1 would do you for a while vs 4 chickens.

- Building. This one is a toss up for most. Like I said in resources, yes it does take time to grind logs but you don't really need much. I usually have a 1x1 with everything I need in HC mode until I gathered enough to start building a decent base against hunters (will get into that in a second). Thing is, that's once again "the point of the game." If you wanna build some big badass base with little consequence then go play Rust.

- Weaponry. A lot complain "well theres no point in any gun other than shot gun." That statement is 50/50. If you play HC mode you learn ammo conservation as well as how many hits it takes to kill a wolf/bear/hunter etc. with each weapon, utilizing your hunting. I have my own personal tricks for farming bears easily without even a bow but that's just me. I actually played the game and learned. What these players also don't realize is there's an upgrade bench later on to strengthen each weapon (spoilers you can make a "better" bow as well).

Pros:
- For the price you pay you get your monies worth. For those that say "this game isn't even worth blah blah blah." Ya y'all can just shut it right there. Do you know how many poly games are coming out now for the same price if not more? That's right too damn many. Yet those people (with no taste) who belittle games like this will praise THOSE abominations to no end.

- This game does an overall great transition into the 4 seasons. I live in an area relatively similar to this game and the sounds and sight are fairly similar (mostly sounds). I hate winter but the blizzard effect is really fun if you go out in it. Also you can set the yearly timer which is very nice. I.e. if you wanted to play a true day by day for a full year you can do that (not like actual time in life but in the games time). Or, you can set it to 60 days per year etc and rush the seasons to your desire.

- Straight up PVE. So far there really isn't a "point of interest" or "activity compound" in a manner of speaking. This is just raw you vs nature and hunters. Hunters are tough and I do mean tough. Whilst the aiming in the game isn't technically "all there" it's just something you basically "git gud" at. Oh ya forgot to mention if anyone cries about the hunters they more than likely haven't played HC mode being raided by 3 hunter groups. Easy mode has 3 hunters per group, HC has 4 per group. For those that don't know, they are pretty accurate and you have to highly strategically take them out. Sometimes I'll defend and take 2 shots to the head and be at 10 hp pull back and heal back up fully while waiting for their reload sound to engage. Also if you die to animals they level up (which is actually a good thing if you know you know, no more spoilers). Many would say (that I've noticed a trend in the complaints) "This is so dumb why would a lvl 1 wolf be lvl 2 and every time I try to get my loot back I keep dying and now it's lvl 5!!!!!" No sirs/madams that is not dumb. You are just dumb for running back with nothing to begin with or had a backup plan. People also complain "well ammo is hard to make etc." Lulz ya that goes under the category of "git gud" because in 30 min of HC game play I can have a stack of arrows no prob. How? Because I know what to look for and how to as well. Speaking of dying, this game does punish you for it well. So if I had a stack of rifle ammo (50 bullets) and I die, I loot my body for half value. For those that know that's painful if you don't have a mining setup for infinite supply. From the start of this I mentioned this is a "dark souls" of open world survival for THIS specific reason.

- Building. Yes it's tedious but only if you want some sort of mega base as a soloist. In reality you can make a small base and survive just fine on normal/easy mode. Once you get into HC mode you have to get fairly sturdy (if solo) which means lots of farming. But that's all you'll practically do in this game since there's no objective or POI. You build to survive it all and thrive. Aesthetics are fairly nice. Also your base doesn't need upkeep which is lovely if you do invest into a mega base.

My only personal complaint is that hunters on HC mode farm out a rifle within like 2 days of their spawn. Kinda wish they would start out with bows and progress a tad bit slower. I'm fine with normal mode hunters having a pistol (as you yourself start out with one which makes sense). But dang man, they farm quick on HC mode so you have to turn them off temporarily so you yourself can put up a BCU and start functioning equal to their 3-5 day start which took you 1/2 months in the game to farm.

All in all, solid 8.5/10 for an EA solo dev and enjoy watching each new update for me to hop on.\t\t\t\t\t
