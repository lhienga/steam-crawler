\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is... so hard to explain why its fun.
For all basic resources, you must walk around the map and find an assortment of small boxes that can hold different types of items like healing supplies, bullets, crafting supplies, ETC. The thing this game does best, is make it so you have to do this for hours and hours to get anywhere or build anything. When your starting out and have absolutely nothing, just getting food to keep running so you dont have to walk around is insanely hard. you need water for thirst, but you have to cook it to clean it first or there is side affects, you need 2 types of foods, protein (mostly meats) and veggy/fruits. The latter is mostly easy to come by, the meats are not. with how much running around you do and how long it takes to gain supplies to build anything useful, getting meats is nearly impossible. Wolves, Cougars, Moose, Bears, everything takes many hits with the axe you get at the start, and can kill you in 2-3 hits while also breaking your legs (which makes you walk like a snail) and can make you bleed, both of which you need to FIND MATERIALS TO FIX So its a lot of downhill struggle. Once you can manage to start building a base, you pray to RNGesus that you get the electronics you need as well as finding moose or deer so you can craft higher tier materials and then craft things that are useful like solar energy panels to make sure you can even turn anything on in your house (like refrigerator, mass creator, lights, ETC). Okay, so you've gotten this far eh? now lets sprinkle in some janky AI. animals if they get too close to you can get stuck in this faster attack status where they hit like 3x faster than they are supposed to (not sure if thats actually supposed to happen) AI hitboxes are a bit strange, for example they can hit u further than u can hit them. But what about guns you say?!? i will tell you this now, if you use a gun to kill everything, your ammo will dwindle so fast you might as well use it as a blunt rock and hit things. Which leaves the bow. The bow is a bit janky, the flight of the arrows is very strange (as in not very realistic, doesnt go very far before dropping off massively), but u can sometimes recover your arrows shot which can save you on materials, but finding feathers to create these arrows you have two options. find some in boxes around the map (not very common but can occasionally find a few), or chase chickens around like a mad man. chasing chickens can give you protein as well which can be nice, until you realise you just chased this chicken around using up all your stamina, dug into your protein meter so your stam doesnt refill as fast, and you basically break even after you farm the resources to start a fire to cook the darn thing, oh have i mentioned you now have 2 feathers! woohoo!

Without going on and detailing every aspect, these are just a FEW of the inconveniences/struggles you will have starting out, and it does sort of get better going on, but expect the long grind. If you are someone who does actually enjoy hard games to play, this is for sure difficult, but its more about time. If you can sit down for multiples of hours at a time, then you will be able to progess, if your a person who has other business to handle on the regular (kids, work and have limited time to play) this game may be nearly impossible to play, at least solo.

All this being said.. it is a strangely fun game. Now to each their own and your experience may vary, but the grind suck so bad... then feels MASSIVELY rewarding when you accomplish the simplist goals. YES! IVE FINALLY CRAFTED THAT SOLAR PANEL AND GAIN .2 ENERGY ON SUNNY DAYS! The combat and ai might be a bit janky, but when you can kill enemies and storm their base (oh my god takes so ******* long to break in....) you feel accomplished and may even gain some sweet plunder to make the booboo's go away. As far as i know, this is either a single person developer, or a very small team that works on this game, and for what it is, its really not too bad. is it worth more than 10$? ehhhh.... id hard cap that at 10$ or less.

Going forward though.. this game SEVERELY needs to up their game on who the target audiences are. The average players for this game are not many, and looking at the hosted servers numbers, thats clear as day. This game is for a sort of hardcore survival enthusiast (masochist). Being able to add things in to vary the type of game experience you can have is definately worth while and VERY successful. Just by being able to adjust things to even a slight extent would give everyone a chance to have great fun with this game. Putting in sliders from 1-3x for things such as Materials, Slower decay of food/water, as additions to the already easy/normal type settings would be a game changer. for those who like the difficulty and have the time, play the game normally as it was intended, for those who like the game but absolutely cannot get into the grindfest it is, poke up a few settings to make the game fun for you as well. There is absolutely ZERO harm in putting these types of things in. Your characters inventory doesnt transfer with you to other servers, theres no skill or exp to be gained, so it wont affect anything outside of the solo or coop game you choose to play yourself, or set up online for others to join. And to all of the people whos reply to anyone asking for more expansion into the settings such as these saying, "well then dont play scrub! ha peasant! too hard for you? boohoo!" how bout you slow your roll you mushy brained toddlers and focus on playing the game how YOU like it, and leave others to do the same?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSubsistence is a Solo-Indy game. Made as a pet project it has evolved into something actually substantial. The game's price should be an indicator of what you're walking into, but this is probably one of the highest levels of professionalism you'll find in this development genre.

The game is based but not obsessed with realism, meaning gameplay is about realistic aspects but not cemented down by them, allowing for fun to take precedence. The AI hunters are a bit clunky with their own meta systems, but you're not going to find better in AAA titles and you can see how the dev changes them in the patch notes.

But is the game fun?! Why yes it is. It's a great little time waster with a ton to do. If you're looking at this game you probably want something DIFFERENT, something NEW. At this price tag you can surely get something new to stimulate your enjoyment for whatever amount of time you find the game compelling. It's a great game, better to some more than others, but for what it is and what it's worth, it exceeds it's category substantially.

You don't have to like the game, but me? I like it, I like it a lot, and it hasn't disappointed me.\t\t\t\t\t
