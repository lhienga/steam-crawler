\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game. The rifle in the game is modeled after an SVT-40, which is a really cool soviet rifle that you don't see too often. I haven't played Tarkov yet, so I don't know if it's in that one. Anyway, there are too many animals and the wood chopping sound is just painful. It goes Chop...chop...chop-kashoo-chop...chop...chop-kashoo every time and it's annoying. The visuals are not bad and the crafting system is ok, but there aren't enough UI details to make it a smooth experience. Others have already mentioned the lack of being able to mark the map. I couldn't find any way to sort inventory. I wouldn't mind the difficulty level if there were more intuitive control schemes overall. I will check it out again after it leaves early access.

...I also wanted to mention that it's odd the developer went to the trouble of modeling a Beretta M9 and actually calling it an M9 rather than just a "pistol" like they did in 7 days to die, however it only holds 7 rounds in the game. A real M9 holds at least 15 rounds. Heck, even a California or NY compliant magazine would hold 10. I played for long enough to find the materials to craft a few more rounds of ammo before daylight faded and I realized I just didn't want to play any more. It does have potential, though.\t\t\t\t\t
