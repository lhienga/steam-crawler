\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a wonderful game on so many levels. Survival remains challenging at all points during the game, even when you have become well established in the world. This is in stark contrast to other survival games where the early game is hard but the difficulty tapers off with time and thus one's ability to remain engaged with the world diminishes. Subsistence keeps you captivated from day 1 to the end game, a tremendous feat in the genre.

The pace of the game is decidedly slow, allowing the player to appreciate the small details, which once again places emphasis on surviving. Farming is not an automated process of plant, wait and collect like in Minecraft; in Subsistence, one has to craft a plant bed, place said bed in sunlight, plant the seeds, add water and fertiliser. Nighttime is extremely dark, as in real life and creates the perfect environment for one to enjoy the beautiful lighting from the Unreal Engine, as the light from a campfire or base light penetrates the surrounding darkness.

There are a few problems with the game that immediately come to mind, that being the overabundance of hostile wildlife, leading to the player having to crawl around to reach resources, a bit tedious to say the least. Furthermore, the mobs can't actually hit you if you keep running, they will never catch you and this takes some of the excitement out of the game.

Overall, this is an exceptional game, a true demonstration of indie creativity. The small-scope of Subsistence leads one to appreciate our daily comforts, as building something as simple as a baking tray is a serious endeavour in the game, while in the modern world, it is worth an hour of minimum wage labour.

Well done ColdGames!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game claims to be hardcore, however it is simply grindy. Basic materials like fiber is a pain in the ass to find. Body temperature system is very barebone. Nutrition system is barebone. Hunting feels weird and trapping animals is purely on luck. Domesticating and breeding animals makes no sense.

This game tries to incorporate so many ideas but providing a very simple version of it, thus making them annoyances instead of challenges. Other survival games at least shine in one aspect of it.

Looking for a detailed nutritional system and better body temperature system? Go play SCUM.
Looking for better gunplay and grinding curve? Go play RUST.
Still looking for better gunplay and a harsher world that making more sense? Go play DayZ.
Looking for proper taming and breeding system? Go play ARK (and Dinos & mystical creatures!)
Looking for better and more lively world and NPC interaction? Go play Conan Exile (and thrall system)
Looking for base-building and defending them from enemies? Go play 7 Days to Die.
Looking for better base-building and overall better looking worlds? Go play Satisfactory (and the automation aspect of it is awesome)
Looking for better generally a better PvE progression and an overall great survival sanbox? Go play Valheim. (Play Valheim nonetheless, it simply a great survival sandbox)

So so many survival sandbox are better than this. You may not like some aspect of the games I mentioned above, but you have to admit they have at least ONE thing that makes it different. Meanwhile, this game sucks at almost evereything.


TL;DR: Save your money and buy Valheim.\t\t\t\t\t
