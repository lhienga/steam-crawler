\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is currently my favorite game. It's all I've been playing for the past couple of months.

But it did not start out that way. When I think back to my first day, those first few hours, it reminds me of most of the negative reviews here. There are too many predators, my gawd, there are 50 wolves per acre! I can't find any fiber! There is no food and I don't know how to get any, I'm starving again! I't raining and I'm cold! This game is impossible!

Only, none of those things are actually true. There are not that many predators and they are very easy to avoid. There's fiber everywhere and there's food everywhere, and you don't need to stand out in freezing rain.

There is a learning curve to the game. You have to just learn the basic game and then it will be nothing but fun.

Here's my suggestions to everyone who thinks it's not worth sticking around for a few hours, even though you've spent your first few hours dying, again and again.

First of all, learn to avoid predators. Just stay away from them early game. It's not hard to avoid them and if you get too close to one, they will give a warning growl. You can outrun any predator in the game, even a cougar. And don't be tempted to confront them with a pistol, this will probably just get you dead. Avoid them completely until you have a bow and a shotgun, there's no good in confronting them, just avoid, period.

Now, there is fiber everywhere. You just need to 'see' it, and you will after you get used to it. It's everywhere. The first thing you need to do is gather fiber, chop wood and make yourself a bow. Then learn to hunt chickens and rabbits. Then make yourself a fishing pole and catch fish. Once you have both, you will not be hungry again.

Now the weather. Just build a simple foundation and make it high enough off the ground that you can get under and build a fire, to keep you warm and out of the rain.

Just those few simple things and you have yourself a very fun game to enjoy for many many hours to come.\t\t\t\t\t
