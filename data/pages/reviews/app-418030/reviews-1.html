\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played 92.8 Hours of this game I will share my thoughts on what this game does right, and what it does wrong.

I will say playing this game solo is a much better experience than playing multiplayer, unless you plan on having separate bases. The loot does not appear to increase alongside the number of players, so you and your friend will be fighting over the already low amount of items you have to take care of yourself.

With that out of the way, the majority of my hours are single player so I will review in that regard as the only multiplayer specific problem is what i shared above.

Subsistence is a survival game that truly feels like one to me. Don't get me wrong I LOVE the forest and other survival titles that have risen to popularity over recent years - but usually the survival aspect is secondary and not usually challenging unless you play on the hardest difficulty.
That being said what you do in this game is exactly as the title says. You subsist. Some other reviewers have a large problem with this, but I think it is what makes this game unique compared to other survival games. You will not be fully kitted within a day of playing. You may not even have a finished base with all walls and ceilings after a day of playing. You are not OP in this game. Many gamers like to just be god, and if that's your thing this game is not for you.

Pros:
- PVE Combat is a really unique aspect of this game introducing "hunters" that move into your world and try to destroy your base and you can raid their base for more loot. I've always wanted a game like rust that is strictly PVE, so theres no need to worry about cheaters or someone offline raiding your base etc.
- There are a lot of unique energy management systems in this game which makes the player decide how they will manage the power of their base. Will you keep the fridge running? Or will you install lights in your base so its not so dark? ETC
- In order to receive good loot you need to work for it. This is not the type of game where you can get lucky and find god items in a crate off of the spawn.
- The game wants to encourage exploration by only allowing certain resources to be gathered from certain places. This means you will have to leave your base consistently if you want to progress.
- You easily get your 14$ worth if you are a fan of survival games and you follow the 1$ / an hour of gameplay rule.
- Every fight you get into is somewhat challenging (even with the animals, some can outrun you)
- The building is fun, there are a lot of ways to make your base look nice geometrically so you dont live in a cube. (You can if youd like, though)

Things that could be PROS or CONS depending on perspective:
- This game is made by a solo developer I believe, which given that - the game is still being updated after all these years and clearly is a passion project from the dev. Hats off. This might not be for you however if you want consistent and steady updates.
- This game is very grind heavy. Your resources get swallowed up fast if you are not consistently going out and getting more. To many this makes the game have "fake gameplay" where you are given busy work disguised as gameplay. If you want to chop down 3 trees and have enough wood for the rest of your world this is not the game for you. You are subsisting, and constantly trying to stay afloat. I have this listed under pros and cons because once you get into a rhythm this gets a lot better than when you are first starting off in a world.
- Progression is slow. This follows the above statement in that you will always have something to work towards because you need to save up resources over the course of many days to craft the one machine you wanted to make.
- The Looting system. There are randomized crate spawn locations all around the map that come in the form of food crates, ammo crates, medical crates, construction crates, and locked crates (with goodies inside). This is the only way to get certain materials reliably. You either love this or you hate it.
- The animal spawns are insane, you are constantly running into wolves and bears and you better hope you came prepared if they start chasing you and you have no stamina.

CONS:
- Gun play is extremely clunky. Never try to hit a moving target, your bullet will not land 95% of the time.
- The Winter season is incredibly devoid of content to make it fun. I constantly switch my season back to spring when winter rolls around just so I have something to do. (You can easily do this from the main menu when selecting your world)
- Many of the machines you make have very few purposes (very specialized, they do 1 thing and 1 thing well).
- Multiplayer resources are not doubled, you and your friends will be suiciding to increase hunger meter / stamina etc.

And down to some minor complaints
- Blood does not wash off your hands when it rains, but if you go swimming it will.
- It took me a while to realize that when you die you lose a little bit of everything you have in your inventory, the game does not tell you this in an obvious way (That I know of)
- Would be nice to have more options when it comes to upgrading your base to metal, as both the wood AND the metal textures feel very rough. Would be cool if there were a decor update or something of the sort, just to spruce things up a bit.
- Beds cost power to respawn you at your base. I believe this is to prevent your base from being a safe haven, but why not just put a respawn delay on it? (You can only spawn at the bed every 15 IRL minutes).
- Its nearly impossible to get out of the water after swimming/diving.

CONCLUSION:
I think this game is worth exactly what the developer charges. Its 14$, which is what many people would spend at a restaurant or the movies. It is easily 14h worth of entertainment if you enjoy survival games. It is goofy, and it is a little clunky at times, but for a single developer this is some really good work. You eventually learn how to play along with the quirks as you would any other game (see: source engine movement tech, etc). Maybe the real subsisting was the friends we made along the way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so I am simply confused on why there are so many negative reviews about this game. This review will also include my thoughts on this game about 11 hours in, if I need to add anything I will probably.

The game is grindy although not in a tedious way, the grind is satisfying although time consuming. Although I like the game's grindiness (to an extent) the reviews clearly show that there is quite a bit of demand for a more lax/less grindy game which I think could be solved by adding a "casual" gamemode to the game that gives like 3x spawn rates and drop rates.

I've also heard a good amount of people say that this game is hard/difficult, which yes the game certainly isn't easy it's most definitely fair (which is uncommon to see in a lot of survival games imo) I've died a good 40ish times in my 11 hours, and I did not think once that my deaths were unfair in any way.

Either way, it's a nice experience and it's definitely surprising that a one man (or possibly a very small group) were able to develop this.\t\t\t\t\t
