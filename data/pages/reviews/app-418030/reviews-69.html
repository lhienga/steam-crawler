\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a weird and wonderful little game.

I'll be honest, this game is a real challenge to get going on. Foodstuffs, weaponry, ammunition etc, it's all very taxing, and difficult, and I honestly need to play this game singleplayer more to really get into the setting. The multiplayer element is solid though, which is a great start.

However, the game is indeed early access and needs a lot of work still. Materials in this game are not well balanced at all; looting is not great, plant fibers are a scarcity, the spawn locations for the various animals are same-y after awhile and just become a regular thing to avoid... and don't get me started on the underwater aspect of the game. Some of the recipes are also a tad over-the-top (biofuel in a campfire. Hm.)

The scaling of the AI is not well balanced either. They're capable of levelling their bases and loot considerably faster than the base game for a single player, pretty much forcing you to play multiplayer to keep up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like games like the Forest, 7 Days To Die, and possibly even the Unreal World - that kind of one person against the world type scenario, you'll probably enjoy this. It focuses more on hunting and foraging, at least at the beginning - but then again, like many of these types of games, you are just getting started. There's three stats to keep track of: protein, fruit and veg, and hydration which ties in to an overall "hunger" meter, which affects stamina and health regeneration, as well as how well you can run - so low protein means you will run out of stamina much faster *and* it will take ages to regenerate, not great if you're being chased!

Resources such as ore and wood are relatively plentiful - I'm not keen on the mechanic of the trees not being cut down (they stay in place and it's like they run out of "charges", and just "regenerate" so you can harvest them for wood again) so you can never really clear a nice flat area to make a base, but that is also a little more realistic in that you can't level the ground etc. to build a "perfect" base, and have to adapt it to your surroundings. Animals will give you various protein sources (including liver, used for an antidote recipe) and resources to eventually help you craft better weapons and armour. They can also level up, which affects their hit points, but does not seem to affect the quality of loot they give, so you are often best trying to find a level one wolf or bear to give you an easier time. Loot boxes spawn quite regularly, with various ammunition types, and sometimes water and foodstuffs, amongst other things but their contents can be a little random.

Progress is gated to a certain degree until you make a Base Command Unit, which unlocks further recipes and allows you to build various facilities at your base - but then that will spawn hunters into the world which can be a pain unless you turn them off. You can modify their behaviour however, so that they are less "aggressive" as they will eventually attempt to raid your base - but I have not personally tried this out as of yet and have just left it at the default settings. A nice touch is that the wildlife will react to them - I haven't tried it yet but have had the idea of trying to bait a wolf or bear to attack the hunters while I try to take them down! Destroying their bases requires having to create a special "bomb", but it does seem that the wildlife helps to keep their numbers down (I believe three or four camps can spawn near your "main" base). But they can carry some weapons and keep items at their base which can be hard to come by normally.

I also like the idea of seasons in the game, and the appearance of seasonal elements (beehives, white wolves) which reminds me so much of the Unreal World. The cold can kill you in winter, as you're desperately try to make a hole in ice to get water from, or to harvest a little more wood to keep you warm. You can also catch rabbits and chickens to breed them, and grow crops for vegetables - as diving for kelp can be a little hit and miss, plus if a predator follows you into the water... good luck! Fishing is quite easy too, but you require grubs which can be gathered from "special" trees dotted around the landscape.

The developer is quite active, having released several updates since the game's release and they continue to support the game with new additions, and quality of life improvements and further optimisation. It is obvious that this is much a labour of love - I haven't even mentioned the "secret" cave system I have yet to explore myself!

Overall, this is one of the games that I really enjoy, which can be somewhat chilled (unless hunters are involved). I don't generally have problems with the number of predators (I do keep them at default settings, however) that others seem to have had, and as long as I'm careful and scout the area properly, and keep tabs on my nutrition levels, I usually have an escape route and the capability to outpace most things until they stop chasing you. Apart from trying to find particular types of ore, I don't tend to have major shortages of most things and I feel the game is quite balanced - just don't go in all guns blazing like you're on God Mode, or the wolves, bears and boars (and even stags during the rutting season) will make short work of you.
