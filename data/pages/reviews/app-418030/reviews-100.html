\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent foundation with good ideas for a strong survival game. I bought the game years ago shortly after it first hit early access. Unfortunately, coming back after a few years i was hit with the same issues i found back when i first bought it. Firstly, the game is not intended for solo players that also have a life. the poor resource gathering to over intensive micro managing (while doable) prevents a solo player from feeling like they are making progress. Just staying fed properly in the beginning is a daunting task that will take all your time. I often found myself starving through the night by my fire because i did not find enough food throughout the day to really last. Second, the resource gathering is atrocious. I dont mind the heavy grind to get the materials needed for high end gear. Thats what these games are about. My issue lies with the main resources that you cannot go without but have absolutely no control over obtaining them. There needs to be some kind of change. Nails, should be made from scrap metal, and so should arrows. scrap metal, electronic components should be obtained in a new fashion or the amount of supply boxes that spawn resources needs to be increased for solo players. 30 in game days to get my tshirt and bed is too long for any survival game. This simply came down to not "finding" enough resource boxes. Sometimes there are all kinds of boxes and bags in my chosen area and other days ill find 1 or even none. you cant venture too far to look for these without running the risk of all your micro managing at base falling apart. food consumption needs to be adjusted for people and animals. chickens eat very little compared to how much they eat in game. Same for people. I can barely get through one steak, yet in game i need 6 steaks a day to keep my protein up. I like the game. I want to love it. But Cold Games, I think its time to hold off on new content and work on some tweaks and quality of life. Like the hunters and rogues. there should be some sort of aggression setting as well as attack settings.....no reason why one them should start chasing me down cause he was in render range when i picked a fiber plant. And maybe tone down the auto aim, or adjust damage output to be consistent with players. Not sure why a bare chested hunter can kill me with 4 shots when it takes me 6, when im lucky to hit headshots\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoy crafting and building, and this game was recommended to me as a game with advanced building capabilities. I am not interested in combat games, but this game allows disabling of the AI hunters, so I thought I would give it a try as a simple player vs. environment game.

I finally gave up after over 70 hours of singleplayer game play. I really tried to like this game, but in the end I had to admit that I wasn't enjoying it at all. I keep hearing from fans of this game that it is such a realistic survival game, but nothing about it is realistic. Around every corner there is a bear or a lone wolf that you have to tiptoe around in order not to be killed on day one. Beginners are advised to walk at a snail's pace to conserve stamina in case they have to flee from a bear or a wolf. Bags of loot magically and unrealistically respawn in the countryside every day. So you spend all day plodding about, looking for magic bags and resources and avoiding predators. Crafting is also magic. You only need the proper resources in your inventory to craft gigantic items like walls and foundations while you are going about your daily business of fishing, hunting, chopping trees, etc. etc. So you end up carrying walls, ceilings, foundations, etc. around with you until you place them down. This is another example of how the game is unrealistic. Lack of realism alone is not a bad thing as long as a game is fun to play. I didn't enjoy it at all and I regret investing so much time in it.
