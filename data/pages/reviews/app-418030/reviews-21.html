\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's face it. I'm one of the older players. I don't adapt as fast as I once did, but that being said, the learning curve is steep. Especially when you first start. My advice is F8 after picking up ANYTHING or doing anything. The game itself will autosave but not often enough to avoid you losing everything if you die and can't get back to body before poof.
Build a foundation and base wall and base unit. Make sure wall has 2x2 facing INWARD otherwise when you build roof later there's a leak. Build storage unit fast. Save wood. If not absolutely necessary, DON'T eat fish raw, it's worth 3x as much if cooked and you will last longer. On the other hand, DON'T catch more than a days worth at a time til you can build a frigerator. It will rot. If it does, KEEP the spoils. Recipes use it.
You can repair weapons with hammer and some metal but you can't repair pick axe (at least it won't give me that option) so after about 10 nodes, you'll need to build another.
Build a foundation platform at those huge trees. Something to stand on. Wolves won't bug you and if bears come, move to far side and stand still. If there's no blood, they will turn and leave.
If in doubt, about trying something new, save first; Try. Fail? Reboot it.
I could not find a liver so had to endure my first winter while poisoned. Let's just say I am not in a hurry to repeat that. That's all I have for now.

Update after playing a while:
New things I learned.
1) hold down the left mouse button and you will keep chopping/mining whatever without having to click. (duh! I felt like a dummy but since I mightn't be the only one doing the 10,000 clicks I thought I'd add it)
2)if you don't have insect repellent, wait for a rainstorm to gather (with the pickaxe!!) the honey, they will bite some but barely hurt me. It took roughtly 13 hits with pickaxe to gather 4 honey.
3) if, when you free range your chickens/rabbits, you don't SEE them roaming near your base, they are glitched. I restarted a game and was surprised to see them hanging out nearby. In my Original game, in 5 game years, never saw them.
The reason this is important is that as they level, they can be picked up (if seen) and replaced with a new animal in hutch. Kill the level 4 and above one up to level 10 and you get premium meat, premium and regular feathers, fur, and a lot of fat off the level 10s. If you replace 3 level 10s in november with new animals and kill the level 10s you have enough oil to burn the generator non-stop all winter. Big win!
4) spending the resources to get all 3 of the oven equipments is worth it. The higher level foods up you by more than a point of health/stamina with each meal thus allowing you to quickly get above 100 and reach the achievement for 125. You die less. You survive more. *Worth it*
5) when your pickaxe is down to 25% make another and carry with you. Keep gathering all forms of rocks as often as possible. This allows you to do whatever crafting you want because you will have kept smelting and have resources. Especially true for the lockbox keys. I carry 2 on me most times. Definitely keep one on me at all times. The stuff in those boxes is frequently very useful and a life saver!
Good luck!\t\t\t\t\t
