\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like hardcore survival, this is your game. I like that you have the option to turn hunters off. I feel the game is hard enough to get the hang of with the animals alone. Once you get a decent base and better weapons you can turn them back on for the challenge or leave them off. It's a very grindy game which I don't have a problem with. I do have a problem with how expensive some items are to craft. When you get a fishing pole, bow, and pistol protein isn't that hard to come by. Veggies and berries are a totally different story. Super long time to grow veggies and berries are hard to find. I feel there definitely needs to be a better balance on void spoilage. When you are finally able to get a fridge that helps a lot but once in your inventory a timer starts. The berries and veggies show how long you have but protein does not. It expires way too fast in my opinion. I think power could be easier to generate especially when adding solar panels and stuff, but it is still tough. Mass is also hard to generate. I think it's a very pretty game and I like the building options. Just wish everything wasn't so resource expensive and food didn't expire so quickly. Other than those 2 things I really like the game and the grind is part of its charm. I like that it's not super easy just think a few things could be balanced better. Will definitely continue to play and get better at the game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy First 3 days in-game:
If you like constantly dying I guess it's an ok game. After reading the guides I thought I had it figured out. But no. I survived the first day and night, barely. But the second day found me trying NOT to starve to death. I spent the entire day trying to find enough to eat so I wouldn't die, and found myself too far away from the campfire, so I started to freeze to death. Before I could collect enough resources to build another fire I was dead. This was on EASY mode. It's just too stringent. I know we can freeze to death in relatively short time, but I shouldn't have to worry about dying because I went a day without food. Hungry yes, dead no. System needs to slow down health loss due to lack of nutrition. Either that or have so many damn resources on hand I trip over food growing all around me. Food just doesn't do much to provide health. And forget about shooting chickens and rabbits. The carcass is inedible if you shoot them with your pistol. And you spend very valuable time trying to get resources for the bow and arrows. Here again, you will die from freezing if you spend valuable time trying to build a bow. You just can't win. I'm wondering if I should just start the game in the middle of summer instead of the default month of March. Maybe that would make a difference???

Currently:
Started over and picked May as my beginning month. Full 365 days in a year. That should give me plenty of time to prepare for winter without freezing in the first two days of the game. Made a bow and arrows. Found Grub Trees. Killed a wolf and a bear. Died once during my first 8 days due to malnutrition and exposure. Started working close to base and concentrated on growing vegetable crops, killing rabbits for their fur and chicken for their feathers. Now I have a bed. Built a wood stove for better fuel efficiency. Things are getting better now, and I can almost catch my breath...

So would I buy it again? You bet I would!!\t\t\t\t\t

No making nails until you build a base and unlock the smelter though. This game has potential. But currently needs work. Guns don't do enough damage, bow has no aiming reticle, and managing your food (protein, veggies, water) drops too fast to be worth it. Nothing can be crafted right away and things that can take away from the precious resources to build a base. I suggest the dev(s) look at ARK and get a good sense on how to structure costs and what can be crafted right away.\t\t\t\t\t
