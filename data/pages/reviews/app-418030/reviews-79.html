\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSubsistence is currently my top survival game, next to Green Hell. I did have to start again on easy, though, because the "normal" setting has just too many predators roaming around for a beginner. So, to everyone who doesn't like to die again and again in the first hours of playing, do yourself a favor, set your pride aside and play on easy (plus hunters on revenge attacks only or turned off completely, they're kind of OP). Also, the easy setting allows you to load your last save when you die instead of respawning - the punishment for death is severe!
Then, you'll find yourself on a beautiful map with lots of different animals, plants and landscapes. You'll need to grind for wood and fibers in the beginning, but slowly you'll start finding all different types of materials and unlock new recipes, and build a bigger and better base each day.
Totally recommend for all survival fans!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are a lot of things this game does well. Friends and I have put over 100 hours into it. It has a beautiful map and great sound quality. With a few tweaks I think this could be a really great game. As it is now, it takes an absurdly long time to collect resources and some items required for higher level crafting are unavailable unless you find them in a special rare box that takes even more resources to open. That combined with sudden death/missing inventory glitches, infinite death loops due to respawning on the opposite end of the map and freezing to death over and over make this game unplayable.
After several hours spent attempting to replace resources due to multiple game glitches, server crashes etc, I've given up on it. Current gameplay feels like one step forward, two steps back.
This game in particular has led me to believe that if developers are going to be allowed to sell a game in early access, they should be required to include console commands or at least have to disclose if they do not, so players can know which ones to avoid.\t\t\t\t\t
