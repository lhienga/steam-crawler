\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has unexpectedly become a gem in my library. Like most other people I tend to jump around from game to game and usually never touch them again. But this is one of those rare games that I keep coming back to and I don't totally understand why. The graphics are good not great. The animations and UI are meh. The inventory and building system are okay. And yet...there is something exciting and relaxing when i start a new game and slowly start making my way to the same lake i usually start my base. Despite the mediocre graphics the warm color palate and varying terrain is pleasing to the eye and gives the environment a nice mood. The animations and UI are good in some aspects and bad in others but it doesn't take away from the experience. Finding and unzipping a loot box will always be satisfying. While at certain times building up your base and surviving can be tricky and tiresome, putting the last piece of roofing on your house and eating some elk stew while watching a bear kill a raider makes you feel at home. And maybe that's why I keep coming back...\t\t\t\t\t

It just hits a certain "mood" just right, and i keep coming back, starting again, and just enjoying what it has to offer. It's an absolutely incredible one-man-project. The updates are far between, but substantial, and the game just keeps getting better by leaps each time. Highly recommend this game if you like survival games, especially if you lean toward the more difficult side (though it doesn't have to be). This game is deserving of good reviews from folks that have played more than a few hours (no offense to them, it's just that if you put this game down within a few hours, it probably wasn't the type of game for you in the first place).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll say more once I've played for longer. So far (after about 7 hours) I'm having a great time on easy mode. I dislike that blueberry bushes only have a single blueberry, and I think there are too many bags laying around. I'd probably enjoy it even more if I could learn to make nails, or use stones for lesser arrows. But that's only because it feels like all I'm doing is picking up bags, when really I'd like to build a pretty base, and maybe a greenhouse. My play style is more exploring/ building/ gathering.

All that said though, it is relaxing to run around and gather food. Hunting took me a while to get the hang of, but I've managed to kill a couple bears and wolves. I do wish bears would always provide a steak, or that they could drop fur, but maybe that's something for down the line. I am excited to experience seasons, but it'll be a while yet. I have it set to 200 days, and I started in April (rather than the suggested January.) I'll update my review after 100 hours.

Edit: unfortunately I only made it to 45ish hours before I stopped. It was getting grindy and tough to enjoy in my limited playtime. I wanted to build and decorate, immerse myself in the world. But my vegetables rotting really took some joy away. Potatoes will last months in a cool place, so it was frustrating to have them go bad so fast. Anyway, I'll keep an eye on the updates. It was really lovely for 40 hours. I more than enjoyed my money's worth, but I can't say I'd recommend the game whole-heartedly yet to my friend's who also enjoy survival building games.\t\t\t\t\t

After a few weeks the npc hunters show up in our area, now we can longer run the usual circuit for boxes and bags and other items. The npc builds way faster than you and seems to have unlimited ammo. Dying is not the end of the world, but sometimes you respawn halfway across the map. We were never able to build a bed to spawn in.

It seems to me the developer thinks the game has to be impossible to keep you interested, except it is impossible, and this makes it untenable. It would behoove him to actually play his game, and if it is easy to him, consider making a video about it. I liked the game but it quickly becomes unplayable with the npc's. I have played a dozen or more survival games and love the genre, he should make it so players on a coop can toggle difficulty, we turned the npc's to the lowest level and they still crushed us. I conquered Hobo: A tough life, and the rage quit in that game was high, if someone has a suggestion on how to keep up with the npc bases I would be happy to hear it.

The food, water and hunger are doable. The mats for making bullets needs to be reconsidered. Or just turn off the unlimited ammo of npc's for a start. I would play this again if it had some sensible levelling system, and change my recommendation. I am fine with spending the 15 bucks it costs me that for lunch at a fast food joint, just see no way to continue at this juncture.
