I do have gripes with the game on Normal, however; There's no Aim Down Sights sensitivity slider, so you unfortunately have the same sensitivity setting for general turning, and aiming turning, which means that in order to be able to quickly perform a 180 degree turn, you have to have horrendously sensitive aiming speed. Given that this is a first-person shooter, it's kind of laughable that no such setting for ADS Sensitivity exists yet. Other than that, the game is beautiful visually, and the gameplay mechanics/base building are enjoyable and satisfying. But it should also be noted that raiding Hunter bases, at present, is a waste of time unless you're really far into the game. I spent 3 pickaxes raiding a hunter base, but apparently it wasn't late enough in the game to do so, and despite the ammo and pickaxes and various other resources I spent to do it, I was only rewarded with 2 Biofuel and 40 Ash in exchange for the arduous task of raiding a hunter's base. The game's okay, but really not spectacular. The dev needs to do more work, much more, in order for my review to change. Is it worth playing? Yes, but it has glaring flaws that are gonna irritate you, and the dev isn't very industrious or committed to the development of the game. You could look at gameplay from five years ago on Youtube, and realize that not much has changed. Like I said, there's still no ADS Sensitivity slider, and raiding Hunters can be profoundly unrewarding depending on your game stage. It needs a lot of work, and I don't know if this dev is committed to completing that work.\t\t\t\t\t
