\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat i like:
- Nice weather system
- Bots
- Balanced crafting

What i dislike and needs to be handled urgently:
- Cooking mechanics are completely useless , needs to be balanced
- Bots needs to farm like players too, no backsies , its annoying they can have armors without any building at all..
- Heavy grinding is necessary to keep it going, it needs some automation
- Gardening definitely needs more mechanics and different plots
- Seeing same animals over and over and over and over again while it needs heavy grinding is annoying, needs some more animals, or at least different behavior pattern, means a bit more clever AI
- Sounds in the game is nice but has no control to customize , it gets annoying pretty quickly, i need to have control over each audio channel
- Random bags/boxes appear around the world ? Really ? It needs a better and logical approach.
- Trees do not fall down. Well I understand it; If you make them disappear after cutting it down, the you need a way to respawn them and it can be tricky. Ok. But you can let them disappear and start as seedling and it can take few days to spawn while changing the mesh/model accordingly, while increasing the amount of wood you harvest.
- There are lots of multiplayer bugs needs to be fixed asap, like dropping from game, animal corpses disappear most of the time for the client players, while players looting the animals they can get upside down and many more bugs.

Overall its an enjoyable game which i still play with friends but when we got all the stuff from the crafting table then game is pretty much done for, no more content, no goals, no reason to continue at all. Hope they attend these issues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATED TO POSITIVE REVIEW:
OK so here's the thing. I put a decent chunk of time into this game and wasn't happy with it (see below). I came back to it for a third time out of a lack of options. I love the genre and consume these games like a fat kid eats cake so i was struggling to find something and figured i would try again. There's been some improvements.
-Most of the jank that made it unplayable is gone. The animals used to glitch about when approaching you like Neo in The Matrix. It was amazingly frustrating to try and hit them. Considering how hard they hit it was unplayable. Now they run at you and still murk you with zero effort, but at least they're not stop-motion ghosting at you anymore. Game is still janky, but now it is more of that "early PUBG kind-of charming in it's jank" rather than "hurling my monitor through the wall" stuff.
-Hit reg is better. F*** me it was bad before. It is still a little jank but better. The bow is delightful to play with.
-Lots of added end-game stuff if you want to invest your twenties into farming just to get there.

There is a lot to love, but it has it's problems. The entire meta is "walk around looking for random boxes, avoid death-by-bear, and pick up the random camouflaged plants". Like why the f are there random loot boxes constantly spawning everywhere? It's the weirdest design, feels super lazy like they cbf making actual mechanics for some of the mats so they just chuck em in a box and randomly spawn it. The hunters are somehow dumb af but still OP, dont ask me how that works, if you play it you will feel it too. The map becomes quite dull and empty once you realise you are just walking around looking for random spawn stuff.

AND THE GRIND... Jesus f*** the grind. To make a stove you need an immense amount of banked power and mass, plus a biiiig chunk of metal, which requires a biiiig chunk of iron ore. THEN, once you make the stove, you can't actually use it till you make a pan or tray for it, which requires MORE IRON THAN THE STOVE. Plus adhesive which is a mega craft too. Every little thing you do in this game requires an investment of several hours. Most games you start by chopping down a couple of trees to make a work bench. This game is like a dozen hours before you can make a bench. It's stupid.

BUT what that means is that when you do eventually make that bench... it's time to dance around in your underwear praising the sun god or the spaghetti monster or Jesus or whatever other imaginary thing you like. I feel like this is Tarkov as a survival game - it will constantly feel like you are being punished for playing it, like the game actually hates you and wants you to go away. So much so, that eventually the tiniest win feels epic.

This time around I have created an epic base, farmed for an eternity, murdered everything, lost my sanity more than once, and it's been fun. Not amazing, "on the same level as Subnautica / The Forest / Green Hell" but pretty good. Therefore I have finally changed it to positive. If for no other reason than the sheer amount of hours for my money, and for sitting in the back of my brain making me come back thrice for more. 60+ hours is good value for money. See below for prior reviews:



UPDATE:
Still wouldn't recommend. The game is just poorly balanced. I need to eat so much protein to fill up, and I can shoot a wolf seven times in the face with an M9 Beretta and it will keep coming. Hit reg is broken, crafting is just weird and grindey af... dead game. It had so much potential but it's just not good.

OG REVIEW:
Grind for the sake of grind. Was fun with friends for a bit until we all realised the intense balance issues. Nobody wants to go back to it. Huge potential, but I fear the developer is more interested in making their art than making a product that people pay money for. I just can't recommend it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just want to say that I respect the work of the creator and the fact that he makes this game alone.This game has potential. But there is one but... After the latest updates, I can conclude that the developer does not know what "game balance"is. I have compiled a short list of shortcomings for this game.
- Spawn of wild animals, in order to find a chicken or a hare, you need to run through the floor of the map,while 3 bears and 2 wolves walk on 1 square meter. Of course, I am not an expert on the animal world, but in my opinion, bears can not be in one place in SUCH NUMBERS.
- An abundance of resources. Here, in principle, the same situation as with the hares above, 3 pieces of wood fall from one tree(if my memory does not change), from which you can make one board(ONE KARL BOARD), besides everything else, fibers are needed for construction that need to be extracted from" bushes", which are necessary for the production of ropes. Plus, everything else requires nails for construction, which at first, you will have to find in boxes scattered in the forest. Unfortunately, in the process of searching, it seems that the place of action of the game is the late USSR (there is a capital shortage everywhere) and in order to build a 2-by-2 house alone, you need at best to kill half a day of real time(and this is without taking into account the fact that you will be killed by local fauna with 1-2 blows), not to mention something global, because the rest of the resources here are in the same deficit.Or hire 9 Chinese people on the kit starter so that they would extract resources on the server.
- The behavior of weapons and the production of ammunition. Despite the fact that aiming at the target is sometimes (torn), that is, there is no smoothness in movement, plus everything else that would kill the AI bot "often" has to hit the head 2 times... This is not to mention the animals that have a titanium alloy body in the course(and I'm talking about a rifle now, not to mention a shotgun and a pistol that is weaker in terms of slaughter than a bow(what is the meaning of the existence of a pistol?) The production of cartridges is a separate problem, as for me, a more balanced ammunition is arrows for a bow. The rest, in reality, is easier to do than in this game.I do not know what the creator is guided by, indicating the amount of gunpowder per 1 shotgun cartridge, but as far as I know, even in 50 BMG ammunition, such an amount of gunpowder is not used(this is if the unit of gunpowder is converted into grams), despite the fact that the gunpowder itself is scarce like other components, for which you need to run half the map again and kill at least half a day.Here comes the phrase (it used to be better)
- AI must be given credit, AI is well spelled out here, they hide well in shelters and shoot accurately, but the algorithm of their development reminds me of Stellaris, where in half an hour the opponent has 10,000 army and you barely scored 1,000.Just like here, the enemy runs around in bulletproof vests, helmets with a full arsenal at the fifth point and endless ammunition, while you are happy with the made T-shirt. Is it really impossible to slow down their development?
