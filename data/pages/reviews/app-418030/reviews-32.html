\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not in the mood to write a whole novel so here are some of my favorite things about the game and some cons you should have a look at before buying.

PROS:
Game is cheap with simple mechanics
Decent sized map
AI "hunters that you can kill for loot, base raiding included
With weaponry the animals are pretty easy to kill
Co-op so yes your friends can play
you can easily sink 100 hours into this game
interesting survival concepts (heat and cold sensitive)
CONS:
grind is unbearable you will be grinding all the time
some items have to be found and can't be crafted
often times the items that are needed to be crafted are rare
animals can quickly take over your base at any point due to the spawn and leveling system
food is a catch 22 you need ammo to kill and you need food to be able to find ammo so it's pretty annoying, sure you can forage but you won't find much and its basically no nutrition.
the spawn area for loot around your character only so if you travel you will wait a while for loot to spawn
The ai enemies often have guns before a base, which are incredibly difficult to obtain, so battles are one sided


I could go on and on but this is already to long. If you want to play this game and enjoy it you will have to grind until you are about 10 hours in. at that point you should have a decent amount of ammo and weapons which you can use to raid the ai bases. Ai base loot is the best you can get in terms of resources.
Overall in late game I love this game but fresh starts SUCK and you will consider a refund. I'm really just hopeful devs will make this game better and it doesn't stay early access for 20 years.

The patches come through and new content comes fairly regularly too!

BUT the game has just got a better! Vehicles change SO much! Hang Gliding over the area you have had to walk through for HOURS is SO satisfying (watch the landings though hahaha)

For the price this game is awesome.

If I had to pick one criticism? The AI. One one hand, it is still too simple. Once you work out how to avoid the critters' -circles- it is easy to foil them and predict their path when you're shooting them... Oh and the way a moose swims UNDERWATER to attack you? That kinda interrupts the immersion lol

For the price, this is a fun game and it just keeps delivering. For a SINGLE PERSON developing this and putting out this much content? It is a clear win for me\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanna love this game real bad i really do, but it can be so frustrating in the worst ways. IMO its a bit unbalanced in some key aspects. I do actually enjoy the difficulty in gathering some resources. The map isnt super abundantly rich in resources so you really have to hunt and search. But remedial tasks from early game stick with you, where you wish you could develop technology that can help you. As you get more and more advanced, you have more and more tasks that you need to accomplish throughout your day in order to just survive. You hope for a better ax so you can chop wood faster cause you have more need of it on day 50 than you did on day 1, but its still just the same. You can build an ATV (if you want to spend the 100 ish hours of game play to gather the resources) but you cant upgrade your ax to chop wood faster. Or you know how to build windmills and solar panels for more power, you know how to craft lights and ovens and scuba gear, but the only way you ever know how to make rope (which you need a lot of), is to hunt a cougar, of which there is usually only one or two on the map. And as many people have mentioned, there are these dang loot crates which you have to rely on to get the most basic of materials.

Overall, I think this game could be great if it can get some of the balancing right. It is a fun take on the survival craft genre and there are some really cool mechanics that add a fun bit of realism. There is just only so many hours of running around for crates instead of building or hunting or crafting that I can take....\t\t\t\t\t
