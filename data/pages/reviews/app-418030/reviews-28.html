\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I've kept up with this title a few years now, coming back and trying it here and there. There's a lot to love, from the visuals, to the animations and general feel of the game. However, the actual game play itself is what's the issue.

I have roughly 10 and a half hours, split between a few different saves. So 3.5 per try. Think of The Forest, Ark, or 7 days. That's enough time to get decently established, have an okay stock of items. I struggled to build two foundations in the same amount of time, and it was difficult to even pull that off.

The main issue to me is the beginning loop. You can't craft much early on, so most of your supply comes from supply crates scattered everywhere. Because of this, you need to move all around the map to get basic needs, most crucial being nails. You need these for arrowheads (oddly) and building a shelter.

So you're basically just skittering around hoping you get lucky, meanwhile there's bears and wolves everywhere between them. They can kill you in a few hits, your hatchet does almost nothing, and you start off with one mag of ammo. This leads to you either having to very slowly combing the landscape, or bolting the other way when you spot wild life.

With the animals and nails in mind, this leads to it taking ages to get anything built. My experience for the most part with this title has been what would have been the first 20 or so minutes in any other survival game. Keep in mind my playtime.

It's hard to give this a thumbs down, developer seems hardworking and this game is neat in a lot of ways. I'm even willing to try it again down the road. But in it's current state, it's just a very difficult/boring title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK! So this game is fun... BRUTAL... but fun. That is, until you need cotton. Cotton is apparently a gate. I'm currently sitting inside the house while husband goes out with our ONLY jacket LOL Singleplayer, it's probably not as annoying. If doing Multiplayer, get ready to have "community clothing" until you find the rare and elusive Unicorn Cotton plant which will take you 2 in-game years to get enough cotton for a 2nd jacket (unless you find enough seeds for the planter boxes). And you cannot extract seeds from the cotton you grow (makes zero sense). You also cannot extract seeds from Strawberries/blueberries (because that makes sense)... Yes, this isn't real life (don't make that ignoramus comment), but it still needs to make sense -_-

Let's talk about how you can catch animals in a trap with medicinal leaves but you can't feed it to your housed animals... or how plants take 5evers to grow and suck up so much fertilizer that your poor wood stove can't keep up with the Ash required. Maybe multiple stoves could work but I need that wood for other stuff xD Your plants should be continuously harvestable once grown.

You could use animal feces for fertilizer, but apparently your chickens hold in their poop until you get a feces tray... poor bloated chickens :(

How about the wasted pretty plants everywhere that you can't harvest at all or do anything with.

Now, I do love that when you chop trees, they don't actually fall down. Deforestation is always a thing with me in games like Ark, Valheim, The Forest, Atlas, etc. I also love the ice fishing- This was a great implementation!

All said and done, I do recommend this game even with all its annoyances.

P.S. DON'T FORGET TO WASH YOUR HANDS!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game if you have many many many hours to waste. It does take a very long time to get anywhere due to the process of squiring things such as power. Food, water and shelter is not so much of an issue however power just takes too long and requires too much effort to build things like solar panels and wind turbines.

The aim mechanics of the game are just terrible, an ads multipler would be very nice and is very much needed.

Animals are not exactly challenging however when it comes to hunting cougars which you need for materials it can be a drag finding them first of all and then second of all killing them.

I should imagine this game is more friendly towards co op players however for a solo player I really do think the resources need to be boosted or mods introduced to make the game go quicker and get resources faster, it is just too boring having to spend so much time farming.

Overall, not a bad game not a great game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, our rag tag crew of 7 have been having interesting times. My husband has logged more hours then I but weve all been having the same experiences.
- great survival game. Tags saying hardest survival are completely accurate
- beautiful scenery
- easily able to conserve stamina by walking
- animals are animals. Youre able to skirt around them, outrun most of them they have consistent patterns for idle movements, hunting, chasing. If they see you well good luck.
Some cons:
Fiber is easily seen but not easily resourced. I do wish it would be at least 2-3 per drop as they arent plentiful. Same w the other plants. Or even a random drop of 1-3. Something to help offset the scarcity
- hunters are both amazing if youre able to kill them and horrible for those just starting out. We chose to turn off hunters until we have beds. Were excited to go after them and they are great for resource harvesting but you dont bring an ax to a shootout. Those have automatic rifles and a never ending supply of ammo. At best if you kill them, while those guns disappear, you do get a of shell casings, bandages, some food.
- base building will take some time. Work w your team. Itll take All of you to hunt, gather, and build.
- the biggest downside is how small the tribes/teams are. 4 max that weve found. So far we have 2 bases and trying to invite ppl to have a semi split team. Thats my biggest issue w the game. The other is not having enough time to retrieve your body before the timer runs out. Even running and only slowing for stam recovery, you can't clear that map, kill what killed you or even sneak to your body in time. We've all experienced the pain of watching the timer run out just as we manage to get there.
If you enjoy survival games, base building, and exploration - get this. Its hard. Youll rage. You might cry over some loss. But it's still fun and there's a lot of pride that comes w succeeding. It does get easier as you start to get built up. We've had teammates on the server nearly round the clock and we've got a small cozy spot.
Dont let the fact the game is still alpha after 7 yrs dissuade you. I learned this is created by a 1 man team (if intel is wrong please correct me). That itself is amazing that 1 person built the game around their rl. Kudos dude.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so after trying this game a few times after buying it, I have determined that while it is actually a nice game (from what I managed to do in the short amount of time I spent playing) the lack of any initial guide makes playing the game a bit hard...even on EASY. Not knowing what to look for, because nothing "lights" up when you get close to it, to let you know it is lootable (like in other games), I spent the better part of the first hour of playtime running around (on normal mode) trying to avoid predators while trying to catch a rabbit or chicken (sadly, even in this survival game, we cannot run down a chicken). Rather then catch anything I could eat, I instead became something a bear could eat; note to self, ignore the small critters until you can manage to make a bow. Oh, and on that particular topic, making the bow isn't the only hard part, making the arrows is just as difficult...if you don't have feathers....which come from chickens...the same chickens that YOU CAN'T RUN DOWN! The 9 mm that you start the game with, will put down a wolf with most of your ammunition being used up in the process; better hope a hunter doesn't come along. Oh, and don't bother using it on small game unless you want to carry around an animal carcass, because live ammunition + small game = bad.

So, I found that finding food is rather difficult even on easy mode. For me (not sure about other players') finding the fiber was really hard, because it blended into the surroundings. I am old, and my eyes are not that great. The lighting (or lack there of in some areas) actually hurt my eyes. So as far as gathering fiber and other "gatherables", I found it very difficult to do, due to looking out for predators, and the lighting effects causing eye pains. Why am I so concerned about gatherables you ask? Well, to put it simply, not only do we have a food and water meter, but also a "fruits and vegetables" meter! We have to manage 3 different meters to keep the 4th meter in a "happy" place. Proteins (from what else but meat), fruits and vegetables (from gatherables that I couldn't find easily), and water from...well...water. That one is the easiest I think, not very easy to miss a large body of water. Oh, but keep in mind that you also need to be able to boil your water before drinking, and we only have "1 freakin match" in our inventory???? I didn't really check the crafting pages too much, because I couldn't even get the base foundation part done (again, all thanks to hard to find gatherables), but it may be possible that there is a "flint and steel" or something of that nature that you can make...once you make a pick axe.....

I was not very impressed with the level of difficulty of the crafting system in the game, and I absolutely love crafting in games (it is my main focus in fact). I mean, we are in the middle of a forest, with all manner of plant life around us, and we can only gather fiber from one source? The fact that the first building we can construct requires "nails" makes me a little unhappy as well. While I was a boy scout 39 years ago, I seem to recall a basic structure being tree branches and twine or cord or something of that nature....not NAILS! This is supposed to be a survival game, not a Builder Bob game right? Basic survival shelter should not require us to seek out nails from boxes that are surrounded by predators; the very same predators you tell us at the beginning of the game to AVOID! There needs to be more options for actual "survival" in the game, rather then expect us to sate our hunger on Gourmet meals and live in air-conditioned housing! In other words, we should be starting from basic, and working our way up to luxury, not starting from middle class and going for luxury... Survival shelter, tree branches tied together with long woven grass cords are a basic structure for example. Snares are simple traps, requiring very little to construct, but allow us to actually catch small game and helps us to survive.

Okay enough of the rant I suppose. Over-all, I think this game has potential, if there was a basic tutorial that would help us figure out what it is we are supposed to be looking for it would definitely make it better. Equally good would be a better crafting system, which would include actual basic survival facilities and items. (several years ago they used stone tools for example, and they must have worked because we are still here today). But, as for recommending the game to others' I cannot do that based on my experiences. All I can say is if you like something that is really challenging (and have better eyesight then I do) go ahead and give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
