Played for a few hours on the normal difficulty setting and no hunters, slowly building my base and trying to gather enough food and resources to survive and learn how to handle the dangerous wildlife. And die......a lot. Until I made a critical mistake that had a bear kill me and wreck my base, losing everything that I worked for. After that, I felt like I had a good enough grasp of how to play the game and what not to do that I wiped the game save and started fresh. Been playing every day since then.

I love the crafting and building mechanics, the weather and seasonal changes, and the difficulty of surviving. Just when you think you have everything figured out and you start to get a little cocky, that cougar kills you, or the moose, or the bear, or the wolf. If you have a problem with a lot of dying and a game being hard, then this isn't the game for you. Survival in the wilderness is hard and this game plays like how survival should be, difficult, consequences, and taking your needs of survival into account.

It isn't a realistic survival game of course, but it isn't being advertised as such, so don't go in thinking that it will be just like real life survival. It's an entertaining game. The fact that it is created by just one person is astounding. I have all the respect for ColdGames and his dedication and creativeness with this game. He is constantly improving the game fixing bugs, adding new things and so forth. In a time when big game companies with millions of dollars to throw at a large group of people to develop AAA game titles, and then the game turns out to be utter garbage to play, It is amazing that one person can make a game so enjoyable to play. It may not have the ultra high end graphics that AAA developers put out, but graphics mean nothing if the game play sucks.

Keep up the good work ColdGames! Looking forward to whatever new things you bring to the game in the next update.
