\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has tremendous potential as a survival game, but the greatest flaws that prevent me from recommending this game relate to the lazy nerfs or implementation of some items and gameplay mechanics.

Many items, like chicken coops and their upgrades, require advanced and superfluous materials (Like padded cloth, ingots, etc, which make no sense for a makeshift chicken coop in the wilderness. Many items follow this logic as an attempt to nerf or increase the mindless grind of the game.

This is the second problem. You gather wood, shoot animals, shoot people, and hunt for loot boxes. There, that's the entire game with little satisfaction coming from building an advanced base. There is no increase in sufficiency, only a greater need for a larger variety of components, many of which can only be acquired from randomly spawning boxes to provide a further lazy attempt to provide a nerf and increase in "difficulty"

Gun play is also very sloppy. Aiming with firearms is worse than hipfiring in Fallout 3 vanilla. Small adjustments are not possible, even with my sensitivity at the lowest setting possible.

This game has wonderful potential, one solution to the prior mentioned superfluous materials could be a tremendously low durability for basic construction with advanced materials allowing greater durability and efficiency.

The hunting can be fun, as all non predatory animals must be tracked. Fishing is enjoyable and building a base is fun for a while but could provide more payoff and satisfaction with greater degrees of sufficiency. The exploration side mixed with the beautiful weather, scenery, wildlife, and wide open spaces with monumental variety is the whipped cream on top of the melted and spoiled ice cream. The game has been lovingly built in many ways, with core gameplay severely neglected, like a red-headed stepchild.

Currently a 4/10, will play but be burnt out on it very quickly. If the materials and looting irregularities are solved, this game could easily be 7/10 or 8/10 while being much more accessible than time consuming games like Rust.\t\t\t\t\t

REVIEW:

Though still in alpha (= exploratory, experimental) stage Subsistence is a very solidly programmed hardcore action-survival game.
With its current content it offers many hours of good game-play.
Its price is fitting its content.

Overall flow of game-play (timing of item availability, character needs to be satisfied) is consistent, progression is slow and hard earned as is to be expected with a hardcore game.

Despite its slow development due to being the passion project of a single developer its currently contained features can keep up with EA-games already in beta-stage and even with some games declared as fully released in the genre.

It offers a solid performance in SP and MP.
It is free of major bugs, and even glitches are uncommon.

If you are really interested to find out if this game is to your liking I would advise watching YT let´s play series.
They show actual game-play, map design, environmental effects, build-mode, AI control in combat (aggressive animals always, humans optional) better than I could explain.
