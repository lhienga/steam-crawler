\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverview:
This game has potential. I played on normal mode with a couple of people and I really wished I introduced a survival game that made sense.

However, I introduced a really crappy reserve that I believe some very sick animal hoarder owns that calls it a sanctuary and feeds the animals steroids.
You do have random backpacks lying about from all the sorry souls that tried to camp at "Scratch and pull your head off Camp" or "Survive the Predators Sanctuary". Eventually, you will have half-naked men that raid your poorly built homestead as if we owe them some taxes: they're really PETA cultists that inhabit the lands of feral creatures surrounding them and have gained their trust.

Gameplay:
You will have to battle countless level 1 bears, wolves, and cougars every 15 - 30 steps to die after one scratch while trying to avoid and sneak around them. Though you can sometimes outrun bears, not wolves (do NOT f with those).
There are lots of wolves that become more of a BA mofo than 50 Cent!
BELIEVE ME!
50-wolf WILL survive AND thrive from multiple shots!
It has more lives than your sorry butt with opposable thumbs, a handy axe, and your gun. On top of the fact, 50-wolf WILL haunt your backpack because 50-wolf just leveled up with more health shielding your backpack like a good pup. Meanwhile, as great ol fiddy is leveling and literally sitting and walking in circles on your backpack to fight you... TIME is not on your side.

Items:
You have a timer on your backpack AND every time you die, your sad butt has to run further into bear, cougar, wolf, and cultist sanctuary to keep dying and drop said backpack(s) that may or may not have all your items.

Pros:
It's easy to make weapons - somehow you can make a gun with some sticks and random pieces of metal laying about, but don't expect it to kill anything.
The map is introvert-friendly - ain't no-one going to mess with your survival game when you spawn so far away and you're surrounded by darkness everywhere!!!

Cons:
Well, I paid for it and have to wait for it to redeem itself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just got the game in 2023 after watching lots of videos by some gaming channels like 'Old Guy Gaming', 'Kitten', 'MittsYT', 'Kage848' (Thanks to all of them. They work so hard for less views and some of them have 6-7 seasons of playing Subsistence). After playing for 150+ hours, here is my review.

I just love the idea of being alone in such a stunningly beautiful world. The world is created using Unreal Engine 3 and the Canadian wilderness is captured beautifully! The lake side area is stunning and so are rivers, waterfalls and snowy mountains. One day I went on a long walk by the river without any objective. The river that empties as a delta into a large lake is a stunning location. I still remember that walk, which gave me a lot of joy. As a wilderness walking simulator, this game is unbeatable!

The way the seasons change is amazing! Fall colors are stunning. Snowy winters are incredible to look at. I have never seen such details of seasons in any survival game. Small details like Bears hibernating in the winter, Strawberries in the summer, Winter berries from December to Feb, Rivers and Lakes freezing over. You can crack open an ice hole and fish from it or wash your hands. That special sunset. Fishing in a lake. All of this adds to the immersion. It's just a special atmosphere of you and the wilderness.

When I first started, I played a test game on 'Easy' with hunters disabled. Easy works for me, as I don't want too many Wolves and Bears - its unrealistic on Normal, while I'm not bothered about Hardcore. Spotting a Wolf or a Bear should maybe happen once a day and must be the highlight of the day. They are as common as bunnies, which is ridiculous. At the start, the tension is real! You need to get going to find stuff and focus on your first camp at night. This game has no psychological horror with strange sounds like in 'The Forest'. That was very important for me.
The pressure to get food - protein and veggies, wood, biofuel in first couple of days is nerve wracking. I chose to build a platform on an Island in the Birchwood lake. Building even a basic 3x3 base will take time. The game is deliberately designed to keep the progression at a slow pace. I kinda like that. I have seen how games like Empyrion Galactic Survival, The Forest etc makes it so easy to create massive buildings. I think the pacing is okay for a wilderness sim like Subsistence. But this may not suit many people. You need to consider this when you play the game. Ideally you would like to build an enclosed base, a wood burner, a refinery, a fridge to store food, a solar panel for power. But all of this takes a lot of time and a lot of grinding - which involves finding loot boxes, hunting, fishing, gathering. When going in the wilderness, you need to practice constant and vigilant situational awareness.

It's a constant exercise, as you spend 90% of the time doing the above mentioned activities. You spend 5% of your time crafting with inventory management and spend the remaining 5% of your time building, if you love building fancy bases. I prefer tiny bases, so building is not important to me.

Apart from the natural outdoor wilderness, the developer has added a very elaborate underground cave system. You can only access the cave system once you manufacture a diving suit and O2 canisters. This is mid game stuff and is fairly rewarding.
The caves and lava caves feel like a different game. On the day of visiting these caves, it feels like you are planning for an excursion. You stock up on food, water, ammunition and you swim to the caves. The caves are a wonderful change and I really enjoy my excursions there.

I love the fact that there are AI teams that will build their own bases. They are the enemy for you as a player. They make some impressive looking bases which you can raid for materials. Planning for a AI base raid is rewarding and when you get back home, it feels like a satisfying day's work. The AI are fairly intelligent in combat, as they keep moving around and take cover while reloading. There is an element of a tactical battle whenever you face them. If you are in the open you will get surrounded by them. You need to use cover and keep moving. One criticism of the AI from the community is the insane accuracy of their aim. Most shots will hit you unless you are behind cover. I enjoy these encounters and I have seen it does get stressful for some players.

I have played a long solo single player game for more than 100 hours and I have finished building a small base, have tons of food. Power, Water are no longer an issue. I have a chicken coop, that gives me all the eggs for protein, so that I don't have to go out and hunt for food. I still visit regularly just to enjoy the atmosphere.
I have also played in a multiplayer server and the network code has been implemented well. I did not face any lags or issues so far. The chat window is implemented well too. This game is perfect for multiplayer servers to play with friends.

I wanted to know more about the sole developer's thought process, I went through the updates in Steam from 2017 to the latest Alpha 61 update. I was pleasantly surprised. The release notes are filled with images, functional and technical details that are very interesting and informative. The amount of detail on every feature is documented well. I really feel that features are developed with so much thought and creativity. The developer does make some mistakes but has been rectifying them with fixes.

I think the Seasons update of Alpha 50 was the developer's most impressive update. Every type of foliage and tree visually transitioning through the various seasons just blew my mind. I have not seen such a weather system in other survival games.
But there have been some updates like Alpha 60, which have not been well received. The update adds an ability to manufacture an ATV. There has been criticism about the need to manufacture a vehicle. The point of subsisting in the Canadian wilderness is to enjoy nature. The last thing we need is a head ache inducing noisy petrol powered engine. An electric ATV or a Kayak or an electric bike would have been better. I have not bothered to work on the ATV as I don't want noise! Alpha 61 - The latest patch has been much better, as the developer added a water pumping system and lots of QOF features.

There is no modding support in this game. There are no server customization features too.
I wish, users could upload blueprints of bases, structures and other items to the steam workshop. Other users can spawn them in game at a cost. You want a simple 3x3 base? Don't want to build? Pay up and import them from the workshop. This saves a lot of time. All of this is not possible.
Also you cannot change the gameplay balance with server customization. If you want a single whack of the axe to fetch you 20 wood instead of 3, you cannot do it.
I feel by not having modding and an active workshop, the developer is stifling the growth of the game.

With a name like Subsistence you would expect that living off the land would be more farming orientated with elements of hunting. Having actual farming plots rather than a simple small plant beds would have been preferred. Unfortunately we are not sure if Farming is even part of the developers vision, as there is no communication about future plans.
I hope the developer can lay out his vision for the next 2 years, so we get some idea on where this game is going. That kind of communication may also help in sales for him.

Nevertheless, I must say, this game is truly a labour of love. And my respect for the developer is immense.

I recommend this game, because I enjoy it and when in game, I'm transported to the Canadian wilderness and time just flies! However this game is not meant for the impatient types who want action all the time. If you learn the basics and watch some videos online, you will start enjoying this unique wilderness simulator called Subsistence!\t\t\t\t\t
