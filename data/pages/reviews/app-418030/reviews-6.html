\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I read the squeaker negative reviews on this game and a lot talked about how it is too hard. LOL. I love it that is why I got it. It is obvious that the squeakers who neg review anything they have to work at did not realize that there is a option to make the game stupid easy if need be. Don't get me wrong it is still brutal and very challenging but, the game gives you help to delete it. But be warned you will starve, dehydrate, and building is the most realistic as far as needing to gather what you need I have ever seen in any survival game and I have a few. You can turn off hunters (recommend doing this first time you play) the animals and elements are bad enough to challenge you. Avoid contact with violent animals unless you have real weapons a ax will not cut it in most cases a bear will kill you faster than Timothy Treadwell. Wolves are no joke either. I try to avoid them which is not to hard. Every thing is a challenge in this game so once you master the basics turn on the hunters and if you are really brave go for the brutal setting (good luck with that). This game still has some work to be done but at the entry level it is better than most other survival games out there in many ways. I am sure they will improve as time goes on. I do hope they make trees fall and stay spawned out for a bit after you cut them down but, other than that I have no other recommendations at this time. I love the challenge this game offers and if you don't know how to accept learning to overcome things and accept that you will die a lot. This game probably is not for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game just isn't what I expected. Loot seems very luck oriented making grinding for materials a ridiculous process. It takes 6+ headshots with a 9mm to kill a wolf which is not very realistic. I shot a deer in the head TWICE with a bow and it just ran away and reset it's health within a few seconds. Chickens are apparently the smartest and most evasive creatures in the world according to the devs. Even if you crouch walk over to it it'll take off faster than almost any other animal in the game making getting feathers nearly impossible. Why have a base if you have to spend several days at a time out away from your base hoping you find somewhat decent loot just to upgrade it a little bit? I had a bear glitch it's way up the face of a mountain just to one shot me while I was warming up next to a fire at night away from everything. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot really a survival game as your ability to survive is dependent on RNG loot boxes dropping the materials you need to craft your survival gear. No starting tools or clothes with the resources you can gather. The absurd crafting requirements are the only challenge which almost feels like a time-gate to slow progression and is totally dependent on loot boxes. Starting out you have little to no ability to defend yourself from the wildlife. The density of the spawns combined with the RNG crafting materials doesn't strike me as good game design. I bought it on sale for $10 so I guess it was worth the money, but barely. If you are looking for a hardcore survival game, probably look elsewhere. RNG is the only thing hardcore or brutal.

I get that it's early access but if you are going to take money from folks, you should be able to take the criticism too.\t\t\t\t\t
