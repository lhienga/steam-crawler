As alluded to above, SUBSISTENCE is a grinding survival game that, in my opinion, is intelligently balanced in terms of risk-reward.
There is only one world/map.
You start in a random location with not even a shirt on your back.
Hunt
Forage
Build luxuries
Explore
The game immersion is excellent. After hundreds of hours of gameplay, I still get adrenalin rushes when a wolf stalks up behind me and pounces.

https://steamcommunity.com/app/418030/discussions/0/3223871682622163648/
The above link has a post by Zuleica about 3 or 4 replies down that is one of the best I've read for early tips-n-tricks.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNeeds a LOT of work, even on the basics.

After finally finding enough materials to craft a bow and a couple arrows. The Fiber, which is very difficult to spot, and feathers which had to be found in boxes because you can't hunt birds without already having the bow and arrow. I decided to test it out on a lvl2 wolf. The first arrow went into the wind because of the unintuitive control, and wonky aim of the bow. Second shot, I managed to shoot the wolf directly in the face (because it was about 5ft away at this point, and rather angry about my existence) for next to no damage, the wolf then proceeded to maul me in about two bites. These wolves are also everywhere along with bears, making navigating the map rather cumbersome.

I also attempted to start a campfire, when night hit and the temperature dropped. It requires a fire starting kit, which needs biofuel to be lit... a campfire should be the ultimately most basic element of any survival game, it shouldn't take anything more than what you can pick up off of the ground to start.

Also, an other buddy of mine was playing separately, and he managed to make a pistol before he could make a bow (which in and of itself is wacky) had a similar idea with a wolf that ended about the same. Aim seems to work proper on the pistols at least, though the wolf took three shots to the face and still mauled him.

Early game struggle is a thing in survival games, but this just doesn't really feel proportionate in its current state.\t\t\t\t\t
