\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely fantastic job for a sole developer, well done!

This game is one of the hardest ones to survive [based on my personal experience], so the reasons of people hating [or just not liking] it are quite clear.
- Every resource is scarce, every bullet is valuable, and punishing for each mistake is very painful.
- Getting resources (plus energy and base mass) for a device, upgrade, or sometimes even for a base bit, and finally installing it makes you feeling like a true hero.
- Killing a single predator is not that easy, and hunters are absolute kicka...s!
- Not to mention that hunting for particular resources, like sinew, is the whole quest itself...

BUT this is exactly what makes it different from all other survival games [based on my personal experience].
Here you have to learn how to survive step by step, each time paying a high price for your next piece of experience:
- How to move slowly and carefully, only allowing yourself to sprint rather short distances.
How to kill wolves and deers using just arrows, avoiding temptation to make it quick and lose a couple of bullets.
- How to use structures to hunt bears.
- How to keep your food indicators at not maximum, but rather sufficient, level.
- How to focus only on what's needed avoiding the rest...
- Well, eventually, how not to rush

Yes, slow, and yes, grinding, but all your efforts are rewarding at the end of the day.
Just learn how to play it instead of rushing, and you'll have a lot of fun!

A bit about other beautiful aspects:
- Lovely graphics
- Seasons changing, with not just the temperature and weather, but the overall landscape changes! I like that the developer is paying attention to small details
- Beautiful sky effects
- Winter fishing
...to cut it short, if you like the game, you will continue the list by yourself :)

(just for reference and fair judgement of the above, I played 7D2D, ARK, Empyrion, The Forest, Conan Exiles, Green Hell, Valheim)\t\t\t\t\t
