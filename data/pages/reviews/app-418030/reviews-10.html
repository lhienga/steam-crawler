\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMaybe I didn't give this a full chance. But at just shy of 2 hours, I'm ready to call it.

1. Like Taft, I was eaten by wolves. Okay, great. So I respawn without any of my stuff...a full km away. That's obnoxious. I get back to my corpse to gather up my very valuable collection of plant fiber, and that same wolf is still sitting there. Leveled up, based on what the respawn crawl tells me. That wolf then chases me into the water, swimming faster than me, and has seconds. I respawn 1.3 km away this time. Okay, I'll abandon the run.

2. I'm doing all my wood chopping and fiber gathering (and it really bothers me that the tree still just stays there, totally unaltered, after I "chop" 3-4 logs out of it). It gets to nighttime and I finally have enough stuff to make a fire. Great! Heat through the night. But I can't sleep. Like, it's not a function. Well, uh, I guess I'll just sit and craft all these planks some more because the crafting time is not at all quick. Fine. But now I'm hungry. I'm able to craft a bow with a pair of arrows, and to my surprise, a chicken has spawned with the morning sun. Great! But Legolas I am not. I spend a stupid amount of time shooting, missing, and collecting arrows. Desperate for protein, I finally draw my starting 9mm pistol (with one bullet). Having honed my shooting skills long ago in Unreal Tournament, I easily put down that chicken in one shot. Turns out that was a M-m-m-m-m-monster kill, as it dropped a chicken corpse "mutilated by gunfire" and was totally inedible, cookable, or anything besides a droppable waste. When sharing this story with a friend, his only response was, "All of my pheasant hunting has been a lie."

3. I realize now why you start a game with a 9mm pistol with one bullet. A courtesy for a gentleman. Refund requested.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't waste your time with Subsistence.

- Content is very limited and out dated.
- End game is very quick.
- Bugs and crashes are non stop.
- The grind outweighs the satisfaction.
- Dead servers.
- Very un-balanced content and features.
- Limited character inventory slots.
- No communication or feedback from developer.

Subsistence was an OK game a few years ago. However, the developer stopped listening to the needs of customers, doesn't offer new content and only communicates with players when they need servers restarted....which is an every day thing. You might enjoy Subsistence for a day or two. However, it'll end up sitting on the shelf after a short period of time. You might re-install it to check out the latest update (Rare). However, with updates being a consistent disappointment, you'll end up uninstalling again. There are way better survival games out there. Don't waste your money with this one.\t\t\t\t\t
