\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like grinding? Only buy if you can say yes.

How about dying within seconds of starting if you are unlucky and/or just new to this kind of game?

Not frustrated by making through your first night only to die of starvation or get rabies from a wolf bite a bit after dawn?

That said while its a bit tough at the start, it eases up once you get a few essentials or know what to prioritize. Then the toughness is based on grinding/collecting enough to survive winter. What you chose the year length and whether you play with hunters will decide how tough that actually is. I played without hunters for quite a while. You have to grind A LOT more without hunter bases to raid. Finding locked boxes is essential to progression without hunters.

The variety of predators and prey is on the small side though still broader than several games I can think of currently. Far too many wolves and bears... though the wolves become a good source of protein. The predators do give a warning growl provided you are not running directly at them, if you back away fast you won;t be pursued. If they growl twice you had best run or be prepared to attack.

There are lots of resources, but initially it felt like playing 'Where's Waldo?' with the fiber plants. Their dull foliage makes them harder to find when you are in the field/wood sections, but once you start noticing them you'll keep noticing them. In rocky terrain or on the shore lines they stand out far more easily.

The game requires you to balance your diet (to an extent) and eat plants and protein. Early on protein can be a problem. You'll want a bow and arrows ASAP as fighting with your ax means you risk damage and rabies being up close and personal. Catching rabbits on foot is near impossible. You can catch chickens that way but they tend to lead you into the path of a predator if you run after them without paying attention. You do have a gun but you'll want to hoard those bullets initially; especially if you are playing with hunters as you'll want the better weapon for dealing with them.

You can raise chickens and rabbits but I'm not sure it's worth the fuss early on when there are are things to focus your time on. Once you have several garden boxes going, (preferably with tomatoes), and upgrade the coops to let the animals be free range it's far easier to take care of animals while still progressing in other areas.

You have to be aware of the temperature which is impacted by the time of day, time of year, altitude, clothing, weather, whether you are wet, your proximity to a fire, etc.

There are caves you can dive to as well.\t\t\t\t\t

Its fun to mess around on - but the crafting is bad and confusing - loosing your items if you loot one of your two bodies is probably the stupidest thing I have mentioned thus far. About the only good thing I can think to say about the game is the map - its great and really fun to explore.
