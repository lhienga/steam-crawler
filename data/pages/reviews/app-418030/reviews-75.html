\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo didn't spend a whole lot of time but this was a pretty rough game.

To start, spawning on all normal difficulty you are pretty much dying to animals constantly. Not only that, it is extremely hard to get resources you need to get any sort of progress. Sure you might find some loot crates with a few resources, but when you need like 7 crates to build a bow or what not its very frustrating.

Fiber is hard to find. I mean pointlessly hard. There are tiny trees and bushes all over the map but the only thing you can get fiber from is the tiny saplings that are rare to find. And then to craft something like a bow you can use fiber but its like 18 fiber for a single bow.

Nails, the stuff to build a base at the start is using nails. You can only find nails in loot-crates so far. Its barely enough nails after several crates to build a SINGLE foundation of your base.

Overall, way too rough and poorly done as it is. I had high hopes but I guess I will wait for it to polish a bit more.\t\t\t\t\t

I bow before the single developer who is making this game. I did not found any bugs at all.
A lot of crafting options, Superb base building. Battles with hunters are unforgiving. Animal AI is decent.
Can't wait for future updates as it is still in alpha.
Recommended for everyone who likes Valheim, is tired of the infinite bugs in SCUM and likes the open world survival games genre. Community is very nice (despite the amount of Russians on some servers).

This game has very high potential to be one of the best open world survival games once fully released.

=============================SUMMARY===============================

There is something unique and absolute epic about Subsistence. It is hard to find the exact word to describe this super hardcore, ultimate, unforgiving adventure, and one the most complete ultimate survival games out there! There must be no doubt by anybody by now who has put into some decent amount of hours into this very unique and precious diamond, -> the developer had a lot of love for this project. After about 350 hours with this game I am pretty confident to say so.


A GREAT ADVENTURE!

So you start out quite ordinary as you kind of could expect from any survival game. A pretty big map, with beautiful landscapes and breathtaking weather-phenomenon with a complex lunar cycle system all synchronising with the sun each month of the year. From the stunning sunsets to the wide variety of beautiful landscape and faunas, and complex weather system that makes you forced to adapt to different playstyles. When I stand on my roof top watching out over the lake, the view on my 27" 2k with a GPU 1070 and a Ryzen 3600, It sure do the job for me!

Susbsistence is time-consuming, the effort you have to put in to build big if you play solo like me, means you will need to put a lot of time in collecting resources. The grinding aspect to it is very real and you would need to put in some serious effort to be able to stay even with all the tasks that is demanded of you, along with a lot of other things, like hunting or just staying alive. Subsistence can be very unforgiving and brutal, and it demands the player to always be prepared and be smart about how they adapt. If you like survival games, this is an absolute 100% must buy for you.


OVERALL

Positive:
- Can be very challenging and unforgiving, you are basically thrown in to a brutal environment with nothing but an axe out in the wild.
- Lots of details in the landscape, there are different areas with different faunas,
- The visual graphics and overall looking is absolute great!
- Complex building management system with endless possibilities - you can build on every foundation you see. A rock outside in the ocean? Sure. In a cliff side -Why not? Or how about a plain an old school hunter cabin? This is from early access and already a solid foundation. The potential is high,
- Very addictive. the environment and the whole atmosphere is some type of one-of-a-type.
- The Movement feeling and how it synchronise well with the keyboard. Swift and sound, Although I wonder how they would implement it to work with any game-controller. How about fully open configuration for the users?
- If you like games such as Valheim, Scum, The Forrest, The Infected, Stranded Deep or any other in the survival category I think you already know by now that this game should be a must buy for you.

Negative:
- I find it hard to criticise anything with this game at all, as it still is early access and in Alpha. However I would like to point out some factors that I would like to see the Dev focus on:

1: - Not enough cloths, need more variety with cloths, such as a range of helmets, shirts, caps & hats, we need shorts(!) and some different trousers in the crafting menu. And more furnatures and basic stuff for living!
2: - The game is maybe a bit hard on easy. My humble suggestion would be, if set on easy - give the character double amount of wood, feather, more cotton in the suitcases and maybe an overall adjustment in how much loot you get in overall if set on easy. Even better would be a dynamic over view of several adjustments from the main-menu.
3: - Wish there was a alternate way to get wood.
4: - More wildlife - and maybe an area with crocodiles or something to make it more intense and to give more variety.
5: - Even deeper and more complex crafting system.
6: - Even more brutal weather phenomenon

At the end of the day this is a matter of what type of games you like. Subsistence is pretty hardcore, so if you never tried a Survival before? Not a problem. Go grab a copy of Subsistence and you will understand what I talk about, It is an absolute AMAZING game and YES I would recommend it to anybody who likes the Survival-genre!

=============================SUMMARY END===============================

Now if you are a REAL adventurer LIKE ME you go on just like me, out and about, for days and months, for months and years. Unfortunately, 7 years in to my adventure, I found my drowned wife at the south-side by the lake after a heavy rain storm left no hope for a lost soul lost out in the wild. Devastated when I found her I prepared for one last dive. Out of subsistence, but by the divine spirits, my story will for ever live on.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very hard even on easy mode with no hunters...You are constantly hungry. You have no clothes except for a pair of pants. You get a single bullet for the 9mm that you start with and as soon as you install your BCU you will lose said gun on death. Make sure that you make a gun asap. There are no in-game journals or guides. You cannot mark locations on your map so you must remember exactly where your stuff is if you decide to save it in a chest so that you do not drop it all on death. You die a lot! Make sure you save the game often so that if you do die you can easily reload from your last save point. I save every time I collect a chest, ore, kill, etc, I do not recommend using the auto save feature. You only get one save game per profile and an auto save on death will override your personal saved game. You do have the option of turning on a beacon so that you can easily find your base once you install your BCU but beware...If you have hunters turned on they will begin spawning in as soon as you place your BCU. Your plant beds and your animal housing use a lot of water, fertilizer and food so make sure to stock up on ash in the beginning and upgrade your animal housing to collect feces for fertilizer. I am making due with two animal houses and two furnaces for fertilizer. I recommend at least 3 plant beds with tomatoes for maximum use with your animal houses..My initial review was not favorable but after playing the game for awhile now I would give this game a 3.5 rating. I have a few ideas that the developer could use to improve the game in my opinion. Feel Free to ask DEV...\t\t\t\t\t
