\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI play A LOT of survival games. I've read a lot of the reviews for this game and I think that most of the negative ones are unjust. This game isn't forgiving. This game isn't easy, even on easy! If you want a survival game that challenges you...this is it. It isn't like most other survival games where 20 pieces of wood builds you a shelter and gets you a storage chest. Every game day you will have to harvest wood..learn to use A and D and skirt around the tree while you chop. Doing that will give you the ability to see if there are things you need to collect i.e. Chests..Metal..Veg..etc. Don't think you are going to be able to take on the wolves or bears early on in the game. You will fail..you will die! While there is no tutorial if you have played other survival games you will know what to do. If you need help the community page can be a life saver! I highly recommend you start on easy..hunters off..May or June..AT LEAST 60 days. I got to 8 game hours..had two floor pieces one wall(no ceiling. Rain did not put my fire out) and one chest. I died AGAIN (about 7 deaths at this point). I almost broke and gave up. After some encouraging words from my husband I started again, ran across the map, got my things back and continued. I slowed down. Focused on what I would if I really was in this situation. The game got easier. My stats stay mostly in the green from 9 hours on(I am over 24 hours in now). I have died a couple more times...My fault, didn't hear that damn bear! This is a great game. You can really take pride when you accomplish ANYTHING! You work hard for that piece of floor. Nothing is handed to you, this game is not one to coddle you. If you have the patience and the will you will love this game. I highly recommend this game to any survival lover. The only advice I have is FIBER at all costs and chop that freaking wood and take your time...If I can do this game and feel fun and accomplishment so can you!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOf all survival games I've played this is the least realistic one, full of artificial bottlenecks and farfetched mechanics that are merely there to hinder the player's progress. It is simply not fun to play. And for me the main problem is not even the grind, but the bizarre reasoning behind the grind. There are a lot of "what the..." and "why on earth" moments in the game, and one of the biggest ones is the mass requirement for crafting, along with mass fabrication. What exactly is mass and why is it there? How can I combine metal scrap, wooden planks and some circuit boards to make a device that converts energy to mass, when I'm struggling to generate enough energy to power a smelter? I cannot even make nails to finish my basic 4x4 hut or save enough fiber for a new pair of pants, and you're talking of energy-mass conversion? And what is the purpose of this resource? Is it there so that I would need to craft two generators, a mass fabricator and a couple of energy/mass storages before being able to craft such an OP item as fridge? This is mind-boggling. I don't mind the grind, but only when it makes sense. Farming fiber for cordage makes sense. Even farming cougars for sinew is acceptable, with some reservations. Mass fabrication just doesn't make any sense at all.

I don't know where this game is going and what it will be like when/if it leaves EA, but at this current stage I have absolutely no desire to play it further. Waste of time and money.\t\t\t\t\t
