\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI almost exclusively play survival games. I really enjoy them. I saw a YouTube video almost by mistake and bought Subsistence right away. I saw the difficulty from the video and that the amount of materials was pretty laughable. I figured this would be tweaked during Early Access. I wish I noticed how long this game has actually been out before I bought it. Over 5 1/2 years and so many basic things are wrong with this game not to mention it is STILL in Early Access more than 5 years after being released. I knew I made a mistake as soon as I noticed it.

Other reviewers are spot on about the crates. You will rely primarily on crates to give you what you need to survive and they are NOT reliable. you might find 3 within 1 minute and then don't find another for 1 or 2 game days. You need matches to light your fire (and you will need to light A LOT of fires. If you can't find a crate that has matches (rare) then your forced to craft a fire starter kit that requires bio-fuel which is also found in crates early game. Why in the hell would I need bio-fuel???? People have been starting fires with friction for....well since the dawn of man (also a survival game).

Chop down a tree = 1-6 wood (tree specific) 3 is most common
1 wood crafts to 2 stick....HUH?
So I guess cutting a tree down in my back yard in Subsistence is only worth at max...12 sticks (6 most common)

You need to maintain protein, vegetables, and water to survive. Vegetables are pretty much found in crates early game. I found 1 onion on the ground 1 time in 9 hours of actual play time, picked it and I was rewarded with 1 seed. Didn't even give me the frickin onion. I imagine if you want to survive then you'll have to get some seeds and get them planted pretty early in the game. The most reliable solution I found is to swim for kelp and eat that. Protein can be a significant challenge. You will find yourself chasing chickens to catch them. Be careful as the chickens (and bunnies) like to run you right into a wolf or bear which is never very far as they are immensely overpopulated (watch your surroundings more than you watch your intended dinner). Once you craft a bow to make this easier you will use up more arrows than you farm feathers and nails to make them so there's that. Lets not even mention you need to eat 6 cooked chicken or bunnies to fill you up ... crap i mentioned it (I feel like I need to be bulimic in this game.) An arrow is consumed every time you hit your target so that's another irritant. Not sure why i can't reuse arrows 3ish times with a random break now and then. Kill small game with a gun and ruin the kill and make it unusable. I can understand this concept but this should be random as all bullet kills will not ALWAYS ruin the kill. Kill a wolf and you don't always get meat, you get their liver and fat. It seems to be random and some of these mutant wolves will have more than 1 liver but no meat. The liver is used to make medicine related stuff or can be cooked and eaten while the fat is used later game to make bio-fuel (not sure of other uses yet). Why don't you get meat every time devs?

I could go on and on with the lack of survival basics and survival related things that just don't make sense about this game. I can even support questionable things to make more of a challenge but cmon lets keep things within the realm of possibility. I will play it some more as I do like survival and I do like a challenge but in all honesty I feel like I wasted $11 by buying this game. Hopefully I can find a way to survive as a solo survivalist.

Addition to my review

After some trial and error, Crates have become a little easier to find. You will have to be VERY adventurous and cover a lot of ground everyday to locate any amount of them but they are random so you may find 8 of them one day and 1 or none the next. They spawn daily so revisiting areas is a must. Remember you HAVE to find crates to survive. The more the better.

Get a bow then shotgun made asap to protect yourself from dangers. Hunters early game are a waste of resources since they seem to be abundantly stocked with ammo and fire grenades and multiple guns but certainly don't yield these supplies when looting. For gods sake do NOT fight them in the open, stay behind cover and keep moving. They can be quite challenging. Don't do like me and go into a fight with 6 or 8 bullets and a handgun thinking your going to take them like paid sex. This (more times than not) will not be enough. Oh and I setup the game for revenge attacks only...that is utter BS. If your within a country mile sight distance of these hunters that have better eyesight than any wolf (mutant or otherwise) in existence, their coming for you. I have even had them at my base when I return when there is not a hunter base close to mine. That is probably the most irritating to me of all this games flaws, that and the 14 or 15 minute timer to get back to your corpse when you die starts upon death... not re-spawn. I walked away upon death to eat and go use the bathroom and corpse was gone when I returned. Learned it the hard way when I was finally doing pretty good so I lost a lot.

I am mostly enjoying the game but the survival basics are still a joke and not realistic. I still would not buy this game again when there are so many choices for a more realistic and more importantly... enjoyable survival experience.

Last edition

So I have pointed all these things that make this game just not worth it and ya know it really isn't fair to anyone involved in actually designing this game so I would like to point out some things this game really does do well.

The sound effects of the game really are quite good. The storms, lightning, rain, and wind effects really are quite good. I love storms and rain...I sleep like a baby and after I got through the initial frustration of the mechanics and lack of realism of the game I am really able to enjoy those effects. Animal sound effects as well are just good. It would be nice to have more than 1 sound effect for each animal but its pretty spot on what's been done IMO.

The graphics and visual effects are also done well and the game runs really good on my intel i7 with 16gb memory and 1080TI graphics card with all settings maxed. I have had some weird crashes but its not unbearable and I always expect some of that in early access games. Even while swimming things are smooth and pretty realistic for me.

I have not progressed to mid game I don't believe but what I have experienced after getting my base started and BCU placed is a nice set of options to work to give me a sense of accomplishment. I'm referring to things such as the wood stove, power generator, oven, fridge, crafting table, etc. I have not crafted all of these yet but it helps to look forward to it and give it a purpose.

Visuals like blood when chopping up your dinner then having to wash your hands of blood to keep infection away are a nice realistic touch and I think related visuals and sound effects are fantastic. Sounds in general when firing a gun, bow, washing your hands, using one of the crafting stations, swimming, being out of breath from exhaustion, or really just any sound effects are realistic and add significantly to the enjoyment of the game with the exception of chopping a tree, it sounds cheap and lacking. The devs really have put a lot of work into most effects and it shows.

There are other things this Subsistence does really well but in the essence of not making a novel here I will just say I remain firm in my recommendation of not purchasing. The game gets easier and the devs are still active even if content releases are months between. Good luck and if you have $10 or $11 you don't care about when its on sale, give it a try. I've done my best prepare you for what awaits. Many people love it.\t\t\t\t\t
