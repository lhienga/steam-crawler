\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've seen this game played for a few years on different Youtube videos. I've always liked it, especially since I mostly play open-world/survival style games (7DTD, Projoect Zomboid, Subnautica, ARK, The Forest, etc.), but it honestly seemed quite difficult, and like everybody else, I really didn't need another new game to add to my 50 other new games I still haven't played yet.

But, I recently went ahead and bought it (I've been watching Youtuber GameEdged and it just looked too fun to pass up), and I absolutely love it. It's definitely difficult, which is actually a very welcomed relief. Some of these styles games are a bit too easy, especially at first, and pretty much hold your hand through everything. This game pretty much says 'here you go, dropped in the middle of the woods with an axe, have fun!'

I'm about 56 hours in right now, and I think I've died a total of 7 times so far. You will die, it's going to happen, so don't get upset about it. Also, it requires a lot of grinding; you get to a point where you think you're rich with supplies and stocked up, but then you start building and suddenly you're supplies are back down to nothing. Again though, that makes this game different, and fun.

Also, since this is my first playthrough, GameEdged mentioned in some of his videos that if it gets too difficult, you can actually turn off the Hunters, or just adjust their behavior. I have turned them off at times, and then turned them on again later, after I had a more secure base, and was better set up to fight them. Then, if it gets difficult again, you can turn them off and back on, and they reset and start building from scratch again. Also, in his gameplays he adjusts the season lengths, and I did as well, setting it to 90.

Being able to adjust the Hunters on the fly like that helped during my first gameplay, but the problem I discovered is the lack of higher quality loot. With no Hunters, the high quality loot you need to progress, like Gun Parts and Gears, only appears in locked crates. It also appears on Hunters, and in their bases, but with Hunters turned off, they're more scarce being on in locked crates.

Overall, this game is awesome. I find myself playing for hours and then saying 'ok, one more loop around to check for crates, then I'm done. Ok, well, one more loop, and maybe a few trees for logs. All right, maybe a loop around that other area... whatever, I'll play another hour or two.' This game is addicting, in a very fun way.

My final TL:DR is this game is amazing. I play a lot of these style games, and Subsistence is in a category of its own, in a good way. Yes, you grind, but it's fun, not boring. Yes, it's difficult; the lack of supplies/bullets make inventory management important. Yes, the weather, animals, hunters and lack of food/water will kill you, but just sprint back to your pack (setting the bed/spawn later in game helps a ton!) and keep at it. I haven't played this game Multi-player yet, but I think that would be even more fun.

If you get the chance, purchase this game. You will like it. It's a very small team, I even think one person, Cold Games (he responds quite a bit here on Steam), and I like to support talent and skill whenever I can. Also, he's constantly improving the game, with Update 60 coming soon, adding huge new things like 4-wheelers and more.\t\t\t\t\t
