\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this little beauty.

Being quite a fan of survival games I bought it a long time ago but being hooked to other games at that time, I actually never installed it.
After some recent bad multiplayer experiences, needing an escape, I scrolled through my survival list and rediscovered this one.

It was just what I needed.

Playing generally in sissy mode, I tried this game out a first time on easy mode without hunters enabled.
I died a lot the first in-game days which is quite normal in a new game but nonetheless triggered some negativity in me. Then I got a little bit frustrated not finding enough nails and fibers and then I got frustrated building was so expensive and thinking stuff like "how the hell will I ever get enough cotton/sinew/bla bla bla"...

But I moved on - as most people do even in hard times - while discovering nice thunderstorms wondering 'can I get struck', bees, autumn, snow blizzards and frozen lakes enjoying the cracking sound walking on them wondering if it could crack and I would freeze to death...
I enjoyed nice touches like how the sound changes when cooking meat on the wood burner, the pity I had hearing the little peep of my first rabbit I picked up, blood where you killed an animal...

Choosing what to craft first was often a hard choice but making my first of something felt actually really rewarding, except for my bed which I destroyed later to recover some materials to craft a more necessary thing. The diving tank could maybe hold a bit more air too. Oh yeah, I also destroyed my animal housing at a time when it felt just better to eat my food myself as it looked as if that chicken laid one egg a year :).

I must admit, although I was escaping from multiplayer issues, at a certain point, I kinda missed more action.
I got a little bit bored and even didn't go into the caves.
So I decided to try the game with hunters on (still on sissy mode of course).

I died single shot while hitting a tree. Later, I found out I was very close to their wooden base.
It really changed gameplay for me. In the beginning I only dared to chop the trees right next to my little cabin :).

On local gameplay, you can have 5 profiles. So you can easily start and switch between several games.
You can choose easy, normal and hardcore mode, the month in which you start, the year cycle length and if you want hunters or not and their attack mode when enabled. Controls can be mapped, you can scroll through your weapons and tools. Character looks cannot be adjusted.
You can play co-op with friends or join or rent a dedicated server (looking empty at this moment though).

It's true there isn't super much content in this game but what there is is quite well thought of (and still being worked on by the way).
I don't mind really. We've been running a Rust server for two years. I actually got sick of it in the end (8k hours). Finally, I only really enjoyed wipe day, the run for the base spot (they even took away the excitement finding that garage door). Even Empyrion, having a lot of content on a server with Reforged Eden and custom mini games, I had a 'ah, seen it, done that' my second start after the recent big update...

I take a game for what it is. There is enough stuff out there to suit several gameplay needs. It would be a pity if they all had the same content. I can quite easily ignore negative comments on that matter.

So, if you can accept a game as it is and you like to discover a new game, this can be your next thing.

Edit: I just started Keep up Survival after this review. It reminded me how sometimes one takes things for granted. Now, after having a buggy control settings, resolution issues, weird (let not say ugly) graphics, terrible swimming animation, bumpy walking animation, bad sound effects, hill issues leading to death and less pleasant user interface (...) in only a couple of minutes, I feel obliged to emphasize how great this all is in Subsistence.
The developer did great work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA recommendation with an asterisk.

The survival aspects of this game are good. Running burns more calories than walking so you can't (shouldn't) sprint everywhere. Building is good, you can move pretty much everything after placing it, so you don't need to worry about wasting resources.

There are a lot of problems to bear in mind before purchasing.

There is no proper implementation of save slots. You get one, and that's for autosaves and manual saves.

The power/mass system is needlessly tedious. Everything you craft in your base takes power and mass, which verrrrry slowly regenerate. So it doesn't matter if you've got the resources to make something, you need to wait till your base generates enough power/mass. It feels like a mechanic you'd get in a pay to win MMORPG: "you can wait for the magical resource to regenerate over time, or you can pay extra to get it now!"

Combat is very wonky, especially with hunters. They seem to have aim hacking enabled as they can snipe you with a pistol from extreme range. Hunters in general seem very cheaty. They spawn and day 1 they'll have end game armour, rifles, etc. Things they couldn't have possibly found the resources to make or the workshop to make them.

Equipment durability is well balanced, it doesn't feel too harsh. Food spoiling however isn't, food spoils in this game quicker (much quicker) than it would in real life. Hunt a wolf in the morning? Well by the time you get home in the evening the meat will have spoiled lol. The problem is really showcased with traps. If you only check your traps once per day then you'll only ever find spoiled meat inside them.

However even with that in mind it's certainly got potential, and I'm keen to see where it goes.

Edit: You can work around the hunter balance silliness by manually controlling in the options whether they spawn or not. I've started to "ban them" from my game whenever they start impossibly spawning with endgame gear on day 1 by just turning them off in the settings. Then after 10 in game days or so, the "ban" lifts and they can rejoin, until they start cheating again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSubsistence is overly simple with horrible game balance. The number of predators around is enough to suggest that the bears and wolves have teamed up to slaughter you and you alone. The garbage AI makes it feel like the chickens and rabbits have done the same. Finding even barebones materials is up to RNG a lot of the time. And getting rained on early in a game can mean freezing to death three times in a row due to weather you don't have a console to change. (Fires can't be built without a roof in these conditions, and roofs need nails which are only found by chance in boxes.)

Combine this with the fact that you can shoot a rabbit in the face with a 9mm handgun, and it will run off like you hissed in its direction. It takes like 7 or 8 shots to take down a wolf as well. There are no body part modifiers. And animals frequently glitch or spawn in strange places. It's just not very fun at all especially where hunting/combat is concerned.

I think the survival box mechanic is ultimately flawed. It's just not fun to have to run for two hours up and down a beach, just to find enough nails to build a deck. Sure, you can forge them later on, but not at any respectable rate. The grind in this game is way too high given the simplicity of its mechanics. There are only so many times that I'm going to want to strain my eyes for fiber, or unlock a box hoping for nails.

Still, if you have a stretch of good days in-game, it can be somewhat enjoyable. The base-building is relatively strong and has a neat aesthetic to it. The hunters provide a nice bit of challenge and for what the game is there are a decent amount of weapon options. But given the number of other survival sandbox games out there, that far exceed this one in terms of quality, I'd say save your money.\t\t\t\t\t
