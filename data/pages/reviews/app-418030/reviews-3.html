\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe name is accurate, and if you like frustrating challenges, then by all means get the game.

For me, it is unreasonably difficult. I enjoy challenging games, but not hopeless, luck requiring games. On Easy mode, on a dedicated server, after 5 hours of game play, I can't leave the cave I found to build a base in. At night, I'm told I am 'too cold' if I venture out, and when I do, my character lasts about 30 to 45 seconds. In the winter, I can barelly survive in shelter. After struggling with my gaming partner, we have 4 foundation pieces, 1 wall, 1 base controller and 2 boxes. The animal population is unrealistic. Most of the predators in game are territorial with territories of 10's of square miles, and will defend their territory, not allowing other predators to exist there. The cougars and wolves especially are too densely populous.

The opportunities for food scavenging seem to be non-existant. To build a fire you need not only fuel, but a fire-starting kit that has a single use. If you are lucky enough to find a storage box, you will usually lose whatever you've found because you drop everything on death and are spawned in a long distance from your death location. Harvesting is a huge grind. Three logs from an entire pine tree is the average. Three to five fibers from a clump and two to three medicinal herbs per cluster is also average, and the plants to obtain these are few and far between.

There's no way to tweak settings other than the difficulty mode, year length and enemy proliferation.

I play other survival games, many of them challenging (older versions of 7 Days to Die, Conan Exiles, The Forest) and have not been so discouraged that I wanted to stop playing them. I don't enjoy this game and will likely not play it much unless there is significant balancing done.\t\t\t\t\t
