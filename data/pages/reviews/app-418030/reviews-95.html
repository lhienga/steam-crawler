To be honest I bought this game with doubts in my heart, because I've read some negative recommendation which was complaining about how hard , grindy and unfair the game is so its not really worth paying for. For the first sight it may also seem that the game is easy and doesn't have really much content to offer, therefore the game experience can be quite poor, but that's not true.
The game has a different approach of the survival and building concept which I was used to. (I played with 7daystodie, Minecraft, Deadside, SpaceEngineers, Dont Starve Together) So the game itself like every other survival game has its flaws and beauties, but in my opinion this game is quite close to the experience what you could get if you would try to survive in the wild in the real life.

When I start a new survival game, I always watch it from different point of views to get the most proper insight whether it can give "THE TRUE SURVIVING" experience or not:
1) How much statuses (like early, mid, lategame) it can offer and how long it takes to level up into these states and how long these can be extended to not to get bored? (like does it take 2-3 days of grinding to get into lategame or does it need like hundreds or even thousands hours)
2) How complicated and satisfying the crafting, building, survival (wellness) and fighting system?
3) How much content it has, before you get bored of it?
4) How fair the game in terms of getting different resources (are there any items you'll always have to grind for, loot for, or in the end you can produce everything you need for staying healthy, safe and strong)?

I play solo, with hunters on and 60 days takes a year, so like every season has 20 days, and the game is at standard difficulty level.
With these setups the game is quite challenging but also entertaining. I've got enough time to prepare for each season and also to enjoy them. The season system, which is quite rare in survival games (Ive met with it only in DST) is also giving some of new contents and options for you either to survive or die :).

1)
Well the game has 3 states at least: Early, Mid and Lategame. Based on my almost 90 hours of playing I can say that I'm still in mid game and I feel like I'm still miles away from the lategame, so the game itself is quite time consuming, but also entertaining. The early game is truly feels as it should be, you're weak and can easily die (for example in my first game I died at night, because I didnt get enough wood for the night and got frozen to death, in my second game by the day 30 I died almost 30 times), but even these expereiences didn't deter me to continue playing. In the midgame you are getting to certain point, where you can feel safe (got base, food, some ammo), and can't wait to see what the lategame brings.

2)
Well both the crafting and the building systems are ok, easy to learn and self explanatory. The wellness system is also okay, and the fighting system is not what you would expect from an FPS game, but to be honest, this game doesn't require it at all. It is perfect as it is.

3)
So far, I am not finished with finding and trying every single content it can offer, so I can say it has enough to keep you busy for few hundreds of hours. Of course playing in a group, and knowing what you need to do exactly to get faster into mid and lategame, could decrease these hours, but rushing towards it you'd lose the time you'd usually spend on enjoying the game itself.

4)
Well not to spoil anything, you are able to get basicly every resource you need to develop both yourself (gear, food) and your base, but it takes really some time to get certain resources. But I feel like, these high tier materials are okay to be hard to find, because they are really valueable. Just one thing which i realised recently, this game won't be the game where you can craft thousands of ammo and start shooting into the air just because you're bored and full of ammo. =)

Eventually you will start to hate the game because you are not rewarded fairly for your time put in.
In my opinion the developer (Cold Games, one guy) should add modding support to the game; content wise the game is primitive at best. It reminds me of Dragons Den, where the entrepreneur overvalues their business and is reluctant to accept outside help in order to maximise their business' potential.
The game unfortunately is going the same way Miscreated has, started with hype and a developer too stubborn and arrogant to grow the game accordingly.
I cannot recommend this game as much as i'd like to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti Really Really want to like this game. I love survival games, and this seemed to have all the things i love. It is very bare bones. Basically it's the survivor vs Hunger/Weather/Bears & Dogs/NPC OP Hunters.

Pro's
*I love the chests that dont give too much loot and hard to find
*Food you have to fill not just one bar, but protein, fruit & Vegtable along with water
*Building is aesome, though not a lot to choose from, it clicks into place and just enough to be unique with wood stoves and stuff (WARNING: the moment you make the box you have 3 days before Hunters wreck your world)
*Large map, you really have to know landmarks and keep track of directions
*Weather is just beautiful, storms, seasons, even thunder to warn you of coming storms
*Bears and dogs give warning growls when nearby. If you lucky and dont stumble onto them you can back away (hopefully not into another one)

Cons
*Bare bones there is nothing but random loot drops and pieces of metal. occasionally find a carrot or strawberry but very basic
*Hunters - WTH is with 92876423 hunters that have unlimited resources, metal bases, unlimited ammo and guns right off the bat and claymores....yes bombs. While you still barely getting a basic wooden house and counting your bullets. Not only that but they will build their base right next to you and hold you hostage inside of it and they respawn in such a short time if you have to run back to your body you will loose it all. Also no wildlife attacks hunters, so you can't even use the predators around you to advantage running them through. Apparently Hunters are just OP. They killed the game for me.
*Body decay timer of 5 minutes- between dodging bears, dogs and hunters on a large map, pick the body you want to loot because you will time out.
*Bears, dogs and more bears and dogs. There are so many you can't go more than one screen away without running into 1 and sometimes 3-4 of them. Sometimes it's impossible to go forward without skirting miles out of your way threading between groups of them. Seriously what do they eat?! You.

Bottom line...I can't recommend it. It's the only game i've given a bad review on. I do not usually leave negative reviews, but the sheer frustration of trying to get to my body and getting killed by bears, dogs, hunters and then respawned again hunters i just killed way outweighs the pleasure of playing it. There is Zero balance in the game especially in the beginning, not with farming, building, or the npc pvp.\t\t\t\t\t
