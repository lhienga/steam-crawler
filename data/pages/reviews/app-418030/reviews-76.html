\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me put it this way: In Subsistence your character requires in excess of 15000kcal per day to not starve, and the average full size boar carries about 200-300g of edible meat inside of it.
It should also be noted that predatory animals that can kill you in 1 single hit (such as bears) are far more common than edible berries or any other forageable food. Meanwhile, you can go about 2-3 days without drinking a single drop of water, something that would be considered terminally dangerous in real life.

It's... just weird. Considering how long the game has been in development and what a massive lack of polish of its fundamental gameplay has. It is more sensible to repeatedly die when out of food than ever try to eat enough to sate yourself, and there's something significantly wrong in that.

It should also be noted that cutting down an entire tree (not that you can) produces less wood than you need to make one single floor panel, and there is no visual indicator whether or not a tree has been "cut down" or not. The game leaves a massive amount to be desired on the very basic level, where it fails very spectacularly. I'm sure there's a good game hidden somewhere behind the tedium, but it really isn't worth it without proper TLC given on the main areas of gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
