\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far I am really enjoying this game. I enjoy starting a new play-through after I learn more about how a game works and how to optimize things more, even if I didn't get very far. I'm currently on my third play-through and I can't wait to unlock new technology.

But I have to say something first - I really don't understand what I read in the negative reviews.
For one, people complain how this game is missing this or that or some quality of life feature, or that the game has a bad direction that it's going in. Is early access really that hard of a concept to understand? It means it's still being made.

Imagine a food critic going to a restaurant, and after placing his order, he rushes into the kitchen and starts writing his review of the meal. He says "I don't know what the chef is thinking, trying to serve me uncooked pasta! I don't know about you, but every other time that I ate pasta, it was cooked!" And he imagines that he is sounding smart.

Do people not realize that this game is in the alpha development phase? That means very early on. There is a lot of design choices still to be made, balancing and quality of life features to be added. I hear that this game is being made by just one person as a passion project. So I imagine that they work on it aside from their main job, but I could be wrong.

Second, people talk like this game is beyond extremely hard. For me it's not, I find it really fun (I play on normal difficulty with normal hunter aggression). I think this just comes down to personality type. To me, this is a methodical game. I am obsessive and a perfectionist to a fault. But those traits are what get you ahead in this game. And I don't find it hard to have enough ammo or supplies. I can keep busy all the time and always feel like I'm making progress towards something rewarding.

For now I give this a thumbs up.


Update: I've made some progress since my early review, and I need to correct myself a little. I still think the developer has made a lot of good things here, but I think some balance changes need to be done.

Here's what I've noticed: Once the other hunters really get established, the vast majority of loot disappears from the map, even a lot of the plants it seems like. The normal amount of boxes, ore and plants seems to be left within a certain radius that is close to the hunters bases. And when I do find something I want to collect away from the hunters base, there is a wolf or bear right by it, at least much more than before. I didn't realize that all those resources would disappear. This must be the developers current way of making the game hard.

On normal difficulty and normal hunter aggression, and as someone playing solo, there is not enough ammo to ward off hunter attacks and kill animals that are guarding supplies that I need. If I try to save ammo by carefully luring away predators from supplies, it really slows down how much I can gather each outing.

I can appreciate the developer wanting to make the game hard, but the current balance seems too harsh. It would be good if they focused on adjusting the resource availability before creating new content. After a little while, the early game becomes painfully slow.

I still give the game thumbs up, but just barely now...


Update: One final review incoming, I want to get to know the game better.
I'm liking this game more and more...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tbasic tldr/introduction:
i will begin by saying that giving this game a recommendation was hard to do and that in its current state, this game is hard to play while genuinely enjoying it. this review is structured using the same prompts for both pros and cons, please feel free to give critiques and ask any questions you may have! :)

PROS:

finding materials/resources:
while at times finding items can be insanely hard, i will say that it is incredibly rewarding to find the things you need. i find it especially relieving and rewarding when you finally manage to find a locked crate around the map. (im unsure of the exact spawn rate, but i do know it is by far rarer than any other crate and it also requires a lockpick to open).

weather:
the graphics in this game arent spectacular by any means, but this game can be gorgeous at times and the lighting rays and sunrises/sunsets can make you feel pretty awe struck.

pve/pvp(against ai hunters):

pve:
the pve of this game manages to be a challenge, but i wouldnt consider it absolute overkill. sure, it takes a lot of ammunition/arrows to kill each animal, but when you develop your own system to be efficient in killing each animal it makes it quite brisk.

'pvp' with ai hunters:
the pvp aspect of this game against the ai hunters is interesting and a concept i personally have never experienced for sure, but a majority of the ideas need a good polishing/rework.

advancement:
the only thing i can talk about regarding advancement in a good regard would be how insanely rewarding making good progress feels.

indie dev:
being an indie dev trying to develop a game of this scale and what not is far from easy and is an incredible accomplishment. as i also stated, this game has things i havent seen in any games like it (again, upon my personal experience). this single dev has done quite significant things in this game and i would consider it to be a survival game of its own regard.

CONS:

finding materials/resources:
running around the map trying to find things needed to survive isnt exactly a great thing in this game; you can search for literal hours (zero exaggeration) and find nine nails and four cordage in crates, neither of which help with sustenance. you can run around with zero end looking for food in crates and still find nothing to help you. theres no secret to knowing certain spawns or knowing where the best place to search is either. there is no script to finding the necessities in this game. it doesnt help when you add more than just yourself where you need to split things in order to not starve to death constantly. another major aspect of trying to find materials is finding/crafting higher end materials in order to actually enjoy this game. you need to go to certain areas where only certain animals spawn to get their drops to craft certain things. there are insanely particular materials that can only be crafted using TONS of others and after using TONS of power and mass from your base. there is ZERO balance when it comes to the requirements of crafting and the requirements of what you need to do/need to have to get said crafting materials. it is completely unnecessary and only makes the game feel unplayable and beyond drawn out and boring.

weather:
during the nights, no matter the season, you can and will freeze to death if you arent wearing the correct clothing, arent close enough to a fire, and/or arent on a low enough elevation to be up to the correct temperature to live. also, during the winter, you will die constantly unless these condition(s) are met as well. and yes, it makes sense that you shouldnt be able to run through december wearing just pants and not die of hypothermia, but getting the materials to even make higher end clothing takes HOURS and HOURS to do, not to mention that clothes deteriorate every single time you take damage. repairing clothes is indeed possible, but at the same time it also requires materials. another phenomenon of weather would be the rain; it is not only another way you can freeze to death, but it makes it hard to see and hard to do anything productive since you need to be constantly near a fire to not freeze to death.

pve/pvp(against ai hunters):

pve:
there is little balance when it comes to the pve experience in the game. fighting the wolves. bears. mountain lions, boars, etc. is at times far too much to do when you lack good weapons and large amounts of ammo. they all have way too much health, they all deal way too much damage, and they all are far too fast. now, dont get me wrong, i dont think a brown bear should die to a singular arrow since realistically that isnt statistically going to happen, but at the same time i shouldnt have to practically hide away from the hostile animals just because i cant fight them face to face. there needs to be some aspect of greater balance to where i can actually HUNT the hostile animals and not be terrified of dying and having to run back to my gear time and time again.

'pvp' with ai hunters:
when it comes to the pvp with the ai hunters, they are nearly just as bad to fight as the hostile animals, but they have guns that can kill you in two shots. sure, you can change how often the hunters will attack you by changing the difficulty of the game, but if you go anywhere near them, they will attack you and will chase you until you somehow get out of their range, or they kill you. not to mention that they often wear armor and helmets and make their bases near your own which makes it to where running around for materials becomes even harder for the player(s).

advancement:
getting anywhere in this game will take HOURS. you can play day after day and make little to no real progress because of the constant set backs that this game brings. this game is one of, if not the most, unforgiving games i have ever seen/played myself. and i do realize that the definition of really advancing in the game is different per person, but when you think of the fact that building a simple base can take over 10 real life hours of gameplay and constant deaths, stacking up more and more, it makes actually doing anything productive near impossible.

indie dev:
i dont know tons about the dev himself and i havent been involved in this game or the forums for all that long, but from what i have seen/heard, the dev doesnt care for this game or the fans nearly as much as many, including myself, wished they would. i get that developing a game solo is INSANELY difficult and isnt nearly as simple as just "make it better lol", but people suggest loads of things and even offer real help to the dev with no avail.

now i get that after all of these negatives, its unbelievable that i could even give this game a "recommendation" but i do think that this game could truly be great, BUT only after MANY things change. i dont feel like its appropriate of me to not recommend this game overall just because it is currently a mess of a title. dont get me wrong here, do NOT buy this game if you cant deal with the constant deaths (yes, even on easy mode and even without the ai hunters running around) and the constant grinding of this game. i only truly recommend this game to anyone that will enjoy the satisfaction of getting past the immense amount of pain and suffering this game entails.

im more than sure theres more that i could say, but this is all that comes to mind currently. i will edit anything else that i think of that needs to be added here.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm hard to disappoint and upset, as I did multiple youtube Series' about Fallout 4 Survival mode, 7 Days to Die, Skyrim on Legendary Difficulty, Metal Gear Solid 5 TPP challenges, Mount and Blade: Warband and so on and on.

I am used to punishment, difficulty and having to cope in order to enjoy a game.

But this game is an absolute trainwreck. This game is so badly made in all aspects that you have to wonder why the Dev is even supporting this still, and much more so, why it is on Steam.

The difficulty isn't simply hard, or extreme, it's nonexistant. You either run around and dodge animals, or die fighting them. You constantly look at the ground to pick up scrap metal, plants and loot boxes. You hit trees to farm wood that burns away in a fireplace (a fire place of any kind, that is), you constantly starve and need to eat a balanced diet(!!!) in order to stay alive, you freeze to death within moments at night, you can only survive with a fire that you need to make with super expensive to make fire starters. Let me tell you, you have a hatchet you start with, and you can't figure out how to spark some sparks onto a set of dried out grass in order to start a fire? Man. The insanity in the crafting recipes.

Not only that. You constantly look for food, and wood. All in all, this is a "life simulation", you have 0 time to do anything beside looking for wood, so you dont freeze to death at night, and food, so you dont starve to death. There is 0 fun or any sort of other enjoyment in this, not even the sadistical type of self torture, this game is just plain horrible.

Just writing this exhausts my mental capacity, I am beyond disappointed how someone could even make such a game that has none of the fundamental rules of game design in place and instead just appears to have been thrown together to be "super hard and edgy".

At least in My Summer Car I can build a car and go to a store to get food easily, but in this game, I have to chase rabbits that are too fast, deer that are too fast, or fight wolves that kill you in 3 hits, and bears that 2 shot you when you try to take them on, or get too close to them.

As I said, I am used to punishment, torture, and have played and still play games that are absolutely comparable to self-flagellation, but this game has no factor that makes this self-torture worthwhile, there is nothing in this game that makes it fun to self-torture yourself in order to experience... something, anything.

Because in this poo-poo pile of a game, there is nothing to enjoy, there is no content, there is nothing, there is nothing nothing nothing. Never been disappointed from a game, like this, that's a first.

The only microscopic thing that there is: this game can be a meme with friends. The "walking home and dying simulator". That's a meme. That works for a short moment, and after that just becomes tiring and boring.

If you have a group of friends sure, hop on and laugh at each other dying and try to survive as a group, that can be "fun". But that's because you are with your friends, the mechanics of the game provide no substance on which you could build a foundation for a "fun gaming night". You can die millions of times, pointless deaths, in every other game as well.

As I said, it's good for the memes, but outside of that, a worthless pile of manure, this game.\t\t\t\t\t
