\r\n\t\t\t\t\tsee alot of negative reviews from people complaining about the early game and how it's designed to kill you/impossible to live through etc. see a lot of youtubers that say to get used to dying early because it's just the way it is until you get set up.

...let me just say that's complete nonsense. i have less than 80 hours on record at the moment i'm writing this. if i don't want to die, i simply stay alive. i'm not some kind of FPS god, i'm just not an idiot and i learn from every single play through. i'm currently on my SIXTH. let that sink in, i've only reset my game five times in almost 80 hours of play.

...my usual strategy is moronically simple: spawn in, kill every tree i can, grab every box i see. if a predator is guarding a tree i don't need that tree. if a predator is guarding a box i walk into aggro range. if a predator aggro's onto you at their maximum detection range, they will start by walking towards you until they get close enough to become aggressive. the distance for aggression is about equivalent to 5 meters or so by eyeball. so for all the people complaining about unrealistic aggression, i'd say it's pretty fair that in a survival game where predators offer the only realistic challenge besides hunters, said predators can be easily avoided if your not an idiot. which is why i can get basically every box i see by just drawing them away and then circling back.

once i have a few arrows and a bow, i make sure to kill every chicken i possibly can. 1 chicken gives 2 feathers which make 4 arrows. the return is ridiculous, the only problem is saving enough nails to build some shelter. RNG can tell me i'm not getting any nails. but i keep at it and they'll show up eventually. i usually have so much chicken it's spoiling in my inventory after being cooked, so i can sprint as much as i like which helps.

for my first night my objective is getting one foundation down, with one wall, and a ceiling. basically a lean-to. i put my fire inside it to protect it from the rain (like somebody with brain cells). i put a box down for storage. this requires about 80 wood, 50 nails, 16 fiber and 2 biofuel (for fire starting kits). this is perfectly within reason even if i've made more than 20 arrows on my first day (cause i'm not an idiot, and i learn how to play the game rather than expecting it to let me play however i like).

by day 5 i'm usually up to medium clothing, over 40 animal fat stocked and needing a second storage chest to fit all my fruit/veg and seeds (i don't eat fruit/veg. not necessary, pure carnivore diet. the fruit/veg will go to my animal pen once i get one built). i usually wait to drop my BCU until i have over 100 animal fat, decent ore stocks and a single floor 3x3 house. once i drop my BCU my build order is Woodburner > Small Generator > Mass Fabricator.

the woodburner is necessary for rendering my animal fat. the small generator comes before the mass fabricator because the woodburner offsets your power/mass ratio and i don't want to sit on max power waiting for max mass to build the fabricator. i still have to wait a bit for the mass to build back up, but once i have all three built i can just render fat into biofuel, crank biofuel into power and power into mass. with over 100 animal fat i have plenty of biofuel to accelerate my base building once i drop my BCU. after that... well, i've only had 6 runs. i'm working on figuring out cougars right now, so after one or two more runs i'll be farming sinew and have a working strat to reach endgame before day 10.

..i'm not some kind of god at the game. i don't even have 80 hours in (77 hours exactly as of writing this). the game isn't hard, it just doesn't pull punches. if you don't want to bother learning how to play the game, it WILL punish you for being arrogant or ignorant. honestly i love that aspect, it makes every death feel meaningful. "this is why i died. how do i avoid dying like that moving forward?" and there are usually MULTIPLE methods for dealing with every problem. the game isn't hard... it just expects you to deal with the challenges it poses rather than ignore them and charge forward recklessly. ...and once you learn how to deal with most of the early challenges you can certainly charge forward recklessly, as long as your not an idiot about it and know how to do it without getting wrecked.

i would certainly recommend this game to anyone who's looking for something different. it's simple and straightforward, but it won't just bend over for you. it's very different from most other survival games in that regard. the progression is a little overbalanced, but honestly the game would be crap if they went too far in the other direction and over tuned things too much. i hate to admit it, but the challenge and the intricacy it creates is 80% of the draw. get rid of that... and this game would just be another dime-a-dozen piece of indie-studio crap.

if you want a game that empowers you as a player and makes you feel like an unstoppable force or a one man army... this isn't the game for you. if you want a game where every single step you take forward is a challenge, feels meaningful and provides a sense of accomplishment? i don't think i've ever found a better survival game.\t\t\t\t\t
