\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would not recommend this game.

MAJOR ISSUE WITH THIS GAME
Once you install the game, and run it for the first time, you'll have to agree to some (TOS) Terms of Service agreements regarding the Unreal Engine. Once you accept, it'll load the game. If you ever decide to uninstall the game, it will not uninstall the Unreal Engine, and it'll stay in your Add/Remove Programs list pretty much forever, listed as "My Game No Name", until the developer provides a fix. If you try and reinstall the game, and run the game AGAIN, agreeing to all the TOS, then uninstall it, it'll create another instance of "My Game Long Name" and you'll have two instances. I've come across people with over 20 instances, who can't get rid of it.

It's rare that I refund a game, but this one qualifies. This game on the easiest settings makes you angry, because it milks the ever so popular concept of creating a grindy game because "gamers" like the GRIND.

My 7 hour experience has been over 5 restarts on the EASY setting. It takes LUCK to actually survive the initial grind, I've not made it past that point, and I've been gaming since the mid 80's. I consider myself somewhat of a seasoned veteran when it comes to gaming.

I recommend instead of buying this game, you're money would be better spent buying "The Infected", another Early Access game, but it has stunning visuals, and you can DISABLE THE PREDATOR BEARS, AND WOLVES. Also has seasonal changes where the lakes freeze over in winter and you can walk on top of them, and it's not nearly as much of a grind as this game, but at least it's POSSIBLE to SUCCEED.

Too many PREDATOR animals in this game, to the point that the game is not even fun, except watching the bear eat my face off, that was the only exciting moment.

You have to struggle just to find basic PLANT FIBER, which you need for everything. No shacks, no pre-existing buildings, no vehicles, just trees, bears, dogs, and something from the cat family, and they level up each time they kill you. The concept of having to find loot boxes is just utterly stupid, and you'll spend most of your time babysitting your health and stamina bars.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really beautiful game of survival, crafting, building, collecting resources to upgrade and enhance your equipment and base to progress in the world.

A relaxing experience, especially on easy difficulty, feels great to play and runs smooth on my computer, it feels very optimized most of the time (I have above average computer parts for my desktop).

I've enjoyed being able to pick the months I want to play and the days in a year, and being able to turn hunters off or on while playing in my save profile. Save your game often, and turn off auto saves, especially at first, to get a feel for everything.

The nightime duration is also something that I was annoyed about at first, because there isn't much to do in your base when you're early game, and you just want to keep collecting resources. As you progress in the game, the nighttime is a great time to work on your base upgrades and organize your base, cook your food, etc. However it still feels a bit long sometimes and just waiting until morning. The more I progressed in the game the more it becomes routine and not a huge deal, but early game it's frustrating.

I wish the map was even bigger, and that it will be procedurally generated in the future, so you have a different experience if you want to play multiple profiles and/or play in coop.

I've played about 27 hours, not too long, but just enough to get a feel for how the game is played on early and bit of mid game, but not late game.

Here are some things to understand though, that you might not realize when you're playing:
The early game is rough, and it takes a while to get a feel for everything and finding the resources you need such as fiber.
When chopping wood, you can just press the left mouse button once and hold it down to continue chopping on a tree. I didn't realize this at first and I just kept mashing left click a lot.
Getting poisoned will bring up the red hazard symbol on the right side of the screen, and it is very costly in early game as it requires a lot of materials that are harder to get at first. Try to avoid drinking dirty water or eating food that isn't perfectly cooked.
The hunters are very fun to fight, and give you very resourceful loot, however they can attack your base randomly and it will reduce durability of all your base and items whatever they hit. They will increase in difficulty if they build up a base strong enough over time. The movement of the hunters sometimes feel very weird and they can be crazier than you realize, they'll go from using a pistol to a shotgun right in front of you very quickly and you can die easily. The great thing is if you just start getting too annoyed by then you can turn it off or turn it off/on to reset them.
Changing difficulty to anything other than easy - you have to understand what will happen. Wildlife will chase you longer. If you die at all on normal or hardcore, your game will save, regardless of having auto save turned off or on. As well, your stamina and health will reset back to 90, so you will not be able to reload before you die. This is super important to understand, especially if you're really enjoying yourself on Easy and are comfortable with what you have.

I was irritated by not understanding the full scope of what changing difficulty meant from easy to normal and losing all my max stamina and health.

However, despite some of the weirdness of the game, it's quite an enjoyable experience and very fun to play in its current state. I'll be happy to come back to the game periodically when there are large updates throughout development.\t\t\t\t\t
