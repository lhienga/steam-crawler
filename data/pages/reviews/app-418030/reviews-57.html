\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery immersive environment, I can play for hours and not feel like a cartoon character. Build is flexible for the genre, games primary focus appears to be destroy the human like NPCs or other players. However you can play with out them and then its just you against the various wildlife. If you are not into a life or death chase with the wildlife most of it is programmed to wonder around in a small radius with plenty of notification if you get to close. I find the concept of the recovering trees to be the most inventive. Most of the subsistence life styles I have observed find the collection of fire wood not only a necessity but a tedious chore. Especially if you live in a local that does not allow the removal of healthy trees for any reason. This concept is the closest I have seen of providing the need without actually removing the tree. The drop boxes add a bit of fun as well, trying to grab them before the local grizzle catches you. I highly recommend whether you are a hunter, explorer, builder or want to find a virtual arena to chase your friends around in. What ever floats your boat, pretty sure this developer has it for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like this game. Every so often I find a game that really captures my imagination and I can't wait to play it every day, Subsistence is like that for me. I really like the crafting and the way it's set up. I also like how you can move everything even after you've placed it, you can move your entire base if you want. I have a lot of hours in other games like 7 Days to Die. But I prefer the crafting system in Subsistence. In 7 Days by the end of week one I'd be making steel. I mean, come on, you're forging STEEL in one week after starting with a stone axe? It's not very challenging to me, and I play on Insane level. In Subsistence it's harder to craft higher end items but it's possible to do it reasonably quick. It's not so "realistic" that it loses the fun. Games like UnReal World are so realistic, it's just a lot of hard work, too much like real life. Subsistence has a great balance.

I really like diving and the way water is displayed, something 7 Days had a lot of trouble with for years. The diving rig and oxygen bottles is pretty unique. Games like Valheim you can't go under the water at all, if you drop something in the deep water, it's gone.

I like the loot and resources. I enjoy the weather effects, especially the rain storms, they're well done. And the climate system, getting cold, in other games that is SO EASY but in Subsistence it's a challenge. You can get in real trouble real fast in the cold.

Meat decays at a realistic rate, if you slaughter an animal you'd better process that meat or eat it by the end of the day or it goes bad, just like in real life. But unlike 7 Days where food never spoils, in Subsistence you can have a refrigerator that works! I dislike games where there's food but it never spoils, it lacks challenge. You end up with huge amounts of food and it's boring. Same with thirst. What kind of survival game doesn't model food and thirst? One good thing about 7 Days is it mimics dysentery and infection. In Subsistence you even have to wash your hands after butchering an animal!

The game world is a nice place to go and is relaxing. But unlike great games like Medieval Dynasty, there is a little danger and tension. It's fun to have to be careful. And the loot system which is a lot like an Easter Egg hunt, always searching for random resources, I like that system. Instead of cutting down entire forests like in Valheim, this is refreshing. Building a base with a mind to defense is a lot funner than no defense. And the enemies here, WHICH YOU CAN SHUT OFF IF YOU DON'T WANT TO PLAY THAT WAY, will show up at your base with Molotov cocktails and grenades! In the start game screen you can change the month if you've had enough of winter, or change that the enemies are there. It's fun to get a good start and THEN add the NPC enemies when you feel ready. This game is also great in multi-player, it's a lot of fun.

If you like crafting, Base Defense, hunting, looting, this is a great game. To me it's one of the best survival crafting defense games I've ever played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I cannot recomend this game when I compare it with the overwhleming number of titles that do exactly the same thing but better.

Pros:
Really nice and warm environment details and sounds
a decent attempt for such a small development operation
Seems to have a pretty decent roadmap to where its going with regards to content for both game and quality of life

Cons:
UI is clunky and half baked at best
Recipes are unrealistic and unbalanced with content required and volumes of certain resources
Breaks immersion for the sheer number of apex predators every 30-50 feet. More predators compared to prey (chickens and rabbits) by about 5 to 1
weight carrying is a non existent reality. Can carry 100's of massive logs and heavy gear
resources in game, you are in a temperate biome and yes plant matter is exceedingly hard to come across to harvest even though you are surrounded by it and yes you can locate logs by the hundred.
Breaks immersion for the sheer number of loot boxes all over though the contents are lack lustre
Animals are unrealistic in their ability to soak up damage for firearms plus the behaviors, most animals would buck it at the sound of a point blank discharge
Feels unrewarding and unchallenging in its gameplay outside of the apex predators and the lack of balance in the resource gathering... if you choose to just keep yours eyes open and skirt the animals/hunters, you can go through the game bored and unharrassed.

I will keep my eyes on this for the future but currently its just a little underwhelming in an already heavily saturated genre.\t\t\t\t\t
