\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to enjoy this game. It has the elements, but it's very badly done IMO.
The only good point I see is that visuals are nice. Thet's it. Now that I put the positive away, let's point out the negative.

This game has been released in 2016, almost 7 years ago. And yet, the trees don't fall. You get a axe right from the start, so the first thing you do is to cut a tree. Cut is not the right word here because it doesn't take damage. It just gives you logs. And the tree stays right there, like it's been untouched.
The map limits are metal fences. You get important ressources from crates and such you find randomly. You need nails to build a refuge. But, you can guess, nails are only found in crates. You also need a lot of fibers and you think it won't be an issue since there's a lot of bushes around. NO. Fibers are only found from a specific little plant, and it's not that common. So you struggle to get the most needed ressources.
That's not bad enough. The game puts predators. You need to explore the map to get the needed ressources, but there's mean animals all around you have to avoid.
There's also hunters. But you can disable them when creating your game profile if you want. For a first start it's mandatory.
I played this game bafore, years ago and it feels like it's at the same state. I don't know about killing chickens or rabbits and whatever, I didn't even tried. But last time I checked they had very strong skin. Like you needed several bullets to kill a chicken. And yes, you get a gun also. It's nice but don't think it makes things so easy, oh no. Keep the bullets for critical situations.
So for me it's no fun at all. Getting ressources is a pain. Getting food is a pain. and water, you can't drink just going in the lake. I didn't try lol, maybe you can use the canteen, I hope...
Another good point, you can play with friends. So you won't be alone struggling and having no fun. Sharing is great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's sad to see all the negative feedback for Subsistence, yes there's ridiculous amounts of wild animals, trees don't fall, food decays quickly and you're searching for crates. But once you get past those issues and give it a proper chance it clicks and you see what the dev (solo!) is trying to achieve.

At its core it's a fantastic survival game. The high animal spawns and crates are there to keep you on your toes, this is a brutal game to make you feel a constant sense of danger and always on the lookout. There's no leveling up stats to make you op. Day 1 you're vulnerable, day 100 you're vulnerable with slightly better weapons and armour, but hopefully you might have a nice base with all the nice things you've worked hard to craft.

Because of the high animal spawns and the hunters out to get you, if you run around like a headless chicken you're gonna die. You don't prepare for the night you're gonna die, you don't prepare the right survival items you're gonna die. This is what brutal survival is all about and I totally feel like I'm surviving in the
wild.

The audio is amazing, wear headphones, you'll feel the thundery storms, the gentle breeze in autumn, midnight fishing in the moonlight and suddenly a bear can wonder behind you and you'll rip a huge one in your pants lol.

There's base building, seasons (not even implemented in most survival games but makes a huge difference), fishing, farming, vehicles, wild animals hunt, they also attack other ai hunters (its hilarious and satisfying to watch), the list just goes on and all from one very talented dev who takes a lot of negative criticism but has stuck by his vision to create an awesome brutal survival game.

Being lost and thinking about surviving is fun for me so I don't even open the map, to make it even more challenging is to not have the compass points and also to remove health bars of animals as it makes them easy to avoid at night, maybe dev will give the option to remove.

2 hour steam refund doesn't fit well with open world games but If you're into hardcore survival, give this a proper try because I've not had this feeling of surviving in a living, breathing world in a long time.\t\t\t\t\t
