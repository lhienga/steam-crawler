\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile my wife and I enjoy playing this game together, time and time again we encounter something that makes us say "Everyone we know would hate this game"

There are two limiting factors that make us want to nope out- and we play on easy mode with no hunters!

But specifically, the fire starting is ridiculous, and same with the rope crafting. And if you look through the discussions, comments, videos, etc. etc. etc., those factors have been problems for everyone for the entire run of this game. They've never changed.

In the real world you can start a fire with two sticks and some wrist action. You can knock rocks together. But in this game? No no.

Likewise, making rope? It's twisted fibers. People have been making it for, oh, tens of thousands of years. We've got this figured out. But not this game.

Those two things are killing it for us.

And if you notice the achievements? Where only 5.9% of players (as of writing this review) have managed to start a fire? That's a PROBLEM for a survival game. That's a BIG problem.

Likewise, there's just no reason for bottlenecking everything behind the rope-making. Rope in this game is made with sinew. An odd choice to be sure, since that's not how rope is made. It's far worse though, because sinew only comes from moose! And Cougars! For no. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. Reason.

People have been complaining about that for six years. It hasn't changed. It probably won't change. It's incredibly stupid. And it's needlessly frustrating!

So yes. I've got my money's worth. But we really really can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is as grindy and intensely immersive as any survival game comes. For the most part I've enjoyed simply surviving in a beautiful landscape without the traditional tropes of zombies or other post apocalyptic enemies to tend with.

As others have commented, this game is harsh and unforgiving and that's what I like about.

However, one of my biggest gripes early on has to do with the respawn system. If you die you are spawned on the other side of the map and have a certain amount of time to reach your body before any hard earned resources you've gathered disappears.

This is ridiculously difficult even on 'normal' mode. You respawn mostly naked, cold, fighting both the elements and animals to desperately reach your previous body in time.

Depending on the weather and time of day the pitfalls of such a harsh respawn system becomes apparent as you can become locked into a continuous 'deathloop'. Such a system is frustratingly difficult. Forget hard-core. Subsistence's respawn system is simply broken. Here's hoping the develop adapts but judging by player accounts they seem reluctant to take on feedback from the player base.

Some small tweaks here or there could turn a good yet frustrating game into something great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGrindiest survival game I've ever played, 3 and 4 logs per tree and trees don't fall, nails, boards, cordage and logs required to make one single platform. You (might) find 2 or 3 nails in construction crates, so basically you're spending lots and lots of time chopping at trees, looking for crates and cordage and crafting boards all the while. Food is hard to come by and rots very quickly. Your character needs constant nutrition, protein, carbs, water and stamina. You can easily consume two bears, a wolf and a couple chickens and need more protein within 10 minutes of gameplay. Food requirements are a constant search and consume....you character can EAT like you've never seen and never be full for long. Carbs, vegetables and water are the same...never enough, always on the brink of starvation. Animals are everywhere, around every corner and running from them uses precious stamina which regens through eating more food. If you like a long, grindy game, Subsistence is the game for you as the bulk of your time will be spent chopping trees, keeping a fire burning, eating and drinking. There are weapons in game, but again a long grind for resources to craft one. One arrow takes wood, cordage and the ever precious nails. Single developer of the game does make regular updates and seems deaf to player recommendations (as witnessed by the forum). I do recommend the game because I happen to be retired and have lots of free time. Don't forget to scan through the Forum before you buy.\t\t\t\t\t
