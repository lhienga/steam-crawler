\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMany survival games become too easy to play late game. This is one of my favorite of the genre that I keep coming back to because it remains challenging. There are certain components that you need to progress your base and survival that can only be found in very rare randomly generated boxes. Even after setting up a functional base with a sustaining food supply, I am forced to venture deeper into the map to find those precious boxes. There never seems to be enough gun parts, and I have to make choices on whether to make upgrades on an existing gun, or make a different type. Ammo is precious, and reserved for the largest animals (moose) or the most dangerous (cougars), or emergency life saving measures. Bows are the best weapon for hunting, and upgrading to the composite (using gun parts) as fast as possible is highly recommended. There's just never enough gun ammo. With a composite bow and a level 1 upgrade, it can reasonable take down the common wolves in 1 or 2 shots, which helps with clearing an area looking for those rare military crates.

Playing the game with the hunter enemies turned off makes the resource situation even more dire. Rare materials and parts can be looted off hunter raids, but if you turn hunters off, the only way to get those parts is through the military crates.

The base building is nice and uncomplicated. You can pickup and move base parts from one room to another without loss of resources. There's plenty of building pieces to make many types of imaginative bases. Especially if you like sunrooms and greenhouses.\t\t\t\t\t
