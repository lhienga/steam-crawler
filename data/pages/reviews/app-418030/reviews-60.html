\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would love to recommend this game, I really would. However, as of now, there are a few things that need to be fixed before I can.

First, the crafting is very grindy for no reason. I'm talking you need 4 planks to build a single wall, and you need 3 logs to build each plank. So 12 wood total for one single wall. Oh, and you only get three wood per tree. It sounds manageable, but seeing as everything requires wood in this game, it gets old fast.

Second, I'm not fond of the fact that the predators are so overpowered and that they level up every time they kill. It makes it so that, if you die once to an animal near your base, you are screwed permanently because the animals usually hover around the same area.

I also don't like how you have to rely on loot drop for certain items and there is no way to craft them yourself. This is things like gunpowder, nails, etc. Many of the items are instrumental to your crafting as well, so you're stuck looking for loot drops for most of the game. Additionally, the resources in general are far too rare and hard to come by.

I hate it because this game has potential to be something really great, and I'll probably continue to play and explore just to see if it gets easier as I go along. As of now, though, it took me two hours to build a two foundations and the walls that go along with them (which are very small, think Ark sized foundations) and another hour just to get a bed.

Also, you can't spawn in the bed every time you die. That was the dealbreaker for me. You have to "buy" your spawns by waiting on your base's power box to recharge. And given how often you die in this game (it's unavoidable really), a solid spawn point is an absolute must.

That said, the graphics are beautiful and the play style is very user intuitive. I sincerely hope the devs continue to add more to this game because I would love to see what the future holds for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter putting 389 hours into this game I would offer the following comments. For the money, it is worth a try. However, the grind is real and the rewards are little. As a new player it cannot be played with much enjoyment in "normal" mode with the hunters "on". The only way for players to learn the ropes is to put it on "easy" mode and turn the Hunters off. Seems this is a common thing among all players. To me this indicates a severely unbalanced game. The grind is repetitive and boring. The crafting of items is resource heavy and progression is slow. It appears to be designed to be a game of where progression is measured in microns and really never picks up speed. Regarding combat with the AI hunters, they are perfect shots, have unlimited ammo. You cannot hit them when they are running but don't even bother to try and run from them. They are not hampered by the same constraint. The weapons are expensive to build, wear out quickly and repairs are expensive. Upgrading them is mostly a waste of time. The damage increases really don't translate into any meaningful reduction in the amount of ammo it takes to down a hunter so upgrading is rather pointless. On the bright side, the map is beautiful, the building is versatile and enjoyable and the weather/change of seasons are astonishing. While I would still recommend this game, you must go into knowing it is all about the grind. Hopefully some major balancing will take place at some point in the future to allow players to enjoy other aspects of the game besides spending 75% of your time chopping trees.\t\t\t\t\t
