\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIve tried really hard to like this game. But this game tries really hard to hate me.
I like difficulty, but i like customizing my game A ♥♥♥♥ TON MORE.

TLDR This game ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hates you and doesnt want you to enjoy it or have any amount of fun. Remove this title from your wish list till they add game customization and options. Other wise the game is ... OK... i guess...
While some players might loves this ♥♥♥♥. I cannot say i do. I understand its early access and many of these things will change, but im reviewing current build Alpha 58 and my severe displeasure with it.

DIFFICULTY

The game is not challenging. Its just difficult.
4 hours in i've restarted my game 5 times because whenever your killed by wild life it levels up and doesnt despawn, if you die at your base and your starting off you can kiss it all good bye. The animals health is exponential each level up. So even if you did have some "decent" weapons your still not going to win if your just starting the game.
So i just save the game and reload any time i die... Not exactly how im intended to play the game i would think...
My entire 4 hours was walking around just avoiding wild life... Not remotely exciting in any way shape or form... Would be one thing if there wern't millions of aggressive fauna everywhere and they had better reaction distance and acted like wildlife, but they dont and there are way too many for any ideation of realism. It is REALLY cool when you get bucked by a deer, you get impailed and flop around, its great. The bear will smash your face with its claws... Eagles catch fish from the rivers... Love these little details that help bring it all to life....

Which brings me to food, any realistic approach to survival offers an infinite variety of food types.
In a real situation you can eat grass, leaves, bark and grubs. While not exactly nutritious they will at least prevent absolute starvation (if not get you sick and kill you...)
But in this game youll die of starvation before dehydration.
I dont mean to be insulting.... but...
The logic here is ♥♥♥♥. I dont expect a dev to be an actual survivalist but at the very least do your research.
Absolutely needs an overhaul

In this game i have seen berries, grubs and kelp. Thats all i have found.
If this game wants to try and be this difficult at least do so with reason, as well as incentives for out of the box thinking or surviving.

So after all my frustrations i tried to cheat. But it seems theres anti-cheat that blocks any hex edits in runtime.

THE GRIND

I got over the fact i cant cheat, but instantly got bored as ♥♥♥♥.
Its a grind, walk 3 miles to find 3 fiber... Walk 8 miles and pray to god you find some nails... Or food or just a smidgen of anything you need.

So after walking for 20 minutes and only finding a couple berries and 2 fiber 4 nails... I just feel absolutely unaccomplished. This crap literally wont make anything except some twine. That berry is useless without x amount, and 4 nails do me good for nothing. 20 mins, the days over i have one platform down for my base out of the now 4 days its been in game where ive died 3 times from starvation and have absolutely nothing to show for it.
Construction is not expsensive, its just a horrifying grind.
I know it opens up later in the game. But my patience has LONG since worn thin.
Sure i could invite a friend to help aleviate that grind, but i simply dont want to and should'nt have to.
BECAUSE
IM NOT HAVING ANY FUN WHAT SO EVER
im angry and bored by this game, why would i invite a friend or convince them this is the game they want to play?

I wont be touching or recommending this game again till difficulty and game customisations are added and AT THE VERY LEAST more survival mechanics.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy honest opinion, this game is really bad. I knew this when I bought the game but I was expecting for the game to improve over time, but it's been maybe 2 years and the game still sucks. The devs have added a far amount of content, to their credit, but it's not a lack of content that makes this game ♥♥♥♥ it's the horrible progression. It takes hours to get the bare minimum and the devs just keep adding new stuff that is ether useless, like the gliders they just added, or unattainable for most players. Like making a base is a chore. For example; to make a wall you need cordage, wood planks, and nails. The first problem with this is that you need to farm wood and creates at the same time. Which isn't really a problem in other games but finding creates is REALLY inconsistent. there's no place to reliably get loot, it's just luck. There have been time's were I've looked over the entire map and find one create. and other times I see 3 or 4 in one area. second of all, wood planks add unnecessary crafting time and adds nothing to the base game. Frankly I think that planks should be removed from the game. Let me tell you why. You can get a max of 3 logs from most trees in the game, it costs' 3 logs for one plank. You need 4 planks for a wall. that's 4 trees you have to cut down AFTER finding the nails and fiber. Just crafting the planks takes around 90 seconds overall. And that's just for 1 wall, let alone a hole ass base. After you get the basic parts of your base down (walls, floors, roof, base unit,etc), you need to make power and mass storage, but to get that you you have to waste what little power and mass you already have stored to make just one or the other, and you need one of both mass and power for most recipes. witch means that you need to wait like 10-20 minutes before you can make something else. "so why don't you just go out and gather more loot while you wait?" Because as I said earlier it's not like rust were you can go run power plant and there's a high chance of you getting loot. Essentially you have to wait to wait. Don't get me wrong I'm not just tearing into this game to be a ♥♥♥♥. It's constructive criticism, I want the devs to see the problems that plague their game and boy there are a lot of problems that need fixing. And I think fixing what I just listed would be a good start.And I know that there are other ways I could voice my beef, but pointing out the devs shortcomings is probably the best way of improving the game as a player. I want to see this game grow, I want to see this game improve but that can't happen if the devs don't make a move.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have almost exactly 350 hours of playtime in the game so I figured that I would write it the review it deserves. Overall it is a fairly enjoyable game if you enjoy grinding for resources more often then doing anything else. In the beginning stages a new world save, the game can be very grindy and requires a lot of farming materials and resources. It took me almost 30 hours of constantly dying, along with straight grinding resources for hours at a time to get a medium size shelter down and move on from the early/primitive stage crafting and resources. I have played over 200 hours on my first world save and I still would say I have not reached the advanced crafting and resources stage. The ability to find important resources that are essential to advancement in the game definitely should get a rework. I have spent hours at a time grinding the resources that are needed to make a single crafted item. It does become easier to grind resources once you find a good weapon, or gain the necessary tools/resources to craft one and a decent amount of ammunition. Without a higher damage firearm it is very difficult to stay alive due to constant attacks from wild animals. Having a better gun will save you hours of time wasted on running to the location of your last death to collect your dropped items. It is a hard game to play and succeed at and requires a lot of time and effort invested in locating/farming resources. Loot tables and the amount of resources needed for crafting items needs a large reworking in order to make the game more playable/enjoyable for the typical survival gamer. I know I talked a lot about what the game needs in order to improve; however, the game did have several good qualities. The usability of Subsistence makes it easy to learn the controls and get precise/comfortable with the way character movement works. The following major systems listed are very intuitively designed making it easy to understand what is needed to make them work, and they do a lot to help make the overall play ability of the game increase. These systems include: farming, electricity, fabrication/crafting, building/placement of structures. The animal difficulty levels are done decently. Without a working gun it will be quite hard to take down a wolf or a bear; although, once firearms become accessible to you, then the animal difficulty becomes much more manageable. It is still relatively easy for a predator that has a high level, or even 2 or more low level wolves to get the better of you if you're not careful. The way that animal behaviors are set allows for a challenging but survivable game play once you unlock the means to defend yourself. I would recommend this game to anyone who enjoys a hard survival game that is difficult due to slow resource farming and material gathering rates. If you like quick progression and fast reward for the work you put into grinding materials then this game is not for you. Overall I enjoy playing the game and I think it is a decent survival game. Even though Subsistence needs some developmental work, you must consider the fact that it is still an early access game. I am hopeful that future developments/updates to the game will add or redesign certain aspects to help balance and improve the game. Subsistence has a lot of potential as a game, some changes to the spawn rates of resources and new content being regularly added to the game through quarterly updates could turn this game into one of the best survival games out there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing!!! THIS is a perfect example of what Early Access is for. I can't believe it's just one guy making this. He quite his job to make this game specifically because he likes everything about the development process and his passion for it really shows here.

His update scheme is miles ahead of anyone else. Every single update includes at least a handful new and different content along normal changes/balancing and bug fixes (which there aren't many bugs that I've seen). Literally, every update makes my need to play and check out the new things.

My favorite things about the game (in no particular order):
- Rock climbing! I really was not expecting this to be as smooth as it is :)
- With the newest update, we now have HANG GLIDERS!!!!!!!!!!!!
- Scuba gear and underwater exploration/cave systems!
- Weather and seasonal system. I really love the storms now. Vegetation changes with the season as well some animal behavior ;) It truly gives you a feel of having to work hard just so you can hunker down in the winter and relax watching the snow fall and lakes freeze over. I've never felt my hard summer work pay off so much in any survival game before.
- The building and how everything looks. I don't know how to explain it as it has a somewhat primitive feel but modern look and items when building things. For example, you don't progress through building material (like thatch > wood > metal), it's just nice looking wood (and all objects fit together nicely, which for reason seems to be an issue with other survival games) and then you can add metal armor to it or glass objects.
- Wild life! Yes, it is hard to deal with at first but if you pay attention (not only to where you're going but your stamina as well), it's not difficult to navigate around bears/wolves. Seriously, just pay attention! :D
- The elevators were a very welcomed addition as well, especially since I like the building so much XD

If you're new, here are my tips:
-Start with a throw away game just to learn the basics and turn AI hunters off just so you can learn the map (this will be more useful than you think, though, there also wasn't a map when I started playing and there is now). More importantly, how to deal with, or rather avoid, the wild life. Again, pay attention, if you run straight into a bear, you weren't paying enough attention (it happens to all of us though). Keep your eyes and ears open!
-Then, start playing a game with AI Hunters set to retaliation only and learn how they work. Don't worry, you'll eventually unknowingly run past some that will make come and attack you a bit later :P
- Maybe my biggest tip - ALWAYS turn around and check for boxes behind you. They seem to spawn in using a radial pattern around you but often end up being in a straight line and if you just keep running forward you'll miss a LOT. I've never found it difficult to gather resources when focusing on finding boxes. It may sound weird to keep running around the same area but I've found it to be VERY effective.
- It will feel like a grind at first, especially without the material progression you're likely used to. But, once you get your first solar panel, it's off the races! Soon enough you'll be self sufficient and enjoying your hard work from the comfort of your cozy home(s) :)\t\t\t\t\t
