\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight now, I cannot recommend this game. It still needs a ton of work. I like grindy games (I played ark on official servers), but I have a feeling the devs have never actually tried to play their own game. In 3.7 hours of game play, I couldn't even get the materials needed to finish the starting tasks.

In real life, fiber can be gathered from numerous types of plants and trees, but in this game, you have to look for small plants, each one only giving you one fiber. They are few and far between. Everything in the start takes fiber, and a lot of it. In over 3 hours, the only things I managed to make were a campfire, one foundation, 1 wall, the base control unit, the bow four arrows, a shirt, and an animal trap (in hopes of getting chickens to get feathers).

The animal trap requires two fruits to bait it. So far I have only found fruits in loot crates, and only three. I bated it to get me one chicken, which only yielded two feathers. One feather only gives two arrows. You can pick your arrows back up if you can find them, but good luck. Aiming with the bow is nowhere near realistic. You can't sight down the arrow to have any idea of where it is going to go. One of my tasks is to kill a small animal with the bow. I have no more fruit to get another chicken, so unless I get lucky and can catch one by hand, I have no way to get more feathers, so have to hope I can kill something with 4 arrows (yeah right).

I will check out the game again once it has had further development, and edit this review accordingly, but have little hope for this game at this point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came across this game by accident, since I played similar games like RUST, Hurtworld, Ark etc. While I can appreciate a game with a steep learning curve, I think after 40 years of playing games I should be able to get there pretty quick.

Subsistence is by far and without a doubt, one of the worst games I have come across in some time. While it is a survival game, it is just stupid and ridiculous. Harvesting anything you need to survive just the first couple days is almost impossible. The pistol seems to fire rubber bullets as they do not take down ANY of the HUNDREDS of predators you run into. Literally every 100 feet there is a bear or wolf or mountain lion to kill you. Your hatchet has little to no affect and the bullets are useless. The animal basically runs up and instant kills you. When they kill you they level up and are harder to take down than the impossible they already were.

Now you respawn with just a hatchet again and 1500+ meters from your body. So you run frantically trying to get back to your body, zig zagging between the wolves and bears and run out of stamina long before you get to your body. You climb on top of a large rock to rest and then a bear comes at you right THROUGH that boulder and instant kills you. Rinse and repeat. Now you have died about 5 times you have to look at all the skulls in the distance to determine which is about to despawn and hope to get there without dying and before the sun sets. Because if the sun goes down and you dont have the mats farmed again to build a fire, you freeze to death during the night.

Dont bother trying to catch a rabbit to eat. If you chase them they actually run towards bears or wolves so you will be attacked. If youre lucky to hit them with the gun, usually takes at least 2 bullets. Run up to collect your kill, get killed by a wolf.

Chickens? Nope! These arent acutally chickens, they are road runners with unlimited sprint. You cant whack them with your hatchet, you have to get lucky and corner them and hit the E key real quick to pick them up. If you dont do anything with them, they die pretty quick in your inventory.

Resources? Takes a while to learn to spot all the fiber you need to use to craft things. All the crafting you do is slow as hell, but at least you can queue up a bunch of things while youre running around and running for your life.

Stupid loot boxes just randomly spawn all over the map. Some need lock picks to open. Never did get around to figuring how how/where to get a lock pick.

Dont drink the water, youll be poisoned and die.

Nothing about this game makes sense, it is all a bunch of random crap thrown into a square map that is fenced off at the borders. This game is not even alpha material, still easily pre-alpha at best. If you want to play a game where all you do is run around and die and scream WTF constantly, than this is the game for you. It is nothing but an infuriating cluster f*ck of a game and really should not be sold on Steam.

I have requested a refund after trying really hard to like this game. I can't, it is just horrible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Subsistence" is by far my favorite survival game of all time. I've played many of them, eventually either completing them or becoming bored with them. But after logging nearly 2500 hours playing "Subsistence", I'm still crazy about this game. "Subsistence" is a very polished "early access" game. Not once have I experienced a bug with this game.
WARNING>>> "Subsistence" is such an extremely realistic world, you may not want to leave! From the sights of the swaying trees and flowers, to the sounds of the wind and wildlife cries. From the crackling sound of wood burning, to the deafening sound of weapon fire. Everything with detail, everything to perfection.
If you like to build things, "Subsistence" is the place for you. Literally, the sky is the limit.
The game has 365 days of varying 4 season weather (winter, spring, summer, fall), or you can decide which season to play. Same with game difficulty, you control the game difficulty (from easy to hard).
The creator of "Subsistence" is Cold Games. It's captain (we call "CG"), is sort of a "one man band". This game is his ship, and he is dedicated to making the voyage even better with each update. And although some players complain about the time between each update (sometimes 5-6 months apart), they forget that this is an early access game, still in development, by one individual (not a large game company), and I for one look forward to each update with great anticipation.
Lastly, some players may think "CG" stands for "Cold Games", but some of us know it really stands for "Creative Genius".
