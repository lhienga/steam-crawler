\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually love early access survival games, and generally give em time to adjust. But in this case I requested a refund. If this really launched in 2016, then I dont understand how the "normal" difficulty can be so one sided on the ratio of plant growth (for fiber harvesting) vs the overwhelming spawn numbers for predatory animals in every direction that aggro to you upon rendering distance yet never aggro to each other, though they would never realistically "not" aggro to each other, and are entirely too close to one another not to fight. You get 2 plants per sq mile for fiber, 1 fiber each. You need 10 plants to make a bow. Youre in a wooded prarie type area, and theres only .02% of all available plants around you thats harvestable for fiber. Legit like every 30 or so plants you walk across, only 1 of them is fiber giving. Theres 6 predatory animals within a 200 yard radius of your character at all times, regardless the direction you run. Axe's do not seem to damage the animals at all. You get 2 shot by any animal. Its impossible to build a bow to defend yourself on "Normal" difficulty without dying and losing what you did find. All of this could be avoided if the multitude of plants around you supplied fiber, instead of the manipulated single plant that is rarer than a tulip in a desert being the only source of fiber. If I EVER would have got a bow made, each arrow requires "nails" by the way. So theres that. I dont know how the Natives ever survived in North America without "nails" for their arrows. Not rock fragments / flint, sticks and cordage or something historically accurate like that. Manipulated resource difficulty is too obvious and too present in game. Too much "artificial difficulty" blots out the rest of the game, which doesnt seem bad otherwise. You can tell a LOT of work went into the game, yet at the same time its insane to think that the crafting requirements manipulation and resource/predator spawn ratio was thought healthy enough to leave as is for 4 1/2 years.\t\t\t\t\t

I so badly want to give this a positive review. I've played another 10 hours of this game and I've only gotten more frustrated playing this game. For the record, I love grinding in games, in 7D2D all I do is mine and build, in Raft I'll just float for hours collecting resources, I played No Man's Sky at launch and did nothing but resource collect.

This game takes grinding to a new level of tedium. The lack of basic resources is insane. I spent about 5 hours on a new game trying to get back to making a workbench with the hopes that once I am mid-game I would be able to enjoy it more.

After 5 hours I had just built my base beacon and didn't want to deal with the hunters considering that I had 3 foundations, a wall, and a roof. I changed the settings to easy and turned off hunters and it took me another 5 hours to find enough nails to build a 2X3 hut with a roof... For about 3 hours I didn't find a single nail then I'd randomly find 40, then nothing.

There just isn't any early game development and balancing. 3 hours to find a nail is not enjoyable. To add to that I died several times because I also couldn't get resources to start fires every time I died. If you die in a storm, at night, you're just going to keep dying because you're likely to respawn just far enough away so that you'll freeze to death by the time you arrive to your sad base to find your fire ran out of fuel and you have nothing to ignite it.

If this game had some sort of basic non-RNG resources it would be fun, really fun. But you just die over and over again trying to get to the mid-game. I'll update the review once I've played from the workshop on but I doubt it will change.

Just to add into the lack of balancing. I could build a 9mm with 30 rounds more easily than I could build a bow and arrows. I think now I still only have a handful of arrows. It would be nice to see some spears, rock knives, or anything that can be used before guns. Although then the animals would have to be balanced because they're far too over powered.

One last thing, it would be nice to be able to scroll through all items in the tool bar/tool belt. It's odd that you can't.

Original Review:
I really like this game or the idea of the game. It's fun and the Hunter mechanics are great, it's like Rust just without the other ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ players. I want to keep playing this game because it is challenging but there needs to be some serious balance to the game.

The one that most people have pointed out is nails. The 5 hours I played the game I spent almost the entire time trying to find loot bags to get nails. I played multiplayer with a friend and we barely had enough resources to build a 3x2 structure with walls only on one side and 2 roofs. The grind to get these resources wasn't terrible but the outcome was disappointing. We were attacked by a bear and Hunters at the same time. For some reason the bear honed in on us like a missile and only killed us then ran away from the Hunters.

It took us 30 mins of dying and respawning to kill the 2 hunters who had shotguns and rifles while the best we had was a pistol with no ammo and our axes. As soon as we killed the hunters the bear returned and killed us every time we tried to recover our gear. The bear also spent the last 20 mins we played destroying our small hut and the few things we were able to build like a fire, a generator, our one chest, and a couple of other things. We gave up when the bear leveled up for the 9th time with full health after killing one of us.

We didn't want to give up 5 hours of effort because of a bear that suddenly wouldn't leave that one spot no matter what we did or didn't do.

I'd be happy to come back to this game again in the future when some of the early game mechanics are better sorted out. Maybe have a smoother progression of building options, like thatch or fiber (and add in way more fiber because finding that is absolutely obnoxious when it's one of the most basic building materials. It took me almost 45 mins to find 10 fiber at the beginning of the game).

And like I mentioned, I love the Hunter mechanic but they progressed way too quickly. We only played 5 hours and there were already 3 camps around us and they had advanced weapons while we were still scavenging for basic goods. It wouldn't be so bad if they leveled with you or you could easily remove an early base.

Speaking of, why do animals level up yet the player has no levels? It seems like an overly punishing mechanic to have a bear level up every time it kills you only to make it impossible to kill.

Anyway, I think this is a great game, I'd like to see what happens to it but I think it seriously needs some balancing and some early game additions so that it doesn't rely entirely on looting for basic materials.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really really really want to like this game but I just can't do it. I want to give it the benefit of the doubt for being in Early Access still, but it's going on what, five years? I admire the work done by this small company (is it just one person?), the game is actually really well done and has tons of potential, but there are a couple game mechanics that aren't even unbalanced, they feel straight up hostile to the player. I'm looking at you, fiber.

For being one of the most necessary building supplies, it sure is in short supply! You'll literally wander around for 15 minutes just to find enough fiber to build one wall of your house. But by then you'll have died seventeen times and respawned in areas that you have no idea how to get back to whatever semblance of a base you were trying to build.

I'm also not a fan of the game's reliance on loot crates. For example, you need nails to build walls, but you don't have any way to make nails (at least starting out), so you HAVE to find them in crates. For how realistic the game tries to be about survival, that's so not a realistic scenario.

I could go either way on the "crafting supplies to make supplies" mechanic. E.g. needing to find fibers to turn into cordage before you can use them in walls and such. It seems like an unnecessary step and makes crafting more complicated than it needs to be, but at least with that one, you get used to it fairly quick.

I truly wish I could give this game a neutral rating because it has many redeeming qualities and should have been one of my favorite games. But at the end of the day, it's just not fun to play, it's punishing and frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts like they drop you in a bear exhibit at the zoo with an ax and a pair of pants on and go "good luck"... You cant turn around without running into something that's about to eat you and it does so in 2 hits. There seriously needs to be like 90% less animals in this game. The animals level up when they kill you which makes the game harder the worse you are instead of raising their level when you kill them to keep them a challenge. The hunters spawn in with multiple guns and high level clothing before you've gotten off the ground and raid your base and kill you in 1 shot (or grenade in my case). If you do manage to kill a hunter you dont get their guns or clothing (because why should realism be a thing?), but you do get some halfway decent loot like food, gun parts, and seeds. Let me stress this here... YOU NEED GUN PARTS TO MAKE AND UPGRADE GUNS. The only ways to get them are the random spawning locked cases (which consume lock pics) or killing guys who are way better armed than you... good luck.

I'm also not sure if its supposed to happen but whenever I died and went back to my stuff some of it was missing. Like I had 5 or 6 9mm rounds made, died to a bear, ran the 20 miles back to my body and had 2 bullets. Maybe the stuff in your pack decays during the 10 minutes you have to get back to your pack? I dont know. Either way that should not be a thing. Its super annoying.

You chop on trees and they never fall over they just stop giving wood. You need WAY too much wood to make even the simplest things. Look up how many different boards they make out of one log in a real saw mill... they get like 15 different boards out of 1 log not 3 logs to make 1 board! Plant fibers aren't just grab any plant like in Ark or 7 days to die. You have to go hunting for specific plants to get fiber from. the fiber you pick up doesnt craft cloth for clothing. It makes cordage and fire starting materials. Cause that makes perfect sense!

If you want to make a shirt you have to get lucky to find cloth in a random drop, rip your starting pants to rags, or kill a bunch of animals and get 1 cloth from each of them. Or you can make a base, build a base computer, craft a crop plot, run around to dozens of drops till you get a cotton seed and plant that cotton seed. Then after waiting 3 or 4 days for it to grow you get 3 cotton out of it... which yields 1 cloth and it doesnt leave the plant in the plot either! You have to plant a new one and wait! Plus it doesnt give you a seed when you pick the cotton to grow another one so you better hope you found more than 1 seed! You need like 5 cloth to make the basic t shirt! That's right! You dont even start out with a shirt on! Its -5C at night in March (the default month to start out in)... God help you if you start in january!

Lock pics take 18 refined iron chunks (meaning you need to make a refinery), 2 boards (i.e. 6 logs from trees), some scrap iron, and 500 electricity from your base computer to make. Its literally a bent piece of metal!!! A wall mounted light only takes 250 electricity, some scrap metal, and some wood! But you have to have a lock pick to open the secure crates that MAY have gun parts in them (the ones you can also get from the hunters if you haven't turned them off prior to starting your game so they dont raid your base and steal all your stuff), because if you dont find these randomly spawning and despawning cases and have a lock pick on you (while youre dodging bears and wolves every other step) you cant upgrade your gun! That's right folks... YOU CANNOT craft things that you have to have to try to defend yourself from a 2 hit skull crushing death via grizzly bear without finding a random case while you have a lock pick on you or killing said better equipped hunters. Meaning you are going to die A LOT in this game.

The best part about dying in the game is when you respawn (even if its on your bed that you have to PURCHASE respawns for with base electricity) you will spawn "poisoned" and only have 55 of your 80 health and stamina. You can make an antidote but it takes 6 medicinal plant leaves, 6 fish oil (which means you have to craft a fishing reel and tackle [which is used like ammo for the reel], and catch enough fish to give you that much oil), then harvest 5 kelp and the liver of an animal... only problem with that is you have to be able to kill one of the wolves or bears that's been eating you every few seconds and leveling up every time they do, in order to get a liver. Then if you do manage to cure yourself and say jump off of a rock about 10ft up you die instantly and when you spawn... yep, poisoned again. And dont think that you can climb rocks to get advantage of the animals either... They can climb up a sheer cliff face and eat you like you were on a flat prairie. Dont try getting in the water either... they can swim faster and dive deeper than you too. Lastly on the animals the only way to get sinew is to kill a moose or a cougar... If a wolf stands up to several bullets imagine how hard it is to kill a moose! Just to make some rope! Nah fam I'm out.

I know its also sort of a trope of these games but can we get a better model for the starter weapons? 5 pieces of scrap metal, 4 boards and 2 pieces of string dont immediately turn into a beretta 92fs. Maybe something like fallout4's pipe pistols?

The inventory management is absolute garbage. There isn't even a way to sort it! Forget about holding shift and clicking on something to transfer it into the storage crate and having it stack with other like items... nay nay! You have to pick each thing up from your inventory and place it in the box by hand... just ew.

Its a great looking concept though and I'd love to see what it becomes but currently I wouldn't recommend people spend money on it. Maybe they should change the name to grindfest instead... that would give you a better idea of what you're in for. That's coming from someone who put 800hrs into 7 days to die on insane difficulty, solo and only quit playing because they got bored of always winning (seriously I was doing the hardest missions with a club), And over 2000hrs into Ark Survival Evolved as well... so I'm not terrible at survival crafting games by any means and dont give up on them easily... but this game is BROKEN.

If you want to play a game a lot like this but with more polish and dinosaurs play ark survival evolved. If you want to play a game like this but with more polish, terrain modification, and zombies, play 7 days to die. I feel like the only people who played this game played it because they got it for free or they bought it because a big tiddy streamer girl was playing it. Seriously all of the forum posts I find are from 2016-2018 on how to do things. There are even several screenshots here on steam from someone called Elaine.France who has 44 total hours in the game but all of her guns are upgraded, all of her walls are glass and metal... so yeah either this game has changed drastically or b00bs get you free sh!t.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a pretty "light" gamer. When I do play, I prefer something that allows me to be creative, to relax, and to get immersed in a beautiful world (as opposed to high-octane games that are too stressful). I should also note that my generation of "gaming" was when the NES first came out.

This game has me hooked. It is definitely challenging and can get quite involved, but I absolutely love the scaffolding involved in building an advanced base. I find I really have to stop and think about what my daily mission is. Sometimes it is as trivial as harvesting lumber, while other times it is about finding loot, mining, or lake-diving for pearls, sandstone ore, and kelp.

The game mechanics are excellent, in my opinion. The sounds are satisfying and are about as realistic as I could hope for. Hunting and fishing can be quick, or can require strategy and patience. When I finally succeeded in harvesting my first chicken with a bow-and-arrow, I felt such joy and satisfaction.

I play the game on "easy" with "no hunters", and it is plenty challenging for me. Again, I am a bit of an "old-timer" when it comes to gaming. In a world where many of the survival games are cutthroat and borderline depraved, this game is a well-thought-through gem that keeps me coming back. It is a testament to the idea that games can be thought-provoking and fulfilling without gore (that said, I know there is an option to have graphic deaths).

As a mathematician with experience in coding, I can only imagine how much work it must have been to be a solo developer on a game this complex. What a tremendous accomplishment! Kudos to the developer and thank you for a great experience.\t\t\t\t\t
