\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOutlast Trials has been extremely enjoyable since the day I purchased it. There are moments where the game can continue to give you jump scares even if you've played through the same programs multiple times (looking at you skinner man).

There's a good amount of progression in the game that can keep you playing for more than a couple of weeks, and a good amount of replayability.

All programs are balanced around party size, if you play through a program solo, you'll encounter less enemies and task requirements to get to the next area or complete is lowered, where as if you play with friends, depending on the party size, there'll be more tasks to complete the trial along with more enemies, along with one of them being exclusive to playing in a party, but don't let it discourage you from playing with friends, the game does a great job with co-op and solo.

Each player has their own room to decorate, along with being able to change their characters cosmetics, aside from the DLC cosmetic that'll be available on release, everything else can be earned by playing, there's also a lobby where players can enjoy mini games such as arm wrestling (using skill checks similar to dead by daylight) and chess.

I hardly put out reviews, but I wanted to write one for the outlast trials for anyone who's unsure about purchasing it, and it's definitely worth it with or without friends. If you're still sceptical, there's plenty of game play videos available to watch before you decide purchasing :)\t\t\t\t\t
