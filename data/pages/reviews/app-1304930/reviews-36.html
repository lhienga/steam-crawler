\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most addictive, re-playable horror, and maybe even stealth games ever made. It's so fun and tense. It might not be the scariest horror game out there(it's far from that), but it sure is one of the most fun, and incredibly tense ones.
Red Barrels might have just created their magnum opus with this. You'll get a lot of memorable CO-OP experiences with you friends. It's pretty much the perfect CO-OP experience.
The optimization and graphics are exactly what you want for a PC game.
Horror and Stealth fans must NOT skip this game. A must-buy.
The only complaint that I have (so far) are the overly grind-y and ridiculous achievements/badges which adds little to nothing to your experience. Oh yeah, and it would be perfect if Red Barrels comes out with an update that gives us the option to put in music from Outlast 1/2. Outlast Trial's soundtrack is tense and does give you them chills but it's far from being as memorable as the previous two games, ESPECIALLY Outlast 2; when you take your time to compare the music to the previous games' tracks, it's a little... disappointing?
Oh, and the worst part is the fact that you have to go inside the in-game folder files to change your Field Of View. Are you kidding me? It's 2024 and we still don't have Field Of View sliders in new game releases?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIDK how I feel about this game. I really enjoy the co-op aspect to it. BUT I kinda was expecting more of a story here. like a outlast 1/2 but with my friends. instead you play these "trails" with up to 3 other players and play enough of the trails to earn your characters "freedom"(which just basically seems like its a prestige system). I don't really feel like I get any more information on the outlast universe unless I dig for it a little through documents scattered throughout the levels. The gameplay is really enjoyable and the replay value is here (sorta). The replayability is great here BUT suffers due to the fact that your playing the same levels over and over and over again just at different difficulties and different level modifiers. which helps spice it up a little but loses my engagement. If you're going to play this game I HIGHLY suggest playing it with friends. (I did not).

Overall not a bad game, but I'm hoping that the next game in the franchise goes back to its roots with a story driven experience. :)\t\t\t\t\t
