\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen playing this since the early access release, and familiar with the Outlast franchise since the beginning. The short of it is: yes. Now that 1.0 is finally here, I can recommend this adrenaline rush of a game.

Movement and traversal is at it's best here in the franchise, with the ability to slide, jump, hide and smash/close doors in snappier, more refined way over the singleplayer games. The obtuse storytelling that veterans expect is still here, with players needing to look at visual storytelling and read documents to put the narrative together.

But what most might be interested in -- how are the scares? With most co-op horror games, the fright factor will only take you so far. With Outlast Trials, I think, with all things considered, it does a great job of terrifying or (in the case of The Toy Factory) mortifying people rather well. Will it frighten you as much as Outlast 1? No. Will it frighten you /despite/ being a co-op game? Absolutely. And that's where others in the genre usually fail.

Once you've seen all the trials, the scare factor might wane, but that is where another factor comes into play. When you're ready to tackle the harder modes, stress and adrenaline become the moving factors. There is a level of intensity to pushing through the harder modifiers that you need to experience with your friends. You'll sweat bullets.

Audio is quite literally the best in the business when it comes to horror-based audio design. There is no comparison. The lightning is extremely good for this genre, speaking as someone who knows how to use Unreal Engine myself. If you have a good PC and can run with raytracing, Outlast Trials is a beautiful yet horrifying set upon the eyes.

So what are you waiting for? Your "voluntary" therapy awaits. Let's get better together.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSilly, spoopy, gory fun with fanatical and hyper-violent elements. It's not really my thing but if you are in the mood for a mindless gorefest then have at it. Everything the game has to offer is thrown at you as soon as you begin to play and there is not much of a story to follow unless you REALLY go looking for it. The trials are fun and they do have their own unique objectives but they quickly loose their shock value after a few runs. This, of course, is nothing but completely expected for multiplayer levels and there is absolutely nothing that can be done about it. This game is not scary to me because there is nothing this game has to offer other than immediate shock value which quickly desensitized me to everything. Now of course I would be terrified if I were a reagent but portraying the concept of fear through a video game is difficult because of the lack of true danger. All in all this is a fun multiplayer game that provides silly and goofy fun. Though i still do like to play it every once in a while when I am in a survivally mood. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tRed Barrels have once again outdone themselves with another great installment to the Outlast Universe. Outlast Trials has the same grit and horror that Outlast 1 and 2 were known and praised for, yet Trials steps it up a notch by giving players the option to experience this game in two ways: solo or with friends/randoms.

This game's sound design and musical scores add to the scary experience and the enormous amounts of cosmetics you can unlock for both your room and your character are jaw dropping. The enemy designs are grimy and truly may shake someone to their core. There is also a good amount of lore for those who have enjoyed dissecting Outlast's files and dialogue in the first two games.

The official release of the 1.0 version means an influx of new players but there are also a fair amount of old players returning. As someone who has gotten fairly deep into the game (although with only 1 rebirth under my belt), it was refreshing to find a lobby quick and to see that the load times have greatly improved.

I was lucky enough to experience the beta testing and upon completing the tutorial and playing a few trials, I was roped back into this world and wanted to see more. If you like the original games and you want to see what it'd be like to experience something of that sort with friends, grab yourself a copy of the game.

Let the trials begin!\t\t\t\t\t
