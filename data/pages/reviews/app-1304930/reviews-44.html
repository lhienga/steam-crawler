Today I decided to left this game behind, after I reach 600 reborns. New updates will bring me back for a period of time, but probably not forever like the Early Access does. RB created something unique, but sadly they don´t know how to handle this situation right. My tip: to make this game easier and turn your biggest ingame achivement (reborn) to sh*t is not the right way. Cheers\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWaited for this game for years, and it didn't disappoint. Due to not having any friends who play it, I have only put in 6 hours. That was enough to play most of the base content.

If you're coming off of the mainline story Outlast games, this provides starkly painted backstory to foundations of the Murkoff corporation, in that same classically sickening Outlast over-the-top cruel essence. As a solo player, though, I wouldn't recommend it for solo purposes.

This game is definitely best played with friends. Singleplayer mainline Outlast games are built on linear pathways for the lone P1 camera to progress through whilst evading scripted enemy events; This game, being 4P lobby style missions in Outlast fashion, pose more as a unique and infinitely better alternative to Phasmophobia(and other common co-op horror) rather than it does actually mechanically feel like classic Outlast games.
Before it came out, I called it "Outlast 3", but soon discovered that this is instead its own type of creature.

Character creation, personalization and cosmetic shop elements. Gameplay based around evading enemies and achieving particularly odd tasks, using scavenged items which each have a purpose. There are "rigs" you can use(only one type of rig at a time) which work as subclasses.

In short-
The theme, characters, render, and story is fantastically dark and potently saturated in satirical government horror subgenre elements.
The gameplay itself is good as well, but miserably frustrating if solo.\t\t\t\t\t
