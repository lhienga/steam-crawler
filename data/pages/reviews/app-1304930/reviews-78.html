\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI believe this is by far one of the most bang-for-your-buck-game out there.

**DISCLAIMER** I will say the things that I enjoy:
1.Progression in the game is very linear.
2. Creative styles to the game making you find your preference in play style. (Aggro, Defensive & Variable)
3. The increasing in speed as you learn new maneuvers, shortcuts and preservation of resources.

The things I do not enjoy:

1. What I found after achieving rebirth 6 times is the mundane and redundant aspect to the game. You definitely begin to hit a brick wall in this game for sure.
2. The development of the Antagonists is slightly incorrectly tuned, leaving us getting poor interactions and false hits. An example: When hit by a Grunt, if you are overlapping the Grunt you inadvertently become stuck between them and can sometimes die in one fell swoop.
3. Everything becomes so painfully repetitive. Yes, you're doing previous trials to become successful in Program X, but there is no real difference in Program X and leaves me feeling like "Ah, so they quickly worked something to kind of was our throats down with something.

ALL AND ALL: I enjoyed the come up of this game, having a full week of free time and an appetite to continue on with this game, my friends and I successfully beat the game in a week. There is plenty of content, definitely worth every penny! I would totally try and play this COOP and NOT alone! It's very doable alone, but COOP makes it so much more fun and faster!

Very well done Red Barrel, I've enjoyed all of the Outlast games growing up, hopefully there's more to come and this isn't just a nostalgia game like the rest!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Full release has added more content and restructured the progression system a bit. It's interesting and i now have renewed interest in playing. They could still work on the horror elements a lil more but overall this is still a great game with tonnes of potential.

I've played the rest of the Outlast series so i am familiar with basic controls mechanics and whatnot. To fresh eyes this game can be difficult to start with. after about 20-30hrs of gameplay i started to figure out the mechanics and enemy limits and worked out the objectives and maps to the point i no longer feel the fearful immersion i did at the start. Human condition amirite. So basically after 109hrs, i now feel desensitised as i feel I've mastered all there is to know about this game and am now at the stage of optimising my routes and aiming for A+ scores each trial so i can unlock the best loot. This game will reach a peak for me where i won't feel the need to play anymore and i'm going to guess within another 20hrs of gameplay.

I hope that there are efforts made to increase the fearful immersion in the game as the enemy AI can be easily exploited once their habits are worked out. Additionally a lot of the loot is a bit lacklustre and some feel like re-skins. I would love for there to be more prestigious rewards for become Reborn (this game's prestige system). Overall i recommend it solo and with friends and also not to give up when you're first starting out, because as the theme of the game implies, you will get better with time and exposure...
