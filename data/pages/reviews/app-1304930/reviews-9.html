\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start, I wouldn't call myself far above average but I am a competent player capable of finishing program ultra by myself and have rebirthed 3 times before the way Program X worked was rebalanced, with one of those times being completely solo.

This game is phenomenal and absolutely wonderful to play in a group. The setpieces, challenges you face and characters you face off against are all memorable and well-written, the gameplay is just confusing enough to force you to think and plan ahead but not confusing enough to warrant a walkthrough before every new mission. Objectives are intuitive, easy to memorize with just enough challenge to force you to slow down and rationalize the situation without bringing things to a standstill. The AI is brilliant, albeit slightly devolving in chase (most enemies tend to go deaf after starting a chase and follow you to where they last saw you before hearing anything again), but it is just fine when you are using all the tools given to the player to your advantage. I sincerely recommend and my criticisms coming forth are more as a way to communicate with the developers.

My only complaints are the layout of the Fun Park map and the way the Destroy the Sex Toys' conveyor belts work.
The layout in Fun Park is rectangular, but most objective areas in the starting area are circular with enemies patrolling the middle while you are forced to the outskirts, leading to a party constantly dealing with enemies being taken to the outskirts in chases, leaving everybody vulnerable and losing points off your grade.
I have no exact idea on how to fix this as removing any enemies or adding an additional safe path would make this area a complete joke, but something needs to be done.
The second indoor area has this issue persist as the only ways upstairs are on one side of the map while the entire way to the stairs is riddled with enemies or noise traps, which both reduce your score and bring enemies to you to injure you/hinder objective progress.
This can be fixed with a single additional staircase, leaving the middle area still a hotspot for danger as enemies should be guarding objectives but leaves the player with movement options on both sides of the map.

The conveyor belts are confusing to a degree that took several failed attempts to get the right idea - A conveyor that says it leads to the main crusher part instead only leads to the previous zone's drop-off area, not the destination it says it leads to. This confusion caused my ~5 failed trial count to go over the double digit count as I refuse to play with guides because of my desire to be self-reliant in a game that severely rewards self-reliance.
While I understand the requirement for difficulty in a challenge like this, which can be cheesed via not even activating any of the generators and just picking up all the boxes and dropping them if they make noise, but the wording on the conveyor belts needs to change at minimum to make the way of progression more clear.
Also, turn on the conveyor belts in the A zone AFTER the first box has been deposited into the compactor. Albeit embarrassing, it at least caused me and my buds to initially drop the box into a conveyor, become confused with the trapped boxes as we didn't think to turn on the generator to identify the correct box and to assume that we had hard-locked the trial. Anybody that complains about the conveyors' speed is coping. They move just fast enough to require attention without it being mind-numbing, the wording on conveyors is what is confusing.\t\t\t\t\t
