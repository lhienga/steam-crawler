\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let the EA tag fool you; Outlast Trials is probably one of the best games to come out in Early Access. It has enough content to feel like a pretty full game. It took me about 22 hours to get through the first 3 Trials, and I've added another 3 just doing the first 2 parts of the new one, and I haven't even touched the "weekly" trials they've added in yet, which adds a twist to the Trials that you already know.

Let's start with the Rig:

Your life line and one true friend through everything (until it gets disabled in some areas in the new map). You have access to 4 different types of rigs at the moment: Stun, X-Ray, Heal, and Blind. Blind and Stun are relatively similar, though the Stun rig gives you a little modified radio that explodes on impact whereas the Blind rig gives you a mine you place on the ground. X-Ray lets you see through walls and mark enemies as well as items for your team to see. Heal emits a healing mist and can improve your sanity. Personally, I like to run the Stun rig, as I find myself running from enemies with very little idea of a plan most of the time. Rigs also come in tiers, so you can upgrade them with tickets that you earn from gaining levels through surviving the trials.

Enemies:

The game has decent enemy variety. Nothing too crazy. Where the enemies really begin to shine is with the Prime Assets. Currently, you only have 2 Prime Assets currently in game and those are:

Leland "Why you runnin'" Coyle and Mother Gooseberry featuring Dr. Futterman. HOWEVER, there is a third PA and that is Prescott Whitehorn, who will most likely be added in the next big content drop.

Coyle is the cop that you'll see in the prison and courthouse Trials, and Gooseberry will be in the Orphanage and "Fun" Fair. The areas you find them in really attest to who they are as characters, as Coyle is very much on the "justice" train whereas Gooseberry is more whimsical and definitely won't stab you in the mouth with drill-duck.

There are also plenty of other enemy types running around, including X-Ray eyes, A dude with a tank filled with gas that drives you insane if he catches you, a guy that will scream so loud it'll burst your ear drums if you get close, yourself ( who's definitely not an imposter), and 2 variations of giant hulking monstrosities that will smack smack fear into you.

I didn't mention one enemy type, and that's because he's not really real. He's all in your head, but some called it The Skinner Man.

Maps:

The maps in this game are amazing. Each Trial has it's own theme, and the theme carries throughout the main trial plus the side missions. There is procedural generation, so each time you play a trial again, you won't be able to take the exact same path, which is fun and keeps things fresh. Each key item is also placed differently each time, so you can't just run to the same spot each time you play through again and again.

Each map is also littered with traps. Some of these are simple and don't really effect you like noise traps (broken glass or hanging cans), but some can really mess you up like the trapped doors that launch a spike into your chest and then proceed to emit a gas that drives you insane if you can't get it out fast enough.

Prescriptions and Amps:

These are items that buff you as a character. They can't be taken away through the trial mutations or gates that disable your rig. These are the things that you can ALWAYS count on to be there. They do very simple things like give you more health or let you walk across broken glass without making a sound.

Prescriptions go down a list like with your Rig, but you can buy Amps in any order (assuming you have them unlocked). Amps can make or break your build though, but don't be afraid to experiment.

Cosmetics:

These are just fun and good looking. If you play well enough and "beat" the game, you earn one Legendary outfit, but the rest of the cosmetics can be earned by leveling up and completing trials for the first time. Some items are also available from the Limited-Time modes like the Christmas and Halloween events. You can also earn cosmetics for your room, which just lets you add a nice touch to an otherwise bleak and sad main facility.

Overall, if you haven't heard of this game or are on the fence about it, don't be. This is what EA should be, and even on launch this game had so much to offer. Updates to the actual content side of things aren't particularly speedy (we waited almost 7 months for the new trial), but Red Barrels is going for the quality over quantity mindset. That being said, there probably will be a ton of content so long as they can keep dreaming up these nightmare scenarios, and we all know they can.

Lastly, if the Early Access tag scared you off originally, the game launches into 1.0 on March 5th of this year and will include another new Trial, bringing the total to 5 trials and hours upon hours of running around with friends trying to survive.\t\t\t\t\t
