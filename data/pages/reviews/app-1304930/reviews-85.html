\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not going to lie, as great as some of the Outlast games can be, this one has a very weird Goldilocks effect on me.
Let me explain as someone that works a full-time job and trying to enjoy The Outlast Trials on my free time:

Playing solo can be obnoxious:
You can play the trials by yourself if you want to, however you are more than likely going to be spending over an hour on one trial. Most of which you are hiding in the same spot If you're being too careful trying to slowly inch the objective towards its destination. Because someone thought It was a good idea to have way too many enemies roaming around in some of the same locations close to one another. Making it so that if you have to hide in those spots it's nearly impossible to try to do something without getting detected. And the last thing I want to be doing in my free time trying to play a game is spending most of my hour and a half in our trial hiding in the same closet for a good half of the time trying to avoid detection. It's annoying and frustrating to get through these alone, and it's really apparent that Red Barrels Studios primarily focused this to be a multiplayer game rather than a single player. However...

Playing Multiplayer can be boring:
You have people that play this game waaaaay too much to the point where they know exactly what they're doing. So if you're coming in fresh and if you have someone that is at least five times the level that you are, prepared to get carried.
I have had a few games where I was struggling to survive and try to find objectives to help out, only for them to mostly be done because the other people I was playing with already nabbed the objective and solved it without me knowing. Making me feel rather useless in my endeavors and feeling like I've wasted 10 to 15 minutes of my time roaming around aimlessly in hallways while everyone is moving at the speed of a bullet straight towards the objectives. And given the nature and layout of some of the trials, I imagine that some of the objectives are hardly randomized, meaning if you've already completed that trial, you already know what to do and where to go to solve it.

How to enjoy the game "Just right":
So on one end you have trials that are taking too long, and then on the other you have trials there are finishing too fast. Both, to me at least, our very frustrating experiences when trying to enjoy this game.
The only way I can think to be able to enjoy it is to play it blindly with a group of friends who also don't know much about it. Meaning that way you all can start fresh, learn how the game works together and playing the trials at a decent length of time. From the few times I have experienced it, I believe two players is the perfect number to be playing with. Just you and one other person, four people may end up ending the trial a little too early, even if all people playing did not have previous knowledge of how to play it.

In conclusion, I would not recommend playing this game, at least by yourself.
Make plans to play this with someone close to you who hasn't played the game either and enjoy the fun and horrors together. I don't think I'm going to be able to do that now that I have knowledge of some of these Trials myself, and that some of my closest friends have already played quite a number of hours into this game.
That is my best advice to approaching The Outlast Trials, I hope some people found this helpful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tINTRO:

I don’t typically write reviews for the majority of games that I play on Steam, but I wanted to give The Outlast Trials an early access review before the full game releases in March because I love this game so much. Now this review is a short version of an original one that I had, but I had to delete a lot of stuff because it was over the word count. So this review will be me talking about the most important parts from that review. So feel free to skip ahead and read whatever part you want. This review might have some minor spoilers but I’ll try to not give anything huge away just in case someone new is reading this and is interested in playing this game.

MY STATS:

I’ve been playing this game since June and at the time of writing this I have over 650 hours played, have all of the customization and progression, and have 116 Reagents Released/Rebirths. I pretty much have 100% of this game so far and with those kinds of stats I guess I’m considered to be one of the “Veteran Players'' now. I wanted to mention this to show that I’ve been playing this game ever since it came out on Steam for Early Access, so I know what I’m talking about.

DIFFICULTY/CO-OP:

This game is pretty difficult for someone playing for the first time or for someone that’s never really played any of the other Outlast games before, so I highly recommend playing this with friends. You can still play this game solo and it’s still possible to beat the more difficult Trials solo. I also recommend playing with friends because this game is so much fun with more people. “Yeah, that’s the problem with teams…if you don’t win together, then you die together.” -Blisk. This quote from Titanfall 2 best sums up the co-op in this game.

IT’S STILL AN OUTLAST GAME:

One of my biggest worries about this game when first starting out was that Trials wouldn’t feel like an Outlast game. But trust me when I say that The Outlast Trials 100% feels like an Outlast game. It still has the running and hiding mechanics, it mostly has the same Night Vision mechanics, and Outlast still hasn’t lost its identity over the years. Which is something I can’t really say for a lot of other games that I’ve played before. And no, this isn’t a Dead by Daylight copy. I get why people compare these two games and talk about their similarities. Which is fine, but I strongly disagree with the small percentage of people that say that this game is a copy. To me, Outlast Trials is literally just Outlast but with co-op multiplayer, nothing more than that really. And another thing that’s important to mention is that this isn’t Outlast 3. Sure it’s the third game in the series, but Red Barrels have stated that this is a prequel and is not the “official” third game story wise. To my knowledge Red Barrels have said that they’re going to be making Outlast 3 after Outlast Trials is fully done, and I'm also very excited about that.

NOT REALLY A STORY MODE:

One thing that I will say that is really different from the first two games is that Outlast Trials doesn’t really have a story mode. The closest thing to a story mode that it has is the Tutorial in the beginning, and the Endings. I guess you can play the whole game solo and you can kind of count that as a story mode but it’s not really the same in my opinion. If you want to play another Outlast story mode, then the best thing to do is honestly just wait for Outlast 3 after Outlast Trials is fully done.

RELIGIOUS HORROR ELEMENTS:

One of the core aspects I think that make up Outlast is the Christianity/religious horror elements. They’re present in all of the games and It’s definitely here in Outlast Trials. To start off talking about this I want to mention one very important thing: I’m not Christian/religious. I wanted to mention this to tell what my perspective is on this kind of horror, and how you think I formed this opinion. Anyway to start, the religious horror elements are really good and in my honest opinion I think it even works better than Outlast 2. I don’t want to talk about what happens in the actual game because that might be potential spoilers for someone new that might be reading this. But what I love about the religious horror is that it’s all on one map, and all on one program: The Orphanage/Program 3. Because #1: I think that the religious horror works best in Outlast as more of the side horror and not the main horror, unlike Outlast 2. And #2: It also makes the Program it’s in stand out a lot more, which I feel like all of the Programs should be: Unique. I absolutely loved the way Red Barrels handled it in the game.

SMALL DEVELOPMENT TEAM:

One of the things that I feel like I need to remind a lot of the people that play this game is that Red Barrels is a relatively small development team of around 50 people. Why is this important? It’s important because they can’t update this game 24/7. I’ve seen SO many impatient people talk about how this game needs to get more updates/content or this game will die. I understand why they think this, but my biggest piece of advice is be patient and notice that this isn’t like CoD where you have 3+ AAA game development teams working on this. No matter how popular this game will get, it’ll still be an indie game because of how relatively small the team is. There’s nothing wrong with massive development teams, but just give Red Barrels some slack. Sure it would be nice to see more, but for right now I think that they’re doing a good job with the updates on this game.

REPETITIVENESS:

Now one of the biggest criticisms I see for this game is how repetitive it is and how this could lead to them getting bored of the game. That criticism is honestly very fair and I do see why a lot of people think that, but I don’t know if this is just me, but I’ve never really gotten bored of this game once. I’ve definitely felt at some parts it got a little repetitive like trying to go for 100 Reagents Released/Rebirths to get that 100 Trophy, and grinding Program Geister to get all of the cosmetics. But I don’t think I’ve ever truly gotten bored of the game. I’ve also seen people talk about how little replay value this game has. Personally, I heavily disagree with this and I think this game has a ton of replay value. For proof, I just like for you to look at how many hours I have. To be fair, most of the replay value I got from this game is from meeting and playing with a ton of other people. If this game was singleplayer then I would have way less hours in the game. But again I definitely do see how some people thinks that it's kind of repetitive. It’s most likely me that just feels the opposite from this though.

CONCLUSION/FINAL RATING:

I absolutely love The Outlast Trials and I absolutely love the Outlast series as a whole. I know it’s kind of stupid to say this but the Outlast games have a special place in my heart and I still think the Outlast series are still the scariest horror games that you can play. And I think that the Outlast Trials is the scariest horror co-op out there. (And yes, I have played Lethal Company if anyone is wondering.) I think The Outlast Trials is my favorite in the series, but I still think that the first Outlast is objectively better though. I give The Outlast Trials a solid 9/10 and I would highly recommend it to anyone who wants to play a horror game with friends. Or just want to play with friends. It’s obviously on the mature side, but you probably knew this by just looking at trailers/the game rating. I’ve always been very optimistic for this game’s future and I hope Red Barrels still adds more content for this game because I absolutely love it. I even have the 1959 Murkoff Corporation Logo as my profile picture on Steam.

- Sincerely Übercharge!, A Huge Outlast Nerd.
