\r\n\t\t\t\t\tis enjoyable

Unsure what to compare it to, but you progress through levels completing objectives for an ominous psychiatric company. You're held prisoner in a hub area and from there you can pick different themed levels, a police station, orphanage, etc... to partake in.

Within the levels themselves you will be sneaking around completing objectives. Usually something deranged, like lowering a bunch of prisoners into a meat grinder. To complete these objectives you will need to normally locate different objects around the map and interact with them or bring them somewhere. During this there will be enemies walking around, who will attack if they see you. I would say the game is more tense or shocking than it is scary.

When you complete the levels you are evaluated by the company, based off of how you did and then given rewards. I would have expected it to give you higher rankings if you don't use any items/hide/get spotted/take too long, but it is actually quite nice that getting higher rankings means you generally need to do those things. You lose points for getting hurt or triggering traps, but actually using the game mechanics is encouraged which I love.

Higher rankings in levels means more rewards, which are things like new decorations for your room in the hub, clothes, items, cash. This makes the gameplay loop feel nice.

suggest to either play with friends or solo - the matchmaking is kind of trash especially when new. First time I did it I ended up with some high level guy who just ran off and completed the mission without me cause he knew the areas and where everything would be. Considering it was my first time in that level, it wasn't very fun.

And to this day it keeps such great quality and fun aspects to it!

- It is fun to play for me with friends just as much as with randoms!
- There are these moments with the murderous guy stopping right beside you in the dark, and you just clench your cheeks and hold your breath, hoping he doesn't get any closer and notice you. Many very tense moments!
- Ai monsters are quite smart and challenging. They check for hiding spots, lock doors behind them, actively looking for you and don't see you with their back turned, or as they pass you when you sit right by the wall of the door they came through. It makes stealth feel very real, nice and rewarding.
- I don't know if it's just me, but it is immensely satisfying to me, to do every action with your mouse. Picking up, opening/closing, using bashing. Soo so satisfying by just clicking!
- It feels very satisfying to rush the objectives too, when you learn the ropes. Avoiding the dangers with quick thinking and not getting caught in the traps on the way. Although there are always mistakes to be made that will end up being fatal on your fast adventure!

This company made a great great game, so polished and filled with love. Utilizing violence and sexuality in a smart and artistic way. A lovely example of a fun video game.
I cannot wait to see what Red Barrels have in store for the future, and wish to stick around to experience more of it!\t\t\t\t\t
