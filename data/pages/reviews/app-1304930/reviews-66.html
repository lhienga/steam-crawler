Whether you've played the original single-player Outlast games before this or not is irrelevant. Outlast Trials stands above every horror game in the genre. The team at Red Barrels takes the coop horror experience everyone knows and brings it to the highest levels possible in terms of pure adrenaline-inducing horror, excellent animations & attention to the worlds detail, along with masterful audio that I swear will have you screaming wherever you play this.

So what do you do in this game? You are a patient (taken against your will) to essentially an MK-Ultra style mind control facility where the powers that be force you to undergo "Trials" that are meant to harden you and make you stronger in the face of pure horror & this game has so much of it.

Each trial is a different location that has been turned into a grotesque extreme vision of whatever is being portrayed. Your job once inside is to do what Murkoff tells you & then get the hell out. Or die trying. Each character or enemy you encounter is intensely scary and satirical depending on who you get (I'm looking at you Coyle) with what I think is some of the best voice acting in the industry along with truly scary enemies physically as well.

Nothing is scarier after running room to room and being sliced up with everything wanting to hurt you in some way and you finally find a moment of "safety" hiding in a barrel or bathroom stall and you hear Mother Gooseberry's singing muffled on the other side of the wall getting closer to you. Or hearing your friends screams over the radio as you hear them get brutally assaulted by "the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ man" as he chases them attacking the whole time. This game really is ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up. But in the best ways.

If you want a truly horrific coop experience that looks so damn good you'll wonder why other games in the genre aren't even close, with sounds that are so realistic you'll be holding your breath to not miss what's coming around the corner, enemies that are so completely terrifying you'll literally be screaming running for your in-game life like it's real life, with levels that immerse you in every way so much so you'll think you can ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ smell it as the lighting, sound, darkness, and all of the wonderfully done set pieces and assets blend together into what I swear is an experience you have to see to understand. It is the scariest game I have ever played and in the genre it's the best there is.

Are there some negatives to note? Yes. Quickly I'll say this game is the best to play with a friend or several (4 in total). Having your friends there, or even matchmaking while dealing with all this game throws at you is the most intense juggling act, and keeping everyone alive is so not easy. So if you're someone who solos games alone, although I believe you'll still have an amazing time it will not be easy and you'll come out of it like someone who was lost in the woods for 10 years trying to escape.
Are there bugs? Some, but it's been less than 5 in the 60 hours I have played. Some were graphical and I think we had audio issues maybe once.
I know some people might complain there's a lack of variety in the levels but dig this: there are I think 5 main levels currently (with the 6th coming in a month) but the replayability comes with the fact that they take those 5 maps and when they add new trials they'll reconfigure these maps and change them in totally different ways, sometimes unrecognizable with new enemies, different layouts, different objectives and for me the content has not ever lacked.

Some stuff I didn't touch on that's still amazing about this game:
The lobby where you choose your trial you get your own cell/room that can be customized with all sorts of awesome decorations/cosmetics from good looking to guts on the night stand (your choice) kinda like the Sims in Hell. All of the cosmetics are unlocked by playing the game. Each trial rewards you X amount of cash + rewards like clothing cosmetics which are great. In the same lobby you can also play mini-games like arm wrestling & speed chess (which happens to be like the best 3D chess game on the market for some reason?).

I'm sure I missed plenty of good, and maybe even some bad but with this game coming out of early access soon it's already the most polished horror experience on the market next to something like RE: Biohazard but because this is a coop experience also based in reality I think this stands above that as well. It's so well done and I love everything Red Barrels has made here. Completely recommend this experience & I can't wait to play the Toy Factory level when the game goes out from early access which is soon.
