\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm hesitant to call it a "horror" game as the content really veers straight into the realm of shlocky b-movie horror so over the top that it becomes comedic, but it definitely feels terrifying when you're fleeing from machete wielding maniacs through darkened hallways and dodging traps.

It's a great co-operative experience. Solo, not so much, especially as even solo you can't "pause" and there's no save and quit function here. You're in the for the long haul when you start a "Trial". "Challenges" are shorter trials which tend to take less time to complete. Granted, as your skill evolves and you gain access to new Rig abilities or Amps, you become able to complete these missions faster and faster, but it may feel like a grind for some.

At the time of writing I've yet to reach max level and unlock all the abilities. Not that you necessarily need them all, mind you - they are varied enough that some abilities just don't matter if they don't suit your playstyle. Amps are divided into three categories. Some of them are particularly strong and tend to overshadow all other options in their categories, or really only show their value in the unique weekly challenge modes.

They've made some baffling choices about how to handle loading screens which is particularly frustrating. Here's the process to start a trial:
- Load into the Sleep Chamber
- Move to the console that lets you select a trial
- Lock in the trial you wish to play
- Wait for the trial to load enough that you can step into the transit car
- Sit down and watch a 1 to 2 minute video as well as the same "horror" psychotic trip you've seen dozens of times before as you wait for the stage to really load this time (for real)
- Leave the chair
- Enter the stage

Then, whether you finish or fail the trial:
- Sit through a report that serves as a hidden loading screen
- Load into a small chamber with a time delay door that serves as an artificial delay to allow the sleep chamber to finish loading
- Step through the door and walk down a hallway, leading to another transit tube ultimately delivering you to the sleep chamber

All of this means you're spending a lot of time navigating spaces that could honestly just have been a menu. The sleep chamber arm wrestling and chess are fun diversions but the whole setup feels like a consideration made for consoles and its miserable to sit through because it still requires action on your part. It's a loading screen you are required to participate in. It is perhaps the most horrific aspect of the game.

I'd recommend the game as a co-operative stealth experience. Solo stealth fans and horror fans may want to look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t
