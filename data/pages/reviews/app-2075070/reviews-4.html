\r\n\t\t\t\t\tbought this game on a whim, just seemed like my type of experience. I've loved studying computer viruses so a game about spyware similar to Bonzi Buddy? Sign me up!
I went into this game expecting Kinito to be much more mean/a real threat. He just comes across as a very lonely virtual assistant who tries a little too hard. If I had felt like Kinito was dangerous or had more ill intent towards the player, I'd probably have ♥♥♥♥ my pants considering there are some surprisingly effective horror sections in this game (I made a joke about being on the second floor of my house during the bedroom cutscene, I actually felt a little chilled when I saw the ladder in the virtual home section and realized that the game had already thought about that/was drawing parallels. That was cool.)
In terms of criticisms, I think that when the train spawns in the virtual world section, the player should automatically be repositioned so they're looking at it, as it took me a solid 5-10 minutes on my first playthrough to find said train because I walked every other direction without seeing it. There's other smaller nitpicks but it comes down to my personal preference and nothing I'd consider a game flaw.
Glitches, I did notice a few. Occasionally Kinito's voice will cut out if other programs inside the kinitoworld are open. Kinito mistook me for having games open while he was trying to show me something when there was nothing else in the kinitoworld open (No clue what caused that, not replicatible), and on my second playthrough, my video game collection didn't spawn in the virtual section at all, just leaving the room mostly blank. Obviously nothing serious or game-breaking, just little things that could break the immersion sometimes.
I think more of the story should be accessible through the base game without requiring the player to search for it externally, as you can beat the game with all endings (including the secret one) and still have absolutely no clue what's going on. Again, personal gripe.
For a cute game with a short runtime, KinitoPET achieves it's goal and is worth your time and money. It's clearly got a lot of heart behind it and I tip my hat to the dev for getting me to recoil during the hide and seek level. That was amazing.
I really enjoyed this and I'm definitely keeping an eye out for the dev's other projects!

I think my favorite part of the game was the secret ending. I didn't feel bad for KinitoPET until then - now I need a little plushie of him so I can apologize profusely to him and treasure him like the little silly guy he is.

It took me 4 hours to figure out and get all the achievements, and at least a quarter of that was spent trying to beat minesweeper. I had a little pen and paper and was figuring things out the old fashioned way.

So unless you're me (chronically stupid) you'll find everything in about 3 hours give or take.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tdo not look gameplay up, do not interact with the fandom (as an adult who found the game through steam rather than mascot horror youtube)

the game finds clever ways to use your system without damaging it (obviously, wouldn't be on steam otherwise) and a lot of the moments where it does this do get genuinely unsettling, mostly around act 2.
unfortunately it does have some weak spots in the horror department. without spoiling, sometimes it leans too heavily into the banbanness of mascot horror (at times it made me genuinely laugh, hoping this was intentional) and doesn't really deliver a satisfyingly scary conclusion to your 1st playthrough.

however! this game absolutely needs you to play it at least twice, and my achievement hunting brain got a lot more out of this game by doing so. the creepiness factor returns a bit, with new things appearing, and gives you a little taste of what ARGs are like, a la Daniel Mullins games. overall, i enjoyed the game and will go back to losing minesweeper posthaste\t\t\t\t\t
