\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is nothing sadder than a game that has been hyped up when it is, in reality, just a knockoff of a less popular game. KinitoPET is a mascot horror game with a character that doesn't try to be your friend or care about you, but you are expected to care about. The worst part? Almost every aspect of Kinito's personality is taken from the much better written game Buddy Simulator 1984.

And this game is frightening, don’t get me wrong. The scares are well-placed, out of nowhere, tense, and the game does a fantastic job of pushing the boundaries with its fourth wall breaks that make you wonder how far it’s willing to go. But it falls flat when it never actually does. It asks if what you put is your real name, but it doesn’t know or care if you actually put in your real name. It opens your camera, but it doesn’t take a picture to show how much power it has. It messes with your desktop, but it doesn’t actually change anything beyond a couple effects. It threatens to dox you, but it doesn’t find your address.

Of course, it has to be this way to an extent in order to even be allowed on the Steam store, because if it did do anything harmful that’d make it an actual virus. But it doesn’t even give a proper illusion of control. Nothing it does comes up to a meaningful extent, nothing you write or create is shown to mean much besides a couple PNGs that are plastered up later during a short, lifeless 3D section.

Kinito is meant to be your “best friend AI”, but shows absolutely no interest in the player. He has no personality or charm, even in his design with inconsistencies present in each jumpscare. Above all that, he isn’t scary. His over simplistic design doesn’t lend itself well to any of the scares. The designs are too afraid to be interesting that they resort to the bug-eyed, sharp-tooth basics that every other game has already beaten the life out of.

As for how it may have been “inspired” by Buddy Simulator 1984, which first came out in 2021, years before this game’s release… the list goes on and on. The plot revolving around an AI wanting to be the user’s friend, the icebreaker-type questions that it asks you, how it stores files and secrets that can interact and reveal things about the game… if I cared enough to replay KinitoPET for a third mind numbing time, I could list the similarities.

KinitoPET had potential to be a frightening, fourth-wall-breaking horror game that pushed the limits of what the program could do to your computer. But it fell short on its tropes that never became anything, short and unmemorable moments, and obviously stolen ideas from the much better Buddy Simulator.

If you want cheap scares with a boring story and a text-to-speech that can’t even bother to say your name out loud, then this game will be fantastic. But if you’re looking for a game about friendships with AI, and how that AI can become possessive while trying to be your friend, then I really recommend picking up Buddy Simulator 1984 instead so you can see just how many good ideas were turned into mediocre ones in this 90's desktop game.\t\t\t\t\t
