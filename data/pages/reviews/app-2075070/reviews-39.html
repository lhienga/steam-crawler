\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked up this game after i saw a bunch of youtubers playing it, and thinking that it looked cool after reminding me of bonzibuddy

On launch,I ran into issues as this game uses the steam friend system, and crashes if you have a friend in your friends list with an animated icon, so you have to run this game logged out of steam (hence why it says ive only played this game for 0.2 hours).

Generally, I'd say the game's a pretty great example of digital horror, and fact that kinito is basically a massive parody of bonzi buddy is also really good.

The first half of the game kinda fell on deaf ears for me (i think this is because i actively study gamedev and have a healthy knowledge of what a program, let alone one sold on steam can and can't do), but generally it was fine (it felt kinda edgy at times with the spammed placement of hidden dead bodies), but it was generally pretty good, reminding me of classic flash games.

[HEAVY SPOILERS UP AHEAD]
After asking you a series of disturbing questions and kinito signing you up to the membership club, he develops a world for you, based on what you told him and what he found out about you. Kinito also becomes a lot more possessive over you and makes sure youre his one and only friend, which i really like, horrorwise.

He also blurs between the game's fake desktop and your real desktop, and when granting kinito admin perms, i remember manicly saving everything since i was worried kinito would mess with my open projects, which i would consider a great success in terms of horror.

However, i'd say one of the major issues with kinitopet is that digital horror only works when occaisionally sprinkled in, such as the terminals in oneshot that change your background, send you messages on your actual pc. It kind of loses its scariness factor when the whole game is like that.

Despite that, I would highly reccomend kinitopet, after the developer fixes the animated steam profile bug. The new game+ is fun to piece together and reminds me a lot of the ddlc arg.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a cute and interesting game, it really is, but honestly I feel like it shoots its shot way too early. The Web World segment being the first bit kind of makes you expect all of the other horror-related stuff later on. Some of it is more unexpected because of the ways it uses your actual computer, which I did appreciate and was intrigued by, but the scene after Web World where you have to navigate the dark hallway, as well as the corpse being dragged into Sam's house being part of the beginning of the game made everything afterwards feel a bit underwhelming in comparison.

Also, I like Kinito, but he doesn't really feel like a creation from the early 90s; axolotls were way more obscure back then, but maybe that's a nitpick because they did exist. I just feel like a corporation trying to make a marketable mascot probably wouldn't choose an axolotl back then. The secret ending where you delete Kinito and free the other victims from him did hit pretty hard though. I feel bad for the little guy, despite everything.

Overall, I like the content of the game, but the pacing really could've been better. Go ahead and check it out if you're into analog horror and don't mind there being mostly common tropes of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
