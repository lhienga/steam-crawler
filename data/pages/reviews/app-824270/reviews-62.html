\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally I wouldn't recommend it.

The problem with aim training is that you don't see the tangible benefits of doing it for 30+mins every day soon enough, and are better off just aim training in the in-game environment anyway...

I have a lot of experience with aim trainers, I've used voltaic's fundamentals and benchmarks to monitor and track my progress over time and have spoken on a one-to-one basis with people who have 1500hr alone in kovaaks and play it at the highest level, then they joined me for a game on Valorant and played ♥♥♥♥.

It just goes to show that it's not really worth grinding, aim is one of those things where you either have it or you don't, if you're like me and you have decent aim but more of a brain player (in tac-fps) then you are far better off just maxxing out your brain stats, i.e. just focus on in-game decisions, angle advantages, crosshair placement, and general game sense to make the most elo gains.

If you think i'm wrong, please, try for yourself.. just don't come crawling back to this review when you realise you wasted hours of your life to not really improve. It's all based on genetics, upbringing, eyesight, setup.. all these things have an impact on your aim.

It's also to do with age, if you're already over 25 years old you've got better things to do than waste time aim training, just play the game instead.

Before you argue against this, remember that 57 hours is essentially 57 days of using this game, and on top of that i've used other tools/softwares for aim training, and before you argue against that just remember I scored 150ms on a reaction speed test so it's nothing to do with my individual skill.\t\t\t\t\t
