\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a solid game. Detailed representations of many construction processes with just enough abstraction and simplification to make it realistic without being tedious. Has the same kind of 'just one more job' appeal as House Flipper or Power wash sim, and has strong game play similarities to Farming Sim.

The biggest things to mention, imo:

- The game allows you to choose between 3 levels of difficulty per contract, for example on low you might have to place 4-6 roof sections, whereas on high it might be 12+.

- You can also skip either individual subsections of jobs, or entire phases. You don't get the pay or XP for skipping, but if you find particular tasks tedious it's always an option. Fast travel also exists and can be incredibly useful.

- There is a lot of content here. It took me around 30 hours to complete all Europe campaigns on low intensity, and there's still side jobs to do. There's for sure enough content to justify the price at least.

- There is no access to the interiors of buildings once complete, and construction work out of vehicles is generally just press G on a crate of bricks to instantly turn it into a brick wall.

- The economy is pretty easy, but certain upgrade challenges are slightly grindy - for the last level of upgrading your company you need to refuel and repair 50 times each. This happens automatically when vehicles are stored, but for the challenge you need to drive to a fuel station and do it manually.

- The Europe starting campaign recommended a fast erecting crane, but I used it for about 2 jobs before I started finding it unable to reach across the entire job site. Just go for the biggest tower/mobile crane you can for your first, money isn't really an issue after a few jobs. For jobs with digging you can also load soil into a dumper and sell it which is incredibly profitable if slow - the fast option for excavation is just running a bulldozer through.\t\t\t\t\t
