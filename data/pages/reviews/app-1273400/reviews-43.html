\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOPINION:
So far, after 46 hours its disappointing. You constantly ask yourself "did I pay too much?" And the answer is a sorry to say, a resounding YES. Even on promotion this game is not worth the money.

This is good for keeping you busy for a day or two while you wait for the next COD or GTA6 (hint hint) or, ok ok the latest version of pokemon or candy crush or whatever weird and/or wonderful games you guys play.

Point is, this is super fun for the first 10 hours. Everything is new and shiny, and you get nostalgic for a minute. You are back, 10 years old standing next to a construction site and fantasising about driving and operating those big yellow or red machines. They stood like monsters ready to atack. I mean come on, who did not want to drive a CAT dozer down main street and cruch a couple of cars in your way. Ok, so most of us will never get to so this is as close as we are going to get.

Unfortunately, it gets boring and monotonous VERY quickly. You can only drop of so many pallets of wood or steel or whatever with a crane before you look for the “simulate rest” button. Well, you will be looking in vain. There is a “helper” but he/she/them/they (see LGBTQ gender sensitive ROFL) only instantly fills the dump truck with sand, gravel or concrete. So, if you were looking for emersion, consider that broken.

Don’t get me wrong, this a HUGE improvement on 2015, which was utter shayit, but then again anything would have been. I still can’t help myself constantly thinking... what if weltenbauer (developer) did that or what if they did that, because then... well, THEY DIDN’T.

SCORE: a tentative 2/10

GRIPE(s):
Yes a lot

1. Too many niggly bugs that gets VERY irritating very quickly
2. The keyboard is sluggish and very unresponsive, while the mouse is VERY sensitive (yes there is a setting to adjust) and thus your camera panning is violently quick. Xbox and PS controllers are counterintuitive and hard to keep track of where what is.
3. The tutorial popup windows are constantly in the way, and you can try and toggle those off, good luck
with that.
4. The game offers you the option to play in “maximised window” mode but... Your game is in fact your boss and knows your system better than you and has, behind your back already changed the view mode back to “fullscreen”.
5. Physics are better than Giants’ Farming Simulator but still clunky. Way too many times I had to “reset” the crane or vehicle because it got mysteriously stuck on a pole or under the gravel, and my favourite on the side of the dump trucks’ load body.
6. Traffic in this game is a joke, I am not going to even try and explain how rubbish this is (Stll better than Farming Simulator 22 though.
7. Why can’t you change your vehicle colour? I am willing to pay a 130 000 Cr, but I can ONLY get in black. Does sound like the first Mercedes Benz which you could have in any colour as long as its black. Hey maybe it’s a German thing. (You SHALL get it in black and you WILL like it.)
8. SInce when is a Scania, Mack or DAF exactly the same price?
9 Milestones are silly, for instance - go and fill up 25 times at a filling station, or repair 25 times at a filling station.
10 . Skill points hardly makes a difference
11. The camera in this game is designed by Lucifer himself. It was designed in such a way as to drive you insane from the first minutes of the game and it DOES. You will say words you last used when you were watching football (soccer for you US people) and the ref was making controversial calls against your team.
12. I can understand in the UK map, but for Gawd sake is there ANYONE in the entire United States of America that is called Hape? Call him Hank or something.

GAMEPLAY:
Boring, monotonous. The fast travel starts out as your friend because you don’t have to struggle through idiotic AI traffic, but ends up making the game about a continuous stream of loading screens as you fast travel everywhere.

Right now I am standing at the bottom of a wind turbine, which I discovered by driving on a dirt path not indicated on the map, I can hear the blades turning and I am taking a screenshot of the city and a bridge leading to it. I almost want to start it over again it was so immersive. This game IS LIKE REAL LIFE....I buy my materials at the store and accidentally unclick FREE loading.....so then I have to load the 16 items I just bought ONE BY ONE.....OH AND YOU CAN LOAD THEM INTO YOUR FLATBED WITHOUT THE GAME AUTO COMPLETING FOR YOU EVEN WHEN YOU HAVE AUTO COMPLETE ON.....I then drive like a maniac because I m late for my job now, skid out of control RUN OVER A PEDESTRIAN , DO SEVERAL ROLL OVERS AND FINALLY HEAR MYSELF SCREAM BECAUSE I JUST DIED (soo glad I was in first person view mode because I felt like I was there in real life...taught me a lesson)........and then I respawn where I died a second later....and the best part is I only picked up 2 of the 16 items I bought so I will probably repeat this process another 8 times.......Luckily I TURNED OFF TRAFFIC VIOLATIONS IN THE GAME... It is a perfect blend of realistic feel, look, sound, activities of construction jobs and silly fun sandbox gameplay. If you are not sure buy it on sale if you can. First complaint from those who don't recommend it is that it i not realistic enough........in my opinion no simulator game does that perfectly. However, for those who do not operate this kind of machinery in real life it DOES FEEL REALISTIC in the sense that you have to learn how to operate different machinery. The sounds of the machinery and environment are quite realistic. ACTUALLY YOU CAN HEAR THE MACHINERY, BIRDS, CRICKETS, AND PEOPLE IN THE ENVIRONMENT TALKING. YES THAT RIGHT AND YOU CAN ACTUALLY WALK AROUND THE ENVIRONMENT ONCE YOU DISCOVER IT. I had a tough time with clearing a pit of dirt with an excavator (even though I enjoyed thoroughly) so when I completed it I WALKED AROUND THE CITY AND SAW PEOPLE SITTING ON BENCHES AND WALKING AROUND AND EVEN INTO A FOREST SO I COULD RELAX AND LISTEN TO THE DIFFERENT BIRDS IN THE GAME. I FELT SO MUCH BETTER WHEN I RETURNED TO MY TRUCK FOR THE NEXT JOB. You can also hear different styles of music (continuous short loop) from passing vehicles when you walk around the city although muffled because other drivers have their windows closed in the game. Some people complain that it auto completes the last few movements when you are loading or unloading objects. Yes it does but you can still try to put it closer for your own satisfaction. TIP: if you have trouble loading and unloading rotate your view with the mouse so you are right behind the loading arm and it becomes much easier and you may have to fully extend the arm fully raise it and the lower the base arm to get full extension. Also, when using the excavator line up properly with the dig site (straight on and not on an angle) When using the Wheel Loader, you have to look at if from outside and the front so you can tell when your bucket is full otherwise you will spend an hour accomplishing nothing (which is exactly what I did and ENJOYED EVERY MINUTE OF IT) before realising how the game works. I would say it is optimised for keyboard an mouse but I use both the keyboard and a controller depending on which feels best for the task. Yes driving the flatbed delivery truck is super arcadey that is until you actually load it with materials and then it feels more realistic and this goes for the other wheeled vehicles as well. Actually I loved driving around a lot as it makes it feel more realistic because you don,t teleport to the job site in real life. Actually you can teleport in the game with quick travel feature if you don't enjoy the driving. The heavier wheeled machinery DOES FEEL MORE REALISTIC when driving on the road. I thoroughly enjoyed driving the cement mixing truck. Overall I would recommend for anyone who has always dreamed of operating this kind of equipment and the feeling of working through the night into the morning to get a job done. PS. I am only playing the e campaign mode and this is why I bought the game so I cannot rate the multiplayer part. As for the graphics, they're quite good, the sky is quite beautiful at sunrise and the vehicles are quite highly detailed. The controls do take some time to get used to. I did not buy the manual but I am pretty sure there is one in the game anyways and there are some instructions in the mini tutorial for single player mode and I have not really had too much trouble figuring out the controls yet. Are the controls perfect no, is the menu perfect no, are some jobs super grindy and difficult yes........but for 5% of the game which is imperfect the other 95% is AWESOME!!!!!!!\t\t\t\t\t
