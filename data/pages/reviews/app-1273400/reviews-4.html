\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's get the bad out of the way first: The menus are unintuitive and the Steam achievements are mostly broken. In order to get to the screen showing vehicles stored at your company HQ, you have to first open up the screen showing your active jobs, for example, and the screen showing your personal milestone achievements may say 8/32, but they don't tell you anything more for each category. Some can be puzzled, out, but many cannot. The Steam achievements also get awarded either multiple times a session or when you've completed part of them, but not the whole thing.

But that's easy enough to get past -- just learn the menus and ignore the Steam achievements. I've only had one bad bug, where a machine wouldn't do what it was supposed to, and I just skipped that step of the construction task so I could continue with it. Everything else has been fine. This is a fun and relaxing game. When I cause a problem for myself, I can either figure out how to use vehicles to fix it or I can hit some quick vehicle resets and let the game take care of things for me.

Solid 8/10 overall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually avoid reviewing when there are already thousands of reviews, but I figured I'd leave this here just to pay the devs a compliment and maybe provide some feedback.

This is a very well-thought out and detailed game. Every job you finish changes the game world, down to paving asphalt. I think it even stops playing the "rattling" sound effect when you go over that section of road in something like a dump truck. I cannot imagine how much work it must have taken to sort out all the 3D models for the different stages of construction, alone...

It's also cool that you have different options. You can follow campaigns, do a bunch of small jobs (and get an achievement for it) or just wander around treasure-hunting.

It even makes many allowances for people like me, who are "tedium-intolerant." Namely: skipping tasks, fast-traveling and the construction helper. These features are soooo nice to have.

Another thing that would be useful is delivering construction vehicles directly to job sites (from either the vehicle dealer or your construction company.) It would drastically shorten the whole process of taking the vehicle out of storage, entering the vehicle, opening the map and then fast traveling. This could be a single button in the motorpool/dealer labeled "deliver to job" or something.

At any rate, I think this game might soon join the exclusive "centenarian club"--games on my account with more than 100 hours, which currently consists of only 11 games, plus Blender.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnless you get this on sale, its overpriced for its quality. I am enjoying the game, but I have alot of gripes about it.
  • The GPS is terrible, infuriatingly bad, It will route you *literally* in circles, give me a TomTom from the 2000's instead.
  • The stoplights have zero modern logic to them, they will give green to empty intersections, and even crosswalk with nobody there. The mapper needs to seriously fix the nonsense traffic rules. You can speed, drive over medians, run stop signs, but the millisecond you pull past a red, cops swarm you.
  • The level of detail in the jobs is subpar, even on high detail. You simply hover something in a vague area around where it should go and snap it into place, there is no bonus for lining things up properly. Then between stages things magic into nicer stages, without any sort of "you did this" feel to it. I wish someone would learn from landlords super...
  • The keyboard driving mechanics are too jerky, akin to a old GTA game, when euro truck simulator has set the bar for years now, simply put momentum on the wheel return. You may ask, why does this matter with a construction game, well because half of the time you're driving materials to the job site, that's why. EDIT : Turns out you don't have to drive, you can buy/sell materials directly into machines, if the driving was good I would be more mad, but why even have locations for materials if you can just skip it all with no penalty (that I can notice).
  • On a technical level, this uses denuvo (puke) AND appears to not allow local hosted games? I got disconnected from my OWN session?! WHAT?! So basically buying this game means you are totally at the wim of the devs/publisher, the day they decide to shut down, unless they allow people to create their own binaries, you're screwed.
  • There is some sort of weird graphic tech that smears textures while in motion, and creates white flashbangs randomly. Its hard to explain, but its quite annoying.
