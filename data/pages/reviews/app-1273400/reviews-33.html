\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like playing these industrial themed games, but this one is piggybacking on the genre. Its more like 'Order Fulfillment Sim', because you don't get to do any real construction. There's no immersion feeling that you are building anything or satisfaction in completing the tasks because you aim the hook or drive the machine forward, then poof, the project is complete.

The progression is boring. You start with a some nice versatile machines, but by the second task, you are forced to rent anything you need. I was really dumbfounded when I had to pave and couldn't use a dump truck to haul asphalt and bought a compactor that could only be used for gravel and not asphalt.

The final straw for me was the loaders cant level; in reality a loader can be used to flatten and remove a little going forward; and then smooth and fill slightly when you go backward with the loader blade pitch down.

These are the kind of problems that I doubt will be considered, and turned me off to the game. The Sims has better construction elements than this... game.

I sent this message to helpdesk@astragon.de and got no response:

#Discussions_QuoteBlock_Author
"The (USA) first level design is impressive. I was excited when I got in the big crane - with the letters on the roof and the truck right below to load up. I haven't progressed far enough in ConSim 2015 to use this tool, so its very sweet. Looking at the roof, the flying view is good for plucking stuff up but I wanted to zoom in more for a closer view of the little hook. Then I switched to cockpit view and there's a big safety bar obstructing the roof-line. Frustration started to set in halfway through and it took me 3 sittings to finish loading.

The flatbed truck crane task is simpler, but then feels dull after playing with the big guns. I tried the tool camera and what happened?; I couldn't see the roof looking forward, or stretching backwards. I wanted soo badly to access the control panel mounted to the base of the crane. From there, I could see the roof perfectly and confidently do the task, if only it wasn't just for looks.

This game's scope is immense, just like the tools it presents. It's a shame that all of the content is mission-locked. A fair compromise would be a sandbox mode with some freedom to test out different things. What's a dump truck and bulldozer without a sandbox? "
