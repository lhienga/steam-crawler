\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a love hate relationship with this game. As an equipment operator I was disappointed. Controls with a controller are iffy. I am considering buying a dual joystick setup to see if it is better. The camera view is glitchy, especially when running a crane or boom truck. You want the camera to follow the boom and always face the load or apparatus. The camera changes direction on each swing and makes controls difficult as your perspective is constantly changing. There are often jobs when you are asked to backfill and there are piles of spoils around the excavation. However if you attempt to push the piles into the excavation with a dozer, which is the most efficient machine for the job, you gain no progress from doing so. You have to use a loader, excavator or pay to import fill. On top of that, dozer controls are unrealistic. You do not use two separate controls to operate the tracks of a dozer like you would an excavator. One joystick controls the the tracks and another controls the blade. The drill rig is super glitchy. I often have trouble getting the auger or the shaft to continue downward and have to remove the rig from the site and bring it back to fix the issue. The fact that the game advertises a helper in the first campaign but the feature does not show up at all in the game is infuriating. If you are pumping concrete, you have to constantly switch back to the mixer to refill. If you are hauling spoils, you have to constantly switch to the truck to empty. The game has been out for what, two years, and that feature is still not active? This game could be great but the developers seem to have no experience in real world construction. They need input from real world operators to develop this game into what it should be.\t\t\t\t\t

Interesting machines and jobs - sometimes hard to understand what is wanted in the jobs or where you get the materials from. The worst thing is the controls. I have never ever seen such bad controls in a game. They ruin the whole experience. They are so frustrating. My daughter, who has over 700 games on Steam plus numerous others on console, says they are the worst controls she has ever encountered in a game. We play together on multi-player and intend to continue doing so in the vain hope the controls might at some point make sense and we might get used to them but after 35 hours it hasn't happened yet and it's nowhere near to be honest, and it's not for want of trying. I was going to buy the season passes as they looked good but I think I'll hold off for now as I'm finding the game frustrating rather than enjoyable at the moment. It's clear the developers have no intention of changing the controls as the game has been out a while and people are always complaining about them. One of the developers said there were settings to change the camera and controls but I've searched the settings and can't find them. I'll persevere and see how things go but the season passes will definitely have to wait. I'm not impressed that when I do a £743,000 job I only end up with £400.000 because a huge amount of the profit goes on materials and travel which would surely usually be charged to the customer.\t\t\t\t\t

Really kinda meh . . .

It a busy work game that kinda does away with the busywork. You can fast travel all the time. so there is literally no point in even using vehicle transport trailers or driving anywhere, because you can just fast travel too and from everywhere.

Fast Travel to Buy/Rent a vehicle, Fast Travel to the fill/loading point, Fast Travel to the job site.

There is almost no point in the game having a map for players to drive around because you won't.
You'll fast travel.

The game also isn't really a construction simulator, its a machine operator simulator.
When it comes down to construction things, like laying bricks or framing a roof, you don't, you press a button prompt and the game does it, with really bad animations btw, and then poof the wall is built.

The rest is delivering gravel (driving a truck) compacting (driving a roller), concreting a slab (driving a mixer or pump), placing prefab walls (driving a crane) etc etc.. you don't even place re-bar, you again press the button prompt and it magics into place.

I appreciate that things have to be streamlined to make the game less complex and tedious and I get that but its streamlined so much that most of the actual construction has been removed.

Sure tying 150 re-bar ties would be dull but you could have the player actually get out of the vehicle and DO something for once. You don't even operate the vehicle cranes from outside the vehicle from the control panel which is even modelled!

The game-play loop is also the same as every other loop. you make money for the sake of making money in order to make more money, Just like farming Sim and Mechanic Sim and pretty much every other busywork sim the economics system is un-engaging and dull.

The camera is horrific and the controls are also unintuitive, the graphics are average, the sounds are average. Farming Sim did better 5 years ago.

I'm glad I got it in a sale tbh and didn't pay full wack.

The Bad:
It didn't make me feel like I am constructing something cause everything is on a guide train. Every thing is highlighted where you should put it. Almost everything you do would be on a machine or in a crane, so if you like crane games try this. There's no big difference between the brands of vehicles. There are still wonky controls and bugged EULA that will keep popping up on certain controls. Controller support is fine.

The game is playable, Gameplay loop is satisfying. (for the price) Hoping this is not the last game, Promising series.\t\t\t\t\t
