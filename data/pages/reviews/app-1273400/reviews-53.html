Even doing the same tasks can feel different on each project. Maps are surprisingly larger than I anticipated. They've got a good handle on fun vs realism. On the bad side, these are more QoL issues I'm having. Not being able to have my keybinds and settings across all saves. Reading the help left me completely lost. Had to look up a youtube video on how it works.

Overall a 4/5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played earlier versions of Construction Simulator, but here are a few impressions of the game so far.

+ Overall a very fun and entertaining game.
+ Playing this with friends is a serious fun multiplier.
+ Interesting feature with traffic violations.
+ Somewhat varied contracts makes for a solid source of income and fun.
+ Campaign missions are interesting and challenging.
+ You can drift with lorries and do donuts!

- Bugs, wonky physics and poor optimization are all over the place.
- Several keybindings are hard coded.
- Much of the actual building is done for you. You just attach a few walls, pour some concrete and you're done with it.
- Relatively small maps...
- Many vehicle variations but no stats or information on them to know which is better.
- To play with friends, you first have to play the initial campaign solo.......
- The voice acting is beyond terrible...

Overall a fun and enjoyable game, but I'd call the current state of the game a beta given all the issues in it. Make sure you bring a friend, it'll be so much more fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the original Construction Simulator from 2015, and had high hopes for this one. I was genuinely excited. But this sequel doesn't seem to be as good as original. And here's why.

- Game descriptions says there is a lot more vehicles and equipment, which is true, BUT, vehicle quality, especially truck quality is very poor by today's standards. It almost feels like these vehicles were made for a mobile game! Textures are low resolution, even on max. graphics settings. Truck's interiors aren't that functional, only RPM and Speed are animated. It was OK in 2015, but not in 2022, in my opinion.
- Speaking of graphics, i'm not a fan of how the game looks either. It feels like a bunch of "modern effects" were slapped on original game without testing and fine tuning. There are tons of various graphic artifacts, mostly related to lighting. Trees are so damn bad, you can see them change LODs clearly. Interior mirrors eat so much FPS, that having them on makes the gameplay "slideshow"
- AI is bad, traffic vehicles behave weird, they barely obey any traffic laws.
- Player's vehicle behaviour is also not great. There is a delay after you accelerate, then when vehicle starts moving it feels ok, trucks feel heavy, but then they change gear... and just fly like a race car or something, speeding up from 20 to 50 km/h in less than one second.
- GPS is really strange, it leads you with longer routes.

As for gameplay, everything feels familiar, and thats good. You know what to do from the start.

Overall, I'd say 2015 edition is a better game, despite less vehicle and maps. But i hope CS 2022 will get better with continous updates.\t\t\t\t\t
