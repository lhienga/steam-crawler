Construction Simulator 2022 adds those physics perfectly, but WHY have they decided to remove almost ALL of the great features from 2015?

Let's talk about the mobile cranes for a minute: I like to be in the cab for a better experience, but they've put the windshield wiper directly in your sight! It's right there in the middle! So if I have a load far away, I can not even see my hookblock. The tilting cab feature is no more, and when I try to boom up slightly the boom goes down! And where is that beautiful crawler crane that was a job on it's own?? And I'm not even talking about the adventurous transport jobs to and from the dam. The only beautiful jobs to do imo are the bridge and the windmill, and for the windmill that crawler crane would be great!

Really, this game is great in physics and texture but it lacks sooo much other things, that 2015 had.
This is like GTA San Andreas and GTA 4, where GTA 4 was better looking and better physics, but it lost all the options of San Andreas (barber, pimp shop, etc)\t\t\t\t\t

Original 3/8 review;
This is one of those "Wow I already spent 3 hours in this game and didn't realize it" type of games. I have a slew of games I picked up over the past few months and trying to get around to booting them all up for a bit to cull the good from the bad. This one certainly is firmly in the good category so far in my book.

Like most boys, and I am sure plenty of girls growing up I loved playing with toy construction vehicles. There is just something awe inspiring about them. Yet we tend to forget the massive different types there are out there. I grew up on a farm so I had my share of tractors, and while it was after I had long moved out my father got a Cat Backhoe and while he was stingy on letting other's drive it ( I completely understand why ) it was always a blast when I did get to.

For a short time back in my first decade of professional IT work, I went through the burn-out face, and for a short bit I thought about getting some training towards getting certs on big earth movers. Yes it was a lot more dangerous work than what I was doing, but even by that point in my career technically I had already really been doing computer stuff for many, many years before I hit 18. These were the days before the commercial internet so I taught myself the hard way from a very young age. This day and age, no one would think twice about it but back in the early 80s if you were a pre-teen and teenager who knew more than the people at the local IT store, people tended to notice. Anyway I digress just to say that in another life, I wonder if I am writing about some game with the same glee I am about this about a career move that I almost made except the opposite way.

So except when my Dad allows me to drive one of his tractors or backhoe, I just have to use t his game for imagination of one of the possible lives I may be living in an alternate universe. Well along side an Naval Officer, a Microbiologist and a zillion other careers I have thought about and never applied myself to.

Spec wise, I had no issues. I have the game installed on my Steam Deck but haven't tired it on the Deck yet. Instead I sat down and tried it on my aging i5-7400 @3Ghz, 16GB Ram, SSD, with 1060 GTX. I ran into zero problems other than those created by myself by failing to fully read the information clearly given to me. The game runs great here and I am sure it will run great on the Steam Deck. I honestly have yet to find a game that was green checked for the Deck that didn't run just as good on my Deck as my aging gaming rig.

I highly recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is very mid ground, the selected machines look stellar and have the details, i've never been in some so i cant say if interiors are on point but most appear to be spot on besides lack of information clusters/monitors, they are blank/black screens. There are no hands on ie welding, fastening, dry walling, hammering, etc, its all about the machines, the rest is simulated. there are no accessory options but since patch 5 they did add more paint options to trucks, no stickers, dashboard burgers,---> since patch 5 you can do 180s in cockpit view now; a much needed feature and well enjoyed, you cannot lean or duck inside machines to get a better viewpoint though. Driving over 8mph,realistic physics seem to disappear, luckily if you dont want to drive you can fast travel to every location. The dirt reminds me very much of roller coaster tycoon (3). There is a limit of equipment you can have out at one time so if you play solo I would be cautious about taking on 2 mega contracts at same time (when ability is available) AVOID HIGH SCOPE UNDERGROUND PARKING GARAGE in early stages, ill explain more later. If you are a real construction worker and want real deal, I wouldnt recommend it less you wanted to tote around machines you dont get to usually touch, you will never pick a hammer up in this game.It definitely fills a hole for me and is enjoyable and neat, highly recommend if these things interest you. The game could be much better with mods in my opinion but I'm still enjoying the projects and watching the city change.Do the 2 hour tutorial, its a tight window to decide but if constant fast travel isnt your style you will spend hours doing nothing but A to B and back to A then to B. If you arent about it or the machines or tasks, get a refund.End review.For my hard hat homies who do enjoy the game, do not take on campaign's underground parking garage mission on high without multiplayer or until you have a rack of equipment/money and heck i'd say the other foreman to have 2 jobs at once so you can switch between dirt and something else (concrete i bet), I'm quite literally still moving dirt and its been a solid 8 hours minimum of game play, if it wasnt for devils lettuce, id probably would have hit skip but im at 30% and 8 dumpers to fill at once kind of cuts it down quicker.\t\t\t\t\t

The game's graphics and sounds really help with this realism, along with the detailed physics depicted with earth moving, swinging cranes, concrete pouring, and how your loads settle and shift when you haul gravel or sand.
They've still made it possible for a single player to proceed in building up both his company and contracts in a simplified QoL options like teleporting to previously discovered locations including work sites. Also you can quickly load more concrete, asphalt, etc into you machines with just a click of a button, and the cash is deducted for doing so.
I can only imagine how amazing the game would play with friends and other players. Having a fleet of truck drivers to haul in material for continued operations like paving and concrete work, or driving along side you when ripping up the old road to have it shipped out, instead of dumping to the ground to have it just vanish.
