\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed games like Farm Simulator in the past and was always curious about construction, so I decided to give it a try. Unlike a lot of other games on steam, this one is chalk full of tutorials and constant guidance at almost all times, so you rarely wonder what you need to do to proceed (other than some bugs discussed below). A lot of the tutorials and guidance are also voice acted, which is another nice layer of polish.

You start your own company in a city which has many open plots of land, and get contracts to build anywhere from small houses to huge buildings. It's very satisfying seeing it all come together after you've built several things - "Hey, I built that!". There are four different campaign lines, each of which result in building unique, awesome structures.

Each construction machine has a different purpose, from bulldozing and removing topsoil, to pumping concrete 5 stories high, to drilling holes 20 feet deep. They are all fun to learn, operate, and collect.

There are a few issues that need fixed, but they don't hold you back. For one, the drilling seems completely broken (I can only get the holes drilled all the way about 10% of the time before hitting an invisible barrier, despite following the steps in help). There are also some story missions where you need to go pick up specific items but it doesn't tell you where to find them and they're not marked on the map. Or occasionally you'll buy something from a store but not be able to find it in the parking lot to load into your truck, and the store won't let you buy it again because it's story-related. Fortunately for these situations, the game provides an option to skip that specific step of construction on any building so that you can be ignore the issue and move on. That was a genius move by the developers to avoid people getting blocked from progressing while they iron out the issues!

This game is definitely worth playing! Overall I'd give this an 8.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYeah this is my favourite game right now... This is leaps and bounds above any other simulator game besides Euro/American Truck Sim (hard to beat the top dog).

My favourite part is the earthworks so my pet peeve is when the actual work isn't done on the ground but the percentage gets to 100% early and completes the task and removes the Hold Y Guide, for light digging you can probably keep going till it looks how you wanted but when it's ticked off early on top soil work or more complex earth work it takes away the guide so you can't tell where you were up to, and it feels like a bit of a rip off. It would be good to toggle difficulties to have it how it is for easy mode, and as it gets harder you need the earthwork to more closer resemble the final product.

I hope the quarry work with the benches and rock crusher, offroad dumpies driving off in the distance like the post-release roadmap title artwork comes to fruition, it's less construction and more mining related but the machine appearance and controls is absolutely unseen in any other game it'll be so cool to play with it here in a mining setting where all the dirt around you can be moved instead of small patches of land. Perhaps could be worked into Lore that you work a bit of overtime at the quarry to earn some dough to set up the business (although earning money is not a problem in this game, it would be more of a challenge to tank your company). Maybe this quarry thing is already in and it's a feature I've not discovered yet, or will happen with workshop release and custom jobs, maps, mods etc but something to keep in mind. I am worried about running out of jobs eventually especially ones that involve the favourites, dozer, digger and loader.

Things I like:
The convenience of fast travel to skip mundane driving tasks, or to skip tasks altogether such as the more boring activities like crane lifts or fetch materials. I have not once been frustrated or inconvenienced by this game, it is too easy to relax and spend hours on it because it's just so easy going.
The game is beautiful, the machines and great

Constructive criticisms:
- I know in the roadmap it says for reverse head turning, but the CAT Dozer (maybe others) for sure needs the little rear vision mirror that sits on top of the middle windscreen. This is a real accessory, very much so required, impossible to see while you're reversing in first person. Either this or a reverse camera which is usually an aftermarket accessory, usually see them on the right pillar in the cab behind the door, above the ripper joystick.
- No reverse squarkers or beepers on any of the equipment? Only trucks so far.
- Taken my D8 and CAT Digger on about 3-4 jobs now and they don't seem to get dirty? There is what looks to be an in-game image as a loading screen of the D8 dirty, so I assumed it was possible but doesn't seem to be? Would be good for the machines to get dirty in one to two jobs, then maybe keeping them dirty till you get back to the yard and perhaps could have a Gerni there to wash them up. It's a little weird working a D8 for several hours, hopping out and it's sparkling clean and not even a speck of dirt on the tracks and track frame.
- A Forklift would be great for unloading things at the yard or on the work site, would save frustratingly slow crane usage on the work site. (think there's one but can only use it to load up at the shops if I'm correct? It's not in the vehicle dealer, would be nice to have it in there.)
- Definitely would have loved a Fuel truck, although that's already on the roadmap for next year.
- Camera toggle option is clunky at first without some tweaking, some of the default options in general need a re-look.
- It can be hard to use the cranes, a camera view which stays/follows the top of the rope/where the spool is OR stays on the hook might help.
- Using the concrete pumps is terrible but that might have more to do with my skill than anything, but I suspect where I slew the arm side to side it also drops the boom arms? Might be a bug or just me? I just notice as I move from side to side I need to compensate by moving the orange boom up or the grey extension arms up otherwise it hits the ground.


I'd like to see:
- Quarry/mining for fun playing with the machines, or an open area for digging, dozering, loadering, drilling at your will. There are some mods like this for Farming Sim that game is so clunky, the potential here with the graphics, actual licensed models and how well they work is awesome.
- Maybe if we run out of room to build.... Destruction content/dlc/mods? Big wrecking balls, explosives - taking down old buildings and clearing the site before building like we do already.
- Something probably less thought about ... What about railroad construction? Clearing a path, laying the rocks, laying the metal and welding them together, might be a pretty cool dlc/mod.
- Some customisatibility with the machines, say putting the company logo on the side, or a custom logo (perhaps with the ability to uplaod one?), selecting "wear marks" in the paint department, swapping to LED flashers etc.
-In-game radio with ability to upload internet channels like American Truck Sim.\t\t\t\t\t
