\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far so good. Good graphics, it runs pretty smoothly on my rig. The in-cab details are really decent, looks like there is a variety of jobs and machines to mess around with.
The controls are hit and miss. I operate excavators, dozers and wheel loaders for a living and the controls for some are great (excavator, wheel loader, trucks, cranes) but the controls for the dozer has the blade lift back to front for actual joystick replication. For example, pulling back on a joystick on basically any machine lifts the arm/boom/jib, which on a gamepad would technically be "down" on the joystick (moving the stick towards the player). The controls on the game use the joystick "down" to drop the blade down, which feels completely backwards, and even the animation in the cabin has the joystick moving backwards when the blade is dropping down, which is totally back to front. The dozer tracks also drive me crazy, as the game has you control each track individually like an excavator, and they just do not handle that way in real life. It would be more realistic just to have the drive and steering controls set up like a wheel loader or truck, as it would be pretty hard to replicate a decelerator on a gamepad (although if the developers could do that that would be amazing). And unfortunately the blade suffers from the inverted controls the wheel loader has as well. Not sure if key binding is going to be added later but I could not seem to change it.
People who do this for a living usually rely on muscle memory to control an actual machine, so to constantly have the game react different to how the machine would react in real life is irritating, but probably wouldn´t be an issue for casual, non-operator players. But this is also a testament to how well the developers have replicated the look of the machines and made things very immersive for players.
The other gripe I have is with the camera. In-cab view looks great, but when driving wheeled trucks and machines it would be good to have an option to have the camera follow the direction you are steering, as the free-look setting is clunky and not so easy to use (there are only so many buttons on a gamepad, maybe holding a bumper or just having the camera look where you´re steering would be good) and the external camera is quite disorientating as it shifts to look behind you automatically when you reverse but its not super quick, so it throws off your steering inputs a bit.
That sounds like a lot of negatives but in reality the game is a really good step up from previous versions and a good step in the right direction. Casual construction fans will find a lot to enjoy here, the graphics and textures are a big improvement over other similar games. Hopefully we can expect some modding options and add-ons from the developers over time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome game! It is obvious the devs put a ton of effort into it and this has become my favorite sim game to play this year. Graphics are stunning, missions are well designed and there is just enough realism to keep you occupied without the feeling of doing "busy work" as often these types of games are. All work is productive and the ability to play free world or within a campaign is a great duo.

After playing the game for 20 hours, I have noticed a few items to look forward to.
- The ability to skip night. I know this has been mentioned for the next hotfix. Night gameplay is a bit dark on the construction site. The missions do have light fixtures but they are few and far between. Given the ability to rent and place these light fixtures would be a cool feature which could make night gameplay more enjoyable for the player and viewers on stream.

- There is a lack of trailer options. As the flatbed crane truck is a great go to, the ability to attach a smaller ball/hitch trailer to the back of this truck to carry smaller equipment in one trip would be a nice add on.

- The absolute fan feature for me is Multiplayer mode! However it is limited to 4 players. Hopefully with time, this can be increased as more servers come on line? The multiplayer feature is a great chance to play with friends online however it is still set up as you are playing multiplayer on a single player game. Even the largest jobs really only have enough work for possibly 2 people at a time to stay active. The ability to go in a "pick up" a task which is next on the list would be great to keep all players working. But as it is now, there is a lot of standing around for the other players as there is only one crane. If you work ahead and grab materials early, this can sometimes bug out the game when the previous task is completed. We have had materials on site vanish etc as we worked a step ahead. Overall, much much larger jobs to keep a team of 4+ busy would be a great feature.\t\t\t\t\t
