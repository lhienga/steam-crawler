\r\n\t\t\t\t\tConstruction Simulator is good at what it tries to focus on, but has some bugs that need to be ironed out.

First off, be aware there is grinding. You can skip everything you don't want to do, but that is simply the heart of the game: Digging dirt, pouring concrete, that can be tedious and at times repetitive, but also rewards you with a sense of accomplishment.

What the game does well:
- The feel of most machines, the use of different equipment, and how it conveys the need to use the right tool for the right job.
- Skipping driving around! This is a very, very good decision! The road driving experience is not the best - and I forgive this - it's not GTA and it doesn't try to be. My hot tip: Skip every mile/km and concentrate on construction.
- The stories for construction for the campaign are a nice touch.

What could be improved:
- the game will start to break after a while of play and you will have to restart. For example, the construction view will not work or fast travel will be broken.
- After construction, the places may break: The marina in the US harbor campaign will fill up with dirt after the campaign is done. It seems the save stages are not correctly reflecting terrain alterations.
- Wouldn't it be great if you could walk through the buildings you constructed - like the container hotel or planetarium? But no, they turn into shells. This really needs to be changed - it would enhance the sense of accomplishments. Maybe not the private houses, but the public ones.

A suggestion:
Let there be a "private campaign" where you build your own house and your own construction yard. Delete the upgraded yard and have that instead, please! You have a construction company and you cannot even build your own stuff. What a missed opportunity! Why do you just buy an upgrade instead of unlocking to build it instead? I'm talking about the repair shop, gas station, heck even depot and parking lot! Also, have a private residence where you can place memorabilia from the campaigns - like architect models.\t\t\t\t\t

The Bad:

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Auto-complete simulator"


I've uninstalled the game. Eight hours in I found it to be incredibly boring..
Teleporting and auto-completion kinda ruins it for me.

It's like helping your dad out in the garden when you're five years old, but it's really just your dad babysitting you, cause the work you do is of no value to him or mom. No matter how much skill or time you put into it, the game will auto-complete EVERY STEP of a job, if not more.. Like yesterday, "build earth ramp" which means removing dirt until the game auto-completes it (looking waaaay different) and then wants me to dig more so that it can auto-complete the other half of the ramp an hour later. The game could just auto-complete everything and you wouldn't need to do anything..

There's no freedom, there's no creativity. Every build and every auto-completed hole will look exactly the same on every server for all players. Even placing cargo, every tree you place, everything is predetermined.

I thought the digging was cool, but nothing of what I do will remain on the map for very long.

EDIT:
Ok, reinstalled it and played for perhaps 15 minutes before uninstalling it again.
If you're a solo player, I'd recommend taking a look at https://store.steampowered.com/app/451340/Gold_Rush_The_Game/ if you haven't already. Don't expect frequent updates or multiplayer (I haven't played a game where I wanted multiplayer more, but it won't happen). While there's some snapping for major equipment like trommels, shakers and duplex jigs, and mandatory delivery for bigger vehicles like excavators and the dumper, everything else needs to be physically picked up at the store and transported to the claims/sites. You'll need to connect hoses of different size and decide their path, it's the same with electric cables. Equipment will consist of parts (Springs, cables, wheels, batteries, cogs, tubes, buttons etc etc) that can break down and need switching out. It's not a perfect game, could be better optimized, but there's a lot of content for a fairly low price, it's given me hundreds of hours (300+) of entertainment. Up until Construction Simulator, GH has been (correct me if I'm wrong) the best excavator "simulator" out there imo.

So CS might have better ground physics and more excavators to choose from, but the auto-completion of jobs takes away every sense of accomplishment. It basically deletes everything you've done and puts in the creation of the developers, wether it's a hole in the ground, a flat surface, a tree, a wall, cargo pallet placement, etc, etc etc. In GH you have to plan the whole worksite and I think it's really rewarding seeing the claim evolve as you get more equipment on site and the whole place forming into a quarry as you dig deeper and bigger, making it more into your creation instead of the developer's.

2nd edit a year later: It's not fun. Do a good job or a bad job, it ends up looking the same. No options to adjust teleporting or other things in multiplayer, no in game text chat, no other way to communicate in game. Some of the tasks are fun enough, but when the result ends up as the developers creation on every step of a job, there's no creativity left for players to enjoy.

Dig a hole. Move equipment away, and the hole magically transform into the one the developer made.
This game would be awesome if it was skill based, having a road look like if it's done poorly for example. Now, every road, every project, every job, every step of a job, ends up looking picture perfect. There's no way to see if this is done by a 3 y.o or a professional. Really disappointing, and I can only hope the devs will give more room for player creativity in their next game. Lot's of good stuff here, but the total package is really bad. 2/10\t\t\t\t\t
