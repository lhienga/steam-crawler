\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall the game is decent and somewhat playable, but it has some major issues that need to be addressed and makes you wonder why it was released in its current state when it's clearly not ready. It's 90% done and the remaining 10% are all things that need to be (or should have been) fixed before release.

The game is more or less the exact same as the 2015 game, but with a lot more brands of machines and asphalt work added on top of everything that was in the old game. It looks a little better (and runs much worse), the map's much bigger, physics are both better and much worse, and for the most part it plays the exact same. This is why I can't recommend the 2022 version: It's 99% the same game, with much worse performance - bordering on unplayable - and critical issues that shouldn't exist in a supposedly finished product. Until the below issues are fixed you're much better off buying the old game.

Critical issues (that MUST be fixed):
- All the wheel loaders and the front loader on the backhoe lack parallelogram operation, which means they don't work like they should and makes it extremely difficult to use them. You also can't curl the bucket while in material, so you can't fill it completely, and you can't rotate it below 0 degrees, which makes it near impossible to pick up material on uneven ground - and also difficult to empty it into trucks. This is inexcusable for a game that claims to be a "simulator".

- Vehicle physics are terrible - trucks will slide sideways at 30 km/h, while going straight, and god help you if you go over a small bump at any kind of speed. Vehicles also don't turn sharp enough, which makes it especially difficult to get the semis around corners. Combine this misbehaviour with the sluggy unresponsive controls and it becomes inexcusably bad. Even more so when we have a game mechanic that make you spill material if you don't drive unrealistic straight or slow at all times.

- Sluggish response to control input - and sluggish is mildly put. Pressing any control related to moving the vehicle (forward/backward or stop) comes with a huge delay before anything happens. This makes it impossible to do accurate stops (so backing dumptrucks into holes becomes normal rather than an occasional oopsie) and equally impossible to use the tank controls for the tracked vehicles to go in any direction resembling a straight line. It is further aggravated because the stop & go (aka stop & reverse) controls are missing the 'stop' part before changing direction - and you can't even turn that off even though most the vehicles in the game can't be equipped with stop & go IRL.

- Performance is atrocious, but typical for Unity games:

Because it runs on Unity it has all the usual symptoms of this dumpsterfire of an engine:
- Framerate is very poor and incredibly unstable; it struggles to reach 60 fps even when the GPU isn't fully loaded.
- Changing graphic settings makes little to no difference to the performance. As is also completely normal for Unity.

Additionally the menu system is terrible and super illogical laid out, and the most important part - managing your company and your character - is buried under the in-game help (which is very poor and becomes irrelevant after playing for 20 minutes). This means you have 1 button access to something you'll never need, but require 2-3 button presses to get to the stuff that matters. This is so bad that it's difficult to remember how to get to the company oand character menus since the keys to open the menus already make no sense.

Other minor issues:
- there's no low-loader trailer, which means you can't haul stuff like the asphalt machines and are forced to use the fast travel (which costs money).
- mobile crane models are recycled from the old game, and still incorrect, but since it's been 8 years and the devs didn't bother to fix them, it's safe to assume it will never be fixed. Basically the models have the counterweight permanently attached, which you would never do IRL because A. they can't be driven because they're too heavy, B. they're not road legal because they're too heavy and too wide, C. they can't go down a normal street because of A and B

The performance problems likely can never be fixed thanks to Unity, but everything else I listed above needs to be fixed, and until it is I simply cannot recommend this game, and it rightly shouldn't be for sale either.\t\t\t\t\t
