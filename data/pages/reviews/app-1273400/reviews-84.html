\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's quirky and has some issue's but overall it's a very enjoyable game.

Graphics: They are pretty decent to be fair, they are not Crysis level, but still very very decent to look at. There are however, at the time of this review some graphical issue's OR less quality control, that I am sure will be updated. They consist off ghosting effect behind vehicles, moving objects, there are area's of the terrain you can clearly see are flat and under neath the none connecting parts. Even some of the rocks on the cliffs enable you to run inside and are hollow clipping through the map is easy. Graphically it does make it feel rushed and not enough quality control.
There are still some good detailing from mud tyre prints on the road, to the overal detailing effects of the stone, rubble or tarmac, it's really nice.

Audio: All your trucks, diggers, tractor sounds to tarmac screeching with racing around the map to dirt, it's all there and cannot complain.

Controls: Complicated to a degree but overall easy to get used too. Far easier here then any previous construction game!

Game-play and everything else after: So my first issue I would say is around how the lobbies and joining a friend works. Currently the host presses continue, loads their lobby and in, the connector/friend presses START game, then lobby and then searches with the name, this way of joining is very bizarre and very convoluted way when Steam Friend Invite is right there....

The maps are pretty small but you do not need a massive map at all. It's pretty compact to do what you want. Once you unlocked area's, you can fast travel around saving a lot of time, which is great.

One thing I did notice, which I am not 100% sure if this is due to the different difficulties you can select from low (Less money, less tasks) to medium and high (Meaning more money and even doubling the work to do) but when example laying concrete or flattening a road... you can clearly see it's full of bumps and holes BUT it shows 100% complete, leave the area? I would of liked to finish it which technically you can? But still the immersion is ruined a little.

There are a LOT of vehicles to choose and deal with to handle the tasks. All are greatly detailed, different and very fun to control! Some, the controls are HORRID, but it makes sense? Kinda? Why the track vehicles are absolutely ruined with Q and E for forward and left and right to reverse Y and C, why? LIKE WHY! It's insane! I want, up down left right, simple but nope!

Lots to choose from and do, with lots of work to be carried out. You cannot kill civilians or friends, I've tried. There is no health so you can leap off stuff. There are some bugs and glitches as I write this review, which will be fixed in time... Just nothing game breaking.

Digging, building, exploring, driving, racing, ramming, cranes, swinging, trolling, cementing, the list goes on. You can do a lot and it's all really enjoyable. If you wanted something relaxing with a little brain work, this is up there as an odd game very underestimated!

The co-op does work great (Once you've learnt how to join a lobby) and your progression with your character is transferred to your world too, so unlocks, skills etc transfer which is great when supporting, but your world does not. So you will have to repeat some!

Another quirk is the mouse is ALWAYS on the screen and if you have more then one monitor, even when exclusive full screen, moving around it will minimise and click something else, WEIRD and silly!

There is a collectable on the maps (Because yes there are two maps, EU and US) in the form of a magic glowing hammer. All you get for this is an achievement? Making it not worth hunting for personally. A skin unlock or something amusing would of been great to use, other then an achievement I can use S.A.M for.

Would I recommend this game? Yes, it's a great game that is superb cheap and for what you get, it's got hours and hours of fun behind it from laying roads to building towers to removing items off buildings. It's a great game single but mostly fun co-op.\t\t\t\t\t
