One being what seems as its modeled around the US state of California and the other in Europe based in Germany. Both maps do seem very detailed and huge and the graphics are great as well. As far as the vehicle selection and choices it is pretty good. There are many well known licensed brands. However, I am not a fan of using European trailers specifically on the american map. I do like the improvements im seeing in this new version of construction simulator. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not a simulator. This is an on-the-rails, everything scripted, perform A / perform B / perform C game.

There is none of the usual challenge and satisfaction that comes with doing a task properly, since when you get to about 80% of any task (like compacting asphalt, or digging a trench) it magically completes the rest for you and normally completes a bunch of other unspecified tasks too (eg, on almost completing a task to dig a trench for a road, suddenly it was complete, clean, had scaffolding, and 3 ancillary trenches).

The tutorial is mandatory before you can do anything, and you can be forgiven for thinking "maybe its just like this for the tutorial" but nope. Also the tutorial is conveniently just over 2 hours long, conveniently long enough to take you out of the Steam refund period.

All vehicles appear to have welded diffs, and turn like furniture.

Hardware optimisation is terrible.

All this can be yours for ¬£30.\t\t\t\t\t
