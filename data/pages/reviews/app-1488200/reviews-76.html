\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really good game to play all the way through. It definitely feels a bit like Ogre Battle 64 & Fire Emblem were put in a particle accelerator and this is what was created from it.
The way you make units is fun, and thankfully around mid-game you can easily train them up for strength to help things along a little. The movement of units is straightforward. There's a rough weapons/armor/class triangle in place, but there seemed to be a few exceptions to the rules that didn't stick in my brain.
The storyline is good and has a few fun unseen twists and a few you kind of see coming if you've played enough games like this. It did feel a bit rushed, and I would have loved to get a few more chapters with battles along the way. The bonding (romance) part felt super rushed a few times. Like first time meeting, oh hey they're romantically involved now! A few more conversations spread out over a few more chapters would help there too. (By the way, did I mention just having more chapters? I can come up with more excuse-erm, reasons for them when you make the next game!)
Does it have a few rough spots? Yeah, but the team is still working on patches, and none of them really ruin the game.
As of 2/28/2023, there's like two cut scenes where the text just speeds past on its own, but only for like two or three panels and it doesn't seem to have been major since I could still follow the story.
I will say that chapter 27 was a bit frustrating given what's happening plus the bonus objective not being the clearest. After a few restarts I gave up on the bonus objective, but somehow still got it.

All in all this game is definitely worth buying and playing if you enjoy tactics games!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, this game almost ruined my life...I got so hooked that it's the only thing I did for 10 days in my free time (and not so free time). The story is OK I guess, but it's not about the story, at least not for me, it was about the tactical masterpeice of this game.

This game makes you think, especially on warlord mode. You have to plan not only your movements on the battlefield but your squads and thier compositions (items, skills, etc.). There are a lot of possiblities so you can mix and match as you see fit and try things out in the arena.

I cannot recommend this game enough, it was truly a joy playing it (on all difficulties). And for the achievement hunters like myself, it's pretty simple to get everything and you do so just playing the game, the only really challenging thing is beating all enemies on Chapter 12, but it more than doable.

10/10 game for me, will replay it in the future\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\treally fun game overall. Lots of army management options, so you really can build your own force. Having some unique heroes really is a nice touch, as there are no other units that can do what they do, or operate the same way. Artifact system really gives you another level of depth do your squads, as they can equip some items to further customize their role. Each unit can also have 2 traits that can give you some pretty interesting combo choices.

The only things I find a bit lacking are the consistency between games, unit affinities, and the ability to really maximize your army. Items, and abilities, are not consistently available, so if you are going for a certain build, you may never have the option come up. One example is that you start with a unit in the game that has the trait "archery captain" which makes units with a bow cost 2 less leadership capacity to add to your squad. In one run, this is the only "captain" I had the entire time, and did not have the opportunity to ever buy another one. In another run of the game, I was able to get more of these captain types, including one that worked for "firearms", and felt it made a big difference to army power, because your squads can have more units/better artifacts if you have more leadership available. The other issue is the randomness of available market items. I had one play-through that went dragon heavy, and I was not able to get enough gems to promote them to the top tier. You can't buy an army of full dragons, and you have to level them up from a baby when you get them, but you won't have enough gem resources to promote all of them, so your army will be lacking in the end if you went this route, and it's all down to if you were lucky enough to be offered gems to promote them. The other randomness is the artifacts being offered. Some runs you will get multiple opportunities to buy or find some of the better ones, and some runs you will never see them. This all can really make you feel like if you just had a bit better luck, then you could really maximize what you were going for. Also, it can be very random when you are able to get "arena tokens", as in one run I ended up with more platinum tokens then bronze

The thing I really like least is unit affinity. It's some kind of elemental affiliation that dictates growth of stats. With limited resources, and random chance to get what you are looking for, it's just another thing that you may have limiting what you are trying to do, and really does not add to the game.\t\t\t\t\t
