\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is great, very fun and engaging, but you can't recommend it with a straight face to someone who hasn't enjoyed 1995 Ogre Battle and 2003 Fire Emblem 7 in their prime.

Here are random notes:

The good:
- You can speed up the enemy phase by holding L.
- Enemy squad are well designed (in contrast to the random squad formation of Ogre Battle).

The ugly:
- UI is poor, like the 'Scenario Result' disappears I don't know when/how, and the gamepad sticks have a very quick repeat delay.
- When the lights out, it's too dark.
- Game slows down to a crawl (I play on Linux, maybe related).
- Units seem to all have large amount of 'move', even the slow infantry, making it vain to try to 'hide' your archer squad since baddies can just walk around. I think an army skill helps with that, but still takes time to adjust in the first few levels.
- It's quasi impossible to anticipate the range and number of attacks that the enemy will lash out, as it randomly has hidden army skills like 3 range cannons and double firearm attacks. It makes you feel like the army skills are not bonus, but things your army needs to catch up on.
- Brings some of the worst part of Fire Emblem:
- the support/relationship dialogs. I eventually skipped the dialogs even in my first playthrough.
- the 'other' phase. So boring. Who cares about the green army?\t\t\t\t\t
