\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game looks to be a marriage between Ogre Battle (March of the Black Queen) and Fire Emblem. Multiple units are assigned to one squad with a designated leader and there's advantages to making multiple squads of all similar units, or diverse ones and there is quite a bit of diverse units. The game demands a lot of those units, but unlike Ogre Battle, stats and promotion materials are recovered when changing character classes, so it's fairly friendly to experiment by changing your units provided that they have gotten enough "class points" through battles, arenas, consumables, or tech modifiers.

Due to the number of units involved, it is closer to having an "army" than a Fire Emblem game were you simply manage a few select heroes and can essentially carry just about any of those games with 1 - 2 characters. This game has unit moral, a magic-decaying system (healing gets lower each fight,) and limited leveling per scenario, so it's much harder to simply have 1 character or team do all the work throughout the game.

However, the writing, story, and characters are horrible cliche. If I describe a character at all, then I'm probably writing a 'spoiler'. if you know the character 'class', then you pretty much know the backstory, character arch, conversation support, and contribution of that character. Granted, FIre Emblem is rife with reusing their own tropes to the point they become cliche, but this game doesn't seem to have much space for subtly, nuance, or effective foreshadowing so I'd suggest rapidly clicking through any conversation or cut-scene.\t\t\t\t\t
