\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a solid game and I definitely recommend it. An SRPG really only needs to successfully meet two objectives to be worth playing: customizability and replayability. This game lets you build your own armies and outfit them in many different ways. That's customizability. It also has enough unit types, equipment, and traits to have too many combinations to try in one playthrough. That's replayability. With those two points satisfied, the game has tons of content and a strong gameplay loop to keep you interested. Add to that great music and strong visuals (although the portraits look really weird to me) and you have an excellent game.

Now the weakest part of the game is definitely the story and the writing. The story ranges from mediocre to below average. It just feels so standard and by the numbers for most of the game and takes some dips with weird character choices and twists that were expected. It just doesn't do much. The writing itself is the most egregious part of the problem though. It honestly feels like it was written, put through a translator a couple of times, then pasted into the game. It just feels so souless. I can only assume the writer was an amateur and this was their first outing making a story for a game. The skip dialogue button can speed you through this, so it's hardly a problem if you don't want it to be. The only other weakness in the game is the UI can be a bit clunky, and I've accidentally messed up turns because of it.

But let's not get lost of the details of the game's weak points. Having the aforementioned customizability and replayability pretty much put it at an 8/10 right off the bat. The music and visuals easily bump it up to 8.5 or 9/10. I enjoyed this game thoroughly, from start to finish.

The rest of the review is to address what changes can be made to make the eventual sequel even better than this game. First off, don't put us on the rails the whole game. The gameplay loop is great, but it feels a bit disappointing to go from story mission to story mission in the exact same way each playthrough. There's only a single chapter in the entire game that has any side content. I feel that at the very least, there should be side missions every few chapters to give us optional and possibly challenging stuff to do between the story. Ideally, I would like a branching story path, that allows me to do different story missions based on choices made. This could be done either through our performance on missions (S Rank or completing all challenge objectives) or by having us make story choices in the game. For instance, we make it to a city under siege, and we have a choice to either man the forts to protect the city, or leave and rescue a neighboring town that has important resources for the city. Choosing one or the other would alter the path you take in the story and make it more interesting for future playhroughs.

Second, I think the relationship system should be fleshed out a bit. Right now, all it is, is you go to conversations, click one, skip the dialogue, and voila, you have faction xp. I think there should be manual choices you make in the conversations that determine your relationship with people. Through this, you have the agency to decide how close you are with your compatriots. I think this could also be used to unlock side content as well, which could further enrich subsequent playthroughs of the game.

One more change should be considered for the sequel. The store should be able to be refreshed throughout the game. Maybe have it cost gold or have a currency specifically for refreshing the store, but it really needs it. I enjoy roguelite progression in actual roguelite games but here, it hampers my ability to build teams the way I want them to be built. For instance, it wasn't until my fourth run of the game, that I actually got obsidian plate armor. It's not that major of a piece, but I should definitely be encountering it every game. Same with arcane might and arcane vigor. I've only seen each of those traits once in all the time I've played. That is perhaps, the most problematic part of the loot system in this game, especially if you want multiple of the same trait to build specialized groups.

I have some other suggestions but they're mostly nitpicks, and any game that uses this one as a foundation is going to have strong bones, and likely be worth playing, so keep up the good work, Dev.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA mix between Ogre Battle 64 and Fire Emblem. Great art style for the characters, some good sprite work in battles. The story scenes leave something to be desired in terms of graphical fidelity (it's rpg maker style stuff) and story, but also the story isn't as bad as some reviews have made it out to be.

I'm the type of SRPG player who can spend seemingly countless hours in the army management aspect of these games in between battles, and Symphony of War turns out to be a great game for that. The squad mechanic (similar again to Ogre Battle), makes for fairly deep strategic build variety, and after 60 hours I still find myself tweaking squads, adding/removing characters, changing classes and gear loadouts, etc.

The battles themselves are sufficiently challenging (I'm playing on the hardest difficulty) and they oftentimes evoke a fairly grand scale due to the size of the maps and number of enemy squads. Do be warned that if you play with perma-death enabled and you find yourself resetting every time a valued squad member is killed, you will be resetting often. Fortunately there is a quick save/load feature that makes this fairly painless.

Did I mention I like the character art? I like the character art. And oh yeah, plays GREAT on Steam Deck!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSymphony of war may not be a big budget AAA game yet I would go as far as to say that it is a true masterpiece. The passion which the developers put into this game is evident from start to finish, the story is compelling and enjoyable, while the game play is simple yet tactical. It amazes me just how engrossed I managed to become in the story's of each of the individual characters while constantly racking my brain trying to figure out the best strategy going into each mission before they had even begun. This game makes me want to be smart, both through the stress of the combat and the motivation given by the characters. I look forward to seeing what these devs do next and will follow their storytelling as I can tell that they are truly passionate about it. It is titles like this that are what keep the gaming industry alive, and it is the heart of the people behind it that makes it so great.\t\t\t\t\t
