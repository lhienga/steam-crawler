\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIntroduction

A linear turn-based game with elements of an extensive plot and the improvement of units and armies.
Expect plot twists and lots of battles to fight.

Gameplay : 4/5
Graphics : 4/5
Music : 5/5
Sound Effects : 4/5
Voice Over Narrations : 3/5
Storyline: 4/5

Overal Note : 4/5

Positive :

Extensive army management. Many choices and possibilities for configuring units and armies. Lots of interesting maps to try out your strategies.
Decent game quality.

Negative :

Somewhat repetitive and tiring narration of the main characters.
Many of the threads and conversations in the game add a lot to the story, but not all conversations are interesting. Fortunately, there is a "skip" option.

Some of the troops the player leads are almost indestructible, while others drop like flies. The disproportion is sometimes quite significant.

One of my units single-handedly killed all enemies during the mission, regardless of whether they were attacking or defending. The rest was unnecessary :)

The linearity of the game and the lack of choices during the campaign can be a bit irritating.

Overal Review :

An interesting game for fans of turn-based games who appreciate extensive gameplay, micromanagement of troops and armies, and extensive plot threads.
The product is aimed at casual gamers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun game. I did my first play through on warlord difficulty with DLC.

TL;DR combat is great, squad building is great, story is average but is just interesting and insane enough to keep engagement, UI is not made for desktop, in game wiki could use a lot of improvement, mouse/keyboard controls are jank; controls are designed for controllers.

The game handles squad management very well. It can be incredibly fun spending hours reading and theorizing which traits artifacts and units to put in which squad. Certain characters are designed for specific units and specific play-styles and it feels great to get a lore fitting squad for a character to work.

That being said, it can be a bit frustrating in the early/mid-game when you no longer have access to certain squads that you spent time theory crafting for multiple chapters. For example you don't want to invest in building and relying upon a certain character that starts chapter 1 at lvl 8 because he won't be around after chapter 3 for a fairly long time. For that reason alone, having one squad hogging all the kills could be super punishing when that squad is unavailable or when you need a side objective but all your other squads are 5+ levels below the enemies.\t\t\t\t\t
