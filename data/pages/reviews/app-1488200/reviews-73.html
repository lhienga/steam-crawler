\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to say that my experience with SoW was mostly positive, but with some here and there that really got on my nerves towards the tail end of things.

This game is impressive to me, the developer make good use of RPGmaker in a lot of aspects. And not well enough in others, but I'd still say it's overall a great effort.

There's fun to be had in creating armies, forming squads, and upgrading units into whatever you want them to be to make a balanced squad or make someone dedicated to one role. The old fire emblem weapon system of stuff breaking isn't here, thank christ. Instead we have sort of an armor/weaponry circle? It's a little hard to explain but it's easy to understand the offense and defense benefits.

My biggest complaints though is that, for most of my playthrough, I was only really motivated enough to form 3-4 independent squads that did not come from a plot relevant character. Most of the time those important characters already come with their own armies, and even if you make a ton of troops you probably won't get far without the benefits from one of the default leaders, whether you buy stronger ones at a marketplace or not being irrelevant. And it's only really in the last couple of chapters that you get to deploy upward of 20 squads, though for me I was rolling with 17 at the end of my first playthrough.

Guns are annoying. I was fascinated with them at first, but then I realized they were cheap shots to destroy everything with only one unit being able to really survive a full volley, and that's the sentinel. Of course this is before they start throwing rows of 4-6 gunmen at you, and if it's the enemy that goes first then you may as well mark that squad off as KIA for all it's worth because the enemy guns tend to have 2 shots whereas yours only get 1 until a late end game tech tree becomes available.

There are several priority targets and strategic approaches you can take when attacking. For me though I wish I could've had an order that said "Ignore the healers you minges." because seeing 5-7 people dogpile one healer in the center and wasting their turn instead of the row of magic tossers in the back is very VERY aggravating.

I also advice against playing with permadeath mode on. Cause it felt more arduous than it was worth. The plot wasn't really incredibly deep, and the characters are pretty one note. Honestly though, sometimes a straight forward nice time is a good thing instead of a ton of complex twists and turns that make you go "Oh sh!t" every 5 seconds. The game also does great at one point when your resources feel like they're dwindling, I won't go into detail but it matches up with how the plot was up and til that moment and it felt great to build it back up.

It's a good game for the most part with some pretty big red flags, but overall as something to lay back and relax with the challenge felt pretty fair, especially if you try to S rank chapters, though I do not recall any option to replay previous chapters unless you got a save for then, but if you're like me you save before each chapter anyway.

I look forward to whatever this team does next and probably will be investigating their previous products as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome game. It really captured the old school feel of games from my youth while still having some originality.

The story is good but not incredible, it definitely had some cool moments though. Dialogue was fine, not super cringe which is good enough. Characters were overall likable, though none really stood out. Mechanics were fun and I had a good time figuring things out and experimenting. Combat was good. Played on Normal (Captain) difficulty and it got pretty easy by the end, but along the way there was enough challenge. I probably will play on hard difficulty at some point.

The two things I think were worst are the menus and the class balance. The menus are kind of a pain in the ass to go through and some things aren't obvious at first. They kind of felt old school though in how bad they were so it didn't bother me that much, but they should really be improved if they make a sequel. The class balance makes it so certain combinations are significantly more viable than others. My two knight squads basically carried the second half of the game. It's not terrible though since a lot of combinations aren't 'bad', but on a second play through there are definitely a lot of units I won't bother building. The main issue is you have a time limit to get the best score, so making a 'slow and steady' army means you won't get that S rank.

The meh I would say is the art. The combat sprites and animations are on point, the out of combat sprites are shoddy and the portrait art feels a bit off. I do like that they tried a different style of portrait art than generic though, so I give credit for that. I'd like to see this style refined more in future games from the studio. The out of combat sprites definitely need work though, they seem like bland RPG maker stuff.

Overall, really good game. It innovates while still capturing the feel of older games I love. I really hope to see a sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSUMMARY:
An incredible, tactical game which uses tried and tested formulas and technologically limited means to deliver a great gaming experience. Probably my game of the year 2022! If you like strategic/tactical games and you have some patience for moderate menu-ing, it is a wholehearted recommend.

THE GOOD:
I don't actually have any background with the tactics ogre games, so I compare it to the XCOM-games. It has this super-engaging loop, where during the battles, you collect XP and materials, which you then can't wait to spend in your base to upgrade your troops. Having done that, you can't wait to get to the battlefield and tried it out. Repeat.
The great thing in that, is that there is a rather wide variety in unit types, which actually play different. This gives you interesting things to explore tactically in the first playthrough and also provides replay value.
Much bad is said in the comments on the writing and the story. I didn't mind the writing. While the story can't be called "inspiring", it totally served as a further hook in and of itself to keep me playing to see what happens next and to further my characters goals. I even found that it wasn't without emotional impact (crazy, given it is "just" pixel-art).

THE BAD
The difficulty is somehow busted. First, you NEED to have permadeath one, if you don't want things to be entirely trivial. Even with that, on normal difficulty you will at some point likely scale out of reach of the enemies and you will just steamroll everything. Inconsistent balancing of unit strengths, communication of vital combat information as well as an overly convoluted and opaque strength/weakness-systems (light vs. armored vs. magic + all kinds of elemental stuff interacting with weather) keep the game from being traceable enough to tackle it in harder difficulties. I feel that the game only really works for me when approached as a casual experience and enjoyed the power-creep as you become and instoppable force.
Also, quite some playtime is spent in menus. Now, a bit of that is great. But even for me it sometimes became grating and I'm usually quite patient with this kinda stuff.

THE MEH:
- The music was sometimes good, but often just serviceable.
- The bonding system seems either like a wasted opportunity or just a superfluous inclusion. I was psyched on further playthroughs to see what happens if different characters bond, but to my disappointment the impact of the entire system is very minimal.

Also, boobas.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAm amazing homage to (among other things) the outstanding GBA Fire Emblems, with a greater focus on force customization and preparing the right squads for the right situation. Good buy at full price, no brainer on sale.

The good: satisfying force customization and gameplay, fun little characters, and a broad and highly flexible class change and promotion system that lets you bring the right tools to solve the games tactical problems.

The bad: nothing, honestly. Outstanding turn based strategy game.

The details:
1. Great little cheesy fantasy plot that meshes well with the gameplay. Its good fun, and a solid backdrop to the various missions. I would characterize it as a YA fantasy novel.
2. Awesome focus on squad building and customization. The main characters come with examples of how to put squads together, but most of the missions have space to bring more squads that you can build up from minor/filler soldiers. Taking advantage of this can help fill gaps in your army, or let you load up on certain strengths, e.g. a cavalry heavy force, or extra heavy infantry squads to rank the enemy. Theres decent odds youll want to tweak your squads mission to mission, depending on the situation.
3. Decent variety in maps and missions, with the environment and enemy types demanding some thoughtful deployment and use of your forces.\t\t\t\t\t
