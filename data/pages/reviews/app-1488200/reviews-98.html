I hope the devs continue to make games, and experiment with this genre! DLC for this game would also be fun! Personally I'm not a big story person when it comes to games like these, so I would love to see more of these army building and tactics mechanics in sort of a sandbox style like mount and blade, where I can set my own goals.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best game you've never heard of.

Very underrated battle mommy simulator.

Combines the best parts of Ogre Battle and Fire Emblem series.

------------------------------

You move around the map in turn based battle like Fire Emblem, but you control entire squads that fight mostly with direct commands like Ogre Battle. You can change the classes, gear, traits, and element of every character, again just like ogre battle.

Map screen and terrain looks a lot like Fire Emblem or Advance Wars, as do the character sprites. Very enjoyable to look at, if you are a fan of retro games with a super nintendo style.

Lots of really smart design decisions here; if you spend a ton of resources to upgrade a character and then realize you don't like the new class, you get all the resources back if change back to your old class. Healers are smart enough to hold their turn if no allies are hurt, and then drop the heal after the first round of combat. Lots of convenient little details like this are present throughout the game and show that the developer has played these kinds of games before, and has learned from them. Excellently executed.

The writing of the game is mostly smart with a hint of self awareness; the protagonist often voices the same concerns and questions the player will have, which is nice. There are definitely a handful of weird and cheesy parts but thankfully the protagonist and supporting cast usually get straight to the point in most scenes.

Downsides to the game are low res character art, lack of voice acting, some dated looking effects, and rigid menu system that often requires extra clicks to get to the setting you are actually trying to change. You can tell the game had a small budget. The game is also very easy, to the point where playing normal is more of a cakewalk and playing on hard is basically necessary if you want a challenge. Most maps can be completed easily on hard, but completing them with zero casualties is the real challenge and that can be tricky on some maps.

Overall highly recommended, but understand that you are getting a low budget, super nintendo looking game here. The multiple layers of customization and tactical depth underneath more than make up for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWonderful game that last for around 50hrs. To put it simply, it reminds me of Fire Emblem but a more tactical variant. Whereas each person on the map acts as a placeholder for 1-9 individual units, rather than the traditional single unit. This allows more customization in how you build each of your forces and makes the scale seem more grand in comparison. Everything else is very similar, which is to say, it's very good.

Story is good with several twists. I would say it suffers from a bit too much foreshadowing, but that's me being critical. Overall I quite enjoyed it.

I finished the game on Captain difficulty, which is the 'normal' mode. Had decent difficulty in the beginning, coasted in the middle and towards the end, but the last 3ish missions were quite difficult. I slightly wish I had played their hardest difficulty (which would give characters perma death, rather than just taking them out till next mission) simply because I think it would have caused everything to be much more intense and I would have had a more difficult time for the middle and late missions. Also chapter 12... that one's a doozy.

My only real critique is that in missions at the start characters will sometimes give a quick tip on how to best complete the mission where there are irregular goals, but I feel it was too vague in some. An example is when you ambush the Imperial Caravan, you're told to not attack too early or you may lose some of the loot, but the only way you lose the loot is if you wait too long. Just seems counter productive a little. It was pretty rare this happened, but it's still worth noting.

All in all, love the game. Probably give it a solid 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr 8/10 gameplay with a mediocre 4-5/10 story (dependent on how many eye rolls it gets you). If you like fire emblem / advanced wars gameplay with a tendency to min max then you will enjoy the game. Just skip all the cringe cutscenes. you'll get 20$ worth of game.

As far as the game is concerned, the gameplay is what really caries this here. The maps / missions are well balanced, though if you know what you are doing they are pretty easy. The real bread and butter / addiction comes from fine tuning and min maxing your units. the sheer variety you can use is a selling point. Allowing you to easily fine tune your play style, though some are clearly meant for different parts of the game. (looking at you guns) Otherwise the classic fire emblem formula for balance gets put on its head here with the options available to you.

Sprites are fine, if a bit generic. sound assets are fine, though the menu music is a bit to close to one summers day to not be a coincidence.

However this game is far from perfect. The story is pretty dull and frankly uninspiring, with the dialogue being bland, and the voice over work on units poor. The plot will surprise no one over the age of 16, it' isn't "bad" in the terms of mechanics, it is simply boring. You have all the standard tropes for every character, with the worst offender being abigale who is supposedly the canon romance partner for male hero. Which is trite as they have no chemestry whatsoever. Frankly all of the romance with the exception of stefan / diana is fanfic tier.

game side, my only complaints are the UI leaving much to be desired, while it is serviceable and doesn't get in the way of playing, the managment of your units gets quite tedious with the amount of time you have to go through different menus that seem to take just a second too long to cycle through. Lack of tool tips on items means you have to waste time waiting for the description on the bottom of your screen to decide to roll up. Descriptions in of themselves leave much to be desired.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful narration with a more turn-based approach to Ogre-Battle-like gameplay. I was pleasantly surprised to get the ~60 hours of fairly straight gameplay at the most challenging difficulty -- and for those wanting a serious challenge that rewards you for learning the underlying game mechanics, definitely go with the "normal solider permadeath" mode. I personally felt this mode turns the game from a narrative game with tactical elements into a tactical game with narrative elements. Wonderful depth to a beautiful game. Can't wait to see future releases!

My primary gripes are in the squad-manipulation UI (there's some streamlining, but much of the macro work feels fairly clunky) and in the "relationship" options presented throughout the game. It's fairly meaningless in the scope of gameplay, but for the more narrative-minded, it isn't clear WHO could become bonded -- and several of the choices that come up result in the most cringe-worthy dialogue in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe shortest way to describe Symphony of War is "Ogre Battle for the PC crowd". (See also: Fell Seal, our Final Fantasy Tactics.) It's a somewhat simplistic way to describe a long overdue return to this style of tactical RPG -- especially considering Symphony of War hits all the right notes for the genre.

World-threatening demonic evil? Check. Protagonists who learn Very Valuable Lessons™ as they become infused with divine power to battle said evil? Check. Romance-lite character options? Check, though as far as I could tell, it's purely a cosmetic choice -- no significant gameplay is altered by choosing who bonds with whom, though the ending story will change as appropriate.

The roughness around the edges of Symphony of War -- fluctuating difficulty levels, heavy reliance on named characters (seriously, Diana and Barnabas can tank most of the game by themselves), uneven writing -- can be forgiven by the clear amounts of love and effort put into making this tactical JRPG shine like it was ripped straight out of the 16-bit golden age. Heck, you could even argue the writing is a throwback to the oddball translations of the 1990s, intentionally or not.

This is a great game. Buy it and enjoy.

(Completed on Captain difficulty without permadeath enabled.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHello!
After playing this game for 89+ hours I think I can formulate a good opinion on this game. To those who read this, I would recommend this game. This game is an Action Turn-Based RPG game with tactical and strategic elements on top. If you are a fan of games like Fire Emblem you will definitely love this game.

Pros:

1. This game is visually well-designed when it comes to the style of game it aspires to be. The models of the characters are nice, and the terrain makes the map look quite pretty. The maps can be rather large but still close enough for everything to still seem compact and make sense in terms of the narrative and visuals.

2. The combat is great as well, there are a variety of units from Tier 1 to 3 that have various roles to play. You have magicians, soldiers, archers, gunners, cavalry, dragons, medics, and the list continues.. There is definitely a plentiful variety here and due to this you can make some pretty interesting squad compositions. Each squad can have up to 9 members and where you place your members inside the squad will matter in combat as well. Such as heavy infantry in front to cover for your ranged infantry in the back.

3. The narrative is pretty well-written despite the last mission feeling a little rushed. However, everything up to Chapter 27-28 feels connected and well-rounded. The story is cohesive and interesting to watch. I would say first run playthroughs will be especially nice as its the first time experiencing the story. I love the plot twist in the middle and how you got to play on the evil side for a chapter. Definitely a departure from normal plot designs.

4. Artifacts, Items, Affinities, Resources like Iron and Gems, and Tokens are all easy to understand how they work with the UI making these things accessible and easy to use. Resources are used to rank up soldiers from Tier 1 to 2 or 2 to 3 depending on the class. Affinities give various bonuses and debuffs in certain areas like Light giving only ++LDR and ++Loyalty. Along with Artifacts that allow for squad buff/debuff customization the game shines fully in this aspect.

Cons:
(Spoiler Alert for #1)
1. The narrative while good for the most part feels a little rushed at the end. Such as after the downfall of Casamir the focus shifting from Casamir to Zanatus rather quickly. In Chapter 29 you turn towards getting the Lightfury from the shrine and then in Chapter 30 you are immediately facing the supposed "evil god" in this alternate dimensional hell that he resides in. Besides the rushed ending the other issue with the narrative is that some plot twists are very predictable such as Lysander ending up causing a mutiny trying to stop Arthur from getting the lightfury. Despite Arthur explicity forgiving Lysander we still see Lysander end up in prison for a long time because of the mutiny. It just seemed one way and then it took the other way.

2. The graphics are nice in my opinion, but I know it can be a turn-off for others as some people would prefer a more 3D visual design which is fine. Something like Fire Emblem Three Houses is really good at this but is not really available here. I would also point out that while there are a variety of maps you generally have only 4-5 different aesthetics(biomes) that you'll be fighting in.. Dungeon, Castle, Desert, Snow, Plains, Mountains. So the maps can blend together sometimes.

I could go on all day but at this point I would just be nitpicking minor things that really don't affect my opinion of the game. I still think the game is good with some setbacks but ultimately makes for a good campaign experience every once in a while.
