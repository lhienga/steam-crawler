\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR It took me a while to figure it out, but this is a game that not only lets you "minmax the fun out of" it, it actually feels designed to make it almost inevitable. Unbelievably vague stats and clunky UI only exacerbate the problem by making everything into trial and error game followed by respeccing options that easily take three times as long as they reasonably should.

====

(15 hours in:) Game has no revealed damage calculation formula or even a combat result prediction. Honestly that's my biggest gripe because as a game it's fine, the army unit composition is neat, and the story is okay if a bit generic so far. But calling it a tactics game with all these stat numbers but practically no context for what any of them mean is a failure as far as I'm concerned. And practically all the customization you can do just adjusts those numbers a little leading to you still not knowing how much damage you'll deal or receive, or what the hit or crit chance is, or even if there's hidden penalties to certain actions (hint: there is, magic has some kind of recast penalty that decreases it's effect the more its used, but there's no way to know that without trial and error). Units may as well not have visible stats at all.

Game is still decent fun, but in my eyes these failures mean in the end it is not a real tactics game, just a "guess and see!" followed by "save scumming time!" game.

==

updated (30 hours): Played a little more just to try to get through it, but everything about this games screams that it wants to be a rogue-light but it only has the fixed railroaded campaign mode.

Unit planning UI is also abysmal for the level of customization you might want to do for your units. You can sort by any stat EXCEPT CP mastery level (the one that you would want most to figure out who you want to level in places like the Arena). Every time you change class on a unit in your barracks it moves the unit along with where you are in the menu, so if you want to, say, look through your militia to find the best recruits to spend your limited upgrade materials on, once you upgrade one you have to scroll back through all your other units to get back to the "militia" chunk so you can choose the next one. Also, why can't you use "up" to go from the top of the list to the bottom?

Also, last but not least, unit upgrade paths are basically fixed. Despite every class having like 5 or 6 "choices" for upgrades, there is only 1 or 2 they'll actually qualify for (usually one that walks or one that's on a horse). Why even have the options at all? Quite frustrating and ultimately that and the whole UI thing made me give up on the game I think. Better to have limited/no customization than a fake/poorly implemented one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKeeping in mind that I didn't play a lot of Japanese games beside Final Fantasy and Atelier series, for me this game is a masterpiece. It has a lot of things that I love in a strategy game: tactics, customizing heroes and their armies, very well designed missions and even great music.

For those like me, without a lot of experience in Japanese games, there's a brief description of the game: you are a hero who is somehow selected to save the world from a very long war. You make friends with other people who have the same objective as you and you build your armies together, recruiting all kind of warriors and put them in your armies.

There are different classes: archers, soldiers, mages, big cats, assassins, necromants (from DLC) and even dragons. Some of them can be trained to be squad leaders. Every warrior has a few traits, like thief, guardian (who is good at covering their teammates), imposing (the enemies tend to attack them first) or the opposite, vengeful (they do more damage if their colleagues are incapacitated and so on.

You receive a mission, beat the enemies on a manually designed map, then come back to town and customize every member of your armies (if you want) with the goodies that you receive as reward or that you can buy from the town marketplace or from the bazaars in the missions' map. You also have some interactions with your friends and get to build relationships with them and between them.

The combat is automated and that means that winning the battles depends strongly on how you build and customize your armies and your warriors.

And now for a really big surprise for me in a strategy game: the story is very, and I mean very well written. It's like a novel and you can't wait to see what will happen with your heroes or in the world. It also has a lot of twists and is very exciting. The drawback of great writing is that some bits in the middle of the game are kinda depressing and frustrating, but fortunately not for long.

Pros:
- very engaging gameplay
- very interesting characters
- great story, like a very good novel, but without a lot of text
- clever designed maps and tactics
- perfect customizing your armies and warriors
- difficulty can be set as you like
- great music
- exciting: the story and also when you open the marketplace or bazaar and find a great item or recruit (like a dragon)
- deep tactics and strategy and a lot of content

Cons:
- somewhat depressing and frustrating in the midgame.

P.S. I will instantly buy any DLC or sequel made by the devs who made this game. Great work!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the most underrated and underhyped game I've played. It is very obvious that the developers has put in significant time, effort and love in making the gameplay very enjoyable.

Overview:
- If you love Fire Emblem you're gonna enjoy this game. Imagine Fire Emblem but with much in depth game mechanics and RPG style customization.

Pros:
- Cheap price. It's a steal if it's on sales, which happens quite often.
- Decent graphic and animation with nostalgic feels.
- Decent BGM and SFX. Voice-over is repetitive and cringey at times but fortunately you can easily turn it off.
- Easy to learn. In game tutorial/glossary is comprehensive enough so long you have the patience to read them
- Controller compatibility. There is even a built in speed up button. Very much like playing Fire Emblem on emulator.
- Huge variety of classes and items which promotes customization and creative builds
- Despite a lot of people complaining that the menu sucks and lack QOL, I love it very much. Simplicity, less is more. Perhaps kids nowadays have things too easy for them.
- DLC is a must get. It adds significant contents such as new side quests, new class, new items, new traits etc.
- Each playthrough takes around 30-40 hours+ which is quite long and definitely value for money.
- Replayability. There's 100 states if you wish to replay it over and over.
- NG+ (DLC) offers significant customization which allows player to pick the best option for their playstyle and expectation. It's like a mini built in modding tool.
- Small cannons, field cannons, Beatrix's cannons

Need improvement:
- Similar to Fire Emblem (or most strategy games), AI are pretty dumb. Suggest to add more surprise element such as fog, enemies pegging from behind, or simply bullsh-t mechanics just to f-ck with the player. I am a masochist I know.
- Almost all the victory condition is to defeat everything or capture something. Would be great if there are more variety to this.
- Some units are way too overpowered. Necromancer (DLC) broke the game. Free units that hit like truck and tank like a rock. When the Necromancer dies, I expect all the Rizens to turn to dust. But no! They would continue to multiply in numbers. Doesn't make no sense.
- Some units are way too underpowered. Rangers and gunners are useless for a start. Also, halberd units should have better anti-cavalry features. Suggest to add attack priority when defending against cavalry units (like Age of Empires). Probably nerf their damage or limit this feature to Centurion idk
- Quality of life. I'm sure people are already asking for filter function as well as other better ways to navigate the menu.
- LB/RB on controller should rotate between squads instead of acting like left and right button. Also that start and select button are useless and should be used for other QOL purposes.
- There should also be a deploy all option. Would be better if players can choose whether cavalry goes in the front or back.
- Add character portrait on battle map. Also, all story characters should have unique sprite/class.

Conclusion
- Base game and DLC are a must get on sales. Soundtrack and guide are optional and get those only if you want to support the developers.
- Like all strategy games, enemies are either dumb AI or it's artificial difficulty in your face. But it doesn't really matter. What matter most is that I had tons of fun with Symphony of Wars.
