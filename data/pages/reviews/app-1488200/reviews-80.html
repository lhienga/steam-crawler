\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really really fun and offers some very cool customization, progression, and strategy. If you enjoy Fire Emblem then clearly you will enjoy this game, but more broadly if you enjoy turn-based strategy games with progression elements then this is also right up your ally.

Some recommendations/things to consider if you're interested:

1. The game is simply too easy even on the "hardest" difficulty - if you put even in the most basic of planning into your squads you will quickly trivialize all the content. Recommendation: the game badly needs one of two things (or both): it needs a hardcore mode that disables manual saving and automatically saves after each turn (With permadeath - similar to games like Darkest Dungeon) so that your actions have permanent consequences; alternatively it simply needs a harder difficulty.

2. The game continually gives characters which it then takes away at fairly regular intervals throughout the game - it feels bad to build out a squad and then have them suddenly become unavailable and all that development gets "wasted"

3. You are locked into squad leaders for the most part, most of which have a clear archetype you are boxed into. I think the game may be better suited to having the main character be the only locked leader, and give some more flexibility to the player to choose leaders and squad types.

4. I really enjoy some of the optional stretch objectives as a mechanism for additional challenge but because they're so easy, they just become a given. They just need to be harder.

Overall I really enjoy the game, its a few design changes away from being stellar.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did not know what I was getting myself into when I bought this game. Somebody showed me a screenshot, it looked neat, I bought it on a whim. That day I could not stop messaging people about how good a game this is. It could use some polish, QoL improvements, maybe some updated aesthetics, sure. The game, though, the actual mechanics under all the UI and fluff, amazing.

A very complete and well thought-out tactical RPG experience. There's a ton of depth to battles, the building and development of units and squads, and even an overarching progression system in the form of technologies that affect your entire army or unlock new options. The formation system in particular is a twist on most TRPGs that greatly expands the potential for customization and facilitates tactics such as body-blocking for archers and keeping tanks healthy.

The maps are also well constructed, with plenty of optional objectives and twists to make things more interesting than just hacking and slashing and blasting your way through everything.

Overall, just a great game, highly recommended if you're into TRPGs, and I am very much looking forward to seeing where the devs go from here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts not a bad game. It is however quite a different game than i expected. I expected something like diciples 2. The different units seem to be almost identical in how they function and there is autocombat instead of choosing how to attack.
But this is imo a completely different type of game. You dont get ressources per turn, but after every map. You cant purchase units and build armies while playing on the map, you do that between maps instead.

Apart from that, the devs. have made som design choices that i just hate.
1) healers can heal outside combat, but only 3 times. (WHYYYYYYYY). This is something that frustrates me with many games. It breaks my immersion completely. Why is it that a unit suddenly cant heal anymore, except when they are in combat. It makes no sense and i dont think that is a good way to balance anything. If a unit can heal, let it be able to heal.
2) units dont seem to heal naturally. Once they are damage, they are only healed in combat if you have healer.
3) You dont seem to control much with regards to which type of leaders you send around. you are given som leaders and you have to make them work. you only seem to be able to recruit units for the leaders.
4) Because of the way its implemented where you only get resources after you completed a map. Research and purchases etc. doesn't seem like ressources. They seem like map variables that you can choose. For a map that you haven't seen and you dont know what you are going up against.
5) You cant just mouse over stats and get explanation for what they do. This seems really odd since this is standard in quite a lot of games. But everything is hidden rather than letting you make informed decisions.
You dont get to choose which stats upgrade either. this is just automatically.
6) The game seems to be build up to get through the maps as quickly as possible rather than using good strategy and having to evaluate which resources to go for etc.

This seemed like such a great successor for Diciples 2. But this is a completely different type of game.
Seems some people call it a puzzle game because you "need" to choose how you move units to make it though a map in 11 turns. You can use more turns, but get a lower rating for completing.
Its not that its a bad game, i haven't encounted bug etc.
Its just not the type of game that i tought from the reviews that i saw. They dont mention these things.

If this game play had followed diciples 2 gameplay. I think it would have been amazing. But this whole thing of rushing through maps to follow a story rather than being strategy focused just isn't something i find good.\t\t\t\t\t
