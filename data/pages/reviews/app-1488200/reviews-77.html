\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Enjoyable squad based tactical game.

Thoughts:
I recommend going for a higher difficulty if you want a challenge, as the 'normal' difficulty is easy for even vaguely experienced tactical game players, unless you want to play with permadeath on. I appreciate that the game doesn't force it on you.

The story is good. Nothing life changing, but certainly enjoyable. The unique dialogues depending on relationship choices between your characters was a very much appreciated feature.

The graphics are fine. I would have appreciated a few more unique sprites, and a little more variation, but it serves it's purpose.

The tutorials are missing a few key entries, but what's there is enough to get started, and you can figure out the rest.

The menus in the UI can be clunky and unresponsive at times, but the tactical combat gameplay itself is really smooth and quick to navigate.

The music is fine. There's a nice variety of different tracks, and they are all enjoyable to listen to.

All in all, I genuinely enjoyed my time with this game. My wife is also doing a playthrough, and she's enjoying it as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot it for thiccness and gameplay
Stayed for the 2.5m, 6 pack, non-stupid lawful good, humorous, blond hair, blue eyes, paladin waifu.

In all seriousness this game knows what people want: Sexy ladies, great gameplay, great graphics and a hero story.

I haven't really played any fire emblem games so I can't say how similar these are but as far as I can tell this is a game you might like if you like Fire Emblem.

Game is divided in 3 parts: Cutscenes, Headquarters and Missions. In the headquarters you raise squads which are made of 1 to 9 units. During the game you get around a dozen "heroes" which always are leaders for squads and most of them are unique classes. Beside them every other tier 2 or tier 3 unit can become a squad leader. How many units you can add to a squad depends on the leaders leadership stat.
The units itself are also very diverse, there are several tier 1 types that after have their class mastery full can change into several more tier 2 units, of all of them having different benefits. You can even demote them and get ressources back that you need elsewhere which allows you to experiment however you want.
Depending on the units in your squad you also have different types of movement and range of movement, for example a squad made of 2 archers and 3 horse archers is considered cavalry and not only gives them the highest movement range but also to move, attack and then again move (back into safety).
That all together with different items that you can equip on squads and use on units gives you a lot of variety.

The story is nothing to impress with, more standard hero story, but not bad either tbh. Only the writing for the love scenes is really bad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game for every fire emblem fan. There is love in the make of this game, you can tell. After a 100 hours i`ll go for some of the pros and cons.
Gameplay: 8/10 Good interface with a clear tactic board make this a very easy to manouver and to play. Left click select/ right click cancel is a clean and simple design.
Story: 7/10 Not the most eye catching story, with barely any twists or drama. Didn`t read the entire conversations between every character but you can say the AI can make some of these dialogues similar.
Complexity: 10/10 very nice system. Army building composition and many classes with good amplitude of strategies to choose from.
Difficulty: 7/10 Warlord on the first playthrough and is not hard at all, you need something harder for a legendary difficulty. The restart button is to keep your sanity around but i would add more challenges with reinforcements and time constraints that are not made to be possible without a clear sacrifice or clean strategy.
Sound/Video: 9/10 Pixel animation cant go picky with this one. Soundtrack is ad hoc with the game.
Overall: 8.2
Solid choice and far better than many more expensive games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a big-time Fire Emblem nerd. There's something about playing a strategy game in which your units A) have RPG stats and can level up and grow in strength throughout the game, and B) can permanently die if you ♥♥♥♥ up. Symphony of War is a game that scratches a similar itch-- It feels *a lot* like a Fire Emblem game in terms of structure and story. However, there are plenty of differences that help it carve out its own identity.

The squad mechanic is a great example. Rather than controlling individual units on the map, you have dozens of characters in your army who are organized in "squads". When two squads engage in combat, all units involved fight simultaneously, attacking their opponents according to the formation in which they've been arranged. This system adds a ton of depth to the combat, and it feels like after every chapter I'm reorganizing my squads to try and find novel ways to take advantage of the synergies between these characters.

The story isn't great, but it's... I dunno, cozy? It strikes that "generic medieval fantasy" itch and it's pretty easy to turn your brain off when listening to it. Best of all, it's just underwhelming enough that you can get away with skipping all of it without missing anything too important. Just pay attention to the dialogue that happens during battles, as it often gives you advice on what to do.

But yeah, it's a great time. I could spend hours just theory-crafting on how to optimize my units and the arrangements of my squads, and I'm not normally someone who does that sort of thing. Buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
