\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomewhere between Ogre Battle and Fire Emblem, the game boasts allowing you to simultaneously field up to 180 uniquely built characters into a single battle. Too many TTRPGs let you build a large roster of soldiers but give you no reason to actually use more than maybe 10 of them, and that's not true here. Though in some battles a few units get relegated to support duties, I was always glad to have them around.

The game has a good collection of mechanics that encourage diversification of your units, so there's no single clearly optimal army build. Personally, I find combat strategy relies too heavily on first strike advantage. But the fights are clearly designed to incentivize aggressive gameplay. Don't go in expecting to turtle through battles.

The story is pretty stock standard. Some fairly predictable betrayals and secret reveals that you can see coming from a thousand yards. There's a prophecy, and you're the chosen one! Nothing spectacular. The game left me scratching my head on a few plot points, but it seemed clear they want to leave the door open for further titles set in the same world, and I'm honestly excited just to be able to play more content. My first complaint would be that the game wraps up a little too quickly. There's space for a final act that I was expecting that didn't materialize.

Devs have announced further content coming down later this year. Harder difficulty settings, and new unit classes. I'm looking forward to more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk so I finished the game just a couple minutes ago, so here here what I liked about the game and what I didn't:

+ The music in this game is great, even though a couple of OSTs don't loop too well.
+ The art style for the character portraits are good and a couple are great, too, though the facial expressions were really lacking.
+ The game is fun, addictive and more thought provoking than I expected. Even though I've had experience with TRPG games, this game was very challenging for me (on normal difficulty)
+ The story was dark and intriguing throughout the whole game. The turns and twists really hooked me and made me wanted to see what comes next. Though the somewhat amateurish writing held back the story to some extent.

- The writing didn't help with the character dialogues and conversations, either. Especially the romantic conversations. Hope they handle proof-reading the game scripts to someone professional next time.
- The dynamic between the main characters, and especially with and between the side characters were lacking.
- The world-building and character development could've been better.


Overall, There is a great concept behind this game, but it wasn't cooked enough, especially in the script, world-building and character development departments. I give Symphony of War: The Nephilim Saga, 7/10. Definitely recommend the game ti anyone looking for a great and brand-new TRPG.\t\t\t\t\t
