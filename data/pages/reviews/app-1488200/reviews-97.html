\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an absolutely fantastic game! Although the gameplay videos didn’t stand out to me at first, I decided to buy it based on the low price and fantastically high reviews. And am I glad I did! Therefore, I am adding my own review in the hopes that others will buy the game for similar reasons as I did. I also want to support the developers who produced this amazing product.

After a return to gaming and spending a long time playing turn-based war games, I started to look at tactical RPGs similar to those I had played as a teenager. Currently I am playing three such games- Disgaea 1, Banner of the Maid and SOW. Of the three, which are all excellent, SOW has proven itself the superior game to me.

So what separates it from those other games? The unit-based system adds a lot of depth and customization. You’ll spend hours tweaking your forces, seeking that elusive perfection. The controls are easy to learn and the battles run very smoothly. The story and conversation scenes are a perfect accompaniment to the action. Every battle has its place in the story and as such has meaning. Compare with Disgaea, which is much more of a grinding game, and has less meaningful battles. And versus Banner of the Maid, it simply feels smoother and more immersive.

But overall, it is just more FUN for me than its peers. This is something difficult to explain- but spend 2-3 hours on it and you will see what I mean! Disgaea can feel like work at times, grinding and levelling up your characters. In SOW you don’t really need to grind at all (aside from the arenas), and arguably, grinding would rob you of the strategic challenge of playing the campaign in ‘fair’ mode.

TLDR: I recommend this game 100%, and look forward to future releases in the series!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe narrative themes and overall gameplay loops are very much Fire Emblem, except with far more units on the field and combinations to exploit. The game very much encourages it too, especially if you're playing on Permadeath mode. The story campaign, while predictable, was classic and sweet.

Mechanically the game is nigh flawless. My biggest complaints would be that the writing is unconvincing at times, and it breaks you from the story. In fact, most of the issues with the game have to do with that. It's awkwardly paced and very few characters get enough screentime or worthwhile showing to make you care for them (except for mr sly guy, the big paladin lady, and grumpy horse uncle. they are perfect)
The voice acting is also hit-or-miss, but that's just me being nitpicky.

Fantastic game though. Would definitely recommend to anyone looking to scratch that turn based strategy itch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMost certainly a game worthy of the title "Saga" as it took me over 40 hours to breeze through each level.
The core mechanic of this game is fabulous. You level troops, customize each member of your army, and then assign them to squads. After the planning stage, take your army to a field of specialized chess board squares and spam your intelligence and superpowers. My first playthrough took a long time and contained a lot of strategic and tactical problem solving but no challenge (on the hardest setting no permadeath). I see now that if I had played with permadeath the difficulty would have been more enjoyable. The risks are greater, and the losses would count more. However, I just don't want to play again. ...
The lame-o, cheese monster of a story was good when I was a 10 year old but, for me anymore, it is played out. There is no coolness or anticipation building, the toon on toon relationships don't matter to me at all, and I have no real feelings about my generals. It all fades quickly into the background and all you are left with is a very good core mechanic, repeated 30 or 35 times.
The AI Enemy is terrible and will fall for every one of your tricks and baits. It has only one order: charge at your weakest unit. This quickly becomes exploitable.
Despite the imperfections, you will get your money's worth out of this game figuring out how to break it. Enjoy your first playthrough as if it is your last, it likely will be.
/Salute to the Dev and the personable ending, this is an above average game. Please consider my criticism constructive.
I enjoyed my journey and recommend the trip.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a mix of Fire Emblem and the SNES game Ogre Battle. With a multitude of level up options for characters and army compositions there is a lot that keeps the game interesting from battle to battle. From early game archers having a range advantage to firearm units obliterating your frontline and assassins that attack your backline there's a lot that keeps it interesting for the 40 hours of gameplay. The story is solid though lacking a bit in fleshing out the characters. I really hope this company makes another game of this type. I can't recommend it enough if you enjoy turn based strategy games.\t\t\t\t\t
