\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDecent combat system, and a relatively fresh take on the rock-paper-scissors system of units. Unfortunately that's where the 'pros' of this title ends. The writing is dry, unimaginative, disconnected, and poorly thought out. The character dialogues and interactions are also equally poorly executed with interactions feeling forced, coming out of the blue, and with lackluster vocabulary. For a fire-emblem-alike, I was expecting a decent, cohesive, and well executed story, and sadly, the writing for this game misses all the marks.

If you're playing for combat alone, you'll be happy for a long enough time, though the mechanics can be pretty repetitive and don't feel terribly rewarding for unlocking and recruiting top-tier units (except raiders, those are fun every time). Overall, this game is a miss for me, but I do see how and why others like it - especially hardcore, super tacticians.\t\t\t\t\t
