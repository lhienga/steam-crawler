\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Buy it, it's a great tactical RPG with a great storyline.

One of the things I *strongly dislike* about the bigger franchise (I'm looking at you Fire Emblem) is that they have too much of a "classmate" feel and focus more on personal relationships than the overarching event. You know... The war?

This game still includes personal relationships, but the writing is more mature and makes it clear that "getting along" is optional and not mandatory. The fact one of the female heroes gets fed up with some of the advances of the male heroes is awesome (although a relationship chart shows it is still possible).

Another example is the inclusion of guns and cannons which ignore armor. Seeing a company of gunners walking up to your company of overpowered knights is scary since they easily kill most of them. But the reverse is also true since gunners are often much weaker. And planning to use your own gunners when it's raining can be scary as well since they suddenly become less effective.

While I can play Fire Emblem on normal difficulty without much worry, the same is not true for this game. On some maps, it looked as if it was impossible to get all the goals, and there were I had to restart because my tactic wasn't working. It's high praise to the creators that this game requires serious thought.

In most games I turn on permadeath, but in this game, I missed the option. This turned out to be a blessing since I have seen my heroes die more often than expected.

The world building is also great. The story is revealed through scenes that are just the right length. No extreme long dialogs that feel like flavour text here. One of the things I noticed is that I expect to hear a voice whenever I'm reading. Because that is how I recall every scene and that too is a testitomony of how vividly I can recall the story.

Overal this story delivers a better story, and a better gameplay than I would have expected.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a great game that's worth a purchase.

The game is a great return to the fire emblem / advance wars strategy genre. Completed a game a little longer (37 hours) than others because I liked the grind in the Arena and the menus (more on that later0. The story line is pretty basic but keeps the player engaged. The game play is pretty solid with combat and units feeling like they have impact. There is a need to use the players units well as the player is frequently outnumbered. The use of terrain also gives some great advantages (though it's not readily apparent unless one delves into the help / tutorial files).

On the captain difficulty the AI seems to make some pretty novice mistakes such as having melee squads shoot with their one ranged unit. AI composed squads also need some work with even the late game squads seemingly a hodgepodge of units. Furthermore there are squads in missions that do not move or do not move when every other squad moves making it easier to isolate and destroy in piecemeal.

The soundtrack is alright though could use some additional options. The voice acting is pretty good for a small studio as well.

The menus are an absolute mess. The amount of clicks to get through setting up squads and assigning items is just ridiculous. There are huge lists of items with no filters / no way to sort. There is no way to re-arrange squads so a player can keep their favorite squads up top so one does not have to scroll needlessly in the deploy screen. When placing artifacts the player is given the name of the squad leader but there is such a short list of names that there are frequent duplicates making it hard to quickly assign artifacts. The game would really benefit from a re-work of the menu and item system.

The Tech system is a bit opaque with no entry on the tutorial on how tech advances (it's through faction ranking up). Some more tool tips around battle and benefits of certain abilities would be helpful too. A player cannot at a glance see what's going to happen during battle. Damage ranges / percentages for surrender would be a great help.

Lastly, the market seems a bit anemic / the resource drops. It should always be an option to get resources for gold. I captured every mine / stable in the game and still ran short gems, horses, and iron until very late game. I had plenty of gold but nothing to spend it on because I didn't need recruits and the items in the store were not useful. The last store seems to be a better system where it's an infinite store.

All the negativity aside, this is a great game. It's worth the price and will be a fun romp.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm only about 3/4th through the story (most of my play time is due to leaving the game running while I did other things), and I really can't recommend this game. To be clear, it's not because it's bad, but because it doesn't ever exceed being anything other than just competent. If this were an original release on the SNES, it'd be fondly remembered, but as a recent release, it doesn't really do anything with the genre we haven't seen again and again. I was sold on this game when friends told me it offered a wealth of verity and complexity, when in reality, it's just a baserly competent entry into the tactics genre. The only real hook it had is permadeath, which FF Tactics had YEEEEAAAAARRRRSSS ago along with others. And the writing is hardcore middle of the road - we're talking middle of a Nebraskan road here - especially given clear modern sociopolitical influences, as well as revealing some rather "......" inducing kinks of the devs, making for pretty lackluster social commentary we, again, have seen before. If this simply offered regular exploration/travel rather than being a narrative on rails with dialog that comes via a menu screen, it'd likely be fully passable. But as is, it's just more of the same. If you're really in need of a lower mid tier story with tactics gameplay, it's worth checking out. But you're better off replaying something you already have that's likely better written.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game that had a chance to be an indie classic.

The gameplay is a cross between Ogre Battle's squad based combat and objective capturing, and Fire Emblem's grid based tactical movement. It's a blend I can't believe I haven't seen until now -- it's a natural fit and creates a gameplay loop that is both fun and strategically satisfying. Upgrades between missions feel meaningful, and I personally spent hours moving units around between my squads to maximize the number of characters I could have in each one.

The story is serviceable. There were more twists in this than in a typical Fire Emblem game, the characters (mostly) have personality, and killing the big bad was ultimately satisfying.

However, what this game needed more than anything is an editor. The writing is atrocious. You can understand what was happening, and the story *behind* the writing is engaging, but I kept getting tripped up by the writing. Characters tend to be long-winded while saying nothing of importance, while big sections of the story are glossed over just to get to the next fight sequence. Some of the lines characters will say are non-sensical. There will be sudden plot reveals that had NO foreshadowing. You will find yourself going, "Ok, that kind of makes sense I guess. Why wasn't that hinted at even a little bit?"

The gameplay, however, is so strong, and the characters interesting enough, that I still wanted to keep playing in spite of these issues. I really, REALLY hope they make another game, and hire a professional to edit the next script. Another story in the same vein, with some tight writing and an editing pass, and the same style of gameplay, perhaps with some more map variance in the endgame (i.e. -- make the terrain more interesting rather than just trying to overwhelm the players with numbers), will turn an 8/10 experience into a 10/10 masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t

I had fun playing this game and was pleasantly surprised by the depth of both the squad building strategy and the battle map tactics. After taking the time to S rank every mission (approx. 36 hrs) I can wholeheartedly recommend this game to any one looking for and Turn-Based Tactics SRPG to play.
