\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really enjoying this game. It takes a bit to take off and the story is more or less predictable (at least up to the chapter I'm on).
Where it shines, though, is in the tactical options and gameplay. The setup of your armies and squads is very customizable to your preferences and play style and it really impacts each fight. You feel you have options, even though you'll always have a lingering feeling that your army could be more optimized. Most chapters after the initial part of the game play out as multi-front battles, forcing you to split your forces and this creates tension and ramps up the difficulty a bit. It generally feels very good, as you'll be rewarded for sound tactical decisions and punished for rash and impulsive ones.

Overall, I recommend this game to any turn-based strategy fans, particularly those fond of Advance Wars, Fire Emblem and other similar games.\t\t\t\t\t
