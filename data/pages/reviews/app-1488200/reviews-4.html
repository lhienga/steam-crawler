\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI still think the story was a touch generic. I could kind of tell where most of the plot twists toward the end were going. One or two surprised me, but the game's story was at its peak toward the climax in the middle. I was on the edge of my seat for all of that.

Gameplay-wise it was awesome. I've only ever played a couple of Fire Emblem games, but fans of that series are going to adore this.

Aside from the rather generic storyline (in my opinion), the only other thing that felt a little weak were the social aspects. Friendships and romances developing between the characters were very brief and didn't really feel like they mattered save for a few words spoken in some cutscenes. It wasn't enough to make me dislike the time I spent with the game, but I was hoping for a little more.

Overall, loved this game. Might play the DLC one day!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf primarily interested in the game on a mechanical level (making and outfitting squads, minor amounts of research choices, unit positioning, etc) then I can recommend the game. This is especially true if you've been looking for something close to the old Ogre Battle games, although it is a turn based SRPG rather than the real time movement of armies in that series.

Unfortunately, where the game falls extremely short is in the story and writing. Grammatically, I have no issues. However, the story is very bland and standard fantasy fare, and feels like it is written at a grade school level. The relationship/friendship system is particularly laughable and just triggers in camp at certain times and has virtually no effect beyond a slight morale boost if the units are close.

With that said, overall I enjoyed my time with the game and did a couple of other partial playthroughs trying to use only stealth units, mage units, etc just for fun. Thankfully, the game does have the ability to skip through the "cutscenes" which is very good as the story frequently was making me roll my eyes. It also suffers from first attack syndrome that a lot of these SRPGs have where the initial attacker has a huge advantage over most defenders.

If you need a good story and characters rather than just enjoying fiddling with unit compositions stay away from this game.\t\t\t\t\t
