\r\n\t\t\t\t\tif you are an Ogre Battle/Fire Emblem fan, absolutely worth picking this up. Main highlight of this game is the squad based mechanics. They are very well done, and enjoyed tweaking squads, figuring out what worked best, and making them fit into different roles.

Only thing of note is that I hit the top tier units fairly early in most my squads, only had a few squads made near the endgame that were not fully at the highest tier I wanted.
Also never felt in danger of losing most the missions.

Did finish my play through on Captain difficulty and without character perma-death enabled, so that probably had an impact. Will be trying again with harder settings to see how that changes it.

Soundtrack as well is fairly solid.

That said, as great as the mechanics are the rest is ok at best.
Story was average. Don't find it as bad as several other reviewers, and it had a few very memorable moments, but overall isn't anything to rave about either.

large part of that might have been character dialogue, overall just wasn't that good to me. Seemed like a lot of conversations, decisions, and reveals were finished in a matter of sentences. Many of which felt they should have had more of a discussion or reaction from the characters when they happened.

It wasn't bad enough to get me to put the game down or kill my desire to replay it, but something that stuck out to me.

Overall, still worth picking up if this is your kind of game. Especially if you see it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I LOVE the actual game play of this game, everything else around it makes me dread playing it. The story is absolute garbage so I constantly skip every cut scene. The real nail in the coffin for me though is that the UI to manage your squad is atrocious. It was fine when I had to manage a squad of 8, but when you get to managing 12+ squads... my god does it make me question my sanity. Everything is done menu style, where you have to click the option then click the unit. And god forbid you want to do the same operation on another unit, because you guessed it, you have to click the same menu option again before you can do so. Now multiply that for your 12+ squads and you're taking 30+ minutes to just upgrade your guys and make edits to your squads.

Story: 0/10
Gameplay: 10/10
UI: 0/10

I would love to play a sequel where its simpler to mange your squads, because the concept of this game is brilliant and its a fun twist on FE type games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite all the shortcomings and limitations, this game did the class system and squad customization extremely well. All classes are unique and almost all are well balanced, mixed with impactful squad customization, there is just so much to experiment and play with. It's probably the best tactical rpg customization I've played. My only complain is that there could be so much more unique mechanic with classes that can be explored.

There are also issues with this game however, though, nothing too bad.

The last 1/3 of the game kinda falls off. A lot of battles are less meaningful and long. Progression slows, no more new artifacts/traits/classes. New unlocked powers has not much depth and unnecessary that I often forgot the powers exist. Maps and terrain stays the same. Also some classes are over reliant on tech tree.

The lack of coherence in art direction also bothers me. Art isn't bad, just feels like being made by two person with different art style. There should be a singular art direction on how the game characters, sprites, maps, and everything else should look like.\t\t\t\t\t
