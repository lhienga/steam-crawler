Absolutely great game! Was hooked from start to finish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game looks very appealing at first but quickly becomes too easy and too simple. The main pro / unique feature is upgrading your units and story. The main downside is the combat which makes up most playtime. Details below.

Initially, upgrading units is very fun. However, very quickly you have reach max level and so this appeal is lost. Moreover, I got into maxing 1 squad, my favorite, but several times I was not be able to play with them because of story events, which I didn't like because upgrading it was most appealing part of the game.

After the upgrading becomes less appealing, your are left with the story and combat. The combat is interesting at the start but quickly becomes extremely simple and trivial. The main reason is that the enemy AI does the exact some thing every mission every fight, which is attack your lowest strength unit in range. That's it. Enemies will run and bypass everything, just to attack that one unit. Most of the time this is a very bad move for them, so their overall difficulty is very low. Moreover, after you figured the AI out, it becomes trivial to play around the AI and win every match without any losses. Result: after several hours combat became a cakewalk, something to rush asap as it is trivial anyway. To make it even worse, the power level of individual squads very quickly becomes much higher than enemies, so they stomp them completely, further trivializing combat. Lastly, combat is also slow, especially in large battles because every unit's movement and actions are shows (can be reduced with options though).

After combat became trivial, all that was left was the story. The story is okay. It's a fairly generic JRPG story about saving the world. Nothing new. The characters do have some personality (generic RPG though) fleshed out in bonus conversations.

One more point, you can save scrum in this game to reset the items in the shop. This doesn't even require you to do anything "cheaty", just reload the game. Because it's so trivial to do, it felt almost like a game feature. I ended up scrumming to find fun / useful shop items to spend all my money on because it felt boring to have no useful items / units to buy since I like the upgrading part the most.

Overall, I do not recommend getting this game because it lacks in depth. The combat is supposed to be the main appeal of this game but it quickly goes from interesting, to trivial, to boring, to a grind, to let's just play another game. 4.5/10 overall. If you like upgrading, perhaps play Hades or Slay the Spire, both substantially better games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's pretty good!

Think of it as a poor man's Fire Emblem crossed with Warsong and Ogre Battle, whereas the "poor man's" descriptor isn't meant in a derogatory way towards the dev team. You have squards which are formed by a leader unit and some follower minions. At first I didn't realize only promoted classes can become leaders, because as far as I know the game doesn't outright tell you this limitation. So I was trying to form a new squad consisting of only basic archers and nothing was happening. Squad composition is a lot of fun.

You move the squads around like in Fire Emblem, then once you get into a skirmish it turns into an auto-battler like the old Ogre Battle games. The battles are definitely the best part and are enjoyable. The cutscenes reek of RPG Maker and once you see it you can't really unsee it. Not the worst thing in the world but heads up regardless.

I will say that I think a major part of basically all games like this that I don't really care for is the generic heavy geo-political storylines. Just once I'd like for the story to either be 1) more lighthearted or 2) severely streamlined. If you've ever played Triangle Strategy for example that game takes the dialogue to the limits of human bearability! It'd be nice to have another tactics game more in line with something like PS1's Vandal Hearts- Just a small scene of set up before the battle, then straight into the fun.

I waited until it was on sale since I didn't think the normal price tag was worth it, but I'm pretty happy with the game and will likely play it some more when I get sick of Elden Ring again haha..\t\t\t\t\t

Symphony of War

Completion Time: 37 Hours
Verdict: Highly Recommended

Symphony of War has taken the phrase "battles are won or lost before they are fought" and incorporated that theory into a game. The core of the game is that you set up your army, buy and train troops, research technologies, provide buffs and artifacts and then command them on the battlefield. While you move your troops on the battlefield, combat itself is automated and you watch the results. That might sound boring but it's actually removing the boredom from the game - fighting these battles wouldn't be enjoyable and there's too many of them anyway. As the commander, you're concentrating on the bigger picture - setup, movement and overall troop management - while your units do the fighting.

Gameplay

There's enough here for the armchair commander to enjoy. The real important aspects to combat are your formations (which you set in between battles), unit composition (there are about 10 core unit types but they can be upgraded in various ways, resulting in about 35 or so distinct units) and buffing your various squads with traits and artifacts you find on the battlefield. It's the preparation for war that matters in Symphony just like it does in real life.

While you're preparing for that war, the story of 30 Chapters is told in cute Nintendo-style graphics that I quite liked. The story itself is standard RPG fare, starting with combat and political intrigue and then delving into the supernatural. There are twists and turns along the way and the game keeps things interesting and moving along. There's one or two quite amazing twists in the story. There are occasionally some one-on-one conversations between the major characters in the story and even a rudimentary love interest. These are less well done and while they help fill out a bit more about some of your commanders and party members, they feel just a bit tacked on and I'd like some real choice and consequences the next time around.

And while you're advancing the story in camp, you're also buying, buffing, upgrading and positioning your troops. Because again, these battles are won or lost before they're fought.

Once you get to the combat, the graphics are functional but far less attractive. The screenshots are pretty representative of the actual combat maps, which look like the old SNES (pre-N64) graphics. They're not entirely unattractive but I'd like to see some sort of visual upgrade here. Combat maps are flat and the enemy is generally hurling units at you - with both the maps and the AI also needing a bit more work in a future title. There are choke points like bridges to use but there could have been some more terrain variety to spice things up even more. The battles aren't random as each one is set up as a set-piece and you have to figure out how to defeat the enemy whether you're on offense or defense (and there's a good mix of both here).

Graphics, UI and Sound

While the AI, terrain and general graphics could all be better, I want to convey how much fun I had playing these battles. They were an absolute blast to manage. They went by quickly - with rarely a battle lasting more than a dozen turns - but trying to position my troops and watching the animated battles play out between squads was great fun. The devs really focused on making this a faster-paced TBS and they truly succeeded.There's really no drudgery here like in other games where you might have to spend time wiping out the enemy long after the battle has already been won or looking for that last unit. It feels fun, fresh and fast, positioning the right squads to take out just the right enemy squads.

Music is fine and typically bombastic of a game of this type. It didn't get annoying but it's not going to make any kind of playlist either. There are no voice overs here so be prepared to read the dialog (which can be skipped if you want).There are a few voices in the game but they're not really notable with one exception that I truly didn't like.

The UI took me a bit to get used to. I'm not a console player and I was really put off by it. But I stuck with it and while it still feels clunky I'm glad I did. While the UI did get cumbersome when I was juggling 15+ squads after a while, it was never that big of a problem. It feels a bit like a console interface but I think that's what they were going for. I'd like some tool-tips and a bit more player assistance. I won't give the UI any kind of high marks but it's functional.

I didn't encounter any bugs in this game. Great job, devs.

Conclusion

People compare this game to something called "Fire Emblem". I never played that - as I said, I'm not a console gamer. But I am a strategy gamer. I've been playing strategy games since the days of Avalon Hill board games and Civilization 1. This feels fresh, interesting and fun and I'm having a blast playing it and that's not easy to do for me nowadays. While I love games like Civ, XCOM, MOO or EUIV and look forward to every iteration of those games, it's rare that a new strategy title feels fresh. This one does and I had a great time playing it.

As for a replay, I'll do that sometime soon. There's only much this title is going to offer during a replay as the story, maps and units are going to be the same. But there's another difficulty level to try or just seeing how much better I've gotten would be interesting because just playing it was fun. If you're a turn-based strategy fan this one belongs in your library.

Recommendation: Highly Recommended for Turn-Based Strategy Fans at full price\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a very solid game that centers on the resurrection of squad units from the classic Ogre Battle games. Each squad has up to 9 units on a 3x3 grid, mix and match unit types for best results and upgrade to fun classes. Battle movement is more like Fire Emblem and other standard TRPGs. Most of the missions feel well balanced and enemy strength kept up with my army's strength. It's pretty fun to roll out a squad of three dragon riders and burn up a couple squads of light armor squads only to have a squad of knights and pikemen wipe them out on the next turn because I overextended. The game definitely rewards good tactics and understanding of unit strengths vs weaknesses.

Plot is pretty interesting, a few twists and turns as the story develops. It dabbles a little bit in deeper issues of war and violence but doesn't push too far into these themes. Perhaps this is because the actual writing is a little spotty and the main character in particular maintains a strangely flippant tone through a lot of the travails. Most of the characters are well-drawn and memorable enough. Between missions there are "conversations" between characters and some of them can form romantic relationships with each other, though most of these relationships happen fairly abruptly and don't seem to affect gameplay. But even in the weakest moments of writing I was left more amused then annoyed as I skipped on to my next 20 minute session of unit micromanagement in between missions.\t\t\t\t\t
