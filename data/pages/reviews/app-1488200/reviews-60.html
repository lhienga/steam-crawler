\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended with reservations. If you like Fire Emblem, you might like this too

Pros:

- You manage armies rather than individual units, adding an interesting layer of strategy
- Good unit variety, though the lack of any axe users is a bit weird to me
- Good amount of content. I'm not sure about replayability, but there's enough in one playthrough to justify the price

Cons:

- The story isn't bad per se, but it's certainly not the reason you play the game. However, my biggest issue was that it often forces you to lose control of your squads, which can be very frustrating
- The squad mechanics and sheer number of units you get can make managing everything very time-consuming or overwhelming
- XP scaling felt a little unbalanced\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLong story short, it's okay. I've gotten my money's worth and I would recommend buying it on sale. The story isn't the greatest. Overall, it is not a difficult game. In fact, even on the hardest difficulty, it is not hard at all unless you are playing with permadeath and/or going for only S ranks. Without permadeath, S ranks are still pretty easy.

It is a very linear game. The squad system is okay but you pretty quickly realize which squads are worth making so there isn't much to it. The formations also don't really matter because your squad is always facing front in battle so unless you are facing assassins, your front line guys always need to be tanks and that's about it. The game would be more complex with directional attacks added in. I also found the grand strategy layer to be lacking. There is no choices so the game is pretty much linear reducing replayability. I'd really like if the map layer actually let me make strategic decisions.

Overall, I'd still recommend it. It's a good first game. They can and definitely should make a lot of improvements for any sequels though.\t\t\t\t\t
