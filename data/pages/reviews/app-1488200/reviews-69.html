\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got the game a week ago and blazed through it by sinking 35 hours in the past week. I've rarely done that with games, I'll be honest. Its hard doing that working full time jobs and having a social life lol.

The game is great, for sure.
But it's not without some glaring issues.

WARNING - Spoilers ahead!

- The whole appeal is the auto-battle with the customized squad you create and set up. Its incredibly fun. To see a squad you hand crafted win tough battles and survive till the end of the chapter, especially in the beginning 10 chapters or the game where it felt like there was so much you didn't know or understand of the game and what it had to offer.

- I thoroughly enjoyed the campaign when it was outdoors, when you'd go from one fort to another fort and there'd be like 20 battalions of enemy troops coming at you and it felt like an all out war. The feeling of pitting your hand crafted customised army against the enemy's in such a large scale feeling battle was amazing.
I did NOT like it when it was having those indoor battles. I didn't hate it. But it kind of took away from the fun side of this game. The feeling of war was lost in those chapters.

- Talking about the story itself, its whatever. The characters are EH and its a very generic setting. Its not gonna win any awards for the story. But I guess it helps set up some plot points. It helped keep the chapters going which I didn't mind, I guess.

- It was also weird seeing the weird titan form of one of the characters. It just looked so out of place with the rest of the character designs. Even some of the regular female character designs were kinda over the top. Like, the people who designed some of the women designs definitely did it with only one hand and then realised they can't use those designs and decided to draw tight clothes on them.
For example - The character Diana. Her body shape is what you'd see in anime/manga like One Piece.

That being said, I don't mind the muscular women tho. Warriors have muscles. It's not unrealistic or anything.

- The dialogues were corny/cheesy/amateurish at best. But eh. Not really something that bothered me too much. Some of the dialogues could've been cut but there's a "Skip all" button, which is great for those who cannot deal with it.

- I think the game is at its strength for the first 20-23 chapters. The last 7-10 chapters were its weakest. The first 10-12 chapters were its strongest I think.

- And most importantly, the final 2-4 chapters were bland AF.
I was hoping the game would've gone a whole DIFFERENT direction. I wanted the last few chapters and battles to be monumentally larger in scale compared to previous chapters. But instead, the second to last chapter was so weak because it was just you controlling 4 individual characters, not even 4 squads or armies. Just plain single characters.
And despite that, I felt the whole final battle with the main boss in the following chapter to be even weaker.
It felt like the game was leading up to bigger and bigger battles only for it deflate so hard at the end there.

- I was HOPING there would be more classes and upgrades possible but I was kinda surprised at how limited that was. But seems like the game designed with that limit in mind. So, it works. But it kinda feels like there was potential for like at least the double the class types/upgrades.
But like for a fantasy setting, they have dragons and that's it? When I first saw a dragon in the game, I was pumped because I thought I'll see more creatures. But no. That was it. Just dragons.


ANYWAY...

Why did I say I rarely play games for 35 hours in a single weak and then complain so much about this game? Because I still loved the experience for like 95% of my time with it. The last 3-4 chapters I played today were the weakest.
I also think the devs have the ability to make a perfect game next time. They have all the ingredients they need. The foundation is incredibly solid. The ideas are on point. They just need to to understand what the strengths are of such a game and capitalise on it better by offering more customisation, characters, upgrades, larger scale battles (especially towards the end), a more unique story and better dialogues.

Right now this game is a solid 7.5/10. Honestly, if it was 2 days ago, I would've rated it a 9/10 or higher. But the last 2-4 chapters brought it down for sure. I felt the game needed to end on a much grander scale of a note than how it ended. It felt very anticlimatic after all those battles.
Just cannot shake the feeling that the ending was weak AF and that this could've gone a whole different ending direction.

But if you enjoyed games with autobattles before, you might definitely enjoy this one.

Support the devs. They have potential to make the next game, in this genre, ground breaking.
I hope they learn from this and push it forward and expand massively in every possible.\t\t\t\t\t
