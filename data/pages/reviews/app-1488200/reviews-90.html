\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSymphony of War in an enjoyable turn based strategy game with some unique squad mechanics that mix up the formula a little.

The idea of squads works really well and allows you to fix the weaknesses of some units by pairing them with others. For example you can have your mages with a frontline of foot soldiers. However there are certainly some squads that are better than others. Archers always felt a little weak and putting a healer in every squad seemed like a no brainer.

Other than the fun squad mechanics this game plays in a similar way to games like fire emblem or advanced wars. You get missions and the units that fight level up and grow stronger. You can choose permadeath for your units too which makes you play more carefully and adds another layer to the gameplay. I did notice that enemies always seem to attack my units with the lowest threat rating which could be exploited at times.

I had a few issues with the design of the game. The menus while mostly intuitive and with plenty of tutorials had a few issues. It took me way too long to realise that the leadership of your leader unit in a squad determined the capacity of that squad. Initially I also felt that the portraits of the characters clashed with the rest of the art style of the game but I got used to that. There was also the occasional tile mostly on the backgrounds in the cutscenes that looked out of place.

The story was mostly pretty generic but was not as forced as recent fire emblem games feel and you can skip the cutscenes if you want. I found myself skipping a lot of the support conversations where the characters become friends because they didn’t interest me.

Overall this game was really enjoyable. For the most part I like the art style. The gameplay is solid and the squad mechanic is really fun. I spent a lot of time customising my squads. Yet I haven’t finished the game. I’ll probably come back to it at some point but after 20 hours the gameplay began to feel a little repetitive and my squads felt a little too strong even with permadeath on captain difficulty.

I’d completely recommend the game to anyone who likes a turn based strategy game and will most likely come back to finish it. Either way it was definitely worth the price for what I have played.\t\t\t\t\t

Did Diana Just Stomp on a Dragon and Won?

Mommy Giantess War Simulator issssssss really fun!
This is coming from a casual turn based strategy player. I've only played like XCOM EU, Into the Breach, Invisible Inc., Onimusha Tactics. I've also completed the game on the default difficulty. So you know, not the most credible opinion here.
But yeah the game got 80+ hours out of me, so it must've done something right.

Game is pretty, beautiful character portrait illustrations during the dialogue scenes, well made squad unit pixel art and animations during the battlefield section. It's basically a lost Game Boy Advance game that I should've played many years ago if it was one and I love GBA games, man.

As a turn based strategy game, it is addictive enough that you want to keep playing because there's good amount of toys and tools to tinker with when you're setting up your squads for the next battle, and the game is not too complex to the point of analysis paralysis.
I would turn the game on just to move items, units and artifacts around for like 30 minutes, and it's time well spent.

The main section which is the battles is pretty good. It's almost puzzle-like akin to vanilla Into the Breach except with healthy amount of RNG. Some chapters are just set piece scenes not meant to challenge players' skill, and I'm okay with that. They're not too intrusive to the overall experience.
Most of the mechanics are useful here. The Shuffle ability and the Powers you'll get later, you gonna need to use these if you want them S ranks for every chapter.

I don't know how flexible the strategy and players' squads setup can be for this game, but once you figure out what each chapter wants from you, it'll be a smooth sailing from there.

The story is fine. The characters're alrite. The MC has a bit of sass. The conversations can be funny at times. The worldbuilding is efficient for this kind of game. Not thoroughly impressed, but overall I'm entertained by the narratives we got here.

Negatives?

The later chapters, the way I played them anyway took me two hours to complete each one. That's on me, I gotta say.

Had a crash once. Didn't know what triggered it. Restarted the game and it's back on track.

I ignored the terrain and weather conditions throughout my playthrough. I don't really fiddle with units' elemental affinity. Maybe these matters more on the highest difficulty?

Conclusion?

Bring Barnabas to every battle if you can.
There's a quicksave function for your convenience.
Diana is best woman.
The fine lady carried the game in every sense of the word. Like DAMN.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked this game up on sale for $15 cause of the overwhelmingly positive reviews, so this game really doesn't need another good review. But let me try to put into words what makes this game stand out anyway. I have been a big fan of tactical RPG's since I was young and played the first advance wars game on my gameboy. I played games like Fire Emblem and Final Fantasy Tactics and XCOM. Back then there were so many to choose from. Now it seems like the new fire emblems blow everything else out of the water. I never find myself craving to scratch my tactical RPG metaphorical "itch" with anything other than fire emblem these days. The sheer depth and production quality put into the new fire emblem games set them apart from anything else being released recently. And while this cheap indie game that cost me 15 dollars most certainly doesn't have the level of production quality or budget that fire emblem has, it somehow has more depth in the management elements of the game that make it comparable to fire emblem still.

The standard in most games is for each "unit" to be 1 whole recruitable entity right? Well in this game, the unit is capable of holding up to 9 recruitable entities who all attack at once when that squad attacks on the tactical grid map. You can mix and match almost any kind of squad. Want a unit with a front line made of heavy armor, a middle line made of archers and a healer, and a back line made of assassins specializing in attacking the back line of the enemy unit so they can bypass units that have strong front lines? You can do that! Archers can retaliate at close range but are still basically like paper in a melee fight. Still, archers being able to fight back in a melee counter attack feels good. You can put 1 healer in a squad and he gets to cast a heal every attack on whichever of your 9 units is lowest health at the end of the round. I spent so much time swapping out my units from squad to squad trying to maximize potential while obeying the limits that your squad has due to the "squad leader's leadership" stat.

If you go into this game expecting one of the newer fire emblems, you are going to be disappointed. If you go into this expecting advanced wars or fire emblem from 10 or 15 years ago, you will be pleasantly surprised. It feels like it is only half of what my favorite fire emblem game offers, but at the same time I also paid less than half of a fire emblem price for this little indie gem. So I am still extremely satisfied. I hope this helps!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSymphony of war is an immersive 100 experience! It really makes you feel like you are in a symphony of war.

Tactical strategy -> definitely can get your brain juices pumping.
Story -> acceptable
Character dating sim -> well, not really here for it, but it was there I guess...

But the real reason I was hooked on this game was the direct and straight forward preemptive strategy component of the game, or the REAL game as I thought of it. That being SQUAD COMPOSITION. This was where I spent most of my time in the game. I had a great time playing Warlord difficulty and making a 3 sentinel squad with artifacts that granted extra ranged damage reduction and the everlasting potion (free healing). This led to what felt like an innovation in war being the decoy squad which could usually be used to hide squads that would have died if they were attacked by the enemy units but instead the decoy squad would take the blow and leave the battle with but a scratch. For this reason, I really liked this game.

Some actual critiques of the game were that I didn't exactly know how damage worked and I didn't know if damage% reductions for specific types of damage happened before or after the armor reduction to the damage. I think more clarity on how the mechanics of the game worked would have improved my experience. This games squad formation reminded me of one of my favorite parts of Star Sector, which is one of my favorite games of all time.

Thus, this game gets a 10/10 from IGN because there is a little something for everyone.\t\t\t\t\t

Cue: Wagner's Ride of the Valkyries!

For someone who cut their teeth on games like Shining Force, Ogre Battle, Advance Wars, and Dragonforce, and spent a fair amount of time in various RPG Maker systems: this game is a dream come true! The 2D sprites and animations are masterpieces. Organizing my units and watching them battle the enemy is gleefully fun, as is watching my units grow and earn promotions. This is digital tin soldiers at its best!

The story is serviceable, as is the writing in general. Everything else is top-notch--from the visuals and audio, down to the interface--this game is a joy to play.

Full disclosure: I'm playing on the standard difficulty with permadeath (for non-essential characters) disabled, so I'm finding the game fairly easy. In fact, I'm starting to steamroll the AI. But that's fine by me for my first playthrough. Hardcore strategists and veterans will probably want to increase the difficulty and/or enable permadeath, or take on one of the challenges suggested in the discussions.

As a long-time JRPG and SRPG geek, I'm so very happy I discovered this game! 13 hours have flown by. My only complaint so far is that at some point, this experience is going to end.\t\t\t\t\t
