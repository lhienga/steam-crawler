\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts kinda like Shining Force but with squads. Each hero on the board has a bunch of other troops with him that attack with him. And depending on what tropps you assign it has completely different effects and purposes on the battle field. If that makes sense.. I had a lot of fun with this game. Some reviews I watched priior to purchasing it said the story wasn't good, but I disagree with that. The story was pretty solid and it wasn't some lame anime writing that only teenagers would enjoy. The way the story unfolds is kinda like a visual novel, and I know that rubs some the wrong way but it was fine. Combat is why you play this though and its grand. Also the customization which I can't say enough about. If you enjoy fine tunning an army with focus on every little detail then this will have you hooked. I spent as much time bulding my army as I spent fighting with it, and for me thats a good thing. If that is not for you, its not neccesary. But just know that this game lets you fine tune a lot of things to your own liking. Army composition, leveling up every troop in every squad, assigning each individual unit special abilities and artifacts. Changing classes. Horse and Dragon mounts etc etc etc. The game is chalenging if you cracnk up the difficulty and easy on the lighter difficulties as it should be. I enjoyed most of the stages but Im not a super duper fire emblem player so I may not be the best one to judge if all the maps were properly balanced and all that. Read some one elses review if you want that kind of info. I loved it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an interesting Tactical RPG, with a lot of the same peaks and the same valleys. I came into it being a big fan of Fire Emblem, but I haven't played a ton of games in the genre outside of it.

The squad building is by far SoW's greatest feature, with vast amounts of customization to let you build unique squads to support the main unit in it, whether it be a story-important character or a mercenary that you can hire from each chapter's base section (If you're familiar with Fire Emblem, think of Path of Radiance and Radiant Dawn's Base system). This mechanic does take some getting used to, as you can't really get a feel as for what's a good composition just by feel, and just looking at the 'threat' number only gets you so far. The in-game encyclopedia is good, but I feel that it could be a little more comprehensive with giving straightforward tips on what compositions are good to help the player along.
The active part of the gameplay is top-notch. The maps are reminiscent of the best of Fire Emblem to me, Thracia 776. I certainly recommend this game just based on the core gameplay loop of going to your base, setting up your squads, and clearing out a map. It's quite engaging.

The story is about par for the course. My main gripe is that the dialogue seems to shift between formal and informal conversations too quickly. Obviously, realistically we speak differently in different settings with different people, but something just felt off with the informal tone of some dialogue from regularly serious characters. On the broader story, it feels like copied homework from FE4 and FE5, but those games have the best stories of the series so I can't blame the devs if that is what inspired them. The only problem is that those games gave a lot more room for their characters to express themselves. There are only a few support conversations per character in SoW and almost no in-map conversations, and neither are anything special. The good thing is that none of the characters are as egregiously one-note as those in later fire emblem titles, but they don't bring much to the table.

The unit spritework is really impressive, especially the firearm and cavalry units. The character portraits... not so much. It could just be a personal issue, but the faces look too smooth to me. I honestly prefer the classic portraits, which thankfully are still included via an option in the settings.

All in all, I recommend the game to anybody who likes tactical rpgs, it's a good addition to your collection at an affordable price. Just don't expect it to blow your socks off with a unique story or particularly rich characters. The gameplay is (rightfully) the main draw here.\t\t\t\t\t

This is one of the few that break the mold for me. I'm not sure why, I but the gameplay is fun and it's not just pure "move unit, attack/wait" as there's a bit more to it but not enough to turn it into another genre entirely.
The story was acceptable, I'm a hard one to please in that regard but it certainly didn't feel bad. There's a few things I would of liked to see more but overall it also adds a degree of mystery.

I won't bore you with much more. I think it's a great game and if you like what you see and read, give it a go.
