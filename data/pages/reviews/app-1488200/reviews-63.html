Does the game deserve a negative review? no.

I enjoy playing the game, however a bunch of nitpicking comes to mind.

1) The tutorial notes are not easily accessible to me, I can't seem to interact with them to read the rest of the entry.

2) Needs a Quality of Life Offensive off-shoot of their stat screen where you can see the secondary attacks and requirements/effects thereof.

3) Basic (and I do mean basic as in medic, bowman, skirmisher, and fighter) recruits need a refresh button (same for apprentice when you get the proper tech upgrade).

4) Dragons upon gaining loyalty should have their cost reduced by 5 per loyalty grade (at max loyalty equal to the 5 that nibbler is at) this would balance out the eventual cost to power that they give for taking up two character slots.

5) Arena visits should have a request to increase the number of opponents for an increase in the number of allied squads participating (increase should be by grade; bronze 1 ally for 1 enemy, silver 1 A for 2 E, Gold 1/3, Plat 1/4 and capped at 5 additional allies).

6) Needs an in-mission academy a unit could stop at to be trained in specific traits in exchange for them being locked out of use for the rest of the mission. (primarily looking at bowmanship and other things that are very unit specific but still rare in books). It would help if they could also be used to raise the stats of units to some kind of minimum for a given class at maybe 20+level for their primary stat alone. (it kind of sucks that my chosen level 21 apprentice would need at minimum some 20 more odd levels before they could have enough magic to class up)

7) Mastering a class gives a +1 to stat. Mastering 5+ gives a third trait slot. Or something like that.

8) A fourth tier for magicians that lets them attack with their element of affinity after mastering all three basic elements.

9) Class or weapon choice for the MC.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a fair way into this game so I feel I can offer an opinion on what it's like for those that might be on the fence.

I grew up playing these types of games, from the OG pokemon games to Shining Force 2 to FF7. Symphony of War is a really solid tactical turn-based RPG in the vein of those mentioned, built on a solid combat system with good unit variation. I'm especially into the unit upgrade system -- which I recall fondly from SF2 -- since it probably allows you to play through the game a couple of times and try out completely different unit formations and strategies. For instance, I'm moving through the game with these uber archer hit squads, but next time I might try out gunners and/or artillery.

If I had to critique the game I would say that the combat->story->town->combat->story->town... repetition gets a little stale. It's good if you want to lock in a 'chapter' or two every session, but if you want to grind away for a prolonged period you might get a little 'bored'. I think the games I mentioned earlier tackled this by allowing you to free roam around the world and discover things (and combat) at your own pace. My personal opinion is that this game would be even cooler if that was part of the game system.

Having said all this I'm still very happy to be playing this game. I'm 18 hours in at time of writing and am doing a few chapters per session and having a blast. The story is just interesting enough to keep me reading, and the combat and surrounding mechanisms (squads, unit upgrades, artifacts, items.. etc) are really fun. I'm not an expert on the genre, but if you like any of the games I listed above you'll dig this.\t\t\t\t\t
