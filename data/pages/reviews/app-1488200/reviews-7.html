\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a cute retro turn-based strategy game with a lot of polish in a lot of places. While it wasn't exactly what I expected (I expected more of a HoMM clone), I think it is exactly what it is trying to be. It's a love letter to anime as much as it is to those old strategy games, with a little bit of a JRPG flavor to tie them together (e.g., the "bond" mechanic).

It took me about 55 hours to beat the game on normal difficulty, and I dragged my heels. This is a game that lets you take your time and build things up in a way that pays dividends for the rest of the game.

The graphics are cute and genre appropriate. The music didn't get irritating. The gameplay is mostly very smooth and polished. I experienced no crashes.

If there's anything to criticize, it's that the game is a little bit heavy-handed with removing the player's agency. Your character in the game is going to do some stuff that'll make you say, "What? No!" And the story is completely linear. Over the course of the entire game, I think I had one opportunity to make a minor decision (will character A bond with character B or character C) that has minimal effect, only (presumably) changing some end-game text. I would have liked to see some branching options, though I understand that sort of thing can be difficult to implement. But something along the lines of "do you want to approach the castle through the lowlands or through the mountain pass?" where each path provides a unique artifact or unit to carry forward would add a lot.

Along that same line, I wish the story respected gameplay actions a little more. I carefully forced that guy to surrender because I wanted to show mercy? Nope, you find out in the cutscene that you killed him. You absolutely brutally overkill that one guy? Nope, he's still alive. (At least twice. The same guy.) I think if a game is going to do stuff like that, it should set the mission completion at "bad guy X goes below 10% of his health" so the cutscene doesn't contradict what just happened in the game.

The game balance seems pretty good though, as mentioned, I played this very methodically leading to almost everything I did being OP. Which is exactly what I wanted; that's not at all a complaint. Most of the units are useful for something, though some units are pretty good at nearly everything. (An "Oops all Valkyries!" playthrough might be pretty fun with Wagner playing in the background.)

All in all, this game does what it says on the tin. If that's what you want, you're going to have a great time.

Most of things listed is off the top of my head but I would only recommend getting this game on a sale or just playing any Fire Emblem (gba and beyond) instead.
