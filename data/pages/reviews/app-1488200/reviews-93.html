\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe biggest flaw in this game is that it doesn't last any longer.

This game is clearly a love letter to Fire Emblem and Ogre Battle and it shows. There's a lot of variety in what strategies you can play and it allows for very satisfying "self-imposed challenges". Characters are quite likable and the art hits just right, if your into older generation art style, but with modern quality.
For those who like the genre, I couldn't recommend this game more.

That said, if you're a seasoned player, the game might feel a little too easy, even in the hardest possible settings.
I also wish this game would have more replayability. Once you finish it, there's pretty much nothing else to do, unless you start all over and try to experiment different things for yourself, just for fun. There's no "extra-hard content" for you to go against with your end-game squads, no significant side-quest to unlock some particularly cool character or anything like that. Not saying there's no side-quests or optional characters (there are), but it barely meets the expectations if compared to the most obvious titles in the genre. Of course, this is all understandable if you consider the size of the development team and the price tag.

Overall, it's definitely worth checking this game out and getting some solid hours of fun out of it. It might not be an overstatement to say that, if given more resources, this could have been a major reference in the genre and, maybe, a whole franchise.\t\t\t\t\t

Great game. Keeps me wanting to come back
to min-max my squads or though I will admit I spent about an hour reading positive and a lot of negative reviews to get an idea of which problems the game might have.

My impression before buying and playing was:
* The story doesn't pick up until chapter 12.

* The combat becomes stale quickly, as you don't have much impact on it outside of positioning your squads.

* The music is solid.

* There is an extreme number of clicks required to go from aspect to another.

* The art can be hit or miss depending on the person (as with everything).

* The game is too easy which also contributes to the combat becoming boring.

* The Arena is shallow and just a grind.
____________________________________________________________

Now after playing it for 27 hours I can safely say all the above are not entirely wrong. The game has room to improve just like every negative review pointed out.

However here is my Impression after putting 27 hours in:
* The story is predictable and relatively simple until chapter 12. There are however room for different interpretations, meaning you won't know for certain how things turn out, but you probably have a few different ideas for how the story will proceed.

* Great pacing for both story and gameplay throughout the entire game (added in later after finishing the game).

* The music is solid.

* You do require a lot of clicks to navigate menus. (This did not bother me, but it was noticeable).

* Gameplay over art in my book. The art is fine for me.

* The game is designed for you to win. I chose the hardest difficulty with perma death enabled, since enough people mentioned the game being too easy. It has not been overwhelmingly hard but perma death is something I highly recommend, as resources can become a game on its own if you are careless with units.

* The min-maxing in between combat was possibly my greatest joy. Setting up squads and upgrading units praying they wouldn't randomly get 1 shot by a lucky crit or an oversight on my part. Adding gear, traits, stat ups and more felt meaningful and enjoyable on the hardest difficulty with perma death.

* The arena is a limited resource that can easily bring you back after losing 1 or more squads as it rewards a lot, assuming you win with an S grade.

* Perma death is not entirely perma death. Any unit you recruit or obtain that is not tied to the story can perma die, however anything associated with the plot of the story cannot die, even on perma death. You can abuse this or though some extra goals for certain chapters prevent you from abusing this and in general it feels a bit cheap you can even have units survive on perma death that are not your own main character.
______________________________________________________________

I better get back to the game D: there are more chapters to get through and I have a couple of golden dragons to take care of.

Hope this review helps you make your decision and as always, I recommend reading more reviews and forming your own opinion before buying the game.

Man go to war. Man fight war. War beat man. Man fight harder. Man win with the power of love and friendship.

Gameplay isn't to be expected to be Fire Emblem due to your personal power scaling- You can quite literally solo the game with one squad and be done with it if that's what you want, but it'd be tedious. Your squads get pretty powerful about halfway in so endgame just becomes immensely easy.
