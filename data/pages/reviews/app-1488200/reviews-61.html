\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFire Emblem has a love child with Advanced Wars. Story is generic, but the gameplay and missions are decently planned and thought out. You're rewarded for playing well, and you can't just turtle and wait for the enemies to impale themselves upon your weapons. I do wish there was a more consistent grinding mechanic than arenas, but that's just my preference of game pacing and is well balanced even if you can't get arena tokens. There are some units that could use buffs, and without knowing the damage calcs its hard to know just how well a unit can be utilized. The scale and plot gives a great sense of accomplishment and progress, since you're always feeling like the underdog fighting an uphill battle. Large class variety means you can implement a variety of strategies for different missions. My only main gripe is that ambush is difficult to use early-mid game, since it requires you to start a turn already on an ambush tile, have combat on an ambush tile, and have the enemy adjacent to one (excluding ranged attacks).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like tactics games or turn based strategy, the core of this game is good enough on its own to make this a worthwhile purchase. The game is super fun and offers a ton of customization in both units and squads/armies via a research tree, items, and different abilities. There are also a large amount of levels. The main problem with the game is the story. The game's story is bland and has no presence. You can almost completely ignore it since it has such a small amount of impact on actual game-play. The same goes for the characters. There are one or two characters who are interesting and fleshed out, however, the majority are easily forgotten and have little impact. There is a relationship system for the characters but it is incredibly bare bones. I can almost guarantee it was only added because it is now common in these types of games (ie: Fire Emblem).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a mixed bag. Thumbs up and thumbs down is kind of limiting for a review system. In truth this would get an "undecided" or a "not great not terrible" grading from me. The graphics are really cute. The battle system is solid, deep and fair. The rpg elements are rich and there are plenty of classes/subclasses to experiment with. It's similar to the job system of FF tactics plus a lot more teachable traits to each unit and synergies for each squad. Sound is kind of indifferent. So while it does everything right my main problem with this game is the gameplay loop. You spawn your units in the numerous maps and just capture the enemy fort, or survive for a given amount of turns or something and there is no sense of progression. While you get a lot of sense of progression on the rpg side of things, where you get levels and unlock class tiers and a tech tree and all this cool stuff, you have absolutely no clue how far you are in the game. I have played 28 hours I am on chapter 20 or something and I have absolutely no clue if I am in the beginning of the game or just a few hours before finishing the story. I believe a strategic map with continents you conquer or liberate would help greatly in getting a sense of progression relevant to the story.

On the other hand if you care about endless grinding and making big numbers even bigger in a JRPG style game, this is perfect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOther's have already said this, but if you're a fan of Fire Emblem-like games then you'll probably like this.

The game-play itself is very fun, even if some of the larger battles can start to get tedious with how many enemies there are. This is offset by the fact that, a lot of the time, if you're not in an enemy's attack range they're not going to both you. I never really encountered a situation where I wasn't equipped to deal with what I was facing, despite not having a great grasp on the strategy of squad-building. If anything the biggest complaint I had game-play wise is that the game felt too short.

The story, on the other hand, isn't bad but I wouldn't call it great either. It's definitely serviceable but I feel like it also suffers from the shortness of the game, where things either seem to happen too quickly, or are not sufficiently led up to when they do happen. There are some legitimately interesting twists, but I don't think there are a lot of them that you're not going to see coming. I also feel like the relationship system didn't make a whole lot of sense to me and I would have liked to see more inter-character scenes.

Still, a very solid game and one that I do recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTldr; don't get if you want to build your army on your own terms. This is not the strategy RPG for you.

Something like a cross between Fire Emblem with how you move units around, and SNES Ogre Battle with squadrons, class changing, and automated combat.

Would be fun, but there is no way to grind at all. Battles are limited from the tutorial all the way to the end of the game.

This means you are limited to theinear story battles and rare drop/not farmable arena tokens to build your dream team.

If you want to build different ways, level different units, or try out different squadrons, you are extremely limited by the finite number of story missions the game gives you.

If any unit dies, have fun replacing them with a level 1 new hire. If you have the money for that.

If you want a linear SRPG where the army you can build is confined by the non-repeatable story battles and limited resources the game gives you, this one might be for you.

If you like leveling units and the freedom to try out different builds with optional repeatable battles, avoid this. You'll be disappointed.

The developers don't have any intention of adding a way to grind. It's been requested many times since the game launched and it's not something they're adding.\t\t\t\t\t
