\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTactical RPG fans in general and Fire Emblem fans in particular will feel right at home with this one, it has the common characteristics of the genre and the story follows roughly the usual story beats with a few nice twists here and there. The aspect that sets the game apart is the squad management, you can recruit conscript and mercenaries between maps and arrange them in squads of up to 9 units depending on the leadership stat of the squad captain, but these squads cannot be changed or rearranged during maps. Individual soldiers can level up and promote to stronger classes, going up and down the class tree at will by reallocating obtained resources. Squads can equip artifacts and soldiers have different skills providing various benefits. The class system and tech tree are intuitive and the UI is simple to use, although it could be improved in some aspects. Maps provide good enough variety of different terrains and optional objectives, many of which are based on the number of turns taken to capture a stronghold or to reach a spot. The pixel art is good, character sprites are very detailed and character portraits are nice but map backgrounds could use some work in my opinion. The soundtrack is adequate, there a few memorable tunes but none is annoying or bad. Overall it is a very good game and a lot of fun, it is easy to pick up for newcomers and rewarding for veterans of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid recommendation for anyone who loved the Fire Emblem / Advance Wars / Yggdra Union games from the Gameboy Advance. If you are familiar with those games the Symphony of War will be instantly familiar and while they mostly nailed the game play there are a few issues which bring the experience down for me.

Lacks a skirmish mode
A minor gripe because the balancing is pretty good, but I would have liked a way to build and test unit combinations outside of story levels.

Level design
About level 10 the opponent starts fielding massive numbers of units, this slows turns down to a crawl, long enough to go make a barista style coffee and drink it. There is an option to speed up enemy animations but better design would have eliminated the need for it entirely.

There is also an escort mission, the bad kind, the only kind, if there is anything the devs need to be ashamed of, this is it.

Story and Characters
I liked almost all of the characters, most are relatable and occasionally funny.

The story itself is okay, unfortunately the way the devs chose to tell the story could have been better. The cartoonish pixel graphics don't suit the tone and there are levels that are either impossible or brain numbingly simple to accommodate the story. I also felt many of the characters had very little presence or agency within the story.\t\t\t\t\t
