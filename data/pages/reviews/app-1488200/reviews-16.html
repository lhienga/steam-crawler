\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst 10-15 hours is great, but they give you so many cheap shots in their battles that I just can't bear to keep playing anymore. They let the story and their enforcement of the plot, which as you see in other reviews isn't very good anyway, override their good battle mechanics and excellent unit development mechanics - and don't get me wrong, those last two things are great.

Like there's one battle where you're designed to get wiped out as part of the story. OK, if you want to advance the plot and split the players army up then that's doable. Just tell me that's what happened and move on. Why make me spend 20 minutes getting my butt whipped against impossible odds? How does that experience improve anyone's enjoyment of the game?

That's just one battle and not ragequit worthy in and of itself. But there's another thing that the devs seem to love: having enemy units show up out of nowhere in the middle of the battle, units that weren't on the board, and surprise your units. Worse yet, in at least two of those battles (there's likely more but I binned the game after the second time) the enemy unit is literally unkillable. It will wipe out whatever it encounters in one attack and you can't harm it. They do tell you that this unit is showing up, but they don't tell you when or where, which doesn't do you much good.

My thing is, play fair. If you want difficulty give me more enemies. If you want to set time limits (which I don't like either, but OK), then just set a f**king time limit and tell me what it is. Don't resort to that.

It's not even about winning or losing. Likely I would still have won that last battle. The point is that with the unit development system you spend a lot of time working on your squads and promoting characters. Killing them off with cheap tactics like that is, to me, a lack of respect for my time. You don't want to have the impression that you're working harder than the devs did.

Based on the game score most people don't mind the above. But I just can't stand that kind of stuff. At all. To me this game isn't worth the space on my hard drive, much less the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fairly simple on it's surface grid based squad TBS, with dozens of classes to level your units into, a massive number of items to change up the style of any given squad, and 30 chapters to tweak and throw armies at.

Fairly customisable on the difficulty, but overall since enemy AI isn't that smart most of the challenge will be surviving wars of attrition, especially once muskets and cannon units start showing up.

Graphics is RPG-maker pixel art, with audio to match, but it pretty much perfectly suits the Fire Emblem/Classic Final Fantasy vibe it's going for. UX is a little TOO on the nose though and could stand some quality of life improvements and hotkeys (unless I've just missed a "next idle unit" key somewhere).

Storywise nothing too surprising or unpredictable so far, but the plot carries itself well enough to cement the chapters together while your army organically grows and changes.

Battles are fairly simple, although there are some fairly deep mechanics like weather, you don't need them to succeed beyond knowing the key mistakes to avoid. However interactions between units, faction rep, hoping for particular mercenaries or traits, and building out all of your potentially dozens of squads all add into something fairly organic which means no two armies are ever quite going to look alike.

As an added bonus, while the framerate will tank a bit it doesn't burn through an office laptop's battery life too quickly - so add this to the list of power outage proof PC games.

Overall, if you're a fan of TBS with relatively simple battle mechanics, but enough granularity of unit customisation to make the army management a major loop in itself, you should enjoy this.
