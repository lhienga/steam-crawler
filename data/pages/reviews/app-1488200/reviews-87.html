\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is absolutely amazing. I wholeheartedly recommend it. At this point I have finished it once and I started playing it again immediately after that.

The game looks stunning with it's 2d art and beautiful animations. It's got a rich story full of twists and turns and amazing characters which you can't help but love. Gameplay is a mix of tactics, strategy, army management and rpg elements. Every mission is a battlefield where you will command an army of units. Those units are split up in squads with a whole bunch of characteristics, bonuses, abilities and so on. Between the missions you will have a chance to manage your army - build/modify squads, use items, buy/exchange all sorts of units, artifacts and so on. It's all really enjoyable and incredibly satisfying. Replayability is great - every playthrough will be different - different items, different units, different tactics and AI responses. If you like any of the aforementioned elements then you should definitely get this game.

Please support amazing games like this instead of AAA microtransaction infested, broken, unfinished "games"!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn the surface, this game has everything I could want in a video game.

Going deeper, this game really just reminds me of better games. I played 7 chapters of this game and wanted to quit to play Fire Emblem again. Or Final Fantasy Tactics. Or even Tactics Ogre.

And that's the problem with Symphony of War; game plays it far too safe. It doesn't take risks, and it doesn't do anything new. It takes a bunch of really awesome mechanics and plot themes from better games and mixes them into this one, leaving you with a feeling that you've played this before. And, if you've any experience in the genre, you honestly have.

There is absolutely nothing wrong with this game. If you're new to these styles of games, you'll probably love it. I've been playing tactics games since I discovered Shining Force on the Sega Channel back in the early 90s, and I've basically played them all. This game gives me nothing new, and therefore I've tired of it quickly.

Lending to that early feeling of exhaustion and fatigue is the fact that this feels like a port from the console. I didn't like the controls at all. Felt really clunky. I couldn't seem to scroll smoothly through a map, or cycle through units using quick key commands.

In summary, I can't really recommend this game as I feel there are older, better games that pretty much do everything Symphony of War does, but better. It is a solid game, and even a great one in a vacuum, but the entire thing feels like a rushed plagiarism.\t\t\t\t\t
