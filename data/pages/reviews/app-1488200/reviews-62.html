\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my new favourite Indi tactical RPG's! Played through the entire game on the hardest difficulty. Thoroughly enjoyed myself! 7.5/10!

For me, I wish "Warlord Mode" was a bit harder. However, I guess the difficulty is balanced more for Ironman mode (perma death). And the way that works in this game is more annoying than challenging. You can revive units in maps, so you end up just having too spam the resurrect shop before ending a missions. Not fun, I'd prefer a harder mode without annoying Iron man mechanics.

I also wish the final 5+ missions were more epic in enemy sizes. You build up such a massive and powerful army, you just outnumber and wipe the floor easily, even on Warmode + Iron man. The final boss I one shot on turn 1...

Some of the unit types you can upgrade feel under powered compared to others. While the game has like 50+ units, I can only recommend like half of them. When I found out what units were best, I suppose that also made the game easier. And maybe it's balanced more around using the weaker units? Still loved the variety and options this game offered!

Everything else was S-Tier. Great story, Amazing Graphics, Incredible animations, fun mission designs, great characters & heroes. Loved everything about it and can't wait for the DLC and a potential sequel!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually like turned based combat. I don't usually like RPG maker games. This game however, took both genres and pushed them to their potential. I really enjoyed the game start to finish. The story was pretty good, I don't know why people think it's bad. It's not going to win any awards, but the plot was still enjoyable. My favorite part of this game was getting units, forming squads, leveling them up, giving them items to make them stronger, to make the strongest squads that you manage. Then playing with them, especially in arena mode (although arena did get kind of repetitive) was super fun. On the normal difficulty, the game isn't all that difficult, although there are some levels that can be tricky. But it didn't need to be difficult to be super fun. Warlord difficulty however, is a different story. If you want a challenging experience, then play it on that difficulty. People complaining how easy and normal are too easy, when they don't even try warlord kind of makes their arguments fall flat.
This game is a super fun turn based strategy game with really addicting level up mechanics that can be enjoyed from the start of the game until the end. Highly recommend.
8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't know you were still allowed to make games with as much SOVL as this! This game feels like classic Fire Emblem or Ogre Battle, and often a lot like Lufia 2. But at the same time, it doesn't feel self-indulgent; you can sense the _Game of Thrones_ influence throughout. This is a game that's from 2023 and it knows it, not one that's pretending to be from 1996.

The gameplay loop here is deeply satisfying. You review and arrange your units, enjoying a pleasantly Minecraft-like period of tinkering (I was able to make an early push to get a lot of troops on horseback); then you send them into battle, and emerge victorious (at least once you get into the swing of things); then you're done for the night, and you come out of your session feeling fresh yet satisfied, psychologically free to go do other things. This is a game that respects your time; it's not a forever-game, a never-ending dopamine trap.

The story springs a series of surprises on you; avoid all spoilers going in! But it also leaves at least one big surprise unsprung, in a way that I really enjoyed. I hope that the two planned sequels to this game are released, and I'll play them with a good will if they are; if they're not, it will at least be fun to speculate.

(Or maybe some of the earlier games this company released will have answers... I love that all of their titles are set in the same world!)\t\t\t\t\t
