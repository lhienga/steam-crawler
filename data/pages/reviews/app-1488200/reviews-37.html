\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy one complaint is they didn't put more into it.

It is a beautiful game but it feels like the developers just said "good enough" and shipped it. If they had hammered out features between combat sequences and added more to the characterization in this game, it would have been worthy of a Fire Emblem title. Unfortunately, with making it larger than FE, they lost the individuality games need to really shine.

I found myself not caring about the characters because they felt generic (even if they weren't). Sure the soldiers are good, but give us more core characters and flesh them out. I still remember obscure characters from FE like Fiora, Farina, and Florina. There could easily have been characters like this but instead not a single one I even remember. I struggle to even remember the main character's face as I write this and I just turned off the game, yet I can draw most of the Fire Emblem Blazing Blade cast off the name alone.

I still recommend it, but I would have liked to get more out of it than gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm keeping this review positive despite having no plans to finish the game. As many have said, the idea behind this game's combat mechanics is awesome and I really want to see more from this developer. The game is also only 20$ and even with the problems I'll list you'll still get your money's worth from the game's charming features.

What's crazy is that my list of CONS is much larger then PROS and I'd still give the game a 7.5/10 for the price tage because the PROS are just that good.

PROS
- Fighting in this game is awesome, I really loved the variety of units and possibilities that could occur.
- Conquest of objectives makes you really feel like an army winning a war and it's very satisfying.
- The main character is likable, if not a bit generic.
- A basic romance system exists, which is nice but held back by the game engine.

CONS
- Linear: This may be a personal bias, but the story and everything that happens is set in stone. A game with such great war mechanics and ideas would be fantastic as an open world game.
- The Story Is Meh: I paid close attention to what was going on for about 8 chapters but after that it really started to get bogged down. This is a huge part because this game is made using RPG maker which is extremely outdated at this point.
- Character relationships are extremely boring: Again, heavily influenced by the game engine, I just could not care less about the dialogue that you can have between each of the companions.
- Scripted Battle Losses: This is a huge pet peeve of mine, and while YES you can technically win the battle if you knew everything before hand and built an exact army to counter your enemy, that's not how I enjoy playing games. All the more reasons the game would be even better if it wasn't linear, that way any loss you suffer will still feel like I had a choice.
- Shifting Scope: Another personal preference perhaps, but for me the game is at its peak when you're on a large world map conquering towns and forts, but at certain times you'll have to play a whole chapter when you're breaking someone out of prison or into a sewer. And it uses the same mechanics as the world map wars so it really feels....small. Insignificant. And it breaks the feel of the rest of the game.

All and all, I got a bit frustrated after the forced battle loss that happens in the story, but pressed on just to find the story really gets slow afterwards so I've stopped playing completely and I don't see myself coming back. However, due to the nice price tag I'd still say I got my money's worth. A sequel/spin off for this game has HUGE potential based on what they did right for this game. I really look forward to see future developments from this company.\t\t\t\t\t

Ok, long story short, if youve ever played Fire Emblem and liked it, this is a MUST HAVE.

Short story long:
- the story is as ok as it gets, nothing overly bad or good
- the characters on the other hand are quite well done, the interactions are funny and there is even variation for different playthroughs in the relations
- the fights and missions are as much Fire Emblem as it can be

The big selling point for me is the army creation... i guess i spent around 3/4 of the time organising my squads and minmaxing the living hell out of it. I restarted the game 4 times over 20h before going for the 1st playthrough, because whenever i understood another mechanic it ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up my whole plan.

The biggest problem i had was that you need time to get hold of all mechanics and the guides are mostly for outdated patches, so some stuff simply doesnt work like its explained.

Overall, if you a knack for TRPGs get it. I bought it with 40% discount, sales are quite often. The basegame+legends DLC was 17ish€

PS: I am usually npt one to condem someones kinks ... but srsly Diana took it a step too far :D\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCouple hours into this one, a fairy eclectic comparison popped, nay, straight up barged into my head as if out of nowhere: it's like Borderlands 3, but better. Hear me out.
The only good thing about BL3 is its gameplay. The level design is dull and uninspired, the story seems to have been written by a teenager, the characters... I'm not even gonna go there, the weather's rather nice today. But man, the gameplay. All the sliding, the mounting, the shooting,,, Now that's some juicy entertainment. Unfortunately, in the end it's not quite enough to make me consider BL3 a good game. Too much slop, too little common sense.
The only good thing about Symphony of War is its gameplay. The art direction is all over the place, the amount of clicking you'll have to do will probably cut your mouse's lifespan in half. The story is fairly generic, the characters are more or less one-note and boring, The writing and the dialogue will in fact make you want to split your skull right in half. But by the gods, the gameplay. The fact that I can legitimately spend half an hour between missions simply tinkering with my squad comps speaks for itself. The game's a min-maxer's dream/nightmare, and it's absolutely worth experiencing for yourself on this basis alone. Well, maybe don't do DLC missions until you're in NG+, all the new toys and extra resources truly break the difficulty curve.
TL;DR: Dancing Dragon should consider acquiring 2K Games. Maybe also look into hiring a dedicated writer next time. But yeah, good stuff.\t\t\t\t\t
