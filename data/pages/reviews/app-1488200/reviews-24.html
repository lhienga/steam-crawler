\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is pretty sweet. As a fan of Fire Emblem/Tactics type games, it's clearly made by fans of those and made with love and care. The squad system is a standout feature on it's own, coupled with army-wide tech upgrades and individual unit skills gives the player so many knobs to tweak and adjust that it quickly gets easy to spend hours just messing with your army to optimize your squads. The music and audio are all on point, and my complaints are few. A small wishlist of improvements (I've played 10 or so chapters so far) I could use more spots of voice acting in the game. I miss the extras characterization that came in game like FE:Awakening with characters saying short phrases like "Huh?", "Right", "Yes", and so on during dialogue and support conversations. Even without going that far, adding a trigger for an existing line to play when selecting a unit in an out-of-combat menu would go a long way to familiarizing me with the voices of the characters, it feels a little odd to only hear them in one spot. The menu music is great, but pretty short. It could do with a little looping edit. Also, the mouse experience is relatively good, but the menus were clearly designed with a controller/keyboard in mind, and it can be kinda awkward when doing things like exchanging resources. But these complaints are extremely minor, if you've read the review this far, and you like the genre, I earnestly recommend picking it up and giving it a play. This isn't one you should miss.\t\t\t\t\t
