\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA classic style SRPG in the same vein as classic Fire Emblem and Langrisser but with a considerably higher amount of customisation in how you outfit your units for battle which results in a surprisingly polished and satisfying game play experience. While the gameplay and systems can be ranked among the best of its peers, Symphony of War lacks a cohesive visual design and its character art looks like it belongs in cheap mobile game rather than a SRPG. The story is also rather generic and I often found myself skipping through the cut scenes and story moments to get to the meat of the experience. If you can stomach some jarring visual design and a mediocre story, then Symphony of War has some of the best SRPG gameplay around.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first review. I was looking to satisfy that itch from FFT/Tactics Ogre/Fire Emblem and this game did satisfy that for sure. If you are looking for that, then this game is an easy buy.

Gameplay

Gameplay is solid! Unlike some TRPGs you have several squads instead of one, and all the main characters Personally, I spent most of my time arranging my squad, buying items/resources and transforming my units and it was fun.

AI is competent, they will gang up on the weakest squads which would sometimes make me rage at the game. Since, I am playing with permadeath making every unit survive does take a decent amount of forethought, which is again fun. I have managed to not lose a single unit because even if they take damage units are healed to full health as long as they survive.

There are features like quicksaving, quickloading, resetting and undoing during turns which really help and are excellent features. There are arenas where you can compete for money and rewards between each battle in return for tokens which you can pick up during missions. I had a semi-frequent glitch where some tokens I picked up in the battle did not show up after I completed the mission.

The game keeps introducing new characters/elements so even though the pace becomes slower it doesn't become entirely repetitive, more on that in the classes section. Another thing that helps is the music. T/JRPGs are notorious for having irritating music but in this it didn't bother me even though I listened to it for hours!

One thing I would add is that sometimes the gameplay is frustrating, without going into too much detail, the game uses a cover system where the first row covers the second which covers the third. In some cases, if there is one guy left in the second row all your 6 squad members will attack him/her for 500 damage instead of touching anybody in the back row, which makes no sense. I often undo that turn and attack the squad in a different way to eliminate all the units.

Even though the state it is in now is good, I would request more activities during downtime between missions. I would also request for the ability to queue moves and hand out XP for opening chests etc. Some squads are not as useful for battle so it would be a better way to level them up.

Story

I don't play TRPGs/JRPGs for the story, as long as it non-offensive and does not get in the way of gameplay its perfectly fine. In this case the story falls into the same category. However, there is a time where the main character is inaccessible and during those times the game's pace becomes slower, you have access to less parties and your previously made squads are inaccessible for a while, so there is a noticeable dip. Other than that the writing is fairly competent and better than a lot of AAA writing. Plus, for a fast-forward option to skip for people not interested in the lore or who are redoing a turn.

Classes

This falls into gameplay but is the main reason why people will buy the game. The positives are that there are a decent amount of classes which makes it fun to experiment. However, this is where I believe the game is a bit weaker. As I mentioned you can make squads, so you can have squads of either just archers or mages or soldiers. This at first sounds awesome except when you realize that this game prioritizes heavy units/cavalry.

I played in Normal difficulty so not sure if it applies to the hardest one but armor is king. Mages, Archers, Light Infantry, Rogues are strong support units but they will never be as good as heavy units. You have to pair these support units with the heavy units, which again sounds fine at first but then there are issues with that.

Firstly, you can have a squad of entirely archers, for example, but they will always be extremely vulnerable. So the best thing you can do is put heavy units to block the damage. But then you do the same with the mages...and with the rogues.... its a very easy pattern that doesn't take any imagination. Only reason I had unique squads (all archers, mostly mages) was for my own gameplay, but you can steamroll any squad with a group of heavy units usually. Cavalry is especially strong as it allows you to move after you attack, so you can just put a group of heavy cavalry in each squad.

Secondly, the issue with that is that the damage doesn't always offset the vulnerability. If archers are vulnerable you expect them to be lethal but in most cases heavy units do the same damage while not risking their health. I would argue mages, assassins and light infantry are better but again no real reason to incorporate them if you just want to beat the game. I don't remember any instance where I needed a support unit squad on the field.

There is also a gunner class, but in my opinion its a joke for most of the game until you get an upgrade . The artillery units are viable but I have found the gunner class to be useless.

Lastly, heavy units have the best transformations. A unit can transform to become a champion who wields a large sword. That's really awesome. In comparison, an archer transforms to become a (tankier?) archer who can shoot further. I am not really sure what the final stage does for some of these classes but most non-heavy units have disappointing final stages.

Around the middle of the game, I had basically experimented with all the classes. Only reason I had so many hours is because I replayed after I reached the halfway point. Once, I had seen most of the classes, and realized that heavy units would basically get me through, there wasn't really much of reason to continue, which is why I haven't completed the game.

Conclusion

Overall, I may have sound critical towards the end, but even though I haven't finished the game (yet) I thoroughly enjoyed it, which is why I still recommend it. My FFT itch was bothering me for years and its finally quelled for the time being, now I don't have to hound the Steam Store Page for something similar. The team has done a great job, and I would definitely buy a sequel or go back to the game after a while if changes are made!
