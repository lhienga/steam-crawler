\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite an unexpectedly well made game. Kudos to the devs. Did not expect this level of gameplay detail and mechanics from a 3-man dev team (afaik). Having only played some of the older GBA Fire Emblem games, this was a familiar genre for me, but with quite a refreshing take with the squad system. There are ofc some nitpicks and improvements that could be made but nothing too bad. For example, it felt weird that the character sprites were blurry but looked like they should have looked crisp, some of the dialogue felt incomplete or disconnected, although most of it ties together at some later point, some explanations for mechanics felt incomplete or insufficient, and some UI quality of life improvements that could make the squad management much easier and smoother. Looking forward to the DLC. Solid 8/10 overall.

Edit: Finally finished the dlc, and it was very much worth it, adding almost 15 hours of playtime (would be more if I didn't have endgame units for the earlygame dlc chapters) to my base game playthrough of approx. 60 hours. The extra "Legends" chapters give more insights into characters and lore. The dlc also brings a bunch of cool new units and classes, that you can use throughout the game and not just the dlc. I would strongly recommend getting the game along with the dlc, as It's more of a game expansion than a dlc.. The dlc chapters unlock as you progress through the base game and tie in perfectly into the main story progression rather than feeling like a separate section.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thoroughly enjoyed this game. If you remember the old mid-90's Ogre Battle games, this is basically an updated version of that with a lot more depth. If you are not, it is basically a fantasy war simulator where you recruit and upgrade fantasy style units (dragons, mages, etc.), and battle enemies in a variety of different scenarios with some twists and turns thrown in to add to the challenge.

The game does have a full story to drive the narrative along and it's... fine. Nothing to win any awards but solid enough to give a reason for each scenario and battle sequence. It's definitely not the focus though as a lot of work went into the combat to make it diverse, interesting, and adaptable to your play style. Ranging from dragons, mages, foot soldiers, cannon troops, sneaky assassins, heavy cavalry, etc you have plenty of different options to approach any scenario with a reasonable rock/paper/scissors type approach to units you'd expect.

These types of games are really hard to come by and as an old veteran of the aforementioned Ogre Battle games, there are very few options that compare so it's amazing to find one done as well as this one.

Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood enough,
Gameplay is great and the systems are really well fleshed out, the maps could've used a bit more flair, but there's variety, nothing for me to complain about.
My issue with this game is the story and especially the writing, they weren't good, the characters were very bland, and while i can appreciate the good quality of the art associated with those, the writing leaves a lot to be desired, especially with some of these characters.

If i could offer some critique :
- the cast and the writing was very shallow, elaborating and expand on the backstory, refine the exchanges and offer a bit of insight to what is going on with these guys might be a place where to start
- the story was bad, but not overly terrible, the "chosen one" approach might as well work if there's enough compelling motivation as to why MC is the chosen one, but otherwise, i felt it was a tad bland, clich and at times cringe, especially some of the exchanges, which felt more akin to edgy chit chat.
- Bonding and the relationship between characters could use a bit more refinement, we don't need some coomer fanfic, but, expanding on said bonds wouldn't hurt, also, i think we could use a bit more animosity and conflict, differing opinions and what not.

All in all, my time with this game was positive, and i'm looking forward to the next update, the gameplay is solid and the systems in place are very good, expand on those, improve the others and you're golden.\t\t\t\t\t
