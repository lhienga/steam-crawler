\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIgnore played time. Played elsewhere, bought again here because it's worth it.

This is the game I wanted the post GBA Fire Emblems to be. Imagine Fire Emblem mixed with SoulNomad (old PS2 NIS game that got overlooked).

Story is standard fantasy trope meh, the gameplay is top tier though.

Building squads is lots of fun. Watching them wreck faces is even more fun. Buy the DLC to continue face wrecking with your favorite squads once the game ends. If you make it past stage 4 or 5 you probably already have a few favorites already.

Yes, they tend to rely on 'surprise' units showing up mid map, but it's generally pretty easy to know how to deal with them or if you even need to.

If you like older FE games and squad building types of tactics games, this one is pretty close to being a gold standard. The devs have updated the game quite a bit since release, so that's a plus as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually leave reviews, but I feel like I have to for this game. This is the best Fire emblem style game I've played, and it's not close.
I was not expecting much when I installed, the autobattling combat looked like it would be a turnoff and the presentation gave me the impression this was going to be a cheap mobile game... Oh boy was I wrong.

The story is pretty good, but it's not the main draw. The gameplay on the tactical map is really good and the autobattling style combat is actually very well done.
Tinkering with squads of units and organising the army really gave the feeling that I was commanding a military force as opposed to a small ragtag team, and watching a squad that I'd designed obliterate an enemy squad because of the care I'd taken choosing the unit composition and positioning them (as opposed to simply using a sword unit against an axe unit) is satisfying in a way that I have rarely felt playing this style of game.
The different difficulties also give the option to play at any skill level and still have a challenge from beginners to veterans.

I can't recommend this game highly enough to fans of this style of tactical RPG.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an avid Fire Emblem fan, seeing a game that fits within the genre was extremely cool, nonetheless to see it on Steam!!! The main selling point of this game is the Army building. I found this feature to be breathtaking, as it allowed me to create and customize my units to how I saw fit. If you are a fan of the Battalions in FE3H, You would probably like this feature. The strategy plays differently than FE games as well, not having something like a weapon triangle, but rather features involving your units placement and movement (Charges, Ambushing, using Forts for range, etc.) Which was a nice breath of fresh air after more traditional strategy layouts (Pole arms still do bonus damage to Horses).

Things I noticed about the difference between Fire Emblem and this are a couple things: First is that the game is much more centered around the combat battle stages rather than the story. I felt that I was spending a majority of my time either debating the composition of my army or on stages rather than delving into the story. Second was the Usage of Nephilim Powers, in which they are specifically built into the game, and vary from the most common "Time traveler" skill, in which it allowed for much more creative maneuverability on the Map.

Some takeaways I had was the somewhat big holes in the story. I felt that it wasn't intriguing enough, and somewhat followed a generic story line until (Spoiler [somewhat]) there was a twist nearing the end of the game. I also felt that the bonds and connections of characters was a little too "forced" in that everyone follows and befriends you because you're part of a prophecy. I wish that if this game was to ever receive updates, overhauls, or remasters, that I could spend more time delving into the game universe and understanding each character better. I understand that while my standards are those of Fire Emblem games, in which Storytelling and building character is a main selling point of the game, and that this is different, so see this as you see fit, as this is simply my opinion. Lastly, I got somewhat annoyed at how many gosh darn Archers and Cavalry units there are, and how strategically they are placed. More of a nuisance than an actual problem I have with the game, its simply just how difficult I found it when I was laying siege to a castle and i got swarmed by an entire army of cavalry, and that archers got the range of a US Marine Sniper taking out my squishy units.

While my issues may seem like a lot, They are simply my own issues, and still highly recommend you give this game a go! The gameplay is amazing and is a solid game to take around on a laptop and play while travelling or if your access to internet is not stable. Also Sybil is the best and RIP Zorro :(\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving a lot of fun!

Basically you build an army consisting of squads you design by recruiting various classes. Then deploy that army on a hex field, and capture victory points, kill enemy squads, or occupy specific hexes. Very enjoyable turn-based combat that basically relies on the Rock-paper-scissors chess model. You can give specific traits to your troops, give them artifacts, and promote them as they gain experience.

The pixel art is great and the sound track is snappy. The tutorial is sufficient without feeling like you're in 6th grade algebra again, and the story is sufficient to maintain interest.

8/10. Definitely a good pickup.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; Fire Emblem with guns and actual army, now on your PC - a big YES.

It has everything from Fire Emblem and more, from the good, the bad, to the not-so-appealing.

Gameplay-wise, it's very fun. It's well thought and well put together, very solid. The progression is great, it started out slow and simple and gradually gives us more options. The combat is complex (in a good way,) backed by the customization that is so free yet balanced. Can clearly see the dev loves the genre. No weapon triangle, btw.

The story and the writing are all alright. But since it's inspired by FE, it met with the same pros and cons of FE storytelling.

Then there is the art. Like most FE games before FE 13, it suffers from being generic and uninspired, and cobbling 3 extremely different art styles together made them hold themselves worse than the Veridia Empire.

Still, it's a really nice and fun game from the gameplay perspective. Don't let the art discourage you like it did to me for a very long time.

Edit: though the customization is fun, it's also kinda pain. Wish it's made easier or utilize being on PC more, an ability to hover the cursor over units to see their status instead of the needs to use the menu would be nice.\t\t\t\t\t
