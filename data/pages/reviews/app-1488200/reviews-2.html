\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a veritable diamond in the rough and doesn't get enough attention.

If you're a fan of grid-based strategy games like Fire Emblem, Langrisser, etc you will feel right at home with this game. It brings to the table some interesting new features like the ability to endlessly customize your various squads' composition; instead of being static units that never change, you have a real sense of progression as you fill out the 3x3 squad grids with interesting and varied unit types. It adds a whole new element to the strategy of preparation in addition to what you do on the battlefield.

I never felt like the difficulty was unreasonable, and it ramped up at a modest pace as you gradually improve your team. I particularly loved that you can create squads from scratch from units you can buy, so you can have quite the large army - and the best part is they usually don't restrict you too badly in terms of max units - a lot of the mid to late game missions allow you to have 20 squads, which is enough to allow you to give everyone a chance to fight.

The characters are interesting, the plot was surprisingly deep and easy enough to follow, the music is pleasant to listen to, and above all I always had the sense that this dev team cares about their game. A lot of love and care went into this, so if you're looking for something to scratch that grid-based strategy itch, give this game a go.\t\t\t\t\t
