\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have nothing but praise for this.

The TL:DR of this game is, that it has a great art style. a Fantastic Story. And Amazing Gameplay among other things, the only thing i regret about my experience is that i did not play this game sooner if i am honest.

Though, in the longer version.

First the Art Style, absolutely fantastic. Characters look dynamic, realistic and the models feel great to look at.

The Gameplay, is rounded. And fun, It never felt unfair which is an important part about gameplay which i see some strategy games not manage that well, but this one does. Combat never feels like i am downright rolling a dice and hoping it will land 6 up. Tactics, will win the game. So use them to your strongest abilities.

The Relationships were a nice added thing, i feel like it could have gotten some more work in. But at the end of the day its a small added thing to me which just adds to the characters. So while i hope that this gets some more work in future titles its not much of a complaint.

The Story, Its fantastic. So i will not say anything about it, just know. it has twists, turns, and each of them is great. And had me at the edge of my seat. If this intrigues you, i highly emplore you to play this! For the Story alone if i am honest!

Outside of that i am not 100% sure what else to say except for, play this game. If you like Strategy games, tactic games, story driven games. It has all this. And more. Also the estimate with 40 hours per campaign. It was fairly accurate for my playthrough in which i was already kinda steam rolling so, that was extremely neat.\t\t\t\t\t

**SPOILER**

I will say though, having just finished chapter 12, that is the worst level design I've seen in a long time. I get it, the hero has to lose... does it need to take 45 minutes of watching enemy squads move around the map and slowly kill all your squads. It's obvious the moment the trap is sprung that you can't win. It's like "oh, this is that one fight in every RPG that you have to lose." So why make it so agonizingly long. I shouldn't go "well, guess I'll just watch a TV show while the CPU takes hours to play its turn!" And I get bonuses for trying to stick around for 6 freaking turns of that? No way, I was just throwing my units at them so that the fight would end already.

Just have the army come in, and cut to a cut scene that shows us getting destroyed. The whole unwinnable fight is never good game design, and this one is the worst of that kind of thing I've seen because it's SOOOO long! Honestly, it's to the point where I was about to alt-F4 out of it and never come back to the game.\t\t\t\t\t

A indescribable and frankly unintelligible UI, readable by only the most stoic of gamers. A soundtrack that imagines what it would be like if games still cared about such things as a good time. With sound effects straight out of RPG makers most used adventure sounds. Alas, those sounds have blessed my ears for 40+ hours of my life, and, I must say that each one fills me with joy. No matter the absurdity of the hoof sound. I love it. I have a death ball of horses. The sound produces pure serotonin. The feel of the game gloriously encapsulates the beloved feel of care being put into a game. This game at its core is filled with care. It was not created to fuel the ADHD ridden child of today, to flash so many lights across their screen that the child is paralyzed by their mental affliction. No. This game was handcrafted for the most autistic of RPG enjoyers. Three separate art styles, each incredible in their own right. Whether it is questionable bosomful women, or the (extremely gay panic inducing) Lysandre, their portraits and models all look amazing. The story pixel art is truly RPG maker at its finest. But the game play art style, oh lord. I've never been so attracted to the look of a game in my entire life, and I've played the most revered game for its graphics of all time: "Village Bus Driver Simulator." Each and every pixel feels like a Christmas gift that I've just unwrapped, and, surprise, the gift was Diana soloing the campaign. I've seen a complaint or two about difficulty, and this must be because those charletons are playing on baby mode without permadeath. A tearful thank you to the devs for letting me romance the nine foot tall, raging, incarnation of the gods wrath. That was pretty cool of you guys. Also. Her titan form MUST have been a dev favorite because, good lord, business in the front AND back. But why, why, is there one mission with side missions and then side missions are never seen again? It just doesn't seem right. People complain about the obvious plot twists and the non-subtle story, but to that I say, "Things in your life are obvious and yet you don't see them coming" so next time consider karma before everyone in your life knows your wife is with another man, while you are oblivious. The story was fun and cozy, I enjoyed having my squads ripped from my use right after getting them just right. Very epic and very rewarding. Learned my lesson there. I do wonder if the decisions in the beginning have any impact on the game, or are just there to scratch the itch to give us the illusion of choice, but maybe, all along we had no choice. Perhaps that is the message of the game we were all too ignorant to understand. That while we makes choices, in the end, the demons will come and we will die. But in the meantime, why NOT get all four kids killed over and over again because you just don't care. Why NOT make a death ball of siege cannons and cosplay the united states of america in any conflict ever. Truly fun and creativity are what rule your experience here. We may all die, the game will come to an end, but I'll be damned if my meaningless existence is not filled with dragons. God I love dragons. Is this game worth the 20 dollar price tag? I would not know, I got it for cheaper because I'm just like that. Do I believe it would be worth 20 dollars? oh yes. Without a doubt. It could be worth so much more depending on how good of taste you have in games. Think about it, you'd spend 70+ dollars on a dinner with a girl. OR 20 dollars for the best 40+ hours of your life. You make the decision while I have fun. I only wish I could duel my friends in this game, however, the solo experience has been incomprehensibly joyful. I will be playing the game through again, on the hardest difficulty with permadeath. Until I win. Because, what good are friends, when I can watch Garfongle, her two dragon riders, and the king of dragons GET FOUR CONSECUTIVE ACTIONS and kill the final boss in "one" technical turn. The lore of this game is deeper than that of humanity, nay, that of the cosmos. I have scrawled on a nearly hundred page document the interwoven story of this game, and sigh I am still the apprentice. A very deep thank you to the development team. I rate this game the highest rating I can, One "jar of multivitamins I didn't realize were vitamins so i've now been hospitalized for vitamin A toxicity" out of begging the game to increase Jules capacity faster so that I can make a deathball of nine war bows and tickle my brain. Have a great day.

One side tangent, why can I not romance Zanatus? Please. If I go the entire game, shunning the temptress' and the ravenously hot men, allow me to romance the four armed fornicator. It is all I truly desire.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Great game, if you like Fire Emblem you will like this as well.

The game takes the Fire Emblem game mechanics and improves upon them greatly. The battles feel more unique and rewarding because the squads are laid out and planned by you, instead of being "just a character." Some of the new mechanics such as ambushing or the whole day/night cycle feel under emphasised. There never seemed to be a situation where I would actively wait a turn or move to specific terrain to set up an ambush instead of just taking a direct route.

The story is good. Not amazing, certainly not bad, just very good. It keeps you engaged and answers the general question of "why are we fighting here". Some of the character motivations were questionable to downright silly, but never enough to make me start skipping all the dialogue. The lack of animated cut scenes and voice acting makes it difficult to get attached to the characters, but it's not reasonable to expect that of a small developer anyway. Although it's probably not a story you will remember a year after playing, it serves it's purpose to carry the plot forward and set the stage for each battle.

The music was also... surprisingly good. Generally in turn based games I'll mute the music and listen to my own, but I never did that for this one. Very good for setting the mood in each battle.

The army management is a great addition, recruiting mercenaries that could lead their own squads and fight alongside characters throughout the whole campaign adds refreshing variety. Experimenting with different formations was fun, though at the end of the day it generally follows the same rough blueprint of heavy infantry/cavalry up front, healer/mages in the center and either a rear guard or more dps at the back. The ability to add custom traits to every soldier in the army allows for some more strategy, though by the end of the game where you have nearly 150-200 soldiers it feels overwhelming to try and customise them all.

The artifacts also lend some interesting strategy, but most of them felt a bit lackluster. The artifacts that had a large impact (such as lose health each phase but gain lifesteal) changed the way you used that squad whereas the rest of the artifacts just didn't feel like they justified the time spent equipping and managing them. Perhaps in the next game, fewer artifacts but more impactful?

The arena's also felt somewhat out of place. Sure, they are a way to grind levels and gain leadership in between missions, but that feels very risky from a difficulty perspective. You'll tend to either have people who use every arena token they get (by the end of the game I had ~20-30 unused and I did about 10 arenas) or people who don't bother and do the main missions only. It seems difficult to balance the enemy levels in the main missions against both types.

If I had any suggestions for the next game, I would tie moral damage closer to physical damage. It was irritating and somewhat nonsensical for a high morale squad to drop down to shaken/shattered just because a few half dead archer squads targeted them dealing no damage. Larger squads (4x4 or 5x5 grids) would also be fun to play around with, perhaps capacity can be closer tied to unit tier instead of how many units are in a squad already. It would be fun to experiment with smaller elite squads vs larger low tier squads. I think a neat way to incorporate traits across a large army would be for soldiers to pick up/learn traits organically as they level up instead of having them assigned manually via an item.

Overall it's an excellent first game in what I hope becomes a long running series. Congratulations to the devs on a great launch! I hope this feedback helps, and I look forward to the next one.\t\t\t\t\t
