\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I found the deeper dynamics of the game to be quite enjoying, there is some work that should be done to make it better. The tutorials are ok at best, forcing the player to read countless guides online to understand the systems that are not intuitive. Not a fan of games that require an encyclopedia to understand and play. There should have been more hands on tutorials to better understand the system at the start and a better designed UI. Movement and turns need to be numbers rather than singular actions, to better command the battlefield. The ability to move once rather than allotted movement numbers/spaces is hindering to such a degree that it makes combat and movement mechanics frustrating. The enemy has stacked units while you are lucky to get 3 in a unit outside that of your main character's units, and your squishy units are always targeted without a real way in which to protect them, as the enemy simply goes around you, especially cavalry, which enemy gets right away in large numbers while you have to go Google how in the heck you get such a luxury. A unit is done for a turn if your healer within takes a healing action. The amount of turns they expect you to finish in does not work well at all regarding objectives that you MUST take to have enough money and resources to keep up. I like a challenge for sure, but not an illogical challenge to a game. The UI is one of the worst parts, when you look at working on units and having to repeat actions that should not need to happen, over and over again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteam absolutely needs a Middling/Neutral Option!

The TLDR: If you like Fire Emblem/Tactics Ogre, and don't mind an, at times unfair difficulty (regardless of setting): this is a game for you.

If you find RNG frustrating: Stay away from this one. Your experience WILL vary.

WARNING: I do make comments on the Final Chapter, and thus, the Final Battle. Please keep that in mind. I try not to spoil things, but I cannot voice my opinion on it without talking about it. You have been warned!

-=-
I play games on the Easier Difficulties, because honestly, I'm looking to have fun while playing games. The whole purpose and point of ~play~. I started a brand new playthrough after a Friend of mine started his, and while our difficulty selections were different: My experience was significantly rougher on Easy (Cadet) than his on Normal (Captain).

So that's something to consider.

I wanted to like Symphony of War: The Nephilim Saga, but ultimately between the Final Chapter and awful RNG. I just don't care to subject myself to the hours of additional grinding it's going to take to get to the ending.

I feel the game is fine, I actually liked the original presentation before the "Visual Update." As I am an Artist and I understand the difficulty that comes with creating good, appealing character portraits. I would have preferred the visual update work on giving the individual heroes unique combat sprites (as there's quite a few that are nothing more than sprite recolors), rather than redo the Portrait Art, but that's just me.

I feel the game is ultimately marred by everyone's favorite gimmick. Random Generation.

I understand the pros of this system. It allows a degree of replay value. It can also lead to some of the most awful experiences you can ever have in a system.

Each Generic unit you can hire comes with randomized stats (from a collection of Affinities) and randomly determined Traits. In order to build effective squads, some Traits are more valuable than others. Essentially anything that gives you additional Capacity Points. So you can put more soldiers into your squads.

You can further customize your soldiers with additional Traits found from Skill Books that, are, as with everything else: Random drops.

Everything follows the Common (White), Uncommon (Green), Rare (Blue), Uncommon Rare (Purple), and Legendary (Orange) format.

I was midway through the entire game before I got a single randomly generated generic with a Captain Trait. On Easy. It wasn't until I very loudly voiced my frustrations on it (to friends and my Discord), that I actually started getting the Trait books required to actually start turning my army around and start building effective squads.

Yes. You can build squads without these traits. However, the AI targets your weakest squads. So raising an already hampered squad into something usable is a bit difficult more than midway through the game and an absolute chore at Endgame.

Yes. You can get items that can boost your Leadership Stat to increase your capacity, but they are also RANDOM DROPS.

I believe that the fact that the final Marketplace in the game has unlimited supplies is because of the final encounter being an absolutely unfair difficulty spike.

I am not going to restart a 20 or so hour game, again, because of RNG. F- That.

-=- Final Battle SPOILERS-=-
As for the Final Battle itself. I felt just fighting the final boss would have been enough. Extra enemy units is just overkill. You already have an enemy that can deal massive amounts of AoE damage in regular fights and has a turn by turn gimmick going on.

The extra enemy units detracts from the overall experience. It undermines the Final Boss fight by directly implying that the Final Boss cannot stand on it's own and needs an army of minions to actually win. The fact that these squads spawn in sets of four, from three of the four cardinal directions and can be comprised of really annoying high DPS units just adds to that frustration.

I feel it would have been more rewarding and cathartic to beat on the final boss with the army you built, than it is fighting wave after wave of reinforcements while the boss decides to do annoying things like make itself invulnerable.

You already have to cycle out squads that get trashed by it's obscene DPS. Does it really need 12 additional squads appearing to help it out?

I like the concept of the Final Encounter, I just feel it's execution could be better.

I also would have made the Arena for the final encounter allow you to take advantage of Flying units. Since you SPEND THE ENTIRE GAME WORKING TOWARDS THEM, but I digress.

-=-
EDIT: So it turns out the Final Battle is not nearly as awful as I had initially believed. There are mechanics that happen that help even the playing field and makes the battle more accessible than I had originally thought.

I had been so put off by the first two rounds of combat, that I had not bothered (during those play sessions) to progress to the third turn. Hence my negative take on things.

I still believe my original points stand as far as it's design is concerned, but I wanted to make this edit to point out that it was not as bad as I had initially been lead to believe.

-=-
All in all, I enjoyed the setting and the characters. I liked the presentation and what they were attempting to do, but RNG hampered my experience over and over again.

I'm not totally unhappy with the time I've spent with it, but I'm also not going to keep beating my head against a wall trying to defeat a final chapter that has such awful encounter design.

I might continue to take stabs at the Final encounter here and there, but I won't be making anymore of a concentrated effort to beat it.\t\t\t\t\t

Before their proportions were unrealistic or odd, and some of them looked liked they come from other games or had different art styles, like someone compiled a bunch of rpg maker art packs together. If you love classic fire emblem games or strategy games in general, then this game is for you. A couple points of criticism would be that the story isn't particularly incredible, and some of it's plot beats are a little cheesy. I would have liked a more nuanced story, less light vs dark, good vs evil, ect. I also would have liked more supports between all the characters, but those are minor criticisms compared to the fun gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
