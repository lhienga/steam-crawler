\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCons:
  • Lack of explanation. The game does not explain the deeper mechanics it has. As such, it does not really allow you to play strategically until you go back and forth between tutorial and whatever you are currently doing (this is tedious, see next point).
  • The UI is terrible. Everything needs twice to three times the clicks it should. Essential infos are not explained via tooltips. Too much time is spent clicking through menus.
  • Moving around units and attacking enemies does not feel strategic. The encounters are not designed in a way that positioning or strategy is really meaningful (up to the point I've played so far). Blindly attacking enemies with decent squads seems to be sufficient to get through the game at normal difficulty.
  • There seem to be no real synergy between adjacent squads. I have also not noticed any gameplay changes based on relationship status.
  • Dialogues are very awkward.
Pros:
  • Squad-building is fun. This is the part where I actually feel like the game has a strategic side to it.
  • The plot seems to be alright.

The battles are enjoyably strategic and can be focused in different directions depending on your builds. With the addition of one Gold rank mercenary I took a squad that was always one of the first to die into the beefiest high damage high defense squads in my whole roster. I just got on Chapter 17 so I think I am somewhere around midway and it just introduced a new mechanic to the battles for when I go back in.
Cannot recommend this enough; the squads heavily rely on how you make them up with and build out the various units, the battles are satisfying and if you plan poorly you get get crushed by some of the units (especially those fricking gunners, and the story is a grandiose and fueled by the battles of the war that you are raging,
Also dragons, I raised dragons stuck them with a healer with an affinity for them, now they both on their 3rd rank and they can a squad in seconds. Plus I smiled really big when someone would not listen to reason and I called them an oaf and told them they they will die being the lapdog of a traitor. I look forward to see what will happen next in the drama between, after, and during the battles.\t\t\t\t\t
