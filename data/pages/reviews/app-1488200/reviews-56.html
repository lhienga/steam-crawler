The difficulty was never unmanageable, it gets a little too easy towards the end if you don't play with permadeath, but at the same time, it probably becomes a lot more stressful if you do and one of the best things for me was how relaxing it was. The story is pretty much what you'd expect from the genre, but it is good, like getting a burger from your favorite place and being just how you like it.

I am genuinely looking forward to sequels/DLC, and I hope they bring about even more customization options; one thing I'd like to see in particular is more ways of making generic units into "heroes". \r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay: 10/10 - The gameplay feels solid and engaging, offering a truly immersive experience.

Graphics: 2/10 - In my opinion, graphics do not matter significantly, but it's worth mentioning that the visuals in this game are not high quality.

Tactics: 9/10 - I spent a considerable amount of time managing my army and carefully planning tactics to avoid losing any valuable soldiers in battle. However, despite my best efforts, many fell, which added to the challenge.

Storyline: 7/10 - The game's storyline is engaging enough to keep you hooked. It is well-explained, avoiding overly complicated schemes that could confuse players.

Difficulty Scalability: I played the game on the highest difficulty setting with permadeath enabled. This added a new layer of enjoyment, as I found myself valuing my units and each move more. The game is not excessively hard, and even if you lose a significant portion of your army, you can still recruit and train new soldiers to fill the gaps. However, it's worth noting that if you play poorly with permadeath enabled, you may find yourself in a soft lock situation, where restarting the campaign becomes the best option.

Overall, Symphony of War: The Nephilim Saga offers a challenging and immersive gameplay experience, despite its shortcomings in graphics. The game's tactical depth, engaging storyline, and the ability to customize difficulty provide a fulfilling adventure for strategy enthusiasts.
