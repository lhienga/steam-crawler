\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was a huge fan of Final Fantasy Tactics and played the original Tactics Ogre. Over the years I've seen a lot of games claim to be FFT-like or call themselves tactical games and none of them ever really lived up to my expectation of what a true spiritual successor to FFT would be. Oftentimes they will fail on the tactical side, other times they will fail on their job system. Most of the time they have a story that just isn't interesting. SoW succeeds to match FFT on all fronts. It might be 25 years later, but I finally found a game that has reached that level of expectation and even surpassed it.

The story is quite good without being filled with tedious dialogues and has enough twists and turns without being a convoluted mess like a few others I've tried. The main characters are well thought out and have their own niches without being pigeonholed into certain play styles due to squad complexity.

The gameplay is amazing and, while definitely different from FFT, has a unique design that will have you spending time between fights just organizing and optimizing your squads for the most effective output. It wasn't uncommon for me to spend an hour or more between certain chapters doing such reorgnization with the unique artifact and job systems in the game. I never felt like I was wasting my time doing this, either.

The only one complaint I would maybe have about the game is that some early missions are very easy on normal difficulty. If you are tactical RPG veteran, you probably should play on a higher difficulty as the AI is kind of easy to "game" once you learn how it works. There isn't a need to play an easier difficulty, IMHO, Normal is easy enough.

At its current price point, it's very impressive what Dancing Dragon has managed to put together here and I'm very much looking forward to the DLC, which I hear is basically a whole new game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinished the story quest after purchasing the Legends DLC.

Pros:
- Character design is fantastic. Most characters are memorable and interesting enough, and games like these remind me why I love games with beautiful characters.
- Army management is pretty good - Story characters can be squad leaders or soldiers in a squad and random NPCs you buy can be squad leaders, giving you a lot of choices.
- Unit variety is good enough - medics, elemental mages, gunners, knights, crossbowmen and everything in between makes for plenty of choices in what kind of a squad will you have.
- Combat feels good enough, nothing insane but still good.
- Sound design is fine for a pixelated game - honestly, better than what I imagined and the soundtrack is okay.
- The whole "Bonded" system was unexpected but a welcome add to an already rather complete game, which adds a (small) reason to do multiple playthroughs.
- Quite a few traits and artifacts to choose from further allows you to customize your squads; there are quite a few of them.

Cons:
- Story is just fine. With so much media having the same copy-pasted story where we are the heroes, this game feels very similar to them in most ways. As a personal opinion, I did not enjoy the ending too much either so while the story would be meh by default there were a few hiccups for me, and the ending somewhat brought it down quite a bit so I'll rate it as a con.
- Combat AI is questionable at times, which makes certain units worse. A good example is the Blademaster, dealing high damage with a double-hit - issue is if they have a single unit in the front row and 3 units in the back row, all your Blademasters can target the 1st unit and overkill them by insane amounts of damage. Makes Blademaster squads extremely clunky to use because while they can eliminate entire rows, sometimes they'll just focus one enemy.
- Army management can be a bit too workful at times, with you needing to have roughly 16-20 squads by the last chapter, and each unit being customizable in a squad (and each squad can have up to 9 units) customizing each individual unit can be rough; so while I personally liked the game, I'm not sure I'll be doing a New Game+ due to all the clicking.
- Conversations and the Bonded system felt incomplete in a way. With so many characters, I fully expected there to be a lot more interactions between them - and not having too many choices of whom do we want to bond with was mildly disappointing (Yes, the lack of bonding option with Beatrix did kind of ruin my playthrough so my MC died a virgin, RIP homie). For something that's not mandatory to do and more flavor than anything I would fully expect there to easily be from 2x to 5x the amount of conversations the game had between chapters.

Overall it's a fun game. It's nothing crazy and it shouldn't really get you addicted, but if you like pixelated games or Army management RPGs this game is fun - although I'd recommend you wait for a sale, because 20$ is a decent chunk.

And if you want to "bond" with redheads that can rain down meteor showers, prepare to be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

The first half of the game was slow with a boring story, but with a fun gameplay loop and exciting upgrades. The seconds half of the game had a more interesting but clunky story and just became a race to the finish when almost every battle became easy, troops get maxed out, resources are abundant, and enemy turns take forever because there are so many weak units crowding around the same targets or pinch points. I kept accumulating items that could have been helpful but I was reluctant to use because the constant new flow of story characters or new units and story events that split your army made me feel like there was little reason to develop individual units for certain roles, so I ended up with a loaded and useless inventory at the end of the game. When I did create unique and powerful units, the difference from a freshly purchased unit was difficult to notice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing blend of older FE games and the Ogre Battles squad system. The base game is already a lot of fun, but the latest DLC brings a lot of new stuff to play around with. Lunatic is great even as a first playthrough if you want to really struggle and be challenged, I would say most will enjoy warlord though as it is still challenging but not overwhelming like lunatic can sometimes be. Playing around with the squad's unit compositions is probably the most fun i've ever had in a tactical game like this and it really feels like you command an army rather than just 10 or 13 characters like Fire Emblem games can be. The story isn't really anything to write home about, but it can definitely be enjoyable if you ignore how predictable it can be at times. Just don't think about it too hard and enjoy it like a superhero movie. Overall I'd highly recommend the game if you can get it on sale for fans of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
