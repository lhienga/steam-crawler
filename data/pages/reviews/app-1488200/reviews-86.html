\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSTORY
Solid story about a hero saving the world. A bit hasty writing, a bit more text here and there would help explain the story better. Certain parts of the story feel rushed. The relationship conversation is one example. Imagine siblings not seeing each other for a very long time and after reuniting they don't exchange a word - ever. Kinda silly.

GRAPHICS
Top-notch.

GAMEPLAY
A mix of Advance Wars, Yggdra Union and Fire Emblem. Enjoyable, easy to get into and varied enough to keep you playing. The game is separated into chapters and between these you get the chance to tinker with your army and buy new stuff. There are 3 - 4 unit upgrades + extra "upgrade" with certain item.
Enemy AI is a bit basic (within range → ATTACK), but overall it's not a big issue since it's a war game, so barbaric tendencies kinda fit. One issue that is annoying is spoiling "plot twists" in mission objectives - like survive enemy X while it's not on map yet.

MUSIC
Heavy Valkyria Chronicles vibes during mission briefings. Overall very good.

SUMMARY
Great game. A bit rough around the edges but still solid experience. Brings back old-school memories.
I recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

While it's not any of these games it brings back the nostalgia and just great elements that those games created for so many people. On it's own it stands proud and is not only a steal for the price but you'll wish there was a sequel so you could shell out some more of your hard earned cash to be in the middle of the Neph war again. ( Minor Spoiler ahead) I loved the Characters and Diana is one of the best paladins portrayed I've ever seen. I wish there were more story interactions, maybe even a little Fire Emblems awakening where I could play the children of the couples in the story you chose, but altogether what an amazing game. Honestly my favorite Indie title ever passing outward if you could call that one an Indie. Would love to see these awesome developers take a crack at a spiritual successor with their own flavor of the old game Brigandine. Rip to Zorro and thanks for the message at the end!
