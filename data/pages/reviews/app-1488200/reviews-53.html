\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is definitely not perfect, but I sincerely enjoyed it. And it gets points for doing some things that other games aren't.

No one really makes games with Ogre Battle-style combat anymore, and I really miss that sort of thing. This game absolutely does it well. It's fun to build units, it's fun to customize them and try out new things, and it's fun to see the big groups of people flying into battle with each other. There are plenty of ways to break the system and get some super powerful stuff going, but that's the nature of this kind of game.

I really appreciate the fact that the game lets you convince enemy units to surrender. That's not a feature I've seen anywhere else, and I love the fact that I actually DON'T have to kill everyone to win the battle.

The story has interesting elements, but they're not fully realized. Late in the game, they do tackle things like what it means to build a new nation, how a hero can become as bad as a villain, and so on. But since most of the plot is a fight against obviously evil cultists while grabbing magic artifacts, those plot elements never get a chance to truly shine.

But yeah, overall, it's very solid as an indie title. Big hats off to the team for making something cool.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played...a(n) (un)healthy amount of Ogre Battle 64, I can highly recommend this game. It is an auto-battler like Ogre Battle, so if you're expecting to take control of every unit's decisions, that's not what this is. What it is, is a hybrid of Ogre Battle, and some other strategy RPGs like Advanced Wars and older Fire Emblem. How you assembly your squads and use them on the battlefield is crucial. I like the leadership statistic that determines how many members you can have in a squad.

The UI overall isn't the most intuitive in the world, but it makes do. I wish I could mouse-over the traits because I often forget what they do. The character portraits are very good, I love the sprite animations, and overall the presentation is worthy of an Atlus product. The relationships are a bit odd and feel a bit shoe-horned in, but that's fine. I just want to play the game, story is nice to have, but the battle system shines. The game doesn't feel bogged down by story or subplots.

Definitely give this game a try. Indie games are killing it.\t\t\t\t\t
