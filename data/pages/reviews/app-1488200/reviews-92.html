However, the story, lore, plot and characters are extremely poorly written.
When asked on the discord server about the possibility of having a wider inclusivity in further episodes of the game, I was confronted with the dev, who was very angry, sarcastic, and stating he already included 2 female options for a female MC.
What he failed to see is how those 2 options are extremely sexualised female characters who offer nothing more than fan service. For the corresponding audience.

No same-sex male options, no room for open gender rules, and an extremely bigot game dev.

I am very sad I actually bought this game to 2 friends on sale apart from myself, I definitely wouldn't have wanted to spend any money on this had I known what was to come.

Buy on big sale or don't buy at all. I am sure with the poor publicity the game is getting they will be giving it for free at some point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
Challenging scenarios that never got boring. I played the Captain level and it felt like the right level of difficulty. Definitely recommend the "no permadeath" setting.
Stefan and Diana's romance was tear-jerker good. Narima and Jules was pretty good. Jules didn't deserve her ;)

Cons (hopefully constructive criticism?) *Warning, a little spoilers, but I tried to not give away too much*:
Not many options for the player story-wise. Really felt like I was just along for the ride, not making any decisions besides romance. Not really sure why so many people trusted MC so much in the beginning (like Zelos and Diana). Did she get semi-famous for her performance in tactics at the academy or something?
It has the feeling that there was a lot of story discussion between the authors and the authors assumed that people playing the game would know a lot more about where the story was going than was actually revealed. Maybe more "internal monologue" of the MC would help to get more world lore.
It seemed like there was a lot more to the story of Edward Pendergast than was revealed. How he managed to convince Zelos to go on a killing campaign, for example, and why Beatrix wanted to kill him a thousand times over even though he gave her anything she wanted. Even if he didn't love her, why did that make her want to kill him so bad (versus be indifferent)?
I'd like to know what made Lysander so convinced that MC was going down tyrant path. Just because she lost her cool once because she had PTSD (for good reason)? Why did Lysander follow the emperor for as long as he did? Why did he think Zanatus wasn't a threat that would require Lightfury when the cultists were clearly being powered by something sinister? Why did he feel the need to contact Jebediah in the first place in secret?
Zelos confused me. I had my MC romance him because she seemed to have a thing for him and I was hoping for more story, but it ended up kinda clingy and gross. Why did she love him so much when he had a lot of issues? Just because he got revenge on kids in secret for her sake? Why did he like her to begin with?
Jules kept saying "Yes, my lord." Even though my main character was a she.
How did Narima coordinate so much to make Jules king, even though she was a slave?
Didn't really see the point in the dragon riders. No real difference between them and the adult elemental dragons.
Not really sure what the point of the intro questions was? In Ogre battle, those questions determined your starting unit composition.
I'd like a "retreat" option during the gameplay cutscenes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros
Excellent turn based strategy. I played with perma-death on which made it a fun and challenging game. This allowed me to develop and play with different strategies and troop positions in order to increase survival chance. To add, this game has many missions, with a variety of ways of beating them (i.e: using assassin or scout squads to fight in the forest areas or cavalry to attack in the open areas, plainly rushing enemy or using slow pace optimal positioning strats etc...). If you don't feel like your troops are strong enough then you can do optional arena missions when you get them. The story is pretty linear with some opportunities to choose your relationships or "bonds" that impacts game-play positively.
Cons
-Text based game, so if you're not into reading then this game is not for you
-In most missions, you can cheese enemy path-finding to only attack your troops, instead of rushing base (This may be a positive or negative depending on how you see it). Makes defending base useless.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing strategy game, with a heavy focus on squad management. Each "unit" in your army is comprised of 1-9 individual soldiers, which you place into various formations. Each soldier has a class which dictates their actions in combat, as well an attribute and traits which modify their stats. You can also give artifacts to an entire squad, which give them all benefits (and sometimes penalties).

These systems combine to make managing your army really interesting. You have so much freedom in how you make your units, units which constantly evolve as the game progresses via unlocking new classes and unit types. Mages protected by a tanky frontline, Assassins jumping to the backline with just enough frontline to survive reprisals, Cavalry carrying Spearmen to defeat other cavalry, and so much more. Seriously, I think like half the fun in this game is coming up with squad ideas and then refining them.

My only complains for the game are very minor. Difficulty can be inconsistent, particularly later on. There will be a bunch of easy chapters in a row, followed by a big difficulty spike. Maps are often very frontloaded; the start is often near overwhelming but once you get through it the rest of the map will be pretty devoid of enemies. I never thought I would be saying this, but I think more reinforcements would be a good idea to flesh out the later parts of a map.

Overall I thoroughly enjoyed my time with this game, and would recommend it to anyone who is interested in Fire Emblem/Langrisser games, or anyone who enjoys strategy games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed the versatility and range of various classes. The mechanics can take a little bit to understand. For example, cavalry have an "unspoken" resistance to archers etc.. I couldn't put the game down for the first 10 chapters. Very nostalgic feel to old FE like Sacred Stones, which is my favorite FE game. There are many combinations to choose from when making a squad. The gameplay is great at the normal difficulty. I feel as though it is very easy to lose troops if you aren't careful, and ugh I hated losing troops to ANY enemy, even if that individual squad was attacked like 6 times.

Things I loved:
-Good balance between "technology" vs. "magic"
-Story
-Unique maps
-Very satisfying magic animations
-Almost total freedom in composition of squads
-Easy to learn system with tutorials (some reading) - they also have videos on Youtube (Dancing Dragon Games)

Things that I thought could be improved:
-Bonding/Relationship system
-The Champion class is strong and designed very cool, but his single target strategy made me feel like he wasn't worth the cost.
-There doesn't seem to be a point in ever utilizing classes like dragoons (just get the regular gunmen), centurions (just get a sentinel or champion), crossbowmen (warbows), mounted archers (warbows are built different), or scouts (cavalry just does better).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of grid based strategy games, you'll like this. I'm a big fan of Fire Emblem and while this was different, it was equally as enjoyable. For such a small group of people, they really created a very solid game! My first playthru was ~50hrs, but you could get through it in 30-35 I'd think.

Pros
-Music
-Unit Design/Variety
-Combat System
-Story

Cons
-More/Clearer combat details.
Even with the tutorial option you can choose between fights, I found myself going to their youtube channel to look up certain mechanics. There are numerous, smaller mechanics that can have a large affect on your gameplay and decision making. An example of this would be the charge/ambush mechanic. Took me half the game to figure out how it worked.

I really enjoyed this game and would highly recommend it!

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
