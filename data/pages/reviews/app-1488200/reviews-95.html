\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8/10: Solid Fire Emblem-esque game that clearly draws inspiration from many greats in the genre (e.g. supports, arena, Job system) but doesn't fully flesh out or embrace some of those features.

Good:
+ The various systems combine in a way that gives a good amount of variety to your army. You can change things like affinities, traits, items the squad uses, and jobs to differentiate units.
+ Maps vary in size but generally provide a range from cramped castle corridors to sweeping plains. Battles do feel large-scale even if it can be a meatgrinder at times.

Mediocre:
o The UI can be a little convoluted and doesn't always offer the information you are looking for when you need it, or at all.
o Side Quests are offered at one point in the game but never again.

Bad:
- The story is about average for this type of game but is linear and offers little in the way of replayability. There are a handful of points in the story that could benefit from some sort of branching decision.
- The variety of systems are either limited or provide the illusion of choice. For example, the tech tree appears to offer a large selection, but few of the techs really change the way you play and all techs are available ~2/3 into the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm giving this game a thumbs up because it did capture my attention for almost 60 hours however, I hope the dev team can crank out a sequel that surpasses the original Ogre Battle instead of one that can't even match a thirty year old game.

Seeing the gameplay trailer and having played the original Ogre Battle as well as Ogre Battle 64, I was very excited to pick this game up. Unfortunately my expectations that it would be similar to the legacy games left me disappointed. In the end the game falls short and is in fact a lesser clone of those twenty and thirty year old titles.

Pros:

-Exciting to see another title with the same mechanics as the beloved Ogre Battle line.

Neutral:

-Story line was ok.

Cons:

-Starts off the same as Ogre Battle having you make a series of choices presumably to mold your character's alignment along with a handful of other things. False. These choices mean nothing to the game.
-Unit placement within the group means little to nothing. Front row, back row all attacks are the same.
-No map easter eggs whatsoever.
-Beast units outside of dragons are non existent.
-Unit graduation options and type numbers fall short of even the original Ogre Battle.
-Items blow
-Tech tree is dumb and ends up making you wait to unlock simple battlefield tactics.
-Those tactics mean nothing once you do get them.
-Units don't heal in towns until you unlock the tech halfway through the game.

In conclusion, I appreciate the effort the development team put in but was let down by the execution. If you're thinking about buying this game, just go back and play one of the Ogre Battle titles instead.
