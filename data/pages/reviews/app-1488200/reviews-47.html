\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI could go on and on about how obsessed I've become with the game mechanics and army management stuff, but the missions themselves are so well done. I've never really been one for pixel games, but this game single handily proved to me that pixel games can be up there with my favorite titles like God of war, Kotor, Total War.

In terms of just how much fun I'm having around with the game mechanics and building my army 10/10.The pictures make it look like there's a massive learning curve, but honestly it's pretty seemless after mission 2 if you're spending about 10 or so minutes in between missions to just browse the army management stuff. Eventually you'll randomly realize that you've been spending damn near a half an hour in the army menu. The missions get better and better. I usually have something bad to say about most games. I am damn near trying to find something wrong with this game but the only thing I got is only the things I wish were in it like decisions mattering and it being voiced. For people who don't like to read, this isn't for you.

I wish a sequel would come out exactly like this, just having decisions matter and having factions in the campaign you could like...idk be a mercenary for or something and maybe having a good/evil kinda thing with it. Evil decisions draw evil allies and good decisions good allies type of thing. World changes based off your decisions would be cool too. Cause this is just good. Plain and simple. Price isn't high, story good [although if you play a lot of games, the story will seem familiar, but it's still delivered well], resource management feels great, army management is God tier.

You play this game right now and just play 3 or 4 chapters without ADHD [I.e skipping dialogue, not even touching your army management stuff and then complaining the game is hard etc etc] and if you don't like it after that, you won't like it. Not everything is for everyone I get that. However, if you're waiting for a chance to be proven that Indie games can feel like AAA titles. This game might convince of you that. On mission 5, that's where starts to get real. You will probably have to retreat and reform your army multiple times after seeing what you're up against.\t\t\t\t\t
