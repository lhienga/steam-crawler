\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA true indie strategy masterpiece!

It is a strategy game in the line of Final Fantasy Tactics, Advance Wars, and Fire Emblem. The missions are fairly balanced, varied, and provide new challenges along the way. But what really puts it into a class of its own is the army-building phase. It is a very addictive challenge that has you constructing squads around various hero characters (or even non-hero characters).

There is a large variety of unit types and classes and the game encourages experimenting -- pouring resources into a particular upgrade is refunded if you want to demote and try a different path for a unit. The classes an types are fairly balanced and you can often find unexpected niches -- a pair of light infantry squads I have hit hard and specialise assaulting up walls.

Units vary not just in their traditional skills, but their loyalty and leadership abilities. Loyal troops are cheaper to field, meaning you can build a larger squad. But leadership also matters! The higher a leadership score of the squad captain, the more troops they can command. Good leaders also make for good mentors, raising the leadership score of their followers. This means you'll often find yourself spinning off units that have "mentored" with your best leaders to form their own squads. Then there are items, traits, and artifacts that allow you to further supplement/customise squads into niche roles.

I liked the story and the characters and there were several twists and turns to keep you engaged in wanting to know what happens next, though the quality of the combat and the unit-building are the real stars of the game.

An amazing game, can't wait to see what's next from Dancing Dragon Games!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGames like this make me wish for a "maybe recommend" option. This is the kind of game you will play once or twice and never remember again. It is by no means a bad game, but it's also not an amazing one either.

Graphics: 6/10 I played with the art upgrade, and I think the character designs fit their respective areas. Every sprite had a level of detail put into it. That said, many of the unit sprites (including other story characters) look exactly the same as your generic army units. It becomes more noticeable in later levels when you field a large amount of units. The nice character portraits clash with the RPGmaker sprites that make up most story events.

Story 4/10: A young recruit in his kingdom's army is tasked with dealing with a former hero turned rebel. What he learns on the way will soon involve him and his friends in the fate of the entire world. Does it sound like many other stories out there? It's because the plot is incredibly weak and serves to get the players from map to map. The game does add it's own glossary for the lore, but the plot does little to make you interested in it. Even the few twists in the story come across as unexciting.

Characters: 3/10: to put it bluntly, the characters are very flat and very forgettable. Most have little (if any) dialogue during the main story. While the game tries to add side events during the chapters, the characters remain bland and two dimensional. You see little development of them aside from a few love (bond) scenes and events where people become friends. Some major characters have less dialogue than this paragraph.

Music: 6/10: There is nothing to write home about. The music fits for the environment, and the game does have multiple tracks for different levels and battles. While none of the music is bad, most of it is not memorable either.

Gameplay: 8/10: The thing that makes this game worth playing is the gameplay. The player controls customizable squads made up of a leader character and whatever other units you choose. Each squad has a high level of customization from the units in it, the skills you can assign to them, and where you position them on the battle grid. Unit types also matter along with their placement. You can protect your fragile spell casters by putting heavy infantry in the front lines. Certain classes (rogues) can counter this by attacking the back lines, and ranged classes also have this benefit. Your leader starts with limits to how many people can join their squad, but this number increases as they win battles and take locations. You can also upgrade classes into more powerful versions as you progress. While promotional classes have stat requirements, there are items that let you boost and change your character. You can also equip skills and artifacts that allow you to further tweak your characters and their squads.

Challenge: 6/10: The game does some things well, and other things poorly. The game does very well with terrain. Elevated positions allow your ranged characters to shoot farther, Forests can allow certain classes to ambush. There are buildings on the map that have different effects (such as a temple allowing you to revive fallen units). You can also make broken squads surrender for extra exp and end of battle funds. The army eventually gets access to powers that can also help (such as teleporting units or giving a character a second turn). Maps also had a level of variety with things such as choke points and variations to map design.

That said, the game is very hit or miss with difficulty. It's very easy for a few of the main characters to become extremely powerful without any exploits. It is feasible to use the armor blessing on a squad, send it into a group of 8 enemies, and watch you squad wipe the floor with everyone when they attack on the enemy phase. Aside from the last chapter and a few legends chapters, most of the games missions are incredibly easy to S rank. The game does have things like increased difficulty settings and permadeath for non heroes, but the game is still easy to manage if you properly build your squads and utilize basic tactics.

Overall, I would say this is an enjoyable game for people looking for a tactical RPG with some good customization options. The gameplay is one of the few things that makes this game worth finishing. People looking for engaging story and characters are sure to be disappointed.

Final Score 5.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
