\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSymphony of War is an amazing game that is well worth your time. It has an blend of Ogrebattle 64 and Fire Emblem that you don't see very often. The combat is as easy or as complex as you want to make it and there is no shortage of interesting campaign missions to play through. It's items and artifact system allow you to customize your armies to fit your play style, and again it can be as deep or shallow as you want it to be. The main story of the game is really well written for the first 80% of the game, but as with a lot of games they struggle to really stick the ending. By the end of the game it felt like they were rushing to a conclusion and not really giving any of the events time to breath, but this is only a minor detractor as the story still landed pretty well for me. The writing also came up short when it came to relationships with your party. In a few cases I saw multiple conversations that had progression from one conversation to the next, but most cases the conversations felt disjointed and in some cases had absolutely zero build up. In the romantic relationship I sought for my character I had no idea if the option for a romantic relationship would even pop. When it did it seemed to be irrelevant to the main story. There was also a huge gap in missions between private conversations actually happening. This could have been alleviated by having a few conversations after each deployment, and this would have allowed the relationships between the characters to feel more natural. I would have also liked to have seen more from the surrender system, like a system that allows you to try and capture leaders and then interrogate them, this would have given them a great way to sprinkle in another something that is another complaint of mine. Not enough side missions and they aren't spread out enough. But again these really are all minor gripes that I hope are addressed to some extent in a sequel. It really can't be overstated how good this game is. And maybe that is because to some extent I had very low expectations, but this game really knocked it out of the park. I would gladly pay $70 for another game in the series. It doesn't even need to follow this story it could be a whole new story in a whole new land. Either way they have earned my attention and I look forward to playing more from them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a fun turn-based strategy game that hit that itch that Fire Emblem/Advance Wars did.

Assembling a unit with different soldiers (and heroes you get as the story progress), thinking up your own combination of units and watch them all grow up as they reach the requirements for the next class and becomes even stronger is what drove me the most to keep playing (Dragons especially needs a lot of babysitting early on, but they really do pay off once you're done raising them).

And kudos for the devs to make a tech tree that actually DOES things. So many games in recent years has made tech trees with the most boring effects, like 10% more of this or that. Or extremely situational bonuses that really does ♥♥♥♥ all (both Cyberpunk and Darktide had these kind of god awful talent trees that only had major overhauls some week ago).

Tech tree unlocks units, gives you new styles of attacking (Do less damage, but take a lot less damage until next turn. A vice versa, focus on a unit's leader character (whom if they die that unit drops a ton of morale)). And many passive bonuses, one big game changer for example is where Mage units can cast their spell right away when attacking, instead of needing to charge up for one turn.

I enjoyed the characters but the story wasn't much. The gameplay is definitely the reason to play this game. The story really becomes messy in the last 3rd of the game with a attempts of twists and turns the story didn't need (Keeping a story simple sometimes is for the best).

Some units, unfortunately, are just worse than others. While I thought the Samurai was gonna be a nice all-rounder since it works as both a spear unit and an archer, having a unit full of them was one of my weakest units in my playthrough. Light Cavalry also was one unit that felt useless in my hands compared to the AI (on Warlord difficulty, second highest).

The early-mid game you are limited on resources, which you need for certain class upgrades. This is a fun thing to balance out on which units you feel you need more now and which can wait for later when you have access to more of the 6 different resources. If you have an abundance of one you can trade that for something you're lacking of at a set (equal) rate. When you demote a character that you spent resources on you get those resources back. This is a good thing as a class might not play how you expected to and wish your character was something else. Near the end of the game you will have an abundance of resources as long as you bought them every time marketplace refreshed and captured the resource nodes on the battle maps.

The game is a lot of fun. If you're a story playing kinda guy one playthrough will be enough. If you're the kinda person that loves pushing the limits, the NG+ mode and optional rules (permadeath to non-story character) will let you sink even more hours into the game without the need of starting fresh.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNeutral.

I picked negative to balance out the overwhelmingly positive reviews rating.

GOOD STUFF
- The most important: the army building is neat with plenty of variety, items and skills to optimize and roflstomp the game (in normal difficulty at least). It is as addictive as it is effective.
- Good looking sprites for the army
- Music and sound effects are fine and do their job

SO-SO
- Appropriate but somewhat cheap special effects, it gets bad during some cutscenes towards the end of the game with a particular effect overused and upscaled BIG to look like a pixelated mess. It feels most of them came from a special FX asset pack.
- Portraits art style do not fit with sprites art style
- UI works but is cumbersome to use, lots of click to do certain stuff, especially when looking for the optimal unit to allocate a skill item or stat boosters
- Objectives during battles are glorified treasure chests (functionnally they are no different than your good ol' treasure chests), don't worry there are a lot of actual treasure chests too!
- Full screen upscaling gives an acceptable result, but could use polish, it would look much better with some XBRZ shader IMO.

THE BAD
- Cutscenes are using sprites and tilesets suspiciously similar to RPGMaker. It doesn't help that the overall quality of these cutscenes is low. It feels like SoW creators have subcontracted cutscenes implementation to other developers.
- After a couple of chapters, I get so much ahead of the curve power wise (in normal difficulty mode) that I can mindlessly play the battles by sending my god squads ahead and decimate most enemy squads during Enemy Phase. This is true for ALL chapters except a particular chapter you gotta lose. In Fire Emblem you can make god units, but you need to support them and watch out for their counters. Here, god squads can infinitely self-sustain themselves so there's absolutely zero tension in play.

THE HORRIBLE
- Story starts innocent enough, kinda dull but whatever. Pikachu face when the obviously evil guy does some backstabbing evil stuff. Then after half of the game, it degenerates into some kind of fanfic with Mary Sue god mode™ characters I gave no ♥♥♥♥ about. The game then tries to pull some "B1G REVEAL" stuff that fell flat on me as I just yawned through the RPG Maker cutscenes. Support conversation are as bad.\t\t\t\t\t

Solid game, absolutely loved it, highly recommended.

There is no single units (intentionally) and so the game is more then just Spear beat horse, beat arrow - Each squad can have up to 9 units depending on the leader. You can mix them up in many different ways, and as you play you'll level of various aspects of the units, as well as stuff like - healing every round in a village tile.

I've replayed some levels multiple times just to try to get a better outcome. Lot s of objectives and incentive to do well gets you rewarded heavily. Grab everything, kill eveything, and do it time? you just a nice amount of XP for your tech tree.

Its really good, this is better then fireemblem.

Story is decent lots of twists.. I would prefer more games in this genre would try to have branching paths (i'm 18 chapters in and its a linear storyline with no real choices yet). I find you are just resolving to win or survive but you never get to choose where to go next.

if you yearn for some classic turn based tactical RPGness this is the way to go .\t\t\t\t\t
