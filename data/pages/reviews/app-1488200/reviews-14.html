\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a great game this is.

I happily did sink a lot of hours into it, restarting my Ludicrous campaign as I learned new mechanics or better ways to optimise.

Because yes this game has a lot of that, optimisation, min-maxing, and complex party building. All the systems of the game allow you to build your army the way you like and there is way more depths to it than it can first seem to. For instance, if you jump into this game after looking a bit at guides or party compositions, you will see that almost all of them skip on spear men. But boy oh boy can they be deadly if properly built. And that is just an example.

Of course, all of the above only matters if you plan on playing the hardest difficulty, Ludicrous. If you wish to enjoy a fine tactical RPG, chill while overtaking fortresses along to nice music and cute pixel art, or perhaps even take on a nostalgia trip from when you used to play old school tactical RPGs on your favourite portable console, this game is for you aswell.

The game is made with heart, that much you can tell pretty quickly, and it is enjoyable from start to finish. That fact matters a lot in the new wave gaming industry era we live in, so thanks for that devs. The DLC is also a great addition as it adds units diversity and some well needed side missions to explore further specific aspects of the story.

I know it's development has only started as of recently, but I already cannot wait for Symphony of War 2 !\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall great game that's fire emblem-esque whilst featuring an impressive degree of army customisation options from troop compositions to artifacts, individual traits and movement types. Without permadeath the game is fairly easy to beat even on Warlord difficulty but I'm sure I'd be singing a very different tune if I had turned permadeath on. However I'm not a masochist.

The game is a tactical turn based strategy game where you control a set of squads led by heroes or some random mercenary you picked off the merchants and move your troops around to fight enemy armies and complete objectives. The strategy and combat component of the game is great with almost every unit type having some sort of utility (though some are definitely objectively better than others). Army compositions matters but there is a lot of flexibility in how you design your squads. They can all conform to one niche to maximise their specialisation, or you could throw together a chaotic group of misfits with different roles to maximise their flexibility. Chapters can range anywhere from jaw droppingly easy to incredibly challenging without much consistency, especially if you need to try and conform to turn limits to maximise your score for the chapter.

The two things I found quite jarring about the game is the AI and the story. While the AI has its strong points, I often find that it tends to blatantly ignore certain aspects of the game that I would think are pretty important. For instance I often see cavalry smacking themselves against my spear-dominated squads specifically designed to destroy them rather than my squishier light infantry squads when they have the choice of both. I think this is a symptom of the fact that the AI is essentially forced to attack anything that enters its range regardless of whether or not its a favourable encounter, but I would often catch myself shaking my head at how utterly incompetent the AI can be at times.

The story is also what I would say is quite underwhelming. It's a classic case of plot points coming out of the blue with little to no build up when some of them are pretty damn important, and while there were aspects I appreciated about the story, it becomes very confusing by the end of the game even if you try to keep up. Lots of things happen without explanation and often leave me scratching my head or going 'that's it?' when it happens. I think the most jarring examples include instances where enemy generals you fight suddenly decide to swap sides once you beat them when literally seconds before I pressed the 'attack' button you were rattling off on how I spilt the blood of thousands of your countrymen and how they would wreak vengeance. I mean, really?

Story and funky AI notwithstanding, the game is still fantastic in a lot of regards and there really isn't much like it. In spite of the story itself being underwhelming, the developers clearly spent a lot of time crafting the lore of this world and its political strife and history, which I can appreciate even if the main plot was a bit off base. Overall, the gameplay loop is engrossing and the encounters are both intriguing and entertaining to play through, just don't expect a groundbreaking narrative to follow with it.\t\t\t\t\t
