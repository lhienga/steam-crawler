\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery much like Ogre Battle, but a few things I like better:
1. Turn based maps - Ogre Battle was a bit stressful having to pause, give direction to all your units, and then try to watch the whole field at the same time.
2. No hidden items on tiles in battle - I like to just play a game and not have to pre-plan every single move before I make it based on Internet guides.
3. Straightforward class promotion system here as opposed to chasing rare items for needed endgame classes.

The game also feels like it has great design of class balance and tools (powers) to be able to play the game at just the right difficulty, but still having some emergency buttons to avoid getting you hard stuck in case you make a couple of bad moves. Overall very fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper underrated little gem, very addictive strategic game.

The story is naff and full of tropes so I will ignore that part as frankly I started skipping it half way - however the gameplay is where the meat of this game comes into play. Acts like a classic strategy game not too dissimilar from Advance Wars or FF Tactics, with charming retro graphics and unique spins of forming your own army and squads.
What hooks you is the sheer variety of options with creating your squads as time goes by, making well-oiled machines of war that can then be structured to beat different combinations of enemies. And as the game goes on it avoids getting stale early by always adding something new, be it in early game when you start getting cavalry and rifles, to late when you now have dragons and mages and necromancers (with the dlc added), to adding artefacts changing squads and the godly powers you receive.

It took be 50+ hours to beat the main story and I had a blast, beyond the actual plot being a bit meh my only over complaint is that the difficulty curve does drastically decline over time, the later chapters rarely felt too difficult and it was only the odd occasion I found myself needing to re-equip my squad. This may change with new game+ and I hope that difficulty is added with a replay.

Overall though well worth the buy for strategy fans who want a light game to play with a nostalgic feel\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDid I enjoy this? Yes. Would I recommend this game? It depends, but for most folks, no.

I'm actually the target audience of this game as it scratches the squad management itchfrom the early ogre battles. And that's basically what this game offers: arrange and develop individual units and form cohesive squads. If you enjoy that type of play, you will like this game.

However, other things you may expect out of a turn based tactics game are not that great.
Story? Very meh.
Battlefield tactics? Nothing very interesting really. Super barebones compared with others in the genre.
UI/Information layout? Awkward at times. For example, as the whole game resolve around growing your individual "units" and tailor them to the role you want them to do, the game doesn't do a good job at telling you how attribute growth works at all. And also unit scales very differently into late game, potentially landing you in some traps (e.g. do pick up dragons!).

Those said, once I understand the tools available, how different unit work together and start getting more artifacts, I did have a blast at managing/growing my squads.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are a lot of things to like about this game. The gameplay, the mechanics, are all fun and even fantastic! I enjoyed building squads and experimenting with artifacts; I thought there was a great variety of units and unit types that allow for a great experience with lots of customization.

The rest of the game was.... not great. Putting aside the RPG maker story segments, which were visually mediocre or offputting, the story dropped the ball on what would otherwise be a solid game. The plot is strange and almost nonsensical; events happen and more or less follow from one another but... there's not really any consistency beyond that? The game waffles on taking any kind of moral stance. Eugenics, breeding programs, extra judicially executing surrendered POWs, total war in the actual definition... these are all things uncritically accepted by the "good guys," yet killing enemy conscripts on the battlefield is a sign that the MC has lost her mind and gone too far. And of course, the villains are ontologically evil because they kidnap children, brainwash children, indoctrinate children, use children as hostages.... together with how often the bad guys target children and how often breeding, offspring, and having kids comes up on the MC's side, it really does feel like the whole game has some kind of hang up about having children. It was... pretty uncomfortable.

I would be remiss not to mention the support conversations and dialogue in general which is.... incredibly bad. Like, one romance occurs because the "dashing rogue" character kept hitting on a girl despite her rebuffing him 2-3 times before. She just says "oh you outlasted me, congrats on getting a date." There's incel-y conversations, a "nobody liked me when I was a nice guy" rant... god, I honestly can't describe it all and would recommend just skipping all the dialogue you can if you decide to go for the gameplay. Just. Wow.\t\t\t\t\t

That said, if you like Fire Emblem, this is a great PC equivalent albeit probably even better if you don't mind the lack of rendered cutscenes.\t\t\t\t\t
