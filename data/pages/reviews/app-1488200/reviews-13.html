\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf there was ever a game deserving neither a thumbs up nor thumbs down, it would be this one. The gameplay loop is like that of other CRPGs in its genre, which is fun. But in this case it's not well balanced, sometimes allowing a landslide victory, other times creating purposely impossible encounters to progress the story (a bit mundane, found myself skipping through it).

To be honest, I'm not sure this would qualify as a RPG, since you have little control over the outcome beyond your party's build. And it's not an adventure game since you can't really explore. If it were more balanced I think the strategy aspect would work better. Unique loot is always fun, and character builds.

It's rather repetitive, however. And I quickly became overpowered. It's not as dynamic as some other CRPGs, nor as deep. So with these things considered, I don't think I'd recommend buying it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a neutral review but weighted negative due to lack of accurate information from many of the positive reviews.

It's a game made with RPGMaker that despite being one of the better ones out there is still an RPGMaker game: generic names for units and towns; unable to customise how your units look (hair/skin colour/gender); unable to skip enemy turns or bring up the menu during their turn; a portrait art style that looks distinctly Asian regardless of setting; very awkward navigation of menus. These are minor irritants but detract from the flow and enjoyment of the solid story and game play. It's done a good job with the limitations that RPGMaker has but suffers (like all RPGMaker games) from not feeling like a distinct, premium product.

That being said, it is a fairly fun time if you love the genre of games that it's inspired by e.g. Tactics Ogre, Dragon Force, FE and Shining Force III. The game on harder difficulties is a challenge and the music score and story is decent. Worth a punt.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the main characters is a literal muscle mommy. 11/10 gaming experience.

Actual review for those who for some reason need more convincing: This is a TRPG game with a combat mechanic very similar to an old PS1 title, Vandal Hearts, that can be summarized as Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, Spock. Calvary running you down? Spears will do bonus damage. Enemy is wearing heavy armor? Magic is the key. And true to the American lifestyle, a scary amount of people have guns that can ruin your life. So then you play the game of, "Do I want to build unique answers to every combatant problem and hope the enemy does not do the same?" or build 10 squads of Jack-Of-All-Trades-Master-Of-None.

The story is serviceable so far. Westeros (not the actual name) had one ruler who dies which begins a civil war, all the while the REAL bad guy stews in the darkness, ready to take over the world. You play as Hero, a low-level army officer who through a succession of victories at points of militaristic or narrative importance recruit more allies in order to fight increasingly stronger and more varied enemies. And if you are not adapting your army well enough to these new enemy types, that lady over there with her three dragons will just mow you down. No, this is not a summary of that TV show -- these events actually take place here in the game.

Honestly, the story and character relationships/banter are just window dressing to what is really the focal point of the game: the combat missions. Moving your chess pieces around the board to secure objectives and stomp bad guys, see your numbers go up and develop legends for some of your squads. "Oh, you REALLY want to step up against THE HAMMER?! Okay, have fun doing 2 damage, dummy!!"

As always, wait for it to go on sale. Patient Gamers, Unite!\t\t\t\t\t
