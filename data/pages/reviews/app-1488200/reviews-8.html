\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best tactics games I've played in the recent years. While the campaign itself is somewhat short, there's tons of replay value... You get lots of different classes and possible builds (instead of controlling single units, you build squads of up to 9 units, and the combination of units in those squads determine how it attacks, how far it can move, etc), and you can manage traits/skills of each individual unit on the squad, so there's lots of room for optimization / minmaxing.

After the first playthrough you also unlock tons of options of customization for the new game+ campaign, from the usual stronger enemy units, to position changes on the map, different behaviour, cost changes for items and units, unit limit changes and many more. I got it on sale and it's a steal, it's definetly worth full price. Looking forward to more.\t\t\t\t\t

It's an enjoyable experience but may not be for everyone as its SNES-style comes with some of the flaws of that system. Cumbersome menus and individual unit management can eat up a significant amount of time when your army balloons beyond a couple of squads and you're managing 100+ soliders. That aside, the game is still worth a play if you enjoy a grand strategy game. You can expect around 30 hours of game play for a first play through.\t\t\t\t\t
