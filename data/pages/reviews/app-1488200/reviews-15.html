\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's very remisicent of Tactics Ogre, which I loved. Gameplay is very addiciting, despite some clunky interface here and there (I really think it should be much simpler to scroll through units and and upgrade them). I love the different squads you can assmble and the whole level-up and class change mechanics.

Two things do bother me. One fairly minor relative to the other:
I can't understand why they wouldn't make a career path for hero units. These are the units you want to see change the most and they remain fairly stale.

And the more glaring problem with the game is obviously the story. It's just so... insipid. I love narrative and character development, and yet at some point I started skimming the dialogue, and then skipping it altogether.
Dropping a ton of characters and their relationships on the player is not good storytelling. Having conversations which you can only witness but never affect is boring. Especially when they are so INANE. Listen guys, I'm not saying it should always be a masterpiece, but if a story isn't good, it should at least not get in the way of gameplay. I am bored to tears by it. I care nothing for the characters, I do not remember their names and I can't understand why they'd even listen to my character and make them the commander.

Frankly, I was very very close to stop playing because I couldn't take it anymore. Thankfully, skipping makes it bearable.

I'm not saying all this to be mean. This is the main reason I'll be hesitant to buy any future games. Which is truly mind boggling because I love the genre and the gameplay. But I can't take bad writing. It's not even mediocare. It's like somebody took the most cliched story off a fantasy novel and decided to adapt it into a game.

You need a better writer. The game deserves one.

So I'm giving it a thumbs up, but it's mostly because I hope they'll wake up and make something better in the future.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rate the game an 8/10 after completion. I really liked it and it was able to hit a certain spot and espescially the first chapters hooked me completely and I lost my sense of time for a bit.

The positive:
- A fluent gameplay style with the units and their various utility.
- A decent roster of characters. While some of them were a bit tropey, their choices and goals were actually fun and decent
- The UI is decent. Some interesting mechanics in the background. The marketplace saved me more than once and some of the unit concepts were neat.

The negative:
- Learning all mechanics took me a time. I didn't know how to use some of the stuff at the beginnig and couldn't get through them at the first glance.
- Some levels were just tedious. The one that forces you to loose should have come earlier and much clearer in my mind and at the end it was a bit of a drag in some levels.
- The options of levelling units are limited and you have some units that are leveled far beyond the others as you won't use them properly. There some Arena choices with Unit level limits would have helped to even this format.

What lacked to make it a 10/10:

- In my mind there could be more companion interactions. This would make the romances and relationships a tad better. They were still fun though
- More Gaiden Chapters and Side Quest opportunities. While the Arenas were fun at the beginning in the end they were a formality. So Side Chapters were you try to gather more resources, some allies and train your weaker troops (like a training the Slackers one) would be more fun.

But yeah. I really liked it and it was a nice and crunchy game that I could play at a time where I wanted to try something new.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked the game but I think it's far from perfect. During the first half of the story I thought it's amazing but that first impression faded over time.

The good:
- Squad management and upgrading individual units was fun and interesting
- There is a wide variety of unit classes to choose from, and I liked how some of them had clear use cases, while some I found out only after playing around with them for a bit.
- The item/artifact system further supplements upgrading individual units in squads, making you a bit more familiar with the individual people in your army.
- The map used between missions to show what is going on was a cool element (though I think it'd be better if you could view a preview of the entire continent during deployment preparation to check the standing of your faction in terms of territory/enemies/allies etc)

The bad:
- The story itself felt to me like it was really cliche - the fantasy elements were used too much to show your enemies as the "big bad guy doing evil things for no clear reason" for my liking. At the beginning I thought it was fairly okay, but the further I went the more the story segments annoyed me. I think that the game attempts to resolve that later on but I wasn't convinced.
- The AI is easy to manipulate - I figured out early on how it prioritises who to attack after I saw the enemy cavalry focus on my dedicated anti-cavalry squad, effectively self-destroying. It doesn't appear to get better over time - the enemy just gets stronger and has more units.
- Some of the maps make you bring 15+ squads while having only enough room to navigate 1/3 of that number at once. Unless you built your army with a large number of ranged-only units for some reason, a significant part of your army will coil around like a snake in small corridors during some missions.

I liked the game overall and am glad that I finished it but it's definitely nowhere near some of the better tactical games I've played. Both the story and gameplay have flaws that (while not deal-breaking) make the experience considerably worse.\t\t\t\t\t
