\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNice SRPG that where maps play relatively quickly. I haven't felt the need to turtle like I normally would in other SRPGs, since the squad composition and healer mechanics allow you to take some punishment without seriously handicapping yourselves for the rest of the map. The ability to reset turns and quickload, while it can be exploited, also allows you to just be more free with your tactics rather than trying to plan out every little tiny detail before turn 1.

I really enjoy the progression between maps, using resources you've captured to level your soldiers and tweaking squad compositions. You also have an option to do a little grinding (limited by somewhat uncommon items) in case you feel you're falling behind/ want to give that last little boost to a squad before a tough battle.

If you like SRPGs, this one does enough well that it is 100% worth taking a look at. I'm very excited for the potential sequels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid game if you're a TRPG enjoyer.

Greatest boon: I felt that squad management was exceptioanly satisfying, going from a mix of units per squad early to cover bases & get exp for everyone, to having highly specialized squads for specific tasks, such as bait cavalry, bait firearms, assassinate backline, healing. There were a few classes that I fully ignored (Zweihander, Samurai, Seige Cannon, to name a few,) but most seemed to have a niche.

Greatest detraction: I felt it was a little bit on the easy side, even playing on the hardest difficulty + permadeath. Not enough to be a significant detraction, but normally I look to the hardest difficulty to force me to fully engage with all the game systems, whereas in this I felt comfortable largely ignoring the arena, doing only a small amount of artifact work until the end of the campaign, and largely ignoring consumable items (skill tomes, stat boosters, etc.).\t\t\t\t\t
