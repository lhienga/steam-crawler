\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good tactical rpg where the devs take a lot of fun things from various other titles and stuck it in. It even has a good story, though not particularly exceptional, is well written and has enough charm, interesting moments, and fun bits of lore to keep me interested the whole way through.

Though the game isn't perfect, the ui is a bit poor, not in its layout but you will have to click too many buttons to get where you need to go and due to the units being squads, each with their own small things that can can be managed, it can get to be a bit of a pain later on when you have 15 squads with at least 6 units in each. This would be greatly improved with better mouse controls as in the current state you have to click buttons to interact with different buttons that are already visible on the ui.

There also isn't a whole lot of interesting builds which you can do with units which would be the main upside of the the customization. The only one i could make was a low cap squad build with a mage unit that hits the whole map.

I would also suggest any veterans of the genre play at the hardest difficulty, the game is fairly easy on the captain difficulty only having a handful of squad deaths throughout the entire campaign. The game gives plenty of opportunities to level and upgrade your characters so you can always make your squads stronger if you need to.

The game is great fun and if you are looking for a game to scratch that FE itch I would definitely try it out.

RIP Zorro\t\t\t\t\t

My biggest complaints were the character portraits feeling out of place with the rest of the art style (some in-particular are really rough to look at, like Zelos for example) and the lack of explanations of some mechanics, like the ambush tactic or how some units can move through terrain like forests better. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really good indi fire emblem meets advanced wars style game.
While the game is really good I kind of struggled to enjoy the characters that much. About 6 hours in I was trying to figure out why, because it's not like they're bad, they're interesting enough for the amount of dialog but I didn't feel as interested or connected to them as I did in Fire Emblem or Valkyria Chronicles. It hit me while I was at work the next day: They're just not weird enough. Something that Japanese games do well that it feels like this was to embarrassed or self conscious to do; which I don't blame them for not wanting to go so insane but it does hamper the characters a little.
Still for what is about a 6 person team making their second game this is astoundingly good. I highly recommend it if you like Fire Emblem/turn based tactics games\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has done a very good job of scratching an itch somewhere between a traditional JRPG, Heroes of M&M, and Panzer General.

If you enjoyed - Heroes of M&M, Panzer General, 8Bit RPGs, or just don't have time for a game that sucks up your life - you'll probably enjoy this one.


This mix has made for a variety in strategy and is one of the better games I've found on steam for it's price. It feels like an overall value and the developer is very active in the forums. This was clearly made by an avid gamer of the genre's who has a love for making this game.

The Good:

1) Lots of unit and item choices that you can use to your play style
2) The visuals are intriguing.. map transitions, art style - it all fits.
3) It has a bit of Rogue / Civ style replay-ability and that is enhanced by achievements - it's likely you'll play the story more than once.
4) Its not demanding!!! You can pick up and play without issue in a busy life!

The Bad:

1) Late Game - it gets to be a bit to manage 200 units. Some quality of life features would help here.
2) UX Design - Some of UX Choices feel a bit burdensome (I'm looking at you Organize Army screens) - they could be use some streamlining on the number clicks to get things done.
3) Difficulty Level - Even Warlord feels a bit easy when you learn the game, some options to customize difficulty would help with replay-ability for those who want a bit more challenge. (Permadeath makes a big difference, but it hurts to lose a beloved unit)

Overall - its a fun little game that will suck you in and waste a few hours of your life in the best of ways.\t\t\t\t\t
