\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen looking for a game like this? Totally worth it! Never played a game like this before but it looks cool? Totally worth it!

Set difficulty with 3 levels + options for tutorials on/off and permadeath on/off. (I played through the entire game offline on the middle difficulty, Captain, with no permadeath.)

Approachable with tutorials and adding some mechanics as you go. Challenges give some nice bonuses, and maps are restart-able if things go way wrong. BUT I don't feel the need to replay a map to struggle vainly for perfection to scrounge every last gold coin in a forlorn hope to be able to afford that single upgrade. Nope, the game rewards doing it well without punishing the occasional oops too much. (On middle difficulty -go for the hard one if you want.) Oh, and no need to pray to RNGesus -it never felt unfair. The one time it did for a minute, it was environmental damage that I hadn't understood was going on.

Good writing, good story, good cut scenes (option to skip). For me it hits that sweet spot with enough tactics to be fun WITHOUT so many things to consider that I bog down and need 5 minutes and a calculator to decide the best move. It's a pretty world, too, without that deliberately ugly pixel look. It will not amaze on graphics, but clearly a lot of heart went into visuals. The story ends (if you watch the credits) with brief here's what some of the characters are doing now, which was a neat way to wrap up (reminded me of the Fallout games with the settlements.)

Stable, not one single bug that I found.

Anyway, this is a great game and well worth your time and money.

P.S. "SKL" is best thought of as "Dexterity" or "Agility", the main stat for archers and rogues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolute must-play for fans of classic Fire Emblem games, this game mixes the visuals and mission structure of early FE with the squad-building mechanics of Ogre Battle 64, and it largely succeeds.

Gameplay: Again, very normal turn-based tactics fare. The missions had interesting twists that kept me engaged from start to finish. The squad customization was extremely fun and I spent many hours just tweaking my units and trying to build the strongest squads. I like that the simple mission based structure kept the focus on the gameplay and there wasn't any running around to gather crafting materials or anything like that. Everything happens on the field or in the menus in between missions, and that's perfect in my book.

Story/writing: This was somewhat hit or miss for me. At times the story and dialogue and support conversations struck me with how interesting or funny they were. At times things felt rushed or under-explained, especially in the final act of the story. I still can't really fully explain what happened at the end. However, because this aspect is such a small part of the overall experience, I wouldn't let it dissuade anyone. One thing it thankfully does not have is a bunch of cringe, one-note anime trope characters. While the writing didn't always work for me, I do appreciate that a lot of care and love went into each member of the cast.

Other comments: I recommend that tactics veterans start with the higher difficulty. I chose Normal and my units were so strong after a certain point that I absolutely bulldozed over the last third of the game. That's without looking up any kind of optimal builds or strategies online.

Overall score: 4.5/5. An easy recommendation to any tactics fans, especially if you love the likes of old Fire Emblem, Final Fantasy Tactics, Tactics Ogre/Ogre Battle, etc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I heard about the PC launch of Tactics Ogre: Reborn I felt the urge to play a Tactical RPG immediately. That’s when I found Symphony of War and decided to give it a try. I'm glad I did.

If Fire Emblem and Ogre Battle had a baby, this game would be a possible offspring. The inspiration is evident right from the start with the blue-haired main character (Marth) and the tarot reading when you start a new game.

Gameplay is essentially FE but, like in OB, combat is carried out automatically by squads that can range from 1 to 9 units. It’s possible to fast forward combat by holding the left trigger, which is great. Units are not locked onto a certain path once you evolve them. It’s possible to conveniently devolve units and get a full refund on the materials. There’s no need to backtrack to a prior save so you don’t get stuck with a unit you don’t like.

Even though the writing and the menu’s UI/UX could be improved, it’s not enough to justify a thumbs down. If you are itching for some Tactical RPG time, SoW is a solid, budget pick that will run on your kitchen toaster and will keep you entertained for dozens of hours.\t\t\t\t\t
