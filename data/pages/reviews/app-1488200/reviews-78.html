\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThings I like:
-The game has a fun combat system and lots of artifacts that can change up how squads can approach combat situations. It was nice to finally feel like you are commanding squads in an army. Different tactical attack options were an excellent addition! I'm also glad the developers are balancing items in patches. I played though on Warlord difficulty the first time through and found it to be a decent challenge. Once I understood the combat mechanics, I was generally able to achieve bonus objectives without too much difficulty.
-Art style - I liked the way the character portraits were drawn, and I think the scene illustrations look great. Combat sprites are fluid and magic spells are animated well. If they make another game in this series, I think adding some variability to structures like castles/bases/temples will go a long way towards keeping maps looking fresh.
-I enjoyed the soundtrack. Not much of a musician, so I'll leave that feedback to those types.

Things that I think should be added:
-A pause/open menu function during enemy turns, especially in perma-death runs. When I make a tactical mistake and know I'm going to be restarting the chapter, having to wait more than a minute for 15 or so enemy units to make their move so I can reach the options menu on my turn gets very annoying very quickly.
-Let me swap between units in a squad when using the Unit Status function please. Sometimes I just want to look at stats.

Things that could be improved:
-Writing - I wasn't sure where a lot of the plot points came from. There are pieces of information throughout the story that seem highly implausible (I'm not talking about fantasy elements), just characters knowing things without any context given as to why. For example, Narima happens to know who Jules is, despite no context as to the relationship before, and Jules just so happens to be the long-lost king? I think a lot of these could be bridged by having more support conversations. It kind of leads to my next point - romances between characters was ham-fisted and had little to no lead up. Romances work in Fire Emblem because there are at least 3 support conversations and plenty of chemistry before people start proposing marriage. Casamir's rage felt more silly than serious.
-Voice-acting - I'm gonna give them a pass on this one since it's a small studio, but I'm hoping that future projects give them money to hire voice actors. Lines, when voice acted well, can be endearing or reach meme status (Ferdinand von Aegir). I got tired of hearing Mr. MASTER SERGEANT awaiting his orders and benched him as soon as I had a suitable replacement.
-Giant anime tiddies - I get that most of the player base is probably male and 10 years younger than me, but I would love to see a game that puts mages in proper armor and not all women having excessive busts/cleavage. That's my personal opinion.

Verdict:
Overall, this is a game worth playing if you enjoy turn-based strategy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had an incredible time playing this game! It reminded me of the first time I played Final Fantasy Tactics - I was so engrossed in creating my armies and perfecting my team, that I kept replaying the battles over and over again until I was satisfied. The strategy mechanics were so much fun, but I also liked all the characters they introduced even if the story wasn't always thrilling, it had enough twists and turns to keep me intrigued. I found myself eagerly anticipating each new cut scene, because I knew it meant I was about to add a new, powerful general to my team. And let's not forget about the icing on the cake - being able to romance the giant paladin lady!!! Oh, Diana! *swoon*

And, finally the end of the game was fantastic too! Each important character who had traveled with me got their own personalized write-up, which gave me a satisfying sense of closure. I loved the way this game did that, like in DA:O! Why not give individual little paragraphs about the state of the world at the end of rpgs? This game did it right!!! Needless to say, I am now a huge fan of this developer and I can't wait to try out their other games!\t\t\t\t\t

2) Hire a good pro to write the story. I believe you have cash for that now. You quote Shakespeare in attempt to sound clever, but the truth is it could be much better. 4) Characters relationships were a nice idea but it would be nice to make it richer and to have more consequences

5) We should have more diversity of items, some which could enable more team configurations - i.e. making them competitive through item use. For example, ranger is so poor.... how could you make it better with an item?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor everyone who enjoys tactic RPGs like Fire Emblem, Tactics Ogre or FF Tactics.
Lovely spritework and animations that reminds me of Fire Emblem's GBA entries.
But in this game you don't take control of individual hero units but of their entire squads that consist of frontline soldiers, archers, mages, healers and more. How you arrange them in the 3x3 squad grid adds a nice depth to the strategy and there's a niche for every playstyle.

The UI could still use some refinement and polish but this might be my only nitpick.
For me it's like the GBA FireEmblem's grown up, older brother with way more meat to the mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t
