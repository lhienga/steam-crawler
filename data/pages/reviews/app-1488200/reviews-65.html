\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid SRPG so far. Didn't expect this game to grip me but it did. I'm mostly very picky when it comes to SRPG as the pacing are usually slow for that genre but this one moves along rather quick.

It's hard to describe this game for me as it feels like it's easy to get in to but complex at the same time. Story is pretty good and so is the gameplay but what really got me is the army/squad customization. I hate to say it but I will say it anyway in my opinion I think this is actually better than Fire Emblem.

If I would criticize one thing about Symphony of War is that there's no bluetooth controller support for PC or laptop users so you're stuck with a wired controller.

All in all recommended for SRPG nuts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a good rpg strategy game, There are classes and special units like your main characters or dragons as they are hard to find. This is not a friendly tutoring game so you will have to look up on some parts of the game if you're looking for something particular like how to get merit badges for your 3rd tier classes. This game is similar to Fire Emblem in the way of engaging and selecting which characters you want to use. It's quite nice to be able to deploy your own squads and form them. It is tricky at the beginning so you will have to take characters from squads to become the leader as loyalty is low if your using your newly bought mercenaries. Instead of 1 vs 1 like fire emblem. Game uses a group mechanic that builds a whole squad as a whole. If you can get this game on a deal it's worth it if you like these types of games. Even allows you to rename generic characters to help identify your members.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't believe I haven't written a review yet!

TLDR: The game is worth full price. Strategy Turn-Based game with unit classes and formations (And dragons). Great Story, great art, excellent gameplay.

You can tell how much the devs really love this game by even now after its success still pumping out QoL updates and even an upcoming DLC. In my experience, when a dev loves their game this much, it typically becomes a top tier game. And it's not too surprising since the game was inspired by older classic games.

Gameplay: You can have up to nine units in a squad; sword, pike, healer, and their cavalry variants. There are also gunners and cannons, and assassins, and paladins, and valkaries and mages of different kinds. And of course Dragons. Story squad leaders have specialized attacks from the variants. And later in the game you get battle field abilities that diversify any typical strategies. The characters are loveable, and you have freedom to make whatever kind of squads you want.

You also get relics you can equip to enhance all kinds of different squads, ranging from giving the archers more range and accuracy, to making your frontline tanks practically inpenetrable against anything except magic. Some relics will increasee your xp, and some will give you "cool" abilities. You'll understand my joke when you play XD. And not only can you do this sort of thing on a squad level, but also on an individual squad member level.

There's a lot of other cool things I wish to share, but don't wish to ruin. So just buy the game. I rarely say a game is worth full price. This one definitely is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t* This Review is editted to include the review for the new DLC
** These entries were editted to reflect the addition of the DLC

After a turmulent war of nobles; strife has taken hold of the lands and it falls to an orphan and his best friend to set things right again letting destiny and faith guide his path. Rising up as a military commander; do you have what it takes to unify the lands and oppose the machinations of the dark forces who're pulling the strings from behind the scenes?

IF you're a fan of SRPG's like Disgaea, Fire Emblem or Ogre Tactics you could definitely do worse by picking up this game and I enjoyed it very much (looking forward to doing more playthroughs to see some of the different conclusional scenes the game provides).

** Yes I hear you; "The game looks so basic and cheap... not to mention easy", weeelllllll you're kinda right and you're kinda wrong. Whereas the game was easy to get good at and fun to master pre-inclusion of the DLC, the addition of the DLC has seen the game become more balanced on giving players a challenge and that means the AI will be just as much of a behind to you as you were to it pre-DLC. The game still feels and looks easy but there's a remarkable depth of mechanics and strategem going on beneath the surface.
Optional content will deffo give you a challenge and if it didn't then there's always NG+ with the option to add even more pain to your chosen difficulty... like giving enemy squads signiature feats or artifacts of their own

The GOOD
* STILL a surprising amount of depth for the simplicity it provides on the surface
* STILL easy to understand, fun to master
** Replayability WITH a newgame+ mode... bring your old party into your next playthrough... for a cost
* Absolutely the right length in playtime needed to complete
** Alternate story tracks and respective rewards (not to mention a whole hidden unit class... or 3 !!)
** Rebalancing now makes the rock-paper-scissors mechanic more obvious

The MEH
* The story is, in most part, forgettable. Not saying it's not good... it's just nothing you haven't seen before
** STILL not the greatest control scheme (no cancel order button for example; 100% commitment mode at all time... which can become annoying if you happen to expect to heal but instead attack because you were out of you allotment for them)
** If you know what you're doing you can still make snowball/deathball groups

The BAD
* Simplistic graphics... it's OBVIOUSLY a RPG-maker styled game in all aspects. This can be a boon for people who like the more throwback-visuals but can deffo drive you away if you expect high-res GFX
* On rails... no sidetracking you hear! So no way to grind (except for the arena) which in turn can deffo lead to some people making squads that'll prolly just not work and spiral into a deathloop; forcing a complete restart.
* Tentatively exploitable AI.... Though practically impossible you know the AI will always bumrush the weakest squad they can... which means if you give that squad something like 100% evasion you don't have to bother healing anyone else except for them... unless they die...Alternatively... give your deathsquad a taunt ability and see the enemy throw squad after squad against that group without making a dent. This has been changed somewhat in that post-dlc enemy cavalry will now make use of hit-and-run tactics in order to soften up hardier squads and I could be mistaken but I think I actually saw the AI use an assasin squad simply to kill my healers so the main fielded units could simply kill my DPS-squads via healing-attrition... in a game where you're always the underdog these new tactics have caused me to accuse the AI of cheezing it on a few occasions... especially the DLC missions

** TL;DR: 8/10 would 100% put necromancers in my main paladin squad again...

Pick it up if you like SRPG's\t\t\t\t\t
