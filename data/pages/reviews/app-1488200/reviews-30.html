\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMagnificent game. Feels like a thoughtfully modernized Ogre Battle to me. Ive replayed it multiple times, beaten it and gotten every achievement on the highest difficulty it had at release and am slowly going through its newer harder difficulty which is a satisfying tactical change of pace after the base game.

I beat it rushing each of the three different tech trees, Ive played favoring all different unit combinations. Its the kind of game where your strategy is never wrong as long as you put some thought into how you are going to use it.

The story was described by my friend as 'Better than it has any right to be.' I think thats a good assessment. They focus on a story that is easy to be invested in with characters that are easy to like, believe or resent who all fit their respective roles. They also are not afraid of women being pretty, which is quite a nice breath of fresh air in current year.

I look forward to the sequel. Developer that deserves support.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally finished the game today, bought it when it was on sale for $17 a few weeks ago.

I haven't written a review in a long time and I don't enjoy overly long reviews so I'll just say that:

There is a lot of content, the army customization is really cool (it kept me wanting to play to keep upgrading my units), The story is overall pretty darn good for being restricted by game-maker and I enjoyed the game itself enough to do every DLC mission and sidequest I could before finishing the main game.

As for negatives I found it frustrating that enemy cavalry AI would all dogpile one unit, since they can retreat after an attack, any group of more than 2 cav units against a non end-game army unit is a death sentence. Also I wish there was more to the companion interactions. Dialogue is nice and it tries to get you more invested in the characters, but outside of that it's meaningless. No buffs for being near each other or anything like that. Several of my characters formed romances in the very early missions (including myself on mission 11 or something) and the only time you knew they were in a friend/relation-ship was during the pre credits sequence for others, or the post credits sequence for the player. Those seem to be the only time they're acknowledged.

Overall if you like Fire Emblem or other similar tactics games and you want an experience like that on PC, I highly suggest this. Was definitely worth the price and I look forward to the apparent planned sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

Not only does the squad based combat provide a unique twist on the TRPG strategy, but the resource-based promotion and demotion combined with the leadership system really incentivises experimenting with your squad builds and capturing every optional objective in a map.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun squad based gameplay with a ton of options for builds. You can choose the types of units in each squad, individual unit traits and squad equipment. The class system encourages experimentation by letting you downgrade classes without penalty, refunding any resources used to upgrade. Another thing I really liked is that XP gain is extremely quick when your squad's level is below the enemy's level, so bringing your new or underused units up to speed is easy.
I think the writing could use some improvement and the story got kind of nonsensical near the end, but I'd still absolutely recommend this to turn based tactics fans.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to write this review 2 hours into the game. But I felt like I owed the devs a proper review once I finished my first playthrough. I knew immediately this game was going to be an instant classic for me and I am happy to say 76 hours later that I was right. Unlike Fire Emblem and Marvel's Midnight Suns the time spent between missions is largely on force customization. Social aspects aren't neglected they are just streamlined you're not forced to sit through mini games for optimization purposes. Once you have your force the way you want it you can breeze through the between missions sections. This is particularly important as it massively improves the replay factor of the game. I appreciate this game for everything it is, the art is charming the story while not a masterpiece is absolutely interesting and I truly feel like I am commanding an army not just a handful of elite warriors and the game telling me there's 1000 dudes following us around. Squad building feels awesome and is the real joy here. None of the end game units feel truly inadequate and there is benefits to loading a squad with a single type of unit and benefits for building mixed squads which I really appreciate. And finally we have traits, the final layer of optimization for your squads. While some of the descriptions are a little misleading or lack specific info they really set the units up to go above and beyond. The first time you have a maxed out squad whether a full line of cavalry will feel amazing.

In short. Buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
