\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is good. If you like tactical RPGs, this certainly will keep you entertained. The game is carried by its really fun gameplay. That is where it shines and that is where you'll get the most enjoyment from it.

The biggest flaw IMO is the story. It's your vanilla high fantasy setting. You got your usual archetypes: rogues, valiant knights, scheming villains, etc. The writing is pretty weak too and is mostly cringe. So much so that by the second half of the game I decided to skip the cutscenes and dialogues because it's highly predictable.

TL:DR: Great game carried by its gameplay rather than its story. You'll have a ton of fun customizing your army and seeing how they work in battle.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of tactics games, I've had this game in my wishlist for far too long. What was my hold up? I tend to prefer games with single units instead of 3x3 grid squads/armies (Fire Emblem, XCOM, Valkyria Chronicles, etc), where I can have favorites and feel connected to my team.

I was wrong. That's my bad. I admit it. It was a silly thing for me to be hung up on. This game is awesome!

As other reviews have pointed out, I spent more time organizing, training, and outfitting my army than anything else. I love that part. Plus, not only was I able to feel connected to the main characters, but even the non-MC squads I built felt like characters I was journeying with.

Take for example: Robert. He joined as a simple militia troop and fought bravely on the front lines with his squad mates. Over time, his potential was realized and I watched as he became a Knight Captain. Leaving his squad, he became the leader of his own group. Not just that, but he became a beast! Need a group to block an entry point? Send Robert. How about a decoy? Robert is on the way! Need a squad to single-handedly wipe out an entire enemy army? I mean, Robert is good, but maybe have a few archers in the treeline to help whittle down some of the enemy troops. Anyway, you get the point.

Don't be silly like me and wishlist it for too long. Play this game! It's great and I'm looking forward to my NG+ playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNephilim Saga is most of the things I'd want out of an FE-Like. The unit variety is enough to keep me interested, but there is definitely room for expansion as many units hit their ceiling a fair bit before the end of the game. The story was enjoyable though expected, no real complaints there I know what I was getting into. Overall the encounters were enjoyable, never found any overly hard but I've played so many of these that I'm not really looking for challenge. Just an enjoyable run though one of my favorite game types, which this satisfied. The soundtrack was probably one of the games highlights, there was a good amount of variety from battle music to menu music. There are however a couple of asterisks, the final encounter is poorly designed, featuring automatic tick damage that there is no way to nullify, even had the patterns of attack change between rounds on me. Those are each cheap tactics and speak of poor game design. Overall the last encounter was poor. The other note is their choices in the epilogue. A wide number of decisions they made completely nullified player choices throughout the game. This leaves those choices feeling hollow and worthless, that kind of treatment of player agency really rubbed me wrong. The game was very good, but the ending notes were a pretty severe black eye that requires me to take a point off. Overall 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
