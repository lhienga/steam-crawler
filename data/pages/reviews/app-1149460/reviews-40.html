\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wished I'd researched this more. I bought all the packages. I like open worlds, I like progression, I like missions and I like playing online with others. This is what led me to purchase all the packages.

The more I play, the less I like it. The game is fundamentally pointless. The "billiard ball physics" of the game makes it seem old. At some points, you might just ping pong off of things or get thrown up or off a cliff for no apparent reason. Climbing would be nice too.

I started in the open world and it was boring with no one to play with since it is only with "steam friends". There are no missions in the open world and you have to punch out and play the missions. After a few missions, you're level 40 and just playing tier 1 missions constantly repeating the construction stuff as nauseam. After a few credits, you can start taking items with you but the missions all seem the same. Set up scanners in these locations which means running all over the map and dying each time you set up a scanner.

A progression based mission approach in the open world would have been way better since the levels are rather pointless and they sicken you with constant repetition. Too much focus was placed on the environment.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're someone who can look past a few background inconsistencies to see the incredibly robust survival game underneath then you'll be able to have plenty of fun in Icarus.

I love that Rocketwerkz experimented with the drop and retrieve concept at first, because I agreed with the basic premise that often the most fun part of a survival game is that time at the beginning where the world feels threatening. Unfortunately there's something that goes wrong when you're forced to do that process rather than choosing to do that. Fortunately that's now completely optional again because we have the open world saves and missions available there.

To put it simply, it's a robust survival game with all the usual bits, you've got missions you can run while in a long term save, or do quick drops, it's focused on PvE (which to me is a good thing) and the sound design is properly amazing.\t\t\t\t\t
