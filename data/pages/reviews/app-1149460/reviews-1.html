\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe (only) real challenge is surviving the horrible game design. The skill tree is atrocious. Oh I'm sorry, it requires 3 other skills, in a completely different tier, that you don't have. Wasted that skill point.

The world describes itself as an alien planet yet looks surprisingly like Montana right down to the wolves, rabbits, deer and horses. Dev 1 to Dev 2: "Wait! Let's add fangs to the horse, then it's an ALIEN horse right?!". Seriously. It's that bad.

Mission challenges just hopelessly swarm with you random pop wildlife at the end that neither immerse or entertain you in the context of the mission.

All this and the latest update adds water filtering. Nothing brings me closer to Montana Alien Adventures like water filtering. Said nobody. Ever.

Save your money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who plays a lot of survival games, I can't recommend Icarus highly enough. It's the only multiplayer survival game to make the actual wilderness both scary and a legitimate challenge for the player to overcome. Having to do things like cut a a firebreak after a lightning storm lights the forest my base is near on fire or frantically shoveling snow off the roof of my wood hut in a blizzard because the weight of the snow will cave it in to little things like having to occasionally rotate crops to obtain optimal yields; All are great examples of just how far ahead Icarus is compared to the other games in the survival genre. Add to that plenty of options for how to play like outposts, missions and open world that all tie back to the same central character and the drop gear making a really interesting form of long term progression and you have an Early Access survival game that is leaps and bounds better than most of the fully released ones. The near-future NASApunk caveman aesthetic leading to a spaceman tromping around the forest with a crossbow hunting bears are just the absolute icing on the cake. It's well worth the full asking price, and on a sale its a no-brainer if you like the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
