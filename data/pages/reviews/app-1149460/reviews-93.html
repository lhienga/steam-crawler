\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLong story short, I think there's a solid game here, but it needs work. This isn't a strong recommendation Get it on sale if you like survival games and are up for a challenge.

Making a survival extraction game is brilliant, because it dodges the issue of most survival games where you end up having so many resources that you don't have anything to do anymore. You can still buy things in the station (with in-game money) and bring them with you between missions, but it isn't nearly enough to make a permanent living. There's also a longer-term survival mode where you're not under any pressure to leave.

Still, there's a fair few problems. For one, there's enough bugs that playing can be aggravating. I've gotten stuck so many times it isn't funny, and one of them almost caused me to die and fail a quest. Besides that, whether or not your quest objectives will actually work is a toss-up. When I got the game a week or two ago, I didn't know where to go because the map markers weren't appearing. That, and I'd get my progress on that quest kinda-sorta-reset every time I logged back in (it was to deposit materials into a drop ship). They fixed that in the last update, and now on another quest, one of the objectives just didn't spawn at all. I had to pack my stuff up and go back to the station.

Besides that, the straight up combat is aggravating. It feels like the game wants to reward you for playing craftily and sneaking around. Indeed, scoring instant kills on my enemies with stealth archery is satisfying, but there's plenty of wave defense and boss fight missions. Those boil down to spitting out damage as fast as possible before you run out of stamina or get mobbed. It wouldn't be so bad if there were more tactics involved in it -- the only enemy I feel like I have to put thought into fighting is the bear. Everything else is either sneak attacks or just face-tanking damage and hoping my stamina bar doesn't run out and my weapon doesn't break.

Are these things skill issues? Maybe. There are consumables to regenerate health and stamina much faster, although those seem like band-aid solutions. It wouldn't be so bad if enemies actually reacted to damage; right now you can stab a wolf in the face and he won't so much as flinch.

Like I said, there's a real game here. I want to see how it evolves, so I'll keep playing, but for now it's not a strong recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t
