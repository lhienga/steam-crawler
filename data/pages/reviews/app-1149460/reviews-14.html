\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI see this game getting so much hate because apparently all these DLC's were supposed to be in the base game? I do think the $120 price is a bit steep, however - this game is one of the best survival crafting rpg's I've ever played. I have about 1000+ hours into 7DTD, Ark survival evolved and ascended, minecraft, Rust, etc. There is no game as "realistic" or "detailed" as Icarus. I'm absolutely surprised that it seems like nobody plays this game. Weekly updates are a huge plus as well. It's not easy, it's a bit of a grind getting through the first two tiers of levels. The combat is decent, the crafting is like no other. I've never seen a game that has so many deployables and such a HUGE tech tree. Everything that you would essentially do in a workbench in ark/rust is split up into multiple benches (which I personally love, gives a reason to have a big base) and just feels more realistic. I am close to 50 hours (43) in this game and I feel that I've barely scratched the surface with so many things. And this is just from me playing on Olympus on the base game. As for the DLC's and the price, I understand why legacy players would be upset but honestly it doesn't really effect me since I found out about this game a month ago. I would gladly buy one at a time after experiencing it. But to drop $120 on a game I've never played is crazy, but I am very excited for my next playthroughs on the DLC's and just really happy in general I came across this game.

But overall, I would give this game a 9/10.
It's gorgeous, the weather events add such a unique and dynamic element to survival which should've been a factor in games like this forever ago. The combat is decent, the talent tree and tech trees all make sense and are a huge help. The survival/crafting aspect is the best I've seen in a game. I haven't come across many bugs whatsoever, just a hiccup here or there. I would 100% recommend this game to anyone who is a fan of 7DTD, Ark, Minecraft, Rust, etc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is being sold as a full release game where it is EXTREMELY clear that the game, while released on 2021, should still be considered to be in Early Access.
Optimization is absolutely horrendous for an otherwise uncomplicated game. The game's core premise surrounds short-term expeditions to the planet and its 3 versions. 2 of which are expansions/DLC where I have been informed that the majority of THAT content was promised to be in the base game but was instead packaged and sold separately. However, the open world does not hold up under the design of the game which was focused on short form missions that range from a few minutes to a few days.

When sticking to the short form premise, the game holds up well. Unless you have run through the specific mission it can very much be a race against the clock which matches up with the promotional materials the devs have released surrounding the game but the amount of missions are exceedingly small and not well thought out or considered which causes the game to fall into repetitive grind just for the sake of grinding far sooner than it should.

While I can commend the Devs on their commitment to regularly updating their community on progress, I note their overall progress is extremely slow for even mundane implementations meanwhile severe issues with performance and general lack of content remain unaddressed for this full release/early access title.

I have clocked in over 360 hours into this game in order to compare different worlds (base game, Styx, Prometheus) in order to record footage for a planned YouTube Review and to make certain I have explored as much of the game as possible BUT during that time I found the game's seemingly only redeeming quality. It serves as time filler. With very little meaningful to do in the game, it will take a few days to a week-ish of normal play time to grind up a character to the point where you will have unlocked everything you need within Open World. After that, it's a waiting game to make sure you have enough resources to unlock the items you might need to drop with to engage with the missions as-intended. Which results in nothing more than more resources for grind. But their attempts to include a narrative into the game shows to me that they are no great writers or storytellers. Their apparent lack of progress and complete lack of optimization with this full release/early access game tells me they also lack in programming skills for what they are attempting to achieve.

If you enjoy a game just for the grind and the meaninglessness of it, then this might be the game for you. Buggy, unoptimized, and lacking of content that it is in THAT regard it might hold some charm. But $35 for the base game + $30 for Prometheus + $15 for Styx makes this game $80 for what SHOULD be a $30 TOTAL (maybe less) makes this game extremely poor value for the money. Video will be coming out on my YouTube channel within the next couple of weeks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoving the game so far, for some reason my son does not like survival games, bu the likes this one. I love the progress so far, however i do have one complaint.

I love the exp gaining when it comes to farming, I really like how annoying the beasts get that sometimes you just go on a rampage and KoS them. Building is easy to understand and complete and i really like how big the map is and the fact you do need to upgrade gear to get to different biomes, though personally i think they can be a little better in terms of difficulty. That's just me.

The weather can be treacherous and at times annoying, but that just gives it another edge in trying to survive that sometimes we gotta time our runs so the weather doesn't kill us.

I only have one complaint/suggestion. For the love of god PLEASE let us mute the loading music.\t\t\t\t\t
