\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a certified Open World Survival Craft enjoyer, this is a good one. It is pleasing to look at and exist within, and it is quite intuitive for the most part. It also does an excellent job of rewarding players for the various jobs you can do. In my case, I'm getting just as much XP farming and crafting as my buddy did hunting and mining.

Gameplay incentives are a strong point here. We face frequent wind storms in our area and wood structures take damage at the height of the storms, but stone structures have a buff towards wind resistance. The perk trees have incentives for practically every way you could want to play. Example, you can take buffs towards cooking food, or you can take buffs that enhance eating uncooked food- leading to the point that you can even eat spoiled food and gain buffs from it.

There are some minor issues with placing building parts (ex. parts getting placed under the map), and there is some jankiness to the physics (ex. trees entering orbit after falling onto a rock, animals clipping through walls).

One design flaw on the annoying side I have encountered is that this game is pretty brutal when it comes to mining large stones specifically. Oxite nodes will "break" when you reach the 20 or 15% mark for the deposit but stone will not, so you must physically mine the entire thing down to the last atom if you need to clear stone out of your space.

Additionally, it is extremely tedious to place a large amount of buildables (ex. Crop Plots) down because 1) they don't stack and 2) hotbar slots don't automatically remember and refill with the buildable you just placed down. So you must either fill your entire hotbar with buildables to get them down quickly, or you are stuck opening the inventory and clicking each one individually to deploy them all. The only reason I've gone into depth on these problems is because there are lots of other quality of life features in the game already, so I am hopeful these things can be addressed.

I will also throw it out there that older hardware definitely struggles with this game. If you meet the recommended specs, it is a beautiful game, among the most beautiful in the genre. If you don't meet the recommended specs, it's like a nearsightedness simulator.

Overall I'm enjoying it a lot more than I'm not. It takes elements from lots of other games in the genre that you'll find familiar (Ark, Rust), while adding unique twists and rewards in ways that others do not. And since it is 1-8 player co-op, you never have to worry about your creations being griefed offline.

Things would have to really fall apart in the late game for my initial impressions to change drastically. It is an easy recommendation if you're already a fan of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
