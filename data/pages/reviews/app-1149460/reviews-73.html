The map is complete rubbish. Opening the map drops my frame rate from 90's to under 5. It has the ability to place one marker (which is session only - logging out or more often crashing removes it). If I have the map open for more than 5 seconds and try to change the loom level I will crash - both unreal engine and AMD drivers will generate an error report.

The developer is extremely active and has delivered weekly updates for over two years since the dumpster fire launch.

I am running on an i5-9600k and 6600xt and have trimmed down settings to now run well and stable at 1080p- other than the stupid implementation of a the in game map.

Despite still playing this game lots, I still would not put my name to a recommendation until the map system is corrected. The lack of marking is poor (despite advanced items you can make to add markers), however when it slaughters system performance EVERY time the map is opened makes me question the developers focus after two years of game fixes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is a great game that is by no means perfect. In my opinion, it is currently in a fine state to play, but it just does not feel like a 1.0 release to me despite the latest version being called v2.1.6.

For the current price, it is a good deal. The main mission line will likely take over 100 hours, and there is other non-mission goals to chase. The DLC is a little pricey, but Co-op clients without the DLC can join a host with the DLC. There has also been weekly updates with new content (usually free), balancing, and scattered fixes.

Overall, the game does a good job of letting you do what you want while still providing plenty of things to do. Some of the missions can be lackluster, but I enjoy the core gameplay loop, so I am not bothered too much. Most missions are good and show off what the game can provide. A few missions are buggy and might need to be restarted. A common problem with missions is critical items becoming lost due to the large item slot being occupied; some mission items are magically lost when they are crafted and there is no space for them to go.

The progression mechanics are satisfying; there are plenty of skills and equipment to unlock and bring between save files. The progression does a good job in making you feel more capable without trivializing the game. The current level cap seems criminally low to me. You will likely reach it well before finishing all of the missions, let alone the DLC. I reached the maximum level for two different characters before completing the main missions (equipment and mission progression is kept when switching characters).

The balancing is alright. Most things have a use, and there is no one required way to play. Some of the skills are hard to justify considering the low level cap, but there are plenty of useful ones to choose. A small amount of content is nuisance-ly bad such as horses and salt.

The game currently suffers the most from bugs. What comes to mind first is the horrendous central arctic area of the base game that has various issues like falling through the floors and walls, especially when on an animal.

Problems like this are not okay in a game where you can permanently lose hard-earned, expensive items. You can mitigate floor clipping by staying away from ravines and standing away from drop pods, but sometimes you just fall through the floor while walking, seemingly for no reason. You can further mitigate these problems by keeping all important items on your person. The game does a good job of teleporting you back on land if this happens, but not if you are downed; if you fall through the map while dying/down, then you are boned. Animals that fall through the floor do not come back, and the items they carry share the same fate.

Make sure your PC is up to specs. This game has exceptionally long load times and performance problems when not played on an SSD. You will probably want a better video card than the minimum requirements suggests; running low on VRAM means game crash.\t\t\t\t\t
