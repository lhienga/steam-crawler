For a survival game, there is not much surviving to do once you have your hut and base needs covered. There are no, if rarely, big immediate threats to your survival like having your base overruned by zombies in Project Zomboid. Sometimes the weather gets funky but it's about it. You could literally just live in the hut for the rest of your open world session eating fish,meat and berries with enough oxygen to boot.

If you want to go to the end of the technology tiers the gameplay phases are about these.

Phase 1 : You just landed, grab everything plant related, make a hut a fire and a bed.
Phase 2 : Time to start killing everything fauna and upgrade
Phase 3 : Find hidden caves and farm for the rare ores.
Phase 4 : Get a drill, electricity and find ore patches.

So the bread and butter is mostly the included missions to unlock the space station gear you can take on your other expeditions. The timer makes it worth it.

Good luck surviving.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought ICARUS 1.5 years ago and have played over 400 hours. This game will be described with the following description. ICARUS is a great survival game with a space spin on it. ICARUS has its flaws, but what game doesn't? With the variety of weapons, killing the local wildlife may be entertaining. I can confidently state that 360 no-scoping a poIar bear with a shotgun can be really fun. ICARUS can be fun with more people, like the Dedicated Servers that are available where you can play with groups of other players at once. ICARUS is a little challenging at times, so proceed with tasks carefully. The 3 game modes are loads of fun like the Open World, where you get the whole map to yourself. Or you could play on an Outpost with regenerating resources, at a 75% experience reduction. Last but definitely not last, is the Operations which are set missions that drop you on the given map based on said operation. They reward you with Ren and sometimes Exotics which are used to buy in-game items in the "Workshop". These can be brought with you to all the game modes and any map you wish to bring them on. Workshop tools may provide an advantage wherever you bring them. I hope this depiction of ICARUS will help you decide whether or not you will buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t
