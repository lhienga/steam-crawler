\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is a great survival game overall. As all things do, it has its own quirks. The movement feels "off" and I can't even describe it. It's a little awkward in the long run but not game breaking. I really wish there was more "interactive life" to the creatures. Wolves hunting deer and rabbits. Bears eating wolves. The list goes on but the life currently is ignorant to the life around it. It is a bit annoying considering this has been a pain point for several players since EA release, and still nothing has been done about it. The game really tries to push prospectors around the map but, too often it's in a very tedious and unfun way.

Luckily, there has been some great changes over the past couple years. A lot of it was driven by demand from players. Especially the big change from real-time missions to game-time. And doubly so for the persistent open-world. The building system is pretty smooth and has had plenty of additions to make the game much more interesting, especially in the persistent open-world mode.

If you're a hardcore fan of RPG survival builders, I'd say it's definitely worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am on the fence between Yes and No on this game.
It has great survival genre for the first few hours, but it drags on with poor end game and complex manufacturing that requires manual lift and shift rather than automation or consolidated tiering (like Valheim, link anvil to workbench). I do not mind smelt, workbench, textiles, cement, cooking and attachments, but the rest is just too spread out and costly. Recommendations are at the end... but some other aspects of the game:

Missions
Get really frustrating with all the rebuilding every time... and it is the simple building I find pointless, since you can do most of the missions with just the tier 2 bench or less, which only requires a little bit of iron and copper mining. What I struggle with is needing to know what the mission would need to just land and execute it with station gear/goods. They throw a lot of curve balls at you that make missions frustrating rather than purely a fun challenge. Survey devices that sometimes call wildlife... why cant you just tell they will or will not... Missions with snap points for gear and other missions where it is a random spot you have to find, like Exploration and the drill.

Open World
This is where the game should have been focused with you establishing a base and going up and down to the station, but even this is flawed with no realignment of drop point or mission alignment. The result is that some of these missions take an hour just in travel. Running is NOT fun, it is BORING and Boa's are not that much faster than a 70% speed boosted 4 stomach slot player. My struggle is that I love the building aspect of these games, and this game has a lot to offer for building and designing a living space you want to come back to see. If they add more block shapes the building will be come quite epic... wood, stone, cement, aluminum and glass mix so well together.

So from a balance good bad view:
The Good:
Great survival game, especially if you like the resetting missions, like a weekly server wipe.
Interesting Tech Tree up to electricity with a decent spread of weapons, building objects and cooking.
Graphics and game play are very smooth, but you can tell this is the UE4, so lower min specs, but lacking in higher fidelity.
Story and purpose for missions with a little more voice acting it would rock.

The Bad
Repetitive mission restarts gets old when you do not know what preparation you need to complete before fighting. I usually feel like a 3 Skull mission will need Flint arrows and an extra steel knife just in case. That is 45 minutes right there, and you have not even started the mission really...
Fortnight building style for mission completion to get to tops of ridges or down from cliffs. Carry 12 roof pieces around with you... always.
Tech Tree needs a re-org and additions/subtractions.
Material costs either need to be less or automate production for things like Electronics and Sap...

Finally, what is really missing is the end game purpose, which should be our exit from Surveyor into Material Trader for the station in the sky to start receiving settlers, as the story goes. Give me random demands for material and provide high end completed goods as rewards. It would be cool to see a pod drop safely near my base with two wind turbines in it LOCKED until I deposit 200 leather, 200 Iron ore and 100 Copper Ore... Add additional rewards for Vestiges, area achievements when map is completely explored for each area... etc. Lots of end game possibilities, but I think this game is dead now... so my final comment is, buy it on sale, like $40 for all content... it is not worth much more than that anymore, but it will provide that much value. Prometheus pretty much ruined the Olympus and Styx mission progression for me...

EDIT:
The SIMPL3 missions from the Contact device handle some of the end game Open World issues, trading on planet resources for basic stuff, like seeds, food and such. Mission provide higher cost gear along with credits, which you can launch back up to the station to spend and return.\t\t\t\t\t

This is the fail for me. 2. The tech advancements are very extensive which is great- but a lot seem unnecessary for overall game progression. This is ok- just not great. 3. Storms get tiring after a while. Think it good to have and contributes to the immersion but also contributes to the grind. Ok but not great. 4. Biomes are too similar. Not to each other but would expect more variety across similar but separated biomes. This is still good- just not great. Summary as a single play through open world base build it a 4 out 5. have yet to explore all areas- given up on the "orbital exchange," not working for me, but overall still a very good solid game if your interested in base builds and immersive environments.\t\t\t\t\t
