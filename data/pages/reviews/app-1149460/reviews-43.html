\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy opinion after 2 hours of gameplay.

I am a survival fan. On my account I have ARK, 7Days, Rust, Conan Exiles, Raft etc/

In my opinion, Icarus is another average and unremarkable survival game based on the mechanics and gameplay style of existing games. At first I thought it would be like No Man's Sky, but more survivalist. However, I was very wrong. It's full of bugs and shortcomings, the optimization leaves a lot to be desired, and you don't know what to do here.
Taming is completely discouraging, building is very limited, crafting is as common as in mobile survival games.
The building, crafting and collecting system is the simplest possible, like something from a cheap survival game.
And all this on sale for 25 euros? Well, I guess the creators are screwed up here. The game is worth a maximum of 10 euros at the price after the promotion.

WASTE OF TIME AND MONEY, DON'T BUY THIS

Jestem miłośnikiem survivali. Na moim koncie mam ARK, 7Days, Rust, Conan Exiles, Raft itp/

Na początku myślałem, że to będzie taki No Man's Sky, tyle że bardziej surwiwalowy. Jednak grubo się pomyliłem. Pełno tu bugów, niedociągnięć, optymalizacja pozostawia dużo do życzenia, totalnie nie wiadomo co tu robić.
Oswajanie jest totalnie zniechęcające, budowanie mocno ograniczone, crafting pospolity niczym z gier mobilnych surwiwalowych.
System budowania, tworzenia, zbierania jest najprostszy z możliwych, niczym z tanich surwiwalowych gier.
I to wszystko na przecenie za 25 euro? No chyba tu twórców srogo pogrzało, Gra jest warta maksymalnie 10 euro w cenie po za promocją.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really hard one for me, but it has to be a thumbs down. Icarus is a mess of a game. It's like they don't really know what they wanted it to be, so they just mashed a few things together.

I'll start with the good. The environments are beautiful and the character models and creatures are all pretty decent. The tech tree is very fleshed out with each resource having multiple uses. There are very few things that you end up having to just throw out (with fur being perhaps the exception eventually). Base building is well done with only a few issues standing out, like not being able to snap roofs to half walls.

The bad is in the mission design and workshop progression. It works well to begin with and you feel like you are taking 2 steps forward and only one step back. Workshop purchases (which you buy with in-game currencies you earn) initially make you feel more progressed with each mission. However, this quickly fades away with the workshop progression stopping very quickly. Often the 2nd or 3rd item in the tree is the best or only slightly worse than those much further down the tree.

What feels like progression in the early game drops off a steep cliff and the game starts being 8 steps forward, 8 steps back, each mission. The rewards vs effort are way off here. You can spend 2-3 hours doing a mission that half unlocks a 10% upgrade to one tool....................... You can now do the missions in open world, which stops the seesaw progression, but then there's nothing worth spending your currency on because everything you build will be as good if not better, with a couple of exceptions being the enviro-suit and the modules for it.

This could all perhaps be forgiven if the missions were fun, but they're not. They're tedious, time consuming and terribly designed. For example easily half of the missions are go to 2-3 locations and do a thing. Unfortunately, you don't get told what the thing is until you get there, so you could travel for 15 minutes to get to a point only for the mission giver to tell you you need 2-3 things that you have, or need to make back at your base. So you go back and get/make them and waste another hour. Then you do the thing and most likely the task will be defend the thing from angry animals. That's it. "Here's 75 space bucks. Go buy 1/4 of a pick upgrade............."

I haven't played the New Frontiers xpac and supposedly it's better, but it would want to be a hell of a lot better to get a recommendation from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t
Short version:
Cool concept, inspiring structure, good potential, poorly polished, disappointing product.
It fails to grasp most fun parts from those genres it ambitiously tries to learn from.
All kinds of value and number designs are desperately awful, making the acceptable experience only last the first fresh 20 hours, and what remains will be endless, painful grinding.
I purchase it and 2 extension DLCs with 50 bucks during sales, but the content hardly worth even half of it.
Just go play 7 days to die or Escape from Tarkov.
The only kind of people I will recommend to give it a try, is those who are/plan to be game developers. You will learn a lot from it, but not from what it did well.

Long version:
I will admit that I was attracted by its idea, adding global progress and character growth above survival gameplay is super cool. Talents, preparation, expedition, resource managing. It will be so great if a game can combine all this fun together, although it will be extremely difficult to design and balance.
Unfortunately, this one fails to make it.

I will talk about those systems of this game and explain why.

Survival

Fairly speaking, the survival part includes a good amount of features and systems, and they are good on its own. Gathering, hunting, crafting, building. You can definitely tell the potential here.
Sadly, things don't work well when they are arranged together.
Those systems does not interact with each other in a healthy manner that makes the player feel they are rewarding. Instead, they keep adding more and more weight upon you, regardless of which system you are focusing on.
The real experience is more like attempt a running race with shackles. Despite the limited time and the distant goal, there is always something that keeps holding you back no matter what direction you want to go, which is only annoying.

That actually brings up the next problem regarding to the survival part: Lack of contents.
Yes, we have a long tech-tree to climb, a huge bunch of blueprints to unlock, items to craft. However, you will soon noticed that the types of materials is far too less than such a system needs, making the gathering progress tedious and repeating.
Most animals yield same set of loots, making the hunting system lacks of surprises and positive feedbacks.
The improvement in tools and weapon hardly improve the working efficiency, compared to the effort it takes to make them. They mainly works as "keys" to unlock new content, despite such a limited content in general.
The storm/shelter system interrupts your progress periodically, with absolutely no benefits.

All problematic designs, at this point of the review, are solvable with updates. Of course it requires works and efforts, but with so much potential, this game can still become something good after, maybe 1 or 2 years.
However, the survival part is basically the only reason you would want to play this game. What comes next are the things that really make things out of control.

Operation

The "mission" thing really contradicts with how survival games works.
Oh, you spends hours and hours to build up your base and get tier 4 items? Well, good for you, because NONE of them can be carried to next mission and help you accomplish those objectives.
If all the progress will be erased after mission, then what is the point to do those "survival" works, so that you can start all over again in next mission? Doing all those repeated, tedious works?
Why do you want to make a system that encourage the player not to "play"?

And the objectives are not interesting enough.
Go to this place. Find that thing. Defend/hunt down animals. Wait until the device finish its progress. Stack ridiculous amount of resources into a drop pod.
Interesting, exciting, rewarding missions could be the salvation of the whole thing, but they do not seems to exist here.
And something add more frustration into it. The Map.

Map

A typical error made by those who are trapped by the idea of "open-world".
Compared to the number of things that can attract your attention, the map is meaninglessly huge.
I can understand it. The amount hardwork and contents required to decorate such a map is huge, and it costs a lot. It is a bad decision, but I can see why and how it ends up like this.

What I cannot understand, is that it takes you 10 minutes to run from one objective to the next, and nothing, NOTHING worth you stopping by during this journey.
Why? Just, why? Is there any solid reason to make those objectives so distant?
And there is no fast travel. Hey, it is a survival game; but then why do I have to do those objectives?

Talents

What talent system usually do, is to enable the player to different playstyles. For the same content, I can choose to do it in different ways, and I can still enjoy most parts of it.
In this game, you can make a hunter build or farmer build, but you still has to do the full set of annoying works, while the talent only makes one specific part of the torture a little bit less painful.

For example, the game content is like a mountain. Players can go for a hike with their legs, but the talent offers other choices, such as obtain a pair of wings to access to the dangerous sky, or enhance arm muscles to climb on stone cliffs.
ICARUS does not do that. Instead, it amputate both of your legs, and give it backs to you little by little, parts by parts while you level up, so that one day you may have a single healthy leg. Left or right, your choice. Don't forget you have a mountain to hike!

Yes, the game gives you choices, but those choices are what it has taken from you in the beginning.
I will say even just give the player all the bonus and delete the talent system, can make things better than now.

Preparation (Workshop)&Expedition, or the Global Economy system

I am a fond of Escape from Tarkov, and I really like the idea of expedition, the gameplay about investment and return, about risk control.
Once again, it does not work well in this place.
Remember what I said in the Operation part? The mission is not rewarding.
It takes so long to accomplish a mission, and you only received such few credits, which have to be firstly spend to unlock the workshop items before you buy it.
And there is NO other way you can earn more currency, nothing you do in the mission really matters but the objectives and difficulties.
You can trade exotics for currency, yes, but exotic itself is another currency required by workshop for high-level items.
And you can only complete those operations in "Openworld" mode only once in each world, repeatable "missions" yield about 1/10 of operations.
Not to mention that those high-level items are not so much useful regardless of their costs.

Conclusion

There are difficult games I played, and I kind of enjoy those hard games; but they are meaningfully hard, and you will find pleasure challenging yourself.
This one is not. I cannot find pleasure by being tortured, intentionally or unintentionally.
There are games that I rationally know that is good, but I just cannot appreciate it.
This one is not. It is just so poorly polished, making all those potentials only seems miserable.
The numbers and values, including the amount of resources needed in technologies, the time needed to travel and complete missions, the power of weapons and tools, basically ruined every part of the experience.
It has been about 2 years since it released, I really cannot imagine how this will feel like to play at the beginning.
I acknowledge those efforts, attempts, hardworking to develop a game and keep updating it; but I really cannot recommend it to others.\t\t\t\t\t
