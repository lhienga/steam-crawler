\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo let's get this straight. This game runs great if you have not built anything and are only ever going to be playing solo. It has incredible visuals, a decent hunting system, and a spectacular rate of progression in place. However, since this game code does not seem to lean towards being rendered through the GPU, everything is going to be throttled through the CPU. I can walk through a small village that my friends and I have been building for the past 1.5 months and everything runs great......but only when I'm the only one playing. As soon as the second person joins, my frames drop from ~70 down to 48 and then plummets to 27 when the 3rd person joins the game. (Some people might jump to "get better specs" or "maybe it's just your rig" so I'm gonna throw my hardware at the bottom of the review.)

I was hoping for an easy fix, so I popped over to the report site that Rocket Werkz has in place https://icarus.featureupvote.com/ . There, players can call out some of the issues that they've encountered in the game along with being able to make suggestions to make the game better. 4th mega thread from the top speaks to the performance issues that the game has been having. I'm guessing that this report site is as old as the original comment to that thread that was submitted on Aug 28, 2021..... I remember reading other reviews that did not recommend the game because the Devs seemed more interested in adding decoration DLCs and a new map (all locked behind paywalls) rather than optimizing and bettering the initial product. Boy, should I have listened.

If you can get past the performance nightmare, you learn that the more you progress the more the world becomes empty and barren. There are no points of interest, no unfriendly NPCs, no attempted rescue missions to help other prospectors also trying to survive. But let's not forget that you can choose a SIMPL3 mission to make gear to help other prospectors or return the ID tags of prospectors that apparently were there not too far from where you currently are but who are already dead and surrounded by bears....it's only ever bears.

Let's talk a bit more about those SIMPL3 missions. They don't change, the loot table for the rewards and 'lost caches' are only worth it up until level 23, but yet they expect you to mind numbing-ly throw yourself at them over and over and over again to earn pennies. Those pennies are then expected to get up to $20 of the in-game currency to be able to buy better(?) gear that will help you start a new world quicker. So it seems that they think people will want to start over and keep playing a game over and over and over again while putting up with a horribly optimized/coded game.
