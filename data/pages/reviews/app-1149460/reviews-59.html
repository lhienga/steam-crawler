\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst my take on the game, then some tips below.
Short and sweet:
I've only played the single-player Outpost -mode where there are no time limitations, so I can't comment on the other modes. But what I've played is enough that I can say (at least for me), that Icarus is definitely a refreshing take on the open world survival crafting experience. Building is satisfying, though there is a bit of jank here and there, but it's nothing major.
Character creation is very rudimentary, but since you're really wearing your suit the whole run, it doesn't really matter anyways.
Hunting and fighting is good bit of fun and very well made. In the beginning I got stuck on some random stuff a bit here and there, but that got either patched or I was unlucky, since it hasn't been a big issue for me anymore. You can always jump or crouch to try and get loose or if that doesn't work the ESC -menu has "unstuck" button that drops you in an open spot very close to where you are. Works every time.
Your character keeps their experience, talents and blueprints they've learned even if you extract and start a new run with them, which is super neat and a fun little progression system.
The world is beautiful and the graphics do their job nicely enough.

All in all the game seemed to have a bit of a rough start, but the devs seem to have worked a lot to fix things. For me at least, it's very much worth the price tag.

Now some tips and info for beginners:
- Skip the thatched building tech, it's pretty much useless and a bad way to use your very limited Blueprint points, especially since the much better wood building blueprints are available at the same time (RocketWerkz, please don't change this, since it'd make starting so much harder without being able to build from wood)
- You can play this solo very well since alongside the 'normal' talent tree, there is also Solo talent tree that gets its own points as you level up; the solo talent tree is inactive when playing with others, but active when you're solo, so it really makes it possible for people to enjoy single-player mode as well
- Stealth kills give a lot of extra damage and especially as a single player it's a good way to crouch to stealth and throw a javelin, or shoot a bow or some such to get one-hit kill on most prey animals and even on some of the tougher ones if you have gear for it (I've chosen javelins for ranged and spears as melee and they work great)
- The 3-day weather forecast shows you different colored blocks that move in real time; for example if you have a yellow hurricane icon thingy that is currently going it doesn't mean that that entire block is how long a storm lasts, it means that somewhere during that block there (might/will) be some sort of storm or other weather event and you might want to prepare for it (I'll let you figure it out on your own since it's all part of the fun)
- You can go over your weight limit and still move, but just a bit slower so if you find rare resources and weather looks fine you might want to pack everything up and hope to get home with your loot (at least I haven't yet noticed it ever stopping you from moving)
- You can use your tools until they break and fix them then, saving resources especially on the end-game gear; In other words: the amount of resources needed to fix your gear is the same whether it's lost only 1% of durability or gone entirely broken\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis title carries hallmarks of a live service. It's being constantly worked on, and it's in solid state 2 years past it's release. unfortunately Tier 4 of technology tree is very disappointing and seems like Early Access. Yet I had sold hours of fun till that point.

OLD REVIEW
I recommend it.... but with a few things to say as warning

First off, it IS a lot of fun and i've only played it solo until now, likely way more fun with a friend...

But there are a lot of bugs and I have had a few crashes happen to me already. Thankfully the game auto-saves very often, but still. Frame drops don't help (people with gaming PCs, me included, have shared some of the crazy good specs they have and yet frames sometimes randomly drop into the powerpoint levels, even when opening some menus).
One thing the auto-save doesn't save you from is actual game breaking bug that push you to have to abandon a mission and start over, sometimes making you waste hours. One of these I asked around to understand if I was missing something... It was a bug (or bad gameplay design) that means that because the radar I was meant to protect got broken, I couldn't get a replacement... But the mission wasn't failed (plus, if dying doesn't fail a mission, why would the loss of a radar cause you to fail). Anyway, after asking around (and the community actually trying to help before telling me it was likely a bug and there was nothing to do) I gave up the mission because there was no way to keep going. Wasted over 2 hours.
At least the devs seem to update rather regularly (and apparently it has come a long way).

TLDR: The bugs, crashes, frame drops, etc... means I only recommend this if on a big sale and you have friends to play with. It IS fun, but boy does it need fixing and optimization. Devs are still working on this game, so hopefully it keeps getting better.\t\t\t\t\t
