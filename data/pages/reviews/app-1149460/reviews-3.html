\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus, the survival game I scoped out since release but never purchased. I held off on this one at first due to lukewarm reviews and later due to the cost of the experience (if you include Styx and Prometheus in that equation).

That said, I'm glad I did finally pick this game up and I would recommend it to fans of the genre, but in particular people, like me, who really enjoy the early portion of survival games. The mission-based part of the game (the original part that many people did not like) is basically repeatedly starting over with nothing (a bit of a rogue like experience) and as you progress you do get to bring more things down with you that you purchase with meta currency that you are earning on each mission. This gives purpose to constantly restarting and the missions also give finite objectives for each "game".

I've enjoyed the mission bit enough that I haven't even started the other types of games (which you can run concurrently) including open world (more like a classic survival experience with some of the elements from the mission based game mode), and outpost (like a peaceful building option). The same character can run all 3 types of games at once from what I gather which is neat. You don't bring things between the modes but you keep your talents and research unlocks.

Basically, TLDR, I think this one is a must buy if you like the start of survival games especially and if you like the idea of a rogue-like survival game :) It does everything else pretty well and the storms definitely make survival more interesting, but I'd say the biggest difference is the sort of meta game of missions and unlocks. They also seem to be really active (weekly?) in content updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is one of the most well put together survival games that are on steam to this day. Let me tell you some good, and some bad things about this game. Now, I have had Icarus since game preview launch, and have survived around 300 hrs on my most recent save.
I give this game a high rating due to it's constant fixes to the problems the community makes the devs aware of. if you start playing the game new now compared to when they launched it, you wouldn't understand 100% how far they have come with Icarus.
When you first start, the game is a bit rough and it's slightly difficult to survive things like lightning storms, and other bad weather due to the physics. The weather aspect in this game is above and beyond with detail. Buildings can take damage from more severe weather, even heavy wind depending on the materiel used for building. Along with the weather aspects of reality, they have given you the gift of parasites, bacteria, hypothermia, overheating, dehydration, and a few other things that may be a challenge throughout your play through.
Lets get into the building tho cause that is a top interest in this game for sure. Building has come a long way and Icarus has it down to a science. Straight to the point with how to build walls, floor, doors and ceilings, and other basic building blocks such as foundations and stairs. the use of a construction hammer makes it so you can change materiel of said building pieces without removing them, which makes re organizing a lot easier. With a wide variety of materials to use for building as well as crafting, the game offers you a more laid back style of building and crafting. Physics in building is what makes it so much more than just putting floors down and walls. support is Needed for building in this game or it will fall, and it will collapse. between the weather, and the detail in lighting, building gives you a place to come back to from your hunting/gathering missions. It gives you that sense of excitement to come back from an hr long grind of gathering resources to build yourself up to those next items you need, instead of coming back to just a boring 4x4 house with a roof.
Along with the great building perspective i do have to say there are a few things that make it not so great sometimes, but they are working on everything that the community throws at them. Every now and then you'll experience a couple display glitches like weapons not getting pulled out in your hands when you select them in your hot bar. Clay and other resources that require a sledge hammer to break, a lot of the time pieces will fall into the ground and you cannot pick them up. when trying to repair a lantern in your inventory just as you can do with your tools and weapons, it will not repair the lantern. It will say it is repaired but as soon as you turn it on, the burn time falls back to zero time.

If you are looking for a survival game that has not only Misions, Creative mode, And Outposts. This game is the game to get when it comes to a survival game with actual content to keep your mind busy. Highly recommend Starting with a Open World Save, and playing a long term save on each map. it makes losing all the progress you make on a mission not so bad to lose when you can load an open world (which has the ability to earn you just as much if not more credits/exotics along the way!!
