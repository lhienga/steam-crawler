\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I am looking for a game to play, I usually hunt for a rewarding loot experience.
However, there is another genre I enjoy playing, which is survival games.

Icarus is a person vs. environment (PvE) game allowing for up to eight online co-op players.
The planet of Icarus was set to be a second Earth. However, terraforming the landscape did not take and caused the air to become toxic.
Upon further inspection, scientists realized that the terraforming failed due to a rare substance on the planet known as exotics.
This sparked a new interest in the planet, and corporations have enlisted the aid of prospectors, such as yourself.

During your time on Icarus, you will face many environmental dangers.
The weather can be unpredictable, predators unrelenting, and harsh landscapes will have you begging to return to orbit.
However, this world has a beautiful side as well.
Crystal clear lakes and inviting foliage can make a relaxing space to craft and build your base.
Remember, you are on the clock and will need to complete the mission within a specific timeframe.


Icarus was revitalized with the new DLC, and even more predators that want to eat you.
As soon as I was dropped into the new mission area, I was immediately torn apart.
I was stunned into silence .
I got into a less comfortable position in my chair and was on guard for the rest of the mission.

Overall, I had a blast coming back to Icarus; it can be challenging and rewarding.
It feels great to finish a mission before the timer ends, and finally, killing a creature that took you out multiple times is satisfying.
Gameplay can get repetitive as most of the tasks are similar.
However, it is always a great time when you come back after a break.

Do you like hardcore survival games like Subsistence where everything is a struggle, then Icarus is for you.
Do you like survival games that don't hold your hand, then Icarus is for you.
Do you like survival games where everything is trying to kill you, then Icarus is for you.
In the end, I would recommend this game to anyone looking for a hardcore survival title to add to their Steam library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a fan of a binary "Thumbs Up vs. Thumbs Down" scoring system. I actually recommend this game for folks that are interested in the genre, but I don't want to give it a blanket "Yes, this game deserves your time and money". This game might be GREAT for you, or you might absolutely hate it. Or both simultaneously. So yeah, get this game. Unless the stuff I'm going to say makes you think "Yeah, that's not for me."

Icarus is a very pretty game. Looks great. It runs smoothly most of the time, and is very professionally put together. No missing tool tips, mistranslations, incomplete assets, and all that other jank that frequently plagues games of this genre.

Icarus can have a very satisfying loop of "gather resources, build stuff with those resources, get more resources, build better stuff". There's an exhaustive tech tree with lots of things to unlock and eventually build. And now that they've moved the focus away from their previous mission-based game play, I've actually gotten to explore the end of the tech tree.

But if I have to summerize Icarus in one word, that word is FRUSTRATING.
Like many games of this genre, all your gear and inventory drops where you dye, and you respawn on your bed with nothing. So now you get to run back to your corpse to deal with whatever killed you, but now with none of your equipment. Is it 3 jaguars camping your corpse like it's the first long-weekend of the summer? Enjoy the death loop!

And do you know what will make that death loop more frustrating? Random weather events that will literally kill you.

I've watched my whole base get ripped apart and lost everything in it because the roof pieces refused to snap into place together.

I've been killed by a bear, and than had that bear walk over to my base and decide to park itself right by bed, so it could proceed to kill me when I respawned.

I've had gruelling 'boss-fight' scenarios lost because random weather events.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game, but stupid "survival" mechanics that have zero or almost zero relation to reality make me want to rage quit. More on that in a bit. So, this is, essentially the review type that STEAM doesn't think exists. The neutral review.

Crafting: the crafting tree is deep and inventive. Unlocking certain recipes are hidden behind missions, and you definitely need to plan your build if playing solo. Co-op allows much more flexibility.
Combat: both challenging and rewarding. Polar bears are so lethal that it definitely deserves being a meme.
Taming: there are a limited number of creatures that can be tames. Some are supposed to have utility value, but their limited self-defense capabilities tend to make them more of a liability than an asset. If you are in co-op and have someone to "watch the mounts", then they are a good way to increase your hauling capacity. Or have a dedicated hauling play that comes to you to haul things back to base.
Exploration: the flora and fauna in each area is unique, but the rewards from harvesting remain mostly the same.
Graphics: generally good, except for character models. Some of the creatures are very well rendered.
Farming: Very genre typical; "survival" just means grind more. Some materials are in limited supply, i.e. there is a finite amount in each world, that that does make resource management an challenge.
Missions: can be challenging, but soon become very repetitive. They are needed to unlock some items.
Building: excellent visuals for building. Some tiers appear to be just cosmetic. Some building tiers have very "odd" material requirements (I'm looking at you stone).
Bosses: Not sure, haven't made it that far. Based on the combat so far, I imagine that bosses will be very challenging until you figure out their gimmick.

Top Pet Peeves;
1) Having to farm mats for oxygen because my character can't breath the air because it's toxic. Okay, sounds fine, until I find out I can drink the untreated water, eat the berries, pumpkins and watermelon raw, with no side effects. (yes, I occasionally get dysentery, just like drinking untreated water on much of earth). If the air is toxic, it is very likely that the water will be toxic to me as well. But we'll push the I believe" button on this. It's their gimmick.
2) Cave pneumonia. Just the act of entering a cave puts you at higher (very high) risk of getting pneumonia. I'm breathing bottled oxygen, see #1 above, so how the heck does that work?
3) Food. yeah, no survival game gets food right. Not the amount needed to nourish a human, or the amount received from harvesting an animal. Just wrong, all wrong.
4) Counterfactual crafting requirements. For example flint arrows require you to make them at an iron anvil... glad neolithic humans didn't know that and simply hacked the system by making them using other rocks.
5) Temperature management: again, like most "survival" games Icarus gets it so very wrong. The penalties for relatively moderate temperature differences are extreme; the point at which you start taking damage from just temperature, not even extreme weather events, is ludicrous. While on the topic of extreme weather, lets talk about that. It's a great gimmick. If you don't have shelter, they can be very deadly. No issue so far, until the desert. A minor sand storm shows up. I seek shelter in a cave, Kill the denizens said cave, quickly build a shelter at the mouth of the cave, and make my way deeper inside. My temperature is rising so I jump in the pool of water inside. My temperature continues to rise. I take off my armor, and submerse myself in the water. My temperature continues to rise, and I start taking damage (we'll talk about the threshold for this in a second). I use the prescribed "cooling" bandage that is supposed to rapidly drop my temperature. Nope, no such luck. I died due to an external temperature of 103 degrees F. I could see it if I was outside, exposed to the sun, dehydrated and wearing a sweat suit, maybe. Not deep inside a cave, fully hydrated, mostly naked and immersed in water and using the game's prescribed treatment for heat. Yeah, I call BS.

Anyway, Icarus, like many games, needs a neutral rating. It is enjoyable, and it provides some variety to the genre. So, with foreknowledge of my pet peeves, this game could be fun; solo or with friends. And, to meet with STEAM's goal of having people "recommend" games to drum up more business, and since there is no neutral selection, I have to say recommended.
