\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus, a game set in a harsh wilderness, was recommended to me by a friend. In this review, I'll take you through my experience with the game

Gameplay:
Icarus offers a unique survival experience, especially in its early stages. The game's mechanics can be unforgiving; as a novice, I found myself constantly on edge, trying to avoid wildlife encounters. Whether it was the lurking wolves, the bears in the shadows, or the unpredictable crocodiles, the game made survival a true challenge. However, there's beauty in that challenge. I was surprised to discover I could set fire to the forests, much to my friend's chagrin, who had thought of better uses of the trees' wood.

In those early days, there were no open worlds, just missions. The absence of mounts made walking feel like a never-ending journey.

Open World Expansion:
The introduction of the open world breathed new life into the game. My initial mishap - falling off a cliff and losing my inventory - tested my patience, but I decided to give Icarus another chance. With a different friend (flexing: i have a total of two friends), we played missions, and the addition of mounts and fishing expanded our possibilities. The shared open world aspect brought a sense of adventure, and the game grew on me.

Overall Experience:
Icarus shines as both a single-player and co-op experience. Its realistic approach to survival gaming offers a unique challenge. With stealth, the game rewards you with captivating in-game animation when you shoot the wildlife.

Conclusion:
Icarus is a game that can be tough to love at first, but it rewards dedication and patience.
