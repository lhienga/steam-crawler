\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: DO NOT PLAY SINGLE PLAYER, EXPECT LOTS OF REPETITION AND GRINDING.
The environment and the build pieces are beautiful, but the gameplay is very boring, its a real shame because I put like 50 hours in it, but I just cannot go on,. When starting the game it wasn't really obvious what to do so I figured Id do the missions. It quickly becomes clear that for most of the missions you are supposed to progress a significant amount through the crafting and upgrading (taking several hours) to get good enough stuff to be able to do the mission. This is confusing because I've read online you have to do some of the missions to get certain bits of gear. I've gotten 50 hours in, completed 8 of the missions and have barely made use of the money you get from completing missions and exotics, not even close. Some examples of how the missions suck:

1) One of the very first missions (I would expect to be relatively easy and not too time consuming) is to kill some wolves, then an alpha wolf. It says you can do this at tier 1 (lvl < 10). I spent ages getting enough bones for bone armour and a bone spear to go and attack it, got easily killed. Looked online and a guy took over 150 stone arrows to kill it. The recommended strategy is basically cheesing it - build some sort of fence and then poke it through.

2) Again another one of the first few missions out of a total of 67. Go and pick up some scanner parts, build the scanner which then gets continuously attacked by aggressive animals. I even bothered to grind through to make some better gear and built some structure around it. Gets destroyed after scanning like 35% so not even close. Presumably this requires me to either grind a lot to get high powered weapons, or cheese it and build loads and loads of structures around the scanner

3) Another very early mission - repair/build/refuel 3 parts of a drop ship then launch it. I build the required crafting components to be able to repair/refuel/obtain the 3 pars of the dropship, then I activate it and it says that I now have to defend it whilst it gets ready to launch. Well its a bit late for that isn't it? Basically excactly the same thing as the previous missions ive mentioned, either cheese it by building a bunch of stuff around it, or grind to get high level gear.

4) Finally made it to some of the arctic missions, one requires building a base in the arctic, Theres no wood there, but there is stone, so i figure you build the relevant crafting benches and some stone build pieces then lug it over there whilst overencumbered. I do this, it takes me 3 hours, I make my little hut, a polar bear comes in and very easily kills me and destroys my spawn bed meaning I have to try and walk all the way up there again with no gear and not get killed. Again it seems like im supposed to spend hours grinding to a point where i can defend myself. How on earth does that actually work, so for each of the 10 or however many arctic missions there are, Im what - supposed to spend 5 hours getting tier 3 stuff and guns then taking multiple long boring trips lugging it over to where i build my base in the arctic. The stone build pieces also require wood by the way, so how do you build anything in the arctic? Can you even plant crops there, how do you keep fires going - just have lots of coal.

After that I quit the game and uninstalled, spent 50 hours cant even complete 10 of the missions and have barely touched the other biomes and have unlocked sweet ♥♥♥♥ all of the exotics/ren/whatever currency stuff despite having spent so much time that my character is now LEVEL 50!!!! What the actual hell, sort out this boring grindy bs, or make it very clear that this game isn't worth playing unless you do every mission with like 4 people. There are 67 missions, do you seriously expect people to be spending like 5 hours on most of these - doing 90% of the same stuff over and over. This would total hundreds of hours. This would be like if you played an fps and for each mission mission you spent 5 hours cleaning the gun, just so that you could then do a 30 minute mission. What even is the point of the mission time limit being in real time. Most of them are 7 days, this is 24*7 hours whatever that is. Some of them are 30 days. Anyways its a real shame because I actually do want to use some of the higher tier stuff like vapour extractors and water systems but can't be arsed to get there.

Why on earth don't they just allow you to do each of the missions in the area in one sitting, That way i would happily spend 10-20 hours or whatever building a base to get good enough gear to do the missions. But no they force you to redo the most boring part of the game over and over, I genuinely want to understand what the idea behind that was, and how this wasn't immediately obvious it would ruin an otherwise relatively fun game. I might come back in a few years to see if they've got rid of this horrible idea of a game mechanic, made the missions significantly more interesting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm new to the Icarus scene, but it seems there's some history here for those who aren't.

I can't speak on the drama, but I can speak for the game. It's more enjoyable than Ark and runs smoother, IMO. I think for being a small team that says a lot, too.

Dislikes:
- The buffalo has an IQ of 1. Seriously, it JUST moves straight towards you (no path finding) and it does so agonizingly slowly. It'll often get stuck on trees and rocks. Just not fun.
- Moving across the map is generally painful. It's big! The horse handles hills and terrain well, and moves fast, but boy oh boy. It feels like you're driving across a few states moreso than going on a cool and dangerous journey (I play on Hard as well).
- The rate at which you get credits from missions makes it feel more like a minimum-wage job than something fun. I think this is to curb the rate of progression. I believe the latter is generally bad practice and makes a game dull. The fix? Add more items to research and improve the credit rate for completing missions. Not just "more items" either, but actually items we'd want to research.
- Crit spots are all the same: Aim for the head :/ You've got a sci-fi world, would've been cool to take greater advantage of that with creature designs.
- Mid-tier weapons I just ignored completely -- never a threat strong enough to warrant them! I just continued to get a better bow and better arrows, max out my food slots, and used tonics... the latter has carried me through the entire game. The rifle (not bolt, but t4) is the only weapon I'd consider swapping the bow out for, but it scares off other creatures and brings nearby enemies to you, so I only use it for thumper/extraction/etc.
- Lack of enemy diversity. Wolf vs snow wolf doesn't count either :/ They don't even look that different, basically just a different name (same drops).

Likes:
- Landing crits is CRITICAL. Love that you really have to hit a creature's critical spots and shoot carefully
or else you'll die.
- Food and farming is REALLY well done in this game. An absolute blast collecting all of the seeds (almost like pokemon, but not quite that fun). As they all have various uses, they're (mostly) all really useful, and it's an interesting system with fertilizer and watering. It's not too complicated either.
- Weather is no-joke. A part of me wishes it was even a bit rougher, especially in hard, but it's not in a bad spot.
- Electricity and water management is cool!! These two aspects are probably some of my favorite parts of the game. I love this idea of having the harsh wilderness with all of these mundane tasks that are then automated to allow us to focus on moving to the next region (from forest to desert/snow for example). I think Valheim accomplished exploration-progression perfectly, and if that could be better introduced into this game with automation then ohmahgod. Currently the only "automation" is the drilling mines. If you count "self-sustaining", then solar power + turbines + electrical appliances would also count -- as you no longer need to collect coal for the furnaces, as you can just use power created from the sun/air.
- Building is fun! I really love building in this game. Almost as much as I do in Minecraft, and that says a lot. The decorations are fun, the building itself is easy with smooth snapping. Power and water cables/piping can go inside objects, so we can have them mostly-hidden in the walls. It's really quite enjoyable to make a base in this game, especially towards the end of the game.
- Fishing seems robust... but I can't speak on it yet, as I haven't tried it out. I just see lures, tackle boxes, and lots of hook variations in the tech tree.

Closing statement / TL;DR:
Traveling is boring, there isn't enough incentive to go to the other regions of the map, tamed creatures could use some work, but I've enjoyed every hour of this game so far. It's truly been a ton of fun, and it's kept me on the edge of my seat for hours at times.\t\t\t\t\t
