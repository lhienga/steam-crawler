\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, what can you say. Never really played games in the survival genre specifically, but I've always enjoyed a challenge. And a challenge you will have, if you decide to brave the drops out to Icarus.
Following in the steps of 242, I tested the crap out of the beta weekends so knew better what to expect. Come the launch, I was still very pleasantly surprised.
What Rocketwerkz has made is a true diamond in the rough. Are there bugs? Sure. Are there issues with talents? Somewhat. Is the game forgiving in any way? Heck no. But this thing is so good to play. And the unsung hero is the environment. So many different ways to die. You can misjudge your surroundings, and die to the elements - a snowstorm is not to be trifled with. The alien life is not your friend - even the life that just seems more like the earthbound type. Bears will rip you a new one if they catch you. Ain't proud to say, but I've even fallen casualty to being crushed by a tree.
This is to say - be careful out there. The place is definitely not your friend, so you should take some with you if you can.
And a huge congratulations to the team that built and maintain this thing. Keep the comms going, and you will do well.\t\t\t\t\t
