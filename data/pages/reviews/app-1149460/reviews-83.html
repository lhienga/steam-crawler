The Alpha Snow Stalker withstood over 130 mighty blows. And I hated every minute of it.

What kind of difficulty scaling makes this sound like a good design decision? Do they expect players to cheese it and unload obscene amounts of ammo? I can't imagine running around trying to hit it that many times without simply dying. I was already over geared (fabricated bow, crossbow, pistol) for what I thought was a data retrieval mission. Am I supposed to grind the best possible weapons and gear to have on me at all times? As far as I can tell, it doesn't have a weakpoint. I think the intention is to fight it with a group of well equipped friends, or else fight it solo with crazy gear and to cheese it.

High difficulty should not equal absolute insane HP/armor/whatever. Even Dark Souls bosses die relatively fast, they just keep your on your toes the entire time. That's good difficulty.

I wouldn't have picked this mission on hard permadeath if it was clear that I would have to fight this thing (why? I was able to access the corpse to retrieve the data without fighting it). If I had intended on fighting it, I would have came better prepared, but again would have preferred not to altogether.

1) Built a 3x3 shootbox to retreat to and try to get it stuck in.
2) Started shooting it with the pistol (found item), but it kept running away and healing faster then I could hurt it. Yes the shootbox was within its zone.
3) Switched to the fabricated bow because I have the wound perk. I was able to damage it faster than it healed, barely.
4) Got it glitched inside the shootbox. The health regen stopped here since it glitched out attacking a wall instead of retreating and healing, thankfully. Shot it with ~70 arrows.
5) Bow out, shot it with ~20 pistol bullets
6) Pistol out, shot it with ~20 platinum crossbow (found item) bolts
7) Crossbow out, came at it from behind (glitched out, thank god) and hit it about ~20 times with a fabricated knife.

Beyond that. The game has potential. If possible, they need to upgrade to Unreal Engine 5 so they can use nanite technology to simplify creating maps and optimizations (no more LOD concerns).

It really needs the world to feel dynamic with dynamic threats. Even Ark Survival can surprise you with a dinosaur attacking your base or something. There aren't meaningful world bosses, events that unfold and grow in any meaningful ways, etc.

It would be really cool if it was more like The Forest or Don't Starve where threats are more dynamic and evolve over time.

But both the world and threats are as static day 1000k as day 1. Brain dead AI, cold, storms, hunger, and possibly obscenely high HP enemies (brainless difficulty scaling) are as dynamic as the activity gets here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall:
Smooth game that has nice environment, great building system and more which feels complete. What I disliked are the missions in general (too much idle time), the lack in diversity, and combat system. If I could choose neutral rating I would pick that, but I feel like I lean towards the negative one more. It was great 10 hours, but after that, we were just taking missions to hope for more fun missions that never came.

Good:
Graphics look nice.
Base building is really nice and well done. You can build pretty much everywhere.
Large map that has different areas.
Player movements feel smooth.
Co-op was good and did not have many issues like disconnections and etc.
Resource gathering is rather satisfying.
Tech tree and talent tree are quite promising.
Nice leveling system.

Bad:
My biggest issue is the missions are not diverse at all. Most missions can be summarized as 'travel to the objective taking you 15-20 minutes to some place to click on a box, wait for 30 minutes, go back to the place you started'. The worst is the travelling feels idle and the waiting does too, making it feel bit boring.
Resources do not respawn, therefore, building large base just for the sake of building large bases is much more time consuming than it needs to be.
Many animals are faster than you can run and have unavoidable attacks.
Combat system is buggy animals far away or facing away from you hit you. Headshots or critical hits are really inconsistent.
The map is not randomized between sessions, but always the same. This makes missions feel less nice.
Too few tamable animals and too little diversity of animals in general.
There is not much danger so crafting crazy weapons and gear does not really have much purpose in general.
Many developer decisions that felt stupid, such as: Respawning cave-worms after you leave, caves have debuff and 10 minute additional debuff from staying in there, missions start far away from objectives for no reason, exotics are difficult to get and require lots of time, most animal attacks except bears are undodgable which is terrible since if a bear and another animal attack you at the same time, you cannot do much, farming crops has little point to it since you cannot really do anything that requires so much of it.\t\t\t\t\t
