Icarus Review: Great Ideas, Rough Execution

I've put some serious time into Icarus, and I've got to say, it's a mixed bag. The game's got a lot of cool ideas, but they really need to iron out a ton of kinks.

The Good Stuff:

  • The tech and talent trees are huge, which is awesome because you've got lots of options on how to play.
  • The graphics are alright, and the user interface does its job without getting in the way.
  • You can play by yourself or with friends, and there's a variety of stuff to do like timed missions, open-world survival, or outpost building.
  • Farming materials isn't a huge grind, which I appreciate.
  • The different crafting benches having their own inventory is a smart touch. Makes managing resources a lot easier.
  • The crafting queue is slick and lets you stack up your crafting tasks efficiently.
  • The shared XP system is a cool feature when you're teaming up with pals, making teamwork more rewarding.
  • The weather effects are legit; they can get intense and make the world feel dangerously alive.
  • The base story is interesting (even though it doesn't play much into the actual gameplay).
  • Base building and crafting options are solid with lots of variety.

The Bad Stuff:

  • Okay, the technical issues are a nightmare. Solo play is alright, but in co-op, my FPS tanks hard. We're talking dropping from 90-100 down to 15-30, and that's with a good PC.
  • The Level of Detail (LOD) is a letdown. Even on the best settings, things pop in out of nowhere and the shadows look like they can't decide if they want to be there or not.
  • DX12 and Ray-Tracing options seem more like they're for show because they smash your FPS and barely make a difference in how the game looks.
  • The map is boring. There's nothing cool to find, no places that make you want to explore. No cool secrets or spots to discover, just a whole lot of "been there, seen that."
  • The combat system is super basic. Animals just charge if you're close enough, and that's it. No dodging or parrying, and the melee is almost useless. It's all about bows or guns. There's no real skill or strategy needed, just shoot from a distance.
  • The animals might as well have a clingy relationship with you; they follow you forever.
  • Attack animations are rough for both the animals and the player. It's like watching early 2000s CGI trying to be real.
  • The movement and physics feel off. Jumping feels like you're on the moon and the overall movement is just awkward.
  • Building's got potential, but it's bogged down by a fiddly system where you craft each piece one by one and then struggle with the placement.
  • Despite a ton of skills to choose from, a lot of them don't really matter. Like, why have multiple talents for seeing animals in bushes when one talent shows their health from afar? But there is no talent for seeing dead animals in the lush bushes...
  • Missions could've been interesting, but they end up feeling like a grocery list of chores. It's just collecting stuff without any fun twists or challenges.
  • The biomes look different at first, but you quickly realize they're all the same just with different skins. Plus, they're the same every time which gets old.
  • The game's got systems like electricity, but they're only half done. There's no way to control them properly, so you're stuck playing electrician every time you need to switch something on or off.
  • What really bugs me is that they keep dropping paid DLCs and content when there are still so many bugs and basic quality-of-life stuff that isn't fixed.

Bottom line: Icarus has some cool concepts and could be a blast, but it feels rushed. It's rough around the edges and needs some serious updates to be what it could be. If you're a survival game junkie and you've played everything else, maybe take a look. But set your expectations to "work in progress" and brace for some frustration.

Would I recommend it?
Only to survival game veterans looking for a new fix, and only if they've run out of options.\t\t\t\t\t
