\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is a very cool yet very unforgiving game. Whether you are running missions or playing in an open world, it's certainly a beautiful game. It's got a lot of problems as well, biggest of which plagues a lot of survival games and it's that there is no real end game. So at some point once you've build your base, food/drink/oxygen are trivial you kind of end up with nothing to do except run more missions or start a new world.

Speaking of missions, some are laughably easy (kill 25 rabbits!) and are marked as difficult. Meanwhile there are some marked as easy or medium that unless you are playing in a group you've had it.

There are several biomes and yet you can get nearly everything from the starting area so there's no real reason to move anywhere. I'm not saying the game is boring, but I also don't think it's going to be very high on the replayability scale.

In the end, I recommend this game. I have 80+ hours in it as of this writing so I've absolutely gotten my money's worth. But when I'm done with this playthrough? I'll probably uninstall it and never touch it again.\t\t\t\t\t

The Good
• Icarus provides a unique and engaging survival experience, keeping players hooked for extended play sessions.

• The game cleverly blends frustration and enjoyment, turning challenging situations into genuinely enjoyable farce.

• The missions offer small, achievable objectives that add a layer of strategy and planning to the gameplay.

• Icarus successfully creates a sense of consequence for in-game deaths, making each decision and action feel impactful.

• The storms in the game are atmospheric and intense, adding a dynamic and unpredictable element to the environment.

• The game excels at weaving stories out of its systems, creating memorable moments and experiences for players.




The Bad
• The game suffers from various janky elements, including animal behavior, clipping issues, and mission-breaking bugs.

• Combat is somewhat one-note, with a reliance on repetitive strategies that can make encounters feel less dynamic.

• Unlock trees and certain blueprints could be more satisfying, with some perks feeling submerged under minor buffs.




The Ugly
• The grind for resources is a bit overwhelming.

• The game's currency system attached to mission completion feels stingy and near irrelevant, impacting the overall progression.

• The presence of unsatisfying XP debuffs during storms and ailments adds an unnecessary layer of frustration to the gameplay.



Overall Score:
7.2/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI fell in love with the idea of this game when I stumbled across the 7 minute live action trailer back in 2021. The story prompt was a very exciting idea for me and I loved the way it plays with science fiction with a strong grounding in the real world (example: exotic matter is a "real" hypothetical material...not something made up for this game).

I occasionally get urges to play surival games and enjoy the grind of Conan Exiles and Valheim. This game scratches a similar itch with greater sophistication. The graphics are more morealistic (and beautiful vistas can be seen from time to time). The crafting system is pretty in depth and the developers have put a lot of work into the game.

I wish sometimes that there would be more story development. There are hints of the wild world that could be in some of the missions. However, despite my number of hours, I've only done about half the missions on the original mission tree and haven't touched the two newer maps yet. I love exploring this game with new friends online. I love the mission based timers (I'm actually a little sad they walked back the permadeath if you failed to evacaute in time).

This game requies a beefy computer, but it's worth it to me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is a relatively simple to understand survival craft game, that has variety in how its played. The building is very nice, the sound effects are nice, the world is beautiful (Olympus and the new DLC Prometheus), and the crafting is pretty in depth.
The game is not without it's faults, however. There are occasional bugs and/or frame drops, but the devs are hard at work with weekly content drops and bug fixes. So if you encounter a problem, odds are it will be be fixed within a week or two. With that said I have not encountered any major bugs or crashes so far at almost 73 hours.
My time has been spent exclusively in the open world mode, and I have yet to even try the mission based mode that the game originated as. But from what I've heard from others some of the early missions serve as a tutorial of sorts to help introduce new players to this games mechanics for those interested. All in all, I would recommend this game to any fan of base building and survival games.\t\t\t\t\t
