\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game, it does a bunch of things well, but keeps dropping the ball on important things.

Icarus advertises itself as a hardcore survival crafting experience, but it fails to deliver on that promise. Instead it provides a really high quality "rustic dollhouse experience with annoying yet unavoidable combat".

Let's start with that they do well:

Base building: You can make your dream cabin. The building system takes a lot of resources, and it makes every placement seem important. You have to maintain your buildings in the face of storms, and there's a rudimentary structural support system that means you have to think about your decisions, and not just place improbable floating mansions. Everything is a little dark inside since the lighting system isn't fully baked, but honestly that's my only complaint. I enjoy building things in Icarus.

Exploration: They did a rally good job crafting a beautiful world to explore. There's hidden caves that you need to track down in order to get the resources you need for building, and the caves feel creepy and claustrophobic. In general, the darkness, at night and in caves feels dangerous, and that is appreciated. I enjoy exploration in Icarus.

Then there' the systems they want to work well, but don't:

Combat: I think they want the world to feel hostile, but it doesn't, it just feels annoying. You either need to kill things that offer no challenge (e.g. deer, rabbits, birds) in a way that feels too easy and kind of gross, or you need to kill annoying bullet sponges that run after you full speed while you stab them in the face. Or jump on a rock and stab them in the face. Or jump over a stream and shoot arrows in their face. The most interesting enemies are shielded everywhere except their face, which means you have to run backwards for longer, because you need to take more shots to get more face hits. Sometimes there are a lot of things at once that you have to stab/shoot in the face, so you have to run backwards for a while to herd them into a group while shooting them in the face.

Combat in Icarus is a nuisance, and I think the developers realize that, because they've recently released a game mode that requires less of it, but I wish they would just fix it. If you made the creatures and the player less spongy, and turned it into something that more closely resembles danger in the wild, it would be more fun, and fit the game's theme better.

Sessions: The idea behind Icarus is that you can avoid the boredom of tech-tree-climbing that's endemic to all games in the genre by time-limiting the sessions. The idea is that you will land, quickly set up the bare minimum to accomplish the mission, go do the mission, and return to space. Unfortunately since construction is so resource intensive, this means that every mission begins with a lot of boring labor, to build a structure you probably like, only to throw it away at the end of the mission, the majority of which are not particularly enjoyable (1:place macguffin. 2:level-appropriate animals attack. 3: annoying combat to defend the macguffin. 4: rinse, repeat).

The idea here is great, but it runs into a wall as a consequence of poor execution. As it stands, the missions are more boring than the tech-tree climb, so I just kind of ignore them because they use up resources that I could otherwise be dedicating to my dream cabin. I genuinely wish there were some cool settler-related missions, but that would require NPCs and AI, which is not where this game shines.

One of the most frustrating thing about Icarus is how it takes a very interesting premise, and makes it boring. "Be part of colonizing/exploiting a very hostile planet with exotic materials, by running backwards and repeatedly stabbing earth animals in the face while a beeping thing beeps." I honestly dream about some "State of Decay 2" style mechanics being introduced to this game, which would give it stakes, but the developers seem to habitually avoid AI spending time on AI.

To be honest, I think the primary failing of the game comes down to AI. Game AI is hard, but if you don't do it the game is boring or annoying. Until they add some meaningful character interactions to the game, I'm going to avoid it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is an amazing game in so many ways. It has set a bar for me on survival games. I would have bought it and the expansions at full price had I known how good it is. Do your self a favor and just play it. Then buy an expansion or two. I very rarely encountered bugs, and those were easily manageable, The base building is the best overall that I have ever used in the many many games I have played. It's a real adventure and it is not easy. You will need to think your way through missions, finding resources and encounters. I died so many times. Each time I learned, made a new plan and kept going. IDK exactly how many hours I have, but they are all solo. I can only imagine going through the trials of surviving Icarus with friends would make is even richer. BTW of my years on Steam and the hundreds of games I have played, this is the first one to compel me to review. It deserves so much more and the devs deserve so much more than "mostly posive" reviews. Hope this helps someone!\t\t\t\t\t
