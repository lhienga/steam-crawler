\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you love the early grind of survival games (and also dancing in the rain), then Icarus is for you!

I am a huge fan of survival games but tend to get bored once my base is established and staying alive isn't really much of a struggle anymore. I restart at that point, sacrificing all of my hard-earned progress just to taste that sweet, sweet panicked rush of grabbing sticks and rocks while trying not to starve or get killed by nightfall.

The mission-based structure of Icarus, though, lets you "restart" over and over on fresh drops--with all the early stick-grabbing glory--but your skill tree unlocks, blueprints, and any gear you elect to take with you on the new mission gives you some permanent measure of progress. It's a fabulous middle ground between that fresh survivor scramble and late-game power curve.

Then there's the storm mechanics. An old staple of the genre is run around during the day, hide at night, rest to speed up dawn. Icarus' severe weather mechanic shakes up this dynamic, forcing you to retreat indoors when freakishly powerful storms hit day or night... or you can be a wild one and venture out into the hurricane-force winds and watch the wrath of Zeus fry an entire forest during a Flash Storm, then harvest all the animals that died in the resulting forest fire. Good times!\t\t\t\t\t

- Nice stuff to explore in the beginning. A lot of interesting first encounters.
- Great experience as coop.
- Graphics are quite beautiful, but performance is just terrible.
- Nice voice acting.

Cons
- Performance is horrendous. Even with latest hardware. Especially if you are the host. Singleplayer could be playable.

- Economy is quite broken you just have way too much of everything.

- You advance in the techs way too fast.
