\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoring repetitive game play, with little to no replay value despite that being the main selling point.

Starting over every run feels pretty pointless. You drop in, build a house, kill some things for food, and then look for caves. After getting metal, run through a bunch of valleys, finish the mission, run through some more valleys, then leave.

The AI is pretty poor, and the spawning mechanics for enemies is just annoying. The only "difficulty" to be had is that a wolf spawns every minute or two to annoy you and prevent you from finishing your work. Initially they are a threat, but that quickly changes and then they are just a nuisance.

The storms are a joke. You can walk around with no resources, and when a storm warning comes, you have ample time to collect resources and build a hut. Even the most dangerous storms can be "survived" in a one square house while you bang on it with a hammer.

The tech tree is ok, and probably the only worthwhile part of this game. The starting over aspect would feel better if the whole game didn't feel like a big tedious mess. Nothing actually feels hard or rewarding, just annoying.

Doesn't seem to be much of a story.

The fact that this was made by the same guy who headed DayZ (Rocket) explains a lot. He left it in a similar state.

If you want something difficult and rewarding play ark. Tech tree progression, the beginning is difficult, and a ton of things and places to explore, not to mention lots of interesting dinos.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is a lot of fun to be had in the variety of game modes. I find the open world to be a blast and the progression system is satisfying. The major issue with this game comes down to performance. You can tell that the game was initially designed to have missions where assets do not remain in the world for an extended time. The more you do and the more you build the greater your FPS will drop.

My friend and I built a modest base with a few bridges to cross the lake we were near. After 30 hours of playtime and a base only large enough to hold the required crafting tables, my fps as the host drop into the 20s regardless of graphical settings.

I am running a 5800x3d. 7900xtx, and 32GBs of ram all on an nvme ssd. It would be nice if this game mode could be better optimized so we can continue playing in our open world map.

I really do enjoy the game and will give it a recommend because there is a lot of fun to be had in the mission structure. I will be attempting to make a dedicated server to see if this remedies the fps drops, but I really do believe optimization in open world should be a massive priority for the devs.

This game does a lot right in regards to the survival genre it is a shame to see it wasted with optimization being treated second tier to DLC.\t\t\t\t\t
