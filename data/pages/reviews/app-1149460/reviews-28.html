\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus offers a both mission based play style (land do mission, return) and open world (with mission board), which you can switch between/return to as you wish.

Traits and blueprint based advancement gives a feeling of a goal and achievement as you level and build you character. The progression is linked to playing with others as you stop getting "points" to spend before you learn even a 1/8th of the traits available, The idea is that each player specialises in a tree (kind of like character classes), so you might be better for hunting, building, mining, etc.

The local wildlife are generally easy enough to deal with, but numbers are normally what end up killing you, and bears!.

If you watch the short movies and do the first few missions you can pick up the story, although this really isn't a story based game; it just gives you the background.

I've not played this with others so I can't comment on that side of the game.

At the time of writing this I've played 139 hours and still loving the game, and there are still lots of places I haven't been, things I haven't built, or killed!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is great and to be honest I could have sunk tons of hours into it. Already did 250. Positives are:
1) Large enough world and map.
2) interesting crafting maps and tools.
3) Good progression.
4) Devs still do weekly updates even if they are somewhat strange to understand. I would say do a great polish of the game dont add more and more cosmetics to it and fancy items when some core systems broken.

BUT!! And there goes huge but. It's very NOT solo person oriented. Devs tried to fix it by introducing solo talents. But still. The talent level cap just makes people annoyed that they have to choose the build to narrowly. Suggestion to fix it? Give 50 tallent points per tree. Allow me to be the botanist but don't force me to drop down all my crafting + fighting talents. That's just makes no sense. Or just remove the talent/level barrier. I am mostly a solo player and my several characters mostly ended up looking pretty much same. Bows and/or Guns and some crafting + stamina/running speed. Cause the rest quickly becomes crap and just a bit of time saver which is not needed and to farm for respec NOT going to happen.

So all in all a could be fun game. But very niche for specific players. Summary - Great game but with very serious limitations for public:
- Clearly multiplayer oriented for 3+ people
- Mechanics and "rouge like" systems designed for kids who can sit at some time together and do the mission after school or something. Not for grown ups who might barely have time once a week/month with mates. And might have to play separately. (Missions originally were real time based and hence too many were lost)
- Mechanics for long survivals involve too much time running around farming, if no time saving talents. Limiting the talents was strange choice which at this stage should just go.\t\t\t\t\t
