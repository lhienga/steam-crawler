The base game is overall a positive experience, with combat, base building and gathering a formula well established. The massive skill tree gives me the enjoyment of accomplishment.

BUT:
The game does need a few updates, and some quality of life changes for this to be Top-Tier.
There seems to be a massive amount of costly DLC, which is cool but I'd wish there was more customizations.
So firstly, the Crafting menu is very weird, and there is no tutorial (to my knowledge) so its a matter of trial & error.

Visually the game is really pretty, it just feels like the direction should focus on the already established content instead of new content.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus offers a fresh take on survival and base-building. The game allows you to play in a number of ways with more or less persistency of the buildings and equipment you craft during your missions, and give you loads of skills and tech to unlock as you progress your character.

It is also, unfortunately, a game where the developers seem to not care about or respect the players’ time spent to achieve anything. Your typical mission starts with “go to drop-zone to get further info”. In most cases, the follow-up is a simple grocery list of supplies you are requested to supply to complete the mission. While these types of missions are not uncommon in survival games, Icarus manages to make it extra tedious by making sure you will travel across the map several times to first find out what exactly is requested before you even have a clue what kind and how many of a material is required to finish a mission. You then get to decide if you want to make a temporary base in that area, grinding for additional recourse on top of the requested, or travel all the way back to wherever you might have a base already. On top of this, some missions will have you travel to several rockets in one or more zones, to supply with arbitrary resources not necessarily common in that specific environment, further bloating the tedious travelling back and forth in the game.

While I understand Icarus is about survival and exploration, and the travel across the map thus serves as a form of “survival”, this particular form of quest design seems to only serve the purpose of artificially prolonging a game that can already be very long, especially if you do individual missions rather than open world game mode, which is rather unfortunate in my opinion. Enemies in the form of wildlife offer little challenge even early on, especially if you have a mount available, and even apex predators like bears or cougars can be handled by either moving right behind their line of sight as you travel, or simply attacking them up high from a safe position like a rock they can’t climb or destroy.

However, despite the unnecessary long time you will spend travelling around in Icarus, I do think the game is worth picking up (at least on sale). Base building and equipment to unlock makes the (relatively easy) grind worth to explore the game in its open world mode in my opinion, and I hope the devs will focus more on QOL updates, quest types, unlockables, and game mechanics rather than adding yet another outpost map in the future to further improve on an already promising concept for a survival game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview at Patch: Week 103
Hours Clocked: 23.3

I currently do recommend Icarus.

Most survival games start out good, then just end up being some theme park walking simulator. The world doesn't try to kill you, the animals can't kill you, and you are essentially just walking around looking for a resource to plod along. They have a perk tree to explore that is crudely done, or so asinine it only rewards one way to play and that reward is a big fat turd (Looking at you 7D2D).

Icarus will kill you, and do it more than once. If the storms don't kill you, the fauna will certainly get you sooner or later. Like to play in Ironman like me? Get ready for lots of restarts, but you learn, you adapt and find something new to kill you. Once you get the hang of things, you will find that the tech tree rewards variety of play styles, group play and solo.

You get better tech, which lets you live longer and give you a fighting chance. I say that, because even then you might run afoul of some bad luck, or worse get overconfident and find that the one bear you decided to fight is level 46 and you're their lunch. The thing is, it feels like a survival game and you feel like the choices you make matter more. Even restarting feels good, because you'll get to test a new way to start.

If the timed missions aren't your thing, There is also a more traditional setup, which keeps the challenge and you don't have to worry about finishing an objective that is timed and will bring angry wildlife to you. Either way, survival is not only the point, but doesn't feel tacked on.

Overall my recommendation of Icarus is Recommended.

The Good
    \t
  • Tech and Talent Trees are diverse and viable.
    \t
  • Attempting different styles and strategies has still felt impactful.
    \t
  • Survival game that actually makes you survive.

The Bad
    \t
  • While not a bad point per se, running into much higher level aggressive creatures when you first start is rough.
    \t
  • Could use a resource encyclopedia alongside the Bestiary.
    \t
  • Unless I'm missing it, or haven't unlocked it, map could use some more waypoint options.
