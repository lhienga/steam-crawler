\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing by myself for the past couple of days and I gotta say that Icarus is a pretty fun survival game. With gameplay mechanics reminiscent of Ark, Rust and even Valheim, you'll have your hands full in no time. However, different from Ark and Rust (and more akin to Valheim), there is no Player vs Player combat. The focus here is to battle and explore the wilderness of an (not so much) alien planet. And because of that, you'll find a robust, challenging and well developed PvE.

The art design of this game tends towards a very realistic representation of the world. This, in turn, puts a lot of pressure on your hardware. My GTX 1080 is struggling to get 60 fps so bear in mind that you'll need a beefy PC to run this.

The sound design is satisfactorily well made and will keep you entertained throughout the game. However, I would not mind some improvements to the songs. Considering all the time you pass gathering resources and stuff, having good music when you complete these tasks is always welcome. Also, the narrator voice actor is top notch, good work on that.

While playing, I've noticed a few points that deserve more attention from the devs, specially when talking about the building mechanics. Building in this game is somewhat clunky and difficult, not giving the player much space in terms of creativity and satisfaction. I believe the devs could get some insights when looking towards Rust and Valheim and how they approach the building aspects of their games.

All in all, I'm having a great time on Icarus and strongly recommend it if you are into this kind of game, be it with friends or by yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI. Love. This. Game. I'm a big survival, exploration, and builder gamer.

Icarus has so much to offer. You'll find a lot of similarities between Icarus and other survival games, but Icarus has taken the best parts of those games and spliced them together beautifully. I can genuinely feel that the devs love survival games and they've played a lot of other ones.
For me, it scratches that itch so well it's akin to onanism.

It's a very chill game that ramps to 100 when a bear suddenly tries to maul you. You can look forward to murdering mean horses (and birds) and then forcefully adopting and brainwashing (read as taming) their children as mounts. You can shoot nasty cave worms in their face hole while contracting pneumonia; you can slip into despair when you're facing storm exposure and you accidentally insta-mine a boulder you were mining out to hide in. You can get frostbite so so many times; get poisoned by piranha; come to love hunting, not for sport but for epoxy. You can watch as a raging forest fire creeps towards your base.

Icarus hits that sweet spot between the hyper-realism pain of SCUM and deceiving simplicity of Valheim. It's the crazy orgy spawn of every survival game that's come before it. It's not revolutionary, but it's genuinely GOOD and FUN.

Biggest critiques: Graphics can be wonky to start with so play with your settings. The electric wire tool is trash but the electricity payoff is worth it (once you progress far enough). While Weekly updates are a fun gimmick, I'd rather see a larger monthly update. Larger updates can create more excitement for the community and help the game grow.

Also, everyone should start doing a couple missions before trying the persistent world. They help teach you the game.

Buy it. Try it. Love it. Thank me later.\t\t\t\t\t
