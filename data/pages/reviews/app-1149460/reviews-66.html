I was surprised by its polish and interesting gameplay as I had heard negative comments concerning the game. Bear in mind that I have zero interest in any of the co-op/multi-player aspects of the game, so I cannot speak towards those but, for solo play, I have had no problems with the game so far.

It's quite easy to get quickly bored with something too simple and this game will have no danger of that happening. The graphics are quite good maxed out at 4K with 'Quality' DLSS which adds to the experience.

If I come across anything that changes my mind on the game, seeing I have not progressed very far yet, or as I find other cool stuff, I will keep this review updated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed this for the free weekend, then purchased cause I really enjoyed what I saw. Love the way the network code works and how you can hop in and out of instances. Feels like Valheim that way, your character persists between instances but their position and inventory remains relative to the instance you load.

As for the game itself, I quite enjoyed hunting in this. The animals moved and behaved more like real animals than I've seen in other survival games. Cutting town trees and harvesting also very Valheim-ish. Crafting and building was intuitive once I figured out how to bring up multiple varieties of a wall by holding the rotate button ('R'). Just everything feels very solid about this title so I'm happy to support. Purchased the version that comes with both DLCs so I can hop in and out of any map I want. Looking forward to digging into this one more for sure.

Update 1: Couple missions down now and I am liking the game more and more. The concept seems to be that you are dropped onto a planet surface and have to achieve some variety of mission while there. You are able to bring items you've crafted with you, and the way you get the currency to craft permanent items is to complete missions.

The concept definitely works, and I find it to feel like a survival simulation while still retaining the fun of a game. Going to keep sinking time into it as it hasn't started to get stale yet at all.

I believe for any truly innovative game you will always see mixed reviews. Some people want the same thing they've had before, some people like unique experiences. This game is a very unique experience compared to others in the survival genre and it is executed very well in my opinion. The UI is solid and intuitive, as are the controls.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter reading a few reviews i decided to leave one of my own :D

Having what looks like more hours played than the average review
and having played the game since it released in beta i hope i can
give a good tldr because i dont like longform stuff.

The game is amazing, and has only gotten so much better over time!
This isnt a game i would say i could play every single day because of the repition,
but when i dont know what to do on my days off its usually my go to.

Early game is insanely brutal, i've been lucky enough to have a co-op partner
for the early game because its just killer.
Literally- Everything will kill you when you dont know the game.
-Animals, weather, caves, fall damage, food/water/oxygen. Not having access
to later game items really makes the game feel almost unfair at times.

Late game when you have plenty of exotics and ren, and now red exotics
life is good. I can drop in with my op stuff and get a solid starter base established
within the first hour, whereas your first play through would likely take 5+ hours to
achieve the same progress, and that lead persists through the whole playthrough.

Being able to go in prepared and having the knowledge of how to prevent dying,
you will almost never die. I say almost because even with op gear if you get complacent
at the wrong time, you will still die.


You'll have to be patient with most aspects of the game as it is a slow progression.
From the start to the end. The game got alot more enjoyable after they added
tames into the game as it gives us a viable transportation method instead of having
to run 15+minutes across the map on foot, it also solved some long distance
transport needs.

-The tame system is amazing and you need to not overlook it because you would be
severely missing out.
-The cooking system is a little tedious, but provides some pretty massive buffs
-The farming system is OK, i wish there were some quality of life changes for crop management
-The tech and skill tree are pretty good where they are at. Tons of options for different styles

Weakspots, at least for me on a server, aren't always where they should be, or are very specific sometimes and then nonsensically easy the others. It's jank you just sort of have to work with at the moment.

Playing enough and doing the nonsense that I do while I play, I am starting to peak behind then veil and see some of the mechanics in a very flat cardboard way as opposed to how i felt when I first played, where certain mechanics felt powerful and such.

I recommend it I guess. It's fun more often than it's not, and I trust they'll improve stuff as they have been.
I gotta say the price feels harsh, even with the "discount", but I understand that they made a whole new mode for the game and all that, and at the end of the day I get it, so I got.\t\t\t\t\t
