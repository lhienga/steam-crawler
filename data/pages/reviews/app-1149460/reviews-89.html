Overall Score 8/10

I preordered this game and then I refunded since it had bad optimization back then. Tbh I regret I refunded it since I knew I will end up buying it again lol. I did buy it again and I can tell the optimization was quite fixed but still I don't have the best performance while playing it. The story, gameplay, crafting, the atmosphere, everything is just amazing, if they manage to lower the minimum requirement to play it I can quite tell this would be a GOTY.

It was running pretty stable 30fps on High Setting (I have a Ryzen 9 3900x, EVGA 3070 and 32gb ram), but sometimes you get low fps out of nowhere.

I do recommend it but only if you have a computer that can handle the pressure lol.

2/2 - Graphics
2/2 - Puzzles/Difficulty
1/2 - Story
1/2 - Gameplay

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA group of my online buddies migrated to this game and I was hesitant to join them at first and I regret not getting into it sooner. I have been having a great time and I'm gonna keep playing it for at least a few dozen more hours and I'll honestly pick up the DLC and get even more out of it. I like "cozy" games and while this is not is free of challenge, its a very nice experience to play on normal after a long crappy day at work. You can get on for 20 minutes and do nothing and it feels good. You can play for six hours and go, "Wow, I really accomplished a lot!"

The Bad: It can be pretty grindy, resources are sort of finite. The tutorial is threadbare to say the least. I asked my friends questions on how to do things and even they were like, "I dunno man." That is also good though because there is just so much in the game. So either go in with a group of friends, be prepared for a lot of trial and error, or watch a few hours of YouTube videos that can be wildly out of date from a few months ago. The game is not free of bugs and some things are buggier than other (looking at you electric tool). The dedicated servers need help, badly. They have bugs that just don't exist in solo or peer-to-peer play. You will lose so many mounts by falling through the world. Hot tip, when you starting falling, hit F as soon as possible to dismount and it will save your life. There are too many storms and the storms are too punishing to be out in. I do not like the tech and talent tree system, I could write so much more on how they could be better. Make sure you think way ahead before you put points into something. I would recommend going deep into a single combat tree first and then picking another tree to go into. Guns are fun but resource intensive, bow and arrow is very reliable and steady, but after doing both of those for a while I ended up settling on javelins and knives. I use the hunting rifle and shotgun for the "oh " moments. Don't feel bad about editing your character or save.

The good: This will run on a potato, yes you'll have to put the graphics to low but honestly it looks pretty good on low. I can play it on my very aged work gaming laptop. The game looks good but the graphics will need some tweaking for your set up. The building is simple but robust, it uses tiles and snapping which I really like. Some people don't like tile/snapping but there are enough bits and options that it really doesn't stifle creation. The combat is fun. It's very simple and basic, but in a way that is nice. You can just fight and you'll come across something that will just mess you up badly but you never feel like it was cheap, you feel like you learned something. The game and loop is engaging and fun. They give you a lot of things to manage but none of them feel unmanageable. It feels like a good "life simulator" but for a fun video game. You have to sleep and get up and drink water and prepare food and tend to your animals as the "work" part of the game but all that enables you to do the fun parts and the process of the work is fun too. Unless it storming, then you ain't doing outside. Honestly, there are so many good things about the game that I could ramble about it for hours. Don't think you need to play multiplayer, some of the most fun I've had is playing at 3:00 AM in my solo play.

I could see why its overall score is mixed. I am not super familiar with the game at launch as I only started playing a few weeks ago. I think that, right now, this game is more than solid and I have no problem recommending it with the only caveat that you need to be willing to look things up and ask questions. It will not be hand-fed to you. The development team is incredibly active on their official Discord and answer questions and talk to the community about what's going to be added next. They do a patch every week which is just crazy and I respect it. Also no microtransactions! You buy the DLC if you like it and want more, perfect business model! If you enjoy what I like to call "stick-and-rock-games" you're going to like this. It's not as punishing as Rust, it's not as kiddy as Minecraft, its not as cartooney as Valheim, it's the right blend of all of them.

EDIT: A few weeks have gone by since I have posted this review. They have actually addressed most of the things I gripe about.
