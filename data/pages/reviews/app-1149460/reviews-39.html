\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is odd in its design but I like it. It gives you a bunch of tools for building but then puts you in these very unrewarding missions where you build up these temporary bases for only that mission at first. It feels annoying to play all the initial missions and struggle to do basic things due to random storms destroying everything or a stray wolf killing you. You don't have the talents or equipment to get a good amount of resources for creating a bed, or a home in a short period of time so it feels really bad at times starting out. After a few hours though, you get a good amount of levels and skills and even some currency with which to get equipment which will last you through some early missions. Once you get the basic set of equipment that doesn't instantly break, it starts feeling way more enjoyable. There are also some techs that are essential that are pretty easy to unlock but if you don't unlock it, it can be very rough going in the missions. A lot of this is mitigated in multiplayer as each person takes different techs and talents and supports one another with the settlement. Overall, its an enjoyable game and the combat is fairly tough and satisfying.
Edit:
Good note for starters: You can spam the first mission beachhead on the hardest difficulty with one life and then extract instantly for 50 currency a pop. It's not much but it can get you some starter equipment and allow you to skip some of the annoying starter pains of the game.
Edit Edit: You can do a lot of the missions in the open world mode so you actually can keep what you built up so this is completely a non-issue.\t\t\t\t\t

Rating: 4 out of 5 stars

Icarus, an ambitious survival game set in the uncharted realms of space, offers a unique blend of exploration, crafting, and perilous challenges. Three standout reviews encapsulate its distinct features and captivating gameplay.

Review 1: Exploration at Its Apex
"Icarus thrives on its expansive exploration. The game's diverse biomes and procedurally generated worlds provide an immersive experience. Each planet feels distinct, encouraging players to delve deeper, discover hidden secrets, and unearth valuable resources. Exploring these beautifully crafted landscapes feels like a journey into the unknown." - ExplorationEnthusiast99

Review 2: Strategic Survival Mechanics
"Survival is not just about managing hunger and thirst; it's about strategic planning. Icarus demands players to meticulously plan their expeditions, considering the time-limited nature of missions. The tension between risk and reward adds a layer of depth rarely seen in survival games. Balancing resources, weathering storms, and adapting to unforeseen challenges make every decision count." - SurvivalMaster77

Review 3: Cooperative Mastery
"Icarus truly shines in its cooperative gameplay. Working together to build, gather resources, and overcome obstacles fosters a sense of camaraderie. The game encourages teamwork and coordination, elevating the experience to new heights. Whether it's constructing a shelter or tackling formidable adversaries, the synergy among players creates unforgettable moments." - TeamworkTactician123

Icarus stands out for its richly detailed environments, strategic survival mechanics, and cooperative playstyle. However, while its ambition is commendable, occasional technical hiccups and a steep learning curve hinder the overall experience. Despite these setbacks, Icarus offers an exhilarating journey into the depths of space, making it a must-play for survival enthusiasts seeking a unique challenge amidst the stars.\t\t\t\t\t
