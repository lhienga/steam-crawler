\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tend to like basebuilders and explorers in general, so a bit predisposed to like this game. However, I've also played quite a lot of them and would place Icarus somewhere round the 80th percentile. I enjoy playing solo but definitely prefer coop. The hard currency system is a little rough but also totally unnecessary to engage with. The talent and blueprint trees are a lot of fun, most of the game is relatively intuitive, and I've experienced several pleasant moments of either "oh that's good common sense in design" + even a few "hm that solves a problem I didn't even know I wanted solved."

I like the Coop vs Solo talent trees, which helps account for the drastic differences in play styles a player may engage in with a team vs alone. Pretty generous with respec points, so you can play real careful and explore a couple trees with the same character or play fast and loose and have a quick undo.

Anyway I like it so far, I think my team all between lvl 30-40 atm.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Challenging Yet Rewarding Survival Adventure

Hours Played: 50 hours

Introduction:
As an avid fan of survival games, I was naturally drawn to ICARUS, a PvE survival game that promised a blend of mission-based and open-world gameplay on a terraformed planet named Icarus. Over the course of 50 hours of gameplay, I've had the chance to explore the game's intricacies and uncover its strengths and weaknesses.

Visual Presentation:
Due to my gaming setup, I can't provide an in-depth assessment of the game's graphics and visual presentation. However, I can attest to the fact that, despite my outdated hardware, ICARUS managed to deliver a reasonably immersive experience.

Survival Elements:
One of the aspects I appreciate about ICARUS is its adjustable difficulty levels. Starting on the easy setting allowed me to learn the game mechanics without constantly battling for survival. The planet Icarus presents relentless challenges, from its toxic atmosphere to its unforgiving wildlife, apex predators, and unpredictable weather. Planning and preparation are crucial, whether it's securing a source of oxygen, hunting for sustenance, or stockpiling essential supplies for an extended journey into the wilderness.

Game Modes:
ICARUS offers a variety of game modes, including mission-based objectives and a persistent open-world mode. My experience in both modes has been rewarding, with the open-world aspect providing a sense of long-term progression and exploration, while the mission-based gameplay offers clear objectives and structured challenges.

Cooperative Gameplay:
Collaborative play with up to eight players is a highlight of ICARUS. Forming a team with friends enhances the overall experience, allowing for efficient resource gathering and defense against the planet's threats. Cooperative gameplay truly shines as players must strategize and coordinate their efforts to thrive on Icarus.

Crafting and Harvesting:
The crafting and harvesting mechanics in ICARUS are satisfyingly immersive. Foraging for food and seeds to cultivate your own plants adds depth to the survival experience. Additionally, the ability to fish provides moments of relaxation in an otherwise challenging environment.

Difficulty and Balance:
ICARUS strikes a balance between challenge and reward that sets it apart from other survival games. The constant need to manage air, water, and food supplies while dealing with the ever-present threat of hostile creatures keeps players on their toes. This level of difficulty may not be for everyone, but for those seeking a more demanding survival experience, ICARUS delivers.

Memorable Moments:
Watching the dynamic weather systems in ICARUS, including storms that can uproot trees right in front of you, is a breathtaking experience. These moments of natural chaos add depth and immersion to the game world.

Suggestions for Improvement:
While ICARUS excels in providing a challenging survival experience, a "creative" mode that allows players to focus solely on base building without the constant need for air, water, and food management would be a welcome addition for those looking to unleash their creativity.

Conclusion:
ICARUS is a survival game that demands both strategy and teamwork. With its challenging gameplay, cooperative elements, and immersive world, it offers a compelling experience for fans of the genre. Whether you're exploring the wilderness alone or embarking on a cooperative adventure with friends, ICARUS provides a rich and engaging survival journey on the harsh planet of Icarus.\t\t\t\t\t
