\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is a difficult, open world survival game with RPG style skill trees. You'll need to plan ahead for the weather, what gear to bring, and what fauna you'll face. Death is impactful. Missions are often lengthy marathons. The basic loop is to explore the landing zone, build a shelter, complete the objective, and extract. Between missions you'll spend your currency on persistent gear to assist with the next mission. However, the slow-paced struggle against nature makes it a very immersive experience with outstanding sound design and above-average graphics.

Icarus suffered from a rocky launch. Since release there has been a tremendous amount of optimization, and nowadays it runs smooth on a decent machine. Furthermore the developers put out a content patch roughly once a week; new items, new missions, overhauled systems etc. The version history is evidence enough that the developers are actively improving their product-- a rare thing in today's early access hellscape.

As far as downsides: there isn't a great variety of enemies, some of the missions can feel repetitive, and you'll need to rebuild after each mission. To be fair, the primary struggle is versus the environment and creature variety has steadily improved. However, it's still limited and it can feel tedious slaying your hundredth wolf. As far as mission variety and engagement, it seems like they're improving and taking more chances with the New Frontiers expansion. Hopefully this innovation will spill over into the base game. Finally, with the Hypatia updates, *most* missions can be launched without returning to space and starting over, even allowing you to keep some of your crafted gear. Overall it seems like despite the rough launch, development is on the right track.

tl;dr Icarus is an underrated gem that was review bombed initially, but has dramatically improved and continues to improve. Performance and graphics is decent to good. Get it if you're interested in an immersive and grounded survival experience against a hostile environment. Skip it if you need immediate action and frequent rewards to stay interested.\t\t\t\t\t
