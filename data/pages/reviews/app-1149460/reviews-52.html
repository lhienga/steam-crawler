\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOT RECOMMENDED at MSRP. GOOD ONLY IF YOU GET BASE GAME AND NEW FRONTIERS ON SALE! I cannot emphasize this enough. The base map is like a non-procedural Rust map, populated with just Earth like creatures. I think some people are forgetting that the planets are Terraformed to be like earth, thus they will have earth like creatures. The base map though has ONLY existing earth creatures. No new creatures.

New Frontiers was a paid DLC that gives you the game you actually think you are buying when you buy the base game. It has more diverse non-earth like creatures, multiple biomes, etc... The game IS NOT WORTH FULL PRICE BASE GAME AND DLC. You need to purchase the base game and the DLC for around 30-35$ to make it worth it.

The game is NOT BAD, but its NOT 60$ GOOD. The devs seem talented, but I believe there has been some financial mismanagement behind the scenes. They talk frequently about the cost of adding X, Y, and Z to the game in their discord, but do not talk about how they are actively trying to meet the expectations of their current customers. Mod support would apparently cost millions of dollars, which is likely bogus as they want any additional content to be behind a paywall. IT apparently costs $30-50k to add a single creature to the game that is tame-able. This seems ridiculous considering that tamingrecommeded a creature consists of click on it, walking to your base, and trapping it there.

I would wholeheartedly recommend the game if the base game and New Frontiers were $34.99 together. MAYBE even $39.99 if they updated it so it does run at 30-40 FPS on a Ryzen 9 3900x, 64gb of RAM, RTX 3080 with medium settings.

What they should do is add mod support, then just develop frameworks for modders and release them at 14.99 or something. Rimworld DLC does have new content, but the greatest value is in adding new tools for modders to use to create new content. I dont understand why more companies dont follow this model. The community will make sustained micro-content while you focus on larger DLC, and make sure that your DLC provides new frameworks to efficiently add additional content with the new functionalities. This is what creates cult followings like Rimworld, Minecraft, Rust, and 7 Days to Die. The community will even by overpriced DLC, like we do for Rimworld.\t\t\t\t\t
