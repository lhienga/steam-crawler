\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried giving this game a fair shot. Being someone who loves the survival genre, playing everything from the go-to Valheim to the more obscure The Long Dark, I'm fairly versed in the nature of survival games and what makes one good or not.

One key aspect that Icarus does very poorly is leveling. When a title such as Valheim gives you the pure freedom to unlock or "learn" new items simply by finding an item (e.g Copper unlocking Bronze items), it painfully obvious how bad Icarus's system is when tied directly to XP points. You want to progress to Steal, well your stuck to hitting an xp level to manually chose to unlock the option to use Steal items. I understand this is a design choice, but from my point of view only incentives cheesing the game to hitting an xp wall, aka just murdering animals to boost xp. It's a very poorly done system in my opinion.

Another major downside is my opinion is the point of doing any of this. You might finally hit an XP level that gives you the ability to use Iron tools for instance, but after building these items your left questioning, why do I need any of this to begin with?!?!? Is it only to hit the next item in the tier, sooooo this leads to back to the original XP grind of doing things literally just to hit a "level". In short, it does not feel rewarding or dynamic, but instead feels very restrictive.

Lastly, is animal system. It's down right terrible. You want to take a trek into the snow biome and look for new items, good luck fighting a million wolves, bears, etc in route. It's comical the level this is taken too. I've broken 3 weapons in one trip killing 100+ wolves or other enemies in a small few hundred meter distance traveled. Watching enemies literally spawn directly in front you, after having murdered hundreds to that point feels downright stupid. It literally feels like an artificial difficulty metric that is used to slow progression just for the sake of doing so. Never does it feel ground in reality.

I say all this to bring me to my last point and why I uninstalled the game. You work all these broken systems to go out for a long trek to explore and find "cool" new places, only to face broken gameplay after broken gameplay. Break every weapon you have on one outing because the game throws a stupid around of enemies at you or you enter a cave which you later find have no way out and your character and all it's gear is essentially stuck because the terrain navigation doesn't allow you to exit the cave. All in all, it feels like a pointless grind, leaving you to question if you've indeed wasted hours of you time into a broken mess. Sadly, you'll come to realize this is exactly what you signed up for and I just couldn't bear to grind my teeth over it anymore.

Recommendation = Avoid and push down any questions of "this game looks beautiful, I wonder if it's good?". It's not and you will leave disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus is a game with the potential to keep you busy for a long time. At just over 20 hours in, I feel like I have only scratched the surface and so far, that's a good thing, I'm definitely going to keep digging.

I'd like to begin by saying that some of the narrative choices of this game are pretty dumb. The premise is that you are a space prospector sent to the planet Icarus which has recently befallen a terrible terraforming accident, leaving the atmosphere unbreathable and unstable, with frequent deadly storms roaming the surface. However, for some reason, the terraformers also decided to genetically engineer a bunch of creatures from earth and release them on the planet, meaning there are a bunch of apex predators wandering around making your job even harder.

"No problem!", you'd think, as space prospector from the future you're sure to have a laser gun or two knocking about? - No. Despite having travelled many light-years to get to this dangerous and toxic planet you have no weapons on arrival whatsoever. In fact, you only have your spacesuit so you can breathe. So, the first thing you do *in your spacesuit* is fashion some tools from sticks and stones...

According to the intro video this is because you couldn't afford weapons or tools before leaving. So despite having the money to afford interplanetary travel you are too tight to spring for a gun or even a knife. The premise is so lazy and poorly explained that it almost takes the shine off what is a very good game in it's current state. With a few tweaks it could even be a great game.

There is a massive amount of depth to the gameplay and a few nice little touches which I feel sets the game apart from most other survival games. The resource collecting and building are what drives the game and there is enough variety of materials and building options that progression is satisfying but not too much it becomes over-whelming. The building system is also (from the most part) very intuitive and forgiving. The graphics are good and make you want to explore the world of Icarus and see the different biomes it offers. You can interact with pretty much everything you see and everything you interact with provides it's own resource each with their own uses. There are little touches that make the game interesting, for example, drinking water directly from a lake or river might give you a parasitic infection or that spending too long in a cave will give you pneumonia. The survival and crafting system is really fun and I'm very much enjoying advancing through the tech tree and building myself what will eventually be a luxury habitation complex.

Furthermore, if that's not enough for you there are missions that you can complete. I must admit I have only done some of the "SMPL3" missions so far but am very curious to see what the story missions have in store when I feel ready to take them on.

The only real fault I can find with the gameplay so far is that the combat is not rewarding. All the enemies you face act in a similar way and can be defeated by simply back pedalling and firing arrows at them. If the creatures could be made to act more realistically and have differing behaviours then it would really elevate the game to being pretty amazing.

All in all, I'd say Icarus is a good game, verging on very good, but with the potential for being great. If you can get over the stupid backstory then it's a rich, good looking and interesting survival/crafting game.
