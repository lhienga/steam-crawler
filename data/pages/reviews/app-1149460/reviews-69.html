\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot going to try a return, but I don't think I'm going to play this much.

Weird issues:
1. Game doesn't pause, ever; not in the settings menu, inventory, or even switching out of the window. The game keeps running. Want to tweak the mouse sensitivity or change a keybinding? Better build a shelter first so you don't get mauled while making changes. I ended up creating scripts to call SIGSTOP and SIGCONT on the process to get it to pause.
2. Every resource is represented as an icon. No text. So I end up hovering over dozens of images to see what resource is what every time I want to build something. Tiring.
3. Started with open world, which seemed fine, althought I couldn't understand why horses kept attacking me. Then I tried the Potshot: Training mission. Voicover told me it'd be tough... there ended up being no threats in the mission. I hit the 'start' point on the bear and a counter started but I had not idea what I was supposed to do.

Other minor points:
"use" button opens a container but doesn't close it, have to click 'close' or hit 'esc'. Except the bed, which requires 'use' to exit since 'esc' opens the config menu.
I've got 6 hours in this game and I can't even build a cup to hold water.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t**Game needs a way to increase mission rewards** After 40-50 hours of gameplay you have next to nothing to show for it in the "workshop" unlocks. Information is not clear how the system works, but the more I learn and understand the more i realise the grind is NOT worth your time.

It's a real shame because the game is fun solo, but you make such little progression it just feels like you are running fast but going nowhere. Multiplayer is plagued by poor performance issues (hiccuping, low frames) even on high end systems with good internet connection. Memory leaks are brutal in this game, making you have to restart application frequently (every hour or so) as it eats up more and more of your memory. Going to have to use the unstuck button A LOT because you will get locked in spot on the terrain everywhere.

This game is also a Dean Hall creation, so I'd bet my firstborn child that it will never reach a state that feels like a finished project, just a bunch of half-baked ideas poorly executed in an artificially inflated grind. All I can do is hope it implements options similar to ARK where you can tailor settings to suit the play-through and experience you are looking to get out of it.

**Check forums and learn about this game before buying it** You're welcome in advance.
