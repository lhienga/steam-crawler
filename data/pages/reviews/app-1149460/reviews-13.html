First is missions, which function as a starting guide to explore each map and earn persistent upgrades/gear along the way. Once you've completed enough missions to unlock the "must have" gear, it's then over to the open world mode for an infinite survival/build/craft experience. While the missions can become tedious, I found that doing 20 or so produced enough upgrades to transition to open world.

I'd expect most players who are interested in open world survival to get at least 100 hours if not more from the base game (Olympus map). The graphics are stunning, even played at medium settings, and the crafting trees are quite robust across four tiers.

All of that said, the only negative - which is more of a reality than complaint for this genre - is the end game. Once you've maxed out crafting tiers, established passive mining operations, built your dream base, and killed the world bosses (three total but only one in Olympus open world) - there's not much to do. It becomes rinse and repeat, especially after you hit the level cap (60). Again, not really a complaint as most games in this genre haven't figured out a truly viable infinite end-game play loop, at least for PVE solo players like myself.

I'd definitely suggest getting the base game but hold off on the DLC. After spending so much time on Olympus, which is my favorite map, I couldn't bring myself to do the same thing again in Styx and Prometheus. YMMV.\t\t\t\t\t
