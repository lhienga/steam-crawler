\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter about 60 hours in Ill put in my good and bad.

The good:
Overall gameplay and survival is nice. Progressing from building thatch buildings to wood to eventually Steel and beyond.
Missions help getting you out of your comfort zone and force you to explore a bit more of the world
Weather system gives it a nice touch to find.
There are a lot of recipes to level up and discover building wise.
The talent tree is good in giving your character that slight edge of difference from other characters.
I like that you can use various points to level up outside of the game from missions to get special items delivered to you.

The Bad
Because of the nature of needing a lot of resouces for some items when you start leveling up more, the resources can sometimes get to be a bit of a grind running from one cave to the next to find more rare resources.
With that in mind some of the later blueprints of learning how to build lead to more of the excessive grind on resources.
The missions are labeled a certain way 'have tier 1 unlocked' '2 skull out of 4 difficulty.' While playing it and reaching tier 4 starting; doing a challenge like this should have been a breeze but instead turned into a massive pain because after dying and having to run all the way back, an end of mission boss was waiting around my body. Even after clearing out area and trying to sort out the junk from good stuff, continued to be bombarded in area.
There are more recipes than your level cap with more coming out steadily
The talent tree feels a bit unnecessary, a lot of the talents offering things that dont do much to support the gameplay or are redundant.
Outposts are available for purchase if you want to play the game with no threats just regular sandbox and no building toward character development or natural challenges that make sandbox game a little extra fun.

Overall:

After dying off on a mission I have since stopped playing, which is sad because I really enjoyed playing the game with the wife but it really struck a nerve.
The game probably shouldnt have come out of Early Access but at least they are working to constantly improve it. Maybe I will come back to it. There are probably better sandbox games out there for cheaper, this one felt a bit of a cashgrab.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hate to say "no", because I did have fun playing for over 40 hours, but at this point I cannot recommend this game. I am baffled by the fact that this game came out 2 years ago, still struggleling with some basic game mechanics but produced 11! DLCs that cost over 100 in total. That's straight down rude.

The Fun:
  • pleasant look
  • large skill tree
  • coop/multiplayer-option
  • a good variety of building materials and designs
  • an interesting mixture of pre-historic and futuristic elements

The Pain:
  • melee combat is terrible, because I keep missing the target's hit box, despite the visuals clearly showing that I am hitting them
  • the weather system seems nice at first but becomes extremely annoying over time. It is too frequent
  • there is only one world. I don't expect every game to have procedurally generated worlds, but the ones that don't should have more points of interest. Icarus is just an empty world with ressources and animals.
  • speaking of the world: the biomes. There is hardly a difference in the three biomes (forest, snow and desert). Besides a heat and cold effect, even most of the animals drop the same things.
  • tamed animals have the weirdest riding animation and they get stuck on saplings. Imagine fleeing from a bear and getting stuck on a tiny branch sticking out from the earth... brushes don't have hitboxes, why do saplings?? They are almost equally sized
  • You cannot use any items while riding. This means that you need to either enter your inventory and drink or eat something, or you need to jump off your mount first. This also means that you cannot open gates. If you build a fence around your base, you will need to ride to the gate, get off your mount, open the gate, get back on your mount, ride through the gate, get off your mount, close the gate and get back on your mount - seriously?
  • the building menu is not intuitive and unnecessarily difficult to navigate. You have to pre-fabricate a piece, spawn its blueprint and then hit a submenu in order to change its appearance. So instead of having the ressources in your inventory and the necessary crafting station close to you, in order to just select the exact building piece you want to place, you need to gather the ressources, navigate two different interfaces and you do that for every. single. piece. you. build.
  • There are no half-steps in building. You can build a half wall, but you cannot place a floor on that half floor. You can place it at a full step, hovering in the air without breaking apart. But you cannot place it on your half floor height.
  • You cannot split stacks to a size you want. You can either drop the entire stack, you can destroy the entire stack or you can split it in half - but you cannot just take one piece or X pieces.
  • There are three currencies that allow you to buy additional or complementary stuff. On top of that, there's an insurance system, that is not entirely clear to me. This is unnecessarily convoluted - especially considering that you have to pay for an item AND then pay again for crafting it...
  • Lastly, the source of most of my frustration, the navigation of your character. It's wonky at best. You will spend a lot of time trying to jump up ledges, that you should be able to, only to weirdly slide off of slopes. There are invisible walls in the caves that sometimes cut off parts of ressources that you were supposed to harvest. I entered a cave and carefully placed floors in a neat sequence all the way down to the bottom of the pit. But my character slid off the first floor and, like sliding down a slide, went flying straight to his death. A 2 hour corpse run ensued.

Considering all of these issues, some more personal to me, some objectively problematic for a game that has been released for 2 years, I cannot say that I would recommend this game at 34 to anyone at this point. I had fun building and progressing, but the frustrating peaks dwarf that amount of fun. I bought the game at a discount for 22 so I guess that's fine.

Instead of creating paid DLCs, the devs should fix the issues of the core game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never played this game during its rough launch, as a matter of fact, I outright ignored it, but two years later I played this on its free weekend and had a chance to try it. I can say this game is absolutely worth your time. The missions based stuff is no longer the only way to play, and a more traditional "open world" option is now available. Gameplay-wise I have really enjoyed the tech/talent progression. I have a very strong feeling of specialization in something that fits my play style. I went for resource management / habitation trees. The ambient tracks for music this game has are surprisingly good and add a lot to the atmosphere of the game. The combat is something that I found remarkably non-buggy which was a breath of fresh air when compared to games I've played previously. (Ark, Conan etc.) The game does have its bugs though, don't get me wrong. Items I've harvested or dropped occasionally fall through the world, trees I've chopped have instead of falling like normal, have just launched into the air and sometimes nearly killed me. Nothing game breaking or anything that makes it unplayable, but as of this review these are still problems in the game.

All I have to say is this game is a far cry from the mess it was before, and the consistent support and communication for this game from the devs is very encouraging to see.\t\t\t\t\t
