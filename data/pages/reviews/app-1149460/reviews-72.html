\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love survival games! But it's hard for me to put into words how much I dislike this one.

UPDATE: Steam wouldn't give me my money back so I've pushed through and played it more. Also seems to be able to put my dislikes into words.

While I still dislike the building tool the game has grown on me a bit. To be fair I have been playing it as an open world game which I have been told is not how this game is really meant to be played.

LIKE
- The storms. It's exciting that weather means something although it's really over the top.
- Seems to be a lot to do. Lots of different talent paths to focus on.
- Lots of different resources to gather. Again, lots to do.
- Killing something attracts predators. That's really cool.

DISLIKE
- Building tool UI is awful. So often the piece you're looking to build is buried behind the "parent piece" which you need to first make to see what it's variants are. I found myself building pieces I didn't need and wasting resources just to search through the variations for the 1 pieces I wanted. Trying to understand the system while my house blows down in a storm had me rage quitting several times.
- Animals look like people in animal costumes. Look at them. Especially the bears. These are two dudes in a bear suit I'm sure of it.
- I don't see animals attacking each other?? I guess it's a peaceful world except everything hates you? A baby deer can be walking past a wolf and it doesn't care. The wolf REALLY hates you gathering fiber though! Kill on sight for doing that.
- You have to repair a structure before you can tear it down?? What??
- Animals are setup to walk near you rather than randomly wandering. I built on a plateau in which there was one difficult way to reach it. When I go to leave there is always a long line of animals all trying to get up to where I built. Deer, boar, bears, wolves, goats, everything all hopping up and down trying to get to my base. When there was no base there they didn't care about it. Then when they do get up there they just wander around and leave. It seems obvious that the game is telling them to be there for a possible interaction. LAME!
- I could see it on other planets but I have to gather oxygen from rocks in a lush earth-like world? What's all the other earth animals breathing?
- And finally DEATH HORSES! They are the meanest most hyper aggressive animals in the game. If you get too close to one I get it, but they will hear you closing a chest in your house, rip down your walls, and stomp you to death!

So still a dislike from me, but like I said it's growing on me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the lesser popular and known survival crafting games, that actually became one of my favorites (and I play lots of them all the time).

It started out a bit weird (in a unique way), without having a proper open world gameplay with a continuous style of playing... which was really weird for many people (myself included), and had ONLY missions, when you drop down on the planet, must craft survive do some tasks, sometimes less time-consuming, sometimes much more, with a proper base building requirements. And it was kinda strange always leaving that behind and starting anew in each new mission. Even though that has it's charm kinda. But I mean... missions are still here. But so is Open World, which is endless and continuous for that map (we have 3 now, which is pretty cool, and you can get your character up and down the orbit going to different maps and saves if you really wanna).

There's a lot to say about this game. Like it has a really enjoyable levelling and improving and learning blueprints mechanics and growing through tiers of buildables etc. And the weather mechanics, albeit annoying they can be sometimes, are pretty cool too.
Problems are also present though, like kind of a poor optimization and this game just being on UE4 is not completely right for the engine... but...
The thing is, what I really love about this game: the devs are here, they're actually listening (even if that doesnt always look like that) and the updates are weekly, and they are actually seen, stuff being improved, added and fixed in this game. And I LOVE IT. Actually with all the life and other games I have I don't always keep up with all the new things, even if kinda minor. But even going away for a few months is almost surely will guarantee you some new content to check out :)
Anyways, it's really worth checking out if you like the genre :) It's getting better constantly :)\t\t\t\t\t
