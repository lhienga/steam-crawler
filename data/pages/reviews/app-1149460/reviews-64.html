\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has a lot of potential if the developers keep working on it, but it's missing a lot. I'll point some things out.

From the very beginning the game just feels easy, because there's not really any serious threats in the world. There's nothing hunting you or any serious events happening. There's animals which are just easy to kill and weather events which you can just wait out, that's it. Around level 20 I started to feel that "where's the challenge here", because there isn't.

The building system is pretty good, but it's lacking a reason to build. All you need protection from is the weather events so why would you need to put effort into building much?

Missions are very simple and honestly boring. They are something you could typically compare to some MMO's first quests. Go kill x amount of these animals, protect this thing from attacking animals etc. Also why would wild animals attack some electrical devices, that's pretty silly idea. The game needs something else than just wild animals roaming around.

I truly hope the developers would work on some dynamic events and adding a lot more content in the world, because mechanically and visually the game is very decent, but it's missing challenge, content and endgame. The talent and skill system and crafting is also pretty nicely done.

In the game's current state, I can't recommend it and honestly it costs a lot for what it is. I hope the devs read the reviews and improve the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the result of decades of experience in survival games development put into a format that is interesting to casual and hardcore players.
I love how I can recognise Stationeers in many aspects of Icarus.
The mechanics are simple yet well balanced, the survival aspects are not particularly difficult but refined enough to make it important to prepare for long expeditions. Being able to cook more refined food is not necessary but it provides very useful buffs depending on what you cook. This is great because while survival is central to the gameplay, you won't spend most of your time simply trying not to die of thirst.

The ambience and setting are also simple but they are an immersive hard sci-fi equivalent of current day tech minus the FTL trip to Icarus. There are a couple live action cutscenes that develop the lore that I enjoyed a lot.

After 18 hours I progressed with one character to level 20 and unlocked the story missions.\t\t\t\t\t
