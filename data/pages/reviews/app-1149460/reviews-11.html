\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIcarus has come a long way from its rocky launch. There are now significantly more missions, game modes such as open world, and additional content through DLCs. The game mechanics have undergone drastic changes since its initial release, making it a strongly underrated game deserving way more recognition.

Initially, Icarus was a game where players were sent on missions with a timer that continued even when they were not in the game. However, it has evolved into a much richer experience with the introduction of features like an open world gamemode, dedicated servers, and a variety of animals and alien creatures. Players can engage in activities such as taming mounts, hunting, fishing, and intricate building mechanics, which have greatly enriched the gameplay experience.

Whether you're embarking on a solo mission, riding a buffalo into battle against wolves or lions, facing off against the giant worm boss, or peacefully constructing a cabin in the outpost game mode, Icarus offers a diverse range of experiences.

One notable aspect of Icarus is its responsive development team, who are always attentive to player feedback. This makes a positive community atmosphere and contributes to ongoing improvements to the game.

I highly recommend purchasing Icarus and investing in its DLCs. If you enjoy building I would definitely recommend supporting the developers through purchasing in game maps like outposts or furniture packs as this is a great way to show appreciation for their hard work and dedication. Overall, I hope this review was helpful for people considering buying Icarus.\t\t\t\t\t
