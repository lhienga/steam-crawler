\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am really impressed with this game. I've played so many games of this genre and finally one that offers a new take or approach.

People complain about the missions and starting over and I get the argument. However I feel that as you progress your character, starting over and being more capable and expanding to different maps with greater difficulty presents a very nice change. I can't count how many times I wish Minecraft had a true RPG type feel with character progression. Now for me this seems to hit the spot.

When possible I also sometimes play with my son. Because of the way the game is I don't feel trapped on the world waiting for when he can play next. I can lift off and continue to play and progress my character on his own and then come back when my son can play. For me this is a good balance and offers a kind of survival platform with lots of talents to customize my character as I progress.

At first I was put off by the amount for the game, the expansion and the DLC's. However, we started with the base game to make sure we could get into it. Now as we do play more I plan to purchase them as needed. I'm also encouraged by what I hear about the dev's being active and seeing the weekly updates. This really feels like the survival game I've been looking for.

I will also add that the amount of different animals seems refreshing. I was also very impressed when I saw the wind blow trees down, something I've never seen in any other game. For me this is a game I plan on playing for quiet some time.

This game is kind of Conan Exiles, but on another planet called Icarus, with animals and trees and stone and caverns. The missions are aberrant and randomly programmed, like you have to go and do them in another biome, but you don't have access to that biome. Bugs are on another level. Either there were too many ideas to implement, or the programmers said... Let's put everything in. I bought it, don't do like me. There are much better games than this one.
