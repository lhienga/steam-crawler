\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt this moment this is one of the most fun VR games out here. Once the shear glee of feeling like an earthbender passes, you are left with a really satisfying sport sparring game with compelling mechanics. You gain points to rank as you spar with others players, and each rank unlocks new moves. Each moves can be put into a combo with others, and that feels sooo good to find the tricks that you like. Example: You can get out from the ground a pillar, (rank 1) you can punch things in front (rank 1). So for a while you pillar punch adversaries into oblivion. Then you can glide in front of you (rank 3) for a quick boost. Now imagine mastering the pillar, then the quick punch forward and the quicker glide. Pillar, punch, charge, punch, BAM ! Surprise. You just got from part of the map to the other end with a shield that you threw in the face of the other at the last second. An that is one of the simpler tactics.

On the other topics, art style is fun and clean, shapes are great and each "structure" (the rocks) are easely recognizable for quick thinking. There is a training zone for yourself. The UI is rarelly clunky despite the fact that it's almost all turns and levers inside the game that you have to pull to use it (no beam of choosing like other games). Sound effects and haptics immerse well.

TLDR;
- You *feel* like an earth bender. Of the Legends of Korra type. Well a saw someone do the boulder thing so classic earthbender thing also available.
- The fact that it's mostly a PvP game should not fright you, think of it as fine people sparring in a dojo. It's really fun.
- Great community, non verbal communication inside game match that promotes exchange. Choose any rank as the matchmaking and you will always find a sparring partner of higher skill ready to pause the 'match' to explain a move or let you train a new combo. And then it starts again. (there is a option to chat but I never seen anyone use it, probably because it's new)
- Enough players in Europe, Central America and Australia to not let you wait for matches. (that span all the world time zones). I don't know the demographics of the people playing, but off office hours and weekends are the fullest spots.
- Good tracking, albeit you better calibrate the settings on the menu and test them with moves. I got a lot better after I set my arms lenght to be shorter than real to allow for more natural moves (stand in T pose, arch a bit you arms = short arms calibration)
- Diversity of gamestyle with the addition of trinkets (gems) that gave bonus fit for being moving type, blocky type, agressive, defensive etc.
- also dirt cheap when you take into account the price of the gear needed to do VR

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game exceeded what I thought it would be, the idea should have been there with every other vr game: PUT WORK IN TO GET RESULTS. So many other games hold your hand and walk you down a path until you know how to play, rumble doesn't. Rumble gives you a quick tutorial so you can play, but getting good is sort of like a sport, the more you put in the more you get out. Graphics are amazing and even though graphics are good and there are always different requests to my (sort of good) computer, it runs smoothly, especially for a beta game.
I have never seen a game that feels more like a sport in my life and I love it, it's motivating. and for the past 1 week of my life I have been practicing this game and meeting new people. Which takes me to the community, why is everyone so nice. Like yes I also follow the unspoken rule of rumble that you should always help new players, but I have never had a fight with another player. And as long as they use their mic, I also have always had a conversation with another player.
Let me explain two more things before I go.
1.The trailer for rumble needs to be changed, what you see there is an older version, so I recommend watching rumble youtube to see what you are actually getting.
2.How rumble works
Rumble uses hand poses to track what it should spawn, or manipulate. It is very precise, but it is never the games fault, trust me just go to the tutorial board, do what you were doing, and see what is wrong. So please don't drop the game if you can't get it right.
Rumble is my new favorite game and I highly recommend it, if you want to feel like a superhero this is the game, no buttons just your hands. Have fun :)\t\t\t\t\t

I seriously think that if you are considering buying a different VR game, put it on hold. Wishlist it. You have to try this game. Thank me later.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile im sure this game has lots of potential, i cant bare playing it in its current state. Some of these are somewhat personal issues, but the rest seems like issues with the game itself, or possibly the game was not well designed for playing through a quest 2. I also had to change openxr to steamvr and relaunch the game but eh small issue.

First thing after the titlecard i wanted to open options which you have to walk around to find, and i found that snap turn has a fade to black transition that cant be disabled, cant change the amount it rotates, "smooth turn" is just continuous mini snap turns, voice chat is default to on and changing options requires moving a lever which half the time i cant even grip.

After going through the limited options available, i went to learn some move sets which requires performing some poses, which is where the issues i cant just brush away occur. There were two moves available, with a third im assuming i have to walk around to find but had not, but of the two moves available, neither of them consistently work for me. Even trying to do it as best as i can without rushing, it only worked at most half the time. In order for the moves to work, you have to face exactly where your hands are, no looking slightly off another direction, and the attacks appear where youre looking at rather than where your hands are, though for some reason it seemed to keep going towards the left of my view for me. So to aim and hit a target, you have to look directly straight without moving your eyes, do the poses directly in front of you, and aim with the center of your view rather than your hands.

I was already very frustrated by this point, so i gave up and went to look around some more, found you can actually sprint in this game and didnt have to slow walk everywhere, or so i thought, because in order to do so, you have to swing your arms like youre sprinting. Thing is, no matter how i tried swinging my arms, it mostly didnt detect me trying to sprint, and the most i could go is a single step before slowing back down.

In a game like this, where you have to pvp with others, having so much issues even getting the controls to work rather than playing the game is just not gonna work out, especially with the many different moves and competitiveness the game gets later on. Before i could get to the player vs player or player vs ai, i got to the player vs game, and i lost.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had to think carefully about how I would review Rumble. It is a fantastic game that provides deep, complex game-play. The mechanics are tight and responsive, once you take the time to truly learn them. Overall, I would say Rumble is worth picking up. However, there are some glaring flaws.

First, the on-boarding process is abysmal. The existing tutorial does not adequately explain the poses or the significance of hand rotation vs. hand position. Howard the bot opponent is also extremely simplistic, meaning for any meaningful practice, you must play against human opponents. Fortunately, a better tutorial is a planned update.

Second, the player-base is tiny. It averages around 50-80 people on a weekend worldwide. The majority of those players are in the US East and EU regions, as well. This is important because Rumble uses peer-to-peer connections for multiplayer. One player is selected to be the host, and that player has 0ms of latency, while their opponent is the client and has variable latency depending on their geographical distance to the host. Practically speaking, this means that if you are not located in the US East or EU regions, you will be forced to play against people a great distance away, leading to 50% of your matches having horrible latency. It makes your wins feel cheap and your losses feel unfair. The main solution for this really is just to have a decent player base in each region. This will likely improve as a new tutorial is released, and when standalone Quest support is eventually implemented.\t\t\t\t\t
