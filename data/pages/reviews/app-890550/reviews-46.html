\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This game is so fun for those who love the grind of genuine self-improvement.

Before you buy, know that the controls for this game requires practice like real martial arts. That being said, this game is addicting for all the right reasons.

Rumble VR scratches the itch for a vr game that goes beyond interacting with the world. It fully embraces vr motion controls in a way that feels fantastically real. Where many games simulate progress with rising numbers, bright colours and exciting sounds (which is totally valid), Rumbles progression is very real. The dopamine surge is huge with every move you learn and every hit you land on the opponent. Even the happy accidents where different moves collide to create wild interactions is so fun! Like launching a disk at someone only for it to collide with your opponents disk, causing them to ricochet out of the ring at high speed.

I highly recommend this game, though I will leaves some critiques below:
-Though practice will improve consistency with every move, sometimes the game will simply not recognise a move immediately. This either leads to a delay for the move to trigger or it wont trigger at all. This however may be more the fault of current vr hardware and ping.
-The player's slow movement speed makes total sense during a match, however it makes moving around the main hub very slow. The running mechanic also needs a bit of polish as it's more exhausting than the basic moves. I suggest that the developers either increase the movement speed in the hub or implement fast-travel boards in the different areas of the hub.
-This is totally unnecessary, but some level of character customization would be awesome. I know the devs have more content down the pipeline so it may already be in the works.
-I may be nitpicking, but I'd like to see some more options for the movement controls. Specifically I'd like to see more options for the snap turning. I'd like be able to turn less than 90 degrees at once, and the brief fade to black is a bit jarring. Also it seems the game needs to take a second to find the sound sources again every time I snap turn. I know that's all very specific and that this game is still early access, but it's a small level of polish that is only noticed when it isn't there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe folks in the reviews complaining about having to rank up to unlock moves are essentially people who haven't completed the tutorial yet: It's a martial art, folks, Boulder rank isn't gold 5 it's purple belt and you're gonna fight the brown belt because he's who's around right now. Oh no you lost, boo hoo, get ranked up, idiot, you're a Tor now.

Christ is this a good game. Pose-based pseudo martial arts. Almost completely bug free. Very focused gameplay. Drenched in sweat on the daily. Not much BM but man is it funny when it happens.

A couple compromises, sure, but really this is *good*. The poses are fun to learn, their use is nuanced and can be combo'd into other stuff as you become better at understanding both what they do and, more importantly, the specific flexibility in the criteria for casting them. There is certainly fighting game levels of nuance here, and this may be a genre-pioneering game.
