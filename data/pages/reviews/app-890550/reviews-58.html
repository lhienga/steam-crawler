\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great. One of the few games out there that actually utilizes the capabilities of VR controls.

Despite being a multiplayer-only game, I've had no issues with finding a match so far on Europe servers. Whether that keeps up is unclear, as always, but unlikely most VR multiplayer games, this one isn't dead within literal days.

About the "poor" gesture recognition: that's a feature, not a bug. That's the base skill of the game - being able to consistently place your hands in the same pose. Note that I said "pose", not "position". What matters primarily is the palm rotation, and their position relative to each other. Whenever I had issues with casting a spell, looking up its pose revealed I was not rotating one of my palms correclty..

Where the game does suffer is the spell unlock system being connected to playing matchmaking.
Firstly, matchmaking is very loose by default. Currently that's unnecessary, as there are enough players, so I suggest to immediately turning it down to search within 3 ranks, or even fewer. By default it can put players with 0 hours against players with 20, and it will.
As a new player, you might find yourself facing against somebody much more experienced, while also having much fewer spells. I understand why devs wouldn't just drop all of the spells on a new player, as their execution is a bit overwhelming even as it is, but it really does feel terrible to get destroyed by spell combos you can't even learn about inside the game.

Ultimately I feel like the main issue with spell unlocks is that currently the Wall is just too good. At a naive level of play it's the strongest defensive spell, and all of its counters unlock later. Even at higher ranks, the gameplay is very Wall-centric, as it can be one of the best offensive spells, combined with Kick->Uppercut->Straight, etc. Currently a single Wall->Straight can occasionally knock you off across half the arena, and you don't get the Dash until much later to come back from that. It would really make more sense if the Wall was a weaker defense against light attacks, such as disc, and Pillar was a stronger defense against heavy attacks, but smaller in size. That would also mean Pillar->Straight is more useful, and will make low ranks less Disc and Ball dominated, especially with a moving Ball not crushing a grounded PIllar anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

I was in karate as a kid and learning the different earthbending moves feels like learning the form for moves in karate. I've spent 3/4 of my time in this game practising alone and I'll still get blown away by some of the move combos I didn't even think of. As I've been levelling up you inevitably run into players better than you. And its usually a one sided stomp, but more often than not, it turns into a impromptu training session. There is no voip but having someone communicate with gestures to stop a fight and help correct form is a really cool experience.

The moves requiring precision is an amazing design choice, it makes physical fitness an advantage as it's easy to get tired mid fight and lose the autonomic ability to cast the basic spells. I'm pretty fit and 6 hours of this game over 3 days had 3 muscle groups sore as if i lifted weights. I can see this being a problem for alot of people, but I hope they keep it like this.

I've just hit the point where I've started unlocking movement abilities and it makes me super excited to get better and see how this game feels at a high level.\t\t\t\t\t

Sprinting, is annoying. Please add either a way to press down on the joystick as an alternative or make it more forgiving. This is something that will be either done in haste or regularly. And quite frankly its too difficult and unnatural. Please fix.
