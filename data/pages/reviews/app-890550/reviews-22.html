\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR solid PvP with a lot of depth and a very satisfying moveset. *Very* small (but friendly) playerbase but definitely worth a look.

Rumble is one of those games that lets me justify buying a VR set. The devs did a great job of making the gameplay satisfying and giving you a ton of defensive and offensive options with a limited but versatile moveset.
The initial learning curve is pretty rough if you're not used to having to adopt forms and poses like you would in boxing, calisthenics, weightlifting, martial arts e.t.c. The moves don't work if your hands aren't in the right place and rotated the right way, but it's difficult to understand what you're doing wrong unless you spend a lot of time practicing like you would in a physical sport.
But the game does provide a very solid training area so you can do that, and when you really get into the flow of it and start becoming unconsciously competent it's a hell of a lot of fun and very satisfying to engage with. And a lot of the moves can be used or combined in unexpected ways to give the game much more depth than it first seems.

A couple of turn-offs though, as it's in early access it is missing some features but a lot of that would be set dressing around a solid core. One thing that may be more problematic is that there's also a *very* small playerbase, at peak times I typically don't have to wait more than 2-3 minutes for a match, which honestly isn't that much different to For Honor (especially when Rumble has near-instant loading times) however the moveset you've got is determined by your rank, and you can only rank up by fighting other players. You still get points when you lose a match, and there is a lot of value in getting familiar and comfortable with every move before gaining new ones so I wouldn't say this is a flaw, however it does mean that you have to sink a couple of hours in before the moveset really opens up.

Overall it's definitely unique and very satisfying. It's unfinished and the playerbase falls within a tiny Venn diagram of PvP VR users who enjoy physical activity, but I've had a lot of fun with it and I can see it becoming one of my favourite PvP games.\t\t\t\t\t
