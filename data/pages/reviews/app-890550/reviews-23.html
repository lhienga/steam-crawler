\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRumble is a truly unique VR fighting game, that utilizes all that VR can do to make it unique. Every move in the game is performed by moving into specific poses. This makes this game, something that can only be done in VR. It is hard and tiring, in many ways its like learning a real world martial art. You have to practice every move you learn to commit it to muscle memory. Practice is hard and rewarding, and as you progress you unlock more moves. What is so interesting though, is learning how different moves peace together. At the beginning, you have three basic moves, disk, thin wall, and push. As you practice, you begin to see how the different moves play with each other. The thin wall is a defensive tool, but can be pushed to become offensive. The disk does not do a lot of damage, but it is a quick projectile that you can launch as a single move. But as you practice, you begin to see more ways moves fit together. You discover that there is a small enough window to push a disk, launching it farther at incredible speeds. And you learn that by simply rotating your wrist, it becomes easy to make many thin walls, even while moving. This game is hard, tough, and challenging, with no guidebook on how to get better. It takes time to improve, but that makes it all the more satisfying when you are able to defeat stronger and stronger opponents.

There are only really two real issues I have with the game. The first issue, a bit more minor, is some of the balancing with the recent shift stone update. With its initial release, some of the shift stones were definitely stronger then others, with some offering passive bonuses that did not change how you would play. With the most recent balancing changes, most of these issues have been resolved, but there are definitely some shift stones that are stronger then others, but nothing game breaking. The bigger issue with the game is the small player base, and its reliance on online play. The only way to level up your belt and learn new moves, is by playing online matches. Because this is a early access steam only VR title, there is not the largest player base. I can consistently find matches, but typically in adjacent regions, not the one I am in, leading to increased lag that can sometimes pose an issue in matches. Because the game only has two online modes, it is not usable for people with poor internet. For this issue, the only real solutions would be to add some form of single player mode, which the developers seem interested in based on a recent player survey, as well as to to have a standalone version of the game for Quest users, to help expand the market of who can play the game.

TLDR: Its an amazing game, that truly utilized body movement for its game play. Its hard and tiring, but rewarding. Would not recommend for someone who has poor internet connections.\t\t\t\t\t
