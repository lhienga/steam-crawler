It's definitely rough around the edges at it's current stage but still feels great to play and updates seem to be often. It doesn't feel like a game that would cause issues for mildly motion sick players. But with the lack of teleport (which is justified in the fact that the game would be completely unbalanced with the addition) the only people I wouldn't recommend this game for are those who get heavily and easily motion sick.

One of the best and most underrated VR games out there at the moment. Give it the chance it needs, we need more people to go up against!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce you get the hang of it, Rumble is an absolute blast!
It can be very discouraging for the first few hours. Your hand positions have to be very precise, else nothing happens.
Unfortunately the player base is quite small, which is understandable for an action packed "niche" VR 1v1 game.
The community is very eager to help newer players out. I've been shown many combos and other tricks to help me improve my game. All of which I could teach other, newer players at a later point.

Some constructive feedback:
- Snap turning could use some improvements. The angle is not adjustable, the sound gets cut off, the blink duration is quite long.
- Progression to unlock moves is a little questionable. Moves like Dash, Jump, Parry or Hold seem like absolute essentials. Unlocking them at blue or red belt is quite limiting. I understand the decision, though. Going through the belts gives you enough time and practice to get a good grip of the absolute basics such as disc and punch. I feel like Dash and Jump should be available for white belts, Parry and Hold to Yellow or Green Belt.
- Levers feel a little awkward. I feel like I always have to overexaggerate the motion of pulling/pushing.

Other than that I have no complaints.
It's impressively awesome.
