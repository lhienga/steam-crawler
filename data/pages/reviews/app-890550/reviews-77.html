\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed on Oculus Quest 2 without problems.

Now this is a workout I'm happy to get by.

It is an eartbending game that's inspired by martial arts. In fact in order to do anything, you need to learn hand positions precise enough. If they are precise enough you cast a skill. If it's not nothing happens. This forces you to practice your gestures to get better, same as martial arts. You can see you get better as you will be able to cast skills more frequently as you practice. This is satisfying enough on it's own, and triplely more so as you cast the skills you practice to beat someone in the ring.

Skills are unlocked as you get into fights and rank (or belt) up. Faster if you win, slower if you lose. But the default skills are not the end of it. You can combine skills together to create your own combos as you see fit. While it's not too complex at the moment, it is good enough to experiment with different combos. Which is an big plus that I didn't think this game had.

Highly recommended, more so if you're into martial arts, AtLA or both.\t\t\t\t\t
