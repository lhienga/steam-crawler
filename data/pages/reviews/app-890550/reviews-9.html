\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMain takeaways:
- Intensely satisfying once things start clicking
- Great community (will stop fights to show you stuff, generally nice people)
- Feels awesome to lob rocks taller and wider than you
- Visual style is really appealing and clean
- Fighting styles are completely different from player to player and changes the flow of the game pretty much every match.
- It's interesting to see big names on the leaderboard but it's suddenly very real when one of the heavy hitters appears in a match and proceeds to beat your ass up and down the ring.

Main Criticisms
- High skill means that the game is hard to get into
I really want to get my friends into this but it's a hard sell to ask someone to physically practice a videogame if they aren't into it. If you are easily frustrated then this is not the game for you.
The poses are unforgiving and it takes quite a bit of practice to start getting them correct without having to think about it. It's almost always something you are doing but without coaching or someone to watch you and find the error it can be like hitting your head against a stone wall and expecting the wall to break. Not something you want to be figuring out in the middle of a online fight. This is where I think the skill cliff really comes in, if you can get over that the game really opens up. Even then when you get into an endurance match with an opponent it can be a test of who starts to fall apart first and start making mistakes.

- Matchmaking could use some improvement.
Due to the small community size and P2P matchmaking some games are just terrible with high latency and clear host advantage. Summoning a structure and then having to count off two or three seconds before it appears is intensely annoying (potentially described as rage inducing if you are wound up) and completely ruins the flow of the match. Half the time you're about as well off just standing there and letting them pelt you. The other downside to the host advantage is that the client's defense is much more limited in move options available to them as the latency grows. I completely give up on parry or hold at a certain point, next straight, and finally when stomp isn't working I have to do 100% dodging because the object is still going to hit me even after I get the parry/hit effect and sound. Maybe if there was a way to artificially train for this you could get it down but it would further increase the height of the skill floor and ceiling for newer players. With the time of day I'm usually playing I frequently end up with a player from Aus on USEast servers and I can only imagine how bad his experience is when it's the other way around. Shoeless, if you see this it's great playing against you but man it sucks so bad at the same time.

- I honestly think the moveset could be better distributed over the belts.
Having the limited moveset of the pebble (white belt) is probably not helping things with the limited playerbase. There's not really a way to not slam dunk the lower belts once you have more stuff unlocked. The matchmaking experience is really reliant on higher belts having some restraint when they meet up with the lower belts. The bane of my early matches was running into higher belts that had hold unlocked since I had no way to easily break their hold or stop larger moving structures and short of sprinting (which I find to be finicky) and dodging is very difficult since dash is a late unlock and the logical leap to discover disc jump is not something you'd find without seeing someone do it in a fight (I'm still bad at it). I might make the argument that dash should come before jump but I'm so-so on that one. If I was to make one suggestion, it would be to make parry something unlocked early on. Straight works, but it's use as a defensive tool alongside stomp or uppercut isn't immediately obvious as a move literally called "parry." Very rarely do I see new players make any attempt at any kind of defense beyond standing there and taking it when you chuck discs at them. It usually starts to click pretty quick with pillars and they'll straight to block but with discs they just kinda stand there and make no attempts to dodge/block like a deer on the first day of the season.

- XP gaining opportunities beyond matchmaking PVP when?
I predict this will help the most with players that get stomped in early matches giving up. Heck, maybe make Howard a boss battle gateway to yellow belt (this is just spitballing).

- It's only a matter of time before I break a controller, wall, or light fixture via punching it.\t\t\t\t\t
