\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy review needs an update: This game is a ton of fun and there is a definite learning curve. If you end up like me, it'll take a few hours to understand the synchronized wrist rotations and arm movements to perform the combat moves and even then, you're likely to still struggle. Matchmaking is hit or miss on the lobbies and I often have to loosen the match-making to include people 2 or 3 levels above my skill level to find anyone to play against.

If you manage to get into a match, the player base is welcoming, patient, and typically willing to help you practice before they completely pulverize you. Honestly, this is refreshing because you can tell how these dedicated individuals really want this game to take off and succeed. I'd recommend this game because learning how it works is half the fun. Even if
unlocking new moves is a grind and the movement system isn't perfect by any stretch of the imagination, it has a lot of potential going forward.

Some extra game modes or even a simple storyline would make this game feel more "finished", but it's still a fun challenge to pick up and play when you have some spare time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started by reading the bad reviews of this game so I was very skeptical. Let me start by saying all the reviews that say the game isn't good because of its progression system/match making system are blown way out of proportion. Matchmaking is very quick (US West) , you don't need to worry about only playing against people your own rank, even if you don't win it will be a great learning experience on how you can try to counter the more advanced moves. My first match was against a black belt and he helped me figure out my moves before dueling me as he saw I was having trouble with my form.

It does not take long to level up to the next belt (even if you are just losing) . Practice your starting moves until you can consistently cast them before starting multiplayer. The game has a little bit of learning curve but once you can start consistently earth bending you'll be having a blast win or lose.

The game is great fun once you learn how to play and the community is very friendly and very full of sportsmanship. It has an active discord server as well where you can get all the help you need. I think this game has the potential to become one of the best vr titles available. I can't wait to see what the dev team brings in future updates!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually write reviews but this game deserves all the positive attention it can get. This is a very unique game that is so well designed on so many levels. The game feels great to play, once you get the hang of it. The audio, visual and haptic design makes the moves impactful and satisfying.

In the beginning the moves may feel clunky, as the tutorial for the moves isn't that great but once you get the hang of them you will feel like an absolute badass. So please take your time with it, if you're tired or frustrated you'll probably be performing moves incorrectly.

Once you get the hang of it you'll want to jump into some matches, the player base isn't huge but the waiting times really aren't too bad. In my experience most players of higher level will adjust a bit to your level and don't immediately stomp you, allowing everyone to learn and unlock new moves. A great system as you only unlock new moves as you have gotten familiar with your current moveset, there is just one move in the entire set that doesn't feel rewarding to unlock!

Honestly one of my favorite experiences in VR, can't wait to get some friends into this game to have some sparring fun.\t\t\t\t\t
