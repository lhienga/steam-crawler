\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not invest time in this game. The idea and the core elements are extremely well done! The game itself is a good concept, and when you play it, it feels cool; however, it are three elements of the game that make it borderline unplayable.

the first problem is the server setup. It's bad, it's not only bad but it's also designed to benefit one player over another. the servers for this game are currently set up in a way in which one player hosts the lobby and the other joints. if you are the host, you will have a great time, and the game will work as intended. if you're not, however, the game becomes unplayable. This is especially the case when you are playing with people who are not on the same servers as you. The delay is extremely frustrating; it makes summoning rocks and getting the correct hand placement much more difficult. So yeah, that is a huge issue, especially in a game where it's meant to test a skill. Not being able to summon things correctly and having a huge delay half the time makes the game much less enjoyable and takes away the competitive edge. It is also detrimental to the feeling of playing the game; when you win hosting the server, you know you didn't play your opponent at their best, and when your opponent hosts the server, you know you weren't at your best. It is a sad way to ruin a great idea.

2 UI, I like the look of the UI, however, joining and hosting training servers and space matches is a nightmare, it makes it harder to play with friends and needs to be reworked. The difficulty in the social aspect of this game makes the already small player base feel more disconnected. You're never able to see how many training lobbies are active at a given time. You can only make a filter and join a random one. This is likely contributing to the limited number of people still playing the game.

3. The developers are treating this like an experimentnot that this is inherently bad, but the developers are ignoring huge fundamental issues in the game. Instead of fixing issues with tracking, map glitches, and rocks getting stuck to your hands, they are adding COSMETICS! Instead of improving their game to better support a larger player base, they are trying to milk the loyal player base further. Not a good move at all.

That is my problem with the game. Keep in mind that I write this out of love. I love this game; I currently have 75 hours in it, and it's fun. Sadly I am disappointed by the steps the developers have taken since release and wish they would address fundamental issues first before playing around with cosmetics.
