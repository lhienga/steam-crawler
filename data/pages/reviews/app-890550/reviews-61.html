TLDR: At its best, Rumble combat is quick-paced and rightly challenging, but fun. At its worst, it is frustratingly unresponsive and unfair.


The good news is that there's a lot of good here and I think it's worth the price if the premise interests you. However, be aware that Rumble fully earns its Early Access label for a multitude of reasons.


Stuff that's already solid
  • Combat foundation is great - yes, it's 100% an earthbending game and it lives up to the premise.
  • There's a surprising amount of depth, even if it's not immediately obvious.
  • I love that different (player) opponents have recognisably different fighting styles. Skills range from spamming the disc move to punching a flying rock out of the sky.
  • Community has great sportsmanship, be sure to fist punch before the match starts :)


Stuff that's more sand than rock
From minor problems/gripes to high-level problems for the future:
  • Cannot see past dust - if the opponent is spamming you with discs it gets really hard to see. The easiest way to counter this spam is to bring up a wall - but there's no way to check if you did it correctly because you can't see!
  • Low player base: yeah, if you want a game, you pretty much need to connect to the US region - YMMV here.
    The good news is that once you're there, it's easy to find a match
  • Latency: if you're connecting from outside the US, forget having a fair, responsive match. This is true in any other game but especially terrible in competitive VR combat.
    Effectively cuts your viable moves in half - you can only really go on the offensive since defensively using your moves (e.g parrying an incoming rock) is a guaranteed miss.
    Not sure if related to latency, but occasionally no matter what I do my moves aren't recognised, until they suddenly are (almost like "buffering").


Quick tips to potential pebblings
  • The trailers don't tell you this, but the game fully expects you to train and get the movements exactly right - it's fun but not exactly casual
  • Take some time to figure out your own combos, preferably outside of a live match
  • Chin up! It'll take some time before you find your groove
