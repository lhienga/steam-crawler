\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame slaps. It's hard to get competent but when your muscle-memory kicks in and you're slinging rocks left and right you really start to feel like a badass earth-bender.

What really makes this game shine are the combo techs you can do. The hand movements can be chained together to apply properties to existing moves. For example, one of the earliest things you can learn is a "bullet disc". Launch a disc and then quickly do the straight punch all in one motion and it will launch the disc at supersonic speed, which is great for surprising opponents. There are tons of tricks like this.

One potential downside is that most moves get locked behind a progression system where you have to complete matchmaking games to earn them. Thankfully these moves get unlocked pretty fast but it can feel bad when you're starting off and you're wanting to make big, "f-you" walls but can only make a pillar. It functions as a good tutorial though.

Anyways, great game. Big recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know how anyone can stand to play this on an Oculus Quest (2). I'm sure with more expensive VR setups with tracking towers it could be more fun, but you can do the exact same pose for a move 20 different times and only a few will actually register. Between the positioning and spacing required between your hands, specific rotation of the wrist, and every little movement being necessary, the Quest just isn't good at keeping up.

Not to mention some other bugs.
- The move learning/training area ghost gestures are usually straight in your face or behind your avatar. Nowhere near where the gestures is actually supposed to be performed.
- I also love the idea for the menus and levers and how interactive they are, but you have to fight half of the time for your avatar to actually grab them.

Just wayyy to many gesture and motion issues for a game that revolves 99% around strict motions and gestures, and it causes nothing but frustration from the time you boot the game up to the time you uninstall it only a few minutes later.\t\t\t\t\t
