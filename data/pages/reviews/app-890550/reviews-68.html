Also, some moves are just easier to activate than others, everything can be used in combat. Many people also seem to understand that learning new moves is very fun, and are willing help you gain exp to rank up/get new moves.

Also big tip: stretch beforehand, like, actually properly stretch. Your shoulders/biceps will thank you, also make sure you have a fan on or something.

A final small side note, just to get a small idea about the community. For my first game, I accidentely matched myself with someone who was top ranked. They saw that I was new and was having trouble, so rather than absolutely styling on me, they tried to help show me what I was doing wrong, and how to do stuff better. Then they ended it off with setting a bunch of walls placed all around the arena to explode just because it looked cool.

In a different game, I was matched up with someone who was at my level, but I had to leave part way through to deal with some other stuff, and I had jumped off the side of the cliff as a "you can just finish me off I have to go" sort of move. What instead happened, when I got back to my headset, I saw them just casually sitting down in front of me throwing rocks of the side of the cliff, leaving me completely undamaged. They seemed pretty concerned with me being okay, I gave them two thumbs up, and continued the fight.

Everyone who's playing is struggling with the controls, not just you. It's a fun experience to try and get everything to work, and learning alongside fellow players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I initially bought and played this game, I was in the same boat as every other noob. I first tried to summon a simple pillar, but I just couldnt... I tried about a dozen times until FINALLY the pillar decided I was worthy. I kept practicing until I could get at least 3 in a row, and moved onto the disk. I was back at square one, desperately trying to conjure my new found abilities, except this time, I was not deemed worthy. After what seemed like 30 tries later, the disk actually spawned in and launched. I was so happy with this minor victory that I decided to just keep on practicing. After about 30 minutes, that initial joy had left me, and I was now being filled with frustration and anger. I was hopelessly failing my arms around trying to get this damn disk to just spawn in consistently. Boy was I getting mad at this. After about 200 attempts, I chucked my headset down on my bed along with my controllers, completely dead and broken inside. I sat back down at my pc and decided to see if others were having the same problem as I was. And this is where it all started to change. I watched a couple tutorials on youtube, explaining how this game actually works, going in depth with wrist rotation and hand placement. Dumbfounded by this new knowledge, I decided to give this game another shot and hopped back in. This time it only took about 20 tries until I managed to start getting the disk down consistently. I felt some confidence and decided to search for my first opponent. Once the match started, we both were missing casts left and right, trying so hard to just do SOMETHING. I stopped for a second, letting my opponent flail while I stood and observed. The jerky movements this man was doing seemed to be why rocks werent being sent my way. After seeing this, I started to attempt some more casts, this time slower and smoother. To my amazement, I was actually able to send out some rocks at this guy, and did some damage. The rest of our fights were pretty one sided after this and I managed to win, but once the match was over, I was feeling mixed. I knew if I wanted to be better, I needed to train. I saw a lobby joining mechanic at spawn and decided to look for training lobbies. I hopped in this lobby to find two guys casually just working on some stuff in the circle. The lobby host happily greeted me with a wave and kinda just watched me for a little bit. After seeing how bad I actually was at this game, this dude decided to go full sensei mode on me. This man showed me how to lift stuff up, slam it into the ground, power hit my disks, and more. Every time I did something right, he gave me a thumbs up and encouraged me to do more. Every time I did something wrong, my new sensei would shake his head in disbelief, give me a thumbs down, and show me how to do the move again. After about an hour or so of intense training, I felt like I was finally getting somewhere. I could consistently hit disks and pillars with little effort, and I learned how to manipulate the positioning of these rocks. I could send a disk straight into the ground after I casted it, lift it back up in the air, and power hit it all in fluid motion. And it was all thanks to no other than some random guy who decided to put up with my lack of skill. Thank you, Tykem, you deserve a raise.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I come to the good parts I want to first talk about a problem many have pointed out but I want to be more specific.

( - Cons )
The biggest flaw in the game is that you have to unlock the moves, that is true.. BUT the thing that makes it even worse, is the grab mechanic you gain at the second highest or highest rank. THE GRAB
While you learn how to spawn boulders, disks fast, how to box, uppercut or ground stuff to have a high utility of attacks.. throw it away because at one point you can block everything with the grab and throw them directly. Jump and throw, just throw a wall, a block, a boulder no matter. You can even hold the wall or other structures in front of you like a shield!
Add this to the fact that the wall structure is almost indestructable and you get the dash mechanic at the third highest rank makes it hard to counter that.

I would recommend the devs to either change the grab and throw mechanic or delete it since it is way too strong.

( + Pros )
If you leave this out the game is quite fun. Its hard yet rewarding to learn the stances and the attacks, to polish your skill more and more until you can summon and launch anything at will or react fast to your opponents due to the training of getting the forms right. If you do not have someone who only uses grab, even the high rank againt low rank battles can be quite exciting even though its pretty one sided.

The community is very friendy and even stops mid match to show you what you did wrong with the forms you try to spawn for the 10th time in a row. Almost every fight starts with a fist bump and some high ranked players even avoid using the moves that the opponent does not have when fighting low ranked players.

It is a lot of fun to play it and is very rewarding with the skill you get, not the moves.

( Flaws for me )
However the only flaws I saw in the game are: The training bot, some abusing the grab and throw mechanic or other movesthey have as advantage against newbies and the get moves for rank up mechanic which frustrates a lot. Also that walls seem almost indestructable when grounded.. and the uppercut mechanic is useless.


( All in all )
Not perfect but a very decent and fun game. Very very good to put a lot of time into it and very taxing, not perfect but good and fun combat system. Community is very nice and supporting with only a handfull of black sheep in it.

I would recommend it when you are ready to put a lot of efford and time into it and you really enjoy the mechanic.

Otherwise as a casual game its more or less frustrating and takes much practice and not to be recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
