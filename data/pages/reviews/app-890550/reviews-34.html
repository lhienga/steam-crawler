\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile most definitely in an alpha state (clunky menu, shallow in some content areas, unexplored multiplayer potential, needed QOL improvements, sometimes janky), it has a *very* solid foundation for addictive, intense, action packed game-play.
This game will make you want to play past the point where your arms stop working. The rush of adrenaline with a hard won victory, or the burning desire to improve with each crushing defeat, will leave you wanting for more until your arms check out and leave you doubled over.
The physicality of objects surprises me constantly, the way they interact in convincing ways and have a certain feeling of mass. The focus this game places on correct form and posture is amazing, and really sells the concept that you are conducting an elemental martial art. Landing moves can be frustrating at first, but if your VR equipment is dialed in correctly, there is always a form based explanation for a botched move. I felt this frustration at first, only to discover that the rotation of my wrists, and position of my head were to blame.
The in game tips are helpful, but the focus on form isn't made especially obvious at first, its not handed to you as the player, so I would recommend walking around the training area to read a bit.
There's plenty more to say, but I think its best summed up as a strong recommendation to purchase this game even in its alpha state. The developers seem to be coming out with small regular updates, but even if they weren't and the game stopped development in its current state, it would still be a blast to have in any VR library. With that said, I do think this game has potential to be my all time favorite VR title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst impressions: There needs to be a better way to "train" players into making the correct hand gestures. Even if they had a replay that would show you what moves you're doing instead of what moves you're supposed to do (like having the wrist rotating a different way) that would be a huge improvement. Or even a model showing you what your body should be doing and a mirror next to it, and you repeat the actions before you even get to "earth bending". Right now, it kinda feels like being pushed into the deep end, and I'm not sure that's the goal.

I get it's a time/training investment, and it seems like a good way to get physically active. I might start playing this more regularly at a later point. But right now I'm at a place to do so. I really do hope the continue to hone this game.

It's a great idea. And that's the only reason I'm recommending this game ***IF*** you're willing to invest that kind of time in developing the muscle memory on your own. Otherwise you're just going to get frustrated like me and other reviews I've seen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has quite a steep learning curve, if you want a jump-in game that makes you feel cool within a few minutes: This isn't for you. It is a really really cool game that actually requires you to develop skills and investment time in order to grow. That being said: All the mechanics are incredibly cool! They truly make you feel like a mighty rock-magician. The people are nice and want you to do well. I've gone in many matches and came out with a ton of learnings and new things. This is one of the only games that I boot, just to continue practicing rather than to win.

The have some wishes/feedback though:
- The low player-count which makes it difficult to play on weird times unless you accept higher latency.
- Because a player hosts the game, one has a significant advantage over the other.
- While the character work (animation/interaction) is amazing, I hope we get customizable characters some day.\t\t\t\t\t
