Experienced on a Valve Index

All I'll say is the videos of the game on the store are appealing, but you'll take a while to get there, if you can at all. I've had a pretty frustrating experience in my first hour, but with enough patience you can actually master the moves, you just need to be more aware of your hand rotations and movements.

Pros:
- Very innovative concept for VR.
- Very fun to play.
- Rewarding gameplay, as it takes actual practice to be any good, and the skill cap is huge.
- Starting environment/map with everything as interactable items instead of menus.
- Good level of polish, whether it's the art or gameplay concepts and execution.
- Good performance levels, especially as an early-access title.

Cons:
- Steep learning curve, frustrating to get into. You're likely to spend your first couple hours bashing your head against a wall, until you figure out your hand rotations and placement matters a lot, the game is very strict with the movesets.
- Sometimes very laggy matches depending on servers/players, and the lag shows as stuttery movements and delayed attacks. The netcode could use a rewrite.
- The joystick movement and sprinting doesn't feel very good.
- Very weird leg IK. More often than not your viewpoint ends up next to the head, with the legs on the side instead of under you.
- "Smooth" turning with the right joystick isn't very "smooth" if you're sensitive to that.
- No finger tracking on Valve Index, which is a bit of shame. Feels odd having to press-in the trigger to pull-in the index finger on this device.
- Moves are locked behind rank, I understand this is meant to gradually introduce you to moves and give you time to master what you have, but it also gives other players that have a higher rank than you an unfair advantage in matches, as they could have more moves than you.
- Expect to be sore after long sessions lol.

Overall feels like a game with great potential and I'm excited to see where it goes, however you'll be filled with frustration and probably have a bad first impression.

I can also see that the game as little depth so far, the core gameplay is there, a decent moveset, matchmaking, but so much more could be done to make the game more interesting, like different arenas, more mini-games, more moves.

A solid start for an early-access game for sure!
Buy it, try it, if you don't like it, you can always refund within 2 hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love love love the concept, most fun I've had in VR in ages. Avatar was my childhood favourite show so it's amazing to finally live it out in a video game.

Something that won't be spoken about enough by reviewers is the community. The skill ceiling is massive in this game, and every other player I meet in online games is kind enough to teach me how to play the game during online matches. Doing so means that everyone gets better and there are more competitive players. I hope to be able to teach others in the future when I get better.

You can tell it's really early days for the game, there should be more user research done to involve the community who are passionate about this game, and more options in settings, such as the ability to disable flipping on the movement stick. These aren't deal breakers but are definitely making the experience difficult so far, and would help the smaller player base feel more involved in the game and help promote it to their friends.

Another issue is matchmaking which is really hard to solve with a smaller player base. Maybe match experience could scale depending on the opponent you are against, and you should get more for going against higher-skilled opponents. This would mean that my time isn't wasted going against someone who has mastered all moves, and I can progress by gaining skills to challenge those players faster.

Thanks for making my avatar dreams come through, really hope for the success of this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen waiting for this game for a while ever since they let out the barebone beta a few years back. Gotta give it a strong 9/10.

Pros: The game is incredibly fun, like hours will pass by in a few minutes kind of fun. The gestures make sense compared to what they do. The tutorial for the gestures are helpful, it's got an "easy to learn, hard to master" type feel to it. On my first time playing, it did take me about 1.5-2 hours in the tutorial area just trying to figure out the gestures correctly. For a while I thought the game couldn't "sense" what I was doing but I just had to put it in the right spot is all (making sure the hands are vertical, etc.) Pvp is great and is thrilling, can definitely reach fast paced combat. The community is also really nice, everyone in the discord is chill and funny, people will definitely help you out if you're confused or having issues.

Cons: Something about the matchmaking is hosting. It's not a big big deal but one of the two players will always host the game, and that player will have little to no lag while the other player is having slightly delayed connection issues. Again, not a big deal, but if you're a new player just trying to rank up and get moves, it's hard to practice if you have to deal with your opponent on top of trying to create objects with bad connection.

FIXED:
- Locking new moves behind ranked match making. This is a big one. For new players, you'll be given 3 basic moves: Straight, Disk and Pillar. You only get new moves after you rank up. The problem with this is that new players can be paired with black belts who already have the moves and can win easily in a minute. The 3 starter moves are nothing compared to black belts, you really have to grind fights to rank up because of this.
- Lastly, the Parry. Parry is very difficult to do and requires near perfect timing even in training. This is unachievable in multiplayer even with low ping because even a fraction of a second matters when parrying. The move is obsolete and I never see anyone do it successfully, I've just resorted to stomp because it at least has more leeway to doing it.

Anyways, all in all a wonderful game, just takes time and patience.\t\t\t\t\t
