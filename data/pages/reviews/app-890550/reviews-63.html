\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is by far my FAVORITE VR game, I find myself practicing this game like a modern form of martial arts, because thats kind of what it is. You genuinely have to learn almost a new form of karate similar to that of popular Avatar: The Last Airbender, wherein you have to master specific turns and positioning of your arms and hands to get the stance down for the move you're trying to do. Don't even get me started on later game where you throw all those singular moves into fast-paced sick combos. It is not easy, and I do not want to make it seem like it is. It will require lots of practice, just like you would with normal martial arts. You will not get every move the first, second, or even the fifth time. Aka; when you need that move the most. For those of you who cannot spend much time in VR due to motion sickness, headaches and the like I do not recommend starting here, but do please come back and enjoy this game. Only downside is for those who also get easily frustrated, please give the game time. Pace yourself. It will be frustrating not hitting that move when you need it. It will be annoying being beat in a singular move. It is not an easy game. Take your time, be patient... or as King Bumi would say:
"Instead of seeing what they want you to see, you gotta open your brains to the possibilities."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTake it from someone who occasionally plays the game on weekends. This means that I don't have the moves perfected and I cannot spam some moves frequently and fast. The game is great if you don't see those factors as possible problems. If you don't, you might find the review helpful.

Rumble actually makes you feel like an earthbender, which is currently unprecedented when it comes to VR. It has a great move list which can be triggered by hand gestures which will have to be memorized in order to fight other players properly. You can mix those moves in order to create interesting combos. The feeling of successfully chaining them together is amazing. However, besides online matchmaking (which take it from someone that played the game from day one, it becomes increasingly hard to find players that have not mastered the art of spamming), you only have a dummy robot AI that can fight you (which is great in the beginning but gets increasingly boring as you get better at the game).

Besides better matchmaking, I think the game needs more AI robots to train with. Probably something that can replicate what you get from PvP better (player vs AI, other robots that train other skills, etc.). It might be an option to include cooldowns for certain moves in order to decrease spamming and increase strategy and tactics (I find these more fun and fulfilling).

At this stage, I cannot recommend the game strictly because at the end of the day it is a PvP game and I don't have the time to invest into it daily in order to keep up with the competition. If it will get better from this point of view, I will gladly recommend the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA genuinely fantastic and unique VR experience with hours of excellent competitive game-play. However, it can be daunting for individuals with little patience / poor attention span who expect to be nailing moves in the game immediately.

The Positives:

Even though it is in Early Access, the devs have made the excellent choice of focusing on simple, low poly assets that ensure the game runs at high and STABLE FPS. If this was not the case, a game of this style would be extremely frustrating due to the often high-pace nature of competitive matches and complex combo moves. You also do not need alot of room to play this at all! You need enough space to be able to freely punch in your field of view - aside from that I do not move from a spot more than 20cm each way and I have no problem keeping up in fights.

After the increased exposure from Youtube videos by larger creators, matches are easy to find (I've tried the European servers and matches have so far been found instantly) and everyone is very friendly at the beginning of rounds. Higher rank players often go easy on lower ranks as they know they have less moves to perform. I'm glad that the majority of the community would rather have a less intense match and teach newbies instead of relentlessly pounding them into uninstalling. That will be the major driving force in expanding the player base and I hope this continues!

Once you get the moves down, they are incredibly satisfying to perform and you feel almost magical. Being able to spawn / pickup / throw etc..etc.. objects at will with your arms has a unique, yet irresistible niche and when playing against players of similar skill/rank you feel like you're the protagonist of an anime series in a major boss fight.

Things to Improve on:

The tutorials for moves can be pretty confusing as the ghost isn't very clear on what to do at times. The yellow circle caused more confusion which was amplified even further when you're practising moves that spawn balls out of the ground a fair bit too quickly. There needs to be more emphasis on the importance of starting poses and the orientation of your hands as it took me a fair bit of time to work it out on my own.

In my opinion - I think the ghost and circle should be scrapped and replaced with a full body replica of your avatar performing the moves perfectly. Adjustable speed would be a major bonus too so you can really figure out where your hands should be. I am suggesting this as I was able to fix my form perfectly by copying someone's hand movements in a private lobby for a very short amount of time. A quick "tips" tutorial will definitely, definitely help new players understand what is most important to becoming fully fledged earth-benders in the shortest amount of time possible.

For the meantime - there are plenty of great YouTube videos that explain how to perform each move correctly. Main one to note is Rumble Bee for sure. Videos, alongside the Discord group, will be your main source of information for combo moves and other tips and tricks to improve your performance.

The sliders / levers for options in your main ground could be improved on but that's really a small thing to note. They feel a little unnatural but they're not things you have to use often.

----------------

Overall, this is a very solid title and of course, as it is available on Steam, it is subject to up to 2 hours of game-play within 14 days where you can request a refund without any issue. So there are no risks - Just remember to give it a solid try and if you have any problems you can join the Discord and get a response very promptly!

I however, will 100 percent be keeping this title as although it's in Early Access, it already feels polished enough to play on a day-by-day basis.

Feel free to ask questions in the comments!\t\t\t\t\t
