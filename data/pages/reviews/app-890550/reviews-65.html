\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRumble is awesome. Ever since Habie dropped his video on the game, playercounts have been in the hundreds, and you can find a match at any time of the day in the US.

There are a few bugs, sometimes you have to restart the game if matchmaking is bugging out, and I had to restart the game when I first played it to be able to dismiss the prompt preventing you from playing PVP at the start. Overall, the loading times are so quick that a restart doesn't really matter that much.

Lastly, the one big criticism that people have is that new moves are locked behind leveling up. This is not NEARLY as big of a problem as people say it is. I've owned the game for 3 days, and I'm already max level. I only have 7 hours of playtime as I'm writing this, so that should tell you how little time you need to put into this game to unlock all the moves. The move locking mechanic is mainly there to get you to learn to fight without the high level moves like Hold that completely change the way the game is played, so that you have other playstyles you can switch to in order to confuse your opponent. I actually think the low level fights are more fun in some ways, since you have to get more creative when you have very few long ranged options.

I've always liked dueling based games, and this is the perfect VR dueler. I am EXTREMELY sore, and that's how I know this game is good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game.

But wait, there's more.

I have a hard time exercising because I'm lazy and I don't get any intrinsic "this is fun or feels good" feels from it. This game has made me super excited to work up a sweat. It feels so SATISFYING to really get into the groove of that new move. To really hammer home a good attack or perfectly time a defense. The game's feedback (the sounds, visuals, etc) that come when you do things right tickles some animal part in my brain that keeps me coming back for more. I often come back just to sit in the lobby and practice moves because it feels good to do it.

The graphics are simple, yet effective. It doesn't distract you from what you have going on in front of you. It keeps the GFX requirements low so that you have fewer things to chug you down in an intense match. The relatively simple graphics don't detract from the ambiance of the game, though. It looks simple, which appropriately reflects the simplicity of the game mechanics. I don't really feel like 8k textures would make this game better in the same way that sticking pinwheels on your car would make it look better. It'd be just fluff for this kind of game. Your immersion comes from your own grasp of the mechanics.

TLDR: Simple, yet addictively satisfying game mechanics that are fun and good for exercise at the same time. 11/10 would Earthbend again.\t\t\t\t\t
