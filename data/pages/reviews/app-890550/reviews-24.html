\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one is a really hard one to review.

I love the game play elements thoroughly, the whole game just feels fantastic to play. Getting used to doing the poses for the moves is incredibly redeeming and fun.

The real downside though is that not a lot of people are still playing it. I don't know if there was ever a good amount of people playing it and I really don't think this would be a problem unless 90% of the moves weren't blocked because of so. It forces you down a route. You either hope to find a player or a small group of people to play with or get a friend to buy it. In that case, I can see this game being fun since you can both get the new moves and scale together. There's no difference like you find in public lobbies where some people have all the moves and some only have the basic 3 or so.

So in the case you have another friend with VR and are looking for a fun experience, I'd recommend. For anyone who's more like me and is just trying to find a fun VR game, I'd probably look somewhere else. Only so for the reason that there's no single player way to progress. Heck, I'd even play it if you could level up with the practice bot but the ONLY way is through the dead multiplayer. Even then, I'd still recommend it to a particular audience. This game is really fun and it's just a shame this is how it's got to be. Either way, I'm excited for the future games that this developer releases.\t\t\t\t\t
