\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I've only played for 2 hours, however I want to say I would have given this game a good review before the shiftstone update. I dropped the game as I didnt have a good pc to play, but recently upgraded and decided to try the game again. Like the first time I played it there weren't many players on and as a pebble it is hard starting out with seemingly everybody else being 2-3 tiers above you. I could get around that however as even losing will eventually rank you up to gain the new moves. The issue comes in the form of the shiftstones or rather the implementation of the shiftstones. I still being a pebble after waiting for 20 or so minutes get put into a game. Was going well until I noticed my opponent was no longer losing health when I hit them. Several of the shift stones are just break the game for lower tiers of combat. The reason for this is because several of the shiftstones either block 1 damage or heal you over time. This doesn't seem like an issue until you realize the main attack of a pebble (not sure about cobble) is throwing a disc of stone that only deals 1 damage. You see the problem? Having these shiftstones on the lower tiers of combat is basically giving someone god mode when they reach 5 health. The pillar can damage the enemy as it deals more than 1 damage however to deal damage with the pillar you first have to use the ability to bring it up from the floor and then be in range to push it into the opponent. On the maps with objects to hide behind opponents will just "turtle" as I've seen it called on the feedback forums. The turtle strategy is apparently even worse in high tier as some players will put loads of walls between them and the enemy and just wait to heal over time. I'll say this again if shiftstones had been implemented differently or left out of low tier I wouldnt have a problem with the game. It's enjoyable but the shiftstones make it unenjoyable as unless you want to cheese the shiftstones as well you will have a hard time ranking up to unlock moves that will actually begin to damage enemies who use shiftstones that block 1 point of damage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was upset initially about the low player count and refunded it back in July. But I went ahead and gave it another shot since the player count is up a little bit. There still aren't a ton of people online but I actually managed to get into a battle finally and it was quality gameplay.

Hardest part about the game is keeping your form correct in the heat of a match. It's easy to do it over and over when you're alone on the training floor. But you quickly start messing up when stones start flying.

Also look up a YouTuber named Rumble Kai. He made a video that shows how to do every move in the game. It shows not only the in-game view but also a real life view of himself doing the move so you can see what you need to fix. Without this dude I would probably still be swinging my hands wildly while screaming at the game to do something.

Here's to hoping the game can get some more attention. It could be a great competitive game with enough players. One thing I truly love is there is no "stamina" bar. In a sense you are the stamina bar. As long as you can keep your form correct, you can throw stones as fast as your hands can move to position.\t\t\t\t\t
