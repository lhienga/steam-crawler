Initially I found the mechanics to be very janky but with a bit of practice it's manageable, I mean it's a VR game that relies on precise tracking and in a fight this can be very frustrating at times.

Okay i have learned my 3 starter moves, now it's time to learn more right? This is where my main issue lies with the game, to get more moves you need to play online against other players and level up. So what a few online matches is the only thing standing in my way in becoming the ultimate earth bender, easy right? yeah no.

This is VR looking at the EU servers. 7 people were online, still the queue was incredibly short and I got a game instantly. Play 2 games against this guy (very friendly I must say) see him struggle with his moves and combos. I do feel a bit bad only spamming discs because that's the only move I can do reliable damage with.

When all is said and done I see I haven't leveled once, no new moves learned. This is so unsatisfying, I want a more scaling level system. Meaning that when I am low level I get new moves fast, because I only have 3. It’s fine if i have to play up to or even more than 10 matches to get the later moves, but at the start it can't be 3 to get 1 more move. It gets stale really quickly only using the same moves over and over.

Now I don't know if this game has a scaling level system, I am only level 1 after all but then the initial levels need to be lowered. It's fine if they then raise the matches required to level at higher levels to get the same amount of grind in the game and give players a goal to keep them playing.

Woude i recommend it? yeah, if more people play it might be more fun to grind levels. But I think they need to add a better level system and a way to grind levels offline. Right now there is no point in playing when people aren’t online and when only 7 people are playing in Eu at 16:00. It might be hard to fit that in people's schedules.

(The moves work great its just the way to unlock them thats the issue, and some VR headsets with bad tracking might strugle).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot of potential and as you play, you will become fit or possibly patient.

I came so close to returning this game as it crashed my Steam twice on the first evening and each time it took me 30 or 40 mins to get VR to work properly again. I have a Vive Pro (Wireless) with Index Controllers. Each time my controllers just stopped working. On restarting the game, it would go into the intro page where it says to stand in a T and hold button B and it looked fine except it did not mention to push button B. My hand controller would work just fine in steam while being unresponsive in the game. Killing everything potentially related and restarting things worked each time after a couple or three tries. Today is Day 2 and I had none of the hand controller issues :-)

A few bullet points on the game:
  • Playing this game is going to warm you up and burn quite a few calories
  • The walk speed is insanely slow!
    • Maybe it helps in the fight
    • It sucks when walking around and exploring.
    • It is even slower walking up ramps and things.
  • I generally click all the buttons and often cancel.
    • I clicked opt out of providing debug data, but could not see how to cancel out, so had to opt out.
  • Would be nice to be able to talk to opponent.
  • Would be nice to have hint cards you can display without having to revisit training.
  • After a fight, so stats on move frequency might be nice?
  • After completing my first evening playing, I had blood on my left hand!
    • I'm not aware that I hit anything.
    • Perhaps these Dev's have taken immersion to a whole new level?

Looking forward to see how this game progresses.\t\t\t\t\t
