\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally this is my favorite VR game. Tragic that the playerbase is so small.

In terms of gameplay, this game is incredible. The poses in this game remind me of fighting game special moves in that at first they are difficult and seem inconsistent, but after just a couple hours of practice they become very consistent and even fun to perform.

The combat is not very complex as there is only one character with 15 or so moves. However there are enough ways to manipulate the 6 different structures you can create (and yourself) to make combat dynamic and interesting, although with only 20 or so hours I can't really say how deep it is.

Unfortunately there aren't very many people who play the game, which is made worse by the fact that this doesn't run on quest, and I doubt it will anytime soon. That means that you basically can't play the game if you don't have friends that can also play.

However, if you like fighting games, this is by far the best iteration of a fighting game I've seen in VR, and I would still recommend checking it out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played much, but I like this game a lot.
The design is incredible. The UI is always interactible: you can push, pull rotate, slide, and punch to interact with the UI. The game works within the limitations of low-spec VR, and has a very nice art-style. The combat and actions feel responsive and satisfying. I love that this game plays with the idea of gesture-based action, and just goes full ham with it. The sound is also really nice.
The only things I can really say are bad, are the lack of players, and that it's really hard to get into the game. These two things may be intertwined as well: the game has a very good hook (earthbending), but it takes a ton of practice to even get the simplest of combos right, and to be able to perform moves without guide is also hard. In this sense, it really does mimic martial arts but it's not very good for getting new active players.

If you're able to push through the initial learning curve, I think there's a really fun game here!
I personally don't play it much, just because I have social anxiety due to the limited amount of players, unless you find a match through discord, you might not be matched against someone your level. If frantically moving your arms and failing to execute simple moves, while a more skilled player looks at you, isn't your cup of tea then I would stay off this game for now, and check back if there's ever more players.

Edit: nowadays i can pretty easily find people on my skill level to play with through the matchmaker.\t\t\t\t\t
