\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRUMBLE is a VR Experience where you can earthbend. Sound fun? Well that's what I thought when I initially saw it. As a fan of Avatar: The Last Airbender, this was like a dream come true!

Booting the game up, it looks great visually! It runs fairly smooth, and I don't have a lot of framerate problems. No, my problem lies with the gameplay, which is absolutely HORRENDOUS!

First, in order to run, you have to move your arms in a running motion. On paper, this is fine. But in practice, it's tiring, doesn't work half the time, and is just a horrible idea when you're; oh I dunno, in combat! Going from swinging your arms violently to trying to attack is harder than it sounds! It would've been a lot easier to set sprint to something a lot easier, like pressing down on the joystick.

Speaking of combat, let's talk about this! The principle behind the combat is a good idea, it makes sense, it's immersive, I love the idea behind using different poses to shoot rocks, discs, make pillars, stuff like that.

And that would be a good idea if it were done correctly. Whenever you practice them at your own pace? Yeah, it works perfectly. But when you're trying to fight someone? It barely ever works. I couldn't even defeat that dumb training bot on level 1 because of how finicky and unresponsive the combat was! Which is a shame, because this game has so much potential! Again, I love love LOVE the idea of earthbending in VR, but the execution has just made it a frustrating experience for me overall, so I simply can't recommend this... I will be keeping an eye on development to see if things improve, but as is? Unless you commit to mastering the finicky, unresponsive earthbending controls, you will NOT be in for a fun time...

4/10 from me, chief.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've ever dreamed of earthbending. BUY THIS GAME. You wont regret it. There is a learning curve and the game is still evolving but the community is amazingly positive and the game is a good state (meaning few bugs, training bot, basic social features and multiplayer matchmaking, basic accessability features likes smooth vs snap turns etc.). The one thing as of Early december I've noticed is the community is still growing so depending on your region it may take longer for matchmaking. If your in canada highly recommend using the US server closest to you for the time being. But YOU can help change that for the low low low price of like 2.64 starbucks beverages. Also a perfect game if you tend to dislike hearing kids on VC...not that I'm hating on them or anything but it's just odd to be speaking to children like that. Not an issue in this game and like I said, community is super positive so most won't obliterate you to pieces the 2nd round if they did the first.

I hope as the community grows more competitive features come out of it. Would definitely love a tournament/event style mode or ranked modes focused on specific skills (i.e. target aim, #casts per time, races, etc.) sort of like scotish style strongmen tournaments or lumberjack tournaments. Especially as people master the skillset!

NOTE: Requires some amount of space, is also a fairly physical game...you will most likely be sweating after the first round of a game even if you are decently fit. Its very different to cast/bend with rocks coming at your face. Stay calm, BE THE ROCK. The learning curve to your first victory is very rewarding!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame has no servers, still runs on peer-to-peer connection, so there's that entire security gap. All players migrate to US East since there's low game population, so actual east players are stuck versus 160+ ping hosts most matches. Walls are spammable at higher ranks and they are the norm (balance issues). Disk is introduced early, so it promotes a 'spammy' playstyle versus offering boulder at the early stages. Their discord is full of weird sexual innuendo about cartoon characters(not even kidding), I couldn't comfortably invite anyone into it, and had to leave. Can't recommend this game to anyone, as I don't feel like my money was well spent. Seems like this game was trying to be an 'esport' but marketed for children. I'll try it again once they add servers if it isn't overshadowed in a few months.\t\t\t\t\t
