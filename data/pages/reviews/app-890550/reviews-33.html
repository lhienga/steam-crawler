\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is truely INCREDIBLE. There's no other words. This is EXACTLY what we all expect from VR. PvP bear hands fights with supernatural immersive powers. And this is crazy fun !!!! Even the HUD is made for VR, all menus are physical touch based, no pointing, you want to toggle something? Press the button. You want to launch a game ? Take the handle and pull it down. Everything is so well thought, all for a 100% immersion.

As soon as I saw that I could earthbend in VR, I jumped into it.. But yes, this game is an easy to learn / hard to master type of game, although that's exactly how we expect bending elements is supposed to be: hard to master.
The moves are not random. If it doesn't work, then it's coming from you, your placement, the angle of your wrist, the position of your arm, or elbow, every detail matters. As would Earth bending.

This game is amazing and content keeps dropping as well as constant fixes for the very few minor bugs there are. A new arena just dropped on the last update, and they're working on a game changing feature regarding personnalized abilities !!!
This game is still developping and so of course, it's not perfect, but is already incredible, I can't imagine the potential behind it.

If you're still hesitating, go watch some videos on Youtube, that's how I discovered it, and every one is just enjoying this.

Thanks to BucketHead Ent for this amazing game, and for your concern about our feedback !\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off I'd like it start by saying I have over 10 years of mixed martial arts training. A lot of them including Chinese martial arts. Though I am not a master in anything as that takes years. Martial arts are all about imitating the masters and applying the moves you learn in the best scenarios. Why do I bring this up? This game at its current state requires you to imitate the moves perfectly even with all my training I was unable to mimic the moves every single time.

As of writing this review I have 41 minutes of playtime, out of that time I tried to create hundreds of pillars and throw close to the same number of discs. I maybe succeeded in this 30 times each. No matter how many times I adjusted it was still difficult to get down the only two moves I unlocked in that amount of time.

Now lets talk about the tutorial area, it is riddled with many question marks to highlight but it only lets you read about the different moves and rules of the game. There's many switches and levers around that are confusing on what they all do. The tutorial area is not well explained.

Onto to my final complaint, trying to join a party or park as they call it I found one person that was on in the US at around midnight eastern standard time. I joined and the guy immediately left so I was forced to play by myself. It does not seem like many people play the game late at night which is crappy for adults who have to work all day.

The reason I am not recommending this game is it's going to take hard work and dedication like any martial art would. Do not expect to play this game and become Toph from The Last Airbender. You are going to be training like the kids in the training school in the earth village, doing nothing impressive and just trying to learn the basics. If you do not have a large amount of time to dedicate to this game you are never going to win any matches if you can find one. I stand by this is a good game, it's just going to take a lot of practice. It's like trying to become a real earthbender.

TLDR: This game will take weeks to months of dedication to learn the correct moves. You will not start out with a power fantasy but as child trying to learn difficult techniques.\t\t\t\t\t
