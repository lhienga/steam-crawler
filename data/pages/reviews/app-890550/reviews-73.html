\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRUMBLE is earth bending in VR, you know it, they know it, we all know it. And it is GLORIOUS.
Honestly I'm more of an air bender person myself, but this game looks to change my mind.

The graphics are really gorgeous. And strike this great balance of simplistic and stylized that works really well in VR. Looks great and runs smooth. The explosions especially are wonderful (and play a significant role in game).

The controls are extremely simple. You have smooth movement and rotation (snap turn is there but puts you at a disadvantage)....and that's it. As far as buttons anyway. Everything else is done through gestures. And, before you run away, they really really work.

The same thing happens every time I see someone try this. They see you do the motion. Say "okay so it is like this?", you go "yup", they try it in game and nothing happens. Every time. But then they repeat, they modify, try again and again. And eventually they get it down and it works every time they do the motion. There is some magic to this game that really requires you to understand the moves on an internal level. You can't just mimic the motion. It is subtler than that. But it is very responsive. Practice a bit and you will never mess up a move.

Content for the game is perhaps the only sticking point. You have 15 moves, more to come I imagine. A dojo-ish home/practice area, a "park" for friend-multiplayer that I have never used, and an arena for multiplayer 1v1. And that is it. You are here for duels with players. Period.
Hopefully more will be added but what is there does the job very well. The game play carries everything easily.

Personally, I have not had trouble getting a match. Longest I've had to wait is maybe 10 mins and usually it is more like 2. And conveniently you can practice in your dojo until the match-maker find someone.

Highly recommended, can't wait to see what more gets added.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRUMBLE has by far the best combat system I have ever seen in any VR game, the moves are quite easy to learn but to actually master them takes time and effort. Every single move has it’s own purpose and is equally useful. If you’re a fan of the Avatar series and always wanted to be an earthbender this is the exact game you would be looking for.

EDIT: I can no longer recommend this game. I will leave my original review up as it still somewhat holds true. This game has an incredibly low player count and you can only gain new abilities by doing PVP. I would normally be fine with this, but the lower player count leaves exclusively dedicated players in the servers. Because of the way the combat system is structured, not having all the abilities while fighting someone with everything unlocked can feel demoralizing and aggravating. Again, I wouldn't have a problem with this. But as most moves that help with survival are only unlocked in later levels, you physically cannot survive against a max level player. It doesn't help that losing matched give pitiful XP, I imagine it would take at least 15 losing matches to go from level 1 to 2. This isn't fun. Devs, if you're reading this, add some other way to gain experience. Add a PVE horde mode or at least give reasonable amounts of experience for losing. As long as it remains nearly impossible to grow from losing matches, this game will stay unfriendly to newcomers and likely will never grow. Also, while it does let you limit to players of the same level, this feature is useless as almost nobody is in the lower levels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't currently recommend this game, despite how much I love playing it. It's a steal for the price, I will freely admit that.

The problem comes in the progression system. At the first belt level you have only 3 moves, which can be quite boring. As you play matches though, you unlock new moves. The problem begins there. Prior to 0.1.4, the new moves were difficult to do properly, which meant that you couldn't generally do them accidentally. 0.1.4 changed every pose and made the tolerances wider, and I'm personally constantly throwing out accidental moves just going from one pose to the next. Such as trying to do boulder, getting it, but also instantly dashing into it and dealing myself 2 damage. Or summoning pillar just trying to sprint.

Whether this is just a case of getting used to it or not, I can't say. I've played more in 0.1.4 than I did in 0.1.3, and I still haven't gotten used to it. But, I shouldn't feel like the game is getting worse and harder to play the higher rank I get. Especially when previously, it played perfectly. People only complained they couldn't do the moves because they didn't practice.

I really want this game to be fantastic, I want it to spawn a whole genre of 1v1 arena fighters with water, fire and air, so I can live out my ATLA fantasies. Earthbending is a delightfully fun thing to do in VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game rocks.
I started out hardly being able to do the disc at all, and then after practicing and fooling around in a park lobby with friends I can hit it over 9/10 times. It's extremely satisfying to gradually learn and become proficient with the moves.

Then there are also move combos. With some creativity (or advice) you can use a few moves together to do something new. I'm only cobble rank so don't have many moves, but you can:
Pillar, pillar again to make the first one rise and straight it as it's rising to get a very high arc that can hit an opponent behind cover.
Spawn boulder, then straight and kick as it's falling down to get a nice medium height long range projectile.

And some more difficult stuff:
Launch a disc and immediately ground it with stomp
Launch a disc and straight it to make it go very fast. (This is hard and I've only done it once while trying, though I know from a friend it can be relatively consistently done)
Launch a disc, straight, then kick it. Very tough timing and not useful but hilarious to see a disc go so far and high.

I've always thought it would be amazing to have a game with magic controlled by gestures and I'm quite satisfied with this.\t\t\t\t\t
