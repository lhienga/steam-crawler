\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played from Madden '98 to Madden 2004 and loved the franchise. Grew up with it. Since this is my first Madden since 2004, you can imagine that I was happy with how far it had come. I know a lot of people hate this game because it is no different than the last 5 years of Madden, but I didn't experience those last 5 years. So I'm the best case scenario customer for this game. Very easy and happy to please.

With that said... this game is 50/50 for me. I'm addicted to the gameplay and love the feeling I get when I pull off a great play. But I DESPISE how the majority of online players win in this community. There are small exploits that I feel experienced players target for easy wins. Wanna hear me rant for a minute? Here we go (if you want the short TL;DR version, scroll to the bottom):

Are you picking cover plays and predicting a pass in your audibles? Too bad. They're going to throw a deep pass and its going to get caught even though the receiver has 3 of your players covering. Go ahead and try to intercept. It's your best chance. It's pretty much a 50/50 dice roll. If it doesn't work, you're screwed. I've literally seen players catch a ball without ever turning back to look at it. I've also watched my own players run AWAY from someone they're supposed to be covering, which lead to that player getting the ball and running it to the endzone. Do you have the other player at 4th down on their own half of the field? Too bad. They're not punting once. They're going to, instead, pick a passing play and throw it deep. It succeeds more than half the time, too. Want a tactical experience where every yard has to be earned? That's rarely going to happen. They're going to pick a running play and you're going to sit and watch helplessly while the entirety of your defense freezes up and BACKS AWAY from the runner half the time. Want to take control of them and tackle? Great idea! Too bad there's still going to be times where you tackle someone with three separate guys at full speed and the runner still manages to truck 5-10 yards with ease.

That's if you don't experience THIS kind of gameplay first when the opponent has the ball:

RUNNING PLAY. 5 yard gain. NO HUDDLE. RUNNING PLAY. 8 yards. NO HUDDLE. RUNNING PLAY. 4 yards. NO HUDDLE. PASSING PLAY. DEEP PASS. Touchdown.

The worst part is... I had the above happen to me in a game just last night and I predicted every single play and picked the best counter for each one. I even knew when they were going to switch it up to a passing play. Predicted it. Picked a coverage play. Had three guys covering the receiver who was running deep. He bullet passed it to that receiver and the ball went THROUGH my three covering players right into the receiver's hands. 50 yards gained for the other player.

TL;DR:

It seems to me no matter how accurate your play predictions are and how calculated the plays are that you selected... your success is determined by the AI and the AI is extremely unpredictable. No matter how well you plan and how accurately you predict the opponent's play, they'll still leave massive openings half the time and you'll pay the price.

Like I said, I'm practically new to this franchise, having not played since Madden 2004. For some reason, I feel like Madden 2004 was a better game. When I search online, it turns out people agree with me... even down to the mechanics.

Madden 23 is currently on sale for $17.99 and I've seen it at that price before. I'd say that's a worthwhile price... That's what I bought it for. But $59.99? Not worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
