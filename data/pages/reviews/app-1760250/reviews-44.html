\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat if I were to tell you that almost every single criticism I had for NBA 2K23 ALSO applies to Madden 23? Would that surprise you?

It sure doesn't surprise me.

Despite this being an EA title not a 2K title, Madden 23 manages to fail to function on just about every level that it matters.

First and foremost, there are quite literally ADS if you change menus. I'm not kidding. There's popup ads in a fully priced game that you purchased advertising for you to buy packs or different microtransaction bullsh*t. If sports weren't such a universal thing, meaning if the franchise had no real world tie in? All of the current sports franchises besides *maybe* FIFA would have lost their entire playerbase years ago.

Even for the people that don't care about online or franchise mode or any of the typical things you might buy Madden for and just want a singleplayer experience should be warned.

Face of the Franchise currently has a bug that is impossible to avoid and inevitably happens to your savefile where it will at random completely reset your career progress and default to making you a Quarterback. There is no way to salvage the save file, and while you can restart your season and it will keep your upgrades, you have to start on a new team all over again from the preseason. Just made the Superbowl when the glitch hits? Oh well.

I got this game on sale for 18 bucks because I enjoy watching football and have enjoyed playing both in real life and the game my whole life, since Madden 06. The worst part about this is that the game is kinda fun when it works, you just have to jump through hoops to get there. I did mostly face of the franchise so far as a middle linebacker and playing defense is super fun, but again, you have to fight to play the game.

There's still all the weird unskippable stuff, like cutscenes that I'm positive are written by AI. Seriously, just pay like one f*cking writer at this point because jesus christ these are painful to sit through and unskippable. At one point, your agent(?) named Sosa just... buys you an apartment? Like imagine if that happened in real life. Your agent, without telling you, buys you a luxury apartment you haven't seen before. That's grounds for suing the hell out of them lmao

There's even this stupid line they had the announcers record where if you skip the halftime animations they get offended? Yeah, no sh*t I don't wanna watch your stupid little halftime walk off/walk on animation. The immersion is broken 10000 different ways, so forgive me for not buying into the whole "realistic football simulation" aspect of this game. You kinda lost me when the QB got sacked and clipped half of their character model into the ground.

There's no good place to put this tidbit either, but theres this really weird cutscene thing that happens a bunch with Chad Ochocinco? He says like 2 lines then it goes back to the game. Very weird but glad he got a paycheck I guess.

Its painfully obvious that just like 2K, the ultimate team and other modes that allow microtransactions are the real reason these games are still being made. I used to love Ultimate team when it first came out because it was pretty easy to get good cards without spending money and have a fun team to play with against your friends. Now you need to spend in the 100$ range on microtransactions that only last for that version of Madden to be competitive. I feel very bad for the people who are addicted to these games and the game modes that require spending more money, and genuinely, I hope you find other games that are kinder on your wallet.

As with my review on 2K23, I don't forsee EA changing their tune anytime soon. I waited until a sale, but there is an overwhelming amount of people who work normal jobs and don't really have the time to research games that just want to play some Madden with their buddies that will purchase this and other sports franchise games on Day 1 for full price and therefore EA has nothing but positive feedback for their game.

That glitch that completely borks your save file I mentioned earlier? Thats been in the game since day 1. There are about 1000 forum posts about it, youtube videos, the whole 9 yards and they STILL have yet to fix it. This game came out almost a full year ago at this point.

EA is spitting in the face of everyone that buys this game and supports this franchise, and while I'm sure I'll have some fun here and there playing a few games, it is far from worth supporting this soulless cash grab.

EA; please, I beg of you, throat my entire and balls.

Don't buy this game, I don't know if that'll stop you but I can tell you it sure as hell isn't worth the maybe hour or so of fun you get out of it. Thanks for reading.

Edit: completely forgot to mention that the game does not have updated rosters (Played on the Seahawks, Geno Smith was a 65 overall and Drew Locke was starting), and this is a "Steam" game but still makes you install Origin which is essentially malware and have an EA account, so yeah, thats fun :)
