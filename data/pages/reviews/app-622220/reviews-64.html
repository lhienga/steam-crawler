\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a beautiful sequel to Fate Extella in which it improves upon most of the things which were considered negative in the 1st game. Graphics is absolutely beautiful, the game is well optimized and with a decent rig (mid-high tier) pc I was able to play at 4k resolution at 60 fps with a few drops only (30 at max, 10-15 drop at average) at graphically intensive scenes/ abilities.
The sound and voice acting is quite nice, and if you're a fan of the Fate franchise you will immediately recognize most if not all of the servants in the game with their unique quirks and characteristics.
As for the gameplay, well it has improved upon most aspects that were considered dull or dreary in the first game. This is also a dynasty warriors like game (like the first extella) but it's unique take and twists upon the genre will certainly classify it as one of the good dynasty warrior like or "musou" games. One of the things immediately noticeable in the game is the camera sensitivity though, which can be/must be adjusted to a personal acceptable/comfortable levels.

tldr; Amazing graphics, Good optimization, Nice voice acting and soundtrack, Decent dynasty warrior like/musou (genre) gameplay, and plus points if you are a Fate fan since this is like Fate Grand Order (in terms of servant bonding and interaction) without the predatory gameplay practices (gacha and all the BS mechanics in FGO to milk you dry of your money and or time), and a continuation of the Fate Extra universe lore. Best of all is that this game contains no DENUVO/DRM. DEFINITELY WILL RECOMMEND!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the first play-through of story mode, though I have to say the story is weaker than Umbral Star. However, I quite like the Quality of Life changes to battle and skills! Makes the fights that much more awesome.

Update: So I beat the game 100% and got all the achievements. I can safely say that this game is a must for Fate fans. Awesome music, great voice acting, upgraded combat, nice artwork, no bugs (playable even with a HD620 at 900p) and generally just a great experience. There is one drawback though, and it is quite a big one, the story seriously is lacking when compared to the first installment - the character development is weak to non-existent and the plot is very run of the mill predictable.

Do recommend a controller, though KB+M is pretty doable (my controller broke down halfway through the game and I still beat the last Extra stage with KB+M)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first Fate Extella was... tough to play. It was tedious and barebones, but you suffered through it for the story.

Wait, no you didn't. The story of that first game was only hype at the final battle and the rest of it was... Oh, right, you played it for the non-story parts. Fully 80% of that game's dialogue was blushing, stammering affection between you and that game's three main waifus!

Extella Link inverts this in nearly every way. The story is more focused on the action, but with much less pandering, sadly. With less of a far-too-long buildup but also with less of the fun payoff that can only work with that slow boil.

But enough about that, the devs at Marvelous finally PLAYED some Warriors games now and actually understand what they are ripping off. Everything is better. Gone is the pointless stop and go enemy bashing. Now the levels actually have missions, new enemies show up to threaten your allies, your allies put together ploys to counter attack. The missions finally tell stories like a proper musou now. And on top of that, the combat got a punch up. Equippable cooldown active skills give you a lot of customization for the characters and variety in the action, and when you're actually playing, the enemies juggle a bit nicer, hang out in the air just a bit longer, and the physics all come together to make some whiz bang musou action fun juggling mooks or dueling it out with bosses.

The game is just flatly better on every level (Except for the writing) and I can now recommend it to musou fans as a general purpose recommendation.

FYI, if you're just starting out, you can start here, but you need to at least know what a "servant," a "Noble Phantasm," and a "Holy Grail War" are from some Fate familiarity. Link is not a direct sequel to Extella any more than Extella was a direct sequel to Extra, and though Link spoils some of the final twists of Extella in the first 10 minutes, it does not engage with that game's plot directly. Extella may be a bit of a mess, but I think it is still worth playing just for how exceptional the pandering is, how a game can be so good and insistent at writing a maiden's infatuation with (you) that it can feel charming, pure, pandering, and borderline pornographic all at the same time despite being purely T-rated. But if you are just in it for an anime styled musou or your waifu isn't Nero or Tamamo, you can jump straight to this one.

And I'm not a friggen Astolfo loving degenerate. That trap can burn.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you haven't played the previous game of the series the game may not be impossible to play but you'll definitely feel lost for a while since the story expects you to know what's happening. The glossary does explain/remind many parts for those who need some help though.

The gameplay feels more fun compared to the previous game. Rather than just fighting with attacks/extella maneuver, characters have skills and a moon drive mode which charges up for Noble Phantasms. And yes that means you get to actually use them during the stage instead of always needing to save up for the boss.

It seems I was lucky enough not to have the controller problems some others report. It automatically recognized my DS4 and showed the right buttons and even had vibration.\t\t\t\t\t
