\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fate fan of course this game is worth full marks. No one can dare take away the one and only beach flower, Tamamo from me, RRRRREEEEEEEEE.

But as a gamer, this game gets pretty repetitive. The hack and slash style is very fast-paced and smooth (although some characters are smoother than others), and the story is good enough, just good enough, not great. However, the game is simply a hack-n'-slash fest. The challenge quests simply requires you to obtain a higher level, and the animations are pretty much the same every time. It might be impressive at first, but fighting the same servant over and over gets pretty boring......

If you are a hardcore Fate fan (or you have your beloved waifu/husbando) then this game is for you, their martial prowess in here will make you love them even more. But if you are a newbie to fate, this game isn't for you yet.

The price is on the high spectrum too, I fear that it will discourage people in playing this series.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was hard to say if I wanted to recommend or not. I bought this during the winter sale at $53 CAD as I generally know the Fate series but not much about the games. I gave it a try based on the overall positive reviews.

Notes: it’s my mistake to not realize it was a sequel so I can’t comment about the story.

The gameplay itself is truly a hack and slash. That’s all you do. Even the enemy bosses have such basic AI that you can just treat them like the basic enemy cattle with a larger HP bar. Very few missions for the price of a AAA title. In the 10.3 hours steam is showing I played I completed all 27 story missions as well as cleared a bunch of the extra missions. Cleared both of the level 150 end game missions and just decided to leave it at that. Even in those missions the difficulty was quite low.

Would recommend at $10-20 and if you like pure hack and slash without much need for skill.\t\t\t\t\t
