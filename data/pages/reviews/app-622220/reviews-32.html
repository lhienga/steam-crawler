\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPart visual novel and part 'Dynasty Warriors' wannabe, 'Fate/EXTELLA LINK' is a game for... Who exactly? Fans of the original 'Fate/EXTELLA' may find the story to be quite a letdown in this one. Fans of 'Fate/Grand Order' may not find the characters they are looking for (despite the lack of egregious free-to-play mechanics in 'Fate/EXTELLA LINK'). Fans of 'Dynasty Warriors' may be put off by the super-anime character designs and plot (plus the incessant amount of reading in between combat sequences). And the list goes on.

That aside, combat in this game feels decent, if not satisfying, and the addition of fan favorites such as Scathach somewhat prevents the game from becoming stale in the long run. The visuals are what you'd expect out of a console-based anime game, and the sound design is alright.

Overall, 'Fate/EXTELLA LINK' isn't for everybody, but for those out there who want to bond/mow down massive hordes of enemies with the likes of Robin Hood and Lancelot, then I shall recommend this game to you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved Extella, and I love this. More, in terms of gameplay, even though it took a bit to get used to at first. While 1 was very simplistic, this felt overly complicated - at first -. I really enjoy playing it, and the story (and the way it works) is great.

In terms of emotional impact, I personally felt 1 had more impact on me personally. But this one is definitely more immersive because it's a lot more "believable". (I guess, considering the absolutely unreal setting and circumstances lol) That being said, I'm a HUGE fan of France and French history/legend IRL, and this has Charlamagne, Astolfo, Jeanne, and Gilles in spades. SO. A massive plus there too.

The Extra battles and I assume multi (haven't touched Multi yet, but I'm very picky with what I play multi in) is a lot of fun to just replay, or go through in order to unlock outfits, and just have fun. It's also nice, that in the story and Extra alike, the stage victory requirements are more varied and in some cases FAR more complicated than Extella 1. Variation and a challenge, but not ever rage-quit territory.

Lastly, and most importantly: Astolfo is in this game. And Elizabeth Bathory. But I mean, come on. Astolfo.\t\t\t\t\t
