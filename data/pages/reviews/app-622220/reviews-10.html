\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a Dynasty Warriors-style action game set in one of the weirder spin-offs of the Fate series. The action is fun and the special moves are now a separate system instead of being parts of your main combo string. On one side it's a bit more variety, on another, it sometimes ends up with you almost exclusively spamming skills and barely using the main attack - but at least a character with an attack animation you don't like isn't forlorn because of it.
The roster of characters includes everyone from the previous game and a bunch of fun new characters. The graphics look great, although they do go overboard with effects sometimes, but not too much. And if you don't care about being a 100% completionist, there isn't much grind, even while beating all the extra levels. All the characters can be brought up to the level of your highest-level character for a relatively modest cost.
The plot is a sequel to Fate/Extella the Umbral Star, but you can play it without having played the prior game - you'll just be quite confused about one of the characters, Altera, but she's not the central character in this game. Now playing it without knowing anything about the Fate series or the Extra/Extella digital world shenanigans would probably be a trip, but some people say it's fine. For someone familiar with the series, it's a relativley light Fate story, but enjoyable as a backdrop for all the ridiculous action.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's hard to say which of the two Fate/Extella games I'd recommend over the other. This game is mechanically a better gameplay experience than the first, the combats better, the skill usage is better, you can noble phantasm more than once per battle, theres less kinda time waste-y combat mechanics and everything just feels like it flows better. But then the story and side content in this game is infinitely worse than the original game.

The original had several separate arcs to play through, seeing events from different perspectives and that led into an actually pretty good story that actually went somewhere, whilst this game has a branching path layout but that makes very little actual difference to a very dull and uninteresting plot revolving around a completely uninteresting villain. Even trying to somehow make him related to Altera, one of the best things about the first game, is a complete waste.

As for the Side Stories in the first game, where every single playable Servant had a short little story campaign, that were usually just some frivolous dumb lighhearted fun, thats just entirely gone.
Instead we have a series of extra battles, with the barest of story justifications for the battles, and whilst theres a good number of them, they each have a single difficulty and thats it. Outside of gameplay just everything about this game feels inferior and throw-away compared to The Umbral Star.

This game does have Young Altera though, the only DLC actually worth picking up for the game.

In the end, I think I'd actually recommend anyone coming to this series fresh to play Umbral Star instead of this one first, just because the story content is a much better experience. And then if you enjoy that, or are a Musou game lover, you can then pick this one up, because despite its shortcomings in the story department, the gameplay really is a good step up over its predecessor and worth playing (Probably on sale though)
