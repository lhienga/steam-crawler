\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs much better than the previous entry. If anything, it's better as much for the lessening of some features.

Significantly less waifu baiting in the story, so more characters get the spotlight and you can spend more time fighting. Rush attacks are much cooler than the extella manoeuvres, not as powerful but more engaging and need more tricks to land. rather than being 5+ 'get out of jail free' cards you have on hand for any fight. Having the master on the field and needing to be protected rather than in a ring feels conceptually much more FATE like. A lot of new heroes, and you can use them across more content- including using them to advance the story, as soon as you unlock them. I actually feel like friendly NPC servants do things, even on harder difficulties. Game's more stable, I've had it lock a lot less often. There's more stages in this game, and while there are no focused 'side stories' for individual characters, every character gets a stage or two to shine in. Additionally, the stages themselves are much more unique, whereas in the last game a stark majority of the 'unique' side stories were just rehashes from the main story. You can also now Noble Phantasm more than once a stage, and not only that but there's subtitles for them now too so I can understand their chants.

There are some issues, though mileage may vary on whether they are all that bad. The special action system are quite overwhelming, and in most gameplay will probably overtake basic attacks as go to choices whenever they are off cooldown; that said, I still think they are better than the manoeuvres from before, and figuring out how to link them into normal combos and with each other for long chains is satisfying. There aren't many good ways to get out of a combo besides moon drive, which is kind of good because it makes moon drive more precious and necessitates a more cautious approach, but bad because it makes drive granting items really hard to justify unequipping- but again, I like it better than the extella manoeuvres from the last game, which were so much more spammable and so much less interactive. Side mission objectives -which come in a set sequence- can get frustrating when the last objective is 'claim 3 sectors' or something, because by then there is likely no more sectors to claim, but otherwise having dynamic side objectives is kind of cool.

The weakest link is definitely the story. The bad guy in this game is pretty lame, worse than Altera from the first game. 'Humanity a bad because some humans do bad things, therefore killing all humans is a good thing to do' is not a very engaging character motivation at all. There is a lot less depth to the villains here, and they never really get any redeeming moments. The constant waifu-ing in the first game grated on me, but that aside saving Altera from herself was cool. And the other guy turning evil because he touched alien juice was kind of lame, but his gimmick of time travelling was somewhat interesting, and his tenacity made him more satisfying to quash. Meanwhile, beating Rex Magnus feels really anti climactic. Beating the other servants under him, like Karna, was more satisfying.

Overall though, I think it is much much better than the original. I'd go as far as to suggest skipping the first entry entirely, if you are playing it for reasons other than the waifus.\t\t\t\t\t
