The storyline continues for peaceful after the war has ended and many servants have parted way to their own journey. However, it wasn't over. Things have gone wrong and the Servants have been oraclized to face against Master and Main Servants under control by the Evil King Servant. They must deoraclized to all Servants and defeat the Evil King before the whole NPCs will be corrupted.

Anyway, onto the actual review. The gameplay were almost pretty much same as the first one, but has gotten better because things have been change over the time for upgraded and removal. They upgraded for better graphics and still image with mouth and face animation, much like HDN and FFF game for conversation. They removed Extella Maneuver as freeze enemies multiple combos which I think it's a good idea and too cheap for that kind of group kills. Instead, they added for Active Skills to use the ability, but has a cooldown timer after usage. Even better, they added for Rush Attack up to 2 Servants Link nearby after random chance for Active Skills usage and Faceoff Rush Attack when hitting together between player and opponent servant.

There are 16 Servants returning and 10 more who are joining in and clash the battle. The movement combos will be different in the first game because they have Active Skills to use for little power up. Unleashing Moon Drive will do the last attack move from Extella Maneuver and fills up Noble Phantasm meter depend how many enemies you defeated around you.

As you progress through different storyline path, some of the stages were being recycled. It feels like devs may be running out of concept designs, but I guess that's the only map limitations they can get. Pros:
  • Interesting Continuation Storyline, but somewhat short.
  • Better Graphics
  • Awesome Fighting Style Gameplay Mechanics
  • Your favorite Husbando/Waifu Servants to play as, including your favorite Trap... ( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°)
Cons:
  • Some stages are being recycled in different storyline path
  • May lose focus on dialog box during the battle

8/10
It's a good game. Though, this is where it'll end here and I'm pretty sure that they'll be adding more Servants DLC in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story for this game follows the events of Umbral Star and puts you back into the world of Fate with ease. It's even new player friendly, so you really didn't have to play the previous games to understand what is happening. The story line itself isn't very exciting, and it can even be predictable at some points, I'll admit. However, the amount of personality within each and every character easily outweighs the flaws that come from the story itself.

The introduction of the new character, Charlemagne, really makes the game a lot more fun since you can mess around with what he has to offer. Also, his personality is really different for a servant summoned at the time, and his upbeat attitude will make you as happy as he is. His attacks and abilities are super cool, which is why he quickly became my favorite servant within the Fate series.

If you're planning to buy this game to play with friends within the multiplayer, I wouldn't really count on it. The multiplayer mode is OK and there isn't a lot of connection issues, however it can get very boring very quickly. Not to mention, some of the servants easily overpower other ones in the line up, and if you're going against a team full of actual players while you have only 3 players and a bot, you've basically already lost.

Once you've beaten the story of Fate/EXTELLA LINK, you'll quickly find that there really isn't much to do other than the extra battles mode and multiplayer. The extra battles can be entertaining, since some of the scenarios are actually kind of funny, such as having to stop Nero and Elizabeth before they start their idol concert and destroy everyone's ears. They rise in difficulty as you go down the list, but you're own level will rise very quickly with it.

All in all, I'd say to purchase this game if you're a huge fan of the Fate series or if you enjoy a really fun hack and slash game that keeps you moving around the maps quickly. Don't play it for the multiplayer, but instead play it for the single player modes and massive variety of 29 characters to play as and learn more about in the process.

Gameplay: 8/10
Story: 7.5/10
Characters: 9.5/10
Multiplayer: 5/10
Replay Value: 7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn so many words it's a direct improvement over the predecessor. While in the previous game there was all this esoteric lore nonsense that fell flat on its ass and a lot of the things that the game had to offer felt like they were either rushed or half-assed horribly in some manner, among other things, LINK has pretty much none of those problems going.

You're free to stand by whatever servant you favor instead of getting corralled into any particular route cough cough the first game forced you to side with nero cough cough, and battles are won not by taking over arbitrarily-valued territories but by kicking the enemy's ass, which are both good examples of what in general makes this so much better - the game is aware of what its strong points are and focuses on those more than anything else.

This was the biggest problem with the predecessor. Fate/EXTELLA: The Umbral Star wasn't aware of what it was and why people played it. It wanted to be a visual novel even though it was clearly a Warriors-style hack and slash. It thought it was a piece of complex and deep storytelling even though most people bought it because they just wanted to bone an R63'd King Arthur. In direct contrast, however, Fate/EXTELLA LINK knows that it's a hack and slash at its core, and while there's certainly some VN-type stuff going on it takes a page out of John Romero's book - it only exists to tie the action sequences together and fill in some of the characters, and takes a backseat as soon as things start happening.

While with the OG Fate/EXTELLA I could only recommend it if you were someone that enjoys the Fate series and Musou-type games, and even then not wholeheartedly because of the price tag, with Fate/EXTELLA LINK I can safely say that this is worth your time if you're someone that enjoys the Fate series OR if you're someone who likes hack & slash games, and even then with more confidence than before in defiance of the hefty price tag. All in all, it's a solid game that's refined itself into a similar yet entirely new and entirely more entertaining beast compared to what came before it.



There's also the fact that the importance of random combo attacks seems to have been diminished in favor of strategic management of abilities and skills, which is EXTREMELY welcome considering that now you can't just cheese your way through everything like in the first game (cough cough extella maneuver cough cough) so there's some actual sense of risk and reward to things. Which is absolutely a welcome touch.

9.5/10, good ♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t
