\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautifully Done Game.

Brilliant combat that shall appeal to Musou game fans. Good voiceovers. Graphics is a bit dated but brilliant execution on Noble phantasm sequences. Lots of servants added over the last game, my personal favorites being Darius and Arjuna.

Although great in terms of combat sequences, the game suffers in the lore and narration department. The story plays out like type moons typical visual novels...walls and walls of text that shall often be subject to you passing it over by holding fast forward.

However, as true fans shall know, the beauty of the fate series is, in fact, in the lore. The servants aspirations, their dreams, their thoughts in life, remembrances on meeting comrades or rivals in a second life granted through the Heaven's feel, this is what attracts.

While true fans of the Type Moon's Fate and Musou genre will have some serious ass-kicking to do in this game, mainstream gamers or newcomers to the genre might find it wanting in the gameplay and narration department. Fortunately I belong to the "Fate Fanboy" category and I'd say the Game is a Blast overall :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has 3 routes. The 3rd is only unlocked after you beat the 1st two. Story should last you about 11 hours. All the segments can be replayed.

After that there is an EX Battle mode. Each character in the game has at least 1 free costume, those costumes are unlocked through EX Battles. You unlock EX Battles by progressing through the story.

Multiplayer is fun. Netcode is phenomenal. Played with Asians and had 0 lag.

The game looks visually pleasing and has a lot of different combat options. Every character has something called "Active Skills", and they all have 7 of them and all have 4 slots. What this means is that essentially 2 characters should never be alike.

The game has 24 or so characters (none of which are clones) and is pretty fun. If youre a Fate fan then youll like this game. If youre a Type-Moon fan youll like this game. If youre a Musou fan youll like this game.

Give it a shot. I mean if you dont like it you could always refund it.

You know, I like Astolfo, (s)he is one of my favorite characters, but STOP CONFUSING ME!

Disclaimer
Review after finishing all the games stages on Hardest and playing all characters multiple times to get a good feel for each one. I will be completing the game in the future.

TL;DR
Mechanically its one of the best Musou-style games out there with a ton of variety. If you only care about gameplay itself then this is a must-buy. The only problem is that you can totally exploit some mechanics to make the game quite easy if you dont hold back or deliberately challenge yourself by attempting maps with higher level requirements or not maximizing your effectiveness.

However the story is lackluster, especially compared to the first game (Fate/Extella Umbral Star)

For people who dislike musou-games while this game sports some of the best musou-game mechanics its still a musou-game through and through and will not convince you to like the genre.

Description
Fate/Extella Link introduces several changes to the formula of the first game (I assume you are familiar with F/E Umbral Star, or at least familiar with other musou games):
- no more combo unlocking each character starts with the basic combos and the amounts of attacks in a combo is different for different characters, i.e. Tamamo only has attacks up to c4, but Scathach has combos all the way up to c6
- each character has access to several special cooldown-based skills/spells they can use and they acquire more spells as you increase your bond with them these skills can be used standalone or as a finisher during any part of a combo, some c5 and c6 attacks from the first game are now skills (like Tamamos jump kick)
- rush attack has been redone, now instead of activating it with a button press you need to make it happen by performing a series of successful attacks finished with a skill, there is no special resource required for it and if you have allies nearby they will join you this mechanic potentially deals a lot of damage
- Moon Drive (power-up) mode can be finished early with another press of a button and this way you will activate a special musou-style attack with a unique animation
- Noble Phantasm (ultimate attack) can now be charged both by special items found in the level and by killing enemies while in Moon Drive mode. This means you can totally use your super area-clear move multiple times per mission!
- Moon Crux mode once exclusive to Tamamo and Nero has been removed no more mecha-Nero or fluffy-Tammamo
- many questionably bad attacks have been tweaked to make them far more useful (especially Tamamos and Elizas specials got beefed up in usefulness)
- you now have an HQ on the map, which will lose HP if you let enemies near it, and its game over if you lose it
- your other characters are actually useful and given time - will defeat lesser enemies and capture sectors (but still need to be saved a lot, especially on higher difficulties)
- there are no keys to unlock for beating sectors, instead you need to finish specific objectives to progress through the stage
- you dont need to kill basic soldiers to make aggressors appear if you attack sectors adjacent to yours, they will be available to be attacked immediately
- there are no sector traps that would require you to protect yourself by using code-casts
- you can set up to 2 support heroes which will sometimes defend you by interrupting enemy attacks and also sometimes assist you during your own attacks, depending on the bond level
- you can use a 4-directional dodge while locked-on

Code-casts and skill gems return without much change.

All of that is coupled with an increased roster of playable heroes.

The story of the main campaign kinda follows up on events from the first game, but its clear that its an alternate universe type of story as some things just dont make sense in context of the first game. Digital world is under attack by an unknown enemy who takes control of a bunch of heroes and you need to stop him.

Pros:
+ Combat mechanics
Ive explained all changes above and pretty much all of it was good. Moon Crux transformations are missed, but with how much stuff we got for all characters I wont be too hard on this. The changes to Noble Phantasm activation and introduction of skills makes the game much more fun and deepens the combat system, which is solely needed in musou-games. Even the change to have aggreessors immediately available in an area doesnt make the trash-enemies useless as they can be used to charge your Moon Drive and Noble Phantasm.

+ New heroes
The new roster introduces a fan favorite Astolfo who plays as much as you would expect her to play. All new characters are fun to experiment with and total roster is now varied and offers a lot of gameplay.

I hope in 3rd game we will get Vlad, Atlanta, Semiramis and Jack.

+ Improvements to past heroes
My 2 favorite characters from 1st game, Tamamo and Eliza, have received much needed updates to their moves. While Tamamo has lost the ability to perform long melee combos, all her attacks are now very strong and useful. Eliza also had great update with her once awkward spear-glide move now getting a much better hurtbox below her to be able to easily hit enemies all the way through her travel.

Cons:
- Story
Nearly all characters in the game get their 5 minutes of fame, which results in nobody getting enough attention to actually develop the story like you would normally expect.

- Enemy variety
Basic enemies are still as boring as in Umbral Star. Trash enemies dont matter at all anymore, archers dont do enough damage and fliers no longer stun you. It could be viewed as a positive, but this means that the game devolves into fighting aggressors and servants, while treating chaff as a source of Drive and Phantasm bars.

We also got only 1 new type of aggressor undead skeleton that appears only in some stages alongside similarly undead trash enemies. However the skeleton acts in the same way as a regular aggressor program and is just as easy to stunlock.

- Clash
If you attack during an enemy attack there is a chance you will enter an annoying clash where you will have to mash attack to win. This is present in other musou games, but here there are quick, janky animations, lots of weird grunts and shouts and the reward for winning is minor damage and pushing enemy away, which is not what you want. In Dynasty Warriors when you win a clash you stun the enemy and open them up for a combo, which is a much better mechanic.

- Easy
Put in all red gems with bonus to skill dmg and you wont need to use anything else ever again. Put in all yellow gems with bonuses to Drive and you will enter it every 4 casts of a skill and easily fill up your Phantasm bar. You can trivialize most of the game using this setup, at which point you have to ask yourself if you are not using normals and charged attacks are you still playing a musou game?

Lowdown
So heres the problem F/E Link has focused its effort on improving the gameplay itself (and overtuning skills), while putting much less effort into the narrative. This resulted in a game thats a blast to play, but lackluster when it comes to story.

I'd give Fate/Extella Link a
7/10

Should you play Umbral Star first, or skip to Link? Umbral Star is worth experiencing if you like Fate universe, since the story is pretty interesting.

Link is worth full price and a must-buy if its on sale. If you play and enjoy it, but want to experience a better story, play Umbral Star too, however be warned that since its an older game it wont be as engaging to play and will lack many cool features from Link.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a huge fan of the Fate Series, I immediately preordered this when I heard of it. When it was finally released I will in awe over the new system of combat, new servants, story and the overall expansion of this great story. Below will go in depth:

PROS

-Combat: With the addition of active skills special attacks, the combat is more "servant-y" than ever! In the first game, you would typically mash buttons to achieve combos for maximum damage and kills, rely on building up mana for a special attack (that basically attacks simultaneously every enemy in your immediate vicinity in one chained cutscene like attack), and lastly hope to build up enough noble phantasm energy to use your ultimate. With this sequel, you now can still do combo-ing regular attacks, but also have up to 4 special attacks you can equip (with much more to be learned as you level up). In addition, gone are the mana chain attack, but instead are moon drive. Basically charging your energy to give yourself a massive power boost in defense and attack (OR BEST OF ALL, using the drive skill to release all that charged energy as a powerful strike that likely wipes everything infront of you, ending the moon drive). Combat is just so much more exciting than ever.

- New Servants: Child Altera, Archimedes, Charlemagne, Rex Magnus, Astolfo (everyone's favourite), Scathach, and more! Bringing their magnificent noble phantasm and personalities with them! Did I mention Astolfo is here?

-Story: Just like the last installment, there are different paths and story arcs. However instead of being tied to one characters side of the same story. Extella Link goes in a different way: There are 3 main branches with different endings, all based around the same antagonist but not focusing on any one character. In addition, you can pick different strategies or approaches over the course of the game (for example, Attacking the enemy headon or splitting your forces) with the end result being the same, but simply giving you a different stage, boss and challenges.

-Expansion of the story: NO SPOILERS. But it simply adds a new chapter to the servants and Hakuno on the Mooncell.

-Maps: The Maps are as breathtaking as ever, and the new challenges and objectives add to the fun, drastically improving from the repetitive and repeating maps from the last game.



CONS

-Story: Despite the story having a nice new branching system, the story is blandish. With little mentioning of the previous installment until you've reached one of the 3 branches (which only briefly mentions sephyr and the Umbral Star). If you were looking forward to hearing about the next chapter with the fight against the Umbral Star, you'll be disappointed. (As well as the fact that all the servants seem to never talk about last games event.) Basically, everyone is focused on the present and no one thinks about what happened last game, detaching the feelings the player has about the previous installment.

-My room downgrade: You no longer can just watch your servant in a room of their theme, but rather now have a 2d background with your 2d servant. It doesn't help that your servants have their own room with numbers and stuff (because those are all the same looking hotel room with their weapons or items layed out randomly on chairs or walls). You'll basically no longer need to spend time in your room.

-CHARACTER INTERACTION: Last game, you've been able to bond and feel for your servant. With the use of 2D models, you wont be able to interact with your servant as you had before. You'll basically now have just the typical things expected of them (Tamamo talking about being a good wife, and Nero wanting to fight and be well....Nero...umu). I miss being picked up by Altera. Now I just have to stare at her adorable 2d child form without gettin to pat her head. YOU REALLY CANT IMMERSE YOURSELF WITH SERVANT BONDS LIKE THIS. Just take a look at the preview pictures, particularly the one with Astolfo talking about fighting a lot, that is the 2D model of Astolfo in talk scenes. You won't see him happily bouncing around while talking to you or anything.

- Little sense of actual threat: The antagonist doesn't seem to strike me as dangerous, unlike Velber and Archimedes from the previous installment.

- The Base Camp: I see a failed opportunity thinking of base camp. Servants standing around just chilling. You'd hope that they'd talk to one another, walk around, maybe cook/roast some marshmallows by the campfire, but no. They're. Just. Standing/Sitting around. AND YOU CANT TALK OR BOND WITH THEM, for that you have to walk to their rooms for bland chats. While they stand around, they'll just give you their opinion/thoughts about the current situation/mission/you.

-Several servants NEED more spotlight in the story: I happen to like Gilgamesh being....well...Gilgamesh...and Artoria being all hungry and cutesy. Getting back to seriousness, I am certain that unless you like Charlemagne, your favourite servant will get only a brief time in the story spotlight.

CONCLUSION: The game is a good one, I personally think that though there are alot of missed opportunities to make this game even more of a blast, I am just happy that I get to see everyone back. The scenery is still beautiful, the combat is exciting, the graphics are better than ever, and you're in for another chapter on the mooncell! I only am saddened by the lost of such things that made us love Extella, like bonding, animated 3D models in talk, and the major missed opportunity of base camp, where you could've seen your servant interact with other servants! All in all, at the end of the day, I would recommend this game. I really enjoyed it! and look forward to seeing the (likely) third installment in the future (hopefully it's going to deal with Velber).
