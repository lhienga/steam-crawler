\r\n\t\t\t\t\tObviously, this is going to be a positive review because the game has Astolfo in it, and we have an official 3D model of him now. He's unlocked on Day 3, and is just about everything you could want from him, well, that's allowed in a teen rated game. ;)

The actual gameplay is just glorified hack-n-slash, but I find it rather entertaining despite the repetitive nature. One thing to note, is that this game is a cakewalk on easy and normal, so if you actually want a challenge you'll have to go with hard, though I suspect you might still have to inhibit yourself manually after awhile. 'Tis the nature of Fate, I suppose.

The story is subpar, and the ending is meh. Really though, no one is playing this for the story.

Overall, 7/10, but y'know, Astolfo, so like 200/10.
Oh, and the game is in Japanese with English subtitles. Not really an issue for me, just something to note.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite my negative rating, this isn't a BAD game - if you like Fate and this is on a good sale, it maybe worth getting. However, as a 50 dollar game and as a sequel to Umbral Star, the previous Extella, this is.... not it, chief.

Without going into any spoilers, the story is a literal dumpster fire. Gone is the solid and sometimes poetic writing of the previous game; gone are the interesting characters, questionable affiliations, and interesting twists of the previous game. There's no real character conflict here, there's no depth, it's just a comically simplistic and childish good vs evil story, bereft of what made Umbral Star such an interesting act to follow. No one does anything for any good reason, things just happen because... they happen? And even when they 'join you' they may or may not actually join you, at most you get a tiny popup window (among all the others) that suggests you unlocked someone then immediately disappears. And almost nothing that happened in Umbral Star matters in Link, entire plotlines just completely negated, so don't expect followup.

The gameplay is, unfortunately, a definite downgrade from the previous as well. Rather than learning combos, you just mash your four active skills, which generally 1-2 shot everything at your level and are on very low cooldowns with no cost. Going to higher difficulties just makes everything a massive HP sponge but doesn't change the fundamentally garbage mechanics. Oh, and many of the basic combos got murdered compared to Umbral Star anyways, so only a few comically overpowered characters have any reason to use them at all, and even then only to instantly refill one of your stage-oneshot special moves! And while it was supposed to make up for Umbral Star's smattering of mediocre characters with bad combo strings, there are STILL terrible characters now, but they have bad combos AND bad skills to add insult to injury.

The passive upgrade interface is clunkier as well, somehow, but there are enough new mechanics that I would overall say the between-stages upgrade options are overall better and being able to decide which skills to bring to the stage is a very nice touch; it'd be nice if most people had more than 6-7 skills, of which 2-3 are usually useless, and you can only bring 4 - so it's not really that much customization, just figuring out which are the only good skills for that character and stick with them forever.

The roster and its relative accessibility is a clear upgrade from Umbral Star - you can play through stages NOT as whatever story character you don't enjoy playing (Umbral Star's Tamamo stages were pain); once you unlock Gilgamesh you can just play as him for the rest of the game if you want. Similarly, pretty much everyone from Umbral Star returns, though some have been nerfed to unplayability or mediocrity, while there are also plenty of new characters, most of whom I found enjoyable but admittedly poorly balanced - there is a VERY clear division in power levels between various tiers of characters. Then again, you can eventually make almost any character overpowered to the point you can autowin any stage on any difficulty, so.... progress? I guess?

In the end this isn't a bad game in its own right, a slightly above average generic Musou game with a great license. But I cannot in good faith suggest this over the far superior Umbral Star, older and cheaper and better all in one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFate/Extella Link greatly improves the graphics and combat from its predecessor. Overall the game has the similar story idea as the other one where someone is planning to take over Seraph by evil means and its your job to stop them with your servants. Also you get to play with more servants and the most important Astolfo

Story 7/10

The story is a bit better from the last installment by first getting rid of the 3D cutscenes and adding in 2d cutscenes with them talking to each (similar to the Tales conversations). The campaign also has different routes that you can take to form your own unique ending, the issue that I had was that I felt kind of overpower at the end of my first route and the areas don't adjust to your character level even when you change the difficulty. The story also seems to be lacking in some areas such characters motivations on why they join some sides or what happen to them overall. I haven't played all the side stories yet but it feels that it lacks that immersion of trying to understand these characters when going through the campaign. I do the like the idea that the master takes part in the battle so that if the master dies then you lose the fight. This inclusion makes it feel like I am actually part of the story instead of being sideline. Also the campaign is quite short when completing a route, it took me like 4 hours or so to complete the first route.

Combat 8/10

The combat is vastly improved by certain mechanics that they added such as building up for the drive with basic attacks which in returns builds up your noble phantasm. Rush and clash are some great features when facing against other servants to make it seem more intense when doing a boss fight. One feature that really helped was adding the active skills which allows a person to perform a skill when it is ready to be use and has cooldown with it. Unlike its predecessor where you had to keep up your combos to performs certain moves, this game makes it easier to do great attacks with your basic attacks as well. The combat is also very smooth as well with some frames dropping for some noble phantasms. The only issue that I had was that the game was overall easy since you could keep spamming active abilities to get rush to quickly defeat the bosses or so. It didn't have quite the challenge compare to the last game which quite disappointing.

Graphics 9/10

Vastly improve graphics makes the game the looks gorgeous and the 3-d models looks amazing compare to the last game. They also improve the noble phantasms which looks absolutely thrilling and amazing to watch.

Overall 7.5/10

I do think this game made vast improvements from its last game with mechanics and graphics, but the story seems somewhat lacking. The lack of challenge makes the game seem to easy until you reach the final boss but other than that, there really no part was a struggle for me. Again I rate this game as a 7.5/10 for being a vastly improvement compare to the last game but that is only if you are a Fate fan. Otherwise I would rate this game as a 6/10 for people who are not familiar to the game. There are some parts that are explained in the story but it doesn't keep at all what happens with the last game which I am confused since this game starts off after the events from Fate Extella Link.\t\t\t\t\t
