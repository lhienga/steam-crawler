Having played Fate/Extella: The Umbral Star, gameplay is pretty much the same (more or less), but now with a larger cast of characters and even a co-op mode (cannot say much because I haven't played it).

If you look at the System Requirements, the game really shows its age (even for recommended). However, I would say that what you get despite the System Requirements not being "supercomputer" level is amazing.

I have yet to complete the story, so I will be holding off on typing for now, but beware - the FATE series is a rabbit hole that you may or may not regret entering. And you may never forget.

Gameplay:
Good, much better than Extella. Still a musou though so a lot of it is just going through waves of enemies but there's much more depth, especially when fighting major servants (general equivalent if you play dynasty warriors).

Compared to Extella, LINK added active skills on top of the combo system which helps with the monotony of Extella's button mashing. They also added more mechanics to the overall missions such as defending the protagonist (who's also voiced in combat now which is nice). Some of these mechanics are a bit hit or miss (like basically hide and seek) but they aren't annoying and are generally tolerable and help to break up the monotony. Code casts (consumable items basically) and install skills (equipment) are still here. Moon crux was removed in favor of actual super modes now that are good for every single character (instead of being extremely powerful on just Nero and Tamamo) and end with a stronger AoE making it more fun. Extella manoeuvres are gone (thank god cuz all it did was make the game more button mashy) and noble phantasms are no longer once per map, instead you build phantasm while attacking in the super mode which means you can use multiple per stage.

Story:
It's good, Charlemagne is a really fun character and the character moments still shine just like the previous game (Fate/Extella), there could be more interactions overall but the game has a ton already and it's super fun seeing the characters interact. There's also branching paths now instead of different scenarios that helps mix it up a bit.

Presentation:
Amazing, this game has really good cutscenes (one of which is extremely hype and stands out a lot)

Difficulty:
Sadly the game is structured like a JRPG so difficulty is dictated by how close you are or how far you are above the recommended level more than anything.

Playtime:
It's pretty short, if you know what you're doing the playtime's like maybe 10-15 hours max for main story content assuming you have some trouble (the game's not very difficult IMO so you shouldn't have too many issues). Game shines with the side content though, lots of side content which has their own mini side stories alongside.

Postgame/Extras:
Side stories/fights with some lore though ignoreable that unlocks extra outfits and code casts. The outfits aren't the best but it's nice to have the option. There was a bunch more outfits on the console version but for whatever reason they're not here on PC.

Verdict:
Game's good, enough content to make it worth even at full price but I recommend waiting for a sale since $40 is high due to the fact that on average the entire game only runs for roughly 40-50 hours for a completionist.\t\t\t\t\t

While the gameplay mechanics are inoffensive, the story is so empty that I cannot recommend Fate/EXTELLA LINK
In my Steam review of Fate/EXTELLA I actually praised Fate/EXTELLA LINK for addressing gameplay issues of the original Fate/EXTELLA. However, I was only two stages into Fate/EXTELLA Link at the time so I didn't know much else. Well it turns out that review was prophesizing the future, as the only improvement is the gameplay mechanics, but at the cost of downgrading nearly every else in this sequel game.

Let me explain using pasta as a food metaphor. The pasta noodles will represent the gameplay mechanics and the pasta sauce will represent the plot.

The previous Fate/EXTELLA was a pasta dish where the noodles were so undercooked that the noodles either tasted like flour or crunched in your mouth. However, the sauce was so exquisite and delightful that it almost made it worth the noodles. You could agree with people who hated the dish because of the noodles but you could also agree with the people who loved the dish because of the sauce.

However, this Fate/EXTELLA LINK is a pasta dish with just al dente noodles and no sauce. The noodles themselves have improved, but that was a low standard to clear. By no sauce, I mean no seasoning, like no salt and pepper, no butter, no nothing. The dish is easy to stomach but it's so empty that eating something terrible may be a more worthwhile life experience. The only people who should actively eat this dish are the fanatics who are following some completion goal, like those who want to eat every kind of pasta that exists.\t\t\t\t\t
