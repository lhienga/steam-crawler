Fate Extella Link is everything the predecessor should have been & provides Fate fans with solid hack 'n slash musou entertainment for a few hours – however, due to a lack of content, it shouldn't be bought at full price

After I was heavily disappointed with Fate Extella thanks to its repetitive gameplay, uneven mix of battles and dialogue sequences as well as story issues such as pacing, unnecessary length or lack of character development, I was certainly not looking forward to its sequel. However, Fate Extella Link turned out to be a pleasant surprise and is everything the predecessor should’ve been.

The main focus of Fate Extella Link is once again a story mode, featuring multiple “routes”. In addition, there’s a 4-vs-4 multiplayer mode (which I didn’t try) as well as a bunch of “Extra Battles”, like replaying fights from the story mode in higher difficulty. The number of available Servants increased from 16 to 25 (+ one paid DLC character), leading to more variety on the battlefields.

But let’s once again focus on the “heart” of the game, the story mode. Besides from some welcome changes I’ll detail later on, the biggest surprise is probably its length. While a story playthrough of Fate Extella should take about 15+ hours, I got to see all endings of Fate Extella Link in about 6 hours. That’s quite a considerable difference - but in this case, the shorter runtime was definitely for the better. As for the conflict between this amount of content and the high pricing of the game though, I’ll discuss that at the end of this review.

Fate Extella Link is set after the events of Fate Extella but is - more or less - a self-contained story. The events of Fate Extella are rarely brought up and when they are, they are not important to the plot of Fate Extella Link. However, in regards to the world of SE.RA.PH and its inhabitants, the game expects you to have at least some basic knowledge about the Fate universe, e.g. what Servants are. So while I can certainly see someone playing this game without playing Fate Extella, I wouldn't recommend doing so as a newcomer to the franchise. You won't receive much explanation about who is who or what a Servant or SE.RA.PH is, for example.

Generally, the story revolves around a new Servant arriving in SE.RA.PH and – of course – a new war begins. There really isn’t any bigger message behind the story, it’s really just a plot centered around the new character – and of course offering a reason for Servants fighting each other again. That said, thanks to it’s short to-the-point dialogues, the story – while not offering anything big – at least doesn’t actively get in the way of the game, like it happened with Fate Extella. It does an okay job of serving as a lead through the battles and concluding everything it set up.

The “My Room” sequences are now gone, instead “My Room” was banished to one corner of the new hub to visit between missions. It’s purpose now lies mainly in customizing the skills of your Servants and while there still are a few special dialogue lines to be found, they are really just a couple of lines, similar to Fate Grand Order, nothing more. As “My Room” was poorly implemented in Fate Extella Link, that’s probably for the best, but as the main story is focused on the war, there’s little interaction with the individual Servants.

This is reinforced by the “routes” not being associated with specific protagonists, like in Fate Extella, but instead the battles you choose. Thanks to a handy in-game flowchart, you’ll know which decisions get you where – but considering this game is more or less still linear thanks to the latter “routes” having higher level requirements, you will end up following the set paths anyway. Thankfully the game also offers free switching between the battles chosen, meaning you won’t have to replay old story sections if you want to enter another “route” for example.

Overall, the story serves its purpose. It sets up the conflict, the new characters and resolves it – a classic standalone plot. It’s not long enough to get in the way of the action and thanks to a switch from the bland presentation in Fate Extella to a “proper” Visual Novel-style in Fate Extella Link, also more endearing to read through.

On the flipside, it’s short, it treats most of the Servants more as background characters than anything and it feels somewhat inconsequential due to its standalone nature. It’s nice enough for Fate fans who want to spend a few hours battling Servants but really doesn’t go beyond. That’s a letdown on the one hand but still miles better than what Fate Extella Link did on the other. And hey, the gameplay improved as well!

With Fate Extella Link, the developers finally had the great idea to offer more varied objectives throughout the battles of story mode. Sure, in the end, it still boils down to “capture this” or “kill that”. But with priority targets, timers or gimmicks like jammed maps, I had a considerably better time playing through all of story mode. Thanks to gameplay additions like friendly Servants actually supporting my character or the Master needing to be protected, the battles finally feel more alive. Gone is the dreaded feeling of fighting the same battle by myself over and over again, some much needed variety finally arrived.

That said, the game is still a cakewalk even on “normal” difficulty. By just spamming whatever available skill I currently got in my arsenal, I never experienced even the resemblance of trouble and as such, never died even once. The Servant battles are also still as underwhelming, I never experienced any kind of resistance to my skill spamming. But hey, for a few hours, blasting entire armies as a powerful servant is still entertaining when I have to adjust to the objectives of each battle at least.

Lastly, I’ll have to mention the improvements to the look of Fate Extella Link. The new maps, battle effects and character models look considerably better. The featured character designs and overall art, still done by Aruko Wada, is and will always be gorgeous. And, as with Fate Extella, all characters will all have the great voices you already got to know through other Fate iterations.

Concluding, Fate Extella Link is the perfect game for Fate fans, who just want to best entire armies as their favorite Servant. There really isn’t more going on beyond that but for what it is, it’s solid entertainment for a short time. Sure, Fate Extella Link could’ve been way more – a story that does more than being the excuse for battles, a game that delves deeper into each individual Servant and so on. But it could’ve also been way worse, see Fate Extella. It’s not going to make any lasting impressions but hey, for what it is, I can easily recommend it to Fate fans.

Of course, that is when buying this game at a way lower price. I had fun during my 6 hours with the game, but I don’t see myself slowly grinding through the “Extra Battles” or doing multiplayer (if that one even still works) – meaning I’m finished with the game. And for this amount of time and what I got out of it; I certainly wouldn’t have paid the full price this game asks. If you want to buy this game but also don’t expect yourself to do more than a story playthrough, I strongly suggest waiting for a sale that offers a heavy price cut. Fate Extella Link is an okay game for what it tries to be – but not full-asking-price-okay.\t\t\t\t\t
