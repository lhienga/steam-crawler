Diary: Fate/Extella Link

Summary

Gameplay
Fast Action, Very button spammy, Multiple skills per character, Taking over sectors, fighting bosses
Story
Three different endings, Different paths, unlockable true ending
Graphics
Nice environmental designs, bad character graphics, great character designs
Sound
Okay Menu Sounds, Nice Skill Sound Effects, Great Sounding Dialogue
Controls
Had to remap the controls using big picture mode

Day 1 - 3/8/2020
Wow, this game looks pretty good, from the backgrounds to the characters. The combat is even more fluid than the last game. I'm enjoying this game a lot so far.

I have to say, though, there are some odd story element choices. Why in the world would the big bad guy of the game, explain his plot to a kid, like brah, lmao. At least it has a lot of branching in the story depending on your actions, so you end up fighting in different scenarios which is cool.

The customization is pretty nice too, but again a bit of odd choices they made. Like needing a higher bond level in order to equip additional stat enhancements. Though I do appreciate that you can customize skill and equipment to a degree.

Aw yes, let's not forget the combat. Reminds me a lot of Dynasty Warrior like games. Where you just go around and smash on armies of enemies until you fight a slightly smarter AI which would be the generals. The generals are least can be hard at the higher levels when they have much higher stats than you, so they can sort of demolish you instead of the other way around. Normally of course this game is very spammy.

Taking over area after area in this game is quite fun though. I do like the sense of conquest I get from this game, and my other heros are actually capable of taking over stuff or defending their own objectives. Unless of course the enemy is super buffed because of something story related.

Day 2 - 3/10/2020
There are a lot of shenanigans happening. Apparently, I had to complete two different endings to unlock the last one. I noticed as I was playing that each mission unlocked its own hero so that's cool.

Well, I started getting a lot of higher gem drops, which have different symbols for each tier. Turns out though that a lower +10 tier, is still stronger than a higher +2 tier. Which is pretty good news, it means your gems aren't easily replaceable just because you dropped the next tier. You can use the +10's themselves to craft the next tier, also!

I started to play extra missions, also a little. They are nice, added little stories that you play through when you are doing individual missions. A lot of side content from what I see all the way up to level 150.

Day 3 - 3/11/2020
Just chilling, and level grinding now that I beat the game completely. Doing more extra missions and having fun doing more interactions with my servants through the dorm system. I really should have been doing these earlier; they are kind of cute and expand a bit on each character's personality.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start with the positives.
This game feels a lot more refined compared to its predecessor. Graphics and gameplay are improved, a lot of new features, and the combat feels a lot more dynamic. Allied servants no longer follow some scripted event and then be useless for the rest of the match like they did in the previous game. They are now actually going around and capturing enemy territories for you. Still scripted, but a lot more active and don't make you feel like you are the only one fighting.
A lot of cheesy things that let you exploit the game are also gone.


Everything sounds good so far. Then why would I not recommend it, you may ask.

Despite all of these improvements, many mechanics got dumbed down. A lot.
The new active skills system are nice but they make normal attacks and combos become meaningless. I mean, seriously, why even bother learning the combos while circling four active skills is way more effective and easier. And to make matters worse, every character now has identical install skill slots. So instead of building a specialized set of skill for each servant, you can simply pack everyone with the same thing. Classes become somewhat irrelevant.

Some previous problems are still there. The AI is still as dumb as before. Bosses are generally just damage-sponge and they are not much of a challenge at all.

All noble phantasms are just one big strong attacks. To be fair, there is nothing inherently wrong with that, but it is quite a missed opportunity since they can do much more than just dealing damage. If implemented correctly, it can offer a lot more diversity to the gameplay. In fact, we can see the enemy use them for support role from time to time, so I assume this is just lazy game design.
Speaking of noble phantasm, there is one more thing that always bugs me since the previous game. La Pucelle, Jeanne d'Arc's noble phantasm, is a suicide attack in which she burns herself and her enemy. It's actually quite unique, and I liked it. You might think it would have a devastating effect for such a big penalty. But fun fact: it doesn't. The damage output is pathetic even in comparison to other characters in the game.

And the story, I really expected it to be somewhat decent since the previous game did a fairly good job in that regard... but then it turns out to be a huge disappointment.
To keep this spoiler-free, I will sum it up in three words: shallow, dull and childish. You may just ignore the story altogether and enjoy the gameplay (which is not very enjoyable as explained above).
On top of that, it's also really short. A full play-through won't take you more than 7h.



TL;DR:
Despite all of the rantings above, this is actually not that bad of a game. But if you are expecting something similar to Umbral Star, you probably will get disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
