\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFate/Extella Link picks up where Extella left off - a new threat is upon SE.RA.PH and it's up to you and your army of servants to beat back some bad digital programs. If you're a fan of Fate and love the Musou style of game, this one's for you. Text has been significantly toned down from the previous game which makes the pacing between the action stages much, much better. This may disappoint the fans who enjoyed reading the visual novel portions, but I think it is a welcome change. Some new favorites are in here as well, including our best boy Astolfo of Fate/Apocrypha fame, and the beautiful Scathach who many FGO players spent untold amounts of money to obtain - but I'm getting a bit off track.

Currently there is an issue with performance on high refresh monitors, but this only occurs during heavy particle effects (such as using your Noble Phantasm) and can be remedied by capping the game's frame rate at 60FPS using NVIDIA inspector. Other than that it performs well, and the action is more intense and speedy than it's ever been.

You're offered many customization options to build your character, install skills make a return but you are also able to customize special attacks for each servant to suit your playstyle as well. More options will open up to you the higher you get your bonds and servant levels. This is easier to do than ever, thanks to the ability to spend currency to instantly level a servant to your current highest servant level. Building bonds goes faster too, and on occasion one of your servants will ask you to take on an extra task in your home base which allows you to grow their bond two levels, rather than just one. The quality of life improvements really makes this a big step up from the first game.\t\t\t\t\t

Introduction

Fate/Extella Link is a spin-off of Fate/Extella. While it does continue some parts of the story, it has been confirmed to not be a "true sequel" and that the sequel will come after Link. It features 26 servants, with 10 of them being brand new and a history revolving about the mystery of the Roman Emperor Charlemagne. Players will follow a story with options presented as branching paths to take and following a timeline, the idea is to follow a path and then revisit prior "days" in order to take different paths and explore all the game has to offer.

Gameplay

The game is a hack-N-slash type of game like its predecessor but it has made some changes to the usual formula. Both the enemies servants (commanders) and the battlefield are centered towards the principle of "dynamic, ever-changing combat", this is done in the shape of enemies getting I-frames, making combos and having sets of skills that are automatic combos. This reinforces the idea of blocking to the player as just attacking blindly will result in a quick death in hard or above difficulties. The game also allows for players to use traits of servants, skills, Noble Phantasm and to customize/manage these skills and traits.

Some Notes

The game is sort of a spiral as of now. Combat follows a high pace setting but sometimes it becomes either faster or slowdowns a little. When it becomes faster, it is filled with adrenaline since everyone is moving at such a speed it can be hard to track down where is everyone but I don't think this is bad as it makes the player rely on their reflexes. The game runs at a variable frame rate, it moves between 60 and 120 a lot and rarely it will drop to 30FPS especially near walls during boss fights/servants battles.

What Makes It Special

This game is like your Dynasty Warrior kind of game, I don't deny it. However, I like that the developers are constantly trying to craft its own identity by introducing options or entirely new mechanics. I like how unlike the previous game, customization is a big factor in this game as the player can change most (if not all) the skills of a servant, their formations, how they look to some extent and how they play out. I wouldn't say it is revolutionary but it adds a nice touch to the overall game.

Sum up

+ It has Astolfo in it.
+ Dynamic battle always keep maps interesting.
+ Customization that leans towards modifying gameplay.
+ Fleshes out servants that have never been in other media.
+ Battlefields are decorated with lots of details, looking stunning.
+ On harder difficulties, the game deviates from hack'N slash to some extent.
+ Makes up for a good entry point to newcomers, you can jump in without playing the previous game or knowing nothing about Fate.

- No English Dub.
- Low frame rate at times.
- Some Servants barely have screentime.
- Camera with a KBM can be a pain to adjust to.
- Some fan-favorites servants still didn't make it into the game.
- Keybinds don't feel natural, either with controller or KBM, players have to accustom themselves to the binds.
- There are some issues with the game as of right now that are user-specific, will probably be addressed in a patch.

Conclusion

Fate/Extella Link is a good game, it improves upon the first game and introduces any meaningful way it can to find it's own identity in a genre. This is a game that Fate fans simply have to get and that can serve as a start for new players to get introduced into the series. Even if you are not interested in Fate, it is still a great game to consider getting if you had enjoyed Dynasty Warriors or similar games in the past.\t\t\t\t\t

Does it run well? absolutely not. Frame drops happen frequently, especially when there is any kind of smoke, fog or when some enemy types spawn, also 1 area of mission 17 caused a massive drop in frames as long is i was in said area. Before some idiot decides to scream about how my PC thats too ♥♥♥♥ and not the game despite many others having the same issue, here are my specs: GTX 1080, I7-8700K, 16 Gigabytes of RAM and a samsung 970 SSD, so shut up its not my ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ PC.

The Characters are very fun and each feels different in gameplay and feel, a massive imrpovement over the previous game where a lot of characters felt really clunky. They removed a lot of the combo attacks from the previous game(a lot of them were ♥♥♥♥) and instead replaced/turned them really powerful Skills that you can activate and run on a cooldown, Nice. So far every combo feels great to execute, performs really well and looks great. Noble Phantasms(basically area nukes) can be charged multiple times now by intering the "Drive" state or picking up certain objects. Extella maneuvers were removed and now you can unleah one powerful attack when you are in the "Drive" state(basically a buffed state) at the cost of the rest of the Drive state. Overall the gameplay is greatly improved and a lot of fun.

The Maps and missions are well enough designed from a gameplay perspective though most of them are simply recycled from the previous game, They all were improved visually but thats about it which is ok. the New Maps are good enough. But there simply is not enough variety in Maps, there are about 4 or 5 tilesets that are simply re-arranged for each map(Roman Tileset, Tamamo Tileset, City Tileset, Temple Tileset and the New Tileset), causing them to look pretty much the same as many of the others using the same tileset.

The Story is a lot shorter and weaker than the previous game but it also contains a lot less loredumps, each route is interesting enough. i wont go into any detail because Fate has always been pretty story heavy and i wish to avoid any spoilers in the review. I'll simply say its OK for fate standarts(considering several really bad Grand Order campaigns), nothing more nothing less. If this is your first encounter with the Fate franchise and you want to get into the Lore you should consider playing "Fate/Stay Night" or "Fate/Extra" first and not this.

The Story takes about 10 - 20 hours to complete though there are also extra missions you can do and Multiplayer.

Multiplayer exists, i cant say anything about it since i've not played it, simple as that.

The Soundtrack for this game is pretty good, though they also use a lot of Fate/Extra CCC music(amazing music) and Fate/Extella Music(not so good). I wish it would come in FLAC format though instead of MP3.

The Graphics are a lot better compared to the previous game but still kinda bad from a purely technical standpoint. Blurry textures, bad Depth of field effect, god awful Anti-Aliasing, etc. The only exception being the character models which look really nice. its a massive upgrade from the previous game but it still looks bad.
EDIT: Disabling Anti-Aliasing removed blurriness from a lot a textures.

My experience with bugs: sometimes when running around the camera starts to rapidly shake for no reason. and some other minor issues\t\t\t\t\t
