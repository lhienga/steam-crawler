1. More servants to play as. You can use any unlocked servant on any stage of the single player campaign

2. More battle stages, and expanded battle gameplay functions. The campaign has a new game+ part, though I won't spoil anything.

5. Astolfo (For the heck of it, why not?)

So yes, I definitely have to recommend this. But hey, if its not your piece of pie, and you find it prohibitively expensive, don't get it.

Oh, by the way, if you have to get yourself one dlc- you should buy the Young Altera DLC. You get an extra character, with her own moveset, action skills, and Noble Phantasms. That, and you'll probably end up amused using her.


IMPORTANT (READ THIS!)

There is one thing however, but it's somewhat minor, but will likely be an issue for some people. As of my review, you cannot start the game without Steam being online. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this when i was bored (since why not, i like Fate)

TL;DR, This game is very recommended, great story mostly, but a controller stick is recommended to play this.

now here's my long review;

i haven't played Fate/Extella, the "prequel" of this game, but the game made it look like you don't need to, so if you're a broke boy like me who can only buy one, you could buy neither.

Story is wonderful (shed a tear at the end), very much like every Fate Series (except for Fate/Apocrypha wtf they tryna tell about?). Graphics are nice, the story parts as well, overall, no complaints.

Gameplay? its a hack and slash, just slash the enemies till they die, for me its nostalgic because when i was little i've played a hack and slash game on my PS2

No cons at. all. i enjoyed every second of the game.

though, would recommend using a controller stick.

nice job devs!\t\t\t\t\t
