\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight up front, the fact that this is a musou-style game NOT produced by Koei Tecmo is pulling a lot of weight in my recommendation. I loved DW8, and really enjoyed the Hyrule Warriors, Persona 5 Strikers, and Fire Emblem Warriors: Three Hopes spin-offs, but I really appreciate someone else trying to take a stab at a genre that seems to be pretty much cornered by one developer. This review will focus on a lot of my gripes because they're fresh and I can tell there's something here - with a bit more study, work, and playtesting, I think Marvelous Inc. could put out one heck of a game.

That said, I would be hard pressed to give Fate/Extella Link anything over a 6/10. There's a lot of things that just... aren't good, and a lot of the time this game comes off as what I can only describe as 'an early P5Strikers alpha build explained by a guy who REALLY loves the sound of his own voice'. I'm not personally invested or particularly aware of the various Fate series', but... it's a musou game - the story being fluff and not AAA Pulitzer Prize-winning writing is totally fine and to be expected. Though I will say, the Shadow The Hedgehog branching storyline is a neat idea in concept, but the fact it doesn't really change the outcome means it ends up muddying an already difficult-to-parse story. I much prefer the one-off Hypothetical Scenario idea as a way to add some extra flair and depth to a story.

There's an option to skip previously-viewed cutscenes, but as far as I can tell this does absolutely nothing. Whether it's on or off, you'll still see a cutscene of the stage even though you've been on this map twenty times before, you'll still see the cutscene when a named enemy spawns on the map, you'll still be interrupted with a shot of Drake's flat butt every time you first step foot in the same room as her - and these can interrupt what you're doing. In the middle of a Rush Attack and Arjun, three rooms away, decides to orbital laser strike a cannon? Your rush attack's over, and the person you were fighting is already winding up an attack.

The idea behind skills is interesting, but it overshadows and invalidates the normal attack combos of most characters. There's also a chance, if the skill triggers the little cinematic 'show your character winding up this big skill' camera angle, that it'll change the angle you're attacking as the camera whips around, EVEN WITH the lock-on correction enabled. Normal/Heavy attacks are practically useless when you could instead just spam all your skills off cooldown and trigger rush attacks. Also rush attacks are triggered automatically, which means if you have a multi-hit skill (e.g. two swipes and a big slash, or a slash followed by a big AoE explosion), a lot of the time that first weak hit triggers the rush and the rest of the move doesn't go off.

There's also a lot of times where it feels like enemies have priority over you, even if you're 5-10+ levels over the stage's recommended level. I've had times where I use a skill while in Moon Drive, and the enemy decides to ignore it and use their own and I get knocked out of my own move. If you change rooms and there's a servant near the entrance, they can wind up an attack and catch you in a combo before you even have control of your character. This is also true if you happen to die and revive - your character will jump up with absolutely no invincibility and be caught in another combo before their feet touch the ground.

Also I'm sorry but I absolutely hate the giant sword Aggressors. Why are they covered by shields everywhere that make your attacks bounce off them? Why do their hitboxes become GIANT RECTANGLES when they're doing their charge-forward attack, so you get hit even if you're to the side or behind them? Why, if you get clipped by them, do you get GLUED TO THEM and carried the full length, taking damage the whole time even though if an enemy superarmors through your own skill that has multiple damage frames/tics, they only take damage once? Why do they sometimes freeze you? I hate this enemy and I WILL cry about it.

Tl;dr there's some good things in this game, and I have to applaud any Dev team of any experience level trying to break into a genre that's more or less dominated by a small number of figurative titans (Monster Hunter-style games not developed by Capcom is another great example) - the new ideas and innovation is fantastic, even if maybe the whole package isn't as polished as we'd like. But at least with this game, there's some pretty egregious design decisions that should've been fixed during beta testing, and they really make a lot of the experience frustrating.

Now if you'll excuse me, I still need to finish my 400-hour playthrough of Hyrule Warriors: Definitive Edition.\t\t\t\t\t
