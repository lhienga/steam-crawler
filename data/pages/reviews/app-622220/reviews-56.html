\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe sequel to Fate/Extella, not as much of story as the previous installment but everything else is better - the music, graphics, gameplay, amount of characters... overall a great entry in the series. I just wish it wasn't so short. A must play if you're a Fate fan, but I finished the main story in a little over 20 hours. There's still extra battles to do, and I haven't tried multiplayer yet - but it's safe to say I've already explored most of what the game has to offer. I've unlocked all the Servants as well. I certainly don't regret my purchase, and I even bought all the DLC just to support one of my favorite series, but in hindsight, I really wish there was more to the story. The gameplay has been vastly improved, but the story... not that it's bad, but there was so much more to the previous game. You could experience the story through different characters' eyes, and there were vastly different routes. You also had side stories with different characters. There's nothing like that here. It's unfortunate, but oh well. I'd say it's about on par with the last game, if not a little better thanks to the extra playable characters, including the fan favorite, Astolfo.
EDIT: Multiplayer is kind of a mess, everyone has the same install skills to choose from, but it's just everyone spamming area attacks. I suppose that's to be expected. However there's so few people trying to play it can be a several minute wait just to play a quick match. It's so easy to die too. Oh well. It's still fun in its own way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: I just 100% completed it. Pretty good game. Again, it's a lot better than the first in terms of gameplay. For a side-story, it was pretty decent. The plot would've worked better if it was for an already established character, but Charles was still pretty cool. I hope he shows up again.

Also, I noticed there are about 3 empty rooms in the barracks. Assuming one of them is Hakuno's, we might be getting 2 more servants as DLC. Crossing my fingers really hard that we actually do get them.

8/10

----------------

Can't say much for the story since I haven't fully completed it yet, but right off the bat, the gameplay is a massive improvement from Umbral Star (its presequel). I very much like the change from combo finishers to skills with independent cooldowns. This makes it so servants that have really bad normal attack chains can perform just as well as the ones who has good normal attack chains because they can simply perform hit-and-runs instead of having to deal with combo finisher chains. Very much the case for certain classes like Assassins, Casters, and Riders.

The game looks a lot better too. I've only played with Umbral Star on PS4, but this game runs very well on my mediocre gaming PC setup. Overall, I'm quite happy with this game's release here in the west and I can't wait to dig deeper into the story.

The story was alright, but can't be compared to Fate/Extella.
It's shorter and a few elements on it doesn't really make it feel like a direct sequel. There's also no side stories with the other servants. There are some special stages on Extra Battles but they only have a few unique in battle dialogue.\t\t\t\t\t
