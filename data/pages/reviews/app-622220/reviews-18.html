\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFate Excella Link spins a strange yarn about there being a super computer and historical archive on the moon that is protected by "Masters". (Glorified moderators). These tech-support super-stars can use complex sentient programs based upon real life heroes and real life heroines from different time periods to defend the online space against "aggressive malware" as well as rival black-hat-hackers with their own corrupted servants. Additionally "human souls" exist in this virtual space so it seems the last frontier was not space but rather some sort of mainframe acting as a tree of life. Yeah, it does remind me of the movies Tron as well as The Matrix and if you know your way around Japanese rpgs it may also remind you of Shin Megami Tensei in which simple computer programs can summon actual demons into our world. Beneath that is meta commentary about reincarnation,our shared psychic consciousness,past mistakes,redemption,and what it means to be human.

I'm not going to beat around the bush. The game is a joy to play with plentiful customization! All your servants can "level up" and you can assign them orbs that boost various attributes whilst also choosing their technique/magic spell load outs before they go into battle. Beyond that is your bond level (Developing relationship meter between you and all your servants) and the usual assortment of heavy attacks,light attacks,launchers,team-work link attacks,and area effect genocidal war-crimes! (Noble phantasms) Everything is flashy,frantic,and fast making your chosen heroes and heroines look like mobile firework displays of mass destruction!

The weaknesses of Fate Extella Link are the typical ones found in older musou titles. "Areas" are often compartmentalized "boxes" that need clearing out of enemy generals and enemy spawners before you can secure that section and move on. Yet Extella Link should be applauded for making movement between these zones fast,convenient,and even somewhat exhilarating. It gives you that nostalgic feeling of being a kid in a theme park slipping and sliding between different tubes in a huge labyrinthine water slide. Only "here" you get tossed around by wind tunnels or mini portals! (I know people that would pay to experience that in real life! Someone might want to capitalize on that idea!)

If you are big into "open world sand-boxes" this is going to be a detriment for you. Granted I do not know who would expect a musou game to have structural environmental similarities to Far-Cry,Saintsrow,Assassin's Creed Odyssey,Witcher 3, or Sunset Overdrive but there are cetain gamers with rather odd expectations. Dynasty warriors 9 tried to take this genre into the open-world space and to this day it is the black-sheep of its' own franchise,surpassing even DW 6 in its' infamy. I'm sure DW 9 has its' fans but I'll tell ya,the best musou games I played were not open world sandboxes nor did their creators make that lofty overly expensive attempt in the first place. "Sometimes" developers are smart to temper their own aspirations whilst simply going with what worked before. There is a reason "Tried and true equals skies of blue!" is a metaphor.

By the way, most enemies are idiotic fodder as you would expect. "But" there is a rather decent variety of them with the most lethal usually being corrupt shadow variants of preexisting characters or "enemy servants" that serve the mysterious Moriarty to your Sherlock Holmes. Personally I like the robotic neon-lit cyber-punk aesthetic of the bad-guys as it is a refreshing change from fighting swathes of typical soldier grunts in other musou titles. Because of this woven illusion the game manages to feel new and exciting even if under all the appealing aesthetics it is still doing many of the same old tricks.

Last up with the musou monotony? Typical DLC gouging. "In game" each character has one alternate costume but if you want other options (And more sexier ones) you better shell out more money. Granted a lot of the female characters and male characters already look "gorgeous" as it is so the DLC is not necessary but it's still that same dangled carrot as opposed to having these outfits as unlockables in the game proper. If you have the previous Fate game (Umbral star) you will get additional outfits free so it's not quite as scummy as Koei/Tecmo tend to be. I appreciate fans of the series get rewarded for their loyalty so there is that silver lining.

And the good? Everything else,really. Game runs silky smooth,perfectly allocated itself to my controller,has consistent frame rates,and makes you feel like a certified war god or war goddess whenever you unleash an ultimate finisher that wipes out a whole room of lowly thugs! There is likewise more complexity to the fighting,powering up, and customization in comparison to other game franchises cut from a similar cloth.

Now we're in that murky place of uncertainties. What I talk about could be good "or" bad depending on your own tastes. The campaign is short. It's by far not all there is to do but Umbral star did seem to have a more fleshed out story and more interpersonal relationship dynamics to estabalish a heartfelt connection with the main characters. It's peculiar that Extella Link almost doubles the roster of servants over its' previous entry yet manages to shorten the overall story.

What you get in the end is more playable characters bundled with an adventure that moves along at a much brisker pace. Either you'll enjoy that or you will wish that there was more "down time" to better understand the lore as well as those whom inhabit it.

Pros

+ Every servant is unique

+A lot of customization options to finely tune each servant to your liking

+Combat is more robust and layered compared to many other musou games

+Traveling between area hubs is fast frolicking fun

+Flashy yet with a consistent frame-rate (At least on my rig)

+While the story is not as deep as Umbral star's there's still plenty of chances to hold conversations with various characters and to understand what's going on.

+Loyal fans that purchased Umbral Star are rewarded when picking up Extella Link as certain costumes will carry over.

+While basic foes are eager self-ending cannon fodder adversarial servants are much more challenging

+Enemies have an interesting aesthetic

+Astolfo


Cons

-DLC carrot dangling

- Short campaign

-Those unfamiliar with Fate (Be it the anime adaptations,manga,spin offs, or other games) may feel a bit lost

-Most basic enemy units are dumb as rocks (Did you expect otherwise?)

-Not much in the way of true exploration

-Jeanne D'arc's ultimate technique kills her. I understand they wanted to properly convey she was a historic martyr and saint but in technical terms it is a major buzzkill when it comes to using her. "Eventually" she can be equipped with a skill to resurrect immediately after noble-phantasm-ing herself into an early grave yet all this just comes off as needless busy work to make a deliberately broken character up to par with the others.\t\t\t\t\t
