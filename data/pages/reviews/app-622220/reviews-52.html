\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay wise, Extella Link is a great iterative improvement upon Extella. The introduction of RB-based 4-way skill system that doesn't completely suck (looking at you Dynasty Warriors 9) is a decent way to throw in a few curve balls into the combat without sacrificing any core aspects (again, looking at you Dynasty Warriors 9).

However, there are major issues with Extella Link, the biggest of which is the story - after the gripping arcs of Extella (some might say it was a bit too much Nero-wank, but it's an Extra-series game, what did you expect?) culminating in the destruction of something whose power is probably second only to the Alien God from FGO part 2, we come back to fighting a pretty generic "bad guy". With the story branches completely meaningless - they are momentary branches that will basically gather into endings that in the end, aren't even that different from each other. Basically what I'm saying is that the stake of Extella Link feels like a very significant downgrade from Extella - it feels like stalling for time with a "minor" villain while Velber is brewing its next invasion to be revealed in the 3rd game.
The other glaring flaw is one carried over from Extella - the action doesn't feel nice. In that enemies don't feel threatening (par for the course for musou games, but these enemies really don't feel threatening) and hitting them don't actually feel like you hit something. The hit feedback of Extella games are awful - there basically isn't any!

All in all, if you're an avid consumer of all things Type-Moon, look this way. Until the next chapter of the Extella arc arrives, this game will tie you over in between FGO event farming. Otherwise, you may want to look elsewhere if you're either looking for a great musou game (Dynasty Warriors 9 aside, most other Warriors games on Steam are all quite good) or a well-constructed story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFate/EXTELLA LINK, a game that I got instantly hooked on sinking in 100 hours (at the time of writing) in a few months and a game where I was confident, I was going to go for 100% completion. It is a very well-crafted game will great battle mechanics, story and a ton of things to work towards. The art and animations is smooth and well done and voice acting is amazing. I personally was never a huge fan of hack and slash games until picking up this game. For me, the games combat mechanics were easy to learn and well set out with tons of in-game tutorials to help a player out. It has great RPG elements such as levelling, learning skills, install skills (perks) and an in-game currency. Story wise, I have never played the first Extella game (but I’m familiar with the fate series) but this game was very good in introducing characters from the previous franchise and linking them together for players who never played the first game. It has a very unique story will hook you in with multiple endings providing hours of game play and a satisfying feel when you finish one. If you are completely new to the fate franchise with no knowledge of it at all, I would say it might take a bit more time to understand characters and lore however I wouldn't worry about that too much. There are 44 achievements in total and (although being an absolute grind) it was my first game where I aimed to get 100% on.

Story\t
Like I said, a very good story with multiple characters to be able to play, fun and interesting dialogue, multiple endings and is great at introducing new players to the Extella franchise. The story will take a majority of your game play up as it has multiple endings and a level recommendation (something you would either be 1 or 2 levels below or on par most of the time). All story levels are split into 4 difficulties easy, normal, hard and very hard. Each of these difficulties recommended levels vary on each stage. Easy to hard difficulty for most of the game feel like a breeze where else very hard has a massive level difference and not something to be aimed for early into the game. When you complete a level you will then be judged on how many K.OS you did, how much damage you took, time to complete the level, max combo and total sectors cleared. These are all ranked EX A B C D. If you achieved all of the levels requirements (amount of overall K.OS, max combos etc) you will be awarded an EX overall rank. This is the highest possible overall rank and in turn the hardest to achieve.

Game Play and RPG elements\t
This game (on pc) feels very smooth although being capped at 60 fps I almost never drop any frames even playing on the highest graphic settings. Levels feel well optimized and there are quick loading times to and from battles.
From a person who was never into hack and slash games, this game opened to me a new type of game mechanic that was something I really enjoyed, the fighting mechanics of the game are smooth with an easy UI example of which is the activate skills menu. Each level has their own objectives and are mostly straight forward and (with some muscle memory) you can memorise certain important enemy positions that help cut down time. However, the objectives can feel repetitive further through the game.
Mission stages are mostly repeated with different objectives or ambiance setting (day or night).
I play with and Xbox One Elite controller and have found no issues so far with response time or anything that would make me say otherwise. However, playing mouse and keyboard, for me felt clunky and mouse sensitivity felt awkward to move about with. Pros to mouse and keyboard I guess would be you can skip having to press the skill menu button and use a skill instantly.
However, some issues would be on Scathach gate of skye I experience a lot of dropped frames from 50fps to even 10fps occasionally no clue why. The lock on ability to lock on certain targets should have the ability to LOCK ON to that one specific target and not change every time an enemy passes Infront my target. This is extremely annoying in the final extra battle where I’m focusing one robot arm and another passes by it then locks onto THAT one meaning I would miss my special attack.

Characters\t
This game has a lot of well-known characters of the fate series to work with each with their own unique skill, design and dialogue. All characters have their own set of skills that vary from being able to stun/slow the enemy, increase attack and attack speed, CC an area or just completely wipe it off the face of the game or your regular high damage cool down skill. With the help of install skills this provides different builds to be made for characters for different usages. An interesting mechanic is the ‘money is power’ system allowing you to level a character up with the correct amount of currency. This helps keep character on par with level requirements for missions, however it does come at a hefty price of getting more expensive the higher you want the characters level to be.

Extra Battles \t
These are essentially new battles or one from the story mode but much harder that scale in difficulty (last one being the highest possible difficulty) the further you go down. They are just an easy way to pass the time with some giving away a free character costume. However, for a real challenge you can push yourself (like I did for fun) and try to achieve EX on every extra battle. It’s a real challenge ( please please please add an achievement for completing all extra battles on ex thanks 😊 )

Small issues \t
What I found extremely annoying and extremely frustrating early into the game was, when trying to achieve EX on every stage difficulty I had not the faintest clue of actually how many KO.S I need to do, the highest’s combo I need to perform, how much damage I had to take before it was deemed an A instead of an EX. If this was to add challenge to the game I fully understand that but please at least add to the UI in battles or in the pause menu a tracker of how many K.Os I’ve done, what my max combo is and especially the total damage I took. The amount of times I’ve finished a level on Very Hard with everything on EX except total damage because I prayed to God that, that one hit from the Lancer Shadow wasn’t enough to drop me below an EX.
If you are aiming for EX on Very Hard or on an Extra Stage, then from what I have gathered these should hopefully be the average required number of things you would need for an EX(please excuse me if these numbers aren’t always right, they are all rough estimates xd). Other difficulties vary in requirement numbers for EX but to play safe for Very Hard difficulty and Extra Battles try to aim for these numbers.
Total amount of K.Os for an EX : 5000-6000 or above
Total amount of Max Combos for an EX: 1000-roughly 3000 or above
Total amount of Sectors taken for an EX: All. Who would have guessed?
Total amount of Damage taken for an EX: Preferably 0% or anything as low as possible, however in some cases (like a few high-level extra battles) I’ve got away with an EX of 150% total damage taken or below. Again, it varies depending on what difficulty/mission you are playing.
Although completing all these games achievements, I feel like adding a few more (if possible) wouldn’t hurt anyone and would give people something else to aim for.
Multiplayer isn’t something I’ve really tried so I have no words for it.

Conclusion \t
Great game for people who are into hack and slash games and for people that have never played one before. This game is also very fun and enjoyable for Fate fans who want to try something new.
To the developer thanks for the amazing game and the hours of fun, sweat and grind. Please keep updates rolling and keep it up 😊 ! Also please add more costumes, I wouldn’t mind spending a few extra for Scathach.\t\t\t\t\t
