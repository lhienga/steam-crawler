\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have at the moment of this review played about 13 hours of the game so take what you will from my review.

General:

As a Fate fan i give this game a 10/10, as a gamer I give it 8.5/10.
Now for reals though, I recommend this game mainly to two types of people, those who are Fate fans (obviously!) and those that love the "dynasty warriors" genre of games as this game is similar in terms of gameplay (in a more broad sense) to those games.For those that have not played these types of games and want to try out something of this genre, I would not recommend to start with this one, as it is a sequel to Fate/Extella, so you might miss some context.

Gameplay: Pro

The main meat of the game receives a well deserved "Umu" seal of approval, as it is a full improvement over the gameplay of its predecessor. The combat feels more fluid, there are more options for combos, characters feel more balanced due to the install skill tree having been redone (The faker will no longer be worse than the original XP), no more annoying plant spam (easier time doing the EX completions), more diversified missions (still boil down to go here kill stuff but with more flair to it I would say), special abilities (noble phantasms) are no longer one use only, 10 more characters to sink your teeth in (11 if you buy young altera) and older characters got more stuff introduced to their kit. Plus the new skill system feels pretty good and permits you to choose abilities for the character to suit your play style.

Also within this category, the stages this time around feel more diversified and less repetitive, making for much more enjoyable and COOL missions.

Last thing, the multiplayer is a nice addition, though I have not touched it at the time of this review, so I cannot give more insight into it then this.

Graphics/Sound: Pro

I decided to put these two together because they basically are again an upgrade from the previous game and I felt that they are not as important as other things.
To make things short the graphics are very good, not AAA good but they more then hold up. In terms of sound they got a much better music selection this time around compared to Extella, and they still have the soundtrack from that in this game, plus some from Extra and CCC, so props to them for that. "Umu".

Story: Mixed

In terms of the story, I am partially happy and partially disappointed, on one hand its pretty "simple" in the overall and works well for this type of game, on the other hand, in terms of the details, it can get pretty confusing, specially for those who did not play Extella before this game, because even though it tries to explain what happened before this game, it is not detailed enough for new players to comprehend some of the terms, however if you either don´t care at all about the plot or you played the previous title, then you should find the story good enough but wanting for more.

Characters: Pro/Mixed

This one is easy, you get to play with 26 (27 with young altera) from the Fate franchise, who by this point already have their personalities established (except two) and you probably already have your favorite waifu/husbando to play in this game ("cough" Medusa "cough") . If you don´t then you have a lot to select from from the badass characters(Iskandar, Archer, Gilgamesh, Artoria, etc) to more comic relief (Elizabeth, etc) to more story centric ones(Charlmagne, etc), to the cute ones (Young Altera, etc) to Astolfo (you know why it gets its own category). No matter what they each have their own flair and kit that might make you enjoy them or not. The negative to these characters is only one, story involvement, as about half to two thirds get good development in the story of this game, but some of them, especially new ones get almost nothing , and some of the old almost don´t even appear int the story, making me very sad. Well at least they appear though.

Conclusion: Recommended

In the end I believe this game is worth the current price tag and should be an enjoyable experience to any Fate/Dynasty Warriors fan or to any Hack/Slash gamer, with its fast and smooth gameplay. There should be no more doubts about this game mongrels. MUHAHAHAHAHAHA.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR : A marked improvement to its predecessor, and a good game on its own merits

While the main value of this game is IMO the gameplay, it doesn't then just abandon the story altogether. Story's good, if the plot devices are somewhat dated - but then I trope a lot and read a lot, a bunch of plot devices seem dated to me. But still, exploring the duality of a historical figure and his more fanciful, fantastic and legendary self as told by storytellers is pretty novel.

I kinda miss the old school "~bzzt~ wrong choice ~BERSERKERSPLAT~" bad endings of Stay Night, but I can understand that sort of thing missing in a Musou game, on account of doing the fighting yourself (in which doing that will generally turn out into a That Battle Didn't Count cop-out).

-----

As stated, the gameplay is where this shines. It takes the systems of the previous game and refines it nicely. I still remember in the previous game how Gil can just throw out GoB at will when computer controlled, whereas under your control you have to finish the whole string of X-X-... combo, and still risk getting knocked out of it (or how Nameless can just backflip-Hrunting pretty much at will). The Active Skill system here allows you to do what the computer did with a combination of 4 out of 8 skills available.

The Install Skills (which are basically passives you can mix and match) is also very much overhauled, no more of the arbitrary adjacent slots connect, except if they don't. Now all adjacent skill slots connect, so you're not going to have your favorite setup gimped because of not using a certain Servant (like how I tend to prefer to stuff all eight slots with Support Skill, which in the previous title meant that I'm pretty much stuck with Gil if I want to optimize the bonuses).

Changing the Moon Drive to be more like Moon Crux mode in the previous game also helps, seeing how Moon Drive was basically Press B To Clear Trash Mob. It makes the flier enemies even more obnoxious (it used to be that Moon Drive can immediately be used clear them out), but making the overall experience of mob-clearing more interactive.

And where the story stages are kind of limited, there is a lot of Extra Stages, sometimes with their own story (generally the lighthearted sort, or just cool what-ifs - I know you're thinking of Alexander-Darius team-up, the Devs clearly did too). Basically, there's a lot to unlock and not usually locked behind some arcane requirement you can only find out by offering a sacrifice of a black sheep when the stars align just right (or by waiting for online guides).

The addition of new Servants doesn't hurt either. It's nice to see Arjuna as OP as his lore suggests (sorry Grand Order, I love you but here you fail), outdoing even Gilgamesh if such a thing was possible, at least in my experience so far. And the new systems mean that the experience of playing Karna here is pretty different from playing Karna in EXTELLA. And of course, the ever popular, orientation-confusing Astolfo. More Servants to cater to their fans is generally a good thing (assuming they are viable, which so far seems to be the case).

-----

I know I compare the game a lot to its predecessor, but it doesn't change that this game is good on its own merit. Fate/EXTELLA is a good game even without Fate/EXTRA (or CCC, though I'm still holding out for the translation to finish), so I don't think comparing LINK and EXTELLA should be taken as "this game NEEDS you to have played EXTELLA". Definitely recommending this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing plenty of this game, I can tell they listened to feedback about the previous game.A lot of gameplay things have been changed to correct more bothersome mechanics from the previous Extella. Install skills now have descriptions of their effects and the idea of the double-white skills is pretty good. The active skills are a nice touch for doing flashy moves without having to do its combo, but they feel a bit spammy, since by the time you've used all four of them the first one is ready again... Maybe longer cooldowns?

Some harder difficulties would be pretty nice. Even playing 30 levels below the recommended level I could defeat the enemies without much hardship. Never once I KO'd without using Jeanne's Noble Phantasm. And there's 3 revivals anyway.

All the servants from the previous game are back and there's several newcomers. The story is simpler, less loaded with narration and there's no longer "X character's storyline" with things that implied I personally didn't like. The story is no big deal, but there's a lot of quotable dialogues.

Online mode... is not so good. I hope they plan to add new things. Like a much needed coop mode. Games of the Warriors genre don't work so well with pvp modes due to how they work, but even then I didn't really like how the pvp mode is currently. As I said, coop mode would be awesome.

Overall an improvement over the first game, well done.\t\t\t\t\t
