You've got several distinct and intense battle themes that almost never get old. If you're like me and ever so often want to play different music to your games, this is definitely worth purchasing. However, this soundtrack doesn't have much to offer in day-to-day life, really not fitting at all with any of the mundane things you're doing in the real world, unlike, say, Furi's.

If you've already bought Darkest Dungeon: This soundtrack includes the music for the DLC you don't have, and (in the same file) the Pitch-as-black alts to every song. Very worthwhile if you were considering purchasing the soundtrack, but are scared it won't be everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasily one of my favorite games scores, not just because it fits the mood of the game, but because of the composition. The songs are meticulously crafted with subtleties that are hard to describe, mixing traditional sounding instruments with synthetic other worldly sounds. The tracks aren't just lazily taken from the game and given a price tag. They are arranged to be a complete album and not just mindless loops with an eventual fade. A good example is "Explore the Ruins" which recreates the dynamic effect of the torch system, progressively scaling up in tension. I have many game soundtracks on my DAP, but this is one of the few that feels like a proper album. I am not ashamed to admit that this is one I play a lot while driving in the rain. Also FLAC is a nice option, which is generally the deciding factor on if I pick up a soundtrack here or elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t
