Would I Recommend?

Yes

Things I like:
- All three main emergency services represented (police, fire and rescue, ambulance)
- Incredibly good variety of callouts for all services
- Updated map is expansive with multiple spawn points for each service
- In-depth (but not too in-depth) diagnostics and treatments for ambulance patients
- Fully customisable lighting loadouts (program your own lightbar effects in game)
- Good choice of vehicles, with some unique to each service
- Communicative Devs who listen to community feedback and are consistently and actively developing the game both throughout the early access release and since 1.0
- Now includes mod support!

Things to improve:
- Graphics are a little on the lower end but not to too much of a detriment to the game
- Some interactions with menus, weapons and such can feel a little janky on occasion
- Character customisation is very limited and it's easy to meet your twin in multiplayer games

My thoughts

While Steam says I've only played 48 hours of this game, I've played it offline on my laptop for longer when out and about. The game is fun, and feels very different to when I started. Gone are the old, tiny map, the janky physics, the limited vehicles, the zero customisation, the repetitive callouts. Now, this is one of my favourite simulation games when I want to unwind with sirens.

Do I think this is the pinnacle of simulation? No. A smaller developer with a limited budget would struggle to make a game with the polish of some of the big names. It's not the prettiest, sure, but you can see how much thought and care went into the assets. The interactions may be based in simplistic menus, but they are easy to navigate and function well. There may be no tutorial to speak of, but half the fun is learning as you go.

It's clear to see that this is not a game made for a contractual obligation. This is a game made by people who wanted a simulator for every emergency service, a game by people with a passion for creating a fun experience that people can play together, so they went out and made it. I got the game at a very good price a long time ago, pretty much as soon as it came on the store, and I've seen how active the devs were throughout. Sure there have been delays, things have been pushed back, updates to the game rejigged, but the reasons were always communicated and new dates given for when we could expect things.

This isn't an example of a developer saying they care about their players. This is an example of one that SHOWS they care. Through constant updates, roadmaps and engagement, we have a game now that has been built as much for the community that exists as for new players finding the game. And for that, this game deserves every recommendation\t\t\t\t\t
