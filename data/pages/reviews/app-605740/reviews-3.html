\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I'm honest, I can't recommend this game. I have stuck around with this game for years watching how the game's development has panned out and this game has always had a special place in my heart, but after coming back to this game today, I realized why I don't play this game at all. Feature-wise, this game has a lot to do. The medical system is incredibly in-depth, the map is pretty large, there are quite a few different calls for police, medical, and fire, and overall, there are lots of features to explore. However, the game feels like it needs some serious polishing. The animations all feel off and many of the animations (especially the gun animations) feel like they don't even match the object you are holding. The AI leaves a lot to be desired, with randomly changing speeds, sometimes down to 40 mph on the freeway, as well as running into you, never switching lanes, and generally feeling robotic. Graphically, the game looks like a mobile GTA 5 knock-off. All the textures feel dry, the amount of empty space in a bustling city is off the charts, the characters all look like they are straight off the unity asset store, and the menus all look designed for mobile. The physics don't feel satisfying or realistic, there are barely any cars anywhere unless they run into each other as they do every 30 seconds, and every interaction is just pressing up and down through menus to access everything. On top of all of this, there isn't anything to work towards as everything is unlocked right away. You don't have to work to level up your officer to unlock better stuff, you just hop into a sandbox and drive from one call to the next. Personally, this game feels like an alpha or pre-alpha build full of temp-assets and unfinished features rather than a full release. I hope one day to be able to come back and change my review when I see a polished and release-ready game, but I unfortunately don't see this happening because the developers have already moved on to paid DLC and extra features.\t\t\t\t\t
