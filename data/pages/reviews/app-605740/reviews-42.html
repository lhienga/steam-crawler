\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game altogether is pretty fun. I recommend getting it during the promotion as it's not worth the full price. The game has few player customization items, a severe lack of NPC intelligence, and multiple game-breaking bugs. Single-player consists of only solo missions with large accidents that require the usage of the other teams to be uncompleteable. Switching teams during a mission results in the mission being despawned, leaving no way to complete a single-player mission requiring other teams to complete it. Another game-breaking bug causes all NPCs and vehicles to despawn except for mission-spawned NPCs, this bug usually occurs after a couple of hours of the server running, that includes a single-player. The mod shop has a couple of mods but none of them address any of the previously mentioned bugs or any for that matter. The game does have a decent selection of light bars, light customization as well as detailed NPC interaction. Most interaction with NPC's has no animation, especially the medical team. Using the police team is difficult due to cars either stopping in the middle of the road when using EMS sirens and lights, pulling over and then ramming into the player's vehicle, or just completely ignoring the player. As of yet, I would recommend watching other people play the game before buying it. Hopefully, it will convey some more advantages and disadvantages. The game itself has a large potential, the map scale is enormous but the mini-map can be confusing at times. EMS calls in addition also appear to come from the main city, instead of what is closest to the player but I expect that after a while the developers will implement a fix that will make calls come in near the player instead of randomly spawning on the map. The developers do plan to add NPCs EMS services to the game which will aid the player but as of yet they have not been implemented. Most NPC behavior is clunky, the majority of the game appears to be the bare-bones of a concept idea currently, and most if not a pretty decent amount of features have not been implemented yet. The developers claim to have fixed the NPC spawning system, however, it is as of the writing time of this review, not been fixed. There are simply too many bugs to warrant buying the game at full price.

Bug List at the time of writing:
-After 1 hour, all NPCs and cars will despawn This does not include any mission-spawned ones.
-Jumping while holding a handcuffed NPC causes the player to no longer play the handcuff-holding animation
-NPC cars will occasionally ram player-driven vehicles if they have an EMS siren and lights on.
-NPC's sometimes don't register a player aiming at them, and continue running. (Distance required to force surrender is usually directly up against the npc)
-In some areas, varies occasionally, traffic lights do not work, nor is the pedestrian walk sign attached to it.
-Shoplifting Mission vendor NPC can sometimes spawn in the backrooms area of the shop, when delivering the stolen items to the NPC, the NPC will accept them and then proceed to walk through the walls.
-Unsure if this is a bug or a unused feature, when registering an NPC, the NPC disappears and doesn't appear in any holding cell for any duration of time. (Where'd they go? To the backrooms? I mean I guess they went with the ambulance, when the ambulance shows up it just disappears alongside the victim.)
-When accepting a mission for Officer needs assistance after the bug where all NPC and cars disappear, the mission officer/officer car doesn't spawn.
-There is also a glitch during the gunshot wound call on the EMS team where the police car and police officer spawn inside the house\t\t\t\t\t
