\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had this game for a couple years now and finally feel I can make a comprehensive review, so here goes!

Graphics: 6/10
Granted the textures are fairly high resolution, the actual visual aspect of the game suggests their graphics artists are not the greatest, but not the worst by a long shot either!

Mechanics: 9/10
The mechanics in this game are fairly solid! I haven't ran into many bugs (the biggest one that comes to mind is the issue of traffic stops not properly initiating or the peds outright just ignoring you when you start it) but these are far and few between and for the most part have been fixed over the years. The attention to detail with the MDT is pretty impressive, though when vehicles are stolen it does not display a name for the actual owner which can be a little immersion breaking.

Multiplayer: 5/10
For the most part, multiplayer has fallen flat anytime I've actually booted it up, I tend to stick to single player as a result because most online sessions can feel a bit hectic with most people not doing their jobs and just picking a class based on what tools troll the best. There is a high chance that you will be followed by someone for extended periods of time with their sirens on as this would happen to me every other online session I join. I personally think the online mode would benefit from having a simple civilian role (or off-duty for cops with civ clothes and normal cars possibly?) that would allow for more immersive calls/altercations with non emergency personnel. This would probably give the trolls something better to do and it would benefit everyone as a result!

Overall impression and score: 8/10
When I originally bought the game I would have given it a 6 rather than 8, mostly because a couple years ago the game was fairly difficult to run at a decent frame rate, had many bugs, map was uninspired and content was fairly light, but sense then the devs have been fairly active in the community and the game with constant updates and optimizations. They even changed the map to something larger and more detailed which was a big plus! We went from only being able to give out parking tickets and doing the same handful of calls to so much more in the matter of 2 years and that is pretty good in my book!
This may not be the game for you if you're expecting fiveM quality, but if you are into immersive simulation games that has an active community (how ever small) and dev team behind it, join up!
