\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the three series that made up most of my childhood gaming, Megaman Battle Network (MMBN) is a unique series that combines a lot of genres.

You have Deck (Chip) builder/Card (Chip) collect-a-thon (not necessary to do, but some will treat it like that), Action/top down shooter, etc...

Basically, all the "Virus Busting" battles start with you selecting from 5 (more choice with later stuff) "chips" randomly chosen from your "folder" (deck), which are then sent to your Navi. You then start fighting on a 3 by 6 grid in real time using the single use chips and a basic attack from your 3x3 to defeat the enemies on their 3x3.

While there are definitely parts that haven't aged as well (The Era of ZERO auto-saves and game-overs from losing battles, as well as puzzles that weren't always clear or localized well... oh and back-tracking/sort-of-grinding), it's a great series that I none the less struggle to recommend for one reason: Denuvo Anti-Piracy software is included in it, DESPITE the ROMs being out in the wild for forever (in other words the piracy has long since happened).

Denuvo slows down and adds lag to actions on occasion that can't be predicted and will cause you some game overs, especially if you're new. It also means the "big deal" of the remaster, network battles, are crap as well.

So while I recommend the whole series, If there's the possibility of Denuvo being removed in the future, try to wait for that... And don't get this only for multiplayer, because it won't be worth it with that alone (and it was further segmented/badly implemented in a way that makes long wait times... And Denuvo apparently doesn't prevent some cheating either)\t\t\t\t\t
