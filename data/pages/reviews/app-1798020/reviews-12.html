\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI write this review as a long time Mega Man Battle Network fan having jumped onto the franchise when the 3rd game came out over 20 years ago (gosh that makes me feel old writing this). I will also admit that I bought this game on all 3 platforms it was released on. My experience with this game is everything I hoped from this collection. No drastic changes to the game, they even added a filter that smooths out the pixels in the game which I actually like. They even brought back Andrew Francis who lent his voice for Mega Man in the anime dub from 2002 - 2003. All in all, I have thoroughly enjoyed this game and would recommend it to any Mega Man fan as well as those who enjoy a futuristic styled RPG. It's kinda cool how much Capcom predicted smart phones would be capable of doing. The first iPhone wouldn't come out til this franchise came to an end. My hope is that maybe, just maybe, we will get a revival of *THIS* iteration of Mega Man that is finally get some much deserved love and attention. BATTLE ROUTINE.. SET! EXECUTE!!!!!

Pros:
- 3 games (6 if you count each one having two versions) in one package.
- Innovative fast paced, card-based combat with a very strong strategic element.
- New level of strategy in MMBN5 with turn based liberation missions.
- Buster MAX mode speeds up combat which aids heavily to getting through sometimes tedious combat.
- Memorable and creative enemy design and an excellent story both individually and over the series of games.
- Patch, Event, and Special cards which were a Japan exclusive are now all added to the games.
- Multiplayer is fun and has a rather kind fanbase community.

Cons:
- A few audio and visual glitches (likely will be patched out soon).
- No way to change BGM in game using the music gallery.
- Sol Cross from MMBN5 Double Team on the DS was NOT included in MMBN5 which makes sense since this is a GBA port. But on the bright side, there is a special card that gives you Sol Cross' charge attack ability.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love the MegaMan Battle Network franchis and have been waiting for these collections for a long time now. MegaMan Battle Network has been one of my longest standing favorite franchises, ever since I played them way back on GBA, sometime before MMBN5 released. So I'm extremely glad they finally got around to making these collections. MegaMan Battle Network may not have been my intro into Mega Man, that being Mega Man 8, but it's what definitely solidified my undying love for the Mega Man franchise as a whole.

Pros:
[Franchise as a whole]
- Amazing music.
- Lovable cast of characters, both friends and foes alike.
- Beautiful locations, both in Lan's world and in Mega Man's.
- Fun gimmicks per each entry, such as Cross Fusion, Double Soul, etc.
- Amazing pixel art.
- An overall very exciting and enjoyable adventure.

Cons:
[MMBN1]
- Random battle shenanigans are a wee bit too frequent for my taste. I'd say they popped up around every 15-20 steps or so, or feels like it. And the only way to escape is by
- The net is way too same-y, found myself running around for at least an hour at one point, literally like "Were am i!?" All backgrounds were the same, with no real way to identify where you were going, unless you memorized each path.
(Both points have received great improvements with MMBN2, as battles are a little less frequent and you always, excluding certain encounters, have the option of escaping. Even without the Escape Chip.)
