\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBattle Network 4

The black sheep of the family, this game takes the N1 Grandprix scenario from BN3, and stretches it into a full game, with only a tiny bit of story at the very beginning and at the very end.. everything inbetween is pure fluff; there's no sidequests, job boards, or reason to explore, other'n gathering passwords to use in the much superior Battle Chip Challenge

This game has three difficulty modes. Normal, Hard, which you unlock after beating the game, and finally Super Hard, for beating the game again on Hard. The Tournament and the scenarios you get are different depending on which difficulty yer on, which is a neat idea, but I'd rather just have all six scenarios, instead of being forced to play through the game thrice

Instead of the Style Change system, this game has Double Soul, where you essentially "sync" with certain bosses you've beaten to get extra dmg and attacks with certain chip types for a few turns, but you alius end up being weaker'n base form 'cause yer NaviCust upgrades don't affect it for some reason. You unlock three Double Soul during NG, two more on Hard, and a final one on Super Hard.. and you need all six to access the postgame

Thing is, even on NG the game is hard as balls, but not 'cause the bosses have neatly designed attack patterns like in Battle Network 2, no. In this game yer just super weak. I know it's to tempt people to fall over to the "dark side", which is what replaces Bug Style from BN3, where if yer struggling, you can use "dark chips" that are super strong, but permanently lower yer HP with every use, and can "corrupt" Megaman but c'mon, there's better ways to make a game harder'n just gimping you.. that's just not fun.. there's only a reward for sticking to the "light side" in the freakin' postgame, and it's not even that good

Battle Network 5

I remember loving this game as a kid and unlike BN3, it seems I remembered right! The game has a proper story again (but still no sidequests or incentive to explore), Dark Chips are more of a build you plan in advance and accordingly, non-Dark Chips users are powerful once again. Heck, even the Double Souls have been reworked and made actually not only viable, but really good

The Liberation Missions mechanic is this game's main mechanic and man, it's so fun. It plays kinda like a boardgame where you control multiple Navis, it's neither too hard nor too easy, and you get bonus rewards for beating a mission under a target amount of turns. Sadly, that comes at the sacrifice of everything outside of Liberation Missions being pretty mediocre and backtracking-heavy, and even the postgame consists mostly of just a few more missions

Finally, this game's Patch Cards (previously eReader-exclusive) are actually fairly well balanced (unlike BN4 where they're just full on cheat codes), and have some special Double Soul forms that unlock a few hidden cutscenes and boss fights

Overall while it's still not as good as the first two games, it's pretty darn close, and single-handedly makes Volume 2 of the Legacy Collection worth getting.. or at least it would if it wasn't for

Battle Network 6

Sidequests and request boards are back! Cross forms have been simplified, made more viable, and there's even a new "beast form" on top of it that you can use pretty much whenever, and mix with cross forms to create different "cross beast forms" with special abilities, pretty cool. By all means this should be the definitive "modern" Battle Network game, but it's ruined by one simple yet outrageous fact.. this game is pure fascist propaganda

See, Lan moves to this new town that's apparently "the way of the future", and.. it's just a military state, with security checkpoints everywhere and guards that constantly harass you, the "foreigner". In the schools, in aquariums, in bus stations, at the entrance and exit of cities, even the Internet ain't safe.. literally everywhere is strictly controlled and under constant surveillance. Now if the plot revolved around Lan finding out all of those sketchy things and being like "yeah maybe this isn't the direction we should be taking" but no, instead everyone in the game is like "wow, what a cool future we live in, what a superior society we are!"

It's no wonder the Battle Network serie died, this North Korea simulator single-handedly murdered it\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust like the first volume, a decent collection of games. Again, I'm not the biggest fan of Battle Network compared to the other series, but BN5 and BN6 really impressed me in their gameplay and OST quality. Top notch stuff. BN6 especially has very little of that ever-present over-reliance on backtracking compared to the other 5 BN games, so it's by far the most pleasant to go back to. BN5 still has quite a bit of backtracking, but the story in it easily is the best in this sub-series, and the Liberation Missions are fun.

And then you have BN4, because what's a Megaman collection without at least one game that sucks? What made it worse were the achievements. You have to beat each version of BN4 a minimum of THREE TIMES to get everything (for a total of 6 runs of basically the same game). This effectively made BN4 into the Megaman game I hate the most (and I've played pretty much everything other than Star Force at this point).

If you don't care about skipping BN4 and don't plan on 100%'ing the collection, this is very much worth it just for the other two games.\t\t\t\t\t
