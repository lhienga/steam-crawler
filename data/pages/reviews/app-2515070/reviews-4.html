\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot to like in this VN.
Cute characters, funny dialogue, sweet story, really amazing art and music.
But I think there's definitely some things to dislike about this game that others aren't really talking about.
It's a pretty short VN, especially for the price. I think supporting Hololive Projects like this for future content is a good idea from fans like us, but this was lacking IMO.
As it was an "Aqua" focused VN, I didn't expect the side characters to get fleshed out routes, but the fact that they at least have gag routes make me feel robbed. Those gag routes are good too! Just sad that they are more jokes than anything.
Main route, while being cute, also has an abrupt turn of events that feels like its 0-100 real quick, and it continues to be 100 all until a last minute plot twist that feels like I wasted my time playing the game. The proper ending is a common trope that I think we shouldn't even pretend is fun in stories.
In conclusion, its a cute game with cute characters and cute art and nice music, but I think the narrative ends up lacking too much for a VNs main pulling point.\t\t\t\t\t

Introduction

Aquarium is a romance VN developed by Entergram and sponsored by Hololive, the biggest VTuber agency in the world. I'm fairly familiar with VTubers, epecially from Hololive, and when I saw the trailer for this game, I was pretty interested in what would they do with it. Now, I have an answer.

Worldbuilding

It isn't clear what the setting is for the story, but it gave me a strong Victorian era vibes. It establishes a few rules, like the fact that nobles and commoners aren't able to interact with each other and the fact that exists a war of succession of sorts. The first rule is the one I have some contentions with, because in order to break the established rules of a universe, the writers need to provide an in-universe, logical reason why said rule can be broken. Because one of the biggest payoffs of the story relies on breaking the first rule I mentioned, there must be a logical reason to do so. And, the way the writers broke said rule seems a bit contrived to me, because of how key characters are portrayed, I expected more tension and drama. But the resolution was a bit too convenient for me. I have no issues with the second rule, because it is a source of tension and drama towards the end of the game, was properly foreshadowed, and created endearing moments for the main characters.

The plot

Ths story isn't heavily plot-driven, but there is a overarching story. It's... you guessed it... a love story between a noble and a commoner. This is one of the most used tropes in the romance genre, but I don't judge a story for the trope itself, but for its execution. That said, there is nothing groundbreaking in the way the plot is presented, and, like I said in the previous section, I expected more tension and drama, which creates investment on the reader. On the other hand, I found a couple of contrivances in the plot, both towards the end of the story. One of them is very minor, and has to do with the appearance of a cat in a tension-ridden scene. I say it's minor because it didn't change the direction of the story whatsoever. Just needed a bit more foreshadowing. The second one is significant, and it happens after the story ends, because it relies on a very unlikely coincidence that makes the final scene more emotional. A story can be emotionally powerful and intelectually satisfying at the same time, because endearing moments rely on believeabillity, and believeability relies on consistency, which is achieved by intelligent writing. There are also a pair of side plots, but they are played for laughs, and that's the point.

The characters

This is one of the strongest points about the game. It doesn't have an extensive cast of characters. There is the main character, Theo, his love interest and two side characters. Theo's love interest in Minato Aqua, a VTuber from the Japanese branch of Hololive, and so are the main secondary characters, Shirakami Fubuki and Houshou Marine. Marine's characterization in the game is consistent with her VTuber persona, always making lewd jokes and pretending to be a pirate. Her side plot is ridiculously hilarious too. I'm not too familiar with Fubuki, but her obsession with spices is in stark contrast with her more down-to earth personality, and she acts like a mother figure for Theo. I find it strange that Theo and Fubuki are related by blood, when Fubuki is a fox girl. Her side plot is also hilarious. Aqua, on the other hand, is handled more seriously, and she has moments of growth, when she has to overcome her main flaw, which is her inability to handle crowds, in order to accomplish unity on a divided town. Watching her grow as a character was very satisfying to witness. Theo, on the other hand, is a static character and he has no shame in saying or doing embarrasing things, which annoyed Aqua to no end. He also breaks character several times, he being a noble, and acting like a peasant for Aqua's sake. It's interesting to note how the roles are reversed in key instances, which reinforces the theme of the story, which is everyone are human, no matter their social standing.

Presentation

The game is beautiful to see. Very soild art direction, with vibrant colors and detailed and sharp backgrounds. Character artstyle is also very detailed and sharp. Sound effects are also well done, especially the fireworks in the extra story. The voice acting is excellent, and it's a testament of the talent of Hololive's VTubers, but the music not always matches the setting. The game has plenty of customization options, including key bindings, controller and touchscreen support and several accesibility options. There are some technical issues, though. The game doesn't scale well to 1440p displays and above while on fullscreen mode, because the cursor doesn't reach the border of the screen, as if there was an invisible padding area. This is a problem, because the in-game options are in the bottom of the screen, and you won't be able to reach them in that mode. My recommendation is, if you have a 1440p display or higher, set the game to windowed fullscreen, and you shouldn't have any issues.

In conclusion:

Pros:

-Solid writing, with some exceptions.
-Characters are very likable and fun, especially Aqua.
-Excellent art direction, character artstyle and voice acting.
-Extensive customization options.

Cons:

-Contrived ending.
-Some modest worldbuilding issues.
-Some technical problems regarding screen modes.
-At 14 hours, the game is seriously overpriced.

All things considered, I liked how the writers and developers handled this project. There are plenty of things to like about this game, like the art direction and the characters, but there is definitely room for improvement, like some storytelling elements and a bit of technical hiccups. But the biggest issue this game has is the pricing. At more or less $30, it offers only 14 hours worth of content. This is the visual novel equivalent of $70 AAA games which take 12 hours to finish. That being said, I can't base my recommendation on pricing alone, because pricing changes over time, and there are external factors at play, like store discounts. So, my verdict, is that, judging the game by its own merits, it's a good recommendation, but I strongly recommend to wait for a discount, so you get more for your money.
