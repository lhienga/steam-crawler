\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt truly breaks my heart to have to give this game a negative review. Neutral would be much more fitting, but alas, no such option exists. The story is genuinely incredible. But I cannot, in good conscience, recommend a game that feels as incomplete and botched as this one does. The reality is, this game is more like a movie, except every couple of minutes you have to play a jank-ass minigame to progress.

The story really is the biggest thing this game has going for it; perfect for those who love those typical mob vibes. Now I must say, the old version's gameplay was downright horrific; janky engine, unintuitive controls, the whole lot. So I think it's safe to say that the Definitive Edition has come leaps and bounds over the original in some respects.

However, I would be lying if I said its gameplay is good by any metric; there are many glaring issues that make it frustrating to play at best. The engine is jank as all hell, the controls have stupid mappings (and changing them makes certain enemies unkillable, ask me how I know), and the god-awful mouse acceleration cannot be turned off and makes shooting a disgustingly bad experience, to name a few. Overall, they did make some progress towards fixing these issues over the original game, but it feels more like a low effort reskin of an old game, rather than a fix of the archaic museum piece that Mafia is.

Mafia II was a far better game, in my opinion, in almost every respect, and I do think that anyone looking to experience the thrills like Mafia II's again will be disappointed in Mafia: DE. Just about every part of this game is frustrating and immersion breaking; the fistfighting is clunky, the controls don't make any sense, and the enemies don't seem very balanced. Most games will make you tougher to compensate for how many enemies you have to kill. For whatever reason, it feels like Mafia has taken the opposite approach, making you eat sh*t with every bullet you take, while you get nothing but your limp d*ck. It makes it very hard not to die, and this game will make you sit through 20 seconds of watching Tommy flop to the ground, followed by the most braindead loading screen known to man before letting you get back into action. There are a couple missions that beg to reduce their difficulty, because beating them is not a challenge, it's a middle finger to you and your skills.

Now I cannot entirely blame the devs for the way this game feels. It is reminiscent of the original Mafia's gameplay experience, and this presents the dilemma: do they keep some of the original ( highly questionable) design choices to be truthful to the original game, or do they change them to emulate a better execution (like Mafia II's), thus sacrificing the identity of the game, and perhaps even the freshness of experiencing a new playstyle? Me personally, I don't consider the jank to be part of the original charm, so keeping it around is not a plus. But they managed to cut a bunch of what made the older game as immersive as it was, like side quests, the driving missions, the enterable shops and public transport. All of the worldbuilding that was already done for them was just tossed away, and the game feels a lot more barebones because of it.

The graphics and sounds truly are better than the original by miles; this is doubly so the case the cars. But I feel like I'm mostly just spending time comparing this game to its predecessor, which, regrettably, speaks volumes to the execution of this remaster, let alone to its merit as a standalone game. I want to like it, because good mob stories just fill that gap in the heart. But to rework a game as much as they did, and for this to be all that they have to show for it, I am not impressed.\t\t\t\t\t
