Pros
Story
Compared to Mafia 2 & especially Mafia 3, this feels like a proper Mafioso story. Bootleggers trying to get that sweet liquid gold, while doing the occasional illegal act. As well as changing the ending twist for why Tommy & Paulie decide to go against their Don.
Voice acting
Compared to the original, Tommy especially feels vastly improved. No disrespect to the original VA, but in this entry, you can better gauge Tommy's emotions; while in the original, he felt much more one-note.
Shorter Opening Taxi Section
The opening taxi section is much, much shorter. Allowing you to get on with the game.
Being able to skip optional driving
Was nice being able to skip these on my Classic playthough, since I went right into that after finishing up getting all the collectibles on my Hard playthrough.
Gunplay & Cover System
You dive into cover swift and cleanly; as when you aim and fire.
Weapon Variety
Weapon variety isn't too large, yet not too small. Just the right amount that fits the current era.
Driving
I played with my transmissions being automatic, not manual. With that said, the driving felt smooth and responsive. I personally felt little to no difference between regular and simulation options, except the race. There you cannot brake as hard, or else you will spin out.
Movement & Sprint Speed
Sprint feels like an actual sprint, unlike Mafia 2's light jog. You book it when you tell Tommy to!
Collectibles Carry Over Playthroughs
Self-explanatory
Chapter Select & Collectibles
If you missed any collectibles during a mission, you can simply select the chapter, find it and leave.
Ending feels much more realistic
Unlike the original where it felt like some happy ending, this ones feels much more realistic and subtle. You see Tommy's rage at being sent away for 8 years. As well as the other characters being arrested. And they had Tommy go out with dignity; here he knows he's about to be killed and takes it with a smile.

Neutrals
Extremely Simplistic Melee System
The melee system here is even more simplistic than Mafia 2's three button version. Here it's two, with a technical third, if you hold the attack button to do a "power attack"
Reused finishing animations
You will see the same finishing animations quite often.

Cons
Race
Anyone who has heard about Mafia knows about the dreaded race, and it hasn't changed here. It is just as difficult and time consuming as it was in the original. Only real difference is you don't die from a broken neck, if you roll your car over. But you might as well, as if you crash at all, you're going to lose. No ifs, ands, or buts. You have to be close to perfect in order to win the race. My initial Hard playthrough took me 2 hours. While my Classic took half an hour, which I'm going to credit to me immediately going into it after my Hard playthrough. I understand it's to remain faithful to the Original, but come on... There was no reason that it had to remain as hard as it did.
Death Screen Takes 5 Seconds to complete
5 seconds doesn't sound like a lot, but if you're stuck on a section, it can seem like an eternity.
Some story beats feel odd/missing
You'll notice there's no real side stuff brought up. You're never really told when Tommy marries Sarah, have a daughter or get their house, unless you're really paying attention to the conversations that can potentially be missed.
No Backstory on Tommy except at the very end
I understand not everyone cares about character's backstories, but it would have been nice to learn some throughout the story. You instead only really learn about Tommy's past if you read notes in his house in the very last chapter. Notes you can miss, if you speed out of his house and go to the Art Gallery.
No long-term Wanted System
If you shake the police both on foot and in your vehicle, you'll be free to walk/drive right by the same Police Officers who were chasing you seconds ago and nothing will happen. It would have been nice for the team to implement that same Wanted system as they had in Mafia 2. I.E. Remembering your car's plates or what you look like. My main reason for this criticism is after you assassinate Turnbull, you can literally run back to where you make your initial entry into the building. Hide there till the police give up their chase and literally run back out where you came in from, past those officers and end the mission. Seems rather silly.
Fox Collectibles
These feel unbelievably tacked on and an excuse to make the player explore the map. The countryside, especially.
Some weapons feel underpowered
Tommy Gun takes a quarter to half a magazine to kill via bodyshots.
Magnum (Yes, a MAGNUM REVOLVER of all guns) takes 3 bodyshots to kill.
Tommy Gun does not sound tough enough
One of the most iconic weapons of American history and it sounds like a peashooter.
Joe looks terrible in the ending cutscene
This is a nitpick, I know. But c'mon... Tell me he doesn't look awful compared to his original design...

Conclusion
Despite my large list of cons, it's still a good game that I recommend. Everything is vastly improved, compared to the original release. Only warning is to be ready to edure a hell that is Chapter 4/The Race. Once you get past that, it is smooth sailing.\t\t\t\t\t
