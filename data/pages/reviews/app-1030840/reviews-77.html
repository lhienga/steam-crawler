\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was just starting high school when Mafia first came out. The game was great, but incredibly hard at the same time. I remember trying to pass the race mission for like a million times before finally calling it quits. With this remake, I got another shot at seeing the whole story of Mafia.
The mentioned story takes place during the time of prohibition (1930s) in the city of Lost Haven, somewhere in America. You are Thomas Angelo, a regular cab driver with Italian roots. Finding yourself in the right place at the wrong time, you meet Paulie and Sam, a couple of gangsters who drag you into the criminal underworld.
There are two things this remake brings to the table: difficulty levels and improved graphics. I naively started the game at 'classic' difficulty, but was forced to tune it down when I got to the race mission (after I spent a couple of hours trying to pass it... it was even harder than I remember). The improved graphics are nice, but could have been nicer. The rest of the game is basically the same as in 2002 when it first came out. Be warned though, what was good in 2002 is pretty terrible today. Glitchy controls, retarded AI and rows upon rows of enemies you'll be showered with - that's what you should expect. Luckily the game has incredible story, great voice-acting and quality cut-scenes, otherwise it would be completely unworthy of playing. It's also pretty short and linear (which was a common thing 20 years ago), with almost no side-content beside main missions.

This remake could have been great. Instead, it feels like it was made only to make a quick buck with a little face-lift on a 20-year old game. I would recommend buying Mafia: Definitive Edition only if you like gangster movies and only if you find it on sale.

My score: 6/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most short but impactful single player games I have ever played, hands down. The first time I saw this game, I wishlisted it and waited till it was on sale. When I bought it, I never really expected much; but the beginning, it immediately hooked me. The setting felt so damn realistic, along with the small details such as your car radio losing signal under a bridge, really intrigued me. The most important part, though, was 100% the story. Told a classic noir story about a man who goes to many troubles in order to provide himself and his family a better life. The ending was quite tragic, but very meaningful, spreading the important message that family is your greatest weakness but also your greatest strength. This very well could end up being a gaming experience that I will never forget. Amazing storytelling, amazing gameplay, amazing setting. My only real gripe with this game is the slight performance issues at times, but they weren't a big nuisance nor gamebreaking.

9/10 would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
