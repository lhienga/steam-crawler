Gonna do some gatekeeping here, because i think it is worth it.

This game must be played on Classic mode, which refers to original Mafa 1's difficulty. (That game is still difficult than this tho. I remember dying a lot in that game but this one, except chapter 5 *the race*, it was a pretty smooth experience, not that hard.)

Playing on Classic difficulty, means that you must not take any bullet, more than 2-3 anyway. Because you will die obviously. I think this should be intended difficulty setting because, it works better with the story too. Adds another layer to this adult story. It establishes a coherence between story and gameplay and makes narrative more well-seated. Like, this game has reminiscence of movies like The Godfather and Once Upon a Time in America, it is even like their videogame version. Its more of a dram than action-adventure. So, killing every enemies without thinking and running into them like in Mafia 3 in a dram story wouldn't make the narrative receivable.

But in Classic mode, you must take cover, wait for enemies move, observe them, wait for their ammos run out and manage your ammo carefully; these put you into more dangerous situations which is more accurate to a mob's life. Your life is at stake for the job and it is difficult to protect it when bullets are flying in the air. Enemies help to establish this coherence too. When they got shot, they dont get out from their cover and just shoot without teasing themselves. So you must make another move to make them get out or find another angle to see them.

Like FromSoft games, they too make really hard bosses to beat because they want to tell us that they are real deal. They are legends. They wouldnt be like this if you could kill all of them easily. You would have a feeling like ''Huh, this is the Nameless King? Looks more like Fameless to me.'' or smth like that.

Classic mode is not unjust or frustrating in this meaning. You just have to be a little more patient and hesitant. That's it. You wait for the opening, for the right time, you see the opportunity, you shoot and kill. More important, you survive another day. It is smoother experience than the original one.

Classic mode makes you say that ''wow, this is serious, being a gangster is hard''. Reminds you this is not a pure action game. And i loved that. So wanted to appreciate that. I wish they hadn't ruined the rest of the Mafia games.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMany gangster games lean into the romanticized or over-the-top nature of the criminal underworld. Mafia: Definitive Edition takes a different path, telling the much more grounded story of Tommy Angelo. His journey through the NYC stand-in of Lost Heaven as a member of the fictional Salieri crime family is compelling, emotional, and filled with interesting characters. As I progressed through the game, I found myself increasingly more invested in Tommy and the people around him. Without this investment, I don’t think I would have finished the game as the combat is mediocre and really served as filler for me between the story beats. There are a few memorable missions (a high speed chase in a race car sticks out), but many are just cover-based shootouts that grow tiresome quickly. With no sort of character progression (or any progression really outside of the story), there is no hook pulling you forward aside from seeing what happens to Tommy. That being said, if you find yourself interested in what happens to this made man, then you’ll find Mafia: Definitive Edition a fun romp through the 1930s Italian mafia.\t\t\t\t\t
