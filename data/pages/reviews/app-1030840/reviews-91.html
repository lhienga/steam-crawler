\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game looks great, the voice acting is good and the story is pretty decent with a satisfying conclusion. The period setting feels alive and is great to be in. The gameplay is pretty basic and the game is over fairly quickly. I would recommend you get it on sale with deep discount - I picked it up with Mafia 2 & 3 definitive editions for a low price.

The gameplay is a little stale but generally fine. The driving doesn't feel too good but it was tolerable. The gunfighting is rather basic although generally clicky and enjoyable enough. Although I didn't love it, it kind of serves its purpose as an engine for the story.

I didn't play the original Mafia, judging from other reviews the scale was a bit grander and there was more in the way of open world gameplay and side quests. Playing this version, it does feel a bit hollowed out and I did get the sense that something was missing.

It plays fine on Linux with Proton-GE. I didn't even need a workaround for the launcher.

Finally, just a comment on the racing level as I see a lot of people are moaning about it. I thought it was stupid and out of place, but it isn't that hard or that bad. It took a couple of goes but overall it didn't ruin the game or leave a sour taste. Don't let it put you off.\t\t\t\t\t
