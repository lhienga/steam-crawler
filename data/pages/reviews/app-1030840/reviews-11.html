\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMafia was one of my favourite games back on the original Xbox and back in 2020 i was pleased to see they would be remaking the game i beat it back then on a PS4 pro.

fast forward to 2024 and i've just beat it again but on PC which was a much better experience from a 4k maxed out perspective just from the extra frames alone that pc gave me made it much better to play.

so the game itself follows a normal guy called Tommy who is a taxi driver who happens to be in the right place or wrong (you be the judge) to help two gangsters called Sam and Paulie who are part of Don Salieri family who is impressed with your work in helping them.

so thus begins your life into small and big crimes with a really amazing story unfolding in each chapter i don't want to spoil anything as i believe it should be played with fresh eyes.

Gameplay is your typical third person gameplay with ok driving mechanics the shooting is fine for the most part it can feel a little floaty and lacklustre while shooting you can also use cover but again its a little meh. the city of empire bay has never looked so beautiful but sadly its all smoke and mirrors as the game has nothing to offer in terms of things to do in the city or any side quests which i feel they could of added if they wanted to.

overall for the story alone i would recommend this game and would score it 8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story is the star of Mafia and I think my only disappointment with it is how quickly it plays out - though I blame that on being spoiled by larger RPG games with massive hours of storytelling. In comparison Mafia gave me a very Red Dead Remption 2 type feel. You fall in love with the characters but as the story progresses the more foreboding it becomes. It's extremely reminiscient of the Godfather so if you like those movies (particularly the first one) you'll like this game and its story.

It was also interesting seeing how much technology changes from the 1920s and then on, as well as the structure of the buildings and the newspapers and radio broadcasts do a great job of capturing what it was like during that time of American history. Just the evolution of the automobile was interesting to see as year by year they became less antique looking and more modern day, as well as the driving mechanics.

As for actual gameplay, again the game and its missions are over pretty quickly. There is no real customization features and you don't get a lot of weapons to choose from. The cover system is okay but I found it more useful to abandon the cover system and just peek around corners with my gun when engaging in combat.

The game does have a free mode that lets you roam around in any car and use any gun so maybe I'll give that a try but I don't see a lot of replay value with this game. So for that I'd wait for it to go on sale and look at some reviews for its sequels before purchasing.

The gameplay does a lot of things well but it doesn't really do anything that stands out. It actually left me feeling nostalgic for games like GTA, Red Dead, and stealthy games like Metal Gear and Alpha Protocol. Games that give you more options on how to complete a mission.

Grab it if it's on sale and play it on a normal or easy difficulty. The story is what stands out and its worth playing it for that alone.\t\t\t\t\t
