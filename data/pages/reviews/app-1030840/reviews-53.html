\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you were expecting a GTA5 set in 1930s prohibition, I would pass this one. It just doesn't have the polish, and the writing is all over the place.

The atrocious TAA that causes smearing and blur cannot be turned off, even with mods and forcing the setting through NVIDIA's control panel.

The driving physics is atrocious. While setting the driving physics to "Simulation" in-game makes things a little tolerable, it puts you at a disadvantage in the racing and chasing parts.

There are forced stealth sections that should honestly have been cut down on this remake. This isn't 2004 anymore. Hangar 21 had an opportunity to create a modern experience that takes the best parts of the OG mafia and trims the clunky parts but... honestly I think they did the opposite.

The checkpoint system places you at the BEGINNING of the chase or stealth section when you fail them. Quite infuriating when you fail at the end.

With GTA5, the mission designs are flawed, yes. But there is downtime in-between missions where you drive around the city as a breather. In this game, you are thrown into one mission after the other. There is no time to wander and appreciate the city they've built. No freeroam (I know there is one in the menu) as part of the storyline. To do that you'll have to select it in the main menu.

[SPOILERS BELOW]

The story is the most egregious part. It starts off slow. Builds up to a betrayal you can see coming a mile away. The GTAs do this, and Mafia is no exception.

It's the abruptness and missing story beats that sour the whole twist. You find the supposed "diamonds" were narcotics, and the Don has been keeping the truth from your crew.

The "diamonds" serve as cover for cigars, but Sam was the one briefed first by the Don? At this point Tommy is the go-to guy for the Don after having taken care of the most important events in the game. YOU get Frank's ledger, YOU kill the other mob boss, YOU win the garbage race. The Don trusts you the most, enough to disclose the existence of a rat in the family (that turns out to be Frank), yet he keeps the cigar-dope job under your nose? Doesn't make narrative sense.

Ludonarrative dissonance is a word thrown around by video essayists, but it absolutely applies here. Tommy at multiple points shows he doesn't want innocent bystanders killed. He even makes certain jobs harder because of that. Yet in-game, he mows down hundreds of cops and goons who could've been just like him. Who's to say the enemies at the other end of the barrel isn't some heart-of-gold taxi driver down on his luck scooped up by the mafioso so they can pay their bills?

Sam is never shown to be the guy willing to throw his crewmates under the bus to get a leg-up in the mafioso career ladder. Instead we see him looking out for the other two in the Trio. Yet all of a sudden he caps his best friend, and tries to plug Tommy so Don looks at him kindly as the Godfather's favorite pet? What??

How did he even find out about the bank job? Yes he heard about it in the car but how did he know to go straight to the apartment afterwards? Why didn't Tommy split up right then and there? Why would you stay in town longer than necessary after poking at the hive's nest?

Why isn't there a lead-up to the bank job? It's supposed to be thing big end-of-all-heists that's supposed to get them out of their ♥♥♥♥♥♥ lives as low-level grunts, but we don't see any planning. There are no missions that lay out the blueprints of the building, what you'll do, and how you'll get out of there.

It's just "hey lets rob a bank" and you are there at the next cutscene. Tommy does all this for his kids that we don't see until the final cutscene.

Tommy makes a deal with Irish to get back at the Don who betrayed him. The actual arrest and jail-time isn't a mission, but a montage of cutscenes. He gets old, we see his kid(s), now multiple (i thought it was just a "baby girl", and he gets popped at the end by the VERY DON HE PUT IN JAIL.

This one we ALSO see coming because Frank got capped at a dog race, but the Don was NOT in prison at the time. It's just a forced way to say "you can't escape the mafia life bro".

All of this ends with Vin Diesel--sorry, TOMMY--saying some crap about "family". Like the entire game was leading up to a Fast and Furious line. Very disappointing and not even worth the money at a sale price.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm serious, the racing mission is hot garbage. A story driven game should not be this challenging. The way the car handles is like you're driving on ice. It's ridiculously hard to keep control of the car while simultaneously dodging other racers from pit maneuvering you. I strongly suggest downloading a mod for the race. It's what I had to do to keep my sanity.

I am glad I got this game on sale. It is not worth a full price tag, especially since I had to mod the game just so I could progress the story.

If there was an option between "Yes" and "No" for the recommendation aspect, I would choose that. It's a great story driven game and I love that, but again the racing mission is atrocious and I choose to flag it as not recommended in hopes to catch the eyes of would be purchasers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMafia holds a special place in my heart, when everyone else was going nuts over Grand Theft Auto 3 I was exploring Lost Heaven. So I was excited to see this remake. Sadly, it does not live up to expectations. Despite claiming that they added content they have actually produced a game with much less content than the original. The difficulty is way down. The open world aspects are basically gone. Your hand is held through the entire thing.

While the action scenes are still true to the original, and challenging, the heart and soul of the game is gone. You can no longer approach missions as you see fit. If the game wants you to use stealth then you're going to, or the mission will fail. If you want to approach the target from an abnormal area, you can't, you've been plopped down in a location with one way out, through the enemy directly. They made other weird changes like the getaway plane actually being a plane instead of a blimp like in the original.

They added a cutscene where Sarah says the politician you assassinate championed the 19th amendment. Even though this games takes place in the 30's and the 19th amendment was passed in 1968. So not only did they add a historical inaccuracy to a game that was once heralded for its historical accuracy but a game that's already heavy in exposition now has way more exposition. As far as I can tell that's the only content they actually added, cut scenes. There is significantly less game play in this version.

Which brings me to my last complaint. All the car missions are gone, along with the need to learn to pick locks. These were multi-part missions that felt like at least half the game. Just GONE. They did give a nod to these missions through post cards, but they aren't integrated into the story, the cut scenes and dialogue is gone, and instead of entire missions it's just a scavenger hunt.

The raised rail system isn't usable anymore either. In the original game, after you take the train in the story, you could take the train to get around town as much as you wanted. There was also a mission involving the train platform, that's totally gone. In this game you can't take the train, ever.

I expected a LOT more. You can't access the airport or race track in free mode, which I expected in the remake. They REMOVED tons of missions, which I never expected in the remake, and it makes it bad. They added historically inaccurate feminist non-sense, albeit in one cut scene, still ruins it.

Game doesn't even look much better, and has massively higher performance requirements.

If you want to experience Mafia the way we all fell in love with it, ignore this version. The things that make this game bad are the same things that make Mafia 2 and 3 bad, no depth, no immersion, no heart, no soul. Just go to gog.com and get the original version. Mod it into 1080p, mod the original music back into it, play that.

This version is bad and the developers should feel bad.\t\t\t\t\t
