\r\n\t\t\t\t\t 10 Hours of story gameplay only

Graphics: 8/10
Audio: 7/10
Story: 6/10
Gameplay: 5/10
AI: 4/10

> Mafia: Definitive Edition is not a bad game. It is a mediocre game with a mediocre story. The first half of the story is building up our character and the environment pretty well, but after the half of the game you can sense a feeling of rush, and therefore some plot elements makes no sense. But even with all it's failures, the story is decent, and you can pretty sure have fun with it. But I concur that after you beat the game for the first time, most people will not find go back to play it again. The game has no reason to make you play it twice.

> The gamepley was clunky, especially when we travel on foot or shoot with our weapon. The driving is not bad, but the AI could have been better. Apparently they were designed in a certain way and don't notice the player's car at all. The shooting isn't satisfactory either. Even though the gun sounds were decent, it didn't make me feel like I am shooting with a weapon.

> One of the other clunky part of the game was unfortunately the FPS. I played with a solid 90 FPS with ultra settings but the clunkiness of the game made me feel like I was playing with 40 FPS. Your movement feels slow, especially the camera speed and angles. Something definitely feels wrong.

> The voice acting: Our main character's voice acting feels so unnatural and forced. I know that voice acting is not an easy job, I have also had voice acting lessons but this is still not an excuse in my eyes. Every other character's voice felt natural, unless when Don Salieri speaks Italian; it felt very weird but other than that I have nothing bad to say about other voice actors. It is only that our protagonist that I have a problem with.

> The music: The radio on cars were a good touch of the developers, but I would have wished to see a variety for music, especially in action scenes. To be honest, that one action music that plays in every mission and in every scene, whether goofy, sad, or happy or not, I am really bored with it. The last mission of the game should be dramatic, it should have evoked certain emotions in you, but instead we had this same, goofy action music playing on the background, and that's why I didn't feel the emotions that the developers tried to surface inside of me. I wished it could have been better, because that music part was really annoying.

> One thing that also bothered me was the feeling of "finishing the game as quickly as possible and move on". Yes, that was a real issue for me. I can't talk about how other players felt but while I was playing the game I was able to barely tolerate two missions in one day. I just didn't want to play more than two missions because I have had already better games that awaited me around the corner. The game didn't make itself a priority for me like how it would make with other certain games. It turned into a chore for me to finish it, because let's be honest, I didn't want to keep a 50 GB, 10 hours worth video game for what I believed to be mediocre. I am so glad it is finished finally, but that doesn't mean I hate the game and I can't recommend other people when it is on sale.

Conclusion: If you want to buy a Mafia game, you should definitely buy this one on sale. It is not a bad game. You can see that people behind this project tried their best. And I can say that they have managed to do a decent job, but don't expect a mind-blown experience. It will be a one-shot thing that maybe you will play it once in a year just for memories and action.\t\t\t\t\t
