\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, the game is solid and adequately makes you feel like a Mafia badass. But still there are a lot of areas where it falls short. At the end of the day, this is just a game from 2002 with a fresh coat of paint. And that coat of paint is very very good don't get me wrong (see GTA definitive editions for an example of the same idea executed horribly), but the core gameplay feels very dated to me. My biggest gripe here is with gunplay. It's just so unsatisfying. You'll shoot an enemy anywhere but the head and he won't even react to it. Feels like my guy is firing an airsoft gun not a deadly firearm. I was willing to excuse dated level design on account of the game being 20 years old, but the gunplay and clunky movement should have been fixed in the remaster.

At the end of the day it was fun, but I get the feeling of a lot of missed potential. The open world is so cool and interesting to look at from afar, but there's really not much to it, it's just a backdrop. The story is great and well written, but the gameplay doesn't really excite that much. I'd recommend if it's on sale but otherwise I think you'd be better off just watching The Godfather or Goodfellas.\t\t\t\t\t
