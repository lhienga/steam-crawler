Genuinely great game.

Honestly, as a cynical pessimistic dude in my day to day real life, this game actually was absolutely wonderful and I don't have much bad to say about it. I don't see it having much replay value unless you're looking to replay the story at a high difficulty as a challenge to yourself. Otherwise, this game is essentially a brilliant story-focused game with some basic shoot-behind-cover style combat. The story and character personalities are very, very high quality. The driving parts take up about 1/3rd of the game so be prepared for that, but luckily the driving is quite satisfying and the controls aren't too clunky. You will need spend a short amount of time perfecting your ability to brake because the game will be quite unforgiving if you crash or slow down because you hit something during a chase or are evading the cops.

The combat in the game mainly focuses on your sensitive health bar that doesn't regenerate, unless you're on the last sliver of health in which case the game will regenerate you a little bit to make your health bar actually visible. This means you'll be using walls, tables and other environment pieces as your cover while trying to scope out which enemies are where using your 3rd person POV and lining up good shots. I highly suggest going for well-aimed head shots because body shots will take up about 4 to 5 times to ammo (at least this was the case for me playing on medium difficulty, and I suspect the 2 harder difficulties will be even harder). Also, yes, there is a way to heal through medical supplies in levels but some levels are harder than others and healing won't be as available so I highly suggest getting good at the combat system and getting head shots.

This game plays out in back to back missions and cut scenes. I think I remember its about 30 missions in total, give or take a few. Mostly every mission will resume the plot line from the previous mission to move the story forward by starting with a cut scene and then you hit the road in a car to a location to "take care of business" for your mafia family. 95% of these cut scenes are fascinating to watch and don't seem too cheesy or feel like the dialogue is forced.

A great part about this game is how the main character develops from street urchin taxi driver to cold-killing mafia gangster, and you get to watch as it unfolds. Even the loading screen on level load-ups and if or when you die is a model of him in his mafia clothing, and the model changes and gets more elegant, sophisticated and bad ass looking as the story progresses. The main character has moments of pride, moments of regret, moments of mercy, and moments of heartlessness. Its fair to say this game does show how cruel and unforgiving the underworld of society can be to it's members. One decent slip up, and you're usually cut from the gang, usually through being executed by your own boss or your own gang friends being sent by your boss to end your life.

The downsides are the levels can be odd sometimes with how fast or difficult they can be. There is a driving level only a few levels in that is a racing level which is surprisingly very difficult and quite long as it is on a long race track and you must make 3 laps AND come in 1st place. Other than that level, the only annoyances are usually trying to evade police in police chases, which are hit or miss because sometimes you'll just get good random traffic and lose cops very quickly, and other times you'll be driving circles around the same blocks to try and lose the bastards.

Either way, this game is definitely worth the one time play through as there were very, very few times I thought to myself "this is game crap." I bought this game on sale and if I could rewind back in time, I would still 100% buy this game knowing what I know now, no hesitations.

Driving game-play: 8.1 / 10
Shooting game-play: 8.4 / 10
Plot / Story / Cut scenes: 9.3 / 10

P.S.
If you want to make the most of this game, I suggest going straight to the hard difficulty if you are used to 3rd person shooting games, because the only thing I wish I knew before I started playing this for the first time is I should have picked the hard difficulty instead of medium. The reason for this is that there is a good challenge in medium difficulty but I think hard difficulty would've really hit that sweet spot of working hard for the ending, while medium difficulty there were times I breezed through levels once I got the hang of the run-and-cover shooting style of the combat system. Once you beat the incredible story for this game the first time, it loses a lot of it's replay-ability because the suspenseful, twist-and-turn story is now fully known. I'm positive the hard difficulty is going to be a major pain in the ass but knowing what I know now, I think I could've squeezed out double the playtime for the story if I really challenged myself with the harder mode.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA mediocre title. I'm giving it a negative review because with all the other games out there to play, spending time on a mediocre title just doesn't make sense. But if you're a big Mafia fan, maybe it'd be worth playing on sale.

Positives:
- Good dialogue and characters
- Manual transmission setting makes driving more interesting, but more difficult

Negatives:
- Graphics look really bad with the TAA vaseline smear and motion ghosting. Playing at 4k mitigates this somewhat. Dark environments are less noticeable. It's the worst I've seen.
- The gameplay is just... meh. Gun mechanics are very simple, very few weapons and never enough ammo.
- The AI is the stupid-but-tough kind. Wonky pathing, but will turn you into mincemeat pretty quick with their inhuman aim and high damage. I'll have to cower behind cover, still taking damage, while my friendly NPCs run around soaking up bullets. Enemies will snipe you with perfect molotov throws pretty regularly, even in scenarios where they shouldn't know your exact location. This kind of thing I find frustrating.
- Missions are balanced poorly. Many missions are like walking out of a building with max wanted level in GTA, plus all the cars nearby are locked and need to be broken into while you're swarmed with enemies. You have to die repeatedly before you get lucky by fleeing in the route where the game chose not to spawn enemies and roadblocks by chance. You'll do everything right and still have to retry constantly.

Overall, a mediocre game with a good narrative but frustrating mechanics and uninteresting gameplay. I found Mafia II to be far more enjoyable.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a great surprise this game was. As an avid fan of the original Mafia, which I still think is one of the best games ever made, I didn't have my expectations very high. But it seems that this remake has approached the original material with respect and thoughtfulness, improving the more tedious aspect of the old game and refraining from adding too many new elements.

The story remains largely the same, however the characters somehow seem more human. The map remains largely the same as well, which was a very welcome surprise. All the familiar locations are still present, but the city also seems more human, more lively and authentic.

In regards to the gameplay, you obviously DO HAVE TO play it on the CLASSIC difficulty, no silly aimlocks and other helpers. The shooting mechanic is enjoyable and a large step forward in comparison with the old game. I was happy to find that the equally loved & hated episode "Fair Play" is still as difficult as in the original, and a bunch of the missions are quite complicated as well, given you opt for the hardest difficulty (as you should).

But even at the hardest difficulty it's just a short game, which unfortunately is a detriment. Absence of "Free Ride Extreme" then doesn't help as you don't have anything to do after you finish the main campaign (and the unskippable end credits which seem to be about 50% of the game length).

There are of course cons as well. The car physics model is not as fun as in the original game and all cars feel mostly the same. The motorcycle seems like an afterthought and it drives absolutely awful so probably should've stayed in the theoretical.

But more importantly than that the game suffers from bad optimization so it's not completely smooth which then detracts from the immersion and enjoyment of the gameplay.

Overall an awesome homage to the classic and well recommended game.\t\t\t\t\t
