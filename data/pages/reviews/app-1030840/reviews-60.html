\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI pre-ordered this game as soon as the pre order was available and I.. have some things to say.


Gameplay

Absolutely astonishing. I love that they kept the feeling that the first game had in its design of the entire map and its missions that it offers. But it may be the definitive edition of a game that came out in 2002, but it didn't really be that game in a way. Mafia's gameplay was usually that you needed to avoid getting injured usually. The driving in the first game absolutely sucked, but it kinda felt rewarding in a weird way. Health didnt regenerate like we have it now. If you were low at a checkpoint then oh well, thats all the hp you're getting for the entire mission or unless you find a way of healing. Bullets felt lethal, ammo felt needed, different ways to do a mission were impressive. They added some of the feeling back into their classic mode in the definitive edition but it just really didnt feel the same. With that aside, the game really does feel polished, its a fun experience to go through having all of these intense scenes. I love how they remade the missions to feel a bit more enjoyable for the user. Introducing the player to driving in the new running man mission is an amazing way to show off the driving portion of the game. Although, im sure the animations dont feel like Tommy, right? Thats because the entire game runs on the Mafia 3 engine. Usually I dont care about this detail, but the original made Tommy look like someone whos in the mob, not the damn military. The animations for Tommy's actions in combat are really similar if not the same to Lincoln Clay in Mafia 3. Driving do feel smooth tho. Especially the ram feature. The ram feature allows your to make kinda tighter turns while speeding up the car you're driving for a really short time frame. This is really useful in Fairplay mostly. The guns didnt really feel that bad when it came to damage in the remake. The original game had very lethal shotguns that either blow you away or just kill you in one shot. The saw-off shotgun being a prime example of this. It fires 2 shots really quickly but then the reload takes a bit. The pump shotgun fires at a more steady rate but the reload time isnt really as bad on it. But wheres the lethal part in all of this? It's if the enemy has it. There exists a bug in the game where a shotgun can rapid fire, basically allowing a free kill on the main player. NPC's have health in the original where in the remake, they can indeed be shot at by enemies but they dont have health. They have an infinite amount of health since you cant hurt your allies in the remake, but in the original you could actually kill your allies. Of course if you did, the mission would fail usually. But whats interesting is that they fight back if you fight them or if you shoot a certain amount of times near them during the general start of missions where you start off in Saliari's bar most of the time. Ammo didnt carry over clips. Any bullet you didnt use in the clip you were using and you reload, the bullets just gone. For example if you're shooting a pistol and you have 2 bullets left, you reload. The 2 bullets wont carry over to the next clip, instead you have a different clip with the max amount of bullets that can be held within a clip. This detail was really similar to the real world where bullets dont magically transfer over to the next clip.

Story

Mafia's story has always been simple, but the definitive edition made the characters feel more important. Especially Sarah. Sarah was Tommy's love interest in both games, however the original game only had mention of her through dialogue after her main mission. The definitive edition made her feel as a character. They gave her a more important role aswell as including her in some cutscenes and spare dialogue in future plus missions before her main one. Still prefer the old ending cutscene with sam at the art gallery. The message was more meaningful instead of giving us the whole "we had good times Tom, but eventually we all die.". I did love that they made sam's death a bit more satisfying in the remake. Story is just generally explained better in the remake than the original.

Free Ride

Free ride mode in the definitive edition feels... Limited????? Its a weird way to describe it but to be honest you only have a handful of easter eggs, Lucas Bertone missions, and the exploration of the map. The original had a save feature where you had to interact with saliari's bar in order to save your game. You could chose what car you wanted to start with, what part of town you wanted to spawn at, the conditions of npc behavior, and even different varients of the map. You could kill gangsters that can be seen on the street or just driving a car. You get a chunk of money for just killing one of them, but usually they're pretty lethal with their weapon choices.

Summary

I overall enjoy the definitive edition of a game I got to play when I was little. It brings so many memories of the good days in Mafia. No doubt, the game has its flaws. But with all of the improvements, the minor details arent that major. Overall I'd rate the game a 8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs it stands, I would never recommend this game to anyone for one simple reason. That being [Fairplay], the fifth mission in the game. Up until this you've gotten into 1 shootout, about 3 get-away drives, and 1 vehicle combat. That's all the skill they've been requiring of you.

[Fairplay] is a insane difficulty spike that requires you to win a race with the most perfect of precision while driving a car that handles about as well as Bambi on ice. The slightest bump causes you to spin-out, sand causes you to spin-out, bumps launch you into the air and flips you, bumping into another car can lock your wheels with theirs and cause you to spin-out. All this while the enemy racers drive with perfect skill and no mistakes. Add on that the other racers AI seems somewhat random and they're always a smidgen faster than you. All this together and you have a high precision race where if you aren't in First Place after the first turn you might as well reset.

Simply put I'm amazed this game was even able to be released with how bad this mission is. I was genuinely enjoying my self the first four missions, and I hear the rest of the game is great. I just refuse to spend hours and hundreds of attempts just to reach it. With that time I could enjoy other good games that don't spit in the players face for wanting to have fun.\t\t\t\t\t
