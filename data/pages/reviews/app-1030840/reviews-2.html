In this game the main protagonist wants to be in the mob and seems to enjoy it, while in the original game he was forced by events to join the mob and was never that much into it. I like the Tommy from the first game, in this remake I am ambivalent towards the main character at best, just like with Vito in Mafia 2. These are not great characters. The reason why everybody is sad about what happens to Tommy in the first game is because he was a moral person with a lot on internal monologues. This Tommy has none of that. And nowhere is it more obvious than in the Hotel missions where in the original he has an internal monologue, while in the second one it's not existent since they changed the mission.

Another complaint is Sarah. In the first one she was a quiet, more subtle presence, in this one she's been beaten with the feminist stick, where she's all "empowered". Makes her less enjoyable than the original.

Maybe I am biased for the original, but the characters in the remake have less personality than in the original.

Now, for the stinky parts, not related to the story.

The game is very "Hollywood"-ized. It's very flashy and in your face. The dialogues contain a lot of "♥♥♥♥", "sheet" and all that. That's not offensive, it's just cheap. The original kept the swearing to a minimum, which is more in tone with the times. People didn't randomly swear all the time. It's not that they are swearing, it's that they are swearing so much it is unrealistic. Just like Mafia 2, check out the Youtube compilation to see how many times they say "♥♥♥♥" in Mafia 2. It's funny, but like a fart joke, not like a good joke.

The campaign is alright, I see they changed some of the details and the missions are slightly different. The race is just as hard as it was on "Classic difficulty" and I struggled for 2 days to get it done. On Easy is so simple I did it in the first try on a second replay.

The map is fine, the country side is huge, but the fact that you can no longer interact with the the subway is just bad game design. In the first one you could ride the subway. I don't remember if you could ride the trams tho. Neither in this one. A shame and a missed opportunity. The city detail is good, it looks like a real place. That is fine.

The cars are okay and feel mostly fine. The slower ones seem better, but anything with any power is terrible because they constantly skid and drift. This was not an issue in the original. The cars were always slow and terrible to drive, but that was fine and it felt like a car had weight and wasn't just a GTA V mockery of a car, where every car feels made out of polystyrene.

The music is alright, but they left out the most iconic song out of all Mafia: "Django Reinhardt - Belleville". That song is so iconic that even my wife knows that when she hears that song I'm playing Mafia, she even hums to the song. Why they skipped this one, I don't know, but just seems like a mistake. Some other songs are missing, like "The Mills Brothers - Chinatown, My Chinatown" and a few others, but that's a minor complaint.

Now, the worst part, the achievements.

The Classic difficulty trophy makes you work for it. It is HARD and I mean HARD to do. This achievement is not the worst tho and I don't really dislike it.

However, the side missions are really bad.
First of all, the challenges suffer from that classic issue, lack of imagination and originality replaced by TIMED CHALLENGES. This is the cheapest way to make something challenging and it is lazy. Everything is timed. And you gotta do stuff precisely or you don't get to do the achievements.

Example 1:
Smith car mission - timer is a cheap way to make a mission, but the car bugs out. You gotta refuel frequently but when it is time to start the car again, 50% of the time the car does not move. Nice design, developers, a real stinker mission, I tried for 2 days to get it done.

Example 2:
Bike mission - bike handles like garbage. It is the worst vehicle in the series. Uncontrollable pile of sheet that you cannot control and have to do a jump with. I was so frustrated with this one. And of course, timed, because why not?

Example 3:
Alien mission - the car respawned as a taxi. Yep, I was driving something else, then failed, because the whole missing is so bad you can only beat it by memorizing where the ufo spawns and gradually progress every time. But I started the mission with another car, failed and on one of the attempts I got respawned with a taxi instead of the car I started. Bugs like these are so annoying because they make you waste time going back to get your car and travel to the mission again.

Other bugs I encountered were visual, like on the main street, next to the Bertone's garage there is a hole missing but the steam is coming out of the ground in the middle of the street. I mean, come on guys, it's minor but it is so obvious, where is the QA team to check this stuff? You can find it by driving on the main street next to the water and going south from the big bridge, it's in the middle of the road, between the lanes. It's a small bug, but it is so obvious, I wonder if the QA team really earned their money. I say this as someone who has done QA in the past for games. This would not fly past the teams I worked with.

Anyways, I rambled enough, do I recommend the game? I'd say yes, for casuals it is better and easier than the original. The original has the "hard" difficulty of the 2000's, while newer games have that "Call Of Duty" difficulty, which make it easier for the unskilled to play. However, the first one is better due to the story, but is lacking in graphics and combat system.

I'd say, if you're not good at video games, this is great for you, and you're going to enjoy this one more than the original. If you're a sweaty nerd that likes a good story and are willing to get passed the shortcomings of the era, the original is a better pick. I guarantee that you will like the original story more than the new one.

I give it 6/10 on the hardest difficulty, 8/10 on easy and this is probably what casuals should stick with.\t\t\t\t\t
