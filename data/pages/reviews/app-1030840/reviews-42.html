\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNice criminal period drama, very cinematic. Amazing graphics, Lost Heaven looks so detailed, which adds to immersion greatly. Around half the play time is driving. Driving to the next plot point through the big living city and listening to the radio feels meditative. You think about all that is happening with Tommy and get the feeling that this story won’t end well. The game is not long, around 10 hours, but tells a fully fledged story. Gameplay feels dated but that didn't detract from the game much, overall you play for the story and not shootouts. Characters are great, the voice acting is good, they feel like real people, caught in this bad story. Tommy and the gang do not fish for sympathy, you kinda feel bad for Tommy in the beginning but by the middle of the game you understand, this is what he wants to do, no regrets. Finished it in three days, episodic structure keeps you entertained and always wanting to know what’s next. Recommend.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good examples of a maybe button. This reboot has some pretty visuals, which tend to bug out all the time, and a very nice quality of life skip for unnecessary drives, but misses the mark for miles. Voice acting and delivery of the lines sinks the game hard. They decided to give the story a tiny bit more life but it really just boils down to politically correct horse crap that makes sure to tick all those lovely DEI marks. It's a period piece where the original at least had the stones to touch base on that. Here they made sure to make some certain cuts and additions to ensure no one was offended.

As for game play the bread and butter of the shooting is okay but nothing to really write home about. For some reason they thought taking away the vast arsenal from the original was a good idea. Having access to two guns is kind of realistic I guess but once you jam a tommy gun down your pants it kinda defeats that argument. Guns also sound and feel like peashooters. The orginal had them kicking like mules. The 44 mag felt like you were blasting a hand cannon and knocking enemies on their asses with it felt good. Here it feels like every other peashooter of a gun. Absolutely sad they butchered a classic like that; however, disregarding the original the gun play is okay.

Grab it on sale. Otherwise just play the original. You might have to drive around more in the 1st game but the story with the better VO and gun play blow this reboot out of the park.

Edit: Played through to the end and the story telling just gets more virtue signally and tries desperately to make what was once a badass mobster story into a bs tear jerker. They made Pauli into the biggest ♥♥♥♥♥ on the planet. His new VO certainly does enough justice in that regard but now his character ark is total trash cakes. Every additional scene they made with Sarah just makes me want to pull the plug on my PC they feel so pretentious.

Some of the new set pieces are very poorly designed and are extremely annoying to play. The only thing meaningful this reboot does is the combat, which does have some good moments but lord the guns are so bad. The enemies at least on classic difficulty can soak a few bullets with zero reaction. Only if your up close enough to kiss them with the barrel of a shotgun or after taking a few rounds will they show any real feedback. In the original the enemies actually reacted to getting shot each time, which made the combat feel so much more alive then whatever the hell this reboot tries doing.

Tdrl - Stick with the original or pick this one up on a deep sale.\t\t\t\t\t
