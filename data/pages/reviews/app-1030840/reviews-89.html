\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me preface this review by saying I am a hardcore purist who generally hates remakes and has played the original game this is based on. That clarification out of the way: this is a solid remake I'd recommend to most people over the original (which is really the highest praise I can give a remake). TL;DR it's a solid 8/10 with a great story that has been retold with skill and reverence for the original. Not perfect, but worth the 14 hours I spent.

Now, for those who like more detail, let's get into the weeds.

There's some Eurojank in here, namely the AI is pretty rough when it comes to anything (combat, traffic, heck even just pedestrians crossing the street act weird) and also the movement system isn't as polished as something you'd get in a Rockstar game. But, those flaws aside it's a well done retelling of pretty solid video game plot for the time it was released. You may feel something by the end of this, and while that feeling may be frustration at some of the later segments of the game when the rough edges of the movement system and AI start to show themselves as the challenges ramp up, it's much more likely it will be a feeling of sorrow at the fate of the trio of men at the center of the story's narrative.

And narrative is king here, the gameplay doesn't overstay it's welcome unless you drop into the free ride sandbox mode (which is pretty fun, do recommend checking it out, a good place to practice driving if you're struggling as well). The game it's self is a quick ride, I beat it in about 14 hours according to steam and that's including time messing around in sandbox. The story is what you're paying your ticket for, as are the generally nice visuals. Voice acting is solid as well and specifically I really like Paulie's voice actor who made me care way more about the character than I did in my run of the original (though I do miss Tommy's original voice actor, guy was aces).

Gameplay-wise the shooting is passable, but really what makes this game worth a recommendation over the original is the inclusion of more modern systems such as cover and improved stealth. The original was an ambitious game and would lay the groundwork for titles like GTA:SA that would add stealth systems to the open world crime genre, but suffice to say those clunky old stealth systems that feel like they were made in an afternoon of coding can be rough today. This system isn't the best but is solid enough that I found myself rarely reloading due to botched stealth, probably a bit too forgiving but for a more casual game like this that's just right really.

Driving is phenomenal and you can tell is the part of the engine they spent the most time on (which is funny because then they let you skip driving to and between missions like they were ashamed of it, that poor programmer haha). Specifically I really liked the manual drive and recommend playing that way if you want a little more challenge. Learning what gear does what on what terrain was a cool revelation moment and felt very natural aside from me ALWAYS forgetting how to reverse for a quarter second (I blame decades of GTA). It takes getting used to and the real test was that race but it adds a lot of character to the driving, and is very true to the original experience. Also of note is that not every car has the same handling or gear set-up, which really adds another nice level of detail to the world. For example the slick four door luxury in 2nd gear is gonna be going pretty fast while at the same time getting good handling for hills and turns, meanwhile the clunky work truck in 2nd is gonna struggle to get speed but will handle pretty nicely (and it's 3rd gear will be closer to the luxury's 2nd).

Mission structure is pretty solid. Unless I'm mistaken they removed some stuff from the original and also added a bit, but for the most part it hits the same important beats. Some of the changes felt unneeded, but some of the narrative ones felt like they added a lot.

In the end every remake is going to have issues, you can't recreate a game decades after it's release and end up with the exact same product. We will see if this changes because of modern games being so polished, but I have my doubts any true remake will ever be 1:1. Teams change, times change, and people change. The trick with Remakes is recapturing that original spirit and remaining largely true to the original vision while innovating and changing enough to reinvigorate the experience.

Mafia: Definitive Edition manages to deliver what seems to be an increasingly hard thing these days; A captivating solid remake of the original that stays true to the source while knowing what it wanted to accomplish too.
