\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust like the original, the overarching plot is generic gas. Focusing instead, on running us through the 'best of' bombastic mafioso slice of life. And does so better than ever.

Away with the Sepia filter! Lost Heaven is appropriately grim-dark and heavenly, when it needs to be.
Set pieces feel hyper-detailed and dynamic. Shootouts are always entertaining. Mobsters drop dead in dramatic fashion. Gunplay and movement is quick and weighty at the same time. Give me more arenas and I would shoot more.

Sadly, after finishing the story, the city doesn't feel as lively as it should. Public transit is unavailable. Most areas are blocked off by indestructible fences. Bushes. Aside from extraordinarily goofy missions and a ton of collectables, including outfits and vehicles, there isn't anything to do besides drive around.
Going on a rampage isn't a viable pass-time as it is in.. similar games in the genre. Cops drop chase as soon as you exit your vehicle, provided they aren't directly engaged with, you don't even have to move.
Fast travel is jumping through hoops. - Die or find a Bus Station and return to Salieri's. Then, 'walk' around to find the right envelope and 'go to mission' to take you across town to a phone booth you previously unlocked. Good luck figuring out which one's which.

I really believe they went and bought extra time by telling the boss "Hey, we'll throw in a Mafia 2: Definitive Edition" and gave it no love, fleshing this out instead.\t\t\t\t\t
