\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnime Monopoly/ Mario Party.
OJ is a game that takes Fruitbat Factory/ Orange Juice properties and mashes them up in a blender to give you an insane tornado of cracked out anime girls featuring: A kelptomaniac hamster, several homicidal maniacs, a man obsessed with thighs, a depressed cougar, the same gray haired girl in 3 different flavors, and a bunny man that loves the free market.
OJ is a game that's I've played off and on for almost 10 years and have recently hit 1000 hours on so let me tell you what makes this game special.

The game is relatively simple at first, collect stars, land on home panels, maybe kill a few things along the way and have fun. It's something you and your mates can play for a few hours in the afternoon and have fun with even if everyone only has a basic understanding of how to play.
However, there's a surprising amount of depth under the hood. Deck building, character interactions, risk VS reward assessment, mind games, and opportunistic plays are all things you learn about the more you play.

The game (usually) rewards you for thinking on your feet and making smart use of the cards you get.
At the end of the day, the game is still RNG based and we're all at the mercy of the dice, but that's also part of the fun. Randomness keeps matches from getting stale, some days you'll be rolling 7s and 8s and blazing through everything, and other days you'll be on a 1 and 2 streak.
The large variety of characters, cards, and maps also keeps the game from getting old. No match is ever the same due to how many factors are in play at any given time.

Another one of the main draws is the cast. Each character brings something different to the table and it's easy to pick a favorite. If you like pure insanity in anime form then there's at least one character you'll immediately gravitate towards. You can also customize them with a large variety of cosmetics and mods. The only issue with this is that a large chunk of the playable cast is DLC (It's cheap but still.) You'll be shelling out a couple of bucks anytime you want to add a new face or two to your roster.

Speaking of prices though, the game itself is cheap, and goes on sale quite a lot. If you don't like it, you're not out by a lot.

While it's not what you'll be coming to the game for, there is a downside that I feel needs mentioning. The story mode of the game (while it can be funny) isn't the greatest. You'll be fighting A.I.s that play very erratic. They make questionable decisions and are hyper aggressive, which can be very frustrating on higher difficulties. Playing through it on casual mode will net you more base game characters, but if you want those fancy cosmetic items (most namely the crowns) you'll be suffering through the boosted A.I.
As a key:
Casual: AI has worse rolls overall. You get more characters but nothing more
Normal: AI has mostly untouched rolls. You'll get some fancier items from this such as a red crown anyone can wear.
Original: Normal mode but the AI is more prone to having altered rolls.
Extreme: AI will roll constant 5 and 6s but you'll unlock very rare items as well as anything from Normal or Casual difficulty.

The last point I feel that's worth mentioning is the community. The community, for the most part, is alive and really chill. Most people are more than happy to play a few games and shoot the ♥♥♥♥ with you while y'all play. Some people are even happy to show new people the ropes, myself included. In my experience it's really rare to meet someone that's an actual jerk.

(TL : DR)
Pros: Very Cheap
Tons of Replayabillity
Large variety of characters to choose from
Community is alive and welcoming
Lots of customization, especially after modding support got added to the game
Easy enough to learn.

Cons: DLC Problem, over half the cast is DLC
Some card packs are also DLC
Story mode can be a massive pain, especially on harder difficulties.

Don't Get Me Wrong Its A Great Game... If You Like Mario Party It's Very Similar To That... It Has Easy Controls, Great Voice Acting And Decent Art Style... Pair That With Over 200 Achievements And Trading Cards Plus Bonuses Its A Great All Round Game...

However... I Question The AI In This Game... When I First Played The Game And The First Chapter... It Took 5 Attempts On Normal Difficulties... I Played Each Round As Best As I Could And The AI Just Got Better Rolls On Each Round...

I Know Alot Of You Will Say "Just Get Better At The Game"... But When You Roll Six Lots Of One In One Game, One After Another... The Odds Of That Are Astonomic...

Same Goes For The Battle Squeres... You Have No Way Of Seeing What You Will Roll... You Never See The Dice Roll You Just See Your Final Number... The Computer Will Always Win...

I Don't Know If I've Just Been Unlucky... Take What I Say With A Pinch Of Salt...
