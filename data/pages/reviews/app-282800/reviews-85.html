\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100% Orange Juice is a crossover party game primarily drawing from Orange Juice's shmups and some original characters. While there are references to the events of those games or in jokes, these are not required to enjoy the game

Gameplay:

100% OJ includes 3 primary modes: Normal, Coop, and Bounty Hunt. with some minor variations.

Normal Mode is Mario Party with two major twists: each player contributes their own built deck of cards to a center pile and unique player units with their own RPGish stats and Hyper cards, ie a unique and powerful ability. Instead of comparatively skill based minigames, the main equalizer and X factors in game are combat whether against other players or NPCs ala Mario Party 3's Duel Mode and deckbuilding. The changes extend to how players win. Rather than collect mcguffins on different parts of the board, victory is achieved once a player returns to their home space after reaching certain thresholds of Stars (basically coins) or combat Wins against other players or NPCs. Stars are passively gained over time or on spaces, but are easily stolen by other players in battle or burned for card costs. Wins are slower to gain since a convenient battle isn't always available yet can't be taken away. Furthermore, certain characters' stats or unique Hyper card predispose them towards either playstyle but it is possible to switch win conditions. In summary, though derided as a purely random game where players have little control, that understates how much strategy OJ actually has. Picking a fragile or Hyper dependent character naturally leads to more swingy games and you can always hedge your bets by making smart pathing decisions. You can also bring cards that benefit you and are useless if your opponent gets them like cards that prevent combat. However, since a great deal is still dictated by dice, victory can still be snatched from the jaws of defeat and vice versa despite the best laid plans.

Coop forgoes star collecting entirely for up to 4 players teaming up against a high HP boss. A meta progression of sorts is present via a leveling class system that grants additional abilities and stat bonuses. While a welcome change from the cutthroat backstabbing of the base gameplay, the mode lacks content and fewer characters are usable than one would like.

Bounty Hunt trades Mario Party for something closer to Dokapon Kingdom. Players are tasked with random missions to fetch items and bring them to locations or hunt down monsters to earn Fame. While it is possible to fight other players to gain a bit of Fame, Bounty Hunt tends to focus more on the steady albeit less rewarding fetch quests as a quicker path to victory. This also isn't helped by the slower overall pace of the mode and more moving NPCs.

DLC:

The elephant in the room. Orange Juice DLC generally consists of additional characters with alternative playstyles from the base. The closest to "mandatory" dlc would be the card packs which add new cards for all characters. However, while you can bring these new cards, you have no guarantee of pulling them in a match. In short, DLC is not pay to win although a lot of the 'meta' characters in coop are DLC or bonuses from buying other games. Remaining dlc like voice packs have little to no practical effect on gameplay, although they sometimes unlock some characters in the base cast slightly faster. Basically, beyond the relatively cheap All Stars Bundle, it's better to pick and choose characters you want rather than pick up everything. All characters unlocked events and a weekly free rotation give an opportunity to try them out or earn their achievements.

Content cycle:

100% Orange Juice adds frequent free content and has a set of recurring events with rarer new events. These events introduce additional maps, costumes, pets, and other cosmetic items to earn. The devs have stated they don't want to make these seasonal event items impossible to get, so one can wait for a rerun or buy retired event items using ingame currency. Event quality ranges from fun distraction to second job. The grind can be trivial for vets, but newcomers face a daunting amount of costumes to grind out. Oranges and Tokens are not seasonal exclusive and still can buy many items outside events.

In summary:

Pros:
+ Generally bug free and could run on a graphing calculator
+ More strategic than the dice rolling would imply
+ Events come back consistently and new players don't need a lot of unlocks/DLC to participate. Does not preclude being very time consuming
+ Devs active on discord, helping with bugs or other support issues
+ Constant stream of new characters, boards, and cosmetics, not all of which are DLC. Sadly not many new free characters
+ DLC and base game are often on sale
+ Functional online multiplayer for all modes beating out actual Mario Party. May lag or get stuck occasionally
+ Modding support
+ Poppo always wins

YMMV:
+- Lots of DLC, albeit not pay to win. New characters often swamp lobbies on release
+- Even with smart deckbuilding, good combat stats, and intelligent pathing, RNG can screw you at the worst moment
+- Community is generally chill although most people ingame don't chat much
+- Colossal amounts of unlocks which can be intimidating to newer players
+- Lots of achievements, although some are extremely difficult and/or luck based to get. Many rely on DLC characters
+- Evade based or Hyper dependent characters tend to be extremely swingy, either coasting an entire game or turning it into a noninteractive revive roll simulator
+- Besides dailies and grinding out cosmetics, not much to do in between events unless you really enjoy the core gameplay loop. particularly if you don't want to or are unable to go online
+- Character, card art, and costume quality has risen over time, resulting in mediocre early costumes and underwhelming DLC characters
+- Predominantly anime waifu cast. You can nearly have a full game of nothing but alternative Suguri
+- Some characters only playable through buying other games. Most are generally worse than their base counterpart however.
+- Coop and Bounty Hunt tend to favor specific characters and the balance can be a bit off. Normal mode generally has better variety in viable playstyles albeit some are clearly jokes (never use Castle unless you don't value your sanity)
+- Poppo always wins

Cons:
- Events tend to be grindy and time consuming, although not timegated and not always requiring online play
- Single player campaign difficulty mainly consists of rigged/loaded dice in the CPU's favor as difficulty rises
- Bounty Hunt games drag on for too long and encourage passive playstyles with little to no interaction with other players
- Coop trends toward 'meta' characters and loadouts to end bosses quickly and there isn't enough bosses/mixers for lasting replay value
- CPUs are very dumb. Coop and Bounty Hunt CPUs are worse.
- Timezones and less popular modes can result in few open online lobbies
- Minigames tend to be a long slog and often decide a match rather than act as a skill based counterbalance to general gameplay
- Underwhelming single player experience
- Seagull rolling 7 attack haunts my nightmares
- Poppo always wins

Verdict:
Ever wanted Mario Party with functional online, a cast of 99% anime waifus, less minigames, hats, and the Duel Mode from Mario Party 3? 100% Orange Juice is here to fit precisely into that extremely narrow niche with salt geysers 100% intact. If you're willing to put up with its idiosyncracies and have a high tolerance for some of the most salt inducing losses imaginable, 100% Orange Juice can easily provide hundreds of hours of entertainment with thrilling comebacks from impossible odds, horrific combat fails, and occasional 20D chess plans that leave your stupified opponent with no stars. Or you can lose it all to a teleporting hamster at the last possible moment. Definitely worth a shot if you find any of that compelling especially in bundle or on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
