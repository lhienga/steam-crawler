\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn a bizarre way, the modern "witches-and-mecha gals"-style shoot 'em up games have the most in common with the fighting genre in that they're a celebration of characters. From the tidbits of dialogue you get right before the boss fight in the shmup, or fight before the match begins and the victory quote; once the match is over, you end up having enough to flesh out a whole personality.

Now this is where this game shines. At its core, it's a very streamlined "Mario Party" kind of game, replacing the minute+ long minigames with very brief dice-based combat sequences. So much of the charm of 100% Orange Juice is less about the strategy and playing the odds as it is the silly crap that goes on amongst all these very colorful characters and the crazy swings in the game that come about from the unlikely dice rolls. You don't even need to worry about playing any of the games these characters come from beforehand--it won't take long until you find a character or two you like and before you know it, you'll be seeking out the game they're from.\t\t\t\t\t

Even if you build a deck that is optimised to your character's strengths, all four players' decks get pooled into one shared deck that they all draw from, meaning you may get cards that are situational at best, or detrimental at worst if they're not from your original starting deck.

However, that is all part of OJ's charm, particularly in multiplayer. One minute you can be laughing up screwing over your opponent with a gut-wrenching battle-card play, and the next you fall victim to a long-forgotten trap which scuppers your plans.
