\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOrange Juice is a strange masterpiece taking a board game like structure, adding a deck builder/ card system to act as items, and owning an extremely cutesy anime chibi style to form strategic yet chaotic fun. If you can get over the couple hurdles of understanding everything that goes on in a game and the game's hyper colorful look then it can become an instant favorite. Some notes I'll leave is that you will rarely ever need your keyboard while playing since the mouse does pretty much everything except talk in chat and search for the title of cards when building your 'deck'. To get more/ new cards you have to buy them one by one from a card pack. It is up to chance and there are rarities on cards. Thankfully 'Stars', the main currency in the game and what you use to buy cards, are very easy to obtain, especially when there are events. I recommend going through the singleplayer stuff to get characters and some easy Stars. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe simplest way to describe this game is anime Mario Party. There are plenty of different boards & characters to choose from, and you can choose to play either online or offline. Yes, you can still play this if for whatever reason the owner of SpaceX, Elon Musk, gets bored and decides to destroy every satellite in existence. If you're looking for a skill based game, you're at the wrong place, this is chaos central! Basically, your fun is decided by how the game's feeling. Still interested? Well, you're still reading this, so I'll take that as a yes, or you're just bored. The goal is to either collect "stars" or "wins" to "Norma." Stars are treated as currency, which you find around the board, and wins can be obtained by ruining a friendsh- I mean, killing opponents on the board-- and/or cpu enemies, such as a damn SEAGULL who always seems to roll a 6, almost guaranteeing your demise! Get enough wins or stars, and you can level up! This is called "Norma." The first to Norma all the way (5 normas) gets to win. It's not completely RNG based don't worry, around the map you can find cards to assist you in getting what you want, such as "roll double dice" to sprint away from a certain cat character with +2 attack as her base stat. Yes, the characters do different things other than just looking cute--or menacing--depending on who you're staring at. Certain characters can fight better, others can defend better, some have more health, etc. It's not pay to win, due to balancing and luck, although many characters are DLC. They each get their own song when leveling up, and their own "hyper." The hyper is a card which is designed to be very strong, and it can do a variety of things such as allowing the player to become invincible for a turn, or allowing the player to steal stars from other players, and this should lead you closer to victory~! That is, if the dice obliges. The developers keep this game updated, and they unlock 2 free DLC characters, for all players to use with no restraints for a week. Every week. Once a week is up, the old characters are locked again, but new ones are allowed to be used. I-- uh, just hope they aren't kept in some secret prison when they go away. Buy this game. It's addicting, and fairly priced. Even without DLC, you can enjoy 100% of the suffering-- I mean fun. P.S. THE DICE DID NOT PAY ME TO WRITE THIS, I SWEAR.\t\t\t\t\t

That being said, I do feel that although the balancing is mostly good, it definitely has a few flaws. For example, characters that emphasize the evasion stat generally feel too weak and characters that emphasize attack feel too strong.
