\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is strangely addictive.

When you start playing it, there is no "true" tutorial, despite you being able to learn some stuff through the campaigns and the videos from the store page.

This game will not hold your hand on any way, and you can be expected to be DUMPSTERED on by the CPU while you are learning how to play.

If you have friends to play this with, you can all learn the game together. If you don't (or only have 1 or 2), people on online will DEFINITELY help you with the game, teach you some "pro tips" and walk you through the roughest spots. Despite this community being 100% weebs (me included), I haven't found a single creep, but the eventual salty kid is still present, as any other game. There will also be people who won't say a single word, no matter what, so don't be bothered by that.

Regarding the game itself and the availability of characters to choose, the base game selection after finishing the campaigns is pretty diverse, and will give you a lot of options to play as. Some of the best characters are included in the base game. However, ALL of my favorite characters to play as are DLCs.

IMHO, ALL DLCs from this game are worth it (yes, even the music one), since you will not only be supporting an amazing studio, that is Fruitbat Factory, but will also be getting a lot of more content to enjoy.

There is also a weekly rotation for free characters, so even if you don't buy any DLCs, you will be able to try out different characters every week!

This game is worth its full price, same as the DLCs, but if you want to save some money and get more content for the same price, wait for the monthly sale that the franchise goes in. It has been like that without fail for the past 2 years.\t\t\t\t\t
