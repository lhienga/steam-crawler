\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpeaking as the unluckiest person currently alive [no that's not a joke or even an exaggeration], this game legitimately has made me a worse person. It enabled me to realize the extent of just how bad it is. Before playing, I had noticed that in games where RNG elements are integral to the gameplay, I always fall below average. But now, having quantified just how unlucky I am, all of that previous bad luck, the unnaturally high amount of bad life circumstances I've been in, all made sense.

And now it's affected my psychology irreversibly. Every little bad thing that ever happens to me is now just a constant reminder that I'm just at the whims of the sadistic jokes fate has in store. I don't even know if I can properly communicate the magnitude of depression and anger it has caused me. Every time I have tried, I always get the typical cliche responses, such as "it's just a game" or "bad things happen to everyone". Responses from people who don't know what true bad luck is. True bad luck is not rolling a 1 every so often at a bad time. True bad luck is rolling 3 million times and getting an average of less than 2 [yes, my actual average roll is 1.89384 across 3.087 million rolls]

It took me a thousand hours of holding on to let go, and now I have to live with this knowledge against my will. Don't be like me. Avoid this psychological toxin. It's only good use is as a media player.\t\t\t\t\t
