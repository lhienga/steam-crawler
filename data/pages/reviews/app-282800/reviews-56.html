\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100% Orange Juice is a game whose positives and negatives hinge directly with the modes of play that this game has to offer, thus making it a very shaky, yet fun game in terms of reviewing.

Despite the fact that it is a dice-game in nature, the Single-Player aspect is grueling to go through, as the AI can be unrelentingly spiteful toward you, and only you. The excuse of "Super Fast" speed does not fly, as the monotony of having the game force you to get the perfect rolls against CPU loaded with perfect rolls makes it agonizing to complete some of the rewards, especially with Extreme Difficulty Campaign rewards. The difficulty of this game is purely luck-based, which causes victories against the hardest foes to be more of a grind to get the perfect RNG versus scripted perfect RNG by the CPU.

Multiplayer is where this game shines, as the game functions as it is supposed to, with luck aspects being more controlled between players rather than scripted to have loaded dice; however, the communication between other players via text can be clunky. The option to save cards and configurations for each individual character is nice, allowing a quick and seamless transition into builds and character setups that would take minutes per otherwise.

Overall, despite its cheap price tag, I can overall only recommend this game to those looking solely for an online multiplayer experience; otherwise, I do not recommend this game to single-player gamers. The unfairness and imbalance of the games' non-PvP aspects do ruin the experience and drain the fun out of an otherwise fine multiplayer dice game. If you are looking for a game that has an addicting multiplayer atmosphere, this game can suck hours of your time to the sweet nectar of victory or salt.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100% orange juice is like the chocolate milk of milks.
it's that one thing that is part of a huge category known by everyone, yet somehow it's own amazing thing that makes it so good.

a lot about 100% OJ makes it fun. the mechanics are well made and are in a such a way that even playing a character that sucks, is ultra fun. The universe is also one of the big reason that makes this board game unlike any others.
But most importantly, the best is like, half of the community.

The playerbase of this game is mostly divided in 2 parts:
- one being the tryhards that will play only good characters, kill with no hesitation, and who will usually cry in the chat after every 1's they roll or cause of rng.
- the second half is the most amazing people i've had the chance to play with. people are usually funny, chill, and so freaking cool to chat with. and they'll probably be some of your best play buddies ever.

The only thing that could end this review, is with the one tip every OJ player should keep in mind when playing:
don't play the game to win. it's a board game, with 4 players and 1 winner. + it's RNG based. you can be the best at decision making with the best character of the game, and still lose terribly. the game will only get fun if you play characters because you want to play them, and not because they're OP. Also, laughing at rolling 1's (and 0's in rare cases) is usually the go-to for a fun game.

100% Fun Juice\t\t\t\t\t
