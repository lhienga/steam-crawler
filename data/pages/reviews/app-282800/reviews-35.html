\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I'm asked to think of a game that's truly a Labor of Love, still gaining new content (and not just bug fixes) years after its initial release, the first that comes to mind is always 100% Orange Juice. I've nominated different games for this award in years past, and I felt they were also truly deserving of it, but OJ is always a top contender. For nine years now, OJ has been regularly refined, and not merely with DLC you have to spend a bit of extra money on; even the base game is improved, with this year alone adding new content like the OJ-Dex, more Minigames, and an entire set of tiger pajamas for character customization. I have no idea what the upcoming tenth anniversary will contain, but I'm certainly looking forward to it.

As for whether or not I actually enjoy the game, I honestly do! It's a fun online multiplayer party game that doesn't take up a lot of processing power, with various adjustable options that can satisfy both those wanting to dominate all others in winner-takes-all competitions and those wanting to work together at their own pace in order to beat a common enemy and make everyone feel like a winner. Admittedly, the game's random elements can be frustrating at times, such as how you can select ten power-up cards at the start of a match in order to reach your win conditions (stars collected or battles won) more easily, but they're then shuffled into a deck with the other players' cards so you might spend an entire game drawing someone else's cards that don't benefit you the way you would prefer. However, all of the other players have to deal with that same luck, and I consider the times where I've beaten the odds all the sweeter.

In summary, between my own enjoyment of the game and how much the devs clearly enjoy adding more to it, I certainly recommend this game to people with a decent internet connection looking for some fresh fun.\t\t\t\t\t
