\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first time I've written a negative review of a videogame on Steam. I bought this game because I love board games and digital deck builders like Slay the Spire, also this game appears to be fairly well received. All the signs looked good and I was certain I would like this game but I'm honestly baffled at how well received this game is. I'm convinced this game is only fairing well with reviews because of the anime theme, I'm well aware that there is a large audience of snooty anime lovers that are biased towards loving anything anime related even if the content is poor.

The worst aspect of this game is the gameplay. Winning the game feels like it requires 90% luck and 10% skill, progressing and getting revived literally relies on the roll of a dice which requires no skill. The winners almost feel predetermined before you even play the game. Sure, there are cards that mitigate risk and better your odds, but it still feels like skill gets overpowered by poor or good luck. I feel like we're passed the age of board games that rely on luck as a mechanic for the last 2 - 3 decades. It almost feels more like an passive activity instead of an interactive activity because of how little interaction or decisions you really need to make. You can choose which paths to take on the board but the maps are so small and teleporting spaces are littered throughout the map, so pursuing or avoiding other players or even specific spaces can feel futile because of poor luck, even if you think you're making the right decision that would be in your favor.

Also, the system narrator is incredibly obnoxious. I'm not sure what they were thinking when they designed this game and thought the system / gameplay narrator wasn't annoying. She spams the same lines over and over again, usually when a line is spammed with dialogue or sound effects in videogames, they will add variants of the line to prevent it from being annoying. It's like playing an older videogame where if you jump or attack and your character spams the same voice emote everytime, which makes doing that action painful. The system narrator can push out lines that overlap itself so the narrator will be saying multiple things at the same time, they even added an option in the options menu to prevent this from happening. It's like they knew the system narrator was obnoxious and had problems and left that option in, but for some reason left the annoying setting on as the default behavior. What were they thinking? I turned the system narrator off after about 40 minutes of gameplay. I just couldn't take it anymore.

The UX / UI of the game is also incredibly unintuitive. I'm pretty experienced with heavy weighted boardgames but learning how to play this game through 43 images and text from the main menu was honestly painful. They need to make the tutorial interactive that walks you through the game. A manual of how to play should only be something that's in boardgames nowadays because it's not an interactive digital experience, a tutorial like that is inexcusable in a videogame in this day and age. Honestly though, reading the tutorial was just a waste of time anyway because a literal brain dead monkey that could use a mouse and keyboard could probably beat the world champion of this game because of how much this game relies on luck as a mechanic.

Also, the general UI of this is incredibly backwards, for example the cards in your hand are displayed in small icons on the bottom center of the screen, and there are even smaller icons of the cards in your hand on the top left of the screen. You would think that hovering your mouse over the cards in the bottom center of the screen would magnify the cards so you can actually read what the card does, but instead you have to hover your mouse over the small icons on the top right of the screen. Small design choices like that really just set me off and it's honestly baffling.

The game was honestly painful from the very moment the game started. The story about this anime girl getting her pudding stolen just made me feel like I was regressing back through evolution in ways that didn't make any sense, like I wanted to become like an ostrich and bury my head in the sand in the wild so I wouldn't have had to go through the trials of living life in an intelligent civilization to have to experience something like this. The story was just not captivating or engaging at all. It would have been better to just cut the story cutscenes completely, or at least add an option to get rid of the cutscenes entirely.

The game is also trying to milk all the money out of you by distastefully promoting DLC packs for this game in the main menu. Sure, they devs need to make money, but holy crap there's 34 DLC packs available for this game. It costs more to buy all of the DLC packs than the base game itself. If they managed to convince you to give them all of your money spending it on DLC, they also have the nerve to promote a kickstarter for plushies of the characters in the game. Yes, these devs aren't making enough money from the game itself to fund making their own plushies. They need money from the blind consumers that will hail anything that is anime related as if it was handmade by god himself. With how well this game seems to be received, and with all of the DLC it's made, these devs must be making a pretty penny off of this game. You'd think they have enough money to fund making their own merchandise instead of milking their fans for even more money.

Bravo, this is honestly one of the worst games I've played in a very long time. I don't mean to be mean, but there's no way to make this constructive without being furious about design choices of this game. Make it less souless, stop pushing fans to buy more of your stuff in the main menu, and make better design choices like making the game more involved with skill instead of luck. I thought we were past the dark ages of boardgames that relied on luck as a mechanic like games such as Monopoly. This game gets two thumbs down on my end and I really hope this convinces someone out there not to buy this game.

If you enjoy actual good board games such as Pandemic or Gloomhaven, and enjoy digital card games like Slay the Spire or even Dicey Dungeons, you might hate this game like I do. If you love anime, and if you have wet dreams about luck oriented games such as Monopoly being themed around anime with what seems like endless expansion packs in development, then you will absolutely love this game.


I am a game developer myself so when I see predatory things like this in the videogame industry, and when I see design choices like this in a game, I take them very seriously and feel like I need to speak out.\t\t\t\t\t
