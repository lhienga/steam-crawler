\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100% Orange Juice is a cutesy and adorable digital board game. In essence, if you like games like Mario Party, you'll enjoy it, that is, if you can overlook the fact it's missing mini-games for the most part and focus on the gameplay. However, it makes up for this with a huge array of mechanics and a card system that gives you an endless arsenal of options to achieve victory no matter the cost!

The game might seem like a huge investment when you take into consideration the multiple DLC packs it has, but they are completely worth the purchase as they allow you to play with new characters, each with their unique gameplay features, cards, and theme, and they add to the re-playability factor tenfold.
The game features multiple modes like single player, campaign modes, and, of course, the biggest focus is its fun multiplayer mode.

The game has a huge amount of maps you can pick from, each with gimmicks associated with them. The way you progress will have a huge impact and might synergize well or not with the character and deck you picked. Each map is beautiful, memorable, and interesting to play with, as it's a party game you can enjoy with your friends. It's the type of game that's most fun with friends or randoms, more so than with bots that the game features.

The game allows up to four players per lobby session. At the end of each match, you'll gain in-game currency to allow you to gamble it for better goodies or to directly purchase items to customize your character's appearance. You'll also level up and unlock items and different things as you climb up. The host will pick the map, and you'll be allowed to pick your character. A host of cards combined with your foe's will be sent to the deck so anyone has the chance to obtain them. When you're selecting these options, it'll be anonymous, so you can surprise others with any tactic you'd like to go for.

When you start the match, each player has their own pole/flag, where you have to achieve certain objectives, like collecting x amount of stars or y amounts of victories in battle. You'll roll a die and move around the map, engaging enemies, players, bonus tiles, or random effect tiles. You'll have the option to park at your own pole/flag, but not your players, so whenever you roll a higher number than that you need, you'll pass through that checkpoint.

The game has a combat system where there are three stats: ATK, DEF, and EVA. You'll always be forced to attack when engaging in battle unless the character specifically can't fight back. Whenever you hit your enemies, you'll roll a random number, and it'll take into consideration your character's stats, so you can either land big or low numbers, making you better or not at a specific role. Cards can enhance, invert stats, or provide funny and surprising mechanics to spice battles up. Whenever you defend, you'll always take a guaranteed hit. So, let's say they attack you by rolling a +4 and you roll a +5, you'll take a -1 hit. But if you choose to evade and roll a +5, you'd take 0 damage. Beware that whenever you evade and roll a number equal to or below the enemy's attack, you'll take the whole damage. So, if you roll a +5 to a +5 attack, you'll be dealt -5 damage.

Don't let the multiple versions of Suguri bully you, and whenever you see Marie Poppo, bully her! Overall, I give 100% Orange Juice a 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
