\r\n\t\t\t\t\t100% Orange Juice is a board game deck building game. You can play both with friends/online as well as with bots. There is a handful of story mode games. Each character has a slightly different playstyle, stats and special card. The more you play, the more in game currency you gain that can unlock cards, cosmetics and even some characters.

The game can be rather fun, and personally I prefer playing it with friends, as it is a friendly competitive game. (Think Mario Party but toned back a bit.)

The DLC characters can be pretty strong, and fun, but they are paid DLC. The few that I have gotten seem to have story mode games as well as just the normal game, so the DLC's are worth it. They don't take anything from the main game, and just offer different playstyles.

The biggest downside to this game, for me, is that most of my friends don't enjoy it.

Overall, it is worth the $7 cost, and even the DLC isn't too expensive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just realized that at 238 hours, I haven't actually reviewed this game.

100% Orange Juice is a game that I describe as "Mario Party" without the minigames, but really, that might be selling the game short. After 10 years it's still being regularly updated with content and events... In the years I've played this game they've added cooperative mode, and RPG style quest mode, snowball fights, and the recent april fools game modifying Chaos Mode.

At it's most basic, it's a roll and move board game where you are trying to collect stars. These can be obtained either landing on the right spaces, or mugging your friends to take theirs. While there is plenty of strategy relating to picking cards, using every character unique stats or powers, or deciding on objectives... RNG reins supreme. This is the kind of game that'll make you smile as your brilliant plan falls prey to a series of bad rolls, or the last place meme player gets a string of luck that pushes them into a surprise victory.

The games play fast enough that you can run several in one sitting and get some light-hearted fun. There's a massive number of characters with different play-styles, loads of collectibles, and ways to customize your play. This is a game of equal parts joy, jokes and broken friendships that hasn't come close to it's end on my table.

I've puchased all the DLC for and gifted copies... yet I still feel that the developers deserve every penny I've given.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt doesn't take a review from me to know that this Is worth buying, but I might as well write something.

I found out about Orange Juice when a YouTuber I followed had posted a video on It a few years ago, but funny enough that was not my first experience with this game. A mobile game I used to play long before I saw the video actually stole the win fanfare from OJ.

...That aside, I finally got my hands on this game last year. It was having a 4-pack sale around Valentines day, and I had the perfect people to give the other copies of It to. The last time I played party/board games like this was too far back, so this Is pretty much my first real experience with them. What's daunting at first Is the sheer amount of characters. You kind of just end up finding one you like as you play, and want to read more about them against your will. Character designs are always a big factor In a game's popularity so OJ hits the nail on the head there. A lot of the characters have their own gimmick, It always changes the way you get to use certain cards. They even all have their voices which adds so much personality on top of everything else. Every character has their own theme that plays when they Norma In-game, and some of them are straight bangers.

It Is tons of fun to play this game with a group of friends. That's probably one of the only flaws--It's best played multiplayer, and moreso with friends, and there's only room for 4 ( a game mode allowing even more people would be absolutely Insane ). Personally I'm really Introverted, and prefer to be around people I know and people that they know. Either way, games get really chaotic, and It's so fun to see how far the tables can turn, how things spiral out of control as soon as someone Is close to winning. The only other big turnoff Is how many characters and even some cards are locked behind DLC, and the base character voices. If you play this game It's very likely you'll give In to a few DLC packs, but I wouldn't feel bad about It because the company honestly deserves the support ( they are not expensive ) and there's so many to choose from you're bound to find that one character you resonate with.

There's mods, plenty of customization options ( granted I somewhat dislike how things like glasses are complete rng ), cool game modes that add even more niches to specific characters, and recurring events that give you comfortable amounts of time to participate In them.
