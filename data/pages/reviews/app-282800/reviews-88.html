\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game but it really has its own unique good, bad, and ugly and isn't for everyone.

The good:
It's Mario Party with lots of cute waifus where you can beat someone up every time you catch up to them on the map, if you so choose. And each character has unique cards, stats, and passive abilities that make them good in some ways. You also get to choose 10 cards to be part of the community deck and this is one of very few games where instead of just choosing the 10 most powerful cards, you really want the cards that your character uses well since anyone might draw them so being a better card doesn't really help on average. It has many maps and it's easy to play with people online and it has a singleplayer, too.

The bad:
If you really want to play competitively, there's only a handful of good characters as many are just worse or even just obviously much worse to the point where they're just not worth using at all. There are also tons of random factors outside of your control that can just leave you doing little most of the game and having almost no chance to win, and most decisions are quite obvious and braindead ones after a while to the point where you may only make a few difficult and meaningful decisions in an entire game.

The winner of the game is the first to 5 normas. You'll need a certain number of stars to achieve each. You can also go for wins (basically kills) but that is very much harder and almost never worth doing. The problem with the stars is that it takes far too few of them to reach each norma IMO. The problem with this is that you should play very safe and passively from a competitive standpoint as dying will set you back much further than how much you'll gain from killing someone as you'll likely have enough stars to norma without even needing to kill someone anyway. So you may find it best to only attack someone if they're way ahead, close to winning, or if you're at a massive advantage when attacking them. And as simple as it would be to do so, you can't change the stars required to norma. The other issue is that since you have to complete laps to get back to your base to reliably norma, there just isn't enough spare movement to go around attacking people other otherwise moving in any other way freely since you'll just be too slow and late to norma on most maps. You're almost always better off just doing what benefits you the most and just hoping someone else gets the person ahead of you or that they die to the boss or otherwise get back luck.

The ugly:
There may be different purposes in which people play this game. Some really trying to win but others really just trying to mess around and will just attack and use cards way too easily and it make seem like they're ruining it if that's now really how you want to play. You will also just lose many games due to bad luck even if you play optimally since one bad roll on defense and you likely die, lose half your stars, and get to spend the next 1-6 rounds trying to revive rather than even getting a real turn. There are also some cards that can alter the game drastically and may just feel too painful sometimes. I can definitely understand the frustration in this game as it's very easy to feel like nothing is going your way when you just keep getting bad luck and dying and/or losing all your cards or other misfortunes. Mysteriously some of the cards also just cost way too much to the point where they're almost never worth using and it seems like they almost never fix card balance and only make minor adjustments over very long periods of time with character balance.

In conclusion I like this game so much I feel like I have to recommend it, but if you're someone that is really bothered by bad luck you better steer as far away from this as possible. If you're looking for a deep competitive strategy game you'll probably find this to be more of a party game and be letdown by it, but it definitely can be played competitively. I honestly just love optimizing all sort of crazy probabilities in order to maximize my chances of winning but I kinda feel like almost no one else even does that and just sort of plays cards and moves however they feel like. I'd also like to note you'll have to buy your favorite characters via DLC or you won't have many characters to play with.\t\t\t\t\t
