\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best way to describe OJ is a game to just vibe with your friends in VC with.

A very easy game to play; you can play a card, roll your die and do whatever the panel you land on says. If you go into this hoping to be able to win every game through pure skill then you're probably just going to get frustrated. It's more like gambling, its about stacking the odds in your favor. But in the same way that you can be good at poker but still lose because you were dealt too many bad hands, sometimes in OJ there's just nothing you can do. Even the name is a pun on 100% "RNG" Juice. Although if you want a much more in-depth experience, the game also offers Bounty Hunter mode.

But there's still a lot you can do. Each character has different strengths, weaknesses, and abilities. Each player chooses 1/4 of the cards that go into the public deck. And each board/field event will affect the outcome of the game.

And if you want to try working as a team, the Co-op mode is a lot of fun. Rather than everyone getting mad at each other, everyone can get mad at Big the Jonathan. Like I said at the beginning, this is just a great thing to do when you just wanna vibe with the squad online.

The art is adorable and there's tons of customization and cosmetics. Even with my play time, I've only scratched the surface.

The single player mode is garbage though, would not recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisgustingly rigged game. There are four difficulty levels for single player - Casual, Normal, Original, and Extreme. If you want to enjoy this game, DO NOT play anything except for "Casual" mode or multiplayer (with other people, no NPC's). Otherwise, it won't take you very long to get frustrated and lose interest in the game. I tried the VERY FIRST story level on NORMAL difficulty thinking that, as the name suggests, it will be a neutral difficulty which isn't too easy or too hard. Boy was I wrong. Whenever you get remotely close to winning or too far ahead of the AI, the game will do EVERYTHING in its power to make you lose. It will give the AI perfect movement rolls and battle rolls. It will prevent you from ever landing on any home panel to claim your win. There was one situation where all I had to do was get to my home tile to win. Right before my home tile there was a teleport tile, so the only thing I had to do to win was to get a roll that would take me past the teleport tile. Coincidentally, whenever I got close, I landed perfectly on the teleport. This happened FIVE TIMES before the AI managed to catch up and steal the win. I somehow had the patience to try to beat the same level on normal 8 times, and I have not been able to win a single one due to the same factors. The description for normal difficulty claims that on this difficulty "RNG is random and fair" and that "enemies have no advantage or bonuses". This is a straight ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ lie. I honestly can't imagine how atrocious the game must be on "Original" or "Extreme" mode when you already have an extremely minuscule (if any) chance of beating normal. A lot of the other reviews mention "luck" as a huge factor in this game, and those who have never played before may think "oh it's a board game that uses dice, of course luck is involved". You will find out the hard way what "luck" means in the context of this game. I don't know whether the unbeatable AI difficulty is a result of malice or gross incompetence on the part of the developers, but either way it is absolutely disgraceful and completely ruins the game. Otherwise, if you can look past the rotten AI difficulty, the game is fun to play with your friends, but sadly in my eyes the fun is greatly outweighed by the AI.\t\t\t\t\t
