\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame tries very hard to make you feel like you have different choices, but in fact there are just two endings, and for every checkpoint you also have to do certain things in order not to fail. There are basically no actual in-game consequences to your actions, aside from either you manage to progress or you get a game over screen and then have to go back several checkpoints and just mindlessly try different combinations of your answers in order to unlock this one functioning path.
Very often this combination of requirements is not obvious at all and sometimes what leads you to fail also kinda makes little sense.
I'm currently locked out of one of the endings because I have neutral relationship with one of the characters and the only way to improve it is to go back god knows how far and search for all the instances where I interacted with that character and then of course replay everything from that point.

While I understand that all of this is most likely limitations of having actual actors act all these scenes, it is still very annoying, and I feel that it could've been done better. The storyline screen does look like there are multiple branches but in fact most of them are just game overs and it really does feel like there is only one correct storyline and you are here not to play the game but guess the correct storyline.

So no, don't recommend. Unless you like the premise and just want to watch it as a tv series or smth, but be aware that you can't just mindlessly click through given choices coz see above\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFound this one by complete accident, what a lucky find.
It's an FMV game about a "school" that cures teens from internet and gaming addiction. It's a "choose your own adventure" type of game where you mostly choose actions from a list which result in different outcomes of the following scenes. There are also some investigative scenes, where you search rooms, find notes (which can give you more options to choose from in some scenes) and items and occasional QTEs (easy ones !).

Of course it's not that deep, it is basically like a netflix series in which you get to steer the story in smaller ways. But what is important is that the story is quite interesting and the acting is actually pretty good, certainly above many regular TV series nowadays. Sets, music and such is all done well, like I said, this could easily ass for a netflix series (or Amazon or whatever streaming service you prefer).

Technically it's all 1080p videos (no HDR or anything), I encountered no bugs. The subtitles are the only weak point, bunch of typos and weird translations, not often and not to the extent that you can't follow the story, but you will notice it.

Took me about 9 hours to get the 2 main endings and the 5 hidden endings (this includes the DLC which is kinda mandatory here since it is the second half of the story).
For me this was well worth the money (I think I bought it with 15% discount or so) and I am definitely interested in more games from these guys.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTime of writing: half way through
I just finished my first play through of the base game (which is the first half of the story) and now bought the DLC (which is the second half).

Value vs content: Good amount of content for total price
So far I spent about 3 hours, which leads me to estimate the full length of one play-through at about 6 hours. There are many choices to be made, so several play-throughs are expected to see all of the content. The developers anticipated this and did a good job of allowing scenes to be skipped (up until the choices you need to make) as well as increasing play speed. In total I expect to get 9-12 hours out of this game, maybe more. The price of this game (in my country) roughly equals going to having one drink during a 2 hour movie at the theatre.

Interaction: Simple games, some railroading, interesting story
While this is an interactive movie, there are some puzzle scenes. In my opinion these scenes are too simple, because everything you need to click on is highlighted. The supposedly fast (quicktime) events are scarce and simple - only one or two mouse clicks are needed and you have a lot of time. So in the mini-games there is hardly any challenge - I wished the game had some difficulty level, making the mini-games less obvious.

The choices you are presented with are more interesting than the mini-games. The first few chapters ease you into this type of game and the story and it feels like some of the choices you make really do not matter at all. However, as the story progresses, it becomes clear that your choices influence what the main characters think of you and I believe that can really have an effect on the latter part of the story. That remains to be seen, but I am very interested to see how this continues.

During the last part of the first movie, some of the choices have obvious consequences, others stay mysterious - did I do the right thing?

Closing words
I really like this game so far. The game automatically starts in English and I have no problem playing this game without knowing English, because everything has subtitles - even writing in the background sometimes. Sure the game has some flaws, not every translation into English is flawless, some editing of scene transitions isn't really on point and some audio doesn't seem to be cleaned up and polished. But considering that this game was made in a non-native English country during a Pandemic, those flaws can be forgiven. The story is interesting, the actors range from pretty okay to great and I'm sure there will be at least a few satisfying endings to the game/story.\t\t\t\t\t
