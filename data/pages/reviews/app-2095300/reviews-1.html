\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWARNING: Ending only with DLC & Microtransactions

The production value is good. It is not AAA acting. More on soap-opera level, but not as cringy that complaining would be justified. The audio is only in mandarin, but English subs are provided. However, you will - or at least I had to- use the pause button to read the subs, since they talk sometimes rather fast.

The game has the telltale disease. The story is linear resp. you’ll end up at the same main-junctions no matter what your choice is. This is not bad perse. Telling an interesting story does require sometimes some railroading.

You play a teenager, who is trying to flee a re-education centre that uses questionable methods. I haven’t seen in the game anything that induced instant vomit reflex in me. In reality some countries - which shall not be named here, unless you want to end up on their pfui-pfui-list - do way worse. But it is good to see the danger of enforced conformism being shown in a game.

You know what else counts as questionable methods? Selling a story-based game with the main-story ending in a separate DLC. I made an eye role but went ahead and bought the DLC. While waiting for the download to finish I read some reviews. Oh shocker… there are microtransactions. You can buy eggs or roses to decrease or increase your standing with NPCs. The standing with NPCs are relevant for the story. Sure you have the option to return in the storyline to the junction points, but it seems that other players where struggling to find the relevant junction points to fix their standing. So there is not a roadblock but they are nudging you toward the microtransactions. That was the point for me to nope-out. Don’t want to support such a business model. Which is a shame. I think they would have actually produced a decent game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first interactive movie game I've ever come across, and it's surprisingly good! The story is very simple. Ignorant parents send an Internet-addicted children to Yang Yongan (Actually Yang Yongxin in real life, whom is known notoriously by most people in Chinese culture) Older generations are prejudice against new things, parents lack love and care for their children, and fail to notice that the problem with their children is not at all the child themselves, but the child's family. Unscrupulous social scum are making crazy money and abusing young people in the name of "study". Overall, I think the story is very good in terms of material selection, narrative, and final reflection on society.
Cons may include: 1. The actors' performances are sometimes a little inconsistent, which is a common problem with interactive stories because it's difficult to transit smoothly to a point from every choice. 2. The interactivity is not strong, more like watching a movie, but on the other hand friendly to casual players. The main game only has half of the plot. It is recommended to buy the full version at a discount.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBEFORE YOU BUY: Get the dlc & the game or neither. I wouldn't recommend playing the first half only

The story can be kinda cringe and illogical at some points, but I think it's generally a good one. The endings are a great roundup for the story line and it feels like your actions and your relationships influence how characters behave in different scenes. And yes you can buy flowers and eggs in-game using real money and gift them to characters. But this is an additional feature that has nothing to do with the game itself and is part of the extras; so I think it's okay to turn a blind eye to this one. Also you will get free flowers from achievements.

Overall I think the developers put a lot of love and effort into this game and if you like multiple choice games, this one is definitely worth a try.
