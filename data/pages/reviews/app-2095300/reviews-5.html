A Truly Immersive Experience
(English and Chinese review)
vvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvvv

I saw this game on my steam recommended after playing a lot of story driven choice games such as
Detroit: Become Human and Telltale Games . Breakout 13 immediately piqued my interest as a fresh new face breaking into the niche genre of movie games.

Having completed three different ending and over 60% of all the options I feel confident to give my take on the game and hopefully answer some questions for you.


The Story

The story it's self was extremely engaging and realistically eerie. Every single choice you make, no matter how big or small will change your story outcome completely. You really feel the stress of the main character as you go along with his experiences and thoughts.

The Characters

The acting in this game is amazing and the casting choices are perfect. Every single character felt really fleshed out and completely individual. Building your relationships with everyone was very natural and realistic. You start to care for the people you are surrounded by and really see them as your friends.

The Soundtrack

One of the biggest parts of the game for me was the soundtrack, The music chosen for each situation was spot on and had me searching the internet far and wide to find the songs.

The Scene

The fact that the game is mainly set in broad daylight just stands to make the whole experience that much more terrifying. We as humans tend to relate creepy or scary things to darkness and night time but the game flipped the script on this trope and made the safety of day light obsolete, In my opinion having such a dark story during daylight makes it all that more haunting.

The Translation

Breakout 13 being a Chinese game I was sceptical of how good the English Translations would be because most games would be targeted at an east Asian audience. However it seems a lot of the budget went to making a smooth and correct translation for English speakers.

Final Thoughts

Overall Breakout 13 was an amazing experience that sent me on a emotional roller coaster. I even found myself tearing up a lot of the time especially with the ending I got while playing. If you have never played a game such as this I highly recommend giving it a go no matter what language you speak. So if you are interested, i'm employing you to give it a go or if you know someone who may be interested tell them. I suspect this game is going to need a lot of word-of-mouth to reach more people in the west.

Message to ALT Lab

I can't wait to see what other games you come out with. I want to personally thank you for keeping this type of genre of game alive. I really hope you can make a game from the cliffhanger where she has to go to a Korean clinic and they have to go save her. I wish you all and the cast members a promising career in the future.

9/10

Highly Recommended!

人物

I had to go back to chapter 1 in order to get a character to like me, skip to chapter 6 to take a branching path and THEN skip back to the ending in order to get past the unavoidable game over it gave me.
Thankfully when you go back to an earlier branch and raise a character's affection, it saves it globally so you don't need to worry about replaying absolutely everything between the first chapter and the ending.

Also - as other reviews have pointed out - you need to buy the DLC in order to complete the game. I absolutely do not agree with such a decision, even if they reasoned it reasonably well in the responses of those other reviews. Should've just delayed it instead.

Other than that it's fantastic. The camera work is top notch, and I really felt immersed in the world making my own decisions. A big plus for keeping the original language, I'm pretty tired of everything being in English.
If you like CYOA/FMV, you won't regret picking this up.\t\t\t\t\t
