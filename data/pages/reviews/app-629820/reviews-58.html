\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI 100%ed the base game in 1 day

This is both a very good, and very bad thing. I enjoyed this game so much because it was literally just Jaws Unleashed but updated, to the point where half the time I was exploring I was actually looking for a Colossal Squid.

-->The Good<--

The absolute best thing the game has to offer is its movement, until I had played this I had believed Warframe had the most satisfying movement in the games industry but no, Maneater has Warframe beat on account of there never, not even once being a point where I felt that i was not in control of my character. This is very important for a game, it's the mechanics that you're going to be interacting with most so it has to be good.

The plot is fun, and extremely charming but also very short which is good and bad, with the majority of gameplay being tied to side quests which can get very repetitive very quickly.
More than half the quests in this game are literally "Kill 10 X" or if they want to spice it up "Consume 10 X" they are the same quest though, just one requires you to eat something and the other allows you to tail whip them instead.

The environment is big and beautiful but can also be small and cramped which are both good things because it means there's a veriety of environments to explore and experience, however often times they seem empty. Now if you've played the game for a brief amount of time this may be confusing because they're full of collectibles, sites and creatures but that's the thing.They're all the same creatures, maybe 4 creatures per area and of those the only ones you need to care about are Marlins, Alligators, Barracuda Swordfish, Hammerheads, Mako, Great Whites, Orcas and Sperm Whales because those are hostile to you. Now keep in mind that means for half the game the only one you need to care about are Alligators and Barracuda. which means if I'm playing passively there's never a situation where I particularly feel challenged or threatened.

--->The Bad<---

Now that never feeling threatened part is the biggest issue this game has... it's extremely easy, and they make it easy intentionally. There are three upgrade paths in the base game: Armour, Bio-Electric, Shadow.
However it would be easier to describe them as "Damage & Damage Mitigation, Dodge & AOE, Speed & DOT" now what does this mean? Well even if you're going speed & Damage over time you're still never going to be in danger of dying due to taking damage, you literally regenerate health by eating, aka the thing every objective in the game is based around. So you'll always be regenerating. So why do I need to mitigate damage ever? I literally beat every story boss in less than 2 minutes, and that's only because the final boss took almost 2 minutes.

The only threat you have in this game is from human hunters because they will swarm you, but you also have the ability to mitigate their damage entirely by default, so it's a timing game whether you'll get hit at all. I can see skilled players never needing to upgrade anything, and beating the game far faster than I did. What I found was that I was able to take on level 45 enemies at level 20, that's an example of how easy the game is, and enemies go as high as level 60.

Now there were three massive downsides to the game but they're not actually a big deal until exactly 1 section.
The first is the creatures spawn and Despawn in really weird ways, I've literally been about to attack a sperm whale, I look away to check that there aren't any Orca nearby, look back and it's gone. whenever I was lower levelled and grinding this was a serious issue because I was grinding on Alligators and they do a decent amount of damage if you're not being careful, meaning you're trying to dodge and out maneuver them, meaning that if you get bit once or twice, and you dodge away from them, look back and they're gone there's no other way to look at it, that's a net negative, You're injured and were relying on the regeneration from killing the Alligator, you don't get experience from the fight you were in and you don't get nutrients needed to level up. Now thankfully that only happened to me once

The second issue is that it's very hard to just hold something in your mouth, now this may not seem like a problem initially and may seem reasonable initially because you deal damage in three main ways. 1. left clicking, 2. thrashing the mouse left and right, 3. tail whips
So why would you need to hold something?
Well because you can also launch things into the air by holding them in your mouth, and tail whipping... which is needed for unlocking certain shortcuts and even a mission in the pilot for Truth Quest. So the fact that whenever you hold something the game randomly starts auto-thrashing means that those sections are being artificially elongated by something that shouldn't even be happening.
Now factor in that you can't hold anything if your initial bite one shots it and you realise that it can be very difficult to actually do these missions because you're waiting for turtles, sharks, and marlin to spawn for half the time.
Additionally there's no way to lock on to a target so even if you actually tail launch something decrease the chances of it going to wherever you wanted it to go to by 15% for every possible target on the field, and it started at 80% because 20% of the time the tail launch will go in a random direction.

Finally there are a lot of invisible walls.. not in the water, to my knowledge there are none in the water. even the map barrier is visible but more to the point is if you go on land you'll notice that the land is extremely hard to navigate... which seems to be obvious given this is a shark game but the game is set up in such a way that for certain collectibles, and even missions you HAVE to go on lance, so it encourages you to do so, which makes it weird that there are unusually placed inisible barriers. for example if there are 2 objects side by side even if the gap is twice as big as it would need to be for you to pass through it... there is an invisible barrier there. Doesn't matter if it's trees or houses.
Hell there's even a section where you're in an orca pool and you can jump into the stands... but... there's nothing in the stands, there's a giant hallway into the rest of the aquarium which could fit three level 30 sharks but it's an invisible wall preventing you from entering.
at that point I literally thought "Well then why in the hell can I get into the stands? Why is this an option if there's nothing here good or bad" And the answer to that question turned out to be "Because in a side quest later in the game there'll be humans here that you have to eat"
Would it not have been better and more interesting to actually make the aquarium, and connect it underground with the ability to swim through it, into the different enclosures and possibly break the viewing glass?
Of course I'm personally biased on that due to Jaws Unleashed which had an entire section of the game based in an aquarium which looked very similar to the one in Maneater.

--->Appreciation<---

The second best part of the game were the Easter Eggs. Lovecraftian, Subnautica they were all good but the three seashells that revolutionised Bathroom care was easily the best.
And for anyone who thought the game was just making a random joke about that watch the 1993 film "Demolition Man" and you'll get the reference.

--->Resolution<---
The game's bad qualities are extremely specific, and besides from every upgrade in the game being extremely overpowering to the point where I'm honestly just thinking about doing an upgrade-less run the game is a joy to play, and for me - a concept artist with training in the games industry I find it to be inspirational in a way. I've always wanted to make an evolution-based aquatic survival game and to date this is the closest to that idea I've seen.
The game's good qualities far exceed the bad ones, and as evidence I've already bought Hope Quest.\t\t\t\t\t
