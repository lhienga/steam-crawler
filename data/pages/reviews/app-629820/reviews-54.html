\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the only shark games I have ever played so I can't say it's the best one out there or not but I really enjoyed my time playing this, going around killing all that appose me was such a cool feeling, I felt powerful.

Starting off with the story;
I really liked how the story was made, the ending is not a 'oh I chomped and now he's dead goodbye', it had a proper story ending to it. Skaly Pete was a menace to the waters killing a preganenant shark and giving the pup shark a scar that would be permanent it's whole life as an identification. But Pete didn't think the shark would soon be 10m long and bite off all of Pete's limbs eventually.
The story was enjoyable and kept me wanting to play leading me to get the DLC for the game (at full price)
The Apex Predators are just some of the big fish you will be facing and they have some evolutions of their own so watch out for those.

Evolution;
I think there is a good amount of body evolution parts in this game each one having it's own advantages and weaknesses that really play a key part of the game, the Tiger Shark body was a nice thing to have at the start to get all the materials you need to get upgrades faster was a welcome I didn't expect but I liked.
there only being 3 evolution types in the main game and one in the DLC works well because it's not a very big game and other parts would be harder to get or understand (but if they were in the game already I guess it wouldn't be hard to understand)

Achievements;
All of the achievements are pretty easy to get, the one thing I didn't like about them is that to fully 100% the game you have to get the DLC otherwise you will miss out on 1/3 of them and I personally don't think it should have been like that.
Most of the achievements are pretty easy some of them being for getting all the collectables in the game and in all areas and mainly for the story as well but that is to be expected, as well as killing all the Apex Predators.

DLC;
I bought the DLC for full price and I actually loved it, the new Atomic set is crazy. The laser beam you get when the body evolution is unlocked is very handy and I like it a lot.
The missions are interesting and some of them are difficult which is a good thing, I don't want to be able to beat the DLC in one sitting that wouldn't be fun. The Time Trials on the other hand I didn't really enjoy because most of them needed you to jump over bridges and I found that oddly difficult, but that's just a me problem.

Conclusion;
I love this game and I will definitely be re-playing it at some point when I forget about how the story goes, makes any game more enjoyable that way.
I would absolutely recommend this game to my friends or anyone else out there wanting to play a shark game of this calibre. All in all I'd say it is worth the money at any price including DLC I had a lot of fun and the game is about 16-17 hours to 100% so it's not too long.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got Maneater on sale for under $30 and the DLC for 50%. For that price, this is a great game and a fun, solid experience through the entire main campaign. While the game can feel pretty easy, it does a good job of making you feel like some sort of SyFy channel movie monster, the transition from baby shark in the bayou to super-powered megalodon bodying sperm whales in the gulf feeling satisfying. Story's clearly secondary, but shows that the team cared, as it's a step or two above story for the sake of it, with the Moby ♥♥♥♥ inspiration coming through well. Gameplay is solid, though lock on can frustrate as it's more of a soft aim-assist, but gets the job done 9/10. The other obvious flaw is how janky land movement is, sometimes taking away directional control or throwing you random directions. Despite that, the base game doesn't ask much of you on land, and with 90% of the game in the water, you'll find the game is controls to generally be tight and consistent, with movement underwater letting you really feel like a giant underwater death machine. If you're looking for a rampage game crossed with a collectathon, get it, especially if it's on sale. Don't bother with the DLC if you don't feel like fighting the jankier parts of the games controls.

P.S. Would love to see more games with this concept, normal animal becoming a big monster. Cool ♥♥♥♥\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Maneater before on the PS4 and honestly it was a welcomed treat all things considered back in 2020. To some, people scratched their itch for a game with Doom Eternal, but for me as much as that was fun, I ended up getting this here on Steam simply because it's designed for an oddly broad and yet specific demographic.

Narrative:
The game's narrative is a satire on reality/"educational" programming. If you're an old fart like me, you probably grew up watching Mythbusters well before you physics middle school teacher realized that they had some value to it. Over time the educational stuff became reality tv and part of it was due to the sincere (and well deserved) success of Deadliest Catch. But that success, with a demographic that will purchase the DVD's and merch (plus how cheaper it is to film that vs a nature documentary) is why we have this game today as a story.
It follows Scaly Pete who irradiates the mise-en-scene of an interesting narcissist who's family history of shark hunters meets you... the evolutionary bewilderment. The thick Louisiana accent is oddly a veil to a story that makes you sympathetic because, he (like the satire he is based on) is equally human as much as the cameras are on him and the spectacle of shark hunting interests us (the supposed viewers at home). But we, of course are playing a bullshark with a drive that perplexes Darwin. There is no niche, there is no adaptation that you make of it. In the gameplay section I'll expand on this, but it's clear from the start of the game, if you pay attention to the narration, that you have behaviors and traits uncommon for bull sharks and it's how your shark's mother expresses thresher-like expressions and later you have a whale radar. Yeah... it gets nuts quick.
But this is a cut and dry revenge story and in some ways it's cathartic to players like me someone who equally respects the incredible world of real sharks while simultaneously enjoying the Hollywood blockbuster spectacles of red waves like everyone else does. It damns our pollution, our nuclear history with the ocean, and the cruelty we do genuinely do to provoke behaviors for sharks to react to film cameras from the shows we loved during Shark Week.
So it performs this weird balancing act of being a Jaws Unleashed spiritual successor and an effective (but rewarding) commentary. But it does it well.

Gameplay:
Gameplay is broken up between eating fauna, eating humans, eating crates, eating signs, eating licence plates, and all the while getting the evolutionary mutations to make a dream shark out of a bullshark foundation. You start from the freshwaters of a bayou to the open ocean (which actually gets a little trippy and not for those with thalassophobia). It's a lot of eating and the grind is very repetitive and yes there is a grind.
The game's playtime is, from what I remember, something you can blitz through if you have a weekend off work. It's very quick. But the repetitive nature of your gametime is what makes this game a sort of thing you play in bursts so the humor stays relevant, you get immersed in the "episodes" that play in cut-scenes, and you enjoy the scenery.
But the rewards of seeing your 10 year old self's wicked imagination of making a borderline lovecraftian monster of a selachimorpha is fun, gimmicky but FUN and seeing the growth between a aquarium sized "pet the shark" pup to a behemoth "Elder" is nothing short but fun. You might even notice some inspirations of sharks from your body parts and a light spoiler, to prove this, is how you can ALMOST see a tiger shark-like tooth design for the "electric" set. It's stupid but its fun and it makes the game stand above its spiritual predecessors.
You go from place to place, leveling up just enough to either reach the next size or the halfway point to that next phase, and each location has a different theme and parody. Redneck filled bayous, waterway properties, piers, and some other surprises. Your bull shark is naturally evolved to breathe in both fresh and salt waters so this was a welcomed and fun way to diversify a "shark game".
As for combat, you might need to find a mod for a lock on if you don't have a good sense of spatial understanding. You basically bit, dodge, and tailwhip until what you want is dead. You tend to dodge more of the humans shooting you and mindlessly bash the bite button and more circle around and time out the fauna you ingest. But the biggest tip I have is to tail whip when possible, the colliding damage is great against boats and IF you hit a fish or something against a rock, it's an effective stun and great for those momentary cramped areas.

Music:
I actually can't recall a single track that made me look it up on Youtube EXCEPT the Maneater (show) theme song, "Shark in the River" which irradiates the country-rock twang appropriate for the plotline and the muddy/chummy/awesome intro. We may hate reality tv "educational" shows, but the intros are fun, and they delivered on this. Hats off to Daniel James on this.

Visuals and fidelity:
The game runs decent on a 5700xt but there are cases of stuttering and some a.i. errors but other than that it's a great looking game for the art style used to make you, the world, and characters and oddly enough could be effectively placed in Tripwire's other works. It's not perfect, but runs smoother and better than my PS4, which is precisely why I bought it again.


Final thoughts:
Maneater is a treat to play and it's mindless fun. I work 40+ hours a week plus full time school. I don't have a lot of time to get into a longer FPS of methodical thinking, I'm stressed out of my mind, tired, and needing of a good laugh. The reason why I couldn't get into Eternal, way back in the start was I was hoping for more mindless and cathartic fun versus a more thoughtful FPS. No hate, just it came to me at the wrong time. But this was the game that made 2020 so fun and I encourage like-situated gamers to enjoy it.

It's mindless fun and while I DID initially THINK Truth Quest was a horrible direction... guess what... now it's making fun of the alien/illuminati shows that also effected a shared medium that used to have some decent documentaries. So I CANT wait to play through it this time around.

If you like Jaws, you'll enjoy this. If you love an brutally sarcastic means of critiquing our underwater treatment, you'll have a blast. While I don't think it's the most intelligent game, it makes fair points and offers moments of introspection on how you impact the shark community (sorta like how World of Darkness has weresharks who retaliate against the Bikini nukes). It's just a blast and it rekindled a love for sharks that while I won't go into a career over it, it's something I wished more folks appreciated and this game celebrates the filmatic success of shark attack films, criticizes the tv that changed, and is an open letter than you don't need a melancholic game to prove a point.

It's a shark game.
If you like sharks... you'll love this.

Thanks for reading and I actually hope this gets you to support the game and I hope the sequel is equally great. Hell a prehistoric one would be fun. A more balls-to-the-wall installment would be fun. I just want another bite.

Yes I ended this review on a pun.\t\t\t\t\t
