\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is one of the best power trip fantasies made playable. You play as a shark that gets unceremoniously put into a very hostile environment, slowly but surely work your way up the food chain and eventually become the ultimate apex predator.

The game has a pretty good progression system, a nice, big open world to explore, and plenty of action to feed all your hungry shark fantasies. All the different "Evolutions" you collect on your journey to dominance adds a lot of options for creative play styles and adds quite a bit of reply value as well. Overall, a great game to play straight through in one go or in several short bursts through the week because playing as a super predator is never not fun.

Story-wise the game doesn't have much to offer. The main character of the story (who is also you main antagonist) is a flat, one-note caricature of hunting stereotypes. Every one else gets next to no characterization at all and are kinda just there. The lemony narrator provides some much needed levity though, even if he eventually starts repeating a lot of lines if you play long enough

You won't really care for the story that much because you're too busy having fun being a shark that eats anything and everything in front of it. And the story is really just a paper-thin excuse for the game to throw objectives at you. If you do care to really look, there is a deeper lore in here, but not of this actually affects the gameplay. Plus, the game itself pretty much hints that you should not to take it too seriously

Word of warning, this game as a reputation of being a bit buggy, with some people reporting a crash can even wipe your save file. My personal experience: in about 30+ hours of gameplay, I've only crashed twice, neither affected my saves though. Maybe I'm just lucky.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tManeater is a vapid and hollow experience that is a collection of half-baked ideas and features at best and in execution actually far less than that.

The idea of being a rampaging shark is a limited premise, the concept being little more than an unstoppable murderous beast. As such, Maneater had a difficult first hurdle to clear in design and managed to barrel headlong into it as well as the subsequent ones and those in the other lanes as well.

The primary gameplay mechanic and almost the only one is eating stuff. Depending on your current level and that of your prey it may take more than one bite. But beyond spamming the attack button there's almost literally nothing more to it. Even when fighting against humans hunting you down there is nothing to combat. At no point is there any finesse or departure from spamming the attack and possibly dodge buttons as quickly as possible.

With that being the sole gameplay element the main objectives are exceedingly dated 2004-era MMO "go here, kill X Y's." That's the entire game. Repeatedly for several hours. There are several collectibles to try and break up the monotony but unfortunately, they're similarly poorly designed and only add to it. There are no story missions with special mechanics or objectives such as escaping a chase or following someone trying to escape. At the very end there's a brief hint of an attempt to add something as one out of the several zones gets a debuff but it's within the last 10 minutes of the game making it absolutely meaningless.

In addition to the game's lack of gameplay, the controls are also a miserable affair with which to get through the slog. In a water environment where there are 3 dimensions a lock-on camera would be very welcome but is completely missing. Instead, to keep track of enemies that dart around and past the camera you will have to routinely click the right stick in to throw the camera towards the nearest threat which would be incredibly disorienting barring the fact that holding forward and mashing attack is the only thing required of you in a fight. The game also has a "surface" mode that locks the shark to the surface and requires a separate button press to submerge again. Very frequently during combat, especially in shallow water, you will be pushed up to the surface, locked to it, and unable to immediately reengage whatever target is attacking you underwater.

The only thing at all positive about the game is the presentation and it's a mixed back mostly made of misses. The fake reality show presentation is a good one and nails the Discovery Channel shows of the last decade but it's fleeting and infrequent, only capping each chapter and each end of the game. The far more common thing is Chris Parnell constantly narrating. There will rarely be a minute without hearing him and due to this several lines will be repeated constantly. Worse yet, and this is highly subjective, his incessant string of pseudo-facts and jokes are astoundingly unfunny and are a waste of such a talented comedic voice actor.

Maneater is such a horrendously dull and meaningless experience that I honestly cannot recommend it even for free.\t\t\t\t\t
