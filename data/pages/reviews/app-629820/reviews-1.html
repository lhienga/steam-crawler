\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR - Buy the base game on sale, avoid the DLC.

The base game is fun - a basic but decent game-play loop for the most part, but always with an upgrade to aim or save for - and the voice over was of a decent comedic quality and well delivered. I'm not aware of many modern games in this style, so if the 'run from big things, until you eat enough small things to come back and eventually eat them' style of game appeals, then it's fairly good.

Then there's the DLC...
Spoilers ahead - although this game is so old, most potential buyers have probably researched it already!

Once you transition into the DLC "Truth Quest", it takes a 'Conspiracy Theory' angle to the voice-over which is cute for all of about 5 mins then becomes painfully unfunny.

The game-play loop becomes boring and repetitive and uses a targeting system that was almost unused throughout all of the base game - the 'Tail Whip'. You need to launch things into the top of Radio Towers, and the aiming mechanic - if you can call it that - is awful.

After that, there are Time Trials (which in terms of the game, come out of nowhere), Bounty Fights(with bullet sponge enemies and choppers - see aiming 'mechanic' above) and Boss Creature fights.
Oh, and kill hundreds of humans with guns, 10 at a time, usually on land.
It just feels like the Devs ran out of ideas but wanted another payday.

I will finish the game & DLC, to get the achievements.
However, should I ever feel I want to come back and play again, I'll steer well clear of the DLC, which is a shame because it appears in most of the loading screens throughout the game - feels like building FOMO.

Overall a sad, ignominious end for a game that should have produced a much better DLC. Such a shame.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t~*~2024 is the Year of the Backlog!~*~

Maneater is probably the most cathartic game I have ever played. You get to play as a shark. And not necessarily the shark from Jaws (despite several similarities to that old Jaws Unleashed game). No, ol' Bruce was too calm. You're playing as a shark from a Syfy Channel Original Movie: a hulking, mutated freak that attacks everything in sight...which is exactly why the audience roots for it because holy cow that fish is the coolest thing you will ever see. THAT kind of shark.

But when you have a freakin awesome shark as your protag, the antag needs to be just as awesome. Enter Scaly Pete: a ragin' Cajun fisherman with a tough exterior and a complex ball of emotions that he gets out by killing sharks. Scaly Pete's cutscenes are just as engaging as the shark's gameplay. He's well-acted and his scenes are well-paced, giving you just enough emotional attachment to care about him but not enough to care about him getting bit. He's a genuinely interesting character to follow.

And despite all the wackiness, this is one of the most conservation-friendly games I have ever played. The reason the shark is the freak she is and the reason Port Clovis is a polluted craphole is because of how the people of the place decided to treat the ocean ecosystem right outside their doors. It's not told as much as shown (although the "telling" part is done with enough sarcastic humor that its more likely to amuse than frustrate), with the animals being completely psychotic towards each other among piles of trash. It's got a message to tell, but does so by amusing and without preaching.

Great game, absolutely love it, best GTAesque ever made, best psycho shark experience ever crafted, and most fun ocean conservation message I have seen put to a computer screen.\t\t\t\t\t
