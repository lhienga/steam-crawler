\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game a while back, but just started getting into it over the weekend. Started as a fun little time waster, spending a few minutes here and there doing "side quests" and finding collectibles. But it got addicting, and most of my time is from the past two days alone.

Overall,

Pros -

Good graphics/world design - It's both beautiful and gritty. Best way to describe it would be a dystopian, mildly biopunk version of Jaws.
Some RPG elements - while not a true RPG, resource management is there and the ability to "customize builds" is fun.
Interesting story - Not the main premise of the game, but part way through there's a bit of a "I don't quite hate the antagonist" element.
Fun narration and easter eggs - plays out like an almost satire nature documentary and a lot of hidden cultural references.

Cons -

Audio - I'll admit Subnautica just spoiled me for sound underwater games.
Difficulty - The first hour or so, there's some difficulty there, have to choose who you fight. Then with a bit of grinding and a half assed build it gets super easy super quick.
Level caps - Once in the zone, leveling is quick and level caps are hit easily. Would make more sense if the level cap was raised, but each level gave less of a stat boost.

Overall, don't expect hundreds of hours of gameplay, but a fun and entertaining game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame with a unique concept that plays fine for the most part, but starts to feel repetitive fairly fast.
You will be doing quite a bit of combat, which has some cool moments, such as dodging past torpedoes and then throwing them back at the attacks(Yes, the shark can throw people and certain projectiles, it's awesome.)
but it left me wishing there was a bit more to it.
One major point of criticism is the slow progression rate at the start of the game. It can feel like a real drag u
ntil you get a couple upgrades.
The upgrade system system is well done with visually distinct Evolutions with different stats, set bonuses and an ability for the body part.

The story, surprisingly enough, made me feel sorry for a couple of the human characters, but for the most part it's comedic in tone, with almost all of your actions being accompanied by a documentary-esque narrator.

Overall, I did have fun, and I hope the developers decide to do a sequel with improved combat, new evolutions(glider shark and landshark, please!), and a bit more meat on it's bones.\t\t\t\t\t

My PC specs are more than fine. Updated my drivers and made sure everything was playing nice after the first one and it didn't matter. Never made it out of the Bayou.

If your product is that unstable I really don't see why I should bother.

Buyer beware I suppose. Will be requesting a refund.\t\t\t\t\t
