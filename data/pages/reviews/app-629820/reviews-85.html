\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have unlocked 100% the main game and started Truth Quest. It's still a very repetitive experience. Hardly any variety to the content. Each area of the game has the same mission types. Mostly kill/eat a bunch of marine life or people. If you aren't doing that you are swimming around finding collectibles.

I've maxed out all the main game's evolutions and at level 30 I still don't feel like a dominant ocean predator. Humans have ridiculous auto-aim that hits you every time. Fighting them mainly consists of ramming their boat. Ram, ram, ram, spin, ram, ram, ram, spin. As a mega-shark I should be able to completely decimate them with just a few bites.

Fighting boss creatures consists of the same patterns. Bite, bite, bite, dodge, bite, bite, bite, dodge. Starts glowing, dodge and bite. Rinse and repeat.

The only variety to the enemies in Maneater is aesthetics.

The game really needs some patches to allow you to dominate after you have put in the required time to max out everything. Also a patch for the swimming function of the game. My left trigger finger is sore from repeatedly holding L2 on the controller to speed up. This could be fixed with a simple toggle-speed mode feature.

I will say that the narrations from Chris Parnell of "Rick and Morty" fame are hilarious and basically the only thing that kept me motivated to play the game to it's entirety. Overall Maneater has a great sense of humor and it's the levity that makes it an enjoyable experience.

I'd only recommend buying this game if you can get it and the DLC for $10-$20 on a Steam sale. Otherwise don't waste your money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good. Got all achievements in 12 hours.

Gameplay can get pretty grindy towards the end - it's essentially 5~ mission types with 1 additional mission type added in if you get the DLC.

It's essentially Jaws: Unleashed but more fleshed out.

The animations are cool, the mutations are cool, the combat is repetitive and once you figure out the obvious best technique it's pretty trivial.

The best part of the game for me was the first 2-3 hours when you're actually evolving. Once you hit late game the "progression" falls off pretty hard and is largely just used to gate you from content you shouldn't access.

7/10. Not great, not terrible. Fun for what it is.\t\t\t\t\t
