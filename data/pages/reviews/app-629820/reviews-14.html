\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved this game that I never heard about! Seriously, how did I not know about this game until a streamer I follow played it recently? Honestly it's pretty different perspective wise, never played a game as an animal.

I really enjoyed the dark and irreverent humor of the narrator as well as the numerous pop culture culture references throughout the game. It kinda reminds me of Destroy All Humans.

There are some unfortunate drawbacks to the game. The physics can be a little wonky at times or maybe just too accurate and I need to git gud as more often than not my shark ended up flipping over on her back when jumping out of the water. Also the content is very limited. The main game only took me 24 hours to complete and that was with a lot of just roaming around chomping things! Also I found the main game to be very easy but I kind of enjoyed that as a change of pace. I found the DLC much more challenging, like insane at higher threat levels! Speaking of the DLC, it seems to be very poorly optimized. The main game performs fine but when going into the new area for the DLC, all of the menus would cause a massive drop in my frame rates to the point the game would hang for a second or two. Also had a couple of random crashes while playing, we can chalk that up to COVID I guess! ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯

I got the game during the 2023 Autumn sale for $18 and some change, at first I thought that was a little much considering the lack of content but you can keep playing it and just being a murderhobo and the amount of fun I had playing what content there was, I decided it was money well spent. Just sad that they don't seem to be developing this game anymore, it has lot of potential. Hopefully there will be a sequel at the very least!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(mini-review).

Maneater is a fun little game where you play as a baby shark that must consume wildlife and man to grow and evolve. The ultimate goal is to develop into "Mega" status and become the apex predator by knocking off all other "apex" predators. Along the way, you'll find hidden resource caches, uncover tongue-in-cheek stories about your environment and unlock modifications and upgrades, like bony protrusions, bio-electric skin, poison cloud or vampirism. Discovering hidden environments is neat and the combat, while clumsy, can be fun in short doses, as you employ different strategies against different predators. The game runs reasonably well and looks fine but the title crashed more than a few times for me. The game can be a merciless slog at the beginning and shamefully easy at the end so the pacing definitely needs work and the silly tone undermines the fun of being a super predator.

Still worth picking up on a sale as a nice diversion.
7/10.\t\t\t\t\t
