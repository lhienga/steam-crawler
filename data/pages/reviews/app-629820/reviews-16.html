\r\n\t\t\t\t\t TL;DR: A pretty casual open-world game that leverages on having a unique player setting to stand out. Good visuals, world exploration and power fantasy, but low in terms of content and replayability. Buy when on sale



Note: This review does not include the Truth Quest DLC



Pros:
1. Unique underwater setting that allows for 3-dimensional play

2. Great visuals with several different environments, although some of the locations just kinda blend together after a while. The game world is well-populated with wildlife and humans and makes it feel alive.

3. The power fantasy of being a massive apex predator plays out pretty well. You gain power pretty steadily as you level up, and enemies you might struggle with at the start eventually become free protein as you grow in size. Towards the end of the game, most hostile wildlife is basically no threat to you because you can kill them by grabbing them and rendering them helpless while you chow down. Bosses are an exception, although if you're overleveled some minibosses go down the same easy way.

4. You can smack an enemy 40m out of the water which is always hilarious

5. You have a good bit of freedom in choosing your gear. All gear is unlocked permanently once you acquire it, there's no irreversible selections, and no cost to switch gear. The only caveat is you need to be in a Grotto (fast travel location) to do so

6. Almost a complete lack of need to grind for upgrades at all. Resources to upgrade your gear are acquired very naturally as you play.

7. Game is stable and optimised, fast load times, no crashes, no frame drops or anything to indicate lagginess



Neutral:
1. The game is set at a fixed difficulty which is not too challenging. It's clearly designed for casual play. You might struggle if you're well-acquainted with grass, but most shouldn't have a tough time with it if you follow the normal progression. Even the final boss was pretty easy.

2. The game can be finished extremely quickly for an open-world RPG. 100%ed it in 11 hours (does not include DLC), which isn't as hard as it might sound because the game basically tells you where all the collectibles and side quests are. Very little exploration on your own is required. The map isn't particularly big either so you don't spend a lot of time travelling for nothing, which is a good thing.

3. The game has a constant wildlife documentary-style narrator who often adds in dry, tongue-in-cheek humour. His voice is nice to listen to and I found him funny at times, but I'd wager some people might get annoyed by him because he talks quite often

4. This is NOT a realistic shark game. Your gear will make you either colourful, be able to literally turn into electricity, or be able to launch balls of poison at people. There's a gear set that is basically armor and the least goofy looking, but it is pretty much the worst set to take

5. Story is meh. It's not bad but nothing unique or mindblowing. It's just some seasoning to complement the gameplay

6.

Cons:
1. Combat is rather one-dimensional. There's two parts to combat: marine combat with other wildlife, and human combat. Marine combat is basically either steamrolling the enemy because you're too powerful for it, or "dodge the enemy's charge attack and let it stun itself so you can attack it 3-4 times, back off, repeat". There's a few different types of hostile wildlife, but they all follow that basic attack pattern.

Human combat is slightly more interesting, but still repetitive. It mainly consists of attacking boats while the people on it shoot at you or drop dynamite. You can jump and bite people off the boat one at a time, or you can destroy the boat entirely which takes longer but takes out everyone on board, all while dodging attacks, which can hit pretty hard late game. Endgame enemies add in an electric field which HURTS. Unfortunately, it can be janky when trying to grab a hunter off a boat, and you might just bounce harmlessly off even if the person is unprotected. Another frustrating example is trying to take out the electric field on a boat, which needs you to grab something and tail whip it at the generator to break it, but then the game has a habit of aiming at anything except the generator, wasting your "ammo", time and health.

To offset this a little, the gear you unlock can add a bit more variety in your playstyle (e.g., ability to slow down time, electrify enemies, poison enemies), which is a good thing, but the buildcrafting is minor.

2. Some gear is tied to collectible completion, which can get boringly tedious. It's not hard to find the collectibles, but it's not the most engaging thing in the world. You are also greatly encouraged to go for them as they offer you XP and resources. It does force you to explore the game world and take in some interesting sites, but it still just boils down to fetch quests. That might sound cynical, but there's easily over 100 collectibles in the game. The tedium of it can easily get to you.

3. The Gameplay loop itself can get repetitive. The quests for each area are almost exactly the same: kill X miniboss/kill X number of humans/kill X number of prey. Once you complete some quests in a location, you get a quest to kill the boss of the location. This loop repeats for all areas in the game, with a boss fight with the game's primary antagonist midway through the game and at the end.

4. The side quests which require you to kill people gets annoying fast, because killing people anywhere will start a shark hunt and you'll get constant waves of boats coming in to kill you. Only way to get the hunt to end is to hide out of sight for a while. Killing hunters only progresses your "Wanted" status, which basically increases the lethality of hunters permanently, although some gear is tied to it. So either you interrupt your quest completion 2-3 times to hide, or you have to tank shots.

Conclusion
Overall an enjoyable and entertaining open-world game, well worth the $5 I paid for it. While far from the quality of AAA titles like RDR or Horizon, the charm of the game comes from its power fantasy and unique gameplay setting. It's good for a bit of casual fun but don't expect to be amazed by it.\t\t\t\t\t
