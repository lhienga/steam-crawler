\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun game but in the end isn't worth getting unless its on sale. For a sub 20 hour game, it's pretty tedious after the novelty wears off, which happens pretty quickly when you reach a high infamy level. The narration is excellent, and definitely a big redeeming factor. This review was originally a recommendation but the more I wrote the more I realized I wouldn't recommend this.

Pros
-Shark: It's genuinely pretty fun to swim around gnashing your teeth at people in unicorn floaties
-Eat People: You can flop around on land for a limited amount of time and eat people there too
-Narration: It's well written and well read. Not the funniest, but definitely a high point for the game

Cons
-Tedious combat: Combat is fun until you're doing it for the 100th time. Either due to bugs or oversight, using the bio-electric set made combat trivial due to stunlock. This goes for every aquatic creature, as far as I can tell. The only real difference as the game goes is you get attacked by more things at once, and are eventually forced to use the tailwhip to use projectiles
-Projectiles: God awful. Its very amusing when you find out you can grab stuff and tailwhip them into the sky. That amusement lasts until you actually try to hit something. You send it flying either straight where you're looking, or at the highlighted target WHICH YOU'VE GOT VIRTUALLY NO CONTROL OVER. This mechanic more or less becomes necessary in the DLC while also putting your intended target behind a dozen humans, all of whom you are much much much much more likely to target. Supposedly there is a button to help with this, but in my experience all pushing it does is slowly wear away at that key on my keyboard.
-Build versatility: The variety is "there" but each of the shark evolutions have specific uses that you'll be too annoyed to return to the grotto for. I found most of the organs to be useless as they're mostly niche use. A few are always helpful, the rest are not. Being unable to change them outside the grotto makes them extra useless. Oh hey I found a use for that organ I don't use! Just a quick swim back to the grotto and then back here! Or I could just do it without the organ since the benefit doesn't outweigh the time it takes to go swap.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got 50% off deal on this game. I'd justify this game for not more than 30 dollars. I've completed 100% completion within 29 hours.

Pro:
-Simple open world
-Fun upgrades
-Fun being a shark!

Con:
-Not much mechanical skills required. I just smash bite and sprint buttons most of fights. The DLC content bring a bit more fun mechanical skill, but so broken! It makes everything way too easy.
-Most fights against predators and infamy hunters are too easy.

Pro Tips:
-Utilize, upgrade, and abuse the Sonar ability asap. I was pretty lost and confused for a while till I understand that I gonna spam sonar on cooldown.
-On a DLC quest, you will need to destroy a tower. It was very frustrating till I figured that I gonna hold the bite when I whiptail the sailor toward tower. It became easy after that. So, what you will need to do is to bite a sailor, go back in water and head near shore (keep holding the bite button), jump high enough till you can see your target marker is on the tower (keep holding the bite button), whiplash the sailor toward the tower (keep holding bite button when you whiplash), and rinse n repeat from there for a few times!
- On a DLC quest, you will need to destroy a tower. Once you have the automatic body activation power, just use a few of that against tower. It is easy from there.\t\t\t\t\t
