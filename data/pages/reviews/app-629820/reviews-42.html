\r\n\t\t\t\t\t𝐃𝐢𝐯𝐞 𝐢𝐧𝐭𝐨 𝐚 𝐇𝐢𝐥𝐚𝐫𝐢𝐨𝐮𝐬 𝐚𝐧𝐝 𝐄𝐧𝐭𝐞𝐫𝐭𝐚𝐢𝐧𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐒𝐡𝐚𝐫𝐤 𝐑𝐏𝐆

Maneater is a refreshing and humorous take on the RPG genre that allows players to embrace their inner apex predator as a man-eating shark. With its unique gameplay mechanics, vibrant open world, and tongue-in-cheek narrative, Maneater delivers an entertaining and enjoyable experience for those seeking something different.

The core gameplay of Maneater revolves around controlling a growing shark, navigating the waters, and hunting down various prey. As you progress through the game, your shark evolves, gaining new abilities and becoming a more formidable predator. It's incredibly satisfying to terrorize unsuspecting humans, devour marine life, and engage in epic battles against other dangerous creatures of the sea.

One of the standout features of Maneater is its captivating open-world environment. The underwater world is beautifully crafted, featuring diverse marine ecosystems, sunken treasures, and hidden areas to explore. The attention to detail and vibrant visuals bring the underwater world to life, making each exploration feel exciting and immersive.

The game's humor and narrative also deserve special mention. Maneater doesn't take itself too seriously, offering a delightful and self-aware story told from the perspective of a documentary-style narrator. The witty commentary, amusing quests, and exaggerated characters add a layer of fun and lightheartedness to the gameplay experience.

Maneater's progression system provides a sense of accomplishment as you evolve your shark and unlock new abilities. From acquiring powerful jaws and specialized organs to enhancing your shark's overall strength and resilience, the sense of growth and empowerment is satisfying and motivates further exploration and gameplay.

However, Maneater does have a few drawbacks to consider. The gameplay mechanics can become repetitive over time, with a lack of variety in mission types and objectives. Additionally, while the open-world environment is visually appealing, it may feel slightly limited in terms of overall size and available activities.

In conclusion, Maneater offers a unique and entertaining experience as you take on the role of a fearsome shark. Its humorous narrative, vibrant open world, and satisfying progression system make it a standout title in the RPG genre. While some repetitive gameplay elements and limitations in the open world may be present, Maneater's overall charm, wit, and sheer joy of being a man-eating shark make it a game worth diving into. Embrace your predatory instincts, explore the depths, and enjoy the hilariously wild ride that is Maneater.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tManeater is a game i've had on my radar since it was announced and with a recent sale I took the opportunity to buy it and try to 100% it. I'm happy to announce mission successful. Now for the review.

Maneater is a beautiful game with an enthralling story that give a brilliantly satirical take on mankind's destruction of the natural world whilst keeping the user entertained by letting them kill everything in sight on your shark's revenge trip. The bosses are fair and missions don't feel like a grind. Overall the main game is very quick and can be completed leisurely in well under 24 hours.

However where this game does take a turn is in the dlc. In my opinion it is not worth buying if you aren't aiming to 100% the game. Although the dlc story is itself well fleshed out and fun and the dlc island is an interesting change of pace there are a myriad of issues with the dlc content: Firstly is the lag/stutters. Even though my PC's components aren't even close to being maxed and the main game ran fine on high settings, anything to do with the dlc caused an unholy amount of screen freezes which ruined the gameplay for me. This included a massive delay when opening the map (something you do quite often). It got to the point where i had to pause and leave several times during the final boss battle as the laggy nature of the battle made it 50x more difficult than it needed to be which i presume wasn't the intention.
Lastly and this is just my personal criticism but the dlc narrator and his rants pissed me the ♥♥♥♥ off. I enjoyed the sarcastic witty tone he had in the main story but his lunatic plot was just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ annoying, so much so that i just straight turned it off for the last 4 hours of my playthough.

Overall a very well made game that i would recommend.
Main game score: 9.5/10
DLC: 6/10 when not annoying the ♥♥♥♥ out of me, 2,5/10 when lagging (WHICH IS MOST OF THE TIME)

OVERALL: 7.1/10 solid game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Simple and fun" is how lot of games should be and this one is a bright example. It has a ludonarrative mechanism that feels so appropriate and well put - a constant need to grow and evolve makes you collect all the resources like mad, which makes you feel like a hungry shark, always on a prowl. Enemies also up the ante, adding mutations to their abilities and make you wonder of "what will they think of next?" Humans are less imaginative, but that is to be expected. Combat gets too hectic and chaotic by the end, but before it becomes irritating. Time trials are the least amount of fun, you need to put on all of the speed-related mutations to get through.

It's been a pleasure and a sequel would be welcomed.\t\t\t\t\t
