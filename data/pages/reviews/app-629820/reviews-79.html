\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI 100% Maneater on PS4 sometime ago before it arrived on steam (screw Epic and it's stupid *** launcher) and I love this game to bits.

Have you ever played Jaws Unleashed on the PS2? For those who don't know it's based on the franchise Jaws, and you control the Great White going from place to place completing missions in an open world.

Maneater takes this concept and turns it up to 11, the RPG and upgrade elements Jaws had is much more indepth, the world is beautiful and has a lot more interesting areas compared to Jaws, the humour is fantastic, more prey and animals to fight, and is just a really fun time.

I can't really say anymore, Maneater is a simple but extremely fun adventure and I highly recommend it if you want to try something new or just wanna cause havoc as a Shark.

Only complaints I had from playing this and the PS4 is that certain tricks can be used to give yourself a monstrous advantage at the start, for an example the spin attack you do while in the air or surface of the water does damage but also makes you untargetable, I used to this to reach Infamy 10 very early and very easily, giving me rewards helping me out big time in the game, I managed to do this as a Teen Shark on PC, while I did it as an Adult Shark on PS4, and with certain upgrades, it makes it even easier.
I haven't got this section yet onto the Steam version, but on the PS4 version in Prosperity Sands (I think it's called that) a large area with huge buildings and lots of NPCs, caused the game to absolutely chug, I mean I still managed to beat the area, but the FPS would drop to like 10-20 often due to the sheer size of the map, however with my PC being much stronger than a PS4, I don't think I'll have this issue when I get there later, I'll update this review to say if I do or not, because it might be the game itself struggling loading the amount of stuff and not my PC.

*Update* It still lags once and a while, but it's nowhere as bad, however one thing I forgot to mention is that the game crashes sometimes in this area on the PS4 version, same thing happened here, thankfully the game saves so often and quickly that losing progress isn't a problem 99% of the time.

Also this game without a doubt has some of the best Steam Points Store Items, Emoticons and Profile Badges on the whole platform, so if you like collecting those types of bits, this will be an excellent edition to your collection, the avatars, frames and Steam Badges are my personal favourites.

Overall a very fun game, highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove this developer for their work on the Rising Storm franchise - decided to give Maneater a try due to the unique premise where you get to play as a freaking shark.

Maneater is a technical mess; I first played it on release and the last mission bugged crashing the game deleting all my save data. Uninstalled in a fit of rage because I was too pissed off to start over.

Coming back to it now almost a year later the game is STILL full of bugs - your save data could get corrupted deleting your progress, there are constant crashes, the framerate is horrible, there is no manual save function, custom waypoint markers are glitchy...the problems here are numerous and there's been seemingly no attempt to fix them.

The story is dumb and campy but in a good way - Scaley Pete is an endearing cartoonish villain and I love his cajun accent. Too bad you spend most of the campaign mashing the bite button - this allows you to consume other fish or humans for materials which in turn let you to upgrade your shark. Overall combat is shallow and there's no real challenge or skill involved.

It's hard for me to give this a thumbs down because I do have legitimate fun with the game and I absolutely love the development team but the technical issues and repetitive gameplay make this hard to recommend to most people.\t\t\t\t\t
