\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[h]The Good:[/h1]

Jerry Smith as Narrator - Hilarious Fun: First things first, if you've snagged Maneater in a bundle sale, consider yourself lucky! The cherry on top of this aquatic adventure is none other than Jerry Smith as the narrator. His commentary had me in stitches throughout the game. He adds a whole new level of humor and charm that you just can't find elsewhere.

[h]The Not-So-Good:[/h1]

DLC Dilemma - A Bite to the Wallet: Now, here's where things get a bit murky. If you're thinking about picking up the DLC separately, you might want to think twice. The pricing for these extra bits of content is pretty much swimming in the same waters as the original map, but with a much smaller area to explore. It feels a bit like taking a nibble of your favorite snack and paying the same price as a full meal. Not the best deal in the ocean.

In conclusion, Maneater is a sharky sensation that's a blast to play, especially if you grab it in a bundle sale. Jerry Smith's narration adds a layer of comedic brilliance that elevates the whole experience. However, when it comes to the DLC, be cautious about biting off more than you can chew financially. It's a bit pricey for what you get in return. So, while I'd highly recommend diving into the main game, you might want to think twice about splurging on the DLC separately. Happy swimming, fellow gamers!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game did something for me that few games do nowadays, perhaps because I'm a gamer of older generation: it kept me hooked until I did every single trophy, around the 23 hours mark.

I have to be honest: I loved the game! I really did. I was a pup shark, which kept growing into this behemoth of the sea, the one and only true apex predator, the Mega Shark!

The original game is really well done, graphics are beautiful and the game ran smooth as butter on my r7 5700x, RTX 3070, 32 gigs of RAM. You get a lot of activities, which you do, uncovering the areas and leveling up, and of course, ALWAYS eating every creature unfortunate enough to run across your path. The main villain is also really well done.

Than... there's the DLC. I just don't know about that, I'm afraid. They added more of the same, however the main villain is now a generic NWO organization and the new things they added, consisting of time trials, crushing towers, looking for tinfoil head men, just feel out of place. I don't believe time trials, which demand of you to do some quite hard jumps, are something most people would enjoy.

The last boss isn't as much hard as it is annoying, especially in phase 2, where he calls all other DLC bosses, which you have already beaten, to his aid.

SO yes, I recommend buying this game, absolutely, just keep in mind that the DLC is hardly an improvement.\t\t\t\t\t
