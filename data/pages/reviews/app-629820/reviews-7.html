\r\n\t\t\t\t\tManeater only eats your time.

DISCLAIMER: THIS REVIEW DOES NOT EVALUATE THE ENTIRE GAME DUE TO A DEVELOPER KNOWN FOUR YEAR OLD SAVE DELETION BUG THAT HAS NOT AND WILL NOT EVER BE FIXED. THIS REVIEW WILL BE SHORTER AND LESS IN-DEPTH TO MATCH THAT LEVEL OF CARE FOR THE GAME.

Story
There isn't one. You play as a baby shark that swims around eating things and growing until some credits role. Then you do it again.

Gameplay
The game involves swimming around and mashing the bite button on one to twelve creatures, then swimming somewhere else and doing that again. This is the entire game. There is a basic dodge button, a mostly useless tail whip move, and a small pile of body upgrade and evolutions that somewhat change how the shark kills things. There are also tons of meaningless collectibles to waste your time hunting down. The game is tedious, mundane, and does not respect your time even if the save file system did work properly.

Presentation
Sound is repetitive and character models/animations are painfully low quality, but the world itself is quite pretty. The narrator's jokes are never groan inducing but rarely pull more than a chuckle when at their best. The game can randomly reset your save profile to a 0% newly created file, deleting all of your progress.\t\t\t\t\t
