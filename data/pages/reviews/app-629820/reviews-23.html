\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe obvious sexism/misogyny of the game’s title aside it’s actually a decent experience. Although the gameplay is very repetitive and increasing infamy is a horrendous grind, it managed to keep me engaged for the majority of it. The leveling system, perks, and gameplay mechanics encourage the player to continue by providing a sense of progression. Even gathering collectibles was entertaining since it was kept to a healthy dosage and didn’t require looking up much of a guide. What could use a bit of tweaking is everything centered around out-of-water gameplay. I understand that it’s intentionally made to be difficult to navigate outside of water given the theme of the game, however, it becomes infuriating when half the missions take you out of water. Either to devour humans, gather a collectible, or even jump across an obstacle for a time trial. It’s vastly inconsistent and reaching high places is incredibly tedious.

Something else that slowly got on my nerves is the narrator. I didn’t mind it at first, it’s actually a clever way of filling an acoustic void that the game creates by having it take place underwater. But having the narrator comment on everything you do and not even shutting up for a minute truly drives me mad. At the very least they didn’t pick an annoying voice to accommodate it. It began grinding my gears during the Truth Quest DLC where his entire presence just serves to mock conspiracy theorists. We get it, people with tinfoil hats should be wearing a restraining jacket to match their attire. Now please shut up and let me finish this game already. A minor nitpick I would like to add is how the humans during gameplay cutscenes lack facial movement and are generally animated weirdly.

Let’s address the elephant in the room, a game like this shouldn’t have a base price of 35€ when it merely took me 11 hours to acquire all achievements within both the main game and the DLC. The game itself is just too repetitive and doesn’t offer much variation. The main objective of this game is to grind. And while the approach had me hooked, it doesn’t justify such a price tag. I personally don’t hold a major grudge against the developers for being openly sexist/misogynist with their game's title. It may have been a marketing stunt altogether to boost publicity and potentially gain a bigger player base. Regardless of that, the game itself is an above-mediocre experience. Having the game be called ‘Humaneater’ wouldn’t have changed what we have.\t\t\t\t\t
