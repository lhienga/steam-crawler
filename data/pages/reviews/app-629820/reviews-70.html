\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoofy and humorous game, but often repetitive and a bit clunky to control.

The narrative is pretty interesting, styled in an episodic "shark week" show. You're a terror of the seas, munching on anything you can find and being an utter bully to humanity. It's a fun concept, and there's no shortage of power once you become an adult: no-one can stop you as you bring down boats with ease, and crocodiles you once avoided are now like lunch meat. The narrator is comedically narcissistic, and always has some kind of quip for various events. Each region has its own style, showing things that benefit the overall narrative of overhunting and carelessness, making you feel more like you are meant to be here; you're a hand of justice.

Unfortunately, the feeling of being a threat quickly wore off as it became obvious most missions were the same "kill this" quests, using existing fish with little to no variation on them for single targets. Human bosses aren't all that memorable despite the unique styles they were given, and it makes the anticlimax for them weaker than the game wants them to be. Coupled with an XP system, the mood to rise to power quickly became lost on me as I found myself repeating the same quest in the game's different regions. It likes to hold your hand when it comes to moving on to new areas, forcing you to do the same set of tasks before you can progress. The side quests suffer the same thing, and while the bonuses are a nice little hunt, the license plates are usually the same "jump in the air a few times" challenges.

While the writing and visuals are great, I feel like the gameplay certainly weakens the theme it's attempting to portray. It wants you to feel significant, and yet you don't really feel like you're going anywhere with the repetition.\t\t\t\t\t

With enough skill, one can take on foes many times their own and sometimes win, though not without difficulty.
Overall, this is a 9.8/10. Wouldn't have it any other way.\t\t\t\t\t
