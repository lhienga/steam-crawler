I'm a Shark, I swim non-stop, When people Hunt me - I do Hop!
Awesome, Boring, WOW!
Maneater starts great - a baby shark in a hostile swamp, has to eat, stock up on resources, escape from lethal alligator and grow. Killing doesn't come easy - everything tries to kill you! Pikes, reptiles, sharks... Retreating is acceptable.
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3002309190
Luckily, there's plenty of fish, turtles and swimming people, who don't fight back. Adulthood comes quickly. This is where repetitive game takes over.

One button Gameplay
To attack and consume enemies you click a button multiple times. Missions often require you to devour 10 people or eliminate 1 big target. So you click a lot. You eat, increase in sizes (by clicking-clicking), swimming from one bog to cleaner waters, and do so right until the end of the game. Click-click-click.

Fun Times!
Eat too many bipedal beings and you're a naughty shark :3
Everyone hunts you, in your very own water area! Divers follow you in sewers, gunners pinpoint from boats, they even have choppers and tesla boats!
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3003060931
Hopefully, you've been eating healthy and plenty. Your appetite pays back: you can update (evolve) shark's body parts and choose form of health, damage, abilities. You literally change fins, tails, heads and body as lego constructor!

Experiments with new physique is satisfying! Nuclear beam, electric bites and slaps, boulder slams, oof, water fights are turning into fun pits! Did I say you can grab humans or bombs and throw them back at boats and helicopters? Yes, you can!

Final locations are huge. They harbour bigger and deadlier creatures. There are beautiful drowned monuments and landscapes to swim by. And best bosses to challenge too.

Best Easter Eggs
Maneater has many points of interests. Each is a reference to either a movie or a book. Another underwater pleasures to discover.
