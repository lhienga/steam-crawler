\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA shark RPG that is really enjoyable and entertaining. Like what some of the reviews are saying, it doesn't take itself seriously (story wise) but the game-play is good except for the occasional bugs (extremely rare) that will crash your game. Even with the game crash you still retain your progress up to the point of the crash (most of the time).

Base Game:

The vanilla game itself is where the majority of the content can be seen and accessed. The plot and story is good and the mockumentary style of story telling is definitely hilarious. As you progress, like a true rpg experience, you grow and gain access to various parts and mutations that will help you muddle along. Mix and mash or go for a pure set build is completely up to you.

DLC (Truth Quest)

Here is where things get more absurd. In every sense of the word. The story kinda makes sense but the shift in tone does take getting used to. The uber strong enemies, both humans and wildlife, can be a pain unless you unlock some of the newer stuff (which is a definite MUST get ASAP). Not that you have to use them but it definitely makes the newer enemies and content easier to deal with. You get added game missions and all new bells and whistles but the game-play mostly resembles the base game so whether to purchase it or not is up to you. Personally, I recommend getting the DLC for the new shark set and mutations.

Summary:

The game is excellent but at the end of it all, you don't get to do anything anymore once you complete all the missions and side quests. Unless a new DLC comes out that is. Great game and great vibes. Happy prowling XD\t\t\t\t\t

MANEATER

Finally, a proper game that you play as a shark!

I almost got only good positive things to say about MANEATER. Gameplay is very fun, visuals are really great, sound design is impeccable. The levels are separated by zones where you have many objectives which ends up being, EAT, EVOLVE, REPEAT. See, you are a Bull Shark which is why you can go in the Bayou zone, filled with fresh water. The Bull Shark is the most aggressive shark in the world and I found that fitting for this game. The way that you evolve and mutate enhances the gameplay and enables you to fight bigger threats as well.

The game controls takes some getting used to but once you master them, you will be one hell of a perfect killing machine!


PROS:
+Great Graphics! The undersea is very well done! Each zones offer some new sea floor to just swim around.
+Gameplay is very fun. The evolve mechanic makes the game even better.
+Killer sounds! Especially the human crunching!
+Challenging at first, until you level up and evolve more so you can hunt about anything in the sea.
+Story is minimalist but get's the job done.
+Shark controls is pretty good.
+Perfect Ultrawide monitor support.


CONS:
-Epic Game Store exclusivity. It sucks and I hate that... BUT... I got the game here on Steam around 6 months later and working properly.
-At first, the game is punishing until you level up ASAP and evolve some mutations. It is challenging also later in the game where you gotta pick the right mutations for specific fights.
-The objectives can become repetitive. Eat 10 turtles here, consume 10 humans there... in all zones.
-Slow levelling up for the INFAMY. Takes a while to level up by creating chaos and having hunters chasing you... then once you reach proper "chaos level", a special bounty hunter comes to you and after you kill the hunter, you gain a mutation.


TRUTH QUEST DLC:
This DLC opens up a new zone where the fights are harder. You go against NWO soldiers and again, mutate and level up more! You gain the ATOMIC mutations which are very powerful and needed for the last APEX boss fight. Well, maybe not required but highly recommended though! Besides that, you get some more copied-pasted objectives exactly like the main game but are scattered in the new zone. After you progress far in the DLC, you have new objectives unlocking in the main areas. You can now do some "shark racing" too in the many zones and that is where it teaches you how to master the shark controls! Honestly, I only recommend this DLC if you loved the main game and don't mind repeating the same objectives. The DLC can add at least 10 more hours of gameplay.


OVERALL:
I really enjoyed this game, even with the repetitiveness of objectives. That can become tiresome but you do gain evolution points for your mutations. So it is not for nothing! I'd truly play a sequel to this game and I hope one get's made eventually. I didn't experienced any technical issues with the game. All working good until the very end!


RECOMMENDATION:
Oh Yes, I recommend this game. It is very fun! You pick it up someday and you will enjoy it if that is your sort of things! Or give it a try on XBOX GamePass!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tManeater is a 2020 action role-playing game developed by Tripwire Interactive and co-published with Deep Silver. In Maneater, you play a female bull shark out to get revenge for your mothers killers while exploring the open (sea) world and completing its various quests and hunt for collectibles.

TL; DR You play as an angry shark in an ocean open world and get to eat everything in your way. Its loads of fun on top of having a keen eye for detail.

Story (3/5): Played out from the perspective of a documentary, players assume the role of a female bull shark that was brutally separated from her mother at birth and now seeks revenge against the shark hunter responsible. Along the way, you will climb up the ranks ascending to greater levels of shark maturity and face tougher opponents. Cutscenes placed at the end of each chapter help to flesh out the story from the shark hunters perspective. Meanwhile, the game/documentarys narration is provided in real-time by the excellent Chris Parnell.

Gameplay (5/5): Maneater has a large sprawling open world map in the form of a large body of water with the occasional land dwelling. Transitioning between land and sea is surprisingly seamless. Role playing elements come in the form of upgrading your shark by way of mutations. While enemies you face range from ocean predators to increasingly difficult shark hunters. Side quests come in the form of hunting humans/sea creatures and obtaining collectibles. They help progress the main campaign which helps to not feel like a chore to do. Highlights of the campaign include the Apex Predator quests and the hunt for Landmarks around the open world.

Controls (4/5): With a control scheme that feels like a hybrid between an aeroplane and a submarine, Maneaters shark controls do that a fair bit of time getting used to. Playing with a controller is highly recommended due to the finer axis of controls provided by the analogue sticks. Once you get the hang of things though, this shark handles like a finely tuned machine.

Graphics (5/5): Next to the audio, this is one of Maneaters stand out points. You can tell that the team put a lot of effort and research into getting every detail of the ocean world right. From the animation of the shark in motion to the refraction of the light as it breaks the oceans surface, Maneater is a stunning game to behold. The art style is still heavily stylized but, there are some moments (especially in underwater tunnels) where the game is shockingly photo realistic.

Audio (5/5): From Chris Parnells excellent narration to the visceral sound effects when your crunch down on a turtle, Maneater promises aural satisfaction. More than superficial satisfaction, the audio ques are pivotal in dodging potentially deadly attacks in the more difficult boss fights. This is one title I would recommend playing on speakers especially if you have competent setup with a powerful subwoofer that can really emphasize the lows and bass.

Issues/Optimization: For a PC game that looks this good in 2020, Maneater is very well optimized both in terms of frame rate and game stability. Bugs are very far and few in between with only 2 instances throughout my 21 hours playthrough having game breaking bugs.

Overall (4.5/5): One of the hidden gems of 2020 game releases, Maneater is a true labour of love by developers Tripwire Interactive who have done a fantastic job of whats perhaps the only open world shark simulator to date. Highly recommended!
