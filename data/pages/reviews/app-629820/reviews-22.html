\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI purchased Maneater Apex Edition at a discount (about 18$). I completed the base game in just under 10 hours (with prior playtime knowledge, casual) and finished the DLC at about 12 hours.

I had already played Maneater a few years ago through Xbox's Game Pass, so I had an early grasp on the game's combat and controls. The game lets you play as a shark with all of the abilities one can have (and more) such as swimming, knifing (swimming on the surface), biting, eating, and thrashing. Most of the game revolves around you biting whatever you come across, be it a fish, turtle, human, or piece of garbage you find across the many areas of Port Clovis. You can also use a tail whip to knock back opponents and smack them with a large chunk of your body, but most of the time this doesn't work out well unless you already have them in your jaws.

The Story follows a Bull shark after her mother is killed by the main antagonist/protagonist Scaly Pete. You learn more about him through the Television show "Maneater," which the whole game centers around. Everything you do is captured by an underwater camera that's being narrated by the show's narrator. It's basically a parody of those southern-hunting shows (Swamp People, sort-of Deadliest Catch, etc.), with a ton of humor mocking how we treat wildlife and the lifestyle of those who live in the south near the Gulf (mostly Florida, though Louisiana could also fit the bill). As a Floridian, the jokes and humor were pretty funny and the character caricatures were on-point with the shows it's mocking. If you aren't a fan of this setting or those types of TV shows, I can easily see the jokes and setting to become increasingly annoying as you play.

The last part of the game are the unlockables. The base game has three evolutions, which act as cosmetic parts to your shark that give them a boost in power. The most useful I found was the Bone set, which makes attacking highly defensive enemies, most notably boats of bounty hunters, really easy and satisfying to do. The Electric and shadow set are good for other sea creature fights that allow them to deal chip damage over time. I would have liked more evolution sets to collect, but the base game's sets are fun to use.

The New content I hadn't experienced before was the Truth Quest DLC. I was interested in it when playing the prologue quests back when the base game was on Game Pass, so I spent the few extra dollars when the game was on sale and bought the Apex edition with it included. Most reviews seem to pan it harshly due to the low content and high base price. At 15$, the DLC needs to expand the game enough to feel worth it. In the beginning, I found the new location and early quests interesting and new tone of the "Maneater" show a bit funny. But over time the jokes and witty dialog begin to dip in quality usually falling within the same few joke types (government coverup, military is bug people, etc.) that began to get on my nerves. The latter half of the DLC also forces you back to Port Clovis backtracking to the other locations you've already explored to do the same 3-4 tasks with no real explanation as to why other than that you're tracking some government-created creature you want to kill because you do. I know the writing for the game wasn't very deep, but the base game had an easy to follow concept: Scaly Pete killed your Mom, so it's now time to get revenge, ruining his life every time you meet. Here we have no other characters than the narrator, who now has gone into madness after learning of these conspiracies making the same 5 points when he opens his mouth.
As far as positives go, the DLC does give you a cool new atomic set that comes with tail whip blasts and atomic breath when powered up, which comes in handy during some of the DLC missions. The Final Boss is also pretty tough but incredibly satisfying to beat.
For 15$, the DLC is pretty crummy. If it were post-game content, I would praise the game for giving players stuff to do beyond collecting all of the collectables after the credits roll. Unfortunately that's not the case, so for 15$ this isn't a part of the game I would say is necessary. My purchase price of 18$ is well worth it alone for the base game, so I don't feel cheated by this added content.

Altogether, Maneater is a game I highly recommend for those who just want to see how much carnage a Bull Shark can do. I eagerly await a sequel or continuation of some kind where you can play as multiple underwater creatures or even just a different main creature like the Alligator or Barracuda.\t\t\t\t\t
