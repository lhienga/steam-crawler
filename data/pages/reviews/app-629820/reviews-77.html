The evolutions/mutations are interesting and provide some variety of gameplay, but overall you'll just be following a path to an objective with a world full of filler content (Consume 10 Sailors / Kill this Animal / Destroy this Ship).

So maybe get it if you truly want to experience the old school "small fish grows to be the biggest fish and eats everything" kinda arcade games, but otherwise I can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

Overall a great game! Outstanding graphics which I enjoyed greatly as the scenery is fantastic. Not to mention the eargasmic sounds of human screams and yummy crunching noises.

My only issue throughout 100%-ing the game is how much targeting munchies relies on camera view which results in various annoyances such as constant jittering, and the shark losing focus on an intended snack, especially when surrounded by a f**ng deadly buffet. Might have been only me, but I think it could have been improved by 1.) being able to actually lock on and focus on a specific snack, especially during boss fights (e.g., bounty hunters in Truth Quest), and or 2.) have a mechanic such that a focused foodie, like a boat, can be focused by the camera so that we can properly monitor the attacks of the deadly food, and avoid having to turn around for unbearable amounts.

All in all, a fantastic game! As someone also said in their review, I also bought this on sale when it was afterwards free on Epic Games dang. Nonetheless it is worth paying (maybe on sale) and playing for (granted you have time to grind).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnjoyable, but extremely short and very repetitive.

Overall it's a nice, easy-going game, the narration is very good, it looks pretty, sounds decent, but there isn't much to do.
The main quest is about 5 hours, and in my 15 hours I completed both the base game and the DLC to 100%, and it's not like I was speedrunning it or wanted to get it over with. Even on current 50% sale that's 25.63 euros for 15 hours of gameplay.

There is no difficulty setting, very easy overall. The only difficult fight I had in the game was going against a lvl 8 enemy right at the beginning when I was lvl 2-3. But soon after that nothing can stop you. The DLC does provide some challenge, but again: not much.

The evolutions (special parts that you can add to your shark) are fine, but since there's no difficulty they're not relevant enough. They're nothing mindblowing either. The problem is that your main damage is your bite against most enemies, and you get that right at the beginning. This means that besides small increases to your bite damage, damage resistance or movement speed, you won't really get anything exciting or useful.
Nothing even close to what you experience in Evolve or Prototype. In fact if you like this evolution thing and killing stuff in open world, then you're better off with Prototype.. they're probably cheaper as well.
