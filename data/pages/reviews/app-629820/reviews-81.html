\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game - you get to play as a shark, starting from a newborn pup to a massive megaladon evolution, over the course of a straightforward (yet humorously satirical) story following the shark hunter that killed your mother and scarred you. Over the course of the game you unlock different areas of the local seal, new evolutions to equip (and upgrade), and generally be a terrible aquatic monster.

The main drawback to the game is that it's kind of a one-and-done deal: it's not extremely difficult to 100% the game, and there's no real branching paths/alternative choices. Once you've played the game once you've likely seen everything it has to offer.

Graphically the game is beautiful, and the controls are solid. It does have some annoying elements when it comes to how the shark behaves while jumping (especially to get some things very high out of the water and/or on land), but in the water everything handles well.

While not a must-have, if you like sharks, the sea and being the apex predator, definitely worth picking up on sale!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I say anything, I got 100% in this game

Surprisingly fun game! I can't point it out, but there is something satisfying about playing as an apex predator try to grow, evolve, and take revenge on someone who murdered your mother shark. The commentary from the narrator is also fun and adds a lot of flavour to the game. Bosses are challenging, and doable with a help of upgrades. The rest of the game becomes a multiple fetch quests with 100%ing the areas, but it does not get boring. Fetch quest let you grow and unlock more evolutions. On the down side, progress meter to summon bosses is very slow in a main game and faster in dlc, which should be implemented in the main game.

In my mind, dlc is unnecessary. I gives you one of the most important evolution - hp regeneration. It's more of the same flavour. and an increase of the level cap by 5.

I have a ton of fun playing this game, upgrading, and challenging new bosses. Very fun!

P.S. to get 100% achievements in this game is easy, but you will have to 100% all collectables. There are a lot of collectables, but it's not as boring as some other games thanks to funny narrator.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, I've thoroughly enjoyed this game. The narration is humorous and done by Chris Parnell, the same voice actor who does Cyril Figgis on the animated TV show Archer.

The game play is a little repetitive at times in the beginning. There isn't much to unlock in the way of attack moves/combos. So far, I only have the basic bite, tail whip, whip shot (you bite onto something, whip your tail and shoot your prey like a projectile which is hard to aim), and then thrash/chomp (which holds onto enemy and does repeated damage for a period of time).

You can and must go onto dry land at times...which is quite challenging to do at this point mostly because of the controls (I'm using a controller on the PC).

All in all, the physics, story and game play have kept me quite engaged and I imagine will continue to do so for some time to come. Swimming through the various watery depths is very enjoyable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is oddly optimized. Set shadows to "High" unless you want to never break 60FPS. The music is good, the sound effects are fantastic. Attacking, and killing, things feels very satisfying. The environments are nice, even though you spend 90% of your time submerged, and the water effects are top notch. I live in the south and the bayou almost perfectly captures what it's like to be underwater in the nasty, tea coloured waters here. Refuse included.

I've not gotten far (Dead Horse Lake as of writing this) but I absolutely 100% love this game. This is the game I've been waiting for but never knew existed.

I'm terrified of the ocean. I have diagnosed Thalassophoibia. I cannot be in water that I cannot see to the bottom of (Even if it's murky but shallow water, i'm not doing it) I'm also terrified of sharks. All sharks, with the exception of maybe Lemon Sharks and Nurse Sharks.

But there's something about sharks that just absolutely enthralls me. They're so graceful and extremely intelligent and their mouths are so full of teeth. I think my awe of sharks comes from my fear of them.

So with all of that considered, you can't imagine how fun it is to play as the one thing you're most afraid of in life.

Thank you, TripWire.\t\t\t\t\t
