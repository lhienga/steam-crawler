\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey added Denuvo (anti-piracy software) to the game that verifies all the game assets every time you enter a new area that causes lag so bad that it actually slows down your computer and even freezes up your browser (if you have one open to play music in the background). Its by far the most invasive anti-piracy software I ever had the misfortune of dealing with.

While it is a fun game, the anti-piracy software is so invasive with the lag it causes that it honestly soured the game for me to the point that I'm just not gonna play it on PC anymore.
At the time of this review, I have an RTX 3080 and an Intel Core i9 so I know its not a matter that my hardware.

I would say wait for them to remove the software before getting this game or get it on console when its on sale so you don't end up paying full price (like me) for a game burdened with bloatware that makes it barely playable to the point that it can potentially cause harm to your computer since (as far as I'm aware) the console versions aren't hampered with Denuvo.

The game also inexplicably crashes. I can't really even give you a reason as for why beyond probably also being tied to Denuvo.

I would talk about how satisfying the shark mechanics are in the game but, with almost 17 hours of play time, the lag is honestly too bad for me to even list you any pros about the game beyond the first area (the bayou) since the lag will only become glaringly apparent once you unlock more areas in the game.

From what I've read about online, the game wasn't always laggy and in fact actually ran smoothly back in 2021.
With that said, I will say again do NOT buy this game on Steam or even Epic. Get it on console so you won't have to restart your first playthrough like I have to.\t\t\t\t\t
