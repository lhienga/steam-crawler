\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTownscaper is cheap, relaxing and unusual in a good way. You have very limited control, yet can create something quite beautiful.

However, the lack of control (for me at least) means quickly running into limitations. You can see from other player's screenshots that there are other features beyond the simplistic little 'cottage' houses however with idea how to create them and no control to guide the game to make them it's quite limited. I've found a few of those features, but with the limited control you're relying on the game to delight you with these little surprises, and very quickly it's become formulaic with a mix of frustration that I can't get it to create what I'm looking for, and boredom that nothing new is happening.

It's irritatingly static in the graphics too, whilst yes it responds dynamically to your changes, once you stop it all stops. I'd love to see people walk through the town, twee little fishing boats sail through the water, but no. As charming as the graphics are, it's beautiful, but it's held back from being a true masterpiece.

I'd like to say "it's only worth buying if you like this sort of thing" but it's too unique to draw a comparison to other games, so I'll say probably worth it if you don't mind throwing a fiver away for a couple of hours entertainment.\t\t\t\t\t
