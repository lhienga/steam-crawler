\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuiet little game, fun to make a coastal town, brick snake, or floating castle. This is a great game (or app) for wall papers, for a quick detox/stress reliever, and a fun way to get rid of your creative jitters. It's limiting, but also the beauty from the game's simplicity is part of it's fundamental value.

The game is worth 6 bucks, or a few candy bars from the gas station, or a Wendy's biggie bag, it's nothing extraordinary for the price, but that being said it's fun, relaxing, and a great outlet for creative expression. It's satisfyingly even, but yet chaotic enough as not to cause any stress. The game could always use more variation in it's generation, building types, and floor decor, but overall it's style and repetitiveness won't bore.

I wish the game had more life maybe movement inside the windows or small cars, and figures roaming the roads, even an occasional gardener attending his rained on flowers.
I hope that more building variants can come from strategic placement of your quate brick building blocks. I love the clock towers, flying machines, and light houses, if only we had more. More creativity would portrude from this.
The grid is ever changing, but having more options, for example, just a simple plain square grid would be a nice mix up from the normal hexagonal-like landscape.

This is all easier said than done, and providing suggestions through reviews is unorthodox, but it may give some insights to how the game starts to age and stale after awhile.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTownscaper is a solid sandbox building game about erecting little coastal villages. It's a neat game to mess around with different town designs. The look of buildings like the roofs, additional hooks, windows, doors, clotheslines, and greenery changes based on the way you place adjacent buildings. The game is set on a wonky grid allowing for more 'natural' and uneven shaped structures. While messing around with the limited color pallet and the different designs that come from placing these buildings can be fun and suck you in, there are several things missing from this game which stop me from recommending it for many. I found the game disappointing because there wasn't really any soundtrack aside from one song in the credits, and while the splish-splashing noises and other sound effects in the game make it not totally barren I found the soundscape to be lacking. The game also felt pretty lifeless. There are some birds that flock atop your buildings but there aren't any fish or people to populate the rest of the game. In addition the game is aimless. With no objectives or challenges to complete there is not much to do except make your own fun, and for most there's only so long you can mess around without a goal before getting bored. A final aside; I couldn't figure out how to make the floating islands which was very frustrating. While the building component can be enjoyable it could be more fleshed out, the game lacks music and a more lively world, so all in all the game is about a 6/10.\t\t\t\t\t
