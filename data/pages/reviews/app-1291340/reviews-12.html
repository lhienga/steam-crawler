\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery casual. No pressure. Easy to build beautiful structures. You find your own goals. Variety starts running out after an hour or so, but in total there's likely at least 4 hours of fun. However, if you have a VR headset (Quest 2, specifically), that version is much better.

The VR version is much more intuitive and faster to control. With the pancake version, you control the camera translation and rotation separately. Its very awkward. With an hour of practice maybe you can reach the level of fluency that you can reach within seconds with VR. Block placement/deletion is also awkward, because you can only place blocks on surfaces that you can see (or delete blocks that you can see), unlike in VR where you can just move your hand to the 3D position and add/delete. Often Ill need to add support blocks then delete them later, or do tedious workarounds to hollow out structures. On average, actions in pancake takes around 3 times longer than in VR, causing frustration unless youre really patient. Block placement/deletion is also snappier in VR. On my M1 Mac, theres a 200ms delay between mouse click and visual change, whereas in VR it feels near instant.

The VR version also has 2~4 times higher 3D feel. The pancake version feels incredibly flat, especially when still. The VR version is also better for positioning yourself for a first-person view of your world, which makes it feel like youre really inside the city.

However, the pancake version has somewhat better graphics (higher resolution, better shadows), and you can take super hi-res screenshots, as well as share builds with other people and output 3D model files, and the maximum build volume is about 10 times larger.\t\t\t\t\t
