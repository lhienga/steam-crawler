\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like the concept of this game but I feel like it could use some quality of life changes, and it has a lot more potential that is currently under used. I also have some extra suggestions that I just think would be neat.

These suggestions are in order of what I think is most important.

MOST IMPORTANT

Drag to place:
You could quickly place tiles in the same layer by clicking and dragging, and it would place tiles following your cursor. You could hold shift and drag up and it would place blocks in the same column. Maybe you could hold tab or enter and you could quickly place a "square" (because of the irregular tile shapes it wouldn't be a perfect square) of tiles on one layer. Like holding left click and selecting multiple icons at once on your desk top. I'm not super sure how to describe it but I hope that gets my point across. Currently for me it feels better to use my touchscreen rather than my mouse to add tiles because it's faster and I feel like it's easier to not miss click.

Alternate grids:
I'd like to have a square grid or free placement to make more custom placements would be great. I'd like to make circular courtyards in specific areas and such.

Placing grassy areas:
It would be nice to be able to place grassy or foresty areas to make parks, and field plots to flesh out the town a bit.

Optional courtyard:
I'd like the choice between keeping the paved paths where courtyards are automatically generated. Like you could click a brush on the side and turn the courtyards green instead of it being automatic. It would also be nice to be able to use the courtyard tiles as the normal path tiles.

Ability to chose building style:
Theses a few styles of buildings for the different layers of the town, and it would be nice to have an optional advanced building UI that allows you to chose which building you want to place for each layer.

LESS IMPORTANT

Choosing path border colors:
It would be nice to be able to change the border on the path tiles from red to the other colors that are available for the houses.

More background options:
I like the ocean scene, but I think a grassy and/or foresty background would be nice.

More building styles:
More buildings and styles of buildings would be nice, but that would come out to be the very lowest thing on my list of suggestions vs the rest of the building tool updates because it would require making a lot of new models and textures, and I would prefer the extra build tools.

I do really like the ability to export the towns as .obj files, because they would be cool to 3D print. You could use them as model bases or TTRPG terrain. Other formats would be nice, because more options are always nice, but it's cool that you let us export it at all.

I'd personally like the suggestions I listed because I would like to use this game as a reference for my D&D campaign town maps because I feel like it would be a really good and easy to use tool with a few tweeks. I would say the drag to place and the alternate grids would be the most important to do something like what I want to do, since I could just take a screenshot and label the buildings with editing that are important, and it doesn't have to be a 1:1 for it to be a good reference map as long as I had more placement freedom and easier to use building tools.

I understand if you don't want to add all this since it's such a cheap game and it's very worth the $3.59 I bought it for on sale, but these are just things that I think would be really useful for me personally and other DMs that want an easy to use, cheap, and fun map maker.

Thank you for reading and thank you for the game!\t\t\t\t\t
