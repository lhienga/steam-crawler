\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really simple and relaxing town building sim that is a great example of the phrase "limitations make creativity".
You can't place benches or light poles, or create roads. The only things you can place in Townscaper are houses, if enough houses are next to each-other they become stand-able ground, if you put multiple houses on top of each other they will become a tower.
That's the beautiful thing about Townscaper, you only place houses, the simulation makes them into a livable town. You have to work with the limitations of the software to create the town of your dreams. And I love it!

It's not perfect, obviously, the main issue being technical. After building to a certain point the simulation takes a really long time to process the landscape and add small details to it, you end up having to wait a few seconds, doing nothing, for the program to finish loading. But that just might be my pc.

All in all I think Townscaper is worth the price if you're looking for a relaxing time that can yield some interesting and beautiful towns.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is no game here. It's simply a virtual stress toy which is fine to some, but not me. Although the game is clearly all about creativity, there is no form of progression which essentially locks the player into a small box of choices, rather than expanding slowly and inspiring the player to think outside of said box.. Unlocking new building types and being able to choose what types of buildings are placed would make this game way more enjoyable, encourage different flavours of creativity and increase it's longevity tenfold without hindering it's focus on relaxation and avoidance of a set goal.

At 7 minutes I feel like I have seen everything that this "game" has to offer, that is probably a new record.
Notice though that I haven't said that it is bad though, because it isn't. It does what it set out to do quite well, the issue is that what it set out to do is incredibly lacking in content and ironically creativity,since it entirely relies on the player's. On top of that after my 7 minutes of playtime and watching the trailers I really don't see any increase in possibilities, nothing stood out as "that looks cool, I should build that", I've literally done everything.

The only downside I would say is that it bizarrely doesn't allow for player freedom, which inherently is an issue in a creative sandbox game with no goal.
Want to have some stairs that go up to this other block? Nope, that's now a house.
Want a long tunnel under a bridge? Nope, that's now a long solid block.
Want to separate these to roads that are next to each other with a railing or gap? Nope, they are now attached and there's nothing you can do about it.

It desperately needs a feature that allows players to alter a block's type or shape. The game would keep it's simplicity and flow, but will allow players to build what they truly want to. I would argue that this is the primary reason that 7 minutes was enough playtime for me to think I'd seen everything, because the blocks always play out in the same manner.

It really gives off that feeling you would get while playing Minecraft where you're trying to build something but the blocks are just slightly too big for that thing you wanted to build, so now that thing needs to be twice as big as planned. And even Minecraft has started to fix that with the addition of different shaped blocks, fences, walls, windows and other decorative blocks.

Again it isn't necessarily bad, but for a game that does so little it has no excuse to be anything other than perfect, which it just isn't.
It's fine for players that just want to click and build blocks but there really isn't anything more to it than that.

Personally I think I wouldn't have an issue with it if it was free as it comes off as a tech demo for something that might be bigger and better in the future, but right now it is a glorified fidget toy and not worth any amount of money.\t\t\t\t\t

Short Review:

– No puzzles or goals, just creative expression
– Simple to use
– Cute art style
– Little customization, but you can change it up a bit with mods or custom palettes
– Gives me inspiration for other creative projects
– Super chill and relaxing game!

Full Review:

I’ve played for over 40 hours so far, and I can still see myself playing some more. This game is so chill, and I love it! A lovely little sandbox town building game. Cute art style. Colorful buildings. Fun little details that pop up sometimes (like clotheslines between buildings, or little sculptures in gardens). Simple to play. No puzzles or goals or objectives or obstacles, just creative expression.

I’m finding this game is also great for inspiration. Some of the towns I’ve created have happened sort of by accident or just evolved into something once I started messing around, but now they’re giving me ideas for stories or art. I’ve also seen people suggest using it to make maps for D&D campaigns, book worlds, etc. I’ve even seen fan art and animations, so clearly I’m not the only one feeling inspired!

I would personally like if there were a bit more customization. As it is right now, all you can choose is the color of buildings, where to put them on the grid, how to stack them, and the location of the sun for lighting. Certain build features can be manifested by learning little tips and tricks, but they still only work if you build in that exact way (e.g. you can get stairs, but only if you create a platform between two buildings with a certain setup). Roofs, archways, etc. form automatically based on certain rules (so you have some control, but you might have to adjust what you’re building to make it work). You can get a grassy area, but only if you enclose a section fully within buildings. I’d love if you could at least choose between pavement or grass for each separate area, and if you could add walls without whole buildings. Maybe some control over roof vs flat surface. But judging the game on exactly what it is meant to be—a super simple automatic builder—it succeeds. And there is still so much room for variety and creativity, even within the limitations.

Some good news though for people who want a bit more customization is that there are mods, created by other players! (Just search online to find them.) They don’t solve the problems I mentioned above, but so far I’ve seen ones that offer different texture/color palettes (you can also make your own pretty easily), the ability to change plant color, and weather. Just these few things can open up whole new possibilities (e.g. I used some darker color schemes to turn one of my builds into a vampire town).

So if you want something to just chill with, or an easy way to explore your creativity, this is cute, and I recommend it! If you’re looking for real gameplay or detailed customization, it might not be for you, since there’s not much more to it than plopping down buildings. But there can be a lot of creativity and planning involved, if you really get into. Even more so if you download or make your own palettes and textures. With the amount of time I’ve spent playing, clearly I’m enjoying it!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"No real gameplay" is actually kind of overselling this software. There is no gameplay, period. This is not a game, not even a minimalist one, or a casual one. Certainly not a simulation one.

It's a simplified 3d modeller plugin, for slowly drawing building shapes. And it does even that poorly. A fixed selection of colours, a fixed set of shapes for a given set of grid elements, a fixed grid layout, no tool you can drag, no copy/paste or reuse, just one level of terrain or building per click.

It's cheap. But it still somehow feels too expensive for what it does and how poorly it does it.\t\t\t\t\t
