\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't recommend this simply because it's miscategorized. This has nothing that would make something a game. Just because something is 3D, that doesn't mean it's a game. A game should have objectives or a story or both. At least any kind of challenges, achievements, or metrics to show your progress as a player. Like, highest floor built, how many meters of pavement, or how many windows in total. This has none of it.

As a tool to build 3D townscapes, it's also kind of useless. Sure, you can make screenshots of what you have built, but the .OBJ file export is half-baked (it exports 3D object with broken textures). Texturing the 3D model to replicate all the shaders is not something a regular person could do with 3D Builder or Paint 3D apps, it should work out of the box or not be an option at all (like on mobile).

There is one cool thing about it though - you can share your creations really easily, you just copy the state of your build to the clipboard and send it to a friend and they can paste it back into their app. So, when Wordle became viral, I was able to build a tool that converted Wordle results into Townscaper buildings https://tarmo888.github.io/Wordle2Townscaper/

So, while I have tried Townscaper on desktop, laptop, Android mobile phone, Steam Deck, and even in the browser, it's just a cute tool for me, which gets boring after 5 minutes. It feels like a prototype, unfinished.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTownscaper is not so much a game but more of a toy. There is no winning or losing, just clicking to make buildings appear. You can make quaint little towns or something greater if your imagination allows it. The tools at your disposal are fairly limited so if you want to customize every little aspect of your town you'll be disappointed. However, since it is limited it is very easy to get the grips of and make something that looks decent fairly quickly. As mentioned before, this not a game, your creations are little more than just dioramas. Ultimately, I find Townscaper to be a fun toy to mess around with every now then to kill some time, or whenever the mood to build a town arises.
