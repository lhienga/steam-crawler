1. Different build materials, i.e. wood or metal
2. Shop Fronts (Maybe? Not Priority)
3. Market stalls and other things to go on the streets like wells
4. A Big one is people. The game is really colorful but after a time of playing it... It feels dead, like a ghost town. There's no activity or really anything going on.
5. Brush/ placement controls

9. Animals!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a charming "game" (more like a toy?), however it's lacking some needed features:
  • First person mode or Free camera mode (currently there's only Perspective camera which always faces to a point in space);
  • A functional .obj export (in the Steam version the obj export messes up the color of your buildings when exported, meanwhile the GOG version seems to be fixed, imo that discrepancy is unacceptable).
  • Official mod support.
  • Workshop support.
I believe it's missing more things, but if we had at the very least mod support and a workshop, the community could band together and expand the game. That's just wishful thinking tho, as the game is called as complete by the developer.

Don't get me wrong tho, it's impressive what they did with this toy, as in being able to spawn cutesy coastal cities with so little effort and all procedurally, demands an incredible amount of effort and craftsmanship which I respect. But calling this toy complete when one of the features is barely functional (obj export) is weird, more so when the GOG version reportedly doesn't have that problem.

All in all it's an experience that only you can tell if it's worth or not. So give it a go with the demo and if you like it, try the full version (which is not that different). This is just not for me for the aforemetioned reasons, anyway thank you for reading.\t\t\t\t\t
