\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had this theory for a while - base game of ck3 is actually the intended experience, and every DLC that came after is a mixed bag or feature creep. I feel like the game's getting too large for its own good, and the coherent game design vision for it has been long lost.

You can go to university every 20 years for perk points and improved education traits, which sounds like solid long-term progression for your character... until you realize harm events are still on by default and the character most likely won't live long enough to benefit. You can demand hostages after winning a war, but that mechanics is as meaningless as regencies a few months back - you can still get the war declared on you regardless, and killing the hostage won't give you any benefits. You can't even make a hostage your concubine, so like, what's the point? You get the ability to pass one of your traits to your ward (even if there are 3 traits already), which is good if your build requires a particular trait, but that alone does not justify a whole DLC, not to mention makes the game easier than it already is.

You also get a couple new events, but because there's only a few of them they are constantly repeated, which gets annoying quickly. Expect every child of yours to hug you and bring you their teeth. EVERY. SINGLE. ONE.

What else? This DLC was marketed as particularly LGBTQ+ friendly as same sex couples can now adopt children. I asked PDX in a comment why are they bringing wokism into dev diaries for the game grounded in history and they just silently removed it. They also claim to have added a new Eccentric trait as an apology to neurodiverse individuals who were offended by the Lunatic trait - but I haven't encountered that trait once throughout my play with this DLC. So yeah...\t\t\t\t\t
