The hostage mechanic I've found relatively useful, and also it creates a bit of tension when a much larger realm demands a hostage from you, but otherwise I rarely think about it.

These lukewarm feelings reached a turning point when I realized this DLC includes my favoritest favorite activity: University Visits. University visits give your adult characters another chance to improve their education trait, and gain additional attribute points. Or, if you so choose you may decide to entirely goof off and spend your univeristy visit partying, which yield mildly amusing events. In Elder Kings 2 I, the illustrious king of Wayrest had been distracted in my studies by a sultry, 58 year old Nord delinquent, where then my character engaged in some - as he put it - "hands on anatomical studies".

Is this single activity worth $5? Probably not. Compounded with the childhood events and hostage mechanic, I can sorta grasp why Paradox would price this above $5, which is something I certainly would *not* say for Friends & Foes. I would say I recommend it mostly if you get it on sale for a few dollars, or apart of a bundle, although this certainly isn't the worst way to spend five dollars on Steam. You could've spent it on Friends & Foes, after all.\t\t\t\t\t
