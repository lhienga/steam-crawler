\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to go ahead and say it... this game sucks. It's priced like a AAA title but plays like something your 17-year-old computer-engineering cousin might make as a hobby. This game would be fairly priced closer to $10-12, as there is some entertainment value, but from a game development aspect it's almost like they forgot to actually finish it. Almost every aspect of the game is embarrassing if you're a professional developer, you cannot show even one over of gameplay to someone and not have to apologize for at least 12 mistakes. The commentators are watching a completely different game, and not a single thing they say has anything to do with what's actually going on. Which lends itself to the actual problem, which is that clearly no one at the studio played this game before releasing it. Testing? What testing? Why would anyone waste time on testing? This is a game that is about 20% developed, and after all the post-release patches it is at about 25%. If I were a game developer, I would not want to attach my name to this game - it's a great resume for how to not make it in gaming, and when considering that it costs as much as Game of the Year titles, it's quite sickening.

The game is largely unplayable without customized gameplay setting changes due to the fact that almost every ball faced will be edged to the keeper and caught on medium-and-higher difficulty. There is no attention paid to quality-of-life improvements, such as the ability to save your squad from one match to another amidst a tournament, the amount of times you have to rebuild the entire lineup is a waste of precious time. Most other games have learned from game developers before them and added at least the minimum workable UI that is expected in a next-gen game, but this one seems to start from scratch and attempt to rebuild the wheel from a very early 2000s game design that they've improved on only minimally.

This game misses all the crucial dimensions to immerse one into a true cricket match, and after 27 hours of playing the game I still don't have that cricket thrill and feel and seems like I'm mostly just playing Stick Cricket with 3D graphics.

After 3 lackluster and lazy attempts this is largely still the same poor quality cricket game that it has been, and it's probably time to hang it up and let someone more competent take a shot. I can give them a pass on the fact that they don't have enough licensed content to make this game feel authentic, given that the community has done a decent job at bridging that gap (despite the lackluster tools available), and seeing as how no licensor would ever want their brand being tarnished by such a poorly designed and poorly developed game, but even that is a big factor contributing to the failure of this game to present a respectable standard.

If you can get t his game for free, it's worth a play or two after considerable customization and manual setup to make it even somewhat enjoyable. For any price >$0 it starts to exponentially become not worth it. To say I'm disappointed is clearly an understatement, to say I would recommend this game, an outright lie.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJumping from PlayStation to the Steam platform for my cricket gaming adventures, I must say, Cricket 24 has been a revelation. The game physics are nothing short of impressive, offering a level of realism and detail that I've rarely experienced before. Every swing, hit, and pitch feels lifelike, immersing you completely into the world of cricket.

The face data technology utilized in Cricket 24 adds another layer of authenticity to the game. Seeing players with accurate representations adds to the immersive experience, making every match feel like you're watching it live. This attention to detail in the visuals and mechanics truly sets the game apart from its predecessors and counterparts.

However, it's not all a smooth ride. There are a few areas where the game could use some polishing. Some gameplay mechanics and user interface elements feel a bit clunky and could benefit from a more intuitive design. Additionally, the game's performance optimization on certain systems could be improved to ensure a seamless experience for all players.

Despite these shortcomings, the overall experience of playing Cricket 24 has been exhilarating. Transitioning from a console to PC gaming can sometimes feel like a gamble, but in this case, it paid off tremendously. The depth of gameplay, combined with stunning graphics and realistic physics, makes for an engaging and enjoyable experience.

I'm hopeful that the developers will address the current issues with future updates. There's a solid foundation here that could be built upon to make Cricket 24 not just a good cricket simulation game but a great one. Until then, I'm thoroughly enjoying the game and would recommend it to both newcomers and seasoned cricket gaming fans alike.\t\t\t\t\t
