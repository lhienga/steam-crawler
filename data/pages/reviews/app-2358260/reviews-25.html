\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPrepare for a detailed review that will include numerous suggestions.

Although there have been improvements in different areas, Cricket 24 still has significant issues. I cannot recommend purchasing it in its current state. However, the latest patches showed significant improvement and hopefully soon it will reach a stage worthy of its price.

Pros:

- The game surpasses its predecessors in terms of smoothness.

- First slip catches can now be observed.

- Dismissals show higher variations compared to previous iterations.

- Watching batters execute certain shots is a visually pleasing spectacle.

- Two new bowling styles for seam bowlers and two for off spinners have been added, along with a new batting style.

- Licensing has been granted for 8 IPL teams, besides PSL and several associate nations, and a remarkable 50 new stadiums.

- They have heeded my previous suggestion to expand the range of career mode teams available for selection.  

- The skin details for ‘create a player’ have been improved. Without a doubt, the created players in this game outshine those in Cricket 22.

- The ambiance is improved by the sound of the crowd.

- Additional field setting.

Cons:

- Spinners! The developers are struggling to balance the spinners’ power with their ability to turn the ball.

- The presence of a superhuman wicketkeeper undermines the realism. 

- The stumping needs to be fine-tuned.

- The fielding performance has not met the expected standard.

- Adam Gilchrist has been selected as a new commentator, although I had hoped for Danny Morrison or Ian Bishop. The commentary is just as bad as before. 

- The T20 match simulation remains untouched. Playing as a lower order batter, I am devoid of both the batting and the sense of realism.

- There is no rule for foreign players in a lineup, breaking the realism.

- The lineup is lacking dynamism as a whole. Any changes to the batting order or the use of a Nightwatchman will not occur. The utilisation of bench players is neglected by AI.

- Left-hand seam bowlers go around the wicket during the power play, while right hand off spinners go over the wicket. The order should be reversed for both.

- No new delivery types were introduced for arcade controls, like reverse swing or carom ball. The opportunity to bowl leg breaks as an off-spinner or off breaks as a leg-spinner is also not available. These mysterious spinners are a requirement for the game. 

- The visual representation of the generated players is still unappealing. There are female cricketers with a bald head. The issue can be resolved by granting us permission to change the visuals and names of all club players. One possible approach is to pre-generate a group of players in the academy and use their appearance when starting the career.

- The consideration of allowing headbands, such as the one worn by Stuart Broad, and hijabs, similar to a female player from Scotland, should be given in the academy.

- The absence of in-game communication poses challenges for batters and captains in evaluating match situations and adjusting strategies, and resulting in an ordinary AI.

- There should be a revision to the speed standards for fast bowlers in career mode club cricket. A genuine fast bowler should be a rarity in club cricket. (For a fast-medium pacer, the speed proficiency should generate medium pace speed, around 125-130 kph, for the first three stars. The fourth star should be around 135 kph and the fifth star should give 145 kph. For Fast it should give 135 kph up to three stars and 145 kph for fourth and 150+ kph for the fifth star.)

- Similarly, a speed proficiency for spinners would be needed. Rashid Khan, for instance, is a fast spinner known for his bowling speed of around 100 kph. On the other hand, the left arm spinners from Bangladesh have a bowling speed below 88 kph. Moreover, we saw Kuldeep Yadav’s remarkable improvement as a wicket-taking bowler, marked by his enhanced speed.

- Apart from the IPL teams, the licenses offer no valuable contributions to the game, and there are numerous missing faces. Players from different countries make up the rosters of associate nations.

- Please add two or three bowling styles utilised by the primary bowlers from the top 10 nations. The presence of a style akin to that of Muralitharan, Harbhajan, or Shoaib Akhtar does not account for the current bowlers. As a Bangladeshi, I would love to see Shakib and Mustafizur’s action as both are licensed.

- I know the licensed Bangladesh national team or BPL may not be included. But please include a separate fictional NCL, BPL, BCL and DPL. Starting my club career, my dream is to get selected on DPL for List A cricket and NCL for FC cricket. Then I want to play for franchise-based BPL for T20 and BCL for a more competitive FC experience.

The career mode’s gaps can be rectified, by adding the aforementioned suggestions.

To conclude, I do not recommend the purchase of this iteration to those who already own Cricket 22 or 19, as I am the owner of all cricket games produced by Big Ant Studios. If you have other past titles, you can purchase it for the upgrades and the positive direction it is moving in with recent patches.\t\t\t\t\t
