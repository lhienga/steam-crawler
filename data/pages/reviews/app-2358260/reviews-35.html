Seems like they've used an early build of Cricket 22 to re-label it as Cricket 24. Because all the bugs which were fixed in Cricket 22 post-launch through updates have resurfaced again.

Positives:
  • More refined UI
  • Smoother animations (albeit most of which are similar to what you've seen in C22)

Negatives:
  • I can attest that the game has not been play-tested, not a great experience on launch
  • If you already own C19/C22, there's nothing new here except for licenses. This could have easily been a DLC for Cricket 22 similar to "Ultimate Edition DLC" released for Cricket 19. But I guess Nacon/BigAnt wanted to sell this for full-game price rather than DLC price

Game-breaking bugs I have noticed in 30 mins of my gametime:

  • 90% of the balls are counted as 4 byes. Doesn't matter I touch/leave/block/hit the ball as batsman.
  • After hitting the ball, the game automatically forwards to next ball in the over as soon as the ball reaches inner circle (game counts it as 4 byes)
  • Have played 2 games, haven't found any fielders moving. They stand like a statue and ball goes through them.
  • Due to the above 3 points, I haven't found a way to explore rest of the game, which I'm sure would be laden with more bugs

Despite losing all hope on BigAnt Studios with their previous Cricket 22 release, I still went forth and bought the game as a Cricket fan, but I certainly regret it. I will give it a couple of days to see if "any" patches make the game any better (or playable in my case). If it doesn't, I'll refund and accept the reality that any subsequent cricket games will continue to be hot trash as long as BigAnt is the only studio making them. (I sincerely hope Nacon atleast schools BigAnt and Mr. Ross on how to run a studio and deal with criticism)

While I can tell with about 50% certainty that most of the game breaking bugs will be patched out in the subsequent months (as seen with C22 release), I would strongly recommend against purchasing this game if you already own Cricket 19 or Cricket 22 (Unless you have a lot of disposable income and don't care throwing some $$$ into trash can)
