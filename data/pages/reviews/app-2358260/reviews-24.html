\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDidn't buy Day 1 so I can only review on what I've played.

Noticeable missing features that have previously been in past Big Ant Cricket games such as;
- Rain Delay
- Pitch wear and preset choices, such as day 5 wear or heavy cracking. At the moment their is no difference between day 1 and day 5 pitch wear through choices.
- Ability to change opposition lineups in career. Some teams wont bring spinners to test matches.
- Career calender inaccuracies such as Australia 23/24 test summer is against NZ in game, when it should be Pakistan/West Indies split series.
- No ability to choose day/night test matches for Adelaide or upcoming night matches.
- Boxing Day test starts on the 27th in game.
- Run rate comparisons.

Other noticeable but not game breaking bugs/missing features I've noticed;
- No ability to remove batter transparency in Pro Mode.
- Audio bugs such as ball hitting bat sound in real time echoed like in replay mode.
- Opposition AI cheering for away team when they hit 4s or 6s.
- Changes to pads or gloves in between games will be reverted to before change if you exit mid game.

It seems after the first few patches that the game is on par with Cricket 22 at its best which is a good sign, as 22 was significantly improved from its Day 1 release.

Even with a lot of previous missing features, the game feels really smooth and is moving in a positive direction.
Fielding feels really good with the exception of rare overthrows.

CRICKET 24: A Diamond in the Rough

Rating: ★★★★★★☆☆☆☆ (6/10)

Pros:

Realistic player faces: The most striking feature of CRICKET 24 is the impressively detailed player faces. The authenticity they bring to the game is a major selling point.
Ongoing patch updates: The development team deserves credit for their commitment to improving the game. They have been consistently releasing patches, addressing various issues and enhancing stability.
Cons:

Buggy and unstable: It's impossible to ignore the game's current state. CRICKET 24 is plagued by numerous bugs and instability issues, which can seriously hinder the gaming experience.
Limited gameplay modes: At the moment, the game lacks diversity in terms of game modes and features, leaving players longing for more.
The potential for CRICKET 24 to become a game-changer in the cricket gaming world is evident. However, its current state leaves much to be desired. The game's instability and numerous bugs can be frustrating for players, and its limited gameplay options are underwhelming.

While CRICKET 24 may not be worth buying in its current state, the promising aspect is the consistent patch updates that hint at a bright future for the game. If the development team continues to work diligently on improving stability, addressing bugs, and introducing new features, this game may well evolve into a cricket gaming gem.

In conclusion, CRICKET 24 is a work in progress, and its current version should be considered a stepping stone towards a potentially great cricket gaming experience.
