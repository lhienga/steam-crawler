\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a cricket game, so I'm expecting core gameplay to be similar to Cricket 22 / Cricket 19.

However this game is poorly optimised for PC (even on lowest settings, the ball comes down either at a terrible FPS or an amazingly fast FPS which makes timing horrendous), and overall the game feels very stuttery compared to C22.

The game is also buggy (only bug I have encountered is the bowler walking forwards automatically after bowling, which I cannot stop, but initial reviews on PlanetCricket report a variety of other bugs).

On a personal note, I like some of the new animations - but hate the new bat sound. It just sounds.. odd. Not sure where they got that from. Maybe I'm just used to the sounds from DBC14 -> C22.

Also, the new batting stance has no sound when the batsman taps his bat down, and then his feet drift (a la EA games) from middle to middle/leg.

Overall I'd avoid for now, until they fix this up (like they have/had to do for AFL23 and Cricket 22).

Once this is tidied up, it will be a lot better - but I'd go as far to say this release gives Ashes Cricket 2013 vibes for the un-playability. (Although nothing will ever be as bad as that).\t\t\t\t\t

The negatives are that the fielding is still very buggy; fielders sidestep, escorting the ball to the boundary. In Women's cricket, the fielders stand outside the boundary ropes to field the ball.

Unless they either do another pass at rosters, or allow licenced teams to be edited, it's a bit disappointing as it's hard to get an authentic experience, in spite of their great work on photogrammetry and improving the feel of batting and bowling.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Roller coaster Ride: Cricket 24 Review(Bought it again after it got somewhat fixed and writing a fresh review)

Cricket 24 is the latest addition to the virtual cricketing arena, promising an immersive experience for fans of the sport. While the game boasts several positive aspects, it falls short in certain areas, leaving room for improvement.

Positives:

-->Photo symmetry of the Players:Cricket 24 excels in bringing the players to life with exceptional photo symmetry. The attention to detail in replicating the physical features and expressions of real-life cricketers adds a layer of authenticity to the gaming experience.

-->Improved Batting and Bowling:The game has made significant strides in refining the batting and bowling mechanics. The controls feel more intuitive, providing players with a greater sense of control and skill when facing deliveries or sending down a crucial over.

-->Official Licences:Cricket 24 has successfully secured official licenses, enhancing the realism by featuring authentic team names, player likenesses, and tournament branding. This adds a level of immersion that is sure to please cricket purists.

Negatives:

-->Drops in the Frame rate:Unfortunately, Cricket 24 suffers from occasional drops in frame rate, impacting the overall smoothness of the game play. These inconsistencies can be jarring and affect the overall gaming experience.

-->Inaccurate Ball Hitting Direction:
The radar fails to accurately depict the direction in which the ball is hit. This discrepancy can lead to confusion and frustration among players, especially when strategic decisions hinge on reliable information.

-->Run out Issues:Despite being comfortably inside the crease, players often find themselves victims of runouts. This persistent issue disrupts the flow of the game and needs urgent attention through updates.

-->Subpar Fielding:The fielding mechanics in Cricket 24 leave much to be desired. Players report instances of awkward movements, delayed responses, and missed opportunities that detract from the realism the game aims to achieve.

-->Bad Animations:The game suffers from substandard animations that can detract from the overall gaming experience. Unnatural movements and glitches in the animation sequences may impact the visual appeal and immersion of Cricket 24.

-->Below Par Commentary:Cricket 24 disappoints with lackluster commentary. There are instances where a remarkable bowling performance is attributed to the wrong player, undermining the immersive quality of the in-game commentary.

-->Numerous Bugs:The game is plagued by a multitude of bugs that hinder the overall experience. These range from minor glitches to more significant issues, and addressing them is crucial for players to enjoy the game without constant interruptions.

-->Review System Issues:When reviewing a decision, the replay often goes missing, leaving players clueless about what has happened. The direct transition to the big screen without proper replay analysis is a flaw that needs immediate attention.

-->Slow-motion Replay Dysfunction:The slow-motion replay feature in Cricket 24 is plagued by functionality issues. Instead of providing a detailed view, it consistently plays the video at 3x speed, rendering the feature ineffective for reviewing critical moments accurately.

In conclusion, Cricket 24 offers a mixed bag of delights and disappointments. While the game's positive aspects contribute to an enjoyable experience, the negatives, such as frame rate drops, bad animations, and persistent bugs, need urgent attention to elevate Cricket 24 to the level of excellence expected by avid cricket gaming enthusiasts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis will probably be it for me.

For most people in my country, this game costs almost a quarter of their paychecks. Yet, this is the only franchise every game of whom I have bought on the release day. It has always been about supporting the developer as they are the only ones interested in making cricket games. Unfortunately, it appears that Big Ant is also aware of this fact and is willing to milk every last penny out of desperate fans.

Cricket 24 is nothing less than a blatant cash grab. I wish I could even call this an overpriced DLC for Cricket 22 but unfortunately it is not even that. All the bugs that took months of patches to get fixed are back with a whole set of new ones. The AI fielding is somehow even worse despite them claiming a complete overhaul. Apart from licensing, lighting improvements, few additional cutscenes and some new batting/blowing actions, it is just the same game that we have been playing for the last two years and you don't need to put in hours to see it.

This will be my last purchase from Big Ant until I see them putting in some effort. As for now, it is quite clear that for a company that is not even capable of delivering a working product at launch, they sure have the greed on par with EA.\t\t\t\t\t
