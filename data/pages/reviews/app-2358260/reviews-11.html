\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love cricket, I love big ant, I love video games. But this game still has me rubbed the wrong way, Yes it looks fantastic, so real and so great. However it plays very poorly, there is something innately unfun about Cricket 24's game play. The realism barrier is immediately broken, and doesn't feel balanced compared to Cricket 22, the commentary is dumb at the best of times, when a defensive block is sent straight to the bowler and the commentator announces an amazing stop for 4, or when the ball sails into the keepers gloves and the commentator goes, I have no idea what they are appealing for, it feels annoying and ruins the big moments. Fielding is also incredible overpowered, AI fielders can make every single type of physically impossible catch, apart from dollies that kids would catch when you're bowling of course. Not to mention every single fielder has the fastest rocket arm in the world. Bowling also is completely unchanged, working for a wicket feels tedious and stupid, especially when hard work and toil rewards an edge, dropped, and the next ball the batter finds their footwork and smashes it over your head for 6. Batting however for me is the biggest let down, so many new shots, 360 cricket, and yet you find yourself getting out to impossible catches, getting bairstowed while spamming the no run button or pressing the special shots trigger just for your player to play a cover drive and get bowled. It makes no sense, and is such a let down especially when a non-360 game such as cricket 22 felt better to bat in. No part of me understands why this game was released when it was, it feels so unpolished and unfinished, which is such a disservice to the incredible work we all know Big Ant do, just look at the game! Why was this game pushed out of the door when it wasn't ready, It needs so much fixing, and after that it needs more content. Especially around the bone dry Career Mode. Overall disappointing but still probably the best cricket game out there, marginally.\t\t\t\t\t
