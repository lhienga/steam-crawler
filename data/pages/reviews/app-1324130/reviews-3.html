Bringing any characters that aren't part of a specific set of 5 is just blatantly sub-optimal play. On the other hand, the range of possibilities for resource types and high availability overall makes it impossible to be locked out of critical buildings and tools. The 4 character base game limit makes balancing resource gathering vs utilization an engaging challenge, and I never felt like any failures were due to unfair or unbalanced game design.

Not a game type I like to play a ton of, but definitely a game I expect to return to regularly. Rebalance the character selection and this game will have 5x more replayability. There is a game mode with 6 characters and a military-based objective, but a 10 character game with an overflowing abundance of dangerous wild animals in an even less forgiving environment would be AMAZING.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't feel the need to compare this to any other, as it has it's own uniqueness and intrigue.

I've played lots of different games with similarities, but in the end this is it's own game, and I find it's a very enjoyable addition.

Clean graphics and animations with nice detail.
They're good enough that it's fun to just zoom in and watch things up close.

Pretty much everything is alien to you. So there is a considerable amount of discovery early on.

Learning your environment and how it can benefit you, what you can hunt/harvest/farm.

There's a good variety of livestock and animals, and it's great when you have a nice stocked kennel with a half dozen or more trained attack dogs that you can draft into combat and control.

And they're just the starter animals you can train, till you can start herding tougher aliens.
And that's only one of many types of defenses/offenses you can employ.

Attacks and events are usually far enough to give you time to recover. Though natural events and creature attacks can both hinder you at the same time... there's tech to help with nature though.

Later you start delving into some serious tech. Electrifying your base. Building it much, much larger. Setting up hydro growing, refrigerated rooms, turrets, mechs, drones, etc.

Defintely worth the purchase. And I tried it on the medium level, so if you like easier or harder maybe it'd still appeal to you.

Anyway, time for a New Year party for my settlers, then on to year 4, building mechs and a massive hydro farm factory.\t\t\t\t\t
