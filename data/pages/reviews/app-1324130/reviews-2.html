\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've got to give this a negative review just to balance out how positive it's over all rating is. It's not bad but it's not particularly good either.

First off it is not even close to Rimworld in any aspect, so if that is a drawing point to you then look somewhere else.

Positives.
Nice enough looking
Building mechanics are decent, you can create some cool bases
Workshop has some decent content
No bugs other than the ones you have to kill

Cons
No terrain management (roads/paths) making a base fit for OCD is nigh on impossible
The observe feature quickly plays itself out of existence
Manually doing everything is tedious (expeditions are over so quickly and for so little gain that it quickly becomes a chore)
Decent sized map but settlers will struggle to cover long distances without passing out so a lot of wasted space
New settlers constantly arrive whether you want them to or not and rejecting them gives you a -10 happiness penalty to everyone.

It's an ok game but the longer you play it the more frustrating things you notice and the less enjoyment you get. It needs some quality of life improvements at the very least.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is mostly good, I didn't want to put the tumb down only because I had the feeling that the devs tried to do the things right.

It has a nice graphic, many little things are well detailed (like the hot air going out from the AC), has a lot of things to do and has workshop which will make the game great one day (thanks to all modders in the world).

On the downside, many things are scripted (like the horde attack after a character is saved) so its easy to predict what will happen. AI is very stupid, so stupid that you will need to micro manage everything in the mid-end game. Eg: if you have one character doing stuff from the opposite side of the map (after half a day of walking), like salvaging a space ship junk), it will stop doing so just because one plant suddenly become in need to receive a bit of fertilizer, so it will neither finish the task and start the loooong journey back home. Characters interaction is bad, almost useless. Many things are almost useless or incomplete or bad balanced, like exploration, insects hive (they give soooo much carbon...), robots and mechs (one mech kills easy two hundred of insects). Last but not least, terrain can't be flattened, is very annoing and after 8 year I had half of the map full of holes.

This game can be great, right now is a "meh", I hope the devs will balance and improve the game in future\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeems like RimWorld if that game were deeper rather than wide, if that makes sense. Also a lot less quality.

Anyway, that depth comes at the cost of enough breadth than I'm not sure there's as much to do in this game, but it doesn't really matter because the final tutorial won't complete. I mean, I probably don't need it, but it felt like a little encapsulation with the issue of this game: it absolutely pushes me away and refuses to meet me halfway.

I'm not entirely sure what happened, but I had to restart the electricity tutorial three times and I couldn't get it to advance past the 'disallow perishables at your shelves so they get moved to the fridge'. Did that, no dice. Tried disallowing the other items that aren't the specific ones it outlined (crafting supplies, etc). No dice. Disabled storage of the items it wanted from the stockpile, because the tutorial step mentioned moving goods from the stockpiles to the shelves. No dice. They didn't even move anything from the stockpile, whether I disallowed storage at the stockpile or not.

And mind, this is after already restarting it once because I put the freezer in the building and it just didn't react. Like the trigger never fired so it just sat.

I'm sure I'm being reductive, but it took long enough to get through these tutorials (or not, actually; I didn't technically finish the last one) that the refund window is closed and frankly I wish I hadn't bought the expansion with it. I was confident I was going to like that, but I'll stop making that mistake one of these days.

Slower, more graphical, more boring, more unstable RimWorld. Even the music is the same twangy pseudo-Firefly-space-western affair. It's just also more sparse, lower-quality, and more boring.

So yeah, worse RimWorld.

I would recommend you play RimWorld instead.\t\t\t\t\t
