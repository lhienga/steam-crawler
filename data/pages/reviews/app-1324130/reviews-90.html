I have tried making mods to fix the issues but It's just Jury rigging at best.
You have limited access to the original game files and have to work through a modding tool that does not give you much freedom to work with and oftentimes breaks things when you try to implement a simple mod such as making a craftable item which then destroys the crafting table for some reason.

Its plays as a wannabe knockoff of rimworld but without the aspects that make rimworld a great game.
There is very little support for the modding community as there are no troubleshooting or in depth guides available at the moment that I am aware of and yes I did look.
the website that they spam open every time i try to work on a mod is of very limited use as well.

Honest opinion: don't bother and stick to modded versions of rimworld. Vanilla is either unbearable or unplayable, take your pick depending on your difficulty setting.
The only way this game can redeem itself in my opinion is by reworking a lot of it's story, core mechanics and modding support.

Side note; Might make a fun zombie survival game if you combine the mechanics with state of decay first person play mode, set in a city where buildings are can be deconstructed for building materials and switch between first person exploration and 3rd person base building.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to structure my review based on the marketing text about the game:

Discover an expansive, living 3D world with remarkable flora and fauna
- Yes, it's quite pretty. Expansive, not really, the map is relatively small and doesn't have much variety to it.

Manage your survivors’ needs, health and happiness
- This pretty much happens on its own. I'm playing on Very Hard difficulty and all I had to do was place a few entertainment items and they're happy. Oddly enough, they were happier sleeping on the ground in basic shelters, then when I built them a real house with real beds - just because the house didn't have carpet yet. As for health, healing happens automatically after combat, at most you might need to tell someone to heal another survivor instead of waiting to be healed themselves if everyone is injured.

Construct and evolve your own unique base, and devise cunning defences to protect it and survivors from being attacked
- If by "cunning defences" you mean build a wall with a few traps and a guard tower. Again, even on Very Hard difficulty the alien attacks were very tame. The only time I had survivors die is when one of them stupidly wandered into an Alien Shrieker nest, fainted, then I couldn't stop the other survivors from one by one trying to rescue her and fainting then dying themselves.

Plant, grow, harvest and hunt down food. Stockpile useful resources and salvage material from fallen space debris
- This is beyond easy. Once you discover a plant, you can farm it. Even on Very Hard difficulty, I have enough raw food stored for ~900 meals with almost no effort. There's no need to hunt really, because any time you get an alien attack, you just butcher all the corpses for hundreds of insect meat. And once you discover a freezer, things stay good indefinitely.

Research technologies to expand your horizons.
- The technology tree is weird and rather uninspiring. Maybe it's because I found the game so easy in general, but I didn't feel any sort of drive to research the next best thing because what I already have is doing the job just fine. And there was no logical order to the research tree. I researched pulse rifles, but then realized I couldn't craft them because I hadn't researched power cells. Why wasn't the rifle research dependent on it? And why was the power cell research further down the tree?

Provide heat, light, devices for relaxation and more to keep survivors on top of their game, ready to face the next challenge.
- Again, temperature management was pretty simple. Put down a fireplace, a heater, etc, and make sure to turn them off in the summer and give your survivors season-appropriate clothes. The temperature will indeed vary quite a bit inside your building, but it doesn't really seem to matter. The only time I had a problem with temperature was when one of the survivors got hypothermia - in 30C weather. Pretty sure that's a bug.

React to fluctuating weather and environmental events
- I pretty much ignored these. They had minimal impact on anything.

Overall, just unsatisfied and bored with the experience. I LOVED Surviving Mars. I loved failing over and over again as my colony that I thought was stable would suddenly fall apart. I loved progressing through the tech tree, constantly running out of resources, and managing my electric grid. Disasters actually could really mess you up. Here... I just keep increasing the difficulty level waiting for that to happen, but it never did. I'm glad other people seem to enjoy it, but I don't really. I gave it the ol' college try for 14 hours and now I'm done.\t\t\t\t\t
