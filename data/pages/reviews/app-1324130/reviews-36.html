\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a bit torn about this game. It's nice, it's fun, but it's really lacking in terms of automation and quality-of-life. There is a number of annoying bugs. Overall, the game's biggest problem is that everything takes ages - even at max speed non-stop, at times I feel like playing an idle game.

Very slow gameplay

You start with 4-6 survivors. I think you can have 8 maximum. Your survivors have to sleep, eat and relax to keep them happy and working, which already takes a considerable amount of their time. The food needs to be cooked, so you need to also gather sticks for fire. Pretty much everything goes bad over time (until the very end-game when you can store things in fridge/freezer). Clothes wear off over time so you need to craft new ones, so you need to plant and harvest different plants. Same with weapons. Luckily there is no ammo, but automated turrets use electricity, which in the early game means producing gasoline to keep the generators running. Add to it enemy attacks and repairs/healing afterwards. All of it leaves very little time for actual progress. Even if you build a fridge, to prevent food from going bad to save time on harvest - electric devices break every now and then and need repairs (which requires valuable resources), so their advantage is very limited.

I am playing on maximum speed most of the time, which is still too slow for me. One in-game hour takes about 10 real seconds. But crafting a single item easily takes 6-12 hours, plus you often have to craft some components beforehand. Nights are extremely boring - I have a schedule so that all my survivors sleep at the same time (at night) for at least 6 hours, which means 60 seconds of staring at the screen where nothing happens.

Micromanagement

Most of the time the survivors will do pretty well on their own. There is a whole panel with activities priorities, so you can dedicate certain people to certain tasks that they are good at. However, they will sometimes do very stupid things, like going to salvage debris far from the camp, while being hungry, so that when they finally reach the debris, they are starving and running back to the camp. So you are constantly clicking on each of the six characters to check their hunger/sleep/relax bars and potentially changing the task that they picked themselves

The workbenches have some nice options for managing tasks. There is a list of tasks which are done in order. Each task can be given a limit to either craft something X times, or until there is X of that item in storage. Workbenches can mirror the task list of an other workbench. You can also copy/paste tasks list between workbenches. However, I am really missing an option to only craft something, if I have enough ingredients - it's a regular problem of smelting scrap metal into metal alloys, because both of them are needed for variety of items.

There are automated workbenches that don't require a worker, and bots/drones, but it's only in the very end-game and the latter come with a number of bugs and issues.

Survivors' default behaviour when wounded is to go to bed and wait for someone else to heal them, even though they are perfectly fine getting the bandage and using it themselves - but only when told so manually by the player.

Missile Launcher Turret, one of a very few things that can deal area damage, to defend against hordes of aliens, has a "target area" that has to be manually placed by the player, and will only shoot at enemies at this little area... Have fun killing a moving target... Oh, and you cannot select multiple turrets and change all of their "target areas"...

Oh, and sending survivors for balloon expeditions is also all manual...

Bugs and Issues

Micromanagement related:
  • Cannot zoom out the view much (but you can pan the camera, almost to the ground).
  • Double-click selects only identical items in certain radius, instead of those in player's view.
  • Shift-clicking only adds individual items to the group, no option for ctrl+shift clicking - once you have multiple items selected, you can only add new ones one by one.
  • Survivors have two utility item slots, and if one of them loses all durability, they replace the other with the same item as the first, still carrying the broken first item with them.
  • The game requires to regularly check what clothes the survivors are wearing, as they will sometimes not replace the torn piece, or equip something which is non ideal. There is a notification for "old equipment", but if you replace non-destroyed equipment, the old one lands in the storage and you have to manually find it and mark for deconstruction.
  • Sometimes the temperature varies greatly, which requires manually changing survivors' clothes between summer and winter ones.
  • Queuing survivors tasks doesn't always work - the task that is queued is often replaced after the survivor finishes the current task.
  • There is no list of all robots (unlike survivors or tamed animals).
  • There is no ctrl+digit for grouping units, like in RTS games, which makes managing the robots extremely tedious.
  • Selecting a survivor by single-clicking their portrait changes the floor level to the one the survivor is on. It's annoying when you are trying to get someone to the place you are just looking at.
  • Drones and Bots will often try to perform their tasks no matter the incoming horde - you have to manually recall them to base before the attack (usually one by one).
  • Electric producers (such as furnaces or 3D printers) consume power even when they are not producing anything. Have fun playing with switches.

Automation related:
  • Objects are not repaired automatically - you get a notification and have to repeatedly click on it and then on "repair" to individually mark each object for repair. No keys for quick access.
  • Survivors and Harvest Bots carry resources they acquired, instead of dropping them on the floor and letting the flying Delivery Drones do the job.
  • All crafting stations and outdoor farms can have limits set to only produce items until certain limit, but indoor Hydroponic Farm doesn't have such option.
  • Service Bots assigned to the "global" area, don't work in other non-global areas. Unlike Harvest Bots.

Other:
  • Hitting space is supposed to pause/unpause the game, but it frequently has to be pressed twice - you never know whether you have to press it once or twice...
  • Solar Eclipse disables Solar Panels but not Solar Roof. Snow doesn't disable either.
  • When looking at lower floors, you are supposed to see gray contours of what is above, but this system doesn't always work as expected - sometimes objects above hide your view (e.g. bodies), and sometimes contours are shown when they shouldn't be - preventing you from interacting with the objects.
  • It's almost impossible to to see and (re)move ceiling lights.
  • Cannot deconstruct Good Luck Charm equipment, unlike any other clothing or equipment.
  • If your survivors are assigned a work area, and in this area is a bot station whose area is "global", if the bot gets destroyed it cannot be rebuilt, claiming that there is no survivor who has permission to work in "global". However, the bot would be constructed in the survivor's area, so it should be allowed. Same for repairing the robots.

Summary

Overall, the game feels like a bad mix of The Sims (as you are micro-managing a few not-too-smart characters), Surviving Mars (as you are colonising the alien planet), and RTS-style combat.

The game feels like a good Early Access game, however it left EA half a year ago (April 2023), and at the price of Â£39.98 (with a single DLC), I think it's a bit too much. It's sometimes on 30% sale, so I would recommend waiting until then.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"+" for positive and "-" for negative:

+The graphics and optimization work is quite good. With my own HD settings without using vsync on the 2060 card, image details at normal level are still fresh, clear, and beautiful. The first year is 100fps and until the last survivor is evacuated it is 45fps (with 1 huge industrialized base).

+ The game has replay value. Variety in everything, elements such as different lands and survivors make every start of the game not boring. I was truly drawn into the world of the game.

+ Semi Open World: Helping developers keep us abreast of the content and experiences they want to bring.

- Semi Open World: If it's not an online game, it's really boring at the end of the game. I've exploit/exhausted the current zone, can't get out of the zone without using a hot air balloon! Survivors can create almost all technology: Gas can, Disel engine, electronic materials, 3D scan, a fighting machine with machine guns and missile turrets mounted on both sides, 3 types of robots for various purposes, radar, combat/transportation drone... But can't build a simple vehicle to move/transport? We really need a move/transport vehicle to use in the current area, or better, load items and food to move to another land to explore and find other survivors! Or even better, turn it into a mini game like in Fallout Shelter, Control survivors to explore and collect, looking for short stories, Easter eggs,... So much potential to develop. And in the end, they still have to wait for others to pick them up, unable to build their own spaceships

- Except for the game's music, other than when fighting animals, I felt the music transitions were very chaotic and sudden, I replaced minecraft's music while playing, sorry to the game's music department!

Other aspect from my self:
In my opinion, every aspect of the game is invested very carefully and the money spent to buy the game is completely worth it.
A little bit about the "Denuvo" issue, in my country everyone will lose personal data after a few hours (I think after I went to register something related to my phone number, many messages and calls bothering my phone number), to the point that it has become a normal thing in life. So I don't really care.
Because of the above problem, I really have enough strength to find crack versions (if there are any) but no, I still spend money because I appreciate the effort of the game maker.
But really, if "Denovu" is mining virtual money on my GPU and affecting my computer experience using, I will replace the motherboard with a new one and never buy a software related to Denuvo as well as that of this developer!
The "You don't own this game so..." Yeah, I don't care either way!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a cool game. I have 6000+ hours in Factorio, Space Engineers, Surviving Mars, Kenshi, etc. This game is the "modern" version of Kenshi, that is a current GUI, graphics, and resultant polish. Just like Kenshi you take your small squad of characters and survive in an alien wilderness. Crafting, combat (at least against dangerous animals), base building, mining, research, it's all there.
So far no bugs or crashes but I am playing unmodded in this my first play through. I usually play with a ton of mods, but so far in Stranded I haven't needed/wanted any, as yet.
The game handles weather, i.e. seasonal differences between winter and summer exceptionally well. Kenshi does not have that. You better have good boots and a warm coat if your base is in the mountains because winter is going to get cold, and your characters don't like it.
The game has a lot of nice touches, such as when you build an enclosed structure and then heat it with a fireplace the room takes a while, just like real life, to go from 0 degrees to 13 (centigrade).
So, so far, in this first play through, I'd rate it a 4.5 out of 5. My only nit is I can't explicitly make my characters run, unlike in Kenshi you can set the speed at which they will move. In Stranded the characters WILL run, but only at their discretion.\t\t\t\t\t
