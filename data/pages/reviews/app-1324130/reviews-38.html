\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA decent survival colony simulator.

Pros:
- Resources are scarce yet not, you actually feel like you are tying to survive
- Dynamic and fun.
- Research has a sort of 'breakthrough' mechanics, like in Surviving Mars, which fits very well (sadly technologies have fixed price). Decent selection of technologies if a bit questionable (like building an AI core on a workbench).
- Fairly decent enemy behavior and enemy selection (at least for giant bugs)
- Decent selection of characters with traits that significantly affect how you use them
- Well simulated needs, good enough moods and skills (but I feel like there should have been more skills and mood shouldn't flip under a day)
- Nice graphics
- Multy level buildings that work well, have good enough UI (as long as you do not build on hills) and can be made of different materials
- Variety of storage options that function very well, and you actually need that variety (shelves, stockpiles, wardrobes, freedges)
- Crops and plant variety that actually matters
- Seasons and seasonal clothes (if only characters were smart enough to switch those as needed)

Cons:
- Storage really needs priorities.
- Some bugs. Mostly with restrictions of medicines and zones and when trying to link building on different levels (aka, if built on a hill)
- Some Production UI limitations with priorities (ex: you can't specify who can make specific receipts nor flexibly do a priority like clothes > textile > armor or can't limit production by amount of avaliable raw resource, intead limits are by specific dishes only) Production UI could use an expansion in general (ends up with long list of receipts that are hard to manage)
- Characters are happy with bare walls instead of requesting at least some furniture, instead they ask for parquet of all things.
- Global exploration is rather limited, seems like the ballon travels in a single direction further and further, could have been better and much well thought through.
- Apparently no seeds were taken from anywhere so there are only local foods.
- Research tree is kind of oversimplified.
- Characters have a very short 'fuse' and can't stay awake for more than a day.
- Energy system could have been better, machines that do nothing should not be consuming energy
- Animal variety and usability lags behind plants, no wool, no eggs, no milk. Animal fats are craftables instead of harvestables. Hunting products are just leather and 3 types of mostly interchangeable meats.

P.S. Feels a fair bit like rimworld, but less grimdark, smaller, shorter, less complex and in 3D with a lot of changes in mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot worth full price, not even close. Buy it on a steeply discounted sale.

In many ways, this game is a copy of Rimworld (which is fine), but with a number of interesting changes. Things like "shelves can hold more than one thing" which, imo, is great. I'm not a huge fan of Rimworld, so this is a welcome improvement. I like how the game looks, it does environments very well, and your survivors all display the gear you have put on them. This is great.

Where things fall off a cliff: the time it takes to make things, and the number of things produced, is just ridiculous. Food items will take 1-2 hours to cook (not including fetching the ingredients), and will produce 2 items per cycle (compared to 4 in Rimworld). With even just 5 survivors (eactin 2 meals each, per day), this makes it extremely difficult to build up a backlog of cooked meals. While one can argue that scarcity is part of the difficulty, for manufactured items this is a bad argument.

It's one thing for raw materials to be scarce (which is what is normally referred to when speaking of 'scarcity'), but the issue with these production bottlenecks is not one of scarcity, but of having to tie up 2-3 survivors cooking all day, every day, when the colony only has fewer than 6 people. While yes, this makes the game "more difficult", it's on par with making a shooter "more difficult" by giving the enemies more hitpoints: it's boring, unimaginative, and it doesn't require creative thinking to solve. You just... make more people cook. This is bad game design.

Similarly, the rate at which electronic devices break down (solar panels, batteries, freezers, etc) is ludicrously high. Moreover, when they break down they *always* require rare components to repair (electronics) if rare components went into their manufacture. Apparently merely soldering a connection is impossible, the entire PCB needs replacing every. single. time. Given how long it takes to manufacture these items yourself, this is (again) a very uncreative way to suck the time of your survivors away.

Enemy attacks are part and parcel of many colony survival games. In Rimworld/Dwarf Fortress, they appear at the far edge of your map. In other games, they have a visible spawn location (which sometimes you can take out the spawn location, otherwise you can build your defences in response to it). In Stranded? They just pop into existence beside your base. Just... 100+ insects... *pop*. No-one saw them coming, no-one raised a warning, they're just beside your base.

In one of my maps, I had enclosed 3 sides of my base, and created a choke point on the other, and the insects/angry animals sometimes just... spawned inside the choke point.

Meanwhile, there are Insect Nests all over the map, with new ones spawning periodically. Which are completely disconnected from enemies spawning to attack your base. The one has nothing, at all, to do with the other. A thoughtful game designer might have set the nests to spawn new insects every few days, and once a critical mass is reached, they start moving on your base, but the game designers involved in Stranded do not appear to be thoughful.

This game design is neither interesting nor, frankly, competent.

While the DLC brings with it some interesting improvements (ie delivery/battle drones, service/harvesting bots), and I hope that other creators take note, it's just a parsley garnish on a sandwich.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn spite of many negative points from my perspective, I will recommend this game anyway, because I think most people just don't care about the stuff I'm about to say (therefore, please avoid disrespectful and unnecessarily rude comments. Thank you):

+ 3D graphics (realistic) version of Rimworld (but with a lot less content)
+ However, there is enough content to keep us amused for some time

- The only currently availble music track gets old really fast
- The only "event" that happens frequently enough is the "Aggressive animals" one
- Didn't quite like the Expeditions mechanic... It fell short of the rest of the graphics-based gameplay
- After 13 (real world) hours of gameplay, I managed to find only one new survivor. Just one.
- The colony grows and becomes workload-heavy, but it's annoying to micromanage so much with only 5 survivors having to handle dozens of tasks each (especially because most tasks take sooo long)
- Equipment gets damaged and breaks too quickly in my opinion
- Some colonists may have favorite past-times, but for some reason they don't choose it first (it is available, nobody else using/doing)? I mean, it's their favorite... So...
- All of a sudden, shortage of sticks. That's right, sticks. Can't use lumber as fuel, no it must be sticks which takes forever to be chopped by a colonist who now becomes a fixed stick-chopper forever due to the high demand
- Dragging a selection box around a colonist will select everything inside of it. Not just the colonist. You will end up selecting your colonist, a wood fence, some crops, a dead bug corpse and so on. Perhaps "Shift + selection box" could do this but leave the default selection attempt exclusive to the colonists
- Random malfunctions everywhere all the time\t\t\t\t\t
