\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome game, I find it hard to put down. Mostly great all around. The colony sim genre is almost as dangerous as the 4X genre, in terms of how it eats at your time and it's hard to stop playing! Game is as challenging as you want. World is engaging. Characters are really well written with interesting personalities.

However, the mid to late game can be a drag. Once you solve for a base defense that keeps you safe, it can almost be too easy. Next step is to build one or more service and harvesting robots. Once the robots and defenses are up, smooth sailing from there. Figuring out how to get there is fun, but then it's a grind to the finish line because it's not difficult, just time consuming because things still take forever to craft.

Some aspects, are a bit too much micromanagement for my taste. For example,
- Survivors can be really dumb when planning out their activities esp if they are far apart.
- Survivors don't change their clothes automatically.
- Having to manually turn on / off electronics sucks. It's way too much work to manage room temperatures. Better to just put them in versatile clothing (armor) and have them tolerate cold and hot weather. The entire climate management system is pointless, from heating stoves to air conditioners. Just manage their clothing.
- It's hard to micro manage where items go. I still haven't found out how to effectively set up satellite bases, or the best way to extract far resources. I wish more thought was put into this, allowing an expansionist play style maybe.

All that said, I think the positives outweigh the negatives.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this at 35% off, and it still wasn't worth the price. Could have been a thumbs down, but there's nothing egregious about it.

It's an ok little game. More sane and playable than Rimworld, but limited in scope and appeal. Controller support is a huge plus, and I was able to play this from a couch start to finish (thanks for that devs). I guess you could replay this over and over, but I feel no urge to what so ever. It well done, reasonably stable (got one freeze deconstructing a complex building) and polished enough. It's not AAA at all, but still gives you a decent experience although a fairly blend and generic one. The characters are uni-dimensional, no voice, no real story, nothing memorable. This made me appreciate Riftbreaker, which left a stronger impression in the same style, even though it was a small game too. It's a competent product, but overpriced for what it is imo (although I do not doubt how much effort went into making it).

Sort of a take-it-or-leave it kind deal. I don't regret my time with it, but it was once and done for me (which is fine, except for the price tag).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStranded: Alien Dawn is like Civilization for colony sims. It's a gateway game. If you think you might be interested in colony sims, start with Stranded.

It is largely self-explanatory, and pretty forgiving in terms of difficulty, but its management tools are also not strong, because you don't need them to be. A lot of basic colonist behaviors are automated for you, but some things that you might think should be are not. The simulation is high-level enough to be more relaxing than stressful.

The game gives you so many different ways to tackle core problems that you're bound to be able to engage one of them before an issue starts killing colonists. You probably won't survive your first winter, but you'll come to a second playthrough with all the mental tools you need to complete the game.

It's solid, and while it doesn't look like it is doing much that is different from other games in the genre on the surface, its approachability itself is a real selling point.

The only place I find that it routinely falls down is with the building mechanics. They are nicely robust, but also just robust enough that things that seem like they should be simple sometimes don't work at all for no apparent reason. Stairs, in particular, never seem to want to play ball.

Structures with multiple floors take a few attempts to figure out how to make sure your builders can access everywhere they need to go to complete the structure before the structure itself prevents them from doing so. There's no way to level terrain, so you'll always spend more resources to build on uneven (read: natural) terrain rather than being able to use slopes to generate building material as you might in another title.

ENDGAME UPDATE:
In the ending of the main 'crash landing' scenario, the game never gives you the option to evacuate all of your colonists at once. You have to evacuate them one at a time as escape pods become available. While this might be realistic I found it unnecessarily tedious and questionably balanced. Reasonable minds could certainly disagree.

I just want to call attention to the fact that there is a highly rated mod with an alternate endgame goal of building escape shuttles. Each shuttle can only carry two survivors, but the key difference is that once you've built enough of them they all leave at once. This seems like a much better plan to me, but the choice is yours.\t\t\t\t\t
