\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOvercomplicated.
The actual gameplay of this game is almost nothing like what I saw in the trailer/previews.
Combat? Overcomplicated.
Building? Overcomplicated.
Resource management? Overcomplicated.
I played 85 minutes of this game, and it was nothing but tutorials. After the last tutorial I wondered, "How many more tutorials can there possibly be?" and turns out, A LOT, apparently.
I buy a game to play it. If I have to learn it, that's one thing, but I'll need to pass college exams in order to actually get to a playable point in this game.
It's a shame. This showed a lot of promise. The devs have gotten way too tied up in me making sure I open or close a window for a building (no joke) than actual exploration/building/combat.
I wanted to like this, but I don't like overcomplications. Unfortunately, this one is going to be a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

I don't regret paying full price for this, though on sale the premium edition is about the same price as the base game; so that would have been nice. Game does not disappoint however, I've put in a decent amount of time over the past two games. I will say that it is pretty graphically intensive: playing it on a laptop with an i9 and a 3050ti I get around 50-60 fps on medium preset. On high preset 30fps was fine and playable though I do prefer the higher fps and didn't notice too much of a graphical difference between the two. Coming from Rimworld I can say the experience is pretty different aside from it being 3D. I do miss having the occasional tribal or pirate raid on rimworld as right now I'm playing on the Jason moon and medium difficulty so maybe this game doesn't have raids like rimworld or they're experienced later/on higher difficulty. So far I've had 3 insect attacks two of them being the same kind of insect. Though keep in mind I've only put less than 10 hours into this and don't know for sure if there are other kinds of attacks. Overall highly recommend and see a high degree of replay-ability similar to rimworld.\t\t\t\t\t
