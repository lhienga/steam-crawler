\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;wr: Stranded: Alien Dawn (S:AD) shows a lot of promise, but it's not quite there yet.

Everyone compares it to Rimworld, and I'll do the same.

Superficially, Rimworld works because the mechanics are deep, it exposes them all to you, the graphics are easy to grasp, and there's always something interesting going on. It also wholeheartedly embraced mods, and has gone to big lengths to make modding easy, including tutorials on how to decompile the game.

S:AD left me wondering about the mechanics, and while the graphics are gorgeous, the characters routinely fall into the uncanny valley, and uses Denuvo DRM to prevent decompiling, which also gets mentioned a lot in the EULA. So while bugs in Rimworld would get fixed quickly by the community, S:AD will be more limited in what's possible. Which is a shame, because it could be *so good*.

The biggest disappointment is the writing. I can't express how much it feels like someone copied and pasted from ChatGPT. Let's look at the example of 'Hydroponics Racks':

To create a hydroponic rack, we'll need a few basic materials like metal bars and grow lights. However, instead of using water, we'll be using soil to grow our plants. The key to success is ensuring proper aeration and monitoring pH levels of the soil. With a hydroponic rack, we can grow fresh herbs and vegetables year-ground without the need for harmful pesticides. Let's give it a try and see how easy it can be to grow our own produce at home!

What the heck? A hydroponics rack using soil instead of water is called a 'planter box', or a 'greenhouse', or an 'indoor farm'. The game doesn't have aeration, pH, or pesticide mechanics. By the stage you've researched these you've been growing things outdoors for quite a while. The whole thing is just bad writing that doesn't actually tell you *why* you'd want this.

Unfortunately, this is the level of writing throughout all the game. It routinely breaks immersion, and I still don't know why a number of structures and technologies exist.

Where Rimworld exposes lots of information (and has mods that expose more), S:AD makes it hard just to get basic things that should be obvious. Case in point being farming. There are different soil types, and some plants grow better in some types than others. But how can you tell the difference? As far as I know, the *only* way is to try and build a farm, and carefully see what is reported when you mouse over a patch of soil. There's no fertility overlay. One crop depletes the soil it's grown in, but doesn't give any details on how quickly, and I have no idea what happens if it's put in a 'hydroponics farm', or if I can even do that.

So what does the game do well? Well, the graphics and building are good, although there's a learning curve to using them. The discovery mechanics are very good; you can't plant a plant or really know much about an animal or thing until you've spent some time observing it. The animal animations are excellent, everyone loves to play with the big cow-thing I tamed and it's clearly into it. The game has some basic automation baked-in; you can have devices switch on/off based upon movement, temperature, time, and battery levels. You can have workbenches and furnaces mirror each other, which makes a lot of crafting management easier. You can do multi-level constructions, there's some solid tower-defence mechanics. You can 'craft until X' to make sure meals and clothes are automatically stocked up, but you can't 'craft with excess' to automate some of the bumper crops you'll be harvesting.

With better writing, more quality-of-life (eg: fertility overlay, temperature overlay, being able to pin resources, etc), removal of Denuvo, and some more furniture options, and some balancing, S:AD has the potential to be a really great game. The game is still getting lots of updates, and I recommend it because it has so much promise, but right now it's just a 'good' game that's very pretty.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is fun. You can easily get 30 hours out of your first play through/scenario.
It has a solid foundation, however there are areas of improvement.

Sometimes the AI doesn't really do what I want them to do. E.g. I order it to construct a roof, but it stops halfway and does something else which it deems to be more important.

Weapon variety, especially in the early game is somewhat lacklustre. Crafting clothes takes way too long in my opinion. Its very hard to have all your survivors dressed fully at all times, and be prepared for every weather. Its pretty much impossible.

The UI could use some cleanup, and a aesthetic overhaul. There should also be more power generation variety. If there is no wind at night, you can run into trouble if you don't have enough batteries set up.

Overall, I enjoyed the first 20 hours of game-play and I am looking forward to a more polished experience.\t\t\t\t\t
