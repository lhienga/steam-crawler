The first few runs ended in some silly game-overs that were self-inflicted, but as the game continued it became much easier, so much so that I would say after about 50 hours of time under the belt beating the game on some of the higher difficulties became rather trivial. Base design is based on a grid system which somewhat limits creativity, and while you can make some impressive things I found myself finding an optimal build plan and sticking to that after a while.

Definitely worth a purchase if you are into survival games!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review comes after an almost uninterrupted 13 hours of playtime.

The tutorials are helpful. Graphics are fine. Managing your people works well. Developing your settlement is quite fun. Combat is simple and decent. You also get mechs. But despite these, there's still one lingering thing that I can't imagine why it's not in the game: vehicles.

YOU CAN MAKE BIPEDAL MECHS BUT YOU CAN'T MAKE THINGS WITH WHEELS!?!

You have laser pistols but you can't make harvesters or planters to make your farms more time efficient? Your people have to walk so far away for salvage and your mech can't even carry cargo!

Let me contrast this game with an another survival colony builder game called Dawn of Man. These people living in the Neolithic age can have wagons to transport things in long distances and oxens that can help plow the fields. This game about a comparatively primitive people seems to be more "advanced" than this game.

Also, there's no minimap for some reason.

While the fundamentals are working well, the lack of vehicles and minimap is strangely absent. Would still recommend based on overall experience.\t\t\t\t\t
