\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe similarities to Rimworld are fairly obvious, but I think the game managed to miss the points of Rimworld that make it great.

In stranded dawn, you are in control of a handful of not randomized survivors, that dont really have any real personality besides being fussy eaters, and constantly choosing to wear summer clothes when its cold outside. Them breaking down has little to no impact on the game, and is largely forgettable. They will also occasionally catch an illness, that will also lead to nothing dramatic. Nothing seems to be an urgent matter at all in the game, so much so that Im not sure its possible to lose.

This is what they did wrong when making a 3D Analog of Rimworld, Rimworld gets all its flavor from its pawns. Randomized, often snapping for insane reasons, having personality, getting addicted to drugs, being filled with bloodlust to where when they snap they will kill their fellow pawns. They will have conversations about things in game and have differences leading to potentially violent escalations. They can catch a cold and have a real risk of dying from that cold, even after spending the whole time in a hospital bed. In battle they can savagely beat the limbs off another character in hand to hand combat in a trance state. All Flavor adders that Stranded: Alien Dawn lacks.

It really feels more like a more simple resource management and colony simulator style game, no real risk, pretty relaxing, with fairly trivial waves of bugs to fight and uninspiring building mechanics filled with limitations.

Its still a game I enjoy playing and probably will still play in the future considering Mods could probably greatly enhance the title, but its a far stretch from the amount of detail, suspense, and joy Rimworld has.\t\t\t\t\t
