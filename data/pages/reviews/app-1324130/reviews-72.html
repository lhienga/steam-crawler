\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have yet, despite 500+ hours played (or paused ðŸ˜‰), to complete a playthrough! It's not for any other reason than I like to see how the different sets of survivors interact and how their skills work with each other.

One of the times, I had a young man and his grandma on the same set of survivors. I did not expect that they would end up to be enemies. But they did! It was hilarious.

I have also learned that my penchant for loading up a massive base right away does not work well in this game. With just four survivors to start with, you really have to plan out what you're going to do, when, and with what priority in order to do well at the beginning.

So basically, I've been having a grand time replaying the beginnings over and over to see if I could do better. Also, since it has random map seeds, you can play a different location each time. You also have the option to type in any seeds you want to. So if you hit on one you like, you can always type it back in again.

I have been naming my game saves the same as the map seeds. That way, if I ever do feel like being a cheater, I can go back and replay the same map again. This way also I can try to pick up where I left off on some of my previous games if I want to.

Before getting this, I tried to play Dwarf Fortress. The idea of it sounded amazing but it was just so ugly I couldn't do it. But this is pretty nice. The graphics are beautiful, it's deep enough, it's not as full of depth and complexity as Dwarf Fortress, but it is very good. And it is not butt ugly, like Dwarf Fortress is. I vastly prefer Stranded.

I also appreciate that the game comes already set up with excellent in-game descriptions and tips on everything, so I don't spend time reading online. It's actually one of my favorite things about the experience so far. It reminds me of the old days, when I could just play a game and learn while playing. Learning stuff on your own can be a lot of fun!

It's also nice that it has various difficulty levels and scenarios you can work with. If you're looking just a kick back, you can. If you're looking to survive a nightmare, you can. At least you can try. If you want to help them escape, you can. If you want to play so that they want to buy the whole planet, you can.

I like that flexibility. It's nice. And I'm really enjoying this game. I highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so similar to Surviving Mars (another Haemimont Game) that is basically *IS* Surviving Mars from a different view point. Instead of purposely landing on a non-earth planet, you crash onto one. The rest is pretty much the same - all the way down to the insanity your people face from being... well, bored.

My biggest issue was that there are so many tasks to take on, and you almost never find more survivors to help out. In 13 hours of game play, I found ONE, leaving me with four people to do 20+ tasks at once. Cooking has to be done almost constantly. Gathering the meat (hunting) and vegetables (farming) almost constantly. Maybe a small break here and there in those, but not much. Then you have to do tailoring a lot too because your clothes wear out. That leaves maybe one person to build? Oh but they have to do research too. And keep fires lit. And defend the area. And heal others when they are sick or injured. Or maybe use the hot air balloon to find more... metal, maybe? Anything but people to help, right?

Sure, you can reach a point of automating tasks, such as defense, but most tasks still have to be done by a person. And you just don't have enough man-power to go around. And instead of traveling out to find more man-power, you're stuck trying to keep your people fed, clothed, and sane.

Or just risk letting one of them die so you can find another to help. But that defeats the purpose of finding more people to begin with.

And yes, I had the option of finding more survivors turned to the max. But still only found one within 13 hours of playing (on fast forward mode too, because anything less than that leaves you just watching the game for 75% of the play time).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one is hard to recommend, although I want to. It's clearly a copy of Rimworld, but only a narrow amount of the basic aspects of Rimworld are implemented so just by that fact it's disappointing right out of the gate. If someones going to copy a game they should at least be adding to it.

CONS
Compared to Rimworld it's got such a limited number of things to build and therefore a limited technology tree, it's probably 20% of what Rimworld is, and really doesn't have anything that Rimworld DOESN'T have except nice graphics.

The nice graphics apparently comes at a cost though because you can only have 10 settlers maximum, and even at 10 with a decent PC I can notice performance dropping. Also there's no real good reason to keep playing when you've exhausted all the things you can build (which is very limited) because your colony can't grow because you can't have any more settlers.

Also I have a ton of crashes, usually revolving around if I'm task-switching. The autosave is thankfully aggressive and when the population limit is reached there's no reason to build anything more and therefore no work lost really when it inevitably crashes, so I haven't been too inconvenienced.

Also it's unclear how some things work, like what the difference between a fridge and a freezer is other than in a fridge food will still decompose. But logically in the real world some foods shouldn't be put in a freezer, especially when it's fresh cooked (like a stew) yet apparently it's fine to put in a freezer. Anyway a lot of little things like this are unexplained or fiddly.

Toggling things is a UI nightmare. For example you can click on an animal's corpse and then toggle it on/off for a colonist to butcher. But it's not clear of the state of the toggle, it's just got a green checkmark or a red X...so when it's a green check does that mean when I click it that means colonists will be able to butcher the animal or does it mean they can butcher the animal as it is? A tooltip saying "Right now colonists can butcher the animal, click to prevent animals from butchering this animal" is all it would take to clarify things, but it shows that the devs are not really thinking about the user experience because there are lots of little things like this in the game as of writing.

There is no underground mining which is a disappointment. "Mining" in this game is restricted to rock picking. No underground bunkers for YOU.

PROS
The graphics are great and having a 3d version of Rimworld is fantastic in principle. If they add in more reasons to build things, for example building stuff for people's bedrooms to make them happier, this would be a really great game. It's a decent game but should be early access still until there's more added.

SUMMARY
Right now if you already have Rimworld there's no reason to buy this game since this is a watered down version of Rimworld just with nice graphics. Keep checking to see what the devs do, if they continue to add things then I'd eventually recommend this game for sure!
