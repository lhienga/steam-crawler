\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very fun, satisfying base-building survival game. Some features are well developed and polished while in some ways, this game is lacking and needs some quality of life updates. However, what is here is mostly really good, plus there are mods which help.

The good:
- good variability in survivors, biomes, modes, custom settings and random events adding some variety to saves
- the survival and exploration loop is really fun and engaging for people who enjoy that aspect of base-building games
- a good tutorial which helps in games like this (although it is annoyingly on rails and doesn't let you enjoy and observe what you've done but rushes you on to the next tutorial before you have fully understood what you actually did)

The bad:
- some bugs that have not been fixed and endless stutter at the highest speed when you have a lot of survivors
- the AI and pathing is a bit janky
- the DLC story and gameplay is terrible - I have never wanted a character to die as much as I wanted Hope to so that my misery would be over - I abandoned the campaign halfway through. However, it adds great items to the base game so for that alone, I recommend the DLC

Overall, this is a good base-building survival game for more casual players - it doesn't have the same depth as some other similar games but that's one of the things I liked about it. It's a stress-free way to wind down in the evenings (aside from occasional yelling at survivors and their dumb AI).\t\t\t\t\t

I've put a lot of hours in to Rimworld, and I think the reason there is because there are so many permutations and weird stories that come up in that game - thats what keeps me coming back to it. In Stranded : Alien Dawn, that is not the case though. You don't really get the suspense of different interactions and strange permutations of neighboring tribes... endless moments where random people could join or leave. S:AD has a very static approach to forming your caravan and embarking on your journey. Its less chaotic, sure but its definitely a bit more boring to play this way. In S:AD you will only ever encounter the static characters that you can choose your initial group formation. The interactions those characters have is not all that interesting, and accessing the log of what conversations happened, what their preferences are or what makes up their personality is a little cumbersome and not as rewarding by comparison to something like Rimworld.

But if you're the type of player who has never experienced a Survival Sim Base Builder, this could be a good entry in to your series. If you're veteraned up from your experience in Rimworld, you'll probably find this game to be a bit more like the "diet coke/walmart" version... If you are a hardcore Rimworld enthusiast and play with various mods, creating religions, experiencing all that RW has to offer - you'll find this game super boring.

Like I said, its ok though. The graphics are pretty fair for a 3D adaptation. I'm 20 hours in and I feel like I've experienced all this game has to offer, make of that what you will.\t\t\t\t\t
