\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery solid game, a lot of fun to play. It's a deep Rimworld clone (moreso than any other game I've played) and I must confess that after playing this game for 18 hours I stopped and went back and played RImworld for another 30!! But I think that's a complement itself, in some ways.

The game looks great, the core loop (collect, build, defend, explore, repeat) is satisfying and fun. And I think as more content is added (not forgetting it's about 7 years' behind RImworld in terms of dev time) it's going to get more and more fun. The base is already good times.

My main criticisms are that the combat is pretty meh and uninteresting without much variety, and once you've got a colony pretty much up and running and secure the challenge dissolves a fair bit. Most challenges that are thrown at you don't really put you in a state of peril or panic.

My largest gripe is actually the camera. The map is large and gorgeous but without being able to zoom out further and/or have a mini map and/or hotkey locations it's a giant pain in the ass to actually use much of the map that isn't right by your base. So again you start to feel pretty contained. And not a lot narratively happens once the game gets cooking.

So yeah, if you like the genre of colony survival you'll have a grand old time as it's solid, fun, approachable. But if you're hankering for cybernetically augmented vampires battling against a home-brewed all feminine corporate religion like my last Rimworld playthrough, don't expect that level of insane depth.\t\t\t\t\t
