\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDenuvo and their weird choices on the EULA aside... This is one of those "I wish there were a neutral button" reviews. This game was REALLY fun initially and I am hesitant to post a negative review after only paying $30 for 30 hours of entertainment but I probably won't play this game again after getting to a point where the micromanagement of ridiculous tasks started impacting my ability to enjoy the rest of the game. This was sad because I really was having a good time to this point.

The main source of my frustration is from the wear and tear mechanic, or more specifically how to avoid that mechanic. You'll find yourself manually un-equipping and reequipping items constantly in this game for various reasons since there's no interface which allows you to automate this that I'm aware of. Your futuristic weapons have no option for repair or recharge and for some absurd reason even if never fired while equipped will break within a few years. The same goes for your synthetic high tech armor, and presumably just about anything else you can wear in the game. So, you're left with a decision to either remove them manually after combat (season or weather change etc) or watch them wither away. I can understand a straw hat made from hay lasting a year but a pistol or a set of synthetic armor being destroyed after 3 years is pretty silly.

This mechanic would not be a huge deal at all if switching gear was easy, reliable, or if there was a way to automate it but as it stands it takes way too much time to do this and it snaps me right out of any immersion I had going on at that moment. I realize they have to create scarcity but this is just not a well thought out way to do it in my opinion and a very simple solution would be to decrease the decay on items which make sense. There is really no danger from the combat (for the most part) in the normal difficulty mode so I'm assuming this is their way of balancing it a bit once you start getting better items but it just makes for a frustrating experience.

The rest of the game is a solid 8/10 so if you don't mind micromanagement on that scale you'll probably like it.\t\t\t\t\t
