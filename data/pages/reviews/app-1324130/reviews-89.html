\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6/10

Pretty much a carbon copy of Rimworld but better because the characters are more personalized and with better graphics and animations. Unfortunately the game still falls short in a lot of the same ways that Rimworld did including the fact that water isn't a necessary material for survival and that the game is jam-packed with random events that can either make your campaign fun and exciting or force you to start over. Guns are stronger in this than in Rimworld and so both hunting and defending your structures is easier and more enjoyable. One of my characters just had a nervous breakdown because it got dark outside while they were not in the compound while maintaining about 5 happiness boosts including food variety and leisure activities. Poor balance, poor design, and a shameless copy of another game, but still decent especially for the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hesitated for a long time before purchasing this game so many look good and have decent reviews. You purchase them and by the time you find out it is meh you can't return it. Well I am glad I purchased this one. The farther I got into the game the more I enjoyed it.

Like similar survival games you are somewhere you shouldn't be trying to live through the ordeal. In this case you have crash landed on an alien world. You get to pick your four starting survivors from a group of more than a dozen each with different personality traits, which hinder or help. You can of course opt to get four at random from this group and you can elect to find more survivors.

There are several starting options and planet types. Your survivors usually make pretty good choices, but if you want you can give them specific commands. The game follows a logical process of finding shelter, food, tools and weapons. Parts of your ship and others seem to fall to the ground near your base all the time, so scavenging is important. Each survivor will increase their ability scores if a related task is performed enough...Combat, Construction, Research etc.

Your goal is to get off the rock you crashed on and you can go small with just enough infrastructure to do it or build a larger advanced base. The game throws curve balls at you like all such games, but they are not overpowered. An interesting twist at the end, you cannot extract more than one person at a time, so at some point just before the win you will be down to defending your base with one person.

The graphics are adequate and I never ran into any bugs. It plays smoothly even with hundreds of giant bugs attacking your base. I really only have a couple of could be better items regarding the game. At this time there is no way to set a number limit on what your people gather. This means often if your not careful you can fill all your storage with a bumper crop of something and have no room for anything else. This is bad because like many games of this type keeping the people happy is important. Having a variety of food types helps and if your storage is full of one food type..well you get the picture.

The second issue is how fast somethings breakdown. Freezers, electric lights, solar panels, batteries, clothing, weapons, all breakdown at an alarming rate. I never had a freezer last a year without breaking down. It's easier to just hang several A/C units on a storage room and set to below freezing. For some reason A/C units last much longer than many other items before break down. Except for weapons and clothing, which actually wears out and has to be replaced, most everything else can be repaired by your survivors. Expect to see your survivors devoting more than a reasonable amount time and resources to fixing broken down equipment. My first game half my survivors were running around in their underwear at one point, because their clothing had worn out and I hadn't prioritized making new clothes.

You will have walls, buildings etc. damaged by attacks and lightning and a nice thing about this game, even if they are destroyed a ghost image will be retained at their previous location and your survivors set to construction will repair or replace them as they were if resources are available. No tedious replacing every piece of knocked down wall after every attack.

The game actually has a lot of depth and having higher ability scores allows your survivors to use more and create better items. Your people can break if unhappy for too long and it is actually funny to watch. Some will go on a drinking binge. Others will hide or cry, I even had one go berserk running around pounding on the walls and equipment. They all seem to recover after a fit, but if happiness is not improved it will happen again and sooner next time.

One element I liked, but the implementation was a little weird to me was expeditions. Your survivors can make a hot air balloon and use it to explore around their area. This is one special balloon too, because your survivors can somehow direct this balloon to other crash sites and back. This is done abstractly. You see smoke on the horizon etc. and you pick a survivor to go in the balloon on an expedition to that location. Must be a radio in this balloon as well, because they will call back to you after a while with a report and request instructions. The expedition idea works well, the balloon not so much.

Too many games made now end with nothing, no closing, no information. I liked the ending format used by Alien Dawn. First you get an account of important events that occurred during the game in a story telling style, followed by a more standard list of how many deaths etc.

Anyway I really enjoy this game over 60 hours into it already and had it less than a week. (Yes I'm retired gaming is my career now :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the price, the game is very fun and seems like the type of thing one can pick up and put down over time. If you like Rimworld base game, you'll probably like this game for at least long enough to play through the various scenarios. That's worth $35 imo. Modding might stretch that further, although the game just came out so it's hard to predict what the modding scene will look like.

With that said, there are some major concerns:
The game is almost a direct copy of Rimworld but with prettier 3D graphics, down to the social mechanics, work priority assignment screen, mental breaks, color of building schematics, etc. It would not be unfair to say they plagiarized Rimworld, an indie game made and maintained by a single person!

The two improvements over Rimworld's mechanics that I can think of are the implementation of automation in the base game and that light/darkness affects ranged weapon accuracy. The automation system is awesome, but it does leave some to be desired because there are only 9 automation "channels," making it difficult to set up independent automated systems with similar purposes. For example, there are motion detectors that one can use to turn on auto-turrets and floodlights near gates/checkpoints. Well, if you only have one spare automation channel to use and your motion sensors are turning on all your turrets anyway, then it's not really saving you any electricity! It's still very good for things like turning on generators to keep your batteries charged when your wind and solar generators aren't enough or running a day/night cycle for your electric lights though.

The decision to use Denuvo by the devs is also very unfortunate. Anti-cheat software in a single-player game? I fully understand that piracy is a concern for game studios, but Denuvo functions exactly like malware: running in the background without your explicit consent, hogging RAM, causing bloat in the game itself, and excessive wear and tear on your SSD. If it walks like a duck and quacks like a duck... If just one dedicated hacker manages to still crack the game, then there's no point to having it and whatever fees Denuvo charged will be wasted anyway.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRimworld 2.0, in 3D. There is already an active modding community, so if you feel something is lacking rest assured there is or will be a mod for that purpose.
That being said, if clothing could be automated, that would be great.
Also, devs, please allow for some sort of slider for the invasions; might just had been the moon I was under, but getting four invasions in seven days killed a run dead.
What I am waiting on is a mod like the one Rimworld has for customizing your people; their traits, background, age, etc,. and making custom scenarios.
Biggest time sink by far is going to be just waiting on research and observations, so plan around that if you are struggling to get a footing down. Look up Rimworld strats on kill zones and funneling for managing the pests if that is an issue.
Finally, I will say this game is completely doable solo on insane, which is always my test for survival games. Just takes a lot longer. Fun way to learn all the ropes before trying to monkey with the job management aspect yet.\t\t\t\t\t
