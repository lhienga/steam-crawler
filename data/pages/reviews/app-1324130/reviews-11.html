\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey make the game look so fun in the trailer.
The issues with the game start to show when you see the menial AI attacks that hit your base every so often, this can be tweaked sure, but no amount of tweaking frequency in attacks changed the fact that the AI itself sucks donkey shlong. The game play loop ends up becoming the usual, "farm and get ready for winter," with these attacks that really break pace in the game. This creates a system that is frankly kind of lack luster and doesn't know what it wants.

The game introduces expeditions for your survivors, giving little text based decisions that might result in resources or another survivor. The game caps out at 8/8 survivors, show me another base building management game that is that ass. You could honestly play something like this on game cube or something with its sort of bland game loop.

The only thing going for it are its graphics, which already leave a lot to be desired if it is to be considered its redeeming factor. I haven't even gotten into the DLC policy which shows exactly how the company sees you, a money bag. To top this all off, there are whispers a second one is coming out already.

skip this one.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot for 35 dollars, maybe when it goes on sale again. I do like that it has Z levels, something sorely lacking in a lot of colony sims, and I do like some of the character interactions.

But I don't care about realistic graphics so right about there is where the leg up it has on the competition ends because almost every other major colony sim has it pummeled into the ground in terms of scope and depth. The game it reminds me most of is "Going Medieval" which is still in early access.

Immediate reactions were negative as I realized all characters are fixed. With no character customization or randomization it strikes me as very strange, that is almost par the course for these sort of games and seeing it missing was bizarre. Limits replayability and one of my favorite things is modeling the characters after my friends, though I am sure there's a mod somewhere for it.

Very minor complaint, the tutorial railroads you. It is a tutorial so who cares but it got so obnoxious for me that I quit midway through. Every time you complete a certain number of objectives it resets everything and hard locks you out of certain commands, even ones you learned in the last stint of the tutorial. For an obsessive compulsive person like me that was very annoying.

The thing that kind of tore it for me though was the tech tree. It is so strange to me to have area management hidden behind a tech. I didn't even notice on my first play through, but toxic ash happened and I tried to get everyone restricted to in doors. No dice, gotta research that because unless you write a thesis on staying in doors without being perpetually drafted that is too heady a concept for these people.

These are easy enough fixes, so my opinion might change. But even with these fixes for the same price you can get something that is way more immersive and deep, even if your dudes are ASCII or legless Hello Kitty looking freaks\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm giving this a positive review even though I don't think it's really a game I'm going to continue playing and I didn't enjoy it much. That said, the things I didn't enjoy are - I think? - part of the genre of games so it seemed unfair to give them a bad review. Anyway

What I liked
1. The graphics are quite nice
2. It sort of reminded me of Oxygen Not Included, in that you have a limited number of Replicants doing tasks

What I didn't like
1. This is a very weird complaint, but there's too much to do, too fast. It's overwhelming. After four hours of gameplay I've unlocked like 9 types of food and a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of tech and I haven't even gotten to electricity. Because there's so much at once, I didn't really get what the game wanted me to do, in terms of progression and rather than feeling fun to figure it out, it felt like a checklist chore

2. Man, the characters melt down a lot

3. There's this weird mix of micromanagement and overall management that I couldn't quite get the hang of

4. The crafting menu UI is super confusing. Sometimes 1=10 things but sometimes 1=1 thing and it's hard to tell if you have the stuff you need and there's so many recipes it's hard to figure out what to do

Overall I think my dislike of this game is more on me than on the game, so - positive review\t\t\t\t\t
