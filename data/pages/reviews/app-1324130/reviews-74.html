\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Good, but wait for sale, currently worth $20-$25 (imo)
Hope updates and dlc is on its way!

Good game, but stops short of being great. It's similar to other survival sims, but maintains its own unique style and it does it well.
Sadly, it sometimes feels bland. In short: the maps, flora, fauna, and enemies could use more variety and diversity. The Sims lack deep interaction. The story options are limited.
All that being said, I consider those things to be minor problems. The game looks stunning and is still fun to play through. The kind of game you can play for short periods, move on, come back and repeat. The progressing and research tree, while slow at times, feel nice and have some variety, based off of your crew.

In my opinion, I recommend it, but its currently not worth $35 due to how much you really get out of it.
I would say to buy it when it drops to around $20 to $25\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStranded is a solid offering at it's price point. I'd give this game a 3.75 out of 5, and it could easily pick up some ground with some small tweaks. Lots of base building, resource management, a little combat, and the RNG keeps you guessing on some variables. Worth the pickup to relax while between other games if you need a change of pace, or just want to veg out in your chair for an hour before it's time to crash.

The good:
Randomly generated maps, resources, events, and even any potential new characters that you pick up after your start? Nice touch.

I dig the farming system. There is a little variance in terms of what you may or may not find on some maps, but for the most part it gives you a lot of what you need - while putting strain on the amount of time you have to complete tasks. The more you invest in your farming, the less you can craft or build things. Nice balance with this.

The random groups of monsters that attack your little camp is a fun way to force you into action - and avoid this from basically being sim city - from time to time. Anything from little inserts to a giant mantis type creature. Kill em, and harvest those bodies for food or other resources! I like it.

I enjoy the research aspect as well. There are a handful of random projects that will vary from game to game that gives you a nice boost, and can change how you have to play.

Exploration gives a nice twist to getting additional resources, characters, or exploring some of the unknown on the planet.

Middle ground:
The OST is so-so. Could have been better, but it's not a train wreck either.

Equipment feels like the standard offering, so I can't knock it, but the recent addition of mechs so far feels pretty OP. I haven't played on the highest difficulty or with hardcore modifiers, so maybe they are intended to be more for the harder game modes, but wow they mow down anything in site. Jury is out for now.

The bad:
The AI/pathing drives me bonkers half the time. While the system to prioritize/schedule things is a great concept, I find the computer taking longer routes to get to locations it needs to go. There are times when one of your characters will pick up only 50%-75% of the resources around it and head toward the storage location - while they could have still picked up a bit more that they walk right past! I will also watch at times the character that I dedicate to farming switch back and forth between harvesting and planting the same field - or sometimes multiple fields! The pathing is getting marginally better, but it still needs work. I will watch characters bounce back and forth from stack to stack as if it's considering the location of that stack from the distance it is from the starting point, instead of the current location. Over the course of farming a plot of land, harvesting that plot, or having someone run over to pick up food that was just harvested, it makes the total distance traveled a LOT longer, meaning more time invested in the activity. These type of issue forces you to manually manage individual tasks when you shouldn't have to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has a solid foundation and shows promising potential. However, it is crucial that the developers have innovative expansion ideas in order to prevent the game from becoming repetitive. Currently, the gameplay consists of gathering resources, constructing structures, defending against wave attacks, and repeating the cycle.

Here are my suggestions for improvements and enhancements:

Enhance survivor interaction and commands: It would be beneficial to incorporate more opportunities for survivors to interact with each other and for players to give them specific commands. This would add depth to the gameplay and make the survivors feel more dynamic.

Introduce water interaction: Adding water mechanics could introduce new elements such as fishing and hidden dangers. This would diversify the gameplay experience and make the environment more immersive.

Expand enemy attacks: Currently, the game only features waves of bugs as the main enemy. It would be refreshing to introduce larger and more formidable alien creatures to increase the challenge and excitement.

Incorporate vehicle research and salvage: Including a system for researching and utilizing vehicles would provide players with additional strategies and options for exploration and combat. Salvaging resources from vehicles or using them as transportation could add a new dimension to the gameplay.

Improve gear management: The process of switching gear becomes tedious when multiple survivors simultaneously experience wear and tear on their equipment. Implementing a more streamlined system for managing and repairing gear would alleviate this issue and enhance the overall player experience.

Introduce drones: Considering the futuristic setting of the game, incorporating drones as a gameplay element could be a compelling addition. Drones could serve various purposes such as scouting, resource gathering, or assisting in combat.

These are just my thoughts and suggestions to enhance the game and make it more engaging and enjoyable for players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have nearly 100 hours on this game, so I can say fully with confidence: This game rocks.

First off, shout out to the devs, who had been making impactful updates ones a month for 6 months straight. We got a new planet, new scenarios, new characters and abilities - and now that it's out of Early Access, it really has a ton to provide.

I really value the replayability of this game. Though I fully understand the mechanics and functions of the game, each new seed offers a different layout and its own challenges therein, before even having to change the difficulty level. I love that you can randomize characters for an even bigger challenge (or advantage!), and I've pretty much started a new game with every update during early access and now just to see the full effects of it all.

The visuals and design are so much fun and for the most part the survivors are smart enough to get tasks done on their own. The hierarchy system for activities is fantastic, and especially for the scenarios with larger numbers of survivors, being able to designate certain types of tasks for them makes everything really efficient. Having the survivors want to have celebrations, music nights, etc. is really nice, and I wish it happened even more than it already does. It's fun that they can also fall in love and get married - there's nothing like romance when stranded on an alien planet!

The game is not without its flaws, but I would say that only rarely did any of these things annoy me enough to really be a bother.

Nit-picks would be as follows:
- When butchering animals with meat and hides, the survivors always take the hides back to the camp first, and not the meat, which goes bad a lot quicker/birds will eat at. It should probably be the other way around.
- Speaking of, the hauling system I would say could be smarter. Expanding the hauling area when a survivor is going to pick something up would mean they have less trips to make. They can usually handle the quantity of items that are around, but they'll only go for items within a small range of their original haul unless you direct them further.
- The randomized aspects can be too much sometimes: I spent nearly a year scavenging and salvaging on one map before I got any electronics, which meant I couldn't get electric items nearly as quickly. Yes, I could take a balloon, but even just a few of all the electronics bits should exist in the spawns nearish to the starting point.
- More music. The one track is nice, but having some variations of it would even make a better impact.
- Prioritizing activities: right now, when you prioritize multiple activities for survivors, they do them left to right, and you can't switch the order (which is frustrating). I wish there was a way to prioritize the items/actions on the map (i.e. constructing something, tailoring something, salvaging, etc.) so that survivors who have it marked as a priority will just get it done first, and then default to their hierarchy.

Overall, a fantastic game you can play over and over again, and knowing even more updates are coming, one that will always have something new and exciting for you to explore and experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeems like a shallower duplicate of Rimworld (with none of Rimworld's after-release content or expansions), but with the same pool of characters each play through. After ~30 hours, it inspired me to fire Rimworld back up.

The first few hours are enjoyable, and I like the idea of researching the local wildlife/plants in order to be able to domesticate the animals or grow the plants. BUT the plants and animals are the same each play through (e.g. orange mushrooms are always food/healing plant) so there is no "discovery" as the player.

I like that it is "nicer" than Rimworld (fewer gritty backstories, fewer disfiguring injuries, no slavery, and less human killing generally), but that's not enough to make up for the lack of depth.

Honestly, the 3D aspect (which some probably like) is a neutral to me - I do like building multi-level houses, but the UI is painful with uncontrollable layers. Attacking a bug standing under a tree involves pausing, zooming all the way in, and moving the mouse one pixel at a time until the "attack" cursor finally appears.\t\t\t\t\t
