\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved Rimworld; this is not as in-depth as that game on a number of levels. However, it's excellent at what it does which is bringing the scope down; giving a 3D paint job; and centralizing the game to your primary settlement. Go through the MOD list though; there are some great ones that add a little more to the mix such as elimination of endgame reqs, adding androids etc.
My first 13 hour game ended as such:
Researched; production up to a mid sized settlement with 13 survivors.
Fending off multiple attacks over time but ultimately taken down by a group of 43 of those spike throwing aliens. (Definite Starship Troopers vibe)

The only survivor was my first military/soldier focused settler. But his wounds were too grave and he couldn't self heal so he was steadily bleeding out. Also under his health tab was -10 happiness for each of the 12 other settlers who died; my man was PTSD depressed. So what did he do? After the battle he went to the Leisure room I made, played darts as he was bleeding out, worked out on the bench as he was bleeding out. After which when he was done with his work out he just stood up and died. I'm fairly certain if I had bothered to build the items I needed to make Alcohol he would have had a beer first.

I've played for 13 hours total and will stop for awhile with BG3 release tomorrow. However I fully intend on giving it another go down the road after that cinematic ending on my first run. Give it a try if you liked Rimworld; I'm excited with the potential this game has either through further Developer content or the modding community.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game I really want to love, but just don't.

It's beautiful, it has great mechanics (for the most part), the controls are pretty good (that there's no way to turn off edge scrolling is my biggest complaint!) The core of a solid and highly polished game appears to be here...

...but it's just... boring.

There's a deep research tree, but progressing through it manages to feel both trivial and a chore at the same time, and unlocking stuff never feels like a reward, because it just means "more stuff to do" when you're already constrained by how much stuff you can actually get done with your characters.

This ends up being a running theme: every bit of new gameplay you unlock ends up being a "so what?" because you're already capped on how much stuff you can fit into the schedule, and the new things are largely just the same things as the basics, so what's the point? You've already got that covered. You don't ever feel that you're progressing.

Then you get escalating alien attacks that appear to have little to do with your progress and exist "just because". I've encountered no themic explanation of why a bunch of random critters spawn outside my base and attack me every 15 minutes. It just feels super gamey and immersion breaking, and rather than building tension it's just an annoyance, one you either deal with easily or utterly wrecks you.

There doesn't seem to be any actual gameplay mechanism to ride this difficulty curve without tech rushing combat stuff and doing nothing else, and hoping RNG doesn't slap you with a bunch of permanent negative modifiers on your colonists that will degrade your save for the rest of eternity.

None of this feels like a challenge, it just feels arbitrary and leads you to hoping you notice your already-slow colonist has randomly been slapped with a -10% speed modifier for the rest of the game before you progress too much further so that you can save-scum to just avoid having even more fun sapped out of the tedium.

Oh right, tedium. Game is hella tedious. Even on max speed, everything takes FOREEEEEVER to do. Not helped by the fact that colonists will take so much time walking even the length of your base to chop a single tree of wood before immediately turning around to go get a meal, then walk back, chop another tree, then walk back again to play darts, then walk back to chop a tree, then get tired. I get it, building an efficient base is a thing, but colonists move so slowly that you need to build a full set of entertainment buildings and food stores and everything every 20 squares to stop this, and I'm already bored just writing that, let alone hating the idea of idiotic base designs to deal with gameified mechanics.

And ultimately, what's the point? The game never feels like it has a point. It doesn't feel like you're telling a story like Rimworld or other self-directed experiences, nothing that interesting ever happens. I've sunk thousands of hours into games like Dwarf Fortress, Rimworld, Banished, and Factorio, and every single new save I created in those felt like it had a story, even the ones I set out with no theme or idea behind. I remember those experiences even years later.

This game, I load my save from yesterday and I'm like "so, what was I doing? I dunno and I don't really care", at no point have I felt like I have even a self-imposed objective I'm working towards. Nothing at all ever feels like progress or a success. It just feels like a grind to nowhere, and then the game will tell me I've won. Or that I've died.

Either outcome fills me with the same indifference.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is the fastest way to go from "I have nowhere to store this huge amount of stuff" to "where are my f. stuff?"
The game is fun, but lacks a lot of basic mechanics and balance for this genre. It's hard to believe it's not early access.
- Storage system is bad, you can only remove items from storage if they are spoiled or if you block all of the same type to be stored there. Also you run out of storage VERY fast, but you run out of resources also very fast. Sometimes you don't know where else to put them, sometimes you are desperate to get some of the same item.
- If you have a char mining 3km away from your base, it will very often just finish mining 1 rock and return to base to smoke a pipe (leaving the items there). That's frustrating and makes you want to micro-manage everything so the game doesn't make these stupid decisions.
- Some activities in the game demand too much time, which leaves you with just a few minutes to explore, almost no time to spend on travels, I have a lot of points of interest but if I send anyone I'll lack resources.
- Crafting and Cooking take A LOT of time, even if your char is lvl 10 on that.
- You can only tell your chars to craft something if that something is on the queue list and you didn't reach the limit in the queue.
- You'll hardly know if there was something being worked on the crafting table until you see someone taking it out of it.
- Rebuilding rooftops is a terrible experience.
- Animals management is non existent. I mean, there's a screen, but they won't tell you which is male or female, which is pregnant, and which is fully grown up. Also, there's no way to automate the killing for resources and don't overpopulate. Also you can't choose how much food you are placing for the animals, so you waste a lot of resources cause they'll spoil very fast. Also some animals eat a lot, so you must have a dedicated person just on farming.
- Animals in combat are useless, you have to micro-manage them all the time, or they'll stop attacking. (my dog trained lvl 10 is stupid like a door)
- Energy options are very limited.
- Vents don't seem to work.
- Guessing which wire channel you put every equipment is hard. (there's no energy management screen).
- You'll take a lot of time butchering flies for 5-15 meat.
- Map is too big / Travelling takes ages.
