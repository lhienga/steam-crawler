\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like it. I just finished the third scenario. It is hard to put down. I told myself to not start a new fourth scenario for awhile, cause I procastinated other stuff to finish the scenario. We'll see.

I couldn't get into Rimworld, I see they are very similar. This is somehow more accessible, I tried a few times with Rimworld and just didn't like it. (I did have some mods, but it was already overly complex). I'm not making charts and OCD style bases. I'm kinda surprised I liked it, but I liked Frostpunk and some survival games and sometimes a tower defense game (Defense Grid) and there's light RPG elements, as you build out your base an maze the wildlife. The random events can be a nailbiter, I just played the military mission and literally had to unplug most of the base the last few days to barely have enough energy to win -- as an attack started (that would have drained those batteries to nothing).

It could use some minor quality of life adjustments, the biggest one I see is survivors will often be partially unarmed, you have to check on them. Though they will at least dress themselves. I also see them walk a great distance to start a project, and then barely start, turn around and go eat. Bring a box lunch once in awhile geeze (Edit: the survival pack is more trouble than it's worth, it does stop but maybe ain't worth the trouble). Most of the rest of what I thought were issues had to do with me not getting the priorities right (assemble!), but I knew the basic system from failing to like Rimworld.

I bought it during a sale, kinda surprised I did, but it was worth it.

----
I worked my way up to insane, basic level. It's brutal and needs savescumming this run.

The game is surprisingly bug free, often I just don't get a system. The one exception is an npc with a mental breakdown (like about to start a drinking bender) will walk right into a field of bugs -- and they sometimes spawn real close to your npcs in the hinterlands.

My one major complaint is you can't rename the NPCs. Huge pet peeve of mine, esp since I can rename the robots, etc. Why not the NPCs? I'm playing singleplayer for cryin' out loud.\t\t\t\t\t

I love farming games but I like how this one doesn't necessarily focus on it. You can reserve space for certain farming types (food or supplies) and you don't have to worry about manually planting or harvesting or finding seeds. Even fertilizing is automatic as long as you have manure. Crafting is easily managed once you get the hang of it and fighting gets progressively difficult as time passes. The game isn't perfect, there are some annoying controls like not being able to easily store items for drying or reducing the amount of food that is stored for animal feeding, but the rest makes up for it. I've spent many hours (80+) without even realizing how much time has gone by. Definitely worth the buy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA small group survival game with different missions to choose from. You are stranded on an alien world and must complete the different mission types you selected in order to win.

Pros:
Fun and rich environment
Research and study the environment
You can grow crops and farm the native creatures
You can control your survivors by managing there tasks and sleep schedules.
You will be attack by random creatures so get those defenses up.
Each person has there own set of skills and personality.
You get some different gear, weapons, food, and building material.


Cons:
You cannot prioritize construction without having to cancel or prohibit a building if you need the resources for other buildings. This can become a problem if you trying to build defenses or prevent power loss.

The attacks are not organic. The enemy will spawn just outside of your base. Instead of a herd forming and rushing your base. They just appear near your base and then starts the attack.

The workers needs some tweaking when it comes to working hours. If it is getting near the end of there shift they should work closer to the base so they are not out past working hours and come back tired, and mad.
Too many time I have to reassign them to something else near the end of there shift so they do not go across the map to mine something with one hour left on there shift.
