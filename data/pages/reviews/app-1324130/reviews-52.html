\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you ever watched the Love, Death & Robots episode 'Suits' and thought, "That would be a cool game,"* and/or you like colony management sims like Banished, Dawn of Man, or Going Medieval, and you like sci-fi, this is a great game. It feels most similar to Going Medieval, but different in some ways and more polished in others. It's challenging but not frustratingly difficult on normal. You can make terrible decisions and ruin your settlement, so saving frequently is recommended, but once you go through all the tutorials and a few game restarts, you'll stop making terrible decisions, mostly. Progressing technology from stick huts to mech suits feels really satisfying. It takes a long time to build up the settlement in a stable way, but you really get a sense of progression between where your villagers started and where they ended the scenario. Graphically it's the most beautiful colony management game I've played. And the devs have continued to pump out content for it post-release, for free. I recommend this game very highly.

Fun with a few issues

Stranded: Alien Dawn is a enjoyable colony management experience because of all the elements that need to be managed to be successful. As a player you have to dance around keeping each colonist needs met. Be it Rest, hunger, happiness, and relaxation all while dealing with seasons, disasters, illnesses, social traits and attacks.

Through all of this stuff you need to explore the local area and expand your camp while doing research to accomplish your goals. But you also need to skill up your survivors so they can do more complex tasks like build more advanced things and go on expeditions. I found all of this to make an enjoyable gaming experience.

Minor Issues
The game has an odd performance problem that does not seem to have anything to do with how big you build a colony. The game just has some unexplainable performance drops that seem Inexplicable.

it seems like the day / night cycle has a problem during the winter season. During the night time it's too bright and in the morning their is an abrupt drop in light.

Criticisms
Colonist interactions with each other are more often negative causing a problematic happiness drops.

Negative happiness events stack but positive ones do not stack.

Some colonist traits are unnecessarily annoying. More specifically rest recharges at half the speed for one colonist. While another is constantly vomiting because of an implant.

Overall I like the game and the issues are not enough to get in the way of having fun with this one. Thumbs up for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 123 hours of play. I can give my thoughts on this game.

The core of the game is thick. With a lot of improvements waiting to be explored.
Mechanics are not too easy but it's not hard to understand. I see some people having troubles with priorities setting - I did, too. What I would advise you to do is to head over to the workshop and find the set priorities to 9. This enable you to fine-tune the priorities. I've never had problems with people going idle in the beginning until about the end (this is usually I have everything I need in terms of items and more so they will definitely idle). I'm sure I saw a review stating that the game needs to add water consumption, and I definitely agree. This will add another level of interest and something that you will pay attention to. There could be a mechanic to be able to gather snow and melt it. Or craft a bucket to collect. Maybe a rain collector?

Another thing that is frustrating to me at first is the turrets. It does not do much damage. Again, head over to workshop and find the nova turret. This is an upgrade to the current turrets but with more requirement to build.

You can also find a lot of interesting characters in the workshop. All in all, I think you really need to visit the workshop first in order to really really enjoy this game. It's something that's not essential but necessary. You can also add fishing workshop to add more task.

This game is a lot of fun even playing alone. The mechanics are great. The animation are good. The graphics is fantastic. Some might say you'll get bored very quickly, I would disagree, you can add more explorations and task simply by going to the workshop. It's no way perfect as it is, but you'll definitely find a lot of stuff that would make your gameplay a lot more enjoyable and a tad bit smoother. Thanks to the devs for going the modding path. This enables a wider audience to showcase their take on the game and what could be patched into the game itself.

I definitely recommend this game for solo and/or group of friends.\t\t\t\t\t
