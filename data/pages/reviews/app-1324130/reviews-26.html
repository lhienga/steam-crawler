\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStranded: Alien Dawn is that rare gem of fluid base building, colony management, and a bit of tower defense. My four survivors crash landed on an alien world. Three of the four were happy to be alive and got right to work - but Krista broke down crying and was worthless until she got herself sorted. As a father of two daughters, I can tell you this wasn't unexpected behavior. But eventually we built camp shelters, gathered the resources from our ship, and set out to explore the unknown. One of the great features of this game is how you must observe and study the alien flora and fauna before putting it to any use. This makes the early game interesting - especially on the first playthroughs when you, the player, are new to everything.

Once studied, each plant and animal is well described, allowing you to make intelligent decisions regarding their use as resources. This, along with the time honored Research Desk, allows you to advance your knowledge and capabilities as your settlers expand their base, building housing and defenses. And the way this game handles building vertically is, for the most part, intuitive and simple to implement. In fact, I found the user interface as a whole very intuitive and easy to work with. You might benefit from a few YouTube tips and tricks videos here and there, but for the most part you won't need them.

Regarding performance and appearance: this game is streamlined and ran perfectly for me - something many other games can't boast. And the landscapes are highly attractive - both colorful and varied enough to be appreciated.

While no game is perfect, this one offers a complete and enjoyable package for the base building enthusiasts who have enjoyed Rim World, Going Medieval, and the like. My only complaint - and it's a minor one - is that sometimes the end-game pacing can seem too drawn out. My colonists have built everything they need, have all the food and resources they could want, but now I have to wait in-game days or weeks for the end conditions to trigger. In this regard, each playthrough overstays its welcome - but only marginally. And I found this to be the case for each scenario, including Crash Landing, Trade, and Military. Having said that, though, this game offers three very satisfying scenarios; and each scenario feels like it has a beginning, middle, and end. I much prefer this to open-ended forever games, or games where the end takes soooo long to achieve, it's just not worth it. There are too many games, and too few hours in life. But, for the most part, Stranded: Alien Dawn respects your time and effort.

In the end, my base was built, my colonists survived (though some were slightly scuffed along the way), and there is a great little recap at the end of each scenario which highlights key moments of your journey. I am very thankful I found this game, and hope others find it as enjoyable as I did. 9 / 10 for me - well done!

[Next, I have to see if my colonists can survive the desert. Maybe under a different moon. And for goodness sake, will someone please cheer up Krista!]\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version

Well done game that manage to mix resources management, and fight to survive. Once you figure out how to survive, it becomes much easier.

Farming & traps next to your walls

Long version

As many mentioned, the concept of standed survivors on a strange planet having to "observe" surrounding before exploiting resources is a nice addition to the genre. AI is quite good with path while you can assign priorities.

I would however suggest the following improvements:

- list of tasks (queue): maybe it's there somewhere, could not find it, but I wish I could prioritize some task. Example, you build a few towers for protection but lack wood; while gathering resources, your base is attacked. Well, now you truly need the wood to fix your fence first.
- tailoring is slow: on normal scenario, you often end up with ragged cloths because even if you learn tailoring, you need to replace 4 to 6 players cloths. Maybe we could add some option to patch cloths so you get time to craft new cloths
- End game is easy: Usually at year 2, your base has a solid defense, food production is rolling and survivors are well equipped. Even with growing waves of enemy, it is quite easy to defend yourself safely in your towers.
- Game too short: Again, starting year 2, it's quite easy to survive, hence many game's options do not need to be implemented, i.e sensors, laser & missile automated turrets, searchlights to name a few. By then, you can easily repel any invaders;
- Exploration is really a cool feature but I find difficult to find survivors, I would like to be able to bring in many and in constant flow, to reunite family and feel like your truly doing your best to assist fellow stranded travelers.

Overall this is a very good game, well polished (only once I experienced a saving bug), actually pretty, and with innovative features. I highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
