Intergalactic Adventure with a Twist

Stranded: Alien Dawn is a game that caught my eye from the moment I heard about it. Developed by a talented team and published by an ambitious studio, the game aims to take players on a thrilling journey of survival and exploration on an alien planet. I'm happy to report that Stranded: Alien Dawn has delivered a captivating and enjoyable experience that's worth checking out.

Graphics & Visuals: 9/10

The graphics in Stranded: Alien Dawn are nothing short of impressive. The alien world is beautifully realized, with a diverse range of biomes, flora, and fauna that make exploration a visual treat. The character models are well-designed and the animations are smooth, adding to the game's overall polish. Dynamic weather and lighting effects further enhance the immersive quality of the game's visuals.

Gameplay: 9/10

Stranded: Alien Dawn offers a unique blend of survival, exploration, and crafting gameplay elements. Players must gather resources, build shelter, and craft equipment to survive the harsh environment and uncover the secrets of the alien planet. The game also introduces a variety of alien creatures, some of which are hostile, adding an extra layer of challenge and excitement. The gameplay mechanics are intuitive and well-balanced, ensuring that players never feel overwhelmed or bored.

Story & Narrative: 8.5/10

The story of Stranded: Alien Dawn is engaging and filled with intrigue. Players find themselves stranded on an uncharted alien world after a mysterious event, with their primary goal being to discover the truth behind their predicament and find a way back home. The narrative unfolds through exploration and interaction with the environment, keeping players invested in the game's unfolding mystery.

Sound & Music: 9/10

The audio design of Stranded: Alien Dawn is top-notch. The soundtrack is atmospheric and fitting, perfectly capturing the sense of wonder and danger that accompanies exploring an alien world. Sound effects are expertly crafted, from the ambient noise of the environment to the eerie calls of alien creatures. These elements come together to create a truly immersive audio experience.

Replayability: 9/10

With its procedurally generated world, diverse biomes, and dynamic events, Stranded: Alien Dawn offers a high degree of replayability. Players can experiment with different playstyles, tackle challenges in new ways, and discover new secrets with each playthrough. Additionally, the game's developers have promised ongoing updates and content expansions, ensuring that players will have plenty of reasons to return to the alien world.

Overall: 9/10

Stranded: Alien Dawn is an exceptional game that delivers a unique and enjoyable experience. Its stunning graphics, engaging gameplay, intriguing story, and immersive audio make it a must-play for fans of survival and exploration games. With its captivating alien world and high replayability, Stranded: Alien Dawn is an adventure that you won't want to miss.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so close to being absolutely epic! So close...

I love the graphics (albeit could use a slight performance improvement - especially during the winter and thunderstorms). The creatures look nice, and there's a good variety (updates/DLCs hopefully bolster this in future). The game play is satisfying enough, with a very intuitive UI, good schedule/jobs task management, and settlement development is very nice to watch unfold!

The 'bad':
Firstly the music. Let's get this out of the way... It's a relatively short track on loop. Permanently. The only time it changes is for combat. That's it. So the music very quickly starts to irritate!

Maps - Both (grassland and desert) are pre-generated. You cannot really explore much in a single play, if any at all really, so each time you land (which is random) this is what determines what you'll experience more so. This is good! Because it means each time you play you'll have a slightly different experience. But it's bad simply because there's only 2 maps. Once you've played them, you'll quickly figure out what is best for those maps crop-wise, and then for sub-sequent runs there's no initial 'adventure' experience.

Lifespan of game play - I've done 3 scenarios, and for 44 hours I took my time and enjoyed almost all of it. Buuuuut the best bit is the first 1-2 years of game time. Beyond that, it very quickly becomes a, "Run the game on fast speed PERMANENTLY and just wait to win." <<< You have no extra challenge, and the win requirements are literally a waiting game (wait for escape pod - wait for trader - etc). So winning a game within 3 years is best, because you get to start over and enjoy the best bit (building and surviving) sooner. I consider this a con, however, because you should be able to enjoy watching your settlement flourish; doesn't feel like it though.

Creatures - Nice variety; I feel like there could be a little bit more here and there. But to be fair, I'd much rather have just 2 new creatures with an all new map type. This would be a big boost to the game.

'Missions' - In game, there is a hot air balloon to do 'missions' on. These are nice text based missions only, which I'm okay with! But it could definitely use some variety!! There are hundreds of crashed ships, and that's it. Seriously, how in the sweet jesus did these ship pilots get their damn licenses?! Or is this planet some mini-black-hole-level-gravity-planet??! WHY ARE THE AUTHORITIES LETTING PEOPLE STILL TRAVEL HERE?????? It actually makes extremely little sense, and I'd much love this to be updated with some slightly more logical, or immersive, reasoning. And not just, "Spaceship scrap." over and over and over and over again.

---

So overall, I'd give this game a 5.5/10, but 8/10 for the first good few hours of each map. The monotony catches up very quickly, and really brings this score down in the later stages.\t\t\t\t\t

I was about to give this a positive review, but then it started crashing on me every 15 minutes without any indication why.

The game is a carbon copy of Rimworld, key differences:
* You get cooler toys: enjoy laser fences and running around shooting bugs in mechs
* You struggle less with everyone's emotional state (still have breakdowns, but I don't fear them as I do in Rimworld)
* The environment is more dangerous: winter is coming - don't underestimate it
* "Disasters" are still damning, but less annoying: eg. a solar flare can be countered, but at the expense of power. You might also get an "extreme cold snap" in the middle of winter, and that can really hurt if you're not properly prepared.
* It's simplified a bit: eg. you still have a priority matrix, but don't have zones (which it could use in winter, when you might want to stop people running off long disances to get hypothermia)
* Minor bugs exist: my survivors keep randomly deciding to run back and forth up and down stairs
* Expeditions exist, but are also simplified. You send a single person off at a time and you can offer them choices (this one I like, in Rimworld the expeditions got tedious pretty quickly)
* You're fighting bugs/wildlife - not people

Construction is more enjoyable. Probably on-par with Rimworld in difficulty with the exception that combat is far more predictable and I find that I don't save/reload constantly in combat. Building defences is a step up from Rimworld too. You have more creative options and they're not as.. volatile as late-game Rimworld (ok so I'm 14-hrs in, but they become equally as ... explosive)

Will switch to positive review if the crashing issue is resolved.\t\t\t\t\t
