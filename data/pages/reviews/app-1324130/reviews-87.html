\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7/10 and buy for no more than $25/30. It's not a bad time waster, but it's new out of early access and still a bit rough. Even on sale it depends on how much they keep supporting it. You'll easily get 50-60h depending on what you want to do, but how fulfilling you find that will vary. I find equal annoyance with equal enjoyment.

Ultimately, this feels like a game let out of Early Access too early for release to get more money to try and flesh things out more and make more sense of things. But, is it worth it? Nothing screams at me to want to wait to see what comes next when Rimworld already offers a more complete experience.

I say that I don't recommend it, BUT I do if it's on sale for $25/30.

More points:

- Graphics seem less optimised than they could be, but are passable.

- Map size is quite large and it looks great across distances, but the distance is largely useless - you have no need for the size of the map when everything is easy to reach closer to your base. It's only much later you might want to venture out, but there's no need, because you have everything already.

- It's based on being scifi survivors on an alien moon but some of the decisions are weird. There's no thirst, yet there's hunger. There's also nothing relating to thirst actions - collecting the rain that occurs, treating it to make it safe, drinking it, using it on crops, etc.

- You just need to observe crops and you can suddenly grow them like magic. No seeds needed.

- There are ZERO transports in the game. People walk across deserts and if you ever DO want to make use of the massive map, by having outposts that all interlink or something, well... you can't, because they all get pissed and have meltdowns because they're unhappy and stressed from walking so far.

- There are stairs, but no ladders.

- No two-storey building, only elevation-based building. That's right, you're limited to single-floor buildings. Very strange design decision and sounds more like technical incapability.

- There are wild dogs, but no wild cats. Weird choice to choose one and not the other.

- There are alien life forms you can tame, but it is a largely useless mechanic unless you just kill them for meat, in which case, why raise them and waste additional resources when they're EVERYWHERE and you can get meat from the waves that spawn every 7/8 days? You can't use them to haul, flatten land for farming, etc. They just consume resources.

- Very few enemies. You get a few alien nests you can take out with a fully-equipped squad of four survivors and then you have nothing but waves every 7/8 days, which are easy to fend off.

- Random seeds for scenario creation and yet survivors are all pre-created with set statistics and traits, which just screams laziness.

- Music sounds like it was made by a graduate college student, ESPECIALLY the combat music.

- The price sounds more like Frontier pushed it that high to make more profit even though the end result is still average.

These are just some of the oddities.\t\t\t\t\t

Hopefully the bugs get fixed soon otherwise I will have to change this to a Not Recommend.....but seeing as Frontier Developments are involved I'm not holding my breath.\t\t\t\t\t
