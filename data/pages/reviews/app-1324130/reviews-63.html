\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I would rate this game around 3.75/5. It is fairly well balanced and the idea of it is interesting. However there is very few reasons to keep playing after few missions as there is nothing new to try or discover. The only survival aspect is the random attacks, which after a while are quite easy to handle even on insane difficulty (or whatever the hardest was called). Depending on the mission and the planet there are few different strategies and they work every time. So once you figure them out (which shouldn't take too long) it tends to get a bit less interesting.

Besides that the biggest flaw I found with the game is the AI. For example, you can queue tasks to your characters but unless the task fits their current schedule they will ignore it (in that case the queue gets cleared after last completed task). This results in annoying amount of looking after your characters (unless you clear the schedule and giving them free will to do whatever they wish, which also has its problems). The AI also lacks in the tasks the characters are about to do. They might stat traveling to the other side of the playable map to gather resources (and only pick a handful before turning back, leaving rest on the ground) when their energies are extremely low. This results them either turning back before they get to their destination or dropping whatever they were carrying and returning back. There are so many bad things about the AI that I could go on and on about it.

Depending on the mission (crash landing, military, trade) and the planet grassland, jungle, desert you end up pretty much always defending your base (unless you activate the game rules for peace when there is no need to defend). You start your missions by selecting your characters (unless you choose to opt in for random ones). Each character have their own traits (at least I found that many of them are absolutely useless and I would never pick them for a mission). You can have up to 4 characters to start the mission with (6 in military).

In crash landing and military you need to research certain technology to allow you to build a specific building or buildings. Then keep them powered and defended until the end. In crash landing you need to contact ships in orbit to have your survivors be rescued one by one. In military you need to defend long distance communication device for a certain amount of time. In trade mission you need to gather enough money to buy the planet rights to yourself (maybe the easiest mission when playing in grassland or desert, where you can get carbon, or whatever its called). After few trades your done. The planets well have their differences but they affect very little on how you approach things. Whether you need to focus more on winter or summer clothing makes really no difference (and this is pretty much only a factor on the harder difficulties). There is less wood to be found in the desert, but then again there is more stone, it affects a little your initial building strategy, but the impact is has is so minor that it's barely worth mentioning. There is also different moons which apparently have some kind of a factor to the game, but apart from the chaos moon I really didn't notice any difference, so to be honest, I don't even know what the rest are supposed to do.

I wouldn't pay the full price for the game, but if you find it on sale and don't mind micromanaging your characters then I would go as far as to recommend the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Great animations.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a prelude - I've played the game for over 100 hours, finished every scenario, and explored every feature the game has to offer to a fair extent.

This one is a gem! I highly recommend anyone who enjoys colony sims, city managers, and their ilk to give this game a go.

EDIT: Just wanted to add the DLC is awesome and I'm having a great time with it.

The game does just the right amount of hand-holding when you need it to, but otherwise, finding ways to achieve each scenario's general goal is entirely left up to you. The cast of characters is interesting, and while there is a "meta" crew everyone prefers taking, the alternatives provide engaging opportunities for enriching gameplay, including some unique side stories (and a hidden achievement) for some of them.

The technology tree has some depth, but adding the breakthrough sections with random possible techs does spice up the gameplay. The procedural generation of the world from customizable seeds means you can easily repeat playthroughs.

Game difficulty is controlled via two settings, a traditional difficulty setting and a choice of different moons that will affect the chances random events will occur. This means you can increase either the "numbers" (i.e., health and damage of enemies, failure chance to manipulate objects, etc.), the number of things you have to deal with as time passes, or both. Things really get crazy incredibly quickly on the insane difficulty with the chaos moon.\t\t\t\t\t
