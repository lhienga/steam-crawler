\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery familiar concept, vastly improved. I love survival games that give you a reason to attempt survival. No spoilers here>

As a single player most survival games leave you flat - enter a world, collect resources, build a base but the world remains empty and you a ultimately alone with no interaction. This games give you mates to interact with, build a base together, and a reason to survive. It does lack variety in the worlds and danger you face but it is suggested that more is on the way.

Game play is challenging but not overly difficult for those that want to chill, the hard mode settings can change that very quickly. Worth the price - yes. Give it a try.

Edit: Additionally I will add that the developers are actively improving this title. There is an effort to give their audience what is asked for and do so without diminishing the original concept.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAre you like me? Did you put over 1k hours into rimworld, finally lay in bed and think... man imagine if you could have rimworld with sims style building and graphics, but with all the smoke leaf, potential warcrimes, and alcoholism?

Man are you in the right place brother. This game gives you Rimworld but IN 3D!

RIMWORLD IN 3D!

You read that right you illiterate war crime enjoyer.

While this game just hit 1.0 in its release and does require a LOT of polish, I will say the core is solid. I have minor complaints about certain things, but with mods already being made and developed for the game it won't be long till you have everything under the sun from every nerd subculture under the sun.

One thing to remember while this may play and feel like rimworld it isn't. I find myself pressing hotkeys by instinct alone for certain commands. A colony that has been built really does give you something to marvel at. I have lots of hope this game will only get improve further over time. But as it stands currently it is 100% playable, 100% engaging, 100% fun.

9.5/10
Pros:
The Rimworld you dreamed about.
Great Soundtrack, very relaxing.
Seasons are punishing.
AI is punishing.
Beautiful Graphics
Already has Mech Support (working vehicles)
Game doesn't chug. (Currently doesn't have 300 mods tho.)
Intuitive discovery system and research. Very easy to understand and progress through.
Windows for building come STOCK. Imagine that?
Laser fences.

Cons:
Some systems didn't make it into this game that you'd be accustomed to coming from Rimworld. One annoying example is the tattered gear is there, but no slider to control when a colonist will replace it for a new article of clothing.
Rooms you build for your survivors/colonists feel very empty and boring.
Progression doesn't feel smooth.
If you want to add or make a character you need to be a modder.
Temperature control early game is nearly impossible.
No ground wires, only pylons.

While most of these cons are minor they are things I find that could be done better.
However they aren't enough to make me dislike, nor hate this game and will continue to play it.

Final thought: Its Rimworld, but with a better engine and graphics with a whole new group of lil animals to tame and unleash on people.

MY OPINION ON STRANDED: ALIEN DAWN
BY GRUBERT
Stranded: Alien Dawn is a Sims-like game where you manage 3 to 6 Sims. You can't customise them like you can in The Sims 4, you have to pick them from a roster of 30 predefined characters. You can't even change their appearance!!! The other thing I didn't like is you can't buy anything, you have to build it! (WTF??) Also there are no roads, no neighbours, you're kinda lost in the wilderness, so the gameplay is very limited. It's very pretty tho, I've got to admit. But still, you can't really choose what Sims say to each other so I don't really see the point. One last thing that's really weird about the game is the insects are flipping HUUUGE!! I mean HELLO?! Game designers? What have you been smoking???!!! LOL

PS: There are only two types of chair and you can't change the beds colour so I'm gonna stick to The Sims thanks very much.\t\t\t\t\t
