\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun, basically it scratches all the itches I am missing in Going Medieval, so I bounce between both games. Ideally with more time they will both finish development and shine as the very unique games they are.

TLDR: Game is great and it is at the point where I would recommend it even when it isn't on sale. If it is on sale, you better snatch it up. They've fixed a lot of previous bugs. Surviving Mars and its DLC are some of my favorite things to play. (I got it for free on Epic and loved it so much I bought all the DLC). So if you liked that, you'll also like this if you were wishing for a micro settlement builder with the same amount of automation.

PROS:
- unique characters
- custom building system
- the scenarios all have achievable ends, and you can continue playing after 'winning'
- a lot of similarities to rim world
- fun electric grid system
- pet taming and training!

CONS:
- about 70% of the time the characters are ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ to each other for no reason
- swarm sizes get so big that the game starts to stutter if you build too much
- no embark customization like surviving mars (maybe it will one day, no idea)
- no multi level buildings like rim world
- performance issues the longer the game goes if you don't eradicate everything that moves
- feed troughs do not give enough control on amounts of feed provided, leading to tons of wasted resources.

Being a relatively new game, it doesn't quite have all the bells and whistles the Rimworld does, nor does it have all the mods (yet), but this is still a great game. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fantastically fun game (10/10), like a higher-res Rimworld but with fewer people to manage. The game isn't stingy with the amount of resources that you get per trip (e.g., chopping trees, mining rocks, etc.). Lots to do in a game but probably the best part is the construction. Lots of freedom in building houses or warehouses or combinations. The only thing I would like to see in this game is a bit more customization with the starting game and end game goals. I'd really like to have the option of playing after the end game goal has been reached. It'd be great to be able to play the crash landing scenario but have the option to do galactic trading. The modding community thankfully has that sort of thing covered. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a pretty version of Rimworld. Aside from the graphics, it is, in almost all ways, less interesting and more poorly put together than Rimworld.

It's not terrible. It's just not that good.

There are a few systems that are a bit nicer. I like Stranded's power pole system for electricity distribution more than painstakingly laying power conduit everywhere like Rimworld.

And the fact that it doesn't randomly generate people and instead has a specific roster of people with specific backstories is interesting, and I kind of like it, though I don't think it's better or worse than random generation, just different. It does fit well with having good graphics because you can have an artist create all of your characters.

And no, it doesn't have multi-story buildings either.

And I find looking for things on the map, or know where things are in relation to each other to be frustrating. It needs an overhead 'schematic' map of an entire region rather than just zooming out to some level and letting you wander all over the place (and get lost).

But mostly, it's just simpler, duller, and cruder. Except the graphics. They're a lot better.\t\t\t\t\t
