\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt this stage of the game, having played it an hour or two :P I will write my thoughts for you guys to save a penny.

The main challenges you will face besides some occasional bugs like I have seen one of my people have a seizure trying to deliver from a stockpile to a furnace, are the ones that make the game kinda flawed. The graphics are nice, the music gets boring after a few minutes. I only played the crash landing scenario so I will base my review on all the things I found out during the environments within it, desert..sweamp..forest. First the AI, the AI is like your biggest challenge in the game, it's not food, it's not the enemies...it's your own people. Mood wise you will notice that even with like over 150+ you will barely get the bar up slightly over the following hours, but hit a few -8 or -18 in the mood and within minutes it will drop significantly. So technically -8+32 should be going up on the mood bar, but hell no, it stays still most of the time. The good thing about the AI is it seeks food variety on its own, like it won't eat meat pie 24/7. Coming to the tasks, priorities, schedule and such...LOL here we go! You think of setting a task or two as priority 1, they get done with that task and will randomly pick a task from next priority below, even if it's at the other end of the map for you. Could have been a priority 2 or 3 right next to them, but no, let's waste an entire day to walk to the other side of the map to salvage or observe something for 5 seconds then call it a day and go back to base. You can restrict zones but then you are just hiding this problem, not fixing it. Furthermore, when you take the time to setup a schedule, most of the time in Relaxation period they will get up and stand still doing nothing like dummies, they will not use a single relaxation device you have available and start their day when the clock hits, sometimes without even eating! Meaning that they start their day at 70-65% hunger, they go to salvage somewhere a little far off for like 1 hour max, run back home for food asap when they hit below 60% hunger, wasting half a day. The AI acts like this ain't about survival, but more about you surviving their stupidity, because getting off the planet is the easiest thing you can achieve if you beeline for it. Another thing, a lot of those consumables you can create for them to eat/drink, have very low impact to justify their use. The tiny % from coffee for exemple, loses its value in time consumption to gather the grain itself, haul it, and the energy consumed to make it. So all you get is a perishable item that counts as an extra relaxation variety at best. Speaking of energy, the devs will severely penalize you for using technology. The moment you begin installing things that use electricity, circuits, CPU cores, you will be put up against a deadly abyss that is starving for all those materials. The rate at which they break down and the cost to repair them is astounding! I remember a scarab that grazed 1 hp off a solar panel before i got a clean shot at it, and it cost me 1 electronics just for that! You will be running half the day repairing broken lightbulbs, wind turbines, solar panels, batteries. And again due to how dumb the AI is at handling minimal workload, this technology advancement will severely limit your work progress speed while putting a huge dent in your supplies which you may not notice at first. the other thing is the time it takes to craft stuff. If you do yourself a favour and start the game with a solo survivor you will quickly learn that the crafting part of this game is very time consuming. So much so that you will most likely finish your first year with your people half naked if you have many survivors. Clothes deteriorate so fast it's unreal, I had games i tried to keep my people naked until winter arrived to have clothes at that point because I didnt want 1-2 people stuck making the materials for clothes and then the clothes themselves every single day of their existence. To make a set of good clothes for a crew of 4 people, you will probably be well into the end of year 2 before having them in mediocre worn out clothes constantly anyways because you can't keep up. Even with crafting 10, no difference. That crafting level 10 person will NEVER be able to supply a crew of 4 with full summer and also winter clothes even if you manually worked the hell out of them at maximum stress and had another person spending half of their day farming/hauling. Also they have a very poor attempt at automation right now, which is like "here is automation for you to burn your precious fuel when you run out of power" instead of giving the option to turn off all non-essentials to preserve power at certain thresholds. You also see the sensors for enemy activity have such a tiny range, that won't even allow time for those ultra slow turrets to turn and shoot in time. Every electronic piece you build will be consuming power 24/7, even when not being used, further making it necessary for you to have an entire wall space dedicated to having like 10 on/off switches to not have to manually go everywhere when you want to preserve some power because some event occured. So what you have right now is a game that looks beautiful, you trying to plan and make things work while the AI does the complete opposite, and it feels to you like the devs never played their own game to see how annoying the experience is while on your way to "the finish line". The more you play, every exploration event that pops up becomes an annoyance to dismiss and move on with whatever you were doing. It's like evey single one of them is "oh look we found scrap again" but under different names and occasionally different symbols. Not very enticing to burn that precious fuel to explore anymore, is it now? You devs know you can find loads of scrap on the actual map without wasting 30 fuel? do you know how long it takes to craft 30 fuel from manure? Like go haul it, then at the craft table, then time to store each? I doubt it.\t\t\t\t\t
