\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think it does great injustice to call the game Rimworld Lite 3D.

In many ways it reminds me of Rimworld Alpha 14 version, which was the first one I played. Which is not a bad thing, I really loved the game already. Because I had played Rimworld a lot, I was suspicious of buying this game, fearing it might fall short in comparison and would just make me want to play yet another hour of RW. But no, I was pleasantly surprised. In gameplay it feels familiar enough but the 3D world makes it an experience of its own.

The 3D part in Stranded: Alien Dawn is really well made. I mean the game is beautiful and atmospheric. Besides, there just aren't enough games where the discovery of manure is the most important thing, and gives the player feeling of fulfillment!

I hope they'll add human vs human combat. It's ok to fight the bugs, but it would be just awesome to have gunfights in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on sale, and I'm happy I didn't pay full price. Here are a list of things that annoyed me to no end.

- The game is severely unbalanced in SO many ways.

- Do you happen to have a large supply of meat and a few carnivorous animals in your ranch? Well get ready to starve because your survivors will feed it all to the animals and save none for themselves. OR they'll put it all on the drying rack and give none to the animals. There's zero in-between.

- Got food in the pantry? Get ready to starve anyway. Sometimes your survivors would rather take a walk, or go straight to work instead of feeding themselves. I literally had a full roster of starving characters, despite a full pantry and food being left to rot away. This

- There's nothing particularly special about base building; in fact it's incredibly limited. I really wanted to build an upper level to my house, and although it will allow me to lay out the floors and add the stairs, it's bloody impossible to put a roof on the damn thing. Unless your entire house in on one perfectly even level, get ready to have holes in your roof.

- It would be nice to have some decor items for the houses, and more furniture options. As it currently stands, the inside of your house is going to look pretty bare bones. A few ugly statues of random creatures found on each planet doesn't really make for good decor.

- THE MONSTER WAVES. Dear god, the endless f*cking monster waves. By the time you're done recovering from the last one, the next one will come. It artificially stunts your progress, making game play WAY slower than it should be. There's an option to turn off aggressive monsters, but when you do the game becomes incredibly boring very quickly.

TL;DR?: Game lacks substance and brings nothing new to the genre, and has severe balancing issues. I got some hours of distraction out of it, but I wouldn't pay full price.\t\t\t\t\t

Everything(specifically crafting; I can understand research taking long) takes too long in comparison to how fast the various troubles come up.

I have mixed feeling towards this game. The experience is similar to Oxygen Not Included where you have to micromanage your survivors but you don't get to micromanage them enough. For example, I forgot to turn on a turret before a seige, so I "un-drafted" a survivor and turned off all of his activities except Handling, but instead of turn the turret on, he just idled. In ONI, you also had the option of prioritising buildings as well as tasks/activities. The AI really needs to be worked on if we aren't getting more detail.
The other problem I noticed is everything takes too long. I survived a year in the game and I had to spend all of that time just trying to survive without getting to go on the attack against the nests. As usual in survival genre, I spent all of winter trying to stay alive but because of the seed generation, it took forever to gather and observe all of the components to get my base on it's feet. Crafting takes way too long. My 4-6 survivors can only do so much in the time in between attacks as it is but you also have to swap outfits like a fashionista. It would be great to make bases like the preview photos but everything takes forever that you'd probably run out of resources before you get to to that point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRimworld, the Sims, Oxygen not Included, etc.

The game starts out with making a small camp and trying to set yourself up to not starve once your starting food runs out. After that its a fight against time trying to build a camp, research, task your 4 survivors to gathering, and surviving against increasingly hard waves of enemies that appear every hour or so.

Early game is pretty easy just need to create a farm day 1 and by day 8 you've got food. Mid game is all about temperature (which is very poorly done) and trying to get clothes, food, electronics, medicine, and defenses up while your survivors freeze/get depressed, starve, unable to repair or refrigerate, die of a brain worm, and or get overrun by underestimating a horde of critters.

Late game is all about trying to get every resource, maxing out survivors skills, making sure everyone is clothed and having a great stock in every item. After all that you get into the actual late game research and 'perfect' everything to make sure you're defended on all sides and the camp can survive winter/heatwaves.

Overall the game has a pretty good game loop, and it'll take more than 10 or 30 hours to complete the research tree and get into late game.\t\t\t\t\t
