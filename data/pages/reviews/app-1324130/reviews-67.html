\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has so much potential but is ruined by mind-boggling terrible decisions. The way the power/tech and clothing/temp systems are handled are great and interesting. But it doesn't matter because the beginning of the game (or at least the survival scenario, the one every game of this genre focuses on) is so horribly balanced that it becomes seriously unfun. The primary issues are the way things are paced, the terrible AI, and the uneven balance between you and enemy NPCs.

The biggest issue is how slow you move. So many items, buildings, etc require a ton of resources and upwards of 6 to 18 hours to build. That's fine... except it takes that much in additional time just to walk. It should not take 1 or more hours to travel small distances. A small settlement of 30 tiles should not take 2 hours to walk from edge to edge. This becomes further compounded with the atrocious AI which will be working on one thing but not continue doing the same thing right next to them but instead walk across the entire camp to work on another (similar) thing.. before walking back. There's no internal logic of priority or time with the AI. And if you slow yourself down by minimizing spread of planning, like structures, you waste tons of game-time that could have been spent more productively.

The AI is so terrible, you can select a SINGLE animal to hunt, the NPC will go attack it.. get attacked by.. get a literal papercut ("superficial bite"), and try to run to a bed to rest... while the NPC is still alive and attacking. On the other hand, your base can be under attacked and a non-drafted (not directly controlled) npc will randomly decide to fight the BIG insects.. bare-handed. Without being attacked, while behind walls. Any form of healing is more baffling because the game will try to heal non-life threatening people while someone with their guts spilling out is told "good luck". Which, going back to the horrible walking speed of the game, a bleedout time of 5 hours is a death sentence.

Fighting enemies is so terribly uneven it's just unfun. You can build walls and towers, but people on towers can't shoot at 90% of the enemies at the wall because they "can't see" them. Sometimes random plants will block YOUR LoS of enemies but THEY can see/attack you just fine. There is no stunning or instant-killing of enemies, you have to deal 100% of HP to kill them. But they CAN stun/knock-out you or INSTANT KILL you by pure random rolls. Hey, that'd be a cool feature.. if the game was centered around a PRESET roster of characters. Which means there's a very limited amount of people you can recruit, let alone actually find and get into your camp. And losing 1 person out of 6 or 7 people by literal random chance is absurd and effectively ends the game. Especially when finding new people is a random chance that isn't even uncommon, it's straight rare. There's one setting that ups the chance to get your first 2 additional people and that feels like a necessity with how absurdly low the chance is. This is even worse because the "armor" barely offers any protection despite the length of time it takes to make any of it, enemies usually attack at night (lol good luck ever hitting them before you unlock high tech light sources), but THEY have insane armor that makes non-guns practically useless... especially when bows and crossbows have insanely low range that the ranged enemies outrange. Because of course.

TBH 7 people in your base is already pushing it. Because, again, the walk speed is so damn low that building shelters/houses for more than that would make your town/base unfeasibly large time-wise. Which only lightly touches upon how long it takes to build structures (very, very long).

I'm sure the game becomes painfully easy when you can access the high-tech stuff like a base of automated turrets and mechs. But GETTING there is unfun.\t\t\t\t\t

The gameplay is interesting, the story behind it as well, however, it gets repetitive after a while (a few more story options, which I don't doubt are WIP). The ability to switch between characters being controlled by the player and them running around is very useful - that way I don't have to monitor every single character and what they're doing - they detect and do tasks on their own. The scenery is absolutely beautiful, with many different creatures and plants at your disposal for taming, hunting, harvesting and using to improve the quality of life on your little base.
The only thing I would like to see added is a "colonization mode" (like the kind Project X mod adds) - sort of an endless mode.
Otherwise, this game is absolutely worth the price and I certainly recommend the purchase of it.\t\t\t\t\t
