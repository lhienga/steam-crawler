\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot that I like about this game. HOWEVER. The AI, like most of these sorts of survival simulation games, is terrible. After seeing other games like Sapiens, it's clear this is just laziness by the devs. Here are some examples:

--Despite having tons of clothes created for the survivors, they will not dress themselves appropriately. I set until orders and have one for every survivor, yet they will not swap out in the winter, or back in the summer to appropriate clothes. They will get heat stroke or hypothermia instead. LAZY CODING
--You will frequently have workers running halfway across the map, doing one tiny bit of a job, then suddenly realizing they're hungry, walking back across the map, eating, then coming back to finish a job. LAZY CODING
--You will frequently have a survivor grab enough for one single block to construct, bring the materials, construct the one block, then walk back to get more materials. They will do this all day sometimes if you're, for example, making cement in a furnace, rather than doing any sort of batching, or cooperating among themselves in any way. Here is a case where a game like Sapiens, which is so much less bloated than this game, uses cooperative decision making between the survivors, cooperating to accomplish things, you know, like intelligent beings would do who can speak to one another.
--The way the survivors will construct things demonstrates how horrible the coding of their decision making is. I've watched the one Survivor with construction, walk all the way to the other side of a 16x16 building to hit one block (this is with say 30 different pink blocks highlighted that already have materials), then walk all the way back to the opposite corner of the building to hit one, then back. etc. etc. It's very clear from this that the survivors make some sort of a queue of tasks, and then NEVER revise it based on current conditions. They ought to reconsider and rewrite their task list if tons of materials are dropped right next to them, to behave more efficiently. LAZY CODING

These are just things I noticed today.

In short, the promise of this game is high, but the execution still leaves a lot to be desired--and again based on other games, this is not asking for anything impossible, there is code there which the survivors are operating from, but it's badly written code that makes the survivors act like lemmings and not, you know, humans. Please put some more effort into the intelligence of the survivors devs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStranded, as many have pointed out, is basically a light version of RimWorld. The graphics are better, but game play has a very long way to go. It's pretty shallow and lacks a lot of the depth that RimWorld has created over the years.

You basically build to survive, which isn't difficult. Automate where you can. Build some defenses and research until you're out of things to research. Combat is vs bugs and cows that grow in number as you progress, but are easily defeated with a few turrets. Missile as laser turrets are useless, as is any gun that isn't the rail sniper.

I have not played for 195 hours; I just left the game running a lot. I probably have 30 hours in. I'm done/bored and won't play again until they add some depth.

If you like RimWorld, I would recommend Stranded, but don't expect the same level of game play. While it's worth buying, this isn't a game capable of keeping your attention for the hundreds of hours that RimWorld can.
