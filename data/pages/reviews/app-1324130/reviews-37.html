\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe new DLC adds automation via service robots and drones that allow you to upscale and speed the construction, delivery and handling of much larger bases. This automation allows for rapid and agile base changes that are much needed with the increasing difficulty and numbers of bot waves that threaten to overrun you.

Insane chaos with the new DLC is pitched right at the level of difficulty that I search for. On lower settings, the game can become easy fast, but I loved how challenging this was and how the micromanaging and strategic adjustments led to rewarding outcomes.

Some downsides:
- Hope's sentience skill - I dislike the micro-management needed to keep the 'not enough tasks' debuff from her. I love the game for challenging efficiency and a feeling of scaling power that it offers, I don't wish to play nursemaid to a teenage robot.
- By the end of the game I faced waves of 500+ bots, this was an awesome light show, but laggy af. Maybe this is my computer...
- I found a few bugs - ceiling lights disappear and are impossible to find sometimes, and carbon wind turbines stopped producing power for many days without explanation.

Overall, this is an amazing update to the game, making it more playable and challenging. I love the direction this game is going in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrings a few interesting mechanics to the colony builder genre by combining it with tower defense and a discovery/quest based research system. Unfortunately it's severely marred by the frequency of crashes to desktop and other bugs. On Linux, it's necessary to disable reflections in the graphics settings or else the game won't load, but once it's running every couple hours it just freezes up and crashes regardless of settings.

On other notes, an unexpected advantage to Rimworld and Dwarf Fortress low graphics modes is that animations are usually quick to interrupt. The unit animations in Stranded usually need to complete before the next one can be queued. As a result, accidentally starting down the stairs, ladders, climbing in/out of bed, can add significant delays before one of your survivors can execute their next action, which is frustrating when it happens in critical moment. Similarly, while multiple Z levels is a nice addition to the game, there are some bugs when positioning units when recruited for direct control. It's as if the path finding is only aware some of the time that the Z levels exist, so from some locations units can be positioned on battlements, and from other positions on the map it registers as impossible. The decision to always allow units to scale walls so they usually don't get stuck is admirable though, as it prevents problems with building roofs and other inaccessible builds that show up in some other colony sims of this variety.

Like many recent colony sims, there is a lot of waiting at max speed before the next event requires an interruption. Some of the more polished games in this genre do a better job of keeping you on your toes in between events, so it feels like you are constantly adjusting. Stranded spends a lot of time waiting for the next attack or research once you get over some basic mechanics. I'm fine with some building and research tasks that take days to complete, but it seems out of balance with time spent in logistic chores or the animations above. It's nice that it's pretty to look at, but it's a lot of time spent waiting for something to happen. This may be exacerbated by the lack of feedback on how overloaded the survivors are with tasks. Even just an indicator of the total number of jobs in queue would be helpful feedback.

Another UI issue is there doesn't appear to be a zoomed out map. When a new insect hive appears, it's possible to zoom to location, but it's not clear how far that is to base or where that hive is in relation to home. Even distance/directional indicators would help here, but it seems easy to lose one's bearings except for directly around the base.

Overall this is passable entry into the genre, but needs some significant polish and balancing. The graphics are sometimes pretty, but they shouldn't be at the expense of game play, and they certainly should not result in crashes.\t\t\t\t\t
