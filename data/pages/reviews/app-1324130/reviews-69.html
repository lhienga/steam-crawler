\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this on sale. I had read reviews and some had me wary... saying it was just like Rimworld and Going Medieval. However I've played so many city builders (yet I never make a aesthetically pleasing city... le sigh...) and I did play Going Medieval for a long time before I decided to throw some aliens into the mix.

Things that do need tweaked:
-Crafting is just a tad wonky IMO.
-It was trying to get me to do expeditions, yet all my characters were down with something needing antibiotics.
-The in game help needs to be tagged better. Couldn't find where to produce metal (Furnace) or Antibiotics (fermenting barrel).
-But it was within the first year that I needed a bunch of antibiotics. Just got that squared away previous gaming session.
-Some sort of Map option would be nice, see the overview of the area. I had no idea where to start/end per the map.
-Maybe some terraforming options... but just like landscaping. flattening out areas.
-Maybe I missed it but switching from Celsius to Fahrenheit...
-Some tool to help what needs identified... maybe a master log.
-Still have yet to mess with taming...

Overall, having a blast with it. Early release so, yeah, yah know... work in progress. Doesn't stop me from logging in an hour before work and punching into this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have never been very keen on doing reviews. I don't think I am very good at them. That being said, I will give it try. Since the 60s I have had this addiction...hmm...I mean, hobby all my life. So much so, that I even opened two successful retail game stores over the years. On top of that, I have put many hours into computer games since my retirement. I therefore think, I at least I have a somewhat well round idea of what makes a good game, good, or even an what makes an exceptional game, exceptional. Which leads me this my review of Stranded: Alien Dawn.
Stranded: Alien Dawn, is one of the best, most beautiful (as far environment is concern) Sci Fi Colony simulator in existence. And, it is these environments (sometime breathtaking I might add) which makes each game a very immersive experience for the player; not to mention the replay value in each new seed played. Add to that the desert planet, and the Jungle planet (the absolute best vistas in the game) you got yourself more replay value than most games out today. And with each planet comes its own challenges and strategies.
Then there is the modding community. The mods provided by this community help players who wish to "flesh-out" aspects of their game. All of this (and far more) makes Stranded: Alien Dawn a win win as far as I'm concern.
As of today I have over 1400 hours into this wonder and challenging game, in fact I am in the middle of one right now.
In closing, If you are looking for an involving, immersive, exceptional game; a game you will come back to again and again....well darn it...this your game.\t\t\t\t\t
