\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a wonderfully immersive gaming experience that scratches my fantasy base-building itch. Like many others, I'd liken the character-control to Rimworld and the base building to Dawn of Man, yet, the graphics and art style brings this science-fiction based alien theme to brilliantly rendered life with a good serving of The Sims realism in there too.

PROS:
1) Seamless and stable game play...if there are any bugs, I've not encountered any during 124 hours of exploration so far.
2) Breathtaking landscape graphics, and truly beautiful alien animal/insect designs and skins. I enjoyed the 'Observation' tasks to discover and correlate all the species in the current game world. I also love that every biome type will have a couple of different types of fauna and flora, as well as altered recipes to keep things more mixed as you play through Sobrius (Temperate Meadow), Desertum (Hot Dry Desert) and Saltu (Humid Jungle).
3) Micro-management heaven: put item counts on or off and designate what can be stored on a shelf or in a stockpile, and what should not. Have fun building your base its own large storage warehouse because you'll need plenty of space to keep the vast amount of items and ingredients you will encounter.
4) Research, Build & Craft everything you're going to need to first survive, and then, to thrive. There is always plenty to do here, sometimes, more than you can handle with too few colonists.
5) Identify fauna and flora, then farm, cook and produce saleable products in order to conduct trade with passing spaceships, and also, to buy in anything you desperately need with the currency of Galacticoins.
6) Take care of your survivors, pets and animals with God-Like control: you can draft and move all your characters, and give them orders according to what you want them to do immediately, later or when they find the time for it in a task queue system.
7) A WINNER for me: You can turn off the attack threats as you make the choices for your initial game set-up, and play either the chilled out or fighting-tooth-and-nail-for-everything type of game that you most enjoy.

CONS:
1) Character pathing is sometimes needlessly indirect, but it's not the greatest frustration if you just leave them to get on by themselves. They eventually get to where they are going, even if its not via the most direct route.
2) Some of the micro-management needs a little tweaking, for eg. the animal feeding troughs. If you feed a carnivore, expect that your entire stock of 300 dried meat will end up in the trough because you can't designate half your stock to colonist consumption, and the other half to farm animal consumption. But again, only a minor frustration and there are ways to work around that.
3) The Electrical Grids and accompanying producers/users system of 'control switches' is quite complicated, and requires a vast amount of resources to build once you get to the point of trying to establish radio communication with the inter-planetary world, and beyond. Perhaps I just don't understand how to get it to cross-function or I'm doing something wrong, but it never works out correctly for me. I just bypass the automation mishugas and simply make my colonists switch stuff on and off as I need it. It's not brilliant, but it works.

I confidently recommend this game to fans of base-building, micro-management, and jeopardy/combat in colonising fantasy-realism worlds. You're going to enjoy unpacking just about every aspect of this one. The developers have put a lot of love into it and it shows. It is well-worth its current asking price so I am pleased to support the on-going development of this richly created game by taking it on as one I will be buying more expansions for, as and when they are released. I'm looking forward to the onward journey.

You've done a magnificent job so far, Haemimont Games.

Regards,
