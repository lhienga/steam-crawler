\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost a great game.
Beautiful in its beautiful moments.
Exciting in its exciting moments.
Annoying in its annoying moments.

The problem and reason why this is "just" a good and not a great game is that these moments are so wildly mixed together.
On many occassions it feels almost as the devs said: "Hold on - this game might be a little TOO good, a little TOO entertaining or fun." and then opted to change that without reason.

For example:
The pop outs: There are some events, like expeditions that will sometimes jump in your face. As this were not annoying enough it interrupts any action you were doing. After sending out the 10. same expedition i don't need a reminder telling me the exact same stuff the 10. time and interrupts my building cursor.

The AI: The survivors very so often prefer to die than to be smart. They starve in front of full fridges because they are determined to bench press instead. No Joke - they also prefer to miss their own wedding! Just because they love to work. This also is one of the rare events in which they prefer to work instead of getting married to the love of their life. Especially strange considering that they have no problem at all skipping work on music night, their unbelievable often recurring self-inflicted break-downs and on other events like the rememberance day for the week, It is so annoying that it only could be explained that it was intended to destroy any fun.

The combat: The combat is soooo boring and repetitive it would be better it wouldn't exist at all.
There are only 3 types of enemies, all are insects and they have no special skill at all. Especially annoing and unfair is that they very often just spawn in(!) your base instead at the egde of the map. This is not challenging, it is annoying. Also instead of making fights and combats more interesting the AI just keeps adding more and more and more of the same boring enemies. Rimworld had so many enemytypes with so many strategies you had to master.

So while Rimworld manages to never get boring this game is awesome, great and very exciting the first 20 hours. Then it declines with every hour you have seen the exact same thing oder and over and over again.
The maps are huge but that seems to miss the point as your survivors will never see most of the areas because they can only wander a certain amount of steps before they need to sleep, to have fun or even to sleep. After some time wandering around they will faint so you won't be able even to force them to walk a longer distance. The expeditions are very exciting at first but once you realize they just repeat themselves the magic fast wears of.

So - why a recommendation?
It still ha a certain fun and magic the first steps of every new base. Every new survivor and the first attack.
The achievementhunting is fun and cool, some of the randomgenerated landscapes are sooooooo good and nice to explore.

It has way more good elements than bad ones. But the bad ones are sooo omnipresent they're hard to ignore.
Still - give it a try, it is a good game that could have been sooo much better.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou've crash landed on an alien planet... lets explore ... asymmetric pedantry!

What do I mean? Well you can micro manage your survivors; you can tell them not to eat raw meat, duh!. you can tell them how to prioritise farming, scavenging, crafting and other tasks so that they act (fairly) autonomously. You can even tell them not to eat any other survivors favourite foods! (I'm guessing that by default they're fairly self obsessed and don't give a hoot about anyone else, go team!).

But Ken, my favourite psychopathic team member (he likes observing death , +6 happiness, that's not worrying is it?) can't seem to go to the shelves and choose a thermal jacket (crafted days earlier by Vivian) when the weather starts to turn - or can anyone else for that matter.

Ken is unhappy (-6 happiness because he's cold), this is more than counterbalanced by the fact that he has nice 4x5 tile bedroom (+18 happiness). Kens got his priorities right!

No he hasn't, Ken died 10 days later from hypothermia because I had the game on fast forward, desperate to believe that he could dress himself appropriately from the choice of over 25 different items of clothing without me telling him to do so.

Why Ken, why? If you can prioritise your daily tasks and eat when you're hungry - why can't you dress yourself when you're cold?

I would like to think he died happy - because I told everyone else not to eat his favourite meat stew, but now it's going bad because Ken isn't around to eat it anymore.\t\t\t\t\t
