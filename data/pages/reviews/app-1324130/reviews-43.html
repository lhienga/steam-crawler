\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very fun take on the colony-sim. It shares many gameplay elements with Rimworld and similar UI that feel familiar enough with some slight changes. The 3D perspective and animations add an extra layer that makes watching your colonists live and work enjoyable.

While it does share many similarities with Rimworld, Stranded: Alien Dawn gives a few gameplay elements twists that help differentiate it for better or worse. Buildings can have multiple floors and allow you quite a bit of freedom in design regarding storage, bedrooms, etc. that can be very satisfying. Larger materials are laid out on the floor in a pallet like design while smaller items can be loaded onto shelving or stuffed into boxes allowing very easy organisation for those with OCD. Weapons and armors can be stored inside lockers and wardrobes as well, allowing you to still have plenty of form without sacrificing function.

Research feels a little odd with how you need to "observe" plants and animals to plant or tame them. It can be tricky when you need a certain plant but there's none nearby or the fact that the researcher must walk all the to it to observe it, which can be quite far at times.

Combat is simple but enjoyable enough and functions very similarly to Rimworld with a meter showing cooldown before the next attack. Medicine and armor also feel simplified as well which can be a positive or negative depending on much you like to min-max numbers on a piece of clothing or armor. However, there are mechs which are a huge plus and just fun in general watching almost like a tower defense-like attack on your base.

While very fun, it can feel a little rough around the edges with some performance issues and odd gameplay hiccups that can be annoying to work around. Overall, this is a very fun game and I would highly recommend it to anyone who likes colony sims and doesn't mind a few out issues here and there.
