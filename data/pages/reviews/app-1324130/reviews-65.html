Nothing like Rimworld

As someone who has 900+ hours in Rimworld and 500+ in Dwarf Fortress, I love whacky colony management sims/story-generating games. I purchased this game due all the reviews expressing how similar this game is to Rimworld.

Except for the fact your colonists can sad wander and that insects attack on occasion, it's nothing like Rimworld. It has similar mechanics (your colonists need to cook, sleep, tailor clothing, and research), but what game in this genre wouldn't have that?

It lacks the depth to be anything like Rimworld.

Your preset colonists have preset portraits and preset personalities. They're going to "banter" the same few sentences over and over and scarcely form a connection to each other, and further you're not going to care much about them either.

You will always encounter the same wildlife per biome, never anything unusual or new to discover. You're never going to be assaulted by 45 crazed chihuahuas. You're never going to see a Noth in the wild that has asthma or a heart condition.

It's overall lifeless. The game feels like it was headed in the right direction but just utterly fell short.\t\t\t\t\t

Medium difficulty... what is the great terrorizing end-game ultimate assault to fend off?
Four ducks. Four (not forty our 400) fluffy, lovable ducks.

No, this is not 3D Rimworld. This is a weird messy chaos that is very nice in the first 10 hours and then plunges into swampy, tiring, boring nothingness where colonists literally scream and hoot out of happiness at a statue of an alien cow in the base.

PS. no storing area management, weird task queueing, no 2-floors buildings and multi-level buildings (when you manage to plan them) are messy anyway. There are nice points too, but... too few to make up for the bad points.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended but with caveats.

The early to mid game is great, making something out of nothing and getting your settlement going is enormous fun, however the later into the game you get the less interesting it becomes and the more the design issues start to show.

I've enjoyed my 35 hours with it but can't see me playing it much more without some changes.
* Most of the map is impractical to use as you can't get your characters to move past jogging speed. Needs some kind of transport.
* Flagged areas have been created to try and help with the first point but it just means you have to split everyone up it doesn't really help and maintaining satellite bases is just a pain.
* You spend far too much time late game micromanaging silly decisions that the survivors make, such as walking a long distance to kill/chop/farm something only to have them turn around to go to bed and not even take it with them. Again I think the flag system is supposed to help with this but it doesn't.
* I have 2 survivors with ranching set to priority 1 and STILL i have to keep reminding them to feed the livestock.

There is a reviewer on here who's spent 500 hours replaying the early-mid game until they get it perfect. I think they've got the right idea. Ultimately the idea is to get them rescued which in practise just means losing characters same as if they had just died so it's not really an incentive to keep going.

I will probably keep at it see if i can get any of them rescued and then park it for a couple of years and see if it's improved.

Great game don't get me wrong, but it needs some more work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game concept is interesting and engaging, however it needs lots of improvement before it would become a good game. I played on hard difficulty and my experience was pretty controversial. On the one hand, the gameplay is dynamic and interesting at the beginning. And it's not easy (that's what I like). But, on the other hand, the fact that your settlers can't physically survive for 1 day 1 mile away from your base is VERY frustrating to say the least. They won't die right away but they will be so disfunctional that they won't be able to finish any task and they will run back to the base. I wasn't able to even cross the whole map with any of my settlers. That felt sooo wierd. Then 2 of my men settlers got in love and both slept in the same room but they got negative effect for 'sleeping without their beloved ones'. WTF?!? You are literally sleeping WITH each other in one room and you complain that you sleep without each other?! Sorry, but the devs did not make an option to have a bed for 2 people but already made a negative effect for not sleeping together... weird.

Overall, the game is interesting but it also needs lots of work before you can say that it is a finished game. It is interesting and frustrating at the same time.\t\t\t\t\t
