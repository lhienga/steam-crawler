\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStranded: Alien Dawn is like survivalist-The Sims crossed with RimWorld in 3D. Where RimWorld can tell the story of multiple colonies, here you control one colony on an expansive map. There are a range of scenarios such as being marooned on a planet, fortifying a military base, and creating a ranch to save enough money to buy rights to the whole planet.
You can expect the usual cycles of the genre including exploring, investigating new resources, and gradually moving from survival shelters into homesteads, then into massive, militarised bases. There is a good range of equipment and gear to research and outfit your characters with (you can have between eight at most) and several planets with different habitats each with unique conditions and some different creatures to overcome.
While there are some indicators of interpersonal relationships and the inner life of your characters (who all have different skills and preferences which they make known) - compared to RimWorld however, it felt like the emergent story telling was less a central feature of the game, compared to the base building and resource gathering. Overall, Stranded: Alien Dawn is worth checking out.

This game is total rip-off of Rimworld, just simplified. There are about 30% of vanilla Rimworld mechanics, and even then simplified.
Menus, almost the same, priority system, exactly the same, etc.

Saying so, game is nice, both in visuals and in gameplay. I't definately not worth it's price.
I would advice this game if you are not going to play RW due to 2D style or you are intimidated by RW and want to learn on something easier.

I really hope this game will evolve, either steal more features from Rimworld or be itself. right now it is to much tower defense, no stories, to simple... only constant waves of bugs to kill...\t\t\t\t\t
