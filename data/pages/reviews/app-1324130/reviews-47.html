Greetings as always! Stranded: Alien Dawn is a survival colony builder Ive been looking at for some time but have been hesitant trying as early access games always come with a certain level of back and forth content wise.

Survivor, Space Edition: Stranded is a survival colony builder, the type of game where you are not only trying to build a min-civilization but also surviving. Bankruptcy isnt the failure point here, death is. Although you can manually instruct your people to perform tasks most of the time youll be setting up priorities and they will do their best to complete tasks that become available. During this time you will need to manage food, heat and shelter while holding off the wilderness. If you like city builders or survival builders this game is for you. The building is fairly basic but intuitive and allows for mid level customization, especially for an EA game. The music is also great, it fits the setting perfectly, though I did eventually mute it in favor of my podcasts. One thing that should be noted is that this is a colony or community builder, not a city builder. At most you may have 8 colonists and not hundreds or even thousands in city-builders.

Oxygen Included: I know a lot of people say this game is like babys first Rim World but I feel it could also be in the same vein as Oxygen not Included (another one of my favorite games). For some reason I just cant get past the graphics on Rim World so having Stranded was a great middle ground for me. Im also not a huge fan of the Rube Goldberg level of endgame Oxygen. This game finds that middle ground rather nicely. Having some levels of interactivity but not entire assembly lines that some other games have and Id say it probably provide moderate level of complexity verses the others out there. I feel a lot of the game takes the tedious nature of some builders away, allowing many things to become automated without having to micro or set up complex systems. One thing I will say I absolutely loved about this game is that the characters are premade and have their own back stories and character. This is unlike most other games of the genre and I feel it allows for more balance and more interest. Even some characters start with diseases or impairments that could be fatal.

Broke Again: One thing Id love to see is more environmental affects, there are some already but I really feel these could be increased, we have rain and lightning but there could also be degrees, even hurricanes. I have noticed that they are continuing to update the game so Id be surprised if they didnt expand on this. Id also like to see the game do something with water, at no point do you need it but Id see it as a nice additional resource and it honestly feels off not having to concern myself with it. I know I stated that the game gets rid of most of the tedious elements of this genre but equipment is the one exception. Why cant Bob just go put on his winter gear when it gets colder? Heck, Id love to see a personal closet that can be added to each colonists room that contains all their clothes. I dont mind clothing degrading but it would be nice if there was repair ability instead of just waiting for them to get to 10% and destroying them for resources.

Overall, I think this game is a must buy for any survival colony / city building fan and it is quickly becoming my favorite of the genre. It mixes modern graphics with the deep gameplay of the genre and I cannot wait to see what additions they add in the future. Highly recommend!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had high hopes for this game, the trailers looked real good. Got in and immediately realized it's optimized about as well a horse trying to pull a train. The game constantly chugs and stutters. I was willing to put up with it but then I realized how bad the gameplay is. It's very hard to know which units will do what task when instructed, and every task is clunky and the UI is not helping. Half the time I was instructing my people to do something, they'd ignore me and just stand around doing nothing. What made me get my refund was the combat. You have to select each individual unit, "draft" them (preventing them from doing anything non-combat related), and MANUALLY instruct them to fight. Not only will they not defend themselves EVER unless you instruct them, but they run away in all random directions. I couldn't even find one of them after she had ran away to the far corner of the map. Stay away from this one.\t\t\t\t\t
