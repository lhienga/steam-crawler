\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs you can see I slightly exceeded the 2 hrs needed for a refund.

Been awhile since I have played a game that truly felt it was worth the money I spent on it. You will either get annoyed and quit in your first few hours or you will stick it out and learn how to play the game and be briefly addicted.

The biggest problem with the game is, it doesn't make you want to win, you win the crash landing mode for example by getting your people off the planet leaving your base behind, who wants that, the other two modes you can keep playing after victory though. The main good thing about the military mode is you eventually get to screw around with combat mechs that are hard to acquire in the other game modes and make the enemy hoards threat drop off a cliff.

If you play this do the tutorials first they are quite good each is short and covers a specific part of the game, then play the crash landing it is the most challenging mode, trade is for if you want to keep playing long term with your base, military is if your only interested in the combat/tower defence. There is a peaceful option for those that hate combat and just want to build.

You have 3 game modes, crash landing, trade and military outpost, they all largely boil down to the same thing, pick a set number of survivors from a large pool of people with their pro's and cons. Build a base survive until you complete the objective, in crash landing it's to get your people off the planet one at a time, trade is to buy the planet but you can hire as many of the other survivors as your trading enterprise is able to afford but they apply randomly to your job listing, in the military one you build a communications array that you have to keep powered as it slowly establishes communications with Earth, thing is once you switch it on your attacked constantly by waves of enemies.

There are 3 planets one that is temperate your full set of seasons, one that is a desert world that is hot in the day cold at night and another jungle planet that is warm all year round but with extra diseases for both your survivors and your crops.

There are a bunch of moons you can select to go with your planet they determine how the enemies spawn and how often etc.. basically think of it as the blood moon you see in zombie games telling you a hoard is about to attack but with variations to pick from lowering or increasing the difficulty, for example one makes the attacks only happen at night, another causes longer down time between attacks but you get larger hoards etc..

Your survivors during their stay on which ever planet you choose will be attacked by an ever increasing hoard of bugs, various types of big beetles, giant praying mantises, giant scorpion like bugs, with miniature versions as well some sort of long legged bug that is hard to describe but can basically walk through your field of bear traps without setting them off, all those you can deal with eventually just fine with standard tower defence and kill zones. The real pain is the flying enemies that bypass your ground based defences and force you to change tactics, either go out and kill them before they attack, build a bunker and shoot out of the windows or by some miracle find two mech cores and build yourself a combat mech and you will have hit the jackpot in killing the challenge, they are overpowered as heck but hard as hell to find, in all but the military game mode.

For those that hate hoard attacks and just want to build there is a peaceful game option that disables the attacks.

There are also a bunch of other settings that can up the challenge or lower it for example, one that sets all your survivors skills to near max or 0 right at the start, one that gives you a random selection of people but all those with combat skills start with a fancy sniper rifle things like that.

You can also use mods that can make the game brain dead easy or harder if you feel like it. For example their is a mod that lets you control when the enemy attacks and increase or decrease the hoard size from anything to 0% no attack waves to up to 200%.

That said be warned of one thing, the higher the difficulty the bigger the hoards of enemies don't play on the higher difficulties unless you confident in your computers specs, or you will see a massive performance drop off when a hoard of 1K plus bugs spawns in, to put it in perspective on medium I am seeing attack waves of around 2 - 3 hundred at most.

The attack waves increase in size based on the amount of survivors you have, how many years since the start of the game, how many resources you have stockpiled how many defences you have built and how many resources you have used in your base, don't overproduce or you will get a lot more enemies is attacks.

Also the movement speed is slow, you will be playing the game on triple speed almost all the time, don't make the mistake of having a giant base you only have a handful of people to work with, you don't want them wasting hours moving from one side of your base to the other to do jobs, logistics is extremely important in this game, make your base too large and the enemy will decide screw heading to your kill zone and just smash through your walls to get at your people.

Be warned though don't waste your stone it does not re spawn on the map, your limited to walk speed so you will have to go out further and further to find it, the maps are quite large your largely going to be sticking to the area around your chosen campsite.

In terms of research each game mode has slight differences, in the crash landing, trade and military there are a few unique techs that are just part of the default research tree for each but not in the others, aside from that there are special techs called breakthroughs you start with a handful and it takes a lot of time or luck to find others, in both the crash landing and military modes in the trade one you can eventually just buy pretty much all of them just takes time and a fat wallet.

in conclusion to what ever I just thought up, after my time with the game, if you enjoy tower defence, base building, colony sims give it a shot, if none of those interest you and you only have a few hours to play each day I would not recommend this game, it is not the kind of one where you will make a lot of progress without a heck of a lot of spare time, for me this pushed aside all my other hobbies for the better part of a month.\t\t\t\t\t
