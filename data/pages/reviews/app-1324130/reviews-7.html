\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like this game because it reminds me of The Sims:) The Sims: Sci-fi tower defence. AND, survivor's AI acts just like The Sims - when they first wake up they go into other people's rooms and just stand there!! HAHAHAHAHAHAHH :D so gud! But let us talk about THIS game. The beginning and mid-game are really fun and challenging - but the end game can be a bit ... boring :/ In most of the scenarios, by the end game there isn't much for your survivors to do, and they are usually incredibly happy and stress-free, meaning they go into 'Idle' state for long periods of time and cannot interact with leisure items because of their stress-free status. My biggest pet-peeves are the way tools, weapons and clothing degrade so fast, and the healing/medical mechanic. Firstly, the degarding effect is just too fast. And even if the item is only 75% degraded - the survivor, and other survivors complain of their dishevelled clothing. The medical/healing mechanic can be incredibly frustrating! Almost every wound needs a med-kit and a frustratingly long time to be healed - so if your survivor has many wounds and even medical emergencies, they could die simply because the mechanic takes so long!! ARGH!!!! But, overall, I do still recommend the game - the great parts of the game far outweigh the bad ones. Suggestions for the developers if they read this: change/fix the medical mechanic! :) Furthermore - make the survivors more like Sims. By this I mean that they should have actions that are interactions with survivors initiated by the player. They should have more objects to interact with and they should be able to interact with them regardless of how stressed or relaxed they are. Have some objects that can be interacted with by multiple survivors at a time - board games, the arcade, basketball net - and maybe a swimming pool/hot tub ;). Objects that can give them special stats or improve their stats. Social interactions with other survivors that can make them both feel good/bad, like flirting, complimenting, or asking the other to do leisure activities with them. Lastly, more decore type objects would be cool too - maybe wall treatments, like how the floor has floor coverings. Ability to change colours/look of objects and maybe even their armour/clothing. Maybe hit two birds with one stone - maybe the survivors can craft art/decore objects at the crafting table, or make rugs at the tailoring bench. They can get a positive temporary stat or increase one of their permanent stats, AND create an extra object for the compound.\t\t\t\t\t
