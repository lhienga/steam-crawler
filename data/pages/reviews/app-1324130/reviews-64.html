\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is not as expected. I was expecting a city-builder type thing, instead the game plays more like the "sims"; micro-management of tasks and managing personally traits

There are different scenarios, but they are all pretty similar. The basic scenario is... 1/ Crash land on planet with 4 survivors then, build/research your way off. Sounds okay, but it becomes a chore micro-managing tasks and each new game is pretty much the same.

A lot of effort has gone into the visuals, and they are pretty good. The HUD is intuitive and controls easy. The concept is good but there are (fixable) issues with the game-play. For example, you can set general task priorities for each of the characters (nice) but you can't actually control what they do next - I was left wondering for an hour why no-one was supplying my furnace with sticks and no obvious way to remedy (It would be good if the next 20 or so tasks could be shown in a single list and tasks could be added)

I am going to continue giving this a go - i've not given up yet\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy Review Vs ChatGPT's:

So far I have really enjoyed this game, and it quite possibly might be one of my new favourite colony simulators. It has similar aspects to other games like Going Medieval and StoneHearth but at the same time is very much entirely different in its own way.

I've played about 30 hours so far and still have so much to research and new scenarios to experience. There is a lot of stuff packed into this game and has been very well thought out. The progression is also really smooth, and I've found there's never a dull moment and always something that can be done to make your colony more efficient and happy.

Such as building AC units to control room temperature during heatwaves or lightning rods to deter your settlers getting injured when thunderstorms occur. Or in my case you can just let them get zapped - the settlers can actually receive permanent neural damage that alters their state of consciousness, which in-turn makes them immune to debuffs from lack of sleep or relaxation stats at the very very very small cost of some severe damage to their motor function... so they're very efficient workers for me lol.

It's the little details like that that make this game so fun. The communication system for the settlers is also really well-built, and you get to see how their interactions build relationships over time (like in Sims). For example, I have two settlers that are interested in the same person and it results in them having negative interactions.

Overall a great game, would recommend :).
