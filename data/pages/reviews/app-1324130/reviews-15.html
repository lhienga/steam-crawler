\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game came close, but in the end: Do Not Recommend. There's just not enough fine-tuned and well-considered control over the game mechanics. In a competitive category, I don't see what Stranded: Alien Dawn is doing that others aren't doing better.

At first glance, this is Rim World but 2.5D and with character models. But when you look closer, it's actually also a dumbed-down Rim World:
- The tasks, task categories, and task prioritization are nearly identical to Rim World, but the pathing isn't great, and hauling automatically disrupts long-term actions.
- The storage UI is nearly identical, but there are no prioritization details.
- The cooking/crafting UI is nearly identical, but there aren't half as many QoL details.
- The combat/drafting/hunting is nearly identical, but it's not very interesting.
- You can build on multiple floors! But the Foundation->Flooring->Walls->Ceiling->Roof requirement for every location becomes spaghetti construction very fast.
- The map is huge, but traveling to resources/locations that are not-that-far is nearly impossible because you can't take food with you.

In the end, what actually makes this a Do Not Recommend is the imbalance between how long it takes for the survivors to travel, and how long it takes to accomplish tasks -- It takes nearly ~15 hours of max-speed gameplay to get 2 years to pass, and during that time you can barely break free of subsistence living. It's just not a lot of fun.

Rim World is doing the core game better, and if you're looking for 2.5D isometric Rim World, Going Medieval is doing that better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've heard allot of people call this game "3d Rimworld", this Game is most definitely heavily inspired by Rimworld.
The Plot of the game? There is a couple of gameplay styles with a third added by the new dlc Robots and Guardians.

--------------
Game Modes
--------------

- Classic Stranded Scenario (Classic Rimworld Start) Where you start with 4 survivors who crash land on an alien world.
You have to survive, gather resources, defend against bug attacks, build homes for your survivors, provide for their needs (warmth, food, shelter, entertainment, luxuries) to stop them having meltdowns or worse, dying. Researching technology is vital for achieving victory in this scenario, as leaving the planet is the goal and when you discover how to make contact for transportation? The number of threats will greatly increase giving a sense of urgency, only when all stranded are lifted off the planet do you complete the scenario.

- Build a Galactic Trade Outpost.. As the name states, in this scenario you land on the alien world in order to build a trade outpost. Your task is to make goods and sell them to passing trade ships in order to make enough Galactic Credits to buy the planet while fending off against bugs.

- Robots and Guardians Scenario.. In this scenario you must protect 'Hope', a sentient robot on the run from a consortium with high-tech military robots and other means. Hold them off while Hope gains full sentience.

----------------
The Gameplay?
----------------

You establish tasks by clicking on objects like trees, rocks, fallen debris and by placing objects to be built etc. and the survivors will carry those tasks out (Depending if the skills to do those tasks are activated in the skills table). In emergencies? You can take control of Survivors (Drafting) and they will follow your commands, but this comes at a risk of the Survivors mental health, the longer they are under your control; so all those who play Rimworld? Will figure out the controls in no time, for those new to this genre? There is a pretty good tutorial.
Once you have built shelters and primitive means of beds, stoves to cook, production tables to make primitive weapons for hunting and defence? You'll build a research table and start unlocking new technologies to improve your chances of survival, which you will need to do as every couple of seasons? You will be attacked by swarms of bugs. They start off small, but eventually their numbers increase, newer and stronger variants will come along and before you know it? You've got yourself Starship Troopers levels of swarms.

---------------------
The User Interface?
---------------------

The User Interface is a more streamlined imitation of Rimworld's interface. From how you adjust the sleep/work/recreational hours timetable, down to the priorities of every workers tasks from 1 (Top Priority) to 5 (Lowest Priority) in each category. Certain workers can't perform specific tasks which is down to negative traits in their personality that impedes those tasks. There is over a dozen work types, from healing, construction, cutting, planting, harvesting, hunting, crafting, tailoring, assembly etc. Again almost identical to Rimworld's system.

--------------------------------------------
Stranded's own version of the Storytellers?
--------------------------------------------

Other similarities to Rimworld are the use of 'Storytellers', only in Stranded they are called 'Moons', You have the Cassandra Classic style moon for those who want a steady-paced progression in their playthrough.
There is even a Randy Random and Phoebe Chillax Moon that gives you 'random events', 'slow progression' and 'chaotic events' for more hardcore players.

------------------------------------
Is Stranded better than Rimworld?
------------------------------------

- Stranded has allot of things that Rimworld doesn't have, the ability to build houses up to 5 floors, where as Rimworld is just an overhead minecraft system of resource gathering and building.
- Stranded has Mech's, Robots and Drones that you can use for defence, as servants etc.
- Lovely 3d graphics and animations for characters.

-------------
Conclusion?
-------------

Apart from that? There is nothing that sets Stranded: Alien Dawn apart from Rimworld, as Rimworld.. For what it lacks in graphics and the above? It makes up for in the sheer amount of features and things to do in the game.
Stranded: AD has a random seed system for generating land that you must build and conquer.. Where as Rimworld has an entire procedural generated Planet that you can travel to, you're not forced to stay in one place like in Stranded: Alien Dawn.
If you feel there isn't enough resources to make your current settlement work in Rimworld? Pack up and leave with what food and equipment you can carry and settle someplace else? You have that freedom.
The lack of new recruits in Stranded, especially in the crash landing scenario, about 6 people max. In Rimworld? You can have loads of new recruits, it's feeding them all that's the problem.

And most important, Rimworld is about suffering, it's about the mental state of your characters trying to survive in a hostile alien world.. It's about the journey, not the destination.
Where as Stranded is a survival game with Rimworld elements, but not the Rimworld spirit.

Don't get me wrong, Stranded is an amazing game and it's only going to get better when they release new content.
Highly recommend playing it.. But it's not as complex as Rimworld; but still worthy of a try. Played over 300+ hours, it's certainly a fun game to play and what you can build? Is down to the imagination.\t\t\t\t\t
