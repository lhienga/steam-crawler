I like the additional requirements of having to observe and understand local resources before you can use them - although I don't love that random seeds will also sometimes shut you out of certain resources. All in all, though, a solid, enjoyable game with a good production loop. Recent updates have added quality of life concerns, easing development building and helping with late game gathering and production. Well worth your time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy recommendation is that if you have never played RimWorld, try that first. Overall it's a better version of this game. If you have played Rimworld but it was too complicated to enjoy, S:AD may be a better fit for you and you should give it a try (or if you can't get into the simple graphics and think the 3D will help). But if you are a Rimworld expert S:AD will probably be a disappointment for you due to lack of depth in the management tools and how unbalanced a lot of things are.

Tl;dr - Stranded Alien Dawn is good but definitely lacks polish considering it is a copy of Rimworld. Many shortcomings feel like a combination of the devs being lazy and not actually playing their own game to experience how frustrating some mechanics are firsthand, which is sad. Hopefully it gets some balance changes in the future, but the fact it hasn't yet this long after launch makes me doubt that will happen.



The game is basically a simplified version of Rimworld with 3D graphics. Almost all the mechanics are identical but the management systems aren't as fleshed out. For example, clothing wears out the same as in Rimworld, but you need to manually tell colonists when you want them to change gear instead of being able to set a threshold where they automatically switch out what they are wearing when it gets too worn out. And then you need to manually mark worn out gear to get scrapped instead of having anything below a certain HP automatically get scrapped.

Same thing with the storage system. Containers have a "Store here first" option you can check to prioritize where items get put first but if you want something stored in multiple places (ex. food in the fridge, but then also wanting a container near barrels to make loading them quicker) you are out of luck. You also have to choose between allowing any item on the approved list take up the entire container or limit each approved item to only 1 stack. So if you have only two items you want to store but want to ensure there is always space for some of both you're forced into leaving the container half empty. Meanwhile Rimworld lets you set storage priority order and set specific limits on each item in storage. Hell, you can customize each crafting job to specify which stockpile to get inputs from and where to store the item when it's finished! In this you have 0 control over any of that.

Another example is the taming menu, which does show growth and fattening %, training level, etc, but for some reason isn't filterable/sortable and doesn't show which animals are pregnant. And selecting an animal from the taming list doesn't actually select them either, just centers the camera (which has a menu over it) on the animal. So if it's in a crowd you end up needing to click through them individually anyway.

Crafting times are very long and doesn't seem to be impacted by colonist level much either. It's impossible to find info on how each level affects crafting speed but I think someone at level 10 crafts something 2.5-3x faster than someone at level 0. So assuming that is true, a crossbow would take 10 hours to craft at level 0 and 3.33 hours at level 10. To craft a full set of clothes for a colonist it takes 60 hours (at level 0) of crafting time: shirt-12, pants - 12, vest - 18, boots - 12, helmet - 6. So even at level 10 it will take at least 20 hours. And then you can only use 50% of the clothes health before colonists complain they are worn out and need to be replaced.

I'm incredibly picky about the games I play. I usually either fall head over heels for a game and play it obsessively or put it down, forget about it, and move on. This game grabbed me more than I ever expected.
Initially, I was skeptical, thinking it was just a "Rimworld rip-off." However, after sinking 40+ hours into it, I can confidently say it stands on its own two feet with plenty of unique features.

If you enjoy colony building games, I highly recommend it. Even more, I encourage those who haven't tried this genre(s) before to give it a shot. This game is incredibly accessible to newcomers. The tutorial is excellent.. For those worried about steep learning curves, this is a fantastic introduction to the genre and its many subgenres.

Playing on Easy is the perfect way to learn the ropes, and it still presents a challenge as you progress. It's not just a cozy walk in the park, but it's not brutally punishing either.Higher difficulty however prepare for a solid challenge.
The Workshop offers a lot of mods that can tailor the game to your preferences, making it easier, harder, or completely overhauling the experience (think Floris for Mount and Blade or Falskaar for Skyrim).

While I haven't tried the recently released DLC yet, the game continues to receive new content updates even if it is from DLC.

So, if you've read this far, go ahead and buy it. You won't be disappointed. Off I go to buy DLC. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really really good, I was actually surprised at how good it is and how polished it is as well, amazing!

If I could recommend it 10x times, I'd do it.

A few suggestions:
  • Sometimes I want to move a specific group of items from a storage to another, let us do that! The only way I found to do that is to restrict storage of that item in that storage, but it's not the same. For example, sometimes, a set of 100 raw insect meat is about to spoil, while there are other 400 with plenty of shelf life still. I wanna move just the specific 100 that is about to expire to a freezer
  • Let handling be disabled completely (even when butchering or harvesting) when a delivery drone is available, this is easy to micro but it would be very convenient!
  • My survivors tend to go to the roof quite often for no reason while relaxing. There's nothing there, they just go there. It's not very annoying or anything, it's just weird and takes a little bit more time for them to get out of there and do something else. Also, they seem to be able to get items from the floor below if they are in a store shelf as if they were on the ground on the second floor. This just happens to be a very convenient bug since they don't need to walk all the way to the storage shelf xd
  • When customizing the map/world before a game, describe each thing with more details. Such as, what does this moon do exactly? What are the main characteristics of this world (desert, for example, lacks wood almost completely)?
  • Add more scenarios, please! I have yet to play the third one but I already know I'll want to play more (not necessarily the same scenarios). Do anything, a scenario to destroy a super insect nest would be cool, for example (I have yet to destroy an insect nest btw xd)
  • Scavenging is kind of a pain once you have scavenged all nearby ships, it takes a long time and a lot of running from the survivors. Maybe make a research to improve that?
  • More enemies! I'm tired of fighting 60 beetles :P
  • A more ambitious idea: another set of survivors controlled by the AI would build a settlement in the same world as yours, you'd have to compete for resources and either ally with them, trade and live peacefully or make them your enemy and destroy them. Maybe this opens the opportunity for multiplayer?
