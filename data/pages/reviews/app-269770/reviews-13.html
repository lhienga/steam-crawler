\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter following the development of this game for an entire decade it feels strange to finally be able to play it. I thought about getting the early access after Dev Blog #200 but fought against it. There's nothing like being able to play through the entire game from beginning to end without any interruptions and not knowing what to expect.

As far as the game itself, it hark'ens back to two of my most memorable games growing up: Zelda Link to the Past and Secret of Mana.

Music: Although nothing can beat the Secret of Mana soundtrack this game's soundtrack is pretty good. There are some memorable melodies that stick with you long after you've finished a play session.

Gameplay: I'm a simpleton. I've stuck with investing in two melee (single hand) skills, added some shielding protection... and that's it. I hope it is enough to beat the game.

Boss Battles: The boss battles seem ridiculously bloated with ____ all over the screen but it's easy enough to learn the patterns, knowing when to attack and when to cower in a corner. It only takes a few deaths before the experiencing the satisfaction of beating a boss.

Story: I'm all about games with story. I could care less about combat, although good combat design is a bonus. I have no desire to play the Arcade mode. Just give me a decent story and I'm in. The story here is on par with this style of game. Some characters are quirky and there is the occasional humor. It's enough to keep me interested.

Loot: Games like these is all about the loot, of course. Just like with Zelda and Secret of Mana it's nice to find or purchase better weapons, better armor, etc. That is the other hook that keeps me invested in this particular game. It seems the the Devs have done a good job balancing everything out with loot and story progression. I'm never too powerful but also never too weak.

I can't wait to finish this game.

Full review:
I've had so much fun with this game, the story is simple and interesting, with it's hidden lore or archives you find in the overworld/dungeons, which makes it more in depth. The combat reminds me of Legend of Zelda a Link to the Past or any 2D Zelda for that matter, it was a great throwback and a feeling of familiarity that I didn't know I was missing. It is pretty straight forward with the controls, either with keyboard or controller, however the way the skills are assigned to the keys, at first, is a little disorienting, I liked that you can change them however you like. This game is great if you want to put in the hours to grind for the different items or if you want to blitz through it (which I haven't been able and didn't want to do 😅). The music is great and fits the ambiance of the different towns or dungeons in the game. Started it in hard but couldn't go through it, skill issue. Gotta say, sometimes when using a controller the player doesn't turn or has a delay when doing it. Other than that, as I've said, I'm loving it.



I got this game in a discount but I do fully recommend buying it full price, it is worth it imo.



Congrats to the Devs/Artists in this game\t\t\t\t\t
