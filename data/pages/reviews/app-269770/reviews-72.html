\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me preface this by saying: You will get your moneys worth with the content that exists, sure its nice to have a whole game but a lot of the people acting as though they got ripped off with 10 hours at least for a relatively cheap indie game and somehow this means the developers are scammers. doesn't really hold water.

And the first time I played this (2 years ago going by my first achievement, though take no weight to the 59 hours I'm awful for leaving things on and running in the background/over night etc), I probably would have recommended it. In a way I guess the comparison to Starbound is true, because like that game, the more content I experience, the less content I feel is there and the less I enjoyed it.

Grinding, It's in the name....but theres so little of it. And as time goes on there's less of it. In fact the closest thing to a fetch quest you get past the current middle point, is a joke of one where you don't have to do it in the end. Bosses past the 1st temple are not only itemless, they aren't even replayable, a feature showcased early on then forgotten about...why refight bosses for unique drops, right. Cards are pretty uninspiring too, the first one makes you think "oh unique abilities I can't gain through levels", then the rest are mostly dry stat boosts. Again bosses, don't even have cards at all. The lack of story mode achievements showcase this too (the arcade mode now has more then this main game), there's an achievement to get the "massive" sum of 10000...then thats it, it doesn't take much imagination or time to set a few goals with extra zeros, like millionaire. Lack of variety in enemies, even the "elite" monster system is just a circle round a normal enemy and no unique drops at all to them, so no real cause to get out there and grind.

Repetitive puzzles, for updates that come so few and far between why is so much of the content just rehashing the same puzzle mechanic you encounter in the game. Yes I get it sometimes the mirrors are unversed of where they need to be and I need to put one out of the way to swap them round. I got it in the first temple, I didn't need 5 more regions of it. And if it takes 5 months to just reskin what the mirrors look like...is it worth it? Is that quality content worth waiting for? The last area to receive it just made me turn of the game, it just acted as a barrier to caring about the content past that "puzzle".

Rushed yet drawn out story. It was fairly obvious where the story was going for years, and when we finally get a plot dump, it's all crammed into one section of the game, though with lots of padding drawing it out like some of the reveals are truly shocking (they aren't, I don't need an NPC to repeat an obvious point to me time and time again).

Massive tonal shifts. This is probably the biggest thing for me and why I can't see myself being interested enough to check back in the future. It goes from this fairly light romp and mockery of common and old RPG tropes, both gameplay and story ones, including elements people enjoy and would be seeking from the game. To taking itself so incredibly seriously that it almost becomes a parody of itself, past temple 2. The gameplay changes, becoming much more dry, the dialogue does, even a character that had a big step up of being the one you "love to hate" you are expected to like because of some minor event, was it too much work to have a comic relief "villain" since the real enemy was revealed? Seems like more of a reason to have one. And really the story I bet most already know how it will end, doesn't deserve the reverence it gives itself.

And probably the biggest concern is, why if they want to ensure the game is done to a high standard, if there is only three people on the team....why have they basically added a game mode that has nothing to do with the game, in fact they rather proudly announce "Don't worry theres no character twinking benefits" (Like have they ever played a grinding game? One with other modes? Isn't that most of the point? To twink new characters and create more replayability through it? As it is now it just seems like a feature, certain pet, which isn't even that special is just arbitrarily locked to another mode, when its the main mode that uses it), a mode that actually a lot of the updates in the last 2 years have had focus on. A mode that almost seems like a different game, just using the skin of this one. Surely itd make more sense to have the low manpower small team focus on and finish the rather short story mode which has already taken so long to make, rather then cause more delays and distractions. It kind of counteracts a lot of the Devs comments when faced with criticism about dev time.

So all in all, it started as something simple you could easily get your moneys worth out of, but it's changing to something no really worth following anymore. And while I found the complaints of "scamming" really silly, the deflections don't hold up, when their "focus to maintain quality" has been split to a frivolous game mode, to months being taken to add...another simply mirror puzzle and even game features aren't actually maintained and seemed to be forgotten about almost as quick as they were introduced, how is that maintaining a quality? You couldn't even give bosses past the 3rd an item drop or rematch. Maybe instead of 70 uninteresting npcs who don't add as much as they seem to think they do, some addition enemy variety and actual gameplay would be time better spent and go a long way to that goal. Maybe less time reskinning a mirror puzzle there is no "grind" too, there is nothing gained out of, and more time giving elites and bosses unique items and cards. Giving recipes to items that a fair few don't seem to have any, even food based ones, not even being made into meals for pets.

You can get a tenners worth out of the game but if you do pick it up, don't expect more of what you experience in the first few hours. In fact just try and enjoy it for what its because expecting anything else will leave you disappointing years after the initial purchase. And you might find more consistent, complete and endlessly replayable games in free flash ones out there (or with more interesting puzzles at least).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start with the negative.
  • We've seen some latency issues related to some mechanics, which affected maybe one out of every thirty monster types. So some fights were needlessly frustrating.
  • Discrete walking annnoys me a lot. I use a controller but you can only walk in eight directions and there is only one speed, which feels backwards and limiting, especially in a game that generally calls for precision.
  • Some cards (rare drops with passive effects) have extremly low drop rate (0.1%), from enemies that spawn very seldom, yet the card is absolutely amazing (Looking at you pecko, 100 HP card, hours of grinding)

Other than that this game is a dream. Cooperative RPG heavily inspired by Zelda.
  • Awesome boss fights (and actually difficult when playing on expert).
  • Great character progression, talent/skill speccing to heavily tailor your character to your playstyle.
  • Interesting card dynamic with generally minor buffs (except in some cases which I'm not a fan of) that don't feel necessary but give a slight stat boost or oherwise positive tweak.
  • Decent variation of gear and stats which encourages min-maxing and balancing offence vs. defence.
  • SO MUCH CONTENT! I bought this game two large acts ago and I was still satisfied with the amount of content. And we are still getting more, for free.
  • Devs are very communicative and frequently roll out interesting "frontline" patches for those that want to get early access to content
  • There is an entire rogue like dungeon crawler in the game called "Arcade Mode" that could have been its own game that I would have paid for. But as everything else in regards to this game it was added for free.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t//EDIT: Since there has not been any progress on the game for over a year now, i finally give up on believing that the game will ever be finished. I am very disappointed about this.

Sigh. It was a very hard decision to rate this this game either good or bad.

It would not feel alright if i rate it bad, because there has been put so much love into this storyline, the mechanics and the graphics. It is truly an amazing game with the capability of competing with the shining stars of its genre. A piece of art.

On the other hand, It would not feel alright if i rate it well, because there is so little progress. Almost non-existing progress. It seems like the developers themselves have given up on their own project, and i still ask myself why.

Why i decided to recommend this game anyways:
First thought is always what the developers are doing wrong. But in the second thought, i remember how fun and cute it was to play through the game, with the amazing soundtrack (and a little snowman) as my companion. I can only think of good times i had in SoG, and i feel the passion and love of the developers in it. I refuse to think this will not be completed.
I do not regret that i spent my money on this.\t\t\t\t\t
