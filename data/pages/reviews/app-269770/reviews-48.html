\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is it! The plot twist reveals itself, the final dungeon is in sight and... ... ... An NPC blocks the way in and tells me this is as far as the development's been completed. *%$!

Oh well, it's not like I can complain. Secrets of Grindea is already everything it set out to be. Taking inspiration from games like Secret of Mana and the Legend of Zelda, the result is an Action RPG with the same fine blend of deep storytelling and self aware humour that made Evoland 1 & 2 so charming.

Combat is simple and easy, yet engaging and satisfying and the world is varied enough that nothing ever feels repetitive. One dungeon has you manipulate the seasons, while another puts you through time travel puzzles and the one most recently added has you phase in and out of a ghost world.

My only complaint is no controller support, an issue I'm sure they'll fix with a later update. It's not that big an issue, but my hands got cramped after a while jumping from key to key.
Furthermore, the quickslot layout is a little awkward. Sometimes I would try to use the Blink skill to avoid an incoming attack, only to find I hit the wrong key and take a hit.

Good thing you can rebind all the keys.

I only have one other thing to say about this game. I hope you're either skilled or have friends who can help you in co-op because the boss-fights in this game are insanely difficult. They go from merely challenging to "Git Gud son!" by the time you reach the second dungeon.

You'll find yourself bombarded with attacks that you'll swear belong in a space shooter and several times you'll be convinced the game's trolling you as it fools you with false victories. (You cannot convince me the mimic boss wasn't designed specifically to troll the player) Some of these fights actually brought me to the brink of tears. However, they're not impossible and when you beat them, you feel on top of the world.

Even though at the time of this review, the final dungeon has yet to be implemented, I can wait.

This game promises to be a masterpiece when it's completed and until then, the Roguelike Arcade mode can keep me busy.\t\t\t\t\t
