It's one of those games that despite being in early access, could've very easily been pushed out with not a lot of content, and forever been known as "Dang, that game was good but I wish there was more of it." I've been following this game since early 2016, and I'm incredibly happy with how it's come along so far. Every time I come back and play and get to experience a bit more content, see all the extra side content that gets added, and added flavor to the levels I had previously fully explored? That's good ♥♥♥♥.

Now, development has been "slow" yes, but even then, what's the issue? I've gotten almost 40 hours out of an unfinished game. For fifteen bucks? That's a deal if I've ever heard of it. I've gotten less enjoyment out of $60 AAA games than I have out of this early access $15 game on steam. I've gotten my money's worth, and then some. It's good, I love it. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first played this game in 2016, when it was just under halfway done. At the time of this writing in 2022, it's still only almost complete.

But to me, what's here is certainly worth it.

The gameplay is solid. The controls are intuitive, and the skill system is deep. The gameplay loop during the across the main story is engaging, and it's bolstered by the perfect amount of side content: enough to make the world feel full while not distracting from your main objectives.

The presentation is well done. The animations for your character and NPCs are fantastic, as are the sound effects. The spritework for the overworld and the main music is a little inconsistent, but acceptable. At worst, the game can look or sound generic, but at its best it's absolutely beautiful.

The writing is also enjoyable, though it can be a little niche at times. The overarching plot starts off slowly, but picks up very strongly at the games midpoint and carries that momentum all the way to the current end. Different major characters take turns in the spotlight, and they all make a distinctive impression. That said, some are more deep than others, and the games extensively prolonged development time shows with some inconsistent characterization near the end of the current content. And on that note, it may still be a while before we actually get the ending we're waiting for.

There are some other nitpicks to be made about the "final" product. For example, while there is huge amount of customization for skills, the game limits your ability to experiment with different builds by including a gold cost to refunding skill tree points. Players who love to "solve" these kinds of systems, like powergamers or speedrunners, probably won't mind, but it can be disheartening to casual players who might feel forced to stick to a decision they don't like.

Additionally, the game can be quite grindy. Multiple parts of the game encourage the player to stop making progress just to farm resources, such as gear crafting, side missions, and 100% completion. Not helping matters is the difficulty, which swings quite dramatically from hard to easy depending on how much grinding you're willing to do at each stage of the game.

Nonetheless, I found this game thoroughly enjoyable. The gameplay, presentation, and story kept me hooked for hours, and its flaws didn't dissuade me from playing it through to the end. If you're a fan of Action RPGs, this is a must-play.\t\t\t\t\t
