\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thought I had left a negative review of this game a long time ago, and must of remembered it's early access and pumped the breaks a little bit.

I came back to change it after playing through it again after some time. My initial gripes with the game was that it was so meta-y in terms of skills and enemies, but after getting a few more games under my belt I can safely say this is a great game. Artstyle, music, and gameplay are all great. The story ain't too bad either, but nothing to really write home about; it's certainly better than it was, if I'm remembering right.

But it's one of the best "comfy" co-ops. Both players share loot (save card drops - not really that big of a deal - and they're separate, anyways), the enemy AI works really well with multiple players and it really can make some really cool teamwork happen with different builds synergizing, or desperate life saving moves. Especially in the Arcade mode.

Some of the skills may not be entirely "equal" but they are all fun and viable. I'm fairly confident you could beat the game with your skills spent all over the place.

If you enjoyed stardew valley's humor, artstyle, and writing, and wanted a little more combat polish and a little less farming, socializing, etc., this games great for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally fun game, it's exactly as advertised. Fun class/character building system and tons of flexibility and options given to explore new playstyle avenues.

The story is fun enough although nothing mind-shattering, the graphics, music, aesthetics are all very suitable and feels good. I haven't really ran into many bugs aside from improper collision once and a while but it's never been a problem gameplay-wise, just a fun discovery at that point.

Very fun to play solo or with friends. Arcade mode is a blast too although I've burnt out on it. Game has a good difficulty curve with some very challenging optional bosses, grinding has its substantial benefits but is in no way mandatory. Combat generally doesn't make you feel cheated and is mostly just getting consistently good at boss/enemy patterns. It is EXACTLY as advertised IMO and if you think you'd like it based on the steam store page and other media, you probably will like it.

The big main complaint to this game is that you shouldn't expect it to ever actually e completed. Yes, I'm sure it will at one point but that "one point" could be like 5 years from now. Development has been EXTREMELY slow, I think I got this game like almost 6 years ago. The game's current story mode has been slated to have a prologue, 4 "chapters" kind of, and then who knows what after for the conclusion of the storyline. 5+ years ago when I got this game, there were 2 chapters done. I haven't played since the last couple updates but I don't even think the 4th chapter is done by now? Or it was just finished in the last couple updates. Chapters are not long, I can beat them and do most of the side content in a few hours each. Lots of system overhauls/improvements/side content added like housing but the development speed is horribly slow.

Game is super fun, I thoroughly enjoyed my time with it and will thoroughly enjoy it again when I feel like picking it up. Would definitely recommend as long as you don't mind the development speed being abysmal.\t\t\t\t\t
