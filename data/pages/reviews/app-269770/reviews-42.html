Light hearted affair with a bit of a deceiving name

Do you grind?
Somewhat. The game mechanics encourage you to wait till later in the game, where you have access to better skills and weapons before attempting to do so.
Each area, once cleared initially, will progressively dump more monsters into the area, slowly building to a max limit and begin adding the special monsters in as well. The chance for gaining a card, special drop which nets you a permanent bonus, increases with each kill of that particular monster. So, you'll tend to net the card before hitting three hundred kills. So, coming back to earlier levels after clearing later stages makes this process easier.

Can you AFK grind?
Eh, maybe. The developers patch out most methods for doing so, and the areas where you can do it, nets you less gold then the later areas with about ten minutes of active farming. Don't let that stop you from trying to figure out a way to do so.

Boring stuff
Your character isn't important in the grand scheme of things. Other characters tend to be better suited for the task, and you end up in the main plot mostly due to your inability to think things through.
The story tends to be tongue and cheek, with plenty of references, somewhat predictable twists, and plenty of detours.
The side content ranges from frustrating to enjoyable. The phase stuff requires outside-the-box thinking to claim the prizes for.
The perfect block mechanic, may work better for you. I like summoning stuff, and tend to maintain a small army of creatures around me. This makes it hard to spot enemy stuff.
The potions feel rather weak, especially the healing potion. The developers wish for you to spend several level's worth of skill points to make the potions feel stronger. That feels rather unfair.
The bow's alright.
I wish we could get a better fishing rod or something to make the task easier.

The ghost vision tends to reveal hidden things in most areas. I do recommend rebinding the controls, especially when you do the phase course on the ship.
Some gimmicks, like the gas mask, the mining pick, and the net, only work to give you one or two items.
The joke dungeon based off the math puzzles, and the more of a joke math dungeon ought to not challenge anyone. But with the American education being what it is, I can see people failing to solve it and looking up solutions, even the one in the snow.
Outside of that, some areas require fertilizer. One such area leads you to fight the bishop as a hidden boss.

I've basically completed the current content with about 40 hours of game play. Some of the stuff on the to do list, requires me to change out equipment and then try new forms of combat. I don't feel like doing that at the moment.

Still, the game's worth a try at the least.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview as of "The Loose Ends" update. Finished my first playthrough July 4th at around 40 hours, took my time doing as much as I could find, only had to look up a few things that were a bit obscure or really tricky (I'm looking at you sneaky phase jumping puzzle with "hidden" chests in the mountain, well played)

Would absolutely recommend this game. Smooth playthrough, combat is fun and has a lot of potential variation for builds (replayability playing those different builds) Story is fairly simple but well done with plenty of humor along the way, some subtle fourth wall breaking and making fun of itself/games in general.

Overall the game flows really well, there are a ton of little QOL things in place to make the game go along smoothly without feeling like it's slowing you down just to slow you down (wish I wrote some of these down as I played. Text is quick, can even hold a button to skip through dialog which nice for multiple playthroughs or even talking to NPCs if you want to see if they say anything new or you just accidentally clicked a chatty one. Can teleport out of anywhere to waypoints you've unlocked. Mobs start respawning near instantly if you grind the same area, which you will be doing if you want to farm their cards, which all come with improvements of some kind for your character)

Complaints? I'm sure there was something, but whatever it was it was minor enough I've since forgotten.

Looking forward to the finished story, and more content, perhaps some added end game? My first build was really feeling good, and I'd love to be able to play more content using it in a sort of "completed" stage, instead of the mix and match building it up while leveling. Will def mess around with arcade mode and try other builds, much potential for completely different things, and mix and matching.\t\t\t\t\t
