\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKind of like Secret of Mana with much better combat and actual significant rpg progression and build/equipment planning. Boldly, there are no classes or rigid skill trees to choose from. Instead you can pick any skill you want and mix things up however you want. Including this freedom was probably very hard to balance, but is great fun as a result. There is also an "arcade" mode, which basically is just a roguelike side game. For not being the main game, it's surprisingly robust and better than many average games that have only this mode.

I will say the writing is a bit too kid-ish and I ended up skipping most of it which is very unlike me. There's a decent joke here and there, but mostly I just feel like I'm not getting anything out of the typical tropes and lack of surprises. I guess I'm just not the intended audience at this age. I've seen the token jerk character lose at something and go "how could this happen?? Dang you!" enough times in other stories, you know...That's all right, though. I enjoy most of the rest.

Regarding all the "game will never be finished" complaints... While certainly it's been forever and the game still isn't complete, you have to consider what the game already has. I have a sense they don't want to tick the "game is complete" box before it's what they really envisioned. There is actually quite a lot of content in the game already. If they rushed the game out more quickly, but it lacked most of the current content, would you be happy? Obviously no story mode ending yet is upsetting, and nobody can deny this is taking absurdly long. But if you accept the main game has no end yet, and focus on what's there, the game has tons of awesome content. What's here is done very well. It does hurt that we still have no closure, but in the mean time I'm having fun in arcade mode.

Try not to let "game is not complete" scare you away. There's plenty to do and this probably doesn't mean much more than "final dungeon is still missing", ignoring the already big pile of content. Many developers would put less work into all the other content and already have "finished" the game, but with a more barren world outside of the actual main attractions. Definitely hope we get to finish soon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t.: Summary :.

This is a really fun game! It's got witty, on-the-nose, poke-fun-at-the-genera humor, very polished sprite work & animations, bosses that strike that perfect balance of super difficult but SO satisfying to beat, a story that's interesting, excellent character "class" customization, and—once I figured it out—the most satisfying RPG grinding experience I've ever encountered!

.: Visuals :.

The colors are cheerful and vibrant. The sprite work and animations are incredibly clean and crisp. There can be a lot going on on-screen at once, but information is relayed to the player in ways that are both crystal clear and unobtrusive. I know exactly when my buffs run out, when a potion refills, how much health my summon has left, how much health surrounding enemies have, how much damage everyone is doing, and when enemies spawn in as stronger variants, all with a quick glance around the screen. Boss fights are also very good at telegraphing incoming damage to the player, without lowering the difficulty at all. Speaking of...

.: Boss Fights :.

I put the game down years ago after dying to the Cursed Priestess a lot, but picked it back up the other day and beat her on my second try without upgrading my character at all, just using a different strategy. It was SO satisfying! I'm typically more of a casual gamer, so difficulty can scare me away from a game, particularly combat difficulty. But the bosses in this game strike that perfect balance: they're tough-as-nails, but absolutely doable with enough practice and observation. It feels like an actual triumph to overcome them, and they only feel unfair if I haven't figured out the right strategy (as with the Cursed Priestess).

.: Grinding :.

I hadn't done any real grinding in the game until recently 'cause I didn't understand the card mechanic. To be frank, I had no interest in grinding 'cause I thought it'd be as mindless as grinding is in other games. But it's not! This game really lives up to its name and makes the grinding process as fun and engaging as possible. If you clear an area, the game picks up that you're wanting to grind for a while and starts spawning in enemies at a nice clip. The death and loot sounds and animations make grinding super satisfying, as does the steady stream of action without being overwhelming. Best of all, there's an actual end goal with grinding: the cards! These puppies give you incredible bonuses and stat boosts, so you definitely don't want to skip out on collecting them.

.: Customization :.

The way you build your character in Secrets of Grindea is entirely up to you. Armor and weapons that affect the appearance of your character have the option to be equipped as a cosmetic change only. The skill progression is the most open-ended I have ever seen in a game. There are no classes or skill trees, though there are different kinds of skill points, each kind used to acquire or upgrade skills from different categories. It is extremely open-ended and you are truly free to build whatever kind of character your heart desires.

.: In Conclusion :.

I would highly recommend this game! I've had a ton of fun with it and I'm happy to see that the devs are still actively working on it. Sometimes fullscreen and controller support can be a little wonky, but nothing that gets in the way after I load up my save and start playing.\t\t\t\t\t
