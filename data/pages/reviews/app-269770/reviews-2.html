Pros:
•So much charm and personality to this game. I love how every single character in the game has their own
little dialogue with the main character and it changes through out the game.

• The pixel art is fantastic, soooooo many animations to this game. It's fun running through the towns and just look at what everyone is doing.

• The grinding. Yup. That is the whole point to this game and they made it so enjoyable. All the drops and especially when a card drops that is such a fantastic feeling.

• Skills. So many skills and so many different ability builds you can experiment with.

Meh:
• The Story, it was just ok. There were a couple memorable things but it was just meh.

Bad:
• The crafting recipes were usually garbage and I could get better items from the store. This could be because of my 2H Melee build though.

Buy this game if you want want a (mosty) chill game to relax to, potentially the only game I've had fun grinding enemies in. I beat 100% completed the story mode, time to try out arcade! Awesome game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fine game in terms of combat and RPG mechanics, lots of build choices. Although one can be done with his build very early on which is fine for people that want simpler builds. I like the humour of the game, it is kind of ridiculous at times and making fun of some tropes and it does so well in my opinion.

The OST doesn't bother you but with the exception of one standout track for me there really isn't much there. The story starts out weak, develops into something stronger and finishes up quite nicely. Nothing too out there but good enough. The requirements for the true ending are kinda though, the card farming mechanic wastes a lot of time because of how the RNG works. The other requirement forces you to do a very annoying timing puzzle, which from what I have heard can be very rage inducing. Combat difficulty on the bosses can be tough, especially some of the latter bosses may require several tries.

I have completed story mode, no plans for 100% as there are some achievements I don't really want to go for and am content with the game so far. I will try out Arcade Mode next, the town building mechanic with rogue like might end up pretty well, especially with the aforementioned build variety and combat feeling.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game itself is a pretty run of the mill beat-em-up. If you play melee, you'll have a good time. If you play magic, and the game has a whole third of its tree options made for magic, you will be statistically disappointed. Do not expect to be a wizard. At best smacky guy with the occasional spell.

The story is good enough. I like how it builds up the historical and the modern for a collision course. It also has some good heartstring moments.

My negative review comes from the final "true ending" boss on hard mode. Six years of Early Access culminate into a mistake. Beating this boss requires you to specialize your build fine-tuned specifically to him. Something leagues beyond any previous encounter. His attack combos are RNG. Some runs are just ridiculously cleaner than others because of this. Rather than introduce new problematic mechanics, the boss strips you of two concepts you've had for the entire game. I get this is meant to be a power play, but it makes the fight too foreign to the rest of the game. His alternation on attack windows and amount of moves he does in a row moved things towards luck based combat. You end up statting this yourself with a 10% chance to just not get hurt to beat him.

His moves are problematic. Certain combos doom you to the fire with knockback. You find yourselves attacked at awkward diagonal angles. Missiles in a stream that you are deflecting occasionaly hit you anyway. You can position yourself to be in the most favourable spot, but ultimately you will simply get crapped on time to time. What was a clear attack window in one run becomes a surprise continued spam in another.

When we won, it wasn't a rejoicing moment. There was full acknowledgement of things going our way at several moments in the process. We were glad to be done with the massive difficulty spike build soley out of flooding the map. Particle effect combos also become an issue here, with your character simply disappearing in the chaos.

The final boss does not align itself with the rest of the game. There's standing out, and then there's outright being different in principal. You've got a fun short story ahead of you on normal or non-true ending, but if you got for Hard True ending, expect to play a completely different game for the last 0.1%.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game.

I am a fan of MMORPGs and this game does make me feel like I'm playing one. In fact, I think it took inspiration from Ragnarok Online in some aspects, especially the card system (each monster can drop a card which gives passive bonus).

There are lots of customization and the combat is fun. Also, there are lots of bosses and they are mostly unique, with distinct patterns between them.

The story is surprisingly good. It also satirizes lots of common game tropes and I felt the humor was spot on.

Overall a super fun game in almost all aspects but one. There's something that I hated so much that if it was not optional, I would give a "not recommended" instead.

Well, the name of the game makes it clear that there is grind in it. That is not a problem, I like to grind. But the game also has two features that, when combined, make the grinding extremely tedious:

- Monsters drop money, items and a card (only once for each monster, so after getting one, you can't get any more of the same). The drop rate of the cards is abysmally low, almost like a real MMORPG. Since this is not a multiplayer game, I think the cards could have a better drop rate, as I legit spent 40min+ trying to get a single one before I gave up. But that alone is not a problem.

- There is some sort of soft level cap. Each level makes your next level harder to get by (what seems to be) an exponential amount. That means that you cannot really grind your levels and become super powerful, and you are not able to realistically max your skill trees either. That alone is not a problem either (although that defeats the point of grinding, but I digress).

Now, when you have the two problems above together, it becomes a problem. First, you have cards with extremely low drop rates in a single player game. You want to grind them, so you spend minutes (maybe hours) trying to get each one of them. Since the other items and money have a better drop rate, you'll get lots of those before you get your card, so items and money are not a factor in this game. At the same time, since there's a soft level cap, you won't get levels either.

In other words, you can spend an hour killing hundreds/thousands of the same enemy until you get a card, but that's all that you will get, since the money, items and exp you'll get at the same time will be meaningless/negligible.

Grinding is fun, but the reward is not really worth it. The least we could get for such grind should be exp/levels.

Anyway, that part of the game ruined the grinding experience for me, but at the same time, it's entirely optional, so I decided to just ignore it, and enjoy everything else the game has to offer :)\t\t\t\t\t
