Animation is solid, the sprites are extremely charming.

Gameplay is simple, fun, easy to understand.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile still in Alpha, Secrets of Grindea is an *exceptionally* good game, and one of the best indie pixel RPGs I ever played. It not just meets, but exceeds, expectations of a great indie RPG.

The graphics, while technically simple (as with many other pixel RPGs), are artistically beautiful, with lots of cool effects, animations, artwork, and entire biomes that feel alive. The story is engaging and plausible. The mechanics and controls are fluid and feel just right. The pacing is good, and there is lots of content even for an Alpha version. The boss fights are challenging, but not unreasonably so. There are multiple viable builds which present significantly different play styles, yet no one that's too powerful to make the others useless. Some mechanics are uncommon in simpler RPGs, such as the fireball mage build, which works with delayed artillery-style top-down bombardment, requiring the player to carefully plan where the enemy will be when the shot lands, all while evading melee rushers, rather than just spam the fire button. Finally, it has multiplayer as well, although I didn't play it personally.

On top of the main storyline, which easily justifies buying the game on its own, there is the infinitely replayable Roguelike mode, which is very fun (if sometimes aggravating after a death), and present a great case of taking the same builds, items, and mechanics, but through different environmental rules, requiring the player to come up with completely different tactics to stay alive and progress.

There are only two reasons I see to not buy Secrets of Grindea right now:

1. You don't like real-time pixel RPGs. This game isn't turn based and requires quick movement and thinking.
2. You don't want to get really engaged in the game, only to be stopped halfway because this is an ALPHA and half the game isn't complete. This isn't a beta with just some polishing missing, it actually misses half the storyline and locations, and the main quest abruptly comes to a halt. If you like playing a great game from beginning to end, wait for the release version.\t\t\t\t\t
