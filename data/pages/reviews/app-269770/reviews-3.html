\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had this game at the top of my wishlist for over a decade. I won't claim it was worth the wait but it's remarkably good. There's fun combat, quality pixel art and impressive animation. For the price its pretty long with a lot to do with a lot of different skills, upgrades, equipment and so on. The game's music does the job well and fits the 16-bit graphical style. The story is also surprisingly good and has some emotional moments.

One of my favorite aspects is that you can map 4 actions or abilities or items to the face buttons but then map 4 more for when you hold left trigger and 4 more for holding right trigger. So you can you have multiple skills/magic/items right at your finger tips in real-time instead of using the overrated weapon-wheel that so many games use now.

One of my only complaints is how much grinding you have to do to get the "true ending" but at least it was a good ending.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tentatively recommend this game because it seems the Secrets of Grindea developers put a lot of time and effort which I wholeheartedly acknowledge and respect. The early game has giant charm and the grind feels very rewarding. I found myself a very therapeutic experience from a stressful semester at school, and would come back daily to chip away at this game to progress further.

Now how does one "get better" at a game like this?

The game rewards you for grinding (obviously) and when accomplishing achievements, they're generous in giving items that contributes to either cosmetics or equipment that boosts your stats. Often times in the early game I found that they supported having a wide range of builds to do whatever I pleased.

But I specify EARLY GAME. Late game is a gigantic slog. I found myself spending time repetitively and mindlessly killing enemies that got tankier over time that would chunk me if I wasn't careful, so I had to switch to a Summoner build to do small DPS while running away. Sometimes, even forcing me to Perfect Guard as I grinded, which did not give me a peaceful time slaying the same enemies over and over for a single Monster Card that would boost my overall stats. And these cards are essential, which made me feel as if I needed to do more when I had already done so much towards progressing myself. It's when accomplishing these feats suddenly feel burdening that it makes me feel like quitting. I did not, however. I spent money on this game full price and still don't regret it.

There are also puzzles within the game that are quite fun, but specifically there was one reoccuring type of puzzle Phase Shifting that actually became insanely difficult due to its demanding real-time inputs that I would have to time. I am no OSU player or rhythm gamer, and I admit I needed to use a Youtube walkthrough to even get through it. But I wish I didn't have to!

Now, as someone who beat the game's Normal Ending (I am currently attempting to 100%, might not though) this is tough stuff. I played Enter the Gungeon with an almost masochistic fashion and I barely manage most bullet hells. When I move around in Grindea, they encourage the features of Perfect Guard. I'm fine with it being a feature, but I feel as if pulling out the shield is quite sluggish and it doesn't give you directional defense. If someone hits your blind spot, you can consider yourself hit. Realism fits, but in an action combat game, it is quite frustrating.

But despite all those sharp edges, I feel oddly accomplished because I was able to get through it. Which puts me in an odd scenario where I can't imagine recommending this game to my friends, but for anyone who wants an RPG that leaves people scratching their heads at times and getting through where they can, should get this game to feel accomplished. It will deliver on that end, I just wish the game could land this concept better. But maybe that's what sequels are for. I would get the sequel of this game if they did too. It still had more rewarding content than the average game, which appealed me. I guess I desire in games like these to overlevel until I feel confident enough to blitz through the final story content, but I just felt uncomfortable due to how lacking in power I felt in comparison.

So TLDR; get this game if you're good at RPGs, casuals run unless you've got a friend to play with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has character. It is a charming, retro and most importantly co-op JRPG. A unique experience like no other. But there are also flaws.

+ The world and all its characters are so charming i can't even describe it. Naniva was awesome, bag is great, Luke is good too. There are so many interesting, sometimes cliched characters its ridiculous. Writing here is the best thing about the game by far.
+ The humour is also very good. I loved how it connects to real world and various game tropes.
+ Graphics, animations and the OST are all very well made. Everything is very detailed.
+ Fitting for a JRPG the amount of dialogue and general things to do is astonishing. I really enjoyed all the tiny little details and unmarked quests and such.
+ Some very interesting gameplay mechanics including switching seasons, being able to see dead or manipulate time completely change and freshens up the older areas. It opens up the old maps for even more exploration to do.
+ Co op experience works great. This is the only JRPG that can be played like this and its great.
+ Quests are great and varied. It never gets stale.
+ Boss mechanics and variety is pretty good.
+ For JRPG the UI and quality of life features are amazing. Because of this the game feels smooth. No hickups.
+ Grinding is nowhere near as bad as i thought it would be. It is actually kinda seamless and easy to do.

- I wish there was a local map. Some areas are a bit hard to navigate.
- The last boss battle is absolutely ridiculous. The entire game feels alright when it comes to difficulty but the last fight is crazy hard i almost gave up and that was on normal difficulty! This is by far the biggest issue with the game in my opinion. I had to lookup how to beat the boss online.
- Quest log could be a bit more detailed on how to actually progress.
- During some boss fights the visual clutter is pretty high and its difficult to dodge everything.

Overall this is a good game held back a bit by its ridiculously hard final fight. I would still recommend playing it though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely excellent. Properly difficult at times, prompting you to respec, shuffle things around and try new strategies as well as grind for rewards and levels. All the grinding is super satisfying and doesn't really take that long if you know how enemy spawns work, and the loading screen tips tell you that early on. Puzzles range from super simple seen it before, to downright sadistic and mental, making them also super satisfying and rewarding to figure out.
The biggest surprise was how much I ended up liking all of the little character arcs as well as some good setups and payoffs. I don't really care about story and characters in games but it's a huge bonus when a mechanical masterpiece manages to have a satisfying ending and Secrets of Grindea pulled it off.
This was obviously a labor of love by people passionate and enthusiastic about games and I'm so glad I tried it and played the thing to death. I'm not quite obsessive enough 100% it but I found that under 35 hours is apparently enough time to get enough of the pieces to beat the game with the true ending, and I'd be lying if I said I didn't want more. I'll be looking forward to see what these guys do next, but honestly it's awesome to find another RPG love letter game that sits easily up there with titles like Crystal Project, Chained Echoes and Sea of Stars.\t\t\t\t\t
