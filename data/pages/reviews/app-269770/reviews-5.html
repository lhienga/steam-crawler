\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game had been on my radar since I first saw in Steam Greenlight. Was super excited to finally play it after it finally released. Game turned out to be not fun in the slightest.

Game starts off fun enough, and then around the midway point of the story the game deviated from a fun casual rpg to bosses that are bullet hells that require precise movement which the game doesn't offer. The art style is cute and animated, but the animation and effects constantly get in the way. I constantly lost where my character even was on the screen. So many times I would be moving left or right and throw up my shield to block, only for my character to face up or town while still moving to the side. That killed me so many times. Bosses are damage sponges and take several minutes to get through even with perfect guards, which the game requires you to get good at. Had several constant bugs throughout my entire story playthrough. I can consistently dash through walls and go out of bounds, which lets you walk through walls. At one point I walked through a mine cart you have to push to progress and create a shortcut without me realizing and made traveling through one area a complete chore.

Arcade mode was the absolute worst part of the game. I never felt like I got good at the game but just ended up getting lucky with rng. If you can't perfect guard, there's no point in even trying this mode. And even if you are good at perfect guarding, it feels so unreliable. Like I said earlier, sometimes my character would just change directions and I miss a block, and end up getting killed at full health because four enemies attack at once killing my hour I spent in a run. Around Temple of Seasons bosses felt like their HP scaling started to get too high. Everything is just damage sponges and you're nothing but a wet paper bag filled with rocks. There were some rooms you exit south through into a potion shop that's covering the door trapping you. A lot of times a spiked vine or icicle will spawn under you when you walk into a room and you just take damage. Everything about it is way too punishing for how little payoff you receive for beating it.

The only positive thing about the game is the dungeon and puzzle design. Some of the puzzles were genuinely fun to figure out. But like everything else in the game, the closer you get to the end, the more tedious and unfun everything became.

I don't understand who this game was actually made for, nor do I understand how this game took 13 years to develop.\t\t\t\t\t
