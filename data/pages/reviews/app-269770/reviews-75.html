\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: Grindea is basically a finished product now, and thus my review is a bit dated. I'll leave it at the bottom of my post for posterity, but my updated review is as follows:

This is an example of a nearly perfect game. It might not be everyone's cup of tea, but wow does it do everything it sets out to do, and does it well, while leaving a lot of ways to play it your way.

The lore is unique in the way that the world's gamey RPG mechanics are baked into the very core of the setting, which culminates in a very interesting and dramatic way as the story progresses.

One of my favorite things is that the title is not lying: Grinding may not be necessary, but there is no level cap, and there are ways to get effectively infinite stats as long as you meet one of two criteria:

1) don't have a life.
2) are willing to set up your build to AFK farm.

Reason being, even though eventually enemies grant only 1 XP, you will never need more than 10,000 XP to level up. Just kill 10,000 things and you WILL level up. Glorious. I feel seen.
Now they just need to give me a way to increase my movement speed.


Old review for aforementioned posterity:

Grindea is a great game in the making, and well worth the wait. I don't begrudge them the time spent creating the game, and the updates that come out are exceedingly polished.
Whether you're looking for a roguelike, a good story, a grindfest, or a well-done action RPG (that doesn't actually require any grinding!) this game does it all.

Though as you can probably tell, the community is lightly salted about the lengthy development time.
This game has a fully-fledged roguelike mode that is pretty difficult, but again, well polished and basically a complete game in and of itself, to hold you over while they finish the story.

What's currently there for the story is also pretty far along, though naturally it halts at some point. Overall the game has exceeded my expectations on most every front, and is a great game to play with friends or on your own, and again, the Arcade Mode is great all by itself.

I WOULD like a way to increase movement speed, if even a little bit, however. I get the feeling that's a design decision, but Grrr I want my guy to be fast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is deserving of a good rep, and I'm going to tell you why.

1. The characters are lively and enjoyable! I've found myself snickering at many a dialogue (I'm looking at you, Bag) and actually reading said dialogue rather than skipping through.
2. The Devs DO WEEKLY UPDATES on their work. No, I don't mean to say the game is patched weekly, but if you go to the Dev Blog on their website you can see a WEEK BY WEEK release of their progress (what they're working on, beautiful art coming to life, etc.). It's also important to remember that the Devs (Pixel Ferrets) are THREE people.
3. The story is interesting, engaging, yet not smothering. The story is interesting so far (and definitely becomes more so the later you get, but spoilers), but not so overbearing that it leaves you unable to roleplay with your friends and add your own imagined plotline if that's something you enjoy.
4. PUZZLES. Holy ****, the puzzles. I've always been one to enjoy puzzles in a game and this one does everything right. The game features numerous different types of puzzles, as well as varying difficulties--including optional, high difficulty puzzles that I enjoyed immensely.
5. Combat. The combat is decent, with a "perfect guarding" mechanic and a few different playstyles. Respeccing is easy, though I will say toward the end of the current version of the game I believe higher stat gear would be beneficial.

All in all, I LOVED this game. I still love this game. I don't mind taking the time for a good, quality game to come to life rather than demand something of lesser quality quickly. Pixel Ferrets says on their homepage that Secrets of Grindea is currently pre-access, and openly discourage people from buying if what they want is a fully completed game NOW. If you like taking your time to enjoy your games as an experience, and appreciate detail, quality, and liveliness in your games--this is for you. However, if what you desire is playing through your games quickly and moving on to the next--I'm going to have to agree with Pixel Ferrets and recommend skipping this one for now.\t\t\t\t\t
