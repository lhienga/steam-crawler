\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game would've been fine if it was made a decade ago. But the gaming landscape has greatly changed ever since. Independent developers have made amazing strides and keep making great video games left and right. And I'm afraid in modern day a game such as this doesn't make the cut anymore, especially considering how long it took to make.

If Secrets of Grindea has been fully released in it's current state when it first entered early access it would've been considered a good video game. Not amazing, not great, but definitely good. But now? In 2024? It is well below par in every aspect:

- Game design is basic and feels extremely old, flash game level of old. 2010 is calling guys! We've had infinitely superior top down RPG/Adventure games since. Well, from any genre really. This game feels like a museum exhibit.

- Considering how long the game has been in development and how long the devs had people who supported them wait for the full release, it has very little content.

- Production values are no longer impressive, they are barely passable. A game that looks and sounds like this would've been nice back when it first went int early access maybe, but nowadays you have competition that makes this look like some kind of old school PS1/2 era game. Even your average, run of the mill indie devs can nowadays do better in this regard.

- And on the technical level the game is a travesty. Guys, this is 2024, how does your game not support resolutions higher than 1080p? Heck, you don't even have any proper scaling for your pixel art so it can look sharp at higher resolutions. You know, BASIC stuff that is an absolute standard for a game like this in modern day? Instead, the whole game is a blurry mess, like I travelled 20 years back in time to watch a 480p Youtube Let's Play. This doesn't even qualify as an issue, this is quite simply not acceptable anymore. I want to remind you this is a product that costs money, not some freeware student project. And I don't even want to start pointing out what kind of games you can buy for 10$ instead and how they stack up to this one because I would be here literally the whole day...

Here's who I recommend this game to: people who are feeling nostalgic for simple games from the past, who have literally nothing better to play and have somehow exhausted all the other better options, of which there are hundreds upon hundreds in the RPG genre alone. To anybody else: it's not worth your money. I will commend the devs for including the demo, which I recommend you try out before you buy the game, it will give you an accurate representation of what you're getting into.

I had high hopes for this game when I first saw it a long, long time ago. But if this is what 9 years of development have amounted to, then I cannot call this anything else but an utter failure.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDevs don't like when you criticize their game that was released before it was ready...

This game was released in Early Access in 2015 and this is a review from only "Hard" mode playthroughs.

The game is still in Early Access despite the "1.0 Launch" news.

The achievements are still broken, namely the rarest ones, which the developer has not fixed in nearly 10 years, and you can check the percentages and developer forums if you doubt me.

Example: I've brought Mesido all the way to the true ending in under 3 1/2 hours, twice, and still have not unlocked achievements for, either, "Speedrunner" or "Sightseeing".

Certain boss attacks still have a larger hit area than the the size of the on-screen visual representation, most notably, Winter's ice vein attack and Vilya's basic attack with her dual-blade. Both of these hit the player character when out of the visual range of the on-screen animation of the attacks. This isn't much of an issue in Normal mode where the player health and damage are boosted and the bosses are weaker.

The story stands at a 6/10 with no noteworthy impressions or moments that validate the time spent reading the dialogue of the campaign.

In the end, if you're a gamer who enjoys a challenge and the rewards and sense of accomplishment from, this game is not what you're looking for.

The combat is unbalanced and poorly implemented, the achievements are still broken, and after 13+ years in development, the developers continue to ignore player feedback, post release, and are virtually non-existent, save for the premature announcement of 1.0.

This is a pass until the patch notes address the issues I've stated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe visuals and mechanics of the game are solid. The sound effects are nicely done.

Unfortunately, it is a highly repetitive game. Each area is essentially a re-skin of the previous with no variation in how you clear them. A fine example is the fishing mini-game, as it is the exact same across every area. The number of fish to be caught and the difficulty of catching them does not change through-out the entire game. Another would be the enemy sets, each area has the same number, each following the same formula. A couple of melee, a couple of casters and maybe a singular larger enemy in a room.

The other issue is how much hand-holding is done by the game itself. You are shuffled through the game area by area, not allowed to explore ahead of the main story, often with large arrows pointing the way. A problem this creates is a lack of difficulty curve in the game. By the time you are allowed to access a new area you are sufficiently leveled and geared that it isn't any more difficult than the previous. You can backtrack to previous areas instantly but there is little reason to do so as the areas are easily cleared on the initial pass; some exceptions to treasures.

If I had to describe the game in one word it would be "Limited." It is a nice nod to games like the earlier Zelda series, just without the sense of exploration.\t\t\t\t\t
