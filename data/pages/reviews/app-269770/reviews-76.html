\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't buy until the game is finished!

The game's classic combat and charm is oozing in all regards, but the story is a major component of this game! The story has great build up (no zone is too long) and zones have incredible story-density, but you are going to be left hanging (Aug 2018) when the unfinished zone is your next spot.

Building characters is fun, with skill choices roughly on par with a single class of Diablo2 (but not even close on the equipment!) and the rouge-like arcade mode makes trying out the different combinations faster and high-stakes all the while giving you grindy goals to achieve as you hone your skill. Very nice addition, if a touch repetitive.
Playing much arcade exposed two things to me:
1. the skill cap is HIGH in combat. You can learn each monster and get better and better timing.
2. Despite the high skill cap, combat can grow stale -- which is FINE, because you should be playing the story! wait for it to finish and enjoy this game when its done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a solid game, and I'm giving it an 8/10. As the devs have added on content, the story has become better and better. The gameplay is pretty solid for an action RPG with tons of variety in playstyles. The arcade mode is also a nice touch, essentially bundling a rogue-lite onto the game's story mode. There's a solid amount of value here, and anyone that enjoys RPG's will almost certainly enjoy this game.

The biggest positives for this game are the puzzles, characters, self aware humor, and the combat. The biggest downside is the side quests are often times monotonous and boring. It has a pretty generic talent point system but you you can place your tps wherever you want essentially enabling you to create your own class . Want to be a spell casting melee beast? Go for it. 10/10 - My personal enjoyment

This game is very enjoyable, a good sense of progression, lots of fun to play with friends, cute and sweet soundtrack to fit the atmosphere. The character customazation is good but definetly could be expanded more. The patch that brought in the house feature was a blast, and hopefully the expiriment with that a bit more. The gameplay is fun and never underwhelming, constantly introducing new features. Combat is flexible and easy to understand, allowing the player decide their fight style, if they want to go support, damage, or a mix of both, etc. Boss fights are sometimes frustrating and unbalanced but its fine to have a challange every once in a while. Unfortunately there are some major downsides to this game, the biggest thing is the game being unfinished and the devs are very slow in updating but, that's ok. Games take time. The devs are very promising in the future mechanics of this game so its only a matter of time. One nit-picky problem I have with this game are the characters. Dialouge at times seems very stale and not realistic at all, and by that I mean, conversations and motive don't seem authentic enough even if the game is taking a friendlier approach. Townspeople could be more inviting and talkative. The art style in the game is very cute and overall very nice. No place ever feels the same or too alike, every area has its own original aspects that gives the players a need to explore all over. Overall this game is a blast to play with a friend or two, you will enjoy the art, the warm, welcoming soundtrack and diverse combat system. I would recommend even if the game is unfinished because it makes it all the more exciting for the finished product to come!\t\t\t\t\t
