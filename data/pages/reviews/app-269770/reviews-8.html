\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGrindea is not quite what I expected. Going in you'd think it'd be a parody game full of cheap references and lampshades to hang, and while it does have some of those, the bulk of the writing is actually admirably original, with a setting that's fleshed out and which has a few endearing characters and competently executed foreshadowing and payoffs with a solid ending.

The combat is solid, but the game's real standout is its boss fights, which are often challenging, unique, and engaging with a wonderful sense of back and forth between the player and the bosses thanks to the guard and skill charge mechanics. There's some great music in the game supporting these moments too.

What isn't so good is the grind and the balance. Much like Crosscode, you can spend hours grinding for drops to craft items that aren't even remotely worth the time investment. In addition, getting the game's true ending requires you to get every card in the game, a special item unique to every enemy type, and which has an extremely low drop rate. You can easily do what I did and get through the entire main story with no grinding at all, only to then get to the ending and have nothing left to do but fill out a checklist of missing items, which ended up accounting for at least a third of my playtime here. Sure, getting cards throughout the game is nice when it happens, but when you need to get all of them, their drop rates being so low is criminal and amounts to doing nothing but killing the same mobs over and over again for hours at a time depending on your luck. I was not lucky in this game.
I must also say that for how long the game was in development for I did expect it to be longer than it is.

The roguelite mode has already annoyed me with only letting the player accept one quest per run for no reason, and while the boss fights are good, for a roguelite I'd rather be playing something with a particularly robust combat system like BlazBlue Entropy Effect instead. Maybe it'd be better in multiplayer, but I haven't tried that.

tl;dr surprisingly decent story, some great boss fights, dragged down by obnoxious grind to get to the true ending and for various final quests.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is pretty solid from start to finish, with a variety of ways to handle combat situations that you get into. However the only real complaint one may have is the cards, there a few instances where it feels like you will grind these out for a long time in the hopes of getting one. There is a prevention measure to make sure if you unlucky it will eventually drop (not mentioned in game too well) but it may still take some time to kick in if you are very unlucky or the spawn rate for the particular enemy you looking for is really low.

The puzzles are a bit on the tricky side and aren't exactly easy (tho not too hard either) so if you not a fan of puzzles in RPGs, this might not be for you as there are some precision/reflex puzzles involved.

The game can last you quite a long while however, since there is two modes, the story mode which will take you about roughly 15-30 hours depending on how much you want to do. The arcade mode however could take another 10-30 hours but alas I haven't gotten around to finish it so I wouldn't know. But regardless there is some value for the price tag offered.

If you were a huge fan of old school feeling days like snes-like, you may like this as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you recommend this game: Maybe

A lot of other, better games are referenced within this game, some oldies like Chrono Trigger, but also recent games that came out during the early access, like CrossCode. At heart however, Secrets of Grindea is a passion project made by people that loved the original Ragnarok Online. Sound design, locations, equipment, characters and even story, it is hard to take a step in any direction and not think: 'wait, this is familiar'. At one hand this is fine, since the source material is very good, but on the other, it is a very tough benchmark, which was not met.

There are a lot of issues, most of them related to the length of production time, but some are just bad design choices. The art quality varies between portraits and locations, some of it is in a desperate need of remastering, especially the early content. A ton of side content that is pure vanity should have been moved to end-game, it was clearly there to reward backers and early adopters, but does not go well at all with the main content. Some boss fights are in a desperate need of redesign, 3rd section of the mimic chest boss fight is a great example of way too much happening at the same time, you will miss 90% of what's happening on the screen and won't notice why you won or lost. No mouse support, not even in menus, there is absolutely no excuse, not in 2024.

But, there are also bright spots shining through, the whole seasons system in Fae palace was neat, the time mechanics of the hidden city were a joy to play through and the freedom of the skill system was also nice, albeit the flat % increases were not.

All in all, considering the price of admission it is 'okay' enough, I personally wasn't enticed enough however to finish it, which is why this is a negative review with a 'Maybe' recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give a medium thumb here.
First of all good parts
-beautiful art
-cool worldbuilding
-cool music
-huge crafting and quest variety

Bad parts
- most bosses are just not fun, granted I played on hard mode through 2/3rds of the game but I had many bosses where I should have been way above the level requirenment since I collected all cards before doing a boss and I still couldn't get close to beating them without looking up a guide or going down to normal mode. I actually enjoined myself the most with the elite versions of mobs that had their own boss arena. It just felt fair.

-secrets of GRINDea is too grindy for me... I know I get it it's in the name but as someone who got all the cards and 2/3 of the secret ending requirenments I can comfortably say that grinding feels just tedious and takes too long. I think if you tripled the base drop rate of all items and cards it might be cool again (I was using the loot drop potion too)

- Some parts of quests are just unnecessarily vague. Like for example I was supposed to take a pic of the haunted forest ghost and no matter what I did even with the death vision I couldn't get the "right" photo. Or in Port Monai there is a quest where you need to lure a lost cat with fish. I had the fish, I found the cat but found no way to interact with said cat.

To be honest in terms of my enjoyment I would give this game a thumbs down but this is a beautiful indie project and I want to support the work that goes into it and hope that the creators make another game with some better balancing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe start of this game is quite good, if a bit simple. For a lot of the game that was my main issue, so I was having fun. There was plenty of references to games that I - and clearly the creators - love.

Pretty much all my issues come in the last 1/4 of the game and in the optional content. The puzzles/optional content feels like it doesn't value your time at all and includes elements of RNG where it just doesn't make sense to. The bosses towards the end become more and more bullet-hell, which is completely not the game I even signed up for.

Normally I'd finish a game (especially an RPG) to the True End just for the challenge. I put Secrets of Grindea down after the Normal Ending because it's just not fun. I don't know if I've enjoyed any of the last 5 hours of the game (or the last 8 hours if you include me just idling the game to wait for some plants to grow for optional content - complete waste of player's time as they've already got the rare seed drops).

I can only really say buy this game if you enjoy bad games. The early portions of the game are fairly enjoyable, but not better than anything the game is inspired by. The later portions feel like an issue created by the game being in Early Access for as long as it had. Artistic visions changed, I assume it was balanced for players who had been playing it for the whole of the last 10 years.

I'd normally never leave a negative review on a game out of respect for the people who put time and effort into making it, but I feel like they had little respect for the time I put into playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t
