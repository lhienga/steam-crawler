First off, I want to talk about the development time and the game being in early access, because that seems to be the elephant in the room.

I want to say that even if the game never fully releases, it already has more than enough content to be worth it's 15$. I strongly believe the reviews on this game would be astonishing if people didn't leave bad reviews due to the development speed. Every negative review I have seen so far didn't recommend it due to the development time. Go ahead, check yourself. (Filter review < negative reviews.)

As for when the game is going to actually be finished, my prediction is it coming out within the next 2-3 years. Pixel Ferrets are currently working on the final dungeon at the time of posting this review, and are also working on the last 3 arcade mode dungeon counterparts.

The gameplay itself:
The animation in SoG is very smooth. The different skills, talents, weapons, armor, (and more) give you the ability to fully customize your character. This gives the game reason to play through multiple times, trying out new builds and finding new strategies to perform better. The story is good, the characters are charming and funny. If the EA tag STILL puts you down, Pixel Ferrets has made mailing list ( https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSc95c-IinIqx6p9cVRPI7PiSgmhw-3-OBj13HC9xwi6Q9qtMw/viewform ) to send a email to you when the game is in full release.

I would describe my experience as a super fun car ride that is abruptly stopped due to a big ass wall blocking the road ahead and every couple months the wall is moved back an inch allowing you another inch of progress. If that sounds appealing, buy this game.

I do not think the positive reviews adequately take into account how ridiculous it is that the game has not been finished since 2015 (4 years and counting?!) and now only releases bare bones of updates. If they released updates that actually had some meat on them, I would not be as upset about having to wait so long for them. I also do not understand why so many people think that the updates on their blog are an adequate substitute for actual playable game content.

Again, the game is well made but it is unfinished and I do not think it will be finished before I die of old age. If you do buy it, do not expect a full game and I would recommend getting it on sale if you really want to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is being designed by 3 individuals with a grand vision. These last 4 years have gone by slowly but I am not the least bit worried. This is one Early Access game that I feel I don't have to worry about if its going to be finished or not. If you are considering buying this game I would recommend it. Some things you should know before you buy:

This game is not abandoned like a lot of the negative reviews say. These were written by idiots and whiners who can not read or research or who don't have the patience for a good quality game. Updates do take awhile to come out yes. If you follow the dev blog you will see that they constantly release information about what they are working on usually weekly.

http://www.secretsofgrindea.com/index.php/dev-blog

This game has a unique engine. It is not unity or any other commercial engine. The Developers built it themselves from scratch which is again impressive.

The story is not complete but has come a long way since it started and a good bit of it is finished.
You will see a few bugs within the completed content but nothing game breaking in my experience. With that said the game is very playable. Its challenging and I feel like it has just the right mix of combat, story, and puzzles.
The combat is much like the SNES version of Legend of Zelda accept at a faster pace and you need to think about what you are dong more. You gain levels and get stat points you can then use to purchase skills right from the character window.

CO-OP...... I really love this part of it. I have 3 kids between the ages of 12 and 16. When I saw this game I purchased it for myself and loved it and found out it had this option. I then purchased 3 more copies for my kids. Once its fully released we plan to run through the entire game together. My kids are constantly playing arcade mode together and have ran through story mode several times as well.

All in all if your wanting something fun to play solo or with friends Secrets of Grindea has a lot to offer now and in the future. You can tell how much the Devs care about this game and it shows in the work they are putting into it. I think they are asking too little for it. $14.99 is a steal. I am assuming that after its fully release it will go up quite a bit and will still be well worth it. If you have not bought it yet you should do it now while the price is down.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSecrets of Grindea is very fun to play - I binged through the available quests and story in about 2-3 days (18 hours). The controls are tight and the combat mechanics are well balanced. There are a variety of builds available and I had a lot of fun re-speccing on the fly whenever I got bored of one build.

I didn't have the opportunity to try the mutliplayer features, but single player was great. Once I finished most of the story content, I played a bit of Arcade Mode, which is more of a rogue-like experience.

However, it must be said that development of the game seems to have slowed down significantly. There haven't been any major content additions in 2019 so far and that's worrying. It's not a complete game, far from it from the looks of it, and anyone looking to buy should be aware of its early access state.\t\t\t\t\t
