A mixture of really fun RPG with really frustrating elements

To start, I do recommend this game, however it's not without its qualities that I absolutely dislike.

That's not to say they're faults of the game, just that they make me angry enough to need a break.

There are so many things about this game done right, the artwork is well done, the sound and music is pretty spot on, and the story is decent so far.
I enjoy the combat and the leveling+skill system all feels pretty good, despite there still being some unfinished skills.

I also really like the items and collecting in the game, what with unique drops, elite monsters, etc.

About the only thing that turns me off about the game is that some of the boss battles and puzzles can drag the experience on, artificially inflating actual play time.

I fought the first real boss, a giant robot, and kept dying, trying to take it down in its last stage. What I didn't know, without checking the guide, is that it will die automatically, after its last stage, so you basically just have to avoid its bullets for a little while, till it dies. I'm not a fan of having to look at a guide online, just because the game doesn't indicate its mechanics clearly enough.

A character could easily say something like "It's imploding on itself, just stay out of its way, and we're almost there!" or something. There was no indication of what I should do, and this, frankly, is what happens quite a bit in the game. You're supposed to just "know" something completely random in order to progress.

Another gripe I have is that the difficulty spikes a lot, in various different places. You'll be feeling pretty proud of yourself for beating a boss, or grinding up a dozen levels. You'll be killing everything without issue, feeling like a bad@$$, and then suddenly your face gets smashed in by a boss that seems to be dozens of levels too high for you.

This will happen a lot, which left me feeling like "am I going to have a good time, or am I suddenly going to face a challenge that frustrates me for two hours?"

Also on my list of annoyances are the puzzles. I spent two hours trying to solve a single puzzle last night. In the end, I caved and looked at a guide. The solution rather pissed me off, because it was the ONLY solution involving moving around 12 pieces. So, a puzzle that involves 12 pieces on a grid of let's say 40X80, to find the exact right way to place each piece, this wasn't fun. The room design for that puzzle also wasn't at all suggestive of a solution, which is something most good games with puzzles do, suggest a solution through the level design.

You'd think with all my complaining, I'd not recommend this game, and in fact you'd be wrong.

Regardless of its downfalls, things that I personally don't like, it's a really good game. As I mentioned above, the leveling is good (super grindy, but what did you expect from Secrets of Grindea), the skill system, the art style, the music, the item collection, crafting, story...

All of it is done quite well.

If you're into retro-styled RPGs, with very definite nods to the past, you'll probably like this one, even if it does make you feel stupid, from time to time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played this game for 80+ hours, with 5+ different save slots used, each one using a different offensive strategy to play the game with. I have done 1-handed, 2-handed, magic of various combinations and all of them are very enjoyable. That being said, I have enjoyed this game so much all throughout each one of my runs.

The story is phenomenal and I keep noticing new aspects of the story that I didn't before. All of the boss fights are great. Each boss fight has its own mechanic that makes each one unique and fun. There are also a ton of options for character customizations: hairstyles; hair, skin, scarf, shirt, pants, and shoe color.

As with most early access games, there are some bugs and glitches, but the developers are very active and will fix the bugs/glitches that are pointed out to them on the forums.

There is also multiplayer with up to 4 people total. In my experience with trying to use this, it did not work. I could not play local multiplayer with my friends, and when we tried doing online multiplayer using each of our accounts and hooking up via Steam, it did not recognize that I had any friends with the game. It would be nice if that got fixed.

There are people that complain about how grindy the game is, but honestly, there is no need to grind, but if you do grind, the art style is nice to look at while grinding. A lot of the monsters are really cool.

Overall, the developers really care about this game and put a lot of effort into making this a quality game. Definitely worth every bit of the $15 they are asking for. Solid 9.5/10.comes out.\t\t\t\t\t

STANDARD ISSUE JRPG STORY?

BOSSY!

The music was fair.
The art is really high quality.
Controls are fluid.
My butt is acid.
erm...

What else am I forgetting?

Good games create the challenge around the tools it hands to you, not by giving you good tools and then breaking them.

This creates a blind-patch that boosted my crit by 25. That and a dmg-potion and he can be killed after two of his "i just wait 30s until you pass through my ice-maze that totally abuses the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ movement to damage you"-phases. Did i mention that diagonal movment isnt normalized? so you move Root of 2 faster when moving diagonally.

There are Worms that create Area Denial patches everywhere that must be outwaited in order to take no dmg. Mushrooms create blinding fields if you vanquish them. Shell Monsters do a random charge-bounce thing that is so fast that it WILL hit you randomly... All this artificial crippling is shoved into your face one last time with the fight vs the Power-Flower in order to remind you why you hate this games combat.
