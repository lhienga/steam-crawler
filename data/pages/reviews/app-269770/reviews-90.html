\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is beautiful will be the first thing I will say. The pixel art style is usually done in a blocky way now a days, however this game seems smooth with the style. If you couldn't notice from the title this game can be extremely grindy, but you don't have to spend all your time grinding away trash mobs if you don't want to. The story is predictable, but has arcs in it that feel like it hasn't happen in any other game I have played recently. Updates do come out fairly regularly compared to other games, so if you want to play this game for a few hours, put it down, and then return a few months later that is a legit way to play. After hours in the game I still don't have all the cards you can get, nor all the trash mob items, crafting, quests, and fishes. There is just so much to do.

Another thing that makes this game so addicting and fun is the Arcade/ Arena mode. Where to progress you have to get better at the game. some people have over 60 hours in this mode alone, and still haven't got their town filled up, or have beaten the final floor's boss. Basically if you want to buy a game that makes you feel good about yourself when you play it, to see how far you can progress, i highly suggest this game. Another plus is that you can play with friends.

I really don't have anything negative to say about this game other than if you don't like to work for things and grind than this game isn't for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is awesome! If you're a fan of old school 16-bit graphics like from Link to the Past or Secret of Mana, you're going to fall in love with this game. If you like online co-op, a witty story, and action RPGs- pull the trigger and buy it.

Pros:
- Great for online co-op!!! Definitely the highlight of the game, I probably would have never played it otherwise. Playing with a friend made for a great experince.
- The artwork is GORGEOUS!
- Love the HP system. There isn't any healing and you have to rely on health drops which makes the game challenging at times.
- Character build customization is really cool, you can be anything from an air bender swordsman or a mult-magic type summoner or bruiser 2-hand wielder.
- The story and dialogue is hilarious. My friend and I were laughing out loud a lot during the game.
- Boss fights, temples, puzzles are all awesome. The current last boss is crazy.

Cons:
- The game is about ~75% complete. The fourth temple is not in the game yet, and there's also a few things you can't do yet.
- You will probably beat the whole game in under 20 hours, and then there's not really much left to do.
- Low replay value because of the linear story. However, because of character customization and online co-op it could be fun to play with others again and try new builds.
- The game feels a little uneven after the first two temples. The third area/temple is like a whole story in itself, and there's not really a temple. Don't get me wrong I loved the third area, but it did feel a little out of place.\t\t\t\t\t

seeing items that aren't available to use in the very first tier of abilities is a bit disheartening seeing it's been in development for 2+ years now.

That being said, what is here is pretty good for EA.
There's a standard "The Secret Of Mana" style action rpg in adventure mode, and then a rogue-lite arcade mode that seems to have more focus on it.( as opposed to the main story )

The story is you are a collector, and pretty much explains it all right there - you collect loot - and you have a magical talking bag that holds it all for you ,

There's different weapons ( magical, 1 handed and 2 handed )
skills to go along with thoise weapons ( you have 3 hot key maps, one that is neutral, and two more you use by holding L trigger , or R trigger, and then the corrosponding button ( one of the 4 face keys )
There's a sheild ( if you use a 1 handed weapon ) and some magic spells as well ( no cure or heal spell, from what I've seen so far)

you collect virtually everythign from anythignm; including bits of broken pots, and monster's remains.

There's also a neat little outfit mechanic where you have accesories and hats you can wear that also have an effect on the game, it's a visual equip item./

Adding to this, the main character is very customizable, outfit colors, gender, hair, etc. And a multiplayer function as well ( think secret of mana)


All in all, not a bad little game - but it needs to be brought out of Early access to be worth a purchase, imo.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought two copies of it full price to play with my brother - best purchases of my life. This game is fantastic for a number of reasons. Great music (though it can get repetitive) immersive skills, and challenging (oh man challenging) combat and boss fights. Some other reviewers will abstain from recommending this game solely because the bosses are "too difficult". But you have infinite enemies to grind up your loot and levels so this isn't a long-term issue. I mean the game has "grind" in the title.

It is early access and the Devs seem to be updating fairly consistently. But don't let the Alpha dissuade you, the game is fully functional and highly enjoyable in its current state. I have encountered little to no bugs, and the multiplayer plays flawlessly. Many games today are built for singleplayer with some lower quality multiplayer on the side - like a wilted salad or mushy microwaved fries. But this game, while still fantastic solo, takes co-op to the next level including relevant dialogue to having 2 persons and so much more. I can't recommend this game enough.

As a message to the Devs - if they ever read this - I would like to make a few suggestions as this is early access. Firstly, while the music that is present is pleasant, there is only one track per area. And since a large portion of what you're doing in this game is grinding, it can get a little repetitive.

Additionally, it would be nice to be able to re-fight bosses on the path of grinding for levels and loot. Not in arena or endless mode, but by going back to the place you originally fought them and using a summoning item or something if you want to limit it. This would open up the possibility for boss cards, which would be pretty swell.

Finally, age old complaint that I'm sure all developers get sick of. "We want moaar!" Honestly though it would be nice to have more melee combat options. Perhaps rather than having the bow as a side item with semi-limited arrows, have a ranger "class." Right now there are only 1h and 2h weapons. Maybe specify them further to include daggers, 1h swords, 1h clubs/hammers, 2h swords, 2h spears, 2h clubs/hammers, and some specialty mixes each with specific abilities/skill trees.

Honestly I love the game and want to help make it better from my limited perspective as a player.\t\t\t\t\t

Two main points: Bosses and rewards.
1. Bosses are very difficult in this game. And not like dark souls where you learn the bosses patterns and slowly become better and better until you can kill the boss without even getting touched. Every boss fight is terribly unfun and annoying as , extremely gimmicky and straight up . I have no idea how you could possibly defeat most of the bosses playing solo, a friend is almost required if you want to play this game on the lowest difficulty level. Don't even get me started on the hard mode.

2. Me and a friend defeated 4 bosses in our second session of play. All of these bosses stemmed from the same quest line. Not once did we recieve experience points or loot from ANY of these bosses. So we stayed the same level basically this entire questline as it become increasingly difficult to get enough xp to level from simple mobs. And the grand reward for this quest that took 4+ hours? \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is definitely addictive for a while. But I have to say, that the game is not that well balanced. Playing on hard was one of the hardest experiences of my life, way worse than games like Meatboy where its actually supposed to be insanely hard. Dark Souls 2 and 3 are a breeze compared to this.

There are many instances in the game where you feel you literally have no chance, and most of the spells or abilities you have access to won't help you at all in those situations, meaning you have to rely purely on luck and hope the enemy AI doesn't cast spells in a particular order or with a particular frequency. Example: Fighting the two fairies (fall and summer) in the winter palace. And this is, apparently only *3 out of 5 star* difficulty level, according to the author of the game.

What the heck is five star difficulty? I probably don't want to know, it would be inhumanly difficult.

Its definitely an interesting juxtaposition, to be playing a game that looks like its for kids, yet (on hard difficulty) is actually the most painful experience you will ever have.

Anyway that's the main thing I wanted to get across. Besides that, I don't remember there being any music or noticeably good sound effects. The textures are decent. Every character is super-attractive (even the guards) which is kind of funny. Beyond that I liked the sense of humor. The fairies are kind of like cute babies, so I think this game will appeal to girls :P. And me apparently.

There is decent player progression overall, but a lot of the skills are unfinished, and they have been unfinished for years now as far as I can tell. I seriously doubt that this game will ever be finished. I think the developer left to start a different project, because no progress has really been made and its been about 5 years since I played this game last. I doubt any games takes that long to make, unless its Star Citizen in which case you will get to play it when you turn 80.

That's about it for this review. Hopefully I helped!!!\t\t\t\t\t
