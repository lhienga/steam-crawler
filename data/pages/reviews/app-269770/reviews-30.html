\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game may look dead, but it receives weekly updates from the developer directly on their website!

I played Grindea several years ago when it was about half-finished, but as of writing this review, you can play up to the final chapter of the game. The game has a complete set of features, including a pet system, build variety, itemization, cosmetics, crafting, quests, potions, and secrets. There's also a fully developed rogue-lite mode!

Despite its namesake, Grindea is not a grindy experience. Its an adventure title reminiscent of Zelda and Graal Online, with an engaging story and intense boss encounters. I played on Hard, and the challenge level was perfect. The beautiful pixel-art and surprisingly stellar soundtrack are the cherries on top.

The only issues I've come across are those with the Steam Deck. Running the game at 50 fps feel clunky, and occasionally the game freezes up. A quick restart and some fiddling with Steam's internal settings fixes both issues.

Highly recommended when it releases. I'm excited to see the conclusion to the story after all these years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"An Excellent Co-Op Adventure with Some Flaws"

Secrets of Grindea is an impressive co-op adventure game that manages to blend classic RPG elements with modern gameplay mechanics. The game's charming visuals, well-written dialogue, and enjoyable combat system make it an excellent choice for RPG enthusiasts. However, there are a few areas where Secrets of Grindea falls short.

The story of Secrets of Grindea follows four young heroes who set out on a quest to uncover the secrets of a legendary artifact. The game's narrative is engaging and is told through well-written dialogue and cutscenes. The characters are all unique and have their own distinct personalities, which makes them feel more like real people than just game avatars.

Combat in Secrets of Grindea is engaging, with a wide range of weapons and spells to choose from. The game's combat system is easy to learn, but it can be challenging to master. The bosses in the game are particularly impressive, with each requiring a unique strategy to defeat.

One of the biggest strengths of Secrets of Grindea is its co-op mode, which allows up to four players to join forces and explore the game's world together. The game's co-op mode is well-designed and makes the experience even more enjoyable.

However, there are a few areas where Secrets of Grindea could improve. The game's leveling system can be a bit grindy, and the game's world is not as open as other RPGs. Additionally, the game can feel a bit slow-paced at times, which may turn off some players.

Overall, Secrets of Grindea is an excellent co-op adventure game that is worth playing for its engaging story, fun combat system, and charming visuals. While the game has its flaws, they are outweighed by its strengths, making it a must-play for RPG enthusiasts.\t\t\t\t\t

I don't normally review games until I beat them or decide that I'm done with it, but I just wanted to give a positive review to balance out all the people who gave negative reviews because the game has been in Early Access for a long time. I don't think it's quite fair.

Yes, the game has been in Early Access in a long time, but note the following:
- The campaign/story isn't finished, but I got 15+ hours of gameplay from it already.
- The developers have a very active development blog so you can keep up with what they're up to
- The game has never gone on sale because the devs are cool and refuse to do that until their game is finished
- Those people buying the game and giving it bad reviews KNEW that the game is in EA and that the devs have made it perfectly clear for years (in that bright blue box above) that they could be in EA for a long time.
- Notice how few complaints for bugs + multiplayer disconnection there are. The game is solid and it is fun, and it's thanks to the devs taking their time to refine their game and passion project.

I enjoyed what I've seen from Secrets of Grindea and I look forward to the next update patiently.\t\t\t\t\t

This is a rare gem, one of the very few consistently developed passion projects that really delivers, a magnum opus, with more high-quality content than a lot of other fully released games here on Steam. SoG also feature an arcade mode as side content, the equivalent of a complete roguelite game.

I wish other devs would follow this example. The devs didn't bloat the game with more samey content over the last years which is the way many other Early Access titles are handled. It's easy to see the devs really want to develop a great game first before considering how to finance it.

Inspite of all the pressure from several sides, the devs stayed true to themselves. All I can say is: Thank you, just thank you.\t\t\t\t\t
