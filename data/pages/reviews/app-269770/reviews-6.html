\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, the game is not terrible. With that said, I ultimately regret the time spent playing this game for a few reasons. We can boil those grievances down to the story, controls, and game's pacing.

The story is pretty weak overall, though I did start to enjoy the writing much more by the third chapter. Most of the main plot components are cliche (you are the ultimate hero, go collect some strong artifacts, etc). On top of that, the tone is all over the place. It really feels like the devs were not sure what their story was, and it certainly doesn't fee like the story had a decade worth of polish.

I find the controls unresponsive and slow, especially once you get to some of the later boss fights which turn into bullet-hell combat. Your character's speed/animations feels sluggish for a game in this genre, but most of that feeling comes from how you activate abilities and your combat capability. There is some delay built into your commands, which looks to be a design choice by the devs, but I find the choice makes me enjoy the combat significantly less.

Most egregiously of all, the game does not respect your time. I understand that the game is called "Grindea", and some of farming aspects of the game are ultimately related to the story/plot, but the devs moved the needle too far. Drop rates for most items and cards are extremely low, and all cards are required for the true ending of the game. Approximately half of my playtime has been farming cards, items, gold, etc. I understand that is part of the game, but it really feels terrible as a player to kill 500+ of multiple mobs to get an item you need for the true ending of the story.

What is your time worth? I do not like mindless padding added to a game's length, and SoG has a lot of it. Arcade mode is a whole other beast, with its own gold grind... If you want to give the game a shot, you'll probably find out if you like it or not in the first two hours. You can always refund in that time-frame if the game wasn't for you. I was really looking forward to this game. It was in my wishlist for about 10 years... what a shame.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuaint, enjoyable, but the old-school gimmick isn't being respected.
The pixel art is fantastic, but the avatars shown in the dialogue screens don't match. The difference in art style is jarring.

Hit boxes are bad, but at least its consistent and predictably bad. I expect even the last couple of pixels on the tip of a weapon to register as a hit. Generally speaking, the combat isn't too satisfying.

Its charming, but it feels like a parody of
similar retro games, not a faithful recreation/re-imagination.

Those issues aside, the game is worth every dollar (not that its asking for much).
The gameplay loop is fantastic, the exploration is phenomenal, I'm going to recommend this game, for now.
I feel like what little I've played is gripping enough for me to give it the 'full playthrough' treatment.
I'll be back/10\t\t\t\t\t

One of the only downsides: it's just too short.

I realize that it has been made by two (I guess) people, but I just can't shake the feeling that it could have more content, at least in story mode. I mean, I've basically reached the end of the game in little more than 30 hours, and I've spent a lot of time doing side content, grinding and collecting stuff.

I don't know, it just made me sad that I finished up everything in less than 35 hours, lol.
