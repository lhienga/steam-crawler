\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI so badly want to love this game, especially since I've been waiting almost ten years for it to release... But so much about it just doesn't work for me. It wants to be a Zelda styled game, but every single boss is just a bullet hell, and it's impossible to take the world and story seriously when the game treats everything like a joke or a reference.

Monkey enemies throw barrels like Donkey Kong, you literally meet Indiana Jones in a cave, a fairy nags you like Navi from Ocarina of Time, and so much more. Too much more. This is stuff that could have been cute and charming if it was done with restraint, but Every. Single. Aspect. Of this game, is like this. Everything is a reference. Everything is a joke. And yet, the game is also trying to have it's own world. It's grating.

Playing this game is exhausting. There are things to love, like the gorgeous pixel art and animations, some really fun puzzles, and mostly good combat for this type of game... But when I want to play something more akin to an old school Zelda game, the bullet hell bosses are exhausting and predictable, the constant overly on the nose references break any attempt at world building, and just many minor things start to add up and add to the frustrations, like the developers for some reason, to my bewilderment, didn't solve the simple problem of diagonal movement doubling your movement speed, so it is literally more efficient to run around in zig zags than a straight line, an issue that most beginner 2d gamedev courses teach you how to solve, despite the game otherwise feeling really polished... It's all just so weird.

I tried to get through the game, but it just constantly disappoints me. I see that I'm clearly in the minority here, but yeah. The game is beautiful to look at, but that's sadly about it for me personally.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood graphics, combat, story. Been waiting years and wasn't disappointed.

UPDATE: After playing for a while, I'm changing my review to not recommended.

The first half of the game is great. The last half is a slog.

I had to grind pretty heavily to be able to beat one of the later bosses, which is fine. Except that the grinding is super annoying when monsters lay down slime that slows your character down, random sandstorms appear that blind your character (seriously, dev, how did that mechanic get approved by anyone?), and the health orbs have way too small of a pick-up radius.

The skill points you get from grinding don't even feel very impactful after a few levels. A few extra % damage here, a skill you don't have enough buttons to use there, how about a useless buff that only lasts a few seconds and you never have time to cast?

Using potions feels awful. You have to simultaneously press three buttons to use a potion.

The bosses in the later part of the game are just frustrating and not fun. Oh, you thought you beat this guy? Just kidding, here's a cutscene where he heals 33% of his health and you get to keep fighting!

I gave up before I finished the game and I'm not motivated to grind any more to maybe be strong enough to try again. Basically, the only thing leveling up will give me is more hp, which is boring.

Two weeks after release I see that only 1.1% of people have seen the "normal ending" which tells me that most people are in the same boat, and the reason the game is "very positive" is that most reviews were posted by people who've only played for a few hours.\t\t\t\t\t
