\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite this game still being in early access you will be able to play through quite a lot of the actual story, which, depending on your personal preferences, will possibly require quite some time (as you can see on my profile). Therefore, it does not feel like a demo but a real game with updates to the actual story.

The developers post a weekly update on their website to inform their players about their latest changes and when the next patches will arrive. They even showcase some of their work step-by-step. The development occurs under complete transparency, which is highly praiseworthy.

The story is quite interesting. The player sets out on a journey to become a "collector" - someone who collects things - a group of people who are highly esteemed in the world of Grindea. However, to progress in the story, you do NOT! have to grind for ridiculous amounts of hours - you don't have to grind at all, if you don't want to. That being said, to ensure a high credibility of the world's rules and fictional yet established society, the motif of grinding remains omnipresent.

In terms of graphics, the game is beautiful. The character portraits are hand-drawn with a certain style to it, which makes them appear unique, yet not something one is not accustomed to. The rest of the game is drawn in a pixel-art style. As almost every sprite has a charming looping animation to it, the game feels really alive. Coming across an object which does not jump, sing, dance, blink or whatnot is, despite this being an exaggeration, rather infrequent.

The soundtrack is catchy and well-fitting to the game at any times. I especially like the boss fight theme, as it reminds me of Final Fantasy VI.

The different parts of the game offer a lot of variety with every chapter introducing its own gimmick, which I will not spoiler.

In terms of difficulty, the player may choose between normal and hard mode. There are quite some difficult bosses but none feel unfair. Some might be more challenging than others, though.

The game involves a skill point system. By gaining a level-up, the player is granted skill points. These can be spent to various skills in many categories, such as one-handed weapons, fire magic, support skills and so on. Different builds are possible due to the vast amount of different active and passive skills. Re-assigning skill points is possible by spending some gold, so trying out different builds is definitely possible.

The developers did implement an arcade mode, however I have not tested its current iteration yet.

In summary, the game makes probably everything right, one can think of. The developers offer a transparent image of their progress. The game itself comes along with a charming story, beautiful graphics, an interesting story and a charming humour, which is supported by various references to other video games.

I highly recommend playing this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has some flaws, but they're few in number and amount to a minor nuisance at worst. If you like or at least tolerate pixel graphics, if you liked the classic Zelda games, if you like slashing, burning and shooting your opponents into submission, this game is the right pick for you.

As of this point, I've completed the Story Mode as it is so far. I must say the story line was bland and didn't have much to offer..At first. After the first two missions for the Collector's Guild, things start getting interesting. And by the time I finished the current story mode content, I was furious. You get back, talk to your Father in the Collector's Guild HQ to continue the story and "Sorry, it's not finished yet." NOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO! It was JUST getting to the good part! It's like stopping your favorite movie RIGHT at your favorite part and leaving it there. Forever. The tears..So many tears...

The name itself suggests there will be mountains of grinding, but I just didn't see it myself. I spent maybe two hours total grinding for levels and some drops to forge myself a Crystal Shield at the Flying Fortress (I'm good at Perfect Guarding.) All in all, I'd say it's definitely worth the buy just for the Story Mode folks.

Although this game is still in develop,
it's already has so much feature and lovely style.

if you're the old school fans like me, this game is for you.
The classic role play but with much easier system, only need to focus on your equipment, talent and skill.

Want to have a rest?then you can build your own house or just go to the lake and fish some great stuff.

Of course it still have some thing could be better, like add Mounts and cooking system will be much funner.

以下評論供中文玩家參考
雖然這個遊戲仍在開發階段，但是復古可愛的畫風，
精緻但不複雜的系統，仍是非常推薦在現階段就入手支持作者。
以下簡單描述其遊戲內容，如一般角色扮演一定會有的

等級系統、技能以及天賦系統：
一樣有分近戰(單手或雙手武器)以及魔法，
但是並不局限於一定要走純戰士或是法師，
假如技能或天賦點錯了也能重新分配點數。
有招喚獸能幫忙攻擊以及捕捉寵物來提升角色的能力
有趣的點是不論走近戰還是魔法，
每個人都一定會有弓箭跟盾牌可以使用。

解謎：
不論是劇情或是地圖上都會有一些需要小小解謎的地方，
有些地方稍微有點難度，但同時也增加了耐玩度。
有一些地方有所謂的小彩蛋，可以讓玩家自己去尋找。

房屋系統：
儘管現在房價很高，但仍然可以在遊戲中擁有一棟屬於自己的歸屬，
隨著劇情推進，會獲得越來越多種不同的家具可供裝潢自己的房子，
有些可透過製作系統來得到，有些可用金幣跟NPC購買，
有些家具則是屬於在劇情推進時才有機會拿到的，
玩家在探索時可得仔細點才能得到這些有趣的家具。

釣魚系統：
打怪打膩了？何不到湖邊釣釣魚呢！？
透過簡單的釣魚方式即可收集每張地圖所擁有的專屬魚種，
而掉的的魚則能跟商人換取金幣或是用來餵食以及提升自己寵物的能力。

紙娃娃系統：
覺得屬性比較好的裝備不和自己的胃口嗎？
那就用自己喜歡的裝備來打點自己的行頭吧！
遊戲中有許多好看的裝備，隨時都能讓你的角色變的更加可愛，
城鎮也有髮型師能變換其他的髮型及髮色。

以下為筆者心得
筆者有實際去作者的開發日誌看過，作者的更新頻率是滿穩定的，
每周都會有遊戲開發的進度回顧，通常二到四周會有一次的版本更新。
劇情的部分筆者預估還有20~30%以上仍未完成，但就目前的劇情而言都是有品質。
角色上的互動也都滿有趣的，也能在某些NPC身上看到小小的致敬，
相信老玩家們應該更能體會其箇中滋味。\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a polished game! It was very cute and enjoyable to play through the Story mode. I enjoyed my time playing and the story was great. I look forward to the finished product.

Three Wishes:
+ More comprehensive spell/skill options. The spells/skills are limited (though they're fun!) Ideas: Nature magic, void magic, holy magic, etc... Archer, Berserker, Assassin, etc... expand the active skill lists.
+ Spells that interact with the environment (i.e. spells that grow plants in certain areas, spells that can remove brambles, etc...) Facilitate creative spell usage.
+ Limit the shoutouts or make them more subtle. Krono, Link, etc... While they pay due homage to the originators of the genre, they break immersion and distract from the story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has EVERYTHING you'd ever want in an RPG: sidequests, leveling and character development, witty/fun humor in the story, and it can be very challenging at times. Some of the bosses in particular are difficult, and some of the enemies can get that way, too. I have definitely died a few times due to a lot of enemies at once + status effect DOTs on my character. A big part of the game is grinding (big surprise huh?) to farm items and cards that give you special perks. Once you find the best place to farm a card, the enemies start populating and re-populating until you have an endless barrage of enemies trying to kill you. A lot of them have certain attacks or defenses that can put a damper on being able to simply kill them with a few hard blows. For example, some enemies can only be killed by popping your shield at a specific time, some roll quickly around the room in a shell of spikes, some go underground and can only be killed when they pop back up on you. The game is very in depth for a little indie game. It seemingly has it all. Definitely worth $15. I'd even pay up to $45 for it...but don't tell the developers I said that ;)

The game is just fun. And addicting. And pretty cutesy (but I see that as a good thing.)

What's even cooler is the devs are literally adding content as I write this and all of the extra content is FREE. Now, that doesn't mean expansions will be, but this is such a good deal. You gotta get this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoy, where do I start..

This game is wonderful and a must buy if you like games like Terraria and Stardew Valley.

It literally feels like a mix of those two.

The game is loaded with puzzles and secrets that are actually difficult to solve.
The Quests are nice and grinding if actually fun because you can do it in your own play-style.
The leveling/skill system is surprisingly deep and combat is very fun and engaging.
The bosses are actually difficult to defeat and the environment in every area is amazing.
Also using a controller is a must because this game works wonderfully with a controller!

If you want to spend your time not fighting mobs and doing quest then you can farm for crafting material, craft, fishing, housing, sidequests, etc.

If I had to list some CONS I'd say:

Some quests can be rather boring or feel very pointless as the rewards and progress are almost useless/unnecessary, but these are very few.

The story is a bit lacking but its getting there with every update, which brings me to my next con: Sloooow updates to the story. This game has been in Early access for the longest time BUT I'd like to say that the latest "arc" added to this game is absolutely amazing and emotional. A MUST experience! So if anything, you should get this game to play the latest arc.
Overall I'd say this game has about 15 hours of main story content at this moment which is very much worth the price.

8.7/10
Buy this game!\t\t\t\t\t
