\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game that managed to successfully leave early access. Bought it like 7 years ago, and totally not disappointed now, after the 1.0
Secrets of Grindea is a very good arcade similar to a timeless classic like Zelda, Secret of mana and so on.
You control the silent protagonist on its journey to acquire crazy loot and save the world in process. Nice memes and quality jokes are added bonus.

PROS:
- very nice graphics, no stock sprites
- surprisingly good variety of enemies and bosses for this genre
- boss fights are very interesting and engaging
- decent quests (for the genre, not like TES series ofc) and unexpectedly good plot
- two games for the price of one (you can play either story OR arcade mode, the latter is somewhat similar to popular roguelike genre)

CONS:
- kinda grindy, but only if you wish to 100% (really though,its in the name)
- leaves you wishing for more)

All in all, a musthave game, honest 9/10. Even after i played countless games, this one really stands out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is fun to play co-op. It's never boring. I was very engaged in the story and plot when I did a playthrough for story mode co-op, it was very nice and refreshing. The characters you meet come off as meaningful which was really nice. Even the filler characters had some dialogues which was also nice. I'm not really good at games that require alot of brain power or skill level. I felt like this was a casual easy game to play despite that, however I struggled alittle closer to the end of the game when the difficulty of mechanics kicked up levels.

The only issue I have is when the difficulty of the boss fights progress, they throw in alot of mechanics but those also take up alot of screentime. Even in mob fights for grinding, the more enemies on the screen, the harder it is to see where my character is, it can come off as a bit of a cluster f*ck for me. The healing of the potion also felt fairly useless to me because it feels very ineffective at the end of the game. It feels like it heals very little or not enough when the attacks do so much damage and most come off as un-dodgeable to me when they are done in multiple succession. I kind of understand the concept of the potions, you're probably supposed to use them on cooldown, but even with that I could never manage to heal enough to stay up sometimes in boss fights at the end. I still had fun though, it was still enjoyable despite that.

I did come across some bugs in my playthrough. In the tower, my character couldn't get past an invisible barrier on my screen so my co-op playthrough had to do alot of messing around to get around that issue. That was probably the most major bug I encountered, the others weren't as impactful on my play.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy impression is this is a good game for someone, but not for me. Be aware that the experience will start one way and finish another, so consider all aspects before buying.

I play RPGs to grind stats and discover secrets - basically, I want to win with a combination of being clever and being stubborn. ARPG combat elements have been a pretty acceptable tradition going back to Secrets of Mana and others, but it's usually the case that if you're struggling with some aspect of dodging a boss attack or some such, you can over-gear or over-level around it, so that feels "fair" to the player experience. Sneaking in forced quick-reaction requirements for any aspect on the other hand feels a lot like a bait-and-switch experience, and very unfair for those of us who specifically select games to avoid that kind of play. If the hardest boss in the game is a jump or dodge, I avoid that game. This game does that to you hours after you invest your time into it.

Now I'm an old gen-x player, so age slowing me down is part of my reluctance to engage with reflex requirements, but also I have simply always been like this, even as a kid in the NES days. In fact, young me generally didn't get hooked on games until after the first time I picked something up that was different from what I had experienced up to that point. Almost all I had known as a kid were arcade games, all of which were variations on the same "react or lose" core game play loop/skill-set. Zelda changed everything for me, as did games like Final Fantasy. I game to re-experience that magic even now, almost forty years later.

It's very frustrating, frankly, when a game suddenly injects wrong-genre game play out of nowhere, and usually results in hours of real time of my time wasted. My solution in this game for such frustrations was to briefly toy with speedrunner any% tricks and just cheese through where I need to, but it's disappointing to have to break the intended experience and ultimately I don't feel good about it and will request a refund. If that isn't granted, I'll just shelve this game and consider my money wasted.

These types of things are really funky for a game like this. My character is powerful enough to destroy a demigod? Great, but I can't get him to jump across a small gap at just the right angle, so the demigod will have to wait. And if I do get to that boss fight? More perfect timing requirements to blink/dodge/block moves that instantly result in a loss no matter how powerful my gear because of things that disable movement almost completely. So it's either respec into a pure tank to soak unavoidable hits and turtle my way through, or repeat the same fight for hours and pray for perfection.

Having my build forced by not having perfect reflexes is just not what I'm looking for, but it may be right for you if that's your jam.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3.5/5 stars from me

With 25 hours in the game and with me having almost completed the game, I can easily say that this is a very fun experience in a lot of ways. The dialogue is cute and funny, the combat is smooth and fun, and if you're looking for an enjoyable grinding experience, Secrets of Grindea really scratches that itch. It's amazing.

So why only 3.5 stars? The puzzles. The puzzles are so abhorrently awful to go through. Note that not ALL of the puzzles are like so, but if you're a completionist like myself, Tannie's course took me a good hour to get through. Not because the PUZZLE is difficult, but because it relies on such perfect timing that it was absolutely painful. Then there are the math puzzles. If I wanted MATH puzzles, I'd hit up my old high school math teacher. I have no desire for math puzzles in a grinding game.

There are very few games out there that the puzzles were so god awful terrible that I'd literally sit there upon realising that there was a puzzle, put my head in my hands, turn off the game, and walk away. Sometimes it was warranted, sometimes it wasn't but the fact that the puzzles felt so miserable is why I give the low rating. And it's not as if you can even avoid some of the nasty ones in the main story line either. You are FORCED to go through some horrifically unfun puzzles just to beat the game.

To recap- Grinding? Amazing. Combat? Amazing. Characters/story/dialogue? Amazing. Puzzles? Good luck. It's miserable. It's still a fun game and I see myself putting many hours into it and enjoying that time, but be forewarned, unless you enjoy very difficult timing puzzles, there will be points where you will just suffer. Aside from that, I still recommend you give it a try.

Have a nice day and enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuick Version: Game's good. Just don't think about how long it was in development or how it compares to CrossCode.

Long Version: Without the context that I've been waiting for this game to be finished for what...a decade now? I'd probably think more highly of the game than I personally do.

It's a good game. Visually great, has some interesting locations, combat is serviceable, puzzles are mostly fun but occasionally one seems to require frame perfect timing and can be frustrating (though those are usually optional). Story is a bit meta, but manages to still be interesting and not the lazy cynical sort of meta you see a lot.

My big issue is that after waiting -this- long for the game, it felt so...small. As I filled out the enemy list 3/4ths through the game and saw how little was left I was really surprised. Doing pretty much everything there was to do in the story campaign came in a bit under 30 hours, and a good chunk of that was grinding mobs for items (It IS right there in the name, so don't be too shocked) and leaving the game idle while I did other things.

I toyed with the rogue-lite mode a bit, but it didn't grab me. The combat on its own isn't really exciting enough to carry the game detached from the other elements.

If anything, at least the game made me immediately start to replay CrossCode. A much larger game, with a wonderful story and combat that just feels a lot better to engage with, and it's just making my disappointment with Grindea all the more apparent.

The game is -fine-, I'd say it's good even. There's nothing to really dunk on it for without the two factors of how LONG this game took to make with me checking yearly to see if it was finished yet, and having CrossCode to compare it to. If not for those factors I'd probably think a lot more highly of it.
