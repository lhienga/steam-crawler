\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI struggle to describe how enjoyable this game is. Downright magical. I haven't felt so... adventurous... in years.

It plays good.
It looks gorgeous.
It's fun to explore.
Its secrets are obscure enough to make you feel good for figuring them out while being clear enough that you (almost) never need any third party assistance to figure them out.
It has a perfectly simple story that doesn't take itself too seriously as to be pretentious, and its parody setting doesn't overindulge in itself as to ruin the immersion.
Its characters are authentically goofy yet very endearing.
It's brimming with soul in every little detail.
It's so great, in fact, that not even fighting the cataclysmically awfully designed (on the hard difficulty) final boss could ruin it, and the frustration was immediately made up or with the ending.

Masterwork of a game. A passion project of unending passion. I never heard of it in its 13 years of pre-release development, stumbled upon it by pure chance, and am infinitely grateful for that cosmic coincidence to have brought upon me awareness of this game's existence.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a great RPG, was playing it hours straight. Fun little mini games, cool house building and no micro transactions!!
The dungeons have been fun, i don't even think I'm halfway done but I am far enough in to where it's feeling repetitive. The talent build I was going was getting a bit boring and after playing arcade mode (Which is also a great roguelite edition to this RPG) I wanted to try a new talent setup....Now here's my problem with the game.

When I first started the game, I checked how much a reset of talents would cost, it was about 20g per ability point..I thought that was fair as at the start I had roughly 300g...so had fun with my setup knowing the reset is cheap. So after 14 hours of playing, like I stated earlier, I got bored of my build...Went to respec and saw it had DRASTICALLY increased. To reset 5 points (Most talents require 5 points to max) it cost me 2.2k. The last dungeon I did I came out with 9k. So I could reset about 20 points out of my gold.(This is a fraction of the points youve spent already) So it's almost impossible to change your character's build without slowly doing it throughout the game which will leave you handicapped. I understand wanting to add some kind of commitment, but at least add loadouts you can change in town or someway to play different styles without going bankrupt (in game lol). It doesn't let you try new things unless you jump into arcade mode before playing story mode.

Even with that, great game, great price and all around fun!

One more issue exclusive to Steam Deck..
When you launch this game on Steam Deck, it takes such a long time to start but once it's up, it's running great. This typically happens on Steam Deck with CPU/GPU intensive games...So hopefully the devs can figure out why there little pixel game is so slow on Steam Deck :P\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game. Played it through out early access and honestly was the exact kind of game I thought of wanting years ago when I played Link to the Past (Basically a 2d Zelda with leveling and character creation).
The game is humorous, but it didn't get in the way of my being invested in the story.
Lot's of fun build variety. Skills that can be combined in creative ways, ect.
Arcade mode is super addictive.
Fun customization features, like building your house.
Overall this is a high value game; I've played for way more than the 46.4 hours listed on this review throughout story mode and arcade mode, easily beating out many 60$ games I've owned.\t\t\t\t\t
