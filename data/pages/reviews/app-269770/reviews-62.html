This game is fun for people who enjoys grinding. That's not entirely true because you can play through the game without grinding and still find it interesting.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, yeah...

Secrets of Grindea is actually an awesome throwback to the 16-Bit RPG era.
The art style, the music, and even the writing will trigger your Nostalgia Mode.

The gameplay in itself is quite polished, and gives you freedom in how you want to build your character. The parry mechanics and the variety of skills you can acquire makes combat pretty fun, also the bosses are incredibly well designed.
The Hard mode was given special attention, with new attacks for many enemies and for all bosses, making parry extremely important.

It's also got an arcade mode which is kind of like many rogue-likes around. Random everything. It's pretty fun.

But the feature that's the most fun you can get from this game? Online multiplayer. God it's insanely fun to play through this game with friends. However, be warned that there's no local co-op.

This is a game I absolutely love and recommend playing, despite its slow development. (But it's a small team, so...)

Despite the devs not posting much here on steam, there's a devblog here, that's updated weekly: http://www.secretsofgrindea.com/index.php/dev-blog.
