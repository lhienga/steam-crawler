\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a fantastic game. I got it 5+ years ago when it had a long way to go, and even then I knew it would be great when it finally came out. It is finally out, and boy is it fantastic. Great combat and boss fights, the game feels both challenging and fair in the best way possible. The boss fights have mechanics that are often difficult at first but clear enough that you learn and are able to beat it after a few tries. Some bosses you'll kill first try, some will take 5-10 tries, but they generally always feel satisfying and fun. Even regular enemies each have their own mechanics that keep combat engaging throughout. The dialogue is also surprisingly funny. I usually think video game humor is cheesy, but this game has had me laughing out loud numerous times. The story is solid but doesn't drag on, it's a great balance. I really don't have anything to complain about with this game, well done devs.

Also, I recommend playing with a controller. Keyboard and Mouse is fine, but it can feel a little awkward with how many skills you end up having plus WASD movement. I have found that the 12 skill keybinds with a controller are sufficient and feel great to use.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off by saying that I enjoy the game. If I could give this game a neutral review I would. Unfortunately, the terrible controls dampens all of the fun aspects of this game and looking through reviews, I see few talking about the controls and none going in depth about what you should expect. I want to leave this review so that someone looking at this game will know what to expect and whether the controls will be for them or not.

So you start off in the game and immediately think, why are my movement on the arrow keys? What is this, a game from the early 2000's? So you probably try to get the hang of the controls, and pretty quickly decide to modernize it. Shouldn't be a problem right? They include the option to remap keys, so you can quickly change movement to WASD and skills to 1, 2, 3, ect... (the random keys the skills are assigned to make 0 sense to me but whatever). You get it all set up to a modern style of controls and begin your adventure. Things seem fine for a while, you get a skill here and there, you're slowly expanding from using your 1 ability to having to press 1, 2, 3 and maybe even 4. You're feeling powerful and things start to feel good. Until... you see how they made the skills powerup. To use the more powerful version of your skills, you have to hold your buttons you use. This may not seem like a big deal. Just hold the 2 key and release for the big cool skill. Well not so fast bucko.

You see, what this game does differently is it has you not only move with your WASD or arrow keys, but that's also how you aim. There's no mouse directional aiming like in most games. This may not seem like a big deal, but you're going to quickly realize how little you've appreciated this feature before now. Normally in a game, you keep 3 fingers on your left hand on WAD and move one from W to S as needed. You use the mouse to aim and it's easy to quickly free up one finger and press one of your ability keys. This game however, you have to constantly use all 3 of your fingers to move and aim. Moving diagonal and trying to maneuver between the 10 things attacking you means you're constantly using your fingers to move and aim skills. You can kind of clumsily throw in a skill to attack, but if you spam skills, you find yourself locked into place getting beat down because now you can't move since you probably aren't playing with 4 fingers (try just spamming the WASD keys on your keyboard and using your pinky or thumb to use skills). This gets even worse when you try to use your upgraded abilities and have to hold a key down. Now instead of trying to get 1 key press in quickly and then immediately move, you have to hold the key, causing you to not move for even longer. It's very frustrating to feel so clumsy because of the terrible design for moving. Once you get tired of this, you come to realize that there's 2 solutioins.

1. Go back how the devs set up the game that you probably quickly changed in the beginning. This doesn't necessarily mean to use arrow keys, but you pretty much need 1 entire hand dedicated to moving, and 1 dedicated to your skills. You could use arrows keys to move with your right hand, and skills with your left. You could keep movement to WASD and have skills anywhere on the right side of your keyboard. You could even put the skills on your mouse. Overall tho, you're pretty much going to have to play with 2 hands which really feels strange.

2. Play with a controller. This game really feels like it was designed for a controller. Combat aside, even the menus feel controller based with having to press back at times when it would make sense to just move a mouse and navigate the menu instead.

If either of these solutions feel good to you, then I would definitely suggest playing this game. Otherwise, try out the demo and see if you can get the hang of the odd controls before purchasing the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Man do I hate to give this game a Negative review, because it's got great depth and character and I'd love to explore it more, but not having mouse support for directional attacking / blocking just isn't acceptable. I might have missed it somewhere, but there really should be a warning about needing a gamepad to play it correctly, as keyboard-only controls haven't been enjoyable in 30 years. Hopefully they find a way to correct this at some point so that mouse+keyboard players can enjoy it as well, but given the extremely long development time and this not having been implemented already, it's kind of hard to believe.\t\t\t\t\t
