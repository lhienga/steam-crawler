\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe quick version:

This game is like Gameboy Zelda games, meets diablo, meets Sword of mana.
It's a game with a lot of heart, a lot of character and a lot of references to old games.
The game is not complete yet, but it's more than worth the price tag in its current state.
And there's a roguelike mode that is also in development which is also pretty solid.
And you can play both modes with friends online.
Crank it up to hard mode, play around with some builds, take in the charming setting / story, you're gonna have a good time.

A little more detail:

I first purchased this game 2 years ago, at that point the main storyline of the game was about 40% complete by the games own estimate. And the roguelike mode was just in its early stages. And I 100% got my moneys worth. The combat, the character builds, the bosses, the charming storyline constantly winking in the direction of some of my old favorite games. An absolute blast.

And then I came back and now there's a whole bunch of new content for the storyline, and the roguelike mode is incredibly fleshed out. I understand why people who have been waiting for this game for years are frustrated. But work is very clearly still constantly being done. The game is already amazing, and whenever it is truly complete, I think it will be an absolute masterpiece.

Features:
- online co-op
- totally free class building
- zelda-style puzzles
- pets
- tons of side quests
- references to old games
- secrets to find
- a crafting system

Things I would like to see eventually:
- a few more skills in each area
- some skill trees specific to archery

A+ buy if you have kids and know how to setup multiplayer.
D- if you are an adult and were expecting Secret of Mana or Secret of Evermore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith a great sense of dread I must give this game a negative review, which comes with mixed feelings. I played this game 100% of the time with 2 other friends, and enjoyed almost every minute of it, until we stopped, probably never to return.

When the game started I got that nod to games past, and a strong sense of the type of game I was getting into. The game had many hidden references to other games that it clearly got inspiration from. This translated into a well defined scope of where the game was going. However, with the last few major updates I felt the game started to try to one up itself, and lost sight of what the game was.

The good:
1. Multiplayer co-op that worked without any issues.
2. Puzzles that were clever, but not so complicated that you wanted to resort to Google.
3. Minor crafting that involved killing countless enemies (i.e. the name of the game)
4. A sense of progression as you got that next item.
5. The ability to fully customize your character.
6. The first 10+hours of the game

The bad:
1. At the start the areas were large enough to run through, but as you progressed the rooms became smaller and smaller, leading to constantly waiting for all players to go one direction.
2. With these smaller rooms more and more backtracking happened to where you ran through the same room 5+ times for nothing other than wasting time.
3. The later puzzles became more and more about twitch gaming and less and less about solving things, which had this been the type of game it was, I am not against that. It was more about that it changed what the game was before.
4. More go back and read this great lore from those 10 things and put them together. Which while at the start was simply lore and story line became overly tedious to go back 10 screens to read things. (Here comes the Google to solve things)
5. Every puzzle and area had to one up the previous puzzle/room. This led to an ever increasing complexity that brought the game to a grinding halt.
