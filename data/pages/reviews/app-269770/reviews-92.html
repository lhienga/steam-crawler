\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, this is an early access game and should be undstood as such; however the beta was pushed out in 2013 according to their dev blog. With that said, below is a review based on my time playing the game and my concerns regarding its future development.

*Note: This review does not include the multiplayer or arcade aspects and this should be taken into consideration when reading this review.

1.) Visuals: 3/5
- Reminiscent of Super Nintendo games such as The Legend of Zelda, Star Dew Valley and Secret of Mana but nothing new or exciting to the visual experience.
- Animations are very basic but do not add to the genre.

2.) Mechanics: 3/5
- Follows the same templates as older SNES adventure/RPG titles and adds a little bit of depth to the real-time combat with the talent trees and progression systems but doesn't do much to seperate itself from other finished titles.

3.) Sound: 3/5
- Music is well done and helps to serve the landscape equally as well.
- Sound Effects are basic and satisfy the requirements.

4.) Story: 1/5
- It's basic at best and unfinished at worst.

5.) Overall: 3/5
- The game isn't good but isn't bad and doesn't add to the genre. It feels more like a rebuild of older SNES games with features picked from each to replicate what Nintendo has done to it's fans for decades (ie: selling nostalgia.) From the visuals to the sound, the game offers little to be excited about since the story is unfinished (ie: restrictions from proceeding forward to the end of the game's story.) If you're seeking a game that takes you back to the early 90s then this may be the game for you but expect to only get a few hours out of it before you get bored and move on.

Concerns:
- The dev team seems to be all over the place with no real plan for completing the game. I say this because the game is full of features... and that's pretty much it since no single element feels complete.
- Development started in 2011, Beta released in 2013, Early Accessed in 2015, Release date Still TBD... I don't attribute this to laziness but instead is a byproduct of likely poor understanding of tools, workflows and project management overall.
- The dev team wants to polish the game as they go. This means that they will have an extremely slow release cycle of impactful content since they keep revisiting and re-polishing the game over and over and over again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, so Ive played Secrets of Grindea for nearly 100 hours, here's what I have to say about the game so far.

First of all, I'd like to say that Secrets of Grindea can easily be seen as repetitive to those who don't manage to see the beauty of this game beyond just the "grinding". When this game first came out, I was the same way. But now that the developers have worked on this game and polished it up more and more, I've begun to enjoy it more as well.

So, the plot of the story is pretty good! I actually felt compelled to "end" of the story (at the time of writing this the game is not yet entirely done) and see what lies ahead. The characters are actually very memorable! And the names, dont even get me started, are pretty funny if you can catch afew puns here and there in them.

The mechanics are pretty explanitory in the name of the game itself. There is a chance you'll have to do A LOT of grinding if you're new to this kind of game or just want to kinda play it as it was designed to be played. The grinding is actually not boring, since you are actually doing it not just for money, or EXP, but for the rare cards that give you permanant bonuses that will help you out in the long run.

Since there are an abundance of items in this game, you can experiment with different setups. Whether it be 1 Handed, 2 Handed, or Magic, there is plenty for you to play for 15$. Though, at the moment, for me what seems to be the most powerful is Magic (Flamethrower and Cloud are OP in hard mode) and 1H (Shadow Clone is really good).

Now let me just explain a few negatives of this game.

The game can be kind of short depending on how fast you play and what you decide to do along your journey. Even though I took 100 hours, that doesnt quite mean that the game is extremely long, it just means I took my time to go through this game (obviously).

I would like to ask the developers to possibly add a warning regarding taking your character to frontline mode. I recently put my character into the frontline beta mode and now I cannot take my character out of frontline. Please add a better warning so other people dont have to spend 40+ hours trying to get a new character up to speed with their original.

Last Id like to say that sometimes the artstyle sort of changes on the characters picture (the one that pops up when you engage in a convo with them) that can kind of feel slightly out of place. Im not saying it ruins the game drastically but perhaps the devs could look at it if they could and fix that XD

Thanks for making a good game on steam! Im so glad I invested 15$ for some good quality 18 bit awesomeness ^__^
