Secrets of Grindea 8/10

As the name suggests, Secrets of Grindea has a LOT to grind. Being purely luck based, the game could have you playing for hours on end without getting what you want, or simply getting it too quickly. Secrets of Grindea, if I had to explain it in the shortest way possible, I'd say this: Completionist's Wet Dream. Why do I say that? Well, continue reading and you'll see why.

Early Access: 10/10

The reason I'm giving Early Access a 10/10, is because the developer has and continues to update the game, and allows us to see the process in which he is doing it, shows us what he is doing and tells us what he has planned for the future. He promised a housing system when I first got the game, one of the lastest patches gave us that housing system, with enough furniture for me to be pleased. This is what Early Access games should be like.

Fighting: 8/10

Fighting is 90% of what you will be doing in the game. Although there are different things such as Fishing and Housing System, the game is centered around its fighting. It starts off simple, the game gives you your first weapons, a one-handed sword, a two-handed club and a shield. Now, different to the fighting system in most games, you get to use the shield for both. The Two-Handed weapons, and the One-Handed weapons contain different abilities you can learn through the game, 5 abilities for each, allowing you to polish your fighting style. In the future, you may also specialize in magic, having to choose from four different elements: Fire, Ice, Earth and Air.

Personally, I was a bit sad there were no classes in the game, but at the same time you may build your character however you want without any sort of limitation. I loved the fighting, nonetheless. I'll say it's one of the funnest fighting systems. More in-depth explanation in "Talent System"

Talent System: 8.5/10

The Talent System is very easy to use. One-Handed and Two-Handed get a certain amount of abilities, which you will be upgrading as you level up. So the Talent System has multiple points you can utilize, one you can use on your abilities, in a basic matter (1-5) and from there you use another point, called the Golden point (5-10) which enhances your ability. I personally felt the two-handed and one-handed played very differently.

The last point, the third one is used on your passives. This is where you decide whether your character is tanky, by giving it +%HP or building your character crit damage, even Magic Attack. If you end up regreting your decision, you can click the ability and refund, which will cost you a bit of gold. You will be getting a lot of gold if you stick around to grind for everything.

Grinding: 10/10

With that being said, let's move onto the grinding. The grinding in this game, is fantastic. Giving you multiple things to finish, farm, and complete. This is why I said, a completionist will love this game. Every zone has 5 fish which you can catch, it is very fun and fish are good for use in the future. When you get your pet, you feed fish, or the items animals drop to make it stronger. Aside from the fishing in every zone, every monster in the game has a very low drop rate to their "card" .. These cards, give you permanent boosts, some to your magic attack, some to simple things like extra discount on items you buy. The most common card being a 0.3% droprate. If you dedicate yourself to getting all of these cards, it will increase your gametime exponentially.

Controls: 9/10

The controls in this game are very simple. You move with the arrow keys, attack with Z and block with X. Your abilities also being close to the Z and X for easier reaction time (C, A, S, D, V, F, Q, W, E, R) All the keys being modifiable, and controller support making it great.

Misc: 10/10

The Mechanics are very interesting. When you pick up an item of interest (Armor or Weapon) it will appear on the top right, comparing it to the item you have equipped. If you wish to equip your picked up item, you press Tab and ta-da! Your item has been equipped.

As for the menu, while you'll be going there a lot to check your talents, and there is no specific button to open it, it's extremely easy to navigate. Separated into 8 categories (Character, Equipment, Inventory, Crafting, Skills, Journal, World Map, System.) the game is easy to work around. Realistically speaking, you'll be using a maximum of 4 of those 8, but it's still nice to be able to see your character's stats.

Everything you equip in the game can be changed into an item you own. If you have a two-handed weapon you like the way it looks, but it's weaker, you can equip it as "Style" and it will replace your current weapon. It's the same with your Hat, Facegear and Shield. And if you are not the type of person to care for it, you may always hide it by right-clicking on the original item.

The Housing System is great, but I will warn you now, it takes a lot of money. It's great because you may make it as big as you want (As far as I've seen, I haven't tested the limit but I have spent over 50k gold on it) with upcoming furniture and a very intuitive system to change the house around, allowing you to change even the lighting on different rooms, to how you want your house's structure to be. Keep in mind, the game is still beta and housing is still not perfect, missing some furniture to fill your house perfectly, you will get more furniture as you complete more dungeons.

Co-op in the game works beautifully, with a very easy to set-up server for newbies, it's just a point and click with multiple options, You may set the loot to Round-Robin or Every man for himself, where you just run to get the items, first come, first serve. With an Arcade Mode that I haven't truly gotten around to trying and an arena zone, where you can play Player vs. Player, or unite all to try and kill waves of monsters, getting graded by your time and damage you took, making it interesting to have everyone screaming at each other because a person got tickled by a monster and now you can't get that perfect S.

The difficulty is flexible and can be changed inside the game, even during a fight. I wasn't much of a fan on the hard aspect of the game, because I never truly liked bullet hells, and it definitely turns the boss fights into just that. But if it gets too difficult, you can always go back to normal difficulty.

Story: 6.5/10

And last, but not least, the story in this game is pretty basic. Without getting too much into detail, you can play with your friends through it all, it adds no change, apart from the fact that you need to farm for more cards per player. I couldn't get into the story, some very funny characters I enjoyed interacting with, some I couldn't wait to get off my back. I laughed a lot in the dialogue, that I won't lie about. The con of the game, for me, was the storyline. But it did not make the gameplay through the story unenjoyable, I just prefered the aspects of farming in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
