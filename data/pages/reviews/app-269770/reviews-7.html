\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt starts off as an acceptable grinding game. At first grinding feels fun and rewarding. But it never really grows past this. Leveling skills doesn't really make them feel better. Enemies become annoying and unhittable. By the final boss the game really punishes you if you play anything other than beefy melee.

The story.. The humor is boring. The characters never develop. They are all over-the-top tropes played out in the most obnoxious way possible. By the middle of the game I could not care to read anything anymore as it all felt so pointless.

And then there is the netplay. As the host the game was mostly fine, but as someone connecting to the host the bugs were pretty unforgivable. Artifacts of dead monsters fill the screen. Any enemy that moves or any projectile becomes desynced and unavoidable. Then the game obnoxiously blames you for causing these issues by "downloading things in the background".

Overall the game is not ready for release and it's a shame that I am so disappointed in a game that has been in development for almost 10 years. Maybe in another 10 it will be worth playing, but I'm not sure.

Might be better in single player, but the multiplayer bugs are truly unforgivable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGenuinely great game, but be aware the the "hard" mode really is insanely difficult. Everything still in the doable realm but a bit much for casual players with lots of bullet hell elements. The true final boss in hard mode however, is not really in the realm of "possible" for a casual player anymore. I've played my fair share of difficult games in terms of both platforming and mechanics: Soulsborne, Elden Ring, Sekiro, other soulslikes, Celeste (feat "Farewell"), the end is nigh, roguelites like Hades, Dead cells, noita, games like Rain World, Hollow Knight, Terraria master mode (also Calamity mod), core keeper hard mode, Monster Hunter ... all of which contain some at times brutally difficult content. The point of this enumeration is just to give an outlook of what I might consider "difficult" for you to judge my following statement properly for yourself as you consider whether to play this game and if yes on what difficulty: While all the other bosses in this game are mostly challenging but doable, I really consider the hard mode variant of the true final boss of this game Zhamla Meer to be probably THE most difficult boss fight I have ever fought in a video game, by a long shot. And that's considering the fact that I took a break from the boss just to farm up XP for a bunch of levels, only to alleviate SOME of that difficulty. I tried changing builds a bunch of times, then I went out of my way to do a "secret" if you'd call it that to obtain the most powerful shield in the game and yet STILL this boss continues to curb stomp my ass. I'm not complaining about it. I like the fact that this boss is challenging. I even think the boss is mostly fair. About 95% of the times I take damage, it's my mistake that got me there. There are a few times where that might not be the case, at least it doesn't feel that way, but thats more due to the fact that the bosses attack patterns vary from try to try and considering the fact that the boss will react to YOUR movements and actions, this can result in certain scenarios where avoiding damage may become impossible. Just keep this impending doom of a boss in mind when buying this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not really sure if I should recommend it.
The graphics are nice and cute (not a mature look, rather a bit cartoony), and the music is good.
The combat feels quite good also.

But one major flaw I find is that it's not really worth grinding for levels except in early game. It doesn't feel like it's giving something back after a while. I don't mind grinding, but I want to get properly rewarded for it.
I have way more points to spend in skills than I have need to use, and would rather be able to put them more into str/hp/mp/regen or more passives etc.

The story is decent. A lot of the sidequests are just not fun though.
It's not really worth to explore the maps for treasures and the world is not that big.

The screen can get really cluttered and especially when fighting bosses. Many of them are bullet hell, and paired with my spells/summons I just lose track of what is actually happening sometimes because I can't see my character.

Also the game reaches too far in searching for references/easter eggs from other games etc, trying to be funny. Same thing with several of the names of places and characters.
It's standing out like a sore thumb and just comes off as goofy (especially if you're from Sweden, and it's not often I can say this but here it actually makes the experience worse lol).

Grinding for the true ending (specific drops, lots of them) is not that interesting.

The price is not expensive however, which makes the cons so much more bearable.
But personally I don't see myself completing it.

Hm.. The more I think about and write this review, the more I realise that I actually won't recommend it.
It's not bad! Just... not good enough to earn my recommendation.
It does work great however. I've found no issues running/saving/loading the game.

I waited 9 years for it to come out of EA and honestly I can't help to think I expected a bit more, even though I know the devs worked hard. So I'm a bit sad I'm going to give it a thumbs down.

6/10.\t\t\t\t\t
