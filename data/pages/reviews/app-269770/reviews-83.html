\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked this game up because the character building system looked good and the graphics were appealing to me. The plot has a tongue-in-cheek mockery of RPG cliches which I enjoy. As you progress through the game, however, the story-telling takes these same motifs and shows them to you in a more personal and emotional way. (I don't want to spoil the story; playing thought personally gives what I'm talking about meaning.)

The game IS in early access, and yes the story campaign is not complete as of now (Jan '18), however the enjoyment I have gotten from the game is well worth the price. I usually am not one for stategic and intense boss battles, however my emotional involvement in the plot left me no choice but to push through.

I completed the currently avilable quest lines and most side content in 21 hours. I have still not played arcade mode, which allows for challenge based gameplay rather than story driven. A perk to this is you are able to use the same character in both gameplay modes, so you can train up your story character in arcade or vice-versa.

I would 100% buy this game again for the current content alone, however I am looking forward to future content eagerly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith what you read of this review, some might wonder why I’m giving it a positive recommendation. It's because when I do a review, its on the game itself, and only reviews I give negative for most of the time are for abandonment, falsely advertised as completed, or development team has deleted posts in regard to game issues. Trust me, I was tempted, but then why even bother write one to begin with.
Secrets of Grindea is a fantastically written Ragnarok Online clone. You can play either single player, or multi-player, and can play your character either version any time. This means if you play single player, which I highly recommend playing multi-player, because you most likely will die a lot and its quite simple to be resurrected by a friend, but anyway, if you play single player, and suddenly your friends ask to join in, you can quit your game, then reload that same character and continue it in multi-player.
Game play is quite easy, easier than Ragnarok Online was to learn. I highly recommend this game over RO because not only do you get to run your own server with your own rules and not have to worry about strict rules, bots getting in your way, trolls, or even monthly fees for better access, you get to play in an environment you choose to have. This game is so family oriented, the whole family can actually play. Even a 5-year-old can join in with a little help.
The game is full of quests, and unlike RO, you can have and build your own house!!!! Something RO has always lacked for years. Sorry RO, but you met your match and have lost! Something else I love about this game, that RO lacks is the easy ability to change classes, if you can call them that. It used to be in RO that if you realized you don't like the class you're in, you either had to start completely over or wait a few years for a stat or class reset npc for an event to come out for a short matter of time. Or you would have to pay a LOT of real money for some type of item to do this.
The game has been in Early Release status for a while now, but its content is more than complete in the state the game is now. What’s even better, is the fact with the extra DLC they sell, which is actually real, unlike EOS, it adds cute items not normally available to the game. They do this to bring in income, and quite cheaply if you ask me, so they don’t have to say good bye and move on. For $15, the game is more than worth it, and as I said above, having multi-player capability, you can play with family, and as a parent, keep them safe which is a huge PLUS in my book!
The game has many skills, rare drops, recipes, and if you don’t like something you choose, you can refund it and get your points back, to put them into another skill or stat. I should have said that above when I basically flipped off RO. RO isn’t bad, its a good game too, but in my opinion, this game is MUCH better and user friendly!
So with all the positive review, why mention the possibility of giving it a bad review? Simple, it's not the game itself that is the problem, it's the support discussions forum for the game. Its completely overrun by kid trolls, and the developers do absolutely nothing about them. From the looks of it, they actually encourage it. Don't get me wrong, there are some good people there who were nice and helpful, who pmd me because they were too afraid to talk out in public, which is a shame, because many who see the forums, might actually get scared into not buying the game. My issue in the forums, was responding to an old post where someone who asked about healing items, got torn apart by trolls. It was a post over a year old, so figured I'll respond and see if there were any changes since then, which was asking if any healing items exist. I got the same horrible treatment as that poor girl who asked as well. Anyway, like always, I have looked into the game code, and most of the content is activated, and everything is in game, so had this game been put into normal sales, I would give it a thumbs up to say it’s safe.
As for healing items, except for the drops, there are none. So, expect to die a lot until you figure the game out. If you have kids, who are young, and would like to let them play, but without the risk of dying, you can hop on over to CH and buy a trainer for a few dollars which will lock the HP bar. You can do the same with a memory manager, but unless your highly skilled, I do not recommend doing this as you will ruin your game. Be fore warned though, over at CH the rules are never use their trainers on multi-player games. They do make some exceptions to people who run their own server and need the utility to help administrate their own server, but never use it on someone else’s server, especially without the owners permissions. Doing so not only can get yourself banned from Steam, it can get you banned from CH as well. If your not sure, always ask.
Links no longer work so look up CheatHappens and once there Grindea.
I should also mention, if you do let a child play, and use a trainer, they will have to play from the hosting pc. The trainer will not work properly except from the hosting pc. You can see this quite easily when they start dying with a full HP bar. This isn’t due to protections, its due to the fact the calculations and real stats of each player are actually server side, unlike games like Terraria where the game client itself controls your stats.
Lets discuss some more features about this game. The hoster has the ability to make sure EVERYTHING gets split evenly. No more of those days where everyone has to throw what they get in a pile, then more time dividing the items. The game does this for you. It does not matter who picks the items up, they will all be split among the entire party. I’m not sure if there is a limit to the number of players, but if you don’t want just anyone joining your server, just password protect it.
Due to the small staff, the updates are slow, but from what I have seen in the forums, the wait is definitely worth it, because the content that gets added each time, is absolutely HUGE. As for the discussions section, I highly recommend reading them over before even thinking of posting there. Just to get an idea of how things work. If you have questions, but don’t want the hassle of getting trolled, then just ask me. I’m new to the game, but know the basics and be more than happy to help.
Lastly, I’m in the process of opening up my own Review section for games, called L-XGI Lord-Xanthor Game Investigation, which will not only include reviews when it’s done, it will also have mods listed for games without a Workshop on some titles. This is for games where the development team gives permission, I will never post information that shows how to edit someone’s game without their prior consent. Till next time.
