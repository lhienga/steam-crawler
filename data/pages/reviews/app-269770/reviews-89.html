\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is EPIC!!! I can't wait to see it completed. The fluid animations and movement, the fact that you feel in control of your character when you're playing, all good stuff. Arcade mode increases the lifespan of the game greatly and I love everything about this game. The colours are bright, and the world is alive. There are tonnes of skill choices as well as interesting spell choices. It's all very cool.

My only criticism is the limited weapon-variety. It's broken into 1H and 2H weapons, and all weapon skills go under this. I would have liked to see a bow as its own weapon with its own skill tree, like in the Secret of Mana, where you have infinite arrows, but the range is far lower, and the damage isn't as crazy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKuramei here,

I know many are iffy' about early access, alpha, or beta games. Especially those with the tag, "Pixel". I'm a late 80's baby, and grew up on games such as Tibia, Final Fantasy, and more. Those early games shaped my attraction to games. This game is a tad chibi, but so far the quantity and quality of what is in the game (I.E., story, interactable characters, items, skills, talents, etc.) makes up for the child-like esk. And at the same time that esk is refreshing. There is something to be said about games that can bring a customizable twist to a simplstic world. So far... Secrets of Grindea does it well! I thoroughly enjoyed the first 1.5 hours I invested into this game. Think: Tibia meets Hunger Games, meets Boarderlands, meets Pixel, meets sword-and-board, meets magic, meets cute yet attractice game art, meets what the actual-where-have-you-been, meets smooth combat, meets fluid movement and terrain adventuring, meets addictive story element. That's this game. I found myself wanting to find out more and more, even though some games that have this esk have very assumable elements (typically repeated from other bigger games). This is hands down one of the most enjoyable games I have played on Steam in a while. I will be following, updating, and writing more to game devs on anything I see that could be a noteable point for development. I encourage you... the price makes the purchase feel questionable... I don't regret my purchase.\t\t\t\t\t
