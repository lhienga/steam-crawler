\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSecrets of Grindea, currently in Early Access, is absolutely worth checking out.
The combat feels pretty well, and the multiple skills are pretty neat to explore. It's fun on coop but if you're playing with someone on high ping certain "parry"-ish skills could be a pain to execute.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an incredibly fun game to play with friends. One of the rare simplistic games where you and a friend need to work together to go through a story mode (or Arcade mode) to reach a conclusion.

The overall gameplay is your standard rpg, except instead of choosing a class and being stuck with it for that character, you're free to have whatever spells from casting fireballs to making corporeal copies of yourself to making barriers for your team mates.

You can have up to 3 other people to work together towards the conclusion of whatever mode you choose.

The game has your standard grinding aspect (It's in the name after all) for equipment, material, cards that permanantly buff your character, cosmetic items, furniture and levels to upgrade your skills naturally.

That's to be expected of course. The game can still be beaten without having to grind too much, especially with friends.

There'll be bosses naturally and the game changes itself to balance, by making enemies have different moves if you were to be playing anything but singleplayer, so you don't have to worry about the game being easy enough to just gang up on every boss. Wait til you see the Guardians of Seasons

The skills are pretty easy to learn. You have your elemental spells of fire, ice, earth and electricity, which range from calling meteors to smite your enemies to summoning a swarm of insects to feast upon some poor rabbits flesh.

You have your melee skills that range from 2 handed weapons people hitting fast with abilities like dash attacks or conjuring corporeal clones of yourself to 1 handed weapon people who can enter a berserk state to do massive damage or just spin to win.

Then we have support players who just sit in the corner and buff or shield their allies.

Everyone has access to the same tree, so they can change anytime they want providing they have enough in game currency.
Everyone has the same chances to get the same weapons, cosmetics, etc.

It's all on you on what you choose to play with.

Work together or work alone to reach the end game conclusion after some mildly entertaining characters, a linear but coherent plot, some humor worth a good chuckle here and there, a degree of diversity on how you can look and how you can accomplish killing monsters from slimes to giant adorable bears and annoying puzzles that no matter how you look at it, can be infuriating or tedious.

Still enjoyable though. Clever puzzles if not basic reaction speeds.

There's a lot to cover here due to the content here, but just know that there's a high chance that you'll be able to enjoy this game, especially with friends.\t\t\t\t\t
