I only ever just barely dabbled into this game in its early stages some time both around its release, mid-early access, and a little bit in the last 2 years, and I STILL feel qualified to tell you that if you pass this one up, you'd be doing yourself a great disservice.

This title was in the works for 13 years. Don't let that have you believe it can't hold its own with the games of today. I find that the silliest argument people make about gaming is older games not holding a candle to newer ones when over 90% of the time, all they're referring to is their graphical fidelity, and nothing more. Some people wouldn't even manage to keep all of their 99+ LIVES in Super Mario Bros. 3 in some cases even if they were capable of doing so, so I don't want to hear that nonsense. Don't even get me started on Commodore 64 gems like Boulder Dash, and you can forget copping out saying its mechanics are subpar as far as that game series is concerned.

Just watch their trailer leading up to the February 29th full release. I haven't personally experienced the brunt of the content, but I know a good game when I see it. They packed so much into this game that it deserves recognition. The challenge is there, the side content is there. The broadness of diversity of content is there. You won't get tired of playing this game like you do so many other games. Its vibrance only makes it more attractive and likely to be played again for more runs - That "Skyrim effect," if you will. This game hasn't sold 60+ million copies, but man, if we had stuff like this back when Super Mario Bros. 1 was making its rounds, it would've been hailed as the best game on God's green Earth. "Chrono Who? Secret of What? Nah dude, I just want to grind that Secrets of Grindea Arcade Mode..."

Do yourself a favor: Buy it. Stop reading positive and negative reviews, and go enjoy this magnificent piece of gaming history. You'll be nothing short of glad you did.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGrindea somehow manages to join Rhythm Doctor in the "worth a decade's wait" club. Wicked good co-op gameplay filled with content is the main draw, this is an underpriced game and an easy 10/10 if you play it with friends.

At Grindea's core, you've got snappy combat, interesting enemy designs, engaging environments, all supported by a ton of build diversity that lets you succeed with any build you can imagine. Want to take quick fights with a sword and a bunch of dashes? You can do that! Want to burn things with a flamethrower and throw lightning at a distance? Go for it, it'll work. Maybe you want to summon things to fight for you while you slap things with a greatsword? Support your allies with buffs and teleport away from enemies? All of the above at the same time? Just about any combat style you can think of is an option here, and it is glorious.

The game doesn't stop at having amazing combat systems. The dialogue ranges from "good enough" to laugh-out-loud funny, the worldbuilding is top-notch, the pixel art is all done well, and the constant appeal of collecting every monster card to buff up your character will keep you going back for more.

Oh, and the game has one of the best co-op roguelikes I've played, as a side mode. Just in case the base game wasn't already too much content for the price. I can't recommend Grindea enough.\t\t\t\t\t
