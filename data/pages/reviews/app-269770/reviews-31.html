\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like this game and have been actively following its development for a while. Many may seem put off by the fact that it's "still" in early access after 8 years of development, but luckily I'm one of the lucky ones that it doesn't bother at all since the game even in early access is already so rich. The game is full of ambition and love for its genre, I have rarely found an rpg like this on steam that made me rediscover these emotions I used to have as a child playing Minish Cap on my Gameboy Advance, and seeing the game grow in its development over time is another form of fun to me. The small team of Pixel Ferrets take their time to deliver the best they can do with the means they have, and for that I highly recommend playing Secrets of Grindea.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been following this since late 2016, so I've seen a lot of the progress that has been made, with the game being pretty close to fully releasing now I thought it would be a good time to give a review on this game.

So, what type of game is Secrets of Grindea? Secrets of Grindea is an RPG that gives off that classic game feel while also having pinches of modern game mechanics sprinkled in. The game has a decently sized group of locations and puzzles for you to both explore and solve. There's a variety of skills and different playstyles to choose from, the main three being: one-handed, two-handed, and magic. Each playstyle comes with multiple different skills and perks you can choose from every time you level up, there's also special abilities not tied to any playstyle regardless of weapons. Each of these skills have a different level of charge, these levels can be obtained when you put enough points into a particular ability. Due to the variety of different skills, combat in the game never gets old. Don't like your current skills? No problem, you can just respec your points and put them into different abilities.

As stated before, the combat in the game never gets old, this is due to both the variety of skills AND the variety and uniqueness of almost every enemy in the game and how they vary in how you can approach them. Perfect blocks are fun to pull off and are beneficial, completely negating damage and instantly giving you a level 3 charge for one of your special abilities. The bosses are also unique and also fun to fight, especially on hard mode where they gain increased stats and new attacks, definitely play on hard mode you want to challenge yourself(though I would recommend at least to play the game on normal first).

The story is interesting and provides quite a few likeable characters that you will meet on your adventure. The story can take upwards of 20 hours on your first playthrough, and that's not counting the side quests and all of the other side content such as Arcade mode. What is arcade mode? It's a completely separate way to play the game. Arcade mode is a rogue-like mode where you go floor to floor clearing out enemies and bosses while gaining new items and leveling up skills while also building the hub world, which can help you in future runs. Something also unique about arcade mode is that you have the ability to unlock cosmetics that you can wear in both arcade mode and in story mode.

Overall, I would give this game an 9/10, even in it's early access state there's more content in this game than quite a few triple A games. The game has nice pixel graphics, good music that will get stuck in your head, and the devs also post almost weekly updates on the game's progress on their developer's blog, and are quite nice.\t\t\t\t\t
