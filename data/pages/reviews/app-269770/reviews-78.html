\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the grinding aspects of this game and how its integrated as an everyday thing in the world of the game. There's satisfaction to be had in imaginatively hunting for rare items with NPCs or Players in order to complete some set goal or journal entry. Secrets of Grindea has an impressive understanding of how to build a story around its gimmick/gameplay and I am grateful as I enjoyed it from start to finish. It had a good pace between grinding sessions and story-driven moments; delightfully simplistic combat to absolutely charming cutscenes/character interactions. Bosses were also pretty intense when they wanted to be. I'm also a sucker for pixel art--and this game delivers in good visuals and animations. Ultimately, its overwhelmingly charming and fun to play.

My only complaint, as I feel the need to address this, was the occassional bad RNG. It was the worst trying to 100% complete the game, but end up spending hours and hours grinding for a monkey card drop right near the game's end. I almost never want to look at the area again from how long I spent there compared to the rest of the game. I genuinely spent a majority of my gameplay time hunting for that one card than anything else in the game; it ended up doubling my total playtime. As the main draw of this game is grinding and luck, its greatest weakness too is repetitiveness in the event of bad RNG. It's a shame this happened so close toward the end of the game, denying me the complete gratification of having an otherwise fully pleasant, fair-feeling experience. Seriously--real talk for a moment: I hate the monkey card. There's no reason that the rarest item in the game is from such a goofy enemy, is right at the end of the game, and has a pretty worthless ability THAT late in the game. It just chewed time and kind've killed the game for me. Hate that card.

Otherwise, I really do wish this game was complete. While I appreciate the efforts of the creators putting work into the Arcade Mode, I'd personally much prefer a finished story. I'm also not that interested in Arcade Mode, admittedly. Like it looks cool, but I've never been interested in content outside most games' main campaign. Except for developer's notes and art, I guess--arcade modes though? Meh. Not a big fan. Especially when its main draw is the risk of losing all your character data upon death. That feels like a major way to pad the game out with little reward other than bragging rights/steam achievements. Again... meh. Not really my thing.

Still, this game is absolutely delightful. I await patiently and ever eagerly for its story-based completion. If grinding and storytelling is your cup of tea--I definitely recommend it. If not now, then hopefully when its 100% complete.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game when it was very early on in development. Since that time, devs have added a TON of quality content. Even recently, they overhauled the hell out of the brutally difficult Arcade Mode. So if anyone is wondering, YES the devs are continually updating the game, albeit very slowly.

I've played through the story mode every time they've updated it. It's short, but a ton of fun. I recently convinced a friend to play co-op with me and it cranked the fun factor to another level!

I only have 2 complaints:
1. Some of the puzzles are absurdly difficult and take away from the game.
2. Getting certain cards is nearly impossible as the drop rate is insanely low. The devs implemented a potion that increases drop rate, but if the drop rate is .1%, it doesn't really help that much. (Yes, I understand that "grind" is literally in the title.)

Complaints aside, I highly recommend this game. It's a ton of fun!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start by saying I fully endorse anyone who likes classic games like Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past to purchase this game right away. I watched a short Let's Play of it a few years ago and was interested even then and since my purchase I can say I've gotten my money's worth in fun and gameplay in just the first few hours (and continued playing because it's just so dang FUN!). The story is engaging, the characters are interesting, and the humor is everywhere (I can't tell you how many times I broke into laughter at a random Easter Egg reference and/or the antics happening in the game)

However.. if you're wanting a game with an ending.. I'm sad to say this isn't the game for you.
You can see the love and devotion the Devs are putting into this.. but holy crapbaskets! it's been in development for 4-5 years now! I get that they are a small, 3 person dev team and with that size, it takes time to get things polished, but at this juncture, I'd say it's time to start bringing in new members (even if those members are only temporary in order to get the next part done)

Currently, the focus of development has been on the "Arcade Mode". A "Hardcore" tower-scaling mode that has in it a town-building minigame. While I get that appeals to a lot of fans (there's plenty of *coughs* GRINDING involved in that) I'm wishing for more Story. That's why I got this game in the first place... that's why most of the people bought this in the first place.

This has been one of the best purchases I've gotten on Steam, but seriously... finish the game, and polish it afterwards.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game, nice graphics, deep enough skill system and nice loot.
Would like a lot more different items (maybe the same look but different statistics), its not exactly scratching my lootwhore itch but its ok the way it is.
Combat is fine and deep enough, and the story fun and engaging enough till now.
Gladly, it has an online coop mode, which is a HUGE plus in my eyes.
Local Multiplayer would be the icing, but i would not mind buying a second copy to play locally anyway.
Story isnt finish as of writting, but it still has enough content for several hours.
The arcade mode is a really nice "infinite" addition to the game and provides the possibility to do "rogue-like" runs with meta-progression that is interesting and even gives you quests.
That mode will apparently receive a rework, but i hope it will stay the way it is and just getting better (especially in terms of area design).
The story alone isnt that long sadly and doesnt offer that much replayability in my eyes. I hope they scale it up a bit and make it more "open world"-like with more randomness (random boss spawns with special loot, maybe infinite quest-types (kill boss X in area Y) and maybe raid-dungeons.
Other than that the story might hook you for currently about 4 hours. After being finished probably 6 hours playtime. Doing it in coop again will then double that time and arcade mode does the rest.
You can play with controller and keyboard.
The game is in my eyes worth its current price by any means. Like, easily.
Its also good enough already to be worth taking the risk of early access, but thats a subjective opinion anyway.
