\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never made a review for a game on Steam, but with the new arcade update, I was so moved that I just had to make one for this game.

The Pros:

-Simple, charming, cartoon-esque designs on the monsters. I've seen a lot of games that go overboard with their monster design, and often times the designs just look flat-out ugly; not this game.

-Arcade mode. This is the meat of the game, as it is still in early access. Honestly, if this were the entire game, I would be completely fine with it. In arcade mode, you fight through numerous floors, each getting exponentially harder, once you die, your levels, equipment, and skills reset, and you can try again. Playing this mode with friends is honestly such a blast. Each run is almost completely unique, as the rooms of the floors are randomized with shops, nurses, challenge rooms, puzzle rooms, and fishing rooms. With the new update, they've added even more stuff to this list such as Loods.

-Arcade town. This is by far the biggest (any my personal favorite) change to Grindea. When playing arcade, you spawn in a town that you can expand by playing the game. I don't think I've played a game that does this as well as Grindea has. Other games simply have you expand towns for cosmetic effect, and whenever I play these games I just think to myself "that's it?" Not Grindea. Each building has it's own purpose, and attracts different townfolk that give you quests, buffs, and different services for your time in arcade mode.

-Housing. This. This is the reason I decided to make this review. Again, this is a concept that I've seen other games do and fail at... but not Grindea. You can unlock different furniture, wallpapers, etc. to place in your house, but the thing that stuck out the most to me was the awesome house customization. You can add rooms, re-size and even re-shape them into any shape you want! Even if you and your friends have every furniture item in the game, your houses can look completely different just through this feature alone.

-Skills. There are sooooo many to choose from, and each is pretty unique, from magic to melee, to support. The best part is: you're not bound to anything. You can be a battle mage, enchanting your sword and freezing enemies, or you can be a tank, taking aggro and receiving little damage. The nature of skills also adds to the arcade mode, as you can switch up your strategy or try something completely new, there are a plethora of skills to choose from so go wild!

The Cons:

I can really only think of two: slow development and some bugs; however, this can be forgiven, as the game is only being developed by two people, I believe.

Final note:

About three years ago, two of my friends and I were looking for a cheap, low-graphics game that we could play together. We bought Grindea on a whim... I was even against the purchase. Today, this is one of my favorite games to play. Even when I find another game to play, I constantly find myself coming back whenever there's a new update: and honestly, there's few games that can get me to do that.

This is easily one of the best Steam purchases I've made. I even bought each member of my discord server a copy of the game, and they all love it! 10/10 game, hopefully the couple making this game get the recognition they deserve. Good job, guys!\t\t\t\t\t
