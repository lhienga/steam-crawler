\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStill incomplete, so keep that in mind. The humor falls a little flat, and the story just kinda exists.
I think that as of right now, talents, abilities, and all that need a bit of a buff. I care so little about leveling up my character because none of the abilities are really worth striving for. I wanted to play more of a summoner type character where I stack passives and minions on myself, but the minions are dreadfully weak. you'll end up just spamming attack on enemies and sloooooowly watching their health bar drain away. The fights get a little confusing since there's so much going on, and you'll find yourself most likely spamming health potions. I'd say if there was more reason to grind, it'd be a much more enjoyable experience.



Been a while since I first posted this review. None of the issues I've had with this game have been addressed, and the damage sponge enemy problem seems to be only getting worse.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game gets hate for good reason. That reason is because regardless of fun the ability system, which it is, or nostalgia of 16-bit RPG's, and a decent story., these developers have no business sense at ALL. Every future developer can learn what not to do, although it should be common sense.


If you go out and ask for money, raise money, and those people invest in you, and then you treat the game like a hobby, instead of a business, of course people are going to get pissed.

In my estimation, these developers signed their death warrant in the game industry, whether this game gets released or not.


Let's talk about another baffling business decision they made shall we?


They lost a LOT of people when they posted on their devBlog about how so many people loved "arcade mode" big f'ing red flag here.

They spent over 8 months enhancing a side quest? For real? I don't understand the logic at all. You don't have that privilege as a small Indie developer to take that kind of risk, especially because the story isn't bad, it's decently written, and interesting.


Not only did they spend that time, they full fleshed out the side quest before finishing the game.. Again, just baffling decision making devs. It's honestly laughable.

What in the world would drive them to make this decision, regardless of what the data told them? That's if they look at any data.


When you constantly post on your devBlog that development stops because someone got sick, and it happens multiple times, not one person had a big enough brain to say we need help?


Not to mention just because someone gets sick doesn't mean development stops. It means you continue to work and fill in the gaps as you go. Is it a pain in the @$$, yea it sure is, but if you really wanted to deliver this game, that's what would have needed to happen.


8 years into development creating a game at most with that size of a team should take no more than 3-4 years tops.

If you are in the business world and you aren't willing to sacrifice everything to fulfill your dream and bring happiness to others, then get the f*%k out!.



They wasted money and time. Time can never be restored, and if screw with an investors money, you lost any chance of creating a successful business, because nobody trusts you anymore.


They f*$%ed around, treated it like hobby, and its very obvious that's how they treated it. I can't recommend anyone give their money away to a development team that doesn't care whether the game gets completed or not.


Had I treated my business this way, I'd be in the same spot these devs are, but I used my money as an actual investment into myself and my business.


And if the devs are reading this I was a loyal fan, til you f*c&ed around.


This is what happens when you have no business sense and clear path to victory. They made really bad decisions, and this is the result of those bad decisions. Unfortunately for them, these decisions can't be recovered from.



This is a lesson that just because you have the ability to make a game, it requires more skills than this to create a game.



It's honestly a joke that they are this far into development. If this was a triple A studio they would have nuked the game 3 years ago, it is no longer cost effective to get a return on your investment at that point, because you still have to market the game.


These are the kind of games Steam doesn't want to have on their platform. Early Access is needed for Indie Developers, but when developers abuse the system it's bad for all future developers.\t\t\t\t\t
