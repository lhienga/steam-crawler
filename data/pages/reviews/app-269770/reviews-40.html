\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo it's finally finished. I still wonder the real reasons this game took so long in development; the pixel art is pretty yes, but it's nothing special. The sound and music are on part for most RPGs, again, nothing special. The writing is....it's an rpg I guess? It's predictable and not very memorable, etc. It just all feels very generic.

The biggest issues I have revolve around this being both a game with gameplay obviously styled after Secret of Mana-era games, but also feeling at the same time like "this was a game made in the late 2010s". The controls are incredibly clunky, game controller support is very poorly implemented, and there's so much filler in the leveling system you basically have to force yourself to try other builds knowing it's going to kinda suck. There's a lot of balancing issues and a lot of the talents are just kind of worthless. Also, why did you spend *so* much time on this so-called "Arcade Mode" instead of putting more time into the actual game/writing/story? It really is baffling.

This game feels like it would have been great 10+ years ago, now it just feels like it's full of amateurish ideas by someone who really desperately wanted to make a game from their memories of the SNES days, but just didn't know what made those games special.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you miss Secret of Mana or the several games of that genre on the SNES? Secrets of Grindea oozes with classic SNES action rpg feel and wears its influences proudly. Combat is tight and varied in approaches, giving you not just a choice of options in weapon types that vary melee combat up but also a slew of ranged spells for the inner mage in you.

Pros: Good variety in combat. A varied cast of characters with distinct personalities. Stellar SNES era graphics. Plenty of sidequests and exploration. Authentic SNES era soundtrack. Very engaging storyline.

Cons: I don't know if this is a one person development team but its update schedule indicates it probably is. Don't expect frequent updates although the main storyline seems to be almost done. That's about it, I want more of this game and don't have it yet.

Overall this is the easiest thumbs up I have given so far. Takes me right back to my childhood rediscovering SNES games on an emulator in high school. I first bought it about 5 years ago and it has updated on a slow burner since then but the wait has been more than worth it. Just need that last main quest dungeon to drop.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview made at 16.2 hours on 17/09/21. Played 2 player co-op for context purposes.

This game is an absolute gem in the rough. The screenshots don't do this game justice on the Steam Landing Page, it's absolutely far more charming than shown. It seems like a run of the mill standard LoZ clone with bits of loot and crafting thrown in, but it's far more than that.

First, the high fidelity spritework is an absolutely charming and well-animated throughout the game, plenty of frames per character, the environments are beautiful, every enemy is unique (minus a couple of subspecies [Coloured slimes for example]).

Secondly, the story! I won't spoil much here so you're free to read on, but all the main npcs have a charming, sassy wit about them, and the developers are self aware when it comes to a LOT of the flavour text. It's hilarious right the way through the story, and the arcade story. Item text, item sprites, character development; it's all just, charming and witty. My co-op partner and I are frequently laughing at plot points, lines dropped by npcs, and some choice sprites that are cute as a button, or just bada$$ or creative.

Third, the gameplay!! My GOD the gameplay! It starts off as a generic hack-and-slash, but that's because you don't have any spec points. After a couple of levels, you're free to dumpster points wherever the hell you want. No set builds, no waiting to get through a whole skill tree for the cool spells, just, here is everything you're allowed to use in the game from level 2, go nuts. Spells obviously get stronger and gain more functionality the more points you put into them, but you're allowed to be whatever you want, whenever you want, and there are passive perks designed to support that. You want to be a battlemage that swings a club in between fireballs? Go nuts. You want to be a turret caster or minionmancer that hides from the sidelines commanding their minions? Sure. You want to be a flurry berserker that just mashes whirlwind until their healthbars are gone, or yours is? Shoot for the stars you lunatic. You can respec whenever the hell you want, on the fly, for a small gold fee. Get a new weapon or accessory that syncs better with another spec? Change it right now and try it out! The typical types of skill tree are here - Spoils - 1 Handed, 2 Handed, Bow, Ice, Fire, Earth and Wind magic, and Utility for support/buffs/debuffs

Puzzles!! The story is just breathtaking. You don't spend an inordinate amount of time in the same area, a couple of hours to 100% an area and move on. The story changes drastically every movement, and the puzzles are JUST the right amount of difficult that you don't just breeze through them, but you don't need to tab out to Google either. The puzzle types change DRASTICALLY per area and really keep you on your toes, and they are truly so enjoyable. Hell, even the fishing isn't intolerable like it can be in some games. Get to a new area, fish for 8~ish minutes to catch one of everything in that area, and leave. The fishing minigame is sort of an offshoot of Stardew Valley's mechanics, but horizontally and you just follow it with your joystick or left and right keys, with none of the bounce-tapping, just tracking; with the bar being smaller and the fish being more erratic for the better fish.

The loot! My god the loot. It's a collectors wet dream. There is gear for everything and every build. There is also cosmetic slots for everything except armour (Which is just, dye your model whenever you want 3 colours) so you don't have to pick between better stat stick, and fashion-souls. There is a dossier for every single enemy, so you can track for completions sake, just about EVERY SINGLE ENEMY drops a piece of gear or 3, and they ALL drops crafting materials, for either upgrading gear, creating new gear, or buffing your cosmetic pet (which is a permanent stat stick that buffs you and you can nickname and change the colour of, though it's non-combative).:snowflake_ar: The drop rates are very reasonable for everything EXCEPT the cards, which are permanent perks that either add a unique passive, or are just a stat buff. But there is a catch there! The cards are the best collectables in the game, so you're going to want to stop and smell the roses and farm them a bit, but it's not a bad grind because of the mechanics the dev introduced. Not only do the cards have a pity timer that increases their drop rate exponentially as you farm the same mob, the best part is that, when you stop and kill a monster a couple of times in the same area, the set spawns recognise that you're grinding there, and shoot the spawnrate THROUGH THE ROOF! The 2-3 adds you were farming on a 10 second timer, because half a dozen, then 10 at a time, and just continually respawn rapidly, causing a chaos fest with lots of jingly-endorphin/dopamine-collection-noises and visuals that make it hard asf to tear yourself away from the action, even when you've collected 100% of the drops in that pack. It's an absolute blessing of a system that has no right to be as useful as it is.

Now, the bad stuff, if you've been looking for some. There are a couple of bugs that are slightly annoying for the joined friend(s) of the host. We have had teleport pads that disappear for no real reason, soft-locking the characters and requiring a portal back to the start of the dungeon (which you can do anywhere so you just have a 15 second walk back to where you were).
A few crashes for the joined players based on some story-warp nodes, which require the game to be booted again.
And the fact that, this game is best played with a controller, its a bit overwhelming to play on a keyboard unless you like having 12 hotkeys for your spells, but to use a controller at ALL, you need to be in BIG PICTURE MODE, which sucks cause Steam is a bit funky with tabbing out, and replying to messages in BPM.
That's all the qualms I have with the game so far. Nothing game breaking at all.

This game is 100% worth buying for full price, is an absolute pleasure to play with friends, and alone. There is the main story that's long and a lot of fun, and the Binding of Isaac style Arcade mode which is a rogue-like crawler mode with loads of unlockables and is great for testing builds, as well as it's own little story and world building.
I know this game is old, but it is definitely worth picking up with a friend in 2021 and playing the whole way through.

Hope you enjoyed this review! Thank you for reading if you made it this far, and good luck with your runs Collector!\t\t\t\t\t
