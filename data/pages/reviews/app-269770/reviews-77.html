\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9/10. The breakdown:

PROs:
-Aesthetic of Secret of Mana, but plays more like a Link to the Past using abilities instead of items. Good range of abilities to provide single-target, AoE, line attacks, mobility, etc. Remember how, in Secret of Mana, charge attacks were essentially useless because of how slow you moved and how long the charge took, only to hit an enemy once? Charging is SUPER fast here and you can up your move speed, making it a natural choice.
-Build variety. 1H, 2H, and 4 domains of magic make a lot of viable pure or hybrid builds. What's more, respeccing is esy enough to let you experiment without being TOO easy (there's a "mom" joke in there somewhere), and sometimes you'll respec for a hard fight where some abilities clearly outshine others.
-I was not surprised for how good the story is. I'm talking just under Chronotrigger levels of good. There are some hilarious conversations, too.
-Difficulty scaling. Enemies and the first few bosses were a cakewalk for my 1H Charger, but it gets progressively more challenging, with the last few bosses really holding my feet to the fire.
-Puzzles are cleverly designed. They're fun, and they're challenging without making me want to punch a baby.
-Multiplayer + two game modes (Story and Arcade, the latter of which is a "Clear each floor and get as far as you can" mode that saves progress), giving it pretty good replayability.
-Crafting is useful to stay geared, but not required. Ingredients and recipes are tracked conveniently.
-Devs are slowly but consistently rolling out content.

CONs:
-Cards. On the one hand, the passive bonuses are good enough that you can't pass up collecting them without hamstringing yourself, and you only need one from each monster. On the other hand, drop rates are SO. LOW. And spawn rates on certain enemies compound the problem. Killing one enemy type 500+ times for their card were the only times I got legit bored.
-Small gripe: on a gamepad, 2 of the 4 buttons most easily accessible in combat are locked with Basic Attack and Shield Block. Since my build and playstyle rarely used them past the beginning, locking those two buttons out made combat unnecessarily more cumbersome. Maybe allow players to reassign those moves to other keys? If I want to risk having my spine removed and used as a pitching wedge by my enemies because I got cornered with no Energy left at the worst possible time, that is MY decision.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't a game anymore, it's a case study into how indie developers with a lot of talent can drag development on for 3-4 years and still have people writing positive reviews. Secrets if Grindea is a great game, with good combat, skill trees, amazing story, puzzle solving and even the grinding is fun. Never have I said since Runescape when my young child brain was dazzled by mining iron and coal that I had fun grinding. The only negative thing I will say about the gameplay is that crafting is a little stale, you make armor and weapons as far as I remember. There may be chair building mechanics (furnishing a house).

However, it's been 3 years today from the release date in 2015 (although I've read 2014 in some places) and the development has been abysmally slow, but the game will probably be finished. Just not this year. If you have expectations like I did about early access where the game is usually finished within a year or less, do not buy this game. I know they warn about early access and that the release date is tbd because they want to push a high quality product out, which I respect in some ways. But where is the line for early access? 20 years of development before it's finished, where some people who originally bought the game are dead and Steam is now a VR implant? That's the question you have to ask yourself before you buy this game.

Despite the nitpicking about its release I'll give it a positive review because it is a really good game (and the arcade mode is enjoyable too for people who want to hone their skills in a roguelike) but also because it taught me a relatively inexpensive lesson about early access games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is one of the best gaming experiences I've had in my entire life, and you know it must be good because I have never written a review for a game in my entire life I just want to make sure everyone knows about this amazing game. It is however in beta which kind of sucks since I can't play the whole thing, but regardless this game has already been so good and enjoyable anyways. That's the only bad thing about this game in my opinion the game isn't finished yet so we don’t really know if a lot of the things will change and make the game worse, but I would like to list some of my favorite things about this game (get ready it's a long list).

- The new arcade mode, I won't lie I’ve spent a total of my past week just playing this revamped game mode and it is so fun they made a lot of tweaks to make this whole other entire section of the game that has so much replay ability and so many options and fun things to explore like being able to make your run extremely difficult, giving the player a multitude of fun, and challenging quests that are never boring nor tedious. You also get this sense of accomplishment from completing and building your town up from this barren piece of land into a thriving lively town. It is also a breath of fresh air from the story mode for it gives you unique bosses, and mechanics. This arcade mode has brought out a new feeling I've never felt in my entire life of playing videos, and the crazy part is it's not even the whole game it just feels like it could be XD.
- Now onto the soundtrack. and just let me say OMG the soundtrack is so catchy it makes you feel good and completes the game (one of my favorite video game soundtracks ever!!)
- Onto the graphics and visuals, they are so amazing the art style is so charming and creates an amazing look to the game
- Now onto to combat, many games that are centered around grinding always get this aspect wrong you have to make the grinding fun and enjoyable and this is exactly what this game does when you are fighting each and every spell, attack, or maneuver feels good, looks good, and is fun to do , not only that but the fighting is engaging when fighting enemies each is unique having their own attacks and abilities you must dodge and learn to be able to take them down effectively, and they all fit their surrounding areas and each other, and at times some enemies are just annoying and make you want to lose your mind but it's even more satisfying when you make them pop out of existence, not only that but when each enemy dies the animations and sounds are so satisfying you don't get bored killing them making grinding jumping pumpkins for an hour an enjoyable experience and not a grueling task.
- Now onto character creation, you get quite a hefty amount of choices for making your own personal character and it's quite great (no sliders though so you can make abominations D:)
-and on the topic of your character onto classes and the such, this game gives you so many options SO MANY OPTIONS, I have made at least 10 different ideas for characters and classes I wanted to try like a plant growing wizard whom summons earth to do his bidding or a mage warrior mix, or a quick little sword boy who'll summon shadows to mess your face up, the point is you can make any class you want.
-Another worthy point to mention is that there are so many options you can get overwhelmed at times which in a weird way is good to me because I love trying to figure out which abilities to invest in to make my character the best he/she can be
- There is also quite a lot of items and equipment you can obtain in this game some have their own special little affects and others just look amazing when you wield them (good news there's costume slots so you can have your favorite weapon skin but get the amazing stats of another weapon at the same time :D)
- At times getting the one item you need can be very, very tedious, but this is also a good thing because it brings forth this amazing sense of accomplishment.
-They made the fast travel mechanics work quite well in this game they made them in places where it's convenient to go to but don't pull a Skyrim they are in areas where you will still need to walk to places and enjoy the scenery of the world plus you don’t even really need to fast travel (for one exception the floating fortress in the sky) , Also traveling from place to place is very fun because you never know what you're going to find and there are interactive puzzles and things to intrigue you along the way such as puzzles to obtain secret items, hidden sneaky caves, or treasure chest on hills you don’t know how to reach each and every area has engaging fun things to explore as you travel .
-the menus work and function extremely well
-you can tame pets and have cute things follow you around :D, and the minigame to catch them are fun and rewarding.
-I also love, love, LOVE to death every single one of the quests and side quests they made for this game up to this point there are so many funny little jokes whether it be in the text people say or just the names of characters it's all just fantastic, there's a sense of humor here and I appreciate it.
- not only are the quests amazing but so are just normal NPCs around villages they have a lot of funny lines and are enjoyable to seek out and talk to not to mention a lot of the characters are jokes and references to internet culture and have made me chuckle quite a few times
- animations for each and every character item, and enemy, you name are amazing.
- Now this is like absolutely one of my favorite parts of this game the talking bag of holding which is an actual explanation for why you can hold everything in the world not only that this character is amazing and I love him he will criticize you in a good way and is funny and enjoyable to adventure with.
- Also, each and every one of the areas in and around Grindea are unique from each other making it quite fun to explore them
- Also, I found it quite fun you can customize your own little house and they make a good explanation as to why it can be 40 times bigger on the inside then the outside.
-The developers also give a weekly update which is quite amazing allowing you to see the progress on your new favorite game I find that really cool shout outs to the developers, you guys are amazing :D
- And finally, this game at it’s current state is worth any $ amount they would ask for and that is something noteworthy for a game that is only in beta.



I seriously Love this game I can't even believe it's in early access honestly I recommend it highly to anyone and everyone no matter who you are.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo put it in simple terms, the game is extraordinary!
In more detail, the game is an amazingly fun time to play whether you want it for the interesting story or the challengeing roguelike mode (Arcade Mode), and it can be enjoyed with up to 3 friends connecting via Steam or IP, and it is very simple to do. It has many side-quests and items to collect, but don't let the name fool you! The game is not solely about grinding, and the grinding aspect ties in more with the story because you can learn to defeat every enemy and boss without grinding as long as you learn their attack patterns. If you still have doubts about the game, download the demo, which is free, and play the beginning to see whether you like it or not, but if you don't do that and you hate unfinished games, do not buy it until it is a full game because both the story and arcade mode are not fully finished, but story mode in it's current state will take over 10 hours if you play it for the story, but much longer if you want every item in story mode, and not to mention the fact that arcade mode has unlimited replayability (hence my growing 500+ hours).

■ If you ever need help with anything or want to play with other people in the community, feel free to ask in the discord: https://discord.gg/secretsofgrindea
■ Constant updates on the game: http://www.vilyaroo.com/
■ The game's website: http://www.secretsofgrindea.com/
■ The forums: http://secretsofgrindea.com/forum/index.php

---PROS---

■ The game has a beautifully crafted over-arching story
■ Very entertaining and rewarding side-quests
■ Non-existent skill trees because you can use practically anything you want with anything else
■ A load of items to pick and choose from to create your perfect character either cosmetically, for battle, or both
■ A phenomenal soundtrack
■ Loads of in-game easter eggs to find
■ A huge roster of different enemies and bosses to fight
■ A great combat system that allows the use of timing to your advantage, so learning attack patterns is important
■ An amazing cast of side characters
■ If you're looking for more of a challenge, Arcade mode is a rougelike variation of the game with difficult gameplay to test your skills
■ It has some fun and rewarding puzzles that are not too difficult
■ There are practically no bugs at all in the stable version of the game, and any bugs (that I have found) were never game-breaking
■ There is also a very responsive developer team who will usually address pressing issues fairly quickly (the best place to report issues is the discord: https://discord.gg/secretsofgrindea)
■ The developers post updates on the game very frequently on their dev blog: http://www.vilyaroo.com/
■ The community is a great place to ask questions, report bugs, give feedback, and find players for fun in Co-op: https://discord.gg/secretsofgrindea

---Cons---

■ It takes a while to develop such a polished game!
■ Certain skills are not balanced properly as of yet (though this only matters in arcade mode)\t\t\t\t\t
