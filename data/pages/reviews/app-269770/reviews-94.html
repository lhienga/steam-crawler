Would I reccomend this game? Definitely. Is this review biased? Definitely. Does that matter? Well yes, but, you should buy this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots, lots of potential. Beautiful art-style; it's rare to see such a breathtaking view when playing games with this sort of art-style but it really works here. The music is also serene and arcade-y. Will bring mad nostalgia to 80s and 90s kids, and is an exemplary game for younger audience who want to understand the value of classic games when it's executed well.

It is however in early access and is still unfinished, albeit well polished. I do believe it sill has a long way to go, as it's not just missing a few items, but missing whole skill trees. There's also not much NPC interaction, and the combat can get stale if you're grinding.

As you can see, I don't have that many hours in this game so I don't blame you if you don't believe me. I do think it's a good game, but it needs to really pick up and hook the audience if it expects a complete playthrough. The combat in itself is not varied and interesting enough to be the main reason to play this game. Even the art-style, arguably the game's strongest point, is the kind of art-style that will illicit "wow"s and "woo"s and "cool"s and "nice"s, but it's not going to keep gamers coming back. The devs need to work on the longevity of the game, but it has undoubtedly a very strong first impression.

I'm always very cautious when reviewing early access games as I'm not sure whether to review the current build or the final product (which one can only guess).

Current build: 72.5/100
If they manage to deliver everything as promised: 75/100
If they deliver everything as promised and fine tune the combat, add more loot and interactable NPCS: 80/100
