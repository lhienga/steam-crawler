\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am unhealthily obsessed with NSO. I had previously seen trailers for this game pre-release and knowing I would absolutely adore this game. Upon release, I instantly bought it.

Needy Streamer Overload, is an insane management game. The thing you're managing is your mentally-ill girlfriend who has two core identities; Ame, the girl you knew before fame, and K-Angel, the internet angel you help her transform into on social media.

The aesthetics of this game are absolutely gorgeous, with very silly music in the background that often contrasts the experience you're getting on screen, and the fantastic pixel art and animations across your strange "windose" screen whilst you run your girlfriend's social media presence. Throughout taking charge over her online career entirely, there are many interesting gameplay fields; The typical stat-raiser (or stat-decreaser if that's what you want), managing her mental health through various activities, and the actual creation of various media for her online following.

Be warned, this game tests your morality or lack-thereof. You COULD help your girlfriend reach fame in a relatively healthy manner, or lead her into various (and plenty) of very horrible paths as her manager, "P-chan". This game plays off of much irony, and dark content shocks through how de-stabilising mental health can be to one person's life.

Enjoy with caution!\t\t\t\t\t

Plot - empty and linear. Who came here for the plot, character development, empathy - just pass by, there is no such things in the game.

Music - on a fan, someone will like it, someone will not. For me personally - lame.

Graphics - I really liked the style. I think it - and the theme of the game - is actually what attracted people to the game.

Bugs - when you play for the first time, you don't notice them. BUT! If you want to get a different ending or get the desired achievement - bugs will interfere with you and force you to roll back a few days and replay. That's a bad thing.

Visual effects - beware: some effects (like pills and weed) can make you feel achy or dizzy, and I don't envy epileptics at all.

Special warning to sensitive people. Are there any good endings to day 30 here at all? All day-30 endings apparently exist to make the player feel miserable. I was expecting at least one good ending where they live together happily ever after day 30. The game apparently exists to just drive the current 30th (last playable) day endings into ennui, sadness, and melancholy.

Experienced another disappointment from an overhyped promising game.
