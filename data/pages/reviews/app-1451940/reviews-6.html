\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti wanted to wait to write a review on the game until i 100% completed the game and i just have to say it's one of my favorite games now.

ame as a character is so well-written and terrifyingly relatable in some aspects. i love that the game doesn't shy away from its more serious themes and shows how ugly mental illness can truly be.

a lot of ame's emotions i have felt to some extent in the past, and being able to relate to a character like her (especially if you seek validation from the internet) is definitely a sign that you do need to seek help so you can heal, but i already was aware of that (and i have been trying to get help!). with all of that, ame (for me) just proves i'm not alone in my struggles. it makes me feel seen and makes me want to strive to be better, genuinely

this game has been monumental in my healing process for something i won't disclose. it's been very eye opening about how truly awful some of my coping mechanisms have been and that i need to do better and be kinder to myself

if you can stomach themes this heavy, i do strongly recommend the game. definitely do take breaks between playthroughs, because it's certainly a lot. i've had this game since october and i only just now completed it, nearly 6 months later! it's tough to get through at times. be patient with yourself and take care of yourself too!

i very easily could've just been like "she just like me fr" with this review and initially i was going to do that but to be honest with how important it's become to me, i'd honestly feel like i'm doing a disservice if i just went and said that and nothing more

if you relate to ame, hang in there. you're gonna be okay <3\t\t\t\t\t
