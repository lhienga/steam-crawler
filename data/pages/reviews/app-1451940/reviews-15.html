Quick Content Warning for the discussion of serious topics like mental illness and mentions of self harm.
___________
This game demonstrates a lot of aspects rarely touched upon in Western media in a satire lens that makes it easy to approach. As a person suffering from severe mental illness, I enjoyed and related to many themes very much.

Ame is representation of many "taboo" symptoms of mental illness. Her K-Angel persona demonstrates many things associated with identity disturbance and very low self-esteem; she illustrates the urge to morph ones entire being to suit another. The relationship between Ame & K-Angel also demonstrates something rarely discussed - the dependence many mentally ill people have on social media.

Social media addiction is common for any group of people due to the constant dopamine boosts we receive. I feel like this issue is incredibly prevalent with streamers in general from what I've seen, so K-Angel being a streamer makes a lot of sense. Mental illness can amplify this severely, however. Many mental illnesses will inhibit an individual's ability to socialise & have relationships in person. This leads them to rely on the web for that social connection. This allows them to get drawn closer and closer to the life they've built in their screen, leaving them to crave life from within the glass.

Being able to see these themes demonstrated in such a lighthearted sense made me feel okay to recognise that I was not okay. I personally played this game a day after I was released out of the psych ward (for a self-death attempt), so I was really drawn to K-Angel & Ame's character. They highlighted the dark inner thoughts I've constantly suppressed about the world.

Her actions made me reflect on my actions and understand why my past relationships have failed, and that my behaviour was dangerous for both myself and other parties. It made me realise that Ame was a character I should aim to not be, and that codependency was toxic for everyone. Ame is sort of an alternate version of myself in my mind - she's my bad ending.

In a way, this game has really encouraged me to get help. It allowed me to illustrate & express my emotions, leading me to reflect and grow. Nearly a year and a half after my play through, I have a DBT intake session in 4 days and I've finally been able to write down my feelings without throwing up. The distance between my life and Ame's reminds me that it will get better :)

I believe I will always adore Ame's character, but from a distance. She will always stay out of her screen and I will mine.
