\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is something. My friend says it is "very sadistic" for a game. I tend to agree. By being so it completes its intended purpose and explores streamers being "terminally online".

The game has a very interesting UI - Windows 98/95 coupled with the pixelated artstyle, sound and music. I love this.

Gameplaywise it is a management game. You are the manager of a streamer that you live together with. You have to 30 days to complete the game and there are quite a few endings. None of them "good" honestly. And that the endings are the reason my firend says it is "very sadistic".

The biggest plot twist is not that huge of a twist that you can subtily figure out is that the manager you play at is actually Ame, which means you are Ame. And by being her alterego you are actually her defense mechanism. The true ending is her leaving you, AKA finally accepting that she doesn't need help - she can finally do things by herself and be "free"

Good game but other than getting all the endings it is not something worth replaying when the shock factor dies down. You practically know what is going to happen if you have two working brain cells. So get it on sale just to experience the insanisty if you are curious - mostly because of the artstyle and execution, not the story.\t\t\t\t\t
