\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I got this game, I saw a review saying that this was a depressing Tamagotchi. While they were correct, I can't help but feel like this game was so much more than a Tamagotchi. On the surface, the game is about an online streamer (OMGKawaiiAngel/Ame) who you help, as her producer and lover, to reach 1,000,000 followers before you reach day 30. But the game is becomes so much deeper than that. Ame is a girl who has, based on my assumption, Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD), and maybe Dependent Personality Disorder. This becomes clear right of the bat when she has you control nearly every aspect of her life, and craves for other peoples validation. This becomes more apparent when you start to unlock the different endings. In the end, once you unlock all endings, a new save spot opens up. Once you open this save, Ame exclaims that she wants to try something, and begins to do things on her own. She realizes that she is able to support herself, and with a bittersweet goodbye, leaves to become her own person. The reason I enjoyed this game so much is because not only is it complex on many different levels, but I also relate to Ame. While I don't have BPD, I still connect with a lot of things she struggles with, depression and self-harm being the two major ones. This game has helped people understand BPD a lot better, and to that I applaud. I feel however that people have been getting the wrong message while playing this game and have sexualized this mental illness, which is terrible. With that being said, that has nothing to do with the original purpose of the game, which is why I recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
