\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay is similiar to Tamagotchi, basically a virtual creature on a small device where users take care of its needs to keep it happy and healthy, using buttons to interact with the virtual pet. In this game you play as P-chan the SO of Ame our FMC. Ame's goal is to reach one million subscribers within 30 days to satisfy her desperate need for approval. Everyday the player decides what activities Ame-chan does in order to gain more ideas for streams, which will affect the three stats: stress, affection and mental darkness. Each day is split in three stages: noon, dusk and night. Ame-chan can only stream at night, during her livestreams, you have the role of a moderator tasked with deleting inappropriate comments or highlight certain comments for Ame to read. Most activities will take up 1 or 2 stages. Your decisions and stats will affect the ending you will receive (27 endings atm).

In a nutshell, Needy Streamer Overload is a game about managing the mental health of an unstable, depressed female streamer who's aware that all she has going for her are her looks. Whether she becomes a succesful streamer or not is all up to the player.

Overall, It's an amazing ADV game though it can be disturbing sometimes. I enjoyed the game from the beginning to the end, despite it being tedious to get certain endings. Experimenting around to see what happens is a fun experience. The 8 bit BGM is great, the pixel art is lovely and the English translation was also well done, the comments look similiar to the ones you usually see in the streaming comment sections. I rate the game a solid 8.5/10, I would recommend it for people who enjoy playing visual novels, psychological horror games or people who like mentally ill women.

This fun little visual novel satirizes contemporary internet culture, addiction, and relationships - both parasocial and physical! - to an uncannily good level. The more time you spend online, the harder this game will hit you. Oh, and if you've ever been with a clingy girl? Even more so.

Though the game can get quite repetitive in the search for the final ending, it's a tall order for a visual novel not to feel that way eventually. The developers have taken strides to make the grind as palatable as possible, and their efforts ensure that the game doesn't remain boring for very long.

To summarize, the game is funny, heartwarming.. and even scary too, at times. Its graphics, sounds and overall presentation are charming and are definitely enough to suck you into the game's premise, and leave you with food for thought whenever you get a new ending.

"I can fix her."\t\t\t\t\t
