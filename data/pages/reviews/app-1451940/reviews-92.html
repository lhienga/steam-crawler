\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy favorite visual novel I've ever had my hands on.
Nope, dead serious. It knocked itself out of the park, orbited around it, and went to space. That's how good it was.
I already knew twists and turns before I could finish this game, because the only fault it had to me was the fact that it had certain areas that need fixing. Mostly in the coding department. Fortunately? Starting on Day 3 after beating saves fixed the issue I had. Day 2 just has a problem rendering Ame's messages sometimes, resulting in a softlock.

But other than that bug? This mentally ill streamer may have suffered a lot, but I really enjoyed the narrative on the way to each ending, and I felt actively engaged and even had to take long moments to think and plan what I was going to do to balance out Ame's stats and go to an ending I wanted to, or do something completely out of left field and just go nuts to see what I got. It was a genuinely fun time, even if the narrative took more depressing drops. I never thought it was that boring, and this game ranks pretty high up for me thanks to all it provided.

In short though, if you don't mind sensitive topics, you should absolutely pick up this game if you have ANY intrigue in it whatsoever. I was interested thanks to a friend, and I don't regret checking out this precious game. It was wonderful, and a really good example of mixing narrative and even some gameplay into a very fun and strategic experience. If you do check it out, may you have a fun time with it!
