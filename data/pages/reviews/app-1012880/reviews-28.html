\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn theory, the appeal of a fully text based roguelike would be the sheer variety you could put into the game. 60 Seconds.... doesn't have this at all. After like 2 runs, things get incredibly repetitive, and the things being repeated aren't even interesting either. It's 90% complete non-choices you'd have no reason not to say yes to, with occasional choices that do literally nothing. You maybe make like one or two actual meaningful decisions in a whole run.

The game truly just feels like slamming your head into a brick wall hoping you luck out and have the exact combination of random items required for this run and that none of them break.

Funnily enough the best part of the game probably is the 60 seconds of looting your house at the start, since at least there's some variety in positions of things. It's still incredibly bare bones though.\t\t\t\t\t
