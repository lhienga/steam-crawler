\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe initial setup, the setting, the concept, and even the game menu, are extremely fun.
This means your first 5 minutes are excellent.
Gathering whatever you can in 60 seconds, this is also a fun concept. I work on similar games and I like that.
It was great to play it with my 9 year old.

And sadly, this exactly expresses the level at which the game keeps its "surprise and drive" to retry:
it works only if you have extremely low expectations regarding how "dynamic" your experience will be.

If you want something very expected, very repetitive, with mostly the same events in every game, decisions you lack control on, consequences randomly determined, and characters not deep and that you can't really like, it's ok.

Otherwise I don't recommend it.

Honestly I don't understand very well the hype because of how it really plays out.
I can understand how "attractive" the game initially is because of its fun concept, 100 percent.
Also we still have a mysterious drive to see if we will succeed.

But you would have seen most of what the game gives in the first half hour.
Even in a few games, such as three, you'll have the same events several times!!

The game really lacks more comprehensive events, mechanics, or interaction.
Every day...

- "if" you have food and water left, you decide "if" you give food / water (yes/no) and to who,
- "if" an event comes, you decide "if" you send someone (yes/no) and who you send
- and if no event comes and your people are healthy, you can decide to send someone out

And that's about it.
giving food, or giving water, event?, sending someone? done.
the rest is clicking next day.

With a lack of events, a lack of mechanics I would find this proper for a cheaper price than 10 dollars: such as 3 or 4. At this price, you get something nice enough in terms of game duration and experience.

I kept the game because my children like the concept!
But for a 10 dollars games I feel it's a lot more difficult to recommend.

I may still try the other iterations, because marketing+art style are enough to make a sale, so I guess it proves that nowadays only a good intro is enough because it pleases the kids. For myself, giving a good experience to my kids is good enough. But what about all the others?

I have really tried to give a honest feedback about it.
Thanks for reading!\t\t\t\t\t
