\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce you get past the admittedly cool and retro aesthetic, this game offers precious little to the consumer.
The premise is cool, you have only 60 seconds to grab whatever resources you can from your house until you need to lock yourself up in your bunker. However, the gameplay once you get into said bunker is pretty poor and illogical.

I'll give an example, your bunker might occasionally flood, and you need to make a choice about which item you will save. In principle, this game event is fine, but it is made incredibly dumb when you have 2/3 fit and healthy people in the bunker when the event triggers and you only have the ability to save one item out of a potential 3. This is plain bad game design. Having more than one person in the bunker is a huge drain on food/water/medical supplies so I should be able to save EVERY SINGLE ITEM when a flooding/fire event happens in the bunker. DOES TIMMY NOT OWN HANDS?????

I like the art style and the flavour text, but I feel no stakes when I am making decisions, I just throw a random die in my head and go with the result. This is not a game like Papers Please or Frostpunk or XCOM when you could be agonising over a decision for minutes on end. This game does not lend itself to high stakes decision making, it is too casual in nature. Feel free to disagree with this take btw.

It is also mad how you don't get a choice in where to send people off to expeditions which again is very illogical in my mind for this type of game.\t\t\t\t\t
