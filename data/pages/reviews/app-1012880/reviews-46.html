\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember playing this game a few years ago, back before the Reatomized version existed. Maybe it’s just me, but it feels a lot more difficult than it should be. And I’ll admit it, I’m awful at this game. The premise is you’re given around a minute to collect supplies and family members before a bomb drops and must hold up in a fallout shelter until help arrives. Before that you have to decide how to ration supplies, who should go out, and what messages to respond to outside the shelter. The writing is utterly hilarious, satirical, and the only reason I played this game as long as I did. Each in-game day you’re given updates on the family’s mood, hunger, and new developments that require choices. The most important choices being who to send out when and whether to let someone in. With chance playing a huge part every option to go out can be the last choice you make or the first step in getting help.

That’s where the difficulties become relevant. At lower difficulties you’re given more supplies to start and greater luck in encounters with others. Now maybe I just have the worst intuition known to man, but I always have such terrible luck in this game. Even on easy difficulty I was having trouble staying alive until the military arrived. With how many supplies there are it’s far too easy to be utterly helpless because you’re missing a single tool, or it broke when you decided to send someone out. 60 Seconds is like a series of endless unfortunate events. Every time I make a choice it goes as badly as possible, leaving my poor McDoodle family a poor broken husk when they wither away on day 60. Okay fine, it’s meant to be replayed over and over and each session is only around an hour. The problem is the game is super repetitive. Situations will repeat often, making each playthrough relatively the same. It’s like second verse, same as the first in digital form. It’s a fine game, but one I would recommend tenuously as not ideal for binging\t\t\t\t\t

The Story:

The story of 60 Seconds Reatomized isn't rocket science (ironically.) A nuke goes off but you've already prepared for this very scenario by installing a bunker in your home. Your objective is to ration and utilise everything you were able to gather to survive long enough for the military to rescue you.

The Gameplay:

The gameplay can be broken into two segments. The start of the run and the rest of the run. The start of the run is very different from the main bulk of the gameplay and has you gathering all the resources (and family members) you can within one minute before the nuke goes off. You run around your home in an isometric (top-down) kind of way collecting food, water and other things of value such as a weapon and gas mask before jumping into the bunker with your resources.

The second half of the gameplay will likely take up the bulk and is not nearly as intensive as that opening minute. You play using only your mouse from this point. Each passing day you're forced to make decisions that will directly help or hinder your run like rationing food. Answering the door to traders or raiders. Going on an expedition for resources or not as well as other things.

Some Pros:
- Despite the simplicity, the execution is brilliant.
- Plenty of randomness that creates replay value.
- Enjoyable dark humour.
- Can run on older hardware smoothly.
- Very casual experience ideal for restful evenings.

Some Cons:
- Slightly overpriced.
- Prioritising obvious supplies can likely make some scenarios more frequent than others.
- Length of run is subject to some RNG of the spawn locations of supplies. Sometimes they're quick to access, sometimes not.

Conclusion:

60 Seconds Reatomized is a solid game that firmly stands its ground as what it sets out to be. Not as a parody, or a cheap knockoff or a fan game. It's a unique indie game that's simple to play but clearly designed with plenty of depth and thoughtfulness. Although perhaps a bit expensive, I wholeheartedly recommend it for the humour alone. Nothing a sale cannot fix.

10/10, will abandon my kids again :D\t\t\t\t\t
