This game would be the perfect candidate for the ultimate Fallout backstory as you have nothing else to do than collecting supplies in your house during the time limit given by the game as the title explains very straightforwardly and after the bombs fell (assuming that you were able to get into the shelter in time) using the supplies you've just collected to survive as long as you can.
The game has a great sense of humor and even though it gets repetitive after a while because of the somewhat limited amount of events and due to the nature of it's gameplay (not to mention that it's addicting as hell) it's still absolutely fun to play and highly recommended for people who are into these "survival games" or whachamacallthemstuff, but if you'd rather just want to have a great time and have a couple of laughs on something loosely Fallout related instead of being emotionally invested in it, give it a try!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe concept of this game is good, and the art style is solid.
However it is incredibly repetitive and almost entirely based on luck. Aside from the scavenging mini game, there is no skill involved, it's just guessing and memorising the same repeated events over and over again.
The survival challenges in particular are almost impossible, but never because of a difficulty level. They are impossible because if the RNG isn't on your side that round? You die, nothing you can do to stop it. You can get 50 days in, and then suddenly a random event pops up that you have none of the required items for, and boom, game over.
The game is fun for maybe a couple of hours but then it's just endlessly frustrating.
I've put down and then picked up the game a few times now (hence the hours of playtime), but every time it just ends up getting infuriating and unfair.
I wouldn't recommend this game, it's not really a game, it's just rolling dice over and over and hoping you get the numbers you want.\t\t\t\t\t
