\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the many entries in the Total War series that I did not play on release. I don't remember everything but if I had to guess, Empire's lackluster post launch support and the decision to make Napoleon some weird stand alone expansion, turned me off. I of course did check it out years after its release and found an enjoyable game that, like Empire, was under-cooked and not sure what it wanted to be. I also would assume that the game was a continuation of an idea, focusing on a historical person, like the Alexander DLC for Rome.

Napoleon is clearly the focus here. Something like a story campaign exists here following through Napoleons career. The historical battles also loosely follow the historical trajectory of one of the greats military generals of all time. CA also cleaned the game up a bit especially regarding the combat with feels responsive and good to play unlike its predecessor.

But alas even though the game is more focused on a particular point in history, CA unfortunately were not able to completely capitalize on that fact.

Contrary to what some brain dead fans love to proclaim, TW never was a historical accurate and realistic game series. It often tries to be authentic but that is it. And even though the combat plays fine it is also as far removed from actual Napoleonic warfare as was the Egyptian faction in the first Rome from Ptolemaic Egypt. Now, I don't expect TW to be a hardcore simulation but a certain degree of realism and authenticity would have helped the game in its longevity and fun. The battles are to fast and with to many casualties which really screws with the pacing of the various campaigns.

Now lets talk about one of the big issues with the game and that is its scope. That applies to its overall scope recreating Napoleons campaigns and battles and the game world. One prevalent issues regarding all campaigns is the size. The game feels too small and crowded whilst lacking a lot of events. The game is split into three different theaters for its campaigns, Italy, Egypt and Europe. Italy and Egypt were of course certainly smaller ventures than Napoleons fights across Austria, the German States, Poland and Russia. But the first two campaigns are too condensed and lack a lot of important details from history. The big Europe campaign is probably the biggest mess. Omitting a tremendous lot of wars and campaigns from Napoleons life. CA clearly couldn't decide if they wanted to make a Total War game or one focused on Napoleon himself. The whole mess in Spain is relegated to its own DLC for better or for worse. The disastrous invasion of Russia doesn't even exist either as an event or its own start date for the campaign. I am going to stop here before I run out of space but I have to mention one more thing. Even though the Europe map doesn't feel as squished together like it was with Empire, considering how much land mass we lost it does not really feel appropriate what we got in return. You know, issues with campaign length (too short).

The historical battles follow a rough timeline of Napoleons military career but again there is a lot of stuff missing, no Battle of the Nations, no Eylau etc. I think this was also a missed opportunity to make this mode a bit more like a traditional RTS and give the player the option to carry veteran units into the next battle for example.

Same as with Empire the post launch support was abysmal. Unit packs, a separate campaign that should have been already included in the main game, no real big expansion pack fleshing out the game. Again as with its predecessor it feels just lacking.

Is it a good game depicting Napoleonic warfare? No. Is it a good TW game? Maybe. I had fun with it and will return but it also missing so many important things,events, battles and so on. It feels like an experiment,which is fine, but CA never tried to recreate and improve this more focused look on a historical figure and that is just a disappointing and a missed opportunity. I still recommend it because as it is with most TW games, they always tend to be rather good.
