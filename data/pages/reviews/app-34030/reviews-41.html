\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDuring the last few turns of the Italian Campaign: BLITZKRIEG INTO AUSTRIA!!! FRENCH LOGISTICS RULES!!!

During the last turn of the Egypt Campaign: Navel landings and artillery finesse don't lie, in 6 turns I went from having no grasps into Ottoman territory to sending in five of La Grande Arm√©e into the heart of Ottoman cities by both navel and land means, ending the campaign off with Napoleon conquering a Syria territory.
Plus, screening my artillery out from the Ottoman's longer ranged artillery by marching my infantry into battle, then witnessing the Ottoman's last stand of sending in cavalry and squaring my infantry in time just keeps the endorphins pumping.

The music is great and the small details that you see here and there such as the drummers, whistlers, bugle men, and flag bearers marching about makes this game feel alive.

Credits to Darthmod for making the game all the more awesomer
If this is your first Total War: game or thinking about this one to being your second (or more), know that you might not get it on your first, second, third, or dozen battles or campaign restarts, but once you get the hand of it, and you know when you do, then the game will open up to you as well.

TLDR- For my Second entry into the Total War: Trilogy (The first being Shogun 2) it took some learning but once you get the gameplay then the game's action and strategy multiplies your endorphins.
