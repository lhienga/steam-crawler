\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a disappointment.

"Total War: Napoleon" really left me feeling let down. I opted for it over "Empire," hoping for an upgrade – more content, a slicker strategic map, and epic battles. But nope, it fell short.

Trying to strategize on the big map? Good luck. Everything blends together. Everything looks the same, whether you're glancing at the overall picture or diving into battle. And as you hit the early mid-game, each battle feels like déjà vu. Specialized troops don't seem to change much. Even on lower difficulties 50/50 Auto-Battle are no coin toss at all, with the enemy winning way too often. So, you end up stuck fighting battles manually, obliterating enemy armies, scratching your head at why the game thinks this is a 50/50. If you're facing more than one enemy (which is pretty much a given), get ready for a marathon of battles. It gets exhausting real quick. Plus, there's this "Infrastructure" column in the building browser just for "Roads." Why not integrate it into the regular building browser? It's a bit odd.

The game just feels off, like they rushed it or something. The soundtracks are a bit on the gloomy side – not exactly uplifting. I get it's a Napoleon game, but a bit more positive music wouldn't hurt. Honestly, now I wish I'd gone for "Empire" instead. But with the similarities and that same funky reinforcement system I rather go back to "Rome".\t\t\t\t\t
