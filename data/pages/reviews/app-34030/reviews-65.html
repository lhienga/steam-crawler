\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took me a while to get into Empire and Napoleon, despite being a period buff. Or maybe because of that, as I have high expectations and a keen eye for anachronism and other missteps, small or big.
I'd say Napoleon: TW is a fun, believable historical strategy game with a tighter focus than Empire, with good graphics, sound and gameplay - to this day, the battles look and sound good. Music is suitably epic. I only miss the Napoleonic marching music and drumming. The campaign maps are solid, far smaller and more focused than Empire, with more tightly set goals and win conditions, which in turn create cohesive campaigns. In Empire e.g. France was only 1 province. Northern Italy alone has dozens in regions in the first campaign, and with several nations.
Battles are pretty fast with a low tolerance for mistakes. Cavalry is powerful and canister shot in close range is about as dangerous as historically. Annihilating the opponent tends to open the way for capturing large swaths of territory, with reinforcement mechanics being tied to regions' supply efficiency, rather than flat payments of money, as Empire.
Game runs well on Windows 11, performs and looks good on a 2K monitor. Total War has never been famous for being especially good with gunpowder era armies, but I am happy to say the tactical concepts and early 1800s military logic is well presented in the game: combined arms fighting works well and unit balance from what I've played is solid.

Recommended. Game is definitely worth the discounted price, or full if one has even a faint interest toward the era.\t\t\t\t\t
