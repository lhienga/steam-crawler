\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing a bit of Total War Empire, I have to say this game is better in almost every way. A lot of people call TW Napoleon a "Big DLC" to Empire. The scale of the game is certainly more narrow, but the refinement of mechanics pushes this game far ahead of its older brother. Lets start with what this game does right.

Fixed artillery are no longer in the game which is a fantastic blessing. Starting in empire meant that 100% of your artillery are almost entirely useless, as they can't reposition once the battle begins. Obviously you could do research later down the road and acquire Iber artillery, which can move in battle. Later on, you would research techs that allow for horse artillery. All TW Napoleon does differently is removed fixed artillery, which was redundant in the first place.

Another change to core gameplay is that units can now replenish naturally. In Empire, you would have to pay a fee for the unit to begin regenerating. This created another expense in addition to unit upkeep, which was tedious. This is no longer the case in TW Napoleon.

Generals have also undergone changes by having more influence in the battlefield due to the addition of the Rally ability. Both infantry and Calvary have access to more advanced features. An example of this is the "Light infantry movement" ability, which allows some units to space out their formation and use the environment as cover. I find this to be a very unique mechanic, something that should be implemented in the modern titles.

The graphical quality of the game trumps empire by a bit in terms of the campaign map and battlefields. Artillery are more imposing, their cannons louder, their effectiveness more clear to the player in the forms of flying bodies and debris.

Although the game improves upon so much, it still bares similar flaws to TW Empire. Due to the refusal to change certain key gameplay features, Napoleon was doomed to be one of the last "Gunpowder" total war games. Traces of this era in the franchise can be seen in the TW Warhammer games.

A list of Complaints:

-A distinct lack of Diplomatic features in the game
-I don't think this game has a Coop feature, but I might be thinking of Empire
-No real siege battles involving the cities they take place in. For instance, I may be attacking berlin but you wouldnt know by the battle map. I will be either assaulting some Star-Shaped fortress or attacking what looks like a large village. We have artillery in the game, it would be nice to fight some proper siege battles using them.

Overall I think what holds this game back is the lack of features, but what it does offer is quite good.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe last Total War game I played was the original Rome, which, although 17 years have passed since then, I recall enjoying. I'm interested in this era - the boaty era - of history, and I was looking forward to triumphing in some tense naval battles and forging strong alliances with some inspired diplomacy, BUT, TW Napoleon has taught me that I am, in fact, an imbecile.

I plugged away for almost 4 hours one Friday night after work and I barely made it out of the starting city. If I ventured too far, or did nothing at all, some enemy faction would track me down and pulverise me in about turn 6, leaving me with nothing. I tried to learn, but the pop-ups and menus are myriad, and relentless. War's been declared, a city is rioting, a trade deal's fallen through, someone's died, and the adviser seems to delight in giving the most cursory information they can about each one. "Roads are good for armies" excellent, I shall build a road. "You've run out of money, disband a unit..." ok, disband the unit "the city is rioting!" oh I suppose that's because there's not enough units here now. That's before you get into politics, construction, taxes, and the rest... it's just too complex to dive into. Menus have menus, pop-ups have pop-ups, and I cannot comprehend the matrices behind them.

I felt like that to learn the grand strategy portion of the game I would need to alt + tab between the game and the vast TW Forum every time something happened, for many hours. The game also seems to delight in punishing me. Against the wishes of my ego, I set the difficulty to easy to see if that would create an environment conducive to learning without being spanked every turn, and to my delight I finally trounced an army of Danes. They'd all run off. 7/8ths of my army were standing about doing nothing. I had a search of the battlefield, but find an enemy I could not, so I quit the battle. The game informed me, unacceptably, rudely, with malice, that I'd suffered a crushing defeat. It inflated the numbers so that a huge portion of my good French chaps had dropped dead after the Danes had run off. I was robbed.

Having said that, sometimes it's my fault. I was surveying the field at the outset of a battle and noticed that one of my units was taking damage. I thought enemy cannons had reached my lines - no big deal. When I checked back, a pile of dead Frenchmen lay before my cannons. They'd stood there as the artillery, their countrymen, fired salvo after salvo into their backs. Did it occur to the artillery to stop firing? Non. Did the infantry make any attempt to move from the path of the point blank cannon-fire? Absolument pas. Like the brave, dutiful soldiers they were, they stood there, patiently waiting for me to order the cannons to stop, or at least to give the infantry permission to take a few steps to the side. This did little to make me feel like Napoleon.

I'm going to recommend it still because I can see that there's fun to be had here, I can just see that the game wants 50, 60 hours of my time to get there, and the journey will be rife with ruthless beatings. I just wanted to sink into a Nelson fantasy for a few hours after a stressful week at work, but instead, I sank into the Atlantic - in addition to my arm, I had my testicles removed.

If you've got the time, go for it.\t\t\t\t\t
