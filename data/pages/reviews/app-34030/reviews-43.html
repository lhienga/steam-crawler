\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe WORST AI for the real-time battles of any Total War game. You truly have to micromanage every single unit. This is fine for smaller to mid-sized battles, but when you are besieging Paris or some other epic large-scale battle, the game is practically unplayable.

For larger battles, you will spend more time with the game PAUSED than live, just so you can micromanage every unit to prevent suicide.

Specific examples:

1. Artillery will fire at point-blank range into your own units that are in melee with the enemy if "fire at will" is toggled on. If it is toggled off, it won't attack anything... again... lose-lose without micromanagement.

2. For anyone that doesn't know, the style of warfare in this and Empire are completely different, where your units are almost entirely ranged and stand in a line (XXXXXXXX) An artillery unit will turn and fire directly down that line ---> if any unit is being flanked , destroying entire units, killing hundreds of your own men if you have cannister shot, and possibly losing the match for you.

3. The default movement is "walk" not run. You frequently have to manually click the run button for a unit that you have already commanded to move during the heat of the battle, because they are walking. This is pretty much every time you move... even cavalry charges will sometimes have the charging unit WALK to charge???!!... Again micromanagement.

Naval battles? An absolute micromanagement disaster.


You simply cannot tell a unit to do something and then move on to worry about a different unit.

You have to hold a unit's hand through the entire experience to make sure they do not do something suicidal.

These sort of AI quirks have always been around in TW to an extent, but here it is a disaster because of the style of combat. It's not like the medieval ages, where you command a unit of spearmen to attack and can count on them being tied up for a while in melee... No. Here, because it is entirely (almost) ranged combat, units are always sort of free to do whatever they want and you have to watch all of them very closely.

For example, units that you have commanded to attack an enemy will pursue that enemy once the enemy starts routing, as has always been the case in total war.... the problem here in Napoleon though is because the rest of your army are gunmen and artillery, that unit of yours that is chasing the routing enemy will be decimated by your own lines of gunfire and artillery fire.

It is more beneficial to not tell any of your units to attack an enemy in this game. Better to just not touch your own units and let them shoot whoever comes within range with "fire at will" toggled on. This will be a disaster of course, because of the reasons stated above, but at least it will be less of a disaster compared to units chasing down enemies and friendly fire all over the place.

Again... You will be pausing the game. A lot.


OTHER THAN THAT. The campaign is just a worse version of Empire. Same engine. Many of the same exact units and buildings copy-pasted with literally no difference. A map about 1/50th of the size. And a grand total of four factions to choose from.

It's a like a bad DLC for Empire. The worst total war game by a lot.

I don't hate it completely... You can get a fun campaign in, but because there are only 4 factions in the whole game there isn't much replay value at all. One campaign is probably sufficient.

And battles can be really fun, despite everything I said above... it's only when you have large, epic battles that it's a disaster.. which is a real shame.


RATING:

For a Total War game: 1/10

For a RTS game in general: 5.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
