So I got to thinking more about this and the game is pretty gross. If you approach it from a purely gaming perspective, unless you are farming reward points and popping potions of youth, your first sim or sims are going to die. And farming "people" with preferred stats and traits in colony sims is not an uncommon or particularly varied concept. But in The Sims it feels very different. Maybe that's because the people are modeled so much towards human beings or maybe its by design. All I know is that in my game I married a hot criminal mastermind that flew off the handle randomly but was a decent companion. Too real at this point but I'll continue. Farming potions for both was taking all my time so she unfortunately had to go but I keep her urn next to the tv so all good. Now the task ahead was to find an heir to my castle and fortune. And by that I mean moderately sized middle class home and a couple hundred grand. It was tedious at first and involved a LOT of... interactions. And soon I found the only one traited to raising a kid was not into men, at all. This is where it got REALLY strange. Because from a gaming perspective the next logical course of action is to look for the next source of "people" to create new sims with. I was scrolling through the streets on full speed, waiting to see new faces. It got to the point where I was recognizing when teens turned to young adults so I could peg them once and get their profile details to make things quicker and that's when it hit me. The Sims is not a people simulator, its a grooming simulator.\t\t\t\t\t
