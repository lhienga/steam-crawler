\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was a huge The Sims fan. I might still be, can't tell based on the Sims 4. I'm quite hyped for Paralives and LifeByYou though, so I truly believe that I still am in love with the genre. The thing is: The Sims 4 is by far, not even close, the worst experience someone could have. At first I thought, ok, this is The Sims 2.5, but IT ISN'T. It is so much worse than The Sims 2 that I regret ever trying to compare them.
Why this is so bad, you say? Well, here are a few things:
- Even though it has been a while (10 years), I won't EVER FORGET that this """"life simulation"""" game DID NOT HAVE POOLS or BABIES when it launched.
- It is a major steback from its previous iterations yes, considering: ALL THREE previous iterations. It does not feature open world or customization like The Sims 3 had, the features it has were SO MUCH better on The Sims 2 (rental apartments, business, night life, etc). Hell, even The Sims (1) was better in all aspects.
- It feels like every "expansion" is charged twice what it was in The Sims 3 but worth a third of what it delivers. See the December 2023 Expansion, For Rent, that is just so bad. As another commentary said, they ruined apartments: TWICE!.
- EA should never be on the helm of this game. It is a cash grab. That's all it is.
- Allegedely, many "choices" were considering players performance, however this game doesn't perfom well enough to justify any of these choices. Every Expansion, Kit or DLC in general comes ridden with bugs that aren't fixed for a while.
- Customization falls flat. After playing The Sims 3 you can't just ignore the abyssal difference between them.,
- Life simulation is weak at best. Yes, you have some options (after spending more than 300 dollars on this, of course), but they are just bland.

It saddens me to be writing this, but it is the truth. I truly hope that either Paralives or Life By You destroy The Sims or that EA is not involved at all with the next iteration of this franchise.

Rest In Peace, The Sims. Thanks for the memories.\t\t\t\t\t
