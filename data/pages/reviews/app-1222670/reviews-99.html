\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs I've spent some time playing this game and got my mother into it, who has this year alone ranked up over 1000 hours' worth of playtime, this review will combine both a new and "older" player's opinion and insight.

If you're considering getting the game - just get it. I got it when it was still paid for and though I am still a bit salty about paying for it a few months prior to the game becoming free-to-play, both my mother and I have had a lot of fun with this game. Never hurts to try something new. Just YOLO it.

Now, there are a lot (and I mean a lot) of DLCs and packs to buy should you enjoy the game to the point of wanting to invest more money on it, and you can add free mods for both tangible (hairstyles/furniture) and intangible things (personality traits, etc.) from online websites (just make sure you have anti-virus/-malware software just in case). Mods are also easy to add to your game, I mean the program files literally already starts with a folder for mods so you're good to go quick and easy. I've added some mods I've found add to immersion quite nicely, whereas my mother prefers the DLCs and finds them perfectly suitable to what she wants from the game.

We all know EA and how they are, micro-transactions are the air they breathe. I agree that some DLCs should honestly have been base game features, e.g. Seasons, but honestly I understand why they're add-ons instead. Especially from a new player's POV.

Of course, some sims's actions are also buggy/lacking. Unless I explicitly tell my sim mothers to care for their gremlin-like offspring, they just play video games all day and leave everything to dad. Sims WILL put their dirty plates/glasses on half walls and coffee tables, then complain about not being able to reach them. For a game as old as this, there's no reason why this should still be considered acceptable, but this will all depend on what you play the game for so this is really just me nitpicking.\t\t\t\t\t
