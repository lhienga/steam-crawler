Whether I want to create a character, start a family, or let my imagination run wild, the game provides a platform for endless creativity. From designing elaborate homes to orchestrating devious plots, the possibilities are truly limitless.

The ability to cheat adds an extra layer of enjoyment, allowing me to tweak the game to suit my whims. Whether it's unlocking unlimited funds or exacting revenge on unruly Sims, the cheat system adds a whole new dimension to gameplay.

Speaking of gameplay, The Sims 4 manages to strike a balance between fun and frustration. Sure, trying to get my Sims to pull their lives together or navigating the complexities of relationships can be challenging, but it's all part of the immersive experience. And let's not forget the cathartic release of unleashing my inner mischief by trapping or even offing troublesome Sims.

While the base game itself offers hours of entertainment, the world of mods opens up a whole new realm of possibilities. Admittedly, some mods can be pricey, but with a bit of savvy searching online, there's a treasure trove of cheap or even free mods waiting to enhance the gameplay experience.

In conclusion, The Sims 4 is nothing short of amazing. It's a game that has stood the test of time, offering players like myself an outlet for creativity, imagination, and yes, even a bit of chaos. Despite the occasional expense associated with mods, the sheer joy and satisfaction it brings make it a solid 10/10 in my book\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had about 100 or so hrs in before I bought my first set of DLC's. Parenthood and the cats/dogs pack with my first pet added a lil bit more complexity and fun insanity to aging up my sims. At about 390 hrs in I bought vampires and werewolves. They added another layer of complexity to manage.

All in all the base game can get boring after a generation or so on normal lifespan. Long lifespan keeps the sims happier about how long someone has lived but they still get sad and wish it could be longer. I am now at 421 hrs of play and have just started vampire sims.

I have done werewolf sims and enjoyed the chaos that can happen on full moons and when fury gets high. I have multiple generations of one family and am enjoying seeing where it goes.

My thoughts are that the game doesn't really get fun til you start more than one family on the same save, which I recently learned at about the 366 hr mark of time played. Also most of the fun for me has come from DLC's that i have purchased.

If you want a simple occasional play that can still be crazy depending on what you do with your sims the base game is fine. If you want something more complex and generational intertwined then get the parenthood DLC, because it gives you game play with infant and toddler stages of growing up instead of the base newborn straight to toddler iirc that the base game does. If you want something more supernatural and complex, which I'm still learning, then get the vampires and werewolves DLC's. If you want your sim to have pets like a cat or a dog then get cats and dogs as well as the my first pet DLC's.

The possiblities with this game are literally endless its just a matter of how much time and money you want to invest in it. Time=lifespans and money= DLC's which the DLC's portion i would suggest waiting for sales to get because it can get expensive to get them all but it is well worth the money investment in my opinion.
