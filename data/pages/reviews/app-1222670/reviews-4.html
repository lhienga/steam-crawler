\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiven that you have can afford most of the DLC and don't mind the developers being blatantly ignorant to the state of the game, Sims 4 can be really fun. Buttttt considering you have to spend an entire month rent just for the game to be playable I CANNOT recommend it if you intend to just play the free base game. However, its not completely hopeless so below I'll go into more detail.

Sims 4 shines in a few spots like the functional, albeit outdated, gallery which makes downloading and sharing creations pretty easy. And the community is really talented so there are tons of creations to experience, given you have the paid expansion packs for the creations that feature them. Autonomy in the Sims 4 is also far better than the other games allowing you to reasonably manage a large household without a sim stroking out because you left them alone for one second. Granted, its less challenging than the other games but you can turn autonomy off if you so desire. Sims 4 customization and inclusiveness are probably the games best features as you really are limited to your imagination... well and your wallet. It also visually looks decent, for a game that came out the same time the iPhone 6 launched. But, in my opinion, all that doesn't make up for the month of homelessness you have to endure to afford the DLC to make the game any more exciting.

So what made the Sims 4 worth it for me? Mods. The game has a great modding community. Its easy to mod and you can add so much new content it'll make your head spin. Does that make up for having to sell your first born to experience in-game weather? That's your decision. But having to rely on modders or ample cash to make the game fun is why I gave it a negative review. Despite playing it regularly.

TL;DR. GET the sims 4 if you have copious amounts of money or don't mind having to add mods. DONT get the sims 4 if you just want to casually enjoy a life sim as Sims 2 and 3 are leagues better.

-find something you like, click download
-open your file explorer and open up your downloads folder
-open up the file where you will put this content (Documents>Electronic Arts>The Sims 4>Mods) and put the 2 windows next to eachother
-drag your downloaded content into the mods folder (If the content you downloaded is in a
zipped folder, double click on it and insert just the content, not the entire folder)
-Restart your game and enjoy the content you downloaded! :)
***If you download a save file, you will move it to the "saves" folder instead of the "mods"
folder.
