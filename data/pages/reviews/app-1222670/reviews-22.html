\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me begin my review by saying that I've been playing Sims franchise from day one, and was so addicted to The Sims 2 that I played it for months, all day long, until dawn, completely upsetting my biological clock, and almost got expelled from university because of it. Obviously I realise now how insane I was, but I have very fond memories of it. That's why I was very happy when they announced the 4th part after the 3rd - which was a bit of a disappointment for me. I subscribed to all the news, followed the developments, excitedly showed my (now ex) wife what innovative, exciting things were coming in The Sims 4.
It wasn't bad for a first try, after I got used to a few new things I quite liked it, but the vanilla game quickly became boring, so I decided to wait for a few DLCs. My list of problems starts here: for years Maxis/EA have been profiting (rather cheekily) from the fact that most gamers know what a Sims game can do and expect these features to appear; therefore they would be stupid to release it for free with the base game when they can charge 40-50 for a DLC. I know, these amounts are less burdensome for a gamer in a better financially positioned country than, say, me, but it's still a rip-off and a waste of money. Of course, the whole games industry tends to be like that these days, and I'm sadly being a boomer - "everything used to be better in the old days".
While EA's "everything can be DLC and we'll make a fortune of it" strategy is familiar to just about everyone, my problems would be that they are mind-bogglingly, humiliatingly cheeky, sloppy, sloppy and sloppy...
Reddit, EA forums, and the internet in general are full of bugs reports that make the game unenjoyable to play, often rendering it unplayable - sometimes months pass before a bug fix patch comes out - but that's for the better. In most cases, they don't give a about the bugs and flaws that remain in the game or DLC and never fix them. Every patch that fixes one bug brings 3 more, and despite all the feedback from players, they don't care about the happy player, they just want to milk the cow while they can.
And I'm still part of the problem, because I play the game (with some interruptions) and buy the DLCs... And I'm afraid that if suddenly no one would play Sims and buy games, they'd also be more likely to scrap the franchise before they'd realise: we've ripped off the people who make our living, it's time to change ourselves and get back to releasing quality, enjoyable, 100% finished products instead of cobbled together, money-sucking nonsense.
Nevertheless, if you have the money, play the game, enjoy the building, the stories you create, because this game capable of many great things - but don't spend all your lifesaving to this halfassed money-magnet.
