\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall this is currently (2024) a very good game. It would be a truly excellent, 10/10, game if only the makers were not so absolutely fundamentally focused on milking the players for every possible dollar. However, the insistence on constantly cranking out more and more minimum value 'add-on' packs, kits, and expansions, each of which will almost certainly break the true exceptional value of the game (the independent mods and expansions) over and over, many times each year, is close to unforgivable.

Making a game with the kind of massive, passionate, dedicated modding community that The Sims 4 has is, quite simply, an incredible accomplishment. The game makers ought to be seriously proud of this achievement. Instead, they seem to look constantly at that huge community and feel envy and resentment. All those extras that EA Games are not getting paid for, right? Stupidly ignoring the fact that what sales and dedication the game has (a game which was disappointing to fans for the many, many omitted features at launch) is hugely related to the amount of extra content and options that have cost EA nothing at all.

The Sims 4 is a decent enough base-game, but would have ceased to be played much several years ago were it not for the incredible mods that fix annoying bugs, correct silly limitations, and massively expand the possibility for endless play and replay enjoyment. That is not to suggest that every mod out there is going to be appealing, or even mildly useful to your playstyle, but there are many mods that add so much to the game that they are practically essential. Endless free extensions and expansions possible, keeping the game alive and engaging new players all these years and years after launch.

I only wish that EA had the simple, basic, good sense to do more to support those modders, with more resources on how the code functions, and above all, with more consultation, or at least advance warning, of any forced updates that will affect or often break all those mods.

It also needs to be said in any review that most of the expansions and packs that EA release are massively over-priced for what they actually contain. While some of the packs and expansions are worthwhile in terms of what they add to the game play, none add enough to really justify the full price that is asked, and one should only purchase them during the frequent sale events. In fact the sale events are so frequent precisely because that's the only time when most players will bother to add anything other than free mods.

Additionally, each of the expansions is something of a mixed bag unless you have some of the main and most popular mods, especially since so many of them add ever more random chances for sims to die suddenly and stupidly. The Seasons pack introduces random (and high) chances of your sims dying of heatstroke or freezing to death, for instance. The expansion that allows you to have tiny homes with more space-efficient furniture adds the significant chance of any sim with a murphy bed being killed in a silly accident, and so on, endlessly, with each expansion.

The good news is that the creators are currently working on The Sims 5, which will hopefully launch and stop them endlessly meddling with a good game with a great community. The bad news is that in the run-up and work on the new Sims 5, EA seem determined to milk every last cent possible with incessant overpriced packs to add simply things like new clothes, maybe a couple of hairstyles, or some other pointless, unnecessary thing they can charge far too much for and that most players have absolutely no interest in.

This is a game that succeed more in spite of the studio behind it than because of it, but succeed it has. The modding community, completely unpaid, have ensured that success, and continue to do so.\t\t\t\t\t
