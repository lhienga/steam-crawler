\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's decent enough fun for a few hours while you're just setting up the basics. Building a house, training skills, going to work (the same as every Sims game). But when that gets boring you'll need to start draining your savings for all the DLC to get to some actual content. Which is basically the same content that EA has been re-releasing since Sims 1 and 2. It's kind of nuts that the bulk of all expansion ideas came out of those two games. Anything original after that boils down to adapting those ideas for a revised control scheme, or dressing them up a little to jive with current cultural trends. Neither of which really justifies the price staying the same or increasing.

If ALL the DLC was capped at like $150 (full price), I'd probably own a fair chunk of it by now. But instead I've spent nothing beyond the retail price I paid for the base game when it came out (pre-Steam). EA must be really raking it in from the few people who'll occasionally buy a couple DLC packs, or those who dish out for all of it, because alienating regular players hasn't bothered them since Sims 3 which follows the same DLC structure, and the base game is now free.\t\t\t\t\t
