\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was fourteen going on fifteen when the original The Sims was released back in 2000, and have spent countless hours over the past twenty-four years playing the many incarnations of the game, including The Sims, The Sims (Console), The Sims Online, The Sims 2, The Sims 2 (PSP), The Sims 2 (Mobile), The Sims 3, The Sims 3 (Wii), The Sims Social, and now The Sims 4.
That said, Im really satisfied with this version of the game. It feels like a mixture of the three previous (main) titles. It works because its essentially a reboottaking place in an alternate universe inhabited by some familiar faces.
Does the game have bugs? Sure, but what game doesntespecially nowadays, where theres a constant demand for new content, which gets prioritized over fixing existing issues.
My primary grievance with the game is the lack of thought-out backgrounds for existing characters. If youre lucky, you get a short blurb with a brief description of the family dynamic. Otherwise, theyre all one-dimensional cardboard cutouts. Im hoping The Sims 5 is more like The Sims 2 in that regard, where each pre-made character has well-thought-out backgrounds, pre-existing relationships with other Sims, family trees etc.,
Overall, Im happy with the gameplay; the game itself looks beautiful, they continue to do a great job with the expansion packs, stuff packs, and kids -- its clear the devs have a lot of love for the franchise and are doing a great job.\t\t\t\t\t
