\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this has an extremely smooth build-mode, the game is mostly unplayable.

New content is being favoured over bug fixes as EA uses a predatory FOMO to promote its packs, encouraging players to sign up for email notifications and pushing ads for packs on the start screen and in-game. This game skews towards a younger audience more than previous instalments which should concern parents as exposing children to these marketing tactics is particularly nasty. Despite being riddled with bugs, there has been talk of AI being used for bug fixes and a culture of testers being encouraged not to report bugs that could push back DLC release dates. Devs have also been given public platforms, causing them to deal with hate and negativity from players that ought to be directed through proper channels to protect workers.

Below is a summary of my experience with the game:

- This is by far my most played game because of the build mode alone. The controls and graphics for build mode are attractive and the swatches for furniture allow for even extremely colour-ignorant people like myself to make good looking builds. Even so, in 2024, I would not recommend this as an architecture simulator as the player is limited by excessively strict snapping that cannot be disabled even with cheats designed to allow for greater freedom and by the lack of a colour wheel. Persistent problems also exist with the deletion of terrain paints and deformed terrain around platforms and curved walls which, like diagonal walls, were implemented without sufficient support for objects to function with, these bugs have existed since the release of these features. The worlds are often largely unchangeable set-dressing that makes a custom building seem like an eyesore unless it is matched to the world's given style.

- The art style of sims is frustrating as the clay-like texture and cartoonish expressions distort what would otherwise be human looking features. Even when trying to make a sim based on a real person, it is nearly impossible to get a clear resemblance due to the over-exaggerated animation. Customisation is deceptively limited as there are no colour wheels, clothing basics (such as high-rise trousers) are missing, the fit of clothing is extremely poor when adapting to a sim's body shape, and, while passing efforts have been made to include options for a variety of people, these are often added to the game and then dropped. Due to pressure from players, more pronoun options, varied skin tones, and afro-texture hairstyles were added, however, pronoun implementation is often ungrammatical in game, genetics don't function correctly with skin tones, curly hair textures are still lacking, and fat and muscle distribution is unrealistic with poor textures.

- Gameplay is my least used part of the game. Once again, features that have been added have caused bugs that go unaddressed and fleshed-out content (that is also bug infested) is often locked behind DLC. As a life simulator it is unsatisfying that the game centres around dollhouse-style gamplay, with uninteresting worlds that feel devoid of life due to the amount of set dressing and the amount of sims that spawn randomly rather than having an in-game home. The same NPCs live forever, simulation lag is persistent, and sims are over animated. The progression of relationships is extremely fast which causes social connections to feel worthless rather than achieved. Emotions shown in the corner are extremely distracting and often prohibit player actions. Autonomy is bugged and sims disappear off to do things the second an action isn't queued, causing gameplay to feel rushed. This is made worse by consistent action cancelling.

TLDR: Not recommended for encouraging obsessive FOMO buying, outdated spaghetti code filled with bugs, and excessive limitations.\t\t\t\t\t
