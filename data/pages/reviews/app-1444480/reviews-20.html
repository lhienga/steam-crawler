Later on you might get a little stuck, but you can get through by just trying a bunch of things. I'm currently at bit switchers (delay components in a circuit) and these delays can be a tad bit unintuitive in how to setup at first (I had tried to setup a loop and kept getting an error about a short circuit even though that's not supposed to happen with these components...so watched a short youtube video and it was essentially the same thing just spread out a bit more...so basically a tiny bit of a game engine logic problem, but no biggie).
In any case, this is great in a way that reminds me of the logic circuits in Minecraft or from my reading in the book "Elements of Computing Systems, Building a Modern Computer from First Principles by Noam Nissan and Shimon Schocken"...You'll be sure to learn a ton that's actually useful while having fun.
I noticed that in the tutorial window it's starting to mention latch circuits. In Minecraft you can make those to keep a door open or closed with a press of a button, things like that. Haven't gotten to the part of the game where you start to use them yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great logic and computer science simulation. I went into this being already somewhat familiar with logic gates and CPU Architecture. This however is quite challenging.

The advancement per level is quite steep. If you are coming at this completely new then in campaign mode you essentially get a single lesson and a single example before being moved on. The "Show Solution" button doesn't seem to work either. I would love to be able to see the intended or ideal solutions after I have completed a challenge but they just don't seem to be there. I tried going back and "failing" a level that I had already completed to see if the solution is only prevented after try-and-fail but I still could not get it to work.

I would strongly recommend that if you are playing campaign mode and are getting stuck that you have a play in sandbox mode. Make a note of the components available to you and see if you can follow the logic of circuits online to further understand what's happening.

I think this could be an incredible teaching tool. I hope development continues and perhaps optional side challenges added for each "level" to further learning and understanding of the current level of complexity. I hope that the ideal schematics for each level get added in too - though I understand that some of those schematics are steam achievements for the user to work out on their own.

My tips:

In each lesson you have more gate options than you need.
You can build everything out of just NAND gates.
Learn De Morgan's Laws when you get to them
Experiment in Sandbox
Pay close attention to the Truth Tables for each lesson.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am studying electrical/electronics engineering at university and this is teaching me more about digital logic, in a practical way, than my actual course.
As an electronics study resource / 'lab' game? It's excellent! However, if you don't understand logic at all, the latter levels are going to be REALLY hard.

It does a decent enough job at explaining how gates work etc but I found it ramps up significantly when you get to certain points.
I find it a lot easier/more approachable vs Zachtronics games, but it does get very hard; you're going to need to do your own research and maybe even get a digital logic/computer architecture textbook.

I wish the game offered 'hints' - solutions are well explained in videos, but not available for every level.
I also wish the campaign utilised the 'delay' and 'gate' scores. With logic, it is very much about optimisation and reducing the number of gates. The campaign doesn't limit you to this, just limits you in a certain amount of space (well, at least, so far!)

It's a feature in the sandbox/component factory, so I'd love to see it implemented into levels as well.
It would be helpful for when you go 'off the rails' - i.e, designing a solution that is far more complicated than it needs to be!

100% worth the price though. It's a pretty good logic sim in itself as well. Can export VHDL etc.
